INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Chevron Service Station located near the junction of Dairy
Road and Ohekani Street in Kahului on the island of Maui Hawaii The general location
and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering recommenda
tions resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering analyses
These recommendations are intended for the design of foundation site grading and
excavation only The findings and recommendations presented herein are subject to the
limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a new Chevron Service Station near the junction of
Dairy Road and Ohekani Street in Kahului on the island of Maui Hawaii Based on the
information provided the site consists of two lots with a total area of approximately
60 000 square feet We understand that the proposed new facility will consist of a one
story building several pump islands a car wash facility and three underground fuel
storage tanks side by side
The finish floor elevations for the proposed structures such as the food mart car
wash facility and pump islands were not available at the time this report was prepared
Based on information provided we understand that the proposed structures will be
constructed near the existing grades Based on this information and the relatively level
ground at the site we anticipate that minor cuts and fills on the order of less than one foot

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
Maui the second largest island of the Hawaiian Archipelago was formed by the
activity of two volcanoes East Maui better known as Haleakala and West Maui The
broad gently sloping plain connecting the two volcanoes known as the Maui Isthmus
was formed when lavas of the Haleakala Volcano banked against the already existing
West Maui Volcano
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3670 00 Page 5
The East Maui Volcano better known as the Haleakala Mountain makes up the
eastern portion of the island Haleakala Mountain is considered to be dormant with the
last eruption taking place on the southerly side of the mountain around 1790 Haleakala
Mountain consists largely of the Honomanu Volcanic Series that built the mountain from
three rift Zones probably during the Pliocene and early Pleistocene Epochs
The volcanic rocks of the West Maui have been divided into three volcanic series
The Wailuku Volcanic Series is the oldest and is comprised of the basaltic lava flows and
associated pyroclastic and intrusive rocks that built the major shield volcano The
Wailuku Volcanic Series was covered by a thin discontinuous frosting of andesitic and
trachytic flows domes and pyroclastic deposits known as the Honolua Volcanic Series
After a long period of inactivity where erosion incised deep valleys into the volcanic flows
volcanic activity returned with the eruptions that produced the flows and cones of the
Lahaina Volcanic Series
The project site is situated on the western flank of Haleakala Mountain The
project area is flanked by alluvial deposits Recent land development projects have
brought the project site to its present form

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The proposed project site is generally underlain by stiff to very stiff fills over
medium stiff to stiff alluvial soils to the maximum depths drilled of about 21 5 to 30 feet
below the existing ground surface Based on the results of our field exploration and
laboratory testing we believe that a shallow foundation system bearing on the
recompacted near surface stiff soils or compacted new fills may be used to support the
proposed structures Slabs may be supported on grade provided that the subgrades are
prepared as recommended herein
We anticipate minor site grading consisting of cuts and fills on the order of less
than one foot will be needed to provide level areas for buildings In addition we
anticipate that an excavation of about 12 to 15 feet below the existing ground surface and
subsequent backfilling will be required for construction of the new underground fuel
storage tank structures
Detailed discussion and recommendations for design of foundation site grading
excavation and other geotechnical aspects of the project are presented in the following
sections of this report
Foundations
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered during our exploration we
believe that a shallow foundation system consisting of spread and or continuous footings
may be used to support the proposed one story building food mart car wash facility and
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3670 00 Page 8
pump island canopy An allowable bearing pressure of up to 3 000 p s f may be used for
the design of footings bearing on the recompacted stiff near surface soils and or
compacted new fill material The allowable bearing pressure recommended is for dead
plus live loads and may be increased by one third 1 3 for transient loads such as
temporary wind and or seismic forces Footings should be embedded a minimum of
18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade
If foundations are located next to utility trenches or easements they should be
embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane extending
upward from the bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility
lines This requirement is necessary to avoid surcharging adjacent below grade
structures with additional structural loads and to reduce the potential for foundation
settlement
We estimate that footing settlements under the anticipated design loads for
footings bearing on the recompacted in situ soils and or compacted new fills as
recommended herein to be less than one inch total with differential settlements on the
order of 41 2 inch
For footings bearing on the in situ soils or new engineered fills the bottom of the
footing excavations should be recompacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction prior to placement of reinforcing steel or concrete Any soft and or loose
materials encountered at the bottom of footing excavations should be over excavated to
expose the underlying firm materials The over excavation should be backfilled with
select granular material compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction In
addition all cobbles or boulders encountered at the bottom of footing excavations should
be removed and the voids resulting from removal should be backfilled with compacted
select granular material

Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by frictional resistance
between the base of the spread footing and the bearing materials and by passive earth
pressure developed against the near vertical faces of the embedded portion of
footings A coefficient of friction of 0 30 may be used between the base of the footings
and the supporting materials Resistance due to passive pressure may be calculated
using an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth p c f
for footings embedded in the in situ soil These values assume that the concrete for
footings is placed directly against the footing excavations Unless covered by pavements
or slabs the passive resistance in the upper 12 inches below finished grade should be
neglected
We anticipate that the proposed structures such as the canopy for the pump
islands will be subjected to uplift forces Uplift loads may be resisted by a combination of
the following 1 dead weight of the structures 2 weight of backfill material above the
footing where applicable and 3 side shear between the sides of the footings and
surrounding soils Contribution of dead weight from the backfill may be estimated using a
unit weight of 110 p c f For side shear between the sides of the footings and the
adjacent soils a shear value of 150 p s f may be used
Footing excavations should be observed by a representative of Geolabs Hawaii
prior to placement of reinforcing steel or concrete to confirm the competency of the

Slabs On Grade
We anticipate that concrete slabs on grade will be used for the proposed
structures such as the mini mart car wash facility and pump islands The near surface
soils encountered at the proposed site consist of clayey and silty fill soils which exhibit a
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3670 00 Page 10
slight expansion potential Therefore we recommend that the subgrade be properly
prepared and kept moist prior to placement of cushion fill and slab concrete The
subgrades for slabs on grades should be scarified to a depth of at least 8 inches
moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture and compacted
to not less than 90 percent relative compaction Any cobbles or boulders encountered at
the subgrade level should be removed and backfilled with compacted select granular
material Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a
percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil determined in accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91 Optimum moisture is the water content percentage
by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
For more uniform support of interior slabs mini mart we recommend that the
concrete slab on grade be supported on a minimum 4 inch cushion fill The cushion fill
layer should consist of open graded gravels such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33
No 67 gradation which will also serve as a capillary moisture break The slab cushion
fill should be placed as soon as possible after the scarification and recompaction of the
existing subgrade soils as recommended above For slabs on grade with moisture
sensitive surfacing we recommend that an impermeable membrane be placed over the
cushion fill to reduce potential migration of moisture vapor through the concrete slab In
order to promote more uniform curing of the slab concrete and to provide protection of the
vapor membrane we recommend that a 2 inch layer of moist fine sand be placed above
the membrane prior to placing the slab concrete The sand should be moistened slightly
prior to placing concrete
Where concrete slabs will be subjected to vehicular traffic such as the car wash
facility and pump islands we recommend that the slab be supported on a 6 inch layer of
base course compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction in lieu of the
4 inch cushion fill layer and 2 inch sand The base course should consist of crushed
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3670 00 Page 11
basalt aggregate The subgrade should also be scarified to a minimum depth of 8 inches
moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction For design of structural
slabs a modulus of subgrade reaction of 250 pounds per cubic inch p c i may be used
for the compacted base course The thickened edges of slabs adjacent to any unpaved
area should be embedded at least 12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade
Exterior concrete walkways may be required for the proposed project We
recommend that a minimum 6 inch thick layer of select granular material or select borrow
subbase be provided below the concrete walkway slab Construction joints should be
provided at intervals equal to the width of the walkways with expansion joints at right
angle intersections
The area adjacent to the slabs should be backfilled tightly against the slab with
non expansive impervious soil and graded to divert water away from the slab to reduce
the potential of water ponding around the foundation

Final grading plans were not available at the time this report was prepared

one foot relative to the existing ground surface to provide a level pad for the new

subsequent backfilling of up to about 15 feet will be needed for the proposed

Site grading operations should be observed by a_ representative of
Geolabs Hawaii It is important that a representative from our office observe whether the
exposed soil conditions during excavation are similar to those encountered during our
GEOLABS HAWAII

exploration The following recommendations are intended to provide guidelines for the
design of site grading work
At the onset of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable
materials should be removed and disposed of off site
Following the clearing and grubbing operation areas to receive fills and the finish
subgrade should be scarified to a minimum depth of 8 inches properly moisture
conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture content and compacted to
a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction For subgrades of improvements subjected
to vehicular traffic such as the pump islands car wash facility and pavements the
compaction requirement should be increased to a minimum of 95 percent relative
compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a
percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil determined in accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91 Optimum moisture is the water content percentage
by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
Any soft loose weak or yielding areas encountered during site grading should be
over excavated to expose firm ground and the resulting excavation should be backfilled
with engineered fill compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction In
addition all cobbles and boulders encountered during site grading should be removed
and properly disposed of off site
The on site soils may be reused as a source of fill and backfill material provided
that it contains no cobbles boulders and lumps greater than 3 inches in largest
dimension The on site silty and clayey soils should be properly moisture conditioned to
at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture content placed in level lifts not exceeding
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3670 00 Page 13
8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction
If imported fill materials are required the imported material should consist of non
expansive material such as crushed coral mudrock basalt or cinder sand The material
should be well graded from coarse to fine with no particles larger than 3 Inches in largest
dimension and should contain between 10 and 30 percent particles passing the
No 200 sieve The material should have a laboratory CBR value of 12 or more and
should have a maximum swell of 1 percent or less when tested in accordance with ASTM
Test Designation D 1883 Imported fill material should be tested by Geolabs Hawaii for
conformance with these recommendations prior to delivery to the project site for its
intended use
Generally the fills and backfills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding
8 inches in loose thickness moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum
moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction The
compaction requirement of the fill and backfill material within the upper 2 feet below the
finish subgrade in the pavement areas should be increased to a minimum of 95 percent
relative compaction Fill placement should commence at the lowest point and continue
upward in level compacted lifts

underground fuel storage tanks excavation will consist of medium dense sands or
medium stiff to stiff silty clayey soils Therefore we believe that the new underground fuel
storage tanks may be supported on a minimum 6 inch thick open graded granular
material such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation sand or similar

material to serve as bedding material The excavated tank subgrade should be

Excavation
In order to install the new underground fuel storage tanks we anticipate that an
excavation on the order of up to 12 to 15 feet below the existing ground surface will be
required Based on the subsurface conditions encountered we anticipate that
conventional excavation equipment may be used for the proposed excavation However
cobbles and hard material were frequently encountered in the upper 10 feet of our
borings Therefore we envision that cobbles occasional boulders and localized hard
zones may be encountered in the proposed excavation The method and equipment to
be used for excavation should be determined by the contractor subject to practical limits
and safety considerations The excavated materials should be stockpiled no closer to the
edge of the excavation than a horizontal distance equal to the depth of the excavation in
order to reduce the potential for ground movement due to surcharge loading

Utility Trench
We envision that new utility lines would be required for the proposed project In

Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site for geotechnical engineering exploration
were explored by drilling and sampling three borings to depths of about 21 5 to 30 feet
below the existing ground surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan

stem augers

examination in the field by our engineer who monitored the drilling operations on a near
continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by testing in
the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representation of the materials
encountered are provided on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 3

ASTM Test Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils
by driving a 3 inch O D Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling
30 inches The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the last 12 inches of an 18 inch
drive are shown on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths

evaluate the dry density and moisture content relationship of the near surface soils

near surface bulk sample to evaluate the pavement support characteristics of the soils
Results of the test are presented on Plate B 4
