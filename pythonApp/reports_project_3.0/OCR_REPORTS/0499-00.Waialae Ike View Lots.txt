This report contains our recommendation of a soil and founda
tion investigation for the proposed residence in Waialae Iki
View Lots Oahu Hawaii
The purpose of our study was to investigate the subsurface
conditions at the site and recommend an appropriate type of
foundation for the proposed residence
FIELD INVESTIGATION
To explore the subsurface conditions at the site three test
borings were drilled utilizing truck mounted rotary auger
equipment The locations of the borings are shown on the
Site Plan Plate 1 An engineer from our office was present
throughout the field investigation to direct the drilling

operations and observe site conditions The Log of Borings
are presented on Plates 2 A to 2 C All materials have been
described according to the Unified Soil Classification System
and local geologic terminology
The building site is on the northeast corner of the inter
section of Laukahi Street and Kumakani Loop The lots slope
from an elevation of 496 on the northern end approximately
18 down to an elevation at 476 along the southern edge The
site has previously been cleared of most vegetation Some
stockpiles of loose material are located on the site and are
shown on the Site Plan Plate 1 Visible outcrops of boulders
can be observed throughout the site The site is underlain
by very dense reddish brown sandy silt with basalt gravels and
cobbles A cluster of boulder outcrops were found along the
west portion of the lot All three borings encountered
boulders or bedrock at depths of six to seven feet below the
existing surface No groundwater was encountered in any of
the borings
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Except for the loose stockpiles the site appears to be original
ground No evidence of any deep fills were found Therefore
Page 3
W O 499 00
it is our opinion that the site is suitable for the proposed
residence supported on conventional shallow footings Slabs
on grade may be used The proposed residence may be supported
on spread or continuous footings bearing in the undisturbed
site soils A bearing value of 2500 pounds per square foot
may be used for dead plus live loads Continuous footings
should have a minimum width of 12 inches Pressures may be
increased by one third when considering wind or seismic loads
Footings for the two story residence should be founded a
minimum of 12 inches below final adjacent grade We anti
cipate that settlements from the imposed building loads will
be minimal
Due to the presence of some boulders in the upper six feet
some difficulty may be encountered in footing excavations
Jack hammering may be required to break up and remove large
boulders in the footing excavations Bedrock or large
boulders may be encountered at about 6 feet below the existing
surface Any voids due to the removal of rock should be
filled with footing concrete or granular material compacted
to a minimum of 90 of maximum density as determined by
ASTM D 1557 70T

Slabs On Grade
We recommend that a 4 inch open graded granular pad be used
for support of the slabs on grade This granular layer will
act as a capillary break beneath the slab In areas where
moisture sensitive flooring is to be used we recommend that
a waterproof membrane be placed above the granular layer
All slabs on grade should be structurally separated from wall
footings
