FIELD EXPLORATION
The field exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling one boring each
at the eighteen sites for a total of eighteen test borings Arrowhead Contracting Inc
located the test borings in the field We drilled the test borings using a truck mounted
drill rig equipped with continuous flight solid stem augers
Our geologist classified the materials encountered in the test borings by visual
and textural examination in the field and monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified
Soil Classification System as shown on Plate 3 Graphic representations of the
materials encountered are presented on the Logs of Borings Plates 4 through 21
Samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 2 inch
OD standard penetration sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow
counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an 18 inch drive
are shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate
sample depths
TEST BORINGS
The test borings designated as Boring through NSPH 15 except
Plates 2 1 through 2 17 However the test boring location plan for Boring No FS 1 was
not provided to us The test borings extended to depths of about 19 to 20 5 feet below
the existing ground surface The subsurface conditions encountered in the borings
drilled at each site are presented in the following subsections It should be noted that
the groundwater levels encountered in the borings are expected to vary with tidal
fluctuation seasonal precipitation storm surge conditions and other factors
We drilled Boring through NSPH 15 except NSPH 5 to maximum
depths of about 19 to 20 5 feet below the existing ground surface within Naval Station
Pearl Harbor We encountered various materials in the test borings including fill
volcanic tuff coralline deposit harbor sediments lagoonal deposits river deposits and
alluvial deposits as indicated in our logs of borings We encountered groundwater at
depths ranging from about 6 5 to 20 feet below the existing ground surface at the time
of our exploration However groundwater was not encountered in Boring No NSPH 9
We drilled Boring through NCTAMS 3 to a maximum depth of
about 20 5 feet below the existing ground surface within Naval Computer and
Telecommunications Area Master Station In general we encountered a layer of fill
which consisted of stiff to very stiff silty clay clayey silt to depths of about 0 5 to 6 5 feet

LABORATORY TESTING
We tested selected soil samples from the field exploration in our laboratory with
an emphasis on the classification of the soils The following laboratory tests were
performed on selected soil samples obtained from the field exploration
Moisture Content ASTM D 2216 determinations were performed on selected
soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties The test
results are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Forty eight Atterberg Limits tests ASTM D 4318 were performed on selected soil
samples to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits and to aid in soil classification The test
results are summarized on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths Graphic
presentation of the test results is provided on Plates 22 1 through 22 8
Thirty four Sieve Analysis tests ASTM C 117 C 136 were performed on
selected soil samples to evaluate the gradation characteristics of the soils and to aid in
soil classification Graphic presentations of the grain size distributions are provided on
Plates 23 1 through 23 7
Four Organic Content tests ASTM D 2974 were performed on selected soil
samples to evaluate the organic content of the soils and to aid in soil classification The
test results are provided on Plate 24
