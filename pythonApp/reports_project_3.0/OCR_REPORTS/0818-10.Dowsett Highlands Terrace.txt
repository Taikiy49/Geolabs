INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our additional soil
engineering investigation at the above referenced project
site
A preliminary soil and geology study was performed by our
office in 1979 and the following report was issued
Preliminary Soils and Geology Investigation
Dowsett Highlands Terrace Nuuanu Valley
Oahu Hawaii W O 818 00 dated March 2 1979
The above_report included both the Lower Lots 1 12 and
Upper Lots 13 55 development sites
Due to the very steep terrain and heavy vegetation at the
site the initial soil study consisted primarily of site
reconnaissance and shallow near surface hand sampling in
the interior portions of the site The initial study indi
cated generally competent soils at the Lower site and
minimal site grading was planned Therefore the
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 818 10 P 2
recommendations presented in our previous report could
still be utilized here Generally competent soils were
also encountered at the Upper site however due to the
high cuts and fills planned up to 20 to 25 feet addi
tional exploration was recommended in this area after
some site clearing to provide access for drilling equipment
The site has not been cleared however soil data for the
high cuts and fills are required at this time for further
Site grading design
The purpose of this investigation was to obtain additional
subsurface soil data within the Upper development site
to supplement the previous recommendations for the site
grading design of the project
This additional study was concentrated in the natural
drainageways where high fills are planned and in some of
the major slope cut areas
Since site clearing has not yet commenced the area is still
inaccessible for conventional truck mounted drilling equip
ment or backhoe equipment Therefore portable hand carried
drilling equipment were utilized for this additional study
Based on the data from our additional investigation we
have developed general recommendations for the development
site grading

PLANNED DEVELOPMENT
The preliminary plans for the upper development area include
an access road through the site about 1460 feet long and
45 residential lots Major site grading will be required
for the construction of the access road Cuts and fills
up to 20 feet are planned Drain pipes up to 48 inches
in diameter are planned under the fills over the natural
drainageways Most of the lots will not be graded
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located at the slope break on the south
east side of Nuuanu Valley Oahu Hawaii The site is
bounded on the southeast by a spur of the Koolau Range for
ming a steep ridge on the southeast flank of Nuuanu Valley
The north south and western portions of the site are bounded
by residential developments similar in character to the
proposed development
The area consists mainly of four moderately steep ridges
interfingered with four steep sided ravines A small area
in the central southeast section of the site consists of
steep sided 1 1 to nearly vertical rocky highlands
The lower crests of the ridges are moderately steep 4 1
to 3 1 increasing to slopes of up to 2 1 in the upper
sections below the rocky highlands The crests tend to be
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 818 10 P 4
grassy except for the heavily vegetated narrow central
ridge and the ridge at the southern end which has been
stripped of vegetation similar to the ridge crest in the
lower area The ravine areas forming drainageways for the
area are very steep sided up to 1 1 and generally very
heavily vegetated with trees ferns and various shrubs
The bottom of the ravines generally consist of large
boulders stream gravel and some soft silt sand and clay
Each drainageway supports a small stream some of which
may be intermittent but subject to substantial flows during
heavy rains
The large drainageway in the center of the site tends to
be less steep 2 1 Several small springs and seeps were
noted in this area along with heavy surface runoff during
field exploration after heavy rains
At the head of the large central drainageway is a large
talus slope The talus deposit consists of a deposit of
large boulders up to 8 feet in diameter with a moderate
slope of about 4 1
Access to the site is restricted to Ragsdale Place on the
north and Kamaaina Drive on the south There are no known
existing structures on the site except a loose stone wall
running downslope near the center of the site

GEOLOGY
The project site is located on a ridge of the Koolau Range
on the upper slopes of Nuuanu Valley This ridge is an
erosional remnant eroded from the Leeward flank of the
Koolau Range and consisting of steep rocky slopes of rela
tively unweathered dense basalt The upper rocky slopes
consist of massive basalt outcrops forming vertical cliffs
fractured in place basalt and dense boulders some very
large At the foot of the slopes extensive erosion of
the upper valley sides has deposited large quantities of
boulders and other talus debris Much of this boulder
colluvium has weathered in place forming a thick soil
layer of basically clayey silt with some boulders This
layer has been further eroded into rounded ridges and steep
sided valleys to from the present topography
The site is situated near the sharp break in slopes that
marks the upper extent of the older alluvium Some of the
more recent talus deposits presently exist as piles of dense
unweathered boulders and debris Two such talus deposits
were located in the field exploration and it is possible
that other deposits may be masked by the vegetation and
soil cover
Since the valley flanks are actively undergoing erosion
numerous large surface boulders can be found throughout the
site especially in the stream beds of the drainageways

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The additional field exploration generally confirmed the
presence of relatively competent subsoils at the site
Due to the high rainfall in the area the in place moisture
contents of the soils are high and the soils exhibit moderate
compressibility and shear strength
Relatively high fills are planned in the existing drainage
ways therefore special design procedures and field moni
toring of the site grading operations are important to this
development
The difficult terrain and heavy vegetation at the site
limited the field exploration Therefore continuous
monitoring of the site clearing and grading operations
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 818 10 P 8
is essential to attempt to detect site conditions which
would require design modifications
Site Grading
In general the site grading will consist of cutting the
upper ridges and filling the drainageways to construct
the access roadway through the site Most of the proposed
lot areas will not be graded
Cuts and fills up to 20 feet are planned
Site Preparation The site to be graded should be
cleared of all vegetation debris and soft material
Prior to filling the existing ground should be
scarified to a minimum of 6 inches and compacted to
a minimum of 85 of its maximum dry density esta
blished by ASTM D 1557 test method
located in a high rainfall area the on site subsoils
have in place moisture contents about 10 to 30 per
cent above its optimum moisture content
Therefore drying of the excavated material will be
required before using it as fill material
It is our opinion that the normal 90 compaction
standard used in other drier areas should be modified

House Foundations
The design of the house foundations is not included in the
scope of this report and should be done on an individual
lot basis by an engineer Houses constructed on sloping
areas steeper than about 2 horizontal to 1 vertical should
be done with care
The foundation design should consider the topographic and
subsurface conditions at the site The foundations should
be set back from the tops of slopes or embedded a suffi
cient distance below the finish grade to resist the sur
face soil creep movements
Retaining Walls
Retaining walls may be utilized along portions of the
roadway and for some of the driveways
Wing walls are required for the drain inlet structures
An allowable bearing value of 3000 p s f is recommended
for the walls resting on the stiff natural ground or com
pacted fill
Well graded granular material such as base course rock
3B fine gravel sand or equivalent should be used as
backfill material directly behind the walls This would
reduce the compactive effort required for the backfill

Rockfalls
As with any development along a hillside there is a
psssibility of individual boulders rolling down from the
hillside
Typical methods to reduce the hazard of falling boulders
include a boulder sweep and or rock bolting construction
of a boulder catchment area barrier fences or walls and
the use of the natural vegetation to help stop the boulders
Due to the very steep topography and heavy vegetation
the practicality of a boulder sweep or catchment area is
questionable
The natural trees along the upper portions of the site
should be retained to act as a natural barrier against
rolling boulders Some form of barrier fence or wall can
also be considered

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present
during earthwork construction particularly the fill place
ment slope cuts and to monitor the contractor s grading
operations
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be
observed by a soils engineer during construction to see
that adequate bearing has been obtained and that the
subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings
of our subsurface investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive
tested and approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling
to the site

upon the above inspections
