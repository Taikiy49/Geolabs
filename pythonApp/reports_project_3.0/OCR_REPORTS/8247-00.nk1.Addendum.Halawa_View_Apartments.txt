Seismic Design Considerations 
Based on the IBC 2012, the project site may be subject to seismic activity, and 
seismic design considerations will need to be addressed. Seismic shear wave velocity 
profiling using seismic piezocone penetration testing (SCPT) equipment was performed 
in Boring No. 4 to analyze the seismic design considerations for the project site.  
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered in our field exploration, the 
weighted average shear wave velocity for the materials within the upper 100 feet of the 
soil profile at the test location is on the order of about 1,217 feet per second, 
corresponding to a Site Class C (“Very Dense Soil and Soft Rock”) soil profile type 
based on the ASCE Standard ASCE/SEI 7-10 (Table No. 20.3-1), referenced by the 
International Building Code, 2012 Edition.

Revised Seismic Lateral Earth Pressures 
Due to the change in building code and correspondingly lower design peak 
ground acceleration, the seismic lateral earth pressures for retaining structures at the 
project site were re-analyzed.  
Forces due to dynamic lateral earth pressures may need to be considered in the 
design of retaining structures. For non-restrained conditions, dynamic lateral earth forces 
due to seismic loading (a = 0.299g) may be estimated by using 5.7H2 pounds per linear 
foot of wall length for level backfill conditions, where H is the height of the wall in feet. It 
should be noted that the dynamic lateral earth force provided assumes that the wall will be 
allowed to move laterally by up to about 1 to 2 inches in the event of an earthquake. For 
restrained conditions, dynamic lateral earth forces due to seismic loading may be 
estimated by using 10.1H2 pounds per linear foot of wall length for level backfill conditions. 
The resultant force should be assumed to act through the mid-height of the wall. The 
dynamic lateral earth forces are in addition to the static lateral earth pressures provided 
above. An appropriately reduced factor of safety may be used when dynamic lateral earth 
forces are accounted for in the design of the retaining structures.
