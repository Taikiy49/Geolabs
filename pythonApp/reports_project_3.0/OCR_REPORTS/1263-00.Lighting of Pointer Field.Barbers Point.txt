Foundation Investigation
Lighting of Pointer Field No 723
NAS Barbers Point Oahu Hawaii
This report summarizes the findings of the subsurface exploration
at the subject site and presents recommendations for the design and
construction of the foundations of the 8 floodlight poles at NAS
Barbers Point Oahu Hawaii The general location of the site is
shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is our understanding that a lighting system for the softball
playing area of Pointer Field consisting of 8 floodlight poles about
50 feet high will be constructed Each light stand will be a single
pole without guy anchors
W O 1263 00 Page 2
The design overturning moment on the foundation probably will not
exceed 100 kip feet under the design wind conditions The vertical
load is known at this time The horizontal loads probably vary from 5
to 15 kips
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located at Pointer Field inside the Barbers
Point Naval Air Station Oahu Hawaii Pointer Field is situated
between Saratoga and Enterprise Avenues about 1 700 feet south of the
Barbers Point Naval Air Station main gate
The site is graded level and grassed At present it is an
active softball playing field with 2 Steel framed bleachers for
viewing
SCOPE OF OUR SERVICE
To explore the subsurface conditions at the site 8 test borings
were drilled at the proposed light stand location utilizing truck
mounted rotary drilling equipment Depths of the borings ranged from
15 0 to 16 5 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate
locations of the borings are shown on the enclosed Site Plan Plate 2

The soils encountered in the borings were classified by visual
and textural examination in the field by our engineer who cont inuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory All
soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System Descriptions of the soils encountered and a
graphical presentation of the materials encountered are presented on
the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 8
Moisture and density determinations were performed on selected
samples to aid in the classification of soil properties The results
of these tests are presented on the boring logs at the appropriate
sample location
To provide data for evaluating the strength characteristics of
the soils encountered four 4 unconfined compression tests were
attempted on the near surface samples However due to loss of the
weak cementation in sampling process and with the poor core recovery
no suitable sample could be prepared for strength testing
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The site is level with occasional outcrops of coral The
subsurface conditions consist of 1 to feet of clayey silt soil
underlain by thick white to tan white coral sand and gravel deposit

The upper 5 feet of coral sand and gravel deposit is moderately dense
with some cementation The material below contains some voids and is
less cemented Because of the very poor core recovery standard split
spoon sampling was made when possible The coral deposit graded to
moderately cemented again below a depth of 10 feet
No groundwater was encountered during our field exploration
However the groundwater should be expected at about Elevation 1 0
foot mean sea level
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
LOND
For the support of the light stands drilled cast in place
caisson foundations are recommended
For passive earth pressure resistance against the side of the
caisson an equivalent fluid density of 300 p c f may be used The
resistance in the upper 18 inches below the ground surface should be
ignored in the foundation design The coefficient of friction can be
taken as 0 45 at the base and the sides of the caissons The maximum
allowable vertical bearing pressure at the bottom of the caisson
should not exceed 7000 p s

Generally the caissons should have a minimum diameter of 30
inches and should be embedded at least 6 feet below the ground
surface in undisturbed coral formation The bottom of the caisson
should be cleaned of loose materials The caisson excavations should
be inspected by a soils engineer prior to placing concrete or
reinforcing steel
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the light pole foundations should be
forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written comments prior
to construction to see that the intent of our recommendations is
included in the project plans and specifications
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the caisson foundation excavation be
observed by the soils engineer to see that the subsurface conditions
are consistent with the findings of our investigation The
recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the above
inspections
