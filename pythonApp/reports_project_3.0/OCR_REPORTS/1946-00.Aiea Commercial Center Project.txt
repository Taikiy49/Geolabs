SUMMARY OF RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that the project
site was underlain by generally hard clayey silt
fill and stiff clayey silt alluvium over very
stiff or dense decomposed rock
Based on our findings we recommend that shallow
spread or continuous footings be used to support
the proposed commercial center on the hard
surface fill An allowable bearing value up to
3000 p s f may be used for the footing
foundation design of the building
For the eastern building corner adjacent to the
existing underground box drain easement either
deep footing foundations or driven piles should
be considered to reduce the potential adverse
influence of future trench excavation next to the
building foundations
The text of this report should be referred to for
detailed discussion and recommendations
INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
evaluation performed for the proposed Aiea Commercial Center Building
The general location of the project site and its vicinity is shown on
the Project Location Map Plate 1
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this evaluation was to explore the subsurface soil and
water conditions at the site to obtain pertinent soil data for the
foundation design of the proposed structure

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed development will consist of a two story building
approximately 45 by 80 feet in plan The commercial building will be

existing grades
A short retaining wall is planned to provide grade separation at the

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located within an existing shopping center on the
makai side of Moanalua Road in Aiea on the island of Oahu Hawaii Aiea
Shopping Center is located across Moanalua Road and Aiea Stream flows
from east to west adjacent to the shopping center
There are three 3 existing buildings on the approximately 73 000
square foot trapezoidal parcel which include one 1 one story and two
2 two story buildings All buildings are now served by an existing
paved central parking lot and driveway
Two existing underground drainage easements 1 and 7 traverse across
the shopping mall in a north to south direction The smaller Easement 7
is connected to the larger box drain Easement 1 which in turn outlets to
Aiea Stream
The proposed commercial building will be located in the parking area
in front of the existing two story building at the southwestern corner of
the site close to Aiea Stream The eastern corner of the new building
will be located adjacent to the box drain Easement 1
The ground surface slopes down steeply from Moanalua Road at the
development entrance then slopes relatively gently towards the west An
existing retaining wall approximately 6 to 9 feet high traverses along
the boundary of the southeastern parking lot of the site to provide grade
separation between the subject parcel and Moanalua Road The ground
surface at the proposed building and parking area slopes gently towards
the west with ground elevations differing somewhat by about 2 5 feet
Ground cover consists primary of asphaltic concrete pavement The
asphaltic concrete pavement appeared to be in a generally fair condition
with minor cracking in some areas

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions were explored by drilling and sampling
three 3 borings to depths of 20 3 to 21 5 feet below the existing ground
surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general the borings encountered a surface fill layer of medium
stiff to hard clayey silt over an intermediate alluvium layer of medium
stiff to stiff clayey silt underlain by decomposed tuff in the form of
clay silt sand gravel and cobble mixtures The surface fill layer has
gravels and cobbles locally
The depth to decomposed tuff varies from about 12 to 15 feet below
the existing ground surface at the three corners of the proposed
commercial building where the borings were drilled
Groundwater was encountered in the borings from about 13 3 to 15 1
feet below the existing ground surface during our field exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The borings encountered competent subsoils at the proposed building
area Therefore shallow spread or continuous footing foundations bearing
directly on the generally hard in situ surface soil or compacted
structural fill may be used to support the proposed structure
However special foundation designs should be considered for the
eastern building corner area adjacent to Easement 1
Footing Foundation
Based on the anticipated soil conditions and finished grade we
recommend that the proposed building be supported by spread or continuous
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1946 00 Page 5
Dk No 43
footing foundations bearing on the hard in situ surface soil or compacted
structural fill An allowable bearing capacity up to 3000 p s f may be
used for the spread or continuous footing design
The bearing capacity value is for total dead plus live load only and
may be increased by one third for short duration wind or seismic loading
Estimated footing settlements under the design loadings are anticipated to
be on the order of less than one inch total and one half of an inch
differential
Except at the eastern building corner area footings should be placed
a minimum of 12 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade Uplift
wind or other lateral loading conditions may further increase the
embedment of the footing foundations
Deeper footing embedment should be considered for the eastern
building corner area which will be discussed in further detail in the
subsequent section Eastern Building Corner Foundation
Resistance to lateral loads on the proposed structure can be obtained
by passive earth pressure and frictional resistance A frictional
coefficient of 0 4 may be used to compute the frictional resistance
between the bottom of the footings and the hard in situ soil or compacted
structural fill In addition an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 p s f
per foot of depth may be used to determine the passive earth resistance
against the buried vertical faces of the footings
Eastern Building Corner Foundation
The eastern corner of the proposed building will be located
immediately adjacent to the existing drain Easement 1 If required
future maintenance of the box drain in this area may undermine the
building foundations especially if shallow footings are used

In order to reduce the effects of possible future trench excavation
we recommend that footing foundations located within a distance of 10 feet
from the western wall of Easement 1 should be embedded below an imaginary
1H 1V plane extended upward from the footing bottom of the box drain
Alternatively deep foundation such as driven piles may be used to
transfer the building loads to bearing stratum below the invert of the box
sewer
Site Grading
Minor cut and fill are anticipated for the proposed building area and
the proposed repaved parking area
At the onset of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed Any unsuitable
materials such as debris rubbish vegetation and old pavements should
be removed and disposed of properly off site in order to prevent
contamination of the excavated materials
After clearing and grubbing the areas to receive fill footings and
pavement should be scarified to a depth of at least 6 inches below the
subgrade elevation moisture conditioned to optimum moisture content and
recompacted to a minimm of 90 of its ASTM D dry
density Any loose soft wet or yielding spots encountered at subgrade
level should be removed and replaced with on site soil or imported fill
material compacted to the above mentioned standard
The excavated on site soils may be used as a source of backfill and
fill materials If required imported fill should consist of
non expansive select material less than 3 inches in maximum size with less
than 20 passing the 200 sieve
The structural fill should be placed in horizontal lifts not
exceeding 6 inches in compacted thickness and compacted to a minimum of
90 of its ASTM D 1557 78 maximum dry density

Retaining Wall
To provide grade separation a short retaining wall will be
constructed at the new landscape area near the northern corner of the
proposed commercial building The following guidelines may be used for
the retaining wall design
1 The following lateral earth pressure may be used
Wall unrestrained at the top 35 p s f equivalent
active condition level fluid pressure
backfill per foot of depth
2 An allowable bearing value of up to 3000 p s f is recommended
for the wall footing foundation bearing on hard in situ soil
3 The wall footing foundation on relatively flat areas should be
embedded a minimum of 12 inches below the lowest adjacent
finished grade
4 A passive earth resistance of 300 p s f per foot of depth may be
used for the wall footing on level area
5 A base friction factor of 0 4 may also be used to determine the
resistance of the wall against sliding
6 A subsurface drainage system subdrains or weepholes should be
utilized behind the wall Subdrains should be properly
constructed to drain water away from the wall

Construction Observation
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the site
grading and foundation phases of the work This is to observe compliance
with the design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow
design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from that
anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in
this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed soil conditions encountered during construction are
different from those assumed or considered in this report then
appropriate modifications to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as
borings Variations of conditions between the field data points may
occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become
evident until construction If variations then appear evident it will be
necessary to re evaluate the recommendations given in this report
The location of the field data points were approximately determined
by scaling taping from the site plan from Project Design Inc Relative
elevations of the data points are based on an assumed bench mark and were
approximately determined by reference to the Plot Plan from Project Design
Inc The physical locations and elevations of the field data points
should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the method
used
