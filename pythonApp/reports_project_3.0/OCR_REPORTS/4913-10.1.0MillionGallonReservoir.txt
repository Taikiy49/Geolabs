PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is in the Mililani Mauka Residential Development area on the Island of
Oahu Hawaii A new 1 0 Million Gallon MG reservoir is planned at the project site The
new reservoir will be adjacent to an existing reservoir identified as Reservoir No 1
The proposed reservoir will be located to the southeast of Reservoir No 1 Based on the
available information the new reservoir will be situated on a fill embankment that was placed
during construction of Reservoir No 1 A Project Location Map and a Site Plan are shown
on Plates 1 and 2 respectively
We understand that the new reservoir will be a reinforced concrete structure
having a diameter of approximately 96 5 feet and a height of approximately 30 feet The
bottom of tank elevation will be set at 1120 feet Mean Sea Level MSL and the spillway
elevation will be set at 1150 feet MSL
Based on grading plans for construction of Reservoir No 1 fills on the order of
about 15 to 26 feet thick were required for the embankment area In addition our
observations indicated that a 3 foot to 4 foot high surcharge was placed over the new
reservoir footprint area Due to the thickness of the fills placed for the previous project

SITE GRADING AND UTILITY INSTALLATION
Geolabs Inc observed the site grading and utility installation work for the Mililani
Mauka 1150 Reservoir No 2 project from March 10 2005 to The site grading
work consisted of clearing and grubbing and placement and compaction of fills on the order
of about 1 to 3 feet thick to achieve the design finished grades for the project
RCI Construction Group performed the site grading and utility installation work During the
site grading operations samples of the on site and imported materials utilized as fill and
backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing The maximum dry
densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general
accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test results are summarized on
Plate 3
The fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as
determined in accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557 In the reservoir pad and
perimeter road areas the base course fill material was compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative density
During the site grading and utility backfilling operations periodic field density tests
were performed in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 2922 Nuclear Gauge
Method Results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 4 1 through
4 4 of this report These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on
constructed fills and backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by
pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should
be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed to
be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained
and or approved by our field representative based on visual observation and hand probing
Our services consisted of part time field observation of the contractor s fill placement
subgrade preparation and compaction procedures and confirmation of subgrade soil
conditions exposed during the above grading operations
Based on our field representative s observations and the referenced test results it is
our opinion that the site grading and utility installation work for the above referenced project
