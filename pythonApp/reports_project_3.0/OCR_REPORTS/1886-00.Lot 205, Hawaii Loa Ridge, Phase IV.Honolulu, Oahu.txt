SOIL ENGINEERING REPORT
LOT RIDGE PHASE IV
In accordance with your request we have made a study of the near
surface soil conditions at the above referenced lot in order to provide
foundation recommendations for the proposed residence The study
consisted of drilling three shallow borings testing the swelling
potential of the soils in our laboratory and reviewing the subdivision
site grading and the preliminary house foundation plans This report
summarizes our findings and recommendations
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
The proposed residence will be a two story structure approximately
50 by 90 feet in plan dimension with a back up driveway area
Concrete driveway and on grade slab for the front portion of the
basement floor level of the house are anticipated
Post and beam construction is planned for the majority of the
proposed house on sloping terrain

SITE CONDITIONS
The project site Lot 205 is located in Phase IV 4 of the Hawaii
Loa Ridge Development in East Honolulu on the island of Oahu The
down slope lot is fronted by Kaulana Place to the north
The existing lot slopes towards the south with the majority of the
central portion of the lot at a relatively flat gradients of about 4 1
to 5 1 H V varying in elevations from about 574 near Kaulana Place
down to 540 feet at the southern boundary The house lot is bounded by
2 1 H V slope at the top front lot and steeper 1 1 rear slope
At the time of our field exploration the site was a vacant lot
and partly covered with grasses and weeds
SUBSURFACE SOIL CONDITIONS

existing grade at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration disclosed that the lot is underlain by
reddish brown expansive clay The laboratory test results and previous
experience indicate that this material is highly expansive Therefore
the foundation will require special design and construction procedures
in order to reduce the shrink swell effect of the subsoils
Foundation
Spread or continuous footing foundation may be used to support the
proposed 2 story residential structure
In order to reduce the effects of the underlying expansive soil we
recommend that the expansive clays be over excavated down to a depth of
30 inches below the bottom of the individual footings and then replaced
with non expansive off site import borrow soils such as crusher waste
select borrow or coral The replaced soils should be compacted in
6 inch lifts to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM
D 1557 78 The footings can then be placed on the compacted
non expansive soil for details refer to the Schematic Section under
Over Excavation Method on Plate 2
Alternatively the footings may be embedded a minimum of 3 feet
below the lowest adjacent grade for details refer to the Deep Footing
Embedment Method Schematic Section for Footings on Expansive Soil on
Plate 2

An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f can be used for footings
bearing on the above specified compacted fill or in situ soil
If a footing is located next to a utility line it should extend to
the bottom of the utility trench to reduce footing settlement due to
settlement of the trench backfill
For footings located on sloping area the deep footing embedment
method is required to extend the footing deep enough to obtain a 10 foot
minimum horizontal set back distance measured from the outer edge of
the footing out to the slope face This additional embedment depth is
required to help resist the downhill slope creep movement and to develop
the recommended bearing capacity of the soil
Surface water should be diverted away from the foundation This is
to avoid ponding of surface run off water that may affect the expansive
soils around the foundation
Slab on Grade
In order to reduce the expansive effects of the soils we recommend
that a minimum 3 foot thick capping layer of non expansive material be
placed below the slab on grade foundation
The reddish brown expansive clay below the slab should be removed
or to a minimum three 3 feet below outside finished grade and replaced
with non expansive off site borrow materials such as select borrow
crusher waste or coral compacted to a minimm of 90 of its maximm
dry density per ASTM D 1557 78 The underlying clay soil remaining
should be kept moistened by water sprinkling to maintain a moist
condition prior to backfill placement The depth of excavation of the
expansive clay can be reduced to less than 3 feet if decomposed
vesicular basalt is encountered during the excavation

A 4 inch layer of 3 Fine cushion fill should be provided below the
slab In order to protect against moisture infiltration a vapor
barrier may be incorporated below the slab
The thickened edge slab footings can bear on the compacted
non expansive fill The thickened edges should be embedded a minimm of
12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade for details refer to the
Schematic Section on Plate 3
Concrete Driveway
For the concrete driveway recommendations provided for the 3 foot
capping layer for the concrete slab on grade should also be followed
However since the driveway could tolerate some amount of swelling the
non expansive capping thickness could be reduced to 2 feet to reduce
costs if some cracking is tolerable The subgrade soils should be kept
moist prior to concrete placement
A minimm 12 inch thick thickened edge is recommended for the
driveways
Joints are recommended at about 8 to 10 foot intervals in order to
allow for some movements of the concrete

An allowable bearing value of up to 2500 p s f is recommended
for foundations bearing on the stiff on site soil
A passive earth resistance of 300 p s f per foot of depth may be
used for the wall footing on level areas The passive pressure
should be reduced to 250 p s f for walls on slopes
A base friction factor of 0 35 may also be used to determine the
resistance against sliding of the wall
The wall foundations on relatively flat areas should be embedded
a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade
The wall foundations on or near sloping ground should be embedded
deeper to provide an 10 foot minimm horizontal set back distance
from the toe of the wall to the existing sloping ground surface
The wall foundations located next to sewer drainage easements
should be embedded below a H V plane extending upward
from the bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep as the
inverts of the utility lines
Backfills directly behind the retaining wall should consist of
granular material such as select borrow crusher waste or 43B
Fine gravel The top 12 inches of backfill should consist of
non expansive impervious material to reduce water infiltration
behind the wall
A subsurface drainage system subdrains or weepholes should be
utilized behind the walls Subdrains should be properly
constructed to drain water away from the wall

Lot Grading
The fills and backfills within the top 36 inches minimum below the
on grade slabs should consist of non expansive soil such as select
borrow crusher waste or coral Where cut or minimal fill is anticipated
such as the garage driveway and the house areas the on site expansive
clay soil should be over excavated to provide a 36 inch minimm thickness
of non expansive select fill capping underneath the on grade slabs
All imported fill materials should be non expansive select borrow
material tested and approved by the soils engineer prior to being used in
the site
Prior to construction the area to receive fill should be cleared of
vegetation and debris
The fills should be placed in horizontal lifts not exceeding 8 inches
in compacted thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximm
dry density per ASTM D 1557 78
The fills in the existing slope steeper than 5 1 H V should be
keyed and benched
Two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V cut and fill slopes are
recommended
The site should be graded to provide drainage of surface runoff away
from all foundations
Drainage
The finished lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings should
be shaped to shed water away from foundations and to avoid ponding
condition Also it is advised that a gutter system be installed around
the residence and that gutter water be diverted far away from the house
foundation and driveway Excessive landscape watering near foundations
should also be avoided

Planters next to the foundation should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance
of the above recommended foundations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of
strength The foundation excavation should be properly backfilled tight
against the slab footing immediately after setting of the concrete to
prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained
to drain all surface run off away from the slab foundation
Design Review
Plans and specifications for foundation site grading and utility
construction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and
written comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included
CONSTRUCTION MONT TORING
It is recommended that the footing excavations over excavation of
expansive soil and backfilling be observed by a soils engineer during
construction to see that adequate bearing and compaction density have
been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the
findings of our exploration
Any imported material required should be non expansive off site
select borrow materials tested and approved by the soils engineer prior
to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above observations

LIMITATIONS
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations
made in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation
engineering principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all
other warranties either expressed or implied
The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are based
on our site reconnaissance and soil information from boring and
laboratory test data obtained from this soil evaluation
Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encountered and cannot be
fully determined by reconnaissance and borings These unforeseen
conditions such as pockets of organic soils loose fill or seepage may
occur in localized areas Therefore additional expenditures may be
needed during construction to attain a properly constructed project
Some contingency fund is thus recommended to accommodate these possible
costs
