Findings and Recommendations
Additional Geotechnical Engineering Exploration
Heeia Corporation Yard
Office Building and Storeroom Complex
Heeia Oahu Hawaii
In accordance with our proposal dated May 13 1993 we have recently performed
an additional geotechnical engineering exploration at the new building site for the above
referenced project We have prepared this letter report to summarize our work on the
project and to present our findings and recommendations
Project Background and Considerations
The proposed project site is located inside the existing Heeia Corporation Yard
adjacent to King Intermediate School on the windward side of the island of Oahu Hawaii
The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location
Map Plate 1
The original project site is situated along the upper side of an existing retaining wall
which provides grade separation between the Corporation Yard and the adjacent lower
property
A geotechnical engineering exploration was conducted in 1991 at this previously
proposed location of the Office Building and Storeroom Complex Three 3 borings were
performed and their approximate locations are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2

We understand that the proposed Office Building and Storeroom Complex has now
been relocated from their original proposed location after the completion of our field
exploration and soils report In addition we understand that the proposed project design
has been revised from a single story building to a two story structure with heavier
loadings
Based on the marginal subsoil conditions encountered at the original building
location from our previous exploration it is likely that different and poorer subsoil
conditions may exist under the new building site As a result of the building relocation
we are particularly concerned with potential large foundation settlements under the
heavier loadings anticipated with the current two story building design Therefore we
have recommended that an additional geotechnical engineering exploration be performed
at the new building location to verify or modify if necessary the spread footing design
recommendations we have presented for the subject project
Current Exploration
Subsurface Conditions
Our current exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling three 3
borings to depths ranging between 19 2 and 21 5 feet below the existing ground surface
The approximate locations of the borings and the new building site are also shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2
In general our field exploration encountered a surficial layer of fill material
approximately 2 to 5 feet thick consisting of stiff silty clay material overlying very stiff
Clayey silt residual soils to depths of about 9 to 12 feet below the existing ground surface
This was underlain by saprolite materials of silty sand overlying basalt formation extending
to the maximum depths explored
Groundwater was not encountered in the drilled borings during our field
exploration Groundwater levels are expected to vary with seasonal rainfall and time of
year
Detailed descriptions of the materials encountered are presented on the Boring
Logs Plates A 1 through A 3 Laboratory testing was performed on selected samples
obtained and the test results are presented on Plates B 1 and B 2

Discussion and Recommendations
Our field exploration disclosed that the new building site is underlain by stiff soils
at shallow depths over basalt formation at approximately 20 feet below the existing
ground surface Based on our current field exploration results it is our opinion that the
previous spread footing foundation recommendations we have presented for the subject
project at the original location are also applicable to the new building location
Conventional shallow foundations consisting of spread and or continuous footings
may be used for support of the project An allowable bearing pressure of 2 500 pounds
per square foot p s f may be used for the design of footing foundations bearing on the
Stiff in situ soils or imported select material compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the
maximum dry density as determined by ASTM Test Designation D1557 91
Laboratory swell tests indicated that the subsoils at the anticipated slab subgrade
elevation exhibited moderately high shrink swell potential Therefore we recommend that
a minimum 12 inch capping layer of non expansive granular fill Select borrow material
be used for support of the slab on grade to reduce the potential for slab heaving distress
due to swelling of these subsoils The underlying subgrade soil should be moisture
conditioned to 2 to 3 percent above optimum moisture content

Improvements Heeia Corporation Yard Heeia Oahu Hawaii dated May 18 1991 should
be referred to for detailed and special design recommendations
