PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project is located in Andersen Air Force Base on the northerly end of the
island of Guam in the Mariana Islands The project involves the removal of existing
underground storage tanks at two locations and replacement of these tanks with new
above ground storage tanks as well as a 400 square foot fueling station building fuel
dispensing island and an asphaltic concrete paved parking lot at the AGE Fueling station
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface soils
and rock conditions underlying the two sites in order to provide data for the formulation
of geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to the design of the site
preparation and foundations for the new construction

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
Guam is the southernmost of the Mariana Island archipelago which is an island
arc parallelling the trend of the subduction zone forming the Mariana Trench
The base of the island was formed during the Eocene Epoch by the eruption of
basaltic rhyolite lavas forming a volcano which is now the southern portion of the island
On the northern flank of this volcanic massif during the Oligocene and Miocene Epochs
a complex of coral and algal reefs built an elongated platform of limestones and related
deposits Tectonic activity has lifted these coral algal limestones above sea level to form
the northern Guam Plateau
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2968 00 Page 5
Weathering and solution working of the limestones has partially recrystallized the
calcium carbonate of the limestones forming massive limestones in some areas and
poorly to well cemented detrital limestones in others Typically the detrital limestones are
highly variable in strength characteristics and are frequently karstic or cavernous in
nature
The project sites are situated in a relatively level portion of the northern plateau
which forms the main portion of Andersen Air Force Base Our exploration at the two
sites generally encountered relatively massive reef limestone overlain by a thin layer of
detrital coral formation
Typically the surface of the underlying limestone is irregular due to solution
working Frequently formations such as those encountered at the project sites will have
buried pinnacles of massive limestone with spaces between the pinnacles filled with
weathered material or detrital material
As previously discussed Guam is situated adjacent to the subduction zone which
forms the Mariana Trench The island is tectonically active and has experienced
approximately 20 earthquakes exceeding 7 0 since 1902 Of these events 15 occurred
prior to 1950 and five have occurred subsequent to that date

Site Preparation
Detailed grading plans for the proposed projects were not available prior to our
field exploration However it is anticipated that site preparation at each site will consist
of minor grading to provide level building sites and placement of engineered backfill in
excavations for the existing underground tanks
The following recommendations are general guidelines for the design of the
grading and for the development of general specifications
Fills and Subgrade Preparation
Areas that are to receive fills or are subgrades for slabs or other improvements
should be cleared of all vegetation and deleterious materials The resulting
grub spoil material and demolition debris should be disposed of off site Organic
soils and or topsoil may be stockpiled for use in landscaped areas if appropriate
However care should be exercised to reduce contamination of the topsoil with on
site fill and backfill material
If zones of soft or wet soils are encountered at the subgrade levels during grading
over excavation may be required to expose firm soils This should be evaluated
by the geotechnical engineer in the field Contract documents should include
additive and deductive unit prices for over excavation and engineered fill
construction to account for variations in the over excavation quantities
The exposed surface following stripping and or over excavation should be scarified
to a depth of eight inches 8 properly moisture conditioned to at least the
optimum moisture content and compacted to not less than 90 percent relative
compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2968 00 Page 8
expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil
determined in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91 Optimum
moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the
maximum dry density New pavement and slab subgrades should be scarified to
a depth of at least eight inches 8 and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent
relative compaction
The on site coral materials may be re used as engineered fill provided that they
contain no particles or fragments greater than three inches 3 in greatest
dimension All on site fills and backfills should be moisture conditioned to the
optimum moisture content placed in thin lifts normally 6 to 8 inches in loose lift
thickness and compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction
Any imported soil should be select fill Select fill should contain no pieces or
fragments greater than three inches 3 in greatest dimension with less than 20
percent passing the No 200 sieve Fill materials should be tested and approved
by the project engineer prior to delivery to the job site Imported soils having low
expansion potential should be brought to above the optimum moisture content
and compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction All surfaces
should be finished to create smooth unyielding subgrades and should be kept
moist until covered by concrete slabs or pavements
Following the clearing of the site and backfilling of any over excavated areas
pavement areas should be scarified to a depth of at least eight inches 8 brought
to near optimum moisture content and proofrolled with a heavy vibratory roller for
a minimum of four 4 passes Any soft weak or yielding areas disclosed during
proofrolling operations should be over excavated to firm ground and the resulting
excavation should be backfilled with well compacted engineered fill Upon
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2968 00 Page 9
completion of the proofrolling operations the area should be further compacted
if necessary to obtain a minimum of 95 percent maximum dry density per ASTM
Test Designation D 1557 91
In general excavated on site soil materials should be suitable as fill material
Generally the on site coral materials reduced to less then three inches 3 in
maximum size should be suitable for use as engineered fill
Imported material if required should generally consist of well graded coral gravel
having no particles or fragments greater than three inches 3 in maximum
dimension with no more than ten percent 10 passing the 200 sieve Imported
material should be free of decomposable organic matter and other deleterious
materiais
Boulders or cobbles larger than three inches 3 in maximum dimension should
be screened or removed from the excavated material and either disposed of
off site or reduced in size to less than three inches 3

Foundation Design
Based on our field exploration it is anticipated that some of the foundations will
span from cut to fill or from detrital coral formation to the reef limestone In order to
provide uniform support for the foundations we recommend that the foundation sub
grades be scarified to a depth of 12 inches below the bottom of the footings and
recompacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density established by
ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91
An allowable bearing pressure of up to 3 000 pounds per square foot p s f may
be utilized for the design of foundations bearing on the coral materials which have been
recompacted or on new fill which has been placed and compacted in accordance with
the recommendations presented herein This value may be increased by one third for
temporary wind and or seismic loads The footings should be embedded a minimum of
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2968 00 Page 11
two feet 2 below the lowest adjacent grade A coefficient of 0 4 may be used to
calculate the frictional resistance between the concrete of the foundations and the
underlying subgrade materials
Lateral loads may be resisted by passive pressure represented by an equivalent
fluid weighing 300 pounds per cubic foot p c f against vertical or near vertical surfaces
of the buried foundations Except in paved or slab covered areas the upper one foot 1
of embedment should be neglected for development of passive resistance
Slabs On Grade
It is anticipated that the floor of the proposed fueling station building will be
concrete slab on grade It is also anticipated that the slab may be subjected to vehicular
traffic e g forklifts and therefore will function as a rigid pavement Pavement design
will be discussed in a subsequent section
The building slab and its cushion may be supported by the upper fill provided the
subgrade is prepared as recommended above A four inch 4 layer of clean coral gravel
generally conforming to ASTM Standard C 33 No 67 gradation should be provided below
the slab as cushion fill
In order to reduce future moisture infiltration and subsequent damage to the floor
coverings an impervious moisture membrane should be considered below the slab
provided an inch of moist fine sand is placed above the plastic membrane to aid in
concrete curing
Cavity Detection
As discussed above the site is situated in karstic coral algal limestone terrain
Subsurface cavities and other voids are commonly encountered in coral formations To
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2968 00 Page 12
reduce the possibility of foundation or structural settlements resulting from the collapse
of such voids we recommend that a program of probing and grouting of the spread
footing locations be considered when preparing specifications and engineering estimates
for the project
For estimating purposes we recommend that the probes be drilled at each column
location and on ten foot 10 centers for continuous footings The probe holes should
be a minimum of three inches 3 in diameter and should extend a minimum of ten feet
10 below the footing subgrade The holes should be backfilled with sand cement grout
injected at low pressures
archaea be mes
if a cavity or void is suspected at a footing location additional probes shou
drilled to attempt to delineate the extent of the suspected void
in order to avoid having to drill the probes inside the footing excavations which
would increase drilling costs it is suggested that the probing and grouting work be
performed after either site clearing or site grading but prior to the commencement of
foundation excavation work
The probing and grouting operations should be observed by a representative of
Geolabs Hawaii to see that the above recommendations are properly implemented and
to evaluate other solutions for backfilling of potential voids
