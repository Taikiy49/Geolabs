BORING AND LABORATORY TESTING REPORT
AIRFIELD PAVEMENT ANALYSIS
NAVAL AIR STATION
BARBERS POINT OAHU HAWAII
This report presents the results of our borings and laboratory test
results which were performed in support of the load bearing evaluation of
the airfield pavements at the Barbers Point Naval Air Station Oahu
Hawaii
The general location of the project site is shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain pavement thickness and
subsurface soil information below the existing aircraft pavement at the
site in order to provide data for correlation with non destructive testing
surveys to evaluate the load bearing capacity of the pavements
The scope of our exploration was in general accordance with our
proposals of December 9 and 10 1987

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
The island of Oahu was built by two basaltic shield volcanoes
Waianae and Koolau Waianae is the older of the two and reached the end
of its activity before Koolau
As volcanic activity on the island of Oahu drew to a close the
island began to subside slowly While the island was sinking fringing
coral algal reefs began to grow on the seaward margins of the island
During the Pleistocene or Ice Age global sea levels rose and fell
in response to the great continental glaciers As the glaciers grew the
global sea level fell below the existing level and as the glaciers melted
and retreated the sea level rose to heights above the current stand of
the sea
Locally the fringing coral algal reefs continued to grow during the
high stands of the sea leaving fossil reefs which are now exposed above
sea level At the project site and in its vicinity there are exposures
of such fossil reefs which most likely grew during the Kaena 95 feet
stand of the sea Interbedded with these coral algal materials are
alluvial sediments which were transported from the nearby Waianae Range
Field Exploration
The borings were generally drilled at about 200 to 600 foot
intervals along the runways and taxiways alternating on either side of
the centerline For the aprons and hardstands generally one boring was

were identified in the field by ARE Inc The borings were generally
drilled to a depth of about 10 feet below the pavement level although a
few borings were deepened to explore suspected cavities near the bottom of
the holes
A truck mounted hydraulic drill rig was used to drill all of the

Soil Conditions
Generally the borings disclosed similar pavement sections and
subgrade conditions with varying thicknesses of asphalt concrete pavement
14 inches The base course material ranged from mostly silty coral sand
with gravel to locally silty basalt gravel with sand The subgrade
consisted primarily of silty coral sand with gravel or coral formation
with localized filled cavities The subsoils encountered were generally
in a dense condition
Groundwater was not encountered in most of the borings during our
field exploration

EXPLORATION FINDINGS
Surface Pavement Thickness
The thickness of the surface pavement at the site was found to vary
somewhat However generally the borings indicate that the pavement
thickness could be generalized as follows
Runway to 6 inches asphaltic concrete
Runway to 8 inches asphaltic concrete
Runway to 8 inches asphaltic concrete
Taxiway P 8 to 9 5 inches asphaltic concrete
Other Taxiways 6 to 8 inches asphaltic concrete
Apron near Hangars 110 and 111 7 inches PCC reinforced
vc 1 and Transient Apron 9 5 to 11 inches PCC reinforced
Hangar 110 6 to 6 75 inches PCC reinforced
Base Course
Generally it was not possible to distinguish between the base course
and select borrow subgrade layers since the material below the pavement
consisted of silty coral sand with gravel Almost all of the soils
encountered below the surface pavement would not meet current
specifications for base course material
Basalt gravel was only encountered in three 3 of the borings near
the western end of Runway 11 29 and two 2 borings near the intersection
of Taxiways P and T
Subgrade Subbase
Generally it was not possible to visually distinguish between
subbase and subgrade material Most of the soils consisted of silty
coral sand and gravel derived from excavated coral formation The soils
generally contained about 20 to 30 percent fines and would be classified
as SM according to the Unified Soil Classification System

The borings indicate that these soils are generally well compacted
and dense The laboratory tests and previous experience indicate that
these soils are competent and can usually provide CBR values above 25
Cavities
In the general Barbers Point area large areas of coral and
coral algal limestones are encountered These formation grew or were
deposited during a higher stand of the sea
Coral and coral algal limestones are the result of the growth of
marine organisms which secrete calcium carbonate The formations
constructed by these organisms are frequently discontinuous in nature and
have voids where the organisms did not grow Often these voids are
filled with detritus However the voids also frequently act as lines of
weakness for the formation of solution cavities or caves and sinkholes
It appears that cavities may have been encountered in 11 of the 126
borings drilled

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as borings
probes test pits and or observation wells Variations of conditions
between the field data points may occur and the nature and extent of
these variations may not become evident until construction If variations
then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations given in this report
The location of the field data points were determined in the field by
ARE Inc Elevations of the data points were not available The physical
location and elevation of the field data points should be considered
accurate only to the degree implied by the method used
In addition the stratification lines shown in graphic
representations of the field data points depict the approximate boundaries
between soil types and as such may denote a gradual transition The
strata lines shown on profiles or cross section are based upon
interpolation between field data points and may not represent actual
subsurface conditions

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testings
ee
Moisture content determinations were performed on selected samples
as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties The
results of these tests are presented on the Boring Logs at the
appropriate sample depths
Fifty seven 57 sieve analysis tests seventeen 17 Atterberg
Limit tests and eight 8 specific gravity tests were performed to
further evaluate the index properties of the soil samples The test
results are presented on Plates B 1 through B 20
Eight 8 compaction tests were performed on the composite bulk
samples of the subsoils in accordance with the ASTM Test Designation
D 1557 to determine the compaction characteristics of the soils
Nine laboratory CBR tests were also performed on the composite bulk
samples of the subsoils to evaluate their suitability as pavement
support The results of the above tests are summarized on Plates B 1
through B 20
Twenty 20 splitting tensile strength tests were performed on the
concrete core samples for evaluation of the flexual strength of the
existing concrete pavement The test results are summarized on Plates
B 21 and B 22
