INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of the soils engineering investigation con
ducted for the Airside improvements including new aircraft aprons taxiways
and off site roadway construction for the proposed Kahului Airport Terminal
Complex Expansion project State Project No M 1055
The general location of the site and its vicinity is shown on Plate 1 Project
Location Map
Soil engineering investigations for the Landside improvements the proposed
new terminal building and air cargo building have also been conducted under
separate contracts and reports
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our investigation was to evaluate the subsurface conditions at
the site and to provide soil data such as CBR value shear strength
compressibility swell and soil classification for the design of the proposed
aircraft aprons taxiways off site roadways and related structures for the
project

The scope of the investigation which was performed in general accordance with
our revised proposal dated included a field reconnaissance
a review of available soil and geologic literature and data from previous soil
investigation reports the drilling of 37 new exploratory borings laboratory
and field CBR testing engineering analysis of field and laboratory data and
report preparation The log of borings and laboratory test results are
presented in Appendices A and B respectively A list of the references used
in our investigation is also attached
The study was based on design guidelines and data presented in the Design
Criteria for Kahului Airport Terminal Complex Expansion Department of

Managers Hawaii Inc and prepared by Parsons Hawaii As the project design

loading Analysis was also performed to develop recommendations for pavement
thickness design drainage grading and general site development

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The project site is located on the north shore of the island of Maui on the
northwest flank of Haleakala Volcano
The bulk of the Haleakala shield was built during the late Pliocene and early
Pleistocene by thin bedded basaltic lava flows of the Honomanu Volcanic
Series During the Pleistocene the characteristics of the lavas changed to
very hard thickly bedded flows of andesitic composition These lavas have
been grouped as the Kula Volcanic Series
The Kula lava flows buried most of the Honomanu rocks throughout East Maui and
in some areas buried erosional products i e alluvium derived from both
Honomanu and early Kula rocks At the project site most of the area is
underlain by what appears to be a single thick flow of Kula lava that overlies
alluvial soils
In some areas along Kalialinui Gulch recent alluvial soils were found without
any underlying andesitic rock It appears that there was no deposition of
Kula lavas in this area which resulted in the cutting of the gulch into
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O Page 7
softer alluvial soils It is felt that Kalialinui Stream would not have had
sufficient erosive power to cut through the hard dense Kula rock
In the seaward end of the Kalialinui Gulch area deposits of dume and beach
sand were encountered These calcareous sands were probably deposited during
glacioeustatic fluctuations of sea level in the Pleistocene
Also encountered within the project site were varying amounts of man made fill
which may have resulted from grading operations during the construction of the
existing airport facilities surrounding roads and other improvements

Field and laboratory CBR tests were performed on the residual silt sub
grade soils to develop a design CBR value for use in the aircraft pavement
design The laboratory CBR values varied from 6 5 to 41 0 with a mean of
21 and a standard deviation of 13 when compacted to 95 to 100 percent of
its maximum dry density In accordance with the FAA Advisory Circular
design criteria the recommended design CBR value is generally
selected as the value which is one standard deviation below the mean of
the test values Therefore based on the laboratory test results the
design CBR value for the residual silt subgrade soil would be 8
GEOLABS HAWAII

Seven field CBR tests were performed on the subgrade soils below the
existing pavement level The field CBR values obtained in the drilled
holes varied from 4 0 to 25 0 with a mean of 9 7 and standard deviation of
7 1 which were significantly lower than those obtained from the labora
tory compacted samples Undisturbed samples taken near the location of the
field CBR test levels indicated that the existing soils have densities of
only 80 to 85 percent of the maximum dry density which probably accounts
for the lower CBR values
Based on similar FAA design procedures a low design CBR of 2 6 would be
obtained and a very thick pavement section would be required However
Since most of the area will require some fill and assuming the existing
pavement section will be removed the new pavement subgrade soils can be
improved by additional compaction to obtain a minimum of 95 of its maxi
mum dry density and we believe the higher laboratory design CBR value of 8
is justified and should be utilized

Pavement Drainage
Subsurface drainage is very critical for airport pavement The on site
residual soils are relatively impermeable and this will require additional
measures to reduce the build up of water beneath the pavement The pavement
subgrade should be shaped to drain trapped water into drainline catch basins
through weepholes at the subgrade level A gravel drainage layer should also
be placed in the drainline trench backfill at the subgrade level as an
additional source of subdrain for trapped subgrade water

laboratory CBR values in order to allow for general reduction in
soil strength which occurs with time due to changes in the moisture
content of the soils and to account for natural variations in soil

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
am ewe a un
Moisture content unit weight and Atterberg Limit determinations
were performed on selected samples as an aid in the Classifica
tion and evaluation of soil properties The results of these
tests are presented on the log of borings at the appropriate
sample depths The Atterberg Limits are also summarized on
Plates B 1 through B 4 and plotted on Plate B S
Thirteen laboratory CBR tests were performed on bulk samples of
the existing subgrade and base course soils to evaluate their
suitability for pavement support The test results are
Summarized on Plates B 1 through B 4
Seven moisture density tests modified Proctor were performed
on bulk samples of the existing subgrade and base course soils
to determine their compaction characteristics The test
results are summarized on Plates B 1 through B 4
Three specific gravity tests were performed on typical samples
of the underlying soil The test results are presented on
Plates B 1 through B 4
One sieve analysis test was performed on samples of the
underlying alluvial deposit to evaluate its grain size
distribution The test result is summarized on Plate B 1
Four consolidation tests were performed on the alluvial and
residual deposits to evaluate its settlement characteristics
The consolidation curves are presented on Plates B 6 through
B 9
Resistivity and pH tests were performed on selected in situ
samples to evaluate its corrosion potential for utility pipe
design The test results are summarized on Plate B 10
