SOIL ENGINEERING REPORT
PROPOSED RESIDENCE
3095 WOODLAWN DRIVE
TAX MAP KEY 44
As requested we have made a study of the near surface soil
conditions below the above referenced lot to evaluate the suitability of
the near surface soils as foundation support for the proposed residence
The study consisted of drilling three 3 borings within the lot
performing laboratory soil tests and reviewing the preliminary house
layout plans This report summarizes our observations and
recommendations
PROJECT CONSTDERATIONS
Based on the preliminary house plan provided to us the proposed
residence will consist of a two story structure approximately 40 by 60
feet in plan dimensions Post and beam foundation and wood framed
construction are anticipated for the proposed residence

EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The subject lot is located at 3095 Woodlawn Drive in Manoa Valley
The lot is bounded by existing house lots on the southern and western
property lines The lot slopes gently up towards the backyard in the
northeastern direction The proposed building area is gently sloping
At present the lot is covered with grass
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Three borings were drilled within the building area to depths
ranging from 5 0 to 11 5 feet below the existing grade at the approximate
locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
In general the borings encountered a thin surface layer 1 5 to 3 0
feet thick of stiff brown silty clay over a relatively homogeneous layer
of expansive clay Laboratory soil tests on the clay soil indicate that
the soil can be classified as High Plasticity Clay CH according to the
Unified Soil Classification System The test results are summarized in
Plate B 1 Gravel and vesicular basalt cobble were encountered below the
upper 5 feet in Borings 1 and 2 Detailed description of the soils
encountered in the borings are presented on the attached logs Plates A 1
through A 3
Groundwater level was encountered in Boring 1 and found to be about
7 feet below the existing ground surface which is believed to be caused
by perched water or localized seepage zone Groundwater level was not
encountered in Borings 2 and 3

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration disclosed that the lot is generally underlain
at shallow depths by brown to gray clay with sand and gravel to the
drilled depths of the borings The laboratory test results on the clay
soil generally indicate that it has high swelling potential particularly
if the moisture content of the soil is low prior to subsequent
saturation The results of the laboratory one inch ring swell tests are
summarized on Plate B 2 Therefore the slab on grade and footing
foundations would require special design and construction procedures in
order to reduce the shrink swell effect of the subsoils Our
recommendations are discussed in detail and presented in the following
sections
Foundations
Post and beam foundation system could be used for the proposed
house For footing construction we recommend that the expansive
clays be over excavated down to a depth of 3 feet below the lowest
adjacent finished grade and then replaced with non expansive borrow
soils such as crusher waste select borrow or coral compacted to a
minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78 The
spread footings can then be placed on the compacted non expansive
soil Alternatively the footings may be embedded a minimm of 3
feet below the lowest adjacent grade refer to Schematic Section
Plate 2 for details
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f can be used for footings
bearing on the compacted fill or natural ground

It is recommended that the footing bottom be embedded at least 12
inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade If a footing is
located next to a utility line it should extend to the bottom of
the utility trench to reduce footing settlement due to settlement of
the trench backfill
Surface water should be diverted away from foundations to reduce
ponding conditions that may saturate the expansive soils below the
foundation
Slab on Grade
Slab on grade foundations may be used for the proposed house In
order to reduce the expansive effects of the soils we recommend
that a minimm 3 foot capping layer of non expansive material be
placed below the slab on grade foundations
The expansive clay below and for a horizontal distance of 3 feet
around the slabs should be removed for a minimum depth of 3 feet
below the outside finished grade and replaced with non expansive
off site borrow materials such as select borrow crusher waste or
coral compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximm dry density per
ASTM D 1557 78 The underlying clayey soil should be scarified
covered with hydrated lime moistened to above optimum moisture
content and recompacted moderately 85 compaction prior to
backfilling with a 3 foot select fill capping layer refer to
Schematic Section Plate 3 for details
A 4 inch cushion layer of 3B Fine gravel should be provided below
the slab In order to protect against moisture infiltration a
vapor barrier may be incorporated below the slab

The slab thickened edge footings can bear on the compacted
non expansive fill The thickened edges should be embedded a
minimum of 12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade An allowable
bearing value of 2500 p s f can be used for the slab thickened edge
bearing on the compacted fill or natural ground
Since the on site soils are moist to wet and the upper 18 inch crust
consists of less expansive silty clay another alternative is to
construct the slab on grade foundation on a minimum 18 inch thick
imported fill layer on top of the existing grade
We believe that this may provide a more economical foundation than a
minimum 3 foot capping layer of non expansive material below the
slab on grade foundations recommended in the previous section
The fill materials should consist of selected non expansive
materials generally less than 3 inches in size and should be
approved by the soils engineer before hauling to site for filling
purpose The fill materials should be placed in maximm 8 inch
loose lifts and compacted to 90 of its maximum dry density
established by ASTM D 1557 78 The subgrade soils should be kept
moist at all times prior to filling and or concrete placement
The cushion layer and thickened edge recommendations pertaining to
the slab on grade stated in the previous section should also be
followed
Site Grading
Fills and backfills within 3 feet of the finished grade should be
selected non expansive material generally less than 3 inches in size
placed in maximum 8 inch loose lifts and compacted to 90 of its maximm
dry density established by ASTM D 1557 78 The on site clay soil should
not be used for fill material below structures

Prior to construction the area should be cleared of vegetation and
debris The exposed subgrade should be scarified kept moist and
recompacted moderately 85 compaction prior to filling
Drainage
The finished lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings
should be shaped to shed water away from foundations to avoid ponding
condition Also it is advised that gutter systems be installed around
the house and that gutter water be diverted far away from the foundations
and walkways Excessive landscape watering near the foundations and slab
should also be avoided
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance
of the above recommended foundations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of
strength The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled tight
against the slab footing immediately after setting of the concrete to
prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained
to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundation
Design Review
Plans and specifications for foundation site grading and utility
construction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and
written comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included

CONSTRUCTION MONTTORING
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during
earthwork construction to monitor the contractor s grading operations
particularly fill placement
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be examined by a
soils engineer during construction to see that adequate bearing has been
obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the
findings of our study
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above inspections
LIMTTATIONS
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations
made in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation
engineering principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all
other warranties either expressed or implied
The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are based
on our site reconnaissance and soil information from boring and
laboratory test data obtained from this soil study
Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encountered and cannot be
fully determined by reconnaissance and borings These unforeseen
conditions such as pockets of organic soils trash fill or seepage may
occur in localized areas Therefore additional expenditures may be
needed during construction to attain a properly constructed project
Some contingency fund is thus recommended to accommodate these possible
costs
