PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The existing wharves SIERRA Docks S1A B are located in the northeast Loch at
Pearl Harbor Naval Station at Pearl Harbor on the Island of Oahu Hawaii Based on our
plans and documents research we understand that the existing wharves were originally
constructed in early 1940 s Very limited as built information was available at the time this
letter report was prepared
The SIERRA Docks are berthing wharves for the Naval submarine vessels The
wharves extend approximately 1000 feet in the easterly direction from the existing Wharf
1 S10 to the Small Craft Berthing Based on the as built information obtained we
understand that the existing Sierra Docks from Bent 1 to Bent 78 are supported on
18 inch concrete square vertical and batter pile foundations We also understand that the
existing Sierra Docks from Bent 79 to Bent 107 are supported on 18 inch composite
square vertical and batter pile foundation
Based on the underwater inspection conducted by another firm we understand that
a majority of the existing piles supporting the wharves were rated as good condition with
the exception of twenty six piles that were defective

METHODOLOGY OF EVALUATION
To assist the project structural engineer to perform the mooring lateral analysis on
the existing wharves it is desired to estimate the pile foundation spring stiffness Our
lateral analysis was performed for every individual area indicated previously based on the
following assumptions
1 The conditions of the existing foundation are similar to the as built plan
drawings
2 The existing piles are in good condition
3 A reduced modulus of elasticity about was used in our analysis
4 Both fixed and pinned connections were used to model the boundary
condition between deck and pile head
5 Sloping ground condition 2H 1V was used in our models
6 Static loading condition was used
7 Vertical load of 30 kips and zero moment applied on top of the pile was
assumed in our analysis
8 The average lengths of the concrete piles in Areas 1 through 8 were
estimated to vary from approximately 22 to 64 feet
oY The average lengths of the composite piles in areas 9 10 and 11 were
estimated consisting of 40 35 and 35 feet concrete pile on top and 25 20
and 15 feet timber pile on bottom respectively
In general lateral load resistance of the pile is a function of the stiffness of the
surrounding soil the stiffness of the pile allowable deflection at the top of pile and
induced moment in the shaft The computer program Lpile plus was used to calculate
the lateral load resistance of the piles The program computes deflection shear bending
moment and soil response with respect to depth in non linear soils The analysis was
carried out with the use of internally generated non linear p y curves to represent soil
moduli The program can model various pile head boundary conditions and various pile
properties as a function of depth
