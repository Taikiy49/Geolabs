Due to the potential for appreciable settlements resulting from the presence of the

Based on the available subsurface information and our engineering analyses we believe

wind or seismic forces In general piles should be spaced a minimum of 3 feet
center to center to avoid reduction in vertical capacity due to group action
Based on the depths to the hard coral formation anticipated across the SIWWTP
site we believe that the estimated pile tip elevations could range from about 15 to

anticipated pile splicing of the precast piles should be avoided Due to possible variation
in the length of the piles unit prices should be obtained during bidding for add ons
shorter piles etc
Predrilling
In general the upper 10 feet of materials below the existing ground surface should
be predrilled to facilitate the pile driving operations through the dense fill crust The
predrilling operation also serves to reduce the potential for distress to the existing
Solids Handling Building resulting from vibrations generated during the pile driving
operations
The predrilled holes should have a diameter between 14 and 16 inches for the
12 inch square piles It should be noted that the diameter of the predrilled holes
should not be more than 16 inches for the 12 inch square piles Due to the
relatively high lateral load demand at the top of the pile cap we recommend
grouting the annular space between the piles and predrilled holes with tremie
concrete to fill in the voids The required predrilling size and depth should be
verified and or modified by Geolabs during the test pile driving operation depending
on the conditions encountered and pile driving equipment used

Pile Foundation Settlements
Settlement of pile foundations will result primarily from elastic compression of the
pile member and the subgrade response We estimate that the total settlement of
the pile supported foundation to be less than 0 5 inches Differential settlements
between adjacent piles should not exceed about one half of the total foundation
settlement We believe that these settlements are essentially elastic and should
occur as the loads are applied
Pile Hammer Selection
The piles should be driven with a hammer capable of delivering energies in the
range of about 25 000 to 30 000 foot pounds in the field Significantly higher
energy hammers hammers with energies greater than 30 000 foot pounds are not
recommended for this project The hammer should be equipped with an energy
control level such that driving within the soft and or loose soils may be controlled at
a lower hammer energy to reduce the potential for tensile stress development
within the piles which could damage or cause cracking of the piles
Determination of driving equipment to be used for this project should take into
consideration the matching of the pile hammer with the weight of the piles to be
driven This will result in the pile hammer efficiently delivering the required energy
to the top of the pile and also reducing potential damage to the piles Geolabs
should review the pile driving equipment proposed to evaluate whether the
equipment is capable of installing the piles to the recommended capacity
The contractor should forward pile and driving equipment data forms to Geolabs for
review prior to construction Geolabs should review the submittal and assess the
driveability of the piles with the use of the selected hammer

Each pile should be driven continuously without interruption A pile may be rejected

more than 4 hours and the pile cannot be driven to the required depth During soft

developed from our pile analysis using the available subsurface data Therefore a
test pile program should be carried out to confirm our preliminary pile design and to
establish the pile driving criteria The production pile driving criteria may be revised
based on the test pile program
Test Pile Program
In general we recommend that a test pile program be undertaken to fulfill the
following objectives
1
To examine the driveability of the piles with the use of the selected
hammer
2
To estimate the production pile lengths
3
To assess the test pile driving criteria which may be modified for
use as the production pile driving criteria
To achieve these objectives we recommend that the test pile program consist of
approximately 10 percent of the total number of piles for the project The test piles
may be incorporated into the actual foundation system upon evaluation of the pile
driving behavior When the final foundation plan is available Geolabs should be
consulted for selection of the test pile locations Due to the lack of site specific soil
boring information we recommend casting the test piles at least 60 feet long to
account for potential variations in the anticipated subsurface conditions In addition
the test pile program should provide flexibility to allow for addition or relocation of
the test piles as may be deemed necessary by our office to suit the conditions
encountered during the test pile driving Re striking of all the test piles may be
required as part of the test pile program
We recommend that a Geolabs representative be present during pile driving
operations to observe the actual driving behavior and to further evaluate the field
performance It should be noted that the pile design and driving criteria
