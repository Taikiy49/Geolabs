Subsurface Conditions
As previously mentioned the Kona Commons Phase project site is in the
Kailua Kona area in the District of North Kona on the Island of Hawaii In general the
original topography of the project site prior to site grading activities consisted of irregular
or hummocky lava flow terrain with frequent topographic depressions separated by
adjacent higher relief mounds and low ridge features
Based on observations during the mass grading operations the project site
generally is underlain by medium hard to hard basalt formation with interbedded layers of
clinker materials It should be noted that lava tubes cavities and blisters are commonly
encountered in basaltic lava flows in the project vicinity In addition numerous lava tube
features were observed at the project site during the mass grading operations It should
be noted that near surface lava tubes detected within the limits of grading for the mass
grading operations generally were collapsed and the loose materials from within the
collapsed lava tubes were removed The resulting excavation was backfilled with
compacted fill placed in general accordance with the recommendations outlined in the
earthwork specifications and or geotechnical engineering report
Discussion and Recommendations
Based on the grading plan for the project the original ground surface elevations
ranged from about 30 to 40 feet Mean Sea Level MSL at the Circuit City site The
Circuit City site generally was graded to a finished pad elevation of about 32 25 feet
MSL as shown on the As Built Topographic Survey Plate 1 Mass grading at the Circuit
City site generally consisted of placing up to about 3 feet of new fills and excavating up to
about 8 feet to achieve the design grades
At the commencement of the mass grading operations extensive subgrade
preparation procedures were employed within the limits of grading to reduce the potential
for collapse of near surface cavities and or voids within the site The intent of the subgrade
preparation procedures was to assist in detecting and collapsing near surface cavities
and or voids contained in the porous near surface materials It should be noted that the
subgrade preparation procedures employed for this project are consistent with the
standard of practice for similar type projects and similar subsurface conditions along the
west side of the Island of Hawaii
In general the subgrade preparation in fill areas consisted of ripping the original
ground to a depth of about 3 feet or until refusal using a ripper tooth of a large bulldozer to
assist in detecting and collapsing near surface cavities and or voids contained in the
porous near surface materials After the fill surfaces have been ripped the ground
generally was leveled with the use of a Komatsu 375 A bulldozer working the surface for
about four passes to provide a relatively level surface After leveling of the rugged surfaces
with the bulldozer the fill subgrades generally were proof rolled with a Caterpillar 825 B
sheepsfoot roller compactor minimum 20 tons for about eight passes to help detect and
collapse near surface cavities and or voids

In general fill materials consisting of structural fills were placed in the fill areas of
the project site Structural fills consisted of well graded granular materials generally
smaller than 6 inches in dimension
Subgrades in cut areas generally were proof rolled with a Caterpillar 825 B
sheepsfoot roller compactor minimum 20 tons for about eight passes to help detect and
collapse near surface cavities and or voids It should be noted that the portions of the
Circuit City site generally were graded to a cut condition Therefore the presence of basalt
formation and or a a basalt should be anticipated at or near the existing pad elevation in
this area
Base course materials were used as fill and backfill materials under building pad
within utility trench excavations and other areas Samples of the on site crushed
materials utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office
for testing where appropriate The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture
contents were established in our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test results are summarized on Plate 2
The fill and backfill materials generally were moisture conditioned to above
optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent of the maximum
dry density as determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557
During the earthwork operations periodic field density tests were performed in
general accordance with ASTM D 2922 and D 3017 Nuclear Gauge Method where
appropriate Compaction tests results are summarized on Plate 3 of this report These
tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and
backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and
interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed to
be unsatisfactory were generally reworked until the specified minimum compaction was
obtained and or accepted by our field representative based on visual observation and
hand probing
Based on the observations of our field representatives it is our professional
opinion that the earthwork operations during the mass grading for the above mentioned
lot observed by Geolabs in the period covered in this report were performed in general
conformance with the intent of the project plans and specifications and the geotechnical
engineering recommendations presented in the above geotechnical engineering report
and project plans and specifications
It should be noted the above statement of professional opinion is based on the

Limitations
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork may
have been performed without our observation However the level of earthwork
observation and testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some of
the geotechnical risks to the project It should be recognized that there are variations in the
accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the
