PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located along both sides of Wailua Street and west of the
intersection with Lunalilo Home Road in the Hawaii Kai area on the Island of Oahu
Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1 Most of the site is a peninsula constructed of man made
materials extending out into Kuapa Pond The total project area is about 42 65 acres
Parcel B is the portion of the project site to the north of Wailua Street as shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2 This portion of the project generally consisted of providing shoreline
stabilization measures along the northern and western sides of Parcel B
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services
pertaining to the site work construction for the Parcel B Shoreline Stabilization portion of
The Peninsula at Hawaii Kai project
SITE GRADING AND UTILITY INSTALLATION
The site work construction for the Parcel B Shoreline Stabilization portion of The
Peninsula at Hawaii Kai project were observed by Geolabs Inc from to
March 21 2003 The site work construction for the shoreline stabilization generally
consisted of clearing and grubbing placement and compaction of fills installation of sheet
piles and construction of a keystone retaining wall system Fills on the order of up to

Samples of the on site and imported materials utilized as fill and backfill materials
were generally obtained and transported to our office for testing The maximum dry
densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test results
are summarized on Plate 3
The fill and backfill materials were generally moisture conditioned to above
optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum
dry density as determined in accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557
During the earthwork operations periodic field density tests were performed in
general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 2922 Nuclear Gauge Method
Results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plate 4 of this report
These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and
backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and
interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed to
be unsatisfactory were generally reworked until the specified minimum compaction was
obtained and or approved by our field representative based on visual observation and
hand probing
Our services consisted of part time field observation of the contractor s fill
placement subgrade preparation and compaction procedures and confirmation of
subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above grading operations
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the site grading and utility installation work for the
project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report were
performed in conformance with the general intent of the recommendations in the following
