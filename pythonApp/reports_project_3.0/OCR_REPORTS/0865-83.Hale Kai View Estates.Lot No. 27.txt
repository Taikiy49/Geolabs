As requested we have made a study of the near surface soil conditions
below the above referenced lot The purpose of the study was to eva
luate the suitability of the near surface soils as foundation support
for the proposed house The study consisted of excavating two 2
test pits within the lot performing laboratory soil tests and
reviewing the mass grading plan for the subdivision This report
summarizes our observations and recommendations

SUBDIVISION LOT GRADING
A review of the subdivision mass grading plan prepared by Park
Engineering Inc dated March 14 1980 indicates that Lot 27 was
originally covered with a large stockpile about 10 feet high of soil
and boulders left over from previous grading operations in the
surrounding areas
During that grading operation the stockpile was removed down to a
depth of 3 feet below the design finished grade and replaced with
compacted available on site soils The available on site soil
consisted of silty clay and clayey silt soils which had been mixed
with expansive adobe clay from the previous stockpile

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration disclosed that the lot is underlain by more than
5 feet of brown clay The laboratory test results on this material
generally indicate that it has moderate to high expansion potential
Therefore for Lot 27 the slab on grade or spread foundation would
require special design and construction procedures in order to reduce
the shrink swell effect of the subsoils
House Foundation
It is our understanding that slab on grade construction is planned for
the lower level of the house including the garage and patio
In order to reduce the expansive effect of the soils we recommend
that a minimum 3 foot capping layer of non expansive material be
placed below the garage and patio concrete slabs
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Page 5
The expansive clay below and for a horizontal distance of 3 feet
around the slabs should be removed for a minimum depth of 3 feet below
outside finished grade and replaced with non expansive off site borrow
materials such as select borrow crusher waste or coral compacted
to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density per ASIM D 1557 78
The underlying remaining clay soil should be scarified moistened to
above optimum moisture content and recompacted moderately 85
compaction prior to backfilling with the 3 foot select fill capping
layer
A 4 inch layer of base course should be provided below the slab and
compacted to 90 of its maximum dry density In order to protect
against moisture infiltration a polyethylene vapor barrier may be
incorporated below the slab
The thickened edge slab footings can bear on the compacted
non expansive fill The thickened edges should be embedded a minimum
of 12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade refer to the Schematic
Plan Plate 2 for details
Slabs should be separated from wall foundations to allow for some
differential movement
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f may be used on the compacted
fill
Surface water should be diverted away from the foundations

Post And Beam
A review of the preliminary house plans indicates that the upper floor
level will over hang the lower garage and patio level For this
portion of the house post and beam construction is desirable due to
the sloping terrain and expansive soil conditions The post and beam
construction would generally result in lower foundation costs and the
individual footings can be readily adjusted should such a need occur
For post and beam construction we recommend that the individual post
footing be extended a minimum depth of 36 inches below the finished
grade and backfilled with on site soil compacted to a minimum of 90
of its maximum dry density The footings should be poured in place
and the excavation backfilled with the on site clay soils and
compacted to provide an impervious layer over the footing see Plate 3
for details
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f can be used for footings
bearing on the stiff natural ground or compacted fill
I a footing is located next to a utility line it should be extended
to the bottom of the utility trench to reduce footing settlement due
to settlement of the trench backfill

Surface water should be diverted away from foundations This is
especially important in the more level portion of the house to reduce
ponding of surface run off water that may affect expansive soils
around the foundation
Concrete Driveway and Sidewalks
Expansive clay soils were encountered along the proposed driveway
Since the driveway can tolerate some amount of heaving the clay
over excavation and replacement can be reduced to 2 feet below the
driveway subgrade level Where 2 or more feet of fill is planned
select non expansive borrow material should be used to cap over the
expansive clay Where less than 2 feet of fill is planned
over excavation and replacement of the expansive clay with select
non expansive material is required The above requirements should be
followed for a minimum distance of 3 feet beyond the driveway
For the relatively long driveway planned joints are recommended at
about 8 to 10 foot intervals in order to allow for some relative
movement of the concrete
For future sidewalks capping of the clay or over excavation and
replacement similar to the driveway is recommended to reduce heaving
caused by the expansive clay soil

Site Grading
Fills and backfills within 3 feet of the finished grade should be
select non expansive borrow material placed in maximum 8 inch lifts
and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density
established by ASTM D 1557 78 The on site clay soil should not be
used for fill material within the top 3 feet below the structure and
behind retaining walls
Prior to filling the area should be cleared of vegetation and debris
The finished grade outside the slabs pavements and foundations
should be shaped to avoid ponding conditions and to shed water away
from the structure
Retaining Walls
Retaining walls generally less than 5 feet high are anticipated for
grade separation between the garage and the house The following
design procedures are recommended
1 To reduce expansive effects of the existing clay soils on the
wall we recommend that the expansive clays if encountered be
over excavated down for a depth of 2 feet below the bottom of
the footing and then replaced with non expansive borrow soils
such as crusher waste select borrow or coral compacted to a
minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density per ASIM D 1557 78

The retaining wall footings should be embedded a minimum depth
of 12 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade
Therefore the total depth of the above over excavation and
replacement would be 3 feet below the lowest adjacent finished
grade where adobe clay is encountered refer to Schematic
Section Plate 4 for details
To minimize excessive swelling potential that may be exerted
laterally on the retaining wall the expansive adobe clay
should be over excavated for a distance behind the wall equal
to the height of the wall
Retaining wall backfill should consist of granular materials
such as select borrow or crusher waste This type of backfill
would improve drainage reduce the compactive effort required
for the wall backfill and reduce lateral pressure on the
walls Impervious soil such as on site clay soils should be
used for the top one foot of backfill to reduce seepage of
surface run off behind the walls
A subsurface drainage system such as subdrains or weepholes
should be utilized behind the wall and be designed to drain
freely under gravity to lower open areas away from foundations
An allowable bearing value of up to 3000 p s f is recommended
for foundations bearing on compacted fill

Drainage
The finished lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings should
be shaped to shed water away from foundations and to avoid ponding
condition Also it is advised that gutter systems be installed
around the house and that gutter water be diverted far away from the
foundations and walkways Excessive landscape watering near the
foundations and slab should also be avoided
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 865 83 Page 11
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance
of the above recommended foundations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of
strength The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled
tight against slab footing immediately after setting of the concrete
to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained to
drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundation
Design Review
Plans and specifications for foundation site grading and utility
construction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and
written comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during earthwork
construction to monitor the contractor s grading operations
particularly fill placement
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be observed by a
soils engineer during construction to see that adequate bearing has
been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with
the findings of our investigation
