I INTRODUCTION
General
This report presents the results of our limited investigation
to determine the subsurface soil and foundation conditions
for the project The purpose of the investigation was to
_ provide subsurface information necessary to the design of a
foundation support system for the proposed structures The
study included subsurface exploration soil sampling labora
tory testing and engineering analyses The report summarizes
our work on the project and presents preliminary general
recommendations pertinent to foundation requirements Final
foundation recommendations will be provided after recommended
grading of the site is completed

II FIELD EXPLORATION
Two subsurface borings ranging in depth from 16 5 to 26 feet were
completed on August 31 1972 The borings were accomplished
using a minuteman rotary drilling equipment with continuous
flight solid stem augers
The boring locations are shown on the site sketch Plate 1 and
the boring logs are presented in Appendix A
In all borings Standard Penetration Tests or 2 5 inch I D ring
samples were taken at selected depth intervals not exceeding
5 feet The Standard Penetration Test consists of driving a
2 inch 0 D split spoon sampler 18 inches with a 140 pound hammer
free falling a distance of 30 inches The number of blows required
to drive the sampler the last 12 inches is termed the Standard
Penetration Resistance N and is an approximate 1 measure of the
relative density or consistency of a soil These resistance
values are plotted on the boring logs Samples obtained were
visually classified in the field and representative material was
placed in air tight containers and returned to our laboratory
for additional inspection and testing

IV ENGINEERING RECOMMENDATIONS
Foundations
Due to the loose nature of the subsurface soils in the
general vicinity of the proposed structures as shown by
the boring logs it is recommended that the upper 4 feet
of onsite materials be removed and recompacted to a minimum
of 90 of maximum laboratory density determined by ASTM
D 1557 70 This will provide for more uniform foundation
conditions and will reduce differential settlements due to
the loose soils immediately below
In addition to the building site preparation recommended
above it will also be necessary to limit the allowable
bearing value Conventional spread footings may be designed
for an allowable bearing value of 500 pounds per square foot
and should be a minimum of 12 inches in width and 12 inches
in depth All structural footings should be reinforced with
a minimum of 2 No 4 bars top and bottom
Settlements
The recommended recompaction and utilization of the low bearing
pressure should limit Settlements to tolerable values The
maximum settlement should not exceed 1 inch while the maximum
differential settlement should not exceed 3 4 inch

Expansive Soils
The upper soils to be recompacted are considered to be low
to moderately expansive In addition to the above recommenda
tion for footing reinforcement the following additional
recommendations are applicable
1 No exterior open planters should be incorporated into
the design of the proposed structures
2 Floor slabs should be reinforced with a minimum of
6x6 10x10 welded wire fabric and underlain by a 4 inch
thick base course layer The base should be crusher
run base or adequate coral material
3 Slabs which will be provided with a floor covering
should be protected by a vapor barrier of polyvinyl
chloride or similar material The barrier should be
covered with a thin layer of sand about one inch
thick to prevent punctures and to aid in the concrete

LABORATORY TESTING
The laboratory testing program was developed to establish the
engineering properties of the soils and rocks encountered at
the project site
l Classification Tests
AVES ES
a Visual Classification All soil samples obtained from
a
the borings were brought to our laboratory where they were
visually reclassified to confirm or modify the field classi
fication prior to finalizing the Logs of Borings
b Water Content Determinations In addition to visual
classification typical samples were tested for natural
_ water content as an aid in soil classification and in
evaluating soil properties The water content values are
based upon the dry weight of the soil
c Atterberg Limits Atterberg limit tests were performed
on selected clay and silt samples obtained from the borings
for the purposes of identification and correlation of soils
Standard procedures ASTM D 424 for the plastic limit and
ASTM D 423 for the liquid limit were used in the performance
of these tests
d Grain Size Analysis Sieve analysis were performed on
granular soils For fine grained soils the hydrometer
a

grain sizes beyond the No 200 sieve
Shear Strength Tests
a Unconfined Compression Tests Intact core and split
poon soil samples which apparently exhibited high plasticity
_were tested for unconfined strength Length to diameter
ratio of 2 was maintained throughout the tests to avoid
scatter of test results and to facilitate evaluation of
shear strength values for design purposes
b Direct Shear Test In general soil samples were tested
for shear strength using a 2 41 I D direct shear box Each
Set consisted of at least two tests each test run under
different normal loads The results are plotted as Normal
Stress vs Shear Strength with a failure envelope drawn to
determine values of cohesion c and angle of frictional
resistance
