PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this soil engineering exploration was to explore the
subsurface soil and groundwater conditions at the site in order to provide
soil data for use in the design of the proposed wastewater treatment
plant
The work was generally done in accordance with our proposal of July
18 1985
The results of our field exploration shallow seismic refraction
surveys and laboratory tests which we used in the engineering analysis
that formulated our conclusions and recommendations are presented in the
appendices of this report
The proposed wastewater treatment plant is part of a proposed sewage
disposal system for the Turtle Bay Resort development Wastewater will be
pumped by a pump station to the proposed treatment plant and treated
effluent will be used for irrigation of the golf courses of the
development Excess effluent will be disposed by means of subsurface
injection wells Separate reports have been issued for the pump station
and injection well system
The location of the proposed wastewater treatment plant in relation
to the proposed locations of the pump station and injection well system at
Turtle Bay Resort is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The
approximate boring test pit and seismic survey locations at the proposed
wastewater treatment plant site are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2

SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed site is located on the southern edge of an old coastal
plain mauka of the Turtle Bay Hotel and Kamehameha Highway near Kahuku
Point on the island of Oahu Hawaii
The site totalling approximately 20 acres is currently an
undeveloped area completely covered with grasses and trees An existing
soil ditch extends along the upper boundary of the site at the toe of the
hillside
The area slopes down gradually from the south hillside towards
Kamehameha Highway with ground surface elevations from about 50 to 12 feet
above Mean Sea Level
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
The proposed wastewater treatment plant consists of four 4 lined
aerated lagoons with a total capacity of approximately 4 2 M G along with
supporting utility buildings sewer lines access roads and service roads
It is our understanding that each lagoon will be approximately 15
feet deep and 240 by 540 feet in plan dimension at the top with side
walls sloping inwards at a 3 1 H V ratio The proposed lagoons will be
constructed by deep excavation of up to about 27 feet into the existing
ground to the lagoon bottoms at the recommended finished grade of
elevation 9 feet above Mean Sea Level
The high ground area hillside to the south of the lagoons will be
cut back to provide sufficient space for the proposed treatment facility

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The project site is located on the Kahuku Coastal Plain which is
essentially a broad flat windward island fringe located on the flank of
the elongated Koolau Volcanic Range which forms the northern portion of
Oahu Hawaii The range was formed during the Tertiary Period of
Pahoehoe and Aa basalt lava flows from a rift zone roughly paralleling
the existing mountain trends During the Late Tertiary and Pleistocene
Ice Age valleys were eroded by streams During high stands of sea
level in the Pleistocene the valleys and slope breaks at the base of the
ridges were infilled with sediment alluviated grading to the high sea
level stands
The site lies partly on a gently sloping upland slope that consists
of coalesced fans of alluvium and colluvial sediments derived from the
salient volcanic cliffs of the Koolau Range to the south of the site
The site soils consist of stiff clays with varying amounts of silts
and sands underlain by basalt formations
Subsurface Soil Conditions
The subsurface soil conditions at the site were explored by drilling
and sampling thirteen 13 borings to depths of about 16 5 to 40 0 feet
and excavating fifteen 15 test pits to depths of 3 5 to 12 0 feet below
the existing ground surface and by performing two 2 shallow seismic
refraction surveys in selected areas of the project site

The drilled borings and test pits encountered a mantle of highly
expansive clays underlain by other alluvium coalesced with colluvial
sediments which include sandy silt to clay mixed with gravel cobbles and
boulders The highly expansive clay varied from about 2 to 13 feet in
thickness at the proposed lagoon and earth embankment areas but became
generally thinner on the southern hillside except in the eastern portion
of the proposed cut slope
Further underlying the highly expansive surface clays and
alluviun colluvium was highly weathered basalt at depths of about 24 to 34
feet below the existing ground surface in the proposed lagoon and earth
embankment areas The weathered basalt was encountered at relatively
shallow depths of about 2 to 3 feet in Borings 11 and 12 and Test Pits 1
through 6 on the southwestern hillside near the existing soil ditch
However in Boring 13 and Test Pits 7 through 15 at similar high ground
elevation the weathered basalt was encountered at a depth of about 5 to
10 feet It appears that the basalt in this area was previously cut by a
drainageway and filled with clay
Very hard unweathered gray massive basalt was encountered under the
weathered basalt at the depth of about 33 feet below the existing ground
surface in Boring 12
Groundwater level at the site was monitored during our field
exploration A high groundwater level at about elevation 5 to 7 feet
above Mean Sea Level was recorded at the proposed lagoon areas Higher
water elevations of up to 11 feet above Mean Sea Level were encountered at
the hillside areas above the proposed lagoons
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that the site is underlain by
variable soil conditions The soils exposed at finished grade may consist
of expansive clay alluvium or rock formation Therefore the site
grading design should be flexible to consider these conditions

Additionally a high water level of 5 to 7 feet above sea level was
measured at the site We recommend that the bottoms of the lagoons be
raised above this high water level in order to reduce construction
difficulties and potential instability of the lagoons
Aerated Lagoons
Originally the bottom of the lagoons was designed at elevation 5
feet above Mean Sea Level However during the field exploration the
existing groundwater level was encountered at about elevation 5 to 7
feet above Mean Sea Level
The existing high groundwater level would create a muddy subgrade
condition which may pose special construction problems and increase the
construction costs
Additionally the lagoon lining may be subjected to hydraulic buoyant
forces and may become unstable when the lagoons are emptied for routine
maintenance or some other operational purposes
A dewatering system as well as a gravel blanket subdrain system were
considered to lower the existing high groundwater level to below the
proposed lagoon bottom at elevation 5 feet above Mean Sea Tevel
However the proposed lagoon areas are so large that it would be very
difficult to drain the site below the bottoms of the lagoons Moreover
due to the existing flat ground gradient in the vicinity of the proposed
lagoons a pump system may be required to discharge the groundwater
collected from the dewatering or drainage system
Therefore we recommend that the finished grade of the lagoon bottoms
be raised by at least 4 feet from the preliminary design elevation 5 feet
to elevation 9 feet above Mean Sea Level

The proposed lagoons and surrounding earth embankments may then be
constructed by excavation and site filling with selected excavated
materials to the design slope ratio
An impervious liner is planned within the lagoons To reduce the
potential for punctures in the lining a cushion layer of fine grained
soil or sand is recommended below the bottom of the liner in the southern
portion of the site where rock formation is anticipated The cushion
layer should be a minimm of 6 inches thick
Lagoon Slopes
The two southern lagoons Nos 1A and 2A will be formed primarily by
deep excavation while the two northern lagoons Nos 1B and 2B will be
formed partially by cut and partially by fill embankments
Based on our findings we believe that the present design slope ratio
of 3 1 H V could provide adequate stability for the lagoons
Although the lagoons will be lined with an impervious liner there is
a possibility of some seepage Therefore we recommend that the
downstream slope of the northern embankment be constructed with a granular
toe section to allow drainage of seepage and reduce the possibility of
piping through the embankment Alternatively a flat slope ratio of about
7 1 H V could be utilized on the downstream slope in lieu of the
granular toe section
Southern Cut Slope
Based on our findings the proposed cut slope on the southern
hillside will primarily be constructed by cutting into weathered basalt
formations overlain by a variable surface layer of expansive clay soils
We believe that an overall design slope ratio of up to may be used
for cut slopes in rock formation

Some portions of the proposed cut slope may be mantled with
relatively thick expansive clays Due to the low strength of the clay
soils a much flatter slope is required For clay layers up to about 5
feet thick a 3 1 H V cut slope ratio is recommended Where the clay
layer is thicker than 5 feet the clay exposed within the cut slope should
be removed and replaced with a buttress fill consisting of compacted
non expansive on site soil to maintain the stability of the cut slope
The recommended scheme is presented on the generalized section of the
southern cut slope on Plates 4 and 5 and the approximate extent of the
buttress fill area is estimated on the Site Plan Plate 2
Site Grading
Major site grading is anticipated including up to about 27 feet of
cut into the existing ground for the proposed lagoons and up to about 12
feet of fill above the existing ground for the exterior earth embankments
containing the proposed lagoons
Site Preparation
Prior to grading all deleterious materials such as vegetation
should be removed and disposed of properly off site to prevent
contamination of the excavated materials
In the areas to receive fill the existing ground should be scarified
to a depth of 6 inches moisture conditioned to 3 to 5 percent above
optimum moisture content and then compacted to 90 relative
compaction as determined by ASTM D 1557 78
Any localized soft or weak areas should be over excavated to fimn
ground and backfilled in 9 inch loose horizontal lifts compacted to
the above specified requirement

Fill Material and Placement
The surface expansive clay soils should be separated from the
underlying less expansive alluvium and disposed off site The
underlying alluvium which includes silty sand to silty clay with
gravel may be used as a source of fill and structural fill Basalt
gravel and cobbles less than 8 inches in size obtained from the
excavation into the basalt formations may also be used as fill
material
Imported fill material should be free of organic and other
deleterious material non expansive and generally less than 6 inches
in size
The fills should be placed in loose horizontal lifts not exceeding 9
inches and compacted to a minimm of 90 relative compaction as
determined by ASTM D 1557 78
Site grading should provide drainage of surface run off away from all
foundations
Foundations
The stiff on site soils or compacted fill may provide adequate
bearing for building pump and tank foundations We recommend that an
allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f be used for footings embedded a
minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finish grade This bearing
value may be increased by one third when temporary wind or seismic loads
are considered The bottoms of the foundation excavation should be
compacted to a minimum of 90 relative compaction as detemmined by ASTM
D 1557 78
Foundations located close to the top of the embankment slopes should
be embedded a sufficient depth to obtain a 5 foot minimum horizontal
set back distance measured from the outer edge of footing to the slope
face

For the light structural loads anticipated settlements are
anticipated to be minimal on the order of less than one half inch total
and one quarter inch differential
Lateral loads may be resisted by passive earth pressures developed by
the soil and by friction along the base of the footing For design
purposes a passive soil pressure of 300 p c f equivalent fluid pressure
and a coefficient of friction between the footing bottom and soils of 0 4
may be used
Slab On Grade
Due to the high expansion potential of the surface clay soils
Slabs on grade for buildings and tanks should not be supported directly on
the existing surface clay soils Where the slab subgrade is in the highly
expansive clay soils the subgrade should be overexcavated and backfilled
with a minimum of 3 feet 36 inches of non expansive select fill
compacted to 90 relative compaction as determined by ASIM D 1557 78
Prior to the placement of the non expansive select fill the highly
expansive clay soils below the subgrade should be kept moist
For normal floor loads a 4 inch thick concrete slab over 4 inches of
base course compacted to 95 of its ASTM D 1557 78 maximum dry density is
recommended
In areas where dampness in the floor slab would be undesirable or
where moisture susceptible floor coverings are to be used the base course
should be replaced with a minimum of 4 inches of 43 Fine gravel to serve
aS a capillary break Additionally a moisture proof membrane should be
placed over the gravel to serve as a vapor barrier The water proof
membrane should be covered with a thin layer of well graded sand which
will serve to protect the membrane and aid in the curing of the concrete

Access Service Road and Parking Pavements
Asphaltic concrete pavements are anticipated for the access roads
service roads and parking areas If encountered the highly expansive
clay soils should be overexcavated and backfilled with a minimum of 2 feet
24 inches of compacted non expansive select fill
Based on the anticipated light traffic conditions and the CBR test
results the following pavement section is recommended
2 inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 inch Base Course
8 inch Total Pavement Thickness
Additional CBR tests should be performed on the actual subgrade soil
samples during construction and the above design section may be revised
if necessary The recommended pavement section assumes that good drainage
will be provided for areas in adjacent to pavements

Plans for site grading lagoon and embankment design and structure

recommendations is included in the project plans and specifications

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Re
Moisture content unit weight and Atterberg Limits determinations
were performed on selected samples as an aid in the classification and
evaluation of soil properties The results of these tests are presented
on the logs of borings at the appropriate sample depths
Three 3 laboratory CBR tests were performed on bulk samples of the
near surface material to evaluate their swelling potential and subgrade
support capacity The test results are summarized on Plates B 1 B 2 and
B 4
Five 5 Atterberg Limit tests were performed on selected samples of
the soils to evaluate their liquid and plastic limits The test results
are also shown on Plates B 2 through B 5 and B 7
Eleven 11 sieve analyses were performed to determine the gradation
characteristics of the site materials The test results are summarized on
Plates B 1 through B 6
Eight 8 one inch ring swell tests were performed on natural and
remolded samples to evaluate the swelling potential of the existing near
surface soils The test results are summarized on Plate B 8
Two 2 direct shear tests were performed on samples of the clay
soils to evaluate their shear strength parameters for slope stability
evaluation The test results are presented on Plates B 9 and B 10
Two 2 consolidation tests were performed on samples of the alluvial
soils to evaluate their compressibility The test results are plotted on
Plates B 11 and B 12

Seismic Refraction Surveys
StS A ts
In conjunction with the sampling operations geophysical exploration
techniques were used to obtain further subsurface data
A series of two 2 shallow seismic refraction surveys were performed
in selected areas of the project site at approximate distances of 14 and
133 feet respectively to the south of Boring 13 These surveys were
performed using a BISON Model 1570C Refraction Seismograph receiving
seismic signals generated by the impacts of a 10 pound sledge hammer The
signal enhancement feature of this seismograph provided a relatively
noise free signal as the result of repeated blows of the sledge hammer
The approximate locations of the tests are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
and the results are presented on Plates C 1 and C 2
