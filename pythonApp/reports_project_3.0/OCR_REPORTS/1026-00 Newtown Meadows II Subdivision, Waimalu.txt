STANDARD GRADING SPECIFICATIONS
NEWTOWN MEADOWS II
The work under this section includes clearing and grubbing
preparation of fill areas placement and compaction of fill
and removal and backfilling of underground structures
These
specifications are of a general nature and may be superseded
by specific requirements or recommendations contained within
the soils report
I
Clearing
All areas within contract limit lines shall be cleared of
trash debris organic matter and all other deleterious
materials
These materials shall be removed and disposed
offsite
II
Preparation of Fill Areas
A
Loose surface soils shall be removed to expose compe
tent natural soils
Localized soft pockets shall be
undercut and backfilled with compacted select mate
rials
B
All surfaces shall be inspected and approved by the
soils engineer prior to the placement of any fill
C
After approval the surface shall be scarified to a
depth of six inches brought to near optimum moisture
content and compacted as herein specified
2
O
Where the existing surface exceeds a slope of 5
horizontal to 1 vertical 5 1 the ground surface
shall be keyed and benched into competent soil to
bond the fill to the existing ground surface
E
Subsurface drains may be required as directed by the
soils engineer where springs or zones of seepage are
encountered
III
Fill Materials
I V
Material for fill shall consist of on site soils or
approved import material
Import material shall be
tested and approved prior to transportation to the site
Fill material shall contain less than 5 percent organic
matter and other deleterious material and shall not
contain rocks or lumps in excess of eight inches 8 11
in diameter
Placement and Compaction of Fill
A
The fill shall be placed in horizontal layers which
when compacted will not exceed nine inches 9 in
thickness
Each layer shall be spread uniformly and
blade mixed to attain uniformity of material and
water content within each layer
3
B
Rock fragments or boulders less than eight inches
8 in diameter may be utilized provided they are
not concentrated in any one area and that there is
sufficient fine grained material to fill the voids
between the rocks
C
Rocks greater than eight inches 8 in diameter
shall be removed from the fill unless specifically
approved for placement in the fill by the soils
engineer
D
All fill shall be compacted to a minimum of 90
percent of the maximum dry density established by
ASTM Test Designation D 1557 unless otherwise
specified in the soils report
E
The compaction equipment may be sheepsfoot vibra
tory or pneumatic tire rollers suited to the soil
type and capable of producing the specified results
in an expedient and efficient manner
The equipment
shall be in good working condition fully ballasted
and self cleaning
F
The compaction shall be accomplished in a systematic
and uniform manner over the entire fill area to pro
duce a uniformly compacted fill
V
4
G
Fill material placed in an unsatisfactory condition
and not in accordance with these specifications
shall not be approved and shall be removed or reworked
until the required density is obtained
H
The soils engineers shall be provided unrestricted
access to the fill to perform the compaction tests
Remov l and Backfilling of Underground Structures
Underground structures such as cesspools cisterns
septic tanks wells pipe lines etc shall be removed
as directed by the soils engineer
Backfilling of the
excavations shall be in accordance with these specifi
cations
VI
Seasonal Requirements
A
No fill shall be placed during unfavorable weather
conditions
When interruption of work is due to
heavy rain the previous fill surface shall be
approved before resumption of earthmoving operations
B
The contractor shall maintain the surface of the fill
so that it will be free draining and will not pond
surface water
