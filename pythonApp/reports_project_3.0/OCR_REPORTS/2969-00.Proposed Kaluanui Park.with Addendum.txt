Over Excavation and Backfilling
Since the soft and highly compressible layer was encountered at relatively shallow
depths from the existing ground surface about 2 to 5 feet below the surface in our

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
The island of Oahu is composed largely of the weathered remnants of two 2
extinct shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae forms the
bulk of the western third of the island while the younger shield Koolau forms the majority
of the eastern two thirds of the island Waianae became extinct while Koolau was still
active and its eastern fiank is partially buried below Koolau lavas in central Oahu
The project site is located at the southeastern end of Koolau and its
geomorphology and subsurface conditions are directly related to glacio eustatic
fluctuations of sea level during the Pleistocene The base of the stratigraphic section in
this area is Koolau Basalt
The Koolau voicanic shield was built during the late Pliocene and early Pleistocene
by thin bedded basaltic lava flows The main shield building activities ceased
approximately 2 5 million years ago Evidence from drilled wells indicates that the island
of Oahu has subsided as much as 1200 feet since the cessation of this early volcanic
activity Macdonald and Abbott 1970
During the Pleistocene Ice Age there were many sea level changes as a result
of widespread glaciation in the continental areas of the world As the great continental
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2969 00 Page 6
glaciers accumulated the level of the ocean fell since there was less water available to
fill the oceanic basins Conversely as the glaciers receded or melted global sea levels
rose because more water was available The land mass of Oahu remained essentially
stable during these changes and the fluctuations were eustatic in nature These
glacio eustatic fluctuations resulted in stands of the sea which were both higher and lower
relative to present sea level on Oahu
The higher sea level stands caused the accumulation of terrigenous sediments in
the heads of the old bays accumulation of reef deposits at corresponding higher
elevations and lagoonal marine sediments in the quiet waters protected by fringing reefs
The lower sea stands caused streams to carve valleys in the volcanic dome of
Koolau
During the late Pleistocene volcanic activity was renewed on Koolau with eruptions
of the Honolulu Volcanic Series In the vicinity of the site eruptions along the Koko
Fissure deposited pyroclastic materials consisting of tuff and cinder in portions of the
valleys cut into the southeastern end of Koolau
The site is situated at the mouth of Hahaione Valley and was formerly part of
Kuapa Pond a shallow estuary formed at the confluence of Hahaione Kamilonui and
Kamiloiki Valleys Land reclamation efforts filled portions of this estuary to create new
land for development The small pond in the central portion of the site appears to be a
landlocked remnant of Kuapa Pond

General
It is our opinion that the proposed project is feasible from a geotechnical
engineering standpoint provided that our recommendations are followed Our exploration
encountered soft subsoil conditions which could result in ground settlements and
foundation settlement unless special design considerations and construction procedures
are incorporated into the project

Conceptual plans of the site grading indicate that the majority of the earthwork will

Fill Settlements
Our exploration indicates that there are soft or loose subsoils underlying the
existing fill along the boundaries of the pond area The additional loads imposed by new
fill could induce settlements in these soft or loose subsoils
Our field exploration and laboratory testing indicate that the settlements generated
by the new fill will be variable and range from on the order of about two 2 to about four
inches 4 It is estimated that these settlements will be relatively rapid and should occur
within about 30 to 90 days of the fill placement
The new fill should be placed over the existing fill and the underlying soils as soon
as practicable to allow fill ground settlements to occur first prior to the installation of any
new site improvements The new fill should be placed and compacted as recommended
in the Site Grading section of this report
To reduce the potential of failure of the underlying soils due to excessive pore
pressures caused by the rapid increase in loading we recommend that the fill be placed
in stages No more than 18 inches of new fill should be placed and compacted in any
one day
Fill operations should as much as practicable move laterally in towards the pond
area to allow portions of the soft clays underlying the existing fill crust to slump and
squeeze out into the open pond During fill placement operations the fill and the
surrounding area should be monitored for signs of pumping or lateral ground movements
lf these are noted filling should be stopped immediately and the area being worked
should be allowed to rest to allow pore water pressure build up within the saturated soft
soil to dissipate

Site Grading
The following recommendations are general guidelines for design of the grading
and for the development of general specifications
Prior to commencement of grading all areas within the proposed grading limits
should be cleared of the existing structures stockpiled materials all vegetation and
deleterious materials The resulting grub and spoil material should be disposed of
properly off site
Soft or yielding areas encountered during clearing and grubbing should be over
excavated to firm natural material and the resulting depression backfilled with well
compacted engineered fill
In general excavated on site soil materials should be suitable as general fill
material Generally the on site surface soils are expansive and should not be used within
30 inches of foundation or pavement subgrade level Imported material for general fill
should consist of material which is free of organic material less than three inches 3 in
maximum size and capable of forming a dense stable mass when compacted Imported

material for use within 30 inches of structural and pavement subgrades should generally
conform to the requirements stipulated in Section 30 of the Standard Specifications for
Public Works Construction Select Borrow for Subbase Course dated September 1986
General fills required for the project site grading should be placed in level lifts not
exceeding ten inches 10 and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry
density as established by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 Trench backfills and structural

Pond Filling

granular fill materials such as surge rock clean rock material ranging from about one

Slopes
For the design of cut and fill slopes within the park area in general a slope ratio
of two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V or flatter is recommended For the design of the

Fill slopes should be constructed by overfilling and cutting back to the design slope

Slabs On Grade
It is anticipated that the floors of the proposed comfort station will be slabs on
grade Our laboratory testing indicates that the existing surface soils at the site are
moderately to highly expansive
Special subgrade preparation will be required to reduce the potential of distresses
to the floor slab resulting from the swelling of these expansive on site soils Generally
the material within a minimum of 30 inches from the bottom of the slab cushion should
be select non expansive material such as cinder coral crushed rock or silty soil
The material should be well graded with no particles greater than three inches 3
in size with a laboratory CBR swell value not exceeding one 1 percent and CBR value
of 20 or more it should not contain large percentages of boulders organic or other
deleterious material It should contain sufficient fines so that there is a minimum of 25
percent passing the No 200 sieve and should be capable of forming a competent stable
mass when compacted
The slab subgrade areas should be wetted and kept moist prior to placement of
concrete Moisture barriers and capillary breaks may be incorporated below the slabs
to minimize the potential of damage to the floor coverings by infiltration of moisture
The thickened edges of the slabs should be embedded a minimum of 12 inches
below the lowest adjacent exterior grade
A four inch 4 layer of No 3B Fine gravel or basaltic termite barrier should be
provided below the slabs as cushion fill
To minimize future subgrade soils from heaving due to subsequent saturation we
recommend that the subgrade soils should be kept continuously moist by water sprinkling
prior to concrete placement

engineering properties of the material

a relative saturation Tne moisture content expressed as a percentage of the dry weight
of the tested sample is shown at the appropriate location on plates summarizing test

to classify the soil under the Unified Soil Classification System The results of these tests
are shown at the appropriate sample depths on the Boring Logs

size distribution in the Unified Soil Classification System and to provide indices on the
engineering properties The results of these tests are summarized on Plates B 3 through
B 6

of a soil These physicai parameters may be used in engineering analyses pertaining to
the performance or suitability of a soil

and embankment These test resulis are summarized on Plate B 1
