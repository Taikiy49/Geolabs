2 1 Regional Geology
The Island of Pohnpei is a result of extruding basalt and basaltic lava flows from
one shield volcano centered near Kolonia The building stage persisted for about
1 million years Lavas from the main building stage are predominantly thin gently
dipping flows of olivine phyric alkali olivine basalt
After the shield building lavas the Awak Volcanic Series lava flows followed
The Awak Series lavas range in composition from hawaiite to basanite and trachyte
The most recent volcanic series is the Kupwuriso Volcanic Series The lavas of the
Kupwuriso Series are all nepheline normative and range from nephelinite to basanite
During the volcanic subsidence period coral algal reefs grew on the shallow
fringes and created a fringe reef The reef growth essentially kept pace with the
subsidence rate A series of lagoons formed behind the fringe reefs and both
terrigenous and marine sediments accumulated in the lagoons During lower sea
stands streams carved valleys in the sediments and reef deposits
Subaerial erosion of the upper areas of the volcanic dome deposited terrigenous
alluvial soils under relatively high energy conditions within and along the streams
During periods of no significant sea level changes the continued stream action
extended the alluvial deltas and fans seaward and deposited alluvium over the lagoonal
sediments

SECTION 3 DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the review of available information and the subsurface conditions
encountered at the subject areas we believe that the probable cause of the distressed
pavement areas along the runway is due to consolidation settlement of the very soft
alluvium with thickness ranging between about 52 and 62 feet Consolidation
settlement of the very soft alluvium is caused by the weight from the significant amount
of fill about 25 to 30 feet thick needed to construct the runway
The laboratory consolidation test results indicate that the very soft alluvium is
underconsolidated still settling Based on our engineering analyses we estimated an
ultimate settlement of about 6 to 7 feet The estimated time to achieve 90 percent
consolidation is about 25 to 30 years

Continuous Surface Concrete Slab On Concrete Piles
This alternative consists of a continuous reinforced concrete slab at the ground
surface supported on driven piles The surface concrete slab acts as the pavement
section The driven piles would consist of 16 inch square or octagonal precast
prestressed concreted piles installed in a grid pattern with a spacing of about
10 feet The pile would mainly derive support from end bearing on the basalt rock
formation encountered at about 140 to 144 feet below the existing ground surface
The 16 inch precast prestressed concrete piles may be designed with an allowable
compressive load capacity of up to 60 tons 120 kips per pile
Due to the continuing consolidation of the very soft alluvium downdrag loading
negative skin friction on the piles will occur Based on our foundation analyses
we estimate that the downdrag loads imposed on the vertical piles may be on the
order of about 60 kips per pile due to consolidation of the very soft alluvium It

Deep Compaction Grouting
Deep compaction grouting consists of injecting a pumpable low slump grout into
the very soft alluvium The grout fills displaces and densifies the highly
compressible alluvium Typically holes less than 6 inches in diameter are drilled
through the existing asphalt concrete surface and into the very soft alluvium
located between 25 and 85 feet below the existing ground surface The holes are
drilled in a grid pattern with a spacing of about 3 to 5 feet The grout is injected into
the hole at several depths within the very soft alluvium layer to provide sufficient
densification compaction of the very soft alluvium
If this option is selected we recommend constructing a test section to evaluate this
method prior to production work This option has a higher risk as compared to the
concrete slab supported on piles However the total construction duration and
temporary closure of the runway would be much less In addition there would be
much less noise during construction After the deep compaction grouting operation

Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
Fills and backfills should be moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the
optinum moisture content placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose
thickness and compacted to not less than 95 percent relative compaction Relative
compaction refers to the in place dry density of the soil expressed as a percentage
of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with
ASTM D 1557 test procedures Optimum moisture is the water content
percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
Compaction should be accomplished by sheepsfoot rollers vibratory rollers
multiple wheel pneumatic tired rollers or other types of acceptable compaction
equipment Water tamping jetting or ponding should not be allowed to compact the
fill material for this project
Cutand Fill Slopes
Cuts and fill slopes should be designed with a slope inclination of two horizontal to
one vertical 2H 1V or flatter Fills placed on slopes steeper than 5H 1V should be
keyed and benched into the existing slope to provide stability of the new fill against
sliding The filling operations should start at the lowest point and continue up in
level horizontal compacted layers in accordance with the above fill placement
recommendations Fill slopes should be constructed by overfilling and then cutting
back to the design slope ratio to obtain a well compacted slope face

should be anticipated We estimate ground settlements at the runway turnarounds to

We recommend placing new fills as soon as practical to allow the anticipated

We believe that a settlement waiting period of about 12 weeks at the runway

into the construction schedule for the project The settlement waiting period should
commence after completion of the fill placement It should be recognized that it is
difficult to accurately predict the exact time required for the filled ground to settle

the history of the subsoil deposition
For the loose and or soft subsoils anticipated at the project site it is possible that
the estimated settlement period could vary by as much as 50 to 100 percent from the
actual settlement period Therefore the actual settlement rates should be monitored
and a settlement monitoring program should be established to evaluate the magnitude
and rate of the estimated settlements during the settlement waiting period prior to
construction of the new structures In addition provisions should be made for potential
delays in the construction schedule if a longer settlement waiting period is required
To monitor the actual settlement rate we recommend that a minimum of four
settlement gauges be installed at each turnaround and at the ARFF building pad area

Because of the loose and or soft subsurface conditions near the ARFF building

Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a
3 inch OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches In
addition some samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance
with ASTM Test Designation D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of
Soils by driving a 2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same hammer and
drop The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an
18 inch drive are shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Borings at the
appropriate sample depths
Core samples of the rock and coral formations encountered at the site were
obtained using diamond core drilling techniques in general accordance with
ASTM Standard Practice D 2113 Diamond Core Drilling for Site Investigation Core
drilling is a rotary drilling method that uses a hollow bit to cut into the rock formation
The material left in the hollow core of the bit is mechanically recovered for examination
and classification
Recovery REC is used as a subjective guide to the interpretation of the relative
quality of rock masses Recovery is defined as the actual length of material recovered
from a coring attempt versus the length of the core attempt For example if 4 feet of
material is recovered from a 5 foot core run the recovery would be 80 percent and
would be shown on the Logs of Borings as REC 80

The Rock Quality Designation RQD is also a subjective guide to the relative
quality of rock masses RQD is defined as the percentage of the core run that is sound
material in excess of 4 inches in length without any discontinuities discounting any
drilling induced fractures or breaks If 2 5 feet of sound material is recovered from a
5 foot core run the RQD would be 50 percent and would be shown on the Logs of
Borings as RQD 50 Generally the following is used to describe the relative quality
of the rock based on the Practical Handbook of Physical Properties of Rocks and
Minerals

Laboratory Testing

were performed on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of
soil properties The test results are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate
sample depths
Four Atterberg Limits tests ASTM D 4318 were performed on selected soil

results are summarized on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depth Graphic
presentation of the test results is provided on Plate B 1

Hydrometer tests ASTM D 422 were performed on selected soil samples to evaluate
the gradation characteristics of the soils and to aid in soil classification Graphic
presentations of the grain size distribution are provided on Plates B 2 through B 4
Three Direct Shear tests ASTM D 3080 were performed on selected soil
samples to evaluate the shear strength characteristics Direct shear test results are
presented on Plates B 5 through B 7
Three Consolidation tests ASTM D 2435 with time rates were performed on soil
samples to evaluate the compressibility characteristics of the encountered materials
The test results are presented on Plates B 8 through B 10
