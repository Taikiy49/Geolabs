We have inspected the preparation of existing ground surface in
the building area prior to placement of fill on January 25
1983 A field density was taken and indicated compaction of
99 of the ASTM D 1557 78 maximum density Furthermore we
observed the operation of the earthwork equipment over the
prepared surface and believe that the original existing ground
surface was prepared and compacted in conformance with the
intent of the specifications
Imported fill material was brought in to raise the building
area to Elevation 10 83 the rough finish grade The imported
material consisted of gray brown sandy gravel size crashed rock
fragments with maximum size of about 1 5 inches This material
is non plastic non expansive and forms excellent fill
Although no specific CBR test was performed the material is
believed to have CBR value well in excess of 12 the minimum
specified We take no exception to the use of this material

Fill placement for the project started on January 27 and
completed on During the fill placement we
made three site visits consisting of one full day and two
half day visits Based on our observations the fill material
was spread in 6 to 8 inch thick lifts and compacted with a
wedium sized steel wheel vibratory roller model TAMPO RS 16
Five field density tests were taken in the compacted fill
material All densities were in excess of 90 of the ASTM
D 1557 78 maximum density the minimum specified
The locations of our field density tests are shown on Plate 1
The results of field density tests and our two supporting
laboratory compaction tests performed in accordance with the
ASTM D 1557 78 Method D test procedures are shown on Plates 2
and 3
Based on our observation of the fill construction in the
building area and field density test results we believe that a
ill material meeting specified minimum quality requirements
was placed and compacted to the requirements set forth in the
specifications and in accordance with our recommendations in
our report dated and
