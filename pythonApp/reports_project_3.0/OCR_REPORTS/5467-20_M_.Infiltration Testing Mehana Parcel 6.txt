This report presents the results of our infiltration tests performed for the Mehana
Parcel 6 project in Kapolei on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project location and
general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 and Site Plans
Plates 2 1 through 2 3
This report summarizes the results of our infiltration tests and engineering analyses
for the storm water detention infiltration system The recommendations presented herein
are intended for the design of the detention infiltration system for the subsurface disposal
of storm water runoff The findings and recommendations presented herein are subject to
the limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project involves the development of drainage improvements using a
subsurface storm water detention infiltration system for the Mehana Parcel 6 development
in Kapolei on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The Mehana Parcel 6 development is in the
north central area of the Mehana subdivision site between the future Kamaaha and
Wakea Streets and north of Road A Based on preliminary information provided we
understand that underground storm water detention infiltration chambers will extend to
about 10 to 15 feet below the finished grade and that invert elevations range from about
45 to 50 feet
In accordance with the City s new stormwater requirements infiltration tests and
test pits borings are required to support the on site disposal design Due to relatively deep
inverts of the underground detention infiltration chambers planned infiltration tests using a
double ring infiltrometer through test pits was not practical due to the required shoring
support for safety measures To provide information for the design of the
detention filtration system we conducted a geotechnical engineering exploration by

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lava from the
Waianae and Koolau shield volcanoes The older Waianae Volcano is estimated to be
middle to late Pliocene in age and Koolau Volcano is estimated to be late Pliocene to
early Pleistocene in age As the volcanic activity in Waianae Volcano ceased lava flows
from Koolau Volcano banked against its eroded eastern slope forming a broad plateau
now known as the Schofield Plateau The project site is located on the southwestern flank
of the Waianae Volcano
During the Pleistocene Epoch sea levels fluctuated in response to the cycles of
continental glaciation As the glaciers grew and advanced less water was available to
fill the oceanic basins such that sea levels fell below the present stands of the sea
When the glaciers melted and receded an excess of water became available such that
the sea levels rose to above the present sea level
The higher sea level stands caused the formation of deltas and fans of
accumulated terrigenous sediments in the heads of old bays accumulated reef deposits
at correspondingly higher elevations and deposited lagoonal marine sediments in the
quiet waters protected by fringing reefs The lower sea stands caused streams to carve
valleys in the sediments and reef deposits Subaerial exposure of the sediments and
calcareous materials caused consolidation of the lagoonal deposits and induration of
the calcareous reef materials Agricultural development within the last 100 years and
recent mass grading work brought the Kapolei area to its present form
SUBSURFACE CONDITION
We explored the subsurface conditions at the proposed underground
detention infiltration chamber locations by drilling and sampling fifteen borings designated
as Boring Nos 1 through 15 extending to depths of 10 to 15 feet below the existing
ground surface The approximate boring locations are shown on the Site Plans Plates 2 1
through 2 3
Our field exploration encountered a thin layer of coralline fill overlying coral
formation at shallow depths below the existing ground surface The underlying coral
formation is generally dense coral reef formation with localized friable reef detritus pockets
The coral reef formation extended to the maximum depth drilled about 15 feet below the
ground surface
At the time of our field exploration the mass grading operation was still in progress
The existing ground surface elevations were estimated at approximately 65 to 55 feet
based on available construction survey stakes at the site
We did not encounter groundwater in the borings at the time of our field exploration
However it should be noted that groundwater levels are subject to change due to rainfall
time of year seasonal precipitation surface water runoff and other factors

INFILTRATION TESTING
The water infiltration rates into the subsurface soils were tested by performing
cased borehole percolation tests at the bottom of each boring in general accordance with
the procedures described in the Maryland Storm Water Management manual Each boring
was pre soaked with water for at least 24 hours prior to testing Water was introduced into
the boring and the drop of the water level in the boring was measured along with time for
the falling head test until the water level approach the bottom of the borehole or for one
hour
The tests were carried out in several increments until achieving a steady state with
a relatively constant water infiltration rate Detailed testing data and results are presented
on Plates 3 1 through 3 15

The results of the percolation testing indicated that the infiltration rates at the
approximate inverts of the planned storm water detention infiltration chambers may vary
from 0 3 to 36 inches per hour We anticipate that the overall coral formation would have
good infiltration capacity however zones of the hard near surface coral formation
encountered at the site may provide a semi impervious barrier that would inhibit the rapid
percolation of storm water into the substrata during a storm event in localized areas
It should be noted that each percolation test was conducted through a 4 inch
borehole which may not represent the actual infiltration condition within the entire
chamber footprint Therefore it is necessary to require the contractor to verify the actual
infiltration rate during construction A Geolabs representative should observe the
excavation for the detention infiltration chambers to confirm the anticipated infiltration
substrata conditions

Field Exploration
We explored the subsurface conditions at the project site by drilling and sampling
fifteen boreholes designated as Boring Nos 1 through 15 extending to depths of about
10 to 15 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate boring locations are
shown on the Site Plans Plates 2 1 through 2 3 The borings were drilled using a
truck mounted drill rig equipped with continuous flight augers and rotary wash
drilling coring tools
Our geologist classified the materials encountered in the borings by visual and
textural examination in the field in general accordance with ASTM D2488 Standard
Practice for Description and Identification of Soils and monitored the drilling operations
on a near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by
testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general accordance with ASTM D2487
Standard Practice for Classification of Soils for Engineering Purposes Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on the Soil Log Legend Plate A Graphic
representations of the materials encountered and other observations such as
groundwater levels are presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 15
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained in general accordance with
ASTM D3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 3 inch OD Modified
California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches In addition some
samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM D1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 2 inch
OD standard penetration sampler using the same hammer and drop The blow counts
needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an 18 inch drive are
shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate
sample depths

Laboratory Tests
Moisture Content and Unit Weight tests were performed on selected soil samples
to aid in defining the interface between the various soil layers encountered and
correlating the layers between the borings The moisture content tests were performed
in general accordance with ASTM D2216 The unit weight tests were performed in
general accordance with ASTM D2937 The test results are presented on the Logs of
Borings at the appropriate sample depths in Appendix A
Six Atterberg Limits tests ASTM D4318 were performed on selected soil
samples to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits and to aid in soil classification The test
results are summarized on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Graphic presentation of the test results is provided on Plate B 1
Ten Sieve Analysis tests were performed to evaluate the gradation
characteristics and to aid in soil classification The tests were performed in accordance
with ASTM C117 and C136 Graphic presentation of the test results are provided on
Plates B 2 and B 3
