In accordance with your request we are providing the following geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertaining to the foundation design of the proposed
antenna farm towers for the above referenced project
Project Considerations
The project site is located at the Honolulu TRACON Expansion site within Hickam
Air Force Base in Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii Based on the information
provided it is proposed to construct two 40 foot self supporting 4 legged antenna towers
The new towers will be situated at the northwest side of the new expansion building
under construction and supported on a 10 foot by 10 foot pad of approximately 3 feet
thick
Because of time constraints new borings will not be drilled at the specific locations
of the proposed antenna towers Therefore the boring information presented in the
report entitled Geotechnical Investigation Proposed Federal Aviation Administration
Hawaii dated May 19 1997 prepared by Dames Moore will be used for our foundation
engineering analyses
Geotechnical Engineering Recommendations
Our review of the available soil boring information indicates that the project site
may be underlain by very loose coralline sands and soft silty clays at shallow depths
Based on the anticipated subsurface soil conditions and a design bottom of footing
elevation of about 7 feet Mean Sea Level MSL we recommend that an allowable
bearing pressure of up to 2 000 pounds per square foot psf be used for the foundation

Alternatively the bottom of the over excavations may be lined with a layer of filter fabric
such as Mirafi180N or equivalent prior to placement of the structural fill
Structural fill should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture
content placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to
a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place
dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same
soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 91 test procedures Optimum moisture
is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry
density
