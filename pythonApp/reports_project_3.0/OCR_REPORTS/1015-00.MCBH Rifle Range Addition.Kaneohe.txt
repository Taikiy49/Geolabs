SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is situated near Mokapu Point in the
northeastern end of the Kaneohe Marine Corps Air Station
The area is part of an eroded tuff cone The walls of
the cone are steep with Slope ratio of 14 1 H V to
near vertical in places and form the western boundary of
the area
The existing rifle range is situated in the flat to gently
Sloping bottom of the tuff cone and consists of four embank
ment areas approximately 220 to 280 feet long for the firing
and target areas There are also several one story buildings
and paved roads in the area which is gently sloping with
ground elevations ranging from about 160 to 198 feet
The proposed rifle range expansion area is situated directly
east of the existing facility The ground surface slopes
down toward the expansion area at average slopes of about

GEOLOGY
The Rifle Range site is located inside the extinct volcanic
cone of Ulupau Crater The crater is one of about 30 vol
canic events that make up the Honolulu Series These series
occurred long after the Waianae and Koolau Ranges were found
This particular crater was formed as the hot basaltic magma
encountered ground or sea water causing violent steam erup
tions The resulting ash tuff material was piled up in a
nearly circular pattern around the vent The east wall of
the cone has been entirely removed and the north wall has
been deeply scalloped by wave erosion The floor of the
crater has been formed by talus deposition and slope wash
from the surrounding wall of the cone This material is
comprised of decomposed tuff blocks sandy silts and clays
There is a small amount of carolline sand carolline gravel
and calcium carbonite from a reef existing in the area during
the eruptions The north east portion of this lower area has
been deeply eroded by a stream

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed competent soils at the
project site From a soils engineering point of view
the proposed improvements are feasible at the site
Site Grading
Cuts from about 5 to 10 feet are planned in the areas bet
ween the existing target berms in order to generate suffi
cient material for the proposed target embankment exten
Sions Fills up to 50 feet high are planned for the new
embankments
The site should be cleared of vegetation surface debris
and other deleterious material In areas to receive fill
the ground should be scarified to a minimum depth of six
6 inches brought to near optimum moisture content and
then compacted to a minimum of 90 density per ASTM D 1557
70 test method
After the above site preparation the fill should be mois
tened to above optimum moisture content placed in maximum
10 inch lifts and compacted to obtain a minimum 90 of its
maximum dry density established by the above ASTM test
designation

Based on the field exploration and laboratory test results
in general the on site materials are suitable for use in
compacted fill and backfill The laboratory swell tests
indicated that the on site soils have low swelling potential
Where the existing ground is steeper than 5 horizontal to
1 vertical 5 1 benching and keying are required to pro
perly bond the new fill to the Slope The filling operation
should start at the lowest point and continue up in level
compacted layers in accordance with the above structural
fill requirements For construction details refer to
Plate 2
Two horizontal to 1 vertical 2 1 fill slopes are recom
mended
It is our opinion that a shrinkage factor of about 10
would be suitable for the site grading design
Foundations
For the proposed light one story structures spread foot
ing foundations or Slab on grade may be used
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f on compacted fill
and 3500 p s f on the stiff natural ground can be used
Footings should be embedded a minimum of 12 inches below
the adjacent ground surface
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1015 00 P 7
If a footing is located next to a utility trench the
footing should extend to the bottom of the utility trench
or be designed to span over the trench to reduce footing
settlement due to settlement of the trench backfill
Slab on grade should be placed over a 4 inch layer of base
course compacted to 95 of its maximum dry density The
thickened edge around the slab on grade should extend a
minimum of 8 inches below the adjacent ground surface
The slab subgrade soils should be kept moist and not be
allowed to dry out prior to concrete placement
The site should also be graded to drain water away from the
building foundations
Retaining Walls
Retaining walls up to about 10 feet high are planned
for the target storage areas
Well graded granular material should be used as backfill
material behind the walls Impervious material should be
used for the top 2 feet of the backfill
The recommendations given under FOUNDATIONS should be
followed for the design of the wall foundations

Percolation Testing
Falling head percolation tests were performed in 5 locations
of the proposed project site The percolation holes nos
PH 10 to PH 14 are shown on the Site Plan and the test data
are summarized in the appendix
The tests were performed by drilling shallow 2 to 5 feet
deep holes which were then saturated overnight The perco
lation tests consisted of partially filling the holes with
water then recording the water level drop with time
The recorded percolation rates varied from 3 4 to
inch
Based on the test results it is our opinion that the site
is suitable for leaching field disposal system

FIELD EXPLORATION

by drilling nine borings utilizing truck mounted equipment
The depths of all the borings were terminated at 19 to 21
feet below the existing ground surface at the approximate
locations shown on the enclosed Site Plan Plate 1
The soils were classified by visual and textural
examinations in the field by our engineer who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications
were verified by visual inspection and testing in the
laboratory All soils were classified in accordance with
the Unified Soil Classification System A graphic presen
tation of the soils encountered is presented on the Boring
Logs Plates A 1 through A 9
Soil samples were obtained by driving either a 2 4
inch I D ring sampler or a standard penetration sampler
with a 140 pound hammer free falling a distance of 30
inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the last
12 inches are shown on the Logs of Borings at the appro
priate sample depths
