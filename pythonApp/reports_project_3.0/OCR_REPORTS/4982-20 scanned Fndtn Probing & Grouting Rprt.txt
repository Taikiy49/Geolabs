This letter report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations during the
probing and grouting operations for the Ewa Pointe Marketplace project in the Ewa Beach
area from May 12 through 14 2003 The probing and grouting work was performed in
general accordance with our fee proposal dated
Project Considerations
The Ewa Pointe Marketplace site is a rectangular shaped parcel of approximately
2 4 acres in area located at the intersection of Fort Weaver Road and Kaimalie Street in
the Ewa Beach area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The property is bounded on the east
by Fort Weaver Road on the north by Kaimalie Street on the west by the Ocean Pointe
development and on the south by a vacant parcel and the Ewa Beach Shopping Center
Based on the information provided the proposed retail development will include a
two story L shaped main retail building and two individual building pads for future
restaurant development The new building will be surrounded by driveways and parking
areas to accommodate approximately 142 passenger vehicles We understand that the
main retail building will be constructed of CMU walls around the perimeter with steel
columns concrete slab on grade and metal roofing The design finished floor elevations
of the main building are set at about 21 5 feet Mean Sea Level MSL Based on the
existing topography we anticipate that fills on the order of about 2 to 4 feet thick will be
required to achieve the building pad elevation

Probing and Grouting Operations
The probing and grouting operations were performed in general conformance with
the recommendations presented in our report for the project dated
The purpose of the probing and grouting operation was to reduce the potential for loss of
foundation support resulting from collapse of cavities or compression of loose zones below
the main building
The probe hole locations were marked by Cove Development Inc prior to the
probing operation In general the probe holes were located at the center of the column
footings and spaced at approximately 10 foot intervals along the wall footings The
approximate locations of the probe holes are shown on the Probe Hole Location Plan
Plate 1 In general the probe holes at the new building were drilled to depths ranging from
about 11 to 16 feet below the existing ground surface corresponding to approximately
10 feet below the design footing bottom elevations
The probe holes about 4 inches in diameter were drilled by Geolabs using a
truck mounted drill rig Mobile B 80 equipped with continuous flight augers Following the
probing operation a 2 inch diameter PVC pipe was inserted into each of the probe holes
to maintain the integrity of the open hole and to reduce the potential for cave in prior to the
grouting operation A total of 104 probe holes including 4 additional probe holes drilled
during the grouting operation as discussed below with an aggregate length of about
1 250 lineal feet below the existing ground surface were drilled for the project
The probe holes were backfilled with Controlled Low Strength Material CLSM
supplied by Hawaiian Cement injected at low pressures The grouting work was
performed by American Standard Concrete Pumping Hawaii Inc and observed by a field
representative from Geolabs Grout quantities were estimated using the number of
strokes to fill each probe hole compared to the number of strokes to fill up a 5 gallon
bucket The grouting operation generally proceeded in the following manner
e Immediately prior to grouting of a particular hole the temporary PVC pipe
was removed A 2 inch diameter PVC grout pipe attached to the end of the
grouting hose was then inserted into the open hole and lowered to
approximately 2 feet from the bottom of the hole Grout injection then
commenced at the direction of our field representative who provided
direction as to when the injection was complete or whether additional
injection was required
Each probe hole was grouted from the desired depth upward by low
pressure injection of grout through the PVC grout pipe which was retracted
from the probe hole as the grout filled the hole Several injection passes
were needed to grout those probe holes where high grout take was
observed The grouting operation and the approximate grout take were
observed and recorded by our field representative

In general no significant voids and or cavities were observed during the probing
operation However one of the probe holes Probe Hole No 61 took in approximately
2 cubic yards of flowable fill during the grouting operation Therefore four additional probe
holes Probe Hole Nos 61A through 61D were drilled around it to evaluate the lateral
extent of the cavity No additional voids and or cavities were detected on the additional
probe holes drilled during the grouting operation Approximately 6 cubic yards of flowable
fill was utilized during the grouting operation to fill up all the probe holes and cavities
encountered The probing and grouting summary is provided on Plates 2 1 through 2 4
