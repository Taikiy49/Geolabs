In accordance with our proposal dated March 19 2001 we have conducted a
preliminary subsurface exploration in conjunction with the provision of geotechnical
engineering consultation services for the subject project site located in the Moloa a area on
the Island of Kauai Hawaii
This letter report summarizes the results of our preliminary field exploration at the
project site and presents our findings and preliminary recommendations for the project
These findings and discussions are intended for preliminary evaluation only and are
subject to the limitations noted at the end of this letter report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located at the former Meadow Gold Dairy facility in the Moloa a
area on the Island of Kauai Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site
are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 Based on the information provided we
understand that the site was utilized as a dairy and farm facility which has since ceased
its operations Following the closure of the facility some existing farm excavations and
other man made topographic features were graded in an effort to restore the site to the
pre existing conditions
Based on the information provided two shallow depth lagoons identified as
Lagoon Nos 1 and 2 bermed manure holding ponds were backfilled in an effort to
restore the site In addition another area identified as the Boneyard was also graded to
restore the site The general location of the lagoons and the Boneyard site is shown on
the Site Key Map Plate 2 1 The lagoons were located within the area identified as Area
C and the Boneyard is located within the area identified as Area D as approximately
shown on the Site Key Map Plate 2 1
We understand that some compaciion testing of the fill materials placed was
performed during the grading operation However it is suspected that some of the grading
at the site was not properly performed and the fills placed may be of unsatisfactory quality
for future development of the site Therefore a preliminary site evaluation was conducted
to explore the subsurface soil conditions at selected locations of the project site

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our geotechnical engineering consultation services was to obtain a
general overview of the surface and shallow subsurface conditions at the site The
subsurface information obtained was utilized to qualitatively evaluate the quality of the
earth fills placed and degree of compaction performed during restoration of the site To
accomplish this we conducted an exploration program consisting of the following tasks
and work efforts
1
Performance of a general site reconnaissance of selected areas at the
former dairy site by a project geologist and principal engineer from our firm
Excavation of five test pits extending to depths of about 10 to 14 feet below
the existing ground surface The backhoe and operator were provided for
our use by the client
Collection of a bulk soil sample and several grab soil samples from selected
depths of the test pits
Laboratory testing of the samples obtained during the field exploration as an
aid in classifying the materials encountered and evaluating their engineering
properties
Analyses of the field and laboratory data and the presentation of our
preliminary geotechnical engineering findings pertaining to the general
quality of the backfill observed at the project site
Preparation of this letter report summarizing our work on the project and
presenting our findings and preliminary recommendations
Coordination of our overall work on the project by a geologist from our firm
Quality assurance of our work and client design team consultation by a
principal engineer from our firm
Miscellaneous work efforts such as drafting word processing and clerical
support

EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
As mentioned previously the project site is located at the former Meadow Gold
Dairy in the Moloa a area of the Island of Kauai Hawaii The former dairy site is located
southerly of Kuhio Highway The general location of the former dairy site is shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1
The project site is located on relatively flat to gently sloping terrain situated at the
base of the hills that are located toward the south of the site The existing Papaa Stream
traverses the southern portion of the project site Based on our site reconnaissance it
appears that the site is generally underlain by native stiff reddish brown clayey and silty
residual soils that grade with increasing depth to mottled colored saprolitic soil highly to
extremely weathered basalt rock formation
FIELD EXPLORATION
Based on the information provided ihe general locations of land where more
extensive site restoration consisting of backfilling and grading operations were conducted
are located at Area C and Area D as shown on the Site Key Map Plate 2 1 We
understand that the Area C site contains two backfilled ponds that were used as manure
storage pits The approximate location of the backfilled pits is shown on the Area C Site
Plan Plate 2 2 The Area D site consists of the Boneyard site where some cut and fill
grading of the existing slopes was conducted The approximate location of the graded
area is shown on the Area D Site Plan Plate 2 3
In addition to the locations mentioned previously some localized pits and trenches
associated with the former dairy facility were reportedly backfilled within Area A and
Area B at the project site The general locations of Area A and Area B are shown on
the Site Key Map Plate 2 1
Our field exploration program consisted of the excavation and documentation of five
test pits excavated at selected locations using an EX270LC backhoe excavator The client
assisted in the selection of the test pit locations on the basis of his
knowledge of the past land use and construction restoration activity that had occurred at
the site Our geologist who monitored the test pit excavation work on a near continuous
basis logged the test pits A pocket penetrometer was utilized to gauge the consistency
hardness of the soils encountered in the sidewalls of the test pits The penetrometer was
utilized to qualitatively evaluate the compaction of the soils encountered by the test pit
excavation In addition some soil samples were collected from Test Pit Nos 1 and 2 for
laboratory testing and engineering classification purposes
Two test pits identified as Test Pit Nos 1 and 2 were excavated at the backfilled
Lagoon No 1 site The test pits at the lagoon site were excavated to evaluate
qualitatively the compaction of the existing backfill Three test pits identified as Test Pit
Nos 3 through 5 were excavated at Area D where it was suspected that undocumented

LABORATORY TESTING
Selected soil samples from the preliminary field exploration were tested in the
laboratory with an emphasis on evaluating the moisture and compaction characteristics
The following laboratory tests were performed on selected soil samples obtained from the
field exploration
Moisture content ASTM D 2216 determinations were performed on selected
samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties The test results
are presented on the Logs of Test Pits at the corresponding sample depths
One laboratory modified Proctor Test ASTM D 1557 was performed on a bulk
sample of the near surface backfilled soils to evaluate the moisture density relationship of
the soils The test results are presented on Plate 4
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our field exploration Test Pit Nos 1 and 2 conducted at the Area C Lagoon No
1 site generally encountered approximately 12 to 14 feet of medium stiff to very stiff
clayey silt fill soil materials overlying very stiff native clayey soils Traces of decomposed
manure were encountered at test pit depths that correspond approximately with the bottom
elevations of the backfilled lagoon Based on our observations and the penetrometer
testing of Test Pit Nos 1 and 2 it appears that the former lagoons were backfilled with soil
materials that had received some compaction effort for restoration of the site
Several attempts were made to locate the potential earth fills associated with the
backfilling of Lagoon No 2 at the project site Several shallow probe holes were
excavated in the area believed to contain Lagoon No 2 However stiff to very stiff soils
that appear to represent undisturbed native soils were encountered and the probe holes
were discontinued
Our field exploration conducted at the Area D Boneyard site generally
encountered fill soils and alluvium extending to the maximum depth explored of about 12
feet below the existing ground surface In general Test Pit Nos 3 and 5 encountered soft

FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the results of our limited field exploration we believe that the backfilled
lagoons located at Area C appear to have been backfilled with fill soit materials that
have been compacted during the placement of the materials The backfill material
appears io have received some compaction effort based on the results of the
penetrometer readings obtained from the sidewalls of the test pits In general
penetrometer readings of less than about 1 5 to 2 tons per square foot tsf for the moist
soils encountered would indicate that the compaction effort of the fill materials was less
than desirable based on a qualitative evaluation However most penetrometer readings
from Test Pit Nos 1 and 2 were in excess of 2 tsf which indicate that the fill soils were
placed with some amount of compactive effort It should be noted that additional
exploration consisting of drilling borings and further evaluation would be required to
evaluate the site conditions with respect to the proposed future development
Based on the field exploration conducted at Area D it appears that unsuitable
organic materials animal carcass waste and vegetation were buried and covered with fill
soils that received less than desirable compactive effort for the site restoration The silty
and clayey fills encountered in Test Pit Nos 3 and 5 were soft based on visual
observation of the excavation process and the instability of the sidewalls of the trenches
Strong odors and observation of decayed animal flesh and bones were encountered
therefore entry into the test pits for in situ pocket penetrometer testing was not conducted
due to health concerns
Additional field exploration test probe holes conducted at widely scattered
locations within Areas A B and C was generally inconclusive in locating potentially
unsuitable fill materials associated with the site restoration work

Based on our findings it appears that there is evidence that some undocumented
and poor quality earthwork associated with the use of unsuitable fill materials has
occurred in the vicinity of Area D In addition the fill materials encountered in the
vicinity of Area D appear to have been placed with less than desirable compactive effort
Therefore consideration should be given to the performance of additional subsurface
exploration and documentation of the extent of the poor quality fills encountered at Area
D In addition the suspected poor quality fill materials encountered at Area D casts
further suspicion on the potential for the presence of similar poor quality fill materiais at
other portions of the site Therefore a relatively extensive geotechnical engineering
exploration effort should be conducted at the site to further evaluate the site conditions
with respect to future development of the site
LIMITATIONS
The findings and discussions submitied in this letter report are based in part upon
information obtained from site reconnaissance and limited field test pits only Variations of
subsurface conditions between and beyond the test pits may occur and the nature and
extent of these variations may not become evident until further exploration or consiruction
is underway If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
findings and discussions provided in this letter report
The locations of the test pits indicated in this report are approximate having been
estimated from the features shown on the drawings provided by Jeffrey S Lindner on
March 15 2001 The approximate elevations of the test pits were estimated from the
contour lines shown on the drawings provided by Jeffrey S Lindner on March 15 2001
The physical locations and elevations of the test pits should be considered accurate only
to the degree implied by the methods used
The stratification breaks presented on the Logs of Test Pits depict the approximate
boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual transition As
mentioned previously groundwater was not encountered in the test pits at the time of our
exploration However it must be noted that groundwater level changes may occur due to
seasonal precipitation surface runoff and other factors
This letter report has been prepared for the exclusive use of Jeffrey S Lindner for
specific application to the preliminary evaluation of the site restoration at the former
Meadow Gold Dairy in the Moloa a area in accordance with generally accepted
geotechnical engineering principles and practices No warranty is expressed or implied
The owner client should be aware that unanticipated soil conditions are commonly
encountered Unforeseen soil conditions such as perched groundwater soft deposits
hard layers or loose fills may occur in localized areas and may require additional probing
or corrections in the field which may result in construction delays to attain a properly
constructed project Therefore a sufficient contingency fund is recommended to
accommodate these possible extra costs
