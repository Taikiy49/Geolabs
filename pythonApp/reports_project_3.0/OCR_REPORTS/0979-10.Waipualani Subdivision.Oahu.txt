INTRODUCTION
The purpose of the soil investigation was to evaluate the
subsurface soil conditions at the site to provide date for
grading design and construction of the proposed subdivision
The report summarizes our findings and recommendations
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project development will consist of clearing and grading
the site for 67 single family residential lots with a 44
foot wide roadway system into the site Cut and fills
generally less than 10 to 20 feet are planned on the
project site Two retaining walls about 6 feet high are
planned at the western end of the proposed fill slope
The wall will be situated on a small terrace in the rela
tively steep slope between the upper flat area and the
lower stream
SITE CONDITIONS
The site of the development is located roughly between
Ahuimanu Road Apau Loop and Hoopala Street The site
is weeded heavily treed relatively flat criss crossed
with dirt access roads
In the southern portion of the project site are four houses
one garage and a large wooden hot house Existing usable
water lines are below the surface throughout the project
Site Steep slopes are in the north and eastern portions
of the project site

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
In general the field exploration disclosed relatively com
petent bearing soils at the site Shallow foundation bearing
on the undisturbed in situ clayey silt will provide adequate
Support for the proposed light residential structures and
low retaining walls for this subdivision

W O 4
Some fill settlement is anticipated in the high fill area
along the western boundary adjacent to Boring No 8
Settlement gauges should be placed at the approximate
locations indicated on the Site Plan Plate 1 to monitor
the rate and amount of consolidation It is anticipated
that the majority of the fill settlement could occur with
in the grading period
Laboratory tests on the on site soils indicate high in situ
moisture contents in the range of low to moderate expansion
potential Therefore Slab on grade can be designed without
special provisions for expansive soil conditions
The site should be cleared of all structures water lines
trees weeds and other deleterious material In areas to
receive fill the ground should be first scarified to a
minimum depth of 6 inches brought to near optimum moisture
content and then recompacted to a minimum of 85 density
Since the project site is located in a high rainfall area
the on site subsoils have in place moisture contents from
40 to 70 percent The optimum moisture content is about
33 percent Therefore some amount of drying of the exca
vated material will be required before using them as fill
material in the embankment

W P 5
Because of the high in situ moisture contents of the on
site borrow material and the site being located in a
high rainfall area it is our opinion that the normal
90 compaction standard used in other drier areas should
be modified to 85 for this project
Laboratory shear strength tests on compacted samples
indicate that 85 compaction will be adequate for the
roadway embankment fill and light building loads antici
pated
Fill_Placement
The on site cut material are suitable for use in compacted
fill After site preparation the fill should be placed
in about 10 inch lifts compacted to a minimum of 85 of
its maximum dry density established by ASTM Test Designa
tion D 1557 70 modified to compact the soil sample at a
wet to dry molding moisture condition
It is recommended that if additional off site borrow is
required it should be tested and approved by a soils
engineer prior to transportation to the site to assure
that it is suitable for the proposed construction The
material should generally be non expansive and should
not contain large percent of boulders organic or dele
terious matter

Slopes
In general two horizontal to one vertical 2 1 or flatter
cut and fill slopes are recommended for this project
To reduce the amount of run off over the Slopes a drainage
bench should be provided for 2H 1V Slopes having a vertical
height greater than 20 feet For Slope flatter than 3H 1V
the above recommended drainage bench is not required
To minimize erosion all slopes should be planted as soon
as practicable
Foundations
The proposed light residential structures may be supported
by conventional spread or continuous foundations
The post footings on relatively level area should extend a
minimum of 12 inches below the finish grades Foundation
located close to the top of slopes should be embedded a
sufficient depth to obtain a 6 foot minimum horizontal
set back distance from the Slope face

The spread footing foundations can be designed using
the following bearing values
Very Stiff Natural Soil up to 3000 p s f
Compacted Fill Over
Stiff Ground up to 2000 p s f
The bearing values for compacted fill are based on the
assumption that 85 relative compaction can be achieved
The above values of bearing capacity are for total of
dead plus live loads and may be increased by 33 when
temporary lateral loads wind and seismic are considered
Floor slabs should be separated from the foundation
Retaining Wall
Long continuous walls about 4 to 6 feet maximum in height
are proposed for the project see Plate 2 Cross Section
The retaining wall backfill should be well graded granular
materials such as base course rock Weepholes or a sub
surface drainage system should be used to minimize build
up of hydrostatic pressure behind the walls
The proposed retaining walls should be designed to resist
the following lateral earth pressures
Walls unrestrained at the top 40 p c f equivalent
fluid pressure
Walls with 2 1 fill slopes in 60 p c f equivalent
the back fluid pressure

Slab On Grade
It is recommended that Slab on grade be placed over a
4 inch cushion layer of capillary break such as 3 Fine
gravel
The subgrade should be kept moist and protected from drying
prior to concrete placement Subgrade should be compacted
to a minimum of 90 of its dry density
Slab on grade should be separated from foundations
In order to protect against moisture damage of the flooring
material a polyethylene moisture barrier should be incor
porated within the floor slabs
When the slab is located below the outside ground surface
a subdrain system around the perimeter of the wall is recom
mended to relieve seepage water

Roadway Pavement
The proposed access roads will be located both in cut and
fill It has been assumed that fill materials will be
obtained from the project cut areas
Based on the results of our laboratory CBR tests on the
on site material the preliminary pavement design is as
follows
2 inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 inch Base Course
10 inch Select Borrow
Additional CBR tests should be performed on the actual
subgrade soil samples during construction and the above
design section can be revised if necessary The recom
mended section considers medium traffic and that good
drainage will be provided adjacent to paved areas

and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the
findings of our subsurface investigation
