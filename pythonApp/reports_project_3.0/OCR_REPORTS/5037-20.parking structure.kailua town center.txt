Dear Mr Heath
In accordance with the project design requirements a test pile program was
undertaken for the Parking Structure project at the Kailua Town Center between
November 16 and The test pile program included driving 20 test piles
and performing a static compressive pile load test on a selected test pile This report
presents the results of our wave equation analyses field observations during the test pile
program and recommendations for driving of the production piles Results of the static
compressive pile load test have been submitted in a separate report
Based on the results of the test pile program we recommend that the new
estimated pile lengths presented herein with a revised allowable compressive load
capacity be used for the production piles for the project The results of the static
compressive pile load test indicate that the load test pile driven was able to achieve the
design pile capacity 170 kips with a factor of safety of 1 6 with settlement characteristics
compatible with the design of the project In order to achieve a higher factor of safety of
2 0 as required by the Uniform Building Code without significantly increasing the
construction costs we recommend that longer piles about 100 feet below the ground
surface with a higher compressive load capacity 220 kips be used for the production
piles
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The parking structure project is located in the Kailua Town Center in the Kailua
area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The new parking structure will have plan
dimensions of approximately 150 to 240 feet wide by about 272 feet long The new
parking structure will be either 3 or 4 stories in height Ramps will be constructed at the
central portion of the parking structure to allow vehicles to traverse up and down the
various parking levels

Due to the relatively high column loads we recommended that a pile foundation
system consisting of driving 16 5 inch octagonal precast prestressed concrete piles be
implemented for support of the parking structure Based on the results of the static
compressive pile load test the load test pile did not achieve the design capacity with a
factor of safety of 2 0 based on a length of about 85 feet below the ground level
Therefore the pile foundation was re designed by increasing the pile length from 85 to
100 feet below the ground level with a revised allowable compressive load capacity of
220 kips instead of the originally recommended 170 kips Based on the higher
allowable compressive load capacity of 220 kips the total number of the piles for the
pile foundation was reduced from 198 to 158 The revised Foundation Plan is
presented on Plate 1
Based on the information provided we understand that the existing ground
elevation is at about 9 feet Mean Sea level MSL The pile foundation cut off
elevation ranges from approximately Elevation 5 to 8 feet MSL We also understand
that a strand extension of up to 24 inches above the pile cut off elevation is required
WAVE EQUATION ANALYSES
Wave equation analyses were performed prior to the test pile driving based on
the pile driving equipment proposed for the project In addition refined wave equation
driveability analyses were performed after the static compressive pile load test The
wave equation analyses were conducted based on the pile and hammer information
provided by Healy Tibbitts Builders Inc dated and the subsurface
conditions contained in our geotechnical engineering exploration report for the project
dated August 1 2003
Subsurface Conditions
The borings drilled indicate that the project site generally was underlain by 3 to
6 inches of asphaltic concrete pavements overlying by fills extending to depths of about
2 to 3 feet below the existing ground surface The fills were underlain by beach
deposits extending to depths of about 15 to 17 feet below the existing ground surface
The beach deposits encountered in the borings consisted of coralline sands and gravel
with relative densities ranging from loose to medium dense
Below the beach deposits lagoonal deposits were encountered extending to
depths of about 25 to 26 feet below the existing ground surface The lagoonal deposits
consisted of very loose silty coralline gravel and very soft silty clays Coralline detritus
materials consisting of medium dense sands cobbles and boulders were encountered
below the lagoonal deposits extending to depths of about 60 to 70 feet below the
existing ground surface It should be noted that occasional medium hard zones of coral
formation were encountered within the coralline detritus in the borings In addition the
characteristics of the coralline detritus thickness and relative hardness are highly
erratic and quite unpredictable in short distances

The coralline detritus materials were underlain by alluvium extending to the

The alluvium encountered in the borings generally consisted of silty clays with

to stiff to very stiff at about 80 to 90 feet below the existing ground surface
Hammer and Driving System
A hydraulic impact hammer was proposed for the pile driving
operations by Healy Tibbitts Builders Inc Based on the pile driving equipment data
form provided the hammer has a ram weight of 11 kips and a
maximum stroke of 4 feet The maximum rated energy of the hydraulic impact hammer
is about 44 kip feet when set at the 4 foot stroke
The hammer cushion consists of Monocast with a total thickness of 8 inches
Based on the subsurface conditions and the driving conditions anticipated a pile
cushion consisting of 6 inches of plywood cushion blocks was proposed for the pile
driving operations We understand that the Monocast hammer cushion and plywood
pile cushion blocks have cross sectional areas of approximately 445 and 324 square
inches respectively
Pile Details
The specified piles generally consisted of 16 5 inch octagonal precast
prestressed concrete piles with lengths ranging from about 90 to 100 feet for the test
pile program The piles were cast and driven in two segments consisting of a
and spliced with 6 foot long non mechanical splice cans
We understand that the piles will be cast from concrete with a minimum 28 day
compressive strength of 6 000 pounds per square inch psi In addition we understand
that the effective prestress level of the prestressed concrete pile is approximately
899 psi Based on this information the maximum allowable compressive stress in the
precast prestressed concrete pile during pile driving should be about 4 200 psi or less
The maximum allowable tensile stress developed in a precast prestressed concrete pile
during pile driving should be approximately 918 psi or less

Based on our wave equation analyses it appears that the proposed pile driving
equipment hammer is capable of driving the specified pile sections to
the desired ultimate pile capacity of 440 kips by using the proper hammer stroke Our
refined wave equation driveability analyses were performed based on the test pile driving
records re strike records and results of the instrumented static pile load test Based on
our analyses we believe that 25 percent of the gain loss ratio should be used in the
driveability analyses to simulate the effect of soil freeze The results of the refined
driveability analyses indicate that driving resistance for the 16 5 inch octagonal pile based
on 100 feet long needed to attain an ultimate pile capacity of 440 kips should be about
16 blows per foot based on a 2 foot hammer stroke
The computed maximum compressive and tensile stresses in the pile during pile
based on hammer stroke of 2 foot and an ultimate pile capacity of 440 kips These
maximum compressive and tensile stresses induced in the pile due to the pile driving
operations are less than the allowable compressive and tensile stresses specified for this
project Based on the above results we recommend that a 2 foot hammer stroke be used
for driving the 16 5 inch octagonal piles

PRODUCTION PILE LENGTH AND DRIVING CRITERIA
Based on our observations during the test pile program we believe that the
driving equipment and methods of installation are acceptable for installing the specified
production piles in general accordance with the project design requirements In order to
facilitate the pile driving operations through the dense coralline detritus consisting of
medium dense sands cobbles and or boulders we recommend that predrilling with a
minimum 15 inch diameter maximum 18 inch diameter auger be conducted at the
production pile locations The predrilling should extend to depths of about 40 feet below
the existing ground surface
Production Pile Lengths
Based on our analyses of the test pile driving behavior the penetration
resistance and the static load test results we recommend that the 100 foot long
production piles consist of two 50 foot long pile segments spliced with a non mechanical
splice can be used for the project The production pile lengths provided are based on a
cut off elevation of about 6 feet MSL with up to 24 inches of strand extension above
the pile cut off elevation
Pile Driving Criteria
In general the hydraulic impact hammer with a 2 foot hammer
stroke should be used for the production pile installation Based on our engineering
analyses and the test pile program conducted for the project the following pile driving
criteria may be used during the production pile driving operations
1 The pile driving operation may be terminated when the pile driving
resistance meets the following blow counts at the specified depths and
continue to increase to 13 blows per foot bpf at a tip elevation of about
91 feet MSL 100 feet below the ground level
e Average blow count of about 5 bpf from depths 75 to 80 feet
e Average blow count about 6 bpf from depths 80 to 85 feet
e Average blow count about 8 bpf from depths 85 to 90 feet
e Average blow count about 11 bpf from depths 90 to 95 feet
2 The pile driving operation may be terminated when the pile tip elevation
reaches at least 91 feet MSL and the blow counts reach 16 blows per foot
for the last foot of penetration
3 The pile driving operation may be terminated when the top of the pile is
driven to an elevation equal to the cut off elevation plus the 24 inch strand
extension when requested by the field representative from Geolabs

It should be noted that the pile driving criteria may need to be modified during the

for more than 4 hours and the pile cannot be driven to the required depth and or

the hammer stroke or energy should be carefully controlled such that significant tensile
