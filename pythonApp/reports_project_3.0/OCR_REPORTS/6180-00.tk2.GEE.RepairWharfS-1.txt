PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is at Wharf S1 in Quarry Loch within the Pearl Harbor Naval
Station on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project scope generally includes removal of
the existing timber concrete plastic fender piles and the installation of a concrete fender
pile system The scope of the project also includes demolition and removal work
concrete rehabilitation of the existing curbs beams slabs and piles and other
incidental related work
We understand the new concrete fender piles will consist of approximately
159 24 inch square precast prestressed concrete piles Based on the information
provided in the Request for Proposal RFP documents the concrete fender piles will
need to extend from the top of pile at approximately Elevation 108 feet to tip elevations
of 48 feet 60 foot long piles from Bent 1 to Bent 42 and 20 feet 88 foot long piles

The existing Wharf S1 is approximately 1 061 linear feet long by 46 feet wide The

understand the subsurface conditions along the wharf face for the fender pile design

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
We explored the subsurface conditions by drilling and sampling six test borings
designated as Boring Nos 101 through 106 extending to depths of about 75 to 127 feet
below the existing wharf deck The approximate boring locations are shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2
In general the area between about Bent 1 and Bent 56 about 550 linear feet along
the wharf is underlain by relatively thin soft harbor deposits about 2 to 7 feet thick
overlying dense tuff formation extending to the maximum depth explored of approximately
85 feet below the existing concrete wharf deck corresponding to about Elevation 23 feet
The dense tuff formation encountered varied from at least 25 to 35 feet thick based on the
maximum depths explored by the borings drilled along the wharf deck
The area between about Bent 56 and Bent 66 about 100 linear feet along the
wharf is underlain by relatively thin soft harbor deposits about 4 feet thick overlying a
relatively thin layer of dense tuff formation of approximately 10 feet thick The thin tuff
formation was underlain by very stiff to hard alluvium extending to the maximum depth
drilled of approximately 106 8 feet below the existing concrete wharf deck corresponding
to about Elevation 1 2 feet
The subsurface conditions between Bent 66 and Bent 105 about 390 linear feet
along the wharf generally consist of soft harbor deposits and loose lagoonal deposits
varying from about 20 to 66 feet thick overlying stiff to very stiff alluvium The stiff to very
stiff alluvium extended to the maximum depth drilled of about 127 3 feet below the existing

FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
We understand it is proposed to replace the existing timber concrete plastic pile
fender system with a 24 inch square precast prestressed concrete fender pile system In
general the concrete fender pile system will absorb the energy imparted by the berthing
vessels Therefore the lateral load capacity of the 24 inch square precast prestressed
concrete fender piles is the main design consideration for the fender pile system
Based on the information provided the lateral berthing load imposed on a single
fender pile at each hard point location is on the order of approximately 30 kips acting at
about 10 5 feet below the water surface corresponding to about Elevation 89 5 feet For
modeling purposes we used a distributed load of 30 kips applied between about 7 5 and
29 5 feet below the top of the fender pile corresponding to about Elevation 100 5 and
78 5 feet in our lateral load analyses of the fender piles Please note that the top of
fender pile is at approximately Elevation 108 feet
For the fender piles located between the hard point locations the design fender pile
lateral load of about 30 kips was modeled to act at about the water surface and distributed
only along 1 foot of the pile corresponding to about Elevation 100 5 and 99 5 feet in
our lateral load analyses of the fender piles
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered at the project site and the
structural loading provided we believe six different pile tip elevations likely will be required
to provide the necessary lateral load capacity and bearing resistance for the
self supporting fender piles Based on our engineering analyses we recommend the
fender piles extend to the minimum tip elevations presented in the following table For
ease of reference we included the pile tip elevations specified in the RFP documents and

The lateral load analyses were performed using the program LPILE plus for
Windows which is a microcomputer adaptation of a finite difference laterally loaded pile
program originally developed at the University of Texas at Austin The program solves for
deflection and bending moment along a pile under lateral loads as a function of depth The
analysis was carried out with the use of non linear p y curves to represent soil moduli
The lateral deflection was then computed using the appropriate soil moduli at various
depths
In general we assumed the boundary condition near the top of the fender pile at
about Elevation 108 feet is pinned free to rotate with zero lateral deflection In addition
we used a mudline elevation of about 65 feet resulting in a free standing pile length of
about 43 feet The following table summarizes the results of our lateral load analyses for

Predrilling
We also recommend predrilling the pile locations to near the new pile tip elevations
to clear the pile driving path from obstructions and to reduce the need for hard driving
conditions in order to install the fender piles to the new tip elevations where the tuff
formation is present within the depths of the fender piles Due to the presence of dense tuff
formation within the pile depth we recommend predrilling to the new pile tip elevations
utilizing a 34 inch diameter auger for Pile Zones A and B Bent 1 to Bent 66
Due to the presence of soft to medium stiff soil conditions along the pile shaft from
Bent 66 to Bent 106 and beyond we recommend reducing the predrilling depth to
approximately Elevation 45 feet about 20 feet below the mudline to clear the path where

Test Pile Program
We understand a test pile program consisting of pile settlement monitoring is
stipulated in the RFP documents for the project We envision the intent of the test pile
program is to monitor potential creep movement of the installed pile under its own weight
as the driven pile will be terminating within the soft harbor deposit based on a proposed
pile length of 88 feet
Based on the recommended pile lengths presented herein the installed piles will
extend through the soft harbor deposits and terminate at or within the stiff alluvial soils
Therefore we believe the test pile program required in the RFP document would not be
necessary and may be omitted for the project Eliminating the test pile program may be
considered a variance from the requirements contained in the RFP documents It should
be noted that the recommended pile tip elevations will be confirmed and or refined by the
pile probing program to be conducted for the project
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted herein are based in part upon
information obtained from the field borings Variations of the subsurface conditions
between and beyond the field data points may occur and the nature and extent of these
variations may not become evident until additional probing or construction is underway If
variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations
provided in herein
The field boring locations indicated herein are approximate having been taped from
features shown on the Partial Above Deck Demo Plans transmitted by Moffatt Nichol on
Elevation of the existing wharf deck shown on the Logs of Borings was
based on the information contained in the RFP documents The physical locations and
elevations of the borings should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the
methods used
The stratification lines shown on the graphic representations of the borings depict
the approximate boundaries between soil and or rock types and as such may denote a
gradual transition Water level data from the borings were measured at the times shown
on the graphic representations and or presented in the text herein These data have been
reviewed and interpretations made in the formulation of this report However it must be
noted that fluctuation may occur due to variation in tides rainfall storm surge condition

Field Exploration
Our field exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling six test borings
designated as Boring Nos 101 through 106 extending to depths of about 75 to 127 feet
below the existing wharf deck The six borings were drilled to augment the three existing
borings relevant borings only contained in the contract documents The approximate
boring locations are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 We drilled the borings using a
truck mounted drill rig equipped with solid stem augers and coring tools
Our geologist classified the materials encountered in the borings by visual and
textural examination in the field in general accordance with ASTM D 2488 Standard
Practice for Description and Identification of Soils and monitored the drilling operations on
a near continuous full time basis These classifications were further reviewed visually
and by testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general accordance with
ASTM D 2487 Standard Practice for Classification of Soils for Engineering Purposes
Unified Soil Classification System as shown on the Soil Log Legend Plate A 0 1
Graphic representations of the materials encountered and other observations such as the
depth of mudline etc are presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 6
Soil samples were obtained from the borings drilled in general accordance with
ASTM D 1586 Standard Test Method for Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of
Soils by driving a 2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same hammer and
drop The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an
18 inch drive are shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Borings at the
appropriate sample depths
Pocket penetrometer tests were performed on selected cohesive soil samples
retrieved in the field The pocket penetrometer test provides an indication of the
unconfined compressive strength of the soil sample Pocket penetrometer test results are
presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Core samples of the rock materials encountered at the project site were obtained
by using diamond core drilling techniques in general accordance with ASTM D 2113
Diamond Core Drilling for Site Investigation Core drilling is a rotary drilling method that
uses a hollow bit to cut into the rock formation The rock material left in the hollow core of
the bit is mechanically recovered for examination and description Rock cores were
described in general accordance with the Rock Description System as shown on the
Rock Log Legend Plate A 0 2 The Rock Description System is based on the publication
Suggested Methods for the Quantitative Description of Discontinuities in Rock Masses by
International Society for Rock Mechanics March 1977

The Rock Quality Designation RQD is also a subjective guide to the relative
quality of rock masses RQD is defined as the percentage of the core run that is sound
material in excess of 4 inches in length without any discontinuities discounting any drilling
mechanical and handling induced fractures or breaks If 2 5 feet of sound material is
recovered from a 5 0 foot core run the RQD would be 50 percent and would be shown on
the Logs of Borings as RQD 50 Generally the following is used to describe the
relative quality of the rock based on the Practical Handbook of Physical Properties of
Rocks and Minerals by Robert S Carmichael 1989

Laboratory Tests
Moisture Content ASTM D 2216 and Unit Weight ASTM D 2937
determinations were performed on selected samples as an aid in the classification and
evaluation of soil properties The test results are presented on the Logs of Borings at
the appropriate sample depths
Ten Atterberg Limits tests ASTM D 4318 were performed on selected soil
samples to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits The test results are summarized on the
Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths Graphic presentation of test results is
provided on Plate B 1
Six Sieve Analysis tests ASTM C 117 C 136 were performed on selected soil
samples to evaluate the gradation characteristics of the soils and to aid in soil
classification Graphic presentations of the grain size distribution are provided on
Plates B 2 and B 3
Eleven Unconfined Compression tests ASTM D 7012 Method C were performed
on selected rock cores to evaluate their unconfined compressive strength of the rock
formation encountered Unconfined compression test results are presented on Plate B 4
