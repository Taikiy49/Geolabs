The soil exploration of the project site by Soils International disclosed that much of
the site is underlain by expansive clay soils The expansiveness of the on site soils has
been confirmed in the field during the mass grading operations
The grading concept for the development should be to cap over these expansive
soils with sufficient good low expansive soils to reduce the shrink swell effects of the
expansive clay soils on the future house lots The amount of good capping soils
necessary to achieve this depends on the actual expansiveness of the clay soils For
highly expansive clay a capping thickness of 3 feet is usually sufficient to limit
shrink swell movements at the surface For moderately expansive soils a capping
thickness of 1 foot may be sufficient
Based on the swell test results to date we have reviewed the grading plan to
evaluate whether the lots contain sufficient thickness of capping soils Our review
indicates that some of the lots may not contain sufficient capping thickness for the entire
lot Also some of the cut lots may not encounter rock but expose expansive soil
conditions One alternative to remedy this situation is to over excavate the lots prior to
filling to provide the recommended capping soil thickness We feel that this would be less
expensive than designing and constructing the house slab to resist the swelling of the
soils i e post tensioned slab or over excavation and replacement after mass grading is
complete A detailed summary of our findings is presented below

The existing 30 to 40 foot high cut slopes have a slope ratio of 2H 1V with 8 foot
wide benches at a slope height of 15 feet Although the rock material encountered is
moderately hard to very hard this rock material is also highly fractured Loose rock
fragments are present in the rock face It is our opinion that ravelling of small rock pieces
may occur
According to studies by the National Highway Institute NHI a subsidiary of the
US Department of Transportation Federal Highway Administration FHWA the following
types of rock movements will occur at varying slope inclinations
e At a slope inclination of 72H 1V rocks tend to bounce off the slope face
e At a slope inclination of 1H 1V rocks tend to ro l along the slope face

The NHI has also found through experience that benches sometimes fill up with
ravelling material and become launching pads to project rocks out The construction of
benches for tall and steep slopes has been removed from current FHWA guidelines and
specifications Current City and County of Honolulu and State of Hawaii Standard
Specifications have not yet removed the bench requirement
At the present condition small rock pieces may bounce along the slope face and
launch off the bench The five options to reduce the rockfall hazard are

Ds Flatten the entire slope back to 1H 1V

Based on the general filling procedure many of the fill areas contain about 2 to
3 feet of clayey material underlain by silty cobble material and the underlying natural
soil material Generally the typical cut areas consisted of a thin soil layer of about 2 to
6 feet in thickness over cinder rock or basaltic rock material
To evaluate the expansion potential of fill and cut materials within the upper
24 inches from the finish subgrade expansion tests were performed on soil material
taken at the finish subgrade and at 12 inches below the finish subgrade Attached is a
summary of the expansion tests performed throughout the Kapolei Knolls Phase 1
Subdivision
To reduce the potential of structural distress resulting from the swelling of the
subgrade soils we recommend the following moistening procedure for all lots Note that
the following procedure should be considered preliminary and is subject to change
based on field conditions
1 For a period of 1 week prior to house construction the lot should be
intermittently moistened A sprinkler or intermittent watering from a water
truck would be suitable for this purpose The watering should be
temporarily stopped if excessive puddling is encountered
2 During construction of the slabs and foundations the slab subgrade
should be lightly moistened for 5 to 10 minutes at the end of each day
except for the day prior to termite treatment and subsequent days
thereafter
2006 Kalihi Street Honolulu Hawaii 96819

The purpose of soil moistening is to obtain a subgrade that is slightly moist to a
depth of 2 to 3 feet below the finish subgrade The objective is not to over saturate the
soil to the point of softening Intermittent watering is recommended to avoid softening of
the soil To keep the immediate surface dry enough for termite treatment the soil should
not be moistened on the day prior to termite treatment
Just as important it should be stressed to the homeowners that excessive
watering adjacent to the house structure is discouraged A sidewalk directly adjacent to
the house or a sloped grade adjacent to the house that would direct water away from the
house should be considered
For the lots listed below along with the moistening program the house and garage
perimeter footings should be deepened to 24 inches below the lowest adjacent grade In
addition the driveway slabs should be constructed with a minimum of 12 inch thickened
edges Concrete slabs including driveways should be reinforced with

Use of Import Material from Waikele
Kapolei Knolls Subdivision Phase 1A
Kapolei Oahu Hawaii
16 4
On red brown clayey silt import material from Waikele
was brought to the Kapolei Knolls Subdivision site and placed on Lot Nos 70 to 82 as
capping fill material at the upper 12 inches
An air dried ring swell test was performed on a sample of this material The
sample revealed a ring swell of 2 5 at an initial dry density of 101 p c f 96 relative
compaction an initial water content of 26 an air dried water content of 12 and a
final water content of 28 Based on these results the material may be used as capping
fill within the upper 12 inches
Based on our experience with Waikele the area generally contains low expansion
soils with some moderate to high expansion soils We request that prior to importing any
additional material from Waikele we be allowed to visit the borrow site and take samples
for ring swell tests at least 2 days prior to hauling If you have questions or need
additional information please contact our office
