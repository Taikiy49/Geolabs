SUMMARY OF RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that the site is
generally underlain by moist stiff gray clay
lagoonal deposit and moist mottled grayish
brown clayey silt alluvial deposit
Based on the soil conditions and anticipated
foundation loads we believe that spread and
continuous footing foundations may be used for
the proposed theatre project
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f and a
minimum footing embedment of 24 inches are
recommended Estimated footing settlements are
on the order of one half of an inch total and
one fourth of an inch differential

INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our soil engineering evaluation
performed for the proposed theatres development at Enchanted Lake Shopping
Center in Kailua Oahu Hawaii The general location of the site and its
vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map Plate i
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to renovate the interior of an existing building
located in the southwestern corner of the shopping center to create a
tri plex theatre The existing building with plan dimensions of
approximately 115 by 120 feet is situated adjacent to a Safeway store
Elevations of the site were not available at the time of our report
preparation

It is our understanding that the existing exterior walls roof and
interior slabs will be retained as much as possible Two 2 reinforced
concrete walls are planned inside the building to provide the divisions
for the proposed tri plex theatre Excavation through the existing slabs
is anticipated for installation of new walls and footings Estimated
column and wall loads up to 50 kips and 2 kips per foot length
respectively are anticipated
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to examine the subsurface
conditions at the site to provide soil data for the formulation of
recommendations pertinent to the foundation design of the proposed
building modifications
The scope of our work included drilling and sampling four 4 borings
to depths of 21 feet below the existing ground surface laboratory
testing engineering evaluation of the field and laboratory findings and
report preparation The results of our field exploration and laboratory
tests are presented in the appendices of this report
SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed project site is located in the Enchanted Lake Shopping
Center at Kailua Oahu Hawaii The site is occupied by the existing
building with commercial stores A Safeway store sits east of and
adjacent to the building The ground surface of the site outside the
existing building is generally level covered with asphaltic concrete
pavement and used for parking The actual ground surface elevations were
not available at the time of our exploration

Foundation Design
Based on the soil conditions and anticipated column loads we believe
that spread footing foundations may be used for the proposed theatres
project
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f and a minimm footing
embedment of 24 inches are recommended The bearing capacity value is
for total dead plus live loads only and may be increased by one third for
short duration wind or seismic loading Estimated footing settlements
under the design loadings are anticipated to be on the order of less than
differential
Excavation
It is our understanding that excavation through the existing concrete
slab is needed for the construction of new footing foundations
The bottom 6 inches of the individual footing excavations should be
scarified moisture conditioned and recompacted to 90 of ASTM D 1557 78
maximum dry density prior to concrete placement Any loose or soft spots
encountered in the footing excavations should be removed and replaced with
suitable imported non expansive material compacted to the above mentioned
standard
Our laboratory test results indicate that the underlying stiff gray
clay is moderately expansive if it is saturated from a dry condition our
field exploration and laboratory data indicate that the subsoil which is
currently covered by either the existing building or A C pavement is
already moist Therefore it is our opinion that the potential for
shrinkage swelling potential is therefore reduced However we believe
that it is important that the footing excavations be kept moist prior to
concrete placement to reduce drying which could lead to later swelling of
the subsoils

During our field exploration it was observed that an interior wall
footing trench which had already been excavated contained water
Discussions with the contractor s personnel indicated that the soil had
been moist when excavated and that no seepage had been noted It is most
likely that the water is originating from a nearby leaking utility line
We recommend that the water be kept out of the trench to reduce the
potential for saturation and subsequent softening of the footing
subgrade Therefore the contractor should be appraised of the need for
either finding and repairing the leak or dewatering the trench area
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comments
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the foundation recommendations given herein If this review is not made
the geotechnical engineer can not assume responsibility for
misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Observation
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the
foundations This is to observe compliance with the design concepts
specifications or recommendations and to allow design changes in the event
that subsurface conditions differ from that anticipated prior to start of
construction The recommendations given in this report are contingent
upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed soil conditions encountered during construction are
different from those assumed or considered in this report then
appropriate modifications to the design should be made

ULIMTTATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as
borings probes test pits and or observation wells Variations of
conditions between the field data points may occur and the nature and
extent of these variations may not become evident until construction If
variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations given in this report
The location of the field data points were approximately determined
by scaling taping from the Site Plan by R M Towill Corporation
Elevations of the data points were not available at the time of our
exploration The physical location and elevation of the field data
points should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the
method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross sections are based upon interpolation between field
data points and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
Water level data from the field data points was collected at the
times given on the graphic representations and or in the text of this
report This data has been reviewed and interpretations made in the
formulation of this report However it must be noted that fluctuation
may occur due to variations in rainfall tides temperature and other
factors
This report has been prepared solely for the purpose of assisting
the engineer in the preliminary design evaluation of the proposed
project Therefore this report may not contain sufficient data or the
