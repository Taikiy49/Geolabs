Dear Mr Caster
This report summarizes our earthwork observation and testing services performed
during the site grading operations foundation excavation pavement construction and
utility installation for the Island Pacific Academy Phase II project from
through August 3 2006
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located at the eastern corner of the intersection between Wakea
and Haumea Streets in the Kapolei area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii Generally the
parcel is situated to the south of Campbell Square Bank of Hawaii and Kapolei Library
Based on the information provided it is proposed to develop the remaining site for
the Phase II portion of the Island Pacific Academy development In general the project will
involve construction of a 3 story building and an asphalt concrete paved parking lot as
shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
EARTHWORK AND FOUNDATION PREPARATION
The general contractor for the Island Pacific Academy Phase Il project is
Kiewit Pacific Company The site grading operations foundation excavation pavement
construction and utility installation were performed by Jayar Construction Inc The site
grading consisted of demolition and clearing subgrade preparation footing excavation
placement and compaction of fills and backfills pavement construction and utility trench
excavation and backfill

Following the clearing and grubbing operations the existing ground was generally
over excavated where necessary to approximately 18 inches below the slab cushion
within the building limits to provide the recommended 18 inch thick non expansive select
granular fill layer The over excavated subgrades were moisture conditioned to at least
2 percent above the optimum moisture content and compacted to at least 90 percent of
the maximum dry density as determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified
Proctor
During placement of the non expansive select granular fill layer the fill materials
were generally placed in near level lifts brought to above the optimum moisture content
and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent of the maximum dry density determined in
accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor During the foundation excavation and
preparation the excavated footing bottom was generally recompacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative compaction
New sewer water and drain lines were installed to serve the new development In
general a bedding layer consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel was provided below
the piping and manholes for uniform support Free draining granular materials consisting
of No 3B Fine gravel was also used for the trench backfill up to about 12 inches above the
pipes to provide adequate support around the pipes and to reduce the compaction effort
on the initial trench backfill The trench backfill was placed in 8 inch level loose lifts and
mechanically compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction Where trenches
are below pavement areas the upper 3 feet of the trench backfill below the pavement
grade was compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
During the site grading operations samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test
results are summarized on Plate 2 of this report
Periodic field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations to
confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures and soil conditions
were noted A total of 70 field density tests including retests of failed areas were
performed using the nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM D 2922 and D 3017
Compaction tests results are summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 7 These tests were
performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills Test
locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey
stakes These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree
implied by the methods used Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the
specified minimum compaction was obtained and or approved by our field representative
based on visual observation and hand probing

LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork may
have been performed without our observation However the level of earthwork
observation and testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some of
the geotechnical risks to the project It should be recognized that there are variations in the
accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the
earthwork construction and materials
