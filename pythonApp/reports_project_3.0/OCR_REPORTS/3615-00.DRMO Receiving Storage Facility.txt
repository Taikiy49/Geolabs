INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the Existing Pavements at Area 1 DRMO Receiving Storage Facility
project located within the Barbers Point Naval Air Station on the island of Oahu Hawaii
The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location
Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering
recommendations resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering
analyses These recommendations are intended for the evaluation of the existing
pavement sections and the design of new pavements only The findings and
recommendations presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this
report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located within the Barbers Point Naval Air Station in the District
of Ewa on the island of Oahu Hawaii Based on the information provided the existing
pavements at Area 1 of the DRMO Receiving Storage Facility have undergone some
deterioration As part of the DRMO Receiving Storage Facility renovation project it is
desired to evaluate conditions of the existing pavements In general the main objectives
of our geotechnical engineering exploration include the following
1 Determine the thickness of the existing A C and base course materials
at selected locations
2 Evaluate the capacity of the existing A C pavement structure

3 Evaluate the suitability of the subgrade material to support a pavement
4 Determine whether asphalt concrete could be placed on the native material
without providing new base course material in the event that the existing
asphalt concrete is removed and replaced
5 Provide recommendations for the design of new pavements to support
HS 20 vehicle loading

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu is composed largely of the weathered remnants of two extinct
shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae forms the bulk of
the western third of the island while the younger shield Koolau forms the majority of the
eastern two thirds of the island It is believed that Waianae became extinct while Koolau
was still active and its eastern flank is partially buried below Koolau lavas in central
Oahu
During the Pleistocene Epoch sea levels fluctuated in response to the cycles of
continental glaciation As the glaciers grew and advanced less water was available to fill
the oceanic basins such that sea levels fell below the present stands of the sea When
the glaciers melted and receded an excess of water became available such that the sea
levels rose to above the present sea level
The higher sea level stands caused the formation of deltas and fans of
accumulated terrigenous sediments in the heads of old bays accumulated reef deposits
at correspondingly higher elevations and lagoonal marine sediments in the quiet waters
protected by fringing reefs The lower sea stands caused streams to carve valleys in the
sediments and reef deposits Subaerial exposure of the sediments and calcareous
materials caused consolidation of the soft deltaic materials and lagoonal deposits and
induration of the calcareous reef materials Land development and reclamation projects
within the last century have brought the project site to its present form

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field exploration the project site is underlain by silty coral sand and
gravel to the maximum depths drilled of approximately 5 5 to 11 5 feet below the existing
asphalt pavement Observations during our field exploration indicate that the existing
pavements have experienced some deterioration as evidenced by some cracks and small
potholes The base course material encountered in the borings was generally poor in
quality and appears to be a_ major cause of the observed pavement deterioration
However we believe that most of the existing pavement is in fair condition and may still
have several years of service life
Based on the information provided we understand that overlay of the existing
pavements is not feasible due to the existing building floor elevations Therefore if a
longer pavement life is desired we recommend that the existing pavement be removed
and replaced with new asphalt concrete pavement However if new pavements are
planned the existing poor quality base course material should be removed and repiaced
with new basaltic base course for better support of the new pavement structure
Detailed discussion and recommendations for the design of new pavements site
grading and other geotechnical aspects of the project are presented in the following
sections of this report

Pavement Design
As previously indicated the existing pavements may be removed and replaced
with new asphalt pavements for a longer pavement life Based on the information
provided we understand that the vehicle loading for the proposed project will consist
primarily of forklift and truck traffic We also understand that the vehicle loading at the site
will generally conform to the HS 20 truck load designation
Silty coral sands and gravels were encountered near the ground surface in the
borings during our field exploration We anticipate that the subgrade soils will have a
minimum CBR value of 17 Based on the above assumptions and our engineering
analysis we recommend that the following flexible pavement section be used for
preliminary design purposes
2 0 Inch Asphalt Concrete
6 0 Inch Basaltic Base Course 95 Percent Relative Compaction
8 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness on Moist Compacted Subgrade
It should be noted that the above pavement section is designed based on the
subgrade soil conditions encountered the results of the field CBR tests and traffic
assumptions If subgrade soil conditions exposed during site preparation are other than
those encountered during our exploration we should perform additional tests to confirm
or revise the recommended pavement section The recommended pavement section
assumes that good drainage will be provided for the paved areas
The pavement subgrade soils should be scarified to a depth of at least 8 inches
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to not less
than 95 percent relative compaction The finish subgrade should be sloped toward the
drainage system to carry surface and subsurface water off the site and to reduce the
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3615 00 Page 9
potential for ponding of water If silty and clayey soils are encountered at the subgrade
level they should be removed and replaced with compacted select fill material The base
course material should also be compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative
compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a
percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil determined in accordance with
ASTM D 1557 91 Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight
corresponding to the maximum dry density
If sidewalks are planned for the proposed project we recommend that a minimum
of 4 inches of select borrow be incorporated below the sidewalks to provide more uniform
support of the concrete The subgrade soils should be prepared as recommended
above The select borrow should also be compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative
compaction Construction joints should be provided at intervals equal to the width of the
walkways with expansion joints at right angle intersections
Paved areas should be sloped and drainage gradients maintained to carry all
surface water off the site Surface water ponding should not be allowed anywhere during
or after construction Where concrete curbs are used to isolate landscaping in or
adjacent to the pavement areas we recommend that the curbs extend a minimum of
2 inches into the subgrade soils below the aggregate base course to reduce migration of
landscape water into the pavement section For long term performance we recommend
that a subdrain system be constructed adjacent to paved landscape areas to collect
excessive water from landscape irrigation
Site Gradin
To construct the new pavements we anticipate that site grading will generally
consist of removal of the existing pavements preparation of the subgrade and placement
and compaction of base course material for the proposed project In general we believe
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3615 00 Page 10
that only minimal cuts and fills on the order of less than 1 foot relative to the existing
ground surface will be needed for the proposed construction
Site grading operations should be observed by a qualified geotechnical engineer
Itis important that the geotechnical engineer observe the site grading operations to
evaluate whether any undesirable materials are encountered during the scarification and
subgrade preparation process and whether the exposed soil rock conditions are similar
to those encountered in our exploration
After removal of the existing pavements all areas within the contract grading limits
should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed All debris rubbish and other unsuitable
materials from the demolition should be removed and disposed of properly off site Any
soft weak or yielding areas disclosed during the clearing and grubbing operations should
be over excavated to expose firm ground and the resulting excavation should be
backfilled with engineered fill material compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative
compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a
percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil determined in accordance with
ASTM D 1557 91 Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight
corresponding to the maximum dry density
The finish subgrade or the subgrade to receive fills should be scarified to a
minimum depth of 8 inches moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture and
compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction prior to placement of fill
material
Generally the granular on site materials may be reused as a source of general fill
and backfill material provided that they contain no lumps or fragments greater than
3 inches in maximum dimension The basaltic base course material should meet the
specified material requirements
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3615 00 Page 11
If imported fill materials are required as general fill and backfill materials the
imported fill and or backfill material should consist of well graded granular materials of
less than 3 inches in maximum dimension The material should have a laboratory
California Bearing Ratio CBR value of 15 or more and should have a maximum swell of
less than 1 percent when tested in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1883 In
addition it should contain between 10 and 30 percent passing the No 200 sieve
Imported fill material should be tested by Geolabs Hawaii for conformance with our
recommendations prior to delivery to the project site for its intended use
Generally the fill and backfill materials should be placed in level lifts not exceeding
8 inches in loose thickness moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content
and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction The fill and backfill
material within the upper 2 feet below the finish subgrade should be compacted to a
minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
Observations and field density tests should be performed during site grading to
assist the contractor in obtaining the required degree of compaction and the proper
moisture content on each fill lift Where compaction is less than required additional
compactive effort should be applied with adjustment of moisture content to obtain the
specified compaction
Utility Trench
If new utility lines are planned we recommend that a granular bedding consisting
of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation be used under the pipes
for support of the utility lines The backfill up to about 1 foot above the pipes should
consist of free draining material such as No 3B Fine gravel to reduce the potential for
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3615 00 Page 12
damaging the pipes from compaction of the backfill It is critical that a free draining
granular material be used to reduce the potential for formation of voids below the
haunches of pipes and to provide adequate support for the sides of the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 1 foot above the pipes to
2 feet below the finish subgrade should consist of approved on site soils or select
granular material The backfill material should be moisture conditioned to above the
optimum moisture content placed in maximum 8 inch level loose lifts and mechanically
compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for
future ground subsidence The upper 2 feet of the trench backfill below the finish
subgrade should consist of select granular material and should be compacted to not less
than 95 percent relative compaction
We recommend that mechanical compaction equipment be used to compact the
backfill materials at the project site Water tamping jetting or ponding may not be used
to compact the backfill materials

borings to depths of about 5 5 to 11 5 feet below the existing ground surface at the

conformance with the Unified Soil Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic
presentation of the materials encountered are presented on the Logs of Borings
Plates A 1 through A 10

ASTM Test Method D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by
California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts needed
to drive the sampler the last 12 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown on the Logs of
Borings at the appropriate sample depths

APPENDIX C
Laboratory Testing
Laboratory testing was performed on selected samples obtained during our field
exploration A laboratory testing program was formulated to aid in evaluating pertinent
engineering properties of the soil and materials sampled
Moisture content ASTM D 2216 is used as an aid to soil classification and to aid
in evaluating certain properties such as relative saturation The moisture content
expressed as a percentage of the dry weight of the tested sample and unit weight are
shown on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Two sieve analysis tests ASTM D 422 were performed on selected samples of
the soils to evaluate the gradation characteristics of the soils and to aid in soil
classification Graphic presentation of the test results is shown on Plate C 1
Two California Bearing Ratio CBR tests ASTM D 1883 were performed on the
near surface materials to evaluate the strength characteristics for pavement subgrade
support The test results are presented on Plates C 2 and C 3
