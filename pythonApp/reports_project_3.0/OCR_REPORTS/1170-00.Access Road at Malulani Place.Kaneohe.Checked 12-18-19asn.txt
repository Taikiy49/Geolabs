In accordance with your request and our proposal dated September 15
1982 we have performed a soil engineering investigation at the above
referenced site to evaluate the subsurface soil conditions in order to

access road

available soils and geologic information visual observations of the
existing site conditions by a geologist augmented by shallow
hand auger probes and test pits excavated at the project site The
above scope of investigation was developed due to the dense vegetative
cover at the site and the relatively inaccessibility for truck mounted
drilling equipment Therefore this report represents only a
preliminary reconnaissance

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located in the Kokokahi area of Kaneohe Oahu
Hawaii at the southern end of Malulani Place
The site is situated in a broad drainageway which drains towards the
north The ground surface elevations range from approximately 138
feet at the Malulani Place entrance to the project up to approximately
210 feet at the southern end of the proposed access road
The development site is densely vegetated The ground cover consists
primarily of guava haole koa Christmas berry and vines

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is our understanding that the proposed plan is to clear and grade
the site for an access road approximately 275 feet in length It is
anticipated that site grading will involve cuts up to 10 feet deep
and fills up to 10 feet thick
Concrete pavements are anticipated for the proposed access road
development
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The project site is located in the caldera complex of the Koolau
Volcano During the eruptive phase of this volcano rising hot gases
and steam altered the caldera filling lavas into a soft decomposed
rock This decomposed rock under exposure to weathering has broken
down to reddish clayey silt soil Some of this soil has been trans
ported by stream action and deposited in the central areas of drain
ageways
Field Exploration
Four hand auger probes were drilled at the site to a maximum depth of
11 5 feet below the existing ground surface in order to explore the
subsurface conditions and to obtain samples for laboratory testing
In addition two test pits were excavated to depths of 5 5 and 7 0
feet below the existing ground surface

Soil Conditions
Based on the results of our hand auger probes and test pits the site
appears to be underlain by very stiff clayey silts with sand and gravel
derived from the weathering of altered basalts
Alluvial soil up to approximately 8 feet thick was observed in the
central drainageway area This soil is composed of stiff clayey silts
with sand and gravel
Groundwater was not encountered in the test pits at the time of our
exploration
DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
In general our field exploration encountered very stiff soils con
sisting of tan to reddish brown clayey silts Laboratory tests
indicate that these soils generally have low to moderately high
swelling potential depending on the moisture content
Because of the silty nature of this material it tends to be quite
prone to erosion Therefore proper slope planting are recommended
and should be performed as soon as practicable In addition a
qualified landscape architect should be utilized to aid in selecting
the proper slope planting material and details in order to assure that
they can control steep slope surface erosion

Prior to the commencement of site grading the site should be
cleared of vegetation rubbish and other deleterious material
This organic material should be disposed of properly off site
In areas to receive fill the ground should be scarified to a
minimum depth of 6 inches brought to near optimum moisture con
tent and then compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry
density as established by ASTM D 1557 78 If soft spots are
encountered they should be overexcavated to firm ground and then
recompacted
Fills placed on existing slopes steeper than 5 horizontal to 1
vertical should be keyed and benched into the existing ground
This will assist in properly bonding the fill to the existing slope
see Plate 2 for details

Lot Grading
There were no definite plans for the individual lot grading available
at the time of our investigation Due to the steep terrain at the
site we recommend that each future lot purchaser should consult a
licensed engineer to help him in evaluating their proposed grading and
building construction
In addition lot grading is not included in the scope of this investi
gation Therefore no recommendations were formulated for the design
of future lot grading which may be required for the subsequent lot
utilization
Retaining Wall
A low retaining wall about 3 feet high and 100 feet long is planned
along a portion of the southern side of the road at the toe of the
proposed H V cut slope
The wall foundations should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below
the finished grade
