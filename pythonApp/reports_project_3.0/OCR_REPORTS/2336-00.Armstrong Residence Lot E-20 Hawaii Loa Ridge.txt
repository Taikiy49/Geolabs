SUMMARY OF RECOMMENDATIONS
The stiff on site soils encountered below the
existing ground surface can generally provide
adequate foundation support for the proposed house
foundations provided that our design
recommendations are implemented during
construction to minimize the swelling potential of
the underlying soil
To reduce the effects due to soil swelling under
on grade slabs we recommend that the on site soil
be capped with at least eighteen 18 inches of
non expansive select fill and that the subgrade
soils underneath the select fill and slab be
moistened and protected from drying from the start
of construction until the slab concrete placement
Spread or continuous wall footings may be used for
the proposed residence An allowable bearing
value or 2 000 p s f may be used for footings
bearing on the stiff natural soil
The text of this report should be referred to for
detailed and special design recommendations

This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the proposed residence for Mr and Mrs Charles
Armstrong at Lot E Ridge in Honolulu on the island of Oahu
Hawaii The exploration was performed in general accordance with our
proposal dated
This report summarizes our findings and presents our recommendations
derived from analyses based on data obtained during our exploration
These recommendations are intended for design input and are not intended
for construction estimation or for bidding purposes

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration and laboratory tests performed on the stiff
on site silty clay indicate that this material generally has moderately
high swelling potential if it is allowed to dry first during construction
prior to subsequent saturation from landscape and rain water
Lot Grading
Any new fills and backfills within the top eighteen 18 inches
minimum below the on grade slabs should consist of non expansive
off site borrow soils such as select borrow crusher waste or coral
Where cut or minimal fill is anticipated the on site soil should be
over excavated to provide aN eighteen 18 inch minimum thickness of
non expansive select material below the bottom slab
On site soils may be used only for fill if they are located outside
the slab area and compacted at least 3 above optimm moisture content or
placed below eighteen 18 inches from the slab bottoms and away from
retaining walls

At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limits should be cleared of vegetation debris rubbish and other
deleterious materials These materials should be removed and disposed of
properly off site Any loose or soft spots encountered should be
over excavated down to the firm subsoils and then replaced with compacted
non expansive select material
Fill material if required should consist of non expansive granular
materials generally less than three 3 inches in maximm size with less
than 20 passing the 200 sieve All imported select material and or fill
material should be tested for suitability by the project geotechnical
engineer prior to its transportation to the site
The fill areas should then be scarified to a depth of six 6 inches
the subgrade moisture conditioned to about 3 above optimm moisture
content and recompacted to about 90 of its maximm dry density per ASIM
Test Method D 1557 78 Fill on slopes steeper than five horizontal to one
vertical 5H 1V should be keyed and benched into the stiff natural
ground
Fill should be placed in maximum nine 9 inch thick loose level
lifts and compacted to about 90 of its maximm dry density per ASIM Test
Method D 1557 78
House Foundations
Individual spread footings and continuous wall footings for the
residence and deck should be embedded a minimum of twenty 20 inches
below the lowest adjacent finished grade
If located on sloping area footings should be embedded down an
additional amount to obtain a 10 foot minimm horizontal set back
distance measured from the outer edge of the footing out to the
Slope face This additional embedment depth is required to help resist
the downhill slope creep movement and to develop the recommended bearing
capacity of the soil

Slab on Grade
It should be noted that the on site silty clay soils may be
moderately expansive if allowed to dry Therefore the on site soil
should be over excavated down to a depth of eighteen 18 inches minimm
below the slab cushion fill the exposed subgrade thoroughly moistened
and then replaced with non expansive imported soils as previously
discussed The thickened edge of the slab should be embedded a minimm of
eighteen 18 inches below the lowest adjacent outside finish grade
To further reduce future post construction swelling of the underlying
expansive soils we recommend that the in place soils below the upper
capping fill pad be kept moist prior to concrete and cushion fill
placement
A four 4 inch layer of 3B Fine gravel cushion fill should be
provided below the slab In order to protect against future moisture
infiltration an impervious moisture barrier may be incorporated below the
slab
The garage slab should be underlain with a total of eighteen 18
inches of select borrow and base course layers It should also be
properly designed to accommodate wheel loading condition A cushion layer
consisting of six 6 inches of base course is recommended

Drainage
The finish lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings should
be shaped to shed water away from foundations and to avoid ponding
condition Also it is advised that gutter systems be installed around
the house and gutter water be diverted far away from the foundations and
driveway Excessive landscape watering near foundations and slab should
also be avoided
Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance
of the above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of
strength The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled tight
against the wall or slab footing immediately after setting of the concrete
to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and maintained
to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundations

Concrete Driveway

18 inch on site soil over excavation and replacement may be replaced
with one twelve 12 inch thick base course layer if some amount of slab
cracking are tolerable It is critical that the subgrade soils should be
properly maintained in a moist condition during construction and prevented
from drying out prior to base course and concrete placement
Joints are reconmended in the driveway slab at about 8 to 10 foot
intervals and at discontinuities in order to allow for some relative
movements of the concrete

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during the site grading and
construction of the foundations This is to cbserve compliance with the
design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow design
changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from that
anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in
this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field borings Variations of
conditions between the field data points may occur and the nature and
extend of these variations may not become evident until construction If
variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations given in this report
The location of the field borings were approximately determined by
taping from reference points indicated on the plans provided Elevations
of the data points were approximately determined by interpolation from
elevation points on the Site Plan dated by Design Partners
Incorporated The physical location and elevation of the field data
points should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the
method used
