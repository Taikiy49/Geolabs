AS requested we have observed the test pile driving at the
above referenced site
A total of fifteen test piles were driven at the locations indicated
on the Test Pile Location Plan The piles consisted of 16 5 inch
octagonal precast prestressed concrete piles The pile driving hammer
used was a Mitsubishi M 23 delivering a maximm rated energy of about
lbs
Background Information
Our previous foundation investigations at the site disclosed that
the subsurface conditions consist of about 10 feet of compressible
lagoonal deposits underlain by dense coral sand over weathered coral
formation at about 120 feet depth Generally our analysis indicated that

the dense sand or coral formation could provide suitable bearing for the
pile foundations However a large cavity was encountered in two of the
borings drilled near the center of the proposed building The cavity was
7 to 12 feet high and encountered at a depth between 70 to 90 feet below
the existing ground surface
The presence of the cavity presents Significant problems to the pile
foundation since piles which are stopped above the cavity could be
affected by future collapse of the cavity Therefore two systems of
piles are needed one for areas where the cavity is not encountered and
one for areas where the cavity is encountered Since the extent of the
cavity will be unknown until the piles are driven the pile design must be
flexible to accommodate both conditions
Based on the boring data it appeared that if present the cavity
should be encountered from depths of 70 to 90 feet Therefore if a pile
is driven down to a depth of 90 feet and does not encounter the cavity it
can be assumed that the cavity is not present at that location Fora
pile driven to a depth of 90 feet below the existing ground surface into
the dense coral sand an allowable pile capacity of 100 tons was
recommended
Where a cavity is encountered the pile should be driven to a depth
below the bottom of the cavity and into dense sand or coral formation to
obtain a 100 ton capacity

Therefore the design approach used was to specify 90 foot long
piles one section at each location If the pile is driven down to 90
feet and a cavity is not encountered a design capacity of 100 tons may be
used If a cavity is encountered near the bottom of the 90 foot section
a 35 foot long section should be spliced to the first pile and driven
deeper to maintain the 100 ton capacity
Predrilling prior to pile driving was anticipated since some hard
sand coral layers were encountered in the borings
The purpose of the test pile program was to
1 Attempt to delineate the general cavity area which would require
the long 125 feet piles
2 determine the need for predrilling and
3 provide a better estimate of the required length of production
piles in non cavity area
Based on the above the test pile program was designed with 5 piles
located around the perimeter of the structure and 10 piles located near
the center of the structure where the cavity was suspected Ten of the
test piles were scheduled to be 90 feet long with the remaining 5 at 125
feet long

Test Pile Driving
At the start of the test pile program the 35 foot pile sections in
the 125 foot long 90 35 foot sections piles were unavailable
therefore the shorter 90 foot test piles were driven first The first
two piles driven met refusal at relatively shallow depths of 37 to 43 5
feet near the center of the building Based on the hard driving
experienced it appeared that these shorter piles may provide the design
capacity provided they were not underlain by a large cavity Therefore
we recommended that the predrilling of the large diameter 18 inches
holes for the long piles should commence to delineate the cavity
location However a suitable drilling rig to provide the large diameter
predrill holes was not available therefore it was decided to utilize a
smaller rig to drill 4 5 inch diameter probe holes at the pile locations
Five small diameter 4 5 inch probe holes were drilled at test pile
locations 59 and 80 to delineate the extent of the previously
encountered cavities A cavity was only encountered at the Test Pile No
59 location at an approximate depth of 66 0 to 73 0 feet Test Pile No
59 was subsequently predrilled and driven to a depth of 99 5 feet From
the results of the test pile driven lengths it was recommended that a
minimum of two load tests be performed one on Test Pile No 55 short
pile and another at Pile No 59 long pile through cavity
Cavity Probe Drilling

Pile Load Tests
The first pile load test set up was observed by our field engineer
and appeared satisfactory except that the calibration certification for
the hydraulic jack had expired The test was scheduled to commence on
August 16th however the pile load testing was delayed until proper
certification of the hydraulic jack was obtained It was decided that the
ROICC would observe the test and transmit the results to our office for
review and comment
