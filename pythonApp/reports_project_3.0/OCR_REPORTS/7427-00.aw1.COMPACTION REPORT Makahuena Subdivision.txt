This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations and testing

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The planned Makahuena Subdivision development encompasses about 9 acres 
in Koloa on the south shore of Kauai for mass grading and infrastructure construction. 
The project site is located along the shoreline adjacent to an existing developed 
subdivision, The Pointe at Po’ipu. The project location and general vicinity are shown on 
the Project Location Map, Plate 1. 
The proposed subdivision construction consisted of mass grading for 10 individual 
house lots and construction of approximately 700 linear feet of roadway connecting the 
parcel to the existing Pe’e Road with underground utilities, such as drainage, sewer, 
water, and others, as shown on the Site Plan, Plate 2. In addition, approximately 
600 linear feet of retaining walls with a maximum height of about 14 feet were installed for 
grade separation. In general, the site grading operation consisted of fills up to about 
14 feet and cuts up to about 8 feet. 
We understand that each house construction will be conducted under a separate 
contract, including buildings, driveways and utilities. Majority of the roadway was paved 
with asphalt concrete. However, the access roadway to Lot No. 1 was only capped with 
aggregate base course. The pavement for the access roadway to Lot No. 1 will be 
constructed in the future under a separate contract.

SITE GRADING 
In general, site grading construction consisted of fills up to 14 feet high and cuts up 
to 8 feet deep. Prior to filling, the existing ground surface was cleared and grubbed of 
vegetation and deleterious material, then proof-rolled with a compactor. Cuts into the 
existing subgrade were aided by drilling and controlled blasting. Earthworks Pacific Inc. 
performed the earthwork construction. 
Rock formation was generally encountered at a shallow depth throughout the 
development site. To facilitate excavation and utility construction, the rock formation was 
drilled and control blasted to 1 foot beyond the planned excavation depth. The blasted 
rock was then removed to a depth of approximately 1 foot above the bottom of the blasting 
depth. Rock debris was transported to an on-site crusher plant to produce the on-site 
12-inch minus well-graded fill for general fill. The remaining blasted rock was compacted in 
place by a larger dozer and 14-ton vibratory sheepsfoot compactor. In addition, imported 
3-inch minus material from the Kauai Aggregates Quarry was also utilized during the site 
grading. 
Capping fills consisting of imported 3-inch minus general fill materials were placed 
about 1 foot or less on Lot Nos. 1 through 4, while 4 feet or less of the capping fills were 
placed on Lot Nos. 5 through 7. Lot Nos. 8 through 10 were largely in cuts where minimal 
capping fills were provided for leveling. 
During the site grading operations, samples of the materials utilized as fill were 
obtained and transported to our laboratory for testing.  The maximum dry densities and 
optimum moisture contents were established in general accordance with ASTM D1557 
(Modified Proctor), and the test results are summarized on Plate 3. 
Periodic in-situ moisture and density tests were conducted in general accordance 
with ASTM D6938 (Nuclear Gauge Method), to determine the adequacy of compaction of 
fills and backfills for mass grading and infrastructure/utility installations, including 
compaction of the roadway, trench backfills, and sidewalks. 
The fill and backfill materials were moisture-conditioned to above optimum 
moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry 
density as determined in accordance with ASTM D1557.  In the roadway areas, the fill 
and backfill materials within 3 feet of the finished grade were compacted to a minimum 
of 95 percent of the maximum dry density.  
The test results are summarized on Plates 4.1 through 4.5. These tests were 
performed on moisture-conditioned and compacted lifts of fills and backfills.  Test 
locations and elevations were estimated using on-site survey stakes for reference.

HOUSE CONSTRUCTION CONSIDERATIONS 
It should be noted that future house structures at the project site may be situated 
on cut and fill transitions. Consideration should be given to the individual lot owner to 
retain qualified geotechnical services for site-specific foundation design and 
construction observation. 
The finished grades adjacent to the house structures should be sloped to shed 
water away from the foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding. It is also 
advised to install gutter systems around the house structures and to divert the discharge 
away from the foundation and pavement areas. Excessive landscape watering within 
3 feet of the foundations and slabs should be avoided. Gravel strips adjacent to slabs 
and foundations should be avoided. Planters next to foundations should be avoided or 
have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the subsoils. 
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the 
house foundations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation and 
subsequent loss of strength of the near-surface soils. The foundation excavations 
should be properly backfilled against the footings immediately after setting of the
