INTRODUCTION
This report represents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the foundation and site grading design of the proposed reservoirs and
irrigation lakes for the Kaupulehu Resort Infrastructure

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the general
subsurface soils and rock conditions at the project sites in order to obtain data for the
formulation of preliminary geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to the
design of the project site grading proposed reservoir foundations and roadway
pavements
The scope of our preliminary exploration consisted of the following tasks and work
efforts
1 Review of available soils and geologic information pertinent to the project
site and its vicinity
2 Reconnaissance of the project site and its vicinity to make visual
observations of the surface soil conditions and to select locations for the
borings
3 Mobilization of a truck mounted drill rig and water wagon with operators to
and from the project sites
4 Drilling and sampling a total of fifteen 15 borings at the proposed reservoir
and lagoon sites to depths ranging between twenty 20 and forty 40 feet
below the existing ground surface
5 Coordination of the field work and drilling of the borings by a Field Geologist
from our firm
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2702 00 Page 3
6 Engineering analyses of the field data for the formulation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertinent to the design of the proposed
reservoirs
7 Preparation of this report presenting our findings and recommendations
8 Coordination of our overall work on the project and client design team
consultation by a Principal Engineer of our firm
9 Miscellaneous work efforts such as drafting word processing and clerical
support

SITE DESCRIPTION
The five 5 project sites are located upslope easterly of the Kaupulehu Resort
and Queen Kaahumanu Highway and downslope westerly of the Mamalahoa Highway
on the Big Island of Hawaii The general location of the sites and overall project vicinity
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2702 00 Page 5
are shown on The Project Location Map Plate 1 The individual project sites and the
locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plans Plates 2 through 6
The five 5 sites are staged along an existing improved access roadway which
traverses the southern property limits of the Kaupulehu Resort from Queen Kaahumanu
Highway to the Mamalahoa Highway The area is characterized by topography which
slopes slightly to moderately downward toward the west The topography is formed by
the original structure of the volcanic flows which have covered the site Both a a and
pahoehoe flows are evident throughout the area Cinder and spatter cones collapsed
lava tubes and pahoehoe surface blisters are also observable within the limits of the
project site The area is sparsely vegetated
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
Hawaii the largest island of the Hawaiian Archipelago covers an area of
approximately 4 000 square miles The island was formed by the activity of five 5 shield
volcanoes These are Kohala which is long extinct Mauna Kea which has had activity
during recent geologic times Hualalai which last erupted in 1801 and Mauna Loa and
Kilauea which are both still active
The project site is located on the northern flank of Hualalai mountain which makes
up the west central portion of the island and consists partly of the prehistoric basaltic lava
flows of the Hualalai Volcanic Series which were most likely deposited during the late
Pleistocene Epoch Sterns and MacDonald 1946
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2702 00 Page 6
Hawaiian volcanic activity is typified by an abundance of flank eruptions along rift
zones or zones of weakness in the earth s crust The project site is situated in the
northwest rift zone of Hualalai
The lava flows at the sites are of the a a basalt type with dense zones of hard rock
and loose zones of clinker overlying and intermixed with older pahoehoe flows The
clinker phase is highly permeable Lava tubes are generally not encountered in a a flows
but are not uncommon in pahoehoe flows Cinder cones underlain and surrounded by
depositional scoriaceous cinder and spatter cones are located within the project area

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
From a geotechnical engineering point of view the proposed construction appears
feasible provided that our recommendations are followed
The underlying volcanic rock formations should provide adequate bearing for the
proposed structures Due to the potential existence of cavities within the volcanic
formations we recommend that a program of probing and grouting at the locations of the
proposed foundations of the concrete reservoir tanks be conducted during construction
The excavated floors of the lagoon type reservoirs should be surveyed by ground
penetrating radar to locate near surface cavities after which a follow up location specific
probing and grouting program may be utilized to further define and fill the voids These
recommended programs are discussed in greater detail in a later section of this report
Foundations for Concrete Reservoir Tanks
The borings indicate that the foundations for the three 3 concrete reservoir tanks
will be bearing on materials ranging from very hard massive basalt formations to loose
to dense clinker and or cinder
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2702 00 Page 9
The concrete reservoir tank locations exhibit varying subsurface conditions at the
approximate finish floor elevation Therefore special consideration should be given to
subgrade preparation at these locations Where the foundations are to span an area
which exhibits subgrade conditions of varying consistency and hardness i e hard basalt
grading to clinker and or cinder the entire slab and footing area should be overexcavated
to a depth of three 38 feet below the bottom of the slab and replaced with base course
materials compacted to a minimum of 95 of their maximum dry density to lessen the
potential for differential settlement across the structure On site materials processed to
form a well graded gravel with a maximum particle dimension of six 6 inches may be
substituted for the base course material The material selected for use should be tested
and approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to placement
Providing that these recommendations are followed an allowable bearing pressure
temporary wind or seismic loads are considered
Footings should be embedded a minimum of eighteen 18 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grade Footings located next to utility trenches should be extended
down to an elevation equal to or deeper than that of the trench bottom The trench
backfill which should be completed prior to foundation construction should be
compacted to a minimum of 95 of its maximum dry density as determined by ASTM
Test Method D 1557 78 or filled with concrete
All footing excavations should be properly backfilled and compacted to 95 density
per the above specifications
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2702 00 Page 10
The site grading should be designed to divert surface water away from the tank
foundations
Settlement
Based upon the boring data and the anticipated reservoir loads estimated
settlements are anticipated to be negligible at the tank locations
Lateral Resistance
For passive earth pressure an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 p s f per foot of
depth may be used A friction factor of 0 4 may be utilized to determine the sliding
resistance of the structure
Probing and Grouting
To reduce the possibility of footing settlement due to collapse of loose pockets or
cavities in clinker or lava tubes in the underlying lava rock the footing excavations
should be probed at approximately ten 10 feet on centers with a minimum of one
probe drilled at each spread footing location If a cavity is located below a footing
additional probe holes should be made to delineate the horizontal extent of the
cavity The probe holes should have a minimum diameter of two 2 inches and
extend below the bottom of the footing to a depth of at least ten 10 feet The
probe holes should be grouted from the bottom of the hole using low pressures
The probing and grouting operations should be monitored by the soils engineer
or the project inspector to see that these recommendations are carried out
properly and to locate any potential voids near the footing excavations

Irrigation Lakes
Based upon the proposed grading plans provided to form the basis of this report
an embankment of approximately four 4 feet in height will be constructed in the
northwestern portion of the 6 0 M G lagoon type reservoir to form a section of the
retention structure The 2 0 M G lagoon type reservoir appears to have a retention
structure composed entirely of undisturbed native materials At both structures materials
ranging from hard basalt to loose clinker are likely to be encountered in the excavation
It is our understanding that impervious synthetic liners discussed below will be provided
to assure watertight integrity of the reservoirs
The floor of the excavations for the reservoirs should be surveyed by ground
penetrating radar to detect the presence of cavities lava tubes or very loose clinker
layers pockets which may occur below the subgrade elevations If such anomalies are
based upon the survey data suspected of existing further location specific probing and
grouting as discussed previously should be performed to fill the voids or densify the loose
areas In addition proof rolling of the excavated finish floors with a D 9 bulldozer or
similar equipment should be performed to collapse and or expose near surface voids
Impervious Liners
It is our understanding that permanent impervious synthetic liners will be provided
for the proposed lagoon type reservoirs It is opinion that the material type performance
installation and protection details of these liners should be designed by the liner
manufacturer It is critically important that the liner maintain their integrity to prevent
leakage of the impounded water and possible piping of the retention structures
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2702 00 Page 12
Our experience with similar synthetic liner installations indicates that there is
sometimes a tendency for the liners to become detached at the top of the water
impoundment surface It appears that this may be the result of inadequate anchoring of
the top of the liner and insufficient flexibility of the liner material It is our recommendation
that the liner manufacturer be consulted regarding proper construction detailing and
installation of the liners with particular attention to proper anchoring to reduce the
potential of liner failure Effects of fluctuating water levels on the performance of the
impervious liner near the top of the anchoring should be evaluated
A technical representative of the manufacturer of the impervious liner should be
required to be on site at all times during their installation to assure that proper
construction procedures and precautions are followed A sales representative of the
supplier s may not have the technical expertise to adequately perform this function
Due to the nature of the anticipated subgrade it is recommended that a cushion
of sand or fine grained gravel with a minimum depth of six 6 inches be placed below the
liner In the event that slopes exceed a grade upon which sand or gravel may be utilized
as a cushion we recommend the use of a synthetic geotextile such as Tensar DC1200
or equivalent materials

of the structure

Site Grading
In general the site grading will consist of cutting into adjacent slopes or hillsides
as much as twenty five 25 feet in vertical height to create level pads for reservoir tank
foundations and lagoon floors Significant fills are not anticipated with the exception of
the northwest portion of the 6 0 M G irrigation lake where up to four 4 feet of fill is
planned at a limited area
Site Preparation
The site to be graded should be cleared of all vegetation organic matter debris
and soft material
Backfill
If required backfill should consist of select well graded granular material such as
base course or select borrow The backfill should be compacted to a minimum
of 95 of its maximum dry density determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
All backfill material should be tested and approved by the geotechnical engineer
prior to use
Fill Material
In general the excavated rock material less than twelve 12 inches in size may
be used as general fill material If the excavated rock material does not contain
sufficient fines to produce a well graded material which will form a compacted
mass without the occurrence of voids it may be necessary to mix cinder and gravel
with the excavated rocky material to increase the percentage of fines to provide a
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2702 00 Page 14
better graded material for compaction and increase the stability of the fills For fills
and backfills within two 2 feet of finished grade below the structures and
roadways within the reservoir limits the maximum size of the particle in the fill
should be reduced to six 6 inches to facilitate compaction
Fill Placement
General embankment fills should be placed in horizontal loose lifts with a maximum
height of up to eighteen 18 inches thick and compacted to a minimum of 95 of
its maximum dry density Fills below structure foundations should be placed in
loose lift heights with a maximum depth of twelve 12 inches and compacted to
a minimum of 95 of its maximum dry density as determined by ASTM D Test
Method 1557 78
Conventional compaction testing is generally not practicable in excavated volcanic
rock material due to the large particle size Instead a testing program consisting
of visual observations and test strips to determine the lift thickness and number of
passes of compactor required to achieve the desired compaction should be
conducted at the start of the project Tne number of passes can then be used as
the criteria for compaction
Excavation
The field exploration indicates that hard to very hard massive basalt formations will
be encountered at all of the sites with the possible exception of the 0 05 M G
reservoir Most of the material at this site appears to be more weathered basalt
moderately dense clinker and loose cinder and sand It is our opinion that these
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2702 00 Page 15
hard basaltic materials may present difficult excavation and that it may be
anticipated that they be more readily excavated with some amount of blasting
For the purpose of this report rippability of the subsurface materials was assessed
based upon our visual observations of the existing rock outcrops in the area and
interpretation of the core boring data and examination of the cores retrieved
We recommend that all prospective contractors bidding on this project examine the
rock outcrops and the boring data and the cores retrieved and to make their own
interpretation
At the five different sites to be graded it appears that most of the cut slopes will
be in varying materials such as cinder clinker and dense rock An average
design cut slope of two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V is recommended for cuts
less than twenty five 25 feet high in clinker and cinder The design may be
steepened to one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V in dense rock formations
Surface water from areas above the slopes should be diverted away and prevented
from flowing across the slope face
Fill slopes should be designed with slope ratios of two horizontal to one vertical
2H 1V
