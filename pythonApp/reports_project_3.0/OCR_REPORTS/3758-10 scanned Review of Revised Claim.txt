REMARKS As requested we have reviewed the revised claim submittal resubmitted by
Malcolm Drilling The revised claim submittal for the above
project was prepared by Hart Crowser for MDCI dated
Based on our review of the revised claim submittal we are providing the
following comments for your review and consideration
if In general we believe that the evaluation by Hart Crowser MDCI s
consultant does not represent an independent and objective evaluation of
the conditions We believe that the conditions have been presented in a
manner that only favors the Contractor in support of MDCI s claim presented
in the revised claim submittal
Based on our evaluation of the conditions encountered when compared to
the conditions known at bid time we believe that the conditions encountered
do not represent a significant change in the conditions represented at the
time of the bid In general we believe that the amount of hard to very hard
rock encountered during the production drilled shafts was generally about
the same or less than should have been anticipated from the boring logs
based on drilled shafts extending to the original estimated drilled shaft tip
elevations at the time of the bid
However we believe that the amount of medium hard to hard rock
encountered during the production drilled shafts was generally more than
would have been anticipated from the boring logs based on drilled shafts
extending to the original estimated drilled shaft tip elevations at the time of
the bid
Overall we believe that the Contractor failed to demonstrate in the revised
claim submittal that the conditions and the amount of the medium hard to
very hard rock conditions encountered during the production drilled shaft
installation was significantly more than what was represented in the bid

2 We wish to point out that the original estimated drilled shaft tip elevations at
Pier A were at 393 feet MSL The revised tip elevations after the drilled
shaft load test were reduced to 404 feet MSL about 11 feet less than the
original depth As illustrated in the boring logs available at the time of bid
Boring No 2 drilled at Pier A encountered very hard rock with an RQD
value of 100 at about Elevation 395 feet MSL Therefore there should
have been at least 2 feet of very hard material per drilled shaft that would
have been encountered and should have been accounted for in the unit
prices for unclassified shaft excavation in the bid by the Contractor
In reducing the footage of drilled shafts at Pier A the State did not seek a
reduced unit price for the unclassified shaft excavation Therefore when the
Contractor now claims that the amount of hard rock is more than what they
anticipated at the time of the bid we believe that the hard rock condition that
they would have encountered based on the deeper drilled shaft depths
should be taken into consideration We note that the Contractor s consultant
Hart Crowser did consider this fact when preparing the analyses at Pier A
however the Contractor did consider this fact when preparing the analyses
at Pier B We believe that the Contractor did not consider the presence of
the very hard material at Pier A in the analyses because considering the
presence of the very hard material at Pier A would render the claim without
merit
Based on our evaluation we believe that the Contractor would have
encountered about 2 feet of very hard basalt rock per drilled shaft at Pier A
with a total of 12 feet for the six drilled shafts at Pier A Furthermore the
Contractor would have encountered at least 15 5 feet of medium hard to
hard rock per drilled shaft at Pier A illustrated in the boring logs within the
depths of the drilled shafts at Pier A This would then total to 93 feet of
medium hard to hard rock for Pier A
However we note that only 50 feet of medium hard to hard rock was actually
encountered for the shafts at Pier A We also note that only 36 feet of hard
rock was encountered in the six drilled shafts at Pier A The report that was
submitted by the Contractor did not appear to provide any credit to the State
for these differences
