INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this investigation is to evaluate the stability
of the existing reservoir embankments and the subsurface soil
conditions at the site to develop preliminary recommendations
for the site grading and foundations for the proposed sub
division
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The preliminary development plans call for development of the
approximately 49 acre site in 3 phases Phases I and II as
shown on Site Plan Plate 1 are the subject of our investi
gation
One concept of development is to retain the existing reservoirs
and build on the sugar cane field surrounding the reservoirs
To utilize this concept stability of the existing reservoir
embankments has to be evaluated and to determine if any
modifications required to the existing reservoir embankments
Another concept is to relocate the existing reservoirs to
another site and create a network of roadways to develop
the entire 49 acre as a planned subdivision
Relatively light one and two story wood framed residential
houses are anticipated

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located in appro
ximately one half mile mauka of Kaumualii Highway The
49 acre site consists of two adjacent reservoirs surrounded
by cultivated sugar cane fields The reservoirs are currently
in use and operated by the Kekaha Sugar Company providing
irrigation water to a large section of sugar cane extending
beyond the project boundary
As shown on Site Plan Plate 1 the site is bounded to the
_east and southeast by Onaona Road to the southwest by Kauai
Veterans Memorial Hospital to the west by an extension of the
cane fields for future Phase III development and to the
north by undeveloped land
Excluding the reservoir structure the site slopes west south
west at about 2 to 10 percent gradients Elevations at the
site for Phases I and II development range from approximately
32 to 100 feet above sea level The cane fields are criss
crossed by access and cane hauling roads and an irrigation
system The irrigation system consists of mainly unlined
3 to 5 feet ditches some concrete or rock lined ditches
wooden flumes gates small bridge and pipes

The existing reservoirs at the site are separated and
contained by earthen embankments approximately 8 to 12
feet high at the top for the low reservoir and up to 21
feet high for the upper reservoir Heavy vegetation
including grass shrubs and some large trees presently
covers most of the embankments Access roads run around
the perimeter of the lower makai reservoir on both the
top and at the toe of the embankments Control gates used
to regulate the flow of water from the upper to the lower
reservoir and from the lower reservoir to the irrigation
systen are located in the center of the upper berm and
in the southern corner of the lower berm The surface of
the tops and slopes of the embankment structures were dry
and firm at the time of the field exploration Numerous
shrinkage cracks were also noted in the embankment slopes
predominantly in the center and southwest corner of the
lower berm and along the top of the upper berm near Boring
No 3
The reservoirs have an estimated area of 2 acres each and
an estimated total average capacity of 40 acre

approximate elevations of the reservoir and embankments is

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology and Soil Conditions
The project site is located on the lower slope of the south
western flank of the Kauai Volcano A sharp break in the
slope marks the beginning of the Mana Plain a broad fairly
flat alluvial plain along the southwestern coast of Kauai
and abutting against a steep ancient sea cliff At the
elevation of the project site the Mana Plain is generally
underlain by recent alluvium and thin residual soils con
sisting of brown clayey soils Shallow basalt and basalt
outcropping at the mauka end of the site dips steeply under
the makai section of the site to an undetermined depth
Generally the fill soils in the embankment structure con
Sisted of approximately 10 to 15 feet of fairly homogeneous
fill consisting of a dark brown highly plastic CH clay
with a minor amount of weathered boulders and cobbles
The fill stratum is generally stiff to hard and appears
to have been well compacted Boring Nos 1 and 2 drilled
within the lower southeastern embankment of the lower
reservoir encountered a medium stiff layer at about 11
feet Below the embankment fill the underlying natural
soils consist of similar brown clays with increasing boulder
content grading down to weathered basalt Basalt bedrock
was encountered at 20 feet below the top of the embankment
or earthen berm in Boring No 1 23 feet below the top in
Boring No 2 and 24 feet below the top in Boring No 4

Boring Nos 6 through 9 were drilled within Phases I and
II subdivision area Our exploratory borings disclosed
subsurface soil conditions consisting of highly to cri
tically expansive plastic clays in the northern portion
of the site and moderately to highly expansive silty clays
in the southern one third of the site Generally the
approximate dividing line for these two type of soils is
the existing 45 foot contour line as shown on Site Plan
Plate 1 That is the area above elevation 45 feet has
highly to critically expansive soils and the area below
elevation 45 consist of moderately to highly expansive
clayey soils Additional borings should be drilled
within the site in order to more acccurately delineate
the boundary of these two type of soils
Boring 9 was drilled within the upper boundary of the site
Basalt was encountered at 2 feet below the existing ground
surface indicating that the basalt rock dips towards the
south
Detailed descriptions of the materials encountered in each
of the exploratory test borings are presented on the Boring
Logs in Appendix A It should be noted that these descriptions
and related information depict subsurface conditions only at
the locations indicated on the Site Plan and on the particular

Groundwater
Groundwater was not encountered in any of the borings at
the time of the field exploration
No seepage water or groundwater was encountered in the test
borings in the embankment structure Drill water introduced
into the hole during coring in Boring No 4 dissipated slowly

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
In general competent bearing soils were encountered at the
existing Waimea Reservoir embankments and over the develop
ment site for the proposed subdivision
There appears good overall stability of the existing embank
ment and no seepage was observed at the downstream slope of
the reservoir The soils encountered at the embankments are
very stiff to hard except in the lower reservoir embankment
where a layer of only medium stiff moist clay was encountered
at about 11 feet deep Although no records to indicate that
the existing embankment were engineered the overall stiff
consistency of the embankment fill indicates the slopes would
be stable for its existing use
There are numerous large shrinkage cracks at the south embank
ments of both the upper and lower reservoirs Some of these
cracks are up to 3 inches wide 12 inches long and about 12
inches deep These cracks were probably due to the excessive
drying of the embankment soils If these cracks are left
unimproved blocks of soils will peel off and progressly
enlarge to a point that the stability of the embankments
would be questionable or unacceptable if development schemes
are chosen to retain the existing reservoirs with housing
development to the south Therefore special treatment will
be required to eliminate further cracking and drying of the
surface embankment soils especially the south embankment
of the lower reservoir
PrPrnit APIO pa atareaas
The proposed development at the site is feasible from
geotechnical engineering standpoint provided the recommen
dations presented herein are implemented during the design
and construction The on site soils are highly expansive
in nature and we highly recommend the use of post and beam
construction
Earthwork and Grading
A preliminary grading plan for the site was not available
during our study We should review the preliminary grading
plan and perform additional analyses if deemed necessary
prior to preparation of final plans
From the topography of the site it appears that in general
minimal grading will be required other than the cut required
for access roadways Should fill soils are planned the
following grading procedures should be followed
Prior to filling the site should be cleared of vegetation
and debris then scarified and recompacted to 90 of its
maximum dry density established by ASTM D 1557
Due to the highly expansive nature of the on site soils
fills should be placed in 10 inch horizontal lifts compacted
to only 85 of maximum dry density as determined by ASTM
D 1557 method of compaction Moisture content should be at
least 3 above the optimum moisture during compaction
ee
Fills placed on slopes having grades steeper than 5 hori
zontal to 1 vertical should be keyed and benched into the
existing firm ground particularly near the reservoir and
drainageway areas
Slope ratios of 3 horizontal to 1 vertical can be used for
cut and fill slopes due to the highly expansive clay soils
Surface water should be diverted away from slopes and slope
planting utilized to reduce erosion and cracking of the
upper surface soils
Embankment Stabilization
If the existing reservoirs are retained as part of the develop
ment the lower southwestern embankment should be specially
stabilized because of the numerous large cracks Although
Similar cracks were observed at the south embankment of the
upper reservoir the degree of special treatment is less
critical because the heights of this embankment is only
about 8 feet or less However dressing up of the slope
to maintain a uniform slope gradient and adequate planting
will still be required
To reduce further cracking and to maintain adequate stability
of the south lower reservoir embankment in the future we
recommend that the existing slopes be cleared and grubbed
to expose all the dry and cracked soils Then the slopes
should be rebuilt to a slope gradient of 2 1 H V or flatter
using imported non expansive soils
The base of the new fill should be a level bench at least
12 to 15 feet wide and at least 2 feet below the invert of the
adjacent drainage ditch Preferably the existing service road
way adjacent to the toe of the lower embankment can be used as
the base to receive the new fill Keying and benching into
the existing embankments is recommended so that the new fill
will properly bond to the existing embankment This will
automatically remove all the dry and cracked soils from the
slope face
Alternately in lieu of rebuilding the slopes as described above
a buttress fill using on site soils may be considered by raising
the existing service road grades to about the normal water level
of the reservoir or to reduce the existing embankment height to
no more than 6 feet Dressing up of the slope to an uniform
gradient throughout and slope planting should still be required
We would be happy to discuss with your design engineer the details
of the above recommendations subsequent to the completion of
detailed topographic survey around and within the reservoir areas
Foundations
For the proposed light residential structures post and beam
footing foundations is recommended because of expansive soil
conditions Post footings located close to the tops of slopes
should have a minimum horizontal set back distance of 7 feet
from the slope face Post footings away from the tops of
slopes should be placed 24 inches below the finish grade
For the light loads anticipated an allowable bearing pressure
of 2000 p s f can be used for footings resting on the stiff
natural soil or compacted fill
If a footing is located next to a utility trench the footing
should extend to the bottom of the utility trench Surface
water should be diverted away from building foundations
Roadway Pavement
Asphaltic concrete pavements are planned for the development
Based on the results of the laboratory CBR tests it is esti
mated that the lower makai on site material CL soil can
provide a CBR value of about 5 The preliminary pavement
design may be as follows
Z inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 inch Base Course
10 inch Select Borrow
18 inch Total Pavement Thickness
Thicker pavement section would be required in the upper two
thirds of the project site where the underlying soils are
highly expansive CH clays The preliminary pavement design
for subgrade bearing on CH clay soil condition are as follows
2 inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 inch Base Course
18 inch Select Borrow
26 inch Total Pavement Thickness
CBR tests should be performed on the subgrade soils as they are
encountered in the field during construction and the design
sections can be revised based on these tests

It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during
earthwork construction to monitor the contractor s grading
operations
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be observed
by a soils engineer during construction to see that adequate
bearing has been obtained and that the subsurface conditions
are consistent with the findings of our subsurface investiga
tion
Any imported material required should be non expansive and
should be tested and approved by the soils engineer prior
to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon
the above inspections

FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored
by drilling nine borings using truck mounted rotary drilling
equipment with continuous flight solid stem 4 inch auger
Five borings to depths of 20 to 30 feet below the existing
ground surface were drilled in the reservoir embankment
structure Four borings to a depth of 10 feet were drilled
throughout the planned subdivision area An NX core
barrel was also used to extend Boring Nos 1 and 4 into the
underlying basalt Undisturbed and disturbed samples were
obtained and standard penetration tests were performed
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by
visual and textural examination in the field by our engineering
geologist who continuously monitored the drilling operations
The classifications were reviewed by visual inspection and
testing in the laboratory The soils were classified in
general conformance with the Unified Soil Classification System
A graphic presentation of the soils is presented on the Boring
Logs Plates A 1 through A 9
In the borings soil samples were obtained by driving
barrel ring sampler The ring sampler and standard penetration
tests were driven using 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The
blow counts used to drive the sampler the last 12 inches are
shown on the boring logs at the appropriate depths
