INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this investigation was to define subsurface
conditions and to provide recommendations for the design
and construction of the proposed Outpatient Building at the
Waianae Coast Comprehensive Health Center This report
Summarises our findings and recommendations
Page 2
W
Site Description
The proposed building site is located at the southeastern
edge of the Waianae Coast Comprehensive Health Center
The Health Center complex is situated on a relatively level
cleared area located at the lower Puu Mailiilii in Maili
Oahu The makai side of the proposed outpatient building
is near the edge of the slope which drops down about 60 feet
to Mailiilii Road at a two horizontal to one vertical slope
ratio
Coral outcrops and coral boulders were noted on the present
slope along with the brush vegetation There was no visible
sign of slope movements or instability in this area at the
time of our field reconnaissance
Proposed Construction
The proposed building is a 1 story slab on grade structure
of masonry and wood construction A light structural steel
roof is to be placed on bearing walls around the perimeter
Wall loading of 2 4 kips per foot is anticipated
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The Waianae Coast Comprehensive Health Center is located on
the lower slope of Puu Mailiilii The area has been subjected
to stream erosion and to ocean level changes

W
The results of ocean level changes formed the makai coral
formation This was deposited against the mauka clay and
boulderly talus slope which consists of eroded materials from
the cliffs of Pug MailiilTre
Subsurface Explorations
The subsurface exploration for the proposed building site
consisted of drilling four test borings The borings were
drilled at the locations shown on the enclosed Boring Lo
cations Plate 1 The test borings were located by the
drilling contractor Continental Drilling Hawaii Inc A
detailed description of the field exploration methods and
accompanying laboratory testing is given in the appendix
along with the boring logs and laboratory test results
Soils Conditions
The explorations disclosed essentially uniform soil conditions
within the proposed building area The moderately cemented
tan calcareous silty sand coral was encountered in all borings
at ground surface to the full depth of borings No coral cavity
was encountered at the boring locations
Ground water was not noted in the bore holes at the time of
field exploration

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The subsurface conditions encountered during the exploratory
program were relatively good from a soils and foundation en
gineering viewpoint Wall footings bearing on the in situ tan
coral layer will provide adequate support for the proposed con
struction without excessive settlement
Foundation
Spread and line footing foundations may be used for the proposed
building Foundations bearing within the in situ coral formation
can be designed for an allowable bearing pressure of 4 000 pounds
per square foot Generally the bottom of exterior footings should
be a minimum of 18 inches below the existing ground surface
For the portions of the footing foundation located near the top
of the slope at the southeast corner of the building site a
minimum set back of 10 feet should be allowed from the outer
edge of footing out to the slope surface
No cavities were encountered at the borings However during
construction probing should be done below the bottom of the
footing excavation at about 10 feet intervals
A jack hammer could be used to drill a minimum of 3 feet below
the footing bottom elevation If cavities are encountered it
should be backfilled with lean concrete

ElooreSitab
Expansive soils were not encountered at the building site
therefore slab on grade can be utilized without special
provisions for expansive soil
It is recommended that a 4 inch cushion layer of S4C be
placed above the coral subgrade and compacted to a minimum
of 95 of the maximum dry density established by the ASTM
Designation D 1557 72T
Slab on grade with floor coverings should be protected by a
plastic vapor barrier The plastic should be covered by a
thin layer of sand for protection and to avoid punctures
Ground water was not encountered within a depth of 20 feet
of the surface thus it is not necessary to install a coarse
granular base under the slab
Earthwork and Compaction
All area to receive fill should be undercut to remove the surface
vegetation and any previously placed topsoil fills The ground
surface should then be keyed and benched to properly bond the
newly placed fill to the existing soil Prior to the placement
of any new fill the upper six inches of existing ground surface
should be scarified brought to near optimum moisture content
and compacted to a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density
established by ASTM Test D 1557 72T Additional fills should
be placed in horizontal lifts and compacted to the specified

density
Any imported fill material under the slab and walkways should
be non expansive and must be tested and approved by the soil
engineer prior to placing
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the foundation excavation and probing
be inspected prior to the placement of concrete or reinforcing
to assure that adequate bearing has been obtained and that the
subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of our
subsurface investigation
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon
the above inspections

FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling
4 borings utilizing truck mounted equipment The depths of the
borings varied from 13 8 to 20 0 feet below the existing ground
surface The locations of the borings are shown on the enclosed
Site Plan and Boring Location Plate 1
The soils were classified by visual and textural examination in
the field by our engineer who continuously supervised the drilling
Operations The classifications were verified by visual inspection
and testing in the laboratory A graphic presentation of the soils
encountered is presented on the Boring Logs Plate A 1 through A 4
Soil samples were obtained by driving a standard penetration
sampler with a 140 pound hammer free falling a distance of 30
inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the last 12 inches
are shown on the logs of the borings at the appropriate sample
depth
