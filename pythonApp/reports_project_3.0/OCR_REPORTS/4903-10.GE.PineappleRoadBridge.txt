This letter report summarizes our geotechnical observations services performed
during the installation and testing of the tieback anchors for the H 2 Seismic Retrofit of
Pineapple Road Bridge project located in the District of Ewa on the Island of Oahu Hawaii
In general this letter report presents our observations and the results of the load
tests performed on the tieback anchors for the project from July 25 through
August 16 2005 The general contractor for the project was Kiewit Pacific Company and
the specialty subcontractor responsible for installation of the tieback anchors was CMZ of
Hawaii Inc
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Seismic Retrofit Pineapple Road Bridge project site crosses over the existing
Interstate Route H 2 Highway in the District of Ewa on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The
general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map
Plate 1
A geotechnical engineering evaluation of the project site was performed by
Geolabs Inc and our recommendations were presented in the report entitled
Geotechnical Engineering Services Seismic Retrofit of Bridges Interstate Route H 2
Meheula Parkway Golf Course Road and Pineapple Road Bridges Island of Oahu
Hawaii dated August 11 2003 The purpose of our geotechnical engineering services
during construction of the project was to observe the construction and testing of the
tieback anchors for general conformance with the project plans and specifications
As part of the seismic retrofit a permanent tieback anchor system was designed for
the existing concrete retaining wall Nos 1 and 2 located on the outbound and inbound
sides of the existing H 2 Highway respectively The permanent tieback anchor system

Permanent Tieback Anchor Installation
A representative from Geolabs was present to observe the drilling installation and
grouting operations during the construction of the permanent tieback anchors for the
project The permanent tieback anchors were drilled using a Casagrande C8
track mounted drill rig equipped with 8 inch diameter compressed air activated drill bit
The tieback anchor drilled holes were extended to depths of about 65 feet from the
front face of the concrete retaining walls with an inclination of 15 degree from
perpendicular to the face of the retaining walls Based on the information provided on the
plans the thickness of the existing retaining walls is 18 inches Therefore the depth of the
drilled hole generally provided the minimum 1 foot distance beyond the tieback anchor
tendon required by the project specifications
In general two grouting tubes were installed inside and outside of the corrugated
HDPE pipe Grouting began progressively from the bottom to top through the inner
grouting pipe until fresh grout appeared inside of the corrugated HDPE pipe
Subsequently the grouting operation continued through the outer grouting tube until fresh
grout appeared outside of the corrugated HDPE pipe The outer grouting tube was
removed after the completion of the grouting operation while the inner grouting pipe was
left in place
As part of the requirements from the project specification a post grouting tube
consisting of a inch PVC pipe attached along the full length of the tieback anchor was
installed prior to the grouting operation The bottom 40 feet of the PVC pipe consisted of
check valves to allow egress of the grout into the previously grouted tieback anchor
The volumes of grout placed within the tieback anchor holes ranged between
drilled holes Logs of the individual tieback anchors containing our observations and other
pertinent information recorded by our representative for each tieback anchor installed are
provided on the Permanent Tieback Anchor Installation Records in Appendix A of this
letter report
It should be noted that four permanent tieback anchors Inbound No and 8
were installed without the presence of Geolabs representative on It was
our understanding that an engineer from HDOT was present to observe the drilling
installation and the grouting operations for these four tieback anchors Therefore
installation records for these four tieback anchors are not included in Appendix A
Based on the observations of our field representatives we believe that the specified
tieback anchors observed by Geolabs representative have been installed in general
conformance with the project plans and specifications and within the limits of generally
accepted construction practices

Permanent Tieback Anchor Testing
In accordance with the project specifications a minimum of the first three and
ten percent of the remaining tiebacks installed were performance tested Extended creep
test was conducted on the first two tiebacks installed To accommodate two different
locations of the permanent tieback anchors installed two performance tests including one
extended creep test were conducted among the tieback anchors installed on each side of
retaining wall
Based on the sequence of the production tieback anchor installation the
permanent tieback anchors Inbound 2 and Outbound 5 were subjected to extended
creep testing The permanent tieback anchors Inbound 3 and Outbound 3 were
performance tested The remaining tieback anchors total of 14 tieback anchors were
subjected to proof testing
In general the permanent tieback anchors were loaded to the maximum test load
180 kips of 150 percent of the design load 120 kips The associated movements for the
tieback anchors at each load increment up to the maximum test load were measured and
recorded
In general the permanent tieback anchors subjected to performance and proof
tests met all the acceptance criteria specified in the project specifications The criteria for
acceptance of the tieback anchors consisted of the following
The tieback anchor displacement shall be greater than 80 percent of the
theoretical elastic elongation of the unbonded length
e The tieback anchor displacement shall be less than the theoretical elastic
elongation of the unbonded length and half of the bonded length
e The creep per log cycle shall be less than 0 040 inch between the 1 and
10 minute readings and 0 080 inch between the 6 and 60 minute readings
Based on our observations of the permanent tieback anchor tests performed by the
contractor it is our professional opinion that the tieback anchors have been installed to
provide the design load with tolerable movements The test results of the individual
permanent tieback anchors tested on the inbound side of H 2 highway Existing Retaining
Wall No 2 are presented on the Permanent Tieback Anchor Test Data Sheets in
Appendix B of this letter report The test results of the individual permanent tieback
anchors tested on the outbound side of H 2 highway Existing Retaining Wall No 1 are
presented on the Permanent Tieback Anchor Test Data Sheets in Appendix C of this letter
report
