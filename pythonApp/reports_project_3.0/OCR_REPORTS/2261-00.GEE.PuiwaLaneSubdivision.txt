INTRODUCTION
This report represents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the design of the site grading and house
foundations of the proposed Puiwa Lane residential development in the
Nuuanu Valley area of Honolulu Oahu Hawaii The general location of the
site is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is our understanding that the proposed subdivision will consist of
seven 7 residential lots Of particular concern is the stability of the
existing steep slope located near the rear of the parcel along the
southern property boundary
A site grading plan was not available at the time of our exploration
however cuts and fills generally less than 10 feet are anticipated
Retaining walls are also anticipated to provide grade separation at
various locations throughout the site
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface conditions of the project site in order to develop a
generalized soil rock data set for the formation of soil engineering
recommendations pertinent to the stability of the existing steep slope
site grading and foundation design of the project
The scope of work included
1 Drilling six 6 borings utilizing truck mounted drilling
equipment to depths ranging between 5 5 and 29 5 feet below the
existing ground surface at the approximate locations shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2
2 Reconnaissance of the project site and vicinity to make visual
observations of the surficial soil rock conditions
3 Review of available soils and geologic information pertinent to
the project site and its vicinity

exploration to determine the engineering properties and
classification of the materials encountered

STTE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located on the southeastern side of Puiwa Lane in
Nuuanu Valley Oahu Hawaii
The site measures approximately one 1 acre in area and is bounded
by Nuuanu Elementary School to the north and existing residences to the
south and east
Topographically the site slopes down to the south with ground
elevations ranging from approximately 388 to 362 feet above Mean Sea
Level An existing steep slope is located in the southeastern corner of
the site along the property boundary
Currently the site is occupied by two 2 existing residential
structures and is densely vegetated with a large banyan tree bamboo and
other trees and grasses
The site is traversed by an existing concrete drainageway trending
approximately north south Several abandoned cars and miscellaneous
rubbish were noted throughout the site

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
The project site is located in Nuuanu Valley an amphitheater headed
valley carved by erosion into Koolau Basalts Filling the base of this
valley is a thick sequence of alluvium interbedded with pyroclastic
deposits and lava flows of the Honolulu Volcanic Series
At the project site the borings encountered a surface layer of
alluvium overlying basaltic lavas believed to be of the Honolulu Volcanic
Series
Subsurface Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by performing a
surface geologic reconnaissance and by drilling six 6 borings to depths
ranging between 5 5 and 29 5 feet below the existing ground surface
In general the site is mantled with a surface layer of alluvial
clayey silts with gravel cobbles and some boulders approximately 4 0 to
16 5 feet thick Below this hard dense basalt was encountered to 29 5
feet the maximum depth drilled
Groundwater was not encountered in the drilled borings at the time of
our exploration

Existing Slope Stability
Boring Nos 4 and 5 were drilled along the southern boundary of the
site to evaluate the stability of the existing steep slope adjacent to the
site The existing slope is about 15 feet high and presently at a slope
ratio of about The borings indicate that the subsurface
conditions may consist of stiff alluvium over basalt rock formation The
existing steep slope appears to be stable at present however some
erosion or sloughing may occur in the future due to the steep natural
grade It is our opinion that the effect of the planned development on
the steep natural slope would be minimal if new fills and structures are
set back away from the top of slope a minimum of 15 feet and drainage is
diverted away from the slope

Ground Preparation
Prior to the commencement of any earthwork all areas within the
grading limits should be cleared and grubbed of all vegetation
demolition debris rubbish and deleterious materials The resulting
grub and spoil material should be disposed of properly off site Any
underlying soft weak or yielding areas disclosed during the clearing
operations should be over excavated to firm ground and the resulting
excavation should be backfilled with well compacted select granular
materials
Any large surface boulders should be collected and hauled off site
Subsequent to clearing and grubbing the areas designated to receive
fill within the grading limits should be scarified to a depth of
eight 8 inches moisture conditioned to near optimum moisture
content and recompacted to a minimm of 90 of its maximm dry
density per ASIM Test Method D 1557 78 Any rubble or boulders
encountered during the scarification which exceed six 6 inches in
maximm dimension should be removed and discarded Also any soft
weak or yielding areas encountered during the recompaction operations
should be over excavated and backfilled with select on site material
placed in nine inch 9 thick loose lifts and compacted to a minimum
of 90 of its maximum dry density as described above
In areas where existing slopes are steeper than five horizontal to
one vertical 5H 1V the fill should be keyed and benched into the
existing slope to aid in properly bonding the new fill to the in situ
soils See Plate 4 All keys and benches should be observed by the
project geotechnical engineer to ascertain that all unsuitable and or
unstable material has been removed prior to placement of fill
Additional excavation beyond the limits shown on Plate 4 may be
necessary if poor soil conditions are encountered at the bases of the

Fill Placement
After initial site preparation the required select on site soils or
off site borrow fill materials should be placed in maximm nine inch
9 loose lifts and compacted to obtain a minimum 90 of its maximm
dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Based on the field exploration and laboratory test results it
appears that the on site clayey silt soils ALIUVIUM may be used as
a source of fill material if properly moisture conditioned
However any undetected expansive clays which may be encountered
during excavation should not be incorporated into the fills
Imported borrow fill material if required should consist of select
non expansive material such as crusher waste coral or cinder and
should be tested for suitability as fill material by the project
geotechnical engineer prior to its delivery to the project site
The field exploration indicated that the anticipated cuts would be in
the alluvial deposits In general cut slopes ratios of two
horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V are recommended for the underlying
alluvium deposit
Fill slope ratios of two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V are
recommended The embankment fill should consist of on site soils or
off site select non expansive borrow materials over filled and then
cut back to the two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V finish slope
ratio

Individual footings on level areas should be embedded a minimum

effects of subsequent soil shrinkage or swelling All footing
backfill should be properly compacted to obtain a minimm 90

the future subgrade moisture changes due to drying and wetting
around the slab

moist condition from the initial foundation excavation up to the
concrete placement
It is imperative that all lots be graded to drain water away from
house foundations to prevent saturation and softening of the
subgrade soils

Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should he
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comment
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this review
is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the
earthwork and foundation phases of the work This is to observe
compliance with the design concepts specifications or recommendations and
to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ
from that anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations
given in this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If the actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made

recommendations given in this report
