Soil Engineering Investigation
Waiahole Valley Agricultural Park
Hawaii
This report presents the results of our soil engineering
investigation performed for the two proposed reservoir sites in Waiahole
Valley on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general locations of the sites
are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
Purpose and Scope of Services
The purpose of our investigation was to explore the subsurface
conditions at the proposed reservoir sites in order to provide
recommendations for use in the design of the foundations for the proposed
structures
Our investigation was performed in general accordance with our
proposal of except that the pump station has not been
sited and therefore was not included in this report
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests which we
used in the engineering analysis to formulate our conclusions and
recommendations are presented in the appendices of this report The
approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plans Plates
2 and 3
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed project consists of the following
g steel reservoir structure approximately 33 feet in
diameter and 32 feet high Minimal cuts and fills are planned
to create a level pad area for the reservoir
m g steel reservoir structure approximately 66 feet in
diameter and 40 feet high Cuts into the hillside up to about
18 feet are planned to create a level pad area for the
reservoir
SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed 0 2 m g reservoir is located on a knoll to the north
of Uwau Stream near the end of Waiahole Valley Road while the proposed
1 0 m g reservoir is located on a gentle slope to the north of Waiahole
Valley Road and approximately 1 100 feet to the west of a small bridge
across the Waiahole Streain
The topography of the 0 2 m g reservoir site is generally flat
sloping only slightly to the south The existing level area may have
been created by previous fill placement The site is sparsely vegetated
with grasses
The 1 0 m g reservoir site is on a gentle natural slope 8H 1V
down to the east The site is heavily vegetated with small to tall trees
and grasses

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The project sites are located on the Waiahole coastal plain which
is essentially a broad and fairly flat windward island fringe on Oahu
fronting two drowned amphitheater headed valleys
After the Koolau Volcano ceased its activity it was deeply incised
by amphitheater headed valleys subsequently drowned and partly filled
with sediments during Pliocene time Later glacio eustatic sea level
changes during the Pleistocene resulted in the accumulation of hundreds
of feet of marine and non marine sediments
The sites lie on gently sloping upland slopes that consist of
coalesced fans of alluvium and colluvial sediments derived from the
salient volcanic cliffs of the Koolau and Kailua Ranges to the south of
the site
The Kailua Volcanics a denuded rift zone may be exposed near the
upper 0 2 m g reservoir site These rift zone volcanics underlie the
alluvial deposits at depth
Soil Conditions
The soil conditions at the project sites were explored by drilling
and sampling seven 7 borings to depths of 7 to 40 feet Borings 2 to 5
were drilled at the 0 2 m g reservoir and Borings 6 to 8 at the 1 0
Meg ceservoir Boring 1 was planned for the booster pump station but
was not drilled since the final location has not been determined

The 0 2 m g reservoir site is generally underlain by relatively
uniform medium stiff reddish to mottled brown clayey silt which has
gravel locally at depths as indicated by Borings 2 and 4 Boring 3
indicated similar soil conditions and revealed the existence of a sloping
upper old fill layer of medium to very stiff brown clayey silt to a depth
of about 8 feet in the boring Boring 5 was subsequently drilled to
further delineate the extent of the fill which was found to be about 5
feet thick in the boring
The 1 0 m g reservoir site is generally underlain to the maximum
depth drilled by relatively uniform medium to very stiff brown to
mottled brown clayey silt which has some gravel or decomposed gravel at
depths as indicated by Borings 6 and 8
No groundwater was encountered in all the borings during the time
of our investigation but the soils have high in situ moisture contents
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The subsurface exploration at the two reservoir sites generally
encountered compressible underlying soils with high in situ moisture
contents which would experience excessive total and differential
settlements under the reservoir loads at maximum water storage
conditions
Based on the proposed reservoir slab finished grades our boring
and laboratory data the calculated total settlements of the tanks range
from 11 inches to as much as 2 feet and differential settlement between
the center and the edge of the tank fron 4 to 12 inches under maximum
water storage load

The eastern half of the proposed 0 2 m g reservoir will be
supported by an upper old fill of medium to very stiff clayey silt placed
previously with unknown construction procedures It is our opinion that
this could adversely affect later stability of the site and thus the
foundation if the fill was not placed with proper keying and benching
operations at the time of its construction
Recommended Alternatives
Based on our findings we believe that the reservoirs should not be
constructed as presently planned at the currently proposed locations We
are presenting herewith the following altermatives
1
36
The reservoir finished grades could be lowered to a minimum of
about 25 feet below the existing ground In this way the
weight of the new reservoir could be compensated by the weight
of the soil excavated It is our understanding that this may
not be possible due to minimum elevation requirements and lack
of space for the proposed cut slope
The amount of cut required to balance the weight of the
reservoir may be reduced if the height of the reservoir
structure is lowered thereby reducing the loading pressure
It is our understanding that this procedure is also not
desirable due to elevation and space requirements
The two reservoir sites may be relocated to where the soil is
less compressible and can provide substantial amount of site
cutting to compensate for the weight of the reservoir
structure It is our understanding that this alternative will
be pursued
