INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our foundation investigation
for the proposed Polynesian Springs Apartment complex in Hilo
Hawaii The purpose of this investigation was to explore the
subsurface conditions at the site and to provide data for use
in the design and construction of foundations
Page 2
W
PROJECT DESCRIPTION
The property is located on the southeast corner of Manono and
Kawelolani Streets in Hilo Hawaii The site is relatively
flat and with tall grass bushes trees and debris scattered
throughout the site A portion of the site is being used as
pastureland Basalt outcrops are found throughout the site
It is proposed to construct an apartment complex consisting of
two 7 story concrete structures with a 2 level parking struc
ture A single story restaurant and clubhouse will also be
part of the development Maximum column loads on the 7 story
structures are anticipated to be approximately 600 kips
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geologically the site is underlain by relatively thin flow of
a basaltic rock of the Quaternary Kau volcanic series These
lava flows consist of scoracious and clinker zones and lava
tubes The rock is generally dense with much jointing and
fracturing In order to explore the subsurface conditions at
the site nine borings were drilled to depths of from 13 feet
to 21 feet below the existing surface
A percolation test was performed in Boring No 7 by pumping in
a flow of 200 gallons per minute for 8 continuous minutes at
three separate times The locations of these borings are shown
Page 3
W
on the Site Plan Plate 1 A more detailed description of the
field exploration is given in the Appendix which also contains
the Logs of Borings
The site is covered by a dark brown soft silty clay topsoil
which extends to an average depth of about one foot to two
feet Underlying the topsoil is a dark gray Lava Rock The
rock to the depth 4 5 feet is generally dense however
below this depth the rock formation is highly fractured and
contains looser zones and clinkers
Groundwater was encountered in all borings at a depth of 4 0 to
4 5 feet below the existing ground surface Generally rock
coring and wash drilling below the water level experienced a
complete circulation loss of water due to the high permeability
of the thin bedded lava flows
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
GENERAL
Based on our investigation the proposed structures may be
supported on shallow spread and continuous footings founded
in the dark gray fractured lava rock Each footing area will
require probing and inspection pridr to placement of steel and
concrete in order to ascertain competent bearing rock materials

FOUNDATIONS
Spread and wall footings may be designed for a soil bearing
value of 8 000 pounds per square foot dead plus real live
loads A one third increase in the bearing value may be used
when considering wind or seismic loads All footings should
be bottomed a minimum depth of 18 inches below final adjacent
grade
Settlements will be minimal Footings should penetrate the
topsoil loose and soft clinkers weathered lava rock and at
least 12 inches into the competent lava rock In some local
areas such as encountered in Boring 9 the footings may have
to extend up to 7 feet If foundations are to extend below
the water table which was found to be from 4 to 4 5 feet
below the existing surface than footing or elevator shaft
footings will have to be dewatered
LATERAL PRESSURES
Lateral resistance to wind or seismic forces can be developed
by passive pressure acting against footings and by rock concrete
frictional interaction The passive resistance of natural rock
and compacted fill may be assumed to be equal to the pressure
developed by a fluid with a density of 350 pounds per cubic foot
A coefficient of 0 5 may be used between footings and supporting
rock

SLABS ON GRADE
The existing silty clay at surface is soft moist and expansive
and will not be suitable as subgrade materials It is therefore
recommended that in the area of slabs on grade all the clay be
removed and replaced by compacted free draining granular fill
such as Aa materials Compacted structural fill should be
placed in 8 inch loose layers and compacted to a minimum of
90 of the laboratory maximum density using ASTM D 1557 70
as a standard Slabs should be placed on a minimum of four
inches of granular type material
PAVEMENTS

compacted granular fill

INSPECTION
It is recommended that all footing excavations be probed by
jackhammering and inspected by the soils engineer prior to
placing reinforcing steel and concrete The excavations should
be trimmed neat level and square and be free of sloughed
soils at the time of the inspection All footing overexcava
tions should be filled with foundation concrete It is
extremely prudent that footings be confirmed to be underlain
by competent bearing rock and that there exist no loose or
cavitated zones beneath footings

FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling
nine test borings utilizing skid mounted rotary auger equipment
with rock coring attachments The depths of the borings ranged
from 13 to 21 feet below the existing ground surface The loca
tions of the borings are shown of the Site Plan Plate 1
The soils and rock were classified by visual and textural
examinations in the field by our geologist who continuously
supervised the dirlling operations The classifications were
verified by inspection and testing in the laboratory All
soils were classified in accordance with the Unified Soil
Classification System The description of rocks is based on
local geologic terminology Graphical representations of the
soils and rock encountered are presented on the Boring Logs
Plates A 1A through A 11 The percent of recovery of
rock cores are shown on the Log of Borings at the sample depth
