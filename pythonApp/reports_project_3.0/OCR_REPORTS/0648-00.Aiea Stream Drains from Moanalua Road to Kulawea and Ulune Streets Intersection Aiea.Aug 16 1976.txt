INTRODUCTION
This report summarizes the findings of our subsurface investi
gation along the storm drain alignment The purpose of the
investigation was to explore subsurface soil geologic condi
tions along the project alignment and to assess the site condi
tions for the design and construction of the drainage structures
PROJECT CONSIDERATION
The proposed Aiea Stream drainage channel improvements consist
of three sections of open lined concrete channel along the exis
ting stream alignment

SITE DESCRIPTION
The total length of this drainage project improvement is about
1 500 feet The proposed improvement section drops about 55
feet between the two ends
At the time of our boring investigation the width of the stream
was about 6 to 10 feet and the stream water was about 6 inches
deep Large river boulders up to 3 feet in diameter were scat
tered along the bottom of the stream bed Because of large quan
tities of rapid running water practically all the silts and fines
were washed out from the stream deposits Therefore no soft mud
deposit was noted along this section of the existing stream
Existing rubble and concrete retaining wall structures along the
stream area are in a stable condition There was no indication
of slope movement However some surface slope erosion had occur
red which exposed portions of the Aiea Shopping Center retaining
wall footing about 200 feet from the beginning of the makai section

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The Aiea Stream is situated on the southern flank of the coastal
plain on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The coastal plain lies mostly
on ponded lavas of the Koolau Volcano south of the Schofield Plateau
The plain is composed chiefly of marine sediments deposited on the
lava when the sea stood higher in mid Pleistocene time
It appears that the present Aiea Stream cuts into the older con
solidated marine deposits River boulders packed densely with
brown silty clay and river gravel can be observed on the existing
slope banks along this portion of Aiea Stream
Subsurface Exploration
Five test borings were drilled at this site The borings extended
from 5 to 20 feet below the existing stream channel and slope sur
faces The underlying basalt boulders under the stream bed and
slope banks made drilling extremely difficult Three additional

logs of the slope soil profile were used to further identify the
underlying subsurface soil condition
Detailed information on field exploration and laboratory testing

shown on the Site Plans Plates
Soil Conditions
The subsurface exploration and laboratory testing disclosed that
the subsurface soil condition consists mainly of river boulders
in the streambed and boulderly gravel with brown silty clay matrix
on the slope surfaces The basalt boulder portion is estimated
to exceed 50 A thin lava flow mixed with sand and gravel was
noted at the northern slope bank about 300 feet from the begin
ning of the proposed stream improvement At the slope bank next
to boring No 1 pockets of expansive soil with shrinkage cracks
exist on the ground surface This is the only area where expan
sive soil was noted and it is probably part of the off site dump
fill material deposited in this area
DISCUSSIONS RECOMMENDATIONS
The subsurface conditions encountered during the exploratory soil
investigation were relatively good from a soils engineering view
apoint
The wall footings bearing upon the firm in situ clayey gravel and river
boulder deposits will provide adequate support for the proposed
U shaped retaining wall structure and concrete apron on the stream
bed

Retaining Wall
The retaining wall should be designed to receive active lateral
pressure equivalent to a fluid density of 40 pounds per cubic
foot if on site silty soils are used as backfill material
Equivalent fluid density of 55 p c f should be used if the top
of the slope is at a 2 horizontal to 1 vertical ratio behind the
wall For a 1 horizontal to 1 vertical slope ratio behind the
wall 75 p c f equivalent fluid pressure should be considered
Expansive soil near Boring No 1 should be avoided as backfill
material
A coefficient factor of 0 45 can be utilized to determine sliding
resistance of the retaining wall foundations Weepholes or a sub
surface drainage system should be incorporated to prevent the build
up of excessive hydrostatic pressure behind the wall
Concrete Slab In Stream Bed Area
To provide a uniform support for concrete apron construction in the
stream bed a 4 inch layer of No 3B Fine Rock or equivalent granular
material should be provided under the concrete slab Holes should
also be incorporated through the slab to release the hydrostatic
pressure build up under the slab
Earthwork
In order to construct the proposed stream improvements some exca
vations are anticipated to obtain the desired alignment and invert
elevation The earthwork excavation will mainly be in river boulder
and colluvium formations

W
Fill will be required behind the new retaining wall structure
The on site soil is non expansive and is acceptable as backfill
material However boulders greater than 6 inch in diameter
should not be used in the backfill
All areas to receive new fill should be undercut to remove the
surface vegetation and loose topsoil
Prior to placement of fill the exposed surface should be wettea
to near optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90
maximum dry density Additional fill should then be placed in
9 inch horizontal lifts and compacted to the above specified
density
Loose material in the undermined portion below the existing re
taining wall structure should be removed The voids should then
be grouted with lean concrete up to the footing bottom
Slopes
Cut slopes should not exceed one and one half horizontal to one
vertical ratio 1 1 The on site clayey soils with gravel and
boulders can be cut at essentially vertical slopes for short term
construction conditions up to 6 feet deep Beyond that depth un
supported excavation should be sloped back at one horizontal to one
vertical slope ratio 1 1 or properly braced
Except for the backfill behind the retaining wall no major filis
are anticipated for this project The on site silty and gravelly

clay matrix is highly erosive Therefore slope erosion protection
during and after construction will be required
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the foundation excavations be inspectea by a
qualified soils engineer prior to the placement of concrete or rein
forcing to assure that adequate bearing has been obtained ana that
the subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of our
subsurface investigation
During construction if any loose backfill is located beiow the con
struction area the soft material should be removed and repiacea with
granular compacted fill The over excavated area should also be pack
filled with granular material
The backfilling and earthwork operations should be monitored by thc
soil engineer Construction inspection should include the necessary
testing to assure that the proper degree of compaction is consistently
achieved
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the above
inspections

5 borings utilizing truck mounted equipment and portable rotary
wash equipment The depths of all the borings terminated at 5 0
to 20 0 feet below the existing ground surface In addition

exposed on the stream cut slopes The locations of the borings
and slope logs are shown on the enclosed Site Plans Plates 1
