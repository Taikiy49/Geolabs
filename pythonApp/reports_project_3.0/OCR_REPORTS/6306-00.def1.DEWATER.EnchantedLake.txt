Introduction
This dewatering plan has been prepared for Jas W Glover Ltd Glover and
presents the activities and procedures for the dewatering work required in support of the
new meter vault and underground fuel storage tank for the Enchanted Lake Wastewater
Pump Station Upgrade project in Kailua on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project location
and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1

Project Considerations
The project involves upgrade improvements to the existing Enchanted Lake
Wastewater Pump Station in the vicinity of Akumu Street in Kailua on the Island of Oahu
Hawaii The project involves installation of 8 inch force main pipes new meter boxes and

Regional Geology
The geology of the Kailua area is highly complicated and related to the formation of
the Island of Oahu in its current form The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of
basalt and basaltic lavas from the Waianae and Koolau shield volcanoes The older
Waianae Volcano is estimated to be middle to late Pliocene in age and the younger Koolau
Volcano is estimated to be late Pliocene to early Pleistocene in age
The Koolau Pali is a long escarpment extending from Makapuu at the southern end
of the island to Punaluu in the north Conventional wisdom has been that the Pali was
formed by the coalescence of amphitheater headed valleys in the more erodible rocks of
the Koolau Caldera Complex However there are more recent theories that postulate that
the eastern portion of Koolau faulted and slumped off exposing the caldera complex to the
forces of erosion It is most likely that the Pali is the result of a combination of both faulting
and erosion
During the Pleistocene Epoch Ice Age sea levels fluctuated in response to the
cycles of continental glaciation As the glaciers grew and advanced less water was
available to fill the oceanic basins such that sea levels fell below the present stands of the
sea When the glaciers melted and receded an excess of water became available such
that the sea levels rose to above the present sea level
The processes of erosion and deposition were affected by these glacio eustatic sea
level fluctuations When the sea level was high the erosional base level was raised such
that sediments accumulated at higher elevations When the sea level was low the
erosional base level was correspondingly lower and valleys were carved to depths below
the present
Enchanted Lake appears to be a drowned valley or embayment on the eastern side
of Oahu By the beginning of the early 20th century this embayment had become a lagoon
transitioning to a salt to brackish water marsh Development of the area resulted in filling of
much of the margins of the lagoon marsh

Existing Site Conditions
The project site is at the existing Enchanted Lake Wastewater Pump Station
situated on the southern side of Akumu Street in the Enchanted Lake neighborhood in
Kailua on the Island of Oahu Hawaii Access to the site is through a gate facing
Akumu Street At present the site is occupied by the pump station building a generator
building a small wooden shed an above ground propane storage tank and existing piping
and meter vaults To the west of the site there is an existing drainage channel leading to
the lake to the north The other sides of the site are bounded by residential development
The facility is surrounded by chain link fencing
2 3
Subsurface Conditions
Based on the borings drilled by Fewell Geotechnical Engineering Ltd FGE the site
is underlain by about 27 feet of medium dense to dense granular man made fill overlying
soft lagoonal clays Groundwater was encountered at a depth of about 7 feet below the
ground surface which is approximately Elevation 1 Mean Sea Level MSL Due to the
proximity of the drainage channel and lake to the project site it is anticipated that the
groundwater level will fluctuate some in response to tide and runoff entering the lake

SECTION 3 DEWATERING DESIGN
Based on the contract documents and other available information we anticipate that
the primary dewatering work for this project may be for the installation of the new meter
vault and for the installation of the UST Dewatering may not be necessary depending on
the stability of the excavation and the actual water level encountered This will be Glover s
first approach during excavation operations Should dewatering be necessary Glover s
second approach will be to install a tremie plug in the bottom of the excavation to reduce
the inflow of groundwater into the excavation
The inverts of the replacement force mains are above the known water level
therefore no dewatering is anticipated for their installation Detailed discussions of these
items are presented in the following sections herein
3 1
Pumping Procedures
If necessary the excavations will be dewatered using a inch
centrifugal trash pump with a maximum rated capacity of 555 gallons per minute gpm If
additional pumping capacity is needed to control the water an additional 4 inch pump is
available Pumping will be done only during working hours
The proposed plan dimensions of the new meter vault excavation are approximately
8 5 feet in length by 18 feet in width The base of the vault floor slab will be at about
elevation 0 4 to 1 1 feet MSL Therefore about 8 to 10 feet of excavation is required and
the maximum anticipated drawdown for dewatering is about 3 feet
The proposed plan dimensions of the new UST excavation are approximately 12 feet
in length by 12 feet in width The base of the tie down slab will be at about Elevation 0 0
feet MSL Therefore about 8 feet of excavation is required and the maximum anticipated
drawdown for dewatering is about 2 feet
To achieve the planned dewatering of each excavation Glover will partially excavate
the area and install trench boxes to support the sidewalls of the pit Following installation of
the trench boxes if dewatering is necessary Glover will excavate to about 3 feet MSL to
allow for a 2 foot thick minimum lean concrete tremie plug to control water infiltration from

Surveys and Instrumentation
In accordance with the project specifications Section 209 3 F the following surveys
and instrumentation will be implemented as part of the dewatering work
Damage Survey
Glover will conduct a damage survey of existing buildings and structures including
photographs and crack surveys prior to the start of construction
Inclinometer Installation
Geolabs Inc will install a single inclinometer casing near the existing pump station
structure to monitor for ground deflections This inclinometer will be monitored during
dewatering and for a period of up to two months after completion of the dewatering
work
Settlement Points
Glover will install and monitor settlement points adjacent to existing structures on both
sides of the meter box and force main replacement

Design Concerns
The following subsections discuss the various design concerns associated with the
dewatering operations
Estimated Flow
Generalized calculations indicate that the sustained discharge from the excavations
may be on the order of about 25 gpm per excavation It is anticipated that only
one excavation will be dewatered at any given time Higher rates of discharge are
anticipated during the morning start up of the system since it will be necessary to
empty the pit of water that may have infiltrated during non pumping hours
Construction Location
The project site is the existing Enchanted Lake Wastewater Pump Station which is
situated on the southern side of Akumu Street in the Enchanted Lake neighborhood in
Kailua on the island of Oahu Hawaii Access to the site is through a gate facing Akumu
Street
Space Available
The work area indicated on the project utility plan Sheet C 3A is roughly 50 by 60 feet
in plan dimension totaling about 3 000 square feet Since the surface area required for
the dewatering equipment is minimal space is not a major design consideration
Pollutants
Since the site is situated in a residential area and there has not been a UST at the site
no significant pollutants are believed to be present in the groundwater underlying the
site
With respect to construction related pollutants none are anticipated to be released
during the installation of the meter vault and the UST Glover maintains spill control
