Dear Mr Young
This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations performed during
the mass grading operations for Lot No 11285 of Area5 of Ewa by Gentry West from
October 1998 through August 1999
The subject site is located at the western end of Maohaka Place within the Kula Lei
Subdivision by Schuler Homes Inc in Ewa on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The site is
bounded by Maohaka Place Maohaka Way and Lot No 11260 to the east The site is also
bounded by Lot No 11969 to the north an old railroad track to the north and west and the
Area G 2 of the Coral Creek Golf Course to the west and south
Except for the eastern two thirds of Lot No 11285 this single family subdivision was
previously mass graded for Area 5 of the Ewa by Gentry West development and later sold to
Schuler Homes Inc Lot No 11285 was left at a lower ground elevation than the mass graded
lots to handle flood water emanating from the northwestern side beyond the old railroad track
Following the mass excavation and grading performed for the adjacent Area G 2 of the Coral
Creek Golf Course the drainage path for the floodwater was changed to a more southwesterly
direction rather than the previous southerly direction Therefore grading of Lot No 11285
became possible following the mass excavation and grading of the adjacent Coral Creek Golf
Course
Based on preliminary plans it is our understanding that this 1 061 acre lot may be
subdivided into 5 separate houselots with the 40 foot wide strip of land parallel and area
adjacent to the railroad track to be maintained as a drainageway swale In addition Maohaka
Place may be extended to the western extent of the lot with a turnaround end However the
lot may also be sold as is to potential buyers
The mass grading work for Lot No 11285 of Area 5 of Ewa by Gentry West was
performed by Goodfellow Brothers Inc The mass grading work consisted of fills of about 4 to
6 feet in height to achieve a finish grade of roughly 41 to 42 feet Mean Sea Level MSL
The fill materials were generally generated from the western end of Area G 2 of the adjacent

Prior to fill placement debris and vegetation was removed from the site In addition

brought to above optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of

fill material was then placed in level lifts moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density Coral fill
was utilized in the upper 12 inches from finish grade Coral fill mixed with some clayey
materials was utilized at 12 to 24 inches below grade Coral and clayey materials were utilized
for deeper fills
During the grading period various on site soils were tested prior to use in the field The
maximum dry density and optimum moisture content relationships were established in our
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor and
the test results are summarized on Plate 2 of this report
During the fill placement operations periodic field density tests were performed in
general accordance with the American Society of Testing and Materials ASTM Test
Designation D 2922 Nuclear Gauge Method Results of the compaction tests are
summarized on Plates 3 1 and 3 2 of this report
Our services consisted of part time field observation of the contractor s fill placement
and compaction procedures and confirmation of subgrade soil conditions exposed during the
above grading operations
Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site
survey stakes These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the
degree implied by the methods used Where low test results were noted the areas were
reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained

House Foundations
During house construction we recommend that footing excavations and backfilling
operations be observed by a qualified and licensed geotechnical engineer The following are
general guidelines for house foundations
e Perimeter footings should be embedded a minimum of 12 inches below the
lowest adjacent grade
e Footings constructed near tops of slopes or on sloping ground should be
embedded deep enough to provide a minimum horizontal setback distance of
5 feet measured from the outside edge of the footings to the slope face
Footings should be embedded below a 1H 1V imaginary plane extending
upward from the bottom edge of the utility trench or retaining wall heel
e The bottoms of footing excavations should be lightly compacted to provide a
relatively firm and smooth bearing surface prior to placement of reinforcing steel
and concrete
e If soft and or loose materials are encountered at the bottom of footing
excavations they should be over excavated to expose the underlying firm
materials The over excavation should be backfilled with select granular
material compacted to a minimum 95 percent relative compaction or the footing
bottom may be extended down to the underlying competent material
The area adjacent to slabs should be backfilled tightly against the slab and
graded to divert water away from the slab to reduce the potential of water
ponding around the foundation
Gravel strips should not be installed adjacent to the houses as this prevents
proper drainage away from the house
