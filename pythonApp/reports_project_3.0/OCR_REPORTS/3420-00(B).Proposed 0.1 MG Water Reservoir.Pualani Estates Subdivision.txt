INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Water Reservoir within the Pualani Estates
Subdivision in the area of Kailua Kona of the North Kona district on the island of
Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings and geotechnical engineering recom
mendations resulting from our field exploration and engineering analyses These
geotechnical recommendations are intended for design of foundation and site grading
only The findings and recommendations presented herein are subject to the limitations
noted at the end of this report

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
Hawaii the largest island of the Hawaiian Archipelago covers an area of
approximately 4 000 square miles The island was formed by the activity of five shield
GEOLABS HAWAIl
W O 3420 00 B Page 5
volcanoes These are Kohala which is long extinct Mauna Kea which has had
activities during recent geologic time Hualalai which erupted in 1801 and Mauna Loa
and Kilauea which are both still active
The project site is situated on the western flank of Hualalai Mountain which
makes up the west central part of the island The lava formation encountered at the
site appears to be of pahoehoe flow which spread and ponded as it approached the
ocean Pahoehoe is characterized by a smooth rope like or billowy surface and an
internal structure of vesicular porous rock Cavities are commonly encountered in
pahoehoe lavas These cavities were formed when the lava was still in a molten state
and represent both lava tubes intra flow cavities and inter flow cavities such as
blisters and pockets Lava tubes intra flow cavities are formed when molten lava
drains from the cooling flow leaving a hollow tube like structure which may extend for
a large distance longitudinally along the flow Inter flow cavities are generally smaller
in horizontal extent

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our exploration program consisted of drilling two core borings to depths of about
20 and 21 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate locations of the
borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general our field exploration encountered volcanic rock consisting of medium
hard to hard basalt with layers of clinker materials extending from the existing ground
surface to the maximum depth explored of about 21 feet The basalt formation
encountered ranged from slightly to severely fractured and ranged from slightly to highly
weathered The rock formation encountered included dense and vesicular porous
basalt The clinker layers encountered ranged from about 2 to 3 feet thick and
consisted of dark brown highly weathered basalt rock fragments with variable amounts
of soil content
Voids and or cavities were not encountered in the two borings drilled at the
project site However based on the probing conducted for the residential areas of the
subdivision and our experience in the vicinity of the project site the basalt formation
at the site is anticipated to have voids and or cavities in the formation characteristic of
the pahoehoe type lava flows In addition the vertical and lateral extent of the voids
and or cavities in the rock formation could be highly variable

Groundwater was not encountered in the borings at the time of our field
exploration However groundwater levels can fluctuate depending on factors such as
seasonal rainfall groundwater withdrawal and or injection and other factors

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field exploration the subsurface conditions at the project site
consist generally of basalt rock formation with some clinker layers Based on our
evaluation of the subsurface conditions with respect to the proposed development the
principal geotechnical engineering considerations for the proposed water reservoir
development include the following
1 uniform foundation support for the proposed reservoir
2 probing and grouting of the potential cavities and clinker zones beneath
the foundations and
3 site grading for the proposed project site
We understand that the proposed reservoir structure will be located entirely in
cut areas Based on the results of our field exploration we believe that shallow spread
and or continuous footings may be used to support the proposed reservoir structure
Foundations for concrete water reservoir are highly sensitive to differential movements
Therefore we recommend that the upper 3 feet of the materials below the reservoir

Footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest

Slab On Grade
In general accordance with the requirements of the Department of Water Supply
a minimum 3 foot structural fill layer is recommended below the reservoir tank floor slab
to provide more uniform support The 3 feet of structural fill should consist of 12 inches
of compacted base course over 2 feet of compacted structural fill 6 inch minus
materials for more uniform support The structural fill 6 inch minus materials should
consist of well graded granular material less than 6 inches in maximum dimension
compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction The base course should

reaction of 250 pounds per square inch per inch of deflection p c i may be used for
the compacted base course The thickened edges of slabs adjacent to any unpaved
area should be embedded at least 12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade
Foundation Probing
As previously indicated in order to reduce the potential for loss of foundation
support resulting from collapse of cavities below a footing we recommend that a
program of cavity probing and grouting be conducted during construction For
estimating purposes we recommend that probes be drilled at each spread footing
location and 10 foot on center for continuous footings The probes should be at least
3 inches in diameter and should extend to at least 10 feet below the planned bottom
of footing If cavities and or voids are encountered or suspected during the probing

vertical and lateral extent of the cavity and or void
The probes are generally backfilled to aid in the detection of voids and or

Fill and Backfill Materials
As previously indicated we recommend that a 12 inch base course layer be
used below all footings and slabs for more uniform support The base course
material should consist of crushed basalt aggregate and the gradation of the
material should be in accordance with the requirements of Section 31 of the
Standard Specifications for Public Works Construction September 1986
The fill and backfill materials to be used as general fill material should consist
of well graded granular materials in which the majority portion has the size of
less than 12 inches with an absolute maximum dimension of 18 inches The on
site cut and or stockpiled material if less than 18 inches in maximum dimension
may be used as general fill or backfill materials other than for trench backfill
provided that they are processed to meet the above requirements
The structural fill materials should consist of well graded granular material of
6 inches in maximum dimension with sufficient fines to prevent the occurrence
of voids in the compacted mass If the excavated materials do not contain
sufficient fines to produce a well graded material off site borrow or on site rock
crushing of large sized rock fragments or boulders should be considered to
provide the required gradation and particle sizes to develop a well graded
material Any imported material if necessary should be observed and or tested
by Geolabs Hawaii for its suitability prior to being transported to the site

Core samples of rock materials encountered at the site were obtained using
diamond core drilling techniques in general accordance with ASTM Standard Practice
D 2113 83 Diamond Core Drilling for Site Investigation Core drilling is a rotary drilling
method which uses a hollow bit to cut into the rock formation The rock material left
in the hollow core of the bit is mechanically recovered for examination and description
Recovery REC is used as a subjective guide to the interpretation of the relative
quality of rock masses Recovery is defined as the actual length of material recovered
from a coring attempt versus the length of the core attempt such as if 3 7 feet of
material is recovered from a 5 0 foot core run the recovery would be 74 percent and
would be shown on the Logs of Borings as REC 74
The Rock Quality Designation RQD is also a subjective guide to the relative
quality of rock masses RQD is defined as the percentage of the core run that is sound
material in excess of 4 inches in length without any discontinuities discounting any
drilling induced fractures or breaks If 2 5 feet of sound material is recovered from a
5 0 foot core run the RQD would be 50 percent and would be shown on the Logs of
Borings as RQD 50 Generally the following is used to describe the relative quality
of the rock based on the Practical Handbook of Physical Properties of Rocks and
Minerals
