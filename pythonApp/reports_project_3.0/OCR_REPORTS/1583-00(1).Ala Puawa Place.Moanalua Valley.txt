As requested we have made a preliminary geologic reconnaissance of
the near surface soil conditions below the above referenced site The
purpose of the reconnaissance was to evaluate the general suitability of
the near surface soils for development in order to aid in the appraisal
of the land value
The reconnaissance consisted of a review of available soils and
geologic maps and literature concerning the area b a general geologic
reconnaissance of the project site c a walk through geologic
reconnaissance of the proposed site and d reviewing the tax map plan
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The site is fairly flat with a slight gradient towards the east
from the cul de sac at the end of Ala Puawa Place The area covers
about 8000 square feet

Geology
The project site is located on the eastern flank of the Salt Lake
vent complex of the Honolulu Volcanic Series
As the molten magma ascended through these vents it reacted with
the groundwater generating steam This steam caused explosive eruptions
which deposited low cones of ash material that have consolidated to form
tuff locally referred to as mudrock
The Salt Lake tuff is a younger air laid ash deposit that was laid
down when the sea level was receding below its present level The Salt
Lake eruptive event deposited its material over earlier tuffs probably
from the older Aliamanu crater eruption The Salt Lake tuff is a
fine grained compact rock which breaks into chunky blocks
Soil Conditions
The information on the soils likely to be encountered in the
project area was obtained from the Soil Survey of the Island of Kauai
and Lanai State of dHawaii by USDA Soil
Conservation Service August 1972 From their survey and visual
observations of the ground surface it appears that the area is
underlain by rockland where the exposed rock covers up to 90 of the
area

General
The field engineering and geological reconnaissance indicate that
the site may be suitable for single family residential construction
The natural rock formation or properly compacted fill could provide
adequate bearing for light residential structures and related utilities
The following guidelines may be used for preliminary site
development concept purposes
1 Stockpiles of loose soils and boulders below the building areas
should be removed
2 Volcanic tuff rock is exposed at the surface in several locations
We believe that excavation into the rock can be accomplished by
dozer and ripping equipment
3 I deposits of clayey soils are encountered they should be removed
below building slabs and driveways
Additional Field Exploration
The recommendations in this report are presented as a guide for
planning purposes only A detailed and thorough subsurface
investigation including borings or test pits should be conducted for
the project area when details regarding site grading and structure
design are established
