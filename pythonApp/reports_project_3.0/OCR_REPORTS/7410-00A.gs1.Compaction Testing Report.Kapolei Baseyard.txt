This report summarizes our compaction testing services performed for the Kalaeloa 
Baseyard project on November 18, 2016 in accordance with our revised fee proposal 
dated May 1, 2016. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The project site is located southeast of Barbers Point Harbor in the Kapolei area on 
the Island of Oahu, Hawaii.  A Project Location Map and Site Plan are shown on Plates 1 
and 2, respectively. 
The project involves the construction of an asphalt baseyard consisting of batching 
equipment, loading equipment, portable office trailers, and aggregate stockpiles.  Based 
on the grading plans, site grading consists of cuts up to about 10 feet in height and fills up 
to about 2 feet in height. 
This report provides the results of the compaction testing performed to verify the 
compaction of fills placed at the project site. 
COMPACTION TESTING 
The maximum dry density and optimum moisture content of the fill material were 
established in our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D1557 (Modified Proctor), 
and the test results are summarized on Plate 3.

Field density tests were performed in the fill areas of the project site on November 
18, 2016 to verify the compaction of the fill materials placed. The field density tests were 
performed in general accordance with ASTM D6938 (Nuclear Gauge Method). Results of 
the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plate 4 of this report.  Test locations 
were estimated by pacing and interpolating from the grading plan.  These locations should 
be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used.  A minimum of 
90 percent relative compaction was achieved in the field density tests performed. 
Based on our site observations and the results of the referenced tests, it is our 
opinion that the site grading work for the above-referenced project tested by Geolabs on 
November 18, 2016 was performed in conformance with the general intent of the 
recommendations in the following references. 
1.
Chapter 14 of the Revised Ordinances of Honolulu 1990, as amended.
2.
Report by Geolabs, Inc. entitled, “Geotechnical Engineering Exploration,
Kapolei Baseyard, Kapolei, Oahu, Hawaii", dated June 22, 2018.
LIMITATIONS 
Geolabs assumes no responsibility for any alterations made to slopes or finished 
grades on the above project subsequent to our observation and testing performed on 
November 18, 2016 without our knowledge and written acknowledgment. 
It should be noted that it is not possible to test the fill material placed for the entire 
project. However, we believe that the level of testing provided is reasonable in reducing 
the geotechnical risks to the project. It should be recognized that there are variations in the 
accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the 
earthwork construction and materials. Our services were performed in accordance with 
generally accepted standards of geotechnical engineering practice; no warranty is

does it guarantee construction of any type. The contractor shall retain responsibility for the 
quality of the work, for adhering to the plans and specifications, and for repairing defects
