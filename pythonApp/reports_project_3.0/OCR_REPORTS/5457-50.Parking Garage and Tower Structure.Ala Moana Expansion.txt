At your request and as required by the City and County of Honolulu Geolabs Inc
provided geotechnical engineering observation and consultation services during the drilled
shaft foundation installation for the above project The following letter report summarizes
our geotechnical engineering observations during installation of the drilled shaft
foundations for the Parking Garage and Tower Structure Ala Moana Expansion project in
Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii
Geolabs conducted a geotechnical engineering exploration for the Parking Garage
and Tower Structure Ala Moana Expansion project and our recommendations are
presented in the referenced report The purpose of our geotechnical engineering
observation during construction was to provide engineering consultation and assist in
problem resolutions during construction of the drilled shaft foundation In addition we
provided Special Inspection services by observing the drilled shaft foundation installation
at the Parking Garage and Tower Structure Ala Moana Expansion project for general
conformance with the project plans and specifications
Project Considerations
The new parking garage and tower structure project site which is part of the
Shopping Center Expansion Parcel is located on the mauka side of the Ala Moana Center
in Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii as shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The parking garage and tower structure project site is abutted by the future
Nordstrom Building to the west the existing Ala Moana Pacific Building to the east
Kapiolani Boulevard to the north and Kona Street to the south

The initial development plan consists of a new parking garage with specialty retail
stores at the street level and five upper levels of parking We understand that the parking
garage structure is designed for future construction consisting of two additional podium
levels full garage plan and a 16 story tower structure The new tower structure will have
a footprint of approximately 240 by 60 feet superimposed over the base parking garage
structure with plan dimensions of about 370 by 240 feet
The new parking garage and tower structure are supported on deep foundations
consisting of cast in place concrete drilled shafts Drilled shafts with diameters of 30 and
42 inches and lengths ranging from about 40 to 125 feet were used to support the new
structures As part of the construction requirements two static load tests were conducted
to evaluate and confirm the foundation design
The scope of our work discussed in this letter report includes observation of the
drilled shaft foundation construction for the Parking Garage and Tower Structure
Ala Moana Expansion project only The general contractor for the project is
Hawaiian Dredging Construction Company who also installed the drilled shaft
foundations
Subsurface Conditions
The subsurface conditions across the new parking garage and tower structure
project site may be generalized into three different zones designated as Zone A Zone B
and Zone C as discussed more in detail below
The Ewa portion Zone A of the project site is generally underlain by surface fill
materials consisting of stiff clayey silts extending to depths of about 4 to 8 feet below the
existing ground surface The surface fill materials were generally underlain by lagoonal
deposits consisting of soft sandy silts and loose silty sands and gravel extending to depths
of about 20 to 30 feet below the existing ground surface It should be noted that the
lagoonal deposits encountered at the project site are highly compressible when subjected
to structural loads
Below the highly compressible lagoonal deposits our field exploration within
Zone A of the project site generally encountered some coral ledges of varying thickness
and hardness Most of these coral ledges were encountered within depths ranging from
about 20 to 30 feet below the existing ground surface and is only on the order of about 3 to
5 feet thick Based on the available information it appears that some of the coral ledges
could be on the order of up to about 15 feet in thickness in localized areas
The lagoonal deposits and coral ledges were generally underlain by coralline
detritus materials consisting of medium dense silty coralline sands and gravel with
localized loose and dense zones extending to the maximum depth explored of about
128 5 feet below the existing ground surface In some of the borings drilled by others at

It should be noted that the competent coral ledges encountered at the Ewa portion

The coral ledges are generally absent along the Diamond Head portion Zone C of
the project site due to erosion by an ancient alluvial stream channel The buried ancient
alluvial stream channel is generally oriented longitudinally in a northwest southeast
direction The coral ledge within the channel has been eroded away and has been
replaced with thick sequences of alluvial soils extending to depths of about 130 feet or
more below the existing ground surface The approximate delineations of these three
different zones based on our interpretation of the subsurface conditions are shown on the
Foundation Plan Plate 2

exploration The groundwater levels encountered generally correspond to about

Summary of the Drilled Shaft Installation
As part of the project requirements two sacrificial load test shafts were constructed
and tested Following completion of the load tests the drilled shaft lengths for the project
were revised based on the results of the instrumented load tests The following
subsections provide descriptions of the load test shaft construction and discussions on the
production drilled shaft installation
Load Test Shafts
As part of the project specifications two sacrificial 42 inch diameter load test shafts
were installed at the approximate locations indicated on the Foundation Plan Plate 2 The
load test shafts were excavated from February 23 through March 1 2006 The 42 inch
diameter load test shafts extended to depths of about 110 and 131 feet below the existing
ground surface The drilled shaft contractor lowered the reinforcing cage with an Osterberg
Load Cell and embedment strain gauge instrumentation into the drilled holes and placed
concrete in each of the load test shafts on February 25 and March 1 2006

Temporary casing with a diameter of about 46 inches also were installed to about
36 feet below the ground surface for the 42 inch diameter load test shafts Logs of the
materials encountered during load test shaft excavations and details of the load test shaft
construction are presented in Appendix A
After the load test shafts were installed and cured for at least seven days
Load Test Inc performed the two load tests The 42 inch diameter load test shafts were
loaded to approximately 2 582 and 2 944 kips Complete details of the load test program
were presented in the reports prepared by Load Test Inc
From the load test data we consolidated Zones A and B into one category and
Zone C still exists as the area with the weaker subsurface soil conditions The
demarcation lines between Zones A B and Zone C were confirmed and or adjusted further
in the field based on the observed subsurface conditions Based on the results of the
two load tests conducted we recommended revising the drilled shaft lengths for the
project as shown in the table on Plate 3 to support the allowable compressible loads

Production Drilled Shafts
The drilled shaft contractor installed 208 production shafts from March 27 through
The as built diameters and the lengths of the production drilled shafts
installed for the project are summarized on Plates 4 1 through 4 5 of this letter report
Geolabs representatives observed the drilled shaft excavation placement of reinforcing
cage and concrete operations during installation of the production drilled shafts
In general the production drilled shafts were excavated using a drill
rig equipped with 30 and 42 inch diameter auger core barrel and clean out bucket similar
to the load test shafts Temporary steel casings were installed to depths of about 26 to
36 feet below the ground surface in the production drilled shafts In general 36 and
46 inch diameter temporary casings were used for the 30 and 42 inch diameter drilled
shafts respectively
Because the drilled holes stayed open without significant caving in of the sidewalls
polymer slurry was not used during excavation of some of the production drilled shafts
After excavation of the production shafts to their designated tip elevations the reinforcing
cages were lowered into the drilled holes to the appropriate elevations with the use of a
crane After the reinforcing cage was installed in the drilled hole concrete was placed
through a 4 inch diameter steel tremie pipe starting from the bottom During concrete
placement we observed that at least 5 feet of concrete head was maintained above the
bottom of the tremie pipe In addition sand content measurements were performed on an
as needed basis prior to the concrete placement Where the measurements indicated that
the sand content exceeded one percent Insta Floc slurry flocculent was used to remove
the fine grained solids suspended in the slurry
Due to the presence of groundwater within the drilled shaft holes concrete was
placed in the drilled holes using the tremie method Concrete with slump ranging from 6 to
9 inches was used for the drilled shafts Mid Pacific Testing Services Inc also monitored
the concrete used for the drilled shafts including the performance of concrete slump tests
and compression tests
