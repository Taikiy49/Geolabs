Based on our review of the available rainfall data and monthly precipitation
summaries by the National Weather Service Forecast Office Honolulu NWSFOR the
past six month monitoring period generally experienced normal rainfall conditions at the
project site Extraordinary precipitation events were not experienced Precipitation
generally increased during the winter months of October 2006 through January 2007
In general our interpretation of the earth movement response to elevated rainfall is
that if a period of increased or above normal rainfall is experienced following a prolonged
period of normal or lower than normal rainfall the earth movement rates at some of the
inclinometers tend to increase abruptly following the period of elevated precipitation and
then settle back to normal levels In addition following extended periods of below normal
precipitation earth movements generally decrease slightly
Subsurface Water Monitoring
The monitoring of subsurface water levels groundwater is accomplished by
measuring the level of water head contained within the sixteen individual and nested
piezometers installed in drilled borings at the project site A list of the piezometer
installations for the project is provided in the preceding Table entitled Summary of
Instrument Installations The sixteen piezometers are installed in the borings identified as
Boring Nos P 1 through P 8 as shown on Plate 2
Three piezometers identified as P 2A P 4A and P 8 consist of dedicated vibrating
wire piezometer installations that provide a near continuous record of the water level
encountered in the well casing The remaining 13 piezometers consist of simple standpipe
well casings which are monitored manually on about a bi monthly basis
It should be noted that the standpipe Piezometer P 2B has experienced a blockage
in the pipe at a depth of about 22 feet which has rendered the instrument as
non functional for further seepage monitoring
