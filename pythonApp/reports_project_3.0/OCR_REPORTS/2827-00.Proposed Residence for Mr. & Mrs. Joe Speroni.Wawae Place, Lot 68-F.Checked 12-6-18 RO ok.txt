INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of a geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed residence for Mr and located at Wawae
Place Lot 68 F near the town of Lawai on the island of Kauai Hawaii The site and its
vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering recom
mendations resulting from the field exploration laboratory testing and engineering
analyses These recommendations are intended for foundation design input and may not
be appropriate to form the basis for construction cost estimates nor for construction
bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is currently a vacant lot covered with trees shrubs and bushes
It is proposed to construct a new single family residence on the project site
The proposed residence is to be a two 2 story structure with CMU walls and
concrete slab on grade floors The project will also include construction of a vehicular
driveway A cesspool is being considered for on site sewage disposal
The proposed house location has existing topography sloping towards the west
with ground elevations ranging from approximately 780 to 790 feet above Mean Sea Level
MSL Site grading involving cutting and filling to heights of about 3 to 5 feet deep to
provide finish grades at 784 5 to 785 5 feet above MSL is anticipated

proposed residence

EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is located at Lot 68 F Wawae Place near the town of Lawai on the
island of Kauai The site is situated on a ridgeline bordered by drainageways that feed
into Lawai Stream The elevations at the site range from about 730 to 790 feet above
MSL The area is moderately to densely wooded During the time of our exploration
seepage was not observed in the three 3 drilled borings
The site is approximately rectangular with an access dirt road along its southern
boundary Neighboring homes were noted in the area The western half of the site is
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2827 00 Page 5
densely covered with foliage and several large trees including Hau trees The slope in
this portion of the site trends down to the southwest at approximately two horizontal to
one vertical 2H 1V The eastern portion of the site bounded by the top of the ridge is
less densely vegetated and slopes gently to the southwest at approximately three
horizontal to one vertical 3H 1V
To the east of the ridge the slope drops steeply down to a valley with floor
elevations at approximately 580 feet above MSL The elevation difference between the
ridgeline and adjacent valley floor is about 200 feet Based on visual observation there
was no indications of slope failure in the recent past in the vicinity of the proposed
construction area during our field exploration in January 1992
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling three 3 borings to
depths of 16 5 to 31 5 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate locations
of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general the borings disclosed that the site is underlain by residual soils
consisting of very stiff to hard brown clayey silt to the maximum drilled depth
Groundwater was not encountered in the drilled borings during our field
exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
It is our opinion that the proposed project is feasible from a geotechnical
engineering point of view provided that the recommendations presented below are

followed However the feasibility of on site sewage disposal utilizing a leaching system
is open to question A disposal system with a large storage capacity is recommended
A requirement for frequent removal of sewage from the cesspool should be anticipated
Foundations
Shallow spread and continuous footing foundations may be used to support the
proposed residence
An allowable bearing value of 2 500 p s f can be used for footings bearing on the
in situ very stiff clayey silt or compacted fill A footing embedment of eighteen 18 inches
below the lowest adjacent finished grade is recommended Uplift wind seismic or other
loading conditions may require that this minimum footing embedment be increased
For sloping ground it is critical that the footing embedment be extended deeper
to obtain a minimum 8 foot horizontal set back distance measured out horizontally from
the toe of the footing to the slope face It is also recommended that the building and
footings be set back away from the top of a slope or steep bank a minimum horizontal
distance of about eight 8 feet
The recommended bearing capacity is for total dead plus live loads and may be
increased by one third when temporary loads wind and seismic are considered
Footing settlements under design loadings are anticipated to be on the order of
less than one half 12 inch total and one quarter 4 inch differential
Resistance to lateral loads acting on the proposed structure may be provided by
passive soil pressures and frictional resistance We recommend that an equivalent fluid
pressure of 300 p s f per foot of depth be used to calculate the passive pressure
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2827 00 Page 7
developed against the buried vertical faces of the foundations This assumes that the soil
around the foundation is well compacted A frictional coefficient of 0 8 may be used to
estimate the sliding resistance between the bottom of the footings and the in situ soils
If a footing is located next to a utility trench it should extend to the bottom of the
utility trench to reduce footing settlement due to settlement of the trench backfill
If soft or loose pockets are encountered at the bottom of the footing excavations
they should be over excavated to stiff in situ soil and replaced with granular select borrow
compacted to a minimum of 95 of its maximum dry density as determined in
accordance with procedures described in ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Surface water should be diverted away from the foundations This is to avoid
ponding of surface run off water that may affect the strength and swelling characteristics
of the soils around the foundation
Slab on Grade
Concrete slabs on grade are anticipated for the proposed residence
A minimum 4 inch thick layer of No 3B Fine gravel or ASTM C 33 No 67 cushion
fill should be provided below the slabs In order to protect against moisture infiltration
a plastic vapor barrier may be incorporated below the slabs A minimum of one 1 inch
of moist fine sand on top of the plastic vapor barrier is recommended to help concrete
curing
Laboratory swell tests indicate that the in situ clayey soils have low to moderate
expansion potential when saturated from an air dried condition In order to reduce the
expansive effects of the soils we recommend that a minimum one 1 foot thick capping
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2827 00 Page 8
layer of non expansive capping material consisting of quarry materials such as crusher
waste select borrow or equivalent be placed below the cushion fill layer of the
slab on grade foundations Tne capping material snould be extended a minimum of two
2 feet beyond the edges of the siabs on grade
The non expansive capping material should consist of off site borrow materials
such as select borrow crusher waste or coral material The capping material should be
placed in level lifts not exceeding eight 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to
a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density as determined in accordance with
procedures described in ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 The underlying clayey soil
remaining should be moistened by water sprinkling and then maintained in a moist
condition prior to placement of the capping material
The thickened edge of the slabs can bear on the compacted non expansive fill or
in situ soil The thickened edges should be embedded a minimum of eighteen 18
inches below the lowest adjacent finish grade
Sewage Disposal System
It is Our understanding that the project site is located in an unsewered area and
therefore a leaching system for on site disposal of sanitary waste generated by the
proposed residence is planned
Based on our field exploration and percolation test it appears that the encountered
residual soil has gocd initial absorptive infiltration capacity However when the residual
soil becomes saturated with potential soil swelling expansion with time in the infiltration
process the residual soil may become less permeable and the absorptive capacity will
be reduced drastically Therefore the feasibility of on site sewage disposal by means of
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2827 00 Page 9
a leaching system will be open to question in the long term basis Therefore it is
recommended that a disposal system with a large storage capacity should be considered
It may be necessary that frequent periodic removal of the sludge or wastes from
the system by pumping for off site disposal should be considered to prevent overflowing
when the storage is near its maximum capacity
Concrete Pavements
It is anticipated that concrete pavements will be provided for the driveway and the
floor of the garage for the proposed residence
Based on the assumption that anticipated traffic will be composed of light vehicles
the following pavement section is recommended
6 Inch Concrete
4 Inch Base Course 95 Compaction
10 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
on Compacted Subgrade
Joints are recommended at about eight 8 to ten 10 foot intervals and at
discontinuities in order to control shrinkage and contraction cracks and to allow for some
relative movement of the concrete slabs The length to width ratios of the slabs should
not exceed one and one quarter 1 25 to one 1 Slabs exceeding this ratio or which
are otherwise not rectangular in shape should be provided with welded wire mesh
reinforcement
Site Grading
Some site grading involving cuts and fills will be required for the proposed
construction

At the on set of construction all areas within the grading limits should be cleared
of vegetation loose top soils rubbish and other deleterious materials The resulting
grubbed and spoil material should be disposed of properly off site
All areas in cut which will receive structural elements such as slab on grade or
pavements should after clearing and grubbing be over excavated to a minimum of one
1 foot below finish subgrade level and backfilled with select non expansive capping
material as described The over excavation and backfilling should also extend a minimum
of two 2 feet horizontally beyond the perimeter of the structure of pavement
The exposed subgrade should be scarified to a depth of six 6 inches moisture
conditioned and recompacted to 90 of its maximum dry density as determined in
accordance with procedures described in ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Any soft weak or yielding areas disclosed during the scarifying operations should
be over excavated to firm ground and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with
well compacted engineered fill
Laboratory swell tests indicate that the on site soils have low to moderate
expansion potential Therefore the on site soils should not be used for fill within the
uppermost one 1 foot of the finish subgrade Non expansive capping material or
engineered fill snould consist of select material less than three 3 inches in maximum
dimension less than 25 passing the No 200 sieve and a Plasticity Index less than 10
The material should be free of organic matter debris and other deleterious material
All fills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding eight 8 inches in loose
thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 of their maximum dry density as
determined in accordance with procedures described in ASTM Test Method D 1557 78

Retaining Walls
Low concrete retaining walls may be required to provide different finish grades for
the proposed project The following design guidelines for retaining walls are
recommended

Walls unrestrained at the top 35 c f equivalent

Walls restrained at the top 80 p c f equivalent
at rest condition level fluid pressure
backfill and above groundwater
2 A bearing value up to 2 500 p s f with a minimum footing embedment of
eighteen 18 inches may be used for the wall footing design For sloping
ground the footing embedment should be extended deeper to obtain a

footing to the slope face
3 For lateral resistance design a passive earth resistance of 300 p s f may
be used A base friction factor of 0 3 may also be used to determine the

4 Backfill behind the retaining walls should consist of granular non expansive
material such as select on site sandy material select borrow or No 3B Fine

required The upper twelve 12 inches of backfill should consist of
non expansive impervious material to reduce water infiltration behind the

5 A subsurface drainage system should be utilized behind the walls to protect
against build up of hydrostatic pressure
Drainage
The finished lot grade outside the footings slabs and pavements should be shaped
to shed water away from foundations to avoid ponding conditions Also it is advised that
a gutter system be installed around the residence and that the gutter run off water be
diverted as far away from the foundations and pavements as feasible Excessive
landscape watering near the foundations should also be avoided
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have concrete bottoms and
drains to prevent water from entering the foundation and slab subsoils
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2827 00 Page 13
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the above
recommended foundation construction since ponded water could cause excessive
subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation
excavations should be properly backfilled tight against the footings and slabs immediately
after setting of the concrete to prevent ponding conditions
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained to drain
all surface run off away from the foundations and slabs
Design Review
Preliminary and final drawings and specifications for site grading utility and
foundation construction should be forwarded to the project geotechnical engineer for
review and written comments prior to advertisement for bids This review is needed to
determine adherence to the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein
If this review is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot be considered to be
responsible for misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the footing excavations and backfilling be observed by a
competent geotechnical engineer during construction to see that adequate bearing has
been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings
indicated during our exploration
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and approved by
the geotechnical engineer prior to delivery to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the above
observations
