PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The senior housing project is near the northwestern corner of the intersection of
Iwilei Road and North King Street in Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project
site is approximately bounded by Kaaahi Place to the north North King Street to the east

and Iwilei Road to the south Based on the information provided it is proposed to
construct a thirteen story senior residential building and a four story parking garage The
layout of the new senior housing project is shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
Site grading work generally consisted of cuts and fills on the order of about 2 5 and
4 5 feet thick respectively The project will also include the installation of new drain and
sewer lines during the start up phase In addition the construction phase of the project
will include fine grading work backfilling around pile caps pavement construction among
others The scope of work discussed in this letter report includes observation and
compaction testing during the earthwork and infrastructure installation for the senior
housing development

Jayar Construction Inc performed the site grading operations pavement construction
and utility line installation for the Senior Residence at Iwilei project The site grading work
consisted of subgrade preparation placement and compaction of fills and backfills
pavement construction and utility trench excavation and backfill
Following the demolition of the existing parking lot and site clearing the project site
was raised to the design finish grades with approximately 4 5 feet of fill materials A
settlement
monitoring
program
was
implemented
in
accordance
with
our
recommendations presented in our Addendum to Geotechnical Engineering Report dated
prepared for the project A total of six settlement gauges were installed at
selected locations across the project site to monitor the filled ground settlement The
settlement gauges were surveyed on periodic intervals from through
May 17 2012 The filled ground at the project site has achieved equilibrium prior to the
installation of the new drain and sewer lines
Subsequent to the foundation installation work the subgrades underneath future
slab and pavement areas were scarified to a depth of about 8 inches
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and proof rolled with a
minimum 10 ton vibratory drum roller to obtain a minimum of 90 and 95 percent relative
compaction under the building slab and pavement areas respectively of the maximum dry
density determined in accordance with American Society of Testing and Material
ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor
Fill and backfill materials required for the project generally were placed in near level
lifts brought to above the optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of 90 or
95 percent under the building slab and pavement areas respectively of the maximum dry
density determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor

During the site grading operations samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test
results are summarized on Plate 2
Field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations on an
as need basis to confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures
and soil conditions were noted A total of 367 field density tests including retests of failed
areas were performed using the nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM D 6938
Results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 20 of
this report These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on
constructed fills and backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by
pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should
be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed
to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the earthwork operations during the site grading
pavement and utility line construction for the above project observed and tested by
Geolabs in the period covered by this report were performed in general accordance with
the intent of the project plans and specifications and our geotechnical recommendations
presented in the referenced report dated addendum dated
and subsequent recommendations
LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork were
performed without our observation However the level of earthwork observation and
testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some of the
geotechnical risks to the project It should be recognized that there are variations in the
accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor
earthwork construction and materials
Our services were performed in accordance with generally accepted standards of
geotechnical engineering practice no warranty is expressed or implied Provision of
construction monitoring by an engineer is not insurance nor does it guarantee
construction of any type The contractor shall retain responsibility for the quality of the
work for adhering to the plans and specifications and for repairing defects regardless of
when they are found
