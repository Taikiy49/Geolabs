As requested Geolabs Inc provided geotechnical engineering observation and
consultation services during the micropile foundation installation work for the above
project This letter report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations during
installation of the micropile foundations for the New Elevator at Hermes at the Royal
Hawaiian Center in the Waikiki area of Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The
project location and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
Geolabs Inc provided geotechnical engineering consultation services for the
design of the micropile foundation and our recommendations are presented in the
above referenced report
The purpose of our geotechnical engineering observation during construction was
to provide engineering consultation during the construction of the micropile foundation and
to provide Special Inspection services by observing the micropile foundation installation
and testing at the project site for general conformance with the project plans
specifications and our recommendations
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Hermes store is located at the northwestern corner of Building A of the Royal
Hawaiian Center in the Waikiki area of Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii We
understand that a new elevator is planned in the new Hermes store The elevator will be
located between Gridlines 31 and 32 and H and J The location of the site at Building A is
shown on the Micropile Numbering Plan Plate 2

Due to the soft loose ground conditions at the site and to reduce differential
settlement between the existing building supported on driven concrete piles and the new
elevator the new elevator will be supported on micropile foundations
MICROPILE INSTALLATION AND TESTING
Geotechnical recommendations for the micropile foundations were presented in
our letter report dated as referenced above The purpose of our
geotechnical engineering observation and consultation services was to observe the
micropile foundation installation and testing for general conformance with our
recommendations
Based on the anticipated subsurface conditions at the site the micropiles were
specified to extend through the loose lagoonal deposits and derive load bearing
support from the medium dense to dense coralline deposits
Kelikai Inc installed a total of four production micropiles from July 25 through 31
2018 at the approximate locations shown on the Micropile Numbering Plan Plate 2
Details of the micropile installation are presented in the Micropile Installation Records in
Appendix A
In general the micropiles were installed using a Beretta T 24H drill rig equipped
casing to the specified depth of the micropile to create a drilled hole of at least 5 5 inches
Advancing steel casing to the bottom of the specified depth was required in order to
facilitate the micropile installation
After the drilled holes were extended to the specified depth and cleaned out the
micropiles were initially grouted Grout was batched on site utilizing a colloidal high shear
and high speed mixer equipped with a grout pump The grout mix
consisted of one sack of 94 pound Portland Cement Type I and or II 87 pounds of
masonry sand and 5 gallons of water
The grout was pumped in the cased hole through a PVC tremie pipe starting from
the bottom of the cased hole After sound grout appeared at the top of the cased hole a
reinforcing bar was placed in the cased hole with centralizers
The temporary steel casing was removed one section at a time and regrouted to
the top of the cased hole This process continued until the bottom of the steel casing
approached the specified top of the grout bulb zone
Micropile Testing
In order to evaluate the load carrying capacities of the installed micropiles one
proof test was conducted on a production micropile The proof test was conducted on
Micropile No 1 on August 2 2018 The micropile was loaded to a maximum test load
of 80 kips The maximum test load of 80 kips was held for 10 minutes to evaluate the
creep

CONCLUSIONS
Based on our field observations of the micropile installation and micropile proof test
performed by the subcontractor it is our professional opinion that the specified micropiles
for the New Elevator at Hermes have been installed to provide the design load capacity of
60 kips with tolerable movements
