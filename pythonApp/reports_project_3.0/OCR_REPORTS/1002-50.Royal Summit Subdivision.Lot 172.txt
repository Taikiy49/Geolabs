surface soil conditions at the above referenced lot in order to provide

foundation plans This report summarizes our findings and reconmenda
tions

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The stiff reddish brown clayey silt soil encountered generally can
provide suitable foundation support for the proposed house provided that
some care is exercised during construction to minimize the swelling
potential of the soil
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1002 50 Page 4
Laboratory tests performed on the stiff clayey silt soil indicates
that this material generally has low to moderate swelling potential
depending on the moisture content of the soil
Therefore to minimize effects due to soil swelling under on grade
slabs we recommend that the on site soil be capped with at least 12
inches of non expansive select fill and that the subgrade soils
underneath the select fill and slab be moistened and protected from
drying from the start of construction until the slab concrete placement
Select non expansive soils should also be used as backfill behind
the retaining walls to minimize the additional pressure due to the
swelling potential of the natural soil
Iot_ Grading
About 5 feet of fill is planned at the rear pad of the lot The
available on site material at the Royal Sunmit area generally has
moderate to high swelling potential Therefore if this material is
used it should be kept below 24 inches of the finished lot grade and the
soil should be placed and compacted on the wet side of optimm moisture
content The remaining fi11 should consist of non expansive soil such
as select borrow crusher waste or coral Where cut or minimal fill is
anticipated such as the garage driveway and the lower house areas the
on site soil should be over excavated to provide a 12 inch minimum
thickness of non expansive select fill capping

Slab On Grade
It should be noted that some of the on site silty clay soils are
moderately expansive and are marginally suitable to provide direct slab
support Therefore new fills within 24 inches of the finished lot grade
should consist of non expansive fill and the on site soil within 12
inches below the slab bottom should be over excavated thoroughly
moistened and then replaced with non expansive imported soils as
previously discussed The thickened edge of the slab should be embedded
a minimum of 12 inches below the lowest adjacent finish grade
To further reduce future post construction swelling of the
underlying expansive soils we recommend that the in place silty clay
below the upper capping fill pad be kept moist for one week prior to
concrete placement

Drainage
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bettoms and drains to prevent water fram entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper
performance of the above reccmmended foundations since ponded water
could cause excessive subsurface scil saturation and subsequent heaving
or loss of strength The foundation excavations should be properly
backfilled tight against the wall or slab footing inmediately after
setting of the concrete to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and main
tained to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing
foundation
