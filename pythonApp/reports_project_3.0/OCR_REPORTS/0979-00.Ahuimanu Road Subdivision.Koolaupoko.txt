This report presents the results of our test boring and perco
lation testing at the proposed Subdivision on Ahuimanu Road
in Hawaii
The purpose of this investigation is to obtain preliminary
subsurface soil conditions at the site and to evaluate the in
situ seepage characteristics for private sewage disposal design
and construction
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located in adjacent
to and north of Ahuimanu Road Presently the site is a plant
nursery and is covered with grass shrubs and trees
W O 979 00 P 2
The site is bounded on the south by Ahuimanu Road on the
north and east by residential developments and on the west
by Kahaluu Stream
The site is generally flat except for the eastern half which
has a 30 to 40 foot high bank which Slopes down at approxi
horizontal to vertical towards the lower portion
of the site adjacent to Kahaluu Stream
FIELD EXPLORATION
Three 3 inch diameter auger test borings were drilled to a
depth of 30 feet below the existing ground surface at the
approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate l
The drilling operation was monitored by our field engineer who
also logged the borings and obtained representative soil samples
The borings logs are presented on Plates A 1 through A 3
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface exploration disclosed uniform soil conditions at
the site
Boring No 1 drilled in the lower portion of the site near
Kahaluu Stream encountered 14 5 feet of brown clayey silt over
river deposits consisting of silty sand cobbles and gravel to
30 feet the depth drilled Water level was encountered in
this lower area at about 8 foot depth

Borings No 2 and No 3 drilled in the upper portion of the
site encountered brown clayey silt with decomposed rock to 31 5
feet the depth drilled Water level was encountered in
Borings No 2 and No 3 at 29 and 28 foot depths respectively
PERCOLATION TESTS
Falling head percolation tests were performed in the three
drill holes
After completion of the bore hole drilling and sampling each
hole was filled with water and allowed to saturate overnight
The next day each bore hole was refilled with water and the
water allowed to percolate through the subsoil The drop of
water level was monitored and recorded The test data is
summarized on Plate C
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the percolation test results from the three drilled
bore holes it is our opinion that the underlying soils at the
site could provide adequate percolation rates for a properly
designed private cesspool sewage disposal system
Based on the percolation test data obtained in the drilled
holes a 6 foot diameter cesspool extending to a minimum of
20 to 25 foot depth in the upper portion of the site Borings
2 and 3 could provide a sufficient seepage rate for private
