Site Description
The project site is located at the Mililani Mauka Commercial B development in the
Mililani area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The development site is bounded by
Ukuwai Street to the northwest Ainamakua Drive to the northeast and Meheula Parkway
to the south and east
The development site currently consists of a McDonald s fast food restaurant on the
southeast side a Tesoro gas station on the west side and a recently completed office
building on the southwest corner An access road from Meheula Parkway to
Ukuwai Street traverses the middle portion of the site in a south north alignment The
majority of the development site is covered with grass and some trees were observed
along the eastern property line
The existing Ainamakua Drive is a two way City Street traversing in a
southeast northwest direction The northwest bound direction generally consists of
two travel lanes at the intersection with Meheula Parkway and transition to an additional
left turn lane at the intersection with Ukuwai Street The southeast bound direction
generally consists of two travel lanes at the intersection with Ukuwai Street and transition
to a right turn lane a thru lane and a left turn lane at the intersection with
Meheula Parkway The existing pavements appear to be in generally fair to good
condition with no structural defects observed The ride characteristic of the pavement was
generally fair to good
We understand that mass grading of the development site was completed in 2009
A portion of the development site was recently covered with undocumented fills consisting
of materials generated during the construction of the recently completed office building
Based on the updated topographic survey map provided the existing ground surface
elevations across the project site generally range from about 764 to 774 feet Mean Sea
Level MSL
Subsurface Conditions
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by drilling and sampling
two additional borings designated as Boring Nos 101 and 102 extending to a depth of
approximately 11 5 feet below the existing ground surface In addition two bulk samples
of the near surface soils designated as Bulk 101 and Bulk 102 were collected to evaluate
the pavement support characteristics of the near surface soils The approximate boring
and bulk sample locations are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who monitored the drilling operation on a
near continuous basis Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified
Soil Classification System as shown on the Soil Log Legend Plate 3 Graphic
representations of the materials encountered are presented on the Logs of Borings
Plates 4 and 5

Laboratory Tests
A one inch Ring Swell test was performed on a relatively undisturbed sample of
the in situ clayey soils to evaluate the swelling potential of the subsurface soils The
test results are summarized on Plate 6
Two Atterberg Limits tests ASTM D 4318 were performed on selected soil
samples to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits The test results are summarized on the
Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths Graphic presentation of the test
results is provided on Plate 7
Two laboratory California Bearing Ratio tests ASTM D 1883 were performed on
bulk samples of the near surface soils to evaluate the pavement support characteristics
of the soils The test results are presented on Plates 8 and 9
Two Modified Proctor compaction tests ASTM D 1557 were performed on bulk
samples of the near surface soils to evaluate the dry density and moisture content
relationships The test results are presented on Plates 10 and 11
Discussion and Recommendations
Our field exploration generally encountered very stiff to hard clayey silts extending
to the maximum depth explored of approximately 11 5 feet below the existing ground
surface We did not encounter groundwater in the shallow drilled borings at the time of our
field exploration
A flexible pavement section consisting of 2 5 inches of AC on 4 inches of asphalt
concrete base over 6 inches of aggregate base course may be used for the design of the
new right turn lane at Ainamakua Drive The excavated subgrades underneath pavement
areas should be scarified to a depth of about 8 inches moisture conditioned to within
2 percent above the optimum moisture content and recompacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative compaction The aggregate base course material should be
moisture conditioned to near the optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative compaction

Ainamakua Drive Widening
We understand that a new right turn lane will be constructed on the southeast
bound direction of Ainamakua Drive between Meheula Parkway and Ukuwai Street as
part of the project development The following subsections summarize our engineering
analyses and present our preliminary pavement design recommendations for the new
right turn lane at Ainamakua Drive
Methodology of Pavement Design
The design procedure used in determining the new flexible pavement section
discussed herein is based on the Guide for Design of Pavement Structures 1993
developed by the American Association of State Highway and Transportation
Officials AASHTO
Design Traffic Loading Conditions
In accordance with the City s latest requirements new flexible pavements should
be designed for a minimum life of 40 years based on anticipated traffic Based on a
pavement life of 40 years a total Equivalent Single Axle Loads ESALs of
5 4 million was provided by AECOM Technical Services Inc for our pavement
analyses A design lane factor of 0 75 was adopted in the determination of the total
ESALs used in our pavement analyses
Design Subgrade Conditions
Based on our field exploration the proposed new right turn lane alignment is
generally underlain by very stiff to hard clayey silts near the existing ground
surface Laboratory tests performed on the near surface clayey materials
indicated a CBR value of 29 9 and 30 2 and a swell of less than 1 percent The
laboratory CBR test results are presented on Plates 8 and 9 Based on the
subsurface conditions encountered and the laboratory test results a design CBR
value of 30 was adopted for the pavement subgrade material in our pavement
analyses

Design Pavement Section
Based on our pavement analyses we believe that the following new pavement
structural section may be used for preliminary design and cost estimating
purposes Detailed calculations of the pavement design section are presented on
Plate 12
2 5 Inches Asphaltic Concrete
4 0 Inches Asphalt Concrete Base 92 Percent Relative Specific Gravity
6 0 Inches Aggregate Base Course 95 Percent Relative Compaction
12 5 Inches Total Pavement Thickness on Moist Compacted Subgrade
The pavement subgrades should be scarified to a depth of about 8 inches
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative compaction The aggregate base course material should consist of
crushed basaltic aggregates compacted to not less than 95 percent relative compaction
CBR and field density tests should be performed on the actual subgrade soils encountered
during construction to confirm the adequacy of the above section
In addition paved areas should be sloped and drainage gradients should be
maintained to carry the surface water off the site Surface water ponding should not be
allowed on the site during or after construction Where concrete curbs are used to isolate
landscaping in or adjacent to the pavement areas we recommend extending the curbs a
minimum of 2 inches into the soils below the aggregate base course layer to reduce the
potential for migration of appreciable landscape water into the pavement section
Alternatively a subdrain system could be constructed to collect the excess water from
landscaping irrigation For long term performance we recommend that a subdrain system
be considered adjacent to the paved landscaped areas
Storm Water Runoff Disposal System
As part of the project construction a retention pond storage system is planned at
the easternmost corner to accommodate the storm water runoff from the site We have
evaluated the permeability of the in situ soils by performing a falling head permeability test
designated as P 1 at Boring No 102 as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The permeability
test was conducted at about 9 8 feet below the existing ground surface
