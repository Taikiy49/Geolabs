INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our soil investigation
for the proposed Off Site Sewer and Pump Staiton A for
the Nawiliwili Coral Fill Industrial Subdivision located
in
The purpose of this investigation was to evaluate the sub
surface conditions at the site to provide soil data for
the design and construction cost estimate of the proposed
sewer line
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P wf
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located along the flat waterfront area
of Nawiliwili Harbor
The project alignment begins on Waapa Road near Honolulu
Iron Works and follows Waapa Road in an easterly direction
In this portion the sewer line runs between a steep rock
Slope on the north and the harbor on the south Some ware
houses and gas tanks are located near the beginning of the
alignment
The alignment then follows Mokihana Street still following
an easterly direction The alignment intersects and then
follows Waapa Road heading in a northerly direction The
project alignment ends near the southwestern corner of the
intersection of Waapa Road and Wilcox Road where the sewer
line enters the proposed sewage Pump Station A
Except for the western portion of the alignment where the
steep rock slope was noted the surrounding area is generally
level and occupied by warehouses and tank structures
The elevations at the site vary from about 5 to 10 feet

W P 3
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed project consists of about 3400 feet of sewer
line and a sewage pump station
The sewer line will consist of 10 to 18 inches in diameter
pipes with inverts varying from 11 to 20 feet below the
existing ground The sewage pump station facility consists
of a wet well and pumping station extending about 25 feet
below the ground surface a generator building at ground
surface and on A C parking area
The proposed alignment will generally be located within
Waapa Road and Mokihana Street
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The area generally consists of alluvium and calcareous
sediments The surrounding higher areas are essentially
Kaloa volcanic deposits of early Pleistocene Epoch
During the Pleistocene Epoch stream valleys and sea cliffs
were eroded to a base level of up to 100 feet below the
existing sea level Beaches of calcareous sand were formed
and blown inland to form dunes As the sea rose around the
island the valley mouths were alluviated
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P 4
Near the beginning of the alignment in the western portion
of the site the angular boulder fill encountered was pro
bably derived from construction and quarry Operations cutting
into the existing rock ridge The rounded boulder material
encountered is the result of the natural fracturing of the
rock ridge material and subsequent weathering action by the
ocean
Subsurface Exploration
Seven borings were drilled at the site to depths of 7 to
55 feet to explore the subsurface soil conditions and to
obtain samples for laboratory testing Three test pits
were dug in the western portion of the alignment to observe
the condition of the boulder fill material near the ground
surface The approximate locations of the borings and test
pits are shown on the Site Plan Plates 2 3
Detailed information on the field exploration and laboratory
testing is presented in the Appendix along with the boring
and test pit logs and laboratory test results
Soil Conditions
In the western portion of the site the test pits and borings
encountered up to 3 feet of sand over cobbles and boulders
to 6 to 10 foot depths

W P 5
The borings in the rest of the site encountered about 7 to
8 feet of beach sand over medium density gray silty sand
and coral down to depths of 12 to 23 feet Below this
in Boring Nos 2 and 4 loose silty sand was encountered
In Boring Nos 1 and 3 stiff brown clayey silt was encoun
tered at 17 to 23 foot depths
Water was noted generally between about elevation 2 feet
to 1 foot in the borings
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
In general fair to poor subsurface conditions were encoun
tered along the proposed alignment Medium to loose silty
sand deposits were encountered at the invert level along
most of the alignment Boulder fill deposits and rock
were encountered in the western portion of the site
The silty sand deposits will be relatively easy to excavate
However heavy ripping or other special excavation methods
may be required in the rock and boulder fill deposits
Throughout the alignment the presently proposed invert
level will be below the water level and will require de
Watering
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P 6
Bearing Capacity and Settlement
The loose sands encountered below most of the site generally
have low bearing capacity However the proposed sewer line
construction will not materially increase the vertical load
ing upon these subsoils if no additional fills or loads are
placed in the future above the existing ground
Since the net increase in load on the subsoils caused by the
Sewer construction will be small the resulting settlements
should also be relatively small if the sewer line is properly
constructed
Small settlements are anticipated from the sewer construction
in the boulder fill area
Pipe Bedding
The stress distribution against the bottom of a pipe has a
great effect upon the load supporting capacity of the pipe
therefore the pipe bedding is an important design considera
tion
Due to the very loose and soft subsoils encountered at the
proposed sewer invert level a two foot thick crushed hse
cradle is recommended for the sewer line The rock cradle
material should be compacted and brought up to within six
inches of the invert grade
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Pad
A minimum of six inches of compacted granular bedding
material should be placed upon the crushed rock cradle
and the pipe placed on a flat trench bottom The gran
ular bedding should extend beyond the pipe sides a mini
mum width of one fourth the outside pipe diameter and
Should extend halfway up the pipe sides The remainder
of the trench backfill should be in accordance with the
Standard Specifications for Public Works Construction
May 1975 The recommended bedding section is illustrated
On the enclosed Plate 4
In the western portion of the alignment where the boulder
fill material is encountered the two foot rock cradle can
be reduced to a one foot thickness
Sewer Construction
Excavation
The method and equipment used for excavation should
be left to the contractor The anticipated depths
of excavation will vary from about 11 to 20 feet
Some hard ripping may be necessary for the western
portion of the alignment where rock is encountered
The remainder of the alignment will be through loose
sands and should be relatively easy to excavate
GEOLABS HAWAII
W
Ps
Sheeting and bracing is recommended for the project
to prevent a cave in of the trench walls The sheet
ing should be driven into the stiff clayey silt layer
or to a sufficient depth into the loose sands to
resist the lateral loads on the trench walls In
the western portion of the alignment it will be
very difficult to drive the sheeting through the
encountered boulder layer or into the rock formation
Special procedures will be required during excavation
and the placing of the sheeting in this area If
practicable we recommend that the sewer invert be
raised in this area to reduce construction difficulty
The excavation should be made in short sections and
backfilled as soon as possible
Heavy equipment and stockpiles should be kept away
from the tops of the open trenches
Settlements of the adjacent ground due to the trench
excavation operations can be minimized by preventing
loss of ground and by introducing points of Support
for the side walls as soon as practicable and at
appropriate vertical spacing

W P o
Groundwater was encountered above the proposed invert
level throughout the alignment
The method of dewatering should be left open to the
contractor provided adequate stability of the exca
vation is maintained
The deposits encountered in the borings consisted
mostly of beach sand and silty sand with coral in
the eastern portion of the alignment and boulder
fill in the western portion
The project dewatering operations should be done
without pumping out the soil fines pumping clear
water only
The dewatering should be coordinated with the
excavation Support system so that the stability
of the excavation is not jeopardized and existing
structures along the alignment not damaged The
Operations should be carried out without creating
a quick condition or softening the bottom of
excavation
The boulder fill material can supply large quantities
of water to the excavation Special dewatering methods
may be required for this area
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Ps
An alternative to dewatering would be to place
the pipe in a wet trench condition
Backfill
Select granular material should be used as back
fill for the sewer line trench The on site sands
and boulder fill material generally less than 6
inches in size can be utilized for the backfill
The backfill should be compacted to a minimum of
90 per cent of the maximum dry density established
by ASTM D 1557 The space left by the removal of
the sheeting should also be backfilled Proper
compaction of the trench backfill material will
reduce future settlements of the backfill and the
adjacent ground surface
Sewage Pumping Station
The pumping station and wet well will extend about
25 feet below the ground surface probably resting
on medium density silty sand or stiff clayey silt
Recommendations given for the excavating and dewatering
Operations for the sewer line should be followed
An allowable bearing value of 1500 p s f can be used
on the medium density silty sand or stiff clayey silt
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P 1
Generator Building
For the light one story building spread footing foundations
or slab on grade can be used
The vegetation should be removed and the existing sandy
subsoils recompacted to a minimum of 90 per cent of its
maximum dry density
An allowable bearing value of 2000 p s f can be used on
the recompacted sandy soils
The perimeter footings should extend a minimum of 2 feet
below the finish grade
Pavement
Based on the laboratory CBR test and the very light anti
cipated traffic the following pavement section is recommended
2 inch Asphaltic Concrete
4 inch Base Course
6 inch Total Pavement Thickness
The sandy subgrade should be compacted to a minimum of 90 per
cent of its maximum dry density
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
Field inspection and engineering observation during the sewer
construction are important to permit correlation between the
preliminary soil data obtained during this investigation and
the actual soil conditions encountered during construction

by drilling 7 borings to depths of 7 to 55 feet below the

3

Classification System A graphic presentation of the soils
is presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 8
