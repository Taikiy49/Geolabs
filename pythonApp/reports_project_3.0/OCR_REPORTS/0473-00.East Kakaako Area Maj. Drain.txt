I INTRODUCTION
A General
This report presents the results of our soils engineering
investigation for the proposed East Kakaako Area Major
Drain Project
The study consisted of subsurface borings laboratory test
ing and engineering analyses as outlined in our proposal
dated and in general accordance with our
contract
B Project Description
The proposed alignment of the variable size concrete box
drain extends approximately 5460 feet from South and King
Streets along Kapiolani Boulevard Cooke Halekauwila
Coral Ilalo and Keawe Streets to the vicinity of Kelikoi
Street Honolulu Hawaii The location of all borings and
alignment are shown on Plate 1 of this report
The proposed structure varies in size from 12 5 feet x 9 feet
at the makai end down to 8 feet x 8 feet at the mauka end of
the project Also included is a small stretch of 48 inch
RCP lateral drain between Keawe Street and Coral Street on
Halekauwila Street

II FIELD EXPLORATION
The field exploration consisted of 14 borings ranging in depth
from 20 to 40 5 feet The drilling was accomplished using
truck mounted rotary drilling equipment with continuous flight
hollow stem auger The borings were continuously inspected by
an engineer or geologist from our firm The approximate boring
locations are shown on Plate 1 and the boring logs are presented
in Appendix A
In all borings either the Standard Penetration Test or 2 5 inch
O D ring samples were taken at selected depth intervals not
exceeding 5 feet The Standard Penetration Test consists of
driving a 2 inch 0 D split spoon sampler 18 inches with a
140 pound hammer free falling a distance of 30 inches The
number of blows required to drive the sampler the last 12 inches
is termed the Standard Penetration Resistance N and is an
approximate measure of the relative density or consistency of a
soil These resistance values are plotted on the boring logs
Samples obtained were visually classified in the field and
representative material was placed in air tight containers and
returned to our laboratory for inspection and testing

Groundwater
Groundwater along the proposed alignment of the box drain was
approximately 3 feet to 7 feet below existing grade Due to
the close proximity of the harbor the groundwater over at
least the southerly one third of the project length is believed
to be associated with sea level
The design and construction of the box drain will be affected
by the relatively high groundwater table since the proposed
invert varies from approximately 5 to 9 feet below the en
countered groundwater table

Loads
Loads on buried conduits are influenced by width of the
excavation unit weight of backfill material or submerged
weight and frictional resistance between backfill material
and trench walls
Gravity earth forces increase with the width of the excava
tion trench We therefore recommend that the trench
width be held to a minimum
The box walls should be designed for a minimum horizontal
equivalent fluid pressure of 90 psf due to the high water
table and uplift must be considered in the design

Pile Support
Piles are recommended for supporting the proposed conduit
in the area underlain by soft compressible bay mud deposits
between Borings B 8 and B 14 The section of the align
ment concerned begins in the vicinity of the intersection
of Auahi Street Coral Street and extends along the proposed
alignments to the southerly terminus of the project in the
vicinity of the intersection of Keawe Street and Kelikoi
Street Piles should be driven to bear on satisfactory coral
algal reef deposits beneath the soft bay muds
Subsurface conditions along this segment are somewhat variable
therefore pile lengths capacity and driving behavior may
change considerably Generally the pile lengths will increase
in the direction of the harbor southerly direction Because
of the variables involved a minimum pile capacity necessary
to support the proposed drain at the desired spacing should
be determined The piles should then be driven in accordance
with the Uniform Building Code Section 2908 b until the
desired Capacity is reached In the vicinity of Borings B 11l
and B 12 we believe a portion of barrier reef and or patch
reef exists which may allow the drain line to be founded
normally in this area if the areal limits of the reef can be
determined with certainty It is recommended that when the
excavation is made in this local area the areal extent of
the reef deposits along the trench be established to determine

Bedding
XN
Stress distribution along the bottom of the structure can
greatly affect design If the box is designed as a very
rigid structure local irregularities can be spanned It
is however desireable to spread a one foot thick bedding
gravel blanket beneath the box structure prior to placing
concrete to provide more uniform conditions The proposed
invert between Borings B 1 and B 3 and in the vicinity of
B 7 is 1 to 2 feet above the medium hard to hard Coral Algal
Reef deposits It is anticipated that the thickness of the
invert slab will result in the structure being founded on
the reef deposits in these areas Local areas may require
minor over excavation to eliminate small soft spots above
the reef deposits
Generally standard specifications of the governing agencies
should be followed
Construction
1 Excavation
Construction methods and equipment used for trench
excavation should be left up to the contractor within
practical limits The maximum depth of excavation for
this project will be in the neighborhood of 16 to 17 feet

The locally hard Coral Algal Reef encountered along some
portions of the alignment may be difficult to excavate
therefore local hard ripping should be anticipated
However as presently designed the maximum depth of the
proposed invert into hard coral algal reef deposits
varies from 0 to 3 feet between Borings B 4 and B 6 and
the depth in the aforementioned local area around B 11
is about 8 feet
The remainder of the alignment will be easy to excavate
however sheeting and bracing will probably be required
where bay mud and sand deposits are encountered
Excavated material should not be stock piled within
10 feet or a distance equal to one half the depth of
excavation whichever is greater from the edge of the
trench
Sheeting and Bracing
Sheeting and bracing is recommended in the area where
sand and bay muds soft clay silt and clayey sands
were encountered to prevent cave in of the trench wall
and subsidence of adjacent areas and to limit the
extent of excavation
Sheeting and bracing should comply with all applicable
safety requirements The design and safety of the

of the contractor since his representatives are at the

which might affect stability
The design of the support system should be based on the
Apparent Earth Pressure Diagram for the respective soils
shown on Plate 2 These values do not include loads due
to surcharges The side slope for any unsupported exca
vations in sand should not exceed 2 horizontal to 1 vertical
Dewatering
Groundwater was encountered in all of the borings above
the presently proposed invert elevation
Dewatering of the excavation will therefore be necessary
and should be continuous for concrete placement and as
long as necessary to ensure a safe and satisfactory
installation Dewatering may be very difficult near the
southern terminus of the project because of the close
proximity of the harbor Precast structures may be
adaptable to this reach The method of dewatering should
be left open to the contractor provided excavation sta
bility and satisfactory drawdown are maintained Dewatering
method and rates utilized should be such that surrounding
settlements are minimized

Backfill
Backfill material around the sides of the conduit should
consist of selected free draining well graded material
and should be placed by hand or suitable equipment and
compacted to a density consistent with design assumptions
90 to 95 compaction is recommended by jetting or
mechanical equipment
Because to top of the box will be close to grade the
backfill above the box should also be compacted to mini
mize subsidence The on site materials are generally
suitable for this backfilling Compressible organic
materials rubbish debris and other objectionable
material should not be used for backfill Rock greater
than 8 inches in diameter should not be permitted in the
backfill
Prior to repaving the backfill above the box drain
should be constructed in a manner corresponding the
design assumptions for the subgrade usually 90 compac
tion with 95 compaction for the top 6 inches
Before and during the backfilling operation precautions
should be taken to prevent floatation of pipe due to
the entry of the large quantities of water into the
excavation

G Settlements
Settlements of the conduit constructed as recommended in
this report should be minimal provided good construction
practices are followed
Settlements of ground adjacent to the excavation can be
avoided by preventing ground losses due to oversteepened
Slopes or inadequate shoring and bracing
Investigation Limitations
The materials encountered along the project and utilized in our
laboratory testing program are believed to be representative of
the materials that will be encountered along the proposed box
drain alignment Although groundwater levels as shown on
the boring logs were encountered during the exploration on the
dates indicated it should not be construed as representing
the possible groundwater conditions that may be encountered
during construction
Since our investigation is based upon the observed soil and
groundwater conditions selective laboratory testing and
engineering analyses the conclusions and recommendations are
professional opinions These opinions have been derived from
the requested borings in accordance with current standards of
practice and no warranty is expressed or implied

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
LABORATORY TESTING
A laboratory testing program was developed to establish the
engineering properties of the soils and rocks encountered at
the project site The tests where applicable to the subject
project
are included in this Appendix
1 Classification Tests
a
bd
c
Visual Classification All soil samples obtained from
the borings were brought to our laboratory where they
were visually reclassified to confirm or modify the
field classification prior to finalizing the Logs of
Borings
Water Content Determinations In addition to visual
classification typical samples were tested for natural
water content as an aid in soil classification and in
evaluating soil properties The water content values
_are based upon the dry weight of the soil
Atterberg Limits Atterberg limit tests were performed
on selected clay and silt samples obtained from the
borings for the purposes of identification and correla
tion of soils Standard procedures ASTM D424 for the
plastic Limit and ASTM D 423 for the liquid limit were
used in the performance of these tests
1
d Grain Size Analysis Sieve analysis were performed on
granular soils For fine grained soils the hydrometer
test was performed to determine the distribution of
the grain sizes beyond the No 200 sieve
2 Shear Strength Tests
a Unconfined Compression Tests Intact core and split
spoon soil samples which apparently exhibited high
plasticity were tested for unconfined strength Length
to diameter ratio of 2 was maintained throughout the
tests to avoid scatter of test results and to facilitate
evaluation of shear strength values for design purposes
b Direct Shear Test In general soil samples were tested
for shear strength using a 2 41 I D direct shear box
Each set consisted of at least two tests each test run
under different normal loads The results are plotted
as Normal Stress vs Shear Strength with a failure envelope
drawn to determine values of cohesion c atid angle of
frictional resistance 9
3 Consolidation Test Consolidation tests were performed on
selected undisturbed ring samples of the compressible
soils to provide basic data for making settlement calculations
Porous stones were placed on the top and bottom of the samples
to allow drainage Vertical loads were applied in increments
with each load increment being allowed to consolidate prior
2
to adding the next increment Measurements of the time
and consolidation were obtained during each load increment
and rebound was measured during the unloading portion
Consolidation test results are plotted in terms of percent
settlement versus applied loads
Swell Test The ring sample was placed between porous
stones and a 60 psf load was applied to the sample for a
24 hour period The difference between the initial and
final sample heights is the amount of swell which is expressed
as a percentage of the initial height of the sample
Maximum Density Test Samples of soils are compacted in 4
mold of given size with a 10 pound rammer dropped 18 inches
to determine the relationship between moisture content and
density of the soil At least three trial points at dif
ferent moisture content are run to determine the maximum
density and optimum moisture content The test is run in
accordance with ASTM D 1557 70
