The attached specifications allow for the use of oversize materials in controlled fill
situations It is of paramount importance that the parties involved are aware that oversize
materials placed within future utility corridors may present difficulty in future excavations
of the corridors Therefore it is strongly recommended that the use of oversize materials
be restricted to areas which will not require future excavation
lf the use of oversize materials is unacceptable from a construction planning point
of view i e the future utility and or excavation areas are presently unknown or the future
excavation of oversize materials placed as fill in these areas is not desired the entire area
to be graded should be treated as such and the use of oversize materials limited or
prohibited
The above reference specifications are presented with the understanding the
recommendations are tentative based upon future geotechnical exploration The
specifications are forwarded contingent with the premise that Geolabs Hawaii will perform
a geotechnical engineering exploration at the project site to verify the assumptions upon
which the document is based At the completion of the exploration revisions and or
additions may be made in the specifications which will reflect the actual conditions in the
field

A General Site Fill
General fills shall consist of well graded materials with particles not
exceeding two 2 feet in maximum dimension above five 5 feet below
subgrade and not exceeding three 3 feet in maximum dimension below the
five 5 feet from finish subgrade
B Structural Fill
Structural fills shall consist of well graded materials with particles not
exceeding twelve 12 inches in maximum dimension and should be
sufficiently well graded to prevent the occurrence of voids in the compacted
mass
C Retaining Wall Backfill
Backfill behind retaining walls shall consist of free draining well graded
crushed rock with a maximum particle size of 8 inches The final two feet
of backfill should consist of materials sufficiently well graded to create a
dense choking layer upon compaction and shall be graded such that the
occurrence of voids in the compacted mass is prevented
