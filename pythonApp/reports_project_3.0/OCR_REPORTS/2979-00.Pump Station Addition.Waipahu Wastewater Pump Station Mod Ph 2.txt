GEOLOGY
Regional
The island of Oahu is composed largely of the weathered remnants of two extinct
shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae forms the bulk of
the wesiern third of the island while the younger shield Koolau forms the majority of the
eastern two thirds of the island it is believed that Waianae became extinct while Koolau
was still active and its eastern flank is partially buried below Koolau lavas in central Oahu
Local
The project site is located along the southern flank of Koolau Its geomorphology
and subsurface conditions are directiy related to glacio eustatic fluctuations of sea level
during the Pleistocene epcch and the genesis of Pearl Harbor and the Honolulu Coastal
Plain The subsurface conditions at the project site are dominated by fossil Pleistocene
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2979 00 Page 6
age coral algal reef deposits and related deposits laid during high stands of the sea that
are intercalated with terrigenous sediments The base of the stratigraphic section in this
area is Koolau Basalt
The Waianae and Koolau volcanic shields were built during the late Pliocene and
early Pleistocene by thinly bedded basaltic lava flows The main shield building activities
ceased approximately 5 million years ago Evidence from drilled wells indicate that
the island of Oahu has subsided by as much as 1200 feet since the cessation of this early
volcanic activity Macdonald and Abbott 1970
During that period of subsidence coral algal reefs began to grow on the southern
coast of Oahu forming broad bays with barrier reefs across its mouth The growth of the
reefs essentially kept pace with the rate of subsidence A series of lagoons formed
behind the barrier reefs and both terrigenous and marine sediments accumulated in the
lagoons Macdonaid and Abbott 1970
During the Pleistocene Ice Age there were rnany sea level changes as a result
of widespread glaciation in the continental areas of the world As the great continental
glaciers advanced the level of the ocean fell since there was less water available to fill the
oceanic basins Conversely as the glaciers melted global sea levels rose because more
water was available The land mass of Oahu remained essentially stable during these
changes anc the fluctuations were eustatic in nature These glacio eustatic fluctuations
resulted in stands of the sea that were both higher and lower relative to present sea level
on Oahu
The higher sea level stands resulted in the accumulation of deltas and fans of
terrigenous sediments in the heads of the old bays accumulation of reef deposits at
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2979 00 Page 7
corresponding higher elevations and lagoonal marine sediments in the quiet waters
protected by fringing reefs
The concurrent growth of reefs and lagoonal sediments may have also resulted in
deposits of coral algal limestone and maris within the predominantly lagoonal sedimentary
units
The lower sea stands caused streams to carve valleys in the sediments and reef
deposits Subaerial exposure of the sediments and calcareous materials caused
desiccation of the soft deltaic materials and lagoonal deposits and induration of the
calcareous reef materials In addition renewed subaerial erosion of upper areas of the
volcanic dome deposited terrigenous alluvial soils under relatively high energy conditions
During periods of no significant sea level changes continued stream action
extended the ailuviai deitas and fans seaward and deposited alluvium over the lagoonal
sediments
About 15 000 years ago a relatively rapid rise in sea level occurred During that
rise the deep valleys in the project area were submerged Within the last 10 000 years
sea level has adjusted to its present stand Terrigenous and marine sediments have
continued to accumulate in low energy estuarine or lagoonal environments resulting in
thick deposits of soft harbor sediments along the coast in areas that were formerly
valleys and drainageways Along the northerly fringe of Pearl Harbor brackish water
marshes developed resulting in thick layers of organic sediments in a lacustrine
environment

General

medium stiff to very stiff older alluvium Very stiff to hard saprolite underlie the older
alluvium Bedrock formation was not encountered within the depths of our borings which
extended to a maximum depth of 238 feet below the ground surface
Our field observation at the project site indicated that ground surface settlements
on the order of one foot 1 may have occurred at the existing sewage pump station
since constructed in the early 1970 s Based on our analysis it appears that the soft
compressible deposits encountered below the surficial fill layer is still undergoing the
process of primary consolidation under the influence of the existing fill loads and due to
the lowering of the regional artesian head Our analysis indicate that settlements on the

Downdrag Loads
Where regional artesian head is lowered or fill is placed over a compressible soil
the latter consolidates and moves downward relative to the pile The downward
movement results in negative skin friction forces generally known as DOWNDRAG
forces on the pile shaft The magnitude of the downdrag force depends on the
thickness of the compressible layer adhesion between the soil and pile and the
pile perimeter area Due to the on going consolidation of the soft compressible
soils the estimated settlements will result in downdrag loading of deep
foundations extending through the thick layer of recent alluvial deposit Therefore
the allowable compressive load capacities of the piles will need to be reduced by
the expected downdrag loads The downdrag load on the pile shaft is estimated
to be about 100 kips for the 15 inch square pile and 95 kips for the 14 inch square
pile Therefore although the building load from the proposed structures are on the
order of 11 to 50 kips the combined downdrag and building load may require
higher pile capacities Therefore higher capacity friction piles are recommended
Battered piles are not recommended because of the additional bending forces
imposed on the piles due to the ground settlements
To reduce the downdrag forces coating of the portion of the pile shaft within the
compressible layer with bitumen coating may be considered The bitumen coating
should only be applied to the portion of the pile which will be embedded in the
negative skin friction zone or compressible soil layer However due to the
anticipated hot temperature and high humidity depending on the time of the year
GEOLABS HAWAII

recommended

Pile Hammer Selection
The piles should be driven with a hammer capable of delivering a minimum rated
energy of 50 000 foot pounds The hammer should be equipped with an anergy
level control so that driving within the soft compressible soil may be controlled at
a reduced energy to reduce the tensile stress development within the piles which
could damage or cause cracking of the piles
The contractor should forward pile and driving equipment data form to Geolabs
Hawaii prior to bidding for evaluation Geolabs Hawaii will review the submittal and
assess the driveability of piles with the use of the selected hammer Due to
possible variation in the length of the piles unit prices should be obtained during
bidding for add ons shorter piles etc
Pile Driving Criteria
Each pile should be driven continuously without interruption with the exception of
splicing if needed A pile may be rejected or its design capacity may be reduced
when the driving resistance is interrupted for more than four 4 hours and the pile
cannot be driven to the required depth
During soft driving i e when the pile tip is within soft loose soils the hammer
stroke or energy should be carefully controlled such that significant tensile
stresses do not develop in the pile

For preliminary design purposes driving may be terminated when the whole pile
