This report summarizes the findings of our geotechnical engineering review of
available information for the existing subsurface conditions at the project site
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project involves a study to select several potential sites for the location of
new 2 to 6 Million Gallon MG reservoirs to supplement the existing Honolulu
180 System The purpose of our work on the project is to provide preliminary
geotechnical engineering information for the sites to aid in evaluating their suitability for
the intended use
This report addresses our review of available information including published
geologic maps soil surveys and aerial photography for the project site
WAIAWA SITE G 2
Waiawa Site G 2 is on Waiawa Ridge to the northwest of and adjacent to
Waiawa Valley The ground surface slopes down gently to the southeast with elevations
ranging from about 200 to 230 feet above Mean Sea Level MSL
Based on a geologic map published by the US Geological Survey the site is
situated in a location which has been mapped as Koolau Basalt QTkl On this basis it is
anticipated that the site is underlain by residual soils on the order of about 30 feet deep
followed by saprolite and weathered basaltic rock formation derived from the in place
weathering of Koolau Basalt lavas

Using Google Earth and the Preliminary Grading Plan the geomorphology of the
site was reviewed The general morphology confirms that the site is underlain by residual
soil and the ground surface has been only slightly modified by agricultural activities
Based on our review of the site geology and geomorphology it appears that the site
is developable as a reservoir site and that adequate bearing would be available to
support a new reservoir
