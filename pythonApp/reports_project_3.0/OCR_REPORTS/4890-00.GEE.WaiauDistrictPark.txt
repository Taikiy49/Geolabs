FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Detailed discussion of our geotechnical engineering recommendations for design of
play court and parking pavements concrete walkways site preparation and utility trench
are presented in the following sections of this report
Play Court Pavements
New asphalt concrete pavements are anticipated for the new volleyball and
basketball courts Our laboratory test results indicated that the clayey soils encountered at
the site exhibit slight to moderate expansion potential when subjected to moisture
fluctuations Therefore it is our opinion that the following pavement section may be used
for preliminary design purposes
Asphalt Concrete Pavements
2 0 Inch Asphalt Concrete
8 0 Inch Aggregate Subbase 90 Percent Relative Compaction
10 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness on Moist Compacted Subgrade
The subgrade soils under the pavement area should be scarified to a minimum
depth of 8 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture
and compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction The subgrade should be kept
moist prior to placement of aggregate subbase Aggregate subbase material should
consist of crushed basalt aggregate compacted to not less than 90 percent relative
compaction CBR and density tests should be performed on the actual subgrade soils
encountered during construction to confirm the adequacy of the above section
The finished grades along the edges of the new play courts should be sloped to
shed water away from pavements and to reduce the potential for ponding Excessive
landscape watering near the play courts should also be avoided These requirements are
essential for the proper performance of the above recommendations since ponded water
could cause subsurface soil saturation and subsequent loss of strength The pavement
excavations should be properly backfilled against the edges immediately after construction
of the pavements to reduce water infiltration Drainage swales should be provided as
soon as possible and should be maintained to drain all surface run off away from the play
court pavements
Exterior Concrete Walkways
We understand that new concrete walkways are planned alongside the new play
courts for the project Based on the subsurface conditions encountered at the site we
recommend that a minimum 6 inch thick layer of aggregate subbase be placed below the

concrete walkways The subgrade soils below the walkways should be scarified to a
depth of about 8 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum

To reduce the potential for substantial shrinkage cracks in the slabs crack control joints

Parking Pavements
We understand that new pavements may be required for the ADA accessible
stalls planned for the project In general we anticipate the vehicle loading for the new
pavements will consist primarily of passenger vehicles minivans and light pick up
trucks We have made our preliminary pavement design assuming that the pavement
subgrade soils will be similar to the clayey soils CBR value greater than 25 and a swell
of less than 1 percent encountered in our borings Based on the above assumptions
we recommend that the following flexible pavement section be used for preliminary
design purposes
Flexible Pavements
2 0 Inch Asphalt Concrete
6 0 Inch Aggregate Base Course 95 Percent Relative Compaction
8 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness over Compacted Moist Subgrade
The pavement subgrades should be scarified to a depth of about 8 inches
moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture and recompacted
to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction The aggregate base course should
consist of crushed basalt aggregate compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative
compaction CBR and field density tests should be performed on the actual subgrade
soils encountered during construction to confirm the adequacy of the above section
Paved areas should be sloped and drainage gradients maintained to carry surface
water off the site Surface water ponding should not be allowed on the site during or after
construction Where concrete curbs are used to isolate landscaping in or adjacent to the
pavement areas we recommend that the curbs be extended a minimum of 2 inches into
the subgrade soil below the aggregate base course layer to reduce migration of landscape
water into the pavement section Alternatively a subdrain system could be constructed to
collect excessive water from landscaping irrigation For long term performance we
recommend that a subdrain system be constructed adjacent to paved landscaped areas

Site Preparation
Based on the anticipated scope of work under this project we envision that the
majority of the site grading work will involve minor cuts of up to about 6 feet thick Site
grading and earthwork operations should be observed by a representative from Geolabs
It is important that a representative from our office be present to observe the grading
operations to evaluate whether undesirable materials are encountered during the
excavation process and whether the exposed soil rock conditions at the subgrade levels
are similar to those encountered in our exploration
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed Vegetation debris and other deleterious and organic
materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site Soft and or yielding areas
encountered during clearing and grubbing should be over excavated to expose firm
material and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with well compacted fills The
excavated soft and or organic soils should be properly disposed of off site and or used in
landscape areas where appropriate Contract documents should include additive and
deductive unit prices for over excavation and compacted fill placement to account for
variations in the over excavation quantities
After clearing and grubbing existing pavements that are to be demolished should
be completely removed Over excavation resulting from demolition should be backfilled
with compacted select granular fill material Existing utilities to be abandoned should be
removed and the resulting excavation should be properly backfilled with select granular fill
material placed in 8 inch loose lifts and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction Utilities to be abandoned in place under the proposed structures should be
backfilled by pumping lean concrete under low pressure
After clearing grubbing and demolition the over excavated subgrades should be
scarified to a minimum depth of 8 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above
the optimum moisture and recompacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of
the maximum dry density as determined by ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Optimum
moisture is the water content percentage by weight corresponding to the maximum dry
density
Imported fill materials if required should consist of non expansive select
granular fill materials such as crushed coral and basalt gravel The material should be
well graded from coarse to fine with no particles greater than 3 inches in largest
dimension The material should have a CBR value of 20 or higher and a swell potential
of less than 1 percent when tested in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1883
It should also contain between 10 and 30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve

Fill and backfill materials required for the project construction should be placed in
level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness moisture conditioned to above the
optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction Compaction requirements for fills and backfills within 2 feet of the finished
pavement grades should be increased to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
Aggregate base course and aggregate subbase required for the project should
consist of crushed basalt aggregate and should conform to Sections 703 06 and 703 17 of
the State of Hawaii Standard Specifications for Road Bridge and Public Works
Construction 1994 Aggregate base course and aggregate subbase materials should be
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content placed in level lifts not
exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of 95 and 90 percent
relative compaction respectively Imported fill materials should be tested by Geolabs for
conformance with these recommendations prior to delivery to the project site for the
intended use
Utility Trench
We envision that new electrical duct lines will be required to service the future
lighting system In general granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of clean sand or S4C
is recommended under the electrical duct lines Free draining granular materials such as
clean sand or S4C should also be used for the initial trench backfill up to about 6 inches
above the duct lines to provide adequate support around the pipes and to reduce the
compaction effort of the backfill thus reducing the possibility of damaging the pipes It is
critical to use this free draining material to reduce the potential for formation of voids below
the haunches of pipes and to provide adequate support for the sides of the pipes
Improper backfill material and placement procedures could result in settlement of the
backfill and damage to the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 6 inches above the duct lines
to the top of the subgrade or finished grade may consist of on site soils or imported select
granular fill material The backfill should be placed in maximum 8 inch level loose lifts
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and mechanically
compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for
appreciable future ground subsidence Where trenches are below pavement areas the
upper 2 feet of the trench backfill below the pavement grade should be compacted to a
minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
