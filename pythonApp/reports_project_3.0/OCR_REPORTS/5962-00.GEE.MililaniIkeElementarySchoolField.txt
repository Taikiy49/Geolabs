PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located within the Mililani Ike Elementary School in the Mililani
area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii Mililani lke Elementary School was constructed a few
years ago along Lehiwa Drive near the intersection with Ahoka Street The playfield is in a
low area at the southern end of the school The project location is shown on the attached
Project Location Map Plate 1
The ground surface is covered with grass and slopes down toward the south A
drain empties into a swale near the eastern end of the field The swale runs across the
field and along the southern edge of the playfield The playfield surface was reported to
be wet and spongy with moss visible in many places The surface also appeared to be
densely compacted which poses safety hazards for school children playing on the field It
is desired to evaluate methods to improve the condition of the playfield

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
We explored the subsurface conditions at the project site by drilling and sampling
five test borings each extending to a depth of about 6 5 feet below the existing ground
surface The approximate boring locations are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Our field engineer classified the materials encountered in the borings by visual and
textural examination in the field He monitored the drilling operations on a near continuous
basis Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil Classification
System as shown on the Log Legend Plate 3
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained in general accordance with
ASTM D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 3 inch OD Modified
California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches Meanwhile some samples
were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with ASTM D 1586
Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 2 inch OD standard
penetration sampler using the same hammer and drop The blow counts needed to drive
the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown as the
Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Our borings indicate that the project site is generally underlain by stiff to very hard
silty clay and clayey silt extending to the maximum depth explored of approximately
6 5 feet below the existing ground surface We did not encounter groundwater in the
borings at the time of our field exploration It should be noted that groundwater may
fluctuate depending on seepage surface runoff precipitation temperature and other
factors
Detailed descriptions of the materials encountered in the borings are presented on
the Logs of Borings Plates 4 through 8
LABORATORY TESTING

were performed on selected samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
We have reviewed the information obtained from the field exploration at the project
site The subsurface conditions at the project site generally consist of stiff to very hard silty
clay and clayey silt Based on our engineering experience and analyses we believe that a

subdrain system may be needed to improve the condition of the playfield along with
improvements to the surface drainage
Surface Soils
Based on our field observation the ground surface at the playfield consists of
compacted clayey soils We understand that the hard surface poses safety hazards for
school children playing on the field In addition the compacted clayey soil has poor water
infiltration characteristic and causes water ponding at the ground surface
In order to improve the condition we recommend excavating the top 12 inches of
the compacted clayey soils The excavation should be backfilled with 6 inches of a sand
drainage layer overlain by 6 inches of topsoil to facilitate surface water infiltration and to
provide a suitable surface for the playfield
The excavated areas should be scarified to about 8 inches moisture conditioned to
2 percent above the optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent
relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil
expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in
accordance with ASTM D 1557 test procedures Optimum moisture is the water content
percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
The drainage layer and topsoil should be moisture conditioned to above the
optimum moisture content placed in level lifts and compacted to a minimum of 85 percent
relative compaction
Subdrain
As previously discussed the ground surface at the playfield was soggy and spongy
with moss visible in many places This ground condition may be attributed to ponding
water at the ground surface due to poor surface drainage conditions The poor drainage
conditions may be caused by relatively flat surface grade and poor infiltration characteristic
of the compacted on site silty clay materials at the playfield
In general the surface water can be drained by a surface water runoff collection
system such as well graded ground surface surface drainage trench and subdrain
Based on the poor infiltration characteristics of the compacted on site soils we believe that
a subsurface drainage system would improve the condition of the playfield We
recommended using a series of subsurface drains consisting of shallow subsurface trench
drains across the field to reduce the potential for excessive ponding at the surface of the
field and to provide for better surface drainage The Preliminary Subdrain Layout Plan is
presented on Plate 9 The subdrain details are presented on the Typical Subdrain Details
Plate 10
We recommend installing subdrains 18 to 24 inches below the ground surface
Subdrains should extend along the perimeter of the field and include transverse lines

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted herein are based in part upon
information obtained from the field borings Variations of subsoil conditions between and
beyond the borings may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not
become evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be
necessary to re evaluate the recommendations provided in this report
The boring locations indicated in this report are approximate having been
estimated by taping and pacing from the features shown on the Topographic Map
transmitted by M E Pacific Inc on The approximate elevations of the
field borings were estimated from the spot elevations shown on the same plan The
physical locations and elevations of the borings should be considered accurate only to the
degree implied by the methods used
The stratification lines shown on graphic representations on the Logs of Borings
depict the approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual
transition Water level data from the borings were measured at the times shown on the
graphic representations and or in the text of this report These data have been reviewed
and interpretations made in the formulation of this report However it must be noted that
fluctuation may occur due to variation in rainfall tides temperature and other factors
