SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our field exploration program for the lighting system improvements project
consisted of drilling and sampling three borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 3
extending to depths of about 15 8 to 26 5 feet below the existing ground surface The
approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general our field exploration disclosed that the playcourt area is generally
underlain by coral detritus consisting of very dense coral sands and gravel to about 8 to
11 feet deep Below the upper coralline deposit cemented sands of medium dense to
dense consistency were then encountered and extended to the maximum depth explored
of approximately 26 5 feet below the existing ground surface Our field exploration at the
walkway area generally encountered very stiff silty clays overlying medium dense to dense
coral detritus materials at greater depths
Groundwater was encountered in the drilled borings at depths of about 13 4 and
14 7 feet below the existing ground surface at the time of our field exploration The
groundwater levels encountered correspond to approximately Elevation 2 6 and 3 3 feet

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
In general our field exploration encountered very dense coral detritus or very stiff
silty clays in the upper subsoils across the project site Groundwater was encountered at
depths of about 13 4 and 14 7 feet below the existing ground surface in the borings drilled
at the time of our exploration
Based on the anticipated structural loads and the subsoil conditions encountered at
the project site we recommend that drilled shafts with a minimum diameter of 24 inches
and an embedment length of not less than 12 feet below the design finished grade be
used for the 39 foot high new light poles planned at the existing playcourt area The
embedment length may be reduced to 10 feet with a 30 inch diameter drilled shaft In
addition we believe that a standard light pole foundation design consisting of 22 inch
square round drilled shaft sections extending to a depth of 5 feet below the design finished
grade may be utilized for the 16 foot high night security lighting system planned along the
new walkway area
New concrete walkway planned for the park improvements project should be
supported on 18 inches of non expansive select granular fill or aggregate subbase The
non expansive capping fill may be reduced to 6 inches if coralline materials are exposed at
the slab subgrade elevation The subgrade soils beneath the walkways should be
scarified to a minimum depth of about 8 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent
above the optimum moisture content and recompacted to a minimum of 90 percent
relative compaction Detailed discussions of these items and our geotechnical
engineering recommendations are presented in the following sections of this report
Drilled Shaft Foundations
In order to develop the required bearing and lateral load resistances for the new
light pole structures we recommend that drilled shaft foundations embedded into the
upper very dense coral detritus or very stiff silty clays be used to support the lighting
system Based on the anticipated structural loads and the subsoil conditions encountered
at the project site we recommend that cast in place concrete drilled shafts with a minimum
diameter of 24 inches and an embedment length of not less than 12 feet below the design
finished grade be used for the 39 foot high new light poles planned at the existing
playcourt area The embedment length may be reduced to 10 feet with a 30 inch diameter
drilled shaft
The cast in place concrete drilled shafts would derive vertical support principally
from skin friction between the shafts and the surrounding soils An allowable
compressive load capacity of up to 15 kips per shaft may be used for dead plus live
loads and may be increased by 50 percent for transient loads such as wind or seismic
forces

Lateral loads imposed on the light pole foundations generally may be resisted by
resistance of the shaft soil system to deflect Lateral load resistance of the drilled shaft
is a function of the stiffness of the surrounding soil the stiffness of the shaft allowable
deflection at the top of the shaft and induced moment in the shaft Based on the
information provided each drilled shaft 24 inch diameter for the 39 foot high light pole
will be subjected to a lateral load of 1 200 pounds and an overturning moment of about
30 kip feet at the ground surface Based on our foundation analyses the maximum
moment induced in the drilled shaft increases slightly to 30 3 kip feet within a depth of
about 6 to 12 inches below the top of the shaft Our lateral load analyses indicated that
the estimated lateral deflection at the top of the drilled shaft should be less than inch
The anticipated settlements of the light pole foundations are estimated to be on
the order of less than 1 inch We believe that a significant portion of the settlements is
elastic and should occur as the loads are applied
The light pole foundation for the night security lighting system along the new
walkway should consist of a 22 inch square section extending to a depth of 1 foot below
the finished grade followed by a 22 inch round section extending to a depth of 5 feet
below the finished grade Our lateral load analyses indicate that the estimated deflection
at the top of the light pole foundation should be less than inch It is our opinion that the
project structural engineer should be consulted to provide additional inputs on the
adequacy of the proposed standard light pole foundation design
The performance of the drilled shafts will depend significantly upon the contractor s
method of construction construction procedures and workmanship Therefore the
recommendations and general guidelines presented in this report should be followed by
the contractor during the foundation construction work
Construction Considerations
Drilled shafts are desirable for the light pole foundations because of the
significant increase in lateral and uplift load capacities when compared to shallow
foundations However the performance of the drilled shafts will depend significantly
upon the contractor s method of construction and construction procedures
The load bearing capacities of drilled shafts depend to a large extent on the
contact between the drilled shafts and the surrounding soils Therefore proper
construction techniques are important The contractor should exercise care in drilling the
shaft holes and placing concrete into the holes Drilling by methods utilizing drilling fluids
is not recommended A low shrink concrete mix with high slump 6 to 9 inch range
should be used to provide close contact between the drilled shafts and the surrounding
soils Concrete should be placed in a suitable manner to reduce the potential for
segregation of the aggregates from the concrete mix If precast concrete pole bases are
used the gap between the pole base and the surrounding soils should be backfilled with
concrete or lean concrete A representative from Geolabs should be present to observe

Concrete Walkways
We understand that the Americans with Disabilities Act ADA compliant walkways
from the existing comfort station to the playcourt area are planned for the proposed park
improvements project Based on our field exploration and laboratory test results the
walkway alignment may be underlain by a surface layer of highly expansive clayey soils
Unless slabs on grade constructed above these expansive soils are properly designed
there is a potential for future distress to the lightly loaded slabs on grade resulting from
shrinking and swelling of the clayey soils due to changes in the moisture content To
reduce the potential for structural distress resulting from swelling of the subgrade soils we
recommend that a minimum 18 inch thick layer of non expansive select granular material
or aggregate subbase be placed below the new concrete walkways The non expansive
capping fill below the walkway slab may be reduced from 18 inches to 6 inches if coralline
materials are exposed at the slab subgrade elevation
The subgrade soils below the walkways should be scarified to a depth of about
8 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture content
and recompacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction The non expansive
select granular material or aggregate subbase should be placed in level lifts not exceeding
8 inches in loose thickness moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content
and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction The slab subgrades
should be kept moist until the final placement of concrete Where shrinkage cracks are
noted after compaction of the subgrade we recommend that the soils be thoroughly
moistened to close the cracks Saturation and subsequent yielding of the exposed
subgrade due to inclement weather and poor drainage may require over excavation of the
soft areas and replacement with well compacted fill
To reduce the potential for substantial shrinkage cracks in the slabs crack control
joints should be provided at intervals equal to the width of the walkways with expansion
joints provided at right angle intersections The thickened edges of slabs adjacent to
unpaved areas should be embedded at least 12 inches below the lowest adjacent finished
grade Areas adjacent to the slab should be backfilled tightly against the slab edges with
the on site clayey soils and graded to divert water away from the slab to reduce the
potential for water ponding around the slabs
Utility Trench
We envision that new electrical duct lines will be required to service the proposed
new lighting system In general granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of clean sand or
S4C is recommended under the duct lines Free draining granular materials such as
clean sand or S4C should also be used for the initial trench backfill up to about 6 inches
above the duct lines to provide adequate support around the pipes and to reduce the

compaction effort of the backfill thus reducing the possibility of damaging the pipes It is
critical to use this free draining material to reduce the potential for formation of voids below
the haunches of pipes and to provide adequate support for the sides of the pipes
Improper backfill material and placement procedures could result in settlement of the
backfill and damage to the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 6 inches above the duct lines
to the top of the subgrade or finished grade may consist of on site soils or imported select
granular fill material The backfill should be placed in maximum 8 inch level loose lifts
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and mechanically
compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for
future ground subsidence Where trenches are below pavement areas the upper 3 feet of
the trench backfill below the pavement grade should be compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of
soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Optimum moisture is the water content
corresponding to the maximum dry density

information obtained from the field borings Variations of subsoil conditions between and

become evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident Geolabs

transition Water level data from the borings were measured at the times shown on the
