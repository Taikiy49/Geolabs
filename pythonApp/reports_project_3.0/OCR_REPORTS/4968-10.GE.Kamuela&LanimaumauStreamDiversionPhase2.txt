In accordance with your request we have been providing geotechnical engineering
services during the mass grading operations for the Kamuela and Lanimaumau Stream
Diversion Phase II project located in the Waimea area on the Island of Hawaii This report
summarizes our geotechnical engineering observation and testing services performed
during the mass grading operations for the period between August 2003 through
December 2003 In general our services were coordinated and requested by the grading
contractor Willocks Construction Corporation
SITE GRADING
The Kamuela and Lanimaumau Stream Diversion Phase II project site is located
within Parker Ranch in the Waimea area on the Island of Hawaii As part of the planned
Parker Ranch development Kamuela and Lanimaumau Streams were diverted through a
drainage channel to a percolation retention basin This previous work involved the
excavation of channels and the construction of berms to divert and retain the storm water
runoff Based on the calculation of the 500 year design flow it was desired to expand the
system with new basins to the west of the existing basin Earthwork for the project
involved partial excavation for a channel approximately 100 feet in width and 700 feet in
length from the existing retention basin to the new basin area and placement of fill for the
portions of the channel walls to create levees and new retention berms

The site grading operations for the project was observed by Geolabs Inc between
August 20 2003 and The site grading operations were performed by

During the site grading and backfilling operations samples of the on site and
imported materials utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to
our Office for testing The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were
established in our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557
Modified Proctor and the test results are summarized on Plate 1
In general the fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to above
optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum
dry density as determined in accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557
During the site grading operations periodic field density tests were performed in
general accordance with ASTM Test Designations D 2922 and D 3017 Nuclear Gauge
Method The results of the compaction tests performed on the fills and subgrades are
summarized on Plates 2 1 through 2 10 of this report These tests were generally
performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills at the
site A total of 194 field density tests including retests of failed areas were performed in
support of the site grading and utility installation for the project
Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from the
available on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should be considered
accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed to be
unsatisfactory were generally reworked until the specified minimum compaction was
obtained and or approved by our field representative based on visual observation and
hand probing
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our professional opinion that the site grading operations for the
above project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report were
performed in conformance with the general intent of the recommendations in the

LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site In addition since our services were on an
intermittent basis some of the mass grading operation may have been performed without
our observation It should be recognized that there are variations in the accuracy and
statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor earthwork construction
and materials Our services were performed in accordance with generally accepted
standards of geotechnical engineering practice no warranty is expressed or implied
Provision of construction monitoring by an engineer is not insurance nor does it guarantee
construction of any type The contractor shall retain responsibility for the quality of the
work for adhering to the plans and specifications and for repairing defects regardless of
when they are found
