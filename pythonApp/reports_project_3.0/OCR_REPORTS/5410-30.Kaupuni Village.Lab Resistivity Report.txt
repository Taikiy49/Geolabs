PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project involves the installation of over 650 lineal feet of waterline for the
Kaupuni Village formerly Consuelo Subdivision project in the District of Waianae on the
Island of Oahu Hawaii The project location and general vicinity are shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1 The waterline system generally follows the alignment of the
new access road planned for the project as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The purpose of the laboratory resistivity testing is to determine the type of corrosion
protection required for the new waterline as stipulated by the Board of Water Supply
Based on the grading plans and the design pipe elevations of the new waterline we
envision that the new waterline may be surrounded by the in situ clayey soils and or
imported structural fill required for the project Laboratory resistivity testing using the Soil
Box Method was performed on selected samples collected from the in situ clayey soils and
imported structural fill along the waterline alignment

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our laboratory resistivity testing is to obtain an overview of the
corrosion characteristics of the materials surrounding the new waterline planned Our
work was performed in general conformance with ASTM G 57 the requirements provided
in the Board of Water Supply Water System Standards dated 2002 and the Water
System External Corrosion Standards Volume 3 dated 1991 and all subsequent
amendments and additions of the Water System External Corrosion Control
Standards Volume 3 1991 The scope of our work consisted of the following tasks and
work efforts
1 Collection by our field technician of selected samples of the in situ clayey
soils and imported structural fill material around the design pipe elevations
of the new waterline
2 Laboratory resistivity testing of the collected samples using the Soil Box
Method in general accordance with ASTM G 57
3 Analyses of the laboratory test results for the determination of resistivity
values and corrosion rating
4 Preparation of this letter report summarizing our work on the project and
presenting our findings
5 Coordination of our overall work on the project by our senior engineer
6 Quality assurance of our work and client design team consultation by our
principal engineer
7 Miscellaneous work efforts such as drafting word processing and clerical
support
LABORATORY TESTING PROGRAM

by the in situ clayey soils or imported structural fill at about the design pipe elevations
Therefore we collected a total of 15 soil samples for laboratory resistivity testing using the

designated as S 1 through S 6 from the in situ clayey soils at the locations shown on the

FINDINGS AND DISCUSSIONS
Resistivity is generally recognized as one of the most significant soil characteristics
with regard to the corrosivity of the soil to buried metallic objects In general the lower the
resistivity the greater the potential for corrosion of the buried metallic structure
Conversely the higher the resistivity the less likely the soil will contribute to corrosion of
metallic objects
Based on the test results presented on Plate 3 the in situ clayey soils at the
project site generally exhibit low minimum resistivity values in the range of 1 100 to
1 600 ohm cm corresponding to a corrosion rating of 2 indicating that the in situ clayey
soils are highly corrosive to buried metallic structures In addition the imported
structural fill used for the project generally exhibit very low minimum resistivity values in
the range of 210 to 730 ohm cm corresponding to a corrosion rating of 1 indicating the
imported structural fill material are extremely corrosive to buried metallic structures
Therefore we recommend properly designing the new waterline for protection against
potential corrosion A corrosion engineer should be consulted for detailed
recommendations on corrosion protection
LIMITATIONS
The evaluations presented in this report are based upon information obtained from
laboratory resistivity testing on selected soil samples Variation of soil conditions between
and beyond the soil sampling points may occur and the nature and extent of such
variations may not become evident until a later point in time If variations then appear
evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the ratings provided herein
