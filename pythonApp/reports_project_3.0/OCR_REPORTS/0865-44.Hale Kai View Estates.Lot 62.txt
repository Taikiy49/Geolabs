As requested we have made a study of the near surface soil
conditions below the above referenced lot The purpose of
the study was to evaluate the suitability of the near surface
soils as foundation support for the proposed house The study
consisted of three 3 borings drilled within the proposed
building site laboratory soil tests and a review of the
grading and compaction test record on the building site
This report summarizes our observations and recommendations
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed single family residence consists of a one story
wood structure approximately 46 feet wide by 44 feet long
Based on the preliminary plans provided to us the house will
be set back about 19 feet from Kulani Street
Post and beam construction with wood floors will be utilized
for the main portion of the house
Concrete slab on grade is planned for the garage driveway and
lanai
CMU retaining walls are planned around the rear of the garage
and between the living room and lanai
About 3 feet of fill is planned for the rear portion of the
garage
EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
Lot 62 is located on Kulani Street in the southwestern portion
of the subdivision development about 300 feet north of Ainapo
Street It is bounded by Kulani Street on the west and sub
division lots on the north east and south An existing con
crete drainage ditch is located along the lower northeastern
boundary of the lot
The lot generally slopes downward from Kulani Street toward
the east at about 4 5 to 5 6 gradient The elevation of the
lot generally varies from about 156 to 145 feet

SUBDIVISION LOT GRADING
Prior to the recent project site grading operation the north
eastern and northwestern portions of Lot 62 were covered with a
large stockpile of soil and boulders left over from previous
grading operations in the area
During that grading operation the stockpile was removed down
to a depth of 6 feet below the design finished grade and re
placed with compacted available on site soils The available
on site soil consisted basically of silty clay and clayey silt
soils which had been mixed with some adobe clay pockets from
the previous stockpile About 6 to 9 feet of fill were placed
over the remaining portion of the lot
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
A review of the grading compaction test record and the site
reconnaissance indicate that Lot 62 is underlain by grayish
brown expansive clay to the full depth of the compacted on site
soil which was about 6 to 9 feet
Detailed description of the soils encountered in borings are
presented on the attached Log of Borings Plates A 1 through
A 3 The approximate locations of the borings are shown on the
attached Site Plan Plate 1

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that the lot is underlain by
more than 5 feet of grayish brown clay fill The laboratory
test results on this material generally indicated that they
have high expansion potential Therefore for Lot 62 the
slab on grade and foundation construction would require special
design procedures in order to reduce the shrink swell effect of
the subsoils
House Foundation
For this lot post and beam construction is highly desirable
and recommended due to the sloping terrain and expansive soil
conditions The post and beam construction generally results
in lower foundation costs and the individual footings can be
readily adjusted should the need occur Where utilized
concrete slab on grade should also be properly designed for
expansive soil conditions
For post and beam construction we recommend that the expansive
clays be overexcavated down for a depth of 3 feet below the
bottom of the individual post footings and then replaced with
non expansive borrow soils such as crusher waste select
borrow or coral compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum
dry density per ASTM D 1557 78 The spread footings can then
be placed on the compacted non expansive soil Alternatively
the individual post footing can be extended a minimum depth of
36 inches below the finished grade and backfilled with on site
soil compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density
The footings should be poured in place and the excavation
backfilled and compacted to provide an impervious layer over
the footing
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f can be used for
footings bearing on the stiff natural ground or compacted fill
If a footing is located next to a utility line it should
extend to the bottom of the utility trench to reduce footing
settlement due to settlement of the trench backfill
Surface water should be diverted away from the foundations
Retaining Wall Foundation
Retaining walls up to 3 feet high are planned in the south
eastern portion of the house

In order to reduce the expansive effects of the existing clay
soils on the wall we recommend that the expansive clays should
be overexcavated down for a depth of 24 inches below the bottom
of the footing and then replaced with non expansive borrow
soils such as crusher waste or coral compacted to a minimum
of 90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78 The
overexcavation and replacement should extend a minimum distance
of 12 inches beyond the footing width
The wall footing should be embedded a minimum depth of 12 inches
below the outside finished grade Therefore the total depth
of the above overexcavation and replacement would be 3 0 feet
below the outside finished grade
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f can be used
Off site non expansive soil should be used as backfill over the
wall footing and behind the wall
The walls should be designed to resist lateral soil pressure of
35 p c f equivalent fluid pressure
A coefficient of friction of 0 4 between the bottom of the
footing and compacted soil can be used to determine the lateral
resistance of the wall

A subsurface drainage system should be utilized behind the wall
to prevent the build up of hydrostatic pressure
Slab On Grade
Concrete slab on grade is planned for the garage and lanai It
appears that the garage area would require fill up to three
feet in order to obtain sufficient level area for the garage
The on site clay soils are expansive and are unsuitable as slab
subgrade soils In order to reduce the expansive effects of
the clay the expansive clays should be overexcavated down for
a depth of 3 feet below the slab subgrade and then replaced
with non expansive borrow soils such as select borrow crusher
waste or coral compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum
dry density per ASTM D 1557 78
A 4 inch layer of base course should be provided below the slab
and compacted to 90 of its maximum dry density In order to
protect against moisture infiltration a polyethylene moisture
barrier may be incorporated below the slab The membrane
should be protected by a thin layer of sand to aid in the
concrete curing
W Page 8
Concrete Driveway and Sidewalks
Expansive clay soils were encountered along the proposed drive
way Since the driveway can tolerate some amount of heaving
the clay overexcavation and replacement can be reduced to 2
feet below the driveway subgrade level Where 2 or more feet
of fill is planned select non expansive borrow material should
be used to cap over the expansive clay Where less than 2 feet
of fill is planned overexcavation and replacement of the
expansive clay with select non expansive material is required
The above requirements should be followed for a minimum distance
of 3 feet beyond the driveway
Since a relatively long driveway is planned joints are recom
mended at about 8 to 10 foot intervals in order to allow for
some relative movement of the concrete
For future sidewalks capping of the clay or overexcavation and
replacement similar to the driveway are recommended to reduce
heaving caused by the expansive clay soil
Site Grading
The existing terrain at the site generally slopes down at about
5 6 grade or flatter
Minor grading is anticipated for most of the area below the
house
The area to be filled should be cleared of vegetation and debris
prior to filling
Fill and backfill should be placed in maximum 8 inch lifts and
compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density
established by ASTM D 1557 78
The finished grade outside the slabs pavements and foundations
should be shaped to avoid ponding conditions and to shed water
away from the structures
Design Review
Plans for foundations site grading and utility construction
should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and
written comments prior to construction to see that the intent
of our recommendations is included in the project plans and
specifications
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during
earthwork construction particularly the fill placement slope
cuts and to monitor the contractor s grading operations
