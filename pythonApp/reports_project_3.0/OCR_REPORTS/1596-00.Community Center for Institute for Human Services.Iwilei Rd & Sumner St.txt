SUMMARY OF RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that the project
site is underlain at shallow depths by competent
coral subsoils suitable for support of the
proposed fill and building loads
The 2 story building may be supported by spread
or continuous footing foundation bearing on the
weathered coral reef formation or the new
compacted fill
The text of this report should be referred to for
detailed and special design recommendations
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this geotechnical engineering investigation is to
explore the subsurface soil and water conditions at the proposed building
site to provide pertinent scil parameters for use in the design of the
building foundations and pavement thickness for parking lot construction
The general project location is shown on the Project Location Plan
Plate 1

The work was done in general accordance with our proposal dated
Originally preliminary information indicated that a
portion of this area may be underlain by soft soil conditions There
fore deeper borings were planned to evaluate possible deeper foundations
for the building Subsequently during our investigation better soil
conditions were encountered and spread footing foundation appeared
feasible Therefore the boring depths were shortened and the number of
borings increased to evaluate the variability of the competent near
surface soils
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests which
we used in the engineering analysis to formulate our conclusions and
recommendations are presented in the appendices of this report
The approximate boring locations are shown on the Site Plan

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a 2 story concrete post and beam and
CMU structure The building will be approximately 60 by 150 feet in plan
dimension

of the building next to Iwilei Road
Due to the sloping ground at the site about 1 to 4 feet of fill is

The layout of the proposed structure is shown on the Site Plan

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is situated on the mauka diamond head corner
intersection of Iwilei Road and Summer Street in Honolulu Oahu Hawaii
The facility will cover an area of approximately 80 by 200 feet
with access from Iwilei Road and Sumner Street
The topography generally slopes down towards Iwilei Road with
elevations ranging from 8 to 13 feet The area is presently used by the
Salvation Army for storage of cars and trucks
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The project site was originally located in an area where an emerged
coral reef formation was located near the surface while the surrounding
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Page 4
area was lower and used for rice fields Later it appears that the site
was filled for development
Soil Conditions
The soil conditions were explored by drilling six 6 borings at
the building site to depths of 21 0 to 21 5 feet below the existing
ground surface
The soil conditions encountered appear to be relatively uniform and
consisted of a thin surface layer of stiff to hard brown sandy silt 1 to
4 feet thick over dense silty coral sand and gravel weathered coral
reef formation to the full depth of the borings
Groundwater was encountered at depths of 9 5 to 14 feet in the
borings at the time of our field exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed competent soil conditions below the
proposed improvements From a soil engineering point of view the site
is suitable for the proposed new building
Site Grading
Fills of about 1 to 4 feet are planned over the building site
No cutting is planned and therefore imported fill material will be
required

Clearing and Grubbing
The site should be cleared of vegetation debris and deleterious
material The grubbing work should be such that all roots 1 0
inch or larger should be removed
Surface Preparation for Fill Area
ne Sn Ns a Nt a nm
After clearing and grubbing the area to receive fill should be
scarified at least 6 inches below the cleared grade and recompacted
to a minimum of 95 of its maximum dry density Any soft or weak
areas Cisclosed during this recompaction should be overexcavated
removed and then backfilled with engineered fill The base course
rock at the site may be reused in the fill
Fill Construction
The project site will generally require about 1 to 4 feet of
imported fill to raise the building site grades
Generally the new imported fill material should consist of
non expansive select borrow materials less than 3 inches in maximum size
with less than 20 passing the 200 sieve The minimum laboratory CBR
value should be 15 at 95 relative compaction of the ASTM D 1557 78
maximum density with expansion value not greater than 1
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1596 00 Page 6
Structural fill placed within the structure should be placed in
8 inch thick compacted horizontal lifts and campacted to 95 of the ASTM
D density For fill placed outside of the proposed
building area if required the compaction can be reduced to 90 minimum
relative compaction per above ASTM standard
Building Foundation
Spread or continuous wall footings bearing on the dense coral sand
weathered reef deposit or on the new structural fill is recommended for
the structure An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f may be used for
footings bearing on the compacted structural fill material and for the
coral sand formation the allowable bearing value may be increased to
5000 p s f A one third increase of the above bearing value may be used
for temporary wind and seismic loads
All exterior footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches
below the adjacent finished grade For the interior footings bearing on
the coral formation the embedment may be reduced slightly to minimize
cutting into the underlying weathered coral reef layer A 12 inch
minimum embedment may be considered
The bottom of all footing excavations should be free of mud or
debris

W Page 7
Footings located next to utility trenches should extend down to the
bottom of the utility trench or the trench backfill should be well
compacted to minimize building settlement due to settlement of the trench
backfill
Site drainage should be designed to divert surface water away from
the building foundation
Settlement
Based on the boring laboratory test data and estimated loads it
is our opinion that minimal settlements generally less than one fourth of
an inch are anticipated for footings bearing on coral and compacted
structural fill layer
Lateral Earth Pressures
The perimeter retaining wall may be designed for an equivalent
fluid pressure of 35 p c f
For passive earth pressure an equivalent fluid pressure of 300
p s f per foot of depth can be used A friction factor of 0 4 can be
utilized to determine the sliding resistance of the structure and the
foundation

On Grade Slabs
It appears that the finished grade of elevation 13 5 feet will be
about 1 to 4 feet above the existing grade Thus the subgrade of the
slabs will be in compacted fill
The thickness of the concrete slab may be of a conventional
thickness of 4 inches A minimum of 4 inches of granular cushion layer
should be provided under the slab A moisture proofing membrane should
be considered below the slab to protect the floor covering from moisture
infiltration
Driveway and Parking Pavement Thickness
Based on our observation of the near surface materials on the site
and the result of our CER test the subgrade soil in general can
provide a design CBR value of 15 when ccmpacted to 95 relative density
per ASTM D 1557 78 test method Therefore the following is the
preliminary pavement design
Asphaltic Concrete Pavement
2 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 Inch Base Course
8 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
over a Prepared Subgrade

The pavement subgrades should be compacted and sloped to drain
Design Review
Plans and specifications for site grading and foundation
construction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and
written comments prior te construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is reconmended that the site grading spread footing
excavations subgrade preparations and cushion layer constructions for
slabs and pavements be observed by the soils engineer during construction
to see that adequate compaction has been obtained and that the subsurface
conditions are consistent with the findings of our investigation
Imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
