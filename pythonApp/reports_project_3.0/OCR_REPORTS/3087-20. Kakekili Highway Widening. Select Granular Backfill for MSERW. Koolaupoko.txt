In accordance with your request dated December 13th we have reviewed the
recommendations by the Reinforced Earth Company with regard to the resistivity
requirements of the Select Granular Backfill material for the Mechanically Stabilized
Earth Retaining Walls MSERW at the subject project We have also conferred with
personnel from the SDOT Materials Testing Laboratory on this matter The following
explanation and recommendations are provided for your consideration
_ The requirements of the Select Granular Backfill material for the MSERW in
Section 514 05 F of the Special Provisions is based on the Federal Highway
Reinforced Structures The corrosion rates specified for design in the Special
Provisions are also based on the 186 publication This FHWA
publication is considered to be the most current recommended practice for corrosion
evaluation of MSERW structures
We recognize that the corrosion rates specified in the Special Provisions are
about 25 percent higher than the corrosion rates recommended in the FHWA
publication The corrosion rates are higher than the recommended practice in the
FHWA publication due to the environment in which the MSERW is located Based on
resistivity tests performed on the foundation soils and the retained earth some areas
of the project site may be classified as corrosive with minimum resistivity values
between 700 and 2 000 ohm cm Because some of these MSERW structures will be
