INTRODUCTION
This report represents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the design of the site grading pavement and
building foundation of the proposed Church of Jesus Christ of Latter Day
Saints in the town of Hilo on the island of Hawaii
Our work was performed in general accordance with our proposal dated
August 3 1989

The general location of the site is shown on the Project Location
Map Plate 1 The proposed layout of the proposed church building and
parking areas is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface conditions at the site in order to develop a generalized soils
data set for the formulation of engineering design recommendations
pertinent to the proposed church building and parking areas
The scope of our work included the drilling and sampling of eleven
11 borings at the project site to depths ranging between 1 8 and 6 6
feet below the existing ground surface laboratory testing of selected
samples obtained during the field exploration engineering evaluation of
the field and laboratory data the development of design recommendations
and report preparation
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration and laboratory testing
are presented in the appendices of this report
SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed site is located on Kilauea Avenue in the town of Hilo on
the island of Hawaii
The site currently has three 3 buildings and one 1 satellite dish
existing upon it The buildings have an A C driveway and parking stalls
around them
The southern and eastern boundaries of the site are covered with
grasses and some trees The site is generally level with the exception of
the southeast corner which slopes down gradually towards the southeast

PROJECT CONSTDERATIONS
Based on the plans provided to us the proposed church building will
be a single story structure measuring 110 by 220 feet in plan dimensions
It is anticipated that the building will be of concrete and or masonry
construction with on grade slabs for floors In addition parking areas
are planned along the perimeter of the proposed building Asphaltic
concrete pavements are anticipated
Site grading consisting of minimal cuts and fills is anticipated
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The project site is underlain by basaltic lava flows of the
prehistoric member of the Kau Volcanic Series These flows are generally
of Holocene age and were extruded by Mauna Loa
Observations at the project site and in its immediate vicinity
indicate that the area is underlain by basaltic lava flows which were laid
down in the a a phase characterized by a clinkery surface and a core of
dense rock
At the site the lava flows have been mantled by Pahala Ash which is
an air laid deposit of volcanic ash with high in situ moisture contents
and low in situ soil densities Pahala Ash is characterized as having
thixotropic properties i e it loses strength when remolded under
equipment vehicular or seismic loads
Subsurface Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by the
drilling and sampling of eleven 11 borings to depths ranging between 1 8
and 6 6 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate locations

of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The logs of the
borings are presented in Appendix A of this report along with detailed

In general our exploration encountered competent volcanic rock
formation at depths of about three 3 feet or less below the proposed
building site In the proposed parking areas the surface ash layer
varied in thickness from about 1 5 to over 6 0 feet below the existing
ground surface
Subsurface cavities lava tubes were not encountered in the
drilled borings However voids in volcanic formations are frequently
encountered in the Hilo area
Groundwater was not encountered in the drilled borings during the
time of our exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
It is our opinion that from a geotechnical engineering point of view
the proposed project is feasible The volcanic formation encountered at
relatively shallow depths below the surface of the project site should
provide adequate bearing for the proposed church building parking areas
and related site work However due to the thixotropic nature of the
surface ash soils we do not recommend their use to support the
foundations of the proposed building In addition we are recommending
flatter slopes to reduce the potential of slope failure under seismic
loading conditions
Since there is a potential of cavities within the volcanic rock we
recommend that a program of cavity detection be conducted during
construction This is discussed in a later section of this report

Site Grading
Prior to the commencement of any earthwork all areas within the
roadway and right of way grading limits should be cleared of all surface
vegetation and deleterious materials The resulting grub and spoil
materials should be disposed of properly off site or stockpiled for use
in landscaping Any soft weak or yielding areas disclosed during the
clearing operations within the portions of the grading limits designated
to receive fill should be over excavated to firm ground and the resulting
excavation should be backfilled with well compacted engineered fill
It is our recommendation that the existing surface ash soils be
removed to natural rock formation within and to a minimm of three 3
feet outside the proposed building limits If the stripped ash material
does not contain organics rubbish or other deleterious materials and if
it is generally less than eight 8 inches in maximm size it may be
re used as yard fill material Otherwise it should be wasted off site
Imported fill material if required should conform to the
requirements stipulated in Section 30 of the Standard Specifications for
Public Works Construction Select Borrow for Subbase Course dated
September 1986 Structural backfill material should consist of a
well graded granular material generally less than six 6 inches in
maximum size and free of organics or other deleterious material
Fill should be placed in level lifts not exceeding twelve 12 inches
in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 of the maximm dry
density as established by ASIM Test Method D 1557 78 Trench and
structural backfills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding eight
8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimm of 90 of the
maximum dry density as established by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 The
upper two 2 feet of fills and backfills below pavement subgrade level
should be compacted to 95 maximum dry density

Conventional compaction testing is generally not practical in fills
consisting of excavated volcanic material Therefore we recommend a
testing program under the observation of the geotechnical engineer to
determine the number of passes of a compactor needed to achieve the
desired level of compaction This program should be conducted at the
start of grading to establish the compaction criteria for the remainder of
the grading work
Slopes in dense rock formation may be cut to a ratio of one
horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V or flatter Slopes in ash soil should
be cut to a ratio of three horizontal to one vertical 3H 1V or flatter
Care should be exercised to avoid over cutting of the slopes
Fill slopes should be placed at a ratio of three horizontal to one
vertical 3H 1V or flatter Fill slopes should be constructed by
over filling and cutting back to the design slope
Excavation
It is our opinion that the upper ash soil encountered at the site may
be excavated by conventional methods Due to the variable nature of
basalt rock formation encountered in the northern portion of the site it
is anticipated that some hard excavation e g hoe rams chipping and or
blasting may be required for foundation and trench excavations in the
dense underlying rock formation
It appears that excavated basalt rock material will require some
processing e g removal or breaking of fragments greater than eight 8
inches in maximum diameter prior to its use as fill material The basalt
lava material will require further processing to remove or reduce the rock
sizes to less than three 3 to eight 8 inches in size prior to being
used as trench and structural backfill respectively
Shoring and or bracing of the excavations should conform to the
applicable safety requirements The design of the shoring bracing should
be left to the discretion of the contractor performing the work However
GEOLABS HAWAII

excavations in dense basalt rock formation will most likely stand
unsupported for temporary construction purposes
Foundations
Spread or continuous footing foundations are recommended for the
proposed church building Due to the thixotropic nature of the surface
ash soils encountered at the project site we recommend that these soils
be removed from all building areas as discussed in Site Grading
Foundations for the proposed building should bear directly on in situ rock
formation
The bottom of all footing excavations should bear directly on in situ
rock An allowable bearing value up to 6 000 p s f may be used for
footings bearing on the dense in situ basalt rock formation
A minimm footing width of eighteen 18 inches should be utilized
and the bottom of all footings should be placed a minimm of twenty four
24 inches below the lowest adjacent grade and on competent rock
formation In addition the above footing excavation should be monitored
by the soils engineer to see that the aforementioned recommendations are
carried out properly and to locate potential voids or cavities near the
footing excavations For details of cavity probing refer to the
following section under Cavity Probing
Surface water should be diverted away from the building foundations
Anticipated total and differential settlements are expected to be
relatively small on the order of less than one half 1 2 to one quarter
1 4 of an inch for structures bearing on in situ rock formation

Cavity Probing
The field exploration encountered volcanic rock formation at shallow
depths below the project site In order to reduce the possibility of a
potential void situated below a foundation causing settlement and or
foundation failure we recommend that the foundation subgrades be probed
The probes should consist of a 3 inch minimm diameter hole drilled to a
depth of 10 feet below the bottom of the footing Probes should be
drilled at each major footing location and on 10 foot centers along
continuous or strip foundations
The probe holes should be backfilled with a mortar like grout 1 sand
to 1 cement injected at low to moderate pressures in an upstage fashion
i e from the bottom of the probe hole to the top
To simplify the mobilization of the probing equipment it is feasible
to perform the probing and grouting operations prior to structure and
footing excavation
Lateral Resistance
To resist lateral loads a coefficient of friction of 0 5 between the
foundations and underlying rock materials is recommended
In addition a passive soil resistance of 300 p c f may be used
Slabs On Grade
Slabs should be placed over a 4 inch cushion layer of gravel
generally less than three fourths 3 4 of an inch and larger than
one fourth 1 4 of an inch in size The slab cushion should be supported
by structural backfill compacted to a minimum of 90 of the maximm dry
density as established by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2367 00 Page 9
Slabs on grade should have perimeter footings extending down a
minimum of twelve 12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade or competent
rock formation
The slab subgrade should be kept moist and protected from drying
prior to concrete placement in order to minimize slab cracking due to
dehydration The slab subgrade should be compacted to a minimum of 90 of
the maximum dry density established by ASTM D 1557 78
Pavement
Based on our field exploration and laboratory testing we recommend
the following pavement section for design of the project driveways and
parking areas
2 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 Inch Base Course
18 Inch Select Borrow
26 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
CBR and density tests and or field observations should be perfonned
on the actual subgrade soils encountered during construction and that the
above section be revised if necessary The recommended section considers
medium traffic and assumes that adequate drainage will be provided in the
paved areas Weepholes should be provided in parking area drainage inlet
structures in order to relieve the seepage of water which may accumulate
in the subgrade
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comment
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this review
is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for
misinterpretation of the recommendations

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the
foundation phases of the work This is to observe compliance with the
design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow design
changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from that
anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in
this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
LIMTTATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
upon information obtained from the borings Variations of conditions
between the borings may occur and the nature and extent of these
variations may not become evident until construction If variations then
appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations
given in this report
The locations of the borings were determined by taping from
structures indicated on the Site Plan by Paul Louie Associates AIA
dated July 1989 Elevations of the borings were approximately determined
by interpolation from elevation points on the topographic survey map The
physical location and elevation of the borings should be considered
accurate only to the degree implied by the method used

boring points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types and
as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on

Water level data from the boring points were collected at the times
