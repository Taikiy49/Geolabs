PROJECT DESCRIPTION 
The project site is located in the open landscaped area east of the existing Maui 
Beach Hotel at 170 West Ka’ahumanu Avenue in Kahului on the Island of Maui, Hawaii. 
The project involves the development of the site into a hotel facility with two wing buildings 
(east and west), a lobby building, and an electrical building. The two wing buildings will be 
four-story structures with crawl space, the lobby building in the center will be a single-story 
structure with crawl space, and the small electrical building at the west end of the site will 
be a single-story structure with crawl space. The project location and general vicinity are 
shown on the Project Location Map, Plate 1. 
Based on the geotechnical engineering report referenced above for the project, 
we recommended supporting the new hotel facility on a deep foundation system 
consisting of 18-inch diameter augered cast-in-place (ACIP) piles with allowable 
compressive load capacities of 80 and 90 kips (design load) per pile. To achieve the 
design load capacities, the ACIP piles are required extending through the loose to 
medium sandy soils with design pile lengths of about 30 and 33 feet below the bottom of 
the pile cap elevation. Detailed pile number plans are presented on the ACIP Pile 
Identification Plans, Plates 2.1 through 2.3. 
STATIC PILE LOAD TEST 
In order to evaluate the adequacy of the design lengths of the ACIP pile 
foundation, a static pile load test was conducted on an 18-inch ACIP load test pile, 
designated as Test Pile No. TP-1. The load test pile was installed by Kelikai, Inc. on 
March 1, 2023, utilizing a drill rig with a continuous hollow stem auger and a hydraulic 
concrete pump. The 18-inch diameter load test pile TP-1 extended to a depth of about 
34.6 feet below the existing ground surface.  
To better understand the load transfer mechanism at the pile-soil interface, the 
load test pile was instrumented with vibrating wire embedment strain gages installed at 
various depths along the length of the load test pile. The pile load test was conducted in 
general accordance with the Quick Load Test Method of ASTM D1143 and modified to 
suit the design intents. In general, the test pile was loaded to about 160 kips (about 
200% of the allowable compressive load capacity) in approximately 8-kip load 
increments. The static pile load test results are summarized in the following report. 
“ACIP Pile Load Test Report, Maui Pagoda, 170 West Kaahumanu 
Avenue, Kahului, Maui, Hawaii,” dated March 22, 2023. 
Based on our review of the static pile load test results and field observations, it is 
our opinion that the specified 18-inch ACIP pile drilled to the design depth would be 
capable of provide the compressive load capacity exceeding the design load capacity with 
a factor of safety of 2.0 or greater.

ACIP PRODUCTION PILE INSTALLATION 
The General Contractor for the project was RVC Hawaii LLC and the ACIP pile 
installation subcontractor was Kelikai, Inc. The production pile installation program, 
consisting of installing 271 18-inch diameter ACIP piles, was conducted from March 28 
through April 14, 2023 under the full-time observations by our field engineer. A 
Casagrande B300 equipped with a rotary drill and hollow-stem augers was used to 
install the production piles. Automatic monitoring equipment was mounted on both 
drilling and pumping equipment with the intent of providing auger depth, grout volume, 
grout pressure, etc., on a near-continuous basis. 
During the pile installation operation, logs were maintained for each ACIP pile, 
which included information pertaining to pile number, date installed, depth of pile, grout 
volume used, grout return depth, etc. The locations of the production ACIP piles 
installed are shown on the Pile Identification Plan, Plates 2.1 through 2.3. The summary 
of the production ACIP pile installation is presented on Plates 3.1 through 3.8 of this 
letter report. 
It should be noted that some of the ACIP piles were installed slightly shorter than 
the design length, but less than 6 inches. To expedite the production installation 
construction, this situation was mitigated by lengthening other production pile length 
within the same pile cap for compensation. Other piles, when no grout return was 
observed, were redrilled and regrouted until proper grout return to be observed and 
accepted. 
Based on our field observations of the equipment performance and subsurface 
conditions encountered, it is our professional opinion that the production ACIP piles 
have been installed in generally conformance with the plans and specifications, meeting 
the design intends.
