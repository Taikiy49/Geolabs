INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of a geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed residence for Kam Young located at 125
Ho Olako Place in the Kamehame Subdivision on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general
location of the site and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summaries our findings and geotechnical engineering recommendations
resulting from the field exploration laboratory testing and engineering analyses These
recommendations are intended for foundation design input and may not be appropriate
to form the basis for final construction cost estimates or for construction bidding
purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a two 2 story residence with concrete driveway at the
project site It is our understanding that the proposed residence will be a wood framed
structure with elevated wood framed floors
A one 1 story garage with concrete slab on grade is also planned for the
proposed residence
Based on the available as built grading plan the existing house pad at the project
site is graded and relatively level with elevations ranging from approximately 390 to 392
feet above Mean Sea Level MSL It appears that excavations of 1 to 15 feet deep had
been performed to provide the existing house pad Minimal site grading involving cut and
fill operations to provide finish grades for the proposed residence is anticipated

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
soils rock conditions within the project site in order to develop a generalized soils rock
data set for the formulation of geotechnical engineering design recommendations
pertinent to the proposed residence
The work was done in accordance with our fee proposal to Kam
Young dated August 28 1991 The scope of work included the following tasks and work
efforts
1 Review of available soils and geologic information pertinent to the project
site and its vicinity
2 Reconnaissance of the project site and its vicinity to make visual observa
tion of the surface soil conditions and to select locations for the borings to
maximize the value of the information obtained
3 Mobilization and demobilization of a truck mounted drill rig to and from the
project site
4 Drilling and sampling of three 3 borings to depths between 15 3 and 15 7
feet below the existing ground surface using auger drilling techniques
5 Laboratory testing of selected samples obtained during the field exploration
to determine the classification and the engineering properties of the
materials encountered

6 Engineering analyses of the field and laboratory data for the purpose of
developing geotechnical engineering design recommendations
7 Preparation of this report summarizing our findings and presenting our
recommendations

in the appendices of this report
EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is located at 125 Ho Olako Place in the Kamehame Subdivision in
the Hawaii Kai area on the island of Oahu Hawaii
The project site is fronted by Ho Olako Place on its eastern boundary and bounded
by a concrete lined drainage ditch along the western boundary Next to the northern and
southern boundaries of the project site are other house lots in the same subdivision
Based on the available as built grading plan a relatively level house pad occupies
approximately the front half of the project site with current ground elevations ranging from
about 390 to 392 feet above MSL To the north and east of the pad the site slopes
upwards to elevations 400 to 401 feet above MSL with approximate slope ratios varying
from about one and one half horizontal to one vertical to 3H 1V
To the west of the house pad the site slopes downwards with slope ratios of about
390 to 380 feet above MSL The slope continues with a flatter slope ratio to the western

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The site is located on the southeastern flank of the elongated Koolau Volcanic
Range which forms the southeastern portion of Oahu Hawaii The range was formed of
pahoehoe and aa basalt lava flows from a rift zone roughly paralleling the existing
mountain trends during the Tertiary Period
Field Exploration
The exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling three 3 borings to
depths ranging from 15 3 to 15 7 feet below the existing ground surface The approxi
mate locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general the borings disclosed that the site is underlain by very dense to hard
highly weathered vesicular basalt rock formation to a depth of 15 7 feet the maximum
drilled depth
Groundwater was not encountered in the drilled borings during our field
exploration

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
It is Our opinion that the proposed project is feasible from a geotechnical
engineering point of view provided that the recommendations presented below are
followed
Foundations
Shallow spread and continuous footing foundations may be used to support the
proposed residence
An allowable bearing value of 3 500 p s f can be used for footings bearing on the
in situ highly weathered basalt rock formation This value may be increased by one third
when temporary wind or seismic loads are considered A minimum footing embedment
of twelve 12 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade is recommended
If loose soft soil lenses or pockets are encountered at the bottoms of footing
excavations they should be over excavated to dense in situ weathered basalt The
resulting depression should then be backfilled with select granular material compacted
to a minimum of 95 of the maximum dry density as determined in accordance with
ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
If a footing is located next to a utility trench it should extend to the bottom of the
utility trench to reduce footing settlement due to settlement of the trench backfill
Surface water should be diverted away from the foundations to avoid ponding of
surface runoff water that may adversely affect the bearing capacity of the soils below the
foundations

It is anticipated that the footings if constructed in accordance with the above
recommendations will have negligible settlements
Foundation Probing
Our experience in the vicinity of the project site and similar rock formation indicate
that voids cavities and lava tubes may be encountered within the project site In order
to reduce the potential of loss of foundation support resulting from collapse of voids
below a footing foundation a program of cavity probing may be implemented
Probes should be drilled at each individual footing location and every ten 10 feet
on centers along the wall footing location The probes should be a minimum of three 3
inches in diameter and should be drilled to a minimum depth equal to two 2 times the
footing width below the footing bottom
To backfill the probes and to aid in the detection of cavities the probe holes
should then be filled with neat cement grout injected at low to moderate pump pressures
from a grout pump If the volume of grout injected exceeds four 4 times the calculated
volume of the probe hole the grout mix should be adjusted to a one 1 part of sand to
one 1 part of cement mixture
The probing and grouting program should be conducted under the observation of
the project geotechnical engineer to monitor the presence of voids and to allow additional
recommendations to be made if changed ground conditions occur and or if large voids
are encountered

Slab on Grade
The ground floor of the proposed garage is anticipated to consist of concrete
slab on grade
A four 4 inch thick concrete slab on top of a minimum four 4 inch thick cushion
layer of No 3 B Fine gravel or ASTM C 33 No 67 gravel is recommended One 1 inch
of moist fine sand may be placed above the plastic membrane to help curing of the
concrete
The thickened edges of the concrete slab should be embedded a minimum of
twelve 12 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade

Site Grading
Prior to construction the site should be cleared and grubbed Any deleterious
materials such as vegetation debris construction rubbles and organic matters
encountered during grubbing and scarifying should be removed and properly disposed
of off site
The proposed house and driveway areas should be scarified to a depth of six 6
inches after site clearing and grubbing The scarified subgrade should be thoroughly
moisture conditioned and recompacted to obtain a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry
density as determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557 78
Any imported fill and backfill material that may be required should be tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to being transported to the site
The fills and backfills should be placed in loose horizontal lifts not exceeding ten
10 inches loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry
density as determined by ASTM D 1557 78
The site should be graded to provide drainage of surface runoff away from all
foundations
Retaining Wall
Concrete retaining walls may be required to provide different finish grades for the
proposed project The following design guidelines for retaining walls are recommended

Drainage
The finished lot grade outside the footings slabs and pavements should be
shaped to shed water away from foundations to avoid ponding conditions Also it is
advised that a gutter system be installed around the residence and that the gutter run off
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2766 00 Page 11
water be diverted as far away from the foundations and pavements as feasible Excessive
landscape watering near the foundations should also be avoided
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have concrete bottoms and
drains to prevent water from entering the foundation and slab subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the above
recommended foundation construction since ponded water could cause excessive
subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation
excavations should be properly backfilled tight against the footings and slabs immediately
after setting of the concrete to prevent ponding conditions
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained to drain
all surface run off away from the foundations and slabs
Design Review
Drawings and specifications for site grading utility and foundation construction
should be forwarded to the project geotechnical engineer for review and written
comments prior to advertisement for bids This review is needed to determine adherence
to the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this review is not
made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for misinterpretation of the
recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the footing excavations and backfilling be observed by a
competent geotechnical engineer during construction to see that adequate bearing has

LIMITATIONS
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations made in
accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation engineering principles and
practices This warranty is in lieu of all other warranties either expressed or implied
This report has been prepared in order to assist the architect and engineer in the
preparation of the foundation design of this project Therefore this report may not
contain sufficient data or the proper information for use to form the basis for preparation
of construction cost estimates or contract bidding A contractor wishing to bid on this
project is urged to retain a competent geotechnical engineer to assist in the interpretation
of this report and or performance of site specific exploration for bid estimating purposes
The locations of the field borings are approximate having been determined by
taping from corners of the subject lot The elevations of the field borings are estimated
from the elevations and contours shown on the as built grading plan by Richard Sato
Associates Inc undated The physical locations and elevations of the field borings
should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the method used

The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from field borings Variations of conditions between the field borings
may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident until
construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re
evaluate the recommendations given in this report
