PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The planned Kukui ula Development is a resort community encompassing about
1 045 acres in Koloa on the south shore of Kauai and includes a hotel vacation
ownership commercial park golf course and residential uses The Parcel I Aepo Nei
Subdivision project is part of the improvements for the Kukui ula Development
Parcel I is a subdivision project located at the north central portion of the
Kukui ula Resort Community development below the Aepoeha Reservoir overlooking
the 8th Hole of the Kukui ula Golf Course The parcel encompasses approximately 30 7
acres with 35 individual house lots designated as Lot Nos 1 through 35 and
approximately 4 417 feet of new access roads to the new house lots consisting of
flexible asphaltic pavements A Project Location Map Overall Site Plan and Site Plans
are shown on Plates 1 2 and 3 1 through 3 4 respectively
Site grading generally consisted of cuts and fills up to about 38 feet thick On site
soils generated from the cut areas were the primary source of fill material for the project
The project also included the installation of new infrastructure including CRM retaining
walls drainage sewer water and other utilities In addition roughly 425 linear feet of

PARCEL I EARTHWORK OPERATIONS
We observed the mass and pad grading underground utility construction and
roadway construction work for the Parcel I Aepo Nei Subdivision project from
through The mass and site grading work consisted of
clearing and grubbing cuts up to about 38 feet deep and placement and compaction of
fills up to about 38 feet thick to achieve the finished grades Prior to filling the existing
ground surface was cleared and grubbed of vegetation and deleterious material then
proof rolled with a compactor Earthworks Pacific Inc performed the mass and pad
grading and utility and roadway construction work
On site boulder and rock fills were placed within the deeper portions of Lot Nos 19
through 21 The cobble and boulder fills were seated with a dozer then compacted with a
large sheepsfoot wheel compactor
During the site grading operations samples of the materials utilized as fill were
obtained and transported to our office for testing The maximum dry densities and
optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general accordance with
ASTM D1557 Modified Proctor and the test results are summarized on Plate 4
The fill materials were moisture conditioned to near optimum moisture content and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as determined in
accordance with ASTM D1557 In the roadway areas the fill and backfill materials at the
finished subgrade were compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
During the mass and pad grading and utility and roadway construction operations
periodic field density tests were performed in general accordance with ASTM D6938
Nuclear Gauge Method Compaction test results are summarized on Plates 5 1 through
5 55 These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed
fills and backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and
interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed as
unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained and or
approved by our field representative based on visual observation and hand probing
During grading operations an existing tunnel approximately 548 feet in length
was encountered crossing below the Parcel I project site below Lot 36 and
39 at depths between 12 and 67 feet below the Parcel I finish ground surface The
