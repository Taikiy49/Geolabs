GENERAL SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located southwest of Kamehameha Highway and east of
Waikakalaua Gulch Waipio on the island of Oahu Hawaii Mililani Golf Course is located
along the southern boundary of the site The general location of the site and its vicinity
is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
In general the topography of the site is rugged with some steep slopes indicative
of basaltic lava flows at the surface or mantled with a thin soil cover
Elevations at the site vary from about 685 to 550 feet above Mean Sea Level
MSL
An asphalt paved road crosses the northwest corner of the site Most of the site
is densely vegetated with shrubs and small to large trees
SITE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
The central portion of the island of Oahu is composed of a basalt shield volcano
built by the extrusion of the lavas of the Koolau Volcanic Series
Ancient lava flows of the Koolau Volcanic Series cover the site These rocks are
generally characterized as flows of jointed dense vesicular basalt with interbedded thin
clinker layers Weathering of the Koolau lava flows has occurred for the last 1 to 2 million
years The chemical weathering has resulted in the nearly complete in situ mineralogical

EXPOSED SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Visual examination of the project site leads to the conjecture that most of the
subsurface conditions underlying the site are relatively uniform
Based on the scattered outcrops observed during our preliminary reconnaissance
it appears that most of the site is underlain by residual soils basalt saprolite with relatively
low expansion potential
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our reconnaissance of the area our preliminary findings and
recommendations are presented below
General
Our reconnaissance indicates that the site is suitable for the proposed residential
development from a geotechnical engineering viewpoint
The basalt saprolite and residual soils observed throughout most of the project site
are generally competent and should provide adequate bearing for the proposed
development The excavated materials should provide a suitable source of fill material
if reduced to less than 12 inches in size

Site Grading
In general it is our opinion that conventional grading methods may be used for the
site and roadway grading of the proposed development Some hard rock zones may
required hoe ramming depending on the excavation depths
Prior to commencement of grading surface vegetation and organic matter should
be cleared and grubbed The resulting spoils should be disposed of properly off site or
stockpiled for use in landscaping
Soft or yielding areas encountered during clearing and grubbing should be over
excavated to firm natural material and the resulting depression backfilled with well
compacted fill
Any existing miscellaneous or rubbish fill should be removed and wasted off site
In general the excavated soil or rocky materials may be suitable as a source of
fill material
In general the excavated rock materials if reduced to less than 12 inches in size
may be suitable as fill material The material if reduced to less than six 6 inches in size
should be adequate as a source of trench and structural backfill if needed Imported
material if required should be non expansive with no particles greater than six 6 inches
in size and no more than fifteen 15 percent passing the 200 sieve It is recommended
that if additional off site borrow is required it should be tested by the geotechnical
engineer to determine if it is suitable for its intended use prior to its delivery to the jobsite

Additional Exploration
This reconnaissance was performed on the basis visual observations of the
exposed site conditions The subsurface conditions within volcanic formations may be
variable and complex The discussions and findings presented in this report are general
in nature and as such should not be considered adequate for final design
We strongly recommend that a full scale geotechnical exploration including
borings field and laboratory testing be developed and implemented prior to the final
design stage of the subject project This exploration is recommended to obtain data for
analyses pertaining to allowable bearing capacity foundation support systems slope
Stability and building setbacks and earthwork design Our findings or recommendations
presented herein are contingent upon the performance of such an exploration by the
geotechnical engineer
LIMITATIONS
The findings and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from visual observations of conditions exposed at the site
Variations of conditions may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not
become evident until construction or until a detailed field exploration is performed If
variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations
given in this report
