Based on our review of the rainfall information and monthly precipitation summaries
for the current 6 month monitoring period the months of September 2004 through
February 2005 experienced well above average rainfall totals associated with significant
storm events The above average rainfall continues a generally wetter than normal trend
that began in late 2003 and continued through 2004
The increase in earth movements recorded by Inclinometer Nos I 5 I 12 and I 13
may be attributed to the well above average rainfall experienced during the past 6 month
monitoring period The earth movements recorded by the remaining operational
inclinometers appears to be within the normal range of earth movement previously
recorded by the instruments
In general our interpretation of the earth movement response to elevated rainfall is
that if a period of increased or above normal rainfall is experienced following a prolonged
period of normal or lower than normal rainfall the earth movement rates at some of the
inclinometers tend to increase abruptly following the period of elevated precipitation and
then settle back to normal levels

Observations Pavement Structural Deformation
A general reconnaissance of the project site was conducted on March 3 2005 to
record general observations pertaining to the existing site conditions
Waiomao Road Area
The existing elongated longitudinal pavement cracks that were sealed to reduce the
potential for appreciable surface water infiltration along the north side of Waiomao
Road in the vicinity of Area B continue to reside in fair condition Additional
asphalt patch repair does not appear to be necessary at this time
The existing arc shaped pavement cracks that cross Waiomao Road along the
western boundary of the Waiomao Landslide were sealed previously to reduce the
potential for appreciable surface water infiltration The previously sealed
arc shaped cracks in the roadway appear to be experiencing continued very slow
deformation consisting of increasing vertical offset across portions of the length of
the crack As a result the bumps and undulations surrounding the arc shaped
cracks continue to very slowly become more pronounced Additional asphalt patch
repair of this area probably is not necessary at this time
The continued settlement of the roadway pavement surrounding the existing storm
drain manhole fronting 2340 Waiomao Road was observed The pavement
settlement has produced a noticeable bump located along the north side of the
southwest bound travel lane In addition the eastern boundary of the Waiomao

Kuahea Street Area
Observation of the street area along Kuahea Street at the head of the Waiomao
Landslide indicates the roadway pavement and northern road shoulder continue to
experience settlement of the ground surfaces The ground settlements have
produced uneven pavement surfaces with significant bumps that traverse the
roadway Large open cracks 0 5 inches to 1 inch wide and localized settlement
pits of about 1 foot in diameter have formed along the existing pavement repair
patches in the center of the road Localized asphalt patch repair is recommended
to seal the localized larger holes and cracks located in the roadway surface
Observation of the area surrounding the intersection of Kuahea Place and Kuahea
Street and extending downhill toward Helo Place indicates continued surface
deformation of the concrete pavement and sections of concrete curbing It appears
that most of the pavement cracks and the open crack separation along the curb
fronting the 2334 Kuahea Street address were recently patched with cement grout
The pavement patches may have reduced the high volume of groundwater
seepage normally encountered at Piezometer No P 2A as evidenced by the
decrease in head levels recorded since about April 2004
Vacant Graded Hillslopes
The existing graded hillslopes located within the footprint of the Waiomao Landslide
as well as most of the area along Kipona Place at the toe of the landslide do not
exhibit overt signs of earth movement or ground surface deformation Our
observation of the landscaped area indicates that large open ground cracks and
unusual ground surface deformation is not visibly apparent
During a recent site visit we observed a significant mass of soils containing
construction debris and organic matter which was deposited within the eastern
portion of the property identified as 2324 Waiomao Road Based on our
observations it was not determined whether the soil mass represents a temporary
stockpile or permanent fill embankment The soil mass appears to range in height
from approximately 5 to 15 feet with the eastern toe of the soil mass partially
encroaching upon the vacant City lot comprising the adjacent Waiomao Landslide
area
We will monitor the potential effect of the recent soil deposit on the on going earth
movements recorded at Inclinometer Nos I 3 and I 6 which are located in the
vacant lot adjacent to the soil deposit
