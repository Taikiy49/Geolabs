Review of Surcharge Settlement Data
The Home Depot Pearl City
Pearl City Oahu Hawaii
Dear Mr Esplen
In accordance with your request we have reviewed the settlement readings
transmitted to us on October 20 It is our understanding that the project site was graded
and surcharged with about 5 feet of surcharge fill in accordance with the project plans and
specifications Four settlement gages were installed by the contractor after the surcharge
was placed and the elevations of the gages were monitored by surveyors from Park
Engineering
The initial readings were taken on and continued weekly until
Gage Nos 1 and 4 western side of surcharge fill indicated settlements
of about 3 1 to 3 7 inches while Gage Nos 2 and 3 eastern side of surcharge fill indicated
less settlements of about 0 7 to 2 4 inches Based on the surveyed readings it appears
that a large majority of the settlements occurred during the first two weeks and have
stopped or slowed considerably Therefore it is our opinion that the settlements have
stabilized and the surcharge fill may be removed
We appreciate the opportunity to be of service to you on this project If you have
questions or need additional information please contact our office

Site Observations
Retaining Wall A Excavation
Home Depot Pearl City
Pearl City Oahu Hawaii
Dear Mr Esplen
In accordance with your request a site visit was made to observe the condition of
the excavation for Retaining Wall A Present at the site was Mike Peltier of Maryl
Construction
It is our understanding that due to a slight misalignment of the vertical rebars there
was a concern that the lateral resistance of the wall may have been reduced Since rock
formation was visible in the excavation behind the wall it was desired to re evaluate the
design lateral pressures on the retaining wall based on the actual site conditions
At the time of our visit most of the CMU had been placed along Retaining
Wall A The wall was about 8 feet high Hard basalt formation was exposed in the cut
behind the wall However the near vertical cut was about 6 to 8 feet behind the heel of
the wall and therefore out of the primary zone of influence of the lateral pressure on the
wall For this reason the lateral pressure on the wall would be determined by the quality
of backfill used to backfill behind the wall If high quality backfill such as select borrow is
used the design lateral earth pressures may be reduced to 30 pcf If further reduction is
needed flowable fill CLSM may be used to backfill the wall This would essentially
eliminate the static lateral earth loads on the wall
If you have questions or need additional information please contact our office
