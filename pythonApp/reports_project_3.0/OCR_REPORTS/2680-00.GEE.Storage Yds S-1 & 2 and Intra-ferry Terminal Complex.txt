INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Storage Yards S 1 S 2 and Intra Ferry Terminal Complex
in Barbers Point Harbor on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location of the site
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2680 00 Page 2
and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The exploration was
performed in general accordance with our proposal dated
This report summarizes our findings from the field exploration and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations were derived from our analyses based on the
data obtained during the exploration These recommendations are intended for design
input and are not intended to form the basis for preparation of construction cost estimates
or for bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project will include construction of a ferry pier and rigid and flexible pavement
structures as follows
1 Yard S 1 Approximately 4 5 acres of rigid pavement with Hyster 920 Hi Lift
loading and drainage inlet structures
2 Yard S 2 Approximately 3 4 acres of asphaltic concrete pavement for
automobile parking and access roadway The vehicle traffic will be primarily
passenger cars Occasional maintenance use for H20 44 trucks is
anticipated
3 Ferry Pier The pier will extend outward about 150 feet from the shore into
the harbor The foundation system of the pier is to resist mooring and
berthing forces generated from ferry and tug boats
Also planned are revetment for shore protection and electric light poles at the yards
and along the shoreline A small quantity of dredging may be required

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface soil
conditions at the project site in order to develop a generalized soil rock data set for the
formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to design of the ferry
pier foundations and storage yards
The work was done in general accordance with our proposal to Mitsunaga
Associates Inc dated The scope of work was limited to the following
tasks and work effort
1 Review of available soils and geologic information pertinent to the project
site and its vicinity
2 Mobilization and demobilization of a skid mounted drill rig on a raft to drill
two 2 off shore borings to depths of 100 feet below the water level along
the proposed pier structure
3 Mobilization and demobilization of a truck mounted drill rig to drill 17 borings
on land to depths of 6 5 to 55 0 feet below the existing ground surface
4 Seven 7 field CBR tests performed on the in situ soils encountered in the
shallow on land borings
5 Coordination of the field exploration and logging of the drilled borings by a
geologist from our firm
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2680 00 Page 4
6 Laboratory testing of selected samples obtained during the field exploration
to determine the classification and engineering properties of the materials
encountered
7 Engineering analyses of the field and laboratory data for the formulation of
geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to the design of the
proposed ferry pier and container yards
8 Preparation of this report presenting the findings and design
recommendations
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration and laboratory and field CBR testing
are presented in the appendices of this report
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located at the southwestern side of the Barbers Point Harbor
The recently constructed Piers P 5 and P 6 are to the east of the project site Based on
our observation the thickness of the existing concrete pavement for Piers P 5 and P 6
appears to be on the order of 15 inches
The on land portion of the site is generally level with elevations ranging from about
7 0 to 10 0 feet above Mean Sea Level Along the bank the previously dredged slope is
appears to be at an elevation of approximately 40 feet MSL
Currently the project site is being used by the Marisco Company Bushes were
observed at the southern portion near the entrance of the site A pier approximately 260
feet long is located on the western side of the site

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Exploration Program
The exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling two 2 off shore
borings to depths of 100 feet below water level and 17 on land borings to depths ranging
from 6 5 to 55 0 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate locations of the
borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The locations of the off shore borings for the proposed ferry pier were based on
original plans provided to us on Subsequent to the field exploration
the proposed pier location was revised and moved about 150 feet to the west
Based on our previous pile driving experience during construction of the adjacent
Piers P 5 and P 6 and the current exploration results it is our opinion that the drilled
borings are representative of the subsoil conditions in the general area and may be used
for the design of the ferry pier
At the time of our report preparation it was our understanding that the final pier
location had not been determined and further revision may be anticipated It is
recommended that the final pier location when available be forwarded to the
geotechnical engineer for review when available
Subsoil Conditions
The two 2 off shore borings generally encountered a relatively competent coral
ledge between approximately 60 to 70 feet below the Mean Sea Level Medium dense
sand gravel and weak coral were then encountered through the maximum depth drilled
of 100 feet The water depths at the boring locations are on the order of 40 feet

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Due to the 40 feet of water depth in the harbor basin at the proposed pier location
it is anticipated that large size precast concrete piles will be required to provide foundation
support for the ferry pier The foundation system is anticipated to provide lateral
resistance against mooring and berthing forces generated by the ferry and tug boats
Relatively thick rigid concrete pavement is also anticipated for Yard S 1 due to the
heavy traffic loads of Hyster 920 forklift with an anticipated maximum design axle load of
292 5 kips
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2680 00 Page 7
Ferry Pier Foundation
Based on information provided to us it appears that a batter pile system on 20 feet
centers along the pier will be adopted to provide lateral resistance against forces
generated by the ships The piles will extend through about 40 feet of water and be
embedded in the coral formation
It is also our understanding that two 2 batter piles will be required on land to
provide lateral resistance along the longitudinal direction of the pier
Predrilling is recommended to facilitate driving of batter piles through 40 feet of
water in the hard coral formation for the off shore piles and through the competent near
surface coral for the on land piles
Batter Piles
Due to the anticipated large shear and moment resulting from the ships and over
40 feet of free standing length twenty inch 20 inch square precast prestressed
concrete piles are recommended for the foundation support
Based on information provided a batter pile system will be utilized A batter of 5
vertical to 1 horizontal or 12 vertical to 2 4 horizontal may be used
Vertical and Lateral Pile Capacities
The piles may develop bearing capacity mainly by end bearing on the underlying
coral ledges and a small portion from the friction adhesion between the pile shafts
and surrounding soils
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2680 00 Page 8
An allowable vertical capacity of 100 tons per pile may be used A minimum pile
spacing of five 5 feet on centers is recommended
Typically the lateral capacity of a batter pile will depend on the compression pile
Capacity and the pile batter angle For on land piles with a batter of 5 vertical to
1 horizontal an allowable lateral capacity of 40 kips may be used
For off shore piles with the same batter angle it is recommended that the
allowable lateral capacity be reduced to 30 kips per pile to account for potential
difficulties and uncertainties in maintaining the recommended batter during pile
installation due to the presence of over 40 feet of water
Pile Lengths
For driving of the off shore piles it is anticipated that temporary installation of steel
H piles and templates will be required for positioning the piles Therefore for
construction purposes the pile length should be long enough to account for the
height of the temporary set up which limits the lowest elevation the pile driving
hammer can reach
Based on the exploration results it appears that the off shore piles should extend
to about 65 feet below water level to provide the design capacity For budgeting
purposes a total pile length of 80 feet may be used for off shore batter piles
For on land piles based on available exploration results it is our opinion that a
total pile length of 35 feet may be used for budgeting purposes
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2680 00 Page 9
The above pile lengths are for initial cost estimation purposes The actual
production pile lengths should be determined after completion of a test pile
program
Pile Driving Criteria
The piles should be driven with a hammer capable of delivering a rated energy of
about 50 000 foot pounds The hammer should be equipped with an energy level
control so that driving within the loose deposits may be controlled at a reduced
energy to minimize the tensile stresses development within the piles which could
cause damage cracking to the piles
Additional pile driving criteria are as follows
1 The driving process should be continuous without interruption for each pile
A pile may be rejected or its design capacity modified when the driving
process is interrupted for more than four 4 hours
2 During easy driving when the pile tip is still within the loose sandy soil the
hammer stroke should be carefully controlled reduced energy so that
excessive tensile stresses are not induced in the pile
3 The pile driving may be terminated when the blow counts steadily increase
with depth and reach 5 blows per inch for the last three inches or when
aggregated blow counts are 40 blows for the last foot
Predrilling
Predrilling is recommended to facilitate pile driving in the coral formation A
minimum diameter of 30 inches is recommended for the predrill auger
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2680 00 Page 10
For the off shore piles predrilling down to elevation 50 feet is recommended for
driving the batter piles over about 40 feet of water Since the harbor bottom elevations
may vary at different locations the predrilling depths may vary correspondingly depending
on the individual pile locations
For the on land piles a predrilling depth of 20 feet below the existing ground
surface is recommended to help driving through the competent near surface coral The
sandy cuttings generated from the drilling operation may be used to backfill the predrilled
holes after placement of the piles
The above recommended predrill depth should be verified and they may be revised
after the test pile program
Indicator Pile Program
An indicator pile program should be undertaken to fulfill the following objectives
1 To examine the drivability of piles with the use of one or more selected
hammers
2 To more accurately determine production pile order lengths and to further
verify delineate different areas with different production pile lengths
3 To help evaluate and modify if necessary the predrill depths and driving
criteria
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2680 00 Page 11
To achieve these objectives approximately 10 of the total number of piles
required for the project is recommended as indicator piles for test driving The indicator
piles may be retained and incorporated into the final foundation system
When the final foundation plan is available the project geotechnical engineer
should be consulted for selection of the indicator pile locations The indicator piles should
be casted 10 feet longer than the estimated pile lengths Also the indicator pile program
should provide flexibility by allowing for addition of or relocation of the indicator piles as
deemed necessary to suit the conditions encountered in the field
A number of splice cans should be made available at the site during driving of the
on land piles Splice cans may be fabricated with one quarter inch thick high grade steel
and should be at least five 5 feet long
As a part of the indicator pile program one 1 pile load test should be budgeted
for an on land pile The selected indicator pile should be load tested to twice the
allowable load capacity in accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1143
The indicator piles should be driven with the same hammer which will be used for
production pile driving The indicator pile program should be conducted under close and
continuous field observation of the project geotechnical engineer who will determine when
piles may be stopped and to provide guidance should additional measures be required
due to unforeseen driving conditions
The geotechnical engineer should be consulted to evaluate the final pile bearing
Capacity and production pile lengths based on the results of the indicator pile driving and
the pile load test

Potential Weak Zones in Coral Formation
Our previous pile driving experience during construction of the adjacent Piers P 5
P 6 indicated that the underlying bearing coral ledges may contain localized weak loose
zones Therefore it is anticipated that some piles may not provide the design capacity at
the pre determined depths during pile driving
Due to the 40 feet of water splice cans are generally not feasible for off shore piles
because of the following reasons 1 There is no assurance that a splice can once
attached could be driven down over 40 feet of water and below the predrilled depth and
2 Splice cans should not be left in the water due to corrosion concerns
Based on our previous experience additional piles or longer piles will be required
to provide the design capacity should localized weak zones be encountered during pile
driving Therefore a contingency fund is recommended to accommodate the cost for
these possible extra piles

Pavement Design
Based on information provided to us rigid concrete pavement is planned for Yard
S 1 and asphaltic concrete pavement for Yard S 2 The rigid concrete pavement Yard
S 1 will be designed to support Hyster 920 Hi Lift forklift with an anticipated maximum
axle load of 292 5 kips The asphaltic concrete pavement Yard S 2 is for passenger
parking and should be designed for maintenance truck H20 44 traffic
Relatively competent coral formation was encountered near the existing ground
surface and seven 7 field CBR tests were performed to evaluate the strength
characteristics for pavement support The field CBR values generally ranged between 42
to over 180
In general coral materials often produce high CBR values under static test loading
conditions However due to the long term dynamic loading effects of the heavy forklift
and the tendency of the coral materials to break down under repeated dynamic loadings
it is recommended that a reduced CBR value be used in the pavement design A design
CBR value of 45 was selected in our analyses to account for some localized soil variability
and long term strength degradation

Pavement Joints
Joints in rigid concrete pavements should be provided to 1 allow contraction and
expansion caused by temperature and moisture changes in the pavements 2 relieve
warping and curling stresses caused by temperature differences within pavements 3
prevent irregular or random cracking of pavements from unequal subgrade support of
other causes 4 control shrinkage and cracking and 5 expedite construction
Joints should be provided in general accordance with the recommendations of the
Portland Cement Association
Pavement Drainage
Surface run off from the pavement areas should be collected and drained to a
Suitable discharge point Water should not be allowed to pond immediately adjacent to

the pavement areas where practicable The subject site should be graded during initial
construction to provide positive drainage away from the pavement sections
Site Grading
Site grading involving cut up to three 3 feet and a small amount of fill is
anticipated for the project Relatively hard excavation is anticipated in the competent near
surface coral formation
Site Preparation
At the start of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All existing vegetation rubbish old footings and
pavements and other deleterious materials should be removed and disposed of
properly off site
After clearing and grubbing areas at grade or designated to receive fill should be
proof rolled Any loose or soft spots encountered at the pavement subgrade level
should be removed and the depression replaced with select granular material
compacted to a minimum of 95 of the maximum dry density as determined by
ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Fill Material
It is Our opinion that the on site excavated coral material may be used as fill
material provided that the materials are processed down to relatively well graded
six 6 inches minus size
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2680 00 Page 16
The excavated on site coral material is not suitable for use as fill under water level
due to the amount of fines For backfill under water level self compacting clean
granular material such as 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 is recommended
to a minimum level of 12 inches above the highest anticipated groundwater level
If required all imported fill material should consist of non expansive select granular
materials less than 3 inches in maximum size with less than 20 passing the 200
sieve and with a minimum CBR value of 45 when tested in accordance with ASTM
Test Method D 1883 The fill material should be tested and approved by the
geotechnical engineer prior to transportation to the site
Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
No major fill placement is anticipated for the project The fill backfill should be
placed in horizontal loose level lifts up to maximum 10 inches thick and compacted
to a minimum of 95 of the maximum dry density as determined by ASTM Test
Method D 1557 78
Except the riprap for shore protection where boulders will be used alll fill or backfill
below the water level should consist of clean granular material such as 3B Fine
gravel recommended above This material is generally self compacting Additional
densification may be limited to the use of a vibratory compactor on the final level
of the material
Light Pole Foundation
t is our understanding that 60 foot high light poles will be constructed for the
project Drilled shaft foundations are recommended to support each light pole
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2680 00 Page 17
Since the shafts will be subjected to repeated wind loads a critical consideration
will be to limit permanent lateral deflections of the shafts
The vertical compressive load on the poles is relatively light and may be supported
by the friction and end bearing resistance between the shafts and the subsoils It is our
opinion that a 36 inch diameter drilled shaft embedded seven 7 feet below the ground
surface will be adequate to support each light pole
Drilled Shaft Construction
The drilled shaft construction should be closely monitored by the geotechnical
engineer in order to see that adequate foundation support is achieved Since a
portion of the loading capacity come from the frictional resistance between the
shafts and the in situ soils extra care should be exercised to the drilling of the
shaft hole selection of a non shrinkage concrete mix design and the placement of
concrete
Prompt placement of concrete in the prepared hole as soon as _it is completed
is recommended to reduce caving in of the hole The concrete mix should be
designed to minimize shrinkage in order to maintain high friction contact between
the in situ soils and shaft The concrete should be placed in a suitable manner to
avoid segregation of the aggregates
Utility Trench
Construction of new utility lines is anticipated for the project A granular bedding
consisting of six 6 inches of ASTM C 33 No 67 gravel 3 Fine is recommended under
the pipes
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2680 00 Page 18
Granular materials should be used for trench backfill The backfill up to about one
1 foot above the pipes should be lightly tamped to reduce the possibility of damaging
the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level one 1 foot above the pipes
to the finish grade should be placed in maximum 10 inch horizontal loose lifts
mechanically compacted to a minimum of 90 compaction per ASTM Test Method
D 1557 78 to protect against potential future ground subsidence

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from field borings Variations of conditions between the field data
points may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident
until construction If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations given in this report
The locations of the field data points were approximately determined by taping from
objects indicated on the topographic map dated by R M Towill
Corporation Elevations of the field data points were interpreted from the same map The
physical location and the elevation of the field data points should be considered accurate
only to the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the field data points
depict the approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a
gradual transition The strata lines shown on profiles or cross sections are based upon
interpolation between field data points and may not represent actual subsurface
conditions
Water level data from the field data points was collected at the times given on the
graphic representations and or in the text of this report This data has been reviewed
and interpretations made in the formulation of this report However it must be noted that
fluctuation may occur due to variation in rainfall tides temperature and other factors

APPENDIX C
Field Testing
Seven 7 field CBR tests were performed on the near surface soils near boring
locations to evaluate their strength characteristics for pavement subgrade subbase base
support
The field CBR tests were carried out in general accordance with ASTM D 4429 84
Load penetration curves of the individual in place CBR tests are presented on Plates C 1
through C 7
A truck mounted drilling rig with approximately seven 7 tons of dead weight
was used for the load reaction The truck was placed over the location so that one of
the I beams at the rear end was located directly above the center of the hole A
hydraulic jack was then used to jack against this I beam with its piston pipe extensions
and penetration piston connected and resting on the soil
A dial gauge graduated in units of 0 001 inch was used to measure the
deflection and the pressure gauge attached to the hydraulic jack was used to measure
the applied load
