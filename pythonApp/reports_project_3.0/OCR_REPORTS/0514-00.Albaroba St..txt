SUMMARY OF RECOMMENDATIONS
The soils below the basement floor level are firm
deposits of silty coral sand and coral fragments
and the proposed structure may be supported on
spread footings established within these firm layers
Spread footings may be designed for a soil bearing
value of 5000 pounds per square foot
INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our foundation investigation
for the proposed 15 story apartment building located at Algaroba
Street in Honolulu Hawaii The purposes of this investigation
were to explore the subsurface conditions at the site and to provide
data for use in design and construction of foundations
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is presently planned to construct a 15 story building of rein
forced concrete shear wall construction The building will be
approximately 60 by 150 feet in planned dimension The lower two

W O 514 00
floors will contain perimeter parking There will be a basement
area with the floor at elevation 5 some 2 feet below the
existing street level Anticipated maximum vertical wall loads
will be on the order of 57 kips per lineal foot
SITE CONDITIONS
Surface
The site is approximately 180 feet long and 116 feet deep and
located on the makai side of Algaroba Street near its intersection
with Makahiki Way At the time of our investigation the site was
occupied by numerous wooden frame buildings The site is fairly
level and the present grade is approximately 2 feet deep below
the adjacent Algaroba Street level Numerous abandoned cesspools
were found throughout the site
Subsurface Conditions
Geologically the site is underlain by a coral reef formation which
consists of firm deposits of calcareous coral sands sediments In
order to explore the subsurface conditions at the site a total of
5 borings were drilled The locations of these borings are shown
on the Site Plan Plate 1 A more detailed description of the
field exploration and laboratory testing is given in the Appendix
which also contains the Log of Borings and results of the labora
tory testing
Page 3
W
The site is immediately underlain by a clayey silt fill that varies
in thickness from 3 to 4 feet below the surface Underlying the
fill calcareous coral sand sediments were encountered and extended
to the depth of our boring which extended to 40 feet The coral
sediments which are classified as silty sand on the Boring Logs
contained varying amounts of cemented coral fragments The degree
of denseness varied depending on the degree of cementation The
coral deposits are very dense becoming dense to medium dense at
lower depths However because of the variability of reef envir
onments over short horizontal and vertical distances the possibility
of encountering coral or soils with characteristics different than
those found in the boreholes cannot be discounted
Groundwater was encountered in all our borings at elevation 0 to
1 feet
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
nn
Foundations
It is recommended the proposed structure be supported on spread or
continuous footings placed a minimum of 1 foot in the coral material
and at least 2 feet below adjacent floor level Footings may be
designed to impose a dead plus real live load pressure of 5000 pounds
per square foot A one third increase may be used when considering
wind or seismic loads

W O 514 00
The maximum ultimate settlement of the proposed building supported
on spread footings as recommended will be on the order of one half
inch Differential settlements will be less than one fourth inch
Due to the high variability of coral formations it is our opinion
that all footing excavations be inspected by the Soils Engineer to
confirm the conditions on which our recommendations are based
Any encountered loose areas should be compacted to a minimum of
90 of maximum density
Lateral Pressures
Lateral loads may be resisted by the passive resistance of the soils
and by soil friction A coefficient of friction of 0 5 may be used
between the supporting soils and footings The passive resistance
of the soils at foundation level either natural undisturbed soil
or compacted fill may be assumed to be equal to the pressure developed
by a fluid with a density of 300 pounds per cubic foot
Excavation
It is our opinion that excavation of the coral materials can be
accomplished with normal excavating equipment Some local hard
coral areas may require hard ripping or jack hammering

Retaining Walls
The active lateral pressure developed against a wall will depend
upon the unit weight of the retained soil the inherent strength
of the soil the angle of friction between wall and soil the
presence of groundwater and whether the wall will be allowed to
yield Basement walls should be designed to resist the active
lateral pressure developed by the soil behind the wall
Laterally restrained walls that will be backfilled after the wall
is an integral part of the structure should be designed to resist
a fluid weighing 45 pounds per cubic foot
For a yielding wall where the backfill is placed prior to connecting
the wall to the structure the wall should be designed to resist
lateral pressures developed by a fluid density of 35 pounds per
cubic foot
The backfill immediately against the wall should be an open graded
compacted granular material a minimum of 12 inches wide An
adequate drainage system should be designed to prevent the buildup
of excessive pore pressures behind the wall Additional backfill
should be mechanically compacted in layers to a minimum of 90 of
maximum density per

Slabs on Grade
Slabs on grade should be placed on a minimum of 4 inches of com
pacted open graded granular material This gravel will provide a
capillary break beneath the slab The upper 12 inches of sub
grade soils should be compacted to a minimum of 90 of maximum
density
Site Preparation
After all debris structures and utility lines have been removed
from the site the ground surface should be stripped and cleared
of all deleterious material The upper 12 inches of existing
material should be compacted to a minimum of 90 of maximum density
on slab and pavement areas
All voids created by the removal of structures and utility lines
should be scarified brought to optimum moisture and compacted to
at least 90 of maximum density Fill shall then be placed in
horizontal lifts and also compacted
Fill subgrade soils should be inspected and tested by the engineer
prior to placing base materials
Old cesspools should be located and all sludge completely removed
When an old cesspool is located below a footing area the sidewalls

APPENDIX
FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface soils at the site were explored by drilling 5
test borings utilizing truck mounted rotary auger equipment
The depths of the borings ranged from 22 to 40 feet below the
existing ground surface The boring locations are shown on the
Site Plan Plate 1
The soils were classified by visual and textural examinations
in the field by our engineer who continuously supervised the
drilling oeprations The classifications were later verified
by inspection and testing in the laboratory All soils were
classified in accordance with the Unified Soil Classification
System and corals with local geologic terminology Graphical
representations of the encountered materials are presented on
the Boring Logs Plates A 1A to A 1E Soil samples were obtained
by driving a sampler with a 140 pound hammer free falling a
distance of 30 inches The blow counts required to drive the
sampler the final 12 inches are presented on the Boring Logs
at the appropriate sample depth

LABORATORY TESTING
Attempts were made to determine the strength characteristics of
the coral materials However due to the high content of large
coral fragments it was difficult to obtain adequate samples
for strength testing Correlations of strength with blow counts
were made with data of coral materials from previous investigation
by Geolabs Hawaii
In place moisture and density determinations were made on all
undisturbed samples The results of these tests are presented
on the Log of Borings at the appropriate sample location
Two Grain Size Analyses were made of disturbed coral materials
