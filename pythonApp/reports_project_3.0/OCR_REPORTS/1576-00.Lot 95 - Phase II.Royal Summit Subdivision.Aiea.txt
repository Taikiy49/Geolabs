Soil Engineering Report
Lot 95 Royal Summit Subdivision
Phase II
Aiea Cahu Hawaii
In accordance with your request we have made a study of the near
surface soil conditions at the above referenced lot in order to provide
foundation recommendations for the proposed residence The study
consisted of drilling three shallow borings performing laboratory
testing and reviewing the subdivision site grading and preliminary house
foundation plans This report summarizes our findings and recommenda
tions
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
Based on the preliminary house plans the proposed residence will
be a two story structure approximately 43 by 56 feet in plan dimensions
with a 2 car garage

Concrete slab on grades are planned for the basement level
driveway and garage area The house will be of wood frame construction
CMU bearing walls are planned for the entire lower level of the
house CRM retaining walls generally less than 11 feet high are
anticipated to provide grade separation along at the rear lot of the
house and along the eastern boundary of the lot
Cuts up to 8 5 feet thick are anticipated below the residence to
accommodate the proposed basement level

Site Plan Plate l

SUBSURFACE SOIL CONDITIONS
Three borings were drilled within Lot 35 to depths of 5 to 10 feet
below the existing grade at the approximate locations shown on the Site
Plan Plate 1
In general the borings encountered hard grayish brown to gray
clayey silt to the full depth of our borings A review of the mass
grading plan for Royal Summit Phase II dated prepared by
Community Planning Inc indicated that Lot 95 was cut up to 20 feet
below the natural grade
No groundwater was encountered at the time of our field
exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATTONS
General
The hard grayish brown to gray clayey silt scil encountered
generally can provide suitable foundation support for the proposed house
provided that some care is exercised during construction to minimize the
swelling potential of the soil
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Page 4
Laboratory tests performed on the hard clayey silt soil indicates
that this material generally has low to moderate swelling potential
depending on the moisture content of the soil
Therefore to minimize effects due to soil swelling under on grade
slabs we reconmend that the on site scil be capped with at least 12
inches of non expansive select fill and that the subgrade soils
underneath the select fill and slab be moistened and protected from
drying from the start of construction until the slab concrete placement
Select non expansive soils should also be used as backfill behind
the retaining walls to minimize the additional pressure due to the
swelling potential of the natural soil
Lot Grading
The fills and backfills within the top 12 inches minimum below
on grade slabs should consist of non expansive soil such as select
borrow crusher waste or coral Where cut is anticipated such as the
garage and the basement level the on site soil should be over excavated
to provide a 12 inch minimum thickness cf non expansive select fill
capping
All imported fill materials should be tested and approved by the
soils engineer prior to being used in the site
GEOLABS HAWAIi
W O 1576 00 Page 5
Prior to construction the area to receive any fill should be
cleared of vegetation and debris
The fills should be placed in horizontal lifts not exceeding 8
inches in compacted thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its
maximum Cry density per ASTM D 1557 78
Fills placed on existing slopes should start from the bottom up and
should be keyed and benched to bond the new fill to the existing slope
The site should be graded to provide drainage of surface runoff
away from all foundations
House Foundations
It is anticipated that the entire house will be of slab on grade
construction and supported by continuous wall footings individual spread
footings or retaining walls
Spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the proposed
residence An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f may be used on the
compacted soil or stiff natural ground
Interior footings on level areas should be embedded a minimum of 8
inches below the finished grade Perimeter wall footings should be
embedded a minimum of 12 inches below the adjacent finished grade

Surface water should be diverted away from foundations
Slab On Grade
It should be noted that some of the on site clayey silt soils are
moderately expansive and are marginally suitable to provide direct slab
support Therefore the on site soil should be over excavated down to a
depth of 12 inches below the slab bottom thoroughly moistened and then
replaced with non expansive imported scils as previously discussed The
thickened edge of the slab should be embedded a minimum of 12 inches
below the lowest adjacent finish grade
To further reduce future post construction swelling of the
underlying expansive soils we recommend that the in place clayey silt
below the upper capping fill pad be kept moist for one week prior to
concrete placement heavily soaked with water for the first two days and
sprinkled with water for the remainder of the week
A 4 inch layer of 3B Fine gravel cushion fill should be provided
below the slab In order to protect against future moisture
infiltration an impervious moisture membrane may be incorporated below
the slab The on grade slabs should be structurally separated from wall
foundations

Concrete Driveway
Since the driveway can tolerate some amount of heaving for
economy the 12 inch on site soil over excavation and replacement may be
eliminated if some small slab cracking is tolerable within the driveway
It is critical that the subgrade soils should be maintained in a moist
condition and prevented from drying out prior to concrete placement
Joints are recommended in the driveway slab at about 8 to 10 foot
intervals and at discontinuities in order to allow for some relative
movement of the concrete
