Preliminary Geotechnical Engineering Exploration
FY93 H 304 H 305 Family Housing Project
Marine Corps Base Hawaii Kaneohe Bay
Kaneohe Oahu Hawaii
Subsequent to the issuance of our Preliminary Geotechnical Engineering Exploration
Report dated we have conducted additional site reconnaissance of the upper
slopes of the project site to address concerns regarding the potential for boulder fall and the
impact it may have on the project development
Our recent reconnaissance of the upper slopes by a Geologist from our firm revealed
a heavy concentration of boulder deposits on the intermediate and upper slopes of the eastern
portion of Pu u Hawai iloa The basalt boulders ranged in size up to approximately 8 to 10 feet
in diameter and appeared to be stacked angular and well graded in size suggesting they are
colluvial rock fall deposits This heavy concentration of boulders was observed to end at about
the break in slope between the intermediate slope grade and the lower gentle slope grade
Scattered large boulders up to approximately 5 to 6 feet in diameter were observed within the
lower gentle slopes of the proposed building area of the housing development The occurrence
of these scattered boulders suggest that in the past boulders have rolled downslope beyond
the upper boulder concentration and have been deposited on these lower gentle slopes
Therefore it is our opinion that it is possible for future rock fall events to deposit large boulders
within the eastern area of the proposed housing development
Two prominent rocky knolls on the northeastern side of the Pu u at about the 330 foot
elevation appear to be the source area of the observed boulder deposits Deep fracturing
observed in this large rock outcrop suggest the potential for future cleaving of boulder blocks
is possible These large blocks if they maintain enough energy may have the potential to roll

Our reconnaissance ofthe remaining southeast through southwestern upper slope areas
revealed relatively few scattered boulders generally ranging from 2 to 4 feet in diameter These
boulders had likely fallen from adjacent upslope areas of that portion of the site The boulders
were generally isolated and confined to the upper portions of the project boundary The risk
of boulder fall on these slopes appears to be low
As a result of our findings in the eastern area of the project site we recommend a field
survey be conducted to stake and plot the lower boundary of the heavy boulder deposit for
possible relocation of the upper development limits
At this time we would like to reiterate the fact that we have not yet received clearance
to access the upper portions of the site to drill the remaining borings to complete our
subsurface investigation for the project The remaining borings will require trail clearing to
reach the proposed boring locations within the project limits We understand archeological
constraints have delayed this clearance
Because we have not completed our subsurface investigation of the upper slopes we
must remind all parties that our boring data from the lower slopes should not be extrapolated
to the upper slopes as the subsurface conditions are likely to vary considerably
