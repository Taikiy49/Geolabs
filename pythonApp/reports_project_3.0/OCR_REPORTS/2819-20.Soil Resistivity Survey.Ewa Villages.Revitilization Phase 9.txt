SUMMARY REPORT
SOIL RESISTIVITY SURVEY
EWA VILLAGES REVITALIZATION PHASE 9
VARONA VILLAGE INFRASTRUCTURE IMPROVEMENTS
In accordance with our fee proposal dated May 9th we have completed a soil
resistivity survey for the proposed new water line system at the existing Varona Village
in the District of Ewa on the island of Oahu Hawaii The approximate location of the
project site is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 This report presents the
results of our soil resistivity survey and summarizes our work on this project
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located in the Honouliuli area of the District of Ewa on the
island of Oahu Hawaii It is bounded approximately by Renton Road Kihi Koahi and
Paalua Streets as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
We understand that it is proposed to reconstruct the existing Varona Village
The revitalization project will include the installation of new utilities including sewer
water and drainage lines as well as reconstruction of the existing roadways Based on
the information provided to our office approximately 8 000 lineal feet of water line will
be installed along the roadways as part of the revitalization project
Based on the preliminary profile plans of the project the existing ground surface
elevations along the roadways at the site range from about 44 to 50 feet Mean Sea
Level MSL Minor cuts on the order of up to 3 feet will be required to attain the
design finish grades of about 43 to 46 feet MSL along the proposed roadway

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our survey was to obtain an overview of the electrical resistivity
of the subsurface soils at the site Our work on this project was performed in general
conformance with ASTM Test Designation G 57 78 and with the requirements provided
in the Water System External Corrosion Control Standards Volume Ill dated 1991
The scope of our work consisted of the following tasks and work efforts
1 Mobilization Demobilization of equipment and engineers to the project site
2 Performance of 159 in situ soil resistivity tests at 30 selected locations
along the proposed water line alignment across the project site The test
locations generally correspond to a maximum spacing of 300 feet as
required in the Water System External Corrosion Control Standards
Volume Ill 1991
3 Coordination of the field work by an Engineer Geologist from our firm
4 Reduction and analysis of the field resistivity data with respect to the
corrosion characteristics of the soils at the site
5 Preparation of six copies of this brief letter report summarizing our work
on the project
6 Quality assurance of our work and client design team consultation by a
Principal Engineer of our firm
7 Miscellaneous work efforts such as drafting word processing and clerical
support

FIELD SURVEY
Geophysical survey equipment i e electrical resistance meter was mobilized
to the site on May 23rd and our field work was performed in sunny weather and
generally dry surface conditions The soil resistivity tests were conducted at the
existing ground surface elevation on the exposed vegetated barren shoulder area
alongside the existing paved roadways
One hundred fifty nine 159 soil resistivity surveys were conducted at
30 locations using the Wenner 4 Pin Method and a resistance meter Strata Scout
R 40C manufactured by SoilTest Inc The resistance meter is capable of transmitting
120 volts and 24 watts output into the ground This method utilizes four copper surface
probes or pins electrodes to evaluate the average resistivity an indication of
corrosivity of the underlying soil mass The average electrical resistivities of the soil
mass extending from the existing ground surface to the planned invert elevations of the
water line were assessed by using this method at the test locations listed on Plates 3 1
through 3 10 The approximate test locations are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The resistance meter R 40C impresses a voltage between the outer electrodes
causing an electrical current to flow between them The voltage drop between the inner
electrodes is then measured by a sensitive built in voltmeter As a result the soil
resistance in Ohms can be read directly from the dial The soil resistivity in Ohm
cm which is directly related to both resistance and electrode spacing can then be
computed The evaluated soil resistances the computed resistivities and the
corresponding corrosion ratings are presented on Plates 3 1 through 3 10
The average soil resistance was evaluated at each test location with the
electrodes aligned at equal spacings of generally 4 to 9 feet at the existing ground
surface Among the spacings used one spacing may have more significance than the
others allowing more contribution to the evaluated soil resistivity from the soil mass
nearer to the proposed pipeline invert elevation Such spacings have the same
distance as the depth from the existing ground surface at the test locations to the
proposed pipe invert at the test locations Since the exact pipe invert elevations were
not available at the time this report was prepared further evaluation cannot be made
at this time
Resistivity is generally recognized as one of the most significant soil
characteristics with regard to the corrosivity of the soil to buried metallic objects In
general the lower the resistivity the greater the potential for corrosion of buried metallic
structures Conversely the higher the resistivity the less likely the soil will contribute
to the corrosion of metallic objects

LIMITATIONS
The evaluations presented in this report are based upon information obtained
from field data points by means of geophysical surveys Variations of soil conditions
between the field data points may occur and the nature and extent of such variations
may not become evident until a later point in time If variations then appear evident
it will be necessary to reevaluate the ratings provided in this report
Locations and elevations of the data presented in this report are approximate
having been estimated from features and reference points shown on the Plan Profile
Plans prepared by R M Towill Corporation Elevations of the survey locations were
interpolated from the ground surface profile shown on the profile section of the same
plans The locations and elevations of the data presented should be considered
accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used
