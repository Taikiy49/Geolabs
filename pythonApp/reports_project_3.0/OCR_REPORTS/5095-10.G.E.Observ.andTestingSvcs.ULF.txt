Regional Geology
The Island of Hawaii the largest in the Hawaiian Archipelago covers an area of
approximately 4 000 square miles The island was formed by the activity of five shield
volcanoes which include the following Kohala which is long extinct Mauna Kea which
has had activity during recent geologic time Hualalai which last erupted in 1801 and
Mauna Loa and Kilauea both of which are still active
The project site is situated on the western flank of Hualalai Mountain which makes
up the west central portion of the island The lava formation encountered at the site
appears to be predominantly of pahoehoe type flows with some a a lava which spread
and ponded as they approached the ocean Aja lava is typically characterized by a
porous rough and irregular flow surface resembling a jagged accumulation of rock
fragments including cobbles and boulders Pahoehoe is characterized by a smooth
rope like or billowy surface and an internal structure of vesicular porous rock
Cavities commonly are encountered in basaltic lavas especially the pahoehoe type
lava These cavities are formed when the lava is still in a molten state and they represent
both lava tubes intra flow cavities and blisters and pockets inter flow cavities Lava
tubes are formed when molten lava drains from the cooling flow leaving a hollow tube like
structure which may extend for a large distance longitudinally along the flow Blisters and
pockets are generally smaller in horizontal extent
Basalt is considered to be a relatively permeable rock and can transmit water quite
readily in both the horizontal and vertical directions In general water is transmitted
through the porous rock matrix along joints fractures and inter flow contacts cavities and
along clinker layers The permeability of the clinker and cavities is high therefore they
serve as the primary water transmission features
Subsurface Conditions
As mentioned previously the Makalapua Business Center project site is located in
the Kailua Kona area in the District of North Kona on the Island of Hawaii In general the
original topography of the site prior to site grading activities consisted of irregular
or hummocky lava flow terrain with frequent topographic depressions separated by
adjacent higher relief mounds and low ridge features
The project site was mass graded by others Therefore the subsurface materials
at the United Laundry Facility project site generally consisted of compacted fill materials
placed during the mass grading operations In general structural fill consisting of
well graded materials less than about 6 inches in maximum dimension were placed in the
top 2 feet of fill areas Larger sized materials may be present in deeper fill areas in cut
area presence of native materials such as basalt formation and a a basalt should be
anticipated at or near the existing pad elevation

Discussion and Recommendations
As previously mentioned the project site generally was mass graded by others
Fine grading generally consisted of placement of less than about 1 to 2 feet of fills to
achieve the design grades Fill materials consisting of some cobbles and boulders and
structural fills were placed in the fill areas of the project site Structural fills consisted of
well graded granular materials generally smaller than 6 inches in dimension
During the fine grading and backfilling operations samples of the on site and
imported materials utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to
our office for testing The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were
established in our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557
Modified Proctor and the test results are summarized on Plate 2
In general the fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to above
optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum
dry density as determined in accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557 In the roadway
and parking areas the last lift of fill and backfill materials were generally compacted to a
minimum of 95 percent relative density
During the site grading operations periodic field density tests were performed in
general accordance with ASTM Test Designations D 2922 and D 3017 Nuclear Gauge
Method The results of the compaction tests performed on the fills and subgrades are
summarized on Plates 3 1 and 3 2 of this report These tests were generally performed
on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills at the site A total
of 25 field density tests were performed in support of the site grading and utility
installation for the project
Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from the
available on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should be considered
accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed to be
unsatisfactory were generally reworked until the specified minimum compaction was
obtained and or approved by our field representative based on visual observation and
hand probing
As mentioned in the above reference report a program of probing and grouting
under the new building footprint was required for the project We wish to point out that
Geolabs was not present to observe the probing and grouting operation for the project
Based on the observations of our field representatives it is our professional
opinion that the earthwork operations during the construction for the above mentioned
project observed by Geolabs in the period covered in this report were performed in
general conformance with the intent of the project plans and specifications

Limitations
lt should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork may
have been performed without our observation However the level of earthwork
observation and testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some of
the geotechnical risks to the project It should be recognized that there are variations in
the accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the
earthwork construction and materials
