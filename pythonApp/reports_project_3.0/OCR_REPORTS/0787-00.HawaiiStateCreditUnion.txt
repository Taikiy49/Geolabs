INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this investigation is to evaluate the subsur
face conditions at the site to provide recommendations for
the design and construction of the foundations for the pro
posed office building
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P 2
SITE DESCRIPTION
The site for the proposed structure is located on Halekauwila
Street between Punchbowl and South Streets The lot extends
from Halekauwila Street northeastward to Quinn Lane
The site is bordered by a 2 story concrete building on the
south a 4 story concrete building on the southeast and a
3 story concrete building on the north
The southwestern portion of the site bordering Halekauwila
Street is vacant The central and eastern portions of the
lot are occupied by a 1 story concrete warehouse building
The site generally slopes down toward Halekauwila Street at
about 3 percent gradients
The surface elevation at the site generally varies from about
6 feet near Quinn Lane to 4 feet near Halekauwila Street
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
The proposed structure will be a 5 story concrete office build
ing approximately 100 by 60 feet in plan with a basement
The basement level will vary from 0 feet at Quinn Lane to about
8 feet below the ground surface near Halekauwila Street
Maximum column loads of about 1200 kips are anticipated

W P 3
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Subsurface Exploration
Five borings were drilled at the site to depths of 20 to 30
feet below the existing ground surface to explore the sub
surface conditions and to obtain samples for laboratory
testings The approximate locations of the borings are
Shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
Detailed information on the field exploration and laboratory
testing is covered in the appendix along with the boring logs
and laboratory test results
Soil Conditions
In general the borings encountered a layer of medium to loose
gray and brown silty sand and tan sand about 3 to 7 5 feet
deep over very dense coral with silty sand At about 15 to
20 foot depths the coral layer graded to medium density
Water was encountered in the borings at 3 to 7 5 foot depths
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed a layer of sand over a dense
coral formation below the site The ground water level was
encountered several feet above the proposed basement level
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P 4
Dewatering will be required for the proposed construction
Also sheeting and bracing and possibly underpinning will
be required for the proposed basement excavation
Foundation
For the proposed 5 story structure spread or continuous
footing foundations down to the dense coral formation are
recommended An allowable bearing value of 6 000 p s f can
be used for footings resting on the dense coral The allow
able bearing value may be increased up to one third for tem
porary loading conditions such as wind loads
The footings should extend a minimum of 12 inches into the
dense coral formation
To reduce the possibility of footing settlement due to possi
ble cavities in the dense coral formation a minimum of 20
probe holes should be drilled below the building foundations
The probe holes should have a minimum diameter of 2 inches
and should extend below the bottom of footing a minimum of
10 feet The probe holes should be grouted from the bottom
of the drilled hole using low pressures prior to basement
excavation and dewatering operation
If a cavity is suspected below a footing additional probe
holes should be made to delineate the horizontal extent of
the cavity The probing and grouting operations should be
monitored by the soils engineer to see that these recommen
dations are carried out properly
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P 5
For the above recommendations minor settlements are anti
cipated for the proposed structure
Excavation
Excavations up to about 9 feet will be required for the
proposed basement level
Some relatively hard ripping may be required in the dense
coral formation The upper sandy layers should be easy to
excavate
Due to the proximity of the surrounding existing structures
sheeting and bracing or chemical grout stabilization of the
slopes is recommended for the excavation to reduce settle
ment and damage to the adjacent structures
Where there is sufficient distance 2 horizontal to 1 ver
tical side slopes can be utilized for the excavation slopes
in lieu of sheeting and bracing or chemical grout stabili
zation
The southeastern portion of the proposed building will be
constructed up to the property line There is an existing
2 story concrete building located adjacent to the property
line in this area If the foundations for the existing
building do not extend down to the dense coral underpinning
may be required to reduce settlements to this building during
construction
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P 6
Heavy equipment and stockpiles should be kept away from the
tops of the excavation
Settlements of the adjacent ground due to the excavation
operations can be minimized by preventing loss of ground
and by introducing points of support for the side walls
as soon as practical and at appropriate vertical spacing
Dewatering
Groundwater was encountered in the borings above the pro
posed basement level at about elevation plus 1 5 feet
The method of dewatering should be left open to the con
tractor provided adequate stability of the excavation
is maintained The dewatering operations should be done
without pumping out the soil fines pumping clear water
only and without creating a quick condition or soften
ing the bottom of the excavation
Basement Wall
Basement walls up to 8 feet high are planned for the
proposed structure
Well graded granular material such as base course rock
or on site sand should be used as backfill material behind
the walls Impervious material should be used for the top
2 feet of the backfill
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P 7
The recommendations given under foundations should be
followed for the design of the wall foundations
The following lateral earth pressures are recommended for
the wall design
Backfill above water 50 p c f equivalent
fluid pressure
Backfill below water 25 p c f equivalent
fluid pressure plus
water pressure
A friction factor of 0 5 can be utilized to determine the
sliding resistance of the wall foundation
Slab On Grade
The basement Slab on grade will rest partly on the dense
coral and partly on medium to loose sand To reduce differ
ential settlements of the slab due to the different supporting
materials the medium to loose sand should be proofrolled with
a heavy vibratory roller to further densify the underlying
Sand
Slab on grade should be placed over a 4 inch cushion layer
of base course compacted to 95 of its maximum dry density
Earthwork Compaction Requirements
A few feet of fill will be required along the southern por
tion of the site next to Halekauwila Street
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P 8
The site should be cleared of all vegetation debris and
old foundations prior to filling
Select on site material should be used for fill and foot
ing backfill material The fill and backfill should be
placed in horizontal loose lifts of 8 inches maximum
and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry
density established by ASTM Test Method D 1557
Design Review
The plans for foundations site grading and utility con
struction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for
review and comments prior to construction to see that the
intent of our recommendations is included in the project
plans and specifications
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the footing excavations and backfill
operations be observed by a soils engineer during construc
tion to see that adequate bearing has been obtained and that
the subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings
of our subsurface investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive
tested and approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling
to the site

INVESTIGATION LIMITATIONS

dations made in accordance with generally accepted soil and
foundation engineering principles and practices This war
ranty is in lieu of all other warranties either expressed
or implied

are based on our site reconnaissance soil information from
boring data and laboratory tests obtained from this soil
investigation

APPENDIX A
FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored
by drilling 5 borings to depths of 20 to 30 feet below the
existing ground surface at the locations shown on the Site
Plan Plate 1
The soils encountered in the borings were classified
by visual and textural examination in the field by our en
gineer who continuously monitored the drilling operations
The classifications were reviewed by visual inspection and
testing in the laboratory The soils were classified in
general conformance with the Unified Soil Classification
System A graphic presentation of the soils is presented
on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 10
In the borings soil samples were obtained by driving
split barrel sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30
inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the last
12 inches are shown on the boring logs at the appropriate
depths

and B 2

grain size of the subsoils The results are presented
in Plate B 3 Summary of Laboratory Test Results
