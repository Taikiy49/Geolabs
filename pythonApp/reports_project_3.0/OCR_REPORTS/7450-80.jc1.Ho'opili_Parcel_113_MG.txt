Dear Ms. Otake: 
This letter report presents our recommendations of site grading for the Ho’opili 
Development, Parcel 113 project in the Ewa area on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. The 
project location and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map, Plate 1. 
This letter report summarizes our geotechnical engineering recommendations 
resulting from our engineering analyses. These recommendations are intended for the site 
grading only. The findings and recommendations presented herein are subject to the 
limitations noted at the end of this letter report. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The proposed Ho`opili Development project is to the south of the Interstate Route 
H-1 Freeway and west of Fort Weaver Road in the Ewa area on the Island of Oahu, 
Hawaii. The Ho`opili Development will encompass approximately 1,500 acres. The project 
site is gently sloping and is currently used for agricultural cultivation. 
The Parcel 113 area is located at the southeast portion of the development along 
the west side of Fort Weaver Road. Parcel 113 encompasses approximately 6 acres. 
Based on the information provided, we understand that mass grading operation is required 
within the limits of the Parcel 113 property.  
Based on the mass grading plan provided, we anticipate site grading work will 
generally consist of fills between about 5 and 15 feet thick, to achieve the design finished 
grades for the project. A layout of the project site is shown on the Site Plan, Plate 2.

SITE GRADING 
As mentioned previously, we anticipate site grading work will consist of fills between 
about 5 and 15 feet thick, to achieve the design finished grades for the project. Based on 
the observed ground conditions at the site and our experience in the project vicinity, we 
recommend implementing the following site preparation procedures during the mass 
grading construction. 
A Geolabs representative should monitor site grading operations to observe 
whether undesirable materials are encountered during the excavation and scarification 
process, and to confirm whether the exposed soil conditions are similar to those assumed 
in this report. 
Site Preparation 
At the on-set of mass grading, the areas within the contract grading limits should 
be cleared and grubbed thoroughly. Vegetation, debris, deleterious material, and other 
unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of properly off-site or stockpiled 
in a designated area to reduce the potential for contamination of the excavated 
materials. 
Soft and yielding areas encountered during clearing, grubbing, removal and 
over-excavation work should be conducted to expose firm natural material, and the 
resulting excavation should be backfilled with well-compacted engineered fill. The 
excavated soft soils may be used as fill, provided that it meets the fill material 
requirements. 
Saturation and subsequent yielding of the exposed subgrade due to inclement 
weather and poor drainage may require over-excavation of the soft areas and 
replacement with well-compacted fill at no cost to the owner. A Geolabs representative 
should evaluate the need for over-excavation in the field. 
Fills and Backfills 
Generally, we anticipate the earthwork would likely involve primarily general fill. 
General fill may consist of the on-site soils with a maximum particle size of 6 inches and 
should be free of deleterious material. In general, the excavated on-site soils may be 
reused as a source of general fill material, provided they are free of vegetation, debris, 
deleterious material and other unsuitable materials.

General fill materials should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in 
loose thickness, moisture-conditioned to about 2 percent above the optimum moisture 
content, and compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction. 
Relative compaction refers to the in-place dry density of soil expressed as a 
percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with 
ASTM D1557. Optimum moisture is the water content (percentage by weight) 
corresponding to the maximum dry density. 
We wish to emphasize that moisture-conditioning of the fill materials is an 
integral and critical part of the earthwork recommendations provided herein. 
Compaction should be accomplished by sheepsfoot rollers, vibratory rollers, or other 
types of acceptable compaction equipment. 
Because moisture-conditioning and compaction of the subgrade soils are critical 
elements of earthwork, particularly for the elevated moisture content requirements, 
Geolabs should provide observations and perform soil density tests during site grading 
to assist the contractor in obtaining the appropriate degree of compaction and the 
proper ranges of moisture content. Where moisture and/or compaction are less than 
required, additional moisture-conditioning and compactive effort should be applied with 
adjustment as necessary. Where the reduced compaction requirements are applicable, 
it may be necessary to scarify the soil and recompact it to the proper range of relative 
compaction, if the required range of compaction is exceeded. 
Excavation 
It is anticipated that the on-site clayey soils may be excavated with conventional 
heavy excavation equipment. However, our past experience in the project area indicates 
that isolated coralline detritus/formation and cobbles/boulders could be encountered. 
Therefore, excavation for mass grading construction may encounter hard coral and/or 
cobbles/boulders which may require the use of hoerams or trenching machines. 
The above discussion regarding the rippability of the surface materials is based on 
data obtained from the field borings. Contractors bidding on this project should examine 
the site conditions and soil data to make their own interpretation. 
Fill Slopes 
In general, the fill slopes constructed of the on-site soils may be designed with a 
slope inclination of two horizontal to one vertical (2H:1V) or flatter. Fills placed on slopes 
steeper than 5H:1V should be keyed and benched into the existing slope to provide 
stability of the new fill embankment against sliding. The filling operations should start at the 
lowest point and continue up in level horizontal compacted layers in accordance with the 
above general fill placement recommendations.

Fill slopes should be constructed by overfilling and cutting back to the design slope 
ratio to obtain a well-compacted slope face. Surface water should be diverted away from 
the tops of slopes, and slope planting should be provided as soon as possible to reduce 
the potential for erosion of the finished slopes. 
Drainage 
The finished grades should be sloped to shed water away and to reduce the 
potential for ponding. These drainage requirements are essential for the proper 
performance of the above site grading recommendations since ponded water could cause 
subsurface soil saturation and subsequent loss of strength and/or heaving of the near-
surface soils. 
LIMITATIONS 
The analyses and recommendations submitted herein are based, in part, upon 
information obtained from field borings and in-situ testing. Variations of subsoil conditions 
between and beyond the field borings may occur, and the nature and extent of these 
variations may not become evident until construction is underway. If variations then appear 
evident, it will be necessary to re-evaluate the recommendations provided herein.
