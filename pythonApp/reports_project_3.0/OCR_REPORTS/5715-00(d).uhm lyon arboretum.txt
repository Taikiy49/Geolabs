PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed project involves renovating and replacing Cottage A and installing a
septic tank and leaching field The project site is above Paradise Park in Honolulu on the
Island of Oahu Hawaii
Sanitary wastes generated are currently disposed through a cesspool system As
part of the improvements related to the cottage renovation and replacement it is proposed
to install a new wastewater system consisting of a septic tank and leach field pit A flow
test on the existing cesspool was needed prior to abandoning or reusing the existing
cesspool as a seepage pit The estimated wastewater flow is 1 450 gallons per day

INSPECTION AND FLOW TESTING
The maintenance personnel located and opened the cesspool cover We first
visually inspected it in an attempt to obtain a geological log of the formations penetrated
by the cesspool We conducted sounding in the cesspool to determine the depth to
bottom and the depth to fluid Following this inspection we conducted further testing
by placing approximately 3 750 gallons of clean fresh water into the cesspool
using a water truck and then by recording the time required for the water to dissipate
i e a falling head infiltration test Since the actual geometry of the well was not
available and the actual characteristics of the receiving aquifer were not known the
performance test data was not subjected to rigorous analysis to estimate hydraulic
conductivities or maximum capabilities The test data was reviewed on a subjective
basis to estimate the cesspool performance
Inspection of the cesspool interior indicated a rock lining to a depth of about
3 feet below the ground surface The cesspool was dry at the time of our inspection and
testing
Testing indicated that the approximately 3 750 gallons of water placed into the
cesspool had dissipated in approximately 20 minutes Based on the information
provided to us the design capacity for this wastewater system is 1 450 gallons per day
Therefore performance of this cesspool is currently satisfactory and should provide
adequate capacity for the new septic tank system

information obtained from visual observations and short term flow test Variations of
subsurface conditions may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not
become evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be
necessary to re evaluate the recommendations provided herein
