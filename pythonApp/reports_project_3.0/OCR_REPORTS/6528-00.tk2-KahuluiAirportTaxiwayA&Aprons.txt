herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this letter report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is at the existing Kahului Airport OGG in the town of Kahului on
the Island of Maui Hawaii Based on the information provided we understand that the
existing pavements at Taxiway A Apron have exhibited signs of distresses and
damages due to their old age Therefore it is desired to evaluate the conditions and
engineering properties of the existing pavements and underlying subgrade materials for
the formulation of soil parameters pertaining to the design of new pavements for the
project We understand that design of the new pavements will be performed by the client
and is specifically excluded from our scope of work for this project
The following field exploration and laboratory testing program was conducted to
accomplish this objective

Borehole Drilling and Sampling
Our field exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling 44 borings
designated as Boring Nos 1 through 44 at selected locations along Taxiway A
extending to depths of about 3 7 to 11 5 feet below the pavement finished grades The
approximate boring locations are shown on the Site Plans Plates 2 1 through 2 3
Our field exploration indicated that the existing AC and ACB pavements along
Taxiway A are generally on the order of about 15 to 24 inches thick Based on our
field observations and laboratory test results it appears that the AC and ACB layer is
generally underlain by base course and or granular fill materials consisting of dense
sandy gravel and silty sands The pavement structure is generally underlain by very
stiff sandy clays and clayey silts extending to the maximum depth explored of
approximately 11 5 feet below the pavement finished grades Hard basalt rock
formation was encountered below the clayey soils in Boring through 19
24 through and 42 through 44 drilled along the taxiway alignment
Detailed descriptions of the field exploration methodology and graphic
representations of the materials encountered in the borings are presented on the Logs of
Borings in Appendix A
Dynamic Cone Penetration Testing
In addition to borehole drilling and sampling our field exploration program also
included performance of Dynamic Cone Penetration DCP testing at twenty five selected
boring locations to provide additional data and information for our evaluation and analyses
The approximate boring locations where the DCP tests were performed are shown on the
Site Plans Plates 2 1 through 2 3
The Dynamic Cone Penetration test provides a measure of a material s in situ
resistance to penetration The test is performed by driving a metal cone into the ground by
575 mm The penetration of the cone is measured after each blow and is recorded to
provide a continuous measure of shear resistance up to 5 feet below the ground surface
Test results can be correlated to California Bearing Ratio in situ density resilient modulus
and bearing capacity
The DCP tests were generally conducted at 1 inch discrete depth
intervals between depths of about 24 to 72 inches below the existing pavement surface at
the test locations Field logs of the DCP tests are included in Appendix B of this report for
reference Results for the DCP field data are summarized in Plate 3 for ease of reference

Based on our field exploration and laboratory testing results it appears that the
pavement subgrade soils generally consisted of very stiff Sandy Clay CL soils
Laboratory CBR tests suggested that the majority of these pavement subgrade soils
exhibited CBR values ranging between about 25 to 43 in a laboratory compacted
condition Since the pavement subgrade soils will not be over excavated and or
replaced in compacted lifts we believe that the design CBR for the pavement subgrade
soils should reflect the material in an in situ field condition instead of a laboratory
compacted condition Based on the information provided by the Advisory Circular
No 6E Airport Pavement Design and Evaluation prepared by the FAA
the field CBR should be in the range of about 5 to 15 for Sandy Clay CL soils as
shown on Plate 4
Since DCP testing is an in situ testing technique results of the DCP tests should
reflect the in situ condition of the pavement subgrade soils Several authors have

the inverse equation contains more errors and is not suitable to use Considering the

Sandy Clay soils provided in the Advisory Circular No 6E mentioned above
Based on our literature review field exploration and laboratory test results engineering
analyses and evaluation we believe that a design CBR value of 15 may be adopted for
the in situ pavement subgrade soils in the design of flexible pavements at Taxiway A A
corresponding subgrade modulus k value of 200 pounds per square inch per inch of
deflection pci may be used for the design of the rigid pavements

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted herein are based in part upon
information obtained from the field data points Variations of the subsurface conditions
between and beyond the field data points may occur and the nature and extent of these
variations may not become evident until construction is underway If variations then appear
evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations presented herein
The field boring locations indicated herein are approximate having been estimated
by taping from visible features shown on the Bore Hole Location Plan transmitted by
JC Engineering LLC on Elevations of the borings were interpolated
based on the contour lines shown on the same plans The field boring locations and
elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used
The stratification breaks shown on the graphic representations of the borings depict
the approximate boundaries between soil and or rock types and as such may denote a
gradual transition Water level data from the borings were measured at the times shown
on the graphic representations and or presented in the text of this report These data have
been reviewed and interpretations have been made to formulate this report However it
must be noted that fluctuation may occur due to variation in tides rainfall perched
groundwater conditions groundwater withdrawal and other factors

Field Exploration
We explored the subsurface conditions at Taxiway A by drilling and sampling
forty four borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 44 extending to depths of about
3 7 to 11 5 feet below the pavement finished grade The approximate boring locations
are shown on the Site Plans Plates 2 1 through 2 3 The borings were drilled using a
truck mounted drill rig equipped with continuous flight augers
Our geologist classified the materials encountered in the borings by visual and
textural examination in the field in general accordance with ASTM D 2488 Standard
Practice for Description and Identification of Soils and monitored the drilling operations
on a near continuous full time basis These classifications were further reviewed
visually and by testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general accordance with
ASTM D 2487 Standard Practice for Classification of Soils for Engineering Purposes
Unified Soil Classification System as shown on the Soil Log Legend Plate A Graphic
representations of the materials encountered are presented on the Logs of Borings
Plates A 1 through A 44
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained in general accordance with
ASTM D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 3 inch OD Modified
California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches In addition some
samples were obtained from the borings drilled in general accordance with
ASTM D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 2 inch
OD standard penetration sampler using the same hammer and drop The blow counts
needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an 18 inch drive are
shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate
sample depths The penetration resistance shown on the logs of borings indicates the
number of blows required for the specific sampler type used The blow counts may need
to be factored to obtain the Standard Penetration Test SPT blow counts
Pocket penetrometer tests were performed on selected cohesive soil samples
retrieved in the field The pocket penetrometer test provides an indication of the
unconfined compressive strength of the soil sample Pocket penetrometer test results
are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths

Dynamic Cone Penetration Tests
We explored the subsurface conditions at Taxiway A by performing Dynamic
Cone Penetration DCP tests within twenty five borings extending to depths of about
24 to 72 inches below the pavement finished grade The approximate boring locations
where DCP tests were performed are shown on the Site Plans Plates 2 1 through 2 3
Results are shown on the Dynamic Core Penetrometer Data Sheet Plates B 1 through
B 25
The procedures used in our DCP tests are in general accordance with
ASTM D 6951 Standard Test Method for Use of the Dynamic Cone Penetrometer in
Shallow Pavement Applications The DCP apparatus consists of a 5 8 inch diameter steel
rod with a 60 degree conical tip The rod is topped with an anvil that is connected to a
second steel rod This rod is used as a guide to allow a 8 kg hammer to be
repeatedly raised and dropped from a height of 22 6 inches 575 mm The connection
between the two rods consists of an anvil to allow for quick connections between the rods
and for efficient energy transfer from the falling weight to the penetrating rod
Once the test apparatus is assembled the DCP is placed at the test location and
the initial penetration of the rod is recorded to provide a zeroing scale While holding the
rod vertically the weight is raised to the top of the rod 575 mm above the anvil and
dropped The penetration of the rod is measured after each drop
In the DCP tests conducted by Geolabs the same equipment described above
was used to record the number of blows required to advance the cone in 1 inch
depth increments as shown in the field logs provided in Appendix B
