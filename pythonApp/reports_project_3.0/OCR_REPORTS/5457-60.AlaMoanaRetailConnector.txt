PROJECT DESCRIPTION
Based on the information provided a retail connector connecting the future
Nordstrom Building to the center court of the Ala Moana Center will be constructed to
serve the new retail development According to the project structural engineer the typical
column loads 30 x 30 grid of the retail connector is on the order of about 500 kips with
maximum column loads up to about 900 to 1 000 kips per column The layout of the new
retail connector is shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
Based on the referenced report for the project we recommend supporting the new
retail connector structure on a deep foundation system consisting of 16 5 inch octagonal
precast prestressed concrete piles with an allowable compressive load capacity of up to
150 kips 75 tons per pile We envision that most of the driven piles would extend through
the soft and or loose lagoonal deposits near the surface and derive support primarily
from end bearing on the upper coral ledges encountered at depths of about 20 to 30 feet
below the existing ground surface
Due to the variable quality and thickness of the upper coral ledges we anticipated
that some of the driven piles intended for end bearing purposes may punch through the
upper coral ledges during the pile driving operations These piles would then need to be
spliced and driven deeper to derive support from skin friction by embedment into the
underlying coralline detritus materials

Test Pile Program
A test pile program which consisted of installing 16 precast prestressed concrete
test piles was conducted for the project from May 9 through 11 2006 The test pile
locations are shown on the Driven Test Pile Location Plan Plate 2 1 The purpose of the
test pile program was to establish the driveability of the concrete piles given the
subsurface conditions the selected driving equipment and methods of concrete pile
installation In addition the test pile program also provided information on the driving
resistance and contours of the load bearing strata across the project site to estimate the
length of the production concrete piles
Prior to the pile driving operations the pile locations were predrilled to a depth of
about 10 feet below the existing ground surface to facilitate the pile driving operations

Static Pile Load Test
A compression pile load test was conducted on a selected punched through test
pile designated as Pile No 141 on May 18 2006 The purpose of the static pile load test
is to evaluate the amount of embedment length required for the driven pile friction pile in
order to achieve the design compressive load capacity of 75 tons per pile
The selected test pile was driven to a depth of about 131 feet below the ground
surface The test pile was loaded to the maximum test load of 300 kips 200 percent of the
design load in general accordance with ASTM D 1143 Quick Load Test Method Detailed
discussions and the static pile load test results are provided in the report entitled Driven
Pile Load Test Report Retail Connector Ala Moana Expansion Honolulu Oahu Hawaii
dated transmitted separately
Based on the static pile load test results it appears that an embedment length on
the order of about 130 to 135 feet will be required for the driven piles to achieve the design
compressive load capacity of 75 tons based on friction if end bearing on the coral ledges is
not achieved Upon further evaluation with General Growth Properties L P based on cost
comparisons and construction considerations it was decided that end bearing driven piles
would be used for the Ewa portion of the retail connector while augered cast in place
ACIP piles would be used for foundation support of the Diamond Head portion of the
retail connector structure The delineation line between the two foundation support
systems is shown on the Driven Test Pile Location Plan Plate 2 1

Based on the test pile program results the following production pile driving criteria
was adopted for the above project The production piles should be driven with the same
type of hammer with a stroke height of 4 feet at the end of the pile driving
operations The pile driving operation may be terminated when the aggregated blow
counts are at least 60 blows per foot for the last foot or 6 blows per inch for the last
3 inches For those piles that punch through the upper coral ledge and develop the
design capacity through skin friction by embedding in the coralline detritus materials the
pile driving operation may be terminated when the aggregated blow counts are at least
45 blows per foot for the last foot or 6 blows per inch for the last 3 inches In addition the
pile driving also may be terminated when requested by our field representative

MICROPILE INSTALLATION
Micropile foundations were used on several occasions to provide the necessary
compressive and or uplift load capacities during construction of the Retail Connector
portion of the Ala Moana Expansion project The micropile foundation system was adopted
in these situations generally due to the low capacity required as well as accessibility
problems that precluded the use of heavy construction equipment for other foundation
support systems Geotechnical recommendations for the micropile foundations were
presented in our transmittal entitled Ala Moana Center Expansion Micropile
Requirements dated The purpose of our geotechnical engineering
observation and consultation services was to observe the micropile foundation installation
for general conformance with our recommendations
The reinforcing bars for the micropiles generally consisted of the Titan 52 26 bars
with a minimum drill bit diameter of 5 inches for the project The Titan micropile system
consists of hollow steel rods that are continuously advanced into the ground These drill
rods are abandoned in the hole and serve as the reinforcing steel for the micropile The
micropiles were drilled using a forklift mounted rotary percussion drill rig Drilling fluids
consisting of a neat cement grout mixture flushes soil cuttings from the hole and creates a
grout bulb around the drill rod Drilling of the hole installation of the reinforcing member
and grouting of the micropiles generally occur in a single operation
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered in the project vicinity we believe
the micropiles would need to embed a minimum of 12 and 15 feet into the coral formation
to develop an allowable compressive and uplift load capacity of 80 kips 40 tons per
micropile respectively As part of the construction requirements and subsequent field
observations one static load test and six proof tests were conducted on selected
micropiles to evaluate the adequacy of the foundation support system
