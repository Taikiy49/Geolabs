TRIAL SHAFT AND LOAD TEST PROGRAM
KALOI GULCH RAILROAD BRIDGE PROJECT
Dear Mr Young
In accordance with the project specifications a trial shaft program including an
instrumented load test was conducted at a selected location northwest of Pier 1 for the Kaloi
Gulch Railroad Bridge project between July 20 and August 1 1999 This report summarizes
our field observations during the trial shaft program and presents the results of the
instrumented load test conducted
Based on the results of the instrumented load test we recommended new drilled shaft
tip elevations for the bridge foundations for the project The new drilled shaft tip elevations
are shorter than those shown on the project plans and reflect the more favorable skin friction
values obtained from the instrumented load test results
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Kaloi Gulch Railroad Bridge project is located adjacent to the Coral Creek Golf
Course and next to the Kula Lei Subdivision in the District of Ewa on the Island of Oahu
Hawaii The railroad bridge project site is situated alongside the existing railroad tracks and
Chevron pipelines located to the south of Renton Road Based on the project plans the new
bridge will be approximately 159 feet long from abutment to abutment about 28 feet wide
and about 18 feet above the bottom of the new Kaloi Gulch Channel floor The bridge will
be a three span bridge supported at each end by the abutments and by two intermediate
piers located within the new open channel
Based on the available information the structural loading of the bridge at each
abutment is approximately 385 kips compression load and the structural loading at each
intermediate pier is about 1 100 kips compression load Each drilled shaft for the bridge
foundation was designed based on ultimate compression load capacities of about 800 and
1 970 kips for the abutment and intermediate pier structures respectively

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Based on our field explorations in 1995 and 1998 the project site is underlain primarily
by medium dense silty sands and silty clayey coral gravels below the surface clayey
materials Groundwater was encountered in the borings in 1998 at depths of about 40 and
40 5 feet below the existing ground surface at the time of our field exploration The
groundwater levels measured in 1998 corresponded to about Elevations 2 to 2 5 feet
Mean Sea Level MSL Due to the proximity of the project site to the Pacific Ocean and the
permeable subsoils encountered it is likely that the groundwater level would be affected by
tidal fluctuations In addition groundwater levels are also subject to change due to seasonal
precipitation storm surge conditions and other factors
TRIAL SHAFT PROGRAM
As part of the project specifications a trial shaft consisting of an 18 inch diameter
drilled shaft was excavated at a selected location to the northwest of Pier 1 on
by Foundation International Inc The trial shaft was placed at a location approximately 4 feet
northwest of Pier 1 The intent of the trial shaft installation is for the contractor to
demonstrate the adequacy of the drilling method and equipment proposed for the subsurface
conditions at the project site
The trial shaft was excavated using a Watson 3000 track mounted drill rig and the
bottom of the trial shaft was excavated to about 74 7 feet below the existing ground surface
or to Elevation 31 7 feet MSL During the trial shaft excavation it was noted that the
sidewalls of the drilled shaft hole held up relatively well without the need for temporary casing
of the drilled shaft hole Therefore the trial shaft excavation proceeded without using a
temporary casing for drilled hole stabilization During the trial shaft excavation a log of
materials encountered was maintained by an engineer from our office A log of the trial shaft
construction and materials encountered is provided on Plates 1 1 and 1 2
As part of the trial shaft program the trial shaft was converted into the load test shaft
by constructing the trial shaft with a reinforcing steel cage Therefore the contractor placed
a reinforcing steel cage into the trial shaft hole after the trial shaft excavation The reinforcing
steel cage was equipped with seven embedment strain gages manufactured by Geokon
The approximate locations of the seven embedment strain gages are schematically shown
on Plate 2 Concrete was then placed in the drilled hole using a 4 inch diameter steel tremie
pipe to complete the construction of the load test shaft
A reaction frame with two concrete reaction shafts approximately 24 inch diameter
was used to provide the reaction for the load test The two reaction shafts were excavated
about 30 feet apart and to about 75 feet below the existing ground surface The two reaction
shafts were equipped with four 9 reinforcing bars protruding above the existing ground
surface by about 15 feet

The load test shaft was extended by about 2 5 feet above the existing ground surface

strain gages were connected through a multiplexer to a Geokon Micro 10 datalogger to

The test shaft was loaded in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1143
Quick Load Test Method The maximum test load on the shaft was planned at 1 000 kips
In general the test shaft was loaded to the maximum test load of 1 000 kips in 50 kip load
increments Each load increment was held for at least 15 minutes At the 600 kip load
increment the test load was held for 120 minutes followed by four 150 kip decrements to
release the test load The test load was then increased in four 150 kip increments back to
the 600 kip loading Each decrement and increment was also sustained for at least
15 minutes
After loading the test shaft to the maximum test load of 1 000 kips the maximum test
load was held for 12 hours as specified Subsequently the test load was unloaded in
approximately four equal decrements and was held at each decrement until a stable reading
was obtained The load test readings and the load displacement curve are presented on

INTERPRETATION OF LOAD TEST RESULTS
Based on the readings obtained from the embedment strain gages it appears that
seven embedment strain gages were functioning during the load test In order to interpret
the load transfer in the load test shaft the modulus of elasticity of the concrete will be
required On the day before the load test July 30th two concrete cylinder compression
tests were performed on the load test shaft concrete by our laboratory The results of the two
unconfined compressive strength tests were 4 545 and 4 877 pounds per square inch psi
with an average of about 4 713 psi Based on an unconfined compressive strength of 4 713
psi the modulus of elasticity of the concrete is estimated to be on the order of about
psi Based on one elastic modulus test conducted on one of the concrete
samples a modulus of elasticity of psi was obtained Therefore we used an
average modulus of elasticity of about psi in our interpretation of the load test
results

RECOMMENDED PRODUCTION SHAFT TIP ELEVATIONS
Based on our observations of the trial shaft program we believe that the
demonstrated excavation method and equipment are acceptable for installing the production
drilled shafts in general accordance with the project specifications It should be noted that
the trial shaft was installed without the use of a temporary casing Therefore the need for
temporary casing of the production drilled shafts will need to be evaluated on a shaft by shaft
basis by an engineer from our office during the production shaft installation
Based on the results of the load test conducted we recommend that the following new
tip elevations be used for the production shafts These new tip elevations reflect a shorter
drilled shaft length than those originally estimated and shown on the project plans
