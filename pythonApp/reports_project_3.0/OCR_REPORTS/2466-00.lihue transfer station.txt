INTRODUCTION
This report represents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the design of the proposed solid waste transfer station at Linue Kauai
Hawaii
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to make obtain an overview of the subsurface
soils and rock conditions at the site of the proposed transfer station in order to provide
data for the formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to the
design of proposed site grading and building foundations
The scope of our exploration consisted of the following tasks and work efforts
1 Mobilization of a truck mounted drill rig and operators to and from the
project site
2 Drilling and sampling of eight 8 borings to depths ranging between 11 5
and 31 5 feet below the existing ground surface and the performance of
percolation tests at the project site
3 Logging of the borings supervision of the percolation testing and coordina
tion of the field work by a Field Geologist from our firm
4 Engineering analyses of the exploration data for the formulation of
geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to the design of the
project site grading and foundations
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2466 00 Page 3
5 Preparation of this report presenting our findings and recommendations
6 Coordination of our overall work on the project and design team consulta
tion by a Principal Engineer of our firm
7 Miscellaneous work efforts such as drafting word processing and clerical
support
The work was done generally in accordance with our proposals of October 24
1989 and March 7 1990
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration and laboratory testing are presented
in the appendices of this report
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is situated in Lihue Kauai The general location of the site and its
vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The site is located adjacent to Ahukini Road near the old Lihue Airport Terminal in
Lihue on the island of Kauai Hawaii The site is nearly level with a slight slope towards
the south The site has been used for the cultivation of sugar cane and was cleared for
topographic survey and for this exploration
The existing ground surface elevation of the site varies from approximately 76 to
89 above Mean Sea Level
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2466 00 Page 4
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a solid waste transfer station at the site The proposed
transfer station will be roughly L shaped It is anticipated that the site will be ramped up
to a tipping area above a lower area for the parking of transfer trucks The project will
also include asphaltic concrete and portland cement concrete pavements for the
driveways and parking areas
The details of the proposed site grading were made available subsequent to our
field exploration Based on the plans provided it is our anticipation that the grading will
require cuts of about four to eight feet and fills of up to about ten feet above the existing
ground surface elevation
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
The island of Kauai is composed of a single basalt shield volcano built by the
extrusion of the lavas of the Waimea Canyon Volcanic Series during the late Pliocene
more than 2 3 million years before present Following the cessation of this main shield
building phase there was renewed volcanic activity with the extrusion of the basaltic lavas
of the post erosional Koloa Volcanic Series and the concurrent deposition of the alluvial
sediments of the Palikea Formation Our exploration of the site and its vicinity indicates
that the area is underlain by thick soil layers derived from the in situ weathering of Koloa
Basalts

Subsurface Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by drilling and sampling
eight 8 borings to depths ranging between 11 5 and 31 5 feet below the existing ground
surface The approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general the borings encountered a surface layer approximately 18 to 24 inches
in thickness of organically contaminated topsoil Below this topsoil layer the borings en
countered moist very stiff to very hard reddish brown and brownish red clayey silts
Residual Soil to the maximum depths explored of 31 5 feet below the existing ground
surface A slight decrease in soil consistency was noted at a depth of about 14 5 feet
below the existing surface where the material began to grade into saprolitic soil
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings during the time of our exploration
DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
It is our opinion that development of the proposed project site is feasible provided
that our recommendations are followed We recommend that spread or strip footings
bear on the underlying hard residual soils encountered at the site or on fill placed and
compacted in accordance with recommendations presented herein
The bottom of all footing excavations should be inspected by the geotechnical
engineer prior to the placement of the reinforcement to determine if adequate bearing
has been obtained and that loose soils are removed prior to concrete placement
We estimate that for footings designed and constructed in accordance with the
above criteria the potential settlements for foundations bearing on in situ residual soils
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2466 00 Page 6
should be on the order of less than one quarter of an inch total and differential and the
potential settlements for foundations bearing on fill placed and compacted as recom
mended herein should be on the order of less than one half of an inch total and
differential
Site Grading
Prior to the commencement of any earthwork all areas within the grading limits
should be cleared of all vegetation and deleterious materials The surface layer of
organically contaminated topsoil should also be stripped and screened to remove the
sugar cane fragments The resulting stripped and processed material should be
stockpiled for use in landscaping and or general yard fill Any soft weak or yielding
areas disclosed during the clearing operations should be over excavated to firm ground
and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with well compacted engineered fill
Generally the on site soils except for the topsoil should be suitable for use as
engineered fill If additional imported material is required the material should conform
to the requirements stipulated in Section 30 of the Standard Specifications for Public
Works Construction Select Borrow for Subbase Course dated September 1986
All fills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding eight 8 inches in loose
thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density as established
by ASTM Test Method D snould be placed in level lifts not exceeding
six 6 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry
density as established by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 The last two 2 feet of backfill
below subgrade level in paved areas should be compacted to a minimum of 95 of its
maximum dry density as established by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2466 00 Page 7
The on site soils are clayey silt soils therefore mechanical compactive effort is
necessary to achieve the proper degree of compaction for structural fill Sheepsfoot type
compactors are highly suited to the on site soils Water tamping or flooding will not
provide sufficient compaction
Due to the silty nature of the on site soils we recommend that dust control
procedures be implemented for earthwork activities conducted during dry weather
Cut areas underlying structures or pavements should be excavated to rough finish
subgrade and compacted to 90 of its maximum dry density as specified above
Foundation Support
Spread or strip footings should bear on the very stiff to hard residual soils
encountered at the site or on fill placed and compacted in accordance with the
recommendations presented herein
An allowable bearing capacity of 4000 p s f may be utilized for the design of
foundations bearing on the very stiff residual soils An allowable bearing capacity of 2500
p s f may be utilized for the design of foundations bearing on fill placed and compacted
in accordance with the recommendations presented herein These values may be
increased by one third when temporary wind and seismic loads are considered The
footings should be embedded a minimum of two 2 feet below the lowest adjacent grade
If any soft or weak areas are encountered at the bottom of the footing excavation
they should be remedied by over excavation of the soft and or weak materials to very stiff
soils and backfilling of the resultant void with on site soil placed and compacted as
recommended above

Retaining Walls
It is anticipated that retaining walls will be used to provide grade separation
between the tipping area and the transfer vehicle floor The guidelines for design of
retaining walls are as follows
1 For lateral earth pressures the following values may be used
Walls unrestrained at the top 35 p s f equivalent
active condition level fluid pressure
backfill per foot of depth
Walls unrestrained at the top 50 p s f equivalent
active condition 2H 1V fluid pressure
sloping backfill per foot of depth
Walls restrained at the top 55 p s f equivalent
at rest condition fluid pressure
level backfill per foot of depth
Traffic and other surcharge loads are not included in the above recommended
pressures
2 An allowable bearing capacity of up to 4500 p s f is recommended for
retaining wall foundations bearing on very stiff to very hard on site residual
soils
3 A passive earth resistance of 300 p s f per foot of depth may be used for
the wall footing on level areas The passive pressure must be reduced for
walls located on slopes
4 A base friction factor of 0 4 may also be used to determine the resistance
against sliding of the wall
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2466 00 Page 9
5 The wall foundations on relatively flat areas should be embedded a
minimum of 24 inches below the lowest adjacent grade For sloping
ground the footing should extend to obtain a minimum 8 foot set back from
the outside edge of the footing measured horizontally to the slope face
6 A subsurface drainage system subdrains and or weephole should be
utilized behind the walls to minimize the build up of hydrostatic pressure
behind the walls Subdrains should be constructed properly to drain water
away from the wall
7 Select granular backfill material such as ASTM C 33 67 gravel 3B Fine
is recommended behind the retaining wall to minimize excessive compaction
required and to aid in the drainage of the area behind the wall The
granular backfill should have a minimum horizontal thickness of 18 inches
from the back face of the wall
For the backfill of exterior retaining walls the granular backfill should extend
to a height of two 2 feet below finish grade The remaining backfill should
consist of an impervious capping of select non expansive on site soil
For the backfill of interior walls the granular backfill should extend to the
slab or rigid pavement subgrade
8 It is our understanding that the retaining wall in the transfer station building
will retain approximately ten 10 feet of new structural fill In order to
facilitate construction of the wall and to reduce compaction loads on the

Slabs On Grade
It is anticipated that the floors of proposed buildings on the project site will be
concrete slabs on grade Concrete slabs subject to vehicular traffic should be considered
rigid pavements and are discussed in a subsequent section
Building slabs and their cushions may be supported by the upper residual soil or
by fill provided the subgrade is first scarified to a depth of ten 10 inches moisture
conditioned to the wet side of optimum moisture content and recompacted to 90 relative
dry density as determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 A 4 inch layer of ASTM C
Fine gravel should be provided below the slabs as cushion fill
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2466 00 Page 11
To minimize future subgrade soils from heaving due to subsequent saturation we
recommend that the subgrade soils should be kept continuously moist by water sprinkling
prior to concrete placement However care must be exercised to avoid oversaturation
and softening of the slab subgrade soils Such oversaturation and softening could result
in a loss of bearing support for the slab
Slab perimeter thickened edges should extend a minimum of twelve 12 inches
below the lowest adjacent exterior grade

Percolation Testing
Falling head percolation tests were conducted in two 2 areas of the project site
The test results are summarized in Appendix C
These tests indicated that the soil absorption rates for the on site soils are in
excess of 60 minutes per inch It is our opinion that this absorption rate is too low for the
practical use of soil absorption systems such as leach fields or seepage pits for the
disposal of sanitary wastes
Design Review
Plans and specifications for foundations site grading and utility construction should
be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written comments prior to construction
to see that the intent of our recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be present during earthwork
construction to monitor the contractor s grading operations
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be observed by the
geotechnical engineer during construction to see that adequate bearing has been
obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of our
investigation

the soils engineer prior its delivery to the site
If conditions encountered during construction are different from those presented
in this report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the above
inspections

IV Fill Materials
Material for fill shall consist of on site soils or approved import material Fill
material shall contain less than 5 percent organic matter and other deleterious
material and shall not contain rocks or lumps in excess of twelve inches 12 in
diameter
Representative samples of imported materials to be used as compacted fill shall
be tested by the Geotechnical Engineer Samples of the proposed materials shall
be submitted to the Soils Engineer at least ten working days prior to its intended
jobsite delivery
V Placement and Compaction of Fill
A The fill snall be placed in horizontal layers not exceeding eight inches 8 in
loose thickness Each layer shall be spread uniformly and blade mixed to
attain uniformity of material and water content within each layer
Additional fill material shall not be placed on any fill layer which has not been
properly compacted Subsequent removal of any unacceptable layer shall
be done at the Contractor s expense
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2466 00 Plate D 8
Appendix D Continued
B Rocks greater than eight inches 8 in diameter shall be removed from the
fill unless specifically approved for placement in the fill by the Geotechnical
Engineer
C All fill snall be compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry
density established by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 unless otherwise
specified in the Geotechnical Engineer s Report
D Boulders encountered in excavations may be used in the deeper fills
provided that the following are followed
1 Boulders shall be spread out and must not be nested together They
should be placed so that compaction equipment will be able to compact
around them
2 No part of the boulder should enroach any closer to finished subgrade
than the boulder s largest dimension and in no case be closer than 3
feet to finished subgrade
3 No boulder larger than 5 feet in its largest dimension should be placed
in the fill It may be necessary to reduce the size of larger boulders or
dispose of them off site
4 Care must be exercised to avoid placement of boulders in the proposed
utility alignments to prevent difficulty in the later excavation of the utility
trenches

over the entire fill area to produce a uniformly compacted fill
