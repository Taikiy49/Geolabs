PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located on the east side of Hunalewa Street in the District of
Kapahulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project site is bounded by existing
Hunalewa Street that is discontinued on the north and south side and discontinued in the
central portion where the old Pikake Farm was located The location of the project site is
shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The project site includes a total of
11 residential lots and two roadways The site grading work generally consisted of over
excavation of the clay material 4 feet below finish grade and 3 feet beyond the building
pad One foot of on site compacted material moistened to above optimum moisture
content was placed followed by 3 feet of compacted structural fill within lot areas The
driveways consisted of over excavations of clay material 3 feet below finish grade and
3 feet beyond the driveway The excavation was backfilled with 1 foot of on site
compacted material moistened to above optimum moisture content then 2 feet of
compacted structural fill was placed in lifts The grading plan for the site is shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services
pertaining to the site grading operations and infrastructure installation for the project site
SITE GRADING AND UTILITY INSTALLATION

project was observed by Geolabs Inc from to The site
grading work consisted of clearing and grubbing over excavation placement and
compaction of fills up to about 4 feet in thickness to achieve the design finished grades

The site grading and utility backfilling work was performed by Jayar Construction During
the site grading operations samples of the on site and imported materials utilized as fill
and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing The
maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory
in general accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test results are
summarized on Plate 3
The fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as
determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557 In the roadway areas the fill and backfill
materials within 2 feet of the finished subgrade were compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative density
During the site grading and utility backfilling operations periodic field density tests
were performed in general accordance with ASTM D 2922 Nuclear Gauge Method
Results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 4 1 through 4 36 of
this report These tests were performed on the prepared over excavated surfaces and on
constructed fills and backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by
pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should
be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed
to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained
and or approved by our field representative based on visual observation and hand probing
Unsuitable material consisting of trees wood and concrete fragments and some
metal was encountered from about 1 to 3 feet below existing grade in the area between
Lot and Baseline B The unsuitable material was removed and replaced with
structural fill
Our services consisted of full time and part time field observation of the contractor s
fill placement subgrade preparation and compaction procedures and confirmation of
subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above grading operations

covered by this report were performed in conformance with the general intent of the

HOUSE CONSTRUCTION CONSIDERATIONS
The finished grades adjacent to the house structures should be sloped to shed
water away from the foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also advised
that gutter systems be installed around the house structures and the discharge be diverted
away from the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering within
3 feet of the foundations and slabs should be avoided Gravel strips adjacent to slabs and
foundations should be avoided Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have
concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the house
foundations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation and subsequent
loss of strength of the near surface soils The foundation excavations should be properly
backfilled against the footings immediately after setting of the concrete to reduce water
infiltration Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and should be
maintained to drain surface run off away from foundations Landscaping of the house lots
by the homeowners is recommended to reduce the potential for erosion and for altering
the drainage patterns
Consideration should be given to constructing a 3 foot wide sidewalk adjacent to
and surrounding the entire house structure A sidewalk with an 8 inch deep
thickened edge should help stabilize the subgrade soil moisture below the house
structure and therefore reduce the potential for swelling shrinking and softening of the
subgrade soils
