INTRODUCTION
The purpose of the geologic reconnaissance
and soil study for the 2 800 lineal feet
access roadway is to determine the following
1 The general geologic conditions of the
area and the feasibility of access road
way construction
2 The existence of rock and availability
of soil material for embankment fills
3 Site grading recommendations including
cut and fill slope design parameters
4 Design recommendations for stream crossing
structure

FIELD INVESTIGATION
Subsurface conditions were explored by drilling 2 borings
with truck mounted drilling equipment The field explora
tion was supplemented by digging 9 test pits with a hydraulic
backhoe The boring and test pit locations are shown on the
Site Plan Plate 1 Both soil and rock samples were collected
during the field exploration
Soil samples were visually identified and tentatively classi
fied in the field In the laboratory these samples were
further subjected to appropriate tests The field identifi
cations and classifications were then reviewed and modified
to conform with the Unified Soil Classification System
LABORATORY TESTS
Appropriate soil samples were recovered and tested in the
laboratory Laboratory tests include in place moisture
contents Atterberg Limits C B R and Torvane Shear The
results of these tests are presented on the Test Pit Logs
at the appropriate sample locations
SITE CONDITIONS
The proposed access roadway generally runs in a J shaped
alignment at the lower campus of Kamehameha School The
new roadway begins at the end of the existing Makukane Street
then crosses the Kapalama Stream and runs along the Ewa face
of Kapalama Ridge and ends at the existing campus roadway near
the Kindergarten

GENERAL GEOLOGIC SITE DESCRIPTION
The Koolau volcanic shield was eroded during long periods of
volcanic quiessence and gradual oceanic transgressions Allu
vial accumulation in Kahili Valley and the nearby Kamanaiki
Valley increased as the island of Oahu sank at least 1 200
feet This period of erosion lasted at least 2 million years
Eventually the valley broadened out by the Joomeuseing oF the
stream and formed a steepwalled valley The flat floor of the
valley was formed by the thick alluvium and banked itself against
the steep valley walls due to a rise in sea level The abrupt
break in slope between cliff face and the valley floor can be
measured in terms of angles and degrees and is known as the

SOIL CONDITIONS
In general the upper portion of the site above elevation
300 feet is underlain by weathered to hard lava rock layers
Boring No 1 represents this typical condition
In the lower portion of the site between test pits TP 9
and TP 7 the lava rock formation is at the existing ground
surface or under a shallow Adobe talus soil layer In the
lower end section between test pit TP 6 and Boring No 2
lava rock formation or boulder layer is about 5 to 6 feet
below the ground surface except at test pit TP 5 where lava
rock is about 2 feet below the surface The surface layer
in this lower area is composed of brown silty clay or dark
gray Adobe clay soil with occasional boulder or boulderly
layers
DISCUSSION RECOMMENDATIONS
General
It is estimated that the majority of the general excavation
along the roadway may require heavy blasting
GEOLABS HAWAII
W 5
The exposed rock outcrop throughout the site will provide
an excellent foundation support for fill embankment and
stable cut slope bank
The gray Adobe soil encountered at the lower portion of
the roadway has low shear strength and high swell potential
when wetted Therefore it is not recommended as a source
of fill material for the roadway embankment fill
Site Grading
A minimum 2 feet of the surface dark gray talus Adobe clay
soils should be stripped from the sloping surface prior to
embankment fill and in no case should the Adobe clay soils
be located within 3 feet from the proposed finished grade
Fill should be placed in 9 inch compacted lifts to a minimum
of 90 relative compaction Where the natural ground slope
is 5 horizontal to 1 vertical 5 1 or steeper the fill
material should be keyed and benched into the lava rock
formation or soil
Due to the lack of on site fine grained soils additional
source of select and borrow materials would be required to
supplement the excavated rocky materials Fine grained
materials such as coral cinder mudrock and quarry waste
would be required as rock filler materials if rock fragments
or boulders less than eight 8 inches in diameter are utilized

Slopes
In general the overall design cut slope should be one
horizontal to one vertical 1 1 At the time of con
struction this cut slope ratio may be increased up to
horizontal to 1 vertical 4 1 in dense basalt rock
layers upon field verification by the soils engineer
Fill slopes should be designed for two horizontal to
one vertical 2 1 ratio
Benches are not required for the cut slope in rock
formation

Stream Crossing Structure
For the proposed stream crossing culvert structure bearing
on the brown boulderly clay soil allowable bearing pressure
of 3 500 p s f may be used
In order to provide uniform support for the foundation
footing and the culvert structure the excavation should
extend 1 foot below the foundation and slab bottom elevation
and replaced with granular bedding materials The bedding
material should consist of base course rock and compacted to
95 maximum density
If granular material is used to backfill behind the drainage
structure an equivalent pressure of 35 p c f per foot of
depth can be used in the design of the lateral earth pressure
resistance

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
The earthwork should be monitored during construction by a
soils engineer to observe the removal of the surface Adobe
clay prior to site filling and keying operations The soils
engineering inspections should also include the examination
testing and approval of fill materials field observation
density tests of the fill placement and compaction
The soils engineer or geologist may be needed to inspect the
cut formation to assure that adequate stability is maintained
on cut slopes
Additional CBR tests should be performed on actual subgrade
soil samples at the time of construction and the above pave
ment design sections may be revised accordingly
