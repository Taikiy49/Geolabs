INTRODUCTION
This report summarizes data and results from a pavement survey for the
In Town Bus Rapid Transit Primary Corridor Transportation Project conducted in
May 2003 This pavement survey was conducted on the BRT alignment
consisting of Kapiolani Boulevard King Street River Street Hotel Street
Bishop Street Alakea Street Aloha Tower Road Nimitz Highway Ala Moana
Boulevard Ward Avenue Auahi Street Kalia Road Saratoga Street Kalakaua
Avenue Kapahulu Avenue and Kuhio Avenue in the city of Honolulu on the
Island of Oahu Hawaii
The purpose of this pavement condition survey was to identify areas of
damaged or deficient pavement along the proposed In Town Bus Rapid Transit
BRT alignment in need of rehabilitation work and to gather the information
necessary to form the basis of recommendations for improvements to the
existing roadways that are designated to be bus routes
Accurate as built plans of the existing pavement were not available for our
study Therefore only visual observations were utilized

METHODOLOGY
The pavement condition survey was conducted in May 2003 The
pavement segments surveyed included both asphalt concrete pavement and
Portland Cement Concrete pavement During the survey the inspector visually
observed the pavement conditions along the BRT alignment recorded
approximate pavement distress locations on the provided site plans referenced to
the nearby features shown on the same plans and determined pavement
distress types through visual classification
The visual classification was performed in general accordance with the
definitions described in Paver Asphalt Distress Manual and Paver Concrete
Distress Manual dated June 1997 The manuals were prepared by the
Maintenance and Preservation Division FL P of the Facilities Technology
Laboratory FL U S Army Construction Engineering Research Laboratories
USACERL Some of the pavement distress definitions are presented in the
following tables
ASPHALT DISTRESSES
Alligator Crack Alligator cracks are a series of interconnecting cracks
Fatigue caused by fatigue failure of the asphalt concrete surface
under repeated traffic loading
Block Crack Block cracks are interconnected cracks that divide the
pavement into approximately rectangular pieces
Edge Crack Edge cracks are cracks parallel to and usually within 0 3 to
0 5 m 1 to 1 5 feet of the outer edge of the pavement
Longitudinal Crack Longitudinal cracks are cracks parallel to the pavement s
centerline or laydown direction
Transverse Crack Transverse cracks are cracks extending across the
pavement at approximately right angles to the pavement
centerline or direction of laydown
Patch and Utility Patch is an area of pavement that has been replaced with
Cut Patch new material to repair the existing pavement
Pothole Potholes are small bowl shaped usually less than 750 mm
or 10 in in diameter depression in the pavement surface
Rutting A rut is a surface depression in the wheel paths
Weathering and Weathering and raveling are the wearing away of the
Raveling pavement surface due to a loss of asphalt or tar binder and
dislodged aggregate particles

CONCRETE DISTRESSES
Linear Crack Linear cracks divide the slab into two or three pieces and
are usually caused by a combination of repeated traffic
loading thermal gradient curling and repeated moisture
loading
Divided Slab Slab is divided by cracks into four or more pieces due to
overloading and or inadequate support
Faulting Faulting is the difference in elevation across a joint
Patching and A patch is an area where the original pavement has been
Utility Cuts removed and replaced by filler material
A utility cut is a patch that has replaced the original
pavement to allow the installation or maintenance of
underground utilities

LIMITATIONS
The findings presented in this report are based upon information obtained
from our field inspection As stated earlier this pavement condition survey was
conducted by visual inspection The locations of the pavement distresses
indicated in this report are approximate having been estimated by taping from
the existing features shown on the provided site plans The distresses were
classified by visual classification based on two manuals Paver Asphalt Distress
and Paver Concrete Distress The distress area locations and distress types
presented in this report should be considered accurate only to the degree implied
by the methods used
During the pavement condition survey it was noticed that many of the
streets have been overlaid over the years The overlay thickness ranged from
1 inch to several inches Due to the existence of the overlays we were unable to
visually observe the actual conditions of the existing pavements below the
overlay Therefore it should be noted that undetected distress may be present in
some of the streets which are not shown in this report

Transportation Services City and County of Honolulu Parsons Brinckerhoff and
other project consultants for specific application to the In Town Bus Rapid Transit
project located at Honolulu Hawaii in accordance with generally accepted
