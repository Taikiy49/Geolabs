Soil Engineering Report
Lot 99 Royal Summit Subdivision
Phase II
Aiea Oahu Hawaii
In accordance with your request we have made a study of the near
surface soil conditions at the above referenced lot in order to
provide foundation recommendations for the proposed residence The
study consisted of excavating three shallow test pits performing
laboratory testing and reviewing the subdivision site grading plans
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
The preliminary house plans indicate that a 2 story wooden
structure with a semi basement level in the back is planned
Concrete on grade slabs are anticipated for the entire house
W Page 2
SITE CONDITIONS
The subject lot is located south of the end of Road I east in
the Royal Summit Phase II Subdivision in Aiea Oahu Hawaii
Lot 99 is terraced and slopes downward from the front towards the
rear of the lot A relatively level pad about 30 feet wide is
located adjacent to the roadway The site then Slopes downward at
about a 5 1 H V ratio to a lower second level pad at the rear of the
lot The ground surface then continues down on a 2 1 H V slope
below the western lot boundary
There are existing sewer lines 6 and 8 inch along the northern
eastern and southern boundaries of the lot There is also an 18 inch
drain line extending along the northern boundary and a shallow
concrete drainage ditch along the southern boundary
SUBSURFACE SOIL CONDITIONS
Three test pits each to a depth of 3 5 feet were excavated
below the existing grade at the approximate locations shown on the

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The stiff reddish brown clayey silt soil encountered generally
can provide adequate foundation support for the proposed house
provided that some care is exercised during construction to minimize
the swelling potential of the soil
Laboratory tests performed on the stiff on site clayey silt soil
indicates that this material generally has low to moderate swelling
potential if maintained at or above the optimum moisture content 30
more or less However previous experience in the area indicates
that some of the on site soil can exhibit medium to high swelling if
allowed to dry out then wetted Therefore to minimize effects due to
soil swelling under on grade slabs we recommend that the on site soil

below the on grade slabs be capped with at least 6 inches of
non expansive select fill and that the subgrade soils underneath the
select fill and slab be moistened and protected from drying from the
start of construction until the slab concrete placement
Lot Grading
The fills and backfills below the on grade slabs should consist
of non expansive soil such as select borrow crusher waste or coral
The on site soil under the garage and driveway areas may have to be
Over excavated to provide the 6 inch minimum non expansive fill
capping The remainder of the fill outside of the structure area may
consist of either off site borrow or the on site soils in the Royal
Summit area that generally have low to moderate swelling potential
Imported fill materials should be tested and approved by the
soils engineer prior to being used in the site
Prior to construction the area to receive any 111 should be
cleared of vegetation and debris
The fills should be placed in horizontal lifts not exceeding 8
inches in compacted thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its
maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78 The moisture content of the
on site fill should be kept within 5 percentage points wet of the
optimum during compaction

Fills placed on existing Slopes having gradients steeper than 5 1
H V should be keyed and benched into the existing ground to bond the
new fill to the existing slope
The site should be graded to provide drainage of surface runoff
away from all foundations
House Foundations
It is anticipated that the house garage and the driveway will be
of slab on grade construction
Spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the proposed
residence An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f may be used on
the compacted soil
Interior footings on level areas should be embedded a minimum of
12 inches below the finished grade Perimeter wall footings should be
embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the adjacent finished grade to
provide a barrier against future moisture infiltration under the slab
Footings located on the sloping areas should be embedded down a
sufficient depth to obtain a 6 foot minimum horizontal set back
distance measured from the outer edge of the footing to the slope
face This additional embedment depth is required to help resist the
downhill slope creep movement and to develop the required confinement

An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s may be used for
footings on stiff on site soil The footing excavation should be
examined and approved by the soils engineer
The eastern side of the house is located about 7 to 8 feet from
the existing sewer easement The construction plans indicate that the
sewer invert is located about 10 to 5 feet below the existing ground
To minimize the possibility of footing settlement due to settlement of
the trench backfill or due to sewer trench excavation at a later date
for repair purposes we recommend that the footings in this area be
embedded below a H V plane extending upward from the edge of
the easement
Surface water should be diverted away fron foundations
Slab On Grade
Concrete slab on grade is planned for the driveway garage and
the house It should be noted that some of the on site clayey silt
soils are low to moderately expansive and therefore should be
over excavated down to a depth of 6 inches below the slab base course
bottom wetted and then replaced with non expansive imported soils as
previously discussed The over excavation and backfilling should
extend a minimum of one foot beyond the edge of the slabs The
thickened edge of the slab should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches
below the lowest adjacent finish grade

To further reduce future post construction swelling of the
underlying expansive soils we recommend that the soil below the
capping fill pad be kept moist cont inuously
A 4 inch layer of 3 Fine gravel cushion fill should be provided
below the slab In order to protect against moisture infiltration an
impervious moisture barrier may be incorporated below the slab The
on grade slabs should be structurally separated from wall foundations
Retaining Walls
Retaining walls may be required for the proposed residence The
guidelines for the retaining wall designs are listed below
1 For lateral earth pressures the following can be used
Walls unrestrained at the top 35 p s f equivalent
active condition level fluid pressure
backfill per foot of depth
Wall restrained at the top 50 p s f equivalent
at rest condition fluid pressure
level backfill per foot of depth
2 The wall foundations on relatively flat areas should be embedded a
minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade If
located on a sloping area the foundation should be embedded down
an additional amount to obtain a minimum 6 feet horizontal
set back distance measured from the outer edge of the footing to
the slope face

3 An allowable bearing value of up to 2500 p s f is recommended for
foundations bearing on imported select borrow or on stiff on site
soil
4 A passive earth resistance of 300 p s per foot of depth may be
used for the wall footing on level areas The passive pressure
should be ignored for walls on slopes
5 A base friction factor of 0 4 may also be used to determine the
resistance against sliding of the wall
6 Backfills behind the retaining wall should consist of granular
non expansive material such as select borrow crusher waste or
3B Fine gravel and extend a minimum horizontal distance of 5
feet behind the wall
7 A subsurface drainage system subdrains or weepholes should be
utilized behind the walls Subdrains should be properly
constructed to drain water away from the wall
Drainage
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have
concrete bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the
subsoils
GEOLABS HAWAII

These drainage requirements are essential for the proper
performance of the above recommended foundations since ponded water
could cause excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent
heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations should be
properly backfilled tight against the wall or slab footing immediately
after setting of the concrete to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and
maintained to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing
foundations
Design Review
Final plans and specifications for foundations site grading and
utility construction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for
review and comments prior to construction to see that the intent of
our recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the lot grading footing excavations and
backfill operations be observed by the soils engineer during
construction to see that adequate bearing has been obtained and that
the subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of our
investigation
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above inspections
