INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Social Hall and Multi Purpose Building of Lihue Christian
Church located at the end of Kress Street in downtown Lihue on the island of Kauai
Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the site are shown on the Project Location
Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings from our field exploration and laboratory
testing and engineering analyses These recommendations are intended for
foundation site grading and pavement design input and may not be appropriate to
form the basis for construction cost estimates nor for construction bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed project consists of the construction of a Social Hall and a Multi
Purpose Building as additions to the existing Lihue Christian Church in Lihue on the
island of Kauai Hawaii Based on information provided to us the proposed Social Hall
would have a gross building area of approximately 5 952 square feet and the proposed
Multi Purpose building would have a gross area of approximately 1 550 square feet as
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
We understand that the Social Hall will be of concrete masonry construction and
the Multi Purpose building will be of wood framed construction Both structures will be
single story with on grade concrete slabs Detailed grading plans for the project were
not available at the time this report was prepared however we anticipate that minor
cuts and fills of less than 3 feet relative to the existing ground surface will be required
to provide level building pads

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions at the locations of the proposed structures The subsurface information
obtained was used to develop an idealized soil data set for the formulation of
geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to the design of foundations and
site grading for the proposed project Our work was done in general accordance with
our fee proposal dated The scope of work included the following
tasks and work efforts

operators to and from the project site

up to depths of approximately 21 feet below the existing ground surface

Engineer of our firm
4 Laboratory testing of selected soil samples obtained during the field
exploration to evaluate the classification and engineering properties of the
materials encountered
5 Engineering analyses of the field and laboratory data to develop
geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to foundation design
and site grading for the proposed project

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsoil conditions at the subject site were explored by drilling and sampling
a total of three borings to depths of about 19 to 21 feet below the existing ground
surface The approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Based on the results of the three borings performed at the site we generally
encountered residual soils consisting of reddish brown to yellowish brown silty clays
from the ground surface to the maximum depths explored The consistency of the
residual soils ranged from very stiff to hard Based on laboratory tests the near
surface soils appear to be low to moderately expansive when subjected to changes in
moisture contents
Groundwater was not encountered in the drilling borings at the time of our field
exploration It should be noted that groundwater levels are subject to change due to
rainfall and time of year

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
In general our field exploration indicates that the proposed project site is
underlain by very stiff to hard residual clayey soil to the maximum depth drilled
approximately 21 feet below the existing ground surface Based on the field
exploration and laboratory testing data we believe that a shallow footing foundation
may be used to support the proposed building structures
Laboratory tests performed on the near surface clayey soils indicate that the
near surface materials generally have a low to moderate shrink swell potential when
subjected to fluctuations in the moisture content Slabs on grade supported on a6 inch
layer of non expansive select granular fill may be used to minimize the potential
swelling and shrinkage effects of the near surface clayey soils
The proposed multi purpose building will be located in the vicinity of the existing
steep slope bank Therefore we recommend that a minimum horizontal setback
distance of at least 15 feet be provided at the top of the slope bank This set back
requirement is to avoid surcharging of the existing bank and to reduce the potential
impact on the new buildings due to future slope erosion and slope movement
Detailed discussions and recommendations for foundations site grading and
other geotechnical aspects of the project are presented in the following sections of this
report

Foundations
Based on the subsurface soil conditions encountered in our exploration we
believe that a shallow foundation system consisting of spread and or continuous
footings bearing on the very stiff residual soils may be used to support the proposed
building structures In areas where significant grading is expected the shallow
foundations may be placed on the properly placed and compacted fill
An allowable bearing pressure of up to 2 500 p s f may be used for the design
of footings The allowable bearing pressure is for dead plus live loads and may be
increased by one third s for transient loadings such as temporary wind and or seismic
forces Footings should be embedded a minimum of 24 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grade For foundations designed in accordance with our recommen
dations we estimate maximum total settlement of footings to be on the order of inch
with differential settlements of about inch
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by frictional resistance
between the base of the spread footing and the bearing materials and by passive earth
pressure developed against the near vertical faces of the embedded portion of footings
A coefficient of friction of 0 35 may be used between the base of the footings and the
bearing material Resistance due to passive pressure may be calculated using an
equivalent fluid pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth p c f for
footings embedded in the residual soils and engineered fill These values assume that
the concrete for footings is placed directly against the footing excavations Unless
covered by pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the upper 12 inches below
finished grade should be neglected

Where footings are expected to be located adjacent to the existing below grade

these materials should be over excavated to firm soils and replaced with compacted
engineered fill
Slabs On Grade
We anticipate concrete slabs on grade will be used for the proposed buildings
Based on our laboratory test results the near surface silty clay soils appear to exhibit
low to moderate swelling potential when subjected to fluctuations in the moisture
content To reduce the potential for structural distress resulting from the swelling of the
on site clayey subgrade soils we recommend that concrete slabs on grade be
supported on a minimum 6 inch layer of non expansive select capping fill material
The non expansive capping fill should extend a minimum of 2 feet laterally beyond the
limits of the proposed building slab The subgrade soil should be kept moist prior to
conditioning and compaction of fill materials are discussed in the Grading section
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3267 00 Page 9
Slab subgrade should be wetted and kept moist prior to concrete placement A
4 inch layer of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C33 No 67 gradation is recommended
above the underlying 6 inch non expansive capping fill to provide a capillary break
cushion layer under the slab To reduce future moisture infiltration and subsequent
damage to the floor coverings an impervious moisture vapor barrier such as a plastic
membrane is recommended on top of the gravel cushion layer A 2 inch layer of moist
fine sand should be placed above the plastic membrane to protect the membrane and
to aid in concrete curing
For design of structural slabs a modulus of subgrade reaction of 120 pounds per
cubic inch p c i may be used for the compacted subgrade The thickened edges of
slabs adjacent to any unpaved area should be embedded at least 12 inches below the
lowest adjacent grade
Site Grading
Final grading plans were not available to us at the time this report was prepared
However we anticipate that site grading for the proposed project will consist of minor
cuts and fills on the order of about 3 feet relative to the existing ground surface to
achieve the design finish floor elevations
Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork all areas within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris rubbish and other
unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site to
reduce potential contamination of the excavated materials
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3267 00 Page 10
After clearing and grubbing the exposed subgrade should be scarified to a
minimum depth of 8 inches properly moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent
above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90
percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry
density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the
same soil determined in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91
Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding
to the maximum dry density
Any soft weak or yielding areas disclosed during clearing operations should be
over excavated to expose firm ground and the resulting excavation should be
backfilled with engineered fill compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction Prior to placement of the backfill the over excavated subgrade
should be scarified to a depth of at least 8 inches moisture conditioned to at
least 2 percent above the optimum moisture content and compacted to at least
90 percent relative compaction The excavated soft and or organic soils should
be properly disposed of off site Contract documents should include additive and
deductive unit prices for over excavation and engineered fill construction to
account for variations in the over excavation quantities
If off site select material is required the imported fill material should be tested
and approved by Geolabs Hawaii prior to delivery to the site to confirm that it is
suitable for its intended purpose The requirement for imported fill material is
presented in the following subsection Fills and Backfills

Fills and Backfills
In general the on site materials may be used as fill and backfill materials except
where non expansive select fill is recommended It should be emphasized that
the on site silty and clayey soils should not be used as non expansive fill below
slabs on grade because of the expansive nature of the on site soils However
the on site silty and clayey soils may be used as fill material in other areas
provided that they are properly moisture conditioned and compacted as
recommended herein
Where used as fill material the on site soils should be placed in level lifts not
exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent
above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90
percent relative compaction For pavement subgrade a minimum compaction
of 95 percent relative compaction is required
As previously indicated a 6 inch non expansive select fill layer will be required
below the floor slab within the building footprint We anticipate that import fill
material would be needed to provide the recommended 6 inch non expansive
select fill The non expansive select fill material should be well graded from
coarse to fine with no particles larger than 3 inches in largest dimension and
should contain a maximum of 20 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve
The material should have a laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR value of
15 or higher and a swell potential of not more than one percent 1 when
tested in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1883 All select fill
materials should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content
_and_ compacted to 95 percent relative compaction
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3267 00 Page 12
Any imported soil should be select fill Imported materials should be tested and
approved by Geolabs Hawaii prior to delivery to the job site Imported soils
having low expansion potential should be brought to above the optimum
moisture content and compacted to not less than 95 percent relative
compaction All surfaces should be finished to create smooth unyielding
subgrades and should be kept moist until covered by concrete slabs or
pavements
Pavements
Asphalt concrete pavement for the driveway is anticipated for the project
construction We anticipate the vehicle loading for this project will consist primarily of
passenger vehicles and light pick up trucks We have made our preliminary pavement
designs assuming the pavement subgrade soil will be the native clayey and or silty soil
The subgrade soils should be moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the
optimum and compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction Based on the
above assumptions we recommend the following pavement sections be used for
preliminary design purposes
2 0 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 0 Inch Base Course 95 percent relative compaction
8 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness on 12 inches of Compacted Select
Granular Fill 95 percent relative compaction
The base course should consist of crushed basalt aggregate compacted to a
minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Paved areas should be sloped and
drainage gradients maintained to carry all surface water off the site Surface water
ponding should not be allowed anywhere on the site during or after construction
Where concrete curbs are used to isolate landscaping in or adjacent to the pavement
GEOLABS HAWAII

areas we recommend the curbs extend a minimum of 2 inches into the subgrade below
the base course aggregate to reduce migration of landscape water into the pavement
section Alternatively a subdrain system could be constructed to collect excessive

Laboratory CBR tests should be performed on samples of the actual subgrade

Underground Utilities
We envision that new utility lines may be required for the project In general a
granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of select borrow or No 3B Fine gravel is
recommended under the pipes Self compacting granular materials such as No 3B
Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation should be used for the trench backfill up
to about 12 inches above the pipes to provide adequate support around the pipes and
to reduce compaction of the backfill thus reducing the potential for damaging the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 1 foot above the pipes to
the top of the subgrade or finished grade should consist of select granular material or
the on site soils The backfill should be moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent of
the optimum moisture placed in maximum 8 inch level loose lifts and mechanically
compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for
future ground subsidence Where trenches are below pavement areas the upper 2 feet
of the trench backfill below the pavement subgrade should be compacted to 95 percent

Existing Underground Utilities
We understand that the existing underground utility lines may traverse the
proposed access road Therefore we recommend that all existing utility lines within the
project limits be accurately located by the contractor prior to construction The
contractor has the sole responsibility to devise and implement an effective program to
protect or relocate the utility lines during construction
Drainage
The finished grades outside the building should be sloped to shed water away
from foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding Also it is advised that gutter
systems be installed around the building and that the discharge be diverted away from
the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near the
foundations and slabs should also be avoided Planters next to foundations should be
avoided or have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the
subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil
saturation and subsequent heaving because of the swelling potential of the near
surface soils The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled against the
walls or slab footings immediately after setting of the concrete to reduce water
infiltration
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and maintained to drain
all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundations

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content and unit weight determinations were performed on selected soil
samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties The results of
these tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Three one inch ring swell tests were performed on selected samples to evaluate
the swelling potential of the subsoils The test results are summarized on Plate B 1
One Atterberg Limits test was performed on selected sample of the soils to
evaluate their liquid and plastic limits and to aid in soil classification Results of the test
are shown on Plate B 2
