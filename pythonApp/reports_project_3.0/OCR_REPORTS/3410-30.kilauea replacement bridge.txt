PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The replacement bridge project is along Kolo Road on the southeast side of
Liliuokalani Street in the Kilauea area on the Island of Kauai Hawaii The existing
two span concrete bridge will be replaced with a new single span bridge supporting a
two lane roadway across Kilauea Stream The project location is shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1
The replacement bridge will be approximately 110 feet long 35 feet wide and
approximately 25 feet above the lowest point of the stream The new single span bridge
will be supported on two new abutments Each abutment structure will be supported on
two rows of drilled shafts Based on the loading provided by the project Structural
Engineer the maximum axial load on the front and back row drilled shafts for the Strength
Limit State will be on the order of approximately 705 and 190 kips per shaft respectively

Based on the subsurface conditions encountered at the site and the structural
loading a deep foundation system consisting of 48 inch diameter cast in place concrete
drilled shafts was utilized for foundation support of the bridge structure Based on the
drilled shaft load test results we recommended production shaft tip elevations of 244 feet
Mean Sea Level MSL for the West Abutment back row 228 feet MSL for the West
Abutment front row 247 feet MSL for the East Abutment back row and 226 feet MSL
for the East Abutment front row
As previously mentioned the strength limit state loading governed the design
lengths of the drilled shafts for the project The drilled shafts were designed to derive
vertical support primarily from skin friction The end bearing component of the drilled
shafts was discounted in the design due to difficulties associated with obtaining a clean
bottom below the groundwater level during construction
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The borings at the bridge site generally encountered a surface fill layer ranging in
thickness from about 16 5 to 21 feet The fill layer consisted of very stiff clayey silts and
silty clays Below the fills alluvial deposits consisting of stiff to very stiff sandy silts with
boulders and basalt fragments were encountered to depths of about 33 and 40 feet below
the existing ground surface The alluvial deposits were underlain by saprolitic soils and
hard to very hard basalt formation extending to the maximum depths drilled of
approximately 101 3 and 102 5 feet below the existing ground surface
One of the borings was drilled from the existing bridge deck The boring generally
encountered alluvial deposits consisting of silty sands boulders and basalt fragments
from the stream bed about 21 feet below the bridge deck elevation to a depth of about
36 feet below the existing bridge deck The alluvial deposits were underlain by highly to
slightly weathered basalt formation extending to the maximum depth explored of
approximately 110 feet below the existing bridge deck

DRILLED SHAFT INSTALLATION
CMZ of Hawaii installed the load test and production shafts A 48 inch diameter
load test shaft designated as TS was installed between March 24 and
Polymer slurry and a 52 inch diameter temporary steel casing were used in the
construction of the load test shaft
Based on the load test shaft installation program which also served as the trial
shaft the drilled shaft subcontractor generally demonstrated that the proposed excavation
methods and equipment were acceptable for installing the drilled shaft foundations in
general accordance with the project specifications Based on the results of the
instrumented load tests performed the production drilled shaft lengths were determined
Ten 48 inch diameter production drilled shafts designated as 1 through 10 were
installed at the Kilauea Bridge site between May 6 and The approximate
drilled shaft locations are shown on the Drilled Shaft Identification Plan Plate 2 A Geolabs
representative observed the shaft excavation placement of the steel reinforcements and
concrete placement operations during the installation of the production drilled shafts
The production shafts were excavated using a Casagrande RM21 crane mounted
drill rig equipped with 24 40 and 48 inch diameter augers 24 and 48 inch diameter core
barrels 40 and 48 inch diameter digging buckets and a 48 inch diameter cleanout bucket
The production shafts were constructed without the use of polymer slurry or temporary
steel casing The production shafts were excavated to elevations ranging from about
219 78 to 246 1 feet MSL
After the shaft holes were drilled to their designated tip elevations the reinforcing
bar rebar cages were placed into the drilled holes Rebar cage spacers were used to
provide the clearance associated with a minimum concrete cover of 3 inches between the
sidewalls of the drilled hole and the sides of the rebar cage
The shaft concrete was placed after placement of the rebar cage using a 4 inch ID
steel pipe by tremie methods During concrete placement the bottom of the tremie pipe
was immersed in at least 5 feet of concrete as required in the specifications In the test
shaft excavation where temporary casing was installed a minimum of 5 feet of concrete
was maintained above the bottom of the casing during casing removal and concrete
placement

Obstruction was encountered during shaft excavation for Shafts 5 and 7
The obstruction consisted of cemented stones and boulders and appears to be part of an
old abutment structure and backfill The obstruction hours consisted of and

To verify shaft integrity Shaft 7 was cored on July 24 and 25 2008 using HQ
coring tools Based on the coring results we believe that the integrity of Shaft 7 is
satisfactory
Based on our field observations we believe that the drilled shafts for the Kilauea
Replacement Bridge have been installed in general conformance with the project plans
and specifications and within the limits of generally accepted construction practices The
location of the top of the shafts should be measured using optical survey equipment to
verify the shaft position is within the tolerance in the project specifications Based on our
observations of the drilled shaft installation it is our professional opinion that the drilled
shafts have been installed to sufficient depths to provide the design load capacity
