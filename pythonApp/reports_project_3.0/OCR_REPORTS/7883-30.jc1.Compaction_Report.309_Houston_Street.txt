We are pleased to submit our geotechnical engineering earthwork observations and 
testing report for work performed during the site grading operation of the Residence at 
309 Houston Street project in general accordance with our fee proposal dated 
December 14, 2021. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The new residence at 309 Houston Street encompasses approximately 7,000 square 
feet. Based on the information provided, we understand the site grading consists of primarily 
fills up to 2 to 3 feet thick. Special soil inspection was provided by Geolabs during the 
earthwork construction, including foundation subgrade preparation, driveway, trench 
excavation, and backfill, etc. 
Our geotechnical observation and testing efforts were focused on verification that the 
proper degree of compaction has been attained. Goodfellow Bros. performed the 
earthwork operations for the project. 
SITE GRADING 
At the on-set of earthwork, areas within the contract grading limits were generally 
cleared and grubbed of vegetation and deleterious materials. Materials generated from 
the clearing operations were disposed of off-site. Following the clearing and grubbing 
operations, the areas to be cut were excavated. Finished grades were compacted using 
a vibratory tamping plate and/or jumping jack.

Soft and yielding areas encountered during clearing and grubbing were generally 
over-excavated to expose firm natural material, and the resulting excavation was backfilled 
with well-compacted engineered fill. 
The fill and backfill materials were generally moisture-conditioned to above the 
optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum 
dry density as determined in accordance with ASTM D1557.  In the roadway areas, the 
subgrade was compacted to a minimum of 95 percent of the maximum dry density. 
During the site grading operations, samples of the materials utilized as fill were 
obtained and transported to our laboratory for testing.  The maximum dry densities and 
optimum moisture contents were established in general accordance with ASTM D1557 
(Modified Proctor), and the test results are summarized on Plate 1. 
Periodic in-situ moisture and density tests were conducted in general accordance 
with ASTM D6938 (Nuclear Gauge Method) to determine the adequacy of compaction of 
fills and backfills for infrastructure and utility installations, including compaction of building 
footings, garage, and driveway.  
The test results are summarized on Plate 2. These tests were performed on 
moisture-conditioned and compacted lifts of fills and backfills.  Test locations and 
elevations were estimated using on-site survey stakes for reference. These locations and 
elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods 
used. Areas deemed unsatisfactory were reworked until the measured relative 
compaction conformed to or exceeded the minimum specified for the project. 
This report summarizes our field observations and compaction testing 
services during the site grading work for the period covering January 12 through 
September 1, 2022. 
Based on our field representative’s observations and the referenced test results, it 
is our opinion that the site grading for the Residence at 309 Houston Street project, 
observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report, were performed in 
accordance with the general intent of the recommendations in the following references. 
1. 
Standard Specifications for Public Works Construction, County of Maui, 
September 1986. 
2. 
Project Plans entitled “Residence at 309 Houston Street,” dated July 15, 2021. 
To the best of our knowledge, it is our professional opinion that the grading work 
observed by Geolabs has been constructed in general conformance with the approved 
plans and Standard Specifications for Public Works Construction, County of Maui.
