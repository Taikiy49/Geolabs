INTRODUCTION
This report presents the findings of our injection well performance evaluation for the
Kuilima Resort Wastewater Treatment Plant in Kahuku on the Island of Oahu Hawaii
The findings and recommendations presented herein are based on site observations
recorded during a site visit the performance of short term injection testing of the wells and
personal communication with operators and managers of the treatment plant facility The
approximate location of the treatment plant facility and the injection well field are shown on
the Project Location Map Plate 1
Our work was performed in general accordance with our proposal dated
and the State of Hawaii Department of Health Underground Injection
Control UIC Injection Well Status Report Guidelines Version 1 dated June 1992
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Kuilima Resort Wastewater Treatment Plant injection wells are utilized for the
disposal of secondary treated effluent generated within the Kuilima Resort development
The majority of the effluent treated at the plant was used for irrigation of the resort golf
course within the Kuilima Resort complex Therefore the wells were used for the injection
of treated effluent only on a stand by basis during periods of time when the effluent can
not be utilized for irrigation purposes generally due to wet weather conditions
During our current injection well inspection the operator of the Kuilima Resort
Wastewater Treatment Plant informed us that the operational status of the three injection
wells has been modified The secondary treated effluent generated by the wastewater

DISCUSSIONS AND FINDINGS
The wells are located on the easterly side of the tee boxes for the 10th Hole of Golf
Course G 2 The wells are situated in an open grass area between the tees and the moat
around Punahoolapa Marsh The topography of the well field area is generally level with
some topographic mounds and depressions associated with the adjacent golf course
terrain Based on a topographic map of the well field and vicinity ground surface
elevations range from about 5 to 6 feet Mean Sea Level MSL At the time of our site
visit on the weather conditions were partly cloudy with frequent passing
trade wind showers During our site visit the ocean tide was noted as being generally low
in height Based on the tidal information for Honolulu Harbor a low tide of about 0 2 feet
occurred at about 0900 hours which subsequently rose to a high tide of about 2 4 feet at
about 1700 hours No evidence of surface water ponding was visible in the vicinity of the
well field or well vaults upon our arrival at the site
The well heads are enclosed in below ground sump vaults of approximately 4 feet
by 4 feet in plan dimension and about 5 feet in depth The vaults are covered with hinged
steel plate doors and are approximately flush with the adjacent grass covered ground
surface
Inside the vaults the well heads consist of steel tees with flange plates bolted to
their tops for security and to allow the build up of additional head if needed These plates
were removed by plant personnel for the well inspection and performance of the injection
tests In 1994 two inch diameter inspection ports were installed in the well covers to
eliminate the need for removal of the entire top plate during future inspection testing
However to allow complete access to the well interior for the performance of the
short term injection tests and visual inspection of the exposed well casing the top plate of
each well was removed

In general only two of the three wells are placed in use for effluent disposal at any
time The inactive well is taken out of operation on a rotating basis so that at any given
time only two wells are in operation Flow to an individual well is controlled by a valve
located adjacent to each well head A main control valve diverts the effluent flow from the
treatment plant to either the well field or the lagoon which serves as storage for golf
course irrigation water when needed

Injection Well Redevelopment
Based on our conversation with the treatment plant supervisory personnel the
injection wells were redeveloped from May 31 2002 through in an effort to
maintain the disposal capacity of the system A 130 cubic foot per minute cfm air
compressor was utilized to blow out the injection wells by lowering the air line into the
bottom of the well The discharge of well water produced by the airlift cleaning was
contained within the well vault and pumped to a tanker truck for proper disposal
The WWTP personnel reported that the well cleaning procedure was effective in
removing some fine sand and silty sediment from the wells Furthermore following the
well cleaning efforts the measured head levels during injection operation were observed

Injection System
To regulate the system control valves are used to isolate individual wells so that
one or a combination of the wells may be used for the injection of effluent At the time of
our site visit the system operation was set up such that Well Nos 1 and 3 were on line as
the active wells for the injection of effluent Well No 2 was isolated from the system to
serve as an emergency back up to the system
The system is equipped with a flow totalizing meter which measures the flow
volume in gallons This meter measures the total flow to the weil field rather than to
individual wells In addition the meter registers the cumulative flow in gallons

Short Term Injection Tests
Short term injection tests were performed on each of the three wells by diverting
treated effluent from the treatment plant into each well separately Each well was isolated
from the others for the duration of each test by adjusting the valves controlling the effluent
flow to individual wells
During injection testing the head level response was continually measured and
recorded at about 1 minute time intervals by manually measuring the observed water level
in the well casing Readings from the totalizing meter were recorded during the short term
injection test to obtain the total quantity of injected effluent and to estimate the average
flow rate into each well The duration of each short term injection test was
approximately 38 to 42 minutes in length and was dependent upon the head level
response to injection
Well No 1 experienced a rapid build up of head that began immediately after the
test was started The head level continued to slowly rise and reached the top of the well
and overflowed approximately 26 minutes into the injection test The average injection
rate during the Well No 1 injection test was calculated to be about 510 gallons per minute
The injection rate was lower than the injection rate used for the previous injection test
year 2001 for this well Based on the results of the injection test Well No 1 appears to
be operating satisfactorily and may have an estimated injection capacity of less than about
500 gallons per minute therefore the injection well capacity appears to have lost some
capacity since the last injection well inspection in June 2001 The injection test results for
Well No 1 are presented in Appendix B

Recommendations
Based on the injection test results and the well usage data we believe the injection
wells have maintained their disposal capacity since the last inspection which occurred in
June 2001 The maintenance of the injection capacity is believed to be a result of the
recent well cleaning performed on the wells and the continued rotation of the injection well
usage
It is a known fact that injection wells sustain losses in capacity over time due to
natural biological and chemical fouling processes or other introduced factors such as
suspended solids or debris contained in the effluent In addition extended periods of well
inactivity may cause acceleration of the normal losses in capacity
It should be noted that some loss in capacity may be permanent and irreversible
however it has also been proven that redevelopment of injection wells can aid in the
recovery of lost capacity provided that the proper methods are utilized on a regular basis
Based on our injection well evaluation we recommend that the owner operator
continue to operate the injection wells on a routine rotating basis in an attempt to maintain
the disposal capacity of the system In addition we recommend that the owner operator

closely monitor and document the operation of each of the wells to assure that overflows
or back ups related to a well failure are detected prior to their occurrence In the event an
overflow of a well or other failure occurs and the situation cannot be remedied the
owner operator should promptly notify the Department of Health Underground Injection
Control Branch
We believe that by increasing the use of the wells on a rotating basis especially
during periods of low effluent availability the current operational status of the wells should
be maintained Keeping the wells operational is not normally a problem however we
believe it is crucial to maintain rotating operation of all the wells during the periods of time
when low effluent flow are experienced
We recommend that the owner operator continue recording field observations such
as the following 1 notes regarding the overall system operation 2 documentation of well
maintenance performed including well cleaning and replacement of faulty parts and
instruments 3 observation of the head leveis within the wells prior to and during injection
events 4 daily flow volumes 5 a log of individual well usage and 6 notes pertaining to
the increase or decrease of the total flow to the plant for treatment This information will be
used to evaluate the future performance of the injection wells In addition the results of
our short term injection testing of the wells may be used to evaluate the operation of the
wells with respect to prior well inspection data
Generally redevelopment should be considered if head levels continue to increase
over time or when well failures such as overflow or system back ups may be anticipated
However when the wells begin to fail it often times is too late to restore the capacity
Therefore based on the effectiveness of the recent well redevelopment we believe it may
be prudent to continue scheduling well redevelopment at annual intervals
