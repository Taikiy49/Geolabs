REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The Island of Oahu is composed largely of the weathered remnants of two extinct
shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae forms the bulk of the
westem third of the island while the younger shield Koolau forms the majority of the eastern
two thirds of the island It is believed that Waianae became extinct while Koolau was still
active and its eastern flank is partially buried below Koolau lavas in Central Oahu
During the Pleistocene Epoch sea levels fluctuated in response to the cycles of
continental glaciation As the glaciers grew and advanced less water was available to fill the
oceanic basins such that the sea levels fell below the present stands of the sea When the
glaciers melted and receded an excess of water became available such that the sea levels
rose to above the present sea
The project site is located on the southern flank of the Koolau Shield near Kalihi
Stream Therefore the subsurface conditions at the project site are composed of terrigenous
alluvial soils associated with Kalihi Stream deposited by continuous erosion of the volcanic
dome The alluvial soils generally consist of an assortment of cobbles and boulders as well
as sands silts and adobe clays The base of the stratigraphic section in this area is Koolau
Basalt Land development and placement of man made fills in this century have brought the
project site to its present form

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
As previously indicated our field exploration generally encountered a surface fill layer
of approximately 4 5 to 5 5 feet thick consisting of medium stiff to stiff silty clays with some
sands and gravel The surface fill layer was generally underlain by cobbles and boulders
overlying a medium dense to dense silty sand alluvial soil deposit extending to the maximum
depth explored of about 20 2 feet below the existing ground surface Groundwater was
encountered in the borings at depths of about 9 6 to 10 feet below the ground surface at the
time of our exploration
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered at the project site we believe that
a shallow foundation system consisting of spread and or continuous footings may be used
for support of the new warehouse and silo structures planned at the site However it should
be noted that the near surface soils encountered in our borings generally consisted of silty
clays The near surface silty clays encountered may be considered to exhibit a slight
expansion potential when subjected to moisture fluctuations Therefore to reduce the
potential for structural distress to the concrete slabs we recommend that a minimum 12 inch
thick non expansive select granular fill material be provided below the concrete slabs on
grade In addition the clayey subgrade soils below the non expansive select granular fill
material should be properly prepared in accordance with the recommendations presented
in the Site Preparation section of this report Detailed discussion of these items and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations for design are presented in the following
sections of this report
Foundations
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered at the project site we believe that
shallow foundations consisting of spread and or continuous footings may be used for support
of the new warehouse and silo structures planned at the site An allowable bearing pressure
of up to 2 500 pounds per square foot psf may be used in the design of foundations bearing
on the re compacted on site fill materials and or new compacted fills This bearing value is
for dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third 1 3 for transient loads such as
those caused by wind or seismic forces
GEOLABS INC
W O Page 7
In general footings for the proposed structures should be embedded a minimum of
24 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade Where foundations are located next to
utility trenches or easements they should be embedded below an imaginary 45 degree plane
extending upward from the bottom edge of the utility trench or extended to as deep as the
inverts of the utility lines This requirement is necessary to avoid surcharging adjacent below
grade structures with additional structural loads and to reduce the potential for appreciable
foundation settlement
The bottom of footing excavations should be compacted to a minimum of 90 percent
relative compaction prior to the placement of reinforcing steel If soft and or loose materials
are encountered at the bottom of footing excavations the soft and or loose materials should
be removed to expose the underlying firm or dense materials The over excavation should
be backfilled with approved on site fill materials compacted to a minimum of 90 percent
relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed
as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with
ASTM D 1557 91 test procedures Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by
dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
Due to the expansive nature slightly expansive of the near surface soils foundation
subgrades should not be allowed to dry before placing concrete If shrinkage cracks appear
in the foundation excavations the soil should be thoroughly moistened to close the cracks
and re compacted to provide a firm base prior to concrete placement
If foundations are designed and constructed in accordance with our
recommendations we estimate the total settlement of footings to be less than 1 inch
Differential settlements between adjacent footings supported on the recompacted soils
should be on the order of about 0 5 inches
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by frictional resistance
developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil and by passive earth
pressure acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system A coefficient of
GEOLABS INC
W O 4532 00 Page 8
friction of 0 85 may be used for footings bearing on the re compacted on site materials
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid
pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth pcf This assumes that the soils
around the footings are well compacted Unless covered by pavements or slabs the passive
pressure resistance in the upper 12 inches of soil should be neglected
Footing excavations should be observed by a representative from Geolabs prior to
placement of reinforcing steel or concrete to confirm the competency of the bearing materials
and embedment depths Footing subgrades should be firm unyielding and free of
appreciable shrinkage cracks If unsuitable materials are encountered at the bottom of the
footing excavations the unsuitable materials should be over excavated to expose firm soils
and replaced with well compacted fill
Slabs On Grade
We anticipate that concrete slabs on grade will be used for the new warehouse
structure The near surface soils encountered in our borings generally consist of silty clays
which exhibit a slight expansion potential when subjected to moisture fluctuations Therefore
we recommend that a minimum of 12 inches of non expansive select granular fill material be
provided below the concrete slab to reduce the potential for structural distress to the lightly
loaded slabs The select granular fill should extend a minimum of 2 feet laterally beyond the
edges of the slab
The clayey subgrades below the select granular fill should be scarified to a depth of
at least 8 inches moisture conditioned to about 2 percent above the optimum moisture and
compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to
the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the
same soil determined in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91 Optimum
moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry
density
GEOLABS INC
W O Page 9
The non expansive select granular fill recommended below the floor slabs should
consist of imported granular material such as crushed coral basalt gravel or cinder sand
The material should be well graded from coarse to fine with no particles larger than 3 inches
in largest dimension It should also contain between 10 and 30 percent particles passing the
No 200 sieve The material should have a laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR value
of 20 or higher and a swell potential of 1 percent or less when tested in accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1883 The non expansive select granular fill materials should be
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content placed in 8 inch level loose
lifts and compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction The clayey subgrades should
be kept moist until covered by the select granular fill
To provide more uniform support for the slabs we recommend that the concrete
slabs on grade be supported on a minimum 4 inch thick cushion fill placed over the
12 inches of non expansive select granular fill The cushion fill layer should consist of
open graded gravel such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation which would
also serve as a capillary moisture break
For slabs on grade with moisture sensitive surfacing we recommend that an
impermeable membrane be placed over the cushion fill to reduce the potential for migration
of moisture vapor through the concrete slab Placement of 2 inches of moist fine sand above
the plastic membrane may be considered to provide for protection of the membrane and to
aid in curing of the slab concrete Provision of the 2 inches of fine sand above the vapor
barrier will be left up to the discretion of the project structural engineer

Site Preparation
Based on the existing topography we anticipate that the proposed site grading may
consist of minor cuts and fills of about 3 feet or less relative to the existing ground surface
Due to the slightly expansive nature of the on site soils grading operations for the project
should be observed by a representative from Geolabs It is important that a representative
from our office observe whether undesirable materials are encountered during the excavation
and scarification process and whether the exposed soil conditions are similar to those
encountered in our exploration The following recommendations for site preparation should
be used as a guide for the earthwork requirements for the project
At the on set of earthwork areas within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed Vegetation debris rubbish concrete slabs asphaltic
concrete pavements and other unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of
properly off site or in a designated area to reduce the potential for contamination of the
excavated materials Existing utilities that are to be abandoned should be removed The
resulting excavation should be properly backfilled with general fill materials moisture
GEOLABS INC
W O 4532 00 Page 12
conditioned to above the optimum moisture content placed in 6 to 8 inch level loose lifts and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
Soft and yielding areas encountered during clearing should be over excavated to
expose firm natural materials and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with
compacted fill In areas to receive fill and finished subgrades in cut areas the subgrade
should be scarified to a minimum depth of 8 inches moisture conditioned to about 2 percent
above the optinum moisture content and compacted to at least 90 percent relative
compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a
percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91 Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by
weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
Where shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of the subgrade we recommend
that the subgrade soil be re scarified moisture conditioned and recompacted as
recommended above Subgrade soils should be kept moist until covered by subsequent
layers of fill materials Saturation and subsequent yielding of the exposed subgrade due to
inclement weather and poor drainage may require over excavation of the soft areas and
replacement with compacted fill at no additional cost to the owner
In general we believe that the excavated on site soils may be re used as a source of
general fill material at the site provided that the materials are free of vegetation deleterious
materials and clay lumps and rock fragments greater than 3 inches in maximum dimension
However the on site soils should not be used as the select granular fill material
recommended below the slabs
Where imported material is required as fill the material should consist of
non expansive select granular material such as crushed coral basalt gravel or cinder sand
Imported materials should be well graded from coarse to fine with no particles larger than
3 inches in largest dimension and should contain between 10 and 30 percent particles
passing the No 200 sieve The imported fill material should have a laboratory CBR value
GEOLABS INC
W O 4532 00 Page 13
of 20 or more and should have a maximum swell of 1 percent or less when tested in
accordance with ASTM D 1883 Imported materials should be tested for conformance with
these recommendations prior to delivery to the project site for the intended use
General fills should be moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum
moisture content placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and
compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction The compaction requirement for the
last lift of fills placed under the pavements should be increased to a minimum of 95 percent
relative compaction Water tamping jetting or ponding should not be allowed to compact
the fills due to the nature of the on site soils
We believe that permanent cut slopes exposing the on site materials may be
designed with a slope inclination of two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V or flatter
Permanent fill slopes constructed using the excavated on site soils or imported materials may
be designed with a slope inclination of 2H 1V or flatter Fills placed on slopes steeper than

against sliding

We anticipate that rigid pavements will be used at the loading dock location for this
project We have made our preliminary pavement design assuming the pavement subgrade
soil will generally consist of the medium stiff to stiff silty clay fills encountered in our borings
We envision that the loading dock areas will generally be subjected to container trucks
passenger vehicles and light trucks Therefore we recommend that the following rigid
pavement section be used for the new loading dock area

6 0 Inch Portland Cement Concrete
6 0 Inch Aggregate Subbase 95 Percent Relative Compaction
12 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness on a Moist Compacted Subgrade

The pavement subgrades should be scarified to a depth of at least 8 inches moisture
conditioned to about 2 percent above optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of
90 percent relative compaction The aggregate subbase materials should consist of crushed
basalt aggregate compacted to at least 95 percent relative compaction CBR and field
density tests should be performed on the actual subgrade soils encountered during
construction to confirm the adequacy of the above sections
Paved areas should be sloped and drainage gradients should be maintained to carry

to the pavement areas we recommend that the curbs extend a minimum of 2 inches below
the aggregate subbase to reduce the potential for migration of excessive landscape water
into the pavement section
Utility Trenches
New utility lines and utility connections will likely be needed for the proposed
warehouse project In general granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel
ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation is recommended under the pipes Free draining granular
materials such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation should also be used
for the initial trench backfill up to about 12 inches above the pipes to provide adequate
support around the pipes It is critical to use this free draining material to reduce the potential
for formation of voids below the haunches of pipes and to provide adequate support for the
sides of the pipes Improper trench backfill could result in settlement of the backfill and
damage to the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipes to the
top of the subgrade or finished grade may consist of the excavated on site soils with a
maximum particle size of 6 inches The backfill materials should be moisture conditioned to
above the optimum moisture content placed in about 8 inch loose lifts and mechanically
compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for
appreciable future ground subsidence Where trenches are located below pavement areas
GEOLABS INC
W O 4532 00 Page 15
the upper 3 feet of the trench backfill below the pavement grade should be compacted to not
less than 95 percent relative compaction
Drainage
The finished grades outside the new structures should be sloped to shed water away
from the foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also advised that gutter
systems be installed around the warehouse and that the discharge be diverted away from
the foundations and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near the foundations
and pavement areas should also be avoided Planters next to foundations should be
avoided or have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce the potential for significant water
infiltration into the subsurface
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the above
foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation and
subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations should be properly
backfilled against the walls or slab edges immediately after setting of the concrete to reduce
the potential for significant water infiltration In addition drainage swales should be provided
as soon as possible and should be maintained to drain surface water runoff away from the
foundations and pavement areas

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by drilling and sampling
four borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 4 extending to depths of about 15 to
20 2 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate locations of the borings drilled
are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were drilled using a truck mounted drill rig
equipped with continuous flight solid stem augers
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our field engineer who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by testing
in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representations of the materials
encountered are presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 4
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained in general accordance with ASTM
Test Designation D 3550 84 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 3 inch OD
Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches In addition some
samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a
2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same hammer and drop The blow counts
needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown as
the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Pocket penetrometer tests were performed on selected cohesive soil samples
retrieved in the field The pocket penetrometer test provides an indication of the unconfined
compressive strength of the soil sample Results of the pocket penetrometer tests are
presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content ASTM D 2216 and unit weight ASTM D 2937 determinations were
performed on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil
properties The results of these tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the
appropriate sample depths
Two one inch ring swell tests were performed on relatively undisturbed samples to
evaluate the swelling potential of the near surface soils The results of the tests are
summarized on Plate B 1
One Atterberg Limits test ASTM D 4318 was performed on a selected sample of the
soils to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits and to aid in soil classification The results of the
test are summarized on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depth Graphic
presentation of the test results is provided on Plate B 2
One laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR test ASTM D 1883 was performed
on a bulk sample of the near surface soils to evaluate the pavement support characteristics
of the soils Results of the test are presented on Plate B 3
