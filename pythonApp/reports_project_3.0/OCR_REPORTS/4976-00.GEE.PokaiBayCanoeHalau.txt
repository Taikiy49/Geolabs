In accordance with our proposal dated we have performed a
geotechnical engineering exploration for the proposed Pokai Bay Canoe Halau project in
the Waianae area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii This letter report summarizes the
findings from our field exploration at the project site and presents our findings and
recommendations for the proposed project These findings and recommendations are
intended for design input only and are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this
letter report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located at Pokai Bay Beach Park in the Waianae area on the
Island of Oahu Hawaii Based on the information provided we understand that it is
proposed to construct a new canoe halau on the northern side of Pokai Bay Beach Park
The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map
Plate 1
The project involves the construction of a 50 foot by 50 foot structure We
understand the building will have a concrete slab on grade and masonry and concrete
columns supporting a heavy timber gable roof structure Structural details were not
available at the time this letter report was prepared

an exploration program consisting of the following tasks and work efforts

FIELD EXPLORATION
Our field exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling two borings
designated as Boring Nos 1 and 2 extending to depths of about 20 9 and 21 5 feet below
the existing ground surface The borings were drilled using a truck mounted drill rig
equipped with continuous flight solid stem augers The approximate locations of the
borings drilled at the site are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on the Boring Log Legend Plate 3 Graphic
representations of the materials encountered are provided on the Logs of Borings
Plates 4 and 5
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained from the borings drilled in
general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of
Soils by driving a 3 inch OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling
30 inches In addition some samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel
Sampling of Soils by driving a 2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same
hammer and drop The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third
6 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of
Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Pocket penetrometer tests were performed on selected cohesive soil samples in
the field The pocket penetrometer test provides an indication of the unconfined
compressive strength of the sample Results of the pocket penetrometer tests are
summarized on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
LABORATORY TESTING
Selected soil samples from the field exploration were tested in the laboratory with
an emphasis on evaluating the moisture and density Moisture content ASTM D 2216
and unit weight ASTM D 2937 determinations were performed on selected samples as
an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties The test results are presented
on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our field exploration at the project site generally encountered fill material extending
to depths of about 5 5 feet below the existing ground surface The fill material consisted of
medium dense gravel and sand Underlying the fill material was medium dense coralline
sand with gravel extending to depths of 9 5 to 10 feet below the ground surface Following
the sand layer a layer of hard coral formation was encountered extending to the depth of
15 to 17 feet below the ground surface The coral formation layer was underlain by very
dense to hard cemented coralline sand and gravel extending to the maximum depths of
explored
The water table was encountered at a depth of approximately 8 feet below the
existing ground surface at the time of our field exploration However it should be noted
that ground water levels are subject to change due to rainfall seasonal precipitation
surface water runoff and other factors
FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Detailed discussion of our geotechnical engineering recommendations for design of
pavements retaining walls and site grading are presented in the following sections of this
letter report
Shallow Foundations
Based on the generally stiff subsoil conditions encountered at the site we believe
that shallow spread footing may be used for support of the proposed new construction An
allowable bearing pressure of up to 3 000 pounds per square foot psf may be used for
the design of shallow foundations bearing on the in situ soils This bearing value is for
those caused by wind or seismic forces
Prior to placement of the structural fill material soft and or loose materials
encountered at the bottom of the footing excavations should be over excavated to expose
the underlying firm materials The resulting over excavations should be backfilled with
select granular materials compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
Structural fill required below the footing subgrade should consist of well graded
select granular fill material with a maximum particle size of 3 inches or less Structural fill
materials should meet the requirements for imported fill materials presented in the Site
Grading section of this report Structural fills should be moisture conditioned to above the
optimum moisture content placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness
and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Relative compaction
refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry
density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 test procedures

Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the
maximum dry density
We anticipate that the structure may be subjected to uplift forces created by wind
conditions Uplift loads may be resisted by a combination of the following 1 dead weight
of the structure 2 weight of backfill material above the footing where applicable and
3 side shear between the sides of the footings and surrounding soils Contribution of
dead weight from the backfill may be estimated using a unit weight of 110 pounds per
cubic foot pcf For side shear between the sides of the footings and the adjacent soils a
shear value of 150 psf may be used
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by frictional resistance
developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil and by passive earth
pressure acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system A coefficient of
friction of 0 35 may be used for footings bearing on the select granular fill material
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid
pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth pcf This assumes that the soil
around footings is well compacted The passive pressure should be reduced for
foundations located on slopes Unless covered by pavements or slabs the passive
resistance in the upper 12 inches of soil should be neglected
In general footings should be embedded a minimum of 24 inches below the lowest
adjacent finish grades Footings constructed near tops of slopes or on sloping ground
should be embedded deep enough to provide a minimum horizontal set back distance of
6 feet measured from the outside edge of the footings to the slope face Footings located
adjacent to retaining walls should be embedded deep enough to avoid surcharging the
retaining wall foundations or the retaining walls should be designed to resist the additional
structural loads
Foundations located next to utility trenches or easements should be embedded
below a 45 degree imaginary plane extending upward from the bottom edge of the utility
trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility lines This requirement is necessary to avoid
surcharging adjacent below grade structures with additional structural loads and to reduce
the potential for foundation settlement
We estimate that footing settlements under the anticipated design loads for footings
bearing on the compacted select granular fill material over the in situ soils as
recommended herein to be less than 1 inch total with differential settlements on the order
of 0 5 inches
We recommend that footing excavations be observed by a representative from
Geolabs prior to placement of reinforcing steel and concrete to confirm the foundation
bearing conditions and the required embedment depths

Slabs On Grade
Our field exploration disclosed that the project site is generally blanketed by
near surface medium dense sand and gravel with low swelling potential Conventional
slab on grade design may be utilized above the properly prepared subgrade soils
We recommend the area adjacent to the slabs be backfilled tightly against the slab
and graded to divert water away from the slab to reduce the potential for water ponding
around the foundation
We recommend that a minimum 4 inch thick layer of cushion fill be placed below
the slab The cushion fill consisting of No 3 Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation
or Basaltic Termite Barrier BTB should provide more uniform support of the slabs and
should serve as a capillary break
For the design of structural slabs a modulus of subgrade reaction of 100 pounds
per square inch per inch of deflection pci may be used for the compacted subgrade
material A minimum slab thickness of 4 0 inches may be used for preliminary design
purposes The thickened edges of slabs adjacent to any unpaved area should be
embedded at least 12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade
Subgrade soils should be prepared in accordance with the recommendations
presented in the Site Grading section of this report Compaction requirements for the
subgrade soils and select granular fill are also discussed in the same section of this report
Site Grading
A grading plan was not available at the time this report was prepared However
based on the information provided we envision that minor cut and fill on the order of 2 feet
or less will be involved to achieve the design finished grades for the project In general
areas within the contract grading limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed at the
on set of earthwork Vegetation debris deleterious material and other unsuitable
materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site
Soft loose and yielding areas encountered during clearing below areas designated
to receive fill or future improvements should be over excavated to expose firm or dense
material and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with well compacted fill The
excavated soft loose and or organic soils should be properly disposed of off site
The areas to receive fill and finished subgrades should be scarified to about
8 inches moisture conditioned to 2 percent above the optimum moisture and compacted
to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the
in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the
same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 test procedures Optimum

Where shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of the subgrade we

prepared again as recommended above Saturation and subsequent yielding of the
exposed subgrade due to inclement weather and poor drainage may require
over excavation of the soft and or loose areas and replacement with well compacted fill
In general the excavated on site soils may be re used as a source of general fill or
backfill materials provided that they are free of deleterious materials vegetation and are
screened of particles greater than 3 inches in largest dimension Imported general fill
materials needed for the site filling should consist of materials with a low expansion
potential Imported general fill materials should consist of materials with particle sizes of
3 inches or less in maximum dimension The material should have a laboratory California
Bearing Ratio CBR greater than 12 and a swell potential of less than 1 percent when
tested in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1883
Select borrow subbase and aggregate base course materials should meet the
material requirements in Sections 30 and 31 respectively as specified in the Standard
Specifications for Public Works Construction 1986 Imported fill materials should be
tested by Geolabs for conformance with these recommendations prior to delivery to the
project site for the intended use
General fills and backfills should be moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent
above the optimum moisture content placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose
thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction In general
compaction should be accomplished by sheepsfoot rollers vibratory rollers or other
similar types of acceptable compaction equipment
A representative from Geolabs should observe the site grading and earthwork

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that Geolabs be retained to provide geotechnical engineering
services during construction of the proposed project The items of construction monitoring
that are critical requiring Special Inspection include observation of subgrade preparation
foundation construction and fill and backfill placement
Other aspects of earthwork construction should also be observed by a
representative from Geolabs This is to observe compliance with the intent of the design
concepts specifications or recommendations and to expedite suggestions for design
changes that may be required in the event that subsurface conditions differ from those
anticipated at the time this report was prepared The recommendations provided in this
report are contingent upon such observations If the actual exposed subsurface conditions
encountered during construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The findings and discussions submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from the field borings Variations of subsoil conditions between and
beyond the borings may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not
become evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be
necessary to re evaluate the findings and discussions provided in this letter report
The locations of the field borings indicated in this letter report are approximate
having been estimated by taping and pacing from the features shown on the Site Plan
transmitted by Pacific Architects Inc on The approximate elevations of
field borings were estimated from the contour lines shown on the same plan The physical
locations and elevations of the borings should be considered accurate only to the degree
implied by the methods used
The stratification lines shown on graphic representations of the borings depict the
approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual transition
As previously mentioned groundwater was encountered in our borings at the time of our
exploration However it must be noted that groundwater level changes may occur due to
tidal fluctuations seasonal precipitation surface runoff and other factors
