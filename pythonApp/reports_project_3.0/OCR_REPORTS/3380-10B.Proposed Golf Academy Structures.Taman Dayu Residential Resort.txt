Geotechnical Engineering Recommendations
Proposed Golf Academy Structures
Taman Dayu Residential Resort
Pandaan Java Indonesia
As requested we are providing the following geotechnical engineering
recommendations for the design of buildings for the proposed Golf Academy within the
Taman Dayu Residential Resort Project in the Pandaan area on the island of Java
Indonesia The recommendations provided herein are based only on a review of the
boring logs drilled in the vicinity by Testana Engineering Inc
Project Considerations
The proposed Golf Academy project will entail construction of a cluster of three
one story structures The proposed Golf Academy structures will be located at the
proposed driving range location of the project The finish floor elevation of the proposed
Golf Academy was not available at the time this letter report was prepared However we
understand that the finish floor elevation of the Golf Academy structures will be near the
existing grades at the site Therefore we anticipate only minor grading consisting of cuts
and fills of less than 1 meter relative to the existing ground surface to provide a level
building pad for the proposed construction

Foundations
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered in the three test borings B 45
B 65 and B 67 we recommend that the weak and compressible near surface soils
encountered in Boring Nos B 45 B 65 and B 67 be removed to expose firm materials
and be replaced with compacted engineered fill The depths of removal is anticipated to
be on the order of 1 5 to 2 5 meters based on the three borings The actual depth of
weak soil removal will need to be determined in the field based on the exposed soil
conditions The lateral extent of removal of the weak and compressible near surface soils
should extend a minimum of 1 5 meters beyond the limits of the proposed building After
removal of the soft and compressible soils followed by replacement with compacted
engineered fill the proposed Golf Academy structures may be supported on a shallow
foundation system bearing on the compacted engineered fill materials Engineered fill
placement is further discussed in the Site Grading section of this letter report
An allowable bearing pressure of up to 10 tons per square meter tons m may be
used for the design of footings bearing directly on compacted engineered fill This
allowable bearing pressure is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third
1 3 for transient loads such as temporary wind and or seismic forces The allowable
bearing pressure provided is a net value therefore the weight of the foundations may be
discounted when sizing the footings
Footings should be embedded a minimum of 750 mm below the lowest adjacent
finished grade Where footings are to be located adjacent to below grade structures or
utilities the footings should extend to a depth below an imaginary 45 degree plane

projected upward from the bottom of the below grade structure or utility This requirement
is necessary to avoid surcharging adjacent below grade structures with additional
structural loads and to reduce the potential for foundation settlement We estimate that
the total footing settlements under the anticipated design loads to be on the order of
25 mm with differential settlements of less than 15 mm
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by frictional resistance
between the base of the footing and the bearing materials and by passive earth pressure
developed against the near vertical faces of the embedded portion of footings A
coefficient of friction of 0 30 may be used between the base of the footings and the
supporting materials Resistance due to passive pressure may be calculated using an
equivalent fluid pressure of 4 5 tons per cubic meter tons m for footings embedded in
the compacted engineered fill These values assume that the concrete for footings is
placed directly against the footing excavations or the soil around the footing is well
compacted Unless covered by pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the upper
300 mm below finished grade should be neglected
Uplift loads may be resisted by a combination of the following 1 dead weight of
the structure and 2 weight of backfill material above the footing where applicable
Contribution to uplift resistance due to the weight of the backfill above the footing may be
estimated using a unit weight of 1 7 tons m
The bottom of the footing excavation should be recompacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative compaction prior to placement of reinforcing steel and concrete In
addition footing excavations should not be allowed to dry before placing concrete If
shrinkage cracks appear in the footing excavations the soil should be thoroughly
moistened to close all cracks and recompacted to provide a firm base prior to concrete
placement
Footing excavations should be observed by a representative of Geolabs Hawaii
prior to the placement of reinforcing steel or concrete to confirm the competency of the
bearing materials and embedment depths If unsuitable materials are encountered in the
footing excavations these materials should be over excavated to firm soils and replaced
with compacted engineered fill

Academy structures Since we anticipate using the on site clayey soils for engineered fill

materialsmaterials we recommend that provisions be included in the bid documents to
provide a 300 mm thick layer of non expansive select granular fill below all slabs on
grades as a contingent item Additional laboratory testing such as Atterberg Limits and
California Bearing Ratios CBR should be conducted during grading operations to
evaluate if the recommended thickness of non expansive select granular fill material is

We understand that the standard practice for the section directly below slabs on
grade generally consist of 75 mm of lean concrete overlying a 50 mm sand bedding As
an alternative to the lean concrete over a sand bedding interior slabs on grade not
subjected to vehicular traffic may be supported on a 100 mm thick layer of cushion fill
The cushion fill would provide more uniform support of the slab and would also serve as a
capillary moisture break
The cushion fill should consist of a clean free draining gravel such as
ASTM C 33 No 57 or No 67 gradation The cushion fill should be lightly compacted with
a vibratory plate or similar equipment For slabs on grade with moisture sensitive
surfacing we recommend that an impermeable membrane be placed over the cushion fill
to reduce potential migration of moisture vapor through the concrete slab In order to
promote more uniform curing of the slab concrete and to provide protection of the vapor
membrane we recommend that a 50 mm layer of moist fine sand be placed above the
membrane prior to placing the slab concrete The sand should be moistened slightly prior
to placing concrete
Where slabs will be subjected to vehicular traffic a 150 mm layer of aggregate
base course compacted to 95 percent relative compaction is recommended for support of
the slab in lieu of the lean concrete over sand bedding or sand impermeable membrane
over cushion fill Provisions should be made for proper load transfer across the slab
joints which are subjected to vehicular traffic The thickened edges of slabs adjacent to
any unpaved area should be embedded at least 300 mm below the lowest adjacent
grade
Site Grading
As previously mentioned because of the anticipated weak and compressible
the soft to medium stiff near surface soils be removed to expose firm materials and be
replaced with compacted engineered fill The following guidelines are provided for the
anticipated mass grading operations

At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable
materials should be removed and disposed of off site or at a designated area After
clearing and grubbing the soft to medium stiff near surface soils should be
over excavated to expose firm soils The anticipated depths of the over excavation is
estimated to be about 1 5 to 2 5 meters below the existing ground surface The lateral
extent of the over excavation should extend to a minimum of 1 5 meters beyond the limits
of the building structures
At the over excavated level the exposed subgrade should be scarified to a
minimum depth of 200 mm properly moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Relative
compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the
maximum dry density of the same soil determined in accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 1557 91 Modified Proctor Optimum moisture is the water content
percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
Any soft weak or yielding areas disclosed should be over excavated to expose
firm ground The resulting excavation should be backfilled with engineered fill compacted
to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction with moisture contents above the
optimum moisture The excavated soft and or organic soils should be properly disposed
of off site or at a designated area
Based on laboratory tests performed on the on site soils the on site silts and
clays including the over excavated soils within the building footprint may be used as a
source of engineered fill materials provided that the on site soils are moisture conditioned
to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture content placed in level lifts of
approximately 150 to 200 mm in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of
90 percent relative compaction The compaction requirement for subgrades of
foundations slabs on grades pavements and other improvements should be increased
to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction for a depth of 200 mm below the
subgrade level It should be noted that the near surface on site soils have high moisture
contents and would likely pose some difficulties in achieving the required compaction
without some aeration of the soils before placement and compaction
However the on site silty and clayey soils should not be used as a source of the
non expansive select granular fill material recommended below slabs on grades As a
minimum the non expansive select granular fill materials recommended below

slabs on grades should have a CBR value of at least 20 with a swell value of less than
1 percent when tested in accordance with ASTM D 1883 In addition the material should

The filling operations should start at the lowest point and continue up in level
horizontal compacted layers in accordance with the above recommendations for fill
placement Where the existing ground is steeper than five horizontal to one vertical
5H 1V the fill placement should be keyed and benched into the native soils to properly
bond the fills to the native ground as the fill is brought up in layers Fill slopes should be
constructed by over filling beyond the design slope limits and cutting back to the design
slope to provide adequate compaction at the edge of the slope Fill slopes may be
designed with a slope inclination of 2H 1V Cut slopes may also be designed with a slope
ratio of 2H 1V
Surface water should be directed away from the slopes and drainage over the top
of any slope should be avoided Earth berms or ditches should be provided at the top
of slopes to reduce the potential for drainage over the top of the slope In addition the
finish grades outside the buildings should be sloped to shed water away from foundations
and to reduce the potential for ponding Excessive landscape watering near the
foundations should also be avoided Planters next to foundations should be avoided or
have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the subsoils
Where shrinkage cracks are noted at the subgrade level after compaction we
recommend the subgrade be scarified to a depth of about 200 mm moisture conditioned
to above the optimum moisture content and recompacted to specified requirement prior
to placement of additional fills or construction of improvements If the shrinkage cracks
are minor the soil may be thoroughly moistened to close all cracks prior to construction of
improvements Saturation and subsequent yielding of the exposed soil subgrade due to
inclement weather and poor drainage may require over excavation of the soft areas to
expose firm ground and replacement with compacted fill
