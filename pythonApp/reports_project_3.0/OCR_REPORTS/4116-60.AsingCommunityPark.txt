FINDINGS AND DISCUSSIONS
As previously mentioned a field exploration program was undertaken to obtain
additional information and data for our engineering analyses and evaluation Detailed
discussions of our findings and evaluation are presented below
Potential Undermining of Footing Foundations
Based on the information provided and our site reconnaissance piles of Basaltic
Termite Barrier BTB which were used underneath the building slab and possibly
footings were observed alongside the building walls at various interior and exterior
locations According to the building maintenance personnel these BTB piles have been
removed regularly but reappeared on a continuing basis We speculated that the BTB if
used underneath the wall footings may have been mined out by the ants over the past
years causing potential bearing problem to the building walls and resulting in the observed
distresses at the site In order to further evaluate this hypothesis removal of exterior
sidewalk slab and excavation of shallow test pit were performed at a selected location to
evaluate potential undermining problem at the wall footing bottom
A portion of the existing perimeter sidewalk slabs was removed by our
subcontractor Royal Contracting Company on March 25 2009 to expose the underlying
materials at the location shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 After removal of the concrete
slab and the underlying BTB it was determined that the BTB did not extend and wrap
around the bottom of the building wall footings Therefore we believe that the observed
distresses may not be caused by the settlement of the footing foundations resulting from
the mining out of BTB as speculated However it appears that the BTB underneath the
building slab perimeter could have been mined out resulting in perimeter slab settlement
and causing some of the distresses observed at the site
Potential Settlement of Existing Fills
Based on the project grading plans we understand that up to about 4 feet of fills
were placed to raise the original ground to the design finish pad elevation of the
recreation building Therefore there exists the possibility that the in placed fills might
not have been adequately compacted resulting in potential fill settlement and causing
some of the distresses observed at the recreation building
Based on our review of the daily field reports and compaction test data prepared
during the earthwork construction we understand that basaltic structural fill material
was generally used as the fill materials for the project and were satisfactorily
compacted It appears that S4C was used under the Arts and Crafts room for one layer
of about 6 inches thick i e from 39 to 39 5 feet Mean Sea Level MSL Compaction
Test No 19 indicated that the S4C was compacted to a dry density of 146 pounds per
cubic feet pcf which could be considered very high suggesting that the material has
been adequately compacted Additional compaction tests Nos 29 and 36 on the

In order to further evaluate the quality of the existing fills and underlying in situ
soils a field exploration program consisting of drilling and sampling two borings at
selected locations in proximity to the recreation building was undertaken on
August 4 2009 The approximate boring locations designated as Boring Nos 1 and 2

are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Our field exploration generally encountered stiff dense soils throughout the entire
boring depth explored of up to about 26 5 feet below the existing ground surface In
addition moist or wet soils were not encountered in the shallow subsurface that could
be related to potential water leakage from existing utilities Descriptions and graphic
representations of the materials encountered in the borings are presented on the Logs
of Borings Plates 5 and 6
Based on the field boring exploration program it appears that the existing
in placed fills have been adequately compacted In addition the underlying in situ soils
generally consisted of stiff dense soils with no evidence of loose layers or soft pockets
Furthermore we believe that any fill settlement if indeed exists would have occurred
soon after the project completion However it appears that the building distresses are
still on going and therefore would not have resulted from potential fill settlement
Potential Water Leakage from Existing Utilities
Water leakage from existing utilities could infiltrate through the subsoils and
soften the foundation bearing soils resulting in footing settlement and causing some of
the building distresses observed at the site Therefore the City has undertaken field
exploration and testing work on the existing drain pipes and sprinkler system to evaluate
the potential for water leakage at these existing utilities
A video inspection using a remote controlled camera was conducted by the City
on the existing drain pipes Based on the findings we understand that the existing drain
pipes did not appear to have any obstructions and the drain line is clear of any clogging
A review of the landscape drawings for the Asing Community Park indicates
non potable irrigation service with the main line running across the front of the
recreation building We understand that the main supply line for the courtyard runs
under the park management office Plumbers from the Department of Parks and
Recreation have conducted a leakage test on the non portable irrigation lines and
determined that there are no breaks in the lines Therefore the possibility that the
foundation bearing soils have been softened by infiltrated water from leakage of the
existing utilities would be slim However there still exists the possibility that water

Potential Slope Creep Movement
Based on our field observations it appears that most of the damages to the
existing structure have occurred along the south side of the recreation building In order
to provide additional data for engineering analyses and evaluation independent floor
level surveys were conducted by the City and Geolabs across the interior floor slabs of
the recreation building Results of the floor level surveys from the City and Geolabs are
reproduced and presented on Plates 3 1 and 3 2 of this report for ease of reference
Based on the floor level survey conducted by the City within the Arts and Crafts
room the interior floor elevations along the west side range from 41 55 to 41 62 feet
MSL while the east side range from 41 48 to 41 53 feet MSL with the elevations
consistently becoming lower when moving across from the west to the east side of the
Arts and Crafts room The maximum elevation difference across the Arts and Crafts
room is on the order of about 1 68 inches from 41 62 to 41 48 feet MSL which
appears to well exceed the tolerance of standard industry practice for floor slab
construction Therefore we believe that the Arts and Crafts room is settling toward the
east side of the recreation building
Our review of the grading plan indicated a three horizontal to one vertical 3H 1V
back slope was designed behind the Arts and Crafts room going down to the HECO
manhole Our field observations suggested that the actual slope grade could be
steeper than that indicated on the construction drawings Based on the distresses
observed at the site we speculate that the Arts and Crafts room could be creeping
towards the back slope resulting in floor settlement along the east side as indicated by
the floor level survey causing separation of the exterior sidewalk slabs at gridline D 9
pulling off of the roof truss at gridline C 9 etc However we believe this possibility may
be remote as the back slope is only about 10 feet high and leveled off at the toe area
In addition our drilled boring Boring No 2 indicated that the back slope was
adequately compacted and there is no visible tension cracks observed at the slope face
that is normally present with creeping slope
RECOMMENDATIONS FOR REMEDIAL MEASURES
Based on our findings and evaluation there does not seem to be a definitive
cause of the distresses observed at the site In order to evaluate the possibility of
potential creep movement of the back slope we recommend setting up monitoring
points at selected locations to monitor if the width of the existing cracks will keep
increasing with time
Considerations may be given to the installation of Atlas piers to arrest further
building damage from the potential creep movement of the back slope However the

the observed distresses Therefore we recommend installing the piers in phases if

building damage resulting from the lateral ground movements The crack monitoring

appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations provided
