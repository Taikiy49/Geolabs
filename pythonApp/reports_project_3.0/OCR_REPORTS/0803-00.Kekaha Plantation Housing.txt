INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this investigation was to evaluate the sub
surface conditions at the site to develop soil parameters
for the design of the site grading and house foundations
for the proposed housing development Percolation testing
was also performed to obtain data for cesspool design and
construction
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations

PROPOSED DEVELOPMENT
The proposed housing development includes the 9 parcels
designated on the Site Plan Plate 1 The development
scheme would retain about 95 of the existing houses and
construct about 254 new single family and duplex residences
Light one story wood framed residences are anticipated
Several access roads are planned through the development
Minor grading of the site is anticipated
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located in about
3 miles from Waimea Kauai The proposed project consists
of nine separate sites located throughout the Kekaha Sugar
Mill Camp mauka of Mana Kaumualii Highway as shown on the
enclosed Site Plan Plate 1 Most of the project site is
occupied by wooden residential structures with some open
grassy fields and stable facilities Existing paved and
unpaved roads cross throughout the site
The project area is generally flat ranging in elevation
from 5 to 12 feet above sea level The ground cover in
open areas consists of grasses various trees and exposed
sandy soils

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The geology of the Kekaha area is typical of the shoreline
sand deposits of the southwest cost of Kauai The entire
project site is located on ancient alluvial and marine
derived beach and dune sand deposits
These surficial sand deposits were formed on a relatively
flat coastal plain as relative sea level lowered in this
area Percolating fresh water combined with tidal move
ment of the brackish groundwater caused partial cementation
of the sand at and near present mean sea level
Subsurface Exploration and Percolation Tests
Previous exploration in the Kekaha area and the geologic
reconnaissance of the area indicated that the project site
was underlain by fairly uniform and extensive beach and
dune sand deposits A series of six test pits were dug
at the site to confirm the presence and uniformity of
the underlying sand deposit
The test pits were dug with a backhoe to depths of 4 5
to 8 5 feet below the existing ground surface The
approximate locations of the test pits are shown on the
Site Plan Plate 1

Soil Conditions
The subsurface exploration disclosed essentially uniform
shallow soil conditions at the site The test pits en
countered a surface layer of brown silty fine sand over
tan fine sand to 8 5 feet the maximum depth dug A
thin layer about 2 inches to 1 foot thick of cemented
sand sandstone was encountered just above the water
level in most of the test pits
Groundwater was encountered in all of the test pits at
about 3 5 to 8 foot depths
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration disclosed uniform sandy subsoils
over most of the project site For the proposed light
residential structures the site can be developed by
normal grading operations
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P
A portion of Area 10 was under sugar cane cultivation
at the time of the field exploration It is anticipated
that sandy subsoils should be encountered in this area
below the upper topsoil layer The topsoil layer should
be removed or the foundations should extend below this
layer down to the sandy subsoils Otherwise on site
sandy material can be used to fill this area and cap off
the topsoil
The percolation tests indicated adequate seepage rates
for single family cesspool construction
Site Grading
Minimum cut and fill are anticipated for this development
The site should be cleared of all vegetation topsoil and
old foundations
The upper six inches of the existing ground surface should
be scarified brought to near optimum moisture content and
compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density
established by ASTM D 1557
Additional fills should then be placed in 10 inch loose
horizontal lifts compacted to the above specified density
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P 6
Foundation
For the light wood framed residential structures spread
footing foundations are recommended
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f can be used for
footings resting on the compacted sandy subsoils
The footings should extend a minimum of 12 inches below
the finish grade
If a footing is located next toa utility trench the
footing should extend to the bottom of the trench
Surface water should be diverted away from building
foundations
Slab On Grade
Slab on grade can be used on level areas away from the
tops of slopes
Slabs should be placed over a 4 inch layer of base course
size
To protect against moisture damage of the flooring material
a polyethylene moisture barrier should be considered
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P 7
Roadway Pavement
Asphaltic concrete pavements are planned for the project
roads
Based on the high laboratory CBR test value of 56 obtained
from the on site fine sand the preliminary pavement design
for a sandy subgrade soil condition is as follows
2 inch Asphaltic Concrete
4 inch Base Course
6 inch Total Pavement Thickness
Additional CBR tests should be performed on the actual
subgrade soil samples at the time of the roadway construc
tion and the above design section may be revised accordingly
if other subgrade soil conditions are encountered
Existing Cesspools
Existing cesspools are located throughout the site The
cesspool locations should be verified in the field Where
existing cesspool are located below or adjacent to footings
and slabs the sludge should be removed and the cesspool
filled with compacted granular backfill The cesspool
walls within 5 feet of the finish grade should be removed

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the soil engineer be present
during earthwork construction particularly the fill
placement to monitor the contractor s grading opera
tions
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be
observed by a soil engineer during construction to see
that adequate bearing has been obtained and that the
subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings
of our subsurface investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive
tested and approved by the soil engineer prior to hauling
to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent
upon the above inspections
