Dear Mr. Agapay: 
As requested, we have inspected the concrete core sample retrieved from Drilled 
Shaft No. WS-4 and reviewed the compressive strength testing results performed by Hayre 
McElroy & Associates, LLC on July 10, 2017. Based on our review, we provide the following 
findings and recommendations for your consideration.  
Drilled Shaft No. WS-4 was installed on June 1, 2017. Subsequently, we performed 
Crosshole Sonic Logging (CSL) testing on June 9, 2017 for the concrete integrity. The 
results of the CSL testing revealed anomalies with FAT increases up to 15% in various 
testing combinations from about 0 to 0.9 feet below the top of the concrete. 
It was concluded that the above anomalies may be caused by excessive air 
introduced during the concrete placement, subjected to further evaluation via concrete 
coring and compressive strength testing. 
The contractor conducted the concrete coring on July 5, 2017. The core hole was 
extended to a depth of about 6 feet below the top of the concrete level. The core samples 
were retrieved as illustrated in the attached core photographs.  
Based on visual inspection of the core samples, three core sections were selected 
for compressive strength testing as summarized in the following table. The compressive 
strength testing was performed by Hayre McElroy & Associates, LLC on three selected core 
samples on July 10, 2017. Results of the concrete compressive strength testing are 
presented in the Compressive Strength Data Report, dated July 10, 2017, and also 
summarized in the following table for ease of reference.
