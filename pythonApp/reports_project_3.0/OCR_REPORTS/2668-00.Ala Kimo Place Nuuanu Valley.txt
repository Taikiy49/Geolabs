INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of a geotechnical engineering exploration and
analyses performed for the proposed residence for Mr and located
at Ala Kimo Place in the Nuuanu Valley district on the island of Oahu Hawaii The
general location of the site and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
Based on the preliminary plans provided to us the proposed residence will consist
of a two 2 story building with parking garage a concrete slab on grade lanai and
concrete vehicular driveway
A concrete slab on grade foundation is anticipated for the proposed two 2 story
building
The site for the proposed residence is essentially level cut and fill operations for
site grading are anticipated to be minimal However an existing steep stream bank
about sixteen 16 to twenty 20 feet high with slope ratios ranging from approximately
one and a half horizontal to one vertical to one horizontal to one vertical
1H 1V is located immediately southeast of the proposed project site Since the
performance of the foundation system and the integrity of the proposed structure will be

study

Siope Stability
There is an existing steep stream bank abutting the proposed house area Due
to property line setback consideration it is not feasible to move the proposed house back
from this steep slope Based on the soil conditions encountered and our slope stability
analyses conducted for this exploration it appears that there is a potential of this slope
moving in the future
Two 2 cross sections were cut through the existing slope abutting the project site
The locations of these two sections are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 Section A A
represents the steep portion of the slope while Section B B represents the shallower
slope Slope stability analyses were performed for both sections utilizing the computer
program STABR with subsurface soil strength parameters based on information
obtained from the field exploration and laboratory tests The results of the slope stability
analyses are presented in Appendix C of this report
In order to evaluate the long term stability of the existing stream bank a relatively
low cohesion value of 200 p s f a long term cohesive strength value normally selected
for clay soils similar to those encountered at the site was used for the upper silty clay
layer An angle of internal friction of 18 degrees was determined based on data resulting
from laboratory residual shear tests performed on the silty clay sample
Based on the results of the stability analyses it appears that for Section A A a set
back distance of about twenty 20 to twenty five 25 feet away from the existing crest
would be required to realize a factor of safety of the potential critical slip surface of one
distance of approximately ten 10 feet would be required to realize a similar factor of

safety of the potential critical slip surface These slope bank stability results are used to
formulate the foundation design recommendations presented in the following sections
Drilled Pier Foundations
Shallow foundations located within potential slip circles of the existing steep stream
bank with factors of safety against rotational failure of less than one and one half 1 5 are
not acceptable and cannot be recommended Therefore foundations extending deeper
than the potential critical slip circles are recommended to support the proposed
residence
In order to support the proposed residential structure with a minimum potential of
structural distress it is our opinion that special foundation and structural slab system
should be used for the proposed construction
Since it is neither feasible nor cost effective to mobilize a conventional pile driving
rig to the project site it is our recommendation that a combination of structural slab
supported by cast in place drilled piers reinforced with small diameter steel pipes be
utilized to support the portions of the proposed residence which may be affected by
movement of the adjacent slope
The drilled piers should consist of minimum twelve 12 inch diameter drilled holes
with four 4 inch diameter steel pipes inserted The steel pipes will increase the rigidity
of the drilled pier foundations and also serve as tremie pipes If cave in conditions are
encountered the contractor should redrill and clean the individual hole to the originally
recommended depth prior to inserting the steel pipe and pumping the tremie concrete
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2668 00 Page 8
A design capacity of twenty 20 kips per pile with a minimum embedded length
of twenty five 25 feet below the existing ground surface is recommended A minimum
pile spacing of three 3 feet on centers is also recommended
Since groundwater level is encountered at depths of 13 8 to 19 2 feet tremie
placement of pump mix concrete from the bottom of the steel pipes is recommended
The drilled holes should be filled with concrete both inside and outside the steel pipes to
increase the stiffness of the drilled pier foundations Non shrinking type of pump mix
concrete should be used This would also help reduce potential corrosion of the pipes
The installation of the drilled piers should be observed by the project geotechnical
engineer to monitor that the steel pipes and drilled holes are properly concreted
Estimated settlement of the drilled pier foundation is anticipated to be less than
one half 0 5 inch total provided the above recommendations are followed
Based on the results of slope stability analyses two 2 rows of drilled piers one
row each on the northerly and on the southerly wall line are recommended for the
garage portion of the proposed residence which is located next to the steepest section
of the existing stream bank
For the living room and bedroom portion of the proposed residence only one 1
row of drilled piers along the southerly wall line adjacent to the stream bank is required
Columns on the southerly boundary of the proposed lanai should also be supported by
drilled pier foundations The northerly wall can be supported by continuous wall footing
foundations located outside the critical slip surfaces of the stream bank

Footing Foundations
For the portions of the structure which will most likely not be affected by slope
movement spread footing and continuous wall footing foundations may be used
Shallow continuous wall footing foundations may be used to support the living
room and bedroom portion of the proposed residence provided that a minimum horizontal
set back distance of fifteen 15 feet from the crest of the existing stream bank has been
achieved
An allowable bearing value of 2 000 p s f can be used for footings bearing on the
in situ stiff silty clay with weathered gravel A footing embedment of two 2 feet below
the lowest adjacent finished grade is recommended
if soft clay pockets are encountered at the bottoms of footing excavations they
should be over excavated to in situ stiff clay The over excavation should then be
backfilled with select borrow granular soils compacted to a minimum of 90 of their
maximum dry density as determined in accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
If a footing is located next to a utility line the utility trench backfill should be
carefully monitored and adequately compacted to reduce potential of footing settlement
due to settlement of the trench backfill
Surface water should be diverted away from the foundations to avoid ponding of
surface runoff water that may adversely affect the bearing capacity of the soils below the
foundations
Estimated footing settlement is anticipated to be less than one 1 inch total
provided the above recommendations are followed

Structural Siab
The ground floor of the proposed residence is anticipated to be of concrete
slab on grade construction Due to the existing steep terrain and adverse soil
conditions encountered a structural slab supported by drilled pier and spread line
footing foundations is recommended for the slab on grade
Based on laboratory test results the on site near surface clay soils possess
high swelling potential Therefore a minimum twelve 12 inch thick cushion layer of
3B Fine gravel or ASTM C 33 No 67 gravel is recommended underneath the
structural slab The gravel cushion layer will also function as a working platform
during construction and to provide a capillary break beneath the constructed concrete
slab Further protection against moisture infiltration should be provided by provision of
a plastic vapor barrier below the concrete slab One 1 inch of moist fine sand may
be placed above the plastic membrane to help concrete curing
The on site clayey subgrade underneath the cushion layer should be kept moist
by spraying water prior to placement of concrete to minimize the swelling effect of the
expansive clay soil
Post and Beam Construction
It is our opinion that post and beam construction with an elevated wood framed
floor could provide a more economical foundation system than an on grade concrete
structural slab Fewer drilled piers would be required due to the lighter dead weight of
the structure with an elevated wood floor Furthermore the thickness of the cushion
layer recommended underneath a concrete slab may be reduced or it may be totally
eliminated

Site Grading
Due to existing project site topography with an abutting steep stream bank it is
our opinion that site grading to lower the existing ground elevation by two 2 to three
3 feet should be considered The lowering of existing grade will reduce the height of
the stream bank and hence improve its long term slope stability characteristics In
addition the horizontal set back distance of the foundation from the crest of the
stream bank will be increased and the embedment lengths of the drilled piers may be
reduced
Prior to construction the site should be cleared and grubbed Any deleterious
materials such as vegetation debris rubble and organic matter encountered during
grubbing and scarifying should be removed and properly disposed of properly off site
Since the project site is located in a high rainfall area and the surface soil consists of
medium stiff clay which becomes soft and muddy upon wetting we recommend that
the subgrade should be over excavated a minimum of twelve 12 inches The
resulting over excavation should be replaced with 3B Fine gravel or ASTM C 33
No 67 gravel to provide a working platform for construction equipment This working
platform could be incorporated as the recommended cushion layer underneath the
concrete structural slab if the elevated wood framed floor suggested above is not
selected
The exposed subgrade underneath the over excavation should be scarified to a
depth of six 6 inches The scarified subgrade should be thoroughly moisture
conditioned and recompacted to obtain a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry
density as determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557 78
All imported fill and backfill materials that may be required should be tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to being imported to the site

Embankment should be placed in loose horizontal lifts not exceeding ten 10
inches loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry
density as determined by ASTM D 1557 78
The site should be graded to provide drainage of surface runoff away from all
foundations

course and concrete placement The thickened edges of the concrete driveway
should be a minimum of nine 9 inch thick
Joints are recommended at about eight 8 to ten 10 foot intervals and at

Drainage
The finished lot grade outside the footings slabs and pavements should be
shaped to shed water away from foundations to avoid ponding conditions Also it
is advised that a gutter system be installed around the residence and that the
gutter run off water be diverted as far away from the stream bank house
foundations and pavements as feasible Excessive landscape watering near
foundations should also be avoided
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have concrete liner
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the foundations and slabs
subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
foundations since ponded water could cause excessive subsurface soil saturation
and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations should
be properly backfilled tight against the footings and slabs immediately after setting
of the concrete to prevent ponding conditions
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained to
drain all surface run off away from the slab foundation

Plans and specifications for foundation site grading and utility construction

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the drilled piers installation and footing excavations
and backfilling be observed by a competent geotechnical engineer during
construction to assure that the drilled piers are properly concreted that adequate
bearing has been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with
the findings of our exploration
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site

Laboratory Testing
Moisture content determinations were performed on soil samples obtained
The results are presented on the Boring Logs and Probing Logs at the
appropriate sample depths
Seven 7 one inch ring swell tests were performed on remolded and
undisturbed samples to evaluate the swelling potential of the soils in both natural
and compacted conditions The test results are summarized on Plate B 1

soils encountered in the test pits The Plasticity Index of the material is used to
classify the soil under the Unified Soil Classification System The results of these
tests are shown on Plate B 2

Clayey soils to evaluate the long term shear strength properties of the materials

determination of the factors of safety of circular slip surfaces by using both the
