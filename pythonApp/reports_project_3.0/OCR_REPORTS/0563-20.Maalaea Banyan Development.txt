This is the summary of a 50 ton pile load test performed by
us for the above project between May 19 and 25 1977
A total of six 12 inch square concrete piles have been driven
within the main building area by Ben Hayashi Ltd These test
pile drivings were not monitored by this office A copy of
the Ben Hayashi Ltd s test piles driving record and summary
were obtained from and are attached in this
report Appendix A
All the test piles were driven to refusal at about 20 to 36
feet below the existing ground surface A sketch of the test
pile locations is also enclosed on the Pile Driving Summary sheet
One of the test piles pile no 2 was selected for pile load test
At a 100 ton loading 200 of 50 ton design load the pile settled
0 19 inch After rebound a net settlement of 0 06 inch was recorded
The pile load test indicates that the test pile can adequately
support the designed 50 ton load The pile load test results are
tabulated tn Appendix B along with the load settlement curve of
the test pile
Architects Engineers Alliance Inc
P 2
Based on the result of the pile load test and additional engineering
analysts it ts our optnton that in order to provide the pile cut
off at about elevation 9 feet plus 18 inches minimum extension of
the pile reinforced bars the rest of the production piles can be
casted to the following lengths
35 feet long pile for the south eastern
and north western portions
32 feet long pile for the north eastern
portion
25 feet long pile for the south western
portion
Based on a Kobe K 13 diesel hammer used for test pile driving the
blows per foot for final production pile driving shall be at least
60 for the last 2 feet or 15 blows per inch for the last two inches
The piles will be end bearing in the dense sand or upon the basalt
rock formation
Due to anticipated up lift and lateral forces at the pile founda
tion splice cans should be kept to a minimum In case where the
required pile length is longer than the driven pile it is pre
ferable to extend the pile by casting in place concrete section
with reinforced bars overlapping with the extended reinforced
bars from the pile section or lowering the pile cap
Should you have any questions concerning the above please do not
hesitate to call
