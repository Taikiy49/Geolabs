PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The existing BRAVO Docks B22 to B26 are located on the eastern side of the
Naval Shipyard facility along the waterfront at Pearl Harbor on the Island of Oahu
Hawaii The existing wharves extend 2 906 feet in an approximate east west direction
between the Naval Shipyard and Merry Point Landing It provides the primary berths for
ships of the U S Pacific Fleet The dock is a reinforced concrete structure supported by
precast concrete and composite piles Based on the information provided we understand
that the pile foundations consist of 18 inch square concrete piles 18 inch composite piles
and 16 inch composite piles The composite pile consists of precast concrete pile over
tapered timber pile The length of the timber pile varies from 10 to 40 feet The general
pile foundation plan is shown on the Site Plans Plates 1 1 through 1 3
We understand that the existing wharves were originally constructed in segments
between 1941 and 1945 Multiple repair works have been conducted after the original
construction In general both out board and in board edges of the dock are supported on
longitudinal pile rows capped with cast in place longitudinal girders Transverse beams
span between the out board girder and the in board girder to add support to the 8 inch
thick concrete deck The deck slabs include longitudinal duct and transverse beams
Along most of the in board edge of the docks the fill behind the structure is retained by a

METHODOLOGY OF EVALUATION
We understand that the purpose of the project is to evaluate the capability of the
existing BRAVO docks B22 to B26 for resisting the potential large loading from a
combination of Naval vessels Our approach to achieve this evaluation is based on the
following assumptions
1 The pile foundation was installed to the firm soil layer with design capacity
as indicated on the as built drawings
2 The existing piles are in good condition
3 Mudline elevation of the out board edge of the wharves is based on
1997 and 1998 boring information presented on the as built drawings
Sloping mudline condition of was used to extrapolate the mudline
elevation of the in board edge of the wharves
4 The existing docks are considered as a rigid reinforced concrete block
5 The tieback rods and dead man anchor system behind the in board edge of
the wharves embedded into the pile cap are ignored considering the
possibility of corrosion
6 Our analysis is based on loading information provided by Sato Associates
Inc dated
Our analysis consisted of lateral analysis estimation of uplift resistance for the pile
foundation system and passive earth pressure estimation for the in board pile cap The
computer programs used for pile foundation lateral analysis included Group 4 0 and Lpile
Plus 3 0
In general lateral load resistance of piles is a function of the stiffness of the
surrounding soil the stiffness of the pile allowable deflection at the top of the pile and
induced moment in the pile Programs Group 4 0 and Lpile Plus 3 0 were developed by
adaptation of a finite difference method to analyze the behavior of piles in a group or a pile
subjected to axial lateral loadings and overturning moment from the pile cap to piles For
Group 4 0 the program requires a symmetrical pile arrangement Both programs compute
deflection shear bending moment and soil response with respect to depth in non linear
soils for each individual piles The analysis was carried out with the use of internally
generated non linear p y curves to represent soil moduli A solution requires iteration to
accommodate the nonlinear response of each of the piles The equations of equilibrium

The programs can model various pile head boundary conditions and various pile

The individual concrete pile was assumed to crack and an appropriately reduced 50

In general the analyses were conducted based on 2 inch lateral deflection

and less number of existing piles are expected Based on the analysis performed by
Sato Associates Inc we understand that approximately 4 inch lateral deflection at the

two areas are based on 4 inch lateral deflection occurring at the top of the pile
Passive earth pressure of the footing was estimated by ignoring the top 5 feet of
backfill behind the in board pile cap considering erosion and possible settlement

FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Our analysis is based on assumptions and information presented previously
Generally lateral loading from vessels through mooring lines and fenders was transferred
to the top of the piles based on a rational load distribution The critical lateral load was
resisted by the pile foundation Symmetrical configurations of the pile foundation were
simulated based on the geometry of the existing pile foundation in order to perform Group
lateral analysis In the case where symmetrical configuration was not applicable Lpile
lateral analysis was conducted for representative individual pile to estimate lateral capacity
based on acceptable deflection induced bending moment and shear force in the piles
The pile cap at the in board edge of the wharves was analyzed for resistance to lateral
loads from the fenders in the in land direction The following section summarizes our
analysis and discusses our finding and recommendations

Estimation of Passive Earth Pressure
The loading on the fenders pointed towards in land was assumed to be resisted
primarily by the passive earth pressure on the pile cap located on the in board edge of the
wharves Based on the as built drawing provided we understand that the top of the
existing deck is located at Elevation 112 feet USND and the bottom of the pile cap of the
in board edge is located at Elevation 105 feet USND We believe that resistance due to
passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 pounds
per square foot per foot of depth pcf Considering the unknown existing condition the
passive pressure resistance in the upper 5 feet of soil should be neglected
Recommendations
Our analyses indicated that higher lateral deflection occurs in Areas 4 and 5 due to
relatively higher lateral loads and less piles Higher bending moment is also induced in the
piles within these areas As mentioned previously structural analysis indicated that the
existing pile foundation in these two areas are capable to resist about 4 inch lateral
deflection However we believe that the existing tie back and dead man anchor system
behind the in board edge of the wharves may need to be further investigated in order to
evaluate the interaction of the pile foundation and the tie back with dead man anchor
system to resist the mooring loading from Naval vessels
Based on our analyses in Areas 1 2 and 3 we believe that the existing pile
foundation in these areas are adequate to resist the loads from mooring lines and fenders
from the various vessels loading provided
