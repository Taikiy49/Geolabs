Dear Mr. Beyer: 
This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observation and testing 
services performed during the site grading of the Mathewson Residence project from 
January 7, 2016 through October 15, 2016 in accordance with our fee proposal dated 
January 11, 2016.  
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The project involves a planned single-family residence on Lot 7 within Kukui`ula 
Development Company’s M1 Subdivision in Koloa on the Island of Kauai, Hawaii. This 
residential project consists of a single-story house and garage structure, swimming 
pool, and other related site improvements. In addition, several tiered retaining walls are 
planned on the southwestern portion of the lot for grade separation. A Project Location 
Map and Site Plan are shown on Plates 1 and 2, respectively. 
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services 
performed during the site grading at the project site. 
SITE GRADING 
We observed the site grading work for the project from January 7, 2016 through 
October 15, 2016. Site grading for the project generally consisted of excavations up to 
about 5 feet deep for building/retaining wall foundations and swimming pool construction 
and fills and backfills up to about 6 feet thick to achieve the finished design grades.  
At the on-set of earthwork, the area within the limits of grading was cleared and 
grubbed of unsuitable surface materials. Greenwood Homes was the General Contractor 
for the project and performed the site grading. During the site grading operations, samples

of the on-site soils and imported fill materials were obtained and transported to our 
laboratory for testing. The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were 
established in our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D1557 (Modified Proctor), 
and the test results are summarized on Plate 3.  
The fill and backfill materials we observed consisting of on-site soils were 
moisture-conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum 
of 90 percent relative compaction. Select borrow material imported from Kauai Aggregates 
and used below the concrete garage and swimming pool slabs-on-grade were 
moisture-conditioned to near the optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum 
of 95 percent relative compaction. Relative compaction refers to the in-place dry density of 
soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density as determined by ASTM 
D1557. Optimum moisture is the water content (percentage by dry weight) corresponding 
to the maximum dry density. 
During the site grading operations, periodic field density tests were performed in 
general accordance with ASTM D6938 (Nuclear Gauge Method). Compaction test results 
are summarized on Plates 4.1 through 4.3. Test locations and elevations were estimated 
using on-site survey stakes for reference. These locations and elevations should be 
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used. Areas that did not 
meet the required moisture and density conditions were reworked until the required 
minimum compaction were obtained or exceeded. 
Our services consisted of part-time, as-requested field observation and testing of 
the contractor’s grading activities which included foundation subgrade preparation, fill and 
backfill placement, and confirmation of subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above 
grading operations. 
Based on our field representative’s observations and the referenced test results, it is 
our opinion that the site grading work for the Mathewson Residence project, observed and 
tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report, were performed in conformance 
with the general intent of the recommendations in the following references. 
1. 
Section 22.7.18 Specifications for Grading, Grubbing and Stockpiling of the 
County of Kauai Sediment and Erosion Control Ordinance No. 808. 
2. 
Report by Meta Engineering entitled “Soil and Foundation Investigation, New 
Residence, Lot #7 Kukui`ula Subdivision, Poipu, Kauai, Hawaii” dated 
March 2015.
