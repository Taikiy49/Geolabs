In accordance with our proposal dated we have performed a
geotechnical engineering exploration and field percolation testing program for the

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed agricultural subdivision project is at the foot of the north facing
slopes of the Waianae Mountain Range in Mokuleia on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The
project location and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 We
understand the project would involve the development of about 77 large agricultural lots
on gently sloping terrain along the base of the mountains
As part of the development the proposed sewage disposal system for the
agricultural lots will consist of septic tanks and a large leach field Based on the preliminary
information provided we understand that the leach field will be about 250 feet wide by
400 feet long
In order to evaluate the permeability of the subsurface soils and the disposal
capacities of the proposed leach field sites for wastewater disposal field percolation
tests were conducted at selected depths and locations across the leach field sites in
general accordance with the State of Hawaii Department of Health s Administrative Rules
Chapter 2 Wastewater Systems and Chapter 10 of the Ten States Standards

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our field exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions at the proposed leach field location and to perform field percolation tests to
evaluate the subsoil infiltration characteristics to formulate geotechnical
recommendations for the design of the disposal components of the on site sewage
disposal system The work was performed in general accordance with our fee proposal
dated Our scope of work included the following tasks and work
efforts
1 Mobilization and demobilization of a truck mounted drill rig and operators to
and from the project site
2 Drilling and sampling of nine borings to depths ranging from approximately
5 to 11 5 feet below the existing ground surface to evaluate the subsurface
soil conditions at the proposed leach field location
3 Coordination of the field exploration and logging of the borings by our
geologist
4 Performance of eight field percolation tests within the proposed leach field
site to evaluate the subsoil infiltration characteristics to formulate preliminary
recommendations for the design of the disposal components of the on site
sewage disposal system
5 Laboratory testing of selected soil samples obtained during the field
exploration as an aid in classifying the materials and evaluating their
engineering properties
6 Preparation of this letter report summarizing our work on the project and
presenting our findings and discussions
7 Coordination of our overall work on the project and client design team
consultation by our principal engineer
8 Miscellaneous work efforts such as drafting word processing and clerical
support

Our field exploration program at the proposed leach field site consisted of drilling
and sampling nine borings designated as Boring Nos 101 through 109 to depths ranging
from about 5 to 11 5 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate boring
locations are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general the subsurface conditions encountered at the proposed leach field
location consisted of very stiff to very hard silty clay over medium dense to dense coralline

FINDINGS AND DISCUSSIONS
We understand that on site sewage treatment and disposal by septic tanks and
leach field method are proposed for the agricultural subdivision development Therefore
we have conducted field percolation tests to obtain data pertaining to the design of the
sewage disposal system
Field Percolation Testing
Field percolation testing was performed in general accordance with the State of
Systems and Chapter 10 of the Ten States Standards to evaluate the permeability of the
subsurface soils for wastewater disposal The percolation tests were performed by drilling
existing ground surface The boreholes were presoaked for a minimum of 4 hours prior to
the percolation testing
Approximately one inch of gravel was then placed at the bottom of the boreholes to
protect the bottom of the holes from scouring and sediments During testing the holes
were carefully filled with water to a minimum depth of 6 inches above the gravel pack The
time intervals and water drops were then recorded to provide data upon which calculation
of the percolation tests were based

Based on the test results obtained the average percolation rate in the clay soils
is about 100 minutes per inch The average percolation rate of the clayey soils indicates
that the clayey soils have a relatively low permeability We believe the percolation
characteristics of the clayey soils may not be favorable for the construction of the new
leach field due to the extremely slow percolation rates
The average percolation rate in the coralline detritus and sandstone is about
8 minutes per inch which indicates a relatively high permeability Based on the test results
in the coralline detritus and sandstone we believe the new leach field should be
constructed in the coralline detritus and sandstone The coralline detritus and sandstone
was encountered at depths of about 3 5 to 5 5 feet below the existing ground surface at
the south side of the leach field and about 4 to 9 feet below the existing ground surface at
the north side of the leach field The depths of the coralline detritus and sandstone
correspond to elevations of about 10 to 15 feet Mean Sea Level MSL at the south side
and about 6 to 10 feet MSL at the north side of the leach field
Due to the variability of the subsoil conditions the absorption capacity of the
disposal system should be confirmed by additional percolation tests during construction

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
Our field exploration consisted of drilling and sampling nine borings designated
as Boring Nos 1 through 9 extending to depths of about feet below the
existing ground surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 3
We drilled the borings using a track mounted drill rig with continuous flight augers
Our geologist classified the materials encountered in the borings by visual and
textural examination in the field and monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified
Soil Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representations of the
materials encountered are provided on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 9
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained from the borings drilled in
general accordance with ASTM D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving
a 3 inch OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches In
addition some sampies were obtained from the borings drilled in general accordance
with ASTM D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a
2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same hammer and drop The blow
counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an 18 inch drive
are shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate
sample depths
Pocket penetrometer tests were performed on selected cohesive soil samples in
the field The pocket penetrometer test provides an indication of the unconfined
compressive strength of the sample Pocket penetrometer test results are summarized
on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
