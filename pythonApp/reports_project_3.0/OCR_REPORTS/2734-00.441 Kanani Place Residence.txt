INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed new residence at 441 Kanani Place in Honolulu on the island
of Oahu The general location of the site and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location
Map Plate 1 The exploration was performed on in accordance with our
proposal to Mr Christopher Gist dated
This report summarizes our findings resulting from the exploration and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analyses based on the data
obtained during the exploration and laboratory testing These recommendations are
intended for design input and are not intended to form the basis for preparation of
construction cost estimates or for bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a two 2 story building at the site Slab on grade type
of construction is planned for the ground floor
Based on plans provided to us it appears that the perimeter walls will serve as
retaining walls for retaining engineered fill within the building footprint to provide a level
pad for construction of the slab on grade Except for filling within the building and
driveway areas no other major site filling operation is planned
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions at the project site in order to develop a generalized soil rock data set for the
formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to foundation
engineered fill and pavement design of the project
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2734 00 Page 3
The work was done in accordance with our proposal to Mr Christopher Gist dated
The scope of work consisted of the following
1 Review of available soils and geologic information pertinent to the project
site and its vicinity
2 Reconnaissance of the project site and vicinity to make visual observations
of the surface soil conditions
3 Drilling and sampling three 3 borings to depths of 15 0 to 21 5 feet below
the existing ground surface
4 Laboratory testing of selected samples obtained during the field exploration
to determine the classification and engineering properties of the materials
encountered
5 Engineering analyses of the field and laboratory data for the purpose of
developing foundation and pavement design recommendations
6 Preparation of this report presenting the findings and design
recommendations
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration and laboratory testing are presented in
the appendices of this report

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located at 441 Kanani Place near the junction of Lilina Street
and Kanani Place on the island of Oahu Hawaii The existing terrain is lower than Kanani
Place to the east and slopes down toward the westerly side at a slope ratio of
approximately 6 to seven 7 horizontal to 1 vertical 6 to 7H 1V
Along the northern and western boundaries the site is bounded by an existing
rubble wall The height of the wall ranges from about two 2 feet at Kanani Place to
about six 6 feet at the southwestern corner of the site The wall consists of randomly
placed boulders without masonry or grout
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling three 3 borings to
depths of 15 0 to 21 5 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate locations
of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were drilled on
The results of the exploration indicate that the subsoils consist of a grayish brown
Clay Adobe layer near the surface ranging from two 2 feet by Kanani Place Boring No
2 to about four 4 feet by the existing rubble wall Boring No 3 The borings then
encountered stiff clayey silt overlying sand and decomposed rock fragments to the
maximum depth drilled of 21 5 feet
Groundwater was encountered at 8 0 and 13 0 foot depths in the borings at the
time of our field exploration It is our opinion that the encountered groundwater level
reflects localized perched groundwater conditions in the general area It is anticipated
that the groundwater level will vary with climate rainfall and other factors

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration generally encountered expansive clay near the surface It is
recommended that the perimeter wall footings of the building be embedded sufficiently
deep to bypass the expansive clay and bear on the underlying stiff clayey silt soils
A schematic section illustrating the conceptual design of site grading and
embedment of the perimeter wall footings is shown on Plate 3 The deeply embedded
wall footings should bypass the near surface adobe Clay and this may reduce potential
impact from the existing rubble wall since the wall was not designed and constructed to
retain expansive soils and building loads
The quantity of imported fill is also reduced because site filling will be limited to
within the building footprint area by using the perimeter walls as retaining walls
It is strongly recommended that a registered professional civil engineer be
consulted for design of site grading and drainage of the project
Site Grading
A preliminary grading plan of the project was not available at the time of our report
preparation However based on information provided to us it is anticipated imported fill
will be needed to provide a level pad within the building footprint area for siab on grade

Separation between the finished floor and existing grade outside the building area
Therefore the area outside the building may remain at the existing grade with a sloping
terrain at approximately 6 to 7 horizontal to 1 vertical 6 to 7H 1V ratio
At the start of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
cleared of vegetation debris rubbish old footings and other deleterious materials These

The area should then be scarified to a depth of six 6 inches moisture conditioned
and recompacted to a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density as determined by
ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 Any loose or soft spots encountered should be
over excavated down to firm natural ground and the resulting depression backfilled with

above
The finished elevation of the proposed slab on grade has not been determined at

Foundation Design
Continuous perimeter wall footings and internal spread footings may be used for
the proposed new structure The perimeter wall footings should bypass the adobe clay
and bear on the underlying stiff native soils The inner thickened edges and slabs may
bear on compacted fill material
Based on the borings drilled the required embedment for the perimeter wall
footings ranges from a minimum of two 2 feet by Kanani Place at the eastern portion of
the site to about four 4 feet below the existing ground surface along the southwesterly
foundation wall It appears that the required embedment increases gradually from Kanani
Place to the southwesterly foundation wall
The materials encountered at the bottom of footing excavations should be scarified
to a minimum depth of six 6 inches moisture conditioned and recompacted to a
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2734 00 Page 8
aminimum of 95 of the maximum dry density as determined by ASTM Test Method
D 1557 78
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f may be used for design of the
foundations The bearing value is for total dead plus live loads and may be increased by
one third when temporary wind and seismic loads are considered
If foundations are located next to sewer or drainage easements they should be
embedded below a 1H 1V imaginary plane extending upward from the bottom edge of
the utility trenches or as deep as the inverts of the utility lines
It is recommended that a subdrain system consisting of filter fabric gravel and
perforated PVC pipes be installed along the wall footings parallel to Kanani Place refer
to Plate 3 to intercept potential surface runoff and seepage and divert water away from
the wall foundations
The prepared footing bearing surfaces should be observed by the project
geotechnical engineer to determine if they are located below the near surface adobe clay
and that adequate bearing has been obtained prior to placement of reinforcement and
concrete For footings properly constructed and observed as recommended above the
estimated settlements are on the order of less than one half inch total and one fourth inch
differential
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive earth pressure
developed against the buried vertical faces of the foundation system and by frictional
resistance developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil

Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be calculated using an equivalent
fluid pressure of 300 p c f This assumes that the soil around the foundation is well
compacted A coefficient of friction of 0 40 may be used Unless covered by pavements
or slabs the passive resistance in the upper 12 inches of soils should be neglected
Drainage
The finished lot grade should be shaped to shed water away from foundations to
avoid ponding conditions Also it is advised that a gutter system be installed around the
building and gutter water be diverted as far away from the house foundations and
driveway as possible Excessive landscape watering near foundations should be avoided
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have concrete bottom and
drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for proper performance of the
foundations since ponded water could cause excessive subsurface soil saturation and
subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations should be properly
backfilled after setting of the concrete to prevent ponding conditions
It is strongly recommended that a registered professional civil engineer be
consulted for design of site grading and drainage of the project

Slab On Grade
For the design of slab on grade floors the use of four 4 inch thick concrete slabs
placed over a four 4 inch layer of No 3B Fine gravel or ASTM C 33 No 67 gravel
cushion is recommended To protect against moisture damage of the floor covering
material a moisture barrier such as a polyethylene membrane should be used below the
slabs A minimum two 2 inch thick layer of sand should also be placed over the
membrane prior to concrete placement to aid in the curing of the concrete
Due to the expansive nature of the near surface soils cut and fill within the building
footprint should provide a minimum of two 2 feet of properly engineered non expansive
select borrow capping fill below the proposed slab cushion The subgrade clay soils
should be kept moist prior to the placement and compaction of the imported select
borrow materials

The slab on grade should be separated from the perimeter walls and the thickened
edges of the on grade slabs should extend a minimum of 12 inches below the finished
floor elevation
Concrete Driveway
Based on the anticipated expansive characteristics of the subsoils and need for
some amount of fill for the driveway area the concrete driveway should consist of a
minimum of six 6 inches of Portland Cement Concrete overlying about two 2 feet of
compacted non expansive granular off site select borrow material
A minimum of 9 inch thick thickened perimeter edge is also recommended for the
driveway Joints are recommended at about 8 to 10 foot intervals and at discontinuities
to allow for some relative movements of the concrete
Laboratory CBR tests should be performed on the actual subgrade soils during
construction to verify the adequacy of the above sections The above pavement section
assumes medium traffic and that adequate drainage will be provided in the paved area
Utilities
New underground utility lines may be anticipated for the project A granular
bedding consisting of six 6 inches of No 3B Fine or ASTM C 33 No 67 gravel is
recommended under the pipes
Granular materials should be used for trench backfill The backfill up to about one
1 foot above the pipes should be lightly tamped to reduce the possibility of damaging
the pipes

The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level one 1 foot above the pipes
to the finish grade should be placed in maximum 10 inch horizontal loose lifts
mechanically compacted to a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density as determined
by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 to protect against potential future ground subsidence
It is considered to be the contractor s responsibility to protect all existing utility lines
during construction
Existing Rubble Wall
The rubble wall located along the northern and western boundaries of the property
consists of randomly placed boulders without mortar or grout Our field observations of
the wall revealed no indication of it being keyed or embedded into the original ground
surface
It appeared that the wall was not constructed to withstand exterior loads Our
recommendation to construct deeply embedded perimeter wall footings for the new
residence also intends to transmit building loads further down to reduce potential impact
on the existing rubble wall
However there is no provision to improve its stability in the event of future
construction activities or long term performance of the wall It also appears that portions
of the existing rubble wall may be outside the current project property line Therefore
future communication and discussion with the owner of the neighbor property is
anticipated prior to repair the existing rubble wall A registered professional structural
engineer should be consulted in performing the repair work

Construction Precautions
The recommendations presented in this report have been formulated with the
above factors in mind The currently proposed residential construction will have no
minimum impact on the rubble wall provided that the following precautions are followed
1 Stockpiles of materials resulting from clearing and grubbing from
foundation excavations for the imported fill and materials required for
construction of the building shall not be permitted to be within six 6 feet
from the wall
2 Equipment necessary for construction operations shall not be permitted to
be within six 6 feet from the wall The equipment and methods of
excavation and construction shail be selected by the potential contractor
3 Excavations shall be held to the minimum required for proper construction
of the foundations and shall not remain open longer than necessary Work
over weekends may be required if necessary to fulfill the objective
4 A plan of accomplishment including equipment materials methods
stockpile locations for excavated and imported fill and a schedule showing
the various items of work shall be submitted to the project geotechnical
engineer for review and comments a minimum of seven 7 working days
prior to initiation of the work

Construction Monitoring

geotechnical engineering services during site grading and construction of the foundations
This is to observe compliance with the design concepts specifications or
recommendations and to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions

from those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made
