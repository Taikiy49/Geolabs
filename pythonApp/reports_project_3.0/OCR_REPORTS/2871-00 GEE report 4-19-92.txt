INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed drainage project in Hanapepe on the island of Kauai Hawaii
The general location of the project site and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location
Map Plate 1
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2871 00 Page 2
This report summarizes the findings of our exploration and presents our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from analyses of the data obtained
during the field exploration These recommendations are intended for design input and
may not be appropriate to form the basis for construction cost estimates or for
construction bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to improve the existing drainage system to accommodate a 100 year
storm runoff at the project site The improvement will include the construction of three
feet by 7 0 feet new concrete box culverts across Hanapepe Road at Location A
construction of three feet by 10 0 feet concrete box culverts in addition to the two
existing across Kaumualii Highway at Location B and construction of one feet by
13 0 feet concrete box culvert in addition to the three currently existing across Puolo Road
at Location C
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions at the project site in order to develop a generalized subsurface data set for the
formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to the design for
construction of the box culverts and headwall structures comprising the project
The work was done in accordance with our proposal dated
addressed to Kodani Associates Inc of Lihue Kauai The scope of work consisted
of the following tasks and work efforts
1 Review of available soils and geologic information pertinent to the project
site and its vicinity

SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed project will involve the construction of new concrete box culverts in
the vicinity of Hanapepe on the island of Kauai
Locations A and B are situated near the intersection of Hanapepe Road and
Kaumualii Highway as shown on Site Plan 1 Plate 2 Presently there are two feet
by 10 0 feet box culverts crossing Kaumualii Highway at Location B The topographic
map provided indicates that the existing ground elevations range from approximately 10 0
to 15 0 feet above Mean Sea Level at these two locations

Location C is situated across Puolo Road to the east of its intersection with Lele
Road Presently there are three 8 7 feet by 13 feet box culverts running across Puolo
Road with the drainage being discharged into Hanapepe Bay The site and its existing
development are shown on Site Plan 2 Plate 3 The existing ground elevation at this
location is approximately eight 8 feet above Mean Sea Level
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling six 6 borings to
depths ranging between 15 0 to 21 5 feet below the existing ground surface One of the
borings was advanced deeper by continuous penetration without sampling to a maximum
depth of 33 5 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate locations of the
borings are shown on the Site Plans Plates 2 and 3
Location A Crossing Hanapepe Road
Boring B 6 located at the mauka side of Hanapepe Road generally encountered
a three 3 foot thick surficial colluvium deposit of very stiff clayey silt with gravel and
cobbles This was underlain by a layer of medium dense sand extending down to the
proposed invert elevation at approximately 10 feet below the ground surface A basalt
formation was then encountered in the boring extending down to 15 feet below the
existing ground surface the maximum depth drilled
Boring B 5 located at the makai side of Hanapepe Road generally encountered a
four 4 foot thick surficial fill layer of medium dense silty sand underlain by a deposit of
loose sand down to approximately 10 feet below the existing ground surface A thin layer
of crushed gravel overlying a deposit of medium dense sand which continued down to
approximately 21 feet below the ground surface was then encountered
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2871 00 Page 5
Location B Crossing Kaumualii Highway
Borings B 3 and B 4 generally encountered a surficial fill layer of stiff clayey silt
approximately four 4 feet thick This was underlain by an alluvium deposit of medium
dense sand down to approximately 20 feet below the existing ground surface
Location C Crossing Puolo Road
Boring B 2 located at the mauka side of Puolo Road generally encountered a thin
surficial fill layer of dense silty sand underlain by cobbles and boulders down to
approximately four 4 feet below the existing ground surface
A surficial fill layer of medium dense sand was encountered at the upper four 4
feet in Boring B 1 located at the makai side of Puolo Road
Both borings then encountered a deposit of medium dense sand between a depth
of four 4 to twenty one 21 feet below the existing ground surface
Groundwater Levels
Groundwater was encountered in all the borings at depths of 7 6 to below
the existing ground surface during our field exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on the subsoil conditions encountered during the exploration it is our
opinion that the proposed project is feasible from a geotechnical engineering point of
view It appears that the underlying medium dense sand encountered at the drilled
borings should provide adequate bearing support for the proposed culvert structures at
the project sites
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2871 00 Page 6
General guidelines for the design of the box culverts and headwall structures are
as follows
Box Culverts and Headwall Structures
An allowable bearing value of up to 2 500 p s f may be used for the box culverts
and headwalls resting on the in situ medium dense sand or compacted fill material
The following lateral earth pressures are recommended for the design of the box
culverts and headwall structures
1 Level Backfill
Walls unrestrained at the top K 35 p c f equivalent
active condition fluid pressure
Walls restrained at the top K 50 p c f equivalent
at rest condition fluid pressure
H V Sloping Backfill
Walls unrestrained at the top K 50 p c f equivalent
active condition fluid pressure
Additional loads due to traffic should also be considered where applicable
A friction factor of 0 4 may be used to determine the sliding resistance of the walls
A passive soil resistance of 300 p c f may be used
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2871 00 Page 7
Backfill Behind Box Culverts
The backfill up to about one 1 foot above the culvert should consist of select well
draining self compacting granular materials with sizes generally between three
fourths 3 4 inch and one fourth 1 4 inch such as No 3 Fine gravel or ASTM
C 33 No 67 The backfill should be lightly tamped to reduce the possibility of
damaging the culverts
The upper portion of the backfill from the level one 1 foot above the culvert to the
finish grade should consist of non expansive material such as the excavated on
site sand To minimize possible future ground subsidence the fill should be
placed in level horizontal lifts not exceeding ten 10 inches in thickness and
mechanically compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of its maximum dry density
as determined in accordance with procedures described in ASTM Test Method
D 1557 78
Backfill Behind Headwall Structures
Headwall structures are anticipated at the inlets and outlets of the proposed box
culverts
Well graded granular materials such as base course rock No 3B Fine gravel or
ASTM C 33 No 67 or sand should be used as backfill material directly behind the
walls This is to reduce the build up of excessive lateral pressures on the walls
The top one 1 foot of backfill should consist of non expansive impervious
material to reduce infiltration of surface water behind the wall
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2871 00 Page 8
Cushion
A twelve 12 inch cushion layer of gravel such as No 3B Fine or ASTM C 33
No 67 is recommended below the culvert structure to provide uniform bearing
and drainage
The cut off wall at the outlet of the proposed box culvert located across Puolo
Road should be embedded an additional amount to reduce potential scouring
action of the tidal waves which would undermine the subgrade bearing material
located below the box culvert
Shoring and Dewatering
During our field exploration it was observed that the lower portions of the drilled
boreholes below the static groundwater level had cave ins or collapsed due to the sandy
subsoil conditions encountered
Due to the proximity of project sites to the ocean front the groundwater table is
anticipated to generally occur above the proposed invert elevations In addition the
future excavations will be susceptible to large water infiltration due to the porous and
granular nature of the sandy subsoil conditions encountered on site
Therefore adequately designed and constructed shoring support and dewatering
of the excavations will be required by the contractor in order to construct the proposed
box culvert structures The method and equipment to be used for the project shoring and
dewatering should be left to the choice of the contractor within practical limits and safety
considerations
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2871 00 Page 9
Rock Excavation
Based on the subsoil conditions encountered in Boring B 6 excavation in a basaltic
rock formation should be anticipated along the mauka section of the proposed box culvert
across Hanapepe Road Care must be exercised to avoid over ripping which would
disrupt the structure of the rock formation resulting in a potential loss of bearing strength
Design Review
Preliminary and final drawings and specifications for the proposed construction
should be forwarded to the project geotechnical engineer for review and written
comments prior to advertisement for bidding This review is needed to determine
adherence to the earthwork and foundation recommendations given in this report If this
review is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot be considered responsible for
misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during the site grading and construction of the
foundations This is to observe compliance with design concepts specifications or
recommendations and to expedite suggestions for design changes that may be required
in the event that subsurface conditions differ from those anticipated at the time of
preparation of this report The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon
such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and approved by
the geotechnical engineer prior to delivery to the site

lf actual exposed soil conditions encountered during construction are different from
those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to the design
should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part
upon information obtained from field borings Variations of conditions between the field
borings may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident
until construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to
reevaluate the recommendations given in this report
The location of the field borings indicated in this report are approximate having
been determined by taping from features shown on an undated topographic map
provided by Kodani Associates Inc on and the Hanapepe Drainage
Project construction plans prepared by Masao Kutaka Civil Structural Engineer dated
Elevations of the field borings were estimated by interpolation from
the contours and elevations shown on the same plans The physical locations and
elevations of the field borings should be considered accurate only to the degree implied
by the methods used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the field borings depict
the approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual
transition
Water level data from the field borings was collected at the time given on the
graphic representations and or indicated in the text of this report These data have been
reviewed and interpretations made in the formulation of this report However it must be

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling six 6
borings to depths of 15 0 and 21 5 feet below the existing ground surface at the
approximate locations shown on the Site Plans Plates 2 and 3 The borings were drilled
using truck mounted equipment
One 1 of the borings was advanced deeper to a depth of 33 5 feet below the
existing ground surface by continuous penetration In this procedure drill rods with a
blunt tip are driven continuously into the ground with a 140 pound hammer falling 30
inches The number of blows for each foot of penetration is counted and recorded on
the Boring Logs at the appropriate depths
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Method D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Spilt Barrel Sampling of Soils by
driving a standard penetration sampler or 3 O D Modified California sampler with a 140
pound hammer free falling 30 inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the last 12
inches are shown on the Boring Logs at the appropriate sample depths
The material encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who continuously monitored the drilling
operations These classifications were further reviewed by visual inspection on return to
our Honolulu office All soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System Graphic presentations of the materials encountered are presented
on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 6
