PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is within the existing Hawaii Campus of Brigham Young University
in the Laie area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project involved the construction of
three townhouse buildings designated as Buildings U V and W two parking lots and a
housing office building at the northwestern side of the campus The new townhouses are
between the existing Stake Center Chapel and the Temple View Apartments
The new townhouse buildings are two stories high and comprised of
eight 2 bedroom units with a building footprint ranging from 3 000 to 4 000 square feet in
area The new buildings are constructed of CMU walls and slabs on grade construction
Flooring for the second floors consists of precast concrete planks with interior partitions
consisting of metal studs and gypboard Roofing for the buildings consist of wood truss
framing with asphalt shingle roofing

The finish floor elevation for the new buildings is set at about 11 5 feet Mean Sea
Level MSL Based on the existing topography and the design finish floor elevation
4 feet of structural fills are planned to achieve the design finish grade at the building pad
areas Due to the presence of loose sands and soft clays underlying the project site we
recommend implementing a surcharge program with settlement monitoring during
construction Therefore a surcharge program with settlement monitoring was undertaken
to induce the anticipated ground settlements to occur
SURCHARGE PROGRAM
As previously mentioned 4 feet of structural fills was required to raise the existing
ground surface at the building pad areas to achieve the design finished floor elevation of
11 5 feet MSL for the new townhouses and housing office As previously indicated loose
sands and soft clays were anticipated at depths of about 2 to 8 feet below the existing
ground surface Therefore some ground settlements would occur when the new fills of up
to about 4 feet thick were placed over the potentially compressible soils
In order to aid in accelerating ground settlements and reducing the settlement
waiting period a surcharge program consisting of 5 feet of surcharge fills with settlement
monitoring was implemented prior to the construction of the structures To monitor the
actual settlement rate eight settlement gauges two at each building location were
installed at selected locations across the building footprints prior to the fill placement
operations The settlement gauges were read optically on a periodic basis by Lindemann
Construction Inc and then transmitted to our office for review
In general imported non expansive select granular material was used as a source
of structural and surcharge fill materials The settlement waiting period commenced on
at Building W and the Housing Office and terminated on
for Building U Filled ground settlements ranging from about 2 5 to 6 inches were recorded
based on the settlement gauge readings provided by Lindemann Construction Inc
SITE PREPARATION
Site grading work for the project generally consisted of structural fills on the order of
about 4 feet thick in order to achieve the finished pad grades for the new townhouses and
housing office building The earthwork operations were performed by Lindemann
Construction Inc
After clearing and grubbing the exposed subgrades were excavated where
necessary to about 6 8 feet MSL in order to provide a minimum 4 foot thick structural fill
below the bottom of the slab cushion The over excavated subgrades were generally
proof rolled with a vibratory sheepsfoot roller to at least 90 percent relative compaction to
provide a firm and unyielding surface prior to the fill placement Following the proof rolling

preparation imported non expansive select granular fill material was brought to the site
and placed as structural fill at the building pad area
During placement of the structural fill we understand that the fill material was
generally placed in near level lifts brought to above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to a minimum of 95 percent of the maximum dry density determined in
accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor
Based on our field observations soft and moist clayey subgrade soils were
encountered at the new pavement areas where the old car wash was previously located
In accordance with our recommendations approximately 18 inches of the soft and moist
clayey soils were removed to reduce the potential for future pavement distress The
bottom of the over excavation was proof rolled with the static weight of the drum roller
without vibratory action to provide a relatively firm and smooth surface The
over excavation was then backfilled with approximately 18 inches of No 3B Fine gravel
sandwiched between a layer of non woven geotextile fabric Mirafi 180N at the top and
the bottom The stabilization layer was then capped with 12 inches of base course
compacted to a minimum of 95 percent of the maximum dry density determined in
accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor
A sample of the imported materials utilized as structural fill was obtained and
transported to our office for testing The maximum dry density and optimum moisture
content were established in our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D 1557
Modified Proctor and the test results are summarized on Plate 2
A total of 74 field density tests including retests of failed areas were performed on
the subgrade and compacted structural fill materials using the nuclear probe method in
accordance with ASTM D2922 and D3017 The results of the compaction tests
performed are summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 4 of this report
The tests were performed on the constructed fills at the site Test locations and
elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes These
locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the
methods used Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified
minimum compaction was obtained
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the earthwork operations during the site grading and
pavement construction for the project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period
covered by this report were performed in general accordance with the intent of the project
plans and specifications and our geotechnical engineering recommendations presented in
the referenced report dated March 28 2005 and subsequent recommendations

LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork may
have been performed without our observation However the level of earthwork
observation and testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some of
the geotechnical risks to the project It should be recognized that there are variations in the
accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the
earthwork construction and materials
Our services were performed in accordance with generally accepted standards of
geotechnical engineering practice no warranty is expressed or implied Provision of
construction monitoring by an engineer is not insurance nor does it guarantee
construction of any type The contractor shall retain responsibility for the quality of the
work for adhering to the plans and specifications and for repairing defects regardless of
when they are found
