INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this investigation was to evaluate the
subsurface conditions at the site and to provide recommen
dations for site grading and foundation design for the
proposed development The work was performed generally in
accordance with our revised proposal of
W O 869 00 P 2
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located on the western side of Kawaihau
Road south of Iiwi Road in Kapaa Kauai
The site is situated on a sidehill area adjacent to and
lower than Kawaihau Road A large drainageway is located
along the western project boundary The drainageway was
covered with trees and was dry at the time of the field
exploration
The proposed development site slopes down from Kawaihau
Road at gradients of up to 50 per cent to a gently sloping
northern and central areas
It appears that the northern portion of the site has been
previously graded Approximately 5 to 8 feet of soil has
been removed from the northern three fourths of the ridge
area creating a gently sloping terrain This cut area
was bare and partially eroded This area slopes down to
the south at a gradient of about 6 to 10 per cent
The central portion of the site was generally irregular with
stockpiles of loose fill with surface boulders and erosion
gullies and was covered with grass and brush This area
slopes down to the south at gradients of 5 to 50 per cent

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Subsurface Exploration
Eight test pits were dug with a backhoe to depths of 5 to
13 feet below the existing ground surface to determine the
subsurface conditions and to obtain samples for laboratory
testing Due to the inaccessibility of the southern one
fourth of the site area test pits could not be dug there
The approximate locations of the test pits are shown on the
Site Plan Plate l
Detailed information on the field exploration and laboratory
testing is covered in the Appendix along with the test pit
logs and laboratory test results
Soil Conditions
Based on the test pits the site is underlain by stiff
residual soils consisting of stiff mottled reddish brown
Clayey silt
A layer of brown clayey silt and cobbles loose fill 3 feet
thick or more was noted over the residual soil in the central
portion of the site and approximately delineated on Plate l
Site Plan
Groundwater was not noted in the pits during the field
exploration

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Except for the loose fill encountered in the central portion
of the site the subsurface exploration disclosed competent
bearing soils at the site Shallow foundations bearing on
the stiff natural soil will provide adequate support for
the proposed one and two story structures
Laboratory swell tests on the on site soils indicate low
expansion potential therefore slab on grade can be designed
without special provisions for expansive soil conditions
Existing Fill Area
The field exploration disclosed loose fill and boulders in
the central portion of the site The approximate limits
of the fill is delineated on the Site Plan Plate 1
Test pits dug in the northern portion of this are disclosed
that the fill is at least 2 to 3 feet thick Due to the
inaccessibility of the southern portion of the site test
pits could not be dug to more accurately delineate the
extent and depth of the existing fill We recommend that
the site be observed after clearing and grubbing of this
area to further delineate the extent of the fill

This existing fill layer was dry and relatively loose
and therefore not suitable as foundation support After
Site clearing the fill should be stripped off moisture
conditioned and reused as compacted fill
Surface boulders were also noted in this area The boulders
larger than 8 inches should not be used in the compacted
fill
Site Grading
Based on the grading plan on Plate 1 the project site
grading consists of cuts and fills generally less than
10 feet
The site should be cleared of vegetation and debris then
scarified and recompacted to 90 of its maximum dry density
established by ASTM D 1557
Fills should be placed in 8 inch lifts compacted to the
above specified density
Where the existing ground is steeper than 5 horizontal to
1 vertical 5 1 benching and keying are required to
properly bond the new fill to the slope The filling
operation should start at the lowest point and continue
up in level compacted layers in accordance with the above
structural fill requirements For construction details
refer to Plate 2
W O 869 00 P 7
It is recommended that if additional off site borrow
is required it should be tested and approved by the
soils engineer prior to transportation to the site to
assure that it is suitable for the proposed construction
The material should generally be non expansive and should
not contain large percentages of boulders organic or
deleterious matter
Slopes
Two horizontal to 1 vertical fill slopes are recommended
For cut slopes in the stiff natural ground 1 horizontal
to 1 vertical slopes can be used
All 111 slopes should be track rolled or overfilled and
cut back to obtain a well compacted slope All fill slopes
should be properly keyed and benched as outlined under Site
Grading
The proposed access roadway off Kawaihau Road will be
located at the edge of a fill slope up to about 10 feet
high To reduce the effects of slope creep on the roadway
this fill slope should be overfilled to a 14 1 slope then
cut back to 2 1 H V
To minimize erosion all slopes should be planted as soon
as practicable
W O 869 00 P 8
Foundations
For the proposed structures spread or continuous footing
foundation are recommended
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f is recommended
for footings bearing on the stiff in situ soil or compacted
fill
On level areas the bottom of footings should be placed
about 18 inches below the finish grade Where the footings
are located near the top of slope they should be embedded
an additional amount such that there is a minimum 7 foot
horizontal set back distance between the outer edge of
footing and the slope face due to the highly erosive site
soils
Footings located near utility trenches should extend to
the bottom of the trench
Surface water should be diverted away from the building
foundations
Retaining Walls
The retaining wall footings should follow the foundation
recommendations presented above
W O 869 00 P 9
Retaining walls should be backfilled using well graded
granular materials such as base course rock Weepholes
or a subsurface drainage system should be used to minimize
build up of hydrostatic pressures behind walls
The walls up to 10 feet may be designed to resist lateral
earth pressures as follows
Walls with level backfill 40 p c f equivalent
fluid pressure
sloping backfill 2 fluid pressure
A friction factor of 0 4 can be utilized to determine the
sliding resistance of the wall Additionally a passive
soil resistance of 300 p c f can be utilized The passive
resistance should be reduced by one third when combined
with the frictional resistance
Slab On Grade
To resist the downhill creep movement of the near surface
soil and to develop the full bearing capacity of the sub
soil the slab perimeter footing close to the top of slopes
should extend down a sufficient depth to obtain a minimum
horizontal set back of 7 feet from the slope face
Conventional slab on grade can be used on level areas
located 5 feet or more away from the tops of slopes
W O 869 00 P 10
Slabs should be placed over a 4 inch layer of base course
size
To protect against moisture damage of the flooring material
a polyethylene moisture barrier should be considered
Roadway Pavement
Asphaltic concrete pavements are planned for the project
roads
Based on the laboratory CBR test on the on site soil the
preliminary pavement design is as follows
2 inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 inch Base Course
8 inch Total Pavement Thickness
Additional CBR tests should be performed on the actual
subgrade soil samples at the time of the roadway construc
tion and the above design section may be revised accordingly
if other subgrade soil conditions are encountered
Additional Exploration
The southern portion of the site was inaccessible for the
backhoe therefore test pits were not dug in this area
Visual observations indicate that this portion may be
Similar to the area investigated We recommend that
visual observation and additional test pits be made after
site clearing to verify the soil conditions in this area

The plans for foundations site grading and utility

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the soil engineer be present during
earthwork construction particularly the fill placement
to monitor the contractor s grading operations
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be
observed by a soil engineer during construction to see
that adequate bearing has been obtained and that the
subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings
of our subsurface investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive
tested and approved by the soil engineer prior to hauling
to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent
upon the above inspections

excavating 8 test pits with a backhoe The test pits

System

the test pit walls to get an indication of the in situ shear
strength of the soil
