REGIONAL GEOLOGY
Hawaii the largest island of the Hawaiian Archipelago covers an area of
approximately 4 000 square miles The island was formed by the activity of five shield
volcanoes These are Kohala which is long extinct Mauna Kea which has had activity
during recent geologic time Hualalai which last erupted in 1801 and Mauna Loa and
Kilauea which are both still active The project site is located in the Hilo area and is situated
on the eastern flank of the Mauna Loa Volcano
The project site is located in the Hilo Harbor area and is situated on land reclaimed
from the bay by the placement of fill over the lagoonal deposits The subsurface conditions
at the project site consist of coralline fill materials placed over coralline lagoonal deposits
overlying basalt formations The lava formation encountered at the project site appears to
be of pahoehoe flow which is characterized by a smooth rope like or billowy surface and an
internal structure of vesicular porous rock In general the basalt formations in the area are
considered to be relatively permeable rock and can transmit water quite readily in the
GEOLASBS INC
W O 4226 00 Page 5
horizontal and vertical directions Water is normally transmitted through the porous rock
matrix along fractures and cavities voids and along clinker layers Therefore the
permeability of the basalt will be highly dependent on the presence of fractures cavities and
clinker layers
EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The shed office project site is located within the Pier 1 area of Hilo Harbor on the
Island of Hawaii Pier 1 is bounded by Puhi Bay to the east and Kuhio Bay to the west as
shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The Pier 1 Shed Office is currently being
utilized by the HT T Stevedore Department The Pier 1 Shed Office in our study is about
55 feet long and 42 feet wide as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general the shed office is a 2 story steel framed structure with corrugated sheet
metal sidings The interior of the shed office consists of wood paneled wails with concrete
floors Based on the plans provided we understand that the 2 story structure is supported
on a driven pile foundation system However the concrete floor slab is supported on grade
and is not structurally supported on the pile foundations Based on our observations the
slab is currently supporting light loads consisting of wall partitions tables desks file
cabinets chairs and other types of office equipment The area fronting the shed office on
the exterior consists of asphalt concrete pavement A drywell for drainage purposes was
observed to be about 15 feet fronting of the shed office storage room A downspout from
the roof gutter system was also observed to discharge in the area outside of the storage
room
The shed office is built on relatively flat ground with a ground elevation of
approximately 9 5 feet Mean Sea Level MSL The Pier 1 Shed Office steel columns are
supported on a deep foundation system consisting of driven precast concrete piles The
concrete piles vary in length from about 30 to 40 feet below the ground surface
corresponding to pile tip elevations of about 25 to 35 feet MSL Based on the available
subsurface information the shed office is underlain by near surface fills followed by coralline
gravel with sand fills and or lagoonal deposits The near surface fills and lagoonal deposits

masonry walls on the remaining sides
During our field exploration significant settlements of the concrete floor slab were

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field exploration and laboratory test results it appears that the
probable source of the settlement observed in the Pier 1 Shed Office floor slab is below
the medium dense to dense near surface fills encountered in the borings Based on the
materiais encountered in the borings and the on going settlements observed in past
20 years we believe that the settlement of the concrete floor slab at the subject site is
likely caused by loss of ground below the slab due to erosion Due to the medium dense
to dense nature of the near surface fills within 5 feet of the existing ground surface we
believe that the source of the observed settlement is unlikely in the upper near surface fill
GEOLASS INC
W O 4226 00 Page 8
materials within about 5 feet of the existing ground surface We believe that the probable
source of the observed slab settlement is in the loose to medium dense granular soils
underlying the upper fill materials The probable explanation of the continued settlement
observed in the slab over the past 20 years is likely due to erosion of the granular soils
underlying the near surface fills due to the movement of water flowing water or tidal
fluctuation Therefore we believe that the observed settlement in the slab will likely
continue in the future due to the continued erosion of the finer soils in the underlying
materials
Based on our field exploration program and engineering analyses we believe that
temporary methods such as grouting and or replacement of the slab with a new concrete
slab on grade to mitigate the observed slab settlement should not be attempted
considering that previous attempts to rectify the situation have been made without success
In addition we believe that the observed settlement in the slab is an on going problem and
the source of the settlement is deeper than the near surface fills Therefore in order to
correct the observed slab settlement we recommend that the existing concrete floor slab
be removed and replaced with a structurally supported concrete slab To structurally
support the new slab we recommend that the new floor slab be supported on mini pile
foundations bearing on the compact finger and tree corals with sand anticipated between
about Elevations 30 and 50 feet MSL

Evaluation of Existing Conditions
Based on our field exploration the top 5 feet of fill encountered below the concrete
slab consisted of medium dense to dense granular fills The medium dense to dense fills
were underlain by very loose to medium dense fills and or lagoonal deposits consisting
of coral gravel with sand Due to the medium dense to dense nature of the near surface
fill layer within 5 feet of the existing ground surface the upper near surface fill materials
GEOLABS INC
W O 4226 00 Page 9
is likely not the source of the settlements observed in the slab We believe that the
materials encountered below the medium dense to dense near surface fills specifically
the materials below the water table is likely the source of the settlements observed in the
slab As previously mentioned the water table was encountered at about 8 feet below the
existing ground surface
Based on the sieve analysis tests performed on the soil samples retrieved it
appears that the soils at the site are relatively clean with few fines less than 5 percent
particles passing the No 200 sieve In addition the percent particles passing the No 40
sieve appears to be higher for the soil samples above the groundwater level when
compared to the soil samples below the groundwater table Therefore we believe that
there may be some loss of ground beneath the slab and near surface fills resulting in the
observed settlement in the slab The loss of ground is likely due to the erosion of sand and
silt size particles of the underlying granular fills and or lagoonal deposits caused by the
movement of water within the fill Erosion of the underlying material transports finer particles
away from the fill mass creating void spaces within the fill mass Several possible factors
that may be the cause of water movement within the fill mass include the following
1 Tidal fluctuations
2 Disposal of storm water runoff into the drywell located in front of the storage
room
3 Underwater springs
Tidal Fluctuations
Due to the proximity of the project site to the Pacific Ocean groundwater levels at the
site are affected by tidal influences The tides can fluctuate from about 1 to 2 feet
between the high and low tides on a daily basis Tidal currents are created by the
horizontal flow caused by the tidal rise and fall near the shoreline This movement of
water causes erosion of soil materials
Based on the information provided the fills consisting of coral gravel with sand
underlying the Pier 1 Shed is retained by sheet piles and a rock wall bulkhead located
GEOLABS INC
W O 4226 00 Page 10
at the western side of the shed We believe that some of the fine materials in the fills
below the groundwater level may migrate through the narrow crevices of the rock wall
bulkhead and sheet piles due to the movement of water contributing to the loss of
ground below the Pier 1 Shed Office
Disposal of Storm Water Runoff
Based on our field exploration a drywell was located about 15 feet to the south of the
Pier 1 Shed Office A downspout from the roof was also observed in the vicinity The
disposal of storm water runoff into this particular drywell may be a factor in the erosion
of the cohesionless soils underlying the shed office Based on our observations
storm water runoff from the parking area and from portions of the shed roof flow into
the subject drywell Considering that Hilo is an area of high rainfall this drywell may
dispose of storm water runoff at a flow rate of more than 300 gallons per minute
gpm Considering the high flow rates of storm water disposal in the drywell erosion
of the loose granular fill materials in the underlying subsurface is likely
Underwater Springs
Basalt is considered to be relatively permeable rock and can transmit water quite
readily in both the horizontal and vertical directions In general water is transmitted
through the porous rock matrix along joints fractures and inter flow contacts cavities
and along clinker layers The permeability of the fractures and cavities is high
therefore they serve as the major water transmission features In addition lava tubes
are also common features in the basalt rock formation Lava tubes are also major
water transmission features which are commonly encountered in the Hilo area
Fresh water from rainfall infiltration flows to the edges of the island and encounters
the sait water This fresh water is known as the basal groundwater body The basal
groundwater does not move into the ocean with relative ease due to frictional
resistance Instead the basal groundwater builds up until it has sufficient hydraulic
head to overcome the resistance The basal groundwater develops a hydraulic
GEOLASBS INC
W O 4226 00 Page 11
gradient toward the ocean In areas of high rainfall such as Hilo the hydraulic
gradient is much steeper compared to dry areas with low rainfall
The basal groundwater becomes channelized in structures such as lava tubes and
large fractures in the basalt and escapes into the sea These are known as basal
springs The basal springs can occur above or below sea level Some basai springs
can discharge as much as several million gallons of water daily Macdonald and
Abbott 1970 In the case of Pier 1 Shed Office underwater springs may be a cause
to the erosion of the granular soils underlying the shed office
Remedial Construction Work
As previously indicated the concrete floor slab at Pier 1 Shed Office has
experienced on going settlement for the past 20 years It is our opinion that the settlement
of the slab is caused by loss of ground due to erosion of the granular soils below the
groundwater table underlying the slab area Erosion of the underlying granular soils is an
on going problem therefore settlement of the floor slab will likely continue to occur in the
future It is our opinion that temporary methods such as grouting and or placement of a
new concrete slab on grade to mitigate the settlement of the slab should not be attempted
because these remedial measures do not address the probable cause of the problem
observed at the site Therefore we recommend that a more permanent method of
mitigating the settlements be considered for the Pier 1 Shed Office In general we
recommend that the existing concrete floor slab be removed and replaced with a new
structurally supported floor slab
There are several different methods to structurally support the concrete floor slab
Due to the presence of the loose granular fill materials the depth to a competent bearing
stratum and the low head room in the shed office area we recommend that mini piles be
considered for support of the concrete floor slab The mini piles should be designed to
resist the generally light structural loads of the concrete floor slab by end bearing

In general the mini piles should extend vertically down to a firm bearing stratum
Based on the foundation plans for the Reconstruction of Pier Sheds dated
the pile foundation system used to support the Pier 1 Shed is about 30 to 40 feet below the
existing ground surface This corresponds to about 25 to 35 feet MSL Based on the
boring logs taken from Board of Harbors Commissioners Drawings H C 455 dated
January 1924 a firm bearing layer consisting of compact finger and tree corals with sand
was encountered between about Elevations 30 to 50 feet MSL
Due to the variation in the depth to a competent bearing layer in the subsurface
conditions we recommend that additional field exploration be conducted to provide the
necessary subsurface information for design of the deep foundation system for support of
the floor slabs and or new structures It is also recommended that Geolabs Inc be
retained to provide the follow up consultation work related to the foundation design of the
floor slab and or new structures planned at the Pier 1 area
