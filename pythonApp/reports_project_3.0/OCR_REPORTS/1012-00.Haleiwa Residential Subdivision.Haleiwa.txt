This report presents the results of our boring exploration
and percolation testing for the proposed Haleiwa Subdivision
Oahu Hawaii
The purpose of this investigation is to obtain preliminary
subsurface soil conditions at the site and to evaluate the
in situ seepage characteristics for private sewage disposal
system design and construction
SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed project site is located on the northern shore
of Oahu roughly one mile from Haleiwa Beach Park heading
towards Kahuku
W O 1012 00 P 2
The site consists of an area of approximately 13 acres
It s primary boundaries are Kamehameha Highway on the
southeast and Papailoa Street on the northwest
The relief is essentially level with ground elevations
ranging from 9 to 12 feet throughout the site
There were no existing structures on the site during the
time of our investigation and field testing
Vegetation consisted of a moderate to heavy growth of
grass Haole Koa and Ironwood trees
FIELD EXPLORATION
Nine 4 inch diameter auger test borings were drilled to
depths ranging from 1 5 to 30 feet below the existing
ground surface at the approximate locations shown on the
Site Plan Plate 1
The drilling operations were monitored by our geologist
who also logged the borings The boring logs are presented
on Plates A 1 through A 15
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface exploration disclosed essentially poorly
graded sand and silty sand to the entire depth of the
borings In the open area where there are no ironwood
trees present a surficial layer of reddish brown silty
clay soil appears in some of the interior borings Nos
4 to 8
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1012 00 P 3
The surface reddish brown soil ranged in thickness from
1 foot in boring no 9 to 6 5 feet in boring no 5 and
it probably was an import fill material replaced after
sand mining operation
Boring and 3 drilled along Papailoa Street
revealed the sand to be at the surface extending to the
bottom of the borings
Groundwater levels were encountered in most of the bore
holes varying in depths of 5 to 9 2 feet
For more detailed description of the soils encountered
and the groundwater levels refer to the boring logs in
the appendix
PERCOLATION TESTS
Five falling head percolation tests were performed in bore
holes 5 and 6
After completion of the bore hole drilling a constant flow
of water was introduced into it to develop a constant head
water level within the hole A constant head was developed
near ground level in boring 3 and 6 Boring no 5
did not develop any measurable head with an inflow rate of
6 3 g p m
The percolation test data are presented on Plates A 16 to
A 20 and the laboratory test results are summarized on
Plate B l
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 4
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the test results from the drilled bore holes it
is our opinion that the underlying natural coral sand depo
sits below the upper red soil layer will provide adequate
percolation rates in a private cesspool sewage disposal
System with adequate design and maintenance Also the
sandy material has high percolation rate and therefore
can be utilized to dispose of future storm water runoff
collected in a silting basin
Based on the percolation test data obtained in the drilled
holes a 6 foot diameter cesspool extending a minimum of
5 feet below the water level would provide a more than
adequate seepage rate for a single family residence
Our percolation tests performed in the bore holes indicated
that sand can provide a very high percolation rate ranging
from 650 to 1650 gallon day square foot of surface area
Should you have any questions concerning our findings please
do not hesitate to call this office
