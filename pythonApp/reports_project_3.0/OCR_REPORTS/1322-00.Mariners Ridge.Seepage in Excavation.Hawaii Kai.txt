In accordance with your request an engineer from our firm visited
the above site on October 27th and examined the seepage noted in one of

Site Conditions
At the time of our visit the site had already been excavated and
roughly graded into 2 levels of terraces with the lower level about 14
feet below the upper level A near vertical excavation separating these
levels was made along the originally planned retaining wall
W O 1322 00 Page 2
The two existing test pits were made in the lower terrace in an
attempt to reach solid ground for wall footing as designed by the
structural engineer It is our understanding that the first test pit
was excavated to 7 foot depth and encountered seepage water at about 5
feet below the lower level The other test pit was also dug to 7 feet
deep to check for seepage conditions However no seepage was observed
in the second test pit
The terrace cut and test pit excavations disclosed that the fill
soils consist of gray brown sandy clayey silt and rock fragments that
extend from the street level to at least the bottom of the test pits
It appears that the fill was moderately to well compacted The stiff
sandy clayey silt portion appears to be expansive A disturbed sample
of this portion of soil was obtained and tested in our laboratory The
sample was remolded into a one inch ring and soaked overnight under 55
p s f of surcharge The sample exhibited a swell of 9 2
Seepage Control
The water seepage was still present at the time of our field
observation indicating that it is an active seepage Care should be
exercised to redirect the seepage out from the fill so that water will
drain freely off onto an open area to prevent build up of hydrostatic
pressure

To redirect the seepage water we recommend that a subdrain be
installed from the identified seepage point to daylight out on the
existing slope outside of the building area A layout and a sketch of
the subdrain construction are presented on Plates 1 and 2 respectively
The construction of the subdrain should include the following
A trench should be excavated and a filter fabric Placed at the
bottom and walls of the trench A 3 inch thick bedding layer of No
3 Fine crushed rock should be placed at the bottom and a drain pipe
of at least 3 inches in diameter placed The pipe should then be
covered by at least 18 inches of No 3 Fine crushed rock in 9 inch
lifts and each lift tamped by a vibratory plate The filter fabric
should wrap over the entire crushed rock material in the trench
The remainder of the trench should be backfilled carefully with
select on site material that has no particle larger than 3 inches
Care must be exercised to avoid rupturing of the filter fabric The
backfill should be placed in 6 inch lifts and compacted to 90 of
the maximum ASTM D 1557 78 density
The drain pipe should be placed so that the gradient will be 1 to 2
percent or about 3 inches drop in 20 feet of horizontal distance
The first 5 foot section of the pipe at the seepage area should be
perforated with the perforations facing down The downslope end of
the pipe should be daylighted out and not be blocked A screen or

Recommendations
Our current observations on the exposed soil conditions in the
retaining wall and test pit excavations confirm those stipulated in our
previous report dated a copy of which is in your
possession
In general the previous recommendations for site preparation lot
grading foundations and slab on grade should be followed except for
some modifications presented in the following sections In addition
recommendations for retaining wall design are presented herein for your
consideration
Site Grading
Loose 111 materials were noted on the steep slopes on the
downhill side of the lower terrace It was reported that this material
consisted of recently excavated spoils that spilled over the Slope It
is recommended that the loose material on the slope be cut back to the
original site grading slope
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1322 00 Page 5
The two test pits should be carefully backfilled All loose
materials in the test pits should be thoroughly removed and pits
backfilled with compacted on site material except that portion required
for subdrain construction The backfill material should be placed in
9 inch thick horizontal layers and compacted to a dry density of at
least 90 of the maximum ASTM D 1557 78 designation
The backfilling operation should be monitored by a soils engineer
Foundation
In view of the moderately expansive clayey silt mixed in the gravel
and cobbles the footing bottom should be embedded at least 18 inches
below the lowest adjacent grade to minimize the swelling effect of the
soils
Furthermore in view of the relatively steep slope below the lower
terrace we recommend that the set back distance from the Slope face be
increased to 8 feet minimum Footings on the steep slope
should be embedded at least 5 feet as recommended in the previous report
Slab On Grade
It is our understanding that slab on grade construction will be used
only in the driveway garage and the utility and storage area attached
to the garage The main dwelling areas will be supported on
post and beam foundations
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1322 00 Page 6
Due to the moderately expansive nature of the on site materials we
recommend that the on grade slabs be placed over a pad of imported
non expansive soil at least 12 inches thick plus 4 inches of cushion
fill Any imported fills should be tested and approved by a soils
engineer
Prior to placing the non expansive fill the on site fill as
constructed per SITE PREPARATION and LOT GRADING of the June 27
1980 report should be moistened for at least 2 days to allow the upper
portion of the subsoils to absorb moisture It is recommended that the
imported fill be placed somewhat wetter than the optimum moisture
content Before the on grade slab area is covered with the moisture
barrier membrane the subgrade should be moistened It is import ant
that the subsoil be kept moist and not allowed to dry excessively to
Limit potential swelling which may result in heaving and cracking of the
slabs

An allowable bearing value of up to 2000 p s f is recommended for

A passive earth resistance of 250 p s f per foot of depth may

Drainage
The finished lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings
should be shaped to shed water away from the foundations and to avoid
ponding conditions Also it is advised that gutter systems be
installed around the house and that gutter water should be diverted far
away from the foundations and walkways Excessive landscape watering
near the foundations and slab should also be avoided
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance
of the above referenced foundations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of
strength The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled
tight against the wall or slab footing immediately after setting of the
concrete to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained
to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundation
Design Review
Plans and specifications for foundations site grading and utility
construction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and
written comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the footing excavations and backfill
operations be observed by the soils engineer during construction to see
that adequate bearing has been obtained and the subsurface conditions
are consistent with the findings of our observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above inspections
