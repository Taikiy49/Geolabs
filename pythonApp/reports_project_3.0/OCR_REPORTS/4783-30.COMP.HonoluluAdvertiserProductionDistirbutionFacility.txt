This report summarizes our earthwork observation and testing services performed
during the mass grading operations and press mat foundation preparation work for The
Honolulu Advertiser Production and Distribution Facility at Kapolei project from
September 3 through in accordance with our fee proposal dated
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
Based on the information provided we understand that the Honolulu Advertiser is
developing a new production and distribution facility adjacent to the City of Kapolei in the
District of Ewa on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The property of the new facility is bounded
approximately by Kapolei Parkway Extension future to the north Kalaeloa Boulevard to
the east an old Oahu Sugar Railroad track to the south and open undeveloped land to
the west

Based on the site plan provided we understand that the proposed development will
consist of a new building that will house a mailroom newsprint room plant room and a
press hall A perimeter road for heavy delivery trucks and loading zones for newsprint
delivery and dispatch will be provided to serve the new facility In addition an asphalt
concrete paved parking lot will be constructed on the west side of the new building as
shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
We understand that the finished floor elevation of the new building will be set at
about 67 feet Mean Sea Level MSL Based on the existing topography at the project
site we envision that the mass grading work will generally consist of minor cuts and
substantial fills on the order of about 5 to over 10 feet thick in order to achieve the design
finished grades for the proposed development
In our addendum dated prepared for the above project we
recommended that the press hall be supported on a mat foundation system bearing on
compacted structural fill select borrow subbase material placed over the dense coralline
detritus materials anticipated at greater depths In order to provide the compacted
structural fill below the mat foundation significant excavation on the order of about 20 to
22 feet deep below the existing ground level was required in order to remove the upper
clayey soils and expose the underlying dense coralline detritus materials
SITE PREPARATION
As previously mentioned the mass grading work consisted of minor cuts and
substantial fills on the order of about 5 to over 10 feet thick in order to achieve the design
finished grades for the project The mass grading operations and press mat foundation
preparation was performed by RHS Lee Inc
Observations during our geotechnical engineering exploration indicated ihat
abundant shrinkage cracks and remnants of previously excavated and backfilled test pits
existed across the project site Shrinkage cracks located adjacent to the building
foundations are potentially detrimental since water can readily infiltrate through the crack
opening and soften the subsurface soils around the building foundations thus affecting
their performance In addition it was not clear whether the previously excavated test pits
have been properly backfilled Therefore special subgrade preparation procedures were
followed during the site grading operations for the project development
After clearing and grubbing the exposed subgrades were proof rolled with a 10 ton
vibratory drum roller a minimum of six passes to assist in detecting and collapsing
near surface voids and loose zones created by the shrinkage cracks and remnants of the
old test pits The exposed subgrades were proof rolled without moisture conditioning of
the subgrade soils at the pre existing moisture contents Loose areas disclosed during
the proof rolling operation were backfilled with general fill material compacted to a
minimum of 90 percent relative compaction The proof rolling operations were conducted
under the observation of a representative from Geolabs
GEOLABS INC
Hawaii California

Subsequent to the proof rolling operations the subgrades were scarified to a depth
of about 12 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum
moisture and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density
determined in accordance with American Society of Testing and Material ASTM Test
Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor
Following the subgrade preparation both imported materials from the Nanakuli
area and stockpiled materials from the Marketplace at Kapolei project were placed as
general fill at the project site During placement of the general fill the fill materials were
generally placed in near level lifts brought to above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density determined in
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor
Limestone fines from Grace Pacific Corporation was used for the 24 inch thick
non expansive capping layer required below the building slab and pavement areas The
24 inch thick non expansive capping fill under the building slab and pavement areas were
compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to
the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of
the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 test procedures Optimum
moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum
dry density
Based on the information provided the press mat foundation measured
approximately 214 5 feet long and 23 3 feet wide in plan dimensions The base of the mat
foundation excavation was extended to at least 30 feet wide and sloped up at a gradient of
one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V on all sides In addition benching of the side slopes
was performed during backfilling of the excavation The press mat foundation excavation
was backfilled with select borrow subbase material conforming to the requirements of
Section 31 of the City and County of Honolulu Standard Specifications for Public Works
Construction September 1986 The material was moisture conditioned to above the
optimum moisture content placed in level compacted lifts of about 8 inches and
compacted to at least 95 percent relative compaction
During the mass grading operations samples of the on site imported and
stockpiled materials utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to
our office for testing The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were
established in our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557
Modified Proctor and the test results are summarized on Plate 2 of this report
Periodic field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations to
confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures and soil conditions
were noted A total of 277 field density tests including retests of failed areas were
performed using the nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM Test Designations
D 2922 and D 3017 The results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on
Plates 3 1 through 3 16 of this report
