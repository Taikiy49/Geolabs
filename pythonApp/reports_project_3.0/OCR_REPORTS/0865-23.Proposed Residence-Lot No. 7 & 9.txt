Dear Mr Matsui
As requested we have made a study of the near surface soil
conditions below the above referenced lots The purpose of
the study was to evaluate the suitability of the near surface
soils as foundation support for the proposed houses The
study consisted of 4 hand auger probes within each proposed
building site laboratory soil tests and a review of the
grading and compaction test record on the building sites
This letter summarizes our observations and recommendations
PROJECT CONSIDERATION
The preliminary house plans for both lots were not available
during the time of our soil investigation

EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
Lots 7 and 9 are located on the eastern portion of the sub
division development about 450 feet north of Ainapo Street
They are bounded by Kahena Street on the southwest subdivi
Sion lots on the southeast and northwest and a concrete
drainage channel on the north The lots are gently sloping
downward from Kahena Street toward the north at about 5 0
to 6 0 slope The elevation of Lot 7 generally varies from
145 to 155 feet and Lot 9 generally varies from 151 to 164
feet
SUBDIVISION LOT GRADING
Prior to the recent project site grading operations the
southern half of Lots 7 and 9 were covered with a large
stockpile of soil and boulders left over from previous
grading operations in the area
During this grading operation the stockpile was removed
down to a depth of 2 to 6 feet below the design finished
grade and replaced with compacted available on site soils
The available on site soils consisted basically of silty
clay and clayey silt soils which had been mixed with some
Adobe clay pockets from the previous stockpile during the
recent lot grading operations A few feet of fill were
placed over the northern half of these lots

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
A review of the grading compaction test record and the site
reconnaissance indicates that Lots 7 and 9 are underlain by
grayish brown moderately expansive clay fill to the full
depth of the hand auger probes which were about 4 0 feet

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that the lots are underlain
by two to six feet of grayish brown clay fill The laboratory
test results on this material generally indicated that they
have moderate to high expansion potential Therefore for
these 2 lots the slab on grade and foundation construction
would require special design procedures in order to reduce
the shrink swell effect of the subsoils

Foundation
The laboratory tests indicate that the on site soils have
moderately high expansion potential To reduce the expansive
effects of the clay layer the following procedures may be
used
For these sloping lots post and beam construction is highly
desirable and recommended due to the above soil condition
Slab on grade and driveway should be properly designed for
expansive soil condition
For post and beam construction the individual post footings
should be extended to a minimum depth of 24 inches below the
ground surface The footings should be poured in place
If slab on grade for garage and house foundation is required
the following three 3 alternatives could be used
1 Where minimum site fill is required for lot
grading the expansive clay below and for a
distance of 3 feet around the slab should be
removed for a minimum depth of 3 feet below
the outside finished grade and replaced with
compacted non expansive off site borrow material
such as quarry waste The underlying remaining
clay soil should be scarified moistened to above
optimum moisture content and recompacted moder
ately 85 compaction prior to backfilling
with the 3 foot capping select fill

W O 865 23 P 5
Ideally the most economical solution the lot
should be filled up in the slab on grade and
garage areas to provide a 3 foot minimum capping
layer of compacted non expansive borrow material
over the existing expansive soils at finished
lot grade Also the underlying expansive clay
subgrade should be scarified moistened to above
optimum moisture content then recompacted to
85 density prior to filling
With the above procedures conventional slab on
grade construction with a 12 inch thickened edge
can be utilized
The above two alternatives discussed can be
considered a more straight forward and rela
tively safe solutions for the underlying expan
Sive subgrade soil conditions
A third alternative is to leave the expansive
clay soil in place and design the slab for
expansive soil conditions A deep perimeter
footing around the house slab will help to
reduce the shrink swell effects of the clay
soil The perimeter footing should extend a
minimum of 30 inches below the outside ground
elevation and have a minimum width of 12 inches

Two 4 bars should be placed at the top and
bottom of the perimeter footing
In addition the concrete slab should be rein
forced with No 3 bars at 24 inch intervals both
ways in lieu of the conventional wire mesh to
resist the high expansive forces of the subsoils
We recommend that the subgrade soils be saturated
by constant sprinkling at least 2 to 3 days prior
to concrete placement to reduce the swelling poten
tial of the underlying subsoil
Hydrated lime should be placed at the bottom of
the deep perimeter footing excavation and thick
ened edges of slabs and driveway prior to water
saturating of the subgrade soil About 2 pounds
of lime per lineal foot of excavation should be
applied
The soils engineer should inspect the subgrade
preparation and check the moisture contents of
the underlying soils prior to pouring of the
slab and perimeter footing
The bottom of the footing excavation should be
neat and free of loose soils or debris

The concreting of footing and slab should be
coordinated in such a way that the time period
between the initial excavation and final concrete
placement at each lot should be kept to a minimum
The above recommendations for saturating the
subgrade should also be followed for the side
walk driveway and patio slabs
The finish grade outside the slab and pavements
should be shaped to shed water away from the foun
dations and to avoid ponding condition near the
slab area Also the owner should be made aware
of gutter water which should be diverted far away
from the perimeter footings and walkways
An allowable bearing value of 2000 p s f may be
used for the above recommended footings on com
pacted fill
Driveways
For concrete driveways recommendations provided for concrete
slab on grade should be followed However since the drive
ways could tolerate some amount of swelling the non expansive
capping thickness could be reduced to 2 feet or the deep peri
meter footing depth could be reduced to 18 inches

Design Review
The plans for foundations site grading and utility construc
tion should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and
comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included in the project plans and specifi
cations
Site Grading
If required fills and backfills should be placed in 8 inch
maximum loose lifts and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its
maximum dry density established by ASTM D 1557
Prior to filling the area should be cleared of vegetation
and debris
The on site clay soils are expansive and unsuitable as a source
of structural fill
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during
earthwork construction particularly the fill placement to
monitor the contractor s grading operations
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be
observed by a soils engineer during construction to see that
adequate bearing has been obtained and that the subsurface
conditions are consistent with the findings of our subsur
face investigation
