INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of a geotechnical engineering
evaluation performed for the proposed Building K of Pono Kai Resort
Condominium at The general location of the site and
its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed project will include the construction of i a
three story resort hotel condominium known as the Building K ii a
one story lobby structure with patio and iii new water feature
expansion
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface conditions within the proposed building area in order to
develop a generalized soils data set for the formulation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertinent to the proposed construction
The work was done generally in accordance with our fee proposal to
Glen Ivy Financial dated August 14 1989 The scope of work included the
following
1 Mobilization and demobilization of a truck mounted drill rig to
the project site for the boring exploration
2 Drilling and sampling of four 4 borings to depths ranging from
5 8 to 31 0 feet below the existing ground surface using auger
drilling and rotary wash techniques
3 Reconnaissance of the project site and its vicinity to make
visual observations of the surface soil conditions
4 Laboratory testing of selected samples obtained during the field
exploration to determine the engineering properties and
classification of the materials encountered
5 Engineering analyses of the field and laboratory data for the
purpose of developing geotechnical engineering design
recommendations
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2374 00 Page 3
6 Preparation of this report summarizing our findings and
presenting our recommendations
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration and laboratory testing
are presented in the appendices of this report
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located at Highway makai side in
Kapaa on the island of Kauai Hawaii The proposed Building K will be
situated next to the existing parking area at the entrance to Pono Kai
Resort Presently the site is a grass lawn area with some planted trees
Existing tennis court and water feature are located to the east of
the proposed building area Existing office building and maintenance shop
are located next to the southern and northern ends of the proposed site
respectively
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Field Exploration
Four 4 borings were drilled to depths ranging from 5 8 to 31 0 feet
below the existing ground surface The approximate locations of the
borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
It appears that our drilled Boring No 2 had probably encountered the
outer edge of an existing underground concrete conduit running across the
project site Therefore drilling operation of Boring No 2 was
terminated at a depth of 5 8 feet and Boring No 2A was drilled
approximately 10 feet away from Boring No 2 to a depth of 27 feet below
the existing ground surface to obtain subsoil information

Soil Conditions
In general the borings encountered an approximately 5 foot thick
surface layer of medium stiff reddish brown clayey silt overlying about 15
feet of medium dense to very dense sand Below these loose to medium
dense sandy gravel and silty sand were encountered at approximate depths
of 20 to 31 feet below the existing ground surface
Boring No 3 encountered dense basalt gravel fill at a depth of about
5 to 6 feet below the existing ground surface probably indicating that
part of the project site may be underlain by loose gravel boulder fill
within the upper 6 foot zone This may be a result of previous
uncontrolled site fill or inadequate ground preparation
Groundwater levels were encountered at approximate depths of 10 0 to
11 5 feet below the existing ground surface during the time of our
exploration
Detailed descriptions of the materials encountered are presented in
the Boring Logs in Appendix A Field Exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration encountered about five 5 feet of surface fill
layer consisting of stiff reddish brown clayey silt with boulders
overlying a 15 foot thick deposit of very dense sand Below the very
dense sand layer and to the depths of the borings drilled loose to medium
dense silty sand and sandy gravel were encountered
It is our estimate that very likely loose to dense gravel boulder
fill may be present randomly within the upper 6 foot zone due to previous
land site filling
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2374 00 Page 5
Laboratory tests performed on the samples of the clayey silt obtained
from the borings indicate that this material generally has moderate to low
swelling potential depending on the initial moisture content and
compaction density prior to subsequent saturation
We believe that the upper crust of clayey silt which might have
possible loose gravel boulder zones can not provide adequate foundation
support for the proposed building without over excavation and recompaction
of the underlying soils to provide the bearing support to the proposed
spread footing foundation Therefore we recommend the footing excavation
to extend through the upper fill and bear directly on the underlying dense
sand layer Alternatively the footings can be placed on a minimm three
3 foot of recompacted structural fill
Foundations
We believe that shallow spread or continuous footing foundations may
be used to support the proposed Building K and lobby structures
However to reduce the potential of differential excessive footing
settlements we recommend that all spread line footing excavations be
extended down to a minimum depth of about five 5 feet below the existing
ground surface and to bear the footings directly on the underlying dense
sand layer We recommend that concrete placement for the spread or
continuous footings should be separated from the proposed slab on grade
which will bear on recompacted subgrade soil and base course cushion
layer
As an alternative we recommend the expansive clayey silt be
over excavated down to a minimum depth of three 3 feet below the bottom
of the individual footings and then replaced with either select on site or
non expansive borrow material such as crusher waste select borrow or
coral
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2374 00 Page 6
The replaced soils should be placed in 9 inch maximum loose lifts and
compacted to obtain a minimm 90 of its maximm dry density per ASIM Test
Method D 1557 78 The footings can then be placed on the above compacted
structural fill material
An allowable bearing value of about 3 000 p s f can be used for
footings bearing on underlying dense sand formation or on the above
recompacted fill
For the above design spread footing foundation it is estimated that
footing settlements are on the order of less than one inch total and
one half of an inch differential
If a footing is located next to a utility line it should extend to
the bottom of the utility trench to reduce footing settlement due to
settlement of the trench backfill
Surface water should be diverted away from the foundation This is
to avoid ponding of surface run off water near the building slab area
which may affect the subgrade soils
Slab On Grade
In order to reduce the expansive effects of the soils we recommend
the slab be placed over a minimum 1 foot thick capping layer of
non expansive material
The expansive clayey silt below the slab subgrade and 1 foot outside
the slab perimeter should be removed for a minimum depth of one foot below
the outside finished grade and replaced with non expansive off site borrow
materials such as select borrow crusher waste or coral compacted toa
minimum 90 of its maximm dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 We
recommend the upper 6 inches of the clayey silt subgrade soil beneath the
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2374 00 Page 7
1 foot minimum over excavation should be scarified moisture conditioned
and recompacted to 90 of its maximm dry density per ASIM Test Method
D 1557 78
A 4 inch layer of 3 Fine cushion fill should be provided below the
slab In order to protect against moisture infiltration a vapor barrier
may be incorporated below the slab
The thickened edge slab footings can bear on the moist recompacted
subgrade soil The thickened edges should be embedded a minimm of 12
inches below the lowest adjacent outside grade
Retaining Wall
It is our understanding that CMU retaining walls may be used for the
elevator shaft The guidelines for retaining wall design are listed
below
1 For lateral earth pressure the following can be used
Walls unrestrained at the top 35 p s f equivalent
active condition level fluid pressure
backfill per foot of depth
Wall restrained at the top 55 p s f equivalent
at rest condition level fluid pressure
backfill per foot of depth
An allowable bearing value of 3 000 p s f is recommended for
foundations bearing on the underlying dense sand or compacted
fill
2 A passive earth resistance of 250 p s f per foot of depth may be
used for the wall footing on level areas The passive pressure
must be reduced for walls on slopes
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2374 00 Page 8
A base friction factor of 0 35 may also be used to determine the
resistance against sliding of the wall
The wall foundations on relatively flat areas should be embedded
a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade
The wall foundations located next to sewer drainage easements
should be embedded below a one and a half horizontal to one
vertical plane extending upward from the bottom edge of
the utility trench or as deep as the inverts for the utility
lines
Backfills directly behind the retaining wall should consist of
granular non expansive material such as select borrow crusher
waste or 3B Fine gravel The top 12 inches of backfill should
consist of non expansive impervious material to reduce water
infiltration behind the wall
A subsurface drainage system subdrains or weepholes should be
utilized behind the walls Subdrains should be properly
constructed to drain water away from the wall
Site Grading
Approximately 1 foot of fill is anticipated to the project site We
recommend that fills and backfills within the top 12 inches minimum
below the on grade slab cushion fill should consist of non expansive soil
such as select borrow crusher waste or coral Where cut or minimal fill
is anticipated the on site soil should be over excavated to provide a
12 inch minimum thickness of non expansive select fill capping underneath
the slab s cushion layer
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2374 00 Page 9
Prior to construction the building areas should be cleared of
vegetation and debris The upper 6 inches of the exposed subgrade beneath
the fill backfill areas should be scarified moisture conditioned and
recompacted to 90 of its maximm dry density per ASIM Test Method
D 1557 78
All imported fill materials should be tested and approved by the
soils engineer prior to being used in the site
The fills should be placed in horizontal lifts not exceeding 9 inches
in loose thickness and compacted to a minimm of 90 of its maximum dry
density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
The site should be graded to provide drainage of surface run off away
from all foundations
Water Feature
It is our understanding that new water features will be added to the
existing one We recommend that impervious liner should be provided for
ponds in the existing and new water features to prevent possible water
seepage into the foundation soils causing subsequent heaving bearing
problems Recommendations provided in the following Drainage section
should also be followed
Drainage
The finished grade outside the slab footings and water features
should be shaped to shed water away from foundations and to avoid ponding
condition Also it is advised that a gutter system be installed around
the building area and that gutter water be diverted far away from the
building foundation and driveway Excessive landscape watering near
foundations should also be avoided
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2374 00 Page 10
Planters next to the foundation should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance
of the above recommended foundations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of
strength The foundation excavation should be properly backfilled tight
against the slab footing immediately after setting of the concrete to
prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained
to drain all surface run off away from the slab foundation
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written conments
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein The
geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for misinterpretation
of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during grading and foundation
phases of the work This is to observe compliance with the design
concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow design changes in
the event that subsurface conditions differ from that anticipated prior to
start of construction The recommendations given in this report are
contingent upon such observations
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2374 00 Page 11
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
upon information obtained from the borings Variations of conditions
between the borings may occur and the nature and extent of these
variations may not become evident until construction If variations then
appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations
given in this report
The locations of the borings were determined by taping from
structures indicated on the topographic survey Elevations of the borings
were approximately determined by interpolation from elevation points on
the topographic survey map The physical location and elevation of the
borings should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the
method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
boring points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types and
as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross sections are based upon interpolation between borings
and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
Water level data from the boring points were collected at the times
given on the graphic representations and or in the text of this report
These data have been reviewed and interpretations made in the formulation
of this report However it must be noted that fluctuation may occur due
to variations in rainfall tides temperature and other factors
