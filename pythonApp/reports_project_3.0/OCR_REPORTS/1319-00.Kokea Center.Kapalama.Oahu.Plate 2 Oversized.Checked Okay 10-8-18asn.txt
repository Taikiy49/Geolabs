INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of a foundation investigation
prepared for the proposed Kokea Center project to be located along the
eastern side of Kokea Street south of Dillingham Boulevard in Kapalama
Oahu Hawaii The general vicinity of the project site is shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1
PURPOSE AND SCOPE OF SERVICES
The purpose of this investigation is to explore the subsurface
conditions and provide soils and foundation design recommendations for the
proposed development
The scope of investigation included drilling and sampling 11 borings
laboratory testing of samples obtained engineering evaluation of the
field and laboratory data developing design recommendations and report
preparation

Detailed description of our field exploration logs of borings and
laboratory tests results are presented in the appendices of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATION
It is our understanding that the proposed Kokea Center will cover an
area of about 2 9 acres and will be developed in at least two increments
The first increment construction will include demolishing the southern
most existing building and constructing 3 new steel framed buttler type
warehouse structures Buildings A B and C The proposed layout of these
structures are presented on the Site Plan Plate 2 For the remaining
increments the existing metal buildings in the northern half of the site
will be demolished and new warehouse structures constructed
The design foundation loading will vary up to 21 kips per column for
Building A and 41 kips per column for Buildings B and C A CMU wall is
planned for the portion of Building A along the southern property line
The CMU wall loading would be about 2 1 kips per foot
It is our understanding that the finished floor grade for Building A
would be at elevation 6 0 feet and Buildings B and C would be at elevation
5 5 feet Therefore 2 to 2 5 feet of fill will be required over the
southern portion of the site where these structures will be located
In addition the existing Kokea Street will be improved with curb and
gutter on the project side
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 1319 00 Page 3
SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is relatively level It has an elongated
trapezoidal shape and extends southerly from Dillingham Boulevard along
the eastern side of Kokea Street for about 850 feet to the existing City
and County Building Department Maintenance Shop on the southern side
The northern half of the site is conjested with existing structures
including a one and two story concrete masonry building at the inter
section of Dillingham Boulevard and Kokea Street and two large metal
framed warehouse type structures along Kokea Street In addition there
are at least two buried fuel tanks some underground structures such as
cesspools drains and other utility lines
The southern half of the site is a contractor s open storage yard
Several concrete slabs are located at the site including those in the
existing buildings It is our understanding that the slabs in the
southern half of the site were originally a part of the previous concrete
precasting facility that once occupied the general area
Regional Geology
The project site is located on the Honolulu Coastal Plain which is
composed primarily of interbedded terrestrial and marine sediments
During geologic history this plain had undergone several sequences of
erosion and deposition as a result of glacio eustatic sea level changes
during the Pleistocene
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Page 4
Due to its proximity to Kapalama Stream these sequences have had a
significant effect upon the formation of the project site During high
stands of the sea the area was a lagoon marsh or estuary area subjected
to low energy deposition of marine and terrestrial sediments During low
stands of the sea portions of these sediments were eroded away
Following the last low stand of the sea Koko at minus 15 feet
elevation the area was gradually filled with soft and loose sediments in
a lagoonal marsh environment The area was filled during land reclamation
projects during the early part of the century
Subsurface Conditions
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and
sampling 11 borings at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan
The depths of the borings varied from 1 5 to 40 feet below the existing
ground surface
In general the subsurface conditions across the site are relatively
similar and consisted of the following units
A 3 to 4 foot thick of surface fill layer mostly of dense coral sand
and gravel with occasional pocket of stiff clayey silt
0 to 2 feet of soft to medium stiff organic silt and clay of marsh
deposits occurring in infrequent patches
8 to 12 feet of loose gray sand and gravel of lagoonal deposits which
graded to more clayey and silty with depth The deposits are thinner
under the northern half of the site
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1319 00 Page 5
5 to 7 feet of stiff to very stiff dark brown clayey silt of alluvial
deposit
2 to 6 feet of stiff to very stiff brown to gray calcareous silty
clay with sand seams probably of marine origin
Underlying the above units was a layer of loose tan to brown coral
sand and gravel with some cementation to 40 feet the maximum depth
explored
Groundwater Level
Groundwater was generally encountered in the borings at about sea
level The depths to the groundwater level measured in the borings are
listed and shown on the logs of borings The groundwater levels
fluctuated somewhat with tide changes and should be influenced by the
adjacent Kapalama Stream water levels
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDAT IONS
General
The project site is underlain by loose compressible sandy lagoonal
deposits Settlements should be anticipated due to the new fill and
building loads However we believe that the majority of the settlements
would occur rapidly because of the predominantly granular nature of the
compressible deposits To induce ground settlements to occur first prior
to foundation construction we recommend that the required 2 to 2 5 feet
of new fill be placed as soon as possible
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1319 00 Page 6
The building construction should be delayed until most of the
settlements due to the site filling have occurred about 1 to 2 months
Then spread footing foundations may be used for the building support with
allowance for some settlements due to the structural loads If the
anticipated settlements are not tolerable pile foundations may have to be
considered
Our recommendations are discussed in detail and presented in the
following sections
Settlements
Based on the findings we believe that some settlements will occur
due to 1 the weight of the new fill 2 the building loading and 3
additional settlements may develop if a heavy warehouse floor loading is
applied
The compressible strata consist mainly of loose gravelly and silty
sand Therefore we anticipate that settlements in most areas would
develop quickly probably the majority occurring within one to two months
after loading is applied In areas underlain by pockets or lenses of soft
organic silts and clays such as the areas in 8 10 and 11
a portion of the settlements would develop more Slowly after the majority
of the settlements are complete
GEOLABS HAWAII
The anticipated areal settlements due to the 2 to 2 5 feet of new
fill are estimated to range from 2 to 4 inches Additional areal
settlements under warehouse loading are about 1 to 2 inches under 150
surcharge It is our estimate that the slower occurring settlements from
pockets of organic silts and clays may range from one half to one inch
under the new fill
Settlements under foundations are discussed under the FOUNDATION
section
We recommend that the settlement of the fill be monitored to
determine when the majority of the settlement has occurred and when
building construction can begin
If it is desired to reduce future settlements from the building and
floor loads a surcharge fill program may be utilized We would be glad
to consult with you on such a program
Foundation Support
Based on our past experience we believe that shallow spread footing
may be considered for the foundation support of the proposed warehouse
structures Although some settlements will result these structures are
generally flexible to tolerate moderate amounts of settlements Where the
settlement of foundation cannot be tolerated pile foundation should be
considered

Spread Footings

In general spread footings should be placed at a level as high as
possible to reduce the footing load applied over the underlying
compressible deposits It is recommended however that the footing
bottom be embedded at least 12 inches below the lowest adjacent
grade A maximum net allowable soil bearing pressure of 2000 p s f
may be used for the design Foundations should not be placed below
about elevation 3 feet or special design may be required due to the
close proximity of the soft subsoils and water level
The anticipated footing settlement under 41 kips of column loading
may range from three fourths of an inch to 1 5 inches It is
probable that some footings would be underlain by organic silt or
clay lens beneath the fill For these footings we believe that
settlements would be slightly more and occur slowly
It is recommended that the actual settlement at each major column be
monitored prior to roof construction so that appropriate adjustments
could be at a later date Provisions should be made so that these
columns can be adjusted as deemed necessary
The wal foundation for the 20 foot high CMU wall should be at least
2 feet wide and embedded at least 12 inches below the lowest adjacent
grade

The settlements under the CMU wall will probably range from 1 to 2
inches with differential settlements of about three fourths of an
inch over a 15 foot span
Pile Foundation
For pile foundation support a 12 inch square concrete pile extending
15 feet into the lower very stiff clayey or coral layer will develop
an allowable bearing capacity of 40 tons per pile This pile should
be driven to a final driving resistance of 65 blows per foot for the
last two feet or 8 blows per inch with a hammer delivering a rated
energy of 15 000 foot pounds For cost estimate and comparison
purposes an estimated average pile length of about 40 feet may be
used
Prior to production pile driving 6 test piles of 45 feet long should
be driven to establish more accurately the order lengths and final
driving resistance criteria
The piles should be spaced no closer than three pile diameters
otherwise group action may reduce the pile capacity The driving
criteria may be modified during construction if excessive resistance
is encountered

Site Preparation
The overall site preparation will include removal of any vegetation
debris cesspools and their organic matter and other structures The
existing concrete slabs if situated within 12 inches of finished grades
should be removed to provide a more uniform subgrade support of pavements
and slabs Further removal of concrete slabs below this depth will be
dictated by other considerations such as footing and utility
constructions
The resulting excavations should be backfilled with the on site
granular material placed in 9 inch loose lifts and compacted to at least
90 of the ASTM D 1557 78 maximum density
The existing ground in the Kokea Street improvement area should be
scarified to a depth of 6 inches after clearing and grubbing moisture
conditioned and compacted to a density of at least 95 of the ASTM
D 1557 78 maximum density
Fill Materials
Imported fi11 will be required to raise the grades of the site For
the anticipated project use the imported materials may consist of the
following
GEOLABS HAWAII

The material below the slab or pavement base course but within 16
inches of the finished grade should consist of select borrow type of
material as required in Section 30 of the Standard Specifications for
Public Works Construction City County of Honolulu May 1975
The material below the top 16 inches may consist of the general
borrow type material as required in Section 16 of the abovementioned
standard specifications
For Kokea Street improvement to minimize the thickness of the
pavement section the fill under the pavement section should consist of
select borrow material
Fill Placement
All fills and backfills should be placed in horizontal lifts not
exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a dry density of
at least 95 of the maximum ASTM D 1557 78 maximum density Cohesive
materials if used below the top 16 inches plane may be compacted to 90
minimum
For Kokea Street construction the required compaction should be 95
minimum for all materials as the required fill thickness will probably be
less than 1 5 feet

Warehouse Slab
Based on the projected settlements flexible A C pavement would be
appropriate for the proposed warehouse in lieu of concrete slabs The
following pavement section may be used for designing the new warehouse
slab pavement
2 Inch Asphalt Concrete
6 Inch Base Course
8 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
The base course should be crushed rock or other granular material
with a CBR of 85 and conforming with Section 31 2 of the abovementioned
Standard Specifications or equivalent The subgrade soil which is
assumed to be the select borrow type material for at least the top 6
inches should be compacted to a minimum of 95 compaction
If concrete slab is utilized it should be designed and constructed
to accommodate future ground settlements and to control crack pattern
Pavements
The project will also include improving the property side of Kokea
Street The existing roadway pavements apparently were constructed at
different times and are showing various degree of deterioration However
no deep seated pavement failure was noted in most areas It is
recommended that deteriorated section of the pavements be broken down
before new pavement is constructed
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1319 00 Page 13
In general the subgrade materials at Kokea Street appear to consist
of mixtures of dense silty coral sand and gravel
The following pavement section may be used for design of the roadway
5 Inch Asphalt Concrete
Q Inch Base Course 95 compaction
5 Inch Total Pavement ickness
The subgrade soil should be scarified to a depth of 6 inches
moisture conditioned and compacted to 95 compaction
The above pavement section should be used in the main traffic lanes
of the Kokea project site For lighter passenger automobile pavements
the asphalt concrete thickness may be reduced to 2 inches
Utility Lines
The underground utility lines should be placed as late as possible
after the completion of site grading to minimize post installation
settlements In addition flexible joints should be considered
particularly the connections at building should have a higher degree of
flexibility to allow differential settlements
The trench excavation would require proper shoring particularly if
patches of soft organic clays or silts are encountered Since dewatering
may affect adjacent structures a soils engineer should be consulted if
water level is to be lowered
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Page 14
The backfilling of trench should be consistent with the materials and
compaction requirements recommended under FILL CONSTRUCTION section
Generally for backfilling the utility trenches the compacted
density of the backfill from the pipe bed up to 12 inches above the pipe
should be a minimum of 85 of ASTM D 1557 78 The remaining portion
should be 90 If trenches are located under roadways the top 16 inches
of the backfill from the finished grade should be compacted to 95 minimum
Design Review
Plans and specifications for foundation and pavement should be
forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written comments prior to
construction to see that the intent of our recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
The earthwork operation including proofrolling and fill placement
should be monitored by a soils engineer during the early stage of site
grading in order to detect soft pockets and determine their removal
We recommend that the footing excavations be inspected by the soils
engineer prior to placement of concrete or reinforcing steel to check that
adequate bearing has been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are
consistent with the findings of our subsurface investigation
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1319 00 Page 15
If the structural columns are to be supported on a pile foundation
the pile driving operations should be monitored by a qualified soils
engineer to verify that the piles have been driven adequately and in
accordance with our recommendations
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above inspections

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling
11 borings and obtaining 4 bags of surface samples at the approximate
locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 A truck mounted drilling
rig was used to drill the borings
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by visual
and textural examination in the field by one of our engineers who
continuously monitored the drilling operations The classifications
were further reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the
laboratory The soils were classified in general conformance with the
Unified Soil Classification System A graphic presentation of the
materials encountered is presented on the Log of Borings Plates A 1 1
to A 11 1
Soil samples were obtained by driving a 2 inch standard split
hammer free falling 30 inches The blow counts to drive the sampler
for 12 inches are shown on the log of borings at the appropriate
sample depths

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content and unit weight determinations were performed
on selected samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of
soil properties The results of these tests are presented on the log
of borings at the appropriate sample depths
To further identify soil characteristics additional tests were
performed These include 2 Atterberg Limit tests and 7 Sieve
Analyses The results of these tests are presented on Plates B 1
through B 3
Two moisture density relationship tests modified Proctor were
performed on bag samples of the surface materials to determine their
compaction characteristics The test results are summarized on Plates
B 3
One laboratory CBR test was performed on a bag sample of the
existing surface soil to evaluate its subgrade supporting
characteristics The test results are summarized on Plate B 3
Three direct shear tests were performed on undisturbed samples
to evaluate the strength characteristics of the subsurface soil The
test results are presented on Plates A 4 through A 6
Three consolidation tests were performed on selected
undisturbed samples to evaluate the settlement characteristics of
subsoils The test results are presented on Plates B 7 through B 9
One unconfined compression test was performed on an undisturbed
sample to further evaluate soil strength characteristics The test
results are presented on Plate B 10
