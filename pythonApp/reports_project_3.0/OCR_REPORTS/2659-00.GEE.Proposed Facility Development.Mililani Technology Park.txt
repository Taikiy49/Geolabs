INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed facility development in the Mililani Technology Park Mililani
on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location of the site and its vicinity is shown
on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The exploration was performed on March 25 and
26 1991 in accordance with our proposal dated March 4 1991
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2659 00 Page 2
This report summarizes our findings resulting from the field exploration and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analyses based on the data
obtained during the exploration and laboratory tests These recommendations are
intended for design input and are not intended for final construction estimation and
bidding purposes The contents of this report pertain only to Lot 13049 which comprises
approximately four and one third 4 3 acres within the Mililani Technology Park The area
is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The findings and recommendations presented herein
should not be considered applicable to any other lot in the same vicinity within or outside
the proposed Industrial Park
PROJECT CONSIDERATION
The project site is located near and to the west of the H 2 Interstate Highway in the
central portion of the island of Oahu Based on the preliminary information available the
proposed development wouid occupy only a portion of the thirty nine 39 acre parcel
it is anticipated that the proposed development will consist of a light one and two story
structure approximately 36 800 square feet plan area New utility lines and asphalt
pavements for the parking areas are also planned for the proposed development
Site grading work involving cut and fill is anticipated for the project in order to
achieve suitably graded area for the proposed construction
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions at the project site in order to develop a generalized subsurface data set for the

SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed project site is located in the Mililani Technology Park to the south
of Leilenua Golf Course on the island of Oahu Hawaii The current development area is
located on Lot No 13049 at the northeast corner of Akamainui and Wikao Streets
Currently the site is undeveloped and covered with tall grasses in some areas The
terrain at the site slopes gently to the north at a gradient of about four 4 percent The
existing ground elevations range from about 810 feet southern portion to 834 feet
northern portion above Mean Sea Level MSL
The site and the proposed development are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling six 6 borings to
depths of about 16 5 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate locations
of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The field exploration indicates that the site is underlain by very stiff to hard silty clay
and clayey silt residual soil and saprolite to at least the full depth of the borings drilled
for this exploration These soils developed in residual materials were derived from basic
igneous rock
Groundwater was not encountered in the drilled borings at the time of our field
exploration Seepage was however noted at about fifteen 15 to sixteen 16 feet below
the existing ground surface in Boring No 6

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration generally encountered very stiff to hard silty clay and clayey
silt extending to at least a depth of 16 5 feet below the existing ground surface It is our
opinion that the stiff on site soils may provide adequate bearing support for the proposed
structure and pavements
Our laboratory testing indicates that the in situ materials have low to moderate
expansion potential when saturated from an air dried condition Therefore slab on grade
construction may be used if the slab subgrades are kept moist and prevented form drying
prior to placement of the overlying cushion fill and concrete
Site Grading
Based on a project Site Plan dated May 8 1991 provided to us site grading work
involving up to approximately 4 feet of cut and 6 feet of fill is anticipated to be required
for development of the project
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
cleared of vegetation debris rubbish and other deleterious materials These materials
should be removed and disposed of properly off site
Area designated to receive fill and subgrades of slabs and pavements should be
scarified to a depth of six 6 inches moisture conditioned to about 2 wet of optimum
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2659 00 Page 6
moisture prevented from drying and recompacted to a minimum of 90 of the maximum
dry density as determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 Any loose or soft spots
encountered should be over excavated down to the firm subsoils and the resulting
depression backfilled with properly compacted imported non expansive granular material
Imported fill material should consist of non expansive select granular materials less
than three 8 inches in maximum size with less than 20 passing the 200 sieve Any
imported fill material should be tested and approved by the geotechnical engineer prior
to delivery to the site
The fill and or backfill should be placed in maximum 10 inch thick loose level lifts
and compacted to a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density as determined by
ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
The site should be graded to drain water away from the foundations
Foundations
Spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the proposed structure If
loose or soft areas are encountered in the footing excavations they should be
overexcavated and moisture conditioned and recompacted to a minimum of 90 of the
maximum dry density as determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
An allowable bearing value of 3000 p s f may be used for footings bearing on
imported select fill material which has been properly compacted or on recompacted
natural ground The bearing value is for total dead plus live loads and may be increased
by one third wnen temporary wind and seismic ioads are considered
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2659 00 Page 7
Individual footings and continuous wall footings should be embedded a minimum
of eighteen 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade Uplift wind seismic
or other loading conditions may require that this minimum footing embedment be
increased
Estimated settlements are anticipated to be on the order of less than one half inch
total and one fourth inch differential
If foundations are located next to utility trenches or easements they should be
embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane extending
upward form the bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility
lines to reduce the possibility of footing settlement due to insufficient trench backfill
compaction
The bottom of all footing excavations should be observed by the geotechnical
engineer prior to placement of the reinforcement to determine if adequate bearing has
been obtained and that loose soils are removed prior to placement of the concrete
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive earth pressure
developed against the buried vertical faces of the foundation system and by frictional
resistance developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be calculated using an equivalent fluid
pressure of 250 p s i per foot of depth This assumes that the soil around the foundation
is properly compacted A coefficient of friction of 0 4 between the bottom of the footings
and the properly prepared subgrade may be used Unless covered by pavements or
slabs the passive resistance in the upper 18 inches of soii should be neglected

Utility Trenches
New utility lines are anticipated to be required for the project A granular bedding
consisting of six 6 inches of 3B Fine gravel or C 33 No 67 is recommended under the
pipes
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2659 00 Page 10
Granular materials such as the above 3B Fine gravel should be used for the
trench backfill up to about one foot above the pipes to reduce the possibility of damaging
the pipes
The upper portion of the subsequent trench backfill from a level one foot above the
pipes to the top of subgrade or finished grade should be placed in maximum 10 inch
horizontal lifts mechanically compacted to a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry
density as determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 to minimize future ground
subsidence Below pavement areas the subgrade level compaction criteria should be
increased to 95
Drainage
The finished grade outside the building should be shaped to shed water away from
foundations and to avoid ponding conditions Also it is suggested that gutter systems
be installed around the building and that the discharge be diverted away from the
foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near foundations and
slabs should also be avoided
Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have concrete bottoms and
drains to prevent water form entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the above
foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause excessive subsurface soil
saturation and subsequent heaving and loss of strength The foundation excavations
should be properly backfilled tight against the wall of slab footing immediately after setting
of the concrete to prevent development of ponding
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2659 00 Page 11
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and maintained to drain
all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundations
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be forwarded to the
geotechnical engineer for review and written comments prior to advertisement for bidding
This review is needed to determine adherence to the earthwork foundation and pavement
recommendations given herein If this review is not made the geotechnical engineer
cannot assume responsibility for misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during site grading and construction of the foundations
This is to observe compliance with design concepts specifications or recommendations
and to expedite suggestions for design changes that may be required in the event that
subsurface conditions differ from those anticipated prior to the start of construction The
recommendations given in this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material that may be required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed soil conditions encountered during construction are different from
those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to the design
should be made

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from field borings Variations of conditions between the field borings
may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident until
construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re
evaluate the recommendations given in this report
The location of the field borings indicated in this report are approximate having
been determined by taping from features shown on the Topographic Map by Towill
Shigeoka Associates Incorporated dated March 13 1991 Elevations of the field
borings were estimated by interpolation from elevation contours on the same plan The
physical locations and elevations of the field borings should be considered accurate only
to the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the field borings depict
the approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual
transition
