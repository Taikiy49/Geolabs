general subsurface soils and rock conditions at the project site in order
to obtain data for the formation of preliminary geotechnical engineering
recommendations pertinent to the design of the project site grading
proposed building foundations pavements and infrastructure including the
on site disposal of sanitary wastes

GENERAL SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located in the town of Waimea Kamuela South
Kohala on the island of Hawaii The project site is bounded by the
proposed Carter Professional Center to the southwest Waikoloa Stream to
the northwest an existing office apartment complex to the northeast and
Mamalahoa Highway to the southeast
The topography of the site is generally level except for the northern
corner where small hills and depressions were observed Most of the
site s ground surface is covered with a thick mat of Waimea grass
However the northern portion of the proposed commercial center site is
vegetated with a dense growth of eucalyptus trees and underbrush
Several old concrete and masonry foundations were noted at various
locations on the site Discussions with local residents indicate that the
site was formerly used as part of an army encampment during World War IT
and most recently as the site of a small hotel In view of these previous
uses there is a possibility that there could be abandoned cesspools and
buried rubbish on the site

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The project site is underlain by basaltic lava flows of the Hamakua
Volcanic Series of Mauna Kea which were deposited during the Pleistocene
Stearns and Macdonald 1946
The Hamakua series is typically composed of basaltic lava flows which
are mantled by a surface layer of palagonitized ash locally referred to
as Pahala Ash The ash soil at the project sites differs from Pahala Ash
found in other portions of the island of Hawaii in that the in situ
moisture content is generally lower and the soil exists in a friable
state
Based on previous experience in the Waimea area the lava flows
underlying the sites are in the A a form which is characterized by a
rough spiny or rubbly surface of fragmental clinker underlain by a dense
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2396 00 B Page 5
core of massive basalt which frequently has a thinner less continuous
layer of clinker below the massive core The clinker surface of the lavas
at the site have been mantled by the Pahala Ash as discussed above
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Based on our exploration the subsurface conditions throughout the
site appear relatively uniforn
The surface soils to a depth of about fifteen 15 feet below the
existing ground surface consist of yellowish brown to reddish brown medium
stiff to stiff silt soils Pahala Ash These soils are predominately
dry low plasticity silts which contain occasional gravel and cobbles
The silts have very little cohesion and once exposed is friable and
subject to wind and water erosion
Based on our exploration the ash soils generally grade into a layer
of volcanic clinker at about fifteen 15 feet below the existing ground
surface This clinker material consists predominantly of cobble and
gravel sized basalt fragments intermixed with some ash and represents the
original surface of A a flows this clinker stratum extended to at least
the maximum depths explored of 16 5 feet below the existing ground
surface
Groundwater was not encountered in the drilled borings at the time of
our exploration

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
From a geotechnical engineering viewpoint our exploration indicates
the site is suitable for the proposed development
The in situ ash soils encountered at the project sites are generally
in a moderately dense state After additional compaction they should
provide adequate bearing to support a spread footing type of foundation
system for the proposed commercial development
Our percolation testing indicates moderate percolation rates within
the ashy soils It is our opinion that on site disposal of primary
treated wastewater is feasible depending upon the amount of wastewater
generated and the amount of space available for the construction of the
Seepage trenches
Wastewater Disposal
The project is located in an unsewered area and there is no known
regional facilities plan scheduled to be implemented in the foreseeable
future Therefore it will be necessary to use on site treatment and
disposal for sanitary wastes generated within the development area It is
our understanding that it is currently planned to use filtration trenches
with seepage pits as the disposal component of the on site wastewater
systems Ideally the disposal components should provide a connection to
the underlying clinker layer
Based on our field percolation tests and the methods given in Public
Health Service Publication No 526 the wastewater disposal system should
be designed for the application of no more than 1 6 gallons per day of
primary treated wastewater per square foot of trench bottom Therefore
approximately 500 square feet of trench bottom would be required for the
disposal of 800 gallons of wastewater per day
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2396 00 B Page 7
It is our understanding that the County of Hawaii ordinances require
the on site disposal of run off water generated at new development site
The usual County standard is to design for the disposal of six 6 to ten
10 cubic feet per second per drywell or other type of disposal
component
It is our opinion that a filtration trench system within the upper
silty ash soils to handle flows of that magnitude would be prohibitive in
terms of land area required It appears that a more feasible approach
would be to install deep drywells into the underlying basalt clinker zone
to handle large storm water flows
Our current percolation testing and the shallow depth borings drilled
for the preliminary geotechnical engineering exploration did not provide
sufficient data for the design of such drywells or the required data for
the preparation of the required Department of Health permits for the
drywells We recommend that additional deeper boring exploration and
larger flow injection testing be performed to obtain these data
Site Grading
In general it is our opinion that conventional grading methods may
be used for the site grading of the proposed development The proposed
development is still in the conceptual stages and detailed grading plans
were not available at the time of our exploration However minimal cuts
and fills are anticipated The following grading recommendations are
presented as guidelines for the design of the site grading for the
proposed project
Site Preparation
Prior to commencement of grading surface vegetation and organic
matter within the contract grading limits should be cleared and
grubbed The resulting spoils should be disposed of properly
off site or stockpiled for use in landscaping
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2396 00 B Page 8
Generally the Pahala Ash soils generally have relatively low
strength and if the in situ moisture contents are high enough they
are thixotropic i e they lose strength when remolded The ash
soils encountered at the project site are ina relatively dry friable
state The near surface soil has an average moisture content in the
mid 40 per cent range Therefore it is our opinion that the soils
will need to be further wetted or moisture conditioned for
workability in the project site grading work Our laboratory
compaction test indicated an optimum moisture content in the range of
about 55 percent However the moisture conditioning should be
carefully controlled to avoid over wetting of the soils
It is also advisable to institute a dust control program during
grading operations
Soft or yielding areas encountered during clearing and grubbing
should be over excavated to firm natural material and the resulting
depression backfilled with select on site or imported fill compacted
to a minimum of 90 maximm dry density per ASTM Test Method
D 1557 78
Any existing miscellaneous rubbish fill encountered should be removed
and wasted off site
Areas designated to receive fill should be scarified to a depth of 12
inches moisture conditioned to near optimum moisture content and
compacted to a minimm of 90 maximum dry density per ASTM Test
Method D 1557 78
Fill Placement
In general the on site ash soils with any rock particles less than
six 6 inches in size if properly moisture conditioned may be
suitable as fill material
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2396 00 B Page 9
Generally fills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 12
inches in loose thickness and compacted to 90 maximm dry density as
established by ASTM D 1557 78 Fill material should be moisture
conditioned by the addition of sufficient water to allow the silty
soils to be compacted into a dense stable mass without causing
pumping or liquefaction
Any fills to be placed on slopes having ratios steeper than five
horizontal to one vertical 5H 1V should be keyed and benched into
the existing ground formation to lessen potential instability of the
new fill see Plate 3 The keys and benches should be based in firm
native soil
Provided the on site silty ash soils are utilized for embankment and
other fills we recommend that flatter design fill slopes of three
horizontal to one vertical 3H 1V be used in grading design These
silty soils are easily eroded and should be protected or planted as
soon as practicable

Foundation Support
For the design spread or strip footings we recommend that an
allowable bearing capacity of 2 5000 p s f be utilized This value may
be increased by one third when temporary wind and seismic loads are

considered The bottom of the footings should be embedded a minimim of
two 2 feet below the lowest outside or adjacent grade
In order to provide uniform Support for the foundations we reconmend
that the footing subgrade be over excavated to a depth of eight 8 inches
below the bottom of footing grade and this material replaced with select
granular material such as base course compacted to a minimm of 90
maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 The over excavated
area should extend a minimm of one 1 foot outside the footing edges
If any soft or weak areas are encountered in the over excavation
they should be remedied by further over excavation of the soft and or weak
materials to firm natural soils and backfilling the resultant void with
select on site soil placed in nine 9 to ten 10 inch lifts anda
compacted to 90 maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
For foundations designed in accordance with the above
recommendations we estimate total and differential settlements on the
order of three eights 3 8 of an inch

ground floors of the proposed buildings We recommend a minimm 4 inch

cushion A moisture barrier membrane should be incorporated between the
Slab and the underlying cushion layer to protect floor coverings
The thickened edges of the slabs should extend a minimm of twelve

Excavation
The field exploration indicates that the site is underlain by medium
stiff to stiff silty ash soils to depths of about fifteen 15 feet below
the existing ground surface These soils exist in a relatively dry
friable state
It is anticipated that the ash soils may be conventionally
excavated Some hard excavation may be encountered in excavations deeper
than about fifteen 15 feet below the existing ground surface
Since the subsoils encountered at the site are in a dry friable
state it is anticipated that there will be a tendency for the sidewalls
of excavations to unravel or slough when exposed Therefore shoring or
bracing should be considered to comply with the applicable health and
safety regulations The design and implementation of the shoring and or
bracing should be left to the contractor

Design Review
The project geotechnical engineer should also be retained to review

If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as borings
probes test pits and or observation wells Variations of conditions
between the field data points may occur and the nature and extent of
these variations may not become evident until construction If variations
then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations given in this report
The locations of the field data points were approximately determined
by triangulation and measurement from reference points shown on the
preliminary plans provided to us Elevations of the data points were not
available at the time of our exploration The physical location and
elevation of the field data points should be considered accurate only to
the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines show in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross sections are based upon interpolation between field data
points and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
Water level data from the field data points was collected at the
times given on the graphic representations and or in the text of this
report This data has been reviewed and interpretations made in the
formulation of this report However it must be noted that fluctuation
May occur due to variations in rainfall tides temperature and other
factors
