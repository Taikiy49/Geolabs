Predrilling Consideration
Based on the field exploration results we understand that the project site is
generally underiain by surficial soft loose man made fill and lagoonal deposits over
a hard coral ledge at an elevation of about 7 feet Mean Sea Level MSL In
order to develop the required lateral load capacity for the foundation system and to
avoid the possibility of supporting a large number of piles on a thin coral ledge it is
our opinion that the driven piles should extend through this shallow coral ledge and
end bearing on the very dense coralline materials underlying the site at greater
depths Therefore we recommend that predrilling at the proposed pile locations
be considered in order to facilitate the pile driving operation through this
obstruction In addition we believe that predrilling would serve to reduce the
anticipated vibration impacts on the daily operations of the adjacent existing
facility If vibration impacts is not a critical consideration for the subject project we
suggest that the indicator piles may be installed without predrilling to evaluate
whether the shallow coral ledge can be punched through by pile driving The
information obtained may then be used to determine the need and depth of
predrilling during the production pile program

Items 2b and 2e Subgrade Preparation
After demolition clearing and grubbing the subgrades should be scarified to a
minimum depth of 10 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture
content and compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction
ltem 2c Number of Pile Load Tests
Since the driven piles will develop their capacity primarily from end bearing on the
very dense coralline materials underlying the site at greater depths we believe that
a total of two pile load tests would be sufficient and adequate to evaluate the
end bearing capacity of the driven piles
Item 2d Load Test Capacity
Based on our evaluation it is our opinion that the pile load test should be
performed in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1143 loading the
test pile to 200 percent of the anticipated design load
