As requested we have made a study of the near surface soil
conditions below the above referenced lot The purpose of the study
was to evaluate the suitability of the near surface soils as
foundation support for the proposed house The study consisted of
drilling three 3 borings within the proposed building site
performing laboratory swell tests and a review of the subdivision
grading plan This report summarizes our observations and
recommendations

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed single family residence consists of a two story
wood structure approximately 44 by 52 feet in plan dimension Based
on the preliminary plans provided to us the garage will be set back
about 23 feet from Kahena Street
Post and beam construction with wood floors will be utilized
for the entire house
Concrete on grade slab is planned for the lower level garage
and driveway
CMU retaining walls generally less than 7 feet high are planned
along the lower level garage perimeters to provide grade separation
between the garage and the finished floor level of the house
Cuts up to 7 feet thick are anticipated to accommodate the
garage level
EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
Lot 51 is located on Kahena Street in the southwestern portion
of the subdivision development about 520 feet northwest of Ainapo
Street It is bounded by Kahena Street on the east and subdivision
lots on the north south and west An existing concrete lined
drainage ditch is located along the western boundary of the lot

Minimal site grading was required for this lot during the

SUBDIVISION LOT GRADING
A review of the subdivision mass grading plan prepared by Park
Engineering Inc dated March 14 1980 indicates that Lot 51 was
originally covered with a large stockpile 20 to 28 feet high of soil
and boulders left over from previous grading operations in the
surrounding areas
During that grading operation the stockpile was removed down
to a depth of 6 feet below the design finished grade and replaced with
compacted available on site soils The available on site soil
consisted of silty clay and clayey silt soils that had been mixed with
expansive adobe clay from the previous stockpile

Based on a review of the subdivison grading plan test borings
and a site reconnaissance Lot 51 is overlain by grayish brown
expansive clay from 2 5 to 7 0 feet thick grading with boulders to the
full depth of our borings

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that most of the lot is
underlain by grayish brown clay fill The laboratory test results on
this material indicate that they have high expansion potential
Therefore for Lot 51 the slab on grade and foundation construction
would require special design procedures in order to reduce the
shrink swell effect of the subsoils
House Foundation
For this lot post and beam construction is highly desirable
and recommended due to the sloping terrain and expansive soil
conditions The post and beam construction generally results in lower
foundation costs and the individual footings can be readily adjusted
should the need occur Where utilized concrete slab on grade should
also be properly designed for expansive soil conditions
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Page 5
For post and beam construction we recommend that the expansive
clays be over excavated down for a depth of 3 feet below the finished
grade and then replaced with non expansive borrow soils such as
crusher waste select borrow or coral compacted to a minimum of 90
of its maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78 The spread footings
can then be placed on the compacted non expansive soil Alternatively
the individual post footing can be extended a minimum depth of 36
inches below the finished grade and backfilled with on site soil
compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density The
footings should be poured in place and the excavation backfilled and
compacted to provide an impervious layer over the footings refer to
the Schematic Section on Plate 2 for details
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f can be used for
footings bearing on the stiff natural ground or compacted fill The
bottom of the footing excavation should be neat and free of loose
soils or debris
If a footing is located next to a utility line it should
extend to the bottom of the utility trench to reduce footing
settlement due to settlement of the trench backfill
Surface water should be diverted away from foundations

Retaining Wall Foundation
Retaining walls generally less than 7 feet high are planned
along the lower level garage
In order to reduce the expansive effects of the existing clay
soils on the wall we recommend that the expansive clays be
over excavated down for a depth of 24 inches below the bottom of the
footing and then replaced with non expansive borrow soils such as
crusher waste or coral compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum
dry density per ASTM D 1557 78 The over excavation and replacement
should extend a minimum distance of 12 inches beyond the footing width
The wall footing should be embedded a minimum depth of 12
inches below the outside finished grade Therefore the total depth
of the above over excavation and replacement would be 3 0 feet below
the outside finished grade refer to the Schematic Section on Plate 3
for details
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f can be used
To minimize excessive swelling pressure that may be exerted
laterally on the retaining wall the expansive adobe clay should be
over excavated for a distance behind the wall equal to the height of
the wall
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 865 84 Page 7
Off site non expansive soil should be used as backfill over
the wall footing and behind the wall
The walls should be designed to resist lateral soil pressure of
35 p c f equivalent fluid pressure
A coefficient of friction of 0 4 between the bottom of the
footing and compacted soil can be used to determine the lateral
resistance of the wall
A subsurface drainage system should be utilized behind the wall
to prevent the build up of hydrostatic pressure
Slab On Grade
Concrete slab on grade is planned for the driveway and garage
area It should be noted that the adobe clay soils are expansive
and unsuitable for direct slab subgrade support
In order to reduce the expansive effects of the clay where
encountered they should be over excavated down to a depth of 3 feet
below the slab bottom and then replaced with non expansive borrow
soils such as select borrow crusher waste or coral The
over excavation and backfilling should extend a minimum of 3 feet
beyond the edge of the slabs refer to the Schematic Section on Plate
4 for details
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 865 84 Page 8
The non expansive borrow soil should be compacted to a minimum
of 90 of its maximum dry density as per ASTM D 1557 78 Similar
over excavation and replacement of the expansive adobe clay soils
are recommended for the lanai slabs if constructed on grade
To further reduce future post construction swelling of the
underlying expansive soils we recommend that the adobe clay below
the capping fill pad be kept moist
A 4 inch layer of base course should be provided below the slab
and compacted to 90 of its maximum dry density In order to protect
against moisture infiltration a moisture barrier may be incorporated
below the slab
Concrete Driveway and Sidewalks
Expansive soils were encountered near the proposed driveway
finished elevation Since the driveway can tolerate some amount of
heaving the adobe clay over excavation and replacement may be
reduced to 2 feet minimum below the bottom of the driveway slab
After moisture conditioning of the exposed adobe clay it should
then be immediately backfilled with a select non expansive borrow
material which would act as a capping over the underlying adobe clay
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Page 9
Where more than 2 feet of fill is planned select non expansive
borrow material should be used to cap over the expansive clay Where
less than 2 feet of new fill is planned over excavation and
replacement of the adobe clay with select non expansive material is
recommended to provide a minimum 2 feet of capping The above capping
requirements should be followed for a minimum distance of 1 foot
beyond the driveway edge
Joints are recommended in the driveway slab at about 8 to
10 foot intervals and at discontinuities in order to allow for some
relative movement of the concrete
For future sidewalks capping of the clay or over excavation
and replacement similar to the driveway is recommended to reduce
heaving caused by the expansive clay soil Over excavation beyond the
edge of the walkway may not be necessary
Site Grading
The existing terrain at the site generally slopes down at about
11 grade
Minor grading is anticipated for most of the area below the
rear portion of the house with cuts up to 7 feet deep planned for the
garage area at the front portion of the lot

The area to be filled should be cleared of vegetation and
debris prior to filling
Fill and backfill should be placed in maximum 8 inch lifts and
compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density established
by ASIM D 1557 78
The finished grade outside the slabs pavements and
foundations should be shaped to avoid ponding conditions and to shed
water away from the structures
Design Review
Plans and specifications for foundations site grading and
utility construction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for
review and written comments prior to construction to see that the
intent of our recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during
earthwork construction particularly the fill placement slope cuts
and to monitor the contractor s grading operations
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be examined
by a soils engineer during construction to see that adequate bearing
has been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent
with the finding of our investigation

Any imported material required should be non expansive tested
and approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon
