INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed golf cart bridge crossing part of the Makakilo Golf Course
development The project site is located on the eastern slopes of Puu Makakilo in the
Ewa District of leeward Oahu Hawaii The general location of the project site is shown
on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering recommenda
tions resulting from the field exploration laboratory testing and engineering analyses
These recommendations are intended for foundation design input and may not be
appropriate to form the basis for preparation of construction cost estimates or for
construction bidding purposes

It is proposed to construct a golf cart bridge with a span of approximately twenty
20 feet as part of the golf course development The proposed bridge will cross over a
future stream an artificial water feature connecting irrigation lakes E and F

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
The island of Oahu is composed of two 2 shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau
built up by the extrusion of basaltic lavas during the late Pliocene and Pleistocene epochs
Puu Makakilo is situated on the southeastern flank of Waianae along the southern rift
zone
Puu Makakilo is a cinder and spatter cone that was built by one or more eruptive
events which produced large quantities of pyroclastic material such as cinder spatter and
ash In some areas welding and or hydrothermal action has fused or cemented the
pyroclastics into weak to strong rock
In addition to the pyroclastics the eruptions produced lava flows of very dense
basalt which are interbedded with the pyroclastic materials Structurally Puu Makakilo
is a heterogeneous mixture of interbedded cinder tuff and dense basalt The units are
discontinuous and difficult to differentiate on an areal basis in the field
In situ chemical weathering of the pyroclastics and basalts has produced a surface
layer of residual soil which mantles rnost of the site This residual soil grades rapidly to
the underlying volcanic materials

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
It is our opinion that the proposed bridge can be supported by shallow footing
foundations bearing on the in situ dense cinder sand and gravel provided that the
recommendations presented herein are followed
Detailed recommendations are discussed in the following sections
Abutment Foundations
The borings drilled at the abutment locations generally encountered a thin surface
soil layer overlying medium dense to very dense cinder Spread footing foundations
bearing on the in situ dense cinder material may be utilized to support the bridge abut
ments
An allowable bearing capacity up to 3 500 p s f may be used for footings bearing
on the in situ dense cinder A minimum footing embedment of two 2 feet is
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2417 60 Page 6
recommended However additional embedment is recommended for footings on or
adjacent to a slope to provide a minimum of ten 10 feet horizontal set back distance
measuring from the outer edge of the footing toe out horizontally to the slope face
If soft or loose soil conditions are encountered at the bottom of the footing
excavation the soft and loose soils should be over excavated to stiff and firm natural
ground The resulting depressions should be backfilled with select borrow coral crusher
waste or base course material compacted to a minimum of 95 percent of the maximum
dry density as determined in accordance with procedures described in ASTM Test
Method D 1557 78
We recommend that the excavated footing subgrade be observed by the project
geotechnical engineer to see if competent foundation support has been obtained and
loose material has been removed prior to placement of concrete
Estimated footing settlements under the anticipated abutment loads are on the
order of less than one 1 inch total and one half 0 5 inch differential

Lateral Resistance
Resistance to lateral loads may be obtained by passive pressure developed by the
earth against the completely buried portions of the abutment walls We recommend that
a passive earth resistance of 350 p c f may be used where the ground in front of the
abutment wall is level If a sloping ground of two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V is in
front of the wall a reduced passive earth resistance of 150 p c f should be used
A coefficient of friction of 0 4 can be used to estimate the frictional resistance
against sliding between the base of the wall and the underlying soil
Slab On Grade
It is our understanding that concrete slabs on grade will be utilized as approach
slabs to the bridge deck of the proposed golf cart bridge crossing
It is recommended that a minimum of six 6 inches of base course material
compacted to a minimum of 95 percent of its maximum dry density as determined in

provided below the concrete slabs The upper six 6 inches of the subgrade should also
be scarified and recompacted to a minimum of 95 percent of its maximum dry density
The approach slabs will be bearing on top of the backfills behind the bridge
abutment walls It is recommended and important that granular backfill material such as
select borrow coral or select on site cinder sand be used behind the abutment walls to
reduce potential backfill settlement and subsequent slab damages The granular backfill

procedures described in ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Erosion Protection
The bridge abutments will be located at low lying areas adjacent to the proposed
stream which will connect the irrigation lakes The banks of the stream will be susceptible
to erosion or scour during periods of high storm runoff Therefore it is our opinion that
some amount of adequately designed stream bed protection should be provided
A minimum four 4 inch thick gunite surface or concrete lining properly reinforced
with welded wire mesh installed in areas susceptible to erosion or scour should be
installed to provide resistance to scouring A six 6 inch layer of filter gravel material is
recommended below the lining to provide a filter layer between the upper concrete lining
and underlying silty soil
The lining should also incorporate cutoff walls along its perimeter in order to
reduce the potential for undermining that may result from scouring below the lining The
cutoff walls should extend a minimum of three 3 feet below the water level and ground
surface

Design Review
Drawings and specifications for the proposed construction should be forwarded
to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comment prior to construction This
review is needed to determine adherence to the earthwork and _ foundation
recommendations given herein If this review is not made the geotechnical engineer
cannot be considered to be responsible for misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during the foundation construction phase of the work
This is to observe compliance with the design concepts specifications or recommenda
tions and to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from
those anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in this report
are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and approved by
the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed soil rock conditions encountered during construction are different
from those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made

underway If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations given in this report
