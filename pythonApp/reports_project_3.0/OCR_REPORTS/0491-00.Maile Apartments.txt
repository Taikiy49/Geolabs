SUMMARY
The proposed 3 story apartment structure can be
supported on footings bearing either on the surface
sand or underlying sand and gravel fill found from
elevation 11 to 13
Bearing pressures of 2500 psf may be used for foot
ings supported on sand and 4500 psf for footings
supported on the compacted sand and gravel fill
INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of a soil and foundation inves
tigation for the proposed project The study consisted of
subsurface borings laboratory testing and engineering analyses
as outlined in our proposal dated August 28 1973 The purposes
of our investigation were to determine subsurface soil condi
tions and to develop recommendations for the proposed apartment
foundation design and site grading
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed project plans are to construct a 3 story semi
circular concrete block wall apartment structure with a parking

PROJECT DESCRIPTION
The proposed site is situated on the Makai side of Farrington
Highway and about 150 feet south of its intersection with St
John s Road in
The topography of the property is generally level with a slight
gradient down toward the northeast corner
The western third portion of the property is beach and will not
be developed The beach slopes down from Elevation 14 feet to
the highwater line The upper eastern portion of the property
is presently overgrown with scattered brush
FIELD EXPLORATION
To explore the subsurface conditions at the site four borings
were drilled to about 20 foot depths at the locations shown on
the Site Plan Plate l
Page 3
W O 491 00
The drilling was accomplished using truck mounted rotary drill
ing equipment with continuous hollow stem auger The borings
were continuously inspected by an engineer from our firm
In all borings Standard Penetration Test was taken at selected
depth intervals The Standard Penetration Test consists of
driving a 2 inch 0 D split spoon sampler 18 inches with a 140
pound hammer free falling a distance of 30 inches The number
of blows required to drive the sampler the last 12 inches is
termed Standard Penetration Resistance N and is an approx
imate measure of the relative density of a soil The resistance
values are plotted on the boring logs Samples obtained were
visually classified in the field and representative material was
placed in air tight containers and returned to our laboratory
for further inspection and testing
LABORATORY TESTING
Selected samples were subjected to various laboratory tests such
as moisture content expansion test and grain size analysis
The results of the laboratory tests are presented on the borings
and in Appendix B

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
GENERAL
The current development plans are to construct a 3 story semi
circular concrete block wall apartment structure with a parking
area located in the eastern front portion of the site
It is our professional opinion that the site soils are capable
of supporting the proposed structure on spread foundations
bearing on the upper sandy soil or lower gravelly fill
FOUNDATIONS
Spread and or continuous footings may be utilized for the pro
posed structure Footings can be founded either on the surface
Page 5
W O 491 00
sand layer or all on the underlying compacted sand and gravel
fill The footing may be designed for the following allowable
soil bearing pressures
Allowable
Footings On Bearing Pressures
Surface Sand 2500 psf
Compacted Sand and Gravel Fill 4500 psf
If footings are supported on the underlying fill material the
foundation and wall may be constructed before filling operations
The bottom of all footing excavations should be moistened and
tamped prior to placing of steel and concrete
Footings should be bottomed at least 24 inches below the adjacent
ground surface and should have a minimum width of 18 inches
Total settlements for the structure should be less than 3 4 inch
with the differential settlement less than 1 4 inch
SITE GRADING
Structural fills up to 4 feet high are proposed The onsite
material is granular and non expansive and will be suitable for
structural fill Because the surface fine sandy material is
dry dusting may be a problem during construction
1 Clearing and Grubbing
All suitable material such as vegetation trash etc should
Page 6
W O 491 00
be removed and wasted from the site
Preparation of Fill Areas
Areas close to finish grade or to receive fill should be
scarified to remove uneven features and should be watered
and recompacted according to the requirements for struc
tural fills
Fill Material
The onsite surface layer of sand and excavated materials
may be utilized for compacted structural fills along with
the underlying sand and gravel material
Imported fill material should be non expansive and must
be approved by the soils engineer prior to placing
Structural Fill Placement
Approved fill material should be placed in 8 inch lifts
and compacted to 90 relative compaction using ASTM D 1557
70 as a standard
Drainage
Provisions for a well designed and maintained site drainage
both during and after completion of the construction must

be considered Water should not be allowed to pond in the
vicinity of the structure
6 Inspection and Testing
Continuous inspection and testing during grading should be
performed under the supervision of a soils engineer to
ensure that these recommendations are followed
CONCRETE FLOOR SLAB ON GRADE
A four inch layer of compacted free draining granular fill
should be placed beneath all floor slabs The site granular
materials may be used for this layer provided it is approved
by the soils engineer The concrete floor slab on grade
should be a minimum of 4 nominal thickness and be reinforced
with a minimum of 6 x 6 10 x 10 welded wire mesh
