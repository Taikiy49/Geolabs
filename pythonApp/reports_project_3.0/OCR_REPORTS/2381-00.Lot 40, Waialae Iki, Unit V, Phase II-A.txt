SUMMARY OF RECOMMENDATTONS
The field exploration disclosed that the lot is
underlain by medium stiff to very stiff silty clay
and clayey silt residual soil overlying a
weathered rock formation
Laboratory testing and previous experience have
demonstrated that the residual soil has moderately
high swelling potential when saturated from a dry
condition
To reduce the effects due to soil swelling under
on grade slabs it is recommended that the site be
capped with a minimum of 16 inches of
non expansive select fill The subgrade soils
should also be protected from drying
The text of this report should be referred to for
detailed and special design recommendations
INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
evaluation performed for the proposed residence at Lot 40 Waialae Iki
Unit V Phase II A on the island of Oahu Hawaii The exploration was
performed in general accordance with our fee proposal dated August 23
1989
This report summarizes our findings from the exploration and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analysis based
on the data obtained during the exploration These recommendations are
intended for design input and are not intended for construction estimation
or bidding purposes

PROJECT CONSTDERATTONS
Based on the plans provided to us the proposed new residence will be
a two 2 story split level structure approximately 58 by 72 feet in plan
dimensions Concrete on grade slab is planned for the structure
Site grading involving cuts up to 7 feet deep and fills up to 6 feet
high are planned A CMU retaining wall about 6 feet high is planned at
the mauka portion of the lot

The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface conditions at the project site in order to develop a
generalized soil rock data set for the formulation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertinent to site grading and foundation
design of the project
The work was done in general accordance with our confirming proposal
to dated August 23 1989 The scope of work was limited
to

feet below the existing ground surface

observations of the surficial soil rock conditions
3 review of available soils and geologic information pertinent to
the project site and its vicinity
4 laboratory testing of selected samples obtained during the field
exploration to determine the engineering properties and
classification of the materials encountered

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The medium stiff to very stiff on site residual soils encountered
below the existing ground surface can generally provide adequate
foundation support for the proposed house provided that some care is
exercised during construction to minimize the swelling potential of the
soil as discussed herein
The field exploration and laboratory tests performed on the on site
silty clay and clayey silt residual soils indicate that this material
generally has moderately high swelling potential if it is allowed to dry
prior to subsequent saturation Therefore to reduce the effects due to
soil swelling under on grade slabs we recommended that the on site soil
be capped with at least 16 inches of non expansive select fill and that
the subgrade soils underneath the select fill and slab be moistened and
protected from drying from the start of construction until the slab
concrete placement
Tot Grading
The fills and backfills within the top 16 inches minimum below the
on grade slabs should consist of non expansive soils such as select
borrow crusher waste or coral Where cut or minimal fill is anticipated
the on site soil should be over excavated to provide a 16 inch minimm
thickness of non expansive select fill capping below the on grade slab
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2381 00 Page 5
At the on set of the earthwork the area within the contract grading
limits should be cleared of vegetation debris rubbish and other
deleterious materials These materials should be removed and disposed of
properly off site Any loose or soft spots encountered should be
over excavated down to the firm subsoils and then replaced with compacted
non expansive select material
The fill material should consist of non expansive granular materials
generally less than 3 inches in maximum size with less than 20 passing
the 200 sieve All imported fill material should be tested and approved
by the soils engineer prior to transportation to the site
The fill areas should be scarified to a depth of 6 inches moisture
conditioned and recompacted to 90 of its maximm dry density per ASIM
Test Method D 1557 78 Due to the sloping nature of the site the fills
behind the retaining walls should be keyed and benched into the natural
stiff ground
The fill should be placed in maximum 8 inch thick loose level lifts
and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximm ary density per ASTM Test
Method D 1557 78
Foundations
Concrete slab on grade construction is planned for the proposed
residence It should be noted that the on site silty clay and clayey silt
soils may be moderately expansive if allowed to dry Therefore the
on site soil should be over excavated down to a minimum depth of 16 inches
below the slab bottom thoroughly moistened and then replaced with
non expansive imported soils as previously discussed The thickened edge
of the slab should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest
adjacent finish grade
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2381 00 Page 6
To further reduce future post construction swelling of the underlying
expansive soils we recommend that the in place soils below the upper
capping fill pad be kept moist prior to concrete and cushion fill
placement
It is our understanding that a basaltic termite barrier will be
used under the slab This termite barrier gravel thickness may be
included as part of the recommended 16 inches of select fill In order to
protect against future moisture infiltration an impervious moisture
barrier may be incorporated below the slab
Spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the proposed
residence An allowable bearing value of 2 500 p s f may be used for
footings bearing on the natural soil or compacted fill
Individual footings and continuous wall footings for the residence
should be embedded a minimm of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent
finished grade
If located on a sloping area the footing should be embedded down an
additional amount to obtain a 10 foot minimm horizontal set back
distance measured from the outer edge of the footing out to the slope
face This additional embedment depth is required to help resist the
downhill slope creep movement and to develop the recommended bearing
capacity of the soil

provide grade separation from the house The guidelines for retaining
wall design are listed below

Drainage
The finish lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings should
be shaped to shed water away from foundation and to avoid ponding
condition Also it is advised that gutter systems be installed around
the house and gutter water be diverted far away from the foundations and
driveway Excessive landscape watering near foundations and slab should
also be avoided
Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance
of the above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of
strength The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled tight
against the wall or slab footing immediately after setting of the concrete
to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and maintained
to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundations

Concrete Driveway
Since the driveway can tolerate some amount of heaving the 12 inch
on site soil over excavation and replacement may be replaced with 6 inch
thick base course layer if some amount of small slab cracking are
tolerable It is critical that the subgrade soils should be maintained in
a moist condition and prevented from drying out prior to base course and
concrete placement
Joints are recommended in the driveway slab at about 8 to 10 foot
intervals and at discontinuities in order to allow for some relative
movements of the concrete
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written coments
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this review
is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for
misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during site grading and
construction of the foundation This is to observe compliance with the
design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow design
changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from that
anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in
this report are contingent upon such cbservations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field borings Variations of
conditions between the field data points may occur and the nature and
extent of these variations may not become evident until construction If
variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations given in this report
The locations of the field borings were approximately determined by
taping from structures indicated on the plans provided Elevations of the
data points were approximately determined by interpolation from elevation
points on the plans The physical location and elevation of the field
data points should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by
the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross sections are based upon interpolation between field data
points and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
