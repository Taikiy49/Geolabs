Dear Mr Fujita
In accordance with your request we have made a study of the near
surface soil conditions at the above referenced lot in order to
provide foundation recommendations for the proposed residence The
study consisted of drilling two shallow borings laboratory testing of
the swelling potential of the soils reviewing of the subdivision site
grading plans and the preliminary house foundation plans This report
summarizes our findings and recommendations
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
The proposed residence will be a two story wooden structure with CMU
walls included for the basement The house is approximately 38 by 48
feet in plan dimension with a 2 car garage at the rear of the lot

CMU retaining walls are anticipated along the southern boundary to
minimize foundation undermining in the adjacent lot Walls up to 9
feet high are also planned along the ground floor foundation perimeter
Concrete slab is planned for the house garage and driveway
Cuts and fills of up to 5 feet are anticipated for the basement level
The layout plan for the house is shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
SITE CONDITIONS
The subject lot is on the eastern side of Kapukapu Place in the Royal
Summit Phase 1 Subdivision located in Aiea Oahu Hawaii
At the front portion of the lot a 20 foot wide level pad slopes
upward toward the east at about 5 1 H V slope ratio to a second
level pad There is a 2 1 H V bank about 8 feet high along the
rear lot boundary
SUBSURFACE SOIL CONDITIONS
Two borings were drilled to a depth of 5 5 and 11 0 feet below the
existing grade at the approximate location shown on the Site Plan

The borings encountered stiff residual reddish brown clayey silt to
the depth of our borings A review of the site grading plans for the
subsidivision also indicates that this lot was cut from 1 to 7 feet
below the natural grade
Laboratory swell tests performed on selected samples indicate a low
in situ moisture contents of 24 to 30 percent versus typical
moisture ranges of 30 to 40 percent for this locale and type of soil
In addition the clayey silt soils at the proposed foundation subgrade
level generally has a low to moderate swelling potential
However previous experience with these soils indicates that the
swelling potential may increase to the higher swelling range if they
are allowed to dry out Therefore special site preparation and
monitoring will be necessary as discussed under the following sections
of the report

Site Grading
Prior to filling the areas to receive fill should be cleared of
vegetation and debris
Fills and backfills should be placed in maximum 8 inch lifts and
compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density established
by ASTM D 1557 78
If the on site soil from Royal Summit is used for fill it should
first be approved by a qualified soils engineer and should be placed
wet of optimum moisture content 30 to reduce the swelling
potential
Fills on slopes having ratios greater than 5 1 H V should be keyed
and benched
Slab On Grade

Where a minimum of 2 feet of imported non expansive soil such as

with an 8 inch deep thickened edge may be used
Where on site soil is encountered at the subgrade level a deeper
18 inch perimeter footing embedment and special subgrade preparation
with lime saturation are recommended The general procedure listed
hereunder for lime saturation should be followed

Drainage
The finish lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings should be
shaped to shed water away from the foundations and to avoid ponding
condition Also it is advised that gutter systems should be
installed around the house and that gutter water should be diverted
far away from the foundations and walkways
Excessive landscape watering near the foundations and slab should also
be avoided
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance
of the above recommended foundations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of
strength The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled
tight against the slab footing immediately after setting of the
concrete to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained to
drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundation

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the footing excavations and backfill operations
be observed by the soils engineer during construction to see that
adequate bearing has been obtained and that the subsurface conditions
are consistent with the findings of our subsurface investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the above
inspections
