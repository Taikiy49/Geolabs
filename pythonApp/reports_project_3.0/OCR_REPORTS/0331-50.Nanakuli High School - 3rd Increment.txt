FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface exploration consisted of 3 three borings ranging
in depth from 14 to 24 feet The drilling was accomplished using
truck mounted rotary drilling equipment with continuous flight
hollow stem auger and NX coring equipment The borings were
continuously inspected by an engineer or geologist from our
firm The approximate boring locations are shown on Plate 1
and the boring logs are presented in Appendix A
In all borings either Standard Penetration Test or 2 5 inch 0 D
ring samples were taken at selected depth intervals not exceed
ing 5 feet The Standard Penetration Test consists of driving
a 2 inch 0 D split spoon sampler 18 inches with a 140 pound
hammer free falling a distance of 30 inches The number of blows
required to drive the sampler the last 12 inches is termed the
Standard Penetration Resistance N and is an approximate
measure of the relative density or consistency of a soil These
resistance values are plotted on the boring logs Samples obtained
were visually classified in the field and representative material
was placed in air tight containers and returned to our laboratory
for inspection and testing

GEOLOGY AND SOIL CONDITIONS
The Nanakuli School grounds are located in a deep alluvial valley
between two ridges consisting of interbedded basalt lava and
tuff deposts The valley fill consists of BASALT BOULDERS
GRAVEL SAND SILT and CLAY eroded from the surrounding ridges
The firm to very stiff gray to black CLAY is believed to have
been derived by erosion of soils from the surrounding ridges
Boring No 1 passed through 5 feet of medium to dense brown
SILTY SAND COBBLES and broken rock fill overlying alluvial CLAY
and BOULDERS Borings No 2 and No 3 encountered only CLAY and
BOULDERS over their 15 foot length
No free water was noted in any of the borings A small 1 foot
wide 6 inch deep stream was seen about 100 feet south of the
central kitchen building area

Settlement
The structure should experience less than 3 4 inch total
settlement and less than 1 2 inch post construction differen
tial settlement
Structural Fill
All topsoil must be removed prior to placing structural fill
Structural fill beneath buildings and pavement areas should
be compacted to 90 of the laboratory maximum density using
ASTM D 1557 70 as a standard
Backfill for walls and trenches should be compacted free
draining granular material
Maximum allowable cut or fill slopes should be 2H 1V Fills
placed on existing hillsides should be keyed and benched
as necessary
All compacted structural fill must be placed under the
supervision of a qualified soils engineer

Lateral Earth Pressure
Basement or retaining walls should be designed for an equiva
lent fluid pressure of 35 pounds per cubic foot provided only
moderate compaction of backfill is required If a high degree
of backfill compaction is required or the walls cannot yield
even slightly an equivalent fluid pressure of 60 pounds
per cubic foot should be used Surcharges due to adjacent
footings hydrostatic pressure construction equipment etc
must be added to the above values
inspection
All excavations for footings and slabs should be inspected by
a soils engineer or his representative prior to placing
forms reinforcing steel or concrete
