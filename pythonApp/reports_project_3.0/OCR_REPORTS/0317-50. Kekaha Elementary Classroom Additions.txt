This report presents the results of a subsurface investigation
conducted at the proposed site for additional classroom buildings
and related facilities of Kekaha Elementary School This study
was done to determine the underlying soil coriditions and their
engineering properties for the design of a foundation support
system for the proposed structures
This report summarizes our work and presents recommendations
pertinent to site development

FIELD EXPLORATION
Field work consisted of conducting three exploratory borings
and one cone penetrometer pounding at locations shown on the
Site plan Plate 1 The two test borings designated as B 1l
26 5 feet and the sounding designated as P 1 was terminated
at 15 feet below natural ground surface
In all holes Standard Penetration Tests SPT were made at selected
intervals The test consists of driving a 2 inch 0 D split spoon
sampler 18 inches with a 140 pound hammer free falling a distance
of 30 inches The number of blows required to drive the sampler
the last 12 inches is termed the Standard Penetration Resistance
N and is an approximate measure of the in situ relative density
or consistency of a soil These resistance values are plotted on
the boring logs Samples obtained in this manner were visually
classified in the field and representative material placed in
jars for re inspection and classification in our laboratory
To obtain a more complete picture of the relative density of the
underlying soils a steel conical point attached to a string
of A rods was driven into the soil Blow counts per foot pene
trations were recorded in the logs The result is shown graphically

two distinct soil strata at the subject site The first or top
fifteen feet consists of medium dense to very dense light brown
medium to fine SAND It was observed that about 10 exhibit
slight cementation These slightly cemented portions no more
than 2 in diameter are found unevenly scattered both laterally
and vertically

CONCLUSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the results of the field investigation and engineering
analyses the following conclusions and recommendations are
presented
A Foundations
Spread or continuous footings founded in the medium dense to
dense brown fine SAND may be designed for an allowable
bearing capacity of 2000 psf The footings should be based
at least 24 inches below adjacent ground or slab surfaces
and should have a minimum width of 12 inches All footings
must be based on natural ground
Slabs on grade may be utilized provided ail organic topsoil
is removed and a six inch layer of free draining sand or
gravel is placed beneath the slabs A vapor barrier i e

to minimize floor dampness
Settlements
Provided that foundation recommendations are followed we
anticipate that the structure should experience less than
1 inch of total settlement and less than 1 2 inch differential
settlement The dead load settlements will occur by the
end of construction
Lateral Design
Resistance to lateral loading may be provided by friction
acting at the base of foundations An allowable coefficient
of friction of 0 4 may be used with dead loads Passive
earth pressure shall be neglected
Grading
Where grading is required field density tests should be
performed at representative intervals and locations to
verify the recommendations as included in this report
Our grading specifications are attached and should be
followed unless specifically superseded herein Any

It is recommended that all excavation for footings and

Supervision of Earthwork
Field density tests shall be made by the soils engineer
during the earthwork operation such that he may certify
that the fill was placed according to accepted specifica
tions In the event that field density tests of a layer
or any portion thereof is less than the required density
the particular layer or portion shall be reworked until

Seasonal Requirements
No fill shall be placed during unfavorable weather condi
tions as determined by the soils engineer After interrup
tion of work due to heavy rain the soils engineer shall
approve previously placed fill before resumption of earth
moving operations
