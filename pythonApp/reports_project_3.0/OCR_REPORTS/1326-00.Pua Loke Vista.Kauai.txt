During our recent review of the plans it was noted that large
diameter drainlines crossed under the waterlines near each leakage
location Therefore it would appear that there is a high possibility
that the waterlines could have been damaged by settlement of the
drainline backfill underneath the waterline
The purpose of our boring investigation was to evaluate the
compaction density of the drainline trench backfill below the waterline
and to determine possible causes for the waterline leakages Our work
was done in general accordance with our proposal of
FIELD EXPLORATION
One boring was drilled near the intersection of the waterline and
drainlines at each leakage location Samples of the backfill material
were obtained with a soil sampler from auger bored holes in order to
determine the in place density of the soil The approximate locations
of the borings are shown on the Site Plans Plates 1 2 and 3

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the field exploration it appears that the waterline
leakages may have resulted from settlement of the underlying drainline
trench backfill
The borings indicate that the drainline trench backfill at the
crossing on Aukoi Street is fairly well compacted while the backfill at
the crossing on Puanani Street has moderate compaction and at the
crossing on Aheahe is very soft near the invert of the drainpipe
Based on the field exploration it is our opinion that the
drainline backfill at the waterline crossing on Aukoi Street Extension
is generally adequate and future settlements would probably be small
The future performance of the trench backfill at the other two
locations is difficult to assess Although the trench backfill was
found to be soft to medium stiff arching action along the sides of the
trench may limit additional settlements Future performance would also
depend on other factors such as traffic vibrations and possible
groundwater saturation
