Soil Corrosion Study
Royal Kunia Site 4
Dear Mr Gaston
Our corrosion study of the on site soils consisted of four resistivity tests three pH
tests one sulfates test and one chlorides test The resistivity tests were performed in
general conformance with ASTM Test Designation G 57 78 for laboratory resistivity
tests with a Miller box The resistivity tests were also performed in general
conformance to the requirements provided in the Water System External Corrosion
Control Standards Volume 3 dated 1991 The pH tests were performed in general
conformance with ASTM Test Designation G 51 for laboratory pH tests and in general
conformance to the requirements provided in the Water System External Corrosion
Control Standards Volume 3 dated 1991 The sulfates test was performed in general
conformance with Test Method The reporting limit of the equipment used
and the test procedure was The chlorides test was performed in general
conformance with Test Method The reporting limit of the equipment used
and the test procedure was The results are shown on Plate 1 The locations
of the test samples are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Based on the tests performed for the soil corrosion study the tested soils fall into
Corrosion Ratings 2 to 4 However due to anticipated variable conditions found at the
site and that created from the cut and fill process we recommend that a soil corrosion
rating of 2 be used for design of water utilities

Scour Potential of Fill Slope
Royal Kunia Site 4
Dear Mr Gaston
It is our understanding that there is some concern about the scour potential of
the fill slope at the existing drainageway Previously we were informed that the scour
potential would be handled at a later date However since the slope is contained within
a drainageway belonging to another owner the date in which this problem will be
addressed is unknown
Based on the information provided future improvements to the drainageway will
consist of extending the existing box culvert located beneath the Kupuohi Street in a
westward direction and raising the grade at the bottom of the drainageway It is also
our understanding that the drainageway will later be developed into a golf course
Based on the low plasticity and small particle size of the on site soils we believe
that the slope has the potential to scour from storm water flows within the drainageway
Depending on the time anticipated until the implementation of future improvements it
may be prudent to provide some type of temporary scour and erosion protection to the
slope
We believe that the boulders could be utilized for temporary or permanent scour
protection improvements Boulders underlain by a geotextile fabric or embedded into
concrete are potential solutions Note however that a scour potential study should be
performed by a qualified civil engineer prior to implementation of any protection
scheme We can help in providing design parameters such as the d soil particle
diameter of the on site soils which is the particle diameter where 50 percent of soil
particles are of a smaller diameter We can also assist in developing a slope protection
design

Recommended Settlement Monitoring Program
Royal Kunia Site 4
Dear Mr Gaston
Based on the decision to substitute the pipe pile foundation solution with a
structural mat foundation which is more sensitive to ground settlements we
recommend that a more intensive settlement monitoring program be implemented at the
site We recommend the following settlement gauges be established
1 Two surface monuments per lot middle and rear of Lot 20 21
and 22
2 Two vibrating wire settlement gauges at an elevation of about 12 feet of fill
above the existing ground surface at the rear of Lot Nos 20 and 21
The surface monuments should be installed by or under the direction of a
qualified professional surveyor The surface monuments should be installed after
completion of the filling process Note that once the surface monuments are installed
the nearby ground should not be disturbed Upon establishment of the surface
monuments we recommend that the surface monuments be monitored on a weekly
basis by a qualified professional surveyor for a minimum of 9 months This data should
be transmitted to our office for review
The vibrating wire settlement gauges should be installed by Geolabs Hawaii
The vibrating wire settlement gauges should be installed as the fill is placed at an
elevation of about 12 feet of fill above the existing ground surface Upon establishment
of these settlement gauges the location and elevation of the gauges should be
determined by a qualified professional surveyor This data should also be transmitted
to our office for review
