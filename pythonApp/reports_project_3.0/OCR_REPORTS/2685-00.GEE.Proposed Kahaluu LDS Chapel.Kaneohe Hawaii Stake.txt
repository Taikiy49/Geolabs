INTRODUCTION
Page 2
In accordance with the request of Paul Louie Associates Inc we have
conducted a geotechnical engineering exploration for the proposed LDS Chapel
construction in Kahaluu on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location of the
project site and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The field
exploration was performed on May 20 1991 in general accordance with our fee proposal
dated March 25 1991
This report summarizes our findings resulting from the field exploration and
presents our geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from analyses based
on the data obtained from the field exploration and laboratory testing The recommenda
tions presented herein are not intended to form the basis of preparation of construction
cost estimates or bidding purposes

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is situated in the Kahaluu area on the island of Oahu Hawaii
It is proposed to construct a new chapel of approximately 180 feet by 86 feet in
On grade parking will be provided along the sides and behind the building
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface soil
conditions at the project site in order to develop a generalized soil rock data set for the
formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to the site grading
and foundation design of the proposed construction
The work was done in accordance with our proposal to Paul Louie Associates
Inc dated March 25 1991 The scope of work included the following tasks and work
efforts
1 Reconnaissance of the project site and vicinity to make visual observations
of the site conditions and to select locations for the borings
2 Trail cutting to make the site accessible to our truck mounted drilling
equipment
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2685 00 Page 4
3 Mobilization of truck mounted drilling equipment with operators to and from
the project site
4 Drilling sampling and probing six 6 borings three 3 of which included
penetration probes in the lower depths to depths ranging between 16 5 to
30 0 feet below the existing ground surface
5 Coordination of the field exploration including supervision of the drilling
operations and logging of the borings by a Field Geologist from our firm
6 Laboratory testing of selected samples obtained during the field exploration
to determine the classification and engineering properties of the materials
encountered
7 Engineering analyses of the field and laboratory data for the purpose of
developing geotechnical engineering recommendations pertaining to the
design of site grading and foundations
8 Preparation of this report presenting the findings and design recommenda

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located on the Ahuimanu Road between the Kahaluu Flood
Control Channel to the east and a private road owned by the to the west
The site slopes gently downward from south to north with a steeper slope at the west side
to the private road which is about eight 8 feet higher Haole Koa and tall grass cover
At the time of our field exploration the site was vacant However some trash
concrete rubble rebars and other miscellaneous iterms had been dumped there
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by drilling sampling
and probing six 6 borings three 3 of which included penetration probes in the lower
depths to depths ranging between 16 5 and 30 0 feet below the existing ground surface
The approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general the borings encountered a thin surface layer possibly dumped fill
material approximately three 3 to seven 7 feet thick of medium stiff brown clayey silt
to silty clay Underneath the surface layer soft to very soft lagoonal deposits consisting
of dark gray organic silty clay with weathered gravel were encountered to depths of about
20 feet below the existing ground surface Continuous probing was performed in three
3 of the six 6 drilled borings in an attempt to delineate the thickness of the soft
lagoonal deposits It appeared that medium stiff soil conditions were encountered at
depths of about 25 to 30 feet the maximum probed depth

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
In general it is our opinion that development of the site for the proposed new
chapel construction is feasible provided that the recommendations presented herein are
followed Due to the fact that the thin relatively competent surface layer is underlain by
soft lagoonal deposits special design considerations will be required for the site grading
and foundation construction
Site Grading
The near surface groundwater level and soft soil conditions have a critical effect
on the site grading design
Due to the adverse conditions encountered at the site consisting of a thin
relatively competent surface layer underlain by soft lagoonal deposits cuts and fills should
be held to an absolute minimum consistent with the requirement to provide drainage and
support for the structural loads and for vehicular parking
GEOLABS HAWAIi
W O 2685 00 Page 7
Site grading involving cuts will reduce the thickness of the existing thin surface
layer which will become the supporting stratum for shallow footing foundations
Therefore special construction procedures as recommended in the section entitled
Footing Foundations will be required for the footing design
Site grading involving general excavation will also have an adverse affect on the
support capacity for parking areas and therefore should be avoided
In general fills on soft ground conditions require special consideration to minimize
deleterious effects caused by excessive settlement which may result
However it is our understanding that up to three 3 feet of fill is planned for the
northwestern portion of the project site
Our geotechnical engineering analyses indicate that about four 4 to five 5 inches
of ground settlement may result from the loading imposed by three 3 feet of fill
Because of the low permeability of the underlying soft silty clay the rate of ground
settlement will be slow The laboratory test data indicates that it would take about twenty
20 months for 90 of the total primary settlement to take place
To reduce potential structural damage which could be caused by future on going
settlement foundation and building construction should be delayed until the majority of
the primary settlement has been completed In order to shorten the waiting period before
construction may begin a Surcharge and Settlement Monitoring program as discussed
hereinbelow is recommended

W O 2685 00 Page 8
Site Preparation
Prior to commencement of grading all areas within the proposed grading limits
should be cleared of all vegetation trash and deleterious materials The resulting
grubbed and spoil material should be disposed of properly off site
Any soft or yielding ground conditions encountered during clearing and grubbing
operations should be over excavated to firm natural material and the resulting
depression backfilled with well compacted engineered fill
We recommend that the upper six 6 inches of the existing subgrade soil should
be scarified moisture conditioned and recompacted to 90 of its maximum density
as established by ASTM D 1557 78 Test Method After completion of the
surcharge program and removal of the surcharge material the subgrade should
also be scarified for a minimum of six 6 inches and compacted to a minimum of
90 of its maximum dry density as determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
prior to foundation work
Fill Material
The on site surface clay soil is expansive and therefore it is our opinion that it is
not suitable for use as engineered fill material It may however be used as
surcharge material but should be removed and disposed of off site when
settlement is complete
The on site surface clay soil may remain under embankments supporting slabs on
grade and pavements only if it is not disturbed by construction operations
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2685 00 Page 9
In general embankments should be constructed of imported non expansive select
granular materials less than three 8 inches in maximum size with less than 20
passing the No 200 sieve such as select borrow crusher waste cinder or coral
All imported fill material should be tested and approved by the project geotechnical
engineer prior to delivery to the site
Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
The fill and backfill should be placed in horizontal loose level lifts up to a maximum
of 10 inches thick and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry
density as determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
The requirements for structural fill and backfill under footings are presented in the
subsequent section entitled Footing Foundations
Cut and fill slope ratios of two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V or flatter are
recommended Fill slopes should be constructed by overfilling then cutting back
to the design slope to obtain a well compacted slope face
Surface run off water should be diverted away from the top of slopes and slope
planting should be utilized as soon as possible to minimize erosion
Interception of surface run off is especially important at the west and south sides
of the site

Surcharge and Settlement Monitoring
In order to shorten the waiting period before building construction may begin a
surcharge program comprising placement of five 5 feet of temporary fill is recommended
at the portion of the project site where fills are anticipated The surface materials
excavated from cut areas in the project site grading may be used as temporary surcharge
fill
It is our opinion that the surcharge fill will accelerate ground settlements and
shorten the construction waiting period to about six 6 to eight 8 months However
it should be noted that the actual settlement rate will depend on many factors such as
thickness homogeneity permeability and minute clay mineral particle behavior of the soft
deposits These factors cannot be accurately determined Therefore settlement gauges
to measure the magnitude and rate of settlement due to the surcharge should be installed
and monitored to determine when building and utility construction may commence
It is recommended that a minimum of four 4 settlement gauges be installed after
subgrade preparation and prior to placement of the surcharge to monitor the rate and
magnitude of the settlement
Conventional settlement gauges see Plate 3 may be utilized They should be
monitored by level readings made on a weekly basis by the project surveyor until the
settlement slows down to a tolerable rate All readings obtained from these gauges
should be submitted immediately to the project geotechnical engineer for review and
anaiysis
Since the settlement gauges are susceptible to damage by construction equipment
during earthwork the contractor should be informed about the importance of the
settlement readings and cautioned against damaging them The geotechnical engineer
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2685 00 Page 11
should be informed immediately if any gauges are damaged so that they can be replaced
as soon as practicable
Footing Foundations
Shallow spread and continuous wall footing foundations can be used to support
the anticipated light structure of the proposed chapel provided some settlements are
tolerable
An allowable bearing capacity of 2 000 p s f is recommended for spread and wall
footing foundation design A minimum footing embedment of 12 inches below the lowest
adjacent finish grade is also recommended
We recommend that the footings should be tied together with grade beams to
improve the rigidity of the foundation system to reduce the differential settlement
Due to the adverse subsoil conditions encountered and the anticipated project site
grading the following special footing construction procedures are recommended
Footings in Cut Areas
Our engineering analyses indicated that for a wall footing of one and one half 1 5
feet in width with a wall load of two 2 kips per foot footing settlement on the
order of two 2 inches total is anticipated If the soil underneath the bottom of the
wall footing is replaced with gravel and compacted engineered fill to a depth of
three 3 feet two times the footing width the estimated magnitude of footing
settlement will be reduced to about one and one half 1 5 inches If up to five 5
feet is removed and replaced with gravel and compacted engineered fill the
estimated footing settlement is further reduced to the order of one 1 inch
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2685 00 Page 12
Therefore we recommend that the wall footing excavations should be over
excavated to a minimum depth of about five 5 feet below the footing bottom
elevation The over excavation should also extend beyond the edge of the footings
to include the stress influence zone one horizontal to two vertical plane from the
footing edge as shown in Plate 4 The excavation should first be lined with a layer
of geotextile and then backfilled with 3B Fine gravel to raise the backfill a
minimum of 12 inches above the groundwater level This material is generally self
compacting therefore compaction may be attained by the use of a vibratory
compactor on the final level of the material
Then non expansive borrow materials such as crusher waste or coral should be
placed in horizontal loose lifts not exceeding eight 8 inches thick and compacted
to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density as determined by ASTM Test
Method D 1557 78 prior to footing construction
For spread footing foundations we recommend that the footing excavations should
be over excavated to a minimum depth of one and one half 1 5 times the longer
dimension of the spread footing below the footing bottom elevation see Plate 4
The over excavation should be backfilled in the same manner as that recommend
ed for the wail footing foundations Estimated footing settlement under a column
load of 32 kips is on the order of one 1 inch total
Footings in Fill Areas
The above recommended over excavation and replacement with compacted
engineered fills is not required for footing foundations located in areas where two
2 feet or more of fills are planned in the project site grading However
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2685 00 Page 13
construction of footing foundations in these areas should be delayed until a
majority of the ground settlement due to the new fill loads has occurred
Steeple
It is our understanding that the anticipated column load under the steeple structure
will be about 121 kips Our engineering analyses for a square footing of eight 8
feet on a side with eight 8 feet of over excavation and backfill indicated that
footing settlement of one and one half 1 5 inches total may be anticipated
Deeper over excavation or drilled pier pile foundations may be considered to
minimize this amount of anticipated total footing settlement Shoring and
dewatering may be required for footing over excavation deeper than eight 8 feet
Drilled Pier or Pile Foundations
Drilled pier or pile foundations may be considered as an alternate foundation
system to the shallow footing foundations With drilled pier or pile foundations the
recommended over excavation and replacement with engineered fill for footings in the cut
areas can be eliminated A comparative cost study should be performed to evaluate the
economic benefits of each foundation system
The present scope of our work as described in our proposal does not include deep
borings to attain data necessary for analysis of the drilled pier or pile foundation design
Therefore it is recommended that additional borings be drilled to provide the embedment
length and other design parameters of the drilled pier or pile foundations

Slabs On Grade
It is anticipated that slab on grade construction will be used for the proposed
chapel
The thickened edges of the slab on grade should be embedded a minimum of 12
inches below the lowest adjacent finish grade A minimum six 6 inch thick layer of 3B
Fine gravel or ASTM C 33 No 67 should be provided below the slabs as cushion fill In
order to prevent future moisture infiltration and subsequent damage to the floor coverings
an impervious moisture membrane should be used Also recommended are two 2
inches of moist sand on top of the membrane to help curing of the concrete
The subgrade underneath the recommended six 6 inch thick cushion fill should
be kept moist prior to placement of the slab concrete
Structural Slabs
We believe that a floor system consisting of a combination slabs on grade and
elevated wood framed structural slabs may be considered to minimize cut and fill
requirements at the project site and hence to provide a more economical foundation
system
Post and beam construction with a floor system constructed entirely of elevated
wood framed floors can be used to reduce or eliminate the requirement of cuts and fills
Under this concept surcharge fill and settlement monitoring cuts and fills and footing
over excavation will not be required If this concept is selected the design and
construction should incorporate levelling devices to periodically jack up the structure to
compensate for possible continuing footing settlement if any

It is anticipated that flexible asphaltic concrete pavements will be utilized for the

ifnecessary The recommended section considers medium traffic and that good drainage
will be provided adjacent to the paved areas
installation of Utilities
Installation of utilities such as new water and sewer lines is anticipated for the
project A granular bedding consisting of six 6 inches of 3B Fine gravel or ASTM C 33
No 67 is recommended under the pipes
Deeper excavations may encounter soft soils and groundwater levels requiring
trench shoring and dewatering
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2685 00 Page 16
Granular materials should be used for trench backfill The backfill up to about one
1 foot above the pipes should be lightly tamped to reduce the possibility of damaging
the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level one 1 foot above the pipes
to the finish grade should be placed in maximum 10 inch thick horizontal loose lifts
mechanically compacted to a minimum of 80 compaction as determined by ASTM Test
Method D 1557 78 to protect against potential future ground subsidence
Design Review
Drawings and specifications for the proposed construction should be forwarded
to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comments prior to construction This
review is needed to determine adherence to the earthwork surcharge grading and
foundation recommendations given herein If this review is not made the geotechnical
engineer cannot assume responsibility for misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during site grading and foundation construction of the
project This is to observe compliance with the recommendations design concepts or
specifications and to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ
from those anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in this
report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and approved by
the geotechnical engineer prior to delivery to the site

Laboratory Testing
Moisture content unit weight and Atterberg Limit determinations were performed
on selected soil samples obtained The results are presented on the Boring Logs at the
appropriate sample depths The Atterberg Limits are further summarized on Plate B 1
One 1 CBR test was performed on surface bulk sample to evaluate subgrade soil
strength for pavement design The test results are summarized on Plate B 1
Three 3 one inch ring swell tests were performed on undisturbed and remolded
samples to evaluate the swelling potential of the soils in both natural and compacted
conditions The test results are summarized on Plate B 2
Four 4 consolidation tests were performed on undisturbed fine grained soil
samples to evaluate their compressibility and settlement characteristics The test results
are presented on Plates B 3 through B 6
