SUMMARY REPORT
GROUNDWATER SAMPLING AND TESTING SERVICES
KAPAHULU AVENUE 12 INCH WATER MAIN
This report summarizes our groundwater sampling and testing services at the
subject project Our work was performed in general accordance with our revised fee
proposal dated Laboratory tests were performed by Brewer
Environmental Industries Inc and Pace Analytical
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located in the Waikiki area on the island of Oahu Hawaii It is
proposed to construct a new 12 inch water main and appurtenances along Kapahulu
Avenue from Kuhio Avenue to Paki Avenue The proposed water main will be
approximately 1 130 feet long and will be installed at approximately 4 to 8 feet below the
existing ground surface The site elevation is at about 8 feet Mean Sea Level MSL
It is anticipated that groundwater will be encountered during excavation for the
new water main Based on current design concepts dewatering of the water main
excavation will likely be needed to facilitate the water main construction
Dewatering effluent will probably be discharged back into the trench If the trench
cannot absorb the water fast enough excess water will be discharged into the storm
drain Therefore a permit under the National Pollutant Discharge Elimination System
NPDES may be required for this discharge

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our work on this phase of the project was to provide the drilling
monitoring weil installation and groundwater sampling and testing services necessary for
the preparation of the NPDES permit application In order to accomplish these tasks our
scope of work generally consisted of the following work efforts
1
Procurement of the necessary excavation permit and clearance from
underground utility companies as well as coordination for set up of traffic
and safety signs prior to drilling operations
Mobilization and demobilization of a truck mounted drill rig and operators to
and from the project site
Drilling installation and development of one groundwater monitoring well to
a depth of about 20 6 feet below the existing ground surface
Logging of the boring by a field engineer from our firm
Collection of groundwater sample from the well by a field engineer from our
firm after a minimum of 3 days following completion of the monitoring well
The water sample chain of custody documentation and transportation to the
laboratory were also performed by our field engineer
Preparation of a brief report summarizing our work on the project and
presenting our findings

Field Investigation
Our field investigation was conducted on August 13 1996 Drilling of the
monitoring well was accomplished using a truck mounted drill rig advancing 8 inch
outside diameter O D hollow stem augers The hollow stem augers and associated
drilling tools were steam cleaned prior to drilling the borehole
Subsurface Conditions
Monitoring Well No was installed near the entrance to Thomas Jefferson
School The approximate location of the monitoring weil is presented on the Site Plan
Plate 2 In general MW 1 encountered a surface layer of man made fill of about 4 feet in
thickness The fill layer generally consists of clayey silt with sand and gravel Underlying
this fill layer our boring encountered loose to dense sands with gravel overlying dense
sandy limestone gravel to the maximum depth drilled of about 20 6 feet below ground
level The boring log of MW 1 is presented in Appendix A
Subsurface materials were examined and classified in general accordance with
ASTM Standard Method D 2488 84 by a field engineer from our firm Soil samples were
obtained from the drilled boring in accordance with ASTM Standard Method D 1586 84
Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 3 inch O D split barrel
sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches
Groundwater Conditions
Shallow groundwater conditions were encountered in the drilled monitoring well at
a depth of about 4 feet 8 inches below the existing ground corresponding to about
1 1 feet MSL
Due to the proximity of the shoreline and the Pacific Ocean it is likely that the
groundwater level measured in the boring during our field exploration may be affected by
tidal fluctuation of the sea level In addition the groundwater level at the project site may
be influenced by seasonal precipitation and storm surge conditions
Monitoring Well Installation
After drilling to a depth of about 20 6 feet the bottom of the borehole was
backfilled with approximately 2 5 feet of filter sand and a monitoring well casing was
lowered into the borehole The casing consisted of 2 inch O D Schedule 40
GEOLABS HAWAII
Inc Page 4
W O 3635 00 Rev
flush threaded PVC pipe end cap composed the lower portion of the well Two feet of
blank 2 inch O D Schedule 40 flush threaded PVC pipe composed the upper portion of
the well The blank PVC casing extended upward to the ground surface The well was
enclosed in a traffic rated box The well was backfilled with a No 3 filter sand pack from
the bottom of the screen extending to one foot above the screen The filter sand
consisted of clean uniformly graded kiln dried Monterey Sands which should be
chemically stable within the monitoring well environment to reduce potential sorption from
the groundwater Typical grading parameters of the Monterey Sands are presented on
Plate 3 Approximately 6 inches of bentonite seal was installed on top of the filter sand
and the remaining 6 inches was sealed with grout The well housing was then cemented
in place The bentonite and grout were not in contact with the underlying groundwater
which is approximately 5 feet below the existing ground surface
Water sampling occurred on August 16 and 19 and which
was more than 3 days after well installation The well was bailed and a minimum of three
well casing volumes were removed by purging prior to sample collection Groundwater
samples were collected and transported to a laboratory for analyses Results of the
laboratory tests performed by Brewer Environmental Industries Inc and Pace Analytical
are presented on Plates B 1 thru B 16 of Appendix B
Abandonment of Monitoring Well
Abandonment of the monitoring well is not included as part of our current scope of
work therefore we recommend that abandonment of the monitoring well installed be
included in the construction documents In general the monitoring well should be
properly abandoned and sealed with cement grout
