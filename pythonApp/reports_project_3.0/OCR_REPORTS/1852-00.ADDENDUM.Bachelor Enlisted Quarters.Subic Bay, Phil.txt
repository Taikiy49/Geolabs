Based con our communication with the project structural engineer
American Structural Engineers it is our understanding that larger
diameter piles are needed to provide adequate earthquake mainly lateral
resistance for the proposed building
Based on our seismic lateral pile analyses and criteria provided by
the structural engineer we recommend that 16 inch square prestressed
concrete piles be used in lieu of the originally recommended 14 inch
square piles
Accordingly the allowable vertical pile capacity of the larger
16 inch square piles may be increased to 125 tons per pile while the
anticipated downdrag on the piles is estimated to be 35 tons per pile As
a result a net vertical capacity of 90 tons per pile may be used for the
16 inch square piles The piles should be load tested at two select
locations to 250 tons in accordance with the ASTM D 1143 test procedures
Estimated allowable uplift capacity of the 16 inch square piles is on
the order of 20 tons per pile
The net allowable vertical and uplift pile capacities may be
increased by one third for short duration seismic loading
