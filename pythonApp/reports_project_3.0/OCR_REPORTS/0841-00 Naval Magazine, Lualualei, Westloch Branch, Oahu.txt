INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this investigation was to evaluate the
subsurface conditions at the site to provide recommendations
for the design and construction of the foundations of the
proposed structures

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located in the Naval Magazine Lualualei
West Loch Branch Oahu Hawaii Project Location Map Plate 1
The site is situated at the extreme southern corner of the
Naval Magazine just inside the perimeter fence adjacent to
Iroquois Drive
The site is generally flat sloping gently southward toward
the ocean at a gradient of about 1 foot in 250 feet Surveyed
elevations range from about 8 to 12 feet above sea level
Scattered throughout the site are small piles of loose dirt
metal and concrete along with several large concrete slabs
discarded telephone poles and miscellaneous debris Several
coral gravel road criss cross the site as shown on the Site
Plan Plate 2 At the time of the field exploration the site
was otherwise heavily overgrown with Kiawe trees Koa dense
grass and shrubs

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The project site is located on the eastern edge of the Ewa
coastal plain which consists of a thick horizontal series of
interbedded marine and non marine sediments capping the Koolau
and Waianae Basalts The near subsurface structure at the site
consists of a broad elevated ancient coral reef of highly vari
able structure and composition
GEOLABS HAWAII
Department of the Navy
W
P 4
Coral reef deposits as they occur in Hawaii are predo
minately calcareous algae skeletons which support a relatively
small percentage of coral growth As a reef grows the void
spaces in the growing coral become filled with sand and other
calcareous debris This type of reef formation is varied
and complex consisting of zones biologically deposited coral
and algae dense recrystallized coralline limestone variably
cemented breccia dune and beach sand and marls derived from
the breakup and decay of the reef deposits
The present reef platform at this portion of the Ewa plain
stand approximately 8 to 10 feet above sea level and is
apparently a wave cut terrace formed by wave erosion of an
ancient reef which originally stood over 25 feet above sea
level As a result of a complex series of Pleistocene sea
level changes the coralline deposits have become partially
cemented and recrystallized at various depths by the action
of percolating fresh water In addition sinkholes and
solution cavities are commonly encountered in these reef
formations No other apparent adverse geologic conditions
are known to exist at the site

Subsurface Exploration
Thirteen test borings were drilled at the site to depths
of 5 0 to 18 2 feet below the existing ground surface to
explore the subsurface conditions and to obtain samples for
laboratory testing The approximate locations of the test
borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Detailed information on the field exploration methods and
laboratory testing is covered in the appendices along with
the boring logs and laboratory test results
Based on the test borings and field reconnaissance the
site is underlain by an extensive coral reef structure
with associated reef deposits The site appears to have
been previously graded and leveled with coral gravel fill
material Boring No 2 encountered apparent fill material
to a depth of 6 feet In general the site is underlain by
a 7 to 9 foot layer of medium dense to very dense cemented
carbonate sand and gravel chiefly coral and calcareous
algae Underlying this upper layer is a 5 to 10 foot
layer of loose carbonate silty sand and gravel with the
top of the layer occuring approximately at the water table
Under the loose layer to an unknown depth is a layer of
very dense cemented and recrystallized coral algal limestone

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Foundations
The field exploration disclosed that the site is underlain
by a coral formation at shallow depths The coral formation
generally consists of a very dense surface layer over a loose
sand layer at about 7 to 9 foot depth Isolated pockets of
fill or alluvial deposits B 2 and B 5 may be encountered
above the dense coral
Spread or continuous footing foundations bearing on the near
surface dense coral are recommended for the structures Due
to the existing loose layer at 7 to 9 foot depths the footings
should be kept as high as practical however for bearing
Capacity they should also be placed a minimum of 2 feet
below the finish grade Due to the variable nature of the
existing fill and alluvial material when they are encountered
in the footing excavation the footing excavation should be
carried down to the dense coral and backfilled up to the
design footing elevation The backfill should consist of
on site coral or imported granular material such as base
course rock compacted in 8 inch maximum horizontal loose
lifts to 95 of its maximum dry density established by ASTM
test method D 1557
An allowable bearing value of 4000 p s f is recommended for
footings bearing on the dense coral and 3000 p s f on com
pacted granular backfill
GEOLABS HAWAII
Department of the Navy
W
P 7
The bottom of footing excavation should be tamped prior to
pouring the concrete
Cavities may be located in the dense coral formation To
reduce the possibility of footing settlement due to cavities
the footing excavations should be probed A preliminary
test probe layout would include probes at every column
footing and at 15 feet on centers at the wall footings
The probe holes should have a minimum diameter of 2 inches
and should extend below the bottom of footing a depth equal
to 2 times the footing width The probe holes should be
grouted from the bottom of the hole using low pressures
If the drilling or grouting operations indicate that a
cavity is present additional probe holes should be made
to delineate the horizontal extent of the cavity The
probing and grouting operations should be monitored by a
soils engineer to see that these recommendations are carried
out properly
Estimated settlements for the foundations are on the order
of one half inch total and one fourth inch differential
Surface water should be diverted away from building foundations
GEOLABS HAWAII
Department of the Navy
W

Site Grading
A few feet of fill is planned to raise the site
The site should be cleared of all vegetation debris
topsoil and old foundations prior to filling
Select granular material such as the on site coral should
be used for fill and footing backfill material The fill
and backfill should be placed in 8 inch maximum horizontal
loose lifts and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum
dry density established by ASTM test method D 1557
Lateral Resistance
To resist lateral loads a coefficient of friction of
0 50 between the foundation and coral is recommended
Additionally a passive soil resistance of 300 p c f
can also be used
Slab On Grade
Slab on grade should be placed over a 4 inch layer of
base course generally less than 3 4 inch and greater
than 1 4 inch in size The subgrade should be kept
moist prior to pouring the concrete
Parking Area
An asphaltic concrete access roads and parking areas
are planned for the project
GEOLABS HAWAII
Department of the Navy

P 9
Based on the laboratory CBR tests on the near surface
coral sand samples the preliminary pavement design is
as follows
2 inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 inch Base Course
8 inch Total Pavement Thickness
Additional CBR tests should be performed if different
materials are encountered at the subgrade level
The subgrade should be recompacted to obtain 95 of the
maximum dry density established by ASTM D 1557 prior
to placing the base course layer
Existing Cesspools
Existing cesspools may be located on the site Where
existing cesspools are located below or adjacent to
footings and slabs the sludge should be removed and the
cesspool filled with compacted granular material or concrete
The cesspool walls within 5 feet of the finish grade should
be removed
Design Review
The plans for foundations site grading and utility construc
tion should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review
and comments prior to construction to see that the intent
of our recommendations is included in the project plans
and specifications

APPENDIX A
FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored
by drilling thirteen test borings to depths of 5 0 to 18 2
feet with 4 5 inch continuous flight solid stem auger
Continuous penetration tests utilizing a 2 inch diameter
blunt point were performed at the bottom of
and through 8 terminated at depths of 13 5
to 18 2 feet to test the overall subsurface conditions
at the site Borings 9 through 13 terminated at a depth
of 5 feet to test the near surface condition of the road
and parking areas The approximate location of all borings
is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The soils encountered in the borings were classified
by visual and textural examination in the field by our
geologist who continuously monitored the drilling opera
tions The classifications were reviewed by visual inspec
tion and testing in the laboratory The soils were classified
in general conformance with the Unified Soil Classification
System A graphic presentation of the soils is presented
on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 13
In the borings soil samples were obtained by driving
a standard split spoon sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling
30 inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the last
12 inches are shown on the boring logs at the appropriate
depths

APPENDIX B
LABORATORY TESTING
Twenty four moisture content determinations were made
on selected samples as an aid in the soil classification and
evaluation of soil properties The results of these tests
are presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 13
at the appropriate depth
Seven sieve analyses were performed on selected samples
to evaluate the grain size distribution of the material and
aid in the soil classification Because the split spoon
sample is highly disturbed large originally cemented
particles may be broken by driving the sampler The actual
in place grain size of the dense cemented samples may vary
from the tested distribution The test results are shown on
Plates B 1 and B 2
One direct shear test was performed on a selected
remolded sample to evaluate the strength characteristics
of the soil The test result is plotted on Plate B 3
Two moisture density tests ASTM D 1557C Modified
Proctor were performed On near surface bulk samples to
evaluate the soils for fill material The results are
summarized on Plate B 2 The maximum density and moisture
curves are plotted on Plates B 4 and B 5
Two CBR tests were performed on bulk samples of the near
surface soil in the parking and roadway areas to evaluate its
suitability as pavement support The results are summarized
on Plate B 2
