Regional Geology
The Island of Kauai is composed of a single dissected basaltic shield volcano built
by the extrusion of lavas of the Waimea Canyon Volcanic Series beginning about 5 to
6 million years ago The eruption of the Waimea Volcanic Series ended about 2 million
years ago and was followed by a long period of erosion Following the cessation of this
main volcano shield building phase about 1 million years ago there was renewed
volcanic activity with the extrusion of basaltic lavas of the post erosional Koloa Volcanic
Series and the concurrent deposition of the thick alluvial sediments of the Palikea
Formation

Rock formations of the Koloa Volcanic Series are generally characterized as thick
lava flows composed of dense basalt extruded from groups of vents aligned in north south
trends at various locales Associated with the Koloa Volcanic Series lava flows are some
deposits of pyroclastic materials volcanic ash and cinders which usually form cinder
cones surrounding the vent Rock formations of the Koloa Volcanic Series cover most of
the eastern half of the Island of Kauai including the project site
During the Pleistocene Epoch there were many sea level changes as a result of
widespread glaciation in the continental areas of the world As the great continental
glaciers accumulated the level of the ocean fell since there was less water available to fill
the oceanic basins Conversely as the glaciers receded or melted global sea levels rose
because more water was available The landmass of Kauai remained essentially stable
during these changes and the fluctuations were eustatic in nature These glacio eustatic
fluctuations resulted in stands of the sea which were both higher and lower relative to the
present sea level of the Island of Kauai
The project site is situated on ground underlain by hard basaltic rock lava flows of
the Koloa Volcanic Series In some places generally thin surficial soils representing silty
and clayey residual and alluvial materials may mantle the near surface rock formation
The basalt rock formation observed at the site appears to be representative of the
pahoehoe lava flow type which spread and ponded as it approached the ocean from
inland areas Pahoehoe lavas are typically characterized by a smoother billowy surface
and internal structure of vesicular porous rock with limited clinker materials In contrast
Ava lavas are typically characterized by porous rough and irregular flow surfaces
resembling a jagged accumulation of rock fragments including cobbles and boulders A a
lavas typically contain thick sequences of clinker rock rubble which bound a massive rock
internal flow core
Buried cavities are commonly encountered in basaltic lava flows especially the
pahoehoe lavas Cavities are formed when the lava was still in a molten state and
represent both lava tubes intra flow cavities and localized blisters and pockets inter flow
cavities Lava tubes are formed when molten lava drains quickly from the feeding conduit
of a cooling flow leaving a hollow tube like structure which may extend for large distances
longitudinally along the flow Lava tubes may also consist of a network of branching and
merging segments that may change directional course over relatively short distances
Lava tubes may extend from far inland all the way to the ocean since they serve as the
primary lava conduit for advancing flows Blisters and pockets are more isolated elliptical
shaped features which are generally smaller in horizontal and vertical extent
Basalt is considered to be a relatively permeable rock and can transmit water quite
readily in both the horizontal and vertical directions In general water is transmitted
through the porous rock matrix along joints fractures and inter flow contacts cavities and
along clinker layers The permeability of the clinker and cavities is high therefore they
serve as the major water transmission features

Existing Site Conditions
The project encompasses approximately 25 acres of vacant land located between
Poipu Road toward the north and Hoonani Road toward the south in the Koloa Poipu area
of the Island of Kauai Hawaii The site was recently cleared of vegetation to expose the
ground surface condition The cleared vegetation consisted mainly of Haole koa brush
dryland grass and cactus growth The project site slopes down toward the south ocean
with ground surface elevations ranging between about 50 feet Mean Sea Level MSL at
the northern portion of the site to about 20 feet at the southern portion of the site
The ground surfaces encompass gently rolling to relatively flat terrain with frequent
surface boulder deposits and scattered outcroppings of hard to very hard basalt rock
Some thin surface soils were observed mainly located at the southern and western
portions of the property where the ground surfaces appear to have been previously
graded The near surface soils consist of light brown to brown clayey silt with fine sand
soils believed to represent alluvial residual and some volcanic ash soil materials
The widespread surface boulders observed at the site generally range in size
between about 1 and 3 feet in diameter Some larger stockpiles or areas of boulder
concentration exist at scattered locations of the site Larger size boulders of about 3 to
6 feet in diameter comprise these features
An existing graded trail approximately 15 feet wide with an accompanying rock wall
embankment of about 100 feet in length was observed located at the southern portion of
the project site The rock wall feature appears to be part of an old trail or road alignment
which traverses northwest to southeast along the southern portion of the property Several
areas of earth cuts in the near surface basalt rock formation ranging up to about 6 feet in
height were observed bounding the old trail alignment The exposed basalt rock material
appears to be slightly weathered moderately jointed and hard to very hard with limited
clinker
An existing near surface lava tube cavity with two surface openings was
encountered at the central portion of the site The lava tube appears to have been partially
collapsed in between the two openings The lava tube has about 2 to 4 feet of hard rock
cover lava tube roof overlying the cavity The cavity is generally circular to elliptical in
shape and has a meandering trend The southern surface opening appears to pinch close
about 15 to 20 feet into the tube in the southwesterly direction The northern surface
opening appears to continue for an appreciable distance in the westerly direction

In an effort to reduce the potential for collapse of near surface cavities and or voids
within the property we recommend that subgrade preparation procedures be employed
within the limits of grading at the commencement of the mass grading operations for the
project The intent of the subgrade preparation procedure is to assist in detecting and
collapsing near surface cavities and or voids contained in the near surface rock
formations The subgrade preparation procedure recommended for this project is
consistent with the standard of practice for similar type projects and similar subsurface
rock conditions throughout the State of Hawaii
In general the subgrade preparation in cut areas consisted of proof rolling with a
Caterpillar 825 sheepsfoot roller or similar compactor minimum 20 tons for about eight
passes to help detect and collapse near surface cavities and or voids Where cavities
and or voids were encountered during the proof rolling operations the cavities and or
voids generally were exposed and backfilled in near level lifts
To reduce the possibility for collapse of deeper buried cavities which may
adversely affect the building foundation performance it may be desired to perform a
program of cavity probing and grouting along the major foundation systems Probing and
grouting essentially consists of drilling an array of probe holes to depths of about 10 feet
below the finish grade in earth cut areas Cement grout is pumped at low pressure in the
drilled probe holes in an effort to stabilize the buried voids encountered by the probe holes
against potential collapse
It is recommended that exploration of the subsurface conditions at the project site
be conducted to evaluate the subsurface conditions for design of the proposed
development The exploration could be better defined when the footprint of the
proposed structures and site grading criteria are known
