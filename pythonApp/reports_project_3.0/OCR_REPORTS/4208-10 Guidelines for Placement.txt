Dear Ms Rice
At your request we have evaluated the proposed use of rubble fill for the mass
grading planned at the subject site In general we believe that rubble fill may be used at the
deep fill areas of the site and we are providing the following guidelines for placement of
rubble fill to be followed by the grading contractor Goodfellow Brothers Inc We understand
that the rubble fill will be used as fill below the future house lots at the site We wish to
emphasize that the guidelines provided herein for placement of rubble fill is specifically for
the Sea Cliff Development at Mauna Lani Resort site and should not be used for other
locations without the written consent of Geolabs Hawaii
For purposes of our discussion on this project the construction debris which may be
used as fill will be termed rubble fill Rubble fill will be defined as intact concrete pieces
such as slabs walls and columns in a soil matrix In general rubble fill may be placed in fill
areas provided that the following recommendations are followed
1 Rubble fill should be spread out and must not be nested together Intact
pieces of concrete slabs walls and columns to be used as rubble fill should
be laid flat on the largest dimensions of the slabs walls and or columns
followed by a layer of structural fill material of approximately 6 to 12 inches
minimum thickness The structural fill material should consist of granular
materials of 6 inches in maximum dimension with sufficient fines to prevent the
occurrence of voids in the compacted mass The structural fill material should
be compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction prior to
placement of a subsequent layer of rubble fill

2 Subsequent layers of concrete slabs walls and or columns may be placed on
the previously compacted layer of rubble fill provided that a relatively smooth
and level surface is maintained for placement of the next layer of rubble fill
The fill surface should be level prior to placement of the next layer of rubble fill
to allow for proper seating of the concrete slabs walls and or columns
Rubble fill should be placed in a manner such that significant voids are
properly filled Fill material should be placed such that compaction equipment
will be able to compact the material
3 Any reinforcing bars rebars protruding beyond the edges of the concrete
pieces will need to be cut off Rebar protruding from the concrete pieces may
inhibit proper compaction of the rubble fill Therefore where this condition
occurs the rebar should be cut off In general rebar protruding more than
6 inches beyond the edges of the concrete pieces should be cut off This
should be further evaluated by a representative of Geolabs Hawaii during
construction
4 Rubble fill may be placed in fills up to a level of approximately 10 feet below
the finished subgrades of the subdivision The rubble fill should not protrude
into this 10 foot zone measured from the subgrade elevation Compacted fill
material should be used within 10 feet of the finished subgrades above the
rubble fill areas to serve as a cap for the rubble fill
5 Care must be exercised to avoid placement of rubble fill in the proposed utility
alignment and within the depths of the proposed utility lines or within 2 feet
below the inverts of utility lines to reduce the potential for encountering the
rubble fill during excavation for the utility trenches
6 The locations where rubble fill is used as fill material should be surveyed and
plotted on the as built grading plans This is to inform future owners of the
property that the rubble fill exists in the areas designated Removal of the
rubble fill should be performed with care as not to disturb the adjacent pieces
of the rubble fill during excavation 8
Fill materials should be compacted to obtain a dense and well compacted fill mass
It should be noted that if the rubble fill is not placed in such a manner to obtain a
well compacted fill mass the rubble fill will need to be removed and replaced to obtain a
well compacted fill mass Therefore a representative of Geolabs Hawaii should observe the
placement and compaction of the rubble fill mass on a near continuous basis to confirm that
our recommendations are executed in the field
