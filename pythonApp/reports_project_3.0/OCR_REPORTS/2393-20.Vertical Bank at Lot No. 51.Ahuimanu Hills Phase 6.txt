PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
Based on our on going mass grading observation and our field reconnaissance
the rear of Lot No 51 and its adjacent driveway appears to be situated at the top of a
near vertical bank of approximately 25 feet above the stream bed elevation
It is our opinion that a retaining structure should be considered at this area to
reduce the potential deterioration or failure of the existing steep bank which could
undermine the access driveway to the subject lot Several potential retaining structures
were evaluated including construction of a conventional retaining wall a cantilevered

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling one 1 boring to a
depth of approximately 51 5 feet below the existing ground surface The boring was
drilled near the top of the steep bank at the rear of the subject lot The approximate
location of the boring is shown on the Site Plan Plate 1

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the subsoil conditions encountered and the existing topography various
schemes were considered in the following sections of this report to reduce the potential
deterioration and erosion of the existing steep bank due to stream action
Four 4 alternative schemes were considered for use to reduce potential
deterioration and erosion of the stream bank These schemes include the construction
of a conventional retaining wall a cantilevered sheetpile wall a gabion retaining wall or
a grouted rip rap slope along the existing steep bank The following are detailed
discussions pertaining to each of these schemes
Conventional Retaining Wall
a a
A conventional retaining wall was considered impractical to reduce the potential
deterioration and erosion of the steep banks because of the large free standing
height and large footing size anticipated
In addition due to the presence of the existing stream in front of the wall it also
appears that the large wall footing may have to be embedded at least 3 to 5 feet
below the water level to reduce the potential of scouring of the footing subgrade
material This embedment will further increase the free standing height and the
size of the footing of the retaining wall making the wall construction more difficult
and costly

Cantilevered Sheetpile Wall
The construction of a cantilevered sheetpile wall to protect the existing steep bank
from further stream action was also considered However it should be noted that
there is a possibility that the sheetpile may not be able to achieve the desired
embedment due to the presence of boulders and stream bedrock In addition the
sheetpile driving operation will generate vibration waves which may affect the
stability of the existing steep bank Furthermore thick sheetpile sections will be
required to reduce the deflection of the cantilevered sheetpiles due to the
significant free standing height
Gabion Retaining Wall
The permeability of gabion retaining wall offers a distinct advantage over other
types of protection schemes especially in a high rainfall environment like the
Ahuimanu area However it is our understanding that the gabions has to be
imported from the Mainland which makes it relatively impractical due to the small
number of gabions required for this particular project
Grouted Rip Rap Slope
It appears that the potential deterioration and erosion of the steep banks may be
reduced by a grouted rip rap facing to resist future sloughing of the steep bank
The rip rap facing should be extended as far away from the steep bank as
possible A slope ratio of 1 H V may be used for the construction of the
grouted rip rap facing A prefabricated drainage board may be used between the
