COMPACTION TESTING REPORT
REMOVAL ACTION AT MAGAZINE LOCH SITE
PEARL HARBOR OAHU HAWAII
Dear Mr Yamasaki
This report summarizes our compaction testing services performed during the
earthwork operation of the Removal Action at Magazine Loch Site project from August 14
through in general accordance with our fee proposal dated March 30 2001
EARTHWORK OPERATION
The project site is located in the FISC and Naval Submarine Base SUBBASE
controlled areas of the Pearl Harbor Naval Complex The project site is located along the
head of the Magazine Loch within the paved parking area between Hunter and Gilmore
Avenues The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1
The backfilling and compaction of the collection trench was performed on the
mauka side of the Magazine Loch The collection trenches were backfilled in about 6 to
8 inch in loose lifts and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent of the maximum dry
density as determined in accordance with American Society of Testing and Material
ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor
During the backfilling and compaction operation of the collection trench samples of
the imported material utilized as backfill were obtained and transported to our office for
testing The maximum dry densities optimum moisture contents and grain size
distribution were established in our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM Test
Designations D 1557 Modified Proctor C 117 C 136 Grain size distribution The test
results are summarized on Plate 2 of this report

Periodic field density tests were performed during the backfilling operation A total
of 78 field density tests including retests of failed areas were performed using the nuclear
probe method in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 2922 The results of the
compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 5 of this report
These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed
engineered fills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and
using a tape measure from features shown on the construction plans These locations
and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods
used Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum
compaction was obtained and or approved by our field representative based on visual
observation and hand probing
Based on the results of the referenced tests it is our opinion that the earthwork
operation during construction for the above project tested by Geolabs in the period
covered by this report was performed in general conformance with the intent of the project
plans and specifications
LIMITATIONS
It should be recognized that there are variations in the accuracy and statistical
repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the earthwork construction and
materials Our services were performed in accordance with generally accepted standards
of geotechnical engineering practice no warranty is expressed or implied Provision of
compaction testing is not insurance nor does it guarantee construction of any type The
contractor shall retain responsibility for the quality of the work for adhering to the plans
and specifications and for repairing defects regardless of when they are found
