In accordance with our fee proposal dated we recently completed the
field exploration work for the PACFLT Sub Drive In MSF project near the entrance to
Middle Loch at Beckoning Point of Pearl Harbor Oahu Hawaii This letter summarizes the
work performed thus far on the project and presents our preliminary geotechnical
recommendations for the design of building foundations slabs on grade site grading and

geotechnical recommendations presented herein are based on the available subsurface
information contained in the RFP documents and our field exploration program conducted
at the project site A detailed summary of our findings and recommendations will be
contained in our geotechnical engineering report The final report should be consulted
when it becomes available
Project Considerations

new non magnetic concrete drive in deperming pier for submarine and a new rectifier
building The project is along the north shore of Waipio Peninsula near the entrance to
Middle Loch at Beckoning Point of Pearl Harbor on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project
location and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The site is occupied by an existing magnetic silencing facility which includes a
deperming pier and other landside support facilities This project will replace the existing
magnetic silencing facility This project includes both design bid build Part A and
design build Part B work The findings and preliminary recommendations presented in
this letter specifically pertain to the design build Part B portion of the work only In
general the design build Part B portion of the work involves primarily the Upland
Facilities such as the following

Subsurface Conditions
Our field exploration at the project site consisted of drilling and sampling
six borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 6 extending to depths of about
9 6 meters below the existing ground surface In addition two bulk samples of the
near surface soils designated as Bulk 1 and Bulk 2 were collected for laboratory CBR
tests to evaluate the pavement support characteristics The approximate boring and bulk
sample locations are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Based on our field exploration the project site generally is blanketed by a thin
surface layer of fill soils consisting of about 0 3 to 1 meter of loose silty gravel and soft
sandy silts Below the surface fill soils coralline detritus deposit consisting of medium
dense to very dense weakly cemented coralline sands and gravel with localized hard
coral and volcanic tuff ledges were encountered to depths of about 1 5 to 4 meters below
the existing ground surface Older alluvial deposits consisting of medium stiff to hard
sandy silts and sandy clays and medium dense silty sands and clayey sands were then
encountered and extended to the maximum depth explored of approximately 9 6 meters
below the existing ground surface

Building Foundations
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered and the structural loads
anticipated for the project we recommend utilizing shallow foundations consisting of
spread and or continuous footings to support the proposed building structures An
allowable bearing pressure of up to 192 kPa may be used for the design of foundations
bearing on the recompacted near surface soils or new compacted fills The bearing
pressure provided above is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by up to
50 percent for transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic forces
In general the bottom of footings should be embedded a minimum of 460 mm
below the lowest adjacent finished grade Footings adjacent to planned retaining walls
or other below grade structures should be embedded deep enough to avoid surcharging
the retaining wall foundations or below grade structure Foundations next to utility
trenches or easements should be embedded below a 45 degree imaginary plane
extending upward from the bottom edge of the utility trench or the footing should extend
to a depth as deep as the inverts of the utility lines This requirement is necessary to
avoid surcharging adjacent below grade structures with additional structural loads and
to reduce the potential for appreciable foundation settlement
If the footings are designed and constructed in accordance with the
recommendations presented herein we estimate total settlements of footings to be on
the order of about 25 mm with differential settlements between adjacent footings
supported on similar materials to be on the order of about 13 mm
Due to the potential cavities as commonly encountered in coralline deposits we
recommend implementing a program of probing and grouting of the foundation locations
during construction Probing and grouting requirements are discussed further in the
following Foundation Probing section of this letter

Foundation Probing
Because of the potential for presence of cavities and or voids within the coralline
deposit at the project site we recommend implementing a program of probing and
grouting the foundations during construction
For estimating purposes we recommend drilling probe holes at 3 meter on
centers for the continuous strip footings In addition probe holes should be drilled at
each isolated spread footing location one probe per 4 6 square meters The probe
holes should be at least 75 millimeters in diameter and should extend to a depth of at
least 3 meters below the planned bottom of foundation
If cavities and or voids are encountered or suspected during the probing
operation additional probe holes should be drilled at closer spacing to help delineate
the vertical and lateral extent of the cavity and or void The probe holes and cavities
discovered should be backfilled with Controlled Low Strength Material CLSM with a
slump of about 150 to 225 millimeters The CLSM should be injected pumped at low to
moderate pump pressures As an alternate to CLSM sand cement grout with a slump of
about 150 to 225 millimeters also may be used for the grouting operations
Because of the potential for encountering cavities and or voids at the project site
we recommend obtaining unit prices for additional probing and grouting during pricing
In addition the probe drill should be available on site until the probing and grouting
operations are completed The contractor also should be made aware that a longer lag
time between probing grouting operations and foundation construction might be
required in the construction schedule
The probing and grouting program should be conducted under the observation of
a Geolabs representative This would allow our firm to monitor the presence of cavities
and or voids and to allow additional recommendations to be made if excess grout take
and or changed conditions are observed

Slabs On Grade
Based on the anticipated design finished floor elevation of the building structures
the building slabs may be underlain by the existing soils or new compacted fills placed to
raise the existing ground to the finished subgrades
Available laboratory test results indicated that the surface clayey fill soils exhibit low
to moderate swelling potential with Expansion Index of about 13 and 37 Therefore we
recommend scarifying the clayey subgrade soils if exposed at the subgrade level to a
minimum depth of about 200 millimeters moisture conditioning to at least 2 percent above
the optimum moisture and recompacting to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
The slab subgrades should be kept moist prior to the final placement of concrete
Where shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of the subgrade we recommend
thoroughly moistening the soil to close the cracks Saturation and subsequent yielding of
the exposed subgrade due to inclement weather and poor drainage may require
over excavation of the soft areas and replacement with well compacted fill
Where coral formation is exposed and scarification of the subgrades is not
practical the subgrade soils should be proof rolled with a minimum 9 metric ton vibratory
drum roller for a minimum of eight passes to help detect and collapse near surface cavities
and or voids
Because the concrete slab on grade of the new rectifier substation and generator
buildings will be subjected to vibration due to equipment operations we recommend
providing a 200 mm layer of aggregate subbase in lieu of the normal gravel cushion and
moisture barrier between the slab concrete and the underlying soils The aggregate
subbase should consist of crushed basaltic aggregates compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative compaction For design of structural slabs supported on 200 millimeters
of aggregate subbase a modulus of subgrade reaction of about per
square meter per meter of deflection kKN m may be used for the compacted aggregate
subbase
The thickened edges of slabs adjacent to unpaved areas should be embedded at
least 300 millimeters below the lowest adjacent grade The area adjacent to the slabs
should be backfilled tightly against the slab edges with relatively impervious soils on site
clayey soils and graded to divert water away from the slabs to reduce the potential for
appreciable water ponding around the foundation

Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork areas within the contract grading limits should be
cleared and grubbed thoroughly Vegetation debris deleterious materials
and other unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed properly
off site to reduce the potential for contaminating the excavated materials to
be used as embankment fill materials
Slabs and foundations of the existing structures and pavements to be
demolished should be completely removed Over excavations resulting from
the demolition operations should be backfilled with compacted granular fill
material Existing underground utilities to be abandoned should be removed
and the resulting excavation should be properly backfilled with the
excavated on site materials The on site materials should be
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content placed in
150 to 200 mm level loose lifts and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent
relative compaction Utilities to be abandoned in place under the proposed
structure should be backfilled by pumping lean concrete or CLSM
Controlled Low Strength Material under low pressure
After clearing grubbing and demolition areas at grade or designated to
receive fills should be scarified to a minimum depth of about 200 millimeters
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and
proof rolled with a minimum 9 metric ton vibratory drum roller for a minimum
of eight passes to assist in detecting and possibly collapsing near surface
cavities The vibratory drum roller should be operated at a speed of about
4 to 5 5 kilometers per hour
Soft loose weak or yielding areas or cavities disclosed during site
preparation operations should be over excavated to expose firm ground and
the resulting excavation should be backfilled with structural fill material
well graded 75 mm minus granular materials compacted to a minimum
95 percent relative compaction The material resulting from the
over excavation should be removed and properly disposed of off site A
Geolabs representative should observe the proof rolling operations

Fill Materials
In general the excavated on site coralline materials may be used as a
source of fill material provided that deleterious materials such as vegetation
are removed and over sized materials greater than 75 millimeters in
maximum dimension are screened Imported fill materials required for the
project construction should consist of non expansive select granular
material such as crushed coral and basalt The material should be
well graded from coarse to fine with particles no larger than 75 millimeters in
largest dimension and should contain between 10 and 30 percent particles
passing the No 200 sieve The material should have a
laboratory CBR value of 20 or more and should have a maximum swell of
1 percent or less when tested in accordance with ASTM D 1883
Imported fill material should be tested for conformance with these
recommendations prior to delivery to the project site for its intended use An
accredited testing laboratory should test the imported fill materials for
conformance with these recommendations prior to delivery to the project site
for the intended use
Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
Select granular fill materials should be moisture conditioned to above the
optimum moisture placed in level lifts of about 200 millimeters in loose
thickness and compacted to at least 95 percent relative compaction
Aggregate base course and subbase materials should be
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content placed in level
lifts not exceeding 200 millimeters in loose thickness and compacted to a
minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a
percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in
accordance with ASTM D 1557 Optimum moisture is the water content
percentage by weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
Compaction should be accomplished by sheepsfoot rollers vibratory rollers
or other types of acceptable compaction equipment

Cut and Fill Slopes
In general we envision most of the cut slopes would expose medium dense
weakly cemented coralline detritus deposit We believe the cut slopes
planned may be designed with a slope inclination of two horizontal to
one vertical 2H 1V or flatter Permanent fill slopes may be designed with a
slope inclination of 2H 1V or flatter This assumes that the fill slopes will be
constructed using well graded granular materials
In addition fills placed on slopes steeper than 5H 1V should be benched
The fill slope face should be finished to a relatively smooth and
well compacted surface The fill slope face should be free of voids which
would allow erosion and migration of fines to occur In addition slope
planting or other means of slope protection should be provided as soon as
possible to reduce the potential for significant erosion of the finished slopes

Utility Trenches
We envision that new utility lines will be required to serve the proposed new
construction In general good construction practices should be utilized for the installation
and backfilling of the trenches for the new utilities at the site The contractor should
determine the method and equipment to be used for trench excavation subject to
practical limits and safety considerations In addition the excavations should comply with
the applicable local safety requirements The contractor should be responsible for trench
shoring design and installation
In general we recommend using granular bedding consisting of 150 millimeters of
No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation for support under the pipes
Free draining granular materials such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67
gradation should also be used for the initial trench backfill up to about 300 millimeters
above the pipes or 300 millimeters above the groundwater level to provide adequate
support around the pipes It is critical to use this free draining material to reduce the
potential for formation of voids below the haunches of pipes and to provide adequate
support for the sides of the pipes Improper trench backfill could result in backfill
settlement and pipe damage
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 300 millimeters above the
pipes or groundwater level to the top of the subgrade or finished grade should consist of
select granular material such as coral sands The backfill should be placed in maximum
250 mm level loose lifts and mechanically compacted to no less than 90 percent relative
compaction to reduce the potential for appreciable future ground subsidence Where
trenches are below pavement areas the upper 1 meter of the trench backfill below the
pavement grade should be compacted to no less than 95 percent relative compaction

Drainage
The finished grades outside the new facility should be sloped to shed water away
from foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also advised to install gutter
systems around the buildings and to divert discharge away from the foundation areas
Excessive landscape watering near the foundations and slabs should also be avoided
Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have concrete bottoms and drains to
reduce the potential for water infiltration into the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the above
foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation
and subsequent loss of strength The foundation excavations in sandy soils should be
properly backfilled against the walls or slab footings immediately after setting of the
concrete to develop its bearing capacity to support the design footing load Drainage
swales should be provided as soon as possible and should be maintained to drain surface
runoff away from the slab and footing foundations
