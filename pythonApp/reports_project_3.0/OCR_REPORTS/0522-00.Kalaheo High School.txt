INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our soil and foundation
investigation for the proposed Industrial Art Building located
at the Kalaheo High School In Kailua Oahu Hawaii
The scope of this study was to investigate the subject site
to evaluate subsurface conditions determine foundation
requirements and probable behavior of foundations for the pro
posed buildings

PROJECT CONSIDERATION
The proposed building will be a 2 story building of masonry
wall construction 125 by 35 feet in plan dimension An
addition of 75 feet by 35 feet is planned for the future
Maximum vertical dead plus live loads will be on the order of
70 kips for columns and on the order of 10 kips per lineal
foot for wall loads Minimal grading operations will be
required to prepare the site for construction
SITE CONDITIONS
Surface
The proposed building site is located on the Kalaheo High
School campus near the junction of Makapu boulevard and HC D
quarry road on Kailua Oahu Hawaii
The project site is presently used as a baseball field fairly
level and covered with grass
Geology
The geologic history of the area is characterized by the Kailua
Volcanic Series of Tertiary time The Kailua Yolcanic Series
is greatly altered by hydrothermal actions and lava vents were

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS

General
The site is suitable for construction of the proposed structure
supported on conventional shallow footings with concrete slabs
On grade
Foundation
The upper onsite clayey silt fill is moderately stiff and can
adequately support foundations at shallow depths A bearing
pressure of 2500 pounds per square foot may be used for dead
plus live loads Thts value may be increased by one third
when considering wind or seismic loads Footings should be

W
placed a minimum of 18 inches below final adjacent grade
Continuous footings should have a minimum width of 12 inches
The site soils are moderately expansive therefore a minimum
of two No 4 reinforcing bars one top and bottom should
be provided in all interior and exterior continuous footings
No additional reinforcement for expansive forces will be
required in spread footings
Settlements
Maximum total anticipated settlements will be on the order of
one half inch Differential settlements between columns and
walls will be less than one quarter inch
Slabs On Grade
The upper site soils have exhibited medium expansion It is
therefore recommended that slab on grade be placed on a mini
mum of 12 inches of non expansive material A capillary break
consisting of a four inch layer of gravel should be provided
directly beneath the slab Slabs should be provided with a
minimum of 6 x 6 10 welded wire mesh In areas where damp
ness in the floor would be undesirable or where moisture
sensitive flooring such as carpeting or vinyl asbestos tile

W
is to be used a plastic membrane should be placed over the
gravel A one tnch protective cushion of sand should be
placed over the membrane which will also aid in curing of the
slab The sand may be deleted if the slab is cured for at
least seven days and extreme care is taken to avoid puncturing
the membrane
All slabs on grade should be structurally separated from column
and wall footings
Lateral Loads
Lateral loads on the buildings may be resisted by either passive
pressures or by friction along the base of footings or a combi
nation of both Passive pressures of natural and compacted fill
soils may be assumed to be equal to the pressure developed by
a fluid with a density of 250 pounds per cubic foot acting from
the adjacent ground surface The coefficient of friction
between footings and soil may be taken as 0 35
Site Preparation
After all deleterious materials have been removed the ground
surface should be stripped to remove all vegetation The slab
areas should then be overexcavated to the depth recommended in
this report All areas to receive fill should then be

Field Exploration
The subsurface soil conditions at the site were explored by
drilling four test borings utilizing truck mounted rotary
equipment The depths of the borings ranged from 20 to 40
feet below the existing ground surface The locations of the
borings are shown on the site Plan Plate 1
The soils were classified by visual and textural examinations
in the field by our geologist who continuosly monitored the
drilling operations The classifications were verified by
inspection and testing in the laboratory All soils were
classified in accordance with the Unified Soil Classification
System Graphical representations of soils encountered are
presented on thelog of borings Plate A 1A through A 1D Soil
samples were obtained by driving a 2 4 inch I D sampler with
a 140 pound hammer free falling a distance of 30 inches The
blow counts to drive the sampler are shown on the log of
borings at the appropriate sample depth
