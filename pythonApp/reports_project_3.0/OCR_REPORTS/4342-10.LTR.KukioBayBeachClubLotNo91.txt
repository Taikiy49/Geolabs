Regional Geology
The Island of Hawaii is the largest in the Hawaiian Archipelago and covers an area
of approximately 4 000 square miles The island was formed by the activity of five shield
volcanoes which include the following Kohala long extinct Mauna Kea activity during
recent geologic time Hualalai last erupted in 1801 and Mauna Loa and Kilauea both
still active
The project site is situated on the northwestern flank of Hualalai Mountain which
makes up the west central portion of the island The lava formation observed at the project
site prior to site grading activities appears to consist of both a a and pahoehoe type lava
flows which spread and ponded as they approached the ocean A a lava typically is
characterized by a porous rough and irregular flow surface resembling a jagged
accumulation of rock fragments including cobbles and boulders Pahoehoe lava is
characterized by a smooth rope like or billowy surface and an internal structure of
vesicular porous rock
Cavities commonly are encountered in basaltic lavas especially the pahoehoe type
lava Cavities form when the lava is still in a molten state and they represent both lava
tubes intra flow cavities and blisters and pockets inter flow cavities Lava tubes form
when molten lava drains from the cooling flow leaving a hollow tube like structure which
may extend for a large longitudinal distance along the flow Blisters and pockets are
generally smaller in horizontal extent
Basalt is considered to be a relatively permeable rock and can transmit water quite
readily in both the horizontal and vertical directions In general water is transmitted
through the porous rock matrix along joints fractures and inter flow contacts cavities and
along clinker layers The permeability of the clinker and cavities is high therefore they
serve as the primary water transmission features
Subsurface Conditions
As previously mentioned the Kukio Bay Beach Club project site is in the
Kaupulehu area in the District of North Kona on the Island of Hawaii In general the
original topography of the site prior to site grading activities consisted of irregular
or hummocky lava flow terrain with frequent topographic depressions separated by
adjacent higher relief mounds and low ridge features
Based on observations during the mass grading operations the project site
generally is underlain by medium hard to hard basalt formation with interbedded layers of
clinker materials It should be noted that lava tubes cavities and blisters commonly are
encountered in basaltic lava flows in the project site vicinity In addition numerous lava

Discussion and Recommendations
Based on the grading plan for the project the original ground surface elevations at
the building pad areas of Lot No 91 ranged from about 24 to 46 feet Mean Sea Level
MSL Lot No 91 generally was mass graded to a finished pad elevation of 44 feet
MSL Based on a pad elevation of 44 feet MSL mass grading at Lot No 91 generally
consisted of placement of up to about 20 feet of new fills and cuts of about 2 feet to
achieve the design grades
During the mass grading operations extensive subgrade preparation procedures
were employed within the limits of grading at the commencement of the mass grading
operations for the project to reduce the potential for collapse of near surface cavities
and or voids within the lots The intent of the subgrade preparation procedures is to assist
in detecting and collapsing near surface cavities and or voids contained in the porous
near surface materials It should be noted that the subgrade preparation procedures
employed for this project are consistent with the standard of practice for similar type
projects and similar subsurface conditions along the Kohala Coast on the Island of Hawaii
In general the subgrade preparation in fill areas consisted of ripping the original
ground to a depth of about 3 feet or until refusal using a ripper tooth of a large bulldozer to
assist in detecting and collapsing near surface cavities and or voids contained in the
porous near surface materials After the fill surfaces have been ripped the ground
generally was leveled with the use of a Komatsu 375 bulldozer working the surface for
about four passes to provide a relatively level surface After leveling of the rugged surfaces
with the bulldozer the fill subgrades generally were proof rolled with a Caterpillar 825
sheepsfoot roller compactor minimum 20 tons for about eight passes to help detect and
collapse near surface cavities and or voids
Fill materials consisting of some cobbles and boulders and structural fills were
placed in the fill areas of the project site Structural fills consisted of well graded granular
materials generally smaller than 6 inches in dimension In general the fill materials within
the top 2 feet of the lots within the pad areas consisted of structural fill materials It
should be noted that general fills consisting of cobbles and boulders of up to about 2 feet
in dimension generally were placed at least about 2 feet below the finished grades within
the pad areas in fill areas and choked off with finer sized materials In addition boulders
of up to about 5 feet in dimension generally were placed at least about 4 feet below the
finished grades within the pad areas and choked off with finer sized materials Boulders
