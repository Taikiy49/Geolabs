INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of a soil engineering investigation
performed for the proposed dredging berth area at Wharf S 21 in Pearl
Harbor Oahu Hawaii
The general location of the project site is shown on the Project
Location Map Plate l

PROJECT CONSIDERATION
It is proposed to construct a transitional slope adjacent to the
seaward side of the existing S 21 Wharf which will require a clear
berthing depth of 39 feet below MLLW at 20 foot set back from the edge of
the wharf The slope criteria for the Y 2 Berth area located to the
northwest of the pilot boat house has not been determined yet
In addition an approximately 300 by 1600 feet area adjacent to both
berthing wharfs will be dredged from the present nominal water depth of 35
feet below MLLW to a proposed depth of 39 feet below MLLW
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of the investigation was to explore the subsurface
conditions below the site to provide dredging information and pertinent
soil parameters for the design of the transitional slope adjacent to the
wharfs
The scope of our investigation which was performed in general
accordance with our proposal of August 27th included a review of oid
plans and reports site reconnaissance drilling and sampling of five 5
land and five 5 offshore borings laboratory soil testing engineering
analysis utilizing the field and laboratory data and report preparation
Details of our field exploration and laboratory testing are presented
in the appendices of this report
SITE CONDITION
The project site is situated on the western end of Magazine Loch in
the Pearl Harbor Submarine Base on the island of Oahu Hawaii
GEOLABS HAWAII
I D 1709 Page 3
Wharf S 21 was constructed in 1942 and 1943 The entire deck area of
the wharf which measured at about 48 by 800 feet is supported on 13 inch
square precast concrete piles Center to center spacings of the piles in
the directions parallel and perpendicular to the wharf are approximately
8 and 16 feet respectively Behind Wharf S 21 is a curtain wall which is
formed by a series of steel sheetpile Previous undermining of a section
of this sheetpile wall had resulted in loss of backfill ground surface
subsidence and pavement distresses which had required periodic repair work
to be carried out
The Y 2 Berth is located west of and across the pilot boat house from
Wharf S 21 The berth is formed by a concrete bulkhead wall which
measures at about 300 feet in length The unpaved area behind the wall is
presently used for storage purposes
The proposed dredge area extends about 300 feet offshore from the
wharf The mudline is presently about 35 to 41 feet below MSL
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The geologic conditions underlying the site are the result of a
complex series of depositional and erosional events brought on by
glacioeustatic sea level changes and post erosional volcanic activities
during the Pleistocene Following the cessation of activity of the Koolau
Volcano alluvial materials were deposited in a deltaic or estuarine
environment in the area of Pearl Harbor
The sea level fluctuated several times receding perhaps 300 feet
below and rising about 95 feet above the present sea level
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1589 00
I D 1709 Page 4
Concurrent with a rise in sea level during late Pleistocene era
eruption of the Salt Lake Makalapa member of the Honolulu Volcanic Series
mantled the older alluvium with thick deposits of volcanic ash which
consolidated to form volcanic tuff locally called mudrock layers
Subsequent erosion dissected and thinned those mantles of tuff
During the Recent epoch as sea level rose to its current stand the
area waS covered with soft and loose lagoonal marine deposits Those
deposits were subsequently covered by man made fill placed during the
middle portion of this century for land reclamation projects during the
construction of the existing Naval Submarine Base

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Slope
Wharf S 21
The existing slopes below the deck of Wharf S 21 were found to have a
were further found to consist of layers of very soft silt and loose
to medium loose sand and gravel
An underwater slope stability analysis was carried out using the
computer program STABR The results of the analysis indicated that
with the stabilizing effect of the existing piles supporting the deck
of Wharf S 21 the underwater slopes below the deck are in general
stable under static condition The analysis further indicated that a
deep seated slope failure through the medium loose sand and gravel
layer was unlikely However we believe that some minor surface
sloughing of the very soft silt is likely to happen when subjected to
disturbance caused by the dredging operation or under seismic
condition Therefore some minor maintenance dredging should be
anticipated in the future
For the design of the transition slope in front of Wharf S 21 three
major design criteria should be satisfied

1 During the excavation of the slope the amount of soil removed
from the toe of the existing slope below the deck of Wharf S 21
should be kept at a minimum so that the stability of the upper
slope may not be affected
2 The transition slope which does not benefit from the stabilizing
effect of the existing piles should remain
3 At a location 20 feet from the seaward edge of the wharf the
depth to mudline should not exceed 39 feet below MLLW
Based on the above criteria we recommend that a slope of 2 1 H V
be used for the transition slope in front of the Wharf S 21 All the
slopes are anticipated to consist of very soft silt and loose to
medium loose sand and gravel Two cross sections through Wharf S 21
showing the existing and the recommended slope configuration are
presented on Plates 3 and 4

of about H V At a distance of 50 feet in front of the

It is our opinion that the transition slope in front of Y 2 Berth can

Dredging
Wharf S 21
The proposed dredged area in front of Wharf S 21 is shown on the Site
Plan Plate 2
From the results of our exploration and previous borings by others
the entire area should be underlain to the dredged depth by recent
sediments consisting of soft silt clay and loose sand The existing
depth from MLLW to the mudline was found to vary between 35 and 41
feet with an average of about 36 5 feet Therefore the average
dredged depth is anticipated to be about 2 5 feet
For shallow dredging of loose and soft material conventional
mechanical or hydraulic dredging equipment can be used Overdredging
in area close to the transitional slope should be avoided in order to
prevent slope instability

Y 2 Berth
As shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 over 50 of the proposed dredged
area in front of Y 2 Berth is already below the proposed dredged
elevation Previous borings indicate that the area should also be
underlain by recent sediments However within an area of about 30
feet seaward of Y 2 Berth our borings encountered a layer of
slightly cemented sand of coral gravel The extent of this slightly
cemented layer within other parts of the proposed dredged area is
currently unknown and can only be determined by additional field
exploration However based on the existing available information
about this slightly cemented layer we believe that it should have
very little impact on the dredging operation We also believe that
the material can be excavated with conventional mechanical or
hydraulic dredging equipment
Although our scope of work does not include analytical laboratory
testing to determine the chemical content of the dredged material we feel
that special procedures for the disposal of dredged materials may be
required Some of the soil samples that we obtained close to or below the
deck of Wharf S 21 were found to contain residue of petroleum and other
organic products

are consistent with the findings of our investigation
