As requested we have made a preliminary geologic reconnaissance of
the near surface soil conditions below the above referenced site The
purpose of the reconnaissance was to evaluate the general suitability of
the near surface soils for development in order to aid in the appraisal
of the land value
The reconnaissance consisted of a review of available soils and
geologic maps and literature concerning the area b a general geologic
reconnaissance of the project site and c reviewing the tax map plan
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The site is located along the north side of Ala Lani Street on a
very steep rocky hillside that slopes down towards the south from a
ridge line at approximately 1 2 to 1 1 H V slopes with steeper
gradients in localized rock cliffs

The two lots cover an area of about 0 88 acre The area is mostly
overgrown with bushes and small trees Weathered rock outcrops were
noted at several locations
Geology
The project site is located on the southern flank of the elongated
Koolau Volcanic Range which forms the southeastern portion of Oahu
Hawaii The range was formed during the Tertiary Period of pahoehoe
and aa basalt lava flows from a rift zone roughly paralleling the
existing mountain trends
Some of the exposed rock outcrops have undergone weathering and
decomposition of the lava rock formation
Soil Conditions
The information on the soils likely to be encountered in the
project area was obtained from the Soil Survey of the Island of Kauai
and Lanai State of Hawaii by USDA Soil
Conservation Service August 1972 From their survey and visual
observations of the ground surface it appears that the area is
underlain by rockland where the exposed rock covers up to 90 of the
area
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on limited data and a field reconnaissance of the area our
general observations and recommendations are presented below
General
Because of the very steep topography at these two lots it is
difficult to evaluate the feasibility of excavating into the lower
portion without a topographic map It does not appear feasible to
flatten the steep natural hillside slope and therefore the lower
portion of the lot is very susceptable to rockfalls

Our reconnaissance indicated that portions of the fractured rock
outcrops may be unstable and could be a potential danger to property and
people located downslope
The geological reconnaissance indicates that although residential
houses could possibly be constructed on the lots the houses would be
very susceptable to rockfalls This potential problem should be
transmitted to future buyers
In our opinion these lots are marginally suitable for development
LIMITATIONS
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations
made in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation
engineering principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all
other warranties either expressed or implied
The recommendations submitted in this report are general and not
intended to be used for any design purposes since they are based on
brief and limited preliminary geologic site reconnaissance of the site
and a literature review of available soil and geologic information
Unanticipated soil and rock conditions such as gully deposit or
pockets of clays ash fill cavities etc are commonly encountered
and may occur in localized areas Therefore additional field
exploration and studies should be made after plans are further developed
before design and construction of the project
This report has been prepared in order to assist the developer in
determining the suitability of this site for development
