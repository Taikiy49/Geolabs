MICROPILE OBSERVATION REPORT
STRUCTURAL INVESTIGATION AT
MAKAKAE HOUSING AT 21 SOUTH KUAKINI STREET
Dear Mr Muranaka
As requested Geolabs provided geotechnical engineering observation and
consultation services during the micropile foundation installation for the Makamae Housing
at 21 South Kuakini Street Structural Repair project in the Nuuanu area of Honolulu on the
Island of Oahu Hawaii
This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations performed
during the micropile installation for the above project In general the purpose of our
geotechnical engineering services was to observe the micropile foundation installation for
general conformance with the project documents As part of the project we performed
two load tests on two sacrificial micropiles prior to production micropile installation and
conducted proof tests on all production micropiles installed The results of the load test
program are also presented in this report
In addition one micropile designated as Micropile No 15 failed to resist the testing
load during the proof test and compression test An Atlas Pier was installed as a remedial
measure Detailed discussions are presented in this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located at the existing Makamae Housing at 21 South Kuakini
Street in the Nuuanu area of Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii In general slab and
wall distresses were observed at Makamae Housing project Based on the information
provided the observed slab and wall distresses were confined to the mauka and ewa ends
of the existing building It appears that portions of the ground floor slabs on grade and the
walls of the existing building have experienced continued distress

Micropile Pre Production Load Testing
In accordance with the project specifications we conducted two static load tests on
two sacrificial micropiles designated as 1 and No LT 1
is located between Axis 3 and 4 1 and Micropile No LT 2 is located between

and LT 2 on and respectively utilizing a

existing ground surface respectively
Based on our field observations during the micropile installation we encountered a
basalt layer at depths of about 12 feet and 19 feet below the existing ground surface
Overlying the basalt layer it appears that alluvial deposits consisting of highly expansive
adobe clays were encountered After reaching the micropile tip elevations 1 125 inch
diameter all thread center bars Grade 75 with centralizers were installed to the full depth
of the drilled and cased holes A sand cement grout was used to backfill the drilled holes
After the holes were filled the casings were withdrawn approximately 4 feet and
re advanced into the holes for about 1 foot The bottom 3 feet of the micropiles was
uncased to provide a bond length within the basalt layer

Micropile Installation
The production micropiles installed at the site consisted of the cased micropile
system The components of the cased micropile system include an unbonded length
permanent steel casing a bonded length at the bottom to provide vertical load resistance
primarily from friction between the grout and surrounding soils and a steel reinforcement
bar extending the full depth of the drilled hole
The micropiles were installed by drilling 5 5 inch outside diameter steel casing with
a Beretta T59 track mounted drill rig equipped with a down the hole hammer drill bit The
steel casing was extended down to a depth to provide a minimum bond length of 3 feet in
the basalt layer The steel casing was left in the drilled hole and cleaned out by blowing
air through the drill rods After the drilled hole was cleaned out a single epoxy coated
steel reinforcement bar with a diameter of 1 inch was inserted into the drilled hole down to
near the bottom of the hole The micropile was grouted by tremie methods until relatively
clean grout was observed at the top of the steel casing The grout consisted of a
sand cement mixture The steel casing was extracted a length of about 4 feet and
immediately pushed back for about 1 foot to provided a minimum bond length of 3 feet
with a good bond between the steel casing and grout bulb of micropile
The production micropiles generally extended to depths ranging from about 11 6 to
44 8 feet below the existing ground The production micropiles extended about 3 feet into
the underlying basalt formation for allowable compressive load capacities of 50 kips The
micropile installation summary is presented on the Micropile Installation Summary
Plates 3 1 through 3 4
Micropile Proof Testing
In accordance with the project documents all production micropiles installed were
subjected to proof tests In general the proof tests consisted of loading the micropiles in
tension to a maximum load of 66 5 kips which is 133 percent of the allowable
compressive load capacities of 50 kips Geolabs Inc measured and recorded the
associated movements of the micropile head at each load increment up to the maximum
load In general the maximum load was held for a minimum of 10 minutes and the creep
movement between 1 and 10 minutes were less than 0 04 inches
However Micropile No 15 experienced continuous upward movement when the
test load reached approximately 30 kips during the proof test It appeared that this
micropile failed to resist the test load of 30 kips which was less than the design load of
50 kips Based on the test criteria Micropile No 15 did not pass the proof test

Re Test Micropile No 15
The proof test is a tension test designed to test the friction resistance along the
micropile length This test basically ignores the end bearing capacity of the micropiles
In order to further verify the micropile load bearing capacity we conducted a
compression test on Micropile No 15
In general the micropile was loaded to a maximum test load of 75 kips in
6 25 kip load increments Each load increment was held for at least 5 minutes The
test load was held for 240 minutes 4 hours at 75 kips It was observed that the
micropile top displacement at the 75 kip test load was about 0 43 inches at the first
hour The load test readings and pertinent information are presented on the Micropile No
15 Compressive Load Test Data Plates 5 1 through 5 4
Based on the load test results it appeared that micropile top displacement was
about 0 5 inches under the test load of 68 75 kips However the total micropile top
displacement increased significantly and reached over 1 inch under the test load of
75 kips This total displacement does not meet the performance criteria of micropile
We believe that Micropile No 15 has a bearing capacity of about 60 kips which
is less than the ultimate design load of 100 kips 50 kips allowable load capacity with a
safety factor of 2 Therefore remedial measure is required
ATLAS RESISTANCE PIER
The Atlas Resistance Pier system consists of pier pipe friction reduction collar
and top pier platform The segments of pier pipes are pushed into the ground by hydraulic
pressures to develop friction resistance between pipes and surrounding soils A pressure
gauge is used to measure the pushing force acting on the pier pipes The pier pipe
installation is terminated when pier pipes are installed in proper depths and hydraulic
pressure reaches desired value After the pier pipes are in place top pier platform is
installed on top of the pier pipes and under the existing footing to transfer the loads from
structure to pier pipes The pier may be pre loaded to a required pressure before locking
An Atlas Resistance Pier was installed on to supplement the
load capacity of Micropile No 15 The pier was located at the side of the existing footing
in line with Micropile No 15 The pier was installed to a depth of about 12 feet with a
resistant force of approximately 40 kips After the installation the pier was locked up with
