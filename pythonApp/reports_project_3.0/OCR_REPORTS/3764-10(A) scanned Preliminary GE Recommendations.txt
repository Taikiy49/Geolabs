This letter summarizes the preliminary geotechnical engineering recommendations
for the proposed Water Transmission Replacement Line project for Molokai Ranch Ltd
The preliminary recommendations pertaining to thrust block design and earth pressures
acting on the water pipes provided herein are for information purposes only based on the
subsurface conditions encountered in our field exploration from last week More detailed
discussions and recommendations will be presented in our forthcoming final report
Project Considerations
We understand that it is desired to install 8 inch and 12 inch replacement
waterlines for Molokai Ranch Ltd on the Island of Molokai Hawaii The proposed project
will consist of replacing approximately 48 000 lineal feet of waterline starting from about
the existing water meter connection at Kualapuu to the existing Kaluakoi Reservoir The
approximate locations of the proposed waterlines are shown on the General Site Plan
Plate 1
In general the proposed project will include approximately 48 000 lineal feet of
trench excavation water pipe installation and trench backfilling We understand that the
proposed waterline inverts will generally be about 4 to 8 feet below the existing ground
surface In addition the proposed water main alignment will traverse Manawainui and
Kaluapeelua Gulches Based on the preliminary information provided we understand

Existing Conditions
The proposed 8 inch and 12 inch waterlines start from the proposed Molokai
Ranch Reservoir and extend through Hoolehua Plain to Kaluakoi Reservoir on the island
of Molokai Hawaii Based on the preliminary plans provided the proposed waterlines will
cross Manawainui Gulch at about Station 99 50 and will cross Kaluapeelua Gulch at
about Station 229 50 as shown on the General Site Plan Plate 1 Elevations of the
existing ground surface along the proposed waterline alignment vary from about 255 to
1 372 feet Mean Sea Level MSL
At the eastern end of the proposed waterline from about Station 00 00 to
Station 94 00 the terrain slopes slightly down along the alignment Much of the
alignment appears to run along the existing dirt road Where the waterline diverges away
from the existing dirt road moderately dense vegetation was observed
Outcrops of basalt andesite rock formation are abundant in areas around
Manawainui Gulch between Station 94 00 and Station 106 00 The vegetation is
generally moderate to heavy and the slope banks of the gulch are as steep as
Maunaloa Highway and runs nearly straight to the existing control tank and pump station
at about Station 373 00 Most of the area between Station 106 00 and Station 373 00
is existing farmland and the terrain is nearly level
The proposed waterline alignment travels up a hill from the control tank area to the
existing Kaluakoi Reservoir at Puu Nana Although the areas are only lightly vegetated
slopes as steep as exist and boulders on the order of 1 to 2 feet in diameter are
prominent Most of the existing dirt roads have been abandoned and access is generally
by foot or by horse only

along the proposed alignment In addition a site reconnaissance was conducted along
the proposed alignment The approximate locations of the borings are shown on the

In general our borings encountered stiff to very stiff silty clays residual soils

Discussion and Recommendations
In general this letter provides a summary of the materials encountered in our
exploration conducted last week and presents our preliminary recommendations for the
thrust block design and earth pressures acting on the waterlines only Detailed
discussion and recommendations will be presented in our forthcoming final report when
all the laboratory testing is completed
We envision that lateral and vertical thrust blocks will be required at some locations
along the waterline alignment to provide resistance to the thrust forces due to the
pressurized pipes For preliminary design of the lateral thrust blocks the Pressure
Diagram provided on Plate 5 may be used as a guide For the lateral thrust blocks the
thrust force acting on the pipes may be resisted by two components of passive earth
pressure friction and cohesion acting against the near vertical face of the thrust block
Resistance due to frictional passive pressure may be calculated using an equivalent fluid
pressure of 170 pounds per square foot per foot of depth p c f above the groundwater

To estimate the sliding resistance between the base of the thrust block and the
supporting soils a coefficient of friction of 0 3 may be used In addition an adhesion
factor of 200 p s f may also be used in conjunction with the frictional resistance Based
on our site reconnaissance the soils surrounding the pipes were found to have been
eroded away and the existing waterlines were exposed in many areas Therefore
resistance to lateral loads should only be considered from the base of the thrust blocks
Sliding resistance due to the side walls and top of the thrust block should be neglected
These values assume that the subgrade soil is well compacted and the thrust block
concrete is cast neat against the sides of the trench
For vertical thrust blocks resistance to movement may be calculated from the
dead weight of the concrete thrust block only As mentioned earlier the soils surrounding
the pipes were observed to have been eroded away and existing waterlines were
exposed in many areas Therefore resistance due to the weight of the overburden soils
and sliding resistance along the side walls should also be neglected
Loads on buried pipes are influenced by the width of the trench the size of the
pipes the unit weight of the backfill material and the frictional resistance between the
backfill material and the trench walls For a preliminary estimate of the vertical loads on
the buried waterline pipes an average unit weight of 120 p c f for the backfill material and
a coefficient of friction of 0 3 between the backfill and trench sidewalls may be used
Earth pressure forces upon the pipe increase rapidly with increased width of the trench
Therefore the width of the waterline trench should be kept to a minimum Traffic loads on
the buried pipes should also be considered where the proposed waterline alignment is
located within traveled ways

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling 24 borings to depths
of 5 to 10 feet below the existing ground surface at the approximate locations shown on
the General Site Plan Plate 1 and Detailed Site Plans Plates 2 through 4 The borings
were drilled using a truck mounted drill rig equipped with continuous flight augers and
hollow stem augers
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineers who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by
testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified
Soil Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representation of the materials
encountered are provided on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 24
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils
by driving a 3 inch O D Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling
30 inches The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the last 12 inches of an 18 inch
drive are shown on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
