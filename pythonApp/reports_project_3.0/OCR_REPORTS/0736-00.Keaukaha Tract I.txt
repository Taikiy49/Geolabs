INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this soil investigation ts to determine the
nature of the soils underlying the project site and to
provide recommendations for the design of the proposed
roadway and drainage improvements
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed development scheme consists of sections of
roadways to be tmproved in three tncrements Increment I
includes sections of roadway on King
and Kapil Avenues Increment II its the alley between Todd
and Desha Avenues Increment III ts the alley between Desha
Avenue and Kalantanaole Street The proposed roadway loca
tions are shown on the enclose Site Plan Plate 1
In general grading for the proposed roadway construction
will be less than 10 feet of cut and fill
SITE CONDITIONS
The project roadways are located in
The general area in whitch the roadways are to be constructed
is bordered on the north by Kalantanaole Avenue and on the
south by General Lyman Field The west boundary lies just
beyond and roughly parallel to Andrews Avenue The total
length of the project roadways is approximately 2 miles
The relief of the general area is basically level when
considering vertical differences in elevation over 100 s
of feet in horizontal distance but locally over short
horizontal distances tn the 10 s of feet the ground
surface is very irregular with many dips and rises

The vertical difference between these local high and
low elevation points are as much as 10 to 15 feet for
the most extreme cases but usually vary from 3 to 5
feet Two muddy areas with ponded water were noted on
the project roadway in the northeastern end of Increment
III between Andrews Avenue and the property line appro
ximately 500 to 700 feet east of Andrews Avenue inter
section
Several relatively small cavities were noticed open to
the surface throughout the project roadway A larger
cave like cavity also open to the surface about 5 by 8
feet and approximately 6 feet deep was noticed in the
southwestern end of Increment III between Kauhane Avenue
and the property line approximately 450 feet west of
Kauhane intersection
Access through the site during the time of the investigation
was by way of cleared trails There were no existing struc
tures present on the project roadway areas Ground elevations
vary between 5 and 55 feet The vegetatton consists of a
moderate to dense growth of brush and trees
Geology
The rock encountered at the project site comprises of a por
tion of the eastern flank near the coastline of the Mauna
Loa shield volcano More specifically tt represents the
lava of Katu Volcanic Series which occurred during the
Pleistocene Epoch
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P 4
The rock throughout the project roadways and the sur
rounding area primarily solidified from a pahoehoe lava
flow Contrary to the usual relatively smooth surface
of a pahoehoe lava flow the project site s rock has an
irregular surface with many relatively small dips and
rises These dips and rises were probably due to the
wrinkling of the flow surface or crust as the semi har
dened crust was pushed and contorted by the more fluid
Oncoming lavas In certain areas of the site especially
in the northeast end of Increment III some very smooth
lava surfaces in a disarrayed uplifted attitude tumuli
were present again suggesting a bunched up or heaved crust
which was once level
Cavities and lava tubes are usually a common feature of
pahoehoe lava flows and some relatively small cavities
were noticed throughout the site open to the surface
Subsurface Explorations
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by
drilling four test borings ranging from 13 to 15 feet
deep at the locations shown on the enclosed Site Plan
Plate 1
Competent rock was encountered at or near surface and
subsequent diamond bit coring was used from ground surface
down to the full depth of all the borings

W P 5
A detailed description of the field exploration methods
is given in the appendix along with the boring logs
Soil Conditions
The exploration disclosed essentially uniform soil and
rock conditions within the project area The site appears
to be underlain by a surface layer 0 to 1 foot of soft
brown volcanic ash over a fractured to partially fractured
vesicular basalt rock formation
Up to about 3 feet deep of soft mud with ponding water was
noted at the northeastern end of Increment III between
Andrews Avenue and the property line The soft mud appears
to be located on a drainageway area
Groundwater was noted only in Boring No 2 about 12 feet
below the existing ground surface during the field explora
tion
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration indicated a thin surface layer of soft
volcanic ash over vesicular basalt formation To provide
more uniform support the soft volcanic ash material should
be removed and roadway pavements should bear on the under
lying lava rock or compacted fill
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P 6
As indicated in our boring logs during the coring it
was noted that the drilling water did not return to the
surface below certain depth in the boring This indicated
that the underlying basalt rock could be used to dispose
of surface run off tn this project
Large cave like cavities were noted outside the improvement
area If cavities are encountered during the grading opera
tion they should either be backfilled or the pavement should
be designed structurally to bridge over the openings
Earthwork and Compaction Requirements
Cuts and fills generally less than 10 feet high are conten
plated for the site
Vegetation debris and surface ash should be stripped off
from the site prior to grading
Most of the excavation will probably be in fractured lava
rock It is our opinion that it can be accomplished by
hard ripping with heavy equipments If sufficient fines
cannot be generated by the excavation operation some
blending with finer off site borrow materials such as
aa select borrow might be necessary to obtain a better
graded fill material In general material larger than
8 inches should not be used in the fill
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P 7
Rocks with diameters greater than 8 inches and less than
2 feet may be used tn the deep fill at least 3 feet below
finish grade provided that they are well embedded and the
voids filled with smaller pieces and other filler materials
No rocks should be allowed to cluster in piles
Fill should be placed in 12 inch loose lifts and compacted
to a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density established
by ASTM Test Method D 1557 Conventional sand cone field
density test is generally not practicable in the excavated
broken up lava rock material Instead a testing program
to determine the number of passes of a compactor required
to achieve the desired compaction should be conducted at
the start of the project The number of passes can then
be used as the criteria for compaction
Fills placed on slopes having grades steeper than 5 horit
zontal to 1 vertical should be keyed and benched into the
existing firm ground
Slopes
Fill slopes should be constructed at slope ratios of
2 horizontal to 1 vertical 2 1 or more
For cut slopes in fractured lava rock design slope ratios
of 1 horizontal to 1 vertical 1 1 can be used
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P 8
For cut less than 6 foot high steep slope ratios up
to horizontal to 1 vertical may be used itn the lava
rock formation
Where ash or sandy gravel formation is encountered in
the field the cut slope should be pulled back to a
2 horizontal to 1 ratto
Roadway Pavement
Along the proposed roadway the soft surface ash material
should be removed and replaced with selected granular fill
For pavement section on basalt rock formation or on well
compacted granular backfill the following design criteria
may be used
2 inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 inch Base Course
8 inch Total Pavement Thickness
The base course should be crushed aa rock or other
granular material with CBR of 85 and conforming with
Section 31 2 Standard Specification of Public Work
Construction or equivalent
Retaining Wall Design
Retaining wall may be utilized for the proposed roadway
improvements For the retatning wall bearing directly on
the in situ lava rock formation an allowable bearing

pressure of 8 000 pounds per square foot may be used
For the retaining wall footing bearing on compacted
granular fill the allowable bearing pressure of 3 000
pounds per square foot may be utilized
It ts recommended that granular material should be used
to backft11 behind the retaining walls Weepholes or
subsurface drainage system should be incorporated in the
design to relieve the build up of hydrostatic pressure
With the above provisions the wall can normally be
designed for an equivalent fluid pressure of 30 pounds
per square foot per foot of depth However due to the
possible earthquake vibration in the Hilo area it is
recommended that the equivalent fluid pressure be 60
pounds per square foot per foot of depth for the above
retaining wall design
To determine the sliding resistance of the retaining wall
foundation a frictional factor of 0 70 may be used for
footing bearing on rock For footing bearing on granular
fill a value of 0 55 may be utilized

The plans for site grading should be forwarded to the soils
engineer for review and comments prior to construction to
see that the intent of our recommendations is included in
the project plans and specifications

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It ts recommended that the soils engineer be present during
earthwork construction to monitor the contractor s grading
Operations The necessary inspections include the obser
vation of the site grubbing removal of brown ash prior to
filling keying and benching operation to prepare the slope
site to receive the new fill fill placement thickness
gradation of the broken up lava rock fill materials and
to verify that suffictent passes have been performed to
obtain the required density on each fill lift
Any imported material required should be non expansive and
should be tested and approved by the soils engineer prior
to hauling to the site
