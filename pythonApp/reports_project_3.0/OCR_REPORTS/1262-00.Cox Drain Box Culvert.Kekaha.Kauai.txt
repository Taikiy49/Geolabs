Soils Investigation
Proposed Reconstruction of Cox Drain Box Culvert
Job No DFR 116291
This report presents the results of our soils investigation for
the proposed box culvert replacement at Kekaha on the island of Kauai
Hawaii The general location of the site is shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1
The investigation was performed in accordance with our revised
proposal dated and consisted of drilling two
borings at the bridge site performing laboratory testing and
preparing this report to present our findings and recommendations
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located on Kekaha Road about 30 feet east of
the intersection with Manu Road in Kekaha Kauai
W O 1262 00 Page 2
The existing box culvert spans about 14 feet carrying a two lane
highway and its shoulders over a small stream Standing water was
noted in the stream
The ground adjacent to the bridge slopes gently toward the stream
with heavy vegetation noted along the stream banks
Large stockpiles of coral sand were also noted along the southern
stream banks which may have been dredged from the stream
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
A new box culvert structure is planned to replace the existing
storm damaged structure
The new structure will be about 32 feet long and 6 feet high
There will be a center support forming double boxes with each box
spanning a 10 foot clear span Fill over the culvert slab is
anticipated to be minimum and only a 4 inch thick layer of asphalt
concrete pavement is scheduled over the box culvert
SUBSURFACE EXPLORATION
Buried and overhead utility line along with narrow road shoulders
prevented any drilling operation next to existing abutments

Due to budget limitations two borings were drilled on the two
Sides of Kekaha Road on the eastern side of the box culvert The
approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate
2
The borings were drilled to depths of 21 5 and 22 5 feet below
the road elevation to explore the subsurface conditions underneath the
culvert structure and to obtain soil samples for laboratory testing
Detailed information on the field exploration and laboratory
testing is presented in the appendices of this report
SOIL CONDITIONS
The two borings disclosed relatively similar soil conditions
consisting of 1 5 to 3 feet of topsoil 1 5 to 3 feet of dense coral
sand underlain by 12 to 17 feet of loose estuarine sand deposits The
loose estuarine sand deposits graded silty and gravelly at a depth of
about 20 feet
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
It is our understanding that the existing box culvert is
suffering settlement from loss of foundation materials under a severe
condition developed during Hurricane Iwa
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1262 00 Page 4
The loose sand deposits disclosed in our borings below the
culvert invert level are generally very low in their Stability against
scouring settlements and other forces Therefore a deep foundation
system such as piling extending below the scouring zone and into the
denser clayey gravelly sand layer should be considered for recon
struction However the reconstruction will probably be limited to
shallow footings and may not allow the use of pile foundation because
of the limited funding available
Pile Foundation
We believe that a pile foundation system will be more able to
resist scouring under the culvert box and possible settlements
Based on our findings we project that 12 inch square prestressed
concrete piles driven to a depth of about 30 feet below the roadway
grade would provide a capacity of about 10 tons each These piles may
be driven by a hammer delivering about lbs of energy per
blow such as the McKiernan Terry 10B3 or 9B3
Pile driving may be terminated when the pile reaches a penetration
resistance of 30 blows foot provided that the pile has penetrated at
least 20 feet below the roadway surface otherwise it should be
driven to 30 feet below the roadway grade
The pile installation work should be closely monitored in the
field by a qualified soils engineer to further assess the pile driving
behavior and to determine if satisfactory embedment has been achieved
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Q 1262 00 Page 5
Shallow Foundations
Shallow spread footings may be utilized for the reconstruction of
the box culvert provided that the risk of suffering a similar damage
from severe conditions such as those experienced during Hurricane
Iwa is acceptable
To resist against loss of ground the loose sand below the
footing should be over excavated and replaced with coarse crushed rock
to a depth of at least 2 feet below the bottom of the new box culvert
to form a bedding course layer
The over excavation should extend at least 5 feet beyond both the
upstream and downstream sides of the culvert The backfill material
should consist of No 3 Coarse or coarser material The bottom of the
over excavation should be protected by a layer of filter fabric
The coarse rock backfill is designed to offer a limited resis
tance to scouring below the bottom of the box culvert However the
backfill over the coarse rock behind the culvert box walls also
needs to be protected by a filter blanket to prevent migration of
fines through voids between the coarse rocks
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1262 00 Page 6
The filter blanket should consist of a 10 inch layer of base
course aggregate placed over the No 3 coarse material Alterna
tively a filter fabric such as Mirafi 140 Filter X or equivalent
may be placed over the coarse rock provided that the top of the
coarse rock is smoothed to prevent damage to the fabric
It is further recommended that riprap stones be placed over the
No 3 Coarse backfill and filter fabric for at least 5 feet on both
the upstream and downstream sides of the culvert to provide further
resistance to scouring
The culvert box should also incorporate cutoff walls or collars
along the upstream and downstream openings to minimize seepage along
the contact and therefore scouring The cutoff walls should extend
at least 4 feet below the invert of the box In view of potential
loss of material on the sides of the existing culvert box the cutoff
wall should extend up above the invert level along the sides of the
openings to form flanges wingwalls that will retain the backfill
materials These retaining wingwalls should extend laterally a
distance of at least 2 feet into the backfill
The box culvert may be designed to an allowable soil bearing
pressure of 1500 p s f on the coarse rock backfill at a depth of 5
feet below the mean sea level or higher Footings placed lower than
this depth should be reviewed

Lateral Earth Pressures on Box Culvert
The lateral earth pressures on the culvert should be considered
as acting on a restrained wall The design earth pressures should
consist of that developed by an equivalent fluid weighing 50 p c f
In addition an appropriate surcharge load should be applied
Generally surcharge load may be converted to an equivalent fill
height of 1 foot for every 100 p s f of uniform surcharge load
The backfilling of box wall should be even on either side of the
stream If severe differences in heights of the wall backfill are
made uneven stresses and or relative movement may occur
Dewatering
It is anticipated that the site will be dewatered for the
reconstruction The dewatering probably will consist of damming both
sides of the culverts and sump pumping the culvert excavation dry
Excessive sump pumping may induce settlements of nearby area from
dewatering Therefore care must be exercised in undertaking
extensive dewatering and such a scheme should be reviewed and
authorized by the County of Kauai prior to the contractor s
implementation

Design Review
Plans and specifications for the box culvert construction should
be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written comments
prior to construction to see that the intent of our recommendations is
included
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the dewatering excavation and backfilling
operations be observed by the soils engineer during construction This
is to see that an adequate bearing has been obtained and the subsurface
conditions are consistent with the findings of our investigation The
recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the above
inspections

APPENDIX A
The subsurface conditions at the proposed box
culvert reconstruction site were explored by drilling 2
borings at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2 A truck mounted drilling rig was used to drill the
borings which ranged in depth from 21 5 to 22 5 feet below
the existing ground surface
The soils encountered in the borings were classified
by visual and textural examination in the field by our
engineer who continuously monitored the drilling operations
The classifications were further reviewed by visual inspection
and testing in the laboratory The soils were classified in
general conformance with the Unified Soil Classification System
A graphic presentation of the materials encountered is presented
on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 and A 2
In the borings soil samples were obtained by driving
barrel ring sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches
The blow counts used to drive the sampler the last 12 inches
are shown on the log of borings at the appropriate sample
depths
