INTRODUCTION
This report summarizes the findings of our subsurface explorations
at the subject site and presents recommendations for the design and
construction of the foundations to support the proposed structures
and for the ancilliary earthwork
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The existing ten acre site formerly Unit F of the Waiau Gardens
Development was previously graded for the former development

W O 540 00
The grading included the earthwork construction for the building pads
and the installation of underground utilities The proposed Waiau
Gardens Kai Development will consist of two to four story apartments
on the upper mauka portion of the parcel two ten story mid rise apart
ments on the lower makai half of the parcel near the freeway and two
three story parking structures adjacent to the mid rise buildings on
the north and south sides A recreation pavilion and swimming pool
will be located in approximately the center of the site
The proposed site grading will involve excavations varying from 16 feet
for the middle parking structure to between 5 and 17 feet for the mid
rise structures It is anticipated that the fills will be less than
five feet in thickness and of limited extent
SITE CONDITIONS
Surface
The proposed ten acre site is bound on the south by the H 1 Freeway
and on the north by Noelani Street It was originally Unit EF of
the Waiau Gardens Development and is bound on the east and west by
Units B G of the referenced development
The previous site grading consisted of shallow cuts and fills to create
a series of slope terraces for the then proposed building locations
An entrance roadway with a cul de sac traverses the center of the site
from north to south
The ground surface drops in steps from a high of approximately eleva
tion 170 near Noelani Street to approximately elevation 100 adjacent
to the H 1 Freeway

GEOLOGY
With the exception of the shallow surface fills the soil mantle is
residual soil derived from the in place weathering of basalt the ba
salt was deposited as lava in the Koolau Volcano Range that erupted
during the Pliocene Period Tropical weathering has gradually chemi
cally altered the hard basalt into lateritic soils Leaching of the
soil left only the more stable minerals such as iron oxide giving the
soil it s characteristic reddish color
The exposed slopes and adjacent cuts reveal that the site is underlain
by varying degrees of decomposed basalt The soil matrix is reddish
brown clayey silt containing areas of rock like structure which have
not completely weathered into soil Hard basalts generally occur 13
to 18 feet below the original ground surface

When portions of a structure bear upon rock with essentially no settle
ments and upon compressible soil the differential settements can dis
tress masonry or concrete walls which are sensitive to movements Where
possible the foundations should bear upon similar materials either en

ing level and backfilling with compacted soil to provide a soil cushion
which equalizes the settlements
In lieu of the undercutting expansion joints or other structural mea
sures can be utilized to limit or control the differential movements

Retaining Walls
The retaining walls should be designed for an equivalent fluid pressure
of 35 pcf if the on site soils are utilized for backfill A frictional
factor of 0 55 should be utilized for Sliding resistance of the retaining

wall foundations Non yielding walls should be designed for at rest
conditions of 50 pcf equivalent fluid pressure
Weep holes or subsurface drains should be provided to prevent the build
up of hydrostatic pressures behind the walls
Floor Slabs
Expansive soils were not encountered during the subsurface explorations
and slab on grade construction can therefore be utlized It is rec
ommended that the floor slabs be divorced from load bearing members to
prevent cracking due to differential movements The floor slab sub
grade should be compacted to aminimum of 90 of the maximum dry density
determined by American Society for Testing and Materials ASTM Test
Designation D 1557 72 A base course of four inches of clean granular
material and a plastic vapor barrier should be utilized to maintain a
dry floor slab
Earthwork and Site Grading
It is recommended that all structural fills and fills exceeding three
feet in height be compacted to at least 95 of the maximum dry density
stablished in accordance with ASTM D 1557 72 Full time inspection
should be utilized to verify the degree of compaction when the fill will
be used for structural support The on site soils are acceptable
as backfill At the time of our investigation the soils were near the
estimated optimum moisture content

Special care must be utilized at the site due to the previously placed
fills and utilities Prior to utilization of the existing fills they
should be proof rolled and tested to verify adequate compaction
Following the approval of the existing fills areas to receive addi
tional fill should be scarified to a minimum depth of six inches and
the sacrified surface brought to near optimum moisture content and
compacted The fill should then be placed in six inches maximum hori
zontal lifts and compacted to the recommended density
When the natural or fill slopes are steeper than 5 horizontal to 1

to the underlying soils

APPENDIX
FIELD EXPLORATIONS
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling 14
test borings utilizing truck mounted equipment Test Borings 1 thru
10 were utilized to secure samples of the soils and rocks encountered
Borings 11 thru 14 were auger borings utilized to locate the rock sur
face The locations of the borings are shown on the enclosed Site
Plan and Boring Locations Plate l
The soils were classified by visual and textural examinations in the
field by our geologist who continuously supervised the drilling opera
tions The classifications were verified by visual inspection and
testing in the laboratory All soils were classified in accordance
with the Unified Soils Classification System
A graphical representation of the soils encountered is given on the
boring logs Plate A 1 thru A 14 The soil samples were obtained by
driving either a 2 4 inch I D or a standard penetration sampler with
a 140 pound hammer free falling a distance of 30 inches The blow
counts on the sampler to drive the last 12 inches are shown on the
enclosed boring logs at the appropriate sample depths
