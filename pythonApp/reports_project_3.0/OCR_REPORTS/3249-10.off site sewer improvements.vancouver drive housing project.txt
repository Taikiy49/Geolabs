INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Off Site Sewer Improvements of the Vancouver Drive
Housing project in the Manoa area in Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii The
general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map
Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings and geotechnical engineering recommen
dations resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering analyses
for the proposed project These recommendations are intended for the design of trench
excavation and shoring pipe support and trench backfilling only The findings and
recommendations presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this
report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Vancouver Drive Housing project is located on Vancouver Drive between
University Avenue and Hunnewell Street in the lower Manoa area on the island of Oahu
Hawaii The housing project consists of construction of a three story apartment building
with on grade parking As part of the housing project it is proposed to improve the
off site sewer system for the housing project

The proposed off site sewer improvement project consists of construction of a new
15 inch sewer line along Metcalf Street from about Marques Street to Hunnewell Street
for a distance of approximately 700 lineal feet We understand that the depths of the
proposed sewer line invert will generally be about 15 to 20 feet below the existing ground
surface In general the proposed off site sewer project will include approximately
700 lineal feet of trench excavation sewer pipe installation trench backfilling and
construction of sewer laterals and three manhole structures
As part of our scope of work 12 borings were drilled at selected locations along
Vancouver Drive to evaluate the existing pavement structures for the proposed housing
project Recommendations pertaining to the new pavement design are not included in
our scope of work

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lavas from two
shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be
middle to late Pliocene in age and Koolau is estimated to be late Pliocene to early
Pleistocene in age After a long period of volcanic inactivity during which erosion incised
deep valleys into the Koolau shield volcanic activity returned with a series of lava flows
followed by cinder and tuff cone formations These series are referred to as the Honolulu
Volcanic Series
During the Pleistocene Epoch Ice Age sea levels fluctuated in response to the
cycles of continental glaciation As the glaciers grew and advanced less water was
available to fill the oceanic basins such that sea levels fell below the present stands of the
sea When the glaciers melted and receded an excess of water became available such
that the sea levels rose to above the present sea level
The processes of erosion and deposition were affected by these glacioeustatic sea
level fluctuations When the sea level was low the erosional base level was
correspondingly lower and valleys were carved to depths below the present sea level
When the sea level was high the erosional base level was raised such that sediments
accumulated at higher elevations
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3249 10 Page 6
In the mountainous regions of Hawaii the erosional processes are dominated by
detachment of soil and rock masses from the valley walls and are then transported
downslope toward the axis of a valley primarily by gravity as colluvium Once these
materials reach the stream in the central portion of a valley alluvial processes become
dominant and the sediments are transported and deposited as alluvium
In general stream flows in Hawaii are intermittent and flashy such that the stream
flows transmit large volumes of water for very short durations Because of this transport
of sediments is intermittent and the bulk of the stream s hydraulic load consists of a
poorly sorted mixture of boulders cobbles gravel sands and fines When the erosional
base levels change these sediment loads are left as deposits
The project site is located on the on the eastern flank of Punchbowl Hill The
Punchbowl Hill Puowaina is a tuff cone near the center of Honolulu built against the end
of a spur of the Koolau Range Land development in the last century have brought the
project site to its present conditions

generally of stiff clays and silts or dense gravels with cobbles and boulders underlain by
moderately to highly weathered basalt rock formation Therefore based on our
evaluation of the subsurface conditions with respect to the proposed 15 inch sewer line
the principal geotechnical engineering considerations for the proposed project include the
following
1 Excavation conditions and the need for support of the relatively deep trench
excavations along the proposed sewer line
2 bedding requirements for support of the sewer line
3 trench backfill and material requirements along the sewer line

Trench Excavation
Based on the information provided for the proposed sewer line the sewer line
inverts will be about 15 to 20 feet below the existing ground surface Our field exploration
disclosed that the site is generally underlain by stiff clays and silts or dense gravel to a
depth of about 10 feet overlying moderately to highly weathered basalt rock In addition
cobbles and boulders which may pose some difficulties during trench excavation were
also encountered in the borings drilled
Excavation Method
In general the method and equipment to be used for excavation should be
determined by the contractor subject to practical limits and safety considerations
It is anticipated that the weathered basalt rock may be excavated by means of
digging chipping or hoerams with heavy excavation equipment The excavated
materials from the trench excavation should be stockpiled no closer than a
horizontal distance equal to the depth of the excavation from the edge of the
excavation to reduce the potential for ground movement
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3249 10 Page 10
The above discussions regarding the excavation of the subsurface rocky materials
are based on our field data from the borings performed at the site Weathering
and hardness of the basalt rock formation may vary significantly along the sewer
line alignment Therefore we recommend that all contractors proposing to bid on
this project be encouraged to examine the site conditions and the boring data to
make their own interpretation
Excavation Support
We envision that the bottom of the trenches will generally extend to depths of
about 16 to 21 feet below the existing ground surface Because the proposed new
sewer line will be along the roadway sloping of the sides of the trenches may not
be possible due to the limited space Based on the deep excavations anticipated
support of the sides of the trenches during sewer line construction will be
necessary to facilitate the proposed trench excavations and to protect personnel
working in the trench All trench excavation and construction works should be
conducted in conformance with all applicable Federal State and other safety
requirements
Temporary shoring utilizing sheet pile is generally not practicable due to the
difficult driving into the weathered rock formation anticipated at a depth of about
10 feet below the surface to the sewer line invert elevations Therefore other
means of shoring such as trench shield or steel plates with speed shores may be
considered as temporary excavation support systems For preliminary design of a
braced shoring system the Apparent Pressure Diagram provided on Plate 4 may
be used as a guide
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3249 10 Page 11
The excavation support and shoring system used must comply with all applicable
safety requirements It is recommended that a qualified geotechnical engineer be
retained by the contractor to design and evaluate the shoring system used The
adequacy and safety of the shoring installation should be made the sole
responsibility of the contractor Even if good construction procedures are followed
some movement of the shoring system and the adjacent ground may still occur
due to changes in earth stresses during excavation Due to the complexity of the
stress changes it is difficult to estimate accurately the magnitude of ground
movement The magnitude of ground movements also depends greatly upon
workmanship such as how quickly and tightly the shoring supports are installed
the subsoil conditions the size of the excavation and the rate of excavation
Therefore it is important to realize that the excavation shoring should be installed
properly and as early as practical and if necessary the adjacent ground should be
continuously monitored for cracks dips and or other indications of movements with
instruments such as inclinometers
Pipe Bedding
The stress distribution against the bottom of a pipe has a significant effect on the
load supporting capacity of the pipe Therefore the pipe bedding is an important design
consideration For pipes bearing on the medium hard to hard basalt formation
encountered a minimum of 6 inches of granular bedding material such as No 3B Fine
gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation measured from the subgrade to the bottom of the
sewer pipe should be provided below the pipe A Typical Trench Detail is presented on
Plate 5 for reference
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3249 10 Page 12
The granular bedding should extend beyond the sides of the pipe a minimum width
of one fourth the outside pipe diameter and should extend up to the spring line of the pipe
as shown on Plate 5 Prior to placement of the granular bedding material the bottom of
the trench should be firm and unyielding
Backfill
In general the trench backfill should be placed in accordance with the Standard
Specifications for Public Works Construction September 1986 The first zone of backfill
extending from the bedding material to at least 12 inches above the top of the pipes
should consist of select free draining granular materials to reduce the compaction effort
required and resulting stresses on the pipe The granular material should be less than
1 inch in maximum size and should have less than 10 percent passing the No 200 sieve
Compaction by water ponding or jetting may be allowed provided the granular material is
free draining such as No 3B Fine gravel
The excavated trench spoils will likely contain some over sized rocky materials
The trench backfill from 12 inches above the top of the pipes to within 2 feet below the
finished subgrade may consist of approved excavated on site materials provided that the
over sized materials greater than 6 inches in diameter in largest dimension are
screened out In general the backfill should be moisture conditioned to above the
optimum moisture placed in thin lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and
mechanically compacted to a minimum 90 percent relative compaction Relative
compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the
maximum dry density of the same soil determined in accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 1557 91 The trench backfill from 2 feet below the finished subgrade
elevation up to the subgrade elevation should consist of select borrow subbase material
compacted to not less than 95 percent relative compaction Trenches should be kept free

Earth Pressure Loads on Pipe
Loads on buried pipes are influenced by the width of the trench the size of the
pipes the unit weight of backfill material and the frictional resistance between the backfill
material and the trench walls To calculate the vertical loads on the buried sewer pipes
an average unit weight of 120 pounds per cubic foot p c f for backfill material and a
coefficient of friction of 0 35 between the backfill and trench sidewall may be used in
design Earth pressure forces upon the pipe increase rapidly with increased width of the
trench Therefore the width of the sewer line trench should be kept to a minimum
Traffic loads on the buried pipes should be considered since the proposed sewer line
alignment is located within traveled ways
Manhole Structures
In general we believe that the bottom of the manhole structures will bear on the
medium hard to hard basalt rock encountered in our borings To provide more uniform
support for the manhole structures we recommend that the manhole structures be
supported on a minimum of 6 inches of cushion fill such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM
C 33 No 67 gradation or equivalent For these foundation conditions an allowable
bearing pressure of up to 3 000 pounds per square foot p s f may be used for the
design of the manhole structures bearing on a minimum of 6 inches of cushion fill
The lateral earth pressures acting on the manhole structures will depend on the
type of backfill used the extent of backfill and the compactive effort on the backfill
material around the structures Therefore granular material with less than 10 percent
passing the No 200 sieve is recommended for use as backfill around the manhole
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3249 10 Page 14
structures to reduce the compactive effort required which may cause additional lateral
earth pressures We recommend that the new manhole structures be designed for lateral
earth pressures based on an equivalent fluid pressure of 50 pounds per cubic foot p c f
above the groundwater level The above value assumes that the backfill around the
manhole structures is properly placed and compacted as recommended in the Backfill
section of this report
Surcharge stresses due to areal surcharges traffic loads line loads and point
loads within a horizontal distance equal to the depth of the structure should be considered
in the design For uniform surcharge stresses imposed on the structure a rectangular
distribution with uniform pressure equal to 45 percent of the vertical surcharge pressure
acting over the entire depth of the structure may be used in design Additional analyses
during design may be needed to evaluate the surcharge effects of point loads and line
loads

It is anticipated that asphaltic concrete pavements will be used to repave the sewer
line trenches along the proposed sewer line alignment Imported select borrow subbase
fill material is anticipated for use in the upper 2 feet below the road pavement subgrade
within the sewer line trenches as discussed in the Backfill section of this report The
select borrow subbase material should be moisture conditioned placed in 8 inch level
loose lifts and compacted to at least 95 percent relative compaction In general the new
pavements along the sewer line trenches should match the existing pavement sections or
the following pavement section whichever is greater

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part
upon information obtained from field borings Variations of conditions between the
borings may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident
until construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to
reevaluate the recommendations provided in this report
The locations of the field borings are approximate having been estimated by
taping from features indicated on the project plans and profiles provided by Hida
Okamoto Associates Inc Elevations of the borings are also approximate having been
estimated by interpolation from the same plans and profiles The physical locations and
elevations of the field borings should be considered accurate only to the degree implied
by the methods used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the field borings depict
the approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual
transition Water level data from the field borings were collected at the times shown on
the graphic representations and or in the text of this report These data have been
reviewed and interpretations made in the formulation of this report However it must be
noted that fluctuation may occur due to variations in rainfall seepage infiltration variable
subsurface soil conditions and other factors

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling two borings to
depths of about 30 3 and 31 5 feet below the existing ground surface along Metcalf Street
and 12 borings to depths of about 1 to 3 feet along Vancouver Drive at the approximate
locations shown on the Site Plans Plates 2 and 3 The borings were drilled using a truck
mounted drill rig equipped with continuous flight augers and rotary coring tools as
needed
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineers who monitored the drilling operations on a near
continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by testing in
the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representation of the materials
encountered are provided on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 14
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils
by driving a 3 inch O D Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling
30 inches The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the last 12 inches of an 18 inch
drive are shown on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
