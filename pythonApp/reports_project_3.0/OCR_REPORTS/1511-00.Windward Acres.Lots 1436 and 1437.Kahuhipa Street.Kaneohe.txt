In accordance with your request and our proposal of December 5
1984 we have performed a geotechnical investigation for the above
referenced site The approximate location of the site is shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of the investigation was to explore the subsurface soil
conditions within the development site in order to provide recommenda
tions for building foundation pavement design and general subsurface
W Page 2
soil data for site grading and utility line excavations Attention was
also directed to locate the extent and boundaries of a rubbish dump which
was believed to be situated at the northwest corner of the Lot 1437
The scope of investigation included drilling and sampling seven
7 borings excavating eight 8 test pits laboratory testing of
samples obtained engineering evaluation of the field and laboratory
data development of design recommendations and report preparation
Detailed description of our field exploration along with the logs
of boring and test pit are presented in the appendices of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATION
The proposed site development plan consists of clearing and
partially grading the site for five 3 story buildings each about 120 by
120 feet in plan The ground floor level will be used for parking The
upper two stories will be wood framed with metal roof The lower floor
and columns will consist of concrete Maximum column loading is
anticipated to be about 110 kips
Part of Building E will be constructed over an existing slope For
this particular building it is our understanding that retaining walls

construction Along the southern side of Driveway A see Site Plan
Plate 2 parallel to the southern property line another retaining wall
will be constructed against and below an existing short retaining wall
It is our understanding that underpinning of the latter wall may be
required during the construction
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site consists of an area about 420 feet wide by 500
feet long located on the north side of Kahuhipa Street and about 500
feet east of Kahekili Highway in Kaneohe Oahu Hawaii The Windward
Estates development forms the northerly boundary of the site
The upper area from Kahuhipa Street for about 300 feet to the north
is relatively flat about 2 gradient towards the north From there
the ground slopes down towards a drainageway at Slope gradients of about
5 1 to 10 1 H V at the upper portion where new fill was constructed in
1981 The lower portion slopes down at a steeper gradient of about 2 1 to
3 1 H V at the western portion to a nearly flat area near the existing
sewer easement that forms the northerly boundary of the site
The northwest corner of Lot 1437 was previously used as a dumping
area A lot of rubbish and miscellaneous fill which included abandoned
vehicles broken household appliances concrete debris with rebars etc
were encountered during our exploration

SUBSURFACE CONDITION
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and
sampling seven depths of 16 5 to 26 5 feet below the
existing ground surface In order to locate the boundaries of the
dumping area eight 8 test pits were also dug to depths of 2 to 10 feet
below the surface The approximate locations of the borings and the test
pits along with the approximate boundaries of the two rubbish dump
areas are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Based on the borings the relatively level part of the site is
underlain by alluvium brown to gray brown clayey silt to the depths
drilled 16 5 feet On and along the edge of the slope located at the
northwestern part of the site 3 to 6 feet of fill consisting of medium
stiff clayey silt with boulders and other debris were encountered
overlying stiff alluvium
No groundwater was encountered in the drilled borings and test pits
during our field exploration
Two major rubbish dump areas were encountered by our test pits
At the south dumping area rubbish was generally encounterd within the
surface three feet At the north dumping area traces of miscellaneous
debris and other deleterious materials were found to a depth of 6 feet
from the ground surface

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
In general our borings encountered competent subsoil condition for
the construction of the proposed buildings With the exception of the
northwestern portion of Building E where retaining walls will be
required shallow spread footing foundation can be used for all the
project buildings According to the current plan the maximum height of
the retaining wall at Building E is expected to be about 14 feet Such a
high retaining wall with compacted backfill behind the wall will impose
large loading on the slope surface Therefore deeper wall footing
embedment would be required to minimize future movements It is our
opinion that if practicable Building E showld be relocated further
upward to the south of the existing position or the finished floor
elevation be further lowered to reduce the height of the retaining wall
For the construction of the retaining wall along Driveway A we
believe that the upper wall footing bearing on medium stiff soil would be
exposed during the lower wall excavation We believe the contractor
should provide adequate temporary vertical and horizontal bracing to
protect the soil and wall from collapsing We also recommend that the
excavation and the wall construction be carried out in short sections in
order to minimize the possibility of extensive collapse of the existing
wall and its underlying foundation supporting soil

Lateral Resistance
Lateral loads acting on the spread or wall footng may be resisted
by passive earth pressure developed against the buried vertical faces of
the foundation system and by friction resistance developed between the
bottom of the foundations and the bearing soil
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be calculated using an
equivalent fluid pressure of 250 p s f for level backfill in front of
the wall For a downward sloping condition in front of the wall with
be used This assumes that the soil around the foundation is well
compacted The frictional resistance may be calculated by using a
coefficient of friction of 0 4 The above resistance values are maximum
values and do not contain a factor of safety
Site Grading
Site Preparation
Prior to placing the compacted fill the site should first be
cleared of vegetation rubbish debris and other unsuitable
materials Particular attention should be paid towards the
dumping areas In areas where test pits were excavated during our
exploration the scil was disturbed Therefore loose fill is
expected These loose backfill materials in the excavated test
pits should be removed and the pits be properly backfilled and
GEOLABS HAWAI

compacted in 6 inch lifts The building area should be scarified
to a depth of at least 6 inches below grade moisture conditioned
and recompacted to 90 of its maximum Censity as established by
ASTM D 1557 78 test method
Fill Material
Excavated on site material free of any organic and other expansive
component may be used as a source of fill material All imported
fill material should consist of selected non expansive silty sandy
or granular materials less than 3 inches in size with less than 20
passing the 200 sieve For the fill material behind the retaining
wall we recommend the use of granular material such as base
course rock 3B Fine gravel sand or equivalent
Fill Placement
Al fill and backfill materials should be placed in maximum of 10
inch horizontal loose lifts and compacted to about 90 of the
specified maximum density Fill placed on the existing slope at
the northwestern part of the site should be keyed and benched intc
the firm ground to properly bond the new fill to the slope A
sketch of keying and benching is shown on Plate 4
Slab On Grade
It is recommended that slab on grade be placed over a 4 inch layer
of base course compacted to 95 of its maximum dry density

The subgrade should be kept moist and protected from érying prior
to concrete placement Subgrade should be compacted to a minimum of 90
of maxinum dry density For the northwestern part of Building E where
granular backfill will be placed the minimum of 95 of maximum dry
density should be achieved for the subgrade material
In order to protect against moisture damage of the flooring
material a polyethylene moisture barrier should be incorporated within
floor slabs When the slab is located below the outside ground surface
a subdrainage system around the perimeter of the walls is recommended to
relieve seepage water
Pavement
The proposed access roads and parking areas will be located both in
the cut and fill Assuming that fill materials will be obtained from
on site soil and based on the results of laboratory CBR tests on the
ccempacted site material the preliminary pavement design is as follows
2 inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 inch Base Course 95 compaction
8 inch Total Pavement Thickness

Design Revi
Plans and specifications for foundation and pavement should be
forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written comments prior to

Construction Monitoring
The earthwork operation should be monitored by a soils engineer
during the early stage of site grading in order to ensure proper clearing
of the rubbish and other unsuitable materials
We recommend that the footing excavations be inspected by the soils
engineer prior to placement of concrete or reinforcing steel to check
that adequate bearing has been obtained and that the subsurface
conditions are consistent with the findings of our subsurface
investigation
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above inspections
