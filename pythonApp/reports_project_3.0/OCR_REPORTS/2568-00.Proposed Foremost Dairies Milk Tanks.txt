INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the subject project and our geotechnical recommendations for design of
the structure foundations
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain subsurface information within the
proposed construction area to provide the basis for the formulation of recommendations

pertinent to the design of the foundations for the proposed tanks and for site preparation
and grading
Our exploration was performed in conformance with our proposal dated August
13 1990 and included the following items of work
1 Review of information available from previously accomplished geotechnical
studies at the site
2 Field reconnaissance to develop an overview of site conditions as they
relate to future proposed grading site improvements and determining the
optimal location of the boring
3 Drilling and sampling of one 1 boring to a depth of sixty one and one half
61 5 feet below the existing ground surface
4 Laboratory testing of selected samples for determination of the classification
and engineering properties of the subsurface materials
5 Technical evaluation of the field and laboratory data
6 Preparation of this report summarizing the information obtained and
providing engineering recommendations for the geotechnical engineering
aspects of the project

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
In general it is Our opinion that the proposed construction of the tanks at the
indicated site is feasible from a geotechnical engineering point of view provided our
recommendations are followed The field exploration encountered poor subsurface soil
conditions below the proposed site Similar subsurface conditions were indicated by the
borings drilled in August 1988 indicating a reasonable degree of uniformity over the
entire general area Excessive settlements would occur if the tanks were supported on
shallow footings or on unsupported mat foundations Therefore we recommend that pile
foundations be used to support the new tanks
Pile Foundations
The piles would develop their load carrying capacity primarily from frictional
resistance or adhesion between the piles and the medium to dense alluvial soil
encountered about thirty three and one half 33 5 feet below the ground surface
Twelve inch square prestressed concrete piles extending into the denser alluvial
soil are recommended Pile lengths of 60 feet are recommended
The piles should be driven with a hammer delivering about 24 000 foot pounds of
energy to a resistance of two 2 blows per inch for the last three 3 inches
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2568 00 Page 5
For the above recommendations an allowable pile capacity of twenty 20 tons per
pile may be used
Drilled Pier Foundations
As an alternative the use of drilled pier foundations in lieu of the precast pile
foundation may be considered
The drilled pier would develop the load capacity primarily from frictional resistance
or adhesion between the piers and the stiff alluvial soil encountered at about 33 5 feet
depth at the new milk tank s location
For a minimum pier diameter of 24 inches a minimum of 12 feet of penetration into
the stiff alluvium is recommended to develop the 20 ton design load Therefore pier
lengths of 46 feet are recommended
The drilled pier construction should be closely monitored by the soil engineer in
order to see that adequate foundation support is achieved Since the majority of the
loading capacities come from the frictional resistance between the shaft and the in situ
soil extra care should be applied to the drilling of the pier hole selection of concrete mix
design and the placement of concrete
Since the groundwater level is relatively close and the subsoils are very soft casing
is required to prevent caving in of the holes and placement of the concrete by tremie
method will be necessary

Lateral Resistance
To resist lateral loads the passive earth pressure around the pile caps may be
used We recommend an equivalent fluid pressure of three hundred 800 pounds per
square foot per foot of depth Backfill against the foundation should be composed of
selected granular materials with less than 25 passing the No 200 sieve compacted to
a dry density of at least ninety five percent 95 of the maximum dry density as
determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Site Grading
Site grading requirements for this project are minimal comprising demolition of an
existing sidewalk excavation as required for the mat and pile caps and the new sidewalk
and backfilling
All excavated material should be removed and disposed of properly off site Final
grading of the excavated area should be deferred until pile driving is completed to
preclude the necessity of reworking which would be otherwise required to repair
disturbances caused by pile driving operations After pile driving is completed the area
should be levelled lightly scarified moisture conditioned and compacted to 95 percent
of its maximum dry density as determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
A six inch layer of granular base course material should be provided between the
recompacted in situ material and the concrete foundation construction Similar material
should be used to fill the interstitial soace between the sides of the excavation and the
concrete
The material proposed should be tested and approved for its intended use by the
project geotechnical engineer prior to transportation to the site

Sidewalk
The new sidewalk is indicated on Plate 2 to be approximately nine feet 9 wide
A thickness of four inches 4 is typical for sidewalk construction and is recommended
for the project
Contraction joints consisting of grooves formed in the surface prior to the initial set
of the concrete should be provided along the centerline of the sidewalk and perpendicu
larly at four foot 4 intervals Expansion joints should be provided at locations where the
new sidewalk abuts other structures and at abrupt changes in direction
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be forwarded to the
geotechnical engineer for review and written comment prior to construction This review
is needed to determine adherence to the foundation recommendations given herein If
this review is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot be responsible for misinterpre
tation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during construction of the foundation phases of work
This is to observe compliance with the design concepts specifications or recommenda
tions and to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from that
anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given herein are
contingent upon such observations
If actual exposed rock or soil conditions encountered during construction are
different from those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications
to the design should be made

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based upon
information obtained from the boring Variations of conditions as indicated by the boring
may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident until
construction is in progress If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re
evaluate the recommendations given in this report
The location of the boring was determined by taping from structures indicated on
the topographic survey map The elevation of the boring was approximately determined
from the information given on the topographic survey map and site reconnaissance The
physical location and elevation of the boring should be considered accurate only to the
degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in the graphic representation of the boring depicts the
approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual
transition
Water level data at the boring was measured at the time given on the graphic
representation and or in the text of this report This information had been reviewed and
interpretations made in the formulation of this report However it must be noted that
fluctuation may occur due to variations in rainfall tides temperature and other factors
This report has been prepared for the exclusive use of project designers for
specific application to the foundation design of the project in accordance with generally
accepted geotechnical engineering principles and practices No other warranty
expressed or implied is made
