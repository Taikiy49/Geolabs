Dear Mr. Dinsmore: 
This report summarizes our earthwork observation and testing services performed 
during the site grading operations for the Regal Cinemas project at Kapolei Commons 
from December 11, 2014 through December 29, 2015. 
EARTHWORK OPERATIONS 
The Regal Cinemas project site is located within the Kapolei Commons 
development in the Kapolei area in the District of Ewa on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii.  The 
project site is situated on the southwestern corner of the retail development.  A layout of 
the new entertainment complex is shown on the Site Plan, Plate 1. 
Layton Construction is the general contractor and Royal Contracting Company 
performed the site grading operations during the building pad and infrastructure installation 
for the project. The site grading work consisted of clearing and grubbing, footing 
excavation, subgrade preparation, placement and compaction of fills and backfills, 
pavement construction, and utility trench excavation and backfill. 
Following the clearing and grubbing operations, the existing coral fill materials were 
over-excavated down to the new bottom elevation of the 18-inch non-expansive select 
granular capping fill layer.  The bottom of the over-excavation was scarified to a depth of 
about 8 inches, moisture-conditioned to above the optimum moisture content, and

recompacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density determined in 
accordance with ASTM D1557 (Modified Proctor). After the subgrade preparation work, 
the over-excavation was then backfilled with the existing coralline materials (as approved 
by Geolabs for re-use) and capped with additional non-expansive select granular fill 
materials to raise the existing ground to the pad subgrade elevation (i.e. bottom of the slab 
cushion). 
During placement of the non-expansive select granular fill layer, the fill materials 
were generally placed in near-level lifts, brought to above the optimum moisture content, 
and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density determined in 
accordance with ASTM D1557 (Modified Proctor). The base course under the AC 
pavements were generally placed in near-level lifts, brought to above the optimum 
moisture content, and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent of the maximum dry density 
determined in accordance with ASTM D1557 (Modified Proctor).  During the foundation 
excavation and preparation, the excavated footing bottom was generally recompacted to a 
minimum of 90 percent relative compaction. 
New utility connections were installed to serve the new Regal Cinemas 
development. In general, a bedding layer consisting of 6 inches of No. 3B Fine gravel was 
provided below the piping and manholes for uniform support. Free-draining granular 
materials consisting of No. 3B Fine gravel was also used for the trench backfill up to about 
12 inches above the pipes to provide adequate support around the pipes and to reduce 
the compaction effort on the initial trench backfill. The trench backfill was placed in 8-inch 
level loose lifts and mechanically compacted to no less than 90 percent relative 
compaction. Where trenches are below pavement areas, the upper 3 feet of the trench 
backfill below the pavement grade was compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative 
compaction. 
During the site grading operations, samples of the on-site and imported materials 
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing. 
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our 
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D1557 (Modified Proctor), and the test 
results are summarized on Plate 2 of this report. 
Field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations on an 
as-need basis to confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures 
and soil conditions were noted. A total of 499 field density tests, including retest of failed 
areas, were performed using the nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM D6938. 
Results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 3.1 through 3.39 of 
this report. These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on 
constructed fills and backfills at the site. Test locations and elevations were estimated by 
pacing and interpolating from on-site survey stakes. These locations and elevations should 
be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used. Areas assessed 
as unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained.

of the geotechnical risks to the project. It should be recognized that there are variations 
in the accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor 
earthwork construction and materials.
