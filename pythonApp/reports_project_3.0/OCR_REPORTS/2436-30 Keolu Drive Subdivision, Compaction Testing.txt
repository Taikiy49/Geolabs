EARTHWORK OBSERVATION AND COMPACTION TESTING REPORT
KEOLU DRIVE SUBDIVISION
W O 24 1995
INTRODUCTION
This report summarizes the results of our geotechnical engineering services
performed during the earthwork and grading operations at the above referenced project
from through in general accordance with our
proposals dated and August 24 1992 respectively
SITE GRADING
The project site was graded for the development of 23 individual residential lots
with associated construction of an 84 inch and 96 inch RCP drainline extensions Site
grading consisting of fills on the order of up to about 5 feet were needed to attain the
design grades A general layout of the project site is presented on the Site Plan
Plate 1 It is our opinion that the grading was performed in general accordance with
the intent of the recommendations presented in a report by Geolabs Hawaii entitled
Geotechnical Engineering Report Proposed Keolu Drive Subdivision Kaopa Unit 3 B
Kailua dated Detailed observations and
discussions are presented in the following sections of this report
Stabilization of Soft Ground
Thick lagoonal deposits up to an average depth of about 30 feet below the
original ground surface were encountered along the waterfront area during our field
exploration During the mass grading operations a boulder buttress fill was placed
along the waterfront area to serve as a stabilization layer and working platform for the
subsequent CRM wall construction A surcharge program was implemented prior to the
CRM wall construction to accelerate the consolidation of the underlying soft soils

induced by the new fill loads The surcharge fill was removed after the settlement
readings suggested that most of the ground settlements have occurred under the
surcharge loads
Fill Placement
During the mass grading operations representative samples of the on site and
imported soils utilized as fill materials were obtained and returned to our laboratory for
testing The maximum dry density and optimum moisture content were determined in
our laboratory in accordance with the ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Results of our
laboratory tests are presented on Plate 2
Observation and testing of the earthwork in the field were performed by repre
sentatives from our office on an intermittent basis to observe that the grading was
performed in general conformance with the intent of the Geotechnical Engineering
Report and specifications pertaining to the geotechnical aspects of the development
In place density testing was performed periodically in general accordance with ASTM
Test Designations D 2922 Nuclear Method The test results are presented on the
attached Summary of Compaction Data Plates 2 through 5
Expansive Soils and Boulders
Fill materials imported from various sources were used in raising the original
ground to the existing grades during the mass grading operations of the subject project
Laboratory testing were performed on samples taken at various locations of the fill area
within the upper 2 feet to evaluate the swelling potential of these imported fill materials
Based on our laboratory swell test results the imported fill materials within the upper
2 feet generally have a moderate to high expansion potential when subjected to
moisture fluctuations Therefore itis our opinion that measures should be implemented

during house construction to reduce the potential swelling effects on the future slab on
grade construction
We understand that approximately 4 feet of fill has been placed across the
project site in order to raise the original ground surface to the current finish grades for
the proposed subdivision development project In our geotechnical engineering report
prepared for the subject project we had recommended that fill materials used at the
site be no larger than 3 inches in maximum size However larger sized materials
consisting of cobbles and boulders were used as fill material during the course of
grading for the subject project Based on our evaluation and field observations we
believe that these boulder materials along with the other soils as fill materials were
acceptable It is our opinion that the fill materials have been placed and compacted to
support the proposed single family residential structures We believe that some of
these boulders are near the finish grades of the project Therefore future lot buyers
should be made aware that boulders are near the existing ground surface and may
cause excavation and construction difficulties during the construction of the future
house and or utilities
HOUSE FOUNDATION AND CONSTRUCTION CONSIDERATIONS
Our laboratory tests indicated that the existing near surface fill materials consist
of moderately to highly expansive clayey soils that may cause potential distress to the
future slab on grade construction Therefore we recommend that as a minimum the
upper 2 feet of fill below the future slab or pavement subgrade level i e bottom of slab
cushion or bottom of base course consist of non expansive select granular materials
The non expansive select granular fill should extend at least 2 feet beyond the limits
of the proposed building limits and driveway The upper 6 inches of capping fill around
the house structure should consist of non expansive impervious material to reduce
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2436 30 Page 4
water infiltration into the underlying select granular fill material Depending on the finish
slab grade the minimum 2 foot non expansive select capping fill requirement may
require over excavation and removal of the clayey subgrade soils and boulders and
replaced with off site select import fill
The potential swelling of the subgrade fill material may be significantly reduced
if they are properly protected from drying up Therefore it is our opinion that the
following construction procedures and precautionary measures should be observed
Prior to placement of the minimum 2 foot thick layer of non expansive capping material
the subgrade material should be scarified to a depth of about 8 inches moisture
conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture content and
recompacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction The non expansive
capping layer should be placed immediately thereafter to reduce potential loss of
moisture from the underlying subgrade materials The subgrade materials should be
kept moist through wetting of the overlying capping layer prior to concrete placement
The thickened edges of slabs adjacent to any unpaved area should be embedded at
least 18 inches below the lowest adjacent grade A typical detail showing the thickened
edge is presented on Plate 6
In addition we recommend that future improvements not be allowed within a
horizontal set back distance of 20 feet from the existing CRM wall to avoid imposing
additional loads on the existing CRM wall
We strongly recommend that a competent geotechnical engineer be retained by
each individual lot buyer to review the house foundation plans and specifications prior
to construction to determine adherence to the foundation recommendations presented
in our soils report Home owners should be advised to extend foundations sufficiently
down into competent in situ stable soil and not to block or alter surface drainage
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2436 30 Page 5
The finished grades outside the buildings should be sloped to shed water away
from foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding Also it is advised that gutter
systems be installed around the building and that the discharge be diverted away from
the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near the founda
tions and slabs should also be avoided Planters next to foundations should be
avoided or have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the
subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil
saturation and subsequent loss of strength of the near surface soils The foundation
excavations should be properly backfilled against the walls or slab footings immediately
after setting of the concrete to reduce water infiltration Drainage swales should be
provided as soon as possible and maintained to drain all surface run off away from the
slab footing foundations and the edges of slopes
The services of a geotechnical engineer should also be utilized during the
foundation construction and regrading of future lots to observe compliance with the
above recommendations and that the required footing set back and embedment criteria
are followed The geotechnical engineer should test and approve all additional
imported fill material observe the footing excavation soft expansive soil removal
backfill placement and recompaction and footing and slab subgrade preparation prior
to concrete placement Our recommendations are contingent upon the above field
monitoring and verifications Professional geotechnical engineering consultation and
observation should be obtained in the planning design and construction of any future
improvements
