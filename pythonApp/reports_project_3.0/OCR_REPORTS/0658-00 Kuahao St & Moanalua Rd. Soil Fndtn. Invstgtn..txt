INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this investigation is to define subsurface con
ditions and to provide recommendations for the design and con
struction of the proposed Waiau Warehouse Complex located in
Pearl City Oahu Hawaii This report summarizes our findings
and recommendations

SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed warehouse building site is located between Moanalua
Road and Kuahao Street just east of the Pearl City intersection
on the H 1 Highway The site is relatively level at about ele
vation 77 feet However the site slopes steeply down to Moana
lua Road along the southern edge The slope in this area is about
25 feet high
The site area was recently graded It is understood that the site
is blanketed with fill from a few feet deep at the northern edge
of the property to perhaps more than 20 feet along the southern
edge The fill is underlain by stiff residual soil and weathered
basalt rock
At the southwest corner of the property the upper section of the
concrete drainage ditch on the slope surface was badly damaged
A hole about 10 feet long 5 feet wide and 4 feet in depth below
the existing ground surface was noted at the top slope of the
concrete drainage ditch It appears that the damage on the con
crete ditch was due to the undermining by erosion on this area
In general the slope bank at the southern edge is in stable con
dition No ground movement was detected during our investigation
on the job site
An existing warehouse building is along the east property line
The warehouse is a one story structure with perimeter concrete
block wall and no basement A warehouse of steel structure is

PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
The proposed warehouse building is currently planned for two
stories at the southern portion of the building with upper
floor at the Kuahao Street level and the lower floor at the
Moanalua Road level The northern portion of the building
along the Kuahao Street will be a one story structure at the
same level with the Kuahao Street
The southern portion of the site will require extensive exca
vation there is about 18 feet of cut for lowering the existing
grade down to the Moanalua Road level Retaining walls up to
18 feet high are anticipated at the east west and north sides
of the lower level building portion

DISCUSSION RECOMMENDATIONS
The subsurface conditions encountered during the exploratory soil
investigation were relatively good from a soils and foundation
engineering viewpoint Spread and wall footings bearing on the
undisturbed in situ sandy soil will provide adequate support for
the proposed warehouse structure
Foundation
Spread and wall footing foundation bearing on the stiff in situ
red silty soil can be designed for an allowable bearing pressure
of 4 000 pounds per square foot The bearing value can therefore
be increased to 6 000 pounds per square foot when considering seis
mic and wind loadings Footing foundation should bear a minimum of
18 inches below the adjacent final grade
It is estimated that the total settlement for the structure should
be less than one inch with a differential settlement of less than
one half of an inch

W
Slab On Grade
Slightly expansive soils were encountered during the subsurface
explorations Slab on grade can be utilized without special
provision for expansive soil If the slab will have a floor
covering it should be protected with a plastic vapor barrier
under the slab
For floor slabs on the on site silty soil a four inch layer of
base course rock should be provided above the subgrade and com
pacted to 95 of its maximum dry density The upper six inches
of the subgrade below the base course of the floor slab area
should be compacted wetter than its optimum moisture content
to a minimum 95 of its dry density as established by the ASTM
D 1557 72T test method
Retaining Wall
The retaining wall for the lower level portion of the building
should be designed to receive active lateral pressure equivalent
to a fluid density of 45 pounds per cubic foot if granular material
is utilized as backfill together with positive drainage systems
behind and below the wall footing to relieve any excessive hydro
static pressure A friction factor of 0 4 can be utilized for
determining sliding resistance of the retaining wall foundation
For the north retaining wall at the lower level additional lateral
force on the retaining wall should be considered according to the
anticipated live and dead load from the upper level of the building
behind the retaining wall

To avoid any stress overlapped from the existing warehouse foun

level of the building should be kept a clear clearance of 14
feet from the existing foundation
Excavations
The retaining wall and footing excavations in the on site silty
soil can stand vertically with temporary shoring To avoid settle
ment of the adjacent slab on grade and wall units it will be ne
cessary to use sheet piling or shoring to support the excavation
against the existing structure at the eastern side of the building
site
Due to the erosion potential of the on site material it should
be arranged so that the retaining wall construction be performed
as soon as possible after excavation

Earthwork Grading
The proposed site grading will have most of the cut at the
southern portion of the site In general the northern portion
of building will be constructed essentially at grade
For the on site silty soil cut and fill slopes of two horizontal
to one vertical 2 1 ratio or flatter may be used
The on site soil is highly erosive therefore all slopes in this
type of formation will require slope protection during and after
construction
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the foundation excavations for the ware
house conaiex be inspected by the soil engineer prior to the
placement of concrete or reinforcing to assure that adequate
bearing has been obtained and that the subsurface conditions
are consistent with the findings of our subsurface investigation
The utility trench backfilling and earthwork operations should be
monitored by the Soils Engineer Construction inspection should
include the necessary testing to assure that the proper degree of
compaction is consistently achieved
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above inspections

FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by
drilling 3 borings utilizing truck mounted equipment
The depths of all the borings terminated at 8 0 to 20 0
feet below the existing ground surface The locations
of the borings are shown on the enclosed Site Plan Plate 1
The soils were classified by visual and textural examination
in the field by our engineer who continuously supervised the
drilling operations The classifications were verified by
visual inspection and testing in the laboratory All soils
were classified in accordance with the Unified Soil Classifi
cation System A graphic presentation of the soils encountered
is presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 3
Soil samples were obtained by driving either a
ring sampler or a standard penetration sampler with a 140 pound
hammer free falling a distance of 30 inches The blow counts
to drive the sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the logs
of borings at the appropriate sample depth
