INTRODUCTION
This report presents the findings and conclusions of the soil
investigation performed on the subject property The purposes
of the investigation was to evaluate the soils underlying the
Site with respect to the Planned development to define their
engineering characteristics and to provide preliminary recom
mendations for the foundation design site grading pavement
design and compaction control
The investigation included a geologic reconnaissance of the
area six test borings with logging and sampling of the soils
encountered laboratory testing and engineering analysis
W O
PROPOSED DEVELOPMENT
According to the preliminary plan of Nawiliwili Harbor View Estates
prepared by VIN Pacific the property is to be developed into a
townhouse development of approximately 148 units within 12 struc
tures The proposed development will include recreation facili
ties at the northern end of the property and a sewage treatment
plant with injection wells at approximately the center of the
southern half of the development
The site grading will generally entail shallow cuts and fills
not exceeding ten feet It is anticipated that the on site
soils will be utilized for the fills
SITE DESCRIPTION
The site is located in about 1 4 mile
south of the junction of Nawiliwili and Waapa Roads It is gen
erally bordered by Nawiliwili Road to the south Wilcox Road to
the southeast and Waapa and Lala Roads to the north The site
is situated on a terrace with the ground surface sloping upward
from Wilcox Road at a 5 10 gradient West of the proposed
site the ground slopes upward toward a cemetary and Kauai High
School
Most of the ground surface is covered by brush and trees An
existing dirt road and several houses and sheds are present in
the southern half of the site The total site area is approxi
acres
GEOLOGY
The area is essentially Koloa volcanic deposits of early Pleis
tocene Epoch These lava flows of nepheline basalts erupted
from numerous vents Alluvium and calcareous sediments are
also present in the lower reaches
The principal geologic development occured during the Pleistocene
Epoch when stream valleys and sea liffs were eroded to a base le
vel of up to 100 feet below the existing sea level Beaches of
calcareous sand were formed the sand was blown inland to form
dunes which were then covered by later flows of basalt As
the sea rose around the island the valley mouths were also
alluviated A well preserved shoreline is evident approximately
25 feet above sea level
W O
The lava flows of the Koloa Volcanic series are poorly to moder
ately permeable and carry fresh or brackish water The calcar
eous dunes are highly premeable and contain brackish water

CONCLUSIONS RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The subsurface explorations at the site show that it is suitable
for the construction of the proposed townhouses It will be nec
essary to compact the sandy deposits in the lower area to densify
them sufficiently to assure uniform support for the foundations
Following this densification these foundations can be designed
for maximum contact bearing pressures of 4 000 pounds per square
foot
An allowable bearing capacity of 4 000 pounds per square foot
can also be utilized for the foundation bearing within the resi
dual soil in the upper area providing the foundation excavations
are inspected for localized soft areas
Foundations
The foundations for the proposed structures should bear a mini
mum of 12 inches below the final adjacent grade where they may
be designed for allowable bearing pressures of 4 000 pounds per
square foot
The beach sands located below the ancient shoreline are loose
and will settle when subjected to loadings or vibrations To
provide uniform support for the proposed foundations and to
W O
minimize differential settlements it is recommended that the
proposed building areas underlain by sand be proof rolled with
a heavy vibratory compactor to densify the soils prior to the
foundation construction
The heavy vibratory compactor utilized for the densification
should have a minimum impact energy of 30 000 foot pounds
Utilizing this compactor it is anticipated that 10 passes
would be sufficient to densify the soils in phace Saturation
of the buildings areas may be necessary to facilitate the den
sification
The entire building area including carports lanais and side
walks should be included along with a five foot strip around
the perimeter of the structural area The compaction operations
should be monitored on a full time basis to assure that the
desired compaction is consistently achieved The recommended
foundation bearing pressures are contingent upon these inspec
tions
Settlements for the proposed structures based on 4 000 pounds
per square foot allowable bearing pressure is anticipated to
be 1 2 inch total and 1 4 differential
Slabs on grade
Samples of both the sand and the clayey silt were tested in
the laboratory to measure their expansive potential The lab
oratory testing revealed a 1 9 expansion for the clayey silt
and essentially zero expansion for the sand Slab on grade con
struction can therefore be utilized without special precau
tions for expansive soils The subgrade areas should be in
spected for isolated pockets of expansive soils The floor
slabs should be structurally separated from load bearing columns
and members to prevent minor distresses due to differential move
ments between these two elements
Concrete slabs which will be provided with floor coverings sen
sitive to moisture should be protected by a plastic vapor bar
rier Care must be exercised during the construction operations
to prevent puncturing the vapor barrier
Lateral Pressures
The active earth pressure for the retaining wall design should
be based upon an equivalent fluid having a density of 50 pounds
per cubic foot if the on site cohesive soils are utilized as
W O
backfill A design equivalent fluid of 30 pounds per cubic foot
can be utilized if the retaining walls are backfilled with gra
nular soil
Passive earth pressures can be computed as an equivalent fluid
with a density of 250 pounds per cubic foot with a maximum
passive earth pressure of 2 000 pounds per square foot
A frictional factor of 0 4 should be utilized to determine the
sliding resistance of the retaining wall foundations For com
bined passive pressure and frictional resistance the passive
component should be reduced by 1 3
Earthwork Site Grading
The proposed site plan indicates that the major portion of the
cuts and fills will be made within the upper residual soils
These soils should not be excavated at cut slopes exceeding 1
horizontal to 1 vertical 1 to 1 Utilizing the on site
Soils the fill should not be constructed at slopes steeper
than 2 horizontal to 1 vertical 2 to 1
Where the fill will be utilized for structural support or
where it exceeds three feet in thickness the fill should be
compacted to a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density estab
ished by American Society for Testing and Materials ASTM
Test D 1557 72T
All areas to receive fill should be first cleared of all dele
terious and other organic material and the ground surface scari
fied to a minimum depth of six inches The scarified surface
should then be brought to near optimum moisture content and
compacted to the above density Where the natural ground
Slope exceeds 5 horizontal to 1 vertical the ground surface
should be keyed and benched into competent soil to assure ade
quate bond between the existing soil and the newly placed
fill
The backfill behind the retaining walls should also be compacted
to 90 on the maximum density established by the above specifi
cation

Project Description
The proposed site is located about 1 4 mile south of the
intersection of Nawiliwili and Waapa Roads It is bordered
by Nawiliwili Road on the south Wilcox Road to the south
east and Waapa and Lala Roads to the north
The proposed development will consist of 12 townhouse struc

is planned for approximately the center of the southern half

for disposal of the effluent from the treatment plant
Field Exploration and Testing
The enclosed Plate 1 shows the locations of five test borings
drilled as part of the foundation investigation the location

of a percolation test performed at the time of the foundation

In addition to the above borings four borings were drilled
and tested to measure the injection rates for the possible
use of injection well disposal The borings extended to
between 35 to 50 feet below the ground surface to the limit
of the sand formation and produced injection rates of 10 to
15 gallons per minute
Since the design rate of 180 gallons per minute could not
be disposed within a reasonable number of injection wells
four percolation tests were performed to evaluate the use
of seepage pits
A preliminary test at S 4 was performed on March 10 1975 and
produced a rate of 14 inches per minute for a 4 inch diameter
perforated casing extending to a depth of 32 feet The bor

Three additional percolation tests S 1 thru S 3 were performed
on March 13 1975 These tests were witnessed by Mr Charles
Futenma of the Department of Health Test locations S 1 and
S 2 were made in the proposed seepage pit area S 3 was made
in an alternate area The test results are shown on the en
closed Table I Summary of Field Percolation Tests
All tests were performed on 4 inch diameter perforated casing
extending 20 feet below the ground surface The test loca
tions penetrated sand for their full depth
Conclusions and Recommendations
As shown by the enclosed test data the sand at the site pro
vided an average initial percolation rate of 14 inches per
minute In a 4 inch diameter hole this is equivalent to 0 76
gallon per minute
Based upon a circumference of approximately one foot this is
equal to 0 76 gallons per minute per foot of circumference with
a depth of 20 feet
The total required circumference would be equal to or
237 feet A 8 foot diameter seepage pit is approximately 25
feet in circumference On this basis 10 seepage pits would be
required to provide the necessary 237 feet
W Page 3 March 13 1975
The proposed seepage pit locations are shown on the enclosed
Plate 2 The spacing of the seepage pits is in accordance
with Chapter 38 of the Public Health Regulations
The required number of seepage pits given above was derived
for a peak flow of 180 gallons per minute which was the
design maximum flow for the injection wells The seepage
pits differ from the injection wells in the amount of
storage that is available
It is therefore proposed that the number of seepage pits
be based upon providing an absorption rate of three times
the average rate For the average rate of 35 gallons per
minute the design rate would be 105 gallons per minute
This absorption rate could be provided by six seepage pits
which would also provide a storage capacity of approximately
3 750 gallons each This total storage of approximately
22 550 gallons would be equivalent to five hours of capa
city for maximum flows of 180 gallons per minute
We are herein requesting your approval of the proposed ten
seepage pits and your consideration and approval of the
alternative of utilizing six seepage pits
Your prompt consideration of our request would be greatly
appreciated Thank you for your help in expediting our
request

INTRODUCTION
The preliminary plans for the Nawiliwili Harbor View Estates
show that the approximate 14 acre site will be developed for
148 townhouse units within 12 structures The proposed develop
ment will include recreational facilities at the northern end
of the property and a sewage treatment plant with injection wells
W O 554 00 Page 1974
at approximately the center of the southern half of the develop
ment The location of the proposed sewage treatment plant is
outlined on the attached Site Plan and Boring Locations
As can be seen by the Site Plan the project is in Nawiliwili
Lihue Kauai about 1 4 mile south of the junction of Nawiliwili
and Waapa Roads Ground surface at the site rises gently from
Wilcox Road at a 5 to 10 per cent gradient to the vicinity of
the Driveway A where the ground surface begins to rise more
steeply
Geology
Our geological reconnaissance and test borings delineated a well
preserved ancient shoreline approximately 15 feet above the pre
sent sea level The ancient shoreline is located at approximately
ground surface contour of elevation 16 feet and has been super
imposed upon the enclosed Plate 1 Below this contour line as
shown by Borings B l and PT 1 the site is underlain by deep deposits
of loose well graded beach sands Above the ancient shoreline the
site is underlain by cohesive residual soils
Injection Wells
The original drilling program included two injection wells PT 1
and PT 2 At the time of the subsurface investigation access to
PT 2 was denied by the present occupants of the property We
were therefore only able to complete PT 1 It was extended to
a depth of 47 feet and cased to a depth of 22 feeth with 4 inch
W O 554 00 Page 1974
diameter plastic pipe Due to cohesionless sand below the casing
the lower portion caved and partially filled the boring
To obtain preliminary indications of the percolation rate a test
was performed upon PT 1 utilizing the maximum available water
supply of approximately 50 gallons per minute With this flow
rate there was no significant change in the water level within
the well The findings of the subsurface explorations show that
a deep pervious layer of cohesionless beach sand is present be
neath the sewage treatment site Injection wells within this
material will require full depth casing
Based upon the grain size analysis and preliminary testing it
is estimated that the sand has a coeffiency of permeability of
200 X sec An injection well extending to a depth of
50 feet with the lower 30 feet fully screened would produce a
percolation rate in excess of 200 gallons per minute
CONCLUSIONS RECOMMENDATIONS
The subsurface explorations show that a deep pervious layer of
well graded clean sand is available at the proposed injection
well locations This layer will provide adequate percolation
rates for the proposed sewage treatment plant It is recommended
that the proposed injection wells be installed to a depth of 50
feet utilizing complete screens for the lower 30 feet portion
The screens will maintain the borings and prevent caving per
mit flow outwards and prevent the infiltration of the sand
W O 554 00 Page 1974
into the casing Our sieve analysis of the samples obtained at
the site indicate that the screen should have maximum openings
of 0 2 millimeters and a minimum diameter of 4 inches
Utilizing this screen it is our opinion that a filter layer
will not be required since the predominate flow will be outward
and the screen sizing will prevent the inward migration of the
sand grains into the casing
Since the actual installation conditions could not be established
due to the restrictions upon the site access and the caving of
the lower portion of the borings the verification of the actual

Subject Drilling and Percolation Testing

Gentlemen
This letter summarizes the drilling and percolation testing
performed at the subject site during March
In accordance with your request we initially drilled two
injection wells PT 2 and PT 3 at the locations shown on the
enclosed Plate 1 The percolation testing of these two wells
indicated rates of 10 to 15 gallons per minute GPM well
below the specified maximum rate of 180 GPM Following con
sultations with VTN Pacific and Sam Shelton on March 5 we
drilled and tested three additional injection wells to locate
a more pervious zone within the sand deposit These wells
5 and PT 6 also had injection rates of only 10 to
15 GPM
While these percolation rates are not adequate for injection
wells they do indicate an adequate rate for seepage pit
disposal of the sewage effluent
Following additional consultations with VTN Pacific pertain
ing to the feasibility of utilizing seepage pits we tested
the area near PT 6 by the percolation method for seepage
pits This test designated as S 4 resulted in a seepage
rate of 14 inches per minute
W 2 March 25 1975

lation tests The locations of these borings are designated
by S 1 S 2 and S 3 shown on Plate 1 At these locations
the borings were extended to a depth of 20 feet cased with a
4 inch diameter perforated plastic pipe and tested to measure
the rate of drop of the water level within the casing
The seepage tests were performed on March 13 1975 and were
witnessed by of the Department of Health
The test results were included in our March 18 letter to the
Department of Health and are not again enclosed
Copies of the driller s logs for PT 2 through PT 6 are
enclosed for your general information The classifications
given on these logs were made in the field and have not been
verified by laboratory testing The drilling entailed 285
lineal feet for the injection wells and 60 lineal feet for
the seepage pit tests for a total of 345 lineal feet of drill
ing
