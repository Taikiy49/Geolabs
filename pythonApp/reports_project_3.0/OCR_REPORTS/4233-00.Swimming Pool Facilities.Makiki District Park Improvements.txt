INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration performed
for the proposed swimming pool facilities located within the Makiki District Park near the
intersection of Keeaumoku Street and the Interstate Route H 1 Highway in the Makiki area
in the District of Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity
of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering recommendations
derived from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering analyses The
recommendations presented herein are intended for the design of foundations
slabs on grade and site preparation only The findings and recommendations presented
herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lavas from two
shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be
middle to late Pliocene in age and Koolau is estimated to be late Pliocene to early
Pleistocene in age After a long period of volcanic inactivity during which time erosion
incised deep valleys into the Koolau shield volcanic activity returned with a series of lava
flows followed by cinder and tuff cone formations These series are referred to as the
Honolulu Volcanic Series
During the Pleistocene Epoch Ice Age sea levels fluctuated in response to the
cycles of continental glaciation As the glaciers grew and advanced less water was available
to fill the oceanic basins such that sea levels fell below the present stands of the sea When
the glaciers melted and receded an excess of water became available such that the sea
levels rose to above the present sea level
The processes of erosion and deposition were affected by these glacio eustatic sea
level fluctuations When the sea level was low the erosional base level was correspondingly
lower and valleys were carved to depths below the present sea level When the sea level
was high the erosional base level was raised such that sediments accumulated at higher
elevations
The project site is located on the southeastern flank of Koolau on the eastern end of
Punchbowl Crater Late stage volcanic eruptions known as the Honolulu Volcanic Series
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 4233 00 Page 5
form landmarks on Oahu such as Diamond Head Punchbowl Tantalus and Salt Lake
craters The subsurface conditions in the Makiki area are dominated by fill soils near the
surface followed by cinder sand and voicanic tuff cemented volcanic ash deposits from
these late stage volcanic eruptions

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
As mentioned above our field exploration generally encountered stiff to hard silty fill
materials overlying medium dense to very dense cinder materials in the borings drilled at the
site Based on the subsoil conditions encountered in our borings we believe that a shallow
foundation system bearing on the recompacted near surface soils may be used for support
of the proposed swimming pool and other structures planned at the site Considering the
proximity of the new structures to the existing buildings foundations for the swimming pool
and other structures should not encroach below an imaginary plane extending from the
bottom of footings for the existing building to reduce the potential for undermining the existing
building footings during construction
Our laboratory tests indicate that the in situ silty fill soils exhibit a slight potential for
shrinking and swelling when subjected to moisture fluctuations In order to reduce the
potential for structural distress to future slabs on grade resulting from the shrinking swelling
of the on site silty fill soils we recommend that concrete slabs on grade for the new building
be supported on at least 6 inches of non expansive select granular fill materials in addition

Building Foundations
Based on the subsoil conditions encountered at the site we believe that shallow
spread and or continuous footings may be used for support of the proposed swimming pool
facilities An allowable bearing pressure of up to 3 000 pounds per square foot psf may be
used for the design of foundations bearing on the recompacted in situ soils This bearing
value is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third 1 3 for transient loads
such as those caused by wind or seismic forces
in general footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest
adjacent grade If foundations are located next to utility trenches buildings or easements
they should be embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane
extending upward from the bottom edge of the utility trench building foundation or as deep
as the inverts of the utility lines This requirement is necessary to avoid surcharging adjacent
below grade structures with additional structural loads and to reduce the potential for
foundation settlement
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 4233 00 Page 8
The bottom of the footing excavations should be recompacted to a minimum of
90 percent relative compaction prior to placement of reinforcing steel or concrete In
addition the footing subgrade should be dense firm and unyieiding If soft and or loose
materials are encountered at the bottom of the footing excavations they should be
over excavated to expose the underlying firm materials The over excavation should be
backfilled with the excavated on site materials compacted to a minimum of 90 percent
relative compaction or the footing may be extended down to the underlying competent
material
We estimate that footing settlements under the anticipated design loads for footings
bearing on the recompacted on site soils to be less than 1 inch total with differential
settlements on the order of less than 0 5 inches
Lateral loads acting on the structures may be resisted by frictional resistance
developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil and by passive earth
pressure acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system A coefficient of
friction of 0 35 may be used for footings bearing on the recompacted on site soils
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid
pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth pcf This assumes that the soils
around the footings are well compacted Unless covered by pavements or slabs the passive
pressure resistance in the upper 12 inches of soil should be neglected
We recommend that footing excavations be observed by a representative of
Geolabs Hawaii prior to placement of reinforcing steel or concrete to confirm the competency
of the foundation bearing materials and the required embedment depths

Slabs On Grade
We anticipate that concrete slabs will be used for the swimming pool deck area and
for the building of the swimming pool facilities As previous indicated our laboratory tests
indicate that the clayey silts encountered in our borings exhibit a slight potential for
shrink swell when subjected to moisture fluctuations To reduce the potential for structural
distress of the slabs on grade resulting from swelling of the clayey silt soils we recommend
that the subgrade soils underneath the slab areas be properly prepared and capped with
at least 6 inches of non expansive select granular fill capping fill material below the slab
cushion The non expansive select granular fill should also extend beyond the building
perimeter a minimum of 24 inches
The non expansive select granular fill should be placed as soon as possible after
the subgrade soils are prepared in accordance with the recommendations presented in the
Site Preparation section of this report The capping fill and the subgrade soils should be
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 4233 00 Page 11
wetted and kept moist prior to final placement of concrete Where shrinkage cracks are
noted after compaction of the subgrade we recommend that the soils be thoroughly
moistened to close the cracks or prepared again Saturation and subsequent yielding of
the exposed subgrade due to inclement weather and or poor drainage may require
over excavation of the soft areas and replacement with well compacted fill
For more uniform support we recommend that concrete slabs for the building be
supported on a minimum 4 inch layer of cushion fill consisting of free drainage materials
such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation which would also serve as a
capillary moisture break To reduce the potential for future moisture infiltration and
subsequent damage to floor coverings an impervious moisture vapor barrier is
recommended on top of the cushion layer Two inches of moist fine sand may be
considered above the plastic membrane to provide protection of the membrane and to aid
in curing of the concrete slab The cushion fill vapor membrane and sand may be omitted
for the pool deck slab However the 6 inches of non expansive select granular fill should
be placed below the pool deck slabs
The thickened edges of slabs adjacent to unpaved areas should be embedded at
least 12 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade Areas adjacent to the slab
should be backfilled tightly against the slab with relatively impervious soils and graded to
divert water away from the slab to reduce the potential for water ponding around the
foundations

At the on set of earthwork areas within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared Vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable materials within the
grading limits should be removed and disposed of properly off site The existing concrete
sidewalks at the site that are to be removed and construction debris generated from the
removal operations should be properly disposed of off site
Soft and yielding areas encountered during clearing below areas designated to

Existing underground utilities to be abandoned shouid be removed and the resulting

above the optimum moisture content placed in 6 to 8 inch loose lifts and compacted to a
minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
After clearing and demolition areas beneath the future slabs should be
over excavated where necessary to provide the minimum 6 inch thick capping layer of
non expansive select granular fill material below the slab subgrade level i e bottom of the
gravel cushion layer The non expansive select granular fill should extend a minimum of
2 feet beyond the perimeter of the proposed building The excavated cinder materials
generated from the swimming pool excavation may be re used as a source of select granular
fill material provided that the cinder materials are segregated and screened of over sized
materials
Prior to placement of the capping fill the subgrade soils should be scarified to a depth
of about 8 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction In areas where the finished
grades will be subjected to vehicular traffic the compaction requirement should be increased

Utility Trenches
We anticipate that new utility lines will be needed for the proposed project In general
granular bedding material consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67
gradation is recommended under the pipes Free draining granular materials such as
GEOLABS HAWAII

No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation should also be used for the trench
backfill up to about 12 inches above the pipes to provide adequate support around the pipes
and to reduce the compaction effort of the backfill thus reducing the possibility of damaging
the pipes It is critical that the free draining material be used around the pipes to reduce the
potential for formation of voids below the haunches of pipes and to provide adequate support

The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipes to the

are below pavement areas the upper 2 feet of the trench backfill below the pavement
subgrade should be compacted to at least 95 percent relative compaction
Drainage
The finished grades outside the building structures should be sloped to shed water
away from the foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also advised that
gutter systems be installed around the building and that the discharge be diverted away from
the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near the foundations
and slabs should also be avoided Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have
concrete bottoms and drains to reduce the potential for water infiltration into the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the above
foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation and
subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations should be properly
backfilled against the walls or slab edges immediately after setting of the concrete to reduce
the potential for water infiltration In addition drainage swales should be provided as soon
as possible and should be maintained to drain surface water run off away from the
foundations and slabs

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling
four borings extending to depths of about 21 5 to 26 5 feet below the existing ground surface
at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were drilled using
a truck mounted drill rig equipped with continuous flight solid stem augers
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by testing
in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representations of the materials
encountered are presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 4
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with ASTM
Test Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving
a 3 inch OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The
blow counts needed to drive the sampler the last 12 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown on
the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Pocket penetrometer tests were performed on selected cohesive soil samples in the
field The pocket penetrometer test provides an indication of the unconfined compressive
strength of the soil sample Results of the pocket penetrometer tests are summarized on the
Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content ASTM D 2216 and unit weight determinations were performed on
selected soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties The
results of these tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Five 1 inch ring swell tests were performed on the natural soil samples to evaluate the
swelling potential of the near surface soils The test results are presented on Plate B 1
One Direct Shear test ASTM D 3080 was performed on a selected sample of the
soils to evaluate the shear strength characteristics of the material Results of this test are
presented on Plate B 2
