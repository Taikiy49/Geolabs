This letter provides our preliminary findings and recommendations for the above

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
A retail facility for Best Buy is planned in the Pearl City area on the makai side of
the intersection of Kamehameha Highway and Kanuku Street on the Island of Oahu
Hawaii as shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 We understand that the facility will
consist of a two story main building with a planned area of approximately 50 000 square
feet a parking deck structure and other related infrastructures
The project site was formerly occupied by the Tony Nissan and Tony Honda
automobile dealerships Based on the topographic survey map provided by Architects
Hawaii Ltd the existing ground surface generally slopes up to the north with elevations
range from 5 to 16 feet Mean Sea Level MSL
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsoil conditions at the subject site were explored by drilling and sampling
seven borings ranging in depths from about 76 8 to 91 8 feet below the existing ground
surface The approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Our field exploration indicates that the project site is generally underlain by a
surface fill layer ranging in thickness from 4 to 15 feet The fill layer generally consisted of
medium stiff to stiff silty clay and loose to medium dense sand and gravel The fill layer
was underlain by a lagoonal deposit that generally consisted of very soft clayey silt and

PRELIMINARY FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration generally encountered a highly compressible lagoonal deposit
below the project site The very soft lagoonal deposit ranged in thickness from about 6 to
52 feet Groundwater was encountered at depths ranging from 4 7 to 6 8 feet below the
existing ground surface Artesian water conditions were also encountered in the borings
Based on the highly compressible lagoonal deposit encountered at relatively
shallow depths and the moderate to high structural loads anticipated for the planned
development we believe that appreciable settlements will occur if a shallow foundation
system is used to support the proposed structures Therefore we recommend that the
proposed structures be supported on a deep foundation system consisting of 16 5 inch
octagonal precast prestressed concrete piles An allowable compressive load capacity of
up to 100 tons per pile may be used for preliminary design
The driven piles will extend through the very soft lagoonal deposit and into the
underlying stiff alluvial deposit and extremely to slightly weathered basalt formation The
driven piles will derive support primarily from side resistance between the sides of the pile
and the surrounding soils We envision that preliminary pile lengths on the order of about
78 and 65 feet long may be used to estimate the lengths of these piles for the parking deck
and building structures respectively

Due to the thick highly compressible lagoonal deposit beneath the project site new
fills placed will induce ground settlements that will occur for a long period of time In
addition these settlements will cause downdrag loading on the piles and significantly
reduce the allowable pile capacity Therefore the placement of new fills should be
avoided and structural slabs should be considered for the building structure
Should placement of fills be desirable the use of surcharge fill may be considered
However this alternative requires a long settlement period and may induce settlements to
adjacent structures Another alternative is the use of lightweight fill But this alternative
may be very costly
