INTRODUCTION
In accordance with your request we have conducted percolation testing at the
subject sites to provide data for the design of leaching fields for the disposal of treated
sanitary wastes The approximate location of the project sites and their vicinity is shown
on the Project Location Map Plate 1 Our work was performed in general accordance
with our revised proposal dated
This report summarizes our findings for the installation of leaching fields at the
project sites
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Royal Hawaiian Country Club is a new recreational facility under construction
in Maunawili on the windward side of the island of Oahu Hawaii The site development
includes a golf course It is proposed to provide restroom facilities at Tee Nos 14
and 17
The project is situated in an unsewered area It will be necessary to use on site
treatment and disposal for sanitary wastes generated at the facilities The current scheme
is to utilize septic tanks for treatment and leaching fields for disposal

GEOHYDROLOGIC CONSIDERATIONS
The ground surface elevations at the site at the time of percolation testing were
approximately 308 and 232 feet above Mean Sea Level for Tee Nos 14 and 17
respectively
The logs of the drilled borings are presented in Appendix A Generally the borings
encountered surface soils consisting of very stiff brown silty clays to the maximum depths
explored of five 5 feet below the existing ground surface
Groundwater was not encountered in the drilled borings at the time of our field
work and percolation testing
PERCOLATION TESTING
After the drilling of the borings the drilled holes were filled with water and allowed
to saturate overnight The following day the borings were refilled with water for
performance of falling head percolation tests
The tests were conducted by measuring the fall of the water level in the borings
with respect to time The incremental rate of fall for the last six 6 inches is used to
evaluate the absorption capacity of the soil

DISCUSSIONS AND FINDINGS
Leaching Field Design
Our field percolation testing indicates that the soils at the site have absorption rates
ranging from about 20 minutes per inch to about 25 minutes per inch These rates are
relatively low but are still considered to be within the acceptable range for the use of
leaching fields
Based on the methods given in U S Public Health Service Publication No 526
Manual of Septic Tank Practice for a percolation rate of 25 minutes per inch septic tank
effluent may be applied to a standard leaching field trench at a rate of one 1 gallon per
day per square foot of trench bottom In this publication a standard trench is defined as
having six 6 inches of gravel below the perforated pipe
Based on the methods given in Publication No 526 assuming uniform soil
conditions the length of trench may be decreased by increasing the amount of gravel
below the pipe The partial plans provided to us show a leaching field trench design
using 12 inches of gravel below the pipe For this case the length of the trench may be
reduced to about 83 percent of the length required for a standard trench If the thickness
of gravel is increased to 24 inches the length may be reduced to about 62 percent of the
standard trench length
Operation and Maintenance
It should be realized that any effluent disposal system such as a leaching field or
seepage pit which relies on soil absorption will eventually fail due to clogging of the soil

and biological action It is our recommendation that a backup system be installed to
minimize the potential of loss of service in the event of failure of the leaching field
Soil absorption systems are sensitive to the operation and maintenance of the
treatment system and the disposal system Improper operation and maintenance can
lead to rapid failure t is our further recommendation that an experienced septic system
contractor be retained to provide proper operation and maintenance
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from field data points such as drilled borings and percolation testing
Variations of conditions may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may
not become evident until construction and operation of the leaching fields If variations
then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the findings presented in this
report
