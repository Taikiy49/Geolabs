In accordance with your request we have made a study of the near
surface soil conditions at the above referenced lots in order to provide
data for use in the design of the grading work for the above two lots
The scope of this reconnaissance included two hand auger probes
visual observations of the existing site conditions by one of our
geologists laboratory testing and engineering analysis
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
Site Description
The proposed two lots cover an area of about 5 acres located
easterly of the intersection of Mahailua and Waikupanaha Streets in
Waimanalo Oahu Hawaii
The westerly portion of the lots adjacent to Mahailua Street are
located over a grassy and brushy drainageway that runs down towards the
north The easterly portions of the lots are located in a sugar cane
field
The ground surface at the cane field slopes down in a northerly
direction towards the drainageway with gradients of about 6 to 10
percent The side slopes of the drainageway vary from 7 1 H V to
about 2 1
Project Considerations
The proposed grading plan is to fill the drainage area to provide
two level lots for residential development
The existing open drainageway will be replaced with a 54 inch
diameter drain line as shown on the Site Plan Plate 1 The grading
plan indicates that only filling in the drainageway will be required
The filling will cover an area about 400 feet long and varying from 70
to 150 feet wide along the westerly portion of the lots

Discussion and Recommendations
The field exploration and reconnaissance in the drainageway
generally encountered stiff surface materials consisting of brown silty
clay overlying gravel cobbles or softer stream deposits Therefore
the proposed drain line excavation may encounter soft ground Where
encountered some over excavation and backfilling may be required
Because soit soils may underlie portions of the proposed fill

Site Grading
Fills up to about 8 feet thick are anticipated in the gully
Prior to the commencement of grading the site should be thoroughly
cleared of vegetation boulders rubbish and other deleterious
materials This grubbed material should be disposed of properly
off site It is likely that some very soft silt or clay may be
encountered in the drainageway If encountered very soft sediments
should be removed from the fill area
Imported 111 will be required Fill and backfill should be of
low expansion potential materials with CBR of at least 8 and expansion
value less than 3 Boulders or rock fragments in excess of 8 inches in
Size should be removed from the fill and backfill material The
proposed fill material should be tested and approved by the soils
engineer before it is hauled to the site
Following the site preparation fill should be placed in 9 inch
loose horizontal lifts and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum
dry density as established by the ASTM D 1557 78 test method At the
time of compaction the fill material should be at a proper moisture
content to achieve the above recommended compaction If the material is
too wet or too dry it should be dried or wetted as necessary

Drain Line Construction
For the construction of the proposed 54 inch drain line proper
backfilling of the trench is important to minimize ground settlement
Soft material at the bottom of the drain line trench should be removed
to firm soils and the resulting excavation backfilled with granular
material such as No 3B Coarse rock On site material generally less
than 6 inches in size and or approved imported material tay be utilized
as backfill All backfill should be placed in 6 inch horizontal lifts
and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density as
determined by the ASTM D 1557 78 test method Most of the on site
material is fine grained and would not be suitable for compaction by
water jetting or ponding and therefore would require mechanical
compaction

Lateral Earth Pressure on Head Wall
The lateral earth pressures on the head wall may be considered as
that developed by an equivalent fluid weighing 40 p c f In addition
Surcharge loads if required may be converted to an equivalent fill
height of 1 foot for every 100 p s f of the uniform sucharge load
To resist the lateral forces passive earth pressures of an
equivalent fluid weighing 150 p c f may be used A base friction
factor of 0 3 should be used The weights of the structure and soil
below the design water level should be reduced to their respective
buoyant weights for considering the base friction
House Foundations
To minimize the effects of settlements the houses should be set at
the back of the two lots a minimum distance of 25 feet away from the
near edge of the new drain line
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the drain line construction and filling
operations be observed by the soils engineer during construction to see
that adequate compaction has been obtained and that the subsurface
conditions are consistent with the findings of our investigation
If conditions encountered during construction are different from
those presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made

INVESTIGATION LIMITATIONS
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations
made in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation
engineering principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all
other warranties either expressed or implied
The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are
based on our site reconnaissance soil information from probing and
