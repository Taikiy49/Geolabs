This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations and testing services

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is west of Fort Barrette Road north of the future Kapolei Parkway and
south of Kamaaha Avenue in Kapolei on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project involved the
mass grading of one lot approximately 4 acres in area for a commercial development The site
development consists of five commercial structures ranging in plan area from about 2 060 to
36 320 square feet driveways and parking area Underground utilities will be installed such
as water sewer and drain lines A Project Location Map and a Site Plan are shown on Plates
1 and 2 respectively
Based on the grading plans the existing ground surface elevations generally ranged
from about 56 5 to 67 feet Mean Sea Level MSL and the finish grades will range from
about 60 to 64 feet MSL Therefore cut and fill work will be limited to about 8 feet or less in
order to achieve the design finished grades
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services pertaining
to the mass grading operations for the project site

MASS GRADING
The mass grading work for the Kapolei Village Center project were observed by
Geolabs Inc from to March 10 2011 The mass grading work consisted of
clearing and grubbing and placement and compaction of fills on the order of up to about 8 feet
thick to achieve the design finished mass grades for the project The mass grading was
performed by Jayar Construction Inc During the mass grading operations samples of the on
site and imported materials utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported
to our office for testing The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were
established in our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor and
the test results are summarized on Plate 3
The fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as
determined in accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557 In the roadway and parking
areas the fill materials within 2 feet of the finished subgrade were compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative density
During the mass grading operations periodic field density tests were performed in
general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 6938 Nuclear Gauge Method Results of
the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 4 1 through 4 7 of this report
These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and
backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating
from on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should be considered accurate
only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were
reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained and or approved by our field
representative based on visual observation and hand probing
Our services consisted of part time field observation of the contractor s fill placement
subgrade preparation and compaction procedures and confirmation of subgrade soil
conditions exposed during the above grading operations
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the referenced
tests it is our opinion that the mass grading work for the above referenced project observed
and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report were performed in conformance
with the general intent of the recommendations in the following references

Geolabs assumes no responsibility for any alterations made to slopes or finished
grades on the subject project subsequent to our observation and testing period without our
knowledge and written acknowledgment

observation However we believe that the level of earthwork observation and testing

recognized that there are variations in the accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests
