PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project involves the construction of emergency access roads along the
Waianae Coast on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The proposed emergency access roads
will involve improvements of existing roads and construction of new roads to interconnect
existing roads for community use during emergency situations
The Paakea Road project will involve the construction of the portion of emergency
access roads along Paakea Road connecting Hakimo Road to Lualualei Naval Road for a
total length of approximately 1 700 feet The new roadway will generally consist of two
12 foot wide travelways with a 8 foot wide grassed area on both sides As part of the
project construction a new 100 foot long single span concrete bridge will replace the
existing bridge over the Ulehawa Stream crossing Based on the plan and profile

Based on the subsurface conditions encountered and the structural loads provided
deep foundations consisting of drilled shafts were used for foundation support of the new
concrete bridge structure The drilled shafts were designed to derive support principally
from skin friction between the drilled shaft and the upper stiff clayey soils and underlying
soft to medium hard coral formation encountered in our borings The contribution from
end bearing of the drilled shafts has been discounted in the design due to difficulties
associated with obtaining a clean bottom below the water level during construction
Based on the construction drawings for the project the new bridge structure will be
supported by a foundation system consisting of 36 inch diameter cast in place concrete
drilled shafts Each abutment will be supported by four drilled shafts with a design tip
elevation of 0 feet Mean Sea Level MSL and an allowable compressive load capacity of
220 kips The identification numbers and approximate locations of the drilled shafts are
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 As part of the construction requirements a bi directional
axial load test using an expandable load cell Osterberg Load Cell was conducted to
evaluate the adequacy of the bridge foundation design
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
In general our deep borings drilled in the proximity of Abutment No 1
Boring No 3 and Abutment No 2 Boring No 4 locations for the new bridge structure
generally encountered surface fills over very stiff silty clays and sandy silts extending to
depths of about 20 to 22 feet below the existing ground surface Soft to medium hard
coral reef formation was then encountered and extended to the maximum depth explored
of about 80 feet below the existing ground surface
Water level was encountered in the borings drilled at depths of about 39 to
39 7 feet below the existing ground surface at the time of our field exploration These
depths generally correspond to about Elevation 0 8 and 1 0 feet MSL It should be
noted that groundwater levels are subject to change due to seasonal precipitation tidal
fluctuations surface water runoff and other factors

site The load test shaft was excavated by CMZ of Hawaii Inc starting on

PRODUCTION SHAFT INSTALLATION PROGRAM
Based on our observations of the load test program the demonstrated excavation
method and equipment were accepted for installing the remaining production drilled shafts
In addition the design tip elevations were unchanged for the remaining production shafts
based on the results of the load test program
The installation of the majority of the production shafts was also performed by
CMZ of Hawaii Inc Due to the presence of overhead power lines Drilled Shaft Nos 1
and 5 were installed by Foundation International Inc using similar excavation method
The contractor began installation of the production shafts on and
completed production shaft installation on August 23 2005 The as built dimensions of
the production shafts are summarized on Plate 3 of this report Representatives from
Geolabs observed the excavation placement of reinforcement cage and concrete
operations during the installation of the production shafts After excavation of the
production shafts to their designated tip elevations the reinforcement cages were
placed into the drilled holes by use of a crane and temporarily supported at the surface
Rebar cage spacers were used to provide the clearances associated with a minimum
concrete cover of 3 inches between the sidewall of the drilled hole and the sides of the
of the drilled shaft holes using an elephant trunk with the bottom of the elephant trunk
maintained within 5 feet from the top of concrete as required in the specifications For
DS 1 and DS 5 the shaft concrete was placed using a 4 inch ID steel pipe by tremie
method with the bottom of the tremie pipe immersed in at least 5 feet of concrete It
was noted that concrete placement continued until sound concrete was observed at the
top of the shaft Logs of the individual drilled shafts and the materials encountered during
shaft excavation are presented in Appendix A
Based on our field observations we believe that the drilled shafts for this project
have been installed in general conformance with the project plans and specifications and
within the limits of generally accepted construction practices Based on the load test
