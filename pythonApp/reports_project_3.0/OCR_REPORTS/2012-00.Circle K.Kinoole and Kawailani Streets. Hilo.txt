INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
evaluation performed for the proposed Circle K Store in Hilo on the
island of Hawaii The general location of the project site and its
vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The exploration
was performed in general accordance with our proposal dated March 4 1988
This report summarizes our findings from the exploration and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analyses based
on the data obtained during the exploration These recommendations are
intended for design input and are not intended for construction estimation
or bidding purposes

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a Circle K convenience facility at the
site The facility will consist of a building about 40 by 145 foot in
plan dimensions and a 25 by 80 foot pump island with gasoline dispenser
units To supply the gas island an underground gasoline storage area
consisting of three 3 8 foot diameter tanks is planned adjacent to the
pump island A drainage pit is planned for surface runoff disposal of the
project area
Site grading involving cuts up to 17 feet southwestern portion and
fills up to 7 feet northeastern portion at the site is anticipated
Retaining walls up to 12 feet high along the western and southern
boundaries are planned for grade separation
The convenience facility will be surrounded by an asphaltic concrete
parking lot

subsurface disposal of surface runoff

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located on the southwestern corner of the
junction of Kinoole and Kawailani Streets in Hilo Hawaii
The site is bounded to the west by a Chinese restaurant and to the
south by a vacant lot
The site measures 280 by 140 feet in plan dimensions and slopes down
toward the northeast at slope ratios of about 5 1 to 3 1 H V Ground
elevations at the site vary between 148 0 and 183 0 feet above Mean Sea
Level
Presently the site is vacant and generally covered with grass
banana trees African tulip and other trees and shrubs

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling three 3
borings and one 1 probe to depths ranging from 4 0 to 29 0 feet below
the existing ground surface The approximate locations of the borings are
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general the borings encountered about 5 feet of ash clinker
consisting of medium stiff clayey silt and loose silty sand and gravel
over basalt formation aa and pahoehoe in excess of 29 feet
Groundwater was not encountered during our exploration Detailed
descriptions of the materials encountered are presented on the Boring
Logs Plates A 1 through A 4
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on the plans supplied to us cuts up to 17 feet and fills up to
7 feet are anticipated at the site to meet the finished floor elevation of
162 5 feet Thus the proposed store and pump island may be founded
partially on the the surficial ash clinker layer and partially on the
underlying basalt formation as a result of the cuts Shallow spread
footing foundations may be used for the proposed facilities
Site Grading
Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation
debris rubbish and other unsuitable materials should be removed and
disposed properly off site in order to prevent contamination of the
excavated material

Excavation
The excavation into the near surface ash clinker layer may be made
with a dozer or backhoe The more massive underlying basalt may
require special excavating procedures such as hoeram to make the
proposed cuts However the actual rippability of the basalt
formation should be determined by the contractor on site
For permanent cut slopes at the site a slope ratio of 2 1 H V for
basalt formation southwestern portion may be used Alternatively
an average overall slope ratio of 1 5 horizontal to 1 vertical
may be used
Fill Material and Placement
In general the on site ash clinker and excavated basalt broken down
to 6 inches minus size may be used as fill material
If required new imported fill material should consist of
non expansive select granular materials less than 3 inches in maximum
size with less than 20 passing the 200 sieve
The fill should be placed in horizontal loose lifts up to 12 inches
thick and compacted to 90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM
D 1557 78
Fill on areas where the existing surface exceeds a Slope ratio of 5
horizontal to 1 vertical 5 1 should be keyed and benched into the
existing firm ground to properly bond the new fill to the slope for
details see Plate 3 This detail is essential for the stability of
the fills particularly to reduce the potential sliding along the
fill in situ ground contact The filling operation should start at
the lowest point and continue up in level compacted layers

Foundations
Based on our borings and the grading plan the proposed Circle Kk
Store and pump island may be founded partly in the surficial ash clinker
layer and partly on the underlying basalt as a result of the cuts
Shallow spread footing foundations may be used for the proposed
structures
Footings should be embedded a minimum of 12 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grade into the ash clinker layer The bottom 6 inches
of the footing excavations should be scarified and compacted to 90
maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78 The minimum embedment for
footings bearing on the basalt formation may be reduced to 6 inches below
the lowest adjacent finished grade
The recommended minimum footing width is 12 inches For footings
bearing on the compacted ash clinker layer an allowable bearing value of
be used for footings bearing on basalt These values may be increased by
one third when temporary wind or seismic loads are considered
Resistance to lateral loads on the building can be obtained by
passive earth pressure and frictional resistance A frictional
coefficient of 0 4 may be used to calculate the friction between the
bottom of the footings and basalt compacted ash clinker layer In
addition an equivalent fluid pressure of 350 p s f per foot of depth may
be used to determine the passive earth resistance against the buried
vertical faces of the footings The perimeters of the footings should be
properly compacted in order to provide the recommended passive resistance
Minimal settlements are anticipated for the light structural loads

Slab On Grade
The on grade floor slab should be placed over a 4 inch thick cushion
layer of gravel size material such as 3B Fine rock to provide uniform
support A moisture barrier membrane should be placed under the slabs
The thickened edges of the on grade slabs should extend a minimum of 12
inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade or 6 inches into the
basalt formation The backfill against the concrete slab should be
adequately compacted and graded to shed water away from the slab
perimeter
To reduce potential differential settlements where the slab is
partially on cut in rock formation and partially on soil the cut portions
should be over excavated about one foot and then recompacted to match the
adjacent subgrade density
Underground Storage Tanks
It is planned to place three storage tanks underground in an area
adjacent to the pump island
The tanks will probably bear on the underlying rock formation which
has high bearing capacity and can provide adequate support Although our
exploration did not encounter voids it is common that lava flows contain
tubes or cavities Therefore should any large cavities or voids be
encountered during construction they should be evaluated by the soils
engineer and the design should be modified if necessary
Based on our field exploration special excavating procedures such
as a hoeram may be required to excavated down to the underlying basalt
The actual rippability of the material should be determined by the
contractor on site
To reduce the compactive effort required and to reduce the
possibility of damage to the tanks a clean gravel material such as ASTM
C 33 No 67 gravel should be used for the backfill material

Retaining Walls
It is our understanding that retaining walls are planned for grade
separation for the proposed construction
Based on the anticipated subsurface conditions basalt formation may
be encountered along the wall locations In the southeastern portion of
the site however ash clinker may present below the wall footings An
allowable bearing capacity of 3000 p s f may be used for wall footings on
recompacted ash clinker The bearing values are for total dead plus live
loads and may be increased by one third when temporary wind and seismic
loads are considered
A minimum wall footing embedment of 12 inches is recommended
Clean granular materials such as the on site silty sand and gravel
clinker should be used as backfill directly behind the wall This
would reduce the compactive effort required for the backfill and the
build up of additional lateral pressures on the wall

Subsurface Disposal
It is our understanding that a drainage pit is required for surface
runoff disposal of the project area
Based on our field percolation test performed in Boring B 3 the
measured coefficient of permeability is on the order of 1 3 x 1072
gal day sq ft The actual size of the subsurface
disposal system will depend on the design quantity of the future surface
runoff The details of the field percolation test are presented in
Appendix B
It should be pointed out that for the subsurface disposal system
the loss of infiltrative capacity and soil clogging can occur in the
future by combined physical chemical and biological factors The latter
is the most important factor since an organic layer of several millimeters
thick may form at the soil rock liquid interface Other factors such
as rainfall perched groundwater level etc may also affect the
permeability to some extent Therefore the disposal system may probably
require periodic maintenance

Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comment
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earth work and foundation recommendations given herein If this
review is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility
for misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Observation
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during the excavation site
grading and construction phases of the foundation work This is to
observe compliance with the design concepts specifications or
recommendations and to allow design changes in the event that subsurface
conditions differ from that anticipated prior to start of construction
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon such
observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as borings
and probes Variations of conditions between the field data points may
occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become
evident until construction If variations then appear evident it will be
necessary to re evaluate the recommendations given in this report

The location of the field data points were determined by taping from
structures indicated on the Site Plan undated provided by Kauahikaua
Chun Architects on March 22 1988 Elevations of the data points were
determined by interpolation from elevation points on the provided plan
The physical location and elevation of the field data points should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil rock
types and as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines
shown on profiles or cross section are based upon interpolation between
field data points and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
Water level data from the field data points was collected at the
times given on the graphic representations and or in the text of this
report This data has been reviewed and interpretations made in the
formulation of this report However it must be noted that fluctuation
may occur due to variations in rainfall tides temperature and other
factors
