Gentlemen
This report presents the results of our foundation investi
gation for the proposed 1635 Young Street Condominium in
Honolulu Oahu Hawaii Our work was done in general accor
dance with the scope outlined in our proposal dated November
18 1981
The purpose of our study was to investigate the subsurface
soil conditions by drilling test borings in order to provide
recommendations for the design and construction of the foun
dations for the proposed condominium structure

PROPOSED DEVELOPMENT
The proposed condominium structure will be a 4 story con
crete apartment building approximately 110 by 33 feet in
plan dimensions The first floor will be a slab on grade
parking area and the upper three floors will be composed of
seven residential units
Maximum ultimate column loads are anticipated to be about
238 kips
About 2 to 3 feet of fill is planned in the rear portion of
the lot to provide surface drainage toward Young Street
SITE CONDITIONS
The site is presently vacant and was until recently planted
for truck farming of vegetables There are four existing
trees on the site and loose piles of rubbles were noted
along the perimeter of the lot
The ground surface is relatively uniform and slopes down
from Young Street at a slope of approximately two percent

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
The site is located on the upper slopes of the Honolulu
Coastal Plain which is composed of interbedded alluvial
marine and volcanic deposits The post erosional eruptions
of cinder vents such as Tantalus Sugarloaf and Round Top
blanketed extensive areas of the Honolulu Coastal Plain with
thick deposits of sand sized cinder material In place
weathering of this cinder material has yielded a stiff brown
Silty soil at the surface of the project site
Subsurface Exploration
Five borings were drilled at the site to depths of about
21 5 to 41 5 feet below the existing ground surface to
explore the subsurface conditions and to obtain samples for
laboratory testing The approximate locations of the
borings are shown on the Boring Location Plan Plate 2
Detailed information on the field exploration and laboratory
testing is covered in the appendix along with the boring
logs and laboratory test results

Soil Conditions
Based on the borings the site is underlain by about 0 5 to
1 0 foot of topsoil over stiff brown clayey silt to about 7
to 8 foot depth This upper soil generally grades to very
stiff or hard with depths and generally has low expansion
potential
Below this upper layer the subsoils grade to a very stiff
to hard brown sandy silt derived from the weathering of
volcanic cinder to about 14 to 15 foot depth The subsoil
then grade to medium to dense silty sand to the depth of the
borings
Groundwater was encountered at about 17 to 19 foot depth
during the field exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
SEE UMM NDALT IONS
The field exploration encountered competent bearing soils at
the site Shallow foundations can be used for the proposed
4 story building

Foundations
Por the proposed structure spread or continuous footing
foundations are recommended The footings should extend a
minimum depth of 3 feet below the existing ground surface to
by pass the upper soft to medium stiff soil and bear on the
very stiff brown clayey silt
An allowable bearing value of 4000 p S f can be used for
footings bearing on the very stiff in situ soil The above
bearing value may be increased by one third when considering
temporary wind and seismic loads
Por the above recommendations estimated foundation settle
ments are on the order of one half of an inch total and
one quarter inch differential
To resist lateral loads a coefficient of friction of 0 4
can be used between the foundations and very stiff in situ
soil Additionally a passive soil resistance of 300 p c f
can be used
We recommend that all footing excavations be inspected by
the soils engineer to confirm the conditions upon which our
recommendations are based Any loose areas encountered
GEOLABS HAWAII

during excavation should be removed and replaced with con
crete or granular material compacted to a minimum of 95
maximum density The bottom of the footing excavations
should be tamped prior to steel placement Any loose soil
fallen into the footing excavations should be removed prior
to concrete placement
Site Grading
Two to three feet of fill is planned in the rear portion of
the lot to provide surface drainage toward Young Street
The site should be cleared of vegetation and debris
Prior to filling the 6 to 12 inch surface layer of topsoil
should be removed from below the proposed building area
This soil should not be used as structural fill nowever it
could be utilized in the landscape areas The underlying
soil excavated from the footing trenches have low expansion
potential and can be used in the fill
It appears that additional off site borrow material would be
required for the fill Any imported material should gener
ally be less than 3 inch in size and non expansive The
material should be tested and approved by the soil engineer
prior to hauling to the site
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1090 00 Page 7
Fills should be placed in 10 inch maximum loose lifts and
compacted to obtain a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry
density determined by ASTM D 1557
olab On Grade
Slab on grade should be placed over a 4 inch cushion layer
of rock gravel material such as 3B Fine rock to act as a
capillary break and to provide uniform support
The subgrade soils should be kept moist and recompacted
prior to placing the gravel cushion
Design Review
Plans for site grading should be forwarded to the soils
engineer for review and written comments prior to construc
tion to see that the intent of our recommendations is
included in the project plans and specifications
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the footing excavations overexcava
tion and recompaction be observed by a soils engineer during
construction This is to see that adequate bearing has been
obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent
with the findings of our investigation The recommendations
given in this report are contingent upon the above inspec
tions

APPENDIX A
FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored
by drilling 5 borings to depths of 21 5 to 41 5 feet below the
existing ground surface at the locations shown on the Boring
Location Plan Plate 2
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by
visual and textural examination in the field by our engineer
who continuously monitored the drilling operations The classi
fications were reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the
laboratory The soils were classified in general conformance
with the Unified Soil Classification System A graphic pre
sentation of the soils is presented on the Boring Logs Plates
A l through A 6
In the borings soil samples were obtained by driving
barrel sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The
blow counts to drive the Sampler the last 12 inches are shown
on the boring logs at the appropriate depths

APPENDIX B
LABORATORY TESTING
Moisture content determinations were performed on selected samples
as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties The results
of these tests are presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 6
One CBR test was performed on the near surface material to
evaluate the suitability of the soil as pavement support The results
are presented on Plate B 1
One sieve analysis was performed on sample of the underlying
soils to evaluate its grain size distribution The test result is presented
on Plate B 1
One ASTM D 1557 curve modified proctor was performed on a
bulk sample of the near surface soil to evaluate its compaction characteris
tics The test result is summarized on Plate B 1
One one inch ring swell test was performed on an undisturbed
sample of the near surface soil to evaluate its swelling potential The
test result is summarized on Plate B 2
Two direct shear tests were performed on undisturbed samples to
evaluate the strength characteristics of the in situ soil The test results
are presented on Plates B 3 B 4
Two consolidation tests were performed on undisturbed samples of
the underlying soils to evaluate the settlement characteristics of the in situ
soil The consolidation curves are presented on Plates B 5 and B 6
