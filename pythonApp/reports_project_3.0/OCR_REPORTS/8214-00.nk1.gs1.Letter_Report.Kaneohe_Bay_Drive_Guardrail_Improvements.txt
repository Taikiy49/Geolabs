Project Considerations 
The project site is located along Kaneohe Bay Drive in Kaneohe on the Island of 
Oahu, Hawaii. We understand that improvements to the guardrail on the makai side of the 
roadway are desired at the previously installed drilled shaft walls.  
The existing drilled shaft walls, identified as Wall Nos. 1 and 2, are about 158 and 
127 feet in length, respectively. Wall Nos. 1 and 2 extend from about Station Nos. 0+41 to 
1+99 and from about Station Nos. 6+75 to 8+02, respectively. The drilled shaft walls consist 
of 3-foot diameter drilled shafts that are spaced about 4 feet on center. The drilled shaft 
lengths range from about 15 to 25 feet. The tops of the drilled shafts are embedded into a 
3-foot thick concrete cap. The existing guardrail is set behind the existing drilled shaft walls 
along the roadway. 
We understand that relocating the guardrail further makai and towards the drilled 
shaft walls is desired at the two drilled shaft wall locations. In addition, the new guardrail will 
be located within the existing concrete cap of the drilled shaft retaining walls.

Existing Site Conditions 
The guardrail improvements project is located along the makai side of Kaneohe 
Bay Drive between Aumoana Place and Aina Moi Place in the Kaneohe area on the Island 
of Oahu, Hawaii. Kaneohe Bay Drive is a two-lane roadway with one lane of traffic in each 
direction. 
Previously, two downslope sections of Kaneohe Bay Drive were remediated using 
drilled shaft retaining walls. The proposed guardrail improvements are located at the 
drilled shaft wall locations and are shown on the Site Plan, Plate 2.

At the time of our field reconnaissance, the asphaltic concrete (AC) pavement 
within the project limits appeared to be in good condition. No signs of movement or slope 
instability (e.g. longitudinal cracks, vertical ground subsidence, leaning utility poles) were 
observed within the project limits. The existing guardrail is located immediately adjacent 
to Kaneohe Bay Drive behind a small AC curb. Behind the guardrail, the existing ground 
surface slopes down to the top of the drilled shaft retaining wall at a slope inclination of 
about two horizontal to one vertical (2H:1V). The backfilled slopes were generally lightly 
vegetated with short land grass. However, one relatively short section exposed bare soil 
that showed signs of mild erosion. Photographs of the project site are shown on Plates 4.1 
through 4.3. 
Based on the available topographic information, the existing ground elevations along 
the roadway vary from about +30 feet Mean Sea Level (MSL) to +34 feet MSL at the project 
site. 
Subsurface Conditions 
Based on our previous field exploration, the project site is generally underlain by a 
surface fill layer ranging in thickness from about 10 to 20 feet. The fill layer consisted of 
about 6 to 8 inches of asphaltic concrete, medium dense to dense sandy gravel, soft to 
stiff clay with gravel, and boulders. The surface fill layer was underlain by alluvium 
extending to the maximum drilled depth of 26.5 feet. The alluvium was comprised of 
medium stiff to very stiff silty clay and clay with sand, gravel, and cobbles.  
We encountered groundwater in two of the drilled borings at depths of about 
12.5 and 21 feet below the existing ground surface at the time of our field exploration. 
These groundwater depths correspond to elevations of approximately +19.5 and +11 feet 
MSL, respectively. It should be noted that groundwater levels may vary with tidal 
fluctuations, seasonal rainfall, time of year, and other factors. 
The previous boring locations are shown on the Site Plan, Plate 2. The logs of 
borings from our previous geotechnical engineering exploration are provided in 
Appendix A. 
Drilled Shaft Wall Foundation 
Based on the project plans for the Kaneohe Bay Drive Roadway Improvements 
project, the drilled shafts at Wall Nos. 1 and 2 range in lengths from about 15 to 25 feet 
and are generally spaced at 4 feet on-center, except near the existing box culvert passing 
through Wall No. 1. The two drilled shafts adjacent to the box culvert are spaced at about 
10 feet on-center.  
For the proposed guardrail improvements project, we understand that the existing 
guardrail will be relocated to bear directly on Wall Nos. 1 and 2. In addition, the pile cap 
thickness will be increased by 4 feet for a total cap height of about 7 feet.

The structural loading at the top of the shaft cap provided consists of a Strength 
Limit State dead load of 9.5 kips and Extreme Event Limit State loading consisting of a 
dead load of 6.5 kips, lateral load of 7 kips per foot over 4 feet of wall, and an applied 
moment of 14 foot-kips per foot over 4 feet of wall. 
For our analyses, we have assumed a level backfill condition between the top of 
the modified pile cap and the existing pavement surface of Kaneohe Bay Drive. To reduce 
the lateral earth pressures on the extended pile cap, we recommend backfilling against 
the extended pile cap using Controlled Low Strength Material (CLSM). A conceptual detail 
of our pile cap backfill recommendations is presented on the Typical Drilled Shaft 
Retaining Wall Pile Cap Backfill Detail, Plate 3.
