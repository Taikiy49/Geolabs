2 1 Regional Geology
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lavas from
two shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated
to be middle to late Pliocene in age and Koolau Volcano is estimated to be late
Pliocene to early Pleistocene in age After a long period of volcanic inactivity during
which time erosion incised deep valleys into the Koolau Shield volcanic activity
returned with a series of lava flows followed by cinder and tuff cone formations These
series are referred to as the Honolulu Volcanic Series
The geologic conditions underlying the site are the result of a complex series of
depositional and erosional events due to glacio eustatic sea level changes and
post erosional volcanic activities during the Pleistocene Epoch The Koolau Volcanic
Shield was built during the early Pleistocene Epoch by thinly bedded basaltic lava flows
Following the cessation of activity of the Koolau Volcano alluvial soil materials
were deposited in an estuarine and deltaic environment in the area of Pearl Harbor
The sea level fluctuated several times receding perhaps 90 meters below and rising
about 30 meters above the present sea level Concurrent with a rise in sea level during
the late Pleistocene Epoch eruption of the Salt Lake Makalapa member of the Honolulu
Volcanic Series mantled the older alluvium with thick deposits of volcanic ash which
consolidated to form volcanic tuff locally called mudrock layers Subsequent erosion
dissected and thinned those mantles of tuff
During the Recent Epoch as the sea level rose to its current stand the area was
covered with additional sedimenis consisting primarily of soft and loose lagoonal marine
deposits Land development and reclamation projects in the past 40 to 60 years have
added man made fills bringing the site to its present configuration

Earthquakes and Seismicity
In general earthquakes that occur throughout the world are caused solely by shifts
in the tectonic plates In contrast earthquake activity in Hawaii is linked primarily to
volcanic activity Therefore earthquake activity in Hawaii generally occurs before
or during volcanic eruptions In addition earthquakes may result from the
underground movement of magma that comes close to the surface but does not
erupt The Island of Hawaii experiences thousands of earthquakes each year but
most of the earthquakes are so small that they can only be detected by

instruments However some of the earthquakes are strong enough to be felt and
a few cause minor to moderate damage
In general earthquakes associated with volcanic activity are most common on the
Island of Hawaii Earthquakes that are directly associated with the movement of
magma are concentrated beneath the active Kilauea and Mauna Loa Volcanoes on
the Island of Hawaii Because the majority of the earthquakes in Hawaii over
90 percent of earthquakes are related to volcanic activity the risk of seismic
activity and degree of ground shaking diminishes with increased distance from the
Island of Hawaii The Island of Hawaii has experienced numerous earihquakes
greater than Magnitude 5 M5 however earthquakes are not confined only to the
Island of Hawaii
To a lesser degree the Island of Maui has experienced numerous earthquakes
greater than Magnitude 5 Therefore moderate to strong earthquakes have
occurred in the County of Maui The effects of earthquakes occurring on the
Islands of Hawaii and Maui may be felt on the Island of Oahu For example
several small landslides occurred on the Island of Oahu as a result of the Maui
Earthquake of In addition some houses on the Island of Oahu were
reportedly damaged as a result of the Lanai Earthquake of 1871 M7
Over the last 150 years of recorded history we are not aware of reported
earthquakes greater than Magnitude 6 occurring on the Island of Oahu We
understand that an earthquake of magnitude 4 8 to 5 0 occurred along the Diamond
Head Fault in 1948 on the Island of Oahu The moderate tremor resulted in broken
store windows ruptured building walls and broken underground water mains

Laboratory Testing
Moisture Content ASTM D 2216 and Unit Weight ASTM D 2937
determinations were performed on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification
and evaluation of soil properties The results of these tests are presented on the Logs
of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
One Direct Shear test ASTM D 3080 was performed on a selected sample of
the soils to evaluate the shear strength characteristics Results of the direct shear test
are presented on Plate B 1
One laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR test ASTM D 1883 was performed
on the near surface soils to evaluate the pavement support characteristics of the soils
Results of the test are presented on Plate B 2
One Modified Proctor compaction test ASTM D 1557 was performed on the
near surface soils to determine the dry density and moisture content relationship Results
of the test are presented on Plate B 3
