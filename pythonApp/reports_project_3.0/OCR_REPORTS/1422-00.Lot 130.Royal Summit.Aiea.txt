In accordance with your request we have made a study of the near
surface soil conditions at the above referenced lot in order to
provide foundation recommendations for the proposed residence The
study consisted of drilling three 3 shallow borings performing
laboratory testing and reviewing the subdivision site grading and
preliminary house foundation plans This report summarizes our
findings and recommendations
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
Based on the preliminary house plans the proposed residence will be a
two story structure approximately 40 by 64 feet with an adjoining
two car garage about 20 by 20 feet in plan dimensions

Concrete slab on grade is planned for the driveway garage the entire
basement level and portions of the main floor level of the house The
house will be of wood frame construction with CMU walls planned for
the lower level
To accommodate the basement level CMU retaining walls generally less
than 6 feet high are planned under the front portion of the main floor
level for grade separation
SITE CONDITIONS
The subject lot is on the southern side of Aloalii Street in the Royal
Summit Phase 1 Subdivision located in Aiea Oahu Hawaii
A relatively level pad about 30 feet wide is situated along the front
portion of the site The ground surface then slopes downward at about
5 1 H V to a second level pad near the middle portion of the lot
At the back of the second pad is an existing CRM retaining wall about
10 5 feet high dropping to a third level pad which is also surrounded
by a CRM retaining wall about 10 5 feet high
During our field investigation a stockpile of rubbish and unsuitable
soil were observed along the northwest corner of the site adjacent to
the existing sidewalk

SUBSURFACE SOIL CONDITIONS
Three borings were drilled within Lot 130 to depths ranging from 5 5
to 10 75 feet below the existing grade at the approximate locations
shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
In general the borings encountered very stiff reddish brown clayey
silt overlying dense highly weathered basalt rock to the full depth of
the borings A review of the mass grading plan for Royal Summit
Phase 1 dated prepared by Community Planning Inc
indicated that lot 130 received up to 8 feet of compacted fills over
the natural grade at the middle portion of the lot and cuts up to 6
feet deep at the front of the site
No groundwater was encountered at the time of our field exploration

General
The stiff reddish brown clayey silt soil encountered generally can
provide adequate foundation support for the proposed house provided
that some care is exercised during construction to minimize the
swelling potential of the soil
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Page 4
Laboratory tests performed on the stiff on site clayey silt soil
indicates that this material generally has low to medium swelling
potential Therefore to minimize effects due to soil swelling under
on grade slabs we recommend that the on site soil be capped with at
least 12 inches of non expansive select fill and that the subgrade
soils underneath the select fill and slab be moistened and protected
from drying from the start of construction until the slab concrete
placement
Backfills up to 4 feet high are planned for the southeast corner of
the site to accommodate the sidewalk and garden planter Select
noh expansive soils should be used as backfill to minimize the
swelling potential of the natural soil against the basement retaining
wall
House Foundations
Spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the proposed
residence An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s may be used on
the on site soil or compacted fill
Individual footings on level areas should be embedded a minimum of 12
inches below the adjacent finished grade Continuous wall footings
should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the adjacent finished
grade If located on sloping area the footing should be embedded
down an additional amount to obtain a 5 foot minimum horizontal

the slope face This additional embedment depth is required to help

The bottom of ail foundation excavations should be cleaned of loose

naan
The laboratory tests indicate that the near surface soils have low
swelling potential if maintained near the optimum moisture content
However if allowed to dry out the soils may exhibit moderate
swelling potential Therefore the slab subgrade must be kept moist
after excavation and protected from drying during the following days
required for preparation prior to final concrete placement
In order to minimize the swelling potential of the soil we recommend
that a minimum of 12 inches of non expansive soil such as crusher
waste be placed below the slab and cushion layer

It is important that the soils engineer should inspect the above
subgrade preparation and check the moisture contents of the underlying
soils prior to pouring of the slab and perimeter footing
The concreting of footing and slab should be coordinated in such a way
that the time period between the initial excavation and final concrete
placement at each lot should be kept to a minimum in order to prevent
excessive subgrade drying
We recommend that 12 inch deep perimeter footings be used measured
from the lowest adjacent grade to act as a cut off wall against the
future subgrade moisture changes due to drying and wetting around the
slab
We further recommend that a 4 inch layer of 3B Fine gravel cushion
ill be provided below the slab In order to protect against moisture
infiltration an impervious moisture barrier may be incorporated below
the slab The on grade slabs should be structurally separated from
wall foundations if any
Retaining Walls
Short CMU retaining walls generally less than 6 feet high are
planned at the front of the proposed house to accommodate the basement
level The following guidelines for retaining wall design are
recommended

Drainage
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance
of the above recommended foundations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of
strength The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled
tight against the wall or slab footing immediately after setting of
the concrete to prevent ponding condition

Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and maintained
to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing
foundations
Design Review
Final plans and specifications for foundations site grading and
utility construction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for
review and comments prior to construction to see that the intent of
our recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the lot grading footing excavations and
backfill operations be observed by the soils engineer during
construction to see that adequate bearing has been obtained and that
the subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of our
investigation
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the above
inspections
