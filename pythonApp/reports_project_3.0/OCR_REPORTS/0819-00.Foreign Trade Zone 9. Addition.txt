INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this investigation was to evaluate the sub
surface conditions at the site to provide recommendations
for the design and construction of the foundations of the
proposed structure
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located on Pier 2 on the southeastern
side of Honolulu Harbor
At this Pier 2 there is an existing warehouse structure
approximately 285 by 1280 feet in plan The warehouse
is a pile supported steel framed structure with concrete
walls There is an existing two story office structure
about 25 by 170 feet in plan in the central portion of the
existing warehouse The warehouse has an asphaltic concrete
floor and is used for the storage of shipping containers
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
The proposed office structure will be located in the middle
of the existing warehouse against the southeastern wall
It will be adjacent to the existing two story office struc
ture
The new steel framed structure will consist of a new second
floor above the existing A C pavement area and will be about
110 by 135 feet in plan dimension
Maximum column loads up to 72 kips are anticipated

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The project site is located along Honolulu Harbor and has
experienced a complex geologic history as a result of the
Pleistocene sea level changes
A coral reef platform was encountered at about 25 to 30
foot depths indicating a wave cut terrace formed when the
sea level was at a lower stage
Back reef or lagoonal deposits consisting of sands silts
and clays are located above the coral reef structure indi
cating a higher sea level and a subsequent depositing en
vironment
Finally above the present water level is a man made fill
placed in recent times
Subsurface Exploration
Four borings were drilled at the site to depths of 26 5
to 51 5 feet below the existing ground surface to explore
the subsurface conditions and to obtain samples for labora
tory testing The approximate locations of the borings
are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2

Soil Conditions
Based on the borings the site appears to be underlain by
a fill layer about 5 to 8 feet thick consisting of medium
to dense silty sand and gravel Loose to medium dense silty
and clayey sand was encountered below this to about 20 foot
depths Loose gray clay and coral was encountered from
about 20 to 30 foot depths over a medium to dense coral
reefrock formation
Water was encountered in the borings at 6 5 to 7 foot depths
during the field exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Foundation
The field exploration disclosed a fairly competent surface
fill layer over loose to medium sandy subsoils
Spread or continuous footing foundations bearing on the
surface fill layer are recommended for the proposed struc
ture
GEOLABS HAWAII
D A G S JOB NO P 5
The borings disclosed that the fill layer varies from
medium dense to dense To create a more uniform bearing
support and to reduce differential settlement we recommend
that the footing excavations be overexcavated 2 feet and
recompacted in 8 inch lifts to at least 95 of its maximum
dry density established by ASTM Test Method D 1557
The bottoms of the footings should be placed about 2 feet
below the finish grade
An allowable bearing value of 2 000 p s f is recommended
for the footings bearing on the recompacted sandy fill
layer
For the above recommendations we estimate that settlements
The new structure should be separated from the existing
structures The column to footing connection should be
detailed to allow for some jacking if it is required in
the future
Concrete Slab
The new first floor concrete slab can be placed over the
existing asphaltic concrete pavement

APPENDIX A
FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored
by drilling 4 borings to depths of 26 5 to 51 5 feet below
the existing ground surface The borings were drilled by
P R Drilling Company The approximate locations of the
borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The soils encountered in the borings were classified
by visual and textural examination in the field by our
engineer who continuously monitored the drilling operations
The classifications were reviewed by visual inspection and
testing in the laboratory The soils were classified in
general conformance with the Unified Soil Classification
System A graphic presentation of the soils is presented
on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 6
In the borings soil samples were obtained by driving
split barrel sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30
inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the last
12 inches are shown on the boring logs at the appropriate
depths
