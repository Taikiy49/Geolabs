PROJECT CONSIDERATION
The project site is located in the vicinity of the intersection of Lualualei Homestead
Road and Puhawai Road in Waianae on the Island of Oahu Hawaii Four boreholes were
drilled to depths of approximately 20 7 to 22 feet below the existing ground surface using a
truck mounted drill rig In accordance with the request of
falling head percolation test was performed in each of the four boreholes
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions at the site to develop a soil rock data set for the formulation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertaining to the proposed aerobic system for the project
To accomplish this we conducted an exploration program consisting of the following tasks
and work efforts
1 Mobilization and demobilization of a truck mounted drill rig and
two operators to and from the project site
2 Drilling and sampling of four boreholes to depths of approximately 20 7 to
22 feet below the existing ground surface at locations designated by the

FIELD EXPLOATION
Four borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 4 were drilled at selected
locations at the project site The borings were drilled to depths of about 20 7 to 22 feet
below the existing ground surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2 The borings were drilled using a truck mounted drill rig equipped with auger
equipment
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on Plate 3 Graphic representations of the materials
encountered are provided on the Logs of Borings Plates 4 through 7
Samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by
driving a 2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same hammer and drop The
blow counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an 18 inch drive
are shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Boring at the appropriate
sample depths

SUBSURFACE CONDITION
In general the borings encountered various alluvial materials consisting of clay silt
sand and gravel extending to the maximum depths explored of about 20 7 to 22 feet
below the existing ground surface
At the time of our field exploration groundwater was not encountered in the borings
drilled at the project site However it should be noted that groundwater levels may vary
significantly depending on seasonal precipitation groundwater withdrawal and other
factors
FIELD PERCOLATION TESTS
Four field percolation tests were performed in four boreholes drilled at the project
site It was originally proposed by Geolabs that the percolation tests would be performed
in conformance with the State of Hawaii Department of Health s Administrative Rules
However in accordance with the request of four falling head
percolation tests were performed instead in the boreholes
Falling Head Permeability Tests
Field percolation tests were conducted in four boreholes at the project site to
evaluate the infiltration characteristics of the subsurface materials encountered at the four
locations The percolation tests were performed in Boring Nos 1 through 4 as shown on
the Site Plan Plate 2
The percolation tests were performed at depths of about 6 7 to 20 3 feet below the
existing ground surface Falling head percolation tests were performed to determine the
average hydraulic conductivity of the underlying subsurface materials Based on our field
exploration the subsurface materials encountered at the depths where the effluent
disposal is planned consists of alluvial deposits The results of our percolation tests are
presented on Plates 8 through 11
