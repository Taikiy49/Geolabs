Kapolei Oahu Hawaii
In accordance with our proposal dated we have performed
laboratory testing on an imported sample for the determination of its suitability as a
non expansive select granular capping fill material under the building slabs at the
above referenced project
The sample tested consisted of excavated mudrock generated from the Salt Lake
Boulevard Widening project The material was imported to the project site crushed and
screened to 3 inch minus and stockpiled for future use Laboratory CBR test sieve
analysis and Proctor tests were performed on a sample taken from the stockpile and the
test results are presented on Plate 1 Based on the test results it appears that the
sample tested meet the project specifications and may be used as the non expansive
capping fill layer under the building slabs
During the placement of the mudrock material at Building C it was observed that
the compaction effort of the roller has further broken down the mudrock aggregates and
exposed the embedded clay particles Based on this field observation another sample
was taken from a compacted lift of the mudrock material to further evaluate its suitability
as a non expansive select granular fill material Results of the additional tests are also
presented on Plate 1

Building C may be left in place however the material should be moisture conditioned and
