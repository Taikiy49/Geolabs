PROJECT DESCRIPTIONS
It is proposed to construct a new communication antenna on the summit
of Moanuiahea Cinder Cone on the island of Hawaii
The facility includes a 60 foot high three legged antenna It is
our understanding that the tower will be designed for the following base
reactions
Moment 112 0 Kip Foot
Maximum Shear 3 3 Kips Total
Maximum Compression 19 0 Kips Per Leg
Maximum Uplift 17 4 Kips Per Leg
SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is situated on the summit of Moanuiahea Cinder Cone
located on the western Slopes of Hualalai Volcano on the island of
Hawaii The site measures 10 by 20 feet in plan and slopes gently to
south and west Presently the site is used as pasture land and is
covered with grass Several antennas and two small buildings are located
approximately 40 feet north of the site

Excavation and Backfilling
Excavation and backfilling would be required for the construction of
the tower foundations The bottom of the excavations should be thoroughly
tamped prior to the placement of concrete
Based on our field exploration special excavating procedures such
as hoeram may be required to excavate down to the underlying basalt The
actual rippability of the material should be determined by the contractor
on site
Excavated on site cinder sand free of any organic and other
expansive component may be used as a source of fill material If
required all imported fill material should consist of selected
non expansive silty sandy or granular materials less than 3 inches in
size with less than 20 passing the 200 sieve All backfill materials
should be placed in maximum of 8 inch horizontal loose lifts and compacted
to 90 of the maximum density as established by the ASTM D 1557 78 test
method
The soil around the footings should be properly compacted in order to
produce the recommended passive soil pressure resistance
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comment
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earth work and foundation recommendations given herein If this
review is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility
for misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Observation

provide geotechnical engineering services during the excavation and
construction phases of the foundation work This is to observe compliance

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as borings
probes test pits and or observation wells Variations of conditions
between the field data points may occur and the nature and extent of
these variations may not become evident until construction If variations
then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations given in this report
The location of the field data points were determined by tape
measurement on the site with reference to the preliminary site plan
provided The physical location of the field data points should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross section are based upon interpolation between field data
points and may not represent actual subsurface conditions

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
A SA OR NS A se
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling one
1 boring to depth of 15 0 feet below the existing ground surface at the
approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 A truck mounted
rotary drill rig was used to drill the boring
The soil and rock encountered in the boring were classified by
visual and textural examination in the field by our geologist who
continuously monitored the drilling operations The classifications were
further reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory The
soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A graphic presentation of the materials
encountered is presented on the Boring Log Plate A 1
Soil samples were obtained from the boring by driving a 2 inch
standard split spoon sampler with a 140 pound hammer free falling 30
inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the last 12 inches are shown
on the Boring Log at the appropriate sample depths
