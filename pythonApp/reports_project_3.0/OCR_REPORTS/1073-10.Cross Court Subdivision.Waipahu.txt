COMPACTION REPORT
CROSS COURT SUBDIVISION
From August 21 1986 to we had observed the
earthwork and grading operations performed at the above referenced site
This report summarizes our observations and compaction test results
The grading operations at the above referenced lots generally
consisted of cuts and fills less than 2 feet though one small area
received up to 6 feet of fill During the fill placement operations
periodic field density tests were done in accordance with the American
Society for Testing and Materials ASTM Test Designation D 1556 Sand
Cone Method
The compaction test results are summarized at the end of the
report Based on the compaction tests and visual observations it is our
opinion that the fill lots have been compacted adequately

RECOMMENDATIONS
House Foundations
For the light residential structures spread footing foundations or
Slab on grade may be used To minimize the effects of slope creep and
erosion slabs should be set back a minimum distance of 5 feet away from
the tops of slopes Also post footings located close to the tops of
Slopes should be embedded a sufficient depth to obtain a minimm
horizontal set back distance of 5 feet measured from the outer edge of
footing to the slope face
For the light loads anticipated an allowable bearing pressure of
2500 p s f can be used for footings resting on very stiff in situ soil
or compacted fill
If a footing is located next to a utility trench it should extend
to the bottom of the utility trench or the trench backfill should be well
compacted to reduce the footing settlement due to settlement of the
backfill

Slab on grade should be placed over a 4 inch layer of compacted base
course The thickened edge around the Slab on grade should extend a

To protect against moisture damage of the flooring material a

Soil Engineering Services
We recommend that the soils engineer should observe the subgrade
preparation backfill placement and recompaction and footing excavations
prior to concrete placement
Site Regrading
Subsequent to completion of lot grading utility trenches within the
let pad should be properly backfilled and compacted under the observation
of a soils engineer
This office assumes no responsibility for any alterations made to
slopes or pads on the subject lots subsequent to the issuance of this
report without our knowledge and written approval

of this subdivision Homeowners should also be advised not to block or
