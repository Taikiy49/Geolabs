As requested we have performed a soil and foundation investigation
at the above referenced site to evaluate the subsurface soil conditions
in order to provide recommendation for the foundation design of the
proposed structure
The investigation which was performed in accordance with our
proposal dated consisted of drilling and sampling
four 4 borings at the proposed building site field reconnaissance
laboratory soil testing engineering analysis and report preparation
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is situated on the northwestern side of Iwaena
Street in the Halawa Industrial Subdivision The general location of
the project site and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map
Plate 1
The lot extends from Iwaena Street approximately 205 feet toward
the hillside The ground surface slopes upward from Iwaena Street
approximately 4 feet to a relatively level pad area which covers about
three fourths of the lot The ground surface in the rear portion of the
lot rises upward toward the hillside at about a 2 1 H V slope ratio
A concrete lined interceptor ditch is situated just beyond the rear
boundary of the lot beyond which the ground then slopes steeply up the
hillside
The ground elevations range from 116 to 148 feet above MSL
During the time of our exploration the lot was covered with grass
and haole koa and stockpiles of soil
There is an existing warehouse structure on the adjacent lot to the
north A warehouse building is presently under construction on the
lower adjacent lot to the south This new structure will have a
finished floor elevation approximately 5 to 10 feet lower than the
existing ground surface on the subject lot A vertical cut has been
made for construction of a retaining wall at the common boundary of the
lots

PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
The proposed warehouse will be trapezoidal in shape with widths of
89 and 111 feet extending to the lot boundaries on both sides and a
length of 197 feet

A loading dock area at elevation 120 feet is planned on the northern

The structure will utilize tilt up precast concrete walls
Retaining and basement walls will be required around the building

Concrete floor slab is planned for most of the warehouse
Asphaltic concrete pavement is planned for the driveway parking and
loading dock area

SOIL CONDITIONS
Four borings were drilled at the approximate locations shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2 Based on the borings and exposed cuts the soil
conditions at the site can be generalized as
1 Dark brown clay adobe with gravel 2 to 10 feet thick
averaging about 4 feet over
2 mottled brown with tan silty clay with cobbles and boulders
boulder alluvium
The near surface dark brown clay soils adobe are highly expansive
and unsuitable for slab subgrade support The expansive clay appears to
be thickest at the northeastern property corner and thins out toward the
rear hillside portion of the lot
The underlying boulder alluvium is dense and can provide competent
bearing support for structures However swell tests on the fines in
the alluvium indicate moderate to high swelling potential Therefore
the overall swelling potential of the alluvium fines and boulders may
be classified as moderate
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings
For more detailed descriptions of the soil conditions encountered
refer to the boring logs in the appendix

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that the site is underlain by a
surface layer of highly expansive adobe clay Where encountered the
expansive clay should be over excavated a minimum of 3 feet below slabs
and foundations and then backfilled with compacted non expansive soil
to minimize shrinking and swelling movements of the soil The
underlying boulder alluvium is moderately expansive and should be kept a
winimum of 18 inches below slabs and foundations
Due to the various finished floor elevations planned it is
anticipated that the depth of over excavation and replacement for the
expansive clay and boulder colluvium will vary within the building
At the proposed basement area cuts of about 5 feet are planned
Based on Boring 3 drilled in this area most of the surface clay will
probably be removed by the required cutting exposing the underlying
alluvium soil
Behind the basement and loading dock area fills up to 5 feet thick
are planned Where a minimum of 3 feet of select fill is planned
over excavation of the clay is not required
The generalized soil profile and proposed finished grades are
illustrated on Plate 3

Foundations
Spread or continuous footing foundations may be used for the
warehouse structure The footings can bear on the 3 foot thick
compacted select backfill material as discussed earlier or they may be
buried a minimum of 18 inches into the boulder alluvium
An allowable bearing value of 3000 p s f may be used on compacted
structural fill or the stiff on site soils
The footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the
finished grade
The northern wall of the structure will be close to the foundation
wall for the adjacent existing building In order to avoid additional
load on the existing footings the new footings should be situated at
the same level as the existing footings
Loading Dock Wall and Retaining Wall
A retaining wall generally less than 5 feet high is planned along
the northeastern frontage of the building to provide the necessary grade
separation for a loading dock Retaining walls up to 17 feet high are
planned around the warehouse structure
An allowable bearing value up to 4000 p s f may be used for the
wall footings bearing on the dense underlying boulder alluvium which is
anticipated at the basement and rear retaining wall locations
GEOLABS HAWAII
An allowable bearing value of 3000 p s f may be used for the wall
footings bearing on compacted fill
Well graded granular material such as select borrow sand or
equivalent should be used as backfill material directly behind the
walls This would minimize the compactive effort required for the
backfill and reduce the build up of additional lateral pressures on the
walls
We recommend that the walls be designed to resist lateral earth
pressures as follows
Wall Unrestrained at top kh 35 p c f equivalent
active condition fluid pressure
Wall Restrained at top kh 55 p c f equivalent
at rest condition fluid pressure
Additional loads due to surcharge or traffic should be included
where applicable
A friction factor of 0 4 between concrete and soil can be utilized
to determine the sliding resistance of the wall
Additionally a passive soil resistance of 300 p c f can be used
A subsurface drainage system should be used to prevent the build up
of hydrostatic pressure behind the walls

Site Grading
Prior to placing any fill the site should be cleared of all
vegetation existing stockpiles and deleterious materials Any soft or
weak areas disclosed during clearing operation should be removed and
properly repaired by backfilling with well compacted engineered fill
Fills and backfills should be select non expansive granular
material generally less than 6 inches in size placed in maximum 6 inch
loose lifts and compacted to 95 of its maximum dry density established
by ASTM D 1557 78 Quarry material such as select borrow or crusher
waste may be used It is critical that the on site expansive clay soil
not be used as fill material below structures and behind retaining
walls We recommend all expansive soils be hauled off site prior to the
importation of new select or granular backfill materials in order to
avoid potential contamination problem
The site should be graded to drain water away from building
foundations

Transportation
For office areas a 4 inch thick concrete slabs over 4 inches of
base course rock may be used
We recommend that the subgrade soil be kept moist prior to
placement of base course and concrete

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during
earthwork construction to monitor the contractor s grading operations
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be observed by
the soils engineer during construction to see that adequate soil
bearing strata have been obtained and that the subsurface conditions
are consistent with the findings of our investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
