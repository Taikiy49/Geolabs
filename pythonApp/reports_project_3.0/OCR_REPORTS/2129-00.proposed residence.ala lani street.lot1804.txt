SUMMARY OF RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field exploration the findings
generally indicate that the proposed home site is
underlain by competent basalt formation capped by
a thin layer of expansive clay soil
It is our opinion that the clay soil is not
suitable for foundation support However the
underlying basalt which was encountered at a
shallow depth can provide adequate bearing for
structural loads
Based on encountering the underlying basalt
formation at the shallow depth it is our opinion
that the subject lot appears to he generally
stable and not subject to deep seated mass
movements

exploration performed for the subject project

site slopes down toward Ala Lani Street at a ratio of about 3H 1V

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions were explored by drilling and sampling one
1 boring to a depth of about 16 0 feet below the existing ground surface
at the approximate location shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
Based upon the boring data the findings generally indicate that the
subsurface conditions at the project site consist of a thin about 1 foot
upper layer of boulders mixed with moderately to highly expansive stiff to
very stiff silty clay Beneath the clay is hard weathered basalt
Groundwater was not encountered in the drilled boring during our
field exploration However groundwater levels or seepage may occur
during and after periods of heavy rainfall
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on our field exploration results it is our opinion from a
soil engineering point of view that the subsurface conditions at the site
can provide adequate foundation support for a single family residence
The subsurface exploration revealed that most of the project site is
underlain by a very thin layer of expansive silty clay Beneath this
layer is the weathered basalt formation
It is our opinion that the clay should be removed and that the
weathered basalt formation beneath the expansive clay layer be used to
support the building loads
Foundation
Spread footing foundations may be used on this site to support the
Single family house provided that the site grading and foundation support
procedures recommended below are followed

After the existing on site clay is removed the footing foundations
may be supported by weathered basalt formation
An allowable bearing value of 4000 p s f may be used for footings
bearing on weathered basalt The allowable bearing value may be increased
by one third for temporary wind and seismic loads
A coefficient of friction of 0 4 can be utilized to determine sliding
resistance of the structure and foundation
House footings should be embedded a minimm of about 12 inches below
the lowest adjacent finished grade on level areas away from slope faces
Retaining wall footings should be embedded a minimm of 18 inches
Footings located on or near the top of slopes should be embedded a
sufficient depth to obtain a minimm 5 foot set back distance measured
horizontally out to the nearest adjacent slope face
Estimated footing settlements under the design loading are minimal
Site Grading
It is our understanding that most of the site grading will be cut and
will be limited to the house and retaining wall area
The on site clay is not suitable for use as fill or backfill
Therefore imported material should be used
Prior to the commencement of earthwork the area within the grading
limits should be cleared of all vegetation debris previous fill clay
and other deleterious materials This cleared and grubbed material should
be disposed of properly off site

Fills and backfills should consist of non expansive select material
less than 3 inches in maximum size and with less than 20 passing the 200
sieve The fills and backfills should be placed in horizontal lifts not
exceeding eight 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a dry
density of at least 90 of the maximm established by ASTM D 1557 78
Any soft spots or soft material encountered within the
grading construction limits should be removed to competent material and
replaced with compacted fill
Fills placed where the existing ground is steeper than 5 horizontal
to 1 vertical should be keyed and benched into the existing firm ground to
properly bond the new fill to the slope This detail is essential for the
stability of the fills particularly to reduce the potential of sliding
along the fill in situ ground contact The filling operation should start
at the lowest point and continue up in level compacted layers

constructed to drain water away from the wall

Driveway Pavement
Based on our field exploration and previous experience the following
preliminary pavement section is recommended for expansive clay subgrade
condition
2 0 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 0 Inch Base Course 95 Compaction
18 0 Inch Select Borrow
26 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
The select borrow may be eliminated if the top 2 feet below the
subgrade is non expansive material or in situ basalt formation
Additional CBR tests and or visual observations should be performed
on the actual subgrade soil samples during construction and the above
design section can be revised if necessary The recommended section
considers light traffic and that good drainage will be provided adjacent
to paved areas
Boulder Protection
Upslope lots situated along valley side slopes may be subject to
boulders rolling down the hillside
Rock falls are general phenomena occurring on all ridge slopes in
Oahu Various methods of boulder barriers or catchments may be used to
reduce the hazard of falling boulders For this site some of the methods
which may be used to reduce the boulder hazard are as follows
1 A boulder sweep along the upper boundaries of the site could be
performed to remove the loose boulders that appear likely to roll
down
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2129 00 Page 8
dk 500
2 A boulder catchment area with a fence or wall could be utilized
to catch and arrest the rolling boulders Interceptor ditches
along the upper boundaries of the site could be utilized for this
purpose
3 Natural trees and vegetation along the upper portions of the site
should be retained to act as a natural barrier against the
boulders and to reduce erosion
4 Where practicable the house should be set back away from the toe
of the hillside slope to create an additional boulder catchment
area
It should be understood that whatever method are used an inherent
risk of falling boulders could still exist for these hillside lots
The state of the art of soil engineering practice has not advanced to
a point that a solution is available for this area It is our opinion
that no matter what one does there is always the risk of boulders rolling
down the hill if not from areas near the house then from areas higher up
the slope Whatever measure is chosen it only reduces the risk It
should be pointed out that no guarantee against future falling boulders
can be made due to changes in soil and rock conditions with time
weathering erosion earthquake construction vibration and improvements
and construction activities at and around the site
Natural Hillside Stability
Based on the shallow depth of the underlying basalt formation it is
our opinion that the subject lot is generally stable and not subject to
deep seated mass movements

Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comment
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this review
is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for
misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Observation
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during the site grading and
foundation construction phase of the work This is to observe compliance
with the design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow
design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from that
anticipated prior to the start of construction The recommendations given
in this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual rock soil conditions encountered during construction are
different from those assumed or considered in this report then
appropriate modifications to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as
borings Variations of conditions between the field data points may
occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become
evident until construction If variations then appear evident it will be
necessary to re evaluate the recommendations given in this report

The locations of the field data points were determined by taping from
the property boundaries as indicated on the plot plan for the project
provided by the owner client
Elevations of the data points were determined by interpolation from
elevation points on the plan The physical locations and elevations of
the field data points should be considered accurate only to the degree
implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
any profiles or cross sections are based upon interpolation between field
data points and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
Water level data from the field data points were collected at times
given on the graphic representations and or in the text of this report
These data have been reviewed and interpretations made in the formulation
of this report However it must be noted that fluctuation in water level
may occur due to variations in rainfall tides temperature and other
factors
