In general accordance with the scope of work in our proposal dated
we have performed the field exploration for a separate
parking structure newly proposed for the subject highrise apartment
building
It is our understanding that the new parking structure sited
southeast of the proposed apartment building will be a one story concrete
deck approximately 55 feet by 130 feet in plan
The purpose of this exploration was to delineate at the location of
the additional structure the depth of basalt formation which was
encountered at relatively shallow depth below the main apartment building
during our previous exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by means of three
3 probings to depths of about 12 5 to 19 0 feet below the existing
ground surface The Probing Logs are attached and the approximate probing
locations are shown on the attached Site Plan

In general the probings encountered refusal at approximately 12 5 to
14 0 feet below the existing ground surface Probing No 1 was also
subsequently cored approximately five 5 feet into the underlying rock
formation when refusal was encountered The quality of the rock core
indicates that the basalt formation is generally hard but severely jointed
at the upper 3 to 5 feet Groundwater levels were measured at
approximately 8 0 to 12 0 feet below the existing ground surface in the
probings during this field exploration
Based on the probing results it appears that hard basalt formation
is also present below the new parking deck at relatively shallow depths
Therefore we recommend that inch octagonal pile foundations be
also used to support the new parking structure An estimated pile length
of 18 feet per pile and an allowable vertical pile capacity up to 120 tons
per pile may be used for the pile foundation design

IMTTATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field probings and previous borings
at the project site Variations of subsoil conditions between the
probings and borings may occur and the nature and extent of these
variations may not become evident until construction If variations then
appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations
given in this report
The locations of the field probings were approximately determined by
taping from the existing structures and or objects indicated on a
topographic survey by Harry K Matsuo dated Elevations of
the probings were estimated from the available elevation contours and
elevation points on the project topographic survey The physical
locations and elevations of the probings should be considered accurate
only to the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
probings depict the approximate boundaries between soil types and as
such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on profiles
or cross sections are based upon interpolation between probings and may
not represent actual subsurface conditions
