PURPOSE AND SCOPE
Our previous report for the foundation and site grading design
aspects of the above referenced project had been submitted on July 22

utilizing the existing cesspools for the proposed development

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions were explored by reviewing information
obtained from our previous borings and the Grilling of one 1 additional
percolation test boring to a depth of 10 0 feet below the existing ground
surface
In general the borings encountered about 4 5 feet of fill consisting
of very stiff to hard dark brown to brow clayey and gravelly silts
underlain by medium dense to dense tan to dark brown silty sands and
gravels Below this the drilling encountered a 3 0 to 3 5 foot thick
layer of hard tannish white coral underlain by dense to very dense tannish
white silty coral gravel and sand to a depth of 16 5 feet the maximm
depth drilled
Groundwater levels were encountered at the time of our exploration
from 6 5 to 8 2 feet below the existing ground surface Due to the close
proximity of the project site to the coastline it is anticipated that
groundwater levels will fluctuate in response to tidal influences

A percolation test was performed in Boring 105 in order to evaluate

I D steel casing was inserted in the boring for the percolation test
The falling head test procedure was used for the test and the test data

From the test data the calculated average soil permeability in

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field test results it appears that the percolation rate
of the underlying soil adjacent to the existing cesspools B 105 is low
This low percolation rate may be the result of silting and clogging of the
receiving formation due to discharge from the existing cesspools
The capacities of the cesspools would be governed by the permeability
of the underlying soils and the dimensions of the cesspools By
extrapolation of the field test data our calculations indicate that a
cesspool located in the general vicinity of B 105 with a diameter of 8
feet and a depth of 12 feet could theoretically accommodate a maximm rate
of sewage effluent application of about 290 gal day with a 4 0 foot head
build up
It should be pointed out however that the loss of infiltrative
capacity and soil clogging can occur in the future by combined physical
chemical and biological factors Other factors such as rise in
groundwater level may also affect the permeability to some extent
Therefore we recommend that a suitable factor of safety should be
utilized in the design
The soil conditions encountered in the borings indicate that
relatively good percolation rates could be expected for new cesspools
installed into the sandy soils and away from the existing cesspools
