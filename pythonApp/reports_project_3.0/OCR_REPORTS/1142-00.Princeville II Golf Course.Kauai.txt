INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this investigation is to evaluate the subsurface con
ditions and to provide soil data for the site grading design of the
proposed 18 hole Princeville II Golf Course at Princeville Hanalei
Kauai Hawaii
The scope of this study includes field reconnaissance geologic
research review of available soil and geologic literature excava
tion of 16 test pits drilling of 25 shallow hand auger probes
laboratory testing analysis of field and laboratory data engin
eering analysis and report preparation
Soils investigations are not normally performed for golf course
developments However due to the many steep existing Slopes and
proposed deep fills involved in the proposed development it was
elected to investigate these areas of concern
GEOLABS HAWAIi
W O 1142 00 Page 2
This study is based on a set of grading plans prepared by Robert
dated April 1982 Our scope of work was done in
general accordance with our revised proposal dated
except that the borings for the bridge crossings of Anini Stream at
Holes 8 and 9 will be performed at a later date
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
GENERAL SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is located on the northern shore of the island of
Kauai It is currently used as pasture land for cattle
Vegetation on the site varies from low thick grasses in the open
pasture areas to thick sub tropical forest of guava mango christmas
berry hao and lauhala in the steeper canyon and drainageway areas
Topography at the project site is generally rolling hills with
localized slopes approaching 1 1 H V The proposed development
area is roughly bisected into eastern and western portions by the
Anini Stream canyon In this central canyon area localized slopes
approach vertical
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1142 00 Page 3
The site drains towards the north through Anini Stream and numerous
unnamed drainageways The western portion of the site is generally
well drained In the eastern area localized ponding of surface
water areas of seepage and springs were observed In the central
canyon and the eastern area several marshy areas along existing or
abandoned stream channels were also noted
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Grading Plans provided for this study indicate that a new 18 hole
golf course is planned on the eastern side of the existing Prince
ville Development Cuts and fills up to 20 feet deep are also
planned In some areas fills will be placed on steep existing
slopes
Cartpaths are also planned for the golf course development In
several areas these cartpaths will traverse steep existing slopes
Culvert structures will be required at several locations where
golf course improvements will cross existing drainageways and small
streams
In addition to the above two bridge crossings of Anini Stream will
be required at proposed Holes 8 and 9 These bridge crossings will
be addressed at a later date in a supplemental report

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Subsurface Exploration
Field investigations were concentrated in but not limited to areas
where proposed fills will exceed 10 feet thick and areas where steep
existing slopes will be involved in the proposed site improvements
Sixteen test pits Nos 1 through 15A were excavated at the site to
depths of 2 5 to 9 0 feet below the existing ground surface to
explore subsurface conditions and to obtain samples for laboratory
testing In addition twenty five hand auger probes were drilled to
depths of 3 5 to 9 0 feet at the site for exploration and sampling
purposes The approximate locations of the test pits and auger
probes are shown on the Site Plan Plates 1 through 3
Detailed information on the field exploration and laboratory testing
is covered in the appendices along with the test pit logs auger
probe logs and laboratory test results
Geology
The project site is situated on a broad gently sloping plateau on the
north shore of the island of Kauai This plateau was built up during
the late Pliocene and early Pleistocene epochs by the extrusion of
basaltic lava and related pyroclastics from for the Pooku vent of
the Koloa Volcanic Series Most of the on site soils are residual
derived from in situ weathering of these basic igneous rocks
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1142 00 Page 5
Anini Stream and other unnamed streams have dissected this plateau
with valleys drainageways and a canyon Along these streams
there are terraces of alluvial materials which were deposited as the
streams became graded during glacio eustatic sea level fluctuations
in the Pleistocene Also in these areas abandoned stream channels
were observed where the streams have changed course either by
natural occurrence or intentional diversion
Several springs both perennial and intermittent were observed in
the eastern portion of the site These appear to occur where the
contact between a pervious stratum and an underlying impervious rock
or ash bed daylights allowing the perched water to flow or seep at
the ground surface
Soil Conditions
General
In general our test pits and hand auger probes encountered
relatively uniform soil conditions throughout much of the site
Most of the test pits and auger probes encountered a surface
topsoil layer of stiff clayey silt to depths of 1 to 2 feet
Immediately underlying this surface layer our explorations
encountered stiff to very stiff residual clayey silts to a depth
of approximately 6 feet Below this depth the soils graded into
GEOLABS HAWAII

very stiff saprolite and highly weathered rock to the maximum
depth explored Saprolite is a soil which retains the original
structures of the parent rock but can be mechanically reduced to
a soil In most cases at the project site mechanical reworking
of the saprolite yields a fine sandy silt

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
It appears that with exception of a few areas which shall be dis
cussed in detail at a later point in this report most of the site is
underlain by relatively competent subsoils Normal site grading
techniques may be utilized for most of the proposed golf course
development
Areas to be graded should be thoroughly cleared of vegetation
debris boulders and other deleterious materials Topsoil should be
removed and stockpiled for later usage and to avoid contamination of
fill materials In areas to receive fill the ground should be
scarified to a minimum depth of 6 inches brought to near optimum
moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 85 of its maximum dry
density as established by ASTM D 1557
Since the project site is located in a high rainfall area the
on site subsoils have in place moisture content of about 19 percent
above their optimum moisture content Therefore some amount of
drying of the excavated material will be required before using them
as fill material in embankments
GEOLABS HAWAIi
W O 1142 00 Page 9
Because of the high in situ moisture contents of the on site borrow
material and the site being located in a high rainfall location it
is our opinion that the normal 90 compaction standard used in other
areas should be modified to 85 for this project Laboratory shear
strength tests on compacted samples indicate that 85 compaction will
be adequate for site grading of embankment fills anticipated on this
project
The fills should be placed in maximum 10 inch loose horizontal
lifts Sheepsfoot type compaction equipment is highly recommended
for this type of soil
The on site cut materials except for topsoil are suitable for use
in compacted fills
We anticipate a shrinkage factor between 7 and 10 when on site exca
vated material is utilized as fill compacted to 85 of its maximum
dry density It is recommended that if additional off site borrow
is required it should be tested and approved by the soils engineer
prior to being transported to the site in order to assure that it is
suitable for the proposed construction The material should gene
rally be non expansive and should not contain large percentages of
boulders organics or other deleterious materials
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1142 00 Page 10
Where the existing ground is steeper than 5 horizontal to 1 vertical
5 1 keying and benching are required to properly bond the new fill
to the slope The filling operations should start at the lowest
point and continue up in level compacted layers in accordance with
the above fill requirements For construction details refer to
Plate 4
In general one and a half horizontal to one vertical cut
slopes are recommended for the site Fill slopes of 2 1 H V are
recommended
Fill slopes should be constructed by overfilling past the design fill
slope and then cutting back
Water should be diverted away from the tops of slopes and slope
planting utilized to reduce erosion
Generally slope benches should be provided for slopes having a
vertical height greater than 20 feet
Subdrainage
Subdrains are recommended for all fills placed in existing drainage
ways or wet areas to facilitate subsurface drainage and prevent the
build up of pore water pressures below the fills
GEOLABS HAWAIi
W O 1142 00 Page 11
For most fills anticipated on this project a simple subdrain of per
forated pipe enclosed in an envelope of 3B Fine gravel along the
centerline of the existing drainageway should be sufficient The
subdrains should be 12 inches wide and about 12 inches deep consis
ting of a 4 inch diameter perforated pipe enclosed in an envelope of
3B Fine gravel for details see Plate 5
At the 10th Fairway the proposed fill will cross two drainageways
one large and one small Our field reconnaissance disclosed that
these drainageways contain several areas of seepage and intermittent
springs In this area we recommend the placement of a subdrainage
System consisting of a central subdrain with lateral subdrains on
about 30 foot centers The final subdrain locations should be
determined in the field by the soils engineer after site clearance
and the location of the laterals should be adjusted to intercept
seepage flow
At the lst Green our field reconnaissance disclosed an area of
ponded surface water in an abandoned stream channel see Site Plan
Plate 2 It is not clear as to whether this water is seepage or
ponded run off
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1142 00 Page 12
The grading plans indicate that this area is to be filled In order
to facilitate construction of the fill in this area we recommend
that the area be drained as soon as possible prior to the intended
placement of the fill to allow the area to dry out In addition to
prevent the build up of any subsurface water due to a possible seep
age condition we recommend that a subdrainage system of a 12 inch
thick granular blanket of 3B Fine gravel and perforated pipes be
incorporated beneath the fill This system should daylight at the
adjacent stream to the south
Culvert Structures
It is our understanding that nine 9 corrugated metal pipe drainage
culvert structures and two cartpath culvert stream crossings are
planned for the proposed golf course development
At the cartpath stream crossing locations we anticipate that thin
deposits of soft wet materials will be encountered To provide
adequate support for the culvert crossing structures we recommend
that the soft wet soils be removed to firm ground and replaced with
a granular cushion of 3B Fine gravel a minimum of 12 inches thick
Following the installation of this cushion the remainder of the
culvert structure should be constructed by using a sand sized
material to fill around the pipes On site soils may be used above
the pipes Provisions should be made for diverting the stream flow
during construction of the culvert crossing structures
GEOLABS HAWAIi
W O 1142 00 Page 13
At the remaining culvert structures the general embankment fill
should first be placed in the drainageway up to a level equal to the
height of the metal culvert pipe A trench should then be excavated
through the fill to the culvert invert and the pipe placed in the
trench Granular material such as sand and base course aggregate
should be used to backfill around the pipe up to about one foot above
the top of the pipe Water tamping may be used to compact the above
specified granular backfill around the lower portion of the pipe
On site material generally less than 6 inch in size may be used as
the remaining backfill which should be mechanically compacted to 85
of its maximum dry density as established by ASTM D 1557 The
trenching operation is recommended to provide sufficient compaction
around the pipe and to minimize possible damage from the general
embankment compaction operations
Cartpaths
In some locations particularly at the proposed 8th Hole the cart
paths will descend steep existing slopes In these areas we recom
mend that the cartpaths be based entirely on cut Normally we
recommend a cut slope of one and a half horizontal to one vertical
however in some areas the existing slopes would require deep cuts
to daylight Where it is necessary a cut slope of one half hori
zontal to one vertical may be utilized for the upper slope

It should be noted that this steep slope will most likely result in
localized unravelling or sloughing that will require maintenance to
keep the paths clear
Run off should be diverted away from the outside slope face to
minimize erosion of the existing slopes below the cartpaths
Both asphalt concrete and Portland cement concrete pavements are
planned for the proposed cartpaths Based on the light loads

Settlements
It is anticipated that there will be settlements where fill is placed
over abandoned stream channels particularly at the lst Green and
17th Fairway see Site Plans Plates 2 and 3 For a 10 foot fill
estimated settlements are on the order of 4 to 6 inches It is
anticipated that most of the settlement will occur during construc
tion However some settling may occur after completion of the
fill These areas may be corrected as a maintenance situation
8th Fairway
The grading plans provided to us do not indicate any grading in this
area However our field reconnaissance revealed that this area is
an abandoned flood plain of Anini Stream The ground in this area
tends to be somewhat wet and marshy In order to provide sufficient
drainage to establish firm ground and golf course ground cover it
may be desirable to raise the ground surface above the existing
marshy area This could be accomplished by spreading a 12 inch thick
blanket of granular fill over the prepared ground surface The
recomended 3B Fine gravel or equivalent granular fill will serve as
both a drainage blanket and capillary break for the overlying fill
The fairway should then be filled with on site soils compacted to
85 of their maximum dry density to a suitable finish grade approxi
mately two feet above the granular blanket

Design Review
Plans for site grading should be forwarded to the soils engineer for
review and written comments prior to construction to see that the
intent of our recommendations is included in the project plans and
specifications
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the filling operations be observed by the soils
engineer during construction This is to see that adequate compaction
has been obtained and the subsurface conditions are consistent with the
findings of our subsurface investigation The recommendations given in
this report are contingent upon the above inspections

APPENDIX B
LABORATORY TESTING
Moisture content and unit weight determinations were per
formed on selected samples to aid in the classification and
evaluation of the soil properties The test results are pre
sented on the test pit and auger probe logs at the approximate
sample depths
Three moisture density relationship proctor tests were
performed to establish the maximum dry density of selected
soils The test results are summarized on Plate B l
Seven Atterberg Limit tests were performed on samples of
the on site soils to classify the soils and to evaluate their
plasticity The test results are presented on Plates B 1 and
Three consolidation tests were performed on relatively
undisturbed and remolded samples to evaluate their compressibi
lity The consolidation curves are presented on Plates B 3
through B 5
Eight direct shear tests were performed on relatively
undisturbed and remolded samples to determine the cohesion and
angle of internal friction of the soils The shear curves are
shown on Plates B 6 through B 13
Five one inch ring swell tests were performed on rela
tively undisturbed and remolded samples in order to evaluate
the swelling potential of the on site soils The test results
are summarized on Plate B 14
