This letter presents the findings and recommendations from our geotechnical
engineering exploration performed for the proposed vault foundation at the Bank of
America Hawaii Operations Center in Honolulu Hawaii The general location and vicinity
of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located in the Ala Moana Building at 677 Ala Moana Boulevard
between Coral and Keawe Streets in Honolulu Hawaii It is proposed to construct a new
modular vault system for the Bank of America Hawaii Operations Center in the existing
Ala Moana Building Based on the information transmitted to our office we understand
that the plan dimensions of the vault development will be about 191 by 30 feet and the
new vault system will weigh approximately 120 tons The key considerations of the new
development will be the foundation bearing characteristics of the subsurface soils

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
We conducted a geotechnical engineering exploration consisting of drilling two
borings at the project site on March 14th The subsoil conditions below the proposed
vault were explored by drilling and sampling two borings to depths of about 11 and 21
feet below the existing concrete slab surface The approximate locations of the borings
are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Based on our field exploration a layer of fill consisting of medium dense silty sands
was encountered to about 3 feet below the existing concrete slab Below this near
surface fill layer medium dense fine sands of about 11 feet thick were encountered The
fine sands were underlain by soft gray sandy silts to about 6 feet below the existing
concrete slab Below the sandy silts medium dense sandy gravels and silty sands were
encountered to the maximum depths explored of 11 and 21 feet below the existing
concrete slab Groundwater levels were measured in both borings at about 6 feet below
the existing concrete slab at the time of drilling Detailed descriptions of the materials
encountered in our borings are presented on the Logs of Borings Plates 4 and 5

FOUNDATIONS
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered in our borings we believe that
a shallow mat foundation bearing on the near surface medium dense silty sand may be
used to support the proposed vault An allowable bearing pressure of up to 1 500
pounds per square foot p s f may be used for design of footings bearing on the
recompacted subgrade soils This bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may be
increased by one third 1 for transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic
forces An embedment depth of at least 12 inches below the lowest adjacent finish grade
is recommended
Lateral loads imposed on the foundations may be resisted by a combination of
passive earth pressure exerted against near vertical foundation faces and soil friction
between the base of the foundation and the supporting subgrade Passive earth pressure
due to an equivalent fluid weighing 300 pounds per cubic foot p c f and a base friction
factor of 0 35 may be used for design This assumes that the backfill on the sides of the
foundations are well compacted Unless covered by slabs passive soil resistance in the
upper 12 inches should be neglected
SETTLEMENTS
We understand that the proposed vault system has a total weight of 120 tons
Based on our field exploration and engineering analyses we estimate total settlements
on the order of approximately 1 inch would occur due to the imposed structural loading
As aresult the anticipated differential settlement between the existing slab and the vault
slab would be on the order of 1 inch Based on the sandy and silty subsoils encountered
rings a majority of the estimated settlement should occur relatively quickly We _
estimate that the settlement would occur approximately 1 week after the loads are

SUBGRADE PREPARATION
The existing concrete slab will be removed prior to construction of the new mat
foundation After demolition of the existing concrete slab the resulting debris should be
disposed of properly off site The exposed subgrade soils should be scarified to a depth
of at least 8 inches properly moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the
optimum moisture content and compacted to not less than 95 percent relative
compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as
a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil determined in accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91 Optimum moisture is the water content percentage
by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
If zones of soft or wet soils are encountered at the subgrade levels during
scarification over excavation may be required to expose firm soils This should be
evaluated by a representative of Geolabs Hawaii in the field The resulting excavation
should be backfilled with engineered fill compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relatively
compaction
