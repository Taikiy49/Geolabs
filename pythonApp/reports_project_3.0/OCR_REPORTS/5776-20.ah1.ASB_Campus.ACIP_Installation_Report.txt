PROJECT DESCRIPTION
In general the American Savings Bank ASB Campus project involves the
construction of an 11 story reinforced concrete structure consisting of five levels of office
floors atop five levels of parking over a ground floor level of lobby and bank The structure
will have overall plan dimensions of about 325 feet by 120 feet The site is generally
bounded by Aala Street to the north North Beretania Steet to the west Nu uanu Stream to
the south and the HawaiiUSA Federal Credit Union to the east The general project
vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
Based on the geotechnical engineering report referenced above we recommended
supporting the new office building on a deep foundation system consisting of 24 inch
diameter ACIP piles with allowable compressive load capacities of 200 and 240 kips
100 and 120 tons per pile The ACIP piles would extend through the near surface fills
and lagoonal deposits and derive support primarily from end bearing on the very dense
boulder layer and or hard basalt rock formation with design pile lengths ranging from about
40 to 80 feet
STATIC PILE LOAD TEST
In order to evaluate the adequacy of the recommended design lengths of the
ACIP pile foundation a static pile load test was conducted on one dedicated ACIP load
test pile designated as TP1 To better understand the load transfer mechanism at the
pile soil interface the load test pile was instrumented with vibrating wire embedment strain
gages installed at various depths along the length of the load test pile The 24 inch
diameter load test pile was installed to a depth of about 53 2 feet below the existing
ground surface
A drill rig equipped with automatic monitoring equipment and a
continuous flight hollow stem auger was used to install the load test pile A truck mounted
hydraulic concrete pump with an average stroke volume of about 0 145 cubic yards per
stroke was used to pump the grout into the pile system The load test pile was loaded in
general accordance with ASTM D1143 Quick Load Test Method The static pile load test
results are summarized in the following report
ACIP Pile Load Test Report Test Pile No 1 American Savings Bank ASB
Campus 230 North Beretania Street Honolulu Oahu Hawaii dated April 18
2017
Based on the static pile load test results the 24 inch diameter load test pile was
loaded to a maximum load of 600 kips It should be noted that the load test pile was
extended down to the boulder layer and was terminated due to refusal conditions defined
as very hard drilling with no advancement Based on our field observations and the results
of the static pile load test it is our opinion that the 24 inch diameter ACIP piles drilled to
refusal conditions would be sufficient to achieve a minimum design compressive load
capacity of 240 kips per pile with a factor of safety of at least 2 0

PRODUCTION PILE INSTALLATION
The general contractor for the project was Nordic PCL Construction Inc and the
ACIP pile installation subcontractor was Hawaiian Dredging Construction Company Inc
HDCC The production ACIP pile installation program consisting of installing 419
24 inch diameter ACIP piles was conducted from March 7 through May 2 2017 under
the near continuous full time observations by our field engineers A drill rig
equipped with hollow stem augers was used to install the production piles Automatic
monitoring equipment was mounted on both drilling pumping equipment with the intent of
providing auger depth grout volume grout pressure etc on a near continuous basis
During the pile installation operation logs were maintained for each ACIP pile
which included information pertaining to pile number date installed depth of pile grout
volume used grout return depth etc The locations of the production ACIP piles installed
are shown on the ACIP Pile Identification Plan Plate 2 The production ACIP pile
installation summary is presented by pile number on Plates 3 1 through 3 13 of this letter
report The ACIP piles installed ranged in lengths from about 32 3 to 74 8 feet
Based on our observation of the equipment performance and subsurface conditions
encountered it is our professional opinion that the production 24 inch diameter ACIP piles
have been installed to sufficient depths and or resistance to provide the design load
capacities of 200 or 240 kips per pile with a safety factor of 2 0
LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site even when our representatives are on the
project site on a full time basis Our observation and consultation services were performed
in accordance with generally accepted standards of geotechnical engineering practice no
warranty is expressed or implied Provision of construction monitoring by an engineer is
not insurance nor does it guarantee construction of any type The contractor shall retain
primary responsibility for the quality of the work for adhering to plans and specifications
and for repairing defects regardless of when they are found
