SITE GRADING
The project site was graded for the development of a high rise hotel structure
The grading entailed the removal of deleterious surface materials clearing and
grubbing stabilization of the soft ground swamp area and placement of between 5
to 10 feet of structural fill It is our understanding that additional fill will need to be
placed to attain the finish grade of the hotel development The mass grading work was
performed by Oahu Construction Company Ltd A general layout of the project site is
presented on the Site Plan Plate 1
Based on the observations of our field representatives and tests conducted it is
our opinion that the grading was performed in general accordance with the intent of the

Soft Ground Stabilization
Subsequent to clearing and grubbing the low lying swamp area in the central
portion of the site was stabilized by the placement of a geotextile fabric and a minimum
of 18 inches of No 3B Fine gravel Import structural fills were then placed in controlled
lift heights and compacted with a sheepsfoot roller
Fill Placement
During the mass grading operations selected samples of the imported soils
utilized as fill materials were obtained and returned to our laboratory for testing The
maximum dry density and optimum moisture content were determined in our laboratory
in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Results of our laboratory
tests are presented on Plate 2
Observation and testing of the earthwork in the field were performed by
representatives from our office to observe that the grading was performed in general
conformance with the intent of the Geotechnical Engineering Report and specifications
pertaining to the geotechnical aspects of the development In place density tests of the
fills were performed periodically in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D
1556 Sand Cone and D 2922 Nuclear Method The test results are presented on
the attached Summary of Field Density Test Results Plates 3 1 and 3 2
LIMITATIONS
This office assumes no responsibility for any alterations made to slopes or
finished grades on the subject project subsequent to our observation and testing period
without our knowledge and written acknowledgement
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore there is a possibility that
some work may have occurred without our observation However we believe that the

the geotechnical risks to the project It should be recognized that there are variations
in the accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor
