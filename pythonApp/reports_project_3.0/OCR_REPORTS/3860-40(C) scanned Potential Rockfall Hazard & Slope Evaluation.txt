As requested we evaluated the potential for rockfall hazard conditions at
Ramps A and B of the North South Road Phase 1C project This letter report
summarizes our findings based on our field reconnaissance of the existing site conditions
and presents our geotechnical recommendations for design of the proposed cut slopes
The findings and recommendations presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at
the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is at the intersection of the proposed North South Road and the
existing Interstate Route H 1 Highway in the District of Ewa on the Island of Oahu Hawaii
as shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 We understand that new vehicular ramps
will be constructed on both the inbound and outbound sides of the highway The proposed
Ramps A and B on the outbound northerly side of the highway will have new hillside
cut slopes constructed to achieve the desired grade The proposed Ramps C and D on
the southerly side of the highway do not have new cut slopes planned
Based on our review of the Pre Final construction plans for
North South Road Phase 1C Ramp A westbound off ramp consists of approximately
1 520 linear feet of new on grade roadway Ramp B westbound on ramp consists of
approximately 1 884 linear feet of new on grade roadway The pre final design specifies
new cut slopes with inclinations of one horizontal to two vertical 1H 2V along the north
side of the proposed ramps In addition we understand a maximum 30 foot wide tapered
shoulder is provided as a rockfall safety setback zone beneath the proposed cut slope

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
High cut slopes up to about 30 to 45 feet are planned for the project Based on our
site reconnaissance and field exploration we believe that the highest potential for rockfall
hazard would occur from the faces of the new cut slopes during construction Based on
our review of the earth materials encountered in the borings drilled for Ramps A and B
we believe that the cut slope design should be modified to reduce the potential for future
rockfall slope raveling and surface erosion conditions from occurring
Potential Rockfall Hazards
We conducted a subsurface exploration of the project site Boring Nos 219 through
223 were drilled at the Interchange Ramp A and B sites as shown approximately on the
Site Plan Plate 2 Boring and 221 were drilled at the Ramp B cut
locations and Boring Nos 222 and 223 were drilled at the Ramp A cut locations
In general it appears that the area northeast of the intersection of Palehua Road
and Interstate Route H 1 Highway contains near surface semi consolidated and
unconsolidated alluvial deposits overlying a buried and irregular basalt rock surface The
area northwest of the intersection appears to contain near surface residual soils overlying
basalt rock formation materials As a result Ramps A and B and the associated cut
slopes traverse differing geologic materials These cut slopes may expose cobbles and
boulders and fractured basalt rock
Based on the topographic survey plan the areas beyond the top of the cut slopes
for Ramps A and B generally slope toward the south with inclination of 5H 1V or flatter
Based on our site reconnaissance the areas beyond the top of the cut slopes contained
very limited or non existent surface rock outcroppings and boulder deposits The limited
occurrence of surface rock outcroppings and relatively low slope inclinations should pose a
relatively low potential for rockfall hazard in its present landform setting

Interchange Ramp B
Based on information obtained from the borings drilled we anticipate the Ramp B
cut slope would expose approximately 8 feet of near surface stiff to very stiff clayey
residual and saprolitic soils and weathered clinker materials overlying generally medium
hard to very hard layered basaltic rock The medium hard to very hard basaltic rock
encountered in the borings consists of some severely fractured clinker layers interbedded
with variable closely fractured to slightly fractured very dense rock materials
The rock materials encountered in Boring Nos 219 through 221 appear generally
competent and are suitable to stand at the planned cut slope inclination We
recommend flattening the top 10 feet of the planned cut slope to a 2H 1V inclination
because the borings indicate the near surface materials consist of potentially erosive
stiff very stiff residual clay soils and weathered basalt clinker materials
In addition the exposed near surface rock formation exhibits a broken or shattered
appearance caused by severe rock jointing with very closely spaced fracture and bedding
discontinuities based on visual observation of some shallow depth near surface road cuts
along the existing Palehua Road about mile northwesterly from the interchange site
The existing Palehua Road cut slopes ranging between about 8 and 12 feet in height
were excavated steeply at sub vertical inclinations The rock materials exposed in the
shallow cuts show evidence of active erosion and _ raveling involving small
generally cobble size pieces of rock material
Based on our observation of the weathered and broken rock materials exposed in
the adjacent Palehua Road cuts we recommend using a 1H 1V cut slope inclination from
Sta No 0 00 to Sta No 2 00 to reduce the potential for rockfall conditions from
developing The proposed 30 foot wide rockfall safety zone should be adequate in
containing potential rockfall debris which is anticipated to consist of mainly cobble sized
materials derived from the 1H 1V cut slope
An alternative option for rockfall control is to maintain the planned cut
slope inclination and allow for a contingent installation of draped wire mesh on the
cut slope The need for draped wire mesh could be evaluated following the slope
excavation based on the condition of the exposed rock materials The purpose of the
draped wire mesh is to control the fall of potential loose rock material from the cut slope
face toward the toe of the slope
Interchange Ramp A

cut slope would expose variable soils generally consisting of stiff silty clay
semi consolidated alluvial cobbles and boulders mixed with silty clay soil and some
cemented cinder sand deposits Boring Nos 222 and 223 were drilled within the planned
cut slope section of Ramp A as shown on the Site Plan Plate 3 3

In general Boring No 222 within the main cut slope zone of Ramp A
encountered alluvial soils consisting of mixed basaltic cobbles and boulders embedded
with clayey soils The alluvial deposits were encountered to a depth of about 28 feet below
the existing ground surface Below the alluvial materials cemented volcanic cinder
consisting of medium dense to dense silty sand was encountered extending to the
maximum depth explored of about 35 feet below the ground surface
In general Boring No 223 easterly of the main cut slope zone encountered
stiff very stiff and medium dense dense alluvial deposits consisting of silty clay soil mixed
with basaltic cobbles and boulders The alluvial deposits were encountered to a depth of
about 10 feet below the existing ground surface Below the alluvial deposits medium hard
to very hard basalt rock formation was encountered extending to the maximum depth
explored of about 35 feet below the ground surface
Based on our review of the materials encountered in Boring Nos 222 and 223 we
recommend reducing the planned Ramp A cut slope inclination from to 2H 1V
because of the presence of potentially erosive alluvial deposits which may be subject to
slope raveling and the production of falling rock hazards The width of the proposed
rockfall safety zone maximum of 30 feet may be reduced subsequently because slope
erosion should be reduced by the flatter cut slope inclination

LIMITATIONS
The interpretations and recommendations submitted in this letter report are based
upon site observations and available test boring information Variations of surface and
subsurface conditions between and beyond our observations and borings may occur and
the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident until construction is
underway If variations then appear evident Geolabs Inc should be contacted to
re evaluate the recommendations presented herein
It should be noted that slopes composed of rock materials rock slopes deteriorate
with the passage of time due to natural weathering processes wet dry and hot cold
cycles and erosion conditions Due to the inherent deterioration of rock slopes resulting
from natural processes weathering wet dry and hot cold cycles and erosion conditions
the potential for rockfall hazard at any site changes with the passage of time Therefore
the findings and recommendations contained herein may be used only within a reasonable
time from the date of issuance of this letter report Land use site conditions and or other
factors may change with the passage of time Therefore additional work to evaluate the
applicability of the findings and preliminary recommendations contained herein due to
changes with the passage of time will be required Finally there are no guarantees in the
professional engineering and architectural design fields with respect to potential rockfall
