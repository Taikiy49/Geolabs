As requested we have made a study of the near surface
soil conditions below the above referenced lot The

and a review of the grading and compaction test record on

and recommendations

PROJECT CONSIDERATION
The preliminary house plans for the proposed single family
residence were not available during our soil investigation

utilizing concrete slab on grade for the garage level and
post and beam construction for the remaining portions would
be constructed
Due to the sloping terrain at the lot it is anticipated
that cuts would be required in the rear portion of the
house to create sufficient level backyard area for the
residence

toward the west at about 34 1 H V ratios The elevation
of the lot generally varies from about 142 to 203 feet

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that the lot is underlain
by a sruface layer of grayish brown highly expansive clay
about 0 5 to 3 0 feet thick over dense weathered rock
Foundations should extend through the surface clay layer
and bear on the dense rock formation Concrete slab should
bear on a minimum of 3 feet of select non expansive soil or
on weathered rock formation The driveway should bear on a
minimum of 2 feet of select non expansive soil or on weath
ered rock formation
Foundations
Individual spread footings for the house should extend
through the surface clay and bear on the dense weathered
rock formation The footings should extend a minimum of
6 inches into the weathered rock formation An allowable
bearing value of 4000 p s f can be used on the dense weath
ered rock
If a footing is located next to a utility line the footing
should extend to the bottom of the utility trench to reduce
footing settlement due to settlement of the trench backfill
Surface water should be diverted away from the foundations
This is especially important in the rear portion of the house
to reduce ponding of surface run off water and possible sat
uration and settlement or heaving of the foundation soils

Slab On Grade
Concrete slab on grade would probably be used for the garage
It appears that the rear portion of the garage area would be
cut in order to obtain sufficient level area for the garage
Therefore it appears that dense weathered rock would prob
ably be encountered in the rear cut portion of the garage
excavation The front portion of the garage may encounter
the expansive clay depending on the amount of cut The clay
soils are highly expansive and are unsuitable as slab sub
grade soils Where encountered the expansive clays should
be overexcavated down to dense weathered rock or for a maxi
mum depth of 3 feet below the slab subgrade and replaced
with non expansive borrow soils compacted to a minimum of 90
of its maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 This overexcava
tion and replacement should be extended for a distance of
3 feet beyond the perimeter of the slab
A 4 inch layer of base course should be provided below the
slab andcompacted to 90 of its maximum dry density In or
der to protect against moisture infiltration a polyethylene
moisture barrier may be incorporated below the slab The
membrane should be protected by a thin layer of sand to aid
in the concrete curing

Concrete Driveway and Sidewalks
A surface layer about 1 5 to 3 0 feet thick of expansive
clay soils were encountered in the front portion of the lot
driveway area In order to reduce the expansive effects
of the clay it should be kept a minimum of 2 feet below the
driveway subgrade level Where 2 or more feet of fill is
planned select non expansive borrow material should be used
to cap over the Adobe clay Where less than 2 feet of fill
is planned overexcavation and replacement of the Adobe
clay with select non expansive material is required The
above requirements should be followed for a minimum distance
of 3 feet beyond the driveway
If a relatively long driveway is planned joints are recom
mended at about 8 to 10 foot intervals in order to allow for
some relative movement of the concrete
For future sidewalks capping of the clay or overexcavation
and replacement similar to the driveway is recommended to re
duce heaving caused by the expansive clay soil
Site Grading
The existing terrain at the site generally slopes down at
about 4 1 H V ratio or flatter A cut slope for the back
yard development may be required
Based on the field development we anticipate that cuts up

to about 2 feet would be in Adobe clay While deeper cuts
would encounter weathered rock The thin clay layer should
be stripped off or flattened as much as possible to reduce
later sloughing or downhill slope creep Cuts in dense weath
ered rock could be made at slope ratios of 1 1 H V
Fill and backfill material should be select non expansive
borrow material If available some of the weathered rock
excavated material smaller than 8 inches could be used in
the fill if it is not contaminated with the clay The clay
soil should not be used for fill material
The area to be filled should be cleared of vegetation and
debris prior to filling
Fill and backfill should be placed in maximum 8 inch lifts
and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density
established by ASTM D 1557

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during
earthwork construction particularly the fill placement
slope cuts and to monitor the contractor s grading operations
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be observed
by a soils engineer during construction to see that adequate
bearing has been obtained and that the subsurface conditions
are consistent with the findings of our subsurface investiga
tion
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested
and approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the
site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon
the above inspections
