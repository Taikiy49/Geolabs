Dear Mr. Muraoka: 
In accordance with the scope of services outlined in our fee proposal dated 
March 10, 2023, we observed the drilled shaft foundation installation work for the 
Kamehameha Schools Kapalama Kunuiakea Videoboard project located at Kunuiakea Field 
within the Kamehameha Schools Kapalama Campus in the Honolulu area on the Island of 
Oahu, Hawaii. The project location and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location 
Map, Plate 1. 
This letter report summarizes our geotechnical observations performed during the 
drilled shaft installation of the above videoboard structure. In general, the purpose of our 
geotechnical engineering services was to observe the drilled shaft foundation installation for 
general conformance with the project plans and specifications. 
PROJECT DESCRIPTION 
The project is located at Kunuiakea Field within the Kamehameha Schools Kapalama 
Campus in Honolulu on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. We understand that a new, larger 
videoboard is planned to replace the existing videoboard. The new location will be near the 
50-yard line on the makai side of the field. 
The videoboard is supported on deep foundations consisting of cast-in-place 
concrete drilled shafts. Drilled shafts with diameters of 60 inches and lengths ranging from 
25.0 to 25.8 feet were used to support the new videoboard. 
The scope of our work discussed in this letter report includes observation of the drilled 
shaft foundation for the Kamehameha Schools Kapalama Kunuiakea Videoboard project 
only. Ralph S. Inouye Company, Ltd. was the general contractor for the project. Hawaiian 
Dredging Construction Company was the drilling subcontractor and was responsible for the 
fabrication and installation of the reinforcing cage and concrete placement.

DRILLED SHAFT INSTALLATION 
Based on the anticipated subsurface conditions and foundation demand, a deep 
foundation system consisting of cast-in-place concrete drilled shaft embedded into the 
residual soil was utilized for foundation support of the new videoboard. 
Hawaiian Dredging Construction Company installed four drilled shafts from June 20 
through 30, 2023 at the locations shown on the Site Plans, Plates 2.1 and 2.2. 
Representatives from Geolabs, Inc. observed the drilled shafts excavation, placement of 
reinforcing cage, and concrete placement operation on a near-continuous (full-time) basis 
during the installation of the production drilled shafts. The diameter and as-built lengths of 
the drilled shafts installed for the project are provided on the Drilled Shaft Installation 
Summary, Plate 3. The drilled shaft records are provided in the Appendix of this report. 
The drilled shafts were excavated by the subcontractor utilizing an IMT International 
A125 track drill rig. Tooling used for the drilled shafts included 36-inch diameter auger and 
core barrel, 48-inch diameter auger and core barrel, 60-inch diameter core barrel, auger, 
digging bucket, and clean-out bucket. The drilled shafts were excavated by the 
subcontractor by initially drilling a 36-inch diameter pilot hole with the 36-inch diameter core 
barrel and auger. The pilot was expanded to a 60-inch diameter using the 48-inch and 60-
inch diameter tools. It should be noted that on the plans, the drilled shafts were specified to 
be 54-inch diameter; however, due to the subcontractor’s limitation on drilling tools, they 
informed our field representative that the drilled shafts would be installed to a 60-inch 
diameter. Groundwater was not encountered during the shaft excavation. 
After excavating the drilled shafts to the designated tip elevation, the drilling 
subcontractor set up a formwork to set the reinforcing cage with a wide flange beam 
(W40x235) in the center of the cage at the target elevation. Five Crosshole Sonic Logging 
(CSL) tubes were connected to the rebar cage(s) and filled with water prior to being lowered 
into the drilled hole. The reinforcing cage was lowered into the drilled hole to the appropriate 
elevations by the drilling subcontractor using a Grove RT 880E crane.  
After lowering the reinforcing cage into the drilled hole, concrete was dropped 
through a chute with the end of the chute about two feet below the ground surface. The 
chute was generally used to prevent the concrete from hitting the rebar cage and wide flange 
beam. Concrete with a measured slump ranging from 7.5 to 9 inches and temperature 
ranging from 82 to 88 degrees was used for the drilled shafts.  
Based on the observations of our field representatives, it is our professional opinion 
that the drilled shafts supporting the new videoboard for the Kamehameha Schools 
Kapalama Kunuiakea Videoboard project were installed in general accordance with the 
intent of the project plans and specifications and the geotechnical recommendations 
presented in our geotechnical engineering report dated November 10, 2022, and within the 
limits of generally accepted construction practices. In addition, it is our professional opinion 
that the drilled shaft has been installed to a sufficient depth to provide the design load 
capacity.
