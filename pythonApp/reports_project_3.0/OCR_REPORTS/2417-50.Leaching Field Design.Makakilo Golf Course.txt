Leaching Field Design
Makakilo Golf Course
As requested we have performed percolation tests in general accordance with our
proposal as stated in our Progress Report dated The percolation
tests were performed to evaluate the subsoil absorption capacity for on site sewage
disposal with leaching field design at the Maintenance Building and Halfway House areas
A truck mounted drilling rig was used to drill and sample a 4 5 inch diameter
borehole to depths of about seven 7 feet below the existing ground surface at each site
The approximate locations of the drilled boreholes percolation test are shown in the
attached Plates 1 and 2 The soil conditions encountered in the boreholes and the
percolation test results are summarized in the attached Plates 3 and 4
The soil samples obtained from the drilled boreholes and the percolation rates from
the performed tests have been reviewed and interpreted to form the basis of our
recommendations
Maintenance Building
Approximately three and one half 3 5 feet of dense clayey silt to silty sand with
gravel overlying weathered cinder formation were encountered in the borehole The
measured percolation rate was 45 minutes per inch or slower indicating that the soils are
relatively impermeable Therefore it is our opinion that a very large leaching field will be
required Alternative sewage disposal systems may be required

Halfway House
Complex and variable soil conditions were encountered in the borehole In
general a surface layer of residual soil consisting of silty clay overlying weathered cinder
formation with thin ledges of weathered basalt were encountered Experience within the
project site vicinity indicate that the thickness of the surface impermeable silty clay layer
may range from two 2 to over five 5 feet The measured percolation rate was 21
minutes per inch It appears that the leaching field design is feasible provided that the
absorption areas penetrate into the permeable zones of weathered cinder and basalt
formations
Moderately hard excavation is anticipated for the proposed construction of the
leaching fields It is our opinion that provisions to clean out the leaching field periodically
should be incorporated in the system operation
