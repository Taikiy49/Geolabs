This report presents the results of a preliminary subsurface
investigation for the above project conducted in accordance with the
scope of services outlined in our revised proposal dated December 9
1982 Our investigation included drilling 2 borings performing
laboratory tests and evaluating site conditions for the proposed
design The general area of the site is shown on the Project Location
Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and design recommendations
W O 1251 00 Page 2
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is our understanding that the project is at a preliminary
conceptual stage and that no definite plan has been developed at this
time In general the present proposed scheme is to construct a
berthing pier about 10 feet wide along the shoreline for small
vessels The pier will be about 50 feet from the present shoreline
It is our understanding that dredging of the harbor bottom aiong the
front of the new pier may not be required if a clear depth of 8 feet
below the Mean Lower Low Water Level is acceptable
PURPOSE AND SCOPE OF SERVICES
The purpose of this investigation is to provide a preliminary
soils design criteria based on a limited subsurface exploration
program Due to a budgetary consideration the scope of our service
was reduced substantially and included only one off shore boring
Boring 1 and one shallow land boring Boring 2 The off shore
boring was drilled to 123 feet deeper than the 70 feet proposed in
the revised proposal because of the lack of a suitable end bearing
stratum This point was discussed with your office during the course
of our field exploration
The soil samples obtained were re examined in the laboratory and
a testing program was undertaken The descriptions of the field
exploration and laboratory testing together with the borings logs and
laboratory test data are presented in the appendices

SITE CONDITIONS
The proposed pier improvement is located along the eastern side
of Pier 37 which had been reclaimed from the Kapalama Basin by filling
at various times Stone riprap placed along the perimeter of the
earth filled pier serves as wave protection There were several
existing large petroleum storage tanks situated in the middle of the
filled area
In accordance with your request the subsurface conditions were
explored by drilling one off shore boring and one land boring at the
approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Below the mudline the subsoil encountered in Boring 1 consisted
of harbor mud with soft gray clayey silt to about Elevation 20 feet
MLLW and underlain by alternating layers of stiff to very stiff brown
silt and clay deposits and medium dense silty coral sand and gravel
of marine or lagoonal origin These deposits ranged in thickness from
15 to 25 feet to a depth of 123 feet the maximum depth explored
The man made fills of Pier 37 consisted of about 1 5 feet of
sandy gravel cover at the surface underlain by stiff brown clayey
silt silty clay and medium dense sandy gravel The thickness of the
fill in Boring 2 was about 10 feet underlain by about 15 feet of soft
to medium stiff gray silty clay or harbor mud The soft mud again
extended to about Elevation 20 feet
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1251 00 Page 4
Based on a review of available boring logs by others for the
adjacent storage tank area it appears that the thickness of the fill
varied up to as much as 20 feet The soft harbor mud varied from 4 to
9 feet These conditions were different from those encountered in our
Boring 2 It is probable that the thickness of the fill and the
underlying harbor mud varies over the entire area because of filling
at different times It is also possible that mud waves formed during
previous filling may have been covered by subsequent fill
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
It is our understanding that the scheme of the proposed berthing
pier has not been finalized at this time Our preliminary findings
and analyses indicate that stability of the existing slope has low
factor of safety and subsequent cutting of this slope due to dredging
requirement could further reduce its stability It is recommended
that further study be made to avoid having to steepen the existing
slope by dredging operation
There are several measures to stabilize the shoreline each with
different advantages and disadvantages
GEOLABS HAWAIi
W O 1251 00 Page 5
Because of relatively thick soft clayey silt under the fill along
the shoreline additional filling without adequate Staging and control
could further aggravate the stability situation and filling would
induce substantial ground settlements and downdrag on the future
piling system
Although the soils information available is limited there
appears to be indication that fill thickness along the shoreline may
be thin underlain by a thick layer of soft harbor mud A portion of
the thicker mud may have resulted from filling over older mud wave
Based on preliminary stability evaluation the stability of the
existing slope is low The estimated factor of safety against sliding
is about 1 1 Thus future dredging would further lower the stability
of the slope which may result in sliding
If dredging or steepening of the existing embankment Slope is
considered we recommend that additional borings and testing be made
to better define the subsurface soil and shear strength conditions
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1251 00 Page 6
The following measures are presented for your consideration
A Tieback Sheetpiles This may include installing a line of
sheetpiles dredging out soft mud under the existing slope
and backfilling with granular fill If the sheetpiles are
installed along the dredgeline it would increase the area of
the wharf and permit deeper dredging
B Cantilevered Retaining Caissons This scheme will be in lieu
of tieback sheetpiles A line of vertical caissons will be
installed to act as a fixed end retaining wall to support the
ili against dredged front This type of offshore work is
very difficult and expensive For higher loading these
caissons may need to be tied back
C Stabilizing Piles This scheme will entail driving a row of
piles acting as shear keys to increase shearing resistance
along the potential slip plane
D Replace Mud with Granular Fill This scheme is to replace
the soft mud with rock and or sand fill to improve the
stability to permit a steeper slope A thorough removal of
mud to assure good contact at the bottom of the excavation is
essential
GEOLABS HAWAIi
W O 1251 00 Page 7
E Flatter Slopes This scheme will include maintaining the
existing slope ratio of 2 1 H V or flatter by cutting the
top of the slope back accordingly This may result in less
area available on land Improvement on the slope stability
is limited
Pile Foundation for Pier
The following preliminary recommendations for pile foundations
are based on our understanding of the present proposed schetie as
discussed under PROJECT CONSIDERATION
Although no distinct end bearing stratum was encountered in our
borings we believe that piles may gain frictional resistance in the
stiff silt clay medium dense coral sand and gravel deposits
We believe that 12 inch square prestressed concrete Piles may be
used for the support of the piers These piles should be driven to a
depth of about 60 feet below MLIW or about 40 feet below the bottom
of the mud for a design capacity of 40 tons This assumes that there
will be some dredging to Elevation 8 feet and no additional fill will
be placed Since additional fill will impose some downdrag force on
the piles and should this situation occur the soils engineer should
be consulted so that an re evaluation on the pile recommendatios could
be made
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1251 00 Page 8
The piles should be driven with a hammer delivering a minimum of
15 000 t lbs of energy per blow The piles should be driven to a
minimum depth of Elevation 55 feet and to a final penetration
resistance of about 20 blows per foot The foundation piles should be
spaced at a minimum of 3 diameters on centers
Spudding may be required to install the pile through the riprap
stones on the shore Alternatively these stones may be removed to
insert piles through them
A test pile program may be required if the final pier improvement
will involve a large number of piles Several additional test borings
should be made to better evaluate pile capacity and embedment length
A more detailed test pile program may be developed when such a
requirement is established In general we believe that load testing
o 40 ton piles may not be required at the site
Pavement Design
In general about 1 5 feet of the surface sandy gravel would
provide good subgrade support for light to moderate traffic However
if heavy wheel loads such as those imposed by a heavy forklift
trucks and other heavy vehicles are planned a thick pavement will be
required because of the clays and silts underlying the surface gravel
fill Additional test pits and laboratory testing should be made to
examine the actual thickness of the surface sandy gravel layer and the
nature of the soils underneath
GEOLABS HAWAIi
W O 1251 00 Page 9
For light truck traffic a flexible pavement consisting of 2
inches of asphalt concrete on 6 inches of base course may be
considered
Before placing any pavement sections the existing surface should
be scarified 6 inches properly moisture conditioned and then
recompacted to a density of at least 95 of the maximum dry density
determined in accordance with the ASTM D 1557 78 test procedure Any
soft or yielding area disclosed during this recompaction work should
be over excavated and backfilled with compacted select borrow fill
Cutoff Wall
It is recommended that a blanket of filter fabric or a layer of
graded filter materials be placed behind any cutoff wall situated
along the shoreline to protect the backfill material from being washed
out The graded filter should consist of at least 8 inches of No 3
Coarse at the bottom of the cutoff wall overlain by 10 inches of No
4 Fine crushed rock or base course aggregates If filter fabrics are
used the base course material may then be deleted The filter fabric
should consist of Filter X Mirafi 140 or equivalent
Riprap Protection
Judging from the appearance of the riprap some localized
movement of stones may have developed This riprap may need to be
reconstructed with a proper filter layer if it is expected to remain
permanently under the berthing pier

The plans for foundations site grading and utility construction

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the test pile driving any pile load test
production driving backfilling subgrade preparation and pavement
construction be observed by the soils engineer during construction to
see that adequate compaction has been obtained and the subsurface
conditions are consistent with the findings of our investigation If
conditions encountered are different from those presented in this
report appropriate modifications to the design should be made

Moisture content and unit weight determinations were
performed on selected samples as an aid in the classification
and evaluation of soil properties The results of these tests
are presented on the log of borings at the appropriate sample
depths
To further identify soil characteristics additional
tests were performed These tests include one Atterberg limits
and five sieve analyses to determine the grain size distributions
The results of these tests are presented on Plates B 1l and B 2
Two unconfined compression tests were performed on undis
turbed and slightly disturbed samples to further evaluate the
soil strength characteristics The results of the unconfined
compression tests are summarized on Plate B 3
Three direct shear tests were performed on undisturbed
Samples to evaluate the strength characteristics of the natural
soils The test results are presented on Plates B 4 through
B 6
Two Consolidation tests were performed on selected
undisturbed samples to evaluate the settlement characteristics
of the natural soils The test results are presented on
Plates B 7 and B 8
