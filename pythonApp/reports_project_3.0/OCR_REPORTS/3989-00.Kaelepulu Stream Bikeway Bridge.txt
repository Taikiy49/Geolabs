WAVE EQUATION ANALYSIS
KAELEPULU STREAM BIKEWAY BRIDGE
In accordance with your request we have performed a wave equation analysis for
the proposed pile driving operations for the Kaelepulu Stream Bikeway Bridge project in
Kailua on the island of Oahu Hawaii The wave equation analysis was conducted based
on the information provided and the subsurface conditions contained in the foundation
investigation report for the project prepared by Ernest K Hirata Associates Inc dated
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
Based on the information provided it is our understanding that the proposed
bikeway bridge will be supported on a driven pile foundation system The proposed pile
section will be a 24 inch square precast prestressed concrete pile The piles will have a
length of about 70 feet each and a tip elevation of about 58 feet Mean Sea Level MSL
The piles were designed with an allowable compression load of 80 tons with an
associated factor of safety of 3 0 Therefore the required ultimate pile capacity is
240 tons or 480 kips
In order to install the piles to the specified tip elevation the pile locations will need
to be predrilled to near the specified tip elevation The predrilled holes will need to be
larger than the width of the pile in order to accommodate the square pile section
Therefore the piles will derive support primarily from end bearing on the coral formation
or rubble stone below the specified tip elevation

Subsurface Conditions
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by Ermest K Hirata
Associates Inc by drilling two borings near the abutments of the bikeway bridge The
two borings are designated as B1 and B2 for the east and west abutments respectively
The logs of the two borings have been reproduced and are provided in Appendix A for
ease of reference
Based on the logs the borings generally encountered medium dense silty sands
overlying dense coral or coral rubble stone extending to the maximum depth explored of
about 60 and 74 feet below the existing ground surface The dense coral or coral rubble
stone was encountered at depths of about 39 and 15 feet below the surface at the east
and west abutments respectively A subsurface profile depicting the bridge and
subsurface conditions based on the two borings is provided on Plate 1
Hammer and Driving System
We understand that a single acting diesel hammer is proposed for
the pile driving operations by Hawaiian Dredging Construction Company Based on the
pile driving equipment data form provided the hammer has a ram weight
of 6 615 kips and a maximum stroke of 10 58 feet with a maximum rated energy of
69 9 kip feet at the maximum pump setting No 4 We understand that all the piles for
this bridge project will likely be driven using pump setting No 4
Based on the subsurface conditions and the driving conditions anticipated a pile
cushion consisting of 9 inches of plywood cushion blocks will be used for the pile driving
operations The plywood cushion blocks will be approximately 24 inches square with the
four corners cut off resulting in a total cross section area of about 544 square inches
Pile Details
We understand that the specified pile will be a 24 inch square precast prestressed
concrete pile with a length of about 70 feet each The piles were designed with an
allowable compression load of 80 tons with an associated factor of safety of 3 0 resulting
in an ultimate pile capacity of 240 tons 480 kips The piles will be cast and driven in one
single piece with no splices planned at this time
Based on discussions with the pile fabricator we understand that the piles will be
cast from concrete with a minimum 28 day compressive strength of 6 000 pounds per
square inch psi The effective prestress level of the prestressed pile will be
approximately 1 000 psi Based on this information and Section 505 of the Hawaii
Standard Specifications 1994 the maximum allowable compressive stress in a precast
prestressed concrete pile during pile driving is 2 600 psi The maximum allowable tensile
stress developed in a precast prestressed concrete pile during pile driving is 1 000 psi

WAVE EQUATION ANALYSIS
A wave equation analysis was performed based on the pile driving system
proposed by Hawaiian Dredging Construction Company and the subsurface conditions
based on the two borings drilled near the abutments of the bridge The wave equation
analysis was performed using the computer program GRLWEAP Wave Equation
Analysis of Pile Foundations which is an extension of the WEAP87 program developed
under the sponsorship of the Federal Highway Administration
Pile Capacity
Based on our wave equation analysis it appears that the proposed pile driving
equipment Delmag D 30 32 hammer is capable of driving the specified pile section to
the desired ultimate pile capacity of 480 kips The required driving resistance for the
480 kip ultimate pile capacity required will be about 80 blows per foot A complete
printout of the results of our wave equation analysis is provided in Appendix B A graph
showing the ultimate pile capacity versus the driving resistance is provided on Plate 2
It should be noted that the proposed hammer has a maximum
required in the project specifications Based on our analysis and our experience with pile
driving operations we believe that the proposed hammer should be
capable of installing the piles to the desired pile capacity Based on our review of the
basis of design the proposed hammer meets the intent of the design by
the project geotechnical engineer
Driving Stresses
The computed maximum compressive and tensile stresses in the pile during pile
driving are 1 300 and 750 psi respectively A graph showing the computed maximum
compression and tensile stresses during driving is provided on Plate 3 Please note that
the graph was prepared based on kips per square inch ksi which is equivalent to
1 000 psi as required by the specifications
Based on our analysis the estimated compression and tensile stresses induced in
the pile during the pile driving operations are within the range specified for this project As
previously indicated the maximum allowable compressive and tensile stresses during pile
driving for the specified pile section are 2 600 and 1 000 psi respectively The range of
stresses allowed during pile driving have been shaded on Plate 3 for ease of reference

ABOUT THE WAVE EQUATION ANALYSIS RESULTS
The GRLWEAP program simulates the behavior of an impact driven pile The
program contains mathematical models which describe hammer driving system
pile and soil during the hammer blow Under certain conditions the models
only crudely approximate often complex dynamic situations
A wave equation analysis also relies on input data which represents normal
situations The data may be the best available information at the time of
the analysis however it may greatiy differ from actual field conditions
The program authors therefore recommend prudent use of GRLWEAP results
Soil response and hammer performance should be verified by static and or
dynamic measurements Estimates of bending or other local non axial
stresses and prestress effects must also be accounted for by the user
Finally the GRLWEAP capacities are ultimate values They MUST be reduced
by means of a safety factor to yield a design or working load
