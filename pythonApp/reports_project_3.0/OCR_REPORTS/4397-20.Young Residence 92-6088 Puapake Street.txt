We found the Weidig report very biased and containing many erroneous and
misleading statements calculations and conclusions Our findings are summarized below
Expansion Potential
The report indicates the soils have high expansion pages 4 and 7 based on swell
tests and Atterberg Limits tests However Weidig s own expansion test results show less
than 1 swell low and his Atterberg Limits tests classify the soils as MH and CL not
highly expansive CH soils The report also acknowledges that the compaction tests taken
during filling show that the soils were placed at moisture contents at least 3 above
optimum which reduces the swell potential of the soils Therefore the mention of highly
expansive soil in the Weidig report is misleading and untrue
Floor Survey
Weidig conducted a floor survey within the house and plotted the results on
Plate A7 There are several facts about the floor survey that are omitted The accuracy of
the survey system is on the order of about 0 125 to 0 25 inch The accuracy is further
lessened when trying to relate readings on different floor coverings with different
thicknesses i e carpet vinyl tile ceramic tile etc Additionally the tolerance of concrete
slabs placed in Hawaii is different than in other places Our measurements on concrete
slabs at construction sites in the Makakilo area indicate that the finished surface of
residential concrete slabs may be on the order of 0 5 to 0 75 inches out of level Another
fact omitted is that garage slabs and patio slabs are constructed with a slope to allow
water drainage and are not a sign of a defect The statements conclusions in the Weidig
report do not take into account these inherent inaccuracies of the level survey and imply a
precision that is not true

Slope Stability
There are several major errors in Weidig s slope stability analyses First of all the
results of the laboratory soil strength tests seem unusually low However even using these
low values our calculated slope stability analyses indicate suitable factor of safety FS
against instability greater than 1 5 the industry standard Part of the reason that Weidig s
FS is so low 1 0 is that a ground water table was included in their analyses even though
none was found in the borings see Plates A3 and A4 and page 5 Groundwater table has
a large adverse effect on the stability of slopes but should not be present at the high
elevation of Makakilo Another reason for Weidig s low FS is that they included the effects
of earthquake forces When earthquake forces are included in slope stability analyses it is
standard practice to reduce the allowable FS to about 1 15 to account for the infrequent
ocurrence of earthquakes Weidig included the groundwater table although not present
as well as the earthquake loads and used a FS of 1 5 not the correct 1 15 to conclude
that the slope was marginally stable Therefore the Weidig slope stability analysis is totally
incorrect and misleading
House Distress
The Weidig report misses the point by not recognizing that the house distress is
primarily where the new addition is connected to the original house During our site visit
there were minimal signs of movements in the remainder of the house Additionally the
homeowner stated that the problems started after construction of the addition they had
lived in the house for about 5 years The recommendation to install piers under the
original house is excessive and unnecessary as this part of the house did not have
problems Piers may have been a viable recommendation for the addition foundations but
we were not involved in this design Use of pier foundations for the addition could have
prevented the movement and damage experienced Our compaction report for the project
states This office assumes no responsibility for any alterations made to slopes or finished
grades on the subject project subsequent to our observations and testing period without
our knowledge and written acknowledgment Lot owners should be advised not to block
the surface drainage and to obtain professional consultation in the planning design and
construction of any future improvements The lot owners are responsible for the design
and subsequent performance of additions and retaining walls
