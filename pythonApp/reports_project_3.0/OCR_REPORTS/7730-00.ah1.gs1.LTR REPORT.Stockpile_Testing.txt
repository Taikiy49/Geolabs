Dear Mr. Maja: 
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration 
performed for the Evaluation of Stockpile at Area 16A project at the Ewa by Gentry West 
development in Ewa on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. The project site is generally located at 
the western end of the Ewa Gentry development and bounded by Geiger Road to the 
north, Kamakana Street to the west and Coral Creek Golf Club to the east. The project 
location and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map, Plate 1. 
This report summarizes the findings and geotechnical recommendations resulting 
from our field exploration and laboratory testing for the project. These findings and 
geotechnical recommendations are intended to evaluate the suitability of the soils for use 
as general fill and/or non-expansive granular fill material only. The findings and 
recommendations presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this 
report. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
Based on the information provided, the existing soil stockpile is located at 
Area 16A of the Ewa by Gentry West Development in Ewa on the Island of Oahu, 
Hawaii. Area 16A is generally located at the western end of the Ewa by Gentry 
development and bounded by Geiger Road to the north, Kamakana Street to the west 
and Coral Creek Golf Club to the east. The stockpile is roughly 470 feet by 370 feet and 
roughly 22 feet in height. We understand it is desired to evaluate the suitability of the 
existing stockpiled materials for use as general fill and/or non-expansive granular 
material.  
It should be noted that our fee proposal was intended to perform a geotechnical 
engineering exploration of the existing stockpiles at Areas 16 and 16A. However, per 
the request of Gentry Homes, Ltd., we focused our evaluation efforts on the stockpile at

SITE DESCRIPTION 
The stockpile site prior to stockpile placement was generally relatively flat and 
covered an area of approximately 4 acres. The existing ground elevations prior to stockpile 
placement ranged from about +29 to +31 feet Mean Sea Level (MSL). We understand that 
the elevations at the top of the stockpile are about +51 to +53 feet MSL. 
The surrounding area adjacent to the existing stockpile is generally covered with 
vegetation consisting of dry and overgrown tall grass and brush. Several small trees were 
observed scattered around the site. The stockpile was covered with vegetation. In addition, 
a drainage way crosses below Geiger Road and is adjacent to the project site along the 
eastern boundary. Water was not observed in the drainage way at the time of our field 
exploration. 
STOCKPILE EXPLORATION 
We explored the existing stockpile at the project site by excavating nine test pits, 
designated as Test Pit Nos. 1 through 9, extending to depths of about 13 to 18 feet below 
the top of the stockpile. The test pits were excavated by Paradigm Construction LLC using 
a Komatsu PC490LC excavator. In addition, soil samples of the excavated stockpile soils 
were obtained at each test pit location. The approximate test pit locations are shown on 
the Site Plan, Plate 2. The Logs of Test Pits are presented in Appendix A. 
Laboratory testing consisting of sieve analysis and CBR tests were performed on 
the recovered materials from the stockpile. Results of the laboratory tests performed on 
selected soil samples are presented in Appendix B. 
The stockpile materials generally consisted of brown to brownish gray clayey gravel 
with some sand, cobbles and some boulders; brown silty cobbles; and reddish brown 
sandy clay with some gravel. It should be noted that appreciable amounts of concrete and 
asphalt pieces were observed in the stockpile material during the test pit excavation. In 
addition, additional boulders may be encountered in the stockpile. 
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS 
Based on the laboratory test results, CBR values of greater than 37 and CBR 
swells of less than 1 percent were obtained from the samples tested. In addition, cobbles 
and boulders were observed in the stockpile material. Based on the above, we believe that 
the materials tested may be used as general fill and fill materials below 2 feet from the 
finished grade provided the materials greater than 6 inches are removed. Should it be 
desired to use the stockpile material as non-expansive granular fill material, the stockpile 
material needs to be screened to a 3-inch minus material. Additional testing should be 
performed on the 3-inch minus material to verify its suitability.

from existing visible features at the project site. Elevations noted on the test pits were 
interpolated from elevations shown on the Stockpile Plan, dated August 21, 2015, 
prepared by Belt Collins Hawaii, LLC. The physical location and elevation of the test pits 
should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used. 
The stratification breaks shown on the graphic representations of the test pits depict 
the approximate boundaries between the soil types and, as such, may denote a gradual 
transition. We did not encounter groundwater in the test pits at the time of our field 
exploration. However, it must be noted that fluctuation may occur due to variation in 
seasonal rainfall, and other factors. These data have been reviewed and interpretations 
made in the formulation of this report.

Field Exploration 
The existing stockpile at the project site was explored by excavating nine test pits, 
designated as Test Pits Nos. 1 through 9, extending to depths of about 13 to 18 feet below 
the top of the existing stockpile. The approximate test pit locations are shown on the Site

textural examination in the field in general accordance with ASTM D2488, Standard

reviewed visually and by testing in the laboratory. Soils were classified in general

Laboratory Tests 
Six Atterberg Limits tests (ASTM D4318) were performed on selected soil 
samples to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits and to aid in soil classification. The 
graphic presentation of the test results is provided on Plate B-1. 
Nine Sieve Analysis tests (ASTM C117 & ASTM C136) were performed on 
selected soil samples to evaluate the gradation characteristics of the soils and to aid in 
soil classification.  Graphic presentations of the grain size distribution are provided on 
Plates B-2 and B-3. 
Nine laboratory California Bearing Ratio tests (ASTM D1883) were performed on 
bulk samples obtained from the existing stockpile to evaluate the suitability of the 
stockpiled materials for fill material.  The test results are presented on Plates B-4 
through B-12.
