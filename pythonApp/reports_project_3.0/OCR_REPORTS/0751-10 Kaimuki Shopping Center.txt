INTRODUCTION PURPOSE SCOPE
This report presents the results of our supplementary geo
technical investigation for the subject site
The lower parking level excavations have been completed
Spread footings up to 9 feet by 9 feet in plan dimension
supporting up to 296 kips load are to be supported on
fractured basalt
The purpose of this supplementary report was to confirm
presence of underlying basalt and verify foundation design
recommendations for the proposed development

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Highly weathered basalt cobbles and boulders are underlain
by rock consisting of hard basalt with thin random layers of
soil or alluvium Based upon the test borings the surface
elevation of the dense fractured basalt ranges from 94 to
960 feet inthe vicinity of Boring 4 Diamond Head and from
elevation 93 to 95 feet in the vicinity of Boring 6 Ewa
Several thin stiff soil layers or extremely weathered and
decomposed lenses were encountered four feet or more below
the surface of the rock

FOUNDATION RECOMMENDATIONS
Based upon the results of the field cxplorations it 1s our
Opinion that an allowable bearing pressure up to 12 000 pounds
per square foot may be used for footings supported on the
fractured basalt rock The construction of foundations should
incorporate the recommendations presented herein
In onder to reduce the possibility of differential footing
settliement due to possible near surface cavittes clay layers
or cavities in the underlying basalt rock we recommend the
following footing excavations should extend to the surface
of the basalt the foundation excavations should be cleaned
and inspected for minor imperfection fractures highly
weathered zones etc and the underlying basalt should be
probed using a jack hammer at frequent intervals or at each
column location The probe hole should have a minimum diameter
of 2 anches and extend bélow the bottom of the footing toa
depth equal to the footing width or a minimum depth of 5 feet
