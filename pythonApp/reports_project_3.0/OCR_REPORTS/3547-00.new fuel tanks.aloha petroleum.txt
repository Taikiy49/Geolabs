INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed New Fuel Tanks Project located within the Aloha Petroleum
Ltd Oahu Terminal in the Barbers Point area on the island of Oahu Hawaii The
general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map
Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering recommenda
tions resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering analyses
The recommendations presented herein are intended for design of foundations site
grading and trench excavation and backfill only The findings and recommendations
presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located near the intersection of Hanua and Kuhela Streets in
the Barbers Point area in the District of Ewa on the island of Oahu Hawaii It is
proposed to expand the existing fuel terminal at the subject site Based on the
information provided the proposed project will consist of construction of nine new steel
tanks with capacities ranging from 5 000 to 68 000 barrels bbl with associated piping
network
The new fuel tanks will be of steel construction We understand that the new
fuel tanks will range in size from about 41 to 92 feet in diameter and about 24 to
60 feet in height The roof structure of the new tanks will span between the perimeter
wall and the center column Details of the proposed project was not available at the
time this report was prepared However we understand that the proposed tank
structures will be constructed near existing grades Therefore we anticipate that
GEOLABS HAWAII

grading at the proposed site would generally require minor cuts and fills on the order
of about 1 foot or less to achieve a level pad for the new fuel tanks
Our previous experience in the area suggest that the site may be underlain by
coral sand and gravel overlying coral formation at shallow depths Therefore special
attention was given to the characteristics of the coral formation with respect to
foundation support for the proposed tank structures

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu is composed largely of the weathered remnants of two extinct
shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae forms the bulk
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3547 00 Page 5
of the western third of the island while the younger shield Koolau forms the majority
of the eastern two thirds of the island It is believed that Waianae became extinct while
Koolau was still active and its eastern flank is partially buried below Koolau lavas in
central Oahu
During the Pleistocene Epoch sea levels fluctuated in response to the cycles of
continental glaciation As the glaciers grew and advanced less water was available
to fill the oceanic basins such that sea levels fell below the present stands of the sea
When the glaciers melted and receded an excess of water became available such that
the sea levels rose to above the present sea level
The higher sea level stands caused the formation of deltas and fans of
accumulated terrigenous sediments in the heads of old bays accumulated reef deposits
at correspondingly higher elevations and lagoonal marine sediments in the quiet waters
protected by fringing reefs The lower sea stands caused streams to carve valleys in
the sediments and reef deposits Subaerial exposure of the sediments and calcareous
materials caused consolidation of the soft deltaic materials and lagoonal deposits and
induration of the calcareous reef materials Land development and reclamation projects
within the last century have brought the project site to its present form

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions at the proposed fuel tank site were explored by
drilling coring and sampling seven borings to depths ranging from about 30 to 38 feet
below the existing ground surface The approximate locations of the borings are shown
on the Site Plan Plate 2
Based on our field exploration fills consisting of loose to dense coral gravels up
to about 1 foot below the existing ground surface were generally encountered at the
proposed fuel tank sites Below the thin cover of fills medium hard to hard
coral limestone formation was encountered in the borings extending to the maximum
depths drilled of about 30 to 38 feet below the ground surface The coral limestone
formation encountered ranged from moderately to severely jointed and ranged from
slightly to moderately weathered Some of the coral formation encountered graded to
a coral detritus which consists of coral fragments with sands and silts Based on our
field exploration it appears that cavities of about 0 6 to 2 feet in height were
encountered at depths of about 6 to 7 feet in Boring Nos 2 and 7 However it should
be emphasized that the vertical and lateral extent of cavities in the coral formation can
be highly variable
Groundwater was encountered at a depth of about 12 feet below the ground
surface in the borings at the time of our field exploration It should be noted that
groundwater conditions at the site could change due to variations in rainfall
groundwater withdrawal construction activities and other factors not apparent at the
time of our field exploration

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field exploration the subsurface conditions at the project site
consist generally of coral fragments and coral limestone formation with some cavities
Based on our evaluation of the subsurface conditions with respect to the proposed fuel
tank structures the principal geotechnical engineering considerations for the proposed
tank structures include the following
1 foundation support for the proposed tank structures
2 probing and grouting of cavities beneath the foundations and
3 trench excavation and backfill of utility lines
Based on our exploration we believe that a shallow foundation system bearing
on the coral limestone formation encountered at shallow depths or bearing on a
compacted subgrade may be used to support the fuel tank structures Due to the
strong potential for presence of cavities as encountered in our borings we recommend
that a program of probing and grouting the tank foundation locations be implemented
during construction A representative from Geolabs Hawaii should observe the probing
and grouting operation to evaluate the extent of cavities where encountered Special
attention should be given to the Foundation Probing section of this report
Detailed discussion of these items and our geotechnical engineering design
recommendations are presented in the following sections of this report

Foundations
We understand that the bottom of the new tank structures will be within 1 foot
of the existing ground surface at the site Based on the subsurface conditions
encountered we recommend that a shallow foundation system bearing on the
coral limestone formation be used to support the new fuel tank structures The walls
of the new tank structures may be supported on a perimeter ring footing embedded a
minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent grade The interior of the tank
structure and the center column may be supported directly on a subgrade prepared as
recommended below An allowable bearing pressure of up to 4 500 p s f may be used
for design of foundations bearing on the coral limestone formation and or on the
prepared subgrade This allowable bearing pressure is for dead plus live loads and
may be increased by one third 14 for transient loads such as temporary wind and or
seismic forces
Because of the potential for presence of voids and or cavities in the coral
formation below the foundations a program of probing and grouting the footprint of the
new tank structures will be required Detailed recommendations for probing and
grouting the foundations are discussed in the Foundation Probing section of this
report In addition we also recommend that the new tank subgrades be proof rolled
using a vibratory drum roller 15 ton minimum a minimum of 6 passes to aid in
detecting and or collapsing near surface cavities prior to construction of the tank
footings
We anticipate that the bottom of the tank structure will be supported on a
bedding layer of sand or similar material to provide more uniform support of the steel
tank structure We recommend that the sand bedding layer consist of a uniformly
graded sand of 4 to 6 inches in thickness The sand bedding should be compacted
using a vibratory plate to provide a firm base for the bottom plate of the tank structure
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3547 00 Page 9
Where the foundations are to be located adjacent to new or existing utilities the
foundations should extend to a depth below an imaginary 45 degree plane projected
upward from the bottom of the invert of the utility pipe This requirement is necessary
to avoid surcharging adjacent utilities with additional structural loads and to reduce the
potential for foundation settlement
If the footings are designed in accordance with the recommendations presented
herein total settlement of footings is estimated to be on the order of less than 1 inch
with differential settlements on the order of inch
Lateral loads acting on the structures may be resisted by frictional resistance
between the base of the foundation and the bearing materials and by passive earth
pressure developed against the near vertical faces of the embedded portion of footings
A coefficient of friction of 0 4 may be used for footings bearing on the coral limestone
formation and or compacted subgrade Resistance to lateral loads due to passive
pressure may be calculated using an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 pounds per
square foot per foot of depth p c f for footings embedded in the coral limestone
formation and or compacted fills This value assumes that the soil around the footings
is relatively undisturbed or well compacted Unless covered by pavements or slabs
the passive resistance in the upper 12 inches below finished grade should be
neglected
Foundation Probing
As previously indicated our field exploration encountered cavities in two of the
seven borings drilled at the site The cavities encountered in the borings were about
0 6 and 2 feet in height To reduce the potential for loss of foundation support resulting
from collapse of cavities below the tank foundation we recommend that a program of
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3547 00 Page 10
cavity probing and grouting be conducted below the footprint of the new tank structures
especially at footing locations
For preliminary estimating purposes we recommend that probes be drilled along
the perimeter ring footing spaced approximately 10 feet on center In addition we
believe that a series of probes with a spacing of about 10 feet on center should be
drilled starting at the center of the tank and radiating out to the perimeter in about
15 foot increments in radius The probe holes should be at least 3 inches in diameter
and should extend to at least 10 feet below the planned bottom of footing elevation
If cavities and or voids are encountered or suspected during the probing operation
additional probes should be drilled at closer spacings to help delineate the vertical and
lateral extent of the cavity and or void Following the drilling of each probe hole a
2 inch PVC pipe should be inserted into the probe holes to preserve the open hole
integrity for grouting A field engineer or geologist from Geolabs Hawaii should be
present to log the probe holes and to determine the locations of any additional probe
holes needed
The probe holes and any cavities discovered should be backfilled with sand
cement grout We recommend that a low strength sand cement grout mixture with an
approximately 6 to 9 inch slump range pumped at low to moderate pressures be utilized
for the grouting operation
Each probe hole should be grouted from the bottom upwards by low to moderate
pressure injection of grout through a PVC conduit which should be retracted from the
probe hole as the grout fills If the grout take is noted to exceed about 1 cubic yard in
one hole the injection should be suspended and the apparatus moved to an adjacent
hole for injection The unfilled probe hole should be marked for later reapplication of
grout The purpose for grout interruption and reintroduction is to allow the grout to set
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3547 00 Page 11
slightly prior to successive injection in an attempt to control excess migration of the
grout off site or away from the area of concern
Because of the potential for encountering large cavities and or voids at the site
we recommend that unit prices for additional probing and grouting be obtained during
bidding In addition the probe drill should be available on site until the probing and
grouting operation is complete In addition the contractor should be made aware that
a longer lag time between probing grouting operations and foundation construction may
be required in the construction schedule
The probing and grouting program should be conducted under the observation
of a representative from Geolabs Hawaii This would allow our firm to monitor the
presence of cavities and to allow additional recommendations to be made if excess
grout take and or changed conditions are observed
Site Grading
Based on the information provided we anticipate that grading at the proposed
site will consist of minor cuts and fills on the order of 1 foot or less Site grading
operations should be observed by a representative of Geolabs Hawaii to observe
whether the exposed soil conditions are similar to those encountered in our exploration
The following recommendations are intended to provide guidelines for the design of site
grading work
At the on set of earthwork all areas within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable
materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site to reduce potential
contamination of the excavated materials The soft near surface silty soils observed
at the southwest corner of the site near Tank T 201 during our field exploration as
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 should be completely removed to expose firm ground
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3547 00 Page 12
Following clearing and grubbing subgrades to receive fill should be scarified to
a depth of 8 inches where possible moisture conditioned and compacted to a
minimum of 95 percent relative compaction As previously indicated the new tank
subgrades should be proof rolled with a vibratory drum roller 15 ton minimum or
similar heavy equipment a minimum of 6 passes to aid in detecting and or collapsing
potential near surface cavities Any soft loose material disclosed during the proof
rolling operations should be over excavated and backfilled with engineered fill
compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers
to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry
density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM Test Designation
D 1557 91
Generally the on site granular materials may be used as a source of fill and
backfill materials Where used as fill and or backfill material the on site soils should
be placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness moisture conditioned
to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent
relative compaction
Any imported material should be tested and approved by Geolabs Hawaii for its
intended use prior to delivery to the job site Where needed imported granular
materials should consist of crushed coral mudrock basalt or cinder sand The material
should be well graded from coarse to fine with no particles greater than 3 inches in
largest dimension The material should have a laboratory CBR value of at least 20 and
a swell value of less than 1 percent when tested in accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 1883 The imported material should also be moisture conditioned and
compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
The finished grades outside the new tanks should be sloped to shed water away
from the foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding Drainage swales should
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3547 00 Page 13
be provided as soon as possible and maintained to drain all surface run off away from
the tank foundations
Utilities
We envision that new utility lines and or piping network will be required for the
proposed project For pipes bearing on the dense soils and coral limestone formation
encountered in the borings a poorly graded granular bedding material with little fines
such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation S4C or equivalent is
recommended below the pipes as bedding material Other alternate pipe bedding
material should consist of granular material containing minimal fines and no particles
larger than 1 inch in maximum dimension Free draining granular materials such as
No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation should be used for the trench
backfill up to about 12 inches above the pipes to provide adequate support around the
pipes and to reduce compaction of the backfill thus reducing the possibility of
damaging the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipe
to the top of the subgrade or finished grade should consist of the excavated on site
granular material containing no particles greater than 6 inches in largest dimension
Trench backfill should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture placed
in 8 inch maximum level loose lifts and mechanically compacted to not less than
90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for future ground subsidence
Where trenches are located below the fuel tank structures and pavement areas the
trench backfill should be compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling coring and
sampling seven borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 7 as shown on the Site
Plan Plate 2 The depths of our borings ranged from about 30 to 38 feet below the
existing ground surface The borings were drilled using a truck mounted drill rig
equipped with continuous flight augers and rotary coring tools
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our Geologist who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by
testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified
Soil Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representation of the
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils
by driving a 2 inch O D standard penetration sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling
30 inches The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the last 12 inches of an
18 inch drive are shown on the logs of borings at the appropriate sample depths
Core samples of rock materials encountered at the site were obtained using
diamond core drilling techniques in general accordance with ASTM Standard Practice
D 2113 83 Diamond Core Drilling for Site Investigation Core drilling is a rotary drilling
method which uses a hollow bit to cut into the rock formation The rock material left
in the hollow core of the bit is mechanically recovered for examination and description
Recovery REC is used as a subjective guide to the interpretation of the relative
quality of rock masses Recovery is defined as the actual length of material recovered
from a coring attempt versus the length of the core attempt such as if 3 7 feet of
material is recovered from a 5 0 foot core run the recovery would be 74 percent and
would be shown on the Logs of Boring as REC 74
The Rock Quality Designation RQD is also a subjective guide to the relative
quality of rock masses RQD is defined as the percentage of the core run that is sound
material in excess of 4 inches in length without any discontinuities discounting any
drilling induced fractures or breaks If 2 5 feet of sound material is recovered from a
5 0 foot core run the RQD would be 50 percent and would be shown on the Logs of
Boring as RQD 50 Generally the following is used to describe the relative quality
of the rock based on the Practical Handbook of Physical Properties of Rocks and
Minerals
