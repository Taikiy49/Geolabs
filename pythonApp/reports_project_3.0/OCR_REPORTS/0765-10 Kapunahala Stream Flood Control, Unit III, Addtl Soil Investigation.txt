SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located in Kaneohe Oahu Hawaii just
north of Likelike Highway near the intersection of Likelike
and Kahekili Highways as shown on the Site Plan Plate l
The upper western section of the alighment runs in an east
west direction toward Kahekili Highway and is in an unde
veloped field of dense tall grass banana and hau trees and
adjacent to residences to the north This section is wet
and swampy with several small streams draining near the
beginning of the alignment The ground is perpetually wet
in the upper section and small springs may exist
At Kahekili Highway the alignment runs northward parallel
to the highway until it reaches an existing drainage channel

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed flood control project consists of constructing
a new drainage structure about 1200 feet long mostly on
the western side of Kahekili Highway
The new drain will cross under Kahekili Highway and empty
into an existing open channel on the eastern side of the
highway The existing drainage channel will be improved
as part of the flood control project
Preliminary plans indicate that the new drain structure
will be a 10 by 6 feet concrete box culvert and the
existing drainage channel will be enlarged to an 18 feet
rectangular section

Soil Conditions
Based on the test borings and field observations the sub
surface conditions can be divided into 2 sections east
and west of Kahekili Highway
The upper western section generally consists of a soft
peat and highly organic clay 4 to 8 feet thick grading
into a loose to very loose silty sand to a depth of 15
to 20 feet The silty sand grades to a dense consistency
at about 20 foot depths Stiff brown clayey silt was
encountered at about 25 foot depths to the end of the
borings
The lower eastern section except the area immediately
beneath Kahekili Highway is generally underlain by a
foot layer of stiff brown silty clay underlain by a
soft gray brown alluvial silty clay layer 10 to 15 feet
thick
The area under Kahekili Highway as reflected in test
boring B 2 did not encounter any soft layers under the
highway fill However previous borings below the high
way have encountered soft alluvial deposits

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Poor subsurface soil conditions were encountered along the
project alignment particularly in the western half of the
Site where soft clays and peat over loose silty sand allu
vial deposits were encountered from near the ground surface
to below the proposed invert level In the eastern portion
the borings encountered soft clays at the invert level
High water levels were encountered in the western portion
of the proposed alignment and therefore dewatering will
be required
Due to the proximity of residences along portions of the
alignment sheeting and bracing should be used in the
trench excavations to reduce loss of ground and the
resulting settlements to the adjacent residences
Dewatering operations should also be done with care
to prevent loss of ground
The future highway embankment fills will induce tremendous
additional loads on the proposed drain line structure
Special procedures and designs will have to be incorporated
in the drain line design to allow for these additional loads

Bearing Capacity and Settlement
The subsoils encountered at the proposed invert elevation
have very low bearing capacities The normal drain line
construction would not materially increase the vertical
loading upon these soils However the planned high
fills for the future highway ramp construction would
cause large ground settlement and therefore damage
to the proposed drain line Estimated settlements of
the drain line due to the anticipated 40 foot high
embankment fill are on the order of 1 to 2 feet total
In order to reduce these large settlements due to the
high fill the soft and loose subsoils below the drain
invert should be removed and replaced with granular
material compacted to 95 of its maximum dry density
established by ASTM Test Method D 1557 Granular
material such Ae coral selected mudrock fill or
crusher waste should be used Based on the borings
and the present invert elevations the estimated depth
of this overexcavation and replacement would be about
5 feet below the drain invert from Stations 3 00 to
6 50 and about 10 feet from Station 6 50 to the end
of the embankment fill
