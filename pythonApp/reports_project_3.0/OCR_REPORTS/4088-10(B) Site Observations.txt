Site Observations
Existing Fill Slope
Niu Valley 170 Reservoir Slope Stabilization
Niu Valley Oahu Hawaii
Dear Mr Umemoto
A review of the plans for the original reservoir construction indicated that a portion
of the access road construction required a significant amount of fill The toe of the fill may
have extended close to the area that will be stabilized under the current contract A site
visit was made on to observe the present condition of the fill and to
evaluate whether revisions to the proposed stabilization project would be needed
Our observations confirmed the presence of the fill in the approximate location
indicated on the drawings It appears that the fill may be located immediately adjacent to
the proposed stabilization work The material exposed on the surface of the fill consisted
of brown clayey silt and weathered basalt gravel and cobbles The fill surface was
observed to be loose however the consistency of the remainder of the fill could not be
determined by visual observations Based on our observations it is our opinion that the
proposed stabilization work as currently designed would not be significantly affected by
the existing fill The excavations for the grouted rip rap and anchors in this area should
encounter fill instead of rock formation making the excavation easier The capacities of
the anchors in this area would be reduced however since larger boulders were not
visible on the slope the capacities may be sufficient for the smaller sized debris
anticipated
It appears that the surface of the fill has experienced some erosion due to the
rainfall over this area It may be advisable to install an erosion mat on the fill slope face
to reduce future erosion

While at the site we observed that the portion of the reservoir access road
upslope and adjacent to the old fill area seems to have experienced some long term
creep movements This is indicated by a separation of about one half inch between the
concrete curbing and the concrete pavement Although the movements are not large we
recommend that the separation be filled with caulking or grout to seal the space and
prevent surface water infiltration which could increase future movements A deeper
curbing or cutoff wall could also help to resist additional downhill movements
