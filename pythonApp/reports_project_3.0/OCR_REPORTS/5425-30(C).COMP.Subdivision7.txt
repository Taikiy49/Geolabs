As requested we provided geotechnical engineering services during the site work
construction for the Subdivision 7 portion of the project within the Kauai Lagoons Resort
in Lihue on the Island of Kauai Hawaii This letter report summarizes our geotechnical

the project only The discussions presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at
the end of this letter report
Project Considerations
The Kauai Lagoons Resort project site is within the existing Marriott Kauai Lagoons
Resort in the Nawiliwili area of Lihue on the Island of Kauai Hawaii Subdivision 7 is
situated in the area formerly planned for the Ritz Carlton Residences Townhomes site
Eleven residential pads designated as Lot 700 through Lot 710 were graded for the
project A General Site Plan showing the lot layout is provided on Plate 1
Site grading for Subdivision 7 generally commenced after substantial completion of
the Mass Grading portion of the project in July 2009 Our observation and testing report
for the Mass Grading portion of the project will be transmitted separately Our observation
and testing services during the site work construction of the Subdivision 7 project
occurred between July 2009 and August 2009 Earthworks Pacific Inc was the site work
contractor for this portion of the project

Discussion and Recommendations
Earthwork at the Subdivision 7 site generally consisted of cuts and fills of up to
about 4 feet in thickness following completion of the mass grading work The grading
plan for the project is shown on Plates 2 1 and 2 2 Fill and backfill materials generally
consisted of excavated on site materials of less than about 3 inches in maximum
dimension It should be noted that some cobbles of about 3 to 12 inches in dimension
also were placed in the fill areas In general cobbles were placed at least 2 feet below
the finished subgrades
Samples of the on site and imported fill materials were obtained and transported to
our office for testing where appropriate The maximum dry densities and optimum
moisture contents of the fill materials were established in our laboratory in general
accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test results are summarized
on Plate 3
The fill and backfill materials generally were moisture conditioned to above the
optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum
dry density determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557
During the earthwork operations periodic field density tests were performed in
general accordance with ASTM D 6938 Nuclear Methods where appropriate The
compaction test results are summarized on Plates 4 1 through 4 5 presented herein
These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and
backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and
interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed to
be unsatisfactory generally were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was
obtained and or accepted by our field representative based on visual observation and
hand probing
Based on the observations of our field representatives it is our professional
opinion that the site work operations for the above mentioned site observed and tested by
Geolabs in the period covered in this report were performed in general conformance with
the intent of the project plans and specifications and our geotechnical engineering
recommendations presented in the referenced reports and subsequent
recommendations
It should be noted that the above statement of professional opinion is based on the
grades existing at the completion of the site work operations of the project in August 2009
and as documented in the grading certification letter prepared by Central Coast
Surveyors dated August assumes no responsibility for any alterations
made to the slopes or finished grades on the above project subsequent to our observation
and testing period without our knowledge and written acknowledgment

Limitations
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork may
have been performed without our observation However the level of earthwork
observation and testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some of
the geotechnical risks to the project It should be recognized that there are variations in the
accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the
earthwork construction and materials Our services were performed in accordance with
generally accepted standards of geotechnical engineering practice no warranty is
expressed or implied Provision of construction monitoring by an engineer is not insurance
nor does it guarantee construction of any type The contractor is responsible for the quality
of the work for adhering to the plans and specifications and for repairing defects
regardless of when they are found
