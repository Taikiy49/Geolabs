INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed one story maintenance building and baseyard for vehicles at
the Molokai Airport on the island of Molokai Hawaii The general location of the project
site is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
GEOLABS HAWAII
This report summarizes findings of the exploration and our geotechnical
engineering recommendations derived from analysis of the exploration and laboratory test
data obtained These recommendations are intended for design input and not intended
to form the basis for preparation of construction cost estimates or bidding
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a light one story maintenance building with a baseyard
for vehicles at the Molokai Airport Asphaltic concrete access roads will also be provided
Other detailed project data about the proposed structure was not available at the
time of our exploration However slab on grade construction is anticipated for the
proposed building and asphaltic concrete pavement is anticipated for the proposed
baseyard and access roads
Minimal site grading is anticipated due to the existing level terrain at the project
site
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions at the project site in order to develop a generalized soil data set for the
formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to site grading
foundation slab and pavement design of the proposed maintenance building baseyard
and access roads
The work was done in general accordance with our proposal dated
to Mitsunaga Associates Inc The scope of work included
1 Review of available soil and geologic information pertinent to the project site
and its vicinity including our previous November 1989 exploration at the
earlier location of the Maintenance Building and Baseyard

observations of the surficial soil conditions and to select locations for the
borings

two on the roads to depths of approximately sixteen and one half 16 5 and
six and one half 6 5 feet below the ground surface respectively

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located at the end of a paved road approximately 800 feet to the
west of the existing terminal building The original proposed site is located southerly of
the existing car rental lease lots and in the vicinity of the existing FAA facility future RTR

facility in the airport on the island of Molokai Hawaii The present relocated project site
is situated approximately 500 feet to the southwest of the original proposed location
The site is presently an open grass area with a generally flat grade at an elevation
approximately 441 5 feet above Mean Sea Level
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions at the proposed development site were explored by
drilling four 4 borings Two of them were located within the proposed building area and
were drilled to approximately sixteen and one half 16 5 feet below the existing ground
surface The other two located on the access roads were drilled to approximately six
and one half 6 5 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate locations of
these borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general the proposed building area is underlain by approximately 14 feet of stiff
to hard silty clay over very stiff clayey silt to the maximum depth of the borings drilled
Stiff clayey silt was encountered down to the maximum depth drilled in the borings
located on the future access roads
Laboratory swell tests indicate that the near surface soils has low expansion
potential
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings at the time of our field
exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration encountered generally competent subsoils that can provide
adequate shallow foundation support for the proposed light one story building
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2672 00 Page 5
However the surface soil has some expansion potential when saturated from a dry
condition and therefore should be properly moisture conditioned prior to placement of
structures and pavement
Site Grading
A site grading plan for the subject project was not available at the time that this
report was prepared It is anticipated however that minimal site grading will be required
due to the essentially level nature of the terrain
At the onset of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All unsuitable materials such as rubbish vegetation
and other deleterious materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site
Any loose soft or yielding areas encountered at the footing slab or pavement
subgrade level should be over excavated down to in situ firm ground and the resulting
depression replaced with on site material or imported backfill compacted in 8 inch thick
loose lifts to a minimum of 95 of the maximum dry density as determined by ASTM Test
Method D 1557 78
Areas to receive structures or pavements should be scarified to a depth of six 6
inches moisture conditioned to approximately three 3 percent above its optimum
moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 95 of the maximum dry density as
determined by ASTM D 1557 78
Embankments should be constructed in horizontal lifts not to exceed eight 8
inches loose thickness moisture conditioned and compacted as described above
GEOLABS HAWAII

imported embankment or backfill materials if required should consist of non
expansive select granular materials less than three 3 inches in maximum size with less
than 20 passing the 200 sieve and should be tested and approved by the geotechnical
engineer prior to hauling to the site
All areas should be designed to direct surface run off water away from all footings
and slabs on grade
Footings
In consideration of the generally competent subsoil conditions encountered at the
site we believe that shallow spread and or continuous wall footings may be used to
support the proposed light structure
An allowable bearing capacity of up to 3500 p s f may be used for footings bearing
on the stiff in situ soils This value is for total dead plus live loads only and may be
increased by up to one third 1 3 for short duration uplift wind or seismic load
considerations
The footings should be embedded a minimum of eighteen 18 inches below the
lowest adjacent finish grade
Any material below the design elevation of the bottom of the footings which is
inadvertently disturbed during excavation should be removed and the resulting depression
backfilled with concrete placed integrally with the footing
Minimal footing settlements on the order of less than one half 14 of one inch total
and one quarter 4 of one inch differential are anticipated under the design loadings

Resistance to lateral loads acting on the proposed structure may be provided by
passive pressure and frictional resistance We recommend that an equivalent fluid
pressure of 300 p s f per foot of depth be used to calculate the passive soil pressure
which will be developed against the buried vertical faces of the foundations and a
frictional coefficient of 0 3 be used to calculate the frictional resistance which will be
developed between the bottoms of the footings and the footings subgrade
Slab On Grade
Based on the laboratory swell test results on the near surface soils we believe that
the following slab on grade section may be used provided the subgrade is properly
moisture conditioned as described herein before and kept continuously moist prior to
placement of concrete Any areas that are permitted to dry prior to slab construction
should be re scarified re wetted and recompacted
5 Inch Portland Cement Concrete Slab
6 Inch Base Course 95 Compaction
11 Inch Total Thickness
Baseyard and Access Roads Pavement
It is our understanding that the proposed baseyard and access roads will normally
serve pick up truck traffic and also a light bulldozer to support maintenance activities
Based on the strength characteristics and swelling potential of the near surface
soils it is our opinion that the following flexible pavement section may be used
2 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 Inch Base Course 95 Compaction
10 Inch Select Borrow 95 Compaction
18 Inch Total Pavement Thickness

The above pavement section is based on the assumption that the pavement
subgrades will also be moisture conditioned as described under the paragraph entitled
Site Grading presented hereinbefore Any areas that have been allowed to dry out prior
to construction of the pavements should be re scarified re wetted and re compacted
In place CBR and density tests should be performed during construction and the
section described above revised if found to be necessary
The finished grades should be designed to direct surface run off water away from
the paved areas
Design Review
Drawings and specifications for the proposed construction should be forwarded
to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comments prior to advertisement for
bids This review is needed to determine adherence to the earthwork and foundation
recommendations given herein If this review is not made the geotechnical engineer
cannot assume responsibility for misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the project geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during the site grading and foundation construction
phases of the work This is to observe compliance with the design concepts
specifications or recommendations and to expedite formulation and implementation of
appropriate design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from those
anticipated prior to the start of construction The recommendations given in this report
are contingent upon such observations

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from borings Variations of conditions between the borings may
occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident it will be
necessary to re evaluate the recommendations given in this report
The locations of the field borings at the project site were determined by taping from
the existing dirt roads indicated on an undated copy of the topographic survey provided
by Mitsunaga Associates Inc Elevations of the field borings were estimated by
interpolation from the elevations of the topographic survey map dated March 5 1991 by
Park Engineering The physical locations and elevations of the borings
should be considered accurate only to degree implied by the methods used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the borings depict the
approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual
transition The strata lines shown on any profiles or cross selections are based upon
interpolation between borings and may not represent actual subsurface conditions

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by drilling four 4
borings to depths ranging between 6 5 and 16 5 feet below the existing ground surface
The approximate boring locations are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 A truck mounted
drill rig was used to drill the borings
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who continuously monitored the drilling
operations The classifications were further reviewed by visual inspection and testing in
the laboratory All soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A summary of the materials encountered are presented on the
Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 4
Soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving a 2 inch standard split
spoon sampler or a 2 4 D split barrel sampler with a 140 pound hammer free falling 30
inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the Boring
Logs at the appropriate sample depths
