In accordance with your request we have performed a soil and
foundation investigation for the subject project The approximate
location of the project site and its vicinity is shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1 Our work was performed in general accordance with
our proposal of August 30 1984
The purpose of the investigation was to explore the subsurface
conditions at the site to provide soil data for use in the foundation
design of the proposed structure
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests which we
used in the engineering analyses to formulate our conclusions and
recommendations are presented in the appendices of this report
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is situated on the eastern side of Ford Island in
Pearl Harbor Oahu Hawaii The existing Naval Oceanographic Processing
Facility is situated between Essex Street and Hornet Avenue and consists
of a 2 story concrete structure
The proposed addition site is relatively flat and presently
landscaped
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed addition consists of an L shaped structure
approximately 28 feet wide to be attached to the eastern corner of the
existing building The addition will be a 2 story concrete structure
The maximum column loads are anticipated to be approximately 150
kips
About 4 feet of 111 is planned below the new floor slab to match
the floor level of the existing building
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The project site is located on the eastern side of Ford Island in
Pearl Harbor on the south central Coastal Plain of Oahu
A complex series of erosional and depositional events resulting
from glacio eustatic sea level changes and post erosional volcanic
activities during the Pleistocene resulted in the accumulation of
alluvial deposits in a lagoonal deltaic environment during higher stands
of the sea

Ford Island is a remnant of the hard reef rock that once covered a
larger portion of the embayment and subsequently was carved away in many
places by streams during lower stands of the sea
During the Recent epoch the sea has adjusted to its present level
and additional sediments have continued to accumulate along the lochs and
channels of the harbor Land developments in the past 40 to 60 years
have added man made fills along the shoreline

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The construction drawings indicate that the existing administration
building is supported on spread footing foundation Three of the five
previous borings drilled for the administration building encountered a
hard layer of tuff at about 5 foot depth below most of the building site
except along the eastern end Therefore the footings were designed to
bear on this 5 foot deep hard layer
The two borings drilled for the proposed addition encountered
significantly different soil conditions The hard tuff layer was not
present and instead loose sand and gravel probably fill were
encountered down to 15 feet depth The recent borings appear to
correlate with the previously obtained subsurface data which indicates
that this portion of the site was reclaimed during the 1940 s
Although the hard tuff layer was not encountered it is our opinion
that spread footing foundation may be utilized for the proposed addition
provided some amount of building settlement is tolerable
Foundations
For spread footing foundation design for the proposed addition we
recommend that the footings be embedded a minimum of 24 inches below the
lowest adjacent finished grade Where footings are adjacent to the
existing building foundations the new footings should extend down to the
same level as the existing footing
The bottom of the footing excavation should be over excavated for a
minimum depth of 12 inches and recompacted to a minimum of 95 of its
maximum dry density established by ASTM D 1557 78
Any soft soil encountered at the bottom of the footing excavation
should be over excavated to at least 2 feet below the bottom of the
footing and backfilled with compacted granular material such as base
course rock or crusher waste
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1451 00 Page 5
A maximum net allowable soil bearing pressure of 3000 p s f may be
used for the above design The bearing capacity is for total dead plus
live loads and may be increased by one third for temporary wind and
seismic loads
If a footing is situated adjacent to a utility trench the trench
backfill must be well compacted to prevent footing settlement due to
settlement of the trench backfill
To resist lateral loads a coefficient of friction of 0 4 may be
used between the foundation and recompacted fill Additionally a
passive soil pressure of 300 p c f may be used to calculate lateral
resistance
The estimated footing settlement under the anticipated column
loading may range from 0 5 to 1 0 inches Due to the granular nature of
the subsoils we anticipate that the majority of the settlements would
occur during the building construction
Up to 4 feet of 111 is planned for the building addition to match
the existing floor slab elevation Ground settlement on the order of
one half of an inch is anticipated due to the weight of the new fill
This ground settlement may affect the adjacent building foundations
depending on the actual soil conditions below the footing If the
footing are founded on tuff minimal settlement is anticipated However
if the footings are founded on loose sand some settlement may occur If
some settlement of the existing building is not tolerable a structural
floor system in lieu of the fill is recommended
The new fill should be placed as early as possible prior to
foundation construction to minimize subsequent footing settlements

Site Grading
Site Preparation
Prior to grading all deleterious materials such as vegetation
debris unsuitable fill and organically contaminated soils should
be removed and disposed of properly off site Following the
clearing the ground surface should be scarified to a depth of 6
inches moisture conditioned to optimum moisture and recompacted to
a minimum of 95 of its maximum dry density as established by ASTM
D 1557 78
Any soft weak or pumping areas disclosed during the recompaction
operations should be excavated to firm ground and backfilled with
select on site material or non expansive import fill material
compacted to the above specified density
Structural Fill Material and Placement
It is anticipated that imported fill material will be required to
raise the site to the proposed finished grade Imported fill
material should be non expansive free of organics and other
deleterious materials and generally less than 6 inches in size
The fills should be placed in horizontal lifts not exceeding 8
inches in compacted thickness and compacted to a minimum of 95 of
its maximum dry density as specified above
Site grading should provide drainage of surface runoff away from
all foundations

Retaining Walls
It is our understanding that the outside perimeter wall will act as
a retaining wall The guidelines for the retaining wall designs are
listed below
1 For lateral earth pressures the following can be used
Walls unrestrained at the top 35 p s f equivalent
active condition fluid pressure per
level backfill foot of depth
Walls restrained at the top 55 p s f equivalent
at rest condition fluid pressure per
level backfill foot of depth
2 The wall foundations should be embedded a minimum of 24 inches
below the lowest adjacent finished grade
3 An allowable bearing value of up to 3000 p s f is recommended for
foundations bearing on the recompacted on site soil
4 A passive earth resistance of 300 p s f per foot of depth may be
used for the wall footing on level areas
5 A base friction factor of 0 4 may also be used to determine the
resistance against sliding of the wall
6 A subsurface drainage system subdrains or weepholes should be
utilized behind the walls Subdrains should be properly
constructed to drain water away from the wall
Design Review
Final plans and specifications for site grading foundations and
utility construction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review
and written comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the site grading footing excavations and
backfill operations be observed by the soils engineer during construction
to see that adequate compaction has been obtained and that the subsurface
conditions are consistent with the findings of our investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
If conditions encountered during construction are different from
those presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made
