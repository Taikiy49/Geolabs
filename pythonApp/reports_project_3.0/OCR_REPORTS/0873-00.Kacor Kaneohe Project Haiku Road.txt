SUMMARY OF RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed competent
subsoils in the upper portions of the site
and variable subsoils consisting of soft
to medium stiff recent alluvium in the
lower drainageways
Conventional shallow foundations are
recommended for buildings in the upper areas
of the site Building construction in the
lower areas of the drainageways should be
delayed until the settlement gauges indicate
that the settlement rates have reduced
sufficiently
Special stabilization procedures are required
in the low southeastern portion of the site
where marshy soil conditions were encountered

The purpose of this soil engineering investigation was to
explore the subsurface soil and groundwater conditions at
the site and to provide data for use in design and con
struction of the building foundations
Specifically the scope of this investigation included the
following

SITE DESCRIPTION
As shown on Plate 1 Site Plan the development will be
located adjacent to Haiku Road and Emepela Place The pre
sent ground surface consists of two gently sloping ridges
higher areas and two valleys drainageways The first
higher area is located at the northwestern corner of the
project next to Haiku Road
The ground elevations in the area vary from about 150 to 160
feet This area is covered with grass and abandoned sugar
cane Some rubbish fill was noted in this area
W O 4
November 1979
Adjacent to the south is a relatively large drainageway
running in a west to east direction through the central por
tion of the site The bottom of the drainageway is gently
sloping with elevations ranging from about 130 to 112 feet
The side slopes of the drainageway are relatively steep with
an average slope of about 2 horizontal to 1 vertical The
bottom of the drainageway is covered with grass while much
of the side slopes are covered with trees An area of
dumped fill was noted along the northwestern portion of the
drainageway side slopes This fill included concrete rubble
asphalt abandoned cars and miscellaneous junk The approxi
mate location of this rubble fill is outlined on the Site
Plan Plate 1
The second higher ridge area is located adjacent to the
central drainageway in the southern portion of the site
This area is gently sloping down toward the east with ele
vations ranging from about 160 to 135 feet
A larger drainageway is located along the southeastern portion
of the site and joins the central drainageway Marshy wet
ground conditions including heavy vegetation and high water
condition were noted in this drainageway Elevations in this
area varied from about 105 to 110 feet

November 1979
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed development consists of 10 3 story wood framed
townhouse structures ranging in size from about 45 by 50 feet
to 85 by 160 feet in plan Most of the units will be situated
on the sloping portions of the site A recreation center complex
is planned in the upper southern portion of the site
A wooden foot bridge is planned across the central drainageway
According to the preliminary finished grades shown on the
Site Plan Plate 1 most of the site cutting will be in the
upper ridge area located in the southwestern pertion of the
site Cuts up to about 10 feet are planned Most of the fill
up to 20 feet will be placed in the lowlying southeastern
portion of the site
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The site of the development is located roughly midway between
the Pali and the coast on the windward slope of the Koolau
Range This area is part of a deeply eroded and weathered
section typified by thick alluvial deposits overlying vol
canic dike complex The extended period of erosion has also
resulted in deposition of quiet water recent alluvium in
valleys and drainageways including localized deposits of
organic silt
W O 873 00 P 6
November 1979
Soil Conditions
Based on the borings and site reconnaissance the following
three major soil conditions were encountered at the site
1 Upper ridge areas Stiff to very stiff clayey silt with
some weathered gravel Consolidated Older Alluvium were
encountered in these areas Ground water level was not
encountered in the borings
2 Central drainageway area Variable subsoils consisting
mainly of medium to stiff clayey silt with weathered
gravel and occasional soft pockets were encountered here
The groundwater level was encountered at about 3 foot
depths
3 Larger marshy drainageway Soft marshy subsoils Recent
Alluvium about 5 to 10 feet deep over stiff clayey silt
were encountered in this area Fluctuating water levels
generally from 0 to 3 feet deep were noted during the
field exploration
The water levels in the drainageways will fluctuate with
rainfall conditions at the upper valley area

CONCLUSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on the results of the field investigations laboratory
testing and engineering analyses the following conclusions
and recommendations are presented
The upper ridge areas are generally underlain by competent
subsoils Normal site grading methods and conventional
spread or continuous footing foundations are recommended
for buildings in these areas
The drainageway in the central portion of the site is
underlain by variable medium to soft subsoils Settle
ment gauges are recommended in this area to monitor the anti
cipated settlement of the proposed fill up to 15 feet
Building and utility construction should be delayed until
tolerable settlement rates are achieved Surcharging can
be used to decrease the waiting period if required
Soft subsoils and high water table were encountered in the
large marshy drainageway in the southeastern portion of the
site Special stabilization methods will be required for
filling this lowlying portion of the site Due to the anti
Cipated settlements surcharging is recommended in this area
to decrease the waiting time and future differential settle
ments of the proposed structures

The soil investigation disclosed variable soil conditions in
the central drainageway Soft pockets were noted in some of
the borings in this area In order to reduce the differential
settlements to the proposed buildings over these areas
Buildings F H I settlement gauges are recommended to
monitor the anticipated settlements of the proposed fill to
determine when building construction can begin
Surcharge
The southern end portion of Building J as it is shown on the
Site Plan is located within the soft marshy soils area in
the southeastern portion of the site To reduce differential
settlements to this building a 5 foot surcharge fill is
recommended over the entire proposed Building J site A one
month waiting period should be allowed between the completion
of site filling and surcharge placement
Settlement gauges should be installed prior to filling to
monitor the settlement rate and determine when building
and utility construction can proceed
The time required for the estimated settlement to occur are
very difficult to predict and may vary due to the various
consistency of the subsoils It is estimated that a 6
month surcharge period will be required for this area

Foundations
The proposed wood framed residential and recreational
structures except Building E may be supported by conventional
spread or continuous footing foundations
The footings should extend a minimum of 18 inches below the
finish grades Foundations located close to the top of
slopes should be embedded a sufficient depth to obtain a
5 foot minimum horizontal set back distance from the
Slope face
The foundations can be designed using the following
allowable bearing values
Very stiff natural soil up to 4000 p s f
Compacted fill over
stiff ground 2500 p s f
The bearing values for compacted fill are based on the
assumption that 90 relative compaction can be achieved
Above values of bearing capacity are for total of dead plus
live toads and may be increased by 33 when temporary lateral
loads wind and seismic are considered

Resistance to lateral loads may be provided by combination
of passive pressure and friction Passive pressure may be
taken as that exerted by a fluid of density equal to 300 p c f
Friction may be taken as normal dead load times a coefficient

Existing Sewer Line
It is our understanding that the existing 8 inch and 12 inch
sewer lines at the site will be abandoned and replaced with
a new sewer line extending into the low marshy area of the
project site The existing sewer lines are about 10 to 15
feet below the existing ground surface
To reduce the possibility of footing settlement due to loose
trench backfill we recommend that the top 5 feet of the
abandoned sewer trench be recompacted to 90 of its maximum
dry density This recompaction will be required for the
portion of the abandoned sewer lines located below the pro
posed buildings For estimating purposes a trench width of
5 feet can be assumed
New Sewer Line
Portions of the new sewer line will be located in the low
marshy area in the southeastern portion of the site
To reduce additional lateral load on the trench
excavation we recommend that the new sewer
line construction in the lowlying marshy area
be proceeded prior to mass filling operations
in this soft area

Backfill
The trench should be backfilled in accordance with the
Standard Specifications for Public Works Construction
City and County of Honolulu May 1975
Proper compaction of the trench backfill is a critical
requirement to reduce later squeezing or lateral
movement due to the new fill slope adjacent to the
realigned sewer All backfill below water level
in the trench at the time of backfilling should
consist of gravel material such as 3B fine Drier
in site soil could be used for the remaining upper
portion of the trench backfill which has to be
tamped to obtain 80 to 85 percent minimum compaction
density
Slab On Grade
It is recommended that slab on grade be placed over a
4 inch layer of base course compacted to 95 of its maximum
dry density
Slab on grade should have deep perimeter footings extending
down a minimum of 12 inches below the finish grade to reduce
ground movement due to drying and wetting around the slab
The subgrade should be kept moist and protected from drying
prior to concrete placement Subgrade should be compacted
to a minimum of 90 of maximum dry density

November 1979
Slab on grade should be separated from foundations
In order to protect against moisture damage of the flooring
material a polyethylene moisture barrier should be incor
porated within floor slabs
When a slab is located below the outside ground surface
a subdrainage system around the perimeter of the walls is
recommended to relieve seepage water
Paving
The proposed access roads and parking areas will be located
both in cut and fill It has been assumed that fill materials
will be obtained from the upper area on site soils

be provided adjacent to paved areas

APPENDIX B
LABORATORY TESTING
Moisture content unit weight and Atterberg Limit
determinations were performed on select samples as an aid in
the evaluation and classification of soil properties The
results of these tests are presented on the Boring Logs at
the appropriate sample depths
Torvane shear strength tests were performed on selected
samples The test results are presented on the Boring Logs
One direct shear was performed on a selected undisturbed
sample to evaluate the strength characteristics of the sub
soils This test result is plotted on Plate B 1
Two consolidation tests were performed on undisturbed
ring samples to evaluate the consolidation characteristics
of the soft recent alluvium materials The consolidation
curves are presented on Plates B 2 and B 3
To obtain the moisture density relationship of the upper
on site soil as a possible source of fill material two ASTM
D 1557 test Modified Proctor curves were developed and are
plotted on Plate B 4 and B 5S
Two CBR tests were performed on bulk samples of the
on site upper material to evaluate the material as a source of
borrow and pavement support The tests are summarized on
