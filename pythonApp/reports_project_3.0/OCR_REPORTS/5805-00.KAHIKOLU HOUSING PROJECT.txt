SECTION 1 0 GENERAL
1 1 Introduction
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the Kahikolu Housing project located in Waianae on the Island of Oahu
Hawaii The project location and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location
Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering
recommendations resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and
engineering analyses for the housing replacement project These recommendations are
intended for the design of foundations site preparation and pavements only The
findings and recommendations presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at
the end of this report
1 2 Project Considerations
The Kahikolu Housing project is located in Waianae on the Island of Oahu
Hawaii Based on the information provided we understand that it is proposed to
construct temporary shelters for the homeless
The construction will generally consist of one or two story wooden structures
Parking areas are also planned as part of the housing development We anticipate only
minimal site grading will be required for the site grading work for the project Key
considerations for the housing development project included the following
e Building foundation design with respect to slab on grade type
construction
e Subgrade preparation procedures with respect to the variable quality of
the near surface fills
e Development of pavement design recommendations

2 1 Site Description
The project site is located between the ends of Ala Hema Street and
Ala Akau Street in the Waianae area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The site consists of
one parcel bounded by Waianae Intermediate School to the southwest and residential
units to the southeast The parcel is irregular in shape and comprises approximately
2 5 acres Previous structures on site were recently demolished
The project site is presently open vacant land Based on the field observation
the majority of the site is blanketed with soil cover and low vegetation However the
northeast portion of the project site near the existing easements is paved with asphalt
concrete In addition an asphalt concrete walkway was observed traversing the site
Large trees were also observed in the project site The project site is generally flat We
anticipate that minimal site grading will be required to achieve the design finished grade

Based on our field exploration the project site is generally underlain by fills
overlying coralline deposits consisting of coral formation overlying coral detritus
extending to the maximum depths explored of approximately 16 5 to 21 5 feet below the
existing ground surface We did not encounter groundwater in the borings at the time of
our field exploration
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered we believe that shallow
foundations consisting of spread and or continuous footings may be used to support the
proposed new self storage building An allowable bearing pressure of up to

bearing on the recompacted on site soils or new compacted fills needed to achieve the

one third 1 3 for transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic forces

common that cavities and or voids are present in coral formation Therefore we
recommend proof rolling the fill areas and finished subgrades in cut areas with a
minimum 10 ton vibratory drum roller for a minimum of eight passes to help detect and
collapse near surface cavities and or voids

3 1 Building Foundations
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered and the relatively light
structural loads anticipated we believe that slab on grade foundations with
thickened edge footings may be used to support the proposed new structures planned
for the project Based on the generally competent subsurface soil conditions
encountered at the project site an allowable bearing pressure of up to may be
used for the design of shallow foundations bearing on the re compacted on site soils or
compacted fill material This bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may be
increased by one third 1 3 for transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic
forces
For thickened edges of the slabs bearing on the on site soils or new compacted
fills the bottom of the excavations should be recompacted to at least 90 percent relative
compaction to provide a relatively firm and smooth bearing surface prior to the
placement of reinforcing steel or concrete Soft and or loose materials encountered at
the bottom of the footing excavations should be over excavated to expose the
underlying firm materials The over excavation may be backfilled with the on site soils
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction or the bottom of footing
may extend down to bear directly on the underlying competent materials
In order to prevent water infiltration thickened edges of the slabs should be
embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grades

Footings constructed near tops of slopes or on sloping ground conditions should
be embedded deep enough to provide a minimum horizontal setback distance of 6 feet
measured from the outside edge of the footings to the face of the slope Footings
adjacent to planned or existing retaining walls should be embedded deep enough to
avoid surcharging the retaining wall foundations or the planned retaining walls should
be designed to resist the additional structural loads
If foundations are next to utility trenches or easements the footings should be

appreciable foundation settlement

new compacted fills Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be estimated using

3 2 Slabs On Grade
In general we envision that slab on grade foundations with thickened edge
footings may be used to support the new structures anticipated for the project As
previously indicated the project site is generally underlain by various fill materials
consisting of sand gravel and slightly to moderately expansive clayey soils
Slabs on grade supported by these expansive soils should be designed properly to
reduce the potential for future distress to the structures resulting from wetting and
subsequent swelling of the clayey soils
To reduce the potential for structural distress resulting from swelling of the
on site expansive soils we believe that non expansive structural fill material should be
provided for a minimum depth of 6 inches below the slab cushion
Prior to placement of the structural fill we recommend scarifying the exposed
subgrades after clearing and grubbing to a minimum depth of 8 inches where
possible moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture and
properly re compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
The subgrade soils should be maintained in a moist condition until placement of
the structural fill The structural fill material should provide a capping layer of moist soil
above the underlying potentially expansive subgrade soil thereby reducing moisture
loss from the clayey subgrade soils In our opinion the reduction of drying and
subsequent wetting of the subgrade soils should reduce the impact of shrink swell
effects on the slab caused by potential heaving of the underlying expansive clayey soils
when wetted The structural fill should be moisture conditioned placed in level loose
lifts not exceeding 8 inches and compacted to no less than 90 percent relative
compaction
Hawaii California

In addition new interior slabs should be supported on a minimum 4 inch thick
layer of cushion fill consisting of No 3 Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation or
Basaltic Termite Barrier BTB To reduce the potential for moisture infiltration a
moisture barrier should be placed above the cushion fill Flexible floor coverings such
as carpet or sheet vinyl should be considered since they can better mask any minor
slab cracking It is also recommended that the interior wall design incorporate some
flexibility to accommodate a small amount of possible ground movements
Exterior concrete walkways may be required for the proposed project We
recommend providing a minimum 6 inch layer consisting of the structural fill material
below the concrete slab The structural fill cushion layer should be compacted to no less
than 90 percent relative compaction Crack control joints should be provided at intervals
equal to the width of the walkways with expansion joints at right angle intersections
We emphasize backfilling the areas adjacent to the slabs tightly against the slab
edges with low expansion relatively impervious soils These areas should also be
graded to divert water away from the slabs and to reduce the potential for water ponding
around the slabs and foundations
3 3 Site Preparation
Based on the grading plan provided we envision that site grading work for the
project may consist of minimal cuts and fills to achieve the design finish grades
In general areas within the contract grading limits should be cleared and
grubbed thoroughly at the on set of earthwork Vegetation debris deleterious material
and other unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site to
reduce the potential for contaminating the excavated materials
After clearing and grubbing we recommend proof rolling the fill areas and
finished subgrades in cut areas with a minimum 10 ton vibratory drum roller for a
minimum of eight passes to help detect and collapse near surface cavities and or voids
The vibratory drum roller should also be operated at a speed of about 300 feet per
minute

3 4 Pavements
We understand that an asphalt concrete paved parking area is planned to serve
the new Kahikolu Housing project In general we anticipate that the vehicle loading for
the pavements will primarily consist of passenger vehicles and light pick up trucks
Based on our field exploration and laboratory test results the near surface soils appear
to have moderate CBR value with low to moderate expansion value Based on the
above assumptions we recommend using the following flexible pavement section for
this purpose
Flexible Pavements Subjected to Passenger Vehicles and Light Pick Up Trucks
2 0 Inch Asphalt Concrete
6 0 Inch Aggregate Base Course 95 Percent Relative Compaction
6 0 Inch Select Borrow Subbase Course 95 Percent Relative Compaction
14 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness over Compacted Moist Subgrade
The pavement subgrades should be scarified to a depth of about 8 inches
moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture and compacted
to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction The aggregate base course and
aggregate subbase materials should consist of crushed basaltic aggregates compacted
to no less than 95 percent relative compaction CBR and field density tests should be
performed on the actual subgrade soils encountered during construction to evaluate the
adequacy of the above sections
In addition paved areas should be sloped and drainage gradients should be
maintained to carry surface water off the site Surface water ponding should not be

3 5 Field Percolation Testing
Two falling head percolation tests were conducted in selected borings drilled at
the project site The percolation tests were performed at depths of about 5 and 10 feet
below the ground surface The percolation test results are presented in Appendix C
Based on our field percolation tests the subsurface materials generally have
coefficient of permeability k of about 2 2 x 10 and 9 5 x 10 feet per minute at the
locations tested
It should be emphasized that the hydraulic conductivity of the on site materials is
highly variable due to the presence or absence of significant voids and the continuity of
the voids in the coral formation which greatly affect the hydraulic conductivity of the
on site soils In general the greater the number and continuity of the voids encountered
the higher the overall permeability of the on site soils

SECTION 3 DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
In general we recommend using granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of
No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation for support under the pipes
Free draining granular materials such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67
gradation should also be used for the initial trench backfill up to about 12 inches above
the pipes to provide adequate support around the pipes It is critical to use this
free draining material to reduce the potential for formation of voids below the haunches
of pipes and to provide adequate support for the sides of the pipes Improper trench
backfill could result in backfill settlement and pipe damage
The upper portion of the trench backfill from a level 12 inches above the pipes to
the top of the subgrade or finished grade should consist of on site soils with a
maximum particle size of 6 inches or structural fill material The backfill should be
placed in maximum 8 inch level loose lifts and mechanically compacted to no less than
90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for appreciable future ground
subsidence Where trenches are below pavement areas the upper 3 feet of the trench
backfill below the pavement finished grade should be compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative compaction
3 7 Drainage
It should be noted that surface and subsurface drainage at the project site are
especially critical considering the moderately expansive nature of the in situ soils
Therefore the finished grades outside the new buildings should be sloped to shed water
away from the slabs and foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding around the
structure It is also advised to install gutter systems around the buildings and to divert
the discharge away from the slab and foundation areas Excessive landscape watering
near the slabs and foundations should also be avoided Planters next to foundations
should be avoided or have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce the potential for
excessive water infiltration into the subsurface
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil
saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by drilling and sampling
five borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 5 extending to depths ranging from
about 16 5 to 21 5 feet below the existing ground surface In addition we collected a bulk
sample of the near surface soils designated as Bulk 1 at a selected location at the
project site to evaluate the pavement support characteristics of the near surface soils
The approximate boring and bulk sample locations are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The borings were drilled using a truck mounted drill rig equipped with continuous flight
augers
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer who observed the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed by visual observation
and testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified
Soil Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representation of the materials
encountered is presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 5
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained in general accordance with
ASTM D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 3 inch OD Modified
California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches In addition some
samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 2 inch
OD standard penetration sampler using the same hammer and drop The blow counts
needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an 18 inch drive are
shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate
sample depths

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture Content ASTM D 2216 and Unit Weight ASTM D 2937 determinations
were performed on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of
soil properties The test results are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate
sample depths
Two Atterberg Limits tests ASTM D 4318 were performed on selected soil
samples to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits The test results are summarized on the
Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths Graphic presentation test results are
provided on Plate B 1
Three Sieve Analysis tests ASTM C 117 C 136 were performed on selected
samples of the soils to evaluate the gradation characteristics of the soils and to aid in
soil classification Graphic presentation of the grain size distributions is provided on
Plate B 2
One laboratory California Bearing Ratio test ASTM D 1883 was performed on a
bulk sample of the near surface soils to evaluate the pavement support characteristics
of the soils The test results are presented on Plate B 3
