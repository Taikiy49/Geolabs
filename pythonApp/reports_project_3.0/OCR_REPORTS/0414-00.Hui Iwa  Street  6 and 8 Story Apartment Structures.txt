APPENDIX A a
METHODS OF EXPLORATION AND LABORATORY TESTING
Exploration
The field exploration is accomplished using truck nounted rotary
drilling equipment with continuous flight hollow stem augers
The boring locations the site plan Plate 1 and the
boring logs are presented in Plates A 1 through A 9
In all borings Standard Penetration Tests were taken at selected
depth intervals not exceeding 5 feet The Standard Penetration
Test consists of driving a a inch O D split Spoon sampler 18 a
inches with a 140 pound hamner free falling a distance of 30
inches The number of blows required to drive the sampler the
last 12 inches is termed the Standard Penetration Resistance NY
and is an approximate measure of the relative density or consis
tency of a soil These resistance values are plotted on the
boring logs Samples obtained were visually classified in the
field and representative material was placed in air tight con
tainers and returned to our laboratory for inspection and testing
Classification
The field classification is verified in the laboratory also in
accordance with the Unified Soil Classification System Laboratory
classification may include visual examination Atterberg Limit
Tests and grain size distribution The final classification is
shown on the Boring Logs

Moisture Density
The field moisture content and dry 1 unit weight are determined for
each of the undisturbed soil samples The information is useful
in providing a gross picture of the soil consistency between bor a
ings and any local variations The dry unit weight is determine ed
in pounds per cubic foot and shown on the Boring Logs The
field moisture content is determined as a percentage of the dry
unit weight
Shear Tests
Shear tests are performed in the Direct Shear Machine which is of
the Strain control type The rate of deformation is approximately
0 025 inches per minute Each sample is sheared under varying
confining loads in order to determine the Coulomb shear strength
parameters cohesion and angle of internal friction Samples are
