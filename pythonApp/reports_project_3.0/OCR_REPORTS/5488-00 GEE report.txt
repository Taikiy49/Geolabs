2 1 Regional Geology
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from two shield
volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be
middle to late Pliocene in age and forms the bulk of the western third of the island The
younger shield Koolau is estimated to be late Pliocene to early Pleistocene Ice Age
and forms the majority of the eastern two thirds of the island Waianae became extinct
while Koolau was still active and its eastern flank was partially buried below Koolau
lavas banking against its eastern flank Both Waianae and Koolau Ranges have lost
most of their original shield outlines and are now long narrow ridges shaped largely by
erosion
The project area is generally composed of basaltic rock built by the extrusion of
lavas of the Koolau Volcanic Series These rocks are generally characterized by flows
of jointed dense vesicular basalt with interbedded thin clinker layers In situ chemical
weathering of the Koolau lava flows has occurred during the last 1 to 2 million years
The weathering process has formed a mantle of residual soils and saprolite In general
saprolite is composed mainly of silty material and is typical of the tropical weathering of
volcanic rocks The saprolite grades to basaltic rock formation with depth

SECTION 3 0 DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Our field exploration indicates that the project site is generally blanketed by a thin
surface fill layer consisting of low to moderately expansive clay soils of about 0 to 7 feet
thick underlain by residual soil consisting of very stiff to hard clayey silts extending to
depths of about 15 to 21 5 feet or more below the existing ground surface Weathered
basalt rock was encountered underlying the residual material in some of the borings at
depths of approximately 15 feet or more below the ground surface Remnant boulders
were also encountered in some of the borings at depths of about 10 feet or more below
the ground surface Groundwater was not encountered in the drilled borings at the time
of our field exploration
Based on the competent subsurface soil conditions encountered during our field
exploration we envision that the proposed commercial development may be supported
on a shallow foundation system consisting of spread or continuous footings
The near surface soils at the site generally consist of silty clay soils of low to
moderate plasticity Therefore the near surface soils are generally susceptible to
shrinking and swelling when subjected to changes in the soil moisture contents Unless
slabs on grade constructed above these types of plastic soils are properly designed
there is a potential for future distress to the lightly loaded slabs on grade resulting from
shrinking and swelling of the clayey soils due to changes in the moisture content As a
result we recommend that a minimum of 24 inches of non expansive select granular fill
material be provided below the building slab areas The select fill should extend beyond
the footprint of the new slab on grade areas by a minimum of 24 inches
The near surface soils at the site and in the general area are susceptible to
heaving and or loss of strength when saturated Therefore special attention should be
given to the design of drainage at the site Proper surface drainage and reduction of
water infiltration into the subsurface are integral components to the recommendations
provided in this report Because moisture conditioning and compaction of the clayey
subgrade soils and new fill materials are critical elements of earthwork observations
and soil density tests should be performed by Geolabs during site grading to assist the

3 1 Building Foundations
Based on the current grading concept we understand that fills ranging up to
about 8 to 10 feet thick may be required to raise the existing grades to the finished floor
elevation for the new building structure In addition portions of the building site may be
located over cuts on the order of about 5 to 8 feet deep
We believe that shallow spread and or continuous footings may be used to
support the new building structure at the site An allowable bearing pressure of
3 000 pounds per square foot psf may be used for the design of foundations bearing
on compacted fill It should be noted that some differential settlement should be
anticipated However we anticipate that most of the settlement would occur as the fill is
being placed and post construction settlements should be minimal
The bottom of the footing excavations should be recompacted to a minimum of
90 percent relative compaction prior to the placement of reinforcing steel or concrete If
soft and or loose materials are encountered at the bottom of the footing excavations
they should be over excavated to expose the underlying firm materials The
over excavated area may be backfilled with new fill material compacted to a minimum of
90 percent relative compaction or the bottom of footing may be extended down to bear
on competent materials at a greater depth
In general footings should be embedded a minimum of 24 inches below the
lowest adjacent finished grades Footings constructed near the tops of slopes or on
sloping ground should be embedded deep enough to provide a minimum horizontal
setback distance of 8 feet measured from the outside edge of the footings to the face of
the slope Footings located adjacent to planned or existing retaining walls should be
embedded deep enough to avoid surcharging the retaining wall foundations or the
planned retaining walls should be designed to resist the additional structural loads

If foundations are located next to utility trenches or easements they should be

Keystone Retaining Walls
Keystone retaining walls are being used for the Wal Mart store currently under
construction on the adjacent property across Kuala Street To provide aesthetic
continuity with that development consideration could be given to using Keystone
walls for this project
The Keystone retaining wall system is a composite wall system which utilizes high
density polyethylene or other reinforcing elements to reinforce the backfill zone and
improve the shear strength of the reinforced soil zone This composite system
essentially forms a gravity wall structure with an ability to tolerate significant total
and differential settlements In addition Keystone retaining walls are also desirable
due to the flexibility of the wall ease of construction high load carrying capacity
and economy

3 5 Liquefaction Potential
Liquefaction occurs when a build up of excess pore water pressure is induced on
saturated cohesionless soils resulting in a substantial loss of strength The most
common cause of this phenomenon is the cyclic loading due to earthquakes This
process may cause both vertical and horizontal movements in the soil and if not
confined can result in significant deformations Loose uniformly graded fine grained
sands and loose silts with little cohesion are most susceptible to liquefaction Generally
liquefaction is not likely to occur if the loose deposit is deeper than 50 feet below the
Hawaii California
SECTION 3 DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
ground surface The greater overburden pressure can be sufficient to prevent
liquefaction in deeper deposits
Based on our field exploration the project site is mostly underlain by very stiff to
hard cohesive soils Additionally the natural groundwater table is greater than 50 feet
below the site Therefore we believe that there is no potential for liquefaction at this
project site

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture Content ASTM D 2216 and Unit Weight ASTM D 2937
determinations were performed on selected samples as an aid in the classification and
evaluation of soil properties The results of these tests are presented on the Logs of
Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Five Atterberg Limits tests ASTM D 4318 were performed on selected soil
samples to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits The test results are summarized on the
Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths Graphic presentations of the test
results are provided on Plate B 1
Five Direct Shear tests ASTM D 3080 were performed on selected samples to
evaluate the shear strength characteristics of the materials tested Results of the Direct
Shear tests are presented on Plates B 2 through B 6
One Unconfined Compression test ASTM Test Designation D 2166 was
performed on a selected sample to evaluate the unconfined compressive strength of the
material tested The test results are summarized on Plate B 7
Eight one inch Ring Swell tests were performed on relatively undisturbed and
remolded samples to evaluate the swelling potential of the near surface soils under
different surcharge loads The results of these tests are summarized on Plate B 8
Two sets of Corrosion tests including pH AASHTO T 289 and minimum
resistivity ASTM G 57 were performed on selected soil samples obtained from our field
exploration The results of these tests are summarized on Plate B 9
