INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this investigation is to evaluate the
subsurface conditions at the site and to provide
recommendations for the design and construction of
the foundations of the proposed structure
GEOLABS HAWAII
WAILUKU ELEMENTARY SCHOOL P 2
SITE DESCRIPTION
The Wailuku Elementary School is located on the Honoa
piilani Highway about three eighth of a mile south of
Kaahumanu Avenue The site for the proposed classroom
building is located on the northwestern corner of the
school grounds The site is bordered by Koeli Street on
the west houses on the north and east and existing
concrete and wooden classroom buildings on the south
The site is presently occupied by a wooden classroom
building sidewalks and playground equipment
The site generally slopes down toward the east at about
6 percent gradients
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
The proposed structure will be a 2 story concrete and
masonry building approximately 107 by 46 feet in plan
Cuts and fills generally less than 5 feet are contemplated

Subsurface Exploration
Five borings were drilled at the site to depths of
about 15 feet below the existing ground surface to
explore the subsurface conditions and to obtain
samples for laboratory testing The approximate
locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan
Plate 1
Detailed information on the field exploration and
laboratory testing is covered in the appendix along
with the boring logs and laboratory test results
Soil Conditions
Based on the borings the site appears to be underlain
by stiff brown clayey silt down to 8 to 15 feet deep
Below this stiff mottled brown clayey silt with traces
of decomposed rock was encountered to the depths drilled
Groundwater was not noted in the borings during the field
explorations

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration indicated competent bearing soils
at the site Shallow foundations can be considered for
the proposed structure
Foundations
For the proposed structure spread or continuous footing
foundations are recommended An allowable bearing value
of 3 000 p s f may be used for footings resting on the
stiff surface soils or compacted structural fill
The bottoms of footings should be placed about 2 feet
below the finish grade
Footing located near utility trenches should extend to
the bottom of the trenches
Surface water should be diverted away from building
foundations
For the above recommendations it is estimated that
settlements will not exceed one inch total and inch
differential
Earthwork Compaction Requirements
Less than 5 feet of cut and fill are planned for the
site
GEOLABS HAWAII
WAILUKU ELEMENTARY SCHOOL P 5
Prior to filling the site should be cleared of all
vegetation debris and old foundations and sidewalks
Select on site material should be used for fill material
The fill should be placed in horizontal loose lifts of
about 8 inches and compacted to a minimum of 90 of the
maximum dry density established by ASTM Test Method
D 1557
Slab On Grade
Slab on grade should be placed over a 4 inch layer of
base course compacted to 95 of its maximum dry density
The subgrade should be kept moist and recompacted prior
to placing the base course
To protect against moisture damage of the flooring ma
terial a polyethylene moisture barrier should be used
To reduce differential settlement where the slab is
partly on cut and partly on fill the cut portion should
be over excavated 2 feet and recompacted to match the
fill density
Retaining Wall
A retaining wall less than 5 feet high and about 100 feet
long is planned along the eastern boundary of the site
GEOLABS HAWAII
WAILUKU ELEMENTARY SCHOOL P 6
Well graded granular material such as base course rock
should be used as backfill material behind the wall
An allowable bearing value of 3 000 p s f may be used
for wall foundations resting on the stiff on site soils
For lateral earth pressures against the wall an equiva
lent fluid pressure of 45 p c f can be used Additional
load due to sloping surcharge should be included where
applicable
A friction factor of 0 4 can be utilized to determine the
sliding resistance of the wall
A subsurface drainage system should be used to prevent
the build up of hydrostatic pressure behind the wall
Design Review
The plans for foundations site grading and utility construc
tion should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review
and comments prior to construction to see that the intent
of our recommendations is included in the project plans
and specifications
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during
earthwork construction to monitor the contractor s grading
operations
GEOLABS HAWAII
WAILUKU ELEMENTARY SCHOOL P 7
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be
observed by a soils engineer during construction to see
that adequate bearing has been obtained and that the
subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings
of our subsurface investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive
and should be tested and approved by the soils engineer
prior to hauling to the site

FIELD EXPLORATION

drilling 5 borings to depths of about 15 feet below the
existing ground surface The borings were drilled by
MAS Drilling Company The approximate locations of the
borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 1

visual and textural examination in the field by our
engineer who continuously monitored the drilling opera
tions The classifications were reviewed by visual
inspection and testing in the laboratory The soils
were classified in general conformance with the Unified
Soil Classification System A graphic presentation of
the soils is presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1
through A 5
In the borings soil samples were obtained by driving
either a standard split spoon sampler or a 2 4 inch
I D split barrel sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling
30 inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the last
12 inches are shown on the boring logs at the appropriate
depth
