PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Keola La i high rise residential building project is at 600 Queen Street
48 008 in the Kaka ako area of Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The
property is approximately trapezoidal in shape and encompasses approximately
117 340 square feet in land area The project site is bounded in a clockwise direction by
South Street Kawaiahao Street Emily Street and Queen Street as shown on the
Site Plan Plate 1
In general the development will consist of a 42 story high rise building providing
approximately 350 residential units The new high rise building tower structure will have
37 levels of residential floors constructed over a five level base parking structure with
commercial spaces at the ground floor level The new high rise building will have a
footprint of approximately 62 by 228 feet superimposed over the five level base parking
structure with a footprint encompassing the majority of the project site

Site grading work generally consisted of minimal cuts and fills of up to about 2 feet
thick In addition excavations extending to depths of about 6 to 8 feet deep were required
for the construction of the mat foundation The scope of work discussed in this letter
report includes observation and compaction testing during the earthwork and infrastructure
installation for the residential development
EARTHWORK OPERATIONS
Jayar Construction Inc performed the site grading operations pavement and
utility line construction for the Keola La i project The site grading work consisted of
subgrade preparation placement and compaction of fills and backfills pavement
construction and utility trench excavation and backfill
Following the demolition clearing and foundation installation operations the
excavated subgrades underneath future slab and pavement areas were scarified to a
depth of about 8 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and
recompacted to a minimum of 90 and 95 percent relative compaction under the building
slab and pavement areas respectively of the maximum dry density determined in
accordance with American Society of Testing and Material ASTM D 1557 Modified
Proctor
Fill and backfill materials required for the project generally were placed in near level
lifts brought to above the optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of 90 or
95 percent under the building slab and pavement areas respectively of the maximum dry
density determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor
During the site grading operations samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test
results are summarized on Plate 2
Field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations on an
as need basis to confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures
and soil conditions were noted A total of 260 field density tests including retests of failed
areas were performed using the nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM D 2922
and D 3017 Results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 3 1
through 3 13 of this report These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces
and on constructed fills and backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were
estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and
elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used
Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum
compaction was obtained

occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork may
have been performed without our observation However the level of earthwork
observation and testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some
of the geotechnical risks to the project It should be recognized that there are variations
in the accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor
earthwork construction and materials
