INTRODUCTION
This report presents the result of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed residence for Ms Lynn Fukumoto at 1051 Lolena Place in
Honolulu Oahu Hawaii The general location of the site and its vicinity is shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1 The field exploration was performed on July 15 and 16
1991 in accordance with our proposal dated
This report summarizes our findings resulting from the exploration and geotechnical
engineering recommendations derived from our analysis based on the data obtained
during our expioration These recommendations are intended for design input and are
not intended to form the basis for preparation of construction estimates or bidding
purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
Based on the drawings provided to us the proposed new residence will be a split
level structure approximately thirty 30 by eighty seven 87 feet in overall plan
dimensions Itis anticipated that the residence will be of wood and masonry construction
Both continuous and spread footing type foundation construction is planned The sloping
nature of the terrain will result in both slab on grade and structurally designed slabs for
the floor construction
A retaining wall approximately thirteen and one half 13 5 feet in height is planned
near the central portion of the structure to support the northerly portion of the lower floor
level

SITE DESCRIPTION
The subject lot is located in Kamehameha Heights on the island of Oahu
The 5 337 square feet lot is generally rectangular in plan and is presently vacant
The lot slopes downwards from Lolena Place with a slope of approximately four horizontal
to one vertical 4H 1V in the southerly direction with ground elevations ranging from 202
to 174 above Mean Sea Level A 1V slope approximately five 5 feet in height
is located within the southern half of the lot

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration generally encountered stiff soils near the surface It is our
opinion that the stiff on site soils may provide adequate bearing support for the proposed
residence However our laboratory testing indicates that these clayey soils have variable
expansion potential ranging from low to moderate Therefore to reduce potential distress
to the proposed structure caused by expansion of the subsoils it is recommended that
twelve 12 inches of select granular material be provided below all foundation and slab
construction bearing on the clayey surface soils
A rock outcrop was noted at the ground surface near the west central portion of
the site as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 Hard excavation may be anticipated in this
area

Lot Grading
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
cleared of vegetation debris rubbish and other deleterious materials These materials
should be removed and disposed of properly off site Any loose or soft spots
encountered at the subgrade areas should be over excavated down to the firm subsoils
and then replaced with compacted non expansive select material
Areas in cut should be graded to the design subgrade elevation and then scarified
to a depth of six 6 inches moisture conditioned and recompacted to at least 90 percent
of the maximum dry density as determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
The subgrade soil and backfills within the top twelve 12 inches below the footings
and on grade slabs bearing on the clayey surface soils should consist of non expansive
soils such as select borrow crusher waste or coral For footings and on grade slabs
bearing on the saprolite or basalt generally at depths greater than five 5 feet the
above requirement may be eliminated
All imported fill material should be tested for suitability by the project geotechnical
engineer prior to delivery to the site
The fill should be placed in maximum eight 8 inch thick loose level lifts and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of maximum dry density as determined by ASTM
Test Method D 1557 78

Slabs On Grade
It should be noted that the on site silty clay soils may be moderately expansive if
allowed to dry and then saturated Therefore the existing surface soils should either be
filled over or over excavated below the slab bottom to a depth sufficient to provide a
minimum twelve 12 inch capping layer of imported non expansive soil between the slab
and the natural ground
The thickened edge of the slab should be embedded a minimum of eighteen 18
inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade
To further reduce the potential for future post construction swelling of the
underlying expansive soils we recommend that the in place silty clay below the upper
capping fill pad be kept moist during construction and prior to placement of the concrete
A four 4 inch cushion layer composed of No 3 Fine gravel or ASTM C 33 No 67
should be provided below slabs In order to protect against future moisture infiltration
an impervious moisture barrier may be incorporated below the slab

Concrete Driveway
It is anticipated that concrete pavement will be used for the new driveway
Based on laboratory tests the following pavement section may be utilized
Concrete Pavement Driveway
6 Inches Portland Cement Concrete
4 Inches Aggregate Base Course
10 Inches Total Pavement Thickness
Additional laboratory tests should be performed on the actual subgrade soils at the
time of construction to confirm the above design
The recommended section is based on light traffic and that good drainage will be
provided adjacent to the paved areas
Joints are also recommended in the driveway slab at about eight 8 to ten
10 foot intervals and at discontinuities in order to allow for some relative movement
of the concrete due to shrinkage of the concrete uneven stress concentration and or
soil movement

Walkways
Although no walkways per se are shown on the plans provided to us it is assumed
that some will be required Portland cement concrete is suggested for this purpose with
a thickness of four 4 inches Grooved contraction joints should be formed in the
concrete prior to initial set at intervals of approximately four 4 feet Expansion joints
should be provided at twenty 20 foot spacing where the walkways abut other structures
and at abrupt changes in the direction of the walkways
All walkways should be placed on a four 4 inch thick bed of compacted granular
material
Drainage
The finish lot grade outside the slab footings and pavement should be shaped to
shed water away from foundations and to avoid ponding conditions Also it is advised
that gutter systems be installed around the house and that gutter water should be
diverted as far away from the foundations and driveways as feasible Excessive
landscape watering near foundations and slabs should be also be avoided
Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have concrete bottoms and
drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled tight against the walls or
footings or slabs immediately after setting of the concrete to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and maintained to drain
all surface run off away from the footing foundations and slabs

Design Review
Drawings and specifications for the proposed construction should be forwarded
to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comments prior to construction This
review is needed to determine adherence to the earthwork and _ foundation
recommendations given herein If this review is not made the geotechnical engineer
cannot assume responsibility for misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to provided
geotechnical engineering services during site grading and construction of foundations
This is to observe compliance with the design concepts specifications or
recommendations and to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions
differ from that anticipated at the time of preparation of this report The recommendations
given in this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and approved by
the geotechnical engineer prior to delivery to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during construction are different
from those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made
