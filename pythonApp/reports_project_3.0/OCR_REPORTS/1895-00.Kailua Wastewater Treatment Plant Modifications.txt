INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the proposed modifications of the existing
Kailua Wastewater Treatment Plant
The general location of the project site and its vicinity is shown on
the Project Location Map Plate 1
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this geotechnical engineering exploration was to
explore the subsurface soil and groundwater conditions at the site in
order to provide soil data for use in the foundation design of the various
new structures of the proposed modifications project
The following items were of particular concern during the
exploration
1 Presence of soft ground conditions which would consolidate under
the weight of the new building loads or new fill if any
2 Depth of competent bearing stratum for pile foundation if
necessary
3 Depth of groundwater level which would be required for evaluation
of possible dewatering and shoring data difficulties for deep
excavation

used in the engineering analysis to formulate our conclusions and

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located in the existing 25 acre Kailua Wastewater
Treatment Plant to the south of Kaneohe Bay Drive in Kailua on the island
of Oahu Hawaii The treatment plant also lies adjacent and south of a
series of ponds connecting Kaneohe Bay and Kailua Bay
The plant consists of a variety of existing structures including a
sewage pump station screening chamber clarifiers trickling filter
sludge tanks digestor control building chlorination building effluent
pump station centrifuge building sludge drying beds and maintenance
building
The topography of the plant site slopes gently down from about
elevation 36 feet above Mean Sea Level MSL at the south end adjacent to
Kaneohe Bay Drive to about elevation 2 feet towards the north end near
the existing chlorination building Adjacent ground elevations of the
existing buildings structures range from about 4 to 25 feet

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The project site is located at the base of the northeastern slope of
Puu Papoa which is essentially an erosional remnant of caldera filling
lava flows of the Koolau Volcano
After the Koolau Volcano ceased its activity it was followed by a
period of erosion and subsequent submergence during which most of the
eastern flank of the volcano was removed later glacio eustatic
sea level changes during the Pleistocene resulted in the accumulation of
several hundred feet of marine and non marine sediments
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1895 00 Page 6
Dk No 40
Our field exploration at the site indicated that the southwestern
upper portion of the site is underlain by highly weathered basalt and
saprolite soils derived from the Koolau Volcano flows while the central
and the northeastern lower portions of the site are underlain by
interbedded sediments of both detrital marine and alluvial origins
During the past century the efforts of man have shaped the land to
its present form by placement of fill
Subsoil Conditions
The subsurface soil conditions at the project site were explored by
drilling and sampling sixteen 16 borings to depths of about 16 to 80
feet below the existing ground surface
The drilled borings encountered rather complex subsoil conditions
which ranged from the better subsoils underlying the southwestern slopes
near Kaneohe Bay Drive to the poorer subsoils underlying the lower areas
at the central and the northeastern portions of the site
At the southwestern higher ground of the site where the proposed new
primary sedimentation tanks will be located a thin fill layer over hard
saprolite and weathered basalt formation was encountered at shallow depth
Towards the north end of the new primary sedimentation tank location
the surface fills are underlain by expansive adobe clay Further
downslope towards the central and northeastern parts of the site where
the other proposed new structures will be located the adobe Clay layer is
interbedded with and or underlain by compressible lagoonal deposits and
coral reef detritus over relatively incompressible saprolite and
weathered basalt formation at greater depths

The adobe clay varies rather erratically in consistency from very
soft to very stiff but generally grades softer and increases in thickness
towards the north Moreover the adobe clay is either submerged below or
at close proximity to the groundwater level thus the clay is generally
saturated and has limited expansion potential under the overburden of the
existing capping fills
The other compressible layers also vary in thicknesses and in
consistency from loose to medium dense
The hard saprolite and weathered basalt formation generally increase
in depth towards the north and was not encountered at more than 80 feet
below the proposed and 4 and Digestor Control Building
location

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATION
General
The field exploration at the project site encountered complex subsoil
conditions which ranged from competent hard saprolite and weathered
basalt formation at the upper areas to poorer subsoil condition consisting
of compressible layers of soft adobe clay loose lagoonal deposits and
coral reef detritus at the lower areas
For structures with shallow embedment or built approximately on the
existing grades we believe that a footing foundation may be used For
structures deeply embedded with their bottoms submerged below groundwater
level a mat foundation may be used
GEOLABS HAWAII

Braced deep excavation is anticipated for constructing the deeply
embedded building structures and installing the new sewer drainlines A
proper efficient dewatering system is required to maintain the stability
and safety of the deep excavation during construction
Foundation
Based on the soil conditions and the present design finished floor
elevations the detailed discussion and recommendations for the foundation
system of each of the proposed new structures are presented below
Expansive adobe clay was encountered at shallow depths near the
existing groundwater table in most areas of the project site For the
structures with finished floor elevations below the ground water table
the expansive potential of the clay is small due to the saturated
conditions of the soil For the structures with finished floor elevations
above the groundwater table the effect of the expansive clay on the
structure foundations will depend on the finished floor elevations
Therefore if the new structures are constructed at or above the present
grades it appears that there would be sufficient thickness of capping
fills over the clay to reduce the swelling effect on the new structures
However if the finished floor elevations are placed a few feet below the
present ground surface closer to the expansive clay overexcavation of
the expansive clay may be required below the new structures to provide a
2 foot minimum thickness of non expansive capping fill
It is thus essential that at the final design stage the soil
engineer should be notified as soon as possible should the present design
finished floor elevations of the structures be subsequently changed so
that additional recommendations could be made to support any necessary
foundation design modifications

Excavation Support
ee a
We recommend that the deep excavation walls in the soft loose
subsoils be supported by sheetpiling It is important that adequate
sheeting be installed prior to excavation and maintained tight
against the excavation walls with proper bracing during excavation
The properly braced sheeting is essential to reduce lateral movements
of the adjacent ground into the excavation which may cause
settlements of the adjacent structures This is particularly
critical for this project since the existing structures are supported
on spread footings or mat foundation bearing on the upper soft and
loose soils
The excavation should be provided with proper bracing in accordance
with the anticipated loading conditions construction traffic
materials stockpile and equipment and to satisfy the applicable
safety requirements The design and safety of the support system s
should be the responsibility of the contractor The apparent earth
pressure diagram presented on Plate 3 may be used as a guide for
construction cost estimate purpose and for preliminary design of the
excavation support system s

The sheeting should extend a sufficient depth below the trench bottom
to increase the stability of the excavation bottom and to reduce the
possibility of bottom heaving or quick conditions Based on our
findings a minimum of 5 feet embedment below the excavation bottom
may be required
The ground adjacent to and near the top of the excavation should be
continuously monitored for cracks or dips other indications of

Such monitoring is particularly critical in areas where existing

The bottom of the deep excavation is anticipated to be close to or
below the groundwater table Therefore a proper efficient
dewatering system is required to maintain the stability and safety of
the deep excavation
The method of dewatering should be left open to the contractor
provided adequate stability of the excavation is maintained The
subsoils encountered in the borings consisted mostly of clays
interbedded with lagoonal sand and gravel and some coral reef
detritus within the anticipated depths of excavation The lagoonal
and coral reef detritus sand and gravel are relatively permeable and
where encountered at the excavation bottom will be susceptible to
piping and quick conditions
The project dewatering operation should be done without pumping out
the soil fines pumping clear water only and should be coordinated
with the excavation shoring support so that the stability of the
excavation is not jeopardized The operation should be carried out

The stress distribution against the bottom of a pipe has a great
effect upon the load supporting capacity of the pipe Therefore the
pipe bedding is an important design consideration
Based on the subsoil conditions the anticipated pipe invert
elevations may lie within soft adobe clay or loose lagoonal
deposits coral reef detritus
Therefore a minimum of six 6 inches of compacted granular bedding
material should be placed to provide uniform support below the pipe
The granular bedding should extend beyond the pipe sides a minimm
width of one fourth the outside pipe diameter and should extend half
way up the pipe sides The bedding material should consist of clean
granular material such as 3B Fine gravel If soft subgrade
conditions are encountered the bedding thickness should be increased
to 18 inches

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
ee
Moisture content unit weight and Atterberg Limits determinations
were performed on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification
and evaluation of soil properties The results of these tests are
presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Fourteen 14 one inch ring swell tests were performed on natural and
remolded samples to evaluate the swelling potential of the existing near
surface soils The test results are summarized on Plates B 1 and B 2
Four 4 Atterberg Limits tests were performed on selected samples of
the soils to evaluate their liquid and plastic limits The test results
are also shown on Plates B 3 through B 5
Six 6 sieve analyses were performed on selected samples to
determine the gradation characteristics of the soils The test results
are summarized on Plates B 3 and B 4
One 1 unconsolidated undrained triaxial test was performed on a
selected soil sample to evaluate its undrained shear strength
characteristics The test results are presented on Plate B 6
Six 6 direct shear tests were performed on selected soil samples to
evaluate their shear strength parameters The test results are presented
on Plates B 7 through B 12
Five 5 consolidation tests were performed on selected samples of
the soils to evaluate their compressibility The test results are plotted
on Plates B 13 and B 17
