PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the electrical resistivity
of the subsurface soils at the site Our work on this project was performed in general
conformance with ASTM Test Designation G 57 78 and with the requirements provided
in the Water System External Corrosion Control Standards Volume 3 dated 1991 and
subsequent amendments and additions
The scope of our work consisted of the following work efforts
1 Mobilization Demobilization of equipment and engineers to the site
2 Performance of in situ soil resistivity tests by Engineers from our firm at 13
road stations along the side of Geiger Road extension These stations
generally correspond to the 300 foot spacing required in the Water System
External Corrosion Control Standards Volume 3 1991
3 Preparation of this letter report summarizing our work on the project
4 Coordination of our overall work on the project and client design team
consultation by a Project Manager of our firm

Field Exploration
Geophysical survey equipment i e resistance meter was mobilized to the site on
October 21st Our fieldwork was then performed on sunny days with daily high
temperatures of approximately 87 F and a generally dry surface condition The ground
surface along the road side was generally hard to penetrate however this was not
unusual when compared to conditions encountered on previous resistivity surveys within
the development
Twenty eight soil resistivity surveys were conducted at 13 locations along the side
of Geiger Road using the Wenner four pin method with a resistance meter Strata Scout
R 40C manufactured by SoilTest Inc The resistance meter has a capacity of 120 volts
voltage output and 24 watts power output into the ground This method utilizes four
surface probes electrodes to evaluate the average resistivity an indication of corrosivity
of the underlying soil mass Therefore the average resistivity of the soil mass extending
from the existing ground surface to the planned invert elevation of the water line was
assessed by this method at the road stations listed on Plates 3 a and 3 b
The average soil resistance was evaluated at road stations about 300 feet apart
staked out for us by Walter P Thompson Inc There are numerous existing utility lines
in the area of the planned water line In order to reduce the potential influences from
existing underground utility lines the resistivity surveys were performed at certain offset
distances from the outermost existing utility line The offset distances were approximately
equal to the depths of the proposed pipe invert from the existing grade The approximate

The resistance meter R 40C impresses a voltage between the outer electrodes
causing an electrical current to flow between them and the voltage drop between the
inner electrodes is then measured by a sensitive built in voltmeter The soil resistance
Ohm can be found directly on the dial The soil resistivity Ohm cm which is directly
related to both resistance and spacing can then be computed The evaluated soil
resistances the computed resistivities and the corresponding corrosion rating are
presented on Plates 3 a and 3 b of this report
Resistivity is generally recognized as one of the most significant soil characteristic
with regard to the corrosivity of the soil In general the lower the resistivity the greater
the potential for corrosion of buried metallic structures Conversely the higher the
resistivity the less likely the soil will contribute to corrosion
LIMITATIONS
The evaluations presented in this report are based upon information obtained from
field data points by the means of geophysical surveys Variations of soil conditions
between the field data points may occur and the nature and extent of such variations
may not become evident until the soil is excavated
Locations and elevations of the data presented in this report are approximate
having been scaled and interpolated from the Preliminary Plans prepared by
Gentry Hawaii dated August 4 1992 Locations and elevations of the data presented
should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used
