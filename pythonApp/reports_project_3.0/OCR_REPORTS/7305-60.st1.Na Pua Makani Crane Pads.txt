Dear Mr. Pickett: 
In accordance with your request, we observed the earthwork construction and 
performed dynamic cone penetrometer (DCP) tests at the proposed crane pads of the 
Na Pua Makani Wind project site on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. This letter report 
presents our observations and findings. It should be noted that our observations and 
findings provided are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this letter report. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The project involves a wind farm facility in the Kahuku area on the Island of Oahu, 
Hawaii.  A total of eight Wind Turbine Generators (WTG), designated as WTG-1 through 
WTG-4 and WTG-6 through WTG-9, will be constructed at the site. We understand that 
it was desired to observe the earthwork construction of the crane pads at the WTG sites.  
In addition, performance of DCP tests were desired at the crane pads. 
EARTHWORK OBSERVATION 
Our earthwork observation services during the crane pad construction of the 
project occurred between August 7 and August 12, 2019.  The crane pads were 
generally constructed by placing on-site coralline materials consisting of sandy gravel 
with some silt.  The on-site coralline materials were generally moisture-conditioned and 
compacted with a loaded dump truck and/or vibratory drum roller.  The compacted 
surface was generally firm and unyielding.  Our earthwork observation reports are 
provided  in  Appendix A. 
Based on the observations of our field representatives, it is our opinion that the 
earthwork operations during the crane pad construction of the project observed by 
Geolabs in the period covered by this report were performed in general accordance with 
the intent of the recommendations presented in the Crane Mat Evaluation letter dated 
August 20, 2019 by Westwood Professional Services.

DCP TESTING 
A total of 35 DCP tests, designated as DCP-1a through DCP-8e, were 
conducted at the crane pad locations.  Five DCP tests were conducted at WTG-1 
through WTG-4, and WTG-6 through WTG-8.  It should be noted that DCP tests 
were not performed at the WTG-9 crane pad.  At each WTG location, DCP tests 
were conducted near the center and each corners of the crane pads.  The 
results of the DCP testing are presented in the Appendix B. 
DCP tests are typically used to assess material properties down to a depth of up 
to about 48 inches below the surface.  The 17.6-pound DCP can be used to estimate 
the strength characteristics of fine and coarse-grained soils, granular construction 
materials and weakly stabilized or modified materials. The 17.6-pound DCP generally 
cannot be used in highly stabilized or cemented materials or for granular materials 
containing a large percentage of aggregates greater than 2 inches. 
Based on the results of our DCP tests, it is our opinion that the crane pad subgrades 
tested meet the general requirements presented in the Crane Mat Evaluation letter dated 
August 20, 2019 by Westwood Professional Services.
