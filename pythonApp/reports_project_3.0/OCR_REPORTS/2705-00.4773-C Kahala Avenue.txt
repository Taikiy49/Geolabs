INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of a geotechnical engineering exploration and
analysis performed for the proposed Kobari Residence Il located at 4773 C Kahala
Avenue in the Kahala district on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location of the
site and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
Based on the schematic drawings provided to us the proposed residence will
consist of a two 2 story masonry building with a swimming pool and vehicular driveway
It is our understanding that the ground floor will be a concrete slab on grade and
cast in place concrete for the second story floors is anticipated for the proposed two 2
story building
The site is essentially level cut and fill operations for site grading will be minimal
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions within the project site in order to develop a generalized soils rock data set for
the formulation of geotechnical engineering design recommendations pertinent to the
proposed construction
The work was done generally in accordance with our fee proposal to Hazama
Corporation dated May 24 1991 The scope of work included the following tasks and
work efforts
1 Review of available soils and geologic information pertinent to the project
site and its vicinity
2 Reconnaissance of the project site and its vicinity to make visual observa
tions of the surface soil conditions
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2705 00 Page 3
3 Mobilization and demobilization of a truck mounted drill rig to and from the
project site
4 Drilling and sampling of three 8 borings to depths of 21 5 to 22 0 feet
below the existing ground surface using auger drilling techniques
5 Laboratory testing of selected samples obtained during the field exploration
to determine the classification and engineering properties of the materials
encountered
6 Engineering analyses of the field and laboratory data for the purpose of
developing geotechnical engineering design recommendations
7 Preparation of this report summarizing our findings and presenting our
recommendations
Detailed description of our field exploration and laboratory testing are presented
in the appendices of this report

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
It is our opinion that the proposed project is feasible from a geotechnical
engineering point of view provided that the recommendations presented below are
followed
Foundations
Shallow spread and continuous footing foundations may be used to support the
proposed structure
An allowable bearing value of 2 000 p s f can be used for footings bearing on the
compacted in situ beach sand or compacted engineered embankment A footing
embedment of twelve 12 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade is recommenda
ed Deeper footing embedment is generally not recommended due to the high
groundwater level encountered in the drilled borings
It is recommended that all footing excavations should be compacted to a minimum
of 90 of the maximum dry density as determined in accordance with ASTM Test Method
D 1557 78
If loose sand lenses or pockets are encountered at the bottoms of footing
excavations they should be over excavated to dense in situ sand The resulting
depression should then be backfilled with select granular material compacted to a
minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density as determined in accordance with ASTM
Test Method D 1557 78
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2705 00 Page 6
If a footing is located next to a utility line the utility trench backfill should be
carefully monitored and adequately compacted to minimize the potential for footing
settlement due to consolidation of the trench backfill
Surface water should be diverted away from the foundations to avoid ponding of
surface runoff water that may adversely affect the bearing capacity of the soils below the
foundations
Total footing settlement for column loads up to fifty 50 kips is estimated to be less
than one 1 inch provided the above recommendations are followed
Slab On Grade
The ground floor of the proposed residence is anticipated to be concrete
slab on grade
A four 4 inch thick concrete slab on top of a minimum four 4 inch thick cushion
layer of No 3B Fine gravel or ASTM C 33 No 67 gravel is recommended In order to
protect against moisture infiltration a plastic vapor barrier should be incorporated below
the slab One 1 inch of moist fine sand may be placed above the plastic membrane to
help curing of the concrete
The thickened edges of the concrete slab should be embedded a minimum of
twelve 12 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade
Concrete Driveway
It is anticipated that concrete pavements will be provided for the driveway of the
proposed residence

Swimming Pool
A swimming pool is to be constructed at the southeasterly side of the proposed
residence It is our opinion that substantial dewatering would be required if the proposed
swimming pool bottom was to locate below the groundwater level due to the fact that the
on site material is highly permeable The groundwater level was encountered in our
drilled borings at depths of three and one half 3 5 to five 5 feet below the existing
ground surface Based on elevations estimated from the available topographic survey
map the measured groundwater levels ranged from elevations of about two tenths 0 2
to one 1 0 feet above MSL at the time of our field exploration Shoring of the excavation
will therefore be required for the proposed swimming pool construction

The swimming pool design should include provision for resistance against
hydrostatic uplift during periods when the partially submerged swimming pool is emptied
for maintenance purposes A minimum groundwater level elevation of two 2 feet above
MSL is recommended for design purposes
Considering the effect of backfilling the following lateral earth pressures may be
considered for swimming pool wall design

above groundwater level fluid pressure
level backfill per foot of depth
Walls restrained at the top s 20 p s f equivalent
below groundwater level fluid pressure
level backfill per foot of depth
not including hydrostatic pressure
Additional lateral earth pressures due to adjacent surcharge or foundation loads
should be considered where applicable
Scheduling of construction of the swimming pool should be considered as it relates
to the schedule for foundation construction We feel that the swimming pool should be
constructed prior to construction of spread footings and on grade slabs in the immediate
vicinity to preclude possible loss of support of those footings caused by excavation and
dewatering for construction of the swimming pool
It is recommended that the backfill behind the swimming pool walls should consist
of compacted granular material such as select borrow or No 3B Fine gravel or ASTM
C 33 No 67 gravel compacted to a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density
determined by ASTM D 1557 78

Site Grading
Prior to construction the site should be cleared and grubbed Any deleterious
materials such as vegetation debris rubble and organic matter encountered during
grubbing and scarifying should be removed and properly disposed of off site
The proposed building and pavement areas should then be scarified to a depth of
six 6 inches The scarified subgrade should be thoroughly moisture conditioned and
recompacted to obtain a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density as determined in
accordance with ASTM D 1557 78 To minimize disturbance to adjacent existing
residences and to minimize pore water pressure build up in sandy subsoils causing
possible soil weakening use of heavy vibratory compaction equipment should be
avoided
Any imported fill and backfill material that may be required should be tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to being transported to the site
The fills should be placed in loose horizontal lifts not exceeding ten 10 inches
loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density as
determined by ASTM D 1557 78
The site should be graded to provide drainage of surface runoff away from all
foundations
Drainage
The finished lot grade outside the footings slabs and pavements should be
shaped to shed water away from foundations to avoid ponding conditions Also it is
advised that a gutter system be installed around the residence and that the gutter run off
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2705 00 Page 10
water be diverted as far away from the foundations and pavements as feasible Excessive
landscape watering near the foundations should also be avoided
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have concrete bottoms and
drains to prevent water from entering the foundation and slab subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the above
recommended foundation construction since ponded water could cause excessive
subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation
excavations should be properly backfilled tight against the footings and slabs immediately
after setting of the concrete to prevent ponding conditions
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained to drain
all surface run off away from the foundations and slabs
Design Review
Drawings and specifications for site grading utility and foundation construction
should be forwarded to the project geotechnical engineer for review and written
comments prior to advertisement for bids This review is needed to determine adherence
to the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this review is not
made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for misinterpretation of the
recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the footing excavations and backfilling be observed by a
competent geotechnical engineer during construction to see that adequate bearing has
been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of our
exploration
GEOLABS HAWAII

Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and approved by
the soils engineer prior to being transported to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the above
observations
LIMITATIONS
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations made in
accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation engineering principles and
practices This warranty is in lieu of all other warranties either expressed or implied
This report has been prepared in order to assist the architect and engineer in the
preparation of the foundation design of this project Therefore this report may not
contain sufficient data or the proper information for use as the basis for preparation of
construction cost estimates or bids A contractor wishing to bid on this project is urged
to retain a competent geotechnical engineer to assist in the interpretation of this report
and or performance of site specific exploration for bid estimating purposes
The locations of the field borings are approximate having been determined by
taping from structures shown on the topographic surveyor map prepared by Control Point
Surveying and Engineering Inc dated The elevations of the field borings are
estimated from the elevations and contours shown on the same map The physical
locations and elevations of the field borings should be considered accurate only to the
degree implied by the method used
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from field borings Variations of conditions between the field borings

may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident until
construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re
evaluate the recommendations given in this report
The owner client should be aware that unanticipated soil conditions are commonly

hard layers or cavities may occur in localized areas and may require additional probing

constructed project Therefore a sufficient contingency fund is recommended to
