This report presents the results of our subsurface grouting
performed for the subject project The approximate location of the
project site and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map
Plate 1
Our work was performed in general accordance with our proposal of
March 2 1987
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our field work was to explore the subsurface
conditions at two affected areas that have subsided and to attempt to
pressure grout at selected depths to strengthen the soils around and
below the catch basin boxes using a zone grouting method

The approximate locations of the grout holes and borings are shown on
the Site Plans Plates 2 and 3
Damaged Areas
Generally the visible evidence of distress is along the curb line
near the shallow catch basin box drain and in the pavement around the
large box drain which is much deeper
Other minor settlements probably occurred along the sidewalk and
adjacent property walls
Soil Conditions
One boring was drilled at each affected area to explore the
subsurface conditions to evaluate the suitability of grouting the loose
soils
The soil conditions at the two areas previously drilled were as
Three to five inches of A C pavement over about 3 feet of stiff
brown silty clay underlain by soft or loose reddish brown silty clay
from about 3 to 18 foot depth at Station 2 54 and 3 to 23 foot depth
at Station 6 82 Below the soft and loose zone was stiff residual
soils
Groundwater was not encountered in the two borings during our field
exploration

Grouting Method
1
Tube a Manchette Method Station 6 82
On March 16th the manchette grout method was begun at one grout
probe No 6
The Tube a manchette method allows the grouter more complete
control over where the grout enters the formation by pumping the
grout mix into 2 inch PVC pipes that were drilled with 3 8 inch
holes spaced at 3 foot intervals and installed between depths of
3 to 23 feet below the bottom of the existing ground The rubber
cover was placed over the holes at each interval spacing The
bottom of the pipe was capped off then the annulus around the
pipe was filled with a mixture of bentonite and cement This is
allowed to set up then a cement slurry mix was pumped into a
straddle double packer system that isolates the grout pumped at
each selected level
Between March 17th and 19th the remaining seven 7 manchette
pipes were pumped with cement slurry

DISCUSSION AND OBSERVATIONS
The injection grouting utilizing the tube a manchette system at
Station 6 82 and the open pipe system at Station 2 54 indicates that the
underlying soils were soft and probably contained voids or loose seams
The volumes of cement slurry pumped at the two sites should help to
densify the soil mass somewhat and hopefully will help to stabilize the
soils reduce future settlements underlying the two catch basins
LIMITATIONS
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations
made in accordance with generally accepted soil engineering principles and
practices This warranty is in lieu of all other warranties either
expressed or implied
Although the grouting should help to reduce the compressibility of
the loose subsoils it is not possible to guarantee that further ground
settlements will not occur
