Probing and Grouting Report
Truck Wash Facility
Honouliuli Wastewater Treatment Plant
Dear Mr Tamayo
This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations performed
during the probing and grouting operations for the Truck Wash Facility of the Honouliuli
Wastewater Treatment Plant on
The subject site is situated within the existing Honouliuli Wastewater Treatment
Plant in the District of Ewa on the Island of Wastewater
Treatment Plant is bounded by North Hanson Road to the west Geiger Road to the south
and the Coral Creek Golf Course to the east and north The general location and vicinity of
the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The subject site is located at the southeastern corner of the plant site The subject
site is bounded by the influent pump station to the west influent screens and an activated
carbon unit to the south and the Coral Creek Golf Course to the east Based on the
grading plans provided the topography at the subject site is relatively level with ground
elevations generally ranging between 33 and 35 feet Mean Sea Level MSL
The probing and grouting operations were performed in general conformance with
Section 2120 Subsurface Cavity Investigation of the project specifications The purpose
of the probing and grouting work was to reduce the potential for loss of foundation support
resulting from collapse of cavities or compression of loose zones below the structures and
tanks
In general the probe holes were drilled at each isolated spread foundation location
and at the center of the main portion of the structure The probe holes were about 4 inches
in diameter and extended to a minimum of 10 feet below the bottom of footing elevations
about 12 feet below existing grade

Loose zones were encountered or suspected based on easy drilling and high grout
intake at Probe Hole and 7 Therefore additional probe holes were drilled at
Probe Hole and 10 Voids were not encountered in the probe holes Probe
holes were backfilled with sand cement grout injected at low to moderate pressures
The probing work was performed by Geolabs Inc and was performed prior to
excavation for the structures to allow for easier access of the drill rig Continuous flight
augers on a truck mounted drill rig were used for the probing work Surveying of the probe
locations was performed by JAS W Glover Ltd A field engineer from Geolabs Inc
observed the probing and grouting work
A total of 10 probe holes with an aggregate length of about 120 lineal feet were
drilled The probe holes were drilled to 12 feet below the existing ground surface
Approximately 0 9 cubic yards of sand cement grout was utilized to fill the probe holes
The sand cement grout consisted of 1 part sand 1 part cement and 0 7 part water In
general the majority of the probe holes took about fifty percent more grout than the
theoretical volume of the hole The exception is Probe Hole No 6 which took about 0 4
cubic yards of grout The approximate locations of the probes are shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2 The field probing and grouting records are shown on the Probe Logs Appendix A
Note that Probe Hole 9 and 10 were probed and grouted at no
additional charge In addition the extra 0 4 cubic yards of grout volume was provided at
no additional charge
Limitations
Elevations of the probes were estimated from the elevations and contours shown
on Sheet S 3 of the Foundation Plans for the truck wash facility undated by GMP
Associates Inc The elevations of the probes should be considered accurate only to the
degree implied by the methods used
