INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this investigation was to evaluate the
subsurface conditions at the site to provide soil data
for the design and construction of the proposed structure
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations

W P 2
SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed site is located on the northwestern side of
Lilipuna Road about 800 feet northeast of its intersection
with Kamehameha Highway The site covers an area of about
0 78 acre
The site is bordered by Lilipuna Road on the southeast
a single family residence on the northeast a townhouse
development Puu Alii on the north and northwest and a
vacant lot on the southwest
The lot slopes upward from Lilipuna Road at an overall
gradient of about 6 horizontal to 1 vertical
The site was vacant during our field exploration and covered
with trees and grass
The ground elevation at the site varies from about 104 to
135 feet
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
The proposed structure will be a 6 story concrete building
with a basement level approximately 60 by 135 feet in plan
An A C parking area utilizing a retaining wall will be
located on the higher northwestern portion of the site

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Subsurface Exploration
Five borings were drilled at the site to depths of about
26 5 to 36 5 feet below the existing ground surface to
explore the subsurface conditions and to obtain samples
for laboratory testing The approximate locations of
the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
Detailed information on the field exploration and labora
tory testing is covered in the Appendix along with the
boring logs and laboratory test results
Soil Conditions
Based on the borings the site appears to be underlain by
stiff residual soils consisting of reddish brown clayey
silt with some decomposed rock
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings during the
field exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed competent bearing soils at
the site Shallow foundations bearing on the undisturbed
in situ clayey silt will provide adequate support for the
proposed structure
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P 4
Laboratory swell tests on the on site soils indicate low
expansion potential therefore slab on grade can be de
Signed without special provisions for expansive soil con
ditions
Foundations
For the proposed structure spread or continuous footing
foundations are recommended An allowable bearing value
of 4 000 p s f can be used for footings resting on the
stiff in situ soil The bearing value can be increased
by 1 3 when considering seismic and wind loadings
The bottom of the footings should be placed about 2 feet
below the finish grade
Footings located near utility trenches should extend to
the bottom of the trench
Surface water should be diverted away from the building
foundations
It is estimated that total settlements for the 6 story
structure should be less than one inch with a differential
settlement of less than one half of an inch
Site Grading
Cuts up to about 10 feet are planned for the basement level
and parking area Site filling is not anticipated
GEOLABS HAWAII
W PL
The reddish brown clayey silt soils at the site are accept
able fill and may be used for footing backfill The back
fill should be placed in horizontal loose lifts of 8 inches
maximum and compacted to 90 of its maximum dry density
established by ASTM Test Method D 1557
Slab On Grade
It is recommended that slab on grade should be placed over
a 4 inch layer of base course compacted to 95 of its maxi
mum dry density
The subgrade should be protected from drying and kept moist
during construction It should be compacted to a minimum
of 90 of maximum dry density
To protect against moisture damage of the flooring material
a polyethylene moisture barrier should be used
Where the slab is located below the outside ground surface
a subdrainage system around the perimeter of the walls will
help to prevent seepage water
Basement Retaining Wall
A basement wall up to about 10 feet high is planned along
the northwestern portion of the building A retaining
wall up to about 10 feet high is planned along the north
western edge of the parking area
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P 6
Well graded granular material such as base course rock
should be used as backfill material behind the walls
Impervious material should be used for the top 2 feet of
the backfill
The recommendations given under foundations should be
followed for the design of the wall foundations
The walls should be designed to resist lateral earth
pressures as follows
Walls unrestrained at the top 45 p c f equivalent
fluid pressure
Walls restrained at the top 60 p c f equivalent
fluid pressure
Additional loads due to traffic or surcharge
should be included where applicable
A friction factor of 0 45 can be utilized to determine the
sliding resistance of the wall
A subsurface drainage system should be used to prevent the
build up of hydrostatic pressure behind the walls
Parking Area
An A C parking area is planned in the northwestern portion
of the site

Design Review
The plans for foundations site grading and utility con
struction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for
review and comments prior to construction to see that the
intent of our recommendations is included in the project
plans and specifications

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the footing excavations and backfill
Operations be observed by a soils engineer during construc
tion to see that adequate bearing has been obtained and that
the subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings
of our subsurface investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive
tested and approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling
to the site

APPENDIX A
FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored
by drilling 5 borings to depths of 26 5 to 36 5 feet below
the existing ground surface at the locations shown on the
Site Plan Plate 1
The soils encountered in the borings were classified
by visual and textural examination in the field by our
engineer who continuously monitored the drilling operations
The classifications were reviewed by visual inspection and
testing in the laboratory The soils were classified in
general conformance with the Unified Soil Classification
System A graphic presentation of the soils is presented
on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 5
In the borings soil samples were obtained by driving
split barrel sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30
inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the last
12 inches are shown on the boring logs at the appropriate
depths
