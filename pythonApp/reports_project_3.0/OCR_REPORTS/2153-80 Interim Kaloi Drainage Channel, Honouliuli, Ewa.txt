EARTHWORK OBSERVATION REPORT
INTERIM KALOI DRAINAGE CHANNEL
HAWAII
Dear Mr Young
This report summarizes the geotechnical engineering observations for the Interim Kaloi
Drainage Channel project in Ewa on the Island of Oahu Hawaii
The project consisted of the excavation and construction of an un lined trapezoidal
drainage channel approximately 950 linear feet in length The channel consisted of a 80 to
140 foot wide bottom with four horizontal to one vertical 4H 1V side slopes and required
approximately 36 000 cubic yards of excavation to construct The invert elevations of the
drainage channel ranged from 13 5 to 14 feet above Mean Sea Level The excavation work
was observed by Geolabs Inc at the Kaloi Drainage Channel project site from May 30 2000
through
The subject site is located south of the Coral Creek Golf Course in the Ewa by Gentry
Development on the Island of Oahu Hawaii as shown on the attached Site Plan Plate 1
The drainage channel excavation work was performed by RHS Lee Inc The
excavated material from the channel mainly consisted of coralline material which was used as
fill material in other areas in the Ewa by Gentry Development Coral boulders from the
excavation were stockpiled in an area adjacent to the channel
Our services consisted of part time field observation of the contractor s excavation
procedures during the above grading operations Based on the observations of our field
representative it is our opinion that the excavation work for the drainage channel for the
project observed by Geolabs Inc in the period covered by this report were performed in
conformance with the general intent of the recommendations in the project documents and
Chapter 14 of the Revised Ordinances of Honolulu 1990 as amended
