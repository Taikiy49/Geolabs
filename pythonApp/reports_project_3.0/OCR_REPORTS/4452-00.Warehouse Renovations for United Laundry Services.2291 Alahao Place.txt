In accordance with our fee proposal dated February 22 we have conducted a
geotechnical engineering exploration for the proposed warehouse renovation project at the
existing United Laundry Services in Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii This report
summarizes our work on the project and presents our findings and geotechnical engineering
recommendations derived from our analyses for the subject project
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located within the existing United Laundry Services at 2291 Alahao
Place in Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the
project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
Based on the information provided we understand that it is desired to renovate the
existing warehouse with the installation of a new concrete floor slab and the addition of a
mezzanine level adjacent to the existing entry office and restroom areas Based on the
information provided the new addition will consist of a mezzanine level constructed of light
steel decking with concrete topping and CMU walls running across the existing warehouse
structure According to the project structural engineer wall loads on the order of about 3 kips
per lineal foot are anticipated for the new addition
Based on our experience in the vicinity of the project site we anticipated that the
subsurface conditions would consist of soft and or loose lagoonal deposits at shallow depths
Laboratory consolidation test was performed on a selected soil sample to evaluate the
compressibility characteristics of the lagoonal deposits

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our field exploration program for the warehouse renovation project consisted of drilling
and sampling two borings designated as Boring Nos 1 and 2 extending to a depth of about
31 5 feet below the existing ground surface The subsurface conditions were then further
explored by continuous penetration with a blunt point from about 31 5 to 49 feet below the
existing ground surface The approximate locations of the drilled borings are shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2
In general our field exploration disclosed that the project site is underlain by a surface
fill layer consisting of medium dense to dense coral sands of approximately 2 5 to 3 5 feet
thick Below the surface fill layer our borings generally encountered beach and lagoonal
deposits consisting of medium dense sands extending to a depth of about 5 feet below the
ground surface Very loose silty sands and or very soft organic silts were then encountered
and extended to the maximum depth explored of approximately 49 feet below the existing
ground surface
Shallow groundwater levels were encountered in the drilled borings at depths of about
4 3 and 5 3 feet below the existing ground surface at the time of our field exploration It should
be noted that groundwater levels are subject to change due to seasonal precipitation
temperature groundwater withdrawal and other factors
Detailed descriptions of the subsurface materials encountered from our field exploration
are presented on the Boring Log Legend and Logs of Borings Plates 3 through 5 of this report
A laboratory consolidation test was performed on a selected soil sample and the test results
are presented on Plate 6
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the subsurface soil conditions encountered at the existing warehouse site
we believe that shallow continuous footings may be used to support the proposed
mezzanine addition planned at the site Due to the presence of loose subsoils at shallow
depths a 2 foot thick layer of compacted structural fill material should be provided below
the bottom of footings for more uniform bearing support An allowable bearing pressure
of 1 000 pounds per square foot psf may be used for design of footings bearing on the
2 foot thick compacted structural fill material over the in situ soils
Loose subsoils were encountered at shallow depths in the drilled borings during our
field exploration Therefore we recommend that the over excavated footing subgrades be
scarified moisture conditioned and recompacted prior to the placement of the structural
fill material Special attention should be given to the footing subgrade preparation
requirements presented in the following section of this report

Foundations
Based on the anticipated subsurface soil conditions at the new warehouse addition we
believe that shallow continuous foundations may be used for support of the proposed
mezzanine addition planned at the project site An allowable bearing pressure of
1 000 pounds per square foot psf may be used for the foundation design of the proposed
new addition The bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third
1 3 for transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic forces
However due to the presence of loose sandy subsoils at shallow depths as
encountered in our field exploration a 2 foot thick layer of compacted structural fill material
should be provided below the bottom of footings for more uniform bearing support The
over excavation for the compacted structural fill should extend beyond the edges of the footing
a minimum of 12 inches if possible Prior to placement of the structural fill material the
bottom of the footing over excavation should be scarified to a minimum depth of 8 inches
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and recompacted to not less
than 90 percent relative compaction Subsequent to the footing subgrade preparation the
bottom of the over excavation should be lined with a layer of geotextile fabric Mirafi 500X or
equivalent prior to the placement of the structural fill material
The compacted structural fill should consist of non expansive select granular material
such as crushed basalt or coral The material should be well graded from coarse to fine with
no particles larger than 3 inches in largest dimension and should contain between 10 and
30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve The material should have a laboratory
CBR value of 20 or more and should have a maximum swell of less than 1 percent
Compacted structural fill should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum
moisture content placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and
compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the
in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same
soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 91 test procedures Optimum moisture is
the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
In general the bottom of footings should be embedded a minimum of 12 inches
below the lowest adjacent finished grades In order to utilize the upper fill crust for bearing
support the maximum footing embedment should be limited to 18 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grades
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by frictional resistance developed
between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil and by passive earth pressure
acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system A coefficient of friction of 0 4
may be used for footings bearing on the compacted structural fills Resistance due to passive
earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 pounds per square
foot per foot of depth pcf This assumes that the soils around the footings are well

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that Geolabs be retained to provide geotechnical engineering
services during construction of the proposed project The items of construction monitoring that
are critical requiring Special Inspection include observation of footing subgrade
over excavation and preparation and placement and compaction of the structural fill materials
Other aspects of earthwork construction should also be observed by a representative from
Geolabs This is to observe compliance with the intent of the design concepts specifications
and or recommendations and to expedite suggestions for design changes that may be
required in the event that subsurface conditions differ from those anticipated at the time this
report was prepared The recommendations provided in this report are contingent upon such
observations
lf actual exposed subsurface conditions encountered during construction differ from
those assumed or considered in this report Geolabs should be contacted to review and or
revise the geotechnical engineering recommendations presented herein

information obtained from the field borings Variations of subsoil conditions between and

become evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident Geolabs
should be contacted to re evaluate the recommendations presented in this report
