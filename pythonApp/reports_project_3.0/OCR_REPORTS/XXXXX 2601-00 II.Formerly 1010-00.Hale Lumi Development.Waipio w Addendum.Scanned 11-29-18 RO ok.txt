INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of a soils investigation performed
for the proposed Hale Lumi Development at Waipio, Oahu, Hawaii. The
general location of the site and its vicinity is shown on the Project
Location Map, Plate l.

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed project will include development of 50 house lots with
related access roadways. Substantial amount of cuts and fills are
required to grade the site for the subdivision lot and roadway
constructions. A general layout of the project is shown on the Site
Plan, Plate 2.
Based on the preliminary grading plan, cuts of up to 14 feet may be
required along the toe of the slope along the western side of the
subdivision. Thus, considerations will be required for steepening the
western slope or providing retaining walls along the western side. The
site grading may also require removing a large mound of soil, boulder and
concrete debris in the southern half of the site.
House foundation loadings are anticipated to be light and typical
for single-family structures.
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of the investigation was to provide recommendations for
site grading, house foundation and pavement designs based on the soil
information obtained from borings drilled and test pits excavated for
this investigation. The borings were generally drilled and sampled to a
depth of about 15 feet, and test pits were excavated to depths varying
from 3 to 10 feet below the existing ground at the approximate locations
shown on the Site Plan, Plate 2.
GEOLABS-HAWAII
1010-00 Page 3
Soil sampling, boring and test pit excavations were performed under
the observation of one of our engineers. The results of our field
exploration and laboratory tests, which we used in the engineering
analysis to formulate our findings and recommendations, are presented in
the appendices of this report.
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations.
SITE CONDITIONS
The proposed development site is located on a gently sloping terrace
just below and to the east of the existing Crestview Subdivision in
Waipio, Oahu, Hawaii. The eastern side of the terraced area slopes
steeply down to Panakauahi Gulch below. At the time of our exploration,
the site was overgrown with Haole Koa bushes and other trees. A trail
was Cleared by a small bulldozer through the bushes to the proposed
boring and test pit locations.
Along the western side of the site, numerous boulders were observed
on the ground surface and on the slope that rose up to the Crestview
Subdivision. There were several outcrops of the basalt rock ledges along
the western slope. A long windrow of boulders were piled and formed a
berm along the toe of the steep western slope.
The southern middle portion of the site was occupied by a large
mound of soil stockpile that contained some concrete slabs, blocks and
some discarded appliances.

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The site is located in a deeply weathered and eroded lower gentle
slope of the Koolau Volcano. The weathering has altered the basalt rocks
to an iron-rich mass of reddish brown colored soils with spheroidally
weathered boulders. Streams have cut deep gullies with steep side slopes
into the residual soils. The site is located on one such stream cut
gully with a wide terraced side slope. The site soils consist of stiff
silts with varying amount of clays and sands.
Soil Conditions
A large stockpile of soil and rock of 5 to 10 feet high covers most
of the southern half of the project site. Our test pits in this area
(TP-5 and TP-6) disclosed that the stockpiled material consisted mainly
of a mixture of reddish brown clay and silt soils and basalt boulders.
However, some concrete slabs and blocks were noted along the sides of the
stockpile. Therefore, it is probable that concrete blocks and some other
construction debris could have been buried in the stockpile.
Underlying the overgrowth and stockpiled material, the natural soils
consisted mainly of stiff to very stiff low to moderately expansive silty
clays and clayey silts with numerous boulders. Some pockets of
moderately to highly expansive clay soils were also encountered at the
site. The soils generally exhibited expansion of 2 to 7 percent with one
remolded sample showed expansion of up to 11% in our laboratory swell
testing.
GEOLABS-HAWAII
1010-00 Page 5
Weathered basalt cobbles and boulders were encountered frequently in the
borings and test pits, and less weathered basalt rock may be expected at
a shallow depth in some portions of the site.
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings which were drilled to
depths of about 15 feet below the ground surface.
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Our findings disclosed that the on-site soils are generally low to
moderately expansive. However, some occasional highly expansive pockets
or layers of clay soils may occur at various locations and depths.
If the subgrade soils are properly prepared, we believe that
slab-on-grade construction, as well as spread foundation support, may be
used for the house construction, with the understanding that some soil
swelling might occur under the floors. It is important that the on-site
soils are thoroughly moistened for at least 3 days before placing the
concrete, and that the perimeters of the slab are deepened at least 12
inches in order to better control future subgrade soil moisture
fluctuations.
GEOLABS-HAWAII
1010-00 Page 6
Due to the variable occurrence of the highly expansive soil at the
site, we recommend that subdivision lots be inspected individually for
highly expansive soils. Should any lots with highly expansive soils be
identified, then special foundation and slab design would have to be
considered.
It should be noted that the boulders on the slopes above the western
side of the development area impose a potential for rockfall. To
minimize rockfall hazard, precautionary measures such as providing a
ditch of about 15 feet wide along the toe of the western slope for
boulder catchment could be used. Detailed recommendations are presented
in the following sections.
Site Grading
Clearing, Grubbing and Stripping

Existing vegetation should be cleared and stripped. Tree roots in
excess of 1.5 inches in diameter should be grubbed.
Any localized soft or loose pocket encountered during clearing and
grubbing operations should be excavated and backfilled with
compacted select material.

The stripped surface soil containing organic matter may be reused as
topsoil in the planting areas, but should not be used as fill.
Entrapped deleterious materials, such as mats of grasses or piles of
wood, under the fill should be avoided.
In addition, the large pile of soil and boulder, concrete debris and
other miscellaneous materials should be removed and the underlying
original ground surface cleaned of decayed vegetation to firm soils.
The stripping operation should be continuously monitored by the
soils engineer to verify removal of unsuitable materials.
Surface Preparation
After clearing and grubbing, the area to receive fill should be
scarified at least 6 inches below the stripped grade, moisture
conditioned, and recompacted to 90% of the maximum density as
determined by the ASTM D-1557-78 test. Any soft or weak areas
disclosed during this recompaction should be over-excavated, removed
and then backfilled with engineered fill.
Monitoring of the surface preparation by a soils engineer is
particularly important especially to identify soft areas or
unsuitable materials.

Slope Construction
Ae ls me ee Sh ae om ae ee ce tae con cm te
Based on our findings, it appears that the required cut slope along
the western edge of the development, from Lots 1, 2, 6, 7 and 8,
would be partly cut in rock and partly in soil. The remaining cuts
along the western edge are anticipated to be in soil. A cut slope
ratio of 2:1 (H:V) is recommended for slope cuts in soil. Where
rock is encountered, slope cut ratios up to 1/4:1 (H:V) may be used.
Fill slope should not be steeper than 2:1 (H:V).
The on-site soil is clay and silt with some sand, and easily
erodable. Therefore, the slopes should be protected from run-off
water to reduce soil erosion. Interceptor ditches or swales should
be provided at the top to direct water away from the top of the
slope.
To provide adequate compaction of fill slopes, the slopes should
first be over-filled with compacted material and then trimmed back
to the design grade.
The construction of fill on slopes steeper than 5 horizontal to 1
vertical should be keyed and benched properly. Keying and benching
should consist of excavating a horizontal bench into the slope. A
typical section showing keying and benching into an existing slope
for this project is shown on Plate 3.

Fill Material § Placement
We believe that most of the on-site natural soils may be suitable
for re-use as general fill provided that they are not contaminated
with unsuitable materials or over-sized particles.
Highly expansive soils, if encountered on-site, should be discarded
or placed at the deeper part of the new fill.
Cobbles and boulders were encountered in the borings and test pits,
and also noted loosely piled at the site. Therefore, a significant
quantity of boulders would probably be generated from the site
grading. Boulders that are less than 2 feet in size may be recycled
in the fill and in accordance with the procedures discussed in the
following paragraphs. The larger boulders should be hauled
off-site.
Imported £111 should in general be of low expansion potential with
maximum particle size not exceeding 6 inches. The CBR expansion
value should be less than 1 percent and the CBR value should be 8 or
greater. We recommend that imported fill materials be tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to being hauled to the site.
GEOLABS-HAWAIl
1010-00 Page 10
The fill material should be placed in horizontal lifts not exceeding
10 inches in loose thickness. Each layer should be compacted to 90%
relative compaction according to ASTM D-1557-78 maximum density.
The top 6 inches of the roadway subgrade should be compacted to 95%
relative compaction.
Boulders or rock fragments in excess of 6 inches should not be
placed within the top 3 feet of the rough finished grade.
Occasional over-sized rocks may be placed in the lower portion of
the fill provided they are not more than 2 feet in maximum diameter
and can be embedded in compacted soil mass.
If the material is too wet or too dry, it should be dried or
moistened so that the moisture content at the time of compaction
will be within 3 percentage points of the optimum moisture content.
Inspection for Expansive Soils
We recommend that all subdivision lots be inspected by the soils
engineer during mass grading to see if highly expansive soil exists
within 3 feet of the finished rough grade of the house and garage pad
areas. Such inspection should also include sampling and performing
laboratory tests. If the soils are found to be highly expansive, then
they should be over-excavated for a depth of 3 feet below structures and
replaced with compacted non-expansive fill.

Boulder Protection
Numerous boulders of various sizes were observed on the western
slope as well as on the ground surface. The boulders on the slope impose
potential for future rockfall or boulders rolling down the hillside.
Rockfalls are a general phenomena occurring in all ridge slopes on
Oahu. Various methods of boulder barriers or catchments can be used to
reduce the hazard of rockfall. For this site, some of the methods which
may be used to reduce the boulder hazard are as follows:
1. Boulder sweeps along the western slope of the site could be
performed to remove the boulders that appear likely to roll down.
2. A boulder catchment area, about 15 feet wide, with a fence or
wall could be utilized along the toe of the western slope to
catch and arrest the rolling boulders. Interceptor ditches
along the upper boundaries, or along the top of the cut slopes
could be utilized for boulder catchment purpose.
3. Fences could be installed on the slopes as check fence to
dissipate and reduce the kinetic energy of the rolling or
skipping boulders.
4. The natural trees and vegetation along the upper portions of the
site should be retained to act as a natural barrier against the
boulders and to reduce erosion.
GEOLABS-HAWAIi
1010-00 Page 12
5. The houses should be set-back away from the toe of the hillside
slope to create an additional boulder catchment area.
It should be understood that whatever methods are used, an inherent
risk of falling boulders could still exist for these hillside lots.
The state of the art of soil engineering has not advanced to a point
that a complete solution is available for this area. It is our opinion
that, no matter what one does, there is always the risk of boulders
rolling down the slope, if not from areas near the house, it could come
from areas higher up on the slope. Whatever measure is selected, it only
reduces the risk. It should be further understood that no guarantee
against future falling boulders could be made due to changes in soil and
rock conditions with time, weathering, erosion, earthquake and
construction vibration, and improvements and construction activities at
and around the site.
The owners of these hillside lots should be made aware of the
aforementioned limitations and cautioned against future improvements in
the upper areas of the lots which may disturb the stability of the
existing boulders.

STANDARD GRADING SPECIFICATIONS
PROPOSED HALE LUMI DEVELOPMENT
W.O. 1010-00 APRIL 1984
The work under this section includes clearing and grubbing, preparation of
fill areas, placement and compaction of fill, and removal and backfilling
of underground structures. These specifications are of a general nature
and may be superseded by specific requirements or recommendations within
the soils report.
I.
Il.
Clearing
All areas within contract limit lines shall be cleared of trash,
debris, organic matter, and all other deleterious materials. These
materials shall be removed and disposed off-site.
Preparation of Fill Areas
A. Loose surface soils shall be removed to expose competent
natural soils. Localized soft pockets shall be undercut and
backfilled with compacted select materials.
B. All surfaces shall be inspected and approved by the soils
engineer prior to the placement of any fill.
Standard Grading Specifications -2-
ITl.
C. After approval, the surface shall be scarified to a depth of
six (6") inches, brought to a moisture content within 3 percentage
points of the optimum moisture content and compacted as herein
specified.
D. Where the existing surface exceeds a slope of 5 horizontal to 1
vertical (5:1) the ground surface shall be keyed and benched into
competent soil to bond the fill to the existing ground surface.
E. Subsurface drains may be required as directed by the soils
engineer where springs or zones of seepage are encountered.
Fill Materials
Material for fill shall consist of on-site soils or approved import
material. Import material shall be tested and approved a minimum
of seven (7) working days prior to transportation to the site.
Fill material shall contain less than 5 percent organic matter and
other deleterious material, and shall not contain rocks or lumps in
excess of six inches (6'') in diameter.
In addition, the imported fill material shall be of low expansion
potential, less than 1% swell, per ASTM D-1883 test method, and
have CBR values of 8 or greater when compacted to 95% of the
maximum dry density according to the ASTM D-1557-78 test procedure.

IV.
Placement and Compaction of Fill
A. The fill shall be placed in horizontal loose layers, not to
exceed ten inches (10'') in thickness. Each layer shall be spread
uniformly and blade-mixed to attain uniformity of material and
water content within each layer.
B. Rock fragments or boulders less than six inches (6'') in
diameter may be utilized, and occasional boulders up to 2 feet in
size may also be recycled in the fill, provided they are not
concentrated in any one area and that there is sufficient
fine-grained material to fill the voids between the rocks.
C. Rocks greater than two feet (2') in size shall be removed from
the fill unless specifically approved for placement in the fill by
the soils engineer.
D. All £111 shall be compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the
maximum dry density established by ASTM Test Designation: D-1557-78
unless otherwise specified in the soils report.
E. The compaction equipment may be sheepsfoot, vibratory, or
pneumatic tire rollers suited to the soil type and capable of
producing the specified results in an expedient and efficient
manner. The equipment shall be in good working condition, fully
ballasted, and self cleaning.

VI.
F. The compaction shall be accomplished in a systematic and
uniform manner over the entire fill area to produce a uniformly
compacted fill.
G. Fill material placed in an unsatisfactory condition and not in
accordance with these specifications shall not be approved and
shall be removed or reworked until the required density is obtained.
H. The soils engineer shall be provided unrestricted access to the
fill to perform the necessary inspections and testing.
Removal and Backfilling of Underground Structures
Underground structures such as cesspools, cisterns, septic tanks,
wells, pipe lines, etc. shall be demolished and removed as directed
by the soils engineer. Backfilling of the excavations shall be in
accordance with these specifications.
Other Requirements
A. No £111 shall be placed during unfavorable weather conditions.
When interruption of work is due to heavy rain, the previous fill
surface shall be approved before resumption of earthmoving
operations.
B. The contractor shall maintain the surface of the fill so that
it will be free draining and will not pond surface water.
