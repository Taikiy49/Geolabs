PROJECT DESCRIPTION
In general the overall project involves the construction of a new four story medical
office building along the southern side and a four story parking garage with seven levels of
parking at the northeastern side of the property adjacent to Waimalu Stream The project
site currently consists of a three story structure with a gross floor area of about
61 690 square feet On grade asphalt concrete paved parking is around the existing
building with the majority of the parking stalls at the front and rear of the building
The new Phase 1 parking structure is supported on a deep foundation system
consisting of 18 inch diameter augered cast in place piles with an allowable compressive
load capacity of up to 320 kips 160 tons per pile The project consisted of 13 indicator
piles test piles and 153 production piles for a total of 166 ACIP piles for the foundation
support of the parking structure In addition 25 micropiles were installed at selected
foundation locations due to physical constraint and inaccessibility of the ACIP pile
installation equipment
Hawaiian Dredging Construction Company Inc HDCC was the general contractor
for the project and also installed the ACIP piles for the foundation support system
Kelikai Inc was the micropile installation subcontractor for the project

ACIP Pile Load Test Program
In order to evaluate the adequacy of the design lengths of the ACIP pile foundation
a load test pile program consisting of two static load tests was implemented The load test
pile program included the installation and observation of reaction piles and
two instrumented load test piles Two static pile load tests were conducted on the
instrumented ACIP piles on April 29 and May 1 2013 The pile load tests were conducted
on two 18 inch diameter ACIP piles with a length of approximately 100 feet relative to the
existing ground surface
The static pile load test results are summarized in the report entitled ACIP Pile

MICROPILE INSTALLATION
Geotechnical recommendations for the micropile foundations were presented in the
afore mentioned geotechnical engineering report dated The purpose of
our geotechnical engineering observation and consultation services was to observe the
micropile foundation installation for general conformance with our recommendations
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered in the project vicinity the
micropiles were specified to extend to a minimum depth of about 80 feet below the ground
level with a 20 or 40 foot load bearing grout bulb developed within the coralline materials
to develop an allowable compressive load capacity of 60 and 80 kips at Zone B and A
respectively
In general the micropiles were drilled using a Beretta TI59 rig equipped with Bull
casing to the specified depth Advancing steel casing to the bottom of the specified depth
was required in order to facilitate the micropile drilling and maintain grouting quality Air
pressure up to 100 pounds per square inch psi was utilized during drilling operation to
create a bulb around the drilling bit for advancing temporary steel casing
After the drilled holes were extended to the specified depth and cleaned with air
pressure a reinforcing bar consisting of Grade 75 all threaded 11 rebar was placed in the
cased hole with centralizers Grout was batched on site utilizing a colloidal high shear and
high speed mixer equipped with a grout pump The grout mix
consisted of one sack of 94 pound Portland Cement Type I and or pounds of
