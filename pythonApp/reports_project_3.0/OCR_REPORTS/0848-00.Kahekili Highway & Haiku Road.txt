INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of a soil and foundation
investigation for the above referenced housing development
The work was performed according to our proposal
dated

recommendations for the apartment buildings were submitted

The planned development will include various 2 3 and 4 bed
room apartment buildings access roads tennis courts pool
and retaining walls The buildings will be 2 and 3 story
wood framed structures some 2 story buildings will have
basement level parking or partial lower floor Many
buildings will be approximately 40 by 145 feet in plan

According to tentative finished grades approximate depths
of cut will range from eight feet near Haiku Kahekili to
thirteen feet along the intermediate or central ridge
depths of fill will be up to roughly 18 feet in the western

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of the investigation is to evaluate the suita
bility of the site for the planned development by exploring
soil and groundwater conditions We also developed soil
parameters for design and construction of foundations
grading stability of cut and fill and paving
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 848 00
P 3
The scope of work included 17 borings disturbed and
undisturbed sampling and site reconnaissance Soil
samples including standard penetration tests were obtained
during the investigation to measure the relative density
and compactness of the underlying soils encountered
Laboratory testing was performed to classify and identify
the characteristics of the subsoils The results of our
investigation together with conclusions and recommendations
are summarized in this report
The approximate boring locations are shown on Plate 1 Site
Plan and the boring logs are presented in Appendix A In
formation regarding the field explorations and laboratory
testing are presented in the Appendices
SITE CONDITIONS
As shown on Plate 1 Site Plan the development will be
located adjacent to Kahekili Highway and Haiku Road The
present ground surface consists of two gently sloping ridges
higher areas and two valleys drainageways The first
higher area is located near the Kahekili Haiku intersection
adjacent to the east is a smaller western valley running
northeast beneath Emepela Place There is an intermediate
ridge between the small valley and the larger southeastern
valley At the time of our field explorations most of the
higher areas were covered with grass and trees Marshy wet
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 848 00
P 4
ground conditions including heavy vegetation were observed
in the swampy valleys Emepela Place is generally supported
on fill embankment
Ground surface elevations range from 150 to 175 feet in
the first high area adjacent to Kahekili Haiku intersection
The natural gradient is relatively flat approximately 25
horizontal to 1 vertical The sides of the valleys are
relatively steep Surface elevations decrease to 133 feet
in the bottom of the smaller or central valley at the drain
beneath Emepela Place Surface elevations of the inter
mediate ridge vary from 145 to 170 feet the natural gradient
is approximately 20 horizontal to 1 vertical Surface ele
vations decrease to 115 feet at the northern end of the
southeastern valley More detailed descriptions of the site
are shown on Plate 1 Site Plan
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The site of the development is located roughly midway between
the Pali and the coast on the windward slope of the Koolau
Range This area is part of a deeply eroded and weathered
section typified by thick alluvial deposits overlying vol
canic dike complex The extended period of erosion has also
resulted in deposition of quiet water recent alluvium in
valleys and drainageways including localized deposits of
organic silt
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 848 00
P 5
Soils
Based upon the borings the dominant soil type consists
of clayey silt Brown very stiff older alluvium was
encountered beneath the upper portions of the site soft
recent alluvium gray color was encountered in the lower
portions and swampy valleys The lower portion of the
southeastern valley side slopes are also underlain by
recent alluvium consisting of alternating layers of stiff
and medium soils Volcanic rocks of the underlying dike
complex were not encountered in the borings
Groundwater
At the present time the water table in the small western
valley is roughly nine feet below the surface approximate
elevation 125 feet The water table in the southeastern
valley is generally within several feet of the bottom of
the valley elevation roughly 119 feet in Boring 8B to
139 feet in Boring 11 Water levels may fluctuate with
climatic conditions
CONCLUSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on the results of the field investigations laboratory
testing and engineering analyses the following conclusions
and recommendations are presented

The upper ridge areas are generally underlain by competent
subsoils Normal site grading methods and conventional
spread or continuous footing foundations are recommended
for buildings in this area
The small drainageway in the central portion of the site
is underlain by variable medium to soft subsoils Settle
ment gauges are recommended in this area to monitor the anti

Building and utility construction should be delayed until
tolerable settlement rates are achieved Surcharging can
be used to decrease the waiting period if required
Soft subsoils and high water table were encountered in the
lower portions of the large drainageway Special stabili

future differential settlements

Site Preparation
Upper Areas and smaller drainageway
The site should be cleared of vegetation debris and
other deleterious material In areas to receive fill
the ground should be scarified to a minimum depth of
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 848 00
P 7
6 inches brought to near optimum moisture content
and compacted to a minimum of 90 density
Lower marshy area Large Drainageway
Prior to site clearance and surface vegetation
removal off site drainage water should be diverted
where possible around the lowlying swamp area to
permit the existing soft area to develop a firmer
soil crust
It is estimated that the undercutting of the surface
vegetation and organic material and scrubbing will
average 6 inches over the fill portion of the site
The existing surface soil in the low areas is marshy
and wet and does not have sufficient strength to
support heavy construction equipment Therefore
a working platform or drainage blanket should be
established prior to the major filling of the
swamp area The working platform may consist of a
3 to 4 foot layer of gravel or a combination of a
synthetic filter fabric and a 12 inch gravel layer
The filter fabric reduces the required gravel
thickness and eliminates the strict gradation
requirements of the drainage blanket since it
will act as a filter medium to prevent gravel
loss into the soft ground and prevent the mud
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 848 00
P 8
and fine soil particles from contaminating the
granular blanket material It also provides
tensile reinforcement to the gravel layer and
permits earlier use of heavy equipment on a
minimum thickness of compacted aggregate see
Plate 2
Fill Placement
After site preparation the fill should be placed in maximum
10 inch lifts compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum
dry density established by ASTM test designation D 1557 70
Fills placed over the soft marshy areas should be placed
at slow rates to avoid squeezing or failure in the soft
subsoils An initial estimated rate of 1 foot per day
is recommended The actual rate should be determined in
the field based on the settlement gauge readings
The on site cut material from the upper areas are suitable
for use in compacted fill and backfill
It is recommended that if additional off site borrow is
required it should be tested and approved by the soils
engineer prior to transportation to the site to assure
that it is suitable for the proposed construction The
material should generally be non expansive and should not
contain large percentages of boulders organic or dele
terious matter

Where the existing ground is steeper than 5 horizontal to
1 vertical 5 1 benching and keying are required to pro

should start at the lowest point and continue up in level
compacted layers in accordance with the above structural
fill requirements For construction details refer to

Slopes
Two horizontal to one vertical 2 1 cut and fill slopes
are recommended for the upper areas of the site
Due to the soft subsoils flatter slope ratios of 3 hori
zontal to 1 vertical 3 1 are recommended for the embank
ment slopes along the southern boundary of the project site
To reduce the amount of runoff over the slopes a drainage
bench should be provided for slopes having a vertical
height greater than 15 feet
To minimize erosion all slopes should be planted as soon
as practicable
Surcharge
Due to the soft marshy soils in the large drainageway
estimated settlements up to 1 foot are anticipated resulting
from the proposed fill
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 848 00
P 10
The estimated settlements will generally occur over a long
period of time The construction of buildings and improve
ments should be delayed until tolerable rates of settle
ment are achieved
To reduce the waiting period a temporary surcharge fill
is recommended over this low area The approximate limits
of the surcharge are shown on the Site Plan A surcharge
height of 5 feet is recommended
A one month rest period is further recommended between
completion of 111 and placement of temporary surcharge
This will allow initial dissipation of excess pore water
pressures and evaluation of initial settlement data Sur
charge fill may be placed loose at slopes of 1 horizontal
to 1 vertical
Settlement gauges should be installed prior to filling to
monitor the settlement rate and determine when building
and utility construction can proceed
The time required for the estimated settlement to occur are
very difficult to predict and may vary due to the various
consistency of the subsoils It is estimated that a 6 to
9 month surcharge period will be required for the project
The actual surcharge period will be determined by the
settlement gauge readings

Foundations
General
The proposed residential structures may be supported
by conventional spread or continuous footing founda
tions
The footings should extend a minimum of 18 inches below
the finish grades Foundations located close to the
top of slopes should be embedded a sufficient depth
to obtain a 5 foot minimum horizontal set back distance
from the slope face
The foundations can be designed using the following
allowable bearing values
Very stiff natural soil up to 4000 p s f
Compacted fill over
stiff ground 2500 p s f
Compacted fill over
soft ground 2000 p s f
The bearing values for compacted fill are based on the
assumption that 90 relative compaction can be achieved
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 848 00
P 12
Above values of bearing capacity are for total
of dead plus live loads and may be increased by
33 when temporary lateral loads wind and seismic
are considered
Resistance to lateral loads may be provided by
combination of passive pressure and friction
Passive pressure may be taken as that exerted
by a fluid of density equal to 25 p c f
Friction may be taken as normal dead load times
a coefficient of friction equal to 0 40
A combination of friction and passive pressure
may be used provided that passive pressure is
reduced by one third
Some of the units will be located partly over
stiff in situ ground and partly over fill under
lain by soft subsoils
Even allowing prolonged time for settlements of
fill and surcharge it is our opinion that some
differential settlement approximately to 3 4
inch should be anticipated from long term secon
dary consolidation of the soft subsoils From a
geotechnical standpoint the most preferable develop
ment scheme would be to structurally separate the
units supported on the stiff natural ground from
the units underlain by fill and soft subsoils
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 848 00
P 13
Alternatively it would be possible to reduce
differential settlements by reducing depths of
new fill in valleys or designing the building
to take care of the anticipated differential
settlements by increasing the stiffness of
continuous foundations spanning the cut fill
areas or utilizing pile foundations
Pile foundations will be expensive due to having
to design for the additional pile loads resulting
from negative skin friction or downdrag caused by
new fill

earth pressures as follows for level backfill

Slab On Grade
It is recommended that slab on grade be placed over a
4 inch layer of base course compacted to 95 of its
maximum dry density
The subgrade should be kept moist and protected from
drying prior to concrete placement Subgrade should be
compacted to a minimum of 90 of maximum dry density
Slab on grade should be separated from foundations
In order to protect against moisture damage of the flooring
material a polyethylene moisture barrier should be incor
porated within floor slabs
When the slab is located below the outside ground surface
a subdrainage system around the perimeter of the walls is
recommended to relieve seepage water

LABORATORY TESTING
ee
Moisture content unit weight and Atterberg Limit determinations
were performed on select samples as an aid in the evaluation and classi
fication of soil properties The results of these tests are presented
on the Boring Logs at the appropriate sample depths
Torvane shear strength tests were performed on selected samples
The test results are presented on the Boring Logs Three direct shear
were performed on selected undisturbed samples to evaluate the strength
characteristics of the subsoils These test results are plotted on
Plates B 1 thru B 3
Two consolidation tests were performed on undisturbed ring samples
to evaluate the consolidation characteristics of the soft swampy materials
The consolidation curves are presented on Plates B 4 and B 5
To obtain the moisture density relationship of the upper on site
soil as a potential source of fill material an ASTM D 1557 test Modi
fied Proctor curve was developed and is plotted on Plate B 6
One swell test on a one inch ring sample was conducted on the
undisturbed on site soil to measure the swelling potential of the
soil The test result is summarized on Plate B 7
Two CBR tests were performed on bulk samples of the on site
upper material to evaluate the material as a source of borrow and
pavement support The test results are summarized on Plate B 7
