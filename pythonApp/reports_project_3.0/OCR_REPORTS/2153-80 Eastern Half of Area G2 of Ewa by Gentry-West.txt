Earthwork Observation and Compaction Testing Report
Coral Creek Golf Course Mass Grading
Eastern Half of Area G2 of Ewa by Gentry West
Dear Mr Young
This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations performed
during the mass grading operations for the Coral Creek Golf Course Eastern Half of
Area G2 of Ewa by Gentry West from through August 31 1995
The subject site is located on the western side of Kapolei Parkway and north of
Geiger Road in Ewa on the island of Oahu Hawaii The site is also bounded by the
Area 5 single family development to the north the Area 7 single family development to
the south an unused railroad track to the north and the Honouliuli Wastewater Treatment
Plant to the west
The mass grading work for the Eastern Half of Area G2 was performed by Oahu
Construction Company Ltd The mass grading work consisted of grading of the
proposed golf course with on site soil and boulder fill materials The mass grading work
generally consisted of small cut and fill heights of about 5 to 10 feet with some isolated
areas up to 10 to 20 feet in height The on site fill materials were generated from
Area G2 and from Areas 5 and 7 single family development to the north and south
respectively The available fill materials generally consisted of reddish brown gravelly
clay to brown silty clay and tannish brown silty coral gravel
Prior to fill placement the existing vegetation was removed the exposed
subgrades were scarified to a depth of approximately 6 inches 6 brought to near
optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum
dry density as determined in accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557 The fill
material was then placed in level lifts and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the
maximum dry density

During the fill placement operations periodic field density tests were performed in
general accordance with the American Society of Testing and Materials ASTM Test
Designation D 2922 Nuclear Gauge Method Results of the compaction tests are
summarized on Plates 3 1 and 3 2 of this report
During the grading period various on site soils were tested prior to use in the field
The maximum dry density and optimum moisture content relationships were established
in our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified
Proctor and the test results are summarized on Plate 2 of this report
Boulders up to 5 feet in diameter were placed at the 11 Green and at the
12 Fairway within deep fills below 3 feet from finish grade or below 3 feet from utility
inverts The procedure recommended for the placement and compaction of the boulder
fill was as follows
1 For fill slopes a keyway with a minimum width of 8 feet and depth of 2 feet
should be constructed at the toe of the boulder fill slope
2 For boulders placed on sloping ground the boulders should be keyed into
the slope
3d Boulder fills should be placed in lifts not exceeding the maximum boulder
dimension
4 Boulder fills should contain fines to fill the interstices should be watered
heavily and should be compacted with a minimum 20 ton compactor for a
minimum of 6 passes
2 After compaction of the boulder fill layer a geotextile Mirafi 700X or similar
should be placed over the boulder fill layer to reduce the potential for
migration of fines from the overlying fill into the boulder fill layer
6 After placement of the geotextile granular fill placed in a loose lift thickness
of 8 to 12 inches should be compacted to a minimum of 85 percent relative
compaction ASTM D 1557 91
if Above the granular fill a fill material with a maximum particle size of
3 inches should be placed and compacted to a minimum of 85 percent
relative compaction ASTM D 1557 91
Our services consisted of full time and part time field observation of the
contractor s fill placement and compaction procedures and confirmation of subgrade soil
conditions exposed during the above grading operations
