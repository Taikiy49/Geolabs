PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this soil engineering investigation was to explore the
subsurface soil conditions at the site in order to provide soil data
for use in the design of the site grading and house foundations for
the proposed cluster development
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests which we
used in the engineering analyses to formulate our conclusions and
recommendations are presented in the appendices of this report
The
approximate boring locations are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 and
the logs of borings are presented in Appendix A

This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
PROPOSED DEVELOPMENT
The proposed development consists of retaining the existing house
and garage in one large lot in the southern side of the Site The
remaining area will be divided into six smaller lots for Single family
residences and an access road from Kaneohe Bay Drive into the site
Slab on grade construction is planned for the residences
Cuts and fills generally less than 10 feet in height are anticipated
for the development site grading
A retaining wall up to 6 feet high and 112 feet long is planned
along the eastern side of the access road
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is situated along Kaneohe Bay in the Kokokahi area of
Kaneohe on the island of Oahu Hawaii The proposed development is
approximately 300 feet northwest of the intersection of Kaneohe Bay
Drive and Miha Street
The site is bounded by the Kokokahi YWCA to the west private
residences to the south and vacant parcels of land to the east and
north
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1205 00 Page 3
Having been previously developed the site has been graded with
ground surface elevations ranging from 16 to 58 feet and sloping down
generally toward the north
Two terraced pads with slopes of about 4 1 H V are located along the
western portion of the site which is generally steeper than the
remaining areas
Four existing structures are located on the site two wooden residences
and one multi bay carport parallel to the southern boundary and one
abandoned asphalt tennis court located in the center of the site
The ground cover consists primarily of well maintained lawn grass
California grass Haole Koa and some trees
Access to the site is through a privately owned narrow paved driveway
from Kaneohe Bay Drive to the existing residences
SITE CONDITIONS
Four borings were drilled at the site to depths of 11 5 to 16 5 feet
below the existing ground surface
The borings encountered stiff alluvial soils consisting of stiff silty
clay and clay to the depths drilled

The laboratory tests indicate that these soils have swelling potential
ranging from low to high It appears that the swelling potential
generally increases if the natural soils are remolded
Groundwater was encountered in Boring 4 drilled in the lowest portion
of the site at about 13 foot depth No groundwater was encountered
in the remaining borings
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration encountered stiff alluvial soils consisting of
mottled tan and brown silty clay and clay The laboratory tests
indicate that the soils can be classified as CH MH according to the
Unified Soil Classification System Laboratory ring swell and CBR
expansion tests indicate that some of the soils have moderate to high
swelling potential Our laboratory test data indicate the swelling
potential of the soil increases if the natural soils are remolded
and or allowed to dry below about 30 to 32 moisture content
In order to reduce the soil expansion potential the fills and natural
soils should be kept moist and not allowed to dry out In addition
special foundation design is recommended for the residences

Site Preparation
eee Se et
Prior to filling the site should be cleared of old foundations
boulders vegetation rubbish and other deleterious material
The ground should then be scarified to a minimum depth of 6
inches brought to near optimum moisture content and then
compacted to a minimum of 85 of its maximum dry density
established by ASTM D 1557 78 test procedure
Pilling and Compaction Requirements
After the above site preparation the fi11 should be moistened to
above 30 moisture content placed in 10 inch maximum loose lifts
and compacted to obtain a minimum 85 of its maximum dry density
established by the above ASTM test designation
The lower 85 compaction requirement instead of 90 is
recommended for two reasons First the site is situated in a
high rainfall area and the natural moisture contents of the
underlying soils are well above optimum moisture content It may
be difficult to dry out the soils sufficiently to obtain 90
compaction It is our opinion that 85 compaction can provide
adequate support for the light structures proposed Secondly it
is desirable to maintain a higher moisture content and lower
compaction requirement in order to reduce the swelling potential
of the soils

Cut and fill slopes generally less than 10 feet in vertical
height are anticipated
Fill slope ratio of 2 1 H V are recommended
Fill slopes should be constructed by overfilling then cutting
back to the design slope to obtain a well compacted slope face
stiff natural soil
Surface water should be diverted away from the top of the slope
and slope planting utilized as soon as practicable to reduce
erosion
House Foundation
Special foundation design is recommended for the lightly loaded
single family residences due to the moderately expansive soil
condition The following are two general approaches for the proposed
foundation design

Non Expansive Capping Fill

The first alternative would be to use select non expansive
off site borrow to cap off the effect of the underlying expansive
soil The capping fill should be a minimum of 2 feet below the
house slabs and driveway subgrade level and for a distance of 3
feet beyond the perimeter of the house and driveway
Conventional slab on grade with thickened edges extending a
minimum of 12 inches below the finished grade can then be used on
the above non expansive capping layer
Special Foundation Design
Ce a ee Lak em me am en me ee
Alternatively a special foundation design using a deep perimeter
footing in conjunction with subgrade saturation and lime applica
tion can be used Detailed recommended procedures are as follows
a A deeper perimeter footing extending a minimum of 24
inches below the outside finished grade for the house and
12 inches below the driveway is recommended to act as a
cut off wall against moisture migration below the slab
b Hydrated lime should be placed at the bottom of the deep
perimeter footing and slab subgrade soil prior to water
saturation About one pound of lime per square foot should
be applied
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1205 00 Page 8
c The subgrade soils should be kept saturated by sprinkling
at least 2 days prior to concrete placement to reduce the
swelling potential of the underlying soil
d After the above wetting procedures very soft mud located
at the bottom of the footing excavation should be removed
prior to steel placement and the excavation should remain
neat and free of loose soils until concrete placement
e The concreting of the footing and slab should be coordinated
in such a way that the time period between the initial
excavation and final concrete placement at each lot should
be kept to a minimun
Slab on grade should be placed over a 4 inch layer of base course
consisting of No 3 Fine gravel To protect against moisture
damage of the flooring material a polyethylene moisture barrier
should be used
The finished grade outside the slab and pavements should be shaped
to shed water away from the foundations and avoid ponding near the
foundations The owner should be made aware that gutter water
should be diverted far away from the foundations and pavements
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f may be used on compacted
fill or the stiff natural soil

Retaining Wall
A CMU retaining wall is planned along the eastern side of the access
road
The wall foundations should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below
the finished grade
An allowable bearing value of 3000 p s f can be used on the very
stiff underlying natural soil
Well graded granular material such as base course rock 3B Fine
gravel sand or equivalent should be used as backfill material behind
the walls Impervious on site silty material should be used for the
top 2 feet of the backfill
We recommended that the walls be designed to resist lateral earth
pressures as follows
Active Condition and 35 p c equivalent fluid pressure
level backfill
A friction factor of 0 35 can be used to determine the sliding
resistance of the wall
In addition a passive soil resistance of 300 p c f can be used
GEOLABS HAWAII
A subsurface drainage system should be used to prevent the build up of
hydrostatic pressure behind the walls
Access Road Pavement
Asphaltic concrete pavement is planned for the project Based on the
laboratory CBR test results of the material near the roadway area and
the very light traffic anticipated the preliminary pavement design is
as follows
2 inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 inch Base Course
8 inch Total Pavement Thickness
on Compacted Subgrade Soil
Additional CBR tests should be performed on the actual subgrade soil
samples during construction and the above design section can be
revised if necessary The recommended section considers light traffic
and that good drainage will be provided adjacent to paved areas
Design Review
The plans for foundation site grading and utility construction should
be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written comments
prior to construction to see that the intent of our recommendations is
included in the project plans and specifications
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during earthwork
construction to monitor the contractor s grading operations
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1205 00 Page 11
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be observed by the
soils engineer during construction to see that adequate bearing has
been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with
the findings of our subsurface investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the above
inspections
