PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed project involves renovating and replacing some of the cottages
within the University of Hawaii at Manoa UHM Lyon Arboretum in Manoa Valley on the
Island of Oahu Hawaii The project site is behind the valley above Paradise Park as
shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The existing Cottage G is a two story wood framed structure It is proposed to
demolish the existing cottage and replace it with a new cottage Details for the proposed
replacement structure were not available at the time this report was prepared However it
is anticipated that the new structure will be a lightly loaded wood framed structure and the
new cottage will be one or two stories high

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our work was to obtain an overview of the subsurface conditions to
formulate design recommendations for the proposed replacement cottage The work was
performed in general accordance with our fee proposal dated Our
scope of work included the following tasks and work efforts
1 Mobilization and demobilization of truck mounted drill equipment and
two operators to the project site and back
2 Drilling and sampling of two borings to depths of approximately 21 5 feet
below the existing ground surface to evaluate the subsurface soil profile
3 Coordination of the field exploration and logging of the borings by our
field engineer geologist
4 Laboratory testing of selected soil samples obtained during the field
exploration as an aid in classifying the materials and evaluating their
engineering properties
5 Preparation of this letter report summarizing our work on the project and
presenting our findings and recommendations
6 Coordination of our overall work on the project by our project geologist
7 Quality assurance of our work and client design team consultation by our
principal engineer
8 Miscellaneous work efforts such as drafting word processing and clerical
support
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our field exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling two borings
designated as Boring Nos 1 and 2 each extending to a depth of approximately 21 5 feet
below the existing ground surface The approximate boring locations are shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by
laboratory testing Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representations of the materials

The borings generally encountered a surface layer approximately 2 to 5 feet

support for the proposed replacement cottage We did not encounter groundwater in the
borings at the time of our field exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the subsurface soil conditions encountered we believe that shallow
spread and continuous footings bearing on recompacted native soils or properly
compacted structural fill may be used to support the building planned provided that the
recommendations presented herein are followed
Detailed discussion of these items and our geotechnical engineering design
recommendations are presented in the following sections herein
Foundations
Based on the anticipated subsurface soil conditions we believe that shallow spread
and continuous footing foundations may be used to support the proposed building An
allowable bearing pressure of 2 000 pounds per square foot psf may be used for the
foundations bearing on recompacted native soils or properly compacted structural fill The
transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic forces
In general the bottom of footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches
below the lowest adjacent finished grades Foundations next to utility trenches should be
embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane extending
upward from the bottom edge of the utility
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by friction developed between
the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil and by passive earth pressure acting
against the near vertical faces of the foundation system A coefficient of friction of 0 4 may
be used for footings bearing on the recompacted native soils or compacted structural fills
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid
pressure of 250 pounds per square foot per foot of depth pcf This assumes that the soils
around the footings are well compacted Unless covered by pavements or slabs the
passive resistance in the upper 12 inches of soil should be neglected

Slabs On Grade
For the interior building slabs not subjected to vehicular traffic we recommend
placing a minimum 4 inch thick layer of cushion fill consisting of No 3B Fine gravel
ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation below the slabs to serve as a capillary break and to
provide more uniform support of the slabs To reduce moisture infiltration and subsequent
damage to floor coverings we recommend placing an impervious moisture barrier on top
of the No 3B Fine gravel Flexible floor coverings such as the carpet or sheet vinyl
should be considered since they can better mask minor slab cracking
Exterior concrete walkways may be required Control joints should be provided at
intervals equal to the width of the walkways with expansion joints at right angle
intersections
The thickened edges of the slab adjacent to unpaved areas should be embedded at
least 12 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade Areas adjacent to the slab
should be backfilled tightly against the slab with on site soil and graded to divert water
away from the slab to reduce the potential for water ponding around the foundations
Site Grading
At the commencement of grading work areas within the grading limits should be
cleared grubbed and scarified to a minimum depth of 6 inches moisture conditioned to
above the optimum moisture content and recompacted to no less than 90 percent relative
compaction ASTM D 1557 Trees should be removed and roots greater than 1 inch in
diameter should be over excavated and then backfilled with fill placed in 10 inch maximum
loose lifts moisture conditioned to at least the optimum moisture content and compacted
to at least 95 percent relative compaction Vegetation debris deleterious materials and
other unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed properly off site or stockpiled
in a designated area to reduce the potential for contaminating the excavated materials
Soft and yielding areas encountered should be over excavated to expose firm
natural material and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with fill placed in 10 inch
maximum loose lifts moisture conditioned to at least the optimum moisture content and
compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction The excavated soils should be
disposed properly off site and or used in landscape areas where appropriate
Imported fill if required should consist of non expansive granular material such as
crushed coral basalt or cinder sand The material should be well graded from coarse to
fine with particles no larger than 3 inches in largest dimension and should contain between
10 and 30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve The material should have a

laboratory CBR value of 20 or more and should have a maximum swell of less than
1 percent when tested in accordance with ASTM D 1883 Geolabs should test imported fill
materials for conformance with these recommendations prior to delivery to the project site
for its intended use
Fills and backfills should be moisture conditioned to within about 2 percent of the
optimum moisture placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and
compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the
in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of
the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 Optimum moisture is the
water content percentage by weight corresponding to the maximum dry density We
wish to emphasize that moisture conditioning of the fill materials is an integral part of the
fill placement recommendations provided herein Compaction should be accomplished
by sheepsfoot rollers vibratory rollers or other types of acceptable compaction
equipment
Because moisture conditioning and compaction of the fill and backfill soils are
critical elements of earthwork Geolabs should perform observations and soil density tests
during site grading to assist the contractor in obtaining the required degree of compaction
and the proper moisture content Where compaction is less than required additional
compactive effort should be applied with adjustment of moisture content as necessary to
obtain the specified compaction It should be noted that the moisture requirement of the
fills and subgrades within about 2 percent of the optimum moisture is an important
requirement for the use of the on site clayey soils
Utilities
We anticipate that some utilities may be constructed The contractor should
determine the methods and equipment to be used for excavation subject to practical
limits and safety considerations The excavation should comply with all applicable local
state and federal safety requirements The contractor should be responsible for trench
shoring design and installation Trench shoring and bracing should conform to the
appropriate health and safety requirements
In general for support of the utility lines we recommend using a granular bedding
consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation under the
pipes The initial backfill up to about 1 foot above the pipes should consist of free draining
backfills such as No 3B Fine gravel to reduce the potential for damaging the pipes from
compaction of the backfill It is critical to use a free draining granular material to reduce the
potential for formation of voids below the haunches of pipes and to provide adequate
support for the sides of the pipes We do not recommend using on site soils as backfill
directly around utility pipes

For protected utility lines such as concrete jacketed electrical and
telecommunications lines the bedding requirements described above are not necessary if
the concrete jackets are cast neat in the utility trench
The upper portion of the trench backfill from a level of 1 foot above the pipes to the
finished subgrade should consist of approved on site soils or select granular material The
backfill material should be moisture conditioned to at least the optimum moisture content
placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a
minimum of 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for future ground
subsidence The upper 2 feet of the trench backfill below the pavement subgrade should
be compacted to no less than 95 percent relative compaction Mechanical compaction
equipment should be used to compact the materials Water tamping jetting or ponding
should not be used to compact the backfill material

Final drawings and specifications for the proposed construction should be

Construction Monitoring
Geolabs should be retained to provide geotechnical engineering services during
construction The critical items of construction monitoring that require Special Inspection
include observation of footing subgrade excavation and preparation and placement and
compaction of fill materials A Geolabs representative should also observe other aspects
of earthwork construction This is to confirm compliance with the intent of the design
concepts specifications and or recommendations and to expedite suggestions for design
changes that may be required in the event that subsurface conditions differ from those
anticipated at the time this report was prepared The recommendations provided herein
are contingent upon such observations
If actual exposed subsurface conditions encountered during construction differ
from those assumed or considered for this report Geolabs should be contacted to review
and or revise the geotechnical engineering recommendations presented herein

LIMITATIONS
The findings and discussion submitted herein are based upon information obtained
from limited field borings Variations of subsurface conditions between and beyond the
field borings may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become
evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be
necessary to re evaluate the findings and discussion provided herein
The borings locations indicated in this report are approximate having been taped
from features shown on the Schematic Site Plan transmitted by Architects Pacific Inc on
The physical boring locations should be considered accurate only to the
degree implied by the methods used
The stratification lines shown on the graphic representations of the borings depict
the approximate boundaries between soil and or rock types and as such may denote a
gradual transition Water level data presented on the logs of borings of this report were
based on measurements recorded in the borings at the times shown on the graphic
representations These data have been reviewed and interpretations made in the
formulation of this report However it must be noted that fluctuation may occur due to
variation in tides rainfall temperature and other factors
