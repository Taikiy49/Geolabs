The project site is located at the proposed Phase Palehua East B Subdivision
located in Makakilo on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The purpose for this letter report is to
summarize our findings pertaining to the surface groundwater seepage conditions
encountered at a portion of the project site and provide recommendations to mitigate the
effects of the groundwater seepage located within the affected lots
Background
In late 2004 three areas of surface groundwater seepage were identified located in
the vicinity of Lot Nos 7 and 8 Park Engineering performed a ground survey of the
observed areas of seepage on as directed by our field representative
Subsequently we prepared a letter report dated addressing the three
areas of water seepage encountered at Lot Nos 7 and 8
In January 2005 following some additional site clearing and the partial excavation
of some new cut slopes located in the vicinity of Lot Nos 1 through 8 several additional
areas of surface groundwater discharge were identified Due to the various seepage
areas being obstructed by construction material stockpiles and debris ground surveys of
the known areas of surface groundwater seepage were not completed until
It should be noted that the limits of the previously identified areas of seepage located at
Lot Nos 7 and 8 were expanded based on the additional exposure and monitoring of the
seepage zone s The limits of the observed groundwater seepage previously identified at
Lot Nos 7 and 8 have been revised and are presented in this letter report

Groundwater Seepage Conditions
Based on our observation of the various surface groundwater discharges
encountered at the project site the seepage appears to be a natural discharge resulting
from percolating groundwater which appears to be perched above a relatively low
permeability geologic material weathered basalt rock The observed seepage appears to
be continuous and relatively unaffected by local precipitation events In general the
seepage discharges are of small magnitude with estimated flow rates of less than
5 gallons per minute observed at each of the individual seepage locations
Based on our periodic monitoring of the identified areas of surface water seepage
over the past 9 months we have compiled the surveyed location of the areas of water
seepage The seepage areas consist of eight locations identified as Seepage Areas A
through H where wicking moisture at the ground surface and visible clear water
discharges were observed emanating from the Sloping hillside The surveyed ground
surface elevations at the various discharge areas range between about 787 feet and
800 feet Mean Sea Level MSL The following is a discussion of the individual
groundwater seepage areas identified as Seepage Areas A through H as shown on the
General Site Plan Plate 2
Seepage Area A
Seepage Area A encompasses a wet area of approximately 80 lineal feet in
length located near the toe of a new cut slope as shown on the Site Plan 1 Plate 3 1 The
seepage area consists of an elongated region of surface moisture wicking and some slight
visible water flow emanating from the cut slope between elevations of about 796 through
about 801 feet MSL The surrounding slope surfaces located adjacent to the seepage
area appears to be dry Wet ground conditions extend along the mapped seepage
boundary and down toward the toe of the cut slope The seepage water appears to
percolate back into the ground at the toe of the slope producing only occasional ponding of
water mainly during wet weather conditions The seepage discharge from Area A is
estimated to be less than about 1 gallon per minute
Because the Seepage Area A is located adjacent to a planned landscape area
and the discharge water appears to be percolating back into the ground along the slope
toe we believe that no action is necessary to collect or divert the seepage occurring at the
site

sewer line alignment and extends approximately 25 feet into Lot No 1 The seepage
area is located approximately 50 feet away from the nearest proposed building

Seepage Area D
Seepage Area D encompasses a wet area of approximately 35 lineal feet in
length located near the toe of the cut slope as shown on the Site Plan 4 Plate 3 4 The
seepage area consists of an elongated region of surface moisture wicking and visible
slight water discharge emanating from the cut slope at elevations between about

seepage down toward the toe of the cut slope where some ponding of water was

shown on the Site Plan 4 Plate 3 4 Due to the anticipated low flow rate of less than
about 2 gallons per minute it may be feasible to provide a subdrain outlet connected to

Seepage Soft Soil Area E
Based on our observations and the contractors report of heavy construction
equipment encountering an area of localized soft yielding ground conditions the area
identified as Seepage Soft Soil Area E was delineated and surveyed Area E is located
within the proposed building pad at Lot No 6 as shown on the Site Plan 4 Plate 3 4
Area E was observed to contain vehicle ruts with an intermittent occurrence of some
water ponding within the wheel ruts The ground surfaces Surrounding Area E were
otherwise observed to be generally firm and dry The ground surface elevations
encompassed by Area E range between about 788 feet and 790 feet MSL
We recommend that Area E be over excavated to remove the existing wet and
softened soils to expose firm subgrade The over excavation should be replaced with
suitable compacted fill materials In addition the subdrains constructed along the site
retaining wall and building wall at Lot No 6 should reduce the seepage encountered at
Area E

Seepage Area F
Seepage Area F encompasses a wet area of approximately 95 lineal feet in
length spanning across the building pads at Lot Nos 7 and 8 as shown on the Site Plan
4 Plate 3 4 The seepage area consists of an elongated region of surface moisture
wicking and slight visible water flow emanating from the existing slope at elevations
between about 787 feet through about 790 feet MSL The hillslope surfaces on either
side of the seepage area appear to be dry Wet ground conditions extend downslope
from the line of seepage discharge indicated on the Site Plan 4 Plate 3 4 The wet
ground area located below the line of seepage appears to have been wetted by water
flowing down from the upslope line of seepage discharge
The continual seepage discharge emanating from Seepage Area F is estimated
to be on the order of about 3 to 5 gallons per minute The seepage discharge is
substantial enough such that water continuously flows along the ground surface toward
the gulch located further toward the north Efforts by the contractor to temporarily
intercept the seepage discharge and channel the surface water flow away from the
building pad at Lot No 9 have been successful The surface discharge flow is currently
extending downslope from Area F and passes north of the proposed building pad at
Lot No 9
We recommend that a subdrain system be constructed to intercept the moisture
and divert it to an appropriate disposal location The subdrain system should consist of a
subdrain located along the site retaining wall and a second subdrain located along the
building walls at Lot Nos 7 and 8 as shown on the Site Plan 4 Plate 3 4

length spanning across the rear area of Lot No 8 as shown on the Site Plan 5

ranging between about 792 feet and about 796 feet MSL The hillslope surfaces on

downslope from the line of seepage discharge as indicated on the Site Plan 5 Plate 3 5

extending downslope as sheet flow from Seepage Area G and across Lot Nos 8 and 9
toward the gulch
Seepage Area G appears to be far enough away from the proposed building

Seepage Area H
Seepage Area H encompasses a_ localized topographic depression of
approximately 10 lineal feet wide located within the backyard area of Lot No 8 as
shown on the Site Plan 5 Plate 3 5 The seepage area encompasses a natural
groundwater spring which produces continuous visible water flow emanating from the
existing ground surface at an elevation of about 791 feet MSL The flow continues
downslope toward the gulch across Lot Nos 8 and 9 The flow is estimated to be
approximately 5 gallons per minute or less The hillslope surfaces on either side
laterally away from the spring appear to be dry The flow extends downslope from the
spring discharge elevation as indicated on the Site Plan 5 Plate 3 5
The continuous discharge emanating from Seepage Area H is estimated to be
on the order of about 5 gallons per minute or less The surface discharge flow is
currently extending downslope as weakly channeled stream flow beginning at Seepage
Area G and across Lot Nos 8 and 9 toward the gulch

locations at Lot Nos 8 and 9 such that a subdrain may not be necessary However the

Lot No 8 and across Lot No 9 toward the gulch We recommend that the existence of the
seepage and or subdrain system be disclosed to the lot purchasers
Subdrain System Concept
In general we recommend that the subdrains consist of a perforated 6 inch PVC
pipe set a minimum of 3 feet below the ground surface The subdrain pipes should be
aligned to intersect the anticipated elevation of seepage indicated by the mapping survey
The subdrain trench and pipe should extend a minimum of 2 feet below the anticipated
seepage elevation to intercept the target seepage elevation zone The perforated pipe
should be placed with a minimum of 1 percent slope gradient to facilitate pipe drainage to
a suitable outlet A typical trench subdrain detail is provided on Plate 5
We envision that the subdrain systems for Lot 6 7 and 8 will consist of
two subdrains The first subdrain should be located along the upslope site retaining walls
and the second subdrain should be located along the lower building wall A sketch of the
recommended subdrain system for the Model 5 homes is presented on Plate 4 We
