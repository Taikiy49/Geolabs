Recommendations for Tunnel Backfilling
Kauai Intermediate School
Increment Two
Dear Mr Lee
Based on our field observations and Clarification Request CR No 22 we understand
that an underground tunnel was encountered during the site grading operations for the
above referenced project It appears that the tunnel traverses across the entire project site
with portions of the alignment running underneath the new building foundations Field
measurements indicated that the top of the tunnel encroached within the bottom of footing as
shallow as 2 feet in localized areas An abandoned pipe was observed inside the unlined
tunnel and appeared to be running the full length of the tunnel alignment The void space
between the crown of the pipe to the top of the tunnel measured up to about 3 feet in depth
In order to reduce the potential for loss of foundation support underneath the footing
foundations as well as future ground subsidence resulting from the collapse of the tunnel we
recommend that the tunnel and pipe be properly backfilled with flowable fill lean concrete or
similar materials injected at low to moderate pressures Sufficient grout intake points should
be established along the tunnel alignment to ensure that the entire tunnel and pipe within the
project limits are completely backfilled The backfilling operations should be conducted under
the observation of a representative from Geolabs Hawaii
We appreciate the opportunity to be of service to you on this project If you have
questions or need additional information please contact our office
