PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located between Tulutulu Point and Nuututai Point in Utulei on
the western side of Pago Pago Harbor on the island of Tutuila American Samoa The site
is currently a tank farm with existing above ground fuel storage tanks It is proposed to
construct three new above ground storage tanks at the site We understand that two of
the tanks will be approximately 100 to 110 feet in diameter and the third tank will be about
50 feet in diameter

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to drill two borings at the project site to depths
of about 100 feet below the existing ground surface The borings were drilled at locations
within the abandoned motor pool parking area as directed by a representative of ASPA
The borings were drilled sampled and logged to provide subsurface soil information to be
included in a design build bid package for the project Geotechnical engineering
recommendations are not included in this scope of work The bidders would need to
perform a geotechnical engineering exploration for geotechnical engineering recommen
dations

DISCUSSION
The boring locations herein referred to as Boring Nos 1 and 2 were marked in the
field by Mr Eugene Smith of ASPA The locations of the borings are shown on the Site
Plan Plate 1 Prior to the commencement of drilling each boring the drilling and sampling
tools were decontaminated by steam cleaning in an effort to reduce potential contamination
of the subsurface materials
Soil Sampling
Fhe subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling two
borings to depths of about 82 and 116 feet below the existing ground surface The
GEOLABS HAWAII
GMP Associates Inc Page 4
W O 3260 00
borings were drilled using a CME 45 truck mounted rotary drill rig advancing hollow stem
augers casing or core drilling tools as appropriate
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our field geologist who observed the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis All soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified
Soil Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic presentation of the materials
Soil samples were obtained from the borings in general accordance with ASTM Test
Method D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 2 inch
O D standard penetration test sampler or 3 inch O D Modified California sampler with a
140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the
last 12 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate
sample depths
Core samples of materials encountered at the site were obtained using diamond
core drilling techniques in general accordance with ASTM Standard Practice D 2113 83
Diamond Core Drilling for Site Investigation Core drilling is a rotary drilling method which
uses a hollow bit to cut into the rock formation The rock material left in the hollow core
of the bit is mechanically recovered for examination and description
Recovery REC is used as a subjective guide to the interpretation of the relative
quality of rock masses Recovery is defined as the actual length of material recovered
from a coring attempt versus the length of the core attempt such as if 3 7 feet of material
is recovered from a 5 foot core run the recovery would be 74 percent and would be shown
on the Logs of Borings as REC 74
GEOLABS HAWAII
GMP Associates Inc Page 5
W O 3260 00
The Rock Quality Designation RQD is also a subjective guide to the relative quality
of rock masses RQD is defined as the percentage of the core run that is sound material
in excess of 4 inches in length without any discontinuities discounting any drilling induced
fractures or breaks If 2 5 feet of sound material is recovered from a 5 foot core run the
RQD would be 50 percent and would be shown on the logs of borings as RQD 50
Boring No 1 Drilling and Sampling
Boring No 1 was drilled by advancing 6 inch diameter hollow stem augers to a
depth of about 15 feet below the ground surface Field screening of the soil cuttings and
samples collected in the upper 15 feet of the boring for organic vapors revealed only
background levels of organic vapors suggesting contamination was not present or present
at undetectable levels by the field instrument The field vapor screening was conducted
utilizing an HNu model PI 101 portable photoionization detector PID meter The results
of the PID readings are provided on the logs of borings
Therefore drilling was continued by rotary wash advancing 4 inch diameter casing
to a depth of 59 feet below the ground surface At this depth the drilling became hard and
a switch to HQ wireline diamond core drilling was warranted to complete the boring to a
depth of 82 feet below the existing ground level
Upon completion of Boring No 1 the open hole was backfilled with collapse material
and cuttings generated from the drilling operation The upper 15 feet of the borehole was
backfilled with a cement bentonite grout to aid in reducing potential contamination of the
subsurface from surface contaminants
_ One environmental soil sample was collected from a depth of about 5 feet below
ground level from Boring No 1 This sample was contained and preserved for shipment

chemical analyses are presented in Appendix B
Boring No 2 Drilling and Sampling
The drilling and sampling of Boring No 2 was initiated by advancing 6 inch diameter
hollow stem augers to a depth of about 15 feet below the ground surface Field screening
of the soil cuttings and collected samples for organic vapors confirmed the presence of
petroleum hydrocarbon contamination within this zone
Because of the detected hydrocarbon contamination the boring was then filled with
a cement bentonite grout and allowed to harden overnight in an attempt to seal the upper
contaminated soil profile from contaminating the formation material below during
subsequent drilling and sampling The following day the boring was continued by rotary
wash advancing 4 inch diameter casing through the grouted zone and into the formation
material below Subsequent field screening for organic vapors in samples collected below
a depth of about 15 feet revealed no detectable contamination Soil cuttings generated
from the auger drilling of the upper 15 feet of the boring were collected and placed on a
plastic sheeting
Boring No 2 was advanced with 4 inch diameter casing to a depth of 64 feet below
the ground surface Due to a change in lithology and hardness at a depth of about 65
feet the remaining portion of the boring was advanced by HQ wireline diamond coring
tools The boring was terminated at a depth of about 116 5 feet below the ground surface
One environmental soil sample was collected from a depth of about 3 feet below
ground level from Boring No 2 This sample was contained and preserved for shipment
GEOLABS HAWAII
GMP Associates Inc Page 7
W O 3260 00
to Honolulu to an analytical laboratory for analysis for BTEX compounds Results of the
chemical analyses are presented in Appendix B
Upon completion of the borehole the open portion of the hole was sealed with a
cement bentonite grout to aid in reducing the spread or migration of potential contamina
tion into the deeper subsurface materials or the groundwater table Cuttings generated
from the potentially contaminated zone were wrapped in plastic sheeting and left at the
boring site for proper disposal by ASPA
Laboratory Testing and Results
The two environmental soil samples collected from near the water table in Boring
Nos 1 and 2 were sealed chilled and sent to an analytical laboratory in Honolulu for
testing The soil samples were analyzed for the presence of BTEX compounds by EPA
Test Method 8020 and 5030 Please refer to Plates B 1 through B 3 for the laboratory
report presenting the results of the analytical testing
The results from the laboratory tests indicate that BTEX compounds were not
detected in the soil samples submitted for testing However according to our field
screening tests conducted with a photoionization detector volatile organic compounds
were detected in the upper 15 feet of Boring No 2 The range of detected concentrations
varied from about 30 ppm in the subsoils just below the pavement to about 9 to 12 ppm
at a depth of about 13 feet below the ground surface Volatile organic concentrations
decreased to less than 1 ppm below a depth of about 15 below ground level
The discrepancy between the field screening and the laboratory test results may be
GEOLABS HAWAII

submittal to the laboratory The standard holding time for chilled soil samples submitted
for BTEX testing was not exceeded
It is our opinion that the field screening results are relatively accurate and that
hydrocarbons are presented in the subsoils at the location of Boring No 2 Additional soil
samples could be collected by hand excavation of grab or composite samples for
laboratory analysis if desired to evaluate the extent of the petroleum hydrocarbons
encountered
LIMITATIONS
The findings submitted in this report are based in part upon information obtained
from field data points such as drilled borings Variations of conditions between the field
data points may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become
evident until construction is underway If variations become evident it will be necessary
to reevaluate the findings presented in this report
The locations of the field borings were chosen by a representative of ASPA
Elevations were estimated from the Alternative No 5 Site Plan submitted to our office on
January 26th by your office
The stratification lines shown on the graphic representations of the borings depict
the approximate boundaries between the soil types and as such may denote a gradual
transition Water level data from the borings were measured at the time shown on the
graphic representations and or in the text of this report However it must be noted that
fluctuation may occur due to variations in rainfall tides temperature and other factors
