Dear Mr. DeMuesy: 
In accordance with the project specifications, we conducted a compressive pile load 
test on a selected test pile (Pile No. 221) for the MALS 24 Aircraft Maintenance Expansion 
project at the Ground Support Equipment (GSE) Holding Shed in the Marine Corps Base 
Hawaii at Kaneohe on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. This letter report presents the results of 
our field observations and the test data collected during the static pile load test conducted 
on December 9, 2014. 
The static pile load test was conducted on a 16.5-inch octagonal precast, 
prestressed concrete pile.  The test pile was driven on December 3, 2014, using a Delmag 
D30-23 diesel hammer capable of delivering a maximum rated energy of about 
66,100 foot-pounds per blow of the hammer. Fuel setting No. 4 (rated energy of about 
66,100 foot-pounds per blow of the hammer) was used throughout the majority of driving 
to install the pile to the tip elevation of about -26.4 feet Mean Sea Level (MSL). During the 
test pile driving operations, a log of the blow counts needed to advance the test pile was 
maintained by our engineer. The approximate load test pile location is shown on the Test 
Pile Location Plan, Plate 1.  The test pile driving record is provided on Plate 2 for 
reference. 
The test pile was loaded in general accordance with ASTM D1143, Quick Load 
Test Method.  The maximum test load on the test pile was planned at about 360 kips.  In 
general, the load test was loaded to a test load of 360 kips (240 percent of the design load 
of 150 kips) in 22.5-kip load increments.

In general, each load increment of the load test was held for at least 8 minutes. In 
addition, 2-hour holds were maintained at 180 and 360 kips to evaluate the creep effects 
of the pile.  After loading the pile to the maximum test load (360 kips), the test load was 
unloaded in approximately four equal decrements and held for at least 5 minutes at each 
decrement. The load test results are presented on the attached Static Load Test Data 
Sheets, Plates 3.1 through 3.5 and are also summarized as follows.

pile is provided on Plate 4.  Based on the shape of the load test curve and the Davisson 
failure criteria, the 16.5-inch octagonal concrete test pile (Pile No. 221) did not fail at the 
maximum test load of 360 kips.
