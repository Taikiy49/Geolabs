INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed UNOCAL Car Wash Facility project within the existing
UNOCAL gasoline station located at 234 Lunalilo Home Road in the Hawaii Kai area
of Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the
project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering recom
mendations resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering
analyses These recommendations are intended for the design of foundation site
grading and pavement only The findings and recommendations presented herein are
subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a new car wash facility within the existing UNOCAL
gasoline station at 234 Lunalilo Home Road in the Hawaii Kai area of Honolulu on the
island of Oahu Hawaii Based on the preliminary site plan we understand that the
new car wash facility will be located within the existing station along the northwestern
boundary where the existing service garage and some storage containers are presently
located We understand that the existing service garage some pavements and other
structures will be demolished in order to construct the proposed new car wash facility
The finish floor elevation for the proposed new structure was not available at the
time this report was prepared Based on our field observations the site is relatively
level therefore we envision that the proposed new car wash facility will be constructed
near existing grades to match the existing structures and pavements Based on this
information we anticipate that minor cuts and fills on the order of less than 1 to 2 feet

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lavas from
two shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated
to be of the middle to late Pliocene in age and Koolau is estimated to be of the late
Pliocene to early Pleistocene in age When the building of the Waianae and Koolau
volcanoes came to an end only a slight embayment existed on the southern coast of
Oahu
The project site is located on the southeastern flank of Koolau and its
geomorphology and subsurface conditions are directly related to glacio eustatic
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3405 00 E Page 5
fluctuations of sea level during the Pleistocene Epoch Ice Age The base of the
stratigraphic section in this area is Koolau Basalt The Koolau volcanic shield was built
by thinly bedded lava flows during the late Pliocene and early Pleistocene Epochs The
main shield building activities ceased approximately 2 to 3 million years ago
During the Pleistocene Epoch sea levels fluctuated in response to the cycles of
continental glaciation As the glaciers grew and advanced less water was available
to fill the oceanic basins such that sea levels fell below the present stands of the sea
When the glaciers melted and receded an excess of water became available such that
the sea levels rose to above the present sea level
The higher sea level stands caused the formation of deltas and fans of
accumulated terrigenous sediments in the heads of old bays accumulated reef deposits
at correspondingly higher elevations and lagoonal marine sediments in the quiet waters
protected by fringing reefs The lower sea stands caused streams to carve valleys in
the sediments and reef deposits Subaerial exposure of the sediments and calcareous
materials caused consolidation of the soft deltaic materials and lagoonal deposits and
induration of the calcareous reef materials Land development and reclamation projects
within the last century have brought the project site to its present form

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field exploration the proposed project site is generally underlain
by medium dense to dense gravels and sands and stiff silts extending to depths of
about 20 to 21 feet below the existing ground surface Groundwater was not
encountered in the borings at the time of our field exploration
Based on the results of our field exploration and laboratory testing we believe
that a shallow foundation system bearing on the near surface medium dense gravelly
soils may be used to support the proposed structure Slabs may be supported on
grade provided that the subgrade are prepared as recommended herein
We envision that the proposed structure will be constructed near existing grades
to match the existing structures and pavements Based on this information we
anticipate minor site grading consisting of cuts and fills on the order of less than 1 to
2 feet will be needed to provide a level area for the proposed structure
Detailed discussions and recommendations for design of foundation site grading
pavement and other geotechnical aspects of the project are presented in the following
sections of this report
Foundations
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered in our exploration we believe
that a shallow foundation system consisting of spread and or continuous footings
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3405 00 E Page 8
bearing on the near surface medium dense to dense gravelly soils may be used to
support the proposed structure An allowable bearing pressure of up to 3 500 p s f
may be used for the design of footings bearing on the medium dense gravelly soils
This allowable bearing pressure is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by
one third Ys for transient loads such as temporary wind and or seismic forces
Footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent
finished grade We estimate that the total footing settlements under the anticipated
design loads to be on the order of less than 1 inch with differential settlements of
approximately inch
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by frictional resistance
between the base of the spread footing and the bearing materials and by passive earth
pressure developed against the near vertical faces of the embedded portion of footings
A coefficient of friction of 0 45 may be used between the base of the footings and the
supporting materials Resistance due to passive pressure may be calculated using an
equivalent fluid pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth p c f for
footings embedded in the gravelly soils These values assume that the concrete for
footings is placed directly against the footing excavations Unless covered by
pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the upper 12 inches below finished grade
should be neglected
Uplift loads if any may be resisted by a combination of the following 1 dead
weight of the structure and 2 weight of backfill material above the footing where
applicable Contribution to uplift resistance due to the weight of the backfill above the
footing may be estimated using a unit weight of 100 p c f
We understand that the proposed car wash facility will be located along the
northwestern boundary where it will be close to the existing rock retaining wall
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3405 00 E Page 9
Therefore we recommend that all new footings be extended to a depth below an
imaginary 45 degree plane projected upward from the bottom of the existing rock
retaining wall to avoid surcharging the adjacent rock retaining wall with additional
structural loads
Footing excavations should not be allowed to dry before placing concrete If
shrinkage cracks appear in the footing excavations the soil should be thoroughly
moistened to close all cracks and recompacted to provide a firm base prior to concrete
placement
Footing excavations should be observed by a representative of Geolabs Hawaii
prior to the placement of reinforcing steel or concrete to confirm the competency of the
bearing materials and embedment depths If unsuitable materials are encountered in
the footing excavations these materials should be over excavated to expose firm soils
and replaced with compacted engineered fill
Slabs On Grade
We anticipate that concrete slabs on grade will be used for the proposed car
wash structure We recommend that the subgrade be properly prepared and kept moist
prior to placement of cushion fill and slab concrete We envision that the slabs on
grade will bear on the medium dense gravelly soils near the ground surface As
previously noted a very thin clayey silt layer which is potentially expansive was
encountered between the existing asphalt concrete and gravels in the three borings
Therefore we recommend that the clayey silt layer be removed where encountered
and be replaced with compacted granular soils In general the subgrades for slabs on
grades should be scarified to a depth of at least 8 inches moisture conditioned to
above the optimum moisture and compacted to not Jess than 95 percent relative
compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 3405 00 E Page 10
as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil determined in accordance
with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91 Optimum moisture is the water content
percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
Since the slabs for the car wash facility will be subjected to vehicular traffic we
recommend that the slab be supported on a 6 inch layer of base course compacted to
a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction For design of structural slabs a modulus
of subgrade reaction of 250 pounds per cubic inch p c i may be used for the
compacted base course The thickened edges of slabs adjacent to any unpaved area
should be embedded at least 12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade
Site Grading
Final grading plans were not available at the time this report was prepared
However we anticipate minimal grading with cuts and fills on the order of 1 to 2 feet
relative to the existing ground surface may be expected for the proposed construction
Site grading operations should be observed by a representative of Geolabs
Hawaii It is important that a representative from our office observe whether the
exposed soil conditions during excavation are similar to those encountered in our
exploration The following recommendations are intended to provide guidelines for the
design of site grading work
After demolition of the existing service garage existing pavements and other
structures all debris generated from the demolition should be removed and properly
disposed of off site All subgrades should be scarified to a minimum depth of 8 inches
properly moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and compacted
to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in
place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3405 00 E Page 11
same soil determined in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91 Optimum
moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the
maximum dry density
Any soft weak or yielding areas encountered during site grading should be over
excavated to expose firm ground and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with
engineered fill compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Where
encountered the clayey silt layer encountered in the three borings should be removed
and be replaced with granular soils Prior to placement of the backfill the over
excavated subgrade should be scarified to a depth of at least 8 inches moisture
conditioned to above the optinum moisture content and compacted to at least 90
percent relative compaction The material resulting from the over excavation should
be removed and properly disposed of off site
Generally the on site sandy and gravelly soils may be reused as a source of fill
and backfill material It should be emphasized that the materials generated from the
demolition should not be used as fill and backfill materials The on site soils should be
properly moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content placed in level
lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of
90 percent relative compaction The compaction requirements should be increased to
a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction for fills placed below areas subjected to
vehicular traffic
Permanent cut and fill slopes may be designed with a slope inclination of two
horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V or flatter Fills placed on slopes steeper than 5H 1V
should be keyed and benched
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3405 00 E Page 12
If imported fill materials are required the imported fill and or backfill material
should consist of well graded granular materials less than 3 inches in maximum
dimension The material should have a laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR value
of 15 or more and should have a maximum swell of less than 1 percent when tested
in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1883 In addition it should contain
between 10 and 30 percent passing the No 200 sieve Imported fill materials should
be tested by Geolabs Hawaii for conformance with our recommendations prior to
delivery to the project site for its intended use Imported materials should be brought
to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to not less than 90 percent
relative compaction and 95 percent relative compaction for fills placed below areas
subjected to vehicular traffic
Pavements
New flexible pavements are anticipated for the driveway and parking stalls for
the proposed construction We anticipate the vehicle loading for this project will consist
primarily of passenger vehicles light pick up trucks and occasional heavy trucks We
have made our preliminary pavement design assuming the pavement subgrade soil will
be similar to the gravelly soils encountered in the borings with a minimum California
Bearing Ratio CBR of 15 and swell of less than 1 percent Based on the above
assumptions we recommend the following pavement sections be used for preliminary
design purposes
Asphaltic Concrete Pavement
2 0 Inch Asphalt Concrete
6 0 Inch Basalt Base Course 95 percent relative compaction
8 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness on a Moist Compacted Subgrade
GEOLABS HAWAII

The pavement subgrade soils should be scarified to a depth of at least 8 inches
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to not
less than 95 percent relative compaction The base course should consist of crushed
basalt aggregate compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
Laboratory CBR tests should be performed on samples of the actual subgrade
soils encountered during construction to confirm the above recommended design
section The recommended pavement section assumes that good drainage will be
provided for areas adjacent to the pavements
Paved areas should be sloped and drainage gradients maintained to carry all
surface water off the site Surface water ponding should not be allowed anywhere on
the site during or after construction Where concrete curbs are used to isolate
landscaping in or adjacent to the pavement areas we recommend that the curbs
extend a minimum of 2 inches into the subgrade below the aggregate base course to
reduce migration of landscape water into the pavement section Alternatively a
subdrain system could be constructed to collect excessive water from landscaping
irrigation For long term performance we recommend a subdrain system be
constructed adjacent to paved landscape areas
Utility Trench
We envision that new utility lines would be required for the proposed
construction In general for support of the utility lines we recommend that a granular
bedding consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation
be used under the pipes The backfill up to about 1 foot above the pipes should consist
of free draining backfills such as No 3B Fine gravel to reduce the potential for
damaging the pipes from compaction of the backfill
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3405 00 E Page 14
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 1 foot above the pipes to
the top of the subgrade or finished grade should consist of select granular material or
on site soils The backfill should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum
moisture content placed in maximum 8 inch level loose lifts and mechanically
compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for
future ground subsidence Where trenches are below pavement areas the upper 2 feet
of the trench backfill below the pavement subgrade should be compacted to not less
than 95 percent relative compaction
Existing Underground Utility Lines
Underground utilities are anticipated beneath the existing ground surface We
recommend that all existing utility lines within the project limits be accurately located
by the contractor prior to construction The contractor has the sole responsibility to
devise and implement an effective program to protect or relocate the utility lines during
construction
Drainage
The finished grades outside the building should be sloped to shed water away
from foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding Also it is advised that gutter
systems be installed around the buildings and that the discharge be diverted away from
the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near the
foundations and slabs should also be avoided Planters next to foundations should be
avoided or have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the
subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil
saturation and subsequent loss of strength and or heaving of the near surface soils
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3405 00 E Page 15
The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled against the slab footings
immediately after setting of the concrete to reduce water infiltration
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and maintained to drain
all surface run off away from the slabs and footing foundations
