SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is located on the northwestern corner of
the intersection of Ka Haku Road and Pepe Street in the
Princeville development area in Hanalei Kauai
The site is bordered by Pepe Street on the north and east
Ka Haku Road on the south and vacant land similar to the
site on the west
The Sandpiper Village I is presently under construction east
of the site across Pepe Street
The topography at the site is gently sloping down toward the
north at gradients of about 10 per cent or flatter The
area is vacant and covered with short grass and weeds
The ground elevations at the site vary from about 234 to 207
feet
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
The proposed construction consists of 2 story townhouse
structures grouped in clusters of 2 to 6 units per building
Wood framed structures utilizing post and beam construction
are anticipated
Asphaltic concrete driveway and parking areas are also planned

Two tennis courts are planned in the western portion of the
site
Cuts and fills generally less than 6 feet are planned for the
project site grading
FIELD EXPLORATION
Six test pits were excavated at the site by a backhoe to depths
of 6 to 8 5 feet below the existing ground surface
Based on the field test pit exploration the subsoils at the
site can be generalized as a surface topsoil layer about
6 inches thick of brown clayey silt with roots over stiff
reddish brown clayey silt residual soil to the depths
excavated
A stockpile of medium brown clayey silt with many roots about
4 to 5 feet high was noted in the southern portion of the
site
Groundwater level was not encountered in the dug test pits
CONCLUSTON AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that the site is underlain by
stiff and competent residual soil From a soil engineering
point of view the site is suitable for the proposed development

Generally shallow spread footing foundations can be used for
the proposed structures and conventional site grading opera
tions can be used
A stockpile about 4 feet high of brown clayey silt with many
roots and organics was noted in the southern portion of the
site The approximate limits of the stockpile are shown on
the Site Plan Plate 1 It appears that the stockpile may
be a result of surface topsoil from previous development
dumped at this area The stockpile material should be stripped
down to the stiff natural ground and either removed from the
site or stockpiled for later landscape use only Because of
its high root content this stockpile material should not be used
as a source of structural fill under the building or parking
areas
Foundations
Spread or continuous footing foundation are recommended for
the structures on level or gently sloping areas An allow
able bearing value up to 3000 p s f is recommended for
footings bearing on the stiff residual soil and 2000 p s f
for footings bearing on compacted fill The above values
are for total dead plus live loads and may be increased by
one third when temporary loads are considered
GEOLABS HAWAII
August 25 1980
Diamond West Development of Hawaii Inc
Page 5
On level areas the bottom of the above designed footings
should be embedded a minimum of 12 inches below the outside
finished grade to resist erosion
Where footings are situated on or near the tops of slopes
the bottoms of the footings should be embedded a minimum
5 foot horizontal set back distance from the outer edge of
footing out to the slope face This additional embedment is
required to resist erosion and slope creep and to develop
the bearing capacity of the supporting soil
Less than 4 inch minor footing settlements are anticipated
for the above designed footings
Surface water should be diverted away from the building
foundations
Site Preparation and Earthwork
Cuts and fills generally less than about 6 feet are antici
pated for the general project site grading Based on the
test pit data relatively easy site excavation can be anti
cipated
The site should be cleared of vegetation and debris then
scarified and recompacted to 90 of its maximum dry density
established by ASTM D 1557
GEOLABS HAWAII
August 25 1980

Fills should be placed in 9 inch loose lifts compacted to
the above specified density
The on site excavated soils free of vegetation and debris
can be used as a source of fill material
It is recommended that if additional off site borrow is
required it should be tested and approved by the soils
engineer prior to transportation to the site to assure that
it is suitable for the proposed construction The material
should generally be non expansive and should not contain
large percentages of boulders organic or deleterious
matter
For cut and fill slopes 2 horizontal to 1 vertical maximum
slope ratios are recommended
All fill slopes should be track rolled or overfilled and cut
back to obtain a well compacted slope
To minimize erosion all slopes should be planted as soon
as practical
Paving for Driveway and Parking Areas
The proposed access roads and parking areas will be located
both in cut and fill

Plans for foundations and site grading should be forwarded
to the soils engineer for review and written comments prior

LABORATORY TESTING
Moisture content and Atterberg Limits were performed on
selected samples as an aid in the classification and eva
luation of soil properties The results of these tests are
presented on the Test Pit Logs Plates A 1 through A 6
Two CBR tests were performed on bulk samples of the near
surface soils in the proposed parking and driveway areas
to evaluate the suitability of the soil as pavement support
The test results are presented on Plate B 1 One moisture
density modified proctor test was performed on a bulk
sample of the near surface soil to evaluate its compaction
characteristics The test result is presented on Plate B 2
The swell tests on one inch thick ring samples with 55
p s f surcharge load were conducted on two remolded samples
to get an indication of the swelling potential of the near
surface soils The results of the swelling tests are pre
sented on Plate B 3
