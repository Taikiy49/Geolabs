PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS

about 1 045 acres in Koloa on the south shore of Kauai and includes a hotel vacation
ownership commercial park golf course and residential uses The Parcel F Kula Makai
Subdivision project is part of the improvements for the Kukui ula Development
Parcel F is a subdivision project located on the western half of the Kukui ula
Resort Community development makai of Noho Kai Road overlooking the 16th hole of
the Kukui ula Golf Course and the Pacific Ocean The parcel encompasses
approximately 7 1 acres with 12 individual house lots designated as Lots Nos 1 through
12 In addition roughly 1 400 linear feet of concrete walkway was installed A Project
Location Map and Site Plans are shown on Plate 1 and Plates 2 1 and 2 2 respectively
Parcel F Lot Nos 1 through 3 is located along the south side of the new
Noho Kai Road at the east and was initially graded during work for the Parcel A project

PARCEL F EARTHWORK OPERATIONS
We observed the mass and pad grading and sidewalk subgrade construction work
for the Parcel F Subdivision project from through The
mass and site grading work consisted of clearing and grubbing cuts of up to about 5 feet
deep and placement and compaction of fills up to about 16 feet thick to achieve the
finished grades Prior to filling the existing ground surface was cleared and grubbed of
vegetation and deleterious material then proof rolled with a compactor Kaiwa
Construction Inc performed the mass and pad grading and Pacific Concrete Works Inc
performed the sidewalk subgrade preparation
The remedial mass and pad grading for Parcel F Lot Nos 1 through 3 occurred
simultaneously with the rest of the project Earthworks Pacific Inc excavated the
non conforming fill materials and placed them in deeper fills within Lot Nos 4 through 7
The materials removed from Lot Nos 1 through 3 primarily consisted of cobbles and
boulders in a clayey silt matrix The cobble and boulder fills were seated with a dozer then
compacted with a large sheepsfoot wheel compactor
During the site grading operations samples of the materials utilized as fill were
obtained and transported to our office for testing The maximum dry densities and
optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general accordance with
ASTM D1557 Modified Proctor and the test results are summarized on Plate 3
The fill materials were moisture conditioned to near optimum moisture content and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as determined in
accordance with ASTM D1557
During the mass and pad grading construction operations periodic field density
tests were performed in general accordance with ASTM D6938 Nuclear Gauge Method
Compaction test results are summarized on Plates 4 1 through 4 13 These tests were
performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills at the
site Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site
survey stakes These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the

HOUSE CONSTRUCTION CONSIDERATIONS
The finished grades adjacent to the house structures should be sloped to shed
water away from the foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also
advised to install gutter systems around the house structures and to divert the discharge
away from the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering within
3 feet of the foundations and slabs should be avoided Gravel strips adjacent to slabs
and foundations should be avoided Planters next to foundations should be avoided or
have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
house foundations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation and
subsequent loss of strength of the near surface soils The foundation excavations
should be properly backfilled against the footings immediately after setting of the
concrete to reduce water infiltration Drainage swales should be provided as soon as
possible and should be maintained to drain surface run off away from foundations
Homeowners should also be advised not to block or alter surface drainage
Professional consultation including a licensed geotechnical engineer should be
obtained in the planning design and construction of houses and improvements
