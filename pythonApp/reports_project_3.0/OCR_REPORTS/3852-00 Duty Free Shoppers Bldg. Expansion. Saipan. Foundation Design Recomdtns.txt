Foundation Design Recommendations
Duty Free Shoppers Building Expansion
Beach Road Garapan Village Saipan
Commonwealth of the Northern Mariana Islands
In accordance with our fee proposal dated July 5th we have performed a
geotechnical engineering evaluation for the proposed expansion of the existing Duty Free
Shoppers Building project at the above referenced site
The project site is located along Beach Road in Garapan Village on the island of
Saipan The proposed expansion consists of a three story concrete building structure
with the upper two levels for vehicle parking We understand that a geotechnical
engineering report entitled Geotechnical Investigation Garapan Galleria Beach Road
Garapan Saipan Commonwealth of the Northern Mariana Islands dated
was prepared by Geotesting Inc for the subject project Based on our review of this
report we understand that continuous concrete spread footings bearing on 3 foot thick
layer of recompacted limestone fill were recommended for foundation support of the
proposed structure
Foundations
According to the project structural engineer maximum column loads for the
proposed structure are estimated to be on the order of about 700 kips Based on our
review of the available information and results of engineering analyses we believe that
isolated spread and continuous wall footings may be used for foundation support of the
proposed structure We estimate that footing settlements under the anticipated design
loads for footings bearing on the near surface in situ dense sand of about 15 feet thick to
be less than 1 inch total with differential settlements on the order of inch

Based on the available subsurface soil information footing settlements would
derive primarily from compression of the loose to medium dense lagoonal deposits below
the surface crust of dense sand Settlement from the near surface dense sand would be
minimal Therefore it is our opinion that over excavation and recompaction of the near
surface dense sand below the proposed footing bottom elevation may be omitted
The footings may be designed for an allowable bearing value of 2 500 pounds per
square foot p s f This allowable bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may be
increased to 3 750 p s f for transient loads such as temporary wind and seismic loads
The tops of the footings should be embedded a minimum of 12 inches below the finished
grade The bottoms of the footing excavations should be recompacted prior to concrete
placement If the bottoms of the footing excavations encounter wet and yielding
conditions during recompaction the loose and wet soils should be over excavated to
dense sand and backfilled with granular fill compacted to a minimum of 95 percent
compaction
Design Review
Drawings and specifications for the proposed construction should be forwarded to
Geolabs Hawaii for review and written comments prior to construction This review is
necessary to evaluate adherence of the plans and specifications to the intent of the
foundation recommendations provided herein If this review is not made Geolabs Hawaii
cannot assume responsibility for misinterpretation of the recommendations presented
herein
Construction Observation
It is recommended that Geolabs Hawaii be retained to provide geotechnical
engineering services during construction of the foundations of the building particularly for
the observation of footing excavation and subgrade conditions This is to observe
compliance with the intent of the design concepts and to expedite suggestions for design
changes that may be required in the event that subsurface conditions differ from those
anticipated at the time this letter report was prepared The recommendations provided in
this report are contingent upon such observations
Limitations
It should be noted that a site specific geotechnical engineering exploration has not
been conducted by our office for the subject project The analyses and recommendations
presented herein are based upon the information provided in the above referenced
report No warranty is expressed or implied If actual exposed subsurface conditions
encountered during construction are different from those assumed and presented in the
above referenced report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
