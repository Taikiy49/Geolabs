In accordance with our fee proposal dated May 5 2003 we have conducted a
geotechnical engineering exploration at the project site to assist in the feasibility
assessment for the proposed Hilton Lagoon Improvements project This report
summarizes our field exploration and analyses and presents our discussions and
recommendations with respect to the proposed lagoon filling
Project Considerations
One objective of the Hilton Lagoon Improvement project is to attain and maintain
the water quality in the lagoon at acceptable levels as specified by the State Department
of Health A key element to achieving adequate water quality is to increase the turn over
rate of the water in the lagoon It is envisioned that water will be pumped into the lagoon
to improve circulation and achieve turn over at an average interval of 6 hours By
reducing the volume of water in the lagoon the inflow water requirement to achieve the
desired turn over rate will be correspondingly reduced
The decrease in lagoon volume will be achieved by decreasing the area as well as
the depth of the current lagoon Various configurations of the reduced lagoon area are
being considered It is anticipated that the lagoon depth will be reduced to about 5 5 feet
below Mean Sea Level by the placement of fill over the lagoon bottom
The existing lagoon bottom is covered with very soft sediments Therefore there
are two alternatives for the fill placement One alternative would be to remove very soft
sediments and replace them with an equivalent amount of imported granular fill Besides
the costs of excavating and hauling the soft sediments off site this alternative would
greatly increase the construction time and traffic Another alternative would be to utilize
special stabilization procedures to allow placement of the new fill over the soft sediments
The feasibility of this alternative is the focus of our study

Discussion and Recommendations
It is desired to decrease the volume of water in the lagoon by filling to reduce the
area of the lagoon and also by raising the bottom level to create a shallow lagoon Special
procedures are required to fill the lagoon due to the presence of the very soft sediments at
the lagoon bottom Normally the soft sediments would have to be removed by dredging
and disposed offsite In order to reduce the costs and disruption caused by this
procedure the feasibility of leaving the sediments in place and filling over them was
evaluated Two alternative design concepts to allow filling over the soft sediments were
developed and are described below
Geotextile
The key element to both of the methods to fill over the soft sediments is the
geotextile layer The geotextile layer provides separation between the very soft sediments
and the sandy gravelly fill above This prevents sand gravel loss into the soft sediments
and prevents the mud and fine soil particles from contaminating the sand gravel fill The
geotextile layer can also provide tensile reinforcement to help spread the load from the
new fill onto the soft sediments thus allowing the earlier use of light equipment and
reducing the creation of mud waves For this project we recommend Mirafi HP665
geotextile or equivalent
Since tensile reinforcement will be a critical requirement for the geotextile seaming
of the geotextile is required Other considerations such as placement method and anchor
trenches are dependent on the alternative selected and are described below
Installation Method No 1 Dry or Dewatered Condition
The present lagoon has a system of pumps and intake boxes that allow dewatering
of the lagoon for periodic maintenance If the lagoon is dewatered to allow
exposure of the mud surface the fill can be placed using conventional soft ground
stabilization methods
First a stabilization layer is created by placement of the geotextile over the soft
mud The geotextile comes in rolls 15 feet wide by 300 feet long Placement may
be accomplished by rolling out the geotextile by laborers The edges of adjacent
rolls are sewed in the field as they are placed
Light equipment is then used to spread a layer approximately 1 5 to 2 feet thick of
coarse gravel such as No 3B Fine over the geotextile To reduce the creation of
mud waves the gravel should be placed in discontinuous rows to attempt to
balance the load on the soft sediments Due to the soft and yielding nature of the
very soft sediments new fills should be placed in thin level lifts at slow filling rates
to reduce the possibility of shear failure and mud waves in the soft sediments

Once the gravel layer is established the sandy soils may be placed using the same
light equipment The edges of the geotextile layer should be placed in anchor
trenches to confine the soft material After the final grades are reached including
anticipated settlements the lagoon water may be restored
The advantage of this method is that this type of soft ground stabilization has been
constructed on many projects on Oahu Therefore local contractors are familiar
with the procedure and have suitable equipment for this construction However the
sediments in the lagoon are softer than most above sea level deposits and will
require very careful fill placement
The disadvantage of this method is that once the sediments are exposed the odors
may be objectionable to the hotel guests We anticipate that the stabilization and
filling process may take one to two months to complete
Installation Method No 2 Placement in Water
The geotextile and fill may be placed through the water without the need for
dewatering pumping Some special equipment may be needed for this alternative
The geotextile may be placed from boats or rafts in the lagoon The geotextile
would need to be cut and sewed prior to placement Since the geotextile can float
we envision that the geotextile would be fan or accordian folded and pulled across
the lagoon by the boats rafts Sand would then be placed on the floating geotextile
causing it to sink to the bottom of the lagoon As with the dry method the sand fill
should be placed slowly and spread around the lagoon bottom to reduce the
possibility of shear failure and mud waves in the soft sediments
Placement of the sand fill will require special sand pumps or large cranes If
off shore sand mining is allowed this method would be particularly attractive since
the sand could be pumped directly into the lagoon
Following completion of the fill placement the edges of the geotextile layer should
be anchored into the coral sand and gravel The water level in the lagoon could be
temporarily lowered to facilitate the construction of the anchor trenches below the
normal lagoon water level to avoid future exposure of the trenches
The advantage of this method is the elimination of the odors from the exposed
sediments and reduced construction traffic particularly if the sand mining is
allowed
The disadvantage of this method would be the specialized equipment needed
which could limit the number of contractors available to bid on this project

Settlements
Compression of the very soft sediments will occur due to the weight of the new fills
It is difficult to accurately estimate the settlements since the soils are too soft to obtain
quality samples for consolidation testing Additionally the sediments may be
under consolidated from the weight of the existing sediments We estimate that the
sediments may compress up to half of the present thickness Therefore additional fill
quantity will be required to compensate for the settlement

necessary to re evaluate the findings and discussions provided in this letter report
The locations of the field borings indicated in this report are approximate having

by Planning Solutions dated The approximate elevations of field

locations and elevations of the borings should be considered accurate only to the

The stratification lines shown on graphic representations of the borings depict the

exploration However it must be noted that groundwater level changes may occur due to
tidal fluctuations seasonal precipitation surface runoff and other factors
