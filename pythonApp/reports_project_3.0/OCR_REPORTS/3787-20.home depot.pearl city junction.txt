INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration performed
for the proposed Kamehameha Highway Sign Bridge as part of the proposed The Home
Depot Pear City Junction Project The proposed sign bridge will be located over the
westbound lanes of Kamehameha Highway about 475 feet east of Acacia Street in the
District of Pearl City on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the
project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering recommendations
resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering analyses These
recommendations are intended to assist in the design of the sign bridge foundations only
The findings and recommendations presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at
the end of this report

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from two shield
volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be middle to
late Pliocene in age and forms the bulk of the western third of the island The younger
shield Koolau is estimated to be late Pliocene to early Pleistocene Ice Age in age and
forms the majority of the eastern two thirds of the island Waianae became extinct while
Koolau was still active and its eastern flank was partially buried below Koolau lavas banking
against its eastern flank These banked or ponded lavas formed a broad plateau referred
to as the Schofield Plateau
GEOLABS INC
W O 3787 20 Page 5
The Schofield Plateau was formed when lavas from the Koolau Volcano ponded
against the already eroded slopes of the Waianae Volcano in the late Pleistocene Epoch
The dips of the lava beds are near horizontal between 3 and 5 degrees from horizontal
The lava flows on the plateau have undergone in situ weathering to depths of 50 to 100 feet
and are characterized by the red colors of the soil The deep narrow canyons such as
Waikakalua Kipapa and Kaukonahua are the result of this in situ weathering
This in situ chemical weathering of the lava has formed a mantle of residual soils that
grade to saprolite with depth In general saprolite is composed mainly of silty material and
is typical of the tropical weathering of volcanic rocks Spheroidally weathered boulders are
typical in the Schofield Plateau The saprolite grades to basaltic rock formation with depth

Drilled Shaft Foundations
Based on the loading conditions provided and the subsurface conditions
encountered we recommend that the sign bridge pole at the median be supported
by a drilled shaft foundation
Based on the diagonal length of the 24 inch square anchor plate 34 inches the
available auger core barrel sizes from the local drilling companies and the shaft
lengths anticipated drilled shaft diameters of 42 inches were considered in our
foundation analyses for the single pile foundations For two pile foundations drilled
shaft diameters of 24 inches with a minimum spacing of 60 inches on center were
considered in our foundation analyses The major considerations in our foundation
analyses included the lateral loads and ground line moments induced in the drilled
shaft foundations
In general lateral load resistance for drilled shafts is a function of the stiffness of the
surrounding soil the stiffness of the shaft allowable deflection at the top of the
shaft and induced moment in the shaft Based on a maximum horizontal deflection
of one fourth inch at the top of the drilled shaft subjected to ground line
overturning moments and lateral forces we have evaluated the required drilled shaft
lengths using the computer software program LPILE Plus for Windows This
GEOLABS INC
W O 3787 20 Page 11
program is a microcomputer adaptation of a finite difference laterally loaded pile
program originally developed at the University of Texas at Austin The program
solves for deflection and bending moment along a drilled shaft under lateral loads
as a function of depth The analyses was carried out with the use of internally
generated non linear p y curves to represent soil moduli The lateral deflection
was then computed using the appropriate soil moduli at various depths The
analyses were performed for a laterally loaded cast in place concrete drilled shaft

Torsion Resistance
In general pole foundations will be subjected to torsional moments resulting in torsion
on the drilled shaft foundations For single pile foundations torsion may be resisted
by the side shear along the drilled shaft surface and adjacent soils For two pile
foundations torsion may be resisted in the same manner as for overturning moments
and lateral loads In our foundation analyses we have evaluated the drilled shafts
subjected to the torsional moments provided by the project structural engineer
Based on our analyses we believe that the recommended diameter drilled shafts
extending to the lengths recommended should be able to resist the torsional moments
without significant movements We wish to emphasize that the sides of the drilled
holes should be relatively free of loose materials and the concrete for the drilled shaft
should have close contact to the in situ soils after the drilled shaft has been
constructed to provide the torsional resistance required
Uplift Resistance
Uplift loads may be resisted by a combination of the dead load of the pole structure
and drilled shaft foundation and by shear along the shaft surface and the adjacent
soils The combined dead load of the pole structure and drilled shaft foundation alone
were determined to be sufficient to resist the uplift loads without significant
movements
Construction Considerations
The performance of drilled shafts will depend significantly upon the contractor s
method of construction construction procedures and workmanship As a result we
recommend that a representative from Geolabs Inc be present to observe the
installation of drilled shafts during construction In our opinion the following may
have a significant impact on the effectiveness and cost of the drilled shaft
foundations
Difficult drilling conditions may be encountered and should be expected during
installation of the drilled shafts due to the potential for encountering hard zones within
GEOLABS INC
W O 3787 20 Page 13
the basalt formation during the drilled shaft installation The drilled shaft contractor
will need to have the appropriate equipment and or coring tools to drill through the
rock formation Appropriate measures by the contractor will also be needed to avoid
dislodging boulders or fractured rock pieces into the drilled shaft excavation prior to
placement of concrete
The load carrying capacity of drilled shafts depends to a large extent on the contact
between the drilled shafts and the surrounding soils Therefore proper construction
techniques are important The contractor should exercise care in drilling the shaft
holes and in placing concrete in the holes The bottom of all drilled shafts should be
cleaned of disturbed material prior to constructing the foundation The bottom should
also be fairly level Backfill against the shaft foundations should not be allowed
Drilling by methods utilizing drilling fluids slurry is not recommended Groundwater
conditions are not anticipated therefore placement of concrete by dry construction
methods is anticipated A low shrink concrete mix with high slump 6 to 9 inch slump
range should be used to provide close contact between the drilled shafts and the
surrounding soils The concrete should be placed in a suitable manner to reduce
segregation of the aggregates from the concrete mix

This section applies for the sign bridge pole mounted on the existing retaining wall
at the sidewalk location Note that based on available plans it appears that the footing of
the existing retaining wall is located about 4 feet below the existing sidewalk The following
parameters should be utilized in the analyses of the existing retaining wall with the loading
from the sign bridge pole
An allowable bearing pressure of may be utilized for the analyses of the
existing retaining wall This bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may be increased
by one third 1 3 for transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic forces

If soft and or loose materials are encountered within the exposed backfill or at the
bottom of the footing excavation they should be over excavated to expose firm materials
The over excavation should be backfilled with select borrow subbase material Section 31
of the Standard Specifications for Public Works Construction Department of Public Works
City and County of Honolulu September 1986 compacted to a minimum 95 percent relative
compaction ASTM D 1557 91 or with CLSM controlled low strength concrete or flowable
fill
Lateral loads perpendicular to the length of the street may be resisted by passive
earth pressure acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system and by
frictional resistance developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid
pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth pcf This assumes that the soil
around the footings is relatively undisturbed or well compacted Unless covered by
pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the upper 12 inches of soil should be
neglected A coefficient of friction of 0 35 may be utilized and a moist unit weight of 115 pcf
may be utilized for the on site soils
Lateral loads parallel to the length of the street may be resisted by friction resistance
developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil The same coefficient
of friction and moist unit weight values given for lateral loads perpendicular to the length of
the street may be utilized for the on site soils
Since a portion of the existing retaining wall will be removed to accommodate the pole
foundation the pole foundation should also be designed to withstand an active pressure of
45 pcf exerted by the retained soil In addition some type or doweling or continuous
reinforcement between the pole foundation and the existing retaining wall is recommended
to create a more integrated unit
