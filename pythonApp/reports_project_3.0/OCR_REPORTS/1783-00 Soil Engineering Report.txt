SOIL ENGINEERING REPORT
PROPOSED RESIDENCE
1635 PALOLO AVENUE
AS requested we have made a study of the near surface soil
conditions below the above referenced lot The purpose of the study was
to evaluate the suitability of the near surface soils as foundation
support for the proposed house The study consisted of drilling three
borings within the lot performing laboratory soil tests and reviewing
the preliminary house layout plans This report summarizes our
observations and recommendations
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
Based on the preliminary house plans provided to us the proposed
residence will consist of a two story structure with carport
Concrete slab on grade is planned for the garage and driveway
Post and beam construction is planned for the residence

EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The fairly level lot is located on the eastern side of Palolo Avenue
in Palolo Valley There is a one story house with a partial basement
presently situated on the site
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Three borings were drilled within the building area to depths
ranging from 4 9 to 9 8 feet below the existing grade at the approximate
locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
In general the borings encountered very stiff grayish brown clay
with cobbles and boulders to the full depths of the borings Detailed
description of the soils encountered in the borings are presented on the
attached logs Plates A 1 and A 2
Laboratory soil tests on the near surface material indicate that the
soil can be classified as Clay cH according to the Unified Soil
Classification System A laboratory one inch ring swell test indicates
that the soil has high shrink swell potential The results of the
laboratory one inch ring swell test are summarized on Plate B 1
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration disclosed that the lot is underlain by brown
clay The laboratory test results on this material generally indicate
that it has high expansion potential Therefore the Slab on grade and
post and beam foundations would require special design and construction
procedures in order to reduce the shrink swell effect of the subsoils

House Foundation
In order to reduce the expansive effects of the soils we recommend
that a minimum 3 foot capping layer of non expansive material be placed
below the slab on grade foundations
The expansive clay below and for a horizontal distance of 3 feet
around the slabs should be removed for a minimum depth of 3 feet below
outside finished grade and replaced with non expansive off site borrow
materials such as select borrow crusher waste or coral compacted toa
minimum of 90 of its maximm dry density per ASTM D 1557 78 The
underlying clay soil should be scarified covered with hydrated lime
moistened to above optimum moisture content and recompacted moderately
85 compaction prior to backfilling with the 3 foot select fill capping
layer
A 4 inch layer of base course should be provided below the slab In
order to protect against moisture infiltration a vapor barrier may be
incorporated below the slab
The thickened edge slab footings can bear on the compacted
non expansive fill The thickened edges should be embedded a minimm of
12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade for details refer to
Schematic Section Plate 2
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f may be used on the
compacted fill
For post and beam construction we recommend that the expansive
Clays be over excavated down for a depth of 3 feet below the bottom of
the individual post footings and then replaced with non expansive borrow
soils such as crusher waste select borrow or coral compacted to a
minimim of 90 of its maximm dry density per ASTM D 1557 78 The spread
footings can then be placed on the compacted non expansive soil for
details refer to Schematic Section Plate 3

An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f can be used for footings
bearing on the stiff natural ground or compacted fill
If a footing is located next to a utility line it should extend to
the bottom of the utility trench to reduce footing settlement due to
settlement of the trench backfill
Surface water should be diverted away from foundations This is
especially important in the more level portion of the house to avoid
ponding of surface run off water that may affect the expansive soils
around the foundation
Existing Basement
The existing basement will be backfilled prior to the new house
construction The bottom slab of the basement should be broken to allow
drainage and avoid water ponding The basement should be backfilled with
imported non expansive soil as described in the Site Grading section
Site Grading
Fills and backfills within 3 feet of the finished grade should he
selected non expansive material generally less than 6 inches in size
placed in maximum 6 inch loose lifts and compacted to 95 of its maximm
dry density established by ASTM D 1557 78 The on site clay soil should
not be used for fill material below structures
Some amount of boulders which were encountered during our
investigation should be anticipated during construction
Prior to filling the area should be cleared of vegetation and
debris

Drainage
The finished lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings
should be shaped to shed water away from foundations and to avoid ponding
condition Also it is advised that gutter systems be installed around
the house and that gutter water be diverted far away from the foundations
and walkways Excessive landscape watering near the foundations and slab
should also be avoided
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance
of the above recommended foundations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of
strength The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled tight
against the slab footing immediately after setting of the concrete to
prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained
to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundation
Design Review
Plans and specifications for foundation site grading and utility
construction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and
written comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during
earthwork construction to monitor the contractor s grading operations
particulary fill placement

It is also recommended that the footing excavations be examined by a

Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and

LIMITATIONS
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations
made in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation
engineering principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all
other warranties either expressed or implied
The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are based
on our site reconnaissance and soil information from boring and
laboratory test data obtained from this soil evaluation
Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encountered and cannot be
fully determined by reconnaissance and borings These unforeseen
conditions such as pockets of organic soils trash fill or seepage may
occur in localized areas Therefore additional expenditures may he
needed during construction to attain a properly constructed project
Some contingency fund is thus recommended to accommodate these possible
costs
