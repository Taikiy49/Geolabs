Dear Mr. Jusuf: 
This report summarizes our earthwork observation and testing services performed 
by Northwest Demolition and Dismantling for the Demolition Permit Package portion of the 
Straub Medical Center Redevelopment project on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The project consisted of demolishing the existing Bed Mart Mattress Superstore 
building at 788 South King Street in Honolulu on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. The existing 
building, including the basement level, was demolished and backfilled in preparation for 
the construction of a new parking garage at the project site.  The general vicinity of the 
project site is shown on the Project Location Map, Plate 1. 
EARTHWORK OPERATION 
Swinerton was the general contractor for the project covered by this report.  
Northwest Demolition and Dismantling was the site work contractor for the project covered 
by this report.  Our observation and testing services during the site work for the project 
occurred from May 2023 through June 2023. 
Fill and backfill materials generally consisted of excavated materials from the project 
site and imported materials. In general, fill materials consist of materials less than 3 inches 
in maximum particle size.  Fills and backfills required to raise the grades were generally

During the earthwork operations, samples of the on-site and imported materials 
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing. 
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our 
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D1557 (Modified Proctor), and the test results 
are summarized on the attached Plate 2. 
Field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations on an as-
needed basis to confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures 
and soil conditions were noted. A total of 93 field density tests were performed using the 
nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM D6938. Compaction test results are 
summarized on Plates 3.1 through 3.5. These tests were performed on the prepared 
ground surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills. Test locations and elevations were 
estimated by pacing and interpolating from on-site survey stakes and topographic 
features. These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the 
degree implied by the methods used. Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked 
until the specified minimum compaction was obtained. 
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the 
referenced tests, it is our opinion that the earthwork operations during the construction of 
the Demolition Permit Package portion of the Straub Medical Center Redevelopment project 
observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report were performed in 
general accordance with the intent of the project plans and specifications and subsequent 
recommendations. 
LIMITATIONS 
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities occurring 
simultaneously on a construction site. Therefore, some of the earthworks may have been 
performed without our observation. However, the level of earthwork observation and testing 
services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some of the geotechnical risks to 
the project. It should be recognized that there are variations in the accuracy and statistical 
repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor earthwork construction and materials. 
Our services were performed in accordance with generally accepted standards of 
geotechnical engineering practice; no warranty is expressed or implied. The provision of 
construction monitoring by an engineer is not insurance, nor does it guarantee construction 
of any type. The contractor shall retain responsibility for the quality of the work, for adhering 
to the plans and specifications, and for repairing defects regardless of when they are found.
