This report presents the results of our preliminary soils
investigation performed at the proposed Varsity Center site The general
location and the vicinity of the site are shown on the Project Location
Map Plate l
This investigation was performed in accordance with our verbal
discussion with you on at your office The scope of our
services consisted of drilling and sampling three 3 borings ranging
from 15 to 20 feet below the existing ground surface performing
laboratory testing and preparing a report

PROJECT CONSIDERATION
It is our understanding that the proposed Varsity Center consists of
clusters of one story wood framed buildings for retail shops with
on grade pedestrian mall and parking areas The existing structures at
the site will be demolished A preliminary layout of the project is
presented on the Site Plan Plate 2
Site grading is anticipated to be minimun
The design loading has not been developed at the time of preparing
this report However it is anticipated that the proposed foundation
loads will be relatively small
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located in the Moiliili area of Honolulu It is
bordered by Coyne Street on the north South Beretania Street on the
south and University Avenue on the east The western side is bordered
by existing buildings and a paved parking lot
At the time of our exploration the existing building structures on
the project site consisted of a service station and a garage one
single story concrete masonry building and two wood framed one story
residences Cracks were noted on the rear CMU wall of the garage at
about 65 feet from the Beretania Street sidewalk Several underground
utility lines and fuel tanks are located in the service station area
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1332 00 Page 3
The relief of the project site is essentially level with the
wood framed residential building area being slightly lower at the western
portion of the site
The vegetation at the site included a few trees on the western
portion around the existing residences
GEOLOGY
The project site is geologically situated in an area that borders
between a Quaternary limestone and recent alluviun Within the
surrounding Moiliili area the subsurface formations are typically
variable and discontinuous as experienced from previous project sites in
the nearby vicinity Some areas are underlain by emerged coral reef
formations relatively near the surface while other areas are underlain
by fairly thick deposits of soft and compressible clays and silts under a
layer of man made fill These variable formations are reflective of the
different depositional conditions changing sea levels and stream flow
Locally the project site appears to cover an area that may be
underlain with soft compressible clays silts that might have been
originated from a former swamp and also an area of coral reef
formation Historically a portion of the site is said to have been a
lily pond

SUBSURFACE EXPLORATION
The field investigation included drilling and sampling three 3
borings with depths to about 15 to 16 feet below the existing ground
surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 In
Borings 2 and 3 the drilling and sampling was followed by continuous
penetration probe down to a depth of about 20 feet The continuous
penetration test was made by drilling a 2 inch diameter blunt point
attached to a string of A size drill rods driven with a 140 pound
hammer falling 30 inches
Detailed descriptions of the materials encountered are presented in
the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 3 Laboratory testings
performed consisted of two consolidation tests three swell tests two
Atterberg Limits tests and several moisture density determination tests
The results are shown on Plates B 1 and B 4 The moisture and density
results are shown on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Subsurface Conditions
The subsurface exploration disclosed that the site is mantled by 3
to 6 feet of fill consisting of stiff to medium stiff brown clayey silt
and some crushed rock fragments It is underlain by 0 to 2 feet of soft
to medium stiff alluvial organic clayey silts overlying either a cemented
coral Boring 3 or 9 to 12 feet of light brown soft to very soft clayey
silt with sand layers to depths of 15 to 20 feet Borings 1 and 2

Water levels were noted in the borings varying from 4 5 to 10 5 feet
below the existing ground elevation
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Our borings disclosed that the majority of the project site is
underlain by soft compressible deposits Borings 1 and 2 Some ground
settlements should be anticipated if new fill is required We believe
that the majority of the settlements would occur quickly probably in
about two months after the fill is placed To induce as much ground
settlements to occur prior to foundation construction we recommend that
any new fill planned should be placed as soon as possible
Boring 3 located at the southeastern portion of the site
encountered the coral formation at a relatively shallow depth Due to
the thin layer of soft to medium stiff clay encountered overlying the
coral heavy column and masonry wall footings should be deepened and bear
on the coral formation Thickened edge slab footing for light
wood framed structures may bear on the upper fill layer
For clarity the southeastern portion of the site which is probably
underlain by thick coral formation is identified as the coral area on
the Site Plan Plate 2 The remainder of the site is underlain by a
thick layer of soft compressible soils and is identified as the
non coral area
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1332 00 Page 6
In the non coral area the building construction should be delayed
until most of the settlements due to site filling have occurred Then
spread footing foundations may be used for the building with allowance
for some settlements due to structural loads If the anticipated
settlements are not tolerable pile foundations may have to be considered
We believe the transitional zone between the coral and the non coral
areas as shown on the Site Plan should be better defined by additional
field exploration because differential settlements in this area may be
significant This additional exploration recommended may consist of
drilling 3 to 4 shallow borings to be located at the future building
areas near Boring 3
Detailed discussions on our recommendations are presented below

The compressible strata consist mainly of soft clayey silts
Therefore we anticipate that settlements in most areas would
develop quickly with the majority of the settlements occurring
within two months after the loading is applied It is likely in
areas underlain by pockets of soft organic silts and clays that fill
settlements would continue after the majority of the settlement has
occurred
The location and amount of filling are not available at this time
Based on our preliminary calculations on 2 to 2 5 feet of new fill
placement the anticipated settlements may range from 2 to 4
inches Settlements due to pockets of organic silts and clays under
the new fill placement might range from one half to one inch They
generally occur slowly and are difficult to predict
Settlements under foundations are discussed under the FOUNDATION
section
We recommend that if the site requires large amount of fills it
should be monitored by settlement gauges in order to determine when the
majority of the ground settlement has occurred and when building
construction can begin
GEOLABS HAWAII
If it is desired to reduce future settlements from the building and
floor loads a surcharge fill program may be considered We would be
glad to consult with you on such a program
Foundation
We believe that shallow spread footings may be considered for the
foundation support of the proposed buildings provided that these
structures are able to accommodate moderate amount of settlements Where
the settlement of spread footing foundation cannot be tolerated pile
foundation should be considered to Support the structures
Spread Footings
A Coral Area
In general we believe that thickened edge slab footings may be
considered for light weight structures The footings may be
designed to an allowable net soil bearing pressure of 1500
p s maximum The footing bottom should be embedded at least
12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade
If heavy column footings and or heavy wall footings are
required they should bear directly on the underlying coral
encountered at 4 5 foot depth in Boring 3 An allowable bearing
pressure of up to 6000 p s f may be used in coral formation
GEOLABS HAWAII
B Non Coral Area
In general spread footings should be Placed at a level as high
as possible to reduce the footing load applied over the
underlying compressible deposits It is recommended however
that the footing bottom be embedded at least 12 inches below the
lowest adjacent grade
Any soft soil encountered at the bottom of the footing
excavation should be over excavated to at least 2 feet below the
bottom of the footing and backfilled with compacted granular
material such as base course rock or crusher waste
A maximum net allowable soil bearing pressure of 1500 p s f may
be used for the above design
The anticipated footing settlement under column loading may
range from 0 5 to 1 5 inches It is probable that some footings
could be underlain by organic silt or Clay lens beneath the
fill For these footings we believe that settlements would be
slightly more and occur slowly
The wall foundation should be at least 2 feet wide and embedded
at least 12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade The
settlements under the wall footings would probably range from 1
to 1 5 inches with differential settlements of about one half
of an inch over a 15 foot span
GEOLABS HAWAII
Pile Foundation
To minimize building settlement a pile foundation should be
considered At present the depth to pile supporting strata have not
been identified in the non coral area If pile foundation support is
considered we believe deep borings should be made to identify depths and
types of suitable bearing strata If this is desired we would be
pleased to render additional investigations
Site Grading
Large site filling should be avoided at this project site
If small amount of fill less than 18 inches is required it should
be constructed as soon as possible to allow the ground to consolidate and
to minimize differential settlements

If any local soft spots are encountered in areas to receive fill
they should be removed and replaced with compacted structural fill
Areas to receive fill should be scarified to a depth of 9 inches
watered and recompacted to the requirement specified for structural
fill
Fill Material
Imported fill material should be of a granular non expansive nature
tested and approved by the soils engineer prior to importation
Structural Fill Placement
Approved fill material should be placed in 9 inch loose horizontal
lifts and then compacted to 90 relative compaction utilizing ASTM
D 1557 78 standard procedure
Drainage
Provision for a well designed and maintained drainage system at the
site should be considered Water should not be allowed to pond in
the vicinity of the structures

Parking Area
As mentioned under SITE GRADING the entire parking areas should
be scarified moisture conditioned and then recompacted to obtain uniform
subgrade condition prior to receiving structural fill and base course
layer
If the subgrade is bearing on granular select borrow or crusher
waste materials the following pavement design may be utilized
2 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 Inch Base Course
8 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
CBR tests should be performed on the actual subgrade soil samples
during construction and the above design section can be revised if
necessary In addition we recommend good drainage be provided for the
paved areas
Utility Lines
The underground utility lines should be placed as late as possible
after the completion of site filling to minimize post installation
Settlements In addition flexible joints should be considered
particularly at building connections to allow differential settlements
The trench excavation would require proper shoring particularly
where patches of soft organic clays or silts are encountered Since
dewatering may affect adjacent structures a soils engineer should be
consulted if water level is to be lowered
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1332 00 Page 13
The backfilling of trench should be consistent with the materials
and compaction requirements recommended under FILL PLACEMENT section
Generally for backfilling the utility trenches the compacted
density of the backfill from the pipe bed up to 12 inches above the pipe
should be a minimum of 85 of ASTM D 1557 78 designation The remaining
portion should be 90 If trenches are located under driveways or
parking areas the top 16 inches of the backfill from the finished grade
should be compacted to 95 minimum
Design Review
Plans and specifications for site grading foundation pavement and
utility constructions should be forwarded to the soils engineer for
review and written comments prior to construction to see that the intent
of our recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
The earthwork operation including hauling operation incidental to
ill placement should be monitored by a soils engineer during the early
Stage of site grading in order to detect soft pockets and determine the
necessity of their removal If fill and or surcharge fill is placed the
settlements should be closely monitored by a surveyor and settlement
gauge readings evaluated by the soils engineer
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1332 00 Page 14
We recommend that the footing excavations be inspected by the soils
engineer prior to placement of concrete or reinforcing steel to check
that adequate bearing has been obtained and that the subsurface
conditions are consistent with the findings of our investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
If conditions encountered are different from those presented in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
If the structural columns are to be supported on a pile foundation
the pile driving operations should be monitored by a qualified soils
engineer to verify that the piles have been driven adequately and in
accordance with our recommendations
