INTRODUCTION
The geotechnical engineering exploration was performed at the site of the existing
electrical substation W 5 for the proposed shoreline protection to the substation The
shoreline protection constitutes a part of the proposed electrical distribution system
improvements to the Naval Inactive Ship Maintenance Facility NISMF in Middle Loch of
Pearl Harbor on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location of the project site and
its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings of our exploration and presents our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from analyses of the data obtained
from the exploration These recommendations are intended for design input and are not
intended to form the basis for preparation of construction estimates or for bidding
purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
An existing electrical substation W 5 is located on land immediately behind the
shoreline of Middle Loch in the naval facility NISMF As a part of the proposed electrical
distribution system improvements a new transformer will be added to the substation
Based on the available information the new transformer will generate approximately 30
p s f bearing pressure on the underlying subsoils
It is our understanding that the shoreline easterly and northerly of the substation
is being progressively eroded due to wave action If the shoreline erosion continues it
appears that the substation including its currently proposed improvements could be
jeopardized
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2667 20 Page 3
Therefore it is proposed to prevent further shoreline erosion in front of Substation
W 5 with protection such as a rip rap slope a conventional retaining wall or a steel
sheetpile wall along the shoreline

The purpose of this exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions at the site to develop a generalized soil and or rock data set for the
formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to the selection and
design of a shoreline protection scheme

engineering exploration

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is situated within an Oahu Sugar Company cane field which is
located southerly of the main area of the naval facility NISMF along the west shore of
Middle Loch
The existing electrical substation W 5 is located approximately 6 to 15 feet from
the shoreline It is bounded on the south and west by an existing dirt road in the cane
field A power pole is located at a short distance of approximately 15 feet westerly of the
substation
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2667 20 Page 5
It is Our understanding that the on land substation is supplying electricity to an
existing power dolphin E 9 formerly called D 9 in Middle Loch by means of submarine
electrical cables Available plans indicate that the electrical cables route inside an existing
concrete stairway descending from the substation down to an existing handhole in a
concrete pedestal which is embedded in the mudline deposits in front of the shoreline
During our field exploration it was observed that the existing shoreline was a nearly
vertical cliff which may be a result of the progressive shoreline erosion Based on the
available plans it appears that the substation site on top of the cliff is approximately 16
to 17 feet above Mean Lower Low Water MLLW level per U S Navy Datum The
mudline at the bottom of the cliff and around the concrete pedestal in front of the
shoreline appears to be at approximately 2 to 4 feet above MLLW
The existing shoreline is fronted with dense mangrove trees and bushes

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on the subsoil conditions encountered and the existing topography various
schemes are considered in the following sections of the report for providing protection
to the existing shoreline in front of the substation W 5 from further wave erosion
Alternative Schemes
Three 3 alternative schemes are considered for use as the proposed shoreline
protection These schemes include the construction of a rip rap slope a conventional
retaining wall or a steel sheetpile wall along the shoreline in front of Substation W 5 The
following are detailed discussion pertaining to each of these schemes
Rip Rap Slope
For this type of protection scheme it is anticipated that the existing shoreline
would have to be either cut back or extended outward by filling over the mudline
to form a stable slope on which rip rap protection can be applied
However it appears that either scheme may not be feasible in terms of cost and
construction practicality due to the following reasons
1 A flat gradient such as one and one half 1 5 horizontal to one 1 vertical
would be needed to form a stable shoreline slope
2 Sufficient space is not available between the existing shoreline and the
substation including its proposed modifications to form such a cut slope
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2667 20 Page 8
3 If a fill slope in front of the existing shoreline is considered the construction
would be difficult and costly since some mudline dredging and a large
quantity of fill material including underwater fill would be needed
4 The difficulty of both cut and fill slope construction would be compounded
by the need to relocate and or rebuild the existing improvements in front of
the shoreline
Conventional Retaining Wall
If a conventional retaining wall is used it appears that a large footing may be
needed to support the wall with an anticipated free standing height of at least 16
feet According to the project structural engineer a wall footing on the order of 15
feet wide may be needed for a 16 foot high wall
Due to the soft mudline deposit anticipated in front of the wall it also appears that
the large wall footing may have to be embedded at least five 5 feet below the
mudline This embeddment will further increase the free standing height and thus
the size of the footing of the retaining wall making the wall construction more
difficult and costly
In addition it is recognized that there is limited space between the existing
electrical substation and the existing handhole in a concrete pedestal embedded
in the mudline It appears that the design and construction of a conventional
retaining wall with a large footing to fit within this space will be very difficult
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2667 20 Page 9
Therefore it is believed that a continuous steel sheetpile wall should provide a
better alternative to the proposed shoreline protection in terms of both cost and
construction practicality
Steel Sheetpile Wall
It is our understanding that a new transformer will be added to the substation as
a part of the proposed electrical distribution system improvements The new
transformer will have to be installed prior to construction of the proposed shoreline
protection If an anchored sheetpile wall is used it is our opinion that it will be
difficult to install anchors in the limited space among the existing and proposed
electrical facilities at the substation
Therefore we recommend that a cantilevered type of steel sheetpile wall be used
to protect the shoreline in front of Substation W 5 from further wave erosion
Cantilevered Sheetpile Wall
Based on the anticipated subsoil conditions a pressure diagram was developed
for the design of a cantilevered steel sheetpile wall protection as shown on Plate 3
The pressure diagram includes the lateral pressure resulting from an assumed
surcharge pressure of 30 p s f due to the existing and proposed substation improve
ments It also includes the lateral pressure from a maximum hydraulic head difference
of 2 5 feet which is anticipated between the water levels behind and in front of the
sheetpile wall for the purpose of the sheetpile design
However if more improvements will be constructed behind the sheetpile wall in the
future applicable surcharge loads if known will have to be incorporated in the pressure
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2667 20 Page 10
diagram for the sheetpile design Otherwise future improvements to the substation
which will add more surcharge loads to the sheetpile wall should be prohibited
It should be noted that no factor of safety is incorporated in the pressure diagram
As a minimum we recommend that a factor of safety of 1 2 be used to increase the
theoretical sheetpile embedment D calculated from the pressure diagram
Based on our rough estimate it appears that the steel sheetpile wall may need to
be driven to a minimum of 45 feet below the existing ground surface at approximately 116
feet MLLW adjacent to the substation
In addition we believe that adequately thick sheetpile sections may be needed in
order to reduce deflection of the cantilevered sheetpiles It is also our opinion that
adequately thick sheetpile sections should be used to withstand potential high impact
stresses which may result during driving of the sheetpiles through the underlying
competent subsoil strata

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from the field borings and probe Variations of subsoil conditions
as indicated by the borings and probe may occur and the nature and extent of these
variations may not become evident until construction is underway If variations then
appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations given in this
report
The locations of the field borings and probe are approximate having been
determined by field measurement with taping from existing structures and or objects
indicated on a topographic survey of the site dated May 31 1991 by R M Towill
Corporation The surface elevations of the borings and probe were estimated by
reference to the elevation points and contours on the topographic survey The physical
locations and surface elevations of the borings and probe shouid be considered accurate
only to the degree implied by the method used

depict the approximate boundaries between soil types and may not represent the actual

Waiter level data from the borings were collected at the times given on the graphic
