INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this investigation was to define subsurface condi
tions at the proposed site and to provide recommendations for the
earthwork and pavement design and for the design and construction
of the foundations to support the proposed structure
W Page 1975
Site Description
The proposed site is immediately adjacent to the existing Zippy s
Drive Inn on the south side of South King Street The essentially
level ground surface is partially covered by loose soil gravel
_and concrete rubble remaining from the previously razed structure
The ground surface varies in elevation from approximately 11 0
feet at the northwestern corner to 7 6 feet at the rear property
line There are some minor depressions in the existing surface
which has resulted in ponding during wet weather
Project Description
The proposed development will consist of a one story restaurant
and surrounding paved parking area It is anticipated that slab
on grade construction will be utilized with masonry walls for the
center section and wood frame wide span construction for the
surrounding areas
The restaurant will be located essentially at grade in the front
center portion of the site Shallow fills will be utilized to
raise the low lying areas at the rear property line
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored utilizing three
test borings drilled at the locations shown on the enclosed Site
Plan Plate 1 All borings were extended to a depth of 20 feet
utilizing truck mounted auger equipment

Soil Conditions
The explorations disclosed that the site is blanketed by 4 to 6
feet of extremely stiff fill The fill is a brown clayey silt
with gravel and sand and is underlain by 2 to 5 feet of medium
dense cinders brown silty sand and gravel Below the cinders
a very stiff to extremely stiff brown clayey silt with sand and
gravel was encountered It extended to the limit of the explora
tions at a depth of 20 feet
Ground water was encountered between 7 and 8 feet below the exist
ing ground surface

LABORATORY TESTING
To aid in the classification and identification of the soils
natural moisture content in place density and atterburg limits
tests were made on selected samples The results of these tests
are shown on the boring logs at the appropriate sample location
A swell potential test on a sample of the surface soil obtained
from Boring 1 showed a potential expansion of less than one per
cent The fill is therefore considered to be non expansive
A bag sample of the surface soil was utilized for moisture
density and CBR value testing of the subgrade support These
test showed that the soil has a maximum dry density of 92 pounds
per cubic foot and an optimum moisture content of 30 5 per cent
The CBR test results summarizied on page 5 show a corrected
CBR value for this soil of 29

Foundations
The existing fill at the site is generally extremely stiff and
should provide adequate support for spread or line foundations
bearing a minimum of 12 inches below the final adjacent grade
and designed for maximum contact bearing pressures of 3 000
pounds per square foot The allowable bearing pressure can be
increased by 1 3 when considering seismic and wind loadings
It is estimated that the maximum settlements should not exceed
It is recommended that the site be proof rolled and the surface
compacted to 90 per cent of the maximum dry density to assure
uniform support for the proposed foundations Any soft zones
uncovered during the proof rolling operations should be removed
and the area backfilled with soil compacted to a minimum of 90
per cent of the maximum dry density
Due to the possibility of buried foundations or other subsurface
irregularities from the previous structure all foundations
should be inspected by a soils engineer to verify that adequate
bearing capacity has been obtained Foundations uncovered in
the building or pavement areas should be undercut a minimum of
12 inches below the proposed subgrade and covered with compacted
backfill

Earthwork Compaction Requirements
All areas to receive fill should be scarified to a depth of 6
inches brought to near optimum moisture content and compacted
to a minimum of 90 per cent of the maximum dry density The esti
mated maximum density is 92 pounds per cubic foot at an optimum
moisture content of approximately 31 per cent The laboratory
testing also indicates that the in place moisture content of the
natural soils generally exceeds the optimum moisture content
These on site soils are suitable as fills but will require some
drying to obtain the proper moisture content for compaction
The earthwork placement and compaction should be inspected to as
sure that the recommended degree of compaction is consistently
achieved All fill should be compacted to 90 per cent of the
maximum dry density
Asphalt Pavement
The CBR testing indicates that the existing soils when compacted
properly will provide good subgrade support for the proposed
pavement The following pavement section should be considered
for the preliminary design of the paved areas
2 in Asphalt Concrete
4 in Base Course
