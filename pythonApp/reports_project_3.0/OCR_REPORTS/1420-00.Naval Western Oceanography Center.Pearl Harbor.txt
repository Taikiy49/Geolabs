site is situated over a 17 to 23 foot fill crust
consisting of silty sand with gravel and concrete
rubble over soft silty clay hydraulic fill Hard
tuff was encountered below the upper fills
Spread or wall footing foundations may be utilized
for the proposed building provided that the upper
portion of the existing fill within 3 feet of

INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our soil and
foundation investigation performed for the proposed
Meteorological Building for the Naval Western Oceanography
Center in Pearl Harbor Oahu Hawaii

The general location of the project site and its vicinity
is shown on the Project Location Map Plate l
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our investigation was to evaluate the
subsurface conditions at the site to provide pertinent soil
data such as shear strength compressibility and classifi
cation for the foundation design of the proposed structures
The scope of our investigation which was performed in
general accordance with our proposal dated May 1 1984 included
field reconnaissance drilling and sampling of 10 borings
laboratory soil testing engineering analysis utilizing the
field and laboratory data and report preparation
The boring logs and laboratory test results are presented
in the appendices of the report
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
The proposed building will be a one story reinforced
concrete structure approximately 20 700 s f in size
Provisions will be incorporated to accommodate a future second
floor

Maximum column loads of 340 kips and wall loads of 8 kips
per foot are anticipated for the structure
Parking areas are planned on the northeastern and south
western sides of the building
Minimal site grading is anticipated
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located on the northern side of
Luapele Drive adjacent to the guard house The area is
surrounded by access roads and parking lots
The site topography is irregular with concrete rubble and
cobbles noted on the ground surface The ground surface
generally slopes down toward the southwest at an average slope
of less than 5 per cent
The project area is presently covered with low brush and
trees

Soil Conditions
The soil conditions encountered in the borings may be
generalized as follows
1 Fill 17 to 23 feet thick consisting of medium dense
silty sand with gravel and concrete rubble Trash
and organic material were not encountered in the
fill
2 Hydraulic Fill 0 northwestern side to 14 feet

encountered at depths between 17 to 36 5 feet
below ground surface
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings at the time
of the field exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The project site is situated near the edge of Makalapa
Crater which is composed of tuff The lower areas of the crater
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1420 00 Page 5
were hydraulically filled in the 1940 s and subsequently covered
with various types of land fills ranging from good quality
Material to trash
The soils encountered in the borings reflect the above
sequence of events The tuff formation was encountered at
deeper depths in the borings and the surface of the formation
slopes down gently toward the south or center of the crater
The soft clay from the hydraulic fill overlies the tuff and is
overlain by the upper land fills
Airphotos of the area indicate that the existing fill over
the soft clay has been in place for many years over 15 years
and theretore would indicate that the ground settlements due to
the weight of the fill has essentially stopped The laboratory
consolidation tests appear to confirm this
For the light structure planned the zone of influence due
to the foundation loads would essentially be confined to the
upper fill layer Therefore if the settlements due to the
weight of the existing fill are completed and additional fills
are not placed then the condition of the upper fill would
govern the building foundation design The borings drilled
indicate that the upper fill material generally consists of
suitable soils although the in place density or consistency is
variable
GEOLABS HAWAII
This is in contrast to previous borings drilled for the
nearby BOQ and Mess where wood metal and miscellaneous rubble
were encountered in the fill
It is our opinion that if additional fills are minimized
to less than 1 foot and if the special preparation of the upper
fill is performed as described below spread or continuous wall
foundations may be used for the proposed structure
Site Preparation
We recommend that the fill within 3 feet of finished grade
below the building and pavement areas should be over excavated
and then stockpiled for re use
The excavated surface should then be scarified to a
minimum depth of 10 inches and flooded with water to attempt to
fill possible voids below the surface After drying to optimum
moisture content the scarified and wetted ground surface should
then be re compacted with a vibratory roller to a minimum of 95
of its maximum dry density as established by ASTM D 1557 70
The 3 foot over excavated stockpiled fill material which
is free of organic material and generally less than 6 inches in
size may be reused to backfill the excavated site
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1420 00 Page 7
The fills should be placed in horizontal lifts not
exceeding 6 inches in compacted thickness and compacted to a
minimum of 95 of its maximum dry density as established by ASTM
D 1557 70
The imported fill material if required should be
granular materials such as coral or tuff free of organic
material non expansive and generally less than 6 inches in
size
Site grading should provide drainage of surface runoff
away from all foundations
Building Foundation
Following the site preparation as described above we
believe spread or continuous footing foundation may be used to
support the proposed building
Footings should be placed a minimum of 18 inches below the
lowest adjacent grade and should bear on the dense re compacted
fill layer
An allowable bearing value of 3000 p s f may be used for
foundations on the above prepared compacted fill The bearing
capacity vaiue is for total dead plus live loads and may be
increased by one third for short duration wind and or seismic
loading
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1420 00 Page 8
A minimum footing width of 18 inches is recommended
The bottom of all footings should be compacted to a
minimum of 95 of its maximum dry density as established by ASTM
D 1557 70 and inspected by the soils engineer prior to concrete
placement
If a footing is situated next to a utility trench it
should be extended to the bottom of the trench or the trench
backfill must be well compacted to prevent footing settlement
due to settlement of the trench backfill
Estimated settlements are anticipated to be on the order
of less than one half inch total and one quarter inch
ditferential
Slab On Grade
Generally the on site soils have a low swell potential
therefore slab on grade construction may be utilized
For the anticipated static floor loads a 4 inch slab over
4 inches of well graded aggregate base course is recommended
A plastic vapor barrier over 4 inches of 3 fine gravel
may be substituted for the base course should a capillary break
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1420 00 Page 9
be deemed necessary to protect the floor covering A thin layer
of sand should be placed over the plastic barrier to aid in the
concrete curing and minimize shrinkage cracks
Loading Dock Wall
A short loading dock wall generally less than 5 feet high
is planned along the southern side of the building
An allowable bearing value of 3000 p s f is recommended
for the wall footings bearing on the compacted fill
Well graded granular material such as base course rock
sand or equivalent should be used as backfill material directly
behind the walls This would minimize the compactive effort
required for the backfill and reduce the build up of additional
lateral pressures on the walls
We recommend that the walls should be designed to resist
lateral earth pressures as follows
Unrestrained at top equivalent
active condition fluid pressure
Restrained at top equivalent
at rest condition fluid pressure
Additional loads due to surcharge or traffic
should be included where applicable

A friction factor of 0 4 between concrete and soil can be
utilized to determine the sliding resistance of the wall
Additionally a passive soil resistance of 300 p c f can
be used
A subsurface drainage system should be used to prevent the
build up of hydrostatic pressure behind the walls
