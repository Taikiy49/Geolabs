SITE DESCRIPTION
Kamalu Road is located in the Wailua Homestead area of Kauai
Kalama Stream presently crosses below the existing Kamalu
Road through a culvert structure The new road crossing and
bridge structure over Kalama Stream is located about 500 feet
north of the present road crossing and culvert structure
The terrain around the area is gently rolling and covered
with grass and trees
The existing Kalama Stream is about 80 feet wide and about
3 to 5 feet deep At the time of our field exploration about

A heavy growth of Hau trees was noted in the stream and along
the stream banks
The ground elevation at the site generally varies from about
285 to 290 feet
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
A new bridge structure is planned over Kalama Stream in con
junction with the planned realignment of Kamalu Road
The bridge structure will be about 42 feet wide and 100 feet
long
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The project site is situated in the Lihue basin or Lihue
depression area which generally extends from Kapaa to Nawili
wili on the north south latitudes respeetively and from
Kalepa Nonou ridge to the major mountain range on the east
west longitudes respectively
One geologic theory about the formation of the Lihue basin
area is that a collapse on the eastern flank of the Kauai
shield volcano occurred resulting in a subcircular caldera
7 to 10 miles wide that eventually became the basin area
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 664 10
Another theory is that the basin primarily resulted from
streams eroding and ultimately destroying the amphitheater
headed valleys which they once formed on the eastern flank
leaving the basin or depression areas which were once the
valley floors
In either case a relatively flat lower topographic area
was created upon which weathering and erosion developed
alluvial and colluvial conglomerates during a volcanic
quiescent period more than 1 5 million years in duration
Volcanic activity then resumed in this area and the new set
of eruptions were named the Koloa Volcanic Series
Presently it is the lavas from the Koloa Volcanic Series
interbedded with sediments which developed during the periods
between successive Koloa eruptions that exists in the upper
depths of the Lihue basin Surface soil layers of various
thicknesses throughout the basin area lastly covered the
Koloa Volcanic Series lavas

Soil Conditions
In general the borings indicated that the site is underlain
by stiff clayey silts to a depth of 10 to 13 feet Dense
basalt rock was encountered below the upper surface soils
and extended down to the depth of the borings
The core samples taken from the basalt rock indicated that
the rock formation is very dense and competent
Groundwater was noted in the borings at depths of 2 5 to 4 3
feet below the existing ground surface during the field
exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Bridge Foundations
The borings indicated that the site is underlain by a stiff
soil layer over very dense basaltic rock formation at about
10 to 13 foot depths
Spread footing foundations bearing on the dense rock formation
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 664 10
are recommended for the proposed bridge structure An allow
able bearing value up to 10 000 p s f is recommended for
footings bearing directly on the dense rock formation
The rock formation was encountered in the borings at about
elevation 276 to 278 5 feet on the west abutment and 277 to
280 on the east abutment
The recommended lateral earth pressures are as follows
Walls unrestrained at top 40 p c f equivalent
active case fluid pressure
Walls restrained at top 55 p c f equivalent
at rest case fluid pressure
Additional loads due to traffic or surcharge should
be included where applicable
A coefficient of friction of 0 6 between the concrete and
dense rock formation is recommended
Granular backfill material should be used and weepholes pro
vided through the abutment walls to reduce the build up of
hydrostatic pressure behind the walls
Excavation and Dewatering
Easy excavation is anticipated through the upper silty soil
layer above the rock formation Lateral bracing for the
excavation will probably be required due tothe high water
level in the area
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 664 10
The footing excavations will extend about 8 to 10 feet below
the water level and therefore dewatering will be required
The method of dewatering should be left up to the contractor
provided adequate excavation stability is maintained and that
the concrete can be placed properly in a dry condition
Design Review
The plans for bridge foundations should be forwarded to the
soils engineer for review and comments prior to construction
to see that the intent of our recommendations in included in
the project plans and specifications
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the footing excavations be observed
by the soil engineer during construction to see that adequate
bearing has been obtained and that the subsurface conditions
are consistent with the findings of our subsurface investi
gation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested
and approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the
site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon
the above inspections

APPENDIX A
FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by dril
utilizing truck mounted equipment The depths of
the borings were terminated at 20 to 60 feet below the existing
ground surface The approximate locations of the borings are
shown on the enclosed Boring Location Plan Plate 2
The soils were classified by visual and textural examinations
in the field by our engineer who continuously monitored the dril
ling operations The classifications were verified by visual
inspection and testing in the laboratory All soils were classi
fied in accordance with the Unified Soil Classification System
A graphic presentation of the soils encountered is presented on
the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 11
Soil samples were obtained by driving either a 2 4 inch I D
ring sampler or a standard penetration sampler with a 140 pound
hammer free falling a distance of 30 inches The blow counts
to drive the sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the Logs of
Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Rock samples were obtained by a double tube NWG size core
barrel attached with a diamond coring bit
