Soil Resistivity Testing
Based on the information provided it is desired to obtain in situ resistivity data at
selected locations in general accordance with the Standard Test Method for Field
Measurement of Soil Resistivity Using the Wenner Four Electrode Method ASTM G 57
The Wenner 4 Pin Method provides an average value of the subsurface resistivity over the
tested depth
Geophysical survey equipment i e ground resistance testing equipment was
mobilized to the project site on for the field resistivity survey Thirty two
resistivity tests were conducted at two selected locations designated as RS 01 and
RS 02 using the in Pin Method and an AEMC Model 4610 ground
resistance testing equipment manufactured by AEMC Instruments The approximate
locations of the field resistivity survey are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The method
utilizes four surface probes or pins electrodes to evaluate the average resistivity of the
underlying mass The ground resistance testing equipment AEMC Model 4610 provides
a voltage between the outer electrodes causing an electrical current to flow between
them The voltage drop between the inner electrodes is then measured by a sensitive
built in voltmeter As a result the soil resistance in ohms is obtained from the voltmeter
dial reading The soil resistivity in ohm cm which is directly related to both resistance
and spacing can then be computed
The average resistivity values of the subsurface mass extending from the existing
ground surface to the test depth was assessed by using this method along both traverse
directions at the survey locations The depth of penetration of a resistivity sounding is a
function of the electrode spacing The electrode spacing evaluated soil resistances and
computed resistivity values along the test alignments are presented on Plate 3 On the
basis of the field resistivity test results the subsurface soils at the test locations have in
field resistivity survey the temperature was in the mid 80 Fahrenheit The existing ground
surface was very dry It should be noted that the in situ resistivity may be influenced by
temperature weather conditions existing underground facilities and other factors

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
Our field exploration consisted of drilling and sampling five borings designated as
10 5 to 41 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate boring locations are
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were drilled using a truck mounted drill rig
equipped with solid stem augers and coring tools
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by a representative from Black Veatch Corporation who
observed the drilling operations on a near continuous basis
A soil sample was obtained from Boring No BV 01B in general accordance with
ASTM D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 2 inch
OD standard penetration sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches
Two bulk samples designated as Bag 1 and Bag 2 were obtained at selected
locations and depths as instructed by the field representative from Black Veatch
Corporation Bag 1 was obtained east of the turbine site along the proposed Papaka
access road The approximate location where Bag 2 was collected is shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2
Core samples approximately 2 4 inches in diameter of rock and or hard
materials encountered at the site were obtained using diamond core drilling techniques
in general accordance with ASTM D 2113 Diamond Core Drilling for Site Investigation
Core drilling is a rotary drilling method which uses a hollow bit to cut into the rock
formation The material left in the hollow core of the bit is mechanically recovered for
examination and classification

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Three Sieve Analysis tests ASTM C 117 C 136 including one Hydrometer test
ASTM D 422 were performed on selected soil samples to evaluate the gradation
characteristics of the soils and to aid in soil classification Graphic presentation of the test
results are provided on Plate B 1
Two Atterberg Limits tests ASTM D 4318 were attempted on selected soil
samples to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits Due to the non plastic nature of the
materials these tests could not be performed as shown on Plate B 1
Two laboratory California Bearing Ratio tests ASTM D 1883 were performed on
the bulk samples of selected materials to evaluate the pavement support characteristics
of the soils The test results are presented on Plates B 2 and B 3
Two Standard Proctor compaction tests ASTM D 698 were performed on the
bulk samples of selected materials to evaluate the dry density and moisture content
relationships The test results are presented on Plates B 4 and B 5
Nine unconfined compression tests ASTM D 2938 were performed on selected
rock cores to evaluate their unconfined compressive strength of the rock formation
encountered Results of the unconfined compression tests are presented on Plate B 6

moisture content and at 95 of standard Proctor density For the rock sample a
thermal probe was installed and TR tests were conducted only in the as received
and in the totally dry condition The tests included the measurement of moisture
content density and thermal dryout characterization thermal resistivity as a
function of moisture content The tests were conducted in accordance with IEEE
standard 442 using our Thermal Property Analyzer Model TPA2000 and laboratory
type thermal probes The thermal dryout curves are presented in Figure 1
