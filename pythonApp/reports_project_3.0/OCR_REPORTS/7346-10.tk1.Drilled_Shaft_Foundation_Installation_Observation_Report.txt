In accordance with the Work Authorization for Consulting Services entered into on 
December 28, 2017 with Hawaiian Electric Company, Inc. (HECO), we observed the 
drilled shaft foundation installation work for the HECO Kahe-CEIP No. 1 & No. 2 
Overhead Relocation project in the Kapolei area on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. This letter 
report summarizes our geotechnical observations during the drilled shaft foundation 
installation for the project. 
Geolabs provided geotechnical engineering consultation services for the HECO 
138 kV Overhead Utility Line Relocation project, and the findings and recommendations 
are presented in the above-referenced letter report. The purpose of our geotechnical 
observations was to monitor the geotechnical engineering aspects of the drilled shaft 
foundation installation for conformance with the plans and specifications and to provide 
subsequent recommendations, as appropriate. In addition, we provided “Special 
Inspection” services in general accordance with Item 1704 of the 2006 International 
Building Code by observing the drilled shaft foundation installation for general 
conformance with the project plans and specifications and subsequent recommendations.

Oahu, Hawaii.  The project location and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location

DRILLED SHAFT INSTALLATION 
Based on the anticipated subsurface conditions and foundation demand, a deep 
foundation system consisting of cast-in-place concrete drilled shaft embedded into the 
underlying coralline and alluvial deposits was utilized for foundation support of the power 
pole structures. 
The drilled shaft contractor installed the drilled shafts from February 6 through 
March 3, 2018 at the locations shown on the Drilled Shaft Location Plan, Plate 2. The 
as-built diameters and the lengths of the drilled shafts installed for the project are 
summarized on Plate 3 of this report. A representative from Geolabs observed the drilled

shaft excavation, placement of reinforcing cage, and concrete operations on a 
near-continuous (full-time) basis during the installation of the drilled shafts. 
In general, the drilled shafts were excavated using a combination of a Soilmec 
R-625 and IMT A125 excavators with an auger torque drill head attachment. Tooling used 
for the various drilled shaft excavations included 60-inch diameter drilling auger, 30 and 
60-inch diameter core barrels, 60-inch diameter clean-out auger, as well as 84, 96, and 
120-inch modified digging buckets. 
Drilling operations generally encountered coralline deposits consisting of medium 
dense to very dense silty and gravelly sands, sandy gravel and hard sandstone, 
interbedded with alluvial deposits consisting of very stiff to hard sandy silts and silty clays 
to depths of about 21 to 47 feet below the existing ground surface. We did not observe 
groundwater level within the depths of the drilled shaft excavations. 
After excavation of the drilled shafts to their designated tip elevations, the 
reinforcing cages were lowered into the drilled holes to the appropriate elevations with the 
use of a crane. After the reinforcing cage was installed in the drilled hole, concrete was 
placed through a 4-inch steel tremmie pipe starting from the bottom. During concrete 
placement, we observed that at least 5 feet of concrete head was maintained above the 
bottom of the tremmie pipe.  Concrete with slump ranging from 8 to 9.3 inches supplied by 
HC&D was used for the drilled shaft. Consolidated Engineering Laboratories monitored the 
concrete used for the drilled shaft, including the performance of slump tests and concrete 
sampling. 
Based on the observations of our field representative, it is our professional opinion 
that the drilled shafts supporting the power poles for the HECO Kahe-CEIP No. 1 & No. 2 
Overhead Relocation project were installed in general accordance with the intent of the 
project plans and specifications and the geotechnical recommendations presented in our 
geotechnical engineering report for the project and subsequent recommendations.  
LIMITATIONS
