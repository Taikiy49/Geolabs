PROJECT DESCRIPTION 
The project is situated at Waialua District Park in Waialua on the Island of Oahu, 
Hawaii. The site is bounded by Goodale Avenue to the north and west, Waialua Beach 
Road to the east, and Waialua Elementary School to the south. In general, the project 
involves the reconstruction of the existing baseball field lighting system. We understand 
that a total of eight new light poles are planned for the project. 
The new light poles are supported on deep foundations consisting of 
cast-in-place concrete drilled shafts. Drilled shafts with diameters of 60 inches and 
lengths ranging from about 25.2 to 26.0 feet were used to support the new light poles. 
The scope of our work discussed in this letter report includes observation of the 
drilled shaft foundation construction for the Baseball Field Lighting System Replacement 
project only. Paul’s Electrical Contracting LCC was the general contractor for the project, 
and CMZ of Hawaii\Drilco, Inc. installed the drilled shaft foundation support system. 
DRILLED SHAFT INSTALLATION 
Based on the anticipated subsurface conditions and foundation demand, a deep 
foundation system consisting of cast-in-place concrete drilled shaft embedded into the 
underlying coralline and alluvial deposits was utilized for foundation support of the light 
pole structures. 
The drilled shaft contractor installed eight drilled shafts from October 28 through 
November 19, 2019 at the locations shown on the Site Plan, Plate 2. The diameter and 
as-built lengths of the drilled shafts installed for the project are summarized on Plate 3 of 
this report. Individual drilled shaft logs for the light pole foundations are provided in 
Appendix A of this report. 
Representatives from Geolabs observed the drilled shaft excavation, placement of 
reinforcing cage, and concrete operations on a near-continuous (full-time) basis during the 
installation of the drilled shafts. In general, the drilled shafts were excavated using a Drilco 
Casagrande B180HD drill rig. Tooling used for the 60-inch diameter shafts included 
60-inch diameter auger, digging bucket, and clean-out bucket. A permanent steel casing 
with an inside diameter of either 62, 63, or 64 inches was installed to depths of about 25.2 
to 26.0 feet below the ground surface. 
After excavation of the drilled shafts to their designated tip elevations, the 
reinforcing cages were lowered into the drilled holes to the appropriate elevations with the 
use of a crane. After the reinforcing cage was installed in the drilled hole, concrete was 
placed through a 4-inch steel tremie pipe starting from the bottom. During concrete 
placement, we observed that at least 5 feet of concrete head was maintained above the 
bottom of the tremie pipe.

Due to the presence of groundwater within the drilled shaft holes, concrete was 
placed in the drilled holes using the tremie method. Concrete with slump ranging from 7 to 
8.25 inches was used for the drilled shafts. Construction Engineering Labs monitored the 
concrete used for the drilled shafts, including the performance of slump tests and 
compressive strength tests. 
Based on the observations of our field representatives, it is our professional opinion 
that the drilled shafts supporting the new light poles for the Baseball Field Lighting System 
Replacement project were installed in general accordance with the intent of the project 
plans and specifications and the geotechnical recommendations presented in our 
geotechnical engineering report (dated September 4, 2018) for the project and subsequent 
recommendations. In addition, it is our professional opinion that the drilled shafts have 
been installed to sufficient depths to provide the allowable design load capacities with a 
safety factor of 2.0.
