INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration for the
proposed renovation and expansion of the existing cafeteria building at the Waimea
Elementary Intermediate School in Waimea on the island of Hawaii The general
location of the site is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The exploration was
performed on March 5 and 6 1991 in accordance with our proposal dated November 2
1990

This report summarizes the findings of the exploration and our geotechnical
engineering recommendations derived from our analyses based on the data obtained
during the exploration and laboratory testing of selected samples These
recommendations are intended for design input and are not intended to form the basis
of preparation of construction cost estimates for bidding purposes

expansion will include a new single story building extension approximately sixty two 62

cafeteria building Also included is a small twenty 20 foot by twelve 12 foot addition
at the northeastern corner of the existing building It is anticipated that concrete slab on
grade with CMU wall construction will be utilized for the proposed cafeteria expansion

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
The project site is underlain by basaltic lava flows of the Hamakua Volcanic Series
of Mauna Kea which were deposited during the Pleistocene epoch Stearns and
Macdonald 1946
The Hamakua series is typically composed of basaltic lava flows which are mantled
by a surface layer of palagonitized ash locally referred to as Pahala Ash The material
at the project site differs from Pahala Ash found in other portions of the island of Hawaii
in that the in situ moisture content is generally lower than that of other Pahala Ash
materials and the soil at the cafeteria site exists in a friable state
Based on previous experience in the Waimea area the lava flows underlying the
sites are of the A a form which is characterized by a rough spiny or rubbly surface of
fragmental clinker underlain by a dense core of massive basalt which frequently has a
thinner less continuous layer of clinker below the massive core The clinker surface of
the lavas at the site have been mantled by the Pahala Ash as discussed above

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration encountered variable soil conditions ranging from silty gravel
clinker fill to weathered cinder sand to clayey silt volcanic ash overlying hard basalt
formation
Spread or continuous footings and slab on grade foundations may be used to
support the proposed cafeteria expansion
Details of our recommendations are discussed in the following sections
Foundations
Based on the soil and rock conditions encountered it is our opinion that spread
and continuous footing foundations may be used to support the proposed cafeteria
expansion An allowable bearing capacity of up to 3 000 p s f may be used for footings
bearing on the in situ silty gravel and sand clinker fill A minimum footing embedment
of 24 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade is recommended A higher
allowable bearing capacity of up to 8 000 p s f could be used for footings bearing on in
situ hard basalt however it would be difficult to definitively estimate the specific depths
at which the rock formation may be encountered
The bearing capacity value recommend is for dead plus live loads and may be
increased by one third when temporary wind and seismic loads are considered
If soft loose soil pockets are encountered at the bottom of the footing excavations
they should be over excavated to stiff in situ soil and the resulting depression backfilled
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2622 00 Page 8
with select granular borrow material compacted to a minimum of 95 of its maximum dry
density as determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
If a footing is located next to a utility line it should extend to the bottom of the
utility trench to reduce potential footing settlement due to settlement of the trench backfill
Provided the above recommendations are followed estimated total footing
settlement under a column load of up to 75 kips is anticipated to be less than one half
0 5 inch
Slab On Grade
It is our understanding that minimal site grading involving cut or fill is anticipated
to be required for the proposed cafeteria expansion For slab on grade foundations
bearing on the in situ soils encountered by our borings we recommend a minimum 6 inch
thick layer of 3B Fine gravel or ASTM C 33 No 67 cushion or equivalent be placed
below the concrete slab in order to provide uniform support
The thickened edge slab footings can bear on the in situ soil or compacted
granular non expansive material with a minimum footing embedment of eighteen 18
inches below the lowest adjacent finish grade
Site Grading
Minimal site grading involving cut or fill is anticipated to be required for the
proposed project
Prior to commencement of earthwork all areas within the construction limits should
be cleared and grubbed of all rubbish and deleterious materials The resulting grubbed
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2622 00 Page 9
and spoil material should be disposed of properly off site Any soft weak or yielding
areas encountered during clearing and grubbing should be over excavated to firm ground
and the resulting depressions backfilled with well compacted engineered fill or granular
select material
The fill and backfill should be placed in maximum 10 inch thick loose level lifts and
compacted to a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density as determined in
accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
All imported materials that may be required should be tested and approved by the
geotechnical engineer prior to being transported to the project site
The finished grade should be shaped to shed water away from foundations to
avoid ponding conditions
Existing Cesspool
It is Our understanding that an existing 8 foot diameter cesspool is located within
the eastern corner of the proposed cafeteria expansion site A septic tank and leaching
field system is proposed to replace the existing cesspool
It is Our Opinion that the non load bearing masonry construction comprising the
existing cesspool should be emptied and properly backfilled as part of the project site
grading work to provide uniform support for the proposed cafeteria expansion Prior to
backfilling the liquids and sludge inside the existing cesspool should thoroughly and
completely removed The concrete cover and the upper four 4 courses of the masonry
block wall of the existing cesspool should be demolished and removed The resulting
cavity should then be backilled with self compacting granular fill such as S4C or 3B Fine
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2622 00 Page 10
gravel or equivalent to within three 3 feet of the elevation of the bottom of the footings
or the slab on grade thickened edges The remaining backfill can consist of select
granular material compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density as
determined in accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Sewage Disposal System
It is our understanding that a septic tank and leaching field system is planned for
disposal of sanitary wastes from the Administration Building These wastes are currently
handled by the existing cesspool which is to be removed
Based on our exploration and percolation test results it is our opinion that a
shallow leaching field may be feasible provided the absorption area penetrates the
underlying permeable silty gravel layer encountered at the site proposed for the leaching
field The percolation test results for the leaching field design are presented in
Appendix B of this report It appears that the subsoil at test locaton P 2 is much more
permeable than at location P 1 Due to the variability of the subsoil conditions the
absorption capacity of the disposal system should be verified by additional field
percolation tests during construction
Design Review
Drawings and specifications for the proposed construction should be forwarded
to the geotechnical engineer for review evaluation and written comments prior to
advertisement for bids This review is needed to determine adherence to the earthwork
and foundation recommendations presented in this report If this review is not made the
geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for misinterpretation of the
recommendations

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during earthwork construction and footing excavation
to monitor the contractor s operations This is to observe compliance with the design
concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow design changes in the event
that subsurface conditions differ form those anticipated prior to start of construction The
recommendations given in this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and approved by
the geotechnical engineer prior to its delivery to the site
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained form field borings and field percolation tests Variations of conditions
between the field borings and tests may occur and the nature and extent of these
variations may not become evident until construction is underway If variations then
appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations given in this
report
If actual exposed soil rock conditions encountered during construction are different
from those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made

having been determined by taping from features shown on the Site Plan transmitted by
Richard Matsunaga Associates on Elevations of the borings and
percolation tests except for Boring B 4 were approximated by interpolation from contours
shown in the Site Grading Plan of the existing Administration Building dated November
