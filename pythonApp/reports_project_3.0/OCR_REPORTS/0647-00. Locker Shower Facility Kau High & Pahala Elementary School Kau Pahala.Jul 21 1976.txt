INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our soil investigation and
recommendations for design and construction of the proposed Kau
High Pahala Elementary School Locker Shower Facility located
on the south eastern side of the Island of Hawaii in the Kau
District in the town of Pahala Present plans call for the con
struction of a single story wood framed structure which will ac
commodate locker and shower facilities and two classrooms
SITE DESCRIPTION
Presently the proposed building site is located within the fenced
pasture used by the Kau High agriculture class It is covered by
one foot high grass Six inch sized diameter boulders scattered
over the site

W
The ground generally slopes down toward the southeast direction
at about 6 percent gradient
The southern most corner of the building is located within the
State of Hawaii easement adjacent to the County s Kau Baseball
Park It is located near the top of a 10 foot high cut slope
which drops down toward the southeast direction
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTIONS
The proposed locker shower facility will be a one story slab on
grade structure with C M U Walls supporting a corrugated galva
nized iron and wood composited roof
Building dimensions are 60 x 190 and proposed finished floor
elevation will be at 947 feet
Site grading will involve 3 feet of cuts along the slab s north
side Structural fill up to 4 feet high will be required along
the lower south portion
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The project site is located on the east flank of Mauna Loa
The major part of Mauna Loa is covered with rocks of the Kau vol
canic series The Kau volcanic series is the assemblage of recent
and latest Pleistocene Volcanic rock laid down by Mauna Loa volcano
The lavas of the present Mauna Loa appear to be mostly younger than
the Pahala Ash although in the region northeast of Mountain View
layers of ash are interleaved with lava flows and the earliest
Page 3
W
Mauna Loa lavas in that area are probably coeval with the main
period of formation of the Pahala ash
In this area the general geologic formation belongs to Kahuku
volcanic series and is capped by Pahala ash The present surface
soil consists of shallow well drained silt loam that formed in
volcanic ash
Subsurface Exploration
The subsurface exploration for the proposed building site con
sisted of drilling 5 test borings with a truck mounted drill rig
Boring locations are presented on the Site Plan Plate 1 A
detailed description of our field exploration procedures and ac
companying laboratory testing is given in the Appendix
Soil Conditions
The subsurface exploration showed that the area is blanked by a
surface layer of Pahala ash deposit which varies from 1 to 7
feet thick Below the brownish silty ash material is the weathered
basalt rock formation The moisture contents of the upper soil
layer are relatively low and the soil consistency is firm consi
dering the surface soil is of volcanicash origin
Groundwater was not noted in the bore holes at the time of field
exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on our investigation the proposed structures may be supported
on spread and continuous footings founded on the underlying weathered
Page 4
W
rock formation
Slab on grade can be supported on compacted on site soil upon
cut and fill areas
Foundation
Spread and line footing foundations may be used for the proposed
building
To obtain uniform bearing condition and to minimize differential
settlement footings should all extend below the surfacial ash silty
layers into the weathered rock For the proposed lightly loaded
structure allowable bearing pressure of about 3 000 pounds per
Square foot would be more than adequate The bearing value may
be increased by one third when considering wind and seismic loads
For footing on weathered rock settlements will be minimal
The footing bottom elevations will vary in order to reach the re
quired formation Therefore the contract documents should provide
clauses for excavation quantity adjustments in the field
Generally the bottom of exterior footings should be a minimum of
18 inches below the finished adjacent grade
Since the bottom of footing elevations will vary in this building
site we recommend that the bottom of footing excavations be in
spected by soil engineer prior to concreting

Slab On Grade
Slightly expansive surface soils were encountered during the
subsurface investigation The laboratory expansion tests on
the near surface soil samples indicated less than 1 swell with
a surcharge load of 60 p s f
A 4 inch layer of compacted Aa base course is recommended
under the proposed slab and walkway construction
Slab with floor coverings should be protected by a plastic vapor
barrier above the base course The plastic sheet should be co
vered by a l inch layer of sand to help concrete slab curing
and to protect the plastic membrane from puncturing
Earthwork
Site grading will involve cuts and fills within the proposed
building area
New fill varying from 1 to 4 feet in thickness will be required
to bring the grade up to finished floor elevation 947 at the
southeast portion of the site
All areas to receive fill should be undercut to remove the surface
vegetation and topsoil Prior to the placement of new fill the
exposed surface should be wetted to near optimum moisture content
and compacted to a minimum of 90 maximum dry density Additional
fill should then be placed in 9 inch horizontal lifts and compacted
to the above specified density

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the foundation excavations be inspected
prior to the placement of the concrete or reinforcing to assure
that adequate bearing has been obtained and that the subsurface
conditions are consistent with the findings of our subsurface
investigations
Structural fill layers should be tested in accordance with the
specified density

Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling
5 borings utilizing truck mounted equipment The depths of all
the borings terminated at 8 3 to 22 0 feet below the existing
ground surface The locations of the borings are shown on the
enclosed Site Plan Plate 1
The soils were classified by visual and textural examination in
the field by our engineer who continuously supervised the drilling
Operations The classifications were verified by visual inspection
and testing in the laboratory All soils were classified in accor
dance with the Unified Soil Classification System A graphic pre
sentation of the soils encountered is presented on the Boring Logs
Plates A 1 through A 5
Soil samples were obtained by driving either a 2 4 inch I D ring
sampler or a standard penetration sampler with a 140 pound hammer
free falling a distance of 30 inches The blow counts to drive
the sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the logs of borings
at the appropriate sample depth

Laboratory Testing
Moisture content determinations were made on the samples as an
aid in the soil classification and in evaluating soil properties
The results of these tests are presented on the Log of Borings
at the appropriate sample location
Two swell tests of one inch thick ring sample were conducted on
near surface samples for measuring the swell potential The
Swell test results are summarized on Plate B l
Modified proctor compaction test was performed on a surface sample
of the on site soil to evaluate the suitability of the soil as fill
material The results are given on Plate B 2
Direct shear tests were performed on selected samples to evaluate
the strength characteristics of the encountered soils The test
results are shown on Plate B 3
