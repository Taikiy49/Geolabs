Geotechnical Engineering Investigation
FY86 Special Project C1 87
Replace Carpenter Shop
Pacific Missile Range Facility
Barking Sands Kauai Hawaii
This report presents the results of our geotechnical investigation
performed for the proposed Carpenter Shop at the Pacific Missile Range
Facility at Barking Sands on the island of Kauai Hawaii The general
location of the project site and vicinity are shown on the Project
Location Map Plate l
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of the investigation was to evaluate the subsurface
soil conditions at the proposed site to provide soil data for the design
W O Page 2
of the foundation for the proposed building and to determine the bearing
and settlement characteristics of the soil underlying the proposed
facility
The scope of our investigation which was performed in general
accordance with our proposal of included drilling and
sampling three 3 borings laboratory soil testing engineering analysis
utilizing the field and laboratory data and report preparation
Detailed description of our field exploration laboratory testing
and boring logs are presented in the appendices The approximate
location of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
PROJECT CONSIDERATION
The proposed carpenter shop consists of a 50 by 60 foot pre
engineered building Total load on each interior column is approximately
12 kips Minimal site grading is anticipated for the construction of the
building

GENERAL SITE CONDITIONS
The proposed site is located approximately 120 feet to the south of
Building 215 at the Pacific Missile Range Facility at Barking Sands on
the island of Kauai Hawaii This location is slightly different from
the preliminary location which was used as a guide for locating the
borings during our drilling operations
The site is an open area currently used as a temporary storage yard
for containers and construction materials Due to limited access and
existing utilities only one of our borings was drilled inside the lot
while the other two borings were located outside the lot to avoid
drilling into any underground utility
Ground level at the site is generally flat Ground cover consists
mainly of beach sand and coral gravel
Subsoil Condition
Three borings were drilled to depths of 15 0 to 15 4 feet below the
ground surface All borings encountered brown loose to medium dense and
fine to medium beach sand grading gradually to brown and medium dense to
very dense beach sand with medium grain size to depths of about 11 0 to
12 5 feet The subsoil then changed to hard white tan coral formation to
the drilled depths of the borings

Groundwater was encountered at a depth of 10 0 to 10 5 feet below
ground surface at the time of our field exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATION
Foundation
Based on the anticipated soil conditions and column load we
believe that shallow spread or continuous wall footing bearing on the
upper in situ recompacted fine to medium beach sand may be used
For footings bearing on sand we recommend they should be placed a
minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade Uplift
wind blast or other lateral loading condition may further increase the
embedment of the footing foundation
The bottom of the footing excavation should be recompacted to a
minimum of 95 of its maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557
An allowable bearing capacity of 2 000 p s f may be used for the
foundation design The bearing capacity value is for total dead plus
live load only and may be increased by one third for short duration wind
loading Settlements under the design loading are anticipated to be on
the order of less than one half of an inch total and one fourth of an
inch differential
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O Page 5
Resistance to lateral loads for the proposed building can be
obtained by passive pressure and frictional resistance A frictional
coefficient of 0 4 may be used between the bottom of the footing and the
medium dense soil In addition an equivalent fluid pressure of 300
p s f per foot of depth may be used to determine the passive soil
resistance
Site Grading
The finished grade level was not available at this time however
due to the relatively flat ground minimal site grading is anticipated
for the site development
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed Any unsuitable
materials such as debris and rubbish should be removed and disposed of
properly off site in order to prevent contamination of the excavated
material
The areas which are to receive pavement should be scarified to a
depth of at least 6 inches below the subgrade elevation moisture
conditioned to optimum moisture content and recompacted to a minimum of
95 of ASTM 1557 to obtain a firm subgrade
GEOLABS HAWAIi
W O Page 6
Loose spots encountered at the footing or subgrade level should be
removed and replaced with compacted on site sandy soil
If fill is required the area to receive fill after clearing and
grubbing should be scarified at least 6 inches below the cleared grade
moisture conditioned and recompacted to 95 of its maximum density as
established by ASTM D 1557 78 test method Any loose spots encountered
should be removed and replaced with select on site soil or with suitable
imported material compacted to the above mentioned standard
The on site sandy soil may be used for fill material If required
new imported fill material should consist of non expansive select
granular materials less than 3 inches in maximum size with less than 20
passing the 200 sieve
Structural fill should be placed in 8 inch thick compacted
horizontal lifts and compacted to 95 ASTM D 1557 78 maximum density
Pavement

preliminary design purpose

Slab On Grade
It is anticipated that concrete slab on grade will be used for the
proposed building The on grade floor slab should be placed over a
4 inch cushion layer of gravel size crushed rock material such as No 3
Fine rock to provide uniform support A moisture barrier membrane should
be placed under the slabs
The slab thickened edges exposed to any unpaved area should be
embedded at least 18 inches below the lowest adjacent grade
Design Review
Plans and specifications for site grading and foundations should be
forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written comments prior to
construction to see that the intent of our recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the site grading subgrade preparation and
footing excavations be observed by the soils engineer during construction
to see that adequate compaction has been obtained and that the subsurface
conditions are consistent with the findings of our investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site

engineering principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration

The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling
three 3 borings to depths of 15 0 to 15 4 feet below the existing
ground surface A truck mounted drill rig was used to drill the borings
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by visual and
textural examination in the field by our engineer who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory All soils
were Classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A summary of the materials encountered are
presented in the Log of Borings Plates A 1 thru A 3
Soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving a 2 inch
a 140 pound hammer free falling 30 inches The blow counts to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the Log of Borings at the
appropriate sample depths
