SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Based on our review of the subsurface soil rock data provided in the reports
prepared by Dames Moore in the 1970 s it appears that the project site is generally
underlain by highly expansive adobe clays extending to depths ranging from about 10 to
20 feet below the existing ground surface Below the surficial clay soils hard basalt rock
formation was encountered and extended to the maximum depth explored of
approximately 25 feet below the existing ground surface Groundwater was not
encountered in the drilled borings at the time of the field exploration It should be noted
that groundwater levels are subject to change due to seasonal precipitation and other
factors
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Previous field explorations conducted at the subject site indicated that the
proposed new sanctuary building location may be underlain by near surface adobe
clays overlying basalt rock formation at greater depths Based on the subsurface
conditions anticipated at the project site it is our opinion that shallow foundations
consisting of spread and or continuous footings may be used to support the proposed
new structures An allowable bearing pressure of up to 2 000 pounds per square foot
p s f may be used for the design of footings bearing on the medium stiff to stiff clayey
soils encountered at shallow depths
Laboratory tests indicate that the near surface clayey soils exhibit high shrink swell
potential when subjected to moisture fluctuations In order to reduce the effects of the
expansive clay soils due to moisture fluctuations we recommend that the footing bottoms
be embedded a minimum of 3 feet below the lowest adjacent finish grade To reduce the
potential for distress to the future floor slab resulting from the swelling of the near surface
expansive soils we recommend that an air space of at least 6 inches be provided below
the floor slab Detailed discussion of these items and our geotechnical engineering
design recommendations are presented in the following sections of this report

Laboratory tests performed on the on site clayey soils indicated that the material

order to reduce the effects of the expansive clay soils resulting from moisture fluctuations
we recommend that the footing bottoms be embedded a minimum of 3 feet below the
lowest adjacent finish grade The bottom of the footing excavations should be lightly
compacted to provide a relatively firm and smooth bearing surface prior to placement of
reinforcing steel and concrete Excessive disturbance or over compaction of the adobe
clay soils at the footing subgrade level should be avoided to reduce the potential for
subsequent heaving of the expansive clay soils If soft and or loose materials are
encountered at the bottom of footing excavations they should be over excavated to
expose the underlying firm materials The over excavation should be backfilled with
select granular material compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction or the
footing bottom may be extended down to the underlying competent material
If foundations are located next to utility trenches or easements they should be
embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane extending
upward from the bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility
lines This requirement is necessary to avoid surcharging adjacent below grade
structures with additional structural loads and to reduce the potential for foundation
settlement If foundations are designed in accordance with our recommendations we
estimate total settlements to be less than 1 inch with differential settlements on the order
of inch
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive earth pressure
acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system and by frictional resistance
developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil Resistance due to
passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid pressure of
300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth p c f This assumes that the soil around
footings is well compacted A coefficient of friction of 0 3 may be used for footings
bearing on the in situ clay soils Unless covered by pavements or slabs the passive
resistance in the upper 12 inches of soil should be neglected

Floor Slabs and Walkways
Based on the information provided to us we understand that 3 inch cast in place
topping on 8 inch hollow core planks spanning across the structural columns will be
utilized for the floor slab construction Previous field exploration indicated that the project
site is generally underlain by a deposit of adobe clay soils which are considered to be
highly expansive when subjected to moisture fluctuations To reduce the potential for
distress to the future floor slab resulting from the swelling of the near surface expansive
soils we recommend that an air space of at least 6 inches be provided below the floor
slab An impervious moisture vapor barrier such as a plastic membrane is
recommended on top of the clayey subgrade to reduce the potential for moisture
fluctuations
Concrete slab on grade such as the patio may be required for the proposed
project To reduce the potential for structural distress resulting from the swelling of the
on site expansive soils we recommend that non expansive select capping fill material be
provided for a minimum depth of 24 inches below the concrete slab The 24 inch capping
layer recommended may be reduced to 12 inches if some amount of slab cracking can be
tolerated Contraction and expansion joints should be provided to permit relative
movement of the slab concrete The subgrade soils beneath the concrete slab on grade
should be prepared in accordance with the recommendations presented in the Site
Preparation section of this report
Site Preparation
Based on the existing topography and the anticipated finish floor elevation
minimal site grading work will be required for the proposed construction At the on set of
earthwork the areas within the contract grading limits should be thoroughly cleared and
grubbed All vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable materials should be
removed and disposed of properly off site Soft and yielding areas encountered during
clearing and grubbing below any areas designated to receive fill should be
over excavated to expose firm natural material and the resulting excavation should be
backfilled with well compacted engineered fill The excavated soft and or organic soils
should be properly disposed of off site or used in landscaping areas if appropriate
Slabs and foundations of the existing structures that are to be demolished should
be completely removed Any over excavation resulting from the demolition should be
backfilled with compacted granular fill material Existing utilities to be abandoned should
be removed and the resulting excavation should be properly backfilled with select
granular fill material placed in 6 to 8 inch level loose lifts and compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative compaction Utilities that are to be abandoned in place under the
proposed structure should be backfilled by pumping lean concrete under low pressure

After demolition clearing and grubbing all areas beneath the future building slabs
should be over excavated where necessary to provide the minimum 6 inch high air
space below the floor slab Areas beneath concrete slab on grade should be over
excavated where necessary to provide the 12 or 24 inch non expansive select granular
capping layer The clayey subgrade at the over excavated level should be moisture
conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture and lightly compacted to
about 85 to 90 percent relative compaction Scarification of the slab subgrade and
recompaction to over 90 percent relative compaction should be avoided to reduce the
potential for future slab distress resulting from the swelling shrinkage of the expansive
adobe soils Where shrinkage cracks are noted after preparation of the subgrade we
recommend that the soil be thoroughly moistened to close all cracks Saturation and
subsequent yielding of the exposed subgrade due to inclement weather and poor
drainage may require over excavation of the soft areas and replacement with well
compacted engineered fill at no additional cost to the owner Contract documents should
include additive and deductive unit prices for over excavation and engineered fill
placement to account for variations in the over excavation quantities
Select granular fill materials needed for the non expansive capping layer should
consist of non expansive imported granular material such as crushed coral mudrock
basalt or cinder sand The material should be well graded from coarse to fine with no
particles larger than 3 inches in largest dimension and should contain between 10 and
30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve The material should have a laboratory
CBR value of 20 or more and should have a maximum swell of less than 1 percent
Imported fill material should be tested by Geolabs Hawaii for conformance with these
recommendations prior to delivery to the project site for its intended use
Fill materials should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture
placed in level lifts not exceeding 10 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a
minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place
dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same
soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 91 test procedures Optimum moisture
is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry
density

Utility Trench
New utility connections will be required for the proposed new facility A granular
bedding consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation is
recommended under the pipes Free draining granular materials such as No 3B Fine
gravel should also be used for the trench backfill up to about 12 inches above the pipes
to provide adequate support around the pipes and to reduce the compaction effort of the
backfill thus reducing the possibility of damaging the pipes It is critical to use this free
draining material to reduce the potential of formation of voids below the haunches of
pipes and to provide adequate support for the sides of the pipes Improper backfill
material and placement could result in settlement of the backfill and damage to the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipes to
the top of the subgrade or finished grade should consist of select fill material The backfill
should be placed in maximum 8 inch level loose lifts and mechanically compacted to not
less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for future ground
subsidence Where trenches are below pavement areas the upper 2 feet of the trench
backfill below the pavement subgrade should be compacted to a minimum of 95 percent
relative compaction
Drainage
The finished grades outside the new building structure should be sloped to shed
water away from foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also advised
that gutter systems be installed around the building and that the discharge be diverted
away from the foundation areas Excessive landscape watering near the foundations and
slabs should also be avoided Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have
concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil
saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations
should be properly backfilled against the walls or slab footings immediately after setting of
the concrete to reduce water infiltration Drainage swales should be provided as soon as
possible and should be maintained to drain all surface run off away from the slab and
footing foundations
