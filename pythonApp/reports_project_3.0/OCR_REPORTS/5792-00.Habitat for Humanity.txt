2 1 Regional Geology
The Island of Oahu is composed largely of the weathered remnants from the
Waianae and Koolau shield volcanoes The older Waianae Volcano forms the bulk of
the western third of the island while the younger Koolau Volcano forms the majority of
the eastern two thirds of the island It is believed that Waianae volcano became extinct
while the Koolau volcano was still active and its eastern flank is partially buried below
Koolau lavas in Central Oahu
The project site is located on the western side of the Waianae Mountain Range
During the Pleistocene Epoch sea levels fluctuated in response to the cycles of
continental glaciation As the glaciers grew and advanced less water was available to
fill the oceanic basins such that sea level fell below the present stands of the sea When
the glaciers melted and receded an excess of water became available such that the
sea levels rose above the present sea level
The higher sea level stands caused the formation of deltas and fans of
accumulated terrigenous sediments in the heads of old bays accumulated reef deposits
at corresponding higher elevations and deposited lagoonal marine sediments in the
quiet waters protected by fringing reefs The lower sea stands caused streams to carve
valleys in the sediments and reef deposits Subaerial exposure of the sediments and
calcareous materials caused consolidation of the soft deltaic materials and lagoonal
deposits and induration of the calcareous reef materials Land development and
reclamation projects within the last century have brought the project site to its present
form

adversely affect the performance of the future driveway slabs and roadway pavements
The ground cracks could serve as conduits for water to percolate into the bearing soils
and cause loss of strength of the bearing soils resulting in potential settlement of the
driveway slabs and roadway pavements Therefore remedial work to correct or mitigate
the observed ground cracks should be implemented prior to fill placement and
construction of the proposed development

equipped with continuous flight auger

examination in the field by our geologist who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified
Soil Classification System as shown on the Log Legend Plate A Graphic
representations of the materials encountered are presented on the Logs of Borings
Plates A 1 through A 5

accordance with ASTM D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 3 inch
OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches In addition
some samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 2 inch
OD standard penetration sampler using the same hammer and drop The blow counts
needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an 18 inch drive are
shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate
sample depths
Pocket penetrometer tests were performed on selected cohesive soil samples in

compressive strength of the sample Results of the pocket penetrometer tests are
summarized on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
