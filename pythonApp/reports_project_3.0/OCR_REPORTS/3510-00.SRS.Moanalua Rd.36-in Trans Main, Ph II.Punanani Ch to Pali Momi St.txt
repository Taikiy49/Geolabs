In accordance with your request a soil resistivity survey was conducted for the
above referenced project in the Aiea area in the District of Ewa on the island of Oahu

Plate 1 Our work was performed in general accordance with our fee proposal dated
May 30 1995 This report presents the results of our soil resistivity survey and
summarizes our work on the project
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located along Moanalua Road in the Pearl City Pearlridge area
of the District of Ewa on the island of Oahu Hawaii as shown on the Project Location
Map Plate 1 It is proposed to install a 36 inch diameter water main along Moanalua
Road from Punanani Channel to Pali Momi Street Based on the information provided
we understand that approximately 6 000 lineal feet of water lines will be installed for the
project In general the average invert of the proposed water line is anticipated to be
at about 7 to 8 feet below the existing ground surface Soil resistivity surveys utilizing
the in Pin Method were performed to evaluate the corrosion rating of the
near surface soils within the depths of the proposed water line

FIELD SURVEY
Geophysical survey equipment i e resistance meter was mobilized to the site
on and 15th for the field survey Our field work was performed in
sunny conditions with generally dry surface conditions
Sixty eight soil resistivity tests were conducted at about 300 foot intervals at
22 locations includes Test No 6A at the project site using the in Pin
Method and a resistance meter Strata Scout R 40C manufactured by SoilTest Inc
The resistance meter is capable of transmitting 120 volts and 24 watts of output into
the ground The method utilizes four surface probes or pins electrodes to evaluate

the average resistivity an indication of corrosivity of the underlying soil mass The
average resistivities of the soil mass extending from the existing ground surface to the
planned invert elevations of the water mains were assessed by using this method at the
approximate test locations shown on the Site Plans Plates 2 through 6 Results of
resistivity survey are presented on Plates 7 1 through 7 4
The soil resistivity tests were performed at the existing ground surface on the
exposed roadway sidewalk and shoulder areas along the mauka side of Moanalua
Road The average soil resistance was evaluated at each test location with the
electrodes aligned at equal spacings of generally 6 to 8 feet at the existing ground
surface along the shoulder of the roadway In addition two tests with 3 and 4 foot
spacings at one location were performed to confirm the readings with 6 to 8 feet
spacings It should be noted that some underground utility lines were observed within
the sidewalk and shoulder areas near some test locations
The resistance meter R 40C impresses a voltage between the outer electrodes
causing an electrical current to flow between them The voltage drop between the inner
electrodes is then measured by a sensitive built in voltmeter As a result the soil
resistance in Ohms can be found directly on the dial The soil resistivity in ohm cm
which is directly related to both resistance and spacing can then be computed The
evaluated soil resistances the computed resistivities and the corresponding corrosion
ratings are presented on Plates 7 1 through 7 4 of this report However it should be
noted that the soil resistivity may be influenced by the existing utility lines near the test
locations temperature weather conditions and other factors
Resistivity is generally recognized as one of the most significant soil
characteristics with regard to the corrosivity of the soil to buried metallic objects In
general the lower the resistivity the greater the potential for corrosion of the buried
metallic structure Conversely the higher the resistivity the less likely the soil will
contribute to corrosion to metallic objects
LIMITATIONS
The evaluations presented in this report are based upon information obtained
from field data points by means of geophysical surveys Variations of soil conditions
between the field data points may occur and the nature and extent of such variations
may not become evident until a later point in time If variations then appear evident
it will be necessary to reevaluate the ratings provided in this report
