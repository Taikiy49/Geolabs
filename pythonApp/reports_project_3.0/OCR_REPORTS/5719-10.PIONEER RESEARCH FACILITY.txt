PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is within the proposed Pioneer Research Facility in the Kunia area
on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project location and general vicinity are shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1 Based on the information provided we understand that
on site sewage treatment and disposal by septic tank and leach field are planned within
the proposed research facility site A geotechnical engineering exploration was performed
by our office for the research facility project and the findings and recommendations are
presented in our report entitled Geotechnical Engineering Exploration Pioneer Research
Facility dated Based on our previous field
exploration results the project site is generally underlain by low permeability soils
consisting of silty clay soils at the shallow subsurface
To evaluate the subsoil profile and permeability of the subsurface soils for
wastewater disposal we performed a field percolation test in a boring in general
accordance with the State of Hawaii Department of Health s Administrative Rules
Chapter 2 Wastewater Systems and Chapter 10 of the Ten States Standards

FIELD PERCOLATION TESTING
Our field exploration program at the proposed leach field site consisted of drilling
and sampling one boring designated as Boring No P 1 to a depth of about 5 feet below
the existing ground surface The approximate boring location is shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2
In general our field exploration encountered relatively impermeable materials
consisting of very stiff silty clay soils throughout the depth explored We did not encounter
groundwater in the boring at the time of our field exploration It should be noted that
groundwater level at the project site may fluctuate depending on seasonal precipitation

the boring are presented on the Log Legend Plate 3 and the Log of Boring Plate 4
We performed a field percolation test in the boring in general accordance with the
Wastewater Systems and Chapter 10 of the Ten States Standards To evaluate the
subsoil profile and permeability of the subsurface soils for wastewater disposal the
percolation test was performed by drilling a 4 0 inch diameter borehole extending to a
depth of about 5 feet below the existing ground surface The percolation test was
performed within the near surface silty clay soils generally encountered in the project area
During the percolation testing the hole was carefully filled with water to a minimum
height of 6 inches above the bottom of the hole The time intervals and water drops were
then recorded to provide data upon which calculation of the percolation rates were based
Based on the test results a percolation rate of about 30 minutes per inch was measured
within the near surface silty clay soils The results of the percolation test are presented on
Plate 5 Based on the relatively slow percolation rate measured in the drilled boring it is
our opinion that the feasibility of using septic tank and leach field method for sewage
disposal should be carefully evaluated for the subject site In addition due to the variability
of the subsoil conditions the absorption capacity of the disposal system should be
confirmed by additional percolation tests during construction
LIMITATIONS
The findings and discussions submitted herein are based in part upon information
obtained from the field boring and percolation test Variations of subsoil conditions beyond
the boring may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become
evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be
necessary to reevaluate the findings and discussions provided herein
The field boring location indicated herein are approximate having been estimated
by taping from the features shown on the Site Plan Concept 07 provided by
Kila Construction Inc on Elevation of the boring was estimated by
interpolation form the contour lines shown on the same plan The physical location and
elevation of the boring should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the
methods used
The stratification lines shown on graphic representations of the boring depict the
approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual transition
Water level data from the boring were measured at the times shown on the graphic
representations and or in the text of this report These data have been reviewed and
interpretations made in the formulation of this report However it must be noted that
fluctuation may occur due to variation in rainfall tides temperature and other factors
