The field exploration encountered a thin surface
fill crust and moderately to highly compressible

We recommend pile foundation consisting of 12 inch
square prestressed concrete piles end bearing on
the underlying basalt formation be used to support
the proposed 17 story apartment building An
estimated pile length of 18 feet per pile and an
allowable vertical pile capacity up to 100 tons
per pile may be used
It is our understanding that up to two 2 feet of

INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the proposed 2500 Date Street Apartments in
Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location of the
project site is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1

This report summarizes our findings of the exploration and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our engineering
analyses of the exploration data obtained These recommendations are
intended for design input and not intended for construction estimate or
bidding purpose
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a 17 story apartment building
approximately 50 by 80 feet in plan at the project site
On grade asphalt concrete pavement is planned over the rest of the
project area
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface soil conditions at the project site in order to develop
generalized soil rock data set for the formulation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertinent to the site grading foundation and
pavement design of the proposed apartment building project
The work was done in general accordance with our initial proposal to
McCully Associates dated May 18 1988 and subsequent proposal dated June
8 1989 The scope of work include
1 drilling and sampling four 4 borings to depths ranging from
20 5 to 35 0 feet below the existing ground surface
2 reconnaissance of the project site and its vicinity to make
visual observations of the surficial soil conditions
3 review of available soil and geologic information pertinent to
the project site and its vicinity

4 laboratory testing of selected samples obtained from the field
exploration to determine the pertinent engineering properties and

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located at 2522 2536 Date Street east of the
intersection of Date Street and Kapiolani Boulevard in the Moiliili area
of Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii
The lots are presently occupied by thirteen 13 one to two story
apartment buildings and asphalt concrete parking areas There are also
unpaved perimeter areas for laundry and other purposes
The site is generally level with ground elevations averaging
approximately 8 to 9 feet above Mean Sea Level MSL
The unpaved boundary areas are planted with sparse vegetation such
as sticker bushes

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The Moiliili area is located on the Honolulu coastal plain on the
southern flank of the Koolau Range which ceased shield building activity
approximately 2 8 million years ago during late Pliocene The
stratigraphic history of this area is complex due to a series of sea level
changes and more recent volcanic activity
The project site in Moiliili is geologically situated in an area that
borders between quarternary limestone sugarloaf volcanics and recent
alluviun During the Pleistocene higher sea level stands caused the
accumulation of coral reef deposits while lower stands increased stream
gradients which allowed streams to carve valleys through the sedimentary
and reef deposits Concurrent with these sea level changes were the
post erosional eruptions of the Honolulu Volcanic Series of which the
sugarloaf vent directly affected the project area
The lava from the sugarloaf crater flowed in a southerly direction
covering the lower portion of Manoa Valley and also the Moiliili area It
overlies limestone and marine sediments of the Waimanalo Stand of the sea
and also forced Manoa Stream eastward to its present location
The lava flow was subsequently covered by more recent alluvium and
low energy swampy lagoonal deposits under man made surface fills
SUBSOIL CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions at the proposed building site were explored
by drilling four 4 borings to depths of 20 5 to 35 0 feet below the
existing ground surface The approximate locations of these borings are
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2

W O 2072 00 Page 5
In general the project site is underlain by soft lagoonal deposits
and loose recent alluvium over dense sugarloaf basalt formation which was
encountered in the borings from approximately 12 to 15 5 foot depths and
extend at least down to a depth of 35 feet below the existing grades the
maximum drilled depth of the borings
The aa basaltic lava flow is generally very hard massive and
unweathered except in Boring No 4 where the basalt formation grades to
more severe joints within the upper 5 feet of the underlying massive rock
formation
Overlying the rock formation are approximately 5 to 7 feet of loose
to medium dense alluvial sand and gravel and also 4 to 6 feet soft clayey
silt lagoonal deposits In Boring No 4 only soft lagoonal deposits were
encountered above the rock formation
The site is mantled by a 1 5 to 2 inch thick asphalt concrete
pavement and a thin crust of clayey silt fill approximately 1 to 2 feet
thick
Groundwater was encountered in the borings at the approximate depths
of 8 2 to 9 0 feet below the existing ground surface during our field
exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration discloses that the subsoils above rock
formation at the project site are moderately to highly compressible in
nature and thus not suitable to provide support for shallow spread footing
type of foundation under the anticipated column loads
Therefore we recommend that pile foundations end bearing on the
underlying basalt formation be used to support the proposed 17 story
apartment building
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2072 00 Page 6
We also believe that the required site filling should be placed as
early as possible to reduce the potential of ground settlements and
downdrag on the pile foundations It is important that the proposed
building ground floor slabs be constructed after the concrete structure is
completed to reduce the potential impact of ground settlements on the
slabs due to the site filling
End ing Pile Foundation
Competent basalt formation is present at relatively shallow depths
and it appears that pile foundations can be used to support the proposed
17 story building The piles would develop their vertical load capacity
primarily from end bearing on the underlying rock formation
concrete piles could be utilized
Pile Capacity
The proposed end bearing piles should be driven through the upper
fill crust and soft soils down to the hard massive basalt formation
encountered at depths of about 15 to 18 feet below the existing
grades in the project site
Provided the anticipated site filling up to 2 feet will be placed
as early as possible prior to pile driving to reduce the potential of
inducing downdrag loads we believe that a net allowable vertical
capacity up to 100 tons per pile may be used for the 12 inch square
concrete piles For a inch octagonal pile the vertical pile
capacity may be increased to about 120 tons These allowable
vertical pile capacities may be increased by about one third for the
consideration of seismic and or wind loads
Pile settlements on the order of less than one quarter 1 4 of an
inch are anticipated
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O
2072 00 Page 7
To resist lateral loads on the proposed building the passive earth
pressure around pile caps and tie beams may be used We recommend an
equivalent fluid pressure of 300 p s f per foot depth of the
caps beams be used Backfill against the caps and beams should
consist of select off site granular material properly compacted to a
minimum 95 of its maximum dry density per ASIM Test Method
D 1557 78 The on site fill and soft lagoonal soils are not suitable
for use
The end bearing piles should be driven with a hammer capable of
delivering a minimum 24 000 foot pounds energy and to a resistance of
about 100 blows per foot for the last foot or 9 blows per inch for
each of the last two inches of pile penetration
The piles should be driven continuously without interruption A pile
may be rejected or its design capacity may be reduced when the
driving process is interrupted for more than 4 hours and the pile
cannot be driven deeper
During easy driving when the pile tip is within soft or loose
soils the hammer stroke or energy should be carefully controlled
so that excessive tensile stresses will not be developed in the pile
The pile driving should be monitored by a qualified geotechnical
engineer to see that the piles are driven to the competent bearing
formation
Test Pile Program
A test pile program is recommended in order to achieve a safe and
economical pile foundation system
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2072 00 Page 8
The test pile program should be undertaken to fulfill the following
objectives
1 To more accurately determine the subsequent production pile
lengths
2 To help evaluate and modify if necessary the pile driving
criteria
We recommend that about eight 8 percent of all piles be test driven
at the site These test piles may later be incorporated into the
actual foundation system of the proposed building
Splice cans should be provided at the site during test pile driving
to expedite pile splicing if required Splice cans may he
fabricated with one fourth of an inch thick high grade steel and
should be at least six 6 feet long
Site Grading
It is our understanding that for surface drainage consideration up
to two 2 feet of site filling is tentatively planned to grade the site
to obtain a 1 gradient toward Date Street
We recommend that the site filling be placed as early as possible to
reduce the potential of ground settlements caused by compression of the
soft loose subsoils under the new fill weight
At the onset of earthwork the area within contract grading limits
should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed after building demolition Any
unsuitable materials such as soft subgrade soils rubbish vegetation
old structures and other deleterious materials should be removed and
disposed of properly off site
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2072 00 Page 9
The area to receive fill should be scarified to a minimm depth of
six 6 inches below the cleared subgrade level moisture conditioned and
recompacted to a minimum 90 of its maximm dry density per ASTM Test
Method D 1557 78
The ground surface below the existing pavement subgrade level should
be scarified to a minimm depth of six 6 inches moisture conditioned
and recompacted to a minimum 95 of its maximum dry density per ASIM Test
Method D 1557 78
Any loose soft or yielding spots encountered at pavement subgrade
level should be over excavated down to firm in situ soil and replaced with
select on site soil or approved imported backfill material compacted in
about nine 9 inch loose lifts to a minimm 95 of its maximm dry
density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
The on site upper surface fills may be used as a source of utility
backfill material New imported backfill materials should consist of
non expansive select granular materials less than 3 inches in maximum size
with less than 20 passing the 200 sieve
Pavement
It is our understanding that asphalt concrete pavement will be used
for the proposed on grade parkings and driveways
Based on the anticipated subgrade conditions the following
preliminary pavement section may be used for design
2 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 Inch Base Course 95 Compaction
8 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
The above section assumes that the top 12 inches of the pavement
subgrade consist of select on site or import fill materials compacted to
at least 90 compaction per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2072 00 Page 10
Laboratory CBR tests should be performed on the actual subgrade soils
during construction and the above design section may be revised if
necessary The recommended section considers medium traffic and good
drainage adjacent to the pavement
Slab On Grade
We believe that construction of the ground floor slabs should be
delayed as much as possible if concrete slab on grade is to be used
We recommend that pouring of the concrete slab on grade be delayed
until the proposed building is completed to reduce the potential impact
of ground settlements induced by the anticipated site filling The slab
should be separated from the pile supported structural elements such as
grade beams and pile caps
Provided the above construction procedures are followed the
following preliminary slab on grade section may be used for design
4 Inch Portland Cement Concrete Slab
6 Inch Base Course 95 Compaction
10 Inch Total Slab Thickness
on Compacted Subgrade

Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be

Construction Observation
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provided geotechnical engineering services during the site grading and
foundation construction phases of the work This is to observe compliance

with the design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow

If actual rock soil conditions encountered during construction are

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from borings Variations of conditions
between the borings may occur and the nature and extent of these
variations may not become evident until construction If variations then
appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations
given in this report
The locations of the borings points were determined by taping from
the property boundaries and existing structures as indicated on the
topographic survey of the project site dated prepared by
Harry K Matsuo Elevations of the borings were determined by
interpolation from elevations points on the plan The physical locations
and elevations of the borings should be considered accurate only to the
degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
borings depict the approximate boundaries between soil types and as such
may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on any profiles
or cross sections are based upon interpolation between the borings and may
not represent actual subsurface conditions

Water level data from the borings were collected at the times given
on the graphic representations and or in the text of this report These
data have been reviewed and interpretations made in the formulation of
this report However it must be noted that fluctuation in water level
may occur due to variations in rainfall tides temperature and other
factors
