AS requested we have performed a soil investigation at the
above referenced site to evaluate the subsurface soil
conditions in order to provide recommendations for the
design and construction of the proposed new road
Our investigation consisted of drilling three borings along
the proposed road alignment and visual observations at the
Site
The results of our field exploration and laboratory testing
which were used in the engineering analyses to formulate our
conclusions and recommendations are presented in the
appendix of this report

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located in the Waipio Peninsula just
south of Waipahu The existing access road has an asphaltic
concrete pavement about 15 feet wide and extends from
Waipahu Depot Road to the existing Waipahu Incinerator
structure
The ground surface along the alignment is relatively flat
with elevations of about 4 to 6 feet Kapakahi Stream is
located adjacent to the northern half of the road alignment
It is our understanding that there are 42 and 48 inch sewer
force mains and a 12 inch water line located below the
existing approach road
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
A new 40 foot wide roadway is planned over the existing
access road Fill is desired to raise the lower road grade
for flood considerations The new road alignment would
extend from near the existing Waipahu Sewage Pump Station to
the southern side of the Waipahu Incinerator structure The
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1063 00 Page 3
scope of our work was limited to the lower portions of the
roadway from Waipahu SPS to the existing ramp from the
incinerator since fill was planned only for this area The
remaining higher areas would be at grade or in cut and would
not be subjected to additional large settlements due to the
new fill
GENERAL SITE CONDITIONS
The three borings indicated that the roadway area is
underlain by about one to three feet of gravelly silt fill
materials over a deep deposit of soft Organic clays and
silts with sand seams extending down to about 80 foot depth
The soft organic clays and silts have high moisture
contents low shear strengths and the underlying layers are
highly compressible
Groundwater level was encountered at about 2 5 feet below
the existing ground surface
Detailed descriptions of the soils encountered in the
borings are presented in the Boring Logs in the appendix

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that the site is underlain
by a very thin fill layer one to three feet thick over a
deep deposit of soft compressible clays and silts
The field exploration and laboratory tests indicate that the
soft soils are generally normally consolidated which indi
cates that large additional fill load would cause signifi
cant ground settlements Therefore we recommend that the
new fill be kept to a minimum
Assuming an embankment width of about 34 feet and the unit
weight of the fill soil equals to 110 p c f the estimated
settlements caused by the new fill are as follows

The existing utility lines are situated below the proposed
alignment and would be subjected to the settlements caused
by the new fill Therefore we recommend that the new road
way fill be kept to minimum in order to reduce the possibi
lity of damage to these existing utilities
Due to the above fill constraints imposed by these existing
utilities it may be advisable to realign the approach road
away from the utilities if practicable In order to con
pletely eliminate the effect of the new fill on the existing
utilities the edge of the road fill would have to be sit
uated away from the utilities a horizontal distance equal to
about one and a half times the road fill width or about 50
feet
It is our understanding that it may not be practicable to
move the road this far away from the existing lines If the
road fill is moved 15 feet away from the lines the calcu
lated settlements for 2 feet of fill are about 3 to 4
inches Our previous experience indicated that the computed
settlements based on the consolidation test data generally
are higher than the actual measured settlements especially
when the fill heights are low It is our opinion

that the actual settlements would probably be about 50 to
75 of the calculated settlements Settlement gauges are
recommended to monitor the actual rate of settlement and to
verify the above estimate In this way the roadway could

occur are very difficult to predict because they depend on

Site Grading
The existing site should be cleared of surface vegetation
and debris then scarified and recompacted to 90 of its
maximum dry density established by ASTM D 1557 test method
Select non expansive material generally less than 3 inch in
size should be used for the fill The fill material should
be tested and approved by the soils engineer prior to being
used in the fill
Fills should be placed in 10 inch maximum loose lifts conm
pacted to the above specified density
Two horizontal to one vertical 2 1 cut and fill slopes are
recommended
