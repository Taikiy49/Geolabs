SUBSURFACE GROUTING
BUILDING 3
NEWIOWN MEADOWS
This report presents the results of our subsurface grouting performed
for the subject project The approximate location of the project site and
its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our work on the project was to inject neat cement grout
under pressure at selected depths within the settlement affected area of
Building 3 to attempt to strengthen the subsoils to reduce the potential for
further settlements and damage to the structure
The scope of our work included the drilling of 37 holes to depths of
approximately ten 10 feet below the existing ground surface the
installation of grout tubes in the drilled holes and the injecting of
approximately 4200 gallons of grout
The approximate locations of the grouted holes are shown on the Site
Plan Plate 2

Grouting Methodology
The grouting program was designed and set up to utilize the Tube a
Manchette technique for injecting the grout into the subsurface In this
technique holes are drilled in 2 inch Pvc grout tubes at three 3 foot
intervals These holes are covered with a rubber sleeve and the bottom ends
of the tubes are capped The tubes are then placed in bored holes in the
ground of the affected area and the annular space between the tubes and the
ground is filled with a cement bentonite slurry After the slurry has set
a straddle double packer is placed in the tube and cement grout is
injected through the holes in the tube
This technique allows the grouting operator more discrete control of
the injection of the grout into the subsoils and also allows re grouting of
zones which have high grout uptakes The injected grout displaces soil
particles in the subsurface resulting in overall compaction of the area
being grouted
Grouting Operations
Grouting operations were commenced on with the
injection of neat cement grout into the lower stages of the grout tubes
installed at the most severely affected part of Building 3 Units 3D1 3E1
and 3F1 The initial injections indicated very high uptakes at low
pressures Over 200 gallons of grout was pumped into the deepest stage of
grout hole 1 at Unit 3E1 at 15 p s i with no noticeable increase of
pressure as the injection continued It was decided to pump grout into the
lower stages of all the installed tubes until either a maximm of 100
gallons had been injected or until the grout visibly broached the ground
surface broke out

This program was followed on January 21 and 25 1988 with totals of 900

On it was noted that the grout injection pressures
were beginning to increase which most likely indicated an increase in the

DISCUSSION AND OBSERVATTONS
The injection grouting utilizing the Tube a Manchette system at
Building 3 indicates that the underlying soils were soft and probably
contained voids or loose seams The volumes of cement slurry pumped should
help to densify the soil mass and help to stabilize the soils reduce future
settlements underlying the building
