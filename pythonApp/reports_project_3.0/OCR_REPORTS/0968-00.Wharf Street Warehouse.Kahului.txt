25 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate
boring locations are shown on the Site Plan Plate 1 and

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed development will consist of a steel frame
warehouse structure approximately 200 feet by 134 feet
in plan dimensions concrete floor slab and on grade as
phalt concrete parking Precast tilt up concrete panel
walls will be used around the perimeter of the structure
Structural and floor loading information is not known at
this time but believed to be moderate
About four feet of new fill retained by the tilt up con
crete panels will be placed inside the warehouse to achieve
the finish warehouse floor slab elevation of 11 17 feet
M S L However no major grading is planned outside the
warehouse area

Subsurface Conditions
Based on the information obtained from the borings the
site is generally underlain by the following materials
1 Fill The surface fill material consists of pre
dominantly loose to medium dense silty sand ranging
approximately from 4 0 Boring No 3 to about 7 5
Boring No 1 feet from the existing ground surface
The fill material encountered near the water table
in Boring No 1 is generally much looser than the
ones noted in the other borings
2 Gray Silty Sand Underlying the upper surface fill
materials gray coarse to medium size silty sands were
encountered extending to the depths of all the borings
The groundwater levels encountered in our borings during
the field exploration are presented on the Log of Borings
They varied from 3 8 to 4 5 feet below the existing ground
surface Minor seasonal groundwater fluctuations should be
anticipated
DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Four feet of new fill will be required under the proposed

warehouse floor slab to an elevation of 11 5 feet Due to
the weight of the new fill and warehouse structural loads
some settlement of the underlying loose fill materials will
occur at this site
To minimize future fill settlement scarification and
proof rolling of the entire site are recommended prior to
site grading required for the warehouse structure
Spread footing foundation can be used to transmit the
warehouse structural loads
For the perimeter footings to be bearing on the existing
upper ground surface fill layer an additional 3 foot of
over excavation and recompaction would be required under
the bottom of the proposed footings
The remaining interior spread footings should be supported
on the new structural fill layer required to raise the new
warehouse to finish grade
Specific recommendations for the site preparation design
of foundations and construction are presented in the
following paragraphs

Site Preparation
The site should be cleared of all the vegatation and debris
prior to filling and foundation excavation
The test borings indicated that the existing fill material
is generally in loose to medium dense condition We recom
mend the following site preparation sequence
1 At least three 3 feet of the existing loose fill
below the proposed warehouse perimeter outside
footings bottom elevation should be overexcavated
brought to near optimum moisture and backfilled
in thin compacted lifts 8 inches Each lift should
be compacted to obtain a minimum of 95 percent of the
maximum dry density as determined by ASTM D 1557
The backfilled material should be free of vegetation
debris and other organic matter
2 The remaining grubbed areas under the warehouse and
A C parking should be scarified down to a minimum
depth of eight 8 inches watered to optimum moisture
content and recompacted alsotoa minimum of 95 per
cent maximum dry density prior to any new fill
placement

Structural Fill
About four 4 feet of fill will be placed inside the
proposed warehouse area under the warehouse floor slab
This should be initiated as soon as possible and prior to
footing excavation and placement
Granular non expansive imported fill material could be
used for structural fill and footing backfill The ma
terial stockpiled at the job site contains debris and is
not suitable for use as structural fill in its present
State However after removal of rubble debris and other
deleterious matter the stockpiled material could be re
used to build up the lower portion of the structural fill
required The existing stockpile contaminated by the demo
lished wooden structure and debris should not be used in
the structural fill
Fill and backfill should be placed in horizontal 8 inch
loose lifts and compacted to at least 95 percent of maxi
mum dry density within the limits of structures and 90
percent outside the structure areas as per above specified
standard
We recommend that the above site preparation grading and
all the footing excavation on this project be conducted

Foundations
Based on the site preparation as described above field
exploration laboratory testing and engineering analysis
the site could be developed as planned
For the proposed warehouse structure shallow spread footing
foundation and or continuous wall footings are recommended
Perimeter footings should be placed on a minimum three feet
of recompacted fill existing Interior warehouse footings
could be found on compacted new structural fill to be
placed under the warehouse slab
An allowable bearing pressure of 2 500 pounds per square
foot could be used to design the footings placed on compe
tent fill materials as specified above A one third increase
may be used to allow for infrequently applied wind and seis
mic loads
The footings should have a minimum width of 12 inches and
be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent

Settlement
Building loads and weight of the new structural fill material
planned to raise the grades inside the warehouse as indi
cated on the grading plan would cause the underlying soil
to settle However most of the ground settlement would
occur during site filling and warehouse building construction
Our preliminary estimates indicate that the total footing
settlement on the order of 3 4 of an inch and differential
settlement equal to about half the total settlement should
be anticipated Differential settlement of footings bearing
on similar material would be minimal

Floor Slab
The slab on grade floor can be supported on engineered fill
In any areas where dampness in the floor slab would be unde
sirable or where moisture susceptible floor coverings are to
be used a minimum of four inches of granular capillary
break material such as 3B Fine rock should be considered
In addition a moisture proof membrane should be placed
over the granular material to act as a vapor barrier The
memebrane should be covered by one inch of sand to protect
it during construction and to aid in curing the concrete
In order to avoid frost damage to the floor slab proper
floor insulation drainage and thick capillary break
layer of 3B Fine aggregate should be provided under the
floor slab areas if part of the floor area is used as a
cold storage freezer unit

INVESTIGATION LIMITATIONS
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommen
dations made in accordance with generally accepted local
foundation engineering principles and practices This
warranty is in lieu of all other warranties either expressed
or implied
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report
are based on soil information derived from boring and labo
ratory test data obtained during this site investigation
Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encountered and
