SUPPLEMENTARY SOILS INVESTIGATION
PROPOSED NEW SALES AREA ADDITION TO COMMISSARY
HICKAM AIR FORCE BASE
PROJECT HIC 83 0136
This report presents the results of our supplementary soils
investigation conducted for the construction of the proposed new sales
area addition to the commissary at Hickam Air Force Base Oahu Hawaii
This is a supplement to our original soils investigation of September 20
1983 prepared for the proposed new checkout addition to the north of the
existing commissary building The approximate location of the site and
its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
Our work was performed in general accordance with our proposal of
January 11th
W O 1293 10 Page 2
PROJECT CONSIDERATION
It is proposed to construct a new addition about 30 feet by 140
feet in plan dimensions in the existing sidewalk and roadway areas on
the northwestern side of the existing commissary It is our
understanding that the existing underground utility lines will be
rerouted In general new floor slabs will match the floor grade of the
existing commissary
The foundation loadings are anticipated to range up to 15 kips per
column and about 1 5 kips per linear foot for CMU walls
PURPOSE AND SCOPE OF OUR SERVICES
The purpose of our investigation was to explore the subsurface
conditions under the proposed addition area and to provide supplementary
geotechnical design recommendations for the proposed construction
For this investigation three 6 7 and 8 were
drilled to depths ranging from 5 0 to 6 5 feet and two open pits Nos
6A and 8A were excavated to 6 0 feet below the existing ground surface
at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 Subsoil
samples obtained were examined and tested in our laboratory
Detailed description of the materials encountered in the borings and
open pits along with the laboratory test data are presented in the
appendices of this report
GEOLABS HAWAI
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
SITE CONDITIONS
General
The site for the proposed sales area addition is partly in the
sidewalk area and partly in the asphalt concrete paved roadway area where
several utility lines are buried underneath
Subsurface Conditions
Based on the 3 additional borings drilled and two open pits
excavated the site conditions can generally be described as follows
The slab of the sidewalk was about 4 inches thick underlain by silty
and sandy coral base course The roadway pavement consisted of 2 to 3
inches of asphalt concrete and 4 inches of crushed basalt base course on
granular subgrade materials The utility trench backfill materials under
the slab and A C pavement sections varied somewhat from location to
location and generally consisted of mixtures of volcanic cinder sand
crushed volcanic tuff fragments of sand and gravel sized particles with
occasional pockets of medium stiff silts and clays The granular
backfill materials were generaly dense to medium dense within the top 4
feet below the existing ground surface and graded somewhat loose with
depth
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1293 10 Page 4
The original soils investigation indicated that the Site was once
underlain by extensive volcanic tuff at depths of 3 to 5 feet However
the tuff was not encountered in our supplementary borings but was
disclosed only at the western side of Open Pits 6A and 8A at depths of
4 5 and 4 0 feet respectively The other sides of the pits showed no
evidence of the tuff except trench backfill materials of dense to loose
sandy tuff fragments
In conclusion we believe that the subsurface conditions outside the
utility trenches would be consistent with those disclosed in the original
soils investigation and consist of about 2 to 3 feet of dense coral sand
and or gravel over about 1 foot of expansive grayish brown stiff silty
clay underlain by moderately hard volcanic tuff at a depth of about 3 0
to 5 0 feet below the existing grade
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings or open pits
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
From a soils engineering point of view the site is suitable for the
proposed addition Spread footings founded on compacted fill may be
used provided that a small amount of differential settlement is
tolerable
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1293 10 Page 5
The existing utility lines and backfill under the footing area
should be removed down to competent undisturbed volcanic tuff formation
The over excavated trenches should then be backfilled with compacted
material On grade slabs may be constructed over the existing A C
pavement for the proposed addition Expansive silty clays were found
mixed in the trench backfills and are also anticipated in the area
outside the trench areas Therefore to minimize future heaving
potential the subgrade soils under the slabs should be kept from drying
during construction
Under the slab area the utility lines located below a depth of 3
feet may be left in place
Site Grading
For the proposed sales addition it is anticipated that about 1 foot
of new fill will be required to raise the grade particularly in the
existing roadway area To prepare the site for the proposed
construction the existing sidewalk concrete slabs would have to be
removed The existing A C pavement in the roadway may remain in place
However the concrete gutters should be removed The existing manholes
and catch basins should be removed down to a depth of at least 18 inches
below the bottom of the new slabs The bottoms of manholes and catch
basins should be cleaned of loose debris and other deleterious materials
and the pipe opening plugged The excavations should be backfilled with
suitable on site granular materials placed in horizontal lifts not
exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to 90 of the ASTM
D dry density
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1293 10 Page 6
The fill within the top 18 inches of the finished grade should
consist of granular imported select borrow material or on site material
approved by the project soils engineer The material below 18 inches may
consist of the general borrow as recommended in the original soils
investigation report of
After the existing concrete slabs are removed manholes and catch
basins backfilled as recommended above the exposed subgrade soil should
be moisture conditioned and recompacted to at least 90 of the ASTM
D 1557 78 maximum dry density for a depth of at least 6 inches below the
exposed grade
Spread Footings
As shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 a telephone line traverses
close to and along the new wall line at about 30 feet west of the
existing commissary wall In other areas the new wall may cross several
existing utility trenches In order to provide a more uniform foundation
support for the new sales addition the following are recommended
1 All soils under the footing areas whether natural or trench
backfill material should be removed to undisturbed volcanic tuff and the
excavation backfilled with compacted suitable granular material
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1293 10 Page 7
This excavation should extend horizontally at least 2 feet beyond
the edges of the footing or to the tuff whichever is the closer The
over excavation for the footing should be backfilled with a suitable
on site granular material approved by the soils engineer or imported
select borrow material placed in horizontal lifts not to exceed 8 inches
in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of 95 of the ASTM
D 1557 78 maximum density
2 The footing excavations should be inspected by a soils engineer to
verify that the undisturbed volcanic tuff has been reached
3 Footings adjacent to any utility trench should be deepened so that
their bottom would fall below a plane rising 45 degrees from the nearest
edge of the adjacent trench bottom
4 The footing bottom should be at least 2 feet below the lowest
adjacent finished grade
5 An allowable soil bearing value up to 2 500 p s f may be used for
footings constructed in accordance with the above guidelines A
one third increase may be allowed for soil bearing under wind or seismic
loads All footings should have a minimum dimension of 16 inches
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1293 10 Page 8
6 If a footing is to be located over an existing utility line that is
to remain active under the sales addition area then the footing should
be designed to bridge over the line and reach the volcanic tuff on the
sides of the trench at the appropriate depths recommended above
7 If the footings are constructed according to the above recommended
criteria it is our opinion that the total and differential settlements
are anticipated to be about one half and one fourth of an inch
respectively
On Grade Slabs
It is our understanding that on grade slabs are being considered for
the new sales addition area However subgrade soil conditions could
vary from location to location
According to our original investigation the soil conditions outside
the existing trenched area probably consist of up to 2 feet of
non expansive granular fill over an expansive Clay layer of about 1 foot
thick In the trenched area most of the original clay had been removed
and replaced with granular backfill materials which may contain various
amount of expansive clay pockets at different elevations
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1293 10 Page 9
To provide a more uniform subgrade Support and better control the
heaving potential of the slab from the swelling of expansive clays the
subgrade should be maintained in a moist condition at all time prior to
new fill and base course placement Wetting of the subgrade soil may be
made at the end of each day during construction in the area where
subgrade will be exposed to drying However excessive wettings that
tend to muddy up the site should be avoided We believe that wetting
will not be necessary in the existing A C pavement area that is to
remain
The moistening work may be done concurrently with other construction
activities and it should be monitored by a soils engineer It should be
done sufficiently in advance of covering the slab area
It is recommended that the on grade slab be supported on a 4 inch
compacted base course layer The granular cushion fill for the slab may
be placed directly on the existing A C and the new slab constructed over
the cushion fill
The on grade slab should be structurally separated from columns or
bearing walls

Design Review
As mentioned in the original investigation report the plans and
specifications for site grading and foundation construction should be
forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written comments prior to
construction to see that the intent of our recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the site grading spread footing excavations
subgrade preparations and cushion layer constructions for slabs be
observed by the soils engineer during construction This is to see that
adequate compaction has been obtained and the subsurface conditions are
consistent with the findings of our investigation and that adequate
densifications are attained
If conditions encountered during construction are different from
those presented in this report appropriate modifications to the design
should be made
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above inspections

The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are
based on our site reconnaissance soil information from boring and
open pits and laboratory test data obtained from this soil
investigation
Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encountered and cannot
be fully determined by reconnaissance and soil borings These
unforeseen conditions such as pockets of organic soils trash fill
or expansive soils may occur in localized areas Therefore
additional expenditures may be needed during construction to attain a
properly constructed project Some contingency fund is thus
recommended to accommodate these possible costs

The subsurface conditions at the site were explored
by drilling 3 borings and excavating two open pits at the
approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 A
truck mounted drilling rig was used to drill the borings
The soils encountered in the borings were classi
fied by visual and textural examination in the field by our
engineer who continuously monitored the drilling operations
The classifications were further reviewed by visual
inspection and testing in the laboratory The soils were
classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System The materials encountered in the
borings and open pits are presented on the Log of Borings
and Log of Open Pits Plates A 1 through A 5
In the borings soil samples were obtained by
driving a 2 inch standard split spoon sampler or a 2 4 inch
I D split barrel sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling
30 inches The blow counts used to drive the samplers for
12 inches are shown on the log of borings at the appropriate
sample depths
