As requested we are providing the following geotechnical engineering
recommendations for the design of the proposed clubhouse facilities within the Taman
Dayu Residential Resort Project in the Pandaan area on the island of Java Indonesia
The recommendations provided herein are based on a review of the boring logs of
the area prepared by Testana Engineering Inc a limited field exploration program
consisting of excavating five test pits and our observations during our site visits in
January and March 1996 A test boring is planned within the footprint of the proposed
clubhouse structure to confirm the subsoil conditions at this site in the very near future

Subsurface Conditions
Based on our review of the logs of the two test borings B 36 and B 37 drilled in
the vicinity the five test pits TP 1 through TP 5 conducted by our personnel and visual
observations the subsurface conditions at the site consist generally of residual soils near
the ground surface grading to saprolite with increased depth The residual soils
generally consist of clayey silts and silty clays with high moisture contents and are
generally soft to medium stiff in consistency Based on the test pits excavated the soft to
medium stiff residual soils generally grade to saprolite at a depth of about 1 5 meters
below the ground surface The saprolite materials consist of clayey silts of stiff to very
stiff consistency For more detailed descriptions of the subsoils encountered in the test
pits please refer to the Test Pit Logs presented on Plates 2 1 and 2 2
It should be noted that the ground surface at the proposed clubhouse area has
been partially graded at the time of our field exploration The approximate locations of the
five test pits excavated in relation to the proposed clubhouse structure are shown on the
Site Plan Plate 1 The locations and elevations of the test pits were surveyed and
provided by The physical locations and elevations of the test pits
should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used
Foundations
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered in the test pits excavated we
believe that the proposed clubhouse facilities such as the clubhouse structure parking
structure with tennis courts and swimming pool may be supported on a shallow
foundation system consisting of spread and or continuous footings bearing on the stiff
saprolite materials and or compacted structural fill materials An allowable bearing
pressure of up to 15 tons per square meter tons m may be used for the design of
footings bearing on the stiff saprolite and or compacted structural fill This allowable
bearing pressure is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third 1 3 for
transient loads such as temporary wind and or seismic forces The allowable bearing

Footings should be embedded a minimum of 750 mm below the lowest adjacent
finished grade Where footings are to be located adjacent to below grade structures
such as basement walls or utilities the footings should extend to a depth below an
imaginary 45 degree plane projected upward from the bottom of the below grade
structure or utility This requirement is necessary to avoid surcharging adjacent below
grade structures with additional structural loads and to reduce the potential for foundation
settlement We estimate that the total footing settlements under the anticipated design
loads to be on the order of 25 mm with differential settlements of less than 15 mm
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by frictional resistance
between the base of the footing and the bearing materials and by passive earth pressure
developed against the near vertical faces of the embedded portion of footings A
coefficient of friction of 0 35 may be used between the base of the footings and the
supporting materials Resistance due to passive pressure may be calculated using an
equivalent fluid pressure of 5 tons per cubic meter tons m for footings embedded in the
stiff soils and or fills These values assume that the concrete for footings is placed
directly against the footing excavations or the soil around the footing is well compacted
Unless covered by pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the upper 300 mm
below finished grade should be neglected
Uplift loads may be resisted by a combination of the following 1 dead weight of
the structure and 2 weight of backfill material above the footing where applicable
Contribution to uplift resistance due to the weight of the backfill above the footing may be
estimated using a unit weight of 1 7 tons m
It is possible that the bottom of the footing may expose the medium stiff residual
soils as described above in localized areas If the soft to medium stiff soils are
encountered at the bottom of the footing excavations the soft to medium stiff soils should
be over excavated to expose firm materials and replaced with compacted structural fill
Therefore footing excavations should be observed by a representative of Geolabs Hawaii

Slab On Grade
We anticipate that concrete slabs on grade will be used for the proposed
clubhouse structure parking structure and swimming pool We understand that the
standard practice for the section below slabs on grade generally consist of 75 mm of lean
concrete overlying a 50 mm sand bedding As an alternative to the lean concrete over a
sand bedding interior slabs on grade not subjected to vehicular traffic may be
supported on a 100 mm thick layer of cushion fill The cushion fill would provide more
uniform support of the slab and would also serve as a capillary moisture break
The cushion fill should consist of a clean free draining gravel such as ASTM
C 33 No 57 or No 67 gradation The cushion fill should be lightly compacted with a
vibratory plate or similar equipment For slabs on grade with moisture sensitive
surfacing we recommend that an impermeable membrane be placed over the cushion fill
to reduce potential migration of moisture vapor through the concrete slab In order to
promote more uniform curing of the slab concrete and to provide protection of the vapor
membrane we recommend that a 50 mm layer of moist fine sand be placed above the
membrane prior to placing the slab concrete The sand should be moistened slightly prior
to placing concrete
The parking garage slab on grade will be subjected to vehicular traffic Where
slabs will be subjected to vehicular traffic a 150 mm layer of aggregate base course
compacted to 95 percent relative compaction is recommended for support of the slab
in lieu of the lean concrete over sand bedding or sand impermeable membrane over
cushion fill Provisions should be made for proper load transfer across the slab joints
which are subjected to vehicular traffic The thickened edges of slabs adjacent to any
unpaved area should be embedded at least 300 mm below the lowest adjacent grade
Retaining Walls
We anticipate that the building walls will act as retaining structures where the
building walls extend below the finished grade In general we envision that the basement
retaining walls planned will be conventional concrete retaining walls Foundations for the
retaining walls should be designed in accordance with the recommendations presented in
the Foundations section of this letter report
All retaining structures should be designed to resist the lateral earth pressures due
to adjacent soils and surcharge effects For the basement retaining walls a restrained
condition is anticipated therefore an at rest lateral earth pressure is recommended The

pressures do not include hydrostatic pressures that might be caused by groundwater

Retaining walls should be well drained to reduce the build up of hydrostatic
pressures A typical drainage system would consist of a 300 mm wide zone of permeable
material such as ASTM C 33 No 57 or No 67 gradation immediately adjacent to the
wall with a perforated pipe perforations down at the base of the wall discharging to a
discharge facility A filter fabric such as Mirafi 140N or equivalent should be placed
between the drainage material and the compacted backfill material As an alternative a
prefabricated drainage product such as MiraDrain or EnkaDrain may be used instead of
the drainage material with filter fabric The prefabricated drainage product should also be
hydraulically connected to a perforated pipe at the base of the wall
Surcharge stresses due to areal surcharges line loads and point loads within a
horizontal distance equal to the height of the wall should be considered in the design For
uniform surcharge stresses imposed on the loaded side of the wall a rectangular
distribution with uniform pressure equal to 50 percent of the vertical surcharge pressure
acting on the entire height of the wall which is restrained at the top may be used in
design For the unrestrained cantilever condition a uniform pressure equal to
35 percent of the vertical surcharge pressure acting on the entire height of the wall may
be used Additional analyses may be needed during design to evaluate the surcharge
effects of point loads and line loads

Site Grading
In general we anticipate that the majority of the proposed clubhouse facilities area
will be in a cut condition with the exception of a small area where fills are anticipated The
following guidelines are provided for the anticipated mass grading operations
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable
materials should be removed and disposed of off site or at a designated area After
clearing and grubbing the exposed subgrade should be scarified to a minimum depth of
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers
to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density
of the same soil determined in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91
Modified Proctor Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight
corresponding to the maximum dry density
Any soft weak or yielding areas disclosed during clearing operations should be
over excavated to expose firm ground The soft to medium stiff residual soils identified in
the test pit logs should also be stripped to expose firm ground The resulting excavation
should be backfilled with structural fill compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative
compaction with moisture contents above the optimum moisture Prior to placement of
the backfill the over excavated subgrade should be scarified to a depth of at least
200 mm moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to
at least 90 percent relative compaction The excavated soft and or organic soils should
be properly disposed of off site or at a designated area
Where the existing ground is steeper than five horizontal to one vertical 5H 1V
the fill placement should be keyed and benched into the native soils to properly bond the
fills to the native ground as the fill is brought up in layers
We understand that the fill materials to be used as general and structural fills will
be obtained from the nearby Village Center Cut The anticipated fill materials will
generally be granular materials Therefore the fill materials should be properly moisture
conditioned to above the optimum moisture content placed in level lifts of approximately
150 to 200 mm in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative
compaction As a minimum all fill materials to be used at the proposed clubhouse
facilities should have a CBR value of at least 20 with a swell value of less than 1 percent
when tested in accordance with ASTM D 1883

The filling operations should start at the lowest point and continue up in level
horizontal compacted layers in accordance with the above recommendations for fill
placement Fill slopes should be constructed by over filling beyond the design slope
limits and cutting back to the design slope to provide adequate compaction at the edge of
the slope Fill slopes may be designed with a slope inclination of 2H 1V or flatter Cut
slopes may also be designed with a slope ratio of 2H 1V or flatter
Surface water should be directed away from the slopes and drainage over the top
of any slope should be avoided Earth berms or V ditches should be provided at the top
of slopes to reduce the potential for drainage over the top of the slope In addition the
finish grades outside the buildings should be sloped to shed water away from foundations
and to reduce the potential for ponding Excessive landscape watering near the
foundations should also be avoided Planters next to foundations should be avoided or
have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the subsoils
Where shrinkage cracks are noted at the subgrade level after compaction we
recommend the subgrade be scarified to a depth of about 200 mm moisture conditioned
to above the optimum moisture content and recompacted to at least 95 percent relative
compaction prior to placement of additional fills or construction of improvements If the
shrinkage cracks are minor the soil may be thoroughly moistened to close all cracks prior
to construction of improvements Saturation and subsequent yielding of the exposed soil
subgrade due to inclement weather and poor drainage may require over excavation of the
soft areas to expose firm ground and replacement with compacted fill

Limitations
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part
upon information obtained from test pits Variations of subsoil conditions between the test
pits may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident
until construction is underway If variations then appear evident Geolabs Hawaii should
be contacted to reevaluate the recommendations presented in this report

This report has been prepared solely for the purpose of assisting the design
engineers and architect in the preliminary design evaluation of the proposed project
Therefore this report may not contain sufficient data or the proper information to serve
as the basis for preparation of construction cost estimates nor contract bidding A
contractor wishing to bid on this project should retain a competent geotechnical engineer
to assist in the interpretation of this report and or in the performance of additional site
specific exploration for bid estimating purposes
The Owner Client should be aware that unanticipated soil conditions are
commonly encountered Unforeseen soil conditions such as perched groundwater
extremely soft loose deposits hard layers or cavities may occur in localized areas and
may require additional probing or corrections in the field which may result in construction
delays to attain a properly constructed project Therefore a sufficient contingency fund
is recommended to accommodate these possible extra costs
