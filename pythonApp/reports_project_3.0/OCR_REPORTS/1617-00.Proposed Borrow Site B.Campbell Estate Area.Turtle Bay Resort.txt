In accordance with your request we have performed a geotechnical
engineering reconnaissance investigation at the above referenced site
Our work was done in general accordance with our proposal of January 9
1986
The scope of our investigation included the excavation of eleven
11 borings geologic observations of the existing site conditions
laboratory testing and engineering analysis
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests which we
used in the engineering analysis to formulate our conclusion and
recommendations are presented in the appendices of this reprot
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The purpose of the investigation was to explore the subsurface
conditions at the site to provide soil data for the borrow material
pertinent to the site grading for the proposed development that is to be
constructed near the study area
The main objectives of the investigation were
1 To determine the suitability of the underlying soils for use in
the construction of the site grading for the proposed new golf
course development
2 To determine the excavation difficulties and depths to
groundwater over the site
3 To determine the allowable side slope cut ratios for slope
design
No preliminary grading plan for the golf course construction was
available at the time of our investigation
SITE DESCRIPTION
The borrow site is located about 1 2 mile mauka or south of
Kamehameha Highway The Turtle Bay Resort Hotel is located about
miles northwest of the site
Access to the site is from Kamehameha Highway along the paved Gate
C access road then along a dirt road

The site is fairly steep with slope gradients that may vary from 5
to 20 or more down towards the southeast Ground elevations vary from
about 100 to 200 feet elevation
At the time of our exploration the site was overgrown with bushes
and trees A trail was cleared by a trackloader through the bushes to the
proposed boring locations
The site for this study covered an area of about 1900 feet long by
600 feet wide 25 acres

it appears that several thousand cubic yards of material has been removed
The location of the site is shown on the Project Location Map Plate

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The site is located in a deeply weathered and eroded lower gentle
slope of the Koolau Volcano The weathering has altered the basalt rocks
to an iron rich mass of reddish brown colored soils Drainageways have
cut gullies with fairly steep side slopes into the residual soils The
site is located on one such drainageway The site soils consist of stiff
clays with varying amounts of silts and sands
Soil Conditions
In general the natural soils at the site consisted mainly of stiff
to hard low to moderately expansive silty clays with some highly
expansive clayey soils in certain areas A few boulders were scattered on
the surface
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings which were drilled to
depths of about 25 to 30 feet below the ground surface
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Our findings disclosed that the on site soils generally consist of
25 to 35 feet or more of very stiff to hard fine grain residual soils with
some boulders It appears that the soils at the site may be suitable for
use in the mass grading of the proposed golf course grading and for use in
the lower portions of the structural fill areas for the Turtle Bay
project
Due to the moderate swelling potential the borrow soils should be
kept a minimum of 3 feet below structures

It is anticipated that the excavated clayey soils from the site can
be sandwiched between sandy borrow soils from the Turtle Bay Resort area
to provide better drainage
Borrow Site Grading
Clearing Grubbing and Stripping
Existing vegetation should be cleared and stripped Tree roots in
excess of 1 5 inches in diameter should be grubbed
The stripped surface soil containing organic matter may be reused as
topsoil in the planting areas but should not be used as fill
Entrapped deleterious materials such as mats of grasses or piles of
wood etc under the fill should be avoided
Some boulders were encountered in some of the borings and also noted
during the excavation of trails at the site Therefore a
significant quantity of boulders could be generated from the site
grading Because the borrow site is an excavation type
construction boulders should be hauled off site or stockpiled for
use in fills at other locations
The stripping operation should be continuously monitored by the
soils engineer to verify removal of unsuitable materials
Slope Construction
Based on our findings it appears that the required cut slope at the
borrow site would be primarily in soil If cuts deeper than the
drilled borings are made then some rock may be encountered A cut
slope ratio of 2 1 H V is recommended for slope cuts in soil
Where firm weathered rock is encountered slope cut ratios up to 1 1
H V may be used

Slope benches are recommended where the vertical height of slope
exceeds 20 feet This height may be increased to 30 feet where
weathered rock is encountered in the slope face
Golf Course Site Grading
Preliminary golf course grading recommendations are as follows
Special grading design is contemplated for the golf course fills due
to landscaping concerns Sewage effluent water will be used for landscape
watering and therefore there is some concern for adequate drainage in
the surface soils which could cause ponding problems and hinder the
growth of the vegetation In order to provide good drainage in the near
surface soils the soils should be moderately compacted to allow proper
drainage of the landscape water Since the soils in the golf courses will
not be required to support structural load it is our opinion that a lower
compaction effort may be used

We believe that most of the borrow site natural soils may be
suitable for use as general fill provided that they are not
contaminated with unsuitable materials or over sized particles
Highly expansive soils if encountered should be used in the deeper
fill areas
In the golf course areas the top four feet of fill should be
compacted to a minimum of 75 but not greater than 85 of ASTM
D 1557 due to landscape watering requirements

Site Grading for Remaining Development Area
Plans for the remaining development areas are not available at this
time Preliminary site grading reconmendations are presented below for
use as a guide Specific recommendations for the individual development
areas should be provided on an individual bases after additional
investigation
Ground Preparation
The site should be cleared of all vegetation debris and existing
structures
Any local soft spots and soft material encountered within the
construction area should be removed and replaced with compacted
fill
Following the clearing and grubbing all areas designated to receive
fill should be scarified to a depth of 6 inches and compacted to a
minimm of 90 of the maximum dry density established by ASTM
D 1557 The moisture content of the soil should be above the
optimum moisture content
Fill Material and Placement
Based on the field exploration and laboratory test results in
general the on site materials are suitable for use in compacted
fill and backfill The laboratory swell tests indicated that most
of the on site soils could have low to moderately high swelling
potential depending on the initial compaction density and molding
moisture content prior to saturation At or below the optimm
moisture content the soil exhibits moderate or slightly higher
swell potential However it was noted at a molding moisture

content slightly above the optimum moisture content the wet side of
the optimum moisture the swell potential is substantially
reduced Therefore in order to reduce the swelling potential of
the soil fill materials should be placed at moisture contents
slightly above the optimum moisture content Fills placed within
the top 3 feet of finished grade should be carefully checked to
verify that they have low swelling potential
Boulders less than 2 feet in diameter may be used in the fill
provided they are not allowed to nest together and the voids between
boulders are filled
The fills should be placed in horizontal lifts not exceeding 10
inches in compacted thickness and compacted to a minimm of 90 of
its maximum dry density

Two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V fill slopes are recommended

Fill slopes should be contructed by overfilling past the design fill
slope and then cutting back
Water should be diverted away from the tops of slopes and slope
planting utilized to reduce erosion
Slope benches are generally recommended for slope heights in excess
of 20 feet

Plans and specifications for the project should be forwarded to the

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during
earthwork construction to monitor the contractor s grading operations
Any imported material required should have fairly low expansion
tested and approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
If conditions encountered during construction are different from
those presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above inspection

Laboratory Testing
en et i ny eS EH
Moisture content unit weight and Atterberg Limit determinations
were performed on select samples as an aid in the evaluation and
classification of soil properties The results of these tests are
presented on the log of borings at the appropriate sample depths The

soils to evaluate their grain size distribution The test result is
presented on Plate B 1

undisturbed samples to evaluate the swelling potentials of the soils in
both natural and compacted conditions The test results are summarized on
Plates B 5 thru B 8
Two direct shear tests were performed on selected undisturbed
samples to evaluate the shear strength parameters of the natural soils
The test results are summarized on Plates B 9 and B 10
