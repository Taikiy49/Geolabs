Dear Ms Loui
This fourth quarterly report is presented to summarize our progress on the Kuahea
Street Area Movement project located in Palolo Valley on the Island of Oahu Hawaii as
shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 This report provides summary information
pertaining to the recorded earth movements and subsurface water levels detected to date
during the Phase III instrument monitoring effort
This report is the final quarterly report covering the initial 12 month instrument
monitoring period which commenced in February 2000 Because some additional
instrumentation was installed after the monitoring period had commenced the additional
instruments will continue to be monitored monthly to complete their 12 month monitoring
phase We anticipate that a second 12 month monitoring phase would commence in
August 2001 once all of the installed instruments have been initially monitored for a
minimum of 12 months
PHASE Il FIELD EXPLORATION
The completed field exploration consisted of drilling 15 borings ranging in depths from
about 40 to 100 feet below the existing ground surface Of the 15 borings drilled
inclinometer casings identified as Boring Nos I 71 through I 8 were installed in eight of the
borings The inclinometer casing installations ranged in depths from about 37 to 92 feet
below the existing ground surface
Seven of the 15 borings were converted to nested piezometers identified as Boring
Nos P 1 through P 7 The nested piezometers consisted of screened pipe casings set to
monitor the potential for groundwater in discrete 10 to 15 foot length intervals in the
subsurface The piezometer installations ranged in depths from about 37 to 69 feet below
ground level

DISCUSSION
Based on our monitoring program to date the following discussion is provided to
record some noteworthy information and recent observations of the existing conditions at the
project site
Rainfall Summary
A review of the National Weather Service NWS rainfall data for the Palolo Valley
area between November 2000 and February 2001 was performed Based on our review of
the available data the rainfall recorded at the Palolo Valley gauge was generally average to
below average for the Palolo Valley area In November 2000 the Palolo Valley area

received about 3 08 inches of rainfall The months of December 2000 and January 2001
received about 0 64 inches and 0 75 inches of rainfall respectively In February 2001 the
Palolo Valley area received about 3 44 inches of rainfall
In general the rainfall was more frequent and briefly heavy as a result of the passage
of upper level low pressure systems and Kona type storm systems in the vicinity of the
Hawaiian Islands during November and again in February The months of December and
January were generally very dry across the region Abnormally large rain and flood
producing storm systems did not affect the Palolo Valley area during the last quarterly
monitoring period
Pavement Structural Deformation
The conditions described in the third quarterly progress report remains mostly
unchanged from the conditions observed during our recent site visits Some widening
pavement crack patterns have been observed on Waiomao Road located in the vicinity of
Boring No I 8 located at 2318 and 2324 Waiomao Road These cracks have widened to
approximately 1 inch in width and appear to be capable of transmitting surface runoff into
the subsurface These recent observations suggest that some significant near surface
ground movements may be occurring in this area
The existing pavement cracks located along Kuahea Street at the head of the
Waiomao Landslide appear to be slowly widening In addition some greater vertical offset
scarps located across the crack boundaries was observed These recent observations of
the ground surface suggest that the head of the Waiomao Landslide remains active
Our recent observation of the area located in the vicinity of the intersection of Kuahea
Street with Kuahea Place indicates that no new ground surface deformations appear to be
evident Based on the available Board of Water Supply records a water line break on the
existing 8 inch water line was repaired in December 2000 The approximate location of the
water line break was fronting 2331 Kuahea Street The water line break occurred in the area
of suspected earth movement located at the eastern half of Area A
