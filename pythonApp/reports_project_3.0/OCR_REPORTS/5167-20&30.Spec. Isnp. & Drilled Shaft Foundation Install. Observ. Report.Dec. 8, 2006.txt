At your request and as required by the City and County of Honolulu Geolabs Inc
has provided geotechnical engineering observation and consultation services during the
drilled shaft foundation installation for the above project This letter report summarizes our

Geolabs conducted a geotechnical engineering exploration for the Moana Pacific
project and our recommendations are presented in the above referenced report The
purpose of our geotechnical engineering observation during construction was to provide
engineering consultation and to assist in problem resolutions during the construction of the
drilled shaft foundation In addition we provided Special Inspection services by
observing the drilled shaft foundation installation at the Moana Pacific project for general
conformance with the project plans and specifications
Project Considerations
The Moana Pacific high rise condominium project is located at the northwestern
corner of the intersection of Piikoi Street and Kapiolani Boulevard in the District of
Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii as shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
2006 Kalihi Street Honolulu Hawaii 96819

The development will consist of a five level above grade parking garage and
apartment storage encompassing the majority of the project site Two oval shaped
residential towers will rise above the parking garage at the northeastern and southwestern
portions of the property Each residential tower will consist of 46 stories of dwelling units
and two levels of penthouse units comprising a total of 416 living units for each tower
The new high rise building and parking structure are supported on deep
foundations consisting of cast in place concrete drilled shafts Drilled shafts with a
minimum diameter of 24 and 42 inches were used to support the new parking structure
For the two high rise residential towers high capacity drilled shafts with minimum
diameters of 42 and 48 inches and lengths of 65 and 110 feet were used for the
foundation support of the heavily loaded structures
As part of the construction requirements and subsequent recommendations
three load tests were conducted to evaluate the foundation design In addition the
project was developed in two consecutive phases Phase consisted of 100 drilled
shafts for the parking structure and 57 drilled shafts for the residential tower Phase II
consisted of 96 drilled shafts for the parking structure and 55 drilled shafts for the
residential tower for a total of 308 drilled shafts for the entire development The scope
of our work discussed in this letter report includes observation of the drilled shaft
foundation construction for the Moana Pacific project Phases 1 and 2 only The
general contractor for the project is Hawaiian Dredging Construction Company who
also performed the drilled shaft installation
Subsurface Conditions
The subsurface conditions at the project site generally consist of fill materials at the
ground surface underlain by lagoonal deposits The fill materials generally consist of
medium dense sands and gravel extending to depths of about 3 to 8 feet below the
existing ground surface The lagoonal deposits generally consist of loose silty coralline
sands and gravel and soft sandy silts extending to depths of about 15 to 20 feet below the
existing ground surface
The lagoonal deposits were underlain by an upper coral ledge which is highly
variable erratic in terms of thickness and quality The upper coral ledge was
encountered from depths of about 15 to 17 feet below the ground surface extending to
depths of about 23 to 44 feet below the existing ground surface The quality of the upper
coral ledge encountered in the borings was variable ranging from soft to hard
Below the upper coral ledge the borings generally encountered coralline detritus
consisting of medium dense coralline sands and gravel with some dense zones of coral
formation extending down to depths of about 34 to 44 feet below the ground surface Soft
to hard coral formation interbedded with sandstone ledges and stiff alluvial clay layers then
were encountered and extended down to the maximum depth explored of 158 feet below
the ground surface

Due to the inter bedded nature of the coralline detritus and coral formations at the
project site the subsurface conditions may be considered highly variable across the
project site The coral formations encountered in the borings generally consist of
algal coral reef and cemented sands The cementation of the coral formations appears
to vary considerably with materials ranging from competent algal coral reef formation to
weak coral and medium dense silty sands with coralline debris The coralline detritus
encountered at the project site generally consist of loose to medium dense sands silt
and coralline debris with weak to virtually no cementation
Groundwater was encountered in the borings drilled at depths of about 3 9 to
7 8 feet below the ground surface at the time of our field exploration The groundwater
levels encountered generally correspond to about Elevations 1 3 to 2 3 feet Mean Sea
Level MSL
Summary of Drilled Shaft Installation
As part of the project requirements and our subsequent recommendations three
sacrificial load test shafts were constructed and tested Following completion of the load
tests the drilled shaft lengths for the project were revised based on the results of the
instrumented load tests The following subsections provide descriptions of the load test
shaft construction and discussions on the production drilled shaft installation
Load Test Shafts
As part of the project specifications and our subsequent recommendations three
sacrificial 42 inch diameter load test shafts were installed at the project site at the
approximate locations indicated on the Site Plans Plates 2 1 and 2 2 The load test shafts
were excavated by the drilled shaft contractor from August 27 through
The load test shafts were extended to depths of about 116 to 120 feet below the existing
ground surface The drilled shaft contractor lowered the reinforcing cage with an
Osterberg Load Cell and embedment strain gage instrumentation into the drilled holes and
placed concrete in the load test shafts on and
Temporary casing with a diameter of about 47 inches were installed to about 37 to
38 5 feet below the ground surface for the load test shafts Details of the load test shaft
construction are presented in Appendix A of this letter report
After the load test shafts were installed and cured for at least 7 days the load tests
were performed by Load Test Inc The load test shafts were loaded to approximately
2 414 to 4 229 kips the maximum capacity of the Osterberg load cell Complete details of
the load test program are presented in the reports prepared by Load Test Inc
It should be noted that during concrete placement for Load Test Shaft No
a significant concrete over pour was recorded and concrete was observed to be flowing
into an adjacent open shaft excavation located approximately 16 feet away A subsequent

investigation utilizing 4 inch diameter boreholes in the vicinity of the test shaft confirmed
the presence of a roughly 4 foot thick zone of concrete extending between the shafts
starting from a depth of approximately 50 feet below ground surface Prior to load testing
HDCC drilled some overlapping 2 foot diameter holes through the concrete between the
shafts in an effort to break through the concrete bridge noted above The holes were
drilled approximately 5 feet from the center of the test shaft
While lowering the reinforcing assembly into the shaft hole at Load Test Shaft No 2
LT 2 the cage resisted further insertion when the tip of the cage was at an estimated
depth of between 85 and 90 feet below grade After repeatedly raising lowering and
attempting to rotate the cage and adjusting the crane boom angle the cage was able to
move relatively freely into the excavation beyond that point Based on the preliminary
results provided by LOADTEST Inc it appears that the load test failed at a load of
approximately 2 400 kips about 70 of the scheduled maximum test load of 3 600 kips
Basically the load test failed by pushing down the test shaft starting at a load of about
1 000 kips and then the load test continued until the load test system failed at about
2 400 kips due to differential loading of the shaft one side was pushed down more than
the other side of the shaft at the end of the test The likely explanation for the failure is
that the shaft was crooked at the bottom of the shaft probably near the load cell resulting
in the differential movement of the shaft which ultimately led to failure of the load testing
system hydraulic leakage of the pumping system
Given the complications from both load tests LT 1 and LT 2 as discussed above
a third load test designated as LT 3 was recommended at the location shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2 2 No unusual problems occurred during construction of this load test
shaft

Production Drilled Shafts
The drilled shaft contractor installed 310 production shafts including two supplemental
shafts Shaft Nos 301A and 301B from through
Geolabs representatives observed the drilled shaft excavation placement of reinforcing
cage and concrete operations during the installation of the production drilled shafts
In general production drilled shafts were excavated using a SoilMec R 825
equipped with 24 42 and 48 inch diameter auger core barrel and clean out bucket
similar to the load test shafts At the latter part of the drilled shaft installation for the
parking structure the 24 inch diameter auger was damaged and the subsequent 24 inch
diameter drilled shafts were drilled using a 30 inch diameter auger Temporary steel
casings were installed to depths of about 16 5 to 37 5 feet below the ground surface in the
production drilled shafts In general 29 47 and 53 inch diameter temporary casings were
used for the 24 42 and 48 inch diameter drilled shafts respectively Because the drilled
holes stayed open without significant caving in of the sidewalls polymer slurry was not
used during excavation of some of the production drilled shafts After excavation of the
production shafts to their designated tip elevations the reinforcing cages were lowered
into the drilled holes to the appropriate elevations with the use of a crane After the
reinforcing cage was installed in the drilled hole concrete was placed through a 4 inch
steel tremie pipe starting from the bottom During concrete placement we observed that
at least 5 feet of concrete head was maintained above the bottom of the tremie pipe
Due to the presence of groundwater within the drilled shaft holes concrete was
placed in the drilled shaft holes using the tremie method Concrete with slump ranging
from 7 to 9 inches were used for the drilled shafts It should be noted that Pacific Testing
Services Inc monitored the concrete used for the drilled shafts including the performance

of slump tests and compression tests Our geotechnical engineering observations during
the drilled shaft installation and the as built diameters and the lengths of the production
drilled shafts installed for the project are summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 11 for
reference
Due to the potential for significant cave ins suspected during the concreting of
Shaft No 198 integrity coring was performed to evaluate the acceptability of the drilled
shaft Based on the coring results conducted on Shaft No 198 it appears that no core
recovery was obtained between 71 and 87 feet below the top of the rebar cage As a
result we recommended that the capacity of the drilled shaft be reduced from 3 400 kips
to about 1 800 kips The Structural Engineer of Record was notified of this condition and
no remedial action was required for the subject shaft
Integrity coring was conducted at Shaft No 275 to evaluate the potential for
cave ins during concreting of the lower portion of the subject shaft Based on the coring
results it appears that good quality concrete was encountered throughout the lower
portion of the shaft length As a result it is our opinion that Shaft No 275 is acceptable
and no remedial action is required
Due to the potential for presence of a cold joint during concreting of Shaft No 301
integrity coring was conducted to evaluate the acceptability of the drilled shaft Based on
the coring results it appears that a cold joint was present at about 44 foot depth In
addition highly contaminated concrete was observed between depths of about 51 and
61 feet below the existing ground surface As a result we recommended reducing the
allowable compressive load capacity of the subject shaft from 2 400 kips to about 750 kips
In addition the ultimate uplift load capacity should be reduced from 1 200 kips to
1 000 kips for the subject shaft The Structural Engineer of Record was notified of this
condition and Drilled Shaft Nos 301A and 301B were installed adjacent to Drilled Shaft
No 301 to supplement the reduced capacity of Shaft No 301 The two supplemental
shafts are 24 inches in diameter and 55 feet long with a design capacity of 500 kips each
Based on our field representatives observations it is our professional opinion that
the production drilled shafts supporting the Moana Pacific project Phases 1 and 2 were
installed in general accordance with the intent of the project plans and specifications and
the geotechnical recommendations presented in our geotechnical engineering report for
project dated and subsequent recommendations In addition it is our
professional opinion that the drilled shafts have been installed to sufficient depths to
provide the allowable design load capacities at the structural column locations
