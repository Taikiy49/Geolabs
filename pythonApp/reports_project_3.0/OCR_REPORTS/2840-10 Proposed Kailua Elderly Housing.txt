Field Exploration

Boring GB 1 was drilled at approximately 15 feet west of the constructed crane
pad Boring GB 2 was drilled between boring nos B 1 and B 3 drilled previously by the
project soil consultant Boring GB 3 was drilled on the east side of the sump pit
excavations The boring logs presented on Plates A 1 through A 3 are enclosed
Samples were taken using 3 inch outer diameter Modified California samplers
throughout the full depths of the borings in order to delineate the subsoil profile All the
retrieved samples were photographed and stored in our laboratory for future reference
Selected samples were tested in our laboratory to determine their grain size distributions
in order to further identify the materials encountered The sieve analysis test results are
presented on Plates B 1 and B 2

recognized that dewatering inside the deeper sump pit excavations would not be
practical and that the sump pits would have to be installed under water
Based on our field data collected during dewatering of the crane pad excavation

Utility Below Basement
It is our understanding that underground utility lines below the basement will be
installed below the natural groundwater table For example an incoming line to
the sump pits will have to be installed with its invert at about 2 4 feet MSL
The changed ground condition indicates that the upper very permeable gravel
layer exists at an elevation of 1 83 feet MSL and potentially higher We believe
that more difficult dewatering and backfilling will be encountered during the utility
line installation and that some lines may have to be installed under water and the
utility tlenches may have to be backfilled also under water rather than in a dry
condition as implied by the soil consultant s borings
It is our current understanding that the project mechanical engineer and the
plumber are working together to alleviate this anticipated difficulty by substituting
the specified metal pipes with PVC or ABS pipes which can be connected above
water and then installed under water
Basement Excavation
It was indicated previously in our dewatering evaluation report dated March 10
1992 that substantial dewatering difficulty was not anticipated during construction

of the basement excavation especially with the installation of a perimeter sheetpile
shoring which will form a cut off wall below the basement A network of shallow
gravel trenches connected to six 6 shallow sump pumping wells or pits installed
around the basement excavation were recommended In general we believe that
these dewatering recommendations may still be followed
However due to the changed ground condition it appears that significant
dewatering difficulty may be encountered if the dewatering system is not designed
to avoid dewatering the upper very permeable gravel layer at the higher elevation
of around 2 feet MSL
To avoid pumping the upper gravel layer the perimeter dewatering pumps would
have to be placed as shallow as possible below the basement excavation level
As aresult the effectiveness of groundwater drawdown by sump pumping at the
perimeter of the excavation would be reduced and more number of pumps and a
closely spaced network of gravel trenches will be needed inside the basement
excavation
To reduce the potential dewatering difficulty during high tide it appears that a
thick filler gravel blanket minimum 18 inches thick will be placed below the entire
basement to aid in the construction dewatering More number of submersible
pumps will also be needed at strategic locations inside the basement excavation
to create sufficient groundwater drawdown effect
It should also be noted that the construction of some of the footing foundations
especially those more deeply embedded below the basement bottom may
encounter standing water in the footing excavations depending on the actual
elevation of the upper very permeable gravel layer at the locations of the footings
It is our current understanding that the project structural engineer may allow the
use of more expensive tremie concrete with special admixture for the footing
bottom portions below water
