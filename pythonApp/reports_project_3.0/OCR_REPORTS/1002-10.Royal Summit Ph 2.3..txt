PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this soil engineering investigation was to
explore the subsurface soil and groundwater conditions at
the site to provide data for use in the design of the site
grading for the proposed subdivision development
GEOLABS HAWAII
1002 10 P 2
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests
which were used in the engineering analysis that formu
lated our conclusions and recommendations are presented
in the appendix of this report
The approximate boring locations are shown on the Site
Plan Plate 1 and the boring logs are presented in
Appendix A Information regarding the field exploration
and laboratory testing are also presented in the appendices
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is situated on a mountain ridge between
Waimalu Valley and the existing Newtown Estates Develop
ment The ridge generally extends in an east west direc
tion
Access to the site is from Kaahele Street at the inter
section with Komo Mai Drive Construction of the Phase 1
portion of the development situated west of Phases 2 and
3 has recently commenced
The site generally slopes down toward the southwest at
up to 2 1 were noted beyong the perimeter areas of the
site which slope down into the adjacent valleys below
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1002 10 P 3
The ground elevation generally varies from about 370 to
680 feet
Most of the site is covered with abandoned sugar cane
and trees Several dirt roads extend into the area
The Phase 2 portion of the development consists of 173
Single family residential lots and the required access
roads A park area is planned in the north central area
Cuts generally less than 10 feet high and fills up to
about 20 feet are planned for the Phase 2 site grading
The high fills will be in the northwestern portion of the
Site
The grading plans for the Phase 3 area were not available
at the time of our investigation Similar site grading
development is anticipated
SOIL CONDITIONS
In general the soils encountered in the borings consisted
of very stiff to hard reddish brown silty clay and clayey
Silt residual soil Laboratory tests indicate that these
soils have basically low expansion potential when wetted
Pockets of moderately expansive clay were encountered in
some borings However we believe that these are isolated
areas and they are not extensive
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1002 10 P 4
Highly weathered rock and boulders were encountered in a
few of the borings at about 7 to 14 foot depths
Groundwater levels were not encountered in the borings
during the field exploration
CONCLUSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed competent residual soils
at the proposed project site From a soils engineering
point of view the site is suitable for the proposed
residential development
Conventional site grading methods could be used for the
site development
Generally shallow spread footing foundation systems could
be used for the single family residences
Isolated pockets of moderately expansive clay soil will
be encountered during the project site grading When
encountered these clay soils should be utilized only
in the deeper portion of the fill areas a minimum of
3 feet below the finished grade We recommend that sui
table deeper fill areas be kept available for disposal of
these clay soils when they are encountered to eliminate
the need for stockpiling and double handling of these
soils for future disposal in the deeper fill areas
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1002 10 P 5
Weathered rock or boulders were encountered in a few
borings at 7 to 14 foot depths Therefore some amount
of boulders may be encountered in the deeper cut areas
and utility trenches Most of these boulders less than
2 feet in size could be utilized in the deep fill areas
if they are properly scattered and voids backfilled with
compacted fines around them
Site Grading
The site should be cleared of vegetation surface debris
and other deleterious material In areas to receive fill
the ground should be scarified to a minimum depth of 6
inches brought to near optimum moisture content and
compacted to a minimum of 90 density per ASTM D 1557
70 test method
After the above site preparation the fill should be
moistened to above optimum moisture content placed
in maximum 10 inch lifts and compacted to obtain a
minimum 90 of its maximum dry density established by
the above ASTM test designation
Based on the field exploration and laboratory test results
in general the on site materials are suitable for use in
compacted fill and backfill The laboratory swell tests
indicated that the on site soils could have low to moderately
high swelling potential depending on the initial compaction
density and molding moisture content prior to saturation
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1002 10 P 6
At or below the optimum moisture content the soil exhibits
moderate or slightly higher swell potential However it
was noted at a molding moisture content slightly above the
optimum moisture content the wet side of the optimum
moisture the swell potential is substantially reduced
Therefore in order to reduce the swelling potential of
the soil fill materials should be placed at moisture
contents slightly above the optimum moisture content
Based on averaging the in place densities of soil samples
obtained from our test borings and maximum density deter
mination it appears that the computed shrinkage factor
varies from about zero percent up to about six per cent
for the on site cut and compacted fill quantities The
in place dry densities averaged about 85 p c f compared
densities
It is recommended that if additional off site borrow is
required it should be tested and approved by the soils
engineer prior to transportation to the site to assure
that it is suitable for the proposed construction The
material should generally be non expansive and should not
contain large percentages of boulders organic or dele
terious matter
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1002 10 P 7
Where the existing ground is steeper than 5 horizontal
to 1 vertical 5 1 benching and keying are required
to properly bond the new fill to the slope The filling
operation should start at the lowest point and continue
up in level compacted layers in accordance with the above
structural fill requirements For construction details
refer to Plate 2
Slopes
Two horizontal to 1 vertical 2 1 fill slopes are
recommended
Cut slope ratios up to 14 horizontal to 1 vertical 14 1
can be used for cuts into the very stiff residual soil
For cut and fill slopes greater than 20 feet in vertical
height 8 foot drainage benches should be placed at mid
height of the slope to reduce the amount of runoff over
the slopes
Surface water should be diverted away from the top of
Slope and slope planting utilized as soon as practicable
to reduce erosion
Foundations
The field exploration disclosed that the site is under
lain by very stiff residual soil
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1002 10 P 8
For the proposed light residential structures spread
footing foundations or slab on grade may be used To
minimize the effects of slope creep and erosion slabs
should be set back from the tops of slopes a minimum
distance of 5 feet Also post footings located close
to the tops of slopes should be embedded a sufficient
depth to obtain a minimum horizontal set back distance
of 5 feet from the slope face Post footings 6 feet or
more away from the tops of slopes should be placed 12
inches below the finish grade
Foundation bearing on fill slope steeper than 3H 1V should
be avoided If they are absolutely required they should be
designed on an individual basis by a licensed soil engineer
For the light loads anticipated an allowable bearing
pressure of 2500 p s f can be used for footings resting
on the very stiff residual soil or compacted fill
If a footing is located next to a utility trench the
footing should extend to the bottom of the utility trench
or be designed to span over the trench to reduce footing
settlement due to settlement of the trench backfill
Slab on grade should be placed over a 4 inch layer of base
course compacted to 95 of its maximum dry density The
thickened edge around the slab on grade should extend
a minimum of 8 inches below the adjacent ground surface
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1002 10 P 9
To protect against moisture damage of the flooring
material a polyethylene moisture barrier should be
considered
Isolated pockets of moderately expansive clay soil may
be encountered at or near finished house pad subgrade
in the cut areas When they are encountered the
clay soil should be overexcavated for a maximum depth
of 2 feet and replaced with select on site material
The slab subgrade soils should be kept moist and not
be allowed to dry out prior to concrete placement
The lots should be graded to drain water away from the
house foundations
Pavement
Asphaltic concrete pavements are planned for the project
Based on the laboratory CBR tests on the near surface
soil and experience in the Phase 1 development area the
preliminary pavement design is as follows
Z2 inch Asphaitic Concrete
6 inch Base Course
0 6 inch Select Borrow Subbase
8 14 inch Total Pavement Thickness
on Compacted Subgrade Soil
We estimate that about one fourth of the roadways would
require the 6 inch select borrow subbase layer
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1002 10 P 10
Additional CBR tests should be performed on the actual
subgrade soil samples during construction and the above
design section can be revised if necessary The recom
mended section considers medium traffic and that good
drainage will be provided adjacent to paved areas
Design Review
The plans for foundations site grading and utility con
struction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for
review and comments prior to construction to see that
the intent of our recommendations is included in the
project plans and specifications
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during
earthwork construction to monitor the contractor s grading
operations
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be
observed by a soil engineer during construction to see
that adequate bearing has been obtained and that the sub
surface conditions are consistent with the findings of
our subsurface investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive
tested and approved by the soil engineer prior to hauling
to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent
upon the above inspections

APPENDIX A
FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored
by drilling 20 borings utilizing truck mounted equipment
The depths of all the borings were terminated at 7 5 to
16 5 feet below the existing ground surface at the approxi
mate locations shown on the enclosed Site Plan Plate 1
The soils were classified by visual and textural
examinations in the field by our engineer who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications
were verified by visual inspection and testing in the
laboratory All soils were classified in accordance with
the Unified Soil Classification System A graphic pre
sentation of the soils encountered is presented on the
Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 20
Soil samples were obtained by driving either a 2 4
inch 1 D ring sampler or a standard penetration sampler
with a 140 pound hammer free falling a distance of 30 inches
The blow counts to drive the sampler the last 12 inches are
shown on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths

APPENDIX B
LABORATORY TESTING
Moisture content and unit weight determinations were
performed on selected samples as an aid in the classifica
tion and evaluation of soil properties The results of
these tests are presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1l
through A 20
Nine swell tests on one inch ring samples of remolded
and natural samples were performed to evaluate the expansion
potential of the soils The test results are summarized on
Plate B 1
Three CBR tests were performed on the near surface
soils to evaluate the suitability of the soil as pavement
support The results are presented on Plate B 2
Three direct shear tests were performed on selected
samples to evaluate the strength characteristics of the
soils The test results are plotted on Plates B 3 through
B 5
Two moisture density determinations ASTM D 1557 were
performed on a bulk sample of the near surface soils to
evaluate its compaction characteristics The test results
aresummarized on Plate B 2

STANDARD GRADING SPECIFICATIONS
ROYAL SUMMIT PHASES 2 3
The work under this section includes clearing and grubbing
preparation of fill areas placement and compaction of fill
and removal and backfilling of underground structures These
specifications are of a general nature and may be superseded
by specific requirements or recommendations contained within
the soils report
I Clearing
All areas within contract limit lines shall be cleared of
trash debris organic matter and all other deleterious
materials These materials shall be removed and disposed
offsite
II Preparation of Fill Areas
A Loose surface soils shall be removed to expose compe
tent natural soils Localized soft pockets shall be
undercut and backfilled with compacted select mate
rials
B All surfaces shall be inspected and approved by the
soils engineer prior to the placement of any fill
C After approval the Surface shall be scarified to a
depth of six inches brought to near optimum moisture
content and compacted as herein specified
III
IV
2
D Where the existing surface exceeds a Slope of 5
horizontal to 1 vertical 5 1 the ground surface
shall be keyed and benched into competent soil to
bond the fill to the existing ground surface
E Subsurface drains may be required as directed by the
soils engineer where springs or zones of seepage are
encountered
Fill Materials
Material for fill shall consist of on site soils or
approved import material Import material shall be
tested and approved prior to transportation to the Site
Fill material shall contain less than 5 percent organic
matter and other deleterious material and shall not
contain rocks or lumps in excess of eight inches 8
in diameter
Placement and Compaction of Fill
A The fi11 shall be placed in horizontal layers which
when compacted will not exceed nine inches 9 in
thickness Each layer shall be spread uniformly and
blade mixed to attain uniformity of material and
water content within each layer
3
Rock fragments or boulders less than eight inches
8 in diameter may be utilized provided they are
not concentrated in any one area and that there is
sufficient fine grained material to fill the voids
between the rocks
Rocks greater than eight inches 8 in diameter
shall be removed from the fill unless specifically
approved for placement in the fill by the soils
engineer
All fi11 shall be compacted to a minimum of 90
percent of the maximum dry density established by
ASTM Test Designation D 1557 unless otherwise
specified in the soils report
The compaction equipment may be sheepsfoot vibra
tory or pneumatic tire rollers suited to the soil
type and capable of producing the specified results
in an expedient and efficient manner The equipment
shall be in good working condition fully ballasted
and self cleaning
The compaction shall be accomplished in a systematic
and uniform manner over the entire fill area to pro
duce a uniformly compacted fill
VI
4
Fill material placed in an unsatisfactory condition
and not in accordance with these specifications
shall not be approved and shall be removed or reworked
until the required density is obtained
The soils engineers shall be provided unrestricted
access to the fill to perform the compaction tests
Removal and Backfilling of Underground Structures
Underground structures such as cesspools cisterns
septic tanks wells pipe lines etc shall be removed
as directed by the soils engineer Backfilling of the
excavations shall be in accordance with these specifi
cations
Seasonal Requirements
A
No fill shall be placed during unfavorable weather
conditions When interruption of work is due to
heavy rain the previous fill surface shall be
approved before resumption of earthmoving operations
The contractor shall maintain the surface of the fill
so that it will be free draining and will not pond
surface water
