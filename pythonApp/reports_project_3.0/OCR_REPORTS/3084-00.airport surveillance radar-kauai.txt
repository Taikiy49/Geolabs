INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the design of the proposed Airport Surveillance Radar ASR facility at the
Linue Airport on the island of Kauai Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the
project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct an Airport Surveillance Radar facility at the site The
proposed facility will be constructed in a square shaped enclosure of approximately 138 feet
on each side The fenced facility will include a 37 foot high steel ASR tower a one story
equipment building a 75 kW power plant concrete pads for HVAC and a transformer a
1000 gallon above ground fuel storage tank and associated site facilities The site will be
surfaced with aggregate base course and an access road is planned between the facility
and the existing dirt access road to the west The approximate locations of the planned
facilities are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
We understand that the radar tower will have four 4 legs spaced approximately
24 feet apart in a square configuration The radar dish will be covered by a plexiglas or
fiberglass structure known as a radome which serves to protect the radar
instrumentation from the potentially corrosive spray from the nearby salt water The
estimated unfactored loads for each footing of the tower were provided in the Request
for Quotations and are as follows
e Maximum Uplift 65 kips
e Maximum Compression 254 kips
e Maximum Lateral 20 kips

We understand that the proposed equipment building will be a one story structure
with masonry walls and a steel deck roof It is anticipated that the building will be
supported by continuous footings with a concrete slab on grade floor
Details of the proposed site grading were not known at the time this report was
prepared However we understand that the Federal Aviation Administration FAA

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is situated on the east side of the Lihue Airport on the windward
side of the island of Kauai The general location and vicinity of the site are shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1
The site is located on the seaward side of and adjacent to an existing unpaved
road approximately 0 85 mile south of Ahukini Road above Opoi Beach The Lihue
Airport is located to the west of the proposed project site The site slopes gently down
eastward towards the ocean at an average grade of about five to seven percent An
approximately four to five foot deep drainage gully bisects the site oriented in an east
west direction The site has been used for the cultivation of sugar cane and was cleared
for this exploration
The existing ground surface elevation of the site varies from approximately 68 to
79 feet Mean Sea Level MSL

the gully

Regional Geology
The island of Kauai is composed of a single basalt shield volcano built by the
extrusion of the lavas of the Waimea Canyon Volcanic Series during the late Pliocene
more than 2 3 million years before present Following the cessation of this main shield
building phase there was renewed volcanic activity with the extrusion of the basaltic lavas
of the post erosional Koloa Volcanic Series and the concurrent deposition of the alluvial
sediments of the Palikea Formation Our exploration of the site and its vicinity indicate
that the area is underlain by thick soil layers derived from the in situ weathering of Koloa
Basalts

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
It is our opinion that development of the proposed project site is feasible from a
geotechnical engineering point of view provided that our recommendations are followed
We recommend that spread or continuous footings bear on the underlying hard residual
soils encountered at the site or on compacted engineered fill placed in accordance with
the recommendations presented herein
The bottom of all footing excavations should be observed by a representative of
Geolabs Hawaii prior to the placement of reinforcing steel to evaluate whether an
adequate footing bearing surface has been exposed and if loose soils have been
removed prior to concrete placement
We estimate that for footings designed and constructed in accordance with the
above criteria the potential total settlements for foundations bearing on in situ residual
soils should be less than inch with differential settlements less than 4 inch Potential
total settlements for foundations bearing on fill soils placed and compacted as
recommended herein should be on the order of less than one inch with differential
settlements of less than inch

Slabs On Grade
It is anticipated that the floor of the proposed equipment building will consist of
concrete slab on grade The building slab and its cushion may be supported by fill
provided the subgrade is scarified to a depth of eight inches 8 moisture conditioned
to above optimum moisture content and compacted to 90 percent of its maximum dry
density as determined by ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91 A six inch 6 layer of
No 3B Fine Gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation should be provided below the slab as
cushion fill to provide more uniform support of the slab and to serve as a capillary break
To reduce the potential for future subgrade soil heaving due to subsequent
saturation the subgrade soils should be kept continuously moist by water sprinkling prior
to concrete placement However care must be exercised to avoid oversaturation and
softening of the slab subgrade soils Oversaturation and softening of the subgrade soils
could result in a loss of bearing support for the slab
Slab perimeter thickened edges should extend a minimum of 12 inches below the
lowest adjacent exterior grade

Site Grading
Prior to the commencement of any earthwork all areas within the grading limits
should be cleared of all vegetation and deleterious materials The surface layer of organic
topsoil should also be stripped and screened to remove any grass roots or sugar cane
fragments The resulting stripped and processed material should be stockpiled for use
in landscaping and or general yard fill only Any soft weak or yielding areas disclosed
during the clearing operations should be over excavated to firm ground and the resulting
excavation should be backfilled with compacted engineered fill
The drainage gully should be over excavated to a depth where firm non yielding
soils are encountered Soft or wet organic soils should be completely removed The
ditch should be over excavated to a bottom width of 15 feet or to a width which will allow
compaction equipment to provide full compactive effort over the entire surface whichever
is less The sides of the gully should be benched to provide level firm surfaces upon
which the engineered fill is to be placed
All surfaces to receive fill should be scarified to a depth of about six inches 6
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to not less than
90 percent of its maximum dry density as established by ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91
Generally the on site soils except for the topsoil should be suitable for use as
engineered fill If additional imported material is required the material should conform
to the requirements stipulated in Section 30 of the Standard Specifications for Public Works
Construction Select Borrow for Subbase Course dated September 1986
All fills and backfills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding eight inches 8
in loose thickness moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture content and compacted
to a minimum of 90 percent of its maximum dry density as established by ASTM Test
Designation D 1557 91 We understand that paved surfaces are not currently planned for
this project although gravel surfaced driving and parking areas are planned The upper
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3084 00 Page 12
two feet 2 of backfill below subgrade level in driving and or parking areas paved or
unpaved should be compacted to a minimum of 95 percent of its maximum dry density
as established by ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91
The on site soils consist of clayey silt soils therefore mechanical compactive effort
is necessary to achieve the recommended degree of compaction for structural fill Sneepsfoot

Due to the silty nature of the on site soils we recommend that dust control procedures

above Excavations for footings should conform to federal state and local requirements
for excavations

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling four 4 borings to
depths of about 16 to 28 feet below the existing ground surface at the approximate
locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 A truck mounted rig was used to drill the
borings
The materials encountered in the borings were examined and classified in general
accordance with ASTM Standard Practice D 2488 84 Description and Identification of
Soils Visual Manual Procedure by a geologist from our firm The logs of the borings
are shown on Plates A 1 through A 4
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in accordance with ASTM
Standard Method D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by
driving a two inch 2 standard split spoon sampler or a three inch 8 O D split barrel
sampler with a 140 pound hammer free falling 30 inches The blow counts required to
advance the sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the Boring Logs at the approximate
sample depths
Core samples of soil and rock materials encountered at the site were obtained
using diamond core drilling techniques in general accordance with ASTM Standard
Practice D 2113 83 Diamond Core Drilling for Site Investigation Core drilling is a rotary
drilling method which uses a hollow bit to cut into the rock formation The rock material
left in the annulus of the bit is mechanically recovered for examination classification and
testing
Recovery REC is used as a subjective guide to the interpretation of the relative
quality of rock masses Recovery is defined as the actual length of material recovered
from a coring attempt vs the length of the core attempt e g if 3 7 feet of material is
recovered from a 5 0 foot core run the recovery would be 74 percent and would be
shown on the Boring Logs as REC 74
The Rock Quality Designation RQD is also a subjective guide to the relative
quality of rock masses This designation is only applicable to cores retrieved from in situ
rock formations RQD is defined as the percentage of the core run that is sound material
that is in excess of four inches 4 in length without any discontinuities discounting any
drilling induced fractures or breaks If 2 5 feet of sound material is recovered from a
5 0 foot core run the RQD would be 50 percent and would be shown on the Boring Logs
as RQD 50 Generally the following is used to describe the relative quality based on
the Practical Handbook of Physical Properties of Rocks and Minerals

Laboratory Testing
eee www ewe
Laboratory testing was performed on selected samples obtained during our field
exploration A laboratory testing program was developed to schedule appropriate tests
to aid in determining pertinent engineering properties of the soil and or rock materials
sampled
Classification Tests
Frequently the classification of a material provides a correlative index to certain
engineering properties of the material
Moisture Content ASTM Test Method D 2216 80 and Unit Weight Determinations
are used as an aid to soil classification and to aid in determining certain properties such
as relative saturation The moisture content expressed as a percentage of the dry weight
of the tested sample is shown at the appropriate sample depths on the Boring Logs
Atterberg Limits tests ASTM Test Method D 4318 84 are used to determine the
plasticity of the fine grained portion of soils The Plasticity Index of the material is used
to classify the soil under the Unified Soil Classification System The results of these tests
are shown at the appropriate sample depths on the Boring Logs
Physical Property Tests
Tests are performed to develop a correlative index of certain physical parameters
