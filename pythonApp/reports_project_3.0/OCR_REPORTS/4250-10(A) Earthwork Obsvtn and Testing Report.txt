PROJECT CONSIDERATION
The Off Site Parking Lot is located adjacent to the Castle Medical Center at
640 Ulukahiki Street in the Kailua area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general
location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
We understand that the Off Site Parking Lot is part of the Five Year Master Plan for the
Castle Medical Center
The Off Site Parking Lot is located at the northwestern corner of the intersection
between Ulukahiki Street and Kalanianaole Highway The Off Site Parking Lot consists of
approximately 240 parking stalls Based on the grading plan the finished grades at the
parking area ranged from about 114 to 124 feet Mean Sea Level Based on the existing
topography and the finished grades at the parking lot cuts on the order of about 2 feet and
some minor fills were needed to achieve the finished grades As part of the project
underground utility lines were installed within the new parking area and along Ulukahiki
Street In addition a retaining wall was constructed along the northwestern side of the
parking area for grade separation

EARTHWORK OBSERVATION AND TESTING
The site grading operations pavement and utility construction for the Castle
Medical Center Off Site Parking Lot project were performed by Jayar Construction Inc
The site grading consisted of the demolition of existing structures and pavements
excavation of the existing ground subgrade preparation placement and compaction of fills
and backfills foundation excavation utility trench excavation and backfill and pavement
construction
During the site grading operations samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor
and the test results are summarized on Plate 2 of this report
Following the demolition and clearing operations the existing ground was generally
excavated to near the finished subgrades within the limits of the parking area The
finished subgrades were moisture conditioned to near the optimum moisture content and
compacted to a minimum of 95 percent of the maximum dry density as determined in
accordance with American Society of Testing and Material ASTM Test Designation
D 1557 Modified Proctor After excavation for the retaining wall foundation and
preparation of the foundation subgrade the foundation subgrades were observed by a
representative from Geolabs and probed with a steel pointed rod
Periodic field density tests were performed during the subgrade preparation and fill
and backfill operations to confirm our field observations as variations in construction
procedures and soil conditions were noted A total of 38 field density tests including
retests of failed areas were performed using the nuclear probe method in general
accordance with ASTM Test Designations D 2922 and D 3017 In general fill and backfill
materials and the base course material required for the project were generally placed in
near level lifts brought to above the optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of
90 or 95 percent of the maximum dry density determined in accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor
Results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 3 1 through
3 3 of this report These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on
constructed fills and backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by
pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and elevations
should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas
assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was
obtained and or approved by our field representative based on visual observation and
hand probing

LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork may
have been performed without our observation since our services were provided on an
intermittent on call basis It should be recognized that there are variations in the
accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the
earthwork construction and materials Our services were performed in accordance with
generally accepted standards of geotechnical engineering practice no warranty is
