SUMMARY OF RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our borings and laboratory test data the
subsurface conditions consist primarily of 2 5 to 3
feet of surficial fill over 2 to 2 5 feet of very
soft clay Beneath the highly compressible clay
layer are very loose lagoonal silty and gravelly
sand deposits to about 20 feet depth below the
existing ground
We believe that the thin crust and the soft loose
highly compressible deposits cannot provide
adequate direct foundation support for the new
building loads
In order to use footing type foundations for the
proposed two story structure it is our opinion
that the highly compressible clay under the footing
foundations should be completely over excavated
about 5 foot depth and backfilled with compacted
structural fill prior to footing construction
The text of this report should be referred to for
detailed and special design recommendations
INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the proposed two story residence at 746 Hauoli
Street in the Moiliili District of Oahu Hawaii The general location of
the site and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1

This report summarizes the findings of our exploration and our

data obtained during the exploration These recommendations are intended
for design input and are not intended for construction estimation or
bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to demolish the existing one story house at the site
and construct a new two story residence The proposed building will have
CMU walls on the ground floor and wood construction above Anticipated
wall and column loads are 1 5 kips per foot of wall length and 18 kips per
column
Site filling up to about 2 feet high is anticipated at some portions
of the building area in order to attain the City and County Regulatory
Flood Elevation

subsurface conditions at the project site in order to develop a
generalized soil data set for the formulation of geotechnical engineering
recommendations pertinent to the design of the site grading foundation
and pavement of the proposed residence

SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is approximately 50 by 64 feet in plan and located
at the makai side of the junction of Hauoli and Fern streets in Moiliili
Currently it is occupied by a single story wooden residence
Besides two concrete driveways the house is fronted by small turfed
areas between the property lines and the concrete curbs along the two
streets The backyard of the house is covered by concrete slabs and
currently used for storage of miscellaneous materials Along the rear
boundaries of the lot are two short CMU walls about 3 to 4 feet high
The site is generally level with ground elevations ranging from about
3 6 to 5 3 feet above Mean Sea Level

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration generally indicates the presence of a 2 to
3 foot thick layer of highly compressible clay below the thin surficial
fill at the project site Loose lagoonal silty sand with gravel was then
encountered to the depth of about 20 feet where a more competent layer
appears to exist
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2106 00 Page 5
Dk No 501
We believe that the thin crust of fill and the underlying highly
compressible deposits cannot provide direct foundation support for the
proposed building and significant settlements may occur under the new
building loads In order to provide adequate bearing for footing type
foundations it is our opinion that the very soft clay deposit below the
footing locations be completely over excavated and replaced with select
compacted structural fill Otherwise pile foundations should be
considered for the proposed building
Footing Foundation
Based on our foundation analyses we believe that shallow spread or
continuous footing foundations may be used for the proposed structure
However to reduce the potential of excessive footing settlements and
bearing failure we recommend that the footing excavations be extended
down to a minimum depth of about 5 feet below the existing ground surface
to completely remove the soft clay deposit The over excavations should
also be extended a minimm of 1 5 feet beyond the edges of the footing as
shown schematically on Plate 3
The excavations should be lined with a layer of geotextile fabric and
backfilled with 3B Fine gravel to a minimum of 12 inches above the
existing groundwater level Non expansive borrow materials such as
crusher waste or coral should then be placed in 6 to 8 inch horizontal
loose lifts over the 3B Fine gravel and compacted to a minimm of 90 of
its maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78 prior to footing placement
The top i1 foot below the adjacent finish subgrade level should be
backfilled with impervious non expansive soil compacted to the above
specified density Plate 3
Based on this special design and the anticipated design loading we
recommend that an allowable bearing capacity of 1500 p s f be used for
the design of the spread or continuous footings bearing on the compacted
structural fill
GEOLABS HAWAII

The footings should be placed a minimum of 12 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grade Uplift wind or other lateral loading conditions
may further increase the embedment of the footing foundations
For the above special foundation design it is estimated that
foundation settlements may be less than one inch total and one half of an
inch differential
We also recommend that all footings be tied together with grade beams
to improve the rigidity of the entire foundation system hence reducing
the potential differential settlement
Pile Foundation
Because of the adverse subsoil conditions pile foundations were
initially considered However due to the stringent budget limit for our
field exploration the drilled borings were not deep enough to locate a
suitable bearing layer for piles
If pile foundations are to be considered for the project or future
design changes may require pile foundations we recommend that some
contingent fees be made available for a second phase exploration to locate
a suitable bearing layer
Site Grading
Up to about 2 feet of site filling is anticipated to raise the
existing grade of some portions of the site to attain the Regulatory Flood
Elevation Site filling should be minimized since the weight of the new
fill would induce long term ground subsidence
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed Any unsuitable
materials such as old footings old slabs and pavement vegetation and
rubbish should be removed and disposed of properly off site
GEOLABS HAWAII

Dk No 501
The ground surface below the new slabs and or pavements should be
scarified to a minimum depth of 6 inches and recompacted to a minimum of
90 of its maximum dry density as established by ASTM D 1557 78 Soft
pockets encountered in the initial site preparation should be removed and
backfilled with select backfill material
The upper sandy and gravelly fill materials may be reused as a source
of fill and or backfill materials If required new imported fill
material should consist of non expansive select granular materials less
than 3 inches in maximm size with less than 25 passing the 200 sieve
The new fill should be placed in 8 inch maximm horizontal loose lifts and
compacted to 90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78
Site grading should provide drainage of surface runoff away from the
proposed structure
Slab On Grade
It is our understanding that concrete slab on grade is planned for
the proposed building The on grade floor slabs should be placed over a
4 inch cushion layer of gravel size crushed rock material such as 3B
Fine rock to provide uniform support A moisture barrier membrane should
be placed under the slabs for moisture sensitive flooring
The exterior slab thickened edges should be embedded at least 12
inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade
Pavement

Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comments
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this review
is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for
misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Observation
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the site
grading and foundation phases of the work This is to observe compliance
with the design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow
design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from that
anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in
this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed soil conditions encountered during construction are
different from those assumed or considered in this report then
appropriate modifications to the design should be made
LIMITATION
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as borings
Variations of conditions between the field data points may occur and the
nature and extent of these variations may not become evident until
construction If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to
re evaluate the recommendations given in this report

Field Exploration

2 borings to depths of 20 5 to 20 8 feet below the existing ground

textural examination in the field by our engineer who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual observation and testing in the laboratory All soils
were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A summary of the subsoils encountered are
presented in the Boring Logs Plates A 1 and A 2
Soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving a 2 inch
a 140 pound hammer free falling 30 inches or a 3 inch diameter thin wall
tube hydraulically pushed The blow counts to drive the sampler the last
