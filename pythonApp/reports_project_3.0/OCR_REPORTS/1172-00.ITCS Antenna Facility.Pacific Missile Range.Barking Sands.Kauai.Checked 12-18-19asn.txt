INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of a geotechnical engineering inves
tigation performed at the Pacific Missile Range Facility on the island
of Kauai Hawaii The purpose of the investigation was to evaluate
the subsurface conditions at the site to provide soil data for the
foundation design of the proposed ITCS Antenna Facility
The scope of the investigation included the drilling of three borings
field observations at the site laboratory soil testing and engineering
analysis based on the soil data obtained
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project is located on the northwest end of Makaha Ridge in the
Waimea district of the island of Kauai The site is located on the
westernmost edge of the Makaha Ridge branch of the Barking Sands
Pacific Missile Range Facility
The terrain over most of the proposed antenna sites slopes down
moderately to the west at a slope ratio of approximately 3 1 H V
with steeper slopes up to H V in some of the eroded gullies
Ground surface elevations vary from 1468 to 1494 feet Most of the
site is barren and eroded soil with scattered outcrops of highly
weathered rock noted on the ground surface The uppermost portion of
the site is currently occupied by two electronic equipment vans used
by the PMRF station
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
The proposed construction consist of 4 concrete antenna pads for an
TCS Tracker System Each of the pads will be about 20 by 20 feet in
plan dimension The uppermost pad will be placed over the level paved
area near the existing equipment vans The remaining pads will be
stepped down along the sloping hillside
The antenna dish will be about 8 feet in diameter and covered with a
14 foot diameter radome

Cut and fill is required to construct the antenna pads
It is anticipated that retaining walls will be required around some of

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The island of Kauai was built by a single shield volcano roughly
circular in plan view This volcano is composed of the basaltic lavas
of the Waimea Canyon Volcanic Series which were extruded during the
Pliocene
Weathering and erosion have disected the original volcanic dome deeply
leaving high ridges between deep valleys On the northwestern coast
of the island headward erosion by wave action has created a specta
cular series of cliffs at the ends of these ridges The project site
is located at the top of one of these cliffs on Makaha Ridge and is
underlain by residual soils developed from the basaltic lavas of the
Napali Formation of the Waimea Canyon Volcanic Series
Subsurface Exploration
Three borings were drilled at the site to depths of about 20 feet
below the existing ground surface to explore the subsurface conditions
and to obtain samples for laboratory testing The approximate loca
tions of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2

At the time of our field exploration the layout of the 4 antenna pads
indicated that they would be adjacent to each other and extend a total

Soil Conditions
The borings encountered stiff to very stiff residual soil consisting
of reddish brown clayey silt and sandy silt to 13 5 to 20 5 foot depth
over highly weathered basalt
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings during the field
exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that the site is underlain by compe
tent residual soil adequate for foundation support of the proposed
antennae
The existing soils are generally erosive and provisions should be
made to divert surface runoff away from the slopes and provide slope
planting to reduce erosion of the soils

Antenna Foundations
The proposed antennae can be supported on shallow spread footing
foundation bearing on the stiff residual soil or compacted granular
material
An allowable bearing value of 4000 p s f on the stiff residual soil
and 3000 p s f on select granular fill material compacted to a
minimum of 95 of its maximum dry density may be used
The footings should be embedded a minimum of 24 inches below the
finished grade
To resist lateral loads a coefficient of friction of 0 4 between the
foundation and soil may be used Additionally a passive soil resis
tance of 300 p c f may be utilized
Retaining Wall Foundations
Retaining wall foundations should bear on the stiff residual soil An
allowable bearing value of 4000 p s f may be used for wall footings
embedded a minimum of 24 inches below the finished grade
The walls should be backfilled with well graded granular material
such as select borrow or base course This would reduce the com
pactive effort required and the build up of additional lateral
pressures
GEOLABS HAWAIi
W O 1172 00 Page 6
A subsurface drainage system should be used to prevent the build up of
hydrostatic pressure behind the walls
The walls should be designed to resist active lateral earth pressure
of 35 p c f equivalent fluid pressure
A friction factor of 0 4 between the footing and stiff residual soil
may be utilized to determine the sliding resistance of the walls
Additionally a passive soil resistance of 300 p c f can be utilized
Site Grading
Prior to filling the site should be cleared of loose boulders and
vegetation
Due to the relatively steep sloping ground over most of the site it
is critical that proper keying and benching of the existing ground be
performed prior to filling This operation is essential to provide
stability of the new fill A sketch of the recommended keying and
benching procedures is provided on Plate 3
After the above specified site preparation the fill material should
be properly moisture conditioned and placed in 9 inch maximum lifts
compacted to obtain a minimum of 90 for on site cohesive soil or
95 for imported granular material of its maximum dry density
established by ASTM D 1557 78

The on site soils may be used as a source of general i11l around the
antenna pads
For the following reasons granular material such as select borrow or
base course is recommended as the backfill and fill materials behind
the retaining walls and under the antenna pads
1 The granular material would provide better bearing for the
antennae
2 It would be difficult to properly compact the on site soil due
to the limited space behind the walls
3 The on site fine grained soil would require substantially more
compactive effort than the granular material resulting in
increased lateral pressure on the walls
4 The granular material would minimize the potential differential
settlement between the portions of the pads on cut and fill
Two horizontal to one vertical 2 1 cut and fill slopes are
recommended
Temporary cut slopes for the retaining wall construction may be made
vertical for periods of less than about 2 weeks
Surface water should be diverted away from the top of the slopes and
slope planting utilized as soon as practicable to reduce erosion

Design Review
Plans for foundation and site grading should be forwarded to the soils
engineer for review and written comments prior to construction to see
that the intent of our recommendations is included in the project
plans and specifications
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during earthwork
construction to monitor the contractor s grading operations
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be observed by the
soils engineer during construction to see that adequate bearing has
been obtained and the subsurface conditions are consistent with the
findings of our subsurface investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the above
inspections

Field Exploration

The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by
drilling 1 to 21 5 feet below the existing ground
surface and 2 deep borings 198 to 199 5 feet at the approximate
locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 Trailer mounted
equipment was used to drill the borings
The soils encountered in the borings were classified
by visual and textural examination in the field by our geologist
who continously monitored the drilling operations The classi
fications were further reviewed by visual inspection and testing
in the laboratory The soils were classified in general
conformance with the Unified Soil Classification System A
graphic presentation of the materials encountered is presented
on the Log of Borings Plates A 1 through A 3
Soil samples were obtained from the boring by driving
a standard penetration sampler or a 2 4 inch I D split barrel
sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow
counts to drive the sampler 12 inches are shown on the log of
borings at the appropriate sample depths
