performed for the proposed revised residential development at
Club View Heights Subdivision on Hui Iwa Street Kahaluu Oahu

and findings in this report incorporated a review and analysis
of previous soil data and an additional study performed to up

The site was at one time planned for mid rise apartment buildings
and multi level parking structures It was consequently rough
graded and piles were driven for such prior to the recent change
into a residential development project The purpose of our study
is to develop site grading recommendations for the new single
family residential development
SCOPE
The scope of this study is for our firm to review the above referenced
reports along with additional investigation work performed at this
time to develop a new soils tnvestigation report that will reflect
Club ViewHetghts Subdivision 3
changes involved under the new plan for single family housing
This work was performed in accordance with our proposal dated
Our study was based on an advanced Site Grading Plan with lot
configuration as built grading and proposed grading prepared
by Gray Rhee Associates Inc all the afore mentioned reports
and additional soils investigation and testing
The previous site grading operation to develop the as built con
ditions of the original apartments development was not under the
observation of this firm The current study assumes that all
the as built fill was placed in compacted layers
PLANNED CONSTRUCTION
The revised development plan is to grade the existing site into
33 single family residential lots with the necessary utility and
roadway improvements
Site grading will involve mainly filling the gully and the low
lying areas Fill up to 10 feet high will be required in the
northwest gullies In the lowlying area along the northern pro
perty line the average fill will amount to about 6 feet high
For the Hui Iwa Street frontage lots the houses will be of split
level construction Cuts behind the retaining wall will be 8 feet
maximum
Club View Heights Subdivision 4
Other site improvement will include a new storm drain constructed
to join the existing storm drain outlet from Hui Palila Place to
the storm drain system under the proposed access roadway from Hui
Iwa Street The existing masonry storm drainage ditch in the western
lowlying area will be abandoned
SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is located south of the Club View Gardens Recrea
tion Center complex on Hui Iwa Street Kahaluu Hawaii Steep
slopes run along the south and west boundaries Present site ele
vations vary from elevation of about 200 feet in the south west down
to 120 feet in the lowlying northern area
At present the site had been partially graded for the previously
planned mid rise apartment and multi level parking structures
The southern central area has been graded into level building pads
terraced with about 4 to 8 feet of grade differences
The northern lowlying portion of this site was left unfinished and
overgrown with thick California grass Large recent stockpile of
loose soil up to 9 feet high occupies a portion of this lowlying
area
The rubble masonry drainage channel is located in the north west
area of the site at the mouth of a natural gully that runs down
from the south west The channel runs in a northerly direction
from the middie of the site It is 18 feet wide at the top 7 feet
wide on its floor and about 3 feet high The channel is filled with
runoff deposits and it is thickly vegetated
Club View Heights Subdivision 5
The gully which feeds this channel is the largest of the two
found in this area A smaller gully is located west of the
larger gully and joins the larger gully at the northern boun
dary The slope ratio of the gully is about one horizontal to
one vertical 1 1 with banks up to 30 feet in vertical height
Slopes up to 40 feet high run along the southern boundary This
sloping area shows signs of grading work performed during previous
construction Slope ratios in this area are two horizontal to one
vertical 2 1 or flatter No evidence of instability was noted on
this slope area during this investigation
Thick growth of California grass and small bushes grow along the
lowlying northern boundary and the west gully areas Little or
no vegetation is present on the previously graded construction
areas Driven in place concrete piles were noted at the central
portion of the project site These piles were remnant of previous
foundation work intended to be used to support the original y mid
rise apartment structures

Geology
The proposed development is located at the foot of the basalt
Koolau Mountain Range The site is underlain by the older allu
vium geologic formation
After the formation of the volcanic Koolau Mountain Range from
lava flows an extended period of weathering and erosion produced
alluvial and colluvial deposits of clay boulders sand and silt
at the foot of the range
A now buried stream bed or swamp is found in the lower portion of
the site
Soils
From past report information the site generally had an upper sur
face soil consisting of 5 to 20 feet of stiff brown silty clay to
clayey silt Beneaththis surface layer was a soft to medium stiff
mottled brown and gray clayey silt varying in thickness from appro
ximately 8 to 30 feet A very stiff to hard clayey silt stratum
underlies the soft layer
Club View Heights Subdivision 7
Upon comparison of the existing contours and former topographic
survey map it seems that the upper southern terraced areas had
been cut 10 feet below the previous elevations recorded at the
time of the previous soil reports This means that stiff brown
silty clay crust became a thinner layer and that the soft to
medium stiff clayey silt could be found closer of even at the
existing surface
In the northern lowlying area a surface layer of soft organic
silt in a very moist and soft condition was found in our recent
additional and A2 of this report This soft
material indicates the underlying layer is a former stread bed
or swamp formation
Along the gully boring no A3 indicated a 6 foot thick of medium
to stiff brown silty clay surface crust This surface layer is
similar to that encountered in the previous borings drilled in the
central area Underlying this stiff surface crust are soft mottled
brown and gray silty clay layers which are about 17 feet thick in
boring no A3
Groundwater
Based on the information obtained from this new and the previous
soil investigations groundwater could be encountered at about
elevation 100 to 115 in the northern lowerlying areas
Groundwater was found at higher elevations in the western gully and
the southern slope areas of the project site The groundwater levels
encountered at the higher areas varied a great deal in elevations
Club View Heights Subdivision 8
The variable groundwater condition may be due to perched water
jin the soil layers at different locations and elevations
DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The lowlying portion of the site and the gully areas are underlain
with thick soft alluvial stream deposits The proposed fill within
these areas will initiate consolidation of the underlying soft de
posits Site filling in these areas should be initiate as soon as
feasible
To shorten the consolidation waiting period and to minimize dif
ferential settlement at these areas a 5 foot surcharge fill is
recommended The approximate surcharge area is outlined in the
Site Plan
No settlement waiting will be required in the upper areas where
the new site filling will be less than 3 feet and in portion of
the new stockpile area where the height of the existing stockpile
is about 5 feet above the proposed finish grade
Site Grading
Before the start of construction the site should be cleared of
all vegetations and debris
The upper six inches of the existing ground surface in areas to
receive fill should be scarified and brought to near optimum mois
ture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry
density established by ASTM D1557 compaction test method
Club View Heights Subdivision
Because of the wet climate and expansive soil conditions at the
Kahaluu area it will be difficult to attain 90 compaction as
required for general fill in the areas An 85 compaction of the
on site soil will be acceptable for the light building loads anti
cipated
Where the slope ratio is greater than 5 horizontal to 1 vertical
5 1 fill over existing slope should start at the bottom Benching
and keying are required to properly bond the new fill to the slope
Additional fill should be placed in 9 inch horizontal lifts and com
pacted to the above specified density
If off site fill material is used the soils should be low to non
expansive material Based on our laboratory test results the on
site stockpile soil is highly expansive It is recommended that
the soil should be used in deep fill area where it can be buried
at an elevation of two or more feet below the proposed finish grade
The remaining portion of the fill can be capped off with other on
site cut soils or off site borrow of a low to non expansive soil
The recent loose fill which stockpiled at the lower portion of the
site should be removed down to the original ground elevation before
reconstructing it back in compacted layers
Slope
Two horizontal to one vertical 2 1 fill and cut slopes as pro
posed by present site grading plans are adequate Based on the soil
Club View Heights Subdivision 10
condition cut slopes up to 1 horizontal to 1 vertical may be
constructed However during the grading operatton the 1 1

correlation between the soil data obtained during the investigation
and the actual soil conditions encountered during construction
To mintmize erosion all slopes should be planted as soon as
practicable
Settlements
Based upon the information obtained from the previous soil inves
tigations and our laboratory testing of the soil samples obtained
from boring no Al of this present investigation it is estimated
that the primary consolidation at the lowlying and gully areas will
settle approximately 2 inches or more under the proposed 5 to 6 feet
of fill
It is estimated that without surcharging for 5 to 6 feet of fill
approximately 3 or more years will be required to obtain a 90
consolidation level The remaining settlement would occur over
an extensive period of several years
Settlement will be negligible at the upper area where the fill is
less than 3 feet high and at the recent fill stockpile area where
the height of the stockpile is 5 or more feet above its proposed
grade
Club View Heights Subdivision 11
Settlement Gauge
It is recommended that a total of three settlement gauges be in
stalled at the two gullies and the lowlying portion of the site
to monitor the ground settlements The surveying of the settle
ment gauges can be performed by the project surveyor in cooperation
with the civil engineer
The fill operations should be started upon completion of the settle
ment gauge installation
Surcharge
To minimize future long term and differential settlements additional
surcharge fill is recommended over the proposed fill underlain with
soft stream and alluvial deposits This surcharge area will generally
occupy the lowlying and gully areas where the proposed grade is 3
or more feet higher than the original ground surface The approximate
limits of surcharge area is outlined on the Site Plan
If a total of 5 feet of surcharge fill were placed sufficient con
solidation would occur in a shorter period of time We estimated
that with the surcharge over the fill the 90 consolidation will
be reduced from about 3 or more years to about 12 months
The information obtained from the settlement gauge monitoring program
will establish the time when the surcharge fill can be removed after
sufficient pre consolidation has taken place The construction of
the site roadway and utility improvements can then begin immediately
after the removal of the surcharge fill
Club View Heights Subdivision 12
Foundation
Spread and line footing foundation may be used for the proposed
single family housing construction Foundation bearing on the
in situ soil or fill can be designed for an allowable bearing
pressure of 1 500 pounds per square foot Because the on site
soil is moderately expansive the bottom of the footing should
be a minimum of 18 inches below the finished ground surface
At the area where the concrete piles have been driven if the
footing or slab foundation is located partly on top of the pile
and partly on soil the top section of the pile should be cut to
allow a clearance of a minimum of 3 feet below the bottom of the
footing or the slab
It is estimated that total settlement for the building structure
should be less than 3 4 inch with a differential settlement of
less than 1 2 inch
Retaining Wall
Retaining walls should be designed to receive active lateral pres
sure equivalent to a fluid density of 45 pounds per cubic foot if
on site clayey MH soils are used as backfill Additional lateral
forces on the retatning wall should be considered according to the
anticipated live and dead load from above A friction factor of
0 35 can be utilized to determine sliding resistance of the retaining
wall foundation Weepholes or subsurface drainage system should be
incorporated to prevent the build up of excessive hydrostatic pres
sure behind the wall
Club View Heights Subdivision 13
Slab On Grade
Due to on site soil expansion slab on grade should be designed
for medium expansive potential soil condition
Concrete floor slab should be reinforced with a minimum of 6 x 6
10 x 10 welded wire fabric and underlain by a 4 inch thick base
course rock layer Slabs which will be provided with a floor
covering should be protected by a polyethylene vapor barrier In
addition deep perimeter footings are required which will act as
a cut off wall against the subgrade moisture changes due to drying
and wetting around the slab The slab subgrade soils should be
protected from drying prior to concreting
For footing and slab design details in these medium expansive soil
conditions refer to plates 2 and 3
Roadway Pavement
Based on the characteristic of the on site soils the following
pavement section may be used for design and cost estimate purposes

with a CBR of 85 and conforming with section 31 2 Standard Speci

select borrow should have a minimum CBR value of 25 and should con
sist of crusher run quarry waste mudrock coral sand or cinder ma
terial Both the base course and select borrow should be compacted

Additional CBR tests should be performed on the actual subgrade

Utility Line
Due to the moisture sensitivity of the on site clay soils it is
recommended that flexible joints be used on all utility pipes
Utility constructions in the gully and the lowlying areas should
be delayed until the surcharge fill is removed or the ground has
settled within a tolerable limit
Design Review
The plans for foundations lot grading and utility plans should
be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and comments prior
to construction to assure that the intent of our recommendations
is included in the project plans and specifications
Construction Observation
To a degree the performance of any structure is dependent upon
construction procedures and quality Monitoring of surcharge and
observation of fill placement cut stopes and foundation excavations
should be carried out by the soils engineer to permit correlation
between the soil data obtained during this investigation and the
actual soil conditions encountered during construction in order to
minimize misunderstanding of the field forces by both the letter
and spirit of our report as well as to note any subsurface condi
