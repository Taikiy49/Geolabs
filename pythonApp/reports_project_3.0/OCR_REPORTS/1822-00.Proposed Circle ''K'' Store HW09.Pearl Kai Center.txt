INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of a geotechnical engineering
evaluation performed for the proposed Circle K Store HWO9 in Pearl Kai
Center at Aiea Oahu Hawaii The general location of the site and its
vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a convenience facility at this site
The facility will consist of a single story 40 by 67 foot store and a
pump island with three gasoline dispensers To supply the gas island an
underground gasoline storage area consisting of three 10 000 gallon tanks
is planned for a 20 by 30 foot area adjacent to the Circle K store
The convenience facility will be surrounded by an asphaltic concrete
parking lot It appears that 1 to 2 feet of additional fill may be
required to raise the site
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our study was to explore the subsurface conditions at
the site to obtain up dated soil data to evaluate the effect of the
recent site filling on the foundation design of the proposed structures
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests were used
in the engineering analysis to formulate our conclusions and
recommendations These results are presented in the appendices of this
report
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located across Kamehameha Highway from the
existing Pearl Ridge Shopping Center in Aiea on the island of Oahu
Hawaii The Circle K convenience facility will be located near the
center of the Pearl Kai complex

SUBSURFACE CONDITION
Geology
The geologic conditions underlying the site are the results of a
complex series of depositional and erosional events brought on by
glacioeustatic sea level changes and post erosional volcanic activities
during the Pleistocene Following the cessation of activity of the
Koolau Volcano alluvial materials were deposited in a deltaic or
estuarine environment in the area of Pearl Harbor
The sea level fluctuated many times receding perhaps as much as 300
feet below and rising up to 95 feet above the present day level
Concurrent with a rise in sea level during the late Pleistocene era
eruption of the Salt Lake Makalapa member of the Honolulu Volcanic series
mantled the Older Alluvium with thick deposits of volcanic ash which
consolidated to form volcanic tuff locally called mudrock layers
Subsequent erosion dissected and thinned those mantles of tuff During
the Recent epoch as the sea level rose to its current stand the area
was covered with lagoonal marine deposits

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Store Building and Pump Island
The soil boring and penetrometer test boring encountered soft
compressible soils underlying a 7 to 8 foot thick crust of dense fill
The compacted clayey silt with sand fill crust can provide adequate
foundation bearing support for the proposed lightly loaded Circle Kk
store building and pump island utilizing spread or continuous wall
footings
The recommended minimm footing width is 12 inches with a minimum
embedment of 18 inches An allowable bearing value up to 3000 p s f may
be used The bearing capacity is for total dead plus live loads and may
be increased by one third for temporary wind and seismic loads
Minimal settlements are anticipated due to the light structural
loads
Future decline of the regional artesian head can result in
additional stress on the compressible soil and corresponding settlement
However we anticipate that this would occur as a regional settlement
and therefore may not induce differential settlement to the structure
Slab On Grade
Concrete slab on grade is anticipated for the proposed structure
It is recommended that the slab on grade be placed over a minimum 12 inch
thick layer of non expansive fill To reduce the soil swelling
potential the subgrade soils should be wetted kept moist and not
allowed to dry out from the start of construction until the placement of
concrete This would require daily sprinkling of the subgrade soils

Underground Storage Tanks
It is planned to place three 10 000 gallon tanks underground in a 20
by 30 foot area adjacent to the Circle K Store It is estimated that
each tank will weight approximately when filled with
gasoline
There is presently a 7 to 8 foot fill crust and the tank diameters
as indicated on the Site Plan are 8 feet The tanks will therefore
bear on the underlying soft soils which have low bearing capacity
Therefore a stabilization pad may be required below the tanks
The stabilization pad may consist of a 1 foot thick layer of 3B
Fine gravel wrapped in geotextile The separating geotextile will retain
the gravel and prevent it from pushing into the soft soil
A schematic of the recommended gravel and geotextile pad is
presented on Plate 3
Pavement and Parking Areas
Based on the results of our field study the preliminary pavement
design is as follows
2 Inch Asphalt Concrete
6 Inch Base Course
6 Inch Select Borrow Subbase
14 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
CBR tests should be performed on the actual subgrade soil samples
during construction and the above design section can be revised
accordingly The select borrow layer may be eliminated if select
imported fill is used below the base course layer
Lot Grading
The fills within the top 12 inches minimum below the on grade slab
should consist of non expansive soil

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
pe a a HH EM
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and
sampling one borings to depth of 50 5 feet below the existing ground
surface A truck mounted drill rig was used to drill the boring In
addition to the boring one dutch cone penetrometer test was performed
The approximate locations of the soil boring and dutch cone penetration
test are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving a standard
penetration sampler or a 2 4 inch I D split barrel sampler with a
140 pound hammer free falling a distance of 30 inches The blow counts
to drive the sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the boring logs at
the appropriate sample depths Hydraulically pushed thin wall shelby
tube samples were taken at regular intervals in the softer materials
Materials encountered in the field exploration were classified by
visual and textural examination in the field by our engineers The
classifications were further reviewed by visual inspection and testing in
the laboratory All soils were classified in general conformance with
the Unified Soil Classification System A graphic presentation of the
materials encountered is presented on the Log of Boring Plates A 1 1 and
A 1 2
During the drilling and sampling operation two 2 field vane shear
tests were performed The field vane shear tests were performed by using
a vane with 2 5 inch diameter and 5 inch length The vane was first
pushed into the soil and then rotated The change of the measured torque
with angular rotation was monitored Maximim torques of both the
undisturbed and remolded soil condition were recorded The test results
are summarized and presented on the Log of Boring Plates A 1 1 and
An 1 2
The cone penetration test was conducted to a depth of 58 feet below
the existing ground surface
The cone penetrometer test consists of hydraulically pushing a steel
cone with an apex angle of 60 degrees and an area of 10 square
centimeters connected to a friction sleeve with an area of 150 square
centimeters into the ground The tip and side friction resistance
induced during penetration are measured by strain gages within the
instrument and are recorded versus the depth of penetration The values
of the tip resistance and friction ratio sleeve friction over tip
resistance can be correlated to soil strength and classification
The record of the cone penetrometer test and the interpretation is
shown on Plate A 2

Laboratory Testing
oH UN RE
The moisture content unit weight and Atterberg Limit determinations
were performed on selected samples as an aid in the classification and
evaluation of soil properties Torvane tests were also performed on both
of the shelby tube samples in order to determine their undrained shear
strength The results of the moisture content Atterberg Limit and
torvane shear are presented on the Log of Boring at the appropriate
sample depths
Three 3 consolidation tests were performed on undisturbed
fine grained samples to evaluate the compressibility and settlement
characteristics During most of the tests settlement time readings were
recorded in order to determine the rate of consolidation Some loadings
were allowed to act on the soil sample for a prolonged period of time in
order to study the effect of secondary consolidation The results of the
load settlement curves are presented on Plates B 1 through B 3
Two 2 sets of total number 6 direct shear tests were carried out
on the soft soils to evaluate its shear strength characteristics The
results of the tests are presented on Plates B 4 and B 5
