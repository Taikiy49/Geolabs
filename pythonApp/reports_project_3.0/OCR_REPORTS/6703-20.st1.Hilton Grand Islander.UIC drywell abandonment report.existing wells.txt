As summarized above a total of eight existing wells were abandoned under this
permit Prior to backfilling Well Nos 5 through 10 were initially cleaned using a clean out
bucket attached to a drill rig and or an excavator The bottoms of the wells were
excavated until native soil was encountered After cleaning the wells were backfilled with
2 500 psi sand cement slurry from Hawaiian Cement on July 24 and 25 2014 The
sand cement grout was placed to about 1 to 2 feet below the finish ground elevation Fill
materials consisting of silty sands and gravel was used to backfill above the poured grout
to finish grade
Because the six wells were interconnected it appeared that the sand cement grout
placed in Well and 9 on partially migrated to Well and
10 Therefore the volume of grout placed in Well and 9 on
appear to be much more than the volume of grout placed in Well and 10 on
In lieu of grouting the wells the contractor cleaned and removed the entire well
structure for Well Nos 3 and 17 After cleaning the wells using an excavator the
reinforced concrete rings of the existing wells were removed The excavation was
backfilled with 3B Fine gravel to about 1 foot below the finish grades Fill materials
consisting of select borrow subbase or silty sands and gravel was used to backfill above
the 3B Fine gravel
An UIC Abandonment of Injection Well Summary Report is attached Photographs
of the wells before and during backfilling are provided with this letter In addition copies of
the grout purchase order receipts are attached
