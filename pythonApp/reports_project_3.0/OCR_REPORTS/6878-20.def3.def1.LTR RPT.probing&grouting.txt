This letter report summarizes our observations of the probing and grouting 
operations at the above project site between December 3 and December 7, 2013.  
Probing and Grouting Operations 
Swinerton Builders, Inc. performed the probing and grouting operations in general 
conformance with the recommendations presented in the referenced geotechnical 
engineering report.  The purpose of the probing and grouting operation was to reduce the 
potential for loss of foundation support resulting from collapse of cavities or compression 
of loose zones below selected educational structures currently under construction at the 
site. 
The layout of the probes was done by Swinerton based on their interpretation of the 
project plans and our geotechnical engineering report.  In general, the probe holes were 
located at the centerpoint of individual spread footings. 
The approximate probe hole locations are shown on the Probe Hole Location 
Plans, Plates 2.1 through 2.4. In general, the probe holes were drilled to a depth 
equivalent to about 10 feet below the bottom of foundation grade. 
The probe holes, about 6 inches in diameter, were drilled using 6-inch diameter 
continuous flight augers.  After drilling of the probe holes, temporary pipes (2-inch PVC) 
were inserted into the holes to keep the holes open prior to the grouting operations.  A

The probe holes were backfilled with a sand-cement slurry grout.  Each probe hole 
was grouted from the drilled depth upward by low pressure injection of grout. Our engineer 
observed the grouting operation and recorded the approximate grout take.  Grout 
quantities were estimated using the number of strokes to fill each probe hole.  
The probing and grouting operations are summarized on Plate A. The results are 
tabulated on Plates A-1 through A-3.4. 
In summary, the foundation probing and grouting operations have been conducted 
in general conformance with the recommendations presented in the referenced 
geotechnical engineering report. We did not observe significant voids or cavities during 
the probing and grouting operation. 
Limitations 
It should be noted that it is not possible to detect and remediate all cavities and/or 
voids present beneath the buildings.  Therefore, some of the cavities and/or voids may not 
have been detected even with the probing and grouting program undertaken for the 
project.  However, the level of foundation preparation undertaken for the structures and the 
subgrade bearing on the on-site coral formation appears to be reasonable in reducing the 
geotechnical risks to the project.  Performing probing and grouting of the foundations 
bearing on the on-site materials provides the owner with a higher degree of assurance that 
substantial cavities and/or voids are not located directly below the buildings. 
Our services were performed in accordance with generally accepted standards of 
geotechnical engineering practice; no warranty is expressed or implied.  It should be noted 
that provision of construction observation by an engineer is not insurance, nor does it 
guarantee construction of any type.
