PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed llalo Street Improvements project is located in the Kaka ako area
of Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the
project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 We understand that the
proposed llalo Street Improvements project is part of a master plan development of the
Kaka ako area known as Kaka ako Makai
Based on available information the proposed project will consist of various roadway
and infrastructure improvements along the existing Ilalo Street from Ahui Street to South
Street This project has been designated as Improvement District 9 ID 9 The project
will eventually connect the future Ward Avenue Extension and Punchbowl Street Extension
projects at Ahui Street and South Street intersections respectively Based on the available
plans numerous utility lines such as water sewer and drain lines are currently present
along the existing llalo Street We understand that a new box culvert and water sewer
and drain lines will be installed for this project
Based on available information relatively deep trench excavations of up to about
20 feet deep will be required for the installation of the various utility lines planned at the
site Due to the close proximity of the project site to the Pacific Ocean and relatively
shallow groundwater elevations at the project site dewatering of the proposed excavations
will be necessary during construction of the utility lines Because excavation dewatering
will likely involve the discharge of groundwater into an adjacent storm drainage system a
National Pollutant Discharge Elimination System NPDES permit will be required

SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is located in the Kaka ako area of Honolulu on the Island of
Oahu Hawaii The Ilalo Street Improvements project will connect the future Ward
Avenue Extension and Punchbowl Street Extension projects as shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2
The project site is located within a generally commercial industrial area with a variety
of buildings and warehouses along both sides of the road The existing llalo Street is a
two lane roadway We understand that the project will involve widening of the Ilalo Street
right of way to about 94 feet in width Therefore the new roadway alignment will encroach
into the existing properties generally on the makai side of the roadway Demolition of some
existing buildings and or warehouses is anticipated Several large trees such as banyan
monkey pod and coconut trees are also present along the sides of the existing road The
trees may be as large as 4 feet in diameter and 50 feet in height
In general the site is relatively level with the existing ground elevations ranging
from approximately 3 to 7 feet Mean Sea Level MSL
MONITORING WELL INSTALLATION
The drilling well installation and well development tasks were conducted on
The groundwater monitoring well consisted of 2 inch diameter Schedule
40 PVC pipe installed to a depth of about 20 feet below the existing ground surface The
well generally consisted of about 15 feet of screen with 0 020 inch slot opening and 5 feet
of solid casing The surface completion consisted of an 8 inch well cover that was
cemented flush with the existing ground surface
Drilling of the monitoring well was accomplished by using a truck mounted drill rig
advancing 6 inch outside diameter OD hollow stem augers The hollow stem augers
and associated drilling and sampling tools were steam cleaned prior to drilling of the
borehole

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The monitoring well was installed near the proposed utility line alignment for the
llalo Street Improvements Subsurface materials were examined and classified by an
engineer from our firm Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained in general
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 3550 84 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of
Soils by driving a 3 inch OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer
falling 30 inches In addition some samples were obtained from the drilled borings in
general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split
Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the
same hammer and drop The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the second and
third 6 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown as the Penetration Resistance on the
Logs of Boring at the appropriate sample depths
In general the boring designated as Boring No 102 encountered about 5 feet of
surface fill materials underlain by deposits of lagoonal sediments The lagoonal deposits
consisted of silty sands and sandy silts and extended to approximately 50 feet below
the existing ground surface The lagoonal sediments ranged from very soft loose to very
stiffidense The lagoonal sediments were underlain by dense silty coralline gravel
coralline detritus extending to the maximum depth of about 53 feet below the existing
ground surface
The groundwater level in Boring No 102 was measured at a depth of about
4 4 feet below ground level on It should be noted that groundwater levels
are expected to vary with seasonal rainfall tidal fluctuations and time of year Detailed
description of the soil materials encountered in the drilled boring is presented on the Log
of Boring Plate 5
GROUNDWATER CONDITIONS
Relatively shallow groundwater conditions were measured at the project site
Due to the relatively close proximity of the project site to the Pacific Ocean it is likely
that groundwater levels measured in the monitoring well will be affected by tidal fluctuation
of the sea level In addition the groundwater levels at the project site may be influenced
by seasonal precipitation and storm surge conditions
The groundwater samples were collected by an engineer from our office and
submitted to Environmental Laboratory of the Pacific EL Pacific for testing Generally
the groundwater sample was obtained from the monitoring well using a disposable bailer
In order to obtain an adequate amount of groundwater for testing about three bailer full
of groundwater was sampled and collected into containers provided by EL Pacific The

Pacific within 2 hours
Based on our review of the analytical laboratory results it appears that no
significant adverse water quality conditions were detected in the analyses of the water
samples that were tested The water sample test results are provided in the Appendix
and include the Department of Health NPDES NOI Form A and the official laboratory
report
Most of the test results for the NOI Form A Part A Water Quality Constituents
appear to be within normal values for typical brackish groundwater For the NOI Form A
Part B Toxic Constituents cadmium lead benzene toluene ethyl benzene xylene and
hydrocarbons were tested The test results except hydrocarbons were reported as
not detected which means the sample results were below the reporting limit for the
particular test method and analyte
The reporting limit is dependent on the testing equipment and the statistical control
applied by the laboratory to the test results Therefore laboratories adopting the same
standard test method may have different reporting limits
