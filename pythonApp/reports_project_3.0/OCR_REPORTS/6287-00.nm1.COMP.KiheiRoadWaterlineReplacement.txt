This report summarizes our testing services performed during site utility installation for
the Kihei Road Waterline Replacement Waiakoa Stream to Ohukai Road project from

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is in the Northern Kihei area on the Island of Maui Hawaii The project
involves the installation of a new 12 inch waterline main for a distance of approximately
3 177 linear feet service laterals and fire hydrants The 12 inch waterline main was installed at
the typical depth of 3 foot minimum cover from the top of the existing pavement elevation with
the exception for the siphon locations where the waterline was encased in concrete jackets
and routed under existing structures
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services pertaining
to the site grading operations and infrastructure installation for the project site
SITE GRADING AND UTILITY INSTALLATION
Geolabs performed field density testing services on the site utility installation work from
January 19 to The site utility installation work consisted of excavating the
existing asphalt pavement by conventional excavator methods the placement of pipe bedding
material placement of the 12 inch ductile iron pipe with poly wrap which was followed by the

placement and compaction of fills The fill and backfill materials consisted of both the native
on site soil and imported base course material Goodfellow Brothers Inc performed the site
utility installation work In addition several siphon locations were added to the project to
address unforeseen site conditions At these siphon locations the 12 inch waterline sections
were precast into the siphons to suit the individual site requirements and were installed at
depths exceeding 5 feet below the water table 3F crushed gravel ASTM C 33 No 67
gradation was placed around the siphons and filled to an elevation that was approximately
1 5 feet above the water table Then standard backfill operations continued to finish grades
During the site grading operations we obtained samples of the on site and imported
materials utilized as fill and backfill materials and transported them to our laboratory for testing
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory
in general accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test results are
summarized on Plate 3
The fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to the optimum moisture
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as
determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557 for elevations below 2 feet from finish grade The
fill and backfill materials within 2 feet of the finished grade were compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative density
During the site utility backfilling operations periodic field density tests were performed in
general accordance with ASTM D 6938 Nuclear Gauge Method Results of the compaction
tests are summarized on the attached Plates 4 1 through 4 12 These tests were performed on
the constructed fills and backfills at the site within the excavated trenches Test locations and
elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes These
locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the
methods used Areas assessed as unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum
compaction was obtained and or approved by our field representative based on visual
observation and hand probing
Our services consisted of on call field testing of the contractor s fill placement subgrade
preparation and compaction procedures and confirmation of subgrade soil conditions exposed
during the above grading operations
