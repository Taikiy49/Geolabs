INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of a geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed new retaining walls at Rothschild residence located in Lot
No 61 of the Maunalua Bay View Lots Subdivision on the island of Oahu Hawaii The
general location of the site and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summaries our findings and geotechnical engineering recommendations
resulting from the field exploration laboratory testing and engineering analyses These
GEOLABS HAWAII

recommendations are intended for retaining wall foundation design input and may not be
appropriate to form the preparation of construction cost estimates or for construction
bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located at the southeast corner of Moloaa Street and Hanapepe
Loop in the district of Maunalua on the island of Oahu As part of the residential
improvement it is proposed to construct new retaining walls up to eight 8 feet in height
Due to the wall height limit walls along the northwest corner of the site will consist of a
dual tier retaining walls It is our understanding that the lower wall shall consist of RC
retaining wall and CRM retaining wall will form the upper wall
The location of the retaining walls are shown on Plate 2

Hawaii Kai area on the island of Oahu Hawaii The lot is currently occupied by a single
family residence house with a swimming pool
The site is bounded on the north by Moloaa Street and Hanapepe Loop on the
west On the east and south the site is bounded by adjacent lot properties

Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork the project area within the contract grading limits
should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed All existing vegetation debris rubbish
and other unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of properly off
site
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2976 00 Page 8
After clearing and grubbing areas at grade or designated to receive fill should be
scarified to a depth of at least six 6 inches and recompacted to a minimum of
90 of the maximum dry density as determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
The footing subgrade should be compacted to a minimum of 95 of the maximum
dry density determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Soft loose spots encountered at foundation subgrade levels should be removed
and replaced with select on site or imported material compacted to a minimum of
95 maximum dry density
Fill and Backfill
The excavated on site granular soils excluding silts clays and organic materials
may be re used as a source of fill and backfill materials
If required new imported materials should consist of non expansive granular
materials less than 3 inches in maximum size with less than 20 passing the 200
sieve Fill and backfill materials should be placed in maximum 9 inch thick loose
horizontal lifts and compacted to a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density
determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by the drilling and sampling
of three 3 borings to depths of about 14 5 feet below the existing ground surface The
borings were drilled by a portable drill rig advancing continuous flight augers or rotary
core drilling tools as appropriate The iogs of the borings are shown on Plate A 1 through
A 3
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Method D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by
sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the boring logs at the appropriate sample
depths
The soil materials encountered in the borings were examined and classified in
general accordance with ASTM Standard Practice D 2488 84 Description and
identification of Soils Visual Manual Procedure by a geologist or an engineer from our
firm
Core samples of rock materials encountered at the site were obtained using
diamond core drilling techniques in general accordance with ASTM Standard Practice D
2113 83 Diamond Core Drilling for Site investigation Core drilling is a rotary drilling
method which uses a hollow bit to cut into the rock formation The rock material left in
the hollow core of the bit is mechanically recovered for examination description and
evaluation
Recovery REC is used as a subjective guide to the interpretation of the relative
quality of rock masses Recovery is defined as the actual length of material recovered
from a coring attempt vs the length of the core attempt e g if 3 7 feet of material is
recovered from a 5 0 foot core run the recovery would be 74 percent and would be
shown on the Boring Logs as REC 74
The Rock Quality Designation RQD is also a subjective guide to the relative
quality of rock masses RQD is defined as the percentage of the core run that is sound
material that is in excess of four 4 inches in length without any discontinuities
discounting any drilling induced fractures or breaks e g if 2 5 feet of sound material is
recovered from a 5 0 foot core run the RQD would be 50 percent and would be shown
on the Boring Logs as REC 50 Generally the following is used to describe the relative
quality based on the Practical Handbook of Physical Properties of Rocks and Minerals
