Soil Engineering Report
5225 Likini Street
Honolulu Oahu Hawaii
In accordance with your request we have made a study of the near
surface soil conditions at the above referenced lot in order to provide
foundation recommendations for the proposed residence The study
consisted of drilling two shallow borings laboratory testing of the
soils and reviewing of the subdivision site grading plans and the
preliminary house plans This report summarizes our findings and
recommendations
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
The proposed residence will be a two story house approximately 60 by
66 feet in plan dimensions with CMU walls and wood framed construction
Concrete on grade slab is planned for the entire house including the
garage and driveway areas
A layout plan for the house is shown on the Site Plan Plate 1

SITE CONDITIONS
The proposed house is located on the north side of Likini Street
with Ala Nanu and Ala Nanala Streets on the eastern and western sides
respectively
The site is generally flat and sparsely patched with tall grasses

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Our subsurface exploration encountered a generally competent upper
fill crust which will provide bearing support for the proposed building
foundation Shallow spread or continuous wall footings bearing directly
on the existing mudrock fill may be used

It is our understanding that Salt Lake had been filled a long time
ago It is our opinion that consolidation fill settlement due to the
remaining soft subsoil would have completed by now
Minimal site grading is anticipated for the construction of the
proposed building
Foundation Support
Based on the anticipated soil conditions and light design loading we
believe that an allowable bearing capacity of 2500 p s f may be used for
spread or continuous footing bearing on the existing fill materials
Footing should be placed a minimum of twelve 12 inches below the
lowest adjacent finished grade Uplift wind or other lateral loading
condition may further increase the embedment of the footing foundation
The bearing capacity value is for total dead plus live loads only and
may be increased by one third for short duration wind loading Minimal
settlements less than 1 2 of an inch under the design loading are
anticipated with the dense mudrock fill condition encountered in our
exploration
Resistance to lateral loads for the proposed structure can be
obtained by frictional resistance and passive pressure A frictional
coefficient of 0 4 may be used between the bottom of the footing and the
recompacted existing fill In addition an equivalent fluid pressure of
300 p s f per foot of depth may be used to determine the passive
resistance

Slab On Grade
It is our understanding that 4 inch concrete on grade slab is planned
for the entire house The thickened edge of the slab should be embedded a
minimum of twelve 12 inches below the lowest adjacent outside finished
grade The bottom of all thickened edge excavations should also be wetted
and kept moist until the concrete is poured The backfill against the
poured concrete slab should be adequately compacted and graded to shed
water away from the slab perimeter
A 4 inch layer of 3B Fine gravel cushion fill should be provided
below the slab In order to protect against future moisture infiltration
an impervious moisture membrane may be incorporated below the slab
Site Grading
No major site grading is anticipated
At the on set of earthwork the areas within the building grading
limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed Any unsuitable
materials such as grass debris and rubbish should be removed and
disposed of properly off site in order to prevent contamination of the
excavated material
After clearing and grubbing the areas to receive footing and
pavement should be scarified to a depth of at least 6 inches below the
bottom of footing or subgrade elevation moisture conditioned and
recompacted to 95 of its maximum density as established by ASTM D 1557 78
test method Any loose or wet spots encountered at the footing or
subgrade level should be removed and replaced with compacted select
on site granular soil or with suitable imported material compacted to the
above mentioned standard

The on site gravelly soil broken up mudrock may be reused as
backfill material If required new imported fill material should consist
of non expansive select granular materials less than 3 inches in maximm
size with less than 20 passing the 200 sieve
Pavement
A concrete driveway is planned for the proposed house
For light traffic considerations the following design pavement
section is recommended
Rigid Pavement
4 Inch Portland Cement Concrete
4 Inch Base Course Over Moist Subgrade
8 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
The base course should consist of crushed rock compacted to 90 to
95 of the ASTM D dry density
CBR tests should be performed on the actual exposed subgrade soil
samples during construction to verify the above design section which may
need to be revised if necessary
Drainage
The finished grade outside the slab pavement and footings should be
shaped to shed water away from the foundations and to avoid ponding
condition Excessive landscape watering near the foundations and slab
should also be avoided

These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance
of the above recommended foundations since ponded water may cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent loss of strength The
foundation excavations should be properly backfilled tight against the
slab footing immediately after setting of the concrete to prevent ponding
condition
Design Review
Plans and specifications for site grading foundation and pavement
should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written comments
prior to construction to see that the intent of our recommendations is
included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the grubbing site grading foundation
excavation backfilling and subgrade preparation be observed by the soils
engineer during construction to see that adequate compaction has been
obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the
findings of our investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site for use
If conditions encountered during construction are different from
those presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made
