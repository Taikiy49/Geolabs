Geotechnical Engineering Investigation
FY86 Special Project C101 85
Vandal Launch Pad Facility
Pacific Missile Range Facility
Barking Sands Kauai Hawaii
This report presents the results of our geotechnical investigation
performed for the proposed Vandal Launch Pad Facility at the Pacific
Missile Range Facility at Barking Sands on the island of Kauai Hawaii
The general location of the project site and vicinity are shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1
PROJECT CONSIDERATION
The Vandal Launch Pad Facility includes an 8 by 12 foot CMU
building to house the MOM 8G power units a 30 by 50 foot concrete launch
pad and a 14 by 48 foot concrete launch pad for the installation of
MOM 8G Launchers and support equipment The thickness of the concrete
pad is anticipated to be 24 inches at the main launching zone and 12
inches at the edge of the pad Loading on the pad including thrust
during missile launching is anticipated on the order of 164 kips
Minimal site grading is anticipated for the launch pad facility
construction
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of the investigation was to evaluate the subsurface
soil conditions at the proposed site to provide soil data for the design
of the foundation for the proposed building and to determine the bearing
and settlement characteristics of the soil underlying the proposed
facility
The scope of our investigation which was performed in general
accordance with our proposal of included drilling and
sampling three 3 borings laboratory soil testing engineering analysis
utilizing the field and laboratory data and report preparation

GENERAL SITE CONDITIONS
The proposed new 30 by 50 foot launch pad is located at
approximately the same area of the existing Launch Pad 5 or Structure
572 while the new 14 by 48 foot launch pad is added to the existing
Launch Pad 4 or Structure 571 The proposed 8 by 12 foot CMU building is
located adjacent to the southern side of Building 566 All the above
mentioned existing structures are presently located at a level pad on top
of a small hill near Bench Mark Lapa at the Pacific Missile Range
Facility at Barking Sands on the island of Kauai Hawaii
The site is presently occupied by the existing Launch Pads 4 and 5
a cable termination hut or Building 566 and several other smaller missile
or met rocket launch complexes
The whole leveled pad area is generally paved with asphaltic
concrete pavement which appears to be in fairly good condition The
surface elevation within the area varies from approximately 989 14 to
91 33 feet above MSL

Subsoil Condition
Three 3 borings were drilled to depth of 20 feet below ground
surface All three borings encountered fine to medium grain sized beach
sand from ground surface to the end of the borings However the
consistency of the sand deposits varies from a dense to medium dense
condition at the top 3 feet to a medium dense to loose condition for the
following 10 to 15 feet The consistency becomes dense at depth below 18
feet Groundwater was not encountered in the drilled borings
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATION
It is anticipated that the loads from the 8 by 12 foot CMU building
and the concrete launch pads plus launching thrust should be light
Based on these anticipated loadings and the soil conditions we
believe that the CMU building may be supported by shallow spread or
continuous wall footing bearing on the recompacted in situ medium dense
to loose sand and the concrete pads may be placed directly on the
recompacted in situ sand
Footings should be placed a minimum of 18 inches and concrete pads
should be placed a minimum of 12 inches below the lowest adjacent
finished grade Uplift wind blast or other lateral loading condition
may further increase the embedment of the footing foundation and the
pads

An allowable bearing capacity of 2 000 p s f may be used for the
foundation and the pad design The bearing capacity value is for total
dead plus live load only and may be increased by one third for short
duration wind loading Settlements under the design loading are
anticipated to be on the order of less than one half of an inch total and
one fourth of an inch differential
Resistance to lateral loads for the CMU buildings and the launch
pads can be obtained by passive pressure and frictional resistance A
frictional coefficient of 0 4 may be used between the bottom of the
footing launch pad and the medium dense soil In addition an equivalent
fluid pressure of 300 p s f per foot of depth may be used to determine
the passive resistance
Site Grading
Fills up to 2 feet is anticipated due to the relocation and
construction of the concrete pads while minimal site grading is
anticipated for the CMU building
At the on set of earthwork the site should be cleared of existing
pavements structures debris and other unsuitable materials After
clearing the areas to receive foundation footing should be kept moist
and tamped prior to placement of the footing if they are loosened during
the excavation and the area to receive fill should be scarified at least

6 inches below the cleared grade and recompacted to a minimum of 95 of
its maximum dry density Any loose area encountered should be removed
and backfilled with engineered fill
The on site sandy soil may be used for fill material If required
new imported fill material should consist of non expansive select
granular materials less than 3 inches in maximum size with less than 20
passing the 200 sieve
Structural fill should be placed in 8 inch thick compacted
horizontal lifts and compacted to 95 ASTM D 1557 78 maximum density If
the on site sandy soil is used the soil should be moisture conditioned
and compacted to 100 ASTM D 1557 78 maximum density for the top 6 inches
below pavement
Pavement
Depending on the traffic conditions the following pavement section
recommendation may be used for preliminary design purpose
Rigid Pavement No Vehicular Traffic
4 9 Inch Portland Cement Concrete
4 0 Inch Base Course 95 Compaction
8 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
Flexible Pavement Light Traffic and Parking Area
2 0 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 0 Inch Base Course 95 Compaction
8 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness

It is our opinion that the asphaltic concrete pavement surrounding
the new launch pads should be designed with the same thickness to match
with the existing pavement which is in fairly good condition
Design Review
Plans and specifications for site grading and foundations should be
forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written comments prior to
construction to see that the intent of our recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the site grading subgrade preparation
footings and pad excavations be observed by the soils engineer during
construction to see that adequate compaction has been obtained and that
the subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of our
investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
If conditions encountered during construction are different fron
those presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made

INVESTIGATION LIMITATIONS
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations
made in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation
engineering principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all
other warranties either expressed or implied
The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are based
on our site reconnaissance soil information from boring data and
laboratory tests obtained from this soil investigation
Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encountered and cannot
be fully determined by taking soil samples Unforeseen conditions such
as expansive soil and soft spots may occur in localized areas and may
require additional probing or corrections in the field Therefore
additional expenditures may be needed during construction to attain a
properly constructed project Some contingency fund is thus recommended
to accommodate these possible costs
