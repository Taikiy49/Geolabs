This letter report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations during
the probing and grouting operations for the Import Terminal for the Hawaiian Cement
Company at the Barbers Point Deep Draft Harbor from May 8 through 19 2000
Project Considerations
The import terminal site is located to the northeast of Pier No 6 at the Barbers
Point Deep Draft Harbor on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The majority of the site is
surrounded by open land except towards the southwest which is abutted by the existing
concrete pavements of Pier No 6 The general location and vicinity of the project site are
shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The import terminal will consist of two monolithic concrete domes with a diameter
of about 145 feet and a height of about 77 feet Each dome will have an underground
tunnel located approximately 16 feet below the design finished grade We understand
that the new dome structures will generally have a finished floor elevation of about
22 7 feet Mean Sea Level MSL The underground tunnel floor is set at an elevation of
about 7 feet MSL In addition we understand that the floor loading of the domes will be
on the order of about 7 000 pounds per square foot psf

As part of the import terminal project the project will also entail the construction of
truck loading structures steel towers to support pneumatic conveying pipes and several
one story service buildings In addition new concrete parking and roadways will be
constructed for the proposed project
Site Preparation
Prior to the probing and grouting operations at the two dome locations the existing
ground at the domes was over excavated to provide the 2 foot thick layer of compacted
fill material below the dome slab subgrades The over excavated subgrades were then
proof rolled with a 10 ton vibratory sheepsfoot compactor a minimum of six passes to
detect the presence of near surface cavities Based on our field observations near
surface cavities were not evident during the proof rolling operations
The 2 foot thick compacted fills consisting of the on site coralline materials were
then placed and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction For the
truck load area the existing ground was generally excavated to about 6 inches below the
bottom of the concrete slab prior to the probing and grouting operations The earthwork
operations were performed by Jayar Construction with Geolabs providing the field
monitoring and compaction testing services
Probing and Grouting Operations
The probing and grouting operations were performed in general conformance with
the recommendations presented in our report for the project dated
The purpose of the probing and grouting work was to reduce the potential for loss of
foundation and floor slab support resulting from collapse of cavities or compression of
loose zones below the dome structures and truck load area
The centers of the two domes were surveyed by others and the probe hole
locations were marked by our field representatives prior to the probing operations The
probe holes were marked along the perimeter footings spaced at about 10 foot on center
Additional probe holes were marked based on 10 foot on center starting at the center of
the tank and radiating out to the perimeter in about 10 foot radial increments The
approximate locations of the probe holes at the domes are shown on the Probe Hole
Location Plan Plate 2
In general the probe holes along the perimeter footings of the dome structures
were drilled to depths of about 12 feet below the top of the compacted fill Probe holes
within the remainder of the dome structure were extended to depths of about 10 feet
below the top of the compacted fill Probe holes were not drilled within the tunnel area
due to the planned excavations for the tunnel structure For the truck load area the
probe holes were spaced at 15 foot lateral intervals on centers in a rectangular grid
pattern and were extended to depths of about 10 feet below the prepared ground surface

The probe holes about 4 inches in diameter were drilled by Geolabs using a
truck mounted drill rig equipped with continuous flight augers Following the probing
operation a 2 inch diameter PVC pipe was inserted into the probe hole to maintain the
integrity of the open hole and to reduce the potential for cave in prior to the grouting
operation A total of 378 probe holes with an aggregate length of about 3 951 lineal feet
were drilled The approximate locations of the probe holes are shown on the Probe Hole
Location Plan Plate 2
The probe holes were backfilled with 500 pounds per square inch psi flowable fill
supplied by Hawaiian Cement injected at low to moderate pressures The grouting work
was performed by American Standard Concrete Pumping Hawaii Inc and observed by a
field representative from Geolabs The grouting operation generally proceeded in the
following manner
e Immediately prior to grouting of a particular hole the temporary PVC pipe
was removed A 2 inch diameter grouting probe was inserted into the open
hole and lowered to approximately 2 feet from the bottom of the hole Grout
injection then commenced at the direction of our field representative who
recorded the approximate grout take and provided direction as to when the
injection was complete or whether periodic injection was required
e Each probe hole was grouted from the desired depth upward by low to
moderate pressure injection of grout through a PVC conduit which was
retracted from the probe hole as the grout filled the hole Several injection
passes were needed to grout those probe holes where high grout take was
observed The grouting operation and the approximate grout take were
observed and recorded by our field representative
Approximately 81 6 cubic yards of flowable fill was utilized to fill the probe holes
In general the majority of the probe holes at Dome No 2 and the truck load area took
about twice the theoretical volume of grout to fill the probe holes However significant
grout take was observed in the shaded area in Dome No 1 as shown on the Probe Hole
Location Plan Plate 2 The probing and grouting summary is provided on Plates 3 1
through 3 16
Limitations
The elevations of the probe holes were estimated from the elevations and
contours shown on the Grading Plan prepared by River Consulting Incorporated The
elevations of the probe holes should be considered accurate only to the degree implied
by the methods used
