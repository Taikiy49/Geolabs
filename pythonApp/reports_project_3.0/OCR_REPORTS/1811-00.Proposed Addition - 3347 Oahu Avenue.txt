SOIL ENGINEERING REPORT
PROPOSED ADDITION 3347 OAHU AVENUE
As requested we have made a study of the near surface soil
conditions below the above referenced site The purpose of the study was
to evaluate the suitability of the near surface soils as foundation
support for the proposed addition The study consisted of drilling two
2 borings within the site performing laboratory soil tests and
reviewing the preliminary Plot Plan for the addition This report
summarizes our observations and reconmendations
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a one story addition measuring 28 by 43
feet in plan dimension to the lower portion of the existing house at the
site
Wood frame construction is planned for the addition with the
finished floor level matching the existing lower level of the house

SITE CONDITIONS
The existing house is located on the southeastern side of Oahu
Avenue in Manoa Valley Oahu Hawaii
The addition area is situated adjacent to the lower southeastern
portion of the house
The area is generally a flat well trimmed grass lawn area with a
slight slope towards the northeast corner of the site
At about 12 feet southeast of the proposed structure the ground
slopes down steeply at about H V or slightly steeper slope ratios
to an existing 5 foot high retaining wall approximately 20 feet below
SUBSURFACE SOIL CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions were explored by drilling and sampling two
2 borings to depths of 20 feet below the existing ground surface
The borings disclosed that the site was mantled by a stiff brown
clayey silt with sand and gravel approximately 20 to 7 5 feet thick
In Boring 2 a soft brown to brownish gray clayey silt was
encountered from 2 0 to 9 0 feet below the existing ground surface

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Our subsurface exploration encountered generally stiff layers of
clayey silt with occasional soft zones at the proposed addition site
Despite the localized soft soil condition shallow spread or continuous
wall footings bearing directly on the existing clayey silt may be used
for foundation support for most of the light structure
Pipe pile supports are recommended for the end wall of the addition
near the existing steep slope
Minimal site grading is anticipated for the construction of the
proposed building
Foundation Support
Spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the proposed
addition An allowable bearing value of 1500 p s f may be used on the
in situ soil
Individual footings on level areas should be embedded a minimum of
12 inches below the adjacent finished grade Continuous wall footings
should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the adjacent finished
grade

The pipe piles should consist of minimum 6 inch diameter drilled

An allowable capacity of 5 kips per pipe pile may be used for a

Surface water should be diverted away from foundations
Slab on Grade
Due to the high natural moisture content the on site soils have low
expansion potential if they are prevented from drying out Therefore
the subgrade soils should be maintained in a moist condition
A 6 inch layer of 3B Fine gravel cushion fill is recommended below
the slab In order to protect against future moisture infiltration an
impervious moisture membrane may be incorporated below the slab
Lot Grading
If required structural fills and backfills should consist of
non expansive soil such as select borrow or crusher waste and be placed
in maximum 8 inch loose lifts and then compacted to a minimum of 90 of
its maximum dry density established by ASTM D 1557 78
Prior to construction the area to receive any fill should be
cleared of vegetation and debris

The site should be so graded to provide drainage of surface runoff
away from all foundations
Drainage
The finish lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings should
be shaped to shed water away from the foundations and to avoid ponding
condition Also it is advised that gutter systems should be installed
around the house and that gutter water should be diverted far away from
foundations and walkways Excessive landscape watering near foundations
and slabs should also be avoided
Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance
of the above recommended foundations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of
strength The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled tight
against the slab footing immediately after setting of the concrete to
prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained
to drain surface run off away from the slab and footing foundation
CONSTRUCTION MONTTORING
It is recommended that the footing excavations and backfill
operations be observed by the soils engineer during construction to see
that adequate bearing has been obtained and that the subsurface
conditions are consistent with the findings of our subsurface
ivnestigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site

LIMITATIONS
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations
made in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation
engineering principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all
other warranties either expressed or implied
The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are based
on our site reconnaissance soil information from boring data and
laboratory tests obtained from this soil exploration
Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encountered and cannot be
fully determined by taking soil samples Unforeseen conditions such as
expansive soil and soft spots may occur in localized areas and may
require additional probing or corrections in the field Therefore
additional expenditures may be needed during construction to attain a
properly constructed project Some contingent fund is thus recommended
to accommodate these possible costs
