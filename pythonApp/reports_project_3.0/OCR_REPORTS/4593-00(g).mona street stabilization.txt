This progress report summarizes our work efforts and the field instrumentation
monitoring results for the Mona Street Stabilization Aina Haina Slide Zone Remediation
project in Aina Haina Valley on the Island of Oahu Hawaii Our work was performed in
general accordance with the scope of services outlined in our fee proposal dated
May 9 2007
This report provides updated information on the lateral earth movements
subsurface water levels and geotechnical recommendations for remediation of the
ongoing landslide We included general information from our instrumentation program in
our progress report dated The geotechnical exploration results
were presented in our report entitled Revised Subsurface Investigation Program

instrumentation monitoring period which officially commenced in January 2002

order to measure the lateral deflection of the soil at various depths We drilled an
additional boring B 4 to a depth of approximately 100 feet in September 2003 An
inclinometer was installed in this boring to better delineate slide limits Locations for B 1
through B 4 are shown on the Site Plan Plate 1

It should be noted that data loggers for Piezometer Nos P 1a and P 1b were
removed for repair and maintenance Re fabricated data loggers and piezometers were
installed in late April 2007 after current contract execution It should also be noted that
the data from Piezometer No P 2b was lost between April and July 2007 due to
damaged battery
Based on the data interpretation and review the water level in Boring No 1
Piezometer No P 1b dropped down from 16 feet to 21 feet below the ground surface in
the last six month monitoring period The dropping water level may be related to the
additional ground lateral movement indicated by Inclinometer No I 1 within the last
six month monitoring period
PAVEMENT DEFORMATION MAPPING
Ground surface observations were recorded in our previous reports
Approximately 1 to 2 inch wide pavement cracks on the surface of the roadway were
observed between 1189 and 1199 Mona Street from May 2002 to September 2002
No significant continuous cracking development was recorded from September 2002 to
January 2003
Pavement resurfacing was performed in localized areas of Mona Street during
January 2003 to August 2003 A majority of the cracks previously recorded was patched
with asphaltic concrete No significant continuous cracking development was recorded
from January 2004 to August 2004
However a majority of the cracks recorded prior to the pavement re surfacing
was again observed on the re surfaced pavement in 2005 Some extended new cracks
were developed and recorded from 2005 to 2006 as indicated in the
Progress Report No 10 dated March 9 2007 We will continue to monitor the
pavement deformation The revised Crack Location Plan will be available in the next
progress report at the end of the 2007 monitoring period
SUMMARY
Inclinometer Nos I 1 I 3 and I 4 indicated some shallow depth subsurface
ground movement at their respective instrument locations However Inclinometer
No I 2 currently does not indicate significant subsurface ground lateral movement Our
piezometer data indicates that subsurface water seepage occurred at the project site
Based on our pavement deformation mapping we believe that lateral near surface earth
movement at the site is still active
Based on the interpretation from Inclinometer Nos 1 and I 3 it appears that the
sliding zone may be relatively shallow approximately 11 to 15 feet below the ground
surface in the area near Boring Nos 1 and 3 Inclinometer No 4 is located between
