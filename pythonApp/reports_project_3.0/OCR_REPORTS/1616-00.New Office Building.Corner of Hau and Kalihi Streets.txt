Project Consideration

on grade slabs

Site Description
The proposed site is located in a predominantly commercial area at
the junction of Hau and Kalihi Streets and borders two existing
businesses
The ground was relatively level and appeared to be approximately one
foot lower than the sidewalk level It appears that the site was
previously occupied by a dwelling
Soil Conditions
The subsurface conditions were explored by drilling and sampling
three 3 borings to depths of 15 to 16 5 feet below the existing ground
surface
The borings disclosed that the site was mantled by a thin layer of
stiff brown clayey silt overlying moderately hard tan white cemented
coral The clayey silt layer generally varied from 0 5 to 1 5 feet thick
and in our opinion exhibited a low swelling potential The coral
consisted mainly of cemented coral sand and gravel which is believed to
be coral algal reef detritus and extended to at least 15 feet below the
ground the maximum depth drilled Generally it is believed that the
coral extends to a substantial depth in this area It should be noted
that the coral surface may contain occasional cavities
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings at the time of our
exploration

Our findings indicate that the site is predominantly mantled with a

Spread Footing Foundation
For the proposed structure spread footing foundations bearing on
the underlying dense coral are recommended
An allowable soil bearing of 6000 p s f may be used for footing
bearing on coral The bearing may be increased by one third when
considering temporary wind or seismic loads A minimum footing size of
16 inches should be used
Non load bearing walls may be placed on new compacted fill or
recompacted on site soil These wall footings should be embedded a
minimum of 12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade and be designed for
an allowable soil bearing pressure of 2500 p s f
It is anticipated that the total and differential foundation
settlements if constructed according to the above design criteria
should be negligible
We recommend that all footing excavations be inspected by a soils
engineer to confirm the subsurface soil conditions upon which our design
recommendations are based
Site Preparation
Prior to placing the on grade concrete slabs we recommend that the
entire site be scarified to a depth of at least 6 inches moisture
conditioned to within 3 percentage points of the optimum and recompacted
to at least 90 of the ASTM D 1557 78 maximum density Any soft or
yielding areas disclosed during this work should be repaired by removing
the soft soils and backfilling with compacted fill as deemed necessary

Fill Construction
It is anticipated that imported fill will be required The fill
material should be non expansive soil such as coral cinder select
borrow or crusher waste and should not contain rock fragements larger
than 6 inches in size The material should be tested and approved by the
soils engineer before it is hauled to the site
All i11 and backfill material should be placed in horizontal lifts
not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a dry density
of at least 90 of the ASTM D 1557 78 maximum density
Cavity Probing and Grouting
It should be noted that the coral rock frequently contains filled
and unfilled voids or cavities After the site is cleared for building
construction the foundation area especially below the footings should
be probed for possible cavity or cavities
It is recommended that a 3 inch diameter probe hole be drilled at
each column location or on 8 foot centers along continuous wall footings
as appropriate The probe holes should be drilled to a minimum of 5 feet
into the coral rock The resulting probe holes should be backfilled with
neat cement grout mix injected at a low pressure
If cavities are encountered they should be grouted with cement or
concrete grout with a compressive strength of at least 2000 p s i For
small cavities with height of less than 6 inches measured at the probe
location cement grout should be used For cavities with height
exceeding 6 inches concrete grout may be used In such a case the
probe hole may need to be enlarged

If excessive grout take is noted filling should be temporary
suspended to allow the grout to set Thereafter the filling should then
be resumed The temporary suspension of filling is to limit the
excessive grout take
We recommend that the probing and grouting operations be monitored
by a soils engineer
On Grade Slab
On grade floor slab may be used for the proposed building after the
site has been scarified recompacted and filled The existing brown
clayey silt is low to moderately expansive Therefore the soil should
be kept moist to minimize its expansion potential
The on grade floor slab should be placed over a 4 inch cushion layer
of gravel size crushed rock material such as No 3 Fine rock to provide
uniform support A moisture barrier membrane should be placed under the
slabs
The slab thickened edges exposed to any unpaved area should be
embedded at least 8 inches below the lowest adjacent grade
Design Review
Plans and specifications for site grading cavity probing grouting
and foundation should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and
written comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the grubbing site preparation cavity
probing and grouting engineered fill placement and compaction
foundation excavation and backfilling be observed by the soils engineer
during construction to see that adequate compaction has been obtained and
that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of our
investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
If conditions encountered during construction are different from
those presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made
Investigation Limitations
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations
made in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation
engineering principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all
other warranties either expressed or implied
The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are based
on our site reconnaissance soil information from boring and laboratory
test data obtained from this soil investigation
Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encountered and cannot be
fully determined by taking soil samples Unforeseen conditions such as
pockets of organic soils trash fill or expansive soil may occur in
localized areas and may require additional probing or corrections in the
field Therefore additional expenditures may be needed during
construction to attain a properly constructed project Some contingency
fund is thus recommended to accommodate these possible costs

three 3 borings to depths of 15 to 16 5 feet below the existing ground

reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory All soils

Classification System A summary of the materials encountered are
presented in the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 3

with a 140 pound hammer free falling 30 inches The blow counts to
drive the sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the Logs of Borings at
