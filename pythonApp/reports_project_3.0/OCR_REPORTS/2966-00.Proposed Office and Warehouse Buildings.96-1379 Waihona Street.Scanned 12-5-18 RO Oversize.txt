performed for the proposed office warehouse buildings development at Pear City on the
island of Oahu Hawaii The general location of the project site and its vicinity is shown
on the Project Location Map Plate 1

laboratory testing and presents our geotechnical engineering recommendations derived
from our analyses based on the data obtained through the field exploration and laboratory
testing

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located at Street in the eastern end of Pearl
City Industrial Park on the island of Oahu Hawaii It is proposed to develop the project
site as a office warehouse facility with the construction of three 8 new buildings an
access roadway and additional parking areas Retaining wall structures are also being
planned along the sloping bank of Waiawa Stream in order to accommodate the new
access roadway
It is our understanding that the warehouse buildings will be flexible single story
metal framed buildings with a mezzanine level Concrete slabs on grade will be utilized
for the building floors Construction of new utility lines and asphalt pavements for the
parking areas are also anticipated for the proposed development
Based on the information provided to us by GMP Associates Inc on November
19 1992 structural loads on the order of 45 kips per column and 1 500 pounds per foot
of bearing wall are anticipated for the proposed warehouse structures

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located at Street in the eastern end of Pearl
City Industrial Park on the island of Oahu Hawaii The subject property is bordered by
Waiawa Stream to the north and the wall of Waiawa Valley to the south
The majority of the subject site was previously graded for the existing site
improvements and is relatively level The existing ground surface elevations of the site
range from approximately 113 feet above Mean Sea Level MSL at the entrance to the
site on Waihona Street to approximately 138 feet above MSL at the eastern end of the
site The slope of the existing stream bank of Waiawa Stream along the boundary of the
site varies in steepness with an average slope ratio between one horizontal to one vertical
1H 1V and In the portion of the stream bank where the meander of Waiawa
Stream narrows the project site the existing slope of the stream bank approaches
vertical
Presently the property is used as a baseyard maintenance storage facility for
construction equipment and materials by Fletcher Pacific Construction Co Ltd The site
is an irregularly shaped lot covering approximately 327 500 square feet in area with an
existing warehouse building covering approximately 26 500 square feet Several other
GEOLABS HAWAII

utility lines including drainlines waterlines and sewerlines crossing the property

Site Grading
Due to the moderately high swelling potential of the near surface soils at the project
site special precautions and procedures should be observed and implemented for the
site grading
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable
materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site
Slabs and foundations of the existing structures being demolished should be
completely removed Any overexcavation resulting from demolition should be backfilled
with compacted non expansive select material
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2966 00 Page 8
Existing utilities to be abandoned should be removed and the resulting excavation
backfilled with compacted non expansive select material Utilities to be abandoned in
place should be backfilled with lean concrete
After demolition clearing and grubbing all future buildings and pavement areas
should be overexcavated to a minimum depth of eighteen inches 18 below the future
slab or pavement subgrade level i e bottom of slab cushion or bottom of base course
Any loose or soft spots encountered at the overexcavated subgrade level should be
removed and replaced with compacted non expansive select granular material The
clayey material resulting from the overexcavation should be removed and properly
disposed of off site
The overexcavated surface should be moisture conditioned to slightly above
optimum moisture content and recompacted to 90 percent of its maximum dry density as
determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 Select granular material should be placed
in level lifts not exceeding ten inches 10 in loose thickness and compacted to a
minimum of 90 percent of its maximum dry density as determined by ASTM Test Method
D 1557 78 The select granular material should consist of crushed coral mudrock basalt
or cinder sand The material should be well graded from coarse to fine with no particles
larger than three 3 inches in maximum size The material should have a laboratory
CBR value of 20 or more and a maximum swell of no more than one 1 percent when
tested in accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1883 It should contain a maximum of
20 percent fine particles passing the No 200 sieve
Alternately the proposed finish grades of the buildings and the pavements may be
designed with higher elevations such that a capping fill consisting of a minimum of
eighteen inches 18 of non expansive select granular fill could be placed in the future
building and parking areas

Building Foundations
According to the structural information provided to us structural loadings on the
order of 45 kips per column and 1 500 pounds per foot of bearing wall are anticipated
In general our field exploration encountered competent subsoil conditions at the
site Therefore we believe that shallow spread and or continuous footings may be used
to support the proposed office warehouse buildings
An allowable bearing capacity of up to 3 000 p s f may be utilized for the design
of footing foundations bearing on recompacted existing fill or on compacted imported fill
material This bearing value is for total dead plus live loads only and may be increased
by up to one third 1 3 for loads of short duration such as those caused by wind or
seismic loads
The subgrades of all footings should be scarified moisture conditioned to slightly
above optimum moisture content and recompacted to a minimum of 90 percent of its
maximum dry density as determined in accordance with the procedures described in
ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 If soft loose pockets are encountered at the bottom of
the footing excavation they should be over excavated to competent material and replaced
with select granular material compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of its maximum dry
density as determined in accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Footings in level areas should be embedded a minimum of twenty four inches 24
below the lowest adjacent finish grade
For foundations situated near slopes the following guidelines should be followed
1 For slopes steeper than one and one half horizontal to one vertical
the foundations should be set back a minimum of 5 feet from an
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2966 00 Page 10
imaginary one and one half horizontal to one vertical line
projected upward from the base of the slope Alternately the foundations
should be embedded deep enough to obtain a minimum eight foot set back
distance measured horizontally from the outside edge of the footing to the
imaginary line
2 For slopes flatter than one and one half horizontal to one vertical
the foundations should be set back or embedded to obtain a minimum
eight foot set back distance measured horizontally from the outside edge
of the footing to the slope face
For foundations located next to utility trenches the foundations should be
embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane extending
upward from the bottom edge of the utility trench or to a depth equal to the invert of the
utility line
Footing settlements under the anticipated design loadings are estimated to be less
than one inch total and one half inch differential
Lateral loads acting on the building structures may be resisted by passive earth
pressure developed against the buried vertical faces of the foundation system and by
frictional resistance developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be calculated using an equivalent fluid
pressure of 300 p s f per foot of depth This assumes that the soil around the foundation
is well compacted A coefficient of friction of 0 8 may be used Unless covered by
pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the upper 18 inches of soil should be
neglected

Retaining Wallis

design of retaining walls or drilled pier foundations

Pier Diameter Embedded Length Allowable Pier Capacity

The above recommendations are based on the assumption that the drilled pier
foundations are embedded in stiff to very stiff clayey silt saprolite material for the
recommended embedment length If the pier is end bearing on weathered basalt rock
formation an allowable vertical pier capacity of 100 tons may be used
The load bearing capacities of drilled piers depend to a large extent on the
contact between the pier shaft and the surrounding soils Therefore proper construction
techniques are important We recommend that the drilled pier construction be monitored
by the project geotechnical engineer
The contractor should exercise the utmost care in drilling the pier holes and placing
the proper concrete mix into the holes Drilling by methods utilizing drilling fluids should
be avoided because the fluids could saturate and weaken the surrounding soil
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2966 00 Page 15
A non shrink concrete mix should be used to minimize shrinkage of the concrete
and to provide close contact between the pier shafts and the surrounding soil The
concrete should be placed in the drilled holes as soon as possible to reduce the potential
of hole cave in The concrete should also be placed in a suitable manner to avoid
segregation of the aggregates in the concrete mix
It should be noted that the above guidelines presented for the retaining wall and
drilled piers design are based on extrapolation and interpretation of the soil data obtained
from the drilled borings which were located some distance from the proposed retaining
wall alignment Further exploration work should be performed to verify or modify if
necessary the above guidelines

Drainage
The finished grades outside the buildings should be shaped to shed water away
from foundations and to avoid ponding conditions Also it is advised that gutter systems
be installed around the buildings and that the discharge be diverted away from foundation
and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near foundations and slabs should
also be avoided
Since the project site is bordered by an existing ridge to the south significant
amounts of runoff into the site from this area may occur after heavy rains It is
recommended that a concrete interceptor ditch be installed to intercept the runoff from
the ridge
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2966 00 Page 17
The interceptor ditch wall should be poured directly against the excavation side
walls to reduce the potential of seepage under the ditch which could cause slope
instability The ground surface next to the ditch should be shaped to drain into the ditch
Weepholes should be provided in the upslope wall of the ditch to reduce the build up of
seepage pressure
Concrete drainage swales should be provided at the base of the ridge to intercept
and drain the remaining runoff
Slope Erosion
Based on our field exploration it appears that the stream bank where the meander
of Waiawa Stream narrows the project site is being progressively eroded due to stream
action The existing stream bank approaches to vertical If this stream bank erosion
continues it appears that the future access roadway could be jeopardized
Therefore it is our opinion that some form of stream bank erosion protection
should be installed to minimize the potential of further erosion along this meander area
These schemes may include the construction of a rip rap slope a conventional retaining
wall or a steel sheetpile wall along the existing stream
It is Our opinion that further field exploration work should be performed in
conjunction with further exploration for the design of the proposed retaining walls along
the sloping bank to provide additional information on the slope stability and subsoil
conditions in this area Based on further analyses with additional data a safe economical
and feasible stream protection scheme may then be developed for the subject project

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling seven
7 borings to depths ranging between 7 0 and 38 5 feet below the existing ground
surface Additional exploration was performed by excavating and logging three 3 test
pits to depths of 8 0 to 13 0 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate
locations of the borings and test pits are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings
were drilled using truck mounted equipment and the test pits were excavated using a
backhoe provided by Fletcher Pacific Construction
The material encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who continuously monitored the drilling
operations These classifications were further reviewed by visual inspection and testing
in the laboratory All soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A graphic and written presentation of the materials encountered
Soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving a standard penetration
sampler or 3 0 D Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer free falling 30
inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the Boring
Logs at the appropriate sample depths
Core samples of rock materials encountered at the site were obtained using
diamond core drilling techniques in general accordance with ASTM Standard Practice D
2113 83 Diamond Core Drilling for Site Investigation Core drilling is a rotary drilling
method which uses a hollow bit to cut into the rock formation The rock material left in
the hollow core of the bit is mechanically recovered for examination and description
Recovery REC is used as a subjective guide to the interpretation of the relative
quality of rock masses Recovery is defined as the actual length of material recovered
from a coring attempt vs the length of the core attempt e g if 3 7 feet of material is
recovered from a 5 0 foot core run the recovery would be 74 and would be shown on
the Boring Logs as REC 74
The Rock Quality Designation RQD is also a subjective guide to the relative
quality of rock masses RQD is defined as the percentage of the core run that is sound
material in excess of 4 inches in length without any discontinuities discounting any drilling
induced fractures or breaks If 2 5 feet of sound material is recovered from a 5 0 foot core
run the RQD would be 50 and would be shown on the Boring Logs as RQD 50
Generally the following is used to describe the relative quality of the rock based on the
Practical Handbook of Physical Properties of Rocks and Minerals
