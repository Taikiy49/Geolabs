One Percolation Test for Sewage Disposal
at 4034 Black Point Road
Honolulu Oahu Hawaii
This report presents the results of our boring exploration and
percolation test performed at 4034 Black Point Road Honolulu Oahu
Hawaii in order to provide subsurface data for use in the design of the
proposed cesspool seepage pit The general location of the project site
and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
Our work was performed in general accordance with our proposal of
March 9 1989
PURPOSE AND SCOPE OF WORK
For the design and feasibility of the cesspool seepage pit the
subsurface soil rock conditions at the project site were explored by
Grilling one 1 boring at the site
A percolation test was then performed in the drilled boring to
evaluate the permeability characteristics of the underlying basalt rock
formation The approximate location of the boring is shown on the Site
Plan Plate 2
W O 2262 00 Page 2
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located on the northern side of Black Point Road
in Honolulu Oahu Hawaii
The ground generally slopes down to the north with a retaining wall
located in the northern portion of the site
An existing residence occupies the southern portion of the site and
was under remodeling construction at the time of our exploration
The northern portion is generally covered with grass and trees A
stockpile of boulders was noted near the eastern boundary of the site
SUBSURFACE SOIL CONDITIONS
To examine the area for a leaching field one 1 boring was drilled
at the approximate location shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The boring
generally encountered a surface fill layer of approximately 1 5 feet
thick Underlying the fill layer was a fractured hard gray basaltic rock
with some vesicles and isolated cavities to about 20 feet below the
existing ground surface the maximum depth drilled
Groundwater was not encountered in the drilled boring at the time of
our field exploration
Detailed description of the materials encountered in the boring is
shown on the Boring Log Plate A 1
PERCOLATION TESTS
After completion of drilling water was discharged into the hole
through a garden hose then a percolation test was performed in the
hole The constant head percolation test procedure was used for the
test The test data are presented on Plate B 1

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field percolation test result it appears that the
percolation rate of the soil at the site is low to moderate This is
further confirmed by visual examination of the rock cores encountered in
the boring The capacity of the cesspool seepage pit would be governed
by the fractured basalt rock underlying the clayey silt fill

effluent application for the test depth of the boring is summarized

The minimum depth of the cesspool seepage pit should be 20 feet into
the rock formation
It should be pointed out however that the loss of infiltrative
capacity and soil clogging can occur in the future by combined physical
chemical and biological factors The latter is the most important factor
since an organic mat of several millimeters thick may form at the
soil liquid interface Other factors such as rainfall rise in
groundwater level etc may also affect the permeability to some extent
Therefore the cesspool seepage pit will probably require periodic
maintenance

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by
drilling one 1 boring at the approximate location shown on the Site
Plan Plate 2 A portable drilling rig was used to drill the boring toa
depth of 20 feet below the existing ground surface
The soils and rock encountered in the boring were classified by
visual and textural examination in the field by our geologist who
continuously monitored the drilling operations The classifications were
further reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory The
soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A graphic presentation of the materials
encountered is presented on the Boring Log Plate A 1
In the boring soil samples were obtained by driving a 2 inch
standard split spoon sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches
The blow counts used to drive the sampler the last 12 inches are shown on
the boring log at the appropriate sample depths
Rock core samples were obtained using an NX core barrel with a
diamond bits The core recovery REC and rock quality determinations
RQD are shown on the Boring Log at the appropriate depths
