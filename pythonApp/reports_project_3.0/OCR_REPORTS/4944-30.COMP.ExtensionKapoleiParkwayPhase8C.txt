Dear Mr Okamoto
This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering services performed during
the construction for the Extension of Kapolei Parkway Phase 8C project in general
accordance with our fee proposal dated
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is in the District of Ewa on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project
is part of a master plan development of the Ewa area The Phase 8C portion of the new
Extension of Kapolei Parkway started from the Park Row to Renton Road The project
generally consisted of roadway construction and infrastructure development
Based on the grading plans provided site grading consisting of cuts and fills of less
than about 5 feet thick was required to achieve the design grades As part of the new
roadway construction the pavement subgrades were over excavated to a depth of
36 inches and re compacted
EARTHWORK OPERATION
The earthwork for the Kapolei Parkway Phase 8C was performed by
Albert C Kobayashi Inc Our observation and testing services during the construction of
the project occurred between October 2008 and September 2009
2006 Kalihi Street Honolulu Hawaii 96819
Hawaii California

The pavement subgrades were generally over excavated to a depth of about
36 inches below the bottom of the pavement section The fill materials were generally
placed in 8 inch loose lifts moisture conditioned and compacted to a minimum of
90 percent of the maximum dry density determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557
Modified Proctor Fill materials placed within upper 3 feet below the pavement grade
was compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
During the earthwork operations samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test
results are summarized on the attached Plate 2
Field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations on an
as need basis to confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures
and soil conditions were noted A total of 250 field density tests were performed using
the nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM D 6938 Compaction test results
are summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 19 These tests were performed on the
prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills Test locations and
elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes
These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree
implied by the methods used Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until
the specified minimum compaction was obtained
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the earthwork operations during the construction
of the Kapolei Parkway Phase 8C project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period
covered by this report were performed in general accordance with the intent of the
project plans and specifications and our geotechnical engineering recommendations
presented in the referenced report dated August 3 2006 and subsequent
recommendations
LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork may
have been performed without our observation However the level of earthwork
observation and testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some of
the geotechnical risks to the project It should be recognized that there are variations in the
accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the
earthwork construction and materials Our services were performed in accordance with
generally accepted standards of geotechnical engineering practice no warranty is
expressed or implied Provision of construction monitoring by an engineer is not insurance
nor does it guarantee construction of any type The contractor shall retain responsibility for
