INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Hauaala Road Drainage Improvements project in the Kapaa
area on the island of Kauai Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site
are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings from our field exploration and laboratory
testing and presents our geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our
analyses for the proposed project These recommendations are intended for design
input only and may not be appropriate to form the basis for construction cost estimates
nor for construction bidding purposes

of the road

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Kauai is composed of a single basalt shield volcano built by the
extrusion of the lavas of the Waimea Canyon Volcanic Series during the late Pliocene
more than 2 million years before present Following the cessation of this main shield
building phase there was renewed volcanic activity with the extrusion of basaltic lavas
of the post erosional Koloa Volcanic Series and the concurrent deposition of the alluvial
sediments of the Palikea Formation
Rocks of the Koloa Volcanic Series cover most of the eastern half of the island
of Kauai Rocks of the Koloa Volcanic Series are generally characterized as thick flows
of dense basalt extruded from groups of vents aligned in north south trends in various
locales Associated with the vents are pyroclastic materials which usually form low
cinder cones at the vent
During the Pleistocene there were many sea level changes as a result of
widespread glaciation in the continental areas of the world As the great continental
glaciers accumulated the level of the ocean fell since there was less water available
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3134 00 Page 7
to fill the oceanic basins Conversely as the glaciers receded or melted global sea
levels rose because more water was available The land mass of Kauai remained
essentially stable during these changes and the fluctuations were eustatic in nature
These glacio eustatic fluctuations resulted in stands of the sea which were both higher
and lower relative to present sea level on Kauai
The higher sea level stands caused the accumulation of deltas and fans of
terrigenous sediments in the heads of the old bays accumulation of reef deposits at
correspondingly higher elevations and lagoonal marine sediments in the quiet waters
protected by fringing reefs

Foundation Support
The underlying stiff to very stiff clayey silts and silty clays encountered in the
borings should provide adequate support for the proposed reinforced concrete drainage
channel and its appurtenances at the project site An allowable bearing value of up to
2 500 p s f may be used for the design of the drainage channel and box culvert
structures resting on the in situ stiff clayey soils This bearing value is for dead plus
live loads and may be increased by one third 1 for transient loads such as those
caused by wind and or seismic forces
Footings for the headwall structures should be embedded a minimum of 2 feet
below the lowest adjacent finish grade If scour of the bottom of the unlined channel
is anticipated foundations should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the
estimated design scour depth Soft soils were encountered near the proposed finish
subgrades of the drainage channel in Boring Nos 1 and 7 during our field exploration
If soft and or loose materials are encountered at the bottom of the footing excavations
they should be over excavated to expose the underlying firm materials The over
excavation should be backfilled with free draining granular materials such as No 3B
Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation wrapped in a layer of filter fabric Mirafi
140N or equivalent

Site Grading
Based on the grading plans provided to us site grading consisting of cuts and
fills on the order of 10 feet in thickness are anticipated for the project construction
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable
materials should be removed and disposed of off site Soft and or loose soils
encountered along the bottom of the existing drainage ditch should be over excavated
to expose firm materials
Slabs and foundations of the existing structures that are to be demolished should
be completely removed Any over excavation resulting from the demolition should be
backfilled with compacted granular fill material Existing utilities to be abandoned
should be removed and the resulting excavation should be properly backfilled with
compacted select granular fill material Utilities that are to be abandoned in place
should be backfilled by pumping lean concrete under low pressure
After demolition clearing and grubbing areas at grade or designated to receive
fill should be scarified to a depth of at least 8 inches moisture conditioned to at least
2 percent above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of
90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3134 00 Page 13
of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil
established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 91 test procedures Loose or soft spots
encountered during the scarification process should be removed and replaced with
compacted select granular fill material
The on site clayey soils generated from the cut areas may be stockpiled and
reused as a source of fill material for the lower portion of the embankment fill provided
they are properly processed as recommended below Generally the on site soils
should be suitable for use as general fill provided that the high in situ moisture contents
can be lowered to achieve the compaction requirements Therefore drying of
excavated materials may be required prior to their use as fill and or backfill
Imported granular fill material needed to raise the ground surface to the finish
grades should consist of materials less than 6 inches in maximum dimension The
material should have a laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR value of 15 or more
and should have a maximum swell of less than 1 percent when tested in accordance
with ASTM Test Designation D 1883 In addition it should contain less than 25 percent
passing the No 200 sieve Imported fill material should be tested by Geolabs Hawaii
for conformance with our recommendations prior to delivery to the project site for its
intended use To reduce additional loading on the walls of the drainage channel
excavation stockpiling of excavated on site and imported fill materials adjacent to the
drainage channel excavations should be avoided
All fills and backfills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in
loose thickness moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture and compacted
to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Fill placement should commence at
the lowest point and continue upward in level compacted lifts In areas to receive fill
where the existing ground surface is steeper than five horizontal to one vertical 5H 1V

Construction Precaution and Monitoring
The selected excavation shoring system if needed must comply with all
applicable safety requirements It is recommended that a qualified geotechnical
engineer be retained by the contractor to design and evaluate the shoring
system proposed
The adequacy and safety of the shoring installation should be the sole
responsibility of the contractor His representative who should be required to
be continuously present on site during excavation and construction work will
have the best opportunity to promptly observe any changed conditions during
construction such as unforeseen subsoils unexpectedly high groundwater table
inappropriate construction sequences and or techniques all of which may affect
shoring stability
However it should be noted that some movement of the shoring system and the
adjacent ground may still occur due to changes in earth stresses during
excavation The magnitude of movement is difficult to accurately estimate due
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3134 00 Page 16
to the complexity of the stress changes The magnitude depends greatly upon
workmanship such as how quickly and tightly the shoring and bracing supports
are installed and also on the subsoil conditions the size of the excavation and
the rate of excavation
Therefore it is important to realize that the excavation shoring should be
installed properly and as early as practical if necessary and that the adjacent
ground continuously monitored for cracks dips and or other indications of
movements with instruments such as settlement markers and inclinometer
Such monitoring is particularly critical in areas adjacent to existing structures and
utilities
We strongly recommend that the contractor retain a competent engineering
consultant to design and to coordinate the implementation of the excavation
support system where necessary

The impact and safety of the dewatering operations should be made the sole

Where encountered at the bottom of excavations permeable granular subsoils
may be susceptible to piping and quick conditions The dewatering operations
should be carried out without creating a quick condition or softening at the
excavation bottoms Therefore the project dewatering operations should be
done without pumping out soil fines pumping clear water only and should be
coordinated with shoring installation so that the excavation stability is not
adversely affected
Any groundwater draw down outside the excavation may cause settlement due
to compression and consolidation of potentially compressible soils Therefore
the use of a deep well system outside the excavation to draw down the
groundwater level should be avoided only allowed if the draw down of the
groundwater table would not affect the surrounding existing structures
Special caution should also be taken to avoid dewatering utility trenches
connected to excavations If this occurs the granular bedding and or backfill in
the utility trenches could act as subdrains and cause significant areal

Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling
eight borings to depths ranging from 14 5 to 21 5 feet below the existing ground surface
at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were drilled
using a truck mounted drill rig
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by
testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified
Soil Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic presentation of the materials
encountered are provided on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 8
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils
by driving a 3 inch O D Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling
30 inches The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the last 12 inches of an
