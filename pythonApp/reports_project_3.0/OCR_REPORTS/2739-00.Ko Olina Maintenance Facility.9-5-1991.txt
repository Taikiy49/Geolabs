INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Ko Olina Maintenance Facility in Ko Olina Resort Ewa on
the island of Oahu The general location of the site and its vicinity is shown on the
Project Location Map Piate 1 The exploration was performed on August 5 and 6 1991
in general accordance with our proposal to Pan Pacific Hoteliers Inc dated June 26
1991
This report summarizes the findings of exploration and our geotechnical engineering
recommendations derived from our analyses based on the data obtained during the
exploration and laboratory testing These recommendations are intended for design input
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2739 00 Page 2
and are not intended to form the basis for preparation of construction cost estimates or
for bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a one 1 level maintenance building with an asphaltic
concrete parking lot and miscellaneous supporting facilities at the site Based on
information provided to us slab on grade type of construction will be utilized for the
structure Minimal site grading is anticipated
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions at the project site in order to develop a generalized soil rock data set for the
formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to foundation and
pavement design of the project
The work was done in general accordance with our proposal to Pan Pacific
Hoteliers Inc dated The scope of work consisted of the following
1 Review of available soils and geologic information pertinent to the project
site and its vicinity
2 Reconnaissance of the project site and vicinity to make visual observations
of the surface soil conditions
3 Drilling and sampling seven 7 borings to depths of 7 5 to 21 5 feet below
the existing ground surface
4 Collection of one 1 near surface bulk sample for determination of
pavement design recommendations
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2738 00 Page 3
5 Laboratory testing of selected samples obtained during the field exploration
to determine the classification and engineering properties of the materials
encountered
6 Engineering analyses of the field and laboratory data for the purpose of
developing foundation and pavement design recommendations
7 Preparation of this report presenting the findings and design
recommendations
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration and laboratory testing are presented in
the appendices of this report
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located within the recently developed Ko Olina Resort area in
Ewa Beach on the island of Oahu The site is near the junction of Ali Nui Drive and the
secondary entrance road of the resort The site together with the proposed
development is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Currently the site is vacant The terrain is generally level with elevations ranging
from 28 to 30 feet above Mean Sea Level
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling seven 7 borings to
depths of 7 5 to 21 5 feet below the existing ground surface A near surface bulk sample
was also collected for laboratory testing for determination of pavement design thickness

The approximate locations of the borings and of the near surface bulk sample are shown
on the Site Plan Plate 2
The exploration results indicate that the subsoils consist predominantly of stiff to
very stiff clayey silt with sand and gravel to the maximum drilled depth of 21 5 feet It
appeared that the near surface material consists of compacted fill placed during site
development of the general area Groundwater was not encountered at the time of our
field exploration
Detailed descriptions of the materials encountered are presented on the Boring
Logs Plates A 1 through A 7 Laboratory testing was performed on selected samples
obtained The test results are presented in Appendix B
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Generai
The field exploration generally encountered stiff material consisting of clayey silt
with sand gravel and some cobbles and boulders at about five 5 feet below the existing
ground surface Minimum site grading is anticipated
It is Our Opinion that continuous wall footings and spread footing foundations may
be used for the proposed maintenance facility
Site Grading
Based on the grading pian provided cut and fill of up to one 1 foot is anticipated
on the northern and southern portions of the site

W O 2739 00 Page 5
At the start of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
cleared of vegetation debris rubbish and other deleterious materials These grubbed
materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site
The area should then be scarified to a depth of six 6 inches moisture conditioned
to about 3 above the optimum moisture content and recompacted to about of 90 of
the maximum dry density as determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Closer control of the moisture contents during moisture conditioning is
recommended in the southwestern portion of the site where expansive soils may be
anticipated as indicated by Boring No 4 and laboratory swell test results In this area
as delineated on Plate 2 the moisture contents of the scarified subgrade soil should be
wetted to between three 3 to five 5 percent above the optinum moisture content prior
to re compaction to about 90 relative density Alternatively the upper one 1 foot of
ansive soil may be removed and properly disposed of off site Off site non
expansive select borrow fill should be used to backfill the above over excavation
Any loose or soft spots encountered should be over excavated down to firm natural
ground and the resulting depression backfilled with moist fill material properly compacted
to a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density as determined in accordance with
ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
The material resulting from excavation in the northern portion of the site is
acceptable for use as fill in the southern portion of the site
Any imported fill material that may be required should consist of select
non expansive granular materials iess than three 3 inches in maximum size with iess

than 20 passing the No 200 sieve All imported fill material should be tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to delivery to the site
All fill material should be placed in maximum 10 inch thick loose level lifts and
compacted to a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density as determined by ASTM
Test Method D 1557 78
The site should be graded to drain water away from the foundations
Foundation Design
Spread or continuous footings may be used for the proposed new one level
structure It is anticipated that the footings will bear on stiff in situ material or compacted
fill
A minimum footing embedment of eighteen 18 inches below the lowest ad
finished grade is recommended An allowable bearing value up to 3000 p s f may be
used for foundation design The bearing value is for total dead plus live loads and may
be increased by one third when temporary wind and seismic loads are considered
if foundations are located next to sewer drainage trenches they should be
embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane extending
upward from the bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility
lines The existing materials encountered at the bottom of footing excavations should be
scarified to a minimum depth of six 6 inches moisture conditioned and recompacted to
a minimum of 95 of the maximum dry density as determined by ASTM Test Method D
1557 78
W O 2739 00 Page 7
The prepared footing bearing surfaces should be observed by the project
geotechnical engineer to determine if adequate bearing has been obtained prior to
placement of reinforcement and concrete For footings properly constructed and observed
as recommended above the estimated settlements are on the order of less than one half
inch total and one fourth inch differential
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive earth pressure
developed against the buried vertical faces of the foundation system and by frictional
resistance developed between the bottom of the foundations and the bearing soil
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be calculated using an equivalent
fluid pressure of 300 p c f This assumes that the soil around the foundation is well
compacted A coefficient of friction of 0 40 may be used Unless covered by pavements
or slabs the passive resistance in the upper twelve 12 inches of the soils should be
neglected
Slab On Grade
For the design of slab on grade floors the use of four 4 inch thick concrete slabs
placed over a four 4 inch layer of 3B Fine gravel or ASTM C 33 No 67 gravel cushion
is recommended To protect against moisture damage of the floor covering material a
moisture barrier such as a polyethylene membrane should be used below the slabs A
minimum of two 2 inch thick iayer of sand should also be placed over the membrane
prior to concrete placement to aid in the curing of the concrete
it is critical that the slab s subgrade soils should be kept moist prior to compaction
of the subgrade materials and subsequent placement of gravel suchion layer and
concrete The thickened edges of the on grade slabs should extend a minimum of twelve
12 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grades

Pavement Sections
Asphaltic concrete pavement is anticipated for the ground level parking Based on
the above anticipated subsoil conditions the following preliminary pavement section may
be used for design
2 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 Inch Base Course
8 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
on Moist Compacted Subgrade
Laboratory CBR tests should be performed on the actual subgrade soils during
construction to verify the adequacy of the above sections The above pavement section
assumes medium traffic and that adequate drainage will be provided in the paved area
Utilities
New underground utility lines may be anticipated for the project A granular
bedding consisting of six 6 inches of 3B Fine or ASTM C 33 No 67 gravel is
recommended under the pipes
Granular materials should be used for trench backfill The granular backfill up to
about one 1 foot above the pipes should be lightly tamped to reduce the possibility of
damaging the pipes
The subsequent upper portion of the trench backfill from the level one 1 foot
above the pipes to the finish grade should be placed in maximum 10 inch horizontal loose
lifts mechanically compacted to a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density as
determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 to protect against potential future ground
subsidence

Design Review
Drawings and specifications for the proposed construction should be forwarded
to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comments prior to construction This
review is needed to determine adherence to the earthwork and _ foundation
recommendations given herein if this review is not made the geotechnical engineer
cannot assume responsibility for misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
lt is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during site grading and construction of the foundations
This is to observe compliance with the design concepts specifications or
recommendations and to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions
differ from those anticipated at the time of preparation of this report The
recommendations given in this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and approved by
the geotechnical engineer prior to delivery to the site
if actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during construction are different
from those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made

information obtained from field borings Variations of conditions between the field borings
may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident until

estimated from contours shown on the same plan The physical locations and elevations

methods used

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by drilling seven 7
borings to depths of 7 5 to 21 5 feet below the existing ground surface at the approximate
locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 A truck mounted drill rig was used to drill the
borings
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer who continuously monitored the drilling
operations The classifications were further reviewed by visual inspection and testing in
the laboratory All soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A summary of the materials encountered are presented on the
Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 7
Soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving a 2 inch standard split
spoon sampler or a 2 4 inch D split barrel sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30
inches The blow counts used to drive the sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the
Boring Logs at the appropriate sample depths
