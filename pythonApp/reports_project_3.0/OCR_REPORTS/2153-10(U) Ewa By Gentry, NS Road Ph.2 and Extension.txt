Earthwork Observation and Testing Report
North South Road Phase 2 and Extension
Ewa by Gentry
This report summarizes the results of our geotechnical engineering services
performed during the roadway and utility construction at the above referenced project
from July 1994 through April 1995 The North South Road Extension Project is located
approximately 900 feet to the north of of Geiger Road within the Ewa by
Gentry development in the District of Ewa on the island of Oahu Hawaii
The North South Road Phase 2 begins at which is near the
intersection with Kahiuka Street Road A Area 7 and extends north to about
The Extension project begins at and ends at near
the existing Railroad Right of Way The approximate location of the North South Road
Extension and Phase 2 project is shown on the Site Plan Plate 1 The roadway and
utility construction was performed by Kiewit Pacific Company Excavation depths on
the order of 19 feet were observed during the construction The excavated materials
were generally used to backfill the utility trench and generally consisted of sandy coral
gravel and silty clay
During the backfill operation and roadway construction various on site soils were
tested prior to their use in the field The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture
contents were established in our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test results are summarized on Plate 2
of this report

During the backfill operations the backfill materials were generally placed in
near level lifts brought to near optinum moisture content and compacted to a
minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density determined in accordance with
American Society of Testing and Material ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified
Proctor The underpass culvert backfill material the road subgrade and base course
materials were compacted to a minimum of 95 percent of the maximum dry density
Periodic field density tests were taken during the backfill operations and roadway
construction in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 2922 Nuclear Gauge
Method The results of the compaction tests are summarized on Plates 3 1 through
3 7 of this report
Field density test locations were selected to confirm field observations as
variations in construction procedures and soil conditions were noted Test locations
and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes
These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree
implied by the methods used Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until
the specified minimum compaction were obtained
Our services consisted of periodic field observations of the contractor s backfill
placement and compaction procedures during the above construction Field
observations made by our field technician during the utility backfill operation and
roadway construction for the project indicated that this work was performed in general
accordance with the project plans and specifications
LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore there is a possibility that
some work may have occurred without our observation However we believe that the
level of utility trench backfill and roadway construction observation and testing services
provided is reasonable in reducing the geotechnical risks to the project It should also
be recognized that there are variations in the accuracy and statistical repeatability of
the tests and methods used to observe the trench backfill operation and roadway
construction Our services were performed in accordance with generally accepted
standards of geotechnical engineering practice no warranty is expressed or implied
Provision of construction observation by an engineer is not insurance nor does it
guarantee construction of any type The contractor shall retain responsibility for the
