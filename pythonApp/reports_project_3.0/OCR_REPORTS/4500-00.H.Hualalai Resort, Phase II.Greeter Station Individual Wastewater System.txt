This letter report presents the results of our percolation test performed in support
of the design of the Greeter Station Individual Wastewater System for Hualalai Resort
Project Considerations
The Hualalai Resort project site is located about 8 5 miles northeast of the Keahole
International Airport in the Kaupulehu area within the District of North Kona on the Island
of Hawaii As part of the Phase I development of Hualalai Resort we understand that
various infrastructure improvements and residential parcels will be constructed We
understand that it is desired to construct an individual wastewater system for the
Greeter Station for Hualalai Resort We also understand that the State of Hawaii
Department of Health requires performance of a percolation test prior to construction of
the individual wastewater system Therefore it was desired to conduct a field percolation
test at the proposed leaching field location to obtain data pertaining to the preliminary
design of the wastewater disposal system
Percolation Test
One field percolation test was performed at the proposed Greeter Station site
The field percolation test was performed in general accordance with the State of Hawaii
Systems and Chapter 10 of the Ten State s Standards to evaluate the permeability of
the subsurface soils for wastewater disposal The test was performed in a 3 5 inch
diameter borehole drilled to a depth of about 9 5 feet below the existing ground surface
Approximately 1 inch of gravel was then placed at the bottom of the borehole to protect
the bottom of the hole from scouring and sediments During testing the hole was
carefully filled with water to a depth of about 6 inches above the gravel and refilled as
necessary The time intervals and water drops were then recorded to provide data
upon which the calculation of the percolation test was based Based on the percolation
