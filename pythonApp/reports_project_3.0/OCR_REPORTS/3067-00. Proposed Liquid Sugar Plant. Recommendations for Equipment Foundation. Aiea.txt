Recommendations for Equipment Foundation
Proposed Liquid Sugar Plant Phase 2
Aiea Oahu Hawaii
We understand that Phase 2 of the Proposed Liquid Sugar Plant project would
consist of the foundation work for the Evaporator and Liquor Tank structures
It is our opinion that the recommendations presented in our geotechnical
engineering report entitled Geotechnical Engineering Exploration California and Hawaiian
Sugar Company Proposed Liquid Sugar Plant Aiea Oahu Hawaii dated February 17
1993 are applicable for the proposed equipment foundations design with the following
modifications
If the existing compacted granular fills are encountered within three feet 3 from
the perimeter of the new equipment foundations we recommend that the new select
granular fill should extend further and tie into the existing compacted granular fills
Due to the tight construction schedule for the subject project we understand that
the Contractor is particularly concerned with the potential weakening of the compacted
bearing surfaces when subjected to prolonged exposure of rainy weather This may
cause a delay in the construction work with the subsequent recompaction work involved
Therefore it is our opinion that more gravelly basaltic type of base course material may
be used in lieu of the current imported material from Sand Island for the upper one foot
1 of the new select granular fill We believe the cleaner basaltic base course material
with less fines may provide a more competent bearing surface under rainy weather and
this material may help to expedite the project construction
