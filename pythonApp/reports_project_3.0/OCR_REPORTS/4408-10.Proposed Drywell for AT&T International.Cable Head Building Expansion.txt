GEOTECHNICAL ENGINEERING EXPLORATION
PROPOSED DRYWELL FOR AT T INTERNATIONAL
CABLE HEAD BUILDING EXPANSION
Dear Mr Koga
In accordance with our fee proposal dated May 25 2001 we have recently
conducted a geotechnical engineering exploration for the proposed drywell for the AT T
International Cable Head Building Expansion project This letter report summarizes our
work performed on the project and presents our geotechnical engineering
recommendations derived from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering
analyses The findings and recommendations presented herein are subject to the
limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to build a drywell to contain stormwater for the AT T International
Cable Head Building Expansion Facility located in Makaha on the Island of Oahu Hawaii
The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map
Plate 1 The proposed site is an undeveloped area at the eastern end of the parking lot
An on site cesspool presently exists west of the parking lot Structural details for the
proposed drywell were not available at the time this report was prepared Based on the
grading and drainage plan for the project the existing ground elevation at the proposed
drywell site is about 42 feet Mean Sea Level MSL
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions at the proposed drywell site were explored by drilling
and sampling one boring extending to depth of approximately 20 feet below the existing
ground surface The approximate location of the boring is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2

The boring generally encountered a surface fill layer approximately 6 5 feet in
thickness consisting of very stiff clay with sand gravel cobbles and boulders The
surface fill layer was underlain by a colluvial soil deposit consisting of very stiff clay with
sand gravel cobbles and boulders extending to the depth explored of about 20 feet
below the existing ground surface Groundwater was not encountered in the boring at the
time of our field exploration
Detailed descriptions of the materials encountered in the boring are presented on
the attached Log of Boring Plate A 1 of Appendix A Results of the laboratory tests
performed on selected soil samples are presented in Appendix B
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration generally encountered very stiff clay with sand gravel cobbles
and boulders Groundwater was not encountered in the boring at the time of our field
exploration
One falling head percolation test was conducted in the boring drilled to provide
data for design of the proposed drywell planned at the site The percolation test was
performed at a depth of about 20 feet below the existing ground surface to evaluate the
permeability characteristics of the fill and colluvial materials The result of the
percolation test performed at the site is presented in Appendix C of this report
The percolation test indicated that the fill and colluvial materials generally have a
coefficient of permeability k of about 8 9 x 10 feet per minute which corresponds to
about 4 5 x 10 centimeters per second Based on the results of the field percolation test
we believe that an average percolation rate of about 0 1 gallon per square foot per day
may be assumed for design of the drywell Since the percolation rate of the subsoil is very
low it appears that a drywell would not be feasible for stormwater removal Other
methods for storm water removal such as retention tanks or connecting to existing
drainage structures should be evaluated
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part
upon information obtained from the field boring Variations of subsoil conditions beyond
the boring may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become
evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be
necessary to re evaluate the recommendations provided in this report
