FINDINGS AND DISCUSSIONS
Based on our field exploration results the boring drilled at the perimeter sidewalk
around the house structure at the northeastern corner encountered moderately
expansive clays extending to more than 10 feet below the existing sidewalk slab
surface The expansive clays generally exhibited moisture contents in the mid
20 percent However the expansive clays encountered in the boring drilled at the
central portion of the house structure near the hallway is only on the order of about 5
feet thick and is underlain by hard coral formation In addition the moisture contents of
the expansive clays encountered at this location are only in the mid to high teens which

are lower when compared to those encountered along the perimeter of the house
structure
Based on the field exploration results and our evaluation we believe that part of
the observed distresses may be attributed to moisture infiltration into the subsurface
soils around the perimeter of the home The water infiltration or excess moisture once
trapped in the subsoils underneath the sidewalk and the perimeter of the house
structure are not able to evaporate as a result of the protective cover provided by the
concrete sidewalk slab Due to the clayey nature of the subsoils the clayey soils exhibit
swelling properties and are susceptible to softening upon wetting The main cause of
expansion in clayey soils is the tendency of individual soil particles to attract and hold
water Clay particles have a net negative electrical charge and attract positively
charged ions present in the soil water matrix In the attempt to attract positive ions
many layers of water molecules are held by the clay particle These layers of adsorbed
water force individual clay particles away from each other resulting in soil expansion
It appears that swelling of the underlying clayey soils has likely resulted in the
lifting of the perimeter building walls which in turn causes the roof trusses to move up
Since the interior non bearing drywall is not attached to the roof trusses this has
resulted in the gap opening observed along the ceiling line of the interior drywall In
general the lifting of the perimeter building walls should not result in significant
distresses to the house structure as those observed at the residence However we
believe that the dowelling of the sidewalk slab into the exterior building wall coupled
with the significant difference in the thickness of the expansive clays encountered along
the perimeter as compared to the central portion of the house structure have resulted in
more pronounced lifting at the perimeter walls resulting in the appreciable elevation
difference across the floor slab and the distresses observed at the subject residence
We wish to point out that this significant difference in the thickness of the expansive
clays within such a short distance between the perimeter and central portion of the
house structure may be considered abnormal and localized in nature
Because the distresses observed at the subdivision are attributed to the
shrinkage and or swelling behavior of the expansive clayey soils it is important that the
remedial measures are directed at stabilizing the moisture content of the expansive
clayey soils Stabilizing the moisture content of the expansive clayey soils should
reduce the potential for further shrinkage and or swelling of the underlying clayey soils
In order to reduce the potential for moisture fluctuations underneath the sidewalk and
house structure we have recommended that consideration be given to chemical
stabilization of the expansive soils at the site
REMEDIAL PROGRAM USING EcSS 3000 CHEMICAL STABILIZATION
Based on the available information we believe that chemical stabilization using the
Electrochemical Soil Stabilizer EcSS 3000 may be an effective remedial measure at the
residence In general the EcSS 3000 chemical stabilization involves the injection of a
diluted chemical diluted in water into the ground to chemically stabilize the expansive

Hawaii California

May 17 2005
nature of the soils Information from the product supplier indicates that the chemical
injection reacts with the clay particles in the soil to form a soil mass that is less susceptible
to shrink swell effects when subjected to moisture fluctuations EcSS 3000 formerly
known as ISS 2500 is a mixture of naphthalene oil sulfur trioxide sulfuric acid and other
additives A proprietary resin catalyst is also added to enhance the reaction times The
ion exchange solution is mixed with water and introduced into the subsoils under pressure
through injection points drilled at a closely spaced grid pattern
The EcSS 3000 electrochemical soil stabilizer creates a cation rich environment
in the injected zone and the individual clay particle s negative charge is effectively
neutralized The structure of the clay particles is changed physically and chemically
from an expanding crystalline lattice to a non crystalline amorphous structure to greatly
reduce the ability of the clay particle to attract and hold water This results in a great
reduction in the shrink swell potential of the clay soils
Based on our recommendation chemical stabilization using the Electrochemical
Soil Stabilizer EcSS 3000 was performed to reduce the swelling potential of the on site
expansive soils at the lot The soil stabilization operation was performed by Structural
Systems Inc the authorized local distributor of the EcSS 3000 Electrochemical Soil
Stabilizer on December 14 and 15 2004 Prior to the injection operation a grid pattern
consisting of a double row spaced approximately 3 feet apart in a staggered pattern
was laid out along the sidewalk surrounding the residence Hand held injection rods
were manually forced downward with a hammer in approximately 12 inch vertical
intervals up to a depth of about 7 feet below the sidewalk slab surface or upon refusal
at the injection points We understand that a diluted EcSS solution at a ratio of about
3 percent 1 gallon of chemical to 300 gallons of water was injected at each one foot
interval into the underlying expansive soils at a pressure of about 200 to 250 pounds
per square inch psi The drilled ports were sealed with grout upon completion of the
work
After completion of the chemical stabilization program crack survey readings
were continued on a periodic basis to evaluate the effectiveness of the remedial
measure implemented at the subject residence Based on the crack gauge readings
obtained through our distress monitoring program implemented at Lot No 174 of the
Kapolei Village 6 subdivision it appears that observable movement in all the crack
gauges installed at the residence has stopped Therefore we believe that the chemical
stabilization process using the Electrochemical Soil Stabilizer EcSS 3000 conducted
around the residence has effectively stabilized the moisture content of the expansive
clayey soils As a result it is our opinion that the repair work planned at the subject
residence may proceed accordingly Consideration should be given to the use of crown
molding along the ceiling line that may mask any residual movement if occurs
