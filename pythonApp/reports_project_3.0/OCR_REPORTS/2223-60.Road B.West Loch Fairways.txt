In accordance with your request we have conducted a limited geotechnical
engineering exploration at the site to obtain subsurface information pertaining to pumping
problems encountered at the subgrade level in Road B between AW 1 and the
intersection of Road B and Road C The following summarizes our current findings and
presents our recommendations
Findings
Three 3 of the through 3 were drilled and sampled in the
subgrade of Road B at Stations 90 and 7 04 respectively to obtain samples
of the utility trench backfills Boring 4 was drilled and sampled in Lot 119 adjacent to
Station 5 75 of Road B to provide a comparison with the existing soil conditions at the
trench site The logs of the borings are shown on the attached Plates A 1 through A 4
Aoisture content determinations and unit weight determinations were performed
on the samples obtained from the borings The results of these tests are shown on the
boring logs at the appropriate sample depths In general it was noted that the moisture
contents were in excess of the reported optimum moisture content of 20 6 of the dry
weight and that the soil sample recovered from the borings indicated densities range from
about 78 to about 106 pounds per cubic foot It was also noted that the moisture

Observation and Recommendations
It is Our Opinion that the subgrade pumping is the result of the relatively high in
place moisture contents of the soils It is not evident whether these high moisture
contents are the result of saturation of the soils during the recent wet weather or the
result of a capillary or artisan rise of moisture from the underlying wet soils However it
is Our opinion that it is not feasible to stabilize these subgrade soils at their current
moisture contents and densities
Therefore we recommend that one of the following actions be taken to stabilize
the road subgrade
1 Re excavate the utility tlenches and replace the moist to wet clayey soils
with a layer of capillary break gravel such as 3B Fine aggregate up to 1
foot above water level and select granular material placed in 8 inch loose
horizontal lifts and compacted to 90 of the maximum dry density
established by ASTM Test Method D1557 78
2 Follow the contractor s recommendation to overexcavate the existing
subgrade level by another 12 inches and backfill the overexcavated area
with a layer of geotextile and an additional 12 inches of subbase course
In our opinion the geotextile should be a woven material such as Mirafi 500x The
first six 6 inches of additional subbase course backfill should be rolled a minimum of 6
to 8 passes by a heavy roller without using vibration The remaining subbase course
should continue to be compacted to obtain 90 relative density We recommend that the
soils engineer be present to observe the above remedial operations and to further
examine the exposed subgrade conditions
