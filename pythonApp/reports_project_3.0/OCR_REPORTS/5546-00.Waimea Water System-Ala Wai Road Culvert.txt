1 1 Introduction
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the evaluation of possible construction methods to install a new water line
across an existing culvert on Ala Wai Road in the Waimea area on the Island of Kaua i
Hawai i The construction methods evaluated consist of trenchless open cut trench
and mini bridge installation The project location and general vicinity are shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings from our field exploration and presents our
geotechnical recommendations derived from our analyses for the water line project The
recommendations are intended to assist in evaluating the preferred construction method
to traverse the existing culvert The findings and recommendations presented herein are
subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report
1 2 Project Considerations
The project involves the construction of a new water main crossing at an existing
twin pipe drainage culvert as part of the Waimea Water System Improvements project in
Waimea on the Island of Kaua i Hawai i The existing culvert encompasses a shallow
drainage ditch which crosses Ala Wai Road An 8 inch diameter water line will be
installed to replace the existing 4 inch water line
We understand that trenchless open cut trench and mini bridge installation
methods are being considered for the new water line installation across an existing
culvert The project site is near the mouth of the large alluvial valley containing the
Waimea River drainage Therefore the project site is underlain by significant recent
alluvial sediments and shallow depth groundwater

2 1 Regional Geology
The Island of Kaua i is essentially a dissected basaltic dome that was initially
formed by a single large shield volcano which has subsequently been deeply eroded
and partially covered with later younger volcanic materials The Island of Kaua i is the
oldest of the main Hawaiian Islands and today encompasses approximately 555 square
miles in area with a maximum elevation of about 5 170 feet above Mean Sea Level
MSL at Mt Wai ale ale near the center of the island It is believed that during the
Tertiary Period about 5 to 6 million years ago in the Pliocene Epoch basaltic lavas
belonging to the Waimea Canyon Volcanic Series built a roughly circular island volcano
from the ocean floor that was estimated to have been about 20 000 feet deep at the
time Near the end of the Pliocene Epoch this main shield building volcanic phase
came to a halt
Following a long period of erosion and island mass subsidence volcanism was
renewed during the early Pleistocene Epoch about 1 5 million years ago with the
eruption of the Koloa Volcanic Series from multiple vents throughout the island This
post erosional volcanic activity laid a veneer of fresh lava and interbedded volcanic
sediments over the older deposits of the Waimea Canyon Volcanic Series Because of
the long period of erosion between the eruption of the Waimea Canyon Volcanic Series
and the Koloa Volcanic Series deposits of the Koloa Volcanic Series typically filled the
erosional valleys and depressions that existed at that time Consequently the basaltic
rocks of the Koloa Volcanic Series are commonly found overlying thick residual soil and
alluvial deposits
The dominant geologic processes of weathering landform erosion and sediment
re deposition followed during the late Pleistocene and Recent Epochs Combined with
the erosional and depositional effects of the Pleistocene sea level fluctuations which
occurred in response to the worldwide advance and retreat of the continental ice sheets
the Island of Kaua i has evolved to its present form

Horizontal Directional Drilling Methods
Horizontal directional drilling HDD is a trenchless steerable installation method
using a multi axis drilling machine to bore a small diameter pilot hole in the
subsurface The pilot hole is bored using either controlled fluid jetting or
fluid assisted mechanical cutting or a combination of the two methods Depending
on the subsurface conditions water only or water mixed with bentonite and or
polymer can be used as a drilling fluid in the directional drilling application The pilot
hole is reamed as necessary to accommodate the pipe to be installed The pipe is
pulled back into the reamed hole by the drilling machine Most HDD systems use
slurry to stabilize the walls of the borehole and to reduce the frictional drag on the
pipeline being installed Steering corrections are made in the soils by rotating the
slanted cutter shoe to the desired orientation and then advancing the shoe and
string without rotation
In general the HDD pilot hole is drilled nearly horizontal with entry and exit angles
between 8 and 15 degrees measured from horizontal Shallow working pits may be
required at each end of the bore to allow placement of the drill pipe and to allow
transition to the conventional pipe trench installation The product pipe should be
installed in one continuous operation preferably without pause and should
protrude a sufficient distance beyond the entry and exit points to allow proper
termination or connection at a later time Typically high density polyethylene
HDPE steel and polyvinyl chloride PVC pipes may be installed by HDD
methods

The vertical and horizontal alignments of the drill hole are typically monitored from
the surface by a locator which tracks signals from the transmitter contained inside
the drill head housing Other methods of tracking the cutting tool such as in hole
steering are generally recommended to provide more precise line and grade
information The contractor should carefully monitor the drilling operation when
approaching property boundaries and existing underground utilities or structures to
assure that proper clearances are maintained
The feasibility of the HDD technique should be established based on ground
conditions diameter and length of the pipe or duct line and the required achievable
degree of accuracy in both alignment and grade HDD is less attractive where
precise line and grade tolerances are required in soils containing cobbles and
boulders in a soil matrix especially with larger diameter pipelines The HDD
method is best suited for soils with some cohesion i e clays However the
method has been successfully used in granular soils by adding bentonite to the
drilling fluid to aid in the borehole stability The HDD method is not particularly well
suited for gravelly soils greater than 50 percent gravel size particles due to
difficulties in drilling the pilot hole and maintaining the stability of the hole The use
of HDD methods in heterogeneous ground conditions may be difficult and
additional care is required in the selection of suitable HDD equipment to maintain
the precise alignment and grade of the bore

b Pipe Size and Material
For the HDD method of water line installation the most important design
consideration for the pipe is the tensile stresses imposed on the pipe
when it is pulled into place through the drilled hole The magnitude of
these tensile stresses is a function of the length of the installation as well
as the subsurface conditions Other important pipe design considerations
are the internal and external pressures acting on the pipe during and after
installation In addition the selected pipe material must have adequate
corrosion resistance or be protected to resist internal and external
corrosion
We envision that HDPE pipes will be used if the water line is installed by
HDD methods HDPE pipes generally have low tensile strength and may
be susceptible to failure during installation unless they are properly
designed This limits the length of installation that can be completed with
an HDPE pipe Therefore it is critical that the pipe be selected with an
adequate allowable pulling tensile capacity Pipe stress analyses should
be performed to evaluate whether an HDPE pipe can withstand the pulling
stresses during pipe installation and the external pressures hydrostatic
and soil on the installed pipeline
The HDPE pipe should be selected to provide the maximum pipe
thickness and strength for the proposed installation based on the results of
the pipe stress analyses considerations regarding HDD installation
procedures probable drill hole sizes and materials in filling the annulus
between the HDD installed HDPE pipeline and the drilled hole The
contractor should adequately and properly ballast the pipelines during
installation into the HDD drill hole to reduce the pipe weight the effect of
buoyancy and the corresponding reduction in the tensile loads on the pipe
string during pullback Finally HDPE pipes should conform to the
requirements of AWWA C 906 Pressure Class 125 as a minimum

3 2 Open Cut Trench Installation
The conventional open cut trench construction method consists of excavating a
trench along the proposed waterline alignment laying the pipe in the trench and
backfilling the trench The installation of waterlines by open trench construction
methods requires personnel to enter the trench Because of the soft loose and
permeable subsoil conditions combined with the shallow groundwater condition at the
site a trench shoring system and significant trench dewatering will be required
Because earth loads acting upon water pipes will increase rapidly with the trench width
the trench width should be kept at a minimum

Pipe Settlement
Primary settlement of the new pipe installation is generally caused by the difference
in the unit weight of the lighter excavated original earth and the heavier compacted
backfill material placed over the pipeline The net increase in loading may cause
settlement of the underlying subsurface soils below the trench invert Based on our

subgrade stabilization layer To reduce the differential settlements between pipe
segments the pipe segments may be supported on a pile foundation

the amount of dewatering within the excavations It should be noted that the
excavations will likely encounter very soft soil deposits at the bottom of the
excavation Therefore there is a potential for bottom heave in these very soft soil

We anticipate that the concrete support structure footings may be constructed near

To reduce settlements micropiles may be used to support the bridge foundations
In general the micropile foundation system consists of a small diameter pile
usually less than 12 inches drilled and grouted with steel reinforcing The
micropile foundation is typically constructed by drilling a borehole with casing
placing reinforcing steel in the hole and grouting the borehole Micropiles are
desired because they can be readily installed in access restrictive environments
and in numerous soil types and ground conditions In addition installation of
micropiles generally causes minimal disturbance to adjacent structures adjacent
soils and the environment
Based on the subsurface conditions anticipated at the project site we estimate that
a micropile system with a grout bulb diameter of 7 inches or larger may be used to
support the new structures For preliminary design we recommend designing each
micropile based on an allowable compressive load capacity of 10 kips This
capacity may be increased by up to 50 percent for transient loads such as wind or
seismic forces
To provide an allowable compressive load capacity of 10 kips we recommend
extending the micropile through the loose and or soft deposits to a depth of about
40 feet The micropiles will derive vertical support primarily from skin friction
between the grout and the surrounding alluvial deposits Higher load capacity may
be obtained by increasing the pile embedment depth The design size and depth of
the micropiles will be determined when the structural loads are available
