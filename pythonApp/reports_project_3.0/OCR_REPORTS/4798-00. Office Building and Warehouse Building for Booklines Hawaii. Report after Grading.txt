Dear Mr Yahiku
This report summarizes our compaction testing services performed during the site
grading foundation preparation utility line installation and pavement construction for the
Warehouse and Office Building project for Booklines Hawaii located within the Mililani
Technology Park in the Mililani area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The compaction
testing services for the project was performed on an on call basis from
through
COMPACTION TESTING SERVICES
The earthwork operations for the Warehouse and Office Building project for
Booklines Hawaii were performed by Jayar Construction Inc In general the majority of
the earthwork operations involved clearing and grubbing and placement of 12 inches of
non expansive select granular fill materials below the building pad footprint
Samples of the on site and imported materials utilized as fill and backfill materials
were obtained and transported to our laboratory for testing The maximum dry densities
and optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general accordance
with American Society of Testing and Material ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified
Proctor The test results are summarized on Plate 1 of this report
Following clearing and grubbing operations the existing ground was generally
over excavated to approximately 12 inches below the finished subgrades in the building
pad areas to provide the recommended 12 inch thick non expansive select granular fill
layer The over excavated subgrades were moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent
above the optimum moisture content and compacted to at least 90 percent of the
maximum dry density as determined in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557
Modified Proctor

During placement of the non expansive select granular fill layer the fill materials
were generally placed in near level lifts brought to above the optimum moisture content
and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent of the maximum dry density determined in
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor
Utility trench backfills were generally placed in near level lifts brought to above the
optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry
density determined in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor
Aggregate base and subbase courses were generally placed in near level lifts brought to
above the optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent of the maximum
dry density
Periodic field density tests were performed on an on call basis during the fill and
backfill operations for conformance with the compaction requirements stated in the project
plans and specifications A total of 113 field density tests including retests of failed areas
were performed using the nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM Test
Designations D 2922 and D 3017 The results of the compaction tests performed are
summarized on Plates 2 1 through 2 6 of this report These tests were performed on the
prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills at the site
Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from
on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only
to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were
reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained and or approved by our
field representative based on visual observation and hand probing
Based on the results of the referenced tests it is our professional opinion that the
earthwork operations during the site grading foundation preparation utility line installation
and pavement construction for the project tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this
report were performed in general conformance with the compaction requirements of the
project plans and specifications
LIMITATIONS
It should be recognized that there are variations in the accuracy and statistical
repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the earthwork construction and
materials Our services were performed in accordance with generally accepted standards
of geotechnical engineering practice no warranty is expressed or implied Compaction
testing by an engineer is not insurance nor does it guarantee construction of any type
The contractor shall retain responsibility for the quality of the work for adhering to the
plans and specifications and for repairing defects regardless of when they are found
