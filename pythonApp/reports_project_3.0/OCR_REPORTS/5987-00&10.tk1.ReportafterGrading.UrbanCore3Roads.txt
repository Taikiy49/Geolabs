Dear Mr Higham
This report summarizes our earthwork observation and testing services performed
during the grading and backfilling work for the Kapolei City Urban Core 3 Roads project
from March 12 2008 through March 18 2016
EARTHWORK OPERATIONS
Based on the information provided the project involved clearing and grubbing of
the original ground installation of various underground utilities construction of concrete
curb and gutter and installation of the roadway pavement sections It should be noted that
Aina Nui Corporation

testing for concrete asphalt paving and asphalt concrete base were specifically excluded
from our scope of work for this project The layout of the new roadway alignments is
shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
Delta Construction Company is the general contractor who also performed the
earthwork for the project The earthwork operation consisted of clearing and grubbing
within the project limits over excavation and fill placement preparation of pavement
subgrade placement and compaction of aggregate subbase and cushion fill and utility
trench excavation and backfill
Following the clearing and grubbing operation the roadway areas were generally
over excavated to a depth ranging from about 27 5 to 35 inches below the pavement
finished grades to provide the recommended pavement structural sections for the Kapolei
City Urban Core 3 Roads Prior to the placement of the aggregate subbase the pavement
subgrade was scarified to a depth of about 8 inches moisture conditioned to above the
optimum moisture and recompacted to a minimum of 95 percent of the maximum dry
density determined in accordance with ASTM D1557 Modified Proctor
The imported coralline material used as a source of aggregate subbase in the
pavement structural sections was moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture
content and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Utility trench
backfills were generally placed in near level lifts brought to about 2 percent above the
optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
Where trenches were below pavement areas the upper 3 feet of the trench backfill below
the pavement finished grade were compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative
compaction
During the site grading operations samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D1557 Modified Proctor and the test
results are summarized on Plate 2
Subsequent to the sidewalk construction distresses such as heaving of the
sidewalk slab separation of the sidewalk slab from the curb and development of
shrinkage crack at the exposed coral capping fill materials adjacent to the sidewalk slab
were observed at various locations along the Urban Core 3 Roads A geotechnical
engineering evaluation program was undertaken to explore the subsurface conditions
underneath the distressed sidewalks in an attempt to evaluate the probable cause s of
the observed distresses Our findings and discussions were presented in our report
entitled Geotechnical Engineering Exploration Sidewalk Distress Evaluation Ala Kahawai
Street Kapolei Oahu Hawaii dated May 26 2015
Some of the sidewalk slabs were subsequently removed to expose the coralline
capping fill materials The capping fill materials were scarified aerated and recompacted
prior to the placement of the new concrete slab In addition a portion of the new sidewalk
slabs was thickened along the edge and dowelled into the concrete curb to reduce the

Field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations on an
as need basis to confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures
and soil conditions were noted A total of 1 403 field density tests including retest of failed
area were performed using the nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM D6938
Results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 76 of
this report These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on
constructed fills and backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by
pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should
be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed
to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the earthwork operations during the site grading and
infrastructure installation for the above project observed and tested by Geolabs in the
period covered by this report were performed in general accordance with the intent of the
project plans and specifications and our geotechnical recommendations presented in the
above referenced reports and subsequent recommendations
LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork were
performed without our observation However the level of earthwork observation and
testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some of the
geotechnical risks to the project It should be recognized that there are variations in the
accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor
earthwork construction and materials
