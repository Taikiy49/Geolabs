INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the pile foundation repair of the existing
Wharf K 10 Naval Supply Center Pearl Harbor Oahu Hawaii The
approximate location of the project site is shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of the exploration was to study the subsurface
conditions at two broken pile locations at Wharf K 10 to provide
pertinent soil parameters for the design of the replacement piles

PROJECT CONSTDERATIONS
It is our understanding that several broken piles and various
miscellaneous pile foundation damage were discovered along the subject
Wharf K 10
It is proposed to install new piles to replace the two existing
broken piles located at the junctions of pile lines 1 and K and 6 and
D for the repair of the wharf The broken piles were designed to
support an allowable vertical compression load of 25 tons
The proposed replacement piles will consist of two vertical and
one slightly battered inch octagonal prestressed concrete piles
The two vertical replacement piles will be driven on each side of
the broken pile at the junction of the pile lines 6 and D and will
thus support one half of the allowable vertical load of 25 tons The
single batter replacement pile will be driven adjacent to the west side
of the broken pile at the junction of the pile lines 1 and K and will
support approximately the full allowable vertical load of 25 tons It
is our understanding that the pile will batter at one half of an inch
per foot away from the adjacent broken pile to reduce the chance of
hitting the broken pile

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located at the western side of Wharf K 10 in
the Naval Supply Center of Pearl Harbor Oahu Hawaii
The existing wharf is a concrete deck structure supported on long
composite piles with lengths varying from about 83 to 110 feet below
the top of the wharf deck which is at an elevation of about 108 9 feet
U S Navy Datum MLIW Elevation 100 feet Timber fender piles
were observed along the ocean side of the wharf
During our field exploration measurements indicated that the
existing mudline at the two broken pile locations was generally sloping
towards the sea with a slope ratio of about H V The depths to
the mudline were measured in Boring Nos 1 and 2 at about 29 and 14
feet respectively below the top of the wharf deck
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The geologic conditions underlying the site are the results of a
complex series of depositional and erosional events brought on by
glacioeustatic sea level changes and post erosional volcanic activities
during the Pleistocene Following the cessation of activity of the
Koolau Volcano alluvial materials were deposited in estuarine and
deltaic enviroment in the area of Pearl Harbor
The sea level fluctuated several times receding perhaps 300 feet
below and rising about 95 feet above the present sea level
As Oahu sank coral reef grew around the shorelines keeping pace
with the subsidence

Concurrent with a rise in sea level during late Pleistocene era
eruption of the Salt Lake Makalapa member of the Honolulu Volcanic
Series mantled the coral reefs and alluvium with deposits of volcanic
ash which consolidated to form volcanic tuff locally called
mudrock Subsequent erosion has dissected and removed the mantles
of tuff at the site
During the Recent epoch as the sea level rose to its current
stand the area was covered with soft and loose lagoonal marine
deposits These deposits were subsequently covered by man made fills
placed during the middle portion of this century for land reclamation
projects during the construction of the existing Naval Supply Center
Subsoil Conditions
The subsurface soil conditions at the site were explored by
arilling and sampling 2 borings close to the two broken pile locations
to depths of about 76 to 91 feet below the top of the wharf deck
level The approximate locations of these borings are shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2
The drilled borings generally encountered loose gravel fill and
loose or soft lagoonal deposits of clay silt sand and coral gravel
beneath the existing mudline which was measured at about 14 to 29 feet
below the top of the wharf deck Alluvial deposits consisting of stiff
clayey silt were encountered beneath the lagoonal deposits to the
drilled depths of the borings
Detailed soil descriptions are presented on the boring logs
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our findings we recommend that the proposed inch
octagonal replacement piles should extend into the stiff alluvial
clayey silt below the loose and soft upper subsoils in order to attain
the design vertical capacities
GEOLABS HAWAII

Dk No 42
The piles would develop their vertical compression load capacities
primarily from adhesion between the piles and the subsoils and
partially from end bearing on the stiff alluvial clayey silt
We recommend that the two vertical replacement piles be driven to
a minimum depth of about 75 feet below the top of the wharf deck while
the batter pile be driven to a minimum depth of about 90 feet below the
wharf deck
The piles should be driven to the recommended depths with a hammer
delivering a minimum of 24 000 foot pounds of energy per blow and to a
resistance of about 2 blows per inch for the last 3 inches or 24 blows
per foot for the last foot
An allowable vertical pile capacity of 12 5 tons per pile may be
used for the vertical replacement piles and an allowable vertical pile
capacity of 25 tons may be used for the batter replacement pile
The pile driving should be monitored by a qualified geotechnical
engineer to see that the piles are driven to the required depth and
that sufficient capacities have been developed
Design Review

Limitations
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are
based in part upon information obtained from drilled borings
Variations of conditions between boring points may occur and the
nature and extent of these variations may not become evident until
construction If variations then appear evident it will be necessary
to re evaluate the recommendations given in this report
The location of the borings were approximately determined by
reference to the Partial Repair Plans Typical Sections for the
subject wharf from Thomas Iam and Associates Elevations of the
borings were approximately determined also by reference to the same
plans and sections The physical locations and elevations of the
borings should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the
method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
borings depict the approximate boundaries between soil types and as
such may denote a gradual transition
Water level data from the borings was collected at the times given
on the graphic representations and or in the text of this report This
data has been reviewed and interpretations made in the formulation of
this report However it must be noted that fluctuation may occur due
to variations in rainfall tides temperature and other factors
