INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed warehouse for Pier 18 in Honolulu Harbor on the island of
Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings from our field exploration and laboratory
testing and presents our geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our

analyses for the proposed project These recommendations are intended for design input
only and are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed project site is located along Honolulu Harbor on the island of Oahu
Hawaii The warehouse will include about 10 000 square feet of area with a
3 000 square foot mezzanine level The site is currently an asphalt paved parking lot An
old existing warehouse is located adjacent to the proposed warehouse site
It is proposed to develop the subject site with the construction of a new warehouse
building at Pier 18 The new building will consist of a warehouse minor boat repair shop
and offices We understand that the new building will consist of ground floor level parking
with asphalt concrete pavement at the front A concrete slab on grade floor is planned for
the warehouse and minor boat repair shop which will be subjected to forklift traffic The
second floor will generally consist of office space Based on the information provided by
the project architect we understand that the proposed building will generally be of CMU
wall construction at the ground floor level with wood construction for the second floor
level and metal roofing The proposed finish floor elevation for the new building was not
available at the time this report was prepared Based on the field reconnaissance minor
cuts and fills are anticipated for the site grading
Based on the information provided by the project structural engineer maximum
column loads are estimated to be on the order of about 25 kips per column with wall loads
of approximately 3 kips per lineal foot

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu is composed largely of the weathered remnants of two extinct
shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The project site is located on the southern flank
of the Koolau shield Its geomorphology and subsurface conditions are directly related to
glacio eustatic fluctuations of the sea level during the Pleistocene Epoch and the genesis
of the Honolulu Coastal Plain The subsurface conditions in the area are dominated by
fossil Pleistocene Age coral algal reef deposits and related deposits which are
intercalated with terrigenous sediments laid during high stands of the sea The base of
the stratigraphic section in this area is Koolau Basalt
In the last 10 000 years or so the sea level has adjusted to its present stand
Terrigenous and marine sediments have continued to accumulate in low energy estuarine
or lagoonal environments resulting in deposits of soft loose sediments along the coast in
the areas which were formerly valleys and drainageways Land development and
reclamation projects within the last 100 years have brought the project area to its present
form

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field exploration the project site is underlain by medium dense to
dense gravelly and silty sand extending to a depth of about 7 feet below the existing
ground surface Based on the maximum column loads of up to 25 kips we believe that
shallow foundations consisting of spread and or continuous footings bearing on the
recompacted sandy soils encountered at shallow depths may be used to support the
proposed building
Detailed discussions and recommendations pertaining to the design of foundation
slab on grade pavement site preparation and other geotechnical aspects of the project
are presented in the following sections of this report
Foundations
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered at the site we believe that
shallow spread and or continuous footings may be used to support the proposed new
W O 4139 00 Page 6
building An allowable bearing pressure of up to 2 500 pounds per square foot p s f
may be used for design of footing foundations bearing on the recompacted in situ sandy
soils encountered at shallow depths The foundation soils should be compacted to not
less than 95 percent relative compaction This bearing value is for dead plus live loads
and may be increased by one third for transient loads such as those caused by wind or
seismic forces
In order to utilize the thin surface fill crust we recommend that footings be
embedded to a depth of about 18 to 24 inches below finish grade
If foundations are located next to utility trenches or easements they should be
embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane extending
upward from the bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility
lines This requirement is necessary to avoid surcharging adjacent below grade
structures with additional structural loads and to reduce the potential for foundation
settlement
We estimate that footing settlements under the anticipated design loads for
footings bearing on the recompacted in situ soils as recommended herein to be on the
order of 1 inch total and inch differential
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive earth pressure
acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system and by frictional resistance
developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil Resistance due to
passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid pressure of
300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth p c f This assumes that the soil around
footings is well compacted or undisturbed Unless covered by pavements or slabs the
passive resistance in the upper 12 inches of soil should be neglected A coefficient of
friction of 0 35 may be used for footings bearing on the recompacted near surface soils
W O 4139 00 Page 7
It is recommended that the bottom of all footings bear on the recompacted
near surface soils If soft and or loose materials are encountered at the bottom of footing
excavations they should be over excavated to expose firm materials The
over excavation should be backfilled with granular material compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of
soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established
in accordance with procedures described in ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91
We recommend that footing excavations be observed by a representative of
Geolabs Hawaii prior to placement of reinforcing steel and concrete to confirm the
competency of the foundation bearing materials and the required embedment depths

minor boat repair shop which will be subjected to forklift traffic The design maximum axle
load of the forklift was not available at the time this report was prepared however we
anticipate that the maximum forklift axle loads will be on the order of 5 to 10 kips or 10 to
15 kips
Generally we recommend that the concrete slab be designed by a structural
engineer for the intended loads and be reinforced as a structural slab A modulus of

of the slab The following slab on grade sections may be used for preliminary design and

The subgrade soils for the slab on grade section should be scarified to a depth of
at least 10 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to not less than 95 percent relative compaction The base course should
consist of crushed basalt aggregate compacted not less than 95 percent relative
compaction The thickened edges of slabs adjacent to unpaved areas should be
embedded at least 12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade
The area adjacent to the slabs should be backfilled tightly against the slab with
non expansive impervious soil and graded to divert water away from the slab to reduce
the potential for water ponding around the foundations
Site Preparation
Although the existing topography and the proposed finish grades were not
available at the time this report was prepared we anticipate that minimal site grading
work is expected for the project construction
It should be noted that the weight of new fills will initiate ground settlement due to
compression of the soft subsoils Therefore site filling should be limited to a maximum
height of about 1 to 2 feet above the existing ground surface
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed Vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable
materials should be removed and disposed of off site
After clearing areas at grade or areas to receive fills should be scarified to a
minimum depth of 10 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture
content and compacted to not less than 95 percent relative compaction Relative
GEOLABS HAWAI

compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the
maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 91
test procedures Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight
corresponding to the maximum dry density
The on site granular materials may be used as a source of fill materials provided
that they are free of deleterious materials and that they may achieve the specified
compaction Fill material required for the project construction should be placed in level
lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness moisture conditioned to above the
optinum moisture content and compacted to not less than 95 percent relative
compaction Where needed imported materials should consist of select borrow crushed
coral or basalt gravel The material should be well graded from coarse to fine with no
particles greater than 3 inches in largest dimension The material should have a
laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR value of 20 or higher and a swell potential of
less than 1 percent when tested in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1883 It
should also contain between 10 and 30 percent passing the No 200 sieve Imported fill
material should be tested and approved by Geolabs Hawaii prior to delivery to the project
site for its intended use
Pavement Design
We understand that asphalt concrete pavement will be required for the parking
area We anticipate the vehicle loading for this project will consist primarily of passenger
vehicles and full size trucks We have made our preliminary pavement design assuming
the pavement subgrade soil will consist of the in situ gravelly sands prepared in
accordance with the recommendations presented in the Site Preparation section of this
report Based on the above assumptions we recommend the following pavement section
be used for preliminary design purposes

The subgrade soils should be scarified to a depth of about 10 inches
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture and compacted to not less than
95 percent relative compaction The base course should consist of crushed basalt
aggregate compacted to not less than 95 percent relative compaction CBR and density
tests should be performed on the actual subgrade soils encountered during construction
to confirm the adequacy of the above section
Paved areas should be sloped and drainage gradients maintained to carry surface
water off the site Surface water ponding should not be allowed on the site during or after
construction Where concrete curbs are used to isolate landscaping in or adjacent to the
pavement areas we recommend that the curbs be extended a minimum of 2 inches into
the subgrade soils below the aggregate base course to reduce migration of landscape
water into the pavement section Alternatively a subdrain system is recommended to
collect excessive water from landscaping irrigation For long term performance we
believe that a subdrain system should be considered adjacent to paved landscaped
areas
Utility Trench
We envision that new utility connections may be required for the proposed project
A granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33
No 67 gradation is recommended under the pipes It should be noted that groundwater
was encountered at a depth of about 5 8 to 7 7 feet below the ground surface Therefore
utility trenches extending near this depth will likely encounter groundwater For pipes with
inverts near the underlying loose silty sand or soft sandy silt deposits an additional
18 inches of No 3B Fine gravel wrapped in filter fabric Mirafi 140N or equivalent should
be provided below the bedding layer to provide more uniform support of the pipe
W O 4139 00 Page 11
Free draining granular materials such as No 3B Fine gravel
No 67 gradation should also be used for the trench backfill up to about 12 inches above
the pipes to provide adequate support around the pipes and to reduce the compaction
effort of the backfill thus reducing the possibility of damaging the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipes to
the top of the subgrade or finished grade should consist of select granular material or the
on site granular soils The backfill should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum
moisture content placed in about 8 inch loose lifts and mechanically compacted to not
less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for future ground
subsidence Where trenches are below pavement areas the upper 2 feet of the trench
backfill below the pavement subgrade should be compacted to not less than 95 percent
relative compaction
Drainage
The finished grades outside the building should be sloped to shed water away
from foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also advised that gutter
systems be installed around the building and that the discharge be diverted away from
the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near the foundations
and slabs should also be avoided Planters next to foundations should be avoided or
have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil
saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations
should be properly backfilled against the walls or slab footings immediately after setting of
the concrete to reduce water infiltration
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and should be
maintained to drain surface run off away from the slab and footing foundations

construction should also be observed by a representative from Geolabs Hawaii This is to

The recommendations provided in this report are contingent upon such observations
If actual exposed subsurface conditions encountered during construction are
different from those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications
to the design should be made

Laboratory Testing
Moisture content and unit weight determinations were performed on selected soil
samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties The results of
these tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Two sieve analysis tests ASTM D 422 were performed on selected sample of the
soils to evaluate the gradation characteristics of the soils and to aid in soil classification
Graphic presentation of the grain size distribution is presented on Plate B 1
One consolidation test ASTM D 2435 was performed on a selected soil sample to
evaluate the compressibility characteristics of the in situ soils The test results are plotted
on Plate B 2
