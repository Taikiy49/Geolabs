Dear Mr. Kingery: 
This letter report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations performed 
during the foundation probing and grouting operations for the Air Pollution Control System 
Improvement Phase 2 project between January 6, 2010 and January 11, 2010. 
The project location is within the H-Power facility at Campbell Industrial Park in the 
District of Ewa on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii, as shown on the Project Location Map, 
Plate 1.  The Phase 2 project includes the construction of the following: 
One (1) Baghouse structure 
One (1) ID Fan structure 
One (1) Duct foundation 
One (1) RA Fan structure

Because of potential subsurface cavities and/or voids within the underlying coral 
formation, a probing and grouting program to locate and grout potential cavities beneath 
foundations was specified for the project.  The probe hole locations were surveyed and 
marked in the field by the general contractor (Parsons Construction Group) prior to the 
probing operations.  The probe holes were spaced at about 10 feet on center along the 
structure foundations.  The total number of scheduled probe holes are as follows: 
Unit #1 Baghouse (104 probe holes) 
Unit #1 ID Fan (9 probe holes) 
Duct Foundation #7 (6 probe holes) 
Unit #1 RA Fan (4 probe holes) 
We understand that the probe holes for Duct Foundation #7 will not be performed.  
The approximate locations and identification of the scheduled probe holes for the above 
remaining structures are shown on the attached Probing Identification Plans, Plates 2.1 
through 2.3.  Five of the scheduled probe holes for Unit #1 Baghouse were located over 
an existing water line and these probe holes were deleted. 
Geolabs performed the probing and grouting work for the Phase 2 project. The 
probe holes were drilled with a truck-mounted drill rig equipped with continuous augers.  
The probe holes drilled were about 4 inches in diameter and extended to about 16 to 
20 feet below the existing ground surface.  The probe holes generally extend to a depth of 
at least 10 feet below the bottom of footing elevations.  A summary of our foundation probe 
drilling and grouting observations is provided on Plates 3.1 through 3.4. 
The materials encountered during the probe drilling operations generally consisted 
of fill materials underlain by tannish brown and gray white coralline materials.  After 
completion of the probe drilling operations, the probe holes were filled with a one-part sand 
and one-part cement grout. Island Ready Mix supplied the grout used to fill the probe 
holes and American Standard Concrete Pumping Hawaii, Inc. operated the boom pump. 
Since the grout intakes for the scheduled probe holes were less than about 4 times the 
theoretical volume of the hole, additional probe holes were not performed.  Approximately 
13.2 cubic yards of one-part sand and one-part cement grout was used to fill the 
scheduled probe holes drilled for the Phase 2 project. 
In summary, the foundation probing and grouting operations for the project have 
been conducted in general conformance with the intent of the project specifications. Based 
on our observations, it is our professional opinion that the relatively small voids and/or 
cavities encountered at the structure foundation locations have been grouted and that the 
subgrade area should provide the recommended foundation support for the structure 
foundation.
