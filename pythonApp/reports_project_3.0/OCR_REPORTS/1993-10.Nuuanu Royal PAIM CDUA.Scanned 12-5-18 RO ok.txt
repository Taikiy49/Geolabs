INTRODUCTION
This report represents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the design of the site grading bridge and house
foundations of the proposed Nuuanu Royal Palm residential development in
the Nuuanu Valley area of Honolulu Oahu Hawaii The general location of
the site is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1993 10 Page 2
The work was done generally in accordance with our proposal of
with the exception that exploration on the eastern side
of Nuuanu Stream was limited to a seismic refraction survey and hand auger
probings due to high water levels of Nuuanu Stream making the site
inaccessible to our portable drilling equipment
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is currently proposed to clear and grade the subject lot to
construct a single family residence measuring approximately 90 by 50 feet
in plan dimensions In addition a bridge crossing of Nuuanu Stream and
driveway are planned to provide access to the development area
Based on the site grading plan provided to us cuts and fills
generally less than 10 feet are planned for the development area
Retaining walls are planned for grade separation at the bridge location
and along the rear southeastern portion of the proposed dwelling
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface conditions of the project site in order to develop a
generalized soil rock data set for the formulation of soil engineering
recommendations pertinent to the site grading and foundation design of the
project

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located at 2525 Pali Highway in the Nuuanu Valley
area of Honolulu Oahu Hawaii The general location of the site and its
vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The site is situated on the eastern or Diamond Head side of Pali
Highway on the eastern bank of Nuuanu Stream
Ground surface elevations range from 210 feet above Mean Sea Level
MSL in the vicinity of Nuuanu Stream to about 280 feet above MSL at the
eastern boundary of the site In general the land surface Slopes down
toward Nuuanu Stream
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1993 10 Page 4
The site consists of generally raw undeveloped land and is densely
vegetated with mango palm bamboo other trees and grasses An abandoned
bridge framework with foundation structures was noted at the site as well
as an abandoned camp style dwelling surrounded by miscellaneous rubbish
Many boulders of varying size were also observed throughout the site
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
The project site is located in Nuuanu Valley an amphitheater headed
valley carved by erosion into Koolau Basalts Filling the base of this
valley is a thick sequence of alluvium interbedded with deposits of lavas
and pyroclastics of the Honolulu Volcanic Series
At the project site the drilled boring and hand driven auger probes
encountered surface layers of slopewash Adobe clay and alluvium
overlying basaltic lavas believed to be of the Honolulu Volcanic Series
Subsurface Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by performing a
surface geologic reconnaissance and by drilling one 1 boring to a depth
of 15 0 feet at the western end of the proposed stream crossing bridge
location and three 3 hand driven auger probes to depths ranging between
1 0 and 4 0 feet below the existing ground surface within the proposed
building footprint
In addition a seismic refraction survey was performed to verify the
underlying materials and to evaluate their excavation properties
In general the site is mantled with an upper slopewash deposit of
expansive clay and the underlying clayey silts with many boulders Older
Alluvium to a depth of up to 10 0 feet Below this hard severely
fractured basalt was encountered to the maximum depth drilled Hard
basalt was also noted along the stream bank and therefore should be
anticipated at shallow depths in the vicinity of the stream banks
GEOLABS HAWAIl
W O 1993 10 Page 5
Groundwater was not measured at the time of our exploration due to
caving in of the drilled boring However our previous exploration on the
western side of Nuuanu Stream encountered relatively high groundwater
levels at elevations ranging between 224 5 to 235 0 feet above Mean Sea
level Therefore high groundwater levels should be anticipated
Due to the nature of the underlying formation and the relative
proximity to the stream it is anticipated that the groundwater levels
will fluctuate in response to periods of heavy precipitation and high
surface flows
DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
In general our exploration indicates that from a geotechnical
engineering point of view the site is suitable for the proposed
residential development provided that our recommendations are followed
Our exploration encountered an upper surface layer of stiff brown
Clay Adobe with a high to critical expansion potential and poor shear
strength characteristics We believe this material should be completely
stripped and removed from all structural and fill areas
For the bridge foundations located on the slopes of Nuuanu Stream
spread footings bearing on stiff in situ alluvium or basaltic rock
formation may be used
For the proposed two story dwelling we recommend that spread or
strip footings bear on the underlying stiff in situ alluvium or on
non expansive backfill material after over excavation of the upper
expansive clay
Shallow groundwater or seepage water conditions should be anticipated
in the proposed fill areas located in the lower elevation portions of the
site and may require the use of special grading techniques as discussed
below

Site Grading
Based on the site grading plan provided to us cuts up to eight 8
feet high and about two 2 feet of embankment fill are required within
the 2 story dwelling location It is also anticipated that grading for
the access roadway from the bridge abutment up to the driveway area will
require some site filling
Ground Preparation
Prior to the commencement of any earthwork all areas within the
grading limits should be cleared and grubbed of all vegetation
demolition debris rubbish and deleterious materials The resulting
grub and spoil material should be disposed of properly off site Any
Slopewash Adobe clay soils and other underlying soft weak or
yielding areas disclosed during the clearing operations should be
over excavated to firm ground Older Alluvium and the resulting
excavation should be backfilled with well compacted select borrow
granular materials Select on site excavated material such as the
underlying clayey silt has high in situ moisture content in the 60
percent range and would require substantial drying before use as a
structural fill
large surface boulders should be collected and hauled off the site
Our exploration encountered a surface layer of stiff brown clay
Adobe with a high to critical expansion potential and poor shear
strength characteristics This material should be stripped and
removed from all structural and fill areas The stripped material
should be removed from the site to prevent contamination of the
underlying alluvial soils
Subsequent to clearing grubbing and stripping of the surface adobe
soils the areas designated to receive fill within the grading limits
should be scarified to a depth of 12 inches moisture conditioned to
near optimm moisture content and recompacted to a minimum of 90

Fill Placement
After initial site preparation the required select fill material
should be placed in maximum nine 9 inch loose lifts and compacted
to obtain a minimum 90 of its maximm ary density per ASIM Test
Method D 1557 78
Based on the field exploration and laboratory test results it appear
that the on site materials underlying the surface Adobe clay are
not suitable for use in the compacted fill and backfill The
laboratory swell tests indicate that the upper 1 to 3 feet of the
surface soil within the proposed cut area has high swelling
potential Therefore these upper soils and any other expansive clay
which may be encountered should not be incorporated into the fills
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1993 10 Page 8
The less expansive underlying select on site soils such as the
brownish clayey silt Older Alluvium may be used as a source of fill
material if properly moisture conditioned
Imported fill material if required should consist of select
non expansive material such as select borrow coral or cinder and
should be tested for suitability as fill material by the project
geotechnical engineer prior to its delivery to the project site
Shallow groundwater levels should be anticipated in the proposed fill
areas in the lower portions of the site We recommend that light
equipment be used to place the first three 3 feet of select granular
fill material to reduce the possibility of subgrade pumping failure and
difficulty of compaction using the upper in situ high moisture soils from
the excavation The compaction of the subgrade should be limited to
between 85 to 90 maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Slopes
The field exploration indicated that the proposed cuts would be in
slope wash and alluviun deposits In general cut Slopes ratios of
two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V are recommended for the
underlying alluvium deposit For the upper slopewash Adobe clay
layer we believe a flatter Slope ratio of three horizontal to one
vertical 3H 1V should be used for the upper three 3 feet of cut
slopes
Fill slope ratios of two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V are
recommended The embankment fill should consist of off site select
borrow granular material over filled and then cut back to the two
horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V finish slope ratio
Where practicable surface run off water should be diverted via swale
or ditch away from the top of the cut slope or retaining wall and
slope planting utilized as soon as practical to reduce erosion

House Foundation Support
It is anticipated that both spread line footing foundations and
slabs on grade will be used for the residential structure
Based on the plans provided to us it appears that the proposed
dwelling will span between cut and fill areas In order to provide
uniform support for and to minimize the inclusion of expansive soils below
the foundations and slabs on grade of the proposed house we recommend
that the building area be over excavated to a minimm of two 2 feet
below the foundation slab subgrade level This over excavated area should
be backfilled with select non expansive material placed and compacted in
accordance with the recommendations given above in Fill Placement
For the design of spread line footings bearing on the non expansive
select fill compacted in accordance with the recommendations presented
herein an allowable bearing capacity of 2 500 p s f may be utilized
This value may be increased by one third when temporary wind or seismic
loads are considered
Spread line footing foundations on level areas should be embedded a
minimum of twelve 12 inches below the lowest adjacent outside grade On
sloping areas the footings should be embedded an additional depth to
provide a eight 8 foot minimm set back distance measured from the
outer edge of footing out horizontally to the slope face
On level area the thickened edges of slabs on grade should be
embedded a minimm of twelve 12 inches below the lowest adjacent
exterior grade Where the slab is located within the eight 8 foot
set back area the perimeter thickened edge footings should be extended
further down to obtain the above set back requirements
A thicker than normal cushion fill which will also act as a
capillary break layer consisting of six 6 inches of 3B Fine aggregate
rock is recommended below slabs A moisture barrier membrane is
recommended between the slab and the cushion to reduce the possibility of
moisture damage to the floor coverings

Bridge Foundations
A 70 foot long single span bridge is planned across Nuuamu Stream
for access into the site
Based on the available boring and field reconnaissance data if
appears that spread or continuous footings extending down to the very
stiff alluvium a minimum of about four 4 feet below the abutment
locations may be used to support the bridge An allowable bearing value
of 3 000 p s f may be used for bridge foundations bearing on the very
stiff alluvial soils
Bridge foundations should be embedded a minimm of four 4 feet
below the lowest adjacent grade The foundations should also be
sufficiently embedded to be protected from potential scour by the action
of the stream

Estimated settlements for bridge foundations bearing on the very

Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the
earthwork and foundation phases of the work This is to observe
compliance with the design concepts specifications or recommendations and
to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ
from that anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations
given in this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made

borings probes test pits and or observation wells Variations of

the same plan The physical location and elevation of the field data

The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross sections are based upon interpolation between field
data points and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
Water level data from the field data points was collected at the
times given on the graphic representations and or in the text of this
report This data has been reviewed and interpretations made in the
