SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located at the mouth of Kahana Valley on the windward side of
the island of Oahu
Kahana Valley serves as a large well developed drainage system for the
surrounding valley walls Therefore a dominant stream and floodplain have developed
on the valley floor
In the northwest portion of the valley the ground surface elevations are fairly
constant ranging between four 4 to six 6 feet above Mean Sea Level Due to the
relatively low flat topography local depressions and ditches usually contained standing
water which appeared to originate from the close proximity of the water table Vegetation
in the areas of stagnant water consisted of tall grass or barren ground surfaces under a
cover of dense plum or hau tree growth
Closer to the valley walls the elevation rises gradually on colluvial soils and talus
materials

FINDINGS AND CONCLUSIONS
Based on our field reconnaissance and permeability test results it appears that the
percolation rate of the sandy subsoil in the lowlying areas is satisfactory However the
Department of Health s administrative rules governing wastewater systems specifies in
Chapter 40 that Absorption trenches shall not be constructed where rapid percolation
may result in the contamination of water bearing formations or surface waters In
addition it is stated that The absorption trench shall be located on the property to
maximize the vertical distance from the bottom of the absorption trench to the seasonal
high groundwater level but under no circumstances shall this vertical separation be less
than three feet 0 9 m Where water bearing formations are in danger of contamination
greater vertical separation may be required
Due to the shallow groundwater levels encountered in these lowlying areas and the
fact that the area is designated as a floodplain it is our opinion that this site does not lend
itself to the proper functioning of a leaching field Besides posing a possible health risk
because of the high water table levels the leaching fields may display a loss of infiltrative
capacity during periods of rainfall and flooding
Other alternative sites target areas No 4 and 5 situated at the toe of the colluvial
slopes fronting the mountain side were tested and found to have moderate to poor
infiltrative capacity Groundwater was not encountered in the drilled borings due to their
higher altitudes Therefore these sites may be feasible for locating the proposed leaching
fields More extensive permeability testing and deeper borings are recommended for
these target areas to delineate the subsurface profile and hydrologic properties

General
Based on our field reconnaissance and permeability test results it appears that the
percolation rate of the sandy subsoil in the lowlying areas i e Area No 1 is adequate
for the operation of leachfields
However the Department of Health s administrative rules governing wastewater systems
states in Chapter 40 that Absorption trenches shall not be constructed where rapid
percolation may result in the contamination of water bearing formations or surface
waters In addition it is stated that The absorption trench shall be located on the
property to maximize the vertical distance from the bottom of the absorption trench to the
seasonal high groundwater level but under no circumstances shall this vertical separation
be less than three feet 0 9 m Where water bearing formations are in danger of
contamination greater vertical separation may be required
Discussions with Department of Health personnel indicate that these portions of
the administrative rules are generally applicable to wastewater systems which use septic

the minimum level of primary treatment to wastewater i e removal of solids with little
reduction of biological oxygen demand or removal of nutrients
Based on our discussions the Department of Health may allow the disposal of

more advanced treatment which were mentioned in our discussions are

e Trickling filters these also may be available on a commercial basis These
filters also require operation by a certified operator and maintenance of the
trickling media
e Slow sand filters these units can be individually designed or may be
commercially available Maintenance requires backwashing of the sand
media and occasional replacement
Selection of a treatment system is beyond the scope of our work and our field of
expertise However all of the above systems if properly operated and maintained

Siting of Leachfields
Based on our field reconnaissance and testing it is our opinion that Area No 1 has
the highest potential for the siting and operation of a leachfield The sandy soils in this
area show relatively rapid percolation characteristics
Other alternative sites Areas No 4 and 5 situated at the toe of the colluvial
slopes fronting the mountain side were tested and found to have moderate infiltration
capacity Groundwater was not encountered in the drilled borings due to their higher
altitudes Therefore these sites are also feasible for locating the proposed leaching fields
However pumping will be required to transport the wastewater from the comfort stations
to these sites This would increase the cost of the system and the long term operation
and maintenance costs
Design of Leachfields
Leachfields should be designed and constructed in general accordance with the
procedures given in the Manual of Septic Tank Practice Public Health Service
Publication No 526 A sketch of a typical leachfield design is shown on Plate 3
Based on the results of our field reconnaissance and testing it is our opinion that
a leachfield sited in Area No 1 should be designed for the application of treated
wastewater at the rate of about four 4 gallons per day per square foot of trench bottom
Using a leaching trench with a width of 24 inches a total of 200 lineal feet of trench will
be required for the anticipated wastewater flows This 200 lineal feet could be distributed
in any number of configurations within Area No 1
