consisted of drilling three shallow borings performing laboratory
testing and reviewing the subdivision site grading and preliminary house
foundation plans This report summarizes our findings and
recommendations

SITE CONDITIONS
The subject lot is located on the southern side of Papalealii Street
in the Royal Summit Phase I Subdivision in Aiea on the island of Oahu
A relatively level pad about 30 feet wide is situated along the
front portion of the site The ground surface then slopes downward at
about 5 1 H V for a horizontal distance of 45 feet to a second level
pad at the rear of the lot There is a 5 foot wide drainage easement and
a 6 foot wide sewer easement along the rear lot boundary

DISCUSSTON AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The stiff reddish brown clayey silt soil encountered generally can
provide suitable foundation support for the proposed house provided that
some care is exercised during construction to reduce the swelling
potential of the soil
Laboratory tests performed on the stiff clayey silt soil indicates
that this material generally has low to moderate swelling potential
depending on the moisture content of the soil
Therefore to reduce the effects due to soil swelling under the
slabs we recommend that the on site soil be capped with at least 12
inches of non expansive select fill and that the subgrade soils
underneath the select fill and slab be moistened and protected from
drying from the start of construction until the slab concrete placement
Select non expansive soils should also be used as backfill behind
the retaining walls to minimize the additional pressure due to the
swelling potential of the natural soil
Lot Grading
The fills and backfills within the top 12 inches minimm below
on grade slabs should consist of non expansive soil such as select
borrow crusher waste or coral Where cut is anticipated such as the
garage driveway and the lower house areas the on site soil should be
over excavated to provide a 12 inch minimum thickness of non expansive
select fill capping
All imported fill materials should be tested and approved by the
soils engineer prior to being used in the site

Prior to construction the area to receive any fill should be
cleared of vegetation and debris
The fills should be placed in horizontal lifts not exceeding 8
inches in compacted thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its
maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78
Fills placed on existing slopes should start from the bottom up and
should be keyed and benched to bond the new fill to the existing slope
The site should be graded to provide drainage of surface runoff away
from all foundations
House Foundations
Spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the proposed
residence An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f may be used on the
compacted soil or stiff natural ground
Interior footings on level areas should be embedded a minimum of 8
inches below the finished grade Perimeter wall footings should be
embedded a minimum of 12 inches below the adjacent finished grade
The western end of the house is located adjacent to the existing
sewer and drainage easements To reduce the possibility of footing
settlement due to settlement of the trench backfill or easement trench
excavation at a later date for repair purposes we recommend that the
footings in this area be embedded below a H V plane extending
upward from the pipe invert level at the edge of the easement
Alternatively pipe piles may be used for support of the portions of
the house foundation next to the sewer and drain lines
The pipe piles may consist of minimum 6 inch diameter drilled holes
with 3 to 4 inch diameter steel pipes inserted and grouted into the
holes

Slab On Grade
It should be noted that some of the on site clayey silt soils are
moderately expansive Therefore the on site soil should be
over excavated down to a depth of 12 inches below the slab bottom and
then replaced with non expansive imported soils as previously
discussed The thickened edge of the slab should be embedded a minimm
of 12 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade
To further reduce future post construction swelling of the
underlying expansive soils we recommend that the in place clayey silt
below the upper capping fill pad be kept moist for one week prior to
concrete placement heavily soaked with water for the first two days and
sprinkled with water for the remainder of the week
A 4 inch layer of 3B Fine gravel cushion fill should be provided
below the slab In order to protect against future moisture
infiltration an impervious moisture membrane may be incorporated below
the slab The on grade slabs should be structurally separated from wall
foundations
Concrete Driveway
Since the driveway can tolerate some amount of heaving the 12 inch
over excavation and replacement may be eliminated to reduce costs if
some small slab cracking is tolerable within the driveway It is
critical that the subgrade soils should be maintained in a moist
condition and prevented from drying out prior to concrete placement

Drainage
We recommend that planters next to the foundations should be avoided
or have concrete bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the
subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance
of the above recommended foundations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of
strength The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled tight
against the wall or slab footing inmediately after setting of the
concrete to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained
to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundation
Design Review
Plans and specifications for lot grading foundation and utility
construction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and
written comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the lot grading footing excavations and
backfill operations be observed by the soils engineer during construction
to see that adequate compaction has been obtained and that the subsurface
conditions are consistent with the findings of our investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site

LIMITATIONS
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations
made in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation
engineering principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all
other warranties either expressed or implied
The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are based
on our site reconnaissance soil information from boring and laboratory
test data obtained from this soil investigation
Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encountered and cannot be
fully determined by taking soil samples Unforeseen conditions such as
expansive soil and soft spots may occur in localized areas and may
require additional probing or corrections in the field Therefore
