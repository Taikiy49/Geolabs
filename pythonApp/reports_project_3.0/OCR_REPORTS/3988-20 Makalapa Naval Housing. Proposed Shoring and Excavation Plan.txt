In accordance with our fee proposal dated we have evaluated the
existing site and subsurface conditions with respect to the proposed shoring and excavation
for the above referenced project Geotechnical engineering explorations were previously
performed for the project by our office and the results of the exploration were presented in
our reports dated and May 18 1999
We understand that a temporary ramp from the existing roadway level about
Elevation 172 feet U S Navy Datum down to the first deck level of Building 250 Elevation
151 feet is planned The temporary ramp will be used to facilitate the excavation work of
the new stairway elevator structure with the excavation anticipated to be on the order of about
30 feet A site plan showing the proposed excavation is presented on the Site Plan Plate 1
An existing excavation for the construction of the exit stairway is located on the west
side of the ramp In addition an existing electrical duct line is located under Makalapa Drive
and crosses the upper ramp area as shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
As previously indicated exploratory borings were drilled in the vicinity of the proposed
excavation These borings were drilled from near the second and first deck levels The
borings drilled near the second deck level generally encountered about 1 5 inches of asphaltic
concrete at the surface underlain by fill consisting of medium dense to dense sandy gravel
extending to depths of about 8 3 to 10 feet below the ground surface Medium hard to hard
volcanic tuff mudrock was encountered below the fill extending to the maximum depth drilled
of up to about 40 feet The boring drilled near the first deck level generally encountered a
surface fill layer approximately 6 feet in thickness consisting of very loose silty sand with
gravel underlain by hard to medium hard volcanic tuff mudrock extending to the maximum
drill depth of about 34 feet Additional subsurface data from the exploration program may be
obtained in our reports dated and May 18 1999

SHORING SYSTEM
Based on our evaluation of the subsurface conditions encountered in the borings we
believe that a temporary shoring system would be required for the proposed ramp and
stairway elevator construction Excavations exposing the surface sandy gravel may be
sloped at 0 5 horizontal to 1 vertical or flatter with some reinforcement of the sandy
gravel fill material Excavations exposing the underlying mudrock may be excavated to near
vertical A minimum 2 foot wide bench measured horizontally from the exposed mudrock
face should be provided at the interface of the sandy gravel and mudrock The depth of the
bench will vary depending on the actual depth to the mudrock layer
As the excavation progresses two rows of 10 foot long No 5 deformed bars should
be driven into the sandy gravel material at a horizontal spacing of 5 feet on center The
deformed bars should be installed at depths of about 3 and 7 feet from the existing ground
surface as shown on Plate 2 Once the deformed bars have been installed a 6 inch square
steel plate with a thickness of about inch should be welded to its exposed end A minimum
of 4 inches of fiber reinforced shotcrete should be applied over the exposed sandy gravel
surface and the deformed bar steel plate as shown on Plate 2
A drainage system consisting of 12 inch wide prefabricated drainage boards such as
MiraDrain 6000 or equivalent should be placed between the sandy gravel face and the
shotcrete The drainage boards should be located vertically between the deformed bars and
should be spaced no further than 5 feet on center Weep holes consisting of 2 inch diameter
Schedule 40 PVC pipes and filter fabric should be provided at the bottom of each drainage
board to reduce the potential for build up of hydrostatic pressure behind the shotcrete face
A typical section of the drainage system is presented on Plate 2
Determination of the necessary temporary shoring system for the excavation exposing
mudrock will need to be made during construction In general if the mudrock is observed to
be in a highly fractured condition it is recommended that 2 to 3 inches of fiber reinforced
shotcrete be applied over the exposed surface Prior to applying the shotcrete the exposed
mudrock surface should be free of loose materials A drainage system similar to that
presented above should be placed between the mudrock face and the shotcrete The
drainage boards should also be spaced no further than 5 feet on center Weep holes are not
necessary for the lower drainage boards in the mudrock however the drainage boards
should extend about 6 inches beyond the bottom of the excavation to reduce the potential for
the build up of hydrostatic pressure behind the shotcrete face as shown on Plates 2 and 3
Care should be taken to prevent blockage of the outlet portion during construction of the
stairway elevator structure Shotcrete and drainage board requirements for the exposed
mudrock should be determined during construction by an engineer from our office

_ INSTRUMENTATION AND MONITORING
Considering the proximity of the proposed shoring system to the existing office
structure retaining wall roadway and utility duct we recommend that instrumentation and
monitoring of the shoring system be provided The extent of construction monitoring may
vary however we believe that the following items of work should be considered We also
recommend that a representative from Geolabs observe the excavated mudrock formation
prior to application of the shotcrete to confirm and or modify our recommendations
Prior to Shoring
Ae Damage survey of the existing buildings and structures adjacent to the
proposed shoring alignment should be conducted including photographs and
crack surveys prior to the commencement of construction
2 Settlement reference points should also be installed as much as possible in
a straight line pattern near the outside edge of the shotcrete berm The
reference points should be installed at approximately 20 foot intervals along the
edge of the excavation
A reference point should also be established at the cover of the existing utility
manhole located to the south of the excavation
During Construction
Frequently once a day observe the conditions of the existing adjacent structures and
shoring system for signs of distress The information should be recorded written on
a log and any unusual findings should be immediately reported for further evaluation
or action
The settlement reference points should be monitored generally on a weekly basis and
on a more frequent basis should distresses be observed
