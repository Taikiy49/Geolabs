INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
evaluation performed for the proposed residence at Lot 121 Waialae Iki
Unit V on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location of the site
and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The
exploration was performed in general accordance with our proposal dated
This report summarizes our findings from the exploration and
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analysis based
on the data obtained during the exploration These recommendations are
intended for design input and are not intended for construction estimation
or bidding purposes

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
Based on the plans provided to us the proposed new residence will be
a two story structure approximately 56 by 54 feet in plan dimensions
Concrete on grade slab is planned for the garage and driveway areas
Site grading involving cuts up to 5 feet western portion and fills
up to 5 feet eastern portion is anticipated Retaining walls are
planned for grade separation at various portions of the lot

SITE DESCRIPTION
The subject lot is located at the end of Kuola Place in the Waialae
Iki V Subdivision on the island of Oahu This approximately
rectangular shaped 9 000 square foot lot is largely barren of
vegetation An existing riprap lining exists along the northern boundary
Slope of the site
The topography of the site slopes downward from east to west with a
gradient of approximately 4 horizontal to 1 vertical 4H 1V The current
ground elevation at the rear eastern end of the lot is about 33 feet
lower than the street level

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The stiff on site residual soil encountered can generally provide
adequate foundation support for the proposed house provided that some care
is exercised during construction to reduce the swelling potential of the
soil as recommended herein
The field exploration and laboratory tests performed on the stiff
on site silty clay indicate that this material generally has low to
moderate swelling potential depending on the initial moisture content of
the soil prior to subsequent saturation Therefore to reduce the effects
due to soil swelling we recommend that the on site soil below on grade
slabs be capped with at least 12 inches of non expansive select fill and
that the subgrade soils underneath the select fill and slab be moistened
and protected from drying from the start of construction until the slab
concrete placement
Lot Grading
The fills and backfills within the top 12 inches minimum below the
on grade slabs should consist of non expansive soils such as select
borrow crusher waste or coral Where cut or minimal fill is anticipated
the on site soil should be over excavated to provide a 12 inch minimm
thickness of non expansive select fill capping below the on grade slabs
The on site cut soils may be used for fill if they are placed below
12 inches from the slab bottom and away from retaining walls and compacted
at a moisture content at least 5 above optimm moisture content
All imported fill material should be tested and approved by the soils
engineer prior to transportation to the site

W O 2148 00 Page 5
At the onset of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limits should be cleared of vegetation debris rubbish and other
deleterious materials These materials should be removed and disposed of
properly off site Any loose or soft spots encountered at fill areas
should be over excavated down to the firm subsoils and then replaced with
compacted non expansive select material
The fill areas should then be scarified to a depth of six 6 inches
moisture conditioned and recompacted to 90 of its maximum dry density per
ASTM D 1557 78
The fill should be placed in maximum 10 inch thick loose level lifts
and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density per ASIM
D 1557 78
House Foundations
Spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the proposed
residence An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f may be used for
footings bearing on the compacted natural soil or fill
Individual footings should be embedded a minimm of 12 inches below
the lowest adjacent finished grade Continuous wall footings should be
embedded a minimm of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade
If located on a sloping area the footing should be embedded down an
additional amount to obtain a 6 foot minimm horizontal setback distance
measured from the outer edge of the footing out to the slope face This
additional embedment depth is required to help resist the downhill slope
creep movement and to develop the recommended bearing capacity of the
soil
The northern wall of the house will be along an existing water
easement Footings along this easement should be embedded down to a depth
below a level which may be excavated in the future

Slab On Grade
It should be noted that the on site silty clay soils may be
moderately expansive if allowed to dry Therefore the on site soil
should be over excavated down to a depth of 12 inches below the slab
bottom thoroughly moistened and then replaced with non expansive
imported soils as previously discussed The thickened edge of the slab
should be embedded a minimm of 12 inches below the lowest adjacent
finished grade
To further reduce future post construction swelling of the underlying
expansive soils we recommend that the in place silty clay below the upper
capping fill pad be kept moist prior to concrete placement
A 4 inch layer of 3 Fine gravel cushion fill should be provided
below the slab In order to protect against future moisture infiltration
an impervious moisture barrier may be incorporated below the slab
Retaining Walls
It is our understanding that retaining walls may be used for grade
separation The guidelines for retaining wall design are listed below
1 For lateral earth pressures the following can be used
Walls unrestrained at the top 35 p s f equivalent
active condition level fluid pressure per
backfill foot of depth
Wall restrained at the top 55 p s f equivalent
at rest condition level fluid pressure per
backfill foot of depth
A passive earth resistance of 300 p s f per foot of depth may be
used for the wall footing on level areas The passive pressure
must be reduced for walls on slopes
2 A base friction factor of 0 4 may also be used to determine the
resistance of the wall against sliding

W O 2148 00 Page 7
3 The wall foundations should be embedded a minimm of 18 inches
below the lowest adjacent finished grade If located on a
sloping area the footing should be embedded down an additional
amount to obtain an 8 foot minimm horizontal setback distance
measured from the outer edge of the footing out to the slope
face An allowable bearing value of up to 3000 p s f is
recommended for foundations bearing on the stiff on site soil
4 If wall foundations are located next to sewer drainage easements
they should be embedded below a H V imaginary plane
extending upward from the bottom edge of the utility trench or as
deep as the inverts of the utility lines
5 Backfills directly behind the retaining wall should consist of
granular non expansive material such as select borrow crusher
waste or 3B Fine gravel The top 12 inches of backfill should
consist of non expansive impervious material to reduce water
infiltration behind the wall
6 A subsurface drainage system subdrains or weepholes should be
utilized behind the walls Subdrains should be properly
constructed to drain water away from the wall
Drainage
The finish lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings should
be shaped to shed water away from foundations and to avoid ponding
condition Also it is advised that gutter systems be installed around
the house and that gutter water should be diverted far away from the
foundations and driveway Excessive landscape watering near foundations
and slab should also be avoided
Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2148 00 Page 8
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance
of the above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of
strength The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled tight
against the wall or slab footing immediately after setting of the concrete
to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and maintained
to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundations
Concrete Driveway
Since the driveway can tolerate some amount of heaving the 12 inch
on site soil over excavation and replacement may be eliminated if some
amount of small slab cracking are tolerable The subgrade soils should be
maintained in a moist condition and prevented from drying out prior to
concrete placement
Joints are recommended in the driveway slab at about 8 to 10 foot
intervals and at discontinuities in order to allow for some relative
movement of the concrete due to shrinkage of the concrete uneven stress
concentrations and or soil movement
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comments
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this review
is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for
misinterpretation of the recommendations

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field borings Variations of
conditions between the field data points may occur and the nature and
extent of these variations may not become evident until construction If
variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations given in this report
The location of the field borings were approximately determined by
taping from structures indicated on the plans provided Elevations of
the data points were approximately determined by interpolation from
elevation points on the plans The physical location and elevation of
the field data points should be considered accurate only to the degree
implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines show on
profiles or cross sections are based upon interpolation between field
data points and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
