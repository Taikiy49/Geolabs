EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is located at Street inside the Manoa Estates
Subdivision at Manoa Valley on the island of Oahu Hawaii The proposed project site is
currently occupied by an existing one story house which will be removed to enable
construction of the proposed residence We understand that the existing residence is
constructed on a cut into the hillsides of Manoa Valley
The project site is bounded by existing homes to the northeast and to the
southwest It is fronted by Pinao Street to the southeast and bordered by Manoa Stream
to the northwest The stream runs approximately in a north south direction with the
stream bed elevations at approximately 25 to 30 feet below the floor elevation of the
existing residence Except for the steep slope located along the northwest side of the
project site the site topography is relatively level with existing ground surface elevations
ranging between 298 to 300 feet Mean Sea Level MSL
Based on our field observation the stream bank along the subject property slopes
downward at a gradient of approximately 2 1 H V for the upper eight feet 8 and
approaches near vertical for the lower portion down to the bottom of the stream channel

be observed on the lower slope and along the bed of the stream channel At the time of

A Board of Water Supply BWS easement is located in the backyard of the existing

SLOPE STABILITY
Based on our test borings and field reconnaissance there were no obvious
indications of distress to the surrounding area of the subject residence due to slope
instability It is our opinion that the existing stream bank is relatively stable provided that
erosion of the slope and toe of the stream bank does not take place We believe that
such erosion may cause instability of the existing stream bank due to its steep gradient
Based on our field observations the existing slope face and bottom of the stream bank
is either heavily vegetated or boulders are present to reduce the potential erosion problem
of the stream bank
The southwest corner of the proposed residence will be located within approxi
mately two feet 2 from the BWS easement as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The
exact location of the waterline within the easement is not available to us at the time this
report was prepared However our conversations with the property owner suggested
that the waterline may be located at approximately eight 8 to ten feet 10 below the
existing ground surface We believe that future excavation of the waterline may cause
some instability of the proposed structure if it is founded on a shallow foundation system
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3032 00 Page 7
In addition the excavation may also undermine the floor slab which is supported on the
future grade Therefore consideration should be given to a foundation system that is
embedded deeper than the waterline to reduce the potential of slope instability and
structural distresses In addition the floor slab should be structurally reinforced to span
between foundation supports to reduce potential distress to the slab due to the loss of
ground support
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
In general our field exploration indicates that the project site is underlain by a
surface layer of stiff silty clay Laboratory tests performed on the on site silty clay soils
indicate that the material generally has a moderate shrink swell potential wnen subjected
to moisture content fluctuations Therefore we recommend that concrete slab on grade
be supported on 12 inches of non expansive capping fill to reduce the potential effects
of the moderately expansive near surface soils
In consideration of the proximity of the proposed residence to the existing steep
bank and BWS easement a deep foundation system consisting of drilled piers founded
below the waterlines is recommended to support the proposed residence structure
Detailed discussions of the geotechnical aspects of the project are presented in the
following sections of this report
Foundations
The proposed two story residence may be supported by a foundation system
consisting of drilled piers deriving support principally from skin friction between the pier
shaft and the surrounding soils The drilled piers may be designed with an allowable skin
GEOLABS HAWAII
i

friction of 600 pounds per square foot psf For piers located on the ewa side and within
eight feet 8 of the ewa side of the proposed building the supporting capacity from the
upper eight feet 8 of soil should be neglected due to potential loss of soil contact in the
future The weight of the foundations may be discounted when sizing the drilled piers in
the foundation design All piers should have a minimum diameter of 16 inches and length
not less than eight feet 8 A minimum pier spacing of three 3 times the pier diameter
center to center is also recommended to avoid reduction in capacity due to group action
Pier settlements are estimated to be on the order of less than 4 inch Settlements
should be elastic and should occur as the loads are applied
Casing of the piers and dewatering may be required during construction due to the
presence of granular soils and groundwater as encountered in our borings Therefore
unit prices for casing and dewatering should be obtained during bidding Isolated hard
horizons or boulders may be encountered in pier drilling during construction as
evidenced by the resistant zones encountered in the borings during our field exploration
Therefore unit prices for rock drilling should also be obtained during bidding and should
be available as a contingency during construction
The load bearing capacities of drilled piers depend to a large extent on the
contact between the pier shaft and the surrounding soils Therefore proper construction
techniques are important The contractor should exercise care in drilling the pier holes
and in placing concrete into the holes Drilling by methods utilizing drilling fiuids is not
recommended because the fluids could saturate and weaken the surrounding soil A
representative of Geolabs Hawaii should be present to observe the installation of drilled
pier during construction

A low shrink concrete mix should be used to provide close contact between the
pier shafts and the surrounding soil The concrete should be placed prompily after drilling
to reduce the potential of caving in The concrete should also be placed in a suitable
manner to reduce segregation of the aggregates from the concrete mix
Concrete Floor Slab
The site is underlain by a layer of silty clay soils which is moderately expansive
when subjected to moisture fluctuations Unless improvements supported by these soils
are properly designed there is a potential for future distress to these improvements
resulting from the swelling of these soils when wetted To reduce the potential for
structural distress resulting from the swelling of the on site expansive soils we
recommend that the concrete slabs be supported on 12 inches of non expansive select
fill material The non expansive select fill should extend at least two feet 2 beyond the
perimeter of the proposed structure
The select fill material should be placed as soon as possible after the scarification
and recompaction of the existing subgrade soils The select fill would serve as a layer
of moist soil above the underlying potentially expansive subgrade soils and would reduce
the loss of moisture from the subgrade soils The reduction in drying should reduce the
potential swelling of the underlying moderately expansive clayey soils Compaction
requirements for the subgrade soils and select fill are discussed in the Site Grading
section of this report
The prepared capping fill should be wetted and kept moist prior to final placement
of concrete Concrete floor slabs should be supported on a six inch 6 layer of No 3B
Fine gravel ASTM C33 No 67 Gradation above the capping fill to serve as a slab
cushion and capillary break In order to protect against future moisture infiltration and
subsequent damage to the floor coverings an impervious moisture barrier such as a
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3032 00 Page 10
plastic membrane on top of the slab cushion is recommended A one inch 1 layer of
moist fine sand should be placed over the vapor barrier membrane to aid in curing of slab
concrete
Where the slab is subjected to vehicular traffic such as the garage slab in lieu of
the No 3B Fine gravel capillary break a six inch 6 layer of base course compacted to
95 percent of its maximum dry density as determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
is recommended
Conventional slab on grade construction may then be utilized for the proposed
structure constructed on the 12 inch thick select fill However it is recommended that
flexible floor coverings such as carpet or sheet vinyl be utilized to mask any minor slab
cracking It is also recommended that interior walls be designed to incorporate some
flexibility to accommodate some possible movements
In addition due to the proximity of the Board of Water Supply easement to the
edge of the proposed residence there is a possibility that the easement may be
excavated to expose the pipings in the future As a result the floor slab for the proposed
residence may be undermined and become distressed due to ground subsidence in the
vicinity of the excavation To reduce the potential of distress to and cracking of the floor
slab it may be prudent to design the slab on the ewa side of the house to span for an
unsupported length of at least ten feet 10 By reinforcing the slab to span for at least
ten feet 10 the potential for a distressed slab due to the possible excavation of the
easement may be reduced

Prior to the commencement of the earthwork the areas within the contract
grading limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation
debris and other deleterious materials including the foundations and

Any soft weak or yielding areas disclosed during clearing operations should
be over excavated to expose firm ground and the resulting excavation
should be backfilled with well compacted engineered fill Contract

excavation and engineered fill construction to account for variations in the
over excavation quantities
Areas that are to receive fills should be scarified to a depth of eight inches
8 moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above optimum moisture
content and compacted to between 85 and 90 percent relative compaction
Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as
a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in
accordance with ASTM D 1557 78 test procedures
All fill and backfill material should be tested for suitability for its intended use
by Geolabs Hawaii before delivering to the site Only select fill should be
used within the upper 12 inches below the finished subgrade in building and
