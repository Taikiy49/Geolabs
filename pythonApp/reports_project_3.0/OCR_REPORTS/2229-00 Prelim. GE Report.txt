INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our preliminary geotechnical
engineering exploration performed for the proposed Intelect Facility in
Mililani Industrial Park of Mililani on the island of Oahu Hawaii
The
general location of the project is shown on the Project Location Map
Plate 1

This report summarizes the findings of our preliminary exploration and
the geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analyses of
the data obtained during the exploration These recommendations are
intended for design input and are not intended for construction estimation
or bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is our understanding that the proposed facility will consist of
three 3 phases of development Only Phase 1 is considered under our
current scope of work
Phase 1 of the development includes a one to two story steel framed
building open parking lots and planter areas
Site grading involving up to about three 3 feet of fill and four 4
feet of cut is planned to achieve a finish floor elevation of 854 0 feet
above Mean Sea Level MSL for the proposed building
The future Phases 2 and 3 may include expansion of the currently
proposed structure and parking lots These phases of development are not
under consideration in the current preliminary geotechnical engineering
exploration
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our preliminary exploration was to obtain an overview
of the subsurface conditions in the Phase 1 development area of the
project site The information obtained will be used to develop a
generalized soil rock data set for the formulation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertinent to the site grading foundation and
pavement design of the proposed Phase 1 development of the project

The subsurface conditions of the Phase 1 development area were
explored by drilling six 6 borings to depths ranging from 14 0 to 15 5
feet below the existing ground surface The approximate locations of
these borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2

stiff to hard clayey silts residual soil and saprolite to at least the
full depth of the borings drilled for this exploration

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration encountered very stiff to hard clayey silts
extending to at least a depth of 15 5 feet below the existing ground
surface at the proposed building location Therefore shallow footing
foundations may be used to support the proposed building without
significant settlements
Laboratory swell tests indicate that the in situ clayey silts have low
to moderate expansion potential when saturated from an air dried
condition Therefore slabs on grade may be used if the slab subgrade is
kept moist and prevented from drying prior to concrete placement
Site Grading
Based on the proposed finished floor elevation it is our
understanding that up to about three 3 feet of fill and about four 4
feet of cut are currently planned to provide a pad for the proposed
At the onset of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits
should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed Any unsuitable materials such
as debris and rubbish vegetation and other deleterious materials should
be removed and disposed of properly off site
Area designated to receive fill and the subgrades of slabs or
pavements should be scarified to a minimm depth of six 6 inches below
the cleared grade level moisture conditioned and prevented from drying
and recompacted to a minimm of 90 of its maximm dry density per ASIM
Test Method D 1557 78
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2229 00 Page 6
Any loose soft or yielding areas encountered should be over excavated
to in situ firm ground and replaced with on site clayey silts or imported
non expansive granular material compacted in 8 inch loose lifts to a
minimm of 90 of its maximm dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
We believe that either the on site clayey silts or imported
non expansive select borrow material may be used as a source of fill or
backfill material Structural fills under the proposed building should be
moisture conditioned to within 3 of optimm moisture content on the wet
side of optimm moisture content and compacted in 10 inch loose lifts to
a minimum of 90 of its maximm dry density per ASTM Test Method
D 1557 78
Site grading should provide drainage of surface run off away from all
building foundations slabs and pavements
Foundation
Based on the subsoil conditions encountered we recommend that shallow
spread or continuous footing foundations be used to support the proposed
building bearing on the in situ very stiff clayey silt or compacted
structural f 1 1
An allowable bearing capacity of 3 000 p s f may be used for the
above foundation design This value is for total dead plus live loads
only and may be increased by one third 1 3 for short duration uplift
wind or seismic loads
The foundations should be embedded a minimm of 18 inches below the
lowest adjacent finish grade Uplift wind seismic or other loading
conditions may require that this minimm footing embedment be increased

Based upon our exploration it is our opinion that the following
pavement section may be used for preliminary design purposes
2 Inch Asphalt Concrete
6 Inch Base Course 95 Compaction
8 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
Laboratory swell and CBR tests should be performed on the actual
subgrade soils during construction and the above design section may be
revised if necessary The recommended section considers medium traffic
and that good drainage will be provided below and adjacent to the paved
areas
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comment
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this review
is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for
misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Observation
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during the site grading and
foundation construction phases of the project This is to observe
compliance with the design concepts specifications or recommendations and
to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ
from that anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations
given in this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the project
site

Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by
drilling six 6 borings to depths of 14 0 to 15 5 feet below the existing
ground surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate
2 A truck mounted drill rig was used to drill the borings

textural examination in the field by our engineer who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory All soils
were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification Systen A summary of the materials encountered are
presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 6
Soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving a 2 inch
a 140 pound hammer free falling 30 inches The blow counts used to drive
the sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the Boring Iogs at the
