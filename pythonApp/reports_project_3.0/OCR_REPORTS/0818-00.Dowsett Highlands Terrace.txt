INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our preliminary soils
engineering and geology investigation at the project site
The purpose of the investigation was to evaluate the general
surface and subsurface conditions at the site to develop soil
parameters for the design of the development site grading
Our investigation consisted of the following
1 A geologic reconnaissance at the site
2 Field borings in areas accessible to a drill rig
3 Hand sampling in areas inaccessible to a drill rig
4 Laboratory testing of surface and subsurface soil
samples
5 A review of the proposed grading plan
6 A review of available grading plans for the existing
subdivision
Based on the data from our investigation we have developed
general recommendations for the development site grading

PLANNED DEVELOPMENT
The proposed residential development consists of two separate
but nearby areas
The preliminary plans for the lower development area include
an access road about 560 feet long into the site and twelve
12 single family residential lots Cuts and fills less
than 5 feet are planned for the road construction Site
grading is not planned for the individual lot development
The preliminary plans for the upper development area include
an access road through the site about 1460 feet long and
45 residential lots Major site grading will be required
for the construction of the access road Cuts up to 30
feet and fills up to 25 feet are planned Drain pipes
up to 48 inches in diameter are planned under the fills
over the natural drainageways Most of the lots will not
be graded
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located at the slope break on the south
east side of Nuuanu Valley Oahu Hawaii The site is bounded
on the southeast by a spur of the Koolau Range forming a steep
ridge on the southeast flank of Nuuanu Valley The north
east and western portions of the site are bounded by resi
dential developments similar in character to the proposed
development

The project site is divided into two areas by present resi
dential development and for the purpose of this report will
be referred to as the upper area and the lower area
Plates 1 2
The site in general is very steep and hilly with a total
topographic relief of 380 feet Surveyed elevations range
from about 470 to 650 feet in the lower area and from about
610 to 850 feet in the upper area Overall the site consists
of a series of moderately sloping ridges and steep sided
ravines headed by very steep sided rocky highlands In
addition two other areas of particular importance can be
delineated One a large talus deposit above the large
central drainageway and two a wet poorly drained area
in the lower center of the site Portions of the site have
been previously graded and several previously filled areas
have been identified Plates 1 2

GEOLOGY
The project site is located on a ridge of the Koolau Range
on the upper slopes of Nuuanu Valley This ridge is an
erosional remnant eroded from the Leeward flank of the
Koolau Range and consisting of steep rocky slopes of
relatively unweathered dense basalt The upper rocky
slopes consist of massive basalt outcrops forming vertical
cliffs fractured in place basalt and dense boulders some
very large At the foot of the slopes extensive erosion of
the upper valley sides has deposited large quantities of
boulders and other talus debris Much of this boulder
colluvium has weathered in place forming a thick soil
layer of basically clayey silt with some boulders This
layer has been further eroded into rounded ridges and steep
sided valleys to form the present topography
The site is situated near the sharp break in slopes that
marks the upper extent of the older alluvium Some of the
more recent talus deposits presently exist as piles of dense
unweathered boulders and debris Two such talus deposits
were located in the field exploration Plate 2 and it is
possible that other deposits may be masked by the vegetation
and soil cover

Since the valley flanks are actively undergoing erosion
numerous large surface boulders can be found throughout
the site especially in the stream beds of the drainageways
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Subsurface Exploration
Due to the difficult terrain and heavy vegetation at the
site five test borings were drilled in the accessible
portions of the area and four hand dug test holes were
made in the inaccessible interior portions The approxi
mate locations of these test borings and test holes are
shown on the Site Plans Plates 1 and 2
A detailed description of the field exploration boring
and test hole logs and laboratory test results are pre
sented in the Appendices
Soil Conditions
The field exploration encountered dark brown to reddish
brown stiff clayey silt with a few basalt boulders over
most of the site except for the lower portions of the
drainageways and the rocky highlands This surficial
layer consists of recent alluvium slope wash about 1
to several feet thick with some unweathered boulders
Underlying this layer is a substantial deposit of wea
thered boulder colluvium of unknown thickness consisting
of stiff to hard clayey silts with some partially weathered
rock

The bottoms of the drainageways are filled with large
boulders river gravel with sand and some soft silt and
clay In the higher sections some of the drainageways
cut into solid rock
The rocky highlands consist of relatively unweathered
hard Koolau Basalt in place with 1 to several feet of
alluvium as cover
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration and geologic reconnaissance at the
site indicated generally competent subsoils However
due to the relatively steep terrain and high rainfall
in the area special design procedures and field moni
toring of the site grading operations are important to
this development
The site is situated over an extensive deposit of Older
Alluvium which is generally a competent material Deep
drainageways have been cut through the site creating a
hilly terrain High fills up to 25 feet will be required
to fill portions of the drainageways Field monitoring of
the drainageway fills is essential
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 818 00 P 9
The field exploration also disclosed areas of talus deposits
relatively fresh rocky hillsides and wet areas The appro
ximate locations of these areas are indicated on the Site
Plans Plates 1 and 2 Development in these areas may be
difficult and should be done with care Additional borings
should be made in these areas to further identify their
subsurface soil conditions
Site Grading
In general the site grading will consist of cutting the
upper ridges and filling the drainageways to construct the
access roadway through the site Most of the proposed lot
areas will not be graded
Cuts up to 30 feet and fills up to 25 feet are planned
cleared of all vegetation debris and soft material
Prior to filling the existing ground should be
scarified to a minimum of 6 inches and compacted
to a minimum of 85 of its maximum dry density
established by ASTM D 1557 test method
located in a high rainfall area the on site subsoils
have in place moisture contents about 10 to 30 percent
above its optimum moisture content
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 818 00 P
Therefore drying of the excavated material may be
required before using it as fill material
It is our opinion that the normal 90 compaction
standard used in other drier areas should be
modified to 85 for this project The 85 com
paction standard will be adequate for the roadway
embankment fill and light building loads anticipated
The fills should be placed in 8 inch maximum compacted
lifts
Based on the results of our in place densities and
maximum density determination it appears that a
shrinkage factor of 5 to 10 percent should be
considered for the on site cut and fill quantities
The in place dry densities averaged about 61 p c f
compared to 65 p c f at 85 of its maximum dry
density
If additional off site borrow is required the
material should be tested and approved by the
soils engineer prior to transportation to the
site The material should generally be non expan
sive and should not contain large percentages of
boulders organic or deleterious material

Fills placed where the existing ground is steeper
than 5 horizontal to 1 vertical should be keyed
and benched into the existing firm ground to
properly bond the new fill to the slope For
details see Plate 3 This detail is essential
for the stability of the fills particularly
the large fills in the drainageways The filling
operation should start at the lowest point and
continue up in level compacted layers
Slopes Due to the high moisture content of the
subsoils two horizontal to one vertical 2 1
cut and fill slopes are recommended for the site
Fill slopes should be constructed by overfilling
past the design fill slope and then cutting back
Water should be diverted away from the tops of
slopes and slope planting utilized to reduce
erosion
Drainage benches should be provided for slopes
having a vertical height greater than 15 feet
Subdrains To reduce piping and seepage pressure
buildup subdrains should be placed in the bottom
of the drainageways prior to filling

House Foundations
The design of the house foundations is not included in
the scope of this report and should be done on an indi
vidual lot basis by an engineer Houses constructed on
sloping areas steeper than about 2 horizontal to 1 vertical
should be done with care
The foundation design should consider the topographic and
subsurface conditions at the site The foundations should
be set back from the tops of slopes or embeded a sufficient
distance below the finish grade to resist the surface soil
creep movements
Drainage Structures
Drain pipes ranging from 24 to 48 inches in diameter will
be required below the fills in the natural drainageways
Wing walls will be required at both ends of the drain
pipes
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 818 00 P 13
An allowable bearing value of 2000 p s f is recommended
for the walls resting on the stiff natural ground or
compacted fill
For lateral earth pressure design an equivalent fluid
pressure of 45 p c f is recommended for walls with
a level backfill and 70 p c f is recommended for a
2 horizontal to 1 vertical backslope The above values
assume that granular material is used as backfill material
and a subdrainage system is utilized behind the walls
A value of 0 35 is recommended for the coefficient of
friction between soil and concrete
Retaining Walls
Retaining walls may be utilized along portions of the
roadway and for some of the driveways
Structures located close to or along slopes will be
subjected to the effects of downhill slope creep To
reduce these effects retaining walls located on or near
the tops of slopes should be embeded such that there is
a minimum horizontal set back distance of 8 feet measured
from the outer edge of the footing foundation out to the
slope face
Additional recommendations for the wall design as presented
for the wing walls should be followed

Rockfalls
As with any development along a hillside there is a
possibility of individual boulders rolling down from
the hillside
Typical methods to reduce the hazard of falling boulders
include a boulder sweep and or rock bolting construction
of a boulder catchment area barrier fences or walls and
the use of the natural vegetation to help stop the boulders
Due to the very steep topography and heavy vegetation the
practicality of a boulder sweep or catchment area is ques
tidnable
The natural trees along the upper portions of the site
should be retained to act as a natural barrier against
rolling boulders Some form of barrier fence or wall
can also be considered
Roadway Pavement
Asphaltic concrete pavements are planned for the project
roads
Based on the laboratory CBR tests on the on site soils
the preliminary pavement design is as follows
2 inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 inch Base Course
6 inch Select Borrow
14 inch Total Pavement Thickness
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 818 00 P 15
The base course should be crushed rock or other granular
material with a CBR of 85 and conforming with Section
31 2 Standard Specification for Public Work Construction
or equivalent Non expansive select borrow should have
a minimum CBR value of 25 and consist of crusher run
quarry waste mudrock coral sand or cinder material
Both the base course and select borrow should be
compacted to 95 of its maximum density
Additional CBR tests should be performed on the actual
subgrade soil samples at the time of roadway construction
and the above design section can be revised accordingly
Additional Explorations
The topography and heavy vegetation on the site restricted
access of our drilling equipment Additional borings should
be drilled in the interior portions of the site after some
clearing and access into the area is provided The borings
should be drilled in the natural drainageways and talus
areas to verify the conclusions of our hand sampling and
geologic reconnaissance
Design Review
The plans for site grading and utility construction should
be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and comments
prior to construction to see that the intent of our recom
mendations is included in the project plans and specifications

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during
earthwork construction particularly the fill placement
slope cuts and to monitor the contractor s grading opera
tions
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be
observed by a soils engineer during construction to see
that adequate bearing has been obtained and that the
subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings
of our subsurface investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive
tested and approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling
to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent
upon the above inspections

FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored in January
and February 1979 by drilling five test borings utilizing truck mounted
drilling equipment and by digging four test holes by hand in areas
inaccessible for the drilling equipment
The depths of the borings varied from 16 5 to 21 feet below the
existing ground surface and the test holes terminated at depths of
0 5 to 2 5 feet
The approximate locations of the borings and test holes are
shown on the enclosed Site Plans Plates 1 2
The soils were classified by visual and textural examination
in the field by our geologist or engineer who continuously monitored
the test hole excavations and drilling operations The classifications
were made in accordance with the Unified Soil Classification System
A graphic presentation of the soils is presented in the Boring Logs
Plates A 1 through A 5 A description of the soils encountered in
the test pit is presented in the Test Hole Log Plate A 6
In the borings soil samples were obtained by driving either
barrel sampler with a 140 pound hammer free falling a distance of
30 inches The blow counts used to drive the sampler the last
12 inches are shown on the Boring Logs at the appropriate sample
depths
