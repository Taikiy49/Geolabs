Regional Geology
The Island of Hawaii the largest in the Hawaiian Archipelago covers an area of
approximately 4 000 square miles The island was formed by the activity of the
following five shield volcanoes Kohala long extinct Mauna Kea activity during recent
geologic time Hualalai last erupted in 1801 1803 and Mauna Loa and Kilauea
both still active
The project site is located on the eastern flank of Mauna Loa which has been
built up by successive accumulation of basaltic lava flows and pyroclastic materials
The site is near the contact between the southeast flank of Mauna Kea and the
northeast flank of Mauna Loa The near surface lava flows are from Mauna Loa and
overlie the older Mauna Kea Shield The lava flows of the Mauna Kea Shield are
believed to be Pliocene to Pleistocene in age and belong to the upper member of the
Hamakua Volcanic Series The near surface lava flows of the Mauna Loa Shield may
be on the order of only 750 to 1 500 years old However the 1881 lava flow of Mauna
Loa is nearby
Halai Hill or Puu Halai is the largest of a string of cinder cones in Hilo which
includes two other adjacent small cinder cones Puu Hono and a smaller unnamed
cone These cones along with Kupapau Hill and Puu Iaone to the northwest of the site
follow a string of cinder cones that delineates the northeast rift of Mauna Loa
The lava formation encountered at the site appears to be pahoehoe type
originating from the creation of Halai itself Pahoehoe is characterized by a smooth
rope like or billowy surface and an internal structure of vesicular porous rock
However closer examination of the rock core samples from our borings indicates that
the lava formation may be welded spatter from the Halai eruptive event rather that a
flow deposition
Cavities are commonly encountered in basaltic lavas especially pahoehoe lava
Cavities form when the lava was still in a molten state and represent both lava tubes

Existing Site Conditions
The project site is adjacent to an existing radio facility at the summit of Halai Hill
in Hilo on the Island of Hawaii The site is accessible via an existing partially paved
service road leading from Ulili Street The project site lies near the end of a paved
access road for the existing radio and communication facilities that are used by various
agencies
The site is bounded by the adjacent existing radio facility to the south and vacant
land to the remaining sides Based on the preliminary site plan provided the proposed
facility will be located on a relatively flat area with a maximum elevation difference up to
5 feet Most of the site is grassed but the northern portion of the site encroaches into
the dense tropical vegetation on the slopes of the hill
Based on the topographic map provided the project site ground elevation is on
the order of about 345 feet above Mean Sea Level MSL

Earthquakes and Seismicity
In general earthquakes that occur throughout the world are caused by shifts in
the tectonic plates In contrast earthquake activity in Hawaii is primarily linked to
volcanic activity Therefore earthquake activity in Hawaii generally occurs before or
during volcanic eruptions In addition earthquakes may result from the underground

Seismic Design Considerations
It should be noted that the Island of Hawaii is within Seismic Zone 4 with an
effective peak ground acceleration of 0 40g in general accordance with the
requirements of the 1997 Uniform Building Code UBC Based on the subsurface
materials encountered and the geologic setting of the area the project site may be
classified from a seismic analysis standpoint as a Very Dense Soil and Soft Rock Profile
corresponding to a Soil Profile Type SC
Based on our review of the available geologic information the project site does
not appear to be located in the immediate proximity of mapped geologic fault structures
Therefore the near source factors Na and Nv may be assumed to be 1 0 for this
project site

Based on our experience in the project area cavities and or voids are commonly
encountered in the pahoehoe lava flows that characterize the project site To reduce the
potential for loss of foundation support resulting from the collapse of cavities below
foundations we recommend implementing a cavity probing and grouting program for
the new structure foundations during construction This would include the mat
foundation option for the antenna tower and the one story reinforced concrete structure
For estimating purposes we recommend drilling probe holes at 10 foot on
centers for the continuous strip footings In addition probe holes should be drilled at
each isolated spread footing location one probe per 5 0 square feet The probe holes
should be at least 3 inches in diameter and should extend to a depth of at least 10 feet
below the planned bottom of foundation

Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling three
borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 3 to depths ranging from about 19 5 to
49 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate boring locations are shown
on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were drilled using a truck mounted drill rig equipped
with continuous flight augers and coring tools
Our geologist classified the materials encountered in the borings by visual and
textural examination in the field in general accordance with ASTM D 2488 Standard
Practice for Description and Identification of Soils and monitored the drilling operations on
a near continuous full time basis These classifications were further reviewed visually
and by testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general accordance with
ASTM D 2487 Standard Practice for Classification of Soils for Engineering Purposes
Unified Soil Classification System as shown on the Soil Log Legend Plate A 0 1
Graphic representations of the materials encountered are presented on the Logs of
Borings Plates A 1 through A 3
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained in general accordance with
ASTM D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 3 inch OD Modified
California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches In addition some samples
were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with ASTM D 1586
Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 2 inch OD standard
penetration sampler using the same hammer and drop The blow counts needed to drive
the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown as the
Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths The
penetration resistance shown on the logs of borings indicates the number of blows
required for the specific sampler type used The blow counts may need to be factored to
obtain the Standard Penetration Test SPT blow counts
Core samples of the rock materials encountered at the project site were obtained
by using diamond core drilling techniques in general accordance with ASTM D 2113
Diamond Core Drilling for Site Investigation Core drilling is a rotary drilling method that
uses a hollow bit to cut into the rock formation The rock material left in the hollow core of
the bit is mechanically recovered for examination and description Rock cores were
described in general accordance with the Rock Description System as shown on the
Rock Log Legend Plate A 0 2 The Rock Description System is based on the publication
Suggested Methods for the Quantitative Description of Discontinuities in Rock Masses by
the International Society for Rock Mechanics March 1977

Laboratory Testing
Moisture Content ASTM D 2216 and Unit Weight determinations ASTM D 2937
were performed on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of
soil properties The test results are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate
sample depths
Two Unconfined Compression tests ASTM D 7012 Method C were performed
on selected core runs to evaluate the unconfined compressive strength of the basalt
rock formation encountered The test results are presented on Plate B 1
One laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR test ASTM D 1883 was
performed on a bulk sample of the near surface volcanic ash soils to evaluate the
pavement support characteristics of the soils The test results are presented on
Plate B 2
Three minimum resistivity tests ASTM G 57 were performed on selected soil
samples obtained from our field exploration The test results are provided on Plate B 3
