Pile Driving Report
Proposed Kalaheo Substation at
Kailua Wastewater Treatment Plant Modification Phase III
Kailua Oahu Hawaii
In general accordance with your request we have observed the pile driving
operation at the above referenced project from January 4th through 7th
Sixteen and one half 164 inch octagonal precast prestressed concrete piles were
utilized A single acting diesel hammer with a rated energy of 49 500
foot pound was used to drive the piles at the locations shown on the Pile Location Plan
Plate 1 Based on our soil report findings and pile transportation consideration we have
recommended that piles be cast to 60 and 65 foot lengths
A total of 14 piles including 2 replacement piles were driven to depths ranging from
about 23 feet to 123 feet below the existing site ground surface The locations of the
driven piles are shown on Plate 1 A summary of the pile driving data is attached for your
reference
Both Pile No 12 and its replacement pile Pile No 12A were broken during the pile
driving operation Their replacement pile Pile No 12B was driven at about 3 feet to the
west of Pile No 12 and it was found to be tilted with an inclination of about 1 inches in
horizontal to 3 feet in vertical Based on the pile driving records it is our opinion that Pile
No 12B will still be able to provide the allowable pile capacity of 50 tons Our telephone
conversation with the HECO s structural engineer indicated that no additional replacement
pile will be needed for Pile No 12
