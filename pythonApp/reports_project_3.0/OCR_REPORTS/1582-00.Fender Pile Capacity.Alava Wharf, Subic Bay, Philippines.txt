INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
investigation performed for the Alava Wharf Fender Repair project at the
Naval Ship Repair Facility at Subic Bay Philippines
The general location of the project site is shown on the Project
Location Map Plate l
PURPOSE AND SCOPE OF WORK
The purpose of the investigation was 1 to explore the subsurface
soil conditions along the seaward edge of the existing wharf and 2 to
evaluate the vertical capacity of the steel fender piles and corres
ponding embedment depth required
The wood fender piles along the landside edge of the Alava Wharf
were not included in the present scope of our work If desired we could
provide additional consultation services for these wood fender piles

A repair program including installation of a new fender system is
presently in progress at Alava Wharf The fender system generally
consists of vertical H piles approximately 6 feet apart connected by
steel wide flange wales to an energy absorbing rubber marine fender
attached to the face of the wharf
About one third Phase 1 portion of the fender system has been
installed along the western end of the wharf
There was concern that some of the initially installed H piles
appear to indicate signs of some settlement as evidenced by uneveness in
the rubber fender
The investigation was performed in general accordance with our
proposal of and included a review of construction
drawings field reconnaissance drilling and sampling of five 5
borings in situ vane shear strength tests laboratory soils testing
engineering analysis of the field and laboratory data and report
preparation

The boring logs and the laboratory test results are presented in
the appendices of this report The location of the borings are shown on
the Site Plan Plate 2
During the field exploration two pile load tests were conducted
in September 1985 under our observation on two existing H piles to
evaluate the vertical load carrying capacity of the piles The pile load
test results were presented in our report The load test
results have also been utilized in our engineering analyses for this
report
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
The three phase fender repair project basically consists of removal
of the existing timber fender system at Alava Wharf and installation of
a new H pile and rubber energy absorber fender system along the seaward
face of the wharf and a timber pile and rubber energy absorber fender
system along portions of the shoreside face of the wharf
A section indicating the new H pile fender system is shown on
Plate 3
It is our understanding that the design vertical load on the H pile
is limited to the weight of the pile plus same contribution due to the
weight of the adjacent wide flange wale system Therefore the total
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Page 4
vertical load or dead weight on the H pile is computed to be about 6 tons
for the 100 foot long design pile length
The horizontal load on the H pile fender system depends on many
variables such as the weight of the ship velocity of the ship angle of
approach wind etc It has been assumed in our pile deflection analysis
that at maximum capacity the rubber marine fender can deflect up to
one half of its normal thickness of 33 inches
SITE DESCRIPTION
Alava Wharf is an open type reinforced concrete wharf structure
with reinforced concrete piles and deck The majority of the wharf
approximately 1 700 linear feet L F was constructed between 1953 and
1956 A 600 L F extension was subsequently added to the northwest end
during a period from 1967 to 1970 bringing the total length of the
structure to 2 300 L F Berth depths along the outboard 2 300 feet are
approximately 40 feet below Mean Low Water Level An additional 800 feet
of berth with a 25 foot draft is available on the inboard side at the
northwest end
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The project site is located in the eastern portion of the U S
Naval Station in Subic Bay The Naval Station and Olongapo City to the
north are situated on the estuarine delta of the Kalaklan River
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Page 5
Examination of the geomorphology of the area leads to the
conjecture that this delta is located in a valley which was cut into
pyroclastic rocks during a lower stand or stands of the sea by older
streams originating primarily in the Mount Balakibok Volcanic Area As
the sea level rose to its current stand this valley became infilled with
terrigenous soft and compressible sediments
Owing to the low energy estuarine or deltaic environment of
deposition these soft and compressible sediments are poorly consolidated
fine sands clays and silts underlain at deeper depths by more weathered
pyroclastic rocks

DISCUSSION AND FINDINGS
The borings encountered relatively uniform subsurface conditions
along the wharf consisting of very loose silty sand and soft clays to
depths in excess of 150 feet below the wharf deck
Bue to the poor soil conditions the fender piles derive minimal
end bearing support and are supported almost entirely by frictional
resistance between the H pile and soil The mechanism of the friction
pile support is shown schematically on Plate 4 The pile load tests
basically measure the pile capacity in the condition shown on Plate 4
Under lateral loading due to ship impact the fender pile deflects
as shown schematically on Plate 5 During deflection the frictional
resistance between the soil and pile from the mudline down to the point
of fixity is reduced or possibly eliminated for a short time due to loss
of contact between the soil and pile
GEOLABS HAWAII
w o 1582 00 Page 7
In our analysis of the vertical fender pile capacity we have
utilized the field and laboratory shear strength data as well as
information from the field pile load tests to evaluate the pile vertical
capacity under zero lateral load Lateral pile analysis was then
performed to determine possible ranges of the depth to the point of
fixity of the pile The net vertical pile capacity was then determined
by subtracting the loss of pile shaft frictional resistance above the
point of fixity from the total vertical pile capacity
Static Vertical Pile Capacity
The static vertical capacity of the friction pile no lateral load
was evaluated based on the shear strength parameters from the following
SOUrCES
a field vane shear tests conducted in the borings to measure the
in situ undrained shear strength
b torvane shear tests conducted on relatively undisturbed thin
wall tube samples in the field and laboratory
c unconsolidated undrained triaxial shear tests conducted in the
laboratory on relatively undisturbed samples obtained from the
borings
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1582 00 Page
The above undrained tests are used primarily for soft to medium
fine grained soils such as the soft clays encountered about 70 to 80
feet below the deck The upper loose silty fine sand deposits
encountered from about 50 to 70 80 feet below the deck were found to have
about 20 to 40 fines therefore it is our opinion that this upper soil
would act essentially as a fine grained soil and the undrained tests
above are applicable
Based on the shear strength parameters obtained from selected
samples at the drilled borings the calculated static frictional capacity
of the 100 foot long HP 14x117 size H pile was found to vary from 27 to
40 tons at 4 and 5 and 20 tons for Boring 2
This compares with the ultimate pile capacity of about 37 tons not
including the weight of the pile determined from the two pile load tests
recently performed on existing fender piles near Boring Nos 4 and 5
Vertical Pile Capacity Under Lateral Loading
During ship impact the fender pile will probably deflect as shown
on Plate 5 A portion of the static capacity of the pile may be lost due
to the pile movement under this condition
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1582 00 Page 9
In order to evaluate the potential temporary reduction of pile
capacity due to impact on the fender pile lateral pile analysis was
conducted to determine the shape of the deflected pile and the point of
fixity zero movement
The lateral load on the fender pile due to the ship impact is
highly variable since it is affected by many conditions In the
analysis we have assumed that at maximum lateral load the rubber fender
will be compressed to one half of its original length or about 17
inches
The lateral pile analysis was then performed by the use of the
beam column on an elastic medium theory supplemented by the use of p y
curves to represent the soil strength
Based on the analysis the point of fixity of the pile at maximum
deflection was calculated to vary fram 10 to 24 feet below the mudline
If the pile resistance above the point of fixity is subtracted from
the total static vertical pile capacity as determined on the previous
section a net pile capacity of 13 to 39 tons is obtained for a 100 foot
long H pile Therefore it appears that the net factor of safety F S
for the 6 ton weight of H pile could vary from a low of 2 at Boring No 2
to about 6 at Boring No 3 location
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Page 10
Based on our analysis it appears that the existing 100 foot long
H piles have sufficient embedment to support the 6 ton weight of the
piles
RECOMMENDATIONS
Existing Fender Piles
The analysis and pile load tests indicate that the existing
100 foot H piles may be adequate to support the 6 ton vertical design
loads However it is our understanding that due to potential
difficulties with the rust proof coating application on the upper section
of the installed piles these H piles may have to be extended and driven
about another 23 feet deeper
The longer length of pile will increase the weight of the pile by
about 117 pounds per additional foot of length Based on the field and
laboratory shear strength measurements obtained we believe that this
additional weight could be supported by the underlying soil
It is our understanding that there is also an alternative procedure
being considered for repairing the coating on the H piles This may
consist of pulling out all of the existing driven in place piles to allow
reapplication of the coating and then redriving the piles back to the
original depth However due to the disturbance of the soft underlying
soils and reduction in soil shear strength resulting from this pull out
procedure it is our opinion that this procedure should not be used
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1582 00 Page 11
New Fender Pile Installation
We reconmend that the 100 foot pile length be retained for the new
fender piles at the remaining Phases 2 and 3 portions of the fender
repair project
The piles may be driven with a hammer delivering about 18 000 to
lbs of energy per blow
The piles should be driven to their full 100 foot length The blow
counts to drive each foot of pile should be recorded to provide a
comparison of the soil resistance along the wharf and to detect unusually
soft conditions
We do not recommend the use of the blow counts with a dynamic
formula as a final basis to determine the pile capacity since the
formulas do not correspond well to very loose or soft soil condition such
as those present at the wharf site Pile driving on soft cohesive soil
breaks down the soil structure so it leses much of its stength
temporarily The soft clay reconsolidates after driving thereby
increasing the shear resistance of the soil In very loose uniform fine
grained sand the pile driving can cause liquefaction of the soils
temporarily reducing the strength of the soils adjacent to the pile
surfaces In both soil conditions the actual resistance of the pile
Will be larger than those indicated by the blow counts
GEOLABS HAWAI
W Page 12
Test Piles
We recommend that new test piles be driven in the Phase 2 and 3
areas prior to the production pile driving to confirm the uniformity of
the soil conditions along the fender pile alignment We believe
approximately 10 of the total piles should be driven as test piles
Pile Load Tests
A minimum of three 3 additional pile load tests should be
performed at three 3 selected locations after the test piles are
Griven The pile load test locations should be selected by the soils
engineer after he has reviewed all of the test pile driving records It
should be noted that the pile load test only measures the static vertical
capacity of the pile without lateral load Our analysis indicates that
during lateral load condition up to one half of the pile embedment or
about 25 feet may be ineffective in providing vertical pile friction
resistance Therefore the piles should be tested in accordance with
ASTM D 1143 81 to a minimum of 24 tons total vertical load
Monitoring
We recommend that the test pile and production pile driving
operation be observed by the soils engineer during construction to see
that adequate bearing has been obtained and that the subsurface
conditions are consistent with the findings of our investigation

W Page 13
If conditions encountered during actual pile driving operations
are different from those presented in this report then appropriate
modifications to the design should be made
Pile Settlement
Small settlements are anticipated due to the light load on the
fender pile
It should be noted that portions of the original Alava Wharf have
experienced considerable settlements in the past Based on previous
investigations it appears that the settlements are a result of
consolidation of the underlying soft compressible soils caused by the
weight of the adjacent land fills and fill placed for the three access
road approaches to the wharf Since the original pile foundations for
the wharf did not extend through the soft sediments they have been
Gragged down by these settlements Although optical survey readings
provided by PWC Subic appear to indicate that the major portion of the
wharf and approach settlements have occured we believe long term
settlements may still continue for many more years Therefore it is
possible that the fender piles in these affected areas could experience
some long term settlements due to secondary consolidation of the soft
compressible soils located below the wharf sites
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 1582 00 Page 14
In light of the above we recommend that the connections between
the marine fender and the wharf should be designed to allow some vertical
adjustments should differential settlement between the wharf and fender
system occur in the future For example the marine fender connection
could be slotted to allow readjustment This detail would also aid in
the installation of the marine fender since it is difficult to drill the
bolt holes and provide a perfectly level fender system
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the fender piles should be forwarded
to the soils engineer for review and written comments prior to
construction to see that the intent of our recommendations is included

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Mn Ms mms i
Moisture content and unit weight determinations were performed on
selected samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil
properties The results of these tests are presented on the Boring Logs
at the appropriate sample depths
To further identify soil characteristics additional tests were
performed These tests include Atterberg Limits and Sieve Analysis to
determine the grain size distribution The results of these tests are
presented on Plates B 1 through B 4
Torvane Shear tests were performed on undisturbed samples to
evaluate the undrained shear strength of the deposits The test results
are summarized on Plates B 1 through B 4
Two Consolidation tests were performed on selected undisturbed
samples to evaluate the settlement characteristics of the natural soils
The test results are presented on Plates B 5 and B 6
Five Unconscolidated Undrained Triaxial Compression Tests were
performed on select undisturbed samples tc evaluate the soil strength
characteristics of the soil The test results are plotted on Plates B 7
through B 12
