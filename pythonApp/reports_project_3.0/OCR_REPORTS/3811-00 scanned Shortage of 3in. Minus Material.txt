Shortage of 3 Inch Minus Material
Kualapuu Wastewater Treatment Plant
We have continued to observe the grading work and test the compaction of the fill
material at the above referenced site on an intermittent basis The contractor is currently
grading the unlined ponds in the southern portion of the site Generally the northern
portions of the ponds and berms require cut while the southern portions are filed The
cut material consists primarily of moderately weathered basalt rock with residual silt soils
Since these ponds will be unlined the plans call for the berms to be constructed with low
permeability silty soils 3 inch minus material to reduce seepage through the berms
It appears that there is insufficient 3 inch minus material generated from the
excavation in the basalt formation to construct the berms In order to accommodate the
type of material being generated from the excavation it is our opinion that the rocky
basalt material may be used in the outer portions of the pond berm provided that the
material is well compacted and the interior portions of the berms are constructed with the
finer 3 inch minus material The contractor has indicated that even with this
modification there is insufficient 3 inch minus material from the excavation Two
alternatives to generate additional 3 inch minus material that have been discussed
include excavating the surface silt soils from an adjacent borrow site and overexcavating
the silt soils within the unlined pond areas then backfilling with coarser rocky material
