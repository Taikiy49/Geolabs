REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from the Waianae
and Koolau shield volcanoes The older Waianae Volcano is estimated to be middle to late
Pliocene in age and forms the bulk of the western one third of the island The younger
Koolau Volcano is estimated to be late Pliocene to early Pleistocene Ice Age in age and
forms the majority of the eastern two thirds of the island The project site is located on the
southern flank of the Koolau Range and its geomorphology and subsurface conditions are
directly related to the glacio eustatic fluctuations of sea level during the Pleistocene Epoch
Ice Age and the genesis of the Honolulu Coastal Plain The subsurface conditions in the
Ala Moana area are dominated by fossil Pleistocene Age coral algal reef deposits and
related deposits which are intercalated with terrigenous sediments laid during high stands
of the sea The base of the stratigraphic section in this area is composed of Koolau
Basalt
Over the last 10 000 years or so the sea level has adjusted to its present stand
Terrigenious and marine sediments have continued to accumulate in low energy estuarine
or lagoonal environments resulting in thick deposits of soft loose sediments along the
coast and in areas that were formerly valleys and drainageways Land development and
reclamation projects within the last 100 years have brought the project area to its present
form
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
We explored the subsurface conditions by drilling and sampling two borings
designated as Boring Nos 1 and 2 to depths of about 16 5 and 19 5 feet below the
existing ground surface The approximate boring locations are shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2
Our field exploration generally encountered a surface fill layer about 1 to 3 5 feet
thick The surface fill layer generally consisted of medium dense to dense coralline sands
and gravels with silt The surface fill layer was underlain by coral detritus to the maximum
depth drilled of about 19 5 feet below the existing ground surface The coral detritus
generally consisted of loose to dense coralline sands and gravels with silt
We encountered groundwater in the borings at depths of 6 8 and 7 9 feet below the
existing ground surface at the time of our field exploration The measured water depths
correspond to about 1 8 and 0 4 feet Mean Sea Level MSL respectively It should be
noted that groundwater levels at the project site may fluctuate depending on tidal
fluctuations seasonal precipitation and other factors Detailed descriptions of the
materials encountered in the borings are presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 and
A 2 in Appendix A Results of the laboratory tests performed on selected soil samples are
presented in Appendix B

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the results of our exploration the project site is generally underlain by fill
and coral detritus consisting of medium dense to dense coralline sands and gravels with
silt We encountered groundwater in the borings at about 6 8 and 7 9 feet below the
existing ground surface at the time of our field exploration
For the anticipated fills up to about 2 feet we estimate the total settlement to be
less than 1 inch We believe that the settlement will occur as the fills are placed Fills
should consist of low expansive materials less than 3 inches in largest dimension with a
CBR swell of less than 1 percent The fill material should also be free of vegetation and
deleterious materials In general the on site coralline sand and gravel materials should be
suitable for fill material Cut and fill slopes at inclination of two horizontal to one vertical
2H 1V and flatter may be used Detailed discussion of these items and our site grading
recommendations are presented in the following sections herein
Site Grading
We anticipate that the site grading work will generally consist of minor cuts and fills
of up to about 2 feet The following grading items are addressed in the succeeding
subsections 1 Site Preparation 2 Fills and Backfills 3 Fill Placement and Compaction
Requirements 4 Excavation and 5 Cut and Fill Slopes
A Geolabs representative should monitor the site grading operations to observe
whether undesirable materials are encountered during the excavation process and to
confirm whether the exposed soil rock conditions are similar to those encountered in our
field exploration
Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork areas within the contract grading limits should be
cleared and grubbed thoroughly Vegetation debris deleterious material and other
unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed properly off site or stockpiled
in a designated area to reduce the potential for contaminating the excavated
materials
Soft and yielding areas encountered during clearing and grubbing should be
over excavated to expose firm natural material and the resulting excavation should
be backfilled with well compacted engineered fill The excavated soft soils may be
used as fill provided that it meets the requirements for fill material
Existing utilities to be abandoned should be removed and the resulting excavation
should be backfilled properly with on site fill materials moisture conditioned to
above the optimum moisture content placed in 6 to 8 inch loose lifts and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction The abandoned

Saturation and subsequent yielding of the exposed subgrade due to inclement

used as fill provided that it meets the requirements for fill material Relative
compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of
the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with
ASTM D 1557
Fills and Backfills
The fill material should consist of low expansive materials less than 3 inches in
largest dimension with a CBR swell of less than 1 percent The fill material should
also be free of vegetation and deleterious materials In general the on site
coralline sand and gravel materials should be suitable for fill material
Select granular fill and imported fill should consist of non expansive granular
material such as crushed coral basalt or cinder sand The material should be
well graded from coarse to fine with particles no larger than 3 inches in largest
dimension and should contain between 10 and 30 percent particles passing the
No 200 sieve The material should have a laboratory CBR value of 20 or more and
should have a maximum swell of less than 1 percent when tested in accordance
with ASTM D 1883 Geolabs should test imported fill materials for conformance with
these recommendations prior to delivery to the project site for its intended use
Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
Fills and backfills should be moisture conditioned to about 2 percent above the
optimum moisture placed in level lifts not exceeding 12 inches in loose thickness
and compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction
refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum
dry density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557
Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to
the maximum dry density

Compaction should be accomplished by sheepsfoot rollers vibratory rollers

Because moisture conditioning and compaction of the subgrade soils are critical

Excavations
Based on our field exploration we anticipate excavations may involve cuts into the
existing fill and coral detritus materials consisting of medium dense to dense
coralline sands and gravels with silt It is anticipated that the coralline sands and
gravels may be excavated with normal heavy excavation equipment such as
ripping with large bulldozers
Cut and Fill Slopes
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered in the borings we anticipate that
cut slopes would expose soils consisting of medium dense to dense coralline sands
and gravels with silt In general we believe that cut slopes into the soil material
may be cut at an inclination of 2H 1V or flatter
Fill slopes should be designed with a slope inclination of 2H 1V or flatter Fills
placed on slopes steeper than 5H 1V should be keyed and benched into the
existing slope to provide stability of the new fill against sliding The filling operations
should start at the lowest point and continue up in level horizontal compacted layers
in accordance with the above fill placement recommendations
Fill slopes in excess of 5 feet in height should be constructed by overfilling and
cutting back to the design slope ratio to obtain a well compacted slope face
Track rolling of slopes in excess of 5 feet in height should not be accepted In the
event that over cutting of a slope occurs keying and benching requirements should
be implemented instead of backfilling the slope to the design grade with sliver fills
Water should be diverted away from the tops of slopes and slope planting should
be provided as soon as possible to reduce the potential for erosion of the finished
slopes

two borings designated as Boring Nos 1 and 2 to depths of about 16 5 and 19 5 feet
