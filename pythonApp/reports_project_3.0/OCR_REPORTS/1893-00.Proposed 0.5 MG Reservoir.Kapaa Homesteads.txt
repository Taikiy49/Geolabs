INTRODUCTION
This report represents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the design of the proposed 0 5 M G reservoir at
Kapaa Homesteads Kauai Hawaii
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our study was to explore the subsurface soil
conditions at the proposed reservoir site in order to provide
recommendations for use in the design of the site grading and foundations
of the proposed facility

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is situated in the Kapaa Homesteads area of
Waipouli Kauai The general location of the site and its vicinity is
shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The proposed reservoir site is located at the northern corner of the
intersection of Olohena Road and Hono Ohala Place and is currently vacant
The site is generally level and covered with low grasses The
existing ground surface elevation of the site varies from approximately
525 to 529 feet above Mean Sea Level To the northwest of the reservoir
site the topography drops sharply into a steep sided drainageway whose
central axis trends towards the northeast
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a 0 5 million gallon capacity reinforced
concrete reservoir structure on the site The reservoir will be
approximately 74 feet in diameter and 16 feet in height

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
The island of Kauai is composed of a single basalt shield volcano
built by the extrusion of the lavas of the Waimea Canyon Volcanic Series
during the late Pliocene more than 2 3 million years before present
Following the cessation of this main shield building phase there was
renewed volcanic activity with the extrusion of the basaltic lavas of the
post erosional Koloa Volcanic Series Interbedded with the Koloa lavas
are the sedimentary conglomerates and breccias alluvium of the Palikea
Formation Preliminary reconnaissance of the site and its vicinity
indicates that the area is underlain by thick residual soils derived from
the in situ weathering of Koloa lavas
Subsurface Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by
drilling and sampling three 3 borings to depths of 31 5 to 51 5 feet
below the existing ground surface The approximate locations of the
borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general the borings encountered a surface layer composed of stiff
reddish brown to mottled reddish brown clayey silt with some sand and
gravel residual soil approximately 13 0 feet in thickness Below this
residual soil the borings encountered 30 0 to 40 0 feet of wet soft to
medium stiff mottled reddish brown clayey silts These clayey silts are
saprolite soils derived from the in situ chemical weathering of rock and
retain some or all of the original structures of the parent rock The
soil materials encountered below this soft to medium stiff stratum were of
similar material and grade from medium stiff to stiff

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The subsurface exploration at the site generally encountered soils
having a moderately to relatively high potential for compressibility and
having high in situ moisture contents Soils of this nature would
experience excessive settlements under the reservoir loads at maximum
water storage conditions
Based on the assumed reservoir slab finish grade our borings and
laboratory data the calculated total settlement of the center of the
reservoir is on the order of 3 5 inches under the maximum water loads
Estimated footing settlement under an assumed column load of 120 kips is
on the order of 1 inch Therefore it is our opinion that the reservoir
should not be constructed as presently planned unless special
alternative design and construction procedures are followed to reduce the
anticipated settlements We believe that either a surcharge program or
removal of overburden to balance the weight of reservoir may be used to
reduce settlements due to the proposed reservoir loads to tolerable
Detailed discussions of the recommended alternatives are presented in
the following sections
Surcharge of Reservoir Site
One alternative to reduce excessive ground settlement due to the
reservoir loads would be to preload or surcharge the site to induce ground
settlements prior to reservoir construction

Dk No 38
The total height of the surcharge should be about 10 feet over the
proposed reservoir finished grade It should be placed in two increments
of about 5 feet each with a one week rest period between intervals to
allow the soft ground to gain enough strength to reduce the possibility of
bearing or slope failure
The length of the surcharge period will depend on the amount of
surcharge used If this 10 foot high surcharge is used our estimate of
the surcharge time would be on the order of 6 months Estimated ground
settlement due to the 10 foot surcharge is on the order of 4 to 8 inches
depending on the actual thickness of the very soft compressible soil
stratum
Settlement gauges should be installed in the surcharge and
surrounding area Periodic survey readings should be taken by the project
surveyor and transmitted to the soils engineer to determine the actual
surcharge time required
After completion of the surcharge program construction of the
proposed reservoir may commence
lowering of Finished Grade
If the delay in construction time associated with the above
recommended surcharge program is not desirable another alternative would
be to lower the finished grade of the proposed reservoir in order to
remove sufficient overburden soil to balance most of the reservoir loads
It is our opinion that by lowering the finished grade 10 feet below the
currently proposed finished floor elevation most of the reservoir loads
would be compensated by the removal of the existing overburden
Therefore the ground settlements would be reduced

Lowering the finished grade by 10 feet would require a larger cut
volume and a higher cut slope than originally planned Therefore the
reservoir site would require a larger area and possibly expansion of
property lines
Reservoir Foundations
The borings disclosed that the site is underlain by a wet soft
clayey silt stratum at an approximate depth of 13 0 feet below the
existing ground surface If the finished floor is lowered as described
above the subgrade would be underlain by this wet soft soil stratun
Therefore in order to provide adequate bearing support for the proposed
reservoir it is our opinion that a gravel mat of at least 3 feet thick
and 4 feet greater than the the width of the footing be provided
underneath the bottom of spread and wall footings The gravel mat
thickness may be reduced to 12 inches below the reservoir slab The
backfill materials should consist of non expansive granular select borrow
and compacted to a minimum of 95 of its maximum dry density as
established by ASTM D 1557 78
If the surcharge method is used the gravel mat under the footings
will not be necessary
We believe that an allowable net bearing capacity of 2500 p s f may
be used for either method
The allowable bearing value may be increased by one third for
temporary wind and seismic loads
Exterior wall footings should be embedded a minimm of 18 inches
below the lowest adjacent finished grade Footings located next to
utility trenches should extend down to the bottom of the utility trench or
the trench backfill be well compacted to minimize footing settlement
The site grading should be designed to divert water away from the
reservoir foundations

Settlements
Provided the recommended surcharge program or overburden removal have
been followed and the gravel mat underneath the footing properly
constructed the estimated settlements would generally be on the
order of less than one inch total and one half of an inch
differential
Lateral Resistance
For passive earth pressure an equivalent fluid pressure of 300
p s f per foot of depth may be used A friction factor of 0 3 may
be utilized to determine the sliding resistance of the structure and
foundation
Site Grading
The details of the proposed grading of the reservoir site were not
available at the time of our exploration However it is anticipated that
the site grading will be minimal
Due to the high annual rainfall the natural moisture content of the
on site soils are generally high 40 to 70 Therefore significant
drying out of the excavated material would be required to attain the
recommended compaction criteria Alternatively drier off site material
may be used for the fill

Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
It is anticipated that no fill excluding gravel mat under footing
and slab will be placed at the tank site Some fill for the access
roadway may be necessary
Since the project site is located in a high rainfall area the
on site soils have high in place moisture contents Therefore it may
not be practical to dry the excavated soil to near optimum moisture
content Due to the high in situ moisture contents and since the
fills are anticipated to be low it is our opinion that the normal
90 compaction standard used in other drier areas could be modified
to 85 for this project
Should lowering of the finished grade be considered cuts of about 8
to 12 feet are anticipated at the tank site A cut slope ratio up to
2 1 H V may be used Slope benches are recommended at 20 foot
maximum vertical height intervals
Near the finished grade the slopes should be cut with extreme care
by the contractor A backhoe should be used to provide the final
finishing of the cut slope surface Overcutting beyond the design
Slope should be avoided since backfilling and recompacting the
resulting overcut areas would create a very loose surface layer which
would easily erode
Surface water from areas above the slope should be diverted away and
prevented from flowing over the slope face The slope should be
planted as soon as practical to prevent unravelling due to erosion

Due to the high rainfall in the area it is not uncommon to encounter
seepage zones exposed in cut slopes If encountered special
provisions such as horizontal drains or toe drains may be required
to stabilize the cut slopes
Roadway Pavement
Asphaltic concrete pavements are anticipated for the project roads
Based on the results of our soil exploration program the preliminary
pavement design is as follows
2 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 Inch Base Course
6 Inch Select Borrow
14 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
CBR tests should be performed on the actual subgrade soil samples
during construction and the above design section can be revised if
necessary The recommended section considers light traffic and that good
drainage will be provided adjacent to paved areas
Design Review
Plans for the site grading and foundation should be forwarded to the
soils engineer for review and written comments prior to construction to
see that the intent for our recommendations is included in the project
plans and specifications
CONSTRUCTION MONT TORING
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during earthwork
construction particularly the fill placement slope cuts and to monitor
the contractor s grading operations
