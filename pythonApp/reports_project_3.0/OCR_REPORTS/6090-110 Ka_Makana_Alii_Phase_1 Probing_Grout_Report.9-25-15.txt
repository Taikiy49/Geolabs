Probing and Grouting Operations
A foundation probing and grouting program was implemented for Building F for the
project The location of the building is shown on the Site Plan Plate 1 Pacific Ground
Systems Inc performed the probing and grouting operations in general conformance with
the recommendations presented in our report dated for the project The
purpose of the probing and grouting operations was to reduce the potential for loss of
foundation support resulting from collapse of cavities or compression of loose zones below
the building structure
Nordic PCL Construction Inc surveyed the footing layout and marked the probe
hole locations prior to the probing and grouting operations In general the probe holes
were located at the column footings one probe hole for every footing The approximate
probe hole locations are shown on the Probe Hole Location Plan Plate 2 In general the
probe holes at the new building structure were drilled to a depth about 15 5 feet below the
existing ground surface corresponding to approximately 10 feet below the design footing
bottom elevations
The probing and grouting operations were accomplished using hollow drill rods with
3 inch diameter drill bits with side discharge holes for water and grout discharge Each
probe hole was advanced by rotary percussion using a TEI 300HT drill mounted on a
Komatsu PC75 excavator Water was injected continuously through the hollow rods while
advancing to the target probe depth Evidence of cavities voids was observed by the loss
of water circulation with rapid receding of water into the drill hole For the probe hole
where cavities voids were not encountered the probe hole was tremie grouted through the
drill rod while the rod is being retracted The grout used for these probe holes generally
consisted of one sack of cement mixed with 7 5 gallons of water with a water cement ratio
of about 0 70
