This report presents the results of our soil and foundation
investigation performed for the above subject project The approximate
location of the project site and its vicinity is shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1
Our work was performed in general accordance with your request and
our proposal of
The purpose of the investigation was to explore the subsurface
conditions at the site to provide soil data for use in the foundation
design of the proposed structure
W O 1415 00 Page 2
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests which
we used in the engineering analysis to formulate our conclusion and
recommendations are presented in the appendices of this report
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a pre engineered metal building with
rigid frames spanning 142 feet and a 36 foot extension to the west In
general the building will be rectangular in shape approximately 180 by
240 feet in plan dimensions The warehouse will be a one story
structure with a two story office shop area located in the northern
middle portion of the building CMU exterior walls and concrete
on grade slabs are also planned for the building Although the finished
floor grades are not finalized at the time of our investigation it is
our understanding that the floors will be at about Elevation 114 U S
Navy Datum or slightly higher
Thue refrigerator area will be located on the northeastern corner
of the building The goods will be stored in high stacked shelves
Small forklift trucks will be used inside the warehouse
The general layout of the proposed medical warehouse construction
is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1415 00 Page 3
The anticipated foundation loadings will range from 40 to 60 kips
per column and the wall footing loading will range from about 4 5 to
6 0 kips per linear foot The maximun design floor live load will be
200 p s f
A new paved perimeter roadway and parking area will be constructed
around the building except along its front side
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site consisted of a relatively level area to the east
of Harris Avenue covering a portion of the existing AC paved basketball
courts to the north of Building 116 and a portion of the adjacent
existing auto parking area inside the Kaneohe Marine Corps Air Station
on the island of Oahu Hawaii At the time of our exploration the
majority of the area to the east of the basketball court was an
unimproved site with a patch of area covered with dense Haole Koa bushes
in the middle of the proposed warehouse building area The existing
surface features at the project site are shown on a topo map from the
Naval Facility Engineering Command and is identified in our report as
the Site Plan Plate 2
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1415 00 Page 4
Geology
Geologically the site is located on the southern shore of Mokapu
Peninsula formed by several vents of the Honolulu Volcanic Series that
jaid down extensive ash and cinder tuff deposits with some lava
flows A more recent rise in the sea level resulted in the deposition
of loose coral materials over a coral reef ina lagoonal environment in
the portion of the peninsula where the project site is located During
the recent past some of the coral reef rock had been quarried and the
excavation backfilled with miscellaneous materials
Field Exploration
fo explore the subsurface conditions fifteen 15 exploratory
borings were drilled to depths ranging from 11 5 to 65 feet below the
existing grades The approximate boring locations are shown on the Site
Plan Plate 2
Subsurface Conditions
Based on the findings from our borings the existing basketball
court pavements generally consisted of 1 5 inches of asphalt concrete
and 6 inches of dense coral sand base course over varying thickness of
the coral gravel fill or stiff brown clayey and or sandy silt The
remaining area outside the A C pavement consisted of stiff clayey or
sandy silt surface fill with thickness varying from 1 5 to 4 5 feet
This upper fill material exhibited low to moderate expansion potential
in our laboratory tests
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1415 00 Page 5
The central portion of the project area was underlain by cemented
coral reefrock under thin surface fill from Boring 7 near the
northeastern corner to Boring 8 near the southwestern corner This
coral reefrock area was flanked on both north and south sides by thick
deposits of medium dense coral sand and or gravel overlying a moderately
hard cemented coral reef rock at depths ranging from 30 to 45 feet in
our borings
Rubbish Fill
Below the upper 2 to 4 foot surface fill a layer of rubbish fill
was encountered in 4 and ll along the eastern or the back
side of the proposed warehouse building area The rubbish fill
generally extended to a depth of about 9 feet below the existing ground
surface down to the water level In Boring 11 the rubbish fill
material could extend to a depth of 16 feet below the surface
elevation The rubbish materials encountered in the borings consisted
of paper plastic wood and other deleterious materials mixed with soft
to medium stiff silt In Boring 11 the amount of debris in the rubbish
fill was less below a 9 5 foot depth Our estimated building area that
might be underlain by rubbish fill is outlined on the Site Plan
GEOLABS HAWAII
Groundwater
Groundwater was encountered in the borings The measured water
levels below the drilled borings are shown on the boring logs In
general the groundwater levels appeared to be about 9 to 10 feet below
the existing grades or at about 1 foot above mean sea level and may
fluctuate due to seasonal and tidal inf luences
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Our borings indicate the site is underlain by partially cemented
coral in the middle of the building area flanked by loose to medium
dense coral deposits However it was noted that a strip of area along
the back eastern side of the proposed warehouse area is underlain by
rubbish i11 down to about the water level We recommend that the
rubbish fill within the building be removed and replaced with compacted
fill If practicable relocating the building further to the west
toward Harris Avenue should be considered in order to avoid the rubbish
fill area with additional borings drilled to accurately define the
optimum off set distance
To minimize future ground settlements due to site filling site
grading should be done as soon as possible
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1415 00 Page 7
After the above preparations we believe that shallow spread
footings may be utilized for the foundation support for the warehouse
building The magnitudes of anticipated foundation settlements are
anticipated to be relatively small
As an alternative we have also considered a pile foundation
scheme However in view of the underlying rubbish fill below the
structure we believe the floor slab will need to be structurally
Supported over the area underlain by rubbish fill If the rubbish fill
is not removed special slab construction may also be required to vent
off potential gas accumulation In this case we believe that such a
pile supported scheme will not be as cost effective as the spread
footing scheme even considering the cost of removing the underlying
rubbish fill
Detailed recommendations are discussed and presented in the
following sections
Site Grading
General
For the proposed warehouse construction it is anticipated that up
to about 2 5 feet of fill will be required to the bottom of the
Slabs Thus the site grading work would consist of clearing and
grubbing rubbish fill removal and excavation backfilled along the
back side of the project area and areal fill construction to
raise the site grades
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1415 00 Page 8
Clearing and Grubbing
The existing trees should be cleared and grubbed Any vegetation
organic or other deleterious materials should be removed from the
project area Generally it appears that the existing asphalt
concrete pavements will be located 12 inches or more below the
finished grades and therefore we believe that they may remain in
place Other existing structures such as posts and their
foundations should be removed as necessary
Rubbish Fill Removal
It is recommended that the rubbish fill encountered in Borings 2
3 4 and 11 along the back side of the warehouse building be
removed by excavating the surface fill first and then the
underlying rubbish fill to firm soil such as the cemented coral or
to the coral sand gravel
In general the depths of over excavation are estimated to be
about 9 feet which is at about groundwater level Although a deep
pocket of rubbish fill may extend down to a depth of 16 feet as
detected in Boring 11 we believe it is unlikely that previous
coral quarrying activities had excavated much below the
groundwater level We anticipate such a loose soil pocket could
be localized Our Boring 11 indicated that it may not contain as
much rubbish as the zone above the groundwater level In general
we believe that a substantial amount of rubbish removal work could
be stopped at the lowest water level as a practical lower limit
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 1415 00 Page 9
During the course of rubbish removal the over excavation work
should be monitored by the project soils engineer to see if
additional removal is necessary below the water level and to
verify that the removal work is acceptable
The actual limits of the existing rubbish fill removal within the
building area should be established in the field For budgetary
estimate purpose only the limit of the area in the project site
that might be underlain by rubbish fill is outlined on the Site
Plan
In the perimeter driveway area the rubbish fill may remain in
place provided that the potential for future irregular settlements
is acceptable However the rubbish removal for the structure
itself should be made a sufficient distance say 5 feet beyond
the proposed warehouse building perimeter These off set limits
refer to the bottom of the over excavation
The over excavation should be sloped back or provided with proper
bracing in accordance with the anticipated project construction
site conditions construction materials stockpile and equipment
and to meet the applicable safety requirements The design and
safety of the sides of the excavation or bracing should be the
responsibility of the contractor For budgeting purposes the
side slopes of the over excavation may be assumed to be on the
average of 1 1 H V

Localized excavations below the groundwater level if deemed
necessary in the field may be made as steep as the soil will
permit and should be inspected and backfilled immediately with
No 3B Fine type crushed rock and compacted with a large vibratory
compactor at the groundwater level
Backfill
The above water portion of backfill from the bottom of the rubbish
fill excavation to 30 inches below the finished grade should
consist of general borrow material free of organic or deleterious
material and would form a dense compact stable soil mass when
compacted The material should have a minimum laboratory CBR
value of 8 at 95 relative compaction of the ASTM D 1557 78
maximum density with expansion of less than 3 The max imum
particle size should not exceed 8 inches This portion of the
backfill should be placed in lifts not to exceed 8 inches in loose
thickness and have a minimum of 90 relative compaction based on
the ASTM D 1557 78 maximum density The portion within 30 inches
of the finished grade should meet the recommended requirements
presented under the SITE GRADING section
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1415 00 Page 11
Fill Construction
The project site will generally require 1 to 2 5 feet of imported
fill to raise the site grades
After the rubbish fill is removed and the over excavation
backfilled the entire existing ground surface except for the A C
pavement areas which is to remain should be scarified to a depth of 6
inches moisture conditioned and recompacted to a minimum of 90
relative compaction of the ASTM D 1557 78 maximum density
Generally the new imported fill material within the top 30 inches
of the finished grade should consist of non expansive select borrow
materials less than 3 inches in maximum size with less than 20 passing
the 200 sieve The minimum CBR value should be 15 at 95 relative
compaction of the ASTM D 1557 78 maximum density with expansion value
not greater than 1
Fill should be placed in 8 inch thick horizontal lifts and
compacted to 90 of the ASTM D 1557 78 maximum density The top 6
inches of the subgrade soil should be compacted to a minimum of 95
relative compaction under the concrete slab or asphalt concrete
GEOLABS HAWAII

Foundation Support
In view of the dense sand and gravel or stiff silt and clays
overlying thick lagoonal coral deposits in some areas we believe that
spread footings can be utilized for the foundation support of the
proposed building
An allowable soil bearing value of up to 2500 p s f may be used
for footings bearing on the stiff silt or dense coral sand and gravel
A one third increase may be allowed for the soil bearing pressure under
wind or seismic loads All footings should have a minimum dimension of
18 inches and the footing bottom should be embedded at least 2 feet
below the lowest adjacent finished grade
Any soft or weak soils encountered at the bottom of the footing
excavations should be removed to firm soils and backfilled with
compacted fill or lean concrete as appropriate The footing
excavations should be inspected by a soils engineer to verify that they
are located in suitable soil
Footings adjacent to any utility trench should be deepened so that
their bottom would be at or below the level of the adjacent trench
bottom
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1415 00 Page 13
For the maximum interior column loading of 60 kips per column the
anticipated spread footing settlement may range up to one half of an
inch The anticipated settlements will be less for other column
footings The differential settlements may range up to one fourth of an
inch between colums It is our understanding that the above magnitudes
of settlements will be acceptable for the proposed construction
For the design wall foundation loading of 6 0 kips per linear
foot the anticipated footing settlements may range up to about one half
of an inch and differential settements may range up to one fourth of an
inch over a distance of 15 feet
Lateral Loads
Lateral loads acting on the foundation may be resisted by passive
earth pressure developed against the buried vertical faces of the
foundation system and by frictional resistance developed between the
bottom of the foundations and the bearing soil
Lateral soil resistance utilizing passive earth pressure may be
calculated with an equivalent fluid weighing 300 p c f This assumes
that the soil backfill around the foundations is well compacted Due to
the loose nature of the underlying coral deposits the lateral soil
resistance should not exceed 1200 p s f below a depth of 4 feet If
the footing is adjacent to an unpaved open area the passive resistance
within the top 12 below the outside grade should be ignored
GEOLABS HAWAII

The base frictional resistance may be calculated by using a
coefficient of friction of 0 4
On Grade Slabs
areas
On grade slabs may be considered for the warehouse and office
Proposed Warehouse
Six inch 6 thick concrete slabs are planned for the warehouse
and will be subject to moderate traffic of small forklift trucks
The concrete slab should be supported on a base course layer of at
least 4 inches thick compacted to a minimum of 95 relative
compaction The subgrade under the base course should consist of
granular compacted fill or the existing asphalt concrete pavement
It is our understanding that a refrigerator area will be located
at the northeastern corner of the warehouse The temperature will
be maintained at above freezing and we believe the slab section
similar to the warehouse will be adequate
Proposed Two Story Office and Shop Area
It appears that the finished grade will be 1 to 1 5 feet above the
existing grade including the existing basketball court A C
pavement Thus the subgrade of the slabs will be in new
compacted fill
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 1415 00 Page 15
In the proposed office shop area the thickness of the concrete
slab may be of a conventional thickness of 4 inches A minimum 4
inches of granular cushion layer should be provided under the
slab Moisture proofing membrane should be considered below the
slab to protect the floor covering from moisture infiltration
Pavements
The actual pavement design will depend on the type of fill
utilized as subgrade soil In general the on site granular coral fill
could be assumed to provide a CBR value of 15 or greater when compacted
to 95 It is also anticipated that the subgrade material within the
top 12 inches of the subgrade will probably consist of imported borrow
having a minimum laboratory CBR of 15 Based on the above the
following pavement sections are recommended
Perimeter Driveway
2 5 Inch Asphalt Concrete
6 0 Inch Base Course
8 5 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
CBR tests should be performed on the actual subgrade soils during
construction and the above design sections may be revised if necessary
The recommended sections consider medium traffic of trucks over
the perimeter driveway and that good drainage will be provided for in
and around the paved areas

Design Review
Plans and specifications for site grading and foundation
construction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and
written comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the rubbish fill removal and backfilling
site grading spread footing excavations subgrade preparations and
cushion layer constructions for slabs and pavements be observed by the
soils engineer during construction This is to see that our
recommendations are properly carried out in the field and to assure
proper imported materials with adequate compaction have been obtained
and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of
our investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
If conditions encountered during construction are different from
those presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content determinations were performed on
selected samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation
of soil properties The results of these tests are presented on
the Boring Logs at the appropriate sample depths
To further identify soil characteristics one sieve
analysis test was performed The test results are summarized on
Plate B l
Two laboratory CBR tests and two compaction tests were
performed on bulk samples of the on site surface soils to
evaluate their pavement subgrade support and compaction
characteristics The test results are summarized on Plate B 1
Four sets of direct shear tests were performed on
undisturbed samples to evaluate the strength characteristics of
near surface soils One of these tests contrasts the soil
strength under both natural and saturated conditions The test
results are presented on Plates B 2 through B 5
One consolidated drained multiphase triaxial compres
sion test was performed on a select undisturbed sample to also
evaluate the soil strength characteristics of the soil The test
results are presented on Plate B 6
Eight one inch ring swell tests were performed on
undisturbed and remolded air dried samples to evaluate the
swelling potential of the soils in both normal and worse case
conditions The test results are summarized on Plate B 7
