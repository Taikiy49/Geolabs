PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located within the northeastern portion of the Royal Kunia
Residential Development Area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The location of the project
site is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The project involves the construction
of a park development in four phases The recently completed Phase 1 project involved
mass grading the entire parcel of land to near the anticipated finish grades and the
construction of two little league baseball fields overlapped by a soccer field within the
southwest corner of the parcel Phase 2 involves the construction of a parking lot and
comfort station at the northern corner of the parcel Phase 2 also involves the construction
of two basketball courts two tennis courts and two biddy basketball courts at the
southeast corner of the parcel Phase 3 will involve the construction of a recreation center
building and a parking lot extension to the south of the Phase 2 parking lot Phase 4 will
involve the construction of a pool and a locker exercise building to the south of the
Phase 3 recreation center building Concrete walkways picnic tables unlined swales and
utilities are planned for the entire parcel
The site grading work for the Phase 2 development generally consisted of fills up to
about 6 feet in thickness for the playcourts The parking lot and playcourts are paved with
an asphalt concrete surface The grading plan for the project is shown on the Site Plans

SITE GRADING
The site grading work for the Recreation Center Phase 2 Royal Kunia Site 11
project was observed by Geolabs Inc from March 18 2002 to The
site grading work consisted of clearing and grubbing and placement and compaction of
fills up to about 6 feet in thickness to achieve the design finished grades for the project
The clearing and grubbing and site grading work was performed by Delta Construction
Corporation During the site grading operations samples of the on site and imported
materials utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office
for testing The maximum dry density and optimum moisture content was established in
our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified
Proctor and the test results are summarized on Plate 3
The fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as
determined in accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557 In the parking and roadway
areas the fill and backfill materials within 2 feet of the finished subgrade were compacted
to a minimum of 95 percent relative density
During the site grading operations periodic field density tests were performed in
general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 2922 Nuclear Gauge Method
Results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 4 1 through 4 9 of
this report These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on
constructed fills and backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by
pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and elevations
should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas
assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was
obtained and or approved by our field representative based on visual observation and
hand probing
From to a total of 19 test pits were excavated within
the playcourt and adjacent slope areas The purpose of the test pits were to evaluate the
extent of the compacted import material from Iwilei placed below the finished subgrade
level The import material from Iwilei encountered within the top 2 feet from finished
subgrade was removed and replaced with compacted on site material
Scattered aggregates greater than 3 inch minus in size were observed at the
finished subgrade level of the tennis and basketball courts The over size materials were
removed and replaced with compacted on site fill material
