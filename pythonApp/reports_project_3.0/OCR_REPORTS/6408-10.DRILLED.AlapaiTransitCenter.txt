Dear Mr Takamiya
At your request Geolabs Inc provided geotechnical engineering observation and
consultation services during the drilled shaft foundation installation for the new bus
shelters at the above project This letter report summarizes our geotechnical engineering
observations during the installation of the drilled shaft foundations for the new bus shelters
for the Alapai Transit Center Renovation project located in Honolulu on the Island of Oahu
Hawaii
It should be noted that Geolabs did not prepare a geotechnical engineering report
for the above project The construction requirements were based on recommendations in
the above referenced report and project plans The purpose of our geotechnical
engineering observation during construction was to provide engineering consultation and
assist in problem resolutions during the construction of the drilled shaft foundation In
addition we provided Special Inspection services by observing the drilled shaft
foundation installation for general conformance with the project plans and specifications
Project Considerations
The project consists of construction of four bus shelters concrete paving potable
water and sanitary sewer service storm drainage electrical telephone and

Based on the project plans the new bus shelters were designed to be supported on
a deep foundation system consisting of 24 inch diameter cast in place concrete drilled
shafts The project plans required that the drilled shafts extend down at least 5 feet into
the dense coral formation A total of 12 drilled shafts were required for the four bus
shelters
The scope of our work discussed in this letter report includes observation of the
drilled shaft foundation construction for the four bus shelters mentioned above only The
general contractor for the project is Designer Built Systems Inc and the drilled shaft
installation subcontractor is Hawaiian Dredging Construction Company Inc
Summary of Drilled Shaft Installation
Installation of test shafts and load testing were not required by the contract
documents for this project The drilled shaft contractor installed 12 production shafts from
through The as built diameters and the lengths of the
production drilled shafts installed for the project are summarized on Plate 3 of this letter
report Detailed installation logs for the production shafts are attached to this letter report
Representatives from Geolabs observed the drilled shaft excavation placement of
reinforcing cage and concrete operations during the installation of the production drilled
shafts
In general the production drilled shafts were excavated using a drill rig
equipped with 30 inch and 24 inch diameter augers and clean out bucket No drilling slurry
was utilized for the production shafts However 30 inch diameter temporary casing was
used during excavation of the production drilled shafts to avoid the potential cave in
condition of the cinder sands near the ground surface and also to provide a safer working
environment The drilled shafts were generally extended to at least 5 feet below the coral
formation as required on the contract plans After excavation of the production shafts to
their designated tip elevations the reinforcing cages were lowered into the drilled holes to
the appropriate elevations using a forklift After the reinforcing cage was installed in the
drilled hole concrete was placed and pumped through a 4 inch I D steel tremie pipe
starting from near the bottom of the excavation During concrete placement we observed
that at least 5 feet of concrete head was maintained above the bottom of the tremie pipe
The concrete for the production shaft was supplied by Hawaiian Cement A special
design concrete mix was utilized for the production shafts which maintains a high slump
7 to 9 inches throughout the concrete pour It should be noted that Designer Built
Systems Inc monitored the concrete used for the drilled shafts including the performance
of slump tests air content tests and compression tests Logs of the individual drilled shafts
installed and the materials encountered in each of the drilled shafts during shaft
excavation are presented on the attached Drilled Shaft Records

Based on the observations of our field representatives it is our professional opinion
that the production drilled shafts for the new bus shelter at the Alapai Transit Center
Renovation project were installed in general accordance with the intent of the project plans
and_ specifications and the geotechnical recommendations presented in the
above referenced geotechnical engineering report In addition it is our professional
opinion that the drilled shafts have been installed to sufficient depths to provide the
minimum embedment into coral formation of 5 feet
Limitations
lt should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Our observation and consultation services
were performed in accordance with generally accepted standards of geotechnical
engineering practice no warranty is expressed or implied Provision of construction
monitoring by an engineer is not insurance nor does it guarantee construction of any type
The contractor shall retain primary responsibility for the quality of the work for adhering to
plans and specifications and for repairing defects regardless of when they are found
