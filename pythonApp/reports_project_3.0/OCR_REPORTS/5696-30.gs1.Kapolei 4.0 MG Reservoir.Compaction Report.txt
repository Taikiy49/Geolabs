PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is on vacant land at the base of Puu Makakilo to the north of the
City of Kapolei in the Ewa District on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The proposed
reservoir will be constructed immediately to the west of the existing Kapolei
215 Reservoir on a pad that was graded concurrently with the construction of the
215 Reservoir
We understand that the new reservoir will be a 4 0 million gallon concrete tank
and will provide additional supply and storage for the existing service area There will
be an access road around the perimeter of the tank A Project Location Map and Site
Plan are shown on Plates 1 and 2 respectively
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services for
the site grading and foundation preparation at the project site

SITE GRADING AND FOUNDATION PREPARATION
We observed the site grading and foundation preparation work for the Kapolei 4 0
MG Reservoir No 2 project from March 14 2018 to The site grading
work consisted of placement and compaction of fills on the order of up to about 1 foot thick
to achieve the finished grades for the project performed the site
grading and foundation preparation work During construction we obtained samples of the
on site and imported materials utilized as fill and backfill materials and transported them to
our laboratory for testing The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents
were established in our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D1557
Modified Proctor and the test results are summarized on Plate 3
The fill materials were moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content
and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as determined in
accordance with ASTM D1557 In the roadway areas the fill and backfill materials within
2 feet of the finished subgrade were compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative
density
During the site grading and foundation preparation operations periodic field density
tests were performed in general accordance with ASTM D6938 Nuclear Gauge Method
Results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 4 1 through 4 3 of
this report These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on
constructed fills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and
interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed as
unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained and or
approved by our field representative based on visual observation and hand probing
Our services consisted of part time field observation of the contractor s fill
placement subgrade preparation and compaction procedures and confirmation of
subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above grading operations
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the site grading and foundation preparation work for
the above referenced project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by
this report were performed in conformance with the general intent of the recommendations
