located in the Honouliuli area of the District of Ewa on the island of Oahu Hawaii The
subject site designated as Subdivision G is approximately rectangular in shape and
covers about 21 acres in area It is bounded by Ewa Elementary School and the
Roman Catholic Church to the north the Ewa by Gentry housing development to the
south the Ewa Elderly Housing project to the east and a stockpile area to the west
The site was previously used for the cultivation of sugar cane and has been
mass graded as part of the Ewa Villages Planned Community Development project by

FIELD ENGINEERING OBSERVATIONS
Based on the recommendations presented in the above referenced report short
cast in place drilled piers were utilized for foundation support of the proposed house
structures Concrete slabs on grade were used for the carport and driveway areas
Our scope of work for the subject project involved the observation of the drilled pier
foundation and slab on grade construction Our field monitoring and testing services
generally consisted of the following items
1 Observe the drilled pier foundation construction work
2 Observe the subgrade preparation at the carport and driveway areas
3 Observe placement of select borrow and base course at the carport and
driveway areas
4 Sample on site import soils for laboratory testing and perform field density
tests
In general it is our opinion that the drilled pier foundation and slab on grade
construction were performed in general accordance with the intent of the
recommendations presented in the above referenced geotechnical engineering report
dated Detailed observations and discussions are presented in the
following sections of this letter report

Slab On Grade Construction
Observation and testing of the earthwork were performed by representatives from
our office to confirm that the slab on grade construction was performed in general
conformance with the intent of our geotechnical engineering report and specifications
pertaining to the geotechnical aspects of the development
Prior to placement of select borrow and base course layers the existing
vegetation was removed and the subgrades were scarified to a depth of approximately
6 inches brought to between 2 and 5 percent above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to a minimum of 85 or 90 percent of the maximum dry density as
determined in general accordance with American Society of Testing and Material
ASTM Test Method D 1557 The 12 inch non expansive select granular fill was then
placed in lifts compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of its maximum dry density A
6 inch base course was placed and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative
compaction prior to the placement of steel reinforcement and concrete for the slabs on
grade
During the slab on grade construction samples of the on site and imported soils
utilized as fill materials were obtained and returned to our laboratory for testing The
maximum dry density and optimum moisture content were determined in our laboratory
in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Results of our laboratory
tests are presented on Plate 2 In place density tests of the fill materials were
performed periodically in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 2922
Nuclear Method The test results are presented on the attached Summary of Field
Density Test Results Plates 3 1 through 3 41
FOUNDATION PERFORMANCE AND POST CONSTRUCTION CONSIDERATIONS
Based on the laboratory test results obtained from our exploration performed for
the subject development the in situ clayey soils generally have a high shrink swell
potential when subjected to fluctuations in the moisture contents In addition it will lose
substantial strength when saturated and will shrink when subjected to drying resulting
in shrinkage cracks Therefore proper surface drainage at the site is essential in the
performance of the foundations In order to reduce the potential for bearing strength
loss of the foundation soils we recommend that the following be implemented at the
subject site
1 Finished grades outside the building should be sloped to shed water away
from foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding

2 Drainage swales should be established and maintained to drain all
surface run off away from the driveway slabs and pier footing foundations
3 Gutter systems should be installed around the building and the discharge
be diverted away from the foundation and driveway areas
4 Excessive landscape watering near the foundations and driveway slabs
should be avoided Planters next to foundations should be avoided or
have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the
subsoils
These drainage requirements and recommendations are essential for the proper
performance of the foundation support system since ponded water could cause
subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of strength We believe that
the potential for structural distress should be significantly reduced if these measures
are properly implemented and maintained
We strongly recommend that the aforementioned recommendations and
restrictions be made available to all home owners of this subdivision
