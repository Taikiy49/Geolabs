PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our survey was to obtain an overview of the electrical resistivity of
the subsurface soils at the site Our work on this project was performed in general
conformance with ASTM Test Designation G 57 78 and with the requirements provided in
the Water System External Corrosion Control Standards Volume 3 dated 1991 The
scope of our work consisted of the following tasks and work efforts
1 Mobilization Demobilization of resistivity equipment and a field engineer to
and from the project site
2 Performance of 34 in situ soil resistivity surveys at nine locations in the
shoulder areas along the proposed water main alignment corresponding to
the 300 foot maximum spacing required in the Water System External
Corrosion Control Standards Volume 3 dated 1991 The resistivity
surveys were performed in general accordance with ASTM Test
Designation G 57 78
3 Coordination of the field work by an Engineer from our firm
4 Reduction and analyses of the data pertinent to the corrosion
characteristics of the subsurface soils at the site
5 Preparation of this letter report summarizing our work on the project and
presenting our findings
6 Quality assurance of our work and client design team consultation by a
Principal Engineer of our firm
7 Miscellaneous work efforts such as drafting word processing and clerical
support

Thirty four soil resistivity surveys were conducted with a maximum of 300 foot
intervals at nine locations at the project site using the in Pin Method and a
resistance meter Nilsson Model 400 manufactured by Nilsson Electrical Laboratory Inc
The method utilizes four surface probes or pins electrodes to evaluate the average
resistivity an indication of corrosivity of the underlying soil mass The average resistivity
values of the soil mass extending from the existing ground surface to the planned invert
elevations of the water main was assessed by using this method at the approximate
survey locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 Results of the resistivity survey are
presented on Plates 3 1 and 3 2
The soil resistivity surveys were performed at the existing ground surface along the
proposed alignment of the 16 inch water main The average soil resistance along the
proposed alignment was evaluated at each survey location with the electrodes aligned at
equal spacings of generally 4 to 17 feet at the existing ground surface The depth of
penetration of a resistivity sounding is a function of the electrode spacing The electrode
spacing for each soil resistivity survey location was determined by the depth of the water
main invert from the existing ground surface lt should be noted that some underground
utility ines were observed within the shoulder areas near some survey locations
The resistance meter Nilsson Model 400 impresses a voltage between the outer
electrodes causing an electrical current to flow between them The voltage drop
between the inner electrodes is then measured by a sensitive built in voltmeter As a
result the soil resistance in ohms can be found directly on the dial The soil resistivity
in ohm cm which is directly related to both resistance and spacing can then be
computed The evaluated soil resistances the computed resistivity values and the
corresponding corrosion ratings are presented on Plates 3 1 and 3 2 of this report
However it should be noted that the soil resistivity may be influenced by the existing utility
lines near the survey locations temperature weather conditions and other factors
Resistivity is generally recognized as one of the most significant soil characteristics
with regard to the corrosivity of the soil to buried metallic objects In general the lower
the resistivity the greater the potential for corrosion of the buried metallic structure
Conversely the higher the resistivity the less likely the soil will contribute to corrosion to
metallic objects
Based on the resistivity values and the corrosion ratings of the subsurface
materials at the site the subsurface materials at the site may be classified as extremely
corrosive to buried metallic structures In general it appears that the proposed water
main will need to be designed based on a Corrosion Rating of 1

LIMITATIONS
The evaluations presented in this report are based upon information obtained from
field data points by means of geophysical surveys Variation of soil conditions between
the field data points may occur and the nature and extent of such variations may not
become evident until a later point in time If variations then appear evident it will be
necessary to reevaluate the ratings provided in this report
Locations and elevations of the data presented in this report are approximate
having been estimated in the field from the features and reference points shown on the
plans prepared by Sato Associates Inc dated May 7 1998 Thus locations and
elevations of the data presented should be considered accurate only to the degree
implied by the methods used
