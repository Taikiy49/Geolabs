INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of a soils and foundation
investigation for the proposed 3 building 7 and 8 story com
mercial and apartment complex The study consisted of subsurface
borings laboratory testing and engineering analyses as outlined
in our proposal dated Based on the results
of our engineering studies conclusions and recommendations per
tinent to site development are presented
PROJECT DESCRIPTION
The property is located at the corner of Waialae Avenue and
Kapiolani Boulevard The site and building locations are indi
cated on the attached site plan Plate 1 The site drains makai
towards the freeway with an estimated relief being on the order
of 10 feet The site is presently covered with apartment build
ings business buildings a shack parking lots a 3 foot high
retaining wall lumber anda few trees and shrubs
The maximum column loads are expected to be on the order of
800 kips A concrete basement floor slab on grade is proposed
FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface exploration consisted of eight 8 borings
ranging in depth from 23 to 65 feet The drilling was accomplished

A 4 carbide rock bit and core barrel were used in the basalt

Services Inc

the boring logs Samples obtained were visually classified in

GEOLOGY AND SOIL CONDITIONS
Geology
During the late Pleistocene to recent Epochs outpourings
of Honolulu Volcanic Series Basalts flooded the previously
eroded valleys of Southern Oahu These flows spread out
over the moist sediments of lower Manoa Valley and apparently
were fractured by the generated steam Subsequent erosion
by seaward flowing streams cut a channel in the central
portion of the proposed building site A eustatic rise in
sea level probably generated by melting glaciers towards
the end of the Pleistocene Epoch permitted marine sedimen
tation fossiliferous olive black sandy CLAYS in the
tidal channel that occupied the former stream channel
Island uplift or a eustatic fall in sea level caused a
recession of the shoreline and in filling of a portion of
the building site with clastic debris boulders to clay
sized material derived from the Koolau Mountains
Fill has been selectively placed on portions of the site

Based on the results of our field investigations laboratory
testings and engineering analyses the following conclusions
and recommendations are presented
A General
The subsurface conditions encountered during the investi
gation varied considerably across the site The proposed
development is feasible from an engineering viewpoint
provided the recommendations included herein are followed
It is our professional opinion that the underlying basalt
encountered onsite is capable of supporting the proposed
structures

Pile Foundations
The exploration program disclosed relatively
poor subsurface soil conditions within the general
area of the two 7 story apartment structures These
upper soft highly compressible soils are not suit
able for supporting relatively heavy structural
loads A pile foundation system to transfer the
structural loads to the competent BASALT bearing
strata is required
A 16 sixteen inch diameter octagonal prestressed
concrete pile driven to firm bearing in the hard
BASALT should be capable of supporting an allowable
design load of 100 tons each A group reduction
factor is not applicable for end bearing piles on
rock

LABORATORY TESTING
A laboratory testing program was developed to establish the
engineering properties of the soils and rocks encountered at
the project site The tests where applicable to the subject
project are included in this Appendix
1 Classification Tests
a
b
c
Visual Classification All soil samples obtained from
the borings were brought to our laboratory where they
were visually reclassified to confirm or modify the
field classification prior to finalizing the Logs of
Borings
Water Content Determinations In addition to visual
classification typical samples were tested for natural
water content as an aid in soil classification and in
evaluating soil properties The water content values
are based upon the dry weight of the soil
Atterberg Limits Atterberg limit tests were performed
on selected clay and silt samples obtained from the
borings for the purposes of identification and correla
tion of soils Standard procedures ASTM D 424 for the
plastic limit and ASTM D 423 for the liquid limit were
used in the performance of these tests
1
dj Grain Size Analysis Sieve analysis were performed on
granular soils For fine grained soils the hydrometer
test was performed to determine the distribution of
the grain sizes beyond the No 200 sieve
2 Shear Strength Tests
a Unconfined Compression Tests Intact core and split
spoon soil samples which apparently exhibited high
plasticity were tested for unconfined strength Length
to diameter ratio of 2 was maintained throughout the
tests to avoid scatter of test results and to facilitate
evaluation of shear strength values for design purposes
b Direct Shear Test In general soil samples were tested
for shear strength using a 2 41 I D direct shear box
Each set consisted of at least two tests each test run
under different normal loads The results are plotted
as Normal Stress vs Shear Strength with a failure envelope
drawn to determine values of cohesion c and angle of
frictional resistance
3 Consolidation Test Consolidation tests were performed on
selected undisturbed ring samples of the compressible
soils to provide basic data for making settlement calculations
Porous stones were placed on the top and bottom of the samples
to allow drainage Vertical loads were applied in increments
with each load increment being allowed to consolidate prior
2
to adding the next increment Measurements of the time
and consolidation were obtained during each load increment
and rebound was measured during the unloading portion
Consolidation test results are plotted in terms of percent
settlement versus applied loads
Swell Test The ring sample was placed between porous
stones and a 60 psf load was applied to the sample for a
24 hour period The difference between the initial and
final sample heights is the amount of swell which is expressed
as a percentage of the initial height of the sample
Maximum Density Test Samples of soils are compacted in a
mold of given size with a 10 pound rammer dropped 18 inches
to determine the relationship between moisture content and
density of the soil At least three trial points at dif
ferent moisture content are run to determine the maximum
density and optimum moisture content The test is run in
accordance with ASTM D 1557 70
