INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed three 3 story apartment building at 1751 Aigaroba Street in
the Moiliili District of Oahu Hawaii The general location of the project site and its vicinity
is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2832 00 Page 2
This report summarizes the findings of our exploration and presents our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from analyses of the data obtained
during the field exploration Tnese recommendations are intended for design input and
may not be appropriate to form the basis for construction cost estimates or for
construction bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a three 8 story apartment building at the project site
It is our understanding that the ground floor level parking will consist of concrete columns
with CMU walls and portland cement concrete paving It is also anticipated that wood
construction will be utilized above the ground floor level for the proposed building
Structural loading information had not been provided to us at the time of
preparation of this report Based on the information provided to us the estimated column
and wall loads will probably be approximately fifty 50 kips and three 3 kips per foot
respectively
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions at the project site in order to develop a generalized subsurface data set for the
formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to foundation site
grading and pavement design of the project

SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed project site is located at 1751 Algaroba Street in the Moiliili District
of Oahu Hawaii it is situated at the corner of Algaroba Street and Hauoli Street
Presently the site is occupied by a single story residence a slab and footings of a
structure previously occupying a portion of the site concrete walkways and debris
broken construction materials and small dirt piles resulting from the demolition of the
previously existing building
The project site is generally level and measures approximately one hundred 100
feet by fifty 50 feet in overall plan dimension The site fronts on Algaroba Street to the
north and Hauoli Street to the east It is bounded by a chain linked fence to the south
and separated from other residences to the west by a chain linked fence atop an eighteen
18 inch high CMU wall
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling two 2 borings to
existing ground surface The approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site
Plan Plate 2
In general our exploration encountered a surficial layer of fill material approximately
three 3 feet thick consisting of medium stiff clayey silt and medium dense silty sand
This was underlain by a compressible layer of loose clayey sand and soft clayey silt down
to a depth of approximately five 5 feet below the existing ground surface

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Our field exploration generally indicated that the site is underlain by a two 2 to
four 4 foot thick compressible layer of soft clayey silt and loose clayey sand below a
three 3 foot thick surface crust of clayey silt and silty sand
Based on the exploration results and the anticipated maximum column loads of fifty
50 kips we believe that these compressible deposits cannot provide adequate
foundation support for the proposed building and unacceptable settlement may occur
under the new building loads In order to reduce the anticipated excessive settlement and
the possibility of bearing failure we recommend that the soft compressible deposits at the
footing foundation locations be over excavated and backfilled with select compacted
structural fill
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2832 00 Page 6
Foundations
It is our opinion that shallow spread or continuous footings may be used to
support the proposed building An allowable bearing capacity of up to 2 000 p s f may
be used for the design of spread footings bearing on compacted structural fill constructed
as described below The bearing value is for total dead plus live loads and may be
increased by one third when temporary wind and seismic loads are considered
In order to reduce the potential for excessive settlement and the possibility of
footing bearing failure due to compressible subgrade soils we recommend that the
footing excavations be extended down to a depth of approximately five 5 feet below the
existing ground surface The footing over excavation should also extend out a minimum
of eighteen 18 inches beyond the edge of the footings All soft and saturated excavated
silty material should not be re used to backfill the above over excavation A schematic
section of the footing excavation and backfill details is shown on Plate 3
The excavation should be lined with a layer of geotextile filter fabric and then
backfilled with No 3B Fine gravel or ASTM C 33 No 67 to at least twelve 12 inches
above the groundwater level Then non expansive borrow material such as select
borrow crusher waste or coral should be placed in level horizontal lifts each lift not
exceeding eight 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 of
its maximum dry density as determined in accordance with procedures described in
ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 prior to footing construction The top one 1 foot below
the finish subgrade level should generally be backfilled with more impervious non
expansive soil compacted as indicated above
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2832 00 Page 7
Individual footings and continuous wall footings bearing on the above prepared
geotextile and gravel backfill should be embedded a minimum of twelve 12 inches below
the lowest adjacent finish grade
If foundations are located next to utility trenches they should be embedded below
a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane extending upward from the
bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility lines
Footing settlements under the anticipated design loadings are estimated to be on
the order of less than one 1 inch total and one half 1 2 of an inch differential provided
that the special design and construction procedures recommended above are followed
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive earth pressure
developed against the buried vertical faces of the foundation system and by frictional
resistance developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be calculated using an equivalent fluid
pressure of 300 p s f per foot of depth This assumes that the soil around the foundation
is well compacted A coefficient of friction of 0 4 may be used Unless covered by
pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the upper eighteen 18 inches of soil
should be neglected
Site Grading
The weight of new fills will initiate long term ground settlement due to compression
of the soft subsoils Therefore any site filling that may be required should be initiated as
soon as possible and it should be limited to a minimum thickness of about one 1 foot
or less over the existing ground surface
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2832 00 Page 8
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable
materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site
We recommend that the areas below pavements or embankments should be
scarified to a minimum depth of six 6 inches moisture conditioned and recompacted to
90 of its maximum dry density as determined in accordance with procedures described
in ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 Any loose or soft spots encountered should be
removed and replaced with compacted non expansive select material
Imported fill material to replace the over excavated material should consist of non
expansive select granular material less than three 3 inches in maximum size with less
than 20 percent passing the No 200 sieve Any imported fill material should be tested
and approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to delivery to the project site
The site grading should be designed to divert surface water away from the building
foundations

the ground floor level The following pavement section may be used for preliminary

The slab thickened edges adjacent to any unpaved area should be embedded at
least twelve 12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade
Provisions should be made for proper load transfer across the slab joints which are
subject to vehicular traffic
The above recommended pavement section is based on the assumptions that the
traffic will be light and that adequate drainage will be provided Laboratory CBR and
density tests should be performed on the exposed subgrade during construction to verify
the subgrade strength and the above pavement section may be revised if necessary
Utility Trench
New utility lines may be required for the project A granular bedding consisting of
six 6 inches of select borrow or ASTM C 33 No 67 is recommended under the pipes
Granular materials such as No 3B Fine or ASTM C 33 No 67 gravel should be
used for the trench backfill up to about one 1 foot above the pipes to reduce the
possibility of damaging the pipes
The upper portion of the subsequent trench backfill from the level one 1 foot
above the pipes to the top of the subgrade or finished grade should consist of select
granular material and placed in maximum ten 10 inch horizontal loose lifts mechanically
compacted to a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density as determined in
accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 to reduce future ground subsidence
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2832 00 Page 10
Drainage
The finished grade outside the building should be shaped to shed water away from
foundations and to avoid ponding conditions Also it is advised that gutter systems be
installed around the building and that the discharge be diverted away from the foundation
and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near the foundations and slabs
should also be avoided
Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have concrete bottoms and
drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the above
foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause excessive subsurface soil
saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations
should be properly backfilled tight against the walls or slab footings immediately after
setting of the concrete to prevent ponding conditions
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and maintained to drain
all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundations
Design Review
Preliminary and final drawings and specifications for the proposed construction
should be forwarded to the project geotechnical engineer for review and written
comments prior to advertisement for bidding This review is needed to determine
adherence to the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this review
is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot be considered to be responsible for
misinterpretation of the recommendations
GEOLABS HAWAII

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during the site grading and construction of the
foundations This is to observe compliance with design concepts specifications or
recommendations and to expedite suggestions for design changes that may be required
in the event that subsurface conditions differ from those anticipated at the time of
preparation of this report The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon
such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and approved by
the geotechnical engineer prior to delivery to the site
If actual exposed soil conditions encountered during construction are different from
those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to the design
should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part
upon information obtained from field borings Variations of conditions between the field
borings may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident
until construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to
reevaluate the recommendations given in this report
The location of the field borings indicated in this report are approximate having been
determined by taping from features shown on the topographic map dated March 4 1992
prepared by DJNS Surveying Mapping Inc Elevations of the field borings were
estimated by interpolation from the contours shown on the same map The physical
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2832 00 Page 12
locations and elevations of the field borings should be considered accurate only to the
degree implied by the methods used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the field borings depict
the approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual
transition
Water level data from the field borings was collected at the time given on the
graphic representations and or in the text of this report These data have been reviewed
and interpretations made in the formulation of this report However it must be noted that
fluctuation may occur due to variation in rainfall tides temperature and other factors
The information discussion and recommendations contained in this report pertain
to Lot No 11 only It is our understanding that consideration is being given to expand
the scope of construction to include Lot Nos 12 and 13 located immediately to the south
of Lot No 11 as covered by this report If this is done it is recommended that additional
exploration testing and analyses be performed to evaluate the subsurface materials in the
additional area

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling of two
2 borings to depths of 31 5 and 36 5 feet below the existing ground surface at the
approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were drilled using
truck mounted equipment
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Method D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Spilt Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving
a standard penetration sampler or 3 O D Modified California sampler with a 140 pound
hammer free falling 30 inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the last 12 inches
are shown on the Boring Logs at the appropriate sample depths
The material encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who continuously monitored the drilling
operations These classifications were further reviewed by visual inspection and testing
in the laboratory All soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System Graphic presentations of the materials encountered are presented
