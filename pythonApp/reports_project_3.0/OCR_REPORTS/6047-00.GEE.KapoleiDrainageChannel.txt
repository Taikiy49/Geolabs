PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
Based on the information provided we understand that it is desired to alleviate
the flooding problem in the Kapolei area by constructing a temporary retention basin
along a portion of the future Kapolei Harborside Drainage Channel alignment situated
between Kalaeloa Boulevard and Kalaeloa Harbor in the Kapolei area on the Island of
Oahu Hawaii The project location and general vicinity are shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1 The materials generated from the excavations into the future
drainage channel alignment will be stockpiled as an embankment adjacent to the
temporary retention basin We envision that the excavated materials will be segregated
into granular and cohesive soils during the embankment construction to facilitate future
use as borrow soils
The proposed temporary retention basin site is about 2 500 feet long by 300 to
400 feet wide in plan dimensions Based on the grading plan provided we understand
that the finish grades at the bottom of the temporary retention basin will be set at about
6 to 10 feet Mean Sea Level MSL Based on the existing topography at the site
excavations on the order of about 20 to 40 feet below the existing ground surface are
anticipated for the temporary retention basin construction

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The northeastern portion of the temporary retention basin has been previously
explored by our office in 2007 by drilling and sampling four borings extending to depths
of about 40 5 to 42 5 feet below the existing ground surface Our current field
exploration consisted of drilling and sampling six borings designated as Boring Nos 1
through 6 to a depth of about 40 feet below the existing ground surface along the
southwestern portion of the temporary retention basin The approximate boring
locations from our previous and current field explorations are shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2
In general our field explorations previous and current conducted within the
project limits generally encountered a surface fill layer up to about 5 feet thick The
surface fill layer generally consisted of soft to medium stiff sandy silts and clayey silts
underlain by coralline deposits extending to depths ranging from about 30 to 40 feet
below the existing ground surface The coralline deposits generally consisted of very
soft to stiff sandy clay very loose to dense sands and gravels soft clayey silt with sand
and gravel soft to hard coral formation and hard sandstone formation In some of the
borings an alluvial deposit was encountered below the coralline deposits extending to
the maximum depth drilled of about 42 5 feet below the existing ground surface The
alluvium was comprised of medium stiff clay and silty clay
We encountered groundwater in some of the borings at depths of about 38 to
39 5 feet below the existing ground surface at the time of our field exploration It should be
noted that groundwater levels may fluctuate depending on tidal fluctuations seasonal
precipitation and other factors Descriptions and graphic representations of the materials
encountered are presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 6 of Appendix A
GEOTECHNCIAL ENGINEERING RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the results of our field explorations as well as our knowledge and
experience in the project vicinity the proposed temporary retention basin site appears to
be underlain by surface fill coralline deposits and alluvial soils These subsurface
conditions should provide adequate support for the proposed retention basin drainage
channel project provided that the recommendations presented herein are followed

Based on the anticipated subsurface conditions we envision that conventional
earthmoving equipment may be used for excavation of the surface fills and the
underlying alluvial deposits It is anticipated that deeper portions of the channel
excavation will encounter dense sandstone and hard coral ledge and or formation For
removal of these ledges and formations hard excavation methods such as hoerams
and heavy ripping may be required
The coral and sandstone formations at the site are variable ranging from hard reef
rock to sand and gravel with minimal cementation Therefore although most of the cut
slopes should perform satisfactorily provisions for slope protection such as riprap and
permanent erosion mats should be included when the final configuration of the drainage
channel is achieved Detailed discussion of these items and our geotechnical
engineering design recommendations are presented in the following sections herein
Site Grading
Based on the grading plan provided for the project we envision the grading work
will generally consist of excavation into the future drainage channel alignment to create
a temporary retention basin and construction of an embankment with the excavated
materials along the southeast side of the temporary retention basin The following
grading items are addressed in the succeeding subsections 1 Site Preparation
2 Keying and Benching 3 Fill Materials 4 Fill Placement and Compaction
Requirements 5 Excavation 6 Cut and Fill Slopes and 7 Slope and Channel
Protection
A Geolabs representative should monitor site grading operations to observe
whether undesirable materials are encountered during the excavation process and to
confirm whether the exposed soil rock conditions are similar to those assumed in this
report and or encountered during our detailed field exploration program
Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork areas within the contract grading limits should be
cleared and grubbed thoroughly Vegetation debris deleterious material and
other unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed properly off site or
stockpiled in a designated area to reduce the potential for contamination of the
excavated materials
Soft and yielding areas encountered during clearing and grubbing in areas
designated to receive fills should be over excavated to expose firm natural
material and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with well compacted
engineered fill The excavated soft soils may be used as fill provided that they
meet fill material requirements

Areas to receive fill should be scarified to a depth of 12 inches moisture
conditioned to about 2 percent above the optimum moisture and compacted to at
least 90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place
dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the
same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 Optimum moisture is
the water content percentage by weight corresponding to the maximum dry
density Compaction should be accomplished by sheepsfoot rollers vibratory
rollers or other types of acceptable compaction equipment These _ initial
compaction operations should be performed in the presence of our
representative
Keying and Benching
Although the topography at the site is relatively flat there are local areas of
steeper slopes Therefore keying and benching is recommended for fills that will
be placed at the site in areas where the existing slope is steeper than
five horizontal to one vertical 5H 1V
Based on the site conditions encountered we recommend moisture conditioning
and proof rolling the keys and benches prior to the placement of the
embankment fill with a Caterpillar D 9 bulldozer or similar heavy equipment for a
minimum of six passes to help verify that adequate support for the fill has been
achieved The proof rolling operations should be performed in the presence of a
Geolabs representative Yielding areas loose areas or cavities disclosed during
clearing and proof rolling operations should be backfilled with compacted fill
materials
Fill Materials
We envision that the temporary retention basin will be constructed by excavating
into the existing ground New fills to raise the existing ground surface for the
retention basin drainage channel construction are not anticipated for the project
lf new fills are required for the retention basin drainage channel construction we
recommend utilizing select fill material with proper slope and channel protection
Fill materials should consist of non expansive materials less than 3 inches in
largest dimension with a minimum CBR value of 10 The fill material should also
be free of vegetation and deleterious materials Geolabs should observe and or
test imported fill materials for their suitability prior to being transported to the site
for the intended use
Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
Fills should be moisture conditioned to about 2 percent above the optimum
moisture placed in level lifts not exceeding 10 inches in loose thickness and
compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers
to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry

Excavation
The temporary retention basin drainage channel excavation will penetrate the
near surface soils and may involve cuts into the underlying coral and sandstone
formation It is anticipated that most of the excavation work may be accomplished
with normal heavy excavation equipment such as bulldozers and scrapers
However excavations into the coral and sandstone formation may require the
use of heavy ripping or hoerams If heavy excavation is required care should be
exercised to reduce the potential of overbreak particularly in the retention
basin channel walls and floor
The above discussions regarding the rippability of the surface materials are
based on field data obtained from the field exploration performed at the project
site Contractors bidding on this project should be encouraged to examine the
site conditions and soil data to make their own interpretation
Cut and Fill Slopes
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered in our field explorations it
appears that most of the cut slopes would expose stiff alluvial soils medium
dense coralline sands and gravel to hard coral and sandstone formation
In general we believe that cut slopes for the retention basin drainage channel
may be made at an inclination of 2H 1V or flatter provided that erosion control
and slope protection is provided for the cut slopes in the final configuration
Fill slopes for the embankment construction should be designed with a slope
inclination of 2H 1V or flatter If necessary slope benches are recommended at
maximum 15 foot vertical height intervals If the existing ground surface is
steeper that 5H 1V fills should be keyed and benched into the existing slope as
discussed above to provide stability of the new fill against sliding The filling

compacted layers in accordance with the above fill placement recommendations
Fill slopes should be constructed by overfilling and cutting back to the design

slopes and slope planting should be provided as soon as possible to reduce the
potential for erosion of the finished slopes
Slope and Channel Protection
Our field explorations disclosed that the site is underlain by alluvial soils at
varying depths below the existing ground surface Where alluvial soils are
exposed in the retention basin channel walls and subject to water flow the
alluvial soils should be protected by riprap or other lining when the drainage
channel is in its final configuration
It is our opinion that the overall stability of the proposed channel cut slope is
satisfactory However erosion will occur where the coral formation has minimal
cementation and the water velocity is high Therefore maintenance will be
required following large storm events This is typical of unlined channels in coral
If the channel is to be virtually maintenance free then a structural lining should
be considered
If it is desired to keep the channel unlined then periodic maintenance will be
required In order to reduce the maintenance selective portions of the channel
could be lined to reduce erosion Slope protection lining should be installed
where the water velocities would be high such as the outlets of culverts into the
channel and where large areas of coral with minimal cementation are
encountered
Shotcrete grouted rubble pavement GRP and articulating concrete blocks may
be considered for the channel lining The costs for these alternatives are believed
to be similar with shotcrete probably a little less costly than the others The
shotcrete and GRP are probably more flexible in terms of installation in localized
areas since the articulating concrete blocks need to be installed in mats covering
the entire slope The articulating blocks are desirable due to its ability to allow
water percolation into the subsurface It is our opinion that shotcrete lining may
be most appropriate for the drainage channel at its final configuration

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
Our current field exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling six
borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 6 extending to depths of about 40 feet
below the existing ground surface The borings were drilled using a truck mounted drill
rig equipped with continuous flight solid stem augers The approximate boring locations
are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on the Soil Log Legend Plate A The materials
encountered are provided on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 6
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained from the borings drilled in
general accordance with ASTM D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a
3 inch outside diameter OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling
30 inches In addition some samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general
accordance with ASTM D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by
driving a 2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same hammer and drop The
blow counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an 18 inch drive
are shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate
sample depths
Pocket penetrometer tests were performed on selected cohesive soil samples in
the field The pocket penetrometer test provides an indication of the unconfined
compressive strength of the sample Pocket penetrometer test results are summarized
on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths

classification Graphic presentations of the test results are presented on Plates B 2 and
