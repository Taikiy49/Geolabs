PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is situated in Lot 12A of the Maui Lani Subdivision in the
Waikapu Wailuku area on the Island of Maui Hawaii The project location and general
vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The project involves mining of
the site to exploit the underlying sand material The exploited sand material will be used
for a variety of purposes such as fine aggregate fill material and other industrial uses
This exploration has been undertaken to comply with the owner s requirement for
a geotechnical engineering report prior to approval of the sand removal In addition to
providing an overview of the subsurface conditions at the site this report provides
grading recommendations for excavation during the sand mining operations and to
provide recommendations related to possible future mass grading operations to restore
the site after exploitation of the sand resource

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
Two major volcanoes built the Island of Maui the older is West Maui Tertiary
Epoch and the more recent is East Maui also known as Haleakala Pleistocene
Epoch The Isthmus of Maui is a narrow gently sloping plain between these
two volcanoes The project site is along the southeast flanks of West Maui

East Maui Haleakala lava flows ponding on West Maui created the Isthmus of
Maui These lavas have been mantled by calcareous sand dunes blown inland from
ancient beaches and alluvium washed from the slopes of West Maui and East Maui
Haleakala that lay over the Haleakala and older West Maui lava flows
During the Pleistocene Epoch sea levels fell to stands lower than the present
sea level exposing large areas of coralline sand on what was previously the ocean
bottom Wind action transported these coralline sands inland forming extensive areas
of sand dunes Over time portions of the dunes cemented and lithified The dune sands
are typically a tan to cream like colored cross bedded fine grained material overlying
some areas of older alluvial deposits In some areas the dune sands have been
covered with more recent Aeolian wind blown deposits of finer sands with some
alluvial silts forming an overburden on the higher quality dune sands
EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is in the existing Maui Lani Subdivision area near Waikapu on
the Island of Maui Hawaii The proposed sand mining area is a portion of the
approximately 50 acre Lot 12A of the Maui Lani Subdivision The proposed sand mining
site will consist of approximately 14 9 acres within Lot 12A
The project site is bounded by the existing Hawaiian Cement sand mining area to
the south and east the 45 7 acre parcel owned by A B Properties to the west and
Maui Lani Lot 11 to the north The surface elevation of the site varies from 200 to about
240 feet above Mean Sea Level MSL
At present most of the site is vacant land with surface vegetation consisting of
dense keawe brush with shrubs and grass A portion of the site was previously used as
a horse stable and ranch _
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsoil conditions at the project site were explored at selected locations by
excavating a series of test pits designated as Test Pit Nos 1 through 6 extending to
depths ranging from about 8 0 to 22 5 feet below the existing ground surface The
approximate test pit locations are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general our field exploration disclosed that the surface soil material consisted of
coral sand with silt overburden on the order of about 1 0 to 1 5 feet thick which covers
most of the site surface This overburden appears to be more recent finer grained aeolian
deposits mixed with silts of alluvial origin Below the surface overburden layer the test pits
generally encountered partially lithified to poorly consolidated dune sand from a depth of
about 1 0 to 22 5 feet below the existing ground surface The consistency of the sands

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Our test pit data indicates that loose fine grained sand with silt overburden soils
overlies partially cemented sands and medium dense sand at the project site Alluvial
materials were encountered in the deeper portions of most of the test pits
Based on the anticipated use of the sand to be mined from the site it is anticipated
that the partially cemented sands and medium dense sand are the most valuable resource
for the sand mining operations The overburden material may have value as fill material
however it is believed that it is too silty for use as aggregate
We understand that the sand at the site will be mined to the maximum extent
possible i e to the underlying alluvial soil materials This excavation may extend below
the future rough finish grade for this portion of the subdivision Therefore mass grading
and backfilling operations would be needed to restore the site to the proposed rough finish
grade elevation Detailed discussion of our recommendations of fill and backfill material
types as well as placement and compaction requirements are provided in the following
sections
Site Grading
Detailed grading plans were not available at the time this report was prepared
However it is anticipated that the majority of the grading will consist of excavation to
depths ranging from 8 to about 22 feet below the existing ground surface to exploit the
sand resource to the maximum possible extent As previously mentioned the sand
mining operation may extend beyond the future rough finish grade for this portion of the
Maui Lani Subdivision Therefore fills on the order of about 3 to 4 feet in height or less

Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork areas within the contract grading limits should be
cleared and grubbed thoroughly Vegetation debris deleterious material and
other unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed properly off site or
stockpiled in a designated area to reduce the potential for contamination of the
excavated materials
Soft and yielding areas encountered during clearing and grubbing should be
over excavated to expose firm natural material The excavated material should
be examined for its suitability as fill material or as usable sand Material not
suitable as aggregate should be segregated and handled separately from
exploitable sand
Cut and Fill Slopes
In general cut slopes may be designed with a slope inclination of two horizontal
to one vertical 2H 1V or flatter If necessary slope benches are recommended
at maximum 20 foot vertical height intervals Permanent fill slopes may also be
designed with a slope inclination of 2H 1V or flatter Fills placed on slopes
steeper than 5H 1V should be keyed and benched into the existing slope to
provide stability of the new fill against sliding The filling operations should start at
the lowest point and continue up in level horizontal compacted layers in
accordance with the above fill placement recommendations Fill slopes should be
constructed by overfilling and cutting back to the design slope ratio to obtain a
well compacted slope face In addition slope planting should be provided as
soon as possible to reduce the potential for erosion of the finished slopes
Sandy soils such as those encountered at the project site are highly susceptible
to erosion when exposed and subjected to flowing water particularly in sloping
areas The soils encountered at the site are also susceptible to wind erosion
Therefore surface water should be diverted away from the tops of slopes and
slope planting or other slope protection methods should be provided as soon as
possible to reduce the potential for significant erosion of the finished slopes

Fills and Backfills
In the event that the sand mining operations result in excavation below the future
rough finish grade for the subdivision imported general fill may be used to restore
the grades at the site This general fill material should consist of soil and rock
materials less than 6 inches with a CBR value of 8 or more and with a maximum
swell of less than 2 percent when tested in accordance with ASTM D 1883
Geolabs should observe and or test imported fill materials for suitability
Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
General fill materials should be moisture conditioned to within 2 percent of the
optimum moisture placed in level lifts not exceeding 10 inches in loose thickness
and compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction
refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum
dry density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557
Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by weight corresponding to the
maximum dry density We wish to emphasize that moisture conditioning of the fill
materials is an integral part of the fill placement recommendations provided herein
Compaction should be accomplished by sheepsfoot rollers vibratory rollers or
other types of acceptable compaction equipment
Because moisture conditioning and compaction of the soils are critical elements of
earthwork Geolabs should perform observations and soil density tests during site
grading to assist the contractor in obtaining the required degree of compaction and
the proper moisture content Where compaction is less than required additional
compactive effort should be applied with adjustment of moisture content as
necessary to obtain the specified compaction It should be noted that the moisture
requirement of the fills and subgrades about 2 percent above the optimum
moisture is an important requirement for the use of clayey soils
