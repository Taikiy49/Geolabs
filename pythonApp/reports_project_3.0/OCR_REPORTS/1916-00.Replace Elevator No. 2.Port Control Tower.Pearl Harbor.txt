INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our foundation evaluation
performed for the design of the proposed Replace Elevator No 32 project
at the Naval Shipyard in Pearl Harbor The approximate location of the
project is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this evaluation was to study the subsurface soil and
water conditions at the site to obtain pertinent soil data for use in the
foundation design of the proposed structures
The main objectives were to explore the thickness and competency of
the underlying soil deposit rock formation for spread footing design
The scope of our exploration included drilling and sampling two 2
borings to 9 5 to 15 feet below the existing ground surface Also
included were laboratory testing of the selected samples obtained
engineering evaluation of the field and laboratory data development of
design recommendations and report preparation
Detailed description of our field exploration and laboratory test
results are presented in the appendices of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a new elevator shaft and a new elevator
machine room to replace the existing elevator structures on the opposite
side of the existing Port Control Tower at the Naval Shipyard
The proposed elevator shaft will be a steel framed structure with
corrugated metal siding approximately 190 feet high
The proposed elevator machine room will be a small one story
building approximately 8 by 15 feet in plan dimensions located adjacent
to the new elevator shaft Concrete pad slab is anticipated for the new
building

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located at the southeastern side of the existing
Port Control Tower in front of Building Nos 64 storehouse and 64A Post
Office in the Naval Shipyard of Pearl Harbor on the island of Oahu
Hawaii
The control tower was modified from an existing elevated water tank
by building a control room on top of the tank and an elevator complex on
the northeastern side of the supporting tower for access to the room The
tower is supported by eight large braced steel legs bearing on concrete
footing foundations The new elevator complex will be built adjacent to
the control tower and opposite to the existing one in a small open space
between two of the steel legs
Based on our plan research it is our understanding that an existing
12 inch diameter freshwater line passes under the southern portion of the
proposed elevator machine room In addition some electrical utility
lines and a large underground water tank also exist in the vicinity of the
project site The approximate locations of some of these underground
utilities are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The ground surface at the site is generally flat Existing ground
cover consists of asphaltic concrete
SUBSOIL CONDITIONS

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The borings encountered generally hard tuff formation at shallow
depths below a thin expansive clay layer at the proposed building area
The laboratory tests indicated that the clay layer may become highly
expansive if recompacted Therefore spread or continuous footing
foundations bypassing the clay and bearing directly on the tuff formation
may be used to support the proposed structures
The footing foundations at the southern portion of the proposed
elevator machine room near Boring No 2 should be located away from the
existing underground 12 inch diameter freshwater line or be designed to
span over the utility trench
Based on the anticipated soil conditions we recommend that the
proposed structures be supported by spread or continuous footing
foundations bearing directly on the tuff formation An allowable bearing
capacity of 5000 p s f may be used for the spread or continuous footing
design
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1916 00 Page 5
Dk No 45
The bearing capacity value is for total dead plus live load only and
may be increased by one third for short duration wind or seismic loading
Estimated settlements under the anticipated design loadings are negligible
for the footings bearing on hard tuff formation
Footings should be placed directly on the tuff formation at about 2 5
to 3 feet below ground surface Uplift wind or other lateral loading
conditions may further increase the embedment of the footing foundations
Resistance to lateral loads on the proposed structures can be
obtained by passive earth pressure and frictional resistance A
frictional coefficient of 0 4 may be used to compute the frictional
resistance between the bottom of the footings and the tuff formation In
addition an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 p s f per foot of depth may
be used to determine the passive earth resistance against the buried
vertical faces of the footings
Southern Machine Room Foundation
It is our opinion that the southern machine room foundation should
not be placed directly on the water line trench backfill in order to avoid
imposing additional compressive stresses on the underlying 12 inch
diameter freshwater line In addition the quality of the trench backfill
is unknown If the backfill is in a loose condition due to poor
compaction footing slab bearing directly on the backfill could experience
settlements
Therefore we recommend that the footing foundations be designed to
span over the utility trench We believe that the footing foundations
placed directly on the underlying tuff formation will not impose
significant additional compressive stress on the adjacent utility line and
trench backfill
We also recommend that the exact location of the 12 inch diameter
freshwater line should be determined prior to final design stage to
facilitate the footing foundation design

Site Grading
Minimal site grading is anticipated for the proposed construction
over the small level project site area
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limits should be thoroughly cleared Any unsuitable materials such as
debris rubbish and old pavements should be removed and disposed of
properly off site in order to prevent contamination of the excavated
materials
Any loose wet or yielding spots encountered at subgrade level should
be removed and replaced with on site imported backfill material compacted
to the above mentioned standard
The on site near surface granular materials below the existing 4 inch
asphaltic concrete may be reused as a source of backfill material If
required imported backfill should consist of non expansive select
material less than 3 inches in maximum size with less than 20 passing the
200 sieve
The site should be graded to provide drainage of surface runoff away
from the structures
Slab On Grade
Concrete slab on grade is also anticipated for the proposed elevator
machine room
To support the anticipated heavy machines in the machine room we
recommend that a 6 inch concrete slab on a 4 inch layer of 3 Fine gravel
cushion fill be used
The finished grade of the Slab on grade is not available at this
stage However the on grade slabs should be placed on approximately the
existing grade to maintain the existing thickness of granular capping fill
over the highly expansive clay If a lower slab subgrade elevation closer

Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comment
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this review
is not made the geotechnical engineer can assume no responsibility for
misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Observation
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the
foundation phases of the work This is to observe compliance with the
design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow design
changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from that
anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in
this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as
borings Variations of conditions between the field data points may
occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become
evident until construction If variations then appear evident it will be
necessary to re evaluate the recommendations given in this report
The location of the field data points were determined by scaling from
the site plan Elevations of the data points were not available at this
stage of report preparation The physical location and elevation of the
field data points should be considered accurate only to the degree implied
by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling two
2 borings to depths of about 9 5 to 15 0 feet below the existing ground
surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 A
trailer mounted drill rig was used to drill the borings
The material encountered in the borings were classified by visual and
textural examination in the field by our geologist who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory The soils
were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A graphic presentation of the materials
encountered is presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 and A 2
Soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving a standard
penetration sampler or a 2 4 inch I D split barrel sampler with a
140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts to drive the sampler
the last 12 inches are shown on the Boring Logs at the appropriate sample
depths
Rock core samples were obtained by using an NX core barrel The core
recovery and RQD values are shown on the Boring Logs at the appropriate
sample depths
In the Boring Logs the following legend is used to represent the
various types of samples
il Split Spoon Sample 1 4 inch diameter
Ring Sample 2 4 inch diameter
NX Core Sample 2 1 inch diameter
