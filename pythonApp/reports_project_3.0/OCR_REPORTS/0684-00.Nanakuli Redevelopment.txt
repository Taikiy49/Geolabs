INTRODUCTION
The purpose of the soil investigation was to determine the
nature of the soils underlying the project site and to provide
recommendations for the design of the proposed pavement widening
and new drainage section This report summarizes our findings
and recommendations
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed redevelopment scheme consists of road and utility
improvements of existing and Mano
Avenues bounded by Haleakala and Nanakuli Avenue
W 2
In addition about 950 feet of open channel drainage section
will be constructed along the rear portion of the existing
residential lots located on the east side of Haleakala Avenue
The new channel will begin about 500 feet mauka of Farringhton
Highway
SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed redevelopment site is located in Nanakuli Valley
between Haleakala and Nanakuli Avenues The three block site
is approximately 1 500 feet mauka from Farringhton Highway in
Nanakuli Oahu Hawaii
In general the existing project roadway asphalt concrete pave
ments are in poor condittons with cracks and uneven depressions
at the surface The heaving pavement indicated swelling of the
underlying expansive subgrade soil Poor subgrade drainage
from the clay soil has contributed to rapid deterioration of
the upper roadway pavement
At the center portion of the project site branches of the site
stream create a depressed zone running in a east to west direc
tion Most of the existing drainage openings under the roadway
crossing in this lowlying area are covered with stream deposits
and debris materials
The existing drainage section was an open ditch about 5 feet
wide and 3 feet deep At the time of this soil investigation
the ditch was dry Dense coral outcrop was noted at most of
W 3
this ditch bank Brown clayey silt surface soil with scattered
coral boulders was noted at the mauka portion The makai end
of the new drainage section will be connected to a lined con
crete open channel
FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface soils at the site were explored by drilling 12
short borings ranging from 1 foot to 10 feet deep The boring
locations are shown on the enclosed Site Plan Plate 1 In
addition to drilling the borings probings were made at the
middle of the existing pavement to obtain actual pavement and
base course thickness and to evaluate subgrade soil type
The results of the probings are summarized on pages 4 5 A
detailed description of the field exploration methods and ac
companying laboratory testing is given in the appendix along
with the boring logs and laboratory test results
SOIL CONDITIONS
General
Based on the subsurface explorations and laboratory testing
results the site east of the existing stream generally con
sists of brown silty clay and grayish brown clay with some
cobbles and boulders which are identified locally as Adobe
soil This clayey subgrade area includes the total project
section of Pililaau Kawao and Kauwahi Avenues
W 4
The Mano Avenue is at a transition zone Hard coral was found
at the west portion and the clayey soil was found at the middle
and east portions of the section Boring No 3 at the Mano Avenue
next to the bridge structure indicated that more than 10 feet
thick of the Adobe soil was present in this area The thicker
Adobet layer may be due to stream deposit
Exposed dense and hard coral covers most portion of the proposed
drainage section Brown stony clayey silt is noted at the mauka
drain portion Coral layer is found at a shallow depth in the
norther portion of the proposed drainage section

DISCUSSION RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Because of the uneven settlement and deterioration of the existing
pavements it is rather difficult to salvage these roadway pavement
sections by adding an additional aspahltic concrete resurfacing
Also the resurfaced pavement will not provide a uniform support
over the entire roadway cross section due to the fact that the
existing pavement is only about 10 to 12 feet wide compared to
the proposed 24 feet wide pavement
W 6
In most of the area the subgrade soil is highly expansive Adobe
clay The present pavement section is inadequate to handle the
expansive soil conditions and traffic load
Where the existing pavement is at or close to the proposed improve
ment roadway elevation the existing pavement will have to be re
moved and over excavated down to the proper subgrade depth which
will be based upon the type of subgrade soil encountered
The broken asphaltic concrete and base course layer could be stock
piled at the job site for future use as a source of select borrow
However care should be exercised not to contaminate the granular
excavated material from the upper section of the roadway with the
underlying clayey soils
Pavement Design
Representative surface samples were obtained during our subsurface
investigation and tested to evaluate their subgrade support capa
city Three California Bearing Ratio tests were performed in
accordance with the American Society for Testing and Material
Test Designation D 1883 utilizing a ten pound surcharge The
test results for the on site clay soils indicated CBR values
ranging from 2 to 4 percent with expansion values of 6 to 13
percent The individual CBR test result is presented on Plates
B 1 to B 3 in the enclosed appendix
W 7
One inch thick remolded and undisturbed in place samples were
also used to measure the swell potential of on site clayey soils
The test results are summarized in the Summary of Swell Test Re
sults on Plate B 4 In general the test results are in close
agreement with the ones obtained from the CBR method
Based upon the results of our field exploration and laboratory
testing the following are our pavement section recommendations

The base course should be crushed rock or other granular material
with a CBR value of 85 or more or conforming with Section 31 2
Standard Specifications of Public Construction or equivalent
The select borrow should be crusher run waste mudrock coral
sand or cinder with less than 15 passing the 200 sieve and
compacted to 95 of its maximum density The borrow soil could
be non expansive soil with a CBR value of 8 or greater The
top 6 inches of the subgrade soil should be scarified and recom
pacted wet of optimum moisture to 90 compaction The subgrade
soil should not be allowed to become dry and to develop shrinkage
cracks prior to the completion of the base course placement
W 8
At the time of the proposed roadway construction additional
CBR tests should be performed on the exposed subgrade soils
and based on the new test results the above pavement sections
may be revised accordingly
Due to the poor drainage characteristics of this Adobe soil a
subdrainage system should be provided in the lowlying sections
of the roadway to avoid rapid pavement deterioration due to the
trapped water in the base course and subbase layer
Sidewalk
Expansive soil under sidewalk shall be removed and replaced with
6 inches of quarry waste base course The subgrade soil below
the base course should be pre saturated prior to the placement
of the concrete The concrete should be poured in alternate
sections and reinforced with wire mesh
Open Channel
For the retaining wall in coral formation active earth pressure
may be designed as an equivalent fluid having a density of 35
pounds per cubic foot if granular backfill is used The equiva
lent fluid density should be increased to 60 pounds per cubic foot
where the section is in clayey soil formation Allowable coeffi
cient of friction between coral and concrete of 0 4 may be used
Although the clayey soil in the mauka portion of the proposed
drainage section is at shallow depth if expansive soil is en
countered at the bottom of the channel and wall footing it should
be undercut and replaced with a mtnimum of 2 feet of non expansive
W 9
compacted backfill
Allowable bearing capacity of 8 000 p s f may be used when the
foundation bears on coral formation
Weepholes or a subsurface drainage system should be incorporated
in all retaining walls to prevent the build up of excessive hydro
static pressure behind the wall
For the channel section with a shallow depth of 3 feet or less
the open channel surface can be protected by a gunite lining
The planned 14 1 horizontal to vertical gunited slope bank
of the channel section will have an adequate factor of safety
for slope stability If the slope bank is steeper than the
14 1 slope it should be supported structurally
To release the hydrostatic pressure build up under the channel
slab holes should be incorporated through the slab and a 6 inch
layer of No 3B Fine Rock or equivalent granular material should
be provided under the slab
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
To a degree the performance of the above recommendations depends
on construction procedures and quality Hence observation of site
subgrade preparation should be carried out by a soils engineer to
permit any necessary field adjustments and correlation between
the preliminary soil data obtained during this investigation and
the actual soil conditions encountered during construction
W 10
The field engineering monitoring should include the necessary
density testing to assure that the proper degree of moisture
content and compaction are consistently achieved
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon
the above inspections
Should you have any questions concerning our findings or recom
mendations please feel free to contact us
