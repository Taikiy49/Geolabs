Dear Sirs
In accordance with your request a preliminary soils investigation
has been made on the subject based on plans made available to
us on March 12 1981 This report presents the results of our
field reconnaissance laboratory tests and analyses of soil
samples taken from seven 7 hand dug probings made at various
locations of the proposed site The observations and recommenda
tions contained in this report are based on visual examination
of soil as revealed by Exploratory Probings and on the Results
of Laboratory Testing
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of the investigation is to evaluate the suitability
of the site for the planned development by exploring soil and
W O 1024 00 P 2
ground water conditions We also have developed soil parameters
for design and construction of foundations
The scope of work included seven 7 hand dug probings Soil
samples were obtained during the investigation Laboratory
testing was performed to classify and identify the characteristics
of the subsoils The results of our investigation together with
conclusions and recommendations are summarized in this report
The approximate boring locations are shown in the attached
Site Plan Plate 1
SITE SUITABILITY
The site is suitable for the proposed development provided
our recommendations are followed A summary of our recommenda
tions is presented in this report Detailed discussions and
recommendations are contained in the body of this report

DRAINAGE
The present site drains to the north northwest There are
steep vertical cut banks to the east and extreme west portion
of the project site
The north portion and to the present existing rock wall in
the southern part of the proposed site is covered with a dark
brown clayey silt ranging from present surface elevation to
approximately a one foot depth which gradually diminishes to
the south The south portion of the site has surface
boulders which range to a depth of three 3 feet or more
Due to the difficulty of access for truck mounted drilling
equipment our soils investigation was limited to small portable
equipments and hand dug probes which could not penetrate beyond
the boulder layer of three 3 feet in the south quarter of the
project

CONCLUSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the results of the field investigation laboratory
testing and engineering analysis the following conclusions
and recommendations are presented
The area is underlain by competent subsoils Normal site
grading methods and conventional Spread or continuous footing
foundations are recommended for the proposed buildings

SITE PREPARATION
The site should be clear of vegetation debris and all other
deleterious material The cleared site should be scarified
to a minimum depth of six 6 inches brought to near optimum
moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of
its maximum dry density established by ASTM test designation
D 1557 70
SLOPES
One and a half horizontal to one vertical 1 1 cut slope is
recommended for the upper area of the site
FOUNDATIONS
The proposed residential structures may be supported by
conventional spread or continuous footing foundations The
footings should extend a minimum of 24 inches below finish
grade Footings located close to the top of slopes should be
embedded a sufficient depth to obtain a 10 foot minimum
horizontal set back distance from the slope face
The foundation can be designed using a bearing value of 3 500
p s f The above bearing capacity are for total of dead plus
live loads and may be increased by 33 percent when temporary
lateral load wind and seismic are considered

In general relatively uniform subsurface soil conditions were
encountered The majority of building area will be in slight
cut Post footing foundations can be supported on very stiff
underlying natural soils Settlements are expected to be
small generally less than inch
Slab On Grade
It is recommended that slab on grade be placed over a four 4
inch layer of base course compacted to 95 percent of its
maximum dry density
The subgrade should be kept moist and protected from drying
prior to concrete placement Subgrade should be compacted to
a minimum of 90 percent of its maximum dry density
Slab on grade should be separated from foundations In order
to protect against moisture damage of the flooring material
a polyethylene moisture barrier should be incorporated within
floor slabs
Post Footings
Post footings should be a minimum depth of 24 inches below
finish grade and should consist of pour in place concrete
Post footings located close to top of slopes should be
embedded a sufficient depth to obtain a 10 foot minimum
horizontal set back distance from the Slope face

DESIGN REVIEW
The plans for foundations site grading and any utility
construction should be forwarded to the soils engineer
for review and comments prior to construction to see
that the intent of our recommendations is included in
the project plans and specifications
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present
during earthwork construction particularly the contrac
tor s grading operations
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be
observed by a soils engineer during construction to see
that adequate bearing has been obtained and that the
subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings
of our subsurface investigation
It is not anticipated that any material will be imported
for the project site However any imported material
should be non expansive tested and approved by the soils
engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent
upon the above inspections
