PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this investigation was to evaluate the sub
surface soil conditions at the project site in order to
provide soil data for the design and construction of the
site grading and foundations of the proposed single family
residential development
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests
which were used in the engineering analysis that formu
lated our conclusions and recommendations are presented
in the appendix of this report
The approximate boring locations are shown on the Boring
Location Plan Plate 1 and the boring logs are presented
in Appendix A Information regarding the field explora
tion and laboratory testing are also presented in the
appendices

SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed development is located along Kahuapaani
Street and is bordered to the north by Kahuapaani Street
and to the south by the Foster Village Subdivision
Most of the site consists of a drainageway which extends
from Foster Village Subdivision toward the northwest to
Kahuapaani Street The drainageway area is generally
about 20 to 30 feet deep with side slopes varying from
drainageway at the time of our field exploration
There is a higher gently sloping area in the northeastern
portion of the site which is at about the same elevation
as the adjacent Foster Village Subdivision
The site is currently vacant and igs covered with a rela
tively heavy growth of haole koa keawe and other trees
and shrubs
Ground surface elevation varies between 32 and 80 feet
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
a ee
The proposed plan is to clear and grade the 5 7 acre site
for a single family residential development with a 44 foot
wide roadway to provide access into the subdivision
The preliminary grading plan available at the time of our
investigation indicates that most of the site grading will
consist of filling the lower drainageway area with some
cuts along the upper perimeters of the site Fills up to

eastern portion of the site are anticipated
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The project site is located on the northeastern flank of
the Aliamanu Salt Lake vent complex of the Honolulu Vol
canic Series As the molten magma ascended through these
vents it reacted with the groundwater generating steam
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Page 3
This steam caused explosive eruptions which deposited low
cones of ash material which have consolidated to form
tuff locally referred to as mudrock
The Aliamanu eruptive event deposited its material over
alluvium originating from Halawa Stream and in doing so
dammed the course of the strean causing deposition of
alluvium on the northern flank of the Aliamanu cone
Subsequent to the deposition of this alluvium the Salt
Lake eruptive event deposited its tuff over this mantle of
alluvium overlying the Aliamanu tuff
Stream erosion and diversion of the course of Halawa
Stream has shaped the area to its present topography

site down to approximately elevation 56 feet Between
these two elevations alluvium was encountered This
alluvium consisted primarily of hard brown clayey silts
with occasional cobbles and gravel

Site Grading
The preliminary grading plan available at the time of our
investigation indicates that the major portion of the
grading will be fills up to 19 feet thick in the lower
drainageway area and cuts to approximately 13 feet in the
upper areas
Prior to filling the site should first be cleared of
vegetation debris and other unsuitable materials The
area to receive fill should then be scarified and recom
pacted to 90 of its maximum density as established by
ASTM D 1557 test method In the case where the base of
the fill will rest on competent tuff these scarifying and
recompacting operations may be omitted
Fills should be placed in about 10 inch horizontal loose
lifts and compacted to obtain 90 of the above specified
maximum density
Fills placed on slopes having grades steeper than 5 1
H V should be keyed and benched into firm existing
ground
Excavated on site material including tuff generally less
than 8 inches in size may be used for fill material
Imported fill material should generally be low to non
expansive soils and should be submitted to the project
soils engineer for laboratory testing and approval prior
to its intended delivery to the project site
Very stiff alluvial soils and competent tuff mudrock
were encountered in the lower portion of the site there
fore it is our opinion that major ground settlement pro
blems are not anticipated for fills over most of this area

Two settlement gauges are recommended in the high fill
area at the start of the access road into the subdivi
sion Existing settlement gauges for the adjacent
development indicate that a few inches of settlement may
be anticipated
Based on our previous experience we believe the tuff
material generally encountered above elevation 56 feet
can generally be rippable by a D 8 dozer or its equi
valent However some isolated pockets of hard ripping
may be encountered Care should be exercised during
grading to avoid over ripping and undercutting of the
proposed finish grades Any areas which are over
excavated need to be backfilled to obtain 95 of its
maximum density as determined by ASTM D 1557
Utility Trenches
Tuff formation was encountered on the site below appro
ximately elevation 30 feet and above elevation 56 feet
If utility trenches are required to extend through these
materials moderate to hard excavation may be anticipated
and some special excavating techniques such as hoerams
may be necessary to excavate the utility trenches
bd
Normal excavation is anticipated in the very stiff to hard
alluvial materials between elevations 30 and 56 feet
Proper backfilling of the trenches is important to mini
mize ground surface settlement along the utility align
ments All backfill should be compacted to 90 of its
maximum density as determined by ASTM D 1557
Slopes
Slope ratios of 2 1 H V can be used for fill slopes and
cut slopes in undisturbed alluvial materials
Steeper cut slope ratios up to 1 1 H V are recommended
in competent volcanic tuff mudrock formation
For cut and fill slopes greater than 20 feet in vertical
height 8 foot drainage benches should be placed at mid
height of the slope
Subsurface water should be diverted away from the top of
the slope and slope planting utilized to reduce erosion

Foundations
In general the field explorations disclosed competent
foundation bearing materials throughout the site
For the proposed light residential structures spread
footing foundations or slab on grade may be used
Slabs should be set back from the tops of the slopes a
minimum distance of 5 feet Post footings located close
to the tops of slopes should have sufficient embedment to
obtain a minimum horizontal set back distance of 5 feet
measured from the outer edge of the footing to the slope
face
For the light loads anticipated an allowable bearing
pressure of 2500 p s f can be used for footings bearing
on tuff alluvium or compacted fill This value may be
increased by one third when temporary wind or seismic
loads are considered
Surface water should be diverted away from building foun
dations
Slab On Grade
Slab on grade should be placed over a 4 inch layer of base
course compacted to 95 of its maximum density as esta
blished by ASTM D 1557 The thickened edge around the
slab on grade should extend a minimum of 8 inches below
the adjacent ground surface
To protect against moisture damage of the flooring
material a ployethylene moisture barrier should be
considered
To reduce differential settlement where the slab is partly
on cut in tuff and partly on fill or alluvium the cut
portion should be over excavated 1l foot and then recom
pacted to match the adjacent soil density
Pavement
Asphaltic concrete pavements are planned for the project
Assuming on site excavated tuff or select off site borrow

of the road fill the preliminary pavement design is as

during earthwork construction to monitor the contractor s
grading operations
It is also recommended that the footing excavation be

subsurface investigation

tested and approved by the soils engineer prior to its
delivery to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent
upon the above inspections

STANDARD GRADING SPECIFICATIONS
PROPOSED ALII ESTATES
The work under this section includes clearing and grubbing
preparation of fill areas placement and compaction of fill
and removal and backfilling of underground structures These
specifications are of a general nature and may be superseded
by specific requirements or recommendations contained within
the soils report
Tl
Clearing
All areas within contract limit lines shall be cleared of
trash debris organic matter and all other deleterious
materials These materials shall be removed and disposed
offsite
Preparation of Fill Areas
A
Loose surface soils shall be removed to expose compe
tent natural soils Localized soft pockets shall be
undercut and backfilled with compacted select mate
rials
All surfaces shall be inspected and approved by the
soils engineer prior to the placement of any fill
After approval the surface shall be scarified to a
depth of six inches brought to near optimum moisture
content and compacted as herein specified unless the
base of the fill will rest on competent tuff
Ill
IV
2
Where the existing surface exceeds a Slope of 5
horizontal to 1 vertical 5 1 the ground surface
shall be keyed and benched into competent soil to
bond the fill to the existing ground surface
Subsurface drains may be required as directed by the
soils engineer where springs or zones of seepage are
encountered
Fill Materials
Material for fill shall consist of on site soils or
approved import material Import material shall be
tested and approved prior to transportation to the site
Fill material shall contain less than 5 percent organic
matter and other deleterious material and shall not
contain rocks or lumps in excess of eight inches 8
in diameter
Placement and Compaction of Fill
A
The fill shall be placed in horizontal layers which
when compacted will not exceed ten inches 10 in
thickness Each layer shall be spread uniformly and
blade mixed to attain uniformity of material and
Water content within each layer
GEOLABS HAWAII
3
Rock fragments or boulders less than eight inches
8 in diameter may be utilized provided they are
not concentrated in any one area and that there is
sufficient fine grained material to fill the voids
between the rocks
Rocks greater than eight inches 8 in diameter
shall be removed from the fill unless specifically
approved for placement in the fill by the soils
engineer
All fi11 shall be compacted to a minimum of 90
percent of the maximum dry density established by
ASTM Test Designation D 1557 unless otherwise
specified in the soils report
The compaction equipment may be sheepsfoot vibra
tory or pneumatic tire rollers suited to the soil
type and capable of producing the specified results
in an expedient and efficient manner The equipment
shall be in good working condition fully ballasted
and self cleaning
The compaction shall be accomplished in a systematic
and uniform manner over the entire fill area to pro
duce a uniformly compacted fill

H The soils engineers shall be provided unrestricted

Removal and Backfilling of Underground Structures
Underground structures such as cesspools cisterns
septic tanks wells pipe lines etc shall be removed
as directed by the soils engineer Backfilling of the

seasonal Requirements

conditions When interruption of work is due to
heavy rain the previous fill surface shall be
approved before resumption of earthmoving operations

surface water
