Discussion and Recommendation
General
In general our ten previously drilled borings at the proposed
redevelopment site encountered a 3 to 4 foot thick layer of highly
compressible lagoonal silty clay with organic lenses below the upper 1 to
4 foot thick surface fill crust Beneath this upper highly compressible
layer is another 7 to 16 feet thick layer of very soft to very loose
moderately compressible coralline silty sands and sandy silts
Due to the underlying soft ground conditions and the high ground
water levels near the ground surface significant settlements are
anticipated particularly in areas where the weight of the new Phase I
Part 2 site grading fill is large
However we believe that the majority of the fill settlements would
occur fairly rapidly because of the limited thickness of the highly
compressible upper lagoonal silty clay and the granular nature of the
underlying compressible lagoonal coralline silty sand and sandy silt
To induce the majority of the ground settlements due to placement of
the Part 2 site grading fill to occur prior to the initiation of gunite
wall construction we recommend that the new site grading fill be placed
as soon as possible Settlement gauges are recommended to monitor the

Additional Site Filling Along Gunite Retaining Wall Areas
It is our understanding that the project site was previously graded
according to the old Phase I site grading plans However according to
the revised grading plans of the project site additional
site filling will be placed along some if the proposed gunite retaining
wall areas The additional site filling may cause further ground
settlements in some gunite retaining wall areas therefore resulting in
differential settlements between the new and old fill areas It is
estimated that the proposed gunite retaining walls to be built in these
areas may be subjected to as much as 1 in 50 to 1 in 100 differential
settlements
To reduce potential excessive settlements along the proposed gunite
retaining walls we suggest that the new additional site filling be placed
at least 3 months prior to gunite wall construction
The same fill placement and settlement monitoring procedures
recommended in our previous soil report should be followed in future
construction of the project

Gunite Retaining Walls
Prior to construction of the proposed gunite retaining walls the
retaining wall areas should be overfilled beyond the anticipated wall face
to a distance equivalent to the free standing heights of the walls A
Slope ratio of about 1 1 H V may be used for the temporary fill slope
Figure 1 We believe that this overfill will serve the dual purposes of
first inducing the ground settlements under the future wall footing
locations and also facilitating the later wall construction by providing a
well compacted fill
After the waiting period the overfilled slopes may be cut back
behind the anticipated wall face locations for the construction of the
proposed gunite retaining walls Granular material such as sand is
recommended for the backfill behind the walls to reduce the compactive
effort required and to minimize the build up of additional lateral earth
pressure due to high compaction effort
It is our understanding that some sections of the retaining walls may
be constructed with the wall footings below groundwater level Before the
underwater wall footing trenches are excavated proper temporary drainage
systems such as trench drains should be installed at the toe of the the
cut back slope and around the excavation to depress the existing
groundwater level below the trench Figure 1 This drainage system is
essential for maintaining the stability of the excavated wall footing
trench for subsequent footing construction
Based on this construction procedure and backfill material criteria
we recommend that the lateral earth pressure parameters in our memorandum
dated may be used for the design of the proposed gunite
retaining walls

Retaining Wall Footings
Provided the proposed retaining walls are constructed following the
above recommended additional site filling procedure the generally lightly
loaded retaining wall footing foundation above water can be placed
directly on the upper soil crust at the project site as previously
recommended in our soil report W O 1752 00
However it is anticipated that some of the footings may be embedded
below elevation 3 feet and in the close proximity of the underlying
soft loose subsoils and high groundwater level As a result we recommend
that these footing areas be overexcavated a minimm of 2 feet deep and 1
foot away on both sides below the footing bottoms The overexcavation
should be replaced with compacted import gravel and or dry on site
sandy gravelly soils confined by a geotextile fabric over the underlying
soft loose soils Figure 1
Building Structure Wall Footings
It is our understanding that the surcharge program previously
recommended in our soil report will not be used for the proposed
buildings structures Therefore special footing design is needed to
reduce the settlement potential of the building structure footings placed
close to the underlying soft loose subsoils
We recommend that the above discussed overexcavation and backfill
method for Retaining Wall Footing be used for the building structure
wall footings The overexcavation may remove some of the underlying
soft loose soils and the compacted sandy gravelly backfill may reduce

Water Tanks
It is our understanding that two concrete water tanks will be
constructed at the southwestern corner of the project site The proposed
water tanks will have capacities of 15 000 and 30 000 gallons Large mat
foundations or base slabs are anticipated for supporting the proposed
water tanks on the underlying soft loose subsoils
Based on our findings the water tanks should be placed at the
optimum embedment depth of about 5 feet below the existing ground surface
elevation 0 feet The excavation for the tank embedment would reduce
the additional pressure on the underlying soft loose soils from the weight
of the water tanks and the water storage by removing about 5 feet of the
existing overburden soils
The hydraulic buoyance uplift force on the tanks due to maximm 2
feet submergency into groundwater could be offset by the dead weight of
the empty tanks alone
Estimated settlement of the tanks under full water storage is about 1
inch total and one half of an inch differential
Deadman Anchor
It is also our understanding that 5 foot cubic concrete deadman
anchors with a cable support system will be used to support the proposed
bird cages These concrete anchor blocks will be embedded to about 5 feet
below the existing ground surface with their bottoms at the elevation of
about 0 feet
