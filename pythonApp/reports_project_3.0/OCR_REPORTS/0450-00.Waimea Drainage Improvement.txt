INTRODUCTION
This report presents the findings and conclusions of a pre
liminary soils investigation performed at the requested boring
locations along the proposed Waimea drainage project alignment
in Waimea Hawaii The purpose of this investigation was to
evaluate the soil conditions underlying the site with respect
to the planned construction to define their engineering
characteristics and to provide general recommendations for
excavations foundations and construction
This investigation included drilling 3 test borings at the
requested locations identification logging and sampling
of the soils encountered laboratory testing and engineering
analyses The locations of borings are shown on Plate l

_ PROJECT LOCATION PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION AND SITE DESCRIPTION
It is our understanding from preliminary information that the
proposed Waimea Drainage project will consist of a 7 foot dia
meter CMP pipe which will roughly parallel the north side of
Konokohau Road until it reaches the intersection of Konokohau
Road and Laelae Road From this point the proposed route will
follow a 10 foot wide drainage easement in a southerly direction
past Kawaihae Road to discharge into Waikoloa Stream See Plate 1
The pipe will generally follow the existing contours and the
invert will be at a depth of approximately 10 feet below exist
ing grade cover of about 3 féet
The site drains in a southerly direction towards Waikoloa Stream
Generally the proposed alignmenttis presently grass covered
pasture land The southerly reaches contain stands of trees
in close proximity to the proposed route

The borings were excavated with a Minuteman portable drill rig

The borings were logged and the soils classified according to

30 inches The number of blows required to drive the sampler
the last 12 inches is recorded and then correlated back to a
Standard Penetration Test which consists of the same procedure
described above but with a 2 inch 0 D sampler The correthated
Standard penetrations are given in the boring logs

in our laboratory investigation are probably representative of

quested borings soil characteristics could vary between borings

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The Kohala volcano was apparently the first of the Hawaiian
Island volcanoes to become extinct There has been sufficient
time for canyon cutting on the slopes of the mountain and the
development of a residual soil on the dividess
The site is underlain by the Kohala volcano basalts at depths
of 3 to 6 feet The basalts are weathered to depths from
10 to at least 14 feet The bedrock is overlain by 3 to 6 feet
of probable slopewash debris and residual soil
Soils
Generally the soils encountered in the requested borings consisted
of from 10 to at least 14 feet of brown to multicolored clayey
silt to silty clay decomposed rock overlying hard basalt bed
rock In boring 3 stiff decomposed material but not hard
unweathered bedrock was encountered to the depth drilled The
consistency of the overburden soils varied from medium to very
stiff The moisture condition was generally damp to moist No
free groundwater was encountered in any of the borings

CONCLUSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based upon the results of the field investigation laboratory
testing and engineering analyses the following preliminary
conclusions and recommendations are presented Due to the
limited exploration variable conditions may be encountered
between borings which will necessitate additional or different
recommendations
Bedding
No material suitable for bedding the proposed pipeline was
encountered during the exploration Adequate imported bedding
will therefore be required Material to be used for bedding
should be granular soil having a minimum san equivalent of 30
The recommended bedding for the subject pipe based on the
conditions encountered is a Class B bedding with the recom
mended granular material beneath the pipe as shown in ASCE
Manual No 37 Design and Construction of Sanitary and Storm
Sewers dated 1970
Trench Excavation
Calculations indicate that the trench sidewalls should stand
vertically to the depths proposed for short periods provided
Page 6
W O 450 00
that no surcharge loads from construction traffic or stock
piled materials are placed within 10 feet of the excavation
The proposed excavation depth of approximately 10 feet should
however be provided with minimum bracing in accordance with
safety regulations Local sloughing can occur anywhere atong
the alignment
Since the spacing between the requested borings is considerable
changed conditions between borings may require alternate side
slope stability recommendations The contractor on the site
should bring to the soils engineer s attention any changed
condition affecting trench excavation stability Large tree
roots or trunks sencountered along the alignment can greatly
affect excavation stability and must be taken into account
In two out of three borings hard basalt bedrock was encountered
near the proposed invert grade It is possible that the depth
to bedrock between borings may become shallow enough to create
problems for conventional excavation methods Local areas may
require some jackhammering and or minor blasting to reach the
proposed invert grade
Groundwater should not be a problem since none was encountered
during the investigation

Bearing Capacity Loading and Settlement
The bearing capacity of the invert soils is generally considered
to be sufficient to support the anticipated overburden loads
provided that the pipe is properly bedded as recommended in
this report and good construction practices are followed No
excessive settlements are anticipated based upon limited con
solidation test data for overburden pressures to be reimposed
Earth pressures on buried conduits tend to increase with in
creasing trench width We therefore recommend that the trench
width be kept to a minimum or utilize sloping trench sides in
design
Backfill
Backfill should be placed so that the pipe will not be displaced
or damaged Backfill material to a depth of one foot over the
crown of the pipe should consist of selected free draining
well graded material and be placed by hand or suitable equipment
and compacted to a minimum of 90 relative compaction in accord
ance with the provisions of ASTM D 1557 70
Due to the relatively thin cover approximately 3 feet the
upper 2 feet should consist of adequate impervious backfill
placed in thin lifts and compacted to a minimum of 90 relative

of the native materials is suitable for the upper 2 feet of
backfilling provided that organic materials and other objec

be removed
Investigation Limitations
Based upon the limited exploration the materials encountered
on the project site and utilized in our laboratory testing
program are believed to be representative of the earth materials
that will be encountered along the proposed storm drain align
ment Although no groundwater was encountered during the
exploration on the dates indicated it should not be construed
as representing the possible groundwater conditions that may
be encountered during construction
Since our investigation is based upon limited observed soil
and groundwater conditions selective laboratory testing and
engineering analyses the conclusions and recommendations are
professional opinions These opinions have been derived from
the requested borings in accordance with current standards

Laboratory Testing
LABORATORY TESTING
A laboratory testing program was developed to establish the
engineering properties of the soils and rocks encountered at
the project site The tests where applicable to the subject
project are included in this Appendix
1 Classification Tests
a
b

Visual Classification All soil samples obtained from
the borings were brought to our laboratory where they
were visually reclassified to confirm or modify the
field classification prior to finalizing the Logs of
Borings
Water Content Determinations In addition to visual
classification typical samples were tested for natural
water content as an aid in soil classification and in
evaluating soil properties The water content values
are based upon the dry weight of the soil
Atterberg Limits Atterberg limit tests were performed
on selected clay and silt samples obtained from the
borings for the purposes of identification and correla
tion of soils Standard procedures ASTM D 424 for the
plastic limit and ASTM D 423 for the liquid limit were
used in the performance of these tests
1
d
Grain Size Analysis Sieve analysis were performed on
granular soils Hor fine grained soils the hydrometer
test was performed to determine the distribution of
the grain sizes beyond the No 200 sieve
2 Shear Strength Tests
a
b
Unconfined Compression Tests Intact core and split
spoon soil samples which apparently exhibited high
plasticity were tested for unconfined strength Length
to diameter ratio of 2 was maintained throughout the
tests to avoid scatter of test results and to facilitate
evaluation of shear strength values for design purposes
Direct Shear Test In general soil samples were tested
for shear strength using a 2 41 I D direct shear box
Each set consisted of at least two tests each test run
under different normal loads The results are plotted
as Normal Stress vs Shear Strength with a failure envelope
drawn to determine values of cohesion c and angle of
frictional resistance 9
Consolidation Test Consolidation tests were performed on
selected undisturbed ring samples of the compressible
soils to provide basic data for making settlement calculations
Porous stones were placed on the top and bottom of the samples
to allow drainage Vertical loads were applied in increments
with each load increment being allowed to consolidate prior
2
to adding the next increment Measurements of the time
and consolidation were obtained during each load increment
and rebound was measured during the unloading portion
Consolidation test results are plotted in terms of percent
settlement versus applied loads
Swell Test The ring sample was placed between porous
stones and a 60 psf load was applied to the sample for a
24 hour period The difference between the initial and
final sample heights is the amount of swell which is expressed
as a percentage of the initial height of the sample
Maximum Density Test Samples of soils are compacted in a
mold of given size with a 10 pound rammer dropped 18 inches
to determine the relationship between moisture content and
density of the soil At least three trial points at dif
ferent moisture content are run to determine the maximum
density and optimum moisture content The test is run in
accordance with ASTM D 1557 70
