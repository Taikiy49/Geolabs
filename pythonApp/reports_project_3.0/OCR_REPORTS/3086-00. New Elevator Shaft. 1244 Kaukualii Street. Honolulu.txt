Based on previous experience in the area the subsoil conditions are likely to
vary significantly over short distances especially near Kapalama Drainage Canal In
addition soft compressible soils should be expected and were encountered in our
boring located at the south corner of the building We had recommended that
additional exploratory work be performed to evaluate the conditions at the site in our
letter dated and fee proposal dated August 9 1994 Since our
recommended course of action was not implemented and considering the potential for
variations in subsurface conditions settlement of the reconstructed footings may occur
and should be expected The magnitude of this settlement may be small or large and
could not be quantified without obtaining additional subsurface information
At this time we understand that some of the footings have been constructed
without the additional exploratory work Therefore after discussions with you and the
owner it is our understanding that the owner is fully aware of the site conditions and
accepts the higher level of risk associated with potential settlement of the footings
The structural plans reviewed generally reflect the recommendations presented
in the above referenced report However as we have explained before we are
uncertain about the applicability of the recommendations contained in the above
referenced report to the proposed footings and relocated elevator shaft especially with
respect to potential settlements
