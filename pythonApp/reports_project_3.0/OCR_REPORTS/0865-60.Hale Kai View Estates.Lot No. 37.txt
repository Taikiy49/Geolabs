As requested we have made a study of the near surface soil conditions
below the above referenced lot The purpose of the study was to eva
luate the suitability of the near surface soils as foundation support
for the proposed house The study consisted of excavating one 1
test pit and drilling two 2 borings within the proposed building
location laboratory soil tests and a review of the grading and
compaction test record on the building site This report summarizes
our observations and recommendations

Lot 37 is located in the northwestern portion of the subdivision
development on the southwestern side of Kahena Street The lot is

upper portion of the lot The lot generally slopes upward from
Kahena Street toward the southwest at about 19 to 24 percent
gradient The elevation of the lot generally varies from about 233 to

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
A review of the grading compaction test record and the site recon
naissance indicate that the lot where most of the house is planned is
underlain by a layer of grayish brown expansive clay and boulders
about 5 feet thick
The remaining portion of the lot beyond the house area is probably
underlain by natural colluvial deposits transported from the upper
hillside area These deposits are typically heterogenous and vary
from clayey silt low expansion potential to clay high expansion
potential

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that most of the lot is underlain by
more than 5 feet of grayish brown clay and boulders The laboratory
test results on this material generally indicated that they have high
expansion potential Therefore for Lot 37 the slab on grade and
foundation construction would require special design procedures in
order to reduce the shrink swell effect of the subsoils
Foundation
Post and beam construction is highly desirable and recommended due to
the sloping terrain and expansive soil conditions The post and beam
construction generally results in lower foundation costs and the
individual footings can be readily adjusted should the need occur
Where utilized concrete slab on grade should also be properly
designed for expansive soil conditions
Due to the expansive soil and sloping grade we recommend that the
individual post footing be extended a minimum depth of 36 inches below
the ground surface
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Page 5
In the rear portion of the house the footing embedment may be reduced
if better gravelly colluvial soil or weathered rock is encountered
All footing should have a minimum embedment of 12 inches below the
finished grade
The footings should be poured in place and the excavation backfilled
and compacted to provide an impervious layer over the footing
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f can be used for footings
bearing on the stiff natural ground or compacted fill The bottom of
the footing excavation should be neat and free of loose soils or
debris
If a footing is located next to a utility line it should be extended
to the bottom of the utility trench to reduce footing settlement due
to settlement of the trench backfill
Surface water should be diverted away from the foundations This is
especially important in the rear portion of the house to reduce
ponding of surface run off water and possible saturation and
settlement or heaving of the foundation soils

Slab On Grade
Concrete slab on grade is planned for the garage area It should be
noted that the on site clay soils are expansive and unsuitable as slab
subgrade soils In order to reduce the expansive effects of the clay
the expansive clays should be overexcavated down for a depth of 3 feet
below the slab subgrade and then replaced with non expansive borrow
soils such as select borrow crusher waste or coral compacted to a
minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78
A 4 inch layer of base course should be provided below the slab and
compacted to 90 of its maximum dry density In order to protect
against moisture infiltration a polyethylene moisture barrier may be
incorporated below the slab The membrane should be protected by a
thin layer of sand to aid in the concrete curing
Concrete Driveway and Sidewalks
A layer of more than 5 feet of expansive soils was encountered in the
front portion of the lot driveway area Since the driveway can
tolerate some amount of heaving the clay overexcavation and
replacement can be reduced to 2 feet below the driveway subgrade level

Where 2 or more feet of fill is planned select non expansive borrow
material should be used to cap over the expansive clay Where less
than 2 feet of fill is planned overexcavation and replacement of the
expansive clay with select non expansive material is required The
above capping requirements should be followed for a minimum distance
of 3 feet beyond the driveway
For the relatively long driveway planned joints are recommended at
about 8 to 10 foot intervals in order to allow for some relative move
ment of the concrete
For future sidewalks capping of the clay or overexcavation and
replacement similar to the driveway are recommended to reduce heaving
caused by the expansive clay soil
