As requested we have made a study of the near surface soil
conditions below the above referenced lot The purpose of the
study was to evaluate the suitability of the near surface soils
as foundation support for the proposed house The study con
sisted of four 4 hand auger probes within the proposed build
ing site laboratory soil tests and a review of the grading
and compaction test record on the building site This report
summarizes our observations and recommendations
PROJECT CONSIDERATION
The proposed single family residence consists of a two story
wood structure approximately 46 to 62 feet wide by 27 to 53
long Based on the preliminary plans provided to us the house
will be set back about 18 feet from Kulani Street
Post and beam construction with wood floors are planned for the
living and family room of the house

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that the lot is underlain by
more than 3 feet of grayish brown clay fill The laboratory
test results on this material generally indicated that they
have high expansion potential Therefore for Lot 64 the
slab on grade and foundation construction would require special
design procedures in order to reduce the shrink swell effect of
the subsoils
House Foundation
For this lot post and beam construction is highly desirable
and recommended due to the sloping terrain and expansive soil
conditions The post and beam construction generally results in
lower foundation costs and the individual footings can be
readily adjusted should the need occur Where ultilized con
crete slab on grade should also be properly designed for expan
sive soil conditions
For post and beam construction the individual post footings
should extend a minimum depth of 36 inches below the ground
surface The footings should be poured in place and the
excavation backfilled and compacted to pro vide an impervious
layer over the footing
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f can be used for foot
ings bearing on the stiff natural ground or compacted fill

Surface water should be diverted away from the foundations
Retaining Wall Foundation
Retaining walls about 3 to 6 feet high are planned in the
northwestern portion of the house
In order to reduce the expansive effects of the existing clay
soils on the wall we recommend that the expansive clays should
be overexcavated down for a depth of 2 feet below the bottom of
the footing and then replaced with non expansive borrow soils
such as crusher waste or coral compacted to a minimun of 90
of its maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 The overexcavation
and replacement should extend a minimum distance of 12 inches
beyond the footing width
The wall footing should be embeded a minimum depth of 18 inches
below the outside finished grade Therefore the total depth of
the above overexcavation and replacement would be 3 5 feet
below the outside finished grade
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f can be used

Slab On Grade
Concrete slab on grade is planned for the garage and portions
of the house It appears that the garage area would require
fill of about one foot in order to obtain sufficient level area
for the garage The on site clay soils are expansive and are
unsuitable as slab subgrade soils In order to reduce the
expansive effects of the clay the expansive clays should be
overexcavated down for a depth of 3 feet below the slab sub
grade and then replaced with non expansive borrow soils such
as select borrow crusher waste or coral compacted to a mini
mum of 90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557

A 4 inch layer of base course should be provided below the slab
and compacted to 90 of its maximum dry density In order to
protect against moisture infiltration a polyethylene moisture
barrier may be incorporated below the slab The membrane
should be protected by a thin layer of sand to aid in the
concrete curing
Concrete Driveway and Sidewalks
Expansive clay soils were encountered along the proposed drive
way Since the driveways can be tolerate some amount of
heaving the clay overexcavation and replacement can be reduced
to 2 feet below the driveway subgrade level Where 2 or more
feet of fill is planned select non expansive borrow material
should be used to cap over the adobe clay Where less than 2
feet of fill is planned overexcavation and replacement of the
adobe clay with select non expansive material is required
The above requirements should be followed for a minimum
distance of 3 feet beyond the driveway
Since a relatively long driveway is planned joints are recom
mended at about 8 to 10 foot intervals in order to allow for
some relative movement of the concrete
For future sidewalks capping of the clay or overexcavation and
replacement similar to the driveway is recommended to reduce
heaving caused by the expansive clay soil

Planter Area
A planter area is planned next to the entry foyer The drainage
system for the planter should be designed to provide rapid
drainage of excess landscape water and avoid ponding of water
which could infiltrate below the foundations and slabs
Site Grading
The existing terrain at the site generally slopes down at about
7 grade or flatter
Minor grading is anticipated for most of the area below the
house
The area to be filled should be cleared of vegetation and
debris prior to filling
Fill and backfill should be placed in maximum 8 inch lifts and
compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density estab
lished by ASTM D 1557
The finish grade outside the slabs pavements and foundations
should be shaped to avoid ponding conditions and to shed water
away from the structures

Design Review
Plans for foundations site grading and utility construction
should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and
written comments prior to construction to see that the intent
of our recommendations is included in the project plans and
specifications
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during
earthwork construction particularly the fill placement slope
cuts and to monitor the contractor s grading operations
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be observed
by a soils engineer during construction to see that adequate
bearing has been obtained and that the subsurface conditions
are consistent with the findings of our subsurface
investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive
tested and approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling
to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon
the above inspections
