In accordance with your request we have made a study of the near
surface soil conditions at the above referenced lot in order to provide
foundation recommendations for the proposed residence The study included
three shallow drilled borings laboratory testing of the swelling
potential of the soils and a review of the site grading and preliminary
house plans This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
The proposed residence will be a two story split level wood frame
structure approximately 50 by 100 feet in plan dimensions A two car
garage and open patios or decks are also included

DISCUSSTON AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The medium to stiff brown clayey silt encountered can generally
provide adequate foundation support for the light structural loads from
the proposed house
Based on our field explorations conventional slab on grade and
post and beam construction may be used to support the house
House Foundation
Spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the proposed
residence An allowable bearing value of 2000 p s f may be used on the
natural soil or compacted fill
Individual footings on level areas should be embedded a minimum of
12 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade Continuous wall
footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the adjacent
finished grade
If located on a sloping area the footing should be embedded down an
additional amount to obtain a 6 foot minimm horizontal set back distance
measured from the outer edge of the footing out to the slope face This
additional embedment depth is required to help resist the downhill slope
creep movement and to develop the recommended bearing capacity of the
soil

If a footing is located next to a utility trench the footing should
extend to the bottom of the utility trench or be designed to span over the
trench to reduce footing settlement due to settlement of the trench
backfill
Lot Grading
The fills and backfills within the top 12 inches minimum below the
on grade slabs should consist of non expansive soil such as select
borrow crusher waste or coral Where cut or minimal fill is anticipated
such as the garage driveway and the basement areas the on site soil
should be over excavated to provide a 12 inch minimum thickness of
non expansive select fill capping
All imported fill materials should be tested and approved by the
soils engineer prior to being used in the site
Prior to construction the area to receive any fill should be
cleared of vegetation tree stumps walls and debris
The fills should be placed in horizontal lifts not exceeding 8
inches in compacted thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its
maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78
The site should be graded to provide drainage of surface runoff away
from all foundations
Slab On Grade
Concrete slab on grade is planned for the garage and driveway In
order to provide a barrier against future water infiltration under the
slab we recommend that the slab perimeter footing be embedded a minimum
of 12 inches below the outside finished ground surface

Perimeter footings situated near the top of the slope should be

Low Retaining Walls
It is our understanding that low retaining walls are planned for
portions of the house The guidelines for the retaining wall designs are
listed below
1 For lateral earth pressures the following can be used
Walls unrestrained at the top 35 p s f equivalent
active condition level fluid pressure
backfill per foot of depth
Wall restrained at the top 55 p s f equivalent
at rest conditions fluid pressure
level backfill per foot of depth
2 The wall foundations on relatively flat areas should be embedded
a minimum of 18 inches below the outside finished grade
Wall situated on slopes should be embedded an additional amount
to maintain a minimum 6 foot horizontal setback distance
measured from the outside slope face to the edge of the wall
footing

An allowable bearing value of up to 2000 p s f is recommended
for foundations bearing on the medium stiff natural soil
A passive soil resistance of 300 p s f per foot of depth may be
used for the wall footing on level areas The passive pressure
should be ignored for walls on slopes
A base friction factor of 0 4 may also be used to determine the
resistance against sliding of the wall
Backfills behind the retaining wall should consist of granular
non expansive material such as select borrow crusher waster or
3B Fine gravel The top 12 inches of backfill should consist
of non expansive impervious material to minimize infiltration of
runoff
A subsurface drainage system subdrains or weepholes should be
utilized behind the walls Subdrains should be properly
constructed to drain water away from the wall
Lot Drainage
The finish lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings should
be shaped to shed water away from the foundations and to avoid a ponding
condition
Aliso it is advised that gutter water should be diverted far
away from the foundations and walkways Excessive landscape watering near
the foundations and slab should also be avoided
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils

These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance
of the above recommended foundations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of
strength The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled tight
against the wall or slab footing immediately after setting of the concrete
to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and
maintained to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing
foundations
Design Review
Final plans and specifications for foundations site grading and
utility construction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review
and comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the footing excavations and backfill
operation be observed by the soils engineer during construction to see
that adequate bearing has been obtained and that the subsurface
conditions are consistent with the findings of our investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above inspections

Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations

principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all other
