during the site grading foundation excavation pavement and utility construction for the

EARTHWORK AND FOUNDATION PREPARATION
The project site is located on the mauka side of Lunalilo Street between Ward
Avenue and Victoria Street in the Makiki area of Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii
The project development consists of four parcels covering approximately 35 870 square
feet of land area The project involves the construction of an assisted retirement living
community with an L shaped building covering a footprint of approximately 19 000 square
feet The building consists of a lower basement level parking garage with 48 stalls an
upper basement level of receiving and community spaces five levels of dementia care and
assisted living units and a reduced sixth level of assisted living at the top providing a total
of 107 living units
Based on the topography at the project site excavations on the order of up to about
25 feet below the existing ground surface was required for the underground basement
construction Because of the physical limitations along the existing developments and the
depth of excavations a shoring system consisting of soil nail walls with shotcrete facing
was used for the project
The site grading operations foundation excavation pavement and_ utility
construction for the Plaza at Punchbowl project were performed by Jayar
Construction Inc The site grading consisted of the demolition of existing structures and
pavements excavation of the existing ground for the underground parking footing

Following the demolition and clearing operations the existing ground was generally
over excavated to approximately 6 inches below the finish subgrades within the building
limits to provide the 6 inch select borrow layer underneath the basement slabs The
over excavated subgrades and select borrow material were moisture conditioned to above
the optimum moisture content and compacted to at least 95 percent of the maximum dry
density as determined in accordance with American Society of Testing and Material
ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor
Fill and backfill materials required for the project were generally placed in near level
lifts brought to above the optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of 90 or
95 percent of the maximum dry density determined in accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor Due to the tight space behind the basement walls
from the excavation face the excavated on site cinder sands were used as a source of
backfill material The on site cinder sands were generally placed in lifts not exceeding
12 inches in loose thickness moisture conditioned and densified with a vibratory plate
tamper or other types of compaction equipment During the foundation excavation and
preparation soft clayey soils and or loose cinder sands exposed at the design footing
subgrade level were over excavated up to a maximum of about 24 inches thick and
replaced with flowable fill prior to the placement of reinforcing steel and concrete
During the site grading operations samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor
and the test results are summarized on Plate 1 of this report
Field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations on an
as need basis to confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures
and soil conditions were noted A total of 33 field density tests including retests of failed
areas were performed using the nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM Test
Designations D 2922 and D3017 Results of the compaction tests performed are
summarized on Plates 2 1 and 2 2 of this report These tests were performed on the
prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills at the site Test locations
and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes
These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied
by the methods used Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the
specified minimum compaction was obtained
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the earthwork operations during the site grading
foundation excavation pavement and utility construction for the above project observed
and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report were performed in general

LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork may
have been performed without our observation However the level of earthwork
observation and testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some of
the geotechnical risks to the project It should be recognized that there are variations in
the accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the
earthwork construction and materials Our services were performed in accordance with
generally accepted standards of geotechnical engineering practice no warranty is

insurance nor does it guarantee construction of any type The contractor shall retain
