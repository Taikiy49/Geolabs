INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of a soils and foundation
investigation for the proposed Administration and Library
Building and Multi purpose Dining Room at the Red Hill Elemen
tary School The study consisted of subsurface borings labora
tory testing and engineering analyses as outlined in our proposal
dated Based on the results of our engineering
studies conclusions and recommendations pertinent to site
development are presented Following plans were used during
our study 1 Set of plans dated February 1973 for the
Multi purpose Dining room 2 Preschematic plot plan dated
November 1972 for Administration Library Building prepared by
Dennis T Toyomura A I A
PROJECT DESCRIPTION
The property is located at the Red Hill Elementary School at
Moanalua The building sites appear to be graded cut in past
and its locations are indicated on the attached site plan Pilate 1
The site drains towards the makai Site for the Multi purpose
Dining Room is about 7 feet higher than the site for the Adminis
tration and Library building The site is covered with lawn grass
The Multi purpose Dining Room will be one story high steel
frame structure with masonary walls and the maximum column loads

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The bedrock of the Red Hill area is composed of Koolau volcanic
series Basalt This group of rocks makes up the bulk of the
Koolau Range of Oahu and has an exposed thickness of approximately
3000 feet These lavas were erupted from three rift zones in
the sea floor during Pliocene time approximately 2 to 3 million
years ago Subsequent weathering extending into recent times
has produced a residual weathering crust on the top of these
flows The weathering oxidation hydrolyzation of the ferro
Magnesian iron and manganese bearing minerals of the basalts
has produced a characteristic red iron oxide rust stain in
the residual soils hence the name Red Hill

Soils
There are at least three basic soil subdivisions of a continuous
sequence from slightly weathered basalt bedrock to a residual
soil silty clay to clayey SILT developed on and from the bed
rock at the proposed building site
a Silty CLAY to Clayey SILT Residual SOIL
The red and gray mottled silty clay to clayey SILT overlies
weathered bedrock basalt to depths ranging from approxi
mately 14 feet to 27 feet
b Weathered BASALT
The hard multicolored mottled sandy clayey silty
material encountered in the borings is highly weathered
fractured basalt It averages 4 feet in thickness and
directly overlies the basalt bedrock
c BASALT Bedrock
Hard gray fractured BASALT Was encountered at depths
ranging from 18 feet to 28 feet from the surface in the
boreholes and it underlies the entire site

Based on the results of the field investigations laboratory
testings engineering analyses and presently available informa

A General
The subsurface conditions encountered during the investiga
tion were relatively good The proposed development is
feasible from an engineering standpoint provided the recom
mendations included in this report are followed
It is our professional opinion that the onsite silty clay
in general is capable of supporting the proposed fill and
structures However the silty clay is moderately expansive
which would require special provisions

2 Preparation of Ground
If any local soft spots are encountered in areas to
receive fill they should be removed and replaced with
compacted structural fill Areas to receive fill should
be scarified to a depth of 9 inches and should be watered
and recompacted according to the requirements for struc
tural fills
3 Fill Material
The onsite surface layer of silty clay soil can be
utilized for compacted structural fills
Imported fill material should be tested and approved by
the soils engineer prior to importation Imported fill
shall be of a granular nonexpansive nature meeting the
following specifications
a Plasticity Index less than 10
b Less than 5 by weight passing the 200 sieve
c Maximum particle size 6 inches but not less
than 90 passing the 3 4 sieve
d Percent expansion less than 4 under a sur
charge load of 60 pounds per square foot

Expansive Soil
Based on the following evidence onsite clayey soil is
classified as moderately expansive a High plasticity
Index b Fine grained particles c High shrinkage in
one of the expansion tests d Observation of cracks on
dry ground and slabs on grade in some of the existing
buildings Hence following provisions for expansive
soils are recommended
All footings should be at least 1 5 feet deep from the
finish grade Continuous footings should be reinforced
with at least two 4 bars one at the top and one at the
bottom 3 above soil of the footing Five feet strip
of ground surrounding the buildings should be covered with
concrete slab Provisions for concrete slabs on grade are
given under item F

Concrete Floor Slab On Grade
The surface one foot of soil in the building pad should be
scarified watered and compacted to a minimum of 90 of
the maximum dry density A moisture barrier consisting of
plastic membrane should be provided under the slab areas
A 4 thick layer of fine gravel should be provided between
the plastic membrane and the finish grade The concrete
floor slab on grade should be a minimum of 4 nominal thick
and be reinforced with a minimum of 6 x 6 10 x 10 welded
wire mesh The slab reinforcement should be extended
through the footings

Laboratory Testing
LABORATORY TESTING
A laboratory testing program was developed to establish the
engineering properties of the soils and rocks encountered at
the project site The tests where applicable to the subject
project are included in this Appendix
1 Classification Tests
a
b
c
Visual Classification All soil samples obtained from
the borings were brought to our laboratory where they
were visually reclassified to confirm or modify the
field classification prior to finalizing the Logs of
Borings
Water Content Determinations In addition to visual
classification typical samples were tested for natural
water content as an aid in soil classification and in
evaluating soil properties The water content values
are based upon the dry weight of the soil
Atterberg Limits Atterberg limit tests were performed
on selected clay and silt samples obtained from the
borings for the purposes of identification and correla
tion of soils Standard procedures ASTM D 424 for the
plastic limit and ASTM D 423 for the liquid limit were
used in the performance of these tests
GQGeataAMe as Awa tara
2
d Grain Size Analysis Sieve analysis were performed on
granular soils For fine grained soils the hydrometer
test was performed to determine the distribution of
the grain sizes beyond the No 200 sieve
Shear Strength Tests
a Unconfined Compression Tests Intact core and split
spoon soil samples which apparently exhibited high
plasticity were tested for unconfined strength Length
to diameter ratio of 2 was maintained throughout the
tests to avoid scatter of test results and to facilitate
evaluation of shear strength values for design purposes
b Direct Shear Test In general soil samples were tested
for shear strength using a 2 41 I D direct shear box
Each set consisted of at least two tests each test run
under different normal loads The results are plotted
as Normal Stress vs Shear Strength with a failure envelope
drawn to determine values of cohesion c and angle of
frictional resistance 9
Consolidation Test Consolidation tests were performed on
selected undisturbed ring samples of the compressible
soils to provide basic data for making settlement calculations
Porous stones were placed on the top and bottom of the samples
to allow drainage Vertical loads were applied in increments
with each load increment being allowed to consolidate prior
2
to adding the next increment Measurements of the time
and consolidation were obtained during each load increment
and rebound was measured during the unloading portion
Consolidation test results are plotted in terms of percent
settlement versus applied loads
Swell Test The ring sample was placed between porous
stones and a 60 psf load was applied to the sample for a
24 hour period After the sample had shrunk it was inundated
and allowed to swell The difference between the initial
and final sample heights is the amount of swell which is
expressed as a percentage of the initial height of the
sample
Maximum Density Test Samples of soils are compacted in a
mold of given size with a 10 pound rammer dropped 18 inches
to determine the relationship between moisture content and
density of the soil At least three trial points at dif
ferent moisture content are run to determine the maximum
density and optimum moisture content The test is run in
accordance with ASTM D 1557 70
