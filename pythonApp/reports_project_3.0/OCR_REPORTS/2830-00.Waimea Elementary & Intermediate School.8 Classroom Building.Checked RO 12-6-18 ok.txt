INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
at the Waimea Elementary and Intermediate Schoo campus on the island of Hawaii The
general location of the project site is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings resulting from our exploration and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analysis based on the data
obtained from the field exploration and laboratory testing These recommendations are
intended for design input and are not intended for construction estimation or bidding
purposes

Presently the project site is a relatively flat open grass field Portable classroom

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
The project site is underlain by basaltic lava flows of the Hamakua Volcanic Series
of Mauna Kea which were deposited during the Pleistocene Epoch Stearns and
Macdonald 1946
The Hamakua series is typically composed of basaltic lava flows which are mantled
by a surface layer of palagonitized ash locally referred to as Pahala Ash The ash soil
at the project site differs from the Pahala Ash found in other locations on the island of
Hawaii in that the in situ moisture content is generally lower and the soil exists in a friable
state
Based on previous experience in the Waimea area the lava flows underlying the
site are of the a a form which is characterized by a rough spiny or rubbly surface of
fragmental clinker underlain by a dense core of massive basalt which frequently has a
thinner less continuous layer of clinker below the massive core The clinker surface of
the lavas at the site have been mantled by the Pahala Ash as discussed above

Details of the site grading for proposed classroom building project were not
available at the time we prepared this report However only minor site grading work is
anticipated The recommendations given in this section are intended to give guidelines
for site preparation and earthwork design
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
cleared of vegetation debris rubbish old pavement and other deleterious materials
These materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site
To reduce the amount of settlement from the anticipated column loads we
recommend that at least twenty four 24 inches of compacted select non expansive

Slabs On Grade
Concrete slab on grade construction is planned for the lower floor area of the
proposed classroom building
Based on the anticipated soil conditions as disclosed by our field borings it
appears that the surface ash deposit will be encountered at the finished grade elevation
Laboratory testing indicates that this material has a moderate swelling potential and high
in situ moisture contents To minimize the potential of distresses resulting from swelling
of the subgrade soils and to aid in developing a capillary break we recommend that a
twelve 12 inch cushion layer composed of 3B Fine gravel or ASTM C 33 No 67 be
installed below the slabs In order to protect against future moisture infiltration an
impervious moisture barrier may be incorporated below the slab
The thickened edges of the slabs should be embedded a minimum of eighteen 18
inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling eight 8 borings to
depths of 5 0 to 25 0 feet below the existing ground surface at the approximate locations
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were drilled by a truck mounted rotary drill
rig advancing continuous flight augers or rotary and core drilling toois as appropriate
The materials encountered in the borings were examined and classified in general
accordance with ASTM Standard Practice D 2488 84 Description and Identification of
Soils Visual Manual Procedure by a geologist from our firm The logs of the borings
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Standard Method D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils
sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the boring logs at the appropriate sample
depths
Core samples of soil and rock materials encountered at the site were obtained
using diamond core drilling techniques in general accordance with ASTM Standard
Practice D 2113 83 Diamond Core Drilling for Site Investigation Core drilling is a rotary
drilling method which uses a hollow bit to cut into the rock formation The rock material
left in the hollow stem of the bit is mechanically recovered for examination classification
and testing
Recovery REC is used as a subjective guide to interpretation of the relative quality
of rock masses Recovery is defined as the actual length of material recovered from a
coring attempt vs the length of the core attempt e g if 3 7 feet of material is recovered
from a 5 0 foot core run the recovery would be 74 percent and would be shown on the
Boring Logs as REC 74
The Rock Quality Designation RQD is also a subjective guide to the relative
quality of rock masses This designation is only applicable to cores retrieved from in situ
rock formations RQD is defined as the percentage of the core run that is sound material
in excess of four 4 inches in length without any discontinuities discounting any drilling
induced fractures or breaks if 2 5 feet of sound material is recovered from a 5 0 foot core
run the RQD would be 50 percent and would be shown on the Boring Logs as RQD
50 Generally the following is used to describe the relative quality based on the
Practical Handbcok of Physical Properties of Rocks and Minerals

APPENDIX C
Laboratory Testing
Laboratory testing was performed on selected samples obtained during our field
exploration A laboratory testing program was developed to identify appropriate tests to
aid in determining pertinent engineering properties of the soil and or rock materials
sampled
Classification Tests
Frequently the classification of a material provides a correlative index to certain
engineering properties of the material
Moisture Content ASTM Test Method D 2216 80 and Unit Weight Determinations
are used as an aid to soil classification and to assist in determination of certain
engineering properties The moisture content expressed as a percentage of the dry
weight of the tested sample is shown at the appropriate sample depths on the Boring
Logs
A Sieve Analysis test ASTM Test Method D 422 63 was performed on a selected
representative sample to determine its grain size distribution The grain size distribution
is used in classification in accordance with the Unified Soil Classification System and to
provide indices of other engineering properties The result of the test is presented on
Plate C 1
Physical Property Tests
Physical property tests are performed to develop correlative indices of certain
physical parameters of soils These physical parameters may be used in engineering
analyses pertaining to the performance or suitability of a soil
Expansion tests are performed to evaluate the swelling potential of soils under
structural loads These tests aid in evaluating the materials for use as foundation support
and embankment These test results are summarized on Plate C 2
A Consolidation test ASTM Test Method D 2435 80 was performed to evaluate
the consolidation characteristics of the soil The result of this test is presented graphically
on Plate C 3
