PROJECT CONSIDERATION
It is our understanding that the proposed jet fuel tank will be
20 feet in diameter and 24 feet in height of steel shell fixed roof
construction This tank will be located at the southeastern portion
of the site and enclosed with low retention dikes The remaining
enclosed areas are reserved for future tanks The proposed layout of
the site is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Cut of up to 2 feet will be required on the southern side of the site
and dikes of up to feet high will be constructed around the
perimeter The foundation loading under the proposed jet fuel tank
is anticipated to range up to 1500 p s f
It is our understanding that the final elevation of tank bottom
has not been selected yet However the tank pad will probably be not
more than 12 inches above the graded surface inside the diked area
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located at the northern edge of Haleakala
Highway approximately 1 500 feet westerly of the intersection with the
existing Hemaloa Street
At the time of our exploration the proposed tank area was a
relatively flat grassed surface at the foot of the highway fill
Occasional small trees spot the site

SUBSURFACE EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by excavating
and sampling 4 test pits at the approximate locations shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2 The test pits were excavated by a rubber tired
backhoe and exploration depths ranged from 5 feet to 10 feet These
excavations were logged by one of our engineers and the logs are
presented on Plates A 1 and A 2
Selected soil samples were tested in our laboratory to examine
their subgrade support characteristics These tests included one
compaction test one set of Atterberg Limits tests and 4 ring
shrink swell tests together with natural moisture determinations
The results of these tests are presented on Plates B 1 and B 2
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
In general the project site is located on the seaward margin of
the weathered Kula lava flows that are frequently encountered
throughout the rest of the general airport area
The subsurface conditions at the site consisted of a layer of
sandy silt topsoil about 9 inches thick containing mats of fine roots
and other organic material underlain by stiff to very stiff reddish
brown fine silty clay residual soil to a depth of 10 feet the maximm
depth of our test pits

DISCUSSION RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on our findings the site is covered by a layer of topsoil
that is in turn underlain by stiff silty clay residual soil It is
probable that the site may have been previously graded and cultivated
for sugar cane or other agricultural products For the proposed tank
construction the topsoil should first be stripped the exposed on
site silty clay soil scarified and recompacted then a pad of
compacted crushed rock layer placed under the tank for foundation
support
Tank Foundation
It is our understanding that a ring wall will not be used for the
tank shell We believe that the proposed tank shell should be founded
on a pad of aggregate base course with inch maximum size
particle placed over the scarified and recompacted on site soils
The thickness of aggregate base course under the exterior tank shell
should be a minimum of 12 inches extending at least 12 inches inside
the tank shell The thickness of the base course under the interior
bottom plate may be reduced to 6 inches minimum

The tank pad should extend at least 3 feet beyond the tank shell
and its edge may be sloped at horizontal to vertical
An allowable maximum soil bearing pressure of 1700 p s f may be
used on the surface of the compacted base course provided that the
tank bottom plate extends at least 4 inches beyond the outside of the
tank shell A one third increase of the allowable soil bearing
pressures may be permitted for wind or seismic loads
The base course material for the tank pad should be placed in
horizontal lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness The top 6
inches of base course under the tank should be compacted to at least
95 of the ASTM D 1557 78 maximum density The material below top 6
inches may be compacted to at least 90 of the maximum density
The subgrade under the bottom of the tank pad should be graded to
drain toward the edges to avoid accumulating water that may perch at
the bottom of the tank pad
For other individual footings such as thrust blocks or pump
pads the footing should be founded in the undisturbed stiff on site
silty clay and the footing bottom embedded at least 12 inches below
the lowest adjacent grade An allowable soil bearing pressure up to
2500 p s f may be used

90 of the maximum density

Site Preparation
Prior to the commencement of earthwork the site should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed of all vegetation and organic
matter Any tree roots that are 1 5 inches or greater in
diameter should be grubbed Where encountered trash or
rubbish fills or other unsuitable material should be removed to
firm soil
The top 9 inches of organic topsoil should be stripped The
resulting stripped and grubbed material should not be used as
fill The use of the topsoil as the dike material should first
meet the fire safety requirements

Perimeter Earth Berm Fill
The fill material for the perimeter earth berm should consist
of the on site low expansion potential soils free of organic
matter that will form a dense stable soil mass when
compacted The largest particle size should generally be
limited to 8 inches maximum except that occasional oversized
particles of up to 12 inches may be placed in the fill
provided that the particles are Firmly surrounded by
compacted soil
If imported fill materials are required for the dike
construction the qualities of the fill materials should be
evaluated by a soils engineer prior to hauling the material
to the site
The dike fill material should be placed in horizontal lifts
not exceeding 10 inches in loose thickness and compacted to
85 of the ASTM D 1557 78 maximum density
If the material is too wet or too dry it should be dried or
moistened so that the proper compaction can be achieved

Pavement Design
It is our understanding that a short driveway and an apron area
will be provided for loading and unloading the fuel tank trucks It
is anticipated that the subgrade material will consist of the on site
silty clay material Based on our past experience from nearby
projects a minimum CBR value of 7 is assigned to the on site subgrade
soils The following design sections for flexible pavements are
recommended

Design Review
Plans and specifications prepared for tank foundation and
pavement construction should be reviewed by a soils engineer prior to

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the clearing and grubbing tank foundation
fill placement and compaction and subgrade preparation be observed by
the soils engineer during project construction This is to see that
an adequate compaction has been obtained and that the subsurface
conditions are consistent with the findings of our subsurface
investigation If conditions encountered are different from those
presented in this report appropriate modifications to the design
should be made

INVESTIGATION LIMITATIONS
Our services consist of professional opinions and reconmen
dations made in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation
engineering principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all
other warranties either expressed or implied
The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are
based on our site reconnaissance soil information from test pit
excavations and laboratory test data obtained for this investigation
Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encountered and cannot
be fully determined by reconnaissance and test pits These unforeseen
conditions such as pockets of organic soils trash fill or expansive
soils may occur in localized areas Therefore additional
expenditures may be needed during construction to attain a properly
constructed project Some contingency fund is thus recommended to
accommodate these possible costs
This report has been prepared in order to assist the engineers in
the preparation of site grading plans and foundation design of this
project
