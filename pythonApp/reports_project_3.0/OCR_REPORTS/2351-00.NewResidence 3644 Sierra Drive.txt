INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
evaluation performed for the proposed residence at 3644 Sierra Drive in
Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location of the site
and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The
exploration was performed in general accordance with our proposal dated
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2351 00 Page 2
This report summarizes the findings from the exploration and
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analysis based
on the data obtained These recommendations are intended for design input
and are not intended for construction estimation or bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a new residence at 3644 Sierra Drive
Based on the plans provided to us the proposed new residence will be a
two story structure approximately 55 feet by 66 feet in plan dimensions
Concrete on grade slab is planned for part of the first floor level
Minimal site grading involving fills and cuts is anticipated
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface conditions at the project site in order to develop a
generalized soil rock data set for the formulation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertinent to site grading and foundation
design of the project
The work was done in general accordance with our proposal dated
The scope of work was limited to
1 drilling and sampling of three 3 borings to depths ranging from
9 5 to 10 5 feet below the existing ground surface
2 reconnaissance of the project site and its vicinity to make
visual observations of the surficial soil conditions
3 review of available soils and geologic information pertinent to
the project site and its vicinity
4 laboratory testing of selected samples obtained during the field
exploration to determine the engineering properties and
classification of the materials encountered

5 engineering analyses of the field and laboratory data for the
purpose of developing pavement and foundation design
recommendations and
6 preparation of this report presenting the design recommendations
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration and laboratory testing
are presented in the appendices of this report
STTE DESCRIPTION
The subject lot is located at the northern side of Sierra Drive near
the intersection of Sierra Drive and Pualele Place The lot is
trapezoidal shaped with a frontage of about 75 feet and a depth ranging
from 58 to 73 feet
The majority of the lot approximately the front two thirds is
relatively flat and sparsely covered with grass The topography then
drops approximately 5 feet toward the rear property line which is bounded
by a CRM retaining wall varying from 6 to 12 feet high A CRM wall of
approximately 15 feet long and 3 feet high is located about 22 feet away
from the northern property line for grade separation A greenhouse is
located near the right rear corner of the site
At the time of our field exploration it appeared that the project
site served as a backyard and parking area for the existing residence in
front of the subject lot
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling three 3

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The dense weathered basalt formation encountered can generally
provide adequate foundation support for the proposed house However the
uncontrolled miscellaneous fill present near the rear one third of the
subject lot may not be suitable for foundation support Some of the
surface fill may be expansive and may require foundation procedures
Lot Grading
The fills and backfills within the top 24 inches minimum below the
on grade slabs should consist of non expansive soils such as select
borrow crusher waster or coral
All imported fill material should be tested and approved by the
geotechnical engineer prior to transportation to the site
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limits should be cleared of vegetation debris rubbish and other
deleterious materials These materials should be removed and disposed of
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2351 00 Page 5
properly off site Any loose or soft spots encountered at fill areas
should be over excavated down to the firm subsoils and then replaced with
compacted non expansive select material
The fill areas should then be scarified to a depth of six 6 inches
moisture conditioned and recompacted to minimum of 90 of its maximm dry
density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
The fill should be placed in maximum 10 inch thick loose level lifts
and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximm dry density per ASTM Test
Method D 1557 78
Fill placed on existing slopes should start from the bottom up and
should be keyed and benched to bond the new fill to the existing slope
The site should be graded to provide drainage of surface run off away
from all foundations
House Foundations
Spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the proposed
residence An allowable bearing value up to 5000 p s f may be used for
footings bearing on the dense weathered basalt formation
In the lower rear portion of the lot the footings should extend
through the uncontrolled fill and bear on the natural rock formation
Footings located on or near sloping ground should be embedded deeper
to provide an 8 foot minimm horizontal set back distance measuring from
the toe of the footing to sloping ground surface
If a footing is located next to a utility line it should extend to
the bottom of the utility trench to reduce footing settlement due to
settlement of the trench backfill
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2351 00 Page 6
Slab On Grade
It is our understanding that the rear one third of the project site
was filled 5 to 10 feet with miscellaneous fill of concrete block broken
tile and soils over the original steep rock slope It is our
recommendation that wood floor with post and beam construction should be
utilized for this portion of the site to minimize additional fill and
foundation loads on this uncontrolled miscellaneous fill
Concrete on grade slab is proposed for the portion of the site
approximately the front two thirds that is relatively flat and underlain
by dense weathered basalt at shallow depths Laboratory tests performed on
the near surface on site silty clay indicate that this material generally
has high swelling potential depending on the initial moisture content of
the soil prior to subsequent saturation Therefore to reduce the effects
due to soil swelling under on grade slabs we recommend that the on site
silty clay soil encountered at slab subgrade level be capped with at least
24 inches of non expansive select fill and that the subgrade soil
underneath the select fill and slab be moistened and protected from drying
from the start of construction until the slab concrete placement The 24
inches of over excavation can be reduced or eliminated if the exposed
subgrade is consisted of dense weathered basalt
The thickened edge of the slab should be embedded a minimm of 12
inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade for details refer to
Plate 3
A 4 inch layer of 3B Fine gravel cushion fill should be provided
below the slab In order to protect against future moisture infiltration
an impervious moisture barrier may be incorporated below the slab
Concrete Driveway
The subgrade of the driveway should be scarified to a depth of six
6 inches moisture conditioned and recompacted to a minimum of 90 of
its maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 The subgrade
W O 2351 00 Page 7
soils should also be maintained in a moist condition and prevented from
drying out prior to concrete placement
Joints are recommended in the driveway slab at about 8 to 10 foot
intervals and at discontinuities in order to allow for some relative
movement of the concrete
Retaining Walls
Retaining walls may be used to provide grade separation for various
portions of the house The guidelines for design of retaining walls are
as follows
1
For lateral earth pressures the following values may be used
Walls unrestrained at the top 35 p s f equivalent
active condition level fluid pressure
backfill per foot of depth
Walls restrained at the top 55 p s f equivalent
at rest condition fluid pressure
level backfill per foot of depth
An allowable bearing capacity up to 5000 p s f may be used for
retaining wall foundations bearing on the in situ dense weathered
basalt
A passive earth pressure resistance of 300 p s f per foot of
depth may be used for wall footings on level areas The passive
pressure must be reduced for walls located on slopes
A base friction factor of 0 4 may also be used to determine the
resistance of the wall against sliding
The wall foundations on relatively flat areas should be embedded
a minimum of 12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade For
sloping ground the footing should be embedded deeper to obtain a

minimum 8 foot set back distance from the outside edge of the
footing measure horizontally to the slope face
6 A subsurface drainage system subdrains and or weepholes should
be utilized behind the walls to minimize the build up of
hydrostatic pressure behind the walls Subdrains should be
constructed properly to drain water away from the walls
7 Select granular material e g select borrow or base course is
recommended as backfill behind the retaining walls to reduce the
amount of compactive effort required The upper two 2 feet of
backfill should be an impervious non expansive material to reduce
the infiltration of surface water behind the walls
8 Care should be taken in the siting of proposed walls and proposed
foundations for structural elements above behind the walls to
avoid imposing an excessive surcharge on the walls due to the
above foundation loads
Drainage
The finish lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings should
be shaped to shed water away from foundations and to avoid ponding
conditions Also it is advised that gutter systems be installed around
the house and that gutter water should be diverted far away from the
foundations and driveway Excessive landscape watering near foundations
and slab should also be avoided
Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the foundation subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance
of the above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2351 00 Page 9
strength The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled tight
against the wall or slab footing inmediately after setting of the concrete
to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and maintained
to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundations
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should he
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comments
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this review
is not made the geotechnical engineer can not assume responsibility for
misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the
foundation phases of the work This is to observe compliance with the
design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow design
changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from that
anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in
this report are contingent upon such observations
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
upon information obtained from the borings Variations of conditions
between the borings may occur and the nature and extent of these
variations may not become evident until construction If variations then
appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations
given in this report

The locations of the borings were determined by taping from
structures indicated on the topographic survey Elevations of the borings
were approximately determined by interpolation from elevation points on
the topographic survey map The physical location and elevation of the
borings should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the
method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
boring points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types and
as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross sections are based upon interpolation between borings
and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
Water level data if encountered from the boring points were
collected at the times given on the graphic representations and or in the
text of this report These data have been reviewed and interpretations
made in the formulation of this report However it must be noted that
fluctuation may occur due to variations in rainfall tides temperature
and other factors

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and
sampling three 3 borings to depths of 9 5 to 10 5 feet below the
existing ground surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site
Plan Plate 2 A truck mounted drill rig was used to drill the borings
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by visual and
textural examination in the field by our engineer who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory All soils
were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A summary of the materials encountered are
presented in the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 3
Soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving a 2 inch
standard split spoon sampler with a 140 pound hammer free falling 30
inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the last 12 inches are shown
on the Boring Logs at the appropriate sample depths
