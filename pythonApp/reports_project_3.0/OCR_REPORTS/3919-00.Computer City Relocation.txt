INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the Computer City Relocation project to be located at the northwest corner
of the intersection between Auahi and Kamakee Streets in Honolulu on the island of
Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings from our field exploration and laboratory
testing and presents our geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our
analyses for the proposed project These recommendations are intended for design of
foundations site grading and pavements only and are subject to the limitations noted at
the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located at the northwest corner of the intersection between
Auahi and Kamakee Streets in Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii as shown on
the Project Location Map Plate 1 Based on the information provided by the project
architect it is proposed to relocate the existing one story Computer City retail store
located at the northeast corner of the same intersection by constructing a new
Computer City retail store across Kamakee Street at the project site which is currently
being used as an existing warehouse complex
We understand that the new retail store will involve partial renovation of two
existing warehouses and in filling the open space between the warehouses with
construction of a new roof structure and concrete floor The existing warehouse
structures were observed to be of steel frame construction with interior concrete floors
Based on the proposed construction the new Computer City store will also be of steel
frame construction similar to the existing warehouse structures at the site with concrete
slabs on grade We anticipate minimal site grading on the order of less than 2 feet of
new fill will be required to attain the design finish floor elevation for the proposed new
GEOLABS HAWAI

Computer City store In addition we understand that a portion of another warehouse will
be demolished to create a parking area
Based on our experience in the vicinity of the project site we anticipate that the
subsoil conditions at the proposed new Computer City site may consist of surficial fill
materials overlying lagoonal deposits The relative density of the sandy lagoonal deposits
is generally loose to medium dense and extends to depths of about 20 to 25 feet below
the existing ground level Therefore special attention was given to the foundation
support characteristics of the near surface fill materials and the depths to the loose
lagoonal deposits at the site in deriving the recommended bearing pressures and
estimated settlements

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lavas from two
shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be
middle to late Pliocene in age and Koolau is estimated to be late Pliocene to early
Pleistocene in age The project site is located on the southern flank of the Koolau Range
and its geomorphology and subsurface conditions are directly related to the
glacio eustatic fluctuations of sea level during the Pleistocene Epoch Ice Age and the
genesis of the Honolulu Coastal Plain The subsurface conditions in the Ala Moana area
are dominated by fossil Pleistocene Age coral algal reef deposits and related deposits
which are intercalated with terrigenous sediments laid during high stands of the sea The
base of the stratigraphic section in this area is Koolau Basalt
In the last 10 000 years or so the sea level has adjusted to its present stand
Terrigenous and marine sediments have continued to accumulate in low energy estuarine
or lagoonal environments resulting in thick deposits of soft ioose sediments along the
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3919 00 Page 5
coast and in areas that were formerly valleys and drainageways Land development and
reclamation projects within the last 100 years have brought the project area to its present
form
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located at the northwest corner of the intersection of Auahi and
Kamakee Streets in the Ala Moana area of Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii It is
bounded to the northeast by Queen Street to the southeast by Kamakee Street and to
the southwest and northwest by an existing warehouse complex as shown on the Site
Plan Plate 2
Based on our field observations and a review of the Topographic Survey Map by
ControlPoint Surveying Inc dated the proposed site is relatively
level with existing ground surface elevations between approximately 3 6 and 5 feet
Mean Sea Level MSL Currently the project site is occupied by a warehouse complex
consisting of six steel frame warehouses with slabs on grade Three of the six
warehouses are located parallel to Kamakee Street and are separated by paved
driveways and parking areas of approximately 60 feet in width between them At the time
of our field exploration the warehouses were occupied with small businesses including
various retailers and service companies

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field exploration the subsurface conditions at the project site
generally consist of near surface medium dense sands underlain by sandy and silty
gravels coral detritus extending to the maximum depths explored of about 21 to 25 feet
below the existing ground surface Based on the subsurface conditions encountered in
our borings and the anticipated structural loads we believe that a shallow foundation
system consisting of spread and or continuous footings may be used to support the
proposed new Computer City retail store
In general the new footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below
the lowest adjacent finish grades New footings to be located adjacent to the existing
structures should be lowered to the same depths as the existing building footings to
GEOLABS HAWAI

avoid surcharging the existing foundations The actual depths of the existing footings
should be confirmed in the field during construction
Groundwater was encountered in our borings ranging in elevations from about
1 0 to 0 1 feet MSL Therefore dewatering procedures should be considered if the
new and or existing foundations are located below the groundwater level Detailed
discussions of these items and other geotechnical aspects of the project including our
geotechnical engineering recommendations are presented in the following sections of this
report
Foundations
Based on the information provided we anticipate that the finish floor elevation of
the new Computer City retail store will be at an elevation of 4 78 feet MSL The existing
floor elevations of the warehouses to be renovated are at 4 78 and 4 61 feet MSL
Based on the relatively dense near surface soil conditions encountered at the project site
we believe that shallow spread and or continuous footings may be used for support of the
proposed new Computer City retail store An allowable bearing pressure of up to
2 500 pounds per square foot p s f may be used for the design of foundations bearing
on the recompacted on site soils This bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may
be increased by one third for transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic
forces Spread and continuous footings should be designed with a minimum width of
18 and 12 inches respectively
In general footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest
adjacent finish grades New footings to be located adjacent to the existing structures
should be lowered to the same depths as the existing building footings or slightly deeper
to avoid surcharging the existing foundations In addition the new footings should not
extend deeper than the existing footings by more than about 6 inches to avoid
undermining the existing footings The actual depths of the existing footings should be
confirmed in the field during construction If foundations are located next to utility
GEOCLABS HAWAI

trenches or easements they should be embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical
1H 1V imaginary plane extending upward from the bottom edge of the utility trench or as
deep as the inverts of the utility lines This requirement is necessary to avoid surcharging
adjacent below grade structures with additional structural loads and to reduce the
potential for foundation settlement
We estimate that foundation settlements under the anticipated design loads for
footings bearing on the recompacted near surface soils as recommended herein to be
less than 1 inch total with differential settlements on the order of inch
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by frictional resistance
developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil and by passive
earth pressure acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system
A coefficient of friction of 0 35 may be used for footings bearing on the recompacted
near surface granular fill soils Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be
estimated using an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of
This assumes that the soil around footings is relatively undisturbed or well compacted
Unless covered by pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the upper 12 inches of
soil should be neglected
Footing subgrades should be compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction prior to placement of reinforcing steel and concrete In addition the footing
subgrade should be firm and unyielding Relative compaction refers to the in place dry
density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil
established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 91 test procedures
As previously indicated some soft soils were encountered in Boring Nos 2 and 3
at a depth of about 3 feet below the ground surface Where soft and or loose materials
are encountered at the bottom of the footing excavations they should be over excavated

weil graded from coarse to fine with no particles greater than 3 inches in largest

In addition due to the relatively shallow groundwater conditions encountered at
the site if the foundation is located below the groundwater table dewatering procedures
should be considered Where dewatering is needed for construction of the proposed

contractor The dewatering method selected should have minimal impact on the

provide safe and dry working conditions in the excavation In addition the dewatering
method should be sufficiently flexible to allow modifications to accommodate various

Slabs On Grade
Concrete slabs on grade will be used for the proposed new Computer City retail
store and will be supported by new fills needed to raise the existing ground to the finish
subgrade elevation In general the asphalt concrete pavement on which the new fills
will be placed should be removed Following removal of the asphalt concrete the
exposed subgrade should be scarified to a depth of about 8 inches moisture
conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and recompacted to a minimum of
90 percent relative compaction We envision that some fills will need to be placed to
achieve the design subgrade elevation Fills should be placed in general accordance
with the recommendations provided in the Site Grading section of this report
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3919 00 Page 10
For more uniform support of the interior slabs we recommend that the concrete
slabs on grade be supported on a minimum 4 inch cushion fill layer The cushion fill layer
should consist of open graded gravels such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33
No 67 gradation which would also serve as a capillary moisture break The slab
cushion fill should be placed as soon as possible after the scarification and compaction of
the subgrade soils
For slabs on grade with moisture sensitive surfacing we recommend that an
impermeable membrane be placed over the cushion fill to reduce the potential for
migration of moisture vapor through the concrete slab Two inches of moist fine sand
may be considered on top of the plastic membrane to provide protection of the membrane
and to aid in curing of the slab concrete Flexible floor coverings such as carpet or sheet
vinyl should be considered since they can better mask minor slab cracking
Slab subgrades should be kept moist prior to placement of cushion fill and or
concrete Where shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of the subgrade
we recommend that the soils be moistened to close all cracks or scarified
moisture conditioned and recompacted Saturation and subsequent yielding of the
exposed subgrade due to inclement weather and poor drainage may require
over excavation of the soft areas and replacement with well compacted engineered fill at
no additional cost to the Owner
Exterior concrete walkways may be required for the proposed project We
recommend that concrete walkways be supported on 6 inches of select borrow subbase
material as specified in Hawaii Standard Specifications Subsection 703 17 The select
borrow subbase material should be compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction The subgrade soils underneath the sidewalk slabs should also be prepared
in accordance with the recommendations presented in the Site Grading section
Contraction joints should be provided at intervals equal to the width of the walkways with
expansion joints at right angle intersections

The area adjacent to the slabs should be backfilled tightly against the slab with
non expansive impervious soil and graded to divert water away from the slab to reduce
the potential for water ponding around the slab and foundation
Site Grading
Based on the existing topography and the proposed finish grades minimal site
grading on the order of less than 2 feet of fill will be required to attain the design finish
subgrade elevation for the proposed new Computer City retail store Site grading
operations should be observed by a representative of Geolabs Hawaii It is important that
a representative from our office observe whether the exposed soil conditions during
excavation are similar to those encountered in our exploration The following
recommendations are intended to provide guidelines for the design of site grading work
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared All debris rubbish and other unsuitable materials should be removed
and disposed of off site In addition we recommend that the existing asphalt concrete
pavement be removed in order to facilitate placement of fills to achieve the finish
subgrade elevation Slabs and foundations of the existing structures that are to be
demolished should be completely removed Soft and yielding areas encountered during
clearing and demolition should be over excavated to expose firm natural material
Over excavation resulting from the clearing and demolition should be backfilled with
compacted fill material Existing utilities to be abandoned should be removed and the
resulting excavation should be properly backfilled Utilities that are to be abandoned
in place under the proposed structure should be backfilled by pumping lean concrete
under low pressure
After demolition and clearing finish subgrades or areas to receive fills should be
scarified to a depth of about 8 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum
moisture content and compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction In
areas subjected to vehicular traffic the compaction requirement should be increased to a
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 3919 00 i Page 12
minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place
dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same
soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 91 test procedures Optimum moisture
is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry
density
The finish subgrade should be kept moist until covered by cushion material base
course and or slabs If shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of the subgrade we
recommend that the subgrade soil be moisture conditioned and recompacted as
recommended above to close all cracks In addition saturation and subsequent yielding
of the exposed subgrade due to inclement weather and poor drainage may require
over excavation of the soft areas and replacement with well compacted engineered fill at
no additional cost to the Owner
The on site sandy soils generated from the excavation may be reused as a
source of fill and backfill material provided that they are well graded from coarse to fine
with no particles greater than 3 inches in largest dimension and free of deleterious
materials Clayey and silty soils resulting from the on site excavation should not be
re used as a source of fill material In general materials to be used as fill should be
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture placed in level lifts not exceeding
8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction In areas subjected to vehicular traffic the compaction requirement should
be increased to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
If imported fill materials are required the imported material should consist of
non expansive select fill such as crushed coral mudrock basalt or cinder sand The
material should be well graded from coarse to fine with no particles larger than 3 inches in
largest dimension and should contain between 10 and 30 percent particles passing the
No 200 sieve The material should have a laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR
value of 20 or more and should have a maximum swell of 1 percent or less Imported fill

material should be tested by Geolabs Hawaii for conformance with these
recommendations prior to delivery to the project site for its intended use imported
materials should be brought to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to
not less than 90 percent relative compaction
Pavements
New flexible pavements are planned for the driveways and parking areas for the
proposed project We anticipate that the vehicle loading for these areas of the project will
consist primarily of passenger vehicles light pick up trucks and occasional heavy trucks
In addition rigid pavements are planned for the loading area where vehicle loading will
consist of heavy trucks We have made our preliminary pavement design assuming the
pavement subgrade soil will be similar to the silty and gravely soils encountered in our
borings with a minimum CBR value of 20 and swell of less than 1 percent Based on the
above assumptions we recommend that the following pavement sections be used for
preliminary design purposes
Flexible Pavement
2 0 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 0 Inch Basalt Base Course 95 Percent Relative Compaction
8 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness on Compacted Subgrade
Rigid Pavement
6 0 inch Portland Cement Concrete
6 0 Inch Select Borrow Subbase 95 Percent Relative Compaction
12 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness on Compacted Subgrade
The pavement subgrade soils should be scarified to a depth of about 8 inches
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to not less
than 95 percent relative compaction The subbase and base course material should meet
the requirements of the Hawaii Standard Specifications Subsection 703 17 and 703 06
respectively and should be compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction

Laboratory CBR tests should be performed on samples of the actual subgrade
soils encountered during construction to confirm the recommended design sections If
the results of the laboratory CBR tests performed on actual subgrade soils are different
than assumed above the recommended pavement sections should be revised to reflect
the actual subgrade soil conditions The recommended pavement sections assume that
good drainage will be provided for areas adjacent to pavements
Paved areas should be sloped and drainage gradients maintained to carry surface
water off the site Surface water ponding should not be allowed anywhere on the site
during or after construction Where concrete curbs are used to isolate landscaping in or
adjacent to the pavement areas we recommend that the curbs extend a minimum of
2 inches into the subgrade below the aggregate base course to reduce migration of
landscape water into the pavement section Alternatively a subdrain system could be
constructed to collect excessive water from landscaping irrigation For long term
performance we recommend a_ subdrain system be constructed adjacent to
paved landscape areas
Utility Trench
We envision that new utility lines may be required for the proposed project and
some of the utility trenches may extend below the groundwater table In general a
granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel
No 67 gradation is recommended under the pipes If loose soft soils are encountered at
or near the invert of the pipes an additional 18 inches of No 3B Fine gravel wrapped in a
filter fabric Mirafi 180N or equivalent should be provided below the bedding layer for
more uniform support Free draining granular materials such as No 3B Fine gravel
should also be used for the trench backfill up to about 12 inches above the pipes or about
12 inches above the groundwater level to provide adequate support around the pipes and
to reduce the compaction effort of the backfill thus reducing the possibility of damaging
the pipes It is critical that the free draining material be used to reduce the potential for
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 3919 00 Page 15
formation of voids below the haunches of pipes and to provide adequate support around
the sides of the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipes or
groundwater level to the top of the subgrade may consist of the excavated on site soils
The backfill should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture placed in
maximum 8 inch level loose lifts and mechanically compacted to not less than 90 percent
relative compaction to reduce the potential for future ground subsidence Where trenches
will be located below paved areas the upper 3 feet of the trench backfill below the
pavement grade should be compacted to not less than 95 percent relative compaction

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling six
borings extending to depths ranging from 6 5 to 25 3 feet below the existing ground
surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were
drilled using a trailer mounted drill rig equipped with hollow stem augers
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our Engineer who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by
testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified
Soil Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representations of the materials
encountered are presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 6
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils
by driving a 3 inch O D split barrel sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches
The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the last 12 inches of an 18 inch drive are
shown on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content ASTM D 2216 and unit weight determinations were performed
on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties
The results of these tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample
depths
Two sieve analysis tests were performed on selected samples of the soils to
evaluate the gradation characteristics and to aid in soil classification The tests were
performed in accordance with ASTM Test Designation C 136 Graphic representation of
the test results is provided on Plate B 1
One direct shear test ASTM D 3080 was performed on a selected sample of the
soils to evaluate the shear strength characteristics Results of the direct shear test are
presented on Plate B 2
One laboratory California Bearing Ratio Test ASTM D 1883 was performed on
the near surface soils to evaluate the pavement support characteristics of the soils
Results of the test are presented on Plate B 3
