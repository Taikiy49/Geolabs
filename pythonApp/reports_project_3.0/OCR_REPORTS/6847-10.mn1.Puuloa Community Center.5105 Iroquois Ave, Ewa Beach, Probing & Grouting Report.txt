Dear Mr. Tschudin: 
This letter report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations performed 
during the foundation probing and grouting operations for the Puuloa Community Center 
project conducted from July 9 to July 10, 2015. 
Because of potential subsurface cavities and/or voids within the underlying coral 
formation, a probing and grouting program to locate and grout potential cavities beneath 
foundations was specified for the project. The probe hole locations were surveyed and 
marked in the field by the general contractor (Allied Builders System) prior to the probing 
operations. The probe holes were generally located at the center of the footings which are 
spaced from about 9.33, 10 and 14 feet on centers. Probing and grouting were conducted 
at the finished subgrade of the building pad generally at an elevation of about + 5.5 Mean 
Sea Level (MSL). 
A total of 59 probe holes corresponding to the number of spread footings for the 
building were drilled and grouted. The approximate locations of the probe holes are 
marked and identified by the grid line intersections as shown on the Probe and Groput 
Location Plan, Plate 1. 
The probing and grouting work for the project was performed by Pacific Ground 
Systems.  The probe holes were drilled using a Komatsu backhoe track rig platform fitted 
with a TEI hydraulic percussion drill equipped with a 3-inch diameter drill bit with grout 
injection portals connected to a hollow core threaded bar. The probe holes drilled were

about 4 inches in diameter and extended from 12.5 to 14 feet below the existing ground
surface. A summary of our foundation probing and grouting observations is provided on

The materials encountered during the probe drilling operations generally consisted
of coral formation. After completion of the probe drilling operations, the probe holes were
filled with a water cement grout mix. A cement grout with water to cement ratio of about 1
was mixed and pumped into the probe holes with an Oberman VS63 grout plant.
The grout intake for the probe holes ranged from a low of about 2 ft.° to a high of
10 ft.2 However, the grout head in the probe holes generally stabilized during the second
grout application when the holes were topped off, and therefore, additional probe holes
were not recommended.
In summary, the foundation probing and grouting operations for the project have
been conducted in general conformance with the intent of the project requirements.
Based on our observations, it is our professional opinion that the relatively small voids
encountered at the foundation locations have been grouted and that the subgrade area
should provide the recommended support for the Puuloa Community Center building
foundations.

questions or need additional information, please contact our office.
