PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Makaha Ocean View Estates Development Makaha Development is a
13 53 acre 76 Lot Single Family residential subdivision located along Kili Drive in
Makaha Oahu Hawaii as shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
Our monitoring services consisted of field observations including surface probing
and field moisture and density testing to determine the adequacy of compaction and
competency of prepared subgrade fills and backfills within the limits of grading as shown
on the Site Plan Plate 2
This report summarizes the results of our field observations and compaction
testing services for the mass grading utilities and infrastructure installation and site
grading operations for the project

MASS GRADING
The mass grading of Makaha Oceanview Development consisted of clearing and
grubbing subgrade preparations cut and fill operation with cuts of up to 5 feet and fills
up to 14 feet The mass grading also included the removal and stockpiling of a
significant amount of cobbles and boulders encountered on site On site cut clayey silt
and silty clays with sand and gravel were used as general fill to raise the low lying
portions of the subdivision to design grade
The fill materials were moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture content
placed and spread in level loose lifts not exceeding 10 inches in loose thickness and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as determined in
accordance with ASTM D1557 Samples of materials utilized as fill were obtained and
transported to our laboratory for testing The maximum dry densities and optimum
moisture contents were established in general accordance with ASTM D1557 Modified
Proctor The test results are summarized on Plate 3
Periodic field moisture content and density tests were performed on compacted
subgrade and fills in general accordance with ASTM D6938 Nuclear Gauge Method
The test results are summarized on Plates 4 1 through 4 25 Test locations and
elevations were referenced in the field from on site survey stakes established by the
contractor s grade setter These locations and elevations should be considered accurate
only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas indicating unsatisfactory
compaction test results were reworked until the measured relative compaction
conformed to or exceeded the minimum specified for the project
SITE GRADING
A rock crushing operation was undertaken on site to make aggregates from the
basaltic boulders encountered during clearing and mass grading The crusher run
aggregates were used on site as a non expansive select borrow subbase for the
subdivision roads driveways and house pad construction
During the site grading and utility backfilling operations the maximum dry densities
and optimum moisture contents of backfill and subbase materials were established in
general accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test results are included
on Plate 3
Periodic field density tests were performed in general accordance with
ASTM D 6938 Nuclear Gauge Method The test results are summarized on Plates 4 1
through 4 25 These tests were performed on prepared over excavated surfaces and on
constructed fills and backfills Test locations and elevations were estimated by referencing
from on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should be considered accurate
only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were
reworked until the measured relative compaction conformed to or exceeded the minimum
specified for the project

HOUSE CONSTRUCTION CONSIDERATIONS
The finished grades adjacent to the structures should be sloped to shed water
away from the foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also advised to
install gutter systems around the house structures to collect and discharge water away
from the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering within 3 feet of
the foundations and slabs should be avoided Planters next to foundations should be
avoided or have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the
subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
building foundations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation and
subsequent loss of strength of the near surface soils The foundation excavations
should be properly backfilled against the footings immediately after setting of the
concrete to reduce water infiltration Drainage swales should be provided as soon as
possible and should be maintained to drain surface run off away from foundations
Landscaping of the house lots by the homeowners is recommended to reduce the
potential for erosion and for altering the drainage patterns
