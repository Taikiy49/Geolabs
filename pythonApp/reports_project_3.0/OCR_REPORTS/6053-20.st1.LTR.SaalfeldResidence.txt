This letter report presents the results of our additional percolation test performed
for the Individual Wastewater Systems at the Saalfeld Residence project
Project Considerations
The project site is within the Kukui Pahu Ranch in the District of North Kohala on
the Island of Hawaii As part of the project we understand individual wastewater systems
will be required for the new residence The State of Hawaii Department of Health
requires performance of a percolation test to evaluate the suitability of the site location
for use of an individual wastewater system Therefore field percolation tests were
performed at the proposed leaching field location
Two field percolation tests designated as PT 1 and PT 2 were previously
performed at the proposed absorption bed areas for the residence Based on the
percolation test results the leach field location at P 1 would not be suitable for the siting
of the absorption system at the location and elevation tested Therefore an additional
percolation test was conducted at the new absorption system location
Percolation Test
One additional field percolation test designated as PT 3 was performed at the
new absorption bed area for the residence A site plan showing the percolation test
locations is provided as Plate 1 The field percolation tests were performed in general
accordance with the State of Hawaii Department of Health s Administrative Rules
Standards to evaluate the permeability of the subsurface soils for disposal of
wastewater effluent
The percolation test was performed in an excavated pit of about 5 8 feet in depth
Approximately 1 inch of gravel was then placed at the bottom of the excavated pit to
protect the bottom from scouring and sediments Due to the silty and clayey nature of the
