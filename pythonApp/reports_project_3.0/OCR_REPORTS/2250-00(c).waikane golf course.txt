INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our preliminary geotechnical
engineering exploration for the design and construction of the site
grading of the proposed Waikane Golf Course The project site is located
in Waikane Valley in the Koolaupoko District of windward Oahu Hawaii
The general location of the project site is shown on the Project Location
Map Plate 1 Our work was performed in general accordance with our
revised proposal dated
This report summarizes our preliminary findings and recommendations
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed Waikane Golf Course development consists of a 27 hole
golf course and related facilities The preliminary golf course grading
plans prepared by BCA Golf indicate cuts and fills of up to about 30 feet
deep In some areas fills will be placed on steep existing slopes

Regional Geology
The island of Oahu is composed of two 2 shield volcanoes Waianae
and Koolau built up by the extrusion of basaltic lavas during the late
Pliocene and Pleistocene epochs Waikane Valley is an amphitheater headed
valley which has been deeply eroded into the eastern flank of Koolau
During the Pleistocene or Ice Age global sea levels rose and fell in
response to the great continental glaciers As the glaciers grew the
global sea level fell below the existing level and as the glaciers melted
and retreated the sea level rose to heights above the current stand of
the sea
During the lower stands of the sea the land surface eroded to depths
below the current sea level The basalt floor of Waikane Valley is most
likely one hundred or more feet below sea level Alluvial deposits were
laid down during higher stands of the sea at elevations above the current
base level Most of the project site is thickly mantled with alluviun
Some of this alluvial material has become deeply weathered and eroded
since its deposition

Soil Conditions
Stiff Soils
Based on our exploration the subsurface conditions at the project
site appear to consist primarily of stiff mottled brown brown and
reddish brown clayey silts to the maximm depths explored of 35 0
feet below the existing ground surface representing deeply weathered
alluvial materials These soils were originally stream laid clastic
deposits consisting of boulders cobbles and gravel In situ chemical
weathering of these clastic deposits has reduced the material to
clayey silts while retaining most of the structure of the original
boulders cobbles and gravel
These stiff clayey silt soils generally exhibit fair to good in situ
bearing strength characteristics and will also exhibit slight
lateritic properties i e they tend to develop slightly higher
strength or a slight cementation when exposed to subaerial conditions

Golf Course Grading
General Grading Methods
Areas to be graded should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed of
vegetation debris boulders and other deleterious materials Topsoil
should be removed and stockpiled for later usage and to avoid
contamination of fill materials Any tree roots or stumps greater
than three 3 inches in diameter should be removed In areas
designated to receive fill the prepared ground surface should be
scarified to a minimm depth of six 6 inches brought to near
optimm moisture content and recompacted to a minimm of 85 of its
maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 Any soft or
yielding areas encountered should be over excavated and backfilled
with on site soil compacted to 85 maximm dry density
Since the project site is located in a high rainfall area the on site
subsoils generally have in place moisture contents in excess of the
optimm moisture content Therefore some amount of drying of the
excavated material may be required before its use as fill and
compaction
Because of the high moisture content of the on site material and
because of the site being situated in a high rainfall area it is our
opinion that the normal 90 compaction standard used in other locales
may be modified to 85 for most of the golf course embankment fills
proposed on this project However the 90 standard i e 90 of the

Pond Embankment Fills
The pond embankments will serve as water retaining structures and as
such will have to resist lateral and artesian pressures Therefore
in order to develop sufficient soil strengths to resist these forces
we recommend that the fill in these embankments be compacted to at
least 85 of its maximm dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
The fill material should ideally come from within the upper five 5
feet of the higher ground areas which generally have in situ moisture
contents in the thirty 30 percent range The fill material should
also be placed and compacted at a moisture content that is above
optimum but not high enough to cause pumping of the fill when
compactive effort is applied

Fill Placement in Soft Soil Areas
Part of the proposed site grading will require the placement of fill
in areas which are presently underlain by soft wet soils In most of
the soft areas encountered during our hand auger probe exploration it
is our observation that the soft soils are thin enough to be removed
during the clearing and grubbing of the site If necessary to develop
a dry working table the contractor should be allowed to place six 6
to twelve 12 inches of ASTM C 67B 3B Fine gravel over the stiff
clayey silt soils underlying the soft soils If this gravel layer
extends below pond embankments it should be separated from the
embankment fill by a layer of filter fabric such as Mirafi 140N or
equivalent
Fill at 4 Blue 5 Blue
In the soft soil area in the vicinity of Holes 4 Blue and 5
Blue it is our opinion that the soft soil encountered is too
thick to be economically removed Therefore special grading
design and stabilization techniques will be required for fill
placement in this area
Prior to the commencement of site clearing and earthwork in this
area off site drainage water should be diverted away from the
lowlying marshy area Surface water and seeps in the grading
area should be drained This diversion and drainage will permit
the existing soft area to develop a firmer crust
The existing surface soil in this lowlying area is marshy and
wet and does not have sufficient strength to support heavy
construction equipment Therefore a working platform or
drainage blanket should be established prior to the major filling
of the swamp area The working platform may consist of a 3 to

Foundation Design
It is anticipated that the golf course development will require small
structure such as comfort stations and rain shelters Based upon the
soil conditions encountered in our field exploration it is our opinion
that shallow spread and continuous footing foundations bearing on
recompacted in situ soils may be utilized to support the proposed
structures The bottoms of foundation excavations should be scarified to
a depth of six 6 inches below footing subgrade moisture conditioned to
near optimm moisture content and recompacted to 90 of the maximm dry
density determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 Any soft wet and or
yielding areas encountered at footing subgrade level should be
over excavated to firm natural material and the resulting excavation
should be backfilled with non expansive material compacted in thin lifts
to 90 of its D dry density
An allowable bearing capacity of 2 000 p s f may be utilized for the
design of foundations bearing on recompacted in situ soils prepared as
recommended above A minimm footing embedment of 18 inches below the
lowest adjacent grade is recommended The above recommended bearing value
is for total dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third when
temporary wind or seismic loads are considered
Based on our exploration and laboratory testing estimated foundation
settlements for foundations constructed in cut areas are on the order of
less than one 1 inch total and one half 1 2 of an inch differential
Foundations constructed in fill areas will experience greater total
settlements

Placement and Compaction of Fill
A
The fill shall be placed in horizontal layers which when
compacted will not exceed twelve inches 12 in thickness
Each layer shall be spread uniformly and blade mixed to attain
uniformity of material and water content within each layer
Additional fill material shall not be placed on any fill layer
which has not been properly compacted Subsequent removal of
any unacceptable layer shall be done at the Contractor s
expense
Rock fragments or boulders less than twelve inches 12 in
diameter may be utilized provided they are not concentrated in
any one area and that there is sufficient fine grained material
to fill the voids between the rocks
Rocks greater than eight inches 8 in diameter shall be
removed from the fill unless specifically approved for placement
in the fill by the Geotechnical Engineer
All fill shall be compacted to a minimm of 85 percent of the
maximum dry density established by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
unless otherwise specified in the Geotechnical Engineer s
Report
The compaction equipment may be sheepsfoot vibratory or
pneumatic tire rollers suited to the soil type and capable of
producing the specified results in an expedient and efficient
manner The equipment shall be in good working condition fully
ballasted and self cleaning
