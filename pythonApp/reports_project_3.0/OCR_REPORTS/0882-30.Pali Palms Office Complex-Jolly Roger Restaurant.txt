INTRODUCTION
This report presents our recommendations for the foundation
design and construction of the proposed Jolly Roger Restaurant
Structure Subsurface conditions at the site were explored
by excavating a total of five 5 test pits each to 10 0 feet
below the existing ground surface The approximate test pit
locations are shown on the Site Plan Plate 1 and the des
cription of the materials encountered in the test pits is
presented on the Test Pit Logs Plates A 1 and A 2
Various laboratory tests were conducted on selected samples
Additionally in place field density tests were performed on
the underlying sandy materials to check their relative
densities Laboratory and field density test results are
presented on Plate A 3 through A 5S

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed restaurant will consist of a Single story wooden
structure with construction consisting of post and wall footings
The maximum structural loads are anticipated to be on the
order of 9 kips
Fill 1 to 2 feet will be placed under the floor slab area
during site grading
SOIL CONDITIONS
Geology
The restaurant site is situated geologically along the coast
line at the north edge of Kailua Bay Along shoreline areas
the subsurface formations are typically variable and discon
tinuous Variable conditions are basically a result of
different environmental conditions caused by changing sea
levels and stream courses
Locally the project site appears to have gone through periods
of changes typical of back reef environment
Subsurface Conditions
The proposed restaurant site is located in Kaneohe at the
intersection of Makapu Boulevard and Kalaheo Avenue The Site
is bordered by Kawainui Canal on the east and Kalaheo Avenue
on the west

DISCUSSTONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field exploration laboratory testing and the
engineering analysis the site could be developed as planned

The surface 2 5 to 3 0 feet of material consists of stiff
Slightly expansive clayey silt However one to two feet
of new fill will be placed under the restaurant floor slab
Therefore stiff clayey silt or engineered fill could provide
the desired foundation support for the proposed restaurant
structure
Due to the surface clayey silt materials being slightly expan
Sive a minimum footing embedment of 12 inches is recommended
Additional recommendations for the design of foundations and
construction are presented in the following paragraphs
Foundation
For the proposed structure post and wall footing foundations
are recommended All footings should be founded on undisturbed
competent clayey silt material or compacted sandy fill
An allowable bearing pressure of 2 000 pounds per square
foot could be used to design the footings founded on compe
tent clayey silt material or fill as described above A one
third increase may be used to allow for infrequently applied
wind and seismic loads
The footings should have a minimum width of 12 inches and be
embedded a minimum of 12 inches below the lowest adjacent
grade and on the above recommended materials
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 882 30 P 5
For the above recommendations it is estimated that the
total and differential settlements will not exceed one quarter
of an inch
We recommend that all the footing excavations be inspected
by the soils engineer to confirm the conditions upon which
our recommendations are based Any loose areas encountered
during excavation should be removed and replaced with either
concrete of granular material compacted to a minimum of 95
maximum density
Floor Slabs
The slab on grade floor can be supported on natural undistur
bed soilor engineered fill or on a combination of both In
any areas where dampness in the floor slab would be undesirable
or where moisture susceptible floor coverings are to be used
a minimum of four inches of granular type material should be
placed to act as a capillary break In addition a moisture
proof membrane should be placed over the granular material to
act as a vapor barrier The membrane should be covered by
one inch of sand to protect it during construction and to aid
in curing the concrete
Site Grading
Prior to receiving any fill the ground surface should be
stripped to remove vegetation and debris All the fill areas
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 882 30 P 6
exposed should then be scarified to a minimum depth of 6
inches brought to near optimum moisture and compacted to a
minimum of 90 percent of the maximum density as determined
by ASTM D 1557 prior to any fill placement
As much as 2 0 feet of new fill are anticipated for the
restaurant floor area
On site sandy materials generated from adjacent Pali Palms
project could be used as a source of structural fill provided
they are free of vegetation debris and other Organic material
Fill should then be placed in maximum 8 inch lifts and com
pacted to a minimum of 95 percent of the maximum dry density
Earthwork
Earthwork operations at the site will include footing excava
tion backfilling and foundation inspection during construction
The required foundation excavations would extend to shallow
depths 12 inches Excavations should be protected from
surface run off which may enter the excavations All loose
soil or mud should be removed from the bottom of all footing
excavations prior to concrete placement
Backfilling
Some minor backfilling operations would be required around the
footings Granular non expansive imported material could be

used for backfilling around footings Fill and backfill
should be placed in horizontal 10 inch lifts and compacted
to at least 95 percent of maximum dry density within the
limits of structures and 90 percent outside the structure
areas as obtained using ASTM D 1557 70 modified Proctor
Standard
Foundation Inspection
It is recommended that all the footing excavations and
filling operations be insepcted by the soils engineer to
verify adequate compaction and that the subsurface conditions
are consistent with the findings of our subsurface investi
gation
Our present recommendations are contingent upon the above
inspection and testing
