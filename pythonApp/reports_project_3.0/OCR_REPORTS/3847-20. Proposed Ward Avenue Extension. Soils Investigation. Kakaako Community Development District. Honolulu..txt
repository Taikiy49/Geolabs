INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our soils investigation performed for Increment 2 Makai Area
of the Kakaako Community Development District The purpose of this investigation was to
determine the nature of the soils underlying the site to ascertain their engineering properties and
to provide geotechnical recommendations for underground utility structure foundations trench
excavations pipe bedding support flexible pavement and site grading
This investigation included drilling 16 exploratory test borings obtaining representative soil samples
selected laboratory testing and analysis and the preparation of this report The general location of
the project site is shown on the enclosed Location Map Plate 1 The approximate exploratory boring
locations are shown on the enclosed Site Plan Also attached is an Appendix which describes the
laboratory testing procedures

FIELD EXPLORATION
The site was explored from June 19 through 29 1991 by drilling 16 exploratory test borings with a
truck mounted drilling machine The borings varied in depth from 19 to 70 5 feet
and B14 were not drilled due to the deeper depths to which several borings were drilled The
remaining boring sites were revised to retain sufficient site coverage
The soils were continuously logged by our field engineer and classified by visual examination in
accordance with the Unified Soil Classification System The approximate boring locations are shown
on the enclosed Site Plan and the soils encountered are logged on Plates Al through A22
Undisturbed and bag samples were recovered from the borings for selected laboratory testing and
analyses Undisturbed samples were obtained by driving a 3 inch O D thin walled split tube sampler
with a 140 pound hammer from a height of 30 inches The blow count required for twelve inches
of penetration is shown on the enclosed Boring Logs

CONCLUSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Grading Plans for the roadway and the proposed invert elevations were not available at the time of
this report Recommendations presented in this report are based on the assumption that no
significant fill will be placed over the site Settlement due to fill placement was not considered and
if significant may affect design recommendations for pipe support and pavement sections
Pipe Support
A conventional crushed rock cradle may be used for support of the various underground utility lines
Based on the estimated invert elevation of 8 0 we believe that the crushed rock cradle will be
founded on either the loose lagoonal deposits or dense to medium hard coralline material
The crushed rock cradle should conform to the minimum thickness indicated in the Standard Details
for Public Works Construction A minimum thickness of 6 inches is recommended The crushed rock
material should conform to the Standard Specifications for Public Works Construction Clean gravel
_ such as 3 Fine gravel ASTM Size 67 may be used
Trench excavations exposing loose lagoonal deposits should be underlain by an additional 12 inches
of compacted granular material Prior to placing the crushed rock granular material geotextile fabric
should be placed at the bottom of trench excavation and should envelope the bedding as well as the
first lift The purpose of the fabric is to minimize migration of the underlying soils into the crushed
rock bedding Fabric such as Mirafi 140N or Supac 4N should be suitable for this purpose

Trench Excavation and Stability
Based on our exploratory borings most trench excavations can be accomplished using conventional
excavating equipment However confined excavations into the occasional medium hard to hard
sections of coralline material may require pneumatic equipment
Considerable sloughing can be expected in the trench excavations especially below the groundwater
level and within the stratum of lagoonal deposits We believe that shoring will be necessary to
maintain trench sidewall stability It should be the Contractor s responsibility to conform to OSHA
safety standards during construction which may require sheeting and bracing for deep excavations
Utility lines will extend below the groundwater level and dewatering will be necessary for placement
of the crushed rock cradle and pipes The owner should be made aware of the potential effect of
dewatering on adjacent structures

In general trench backfill should conform to the Standard Specifications for Public Works
Construction The first lift of backfill extending to about 12 inches above the pipe barrel should
consist of select material such as beach sand or crushed aggregate

The flexible pavement section may be designed based on 2 5 inches of asphaltic concrete underlain
by 6 inches of base course The subgrade and base course should be compacted to a minimum 95
percent compaction as determined by ASTM D 1557 78

Lateral Design
The bearing values indicated above are for the total of dead and frequently applied live loads and
may be increased by one third for short duration loading which includes the effect of wind and seismic
forces Resistance to lateral loading may be provided by friction acting at the base of foundations
and by passive earth pressure acting on the buried portions of foundations
Allowable coefficients of friction of 0 3 and 0 4 may be used with the dead load forces for the loose
silty sand clayey sand and dense coralline material respectively Passive earth pressure may be
computed as an equivalent fluid having a density of 250 pounds per cubic foot with a maximum earth
pressure of 2500 pounds per square foot Unless covered by pavement or concrete slabs the upper
12 inches of soil should not be considered in computing lateral resistance

Site Grading
The project site should be cleared of all asphaltic concrete vegetation and other deleterious material
Prior to placement of fill the existing ground should be scarified to a depth of six inches and
compacted to a minimum 95 percent compaction as determined by ASTM D 1557 78
The onsite soils may be reused in compacted fills provided all rock and coral fragments larger than
six inches in maximum dimension are removed Any imported structural fill shall be well graded non
expansive granular material Specifications for imported structural fill should state that not more than
20 percent of soil by weight shall pass the 200 sieve In addition the P L of that portion of the soil
passing the 40 sieve shall not be greater than 10
All structural fill shall be placed in horizontal lifts restricted to eight inches in loose thickness and
compacted to a minimum 95 percent compaction as determined by ASTM D 1557 78

Consolidation
Settlement predictions of the soil s behavior under load are made on the basis of consolidation test
results Loads are applied in several increments in a geometric progression and the resulting
deformations are recorded at selected time intervals Porous stones are placed in contact with the
top and bottom of each specimen having an inside diameter of 2 40 inches and a height of 1 inch
to permit addition and release of pore fluid Results of tests on undisturbed samples are plotted on
the Consolidation Test Reports Plates Bi through B6
Shear Tests
Shear tests are performed in the Direct Shear Machine which is of the strain control type The rate
of deformation is approximately 0 02 inches per minute Each sample is sheared under varying
confining loads in order to determine the Coulomb shear strength parameters cohesion and angle
of internal friction Eighty percent of the maximum value is taken to determine the shear strength
parameters Test results are presented on Plates Cl through C8
Proctor Tests
Proctor tests are performed on bag samples to determine the optimum moisture content at which
each soil type compacts to 100 percent density The tests are performed in general accordance with
ASTM D 1557 78 and results are shown on Plates D1 and D2

in the design of flexible pavements The tests are performed in general accordance with ASTM D
