Dear Mr Abalos
This letter report presents the findings from our preliminary short term slope stability
assessment update conducted at the Poipu Bay Golf Course in Poipu on the Island of Kauai
Hawaii This report summarizes current findings based on our review of the ground surface
monitoring survey data and our recent reconnaissance of the project site
The intent of this Short Term Slope Stability Assessment No 11 is to provide the
owner with an update of the observed slope stability condition of a portion of sea cliff
adjacent to the 15th and 16th Holes of the golf course The preliminary findings presented
herein are subject to the limitations noted within and at the end of this letter report
Project Considerations
The project site is at the existing Poipu Bay Golf Course which is in the coastal Poipu
area on the Island of Kauai Hawaii A large multiple block type slope failure occurred in
March 2006 along a section of steep sea cliffs bordering the 15th and 16th Holes of the golf
course The cliff failure occurred following an extended period of very heavy rainfall in
March 2006 The collapsed portion of the cliff is within the existing shoreline conservation
zone The collapse area involved the full height of the sea cliff and extended approximately
300 feet long by about 25 feet wide

Site Reconnaissance
We conducted our site reconnaissance on to observe the existing site
conditions and to inspect the shoreline cliff via a drone for visual signs of potential
large scale shoreline slope instability The purpose of our annual reconnaissance update
was to review the existing ground surface conditions along the top of the slope and the
shoreline cliff in the vicinity of the monitoring footprint for visible signs of slope instability
The top of slope reconnaissance spanned between the 16th Hole Tees and the
15th Hole Fairway along a distance of about 1 400 linear feet The reconnaissance and the
drone shoreline cliff inspection were conducted by our engineers under generally dry
fair weather conditions
Selected photographs of the existing site and shoreline cliff conditions in the vicinity
of the ten survey monitoring points are presented on Plates 3 through 19
Existing Condition Observations
Based on our reconnaissance the ten existing ground surface monitoring points
were located and appear to remain in satisfactory condition with no overt visible sign of
significant disturbance or physical deterioration Based on our observations it appears the
monitoring points are suitable for providing useful ground survey data The approximate
locations of the ten monitoring points are shown on Site Plans 1 and 2 Plates 2 1 and 2 2
Based on our reconnaissance and review of the overall existing site conditions no
new area of potential slope instability was observed In addition overt visible signs of
progressive slope instability such as existing ground surface crack propagation and
widening were not observed at the project site

Existing Ground Crack Near Monitoring Point No 3
A ground surface crack within the coralline sandstone formation represents an area
of potential cliff instability partial block detachment The ground crack was identified
during our Short Term Assessment No 4 The area of potential cliff instability was
reviewed for visible signs of crack propagation The open ground surface crack is in
the vicinity of Monitoring Point No 3 as indicated on Site Plan 1 Plate 2 1 Monitoring
Point No 3 is affixed to the potentially unstable block of coralline rock seaward of
the crack Survey data collected from Monitoring Point No 3 with the passage of
time should be useful in evaluating whether subtle and slow movement such as
block rotation or sliding may be occurring
The open surface crack extends about 3 5 feet in length and trends roughly parallel
to the cliff face at a distance of about 10 to 12 feet inland from the cliff face We
observed that the surface crack aperture remains unchanged at 2 5 inches in width
At the ground surface on top of the cliff the vertically oriented jagged crack trends
roughly parallel to the cliff face Beyond the measured 3 5 foot surface length
exposure the crack is not visible on the ground surface The possible surface

Ground Surface Monitoring
As mentioned previously overt visible signs of large scale ground movement along
the top of the slope between the 16th Hole Tees and the 15th Hole Fairway were not
observed at the time of our reconnaissance Although we observed no visible indication
of imminent large scale slope instability the timing of possible large block type slope
failures cannot be predicted and the adequacy of relying on periodic visual observations
to assess the stability of the slope is limited
Based on our review of the available monitoring point survey data no significant
deflection of the ground surface markers was detected by the survey data collected from
Monitoring Point Nos 1 through 10
Due to the limitations associated with visual observations we recommend
continuing the annual site reconnaissance and ground survey monitoring program

Public Access at Top of Slope
We observed that the existing temporary wire safety fencing and signage spanning
across the top of the cliff may be effective in discouraging public access at the top of the
cliff seaward of the fence alignment The existing fencing now spans from Monitoring
Point No 1 through Monitoring Point No 8 and continues along the section of slope
involved in the 2006 landslide avulsion as shown on the Site Plans Plates 2 1 and 2 2
The fencing should assist with reducing public access to the hazardous cliff section where
unstable rock and deep sea cliff undercuts reside Continued maintenance of the existing
safety fence alignment is recommended
Public Access at Toe of Slope
Based on our observations the shoreline along the toe of the cliff alignments are
highly susceptible to the real danger of falling rock hazards Natural weathering and
erosion contribute to periodic toppling failure of existing loose rock blocks and boulders
comprising the top edge of the cliffs The public should be discouraged from accessing
the shoreline directly below the coastal cliffs due to the dangerous rockfall conditions
Long Term Setback Recommendations
We recommend maintaining the existing temporary safety fencing and warning
signage such that public access makai of the fencing is discouraged until a permanent
mitigation scheme is implemented It is our opinion the permanent mitigation could consist
of a setback and realignment of the coastal foot trail and the affected portion of the golf
course further inland and away from the zone of potential coastal sea cliff instability
For preliminary planning purposes a safe use setback may be approximated by
