DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
At the time of our site visit sitework had commenced and then
suspended Thus far the sitework had consisted of rough grading the two
house sites construction of retaining walls along Aina Lani Way and the
cutting of the existing approximately slope in Unit II to an
approximate ratio of Examination of the newly cut slope
indicated that the cinder sand encountered was moderately cemented
Review of the project plans indicates that the portion of the house
abutting this new cut slope will be a CW wall with no openings
Therefore it is our recommendation that the wall be designed as a
retaining wall and that the area between the wall and slope be backfilled
The guidelines for design of retaining walls are as follows

An allowable bearing capacity of up to 3000 p s f is recommended
for foundations bearing on undisturbed moderately cemented cinder
sand
A passive earth resistance of 300 p s f per foot of depth may be
used for the wall footing on level areas The passive pressure
must be reduced for walls located on slopes
A base friction factor of 0 4 may also be used to determine the
resistance against sliding of the wall
The wall foundations on relatively flat areas should be embedded
a minimm of 24 inches below the lowest adjacent grade
A subsurface drainage system subdrains and or weephole should
be utilized behind the walls to minimize the build up of
hydrostatic pressure behind the walls Subdrains should be
constructed properly to drain water away from the wall Select
granular backfill material is recommended behind the retaining
wall to minimize excessive compaction required
It is recommended that the backfill behind the wall consist of
on site cinder sand containing no fragments larger than six 6 inches and
no wood vegetation or other deleterious materials The backfill should
be placed in ten 10 inches thick horizontal lifts at optimum moisture
content and compacted to 90 maximum dry density as established by ASTM
D 1557 78
Fill slopes of 2H V1 are recommended with benches at 20 foot vertical
intervals if needed
If it is not practical or desirable to backfill the area behind the
wall we recommend that the slope be protected from sloughing and erosion
by gunite or chemical stabilization The wall up to one level above the
bottom should still be designed as a retaining wall in the event that
there is sloughing of the slope

Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comment
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this review
is not made the geotechnical engineer can assume no responsibility for
misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the
excavation and foundation phases of the work This is to observe
compliance with the design concepts specifications or recommendations and
to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ
from that anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations
given in this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based

constructed project Therefore sufficient contingency fund is thus
recommended to accommodate these possible extra costs
