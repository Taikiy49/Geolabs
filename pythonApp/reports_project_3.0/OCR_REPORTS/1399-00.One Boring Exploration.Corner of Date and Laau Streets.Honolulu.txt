This letter presents the findings of the one boring drilled at the
above referenced site on the 14th
Soil Conditions
The boring indicated a five foot plus fill crust underlain by another
five feet of stiff clayey silt with some sand and gravel to about the
water level encountered at a depth of about 9 5 feet Below the water
level is a 20 foot layer of lagoonal deposit consisting mainly of very
loose silty sand with shell fragments White coral was encountered at a
depth of about 30 to 35 feet in the boring
Observation
1 Based on the one boring data it appears that pile foundation will be
required to support the proposed 7 story concrete structure and that
pile foundation can obtain its support from the underlying coral
formation
2 Estimated length for low capacity pile will be to about 35 feet below
the existing grade
3 Recommend that additional borings be made at the other portions of
the site to further verify the presence and thickness of the
competent coral formation to support a pile foundation
