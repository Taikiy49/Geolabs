INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for Phase 2 of the Hokulani project in Kailua on
the island of Oahu Hawaii
The general location of the site and its
vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The exploration
was performed in general accordance with our proposal dated October 3
1988
This report summarizes our findings from the exploration and
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analysis based
on the data obtained during the exploration These recommendations are
intended for design input and are not intended for construction estimation
or bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The original development area has been divided into two phases The
subsurface exploration for Phase 1 was performed by others The current
exploration is limited to Phase 2 area
Based on the information provided to us the proposed development
will consist of 2
to 3 story wood framed buildings with concrete
slabs on grade
Asphaltic concrete pavements may be used for the
driveways and parking The anticipated maximum column and wall loads
estimated by the project structural engineer are on the order of 20 to 40
kips and 1 35 kips per foot respectively
Based on the preliminary grading plans supplied to us it is
anticipated that grading for the development will consist primarily of
removing the existing stockpiled surcharge materials up to about 4 to 8
feet in thickness which presently cover most of the central portion of
the site

It is also our understanding that fills up to about 8 feet high are
planned between Buildings E and F in the southern part of the site
Retaining walls will be used for grade separation Fill placement for the
other areas is anticipated to be minimal
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface conditions at the project site in order to develop a
generalized soil rock data set for the formulation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertinent to site grading and foundation
design of the project
The work was performed in general accordance with our proposal to Mr
Ien Chandler dated The scope of work consisted of the
following
1
Drilling and sampling ten 10 borings and two 2 probes to
depths of 20 0 to 46 5 feet below the existing ground surface
2
Reconnaissance of the project site and vicinity to make visual
observations of the surficial soil rock conditions
3
Review of available soils and geologic information pertinent to
the project site and its vicinity
4
laboratory testing of selected samples obtained during the field
exploration to determine the engineering properties and
classification of the materials encountered
5
Engineering analyses of the field and laboratory data for the
purpose of developing site grading foundation and pavement
design recommendations and
6
Preparation of this report presenting our findings and
recommendations

SITE DESCRIPTION
The subject site is located immediately south of Aoloa Street and
bounded by Hamakua Drive to the west and Aoloa Place to the east in
Kailua Koolaupoko on the island of Oahu Kaelepulu Stream borders the
property on the southwest
The eastern part of the area is generally level with ground
elevations ranging from 7 o to 9 0 feet above Mean Sea Level
The
remainder of the area is covered by heavy growth of vegetation and
stockpiled surcharge materials The approximate location of the existing
stockpile is delineated on the Site Plan Plate 2 The existing stockpile
heights range from about 4 to 8 feet above the adjacent Aoloa Street The
approximate existing ground elevations in the areas range from about 8 0
to 16 0 feet above Mean Sea Level
Site clearing was performed in portions of the area in order to
provide accessibility for our field exploration
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling ten 10
borings and two 2 probes to depths ranging from 20 0 to 46 5 feet below
the existing ground surface The approximate locations of the borings and
probes are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The field exploration generally encountered a surficial layer or
crust of about 10 feet in thickness consisting primarily of stiff
mottled brown clayey silt with decomposed rock fragments Underlying the
crust were lagcx mal deposits consisting of silty sand and for silty coral
gravel The upper portion of the sand and gravel strata was generally in
a medium dense condition however the consistency graded to loose from
approximately 15 foot depth to the maximum depths drilled

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The stiff on site soil encountered in the surficial crust layer may
generally provide adequate foundation support for the proposed development
provided that some care is exercised during foundation and slab
construction to minimize the swelling potential of the soil
The laboratory tests performed on the stiff on site clayey silt
indicate that this material generally has low to moderately high swelling
potential
This potential varies depending on the initial moisture
content of the soil prior to subsequent saturation Therefore to reduce
the effects of soil swelling below on grade slabs we recommend that the
on site soil be capped with at least 12 inches of non expansive select
W o 2159 00
Page 6
fill and that the subgrade soils underneath the select fill and slab be
wetted and protected from drying from the start of construction until
concrete slab placement
Site Grading
Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limits should be cleared of vegetation rubbish previously
stockpiled fill and other deleterious materials These materials
should be removed and disposed of properly off site Any loose or
soft spots encountered should be over excavated down to the firm
subsoils and then replaced with compacted non expansive select
material
The ground should then be scarified to a depth of minimum six 6
inches moisture conditioned to near optimum moisture content and
recompacted to 90 of its maximum dry density as established by ASTM
Test Method D 1557 78
Fill Materials
All imported fill should consist of non expansive select granular
materials less than 3 inches in maximum size with less than 20
passing the 200 sieve such as select borrow crusher waste cinder
or coral The fill material should be tested and approved by the
geotechnical engineer prior to transportation to the site
To reduce the swelling potential of the on site soils the materials
within the top 12 inches minimum below the concrete slab on grade
should consist of non expansive soils as recommended above

Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
It is our understanding that fills up to about 8 feet high are
planned between Buildings E and F in the southern part of the
site Retaining walls will be used for grade separation Based on
the grading plans supplied to us no other major fill placement is
anticipated
As a precautionary measure we recommend that the fill be placed a
minimum of one month prior to the wall construction Non expansive
select granular material such as select borrow crusher waste cinder
or coral should be used as fill material The on site mottled brown
clay silt has low to moderately high swelling potential and is not
recommended as fill material behind the retaining walls
The fill should be placed in horizontal loose level lifts up to a
maximum of 10 inches thick and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its
maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 In addition we
recommend that the fill be placed about 3 to 4 feet beyond the
perimeter of the walls and that the over fill be cut back during
later wall construction
Foundations
Conventional spread footing foundations may be used to support the
structures
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f with a minimum
footing embedment of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade
may be used for footings bearing on the on site stiff soils A minimum
footing width of 12 inches is recommended
The bearing value is for total dead plus live loads and may be
increased by one third when temporary wind and seismic loads are
considered
W o 2159 00
Page 8
If any soft or weak areas are encountered at the bottom of footing
excavations the areas should be over excavated to the stiff on site soils
and backfilled with select fill and compacted to a minimum of 95 of its
maximum dry density as established by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
The bottom of all footing excavations should be inspected by the
geotechnical engineer prior to placement of the reinforcement to
determine if adequate bearing has been obtained and that loose soils are
removed prior to concrete placement
It is our opinion that for footings designed and constructed in
accordance with the above criteria the anticipated total and differential
settlements should be less than one half of an inch
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive
earth pressure developed against the buried vertical faces of the
foundation system and by friction resistance developed between the bottom
of the foundation and the bearing soil
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be calculated using an
equivalent fluid pressure of 350 p c f This assumes that the soil around
the foundation is well compacted A coefficient of friction of 0 4 may be
used Unless covered by pavement or slabs the passive resistance in the
upper 12 inches of soil should be neglected
Slab On Grade
It should be noted that the on site clayey silt soils may be
moderately expansive if allowed to dry Therefore the material within a
depth of 12 inches minimum below the slab bottoms should consist of
non expansive select fill as previously recommended
To further reduce the potential for future post construction swelling
of the underlying expansive soils we recommend that the subgrade below
the upper capping fill be first scarified to a depth of ten 10 inches
w o 2159 00
Page 9
moisture conditioned to the wet side of optimum moisture content and
recompacted to 90 of its maximum dry density as established by AS IM Test
Method D 1557 78
The subgrade should be kept moist by water sprinkling
prior to concrete placement
The thickened edge of the slab should be embedded a minimum of 12
inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade
A 4 inch layer of 3B Fine gravel should be provided below the slabs
as cushion fill
In order to protect against future moisture infiltration
and subsequent damage to the floor coverings an impervious moisture
membrane should be incorporated below the slabs provided an inch of moist
fine sand is placed above the plastic membrane to help concrete curing

Utility Trench
If foundations are located next to sewer drainage easements they
should be embedded below a 1 1 H V imaginary plane extending upward
from the bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of
the utility lines
We further recommend that construction of the utility lines be
given the first priority and the adjacent foundation works delayed to
the end of the project
The trench backfill should be properly
compacted to protect against potential future ground subsidence
Drainage
The finish grade outside the buildings should be shaped to shed
water away from foundations and to avoid ponding conditions Also it
is advised that gutter systems be installed around the buildings and
discharge water should be diverted away from foundation and driveway
areas Excessive landscape watering near foundations and slab should
also be avoided

Page 11
Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance
of the building foundations since excess water could cause subsurface soil
saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of strength
The foundation
excavations should be properly backfilled tight against the wall or slab
footing immediately after setting of the concrete to prevent ponding
condition
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and maintained
to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundations
Pavement Section
Asphaltic concrete pavements are anticipated for the driveways and
parking area The following preliminary pavement section may be used
2 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 Inch Base Course
8 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
on Compacted Moist Subgrade
CBR and density tests should be performed on the actual subgrade
soils encountered during construction and the above section may be
revised if necessary The recommended section considers medium traffic
and assumes that adequate drainage will be provided in the paved areas

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained during
earthwork construction to monitor the contractor s grading operations
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be observed by
the geotechnical engineer during construction to see that adequate bearing
has been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with
the findings of our exploration
This is to observe compliance with the recommendations design
concepts or specifications and to allow design changes in the event that
subsurface conditions differ from that anticipated prior to start of
construction The recommendations given in this report are contingent
upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
