REMARKS Crack Monitoring Program Updated Findings and Discussions
A crack monitoring program was set up at the existing recreation building
within the Asing Community Park in January 2013 to document the crack
movements at selected locations across the building structure The original
crack monitoring program consisted of five crack gauges designated as CG
6 through CG 10 and ten separation monitoring points designated as G 1
through G 10
Four new crack gauges designated as CG 11 through CG 14 were
installed in March 2016 at the front and back sides of the CMU wall on the
left north side of the door entrance to the Arts and Craft Room to document
the movements of the new cracks that have developed in the wall The
approximate locations of the crack gauges and separation monitoring points
are shown on Crack Gauge and Separation Monitoring Point Location Plan
Plate 1
During our site visit for the January 2017 reading it was discovered that
some of the crack gauges including 8 and 10 and all of the
separation monitoring points have been painted over during a building
maintenance project As a result a new set of separation monitoring points
was set up for the March 2017 reading In addition the four crack gauges
mentioned above were subsequently replaced with new ones in July 2017
when they could no longer provide a legible view of the cross hairs
The current 2 year crack monitoring program commenced on March 11
2016 and concluded on The crack gauges and separation
monitoring points were visually surveyed by Geolabs on a bi monthly basis
and the results were transmitted to your office on a periodic basis for review
and record
The initial and final readings of each of the crack gauges installed including
the replacement gauges as mentioned above are shown on Plates 2
through 6 Based on our field observations and the readings obtained for the
crack gauges it appears that there is some minor continual movement of the
building walls and ceilings at the recreation building However the

REMARKS Site Meeting Discussions and Recommendations
Based on our site meeting held on we recommend that
the following measures be implemented in an attempt to arrest further
building movement and damage
Re Grading or Hardscape Installation at Existing Courtyard
The observed distresses at the recreation building could be a result of water
infiltration from the existing courtyard causing saturation and softening of
the foundation bearing soils In order to provide positive drainage away from
the foundations we recommend that the existing dirt mound in the courtyard
be removed and the courtyard re graded to allow rainwater to flow towards
the existing drain inlets
Alternatively consideration should be given to changing the existing
courtyard to hardscape to completely eliminate the infiltration and migration
of water towards the foundations
Video Inspection of Existing Downspouts
Based on our field observations it appears that significant movements were
observed at Crack Gauge No 10 at the outside corner of the women s
bathroom where a downspout connects the roof gutter to the drain inlet in
the courtyard We recommend that a video inspection be performed on the
downspout to determine if the piping has been compromised such as loose
joints and cracks which might result in water leaking into the foundation
bearing soils in its proximity
Underpinning of Foundations with Helical Piers
To improve the long term performance of the building foundations we
recommend that the eastern portion of the building be also supported by
helical piers similar to those installed for the other half of the building in
2011
