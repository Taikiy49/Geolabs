PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The project site is situated along Kapolei Parkway in the Kapolei area on the Island 
of Oahu, Hawaii. An affordable housing complex is planned along Kapolei Parkway. As part 
of the project, the existing Wakea Street will be extended to connect to Kapolei Parkway, 
and additional site grading will be required at Lot No. 5. This letter covers earthwork 
recommendations for site grading required at Lot No. 5. 
Based on the preliminary plans provided, a new drainage ditch will be excavated at 
Lot No. 5. The drainage ditch will have a bottom elevation of about +45.3 feet Mean Sea 
Level (MSL). Based on the preliminary plans, we envision site grading consisting of cuts of 
up to about 25 feet deep will be required to achieve the design grades.  
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS 
Based on the borings drilled at Lot Nos. 6 and 7 (adjacent to Lot No. 5), we envision 
that the subsurface conditions at the site generally consist of alluvium intermixed with 
coralline deposits. Fill materials consisting of medium dense silty sands with some gravel 
may be present at some portion of the site.  
Alluvium at the project site generally consists of stiff to very stiff sandy and silty 
clays. It should be noted that the alluvium encountered at the site is generally moderately

Site Preparation 
At the on-set of earthwork, areas within the contract grading limits should be 
cleared and grubbed thoroughly. Vegetation, debris, deleterious materials, existing 
structures to be demolished, and other unsuitable materials should be removed and 
disposed of properly off-site to reduce the potential for contaminating the excavated 
materials. 
Soft and yielding areas encountered during clearing and grubbing below areas 
designated to receive fill and/or future improvements should be over-excavated to expose 
firm natural material, and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with 
well-compacted fill. The excavated soft soils should not be reused as fill materials and 
should be properly disposed of off-site.  
After clearing and grubbing, areas to receive fills should be scarified to a depth of at 
least 12 inches, moisture-conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture 
content, and recompacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction. Relative compaction 
refers to the in-place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry 
density as determined by ASTM D1557. Optimum moisture is the water content 
(percentage by dry weight) corresponding to the maximum dry density.

Fills and Backfills 
Due to their moderately to highly expansive nature, excavated on-site clayey soils 
should not be used as a source of fill except in landscape areas.  Fill materials required 
for the project should consist of select granular fill.  Select granular fill should consist of 
non-expansive granular material, such as crushed coralline and/or basaltic materials. The 
material should be well-graded from coarse to fine with particles no larger than 3 inches in 
the largest dimension and should contain between 10 and 30 percent particles passing the 
No. 200 sieve. The material should have a laboratory California Bearing Ratio (CBR) value 
of 20 or more and should have a maximum swell of 1 percent or less when tested in 
accordance with ASTM D1883. 
Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements 
The general fill and non-expansive select granular fill materials should be placed in 
level lifts of about 8 inches in loose thickness, moisture-conditioned to above the optimum 
moisture, and compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction (or 95 percent relative 
compaction for finished subgrades subjected to vehicular traffic). 
Cut and Fill Slopes 
In general, we believe the cut slopes planned at the site exposing alluvium and 
coralline materials may be designed with a slope inclination of two horizontal to 
one vertical (2H:1V) or flatter. Where cut slopes with inclinations steeper than 2H:1V are 
planned, provisions for slope protection measures, such as placement of grouted rubble 
paving (GRP), should be considered in the project design.  Grouted rubble paving should 
have a minimum thickness of 12 inches with an inclination of 1H:1V or flatter.  A Geolabs 
representative should observe the conditions of the finished cut slope face and evaluate 
the need for slope protection measures. 
Permanent fill slopes constructed of the select granular fill materials recommended 
herein may be designed with a slope inclination of 2H:1V or flatter. In addition, fills placed 
on slopes steeper than 5H:1V should be benched. The fill slope face should be finished 
to a relatively smooth and well-compacted surface.

For cut and fill slopes steeper than 3H:1V with vertical heights greater than 15 feet, 
we recommend providing a minimum 8-foot wide bench at the mid-point of the slope or 
at every 15-foot height interval to reduce the potential for significant erosion due to surface 
water runoff. The 8-foot wide benches may be omitted for cut and fill slopes less than 
15 feet in height. Where slope benches are provided (especially for fill slopes), 
concrete-lined swales should be provided on the benches to collect and divert surface 
water from the slopes to appropriate discharge outlets. In addition, the construction of 
interceptor ditches and the use of geotextile fabrics over the fill slope face should be 
considered to reduce the potential for significant erosion, thus enhancing the long-term 
stability of the fill slopes. 
LIMITATIONS 
The analyses and earthwork recommendations submitted in this letter report are 
based in part upon information obtained from available in-house subsurface data in the 
project vicinity.  Variations of the subsurface conditions between and beyond the field data 
points may occur, and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident 
until construction is underway. If variations then appear evident, it will be necessary to 
re-evaluate the recommendations presented in this letter report.
