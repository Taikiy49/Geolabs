INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration for the foundation and pavement design of the proposed
Munitions Storage Facility The project site is situated on Andersen Air
Force Base on the island of Guam the southerrmost of the Mariana
Islands The general location of the project site is shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1 Our work was performed in general accordance with
our proposal dated
This report summarizes our findings and presents our design
recommendations

PROJECT CONSIDERATION
The project site is located on Andersen Air Force Base in the
northern part of the island of Guam Mariana Islands The project site is
relatively level and is currently used as parking and as an open storage
area
It is proposed to clear and grade the site for the construction of an
enclosed warehouse type storage facility The proposed storage facility
will be a masonry building with reinforced concrete foundations and
slab on grade floors An asphaltic concrete paved driveway will serve the
facility

The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the

foundation and pavement design for the proposed munitions storage
facility

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
The project site is located on the northern end of the island of
Guam the largest of the Mariana Islands The approximate location of the
project is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The foundation of the island is composed of volcanic rocks including
pyroclastic materials which were extruded during early to mid Tertiary
time Volcanic activity ended during the Oligocene epoch
Following the cessation of the volcanic activity extensive
coral algal reef deposits were laid down in the area of what is now the
northern half of the island from the Miocene through Pleistocene epochs
W O 2299 00 Page 4
Tectonic uplift has raised these old reef deposits to form a plateau
upon which the project site is located
Subsurface Exploration

feet below the existing ground surface to explore the subsurface soil
conditions and to obtain samples for laboratory testing The approximate

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field explorations disclosed that the site is underlain by
detrital coral algal reef limestone consisting of very dense sandy
gravel with cobbles and boulders This material should provide adequate
bearing capacity to support the proposed Munitions Storage Facility
Based on the field exploration and laboratory test results shallow
spread footing foundations and slab on grade construction may be utilized
for the proposed structure
Building Foundations
Spread or continuous footing foundations may be used to support the
proposed structure
Footings should be embedded a minimum of 12 inches below the lowest
adjacent grade
W O 2299 00 Page 5
For the anticipated light loads footings bearing on the dense sandy
gravel or coral limestone deposit with an allowable bearing value of 3 000
p s f may be utilized
The above bearing capacity values are for total dead plus live loads
and may be increased by one third when temporary wind or seismic loads are
considered
The bottom of all footings should be free of loose soils and observed
by the project engineer geologist prior to concrete placement
If a footing is located next to a utility trench the footing should
be extended to the bottom of the trench or the trench backfill located
below the footings should be well compacted granular structural fill
material to prevent footing settlement due to settlement of the trench
backfill
Estimated total and differential settlements are anticipated to be on
the order of less than one fourth of an inch for footings bearing on the
dense coral or sandy gravel
Cavity Probing
Although no cavities were encountered in our exploration previous
experience has shown that solution cavities commonly occur in coral
limestone deposits In order to lessen the possibility of footing
settlement due to collapse of potential cavities located close to the
footing excavations we recommend that drilled probes be made at the
center of each major column or five 5 feet on centers along line footing
locations to detect large voids which may exist below the footing
The drilled probes should be three 3 to four 4 inches in diameter
and extend at least eight 8 feet or the width of the footing whichever
is greater below the bottom of the footing The resulting drilled holes

pressures to fill and detect possible large cavities
Lateral Resistance
lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive
earth pressure developed against the buried vertical faces of the
foundation system and by frictional resistance developed between the
bottom of the foundations and the bearing soil
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be calculated using an
equivalent fluid pressure of 350 p s f per foot of depth of the vertical
footing faces This assumes that the backfill around the foundations is
properly compacted to a minimm of 90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM
Test Method D 1557 78
The frictional resistance may be calculated by using a coefficient of
friction of 0 45
The above resistance values are maximum values and do not contain a
factor of safety
Slab On Grade
Generally the on site material has low expansion potential The
existing shallow subsoils appear dense and the existing asphaltic concrete
pavement appears to be structurally sound
For the anticipated forklift and static floor loads a 6 inch slab
over four 4 inches of well graded aggregate base course is recommended

Structural Fill Material and Placement
Any fill and backfill material required should be non expansive
well graded granular material free of organics and other deleterious
substances and should be generally less than six 6 inches in size
Fills and backfills should be placed in horizontal lifts not
exceeding eight 8 inches in compacted thickness and compacted to
90 of its maximm dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Site grading should provide drainage of surface run off away from all
foundations
Pavements
Asphaltic concrete pavements are planned for the driveways and
parking areas We recommend that the A C pavement section consist of the
following
2 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 Inch Base Course
8 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
on Moist Subgrade
The recommended section assumes medium traffic and good pavement
drainage
If feasible the subgrade at the parking lot area should be graded to
drain towards drain inlet structures where weepholes should be installed
It should be shaped to drain both surface and subsurface water away from
the pavement areas The above design consideration is to permit pavement
drainage and to reduce the potential of subgrade saturation

CBR and density tests and or field observations should be performed on
the actual subgrade soils encountered during construction and that the

subgrade areas should be provided in order to relieve the seepage of water

Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comment prior
to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to the
earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this review is
not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for
misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the
foundation phases of the work This is to observe compliance with the
design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow design
changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from that
anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in
this report are contingent upon such observations
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during construction
are different from those assumed or considered in this report then
appropriate modifications to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
upon information obtained from the test pits Variations of conditions
between the test pits may occur and the nature and extent of these
W O 2299 00 Page 9
variations may not become evident until construction If variations then
appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations
given in this report
The locations of the test pits were determined by taping from
reference points indicated on the Site Plan provided to us Elevations of
the test pits were approximately determined by interpolation from
elevation points on the same plan The physical location and elevation of
the test pits should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by
the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the test
pits depict the approximate boundaries between soil types and as such
may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on profiles or
cross sections are based upon interpolation between borings and may not
represent actual subsurface conditions
Water level data from the boring points were collected at the times
given on the graphic representations and or in the text of this report
These data have been reviewed and interpretations made in the formulation
of this report However it must be noted that fluctuation may occur due
to variations in rainfall tides temperature and other factors
