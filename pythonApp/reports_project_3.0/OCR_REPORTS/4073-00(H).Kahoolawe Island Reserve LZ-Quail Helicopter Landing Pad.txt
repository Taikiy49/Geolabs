REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The Island of Kaho olawe is located about 11 kilometers to the south of the Island of
Maui and about 27 kilometers to the southeast of the Island of Lana i as shown on the Project
Location Map Plate is approximately 117 square kilometers in land area with
a maximum elevation of about 450 meters above sea level at the Lua Makika summit on the
eastern portion of the island

Kaho olawe is believed to be composed of a single shield volcano formed by the
extrusion of successive layered thinly bedded pahoehoe and a a basalt lava flows that
emanated from the rift zones and caldera at Lua Makika The primary eruptive rift zones of
the former volcano extend westerly from the summit and to a lesser degree to the north and
east from the summit Two cinder cone remnants can be seen located at Puu Moiwi and Puu
Kamama The area at Lua Kealialalo is believed to represent the weathered remains of a
collapsed crater at the crest of the broad lava shield Some of the more recent aged rocks
composing the upper caldera filling layers of the island have been dated at about 1 million
years old
SITE DESCRIPTION
The LZ Quail project site is located in the vicinity of Lua Kealialalo which is situated at
the central portion of the western half of the Island of Kaho olawe as shown on the Project
Location Map Plate comprises the crest of the central upland backbone of
the Island of Kaho olawe The project site generally consists of very gently sloping terrain
underlain by tan and reddish brown silts and clays with sands and some gravel representing
both residual and saprolite soil products derived from the complete weathering of basalt rock
Based on the available grading plan the existing terrain rises gradually toward the north
as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The existing ground surface elevation ranges from about
252 meters Mean Sea Level MSL at the southeast corner of the site to about 255 meters
MSL along the northern perimeter of the site Some small topographic mounds are present
within the grading limits located at the eastern half of the project site
In general the project site consists of very gently sloping ground vegetated with widely
scattered Kiawe trees and tufts of dryland grass Much of the windswept ground surface is
barren and devoid of vegetation with a hardpan soil surface The ground surface is covered
sporadically with a thin scatter of loose friable gravel chips believed to represent mineral
concretions created by the weathering of basait materials
NEAR SURFACE SOIL CONDITIONS
The soils observed at the project site appear to be relatively uniform in appearance and
may be classified as low plasticity silts and clays with sands and some gravel representing the
in situ residual and saprolitic soil derived from the complete weathering of basalt rock Based
on our observation of the site the tan and reddish brown colored soils appear to be very stiff
though friable due in part to the arid environment The soils appear to have a generally low
shrink swell potential
Most of the very genily sloping terrain has developed a hardpan crust of soil which is
relatively resistant to the initiation of erosion Abrasion and scouring by wind appears to be
responsible for the formation of the surficial hardpan crust However once the surficial crust

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field reconnaissance and laboratory test results we believe that the
principal geotechnical engineering considerations for construction of the proposed helipad and
truck parking area includes the following
e The near surface soils are relatively dry and will require a significant amount of
water to moisture condition the soils for site preparation and grading
Proper backfilling around excavated areas and grading to shed water away from
and around the helipad and parking area
Detailed discussion of these items and our geotechnical engineering recommendations
for design of the helicopter landing pad and site preparation and grading are presented in the
following sections of this letter report
Helicopter Landing Pad
As previously mentioned the helicopter landing pad is to be designed to accommodate
CH 53 helicopters Based on the plans provided we understand that the helicopter landing
pad will be constructed with a Solid Steel Plate SSP landing mat placed over a firm base
Based on the anticipated site conditions we recommend that the base below the SSP landing
mats consist of at least 200 mm of aggregate base course in order to provide a firm base on
which to construct the SSP landing mats and to withstand the repeated loading from the
helicopter take offs and landings

We understand that a plastic geomembrane sheet is to be provided below the
aggregate base course to reduce the potential for migration of POL deeper into the
subsurface To provide some protection of the plastic membrane from damage due to the
placement of the aggregate base course we recommend that a non woven geotextile fabric
be placed below the geomembrane above the compacted subgrade and above the
geomembrane just below the 200 mm thickness of aggregate base course The 200 mm thick
aggregate base course may be placed in one lift and should be compacted to at least
95 percent relative compaction based on ASTM Test Designation D 1557
Because the base of the SSP landing mats will consist of aggregate base course there
may be a concern with FOD from the potentially loose gravel exposed at the helipad area
Therefore consideration may be given to the use of a surface polymer treatment on the
surface of the aggregate base course to reduce the potential for FOD at the surface of the
helipad
Vehicle Parking Area
In general we understand that the parking area and passenger embarkation area will
generally be subjected to relatively light duty vehicles such as pedestrian traffic pick up
trucks light vehicles and occasional moderately loaded trucks Therefore we believe that the
vehicle parking area passenger embarkation area and walkway leading to the helipad may
be surfaced with 150 mm of compacted aggregate subbase material Due to the less stringent
requirements for aggregate outside of the helipad area the aggregate subbase material may
be derived from the processing of rock materials obtained from various sources on the Island
of Kaho olawe

Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork areas within the contract grading limits should be thoroughly
cleared and grubbed Vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable materials
should be removed and disposed of properly off site or stockpiled in a designated area
to reduce the potential for contamination of the excavated materials
Soft and yielding areas encountered during clearing and grubbing should be
over excavated to expose firm natural material and the resulting excavation should be
backfilled with well compacted general fill or controlled fill The excavated soft soils
should be properly disposed of off site and or stockpiled in a designated area where
appropriate
Finished subgrades and areas designated to receive fills should be scarified to a
minimum depth of 200 mm moisture conditioned to between 10 percent below the
optimum moisture and the optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum
of 95 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry
density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil
established in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 698 Optimum moisture is
the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry
density
Where shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of the subgrade we recommend
that the soil be thoroughly moistened to close the cracks or that the soil subgrade be
prepared again as recommended above Saturation and subsequent yielding of the
exposed subgrade due to inclement weather and poor drainage may require
over excavation of the soft areas and replacement with well compacted general fill or
controlled fill

controlled fill materials provided that they are free of vegetation and deleterious
materials and the maximum particle size should be less than 150 mm in maximum

Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
General fill materials and or controlled fill should be moisture conditioned to between
10 percent below the optimum moisture and the optimum moisture content placed in
level lifts not exceeding 200 mm in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density
of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil
established in accordance with ASTM D 698 test procedures Optimum moisture is the
water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
The aggregate base course and aggregate subbase materials should be moisture
conditioned placed in level lifts not exceeding 200 mm in loose thickness and
compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction based on ASTM Test
Designation D 1557
Cut and Fill Slopes
We anticipate that some minor cut and fill slopes on the order of 2 meters or less will
be constructed at the site to provide the level pad for the proposed construction We

envision that the cut slopes would generally expose the residual and saprolitic soils
described in the Near Surface Soil Conditions section of this letter report In general
we believe that cut slopes exposing the very stiff residual and saprolitic soils may be
designed with a slope inclination of two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V or flatter It
should be noted that graded surfaces exposing the on site materials are highly
susceptible to erosion by wind and surface water runoff Therefore freshly graded cut
slopes should be protected by appropriate slope planting or other means to reduce the
potential for erosion of the exposed materials as soon as possible after the slope faces
are exposed
Permanent fill slopes constructed using compacted general fill materials or controlled
fill materials may be designed with a slope inclination of 2H 1V or flatter Fills placed
on slopes steeper than 5H 1V should be keyed and benched into the existing slope to
provide stability of the new fill against sliding The filling operations should start at the
lowest point and continue up in level horizontal compacted layers in accordance with
the fill placement recommendations presented herein In general fill slopes should be
constructed by overfilling and cutting back to the design slope ratio to obtain a
well compacted slope face In addition slope planting should be provided as soon as
possible to reduce the potential for erosion of the finished slopes
Drainage
The finished grades adjacent to and beyond the helipad and vehicle parking area
should be sloped to shed water away from the pad and finished surfaces to reduce the
potential for ponding Surface water ponding should not be allowed on the site during or after
construction These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
helipad since ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving
or loss of strength The edges of the helipad excavations should be properly backfilled to
reduce the potential for significant water infiltration In addition drainage swales should be
provided as soon as possible and should be maintained to drain surface water runoff away
from the helipad

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from the field reconnaissance and laboratory testing of surface soil
samples only Test borings were not drilled to obtain detailed subsurface profile information
Variations of subsoil conditions across the project site may occur and the nature and extent
of these variations may not become evident until construction is underway Therefore it is
highly recommended that a representative of Geolabs be present during the site grading
operations to observe and evaluate whether the exposed soil rock conditions are similar to
those assumed in design If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate
the recommendations provided in this report
The locations of the field soil sampling indicated in this report are approximate having
been estimated by taping from features shown on the preliminary Site Plan provided by Austin
Tsutsumi and Associates Inc on Elevations of the soil samples where
indicated were estimated from the elevation points shown on the same plan The physical
location and elevation of the soil samples should be considered accurate only to the degree
implied by the methods used
