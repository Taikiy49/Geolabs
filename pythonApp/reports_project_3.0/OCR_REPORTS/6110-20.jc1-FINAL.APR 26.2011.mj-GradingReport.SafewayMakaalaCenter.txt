Project Considerations
The project site is adjacent to the existing Waiakea Center in the Hilo area on the
Island of Hawaii The Makaala Center is comprised of a single parcel bounded
approximately by an industrial development to the north Railroad Avenue to the east East
Makaala Street to the south and the existing Hilo Wal Mart store to the west

The approximately L shaped site provides approximately 220 000 square feet of
commercial space The Safeway Store occupies about 60 000 square feet of store space
on the western portion adjacent to the new Target Store which encompasses about
160 000 square feet of store space as shown on the Site Plan Plate 1 In addition the
project entails construction of a parking lot for both stores accommodating over 900 cars
In general shallow foundations consisting of spread and continuous footings with
thickened edge are used to support the interior columns and perimeter wall loads of the
store building structures
The Makaala Center site was mass graded between March 2010 and March 2011
Earthwork observation and testing during the mass grading operations are summarized in
a separated report This letter report covers our observation for the footing and plumbing
excavations and backfilling construction of the loading dock and truck apron slab as well
as foundation probing and grouting operations Koga Engineering Construction Inc was
the subcontractor for the site work operations at the project site Based on our records the
building site work operations for the Safeway Store site were conducted between July
2010 and February 2011
Site Grading
The project site was mass graded to a finish pad elevation of about 76 feet Mean
Sea Level MSL Mass grading at the site generally consisted of placing up to about 5 feet
of new fills and less than about 2 feet of excavation to achieve the design grades Details
of the mass grading activities including site preparation fill materials and fill compaction
are contained in the above referenced Safeway Building Pad Construction Report
Additional site grading conducted after mass grading generally consisted of minor
cuts and fills of less than 1 to 2 feet thick The additional cuts and fills were generally
required for construction of the building foundation parking areas sidewalks utility lines
and other appurtenances
In general fill materials consisting of structural fill were placed at the project site
Structural fills consisted of well graded granular materials generally smaller than 6
inches in dimension Subgrades in cut areas generally were proof rolled with a
vibratory drum roller for about eight passes
Structural fill materials were used as fill and backfill materials within utility trench
excavations roadways parking lot and other areas Samples of the on site crushed
materials utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office
for testing where appropriate The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents
were established in our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D 1557
Modified Proctor and the test results are summarized on Plate 2

The fill and backfill materials generally were moisture conditioned to near or
above optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent of the
maximum dry density as determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557 except the fill
and backfill materials for utility trench and landscape areas were compacted to a
minimum of 90 percent
During the site work operations periodic field density tests were performed in
general accordance with ASTM D 6938 Nuclear Gauge Method where appropriate
Compaction tests results are summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 3 of this report These
tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and
backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and
interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed to
be unsatisfactory were generally reworked until the specified minimum compaction was
obtained and or accepted by our field representative based on visual observation and
hand probing
Based on the observations of our field representatives it is our professional opinion
that the site work operations for the above mentioned site observed by Geolabs in the
period covered in this report were performed in general conformance with the intent of the
project plans and specifications and the geotechnical engineering recommendations
presented in the referenced geotechnical engineering report
Limitations
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork may
have been performed without our observation However the level of earthwork
observation and testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some of
the geotechnical risks to the project It should be recognized that there are variations in
accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the
earthwork construction and materials Our services were performed in accordance with
generally accepted standards of geotechnical engineering practice no warranty is
expressed or implied Provision of construction monitoring by an engineer is not insurance
nor does it guarantee construction of any type The contractor shall retain responsibility for
the quality of the work for adhering to the plans and specifications and for repairing
defects regardless of when they are found
