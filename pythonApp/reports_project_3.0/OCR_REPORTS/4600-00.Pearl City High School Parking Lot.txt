GEOTECHNICAL ENGINEERING EXPLORATION
PEARL CITY HIGH SCHOOL PARKING LOT
OAHU HAWAII
Dear Mr Okuda
In accordance with our revised fee proposal dated we have
conducted a geotechnical engineering exploration at the existing Pearl City High School
parking lot on the Island of Oahu Hawaii This report summarizes our work on the project
and presents our findings and geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our
analyses for the subject project
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The existing parking lot is located southwest of the existing football field on the
campus of the Pearl City High School Access to the site is via an access road from the end
of Hookiekie Street The parking lot consists of approximately 105 parking stalls for
passenger vehicles separated by three concrete islands and bus parking along the eastern
and western curb areas The existing asphalt pavement is deteriorated with ruts and some
cracking particularly along the bus parking areas along the eastern side of the lot Ponding
water was noted in two areas at the easter end of the islands It was reported that the water
ponding is present even during dry periods Several large trees were noted around the
perimeter of the parking lot The pavement in some of these areas showed signs of heaving
due to root penetration
The project includes removal and replacement of the asphaltic concrete wearing
surface only However due to the water ponding noted it was desired to evaluate the
possible sources and need for subdrainage to remove the water

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our field exploration program for the project consisted of drilling and sampling
six borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 6 extending to depths of about 5 to 10 feet
below the existing ground surface The approximate locations of the drilled borings are
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general our field exploration disclosed that the project site is underlain by stiff to
very stiff reddish brown silty clay with gravel extending to the maximum depth drilled
Seepage zones were not detected in the areas with water ponding
Groundwater levels were not encountered in the drilled borings at the time of our field
exploration It should be noted that groundwater levels are subject to change due to
seasonal precipitation temperature groundwater withdrawal and other factors
Detailed descriptions of the subsurface materials encountered from our field
exploration are presented on the Boring Log Legend and Logs of Borings Plates 3 through
9 of this report
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Generally competent subsoil conditions were encountered in the drilled borings at
the site Seepage zones or groundwater water levels were not encountered during the field
exploration Subdrains are recommended below the pavement in the areas where water
ponding was noted to provide an outlet for possible water build up below the pavment
It appeared that some of the pavement damage was a result of pavement uplift from
root penetration by nearby trees Consideration should be given to removal of trees
adjacent to the pavements
Detailed discussion of these items and our geotechnical engineering design
recommendations are presented in the following sections of this report
Pavement Design
The design of new pavements for the parking lot was not included in our scope of
work It is our understanding that the existing pavement section will be replaced Our
borings encountered approximately 2 inches of asphaltic concrete over 6 to 8 inches of base
course confirming the original pavement section indicated on the plans

Site Preparation
Prior to placement of the new base course material the existing grade should be
scarified to a minimum depth of 8 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum
moisture content and recompacted to not less than 95 percent relative compaction
Soft and yielding areas encountered should be over excavated to expose firm natural
material and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with base course placed in 6 inch
maximum loose lifts moisture conditioned to at least the optimum moisture content and
compacted to at least 95 percent relative compaction ASTM D 1557 91 The excavated
fill soils should be disposed of properly off site and or used in landscape areas where
appropriate
Aggregate base course required for the project should consist of crushed basalt
aggregate and should conform to Section 703 06 of the State of Hawaii Standard
Specifications for Road Bridge and Public Works Construction 1994 Aggregate base
course materials should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content
placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to not less
than 95 percent relative compaction
In general paved areas should be sloped and drainage gradients should be
maintained to carry surface water off the site Surface water ponding should not be allowed
on the site during or after construction
Subdrains
Subdrains are recommended below the two areas at the eastern ends of the islands
where ponding water has been noted The subdrains should consist of 4 inch diameter
perforated pipes embedded in porous granular fill such as No 3B Fine gravel wrapped in a
geotextile A schematic sketch of the subdrain is illustrated on Plate 13 The subdrain
should be daylighed to drain into the existing catch basin along the eastern side of the
parking lot

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that Geolabs be retained to provide geotechnical engineering
services during construction of the proposed project The items of construction monitoring
that are critical requiring Special Inspection include observation of preparation and
placement and compaction of the base course materials This is to observe compliance with
the intent of the design concepts specifications and or recommendations and to expedite
suggestions for design changes that may be required in the event that subsurface conditions
differ from those anticipated at the time this report was prepared The recommendations
provided in this report are contingent upon such observations
If actual exposed subsurface conditions encountered during construction differ from
those assumed or considered in this report Geolabs should be contacted to review and or
revise the geotechnical engineering recommendations presented herein
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from the field borings Variations of subsoil conditions between and
beyond the field borings may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not
become evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident Geolabs
should be contacted to re evaluate the recommendations presented in this report
The boring locations in the field were determined by taping from the Sheet C 5 of
DAGS Job No Parking Lot Site Plan undated The physical locations of the
field borings should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown on the graphic representations of the borings depict the
approximate boundaries between the soil types and as such may denote a gradual
transition Water level data from the borings were measured at the times shown on the
graphic representations and or in the text of this report These data have been reviewed and
interpretations made in the formulation of this report However it must be noted that
fluctuation may occur due to tidal fluctuation variation in rainfall temperatures and other
factors
