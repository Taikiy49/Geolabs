EARTHWORK OPERATION
The project site is located at the northern end of Turtle Bay Resort adjacent to the
existing Turtle Bay lagoon in the Kahuku area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii It was
proposed to construct two new three story buildings at the eastern and western sides of
the existing villas and remodel four existing buildings to provide a new three story
configuration The proposed modification project also involves renovation of the existing
swimming pool construction of new dining areas a new access roadway and parking lot
Based on the set of plans provided separate footings was proposed to be constructed
adjacent to the existing footings to support the structural loading from the additional floor
The construction was performed by Armstrong Builder The earthwork operation
consisted of clearing the area within the contract grading limits over excavation of the
surficial expansive clays preparation of pavement subgrades placement and compaction
of select borrow and base course and utility tench excavation and backfill
Following the clearing operation the area within the new building footprint was
excavated to the bottom of the footing elevation The subgrade of the footing was

the recommended pavement section The over excavated subgrades were generally
scarified to a minimum depth of 8 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above
the optimum moisture content and recompacted to a minimum of 95 percent of the
maximum dry density as determined in accordance with American Society of Testing and
Material ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor
The upper 3 feet of utility trench backfills located below the pavement areas were
generally placed with select borrow and base course in near level lifts brought to above or
near the optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent of the maximum
dry density in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor
During the earthwork operation samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor
and the test results are summarized on Plate 1 of this report
Periodic field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations to
confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures and soil conditions
were noted A total of 31 field density tests including retests of failed areas were
performed using the nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM Test Designation
D 2922 The results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 2 1 and
2 2 of this report These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on
constructed engineered fills and backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were
estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes and construction plans
These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied
by the methods used Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the
specified minimum compaction was obtained and or approved by our field representative
based on visual observation and hand probing
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the earthwork operations during the building
roadway and utility construction for the above project observed and tested by Geolabs in
the period covered by this report were performed in general accordance with the intent of
the project plans and specifications and our geotechnical engineering recommendations

LIMITATIONS
lt should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork may
have been performed without our observation However the level of earthwork

the geotechnical risks to the project It should be recognized that there are variations in
the accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the
earthwork construction and materials Our services were performed in accordance with
