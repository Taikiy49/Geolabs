In accordance with your request a site visit was made yesterday to observe the
condition of the excavation for the new UST s near Building 81 at Lualualei
of PACDIV environmental section accompanied the undersigned to the
site
The excavation was only partially completed at the time of our visit as the
backhoe experienced mechanical problems and was removed from the site prior to our
arrival The excavation was approximately 40 by 60 in plan dimension The northern
and southern walls of the excavation were cut vertical to depths of about 10 to 17 feet
below the existing ground surface The eastern wall was cut at a slope of about
1 horizontal to 1 vertical for a depth of about 10 to 12 feet The western side of the
excavation was used as a ramp presumably for access of the backhoe
The soil conditions exposed consisted of tan silty coral gravel and sand with
some isolated zones of tan clayey silt Most of the material ranged from silt to gravel
and would be classified as GM or SM under the Unified Soil Classification System The
material consistency was stiff to dense and slightly moist This material represents a
formation of weathered coral reef detritus formation Pocket penetrometer readings
were taken at various locations in the exposed cut with most of the readings exceeding
4 5 tons per square foot The exception was in the isolated pockets of silty clay where
