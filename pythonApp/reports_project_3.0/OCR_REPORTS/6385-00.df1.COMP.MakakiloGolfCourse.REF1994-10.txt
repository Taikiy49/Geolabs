This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations testing and

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Makakilo Golf Course and Clubhouse Complex were planned above the
H 1 Highway in the 1980 s Mass grading construction commenced in the late 1980 s
however the project was not completed therefore it is desired to close the grading permits
During the grading then current owner Chiyoda Pacific retained Geolabs to provide
grading observation and compaction testing of the fills placed until the project was stopped A
Project Location Map and a Site Plan are shown on Plates 1 and 2 respectively
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services for the
grading operations for the project site during the active construction period between 1991 and
1992 and subsequent consultation and testing conducted to obtain information for the closure
of the grading permit
GRADING WORK
We observed the grading work for the Makakilo Golf Course and Clubhouse Complex
from about to about The grading work consisted of clearing
and grubbing and placement and compaction of fills on the order of up to about 15 feet thick
to achieve the design finished grades Pan Pacific Construction performed the grading work

During the grading operations samples of the on site and imported materials utilized as fill and
backfill materials were obtained and transported to our laboratory for testing The maximum
dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general
accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor
The fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as
determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557
During the grading operations periodic field density tests were performed in general
accordance with ASTM D 2922 Nuclear Gauge Method Results of the compaction tests
performed are summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 29 These tests were performed on the
prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills at the site Test locations and
elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes These
locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the
methods used Areas assessed as unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum
compaction was obtained and or approved by our field representative
During the period of active grading work our services consisted of part time field
observation of the contractor s fill placement subgrade preparation and compaction
procedures and confirmation of subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above grading
operations
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the referenced
tests it is our opinion that the portions of the grading work that were completed for the above
project observed and tested by Geolabs during the active construction period were performed
in conformance with the general intent of Chapter 14 of the Revised Ordinances of Honolulu
1990 as amended
POST GRADING CONSULTATION
Subsequent to the cessation of Chiyoda Pacific s operations in Hawaii the property
was acquired by Grace Pacific for expansion of the existing Makakilo Quarry The grading
permit for the proposed golf course remained open with the transfer of the property The
property is now classified as Preservation and other than expansion of the quarry in the
central part of the property there are no plans to develop the parcel
The Clubhouse Complex was essentially completed However the structures were left
vacant and unused Due to recurrent problems with vandalism and unauthorized occupancy
Grace Pacific had the structures demolished
Since the proposed golf course will not be completed and there are no other plans to
develop the property there is no reason to continue to keep the grading permits open
Grace Pacific Corporation

However since the grading work had been suspended while in progress and
consultation services to Chiyoda Pacific had been terminated the condition of the site with
respect to completion of grading and compaction was uncertain Therefore it was decided to
conduct a brief field investigation to evaluate those conditions
In conclusion Geolabs was retained to conduct an exploration which consisted of
excavating 15 shallow test pits at selected locations based on the original grading plans and
testing the degree of compaction of the soils in general accordance with ASTM D 6938
Nuclear Gauge Method The results of those tests are summarized along with the
original compaction tests on Plates 3 1 through 3 29
In addition to the test pits our exploration included reconnaissance of the golf course
area by our project engineer geologist to visually observe the site conditions with respect to
grading work This reconnaissance and testing generally indicated that the eastern portion
of the proposed golf course had been brought to finish grade and compacted to a minimum
of 90 percent relative dry density the central portion was partially graded and near surface
compaction was close to but less than the required 90 percent and the western portion had
not been graded These areas are mapped on the Site Plan Plate 2 Although two of the
recent tests indicated compaction less than the originally specified 90 it is our opinion
that 85 compaction criteria is more appropriate for the future use of the site
As previously stated the formerly proposed golf course is now being held as
Preservation land and there are no plans to further develop the site Based on the findings
of our exploration and reconnaissance it is our opinion that the current condition of the site
is adequate for preservation with no remedial grading or compaction work required
Therefore the existing grading permit should be closed
LIMITATIONS
Geolabs assumes no responsibility for any alterations made to slopes or finished
grades on the subject project subsequent to our observation and testing period without our
knowledge and written acknowledgment
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities occurring
simultaneously on a construction site therefore some work may have occurred without our
observation However we believe that the level of earthwork observation and testing services
provided was reasonable in reducing the geotechnical risks to the project It should be
recognized that there are variations in the accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and
methods used to monitor the earthwork construction and materials Our services were
performed in accordance with generally accepted standards of geotechnical engineering
