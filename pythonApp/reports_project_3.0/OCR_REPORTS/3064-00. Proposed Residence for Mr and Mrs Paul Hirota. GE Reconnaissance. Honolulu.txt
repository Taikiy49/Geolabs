GEOTECHNICAL ENGINEERING RECONNAISSANCE
PROPOSED RESIDENCE FOR HIROTA
2293 HALAKAU STREET
This letter report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
reconnaissance performed for the proposed residence of Hirota at 2293
Halakau Street at the Waialae Iki V Subdivision in the district of Honolulu on the island of
Oahu Hawaii
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering recommenda
tions resulting from our field reconnaissance laboratory testing and engineering analyses
The recommendations presented herein are intended for design input and may not be
appropriate to form a basis for preparation of final construction cost estimates nor for
construction bidding purposes

Site Grading
Based on the available site plan grading for the proposed residence will consist
of cuts and fills on the order of about four feet 4 to provide a level building pad for the
construction Due to the expansive nature of the subsoil over excavation below the

following guidelines should be observed during the site grading operations

grading limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation
debris and other deleterious materials should be removed and disposed of
properly off site

be over excavated to expose firm ground and the resulting excavation
should be backfilled with well compacted engineered fill Contract
documents should include additive and deductive unit prices for over
excavation and engineered fill construction to account for variations in the
over excavation quantities
3 Areas that are to receive fills should be scarified to a depth of eight inches

Foundations
Spread and Continuous Footings
The proposed two story residence may be supported by a foundation system
consisting of spread or continuous wall footings An allowable bearing capacity
of up to 2 500 p s f with a minimum footing embedment of 18 inches is
recommended The allowable soil bearing pressures is for total dead plus live
loads and may be increased by one third for transient applications such as
temporary wind and or seismic loads
If footings are located near the top of slopes or on slopes the footing should be
embedded deep enough such that the horizontal distance from the outside edge
at the base of the footing to edge of slope is not less than eight feet 8
The foundation subgrade should be recompacted to a minimum of 95 percent of
relative compaction
Footing excavations should not be allowed to dry prior to placement of concrete
If shrinkage cracks appear in the footing excavations the soil should be thoroughly
moistened to close all cracks and recompacted prior to concrete placement
The individual footing bottom preparation should be monitored during construction
by a representative of Geolabs Hawaii to determine if adequate bearing would be
obtained prior to placement of reinforcement and concrete

Slab On Grade
The site is underlain by silty clay soils which are expansive when subjected to
moisture fluctuations Unless structures supported by these soils are properly
designed there is a potential for future distress to structures resulting from the
swelling of these soils when wetted To reduce the potential for structural distress
resulting from the swelling shrinking of the on site expansive soils we recommend
that the concrete slabs be supported on 24 inches of non expansive select fill
material The non expansive select fill material should extend at least two feet 2
laterally beyond the perimeter of the proposed structure
The select fill material should be placed as soon as possible after the scarification
and recompaction of the existing subgrade soils The select fill would serve as
a layer of moist soil above the underlying potentially expansive subgrade soils and
would reduce the loss of moisture from the subgrade soils The reduction in
drying should reduce the potential shrink swell effects of the underlying moderately
expansive clayey soils Compaction requirements for the subgrade soils and select
fill are discussed in the Site Grading section of this report
Conventional slab on grade construction may then by utilized for proposed
structures constructed on the above prepared capping granular fill layer However
it should be noted that minor cracks in the slab can be expected Therefore we
recommend that a flexible floor covering be used on the slab We also
recommend that interior walls be designed to incorporate some flexibility to
accommodate some possible movements

Lateral Load Resistance
Lateral load acting on the structure may be resisted by passive earth pressure
developed against the buried vertical faces of the foundation system and by
frictional resistance developed between the bottom of the foundation and the soil
system

Construction Observation
It is recommended that Geolabs Hawaii be retained to provide geotechnical
engineering services during the project construction to observe the grading operations
and footing excavations This is to observe compliance with the intent of the design
concepts specifications and or recommendations and to allow design changes in the
event that subsurface conditions differ from those anticipated during design The
recommendations presented in this report are contingent upon the above observations
Any imported material required should be tested by Geolabs Hawaii for confor
mance with the project specifications prior to hauling to the project site
If the actual soil conditions encountered during construction are different from
those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to the design
should be made

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from field reconnaissance Variations of subsoil conditions may not
become evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will
be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations presented in this report
The recommendations of this report are also based upon the assumption that the
project site grading has been accomplished in accordance with the general
recommendations of the subsurface investigation report submitted by Fewell Geotechnical
Engineering and entitled Waialae Iki 5 Subdivision Phases 2B and
Oahu Hawaii

or implied
