As requested this letter report presents our geotechnical recommendations
pertaining to the reconstruction of the concrete cart paths near the shoreline at the
Hualalai Resort due to damages caused by the tsunami generated by the Tohoku
Earthquake of Japan on March 11 2011
Project Considerations
Hualalai Resort is located about 7 miles northeast of Keahole International Airport
in the District of North Kona on the Island of Hawaii We understand the resort has
sustained various damages due to the tsunami event generated by the Tohoku
Earthquake of Japan on March 11 2011 We understand some of the existing concrete
cart paths along the shoreline were partially undermined due to erosion caused by the
tsunami Therefore reconstruction of the beachside cart path is desired
We understand the cart path is being used as an access road to conduct various
clean up and repair work related to the tsunami event In addition the resort is planning to
reopen by April 30th Therefore expeditious reconstruction of the cart paths will be
required
Cart Path
The subgrade materials along the cart path generally consist of clean beach sands
Therefore design of the new cart path will need to consider the highly erodible nature of
the clean beach sands In addition the clean beach sands are highly susceptible to
disturbance caused by even foot traffic due to the clean and poorly graded nature of the
beach sands The high susceptibility to disturbance would cause difficulty in maintaining
the beach sands in a densely compacted state until the concrete for the cart path is placed
on the prepared subgrade

We understand it is desired to utilize an 8 inch thick concrete section for the cart
paths in lieu of the 4 inch thick concrete section with 8 inch deep thickened edges on both
sides to provide confinement to the erodible beach sand subgrade Due to the nature of
the clean beach sand subgrade it would be difficult to maintain the subgrade for the cart
path in a dense state at the transitions from the 4 inch section to the 8 inch thickened
edges For that reason we recommend a uniform thickness concrete slab for the cart
paths
It should be noted that the concrete shrinkage due to drying of the local concrete
products is generally more pronounced than similar concrete on the US Mainland due to
the nature of the local basaltic aggregates even with the use of fiber reinforcement in the
concrete To reduce the potential for substantial shrinkage cracks in the concrete slabs
crack control joints should be provided at intervals equal to the width of the cart paths with
expansion joints at right angle intersections The crack control joints should be made at
the appropriate time following concrete placement to reduce the potential for uncontrolled
cracking of the concrete slab
In general we believe the new concrete cart path may be constructed directly on
the compacted sandy subgrades provided that the recommendations herein are followed
Subgrade preparation is a critical item for the performance of the new cart path It should
be noted that the poorly graded clean beach sands are generally difficult to compact to a
dense state and maintained in that dense state unless adequate moisture is preserved in
the material Thorough wetting of the beach sands will be required to compact the
subgrade properly utilizing a vibratory plate or similar compaction equipment In addition if
the materials are allowed to dry out following compaction the sandy soils will have a
tendency to loosen Therefore we recommend cement treating the beach sands to
provide a more stable subgrade Details of the recommend cart path section are provided
on the attached Plate 1
For cement treatment of the beach sand subgrades we recommend applying one
sack of dry cement 94 pound bag per 60 square feet of subgrade area The cement
treatment of the subgrades should extend a minimum 12 inches beyond the edges of the
cart path as shown on Plate 1 The sandy subgrades should be moisture conditioned to
above the optimum moisture content followed by applying the dry cement The dry cement
may be spread manually and raked into the sandy subgrades to a depth of about 6 inches
The cement treated subgrades should be compacted to a minimum 90 percent relative
compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a
percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with
ASTM D 1557 Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by weight
corresponding to the maximum dry density Compaction should be accomplished by
vibratory plate tampers or other types of acceptable compaction equipment

Limitations
Geolabs should be retained to provide geotechnical services during the cart path
reconstruction work The critical items of construction monitoring include observation of
subgrade preparation and compaction prior to concrete placement A Geolabs
representative should monitor the construction to observe compliance with the intent of the
design concepts or recommendations and to expedite suggestions for design changes that
may be required in the event that subsurface conditions differ from those anticipated at the
time this letter report was prepared The recommendations provided herein are contingent
upon such observations
lf the actual exposed subsurface conditions encountered during construction are
different from those assumed or considered in this letter report then appropriate design
modifications should be made
