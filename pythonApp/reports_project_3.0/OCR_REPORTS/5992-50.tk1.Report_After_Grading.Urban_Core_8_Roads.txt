This report summarizes our earthwork observation and testing services performed 
during the mass grading work for the Urban Core 8 Roads project from 
December 24, 2013 through July 7, 2014. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
Road “B” extends from the intersection with Kapolei Parkway to the intersection 
with Kamokila Boulevard. Road “B” is a 60-foot right-of-way roadway measuring 
approximately 1,000 feet long. Minimal fills and cuts of up to about 6 feet thick are 
anticipated to achieve the design finish grades for the roadway. In addition, new utilities, 
such as water, drain, and sewer lines, are planned for the project.

Site grading work at Lots 40002 and 40003 will be included as part of the roadway 
construction project. The design finish grade at these two lots is generally set at about 
+64 feet Mean Sea Level (MSL). 
We understand that Lot 40002 will be leveled to generate fill materials for the 
grading at Lot 40003.  In addition, the excavated materials generated from Road “B” 
construction will also be used to fill the existing retention basin at the northern corner of 
Lot 40003.  The bottom of the retention basin is at an elevation of approximately +44 feet 
Mean Sea Level (MSL).  Therefore, we anticipate that up to about 20 feet of fill will be 
required to achieve the design finish grade of +64 feet MSL at the retention basin. 
EARTHWORK OPERATIONS 
Royal Contracting Company, Limited performed the mass grading operations for 
the project. The site grading work consisted of clearing and grubbing, subgrade 
preparation, and placement and compaction of fill materials to the design finished 
subgrades. 
Following the clearing and grubbing operations, the existing subgrades were 
scarified to a depth of about 12 inches, moisture-conditioned to about 2 percent above the 
optimum moisture content, and recompacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative 
compaction. Relative compaction refers to the in-place dry density of soil expressed as a 
percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with 
American Society of Testing and Material (ASTM) D1557 (Modified Proctor). Optimum 
moisture is the water content (percentage by dry weight) corresponding to the maximum 
dry density of the maximum dry density. 
Excavated on-site soils generated from the cut areas were used to backfill the lower 
portion of the existing retention basin and in the deeper fill areas across the site.  The 
on-site fill materials were placed in near-level lifts, brought to about 2 percent above the 
optimum moisture, and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction.  The 
upper portion of the fill placement generally consisted of imported non-expansive, select 
granular material, placed in near-level lifts, brought to above the optimum moisture, and 
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction. 
The roadway areas were generally over-excavated, where necessary, to a depth of 
about 27.5 inches below the pavement finished grades to provide the recommended 
pavement structural section for Road “B”.  Prior to the placement of the aggregate 
subbase, the pavement subgrade was scarified to a depth of about 8 inches, 
moisture-conditioned to above the optimum moisture, and recompacted to a minimum of 
95 percent of the maximum dry density determined in accordance with ASTM D1557 
(Modified Proctor).  Imported coralline material used as a source of aggregate subbase in 
the pavement structural section was moisture-conditioned to above the optimum moisture 
content and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction.  Utility trench 
backfills were generally placed in near-level lifts, brought to about 2 percent above the 
optimum moisture, and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction.

During the site grading operations, samples of the on-site and imported materials 
utilized as fill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing. The

Field density tests were performed during the fill operations on an as-need basis to 
confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures and soil conditions 
were observed. A total of 212 field density tests were performed using the nuclear probe 
method in accordance with ASTM D 6938. Results of the compaction tests performed are 
summarized on Plates 3.1 through 3.13 of this report. These tests were performed on the 
prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills at the site. Test locations 
and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on-site survey stakes. 
These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied 
by the methods used.

report were performed in general accordance with the intent of the project plans and 
specifications and our geotechnical recommendations presented in the referenced reports 
dated February 14, 2009 and November 27, 2013 and subsequent recommendations.

accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor 
earthwork construction and materials.
