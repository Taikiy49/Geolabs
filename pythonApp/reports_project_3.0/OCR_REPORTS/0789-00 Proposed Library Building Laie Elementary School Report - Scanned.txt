INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this investigation was to evaluate the sub
surface conditions at the site to provide recommendations
for the design and construction of the foundations of the
proposed structure
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
GEOLABS HAWAII
SITE DESCRIPTION
The Laie Elementary School is located on Iosepa Street
about 500 feet west of Kamehameha Highway
The site for the proposed library building is located near
the central portion of the campus
The building site is surrounded by various one story con
crete buildings and portable classrooms
The area was vacant at the time of the exploration and
covered with grass
The general area is quite level
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
The proposed structure will be a one story concrete and
masonry building approximately 51 by 103 feet in plan
A maximum column load up to 60 kips is anticipated
Minor grading is anticipated for the site
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The project site is located in the lowlying coastal area
around the northeastern portion of Oahu
The area is underlain by alluvial deposits derived from
the weathering of upper coral formations and dune sand

Subsurface Exploration
Three borings were drilled at the site to depths of 25 to
26 5 feet below the existing ground surface to explore the
subsurface conditions and to obtain samples for laboratory
testing The approximate locations of the borings are
shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
Detailed information on the field exploration and laboratory
testing is covered in the appendices along with the boring
logs and laboratory test results
Soil Conditions
Based on the borings the site appears to be underlain by
a surface layer about 4 to 8 feet thick of medium to
dense silty sand Below this silty sand coral shells
with some gray clay generally in a loose condition was
encountered
The water level was encountered at 5 to 6 foot depths in
the borings
GEOLABS HAWAII
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed fairly competent sandy
subsoils at the site
Proofrolling of the building site is recommended to den
sify the near surface soil and to create a uniform bearing
layer for the proposed structure
The proofrolling should be performed with a 10 ton vibra
tory roller A minimum of 6 passes is recommended
Foundations
After proofrolling the building site spread or continuous
footing foundations are recommended An allowable bearing
value of 2 000 p s f is recommended for footings resting
on the dense silty sand
The bottoms of footings should be placed about 2 feet below
the finish grade
The bottom of the footing excavation should be tamped prior
to pouring of concrete
Grade beams around the perimeter of the building will help
to reduce differential settlement
Surface water should be diverted away from building foun
dation

Earthwork Compaction Requirements
Minor grading is planned for the site
Prior to filling the site should be cleared of all
vegetation debris and old foundations and sidewalks
Select on site material should be used for fill material
The fill should be placed in horizontal loose lifts of
about 8 inches and compacted to a minimum of 90 of the
maximum dry density established by ASTM Test Method D 1557
Slab On Grade
Slab on grade should be placed over a 4 inch layer of
base course generally less than 3 4 inch and larger than
inch in size The subgrade should be recompacted prior
to placing the base course
To protect against moisture damage of the flooring material
a polyethylene moisture barrier should be used
Cesspools
Existing cesspools are located near the proposed building
site The locations of the cesspools should be verified
in the field
GEOLABS HAWAII
To reduce building settlements due to the possibility of
loose backfill around the cesspools the proposed build
ing should be kept a minimum of 10 feet away from any
existing cesspools
If abandoned cesspools are encountered in the proposed
building site they should be cleaned out and backfilled
with granular material The cesspool linings within 5
feet from the ground surface should be removed
Design Review
The plans for foundations site grading and utility con
struction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for
review and comments prior to construction to see that
the intent of our recommendations is included in the pro
ject plans and specifications
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during
earthwork construction to monitor the contractor s grading
operations
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be
observed by a soils engineer during construction to see
that adequate bearing has been obtained and that the sub
surface conditions are consistent with the findings of our
subsurface investigation
GEOLABS HAWAII
Any imported material required should be non expansive
tested and approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling
to the site

FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored
by drilling 3 borings to depths of about 25 feet below the
existing ground surface The borings were drilled by P R
Drilling Company The approximate locations of the borings
are shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
The soils encountered in the borings were classified
by visual and textural examination in the field by our engin
veer who continuously monitored the drilling operations The
classifications were reviewed by visual inspection and test
ing in the laboratory The soils were classified in general
conformance with the Unified Soil Classification System A
graphic presentation of the soils is presented on the Boring
Logs Plates A 1 through A 6
In the borings soil samples were obtained by driving
split barrel sampler with a 14Q pound hammer falling 30
inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the last
12 inches are shown on the boring logs at the appropriate
depths
