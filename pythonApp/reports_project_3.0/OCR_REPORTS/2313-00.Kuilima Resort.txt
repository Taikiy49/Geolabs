PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this geotechnical engineering exploration was to
explore the general subsurface soil and ground water level conditions along
the beach frontage roadway alignment to provide geotechnical engineering
recommendations for roadway fill embankment construction utility
installation pavement thickness pipe bedding design and preliminary
roadway bridge foundation design
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests which we
used in the engineering analysis to formulate our conclusions and
recommendations are presented in the appendices of this report
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
The scope of work performed in this exploration was in general
accordance with our proposal dated Our scope included
drilling and sampling a total of nineteen 19 borings to depths varying
from 17 to 67 feet below the existing ground surface laboratory testing
engineering evaluation of the field and laboratory data and report
preparation The nineteen 19 drilled borings also included four 4
borings at each of the two 2 access roadway bridge sites crossing the
proposed east and west main drains
The proposed beach frontage road project will connect the proposed
Kawela Entry Roadway to the new golf clubhouse to provide another roadway
system for the Kuilima Resort expansion The major elements of the roadway
improvement are as follows
1 A new access road about 80 feet wide and 7 400 feet long It is
anticipated that the road construction will involve primarily
embankment fill to raise the finish grade above the existing
ground

GENERAL SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed roadway alignment is located mostly within the existing
Turtle Bay Hilton Golf Course on the North Shore of Oahu
In general the topography is undulating with ground elevations
ranging from 4 to 13 feet above Mean Sea Level
The site is mostly covered with grass and many large coconut pine
ironwood and wiliwili trees
Existing golf cart paths and dirt roads transect the proposed road
The proposed alignment runs off the golf course near the existing corral
and stable area In this area thick bushes and coral outcrops were noted
at the grass covered ground surface Coralline boulders were observed
scattered throughout the jobsite area and mostly near the drainage canal

Bearing Capacity and Settlement
The underlying layers of coral and marine deposits have good to poor

Cavity Probing

line In general if cavities are encountered they may either be
grouted through the probe holes or broken open and backfilled with
granular material
Depending on the size and depth of the cavity below the pipe invert
elevations several methods are available that could be used to
stabilize the cavity or to provide structural support for the sewer
pipe Some of the alternatives are as follows
a Smaller cavities may be grouted through the probe holes
b Cavities near the invert level may be broken open and
backfilled with granular aggregate material

For the vertical piles an allowable lateral pile capacity of 10 kips
per pile may be used with an estimated horizontal deflection of less
than one half of an inch at the top of pile The lateral pile
capacity must be reduced for group action where more than one row of
piles is used In this case the first row of piles may be designed
for the full lateral capacity however the piles in the remaining
rows should utilize a reduced lateral capacity of five 5 kips per
pile
Additional foundation recommendations will be provided when
structural loading data is available
