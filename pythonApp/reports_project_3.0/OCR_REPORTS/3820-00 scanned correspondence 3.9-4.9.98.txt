Discussion and Recommendations
It is unlikely that the cracks are due to expansion or settlement Expansion cracks
are typically in a concentric pattern with an uneven floor slab Unless underlain by a soft
deposit of soil settlement cracks typically occur near load bearing walls and will be
localized as the influence of the load is shallow Also along with slab curling expansion
and settlement cracks are typically identified with differential elevations of the crack
edges
Based on the observations made it seems that the cracks are likely due to plastic
shrinkage and drying shrinkage The cracks may have also been due to heavy
construction equipment running over the slab in a direction perpendicular to Road N
It may also be that the control joints were spaced too far apart
The following are explanations of the two types of shrinkage cracks
Plastic Shrinkage
Plastic shrinkage cracks occur before and soon after the concrete sets It is
caused by a rapid evaporation of water from the concrete surface Cracks
usually form perpendicular to the wind direction The likely factors that lead to
this rapid evaporation are
Wind
e High concrete temperature
Moderate to high air temperature
An effective method to reduce the evaporation rate is to start curing as soon as
possible and avoid wet and hot concrete mixes
Drying Shrinkage
Drying shrinkage cracks occur about 1 to 2 months after the concrete sets It is
caused by drying and cooling of the concrete Not all drying shrinkage cracks
can be eliminated though their location and magnitude can be controlled
An effective method to reduce the amount and rate of shrinkage is the same as
for plastic shrinkage
