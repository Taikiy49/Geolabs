This letter report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the Boatslip Seawall at 525 Portlock Road in the Portlock area of Honolulu
on the island of Oahu Hawaii Our findings are based on our field exploration results of
the laboratory testing and engineering analyses The general location and vicinity of the
project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
Project Considerations
The project site is located at 525 Portlock Road in the Portlock area of Honolulu on
the island of Oahu Hawaii We understand that the seawall of an existing boatslip has
been undermined Based on the information provided we understand that an underwater
survey was conducted by others to evaluate the extent of the void Based on the
underwater survey the void was approximately 15 feet wide along the face of the seawall
by 3 feet deep behind the face of the seawall Therefore a geotechnical engineering
exploration was desired to evaluate the extent of the void observed and the erosion
characteristics of the underlying subsurface materials
Purpose and Scope
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions at the project site to develop a soil rock data set to evaluate the extent of
undermining of the seawall for the formulation of geotechnical engineering

Regional Geology
The island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lavas from two
shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be
middle to late Pliocene in age and Koolau is estimated to be late Pliocene to early
Pleistocene in age When the building of the Waianae and Koolau Volcanoes came to an
end only a slight embayment existed on the southern coast of Oahu
After a long period of cessation of the shield building volcanic activity returned
with the formation of a series of lava flows cinder cones and tuff cones Many of the
eruptions were rather explosive in nature due to the interaction of groundwater with the
rising magma resulting in steam explosions which expelled large quantities of
pyroclastic material predominantly ash and cinder Some explosive eruptions
produced ash that cemented together to form a firm rock known as tuff locally known
as mudrock
The project site is located on the western flank of the Koko Head Crater
Because the Koko Head eruption was hydromagmatic many fragments of coral
limestone and Koolau basalt torn from the underlying corals and rocks by the
explosion are scattered throughout the tuff During periods of higher sea level stands
formation of deltas and fans accumulated terrigenous sediments in the heads of old bays
and lagoonal marine sediments in the quiet waters protected by fringing reefs These
sediments overlie the tuff formation found at greater depths Land development and
reclamation projects within the last century have brought the project site to its present
form
Existing Site Conditions
The project site is located in the Portlock area of Honolulu on the island of Oahu
Hawaii The subject property lies on the makai side of Portlock Road The boatslip is
located northwest of Portlock Road as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The area within
the boatslip is sheltered from ocean currents and waves by a series of dikes built off
shore Therefore the water at the boat launch is generally calm and nearly stagnant
We understand that a significant void was recently found under a section of the
boatslip seawall during an underwater survey to evaluate the condition of the seawall
Based on the underwater survey we understand that a void of approximately 15 feet wide
along the face of the seawall by 3 feet deep behind the face of the seawall was observed
It has been speculated that propellers of boats mooring in the boatslip may have come
close to or in contact with the seawall facing causing the development of a void by
means of undermining the soils below the seawall foundation

Field Exploration
A field exploration consisting of drilling two borings was conducted on October 23
and 24 1997 The two borings designated as and 2 were drilled to
depths of about 15 and 16 feet below the existing ground surface at the approximate
locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were drilled using portable drilling
equipment
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our Engineer who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by
testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified
Soil Classification System as shown on Plate 3 Graphic representations of the materials
encountered are provided on the Logs of Borings Plates 4 and 5
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils
sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts needed to drive
the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown on the Logs of
Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Core samples of rock materials encountered at the site were obtained using
diamond core drilling techniques in general accordance with ASTM Standard Practice
D 2113 83 Diamond Core Drilling for Site Investigation Core drilling is a rotary drilling
method which uses a hollow bit to cut into the rock formation The rock material left in
the hollow core of the bit is mechanically recovered for examination and description
Recovery REC is used as a subjective guide to the interpretation of the relative
quality of rock masses Recovery is defined as the actual length of material recovered
from a coring attempt versus the length of the core attempt If 3 7 feet of material is
recovered from a 5 0 foot core run the recovery would be 74 percent and would be
shown on the Logs of Borings as REC 74
The Rock Quality Designation RQD is also a subjective guide to the relative
quality of rock masses RQD is defined as the percentage of the core run that is sound
material in excess of 4 inches in length without any discontinuities discounting any drilling
induced fractures or breaks If 2 5 feet of sound material is recovered from a 5 0 foot
core run the RQD would be 50 percent and would be shown on the Logs of Borings as
RQD 50

Laboratory Tests
Laboratory tests were conducted on selected samples obtained with an emphasis
on moisture content unit weights and grain size distribution Moisture content
ASTM D 2216 and unit weight determinations were performed on selected soil samples
as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties The results of these tests
are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Two sieve analysis tests ASTM C 136 were performed on selected samples of
the soils to evaluate the gradation characteristics of the soils and to aid in soil
classification Results of the tests are presented on Plate 6
Subsurface Conditions
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by drilling and
sampling two borings designated as Boring Nos 1 and 2 to depths of about 15 and
16 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate locations of the borings are
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Our field exploration at the seawall where undermining was observed generally
encountered 3 to 4 inches of concrete slab underlain by loose to medium dense sandy
gravels and gravelly sands to about 12 feet below the existing ground surface A tuff
formation was encountered from about 12 feet to the maximum depth explored of about
16 feet below the existing ground surface
The borings did not appear to have encountered significant voids at the locations
drilled The borings designated as Boring Nos 1 and 2 were setback about 5 5 and
7 5 feet from the edge of the seawall respectively It appears that the large void found at
the face of the seawall does not extend back to where the two borings were drilled
Groundwater level was encountered in Boring Nos 1 and 2 at about 5 feet below
the existing ground surface during our field exploration It should be noted that the water
levels at the project site may be influenced by seasonal precipitation tides storm surge
conditions and other factors
Detailed descriptions of the materials encountered in the borings are presented on

Discussions and Recommendations
Our field exploration was directed at attempting to find the vertical and lateral
extent of the observed void and evaluating the erosion characteristics of the underlying
materials Based on our field exploration the large void observed at the face of the
seawall did not appear to extend back to where the borings were drilled It is our belief
that the void observed should not cause loss of foundation integrity of the structure
supported by the soils behind the boatslip seawall provided that the void is backfilled as
recommended herein
Materials consisting of loose granular soils such as sands and gravels are highly
susceptible to erosion The subsurface conditions encountered in the borings suggest
that the underlying loose granular materials at the boatslip area are potentially erodible
Therefore the observed void should be backfilled with sand cement grout injected at low
to moderate pressures by tremie concrete methods We recommend that a
low strength sand cement grout mixture with a slump range of 6 to 9 inches be utilized
for the grouting operations The sand cement grout should be placed by tremie
methods such that the entire void is backfilled
In addition we recommend that the face of the seawall be extended vertically
down to the tuff formation non erodible surface found at a depth of about 12 feet
below the boatsiip deck surface In addition the face of the seawall should have a
minimum thickness of 12 inches These requirements are intended to reduce the
potential for future undermining of the seawall area To better visualize the
recommendations presented please refer to the Schematic Section at the Boatslip
Seawall Plate 7
We understand that the underwater survey conducted recently did not show signs
of undermining at other areas of the seawall Although the void was concentrated only in
one area at the time of our field exploration the Owner should be made aware of the
potential for future undermining of the seawall beyond the area where grouting is
recommended may occur We recommend that periodic inspection of the seawall be
made to evaluate the conditions and potential undermining of the boatslip seawall
Detection of undermining at early stages may reduce stabilization costs and risks
associated with foundation integrity
