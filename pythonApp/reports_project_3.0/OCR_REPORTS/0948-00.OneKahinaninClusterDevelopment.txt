SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed development site is located on the southwestern
side of Mokapu Saddle Road at the intersection with Kahinani
Place in Kaneohe Oahu Hawaii The site is bordered by
Mokapu Saddle Road on the northeast Kahinani Place on the
northwest and similar undeveloped land around the remainder
of the site
The site is situated on the lower elevations of a ridge formed
from the Koolau mountain range It is generally hilly and
Slopes down toward the north at gradients of up to 3 1 H V

A steep sided gully crosses the western portion of the site
generally heading in a north south direction Steep slopes
as much as 1 1 H V were noted along the gully banks The
gully was dry during the time of the investigation Steep
boundary for the construction of Mokapu Saddle Road Severe
erosion was noted on these over steepened slopes
An existing winding concrete driveway about 14 feet wide and
250 feet long extended from Kahinani Place up into the pro
perty A steep cut slope about 1H 1V approximately 15 feet
high was noted adjacent to the concrete driveway showing erosion
taking place on this over steepened slope Also existing was a
remnant concrete slab foundation from a previous house noted
near the end of the existing concrete driveway
Ground elevations throughout the site varied from about 70 feet
at Kahinani Place up to 196 feet at the southeastern corner
of the site
The vegetation at the site consisted of grass and a moderate
to heavy growth of Keawe and Guava trees Larger mango plum
paperbark and monkey pod trees also grew throughout the site

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The island of Oahu was formed by two major volcanoes the
Waianae and Koolau Shield The project site is situated on
the windward side of the Koolau Range near the ancient caldera
of the volcanoes Numerous dikes are present in this area and
are visible in the highway cuts and in exposed portions of
the site
The shallow surface conditions at the site basically consists
of residual soil formed by in place weathering and decomposi
tion of the lava rock formation
Basalt outcrops were noted in portions of the gully where
erosion has removed the surface soils
Some amount of surface colluvium were noted along localized
portions of the gully side slopes and in a drainageway entering
the gully area from beyond the project limits These colluvial
deposits were formed by weathering and deposition of the upper
residual soils
Subsurface Conditions
Five borings were drilled at the site to depths of about
17 5 to 21 5 feet to explore the subsurface soil conditions
and to obtain samples for laboratory testing Due to the
inaccessibility of the western portion of the site across
the existing steep gully hand probings were utilized to
evaluate the soils in these steep areas

clayey silt residual soils Weathered rock was encountered

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration disclosed competent foundation bearing
soils at the proposed project site From a soil engineering
point of view the site is suitable for the proposed cluster
development
Shallow foundations can be used for the proposed structures
except where they are situated on steeply sloping areas
particularly near the existing gully
The near surface soils are moderately expansive and will
require special procedures for Slab on grade construction
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 948 00 P 7
Foundations
Spread or continuous footing foundations are recommended
for the structures on level or gently sloping areas An
allowable bearing value up to 3000 p s f is recommended
for footings bearing on the stiff residual soil and 2500
p s f for footings bearing on compacted fill The above
values are for total dead plus live loads and may be
increased by one third when temporary loads are considered
On level areas the bottom of the above designed footings
should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the outside
finished grade to resist erosion and to reduce the effects
of the moderately expansive upper soils
Drilled Pier
Footings located on or near the tops of steep slopes
will be subject to erosion and slope creep of the
surface soils therefore these footings should be
embedded an additional amount to help resist downhill
movement
Based on the present building layout plans it appears
Situated on steep slopes ranging from about 2 1 H V
to 14 1 H V For the portion of the units situated on

Or next to the steep slopes deeper foundation embedments
are required and therefore drilled pier type foundations
are recommended
The actual number of drilled piers required will depend
on the final grading and structural foundation layout for
each building
The piers should be drilled to a minimum depth of 4 feet
below the finished ground surface or 6 inches into rock
formation Assuming an 18 inch diameter drilled pier an
allowable end bearing capacity of 8 kips plus a perimeter
shearing resistance of 7 kips per foot of embedment can be
used The top 2 feet of embedment should be neglected in
determining the perimeter shearing resistance
Resistance to lateral loads may be provided by combination
of passive pressure and friction Passive pressure may be
taken as that exerted by a fluid of density equal to 300
p c f Friction may be taken as normal dead load times
a coefficient of friction equal to 0 4
A combination of friction and passive pressure may be used
provided that passive pressure is reduced by one third
Anticipated settlements for foundations bearing on the
natural ground or properly compacted fill are estimated
to be on the order of inch total and inch differential
Surface water should be diverted away from the building
foundations

Cuts and fills generally less than about 8 feet are anti
cipated for the general project site grading Based on the
boring data relatively easy site excavation can be antici
pated except in the deeper utility excavations where weathered
rock may be encountered Rock formation may be encountered
at relatively shallow depths near the existing gully area
The site should be cleared of vegetation and debris then
scarified and recompacted to 90 of its maximum dry density
established by ASTM D 1557
Fills should be placed in 9 inch loose lifts compacted to the
above specified density

Slopes
The on site soils are generally Silty and very erosive as
evidenced by the existing eroded steep cut bank along Mokapu
Saddle Road and along the existing concrete driveway
It is also difficult to mulch or plant these slopes if they
are cut too steep and this will also increase erosion potential
For these reasons 2 horizontal to 1 vertical cut and fill slopes
are recommended for the site
All fill slopes should be track rolled or overfilled and cut
back to obtain a well compacted slope All fill slopes should
be properly keyed and benched as outlined under Site Grading
To minimize erosion all slopes should be planted as soon as
practicable
Basement and Retaining Wall
Retaining walls generally less than 8 feet high and basement
walls up to about 13 feet high are planned for the develop
ment

Well graded granular material such as base course rock
should be used as backfill material behind the walls
Impervious material should be used for the top 2 feet
of the backfill
The recommendations given under Foundations should be
followed for the design of the wall foundations
The walls should be designed to resist lateral earth
pressures as follows
Walls unrestrained at the top 40 p c f equivalent
fluid pressure
Walls restrained at the top 55 p c f equivalent
fluid pressure
Additional loads due to traffic or surcharge
should be included where applicable
A friction factor of 0 4 can be utilized to determine the
Sliding resistance of the wall
Additionally a passive soil resistance of 300 p c f can
be used However the passive resistance should be reduced
by one third when combined with the frictional resistance
A subsurface drainage system should be used to prevent the
build up of hydrostatic pressure behind the walls

Slab On Grade
The laboratory soil tests indicate that the on site soils
have moderate swell potential therefore slab on grade
should have deep perimeter footings extending down a
minimum of 12 inches below the finish grade to reduce
ground movement due to drying and wetting around the
slab
The slab subgrade should be kept moist and protected from
drying prior to concrete placement Subgrade should be
compacted to a minimum of 90 of maximum dry density
It is recommended that slab on grade be placed over a
4 inch layer of base course compacted to 95 of its
maximum dry density
Slab on grade should be separated from foundations
When a slab is located below the outside ground surface a
subdrainage system around the perimeter of the walls is
recommended to relieve seepage water
Roadway Pavement
For the proposed access roadway design a 6 inch concrete
pavement with 6 x galvanized wire mesh over 6 inch
base course can be used over a compacted subgrade The
subgrade should be compacted to obtain 95 of maximum density
