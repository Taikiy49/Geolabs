GEOTECHNICAL ENGINEERING EXPLORATION
COMFORT STATION AT KAWAINUI MODEL AIRPLANE FIELD
Dear Mr Ichiyama
In accordance with our proposal dated March 31 2004 we have performed a
geotechnical engineering exploration for the proposed new comfort station located at the
KawaiNui Model Airplane Field in Kailua on the Island of Oahu Hawaii This letter report
summarizes the findings from our field exploration and laboratory testing and presents our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analyses for the proposed
project These recommendations are intended for design input only and are subject to the
limitations noted at the end of this letter report
Please note that the soil samples recovered during our field exploration remaining
after testing will be stored for a period of two months from the date of this report The
samples will be discarded after that date unless arrangements are made for a longer
sample storage period Please contact our office for alternative sample storage
requirements if appropriate
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed project involves the construction of a new comfort station at the
KawaiNui Model Airplane Field in Kailua on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The planned
improvement involves a precast concrete toilet facility septic tank oump removal system
and two new accessible stalls to the existing 30 parking stalls and one accessible aisle
space and walkway to the restroom Structural details were not available at the time this
letter report was prepared

geotechnical engineering recommendations pertaining to the design for the above project

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located along Kapaa Quarry Road at the KawaiNui Model
Airplane Field in Kailua on the Island of Oahu Hawaii According to the site plan provided
the re striped handicap accessible parking stalls will be located at the southeastern corner

FIELD EXPLORATION
Our field exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling two borings
designated as Boring Nos 1 and 2 extending to depths of approximately 21 5 to 31 5 feet
below the existing ground surface Two additional borings designated as Boring Nos 3
and 4 were also drilled for percolation tests These two borings were drilled to depths of
approximately 4 5 and 5 feet below the existing ground surface The borings were drilled
using a truck mounted drill rig with continuous flight solid stem augers The approximate
locations of the borings drilled at the site are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on the Boring Log Legend Plate 3 Graphic
representations of the materials encountered are provided on the Logs of Borings
Plates 4 through 7
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained from the borings drilled in
general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of
Soils by driving a 3 inch OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling
30 inches In addition some samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel
Sampling of Soils by driving a 2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same
hammer and drop The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third
6 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown as the Penetration Resistance on the
Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths Bulk samples were also collected near
ground surface

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The subsurface information obtained indicates that from a geotechnical engineering
point of view the site is suitable for the proposed comfort station

Percolation Test
Two field percolation tests were performed at the two proposed leach field sites for
this project The field percolation tests were performed in general accordance with the
Wastewater Systems and Chapter 10 of the Ten State s Standards to evaluate the
permeability of the subsurface soils for disposal of wastewater effluent The tests were
performed in 4 inch diameter boreholes drilled to depths of about 4 5 to 5 feet below the
existing ground surface Approximately 1 inch of gravel was then placed at the bottom of
the boreholes to protect the bottom of the boreholes from scouring and sediments During
testing the boreholes were carefully filled with water to a depth of about 6 inches above

recorded to provide data upon which the calculation of the percolation test was based
However as indicated in the test results the two percolation tests could not be
performed for the duration of one hour as required by the standard test procedure due to
the permeable nature of the on site soils During testing 20 gallons of water for Test No 1
and 10 gallons for Test No 2 were used to establish a 6 inch hydraulic head
Based on the percolation tests conducted we recommend a percolation rate of
approximately 10 minutes per inch at the test locations
According to the Manual of Septic Tank Practice prepared by the

the percolation rate obtained at the site would be suitable for the siting of the absorption

Slab On Grade Foundations
We envision that slab on grade foundations with thickened edge footings will likely
be used to support the proposed new toilet facility anticipated for the above project Based
on our field exploration an allowable bearing pressure of up to 2 000 pounds per square
foot psf may be used for the design of shallow foundations bearing on the recompacted
in situ soils or select granular fill material This bearing value is for dead plus live loads
and may be increased by one third 1 3 for transient loads such as those caused by wind
or seismic forces In general thickened edge footings should be embedded a minimum of
12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade The thickened edges of concrete walkway
slabs adjacent to unpaved areas should be embedded at least 6 inches below the lowest
adjacent grade
We recommend that the bottom of footings bear on the firm and unyielding
near surface soils The bottom of the footing excavations should be recompacted to at
least 90 percent relative compaction to provide a relatively firm and unyielding bearing
surface prior to placement of reinforcing steel or concrete If soft and or loose materials
are encountered at the bottom of footing excavations they should be over excavated to
expose firm materials The over excavation should be backfilled with granular material
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction or the bottom of footing
should be extended down to the underlying competent material Relative compaction
refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry
density of the same soil established in accordance with procedures described in
ASTM Test Designation D 1557
If foundations are located next to utility trenches or easements they should be
embedded below a 45 degree imaginary plane extending upward from the bottom edge of
the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility lines This requirement is necessary
to avoid surcharging adjacent below grade structures with additional structural loads and

to reduce the potential for appreciable foundation settlement If foundations are designed
and constructed in accordance with our recommendations we estimate total settlements
to be less than 1 inch with differential settlements on the order of about 0 5 inch
Lateral loads acting on the structures may be resisted by frictional resistance
developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil and by passive earth
pressure acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system A coefficient of
friction of 0 30 may be used for footings bearing on the recompacted in situ soils or select
granular fill materials Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be estimated using
an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth pcf This
assumes that the soil around the footings is well compacted or undisturbed Unless
covered by pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the upper 12 inches of soil
should be neglected
Based on our field exploration the project site is underlain by near surface clayey
materials with low to moderate expansion potential when subjected to moisture
fluctuations To reduce the potential for future distress to the lightly loaded slabs on grade
resulting from shrinking and swelling of the clayey soils due to changes in the moisture
content we recommend that the subgrade soils below the light loaded structure be
properly prepared and capped with at least 6 inches of structural fill material capping fill
below the slab cushion The capping fill should also extend beyond the new slab on grade
footprint a minimum of 24 inches
The non expansive select granular capping fill should be placed as soon as
possible after the subgrade soils are prepared in accordance with the recommendations
presented in the Site Preparation section of this report The prepared capping fill and the
underlying subgrade soils should be wetted and kept moist prior to placement of concrete
Where shrinkage cracks are observed after compaction of the subgrade we recommend
that the soils be thoroughly moistened to close the cracks Saturation and subsequent
yielding of the exposed subgrade due to inclement weather and poor drainage may require
over excavation of the soft areas and replacement with well compacted engineered fill
in generai we recommend that a minimum 4 inch thick layer of cushion fill
consisting of No 3 Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation be placed below the slab to
serve as a Capillary break and to provide more uniform support of the slab To reduce the
potential for moisture infiltration and subsequent damage to floor coverings an impervious
moisture barrier is recommended on top of the No 3 Fine gravel
Areas adjacent to the slab should be backfilled with on site clayey soil tightly
against the slab and graded to divert water away from the slabs and to reduce the
potential for water ponding around the slabs and foundations

Pavement Design
Asphaltic concrete pavements are anticipated for the proposed new parking stalls
Based on the CBR value of the on site natural subgrade material the following pavement
section may be used for preliminary budgeting purposes until the finish subgrade material
is verified and laboratory testing of this material is conducted
Asphaltic Concrete Pavements
2 0 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 0 Inch Aggregate Base Course 95 Percent Relative Compaction
6 0 Inch Aggregate Subbase Course 95 Percent Relative Compaction
14 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness on Moist Compacted Subgrade
The subgrade soil under the pavement area should be moisture conditioned to
about 2 percent above the optimum moisture and compacted to at least 95 percent
relative compaction The subgrade should be kept moist prior to placement of select
borrow subbase and base course Base course and select borrow subbase materials
should consist of crushed basalt aggregate compacted to not less than 95 percent relative
compaction CBR and density tests should be performed on the actual subgrade soils
encountered during construction to confirm the adequacy of the above sections
Aggregate base course and aggregate subbase course materials should meet the
material requirements for Base Course and Subbase Course as specified in Sections 30
and 31 respectively of the Standard Specifications for Public Works Construction
Department of Public Works City and County of Honolulu September 1986 Imported fill
material should be tested for conformance with these recommendations prior to delivery to
the project site for its intended use
Paved areas should be sloped at 2 to 5 percent and drainage gradients should be
maintained to carry surface water off the site Surface water ponding should not be
allowed on the site during and or after construction
Site Preparation
Based on our field observation we anticipate that minimal site grading work on the
order of less than 1 foot will be required for construction of the new comfort station at the
project site In general the area within the contract grading limits should be thoroughly
cleared at the on set of earthwork Vegetation old foundations debris deleterious
materials and other unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of off site
Soft and yielding areas encountered during clearing below areas designated to
receive fill should be over excavated to expose firm natural material and the resulting
excavation should be backfilled with compacted granular fill material The excavated soft

and or organic soils should be properly disposed of off site or used in landscaping areas if
appropriate
After clearing and grubbing areas designated to receive fills should be scarified to
a depth of about 8 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content
and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction
refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry
density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 test procedures
Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the
maximum dry density
Slab subgrades should be kept moist and free of shrinkage cracks until covered by
slab cushion fill and concrete Where shrinkage cracks are observed after preparation of
the subgrade we recommend that the soil be thoroughly moistened to close the cracks
Saturation and subsequent yielding of the exposed subgrade due to inclement weather
and poor drainage may require over excavation of the soft areas and replacement with
compacted granular fill material
In general the on site soils generated from the excavations may be used as a
source of general fill materials However imported fill materials will be necessary for
structural fill capping fill The imported fill materials should consist of non expansive
granular material such as crushed coral basalt or cinder sand The material should be
well graded from coarse to fine with no particles larger than 3 inches in maximum
dimension and should contain between 10 and 30 percent particles passing the No 200
sieve The material should have a laboratory CBR value of 20 or more and should have a
maximum swell of less than 1 percent when tested in accordance with ASTM D 1883
Imported fill materials should be tested by Geolabs for conformance with these
recommendations prior to delivery to the project site for the intended use Imported fill
materials should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture placed in level
lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent
relative compaction
Utility Trench
New utility lines may be required for the proposed project in general granular
bedding consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation is
recommended under the pipes Free draining granular materials such as No 3 Fine
gravel should also be used for the initial trench backfill up to about 12 inches above the
pipes to provide adequate support around the pipes and to reduce the compaction effort of
the backfill thus reducing the possibility of damaging the pipes It is critical to use this
free draining material to reduce the potential for formation of voids below the haunches of
pipes and to provide adequate support for the sides of the pipes Improper trench backfill
could resuit in settlement of the backfill and damage to the pipes

Drainage
The finished grades outside the new comfort station should be sloped to shed water
away from foundations and slabs to reduce the potential for ponding It is also advised
that gutter systems be installed around the building and that the discharge be diverted
away from the foundation and slab areas Excessive landscape watering near the
foundations and slabs should also be avoided Planters next to foundations should also
be avoided or have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the
subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the above
foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation
and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations should be
properly backfilled against the walls or slab edges immediately after setting of the concrete
to reduce the potential for water infiltration Drainage swales should be provided as soon
as possible and should be maintained to drain surface water runoff away from the
foundations and slabs
