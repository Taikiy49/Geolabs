In accordance with our fee proposal dated we have completed the
geotechnical engineering services pertaining to the evaluation of a dewatering system for

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located within the existing Sand Island Wastewater Treatment
Plant WWTP in the Sand Island area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general
location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 It
is proposed to construct a new ultraviolet disinfection facility with an associated effluent
pump station structure as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Based on the information provided we understand that the bottom of the new
UV disinfection facility and effluent pump station structure will vary from about
Elevation 4 0 to 26 feet Mean Sea Level MSL The depth of the excavation required
for construction of the effluent pump station structure is approximately 26 to 34 feet deep
The plan dimension for the effluent pump station structure is approximately 45 by 145 feet
as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
To evaluate the feasibility of a dewatering system consisting of pumping wells for
the relatively deep excavation at the project site a pumping test was designed and
conducted at the effluent pump station site A pumping well 24 inches in diameter and
55 feet in depth was constructed at about the middle of the effluent pump station
excavation with a maximum excavation depth of about 34 feet below the ground surface
Nine observation wells with different lateral distances from the pumping well were installed

FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the pumping test it is evident that the subsurface formation underlying
the effluent pump station structure site is highly permeable Based on an excavation with
plan dimensions of about 45 by 145 feet by about 34 feet deep bottom of excavation
at 26 feet MSL the estimated rate of dewatering for the effluent pump station excavation
would be about 11 000 to With an adequate number of pumps and proper
maintenance of the dewatering system it is conceivable that the excavation may be
dewatered and kept in the dry during construction However the most problematic aspect
of the dewatering is the disposal of the dewatered effluent at the site
Based on the pumping test the infiltration basin of 100 by 100 feet by about 12 feet
deep was not adequate to dispose of an average pumping rate of about 900 gpm of
dewatered effluent over an 18 5 hour period Therefore it is unlikely that using an
infiltration basin would be adequate to dispose of the anticipated volume of dewatered
effluent for the effluent pump station excavation about 11 000 to over a
construction period of about 8 to 10 months In addition the area available on the site for
construction of a large enough infiltration basin for disposal of the dewatered effluent is a
limitation It should be noted that the disposal capacity of the infiltration basin would
diminish with the passage of time due to reduction in the efficiency of the infiltration basin

from clogging of the pore spaces Based on these limitations we believe that dewatering
of the effluent pump station excavation using a pumping well system would not be viable
Based on our field observations during the pumping test relatively clear water was
pumped into the infiltration basin Based on these observations we believe that the
combination of the slot size of the well casing and the filter medium selected worked well
in preventing segregation and clogging of the pumping well However the infiltration basin
did not perform as well as the permeability data suggested It appears that the
near surface layer of gray silty lagoonal deposit present at the site resulted in less than
favorable disposal capacity of the infiltration basin
Based on above findings and discussion the following is a summary of our
recommendations for dewatering at the UV Disinfection Facility project
1 Dewatering of the relatively deep effluent pump station excavation using a
pumping well system is not a viable option for the project Therefore we
recommend that a positive dewatering cut off wall along with a bottom plug
be used to control the infiltration of groundwater into the planned excavation
A steel sheet pile cut off wall with a jet grouted bottom plug or a similar
system should be used to control groundwater infiltration into the planned
excavation
2 Dewatering using pumping wells may be considered for smaller and
shallower excavations such as excavations extending down to about
Elevation 5 to 7 feet MSL For these shallower excavations sheet pile
cut off walls should be used for shoring of the excavations and the sheet
piles should be extended to a tip elevation of about 30 feet MSL to serve as
a positive cut off wall for dewatering considerations
3 As a minimum we recommend that 18 inch or 24 inch diameter well casings
be used for the pumping well casing The pumping well casing should
consist of screens with 0 125 inch slots 125 slot size In addition the
casing should have a minimum of 160 square inches of opening per foot of
slotted casing We also recommend that the borehole for the pumping well
be large enough to accommodate the well casing 18 inch or 24 inch
diameter and a minimum 9 inch thick filter medium around the casing
4 For the subsurface soils at the site we recommend that filter materials
consisting of Basalt S4C gravelly coarse sand produced by Grace Pacific or
equivalent be placed around the casing of the pumping well as the filter
medium The Basalt S4C gravelly coarse sand material had the following

5 To improve the efficiency of the infiltration basin and to prolong the longevity
of the infiltration basin for disposal of the dewatered effluent we recommend
that additional precautionary measures be undertaken to reduce the
potential for clogging of the pore spaces of the subsurface materials As a
minimum we recommend that a cofferdam system be installed within the
infiltration basin and that the dewatered effluent be discharged directly into
the cofferdam system The purpose of the cofferdam is to serve as a
chamber for fine sediments in the dewatered effluent to settle out before
flowing into the larger infiltration basin for infiltration into the subsurface
6 Geotextile fabric and an impervious membrane should be placed on the
slopes of the infiltration basin to reduce the potential for significant erosion of
the infiltration basin slopes In addition the geotextile fabric and impervious
membrane should be keyed into the upper portion of the infiltration basin
