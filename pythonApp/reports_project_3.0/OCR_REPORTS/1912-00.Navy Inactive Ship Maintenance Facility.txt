INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed in support of the proposed moorings design for the
Navy Inactive Ship Maintenance Facility NISMF in Middle Loch Pearl
Harbor Oahu Hawaii
The general location of the project site and its vicinity is shown on
the Project Location Map Plate 1
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this geotechnical exploration was to explore the
sub bottom soil conditions below the water surface at five locations
within Middle Loch to evaluate the general soil characteristics and
strength parameters in support of the design of mooring anchors at NISMF
Pearl Harbor
Evaluation of the thickness geotechnical properties and shear
strength parameters of the upper soft sediment layer was of particular
concern
The scope of our exploration was in general accordance with our
revised proposal dated August 24 1987 for the two 2 initial borings
and our correspondence for the three 3 additional
borings
The main items of work performed were as follows
1 Drilling and sampling five 5 marine borings to depths of about
85 to 124 5 feet below MLIW US Navy Datum MLIW Elevation

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to redesign the fleet moorings currently used by the
Inactive Ship Maintenance Facility in Middle Loch Pearl Harbor
The present design project provides the alternative to use different
anchor types such as piles plates drag and deadweight anchors for the
hurricane moorings
It is our understanding that subsequent to a previous multi sensor
geophysical seismic survey of the seafloor in Middle Loch it was

recommended that soil borings and sampling be performed to evaluate site
specific geotechnical properties and to develop pertinent design soil
parameters of the sub bottom soils to aid in the design selection of the
mooring anchors
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located in Middle loch within the Pearl Harbor
Naval Base on the island of Oahu Hawaii The Loch is bounded by Pearl
City Peninsula to the east and Waipio Peninsula to the west
Presently 33 fleet moorings in the Loch are operated and maintained
by the Navy Public Works Center at Pearl Harbor The moorings are being
used by the NAVSEADET NISMF Pearl Harbor
Based on the May 1987 bathymetric Survey by Pelagos Corporation the
water depth in Middle Loch generally varied from about 20 feet at the
northern end to about 40 feet towards the southern outlet
The borings were located relatively close to the western shore and
primarily in the northern half of the loch where shallower water depths
are encountered The measured water depths at the boring locations ranged
from about 22 to 25 feet below MLIW level
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The geologic conditions underlying the Pearl Harbor lochs are the
results of a complex series of depositional and erosional events brought
on by glacioeustatic sea level changes and post erosional volcanic
activities during the Pleistocene
Following the cessation of activity of the Koolau Volcano alluvial
materials were deposited in an estuarine and deltaic environment in the
area Of Pearl Harbor
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1912 00 Page 5
Dk No 43
The sea level fluctuated several times receding perhaps 300 feet
below and rising about 95 feet above the present sea level
As Oahu sank coral reef grew around the shorelines keeping pace with
the subsidence
Concurrent with a rise in sea level during late Pleistocene era
eruption of the Salt Lake Makalapa member of the Honolulu Volcanic Series
mantled the coral reefs and alluvium with deposits of volcanic ash which
consolidated to form volcanic tuff layers Subsequent erosion has
dissected and thinned the mantles of coral reefs and tuff at the lochs
During the Recent epoch as the sea level rose to its current stand
the Middle Loch area was covered with thick deposits of very soft harbor
sediments of cohesive soils
Field Exploration
The sub bottom conditions at the project site were explored by
drilling and sampling five 1 to 5 at five designated
locations Field vane shear tests were performed in all borings except
Boring No 3 which was drilled primarily to determine the depth to the
stiff alluvium layer at that location
The initial budget permitted drilling of two and 2
Boring No 1 indicated 91 feet of very soft harbor sediments over stiff
alluvium while only 20 feet of very soft harbor sediments were encountered
in Boring No 2 The differences in thickness of the upper soft layer
imply large uncertainty in the design and placement of the proposed
moorings to be located in the large area along the northwestern portion of
Middle Loch

Subsequently Boring No 3 was drilled between Boring Nos 1 and 2
and the very soft harbor sediments were found to be about 85 feet thick
Boring Nos 4 and 5 were subsequently drilled to further delineate
the variation in soft strata thickness at areas south of Boring No 1
towards the southern outlet of Middle Loch About 66 5 and 49 feet of

Subsoil Conditions
The harbor sediments consist primarily of very soft cohesive soils
ranging from clayey silt silty clay to clay with traces of organic
matter sand and shells Laboratory consolidation tests generally
indicated that the soft deposits were normal to very slightly under
consolidated Field vane shear tests and other laboratory strength tests
confirmed that the harbor sediments were in a very soft to soft
consistency
The underlying alluvium consists of stiff to very hard cohesive soils
such as silty clay and clay with traces of sand locally Field strength
tests including Standard Penetration Tests and laboratory triaxial and
direct shear tests indicated that the consistency of the alluvium
generally increased from medium stiff to very hard with depth It appears
that the stiff alluvium soils are overconsolidated

DISCUSSIONS AND FINDINGS
General
Based on our findings it appears that there are two different soil
units which should be considered in terms of critical soil properties and
strength parameters for the proposed moorings design These are the upper
very soft harbor sediments below the midline and the underlying stiff
alluviun
Based on the short term transient nature of anticipated loadings on
the new mooring anchors it is our opinion that the undrained shear
strength of the soil units obtained from the field vane shear tests and
laboratory UU triaxial test results may be appropriate for the mooring
anchor analysis and design Estimated drained Shear strength parameters
are also provided for long term condition analysis These values were
obtained from correlation of the current laboratory test data such as
plasticity indices of the harbor sediments with an established strength
relationship Plate 5 and direct shear test results of the alluvium with
other soil data from Geolabs Hawaii s previous geotechnical studies made
in the general area
Methodology
To determine the geotechnical properties of the two main soil units
laboratory consolidation and Atterberg Limits tests were performed on
selected samples
To evaluate the in situ undrained shear strength parameters of the
soil units field vane shear tests were performed at various intervals in
four borings Laboratory unconsolidated undrained UU triaxial tests and
torvane tests were also performed on selected samples from both the harbor
sediments and alluvium to further evaluate their undrained shear strength
parameters Laboratory strength tests could not be performed on the near
mudline clayey silt sediments which were too sensitive for quality soil
sampling and testing

Direct shear tests were also performed on the stiff alluvium to
evaluate the drained shear strength parameters
Data _ Reduction
Based on the field vane shear tests the uncorrected undisturbed
in situ undrained shear strengths of the soil units were first computed
and then corrected with respect to their plasticity indices P I The
remolded in situ undrained shear strengths of the soil units were also
computed and compared with the uncorrected undisturbed in situ undrained
shear strength parameters to determine the sensitivity of the soils
These computed values are tabulated on Plate B 1 Summary of Field Vane
Shear Test

appropriate ASTM standards and the computed test results are presented in
Appendix C Laboratory Testing
Data _ Correlation and Comparison
Geotechnical Properties
Based on the test results the harbor sediments generally have low to
moderate sensitivity with occasional very sensitive zones near the
mudline as normally anticipated for these types of marine clays
The variation of soil sensitivity with depth may be noted by
comparison of the undisturbed and remolded vane shear test results on
Plate B 2 Summary of Undisturbed and Remolded Field Vane Shear
Tests
The laboratory consolidation tests and Atterberg Limits tests
indicated that the harbor sediments were normally consolidated to
slightly underconsolidated cohesive soils with generally high
plasticity indices The compression ratios are quite high as

normally anticipated for these types of highly compressible soil
deposits This implies that only a very low allowable bearing
capacity value may be used even if a very large foundation
settlement is acceptable
Undrained Shear Strength
The laboratory torvane and triaxial UU test results along with the
corrected field vane shear test results for both soil units in each
of the four borings are shown on Plates B 4 through B 7 and for the
harbor sediments in all four borings combined on Plate B 8
It can be observed from Boring No 1 Plate B 4 that the torvane
test results are lower but correlate quite well with the vane shear
test results up to about 90 feet below MLUW The laboratory UU
triaxial test results for the harbor sediments are generally higher
than the field vane shear test results Plate B 3 These
comparisons indicate that the corrected field vane shear test results
may represent an approximate average estimate of the undrained shear
strength especially in the upper portion of the very soft harbor
sediments Therefore the undrained shear strength parameters of the
sediments within the anticipated embedment depths of the mooring
anchors may be represented by a range of values
The undrained shear strength as a ratio of effective overburden
pressure is derived from the test results for the normally
consolidated harbor sediments as shown on the plastic overlay for
Plate B 8 Summary of Test on Harbor Sediments This ratio also
appears to correlate quite well with the computed undrained shear
strength based on the plasticity indices of the soil unit

For the overconsolidated alluvium either the field vane shear test
results or the laboratory triaxial UU and direct shear test results
may be used However the lower field vane shear test results appear
to be a more reasonable estimate of the undrained shear strength
parameters of the alluvium since the mooring anchors will probably be
installed in the upper portion of the layer where the consistency is
only medium stiff to stiff The higher values should not be used as
the design undrained shear strength of this soil unit unless deep
penetration into the stratum is anticipated
Drained Shear Strength
The drained shear strength for normally consolidated cohesive soils
is assumed to depend on the frictional component only
The drained shear strength of the normally consolidated harbor
sediments may be estimated by correlating the plasticity indices of
the soil unit with an empirical relationship between effective
drained friction angles and plasticity indices of normally
consolidated clays Plate 4
For the overconsolidated alluvium soil data from a previous study
Plate 5 in the vicinity of the project site in Pearl Harbor were
compared with the direct shear test results of the soil unit It was
observed that the direct shear test results correlate well with the
anticipated range of drained shear strength for this overconsolidated
soil unit

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and findings submitted in this report are based in part
upon information obtained from field data points such as borings
Variations of conditions between the field data points may occur and the
nature and extent of these variations may not become evident until
construction If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to
re evaluate the findings given in this report
The location of the field data points were determined by field
surveying with coordinates obtained from VSE Corporation Elevations of
the data points MLLW were determined by reference to Bathymetry NISMF
Site by Pelagos Corporation dated May 1987 The physical location and
elevation of the field data points should be considered accurate only to
the degree implied by the method used

It must be noted that fluctuation of water level about MILIW
frequently occurred during the field exploration due to variations in
tides and other factors Based on our periodic measurements of the water
depths at the boring locations the water level may have fluctuated
approximately by 1 3 feet above to 1 5 feet below MLIW during our field
exploration Therefore the elevations of the soil samples and
stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the field data

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling
five 5 borings to depths of 85 0 to 124 5 feet below the Mean Low Low
Water MLLW surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site
Plan Plate 2 The borings were drilled with raft mounted hydraulic
drill rig Versa Dril1l using rotary wash boring method to advance a
4 inch diameter casing In addition field vane shear tests were
performed during the drilling and sampling Operations The field vane
shear testing is described in further details in Appendix B
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by visual and
textural examination in the field by our engineer or geologist who
continuously monitored the Grilling operations The classifications
were further reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the
laboratory The soils were classified in general conformance with the
Unified Soil Classification System A graphic presentation of the
materials encountered is presented on the Boring Logs Plates 1 1
through 5 3
Soil samples were obtained by driving a 2 0 inch 0 D standard
penetration sampler or a 2 4 inch T D split barrel sampler with a 140
pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts to drive the sampler
the last 12 inches are shown on the Boring Logs at the appropriate
sample depths A 3 0 inch diameter stationary piston sampler was also
used to obtain relatively undisturbed soil samples Hydraulic pressure
was used to advance the shelby tube sampler into the subsoils

The field vane shear tests were performed at various
depths intervals in the two soil units of very soft harbor sediments and
stiff alluvium in Boring 4 and 5 Field test was not
performed in Boring No 3 since the boring was drilled mainly to
determine the depth of the stiff alluvium below the soft harbor
sediments
Shorter test interval was used in the two initial Boring Nos 1 and
2 to obtain more detailed evaluation of the in situ undrained shear
strength profile of the soils The test interval was subsequently
increased in Boring Nos 4 and 5 mainly to obtain additional data to
improve the accuracy of the evaluation
The field vane shear tests were performed using a 5 inch long by
2 5 inch diameter vane which was first pushed into the soil and then
rotated The change of the measured torque with angular rotation was
monitored Maximum torques of both the
conditions were recorded The test results are summarized along with
laboratory moisture contents torvane test and unconsolidated undrained
UU triaxial test results on Plates B 1 through B 8

APPENDIX
Laboratory Testing
The moisture content unit weight and Atterberg Limits
determinations were performed on selected samples as an aid in the
Classification and evaluation of soil properties The results of the
moisture content and unit weight tests are presented on the Boring Logs
at the appropriate sample depths The Atterberg Limits are summarized
on Plate C1
Two 2 consolidation tests were performed on samples obtained from
the very soft harbor sediment layer The purpose of the tests were to
evaluate the consolidation characteristics such as preloading and
compression ratios of the soft soils The test results are presented on
Plates C 2 and C 3
Five 5 unconsolidated undrained UU triaxial tests were
performed on both the selected harbor sediment and alluvium samples
The purpose of the tests was to evaluate the undrained shear strength
parameters of the soils The test results are summarized on Plates C 4
through C 8
Three 3 direct shear tests were performed on selected samples of
the alluvium to aid in the evaluation of their drained shear strength
parameters The test results are summarized on Plate Cc 9
