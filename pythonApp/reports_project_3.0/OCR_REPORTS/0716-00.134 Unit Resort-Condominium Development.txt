INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this investigation is to evaluate the
subsurface conditions and provide preliminary recommen
dations for the design and construction cost estimates
of the proposed resort condominium development on Kuhio
Highway
Grading or drainage plans were not available during our
investigation
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations

W P 2
SITE DESCRIPTION
The site is located on the south eastern side of Kuhio
Highway about 700 feet north of the intersection of
Kuhio Highway and Haleilio Road
The site is bordered by Kuhio Highway on the northwest
houses on the northeast Papaloa Road on the southeast
and Lanikai Street on the southwest
The lot is approximately rectangular in shape with side
dimensions of about 375 and 700 feet
The site is covered with abandoned sugar cane and trees
A drainage easement ts located along the northeastern
boundary of the site An unlined dratnage ditch gene
rally draining northwest to southeast was noted in the
northwestern portion of the easement The southeastern
portion of the ditch appears to have been filled in with
soil and boulders A few feet of water was noted in the
drainage ditch
The site appears to slope down toward the north at about
2 to 5 percent gradients
The northern portion of the site appears to be about 3 to
5 feet lower than Kuhio Highway

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The subsurface exploration generally indicates that com
petent bearing soils are located beneath most of the site
Some recently placed fill or soft material may be located
near the drainage easement along the northeastern boundary
of the site Overexcavation and recompaction building
relocation or other means may be required for Building D
to reduce differential settlements if poorly compacted
fill or soft material is detected
If these procedures are followed the stiff surface soils
should provide adequate support without excessive settle
ment The laboratory tests indicate that the surface soils
appear to have low expansion potential
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P 6
Earthwork Compaction Requirements
It is suspected that some recently placed fill may be
encountered near the drainage easement along the north
eastern boundary of the site Some soft alluvial material
may also be present at the northern corner of the site
see Soil Survey This northeastern area should be proof
rolled with a heavy roller to locate soft spots Any soft
spots encountered should be removed and replaced with select
material
The site should be graded to prevent the ponding of water
particularly in the northern corner of the site and pro
vide positive drainage away from all foundations
The drainage easement should be maintained to drain water
through the site and prevent ponding
Cuts up to about 3 feet and fills up to about 5 feet may
be utilized at the site
Fill should be placed as soon as practicable to allow the
underlying soils to adjust to the new load
Surface vegetation and topsoil should be removed prior to
filling
Select on site material can be used The fill should be
placed in horizontal loose lifts of 8 inches and compacted
to 90 of the maximum dry density established by ASTM Test
Method D 1557
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P 7
Some boulders were encountered in the borings Boulders
may be encountered during site grading particularly in
the deeper utility trenches
Foundation
One end of the proposed 4 story Building D may be under
lain by some recently placed fill To reduce possible
settlements due to loose fill additional field explora
tion should be performed at the building corner or the
building moved away from the northeastern boundary
For the proposed structures spread or continuous footing
foundations are recommended An allowable design bearing
value of 4 000 p s f may be used for footings resting on
the stiff natural soil and 3 000 p s f for footings rest
ing on compacted fill The foundation design should be
re evaluated after more detailed information on the struc
tures is available
The bottom of footings should be placed about 2 feet below
the finish grade
Footings located near utility trenches or drainage ditches
should extend to the bottom of the trench or ditch
Slab On Grade
Slab on grade should be placed over a 4 inch layer of base
course compacted to 95 of its maximum dry density
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P 8
The subgrade should be kept moist and recompacted prior
to placing the base course
To protect against moisture damage of the flooring mat
erial a polyethylene moisture barrier should be used
To reduce differential settlement where the slab is
partly on cut and partly on fill the cut portion should
be overexcavated 2 feet and recompacted to match the fill
density
Pavements
Asphaltic concrete pavements are planned for the driveway
and parking areas
For light traffic and drained subgrade conditions the
pavement design in these areas may be as follows
2 inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 inch Base Course over a
prepared subgrade
8 inch Total Pavement Thickness
This pavement design is based on the test results from S 2
which appears to be representative of the probable subgrade
soils
Additional CBR tests should be performed on the actual sub
grade soil samples at the time of roadway construction and
the above design sections can be revised based on the actual
CBR values of the subgrade soils

Design Review
The plans for foundations site grading and utility con
struction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for
review and comments prior to construction to assure that
the intent of our recommendations is included in the pro
ject plans and specifications
Additional explorations and calculations may be required
when the grading plans and the design of the drainageway
along the northeastern boundary are developed
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the footing excavations be observed
by a soils engineer during construction to see that the
subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings
of our subsurface investigation
The fill under slabs and pavements should be tested to see
that the proper degree of compaction has been achieved
The recommendations given in this report are contingent
upon the above inspections
