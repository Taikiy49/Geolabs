Highway and Kanuku Street in the Pear City area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The
property is approximately rectangular in shape with plan dimensions of about 200 feet by
730 feet The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1
The project consists of the construction of a new retail store structure and on grade
parking area The new retail store will be a two story structure with plan dimensions of
about 155 feet by 178 feet We understand that column loads are estimated to be on the

Based on the findings and recommendations presented in our geotechnical
engineering report entitled Geotechnical Engineering Exploration Best Buy Retail Facility

Healy Tibbitts Builders Inc
Test Pile Program
A test pile program which consisted of driving 10 concrete piles at selected
locations across the building footprint was conducted on The locations of
the test piles are shown on the Pile Identification Plan Plate 2 The purpose of the test
pile program was to establish the driveability of the concrete piles given the subsurface
conditions at the project site the selected driving equipment and methods of concrete pile
installation In addition the test pile program also provided information on the driving
resistance and contours of the load bearing strata across the project site which was used
to estimate the length of the production concrete piles
Prior to the pile driving operations the pile locations were predrilled up to a depth of
about 12 feet below the existing ground surface to facilitate the pile driving operations
through the surface fill crust A Delmag D 30 23 diesel hammer was used to drive the
16 5 inch octagonal precast pre stressed concrete piles test piles Our field
observations indicated that a stroke height of about 8 feet used for the Delmag D 30 23
pile hammer had a maximum rated energy of approximately 54 800 foot pounds per blow
of the hammer
A total of 10 test piles with a pile length of 90 feet were driven to tip elevations
ranging from about 46 25 to 71 feet Mean Sea Level MSL A summary of the test pile
driving conditions and observations is provided on Plate 3 of this report for reference

Based on our field observations and the results of the test pile program we believe
that the driving equipment and methods of installation are acceptable for installing the
specified production piles in general accordance with the project specifications In
addition it is our professional opinion that the test piles have been driven to sufficient
depths and or resistance to provide the design load capacity of 240 kips 120 tons with a
factor of safety of 2 25 For the piles subjected to downdrag loading we believe that the
design load capacity of 114 kips 57 tons may be used
Based on the results of the test pile program we believe that the following
production pile driving criteria should be used for the project The production piles for the
project should be driven with the same hammer delivering a rated energy of approximately
54 800 foot pounds at the end of the pile driving operations The pile driving operation
may be terminated when one of the following driving criteria is satisfied
1 When the aggregate blow counts are at least 60 blows per foot for the last
foot or 6 blows per inch for the last 3 inches
2 When driven to about the full depth of the production pile ordered 80 and
90 feet
3 When requested by the field engineer from Geolabs
It should be noted that the production piles have already been cast by the general
contractor prior to the completion of the static load test Twenty 20 90 foot long piles
and twenty eight 28 80 foot long piles were cast Based on our analyses of the test pile
driving behavior the penetration resistance the static load test and the required design
load capacity we believe that the ordered piles are satisfactory for the remaining
production piles
