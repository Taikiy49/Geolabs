Soil Engineering Services
Lot 3781 Ala Puumalu Street
Moanalua Oahu Hawaii
AS requested we have made a study of the near surface soil
conditions below the above referenced lot The purpose of the study was
to evaluate the suitability of the near surface soils as foundation
support for the proposed construction The study consisted of drilling
and sampling two 2 borings within the proposed building site
performing laboratory soil tests and engineering analysis This report
summarizes our findings and recommendations
Project Consideration
The proposed single family residence consists of a one story wood
structure which measures approximately 50 by 88 feet in plan dimension
and a three car garage
Based on the preliminary plans the house will be set back about 24
feet from Ala Puumalu Street Concrete on grade slab is planned for the
garage floor and driveway Post and beam construction is anticipated
for the proposed house

Existing Site Condition
Lot 3781 is located in the southwestern corner of the subdivision
development on Ala Puumalu Street The existing lot with a total area
of 11 313 square feet slopes downward towards the west at about 6
percent gradient The existing elevation of the lot generally varies
from about 20 to 26 feet
Ground cover within the lot varies from thick grass at the
southeastern corner to very little grass at the remaining part of the
lot
Geology
The project site is located near the southeast flank of Aliamanu
Vent and on the northeast flank of Salt Lake Vent complex of the
Honolulu Volcanic Series
As the molten magma ascended through these vents it reacted with
the groundwater generating steam This steam caused explosive eruptions
which deposited low cones of ash material that have consolidated to form
tuff locally referred to as mudrock
The Aliamanu tuff is an older water laid ash deposit that was laid
down when the sea level was higher than the present The Aliamanu
eruptive event deposited its material over alluvium originating from
Halawa Stream
After the phase of volcanic activity ceased sedimentary deposits
accumulated over the Aliamanu tuff before the next phase began Then
the Salt Lake Volcanic eruptions began when the sea was receding to
about 40 to 60 feet below its present level The Salt Lake eruptive
event deposited its material over earlier tuffs probably from the older
Aliamanu crater eruption

The water that accumulated in the crater was believed to originate
from depressional springs and rain water During periods of little
rainfall the shallow ponded water dried up by evaporation and over many
years formed accumulations of salt
Recently man has developed the crater floor by filling to build up
the area for residential and golf course purposes
The residential lot is probably near the edge of the lake shoreline
where previous filling was done before 1972
Subsurface Conditions
The subsurface conditions at the existing Lot 3781 were explored by
drilling and sampling two 2 borings to depth of 16 feet below the
ground surface The locations of the borings are shown on the Site
Plan Plate l
In general the lot is underlain by 9 to 11 feet of well compacted
fill consisting of dense to very dense silty sand and tuff gravel
Between the bottom of the fill and the bottom of our borings dense
silty sand was encountered
According to the topograhic survey map prepared for the lot two
easements pass underneath the mid western and southern parts of the
lot The inverts of the easements vary from about 7 to as much as 15
feet below the ground surface
Groundwater was not encountered during our exploration

Discussion and Recommendations
The field exploration disclosed that the lot is underlain by
well compacted sandy fill material The laboratory test results on this
material generally indicated that they have low expansion potential
Therefore for this lot conventional design procedures for the
construction of on grade slab and foundation can be adopted
House Foundation
Post and beam construction is anticipated for this project To
support the proposed house we believe that shallow spread footing
foundation bearing on the in situ dense fill material can be used
An allowable bearing pressure of up to 3000 p s f can be used for
the dense fill material All footings should be embedded a minimum of
12 inches below the lowest adjacent outside grade For footings located
on sloping ground sufficient footing embedment should be provided to
obtain a 5 foot minimum horizontal set back distance
The southwestern corner of the house is located about 2 feet from
the existing sewer and drainage easements To minimize the possibility
of footing settlement due to settlement of the trench backfill or
easement trench excavation at a later date for repair purposes we
recommend that the footings in this area be embedded below a H V
plane extending upward from the edge of the easement
Settlement under the design loading is anticipated to be small and
is estimated to be less than one half of an inch total and one fourth of
an inch differential
Slab On Grade
It is recommended that slab on grade be placed over a 4 inch layer
of base course compacted to 95 of its maximum dry density

The subgrade should be kept moist and protected from drying prior
to concrete placement Subgrade should be compacted to a minimum of 90
of maximum dry density In order to protect against moisture damage of
the flooring material a polyethylene moisture barrier should be
incorporated within floor slabs When the slab is located below the
outside ground surface a subdrainage system around the perimeter of the
walls is recommended to relieve seepage water
The thickened edge slab footings can bear on the compacted
non expansive fill The thickened edges should be embedded a minimum of
12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade
Site Grading
The existing terrain at the site generally slopes down towards the
west at about 6 percent gradient The grading work will consist of
minor cut and fill
The area to be filled should be cleared of vegetation prior to
filling All the excavated areas for the footing should be thoroughly
recompacted prior to placement of footing
For backfill material we believe that the on site soil which is
free of expansive components can be used
Fill and backfill should be placed in maximum 8 inch lifts and
compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density established by
ASTM D 1557 78 method
The finished grade outside the slabs pavements and foundations
should be shaped to avoid ponding conditions and to shed water away from
the structure

Design Review
Plans and specifications for foundations site grading and utility
construction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and
written comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the footing excavations be examined by a
soils engineer during construction to see that adequate bearing has been
obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the
findings of our investigation
If imported material are used they should be non expansive tested
and approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
If conditions encountered are different from those presented in
this report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
