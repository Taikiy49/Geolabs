As requested one of our engineers visited the site at 2 00
p m on May 19 1983 and observed the trench excavation at Sta
20 50 along Lines CA 1 and SA 1 in Area C of the subject project
The exposed subsurface conditions consisted of 2 to 3
inches of asphaltic concrete pavement 6 to 8 inches of crushed
basalt base course of dense sand and gravel sized fragments and
about 2 feet of compacted fill of silty sand with rock
fragments underlain by thick brown gray moderately hard dense
massive volcanic tuff The maximum depth of the excavation
noted during the time of our inspection was about 7 feet
For the trench excavation we believe that minimum shoring
will be required under no surcharge conditions Our recommended
earth pressure design diagram for shoring is presented on the
attached Plate C A
During the course of our inspection heavy gantry crane
rails were noted running parallel to and on both sides of the
trench In general we believe that for the soil conditions
disclosed the surcharge loading such as slow moving gantry
crane loads may not significantly affect the shoring provided
that loading falls below a plane 30 degrees from vertical along
the bottom edges of the trench excavation A sketch showing
such a condition is indicated on Plate C B

loadings on the trench shoring would normally require special
analysis Because the impact of the gantry crane load may be
Significant a preliminary evaluation of its influence on the
trench shoring should be made We will be pleased to perform
such an analysis when the loadings and distances from the trench
are known

Consultation Letter No 4
New Compressor Foundations
Existing Power Plant Building 149
Pearl Harbor Naval Base
Pearl Harbor Oahu Hawaii
Pursuant to your request one of our engineers visited the site
on to discuss the possible subsurface exploration
for the new compressor foundations at the existing power plant
Building 149
It is our understanding that the new compressor will be founded
on existing footings However you have noted discrepancies in
the footing sizes and condition from those shown on the plans
therefore design of foundation is to be re evaluated
A visual inspection was made of the exposed soils in a sump pit
at the proposed compressor pad area in Building 149 It was
noted that dense and moderately hard gray brown volcanic tuff
was exposed in the sump pit to about 10 feet below the floor
level Some coral fi11 probably ca part of trench backfill of
about 4 feet thick was noted on the southern side of the sump
pit Shallow perimeter wall footings were exposed and these
showed volcanic tuff located immediately below the footings
A search for existing information was made at the PACDIV Plan
File and this resulted in locating 18 boring s drilved in 1930 s
for the power plant A plan showing the locations of these old
borings and selected portion of logs that are pertinent to the
new compressor area are attached
Hawaiian Dredging Construction
W
Page 2
In general these borings indicate the site to be underlain by
volcanic tuff of 10 to 12 feet underlain further by thick coral
limestone of about 50 feet The upper surface of the volcanic
tuff ranged from elevation of 112 to 114 feet U S Navy Datum
and was covered by 2 to 3 feet of adobe soil
Accordingly we believe that tuff rock will provide excellent
foundation support at this site However in view of
different conditions noted we believe that it would be
prudent to examine the existing footing contact conditions by
taking core samples at each of the footing pad before accepting
these pads These cores should be drilled through the existing
footing pads and at least 3 feet into the underlying volcanic
tuff formation
The drilling should be observed closely by a soils engineer to
detect if any weak material that may have been sandwiched
between the existing footing and the bearing tuff layer

Trench Backfill and Compaction
Areas A and C
Utilities Improvement Project
Pearl Harbor Naval Shipyard
Pearl Harbor Oahu Hawaii
At the request of Mr Frank Watanabe of your office on July 15
1983 we have made a review of the specified trench backfill and
Compaction requirements for the above project This letter presents
our preliminary opinion for modifying the requirements and
recommendations for a testing program to maximize savings of time and
material
It is our understanding that the current requirements for paved
roadway areas are as follows
1 Asphalt concrete A C 3 inches minimum thickness
2 Base course 8 inches minimum thickness 95 relative
compaction
W O 1225 30
Page 2
3 Upper backfill within 30 inches below the bottom of the A C
95 relative compaction The type of material being used
is an imported crusher waste or on site coral
4 Lower backfill 30 inches below A C to 12 inches above the
top of the pipe 90 relative compaction Imported crusher
waste or on site coral material is also being used
5 Bedding material from 12 inches above the pipe to the bottom
of trench 95 of relative compaction
All the percent relative compactions are referred to the ASTM
D 1557 78 maximum dry densities Our discussions and recommendations
presented in this letter will also be referred to that standard
Based on our observations of the trench excavations in Areas A
and C for Lines CA 1 and SW 1 we believe that the following
modifications may be made to the compaction requirements in order to
speed up the trench backfill operation It is our opinion that these
modifications would not affect the overall performance of the trench
backfill
A From the visual observation of the existing material below
the pavements exposed in Areas A and C trenches a high
degree of densification may not be needed to the depth
specified We believe that the depth of the 95 relative
compaction could be reduced from 30 inches to 20 inches below
the bottom of the A C The relative compaction below this
zone may be reduced to 90
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1225 30
Page 3
B
The relative compaction of all bedding including that for
cradle may be reduced from 95 to 90
The backfill material above the bedding 12 inches above the
pipe may also consist of crushed volcanic tuff excavated
from the site The volcanic tuff may first be placed in the
stockpile area and crushed down to smaller sizes by a
bulldozer Larger rock fragments may be bladed off to be
crushed further
The required upper limit of the plasticity index of backfill
material using the on site coral or crushed tuff rocks can be
increased from less than 8 to less than 12
To achieve minimum 95 compaction the maximum particle size
within 20 inches below the A C bottom of the crushed
volcanic tuff fragments may be limited to 1 5 inches A test
section should be made to verify the densification that could
be achieved
We further recommend that a test program be undertaken to verify
the crushing of tuff fragments to smaller particles at the stockpile
area and densification of such a crushed material in a trench A
series of laboratory CBR tests should be performed on the crushed tuff
to examine its subgrade support capacity

The above testing program may be also be applied to other type of
backfill materials such as the existing crusher run type materials
noted in the trench excavation These crusher run type materials

excellent trench backfill material when properly compacted in the
trench
We are now proceeding with a series of laboratory CBR tests on

90 relative compaction We suggest that an area be allocated where
the tuff fragment can be crushed to smaller sizes if too coarse and
stockpiled for later use We will then obtain samples for laboratory
testing to examine its compaction and CBR values
The above recommended modifications to the trench backfilling
work may be extended to other areas if the traffic there are not heavy
and or the existing subgrade conditions are observed to be comparative
with those disclosed in Areas A and C

At your request one of our engineers visited the project site
to visually inspect two existing portal crane rail foundations
in which cracks were noted in Dry Dock 2 Area H
The first crane rail footing examined was located at where Line
STQ 6 inch steam line penetrates through the eastern portal
crane rail footing near the eastern head end of Dry Dock 2
There were a vertical crack in the concrete footing through the
top of the 24 inch horizontally cored hole and two horizontal
cracks along the lower half of the cored hole This portion of
footing may have been constructed in two stages The first
stage was for the central portion of footing directly under the
rail resting on pile foundation and the second stage was for
the winged side portion resting on backfill The backfill
appeared to have settled leaving a gap of about 1 to
inches under the winged portion of the crane rail foundation
No pile was noted under the winged foundation The depth of
concrete above the cored hole was about 12 inches
The cause of the gap under the winged portion of the rail
foundation at this site was probably caused by densification
of the backfill The effect of backfill densification on the
existing piling will depend on the thickness of the backfill

Site should be examined to evaluate the possible downdrag force

0 39 penetrates the footing at the caisson end of Dry Dock 2
A 20 inch hole was cored through an existing vertical cold joint
