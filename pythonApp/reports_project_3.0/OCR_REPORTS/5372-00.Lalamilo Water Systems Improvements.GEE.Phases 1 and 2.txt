Slabs On Grade
We anticipate that concrete slabs on grade will be used for the floors of the new

2 1 Regional Geology
The Island of Hawaii the largest in the Hawaiian Archipelago covers an area of
approximately 4 000 square miles The island was formed by the activity of five shield
volcanoes which include the following Kohala which is long extinct Mauna Kea which
has had activity during recent geologic time Hualalai which last erupted in 1801 and
Mauna Loa and Kilauea both of which are still active
The project site is situated on the northwestern flank of Mauna Kea which has
formed the west central portion of the island The project site appears to be underlain by
basaltic lava flows of the Hamakua Volcanic Series of Mauna Kea which were
deposited during the Pleistocene Epoch Stearns and Macdonald 1946
The Hamakua Volcanic Series are typically composed of basaltic lava flows
which are mantled by a relatively thin layer of palagonitized ash locally referred to as
Pahala Ash The ash soil at the project site differs from Pahala Ash found in other
portions of the Island of Hawaii in that the in situ moisture content is generally lower
and the soil exists in a dry friable state The soils at the site have been subjected to
wind erosion resulting in the formation of a desert pavement on the surface This desert
pavement is a surficial layer of cobbles and gravel which were left behind as the finer
ash was blown away
The lava formation encountered at the site appears to be of both a a and
pahoehoe type flows which spread and ponded as they approached the ocean A a
lava is typically characterized by a porous rough and irregular flow surface resembling
a jagged accumulation of rock fragments including cobbles and boulders Pahoehoe is
characterized by a smooth rope like or billowy surface and an internal structure of
vesicular porous rock
Cavities are commonly encountered in basaltic lavas especially pahoehoe type
lava Cavities are formed when the lava is still in a molten state and represent both lava
tubes intra flow cavities and blisters and pockets inter flow cavities Lava tubes are

2 4 Earthquakes and Seismicity
In general earthquakes that occur throughout the world are caused solely by
shifts in the tectonic plates In contrast earthquake activity in Hawaii is linked primarily
to volcanic activity Therefore earthquake activity in Hawaii generally occurs before or
during volcanic eruptions In addition earthquakes may result from the underground
movement of magma that comes close to the surface but does not erupt The Island of
Hawaii experiences thousands of earthquakes each year but most of the earthquakes
are so small that only instruments can detect the minor earthquakes However some of

2 5 Site Specific Response Spectrum Analysis
In accordance with your request we have performed a seismic hazard analysis
and site response evaluation with respect to seismic design considerations for the
proposed project The purpose of our seismic hazard analysis and site response
evaluation is to establish appropriate design elastic response spectra taking into
consideration the proximity of the site to seismic sources regional seismicity and site
specific subsurface conditions A response spectrum is used by the structural engineer
to perform an analysis of the structure given the dynamic loading conditions and the
ground motions at the foundation elevations
The response spectrum is a graphical representation relating to the maximum
response of a single degree of freedom damped elastic system with different
fundamental periods to dynamic forces The anticipated earthquake ground motions and
dynamic response of the subsurface materials represented by site specific elastic
response spectra
Design Ground Motion
Based on the 1997 Uniform Building Code UBC the Island of Hawaii is located
within Seismic Zone 4 with an associated peak ground acceleration of 0 40g
Alternatively site specific design ground motions may be developed based on a
site specific probabilistic seismic hazard analysis PSHA or determined based on
seismic hazard maps In any case the site specific design ground motions should
be based on a 10 percent probability of exceedance in 50 years This level of
seismic hazard is equivalent to an annual probability of occurrence of 0 002107
events per year or a return period of approximately 475 years

and boulders with varying amounts of volcanic ash materials overlying basalt
formation Based on the subsurface conditions encountered in our field exploration

The elastic response spectra were prepared based on 0 5 and 5 percent damping
The elastic response spectra should be appropriate for scaling and may be scaled

addition to subsurface conditions As previously indicated there are no known

not anticipate seismic events to occur within 5 miles of the project site therefore
we do not expect the site specific elastic response spectrum to be affected by
near source effects from seismic events

compacted base course or subbase Resistance to lateral loads due to passive earth
pressure may be calculated using an equivalent fluid pressure of 450 pounds per square
foot per foot of depth pcf for foundations embedded in the compacted base course or
subbase These values assume the soils around the foundations are well compacted
Unless covered by pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the upper 12 inches

3 2 Foundation Probing
Based on our exploration and experience in the area voids and or cavities
appear to be present in the basaltic lava flows at the project site To reduce the potential
for loss of foundation support resulting from collapse of cavities below foundations we
recommend a program of cavity probing and grouting of the reservoir tank foundations
be implemented during construction Foundation probing and grouting may be omitted
for other minor structures planned at the site due to the light structural loads and
reduced risk of damage due to cavity collapse
SECTION 3 DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
For estimating purposes we recommend probe holes be drilled at 10 foot on
centers for the perimeter ring foundation In addition probe holes should be drilled at
each isolated spread footing location and should also be drilled within the tank floor
area at about 10 foot on center starting at the center of the tank and radiating out at
10 foot radial increments The probe holes should be at least 3 inches in diameter and
should extend to a depth of at least 10 feet below the planned bottom of foundation
If cavities and or voids are encountered or suspected during the probing
operation additional probe holes should be drilled at closer spacings to help delineate
the vertical and lateral extent of the cavity and or void The probe holes and cavities
discovered should be backfilled with Controlled Low Strength Material CLSM with a
slump of about 6 to 9inches The CLSM should be injected at low to moderate
pressures As an alternate to CLSM sand cement grout with a slump of about
6 to 9 inches also may be used for the grouting operations
Because of the potential for encountering cavities and or voids at the site we
recommend unit prices for additional probing and grouting be obtained during bidding
In addition the probe drill should be available on site until the probing and grouting
operations are completed The contractor also should be made aware that a longer lag
time between probing grouting operations and foundation construction might be
required in the construction schedule
The probing and grouting program should be conducted under the observation of
a Geolabs representative This would allow our firm to monitor the presence of cavities

Fills and Backfills
In general we recommend structural fill materials be used to fill the project site from

materials should consist of well graded granular materials 6 inches in largest

Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
Structural fill materials should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 12 inches in
loose thickness moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to at least 95 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to
the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry
density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 Optimum
moisture content is the water content percentage by weight corresponding to the
maximum dry density Compaction should be accomplished by sheepsfoot rollers
vibratory rollers or other types of acceptable compaction equipment
Conventional compaction testing is generally not practical in fills composed of rocks
and or cobbles Instead a testing program to evaluate the number of passes
needed by a compactor to achieve the desired level of compaction should be

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture Content ASTM D 2216 and Unit Weight ASTM D 2937
determinations were performed on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification
and evaluation of soil properties The results of these tests are presented on the Logs of
Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Eight sets of Corrosion tests including Minimum Resistivity
Chloride Content and Sulfate Content EPA 300 were
performed on selected soil samples obtained from our field exploration The pH
Chloride Content and Sulfate Content tests were performed by Oceanic Analytical
Laboratory Inc and Del Mar Analytical The results of these tests are provided on Plate
B 1
Four Unconfined Compression tests ASTM D 2938 were performed on selected
rock core samples to evaluate the unconfined compressive strength of the rock
formation encountered Results of the unconfined compression tests are presented on
Plate B 2
Two Sieve Analysis tests ASTM C 117 C 136 were performed on selected
samples of the soils to evaluate the gradation characteristics of the soils and to aid in
soil classification Graphic presentations of the grain size distributions are provided on
Plate B 3
Two laboratory California Bearing Ratio tests ASTM D 1883 were performed on
bulk samples of the near surface soils to evaluate the pavement support characteristics
of the soils The samples were remolded to near the optimum moisture content to
perform the tests Results of the CBR tests are presented on Plates B 4 and B 5
Two Modified Proctor compaction tests ASTM D 1557 B and ASTM D 1557 C
were performed on bulk samples of the near surface soils to evaluate the dry density
and moisture content relationships The results of the tests are presented on Plates B 6
and B 7 respectively
