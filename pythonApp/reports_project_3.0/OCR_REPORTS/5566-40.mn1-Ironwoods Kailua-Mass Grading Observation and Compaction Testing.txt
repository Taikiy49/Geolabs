This report summarizes our earthwork observation and testing services performed
during the mass grading operations for the site development including building pad
construction and utility installation for Ironwoods at Kailua a multi family apartment
complex project along Kailua Road in Kailua on the Island Oahu Hawaii This report
summarizes our mass grading observations and field testing from February 27 through
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project involves the development of parcels
58 a vacant residential parcel consisting of about 7 2 acres along the west side of Kailua
Road in Kailua on the Island of Oahu Hawaii Plates 1 and 2 The previous buildings at
this property were demolished and the site leveled to make way for a new apartment
complex The new site development scheme of the above parcel includes relatively low
fills for the construction of a multi family residential development including the installation
of utility lines for sewer and water Prior to mass grading the environmental consultants for
this project Tetra Tech EM Inc determined that the near surface soils immediately under
the previous buildings contained chlordane a now banned pesticide that was used to
protect the previous structures against termite infestation To address the environmental
and health concerns resulting from the off site disposal of the pesticide impacted soils
PIS the PIS were buried to a depth under the new buildings from about 2 feet above the
groundwater elevation to a level below the future building foundation and utilities elevation

SUMMARY OF EARTHWORK OPERATIONS
Jayar Construction performed the mass grading operations The initial stages of
site preparation involved decontaminating the site from pesticide impacted soils PIS by
stripping the upper 1 5 foot layer of onsite soils and burying below the building pads
between Elev 2 and 6 ft under approximately 4 5 to 5 5 feet of capping fill In addition
to pesticides the stripped surface soils also contained a very small amount of organic
materials such as decaying grass and grass roots To improve the engineering properties
of the slightly organic soils Portland cement was added and thoroughly mixed on site
before compaction
The building pads receiving the PIS soils namely Buildings IIA and IIC
were excavated to a depth of approximately 7 5 feet below the existing ground surface
Several CMU lined cesspools that were exposed during the excavation of
these building pads were demolished and backfilled with on site materials after
dewatering
The pesticide impacted soils consisting of fine to medium grained sand with traces
of gravel decomposed grass and roots were placed in approximately 8 to 12 inch evenly
spread loose lifts at the bottom of the excavations Portland cement was added into the
first lift at a frequency of about 1 bag for every 10 feet at a rectangular grid pattern over the
entire layer The cement treated lifts were thoroughly scarified several times to the full
depth of the lift to achieve a good sand cement mix consistency prior to compacting until
dense and unyielding with a 13 2 ton operating mass smooth drum roller It should be
noted that only the bottom lift of pesticide impacted soil contained organic materials and
was treated with Portland cement Subsequent lifts of impacted soil without the organics
i e from Elev 3 to 6 ft were not blended with cement
Geolabs performed laboratory triaxial compression tests ASTM D 2850 on the
untreated and cement reconstituted samples of the PIS materials to measure the
comparative improvement in soil strength after treatment The tests indicate a significant
increase in the shear strength of the cement reconstituted soil sample The test results are
shown on the attached Plates 5 1 and 5 2
A layer of geotextile filter fabric was laid over the PIS fills before capping with
general fill General fills consisted of the on site soils that were excavated below the
1 5 foot surface layer of pesticide impacted soils These soils are generally free of organic
contaminants and consist generally of light tan clean fine to medium grained beach sand
with varying amounts of gravel
The general fills were placed in 10 to 12 inch loose lifts moisture conditioned and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density During the mass
grading operations samples of the on site PIS and general fill were obtained and
transported to our laboratory for testing whenever changes in soil properties were
observed The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established

Field density tests were performed during the fill placement operations to confirm
our field observations A total of 157 field density tests were performed using the nuclear
probe method in accordance with ASTM D 6938 Results of the compaction tests

operations Test locations and elevations were estimated by extrapolating from on site
survey stakes and visible site features These locations and elevations should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Fills that did not
meet the specified relative compaction were recompacted until the specified minimum
relative compaction was attained
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the earthwork operations during the site grading of
the above residential parcels observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by
this report were performed in general accordance with the intent of the project plans and
generally accepted geotechnical engineering principles and practices
LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that we observed the grading operations on a part time basis
Therefore some of the earthwork may have been performed without our observation
However the level of earthwork observation and testing services provided appears to
be reasonable in reducing some of the geotechnical risks to the project It should be
recognized that there are variations in the accuracy and statistical repeatability of the
tests and methods used to monitor the earthwork construction and materials
