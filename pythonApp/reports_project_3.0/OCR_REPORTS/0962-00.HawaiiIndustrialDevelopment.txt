INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our soil and foundation
investigation for the proposed Hawaii Industrial Develop
ment to be located on Hanua Street in the Campbell Industrial
Park The general area of the site with
relation to the surroundings is depicted on Plate 1A Map
of Area
The purpose of this investigation was to investigate the
subsurface conditions at the site in relation to the foun
dation requirements and behavior of the underlying materials
under anticipated loading conditions
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed devleopment of the 15 4 acre parcel will consist
of a total of nine precast concrete warehouse structures
with mezzanies On grade asphalt concrete parking would be
provided adjacent to the warehouse structures
Structural and floor loading information is not known at this
time but is believed to be moderate
The proposed site grading plan was not available at the time
of our investigation It is assumed that site grading work
will be minimum

SITE CONDITIONS
Geology
The project site is located west of Honolulu on the edge of
a coastal plain Ewa Plain which consists of a thick hori
zontal series of interbedded marine and non marine sediments
capping the Waianae Basalts The near subsurface structure
at the site consists of a broad elevated ancient coral reef
of highly variable structure and composition
Coral reef deposits as they occur in Hawaii are predomi
nately calcareous algea skeletons which support a relatively
small percentage of coral growth As a reef grows the void
spaces in the growing coral become filled with sand and other
calcareous debris This type of reef formation is varied
and complex consisting of zones of biologically deposited
coral and algae dense recrystallized coralline limestone
variably cemented breccia dune and beach sand and marls
derived from the break up and decay of the reef deposits
The present reef platform at this portion of the Ewa Plain is
apparently a wave cut terrace formed by wave erosion of an
ancient reef which originally may have stood over 25 feet
above sea level As a result of a complex series of
Pleistocene sea level changes the coralline deposits have
GEOLABS HAWAII

become partially cemented and recrystallized at various
depths and places by the action of percolating fresh water
In addition sinkholes and solution cavities are commonly
encountered in these reef formations
Surface Conditions
Most of the site is covered with dry grass and brush Pre
sently no structures exist on the site A 2 inch PVC pipe
crosses the site as shown on Site Plan Plate 1 The site
slopes gently from north to south with ground surface eleva
tions ranging from 19 to 11 feet respectively
The surface materials at the site consist of coral debris
mixed with silty sand and decayed vegetation The coral
outcrop below the debris contains surface solution cavities
some partially filled with coral gravel and sand The
approximate locations of some of the encountered surface
cavities are shown on Site Plan Plate 1
Subsurface
Twenty one 21 borings were drilled at the proposed develop
ment site to explore the subsurface conditions and to obtain
samples for laboratory testing The borings were drilled
with 4 inch auger ranging in depths from 8 to 12 feet below
the existing ground at the approximate locations shown on

Based on the information obtained from the borings and
field reconnaissance the site is generally underlain by an
extensive coral reef structure with associated reef deposits
The presence of loose surface material could indicate that
the site has been graded previously The loose surface ma
terial consists generally of silty sand gravel and debris
ranging in thickness from about 6 to 8 inches
Underlying the surface material a dense to hard cemented
and crystallized coral reef formation was encountered exten
ding to the depths of all the borings drilled
Except for the surface solution cavities noted at the site
during our field exploration no other large subsurface
cavities were detected in our borings However based on
our previous work experience with the adjacent area and the
above observation of surface cavities during our recent
field exploration at the site it should be anticipated that

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration disclosed generally competent coral
formation near the surface which could provide the desired
shallow foundation support to the proposed warehouse struc
tures
Surface cavities are known to be present at the site Some
subsurface cavities may also be located within the dense
coral formation
To reduce the possibility of foundation settlements due to
the presence of near surface cavities and loose sand pockets
GEOLABS HAWAI
W
Page 7
the footing excavations should be probed for these near sur
face cavities
Due to the varying degree of cementation of coral hard
ripping in some areas may be encountered during excavation
Specific recommendations for the design and construction of
foundations are presented in the subsequent sections of this
report
Foundations
For the proposed warehouse structures shallow spread footing
foundations and or continuous wall footings placed on near
surface dense coral formation can be used to transmit the
proposed warehouse loads
An allowable bearing pressure of 5000 psf can be used for
the footings bearing directly on the dense coral formation
The allowable bearing value may be increased up to one third
for temporary loading conditions such as wind and seismic
loads
The footings should have a minimum width of 12 inches and be
embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent
grade and on the above recommended dense coral

Page 8
Surface cavities are present at the site If cavities are
found below the footing excavation the footing should be
carried down to the dense coral below the cavity
Subsurface cavities may also be present in the dense coral
formation below and close to the footing excavation To
reduce the possibility of footing settlement due to these
large cavities located within 5 feet below the footing exca
vations the footing excavations should be probed A pre
liminary test probe layout would include probes at every co
lumn footing location and at 15 feet on centers at the wall
footings The probe holes should have a minimum diameter of
2 inches and should extend 5 feet below the bottom of footing
The probe holes should be cement grouted from the bottom of
the hole using low pressures If the drilling or grouting
operations indicate that a cavity is present additional
probe holes should be made to delineate the horizontal ex
tent of the cavity The probing and grouting operations
should be monitored by a soils engineer to see that these
recommendations are carried out properly
Cavities if encountered below the footing surface should
be excavated cleaned of all the loose material present and
backfilled with compacted granular fill Depending upon the

size and horizontal extent of the cavity encountered lean
concrete may also be used to block and or backfill the
smaller cavity
Footing settlements are anticipated to be minimal less than
44 inch provided our recommendations are followed during
the design and foundation construction
We recommend that all the footing excavations and probing
for cavities be inspected by a soil engineer to confirm
the conditions upon which our recommendations are based
Any loose areas encountered during the excavation should be
removed and replaced with either lean concrete or granular
material compacted to a minimum of 95 percent maximum density
Site Preparation
The site should be cleared of all the vegetaion and debris
prior to foundation excavations and filling
The surface cavities present at the site should be cleaned
of all the debris and backfilled with compacted granular
fill
General site grading plan for the project was not available
during our study However site grading is believed to be
minimal

Earthwork operations at the site will include footing exca
vation and backfilling during construction
The required foundation excavation would extend to shallow
depths It is anticipated that conventional earth moving
equipments generally could be used to conduct all the exca
vations for the foundations However due to varying degree
of cementation present throughout the coral formation re
latively hard ripping may be required in some areas and
during deep utility trench excavations
Loose surface material about 6 to 8 inches is mixed with
miscellaneous debris and should not be used for structural
fill However this material could be stockpiled for land
scaping purposes if required Excavations should be
protected from surface runoff which may enter the excava
tions Loose soils or mud should be removed from the
bottom of all footing excavations prior to concrete place
ment
Backfilling
Some minor backfilling operations would be required around
the footings On site excavated coral broken up to less
than 6 inch sizes or non expansive imported granular
material could be used for backfilling around footings
under slabs and parking if required

It is recommended that the off site borrow if required
should be tested and approved by the soil engineer prior
to transportation to the site to assure that it is suitable
for the proposed construction The granular material should
not generally contain large rock 6 inches or more organic
or deleterious matter
Fill and backfill should be placed in horizontal 8 inch
lifts and compacted to at least 95 percent of maximum dry
density within the limits of structures and 90 percent out
side the structure areas as obtained using ASTM D 1557 70
Modified Proctor as a standard
Floor Slabs
The slab on grade floor can be supported on natural undis
turbed coral or engineered fill or on a combination of
both In any areas where dampness in the floor slab would be
undesirable or where moisture susceptible floor coverings
are to be used a minimum four inches of granular gravel
type material should be placed to act as a capillary break
and provide uniform slab support If moisture sensitive
flooring material is used a plastic membrane could be
used over the granular material to act as a vapor barrier
The membrane should be covered by one inch of sand to protect

FIELD EXPLORATION

by drilling 21 borings utilizing truck mounted equipment
The depths of the borings ranged from 8 0 to 12 0 feet below

soils were classified in accordance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A graphic presentation of the soils
encountered in the borings is presented on the Boring Logs
Plates A 1 through A 21

tration sampler with a 140 pound hammer free falling a dis
tance of 30 inches The blow counts to drive the sampler
the last 12 inches are shown on the logs of borings at the
appropriate sample depths

STANDARD BASE COURSE SPECIFICATIONS
PROPOSED HAWAII INDUSTRIAL DEVELOPMENT
SECTION 31 AGGREGATE BASE COURSE
31 1 DESCRIPTION
This work shall consist of furnishing spreading and
compacting untreated aggregate base course on the prepared
subbase or subgrade or on existing improved surfaces in
conformity with these specifications
31 2 MATERIAL
Aggregate for untreated base shall consist of a crushed
product of stone or coral It shall be free of vegetable
matter and other deleterious substances and shall be of such
nature that it can be compacted readily under watering and
rolling to form a firm stable base
Where the mineral aggregate does not contain sufficient
natural cementing material to be readily compacted a binder
material consisting of rock screenings or other approved
cementaceous material shall be uniformly added to and mixed
into the aggregate before compaction
The crushing shall be so regulated that at least 80 per
cent by weight of the material retained on the No 4 sieve is
crushed particle A crushed particle is defined as one having
at least one mechanically fractured face
When tested in accordance with the designated methods
the aggregate base in combination with the binder material if
used shall meet the requirements as set forth

31 3 DETAILS
A Preparation of Foundation The new subbase
compacted subgrade or re shaped road surface upon which the
aggregate base is to be placed shall be prepared as specified
under other applicable sections of these specifications
B Placing and Compacting Aggregate Base The base
material shall be delivered to the site and spread on the
approved prepared foundation by means of vehicles equipped with
spreading devices The material when spread shall be uniform
in gradation and free from large pockets of segregated parti
cles Segregated material shall be remixed until uniform
Material shall not be stockpiled nor dumped in piles
on the roadbed but shall be spread longitudinally and only in
such quantity to obtain the required thickness After watering
and compacting the completed base shall conform to the required
grade and cross section within the tolerances specified here
inbelow
When the specified thickness of the base is 6 inches
the base shall be constructed in one lift When the specified
thickness is greater than 6 inches the base shall be construc
ted in two or more equal lifts The maximum thickness of each
lift shall not exceed 6 inches
