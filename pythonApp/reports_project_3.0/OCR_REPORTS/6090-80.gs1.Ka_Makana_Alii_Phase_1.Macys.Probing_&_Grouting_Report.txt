Probing and Grouting Operations
A foundation probing and grouting program was implemented for Macy s
Building A for the project The location of Macy s Building A is shown on the Site Plan
Plate 1 Pacific Ground Systems Inc performed the probing and grouting operations in
general conformance with the recommendations presented in our report dated
for the project The purpose of the probing and grouting operations was to
reduce the potential for loss of foundation support resulting from collapse of cavities or
compression of loose zones below the building structure
Swinerton Builders surveyed the footing layout and marked the probe hole locations
prior to the probing and grouting operations In general the probe holes were located at
the wall continuous footing one probe hole at 10 feet on center The approximate probe
hole locations are shown on the Probe Hole Location Plan Plate 2 In general the probe
holes at the new building structure were drilled to a depth of about 15 25 feet below the
existing ground surface corresponding to approximately 10 feet below the design footing
bottom elevations
The probing and grouting operations were accomplished using hollow drill rods with
3 inch diameter drill bits with side discharge holes for water and grout discharge Each
probe hole was advanced by rotary percussion using a TEI 300HT drill mounted on a
Komatsu PC75 excavator Water was injected continuously through the hollow rods while
advancing to the target probe depth Evidence of cavities voids was observed by the loss
of water circulation with rapid receding of water into the drill hole For the probe hole
where cavities voids were not encountered the probe hole was tremie grouted through the
drill rod while the rod was being retracted The grout used for these probe holes generally
consisted of one sack of cement mixed with 7 5 gallons of water with a water cement ratio
of about 0 70
Our field representative observed the probing and grouting operations and recorded
the probe time grout time and approximate grout take A total of six probe holes with an
aggregate length of about 91 5 lineal feet below the existing ground surface were drilled
for the building as summarized in the following table and on Plate 3 1 The probing and
