PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project is located at the northern corner of Ahua and Kahikapu Streets in the
area of Mapunapuna on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of
the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 It is proposed to construct
a four story reinforced concrete storage building
Based on the information from the previous field exploration we understand that the
project site is underlain by a thick deposit of soft compressible clay lagoonal underlain by
varying conditions and depths of Salt Lake Tuff mudrock basaltic gravel cobbles and
boulders or sand and clay layers Based on the generally poor subsoil conditions a deep

elevation is required Due to changes in the pile foundation plan most of the test piles driven
during the test pile program will not be used as production piles and will be abandoned
PILE DRIVING CRITERIA
Based on the results of the test pile program and our field observation of the
production pile driving and analyses the following pile driving criteria were used for the
production pile driving

Each pile should be driven continuously without interruption A pile may be rejected
or its design capacity may be reduced when the driving resistance is interrupted for more
than 4 hours and the pile cannot be driven to the required depth During soft driving such
as when the pile tip is within soft soils the hammer stroke or energy should be carefully
controlled such that significant tensile stresses do not develop within the pile
PRODUCTION PILE DRIVING OBSERVATIONS
At the beginning of the pile foundation installation the pile locations were generally
predrilled to depths of 10 to 30 feet below the existing ground surface to reduce the
potential vibration impacts on the nearby existing structures

A Delmag single acting diesel hammer Model D30 23 mounted on the fixed leads
of a Manitowoc crane was used to drive the 1672 inch octagonal pre cast prestressed
concrete piles The hammer has a rated energy between 33 700 and
66 100 foot pounds Based on our field observations the pile hammer was generally
delivering a driving energy of approximately 33 700 to 66 100 foot pounds during the pile
driving operations The hammer was equipped with an energy level so that driving within
soft loose deposits may be achieved at a reduced energy level to minimize the tensile stress
within the pile which could cause damage to the pile
During the Pile driving operation Pile and 195 were replaced by test
piles designated as Piles 19 and TP 21 because these test piles were located
either in the way or very close to the production piles These test piles were re struck to
evaluate the compressive load capacity of the piles A total of 197 production piles were
driven between August 16 and for the subject project The pile driving
operations were conducted under the near continuous observation by a representative
from our firm During the pile driving operation logs were maintained for each pile which
included information pertaining to pile number date driven predrilled depth blow counts
observations size and length of the piles The locations of the production piles driven are
shown on the Pile Layout Plan Plate 2 The Production Pile Driving Summary is presented
both by pile number and by pile driving date Individual pile driving records were
transmitted to Healy Tibbitts Builders Inc and Nordic Construction Ltd during the course
of the pile driving operations
It is our opinion that the piles for this project have been installed in general
conformance with the project plans and specifications and within the limits of generally
accepted construction practices Based on our observations of hammer performance and
pile driving behavior it is our professional opinion that the piles have been driven to sufficient
depths and or resistance to provide the design vertical compressive load capacity
