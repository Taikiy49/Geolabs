SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed subdivision area covers approximately 475
acres The site is located on the lower south flank of
Aniani Nui Ridge near the eastern base of the Koolau Range
The site is approximately 100 to 1000 feet north of Kumuhau
Street in Waimanalo
The topography of the area is hilly with many small gullies
or drainageways having side slopes of about 2 1 H V tra
versing the proposed road alignment These drainageways
originate on the southern slopes of Aniani Nui Ridge and
drain generally to the south
The site is currently vacant although it may have been used
for the cultivation of sugar cane up until about 1950 The
area is now densely vegetated with Christmas berry java
plum guava and various shrubs and grasses
PROPOSED DEVELOPMENT
The total development consists of 15 large agricultural
lots and a 44 foot wide roadway through the subdivision

Proposed cuts up to about 25 feet and gully fills up to

GEOLOGY
The project site is located in the south central portion of
the former Koolau Shield Volcano near the southern boundary
of the caldera complex During the Tertiary period succes
sive eruptions built a volcanic dome of interbedded Aa and
pahoehoe basalt lava flows to an approximate height of 6000
feet above sea level Macdonald and Abbott 1970
During the eruptive phase of the Koolau Volcano the rocks
in the caldera complex were altered by rising volcanic gases
to easily eroded materials
GEOLABS HAWAIl
Following the eruptive phase at the end of the Tertiary a
long period of erosion began which deeply dissected the
eastern flank of the Koolau Dome During this period of
erosion the rapid coalescence of amphitheatered headed
valleys resulted in the formation of the Koolau Pali As a
result of the headward erosion of these valleys during the
Quaternary period the lower areas were deeply buried by
alluvial and colluvial conglomerates which have now been
weathered to a residual type soil

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The subsurface exploration generally indicates that most of
the site is underlain by stiff alluvial deposits
Some soft soils and high water levels were noted in two of
the drainageway crossings and 9 50 A granular
stabilization layer along with a subdrainage system is
recommended in these areas to stabilize the soft ground con
ditions prior to filling and culvert construction
The natural moisture contents of the on site soils are gen
erally high 40 to 60 Therefore some drying out of the
excavated material may be required to attain the recommended
compaction criteria

Site Grading
In general the site grading will consist of cutting the
upper ridges mostly near to
14 00 to 21400 and Sta 26400 and filling the five major
drainageways to construct the access roadway through the
Site Most of the proposed lot areas will not be graded
Cuts up to 25 feet and fills up to 15 feet are planned
Site Preparation
The site to be graded should be cleared of all vege
tation debris and soft material Prior to filling
the existing ground should be scarified to a minimum
of 6 inches and compacted to a minimum of 85 of its
maximum dry density established by ASTM D 1557 test
method
Conventional grubbing and clearing operations are
anticipated for most of the road alignment except in
the drainageway crossings near and Sta
9 50 Soft surface soils and high water level were
encountered in these two areas therefore the
following special stabilization procedures are recom
mended

Compaction Requirements
Since the project site is located in a high rainfall
area the on site subsoils have in place moisture
contents about 10 to 30 percent above its optimum
moisture content Therefore drying of the excavated
material will be required before using it as fill
Material
It is our opinion that the normal 90 compaction
standards used in other drier areas should be modi
fied to 85 for this project Laboratory shear
strength tests on compacted samples indicate that 85
compaction will be adequate for the roadway embank
ment fill The fills should be placed in 10 inch
maximum compacted lifts
GEOLABS HAWAII

Based on the results of our in place densities
obtained from the soil samples and laboratory maximum
density determination it appears that a shrinkage
factor of 5 to 10 percent should be considered for
the on site cut and fill quantities The in place
dry densities averaged about 74 p c f compared to
79 p c f at 85 of its maximum dry density
If additional off site borrow is required the
material should be tested and approved by the soils
engineer prior to transportation to the site The
material should generally be of low expansion
potential and should not contain large percentages of
boulders organic or deleterious material
Fill Placement
Fills placed where the existing ground is steeper
than 5 horizontal to 1 vertical should be keyed and
benched into the existing firm ground to properly
bond the new fill to the slope For details see
Plate 3 This detail is essential for the stability
of the fills particularly the large fills in the
drainageways The filling operation should start at
the lowest point and continue up in level compacted
layers

Water should be diverted away from the tops of slopes

Culvert Structures
Pipe culverts of and 72 inch diameter plus a
double 16 6 by 6 8 box culvert are planned at the
drainageway crossings
In order to provide sufficient compaction around the culvert
structures and to minimize possible damage to the culvert
structures from the compaction operations the general en
bankment fill should first be placed in the drainageway up
to about 2 feet above the top of the culvert structure A
trench should then be excavated through the fill to the cul
vert invert and the pipe placed in the trench
Trench Backfill
In general the on site soil is clayey and is not
suitable for water tamping Therefore mechanical
tamping would be required if the on site soil is used
as trench backfill Alternatively granular mate
rial such as sand or select borrow could be utilized
with water tamping

Cushion
A six inch cushion layer of 3B Fine gravel is recom
mended below the culvert structures to provide uni
form support
Headwalls
Headwall structures are required at the inlet and
outlet of the culverts
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f is recom
mended for the walls resting on the stiff natural
ground or compacted fill
Well graded granular material such as base course
rock 3B Fine gravel sand or equivalent should be
used as backfill material directly behind the walls
This would reduce the compactive effort required for
the backfill and reduce the build up of additional
lateral pressures on the walls Impervious on site
Silty material should be used for the top 2 feet of
the backfill
