PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
Waonahele Road extends from Princess Drive up to the Girls Road a distance of
about 2 200 linear feet The former road was a winding two lane asphaltic concrete
road The pavement exhibited signs of local distress and aging as well as indications
that surface and subsurface drainage improvements may be necessary for extended
life The pavement was reconstructed under this project generally maintaining the
alignment and surface elevation
A new 6 foot wide concrete sidewalk was added along the left side of the road
There was sufficient space to construct an on grade sidewalk adjacent to the road
pavement along portions of the area However at six locations along the alignment
retaining walls were required due to the steeply sloping terrain adjacent to the road
pavement
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services
pertaining to the site grading operations at the project site

SITE GRADING
The site grading work for the project was observed by Geolabs Inc from July 2002
to November 2002 on an intermittent basis The site grading work consisted of Clearing
and grubbing removal of the old pavement and placement and compaction of minor fills
for the pavement section In isolated retaining wall areas fills up to about 10 feet in
thickness was required to achieve the design finished grades for the project The site
grading work was performed by Goodfellow Brothers Inc
During the site grading operations samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor
and the test results are summarized on the Summary of Optimum Moisture Maximum Dry
Density Relationships Plate 3
The fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as
determined in accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557
During the site grading operations periodic field density tests were performed in
general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 2922 Nuclear Gauge Method
Results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 4 1 through 4 29 of
this report These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on
constructed fills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and
interpolating from the grading plan These locations and elevations should be considered
accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed to be
unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained and or
approved by our field representative based on visual observation and hand probing
Our services consisted of part time field observation of the contractor s fill
placement subgrade preparation and compaction procedures and confirmation of
subgrade soil conditions exposed during the grading operations
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the site grading and utility installation work for the
project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report were
performed in conformance with the general intent of the recommendations in the following
references
