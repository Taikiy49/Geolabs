Dear Mr. Silva: 
This addendum to our report entitled “Geotechnical Engineering Exploration, 
Hauula Subdivision, Hauula, Oahu, Hawaii,” dated November 4, 2020, provides grading 
recommendations for the permanent detention basin planned for the project. 
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered in the borings, it appears that 
high cut slopes planned at the permanent detention basin should generally expose stiff 
silty clay.  These highly plastic clayey soils tend to creep downslope over time and 
should be placed at a flatter slope inclination to reduce the potential for creep.  We 
understand that a future residential structure may be built above the detention basin. 
Based on the above and our analysis, cut slopes and permanent fill slopes of the 
on-site soils for the permanent detention basin may be designed with a slope inclination 
of five horizontal to one vertical (5H:1V) or flatter. Fills placed on slopes should be 
keyed and benched into the existing slope to provide stability for the new fill 
embankment against sliding. The filling operations should start at the lowest point and 
continue up in level horizontal compacted layers in accordance with the above general 
fill placement recommendations.

Fill slopes should be constructed by overfilling and cutting back to the design
slope inclination to obtain a well-compacted slope face. Surface water should be
diverted away from the tops of slopes, and slope planting should be provided as soon
as possible to reduce the potential for erosion of the finished slopes. For slope heights
greater than 20 feet, an 8-foot wide bench should be provided.
For additional site grading recommendations, please refer to our report dated
November 4, 2020.
