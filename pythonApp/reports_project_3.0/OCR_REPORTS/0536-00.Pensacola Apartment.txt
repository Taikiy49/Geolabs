SUMMARY OF RECOMMENDATIONS
The proposed structure can be supported upon
spread or line foundations design for maximum
bearing pressures of 5000 psf bearing a mini
mum of five feet below the existing ground
surface
Utilizing special site preparations consisting
of undercutting compaction and backfilling
the allowable bearing pressure can be increased
to 8000 psf
INTRODUCTION
This report summarizes the findings of our subsurface investigation
and presents recommendations for the design and construction of
the foundations to support the proposed 22 story structure

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is presently planned to construct a 22 story reinforced concrete
high rise condominium approximately 35 by 160 feet in plan A 4
to 5 level parking structure will surround the tower and will
essentially occupy the entire site at the lower level
The maximum vertical column loads for the main building will be on
the order of 300 kips Normal site grading operations will be
required to prepare the site for construction since the maximum
cuts are expected to be in the range of 8 to 12 feet
SITE CONDITIONS
Surface
The present site is rectangular with plan dimensions of approxi
mately 115 by 300 feet Presently the site is occupied by thirteen
one and two story dwellings and with a narrow driveway entrance
in the center Numerous royal palm trees line the driveway The
remaining portion of the site contain scattered fruit trees
The ground surface varies from approximately Elevation 76 at Pensa
cola Street to approximately Elevation 60 feet at the rear property
line

Geology
The proposed building site is underlain by alluvium water
deposited soils and cinders derived from the Tantalus Volcano
and the nearby Punchbowl Volcano It appears that the area has
experienced several sea level changes as well as numerous periods
of active volcanic eruptions
Cinders erupted from Tantalus blanketed the area as recently as
5 000 years ago The cinders were subsequently reworked by wave
action which formed a rounded gap graded black sand zone This
black sand was subsequently overlain by brown silt a recent
alluvium eroded from the nearby mountains by tropical weathering
of the volcanic basalt
Subsurface Conditions
Subsurface conditions at the site were explored utilizing six text
borings drilled at the locations shown on the Site Plan and Boring
Locations Plate 1 A detailed description of the field explora
tion methods and the accompanying laboratory testing is given in
the appendix which also contains the logs of the borings and the
results of the laboratory testing
The subsurface explorations disclosed that the site is blanketed by
a uniformed upper layer of alluvium varying from 16 to 17 feet in
Page 4
W
August 7 1974
thickness The upper alluvium is composed of brown silt with
traces of sand and clay It varies in consistency from very stiff
to hard where cohesive and from medium dense to dense where it is
cohesionless
Beneath the upper alluvium a black sand deposit was encountered
at Elevations 45 in Boring 6 and Elevation 58 in Boring 1 The
black sand varied in thicknesses from 20 to 24 feet and was
generally medium dense to dense The black sand is underlain by
stiff to hard brown silty clay with decomposed rock fragments
which is a residual soil derived from the in place weathering of
the bedrock
Groundwater was only encountered in Boring 3 at a depth of 35 feet
The remaining borings did not encounter groundwater
DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
At the proposed finish floor levels of Elevation 74 for the upper
section and Elevation 66 for the lower portion the foundations
will be bearing within the upper alluvium The upper five feet of
this material cannot provide adequate support for the proposed
structures Foundations must therefore bear a minimum of five
feet below the existing ground surface where they may be designed
for maximum contact bearing pressures of 5000 psf For this

bearing pressure the estimated settlements are 1 inch total and
4 inch differential
It is our understanding that the anticipated column loads are in
the order of 300 kips which will result in relatively large spread
foundations if designed for 5000 psf In lieu of the recommended
5000 psf additional site preparation can be utilized to increase
the allowable contract bearing pressure to 8000 psf
In order to utilize this higher value the foundations must bear a
minimum of 5 feet below the outside ground surface and a minimum
of 3 feet below the interior finished floor level In addition to
these requirements the entire building area must be undercut 4
feet and the exposed surface subjected to 15 passes of a 10 ton
vibratory roller prior to backfilling to the proposed foundation
bearing level The vibratory compactor must deliver a total
impact loading in excess of 45 000 foot pounds Following the
compaction of the exposed surface the area should be backfilled
utilizing the on site soils compacted to a minimum of 95 percent
of the maximum dry density established by The pro
posed foundation construction is illustrated on Plate 2
This method of construction creates a compacted mat having a thick
ness equal to the 4 feet of backfill plus an additional 5 feet of
soil compacted in place Foundations resting upon this nine feet

Foundations constructed upon the compacted mat can expect total

It is essential for the utilization of this method that the proof
rolling and compaction operations be closely supervised by a soils

The upper foundations in the transition zone between the two floor

pressures to the retaining wall and lower foundations It is
recommended that the 45 degree angle of draw be maintained from
the edge of the upper foundation and the base of the retaining wall
for the lower section

for an equivalent fluid pressure of 35 pcf if the on site soils
are utilized as backfill The backfill should be compacted to a
minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density determined by
ASTM Test Designation D 1557 72T A fictional factor of 0 55
should be utilized to determine the sliding resistance of the
retaining wall foundations
It is recommended a drain be placed outside of the retaining wall
to prevent the build up of hydrostatic pressure Groundwater was
not encountered during the subsurface explorations but due to the
granular nature of the materials some accumalation of water can
be expected during periods of high precipitation
Floor Slabs and Paving
No expansive soils were encountered during the subsurface explora
tions and slab on grade construction can be utilized providing
that the floor slabs are divorced from the load bearing members
It is recommended that the subgrade be compacted to 90 percent of
the maximum dry density as determined by ASTM D 1557 72T prior to
the placement of the slab on grade This will provide relatively
uniform slab support
Slab on grade to be provided with floor coverings should be pro
tected by a plastic vapor barrior This barrior should be covered
by a thin layer of sand about one inch thick to prevent puncture

APPENDIX
FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling
six borings utilizing truck mounted equipment The depths of the
borings ranged from 25 to 75 feet below the existing ground sur
face The locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan
included in the text of this report
The soils were classified by visual and textural examinations in
the field by our geologist who continuously supervised the
drilling operations The classifications were verified by visual
inspection and testing in the laboratory All soils were classi
fied in accordance with the Unified Soil Classification System A
graphical representation of the soils encountered is presented on
the Boring Logs Plates A 1 thru A 6
Soil samples were obtained by driving either a 2 4 inch I D
diameter or a standard penetration sampler with a 140 pound hammer
free falling a distance of 30 inches The blow counts to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the Log of Borings at the
appropriate sample depths
