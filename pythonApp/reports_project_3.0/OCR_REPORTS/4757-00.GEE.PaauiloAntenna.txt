This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the new monopole antenna by Crown Castle GT for Verizon Wireless at the
Paaulio site along Hauola Road on the Island of Hawaii The general location and vicinity
of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering
recommendations resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering
analyses These recommendations are intended for the design of foundations and site
preparation only The findings and recommendations presented herein are subject to the
limitations noted at the end of this report
Project Considerations
The project involves the construction of a 168 foot high monopole antenna for
Verizon Wireless at a new facility along Hauola Road in the Paauilo area on the Island
of Hawaii A new one story building paved access driveway and parking area are also
planned Additional buildings for other carriers are planned for the future but are not
included in this phase of work
Purpose and Scope
The purpose of our geotechnical engineering exploration for the subject project
was to obtain an overview of the subsurface conditions at the site to develop a subsurface
data set for the formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations pertaining to the
design of the antenna foundations Our work was performed in general accordance with
our fee proposal dated Our scope of work included the following tasks and
work efforts

Discussion and Recommendations
Based on our field exploration the project site is generally underlain by about
15 feet of fill and saprolite over highly weathered basaltic rock Based on the subsoil
conditions encountered at the project site we believe that a deep footing type foundation
may be used to support the monopole antenna Shallow spread footings or mat
foundation bearing on the recompacted fill or compacted structural fill material may be
used to support the proposed control building
Detailed discussions and recommendations for design of foundations site
grading and other geotechnical aspects of the project are presented in the following
sections of this report
Antenna Foundations
It is our understanding that the monopole foundations typically consist of large
10 to 15 foot diameter by 20 to 30 foot deep foundation systems Lateral loads acting on
the structure may be resisted by passive earth pressure acting against the near vertical
faces of the foundation system and by frictional resistance and adhesion developed
between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil Resistance due to passive
earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 pounds per

Building Foundations
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered at the project site we recommend
that shallow foundations consisting of spread and or continuous footings be used to
support the proposed control building at the site An allowable bearing pressure of up to
may be used to design the new footings bearing on compacted structural fill
transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic forces
In general footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grade Footings constructed near the tops of slopes or on slopes should
be embedded deep enough such that the horizontal distance measured from the outside
edge at the base of the footing to the slope face is not less than 6 feet Foundations
located next to utility trenches or easements should be embedded below a one horizontal
to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane extending upward from the bottom edge of the
utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility lines This requirement is necessary to
avoid surcharging adjacent below grade structures with additional structural loads and to
reduce the potential for appreciable foundation settlement

Soft and or loose materials encountered at footing subgrade level should be

Slabs On Grade
For concrete slabs on grade not subjected to vehicular traffic we recommend that
a minimum 4 inch thick layer of cushion fill consisting of No 3 Fine gravel ASTM C 33
No 67 gradation be placed below the slabs to serve as a capillary break and to provide
more uniform support of the slabs To reduce moisture infiltration and subsequent
damage to floor coverings an impervious moisture barrier is recommended on top of the
No 3 Fine gravel Two inches of moist sand may be considered above the moisture
barrier to provide protection of the moisture barrier and to aid in curing of the slab
concrete Provisions of the 2 inches of moist sand above the moisture barrier will be left
up to the discretion of the project structural engineer Flexible floor coverings such as
carpet or sheet vinyl should be considered since they can better mask minor slab
cracking
Exterior concrete walkways may be required for the proposed project We
recommend that concrete walkways be supported on 4 inches of non expansive select
granular fill The select granular fill should be compacted to at least 90 percent relative
compaction Crack control joints should be provided at intervals equal to the width of the
walkways with expansion joints at right angle intersections Areas adjacent to the slab
should be backfilled tightly against the slab with on site soil and graded to divert water
away from the slab to reduce the potential for water ponding around the foundations

Grading
Based on the existing topography we envision that the site grading work for the
proposed project will generally consist of minor cuts and fills
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared Debris rubbish and other unsuitable materials should be removed
and disposed of off site Soft and yielding areas encountered during clearing below
areas designated to receive fill or future improvements should be over excavated to
expose firm material and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with
well compacted engineered fill The excavated soft and or organic soils should be
properly disposed of off site Contract documents should include additive and deductive
unit prices for over excavation and engineered fill placement to account for variations in
the over excavation quantities
The materials generated from the cut areas may be reused as a source of general
fill material provided that they are free of vegetation deleterious materials and rock
fragments greater than 6 inches in maximum dimension If required import fill should
consist of non expansive select granular material such as crushed basalt or coral The
material should be well graded from coarse to fine with no particles larger than 3 inches in
largest dimension and should contain between 10 and 30 percent particles passing the
No 200 sieve The material should have a laboratory CBR value of 20 or more and
should have a maximum swell of less than 1 percent Imported fill material should be
tested by Geolabs for conformance with these recommendations prior to delivery to the
project site for its intended use
Fill materials should be moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the
optimum moisture content placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness
and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction
refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry
density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 test procedures
Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the
maximum dry density
Compaction should be accomplished by sheepsfoot rollers vibratory rollers or
other types of acceptable compaction equipment Water tamping jetting or ponding
should not be allowed to compact the on site soils

Utility Trench
We envision that new utility connections may be required for the proposed project
In general for support of the utility lines we recommend that a granular bedding consisting
of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation be used under the pipes
Free draining granular materials such as No 3BFine gravel
No 67 gradation should also be used for the trench backfill up to about 12 inches above
the pipes to provide adequate support around the pipes and to reduce the compaction
effort of the backfill thus reducing the possibility of damaging the pipes It is critical to use
this free draining material to reduce the potential for formation of voids below the
haunches of pipes and to provide adequate support for the sides of the pipes Improper
trench backfill could result in settlement of the backfill and damage to the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipes to
the top of the subgrade or finished grade may consist of select fill material The backfill
should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture placed in maximum
8 inch level loose lifts and mechanically compacted to not less than 90 percent relative
compaction to reduce the potential for future ground subsidence Where trenches are
below pavement areas the upper 3 feet of the trench backfill below the pavement finish
grades should be compacted to at least 95 percent relative compaction
Drainage
The finished grades outside the structures should be sloped to shed water away
from foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also advised that gutter
systems be installed around the structures and that the discharge be diverted away from
the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near the foundations
and slabs should also be avoided Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have
concrete bottoms and drains to reduce the potential for water infiltration into the subsoils
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and maintained to drain surface
run off away from the slabs and foundations
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the above
foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation
and subsequent loss of strength and or heaving of the near surface soils The foundation
excavations should be properly backfilled against the footings immediately after setting of
the concrete to reduce the potential for water infiltration
