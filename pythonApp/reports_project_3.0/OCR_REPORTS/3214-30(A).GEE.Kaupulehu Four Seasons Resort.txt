INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering reconnaissance
and exploration performed for the proposed Kaupulehu Four Seasons Resort in the
District of North Kona on the island of Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the
project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings from our field reconnaissance and
exploration and presents our geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from
our analyses pertinent to the mass grading and foundation design at the subject site
These recommendations are intended for preliminary design input only and may not be
appropriate to form the basis for construction cost estimates nor for construction bidding
purposes

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Hawaii the largest in the Hawaiian Archipelago covers an area of
approximately 4 000 square miles The island was formed by the activity of five shield
volcanoes These are Kohala which is long extinct Mauna Kea which has had activity
during recent geologic times Hualalai which last erupted in 1801 and Mauna Loa and
Kilauea which are both still active
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3214 30 A Page 7
The project site is located on the northwestern flank of Hualalai mountain which
has formed the west central portion of the island and consists of the prehistoric basaltic
lava flows of the Hualalai Voicanic Series The prehistoric flows comprising Mount
Hualalai were most likely erupted during the late Pleistocene Epoch Stearns and
MacDonald 1946 In contrast the single historic eruption of Hualalai took place in
about 1800 or 1801 The lava flows associated with this eruptive event are located to
the north Kaupulehu Lava Flows and south unnamed flows of 1801 of the proposed
resort development However some portions of the proposed residential development
may be located on or within erupted volcanics associated with the 1801 eruptions
Hawaiian volcanic activity is typified by an abundance of flank eruptions along
_ rift zones or zones of weakness within the earth s crust The project site is situated just
to the north of the northwest rift zone of Hualalai
The project site is situated on the northwestern flank of Hualalai Mountain which
makes up the west central part of the island The native basalt formation observed at
portions of the site appear to be of pahoehoe lava which spread and ponded as it
approached the ocean Pahoehoe is characterized by a smooth rope like or billowy
surface and an internal structure of vesicular porous rock Cavities are commonly
encountered within pahoehoe lavas Most cavities are formed when the lava remains
in a molten state and may represent both lava tubes intra flow cavities and blisters
and pockets inter flow cavities Lava tubes intra flow cavities are formed when
molten lava drains from the cooling flow leaving a hollow tube like structure which may
extend for a large distance longitudinally along the flow Inter flow cavities are
generally localized and smaller in horizontal extent
Basalt formations are considered to be relatively permeable rock and can
transmit water quite readily in both the horizontal and vertical directions Water is
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3214 30 A Page 8
transmitted through the porous rock matrix along joints fractures and inter flow
contacts cavities and along clinker layers The permeability of the clinker and cavities
is high therefore they serve as the major water transmission features
SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed Four Seasons Resort development is located to the southwest of
the existing Kona Village Resort near the coastline in Kaupulehu North Kona on the
island of Hawaii The site is bounded to the northwest by the Pacific Ocean and to the
west south and east by the proposed Kaupulehu residential subdivisions and golf
course
A field reconnaissance was conducted to map and evaluate features such as
the exposed native basalt formation beach sand deposits old fills pit excavations
stockpiles and the existence of potential lava tubes The undisturbed basalt formation
observed at portions of the site was mapped as Quaternary
The exposed basalt formation consists of slightly weathered hard brownish gray
pahoehoe basalt forming relatively smooth and level ground surfaces
The beach sand deposits located near and along the shoreline were mapped as
Recent Sand Rs These sand deposits occur as very loose beach sand derived from
shoreline processes and deposited by wind and wave action Some of the beach sand
appeared to have been artificially placed and spread The remaining surface area had
undergone some previous grading including cuts and fills and was mapped as Recent
Fill Rf on the Site Plan Plate 2

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our subsurface exploration consisted of the excavation logging and backfilling
of 63 test pits located in previously graded areas throughout the site At the direction
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3214 30 A Page 10
of Kaupulehu Land Company the test pits were confined to areas which had been
disturbed by past grading efforts in order to reduce the impact on the undisturbed lava
flows and features The test pits were excavated to confirm the extent character and
depth of the existing on site fill and beach sand deposits The test pit locations were
measured from reference markers numbered 1 through 18 which were surveyed and
staked in the field The approximate locations of the test pits and reference markers
are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general our field exploration encountered fills ranging from less than 1 to
greater than 9 feet which is the maximum depth explored The fills encountered
generally consist of well graded sandy gravel material with some basalt cobbles and
boulders of about 15 inches or less in size Within the rubble fill area outlined on the
Site Plan Plate 2 the excavated materials generally consist of a thin surficial cover
of sandy gravels underlain by sandy gravels with some concrete basalt cobbles and
boulders and steel rebar Solid concrete footings were buried in place and were
encountered at shallow depths in the test pits located within the rubble fill area Based
on the test pit data the rubble fills appear to be medium dense to dense and relatively
tight with sufficient fines Significant void spaces were not noted in the rubble fills
exposed in the test pits Where penetrated the underlying basalt rock formation
appeared to be hard to very hard Voids or lava tubes in the basalt rock formation were
not encountered in the test pits during our subsurface exploration However voids and
cavities are common in the basalt formation at the site
Groundwater was encountered in Test Pit No 2 at a depth of about 3 to 3 feet
below the existing ground surface The groundwater is believed to be associated with
the nearby anchialine pond and due to the close proximity to the shoreline and sea
level elevation Groundwater was also observed to be present in the existing
excavated pit located near the future golf clubhouse and within the natural anchialine

and for footings bearing on compacted fill where the fill thickness is less than 5 feet
between the bottom of footing and the basalt formation Generally the probing and
grouting program is required specifically for the golf clubhouse Building B and the
ballroom building Building C

footing location and on 20 foot centers for continuous footings The requirement for

diameter and should extend to at least 10 feet below the planned bottom of footing

vertical and lateral extent of the cavity and or void The probe holes and any cavities

pressures A typical sand cement grout would consist of 1 part sand 1 part cement
