SITE CONDITIONS
The subject site is situated within the boundaries of the
present Molokai Airport west of the fire station and north
of the two 2 existing wind direction and wind velocity towers
These towers are north of the present east west runway and taxi
way
The site is flat and heavily grass weed covered The four 4
property corner survey control hubs had been staked out and
were in place at the time of our field exploration
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
Molokai was originally formed by the junction of two separate
volcanoes Later either a eustatic rise in sea level or
submergence of the islands caused the area between the two
mountains the present Hoolehua Plain to be inundated by
the sea The project site is located on the Hoolehua Plain
The island subsequently emerged although with several inter
vening periods of partial emergence and submergence Sub
aerial erosion of the emerging East and West Molokai Volcanoes
and subsequent deposition of the clastic debris as coalescing
alluvial fans formed the present configuration of the Hoolehua
Plain

Late Pleistocene to recent weathering has produced a residual
lateritic soil on the thin veneer of sediment over highly
weathered basalt bedrock underlying the site
FIELD EXPLORATION
Our field exploration consisted of drilling a total of four 4
4 inch diameter auger drilled borings and one test pit
In the borings soil samples were taken at about every
s foot intervals and or every soil change A falling head
percolation test was made in Bore Hole No 2 after an over
night saturation No groundwater was encountered in any of
the borings

SOIL CONDITIONS
Based upon borings the dominant soil type consists of clayey
Silt soils which can be classified as CL ML according to
the Unified Soil Classification System These soils are
reddish brown in color extends to a depth of approximately
28 feet and generally exibit very Slight to low expansion
potential

CONCLUSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the results of the field explorations laboratory
testing and engineering analysis the site is suitable for
the proposed tower construction Shallow spread and contin
uous footings can be utilized to support the 40 foot high
aircraft control tower structure
Foundation
For the proposed tower spread or continuous footings embedded
a minimum depth of 24 inches below the finish grade may be used
An allowable bearing value up to 4000 p s f is recommended
The above value is for total dead plus live loads and may be
increased by one third when temporary loads are considered
Minimum settlement less than one quarter of an inch is anti
cipated for footings bearing on the very stiff in situ clayey
Silt soil
Site Preparation
The building and parking areas should be cleared of existing
surface vegetation debris and other deleterious matter
The existing ground should then be scarified to a minimum
depth of 6 inches brought to near optimum moisture content
and recompacted to a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density

Slab On Grade
It is recommended that slab on grade be placed over a 4 inch
layer of base course compacted to 95 of its maximum dry
density
The subgrade should be kept moist and protected from drying
prior to concrete placement It should be compacted to 90
of its maximum dry density
Slab on grade should be separated from foundations
When the slab is located below the outside ground surface
a subdrainage system around the perimeter of the wall is
recommended to relieve seepage water
Parking Pavement
Based on the laboratory CBR test on the site surface material
the preliminary pavement design is as follows
2 inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 inch Base Course
8 inch Total Pavement Thickness
on Compacted Soils
The parking area subgrade should be moisture conditioned to near
optimum moisture content and compacted to 95 of its maximum dry
density The base course layer should have 95 relative density

PERCOLATION TEST
Falling head percolation tests were performed in Boring No 2
After completion of bore hole No 2 it was then filled with
water and allowed to saturate for 24 hours The hole was
refilled the next day with water to the top of the bore hole
and the water was allowed to percolate through the subsoils
The changes of water level drop with time was monitored and
recorded
The falling head percolation test results are summarized in
the appendix
Based on percolation test results it is our opinion that the
underlying rocky strata at the site could provide adequate
percolation rates for a properly designed septic tank Based
on calculated average percolation rate it is our opinion that
a 6 foot diameter cesspool excavated through the upper clayey
Silt soils and extended into a minimum depth of 6 feet into the
underlying rock formation would be adequate
A full scale percolation test should be provided in the dug
cesspool to verify that adequate design percolation rate can
be obtained at the selected septic tank location
