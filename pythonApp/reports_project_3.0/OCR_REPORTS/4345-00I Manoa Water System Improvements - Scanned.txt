PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located along Lowrey Avenue Vista Place Loomis Street Kaaipu
Avenue Puu Nanea Place East Manoa Road Kahewai Place and Kanawai Street in the
Manoa area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site
are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 Based on the information provided we
understand that it is proposed to construct new 8 inch and 4 inch water mains and
appurtenances along these streets for water system improvements The water system
improvement area is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Based on the plan and profile drawing provided the pipe inverts are not known at
this time although they are expected to be about 3 feet below ground surface In order to
evaluate the need for corrosion control protection field resistivity testing was conducted
along the water main alignment in general accordance with the Water System External
Corrosion Control Standards Volume 3 1991 Detailed discussions and results of our
corrosion tests are presented in the following sections of this letter report

site Our work was performed in general conformance with ASTM Test Designation G 57 95
and the requirements provided in the Water System External Corrosion Control Standards
Volume 3 1991 The scope of our work consisted of the following tasks and work efforts

Our field work was performed under clear and sunny conditions with

132 soil resistivity surveys were conducted at a maximum of 300 foot intervals at
33 locations designated as R 1 through R 32 and R 21A along the sidewalk of the streets
using the in Pin Method and a Nilsson Model 400 resistance meter
manufactured by Nilsson Electrical Laboratory Inc The method utilizes four surface probes
or pins electrodes to evaluate the average resistivity an indication of corrosivity of the
underlying soil mass The average resistivity values of the soil mass extending from the
existing ground surface to the test depth was assessed by using this method at the
approximate survey locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 Results of the resistivity
surveys are presented on Plates 3 1 through 3 7
The soil resistivity surveys were performed at the existing ground surface along the
sidewalk of the existing streets of the proposed 8 inch and 4 inch water mains The average
soil resistance along the proposed alignment was evaluated at each survey location with the

electrodes aligned at equal spacing of generally 3 to 9 feet at the existing ground surface The
depth of penetration of a resistivity sounding is a function of the electrode spacing Since the
depth of the water main invert was not available at the time of our field exploration spacings
of 3 to 9 feet was used for the electrode spacing for each soil resistivity survey location
The resistance meter Nilsson Model 400 impresses a voltage between the outer
electrodes causing an electrical current to flow between them The voltage drop between the
inner electrodes is then measured by a sensitive built in voltmeter As a result the soil
resistance in ohms can be found directly on the dial The soil resistivity in onm cm which
is directly related to both resistance and spacing can then be computed The evaluated soil
resistances the computed resistivity values and the corresponding corrosion ratings are
presented on Plates 3 1 through 3 7 of this report Field Resistivity Testing Summaries are
presented on Plates 4 1 through 4 8 It should be noted that the soil resistivity may be
influenced by temperature weather conditions existing underground facilities and other
factors
FINDINGS AND DISCUSSIONS
Resistivity is generally recognized as one of the most significant soil characteristics with
regard to the corrosivity of the soil to buried metallic objects In general the lower the
resistivity the greater the potential for corrosion of the buried metallic structure Conversely
the higher the resistivity the less likely the soil will contribute to corrosion of metallic objects
On the basis of the field resistivity test results a majority of the tests indicate the
subsurface soils along the proposed water mains of Lowrey Avenue Vista Place Loomis
Street and Kahewai Place within the project site have corrosion ratings of 1 and 2 This
indicates the subsurface soils have extremely corrosive to very corrosive potential Some
resistivity measurements indicating corrosion ratings of 3 and 4 were noted along Lowrey
Avenue The tests indicate the subsurface soils along the proposed water mains of East
Manoa Road and Puu Nanea Place within the project site have corrosion ratings of 2 and 3
This indicates the subsurface soils have very corrosive to corrosive potential A majority of the
tests indicate the subsurface soils along the proposed water mains of Kaaipu Avenue and
Kahawai Street within the project site have corrosion ratings of 1 to 3 This indicates the
subsurface soils have extremely corrosive to corrosive potential Some resistivity
measurements indicating corrosion rating of 4 were noted
LIMITATIONS
The evaluations presented in this report are based upon information obtained from field
data points by means of geophysical surveys Variation of soil conditions between and beyond
the field data points may occur and the nature and extent of such variations may not become
evident until a later point in time If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to
reevaluate the ratings provided in this report
