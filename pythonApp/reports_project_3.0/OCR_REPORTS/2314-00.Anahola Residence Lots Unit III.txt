ne SITE DESCRIPTION
The Anahola Residence Lots Unit III subdivision area consists of
two 2 parcels adjacent to the existing Hawaiian Homes Residence Lots at
Anahola Kauai
The smaller of these parcels is roughly rectangular in shape with
plan dimensions of approximately 950 by 125 feet It is bordered to the
north by Kukuihale Road to the west by Mahuahua Road to south by
existing residences and to the east by sugar cane fields
The larger parcel is an irregular polygon extending from
approximately 125 feet west of Mahuahua circle to approximately 1200 feet
east of the intersection of Mahuahua and Kukuihale Roads It is bordered
to the north and west by a tributary drainageway of Anahola Stream to the
south by existing residence and Kukuihale Road and to the east by sugar
cane fields
Since our previous exploration in 1982 the site has been graded to
provide buildable lots for single family residences Based on the
available grading plans prepared by Portugal Ibara and Associates Inc
Gated the grading consisted of shallow cuts and fills on
the order of about 1 to 2 feet in depth or thickness

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
upon information obtained from the borings Variations of conditions
between the borings may occur and the nature and extent of these
variations may not become evident until construction If variations then
appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations
given in this report
The locations of the borings were determined by taping from reference
points shown on the Grading and Utility Improvement Plan by Portugal
Ibara and Associates Inc dated Elevations of the
borings were approximately determined by interpolation from elevation
points on the same plan The physical location and elevation of the
borings should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the
method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
boring points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types and
as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross sections are based upon interpolation between borings
and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
Water level data from the boring points were collected at the times
given on the graphic representations and or in the text of this report
These data have been reviewed and interpretations made in the formulation
of this report However it must be noted that fluctuation may occur due
to variations in rainfall tides temperature and other factors
