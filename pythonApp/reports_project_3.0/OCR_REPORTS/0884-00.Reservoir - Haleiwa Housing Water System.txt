INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this investigation was to evaluate the
subsurface conditions at the site to provide soil data for
the design and construction of the proposed reservoir
structure and adjoining control building
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located in Haleiwa Oahu Hawaii approximately
one mile east of Kamehameha Highway The reservoir site is
situated ina cleared portion of cultivated sugar cane land
adjacent to Helemano Stream Valley Access to the site is by
paved cane hauling roads off of Kamehemecha Highway The site
is bordered to the north by a paved road and irrigation
ditch and to the south by a dirt road and steep sided gully
approximately 150 feet deep The side slopes uniformly west
at a 10 percent gradient Surveyed elevations range from
about 200 to 230 feet above sea level At the time of the
field exploration Well No 2 was installed and Well No 1
was under construction see Site Plan Plate 1

Subsurface Exploration

Plate 1 Detailed information of the field exploration

the boring logs and laboratory test results
Geology and Soil Conditions
The reservoir site is located on the northwestern extreme of
the Koolau Range The site lies on an eroded ridge between
two deep gullies At the elevation of the site these ridges
are typically composed of residual soils derived from the
underlying basic igneous rock
GEOLABRS HAWAH
W O 884 00
P 4
The field exploration disclosed essentially uniform soil
conditions at the site Based on the test borings the
site is surficially covered by 7 to 10 feet of brown to
reddish brown very stiff clayey silt soils Underlying this
layer is highly decomposed basalt consisting of a very stiff
to hard gray brown clayey silt which grades into weathered
basalt at a depth of about 16 to 18 feet The underlying
basalt although weathered is generally very dense with
random highly weathered zones encountered to the depth of the
borings
Ground water was not encountered in any of the borings during
the field exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the field exploration laboratory test results and
analyses it is our opinion that the proposed reservoir site
is suitable for the planned construction
Foundation
Spread or continuous footings can be used for the reservoir
All the footings should bear on the stiff residual reddish
brown or gray brown clayey soils An allowable bearing
value of 3500 psf can be used for footings resting on the
stiff residual soils The footings should bear a minimum
of 12 inches below the adjacent finish grade The ground

surface around the footings should not be excavated or allowed
to erode If the footing is located next to a utility trench
it should extend down to the bottom of the utility trench
Surface water should be diverted away from the reservoir
foundation
Based on the test data and estimated reservoir loads very
small settlements are anticipated for the reservoir structure
A coefficient of friction of 0 4 can be utilized to determine
the lateral resistance of the structure and foundation
Additionally a passive soil resistance of 300 pcf may be used
The site should be cleared of all surface vegetation debris
and loose top soil
Based on the site exploration and analyses a cut slope ratio
of 14 1 horizontal vertical may be used We recommend that
the cut slope be inspected by a soils engineer during
construction to verify if the actual slope materials are as
anticipated Some slope adjustment may be required in the
field if soft pockets or seepage is encountered during the
slope excavation

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during
earthwork construction particularly the subgrade preparation
for the reservoir sites
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be observed
by a soils engineer during construction to see that adequate
bearing has been obtained and that the subsurface conditions
are consistent with the findings of our subsurface investi
gation
Any imported material required should be non expansive
tested and approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling
to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent
upon the above inspections

Settlements
Based on the test data estimated settlements for the
reservoir foundations are about 1 4 inch total Since the
subsoils are not fully saturated most of the settlements
will be elastic deformation and should take place during
construction

at about 5 feet depths near the proposed footing elevations
indicated very low swelling potential less than 1 at nat
ural moisture contents of 24 to 27 Based on the Atterberg
Limits these samples can be classified as clayey silt ML
Soils which are generally recognized as having low swelling
potential
