specifications and to provide subsequent recommendations as appropriate
PROJECT DESCRIPTION
The project is located on Interstate Route H 1 between Kapiolani Interchange and
Ainakoa Avenue in the Honolulu District on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project includes
upgrading and or improving the existing shoulders and guardrail systems to reduce crash
severity due to errant vehicle highway runoff This project also include relocating an existing
traffic signal pole structure
The scope of our work discussed in this letter report includes observation of the drilled
shaft foundation construction for the relocation of an existing traffic signal pole structure
between the Interstate Route H 1 and Ainakoa Avenue only Paul s Electric Co was the
contractor for the project They were also responsible for drilling the shaft installing the
reinforcing cage and concrete placement

DRILLED SHAFT INSTALLATION
Based on the anticipated subsurface conditions and foundation demand a deep
foundation system consisting of cast in place concrete drilled shaft embedded into the
underlying alluvial deposit was utilized for foundation support of the relocated light pole
structures
The drilled shaft contractor installed the one drilled shaft from November 17
through 30 2020 at the location shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 A representative from
Geolabs Inc observed the drilled shaft excavation placement of reinforcing cage and
concrete operation on a near continuous full time basis during the installation of the
production drilled shaft The drilled shaft was excavated by the contractor utilizing a Watson
Truck Mounted Drill rig with a 36 inch diameter auger and a 34 inch clean out bucket
Groundwater was not encountered during the excavation of the drilled shaft
After excavation of the drilled shaft to the designated tip elevation the reinforcing
cage was lowered into the drilled hole to the appropriate elevation with the use of a Manitex
30100C Boom Truck Crane After the reinforcing cage was installed in the drilled hole
concrete was placed through a 4 inch diameter tremie tube During concrete placement we
observed that at least 5 feet of concrete head was maintained above the bottom of the tremie
pipe Concrete with a slump of 7 inches was used for the drilled shaft The drilled shaft
installation records are presented on Plates 3 1 through 3 5
Based on our field observations we believe that the drilled shaft for the relocated
traffic signal pole structure has been installed in general conformance with the project plans
and specifications and within the limits of generally accepted construction practices The
location of the top of the shaft should be measured using optical survey equipment to verify
the shaft position is within tolerance in the project specifications Based on our observations
of the drilled shaft installation it is our professional opinion that the drilled shaft has been
installed to sufficient depth to provide the design load capacity
LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site even when our representatives are on the
project site on a full time basis Our observation and consultation services were
performed in accordance with generally accepted standards of geotechnical engineering
practice no warranty is expressed or implied Provision of construction monitoring by an
engineer is not insurance nor does it guarantee construction of any type The contractor
shall retain primary responsibility for the quality of the work for adhering to plans and
specifications and for repairing defects regardless of when they are found
