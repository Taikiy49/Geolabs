SOIL NAIL INSTALLATION OBSERVATION AND TESTING REPORT
CRM WALL REPAIR AT BUILDING 24
KALIHI VALLEY HOMES
Dear Ms Yoshimori
This letter report summarizes our geotechnical observation and consultation
services performed during the installation and testing of the soil nail anchors for the CRM
Wall Repair at Building 24 project at Kalihi Valley Homes in Honolulu on the Island of
Oahu Hawaii
In general this letter report presents our field observations and results of the soil
nail anchor installation and testing as well as periodic field observation of the shotcrete
placement for the project from February 28 through May 10 2012
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The retaining wall repair project site is in front of Building 24 at Kalihi Valley
Homes public housing facility in Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project
location and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The
existing CRM retaining wall measuring approximately 280 feet long and 10 feet high
provides grade separation between the building above and the parking lot access road
below We understand that a 60 foot section of the CRM wall failed during construction
activities in its proximity on Appendix D Figure 1 Based on our
evaluation we recommend using a soil nail retaining wall system to repair the 60 foot
long failed portion and to stabilize the remaining 155 foot long intact section of the
existing CRM retaining wall
The purpose of our geotechnical engineering services during the wall repair work
was to observe the installation and testing of the soil nail anchors for general
conformance with the project plans and specifications Prometheus Construction is the
general contractor for the project who is also responsible for the installation of the soil
nail anchors

In general the soil nail retaining wall system consisted of a series of individual
reinforcing bars grouted into drilled holes to stabilize the soil mass in front of Building 24 A
structural shotcrete layer is applied to the face of the reinforced wall and an architectural
rock veneer is then placed on the wall face for aesthetic appearance
Based on our engineering analyses the new soil nail retaining wall will comprise of
a total of eighty six 86 soil nail anchors designated as T1 through T43 for the top row and
B1 through B43 for the bottom row A layout of the soil nail anchors is shown on the Soil
Nail Location Plan Plate 2 The soil nail anchor lengths were specified as 18 feet for the
top row and 15 feet for the bottom row with an inclination of 15 degrees from horizontal
As part of the project requirements two pre production verification soil nails sacrificial
were installed and tested prior to the production works The locations of the pre production
verification soil nails are also shown on the Soil Nail Location Plan Plate 2
The two pre production verification soil nails were installed on
and tested on March 7 and 8 2012 in general accordance with the pre production
verification testing procedures outlined in the project specifications The installation and
testing were conducted by Prometheus Construction and observed by Geolabs Inc The
installation and test results of the two pre production verification soil nails are presented in
Appendix A
Based on the results of the pre production verification soil nail testing we believe
that the design soil nail bond stress is adequate Consequently production soil nail
installation and testing work commenced on March 16 2012 and was completed on
A Geolabs representative was present on site on a full time basis to
observe the drilling installation and grouting operations during construction of the
production soil nail anchors

During the course of project construction our field observations indicated that the
existing CRM wall appeared to be constructed of loose rocks that were not grouted
together In addition the backfill immediately behind the existing CRM wall consisted of
approximately 2 foot wide drainage layer of gravel material which is atypical for this type of
wall construction Because of the loose rocks and gravel backfill condition the face of the
soil nail installation had to be pushed back further inside beyond the crumbled wall
resulting in additional shotcrete placement and soil nail extension
Our field observations also indicated that stiff in situ soils were encountered during
removal of the soil materials at the failed section As a result the excavation depth into the
failed slope was terminated earlier prior to reaching the specified 10 foot deep excavation
shown in the construction plans Based on our evaluation it is our opinion that the lean
concrete and rebar placement stipulated in the construction plans for the failed section may
be eliminated and the soil nail construction at this area may proceed in the same manner
as the rest of the CRM wall Individual soil nail anchor logs containing our observations
and other pertinent information recorded by our representative are provided in Appendix B
and are summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 4
In accordance with the project specifications nine proof tests were conducted on
selected soil nail anchors including B22 B31 and B40 The
proof test results of the selected soil nails are presented in Appendix C In general the soil
nail anchors were loaded to 150 percent of the design load and held for 10 or 60 minutes
depending on the creep movement Typical anchor testing set up is shown in Figure 6 of
Appendix D
In general the soil nail anchors subjected to verification and proof tests met the
acceptance criteria specified in the project specifications The criteria for acceptance of the
soil nail anchors consist of the following
For the verification tests a total creep movement of less than 0 08 inch
per log cycle of time between the 6 and 60 minute readings is measured
during creep testing and the creep rate is linear or decreasing throughout
the creep test load hold period
For the proof tests a total creep movement of less than 0 04 inch is
measured between the 1 and 10 minute readings or a total creep
movement of less than 0 08 inch is measured between the 6 and
60 minute readings and the creep rate is linear or decreasing throughout
the creep test load hold period
Total measured movement at the maximum test load exceeds 80 percent
of the theoretical elastic elongation of the test nail unbonded length
Pull out failure does not occur at the maximum test load
Based on our field observations and test results we believe that the soil nail
anchors have been installed in general conformance with the project plans and
