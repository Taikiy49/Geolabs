This letter report summarizes our probing and grouting operations at the above
reservoir site during December 2010 and January 2011
Probing and Grouting Operations
The probing and grouting operations were performed in general conformance with
the project specifications The purpose of the probing and grouting operation was to
reduce the potential for loss of foundation support resulting from collapse of cavities or
compression of loose zones below the reservoir
Delta Construction Corporation marked the probe hole locations at the column
locations and around the reservoir perimeter prior to the probing operation Our geologist
marked the probe locations between the column foundations
In general the probe holes were located on a 10 foot nominal on center grid
pattern The approximate probe hole locations are shown on the Probe Hole Location
Plan Plate 2 In general the probe holes were drilled to a depth of about 10 feet below
the bottom of the foundation
The probe holes about 4 inches in diameter were drilled using a truck mounted
drill rig equipped with an air percussion down the hole hammer After drilling of the probe
holes temporary pipes 2 inch PVC were inserted into the holes to keep the holes open
prior to the grouting operations A total of 147 probe holes were drilled with an aggregate
length of about 1 470 lineal feet below the existing ground surface
Suspected voids were encountered when drilling Probe Nos 4 and 21 At each
location four additional probe holes were drilled to further explore the extent of the
suspected voids The total aggregate footage for the additional probes was 80 lineal feet
below the existing ground surface

The probe holes were backfilled with a sand cement slurry grout supplied by Island
Ready Mix American Standard Concrete Pumping Hawaii Inc provided grout pumping
services under the observation of our geologist A 3 inch pipe attached to the
4 inch diameter rubber hose on the concrete boom pump trunk was inserted into each
open hole and lowered to near the bottom of the hole Each probe hole was grouted from
the desired depth upward by low pressure injection of grout through the rubber hose
which was retracted from the probe hole as the grout filled the hole Our field
representative observed the grouting operation and recorded the approximate grout take
Grout quantities were estimated using the number of strokes to fill each probe hole
In general we did not observe significant voids and or cavities in the majority of the
probes during the operation We did observe significant grout intake for Probe Hole
Nos 4 and 21 during the grouting operation Approximately 1 2 cubic yards of
sand cement grout was placed into Probe Hole No 4 and approximately 0 4 cubic yard
was placed in Probe Hole No 21 The supplemental probes did not exhibit significant
quantities of grout intake The probing and grouting summary is provided on Plates 3 1
through 3 5
In summary the foundation probing and grouting operations have been conducted
in general conformance with the project specifications Based on our field observations it
is our professional opinion that the cavities encountered below the reservoir subgrade
have been probed and grouted and that the subgrade area should provide the
recommended foundation support for the reservoir
Limitations
It should be noted that it is not possible to detect and remediate all the cavities
and or voids present beneath the reservoir Therefore some of the cavities and or voids
may not have been detected even with the probing and grouting program undertaken for
the project However the level of foundation preparation undertaken for the reservoir and
the subgrade bearing on the on site basalt formation appears to be reasonable in reducing
the geotechnical risks to the project Performing probing and grouting of the foundations
bearing on the on site materials provides the owner with a higher degree of assurance that
substantial cavities and or voids are not located directly below the reservoir
Our services were performed in accordance with generally accepted standards of
geotechnical engineering practice no warranty is expressed or implied It should be noted
that provision of construction observation by an engineer is not insurance nor does it
guarantee construction of any type
