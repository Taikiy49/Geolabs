In accordance with your request we have performed a soil and
foundation investigation at the above referenced site Our work was
done in general accordance with our proposals of and
The purpose of the investigation is to explore the subsurface
conditions at the site to provide soil data for use in the foundation
design of the proposed structures
Subsequent to our initial investigation the sites for Buildings
C and F were relocated and therefore a supplementary investigation
had been performed for the new locations and the results are incorporated
in this report
W O Page 2
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests which we
used in the engineering analysis to formulate our conclusion and
recommendations are presented in the appendices of this report
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The storm damage restoration project consists of the construction
of the following six buildings at various locations on the base to
replace structures damaged by Hurricane Iwa
Building A Fire Station
A one story structure approximately 65 by 85 feet in plan
dimension is planned The structure will be constructed of reinforced
concrete with open web steel joist roof framing concrete floor and roof
Building B Proposed Public Works Storage Building
A one story structure approximately 50 by 110 feet in plan
dimension is planned It will be constructed of CMU walls asphaltic
concrete flooring and built up roofing Maximum column loads up to 36
kips and wall loads of 1 8 kips foot are anticipated Anticipated
vehicle traffic will include 4000 1b capacity forklift trucks

Building C Brigade Service Support Group Warehouse Flammable Storage
A one story warehouse approximately 80 by 238 feet in plan
dimension is planned The structure will be of pre engineered metal
construction with masonry and metal walls metal roof and concrete
floor Maximum column loads are anticipated to be about 35 kips
Anticipated vehicle traffic inside the warehouse will include 4000 1b
and 6000 1b capacity forklift trucks
In addition Building C 1 Flammable Storage which will be
approximately 20 by 50 feet in plan dimension and consist of a

Building D First Radio Battalion Electronic Communications

SITE DESCRIPTION
The six building sites are located in the Marine Corps Air Station
Kaneohe Oahu Hawaii as shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
Building A Fire Station
This site is situated at the northwestern corner of the
intersection of Third Street and G Street The EM Club Package Store
Building 822 which is a one story warehouse type structure presently
occupies the site There is an A C parking area on the western side of
the building and an A C road adjacent to the northern portion of the
building The remaining areas are covered with grass
Building B Public Works Storage Building
This site is located west of the intersection of 2nd and E
Streets Two existing structures Buildings 240 and 241 presently
occupy the site These structures would be demolished prior to
construction of the new building The area is relatively flat and
presently covered with asphaltic concrete pavement

Site Grading
Up to 4 feet of fill may be required at Buildings B D and E In
general the other sites will require fills of less than two feet
Demolition of existing structures are required at Buildings A B and C
Site Preparation
Existing concrete slabs foundations and asphaltic concrete
pavements below the structures should be removed
Vegetation organic or other deleterious material should be removed
The existing ground should be compacted to a minimum of 95 of its
maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78
Fill Material
a ee me ee ta ae
Imported material will be required for the fill The material to
be imported should be non expansive free of organic matter and
should form a dense stable mass when compacted to the required
densities
GEOLABS HAWAH
W O Page 19
In general the structural fill material should be well graded
granular material less than 3 inches in size with less than 20
passing a 200 sieve The soil should also provide a minimum CBR
value of 15
Fill Placement
Fills should be placed in horizontal lifts not exceeding 6 inches
in compacted thickness and compacted to a minimum of 95 of its
maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78
The site should be so graded to provide drainage of surface runoff
away from all foundations
Design Review
Plans and specifications for site grading and foundation
construction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and
written comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the site grading spread footing
excavations and subgrade preparation for slabs and pavements be
observed by the soils engineer during construction This is to see
that adequate compaction has been obtained and that the subsurface
conditions are consistent with the findings of our investigation and
that densification is attained
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O Page 20
If conditions encountered are different from those presented in
this report then appropriate modifications to the design should be
made

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by
drilling to 15 5 feet below the existing ground
surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plans
Plates 2 through 6 A truck mounted drilling rig was used to
drill the borings
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by
visual and textural examination in the field by one of our
engineers who continuously monitored the drilling operations
The classifications were further reviewed by visual inspection
and testing in the laboratory The soils were classified in
general conformance with the Unified Soil Classification System
A graphic presentation of the materials encountered is presented
on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 1l
Soil samples were obtained by driving a 2 inch standard
split spoon sampler or a 2 4 inch I D split barrel sampler with
a 140 pound hammer free falling 30 inches The blow counts to
drive the sampler for 12 inches are shown on the Boring Logs at
the appropriate sample depths

Laboratory Testing
Moisture content and unit weight determinations were
performed on selected samples as an aid in the classification and
evaluation of soil properties The results of these tests are
presented on the boring logs at the appropriate sample depths
To further identify soil characteristics seven
mechanical sieve analysis tests were performed The test results
are presented on Plates B 1 through B 3
Five laboratory CBR tests were performed on bulk samples
to evaluate the subgrade support characteristics of the soils for
pavement design The test results are summarized on Plates B l
through B 3
Four direct shear tests were performed on selected
undisturbed samples to evaluate the shear strength parameters of
the subsoils The test results are presented on Plates B 4
through B 7
Six one inch ring swell tests were performed on
undisturbed and remolded samples to evaluate the swelling
potential of the soils The test results are summarized on Plate
B 8
