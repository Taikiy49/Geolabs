PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this soil engineering investigation was to
explore the subsurface soil and groundwater conditions along
the proposed force main alignment and at the proposed SPs
building extension in order to provide soil data for use in
the design and construction of the proposed sewer pipeline
and building foundations
The work was generally done in accordance with our proposal
of
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests
which were used in the engineering analysis that formulated
our conclusions and recommendations are presented in the
appendix of this report
The approximate boring locations are shown on the Site Plan
Plate 1 The boring logs and information regarding the
field exploration and laboratory testing are presented in
the appendix
GENERAL PROJECT DESCRIPTION
It is proposed to modify the existing Kahala Sewage Pumping
Station by making an addition to the existing building to
accommodate new pumps and controls The proposed building
addition will be about 20 by 28 feet in plan and have a
basement level for the force main and control valves which
will be about 8 to 10 feet below the existing ground level
In addition it is proposed to construct a new 24 inch Force
Main extending from the Pump Station along Kahala Avenue
and Kealaolu Avenue to a location mauka of the H 1 Freeway
overpass The total length of the proposed force main is
approximately 4550 L F and it will include a pile supported
crossing over Kapakahi Stream The force main invert will
vary in elevation between 5 5 and 35 5 feet The proposed
inverts are approximately 6 to 10 feet below the existing
ground surface

SITE DESCRIPTION ALONG SEWER ALIGNMENT
The existing Kahala Sewage Pump Station is situated on the
southern side of Kahala Avenue east of Kapakahi Stream
The existing pump house is approximately 20 by 32 feet in
plan The area in general is relatively flat
The proposed force main alignment extends from the pump
house out to Kahala Avenue then along Kahala Avenue to the
existing bridge over Kapakahi Stream The force main will
extend along the southern side of the bridge structure then
continue along Kahala Avenue to Kealaolu Avenue The align
ment continues northwest along Kealaolu Avenue across
Kalanianaole Highway under the 4 1 viaduct and then
westward across Kilauea Avenue
Most of the sewer alignment is surrounded by residential and
commercial development
The relief of the Surrounding area is essentially level with
a Slight grade down towards the ocean Underground utility
lines are situated throughout the site servicing the resi
dences along the roadways
Regional Geology
The project site is located on the Wailupe Coastal Plain
which was built up on the southern flank of the Koolau
Shield by alluvium washed down from the Koolau Range by
Stream action and by beach and lagoon deposits along the
coastal margin

Bearing Capacity and Settlement
The stiff alluvial soils encountered in the upper northern
half of the alignment have good bearing capacity therefore
minimal settlements are anticipated
The sandy soils encountered in the lower southern half of
the alignment were loose to medium dense and have fair to
good bearing capacity
The new sewer line construction in general will not
materially increase the vertical loading upon these under
lying soils if no additional fills are placed upon the
existing ground surface The resulting net loading between
the weight of the excavated material and backfill will be
essentially small therefore significant settlements are
not anticipated if proper construction techniques are
utilized
Pipe Bedding
The stress distribution against the bottom of a pipe has a
great effect upon the load Supporting capacity of the pipe
therefore the pipe bedding is an important design consi
deration
A minimum of eight inches of compacted granular bedding
material should be placed below the Pipe The granular
bedding should extend beyond the Pipe sides a minimum width
of one fourth the outside Pipe diameter and should extend
halfway up the pipe sides The bedding material should
consist of clean granular material such as 3B Fine gravel
Below the water level loose to medium sands are anticipated
at the pipe invert level in the lower southern portion of
the alignment to To provide more
uniform support for the pipe a one foot layer of granular
material such as 3B Fine gravel should be provided below
the sewer pipe and bedding layer in this portion of the
alignment

The method and equipment used for excavation should be the
choice of the contractor within practical limits The

Relatively easy excavation is anticipated for most of the
alignment Harder excavation is anticipated in the northern
portion where many cobbles and boulders were encountered
Trench Wall Supports
It is our opinion that the trench walls in the southern half
of the alignment where beach sand deposits and high water
level were encountered will require shoring
The northern portion may also require shoring in the boulder
and cobble deposits
The excavation should be provided with proper bracing in
accordance with applicable safety requirements The design
and safety of the trench supports should be the responsi
bility of the contractor
If sufficient space is available the excavation may be cut
above wall supports However it should be taken into con
sideration that greater amount of vertical pressures will be
transmitted to the pipe by widening of the trench
Heavy equipment and stockpiles should be kept away from the
tops of the open trenches
The ground adjacent to and near the top of the excavation
should be continuously monitored for cracks or dips and
other indications of movements and possible sloughing of the
trench walls Such monitoring is particularly critical in
the areas where existing structures are adjacent to the
trench excavation
Dewatering
High groundwater levels were noted in the lower southern
portion of the alignment
The method of dewatering should be left open to the contrac
tor provided adequate stability of the excavation is main
tained The deposits encountered in the borings along this
portion of the alignment consisted mostly of silty sands
which are relatively permeable and susceptible to piping and
guick conditions

The project dewatering operation should be done without
pumping out the soil fines pumping clear water only It
should be coordinated with the excavation support system so
that the stability of the excavation is not jeopardized
The operations should be carried out without creating a
quick condition or softening the bottom of the excavation
Another alternative to dewatering would be to place the pipe
in a wet trench condition
Backfill
In general the trench backfill should be in accordance with
the Standard Specifications for Public Works Construction
May 1975
Granular material such as sand should be used for the
backfill up to about one foot above the pipe to reduce the
compactive effort required and possible damage to the pipe
Except for the organic clays silt and boulders most of the
excavated on site soil can be reused as the upper portion of
the trench backfill from above one foot over the pipe to
within 2 feet of the finished grade Backfill should be
mechanically compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum
dry density established by ASTM D 1557 Below roadway
areas the backfill within 2 feet of the finished grade
should consist of select borrow and be compacted to 95 of
its maximum dry density
If practicable the trenches should be kept free of water
during backfilling Should backfill under the water level
be ncesssary granular material such as base course rock
should be utilized to minimize excessive ground settlement
of the trench backfill in the future

the pipe flexible pipe joints are recommended to allow for

Pipe Crossing Over Kapakahi Stream
At the crossing over Kapakahi Stream the force main will be
located on the southern side of the existing bridge It is
our understanding that the existing bridge foundations may
not have sufficient additional capacity to support the new
force main Therefore a separate foundation system is
required for the force main
Boring and 3A indicate that this area is underlain
by very loose to loose silty sand deposits which grade to
dense from about 35 foot depth Pile foundations extending
down to the dense sand level are recommended for the pipe
Support
Ten inch square precast prestressed concrete piles if
available may be used to support the sewer pipe The piles
should be driven with a light hammer delivering about 12 000
ft lbs of energy
The piles should be driven to a final driving resistance of
about 35 blows per foot or 5 blows per inch for the last 2
inches
An allowable pile capacity of 25 tons per pile is recom
mended
Anticipated pile lengths are about 40 to 45 feet below the
existing ground surface

Dewatering
Water level was encountered at about 5 foot depth in this
area therefore dewatering is anticipated for the building
excavation which will extend about 8 to 10 feet below the
existing ground surface Medium density beach sand was
encountered below the water level in this area The sand
deposit is relatively permeable and susceptible to loosening
if improper pumping methods are adopted
The project dewatering operation should be done without
pumping out the soil fines pumping clear water only It
should be coordinated with the excavation support system so
that the stability of the excavation is not jeopardized
The operations should be carried out without creating a
quick condition or softening the excavation bottom
The sand deposit has minimal cementation and will be subject
to sloughing Sheeting and Sracing will be required for the
excavation Alternatively if sufficient room is available
the excavation may be cut back at a temporary slope of
Basement Walls
Basement walls up to 10 feet high would be required for the
proposed structure Pile foundation would be utilized to
Support the basement wall and structural loads
Well graded granular material such as base course rock or
on site sand should be used as backfill material behind the
walls Impervious material should be used for the top 2
feet of the backfill

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
The pile driving operations should be monitored on a
full time basis by a qualified soils technician under the
direction of a soils engineer to verify that the piles have
been driven in accordance with our recommendations
It is recommended that the excavation and backfill opera
tions be observed by a soils engineer during construction to
see that adequate bearing has been obtained and that the
subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of
Our subsurface investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive
tested and approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling
to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon
the above inspections

LABORATORY TESTING
Moisture content Atterberg Limits and unit weight deter
minations were performed on selected samples as an aid in the
classification and evaluation of soil properties The test
results are presented on the boring logs at the appropriate
sample depths
Two direct shear tests were performed on selected samples
to evaluate the shear strength of the underlying soil The
test results are presented on Plates B 1 and B 2
Five sieve analyses were performed on samples of the
underlying sandy soils to evaluate its grain size distribu
tion The test results are summarized on Plates B 3 and B 4
