Dear Mr. Matsuda: 
In accordance with the scope of services outlined in our fee proposal dated 
June 8, 2020, we observed the micropile foundation installation work for the Piinaau Bridge 
Repairs project located along Hana Highway (Route 360) at the Vicinity of Keanae, Island 
of Maui, Hawaii. The project location and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location 
Map, Plate 1. This letter report summarizes our geotechnical observations during the 
micropile foundation installation and testing for the project. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The Piinaau Bridge is located near Mile Post 17 on Hana Highway (Route 360) in the 
Vicinity of Keanae, Island of Maui, Hawaii. The Hana Highway is a winding, narrow, two-lane 
undivided road with very steep cut slopes on one side and steep natural slopes on the 
downhill side. The area is heavily vegetated due to high rainfall experienced. Additionally, 
the road is heavily used by residents and tourists. 
Back in the fall of 2018, a storm event caused damage to the Piinaau Bridge northern 
railing and sidewall on both west abutment (Kahului bound) and east abutment (Hana 
bound). New resting walls supported by micropiles were designed for replacement and 
stabilization. Based on the micropile specifications, the services of geotechnical engineering 
observation and testing are required in support of the new bridge abutment construction, 
including the micropile installation and testing, as well as material testing. 
PRE-PRODUCTION MICROPILE LOAD TESTS 
In order to evaluate the design capacity of the 5.5-inch outside diameter (OD) 
micropile foundation, one static tensile load test was conducted on a sacrificial test micropile 
designated as TP-1. Sacrificial Micropile TP-1 was located on the Kahului bound of the

Piinaau Bridge. The test micropile were installed on May 13, 2020 by Hawaiian Dredging 
Construction Co. utilizing a TEI Rock Drill with an TEI RDS550 Rotary Head. 
Subsequent to the installation, the pre-production micropile was tested in general 
accordance with ASTM D3689 for the tensile load test in lieu of ASTM D1143 for the 
compressive load test per RFI 14. The results of the pre-production load test are 
summarized and presented on Plates A-1.1 through A-1.5 of Appendix A. 
Based on our field observations and the results of the tensile load test, we believe 
the 5.5-inch OD micropile installed at the project site with a micropile length of 11 feet is 
capable of supporting the design allowable load capacity of 25 kips with a factor of safety of 
2.0, meeting the design requirements. 
PRODUCTION PILE INSTALLATION 
The production micropile installation program, consisting of installing two 5.5-inch OD 
micropiles on the Kahului wall and four 5.5-inch OD micropiles on the Hana Wall, was 
performed from May 21 to July 1, 2020. A TEI Rock Drill with an TEI RDS550 Rotary Head 
was used and monitored under near-continuous (full-time) observations by our 
representatives. 
The locations of the installed production micropiles are shown on the Micropile 
Location Plan – Kahului Wall, Plate 3, and Micropile Location Plan – Hana Wall, Plate 4. A 
summary of the micropile installation is presented on Plate 5 of this letter report. The Kahului 
wall micropiles, designated as MP-1 and MP-2, were installed to a depth of approximately 
10 to 11 feet below the bottom of pile cap with a minimum of 5-foot embedment in the rock 
formation. 
It should be noted that soil nail ground anchor system was initially planned and 
designed to support the Hana wall. We understand that design modification was issued via 
RFI 14 dated May 22, 2020, using micropile system to support the Hana Wall. Four 
micropiles, designated as MP-3 through MP-6, were drilled to approximately 17.5 to 18 feet 
below the bottom of pile cap on the Hana Wall with a minimum of 5-feet embedment into 
the rock formation. In according to RFI 21, full depth temporary casing to the rock formation 
was used to prevent the borehole from caving. 
After the drilled holes were extended to the specified depths and cleaned out, the 
specified grout was placed through a PVC tremie pile starting from the bottom of the cased 
hole. Grout was batched on-site utilizing a colloidal high-shear and high-speed Atlas Copco 
Unigrout mixer equipped with a grout pump. The grout mix consisted of one sack of 
94-pound Portland Cement Type I/ II, and 5 gallons of water. 
Upon clean grout appeared at the top of the cased hole, a reinforcing bar with 
centralizers was inserted in the cased hole. Consequently, the steel casing was withdrawn 
gradually to form the required bonded length with a minimum of 5 feet of grout head above

MICROPILE PROOF TESTING 
Four production micropiles, designated as MP-1, MP-2, MP-3, and MP-5 were 
selected for tensile (pull-out) proof testing. The proof testing was generally performed in 
accordance with ASTM D3689 in lieu of ASTM D1143 per RFI 14 and the project 
specifications. At the Kahului wall, the allowable load capacity and maximum testing load of 
the micropiles were specified as 25 and 50 kips, while at the Hana Wall, to be 20 and 30 
kips. 
A hydraulic jack system reacting against a load frame supported by dunnage at each 
end was used to apply the test loads to the proof testing micropiles. A load cell was used to 
measure the test loads applied. The micropile displacements were measured with two dial 
gages attached to two parallel independent reference beams.  
In general, the proof testing micropiles were loaded to a maximum testing load of 50 
kips in 2.5-kip load increments for the Kahului Wall micropiles, and 30 kips in 5-kip load 
increments for the Hana Wall micropiles. Each load increment was held for at least 4 minutes 
or until stable. The maximum test load of 50 kips for the Kahului Wall micropiles (about 
200 percent of the design allowable load capacity) and 30 kips for the Hana Wall micropiles 
(about 150 percent of the design allowable load capacity) were held for a minimum of 10 
minutes to evaluate the effects of creep. Results of the proof tests are presented in Appendix 
B of this report.  
Based on our field observations and the above proof testing results, it is our 
professional opinion that the 5.5-inch OD production micropiles have been installed to 
sufficient depths and resistance to support the design allowable load capacity of 25 kips per 
micropile for the Kahului Wall, and 20 kips per micropile for the Hana Wall in accordance 
with the specifications.
