PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The planned Kukui ula Development is a resort community that encompasses
about 1 045 acres in Koloa on the south shore of Kauai and includes a hotel vacation
ownership commercial park golf course and residential uses The Parcel G Makani
Kai Subdivision project is part of the improvements for the Kukui ula Development
The Parcel G subdivision project is located on the northwest side of the Kukui ula
Resort Community development below agricultural parcels and east of Ala Makani Kai
Road overlooking the 10th hole of the Kukui ula Golf Course and the Pacific Ocean The
parcel encompasses approximately 2 5 acres with four individual house lots designated
as Lots No 1 through 4 A Project Location Map and Site Plan are shown on Plates 1
and 2 respectively
Site grading generally consisted of cuts up to about 8 feet deep and fills up to
about 12 feet thick On site soils generated from the cut areas were the primary source
of fill material for the project However fill materials consisting of reddish brown clayey
silts and silty clays were imported from the adjacent Parcel E Subdivision site within the
Kukui ula Development The project also included installation of new infrastructure
including roadways drainage sewer water and other utilities In addition roughly 150

PARCEL G EARTHWORK OPERATIONS
We observed the mass and pad grading and utility and roadway construction work
for the Parcel G Makani Kai Subdivision project from August 13 2020 to
The mass and site grading work consisted of clearing and grubbing
cuts of up to about 8 feet deep and placement and compaction of fills up to about 12 feet
thick to achieve the finished grades Prior to filling the existing ground surface was cleared
and grubbed of vegetation and deleterious material then proof rolled with a compactor
Earthworks Pacific Inc performed the mass and pad grading and utility and roadway
construction work
On site boulder fills were placed within the southwest portion of Lot No 4 The
cobble and boulder fills were seated with a dozer then compacted with a large sheepsfoot
wheel compactor
During the site grading operations samples of the materials utilized as fill were
obtained and transported to our office for testing The maximum dry densities and
optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general accordance with
ASTM D1557 Modified Proctor and the test results are summarized on Plate 3
The fill materials were moisture conditioned to near optimum moisture content and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as determined in
accordance with ASTM D1557 In the roadway areas the fill and backfill materials at the
finished subgrade were compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
During the mass and pad grading and utility and roadway construction operations
periodic field density tests were performed in general accordance with ASTM D6938
Nuclear Gauge Method Compaction test results are summarized on Plates 4 1 through
4 4 These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills
and backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and
interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed as
unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained and or
approved by our field representative based on visual observation and hand probing
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the grading utility and roadway installation work for
the above project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report
