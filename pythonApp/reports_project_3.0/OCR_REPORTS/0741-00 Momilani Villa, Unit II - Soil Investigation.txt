INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this investigation is to evaluate the subsurface
soil conditions and to provide soil data for the design and
construction of the proposed Momilani Villa Unit II at Manana
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed plan is to clear and grade the site for a single
family residential development which consists of 15 lots along
with a 32 foot wide access roadway through the site
To develop the site cuts up to 12 feet are planned in the
upper portion of the site and fills up to 8 feet are planned

Most of the lower portions of the site will not be graded

The proposed site is located on Komo Mai Drive on the northern
side of Waimano Valley The upper portion of the site is

The upper area is covered with trees Some bowed trees were
noted near Test Pit No 4 location This generally indicated

area

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The project site which is located in the relatively narrow
lower Waimano Stream Valley area has basically the same
geologic history as the rest of the valley The valley
itself has a similar geologic history as compared to the
nearby valleys that surround and empty into Pearl Harbor
GEOLABS HAWAII
MOMILANI VILLA UNIT II P 4
These valleys were principally cut by stream erosion over
millions of years as sea level fluctuated and incluenced
the erosion and deposition process of Pearl Harbor and the
surrounding lower valley areas
Within the limits of the project site rock is encountered
at a relatively shallow depth beneath the clayey soil layers
in the higher elevation areas of the site near the base of
the ridge or valley wall Outcrops of rock on the up slope
ridge area appear as two distinctive layers forming a step
like feature as seen from the lower elevations of the iter
The prominent layering of the rock is probably due to the
existence of denser more competent layers of rock separated
by less dense fractured or clinkery layers of rock Under
these conditions weathering and erosion would produce a
different rate of decomposition for each layer This would
then result in the less dense layers weathering and eroding
away faster than the harder rock layers leaving the harder
layers behind thus creating the steplike feature
An interesting point about this type of rock layering is the
favorable condition it creates for the production of boulders
As the weaker layers of rock wear away they naturally begin
to isolate the more massive layers As decomposition con
tinues the massive layers themselves begin to crack and
decompose and finally blocks of rock may separate from the
original massive layer thus producing boulders

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration generally indicated that the upper
eastern slope areas were blanketed with a layer of low
strength talus clay soils underlain by a rock formation
The conventional grading procedure of cutting the up
slope area in order to provide building pad area generally
is not a good design concept for clay talus slope topography
To reduce the down hill slope creep movements immediately
above the proposed building area due to the required site
grading the following alternate site preparation procedures
are recommended for Lot Nos 5 through 10
1 Remove the clay soil layer down to rock
and extend the removal up to the rock
outcrops above the property line
2 Construct a stabilization key immediately
above the proposed building location

For Lot Nos 3 and 4 the rock outcrops are within the
property boundary and the clayey soil is shallow Complete
removal of the adobe soil within the construction area
is recommended
Although the adobe clay layer encountered at the lower
portion of the site was thicker it is our opinion that
after the upper slope clay soil layer is removed upper
driving force against lower level will decrease the
stability of this lower flat area should have a greater
factor of safety against sliding However surface
constructions in this level area should be designed for
the highly expansive soil conditions
Earthwork and Grading
The present plan is to cut the upper slope portion of the
site about 12 feet high to provide level building areas
for the two story units
Fillsup to about 8 feet are planned in the lower area for
access road construction
Based on the laboratory testing results the clayey soils
encountered at the site have low shear strength This
clayey material is about 5 feet deep on the upper slope
area of Lot Nos 5 to 10 Based on our field reconnaissance
and surface vegetation it is our opinion that there is a
slow creep occurring within the upper clay layer at the
subject area

Foundation
For Lot Nos 3 to 10 spread footing foundations are
recommended The footings should be placed on rock
formation Allowable bearing pressure of 6000 p s f
can be used Generally the bottom of all footings
should be a minimum of 18 inches below the finish grade
Footings on sloping area should have a minimum horizontal
set back distance of 5 feet from the slope face

For the remaining lots post footing foundations are
recommended due to adobe clay soil condition
For the light load post footing foundation an allowable
bearing pressure up to 1500 p s f may be used on the
on site adobe clay soil
The bottom of the post footings should embed a minimum
of 3 feet below the finish grade
Due to the expansive nature of the underlying adobe
clay soil footing excavation should not be allowed to
dry and crack prior to placing concrete Visible cracks
developed in the bottom of the footing excavations should
be closed by soaking
Surface water should be diverted away from building
foundations
Slab On Grade
Conventional slab on grade may be used where the slab
subgrade is on site weathered rock formation or on non
expansive capping material a minimum of 2 feet above the
site expansive clay soil It should be placed over a
4 ijnch layer of base course compacted to 95 of its
maximum dry density
To protect against moisture damage of the flooring
material a polyethylene moisture barrier should be
considered

Retaining Wall Design
Retaining walls will be utilized at the garage area and along
the south eastern property line where fill is expected for
the proposed access road construction The foundations for
retaining walls should be designed in accordance with the
recommendations for the above footing foundation
It is recommended that a subsurface drainage system be provided
to prevent the build up of hydrostatic pressure behind all the
walls Granular rock backfill should be utilized as backfill
behind the retaining walt This will permit the wall to be
designed for an active lateral pressure equivalent to a fluid
density of 40 p c f for a free standing retaining wall and
of 60 p c f for a wall with support at the top
At Lot Nos 1 and 2 retaining wall will be utilized at the
cut in adobe clay soil The retaining wall should be
designed for an active lateral pressure equivalent to a
fluid density of 50 p c f for a free standing retaining
wall and of 75 p c f for a wall with support at the top
