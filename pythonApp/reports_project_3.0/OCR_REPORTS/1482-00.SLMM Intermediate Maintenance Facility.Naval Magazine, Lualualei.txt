SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is situated within the Naval Ammunition Depot which
is located along the southern shores of the West Loch branch of Pearl
Harbor Hawaii
The topography at the site is relatively flat with a slight gradient
down toward the south
The existing Building 8 is a steel frame structure with metal roof
and siding and plan dimensions of 70 by 30 feet There are asphaltic
concrete roads around the building The surrounding areas are covered
with kiawe and hale koa trees

The project consists of modifying the existing Building 8 and

Soil Conditions
The borings encountered a thin layer generally less than 2 feet
thick of fill or alluvium over coral formation Below the coral
formation the deeper borings encountered a medium dense clayey gravel
layer at about 12 5 feet depth
Groundwater was encountered in the deeper borings at 12 5 to 13 5
feet below the existing ground surface at the time of our field

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
In general our borings encountered competent sandy subsoils and
coral formation at the site which can provide adequate foundation support
for the proposed one story structure and pavements
Foundation Design
Spread or continuous footing foundation bearing on the dense in situ
coral formation may be used to support the proposed building
An allowable bearing value of up to 5000 p s f may be used on the
dense coral material The bearing value is for total dead plus live
loads and may be increased by one third for temporary wind and seismic
loads
The footings should be embedded a minimum of 12 inches below the
lowest adjacent grade
Any loose pockets encountered at the bottom of the footing excavation
should be recompacted to a minimum of 95 of its maximum dry density

established by ASTM D 1557 78 If a footing is situated adjacent to a
utility trench the trench backfill must be well compacted to prevent
potential footing settlement due to settlement of loose trench backfill
Minimal footing settlements are anticipated for the design loads if
the above recommendations are followed
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive
earth pressure developed against the buried vertical faces of the
foundation system and by frictional resistance developed between the
bottom of the foundations and the bearing soil Resistance due to
passive earth pressure may be calculated using an equivalent fluid
pressure of 300 p s f This assumes that the soil around the foundation
is well compacted The frictional resistance may be calculated by using
a coefficient of friction of 0 4 between the foundation concrete and
coral formation The above resistance values are maximum values and do
not contain a factor of safety

traffic are planned for the project

encountered at the proposed roadway locations Dense coral formation was
encountered at the ground surface at the southern portion of the site

Site Grading
Site Preparation
Prior to grading all deleterious materials such as vegetation
debris unsuitable fill and organically contaminated soils should be
removed and disposed of properly off site Following the clearing the
ground surface should be scarified to a depth of 6 inches moisture
conditioned to optimum moisture content and recompacted to a minimum of
95 of its maximum dry density as established by ASTM D 1557 78
Any soft weak or pumping areas disclosed during the recompaction
operations should be excavated to firm ground and backfilled with select
on site material or non expansive import fill material compacted to the
above specified density
Structural Fill Material and Placement
The on site coral sandy and gravel soils are suitable for structural
fill material The fills should be placed in horizontal lifts not
exceeding 8 inches in compacted thickness and compacted to a minimum of
95 of its maximum dry density
The site grading should provide drainage of surface runoff away from
all foundations

It is recommended that the site clearing and grubbing fill placement

preparation be observed by the soils engineer during construction This

subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of our
investigation If conditions encountered are different from those
presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the design
should be made

Field Exploration

drilling 7 borings at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2 A truck mounted drilling rig was used to drill the borings

encountered is presented on the Boring Logs Plates A l to A 7

standard split spoon sampler or a 2 4 inch I D split barrel sampler with
a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts used to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the boring logs at the appro
priate sample depths

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content and unit weight determinations were performed
on selected samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of
soil properties The results of these tests are presented on the Boring
Logs at the appropriate sample depths
Four sieve analysis tests were performed to determine the
gradation and classification of the on site soils The results are
presented on Plates B l and B 2
Three laboratory CBR tests were performed on bulk samples of the
near surface soils to evaluate the subgrade support characteristics of
the soils The test results are summarized on Plate B l
One direct shear test was performed on an undisturbed sample to
evaluate the strength characteristics of the in situ soil The test
result is presented on Plate B 3
