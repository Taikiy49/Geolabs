The soils encountered at the cut pad finished grade generally
consisted of very stiff brown clayey silt Field observations indicate
that these soils are stiff to hard in consistency and can provide
adequate bearing support for the proposed structure
Building Foundations
General foundation recommendations for preliminary design of the
residential structures are as follows
1 Individual footings on level areas should be embedded a minimm
of 12 inches below the ground surface All footing backfill
should be properly compacted to obtain 90 compaction and graded
to drain water away from the footing
We recommend that the soils engineer should observe the bottom of
all footing excavations prior to concrete placement
2 An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f may be used for
foundations bearing on compacted fill or stiff natural soil
3 Slab on grade construction should be set back at least 5 feet
from the tops of slopes
Twelve inch deep perimeter footings below the lowest adjacent
grade are recommended to act as a cut off wall against future
subgrade moisture changes due to drying and wetting around the
slab
For the structures it is critical that the subgrade soils below the
foundations be kept moist fora few days prior to concrete placement
It is imperative that all the lots be graded to drain water away
from house foundations to prevent saturation and softening of the
subgrade soils
