Gentlemen
The purpose of our exploratory drilling and percolation
testing was to locate potential deep injection zones
beneath the proposed project site for the purpose of
sewage effluent disposal
Our findings and recommendations are based on a required
injection rate of 95 gpn
Based on our field exploration test borings and percolation
testing it is our opinion that a deep injection well system
for the required injection rate is not feasible at this site
W O 843 00 Pee
Field Exploration
Two test borings to depths of 31 5 and 119 5 feet were drilled
at the site as shown on the enclosed site plan The deeper
boring at the makai end of the site encountered a shallow
layer of beach sand from the surface to about 14 feet
Below this surficial layer is an extensive alluvial clayey
silt layer to a depth of about its eet Hard basalt rock
was encountered at Boring 1 from 115 to 119 5 feet where the
boring terminated The shallow mauka boring Boring 2 encoun
tered an 8 foot silty sand layer over a similar alluvial clayey
silt to a depth of 31 5 feet
Based on these test borings the site is underlain entirely
by land derived alluvium capped by a thin layer of carbonate
beach sand
Percolation Testing
During the drilling operations water dissipation rates were
noted during sampling and a percolation test was run on the
makai well at a depth of 79 5 feet At depths shallower than
79 5 feet the water in the boring hole dissipated very slowly
with injection rates estimated from near zero to less than
A percolation test was run when the boring reached
a depth of 79 5 feet The boring hole was partially cased
leaving about 41 feet of open hole The test data i contained
in the attachment
