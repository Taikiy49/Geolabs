This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering services performed during

EARTHWORK OPERATION
It is desired to extend the existing Haumea Street from Wakea Street to
Uluohia Street in the Kapolei area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The new roadway
extension will measure approximately 40 feet wide and 690 feet long as shown on the
Site Plan Plate 1 Based on our field observations and the plan and profile drawing
provided we anticipate that cuts and fills of less than about 2 feet thick will be required to
achieve the finished grades for the project construction In addition new waterline and
drain line connections are planned as part of the roadway construction
Jayar Construction Inc performed the roadway and utility construction for the
Haumea Street Extension project The earthwork operation consisted of clearing the
existing ground over excavation of the surficial expansive clays preparation of pavement
subgrades placement and compaction of select borrow and base course and utility trench
excavation and backfill
Following the clearing operation the existing ground was generally over excavated
to approximately 27 5 inches below the finish grades to provide the recommended
pavement section The over excavated subgrades were generally scarified to a minimum

depth of 8 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture
content and recompacted to a minimum of 95 percent of the maximum dry density as

Utility trench backfills were generally placed in near level lifts brought to about
2 percent above the optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the
maximum dry density determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor
Select borrow and base course were generally placed in near level lifts brought to above
the optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent of the maximum dry
density
During the earthwork operation samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test
results are summarized on Plate 2 of this report
Periodic field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations to
confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures and soil conditions
were noted A total of 196 field density tests including retests of failed areas were
performed using the nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM D 2922 The results
of the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 19 of this
report These tests were taken on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed
engineered fills and backfills Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and
interpolating from on site survey stakes and construction plans These locations and
elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used
Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum
compaction was obtained and or approved by our field representative based on visual
observation and hand probing
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the earthwork operations during the roadway and
utility construction for the above project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period
covered by this report were performed in general accordance with the intent of the project
plans and specifications and our geotechnical engineering recommendations presented in
the referenced report dated and subsequent recommendations
