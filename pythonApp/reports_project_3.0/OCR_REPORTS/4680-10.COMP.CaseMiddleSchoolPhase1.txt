PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The new middle school project site is located within the existing Punahou School at
the intersection of Punahou Street and Wilder Avenue in the Makiki area of Honolulu on
the Island of Oahu Hawaii The middle school site is situated on the Diamond Head side
of the campus approximately bounded by Bishop Hall Castle Hall and Winnie Unit to the
north Slate Drive to the east Piper s Pali to the south and existing parking lots to the
west
The scope of work covered by this report includes the Phase portion of the Case
Middle School project and the new parking building structure The Phase portion
generally included the new roadways site retaining walls and underground utilities The
layout is shown on the Site Plan Plate 1 The scope of work discussed in this letter report
includes observation and compaction testing during the earthwork operations foundation
excavations and infrastructure installation

EARTHWORK OPERATIONS
Albert C Kobayashi Inc performed the site grading operations foundation
excavations utility installation and pavement construction for the Case Middle School
Phase and Parking Building project The site grading work consisted of subgrade
preparation placement and compaction of fills and backfills footing excavation and
preparation pavement construction and utility trench excavation and backfill
Following the demolition and clearing operations the over excavated subgrades
underneath future building slabs and pavement areas were scarified to a depth of about
8 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture content
and recompacted with a vibratory drum roller to a minimum of 90 or 95 percent relative
compaction of the maximum dry density as determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557
Modified Proctor
In accordance with the recommendations presented in our geotechnical
engineering report the bottom of the retaining wall footings were generally over excavated
a minimum of 18 inches and replaced with structural fill materials compacted to a minimum
of 90 percent relative compaction Soft and or loose soils encountered at the bottom of the
over excavation in localized areas were further removed by an additional 6 to 12 inches to
expose the underlying firm soils A layer of geotextile fabric was placed at the bottom of
the additional over excavation prior to backfilling with compacted structural fill materials
Due to the presence of adobe soils at the parking building some of the footing
locations were over excavated by an additional 24 inches in addition to the 18 inches
recommended for a total of 42 inches of over excavation The over excavation was
replaced with structural fill materials compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction prior to the placement of reinforcing steel and concrete
In addition our field observations indicated that the Diamond Head side of the
parking building was underlain by expansive adobe soils To reduce the potential for slab
heaving resulting from the swelling of the expansive adobe soils the slab subgrade on the
Diamond Head side was over excavated by 3 feet in lieu of 12 inches as recommended in
our report and replaced with non expansive select granular fill material Furthermore the
capping layer underneath the slab subgrade on the Ewa side of the parking building was
also increased from 12 inches to 18 inches in order to provide better transition and uniform
support During placement of the non expansive select granular fill layer the fill materials
were generally placed in near level lifts brought to above the optimum moisture content
and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
The areas within the new pavement limits were generally over excavated to the
bottom of the aggregate subbase layer The over excavated subgrades were
moisture conditioned to about 2 percent above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to at least 95 percent relative compaction The select borrow subbase and

During the site grading operations samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor
and the test results are summarized on Plate 2 of this report
Field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations on an
as need basis to confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures
and soil conditions were noted A total of 1253 field density tests including retests of
failed areas were performed using the nuclear probe method in accordance with
ASTM D 2922 and D 3017 Results of the compaction tests performed are summarized
on Plates 3 1 through 3 67 of this report These tests were performed on the prepared
ground surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills at the site Test locations and
elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes These
locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the
methods used Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified
minimum compaction was obtained
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the earthwork operations during the site grading
foundation excavation pavement and utility construction for the above project that we
observed and tested in the period covered by this report were performed in general
accordance with the intent of the project plans and specifications and our geotechnical
engineering recommendations presented in the referenced report dated and
subsequent recommendations
LIMITATIONS

some of the geotechnical risks to the project It should be recognized that there are
variations in the accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to
monitor the earthwork construction and materials
