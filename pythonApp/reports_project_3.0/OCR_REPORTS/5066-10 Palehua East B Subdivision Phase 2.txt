We have performed a geological engineering evaluation of the rockfall potential of
the upslope hillside area located adjacent to the proposed Palehua East B Subdivision
Phase II project site located in the Makakilo area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii Our
evaluation consisted of a site reconnaissance and computer modeling of potential rockfall
behavior using the Colorado Rockfall Simulation Program CRSP
The project site encompasses the existing undeveloped hillside region
Puu Makakilo located upslope from a portion of the proposed Palehua East B Phase II
subdivision as approximately shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 It was desired
to evaluate the upslope hillside region with respect to potential rockfall hazards which may
affect the proposed downslope subdivision development The potential rockfall study area
resides upslope from and adjacent to the proposed Palehua East B Phase II Subdivision
Lot Nos 110 through 134 Lot Nos 110 through 134 are located along the southern side
of Pueonani Street as shown on the Rockfall Hazard Mitigation Plan Plate 2
Existing Site Conditions
In general the natural north facing hillside of Puu Makakilo located upslope from
the proposed subdivision slopes down toward the north at average inclinations of about
five horizontal to one vertical 5H 1V The ground surfaces were observed to be
composed of reddish colored very stiff to hard residual soils overlying weathered volcanic
rock Rock outcroppings encountered at the project site generally consist of flattened low
relief exposures of hard volcanic rock The ground surfaces are vegetated with mixed
shrubbery and grasses with some widely scattered trees
Based on our site reconnaissance very few surface boulders and rock outcrops
were encountered on the hillside area located upslope of the proposed subdivision The
surface rock materials encountered at the project site generally consist of a limited number

Due to the relatively gently sloping terrain and typical tabular shape of the surface
rock fragments encountered we believe that the potential rockfall hazard from the upslope
area is generally low Furthermore based on our computer modeling of potential rockfall
behavior on the slopes our analysis indicates that over 95 percent of possible rolling
boulders on the hillside should stop within 10 to 30 feet of the initiation point on the slope
In addition previous rockfall behavior studies have shown that the initiation of rockfall
movement by natural forces on slopes flatter than about 3H 1V is rare Therefore we
believe that the proposed subdivision is at low risk for potential rockfall hazards from the
upslope hillside because of the limited surface rock source and relatively gently sloping
terrain
Potential Rockfall Hazard Mitigation
Based on the anticipated low level of potential rockfall hazard at the project site we
believe that a specialized rockfall impact barrier fence is not necessary for rockfall
protection at this project site It is our recommendation that a concrete drainage
interceptor ditch combined with a standard duty chain link fence located along the upper
property boundary is an appropriate protection system to mitigate the remote possibility of
falling rock to encroach at the rear subdivision property boundary considering the low
level of potential rockfall hazard at the site
Based on our evaluation we recommend the use of a minimum 3 foot wide by
3 foot deep concrete drainage ditch with a 6 foot tall standard duty chain link fence located
along the upper subdivision property boundary as indicated on the Rockfall Hazard
Mitigation Plan Plate 2 To facilitate the future maintenance of the chain link fence the
chain link fence should typically be located 3 feet upslope from the concrete ditch
We understand that portions of the proposed drainage ditch alignment located at
Lot Nos 124 and 125 will be positioned close to the upslope property boundary to
maximize the usable space within the lots As a result we also understand that the new
chain link fence will be located immediately adjacent to the upslope edge of the drainage
ditch in this area Therefore the fence setback distance at portions of Lot Nos 124 and
125 will be reduced from the typical 3 foot setback distance from the drainage ditch A
detail of the recommended rockfall hazard mitigation system is presented on Plate 3
