INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the design of site preparation foundations and earthwork for the proposed
diversion of the effluent disposal system of the Whitmore Village Wastewater Treatment
Plant in the town of Wahiawa on the island of Oahu
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions along the alignment of the proposed sewer line in order to develop a
generalized soils rock data set for the formulation of engineering design
recommendations pertinent to the proposed wastewater treatment plant diversion
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2432 00 Page 2
The work was done in accordance with our fee proposal to Belt Collins
Associates dated The scope of work included the following tasks
and work efforts
1 Review of available soils and geologic information pertinent to the project
site and its vicinity
2 Reconnaissance of the project site and its vicinity to make visual
observations of the surface soil conditions and to select locations for the
borings
3 Mobilization and demobilization of truck mounted drill rig to and from the
project site
4 Drilling and sampling eight 8 borings to depths of 11 5 feet below the
existing ground surface using a truck mounted auger drill rig
5 Laboratory testing of selected samples obtained during the field exploration
to determine the classification and engineering properties of the materials
encountered
6 Engineering analyses of the field and laboratory data for the purpose of
developing geotechnical engineering design recommendations
7 Preparation of this report summarizing our findings and presenting our
recommendations

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is our understanding that approximately 7 150 lineal feet of sewer line will be
installed to serve the Whitmore Village area of Wahiawa The sewer includes
approximately 4 900 lineal feet of fifteen 15 inch diameter vitrified clay pipe and
approximately 2 250 lineal feet of ten 10 and twelve 12 inch diameter ductile iron pipe
The anticipated invert of the new sewer line will range from about five 5 to eighteen 18
feet below the existing ground surface except for a length of approximately two hundred
200 lineal feet of the ductile iron portion which is suspended from the Karsten Thot
bridge structure The project also includes construction of a headworks facility
Considzration and recommendations regarding the geotechnical aspects of this
construction are not included in our scope of work
SITE DESCRIPTION
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by drilling and sampling
eight 8 borings to depths of 11 5 feet below the existing ground surface using a truck
mounted auger drill rig The borings were drilled on December 28 and 29 1989 The
approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plans Plates 2 through 7
The Boring Logs are presented in Appendix A of this report together with a detailed
description of the field exploration procedures utilized
In general the borings encountered residual soils consisting of medium stiff to hard
dark to reddish brown clayey silt to clay to the maximum depths drilled with exceptions

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
lt is our opinion that the proposed project is feasible from a geotechnical
engineering point of view The underlying stiff residual soils encountered at shallow
depths below the existing ground surface along the project site should provide adequate
bearing support for the proposed sewer line
Earth Pressure Loads on Pipe
Loads on buried conduits are influenced by the width of the trench the size of the
pipe the unit weight of backfill material and the frictional resistance between the backfill
material and the trench side walls To compute the vertical loads on the pipes an
average unit weight of 120 p c f for backfill material and a coefficient of friction of 0 25 are
recommended Earth forces acting upon the pipe increase rapidly with the width of the
trench Therefore the width of the trench should be kept to a minimum with
consideration given to the requirement for adequate working space
Bearing Capacity and Settlement
Our field exploration and laboratory testing indicated that the underlying stiff
residual soils encountered have good bearing capacity
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2432 00 Page 5
installation of the new sewer line in general will not materially increase the vertical
loading upon the underlying soils if no additional embankments are placed upon the
existing ground surface The net difference in loading between the weight of the
excavated materials and the backfill will be minimal Therefore significant settlements are
not anticipated if proper construction techniques particularly for shoring and bracing are
utilized
Trench Excavation
The anticipated depth of excavation is six 6 to nineteen 19 feet Selection of the
method and equipment to be used for excavation should be at the option of the
contractor within practical limits
Excavation of the residual soils anticipated to be encountered should not pose any
untoward difficulty although the presence of some localized boulders should be expected
Due to load considerations discussed above the width of the sewer trench
excavation should be kept to a minimum while allowing for proper installation of the
bedding sewer pipes manholes and backfill
Trench Wall Support
Due to the depth of the excavation anticipated braced shoring of the trench walls
is recommended to minimize caving in of the trench walls
Shoring and bracing must comply with all applicable safety requirements The
design of the shoring and bracing and the adequacy and safety of installation operations
should be made the responsibility of the contractor He should be required to have a
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2432 00 Page 6
qualified representative continuously present at the site to observe any conditions which
might affect stability
For calculating earth loads on struts for bracing of the sides of the trenches the
apperent pressure diagram presented on Plate 8 may be used as a quide These
pressures do not provide a factor of safety Additional loads due to surcharge traffic or
sloping ground conditions close to the edge of the excavation should be added to the
recommended pressures indicated
Even with good construction procedures some movement of the bracing system
may occur because of changes in stress during excavation The magnitude of these
movements depends greatly on workmanship for example how quickly and tightly the
supports are installed It also depends on the type of soil the depth and width of the
excavation and the type of support selected in view of the complexity of the problem
it is difficult to accurately predict the magnitude of movement of the bracing system and
adjacent ground Therefore it is important to realize that the trench wall supports should
be installed as early as practicable and that measurements of vertical and horizontal
movements should be made especially in areas where damage to existing structures may
result from such movement
Pipe Bedding
The stress distribution against the bottom of a pipe has a great effect upon the
load supporting capacity of the pipe Therefore the pipe bedding is an important design
consideration
For pipes bearing on the stiff residual soils a minimum of six 6 inches of
compacted granular bedding material should be piaced below the pipe The granular
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2432 00 Page 7
bedding should extend beyond the pipe sides a minimum width of one fourth the outside
pipe diameter and should extend half way up the pipe sides The bedding material
should consist of clean granular material such as 3B Fine Gravel or ASTM C 33 No 67
Should loose or soft sci conditions be encountered at the trench bottom an
additional eighteen 18 inch layer of the bedding material should be provided below the
pipe and the six 6 inch bedding layer to provide more uniform support for the pipe
Backfill
The trench excavations should be backfilled as soon as the proposed sewer pipes
are properly bedded and installed
In general the trench backfill should be in accordance with the Standard
Specifications for Public Works construction September 1986
The first zone of backfill extends from the bedding material to at least twelve 12
inches above the top of the pipe The first zone should consist of selected free draining
well graded granular material placed by hand or by suitable equipment so that the backfill
material is in contact with the entire periphery of the upper half of the pipe and in such
a manner as not to disturb the pipe The granular material is recommended to provide
high density backfill with minimum compactive effort Compaction of this portion of the
backfill by ponding and jetting may be permitted provided free draining conditions can be
attained The material should be well graded less than three 3 inches in size and with
no more than 10 passing the No 200 sieve
Most of the excavated on site soils can be re used as the upper zone of the trench
backfill from above twelve 12 inches over the pipe to within two 2 feet of the finished
GEOLABS HAWAII

grade except in the paved areas Backfill should be mechanically compacted to a
minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density as determined by ASTM Test Method
D 1557 78 Below paved areas the backfill within two 2 feet of the finished grade
should consist of select borrow compacted to 95 of its maximum dry density as
determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78

Manhole Structures
Structures supported on the underlying stiff residual soils may be designed using
an allowable bearing value of 3 000 p s f Minimal settlements are anticipated for these
structures
If it is necessary to place the manhole structures on loose soft soils an eighteen
18 inch layer of gravel such as 3B Fine or ASTM C 33 No 67 should be provided
below the structure to provide uniform support An allowable bearing value of 1 500 p s f
may be used on the gravel layer The net increase in load on the subsoils due to the
manhole structures is small Therefore large settlements are not anticipated if proper
shoring and bracing techniques are utilized
The lateral earth pressure acting on the underground structures will depend on the
type of backfill used the extent of the backfill material and the compactive effort required
on the material behind the structure walls Granular material such as base course rock
or sand is recommended for the backfill adjacent to the structures to reduce the
compactive effort required and to minimize the build up of additional lateral earth
pressures We recommend that the manholes be designed to resist the following lateral
earth pressure

Existing Utility Lines
Due to the fact that the project is located in a developed urban area it is
anticipated that many existing utility lines may be encountered during construction of the
sewer diversion
Therefore it is important that all existing utility lines along the proposed sewer
alignment be located accurately prior to excavation and an adequate program be devised
to protect the existing utility lines during construction

Design Review
Drawings and specifications for the proposed construction should be forwarded
to the project geotechnical engineer for review and written comments prior to
advertisement for bid This review is needed to determine adherence to the
recommendations given herein If this review is not made the geotechnical engineer
cannot assume responsibility for misinterpretation of the recommendations

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during construction of the work This is to identify
possible deviations from the design concepts specifications or recommendations and to
allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from those described
in this report The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon such
observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and approved by
the project geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during construction are different
from those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based upon
information obtained from the borings Variations of conditions between the borings may
occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident until
consiruction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re
evaluate the recommendations given in this report
The locations of the borings are approximate having been determined by taping
from structures indicated on undated Sewer Plan and Profile Sheets prepared by Kwok
Associates Inc and transmitted by Belt Collins Associates on

implied by the metnods used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the borings depict the

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is our understanding that the proposed headworks facility approximately fifty 50
feet by twenty nine 29 feet in plan dimensions will contain two 2 grit chambers two
2 communitor channels a by pass bar rack channel and an emergency overflow
channel Construction will require an excavation approximately eighteen 18 feet deep
Asphaltic concrete pavement is anticipated for the access road to the proposed
headworks facility
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
In general it is considered that the residual soils encountered in the drilled boring
can provide adequate bearing support for the proposed headworks facility
The recommendations presented in our soils report dated as
supplemented by this letter report should be followed
Excavation
The anticipated depth of excavation is approximately eighteen 18 feet The
method and type of equipment used for excavation should be the choice of the
contractor within practical limits

Belt Collins Associates Page 3

Excavation utilizing conventional excavation equipment should present no untoward
difficulty although some hard material should be anticipated in the lower depths
Excavation Supporti
Due to the depth of the excavation anticipated braced shoring of vertical excavated
faces are recommended to minimize the potential for caving in of the excavation
However sloping excavations may be considered provided sufficient space surrounding
the proposed excavation is available A slope of one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V
or flatter is recommended for a temporary sloping excavation without shoring
Construction equipment and stockpiling of the excavated material should be kept at least
ten 10 feet away from the edge of excavation
Shoring or sloping excavations must comply with all applicable safety equipments
The adequacy and safety of the excavation should be made the responsibility of the
contractor since and his representative should be required to be continuously present on
site to observe any changing conditions which might affect the excavation stability
Basement Wall
The lateral earth pressure acting on the wall of the proposed headworks facility will
depend on the type of backfill used the extent of the backfill material and the compactive
effort required on the material placed behind the structural walls Granular material such
as base course or sand is recommended for the backfill behind the wall of the
headworks to reduce the required compactive effort and to minimize the build up of
additional lateral earth pressures
The top twelve 12 inches of backfill should consist of non expansive impervious
material to reduce water infiltration behind the wall A subsurface drainage system

Footing Foundations
The footing foundations supporting the walls of the proposed headworks facility
may be designed using an allowable bearing value of 3 000 p s f Minimal footing
settlements are anticipated
If soft or loose soil pockets are encountered at the bottom of the footing
excavations the soft loose soil should be over excavated to stiff in situ soil and replaced
with select borrow compacted to a minimum of 95 percent of its maximum dry density as
determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78

Slab On Grade
Aconcrete slab on grade is anticipated for the bottom of the proposed headworks
facility It is our opinion that the residual soils encountered in the drilled boring shouid
provide adequate support for the anticipated concrete slab on grade
A minimum six 6 inch thick cushion layer consisting of No 3B Fine gravel or
ASTM C33 No 67 gravel underneath the concrete slab is recommended If soft or loose
soil pockets are encountered at the bottom of the slab it should be over excavated to stiff
in situ soil and replaced with No 3B Fine gravel or ASTM C33 No 67 gravel
