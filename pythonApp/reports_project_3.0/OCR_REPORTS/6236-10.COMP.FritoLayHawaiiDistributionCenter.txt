PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project involves the construction of a warehouse with office spaces for
Frito Lay Inc Hawaii Distribution Center in Waipahu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The
project site is triangular in shape and bounded by Waipahu Street to the southwest
Malakeke Street to the northwest and Malakeke Place to the east The Tax Map Keys for
the project site are 161 010 through 013 The distribution center will consist of steel
framed warehouse structures Site development involved minor cut and fill sections
installation of utility lines and construction of parking stalls
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services for
the site grading operations footing excavation fill and backfill placement and subgrade
preparations at the project site

EARTHWORK OPERATION
The earthwork for the Frito Lay Inc Hawaii Distribution Center was performed by
Royal Contracting Company Our observation and testing services during the construction
of the project occurred between November 2009 and September 2010
During the earthwork operations samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test
results are summarized on the attached Plate 2
The fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to near the optimum
moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry
density as determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557 In the driveway and parking
areas the subgrade and base course fill materials were compacted to a minimum of
95 percent of the maximum dry density
During the site grading and utility backfilling operations periodic field density tests
were performed in general accordance with ASTM D 6938 Nuclear Gauge Method
Results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 28 of
this report These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on
constructed fills and backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by
pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should
be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed
to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the earthwork operations during the construction
of the Frito Lay Hawaii Distribution Center project observed and tested by Geolabs in
the period covered by this report were performed in general accordance with the intent
