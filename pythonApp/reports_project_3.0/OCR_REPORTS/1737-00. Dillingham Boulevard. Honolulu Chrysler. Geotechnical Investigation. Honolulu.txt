SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The project site is located on the Honolulu Coastal Plain which is
composed primarily of interbedded terrestrial and marine sediments
During geologic history this plain had undergone several sequences of
erosion and deposition as a result of glacio eustatic sea level changes
during the Pleistocene
When the sea stood at higher levels than the present the coral
reefs grew to elevations above present sea level During low stands of
the sea portions of these reefs were eroded away During high stands of
the sea the area was a lagoon marsh or estuary area subjected to
low energy deposition of marine and terrestrial sediments
Following the last low stand of the sea the area was gradually
filled with soft and loose sediments in a lagoonal marsh environment
The coral reef is exposed in a cut bank on the mauka side of
Dillingham Boulevard across from the site
The coral reef appears to be located near the surface along the
Dillingham Boulevard side of the site
The area was filled during land reclamation projects during the
early part of the century

Soil Conditions
The soil conditions at the project site were explored by drilling
and sampling seven 7 borings to depths of 21 0 to 31 5 feet below the
existing ground surface The approximate locations of the borings are
probed further down to depths of 39 to 49 feet in an attempt to explore
the depth of a stiff soil layer underlying the loose soft saturated
lagoonal deposits Holes P 1 and P 2 were probed in order to delineate
the junction between the dense coral formation and the loose lagoonal
deposits Due to limited space accessible holes P 3 and P 4 were probed
as close as possible to the area where an addition of a mezzanine level is
to be built inside the existing warehouse The approximate locations of
the probe holes are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general the subsurface conditions disclosed by our exploration
program consist of the following units
1 A3 to 4 feet thick of surface fill layer mostly of dense coral
sand and gravel
2 a0 to 30 feet thick layer of lagoonal deposits composed of soil
varying from soft black silty clay to loose gray silty gravels
3 a coral reef formation near the surface located on the
northeastern corner of the site and
4 a dark brown silty clay to clayey silt alluvium underlying the
lagoonal deposits located on the Ewa side of the site

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The project site is underlain by loose compressible silty to sandy
lagoonal deposits Settlements should be anticipated due to the new fill
and building loads However we believe that the majority of the
settlements would occur rapidly because of the predominantly granular
nature of the compressible deposits To induce maximm ground settlements
to occur first prior to building foundation and slab construction we
recommend that the required 1 to 2 feet of new structural fill be placed
as soon as possible
The building on grade slab construction should be delayed until most
of the settlements due to the site filling have occurred about 2 to 3
months Spread or continuous footing foundations may be used for the
building support with allowance for some settlements due to the structural
loads and site filling
Our recommendations are discussed in detail and presented in the
following sections
Foundation
Our subsurface exploration encountered a generally competent upper
crust of fill at the anticipated foundation elevation for the proposed
buildings Shallow spread continuous footing may be used for foundation
support of the structures

It is to our understanding that the finished grade for the twin
showroom buildings is at elevation of 6 5 feet above Mean Sea Ievel This
requires a maximum fill of about 2 feet We recommend this site filling
operation be started immediately prior to building footing and slab
constructions With this construction procedure we believe most of the
ground settlements due to the site filling should have occurred and
detrimental effects to the structures should be minimized
We recommend that the size of spread footings be kept to a minimm
and no bigger than 5 by 5 feet Continuous footing should have a minimm
width of 2 feet Allowable net bearing capacity up to 1500 p s f may be
used in the footing design
In general spread footings should be placed at a level as high as
possible to reduce the footing load applied over the underlying
compressible deposits It is recommended however that the footing
bottom be embedded at least 12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade
Foundations should not be placed below about elevation 3 feet or special
design may be required due to the close proximity of the soft subsoils and
water level
Estimated footing settlements under the design loading are
anticipated to be on the order of less than 1 inch total and 0 5 inch
differential It is probable that some footings would be underlain by
organic silt or clay lens beneath the fill For these footings we
believe that settlements would be slightly more and occur slowly
It is recommended that individual spread footing should be separated
from slab on grade and provided with devices for periodic relevelling of
each structural column

Settlements
Based on our findings we believe that some ground settlements will
occur due to the weight of the new fill and the building loading
The compressible strata consist mainly of loose gravelly and silty
sand Therefore we anticipate that settlements in most areas would
develop quickly probably with the majority of ground settlements
occurring within the first two to three months after loading is applied
In areas underlain by pockets or lenses of soft organic silts and clays a
portion of the settlements would develop more slowly after the majority of
the settlements are complete
The calculated total areal settlements due to the 2 feet of new fill
are estimated to range from 1 to 2 inches
Settlements under foundations are discussed under the FOUNDATION
section
We recommend that the settlement of the fill be monitored to
determine when the majority of the settlement has occurred and when
building construction can begin
Slab On Grade
Concrete on grade slab is anticipated for the proposed structures
It is recommended to separate the slabs from the structural columns
and delay pouring of concrete slab as much as posible preferably near the
end of construction Concrete on grade slab should have sufficient number
of joints to accommodate future slab settlements

Site Preparation
No major fill is recommended since the additional weight of fill
will initiate ground settlements of the underlying loose and soft
subsurface soils condition encountered in the proposed project site
It is recommended that the existing concrete slab be removed in
order to provide a uniform subgrade support of slabs and new A c
pavements We recommend the upper 6 inches of the subgrade soil beneath
the concrete slab and pavement should be scarified moisture conditioned
and recompacted to 95 of its maximm density as established by ASIM
D 1557 78 test method Any loose or soft clayey soils encountered at
subgrade level should be removed and replaced with compacted select
on site granular soils or with other suitable imported select borrow
material compacted to the above mentioned standard
The required fill about 1 to 2 feet maximm to raise the finished
floor elevation for the proposed showroom and car service buildings should
be placed as early as possible in order to minimize detrimental effects of
future fill settlement on the proposed structures Select on site
granular materials and base course material removed from the existing
pavements could be re cycled for the new structural fill usage New
imported fill material should consist of non expansive select granular
materials less than 3 inches in maximm size with less than 20 passing
the 200 sieve All fill material should be placed in horizontal lifts
not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a dry density
of at least 95 of its ASTM D 1557 78 maximum density
