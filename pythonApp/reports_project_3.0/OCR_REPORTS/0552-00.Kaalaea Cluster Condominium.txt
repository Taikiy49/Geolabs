INTRODUCTION
This report presents the findings and conclusions of a soil in
vestigation performed on the subject property The purpose of
the investigation was to evaluate the soils underlain the site
with respect to the planned development to define the engineer
ing characteristics of the soils including percolation rate and
to provide recommendations for site grading pavement design and
design and construction of the foundations to support the pro
posed units
W Page 1974
The investigation included a geologic reconnaissance of the site
test borings identification and classification of the soils en
countered laboratory testing and engineering analysis
In addition to the test borings percolation tests were performed
to measure the permeability of the subsurface soils for evalua
tion of on site sewage disposal utilizing cesspools
Proposed Development
According to the site plan prepared by Philip R Ladd Architect
the property is to be developed for 12 single family residences
A J shaped roadway will be constructed along the southern property
line from Kamehameha Highway westward for approximately 300 feet
The roadway then makes a U shape turn and traverses the site to
the northern property line The proposed earthwork at the site
will generally entail cuts of 10 to 15 feet into the residual
soils in the upper reaches of the property Shallow fills of 5
to 10 feet will be placed throughout the center section and the
lower portion will remain essentially at grade
It is proposed to use cesspools for on site sewage disposal
SITE DESCRIPTION
As shown by the Location Map of Plate 1 the proposed site is in
the Kaalaea area of windward Oahu It extends from Kamehameha
Highway next to Kaneohe Bay to the top of Puu Kauai Ridge with an
W O 552 00 Page 1974
elevation of 615 feet The entire site has a heavy growth of
vegetation including Kaolekoa and California grass A temporary
roadway extends from Kam Highway for approximately 150 feet near
the center of the property Apparently the site was utilized for
the production of precast cesspool casing
Bedrock is exposed on the upper portion of the site at approxi
mately elevation 90 Large boulders were observed along the
northern end of the site and may have resulted from the grading
operationsof the adjoining property Several erosion gullies were
also noted throughout the middle portion of the site
The ground surface rises from Kamehameha Highway at an gentle
slope for about 250feet from approximately elevation 18 to elevation
40 From this level the ground surface rises steeply from eleva
tion 40 to elevation 80 at the rear of the proposed development
area
Geology
The Puu Kauai Ridge is a branch of the Koolau Range The rock
within the site belongs to a dike complex of the Koolau Volcanic
Series Various stages of alluvium blanket the lower portion of
the site The older alluvium is moderately to well consolidated
and is overlain by younger and generally poorly sorted alluvium
Near the ocean at the south portion of the property thin zones of
calcareous materials may be encountered but should be of limited
extent
W O 552 00 Page 1974
Ground Water
Generally brackish ground water can be expected between elevations
10 to 20 feet The permeability of the near surface alluvium is
low This alluvium together with the weathered zone of lava forms
a rather impermeable capping that confines water in the underlying
unweathered rock The expected percolation rates at the site are
generally poor

Representative samples were obtained from selected locations for

DISCUSSIONS RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The subsurface exploration indicated that the site is suitable for
the proposed development with the exception of the on site disposal
of sewage The permeability rates measured in the test borings in
dicate that cesspool disposal would not be feasible
Foundations
The proposed foundations will generally be bearing upon the very
stiff to extremely stiff soil which will provide an allowable bear
ing capacity of 3 000 pounds per square foot The bearing value
may be increased by 1 3 when considering wind and seismic loads
The foundations should bear a minimum of 18 inches below the final
adjacent grade and should maintain a minimum 4 foot setback from
the edge of the foundation and the finish slope
W O 552 00 Page 1974
Where decomposed rock is encountered within the foundation excava
tions the bearing capacity can be increased to 6 000 pounds per
square foot It is recommended however that all foundations bear
upon similar materials Based upon the recommendated bearing values
given above the estimated settlements are 1 2 inch total and 1 4
inch differential
The foundation excavations should be inspected prior to placement
of concrete to assure that adequate bearing has been obtained
Slabs on grade
It is our opinion that concrete slabs on grade can be utilized ex
cept for deep excavations in the vicinity of Borings 2 and 3 As
a preventive measure it is recommended that all building areas be
inspected for isolated pockets of expansive soils
Near Borings 2 and 3 expansive soils may be encountered within the
excavations Where encountered the expansive soil should be under
cut two feet and replaced with compacted non expansive soil The
backfill should be compacted in accordance with the earthwork and
grading recommendations
Where concrete slabs will be covered with moisture sensitive mate
Yials such as tile or carpeting it is recommended that a vapor
barrier be provided beneath the concrete slab Care must be exer
cised during construction so that the vapor barrier is not punctured
W O 552 00 Page 1974
Retaining Wall Design
It appears that retaining walls will be utilized at the rear of
the upper structures These retaining walls will be excavated
into residual soils and their foundations can be designed for
average bearing pressures of 3 000 pounds per square foot The
maximum toe pressure should not exceed 4 500 pounds per square
foot
Assuming that the on site soils will be utilized as backfill be
hind the retaining walls it is recommended that the walls be
designed to resist a lateral load equivalent to a fluid pressure
of 45 pounds per cubic foot A frictional factor of 0 4 should
be utilized in determining the sliding resistance of the retain
ing wall foundations
e
Weep holes or a subsurface drainage systems should be provided to
prevent the buildup of excessive hydrostatic pressures behind the
walls A surface drainage ditch may also be required to prevent
large quantities of water entering the soil behind the retaining
walls
Earthwork Site Grading
The general soil at the site are stable residual soils which may
be cut at slopes not exceeding 14 horizontal to 1 vertical 14 1
The fill slopes if properly keyed benched and compacted can be
constructed to maximum slopes of 2 horizontal to 1 vertical 2 1
W O 552 00 Page 1974
All areas to receive fill should first be cleared of all deleterious
and other organic material The ground surface should be scarified
to a minimum depth of six inches the scarified surface brought to
near optimum moisture content and the soil compacted to a minimum of
90 of the maximum dry density as determined by the American Society
for Testing Materials ASTM Test Designation 1557 72T Where the
natural ground surface is steeper than 5 horizontal to 1 vertical
the fill material should be keyed and benched into competent soil
as approved by the soils engineer This us to assure proper bond
between the newly placed fills and the existing soils
The fill placement operations should be inspected on a continuous
basis to assure that the required degree of compaction is consis
tently achieved and that the keying and benching is done in accor
dance with our recommendations
Due to the limited scope of this subsurface exploration it is re
commended that the foundation excavations be inspected prior to
concrete placement to assure that adequate bearing has been ob
tained and that subsurface conditions are consistent with the find
ings of our explorations This inspection can also be utilized to
locate and identify possible limited areas of expansive soils

FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface soil conditions at the site were explored by drill
ing five test borings The borings were drilled utilizing rotary
auger equipment with rock coring attachments The depths of the
borings varied from 15 to 55 feet below the existing ground surface
The locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan in the text
of this report
The soils and rock were classified by visual and textural examina
tions in the field by our geologist and engineer who continuously
supervised the drilling operations The classifications were
verified by inspection and testing in the laboratory All soils
were classified in accordance with the Unified Soil Classification
System The description of rocks is based on local geologic termi
nology Graphical representations of the soils and rock encoun
tered are presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 4
Soil samples were obtained by driving a 2 4 inch diameter or a
standard penetration sampler with a 140 pound hammer free falling a
distance of 30 inches The blow counts to drive the sampler are
shown on the Boring Logs at the appropriate sample depths
