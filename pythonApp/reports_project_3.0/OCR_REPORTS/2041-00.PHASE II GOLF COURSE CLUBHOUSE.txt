INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the proposed Phase II Golf Course Clubhouse in
Princeville at Hanalei on the island of Kauai Hawaii The general
location of the project site is shown on the Project Location Map Plate
1
This report summarizes the findings of our exploration and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analyses based
on the data obtained during the exploration These recommendations are
intended for design input and are not intended for construction
estimation or bidding purposes

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a clubhouse building at the site to
serve the new Phase II golf course which is currently under
construction
The proposed building will be a three level masonry and wood
structure with a basement which will extend about 15 feet maximum below
finished grade
A new access road and a new parking area are planned on the eastern
side of the new building
Site grading involving cuts or excavation up to about 15 feet in
depth is anticipated for the construction of the proposed building
Minimal site grading is anticipated for the proposed access road and
parking area

SITE DESCRIPTION
The Princeville Phase II Golf Course is situated to the north of
Kuhio Highway along the northern coast of the island of Kauai Hawaii
The project site is located at the crest of a ridge on the east side of
Anini Stream at a distance of about 500 feet from the highway
Formerly a pasture for the grazing of cattle and horses the site
and its vicinity are presently undergoing earthwork and grading
operations There are currently windrows of topsoil stockpiled on the
site for the on going golf course construction

Based on our field reconnaissance the available topographic map
and site plan it appears that the site slopes gently towards the west
and the existing ground elevation ranges from about 320 to 350 feet
above Mean Sea Level MSL
As a result of the earthwork operations ground cover at the site
has been reduced to some sparse grasses
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The project site is situated on a small ridge in a broad gently
sloping plateau along the north shore of the island of Kauai This
plateau was built during the late Pliocene and Pleistocene by the
extrusion of basaltic lavas and related pyroclastics from the Pooku vent
of the Koloa Volcanic Series
Most of the soils in the project area are residual soils derived
from the in situ weathering of these basic igneous rocks The residual
soils grade with depth to saprolite a form of residual soil which
retains the relict structures vesicles and joints of the parent rock
In pasture areas the upper foot or so of soil has been reworked as
a result of agricultural activities and generally has a very high
organic content

The exploration of subsurface conditions at the new access road and

However we anticipate that the subsoil conditions in these areas should

In general the soil conditions encountered throughout the project
site appear relatively uniform Most of our drilled borings encountered
a surface fill layer between 1 and 2 feet thick of medium stiff to
stiff clayey silt topsoil This fill is from the stockpiling of topsoil
at the site and it is anticipated that it will be removed when the
clubhouse is constructed
Below the topsoil layer the borings generally encountered stiff to
very stiff brown to mottled light brown clayey silts varying in
thickness between 6 5 and 13 5 feet These are residual soils that
derived from the in situ weathering of Koloa Basalts and are generally
structureless
Underlying the residual soils our borings encountered generally
medium stiff to stiff saprolite soils consisting of clayey silts and
silts having predominantly brown color mottled with black spots or
streaks to the maximum drilled depth of the borings Occasional soft
pockets and or seams were encountered in the saprolite layers at depths
generally less than 15 feet below the ground surface
During our field exploration groundwater was encountered in the
two deeper Borings 1 and 3 at the depths of about 30 9 to 34 5 feet
below the existing ground surface This groundwater is ina perched
condition and is generally the result of low permeability ash beds or
lenses within the Koloa Volcanic

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration encountered generally competent subsoils at
the proposed clubhouse building site However modified grading
techniques are required for the earthwork at the building site due to
the relatively high in situ moisture content of the subsoils and the
relatively high rainfall occurring in the area
We recommend that shallow footing foundations be used to support
the the proposed clubhouse structure
Site Grading
The proposed development is in the conceptual stage and the grading
plans are not available However up to 15 feet of cuts and some minor
fills are anticipated to grade the area for the proposed clubhouse
construction
Fill Compaction
Since the project site is located in a high rainfall area most of
the subsoils within the top 15 feet had in situ moisture contents
of about 38 to 60 per cent at the time of our exploration These
moisture contents are considerably higher than the ASTM D 1557 78
optimum moisture contents which range from about 32 to 36 per cent
for similar soils at nearby golf course area see Plate B 3
Therefore considerable drying of the excavated materials would
normally be required prior to their use as fill materials
However in view of the relatively frequent rainfall that generally
occurs in the area throughout the year drying of large quantities
of soils might not be readily achieved during mass grading

operation To facilitate construction it is our opinion that the
normal 90 ASTM D 1557 78 compaction standard for general site
fills as used in drier areas may be reduced to 85 for this
project Our previous experience in Princeville and other similar
areas indicates that 85 compaction is generally adequate for site
fills under roadway and light building loads
Fills within 12 inches below roadway or footing subgrade should be
compacted to a minimum of 90 of its ASTM D 1557 78 maximum dry
density
Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed All stockpiled
topsoil should be removed to original ground Any unsuitable
materials such as debris rubbish vegetation and other
deleterious materials should be removed and disposed of properly
off site
The area to receive fill should be scarified to a minimum depth of
6 inches below the cleared grade level air dried if necessary
and recompacted to the above recommended minimum of 85 of its
maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
The ground surface below pavements or footings should be scarified
to a minimum depth of 6 inches below subgrade level air dried if
necessary and recompacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry
density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78

Any loose soft or yielding areas spots encountered at the pavement
or footing subgrade level should be over excavated down to in situ
firm ground and replaced with air dried select on site soil or
imported backfill compacted to a minimum of 85 of its ASTM
D 1557 78 maximum dry density Backfill within 12 inches below
roadway or footing subgrade should be compacted to a minimum of 90
of its ASTM D 1557 78 maximum dry density
Fill Placement
Based on the field exploration and laboratory test results it is
our opinion that the on site soils have low expansion potential and
may be used as a source of fill and backfill materials If
additional off site borrow is required the material should be
tested and approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling
to the site to see that it is suitable for the proposed
construction The material should generally be non expansive and
should not contain large percentages of boulders organic or
deleterious matters
It is noted that most of the on site soils within the top 15 feet
have dry densities in the range of about 65 to 88 p c f When they
are compacted to higher densities corresponding volume reduction
will occur For earthwork quantity estimate we believe a
relatively high shrinkage factor should be used A shrinkage
factor of 8 to 19 for densification alone may be considered when
the above modified densification criteria are followed A higher
shrinkage factor may result if the soils are further dried and
compacted to 90 or more of the maximum dry density

Foundation
Based on the soil conditions we believe that shallow spread and
continuous footing foundations bearing on the recompacted in situ
subsoils may be used to support the proposed structure
An allowable bearing value of up to 2500 p s f with a minimum
footing embedment of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent grade is
recommended The bearing value is for total dead plus live load only
and may be increased by one third for short duration wind or seismic
loading
If a footing foundation is located next to a utility trench the
footing should be extended to the bottom of the utility trench or be
designed to span over the trench to reduce footing settlement due to
settlement of the trench backfill
Based on the boring data estimated total footing settlements under
the design loads are anticipated to be on the order of less than one 1
inch total and one half 1 2 of an inch differential
Resistance to lateral loads on the proposed structure may be
provided by passive soil pressure and frictional resistance A
frictional coefficient of 0 4 may be used to compute the frictional
resistance between the bottoms of the footings and the recompacted
in situ soils In addition an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 p s f
per foot of depth may be used against the buried vertical faces of the
footings to determine the passive soil pressure
Basement Walls
The walls of the maximum 15 foot deep basement of the proposed
building should be designed as retaining walls under a restrained
condition

An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f is recommended for the
walls resting on the recompacted natural ground
Granular materials such as base course rock 3B Fine gravel sand
or similar should be used as backfill material directly behind the
walls This would reduce the compactive effort required for the
backfill and reduce the build up of additional lateral pressures on the
walls Impervious on site silty material should be used for the top 2
feet of the backfill
We recommend that the walls be designed to resist a lateral earth
pressure of 55 p c f equivalent fluid pressure under restrained
condition
A subdrainage system and waterproofing should be used to prevent
the build up of hydrostatic pressure behind the walls and to reduce the
potential of moisture migration through the walls to the interior of the
building
A friction factor of 0 4 between the concrete footing bottom and
soil may be utilized to determine the sliding resistance of the walls
Additionally a passive soil resistance of 300 p c f equivalent fluid
pressure may be used

the project parking area and driveways

The above pavement sections are assumed to be placed over the
subgrade of on site materials having a relative compaction of at least
90 of its ASTM D 1557 78 maximum dry density within 12 inches below the
subgrade
Laboratory CBR tests should be performed on the actual subgrade
soils during construction and the above design sections may be revised
if necessary The recommended section considers medium traffic and that
good drainage will be provided adjacent to paved areas Due to the high
rainfall in the area it is essential that the road subgrades be shaped
to drain water away from the pavement sections Weepholes should be
provided in the parking area drainage inlet structures in order to
relieve the seepage of water which may accumulate in the subgrade
Slab On Grade
Slab on grade construction with 4 inch concrete slabs placed over a
4 inch layer of compacted base course may be used for the proposed
structure The thickened edges of the on grade slabs should extend a
minimum of 8 inches below the lowest adjacent ground surface
To protect against moisture damage of the flooring material a
moisture barrier should be considered for use below the slabs
Our laboratory test results indicate that the on site soils in
general have low swelling potential at their natural moisture
contents Therefore the slab subgrade should be kept moist and not
allowed to dry out prior to concrete placement
Utilities
Underground utilities are anticipated for this development It is
anticipated that most trenching will be in medium stiff residual or
saprolite soils

Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comment

Construction Observation
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during the excavation and
foundation construction phases of the work This is to observe
compliance with the design concepts specifications or recommendations
and to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions
differ from that anticipated prior to start of construction The
recommendations given in this report are contingent upon such
observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as
borings Variations of conditions between the field data points may
occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become
evident until construction If variations then appear evident it will
be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations given in this report
The locations of the field data points were determined by taping
from Kuhio Highway as indicated on the Topographic Map undated
provided by Princeville Development Corporation Elevations of the data
points were determined by interpolation from elevation points on the
map The physical location and elevation of the field data points
should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the method
used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown
on profiles or cross section are based upon interpolation between field
data points and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
Water level data from the field data points were collected at the
times given on the graphic representations and or in the text of this
report These data have been reviewed and interpretations made in the
formulation of this report However it must be noted that fluctuation
in water level may occur due to variations in rainfall tides
temperature and other factors
