PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this exploration was to study the subsurface soil and
water conditions at the site to obtain pertinent soil data for use in the
foundation design of the proposed structure
The main objectives were to explore the thickness and competency of
the upper crust of fill and the underlying soil deposit for spread
footing design

PROJECT CONSTDERATTIONS
It is proposed to construct an ABC Store facility at this site The
facility will consist of a single story 56 by 81 foot store and an
existing 3 by 19 foot pump island with two 2 gasoline dispenser
units To supply the gas island an underground gasoline storage area
consisting of two gallon tanks and tie down anchor slab is
planned for a 30 by 38 foot area adjacent to the ABC Store The
10 000 gallon tanks will replace two 2 existing 40 year old tanks A
3 by 8 foot freezer is planned within the store facility
An asphaltic concrete parking lot is planned in front of the
building
STTE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located at the corner of Kuhio Highway and Kukui
Street in Kapaa on the island of Kauai Hawaii
The lot is presently occupied with a Union 76 Service Station and is
closely triangular in shape with a frontage of approximately 115 feet on
Kuhio Highway and approximately 130 feet on Kukui Street

SUBSOTL_ CONDITIONS
The subsoil conditions at the project site were explored by drilling
and sampling three 3 borings to depths of about 16 to 25 feet below the
existing ground surface Boring Nos B 2 and B 3 were probed to a deeper
depth of about 37 and 40 feet respectively in an attempt to determine
the thickness of the soft loose saturated lagoonal deposits The
approximate boring locations are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general the subsurface conditions encountered in the borings
consisted of the following units
1 A 4 0 to 4 5 feet thick surface fill layer of stiff clayey silt
and loose to medium dense fine coral sand and gravel
2 a 8 0 to 9 5 feet thick layer of medium dense to dense beach
sand deposit and
3 a 23 0 to 25 5 feet thick layer of loose lagoonal deposits
composed of silty sand with coral gravel
Medium dense to hard soils were encountered below the loose lagoonal
deposits at depths of about 36 to 38 feet below the existing ground
surface
Groundwater was encountered in the borings from about 5 3 to 5 7
feet below the existing ground surface during our field exploration

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Store Building Foundation
Our subsurface exploration generally encountered loose compressible
soils underlying a 12 5 to 13 5 foot thick crust of medium dense fill
and medium dense to dense beach sand deposits We believe the upper sand
with gravel crust can provide adequate foundation bearing support for the
proposed ABC Store building utilizing shallow spread or continuous wall
footings Footing settlement due to compression of the loose lagoonal
deposit from the proposed structural loads is anticipated to be less than
one half of an inch total
The recommended minimum footing width is 12 inches with a minimm
embedment of 18 inches below the adjacent ground level An allowable
bearing value up to 3000 p s f may be used The bearing capacity is for
total dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third for
temporary wind and seismic loads
Freezer
Based on the soils encountered in the borings and water level
readings the area below the freezer appears susceptible to frost action
Frost action occurs when there is a combination of freezing
temperature high water level and subgrade soils which have high
capillary rise potential such as fine sands and silts Floor slab
heaving can occur as a result of frost action
In order to reduce the possibility of frost action below the new
freezer a capillary break layer is recommended to prevent upward
moisture migration from the water level to below the freezer A 3 foot
layer of clean gravel such as 3 Fine gravel is recommended below the
freezer slab to act as a capillary break layer

Underground Storage Tank Area
It is planned to place two 2 approximately 10 000 gallon tanks
underground in a 30 by 38 foot area adjacent to the ABC Store Each
tank will bear on and be tied down to a 10 by 29 foot by 8 inch thick
concrete anchor slab
There is presently a 12 5 to 13 5 foot thick crust and the
proposed plans indicated a total excavation of at least 11 to 12 feet
below the ground surface Therefore the anchor slab may bear on the
underlying loose soils which have low bearing capacity A stabilization
pad may be required below the anchor slab where the loose soils are
encountered
The stabilization pad may consist of a 1 foot thick layer of 3B
Fine gravel wrapped in geotextile The separating geotextile will retain
the gravel and prevent it from pushing into the soft soil
A schematic section of the recommended gravel and geotextile pad is
presented on Plate 3
Because the excavation will extend below the water level
sheeting shoring and dewatering may be required to provide a stable deep
12 foot plus excavation for concrete work We believe the braced
excavation should be properly constructed utilizing continuous sheetpiles
and extending sufficiently deep into the underlying soils below the
bottom of excavation to develop required lateral resistance
Backfill of Existing Storage Tanks
It will be necessary to remove the existing underground storage
tanks and other buried utilities In areas away from the new tanks the
resulting excavations should be backfilled up to two 2 feet below
finish subgrade with non expansive select borrow material placed in
6 inch thick compacted level lifts to 90 of its maximum dry density per
ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 From two 2 feet below finish subgrade to

Slab On Grade
Concrete slab on grade is anticipated for the proposed structure
The on grade floor slab should be placed over a 4 inch cushion layer of
gravel sized crushed rock material such as 3 Fine rock to provide
uniform support A moisture barrier should be placed under the slabs
The bottom of all thickened edge excavations extending down to
depths of 18 inches below outside the finish grade should be wetted and
kept moist until the concrete is poured The backfill against the poured
concrete slab should be adequately compacted and graded to shed water
away from the slab perimeter
Site Grading
No major site grading is anticipated
Due to the removal of the existing tanks it will be necessary to
remove the existing asphaltic concrete pavement and dispose of properly
off site or if necessary reuse in the fill
At the on set of earthwork the areas within the building grading
limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed Should unsuitable
materials such as black organic clays and silts vegetation and rubbish

off site in order to prevent contamination of the other excavation

After clearing and grubbing the areas to receive slab on grade

The site should be graded to provide drainage for surface run off
away from all foundations

Utilities
Existing utilities and previously buried cesspools should be located
and verified It may be necessary to move an existing line or adjust
structural details to accommodate the utility Should an unanticipated
condition or buried structure be encountered during construction each
condition should be evaluated by the soils engineer and the design

Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comment
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this
review is not made the geotechnical engineer can assume no
responsibility for misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Observation
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the
foundation phase of the work This is to observe compliance with the
design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow design
changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from that
anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in
this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as borings
and probes Variations of conditions between the field data points may
occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become
evident until construction If variations then appear evident it will
be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations given in this report
The locations of the field data points were approximately determined
by taping from references on the preliminary site plan from Paul Louie
Associates for the originally proposed two story building Elevations of
the data points were not available at the time this report was prepared
The physical locations and elevations of the field data points should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross sections are based upon interpolation between field
data points and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
Water level data from the field data points was collected at the
times given on the graphic representations and or in the text of this
report This data has been reviewed and interpretations made in the
formulation of this report However it must be noted that fluctuation
may occur due to variations in rainfall tides temperature and other
factors

Stores for specific application to the foundation design of the proposed
building in accordance with generally accepted geotechnical engineering
principles and practices No other warranty expressed or implied is
