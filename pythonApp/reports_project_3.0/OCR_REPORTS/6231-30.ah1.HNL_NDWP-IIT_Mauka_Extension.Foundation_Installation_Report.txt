PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The new IIT Mauka Extension is located north of the original interisland terminal
building and encompasses a portion of the previous commuter terminal building and
parking lot in Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii
The IIT Mauka Extension project includes twelve new aircraft gates new aircraft
apron pavement hydrant fueling system improvements a new concourse inclusive of
passenger holdrooms and circulation areas for passengers to access the gates The new
concourse also includes a connection to the existing Interisland Terminal The Departure
Level also will contain public amenities such as concessions restrooms and information
kiosks and associated signage
The new IIT Extension will be an L shaped building that will extend across part
of the previous commuter parking lot with a portion extending into the previous commuter
terminal building and apron The new extension is approximately 130 to 170 feet wide
and extends north mauka from the existing terminal by approximately 500 feet and then
extends west by approximately 600 feet to make the L shape The building extension
generally is a steel framed two story structure with a concrete base The approximate
building footprint of the new concourse is shown on the Overall Drilled Shaft Foundation
Plan Plate 2
The General Contractor for the project is Hensel Phelps Construction Company
and drilled shaft installation subcontractors were Healy Tibbits Builders Inc and Paul s
Electric for hardstand light poles only

a 42 inch diameter load test shaft and a total of 366 production drilled shafts with
diameters ranging between 24 and 60 inches were installed for the project A summary

Crosshole Sonic Logging CSL Tests
Crosshole Sonic Logging CSL tests were performed on the drilled shaft installed
between and starting from the trial shaft through all the
production drilled shafts for the building extension Results of the CSL tests were provided
in various reports as they were completed The following summarizes the reports issued
related to the CSL tests
CSL test results on the trial shaft were provided in the report entitled Trial Shaft
Installation Observation and Testing Report Mauka Extension
Daniel K Inouye International Airport Honolulu Oahu Hawaii prepared by
Geolabs Inc dated May 25 2018
CSL test results for the production drilled shafts for the building extension were
provided in individual reports for each drilled shaft prepared by Geolabs Inc dated
between and
Based on the CSL test results the increased first arrival times FATs were
generally less than 10 percent corresponding to a calculated wave velocity reduction of
less than 10 percent and the wave energy profiles were relatively uniform in all the
profiles with the exception of a few anomalous questionable or poor concrete quality
locations within thirteen of the production drilled shafts Anomalies encountered within
Drilled Shaft C31 C39 and G14 A were considered to be minor very
small in extent and or chipped out when obtaining cut off elevation These minor
anomalies were considered insignificant to the capacity of the foundation and accepted
without further investigation
However the anomalies identified in the remaining eight drilled shafts designated
further investigation as discussed in the following subsection

RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field observations we believe the production drilled shafts for the
Mauka Extension project has been installed in general conformance
with the project plans and specifications and within the limits of generally accepted
construction practices Based on our observations of the drilled shaft installation CSL
test results and integrity coring results it is our professional opinion that the drilled shafts
observed by Geolabs have been installed to sufficient depths to provide the design load
capacity
