At your request this firm has performed a preliminary soil
investigation for master planning of the proposed Ala Moana
sewage pump station expansion The investigation consisted
of subsurface borings laboratory testing and engineering
analyses as outlined in our proposal dated May 14 1973
The scope of this investigation was to determine the general
subsurface conditions and make preliminary recommendations
for site location and preliminary foundation selection

II FIELD EXPLORATION
The field exploration consisted of 3 borings ranging in depth
from 40 to 55 feet The drilling was accomplished using
truck mounted rotary drilling equipment with continuous flight
hollow stem auger The borings were continuously inspected by
a geologist from our firm The approximate boring locations
are shown on Plate 1 and the boring logs are presented in
Appendix A
In all borings either the Standard Penetration Test or 2 4
inch I D ring samples were taken at selected depth intervals
The Standard Penetration Test consists of driving a 2 inch O D
split spoon sampler 18 inches with a 140 pound hammer free
falling a distance of 30 inches The number of blows required
to drive the sampler the last 12 inches is termed the Standard
Penetration Resistance N and is an approximate measure of
the relative density or consistency of a soil These resistance
values are plotted on the boring logs Samples obtained were
visually classified in the field and representative material
was placed in air tight containers and returned to our labora
tory for inspection and testing
III LABORATORY TESTING
Samples were inspected in our laboratory and tested for moisture
content and gradation Moisture contents are plotted on the
boring logs Selected samples were subjected to grain size

IV SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The proposed Ala Moana Sewage Pump Station site is located
within the Honolulu Harbor situated on the southwest flank
of the elongated Koolau Volcanic Range forming the south
east portion of Oahu Hawaii
Honolulu Harbor borders on and participated in much of
the Pearl Harbor embayment s geologic history During the
Late Pleistocene Epoch coral algal reefs grew in the shallow
probably less than 100 feet deep slowly submerging embay
ment Influxes of clastic non marine alluvial debris clays
and silts apparently smothered most of the reef allowing
only a few patch reefs to grow Fluctuations of sea level
finally gave the harbor its latest natural configuration
Recent fill operations have given Honolulu Harbor its present
outline

6 Coral Algal Reef
Reef rock was encountered at about 30 feet in all three
borings The material is at least 10 feet thick as
indicated by the borings It is characterized by a
Coral Algal matrix containing pukas both empty and
containing loose SAND
B Conclusions on Geology
The site is underlain by a typically variable nearshore
reef complex It is characterized by the presence of patch
and platform reefs intermingles with reef debris sand and
gravel and land derived clays The soft to very loose
wet clays and clayey sands and gravels will have to be
taken into account during construction
C Groundwater
Groundwater was encountered in all of the three borings at
very shallow depths as shown on the boring logs Groundwater
in this area will be subjected to tidal fluctuations
V DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
In all borings very dense cemented sand and or coral algal reef was
encountered at depths varying from 28 to 30 feet below existing
ground surface A mat type of foundation is capable of support

In Boring 1 pukas or cavities were encountered in the coral

Final site selection will depend upon the difficulties in

Unconfined Compression Tests Intact core and split
spoon soil samples which apparently exhibited high
plasticity were tested for unconfined strength Length
to diameter ratio of 2 was maintained throughout the
tests to avoid scatter of test results and to facilitate
evaluation of shear strength values for design purposes
Direct Shear Test In general soil samples were tested
for shear strength using a 2 41 I D direct shear box
Each set consisted of at least two tests each test run
under different normal loads The results are plotted
as Normal Stress vs Shear Strength with a failure envelope
drawn to determine values of cohesion c and angle of
frictional resistance 4
Consolidation Test Consolidation tests were performed on
selected undisturbed ring samples of the compressible
soils to provide basic data for making settlement calculations
Porous stones were placed on the top and bottom of the samples
to allow drainage Vertical loads were applied in increments
with each load increment being allowed to consolidate prior
ee
to adding the next increment Measurements of the time
and consolidation were obtained during each load increment
and rebound was measured during the unloading portion
Consolidation test results are plotted in terms of percent
settlement versus applied loads
Swell Test The ring sample was placed between porous
stones and a 60 psf load was applied to the sample for a
24 hour period The difference between the initial and
final sample heights is the amount of swell which is expressed
as a percentage of the initial height of the sample
Maximum Density Test Samples of soils are compacted in a
mold of given size with a 10 pound rammer dropped 18 inches
to determine the relationship between moisture content and
density of the soil At least three trial points at dif
ferent moisture content are run to determine the maximum
density and optimum moisture content The test is run in
accordance with ASTM D 1557 70
