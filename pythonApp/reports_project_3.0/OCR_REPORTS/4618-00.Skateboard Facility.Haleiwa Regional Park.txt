In accordance with our proposal dated March 10 2000 we have performed a
geotechnical engineering exploration for the Skateboard Facility at the Haleiwa Regional
Park on the Island of Oahu Hawaii This letter report summarizes the findings from our field
exploration at the project site and presents our findings and recommendations for the
proposed project These findings and recommendations are intended for design input only
and are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this letter report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located at the Haleiwa Regional Park in the Haleiwa area on the
Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1
Based on the information provided it is proposed to construct a skateboard facility
consisting of a Portland cement concrete paved skating area The skating area will have
plan dimensions of approximately 90 feet by 90 feet as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The skating area will be set at approximately Elevation 11 feet Mean Sea Level MSL with
the low areas set at approximately Elevation 6 feet MSL Based on the available grading
plans the skating area will consist of fills on the order of about 7 feet thick or less and cuts
of about 1 foot deep
In addition a parking area and driveway are planned for the project We envision that
the parking area and driveway will be constructed of asphaltic concrete Exterior concrete
walkways are also planned from the parking area to the skating area

FIELD EXPLORATION
Our field exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling four borings
designated as Boring Nos 1 through 4 extending to depths of about 11 5 feet below the
existing ground surface The borings were drilled using a truck mounted drill rig equipped
with continuous flight hollow stem augers The approximate locations of the borings drilled
at the site are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on the Boring Log Legend Plate 3 Graphic representations
of the materials encountered are provided on the Logs of Borings Plates 4 through 7
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained from the borings drilled in general
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 3550 84 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils
by driving a 3 inch OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling
30 inches In addition some samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel
Sampling of Soils by driving a 2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same
hammer and drop The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third
6 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of
Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Pocket penetrometer tests were performed on selected cohesive soil samples in the
field The pocket penetrometer test provides an indication of the unconfined compressive
strength of the sample Results of the pocket penetrometer tests are summarized on the
Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths

Ground Settlements
As previously indicated ground surface settlements will occur as a result of the new
fills planned at the project site Due to the variable soil conditions and the variable fill
thickness planned at the site some differential ground settlements should be anticipated
Therefore we recommend that a settlement monitoring program be implemented at the site
to evaluate the magnitude and rates of settlements
Based on the granular nature of the compressible soil deposits encountered at the
project site we believe that a settlement waiting period of about 8 weeks should be
incorporated into the construction schedule for the project The settlement waiting period
should commence after completion of the fill placement It should be recognized that it is
difficult to accurately predict the exact time required for the filled ground to settle since the
settlement rates will be affected by variations of the subsoil structure and the history of the
subsoil deposition For the loose subsoils anticipated at the project site we believe that the
estimated settlement period could vary by as much as 50 to 100 percent from the actual
settlement period Therefore the actual settlement rates should be monitored and a
settlement monitoring program should be established to evaluate the magnitude and rate of
the estimated settlements during the settlement waiting period prior to construction of the
improvements such as the concrete flatwork on the fills In addition provisions should be
made for potential delays in the construction schedule if a longer settlement waiting period
is required
To monitor the actual settlement rate we recommend that a minimum of
five settlement gauges be installed at the locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 A typical
settlement gauge detail is presented on Plate 11 of this report The settlement gauges
should be read optically by a qualified professional surveyor and the readings should be
transmitted to Geolabs for review in a timely manner We recommend that two readings
minimum 24 hours apart for each settlement gauge be taken prior to site filling to establish
a baseline Subsequent readings of the settlement gauges should be taken on a weekly
basis for the entire settlement waiting period

Slabs On Grade
As mentioned previously the skating area will consist of concrete slabs on grade To
reduce the potential for future distress to the lightly loaded slabs on grade resulting from
shrinking and swelling of the general fills and or on site clayey soils due to changes in the
moisture content we recommend that a 12 inch thick layer of aggregate subbase course be
provided below the slabs The 12 inch thick layer of aggregate subbase course should also
extend a minimum of 12 inches beyond the limits of the slabs The aggregate subbase
course should be compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
The aggregate subbase course should be placed as soon as possible after the
subgrade soils are prepared in accordance with the recommendations presented in the Site
Preparation subsection The aggregate subbase course and the underlying subgrade soils
should be wetted and kept moist until final placement of concrete Where shrinkage cracks
are noted after compaction of the subgrade we recommend that the soils be thoroughly
moistened to close the cracks or the subgrade soils should be prepared again Saturation
and subsequent yielding of the exposed subgrade due to inclement weather and poor
drainage may require over excavation of the soft areas and replacement with compacted fill
We understand that some exterior concrete walkways will be required for the
proposed project We recommend that concrete walkways be supported on at least 6 inches
of aggregate subbase course The aggregate subbase course also should be compacted
to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Crack control joints should be provided at
intervals equal to the width of the walkways with expansion joints provided at right angle
intersections
The thickened edges of slabs adjacent to unpaved areas should be embedded at
least 12 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade Areas adjacent to the slab should
be backfilled tightly against the slab edges with the on site soil and graded to divert water
away from the slab to reduce the potential for water ponding around the slabs

Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed Vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable
materials within the grading limits should be removed and disposed of properly
off site
Soft loose and yielding areas encountered during clearing and grubbing below areas
designated to receive fill or future improvements should be over excavated to expose
firmor dense material and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with
well compacted fill The excavated soft loose and or organic soils should be properly
disposed of off site Contract documents should include additive and deductive unit
prices for over excavation and compacted fill placement to account for variation in the
over excavation quantities
In general existing utilities to be abandoned within the skateboard facility footprint
and or limits of the proposed improvements should be removed The resulting
excavation should be properly backfilled with well compacted fill materials
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content placed in 6 to 8 inch
loose level lifts and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
Utilities to be abandoned in place under the proposed structures should be backfilled
by pumping lean concrete or CLSM Controlled Low Strength Material under low
pressure
After clearing and grubbing areas to receive fill and finished subgrades should be
scarified to a depth of about 8 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent
above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent
relative compaction The compaction requirement of the subgrade should be
increased to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction in areas subjected to
vehicular traffic Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil
expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established
in accordance with ASTM D 1557 91 test procedures Optimum moisture is the water
content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density

that deleterious materials vegetation are removed and over sized materials greater
than 3 inches in maximum dimension are screened We envision that substantial
imported fill materials will be used at the project site Imported general fill materials
needed for the site filling should consist of materials with a low expansion potential

The aggregate subbase course materials required under the skateboard and walkway

recommendations prior to delivery to the project site for the intended use
General fill materials should be moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the
optimum moisture placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction The aggregate subbase
course materials under the skateboard and walkway slabs should be placed in level
lifts of about 8 inches in loose thickness moisture conditioned to above the optimum
moisture and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
The aggregate subbase course required under the pavement areas areas subjected
to vehicular traffic should be compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative
compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed
as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in
accordance with ASTM D 1557 91 test procedures Optimum moisture is the water
content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
Compaction should be accomplished by sheepsfoot rollers vibratory rollers or other
types of acceptable compaction equipment Water tamping jetting or ponding should
not be allowed to compact the fill materials
Fill Slopes
Fill slopes planned for the subject project should be constructed at an inclination of
2H 1V or flatter Fill slopes planned that will be covered by concrete flatwork may be
constructed at a maximum slope inclination of Fills should be keyed and
benched into the existing slope to provide stability of the new fill against sliding The
filling operations should start at the lowest point and continue up in level horizontal
compacted layers in accordance with the fill placement recommendations presented
above Fill slopes should be constructed by overfilling and cutting back to the design
slope ratio to obtain a well compacted slope face Excessive water should be diverted
away from the tops of slopes and slope planting should be provided as soon as the
fill slopes are completed to reduce the potential for significant erosion of the finished
slopes

Pavements
We understand that a driveway and parking area are planned for the project We
anticipate that the driveway and parking area will consist of asphaltic concrete pavements
In general we anticipate that the vehicle loading for this project will consist primarily of
passenger vehicles and light pick up trucks Based on our field exploration we anticipate
that the pavement subgrade soils will consist of both silty clays and silty sands We have
made our preliminary pavement design assuming the pavement subgrade soil will be similar
to the silty clays encountered in our borings or the imported general fills Based on the above
assumptions we recommend that the following pavement section be used for preliminary
design purposes
Driveway and Parking Pavements
2 0 Inch Asphaitic Concrete
6 0 Inch Aggregate Base Course 95 Percent Relative Compaction
6 0 Inch Aggregate Subbase 95 Percent Relative Compaction
14 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness on Moist Compacted Subgrade
The pavement subgrade soils should be scarified to a depth of about 8 inches
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to not less
than 95 percent relative compaction The aggregate subbase and aggregate base course
materials should meet the requirements of Subsections 703 17 and 703 06 respectively of
the Hawaii Standard Specifications for Road Bridge and Public Works Construction 1994
HSS The aggregate subbase and aggregate base course should be compacted to a
minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
Laboratory CBR tests should be performed on samples of the actual subgrade soils
encountered during construction to confirm the recommended pavement design sections
Field density tests should be performed on the compacted subgrades to confirm the
compaction requirements of the subgrades The recommended pavement sections assume
that good drainage will be provided for areas adjacent to pavements
Paved areas should be sloped and drainage gradients should be maintained to carry
surface water off the site Surface water ponding should not be allowed on the site during
or after construction Where concrete curbs are used to isolate landscaping in or adjacent
to the pavement areas we recommend that the curbs extend a minimum of 2 inches into the
subgrade to reduce the potential for migration of excessive landscape water into the
pavement section Alternatively a subdrain system could be constructed to collect excess
water from landscaping irrigation For long term performance we recommend a subdrain
system be constructed adjacent to paved landscaped areas

Drainage
The finished grades outside the structures should be sloped to shed water away from
foundations and slabs to reduce the potential for ponding The foundation excavations
should be properly backfilled against the slab edges immediately after setting of the concrete
to reduce the potential for significant water infiltration into the subsurface In addition
drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and should be maintained to drain
surface water runoff away from the foundations and slabs

services during construction of the proposed project The items of construction monitoring
that are critical requiring Special Inspection include observation of subgrade preparation

Other aspects of earthwork construction should also be observed by a representative
from Geolabs This is to observe compliance with the intent of the design concepts
