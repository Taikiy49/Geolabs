INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the two 2 proposed office warehouse buildings
at Lot 19 of the Halawa Business Park in Halawa Valley on the island of
Oahu Hawaii The general location of the site and its vicinity is show
on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The exploration was performed in
general accordance with our proposal dated
This report summarizes the findings of the exploration and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analyses based
on the data obtained during the exploration These recommendations are
intended for design input and are note intended for construction
estimation or bidding purposes This report was prepared solely for Lot
19 of the Halawa Business Park The findings and recommendations
presented herein should not be considered applicable to any other lot in
the same vicinity
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct two 2 office warehouse buildings on Lot
19 of the Halawa Business Park in Halawa Valley Oahu Hawaii The
proposed buildings are rectangular in shape with the front building
approximately 55 feet by 130 feet and the rear building approximately 40
feet by 100 feet in plan dimensions Based on the information provided to
us the front building will be a steel framed structure with a mezzanine
level and the rear building will be a 2 story steel framed structure with
exterior CMU walls Concrete slab on grade will be utilized for the
building floor Construction of new utility lines and flexible asphalt
pavements for the access roadway and parking areas are anticipated
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface conditions at the project site in order to develop a
generalized soil rock data set for the formilation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertinent to site grading pavement and
foundation design of the project

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration generally encountered stiff soils below the
lot It is our opinion that the stiff on site soils may provide adequate
bearing support for the proposed structures and pavement
Our laboratory testing indicates that these soils have variable
expansion potential ranging from low to high Therefore special grading
will be required to reduce potential distresses to the proposed structures
caused by heaving subsoils Ideally about 18 inches of off site select
borrow fill should be utilized overlying the existing clayey soils at the
future building slab on grade area Otherwise over excavation of 24
inches of the on site surface soil and replacement with select material
would be required
The proposed 2 story building is located adjacent to an existing
concrete lined drainage channel at the rear of the lot To prevent
over stressing the channel walls which may cause wall movement and
subsequent foundation settlement the footing foundations should be
embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane
extending upward from the bottom edge of the channel
As an alternative drilled piers which extends to below the depth
specified above may be used to support the rear wall of the building A
structural slab may be used to span the existing rear slope Hard
drilling should be anticipated since boulders were encountered in the
borings
Groundwater was encountered in Boring B 2 at a depth of about 5 feet
below the existing ground surface at the time of our field exploration
Subdrains may be required if seepage water is encountered during
construction

Site Grading
Minor site grading work is planned
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limits should be cleared of vegetation debris rubbish and other
deleterious materials These materials should be removed and disposed of
properly off site
Due to the variable expansion potential of the subsoils encountered
near the anticipated finish subgrade level we recommend that the areas
below the buildings be over excavated to a depth of at least twenty four
24 inches below the slab subgrade level
When the cut reached this over excavation level all structural and
pavement areas should then be scarified to a depth of six 6 inches
moisture conditioned and recompacted to 90 of its maximum dry density per
ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 Any loose or soft spots encountered should be
over excavated down to the firm subsoils and then replaced with compacted
non expansive select material
Imported fill material to replace the over excavated material should
consist of non expansive select granular materials less than three 3
inches in maximm size with less than 20 passing the 200 sieve Any
imported fill material should be tested and approved by the geotechnical
engineer prior to transportation to the site
The replacement fill should be placed in maximm 10 inch thick loose
level lifts and compacted to a minimm of 95 of its maximum dry density
per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
The site should be graded to drain water away from the building
foundations

Foundations
Spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the proposed
structures The subgrades of all footings should be scarified and
recompacted to 95 of its maximum dry density per ASIM Test Method
footings bearing on the on site or select fill material which has been
recompacted The bearing value is for total dead plus live loads and may
be increased by one third when temporary wind and seismic loads are
considered Estimated settlements are on the order of less than one half
of an inch total and one fourth of an inch differential
Individual footings and continuous wall footings should be embedded a
minimum of 24 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade
Foundations located next to the existing drainage channel should be
embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane
extending upward from the bottom edge of the utility drainage channel
The bottom of all footing excavations should be observed by the
geotechnical engineer prior to placement of the reinforcement to
determine if adequate bearing has been obtained and to see that loose
soils are removed prior to concrete placement
lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive
earth pressure developed against the buried vertical faces of the
foundation system and by frictional resistance developed between the
bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil Resistance due to passive
earth pressure may be calculated using an equivalent fluid pressure of 300
p c f per foot of depth This assumes that the soil around the
foundation is well compacted Unless covered by pavements or Slabs the
passive resistance in the upper 18 inches of soil should be neglected A
coefficient of friction of 0 4 may also be used

Drilled Piers
As an alternative drilled piers which extend to a depth below the
one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane specified
previously may be used below the footings near the drainage channel
Hard drilling should be anticipated since boulders were encountered
during our field exploration
Column loads of 54 2 kips were estimated by the structural engineer
For the given loading 30 inch diameter drilled piers are recommended
under the column footings A structural slab may be used to span the
existing slope at the rear of the lots
If this drilled pier alternative is selected additional infommation
on the structural details and grading is needed to evaluate lateral
loading on the drilled piers and to finalize the pier design
The drilled pier construction should be closely monitored by the soil
engineer in order to see that adequate foundation support is
achieved Since the majority of the loading capacities come from the
frictional resistance between the shaft and the in situ soils extra
care should be applied to the drilling of the pier hole selection of
concrete mix design and the placement of concrete
Prompt placement of concrete in the prepared hole as soon as it is
completed is recommended to reduce caving in of the hole The
concrete mix should be designed to minimize shrinkage in order to
maintain high frictional contact between the in situ soils and pier
concrete The concrete should be placed in a suitable manner to
avoid segregation of the aggregates

Utility Trench
New utility lines are anticipated for the project A granular
bedding consisting of six 6 inches of ASIM C 33 No 67 gravel 3B Fine
is recommended under the pipes
Granular materials should be used for the trench backfill The
backfill up to about one foot above the pipes should be lightly tamped to
reduce the possibility of damaging the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level one foot
above the pipes to the subgrades or finished grade should be placed in
maximum 10 inch horizontal lift mechanically compacted to a minimm of
90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 to reduce
future ground subsidence

Drainage
The finished grade outside the building should be shaped to shed
water away from foundations and to avoid ponding condition Also it is
advised that gutter systems be installed around the building and the the
discharge be diverted away from the foundation and pavement areas
Excessive landscape watering near foundations and slabs should also be
avoided
Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance
of the above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of
strength The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled tight
against the wall or slab footing immediately after setting of the concrete
to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and maintained
to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundations
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comments
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this review
is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for
misinterpretation of the recommendations

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
upon information obtained from the borings Variations of conditions
between the borings may occur and the nature and extent of these
variations may not become evident until construction If variations then
appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations
given in this report
The locations of the field borings were approximately determined by
taping from the Site Plan from Halawa Pacific Elevations of the data
points were approximately estimated by interpolation from elevation points
on the Grading Plan by Community Planning Incorporated dated November 20
1985 The physical location and elevation of the field data points
should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the method
used
