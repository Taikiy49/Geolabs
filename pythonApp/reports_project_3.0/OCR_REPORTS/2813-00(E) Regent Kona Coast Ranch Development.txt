From March 9 to we observed the earthwork and grading operations
performed for the above referenced project Phase construction involved the following sites
Reservoirs A B and 1 Roads B and C and Well Sites 2 and HR 4 This report
summarizes our observations and compaction tests performed at the project site
The above project site is located west of Mamalahoa Highway in the district of North Kona
of the island of Hawaii
Water Reservoir Tanks A B and 1
To reduce the possibility of foundation settlement due to collapse of loose
pockets cavities in clinker or lava tubes in the underlying basalt rock the bottoms of the reservoir
footings were probed and grouted
Prior to the commencement of the probing and grouting effort the reservoir pads were
excavated to a minimum of three 3 feet below the bottom elevation of the footings These
excavated pads were then probed with a track drill to depths of seven 7 to eleven feet 11
below the ground surface or to a minimum of ten feet 10 below the design elevation of the
bottom of the footings
The probes were spaced ten feet 10 on center around the perimeter footings with three
3 probes at each of the four 4 column footings within each tank

The probe holes and adjacent voids were grouted with a low pressure grout injected into
the holes through a PVC pipe which was extended to the bottom of the hole
Based upon our observations it is our opinion that the probing and grouting operations
were performed in a satisfactory manner and that the voids encountered in the probings were
properly filled with grout
The subgrade and base course fill placement and compaction was monitored during the
construction of the subgrade and pad Compaction of the subgrade material 6 minus material
was visually observed and probed with a steel rod prior to the placement of each successive lift
The number of passes with the sheepsfoot and vibratory roller to achieve proper compaction was
noted and maintained during the compaction effort
During the fill placement operations periodic field density tests were performed in
accordance with the American Society for Testing and Materials ASTM Test Designation D 1556
Sand Cone Method The results of the compaction tests are summarized and presented with
this report
Roads B and C and Associated Water Transmission Lines
A Geophysical Survey was performed utilizing Ground Penetrating Radar GPR within the
roadway alignments to aid in the determination of the existence of subsurface anomalies such
as possible lava tubes and cavities which have the potential to adversely affect the performance
of the finished roadway
This GPR survey was performed after a rough cut of the roadway alignment was made
Subsequent to completion of our GPR survey suspected anomalies within the upper five
5 feet of the ground surface were tipped open with a Komatsu 355D dozer The excavations
were backfilled after ripping with on site rocky materials that was moisture conditioned and
compacted with a sheepsfoot and smooth drum vibratory roller
Compaction of the subgrade material 6 minus material was visually observed and
probed with a steel rod prior to the placement of each successive lift The number of passes
with the sheepsfoot and vibratory roller to achieve proper compaction was noted and maintained
during the compaction effort
For the base course materials fills were placed in horizontal lifts not exceeding twelve
inches 12 in thickness and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent of the maximum dry
density
During the fill placement operations periodic field density tests were made in accordance
with ASTM Test Designation D 1556 Sand Cone Method and D 2922 Nuclear Gauge Method

During the grading period various on site soils were tested in our laboratory The

Initially compaction tests were performed on the waterline trench backfill to ascertain that

testing of the trench backfill material was determined to no longer be practical as the contractor
began using six inches 6 minus on site material as backfill in place of the 3 4 minus material
This change in fill material satisfied the project specifications Hand probings using a steel rod
