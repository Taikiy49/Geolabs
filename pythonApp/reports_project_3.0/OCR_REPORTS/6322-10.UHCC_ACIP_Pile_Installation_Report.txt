PROJECT DESCRIPTION
In general the University of Hawaii Cancer Center UHCC project site is within the
existing John A Burns School of Medicine JABSOM campus in the Kaka ako area of
Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The medical school campus is approximately
rectangular in shape and encompasses approximately 10 acres It is bounded in a
clockwise direction by Ilalo Street to the north Cooke Street to the east Kaka ako

Waterfront Park to the south and Keawe Street to the west The new UHCC will be
located on the southwestern side of the medical campus The layout of the new research
building is shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
Based on the referenced report for the project we recommended supporting the
new building structure on a deep foundation system consisting of 18 inch augered
cast in place piles with an allowable compressive load capacity of up to 300 kips
150 tons per pile Most of the ACIP piles would extend through the soft and or loose
lagoonal deposits near the surface and derive support primarily from skin friction and
end bearing within the coral ledges and or coralline detritus materials encountered below
the lagoonal deposit Due to the variable quality and thickness of the upper coral ledges
typical pile lengths would be about 60 and 90 feet at the western and eastern portions of
the site respectively
The general contractor for the UHCC project was Albert C Kobayashi Inc and the
ACIP pile installation subcontractor was Morris Shea Bridge Company Inc MSB
STATIC PILE LOAD TEST
In order to evaluate the adequacy of the recommended design length of the
ACIP pile foundation a static pile load test was conducted on a dedicated ACIP pile on
November 24 and 25 2010 The pile load test was conducted on an 18 inch diameter
ACIP pile with a length of approximately 90 feet relative to the existing ground surface To
better understand the load transfer mechanism at the pile soil interface the load test pile
was instrumented with nine vibrating wire embedment strain gages installed at various
depths along the length of the load test pile The location of the load test pile is shown on
the ACIP Test Pile Location Plan Plate 2
A Manitowoc 3900 series crane with a crane mounted hollow stem auger was used
to install the load test pile A Schwing 1000X pump with an average stroke volume of
0 032 cubic yards per stroke was used to pump the grout into the system The test pile
was loaded in general accordance with ASTM D 1143 Quick Load Test Method The
maximum test load on the pile was planned at 675 kips Detailed discussions and results
of the static pile load test are provided in the report entitled ACIP Pile Load Test Report
University of Hawaii Cancer Center Kaka dated August 18 2012
transmitted under separate cover
Based on the static pile load test results it appears the load test pile behaved like a
friction pile with nearly 100 percent of the maximum test load being resisted by adhesion
between the ACIP pile and the surrounding soils Based on our field observations and the
results of the static pile load test we confirmed the design embedment lengths would be
sufficient to achieve the design compressive load capacity of 150 tons per ACIP pile with a
factor of safety of at least 2 0

ACIP TEST PILE INSTALLATION
A test pile or indicator pile program which consisted of installing 14 ACIP test
piles was conducted for the project from November 30 through The
test piles were selected from the future production pile locations and are shown on the
ACIP Test Pile Location Plan Plate 2 The purpose of the test pile program was to confirm
the subsurface conditions at the project site the selected ACIP equipment and methods
of ACIP pile installation
A summary of the test pile driving conditions and observations is included in the
ACIP Pile Installation Summary Plates 4 1 through 4 11 of this report for reference Based
on the results of the test pile program it appears that the recommended design lengths
are sufficient to carry the design loads
PRODUCTION ACIP PILE INSTALLATION
The production ACIP pile installation program consisting of installing 197 augered
cast in place piles 18 inch diameter was conducted from through
under the near continuous full time observations by a field
representative from our office Morris Shea Bridge Company Inc MSB used the same
drilling grouting equipment utilized for the test pile program to install the production piles
Automatic monitoring equipment was mounted on the drilling pumping equipment with the
intent of providing auger depth grout flow rate grout pressure etc on a near continuous
basis However the equipment was not functioning reliably throughout the project and
manual monitoring was conducted by our field representative for the piles installed During
the pile installation operation logs were maintained for each ACIP pile which included
information pertaining to pile number date installed depth of pile grout volume used
grout return depth etc The locations of the production ACIP piles installed are shown on
the ACIP Pile Location Plan Plate 3 The production ACIP pile installation summary is
presented by pile number on Plates 4 1 through 4 11 of this letter report
Based on our observation of the equipment performance and subsurface conditions
encountered it is our professional opinion that the production ACIP piles have been
installed to sufficient depths and or resistance to provide the design load capacity of
300 kips 150 tons per pile with a safety factor of 2 0
