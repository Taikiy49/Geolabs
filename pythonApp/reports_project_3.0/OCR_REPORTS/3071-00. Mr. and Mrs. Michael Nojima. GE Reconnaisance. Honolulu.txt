Based on our site reconnaissance house foundation excavation observation and
our experience at the vicinity of the project site the following are our recommendations
for the geotechnical engineering design parameters
Foundations
Post and Beam
According to the architectural plans the proposed two story residence addition will
be supported by a foundation system consisting of posts and beams Based on
information obtained during our site reconnaissance an allowable bearing capacity
of up to 2 500 pounds per square feet p s f with a minimum footing embedment
of 36 inches is recommended The allowable soil bearing pressures is for dead
plus live loads and may be increased by one third for transient applications such
as temporary wind and or seismic loads
If footings are located near the top of slopes or on slopes the footing should be
embedded deep enough such that the horizontal distance from the outside edge
at the base of the footing to edge of slope is not less than eight feet 8
Footing Subgrade Preparation
The foundation subgrade should be recompacted to a minimum of 95 percent
relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil
expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density as determined by ASTM
Test Method D 1557 91

Footing excavations should not be allowed to dry before placing concrete If
shrinkage cracks appear in the footing excavations the soil should be thoroughly
moistened to close all cracks and recompacted prior to concrete placement Also
where utilities cross perimeter footing lines the utility trench backfill should consist
of a vertical barrier of impervious type material or lean concrete
The individual footing bottom preparation should be monitored by representative
of Geolabs Hawaii during the construction to evaluate if adequate bearing would
be obtained prior to placement of reinforcement and concrete
Lateral Load Resistance
Lateral load acting on the structure may be resisted by passive earth pressure
developed against the buried vertical faces of the foundation system and by
frictional resistance developed between the bottom of the foundation and the soil
subgrade
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be calculated using an equivalent
fluid pressure of 300 p s f per foot of depth This assumes that the soil around
the foundation is well compacted A coefficient of friction of 0 85 may be used to
calculate frictional resistance between the bottom of the foundations and the
bearing soils Unless covered by pavements or slabs the passive resistance in
the upper 12 inches of soils should be neglected The passive earth pressure
should be reduced for footings located on slopes

Settlements
The estimated footing settlements should be on the order of 1 inch total and
inch differential
CONSTRUCTION OBSERVATION
It is recommended that Geolabs Hawaii be retained to provide geotechnical
engineering services during the project construction to observe the footing excavations
This is to observe compliance with the intent of the design concepts specifications
and or recommendations and to allow design changes in the event that subsurface
conditions differ from those anticipated during design The recommendations presented
in this report are contingent upon the above observations
lf the actual soil conditions encountered during construction are different from
those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to the design
should be made
LIMITATIONS
The recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon information
obtained from field reconnaissance Variations of subsoil conditions may not become
evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be
necessary to re evaluate the recommendations presented in this report
The recommendations of this report are also based upon the assumption that the
project site grading has been accomplished in accordance with the project specifications
and the grading ordinance of the City and County of Honolulu
