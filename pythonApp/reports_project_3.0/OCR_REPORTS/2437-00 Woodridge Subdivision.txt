SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
The project site is situated at the base of the Koolau Pali a high
cliff which extends along most of the windward side of the Koolau Range
The climate along the Pali is typified by a high to very high average
annual rainfall
Following the main shield building stage of Koolau volcano in the
late Pliocene early Pleistocene stream erosion became the dominant
agent affecting the morphology of the area
The project site is located in a major amphitheater headed valley
along the two ridges of a drainageway Along these ridges in situ
chemical weathering of the parent Koolau Basalt has formed deep residual
soils and saprolites On the side slopes of the ridges and in the floor
of the drainageway mass transfer materials such as alluvium colluvium
and landslide debris have accumulated Some of these transported
materials are sufficiently old enough to have been subjected to in situ

Subsurface Exploration
Thé subsurface conditions at the project site were explored recently
by drilling and sampling four 4 borings to depths ranging between 16 5
and 26 5 feet below the existing ground surface with a truck mounted drill
rig Two additional hand probes and surface sampling of soils were also
performed Data obtained from previous subsurface exploration in the area
was also reviewed to augment our current information
In general the borings encountered stiff clayey silts and silty
clays to the maximum depths drilled Colors of the soils ranged from
browns to reds with frequent mottled areas resulting from the deep
chemical weathering which has occurred at the project site
No groundwater was encountered in any of the borings drilled during
our exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
It is our opinion that the proposed project is feasible provided that
our recommendations are followed However the wet soil conditions and
high to very high average annual rainfall at the project site will require
special remedial earthwork design and special construction methodology
Continued Site Grading
For the remaining minor grading to be performed the on site soils
if available appear suitable for use as engineered fill However the
project site is located in a high rainfall area the on site materials
also have very high in situ moisture contents of about 15 to 35 percent
above optimm Therefore some drying of excavated materials will be
required prior to their use as fill and or backfill

Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comment
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this review
is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for
misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the
excavation and foundation phases of the work This is to observe
compliance with the design concepts specifications or recommendations
and to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ
from that anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations
given in this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
