Project Considerations
The project site is located within the Naval Computer and Telecommunication Area
Master Station NCTAMS in the Wahiawa area on the island of Oahu Hawaii The
general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map
Plate 1
Based on the information provided it is desired to remove two existing
5 000 gallon underground diesel storage tanks adjacent to Building 261 near the
entrance into the station We understand that the planned excavations for the
underground tank removal may encroach within 5 feet of the adjacent building footprint
Therefore it is desired to evaluate the subsurface conditions at the project site for the
formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to the shoring
requirements of the planned excavations

Subsurface Conditions
Our field exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling two borings
designated as Boring Nos 1 and 2 extending to a depth of about 26 5 feet below the
existing ground surface The approximate locations of the borings are shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2
In general our field exploration at the far side of Building 261 Boring No 1
generally encountered stiff to very stiff silty clays and clayey silts throughout the depths
explored Hydrocarbon odor was not detected in the soil samples retrieved from the
drilled boring at this location However soft to medium stiff clayey soils were encountered
in the upper 10 to 15 feet of subsoils in Boring No 2 which is located close to
Building 261 In addition strong hydrocarbon odor were detected in the soil samples

Discussion and Recommendations
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered at the project site it is our
opinion that a properly designed shoring system such as steel sheet piling or soldier pile
with lagging should be installed for the planned excavations In addition it is our opinion
that excavated on site soils with contamination should not be used for backfilling and
should be disposed of properly in accordance with the applicable regulations Detailed
discussion and our geotechnical engineering design recommendations are presented in
the following sections of this letter report
Excavation Method
In general the method and equipment used for excavation should be determined
by the Contractor subject to practical limits and safety considerations Based on our field
exploration results we believe that the in situ stiff clayey soils encountered in our borings
may be excavated by conventional earthmoving equipment such as_ bulldozers
backhoes or other heavy excavation equipment
The excavated soils should not be stockpiled closer than a horizontal distance
equal to the depth of the excavation measured from the outside edge of the excavation in
order to reduce the potential for ground movement In addition the excavated soils
should be stockpiled at least 15 feet away from any underground utilities to reduce the
potential for ground movement or subsidence which may damage the underground utility
lines
Excavation Support
Based on the information provided we understand that the planned excavations
may extend to depths of over 10 feet below the existing ground surface In addition the
planned excavations will also encroach within 5 feet of the adjacent building footprint
Because of the proximity of the planned excavations to the existing structure sloping of
the sides for an open cut excavation will not be possible Therefore a properly designed
shoring system will be required for the planned excavations

Based on the soft to medium stiff subsoil conditions encountered in our field
exploration we believe that shoring of the sides of the excavations will be necessary to
protect personnel working in the excavations and to reduce the potential for
undermining the foundations of the existing structure In general we believe that
cantilever interlocking steel sheet piling or soldier piles with lagging may be used for
temporary shoring at the planned excavations Care should be exercised to avoid
truncating the existing utility lines if sheet piles are to be used as shoring If sheet pile
shoring is used the sheet piles should be driven with a suitable hammer to a sufficient
depth to reduce the potential for areal ground subsidence

be used An appropriate safety factor should be included by the shoring designer

It is important that adequate sheeting be installed prior to excavation and
maintained tight against the excavation walls with proper bracing during excavation The
properly braced sheeting is essential to reduce lateral movements of the adjacent ground
into the excavation which may result in potential settlements or distress to adjacent
structures or other improvements such as roads or utilities
The adequacy and safety of the shoring installation should be made the sole
responsibility of the Contractor His representative who should be required to be
continuously present on site during excavation and construction work will have the best
opportunity to promptly observe any changing conditions during construction such as
unforeseen subsoil conditions unexpectedly high groundwater table inappropriate
construction sequence or techniques etc which may affect shoring stability
it must be noted that some minor movements of the shoring system and the
adjacent ground may still occur due to changes in earth stresses during excavation Due
to the complexity of the stress changes it is difficult to accurately estimate the magnitude
of movement The magnitude also depends greatly upon workmanship such as how
quickly and tightly the shoring and bracing supports are installed on the subsoil
conditions the size of the excavation and the rate of excavation Therefore it is

Limitations
The findings and discussions submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from the field borings Variations of subsoil conditions from the
borings may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident
until construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to
reevaluate the findings and discussions provided in this report
The locations of the field borings indicated in this report are approximate having
been estimated by taping from the features shown on the Site Plan provided by
Environmental Chemical Corporation The physical locations of the borings should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown on graphic representations of the borings depict the
approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual
transition Water level data from the borings were measured at the times shown on the
graphic representations and or in the text of this report These data have been reviewed
and interpretations made in the formulation of this report However it must be noted that
fluctuation may occur due to variation in rainfall tides temperature and other factors
