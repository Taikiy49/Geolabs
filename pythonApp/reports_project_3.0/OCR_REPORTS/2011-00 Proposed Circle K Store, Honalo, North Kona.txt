INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
evaluation performed for the proposed Circle K store in Honalo Kona on
the island of Hawaii The general location of the project site and its
vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a Circle K convenience facility at this
site The facility will consist of a single story 40 by 67 foot store
and a 25 by 40 foot pump island with gasoline dispenser units To supply
the gas island an underground gasoline storage area consisting of three
storage tanks is planned adjacent to the pump island The convenience
facility will be surrounded by an asphaltic concrete parking lot

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our evaluation was to explore the subsurface soil
conditions at the site in order to provide pertinent soil data to aid in
the design of the site grading slabs on grade foundations and subsurface
disposal of surface runoff
The scope of our work included drilling and sampling four 4 borings
to depths ranging between 10 5 and 15 0 feet below the existing ground
surface Also included were laboratory testing of selected samples
obtained engineering analysis and evaluation of the field and laboratory
data development of the preliminary design recommendations and report
preparation
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration and laboratory test
results are presented in the appendices of this report
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located on the eastern side of Mamalahoa Highway
and south of the junction of Kuakini and Mamalahoa Highways in Honalo
Kona on island of Hawaii
The site measures about 203 by 88 feet in plan dimensions and is
generally flat Presently the site is vacant and covered with loose
gravel and broken up A C pavement
The site is bounded to the north by Teshima s Restaurant A
retaining wall approximately 10 foot high bounds the site on the east
and south

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling of four
4 borings to depths ranging from 10 5 to 15 0 feet below the existing
ground surface The approximate locations of the borings are shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2
The boring locations were based on the preliminary plans Schemes I
and II dated March 2 1988 supplied to us Subsequent to our field
exploration however the siting of the facilities was revised and two
2 of our and 4 are not within the actual areas of the
proposed pump island and storage tanks Nevertheless we believe that the
drilled borings are representative of the general subsurface conditions at
the site and are valid in developing preliminary geotechnical engineering
recommendations for the project
In general the borings encountered less than about 2 feet of
surficial fill material consisting of stiff clayey silt and medium dense
silty sand over medium hard basalt formation Cavities and loose clinker
zones were encountered in the basalt formation
Groundwater was not encountered during our exploration Detailed
descriptions of the materials encountered are presented on the Boring
Logs Plates A 1 through A 4
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Site Grading
At the time of report preparation a grading plan was not available
However minimal site grading is anticipated All imported fill material
should be non expansive free of organic and other deleterious matter and
form a dense stable soil mass when compacted to the required density
Generally the soil particles should be well graded and less than 6 inches
in maximum dimension Additionally the imported fill material should
have a minimum CBR value of 15
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2011 00 Page 4
Dk No 52
Prior to the commencement of fill placement the areas within the
grading limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed of vegetation and
deleterious material
The existing surface soils should be scarified to a depth of 6
inches moisture conditioned to near optimum content and compacted to 90
of their maximum dry density as established by ASTM D 1557 78 Any soft
yielding or pumping areas noted during the compacting process should be
excavated to firm material and backfilled with suitable material placed in
9 inch thick loose level lifts and compacted to a minimum of 90 maximum
dry density as recommended above
Store Building and Pump Island
Our subsurface exploration generally encountered competent surface
soils underlain by basalt formation which can provide adequate support
for the proposed lightly loaded Circle K Store building and pump island
Shallow spread or continuous wall footings bearing on the rock formation
or compacted fill may be used
The recommended minimum footing width is 12 inches with a minimum
embedment of 12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade Allowable bearing
values up to 5000 and 3000 p s f may be used for foundations bearing on
the basalt and compacted fill respectively The bearing capacity is for
total dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third for temporary
wind and seismic loads
Minimal settlements are anticipated due to the light structural
loads
Cavity Probing
lava flows often contain tubes or cavities and three 3 of the four
4 drilled borings did encounter small cavities Therefore to
reduce the possibility of footing settlement due to possible
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2011 00 Page 5
Dk No 52
occurrence of cavities or loose lenses near the footing bottom
elevation drilled probes may be made below each column footing and
at 15 foot centers for continuous wall footings The probe holes
should be a minimum of 3 inch in diameter and extend below the bottom
of the footing to a depth equal to two times the footing width or a
minimum of 5 feet The probe holes should be grouted with a
sand water cement mixture under low pressures
If a cavity or loose lens is suspected below a footing additional
probe holes should be made to delineate the horizontal extent and
thickness of the cavity or loose lens Then the cavity may be filled
by grouting through the probe holes Alternatively if a large
cavity is encountered near the ground surface it may be more
economical to expose the cavity and backfill with select fill The
probing and grouting operations should be monitored by the soils
engineer to see that the aforementioned recommendations are carried
out properly and to evaluate other field solutions which may be
required to properly backfill potential voids and densify the loose
soil which may occur near the footing elevations
Slab On Grade
The on grade floor slab should be placed over a 4 inch thick cushion
layer of gravel size material such as 3B Fine rock to provide uniform
Support A moisture barrier membrane should be placed under the slabs
The bottom of all thickened edge excavations should be wetted and
kept moist until the concrete is placed The backfill against the
concrete slab should be adequately compacted and graded to shed water away
from the slab perimeter
Underground Storage Tanks
It is planned to place three storage tanks underground in an area
adjacent to the Circle K store

The tanks will probably bear on the underlying rock formation which
has high bearing capacity and can provide adequate support Since small
cavities and voids were encountered in the borings should any large
cavities or voids be encountered during construction they should be
evaluated by the soils engineer and the design should be modified if
necessary
Based on our field exploration special excavating procedures such
as hoeram may be required to excavate down to the underlying basalt The
actual rippability of the material should be determined by the contractor
on site
To reduce the compactive effort required for the backfill a clean
gravel such as ASTM C 33 No 67 is recommended
Pavement and Parking Areas
Based on the results of our field study the preliminary pavement
design is as follows
2 Inch Asphalt Concrete
6 Inch Base Course Basalt Aqgregate
8 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
CBR tests should be performed on the actual subgrade soil samples
during construction and the above design section should be verified in
the field
Subsurface Disposal
It is our understanding that a subsurface disposal system such as
dry well or cesspool may be required for surface runoff disposal of the
project area
GEOLABS HAWAII

Based on our field exploration the basalt formation underneath the
site is moderately jointed with numerous voids and clinker zones We
believe that the formation may have a medium degree of permeability with
good drainage characteristics The actual size of the subsurface disposal
system will depend on the design quantity of the future surface runoff
It should be pointed out that for the subsurface disposal system
the loss of infiltrative capacity and soil clogging can occur in the
future by combined physical chemical and biological factors The latter
is the most important factor since an organic layer of several millimeters
thick may form at the soil rock liquid interface Other factors such
as rainfall perched groundwater level etc may also affect the
permeability to some extent Therefore the disposal system may probably
require periodic maintenance
Utilities
Existing utilities should be located and verified in the field It
may be necessary to move an existing line or adjust structural details to
accommodate the utility Should unanticipated condition s or buried
structure s be encountered during construction they should be evaluated
by the soils engineer and design modified if necessary
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comments
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earth work and foundation recommendations given herein If this
review is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility
for misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Observation
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the
excavation and foundation phases of the work This is to observe

to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ
from that anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations

Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as
borings Variations of conditions between the field data points may
occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become
evident until construction If variations then appear evident it will be
necessary to re evaluate the recommendations given in this report
The location of the field data points were determined by taping from
structures indicated on the Site Plan dated March 17 1988 provided by
Kajioka Okada Partners Inc Elevations of the data points were not
available at the time of report preparation The physical location of the
field data points should be considered accurate only to the degree implied
by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling four
4 borings to depths of 10 5 to 15 0 feet below the existing ground
surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 A
truck mounted drill rig was used to drill the borings
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by visual and
textural examination in the field by our geologist who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory All soils
were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A summary of the materials encountered are
presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 4
Soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving a 2 inch
standard split spoon sampler with a 140 pound hammer free falling 30
inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the last 12 inches are shown
on the Boring Logs at the appropriate sample depths
Rock core samples were obtained by using a rotary wash NX core
barrel
