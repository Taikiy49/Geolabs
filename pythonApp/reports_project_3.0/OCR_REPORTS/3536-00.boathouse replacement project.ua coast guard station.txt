REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lavas from
two shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated
to be of the middle to late Pliocene in age and Koolau is estimated to be of the late
Pliocene to early Pleistocene in age The project site is located on the seaward fringe
of the Honolulu Coastal Plain The coastal plain is composed of interbedded alluvial
marine and post erosional volcanic deposits Extensive development of the plain and
reclamation of the low lying areas have given the land its present shape
There is evidence that a basaltic flow of the Honolulu Volcanic Series possibly
originating from the Makuku vent in Nuuanu or from Punchbowl reached the eastern
portion of what is now Sand Island During the Waipio stand of the sea at elevation
60 feet Mean Sea Level MSL this layer of basalt would have provided a resistant
surface which could have diverted the flows of Nuuanu and Kapalama Streams to the
west The diversion of these two streams would have resulted in deposition of
extensive subaerial sediments consisting of alluvial clays and silts These exposed
alluvial deposits would have become consolidated due to seasonal moisture changes
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3536 00 Page 8
Subsequently as the sea level rose to the Waimanalo stand at elevation 25 feet
MSL and then fell to its present level the stream laid alluvium was buried beneath
backreef deposits of sands as a fringing coral reef grew and emerged on the seaward
margin of the island Land reclamation projects during this century have built Sand
Island to its current topography
SITE DESCRIPTION
The Coast Guard Reservation facility is located on Sand Island on the southern
shore of the island of Oahu Hawaii Sand Island is bounded by Honolulu Harbor to the
northwest through southeast and the Pacific Ocean to the south as shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1 Sand Island is comprised of reclaimed land built from
coral and marine earth materials dredged from the Honolulu Harbor and the adjacent
Keehi Lagoon These dredged materials were placed on top of a submerged coral reef
platform
The proposed boathouse replacement project site is located within the Coast
Guard Reservation on the northeast side of Sand Island The project site is accessible
by Sand Island Access Road from Nimitz Highway The Matson Sealand shipping
facility is located immediately west of the Coast Guard Reservation facility The
existing boathouse site is bounded by Kimball Drive to the west and Healy Avenue to
the south as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 Honolulu Harbor resides to the north
through east of the site
The site is generally level with existing ground surface elevations ranging from
about 5 to 7 feet MLLW Concrete wharfs border the northern portion of the site and
Honolulu Harbor where a fueling station ferry terminal and rescue boathouse currently
exist The surrounding ground surface outside of the existing boathouse footprint is

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Our field exploration at the proposed boathouse replacement site indicated that
the area is generally underlain by surficial fill and lagoonal deposits extending to
approximately 12 to 18 feet below the existing ground deck surface The fills were
generally underlain by medium hard to hard coral formation of approximately 20 feet
thick overlying less competent coralline deposits of varying densities with occasional
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3536 00 Page 11
dense layers extending to the maximum depth explored of about 99 feet below the
existing deck surface Water levels were encountered at depths of about 3 3 to 6 feet
below the existing ground deck surface Our laboratory test results performed on the
in situ soils indicate a highly corrosive environment to steel and metals in contact with
the soils Therefore we recommend that all sub structures be properly designed for
protection against potential corrosion
Based on our field exploration and engineering analyses we recommend that
16 inch octagonal precast prestressed concrete piles be used to support the
proposed building and finger pier structures The piles would derive support primarily
from end bearing on the hard coral formation underlying the site as encountered in our
borings drilled for the project An allowable compressive load capacity of 40 tons may
be used for the recommended pile foundation design The allowable compressive load
capacity for the piles is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third for
transient loads such as wind or seismic forces An uplift capacity of 8 and 7 kips per
pile may be used in design for the on land and off shore piles respectively
In general we believe that the on land piles should be embedded a minimum of
12 inches into the hard coral formation underlying the site Because of the potential
presence of boulders and localized hard coral ledges at the project site predrilling at
the proposed on land pile locations may be required prior to pile installation to facilitate
pile driving operations through the obstruction Therefore we recommend that
predrilling at the proposed on land pile locations be included in the contract documents
as a contingent item with additive and deductive unit prices Our field exploration
indicated that hard coral formation may be encountered at relatively shallow depths
below the mudline at the project site Therefore we believe that predrilling into the
hard coral formation for a minimum of 10 feet below the dredged bottom would be
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3536 00 Page 12
necessary for the off shore piles to facilitate the pile driving operation and help to
maintain plumbness of the piles
A concrete sheet pile bulkhead is planned for the project construction We
envision that the sheet piles may either be placed in an excavated trench and backfilled
with tremie concrete or driven through predrilled holes to the desired embedment
depth The first approach will allow the contractor to maneuver the sheet piles with
greater flexibility for conformance with the lines and grades prior to setting the sheet
piles in place with tremie concrete However it will require excavating a relatively deep
trench into the hard coral formation which may present difficulties during the excavation
operations due to localized zones of very hard coral formation For the second
approach the diameter of the predrilled holes should not exceed the smallest
dimension width of the concrete sheet piles in order to provide adequate contact
between the sheet pile wall and the adjacent coral formation If an over sized predrilled
hole is encountered during construction the annular space between the sheet piles and
predrilled holes should be grouted with tremie concrete to provide the recommended
lateral load capacity Based on a preliminary estimate the concrete sheet piles should
be embedded a minimum of 12 feet below the dredged bottom to provide the lateral
restraint for a cantilevered sheet pile wall It should be noted that the installation
procedures of the sheet piles may have a significant effect on the lateral earth
pressures and lateral load resistances of the sheet piles Therefore the contractor
should submit their sheet pile installation procedures for our review and comment for
conformance with our intent and recommendations
In general the bearing walls of the boathouse structure should be supported by
pile foundations designed in accordance with the recommendations presented in the
Pile Foundations section of this report We believe that the non bearing walls of the
boathouse structure may be supported on a shallow foundation system provided that
GEOLABS HAWAII

new fills are not anticipated An allowable bearing pressure of 2 000 p s f may be used
for design of continuous footings bearing on the recompacted surficial granular fill soils

connections to the wall and column foundations be used

Pile Foundations
Based on the current design concept driven pile foundations are proposed for
support of the new boathouse building and finger pier structures Based on our field
exploration and engineering analyses we recommend that 16 inch octagonal precast
prestressed concrete piles be used to support the proposed building and pier
structures Pile foundations are recommended for support of the load bearing walls and
columns For the non bearing walls a shallow foundation system may be used for
support which is further discussed in the Building Foundations section of this report
The piles would derive support principally from end bearing on the coral formation
underlying the site as encountered in our borings drilled for the project Based on the
relatively light structural loads anticipated for the subject project an allowable
compressive load capacity of 40 tons may be used for the recommended pile
foundation design The allowable compressive load capacity for the piles is for dead
plus live loads and may be increased by one third for transient loads such as wind or
seismic forces Piles should be spaced a minimum of 3 feet center to center to avoid
reduction in capacity due to group action

Uplift Load Resistance
Uplift loads may be resisted by a combination of the dead load of the driven pile
and by shear along the pile surface and the adjacent soils An uplift capacity of
8 kips per pile may be used in design for the on land piles driven without
predrilling For the off shore piles an uplift capacity of 7 kips may be used in the
design of the pile foundations These uplift load capacities already include the
dead weight of the piles
Lateral Load Resistance
Lateral loads imposed on the building structure may be resisted by a
combination of the lateral load capacity of the driven piles and the passive
pressure against the vertical faces of the pile caps Passive earth pressure
against the vertical faces of the pile cap may be estimated using an equivalent
fluid pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth p c f Lateral
loads imposed on the finger pier structure may be resisted by the lateral load
capacity of the pile only

Pile Foundation Settlements
Settlements of pile foundations will result primarily from the elastic compression
of the pile member and subgrade response We estimate that the total
settlement of the pile supported foundation to be less than inch with differential
settlements between piles not exceeding about one half that amount We
believe that these settlements are essentially elastic and should occur as the
loads are applied
Pile Hammer Selection
The piles should be driven with a hammer capable of delivering a rated energy
between 30 000 and 50 000 foot pounds The hammer should be equipped with
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3536 00 Page 19
an energy level control such that driving within the loose soft soil may be
controlled at a lower energy to reduce the tensile stress development within the
piles which could damage or cause cracking of the piles
Determination of driving equipment to be used for this project should take into
consideration the matching of the pile hammer with the weight of the piles to
be driven This will result in the pile hammer efficiently delivering the required
energy to the top of the pile and also reducing damage to the piles Geolabs
Hawaii should review the pile driving equipment proposed to evaluate if the
equipment is capable of installing the piles to the recommended capacity The
contractor should forward pile and driving equipment data forms to
Geolabs Hawaii prior to construction for evaluation Geolabs Hawaii should
review the submittal and assess the driveability of piles with the use of the
selected hammer Due to possible variation in the length of the piles unit prices
should be obtained during bidding for add ons shorter piles etc
Pile Driving Criteria
For preliminary design purposes pile driving may be terminated when the
estimated pile tip elevation is achieved and when steady aggregated blow counts
are at least 40 blows per foot for the last foot or 4 blows per inch for the last
3 inches This preliminary driving criteria should be confirmed during the test
pile driving and may be modified to accommodate variations of soil conditions
in the field
Each pile should be driven continuously without interruption A pile may be
rejected or its design capacity may be reduced when the driving resistance is
interrupted for more than four hours and the pile cannot be driven to the required
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3536 00 Page 20
depth During soft driving i e when the pile tip is within soft loose soils the
hammer stroke or energy should be carefully controlled such that significant
tensile stresses do not develop within the pile
Because variations in soil strength characteristics are likely to exist in the
bearing soils the supporting capacity of each pile must be confirmed in the field
by a representative from our office during driving It should be noted that the pile
design was developed from our pile analysis using the field exploration data A
test pile program should be carried out to confirm our preliminary pile design and
establish a driving criteria The production pile driving criteria may be revised
based on the test pile program

Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling
three borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 3 at the approximate locations
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The depths of our borings range from about 49 to
99 feet below the existing ground deck surface The borings were drilled with a
truck mounted drill rig equipped with continuous flight augers and rotary tools as
necessary
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by
testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified
Soil Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representation of the
materials encountered are presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 1 through

Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils
California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts
needed to drive the sampler the last 12 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown on the
Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Core samples of rock materials encountered at the site were obtained using
diamond core drilling techniques in general accordance with ASTM Standard
Practice D 2113 83 Diamond Core Drilling for Site Investigation Core drilling is a
rotary drilling method which uses a hollow bit to cut into the rock formation The
rock material left in the hollow core of the bit is mechanically recovered for
examination and description
Recovery REC is used as a subjective guide to the interpretation of the
relative quality of rock masses Recovery is defined as the actual length of material
recovered from a coring attempt versus the length of the core attempt such as if
3 7 feet of material is recovered from a 5 0 foot core run the recovery would be
74 percent and would be shown on the Logs of Borings as REC 74
The Rock Quality Designation RQD is also a subjective guide to the relative
quality of rock masses RQD is defined as the percentage of the core run that is
sound material in excess of 4 inches in length without any discontinuities discount
ing any drilling induced fractures or breaks If 2 5 feet of sound material is
recovered from a 5 0 foot core run the RQD would be 50 percent and would be
shown on the Logs of Borings as RQD 50 Generally the following is used to

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content and unit weight determinations were performed on selected
soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties The
results of these tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate
sample depths
Three sets of pH ASTM D 2976 and minimum resistivity ASTM G 57 tests
were performed on selected soil samples obtained from our field exploration The
results are summarized on Plate B 1
Two sieve analyses with hydrometer ASTM D 422 test were performed on
selected samples of the soils to evaluate the gradation characteristics of the soils
and to aid in soil classification Graphic presentation of the grain size distribution is
presented on Plates B 2 and B 3
One California Bearing Ratio CBR ASTM D 1883 test was performed ona
bulk sample collected for pavement design Results of the CBR test are presented
on Plate B 4
