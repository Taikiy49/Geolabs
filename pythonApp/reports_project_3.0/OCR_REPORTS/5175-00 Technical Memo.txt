REMARKS Per your request this technical memorandum has been prepared to
provide our recommendations on concrete pavement design for the
proposed fire lane to serve the above mentioned project In general we
anticipate that the vehicle loading for the new pavements for the project
will consist primarily of fire trucks Based on the near surface soil
conditions encountered in our field exploration we believe that the
following rigid pavement section may be used for preliminary design
purposes
Rigid Pavements
6 0 Inch Portland Cement Concrete
6 0 Inch Aggregate Base Course 95 Percent Relative Compaction
12 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness over Compacted Moist Subgrade
The pavement subgrades should be scarified to a depth of about 8 inches
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to a minimum 95 percent relative compaction The aggregate
base course should consist of crushed basalt aggregate compacted to not
less than 95 percent relative compaction CBR and field density tests
should be performed on the actual subgrade soils encountered during
construction to confirm the adequacy of the above sections
Paved areas should be sloped and drainage gradients should be
maintained to carry the surface water off the site Surface water ponding
should not be allowed on the site during or after construction Where
concrete curbs are used to isolate landscaping in or adjacent to the
