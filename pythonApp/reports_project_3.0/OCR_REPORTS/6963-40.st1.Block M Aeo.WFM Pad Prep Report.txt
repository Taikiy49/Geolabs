As requested, we have been providing geotechnical engineering services during the 
site grading operations at the Block M Aeo project within Ward Village in Honolulu on the 
Island of Oahu, Hawaii. This letter report summarizes our geotechnical engineering 
observation and consultation services performed for the new Whole Foods Market portion 
of Block M Aeo project. The discussions presented herein are subject to the limitations noted 
at the end of this letter report. 
Project Considerations 
The project site is at the southwest corner of Queen and Kamakee Streets in the 
Kakaako area of Honolulu on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. The project site was occupied by 
the former Office Depot and Nordstrom Rack stores and an on-grade parking lot. 
The Block M Aeo project consists of constructing a new high-rise building with retail, 
commercial space and a parking garage at the podium levels. The new 40-story building 
will have a total of 466 studios and one-bedroom, two-bedroom and three-bedroom units 
with over 498,164 square feet of total residential living space. Swimming pools, a fitness 
center, covered picnic and lounging areas will be provided on the seventh podium level. 
The development also will provide a total of about 81,399 square feet of commercial 
space, including a 49,944-square foot Whole Foods Market store that will occupy the 
Kamakee Street side of the property. In addition, a total of 1,297 residential and commercial 
parking spaces will be provided by the development.

Subsurface Conditions 
The project site generally is underlain by surface fill materials placed over lagoonal 
deposits followed by coralline deposits. In general, surface fills consist of medium dense to 
dense silty sands and gravel and extend down to about 3 to 5 feet below the original ground 
surface (before site grading). The surface fill layer generally is underlain by lagoonal 
deposits consisting of very loose to loose silty sands and gravel. The lagoonal deposits 
generally extend to depths of about 17.5 to 22 feet below the existing ground surface. The 
coralline deposits below the lagoonal deposits generally consist of interbedded soft to 
medium hard coral formation and medium dense to dense sandy and gravelly coralline 
detritus. Pockets of loose silty sands may also be present in the coralline detritus in some 
areas. In addition, a thin layer of weathered volcanic tuff formation is present at about 95 to 
105 feet below the ground surface. 
Groundwater may be anticipated at depths of about 3 to 5 feet below the existing 
ground surface (before site grading). The groundwater levels anticipated generally 
correspond to about Elevations -1 to +2 feet Mean Sea Level (MSL). Based on the proximity 
of the project site to the Pacific Ocean, groundwater levels generally are expected to vary 
with tidal fluctuations. In addition, groundwater levels may change due to seasonal 
precipitation, surface water runoff, and other factors. 
Discussion and Recommendations 
Based on the grading plan, the original ground surface elevations within the Whole 
Foods Market building envelope ranged from about +4 to +5 feet MSL.  The site was 
generally graded to a finished pad elevation of about +7.5 to +7.8 feet MSL.  Site grading 
generally consisted of placement of up to about 2 to 3.5 feet of new fills to achieve the 
design grades. 
After clearing and grubbing, areas to be filled and subgrades in cuts were generally 
scarified to a depth of about 8 inches, moisture-conditioned, and compacted to a minimum 
of 90 percent of the maximum dry density determined in accordance with ASTM D1557 
(Modified Proctor).  The compaction requirement for the finished subgrades in areas 
subjected to vehicular traffic was increased to a minimum of 95 percent relative 
compaction. 
Fill and backfill materials generally consisted of excavated materials from the project 
site and some imported materials. In general, fill materials consisted of materials less than 
3 inches in maximum particle size.  Fills and backfills required to raise the grades were 
generally placed in level lifts of about 8 inches in loose thickness, moisture-conditioned and

Limitations 
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities occurring 
simultaneously on a construction site.  Therefore, some of the earthwork may have been 
performed without our observation.  However, the level of earthwork observation and testing 
services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some of the geotechnical risks to 
the project.  It should be recognized that there are variations in the accuracy and statistical 
repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the earthwork construction and 
materials.  Our services were performed in accordance with generally accepted standards 
of geotechnical engineering practice; no warranty is expressed or implied.  Provision of 
construction monitoring by an engineer is not insurance, nor does it guarantee construction 
of any type.  The contractor shall retain responsibility for the quality of the work, for adhering 
to the plans and specifications, and for repairing defects regardless of when they are found.
