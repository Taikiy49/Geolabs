ESTIMATED SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Based on our visual observations and previous explorations in the general area we
anticipate that the debris basin and culvert excavations will generally be in basalt rock
formation Permanent groundwater levels are not anticipated within the excavations The
subsurface conditions will be verified with drilled borings at the debris basin and culvert
sites
PRELIMINARY RECOMMENDATIONS
Debris Basin Culvert Excavation
We anticipate encountering a basalt rock formation within the debris basin and
culvert excavations We understand that basin excavation depths will range from about
10 to 50 feet deep It is anticipated that the near surface materials may be readily
excavated with normal heavy excavation equipment such as excavators and ripping with
large bulldozers However the deeper excavations into the dense basalt formations may
require the use of hoerams chipping or blasting
The above discussions regarding the rippability of the surface materials are based
on visual observation of the existing basalt formation and field data from previous field
exploration performed in the general area of the project site
We believe that the excavated rock materials would likely break down to boulders
and cobbles with some gravel and few fines However it should be noted that large
boulders will likely be generated and should be expected
Debris Basin Cut Slopes
Based on the available information it appears that most of the cut slopes would
expose basalt rock with frequent fractures In general we believe that the cut slopes
planned at the basin sites may be designed with a slope inclination of one horizontal to
one vertical 1H 1V or flatter However we anticipate that clinker zones and or voids may
be encountered at the cut slope face In the event that clinker zones are exposed at the
cut slope face the cut slope may need to be flattened or protected with GRP Large
cavities that may be exposed on the cut slope face should be backfilled and or grouted if
necessary We believe that the permanent cut slopes in basalt formation do not need
benches for cut slopes up to 30 feet in vertical height

Surcharge stresses due to areal surcharges traffic loads line loads and point

in the design For uniform surcharge stresses imposed on the loaded side of the culvert
wall a rectangular distribution with uniform pressure equal to 29 percent of the vertical

Additional analyses during design may be needed to evaluate the surcharge effects of

Lateral loads acting on the structures may be resisted by frictional resistance
developed between the bottom of the foundation and the supporting subgrade soils and
passive earth pressure developed against the embedded near vertical faces of the
foundation system A coefficient of friction of 0 45 may be used between the base of the
structure and the granular bedding material to resist lateral loads
