PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located along Kapili and Poipu Roads in the Poipu area on the
Island of Kauai Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on
the Project Location Map Plate 1 Based on the information provided it is proposed to
relocate the overhead lines to underground along Kapili and Poipu Roads as part of the
Road project
Based on the information provided it is desired to evaluate the excavation
characteristics of the subsurface materials along the utility alignments by drilling a total of
six borings four along Kapili and two along Poipu Roads extending to a depth of about
10 feet below the existing ground surface Unconfined compression tests will be
performed on selected rock cores to evaluate the hardness of the rock formation
encountered during our field exploration

FIELD EXPLORATION
Our field exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling six borings
designated as Boring Nos 1 through 6 extending to depths of about 10 5 and 15 5 feet
below the existing ground surface The borings were drilled using a truck mounted drill
rig equipped with continuous flight solid stem augers and coring tools
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on the Boring Log Legend Plate 3 Graphic
representations of the materials encountered are provided on the Logs of Borings
Plates 4 through 9

Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained from the borings drilled in
general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of
Soils by driving a 3 inch outside diameter OD Modified California sampler with a
140 pound hammer falling 30 inches In addition some samples were obtained from the
drilled borings in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1586 Penetration
Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 2 inch OD standard penetration
sampler using the same hammer and drop The blow counts needed to drive the sampler
the second and third 6 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown as the Penetration
Resistance on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Core samples of rock materials encountered at the site were obtained using
diamond core drilling techniques in general accordance with ASTM Standard Practice
D 2113 Diamond Core Drilling for Site Investigation Core drilling is a rotary drilling
method that uses a hollow bit to cut into the rock formation The rock material left in the
hollow core of the bit is mechanically recovered for examination and description
Recovery REC is used as a subjective guide to the interpretation of the relative
quality of soil rock masses Recovery is defined as the actual length of material recovered
from a coring attempt versus the length of the core attempt For example if 3 7 feet of
material is recovered from a 5 0 foot core run the recovery would be 74 percent and
would be shown on the Logs of Borings as REC 74
The Rock Quality Designation RQD is also a subjective guide to the relative
quality of rock masses RQD is defined as the percentage of the core run that is sound
material in excess of 4 inches in length without discontinuities discounting drilling induced
fractures or breaks If 2 5 feet of sound material is recovered from a 5 foot core run the
RQD would be 50 percent and would be shown on the Logs of Borings as RQD 50
Generally the following is used to describe the relative quality of the rock based on the
Practical Handbook of Physical Properties of Rocks and Minerals

LABORATORY TESTING
Selected soil samples and rock cores from the field exploration were tested in the
laboratory with an emphasis on evaluating the moisture density and strength
characteristics The following laboratory tests were performed on selected soil samples
and rock cores obtained from the field exploration
Moisture Content ASTM D 2216 and Unit Weight ASTM D 2937 determinations

properties The test results are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate

formation encountered Results of the unconfined compression tests are presented on

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our field exploration program for the project consisted of drilling and sampling six
borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 6 extending to depths of about 10 5 and
15 5 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate locations of the borings are
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Our borings generally encountered a surface soil layer consisting of soft to stiff
sandy silts and silty clays extending to depths ranging from about 0 5 to 4 5 feet below the
existing ground surface Hard to very hard basalt rock formation was then encountered
and extended to the maximum depth explored of approximately 15 5 feet below the
existing ground surface In filled cavity and or possible void appeared to be present in
Boring No 4 between depths of about 5 0 and 11 5 feet below the existing ground surface
Groundwater was not encountered in the drilled borings at the time of our field
exploration It should be noted that groundwater levels are subject to fluctuation due to
seasonal precipitation time of the year and other factors occurring off site Detailed
descriptions of the subsurface materials encountered from our field exploration are
presented on the Logs of Borings Plates 4 through 9 of this report

FINDINGS AND DISCUSSION
In general our field exploration encountered silty and clayey soils overlying basalt
rock formation extending to the maximum depth explored of approximately 15 5 feet below
the existing ground surface Groundwater was not encountered in the drilled borings at
the time of our field exploration
In general good construction practices should be utilized for the installation and
backfilling of the trenches for the new underground utilities at the project site The method
and equipment to be used for trench excavation should be determined by the contractor
subject to practical limits and safety considerations In addition the excavations should
comply with the applicable federal state and local safety requirements Trench shoring
design and installation should be the responsibility of the contractor
In general we recommend that granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of
No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation be used for support under the pipes
Free draining granular materials such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67
gradation should also be used for the initial trench backfill up to about 12 inches above
the pipes to provide adequate support around the pipes if appropriate It is critical to use
this free draining material to reduce the potential for formation of voids below the
haunches of pipes and to provide adequate support for the sides of the pipes Improper
trench backfill could result in settlement of the backfill and damage to the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipes to
the top of the subgrade or finished grade may consist of on site soils with a maximum
particle size of 3 inches or select granular fill material The backfill should be placed in
maximum 8 inch level loose lifts and mechanically compacted to not less than 90 percent
relative compaction to reduce the potential for appreciable future ground subsidence
Where trenches are below pavement areas the upper 3 feet of the trench backfill below
the pavement finished grade should be compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative
compaction
Based on the unconfined compression tests ASTM D 2938 performed on selected
rock cores obtained from our field exploration the unconfined compressive strength of the
rock formation encountered at the project site generally range from about 8 800 to
20 700 pounds per square inch pci which may be considered hard to very hard in terms
of strength characteristics In addition the Rock Quality Designation RQD of the majority
of the rock formation encountered at the site range from about 70 to 100 percent which
may be considered good to excellent as shown in the table presented under the Field
Exploration section
Based on our field exploration and laboratory test results the majority of the basalt
rock formation encountered in our borings appeared to be hard to very hard in nature
Therefore we anticipate that difficult excavation conditions may arise during construction

The above discussions regarding the rippability of the materials are based on our

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from the field borings Variations of subsoil conditions beyond the
field borings may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become
evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident Geolabs should
be contacted to re evaluate the recommendations presented in this report
The locations of the field borings indicated in this report are approximate having
been estimated by taping from the features shown on the Electrical Site Plans transmitted
by Nakamura Oyama and Associates on The physical locations of the field
borings should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown on the graphic representations of the borings depict
the approximate boundaries between the soil types and as such may denote a gradual
transition Water level data from the borings were measured at the times shown on the
graphic representations and or in the text of this report These data have been reviewed
and interpretations made in the formulation of this report However it must be noted that
fluctuation may occur due to tidal fluctuation variation in rainfall temperatures and other
factors
