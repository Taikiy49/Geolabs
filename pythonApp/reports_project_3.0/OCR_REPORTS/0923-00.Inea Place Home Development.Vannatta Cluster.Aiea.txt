SITE CONDITIONS
Geology
The existing Aiea Heights ridge is believed to be a part of
the old Koolau Range volcanic series The project site is
located on this ridge Below the south side of the property
several small drainageways appear to have been formed on the
steep decomposed rock slope faces
The surface soil is a reddish brown clayey silt which originated
from the in place weathering of the underlying parent basalt
rock on this ridge The soil Survey maps prepared by the
Soil Conservation Service describes the surface material as
Manana silty clay over basalt
Surface
The proposed development site slopes down from north to
south towards the valley The ground surface elevations
range from about 560 at the north to about 450 feet
in the south

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on the results of our limited geologic reconnaissance
it is our opinion that the site can be developed for the
proposed residential development
Erosion Control
During heavy rains erosion of the upper foot of the slope
will occur Site grading should provide drainage away from
the slope surface and should run off either to the existing
Inea Road or down to the valley The existing trees and
vegetation on the slope surface provide ground cover for
erosion control and slope Stability protection Site
clearance and grubbing on the existing slopes should be
minimized for building construction
Foundation
It is our opinion that the in place soil and weathered
basalt rocks below the surface material can support the
proposed residential structures
Spread and line footing foundation can be utilized to
Support the post footings The footing should bear a mini
mum of 18 inches below final adjacent grade into stiff or
decomposed rock materials
