performed for the proposed new Robello Residence to be located at Lot No 102 of the
Kahalakua Subdivision in the District of Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii The

noted at the end of this report

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from two shield
volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be middle
to late Pliocene in age and forms the bulk of the western third of the island The younger
shield volcano Koolau is estimated to be late Pliocene to early Pleistocene Ice Age and
forms the majority of the eastern two thirds of the island Waianae became extinct while
Koolau was still active and its eastern flank was partially buried below Koolau lavas
banking against its eastern flank
The project area is located on the southern flank of Koolau and is composed of
basaltic rock built by the extrusion of the lavas of the Koolau Volcanic Series These
rocks are generally characterized by flows of jointed dense vesicular basalt with
interbedded thin clinker layers For the last 1 to 2 million years in situ chemical
weathering of the Koolau lava flows has occurred forming a mantle of residual soils
which grade to saprolite with depth In general saprolite is composed mainly of silty
material and is typical of the tropical weathering of volcanic rocks The saprolite grades to
basaltic rock formation with depth Grading operations for the housing developments in
the area within the last 5 years have brought the site to its present form

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Our field exploration generaliy encountered near surface clayey silt fill soils
overlying weathered basalt rock formation at greater depths Based on the subsurface
conditions encountered it is our opinion that shallow foundations consisting of spread
and or continuous footings may be used to support the proposed new residence An
allowable bearing pressure of up to 2 500 pounds per square foot p s f may be used for
the design of footings bearing on the stiff to very stiff clayey silt soils encountered at
shallow depths
Laboratory tests indicate that the near surface clayey soils exhibit moderate
shrink swell potential when subjected to moisture fluctuations In order to reduce the
effects of the moderately expansive soils due to moisture fluctuations we recommend
that the foundations be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent
finish grade To reduce the potential for distress to the future slab on grade resulting
from the swelling of the near surface moderately expansive soils we recommend that
concrete slabs on grade required for the project be supported on at least 6 inches of
non expansive select granular fill materials Special attention should be given to the site
preparation recommendations to reduce the effects of the moderately expansive soils on

Siabs On Grade
Our field exploration disclosed that the project site is generally underlain by
near surface clayey soils with moderate swelling potential when subjected to moisture
fluctuations Unless floor slabs constructed above the moderately expansive soils are
properly designed there is a potential for future distress to the lightly loaded slabs on
grade resulting from shrinking and swelling of these soils due to changes in the moisture
content To reduce the potential for structural distress resulting from the swelling of the
subgrade soils we recommend that the subgrade soils be properly prepared and capped
with at least 6 inches of non expansive select granular fill material The select granular fill
material may stop at the perimeter footings A 6 inch thick capping layer of clayey
material such as the on site clayey silt should be placed at the surface adjacent to the
slab to inhibit water infiltration below the slabs The area adjacent to the slabs on the
exterior should be backfilled tightly against the slab and graded to divert water away from
the slab to reduce the potential of water ponding around the foundation In lieu of the
clayey capping material on the exterior an exterior walkway may be considered
The select granular fill should be placed soon after moisture conditioning and
compaction of the clayey subgrade soils The prepared capping fill and the underlying
subgrade soils should be wetted and kept moist prior to final placement of concrete
Subgrade soils should be prepared in accordance with the recommendations presented
in the Site Grading section of this report Compaction requirements for the subgrade
soils and select granular fill are also discussed in the same section of this report
For the interior building slabs not subjected to vehicular traffic we recommend
that a minimum 4 inch thick layer of cushion fill consisting of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM
C 33 No 67 gradation or Basaltic Termite Barrier BTB be placed below the slab to
provide more uniform support of the slabs and to serve as a capillary break To reduce
future moisture infiltration and subsequent damage to floor coverings an impervious
moisture vapor barrier such as a plastic membrane is recommended on top of the gravel

Fills and Backfills
In general the excavated on site clayey silt soils may be re used as a source of
general fill material provided that they are free of vegetation deleterious materials
and rock fragments greater than 6 inches in maximum dimension The on site
clayey silt soils may not be used as the select granular fill material recommended
under the building slabs should consist of non expansive select granular material
such as crushed coral mudrock basalt or cinder sand The material should be
well graded from coarse to fine with no particles larger than 3 inches in largest
dimension and should contain between 10 and 30 percent particles passing the
No 200 sieve The material should have a laboratory CBR value of 20 or more
and should have a maximum swell of 1 percent or less when tested in accordance
with ASTM Test Designation D 1883
The select borrow subbase material should meet the material requirements for
Select Borrow for Subbase Course as specified in Section 30 of the Standard
Specifications for Public Works Construction Department of Public Works City
and County of Honolulu September 1986 Imported fill material should be tested
by Geolabs Hawaii for conformance with these recommendations prior to delivery
to the project site for its intended use

moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture placed in level lifts not

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part
upon information obtained from borings Variations of subsoil conditions between the
borings may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident
until construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to
reevaluate the recommendations provided in this report
The location of the borings indicated in this report are approximate having been
estimated by taping from the features and property lines shown on the Grading and
Erosion Control Survey dated prepared by Fujita and Associates Inc
Elevations of the borings were also estimated from this plan The physical locations and
elevations of the borings should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the
methods used
The stratification lines shown on graphic representations of the borings depict the
approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual
transition Water level data from the borings was measured at the times shown on the
graphic representations and or in the text of this report This data has been reviewed and
interpretations made in the formulation of this report However it must be noted that
fluctuation may occur due to variation in rainfall tides temperature and other factors

two borings designated as Boring Nos 1 and 2 to depths of about 16 5 and 20 feet

continuous flight augers The approximate locations of the borings are shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2

testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified

encountered in the borings are presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 and A 2
