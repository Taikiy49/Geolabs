SITE CONDITIONS
The subject lot is located on the lower makai side of Kihi
Street approximately 350 feet west of Laukahi Street in the
Waialae Iki Unit 4 area It has an area of 11 068 square feet
The existing ground surface slopes down from Kihi Street at
gradients of about 2 horizontal to 1 vertical
Overcast soil and rubbish were noted over the upper portions
of the lot near Kihi Street The entire lot is presently
covered with weeds
Houses were presently under construction in adjacent lots
on both sides of the subject lot
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
The proposed residence will be a split level structure utilizing
three floor levels Concrete slab on grade is planned and it
will be bearing on new backfill behind the proposed retaining
walls

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Our review of the project subdivision grading plans indicates
that the subject lot is situated over a former gully and about
10 to 15 feet of fill had been placed over the upper northern
half of the lot to construct it to existing grade The two
borings drilled near the upper boundary of the lot indicates
that the fill in this area appears to be relatively stiff or
compacted and consists of a mixture of clay and gravel with
boulders

Due to the inaccessibility of the middle and lower portions of
the sloping lot borings were not drilled in those area Based
upon the above boring data it appears that it could be assumed
that the fill at the lower portion of this lot has been properly
compacted However the actual fill condition should be further
inspected inthe field after it is exposed during foundation
excavation
Laboratory soil testing indicate that the near surface soils
at the site are moderately to highly expansive Special
foundation preparation will be required for slabs and foun
dations on these soils
Site Preparation and Fill Placement
The site should be cleared of vegetation debris and loose
overcast soil
Fills and backfill should be placed in 9 inch loose lifts
compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density
established by ASTM D 1557 They should also be keyed and
benched into the existing ground to properly bond the new
fill to the slope
The on site soils have moderate to high swelling potential
and therefore should not be re used as fill material within
2 feet below concrete slab on grade swimming pool and other
walkway This expansive on site material should not be used
as a backfill behind retaining walls unless it is placed a

It appears that additional off site select borrow will be

Foundations
Spread or continuous footing foundations can be used for
the proposed residence An allowable bearing value of
2500 p s f can be used on properly compacted fill or
natural ground
Footings on level areas should be embedded a minimum of 18
inches below the adjacent finished grade to resist the
expansive effects of the near surface soils Footings on
sloping areas should be embedded down an additional amount
to obtain a 5 foot minimum horizontal set back distance
measured from the outer edge of the footing out to the slope
face This additional embedment depth is required to help
resist the downhill slope creep and to develop the recommended
bearing capacity of the soil

The bottom of all foundation excavations should be cleaned
of loose material and bear on firm soil The excavations
should be observed by a soils engineer to see that adequate
bearing soil has been obtained
The ground surface should be shaped to drain water away from
the foundations
Slab On Grade
The laboratory tests indicate that the near surface soils
are moderately to highly expansive therefore we recommend
that the on site soils within 2 feet below the slab subgrade
level should be overexcavated and replaced with select non
expansive soil Quarry waste is an acceptable source of
select fill
The slab subgrade should be kept moist and protected from
drying prior to concrete placement The subgrade soils
should be compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry
density
It is recommended that slab on grade be placed over a 4 inch
layer of base course compacted to 95 of its maximum dry density

Slab on grade should be separated from foundations When a

system around the perimeter of the walls is recommended to relieve

Basement and Retaining Wall
Retaining walls generally less than 5 feet high and basement
walls up to about 10 feet high are planned for the residence
Well graded granular material such as 3B Fine Aggregate should
be used as a granular drainage filter backfill material minimum
18 inches behind the walls Granular material such as select
borrow should be used as backfill a minimum of 5 feet behind the
walls
The recommendations given under Foundations should be followed
for the design of the wall foundations
The walls should be designed to resist lateral earth pressures
as follows
Walls unrestrained at the top 35 p c f equivalent
active condition fluid pressure
Walls restrained at the top 45 p c f equivalent
at rest condition fluid pressure
Additional loads due to surcharge should be included
where applicable
A friction factor of 0 4 can be utilized to determine the sliding
resistance of the wall

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the footing excavations and backfill
operations be observed by a soils engineer during construction
to see that adequate bearing has been obtained and that the
subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of our
subsurface investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested
and approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the
site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon
the above inspections

APPENDIX A
FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling 2
borings to depths of 20 to 23 feet and 3 hand auger probings to 1 5 feet
below the existing ground surface at the approximate locations shown on
the Site Plan Plate 1 Truck mounted drilling equipment was used to
drill the borings in the accessible area and hand auger equipment was
utilized for the probings in the inaccessible areas
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by visual
and textural examination in the field by our engineering geologist
who continuously monitored the drilling operations The classifica
tions were reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory
The soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A graphic presentation of the soils is presented
on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 7
In the borings soil samples were obtained by driving either a
a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts to drive the sampler
the last 12 inches are shown on the boring logs at the appropriate depths
In the hand auger probings disturbed samples were obtained from
the auger cuttings
