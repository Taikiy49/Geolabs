Production Pile Driving Report
Royal Ko Olina Condominiums
MDA Parcels Lot 4616
Ko Olina Resort
As requested we have observed the production pile driving operations at the
above referenced project site from May 1 1990 to
Sixteen and one half inch octagonal precast prestressed concrete piles were
utilized for the foundations A single acting diesel hammer with a rated
driving energy of approximately lbs was used to drive the piles A total of 4 123
piles were driven for the above project Individual pile driving records have been
transmitted during the course of pile driving The total driven depth below ground level
is 222 113 feet and 1 118 splice cans have been used
Variable Subsurface Conditions
Based on the pile driving observations very erratic subsoil conditions were
encountered at the Mauka area of the project site The coral formation in this area
appears to be weakly cemented with isolated ledges and not strong enough to provide
the design pile end bearing capacity in many locations
As a result piles in certain areas had to be driven over 120 feet deep almost 60
feet deeper than the originally estimated pile length to provide the required design
capacity Due to the erratic nature of the coral formation determination of production pile

Questionable Piles
Due to the erratic nature of the coral environment some piles were broken or out
of plumb during driving These piles of questionable capacity are summarized in
Appendix B Note that over 80 of the questionable piles are located in the mauka area
of the project site Zones A through E
Replacement piles were driven for the questionable piles where low capacities
were anticipated At Pile driving of replacement piles was waived by
redesigning the caps and grade beams A memo dated was issued
to the project structural engineer explaining the rationale and our opinions
Pile No AQO1 was driven to 160 feet below grade and the soil set up freeze
effect was evaluated by redriving the pile Based on the retap data it appeared that Pile
No A001 may provide the design capacity by friction and no replacement pile was
necessary Per the contractor the replacement pile for Pile Number C170 may be waived
as authorized by the project structural engineer
Based on our observations and pile load test results we believe that the piles were
driven to sufficient resistance and or depths to provide the design allowable bearing
capacity of 75 tons per pile
