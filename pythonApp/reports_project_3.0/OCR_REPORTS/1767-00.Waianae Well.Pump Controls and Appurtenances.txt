Based on our boring and laboratory test data the
subsurface conditions at the project site in
general consist of a 4 to 5 foot thick layer of
highly expansive bouldery adobe clay over a
relatively thick 12 to over 40 foot thick layer
of generally tightly packed boulders and cobbles
It is our opinion that the reservoirs may be
constructed on the underlying dense boulder layer
as the stiff to locally cemented soil matrix is
generally thin

reservoirs due to ground swelling and shrinkage
we recommend that expansive clay be removed below
the footings and slabs and replaced with
compacted select borrow to provide a uniform
bearing layer Beneath the concrete cut off

and H V beneath the bench mainly in
boulders

cut slope ratio is 2 1 H V above 5 feet vertical
height and H V for the bottom 5 feet of
the cut

INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this study is to explore the subsurface conditions at
the project site to
1 Evaluate the subsurface soil rock conditions in order to provide
recommendations for the design of the foundations for the two
2 proposed reservoirs and one 1 pump control building
2 Evaluate the overall slope stability of the proposed reservoir
site grading excavations
3 Identify the general subsurface soil conditions along the
proposed access road for pavement section design and site
stability evaluation of the upper soil layer for waterline
installation
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests which we
used in the engineering analysis to develop the basis of our
recommendations are presented in the appendices of this report
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located on the northern foothill of the Waianae
Valley approximately 5000 feet east of Farrington Highway and just north
of Kawiwi Stream
In general the topography slopes southward into Waianae Valley at
about 5H 1V or less The ground elevations at this site vary from about
190 to 425 feet above Mean Sea Level The proposed reservoirs will be
located at approximately elevations 371 and 415 feet

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Foundation
The borings test pits and laboratory testing disclosed that the
project site area is in general covered by a 4 to 5 foot thick highly
expansive clay adobe layer which contains a large amount of cobbles and
boulders The adobe clay is fissured and Slickensided indicating the
probable occurrence of past Slope surface creep movements This upper
clay layer is expansive and unsuitable for direct support of the
reservoir structure Immediately beneath the adobe clay is a 12 to
over 40 foot thick layer of generally tightly packed boulders and
cobbles
Based on the field exploration the underlying boulders and cobbles
appear to be fairly tightly packed with thin stiff to locally cemented
soil matrix Therefore it is our Opinion that the underlying boulder
layer should be used to support the reservoir loads
0 5 M G Reservoir
Based on the boring and test pit information it is our opinion that
the spread footing foundation may be constructed on the underlying
dense boulder layer Cuts of 2 to 14 feet are planned at the
reservoir site It is anticipated that site grading at the
reservoir site will remove the surface clay layer below most of the
tank structure except at the downslope end where smaller cuts are
planned Where encountered at subgrade level the expansive clay
should be removed and replaced with compacted selected borrow fill
If the boulder surface at the finished grade is irregular we
recommend that the ground be over excavated a minimum of 12 inches
below the footings and slab and replaced with compacted off site

Pump Control Building
Boring No 8 drilled at the site encountered about 3 foot of
cobbles and boulders over rock formation The upper half of the
building site will be cut and therefore cobbles and boulders are
anticipated at subgrade level However the lower half of the site
appears to consist of uncompacted clay and boulder fill which was
probably generated from previous water well drilling site grading
The remaining previously placed loose fill should be removed down to
natural ground and replaced by compacted off site select borrow
fill Select borrow fill material such as crusher waste should be
compacted in 8 inch loose lifts to obtain a dry density of at least
95 of its ASTM D 1557 78 maximum density
For the above replacement fill slope which may eliminate the need
for the proposed CRM wall a 2 1 H V slope ratio is recommended
Fills placed where the existing ground is steeper than 5 horizontal
to 1 vertical should be keyed and benched into the existing firm
ground to properly bond the new fill to the Slope For details see
Plate 3 This detail is essential for the stability of the fills
particularly to reduce the potential of sliding along the
fill in situ ground contact The filling operation should start at
the lowest point and continue up in level compacted layers
Shallow spread or continuous footing may be used for foundation
Support of the structures provided that the above suggested special
site grading and backfill procedures are properly followed It is
recommended that the footing bottom be embedded at least 12 inches
below the lowest adjacent grade

consist of cutting the hillsides up to about 28 feet for the 0 5 M G

grading for the control building will consist of minor cut into exposed
basalt bedrock Also included are excavation and filling for the
construction of concrete cut off ditches CRM headwalls and GRP outlet
instruments and valves housing facilities drop intake structures and the
access road

Adobe Clay Subgrade
Should adobe clay still remain below the excavation bottom during
excavation for cut off ditches headwalls and other related
structures a 12 inch over excavation should be made and replaced
with base course type gravel The adobe clay subgrade below the
gravel layer should be kept moist to minimize excessive ditch and
other structure damages due to subsequent ground heave
Prior to backfilling and construction of the ditches and other
structures hydrated lime should be sprinkled over the adobe
subgrade which should then be moistened using a sprinkler for a
minimum of 24 hours
The fissured and slickensided surficial adobe clay material
indicates the previous occurrence of and the future potential in
progressive surficial downslope ground movement For the proposed
reservoir sites the hillsides behind the tank will be cut for a
vertical height of about 16 to 28 feet for the 0 05 M G and 0 5
M G reservoirs respectively
Based on the boring information the surficial adobe clay layer is
about 4 to 5 feet thick at the 0 05 M c reservoir site The
proposed cuts will therefore be made partly in adobe clay near
the top and partly in bouldery materials along the lower portion of
the cut slope

Excavation
The field exploration indicates that the two reservoir sites are
underlain at fairly shallow depths by hard basalt cobbles and

require special excavating procedures such as hoe ram equipment or
blasting to make the proposed cut
The above discussions regarding the rippability of the subsurface
materials are based on our visual observations of the existing rock
outcrops in the area and interpretation of the boring core data

Trench Backfill for Water Main
Based on boring and test pit information portions of the 12 inch
diameter water main along the access road alignment will be placed in the
bouldery adobe clay layer To avoid potential saturation of the
expansive clay and subsequent loss of strength due to water pipe breakage
because of potential progressive downhill Slope creep we suggest the use
of 3B Fine gravel as trench backfill beneath the minimm 3 foot
compacted off site select borrow cover The gravel surrounding and
beneath the water pipe can act as a subdrain in the event of a water pipe
breakage The 3B Fine gravel subdrain should be daylighted to drain
into suitable outlets

remolded samples to evaluate the swelling potential of the soils in both
natural and compacted conditions These test results are summarized on
