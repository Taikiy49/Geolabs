Precision Wood Site
Campbell Industrial Park
As requested we have evaluated the proposed asphalt concrete pavement to be
placed on the existing ground surface of the Precision Wood site at Campbell Industrial
Park Based on our reconnaissance of the site Daily Field Report dated July 2nd the
subject site is generally underlain by coral formation which is exposed at the ground
surface over a large portion of the site Based on these site conditions we believe that a
flexible pavement section of 2 inch thick asphalt concrete over a compacted coral
formation subgrade may be used provided that the coral subgrade is proof rolled to obtain
a dense and unyielding condition as recommended herein
We understand that the vehicle loading for this site will generally consist of trucks
80 000 pound tri axle truck and a 65 000 pound truck operating on the order of two
times a week Occasional fork lift traffic 6 000 pound fork lifts may also be anticipated
Based on this frequency of operation and a 10 year design life this amount of traffic is
approximately equivalent to a Traffic Index of 4 to 4 5 Based on our reconnaissance and
field testing the strength of the near surface coral formation at the site should be
approximately equivalent to a base course layer Therefore we believe that if the near
surface coral formation were proof rolled to attain a firm and unyielding surface the
proof rolled near surface coral formation would function similar to a base course layer
Based on the above assumptions we believe that a flexible pavement section of 2 inches
of asphalt concrete placed on a prepared coral subgrade may be used at this site
