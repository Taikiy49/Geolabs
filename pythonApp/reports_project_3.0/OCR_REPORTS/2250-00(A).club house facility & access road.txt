INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our preliminary geotechnical
engineering exploration for the site grading and foundation design of the
proposed Waikane Golf Course club house facility and access road The
project site is located in Waikane Valley in the Koolaupoko District of
windward Oahu Hawaii The general location of the project site is show
on the Project Location Map Plate 1 Our work was performed in general
accordance with our revised proposal dated
This report summarizes our preliminary findings and recommendations
PROJECT CONSTDERATIONS
The proposed Waikane Golf Course development consists of a
twenty seven hole golf course and related facilities As part of the
development and site improvements it is proposed to construct a club
house facility and access road to the club house area
The preliminary plans for the club house facility indicate site
grading involving cuts and fills on the order of 10 to 15 feet in depth
The club house facility includes the main club house structure the golf
cart storage building a parking structure and amenities such as tennis
courts and a swimming pool The club house and parking structure will be
relatively heavy structures of concrete and or masonry construction It
is anticipated that retaining walls will be used for grade separation and
basement walls
The access road will lead from the existing Waikane Valley road to the
club house area The final alignment of the road and details of the road
grading were not available at the time of our exploration

GENERAL SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is situated in Waikane Valley in the Koolaupoko
District on the windward shore of the island of Oahu Hawaii The general
location of the site and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location
Map Plate 1
The project site is rural land that is used partially for truck
farming and pasture Most of the site is fallow land that is densely
vegetated by guava christmas berry and other semitropical trees shrubs
and grasses
In general the topography of the Waikane Valley is rolling with
locally steep relief where drainageways cross the site The overall slope
and drainage is towards the east
The proposed club house site is located on the crest of a ridge
overlooking the lower portions of Waikane Valley and the proposed golf
course This ridge is cut longitudinally by several smaller
drainageways Elevations at this site vary from about 70 to 145 feet
above Mean Sea Level
The proposed access road alignment follows the existing Roberts Road
Waikane Valley Road throughout its partially paved portion and then
turns slightly towards the left to gently ascend to the club house area
Elevations along the alignment range from about 6 to 80 feet above Mean
Sea Level

Regional Geology
The island of Oahu is composed of two 2 shield volcanoes Waianae
and Koolau built up by the extrusion of basaltic lavas during the late
Pliocene and Pleistocene epochs Waikane Valley is an amphitheater headed
valley which has been deeply eroded into the eastern flank of Koolau
During the Pleistocene or Ice Age global sea levels rose and fell in
response to the great continental glaciers As the glaciers grew the
global sea level fell below the existing level and as the glaciers melted
and retreated the sea level rose to heights above the current stand of
the sea
During the lower stands of the sea the land surface eroded to depths
below the current sea level The basalt floor of Waikane Valley is most
likely at one hundred or more feet below sea level Alluvial deposits
were laid down during higher stands of the sea at elevations above the
current base level Most of the project site is thickly mantled with
alluvium Some of this alluvial material has become deeply weathered and
eroded since its deposition
Subsurface Conditions
Based on our exploration the subsurface conditions at the project
site appear to consist primarily of stiff mottled brown brown and reddish
brown clayey silts to the maximm depths explored of 31 5 feet below the
existing ground surface representing deeply weathered alluvial
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2250 00 A Page 6
materials These soils were originally stream laid clastic deposits
consisting of boulders cobbles and gravel In situ chemical weathering
of these clastic deposits has reduced the material to clayey silts while
retaining most of the structure of the original boulders cobbles and
gravel
These stiff clayey silt soils generally exhibit fair to good in situ
strength characteristics and exhibit slight lateritic properties i e
they tend to develop slightly higher strength or a slight cementation when
exposed to subaerial conditions
Groundwater was not encountered in the drilled borings at the time of
our exploration However due to the high in situ moisture contents of
the on site soils and the high local annual rainfall at the project site
zones of seepage could be encountered in large or deep excavations or near
the ground surface
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
In general our exploration indicates that most of the site is
underlain by relatively competent subsoils It is our opinion that
modified site grading techniques should be used due to the high in situ
moisture content of the underlying soils in this high rainfall area More
detailed discussion and modified grading recommendations will follow under
Site Grading
Site Grading
Areas to be graded should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed of
vegetation debris boulders and other deleterious materials Topsoil
should be removed and stockpiled for later usage and to avoid
contamination of fill materials Any tree roots or stumps greater than
three 3 inches in diameter should be removed In areas designated to
receive fill the prepared ground surface should be scarified to a minimm
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2250 00 A Page 7
depth of six 6 inches brought to near optimm moisture content and
recompacted to a minimm of 85 of its maximum dry density per ASIM Test
Method D 1557 78 Any soft or yielding areas encountered should be
over excavated and backfilled with on site soil compacted to 85 maximm
ary density
Since the project site is located in a high rainfall area the on site
subsoils generally have in place moisture contents in excess of the
optimum moisture content Therefore some amount of drying of the
excavated material may be required before use as fill
Because of the high moisture content of the on site material and
because of the site being situated in a high rainfall area it is our
opinion that the normal 90 compaction standard used in other locales may
be modified to 85 for most of the golf course embankment fills proposed
on this project However the 90 standard i e 90 of the maximm dry
density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 should be maintained for off site
borrow or select borrow to be utilized in structural fills structural
backfills and utility backfills below paved areas
General fills should be placed in maximm twelve inch 12 thick
loose horizontal lifts and compacted to the appropriate level of dry
density Sheepsfoot type compaction equipment is highly suited to the
materials encountered at the project site For structural fills and
trench backfills the loose lift thickness should be reduced to a maximm
of ten 10 inches
We anticipate a significant shrinkage factor probably varying between
10 and 20 when the on site soils are utilized as fill compacted to 85
maximm dry density It is recommended that if additional off site
borrow is required it should be tested by the geotechnical engineer to
determine if it is suitable for its intended use Generally the material
should be non expansive and should not contain boulders organics or other
deleterious materials

Slopes
Where the existing ground surface is steeper than five horizontal to
one vertical 5H 1V keying and benching are required to properly bond
the new fill to the existing slope See Plate 3 for details The filling
operations should start at the lowest point and continue up in level
horizontal compacted layers in accordance with the above recommendations
for fill placement
Subdrains should be provided at the base of the new fills to reduce
the potential of water accumulating at the interface between the fill and
the existing subsoils See Plates 4 5 Such accumulation of water
could create a plane of weakness between the fill and the original ground
potentially causing slippage or creep of the fill
In general cut slopes of two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V or
flatter are recommended for the site grading design Fill slopes should
be constructed at ratios of three horizontal to one vertical 3H 1V due
to the reduced compaction level and should be constructed by over filling
past the design slope and cutting back Care should be exercised to avoid
over cutting of cut slopes
Water should be diverted away from the tops of slopes and slope
planting utilized to reduce potential erosion and or instability of the
finished slopes
In general mid point slope benches should be provided for slopes
steeper than four horizontal to one vertical 4H 1V and having vertical
heights greater than 20 feet Modification to the above recommended bench
interval should be based on review by the project geotechnical engineer on
an individual basis

Foundation Design
Based upon the soil conditions encountered in our field exploration
it is our opinion that shallow spread and continuous footing foundations
bearing on recompacted in situ soils may be utilized to support the
proposed structures The bottoms of foundation excavations should be
scarified to a depth of six 6 inches below footing subgrade moisture
conditioned to near optimum moisture content and recompacted to 90 of its
maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 Any soft wet and or
yielding areas encountered at footing subgrade level should be
over excavated to firm natural material and the resulting excavation
should be backfilled with non expansive drier select fill material
compacted in thin 6 inch compacted lifts to 90 of its maximum dry density
per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
An allowable bearing capacity of 2 000 p s f may be utilized for the
design of foundations bearing on recompacted in situ soils prepared as
recommended above A minimm footing embedment of 18 inches below the
lowest adjacent grade is recommended on level ground surface The above
recommended bearing value is for total dead plus live loads and may be
increased by one third when temporary wind or seismic loads are
considered
For footings located on sloping ground or near the top of the bank
additional set back should be provided such that the horizontal distance
measured from the footing out to the slope should be a minimm of fifteen
15 feet Additional footing embedment may therefore be required to
obtain the above 15 feet set back requirement
Based on our exploration and laboratory testing estimated foundation
settlements are on the order of less than one 1 inch total and one half
1 2 of an inch differential

A base friction coefficient of 0 4 may be used to compute the
frictional resistance between the bottoms of the footings and the
recompacted on site soils In addition a passive resistance of 300
p s f per foot of depth of the buried vertical faces of the footings may
be used to calculate resistance to lateral loads
If a footing foundation is located next to a utility trench the
footing should be extended to the same depth as the bottom of the trench
to reduce the potential of footing distress resulting from the settlement
of the trench backfill If a continuous footing foundation crosses a
utility trench it should be designed to span the trench to reduce the
potential of foundation distress due to settlement of the backfill

recompacted on site alluvial soils

3 A passive earth resistance of 300 p s f per foot of depth
may be used for the wall footing on level areas The passive
pressure must be reduced for walls located on slopes
4 A base friction factor of 0 4 may also be used to determine
the resistance against sliding of the wall
5 The wall foundations on relatively flat areas should be
embedded a minimm of 24 inches below the lowest adjacent
grade For sloping ground the footing should extend to
obtain a minimum 15 foot set back from the outside edge of the
footing measured horizontally to the slope face
6 A subsurface drainage system subdrains and or weepholes
should be utilized behind the walls to minimize the build up
of hydrostatic pressure behind the walls Subdrains should be
constructed properly to drain water away from the walls A
minimum 2 foot of select free draining granular backfill
material such as 3B Fine gravel is recommended behind the
retaining walls to minimize excessive compaction required
For exterior wall backfill the upper two 2 feet of backfill
should consist of a non expansive impervious material to
reduce the potential infiltration of water behind the walls
Slabs On Grade
It is anticipated that slabs on grade will be utilized for the
floors of the club house related structures and the proposed parking
structure
For the club house and related structures a minimm 4 inch thick
slab with a 6 inch thick cushion of ASTM C 67B 3B Fine gravel may be
used The slab perimeter should have a thickened edge footing which

For the parking structure the slabs on grade will actually

Prior to the placement of the cushion or base course layer the

Infrastructure
The use of underground utility lines is anticipated for this
project Based on our exploration most of the trenching will be in

encountered

material may be required before its use as backfill

the normal 90 compaction standard used in other locales may be
modified to 85 for general yard area backfills on this project

The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based

data points and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
Water level data from the field data points was collected at the
times given on the graphic representations and or in the text of this

Laboratory Testing
Moisture content and unit weight determination were performed on
selected samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil
properties The results of these tests are presented on the Boring Logs
at the appropriate sample depths
One 1 swell test was performed on a selected sample of the soils to
evaluate its swelling potential The test result is summarized on Plate
B 1
Five 5 direct shear tests were performed on selected undisturbed
samples to evaluate the shear strength parameters of the soils The test
results are plotted on Plates B 2 through B 6
A proctor ASTM D 1557 78 test and a CBR ASTM D 1883 were
performed on a sample of the near surface soils to determine
moisture density relations and to evaluate pavement support
characteristics These test results are summarized on Plate B 7
