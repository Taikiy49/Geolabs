Dear Mr. Bleasdale: 
The following contains our vibration monitoring work plan, procedures, methods, 
and equipment to be used as required by the specifications for the Sewer Manhole and 
Pipe Rehabilitation, Pearl City WWPS Junction Box project at Pearl City, Oahu, Hawaii.  
In accordance with Specification Section 199 – Monitoring for Ground Settlement 
and Movement, vibration monitoring shall be performed by a civil engineer practicing in 
geotechnical engineering and licensed in the State of Hawaii with a least eight years of 
licensed experience in geotechnical engineering, and with a minimum of two years 
experience in geotechnical instrumentation installation and vibration monitoring. A 
project list is presented on the attached Vibration Monitoring Project Reference List. The 
President of Geolabs, Robin M. Lim, will be the principal engineer in charge of this 
project and his resume also is attached. 
Specification subsection 8A requires performing vibration monitoring near the 
excavations and near existing building and structures. In accordance with the 
specifications, excavation and constructions methods shall be modified if vibrations of 
1.0 inches per second or greater occur during activities. 
Vibration monitoring will be conducted using an Instantel Minimate or Micromate 
equipped with a geophone sensor. The geophone sensor will be placed at selected 
locations prior to performing the excavations to determine background levels and at 
least once a week during the actual construction work. The vibration monitoring units 
will collect data at a sample rate of 1,024 samples per second and will log the highest 
vibration level every 1 minute. The data will be stored to the unit’s onboard memory and 
will be checked manually during vibration monitoring events. The unit will be inspected 
daily to ensure proper function and recording. The specifications of the Instantel 
Minimate and Micromate are attached for reference. In addition, a Vibration Monitoring 
Summary log and sample monitoring event print-out is attached for reference.
