INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this investigation is to define subsurface conditions
and to provide recommendations for the design and construction of the
proposed Honolulu Civic Center located in Honolulu Oahu Hawaii
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed Civic Center site is located between Hotel Alapai
Beretania Streets and Kapiolani Boulevard and occupies the entire
block just mauka of the Municipal Building downtown Honolulu
W 2
In general the site is relatively level with a slight slope
from elevation 19 feet at the corner of Alapai and Beretania
Streets down to about elevation 7 feet close to the corner of
Hotel Street and Kaptolani Boulevard
At present a two story office building is at Alapai Street and
a hollow tile workshop is at the center of the site The remain
der of the area is covered with A C pavement and is being used as
a parking lot A 30 to 50 feet wide planting strip runs along the
entire boundaries along Beretania Street and Kapiolani Boulevard
The planting area also extends partly to the Hotel Street end
A new automatic pump has just been built at the corner of Kapiolani
Boulevard and Hotel Street
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
The proposed Honolulu Civic Center consists of a two story parking
structure and an exposed lopen deck on the roof The structure is
very large and practically occupies the entire city block area
The upper parking will be on the street level and the lower parking
level will lie below street grade to elevation 3 5 feet A planting
strip along the perimeter of the structure will be graded up from
street level to the roof elevation of the upper parking deck
The given column load for the structure is about 350 kips at a 30
feet by 27 feet column spacing A retaining wall at the perimeter
is anticipated for the lower and upper parking deck An entrance
ramp from street level to the lower parking level is planned at
W 3
Hotel Street and an exit ramp is planned at Kapiolani Boulevard
