We are pleased to submit our geotechnical engineering observation and testing 
services report for work performed during site grading construction of the Ka Malanai 
Phase 2 project in general accordance with our Additional Services Agreement, Contract 
No. 200006OF, dated April 12, 2013. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
Ka Malanai Phase 2 project involves the development of a two-acre residential 
parcel, bounded by Kailua Road on the north and Aoloa Street on the west in Kailua on the 
Island of Oahu, Hawaii, as shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 and Site Plan, 
Plate  2. The construction consisted of demolition of existing buildings, mass grading, and 
two new three-story condominium style townhomes, with installation of utility lines, 
driveways and sidewalks. 
This report summarizes our geotechnical observation and compaction testing 
services during site grading construction from March 4, 2015 to June 13, 2017. The site 
grading construction was performed by Delta Construction Corporation. 
SITE GRADING 
During the site grading operations, samples of the materials utilized as fill were 
obtained and transported to our laboratory for testing.  The maximum dry densities and 
optimum moisture contents were established in general accordance with ASTM D1557 
(Modified Proctor), and the test results are summarized on Plate 3.

Grading of the site generally consisted of cuts and fills less than 2 feet. Periodic 
in-situ moisture and density tests were conducted in general accordance with ASTM D6938 
(Nuclear Gauge Method) to determine the adequacy of compaction of fills and backfills for 
foundation subgrade preparation and infrastructure installations, including utility trenches, 
manhole structures, and driveways. 
At the onset of earthwork, areas within the contract grading limits were generally 
cleared and grubbed of vegetation and deleterious materials. Materials generated from 
the clearing operations were disposed of off-site. The areas identified with pesticide soils 
were scraped down to approximately 1.5 feet below the finished subgrade. These 
contaminated soils were buried in an identified pit on-site and capped with at least 2 feet 
of uncontaminated fill below the finished subgrade. 
Following the clearing and grubbing operations, subsurface utilities for the project 
were installed and connected to the Kailua Road utility corridor. Multiple of cesspools 
encountered during the site grading were cleaned by high pressure water and a vacuum 
truck down to about 6 inches below the bottom of the cesspools prior to filling with 
controlled low strength material (CLSM). The CLSM backfill was placed up to about 1 foot 
below the finished subgrade and capped with on-site soils.  
The fill and backfill materials were generally moisture-conditioned to near or above 
optimum moisture content, and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum 
dry density as determined in accordance with ASTM D1557.  In the driveway areas, the 
fill and backfill materials within 3 feet of the finished grade were compacted to a minimum 
of 95 percent of the maximum dry density. 
The test results are summarized on Plates 4.1 through 4.25. These tests were 
performed on moisture-conditioned and compacted lifts of fills and backfills. Test locations 
and elevations were estimated using on-site survey stakes for reference. These locations 
and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods 
used. Areas deemed unsatisfactory were reworked until the measured relative 
compaction conformed to or exceeded the minimum specified for the project. 
This report summarizes our field observations and compaction testing 
services during the site grading construction for the period covered by this report. Our 
services consisted of part-time field observation of subgrade preparation, fill and backfill 
placement, compaction procedures, and confirmation of subgrade soil conditions 
exposed during the above site grading operations. 
Based on our field representative’s observations and the referenced test results, it 
is our opinion that the site grading for the Ka Malanai Phase 2 project, observed and 
tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report, were performed in accordance 
with the general intent of the recommendations in the following references.
