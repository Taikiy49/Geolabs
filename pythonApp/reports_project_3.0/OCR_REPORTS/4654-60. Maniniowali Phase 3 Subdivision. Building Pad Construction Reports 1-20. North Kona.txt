Regional Geology
The Island of Hawaii is the largest in the Hawaiian Archipelago covering an area of
approximately 4 000 square miles The island was formed by the activity of the following
five shield volcanoes Kohala long extinct Mauna Kea activity during recent geologic
time Hualalai last erupted in 1801 and Mauna Loa and Kilauea both still active
The project site is situated on the northwestern flank of Hualalai Mountain which
makes up the west central portion of the island The lava formation observed at the project
site prior to site grading activities appears to consist of both a a and pahoehoe type lava
flows which spread and ponded as they approached the ocean Aja lava is typically
characterized by a porous rough and irregular flow surface resembling a jagged
accumulation of rock fragments cobbles and boulders Pahoehoe lava is characterized by
a smooth rope like or billowy surface and an internal structure of vesicular porous rock
Cavities are commonly encountered in basaltic lavas especially the pahoehoe type
lava Cavities form when the lava is in a molten state and they represent both lava tubes
intra flow cavities and blisters and pockets inter flow cavities Lava tubes form when
molten lava drains from the cooling flow leaving a hollow tube like structure which may
extend for a large longitudinal distance along the flow Blisters and pockets are generally
smaller in horizontal extent
Basalt is considered to be a relatively permeable rock and can transmit water quite
readily in both the horizontal and vertical directions In general water is transmitted through
the porous rock matrix along joints fractures and inter flow contacts cavities and along
clinker layers Clinker and cavities serve as primary water transmission features because of
their high permeability
Subsurface Conditions
The Maniniowali project site is in the District of North Kona on the Island of Hawaii
In general the original site topography prior to site grading activities consisted of irregular
or hummocky lava flow terrain with frequent topographic depressions separated by
adjacent higher relief mounds and low ridge features
Based on our observations during the mass grading operations the project site is
generally underlain by medium hard to hard basalt formation and clinker materials It
should be noted that lava tubes cavities and blisters are commonly encountered in basaltic
lava flows in the project vicinity In addition numerous lava tube features were observed
at the project site during the mass grading operations It should be noted that near surface
lava tubes detected within the limits of grading for the mass grading operations were
generally collapsed and the loose materials from within the collapsed lava tube were
removed The resulting excavation was backfilled with compacted fill in general accordance
with the recommendations outlined in the earthwork specifications

Discussion and Recommendations
Based on the grading plan the original ground surface elevations within the building
envelope boundary ranged from about 172 to 180 feet Mean Sea Level MSL at the
majority of Lot No 1 A Site Plan of the lot is attached as Plate 1 Lot No 1 was generally
graded to a finished pad elevation of about 179 feet MSL Mass grading at Lot No 1
generally consisted of excavations of up to about 1 foot deep and placement of up to about
8 feet of new fills to achieve the design grades
At the commencement of the mass grading operations extensive subgrade
preparation procedures were employed within the limits of grading to reduce the potential
for collapse of near surface cavities and or voids within the lots The intent of the subgrade
preparation procedures is to assist in detecting and collapsing near surface cavities and or
voids contained in the porous near surface materials It should be noted that the subgrade
preparation procedures employed for this project are consistent with the standard of practice
for similar type projects and similar subsurface conditions along the Kohala Coast on the
Island of Hawaii
In general the subgrade preparation in fill areas consisted of ripping the original
ground to a depth of about 3 feet or until refusal using a ripper tooth of a large bulldozer to
assist in detecting and collapsing near surface cavities and or voids contained in the porous
near surface materials After the fill surfaces have been ripped the ground was generally
leveled with the use of a Caterpillar D 9T and or Caterpillar D 10T bulldozer working the
surface for about four passes to provide a relatively level surface After leveling of the
rugged surfaces with the bulldozer the fill subgrades were generally proof rolled with a
Caterpillar CS76 roller minimum 20 tons for about eight passes to help detect and collapse
near surface cavities and or voids
Fill materials consisting of some cobbles and boulders and structural fills were placed
in the fill areas of the project site Structural fills consisted of well graded granular materials
generally smaller than 6 inches in dimension In general the fill materials within the top 2 feet
of the lots within the pad areas consisted of structural fill materials It should be noted that
general fills consisting of cobbles and boulders of up to about 2 feet in dimension generally
were placed at least about 2 feet below the finished grades within the pad areas in fill areas
and choked off with finer sized materials Boulders of up to about 3 feet in size were placed
at least 4 feet below the finished grades within the pad areas
Subgrades in cut areas generally were proof rolled with a Caterpillar CS76 roller
minimum 20 tons for about eight passes to help detect and collapse near surface cavities
and or voids It should be noted that the mauka areas of the Lot No 1 subgrades were
graded in acut condition Therefore the presence of a a basalt and basalt formation should
be anticipated at or near the existing pad elevation
