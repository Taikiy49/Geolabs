It is proposed to construct a new Salt Water Pump Station for Sea Life Park at
Waimanalo on the island of Oahu in Hawaii
Project Description
The pump station will be of reinforced concrete construction approximately ten and
one half 10 5 feet by sixty five 65 feet in plan dimension The structure will be below
ground with a pumphouse about eight 8 feet deep above an unlined wet well
approximately nine 9 additional feet deep with its bottom at an elevation of approximately
seven 7 feet below mean sea level The depth of the excavation required will therefore
be about seventeen 17 feet
Permanent well bores will be provided below this depth to facilitate inflow of ground
water Sea water into the wet well

Subsurface Exploration
One 1 boring was drilled at the project site for the purpose of evaluating
geotechnical and hydrogeologic conditions underlying the proposed location of the
structure The specific location of the boring was selected by Sea Life Park The
approximate location of the boring is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The drilling was
done by P R Drilling Company Inc who selected the equipment for use The upper
thirty five 35 feet of the boring operation was observed and recorded by a representative

the direction of of Waimea Water Services the hydrogeologist retained
for the project

augering operations indicated it to be gray black moderately hard vesicular basalt with
lenses and pockets of sand Cores were obtained from ten 10 to thirty five 35 feet

Excavation
Due to the method of drilling employed by the drilling contractor and of the number
one only and location of the boring with respect to the location and extent of the
Proposed structure direct observation of the material which will comprise of the
excavation for the upper pumphouse portion of the facility was not possible Based on
observation and evaluation of the cuttings resulting from the augering operations in this
material which will comprise somewhat more than half of the excavation it is only prudent
to assume that moderately hard basaltic rock will be encountered This assumption is
reinforced by inspection of the core recovered from the elevations of the proposed wet
well and below Due to the nature of the facility and particularly of the type of foundation
system currently contemplated it is recommended that the use of explosives for
excavation should be expressly prohibited This will result in the necessity for drilling and
chipping or other chemical or mechanical methods of rock excavation This portion of
the work should be under the supervision of a geotechnical construction specialist having
a record of successful experience with the method s selected by the contractor who
should be solely responsible for such selection The method s equipment and products
Proposed by the contractor should be Submitted to the project structural and geotechnical
engineers for review The material to be submitted should include but be not limited to
documented history of successful application under similar conditions It should be made
Clear that such review does not relieve or reduce the burden on the contractor for
complete responsibility and successful execution or performance of the system s
selected

Foundations
Foundation construction should not be initiated until all excavation for the entire
structure including possible overexcavation if found to be required is complete to
preclude splitting or overbreak of the material upon which the foundations are to bear
It is Our Current understanding that spread footings are currently planned for the
structure For preliminary design purposes a bearing value of 5000 p s f may be used
for design of the footings resting on surfaces prepared as described in the following
paragraphs The footings should be located such that their inner face at the bearing

Pumphouse Structure
The concrete forming base of the walls of the pumphouse structure should be
placed directly on the rock lip of the wet well to preclude entry of backfill material into the
wet well and possible clogging of the permanent well bores The lip of the wet well
should not be considered for design purposes to be a competent bearing formation
The surface should be carefully cleaned of loose material resulting from excavation and
any loosened material scaled off and completely removed The surface should be
