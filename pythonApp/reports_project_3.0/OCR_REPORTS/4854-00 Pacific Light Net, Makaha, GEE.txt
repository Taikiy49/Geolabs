GEOTECHNICAL ENGINEERING EXPLORATION
PACIFIC LIGHT NET
CONCRETE ENCLOSED VAULT CEV BUILDING
Dear Mr Takeda
In accordance with our fee proposal dated we have conducted
a geotechnical engineering exploration for the proposed Pacific Light Net Concrete
Enclosed Vault CEV Building in Makaha on the Island of Oahu Hawaii This report
summarizes our work on the project and presents our findings and geotechnical
engineering recommendations derived from our analyses for the subject project
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located to the east of Makaha Beach Park adjacent to the
existing AT T telecommunications facility in Makaha on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The
general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map
Plate 1
At present the site is vacant undeveloped land situated to the north and northeast
of the Makaha Beach Park comfort station and parking areas Based on our
reconnaissance of the site it appears that the area was previously partially graded
Surface vegetation at the site consists of dryland vegetation such as kiawe trees and
scrub grasses
Based on the information provided we understand that the project will involve the
construction of a one story prefabricated building on a 50 foot by 50 foot graded area on
the east side of Farrington Highway The site improvements will also include the
construction of a new entrance and access road leading from the highway to the new
CEV building It is anticipated that minor site grading will be required for the new
CEV facility and access road

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our field exploration program for the project consisted of drilling and sampling
two borings designated as Boring Nos 1 and 2 extending to depths between about
19 and 20 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate locations of the drilled
borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The borings generally encountered a surface fill layer approximately 5 feet in
thickness consisting of basalt boulders in a matrix of clayey silt and sand The surface
fill layer was underlain by an interbedded littoral and colluvial soil deposit consisting of
medium dense clayey sand with boulders extending to the depth explored of about
20 feet below the existing ground surface Groundwater was not encountered in the
borings at the time of our field exploration
Visual reconnaissance of the site also indicated the presence of minor areas of
soil loss through piping around the boulders in the fill Boring No 2 encountered a
possible void at a depth of about 8 3 feet below the existing ground surface This
indicates that the fill may not have been an engineered fill
Detailed descriptions of the materials encountered in the borings are presented
on the attached Logs of Borings Plate A 1 and A 2 of Appendix A Results of the
laboratory tests performed on selected soil samples are presented in Appendix B
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the subsurface soil conditions encountered at the project site we
believe that shallow spread and continuous footings bearing on properly compacted
structural fill may be used to support the proposed CEV building planned at the site
Detailed discussion of these items and our geotechnical engineering design
recommendations are presented in the following sections of this report
Foundations
Based on the anticipated subsurface soil conditions at the project site we believe
that shallow spread and continuous footing foundations may be used for support of the
proposed CEV building An allowable bearing pressure of 3 000 pounds per square foot
psf may be used for the foundations bearing on properly compacted structural fill The
transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic forces

Our exploration indicates that the fill and colluvium underlying the site are variable
in consistency and quality In order to provide a uniform supporting layer for the structure
we recommend that a minimum of 2 feet of structural fill be provided below the building
Portions of the building area may require over excavation of the existing fill in order to
provide this minimum 2 foot layer The structural fill should extend outside the building
footprint for a minimum of 5 feet on all sides
In general the bottom of footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches
below the lowest adjacent finished grades If foundations are located next to utility
trenches they should be embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V
imaginary plane extending upward from the bottom edge of the utility
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by frictional resistance
developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil and by passive earth
pressure acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system A coefficient of
friction of 0 4 may be used for footings bearing on the compacted structural fills
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid
pressure of 250 pounds per square foot per foot of depth pcf This assumes that the
soils around the footings are well compacted Unless covered by pavements or slabs the
passive resistance in the upper 12 inches of soil should be neglected
Active soil pressure on retaining walls and structures may be estimated at
35 pounds per square foot per foot of depth pcf
Site Grading
At the commencement of grading work areas within the grading limits should be
cleared grubbed and scarified to a minimum depth of 12 inches moisture conditioned to
above the optimum moisture content and recompacted to not less than 90 percent relative
compaction ASTM D 1557 Boulders should be removed to a minimum depth of
12 inches below the structural fill subgrade Trees should be removed and roots greater
than 3 inches in diameter should be over excavated and be backfilled with fill placed in
12 inch maximum loose lifts moisture conditioned to at least the optimum moisture
content and compacted to at least 95 percent relative compaction
Soft and yielding areas encountered should be over excavated to expose firm
natural material and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with fill placed in 12 inch
maximum loose lifts moisture conditioned to at least the optimum moisture content and
compacted to at least 95 percent relative compaction The excavated soils should be
disposed of properly off site and or used in landscape areas where appropriate Boulders
produced from the site grading operations should be segregated and disposed of at an
appropriate off site location

The compacted structural fill should consist of non expansive select granular
material such as crushed basalt or coral The material should be well graded from coarse
to fine with no particles larger than 3 inches in largest dimension and should contain
between 10 and 30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve The material should have
a laboratory CBR value of 20 or more and should have a maximum swell of less than
1 percent
Compacted structural fill should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum
moisture content placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to
the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of
the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 test procedures Optimum
moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum
dry density
Slab On Grade
The area adjacent to the slab should be backfilled tightly against the slab and
graded to divert water away from the slab to reduce the potential of water ponding around
the slab
The thickened edges of slabs adjacent to unpaved area should be embedded at
least 12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade
Utilities
lt is anticipated that some utilities may be constructed for the proposed project
The methods and equipment to be used for excavation should be determined by the
contractor subject to practical limits and safety considerations The excavation should
comply with all applicable local state and federal safety requirements Trench shoring
design and installation should be the responsibility of the contractor Trench shoring
and bracing should conform to the appropriate health and safety requirements
In general for support of the utility lines we recommend that granular bedding
consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation be used under
the pipes The initial backfill up to about 1 foot above the pipes should consist of
free draining backfills such as No 3B Fine gravel to reduce the potential for damaging
the pipes from compaction of the backfill It is critical that a free draining granular material
be used to reduce the potential for formation of voids below the haunches of pipes and to
provide adequate support for the sides of the pipes The use of on site soils as backfill
immediately around utility pipes is not recommended

For protected utility lines such as concrete jacketed electrical and
telecommunications lines the bedding requirements described above are not necessary if
the concrete jackets are cast neat in the utility trench
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 1 foot above the pipes to the
finished subgrade should consist of approved on site soils or select granular material The
backfill material should be moisture conditioned to at least the optimum moisture content
placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a
minimum of 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for future ground
subsidence The upper 2 feet of the trench backfill below the pavement subgrade should
be compacted to not less than 95 percent relative compaction Mechanical compaction
equipment should be used to compact the materials at the project site Water tamping
jetting or ponding should not be used to compact the backfill material
Access Road Pavement
lt is anticipated that the proposed access road for the CEV building will be paved
with asphaltic concrete It is further anticipated that traffic loading on the access road
will be very light i e limited to periodic use by maintenance personnel Based on the
results of our exploration and laboratory testing the following pavement section is
recommended
1 5 Inches Asphaltic Concrete
6 0 Inches Aggregate Base Course 95 Percent Relative Compaction
12 0 Inches Structural Fill 95 Percent Relative Compaction
19 5 Inches Total Pavement Thickness on Compacted Subgrade
The structural fill should consist of non expansive select granular material such
as crushed basalt or coral The material should be well graded from coarse to fine with
no particles larger than 3 inches in largest dimension and should contain between
10 and 30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve The material should have a
laboratory CBR value of 20 or more and should have a maximum swell of less than
1 percent
Due to the anticipated pattern of the site drainage i e across and along the
access road the use of embedded curbs or headers along the edges of the pavement is
recommended to reduce the potential of erosion or loss of support of the soils
immediately adjacent to the road

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that Geolabs be retained to provide geotechnical engineering
services during construction of the proposed project The items of construction monitoring
that are critical requiring Special Inspection include observation of footing subgrade
over excavation and preparation and placement and compaction of the structural fill
materials Other aspects of earthwork construction should also be observed by a
representative from Geolabs This is to observe compliance with the intent of the design
concepts specifications and or recommendations and to expedite suggestions for design
changes that may be required in the event that subsurface conditions differ from those
anticipated at the time this report was prepared The recommendations provided in this
report are contingent upon such observations
If actual exposed subsurface conditions encountered during construction differ
from those assumed or considered in this report Geolabs should be contacted to review
and or revise the geotechnical engineering recommendations presented herein
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from the field borings Variations of subsoil conditions between and
beyond the field borings may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not
become evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident Geolabs
should be contacted to re evaluate the recommendations presented in this report
The boring locations in the field were determined by taping from the site layout plan
transmitted to our office by R M Towill Corporation The physical locations of the field
borings should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown on the graphic representations of the borings depict
the approximate boundaries between the soil types and as such may denote a gradual
transition Water level data from the borings were measured at the times shown on the
graphic representations and or in the text of this report These data have been reviewed
and interpretations made in the formulation of this report However it must be noted that
fluctuation may occur due to tidal fluctuation variation in rainfall temperatures and other
factors
