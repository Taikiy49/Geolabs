SITE DESCRIPTION
The Ko Olina Resort development is located south of Farrington Highway between
the Honokai Hale Subdivision and Kahe Point The resort extends from Farrington
Highway to the ocean and adjacent to the Barbers Point Deep Draft Harbor
The subject Parcels Nos 16 and 33 are located in the northern portion of the
development and encompasses an area in excess of 55 acres The topography in the
area is gently sloping upward to the east at an average gradient of about two 2 per
cent The sites are surrounded by the recently completed Ko Olina Golf Course
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
It is planned to raise the sites 2 to 8 feet by filling with the fill material from the
nearby condominium developments The finished grade surface will probably be relatively
flat with a gradient of about two 2 per cent

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The previous exploration disclosed that the general area was underlain primarily
by alluvial deposits consisting of stiff silty clay and boulders over basalt formation at
deeper depths During the mass grading of the Ko Olina Resort about five to fifteen feet
of fill was placed above the natural ground surface
Prior to the above site filling the existing ground surface vegetation was removed
and the natural ground surface was scarified and recompacted The fill was then placed
in horizontal lifts and compacted to a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density
according to ASTM D 1557
The fill material used in the area consisted of soils excavated from other portions
of the development and ranged from clay to coralline sand and gravel Additionally
basalt boulders were used in portions of the fills within both lots
During the fill placement operations periodic field density tests were done in
accordance with ASTM D 1556 Sand Cone Method and ASTM D 2922 Nuclear
Method Based on the test results and field observations it is our opinion that the
existing fills have been placed properly
Since the present site is underlain by compacted fills overlying stiff in situ alluvium
it is Our Opinion that the underlying fill is capable to support the proposed additional fills
with minimal ground settlement Other guidelines for the fill placement are presented
below

1 Areas to be graded should be thoroughly cleared of vegetation boulders
debris and other deleterious materials Topsoil should be removed and
stockpiled for later usage and to avoid contamination of fill material
2 In areas to receive fill the ground should be scarified to a depth of six 6
inches brought to near optimum moisture content and recompacted to 90
of its maximum dry density as established by ASTM D 1557 78 If soft
areas are encountered they should be over excavated and stabilized by
backfilling with select material placed in 10 inch maximum loose lifts
compacted to 90 maximum dry density
3 Fill material should be placed in 10 inch maximum loose horizontal lifts and
compacted to 90 of its maximum dry density as established by ASTM
D 1557 78
4 The borrow soils should be examined by the geotechnical engineer for its
suitability as fill Borrow soils should generally be less than 6 inch in size
and capable of forming a dense stable mass
5 Select material for the upper three 3 feet of fill should consist of low
expansion granular material such as crushed coral The material should
be less than 3 inch in size with a laboratory CBR swell value not exceeding
1 and CBR value of 15 or more It should not contain large percentages
of boulders organics or other deleterious material should contain sufficient
fines so that there is a minimum of 20 passing the No 200 sieve and
should be capable of forming a competent stable mass when compacted

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during construction of the earthwork This is to
observe compliance with the design concepts specifications or recommendations and
to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from that
anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in this report are
contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and approved by
the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during construction are different
from those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based upon
information obtained from the borings Variations of conditions between the borings
may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident
until construction If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate
the recommendations given in this report
The locations of the borings were determined by taping from structures
indicated on the topographic survey Elevations of the borings were approximately
determined by interpolation from elevation points on the topographic survey map The
