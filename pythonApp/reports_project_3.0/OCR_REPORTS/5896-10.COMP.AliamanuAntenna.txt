PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The antenna facility is in the Aliamanu area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The
existing antenna tower is about 50 feet tall is at the north eastern side of the reservoir
and is supported on three legs A one story CMU equipment building is also at the
eastern side of the reservoir Based on the available plan the existing antenna tower
will be replaced with a new three leg 60 foot antenna tower The new tower will be on
the east side of the CMU equipment building and will be designed to withstand a
maximum wind speed of 155 mph Additionally the equipment building will be
renovated and expanded
Based on the existing topography fills and cuts on the order of about 1 feet or less
were required to achieve the design grades
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services for
the site grading operations and utility installation
SITE GRADING AND UTILITY INSTALLATION
We observed the site grading and trench backfill work for the Aliamanu Antenna
Tower Replacement project from to The site grading
work consisted of clearing and grubbing and placement and compaction of fills on the
order of about 1 foot thick to achieve the design finished grades Summit

Construction Inc performed the site grading and utility installation work During the site
grading operations Summit Construction Inc provided laboratory test results in
addition to our own The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were
established in general accordance with ASTM D 1557 and the results are summarized
on Plate 3
At the sidewalk area around the building structures and the new antenna pad the
fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture content
and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as determined
in accordance with ASTM D 1557 In the LPG tank pad new building extension pad and
the utility trench area the fill and backfill materials were compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative density
During the site grading and utility backfilling operations periodic field density
tests were performed in general accordance with ASTM D 6938 Nuclear Gauge
Method The compaction test results are summarized on the attached Plate 4 These
tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and
backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and
interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed
as unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained
and or approved by our field representative based on visual observation and hand
probing
Our services consisted of intermittent field observation of the contractor s fill
placement subgrade preparation and compaction procedures and confirmation of
subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above grading operations
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the site grading and trench backfill work for the
above project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report were
performed in conformance with the general intent of the recommendations in the
