Due to the poor subsurface soil conditions it is desired to perform shear wave
velocity profiling at the site in an effort to more closely analyze the seismic design
considerations for the project In general we proposed to use Cone Penetrometer Testing
CPT with seismic shear wave velocity tests for the additional field exploration work The
CPT method involves inserting a sensitive probe into the soil with constant speed Various
sensors embedded in the probe continuously measure the tip resistance side friction and
induced pore pressures as the probe is systematically pushed through the soil In general
subsurface explorations consisting of CPT yields more accurate results for highly sensitive
materials such as the loose granular soils because the data is obtained directly from the
in situ soils Therefore CPT with seismic shear wave velocity profiling is considered to be
the industry standard for higher order analysis such as the detailed seismic analyses
especially in these subsurface conditions

Field Exploration
Seismic shear wave velocity profiling of the subsurface materials was performed
using seismic Cone Penetrometer Testing SCPT equipment to more closely analyze the
seismic design considerations for the project Shear wave velocity testing using seismic
cone penetration test equipment was performed at two locations designated as CPT 1
and CPT 2 shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 Testing was performed at various depths
extending to the maximum depth of about 100 9 and 101 5 feet below the existing ground
surface
In order to conduct testing the probe hole was advanced utilizing rotary coring
methods to the maximum depth of 101 5 feet below the existing ground surface The
probe hole was then backfilled with 0 25 inch diameter coated bentonite pellets The
temporary casing from the coring operations was used as a tremie pipe to place the
bentonite pellets starting from the bottom and advancing upward When the bentonite
pellets encountered the groundwater in the borehole they began to slowly hydrate As this

SCPT
The seismic shear wave velocity test consists of hydraulically pushing a 10 ton
steel electronic subtraction cone with an apex angle of 60 degrees and a projected surface
area of 1 55 square inches 10 square centimeters into the ground at a rate of
approximately second The depth of the cone penetration was recorded at
about 2 inch 5 centimeter intervals using an electronic depth sensor attached to the
cone rod
The cone carries a uniaxial horizontal geophone to detect the arrival of a shear
wave generated and propagated from the ground surface The seismic measurements
are made when the SCPT has stopped and a shear wave is sent into the subsurface A
shear wave is generated at the surface by striking a loaded plank with a switched
hammer The propagation time of the wave from the hammer blow to the cone was
measured at each discrete depth interval The vector difference of these depths divided
by the time difference for the shear wave to arrive at the various depths provided the
average shear wave velocity over the depth interval
Soil Profile Type
The shear wave velocities measured and the weighted average shear wave
velocity calculated for the top 100 feet of the soil profile at the test locations are presented
on Plates 3 and 4 The weighted average shear wave velocity was calculated based on
the average shear wave velocity method described in Section of the Uniform
Building Code 1997 Edition
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered in our field exploration the
weighted average shear wave velocity for the materials within the upper 100 feet of the soil
profile is on the order of about 530 to 540 feet per second Based on the shear wave
velocity test results we believe that the project site may be classified from a seismic
analysis standpoint as being a Soft Soil site corresponding to an Se soil profile type
based on the Table No 16 J of the Uniform Building Code 1997 Edition
Limitations
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this letter report are based in
part upon information obtained from the seismic cone penetration tests Variations of
subsurface soil conditions and shear wave velocities between and beyond the test probes
may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident until
construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to
reevaluate the recommendations provided in this letter report
