Geolabs, Inc. is pleased to submit the results of our percolation testing performed 
at the proposed project. This letter report summarizes our findings and geotechnical 
recommendations derived from our field percolation testing only. These geotechnical 
recommendations are intended to assist the client in the design of individual wastewater 
system (IWS) at the project site. The findings and recommendations presented herein are 
subject to the limitations noted at the end of this letter report. 
Project Considerations 
The project involves construction of a new warehouse and office building for 
Carrier Hawaii at Kaloko Commercial Center (TMK: 7-3-051: 138, Lot 4) in the District of 
North Kona on the Island of Hawaii. 
As part of the project, we understand that an IWS with a septic tank and leach field 
will be constructed. To aid in the design of the IWS, a percolation test was performed at 
the proposed leach field location in accordance with the State of Hawaii, Department of 
Health's Administrative Rules, Chapter 11-62-31.2, "Wastewater Systems" and 
Chapter 10 of the Ten State’s Standards to evaluate the infiltration characteristics of the 
subsurface soils for disposal of wastewater effluent.  
Percolation Testing 
One percolation test, designated as P-1, was performed at the proposed leach field 
location in general accordance with the procedures outlined by the Department of Health

The percolation test was performed in a test pit excavated to about 1 foot below 
the existing ground surface. Approximately 1 inch of gravel was placed at the bottom of 
the test pit to protect the bottom from scouring and sediments. 
During testing, the test hole was filled with water to a depth of about 6 inches above 
the gravel layer and refilled as necessary. The time intervals and water drops were then 
recorded to provide data upon which the calculation of the percolation rate was based. 
Based on the percolation test conducted, the percolation rate at the location tested 
is about 2.3 minutes per inch at the test elevation. In accordance with the "Manual of 
Septic-Tank Practice" prepared by the U.S. Department of Health, Education, and 
Welfare, the leach field location with the percolation rate obtained at the site would be 
suitable for siting of the absorption system at this location. The percolation test results 
are presented on Plate 2. 
Limitations 
The analyses and recommendations submitted herein are based, in part, upon 
information obtained from our in-situ testing. Variations of subsoil conditions beyond the 
in-situ testing location may occur, and the nature and extent of these variations may not 
become evident until construction is underway. If variations then appear evident, it will be 
necessary to re-evaluate the recommendations provided herein. 
The percolation test location indicated herein is approximate, having been estimated 
by taping from visible features shown on the Site Utility Plan transmitted by Dempsey Pacific 
on July 16, 2019. Elevation at the test location was interpolated based on the contours 
shown on the same plan.  The physical location and elevation of the test pit should be 
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used. 
This letter report has been prepared for the exclusive use of Dempsey Pacific for

for Carrier Hawaii in accordance with generally accepted geotechnical engineering

The owner/client should be aware that unanticipated soil conditions are commonly 
encountered. Unforeseen soil conditions, such as perched groundwater, soft deposits, 
hard layers or cavities, may occur in localized areas and may require additional probing 
or corrections in the field (which may result in construction delays) to attain a properly 
constructed project. Therefore, a sufficient contingency fund is recommended to 
accommodate these possible extra costs.
