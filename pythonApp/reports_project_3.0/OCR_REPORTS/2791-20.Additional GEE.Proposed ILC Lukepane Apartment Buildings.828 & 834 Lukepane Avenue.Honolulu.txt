INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our additional geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the proposed Lukepane Apartment Buildings in the Kapahulu
area of Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the
project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings from our field exploration and laboratory
testing and presents our design level geotechnical engineering recommendations derived
from our analyses for the proposed project These recommendations are intended for
design input only and may not be appropriate to form the basis for construction cost
estimates nor for construction bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed project site is located at Avenue in the
Kapahulu area of Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii It was previously proposed to
develop the subject site with the construction of a 4 story apartment building with
concrete and masonry construction
A geotechnical engineering exploration has been conducted and our findings and
recommendations are presented in our report entitled Geotechnical Engineering
Exploration Proposed ILC Lukepane Apartment Building Avenue
Honolulu Oahu Hawaii dated
We understand that currently the proposed project design has been revised to
consist of two 3 story wood framed buildings with lighter loadings Our review of the
previous exploration indicated that no borings have been drilled at one of the new building
footprints Therefore three additional borings about 20 feet deep each were performed

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located on two adjacent lots at Avenue in
Kapahulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii
At the time of our recent field exploration the northeast lot was still vacant and the
rear portion of the southwest lot was occupied with a wooden residential structure
approximately 31 feet by 34 feet in plan dimension A 6 foot high hedge was noted
between the two lots
An adjacent 3 story apartment building of concrete and masonry construction is
located along the northeast boundary of the site In general the site is relatively level with
existing ground elevations ranging from approximately 11 to 13 feet MSL
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our current additional exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling three
borings to depths ranging from about 18 5 to 21 feet below the existing ground surface
The approximate locations of these new borings B 5 to B 7 along with the previous
borings B 1 to B 4 drilled in 1991 are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general our field explorations disclosed that the subject site is generally
underlain by a surface layer of alluvial deposit approximately 8 5 to 11 5 feet in thickness
consisting of medium stiff to stiff silty clay and clayey silt materials This was underlain
by a medium dense to dense coralline deposit to depths of at least 20 feet below the
existing ground surface
Previous exploration with deeper borings indicated that the coralline deposit
extends to depths of 29 5 to 35 feet below the existing ground surface at the northeast
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2791 20 Page 6
lot This was underlain by an older alluvial deposit consisting of very stiff to hard silty
clay and clayey silt materials extending down to depths of about 35 to 47 feet below the
ground surface Basalt formation was then encountered extending to a minimum depth
of 61 feet the maximum depth drilled
In Boring Nos 1 and 7 a very soft alluvial soil layer approximately 1 to 3 feet in
thickness was encountered above the coralline deposit at depths of about 8 5 to 9 5 feet
below the existing ground surface
In Boring and 7 a very loose coralline deposit approximately 3 5 feet in
thickness was encountered below the surficial alluvial layer at depths of about 9 to 10
feet below the existing ground surface
The groundwater levels in the borings were measured at different times during our
field explorations and were encountered at depths of approximately 10 to 12 feet below
the existing ground surface These depths generally correspond to elevations of
approximately 0 0 to 1 9 feet MSL
Due to the proximity of the site to the Ala Wai Canal and the Manoa Palolo
Drainage Canal it is likely that the groundwater levels measured in the borings during our
field explorations may be affected by the tidal fluctuations In addition the groundwater
levels at the project site may be influenced by seasonal precipitation and storm surge
conditions

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Our field explorations indicate that the project site is underlain by medium stiff to
stiff alluvial deposit at shallow depths Based on the subsoil conditions encountered at
the project site and the anticipated light structural loadings it is our opinion that shallow
slab on grade foundations bearing on 18 inches of structural fill may be used to support
the proposed apartment buildings
Laboratory tests performed on the near surface clayey soils indicate that the
material generally has a moderate shrink swell potential when subjected to moisture
fluctuations Therefore we recommend that concrete slab on grade should not be placed
directly over this clayey subgrade soil The concrete slab should be supported directly
on 18 inches of select structural fill to reduce the potential effects of the moderately high
expansive surface soils Detailed recommendations are presented in the following
subsections
Foundations
Based on the generally stiff subsoil conditions encountered at the site and the
anticipated light structural loadings we believe that shallow slab on grade foundations
bearing on 18 inches of structural fill may be used to support the proposed apartment
buildings
An allowable bearing pressure of 2 000 p s f may be used for the design of
shallow slab on grade foundations bearing on 18 inches of compacted structural fill
material This bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third
for transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic forces
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2791 20 Page 8
The perimeter thicken edge footing foundations should be embedded a minimum
of 12 inches below the lowest adjacent finish grade If foundations are located next to
utility trenches or easements they should be embedded below a 1H 1V imaginary plane
extending upward from the bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of
the utility lines
We estimate the footing settlements under the anticipated design loads for slab on
grade foundations bearing on 18 inches of compacted structural fill to be less than 1 inch
total and inch differential
Lateral loads acting on the structures may be resisted by passive earth pressure
acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system and by frictional resistance
developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil Resistance due
to passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid pressure of 300
pounds per square foot per foot of depth p c f This assumes that the soil around
footings is well compacted Unless covered by pavements or slabs the passive
resistance in the upper 12 inches of soil should be neglected A coefficient of friction of
0 35 may be used for foundations bearing on the compacted structural fill material
We recommend that foundation excavations be observed by a representative of
Geolabs Hawaii prior to the placement of reinforcing steel or concrete to confirm adequate
foundation bearing conditions and the required footing embedment depths
Slab On Grade
We understand that concrete slab on grade parking is planned at the ground floor
level of the proposed project Our field explorations disclosed that the project site is
underlain by a surficial alluvial deposit of clayey soils which are considered to be
moderately expansive when subjected to moisture fluctuations Unless floor slabs
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2791 20 Page 9
supported by these soils are properly designed there is a potential for future distress to
the lightly loaded slab on grade construction resulting from the shrinking and swelling of
these clayey soils due to changes in the moisture content To reduce the potential for
structural distress resulting from the swelling of the on site moderately expansive
subgrade soils we recommend that the concrete slab on grade be supported on 18
inches of structural fill material The structural fill material should extend at least 2 feet
beyond the perimeter of the proposed structures
The structural fill should be placed as soon as possible after the over excavation
and recompaction of the existing clayey subgrade soils The prepared new structural fill
and the underlying subgrade soil should be wetted and kept moist prior to final placement
of concrete Subgrade soils should be prepared in accordance with the recommenda
tions presented in the Site Preparation section of this report The new structural fill
would serve as a capping layer of moist soil over the underlying potentially expansive
subgrade soils and this capping fill would reduce the potential moisture loss from the
prepared moist subgrade soils This reduction in moisture loss should reduce the
potential swelling of the moderately expansive clayey soils Compaction requirements for
the subgrade soils and the capping fill are discussed in the Site Preparation section of
this report
Since the slab on grade parking at the ground floor level will be subjected to
vehicular traffic we recommend that a 6 inch layer of base course compacted to 95
percent relative compaction be provided below the concrete slab Relative compaction
refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry
density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 91 test procedures
Provisions should be made for proper load transfer across the slab joints which are
subjected to vehicular traffic
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2791 20 Page 10
Exterior concrete walkways may be required for the proposed project We
recommend that a minimum 4 inch thick cushion layer of No 3B Fine gravel be provided
below the concrete slab Construction joints should be provided at intervals equal to the
width of the walkways with expansion joints at right angle intersections
Perimeter Fence Walls
A new concrete masonry unit CMU fence wall up to 6 feet high is anticipated
along the perimeter of the project site It is our opinion that continuous footings may be
used to support the proposed perimeter wall provided that the bottom of the footing
excavations is adequately compacted and the footing is embedded a minimum of 2 feet
below the lowest adjacent finish grade
An allowable bearing pressure of up to 2 000 p s f may be used for the design of
the wall footing foundations bearing on the recompacted on site soils
Site Preparation
Based on the existing topography and the proposed finish floor elevation minimal
site grading work is anticipated for the project construction
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable
materials should be removed and disposed of off site
Slabs and foundations of the existing structures that are to be demolished should
be completely removed Any over excavation resulting from the demolition should be
backfilled with compacted granular fill material Existing utilities to be abandoned should
be removed and the resulting excavation should be properly backfilled with 6 to 8 inch
thick horizontal lifts of compacted select granular fill material Utilities that are to be
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2791 20 Page 11
abandoned in place under structure should be backfilled by pumping lean concrete under
low pressure
After demolition clearing and grubbing all areas beneath future slabs and
pavement areas should be over excavated to a depth of at least 18 inches below the
bottom of the base course layer and replaced with non expansive structural fill If zones
of soft loose or saturated soils are encountered at the over excavated subgrade level
deeper over excavation may be required to expose the underlying firm material The
resulting over excavation should be backfilled with compacted non expansive select fill
material The clayey material resulting from the over excavation should be removed and
disposed of off site
The over excavated subgrade surface should be moisture conditioned to at least
2 percent above optimum moisture content and compacted to at least 90 percent relative
compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as
a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with
ASTM D 1557 91 test procedures
Select structural fill material needed for the 18 inch capping layer should be
placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to not less
than 95 percent relative compaction Select structural fill material should consist of
imported granular material such as crushed coral basalt gravel or cinder sand The
material should be well graded from coarse to fine with no particles greater than 3 inches
in largest dimension The material should have a laboratory California Bearing Ratio
CBR value of 20 or higher and a swell potential of not more than 1 percent when tested
in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1883 It should contain less than 20
percent passing the No 200 sieve Any imported fill material should be tested and
approved by Geolabs Hawaii prior to delivery to the project site for its intended use

Pavement Design
We understand that asphaltic concrete pavement will be required for the motor
court between the two apartment buildings We anticipate the vehicle loading for this
project will consist primarily of passenger vehicles and light pick up trucks We have
made our preliminary pavement design assuming the pavement subgrade soil will consist
of at least 18 inches of imported select structural fill material The select structural fill
material under the pavement areas should be compacted to 95 percent relative
compaction The subgrade soils below the structural fill material should be prepared in
accordance with the recommendations presented in the Site Preparation section of this
report Based on the above assumptions we recommend the following pavement section
be used for preliminary design purposes

Paved areas should be sloped and drainage gradients maintained to carry all
surface water off the site Where concrete curbs are used to isolate landscaping in or

into the subgrade soil below the base course aggregate to reduce migration of excessive
landscape water into the pavement section Alternatively a subdrain system could be

Utility Trench
We envision that new utility lines may be required for the project A granular
bedding consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation is
recommended under the pipes For pipes with inverts near the underlying soft clays or
loose sand deposits an additional 18 inches of No 3B Fine gravel wrapped in filter fabric
Mirafi 140N or equivalent should be provided below the bedding layer to provide more
uniform support of the pipe Self compacting granular materials such as No 3B Fine
gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation should also be used for the trench backfill up to
about one foot above the pipes to provide adequate support around the pipes and to
reduce the compaction effort of the backfill thus reducing the possibility of damaging the
pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level one foot above the pipes to
the top of the subgrade or finished grade should consist of select granular material The
backfill should be placed in about 10 inch loose lifts and mechanically compacted to not
less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for future ground
subsidence Where trenches are below pavement areas the upper 2 feet of the trench
backfill below the pavement subgrade should be compacted to 95 percent relative
compaction
Drainage
The finished grades outside the buildings should be sloped to shed water away
from foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding Also it is advised that gutter
systems be installed around the buildings and that the discharge be diverted away from
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2791 20 Page 14
the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near the foundations
and slabs should also be avoided Planters next to foundations should be avoided or
have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the above
foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation
and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations should be
properly backfilled against the walls or slab footings immediately after setting of the
concrete to reduce water infiltration
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and should be
maintained to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundations

The recommendations provided in this report are contingent upon such observations

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part
upon information obtained from field borings Variations of subsoil conditions between
the borings may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become
evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be
necessary to re evaluate the recommendations provided in this report
The location of the field borings indicated in this report are approximate having
been estimated by taping from the features shown on the Topographic Survey Map by
Controlpoint Surveying and Engineering Inc dated Elevations of the
field borings were estimated by interpolation from the elevation points shown on the same
map The physical locations and elevations of the borings should be considered accurate
only to the degree implied by the methods used
The stratification lines shown on graphic representations of the borings depict the
approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual
transition
Water level data from the borings were measured at the times given on the graphic
representations and or in the text of this report These data have been reviewed and
GEOLABS HAWAII
