INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the proposed subdivision development along Keolu
Drive in Kailua on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location of
the project site and its vicinity is show on the Project Location Map
Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings of the exploration and presents
our geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from analyses of the
data obtained during the exploration These recommendations are intended
for design input and are not intended for construction estimation or
bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to grade the lakefront site with the placement of
about two 2 to four 4 feet of imported fill and subdivide the parcel
into eighteen 18 residential lots
The proposed site fills will Slope towards and terminate at the Corps
of Engineers wet land boundary along Kaelepulu Pond The proposed fill
slope facing the lake will have a 2 1 Horizontal Vertical gradient with
a 1 foot high temporary berm at the top for erosion control
An 84 inch I D new drainline extension C will be constructed at
the northern end of the site The drainline extension installation will
include construction of a new manhole with bottom at about 5 feet Mean
Sea Level MSL
The construction of another similar drainline extension D was
also planned across the southern portion of the site However it is our
understanding that this new drainline is no longer included in the current
proposed development

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located in the Enchanted Lake area of Kailua on
the island of Oahu Hawaii The site is situated between Kaelepulu Pond
and Keolu Drive on the western side of Kaelepulu Pond This long narrow
lakefront parcel covers an area of approximately 9 5 acres
The site is reclaimed land bounded by the Corps of Engineers
designated wet land boundary of Kaelepulu Pond which is delineated by an
old earth berm along the waterfront The enclosed basin was partially
filled and graded during the earlier development of the Enchanted Lakes
area
At the time of our field exploration approximately two 2 to three
3 feet of new fill had been imported and placed in most of the site
except along the waterfront edge Ponding water was observed at the
existing outlet of Drainline C at the northern end of the site
Based on the original topographic survey prior to the current site
filling the site sloped gently toward Kaelepulu Pond at an average
gradient of approximately 10 percent and the ground surface elevations
ranged from approximately two 2 feet to ten 10 feet MSL
At present the waterfront boundary of the site is still covered with
tall trees There is also a hedgerow of haole koa left uncut along the
Keolu Drive boundary of the project
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Subsoil Conditions
It is our understanding that the subsurface soil conditions at the
project site except the waterfront boundary had been explored by others
on a preliminary basis During our field exploration we focused our
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2436 00 Page 5
effort by drilling and sampling three 3 borings to depths of 41 5 to
59 5 feet below the existing ground surface along the lakefront The
approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The drilled borings encountered approximately 1 to 9 feet of older
and new stiff clayey silt fill overlying lagoonal deposits which extended
to about 30 feet below the existing ground surface The lagoonal deposits
are moderately to highly compressible subsoils generally consisting of
soft to medium stiff clays in the southern portion of the site and loose
to very loose silty gravel in the northern portion of the site A thin
layer of very soft lagoonal clay was also encountered above the silty
gravel stratum along the new drainline C extension at the northern end
of the site
The compressible lagoonal deposits were underlain by stiff to very
stiff clayey and silty soils to the maximum depths drilled in the borings
Groundwater Level
During our field exploration groundwater levels were measured at the
approximate depths of 4 0 to 5 0 feet below the existing ground surface
corresponding to 2 0 to 3 0 feet MSL elevation in the borings It
appears that the highest groundwater level measured at 3 0 feet MSL
could be partially due to excess porewater pressure in the clayey subsoils
built up under the weight of the newly placed fills
Since the site is next to Kaelepulu Pond it is likely that the
groundwater level will be affected by tidal fluctuations and storm
conditions Therefore the groundwater levels measured may not reflect
the highest possible levels at the project site

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on our slope stability analysis it is our opinion that the
lakefront slope of the new site embankment fills may generally be stable
However we believe that stage loading should be used in filling the
northern end of the site which is underlain by a thin layer of very soft
lagoonal clay
In addition significant ground settlements may occur under the
weight of the new site fills It is recommended that sufficient time be
allowed for the ground to settle prior to any drainline and or building
construction
To reduce the waiting time required a surcharge program may be used
However it is very difficult to obtain an accurate estimate of the
actual waiting time required Therefore we recommend that settlement
gauges be installed to monitor the actual rate of ground settlement under
the new fills
After the ground settlement under the new fills are mostly complete
drainline and building construction may proceed
We believe that shallow footing foundations may be used to support
the one to two story residential structures provided the grading
recommendations in this report are closely followed
Adequate shoring support should be provided for deep
trench excavation below groundwater level for construction of the new
drainline extension and manhole structure
Due to the soft subsoil conditions special bedding should also be
used to provide more uniform ground support to the new drainline

Slope Stability
Slope stability analyses for the waterfront perimeter of the new site
fills were performed Based on a maximum fill height of four 4 feet and
a slope ratio of two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V the analyses
results indicate that the lakefront slope should generally be stable
However it is our opinion that staged or incremental grading may be
required to maintain the slope stability in the northern end of the site
proposed Lot Nos 16 through 18 which is underlain by a thin layer of
very soft clay at about 9 to 13 feet below the existing ground surface
Generally the site filling in this potentially unstable area should
be placed in two stages particularly near the waterfront We recommend
that a waiting period of at least two 2 weeks should be allowed between
the first and the second stages of site filling to allow the soft clay to
rest If surcharging is utilized the surcharge should also be placed in
two 2 stages but separately from and subsequent to the placement of the
initial fills
Ground Settlements
Based on our settlement analyses it appears that up to 5 inches of
primary ground settlement may occur under the weight of the proposed four
4 feet of fill and most of the ground settlements would occur in about 8
months Therefore the site grading design should include additional fill
thickness to compensate for the anticipated settlement
However the actual ground settlements and rate may vary
substantially with location since the thicknesses of the new fills will
vary from 2 to 4 feet and the underlying subsoils also vary from
predominantly very loose silty gravel in the northern portion to
predominantly soft clay in the southern portion of the site Generally
more settlement will occur towards the lakefront
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2436 00 Page 8
In addition to the primary settlement which will occur as a result
of consolidation of the subsoils long term secondary settlement due to
soil deformation will also occur over the design life of the subdivision
development It is our estimate that the secondary settlement may amount
to approximately half an inch over a period of 50 years
Since the underlying subsoils are so variable in type thickness and
also compression characteristics it should be understood that it is very
difficult to accurately predict the actual time of ground settlement
Therefore we recommend that field instruments such as settlement gauges
be installed during site grading construction to monitor the actual rate
of ground settlements
The drainline infrastructure and residential construction may he
commenced after most of the ground settlements have occurred
Surcharge
If a shorter waiting period is desired the project site may be
surcharged Sufficient surcharge should be placed on the site for an
adequate period of time to induce the anticipated ground settlements due
to the new fills
We estimate that four 4 feet of surcharge will reduce the waiting
period prior to any drainline installation and building construction to
about two 2 months
It is also recommended that a settlement monitoring program be
executed in conjunction with the above recommended surcharge program in
order to avoid premature removal of the surcharge loads The settlement
gauge readings should be forwarded to the project geotechnical engineer
for review Determination of the proper time to remove the surcharges
should be based on the review of these readings

Site Grading
Based on the current grading concept up to four 4 feet of fill is
needed to raise the site for the proposed residential subdivision
development
At the on set of earthwork the project area within the contract
grading limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed All existing
vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable materials should be
removed and disposed of properly off site in order to prevent
contamination of the cleared grades
After clearing any area to receive fill should be scarified at least
6 inches below the cleared grade moisture conditioned to near optimum
moisture content and then recompacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum
ary density per ASIM Test Method D 1557 78 Loose soft or yielding spots
encountered during clearing and or recompaction should be removed and
replaced with select fill materials compacted to the same density
requirement
Due to the volume of fill anticipated off site fill material will be
required The imported fill material showld consist of non expansive
select materials less than 3 inches in maximm size with less than 20
passing the 200 sieve
Very soft lagoonal clays and other highly compressible marshy
deposits may be excavated during the construction of the drainline
extension These materials are considered unsuitable for use as a source
of fill or backfill and should be disposed of properly off site to
prevent contamination of the project site
Fill materials should be placed in maximm 8 inch thick loose
horizontal lifts and compacted to 90 of its ASIM D 1557 78 maximum dry
density

Buil Foundation
Provided that the new site fills are properly placed and compacted
we believe that shallow footing foundations bearing on the compacted fills
may be used to support the future one to two story lightly loaded
residential structures
However we recommend that site specific soil explorations be
performed for the individual lots when house plans are available The
additional exploration is required to evaluate the feasibility of
site specific house construction with respect to the specific underlying
subsoil conditions
Excavation
Based on the current design concept it appears that trench
excavation up to about 10 feet below the existing ground surface may be
required for the construction of the proposed drainline extension and
manhole
Excavation Method
Easy excavation is anticipated at the project site The method and
equipment used for excavation should be the choice of the contractor
within practical limits and safety considerations
Due to load consideration the width of the drainline trench
excavation should be kept to a minimm to reduce the potential of
causing significant settlements of the utility line under the weight
of the overlying trench backfill
Excavated material should not be stockpiled within 15 feet of the
excavation in order to reduce loading on the excavation sidewalls
and also to reduce the potential of ground movement or subsidence
which may damage the adjacent roadway and or sidewalk pavement

Excavation Support
Based on the soft loose subsoils and high groundwater condition we
believe that adequate shoring with bracing should be provided to
support the trench excavation
The excavation support system should be the choice of the contractor
within practical limits and safety considerations
Generally it is essential that properly braced shoring such as
continuous interlocking steel sheeting be installed prior to
excavation and be maintained tight against the excavation faces to
reduce the potential of adjacent ground loss or movement which may
jeopardize the existing adjacent structures
The interlocking steel sheeting should be driven properly to
sufficient depth to mobilize enough soil resistance and to reduce the
possibility of bottom heaving or quick condition which may be
created by upward seepage pressure during dewatering if it is
necessary to dewater the excavation
Joints and Connections
Due to the anticipated long term ground settlement conditions we
recommend that flexible joints and connections be used for the new
drainline to allow for some amount of differential settlement

across the northern end of the site

Backfill
The trench excavation should be backfilled as soon as the drain pipe
is properly bedded and installed In general the trench backfill
requirements should follow the current Standard Specifications for
Public Works Construction of the City and County of Honolulu
The first zone of backfill extends from the bedding material to at
least 12 inches above the top of the pipe Select free draining
well graded granular material is recommended for this first zone of
backfill to minimize the compaction effort required and the resultant
stresses in the pipes The granular material should be well graded
less than three 3 inches in size and have no more than 10 passing
No 200 sieve Compaction by water ponding or jetting may be allowed
provided the granular material is self draining
The trench backfill from 12 inches above the top of the pipes to
within two 2 feet of the finished grade may consist of excavated
on site soil except for clays and organic materials The backfill
should be mechanically compacted to a minimum 90 of its maximum dry
density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
The trench backfill within two 2 feet of the finished grade should
consist of select borrow compacted to a minimm 95 of its maximum
dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Precautions should be taken against pipeline flotation before and
during trench backfilling due to possible influx of groundwater into
the trench The buoyant forces due to groundwater influx may lift
empty or partially filled pipes

Pipe Bedding
Distribution of bearing stresses against the bottom of a pipe has a
great impact upon the load carrying capacity of the pipe itself
Pipe bedding is therefore an important design consideration
Due to the prevailing high groundwater level and the soft loose
subsoil conditions we recommend that an eighteen inch thick crushed
rock cradle be used to stabilize the bottom of the utility trench and
provide a working platform for the pipe installation The rock
cradle material should consist of clean granular material such as
3B Fine gravel and be brought up to within six 6 inches of the
invert grade
We also recommend that clean granular material such as 3B Fine
gravel be used as the bedding material for the new utility A
minimum of six 6 inches of compacted or clean granular bedding
material such as 3B Fine gravel should be placed below the new
pipes The granular bedding should extend beyond the pipes a minimum
width of one fourth the outside pipe diameter and also half way up
both sides of the pipes The recommended pipe bedding detail is also
illustrated on Plate 3
