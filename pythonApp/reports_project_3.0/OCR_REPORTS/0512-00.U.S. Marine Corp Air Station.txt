SITE CONDITIONS
The building site is located on the northwest corner of Mokapuu
and Harris Road in the Marine Corp Air Station The site is
approximately 300 by 300 feet The site is fairly level and
covered with a moderate growth of grass The location of the
proposed structures with respect to the adjacent streets is
shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
Geologically the site is underlain by coral reef structures
that were formed intermittently with lagoon deposits Fresh
water streams eroded out portions of the coral reef These
cavities were eventually filled with coral sands and fragments
A lowering of the sea level or elevating of the land mass has
left the reef exposed
The site is immediately underlain by a medium dense to dense
coral reef structure In Boring 3 which is immediately under
lain by a foot of organic silt coral structure was found to
a depth of about 5 feet and which was underlain by very dense
to medium dense coral sands and coral fragments This area
may have been an eroded stream channel that was subsequently
filled in by coral deposits However because of the varia
bility of coral environments over short vertical and horizontal
distances the possibility of encountering coral with charac
teristics different than those found in these boreholes cannot
be discounted

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The site is suitable for the proposed service stations and a
building supported on conventional shallow footings Slabs
on grade may be used
Foundations
It is our recommendation that the foundation be supported on
the coral reef or sands located at or near to the existing
ground surface A bearing value of 2000 pounds per square
foot may be used for dead plus real live loads Footings
should be placed a minimum of one foot below final adjacent
grade Pressures may be increased by one third when consi
dering wind or seismic loads Continuous footings should have
a minimum width of 12 inches We anticipate that the settle
ments from the proposed building loads will be less than one
quarter of an inch
Slabs On Grade
Slabs on grade may be supported on the coral reef formation
The silty material encountered in the vicinity of Boring 3

should be removed prior to construction We recommend that a
compacted 4 inch open graded granular pad be used for support
of slabs This granular layer will act as a capillary break
beneath slabs In areas where moisture sensitive flooring is
to be used we recommend that a waterproof membrane be placed
above the granular layer
Tank Excavations
The coral reef encountered in our borings will contain local
hard areas that may require hard ripping or jack hammering
The exact locations of these hard areas will be very difficult
to determine at this time based on the high variability of
reef formations However the sand deposits found in Boring 3
will not be difficult to excavate If this large sand deposit
proves to be extensive the tank excavation may have to be
provided with shoring in accordance with all applicable safety
requirements The design and safety of the shoring and sheet
ing should be the responsibility of the Contractor It
appears that the bottom of the tank excavation will be approx
imately one foot above the encountered water table
Backfill
The backfill around and above the gas tank should be compacted
sufficiently so that no settlement on the ground will occur in
that area
