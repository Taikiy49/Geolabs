INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the foundation design of the proposed Aviation
Physiology Training Building at the Naval Air Station Barbers Point
Oahu Hawaii
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this study was to explore the subsurface soil and
water conditions at the site to obtain pertinent soil data for the
foundation design of the proposed structure
The scope of our study included drilling and sampling six 6
borings to depths of 15 0 to 16 5 feet below the existing ground
surface Also included were laboratory testing of selected samples
obtained engineering evaluation of the field and laboratory data
development of design recommendations and report preparation
Detailed description of our field exploration and laboratory test
results are presented in the appendices of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed project consists of the construction of a one story
structure approximately 145 by 150 feet in plan together with two
driveways and a parking area The proposed building will be used as an
Aviation Physiology Training Building

The two new driveways are planned to be located in front of and

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located on a parcel surrounded by Midway
Boulevard Leyte Street Saipan Street and the south side of the
airfield in the Naval Air Station
The site is an open area fronted with two big elephant ear trees
facing Midway Boulevard Adjacent to the north side of the site is an
existing asphaltic concrete paved area which is higher than the
surrounding grades The asphaltic concrete pavement is severely cracked
and the area could be a demolished building site and or an abandoned
parking lot
The proposed building area is generally flat and has short
grasses The existing ground surface grades up slightly higher toward
the front areas near the two big trees
SUBSURFACE SOTL CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions were explored by drilling and sampling
six 6 borings to depths of 15 0 to 16 5 feet below the existing ground
surface The borings were drilled at locations around the originally
proposed building site Subsequent to our field exploration the
proposed building site was enlarged and thus portion of the new building
area is not covered by the six drilled borings However we believe
that the subsoil conditions encountered in the drilled borings could
still be representative of the enlarged building site

The borings disclosed that the site was mantled by an approximately
6 inch to 1 5 foot thick surface layer of miscellaneous fill materials
including dense silty sand and stiff clayey silt silty clay with
gravel Laboratory tests disclosed that the clayey surface soil is
expansive Below the thin upper surface fill layer was a generally hard
coral formation consisting of coral reef limestone deposits to the
drilled depths of the borings
Groundwater was not encountered in the shallow borings during our
field exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Our subsurface exploration encountered a thin miscellaneous fill
layer at the surface over generally hard coral reef formation at the
project site We recommend that shallow spread or continuous wall
footings bearing directly on the underlying coral formation or the new
structural fill layer be used for foundation support of the proposed
structure The surface contaminated expansive soil fill layer should be
stripped and hauled off site under the structure and pavement sections
Site Grading
It is our understanding that the proposed finished floor grade of
the building will be 101 5 feet based on an assumed bench mark
elevation of 100 feet on a nail in A C walkway near the intersection of
Midway Boulevard and Leyte Street Based on this proposed finished
floor grade up to about 1 5 feet of site filling is anticipated for the
proposed construction
Thin surface soil layer was found to be moderately to highly
expansive and unsuitable for subgrade support of slabs and pavements
Therefore we recommended that this upper average 1 foot thick layer
should be removed below the building and pavements and replaced with
non expansive borrow soils

At the on set of earthwork the areas within the project grading
limits should be thoroughly cleared Unsuitable materials such as the
surface expansive fill layer vegetation debris and rubbish should be
removed and disposed of properly off site
After clearing the areas to receive new fill should be moisture
conditioned and recompacted to 90 of its maximum density as established
by ASIM D 1557 78 test method Any additional loose or wet spots
encountered at the subgrade level should be removed and replaced with
select granular material compacted to the above mentioned standard
New imported fill material should consist of non expansive less
than 0 5 swell per ASTM D swell test method select
granular materials less than 3 inches in maximm size with less than 20
passing the 200 sieve
Fill layers should be placed in 8 inch compacted thickness and
compacted to 95 of ASTM D 1557 78 maximum dry density of the fill
material
Foundation Support
Based on the encountered soil conditions in the borings we believe
spread or continuous wall footing foundations bearing directly on the
hard coral formation at shallow depths with an allowable bearing
capacity of 5000 p s f may be used
Footing should be placed a minimm of 24 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grade and bear direct on the undisturbed coral
formation Uplift wind or other lateral loading conditions may further
increase the embedment of the footing foundation

The depth to the coral reef formation may be quite irregular due to
the nature of the reef growth Therefore provisions should be made for
deeper footing embedment in localized areas Alternatively a lower
allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f may be used for footings bearing
directly on the compacted select borrow fill layer
The bearing capacity values are for total dead plus live loads only
and may be increased by one third for short duration wind loading
Estimated settlements under the design loading are minimal for footings
bearing directly on the coral For footings bearing directly on the
compacted select borrow fill materials estimated total and differential
settlements are on the order of less than one fourth of an inch
Resistance to lateral loads for the proposed structure can be
obtained by frictional resistance and passive earth pressure A
frictional coefficient of 0 4 may be used between the bottom of the
footing and the coral compacted fill In addition an equivalent fluid
pressure of 300 p s f per foot of depth may be used to determine the
passive earth resistance against the vertical buried faces of the
footing foundation

fresh water streams during the growth stages of the coral which
prevent the growth of the coral It is possible that a cavity may be
part of a small old stream and therefore may extend horizontally
through the site rather than being an isolated occurrence

Slab On Grade
Concrete slab on grade is anticipated for the proposed building
The thickness of the slab section required depends on the anticipated
traffic loads and the subgrade soil consistency Based on the above
site grading recommendations and that the proposed slab on grade will be
placed on compacted imported non expansive select fill materials the
following slab section is recommended
Concrete Slab On Grade No Vehicle Traffic
4 Inch Portland Cement Concrete
4 Inch Base Course
8 Inch Total Thickness
The slab thickened edges exposed to any unpaved area should be
embedded at least 12 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade

LIMITATIONS
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations
made in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation
engineering principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all
other warranties either expressed or implied
The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are based
on our site reconnaissance soil information from borings and laboratory
test data obtained from this study
Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encountered and cannot
be fully determined by taking soil samples Unforeseen conditions such
as cavities pockets of organic soils trash fill or expansive soils
may occur in localized areas and may require additional probing and
corrections in the field Therefore additional expenditures may be
needed during construction to attain a properly constructed project
Some contingency fund is thus recommended for larger grout intake in
cavities and to accommodate this and other possible extra costs
This report has been prepared in order to assist the engineer in
the foundation design of this project

Field Exploration

The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling six
6 borings to depths of 15 0 to 16 5 feet below the existing ground
surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 A
truck mounted drill rig was used to drill the borings
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by visual and
textural examination in the field by our engineer who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory All soils
were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A summary of the materials encountered are
presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 6
Soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving a 2 inch
a 140 pound hammer free falling 30 inches Generally the blow counts to
drive the sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the Logs of Borings at
the appropriate sample depths
Rock core samples were obtained by coring with a Triefus Triple tube
core barrel in five foot runs The core recovery REC and rock quality
designation RQD of each 5 foot run are shown on the Logs of Borings at
the appropriate sample depths
