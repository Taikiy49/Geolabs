INTRODUCTION
This report presents the result of a soils investigation study
of the ground subsidence occurred at the tower s swimming pool area of
the Pearl Ridge Gardens and Tower in Pearl Ridge Oahu Hawaii The
general site location is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
PURPOSE AND SCOPE OF SERVICES
The purpose of the investigation was to explore the subsurface
soil conditions review available soil data evaluate probable causes
of the subsidence and provide recommendations pertaining to the
proposed reconstruction of the pool and equipment room facilities
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1008 10 Page 2
Our study consisted of the following
1 Drilling three 3 borings around the existing pool to
depths of 56 5 to 65 5 feet below the existing ground
surface The boring logs are attached in Appendix A
2 Laboratory testing of the soil samples recovered from
the affected area in order to evaluate their pertinent
engineering characteristics The laboratory test
results are summarized in Appendix B
3 Review of available soil data from previous reports and
settlement surveys A list of the reports reviewed is
attached to this report
4 Settlement surveys of various points around the pool
were taken by us to monitor the settlement rate of the
ground A summary of the settlement readings is
presented in Appendix C The location of the survey
points is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
5 Slope Indicator readings were also taken in Boring No 1
to monitor possible lateral movement of the existing
slope bank next to the stream A summary of the slope
indicator readings recorded is presented in Appendix D
6 Engineering analysis and Teport preparation

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located in the Pearl Ridge area between
Moanalua Road and the H 1 Freeway along the western bank of a small
stream
The swimming pool area is located on the east side of the
existing 22Z story Pearl Ridge Tower The general layout of the site
is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The pool and its deck show considerable Signs of settlement
induced distress These distresses have been discussed in detail in a
report by R M Towill Corp dated December 1976
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our borings encountered 35 to 40 feet of what appears to be
man made fill of clayey silts The consistency of this fill varies
from medium stiff to very stiff Between the depths of 15 feet to
about 35 feet the consistency is generally in the range of medium
stiff
Below this layer of fill material our borings encountered very
stiff to hard clayey silts and silty clays that are most likely of
alluvial origin
Boring Nos 2 and 3 encountered basalt formation at depths of
54 5 and 49 0 feet respectively

HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
Based on the reports made available to us the following is a
general chronology of events at the site prior to our investigation
l Initial borings and soil investigation for the development
was performed in May 1973 by
Associates Four borings were drilled for the tower
structure Borings were not drilled for the pool
construction
2 Site grading took place from March 1975 to March 1976 The
grading plans indicate that the pool and the deck area were
near grade and required only thin fills Fills up to 15 feet
high were placed east of the pool on the sloping area next
to the stream
3 After construction and filling of the pool cracking occurred
on the deck slab and the pool some time between March and
July 1976 The magnitude of settlements experienced is
unclear

4 Borings were drilled in July 1976 by Soils International to
investigate causes of the observed pool distress Settlement
points were then established around the pool and monitored
for almost a month The survey generally indicated little or
ho movement for the one month period
5 Following additional distress to the pool additional borings
were drilled by Soils International in 1979 Resurvey of the
1976 survey points indicated ground settlements up to about 8
inches occurred since 1976
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that the site is underlain by
about 35 to 40 feet of fill varying from medium stiff to stiff over
very stiff alluvium and residual soil The encountered fill layer was
the least competent member of the soil profile and it appears likely
that settlements had occurred in this layer
Several possible causes of the settlements were evaluated and
are briefly discussed below
1 Settlement Due to Weight of Site Grading Fill

feet high were placed in the area east of the pool to form
the high bank next to the stream Based on the laboratory

The previously measured settlement contours center around the
pump house and drainline C Broken lines could wash soil
into the lines and cause ground settlements However it
appears that some ground settlement would be needed to
trigger the pipe breakage Therefore the settlements may
have been increased by broken lines but they are probably not
the initial cause

Review of the project plans indicate that the equipment room
had been planned for below the deck A revision moved it to
its current location This revision was made during site
grading The center of the settlement is located in the area
where the room was supposed to be It is possible that an
excavation could have been made for the equipment room and
may have been filled up again after the revision was made
If i11 was dumped into the excavation without compaction
this would cause settlement over the excavation However
this would only cause localized settlement over the excavation
and would not be responsible for the areal settlements

6 Soil Collapse Due to Saturation
flied teenie tat ke ee
The older 111 material appears to have been placed with
little compaction and at a low moisture content resulting in
a loose open structure Saturation of this soil with time
due to landscape water seepage etc could cause collapse
of the soil structure Comparing previous 1973 and 1979
boring data with our recent borings indicates that the soil
moisture content has increased about 5 to 10 percent and the
Soil generally has a higher density now which would require
some reduction of volume settlement
Based on the available data this appears most likely to be
the source of the settlements observed
Laboratory load saturation tests were performed on
undisturbed samples taken from our boring and to evaluate
the potential for additional settlements The tests indicate
that most of the settlement has probably occurred
Remedial Measures
The settlement readings taken during our investigation measured
minimal movement indicating that the ground settlements may have
stabilized However it should be noted that the 4 month monitoring
period is very short in comparison with the period of time in which
the settlements experienced It is possible that the rate of movement
might be too slow to be perceived within the period of monitoring
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1008 10 Page 9
In addition the movements might be cyclical i e affected by
seasonal changes in soil moisture during wet or dry periods There
fore it is possible that some long term settlements may still occur
although the magnitude of additional settlements are expected to be
much less than those experienced to date
The existing pool has experienced Significant settlement and
may have developed cracks large enough to cause leakage Due to the
questionable present structural condition of the pool and the
possibility of some additional long term settlements we recommend
that the existing pool be abandoned The condition of the existing
utilities below the pool area are also highly suspected and should be
disconnected prior to being abandoned
Several alternatives for the foundation design of a new pool are
presented below
1 Shallow Foundation
Assuming that the settlements have essentially stopped a new
pool could be constructed bearing on the existing fill
The soil within 3 feet of the bottom of the pool excavation
should be overexcavated and recompacted to a minimum of 90 of
its maximum dry density established by ASTM D 1557 78 to
create a uniform compacted bearing layer
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Page 10
The ground surface below future pavements and improvements
should be proof rolled and recompacted prior to construction
Soft spots should be overexcavated and recompacted to the
above specified density
The bottom of the pool slab should be designed as a mat
foundation to tolerate some amount of possible future
Settlement The following soil parameters may be used in the
pool design
a Allowable Bearing Value 2500 p s f
b Active Soil Pressure 40 p c f
c Passive Soil Pressure 250 p c f
d Coefficient of Soil Friction 0 4
It should be noted that the success of this option depends on
the amount of future settlement experienced Since most of
the major settlements had occurred more than 7 years ago and
the recent settlement readings indicated minimal settlements
future settlements are anticipated to be small However
predicting the future performance of the ground settlement is
quite difficult and therefore this option will involve some
amount of risk
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1008 10 Page 11
2 Pile Foundations
ee
The new pool may be supported by pile foundations extending to
basalt rock formation at about 60 feet below the existing
ground surface
High pile capacity in excess of 100 tons per pile can be
developed by large diameter piles bearing on the rock
formation However high Capacity is not required for the
pool structure and it would be more economical to use smaller
piles at a lower capacity
Ten by ten square precast and prestressed concrete piles are
recommended with an allowable pile capacity of 50 tons per
pile
The piles should be driven with a hammer delivering about
lbs of energy and to a driving resistance of about
40 blows per foot but not to exceed 10 blows per inch
Minimal settlements are anticipated for the pile foundations
It should be noted that should some additional ground
settlements occur differential settlements could occur
between the pool deck and other hnon pile supported structures
and the pile supported pool

3 Densification of the Fill
me me ee SH NN eC HO He EO SO Miu tL he ees ee
The underlying medium stiff fill layer could be densified by
compaction piles driving gravel material into the ground or
compaction grouting to create a less compressible layer A
more conventional pool design could then be utilized These
methods are believed to be rather costly and the results are
difficult to assess therefore it is not recommended for the
project
Additional Monitoring
As mentioned earlier settlement readings were taken for this
investigation for a period of about 4 months This is a relatively
short period of time in comparison with the length of time in which
the pool area has been settling It should also be noted that the
recent weather has been rather dry and the ground settlements could be
affected by extended rainy periods which could increase the soil
moisture
It is important to have a reasonably long period of time to
monitor the settlements in order to better assess the anticipated
future settlements
If the amount of future settlement is small the shallow mat
foundation system could be utilized for the new pool at a much lower
cost than the pile foundations
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1008 10 Page 13
In order to help the owners to make an assessment of the
potential risks in utilizing the shallow foundation system we
recommend that the settlement and Slope monitoring surveys be
continued for an additional 6 months
Design Review
Plans for foundation design should be forwarded to the soils
engineer for review and written comments prior to construction to see
that the intent of our recommendations is included in the project
plans and specifications
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during
earthwork construction to monitor the contractor s grading operations
It is recommended that the footing excavation and pile driving
operations be observed by the soils engineer during construction to
see that adequate bearing has been obtained and the subsurface
conditions are consistent with the findings of our subsurface
investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested
and approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above inspections

APPENDIX D
An inclinometer system was installed in the Boring No 1
to monitor lateral movements in the underlying soil deposits
Readings were made during the field exploration and are summarized
on Plate D l The indicator casings were left in place and
monitoring instruments are available for additional readings if
desired
The deflection measurement System consists of a servo
accelerometer probe connected by Special electrical cable to a
digital readout unit The probe has spring loaded guide wheels
which fit into the casing grooves The probe is lowered into
the casing and readings of the casing deflection are made at
2 foot intervals After readings are made in one direction
the probe is rotated 180 degree and read again The reading
process is then repeated for a second axis perpendicular to the
first axis
The inclinometer readings provide a measurement of
lateral displacement of the casing relative to the bottom of
the casing
