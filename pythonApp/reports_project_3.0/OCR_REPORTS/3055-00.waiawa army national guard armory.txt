INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Waiawa Army National Guard Armory Additions and
Renovations in Pearl City on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity
of the site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3055 00 Page 2
This report summarizes the findings and geotechnical engineering recommenda
tions resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering analyses The
recommendations presented herein are intended for foundation design and site grading
The findings and recommendations presented herein may not be appropriate to form the
basis for preparation of final construction cost estimates nor for construction bidding
purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located along Waihona Street in Pearl City on the island of Oahu
Hawaii It is proposed to expand the existing armory facility by constructing two new
additions on the west side of the adjacent existing building complex as shown on the Site
Plan Plate 2 The proposed additions will consist primarily of a two story structure
adjacent to the existing building The two story building will have split level floors with a
finished floor elevation of about 55 feet Mean Sea Level MSL at the northern half of
the building and a finished floor elevation of 53 feet MSL at the southern portion of the
building A one story building covering approximately 1 500 square feet in plan dimension
is also planned for the project We understand that concrete masonry walls with concrete
floor construction will be utilized for the proposed buildings Based on the information
provided to us maximum column loads on the order of 35 and 75 kips are anticipated
for the one story and two story structures respectively
Based on the proposed finish floor elevations grading consisting of minor cuts
and fills on the order of two 2 feet is anticipated to provide a level building pad for the
proposed construction Our experience in the area suggest that the project site may be
underlain by residual soils over decomposed basalt material Special attention is given
to the potential swelling characteristics of the near surface soils on the future floor slabs
supported on grade

The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface soil
conditions at the project site to develop an idealized soil data set for the formulation of
geotechnical engineering design recommendations pertinent to the design of foundations
and site grading for the project The work was done in general accordance with our fee
proposal to the Department of Accounting and General Services dated March 5 1993

engineering properties
5 Analyses of the field and laboratory data for the formulation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertinent to the design of foundation and site
grading for the proposed project

project and presenting our findings and recommendations

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
On the basis of our geotechnical exploration silty and clayey soils with gravel were
encountered at the proposed new armory addition location from the surface to the
maximum depth explored The soils generally grade from stiff to very stiff with increasing
depth On this basis the proposed armory addition may be supported on a conventional
shallow foundation system bearing on the near surface silty soils Because the existing
16 inch concrete pipe traverses the proposed structure and is within proximity to the
W O 3055 00 Page 6
foundation elevation we recommend that the pipe be removed and the excavation
backfilled with compacted fill
Laboratory tests performed on the on site clayey silt and silty clay soils indicate
that the material generally has a moderate shrink swell potential when subjected to
fluctuations in the moisture content Therefore we recommend that concrete slabs on
grade be supported on a layer of non expansive capping fill to reduce the potential effects
of the moderately expansive near surface soils
Recommendations addressing the above considerations and other geotechnical
aspects of the project are discussed in the following sections of this report
Foundations
We believe that a shallow foundation system consisting of spread and or
continuous footings bearing on the near surface native soils may be used to support the
proposed buildings Allowable bearing pressures of up to 3 000 pounds per square foot
p s f may be used for footings bearing on the in situ near surface native soils The
allowable pressures are for dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third for
transient loads such as wind or seismic forces
We recommend that footings be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the
lowest adjacent grade We understand that the footings for the existing buildings are
embedded at least 12 inches below the existing grade Therefore footings for the
proposed buildings adjacent to the existing armory buildings should extend at least to the
same depth as the existing footings or deeper to avoid surcharging the existing
foundations The actual depths of the existing footings should be confirmed in the field
during construction
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3055 00 Page 7
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive pressure acting
against the near vertical faces of the foundation system and by frictional resistance
developed between the base of the foundation and the bearing soil Resistance due to
passive earth pressure may be estimated with an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 pounds
per square foot per foot of depth p c f This assumes that the soil around footings is
well compacted or that the concrete for footings are cast neat against relatively
undisturbed soil A coefficient of friction of 0 35 may be used for footings bearing on the
compacted fill Unless covered by pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the
upper 12 inches of soil should be neglected
We estimate that the total foundation settlements to be on the order of less than
one inch 1 with differential settlements on the order of inch provided that the
recommendations provided herein are followed
It is recommended that the bottom of all footing excavations be compacted to a
minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as determined in accordance with
procedures described in ASTM Test Method D 1557 If soft and or loose materials are
encountered at the bottom of footing excavations they should be over excavated to
expose firm materials The over excavation should be backfilled with granular material
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as determined in
accordance with procedures described in ASTM Test Method D 1557 or lean concrete
Footing excavations should be observed by a representative of Geolabs Hawaii
prior to the placement of reinforcing steel or concrete to confirm foundation bearing
conditions and the embedment depths
If foundations are located next to utility trenches or easements they should be
embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane extending

upward from the bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility
lines
Surface water should be diverted away from the foundations to avoid ponding of
surface runoff water that may adversely affect the bearing capacity of the soils below the
foundations
Slab On Grade
The southern portion of the proposed project site is underlain by silty clay soils
which are considered moderately expansive when subjected to moisture fluctuations
Unless improvements supported by these soils are properly designed there is a potential
for future distress to the improvements resulting from the shrinking and swelling of these
soils due to changes in the moisture content To reduce the potential for structural
distress resulting from the swelling of the on site expansive soils we recommend that the
concrete slabs be supported on 12 inches of non expansive select fill material The non
expansive select fill should extend at least two feet 2 beyond the perimeter of the
proposed structures
The select fill material should be placed as soon as possible after the scarification
and recompaction of the existing subgrade soils Subgrade soils should be prepared in
accordance with the recommendations presented in the Site Grading section of this
report The select fill would serve as a layer of moist soil above the underlying potentially
expansive subgrade soils and would reduce the potential loss of moisture from the
subgrade soils The reduction in drying should reduce the potential swelling of the
underlying moderately expansive clayey soils Compaction requirements for the subgrade
soils and select fill are discussed in the Site Grading section
The prepared capping fill should be wetted and kept moist prior to final placement
of concrete Concrete floor slabs should be supported on a six inch 6 layer of No 3B
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3055 00 Page 9
Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 Gradation above the capping fill to serve as a slab
cushion and capillary break To reduce potential moisture infiltration and subsequent
damage to the floor coverings a moisture barrier such as a plastic membrane on top of
the slab cushion is recommended A one inch 1 layer of moist fine sand should be
placed over the vapor barrier membrane to provide protection for the membrane and to
aid in curing of slab concrete
Where the slab is subjected to vehicular traffic a six inch 6 layer of base course
compacted to 95 percent of its maximum dry density as determined by ASTM Test
Method D 1557 is recommended in lieu of the No 3B Fine gravel capillary break
Conventional slab on grade construction may be utilized for the proposed structure
constructed on the 12 inch thick non expansive capping fill However it is recommended
that flexible floor coverings such as carpet or sheet vinyl be utilized to mask any minor
slab cracking It is also recommended that interior walls be designed to incorporate
some flexibility to accommodate possible movements
Site Grading
We anticipate that site grading for the proposed building will consist of minor cuts
and fills on the order of two foot 2 to provide a level building pad for the proposed
construction In addition over excavation within the limits of the proposed building to
provide the non expansive capping fill may be necessary The following guidelines should
be observed during the grading operations
1 Prior to commencement of earthwork the areas within the contract grading
limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation trees
debris and other deleterious material should be removed and disposed of
properly off site The existing concrete pipe below the proposed building
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3055 00 Page 10
should be removed backfilled with granular material and compacted to at
least 90 percent relative compaction
2 Any loose weak or yielding areas disclosed during clearing operations
should be over excavated to expose firm ground and the resulting
excavation should be backfilled with compacted fill
3 Areas that are to receive fills should be scarified to a depth of eight inches
8 moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above optimum moisture
content and compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction Relative
compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a
percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in
accordance with ASTM D 1557 test procedures
4 All fill and backfill material should be tested for suitability for its intended use
by Geolabs Hawaii prior to delivery to the site Only select fill should be
used within the upper 12 inches below the finished subgrade in building and
slab areas The non expansive select fill material snould be well graded
from coarse to fine with no particles larger than three inches 3 in largest
dimension The material should contain less than 20 percent passing the
No 200 sieve
5 All fill and backfill should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 12 inches in
loose lift thickness and compacted to not less than 90 percent relative
compaction All surfaces should be finished to create smooth and
essentially unyielding surfaces
6 The finished grade outside the building should be sloped to shed water
away from the foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding

Utility Trench
We envision that new utility lines may be required for the project A granular
bedding consisting of six inches 6 of select borrow ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation is
recommended under the pipes Self compacting granular materials such as No 3B Fine
gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation should be used for the trench backfill up to about
one foot 1 above the pipes to provide adequate support around the pipes and to
reduce compaction of the backfill thus reducing the possibility of damaging the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level one foot 1 above the pipes
to the top of the subgrade or finished grade should consist of select granular material
The backfill should be placed in maximum 10 inch level loose lifts and mechanically
compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for
future ground subsidence Where trenches are below pavement areas the upper two feet
2 of the trench backfill below the pavement subgrade should be compacted to 95
percent relative compaction
Drainage
The finished grades outside the buildings should be sloped to shed water away
from foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding Also it is advised that gutter
systems be installed around the building and that the discharge be diverted away from
the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near the foundations
and slabs should also be avoided Planters next to foundations should be avoided or
have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the above
foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation
and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations should be
properly backfilled against the walls or slab footings immediately after setting of the
concrete to reduce water infiltration

Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and maintained to drain
all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundations
Design Review

Construction Observation
It is recommended that the Geolabs Hawaii be retained to provide geotechnical
engineering services during project construction to observe footing preparation This is
to observe compliance with the intent of the design concepts specifications or
recommendations and to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions
differ from those anticipated at the time this report was prepared Geolabs Hawaii should
be accorded the opportunity to provide construction observations to confirm our
assumptions in providing the recommendation presented herein
Any imported material required should be tested by Geolabs Hawaii for
conformance with the project specifications prior to hauling to the project site
If the actual soil conditions encountered during construction are different from
those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to the design
should be made
