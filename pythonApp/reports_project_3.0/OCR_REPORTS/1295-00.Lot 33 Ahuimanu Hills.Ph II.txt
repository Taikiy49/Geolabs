Dear Mr Vernon
In accordance with your request we have made a study of the near
surface soil conditions at the above referenced lot in order to
provide foundation recommendations for the proposed residence The
study consisted of drilling two hand auger probes laboratory testing
of the swelling potential of the soils and a review of the subdivision
site grading and preliminary house plans This report summarizes our
findings and recommendations
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
The proposed residence will be a one to two story wood frame
structure with a Monier tile roof Concrete slab on grade is planned
for the portions of the house on the level lot area while post and
beam construction is planned for the portions over the slope area
Preliminary plans indicate that a swimming pool with a deck supported
by a 10 foot high retaining wall is planned for the slope area
The layout of the proposed structure is shown on the Site Plan
Plate 1
SITE CONDITIONS
The subject lot is located in the Ahuimanu Hills Subdivision Phase
II to the south of Puapoo Place The western third of the site is a
relatively level pad area East of the pad area the ground surface
slopes at about 2H 1V into a drainage easement which covers the
eastern half of the lot Ground surface elevations range from 168
feet in the drainage easement along the eastern property line to 185
feet at the level pad area
The ground cover ranges from low grasses in the pad area to thick
grass and moderately dense brush in the slope and drainage easement
areas
A review of the Ahuimanu Hills Phase II grading plans indicates that
up to 8 feet of 111 was placed in the western half of the subject lot

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on our field explorations conventional Slab on grade and
post and beam construction may be used to support the house
Due to the soft soils underlying the existing fill it is anticipated
that construction of the proposed high retaining wall backfill and
swimming pool will cause significant settlements as a result of the
surcharging effects of the backfill and water Therefore the
swimming pool as planned is not recommended
House Foundation
For the proposed light residential structure spread footing
foundations and or slab on grade may be used To minimize the effects
of slope creep and erosion slab perimeter footings and post footings

Site Grading
Due to the soft soils encountered below the existing fill we
recommend that no new fills be placed on the lot Site grading should
be limited to clearing and grubbing of the vegetative cover Footing
and backfills should be placed in maximum 6 inch lifts and compacted
to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density established by ASTM
D 1557 78
Surface water should be diverted away from the top of the slope and
slope planting utilized as soon as practicable to reduce erosion

Slab On Grade
Concrete slab on grade is planned for portions of the house garage
and driveway In order to provide a barrier against future water
infiltration under the slab we recommend that the slab perimeter
footing be embedded a minimum of 12 inches below the outside finished
ground surface
Perimeter footings situated near the top of the slope should be
embedded an additional amount to maintain a minimum 6 foot horizontal
set back distance measured from the slope face to the outside edge of
the perimeter footing
It is recommended that slab on grade be placed over a 4 inch layer of
3 Fine rock cushion fill base course and should be separated from
foundations
A plastic vapor barrier placed over the base course layer may be used
should a capillary break be deemed necessary to protect floor covering

High Retaining Wall and Swimming Pool
As previously discussed the construction of the high retaining wall
and swimming pool will most likely result in very large settlements
The settlements imposed upon the underlying soft soils may adversely
affect the stability of the existing slope
The settlements may be avoided if the design of the wall and pool
incorporate the use of pile supported foundations or could be
constructed without additional fill required However we do not at
this time have sufficient subsurface data to provide recommendations
for pile foundations
If the pool cannot be constructed without imposing additional load on
the soft subsoils we recommend that the proposed wall and pool be
deleted from the project

Lot Drainage
The finish lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings should be
shaped to shed water away from the foundations and to avoid ponding
condition Also the owners should be advised to install a gutter
system around the house and that the gutter water should be diverted
far away from the footings and walkways Excessive landscape watering
near the foundations and slab should also be avoided
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage considerations are essential for the proper performance
of the above recommended foundations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of
strength The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled
tight against slab footing immediately after setting of the concrete
to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained
to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing
foundation

CONSTRUCT ION MONITORING
It is recommended that the footing excavations and backfill
operations be observed by the soils engineer during construction to
see that adequate bearing has been obtained and that the subsurface
conditions are consistent with the findings of our subsurface
investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above inspections
