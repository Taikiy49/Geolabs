In accordance with the project specifications one pile load test was conducted on a
specified test pile designated as TP 4 for the MCON Project P 587 Sub Drive in MSF
between December 10 and 12 2008 This letter report presents the results of our field
observations and the test data obtained during the tension pile load test
The tension pile load test was conducted on a 24 inch octagonal precast
prestressed concrete pile Based on the project specifications Test Pile TP 4 was
instrumented with 14 vibrating wire embedment strain gages Model No VCE 4200
manufactured by Geokon The embedment strain gages were installed in pairs at levels
spaced at about 15 feet along the load test pile with the first level at about 5 feet above
the tip of the pile and the last level above the mudline as shown in the following table
The mudline elevation at the test pile location was approximately 41 feet Mean Lower
Low Water MLLVW and the test pile was driven to a tip elevation of about 124 5 feet
MLLW A schematic sketch showing the embedment strain gage locations is provided on

In addition the anticipated soil profile based on the boring drilled near the test pile
TP 4 location provided on the plans is presented on Plate 1 Test Pile TP 4 was driven
on The test pile was driven using a Junttan HHK9A hammer
delivering a maximum rated energy of 79 200 foot pounds During the test pile driving a
Geotechnical Quality Control Specialist from Healy Tibbitts Builders Inc maintained a
record of the blow counts and field observations of the driving behavior The test pile
driving log provided by Healy Tibbitts Builders Inc is presented as Plate 2
Test Pile TP 4 was driven to an elevation of about 124 5 feet MLLW The test pile
was driven to a depth where approximately 15 5 feet of the pile was left above the water
The top 3 feet of concrete was chipped off in order to provide sufficient strands to fit the
tensile load testing frame Therefore the total length of the load test pile was 137 feet
measured from the top to the tip of the test pile A multiplexer was used to connect the
14 embedment strain gages to a Geokon Micro 10 datalogger which recorded the strains
in the embedment strain gages during the tension pile load test
The test pile was loaded in general accordance with ASTM D 3689 Based on the
project specifications and amendments to the requirements it was planned to load Test
Pile TP 4 to 350 kips and hold for 24 hours using the Standard Loading Procedure
Provided the test pile did not fail during the holding period the test load was removed and
reloaded until failure occurred or to a maximum test load of 648 kips whichever occurred
first using the Quick Loading Procedure Failure in the project specifications is defined
by a pile head displacement equal to or greater than the following
Pile Head Displacement Pile Elastic Displacement for Full Pile Length inches
Plus 0 15 inches Pile Diameter inches 120
In general the test pile was loaded to a tension test load of about 428 kips which
was higher than the specified test load of 350 kips in eight load increments Each load
increment was held for at least 30 minutes or until the rate of pile head movement was
generally stable or for a maximum of 2 hours

Based on the shape of the curve of the tension load test and the Davisson failure
criteria the 24 inch octagonal concrete pile did not fail at the maximum tension test load of
about 690 kips The strain changes in the 14 embedment strain gages were recorded
using a Micro 10 datalogger during the tension pile load test The data was downloaded
and converted into a Microsoft Excel file The printout of the strain gage readings are
presented in Appendix A The electronic version of the strain data Excel format will be
transmitted via e mail
As part of our quality control procedures on the embedment strain gages we
collected absolute vibrating wire readings from the 14 embedment strain gages on
four different occasions The four different occasions included the time we received the
strain gages from the manufacturer prior to installing the strain gages into the load test
pile after casting of the concrete and stressing of the concrete pile after transportation of
the load test pile to the site prior to pile driving operations and after pile driving prior to
load testing In general the readings from the embedment strain gages indicated that all
the strain gages were functioning properly prior to and after the pile driving operations as
shown on Plate 5
General information pertaining to the embedment strain gages calibration sheet for
the strain gages and calibration sheet for the load cell are included in Appendix B
