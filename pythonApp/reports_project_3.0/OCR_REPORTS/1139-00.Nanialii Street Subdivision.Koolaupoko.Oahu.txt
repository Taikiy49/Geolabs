PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this soil investigation was to explore the soil
conditions at the site to provide data to aid in the design of
the site grading for the proposed subdivision development

The scope of this investigation includes a review of available
soils and geologic information visual observation of the
existing conditions and shallow hand auger probes at the
project site The investigation was performed as a preliminary
reconnaissance due to the dense vegetative cover at the site
and the relative inaccessibility for truck mounted drilling
equipment
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests which

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located in the southern portion of the
Keolu Hills area in the Koolaupoko district of Oahu Hawaii
It is roughly triangular in plan view becoming narrow to the
south and bounded on all sides by existing residences
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1139 00 Page 3
The site is situated in a relatively steep sided drainageway
which drains towards the north The ground surface elevations
range from approximately 124 feet at Nanialii Street entrance
to the subdivision to approximately 210 feet at the southern
tip of the site
The development site is densely vegetated The ground cover
consists primarily of California grass haole koa and vines
There are several piles of yard refuse along the upper borders
of the site
Several poorly defined benches at approximately 10 foot inter
vals were noted on the site These appear to be the result of
previous grading operations
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed plan is to clear and grade the site for a single
family residential development consisting of 6 house lots
It is anticipated that site grading will involve cuts up to 6
feet deep on the eastern and western slopes and fills up to 7
feet thick in the center of the drainageway
Asphalt and concrete pavements are anticipated for the proposed
development

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The project site is located in the caldera complex of the Koo
lau Volcano During the eruptive phase of this volcano rising
hot gases and steam altered the caldera filling lavas into a
soft decomposed rock This decomposed rock under exposure to
weathering has broken down to an easily eroded tan sandy silt
soil
Field Exploration
Four hand auger probes were drilled at the site to a maximum
depth of 3 0 feet below the existing ground surface in order to
explore the subsurface conditions and to obtain samples for
laboratory testing
Based on the probes the site appears to be underlain by very
stiff sandy silts with gravel derived from the in situ wea
thering of altered basalts
A layer of fill up to approximately 6 feet thick was observed
along the upper boundaries of the site This existing fill is
composed of dense silty sands with gravel and appears to be
well compacted
Groundwater was not encountered at the time of our exploration

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration encountered very stiff residual soil
material consisting of tan to brown sandy silts Laboratory
tests indicate that these soils generally have low swelling
potential Because of the sandy nature of this material it
tends to be quite prone to erosion therefore proper slope
planting materials are recommended

Prior to the commencement of site grading the site
should be cleared of vegetation rubbish and other
deleterious material This organic material should be
disposed of properly off site
In the central portion of the site there is an eroded
area which is approximately 4 feet deep and 4 feet
wide This area extends from the inlet of the existing
culvert for approximately 50 feet to the south This
eroded area should be undercut to firm ground and to
equipment width

The resulting depression should be backfilled with

In areas to receive fill the ground should first be
proof rolled with a heavy roller to detect any soft
spots in the existing ground If soft Spots are
encountered they should be over excavated to firm
ground and recompacted The ground should then be
scarified to a minimum depth of 6 inches brought to
near optimum moisture content and then compacted to a
minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density as established
by ASTM D 1557

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that slope cutting and filling operations
be observed by the soils engineer during construction This
is to verify that cut material is as anticipated and that no
seepage zone occurs during construction The soils engineer
should be present during site filling to see that adequate
compaction has been obtained and the subsurface conditions
are consistent with the findings of our subsurface investiga
tion The recommendations given in this report are
contingent upon the above inspections
