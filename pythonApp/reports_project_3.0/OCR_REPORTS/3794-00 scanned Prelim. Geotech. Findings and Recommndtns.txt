Preliminary Geotechnical Findings and Recommendations
The Home Depot Dole
Honolulu Cahu Hawaii
This letter serves to present our preliminary geotechnical findings and
recommendations for the proposed Home Depot project located in the Dole Iwilei area
on the island of Oahu Hawaii as shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 Detailed
discussions and geotechnical engineering recommendations will be presented in our
forthcoming report
ubsurf Conditions
Our field exploration at the subject site consisted of drilling and sampling six
borings to depths ranging from about 56 0 to 61 5 feet below the existing ground surface
In general the subsurface conditions varied from the western boundary along Alakawa
Street to the eastern boundary Along the western boundary our field exploration
generally encountered a surficial fill crust about 2 to 3 feet thick overlying compressible
lagoonal deposits of clayey gravels to a depth of about 15 to 25 feet below the existing
ground surface The loose and soft lagoonal deposits were underlain by stiff to very stiff
alluvial materials consisting of silty sands and gravels extending to depths of about 45 to
50 feet below the existing ground surface Below the stiff to very stiff alluvial materials
very stiff clay was encountered to the maximum depth explored of about 61 5 feet below
the existing ground surface
Better subsurface conditions were encountered along the eastern boundary
where the fill and lagoonal layers were much thinner Our field exploration generally
encountered a surficial fill crust of sandy gravel about 1 foot thick overlying soft lagoonal

Site Grading
We understand that it is proposed to raise the finish grade elevation approximately
4 feet above the existing ground surface for the proposed new building construction in
order to accommodate the truck loading dock elevation The field exploration
encountered soft lagoonal deposits of variable thickness below the entire building site
Significant settlements are anticipated under any new fill and significant building
loads because of the compressible nature of the lagoonal deposit underlying the site at
shallow depths The amount of settlements would vary from location to location
depending mainly on the thickness of the fill required for site grading and the thickness of
the compressible deposit at that location
For 4 feet of new fill we estimate areal settlements on the order of about 6 to
12 inches Due to the presence of soft clayey materials and sand within the lagoonal
deposits the time needed to achieve approximately 90 percent consolidation is estimated
to be about 6 to 12 weeks

Preliminary Foundation R mmendation
Building Foundation
Because of the moderate structural loads anticipated from the building planned
for the Home Depot and the variable nature of the lagoonal deposits a shallow
foundation system may be subjected to appreciable differential settlements due to the
consolidation of the underlying soft lagoonal deposits As a result we recommend that
the building be supported by a deep foundation system of precast prestressed concrete
piles extending through the weak lagoonal deposits to bear in the underlying stiff to very
stiff alluvial materials The piles would derive support principally from skin friction
between the pile shaft and the stiff to very stiff alluvial materials to support column loads
For this project depending on the column structural loads we believe that
16 inch octagonal precast prestressed concrete piles may be used to support the
proposed structures Due to the variability of the subsurface materials encountered
during our field exploration shorter pile lengths may be required towards the eastern
boundary of the site For the 16 inch octagonal piles at the western boundary we
recommend that approximately 50 foot long piles designed for an allowable compression
loading of approximately 50 tons be used for preliminary design and budget estimating
purposes At the eastern boundary we recommend that approximately 40 foot long
16 inch octagonal piles designed for an allowable compression loading of
approximately 50 tons be used for preliminary design and budget estimating purposes
Higher pile capacities may be developed with deeper embedments
Based on the materials encountered in the borings drilled for this exploration we
believe that predrilling for the piles prior to pile installation will not be necessary
Additionally predrilling should be avoided in order to minimize spreading of the
contaminated soil

Pile Supported Structural Slab
An alternative to the site filling and surcharge program is to utilize a structurally
supported slab system The advantages of this system are 1 minimize potential
differential settlements between the structure supported on piles and the floor slab
supported on grade and 2 minimal fill may be needed and the floor slab may
remain above grade to allow space for a soil remediation gas release system of
the near surface contaminated soils encountered at the site Recommendations
for the preliminary design of pile foundations are previously discussed in the
Building Foundation section of this letter

The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling
six borings to depths up to about 61 5 feet below the existing ground surface at the
approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were drilled
using a truck mounted drill rig equipped with continuous flight augers
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and
textural examination in the field by our Engineer who monitored the drilling
operations on a near continuous basis These classifications were further
reviewed visually and by testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general
conformance with the Unified Soil Classification System as shown on Plate A
Graphic representation of the materials encountered are presented on the Logs of
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance
with ASTM Test Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel
Sampling of Soils by driving a 3 inch O D Modified California sampler with a
140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts needed to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown on the Logs of Borings at
the appropriate sample depths
It should be noted that the blow counts appear high and may be
skewed due to the friction on the hammer line and presence of
angular coral gravel in the subsurface materials
