I INTRODUCTION
General
This report presents the results of our engineering
investigation to determine the subsurface soil and founda
tion conditions for the proposed development The purpose
of the investigation was to review the previously acquired
data and provide the additional subsurface information neces
sary for the design of a suitable foundation support system
for the proposed structure
The study included review and analyses of previous informa
tion subsurface borings laboratory testing and engineering
analyses The report summarizes our work on the project
and presents recommendations pertinent to site development
All work was performed in general accordance with our proposal
dated August 1 1972
Project Description
The proposed building site is located on the Diamond Head
side east of Alexander Street between Beretania Street and
Bingham Street in the Moiliili area of Honolulu
The property is approximately 125 feet wide by 190 feet long

The proposed 10 story tower structure approximately 100 ft
by 90 t and the surrounding parking structure will cover
much of the site A basement about 10 feet deep is also
planned
The tower portion of the structure will have maximum column
loads on the order of 850 kips and minimum column loads of
approximately 450 kips The parking structure will have
column loads on the order of 50 kips
II FIELD EXPLORATION
One subsurface boring 61 5 feet deep was completed on October 5
1972 under the supervision of a geologist from our firm The
boring was accomplished using truck mounted rotary drilling
equipment with continuous flight hollow stem auger
Four borings were completed at the site prior to our exploration
program
The boring locations are shown on the Site Sketch Plate 1 and
the boring logs are presented in Appendix A
Either Standard Penetration Test or 2 5 inch O D ring samples
were taken at selected depth intervals not exceeding 5 feet
2
i
W O 401
The Standard Penetration Test consists of driving a 2 inch O D
split spoon sampler 18 inches with a 140 pound hammer free
falling a distance of 30 inches The number of blows required
to drive the sampler the last 12 inches is termed the Standard
Penetration Resistance N and is an approximate measure of
the relative density or consistency of a soil These resistance
values are plotted on the boring logs Samples obtained were
visually classified in the field and representative material was
placed in air tight containers and returned to our laboratory
for inspection and testing

debris derived from the Tantalus volcano
Cinders erupted from Tantalus blanketed the building site
area approximately 5000 years ago Also waterworn cinders
were washed onto the site
Subsequently brown silty CLAYS recent alluvium derived

IV ENGINEERING RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the results of the field investigation laboratory
testing review of previously obtained data and engineering
analyses the following conclusions and recommendations are
presented
A General
The subsurface conditions encountered at the site were
relatively good from a soils and foundation engineering
viewpoint
4
W O 401
It is our professional opinion that the on site soils
in general are capable of supporting the proposed
structures
Grading
Excavations approximately 10 to 12 feet deep will be required
for the basement and footings Relatively steep cuts in
the clay and silt should be stable on a short term basis
however it is advisable to support the excavations to reduce
the risk of settlements on adjacent property
If water or seepage is encountered during excavation dewater
ing may be necessary
Foundations
1 Tower Structure
Spread and or continuous footings may be utilized to
Support the proposed structure Settlement rather
than soil shear strength governs the maximum allowable
soil bearing pressure for this project We recommend
that a maximum allowable soil bearing pressure of
4000 psf be utilized for the largest anticipated column
load Footings for the lesser column loads within the
tower structure should be designed for allowable soil
2
W O 401
bearing values selected to minimize differential
settlement see Pressure Settlement Chart Plate 2
The allowable soil bearing value for the lighter column
loads should not exceed 6000 psf
Footings should be founded at least 24 inches below
finished basement slab elevation and should have a
minimum width of 24 inches
Total settlements for the tower portion of the structure
should be less than 1 5 inches Differential settle
ments should be minimized by proportioning footings
according to column loading and by structural design
Parking Structure
Spread or continuous footings designed for a maximum
allowable soil bearing pressure of 5000 psf may be
utilized for the parking structure
The footings should be at least 12 inches below finished
basement slab elevation and should have a minimum
width of 18 inches
Total settlements for the parking structure should not
exceed 0 75 inches Differential settlement within the
6
W O 401
parking structure should be less than 0 25 inches
3 Differential Settlement between Structures
me Fee nt Se tween otructures
Differential settlements between the tower and parking
structures may be as high as 1 inch The structural
connections between the two portions should be designed
to tolerate differential movement
A construction sequence that would allow more dead
load to be applied in the tower portion prior to connect
ing the structures would tend to reduce differential
settlements
D Slab on Grade
Slabs on grade should have a minimum thickness of 4 inches
and be reinforced with welded wire mesh to prevent cracking
A 6 inch cushion of free draining granular Material should
be placed beneath slabs
E Lateral Earth Pressure
Basement and retaining walls less than 10 feet high should
be designed for an equivalent fluid pressure of 35 pounds
per cubic foot assuming only moderate compaction and
granular backfill If a high degree of backfill compaction
is required or the walls cannot yield even slightly an
J
W O 401
equivalent fluid pressure of 60 pounds per cubic foot should
be used
Surcharges due to adjacent footings hydrostatic pressure
construction equipment etc must be added to the above
values
Walls greater than 10 feet high should be evaluated on an
individual basis to determine the earth pressure distribution
Water was encountered near the proposed basement elevation
during the previous investigation We recommend that a
perimeter drainage system be installed around the basement
to prevent hydrostatic pressure buildup see Plate 3 The
backfill around the drain must provide adequate filtering
to prevent loss of fines from the soil Basement walls
should also have some type of damproofing
Review and Inspection
The grading foundation and basement or retaining wall plans
and details for the structure should be forwarded to the
soils engineer for review and comments prior to finishing
the design
It is recommended that all footings and slab areas be
inspected by the soils engineer prior to placing forms

LABORATORY TESTING
The laboratory testing program was developed to establish the
engineering properties of the soils and rocks encountered at
the project site
le
Classification Tests
tion tests
a Visual Classification All soil samples obtained from
ae sitication
_the borings were brought to our laboratory where they were
visually reclassified to confirm or modify the field classi
fication prior to finalizing the Logs of Borings
b Water Content Determinations In addition to visual
a ee cermminations
classification typical samples were tested for natural
water content as an aid in soil Classification and in
evaluating soil properties The water content values are
based upon the dry weight of the soil
c Atterberg Limits Atterberg limit tests were performed
on selected clay and silt Samples obtained from the borings
for the purposes of identification and correlation of soils
Standard procedures ASTM D 424 for the plastic limit and
ASTM D 423 for the liquid limit were used in the performance
of these tests
a Grain Size Analysis Sieve analysis were performed on
granular soils For fine grained soils the hydrometer
OF Shad Cit Sty
test was performed to determine the distribution of the
grain sizes beyond the No 200 sieve
Shear Strength Tests
a Unconfined Compression Tests Intact core and split
spoon soil samples which apparently exhibited high plasticity
were tested for unconfined Strength Length to diameter
ratio of 2 was maintained throughout the tests to avoid
Scatter of test results and to facilitate evaluation of
shear strength values for design purposes
b Direct Shear Test In general soil Samples were tested
for shear strength using a 2 41 I D direct shear box Each
set consisted of at least two tests each test run under
different normal loads The results are plotted as Normal
Stress vs Shear Strength with a failure envelope drawn to
determine values of cohesion c and angle of frictional
resistance
c Consolidation Tests Consolidation tests were performed
on selected undisturbed Ying samples of the compressible
soils to provide basic data for making settlement calculations
Porous stones were placed on the top and bottom of the samples
to allow drainage Vertical loads were applied in increments
with each load increment being allowed to consolidate prior
to adding the next increment Measurements of the time and
consolidation were obtained during each load increment and
rebound was measured during the unloading portion Consoli
dation test results are plotted in terms of percent settlement
versus applied loads
d Swell Tests The ring sample was placed between porous
stones and a 100 psf load was applied to the Sample for a
24 hour period The difference between the initial and final
sample heights is the amount of swell which is expressed as
a percentage of the initial height of the sample
e Maximum Density Test Samples of soil are compacted in
a mold of given size with a 10 pound rammer dropped 18 inches
to determine the relationship between moisture content and
density of the soil At least three trial points at different
moisture content are run to determine the maximum density
and optimum moisture content The test is run in accordance
with ASTM D 1557 70
