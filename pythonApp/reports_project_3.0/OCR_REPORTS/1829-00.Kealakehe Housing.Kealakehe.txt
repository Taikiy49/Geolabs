SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located on the western side of Kealakaa Street at
the northern end of the street The site consists of a strip of land
about 500 feet wide by 1600 feet long The existing Kealakehe low rent
housing is located east of the site
The ground surface is hummocky and slopes generally to the west The
topography generally reflects the original surface of basaltic lava flows
The area is densely vegetated by Christmas berry guava lilikoi and
other trees and grasses The site has been used as pasture land for
cattle
The ground surface elevations of the site vary from about 710 to 790
feet

PROJECT CONSTDERATIONS
The proposed development consists of twenty five 25 2 story 4 plex

Sewage disposal will be through a cesspool system

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The project site is underlain by basaltic lava flows of the
prehistoric member of the Hualalai Volcanic Series These flows are
generally of Holocene age and were extruded by the now dormant Hualalai
Volcano
Basaltic lava flows generally occur in two forms pahoehoe and aa
Pahoehoe is characterized by a smooth ropy or billowy surface and
frequently contains voids of lava tubes Aa is characterized by a
clinkery surface and has a dense inner core
The lava flows at the project site are a transitional phase between
the two forms and have characteristics of each
Subsurface Explorations
Six 6 borings were made on the project site by utilizing a
truck mounted rotary hydraulic drill rig advancing an NWM core barrel or
4 inch diameter continuous flight augers Core samples were obtained from
the borings for classification and testing

Soil Conditions
The borings encountered volcanic rock formation from the ground
surface to the maximum depth drilled The volcanic rock formation
consisted generally of medium hard gray vesicular basalt with interbedded
layers of medium dense coarse gravel clinker Voids between 0 2 and

Groundwater was not encountered in the drilled borings at the time of
our field exploration

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration disclosed basically competent volcanic rock
formation at the proposed project site From a soil engineering point of
view the site is suitable for the proposed residential development
Shallow foundations may be used for the proposed structures
The rock formation is non expansive therefore slab on grade
construction may also be utilized
Site Grading
Site Preparation
Prior to grading surface vegetation and organic material should be
removed and wasted off site In areas to receive fill or at grade
construction tree roots should be removed to a minimum depth of 12
inches and the resulting excavation backfilled and compacted to 90
of its maximum density as established by ASTM D 1557 78

In fairly level areas prior to receiving fill the existing ground
should be scarified to a depth of 12 inches moistened and compacted
to 90 maximum density as per ASTM D 1557 78
Due to the dry and windy site conditions the contractor should have

Fill Material and Compaction Requirements
In general the excavated rock material less than 12 inches in size
may be used as general fill material If the excavated rock material
does not contain sufficient fines to produce a well graded material
the surficial ash soil may be mixed into the excavated rocky material
to increase the percentage of fines to provide a better graded
material for compaction and increase the stability of the fills
Because of the scarcity of soils in the area excess ash soils may be
stockpiled and saved for later use as topsoil for the development of
the lots
It may be necessary to stockpile the clinker material while the deep
cuts are being made so that the boulder sized material may be
disposed of in the deeper portions of the fill
Boulders encountered in excavation may be used in deep fills provided
that the following recommendations are followed
1 Boulders should be spread out and must not be nested together
They should be placed so that compaction equipment will be able
to compact around them

2 No part of the boulder should encroach any closer to finished
subgrade than the boulder s largest dimension and in no case be
closer than 3 feet to finished subgrade The lower portion of
the 3 foot capping fill will act as a choking layer between the
underlying boulders and the upper fill
3 No boulder larger than 32 feet in its largest dimension should he
placed in the fill It may be necessary to reduce the size of
larger boulders or dispose of them off site
4 Care must be exercised to avoid placement of boulders in the
proposed utility alignments to prevent difficulty in the later
excavation of the utility trenches
Fills should be placed in horizontal loose layers not to exceed 18
Conventional compaction testing is generally not practicable in
excavated volcanic rock material due to the large particle size
Instead a testing program consisting of visual observations and test
pits to determine the number of passes of a compactor required to
achieve the desired compaction should be conducted at the start of
the project The number of passes can then be used as the criteria
for compaction
Fills to be placed on slopes having ratios steeper than 5 1 H V
should be keyed and benched into the underlying consolidated clinker
or rock formation to prevent instability of the new fill The keys
and benches should not be based in the upper ash soil or loose
clinker

Foundations
Spread or continuous footing foundations are recommended for the
proposed structures
The bottom of all footing excavations should be compacted to 95
maximm dry density prior to the placement of reinforcing steel except
when the footing will bear directly on in situ rock
An allowable bearing value up to 10 000 p s f may be used for
footings bearing on in situ volcanic formation For footings bearing on
compacted structural fill a bearing value of 2500 p s f may be used to
design the footings The above values are for total dead plus live loads
and may be increased by one third when temporary wind and seismic loads
are considered
A minimm footing width of 12 inches should be utilized and the
bottom of all footings should be placed a minimm of 12 inches below the
lowest adjacent grade or to rock formation In addition the outside edge
of the footing should be set back a minimum of 5 feet from the nearest
outside slope face This set back may require deepening of the footing
embedment to maintain the above specified minimum set back
To reduce the possibility of footing settlement due to possible large
cavities or lava tubes in the underlying lava rock drilled probes are
recomended below every major footing or on 10 foot centers for continuous
footings The probe holes which could be drilled with a track percussion
drill should have a minimum diameter of 2 inches and extend below the
bottom of the footing a depth equal to two times the footing width or a
minimum of 6 feet

If a cavity is suspected below a footing additional probe holes
should be made to delineate the horizontal extent of the cavity Voids
greater than 6 inches in diameter should be exposed or grouted on the
recommendations of the soils engineer The probing and grouting
operations should be monitored by the soils engineer to see that the
aforementioned recommendations are carried out properly and to locate any
potential voids near the footing excavations
Surface water should be diverted away from the building foundations
Anticipated settlements are expected to be relatively small on the
order of less than one half of an inch for structures bearing on rock or
on compacted structural fill
Lateral Resistance
To resist lateral loads a coefficient of friction of 0 5 between the
foundations and underlying rock materials is recommended
In addition a passive soil resistance of 400 p c f may be used
Slab On Grade
Slabs should be placed over a 4 inch cushion layer of gravel
generally less than three fourths of an inch and larger than one fourth of
an inch in size
Slab on grade should have perimeter footings extending down a minimum
of 12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade or to rock formation to
reduce moisture infiltration below the slab
The slab subgrade should be kept moist and protected from drying
prior to concrete placement in order to minimize slab cracking due to
dehydration The subgrade soil should be compacted to a minimum of 95 of
the maximum dry density established by ASTM D 1557

Percolation Testing
A cesspool system is planned for the development
Percolation tests were performed in and 5 to evaluate
the suitability of the underlying formation for cesspool disposal
The percolation testing consisted of discharging water from a water
truck into the drilled holes and observing the increase in water level or
head in the drilled hole An injection rate of 50 gallons per minute was
used in Borings 3 and 4 In Boring 5 an injection rate of 100 gallons
per minute was used The tests were run for a minimm of 30 minutes in
each boring The injected water dissipated without an appreciable
build up of the groundwater level The percolation test results are
included in Appendix B
Based on our test results it is our opinion that cesspool disposal
is feasible For estimating purposes it may be assumed that the
cesspools will extend to depths of 10 to 12 feet below the existing ground
surface

exceed eighteen inches 18 in thickness Each layer shall be
spread uniformly and blade mixed to attain uniformity of
material and water content within each layer

Rock fragments or boulders less than twelve inches 12 in
diameter may be utilized provided they are not concentrated in
any one area and that there is sufficient fine grained material
to fill the voids between the rocks
Boulders encountered during excavation may be used in deep
fills provided that the following are followed
1 Boulders shall be spread out and must not be nested
together They should be placed in such a way that the
compaction equipment will be able to compact around then
2 No part of the boulder shall encroach any closer to
finished subgrade than the boulder s largest dimension and
in no case be closer than 3 feet to finished grade The
lower portion of the fill capping layer should be a
choking layer and consisting of intermediate size rock
and cobble materials
3 No boulder larger than 3 feet in its largest dimension
shall be placed in the fill It may be necessary to reduce
the size of larger boulders or dispose of them off site
4 Care must be exercised to avoid placement of boulders in
the proposed utility alignments to prevent difficulty in
the later excavation of the utility trenches
Conventional compaction testing is generally not practicable in
excavated volcanic rock material due to the large particle
size Instead a testing program consisting of visual
observations and test pits to determine the number of passes of
a compactor required to achieve the desired compaction should
be conducted at the start of the project The number of passes
can then be used as the criteria for compaction

E The compaction equipment may be sheepsfoot vibratory or
pneumatic tire rollers suited to the soil type and capable of
producing the specified results in an expedient and efficient
manner The equipment shall be in good working condition
fully ballasted and self cleaning
F The compaction shall be accomplished in a systematic and
uniform manner over the entire fill area to produce a uniformly
compacted fill
G Fill material placed in an unsatisfactory condition and not in
accordance with these specifications shall not be approved and
shall be removed or reworked until the required density is
obtained
H The soils engineer shall be provided unrestricted access to the
fill to perform the necessary inspections and testing
Removal and Backfilling of Underground Structures
Underground structures such as cesspool cisterns septic tanks
wells pipe lines etc shall be demolished and removed as
directed by the soils engineer Backfilling of the excavations
shall be in accordance with these specifications
Other Requirements
A No fill shall be placed during unfavorable weather conditions
When interruption of work is due to heavy rain the previous
fill surface shall be approved before resumption of earthmoving
operations
B The contractor shall maintain the surface of the fill so that
it will be free draining and will not pond surface water
