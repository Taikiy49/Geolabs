Dear Mr Mori
This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations performed
during the mass grading detention embankment filling operation and road construction
operations from October 2003 to February 2005 for the above project in accordance with
our fee proposal dated
Project Considerations
The project site is located at the southwestern end of the South Gully in Mililani
Mauka adjacent to the Interstate Route H 2 Wahiawa bound Mililani off ramp The
general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map
Plate 1 The project involves construction of 25 foot high earth embankment with a
slope inclination of three horizontal to one vertical 3H 1V and access asphalt concrete
roadway The site grading work consists of about 25 feet of fills for the new earth
embankment and up to about 12 feet of cuts for the access roadway The approximate
project limits are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Site Gradin
The earthwork operations for the project were performed by Delta Construction
Corporation At the on set of earthwork areas within the contract grading limits were
generally cleared and grubbed of vegetation and deleterious materials Materials
generated from the clearing operations were disposed of off site Following the clearing
and grubbing operations the areas to be cut were excavated Finish grades were
compacted using a sheepsfoot and or smooth drum vibratory roller
In general on site excess soil materials generated from the excavation were
primarily used in the earth embankment area The on site fill materials generally consisted
of clayey silt soils Imported fill materials were utilized for the earth embankment The

imported fill materials were hauled in from Increment 3 of the Mililani Mauka Development

Stockpile Investigation Report by Fewell Geotechnical Engineering Ltd dated
Samples of the fill and backfill materials utilized during construction were obtained
and transported to our office for laboratory testing The maximum dry densities and
optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general accordance with
American Society of Testing and Material ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified
Proctor The test results are summarized on Plate 2 of this report
Due to the intended use of most of the fills to be placed for detention of water
fine grained fill with proper slope and channel protection were recommended In general
fill materials consist of low expansive materials less than 3 inches in largest dimension
with a minimum CBR value of 10 Field density tests were performed with the nuclear
density gauge ASTM Test Designations D 2922 and D 3017 During the fill and backfill
operations the materials were generally placed in near level lifts brought to near the
optimum moisture and compacted to obtain the specified degree of compaction based on
visual observations and or field density tests Periodic field density tests were performed
during the fill and backfill operations to confirm our field observations as variations in
construction procedures and soil conditions were noted
Key and bench were constructed on both sides of the South Gully for earth
embankment fills of the detention basin
A total of 80 field density tests including re tests of failed areas were performed
using the nuclear density gauge method in general accordance with ASTM Test
Designations D 2922 and D 3017 The results of the compaction tests performed during
construction of the above project are summarized on Plates 4 1 through 4 6 of this report
Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site
survey stakes where available These locations and elevations should be considered
accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our professional opinion that the earthwork operations for the South
Gully Detention Basin project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by
this report were performed in general conformance with the intent of the plans and
specifications and our recommendations In general areas assessed to be
unsatisfactory were re worked to achieve the specified minimum compaction
