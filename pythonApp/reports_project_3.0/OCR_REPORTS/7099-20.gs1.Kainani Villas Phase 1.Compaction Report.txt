Dear Mr. Keane: 
This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations and testing 
services performed during the building grading and infrastructure installation for the 
Kainani Villas - Phase 1 project from December 11, 2015 to June 26, 2017 in accordance 
with our fee proposals dated November 19, 2015 and June 15, 2017. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The Kainani Villas - Phase 1 project site is located at Lot E of Kukui’ula 
Development Company’s U/AA Subdivision in Koloa on the Island of Kauai, Hawaii. The 
Phase 1 development consists of the construction of two residential buildings (1C and 
2T), four garage structures, and installation of roadways and utilities. The site is 
generally bounded by Lot F to the north, Ala Pualeikukui to the east, Kainani Place to 
the west, and the intersection of Ala Pualeikukui and Kainani Place to the south. The 
project location and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map, Plate 1. A 
layout of the project site is shown on the Site Plan, Plate 2. 
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services 
pertaining to the building grading, and roadway and utility installation for the Phase 1 
project site. 
BUILDING GRADING AND UTILITY INSTALLATION 
We observed the building grading and utility installation work for the Kainani Villas 
Phase 1 project from December 11, 2015 to June 26, 2017. The building grading work

consisted of footing excavation, and footing and slab-on-grade subgrade preparation for 
the project. The installation of utilities and roadway was also performed. The building 
grading, and utility and roadway installation work were performed by Earthworks Pacific 
Inc. During the building grading operations, we obtained samples of the on-site and 
imported materials utilized as fill and backfill materials and transported them to our 
laboratory for testing. The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were 
established in our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D1557 (Modified Proctor), 
and the test results are summarized on Plate 3. 
The fill and backfill materials were moisture-conditioned to above the optimum 
moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density 
as determined in accordance with ASTM D1557. The finished subgrade and aggregate 
base course materials for the roadways were moisture-conditioned to above optimum 
moisture and compacted to a minimum 95 percent relative compaction. 
During the building grading, and utility and roadway installation work, periodic field 
density tests were performed in general accordance with ASTM D6938 (Nuclear Gauge 
Method). Results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 4.1 
through 4.20 of this report. These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces 
and on constructed fills and backfills at the site. Test locations and elevations were 
estimated by pacing and interpolating from on-site survey stakes. These locations and 
elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used. 
Areas assessed as unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction 
was obtained and/or approved by our field representative based on visual observation and 
hand probing. 
Our services consisted of part-time field observation of the contractor’s fill 
placement, subgrade preparation, and compaction procedures and confirmation of 
subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above grading operations. 
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the 
referenced tests, it is our opinion that the building grading, and utility and roadway 
installation work for the above project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period 
covered by this report were performed in conformance with the general intent of the 
recommendations in the following references.
