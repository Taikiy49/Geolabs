This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering services performed during
the roadway pavement and utility construction for the Kuilima Drive Widening project from
February 19 through in general accordance with the scope of services
outlined in our fee proposal dated
EARTHWORK OPERATION
Based on the information provided we understand that it is desired to widen the
existing Kuilima Drive from a two lane divided roadway to a four lane divided roadway
with new pavements meeting the roadway standards of the City and County of Honolulu
The new pavements are designed in accordance with the Structural Design
Requirements for New Asphalt Concrete Pavements issued under the Engineering and
Policy Memorandum No by the City and County of Honolulu In addition new
utilities such as water drain and sewer lines are planned for the roadway widening
project The layout of the roadway widening alignment is shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
Royal Contracting Company is the general contractor who also performed the
earthwork for the project The earthwork operation consisted of clearing and grubbing of
the existing ground and pavements over excavation and general fill placement
preparation of pavement subgrade placement and compaction of aggregate subbase and
cushion fill and utility trench excavation and backfill
2006 Kalihi Street Honolulu Hawaii 96819
Hawaii California
Pacific Island Realty LLC Page 2
Following the clearing and grubbing operation the existing ground was generally
over excavated to the pavement subgrade level to provide the recommended pavement
structural sections Prior to the placement of the aggregate subbase the pavement
subgrade was scarified to a depth of about 8 inches moisture conditioned to above the
optimum moisture and recompacted to a minimum of 95 percent of the maximum dry
density determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor However due to
the high moisture content of the in situ soils resulting from the inclement weather
overnight showers in the Kahuku area the contractor was not able to achieve the
minimum compaction requirement for the pavement subgrade soils
In order to facilitate the pavement construction work we recommended
stabilizing the subgrade soils by placing a layer of woven geotextile fabric Mirafi 500X
over the proof rolled subgrade prior to placing the aggregate subbase material The
geotextile fabric serves to provide a separation between the underlying high moisture
content soils and the upper aggregate subbase material It should be noted that the
geotextile fabric is not part of the pavement structural section Therefore subsequent
damage to the geotextile fabric resulting from trenching activities will not affect the
performance of the pavement structural section
Aggregate subbase in the pavement structural section was moisture conditioned to
above the optinum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative
compaction Utility trench backfills were generally placed in near level lifts brought to
about 2 percent above the optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent
relative compaction
During the earthwork operation samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing
The maximum dry densities and optinum moisture contents were established in our
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test
results are summarized on the attached Plate 2
Periodic field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations to
confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures and soil conditions
were noted A total of 207 field density tests including retests of failed areas were
performed using the nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM D 6938 The results
of the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 14 of this
report These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed
engineered fills and backfills Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and
interpolating from on site survey stakes and construction plans These locations and
elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used
Areas assessed as unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction
was obtained and or approved by our field representative based on visual observation
and hand probing
