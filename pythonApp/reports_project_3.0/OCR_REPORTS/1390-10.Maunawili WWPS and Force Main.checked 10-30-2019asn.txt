Introduction
This report presents the results of our soil engineering
investigation performed for the subject project The purpose of this
investigation was to explore the subsurface soil and groundwater
conditions along the proposed force main alignment and at the waste
water pump station site to provide soil data for use in the design and
construction of the proposed project The approximate location of the
alignment is shown on the Project Location Map Plate l
Our work was done in general accordance with our proposal of
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests which
we usec in the engineering analysis to formulate our conclusions and
recommendations are presented in the appendices of this report
GENERAI PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed Wastewater Pump Station WWPS will be constructed of
reinforced concrete and concrete masonry units The structure will have
three levels The first level will be an underground compartment with a
finished flcor elevation of about 7 feet above Mean Sea Level MSL and
will house the wet well and sewage pumps The second level with a

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Thick soft deposits were encountered below the proposed pump
station site It is planned to fill the site generally up to 10 0 feet
Since the new fill must be placed after construction of the pump station
significant ground settlements which would affect the new structures are
anticipated Therefore for the pump station foundation end bearing
piles driven down to the conglomerate layer at about 75 feet depth are
recommended to minimize settlements and to accommodate down drag forces
due the new fill settlement Additionally the portions of the sewer
lines under the proposed fill should also be supported by pile
foundations
Dewatering and shoring of the excavation is anticipated for the
pump station construction Along the proposed force main alignment
stiff fill and residual scils are encountered It is anticipated that
some amount of basalt boulders should be anticipated in the trenches in
these soils
The proposed sewer invert level will vary from 2 5 to 11 feet below
the existing ground for this portion of the alignment therefore
adequate shoring and bracing may be necessary We believe they should be
left to the discretion of the contractor

According to the preliminary plans prepared by Park Engineering

for the pump station site We believe the increased vertical
pressure due to the new fill loading will cause consolidation of
the underlying soft alluvial deposits and result in ground
settlements Ideally to minimize settlements te the structure and
pile foundation down drag due to negative skin friction the site
grading filling should be performed prior to the pile
installation to allow the ground to settle prior to foundation

construction However since the new pump station must be in
operation prior to demolition of the existing structures it is

Prior to filling the site should first be cleared of vegetation

recompacted to 90 of its maximum density as established by ASTM
D 1557 78 test methcd
Fills should be placed in maximm 10 inch horizontal loose lifts
and compacted to obtain 90 of the above specified maximm density
Fills placed on slopes having grades steeper than 5 1 H V should
be properly keyed and benched into firm existing ground

soil less than 6 inches in size may be re used as fill material
Imported fill material should be low to non expansive soils less
than 6 inches in size and submitted to the project soils engineer

Foundations
For the proposed structure pile foundations bearing into the dense
basalt conglomerate at about 75 fcot depth are recommended
Sixteen and one half 16 5 inch octagonal prestressed concrete
piles installed into the dense conglomerate may be designed to
carry an allowable load of 100 tons each Negative skin friction
is estimated to be about 60 tons per pile therefore the net
allowable capacity per pile is 40 tons
The following guidelines are recommended for the design and
construction of the pile foundation

Earth Pressure Loads on Pipe
Toads on buried conduits are influenced by the width of the trench
the size of the pipe the unit weight of backfill material and the
frictional resistance between the backfill material and the trench
wall To compute the vertical loads on the pipes a unit weight of
115 p c f and a coefficient of friction of 0 25 is recommended
Earth forces upcn the pipe increase rapidly with the width of the
trench Therefore the width of the trench Bd should be kept to
amirmum The enclosed Plate 5 indicates three alternative types
of trench
Bearing Capacity and Settlement
The stiff older fill and alluvial soils encountered along the
alignment have good bearing capacity therefore minimal
settlements are anticipated
GEOLABS HAWAIIi
W O 1390 10 Page 15
The sewer line construction in general will not materially
increase the vertical loading upon these underlying soils if no
additional fills are placed upon the existing ground surface The
resulting net loading between the weight of the excavated material
and backfill will be essentially small therefore significant
settlements are not anticipated if proper construction techniques
are utilized
The stress distribution against the bottom of a pipe has a great
effect upon the load supporting capacity of the pipe and
therefore it is an important design consideration
A minimum of six 6 inches of compacted granular bedding material
should be placed below the pipe The granular bedding should
extend beyond the pipe sides a minimm width of one fourth the
outside pipe diameter and extend half way up the pipe sides The
bedding material should consist cf well graded granular material
such as 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 Refer to Plate 6 for
details

Backfill
In general the trench backfill should be in accordance with the
Standard Specifications for Public Works Construction May 1975
Granular material such as sand 3B Fine gravel or crusher waste
should be used for the backfill up to about one foot above the pipe
to reduce the compactive effort required and possible damage to the
pipe
Except for the organic clays silt and boulders most of the
excavated on site soils less than 6 inches in diameter can be
re used as the upper portion of the trench backfill from above one
foot over the pipe to within 2 feet of the finished grade
Backfill should be mechanically compacted to a minimum of 90 of
its maximum dry density established by ASTM D 1557 78 Below the
roadway areas the backfill within 2 feet of the finished grade
should consist of select borrow and be compacted to 95 of its
maximum dry density
