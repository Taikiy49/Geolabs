INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed three 3 story apartment building at 2023 Citron Street in the
Moiliili District of Oahu Hawaii The general location of the project site and its vicinity is
shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings of our exploration and presents our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from analyses of the data obtained
during the field exploration These recommendations are intended for design input and
GEOLABS HAWAII

may not be appropriate to form the basis for construction cost estimates or for
construction bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a three story 3 apartment building at the project site
It is our understanding that the new building will consist of ground floor level parking with
concrete pavement It is also anticipated that concrete columns with CMU walls and
wood floor construction will be utilized for the proposed building
Loading information was not available to us at the time of preparation of this report
Based on the information provided to us the estimated column and wall loads will be
approximately fifty 50 kips and three 3 kips per foot respectively

formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to site grading
pavement and foundation design of the project

SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed project site is located at 2023 Citron Street in the Moiliili District of
Oahu Hawaii Presently the site is a vacant lot with a short asphalt driveway down to
the level ground at approximately two 2 feet below the street level
The project site measures approximately one hundred and twenty 120 by fifty 50
feet in overall plan dimension It is separated from other residential units to the west by
a four and one half 4 5 feet high CMU wall with chain link fence on top The lot is also
bounded by a three and one half 3 5 feet high CMU wall at the rear end and a chain link
fence to the east
The project site is generally level with thin vegetation scattered across the site A
three 3 foot wide stairway was also observed at the front end of the lot going down to
the level ground from the sidewalk

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling four 4 borings to
depths ranging between fifteen and one half 15 5 to sixteen 16 feet below the existing
ground surface The approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan
Piate 2
In general our exploration encountered a surficial layer of fill material approximately
two 2 to three 3 feet thick consisting of silty sand with coral gravel This was
underlain by a soft compressible layer of clayey sand and clayey silt with some organic
material down to a depth of four 4 to eight 8 feet below the existing ground surface
Hard coral formation was then encountered extending to a depth of sixteen 16 feet the
maximum depth drilled
Groundwater was encountered in the drilled borings at approximate depths of 3 6
to 3 9 feet below the existing ground surface during the field exploration
Detailed descriptions of the materials encountered are presented on the Boring
Logs Plates A 1 through A 4 Laboratory testing was performed on selected samples
obtained The test results are presented in Appendix B
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Our field exploration generally indicated that a two 2 to six 6 foot thick soft
compressibie layer of clayey sand and clayey silt with some organic material exists
between the upper two 2 feet of surficial fill and the underlying coral formation
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2831 00 Page 5
Based on the exploration results and the anticipated maximum column loads of fifty
50 kips we believe that the thin upper fill layer and the underlying compressible deposit
cannot provide adequate foundation support for the proposed building and unacceptable
settlement may occur under the new building loads In order to reduce the anticipated
excessive settlement and the possibility of ground bearing failure we recommend that the
soft clayey silt deposit at the footing foundation locations be over excavated and replaced
with select compacted structural fill
Alternatively the footings could be placed directly on the hard coral formation
However this method would require more extensive dewatering to allow the concrete to
be placed in a dry condition
Foundations
It is Our Opinion that shallow spread or continuous footings may be used to
support the proposed building An allowable bearing capacity of up to 2 000 p s f may
be used for the design of spread footings bearing on a base of compacted structural fill
constructed as described below The bearing value is for total dead plus live loads and
may be increased by one third when temporary wind and seismic loads are considered
In order to reduce the potential for excessive settlement and the possibility of
ground bearing failure we recommend that the footing excavations be extended down
to a depth of approximately five 5 feet below the existing ground surface The depth of
over excavation may be reduced if hard coral formation is encountered before the
indicated depth The over excavation should also extend a minimum of eighteen 18
inches beyond the edge of the footings A schematic section of the footing excavation
and backfill details is shown on Plate 3
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2831 00 Page 6
The excavation should first be lined with a layer of geotextile fabric and then
backfilled with No 3B Fine gravel or ASTM C 33 No 67 to at least twelve 12 inches
above the groundwater level Then non expansive borrow material such as select
borrow crusher waste or coral should be placed in level horizontal lifts not exceeding
eight 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of its
maximum dry density as determined in accordance with procedures described in ASTM
Test Method D 1557 78 prior to footing construction The top foot below the finish
subgrade level should be backfilled with impervious non expansive soil compacted as
indicated above
Individual footings and continuous wall footings should be embedded a minimum
of twelve 12 inches below the lowest adjacent finish grade
If foundations are located next to utility trenches or easements they should be
embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane extending
upward from the bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility
lines
Footing settlements under the anticipated design loadings are estimated to be on
the order of less than one 1 inch total and one half 1 of an inch differential
All footings should be tied together with grade beams to improve the rigidity of the
foundation thus reducing the anticipated differential settlement
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive earth pressure
developed against the buried vertical faces of the foundation system and by frictional
resistance developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be calculated using an equivalent fluid
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2831 00 Page 7
pressure of 300 p s f per foot of depth This assumes that the soil around the foundation
is well compacted A coefficient of friction of 0 4 may be used Unless covered by
pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the upper 18 inches of soil should be
neglected
Site Grading
The weight of new fills will initiate long term ground settlement due to compression
of the soft subsoils Therefore any site filling that may be required should be limited to
a maximum height of less than one 1 foot above the existing ground surface
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable
materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site
We recommend that the areas below pavements or embankments should be
scarified to a minimum depth of six 6 inches moisture conditioned and recompacted to
90 of its maximum dry density as determined in accordance with procedures described
in ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 Any loose or soft spots encountered should be
removed and replaced with compacted non expansive select material
Imported fill material to replace the over excavated material should consist of non
expansive select granular material less than three 3 inches in maximum size with less
than 20 percent passing the 200 sieve Any imported fill material should be tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to delivery to the project site
The site grading should be designed to divert surface water away from the building
foundations

The slab thickened edges adjacent to any unpaved area should be embedded at
least twelve 12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade
Provisions should be made for proper load transfer across the slab joints which are
subject to vehicular traffic
The above recommended pavement section is based on the assumptions that the
anticipated traffic will be light and that adequate drainage will be provided Laboratory
CBR and density tests should be performed on the exposed subgrade during
construction to verify the subgrade strength and the above pavement section may be
revised if necessary
Utility Trench
New utility lines may be required for the project A granular bedding consisting of
six 6 inches of select borrow or ASTM C 33 No 67 is recommended under the pipes
Granular materials such as No 3B Fine gravel should be used for the trench backfill
up to about one 1 foot above the pipes to reduce the possibility of damaging the pipes
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2831 00 Page 9
The upper portion of the subsequent trench backfill from the level one 1 foot
above the pipes to the top of the subgrade or finished grade should consist of select
granular material and placed in maximum ten 10 inch horizontal loose lifts mechanically
compacted to a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density as determined in
accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 to reduce future ground subsidence
Drainage
The finished grade outside the building should be shaped to shed water away from
foundations and to avoid ponding conditions Also it is advised that gutter systems be
installed around the building and that the discharge be diverted away from the foundation
and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near the foundations and slabs
should also be avoided
Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have concrete bottoms and
drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the above
foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause excessive subsurface soil
saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations
should be properly backfilled tight against the walls or slab footings immediately after
setting of the concrete to prevent ponding conditions
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and maintained to drain
all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundations

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during the site grading and construction of the
foundations This is to observe compliance with design concepts specifications or
recommendations and to expedite suggestions for design changes that may be required
in the event that subsurface conditions differ from those anticipated at the time of
preparation of this report The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon
such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and approved by
the geotechnical engineer prior to delivery to the site
If actual exposed soil conditions encountered during construction are different from
those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to the design
should be made

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling of four
4 borings to depths ranging between 15 5 and 16 0 feet below the existing ground
surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were
drilled using truck mounted equipment
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Method D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Spilt Barrel Sampling of Soils by
driving a standard penetration sampler or 3 O D Modified California sampler with a 140
pound hammer free falling 30 inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the last 12
inches are shown on the Boring Logs at the appropriate sample depths
In soft cohesive soils samples were obtained using a thin walled tube sampler
Shelby Tube The sampler was hydraulically pushed The gauge pressure required to
advance the sampler is shown on the Boring Logs at the appropriate depths
The material encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who continuously monitored the drilling
operations These classifications were further reviewed by visual inspection and testing
in the laboratory All soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A graphic presentation of the materials encountered are presented
on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 4
