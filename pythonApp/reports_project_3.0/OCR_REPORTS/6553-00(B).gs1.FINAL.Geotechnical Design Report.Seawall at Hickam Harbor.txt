PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is at Hickam Harbor within Hickam Air Force Base in the Pearl
Harbor area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project site is south of Worchester
Avenue and adjacent to the Sea Breeze Restaurant and Sand Bar Grill as shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2 The project consists of an engineering study to evaluate the integrity of
the existing seawall and adjacent walkways from the existing boat pier to the recreational
beach area and provide concepts for possible repair of these structures

Based on the available drawings the existing seawall consists of stacked concrete
blocks about 4 feet wide by 2 33 feet high by 4 feet long The blocks were made of
concrete with minimum compressive strength of 3 000 pounds per square inch The top of
the blocks was capped with a 6 inch thick concrete slab about 5 33 feet wide In 1971 a
low CMU wall was constructed behind the stacked concrete blocks The east side of the
stacked concrete block seawall was extended to the recreational beach area using
20 inches wide by 24 inches high stacked concrete beams with lengths of 15 18 and
19 feet to construct the wall extension In 1993 some of the concrete beams had to be
reset and deadman anchors were installed in some of the beams due to wall damage
incurred from Hurricane Iniki
The existing ground elevation behind the seawall ranged from about 4 5 to
6 5 feet Mean Sea Level MSL In front of the seawall the ground elevation ranged from
about 1 1 to 3 feet MSL
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by drilling and sampling
four borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 4 to depths of about 24 to 29 feet
below the existing ground surface The approximate boring locations are shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2
Our field exploration generally encountered a surface fill layer about 3 5 to 5 5 feet
thick The surface fill layer was underlain by coralline detritus and shallow marine
deposits lagoonal deposits beach sand and coral formation extending to about 29 feet
below the existing ground surface the maximum depth explored The estimated
subsurface conditions along the existing seawall are presented on the Generalized
Geologic Cross Section A A Plate 3
The surface fill layer consisted of loose to medium dense silty sand and sandy
gravel and stiff clayey silt with sand The coralline detritus and shallow marine deposits
were comprised of very loose to medium dense silty sands and gravels The lagoonal
deposits consisted of very soft silty clay with sand The coral formation ranged from soft to
medium hard
We encountered groundwater in the drilled borings at depths of about 3 8 to
4 8 feet below the existing ground surface at the time of our field exploration The
groundwater depths correspond to elevations from about 1 2 to 2 feet MSL It should
be noted that groundwater levels at the project site may fluctuate depending on the tide
seasonal precipitation and other factors Detailed descriptions of the materials
encountered in the borings and the results of the laboratory tests performed on selected
soil samples are provided in our report entitled Geotechnical Data Report Seawall at

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the results of our exploration the project site is generally underlain by a
loose to medium dense surface fill layer underlain by very loose to medium dense coralline
detritus and shallow marine deposits very soft lagoonal deposits loose beach sand and
soft to medium hard coral formation We encountered groundwater in the drilled borings at
depths of 3 8 to 4 8 feet at the time of our field exploration
Based on the subsurface conditions and estimated bottom of footing elevation
geotechnical parameters for the evaluation of the existing seawall were separated into
two sections Sections A and B Detailed discussion of these items and our geotechnical
engineering recommendations are presented in the following sections
Existing Seawall Observations
Most of the stacked concrete blocks on the western side of the seawall were
aligned fairly straight However numerous misaligned stacked concrete beams were
observed on the eastern side of the seawall The misaligned beams were tilting towards
the ocean The ocean side edge of the 6 inch thick concrete slab above the stacked
blocks was worn down In some areas the concrete slab edge has been removed due to
erosion
At the western corner near the existing pier depressions on the ground surface
were noted behind the seawall It appears that the depressions may have been caused by
loss of backfill material through the seawall
An existing concrete sidewalk was noted behind the seawall on the eastern side of
the seawall structure

In general an active condition may be used for gravity retaining walls or walls that
are free to deflect by as much as 0 5 percent of the wall height If the tops of the
walls are not free to deflect beyond this degree or are restrained the walls should
be evaluated for the at rest condition
Surcharge stresses due to areal surcharges line loads and point loads within a
horizontal distance equal to the depth of the wall should be considered in the wall
evaluation For uniform surcharge stresses imposed on the loaded side of the wall
a rectangular distribution with uniform pressure equal to 36 percent of the vertical
surcharge pressure acting on the entire height of the wall which is free to deflect
cantilever may be used in design For walls that are restrained a rectangular
distribution equal to 53 percent of the vertical surcharge pressure acting over the
entire height of the wall may be used for design Additional analyses during the
wall evaluation may be needed to evaluate the surcharge effects of point loads and
line loads
Dynamic Lateral Earth Forces
For seismic loading conditions a force of 7 8H2 pounds per lineal foot H in feet
due to dynamic lateral earth pressures based on amax of 0 18g may be used in the
evaluation of the existing seawall with level backfill provided the walls are free to
translate or rotate by about 2 inches laterally The dynamic lateral earth forces
presented above are in addition to the static lateral earth pressures The resultant
force should be assumed to act through the mid height of the wall An appropriate
reduced factor of safety may be used when dynamic lateral earth forces are
accounted for in the evaluation of the seawall
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from the field boring Variations of conditions beyond the field boring
may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident until
construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to
reevaluate the recommendations presented in this report
The field boring locations were estimated by taping from reference points and
visible features shown on the Topographic Survey Map transmitted by The AES Design
Group Inc on The field boring locations should be considered accurate
only to the degree implied by the methods used
The stratification breaks shown on graphic representations of the borings depict the
approximate boundaries between soil rock types and as such may denote a gradual
transition Water level data from the borings was measured at the times shown on the
graphic representations and or presented in the text of this report These data have been
reviewed and interpretations made to formulate this report However it must be noted that
fluctuation may occur due to variation in the tide seasonal rainfall and other factors
