Regional Geology
The Island of Hawaii the largest island in the Hawaiian Archipelago covers an area
of approximately 4 000 square miles The island was formed by the activity of five shield
volcanoes which include the following Kohala which is long extinct Mauna Kea which
has had activity during recent geologic time Hualalai which last erupted in 1801 and
Mauna Loa and Kilauea both of which are still active
The project site is situated on the northwestern flank of Hualalai Mountain which
makes up the west central portion of the island The lava formation encountered at the
project site appears to consist of a a type lava flows at the ground surface which spread
and ponded as they approached the ocean Pahoehoe lavas may be encountered at
greater depths below the ground surface A a lava typically is characterized by a porous
rough and irregular flow surface resembling a jagged accumulation of rock fragments

Cavities are encountered commonly in basaltic lavas especially pahoehoe type
lava These cavities are formed when the lava is still in a molten state and they represent
both lava tubes intra flow cavities and blisters and pockets inter flow cavities Lava
tubes are formed when molten lava drains from the cooling flow leaving a hollow tube like
structure which may extend for a large distance longitudinally along the flow Blisters and
pockets generally are smaller in horizontal extent
Basalt is considered to be a relatively permeable rock and can transmit water quite
readily in both the horizontal and vertical directions In general water is transmitted
through the porous rock matrix along joints fractures and inter flow contacts cavities and
along clinker layers The permeability of the clinker and cavities is high therefore they
serve as the primary water transmission features

Beach Resort in the District of South Kohala on the Island of Hawaii The project site is

Site Preparation
As mentioned previously the project site generally is underlain by loose a a basalt
lava flows at the ground surface In addition lava tubes and cavities may be present at
greater depths below the ground surface Therefore we believe that special attention
should be given to the site preparation aspect of the proposed construction
The near surface materials are relatively porous and will compress when subjected
to structural and vehicular loads Therefore the fill areas of the project site should be
ripped to a minimum depth of 3 feet below the existing ground surface to assist in
detecting and collapsing cavities and or voids contained in the porous near surface
materials In addition site preparation consisting of ripping the existing ground surface
should extend at least 10 feet laterally beyond the cut fill transition line Proof rolling of the
subgrades also will be necessary to assist in detecting and possibly collapsing lava tubes
and cavities The proof rolling process may be conducted using a combination of
Caterpillar D 10 bulldozers and large vibratory drum rollers minimum 20 tons static
weight In addition the proof rolling operations should be observed by a representative

Lava Tubes and Cavities
Based on our experience in the area lava tubes and cavities commonly are
encountered in the pahoehoe basalt formations in and around the project site area Lava
tubes and cavities may vary significantly in size from small blisters to large lava tubes of
20 feet in diameter or more In general the majority of small cavities are limited in lateral
extent However it should be noted that the potential for long lava tubes that may traverse
across the entire project site exists The general orientation of the long lava tubes is
generally in a mauka makai direction
Therefore special attention should be given to the site preparation requirements
prior to site filling and construction of improvements on the cuts and fills Based on our
experience on the adjacent sites we recommend that the subgrades in fill areas be ripped
and proof rolled prior to fill placement during the mass grading operations In addition the
finished cut surfaces should be proof rolled with a large vibratory drum roller in an effort to
detect potential near surface lava tubes and cavities In general lava tubes cavities and
blisters discovered during the site preparation operations and encountered during
construction should be collapsed and backfilled with appropriate fill materials
Foundations
We envision that the buildings for the proposed multi family dwellings generally will
consist of three story structures Based on the subsurface conditions anticipated at the
project site we believe that the proposed structures may be supported on a shallow
foundation system consisting of spread and or continuous footings As mentioned
previously lava tubes and cavities may be present in the basaltic lava flows at the project
site To reduce the potential for loss of foundation support resulting from collapse of the
cavities directly below building foundations a program of cavity probing and grouting of the
foundation locations for buildings located over cut areas and fill areas with less than 5 feet
of fills may be necessary during construction
