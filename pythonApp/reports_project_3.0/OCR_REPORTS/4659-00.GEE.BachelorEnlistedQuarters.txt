performed for the Bachelor Enlisted Quarters project located along Mokapu Road and
separated by Craig Avenue into two parcels in the District of Kaneohe on the Island of
Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering
recommendations resulting from field exploration laboratory testing and engineering
analyses These recommendations are intended for the design of foundations site
preparation and pavements only The findings and recommendations presented herein
are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report

SECTION 4 0 REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The sland of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from two shield
volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be middle
to late Pliocene in age and Koolau is estimated to be late Pliocene to early Pleistocene in
age After a long period of volcanic inactivity during which erosion cut valleys deep into
the Koolau shield volcanic activity returned with a series of lava flows followed by cinder
and tuff cone formations These series are referred to as the Honolulu Volcanic Series
The site of the Marine Corps Base Hawaii MCBH at Kaneohe Bay is located on
the Mokapu Peninsula which was built by three volcanic vents of the Honolulu Volcanic
Series These vents are believed to be among the oldest of the series The pyroclastic
deposits and lava flows from vents formed salients permitting the growth of a coral reef
that merged with the main body of the Island of Oahu
During the Pleistocene Epoch Ice Age the sea level fell below the present sea
level in response to continental glaciation Subsequently sands from emerged coral
reefs were blown inland by the prevailing winds to form sand dunes along the northern
portion of the airfield area An extensive deposit of unconsolidated calcareous marine
sediments primarily consisting of beach sand made up of water worn coral shells and
organisms covers the western tip of Mokapu Peninsula Man made fills consisting of
marine sediments dredged from the ocean floor were used to build up the area around
the southern and western shorelines of Mokapu Peninsula

7 1 Building Foundations
Based on the subsoil conditions encountered at the project site we believe that
shallow spread and or continuous footings may be used to support the proposed
structures An allowable bearing pressure of up to 5 000 pounds
per square foot psf may be used for the design of shallow foundations bearing on
compacted on site soils and or compacted structural fill material needed to raise grade
transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic forces
lf soft and or loose materials are encountered at the bottom of the footing
over excavations the material should be over excavated to expose the underlying firm
materials The resulting over excavations should be backfilled with select granular
materials compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction

developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil and by passive
earth pressure acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation sysiem A

bearing material Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be estimated using an

7 5 Site Preparation
Based on the existing topography and the anticipated design finish floor
elevations we envision that the site grading work for the proposed project will generally
consist of 0 5 meters of fills
At the on set of earthwork areas within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed Vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable
materials should be removed and disposed of off site Soft and yielding areas
encountered during clearing and grubbing below areas designated to receive fill or
future improvements should be over excavated to expose firm material and the
resulting excavation should be backfilled with well compacted engineered fill The
excavated soft and or organic soils should be properly disposed of off site Contract
documents should include additive and deductive unit prices for over excavation and
engineered fill placement to account for variations in the over excavation quantities

After clearing and grubbing the over excavated subgrades should be scarified to a
depth of about 203 millimeters 8 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent
above the optimum moisture content and recompacted to a minimum of 90 percent
relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil
expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in
accordance with ASTM D 1557 91 test procedures Optimum moisture is the water
content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
Where shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of the subgrade we
recommend that the subgrade soil be prepared again as recommended above
Saturation and subsequent yielding of the exposed subgrade due to inclement weather
and poor drainage may require over excavation of the soft areas and replacement with
well compacted fill The need for over excavation due to soft subgrade soil conditions
should be evaluated by a representative from Geolabs in the field
If the subgrade soils are pumping during subgrade preparation the pumping
subgrade may be stabilized by cement treatment As a guide one sack of cement may
be used for approximately 2 32 square meters 25 square feet of subgrade area
Alternatively the subgrade may be proof rolled and a woven geotextile fabric such as
Mirafi 500X or equivalent should be placed on the subgrade prior to placement of
subsequent lifts of fill
The on site soils generated from the cut areas may be reused as a source of
general fill material for non structural areas provided that they are free of vegetation
deleterious materials and rock fragments greater than 152 millimeters 6 inches in
maximum dimension
Compaction should be accomplished by sheepsfoot rollers vibratory rollers or
other types of acceptable compaction equipment Water tamping jetting or ponding
should not be allowed to compact the on site soils

7 6 Utility Trench
In general for support of the utility lines we recommend that a granular bedding
consisting of 152 millimeters 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67
gradation be used under the pipes Free draining granular materials such as
No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation should also be used for the trench
backfill up to about 305 millimeters 12 inches above the pipes to provide adequate
support around the pipes and to reduce the compaction effort of the backfill thus
reducing the possibility of damaging the pipes It is critical to use this free draining
material to reduce the potential for formation of voids below the haunches of pipes and to
provide adequate support for the sides of the pipes Improper trench backfill could result
in settlement of the backfill and damage to the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 305 millimeters 12 inches
above the pipes to the top of the subgrade or finished grade may consist of select fill
material The backfill should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture

7 7 Drainage
The finished grades outside the building should be sloped to shed water away
from foundations and reduce the potential for ponding lt is also advised that gutter
systems be installed around the building and that the discharge be diverted away from the
foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near the foundations and
slabs should also be avoided Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have
concrete bottoms and drains to reduce the potential for water infiltration into the subsoils
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and maintained to drain surface
run off away from the slabs and foundations
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil
saturation and subsequent loss of strength and or heaving of the near surface clayey
soils The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled against the footings
immediately after setting of the concrete to reduce the potential for water infiltration

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling
two borings to depths of about 6 55 meters 21 5 feet below the existing ground surface
The approximate locations of the drilled borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The borings were drilled using a truck mounted drill rig equipped with continuous flight
augers and coring tools
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by testing
in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representations of the materials
encountered are provided on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 and A 2
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 3550 84 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a
hammer falling 760 millimeters 30 inches In addition some samples were obtained
from the drilled borings in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1586 84
Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a inch OD
standard penetration sampler using the same hammer and drop The blow counts
needed to drive the sampler the second and third 150 millimeters 6 inches of a
inch drive are shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of
Borings at the appropriate sample depths

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content ASTM D 2216 and unit weight ASTM D 2937 determinations
were performed on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of
soil properties The results of these tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the
appropriate sample depths
One Atterberg Limits test ASTM D 4318 was performed on a selected soil
sample to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits The test results are summarized on the
Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depth Graphic presentation of the test
results is provided on Plate B 1
Three one inch ring swell tests were performed on selected soil
samples to evaluate the swelling potential of the near surface soils The results of the
tests are summarized on Plate B 2
One Modified Proctor compaction test ASTM D 1557 was performed on a bulk
sample of the near surface soils to evaluate the dry density and moisture content
relationships The test result is presented on Plate B 3
Two laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR tests ASTM D 1883 were
performed on bulk samples of the near surface soils to evaluate the pavement support
characteristics of the soils The results of the tests are presented on Plates B 4 and
B 5
