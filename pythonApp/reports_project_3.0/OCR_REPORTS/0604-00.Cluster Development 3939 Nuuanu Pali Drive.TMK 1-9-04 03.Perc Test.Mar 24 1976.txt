This report presents the results of our percolation testing
for the proposed cluster development on Nuuanu Pali Drive
Honolulu Hawaii
The purpose of this investigation is to obtain subsurface
soil conditions and on site in situ seepage characteristics
for the design of sewage disposal systems
Subsurface Conditions
A 4 inch diameter boring was drilled at the middle portion of
the site down to a depth of 30 feet below the existing ground
surface The location of the boring is shown on Site Plan
Piaver 1
The boring was drilled with a truck mounted rotary rig with a
tri cone bit The drilling was monitored by our field engineer
who also logged the boring A copy of the log is presented on
Plate 2
The subsurface explorations disclosed that the area is blanketed
by a 6 foot layer of clayey silt underlain by a 3 foot thick layer
of weathered basalt boulders with clayey silt matrix Below the
boulder layer interbedding layers of basalt rock and clayey
silt extended to the limits of the exploration
Page 2
W O 604 00
Percolation Tests
Falling head percolation tests were performed in the drilled
hole at different drilled lengths Water levels were measured
at various time intervals from the ground surface Plate 3
summarizes the results of the percolation tests
Based on the test results from the above bore hole it is our
opinion that the subsurface soils and basalt layer will provide
adequate percolation rates for the proposed private cesspool
sewage disposal system For cesspool design a percolation
rate of 0 3 gallon per square foot per hour may be used
For a single family residence a 6 foot diameter cesspool with
a depth of 20 feet below the inlet pipe would be adequate The
space between the block lining and the undisturbed soil sidewail
of 8 foot minimum diameter excavation should be backfilled with
free draining granular materials
Should you have any questions concerning our findings or recommen
dations please feel free to contact us
