From March 22 to we have inspected the earthwork
and grading operations at the above referenced site This report
summarizes our inspection and compaction tests performed at the
project site
It is our opinion that as of this date the above referenced lots
at the Ahuimanu Hills Phase III Subdivision are constructed to final
grade and the new fills compacted adequately The density test
results for the in place fills are indicated in the attached Density
Test Summary Two site plans indicating the location of the tests are
also attached

The grading operations consisted of cuts up to about 10 feet and
fills up to about 17 feet Prior to fill placement the existing
vegetation was removed and the exposed ground was scarified and
recompacted The fill was then placed in horizontal lifts and
compacted to a minimum of 85 of its maximum dry density per ASTM
D 1557 78 test designation
After clearing and grubbing field observations and shallow test
pits were made in various areas in attempt to locate possible
uncompacted stockpile left from previous grading operations
Substantial amounts of loose soil were not found
On sloping areas the fill was keyed and benched into the natural
ground during filling and compacting
During the fill placement operations periodic field density tests
were made in accordance with American Society for Testing and
Materials ASTM test designation D 1556 Sand Cone method Based on
the compaction test results and field observations it is our opinion
that the fill lots have been compacted adequately
During the grading period samples of the on site soils were
tested prior to being used in the fill The maximum soil density and
optimum moisture content was established in our laboratory in
accordance with the ASTM D 1557 78 test designation and the test
results are as follows

RECOMMENDAT IONS
House Foundations
General house foundation recommendations for preliminary design
purposes are as follows
1 Footings located on or near slopes should penetrate down to
firm soil in order to develop a minimum horizontal set back
distance of 8 feet measured from the outer edge of the footing
foundation out to the slope face
Individual footings on level areas should be embedded a
minimum of 12 inches below the ground surface to minimize the
effect of soil shrinkage due to soil moisture loss under the
roof area All footing backfill should be properly compacted
to obtain 85 compaction and graded to drain water away from
the footing

We recommend that the soils engineer should inspect the bottom
of all footing excavations prior to concrete placement
2 An allowable bearing value of 2000 p s f may be used for
foundations bearing on compacted fill or stiff natural soil
3 Slab on grade construction should be set back at least 6 feet
from the tops of slopes
Twelve inch deep below the lowest adjacent grade perimeter
footings are recommended to act as a cut off wall against the
future subgrade moisture changes due to drying and wetting
around the slab
The individual house foundation plans should be reviewed by a soil
engineer on a lot by lot basis to see if the above general
recommendations need to be revised for specific house and lot
conditions
It is imperative that all the lots be graded to drain water away
from the house foundations to prevent saturation and softening of the
subgrade soils

Site Regrading
Subsequent to completion of lot grading utility trenches within
the lot pad should be properly backfilled and compacted under the
observation of a soils technician
This office assumes no responsibility for any alterations made to
slopes or pads on the subject lots subsequent to the issuance of this
report without our knowledge and written approval
We strongly suggest that all of the above referenced
recommendations and restrictions be made available to all future lot
and home purchasers of this subdivision Home owners should also be
advised not to block or alter surface drainage Professional
consultation should be obtained in the planning design and
construction of any future improvements
