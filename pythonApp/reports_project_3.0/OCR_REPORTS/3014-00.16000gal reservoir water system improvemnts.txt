As requested we have performed a study at the site for the proposed
16 000 gallon water tank for the Molokai Water System Phase 3 A The
study consisted of field reconnaissance one 1 hand auger boring and
five 5 hand probes with a 3 8 inch pointed rod
The site is located near the intersection of Moomomi Avenue and
Kulea Street as shown on the Project Location Map Plate l
The existing ground slopes down in a northerly direction at about a
10 gradient towards the top of a steep gully The top of the gully bank
is about 40 to 50 feet from the site The side slopes of the gully are on
about a 70 gradient It is planned to construct a 16 000 gallon 15 foot
diameter steel water tank
Only minor grading is contemplated

Subsurface Conditions
One hand auger boring was drilled and five probes were done at the
tank site to depths of 2 to 9 feet The approximate locations of the
boring and probes are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The boring and probes encountered about 2 feet of soft or loose
residual soil and saprolite over very stiff highly weathered rock near the
bottom of the auger hole
Groundwater was not encountered in the boring during the field
exploration
Discussion and Recommendations
Tank Foundation
The field exploration disclosed that the upper 2 feet of soils at
the site is relatively loose or soft Therefore the tank foundations
should extend below the upper soft layer Alternatively the loose soils
may be over excavated and recompacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum
dry density established by ASTM D 1557 78
General guidelines for tank foundation design are as follows
1 The tank foundation should be embedded a minimum of 24 inches
below the existing ground to rest on the stiff in situ soils or
compacted fill

2 An allowable bearing value of 3000 p s f on the stiff natural
soil or compacted fill is recommended for the anticipated tank
loads
3 The bottom of the foundation excavation should be cleared of
loose material and bear on firm soil The excavations should be
observed by a soils engineer to see that adequate bearing soil
has been obtained
4 The ground surface should be shaped to drain water away from the
foundations
Design Review
Plans and specifications for site grading and foundations should be
forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written comments prior to
construction to see that the intent of our recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the footing excavations be observed by the
soils engineer during construction to see that adequate bearing soil have
been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the
findings of our investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
If conditions encountered during construction are different from
those presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above inspection

Investigation Limitations
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations
made in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation engineering
principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all other
warranties either expressed or implied
The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are based
on our site reconnaissance soil information from boring and laboratory
test data obtained from this soil investigation
Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encountered and cannot be
fully determined by taking soil samples Unforeseen conditions such as
expansive soil and soft spots may occur in localized areas and may require
additional probing or corrections in the field Therefore additional
expenditures may be needed during construction to attain a properly
constructed project Some contingency fund is thus recommended to
accommodate these possible costs
