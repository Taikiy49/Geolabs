This report presents the results of our foundation investiga
tion for the above referenced project Our work was done in
general accordance with the scope of services outlined in our
proposal dated
The purpose of our study was to investigate the subsurface soil
conditions by drilling test borings Data obtained from these
borings and laboratory testing of soil samples were utilized to
provide recommendations for the design of the foundations for
the proposed classroom buildings and retaining wall

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The proposed classroom building project will consist of the
construction of two classroom structures each about 32 by 60
feet in plan dimension adjacent to the existing Art Music
Building
It is anticipated that the buildings will be of concrete
masonry unit construction Structural column loads are anti
cipated to be light ranging from 30 to 85 kips each
A concrete basement retaining wall approximately 10 feet high
will be required for the mauka end building The wall load
will be up to about 10 kips per lineal foot
Minimal site grading is anticipated except for the mauka end
building where a 10 foot deep cut is required for the basement
area
SITE DESCRIPTION
The site is located in the rear western portion of Hanahauoli
School located mauka of the corner of Makiki and Nehoa Streets
in the Makiki area of metropolitan Honolulu The general
vicinity of the site is shown on the Project Location Plan
Plate l

The site is currently occupied by an existing dwelling class
room building and storage building for the school campus
These buildings are surrounded by landscaped areas of trees
shrubs and lawn There is also an existing asphaltic concrete
driveway through and along the northern edge of the site The
plans provided to us also indicate an existing cesspool on the
mauka side of the existing dwelling
The ground surface is terraced to provide level areas for the
existing building The ground surface elevation varies between
130 feet and 142 feet between the western and eastern portions
of the site
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
The site is located on the lower slopes of Round Top During
the late Pleistocene epoch the eruption of this member of the
Honolulu Volcanic Series blanketed the area of the project site
with volcanic cinder locally referred to as black sand
Subsurface Exploration
Six borings were drilled at the site to depths between 10 0 to
23 0 feet below the existing ground surface to explore the sub
surface soil conditions and to obtain samples for laboratory
testing The approximate locations of the borings are shown on
the Boring Location Plan Plate 2

Description of our field exploration and laboratory testing are
presented in the appendices along with the log of borings and
laboratory test results
Soil Conditions
Based on the borings the site is generally underlain by 2 5 to
4 5 feet of stiff brown clayey silt Most of this material is
the result of in situ weathering of volcanic cinder although
some areas of thin fill less than 2 feet thick were encoun
tered
Below this surface layer the subsoils grade to medium dense
dark brown sands These medium to coarse grained sands repre
sent sub aerial deposits of volcanic cinder black sand
which have been only slightly weathered This layer extends to
at least 23 0 feet below the existing ground surface the maxi
mum depth drilled The black sand exhibited a tendency to
cave in when the boring was advanced by augers unsupported by
casing This tendency indicates that there is little or no
cementation in this sand material
Groundwater was not encountered in any of the borings at the
time of our exploration

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
We believe that the natural stiff clayey silts and medium dense
black sands can provide adequate bearing capacity without ex
cessive settlements under the building loads
Demolition and Clearing
The existing structures including all slabs asphaltic concrete
pavements concrete blocks and foundations should be removed
from the site Shrubs and trees should be grubbed to at least
12 inches below the existing grade within the grading areas
The top 6 inches of the existing landscaped areas should be
stripped This topsoil may be stockpiled and reused in the
future landscape areas Any drywells or cesspools if encoun
tered should be cleaned thoroughly and backfilled with suit
able on site material Any portion of the backfill that is
under water should consist of the coarse black sand or crushed
stone such as No 3B fine When No 3B Fine gravel is used
the material immediately above the coarse rock should consist
of 12 inches of compacted base course aggregate to form a
filter zone and the remaining material above the filter zone
should consist of compacted select on site material More
specific recommendations are presented under SITE GRADING in a
subsequent section

Building Foundations
For the proposed structures spread or continuous footing foun
dations are recommended The footings should be embedded a
minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent grade The
bottom of the footing excavations should be compacted to 95
maximum dry density as established by ASTM D 1557 prior to
placement of reinforcing steel
An allowable bearing capacity of 3500 p s f may be used for
footings bearing on the above compacted medium density sands or
stiff clayey silts The above bearing value may be increased
by one third when considering temporary wind or seismic loads
For the above recommendations estimated foundation settlements
are on the order of one half of an inch total and one fourth
inch differential
Retaining Wall Foundations
A basement retaining wall about 10 feet high is planned for
the mauka end of the Art Science Music Math building Well
graded granular material such as on site black sand should
be used as backfill behind the wall Impervious silty material
should be used for the top 2 feet of the backfill
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1128 00 Page 7
The wall foundation should be embedded a minimum of 2 feet
below the lowest adjacent grade The bottom of the footing
excavation should be tamped prior to placement of reinforcing
steel
The topographic map provided to us indicates that a cesspool
is situated over a portion of the retaining wall This wall
footing should extend below the bottom of the cesspool or the
cesspool must be thoroughly cleaned out and backfilled with
granular material compacted to a minimum of 95 of its maximum
dry density
An allowable bearing capacity of 3500 p s f may be used for
the wall foundation bearing on medium dense sand
The wall should be designed to resist lateral earth pressure as
follows
Walls restrained at top 45 0 p c f equivalent
at rest condition fluid pressure
level backfill
Walls unrestrained at top 35 0 p c f equivalent
active condition fluid pressure
level backfill
Additional loads due to traffic or surcharge should be
included where applicable
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1128 00 Page 8
A friction factor of 0 50 may be utilized to determine the
Sliding resistance of the wall Additionally a passive soil
resistance of 300 p c f may be utilized
A subsurface drainage system should be used to prevent the
build up of hydrostatic pressure behind the walls
During excavation for the retaining wall footing caution
should be exercised by the contractor to minimize Sloughing of
the black sand material New footing excavations adjacent to
the remaining structure should be done with care to avoid dis
turbing the existing shallow footings which are also supported
on this black sand layer This black sand exhibits a ten
dency to slough or collapse when the vertical cut bank is
unsupported and may require special sheeting bracing or
chemical grout stabilization However the choice should be
left to the contractor
Slab On Grade
Slab on grade should be placed over a 4 inch cushion layer of
gravel size crushed rock material such as No 3B Fine aggre
gate to act as a capillary break and to provide uniform
Support An impervious membrane should be placed under the
slab to act as a vapor barrier
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1128 00 Page 9
The subgrade soils should be kept moist and undisturbed If
deemed necessary it should be water tamped prior to placement
of the gravel cushion
Site Grading
Although site grading is anticipated to be minimal some cut
and fill may be needed for the construction of building pads
In addition a retaining wall excavation of about 10 feet and
related backfill work are anticipated
On site materials may be used as fill however they should
first be tested and approved by a soils engineer before they
are utilized The off site borrow fill materials if required
should be non expansive and approved by the soils engineer
All fill should be placed in lifts not exceeding 10 inches and
compacted to a dry density of at least 90 of the maximum dry
density
Pavement
Asphaltic concrete pavements may be used for restoration of the
driveway and parking We recommend that the A C pavement
consist of the following

Design Review
Plans for site grading and building foundations should be
forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written
comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the footing and retaining wall exca
vations overexcavation and backfill be observed by the
soils engineer during construction This is to see that
adequate compaction has been obtained and the subsurface
conditions are consistent with the findings of our investi
gation The recommendations given in this report are
contingent upon the above inspections

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by
drilling 0 to 23 0 feet below the existing ground
surface at the approximate locations shown on the Plot Plan
Plate 1 Truck mounted drilling equipment was used to drill
the borings
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by
visual and textural examination in the field by our engineer or
geologist who continuously monitored the drilling operations
The classifications were further reviewed by visual inspection
and testing in the laboratory The soils were classified in
general conformance with the Unified Soil Classification
System A graphic presentation of the soils encountered is
presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 and A 6
Soil samples were obtained from the boring by driving
barrel sampler with a 140 pound hammer free falling at a height
of 50 inches A 2 0 inch 0 D conical drive point penetrometer
driven by a 140 pound hammer free falling 30 inches was also
used The blow counts to drive the sampler the last 12 inches
are shown on the logs of borings at the appropriate sample
depths

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content and unit weight determinations were
performed on selected samples as an aid in the classification
and evaluation of soil properties The results of these tests
are presented on the logs of borings Plates A 1l thru A 6 at
the appropriate sample depth
Two sieve analysis tests were performed on the near
surface sample to evaluate its grain size distribution The
test results are presented on Plate B 1l
Two direct shear tests were performed on two undisturbed
samples to evaluate the strength characteristics of the in situ
soils The test results are plotted on Plate B 2
