INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed relief sewer along Awamoi and Waipahu Streets in the
Waipahu area on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the
project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings from our field exploration and laboratory
testing and presents our geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3924 00 Page 2
analyses for the proposed project These recommendations are intended for design input
only and are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed project site is located along Awamoi and Waipahu Streets in the
Waipahu area on the island of Oahu Hawaii It is proposed to construct approximately
730 lineal feet L F of new 8 inch and 10 inch sewer lines along Awamoi and Waipahu
Streets Approximately 339 L F of the sewer line will have a concrete jacket due to
minimum cover requirements It is our understanding that portions of Waipahu Street will
be filled up to about 2 5 feet above existing grade to improve road grades as part of the
proposed project
It was reported that the existing sewer line may have experienced significant
settlement resulting in one of the manholes being about 3 feet lower than originally
designed Therefore there is a concern for the potential of additional settlements due to
the anticipated 2 5 feet of new fill which may adversely affect the new sewer
Our subsurface exploration concentrated on the portion of Waipahu Street where
fill is planned in particular where the existing manhole has reportedly settled Hence
our geotechnical engineering analyses and recommendations are concentrated on the
settlement characteristics of the subsoil conditions encountered and its potential effect on
the proposed sewer construction

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Our field exploration at the subject site generally encountered approximately
16 feet of fill consisting of stiff silty and clayey soils overlying about 14 feet of soft alluvial
clays Medium stiff organic silts were encountered below the soft alluvial clays to the
maximum explored depth of 41 5 feet below the existing ground surface Based on the
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3924 00 Page 5
relatively competent subsoil conditions encountered in our boring we believe that the
proposed relief sewer project is feasible from a geotechnical engineering point of view
It was reported that the existing sewer line within the project site may have
experienced significant settlement resulting in one of the manholes being about 3 feet
lower than originally designed Our engineering analyses indicated that 3 feet of
consolidation settlement at the soft alluvial clays and organic silts may have occurred due
to the 18 feet of fill placed at the project site to achieve the existing ground surface This
is in general agreement with the reported observation
It is our understanding that about 2 5 feet of new fill is planned for the proposed
project Consolidation settlement on the order of 1 inch is anticipated due to compression
of the soft alluvial clays and organic silts layers from the additional load of 2 5 feet of new
fill Therefore it is recommended that the fill should be placed first and construction of the
proposed sewer lines and manholes should be delayed until substantial completion of the
consolidation settlement has occurred Detailed discussions of our geotechnical
engineering recommendations are presented in the following sections of this report
Site Preparation
It is our understanding that site grading work consisting of fills up to about 2 5 feet
is planned for the proposed project We envision that consolidation settlement on the
order of 1 inch may occur due to compression of the soft alluvial clays and organic silts
layers encountered in our field exploration Therefore it is recommended that the fill be
placed at the commencement of the project and settlement gauges should be installed to
monitor the anticipated settlement A typical settlement gauge detail is presented on
Plate 3 Construction of the proposed sewer lines and manholes should be postponed
until majority of consolidation settlement has been completed as revealed by settlement
monitoring We suggest that a monitoring period of about 2 months be provided and the
actual time required for monitoring shall depend on the settlement data obtained during
the monitoring period
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3924 00 Page 6
In general the following guidelines should be used for site grading At the on set
of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be thoroughly cleared All
vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable materials should be removed and
disposed of properly off site Soft and yielding areas encountered during clearing below
any areas designated to receive fill or future improvements should be over excavated to
expose firm or dense material and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with
well compacted engineered fill The excavated soft and or organic soils should be
properly disposed of off site
After clearing and demolition all areas at finished subgrade or designated to
receive fill should be scarified to a depth of about 8 inches moisture conditioned to above
the optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction In areas where the finished grades will be subjected to vehicular traffic the
compaction requirement should be increased to a minimum of 95 percent relative
compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a
percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with
ASTM D 1557 91 test procedures Optimum moisture is the water content percentage
by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
Where shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of the subgrade we
recommend that the soil be thoroughly moistened to close all cracks Saturation and
subsequent yielding of the exposed subgrade due to inclement weather and poor
drainage may require over excavation of the soft areas and replacement with
well compacted engineered fill
Imported fill materials should consist of non expansive granular material such as
crushed coral mudrock basalt or cinder sand The material should be well graded from
coarse to fine with no particles larger than 3 inches in largest dimension and should
contain between 10 and 30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve The material
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3924 00 Page 7
should have a laboratory CBR value of 20 or more and should have a maximum swell of
1 percent or less Imported fill material should be tested by Geolabs Hawaii for
conformance with these recommendations prior to delivery to the project site for its
intended use
Site preparation and settlement monitoring should be observed by a representative
of Geolabs Hawaii It is important that a representative from our office observe the site
preparation to evaluate whether any undesirable materials are encountered during site
preparation and to determine when construction of the proposed sewer lines and
manholes can begin based on observation of settlement monitoring
Sewer Trench
It is proposed to construct approximately 730 L F of new 8 inch and 10 inch sewer
lines PVC sewer pipes are proposed for this project Due to the stiff subgrade
conditions and minor settlements anticipated it is our opinion that PVC pipes are
appropriate In general a granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel
ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation is recommended under the pipes Free draining granular
materials such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation should also be
used for the trench backfill up to about 12 inches above the pipes to provide adequate
support around the pipes and to reduce the compaction effort of the backfill thus
reducing the possibility of damaging the pipes It is critical to use this free draining
material to reduce the potential for formation of voids below the haunches of pipes and to
provide adequate support for the sides of the pipes Improper trench backfill could result
in settlement of the backfill and damage to the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipes to
the top of the subgrade or finished grade may consist of the on site granular soils or
select fill material The backfill should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum
moisture placed in maximum 8 inch level loose lifts and mechanically compacted to not
less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for future ground
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3924 00 Page 8
subsidence Where trenches are below pavement areas the upper 2 feet of the trench
backfill below the pavement subgrade should be compacted to at least 95 percent relative
compaction
Design Review
Preliminary and final drawings and specifications for the proposed relief sewer
project should be forwarded to Geolabs Hawaii for review and written comments prior to

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that Geolabs Hawaii be retained to provide geotechnical
engineering services during construction of the proposed development The items of
construction monitoring that are critical requiring Special Inspection include observation
of site preparation and settlement monitoring This is to observe compliance with the
design concepts specifications or recommendations and to expedite suggestions for
design changes that may be required in the event that subsurface conditions differ from
those anticipated at the time this report was prepared The recommendations provided in
this report are contingent upon such observations
If actual exposed subsurface conditions encountered during construction are
different from those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications
to the design should be made
