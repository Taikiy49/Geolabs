SUMMARY OF FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS 
Wide flange beams are being considered for the foundation support of the new 
solar arrays for the project. Therefore, a test pile program consisting of installing and 
testing W6x7 wide flange beams for use as foundation support for the proposed solar 
arrays and W6x15 wide flange beams for use as foundation support for the motor piers 
was performed for the project. 
A total of sixteen W6x7 and sixteen W6x15 wide flange beam test piles were 
installed to various depths and locations specified by Nextracker at the Hale Kuawehi 
project site. All piles observed by our field engineer were driven to their specified depths 
without encountering refusal conditions, except for Pile No. 11B (W6x15), which met the 
refusal criteria at a depth of about 7.75 feet below the existing ground surface. A refusal 
condition criterion provided by Nextracker was identified as piles that did not meet the 
target embedment depth and have exceeded 3 minutes (180 seconds) of driving time. 
At nine test locations (PLT-1 through PLT-8, and PLT-18), test piles of varying 
depths were first subjected to tensile load tests (18 tensile load tests), followed by lateral 
load tests (18 lateral load tests). In addition, four test pile locations were subjected to 
compression load tests (eight compression load tests). 
Tensile load tests were conducted on 18 test piles in general accordance with 
ASTM D3689 with some modifications. In general, the test piles were loaded in tension 
until the failure displacement of 0.75 inches have been achieved. Based on the test 
results, the average ultimate skin friction values ranging from 85 psf to 265 psf for the 
driven W6x7 solar array piles and 110 psf to 305 psf for the driven W6x15 motor pier piles 
were determined from the load test results. 
After completion of the tensile pile load tests, the 18 test piles were subjected to 
lateral pile load testing in general accordance with ASTM D3966 with some modifications. 
Based on the results of the lateral load tests, we provided lateral load parameters for use 
in the LPILE program for Analysis and Design of Deep Foundations Under Lateral Loads 
consisting of undrained shear strengths ranging from about 750 to 875 psf with a strain 
at 50% of the maximum deviator stress (ε50) value of 0.01 for the test areas at PLT-1 and 
PLT-2, and an undrained shear strength ranging from 1,000 psf to 1,800 psf with a strain 
at 50% of the maximum deviator stress (ε50) value of 0.005 for the other test areas. 
The text of this report should be referred to for detailed test results and findings.

 GENERAL 
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering services 
performed during the test pile program for the Hale Kuawehi Solar project, located in the 
Kamuela area on the Island of Hawaii, Hawaii. The project location and general vicinity 
are shown on the Project Location Map, Plate 1. 
In general, this report presents our field observations and the results of tests 
conducted on the driven test piles installed and load tested between July 12 and 16, 2021. 
Our work was performed in general accordance with the scope of work outlined in our fee 
proposal dated June 29, 2021, and subsequent testing requirements provided by 
Innergex and Nextracker, Inc. 
The purpose of our geotechnical engineering services was to monitor the 
geotechnical aspects of the test pile installation and testing and to determine and/or 
confirm the pile design parameters. Site Constructors Inc. has been selected as the 
General Contractor for the project, and EPS Construction was the pile driving 
subcontractor installing the test piles and assisting with the load test setup.  
Project Considerations 
It is proposed to develop a new solar project near the Town of Kamuela on the 
Island of Hawaii. The new solar project is planned to the east of and near the intersection 
of Mamalahoa Highway (Route 190) and Saddle Road (Route 200) near the Town of 
Kamuela. The project site encompasses an area of approximately 190 acres. 
A geotechnical investigation of the interconnection, switchyard, and substation 
area of the project site was conducted by our office in 2020, and a geotechnical 
investigation of the solar array areas was conducted by others in 2019. In addition, a test 
pile program was conducted by our office last year to monitor the geotechnical aspects 
of the test pile installation and testing and to determine and/or confirm the pile design 
parameters provided in the geotechnical investigation report conducted by others. 
Results of the previous test pile program conducted at the project site are 
contained in the Geotechnical Engineering Report by Geolabs, Inc. entitled "Geotechnical

The pervious test pile program was conducted on using W8x10 wide flange beams 
as the test piles. We understand that the solar array provider for the project 
(Nextracker, Inc.) will use W6x7 wide flange beams for the foundation support of the new 
solar arrays and W6x15 wide flange beams for the foundation support of the motor pier 
arrays. Therefore, an additional test pile program was implemented to evaluate the 
performance of the W6x7 and W6x15 wide flange beams for the project. It should be 
noted that the test pile locations and testing procedures were provided by Nextracker, Inc. 
for the additional test pile program. 
As part of the additional test pile program, two test piles (one W6x7 and one 
W6x15) were specified to be driven to various depths below the existing ground surface 
at eight designated locations within the project site for a total of sixteen (16) test piles 
(eight W6x7 and eight W6x15 wide flange beams). At each designated location, one 
W6x7 and one W6x15 were first subjected to a tensile load test (ASTM D3689). After the 
specified failure displacement of 0.75 inches was achieved for the tensile load test, the 
same piles were then subjected to a lateral load test (ASTM D3966). Additional test piles 
(one of each size) were driven at locations specified by Nextracker throughout the project 
site to perform compressive load tests (ASTM D1143) and to determine the driveability of 
the wide flange beam piles in areas of the project site where refusal conditions may be 
encountered. The approximate test pile locations are shown on the Overall Site Plan 
(Plate 2) and Site Plans (Plates 3.1 through 3.10).

Based on the results of the lateral load tests, soil parameters were determined 
using the program LPILE-plus for Windows, which is a microcomputer adaptation of a 
finite difference, laterally loaded pile program originally developed at the University of 
Texas at Austin. The program solves for deflection and bending moment along a pile 
under lateral loads as a function of depth. The analysis was carried out with the use of 
non-linear “p-y” curves to represent soil moduli. The lateral deflection was then computed 
using the appropriate soil moduli at various depths. 
The analysis was performed by matching lateral deflection curves to the pile head 
movement obtained during the lateral pile load tests. The analysis was performed on all 
piles laterally load tested for the project. Due to the high subsurface variability at the
