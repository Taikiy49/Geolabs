FIELD EXPLORATION
The geophysical surveys were conducted using a pipe and cable locator toner
manufactured by Metro Tech and a ground penetrating radar GPR system
manufactured by Geophysical Survey Systems Incorporated The GPR system
consisted of a SIR System 3 control unit with a 500 Megahertz antenna set
Ground penetrating radar utilizes impulse radar technology to obtain real time
profiles of the subsurface materials A radar frequency pulse is transmitted into the
subsurface and when it encounters an interface between two materials with differing
electrical properties it is reflected back to a receiver on the surface In a relatively
homogeneous medium this reflection is noted as an anomaly
The reflected pulse is intercepted by the receiver and then electronically
processed and printed on a graphic recorder producing a record similar to a marine
depth recorder chart As the transmitter and receiver antenna array is moved across
the ground surface a real time graphic record of the subsurface is generated Markers
can be inserted into the data print to reference distances as the array is moved
providing a means to physically locate anomalies
Ground penetrating radar is generally considered most applicable to the location
of subsurface voids and buried structures Depth of penetration and data resolution are
dependent upon soil type moisture content electrical properties antenna frequency
and power

The use of ground penetrating radar is limited by the presence of conductive
materials within the profile being surveyed This is particularly true if the conductive
materials are in the upper or surface portions of the subsurface profile Conductive
materials attenuate the electromagnetic signal and interfere with penetration of the
radar pulses Generally ground penetrating radar will not function with conductivities
higher than about meters Mho meters
FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATION
An abandoned and sealed vent pipe for an underground storage tank was
observed on the southerly side of Building 108 An attempt was made to locate the
suspected tank and appurtenant piping by conductive toning using a pipe and cable
locator Although good conductive coupling could not be achieved the toning indicated
that the vent pipe leads in an easterly direction
Geophysical surveys of the site to the south and east of the building conducted
with ground penetrating radar indicated an anomalous zone under the sidewalk at a
distance of about 33 to 39 feet to the east of the wooden utility pole at the start of the
sidewalk
It should be noted that the surface soils observed at the site are clayey in nature
and therefore are conductive The relatively high conductivity of the soils tended to
obscure the data and make accurate interpretation difficult Based on our interpretation
of the geophysical data the anomaly observed is approximately 3 feet in diameter
which is consistent with the size of a 500 to 1 000 gallon capacity underground storage
tank
