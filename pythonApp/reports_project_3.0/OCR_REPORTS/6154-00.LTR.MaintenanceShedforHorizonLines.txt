Dear Mr. Ho:
In accordance with our fee proposal dated February 2, 2009, we provided drilling
and laboratory testing services for the proposed Maintenance Shed for Horizon Lines
project at their container yard on Sand Island on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. This letter
report presents our drilling logs and laboratory testing results.
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project involves the erection of a maintenance shed. Current concepts
involve steel frames and structure with fabric installed over the steel frame.
Foundation design will be provided by the shed vendor. Of particular concern is:
the design of anchors to resist the uplift loads on the shed.
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to provide boring data and lab testing to assist
in the anchor design by the shed vendor. To accomplish this, we performed an
exploration program consisting of the following tasks and work efforts:
1. Coordination to designate borehole locations and verify locations of existing
utilities in the vicinity of the proposed drilling area.
2. Mobilization and demobilization of a truck-mounted drill rig and
two operators to and from the project site.
3. Drilling and sampling of four boreholes to depths of approximately 21 to
25.9 feet below the existing ground surface.
4. Coordination of the field exploration and logging of the borings by our
field engineer/geologist.

FIELD EXPLORATION
Our field exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling four borings,
designated as Boring Nos. 1 through 4, extending to depths of about 21 to 25.9 feet
below the existing ground surface. The borings were drilled using a truck-mounted drill
rig equipped with continuous flight, hollow-stem augers. The approximate boring
locations are shown on the Site Plan, Plate 2.
Our geologist classified the materials encountered in the borings by visual and
textural examination in the field and monitored the drilling operations on a near-continuous
basis. Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil Classification
System, as shown on the Soil Log Legend, Plate A. Graphic representations of the
materials encountered are provided on the Logs of Borings, Plates A-1 through A-4.
Relatively “undisturbed” soil samples were obtained from the borings drilled in
general accordance with ASTM D 3550, Ring-Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils, by driving a
3-inch outside diameter (OD) Modified California sampler with a 140-pound hammer falling
30 inches. In addition, some samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general
accordance with ASTM D 1586, Penetration Test and Split-Barrel Sampling of Soils, by
driving a 2-inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same hammer and drop. The
blow counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an 18-inch drive
are shown as the “Penetration Resistance” on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate
sample depths.
LABORATORY TESTING
Selected soil samples from the field exploration were tested in the laboratory with
an emphasis on evaluating the moisture, density, classification, and strength
characteristics. The following laboratory tests were performed on selected soil samples
obtained from the field exploration.
Moisture Content (ASTM D 2216) and Unit Weight (ASTM D 2937)
determinations were performed on selected samples as an aid in the classification and
evaluation of soil properties. The test results are presented on the Logs of Borings at
the appropriate sample depths.

One Atterberg Limits test (ASTM D 4318) was performed on a selected soil
sample to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits. The test results are summarized on the
Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depth. Graphic presentations of the test
results are provided on Plate B-1.
Three Sieve Analyses (ASTM C 117 & C 136) were performed on selected
samples to evaluate the grain size distribution characteristics of the soils. The test result
is presented on Plate B-2.
Two Direct Shear tests (ASTM D 3080) were performed on relatively undisturbed
samples to evaluate the shear strength characteristics of the soils. The test results are
presented on Plates B-3 and B-4.
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
In general, our field exploration disclosed that the site is underlain by asphaltic
concrete pavement over fill consisting of silty sand and sandy clay to about 6.5 to 9 feet
below the existing ground surface. The fill was underlain by soft/loose lagoonal deposits
consisting primarily of coralline silty sand and gravel to the drilled depths. Boring No. 3
extended to 25.9 feet to locate the underlying hard coral layer.
We encountered groundwater in the borings at depths of 7.0 to 7.5 feet below the
existing ground surface at the time of our field exploration. However, groundwater levels
are subject to change due to rainfall, seasonal precipitation, surface water runoff, and
other factors. Descriptions and graphic representations of the materials encountered are
presented on the Logs of Borings, Plates A-1 through A-4.
LIMITATIONS
The client determined the number, location, and depths of the borings. Variations of
subsurface conditions between and beyond the borings may occur.
The boring locations indicated herein are approximate, having been estimated by
taping and pacing from the features shown on the airphoto and using hand held
GPS units. The physical locations and elevations of the borings should be considered
accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used.
The stratification lines shown on graphic representations of the borings depict the
approximate boundaries between soil types and, as such, may denote a gradual transition.
It should be noted that groundwater level changes may occur due to seasonal
precipitation, surface runoff, and other factors.
