Dear Mr Maluo
This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering services performed during the
mass grading for the Kapolei High School project from through
in general accordance with our fee proposal dated
SITE GRADING
The future Kapolei High School project site is located within the existing Villages of
Kapolei development in the District of Ewa on the island of Oahu Hawaii The high school
site is situated approximately at the southwest corner of the future Makai Villages 7 8
residential development site The future high school site encompasses an area of
approximately 40 acres and is bounded to the north by Kapolei Parkway to the east by the
future Makai Village 8 to the south by the Kapolei Lower Channel and to the west by Fort
Barrette Road as shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
The site grading operations for the future Kapolei High School site were performed
by Oahu Construction Company In general the site grading operations consisted of clearing
and grubbing over excavation of the undocumented fills and placement and compaction of
fills The depths of the excavations and thickness of embankments at the site were on the
order of about 2 to 8 feet Much of the fills needed at the site to raise the pre existing grades
to the design mass grading grades consisted of various sources of imported soil materials
During the site grading operation samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing The suitability
of the imported materials for use as general fill was evaluated based on performing
laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR tests ASTM D 1883 In addition the maximum

dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test results are
summarized on Plates 2 1 and 2 2 of this report
The project site was generally cleared and grubbed of vegetation debris rubbish and
other unsuitable materials Twelve test pits were excavated in areas suspected to contain
some pre existing undocumented fills to determine the depth and the extent of the
undocumented fills The approximate locations of the test pits are shown on the Site Plan
Plate 1 The approximate extents of the removal of the undocumented fill area are also
shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
The undocumented fills were over excavated to remove the undocumented fills in
order to expose firm ground The over excavated subgrade was then scarified to a depth of
about 8 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture
content and compacted to at least 90 percent of the maximum dry density as determined in
accordance with American Society of Testing and Material ASTM Test Designation D 1557
Modified Proctor During the placement of fill materials the fill materials were generally
placed in near level lifts brought to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to
a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density determined in accordance with ASTM
Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor
Periodic field density tests were performed during the fill operations to confirm our field
observations as variations in the construction procedures and soil conditions were noted A
total of 355 field density tests including retests of failed areas were performed using the
nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 2922 and D 3017 The
results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 13 of this
report
The field density tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on the
compacted fills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and
interpolating from the on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed to be
unsatisfactory were re worked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
above referenced tests it is our professional opinion that the earthwork operations for the
mass grading of the above referenced project site observed and tested by Geolabs Hawaii
in the period covered by this report were performed in general accordance with the intent
of the project plans and specifications
