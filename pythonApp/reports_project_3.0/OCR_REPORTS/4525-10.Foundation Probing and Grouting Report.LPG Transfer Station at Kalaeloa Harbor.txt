This letter report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations during the
probing and grouting operations for the LPG Transfer Station at Kalaeloa Harbor for
The Gas Company from August 5 through 8 2002
Project Considerations
The project site is located off Malakole Street to the west side of the existing
parking lot at Kalaeloa Barbers Point Harbor in the District of Ewa on the Island of Oahu
Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1
Based on the information provided we understand that it is desired to relocate the
existing facilities of The Gas Company at Pier 38 Sand Island to the Barbers Point area
The new facility at Barbers Point would consist of the following construction and
improvements
Installation of nine 30 000 gallon gas storage tanks These tanks are
above ground struciures cylindrical in shape measuring approximately
12 feet in diameter and 48 feet in length Based on the information provided
each tank will weigh about 275 kips under fully loaded condition The tanks
will be placed horizontally parallel to one another and supported by a
saddle on both ends The saddles will be supported on a large strip footing
foundation at each end

Our field exploration disclosed that the northern part of the project site is generally
underlain by approximately 10 feet of loose silty sands overlying dense coralline materials
at greater depths Dense to very dense coralline materials were encountered throughout
the depths explored in the remaining area of the project site Therefore special attention
was given to the quality of the near surface fill soils and the potential for presence of
near surface cavities voids in the underlying coral deposits which may collapse under
structural and vehicular loads
Probing and Grouting Operations
The probing and grouting operations were performed in general conformance with
the recommendations presented in our report for the project dated The
purpose of the probing and grouting operation was to reduce the potential for loss of
foundation support resulting from collapse of cavities or compression of loose zones below
the new storage tanks
The probe hole locations were marked by Oceanic Companies Inc prior to the
probing operation In general the probe holes at the footing locations of the new storage
tanks were drilled to depths of about 12 to 13 feet below the existing ground surface The
probe holes about 6 5 inches in diameter were drilled by Foundation International Inc
using a Watson 345 Low Track drill rig equipped with 6 inch solid stem augers on
August 6 through 8 2002 Following the probing operation a 2 inch diameter PVC pipe
was inserted into each of the probe holes to maintain the integrity of the open hole and to
reduce the potential for cave in prior to the grouting operation A total of 98 probe holes
with an aggregate length of about 1 184 lineal feet were drilled This included the
additional probe holes drilled for Probe Hole 35 38 and 54 where cavities
and or loose zones were encountered The approximate locations of the probe holes are
shown on the Probe Hole Location Plan Plate 2
The probe hoies were backfilled with sand cement slurry supplied by Island Ready
Mix Concrete Inc injected at iow to moderate pressures on August 6 and 8 2002 The
grouting work was performed by Foundation International Inc and observed by a field
representative from Geolabs The grouting operation generally proceeded in the following
manner

e Immediately prior to grouting of a particular hole the temporary PVC pipe
was removed A steel grout pipe was inserted into the open hole and
lowered to approximately 2 feet from the bottom of the hole Grout injection
then commenced at the direction of our field representative who recorded
the approximate grout take and provided direction as to when the injection
was complete or whether periodic injection was required
Each probe hole was grouted from the desired depth upward by low to
moderate pressure injection of grout through the steel grout pipe which was
retracted from the probe hole as the grout filled the hole Additional injection
passes were needed to grout those probe holes where high grout take was
observed The grouting operation and the approximate grout take were
observed and recorded by our field representative
Approximately 19 cubic yards of flowable fill was utilized to fill the probe holes In
general the probe holes took about twice the theoretical volume of grout to fill the probe
holes The probing and grouting summary is provided on Plates 3 1 through 3 4
In summary the foundation probing and grouting operations for the project have
been conducted in general conformance with the intent of the project specifications and
our geotechnical engineering exploration report Based on our observations it is our
professional opinion that the relatively small voids and or cavities encountered at the
foundation locations have been grouted and that the subgrade area should provide the
recommended foundation support for the new storage tanks
