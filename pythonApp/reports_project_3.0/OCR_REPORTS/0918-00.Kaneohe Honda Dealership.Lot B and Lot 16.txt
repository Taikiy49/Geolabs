INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our soil investigation
for the proposed Kaneohe Honda Dealership to be located
along Kamehameha Highway in Kaneohe Lot B Lot 16 and
Portion of Lot A 1 The purpose of this investigation
was to explore the existing subsurface conditions at the
Site in relation with the foundation requirements and
behavior of the underlying materials under the anticipated
fill and structural loading conditions

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed two story structure with a basement will be
located in the southern portion of the site over an existing
drainageway Asphaltic concrete parking will be provided on
the western side of the property along Kamehameha Highway
and also on the northern side of the property
Two 2 retaining walls are also proposed One 1 wall
will be along the eastern edge of the property and the
other wall will be perpendicular to the southern end of the
proposed building During the site grading fill will be
placed over the entire area The existing drainageway will
receive fill as much as 12 feet during grading Basement
floor slab elevations are planned to be at about 96 feet
msl The elevations at the site ranges from about 108
on the west along Kamehameha Highway to 76 feet on the
northeast corner of the property

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The existing surface fill material is relatively loose and
varies in thickness from 5 to 9 feet over the site Addi
tional new fill is planned over the existing loose fill for
the project site grading preparation Up to about 12 feet
of fill will be required to fill up the existing drainageway
area
Due to the loose nature of the existing fill material and the
varying thickness of the new fill over it large amounts of
differential settlements are anticipated under the existing
loose fill area To minimize future settlements under the
proposed building area we recommend that a minimum of six 6
feet of the existing loose fill within the limits of the

lifts of new fill material The existing loose fill from
the upper excavation could be utilized as a source of fill
to be placed at the lower portion of the drainageway area
where about 12 feet of new fill will be required to achieve
the desired finish grades

Fill Placement
After the site preparation as described above the
fill should be placed in maximum 10 inch loose
lifts and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent
under the structural areas of its maximum dry
density as established by ASTM test designation
D 1557 70 Outside the structural areas fill
compaction requirement can be reduced to 90 per
cent of its maximum density The on site material
could be used for fill and backfill purposes
It is recommended that if additional off site borrow
is required it should be tested and approved by the
soils engineer prior to transportation to the site
to assure that it is suitable for the proposed
construction usage The material should generally
be non expansive and should not contain large percent
ages of boulders organic or deleterious matter

Two Story Structure
The proposed two story structure may be supported
by conventional spread or continuous footing founda
tions placed on the above specified compacted fill
The footings should have a minimum width of 12 inches
and be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the
lowest adjacent grade
The foundations can be designed using an allowable
bearing pressure of 2 000 pounds per square foot
for the footings placed on compacted fill material
The above bearing value of compacted fill material
is based on the assumptiom that 95 percent relative
compaction of the fill material can be achieved
All of the footings of the structure should be placed
in compacted fill
Above values of bearing capacity are for total of dead
plus live loads and may be increased by 33 percent when
temporary lateral loads wind and seismic are consi
dered

Total settlement of about one inch with some differ
ential settlement approximately 1 4 to 1 2 inch
should be anticipated from long term secondary
consolidation of the remaining soft fill subsoils
Basement walls of the proposed structure will be
subjected to horizontal earth pressures imposed
by compacted backfill material It is recommended
that the lateral earth pressures induced on base
ment walls be computed utilizing an equivalent fluid
unit weight of 65 pounds per cubic foot Additional
loads due to traffic or surcharge should be included
where applicable
We recommend that the basement walls should be damp
proofed A subsurface drainage system should be
used to prevent the hydrostatic pressure buildup
behind the walls
Slab on Grade
The basement floor slab can be supported on the above
specified controlled fill
We recommend that a 4 inch layer of granular material
such as No 3B Fine rock be provided under the floor
slab

Retaining Walls
Retaining walls will be utilized at the southern and eastern
portions of the site to retain about four 4 to six 6
feet of new fill
The retaining wall footing on the southern portion of the
site can be supported on the existing fill materials Due
to the loose nature of the fill we recommend that the
existing fill material under the retaining wall footing be
compacted to a minimum of 95 percent of its maximum dry
density as established by ASTM Test Designation D 1557 70
The retaining wall footing could be designed using an
allowable bearing pressure of 1000 p s f
Retaining wall footings on the eastern side should be founded
on undisturbed in situ clayey silt not fill The footings
can be designed using an allowable bearing pressure of 2000
p s f
Due to the presence of steep slope in this area we do not
recommend placing the retaining wall footing on fill if
encountered The footing should be lowered to natural
ground if fill is encountered If the retaining wall
footing is located next to the existing sewer easement the
footing should extend to the bottom of the sewer easement
