exploration performed for the proposed Masonic Lodge along Kalanianaole
Highway in Kailua on this island of Oahu Hawaii

This report summarizes the findings of our exploration and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analyses of the
data obtained during the exploration

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a metal framed building at the site
about 45 by 100 feet in plan with on grade parking
Site filling up to about 4 feet high was planned in order to raise
the site for drainage and flooding considerations

subsurface conditions at the project site in order to develop generalized
soil data for the formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations
pertinent to the design of the site grading foundation and pavement of
the proposed building

Laboratory testing of selected samples obtained during the field
exploration to determine the engineering properties and
classification of the materials encountered
Engineering analyses of the field and laboratory data for the
purpose of developing site grading foundation and pavement

SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is located on a triangular terrace bounded to the
east by Maunawili Stream to the north by Kalanianaole Highway and to the
west by a section of the old Kalanianaole Highway in Kailua Maunawili
Waste Water Pump Station is about 500 feet west from the site
The site is generally level with ground elevations varying from about
18 5 to 19 5 feet above Mean Sea level The adjacent Kalanianaole Highway
embankment to the north is higher with elevations of about 29 to 34 feet
above Mean Sea Level
The site is heavily overgrown with vegetation and a trail was cut
through the vegetation to allow access for our field exploration
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions at the proposed building site were explored
by drilling and sampling three 3 borings to depths of 80 5 to 95 4 feet
below the existing ground surface The approximate locations of these
borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general the site is underlain by about 80 feet of soft soils
lagoonal deposits below about 2 to 3 feet of firm surface fill
The surface fill layer grades softer with depth to become very soft
to medium stiff near or below the groundwater level which is about 5 5 to
6 feet depth below the existing ground surface
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2143 00 Page 4
The underlying soft soil deposits include up to about 17 feet of soft
to medium stiff clayey silt and about 60 to 72 feet of slightly organic
clayey silt lagoonal deposits which are highly compressible as indicated
by our laboratory consolidation test results
Below the highly compressible subsoils were encountered a 12 foot
layer of medium dense to dense gravel overlying probably either boulders
or an in situ basalt flow
The relatively high groundwater level appears to be due to the close
proximity of the project site to Maunawili Strean It is therefore
anticipated that the groundwater level will fluctuate in response to tidal
influences and flood conditions in the stream
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Our exploration indicates that the site is underlain by a thin
surficial crust of fill overlying soft compressible subsoils The
placement of additional fill over these soft compressible subsoils would
induce long term settlements which could potentially impact structures on
the project site and in the surrounding area
Therefore we believe that site filling should be minimized to reduce
the ground settlements at the building site and the adjacent highway
embankments Post and beam or column type footing foundations may be used
to support the proposed building which should be elevated to allow for
drainage and flooding considerations
It is also our opinion that the thin crust of stiff fill and the
underlying highly compressible soils deposits will not provide adequate
foundation support and that significant settlements will occur under the
new building loads In order to provide adequate bearing for spread
footing type foundations it is our opinion that the very soft clayey or
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2143 00 Page 5
silty deposits within a depth of five 5 feet below the footing bottom
should be over excavated and replaced with select compacted structural
fill Im addition provision for relevelling or adjustment of the future
building should be included in the footing design to accommodate future
settlements
Footing Foundation
Based on our foundation analyses we believe that post and beam or
columns on spread footing foundations may be more suitable to support the
proposed Masonic Lodge structure However to reduce excessive spread
footing settlements and bearing failure we recommend that the individual
footing excavations be over excavated down to a minimm depth of about
five 5 feet below the footing bottom surface to remove the upper soft
clayey and silty deposits The over excavations should also extend a
minimum of 1 5 feet beyond the footing perimeters as shown schematically
on Plate 3
The over excavations should be lined with a layer of geotextile
fabric and backfilled with 3B Fine gravel to a minimm of 12 inches above
the bottom of the excavation Non expansive select borrow material such
as crusher waste or coral should then be placed in six to eight inch
horizontal loose lifts over the 3B Fine gravel and compacted to a
minimum of 90 of its maximm dry density per ASIM D 1557 78 prior to
footing placement The top 1 foot below the adjacent finish subgrade
level should be backfilled with impervious non expansive soil compacted
also to the above specified density For details of the footing
over excavations and backfill see Plate 3
Based on this special design and the anticipated light building
loading we recommend that an allowable bearing capacity of 1500 p s f be
used for the design of the spread footings bearing on compacted structural
fill prepared as recommended above

W O 2143 00 Page 6
The footings should be embedded a minimm of 18 inches below the
lowest adjacent finished grade Uplift wind or other lateral loading
conditions may further increase the embedment of the footing foundations
For the above special foundation design it is estimated that primary
foundation settlements due to structural loading may be less than one inch
total and one half inch differential However it is anticipated that
additional long term secondary footing settlements may occur due to the
organic nature of the compressible subsoils and the anticipated site
grading may also induce some additional ground subsidence of the proposed
footings Any future construction in the general area may also induce
additional settlement problems
Therefore we recommend that all footings be designed with provisions
for relevelling to accommodate all the settlements which may amount to as
much as six 6 inches or more
Site Grading
The placement of up to about 4 feet of fill is planned to raise the
existing grade of the site for drainage and flooding considerations
However it is our opinion that site filling should be minimized since the
weight of the new fill would induce long term ground subsidence at the
building site and also at the adjacent highway embankment areas We
recommend that site filling should be restricted to a minimm of 2 feet or
less to reduce the potential ground settlements
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed Any unsuitable
materials such as vegetation and rubbish should be removed and disposed
of properly off site
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2143 00 Page 7
The ground surface below the new pavements should be scarified to a
minimum depth of 6 inches below the cleared grade and recompacted to a
minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density as established by ASIM
D 1557 78 Soft pockets and pumping or yielding areas encountered in the
initial site preparation should be removed and backfilled with compacted
select backfill material
The upper on site fill materials are moderately expansive at the
in situ moisture contents and should not be reused as a source of fill
and or backfill materials New imported fill materials should consist of
non expansive select granular materials such as base course which are
less susceptible to flood erosion The new fill should be place in 8 inch
maximum horizontal loose lifts and compacted to 90 of its maximm dry
density per ASTM D 1557 78
Site grading should provide drainage of surface runoff away from the
proposed structure
Pavement

flexible pavement section may be used for the proposed ground floor garage
and driveway on compacted site fills

Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be

prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the site grading and foundation recommendations given herein If this
review is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility
for misinterpretation of the recommendations

Construction Observation
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the
foundation phases of the work This is to observe compliance with the
design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow design
changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from that
anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in
this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
upon information obtained from the borings Variations of conditions
between the borings may occur and the nature and extent of these
variations may not become evident until construction If variations then
appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations
given in this report
The locations of the borings were approximately determined by taping
from the existing structures indicated on the topographic survey map
transmitted by Community Planning Inc on Elevations
of the borings were approximately determined by interpolation from
elevation points on the topographic survey map The physical locations
and elevations of the borings should be considered accurate only to the
degree implied by the method used

The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
borings depict the approximate boundaries between soil types and as
such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on profiles
or cross sections are based upon interpolation between borings and may not
represent actual subsurface conditions
Water level data from the borings were collected at the times given
on the graphic representations and or in the text of this report These
data have been reviewed and interpretations made in the formulation of
this report However it must be noted that fluctuation may occur due to
variations in rainfall tides floods temperature and other factors
