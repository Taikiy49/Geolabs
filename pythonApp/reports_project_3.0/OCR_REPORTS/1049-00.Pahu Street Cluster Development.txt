This report presents the results of our soil engineering
reconnaissance study at the above referenced project site
The purpose of our reconnaissance study was to review the
general soil conditions in the area in order to evaluate the
proposed development plan from a soil engineering point of
view Of particular concern was the near surface soil con
ditions in the lower portion of the site where soft subsoils
were anticipated
Our reconnaissance study consisted of a review of the general
geologic and soils maps of the area field observations
three test borings and laboratory soils tests

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed cluster development consists of 1 story single
family residences with the necessary access roads
The preliminary grading plan indicates cuts generally less
than 2 feet in the upper portions of the site and minor fil
ling over the lower area
SITE CONDITIONS
Surface
The project site is situated in the lower southern portion
of Waipahu adjacent to the west loch of Pearl Harbor
The site is roughly tapezoidal in plan view approximately
650 feet long with widths of 120 to 420 feet It is bordered
by Pahu Street on the northwest by Pearl Harbor Gardens Sub
division on the northeast by a utility easement on the south
and by existing homes and gardens on the southwest There
are two existing dirt roads into the site
The site topography can be divided into two areas The higher
northwest portion of the area Slopes down gently toward the
southeast at gradients of less than 5 The ground elevation
in this area varies from 8 to 16 feet

Geology
The project site is situated at the southern juncture of the
Koolau and Waianae Volcanic Ranges and has a complex geologic
history
At the end of the above two volcanic formations only slight
embayment existed at Pearl Harbor Several periods of emer
gence submergence erosion and deposition occurred The
lava flows were deeply weathered and eroded During the Plei
stocene Ice Age Kaena plus 90 foot stand of sea level a
delta of silt sand and gravel built out into the embayment

of Hawaii These deposits now forming the upper terrace
at the site were subsequently cut by streams during low
stands of sea level Later a bay mud deposit formed in
the lower portion of the site during the most recent period
of natural sedimentation reworked stream clays and silts at
the head of Pearl Harbor This soft deposit may extend to
depths of 30 to 100 feet in the general area

Soil Conditions
Three borings were drilled in the lower portions of the site
and they encountered similar soil conditions
The upper 4 to 6 foot crust consisted of reddish brown to
gray brown clayey silt with gravel fill and alluvium
This upper layer was generally medium stiff in consistency
and graded to wet and soft medium stiff conditions near the
water level
A soft to very soft gray bay mud deposit consisting of
clayey silt with shells was encountered below the upper
layer down to the depth of the borings
Water level was encountered at about 4 5 feet below the
existing ground surface
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Our reconnaissance study indicates that the upper portion of
the site is probably underlain by stiff alluvial deposits
while the lower portion is underlain by soft bay mud depos
its The proposed minimum grading concept for the lower area
is particularly suitable for this site From a soils engin
eering point of view the proposed development is feasible
for the site

Site Grading
Small cuts and fills are planned for the project site devel
opment
No unusual site grading problems are anticipated in the higher
portions of the site
Cuts and fills should be kept to a minimum in the lower areas
Additional fill weight would cause ground settlements due to
increased loading on the underlying soft deposits Cutting
in this area would also decrease the thickness of the upper
medium stiff crust over the soft deposit resulting in the
future foundations being closer to the soft soil and ground
water level
Foundations
Light one story wood structures are planned for the develop
ment
Lower Area
Minor settlements are anticipated if additional fill is
kept to a minimum less than 2 feet
Low bearing pressures of 1000 p s f are recommended for
foundations on the medium stiff upper crust
The upper crust soil is variable however preliminary

Additional Studies
Additional soil engineering analysis and borings drilled
within the upper and lowlying areas should be made in order
to verify the subsurface soil conditions at the other parts
of the site and to obtain more specific engineering recom
mendations for the design of the foundations and site grading
