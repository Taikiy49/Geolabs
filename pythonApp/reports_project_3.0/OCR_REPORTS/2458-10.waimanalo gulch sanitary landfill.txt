INTRODUCTION
Geolabs Hawaii under contract to Waste Management International Inc WMI
has performed quality assurance quality control observation and testing for the
construction of the Phase Il ash monofill base liner at the Waimanalo Gulch Sanitary
Landfill The general location of the project site is shown on the Project Location Map
Figure 1
The design of the ash monofill base liner section consists of bottom to top
1 A three 3 foot thick layer of compacted soil liner with design permeability
values equal to or lower than
2 A layer of 60 mil high density polyethylene HDPE geomembrane
3 A geonet drainage layer
4 A layer of 16 ounce per square yard non woven geotextile
This report provides a summary of 1 the observation and soil testing associated
with the construction of the soil liner 2 the results of the geosynthetic material
conformance testing and 3 the observation and testing of the geomembrane liner
installation

SCOPE OF SERVICES
The scope of Geolabs Hawaii s services included construction quality assurance
observation and testing of the soil liner placement and geosynthetic liner installation
during the construction of the Phase II ash monofill base liner Construction of the soil
liner began approximately in Hawaii was not on site full time during
the placement of the soil liner but provided compaction testing services on an as needed
basis Geolabs Hawaii was on site full time during the installation of the geosynthetic liner
The geosynthetic liner was installed between August 14 and August 24 1990 A
summary of the observation schedule by Geolabs Hawaii s on site staff during this period
is shown in Appendix A The contractor involved in the earthwork was Waste
Management of Hawaii The contractor involved in the geosynthetic installation was
National Seal Company of Galesburg Illinois
As specified by the Waste Management Quality Assurance Manual for the
Installation of Geosynthetic Lining Systems dated June 1986 daily field observation
reports were prepared by Geolabs Hawaii s field personnel The daily reports are not
included in this document Copies of the daily reports have been submitted to Waste
Management of Hawaii Inc

GEOMEMBRANE INSTALLATION
Approximately 149 850 square feet 9 rolls of 60 mil high density polyethylene
HDPE geomembrane manufactured by National Seal Company of Galesburg Illinois
was delivered to the site Approximately 94 605 square feet of the 60 mil HDPE
geomembrane was used for the lining system
Geomembrane Deployment
Deployment of the 60 mil HDPE geomembrane began on August 14 1990 and
was completed on August 18 1990 Deployment and welding progressed continuously
throughout this period
The deployment of the geomembrane was observed by Geolabs Hawaii s
personnel Observation duties included measurement of the panel thickness confirmation
of panel overlap and a visual observation of the overall sheet quality A summary of the
deployment observations is included in Appendix E 1
Deployment of the liner began at the southeast corner of the pad and proceeded
to the west and north The slope panels extended a minimum of fifteen feet beyond the
crest of the slopes into the anchor trench The floor panels extended a minimum of five
feet beyond the centerline of the swale A minimum of five inches of overlap was
maintained between adjoining panels The geomembrane panel layout for the ash
monofill base liner is shown on Figure 2
Each panel of deployed 60 mil HDPE geomembrane was given a unique panel
number which identified that panel within the installation The panels were numbered
sequentially as they were deployed
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2458 10 Page 7
Each geomembrane panel was observed for overall sheet quality and measured
for thickness as they were being deployed The thickness measurements were made
using a micrometer Five measurements were taken on each of the leading edge one
side and the trailing edge of each panel The reported thicknesses in Appendix E 1
represent the average of the five readings All panels had an average thickness equal to
or greater than 60 mils and all individual measurements were greater than 54 mils
Weld Observation
The majority of the seaming of the HDPE panels was accomplished using fusion
welding apparatus Extrusion welding was used to make repairs and to weld the seams
at the T junctions of three panels overlapping each other The welding was observed by
Geolabs Hawaii s personnel on a procedural basis The entire length of all seams
patches or other repairs was observed either during welding or shortly after completion
of the welding
Each seam was numbered according to the two panels being welded together
For example seam P1 2 is the seam between panels P1 and P2 Each seam was
visually inspected by Geolabs Hawaii s personnel to determine that the panels were
properly cleaned prior to welding and that the weld was continuous and free of defects
A summary of the welding observations is presented in Appendix E 2
Trial test Seams were required of all welders and seaming apparatuses on a regular
basis as specified in the QA manual The welding personnel each produced a test seam
at the start of each seaming period normally twice a day Three 3 to four 4 one inch
wide test strips were cut from each test sample for testing A minimum of two 2 strips
in the peel test mode and one 1 strip in the shear test mode were tested using a field
tensiometer All test seams were performed in the presence of Geolabs Hawaii s
personnel All welders were required to produce a trial test seam that did not fail in the
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2458 10 Page 8
weld during testing before they were allowed to start welding on the liner A Summary of
the test seams is presented in Appendix E 3 The pass and fail modes for test specimens
were reported based on the pass fail codes shown on Figures 3 and 4
Destructive Testing
In accordance with the QA Manual and Specification Guidelines destructive test
samples of the welded seams were obtained at a minimum frequency of one destructive
test sample per every 500 linear feet of weld The test locations were selected by
Geolabs Hawaii s QA inspectors based either on the completion of approximately 500 feet
of welding or on seaming observations Once a destructive test location had been
located a 12 inch wide by 48 inch long sample centered lengthwise on the weld was cut
The sample was cut into three equal pieces One section was packaged and shipped to
Golder Associates laboratory at Atlanta Georgia for laboratory destructive testing one
section taken for National Seal s archives and the other section was taken for the
owner s archives After the removal of each destructive test sample the sampled area
was patched and the patch seam was non destructively tested The destructive test
locations are shown in Figure 2
Two strips were cut from each destructive test sample for field testing in peel and
shear Destructive sample DT 7 failed the field testing in peel Therefore two samples
one from each end of and approximately 10 feet away from DT 7 were taken and labelled
as DT 7A and DT 7B for additional destructive test following the specification in the QA
manual Both DT 7A and DT 7B passed the field testing A total of 14 destructive test
samples DT 1 to DT 13 were taken for laboratory testing
From each destructive test sample 10 specimens were removed for testing Five
5 specimens were tested for adhesion peel for both the inner weld track and the outer
weld track with the specified acceptance criterion being that four out of five test
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2458 10 Page 9
specimens per weld track will not fail in the weld Five specimens were also tested for
bonded strength shear with the specified acceptance criteria being that four out of five
specimens must have yield strengths greater than 132 pounds per inch and that failure
cannot occur in the weld A summary of the destructive test results is included in
Appendix E 5
Non Destructive Continuity Testing
Non destructive continuity testing of the welded seams were performed using
vacuum box and air pressure testing apparatuses For the vacuum test a soapy solution
was mopped on the seam and the vacuum box was placed over the seam A vacuum
of approximately 5 psi was applied in the box for a duration of 10 seconds Detected
leaks were repaired and retested using the same procedure The seams that were not
vacuum tested were non destructively tested using air pressure testing For this test both
ends of the seam being tested were sealed and a needle with an attached pressure
gauge and air valve was inserted into the air channel between the two welds A pressure
of approximately 30 psi was then applied to the air channel and monitored for five
minutes If the pressure drop over the five minute period was less than 2 psi the seam
was considered continuous and defect free If the loss in pressure exceeded 2 psi the
seam was then pressure tested in sections until the defect was found or the seam was
tested using a vacuum box Leaks detected using the air pressure method were repaired
and retested using a vacuum box A summary of non destructive tests is included on the
weld inspection summary and repair summary of Appendix E 2 and Appendix E 4
respectively

GEONET INSTALLATION
Approximately 86 000 square feet of geonet was left over from the previous phase
of geosynthetic liner installation and 18 096 square feet of geonet manufactured by
National Seal Company was delivered to the project site for the present Phase Il
installation Approximately 93 150 square feet of geonet was used for the lining system
One layer of geonet was placed over the geomembrane except along the centerline
of the leachate collection swale where an additional layer of one roll width was placed
The geonet panels were bond together using white plastic ties spaced approximately
every five to six feet End to end panels were joined by double rows of plastic ties
spaced approximately every six inches The geomembrane was cleaned prior to
placement of the geonet
GEOTEXTILE INSTALLATION
The geotextile delivered to the site is a 16 ounce non woven needle punched
continuous filament polypropylene fabric manufactured by Trivera Inc Approximately
27 000 square feet of geotextile was left over from the previous phase of geosynthetic
liner installation and 90 000 square feet of geotextile was delivered to the site for the
present Phase Il installation A total of 93 675 square feet of geotextile was used in this
project
One layer of geotextile was placed over the geonet The geotextile was
continuously sewn on the slopes and on the pad floor areas At the completion of each
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2458 10 Page 11
day all the placed geonet was covered by geotextile in order to keep the geonet clean
The placement of the geotextile was completed on August 24 1990
CONCLUSIONS
The activities described above associated with the construction and the installation
of the geosynthetic liner of the Phase II ash monofill base were observed by Geolabs
Hawaii s personnel Based upon our observations and the results of the testing reported
herein it is Our opinion that the above mentioned liner system has been installed in
substantial compliance with the design drawings and specifications and WMII s Quality
Assurance Manual and Specification Guidelines except as noted otherwise
