DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
In general our six drilled borings indicated that about 13 to 22
feet of compressible silty sands clays and clayey silt deposits exist
below the upper 4 foot surface crust at the proposed development site
Therefore the main consideration for the foundation design would he
ground settlement caused by the building loads and or new site filling
The foundation system utilized would depend on the magnitude of the
column loads and the ability of the structure to tolerate the resulting
settlements Since the proposed building designs have not yet been
finalized and structural details are unavailable the proposed structures
are assumed to be single story structures It is our present opinion
that shallow spread or continuous footing bearing on the upper crust of
stiff clayey soil and medium dense sand appears suitable for foundation
support of the assumed light structures
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 1796 00 Page 6
Dk No 26
When new fill is placed within the site some amount of ground
settlement is anticipated due to compression of the underlying soft
ground and high groundwater levels near the ground surface However we
believe the majority of the settlements would occur fairly rapidly
because of the generally granular nature of the underlying compressible
deposits To induce the majority of ground settlements to occur first
prior to the construction of retaining wall building foundation and
on grade slab we recommend that the required new structural fill be
placed as soon as possible
Detailed discussion and recommendations are presented in the
following sections
Foundation
Since the structural details were not available at the time of this
report preparation the proposed structures are generally assumed to be
light single story structures
Our subsurface exploration encountered a generally adequate upper
crust of stiff clayey soil and medium dense sand at the anticipated
foundation elevation for the assumed light structures We recommend
shallow spread or continuous footing be used for foundation support of
the proposed light structures
An allowable net bearing capacity up to 3000 p s f may be used in
the footing design except for the proposed Pagoda structure
For assumed column loads up to 20 kips estimated spread footing
settlements are on the order of 1 inch total and 0 5 inch differential
For assumed wall loads up to 15 kips per lineal foot estimated wall
footing settlements are on the order of 1 5 inches total and 0 75 inch
differential
GEOLABS HAWAII

In general the footings should be placed at a level as high as
possible to reduce the footing load applied over the underlying
compressible deposits However it is recommended that the footing
bottom be embedded at least 18 inches below the lowest adjacent grade to
develop the design bearing capacity and to reduce the potential of
erosion or undermining of the footing The bottom of the footing
excavations should be recompacted to a minimum of 95 of its maximum dry
density as established by ASTM D 1557 78 Any soft soils encountered
should be removed and backfilled with properly compacted fill
Since the proposed Pagoda site would be surrounded by a waterway
it is our opinion that the long term soil strength at the site would be
reduced because of potential saturated or submerged conditions
Therefore a reduced allowable net bearing capacity of 1500 p s f is
recommended for footing design at the Pagoda site
About 4 feet of fill is planned at the Chinese Palace to raise the
finish floor of the structure A 4 foot high retaining wall is planned
around the Chinese Palace to retain the new fill The perimeter building
footing foundations located next to the retaining wall should be embedded
at least 4 feet thickness of new structural fill to bear on the
original grade in order to minimize large footing stresses on retaining
wall due to additional footing stresses
Settlements
Based on our findings we believe that some ground settlements may
occur due to the assumed building loads and in some areas the weight of
the new fill
Anticipated settlements under the estimated ranges of loads are
discussed in the Foundation section

It is our understanding that 4 feet of structural fill is planned
for the proposed Chinese Palace Anticipated settlement due to the 4
feet of new fill would be on the order of 1 5 to 2 0 inches Since the
compressible deposits consist mainly of loose silty sand and gravel to
soft clayey silt of limited thickness we anticipate that the settlement
would occur fairly quickly It is our opinion that the majority of the
ground settlments would occur within the first two to three months after
the full load is applied
We recommend that the settlement of the fill be monitored to
determine when the majority of the settlement has occurred and when
retaining walls and building construction can begin Settlement gauges
should be installed in the area and periodic survey readings of the
gauges should be used to monitor the actual settlement The readings
should be taken by the surveyor and transmitted to the soil engineer in
order that the actual settlement conditions can be evaluated
Slab on Grade
It should be noted that some of the on site clayey soils are
moderately expansive depending on the in situ moisture content of the
soil prior to subsequent subgrade saturation Therefore we recommend
that where on grade slabs are planned the on site clayey soil should be
over excavated down to a depth of at least twelve 12 inches below the
slab bottom The exposed subgrade should be thoroughly moistened and
then backfilled with non expansive select borrow imported soils The
thickened edge of the slab should be embedded a minimm of 12 inches
below the lowest adjacent finish grade
We recommend that a 4 inch layer of 3B Fine gravel should be
provided as the cushion fill below the slab The 3B Fine gravel layer
will also act as a capillary break To protect against future moisture
infiltration an impervious moisture membrane may be incorporated below
the slab

The on grade slabs should be structurally separated from wall
foundations
Waterway
A shallow waterway is planned to surround the Pagoda Based on the
boring information the site appears to be mantled with a 4 foot thick
crust of stiff clayey soil and the water percolation rate from the
waterway through the clayey soil to the groundwater level may be slow
Therefore we suggest that automatic shut off control for water flowing
into the waterway should be provided in order to prevent possible
overflow of water into the surrounding areas and the Pagoda An
alternative is to provide suitable spillways for control of potential
overflow
Site Preparation
Since the weight of the new fill would initiate ground settlements
it is recommended that the 4 feet of structural fill planned for the
Chinese Palace and any other planned new fill should be placed as early
as possible at the beginning of construction Settlements due to the new
fill loads should be monitored to determine when the majority of
settlement has occurred and when retaining walls and building
construction can begin
It is recommended that the existing fountain asphaltic concrete
pavements concrete curbs and planters in the proposed building areas be
removed in order to provide a uniform subgrade support for new
on grade slabs and A C pavements The subgrade soil should be scarified
to a minimum depth of 6 inches and recompacted to a minimum of 90 of its
maximum dry density as established by ASTM D 1557 78 Any loose or soft
clayey soils encountered at subgrade level should be removed and replaced
with compacted select on site granular soils on or with other suitable
imported select borrow material compacted to the above mentioned
standard

Fills and backfills should be placed in horizontal lifts not
exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90
of its maximum dry density as established by ASTM D 1557 78
Imported fill material should consist of non expansive select
granular materials less than 3 inches in maximum size with less than 20
passing the 200 sieve
Retaining Walls
It is our understanding that a 4 feet high retaining wall is planned
around the raised finished floor of the proposed Chinese Palace in the
Chinese Village At the present time the details of the proposed
retaining walls have not been firmly established The lateral earth
pressure acting on the retaining walls will depend on the type of
backfill used the extent of the backfill material and the compactive
effort required on the material behind the structure walls Granular
material such as sand is recommended for the backfill adjacent to the
structures in order to reduce the compactive effort required and to
minimize the build up of additional lateral earth pressures
For retaining structures to be built above the groundwater level
the lateral pressure that may act on the structures will mainly involve
earth pressure For structures to be built below the groundwater level
lateral pressures will include both earth and hydrostatic pressures We
believe the following guidelines may be used for preliminary retaining
wall designs

An allowable bearing value of up to 3000 p s f is recommended

A subsurface drainage system subdrains or weepholes should be

Design Review
Plans and specifications for site grading foundation and pavement

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the site grading and footing excavation be

consistent with our findings
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site for use
If conditions encountered are different from those presented in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
