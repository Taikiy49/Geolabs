Dear Mr. Iida: 
We are pleased to submit our geotechnical engineering earthwork observations and 
testing report for work performed during the site grading operation of the James B. Castle 
High School Cafeteria Expansion project in general accordance with our fee proposal dated 
June 14, 2018. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The cafeteria expansion project is located on the south of Kaneohe Bay Drive in the 
Kailua area on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii, as shown on the Project Location Map, Plate 1. 
The project encompasses approximately 12,106 square feet area, including an outdoor 
covered dining area, a fire access lane, new underground utility connections, etc., as shown 
on the Site Plan, Plate 2. 
The site grading consisted of minor cuts and fills. T. Iida Contracting, Ltd. performed 
the earthwork operations for the project. 
SITE GRADING 
At the on-set of earthwork, areas within the contract grading limits were generally 
cleared and grubbed of vegetation and deleterious materials. Materials generated from 
the clearing operations were disposed of off-site. Following the clearing and grubbing 
operations, the areas to be cut were excavated. Finished grades were compacted using 
a smooth vibratory drum roller. 
Soft and yielding areas encountered during clearing and grubbing were generally 
over-excavated to expose firm natural material, and the resulting excavation was backfilled 
with well-compacted imported granular fills.

The fill and backfill materials were moisture-conditioned to above optimum 
moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent of the maximum dry density 
as determined in accordance with ASTM D1557.  In the roadway areas, the subgrade 
was compacted to a minimum of 95 percent of the maximum dry density. 
During the site grading operations, samples of the materials utilized as fill were 
obtained and transported to our laboratory for testing.  The maximum dry densities and 
optimum moisture contents were established in general accordance with ASTM D1557 
(Modified Proctor), and the test results are summarized on Plate 3. 
Periodic in-situ moisture and density tests were conducted in general accordance 
with ASTM D6938 (Nuclear Gauge Method), to determine the adequacy of compaction of 
fills and backfills for infrastructure and utility installations, including compaction of building 
pads, walkways, and fire lane.  
The test results are summarized on Plates 4.1 through 4.6. These tests were 
performed on moisture-conditioned and compacted lifts of fills and backfills. Test locations 
and elevations were estimated using on-site survey stakes for reference. These locations 
and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods 
used. Areas deemed unsatisfactory were reworked until the measured relative 
compaction conformed to or exceeded the minimum specified for the project. 
This report summarizes our field observations and compaction testing 
services during the site grading work for the period covering June 14, 2018 to 
January 22, 2019. 
Based on our field representative’s observations and the referenced test results, it 
is our opinion that the site grading for the James B. Castle High School Cafeteria 
Expansion project, observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report, 
were performed in accordance with the general intent of the recommendations in the 
following references. 
1. 
Chapter 14 of the Revised Ordinances of Honolulu 1990, as amended. 
2. 
Standard Specifications for Public Works Construction, City and County of 
Honolulu, September 1986. 
3. 
Report by Hirata & Associates, Inc. entitled “Geotechnical Investigation, 
Cafeteria Expansion – New Dining Lanai Roof, Castle High School, Kaneohe, 
Oahu, Hawaii,” dated March 24, 2017.
