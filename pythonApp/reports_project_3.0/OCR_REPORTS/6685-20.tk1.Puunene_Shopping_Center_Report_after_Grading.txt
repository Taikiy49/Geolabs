EARTHWORK OPERATIONS
The Puunene Shopping Center development is in the Kahului area on the Island of
Maui Hawaii It is approximately bounded by Hookele Street to the north Pakaula Street
to the east open land to the south and Mokulele Highway to the west A layout of the new
shopping center is shown on the Site Plan Plate 1 Based on the information provided
we understand the site work package for this project generally will include the following
work items

Based on the existing topography at the site and the finished floor elevations for the
new buildings we envision grading consisting of cuts and fills of less than 5 feet in height
will be required to achieve the design grades
performed the site grading operations during the building pad
construction and infrastructure installation for the project The site grading work consisted
of clearing and grubbing subgrade preparation placement and compaction of fills and
backfills retaining wall footing excavation pavement construction and utility trench
excavation and backfill
Following the clearing and grubbing operations the exposed subgrades were
scarified to a minimum depth of 12 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent
above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction Compaction requirement for subgrades under pavement areas was increased
to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
The subgrades below the building slabs were over excavated where necessary to
approximately 12 inches below the bottom of the cushion fill layer to provide the
recommended 12 inch thick non expansive select granular fill layer The over excavated
subgrades were moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture
content and compacted to at least 90 percent of the maximum dry density as determined
in accordance with ASTM D1557 Modified Proctor
During placement of the non expansive select granular fill layer the fill materials
were generally placed in near level lifts brought to above the optimum moisture content
and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density determined in
accordance with ASTM D1557 Modified Proctor The base course and aggregate
subbase under the AC pavement were generally placed in near level lifts brought to
above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent of the
maximum dry density determined in accordance with ASTM D1557 Modified Proctor
During the retaining wall foundation excavation and preparation the excavated footing
bottom was generally recompacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction

New utility connections were installed to serve the new development In general a
bedding layer consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel was provided below the piping
and manholes for uniform support Free draining granular materials consisting of
No 3B Fine gravel was also used for the trench backfill up to about 12 inches above the
pipes to provide adequate support around the pipes and to reduce the compaction effort
on the initial trench backfill The trench backfill was placed in 8 inch level loose lifts and
mechanically compacted to no less than 90 percent relative compaction Where trenches
are below pavement areas the upper 3 feet of the trench backfill below the pavement
grade was compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
During the site grading operations samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our laboratory for
testing The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in
our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D1557 Modified Proctor and the test
results are summarized on the attached Plate 2
Field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations on an
as need basis to confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures
and soil conditions were noted A total of 981 field density tests were performed using the
nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM D6938 Results of the compaction tests
performed are summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 62 of this report These tests were
performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills at the
site Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site
survey stakes These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the
degree implied by the methods used

foundation excavation pavement and utility construction for the above project observed
and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report were performed in general
