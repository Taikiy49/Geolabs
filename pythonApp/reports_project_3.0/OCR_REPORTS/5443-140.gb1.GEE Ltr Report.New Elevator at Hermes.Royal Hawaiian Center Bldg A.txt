PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Hermes store is located at the northwestern corner of Building A of the Royal
Hawaiian Center in the Waikiki area of Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii Building A
was constructed of reinforced concrete and is supported on a driven concrete pile
foundation system
Based on the information provided a new elevator is planned in the new Hermes
store The elevator will be located between Gridlines 31 and 32 and H and J To reduce
differential settlement between the existing pile supported building and the new elevator
the new elevator should be supported on a deep foundation system
Because of difficulties in accessing these areas with our drilling equipment we
evaluated the subsurface soil conditions using the available boring information from the
initial design of the Royal Hawaiian Center contained in the report entitled Report of
Geotechnical Investigation Proposed Royal Hawaiian Center Kalakaua Avenue and
Lewers Street Honolulu Hawaii dated August 16 1976 prepared by LeRoy Crandall and

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Based on the boring information contained in the above mentioned report the
anticipated subsurface conditions at the site would likely consist of fill materials
extending to an elevation of about 1 foot Mean Sea Level MSL It should be noted
that boulders were encountered in the fill layer Below the fill materials medium dense
sand was encountered to an elevation of about 3 feet MSL Loose to medium dense
silty sand was encountered below the medium dense sand and extended to an
elevation of about 35 feet MSL
Below the silty sand dense cemented coralline deposits were encountered to an
elevation of about 49 feet MSL The coralline deposits graded to medium dense to an
elevation of about 95 feet MSL
Groundwater was encountered at the site at elevations ranging between 0 7 and
0 5 feet MSL It should be noted that groundwater levels could fluctuate depending on
tidal fluctuations due to the proximity of the project site to the Pacific Ocean seasonal
precipitation groundwater withdrawal and or injection and other factors
SOIL PROFILE

project site may be classified from a seismic analysis standpoint as a Stiff Soil Profile
Therefore we believe the seismic design of the new elevator may be designed based on a
Site Class D soil profile based on the International Building Code Table No
2006 Edition

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the loose to medium dense silty sand encountered below the fill layer
and to reduce the potential for differential settlement between the new elevator and the
pile supported Building A we recommend using a deep foundation system to support the
new elevator We recommend utilizing micropiles to support the new elevator due to the
limited space for the construction work and noise considerations Detailed discussions of
our geotechnical recommendations for the project are presented in the following sections
Micropiles
In general the micropile foundation system consists of a small diameter usually
less than 12 inches drilled and grouted pile with steel reinforcing The micropile
foundation typically is constructed by drilling a hole with or without casing placing
reinforcing steel in the hole and grouting the hole Micropiles are desirable because they
can be installed readily in access restrictive environments and in numerous soil types and
ground conditions In addition installation of the micropiles generally causes minimal
disturbance to adjacent structures the adjacent soils and the environment
Based on the subsurface conditions anticipated at the project site and the low
headroom conditions we estimate that a micropile system with minimum grout bulb
diameter of 5 5 inches may be used to support the new elevator Based on the
subsurface conditions anticipated at the project site we recommend designing the
5 5 inch diameter micropiles based on an allowable compressive load capacity of 30 tons
60 kips The allowable compressive load capacity of the micropiles is for supporting
as wind or seismic forces
To provide the allowable compressive load capacity of 30 tons 60 kips we
recommend extending the 5 5 inch diameter micropiles through the lagoonal deposits and
embedding the grout bulb a minimum of 27 feet into the bearing layer consisting of
medium dense to dense coralline deposits Based on the boring information we estimate
that the bearing layer starts at an elevation of approximately 35 feet MSL therefore the
tip elevations of the 5 5 inch diameter micropile would extend to approximately 62 feet
MSL The micropiles will derive its vertical support primarily from skin friction between the
grout and the surrounding coralline deposits The center reinforcing steel bar should
consist of a Grade 75 No 10 reinforcing bar with a cross sectional area of 1 27 square

To reduce the potential for caving in or necking of the loose to medium dense silty
sand which would result in less grout cover around the steel reinforcement potential
corrosion issue we recommend using a cased micropile system permanent casing
The outside diameter OD of the permanent casing should be about 5 5 inches same as
the grout bulb size The permanent casing should extend 3 feet into the bearing layer or
to approximately 38 feet MSL This extension into the bearing layer is known as the
casing plunge length The micropiles should have a minimum center to center spacing of
about 30 inches to facilitate drilling of the micropiles
Lateral Load Resistance
It should be noted that the micropile foundation system provides low lateral load
resistance due to the relatively small diameter of the micropile Therefore the
lateral load resistance contributed by the micropiles should be neglected Lateral
loads imposed on the new elevator may be resisted primarily by passive pressure
resistance against the vertical faces of the pile cap and grade beams and by shear
acting along the sides of the pile caps and grade beams Passive pressure
resistance against the vertical faces of the pile cap and grade beams may be
estimated using an equivalent fluid pressure of 350 pounds per square foot per foot
of depth pcf above the groundwater level and 175 pcf below the groundwater
level Additional resistance to lateral loads may be provided by shear resistance
between the sides of the pile caps and grade beams and the adjacent soils A side
shear resistance of up to 150 pounds per square feet psf may be used for
transient loads such as wind or seismic forces only

Micropile Foundation Settlements
Settlements of the micropile foundations will result primarily from elastic
compression of the pile member and subgrade response We estimate the total
settlement of the pile supported foundations to be 0 5 inches or less with differential
settlements not exceeding about one half of the total settlement We believe that
these settlements are essentially elastic and should occur as the loads are applied
Micropile Construction Considerations
A specialty subcontractor experienced in the construction of a cased micropile
foundation system should perform the micropile installation Based on the
subsurface conditions at the project site it should be noted that hard drilling such
as cobbles boulders and concrete may be encountered locally within the fill layer
at the site considering the urban nature of the project site In addition dense
cemented coralline deposits were encountered in the borings drilled in 1976
Therefore some difficult drilling conditions likely will be encountered at the project
site and should be expected
Because a geotechnical exploration was not performed for the expansion project
Geolabs Inc will need to perform the Special Inspection of the micropile
foundation installation during construction as required by the City and County of
Honolulu
Micropile Proof Testing
We recommend performing a proof test pullout test on one production micropile
during construction to confirm the load carrying capacity of the installed micropiles
The proof test should consist of subjecting the micropile to at least 133 percent of
the design load and the maximum test load should be held for at least 10 or
60 minutes Proof testing on the micropiles is an integral part of the design of the
micropile foundation system
