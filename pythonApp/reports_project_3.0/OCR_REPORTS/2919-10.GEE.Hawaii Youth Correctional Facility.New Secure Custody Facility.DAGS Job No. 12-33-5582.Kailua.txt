INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed New Secure Custody Facility at the Hawaii Youth Correctional
Facility HYCF within the Koolau Correctional Complex in Kailua Oahu Hawaii The
general location of the site and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The exploration was performed in general accordance with our proposal dated
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2919 10 Page 2
This report summarizes the findings of our exploration and presents our
geotechnical engineering recommendations based on the data obtained during the
exploration These recommendations are intended to provide design input for the site
grading and foundation and pavement design and may not be adequate to form the basis
for preparation of construction estimates or for bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATION
The proposed construction consists of several irregularly shaped buildings located
in an area about 400 by 400 feet in plan west of the existing Hookipa Cottage
One story structures constructed out of reinforced concrete or steel framing with
decking and CMU walls are planned
Column loads up to 80 kips are anticipated
Paved access roads to and around the proposed structures are planned A
parking area is also proposed at the eastern portion of the site
Cuts up to about six 6 feet and fills up to about five 5 feet are anticipated for the
site grading
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the general
subsurface soil conditions at the site in order to develop a generalized soil data set for
the formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to the site
preparation grading foundation design and pavements for the proposed project

SITE DESCRIPTION
The site is located about 3 000 feet southeast of the intersection of Kailua Road
and Kalanianaole Highway in Kailua Oahu Hawaii and about one 1 mile north of Mount
Olomana
The site generally slopes down in a northerly direction at an approximate gradient
of four 4 percent from an elevation of approximately 210 to approximately 190 feet
above Mean Sea Level MSL The eastern boundary of the project area is next to a
drainageway with side slope ratios varying from about ten horizontal to one vertical
10H 1V to three horizontal to one vertical 3H 1V This drainageway slopes down
towards the north at an average gradient of four 4 percent The flow line bottom
elevation varies from approximately 210 feet above MSL on the upper or southern end
to an elevation of approximately 174 feet above MSL at the lower or northern section
There are existing buildings to the north south and east of the proposed facility
Most of the project area is currently covered with grass
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The materials encountered by the fourteen 14 borings generally indicate that the
site is underlain by residual soils developed from the in situ weathering of Koolau Basalts
The boring and laboratory test data indicate that the residual soils consist of very stiff to
hard brown clayey silts with in situ water contents ranging from about 35 to 50 percent
within depths up to approximately 16 5 feet below the existing ground surface Below the
16 5 foot depth the in situ water contents generally range from about 40 to 65 percent
Groundwater was not encountered in any of the fourteen 14 borings within the
drilled depths at the time of our exploration

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATION
General
It is Our opinion that the proposed project is feasible from a geotechnical point of
view provided that the recommendations presented in this report are closely followed in
design and during construction
Our exploration encountered very stiff to hard near surface deposits of residual
clayey silt with moderate swelling potential It appears that these soils are generally
competent and will provide adequate bearing to support the proposed one story buildings
on spread or continuous footing foundations provided that our recommendations
presented in this report are followed
Site Grading
Our current understanding based on the site plan furnished by DAGS is that the
project site will be graded to form level areas at different elevations stepping up in a
south to north direction This grading concept will involve cuts up to six 6 feet and fills
up to five 5 feet

Site Preparation
Prior to commencement of any earthwork all areas within the grading limits should
be cleared of vegetation and deleterious materials The resulting grubbed and
spoil material should be properly disposed off site or stockpiled for use in
landscaping Any soft weak or yielding areas exposed during the clearing
operations should be over excavated to firm ground and then backfilled with well
compacted engineered fill
Fill Material
Areas requiring fill should be scarified to a depth of six 6 inches moisture
conditioned and recompacted to 90 percent of the maximum dry density as
determined in accordance with procedures described in ASTM Test Method
D 1557 78 Any loose or soft spots encountered during re compaction should be
over excavated down to firm subsoils and then replaced with compacted
engineered fill material
Generally the on site soils appear suitable for use as engineered fill Since most
of the on site soils are fine grained they should be compacted by mechanical
compaction as compaction by water jetting or ponding is not suitable
lf additional imported material is required it should be selected non expansive
material which contains no fragments larger than three 3 inches in maximum
dimension with less than 25 percent passing the 200 sieve and a Plasticity Index
less than 20 as determined in accordance with methods described in ASTM Test
Method D 4318 84 Imported material should be free of organic matter debris and
other deleterious material and should be compacted to 90 percent of their
GEOLABS HAWAII

maximum dry density as determined in accordance with procedures described in
ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Fill Placement
Our exploration and laboratory testing indicate that the on site soils have in situ
moisture contents generally higher than optimum moisture content for compaction
These high in situ moisture contents reduce the workability of soils and frequently
necessitate double or triple handling of the soils to reduce the moisture to a level
where the standard 90 percent compaction may be readily achieved Previous
experience in similar areas with relatively high rainfall and high in situ soil moisture
contents indicates that a reduction of the level of compaction to 85 percent can still
provide adequate bearing strength to support relatively light structures Therefore
to facilitate earthwork construction for this project we are recommending a
reduced compaction criterion of 85 percent of the maximum dry density as
indicated above
All fills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding ten 10 inches in loose
thickness and compacted to a minimum of 85 percent of the maximum dry density
as determined in accordance with procedures described in ASTM Test Method
D 1557 78 Fill placement should commence at the lowest point and continue
upward in level compacted layers
Any fill placed on existing slopes steeper than five horizontal to one vertical 5H 1V
should be keyed and benched to bond the new fill to the existing subsoils This
is graphically illustrated on Plate 3 Sketch of Keying and Benching

Cut areas designated as structural or pavement areas should be excavated to
subgrade level then scarified and recompacted a minimum of 85 percent of its
maximum dry density as indicated above
Two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V cut and fill slopes may be used for the
project Fill slopes should be constructed starting from the bottom and by
overtilling past design slope then cutting back
Water should be diverted away from the tops of slopes and slope planting utilized
to reduce erosion
Drainage benches should be provided for slopes having a vertical height greater
than 20 feet
Building Foundations
It is Our opinion that spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the
proposed buildings An allowable bearing value up to 3000 p s f may be used for
foundations bearing on the residual soil and on fill placed as recommended above
These bearing values are for total dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third
1 3 when temporary wind and seismic loads are considered Estimated footing
settlements are on the order of less than one 1 inch total and one half 2 of an inch
differential
Individual spread footings and continuous wall footings should be embedded to a
minimum depth of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade It is
recommended that the building foundations be located away from the top of fill or cut

bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility lines

Slabs On Grade
Concrete slabs on grade are feasible for the floors of the proposed buildings A
four inch cushion layer consisting of 3B Fine gravel should be provided below the slabs
The slab subgrades should be kept moist prior to placement of the concrete
Where slabs are situated partly on cut and partly on fill there is a potential for
some slab cracking due to the difference in stiffness between the two 2 materials
In order to reduce the differential settlement between portions of the slab situated
partly on cut and partly on fill we recommend that the cut portion be over excavated for
a minimum depth of one foot below the slab bottom and replaced with properly
compacted select material The fill portion below the slab should also be capped with a
minimum of one foot of properly compacted select material
In order to protect against future moisture infiltration and subsequent damage to
floor coverings provision of an impervious moisture barrier is recommended below the
slabs As required by the State a two inch thick layer of Basaltic Termite Barrier BTB
material should be provided above the impervious moisture barrier and a four inch thick
layer of BTB material should be provided below the impervious moisture barrier
Since the project site is in a high rainfall area it is recommended that a four inch
cushion layer of 3B Fine gravel be provided between the BTB layers to provide a
capillary break A typical detail is shown on Plate 4
The slabs on grade should have thickened edges which extend down a minimum
of 12 inches below the outside finish grade surface elevations

Drainage
The finished grade outside the building should be shaped to shed water away from
foundations and to avoid ponding conditions Also it is recommended that gutter
systems be installed on the proposed buildings and that the discharge be diverted away
from the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near foundations
and slabs should also be avoided
Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have concrete bottoms and
drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are important for the proper performance of the
above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause excessive subsur
face soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation
excavations should be properly backfilled tight against the wall or slab footings
immediately after setting of the concrete to prevent development of ponding conditions
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and maintained to drain
all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundations
Utility Trenches
New utility lines are anticipated for the project A granular bedding consisting of
six 6 inches of 3B Fine gravel or ASTM C 33 No 67 gravel is recommended under
pipes
The backfill from the bedding up to about one foot above the pipes should be
granular material such as 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 coral sand or cinder
which does not require excessive tamping On site soils or select material may be used
for the remaining trench backfill

The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level one foot above the pipes to
finished grade should be placed in maximum eight inch horizontal lifts and mechanically
compacted to a minimum of 85 percent of its maximum dry density as determined in
accrodance with procedures described in ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 to reduce
potential ground subsidence

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that a geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during construction of the site work excavation
foundation pavement and subgrade preparation phases of the work This is to observe
compliance with the design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow
design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from those anticipated at
the time of preparation of this report The recommendations given in this report are
contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and approved by
the geotechnical engineer prior to delivery to the site
If actual exposed rock soil encountered during construction are different from
those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to the design
should be made
