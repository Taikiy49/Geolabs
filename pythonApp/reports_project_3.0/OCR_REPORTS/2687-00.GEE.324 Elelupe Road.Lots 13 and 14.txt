INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed residential development at 324 Elelupe Road in Kuliouou
Valley on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general project area is shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings of our field exploration and presents our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from analyses of data obtained during
the exploration These recommendations are intended for preliminary design input but
are not intended for construction cost estimation or bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is situated on a hillside at the base of the westerly side of Kuliouou
Valley The site consists of two 2 contiguous lots which had previously been used for
residential purposes It is proposed to construct new residential structures on these two
2 lots

lt was anticipated that the site would be found to be underlain by potentially
unstable highly expansive adobe clay soils Planning and design of the proposed
development is contingent upon the findings of this geotechnical engineering exploration
Therefore this report was prepared prior to the availability of plans for the proposed
development However it is anticipated that the future development will consist of lightly
loaded one to two story residential buildings and related site improvements

The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface

geotechnical engineering recommendations pertaining to the feasibility of the proposed
residential development

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
eneral
Based on our analyses of the data obtained from our field exploration and
laboratory testing we believe that the two 2 subject lots are suitable for residential
development provided that the geotechnical engineering recommendations presented in
this report are followed closely in the design and construction of the project
In general we recommend that the lot perimeters be stabilized by construction of
adequately embedded retaining walls and or buttress fills In addition the future building
and pavement subgrades of highly expansive adobe clay should be over excavated and
replaced with a three 3 foot minimum non expansive capping backfill Spread footing
foundations with specially prepared subgrades may then be used to support the future
buildings on the stabilized lots
It is both critical and imperative that adequate drainage be provided to divert
surface runoff away from the lots to reduce the potential of water infiltration into the adobe
subsoils which may swell significantly and also lose significant strength upon wetting
and or saturation

Stabilization of Lot Perimeters
Currently the lots perimeter slopes are either steeply graded or supported by
existing retaining walls that are already cracked and bulged Since the adobe subsoil may
lose significant strength upon weiting the long term stability of the perimeter slopes and
the deteriorating retaining walls is subject to question unless remedial measures are
implemented to stabilize these perimeter areas Therefore stabilization of the slopes
along lot perimeters is a critical design consideration in developing the lots
Various schemes of retaining walls and buttress fill slopes were evaluated for
stabilization of the lot perimeters The stability evaluations were performed by using the
computer program STABRG originally developed by the University of California at
Berkeley In performing the analyses it was assumed that the adobe subsoil would be
wetted or saturated and appropriate residual strength parameters were selected for use
Retaining Structures
Based on our analyses it appears that if new retaining structures are keyed into
the underlying soft coral formation the lot perimeters may be stabilized in the long
term

feet into the underlying soft coral formation If constructed of concrete rubble
masonry the rubble material used in the walls should be adequately bonded so
that each wall will function as a solid integral structural unit to minimize local

Building Foundations
Based on our analyses we believe that shallow spread and or continuous footing
foundations may be used to support the anticipated residential buildings if the lots are
properly stabilized along the perimeters
However to minimize the potential of structural distress resulting from the swelling
of the highly expansive soils encountered at the site we recommend that the subgrades
of any future buildings slabs and pavements be over excavated a minimum of three 3
feet The over excavated subgrade should be moisture conditioned to about 2 on the
wet side of its optimum moisture content before being recompacted to about 90 of its
maximum dry density as determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 After this
moisture conditioning and recompaction the over excavated area should be backfilled

and capped with a non expansive select granular fill material such as coral crusher
waste or base course
We recommend that the spread footings be embedded a minimum of 18 inches
below the lowest adjacent finished grades

of up to 2500 p s f may be used for the footing design This bearing value may be
increased by up to one third 1 3 for considerations of seismic loads wind loads and
other short term loadings

Building Slab On Grade
Based on the use of the recommended three 3 foot non expansive capping

Site Drainage
Since the lots are located at the base of a hillside large quantities of surface water
runoff may be generated during heavy rain falls and or storms and flow down rapidly from
the steep terrain behind the lots Therefore it is essential to intercept and divert the
runoff from the upslope sides of the lots to reduce the potential of water infiltration into
the highly expansive adobe subsoil and to reduce potential erosional damage
We recommend that a professional civil engineer be retained to evaluate and to
design an adequate drainage system for the future development at the lots in order to
achieve the above goals At a minimum it appears that an adequately sized open
concrete ditch might be needed along or in the vicinity of the rear property lines of the
lots However the final drainage system should be designed by the civil engineering
consultant upon further evaluation of the drainage requirements for the proposed
development

Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by the drilling and sampling
of thirteen 13 borings to depths ranging between 1 8 and 46 5 feet below the existing
ground surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings
were drilled by portable and truck mounted rotary drill rigs advancing solid stem
continuous flight augers or rotary core drilling tools as appropriate
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Method D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by
driving either a standard split spoon sampler or a 3 0 inch O D Modified California
sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches or a shorter penetration when encountering refusal are shown
on the boring logs at the appropriate sample depths
Core samples of rock materials encountered at the site were obtained using
diamond core drilling techniques in general accordance with ASTM Standard Practice D
2113 83 Diamond Core Drilling for Site Investigation Core drilling is a rotary drilling
method which uses a hollow bit to cut into the rock formation The rock material left in
the annulus of the bit is mechanically recovered for examination classification and testing
Recovery REC is used as a subjective guide to the interpretation of the relative
quality of rock masses Recovery is defined as the actual length of material recovered
from a coring attempt vs the length of the core attempt e g if 3 7 feet of material is
recovered from a 5 0 foot core run the recovery would be 74 and would be shown on
the Boring Logs as REC 74
The Rock Quality Designation RQD is also a subjective guide to the relative
quality of rock masses RQD is defined as the percentage of the core run that is sound
material that is in excess of four 4 inches in length without any discontinuities
discounting any drilling induced fractures or breaks if 2 5 feet of sound material is
recovered from a 5 0 foot core run the RQD would be 50 and would be shown on the
Boring Logs as REC 50 Generally the following is used to describe the relative
quality based on the Practical Handbook of Physical Properties of Rocks and Minerals
Rock Quality RGD
Very Poor 0 25
Poor 25 50
Fair 50 75
Good 75 90
Excellent 90 100

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Laboratory testing was performed on selected samples obtained during our field
exploration A laboratory testing program was developed to schedule appropriate tests
to aid in determining pertinent engineering properties of the soil and or rock materials
sampled
Classification Tests
Frequently the classification of a material provides a correlative index to certain
engineering properties of the material
Moisture Content ASTM Test Method D 2216 80 and Unit Weight Determinations
were used as an aid to soil classification The moisture content expressed as a
percentage of the dry weight of the tested sample is shown at the appropriate sample
depths on the Boring Logs
One 1 Atterberg Limits Test ASTM Test Method D 4318 84 was used to
determine the plasticity of the fine grained portion of soil The Plasticity Index of the
material is used to classify the soil under the Unified Soil Classification System The
results of the test are shown at the appropriate sample depth on the Boring Log and also
on Plate B 1
Physical Property Tests
These tests are performed to develop a correlative index of certain physical
parameters of a soil The physical parameters may be used in engineering analyses
pertaining to the performance or suitability of a soil
Nineteen 19 one inch ring swell tests were performed to evaluate the swelling
potential of soils under structural loads The tests aid in evaluating the materials for use
as foundation support and embankment These test results are summarized on Plates
B 2 through B 5
Two 2 Direct Shear tests ASTM Test Method D 3080 72 were used to determine
the angles of internal friction and the coefficients of cohesion in selected soil samples
These data were used in various engineering calculations The results of the direct shear
tests are presented on Plates B 6 through B 7
