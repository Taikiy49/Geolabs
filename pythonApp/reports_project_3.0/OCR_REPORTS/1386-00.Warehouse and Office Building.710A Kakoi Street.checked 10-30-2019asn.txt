Soil Conditions
Based on the boring drilled the subsurface soils generally
consisted of 3 inches of asphalt concrete over sandy gravel to 1 5 feet
deep underlain by very stiff sandy and clay silt fill to about 5 feet
Below the fill from 5 to 12 feet was a layer of coral sand and gravel
which graded from dense to loose with depth underlain by loose dark gray
Silty sand with silt organic clay to 27 feet The material then graded
to very soft black clayey silt with some organic matter including traces
of shell fragments and decomposed wood to a depth of 124 feet probably
underlain by dense sand For more detailed description of the soils

Discussion and Recommendations
So acc ommendations
It was noted that the gap between the southern boundary wall of the
Property and the northern wall of the adjoining property was wide at the
base and became narrower at the top These walls were about 30 feet
high Numerous diagonal cracks were noted in the exposed northern walls
of the adjoining property It appeared that either or both building
walls had tilted
Our boring disclosed that underlying the surface 7 foot thick crust
were very thick soft loose sediments and probably highly compressible
Thus the tilting of the existing building walls may have been caused by
differential ground settlements However laboratory consolidation test
was not included in our scope of work and therefore the compressi
bilities of various soft loose sediments could not be examined The
tilting may also have been caused by eccentric loading on the building
wall foundations
To better evaluate potential future building settlements the
compressibilities and thickness of various soils should be examined by a
series of laboratory testings Additional borings should be made at
different locations to check the variations in the compressibilities as
well as the thickness of the soil layers The building loading
conditions should also be analyzed
The potential on going continuous settlements of the building
should be monitored by periodically taking elevation readings of
permanent points on the buildings
