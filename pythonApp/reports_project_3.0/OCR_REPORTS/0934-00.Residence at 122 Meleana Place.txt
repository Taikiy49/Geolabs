DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that the site is underlain by
a surface layer of gray brown moderately expansive clay over
stiff brown clayey silt The thickness of the upper clay depo
Sit is generally thin in the eastern side of the site However
Boring No 2 drilled in the western portion of the site en
countered the clay to a depth of about 15 feet
Due to the variable soil conditions encountered and the proxi
mity of the proposed house to the existing steep soil bank
deeper than normal foundations are recommended for the proposed
residence
Foundations
Post and beam construction utilizing individual spread footings
are recommended for the proposed house due to the following
considerations

The near surface soils are moderately expansive when allowed
to dry Deeper foundations are required to reduce the dry
ing and future heaving of the soils below the foundations
Slab on grade would be more susceptible to heaving pressures
due to the relatively light loads on the slab
The proposed residence will be situated close to the top of
a steep soil bank Deeper pier type foundations are re
quired to reduce foundations movement due to downhill slope
creep Slab on grade would require additional fill in this
lowlying area and this would increase the risk of future
foundation movement due to slope creep
Variable soil conditions were encountered at the site The
borings generally encountered stiff subsoils however Boring
No 2 encountered a pocket of soft to medium soil at about
5 foot depth Post and beam construction would be better
able to tolerate some differential settlement due to
variable subsoil conditions
Footings within 10 feet of the top of the steep soil bank should
be embedded a minimum of 3 feet below the ground surface to re
duce movement due to downhill slope creep and erosion
The remaining footings away from the top of bank should be embed
ded a minimum of 18 inches below the ground surface This embed
ment is recommended due to the soft ground surface conditions at
the site and the moderately expansive nature of the soils

General guidelines for the wall foundation design are as
follows
1 The wall foundations should bear on the stiff natural
soil An allowable bearing value up to 2000 p s f is
recommended for footings bearing on the stiff natural
ground
2 Well graded granular material such as base course rock
or 3B Fine should be used to backfill behind the walls
3 Weepholes or a subsurface drainage system should be used
to minimize the build up of hydrostatic pressures behind
the walls
4 The walls should be designed to resist a lateral equi
valent fluid pressure of 40 p c f Additional load due
to sloping surcharge should be included where applicable
5 A friction factor of 0 4 can be utilized to determine
the sliding resistance of the wall Additionally a
passive soil resistance of 250 p c f can be utilized
The passive resistance should be reduced by one third when
combined with the frictional resistance

operations be observed by a soils engineer during construction

subsurface investigation

and approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon
the above inspections
