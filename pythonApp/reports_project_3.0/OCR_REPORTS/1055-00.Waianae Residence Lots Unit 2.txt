This report presents the results of our percolation testing
for the above referenced project
The purpose of this investigation is to obtain preliminary
subsurface soil conditions at the site in order to evaluate
the in situ soil seepage characteristics for private sewage
disposal system design and construction
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located in Waianae Valley about 65 miles
northeast of Farrington Highway between Waianae Valley Road
and Paheehee Ridge The site is bounded to the west by Puna
hauala Street and Kaupuni Stream to the north by a dairy to
the south and east by the toe of Paheehee Ridge The site is
relatively flat with 119 graded house lots and 7 asphaltic
concrete roadways The ground surface is gently sloping

towards the south with ground elevations ranging from 230 to
185 feet above sea level
Three 4 inch diameter auger test borings were drilled to a
depth of 15 to 25 feet below the existing ground surface at
Lot and 115 of Unit 2 Subdivision The borings
were then extended to 25 to 30 foot depths using rotary wash
drilling to reduce side smearing The drilling operation
was monitored by our geologist who also logged the borings
The boring logs are presented on Plate A 1 through A 3
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface exploration encountered a surface fill layer
of brown clayey silt with traces of gravels from 1 0 to 7 5
feet deep underlain by clayey silt cobbles and boulders
to about 25 to 30 feet the maximum depth drilled No ground
water level was encountered in the test borings at the time
of our field exploration
PERCOLATION TESTS
Falling head percolation tests were performed in the three
drill holes
After completion of the bore hole drilling each hole was
filled with water and allowed to saturate Each bore hole

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the percolation test results from the three drilled
bore holes on it is our opinion that the under
lying soils at the site could provide adequate percolation
rates for a properly designed private cesspool sewage dis
posal system
Based on the percolation test data obtained in the drilled
holes a 6 foot diameter cesspool extending to a minimum depth
of about 25 feet could provide a sufficient seepage rate for
private cesspool disposal
