SOIL CONDITIONS
Geology
The area in which the site is located represents a portion of
the broad Honolulu plain that covers most of the city This
plain was principally developed from ancient reef formations
during a higher stand of the sea together with the accumula
tion of older and younger alluvium soil mass transported from
higher elevations In certain areas of the Honolulu plain
secondary volcanic deposits are encountered stemming from
secondary eruptions like Diamond Head Tantalus Punchbowl
etc
Geologically the site is mapped to lie near a contact
separating secondary volcanic deposits of cinders pumice
ash etc and unconsolidated marine calcareous sediments
Both of these deposits are usually found to be covered by a
surficial layer of alluvium clayey soils
The borings themselves revealed a surficial layer of brown
clayey soils underlain by black cinders black sand commonly

associated with the Tantalus series of eruptions This
essentially confirms the mapped description of the area and
probably at greater depths one would encounter the other types
of deposits mentioned above associated with the Honolulu
plain
Subsurface Conditions
A total of three borings were drilled at the site Boring
No 1 and No 3 were drilled to a depth of 20 5 feet Boring
No 2 was drilled to a depth of 41 5 feet These borings were
used to explore the subsurface soil conditions and to obtain
samples for laboratory testing Based on the information
obatined from these borings the site is generally underlain
by the following materials
1 A surface layer of brown clayey silt soils ranging in
depth from 13 feet in boring No 1 to 18 5 feet in boring
No 2 This soil layer was generally stiff to hard and
tended to grade with sand at deeper depths
2 Underlying the upper brown soil layer is a black cinder
black sand formation which extends to the bottom of all
the borings
This black sand has medium to dense density and was encounter
ed at depths of 18 feet in borings No 1 No 2
No 3 respectively

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the results of our field exploration laboratory
testing and the results of our engineering analysis the site
could be developed as planned
Fairly competent materials consisting of clayey silt were
encountered underlying the site The stiff clayey silt could
provide the desired foundation support
For the 10 story structure shallow spread footings bearing
on the undisturbed clayey silt formation could be used
Additional recommendations for the design of foundations and
construction are presented in the following paragraphs
Foundation
For the proposed structure spread footing foundations are

recommended All footings should be founded on undisturbed
competent clayey silt material underlying the site below the
half basement level
An allowable bearing pressure of 4 500 pounds per square
foot could be used to design the footings found on compe
tent clayey silt material as described above A one third
increase may be used to allow for infrequently applied wind
and seismic loads
The footings should have a minimum width of 12 inches Due
to the presence of low expansive near soils we also recommend
that the footings be embedded a minimum of 24 inches below
the lowest adjacent grade and on the above recommended clayey
silt layer For the perimeter retaining wall footing the
embedment requirement could be reduced to 18 inches if a
lower bearing value of up to 3 000 P S F is required
For the above recommendation it is estimated that the total
and differential settlements will be about one quarter of an
inch
We recommend that all the footing excavations be inspected by
the soils engineer to confirm the conditions upon which our
recommendations are based Any loose areas encountered during
excavation should be removed and replaced with either concrete
or granular material compacted to a minimum of 95 percent

maximum density per ASTM D 1557 70 test method
Design of Basement Walls
Basement walls below grade will be subject to horizontal
earth pressure imposed by granular backfill material It is
recommended that the lateral earth pressures induced on
basement walls that are restrained from deflection be
designed for an equivalent fluid pressure of 45 pounds per
cubic foot and 35 pounds per cubic foot for walls that are
free to deflect
Resistance to lateral loads may be provided by a combination
of passive pressure and friction Passive pressure may be
taken as that exerted by a fluid of density equal to 350
pounds per cubic foot Friction may be taken as normal dead
load times a coefficient of friction factor equal to 0 40
A combination of friction and passive pressure may be used
provided that passive pressure is reduced by one third
Basement Parking
Slab on grade may be used for basement parking
Slab on grade should be placed over a 4 inch cushion layer
of granular rock gravel material such as 3B Fine rock to
act as a capillary break Proof rolling of the subgrade
material is recommended prior to gravel placement
The slab on grade should be separated from walls and foun
dations

Because of the low expansive nature of the near surface soil
of about 3 percent the base course layer for the any on grade
parking ramp should be increased to 6 inch minimum All
subgrade soil should be kept moist prior to concrete
placement
Earthwork
Earthwork operations at the site will include excavation
backfilling and foundation inspection during construction
The proposed basement excavation would extend to depths of
approximately 4 0 feet below the existing ground surface
It is anticipated that conventional earthmoving equipments
could be used to conduct all the excavations for the basement
and foundations
Excavations should be protected from surface runoff which may
enter the excavations All loose soils or mud should be
removed from the bottom of all footing excavations prior to
concrete placement
Backfilling
Some minor backfilling operations would be required around
the footings and behind the basement walls Granular non
expansive or select on site material such as sand or cinders
could be used for backfilling around footings

However boring No 3 encountered about 5 5 feet surface of
potentially expansive clayey soil and would be removed during
the basement excavation of about up to 4 0 feet This portion
of the excavated material in the general area of boring No 3
should not be used for structural backfill purposes
Fill and backfill should be placed in horizontal 10 inch loose
lifts and compacted to at least 95 percent of maximum dry
density within the limits of structures and 90 percent outside
the structure areas as obtained using ASTM D 1557 70 modified
Proctor standard
Subsurface drainage system or weepholes should be provided to
prevent the build up of hydrostatic pressure behind the walls
Foundation Inspection
It is recommended that all the footing excavations and filling
operations be inspected by the soils engineer to verify
adequate compaction and that the subsurface conditions are
consistent with the findings of our subsurface investigation
Additionally all the imported fill material if required
should be tested and inspected by a soil engineer before
bringing it to the job site
Our present recommendations are contingent upon the above
inspection and testing
