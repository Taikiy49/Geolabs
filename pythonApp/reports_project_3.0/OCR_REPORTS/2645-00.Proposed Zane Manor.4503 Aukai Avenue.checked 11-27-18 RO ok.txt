INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of a geotechnical engineering exploration and
analysis performed for the proposed Zane Manor located at 4503 Aukai Avenue in the
Kahala district on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location of the site and its
vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
Based on the preliminary plans provided to us the proposed residence will consist
of a two 2 story building with a swimming pool vehicular driveway and provision for
vehicular parking
Concrete ground floor slab on grade construction is planned for the proposed two
2 story building
The site is essentially level cut and fill operations for site grading will be minimal
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this report is to provide geotechnical engineering recommendations
pertinent to the building foundations swimming pool and pavement design for the
proposed residence The recommendations presented herein are based on the soil
information obtained from borings drilled and laboratory testing performed on selected
samples obtained at the site
The study consisted of drilling and sampling three 3 borings laboratory testing
engineering analyses and report preparation The approximate locations of the drilled
borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests which were utilized in the
engineering analyses to formulate our recommendations are presented in the appendices
of this report
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations

EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is located at 4503 Aukai Avenue in the Kahala district of Honolulu
This rectangular 10 905 square foot lot is located at the southeastern corner of the
intersection of Aukai Avenue and Kala Street Developed residential lots are located on
both the south and east sides of the lot forming the subject of this report The general
location of the site and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The topography of the project site is generally level with current ground elevations
varying from about 7 3 to 7 7 feet above Mean Sea Level MSL
Residential structures previously existing on the site had been demolished and the
site had been cleared at the time of our field exploration
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling three 3 borings to
depths of 20 1 to 21 5 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate locations
of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general the borings disclosed that the site is underlain by a surface layer
approximately six 6 feet thick of loose to medium dense sand Below the surface sand
layer medium dense to very loose gravelly sand with silt and sandy coral gravel deposits
were encountered Medium dense to dense moderately cemented sand and silty gravel
was encountered at approximately of 18 to 21 5 feet below the existing ground surface
the maximum depth of the drilled borings
GEOLABS HAWAII

Groundwater level was encountered in the drilled borings at depths of 5 1 to 5 5
feet below the existing ground surface during our field exploration Based on elevations
estimated from the available topographic survey map the measured groundwater level
varied from elevations of 1 5 to 1 9 feet above MSL Due to the proximity of the site to
the ocean it is anticipated that the groundwater level will fluctuate in response to daily
tidal variations seasonal tides and storm surges
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration encountered a surface layer approximately six 6 feet thick
comprising loose to medium dense sand Below the surface sand layer medium dense
to very loose gravelly sand with silt and sandy coral gravel deposits were encountered
Medium dense to dense moderately cemented sand and silty gravel was encountered at
approximate depths of 18 to 21 5 feet the maximum depth of the drilled borings
It is Our opinion that the proposed project is feasible from a geotechnical
engineering point of view provided that the recommendations presented below are
followed
Foundations
Shallow spread and continuous footing foundations may be used to support the
proposed structure
An allowable bearing value of 2 000 p s f can be used for footings bearing on
compacted in situ sand or compacted engineered embankment A footing embedment
of eighteen 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade is recommended
Deeper footing embedment is generally not recommended due to the high groundwater
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2645 00 Page 5
level at the site and the underlying medium dense to very loose sandy silty coral gravel
encountered in our drilled borings
We recommend that the bearing surface of all footing excavations should be
compacted to obtain a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density as determined in
accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
If loose sand lenses or pockets are encountered at the bottom of any footing
excavation they should be over excavated to firm in situ sand The resulting depression
should be backfilled with select granular material compacted to a minimum of 90 of its
maximum dry density as determined in accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
If a footing is located next to a utility line the utility trench backfill should be
carefully monitored and adequately compacted to reduce the potential for subsidence of
the footing due to settlement of the trench backfill
Surface run off should be diverted away from the foundations to avoid ponding of
the water that could adversely affect the bearing capacity of the soils below the
foundations
Provided the above recommendations are followed total spread footing settlement
for column loads up to fifty 60 kips is estimates to be less than one 1 inch and
continuous footing settlement for wall loads up to three 3 kips per linear foot is
estimated to be less than one half 0 5 of an inch

Slab On Grade
The ground floor of the proposed residence is anticipated to be concrete
slab on grade construction
A four 4 inch thick concrete slab on top of a cushion layer of four 4 inch
minimum thickness of 3B Fine gravel or ASTM C 33 No 67 gravel is recommended
In order to protect against moisture infiltration a plastic vapor barrier should be
incorporated below the slab One 1 inch of moist fine sand may be placed above the
plastic membrane to help concrete curing
The perimeter thickened edges of the concrete slab should be embedded a
minimum of eighteen 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade
Concrete Pavements
It is our understanding that concrete pavements will be provided for the proposed
driveway and parking stalls serving the new residence
Based on the assumption that anticipated traffic will be composed of light
passenger and service vehicles the following pavement section is recommended
6 Inch Concrete
4 Inch Base Course 95 Compaction
10 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
on Compacted Subgrade
The thickened edges of the concrete driveway should be a minimum of 9 inch
thick

Additional lateral earth pressures due to effect of adjacent surcharge or foundation
loads should be considered where applicable
Scheduling of construction of the swimming poo should be considered as it relates
to the schedule for foundation construction We feel that the swimming pool should be
constructed prior to construction of footings in the immediate vicinity to preclude possible
loss of support of those footings which would be otherwise caused by dewatering and
excavation for construction of the swimming pool
We recommend that the backfill behind the swimming pool walls should consist of
compacted granular material such as select borrow or 3B Fine gravel or ASTM C 33
No 67 gravel compacted to a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density as
determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Site Grading
Prior to construction the site should be cleared and grubbed Any deleterious
materials such as vegetation debris rubble and organic matter resulting from clearing
and grubbing operations should be removed and properly disposed of off site
The proposed building and pavement areas should be scarified to a depth of six
6 inches The scarified subgrade should be thoroughly moisture conditioned and
recompacted to obtain a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density as determined in
accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 To minimize disturbance to adjacent
existing residences and to minimize potential pore water pressure build up in sandy
subsoils causing possible soil weakening use of heavy vibratory compaction equipment
should be avoided
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2645 00 Page 9
Cesspool structure for on site sewage disposal may be common for old dwellings
in the vicinity If cesspool is encountered during site clearing and grubbing the cesspool
should be properly emptied cleaned and backfilled with self compacting granular
materials such as 3B Fine gravel or equivalent
All imported fill and backfill materials that may be required should be tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to being transported to the site
The fills should be placed in loose horizontal lifts not exceeding 10 inches loose
thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density as
determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
The site should be graded to provide drainage of surface run off away from all
foundations
Drainage
The finished lot grade outside the footings slabs and pavements should be
shaped to shed water away from foundations to avoid ponding conditions Also it is
advised that a gutter system should be installed around the residence and that the gutter
run off water be diverted as far away from the foundations and pavements as feasible
Excessive landscape watering near foundations should also be avoided
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have concrete bottoms and
drains to prevent water from entering the foundation and slab subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
foundations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation and subsequent
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2645 00 Page 10
heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled
tight against the footings and slabs immediately after setting of the concrete to prevent
ponding conditions
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained to drain
all surface run off away from the foundations and slabs
Design Review
Drawings and specifications for site grading utility and foundation construction
should be forwarded to the project geotechnical engineer for review and written
comments prior to advertisement for bids This review is needed to determine adherence
to the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this review is not
made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for misinterpretation of the
recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the footing excavations and backfilling be observed by a
competent geotechnical engineer during construction to see that adequate bearing has
been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of our
exploration
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and approved
by the engineer prior to being transported to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the above
observations

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling three 3
borings to depths of 20 1 to 21 5 feet below the existing ground surface at the
approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 A truck mounted drill rig was
used to drill the borings
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who continuously monitored the drilling
operations The classifications were further reviewed by visual inspection and testing
in the laboratory The soils were classified in conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A graphic presentation of the materials encountered is
presented on the Boring Logs plates A 1 through A 3
Soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving a 2 inch O D standard
penetration sampler or a 2 4 inch D split barrel sampler with a 140 pound hammer
falling 30 inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the last 12 inches are shown
on the Boring Logs at the appropriate sample depths
