PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The existing concrete crib wall is located in the vicinity of 3798 Tantalus Drive and
adjacent to the Honolulu Watershed Forest Reserve 12 19 The crib wall
appears to support the northern side of the road and is about 15 to 20 feet high as shown
on the attached Site Plan Plate 2
It is desired to evaluate alternatives for the repair or replacement of the crib wail
system The purpose of this study is to determine an appropriate repair or replacement
solution Final design of the selected alternative will be done under separate contract
Borings were not drilled for this study Evaluations were made based on the
topographic survey site observations and available general subsurface information only
The existing asphalt concrete roadway above the crib wail ranges in width from
about 22 to 28 feet The roadway currently supports one lane of traffic in each direction
Low concrete rubble masonry CRM walls are present at the edge of the existing road
We understand that utility lines are not present at the project site except for a telephone
conduit running along the existing roadway

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The Island of Oahu is composed largely of the weathered remnants of two extinct
shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae forms the bulk of
the western third of the island while the younger shield Koolau forms the majority of the
eastern two thirds of the island It is believed that Waianae became extinct while Koolau
was still active and its eastern flank is partialiy buried beiow the Kooiau iavas in ceniral
Oahu
After a long period of volcanic inactivity during which time erosion incised deep
valleys into the Koolau shield volcanic activity returned to the Island of Oahu with a series
of lava flows followed by cinder and tuff cone formations These recent volcanic series are
referred to as the Honolulu Volcanic Series The Tantalus Crater is one such extinct
cinder cone formation and is composed mainly of sandy black cinders and ash deposits
The project site is located on the southwestern flank of the Tantalus Crater

SITE OBSERVATIONS
The existing crib wall is constructed using a framework of concrete beams about
6 to 6 5 feet in length with a square cross section about 6 inches by 6 inches The open
space between the concrete beams at the face of the wall is about 6 inches The crib
framing was backfilled with soil Based on our site observations of the soil exposed at the
wall face it appears that the backfill within the cribs may consist of voicanic cinder and ash
materials
The site is heavily overgrown but visual observations indicate that much of the
backfill within the crib wall is missing The missing backfill within the crib wall is most
prevalent in the upper portions of the wall and near the wall face where surface water
erosion may have occurred Near the upper portions of the wall vegetation and glass
bottles were observed within the concrete cribs at the wall face Below the existing
concrete spillway the majority of the crib wall backfill have been eroded away In addition
several trees were observed growing out from the wall face Since the stability of a crib
wall retaining system depends on the weight of the structure the backfill is a critical
component The concrete crib wall components also appear to be deteriorating due to the
age of structure
Cracks are visible in portions of the asphalt pavement indicating possible
movement of the wall system Additionally the natural slopes beyond the toe of the wall
are very steep on the order of one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V
ESTIMATED SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Based on our review of in house subsurface information in the general area we
anticipate that the crib wall area is generally underlain by volcanic cinder and ash deposits
These deposits generally consist of silty sands and partially cemented sands
The silty sands and partially cemented sands are fairly permeable materials that
may induce hydrostatic lateral pressure to retaining structures Therefore proper drainage
of the retaining structures shouid be provided
Because of the high elevation of the project site we do not anticipate encountering
permanent groundwater levels within the crib wall depth It should be noted that
groundwater levels are subject to change due to seasonal precipitation and other factors
DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our review of available subsurface information near the project site we
anticipate that the site is generally underlain by volcanic cinder sand and ash deposits
Groundwater levels are not anticipated within the wall depth at the site

Discussion on wall repair and wall replacement alternatives is provided below In
addition an evaluation of the different wall alternatives is also presented below Based on
our evaluation of the different alternatives we recommend that the existing crib wall be
replaced with a segmental retaining wall system
Wall Repair
Based on observed roadway pavement cracks misaligned crib face units and the
anticipated soil conditions at the project site the existing single stacked crib wall does not
appear to be sufficient to provide long term stability in retaining the 15 to 20 feet of soil
The existing crib units tree roots and wall height would provide difficulties in installing soil
nails to increase the stability of the existing crib wall In addition the crib wall units have
deteriorated due to the age of the structure Furthermore it is not known if proper
drainage is provided for the existing crib wall structure Because of the above it is
recommended that the existing crib wall be replaced with a new wall structure
Wali Replacement Alternatives
Wall replacement alternatives consisting of a conventional concrete retaining wall
segmental retaining wall drilled shaft retaining wall and a soil nailed wall may be
considered Based on the topographic survey the ground slopes downward at an
inclination of about 1H 1V below the existing crib wall Adequate embedment of the base
or footing of the retaining structures should be provided for stability of the structures An
embedment of the base or footing of the retaining structures that provided a preliminary
minimum setback distance of 6 feet from the base or footing face to the slope face was
used
The conventional concrete retaining wall alternative will require a large amount of
excavation for wall construction and backfilling Because of the high wall height the
excavation will extend within the existing roadway and require total roadway closure during
wall construction The existing crib wall needs to be removed and the existing utility line
needs to be relocated Special construction equipment is not required for wall
construction The estimated cost for this alternative is about 1 6 Million with an estimated
construction duration of about three months The estimated cost of the different wali
alternatives was provided by others A sketch of this wall replacement alternative is shown
on the attached Plate 3
Segmental retaining walls consist of modular block face units with a reinforced fill
mass consisting of geogrids and granular fill materials This alternative is the least costly
with a shorter construction time than the other wall alternatives In addition mobilizing
special construction equipment is not required This wall alternative will also require total
roadway closure during wall construction and will require a large amount of excavation and
backfilling Relocation of the existing utility line and removal of the crib wall will be The

The drilled shaft retaining wall alternative is the most costly but will not require total
closure of the existing roadway and has a low risk potential since the existing crib wall
need not be removed The relocation of the existing utility line is not required This
alternative also has a shorter construction duration Special drilling equipment will need to
be mobilized to the project site A closely spaced drilled shaft wall consisting of 4 foot
diameter shafts with shaft length of about 50 feet at 5 feet center to center spacing was
used in estimating the construction cost and duration The estimated cost for the drilled
shaft wall is about 1 4 Million with an estimated construction duration of about
two months A sketch of this wall alternative is presented on the attached Plate 5
Soil nailed walls consist of the installation of a shotcrete facing and soil nails This
wall alternative uses a top to down construction procedure with incremental removal of the
existing crib wall Total closure of the roadway is not required The soil nailed wall
alternative is less costly than the conventional concrete retaining wall and drilled shaft
retaining wall alternatives Because the existing crib wall will be removed incrementally
during the construction of the soil nailed wall the slope area taken up by the crib wall will
be lost and the total width of the top of the slope will be reduced Mobilization of special
drilling equipment will be required Twelve inch diameter by 20 foot long soil nails at 5 feet
center to center spacing were used in estimating the construction cost and duration The
estimated cost of this wall alternative is about 1 2 Million The estimated construction
duration is about two and one half months A sketch of the soil nailed wall alternative is
shown on the attached Plate6 Descriptions of the different wall alternatives are
presented on the attached Summary of Wall Alternatives Plate 7
Since the new wall face will be difficult to see from the roadway elevation at the top
of the wall we believe that wall face aesthetics is not critical Typically the segmental
retaining wall facing is more appealing than the concrete or shotcrete facing of the other
alternatives The typical design life for the wall structures is 75 years
Based on our field observations and evaluation we believe that the existing crib
wall should be replaced with a new wall system Based on our study of potential wall
alternatives we recommend that the replacement wall consist of a segmental retaining
wall system This wall alternative was the one of the least costly with a shorter
construction duration compared to the other wall alternatives
