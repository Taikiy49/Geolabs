Dear Mr. Pang:
As requested, we conducted a field exploration and laboratory testing program in
support of the design of the new Bank of Hawaii (BOH) building within the Kapolei
Commons development in the Kapolei area on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii.
Project Considerations
The new bank building project site is within the Kapolei Commons development
in the Kapolei area in the District of Ewa on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. The BOH
building project is situated adjacent to Kapolei Parkway at the southwest side of the
retail development.
Based on the information provided, we understand it is proposed to construct a
new BOH building on the existing building pad prepared during the mass grading
operations for the Kapolei Commons development. Due to some slab movements
observed at a few of the Phase 1 buildings, we understand there are concerns that
similar type issues could affect the BOH building in the future.
Therefore, it is desired to conduct a drilling and soil sampling program at the
BOH building pad location. The information obtained would be used to analyze the
specific soil conditions that currently underlie this area for expansive characteristics. In
addition, the information will be used to evaluate if the temporary grassing/irrigation
program has compromised the structural integrity of the existing building pad.

Field Exploration and Laboratory Testing Program
Our field exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling four borings,
designated as Boring Nos. 1 through 4, extending to a depth of about 5 feet below the
existing building pad surface. The approximate boring locations are shown on the
Site Plan, Plate 1.
The materials encountered in the drilled borings were classified by visual and
textural examination in the field by our engineer, who observed the drilling operations on
a near-continuous basis. These classifications were further reviewed by visual
observation and testing in the laboratory. Soils were classified in general conformance
with the Unified Soil Classification System, as shown on the Soil Log Legend, Plate A.
Graphic representations of the materials encountered in the drilled borings are
presented on the Logs of Borings, Plates A-1 through A-4. Pocket penetrometer tests
were performed on selected cohesive soils in the field. The pocket penetrometer test
provides an indication of the unconfined compressive strength of the soils. The pocket
penetrometer test results are summarized on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate
depths.
In addition to the field exploration work, samples of the coralline capping fill and
underlying clayey soils encountered in the drilled borings were collected and transported to
our office for laboratory testing. Laboratory moisture content, unit weight, Atterberg Limits,
and ring swell tests were performed on selected soil samples collected, and the test
results are presented on the Logs of Borings and on Plates B-1 and B-2 of this letter
report.
Adequacy of Current Building Slab Design
As mentioned previously, some slab movements have been observed at a few of
the Phase 1 buildings within the Kapolei Commons development. Therefore, it is desired
to evaluate the adequacy of the current building slab design for the Bank of Hawaii based
on the information obtained from our field exploration and laboratory testing program.
In our geotechnical engineering report entitled “Geotechnical Engineering
Exploration, Kapolei Commons, Kapolei, Oahu, Hawaii’ dated December 18, 2006
prepared for the Kapolei Commons development, we indicated that the surface clay soils
across the development site exhibit moderate to high shrink/swell characteristics when
subjected to fluctuations in the soil moisture contents. Therefore, we recommended
providing at least 18 inches of non-expansive select granular fill materials below the
building slabs.
In order to evaluate the adequacy of this recommended building slab design based
on the site-specific subsurface soil conditions at the Bank of Hawaii building pad, we
focused our field exploration and laboratory testing program on the following aspects:

Expansive Characteristics of the Underlying Clay Soils
The expansive characteristics of the on-site clay soils underlying the BOH building
pad play a major role in determining the adequacy of the current 18-inch non-expansive
capping fill requirement for the BOH building slab. In order to evaluate the expansive
characteristics of the underlying clay soils, Atterberg Limits and one-inch ring swell tests
were performed on selected soil samples obtained from our field exploration program. The
Atterberg Limits test results are presented on Plate B-1, and the ring swell test results are
presented on Plate B-2.
The clay soils underlying the BOH building pad generally are classified as Silty Clay
(CH) with a plasticity index of about 31. Laboratory tests performed on the silty clay soils
yielded a ring swell of up to about 6 percent, indicating that the underlying clay soils exhibit
a low to moderate potential for shrinking and swelling when subjected to soil moisture
fluctuations. Based on the test results, it appears that highly expansive clays were not
encountered at the BOH building location. Therefore, we believe that the current 18-inch
thick non-expansive capping fill layer recommended for the retail development should be
adequate for the BOH building slab design.
Thicknesses of the In-Place Non-Expansive Capping Fill
In order to confirm that a minimum of 18 inches of non-expansive select granular fill
materials have been placed above the on-site clay soils at the BOH building pad location,
continuous soil sampling was conducted at four boring locations starting at a depth of
about 6 inches below the existing pad surface (topsoil at the surface) during our field
exploration program. Based on our field observations and measurements, it appears that
the existing BOH building pad is underlain by approximately 6 inches of topsoil over a
plastic membrane. This was followed by the non-expansive select granular fill materials as
discussed above, consisting of tannish brown silty coralline gravel with sand (coralline
capping fill) in a medium dense to dense state. The thicknesses of the non-expansive
capping fill were measured at each boring location (below the topsoil and plastic
membrane) and are summarized below.

Structural Integrity of Existing Building Pad
As mentioned previously, it is also desired to evaluate if the temporary grassing
and/or irrigation program has compromised the structural integrity of the existing building
pad. In order to evaluate this potential, we focused our geotechnical engineering
evaluation on the following aspects:
° Compaction of the Coralline Capping Fill
® Compaction of the Underlying Clay Soils
In accordance with the afore-mentioned geotechnical engineering report and the
project plans and specifications, the non-expansive capping fill and the underlying clayey
subgrade soils should be compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction.
Relative compaction refers to the in-place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of
the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557.
Optimum moisture is the water content (percentage by dry weight) corresponding to the
maximum dry density.
The relative compaction of the coralline capping fill and underlying clayey soils
were evaluated by comparing the in-situ dry density of the material to its maximum dry
density (proctor value). Results of the relative compaction of the soil samples obtained
at each depth increment are summarized in the following table.

compaction of some portion of the capping fill material has been compromised slightly
(with 86 to 89 percent relative compaction), probably due to the temporary grassing
and/or irrigation program implemented at the BOH building pad surface. Therefore, we
recommend implementing the following procedures during the re-grading work of the
BOH building pad.
The existing topsoil and the underlying plastic membrane should be removed to
expose the underlying select granular capping fill material. Then, the top 12 inches of
the select granular fill should be over-excavated and stockpiled separately for reuse as
a source of select granular fill for the building pad construction. The over-excavated
subgrade (after removal of the top 12 inches of select granular fill) should be
proof-rolled and recompacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction. Select granular
fill material to be placed on top of the proof-rolled subgrade to the new finished pad
elevation for the BOH building should be moisture-conditioned to above the optimum
moisture, placed in level lifts of about 8 inches in loose thickness, and compacted to at
least 90 percent relative compaction.
Limitations
The findings and recommendations submitted herein are based, in part, upon
information obtained from our field exploration, laboratory testing, and past project
experience in the project vicinity. Variations of subsurface conditions between and beyond
the boring locations may occur, and the nature and extent of these variations may not
become evident until construction is underway. If variations then appear evident, Geolabs
should be contacted to re-evaluate the findings and recommendations presented in this
letter report.
The field boring locations are approximate, having been estimated by pacing and
measuring from visible features noted on the site plan. Elevations of the field borings
were interpolated from the contour lines shown on the same plan. The boring locations
and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the
methods used.
The stratification breaks shown on the Logs of Borings depict the approximate
boundaries between soil and/or rock types and, as such, may denote a gradual
transition. Water level data from the borings were measured at the times shown on the
graphic representations and/or presented in the text herein. These data have been
reviewed and interpretations made in the formulation of this letter report. However, it
