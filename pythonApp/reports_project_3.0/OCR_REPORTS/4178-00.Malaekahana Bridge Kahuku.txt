Dear Mr Bumgarner
In accordance with your request we have performed a wave equation analysis for the
proposed pile driving operations for the Replacement of Malaekahana Bridge project in the
Kahuku area on the island of Oahu Hawaii The wave equation analysis was conducted
based on the information provided and the subsurface conditions contained in the plans for
the subject project
Previous wave equation analyses were performed for the replacement bridge project
by Atlanta Testing and Engineering ATE of Tampa Florida The results of their wave
equation analyses are contained in the report entitled Malaekahana Stream Bridge
Replacement Evaluation of Proposed Pile Driving System Using WEAP Analysis dated
In addition a supplemental wave equation analysis was performed by
ATE and the results are presented in a letter dated The information
contained in these reports were used where applicable for our analyses
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
Based on the information provided it is our understanding that the proposed
replacement bridge will be supported on a driven pile foundation system The proposed pile
section will be a 16 5 inch octagonal precast prestressed concrete pile The test piles will
have lengths of about 76 and 104 feet with tip elevations of about 72 and 100 feet Mean
Sea Level MSL for Abutments 1 and 2 respectively The piles were designed with an
altowable compression load of 50 tons 100 kips with an associated factor of safety of 3 0
Therefore the required ultimate pile capacity is 150 tons or 300 kips
In order to install the piles to the specified tip elevation the pile locations will need to
be predrilled to an elevation of about 33 to 45 feet MSL due to the presence of a coral

Subsurface Conditions
Based on the four boring logs the borings generally encountered interbeds of medium
Stiff to stiff cohesive soils with compact coral sand extending to the maximum depth explored
of about 120 feet below the existing ground surface At some depths the cohesive soils
grade to soft in consistency as evidenced by the low blow counts The subsurface soil
conditions encountered appear to be alluvial soil deposits associated with development of
the stream
Hammer and Driving System
We understand that a single acting diese hammer is proposed for
the pile driving operations by Hawaiian Dredging Construction Company Based on the pile
driving equipment data form provided the hammer has a ram weight of
at the maximum pump setting 4 Schematic details of the driving system are presented on
the Pile and Driving Equipment Data Form Plate 1 The hammer has a minimum rated
energy of 35 4 kip feet corresponding to pump setting 1
Based on the subsurface conditions and the driving conditions anticipated a pile
cushion consisting of 9 inches of plywood cushion blocks will be used for the pile driving
operations The plywood cushion blocks will have a cross section area of approximately
342 square inches
We also understand that a follower may be used to chase the pile down to the desired
depth if the subsurface conditions require that the pile tip elevation be extended deeper It
appears that the pile follower is made up of a modified H pile section HP12x84 with some
additional steel plates web welded to the H pile section The follower has a length of about
20 feet including the skirt and a weight of about 4 500 pounds as shown on Plate 2
Pile Details
We understand that the specified piles will be 16 5 inch octagonal precast
prestressed concrete pile with lengths of about 76 and 104 feet for Abutments 1 and 2
respectively The piles were designed with an allowable compression load of 50 tons with
an associated factor of safety of 3 0 resulting in an ultimate pile capacity of 150 tons
300 kips The piles will be cast and driven in one single piece with no splices planned
We understand that the piles will be cast from concrete with a minimum 28 day
compressive strength of 6 000 pounds per square inch psi The effective prestress level
of the prestressed pile will be approximately 1 000 psi Based on this information and
Section 505 of the Hawaii Standard Specifications 1994 the maximum allowable

WAVE EQUATION ANALYSIS
A wave equation analysis was performed based on the pile driving system proposed
by Hawaiian Dredging Construction Company and the subsurface conditions shown on the
plans The wave equation analysis was performed using the computer program GRLWEAP
Wave Equation Analysis of Pile Foundations which is an extension of the WEAP87
program developed under the sponsorship of the Federal Highway Administration
Pile Capacity
Based on our wave equation analysis it appears that the proposed pile driving
equipment Delmag D 30 32 hammer is capable of driving the specified pile section to the
desired ultimate pile capacity of 300 kips The required driving resistance for the 300 kip
ultimate pile capacity required for the 76 and 104 foot long piles will be about 40 blows per
foot A complete printout of the results of our wave equation analysis is provided in
the driving resistance is provided on Plates 3 and 4
As previously indicated there is a possibility that the piles may need to be driven to
tip elevations deeper than shown on the plans In the event that the piles need to be driven
deeper a pile follower chaser will need to be used We have conducted additional wave
equation analyses to model the effect of the pile follower on the pile driving resistance
Based on our analyses we believe that the driving resistance needed to achieve a 300 kip
ultimate pile capacity will need to increase from about 40 blows per foot to 62 blows per foot
with a pile follower The computer printout of our analyses incorporating the use of a follower
Driving Stresses
The computed maximum compressive and tensile stresses in the pile during pile
driving are 2 600 and 440 psi respectively based on an ultimate pile capacity of 300 kips
A graph showing the computed maximum compression and tensile stresses during driving
is provided on Plates 5 and 6 Please note that the graph was prepared based on kips per
square inch ksi which is equivalent to 1 000 psi as required by the specifications
Based on our analysis the estimated compression and tensile stresses induced in the
pile during the pile driving operations for the specified ultimate capacity of 300 kips are within
the range specified for this project As previously indicated the maximum allowable
compressive and tensile stresses during pile driving for the specified pile section are 2 600
and 1 000 psi respectively The range of stresses allowed during pile driving have been
shaded on Plates 5 and 6 for ease of reference

ABOUT THE WAVE EQUATION ANALYSIS RESULTS
The GRLWEAP program simulates the behavior of an impact driven pile The
program contains mathematical models which describe hammer driving system
pile and soil during the hammer blow Under certain conditions the models
only crudely approximate often complex dynamic situations
A wave equation analysis also relies on input data which represents normal
situations The data may be the best available information at the time of
the analysis however it may greatly differ from actual field conditions
The program authors therefore recommend prudent use of GRLWEAP results
Soil response and hammer performance should be verified by static and or
dynamic measurements Estimates of bending or other local non axial
stresses and prestress effects must also be accounted for by the user
Finally the GRLWEAP capacities are ultimate values They MUST be reduced
by means of a safety factor to yield a design or working load

ABOUT THE WAVE EQUATION ANALYSIS RESULTS
The GRLWEAP program simulates the behavior of an impact driven pile The
program contains mathematical models which describe hammer driving system
pile and soil during the hammer blow Under certain conditions the models
only crudely approximate often complex dynamic situations
A wave equation analysis also relies on input data which represents normal
situations The data may be the best available information at the time of
the analysis however it may greatly differ from actual field conditions
The program authors therefore recommend prudent use of GRLWEAP results
Soil response and hammer performance should be verified by static and or
dynamic measurements Estimates of bending or other local non axial
stresses and prestress effects must also be accounted for by the user
Finally the GRLWEAP capacities are ultimate values They MUST be reduced
by means of a safety factor to yield a design or working load
