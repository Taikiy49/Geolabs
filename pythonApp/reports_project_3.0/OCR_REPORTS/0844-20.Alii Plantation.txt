PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this soil engineering investigation was to
explore the subsurface soil and groundwater conditions at
the site and to provide data for use in the design and
construction of the proposed access roadway and townhouse
building foundations The work was performed generally
in accordance with our proposal of August 21 1980
The development site was filled previously in 1979 The
earthwork operations were monitored and compaction tested
by our office
For the proposed housing development additional embankment
filling is required for an access road into the site from
the Halawa Heights Road and a revised townhouse lay out deve
lopment scheme has been developed Previous preliminary
soil investigation by others had disclosed soft subsoils
below the project site The additional soil investigation
was required to evaluate the effects of the proposed access
roadway embankment construction and how this will affect the
townhouse building construction at the site
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests
which were used in the engineering analysis that formulated

appendix of this report

PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
Based on the latest plan the proposed townhouse development
will consist of eighteen 18 separate 2 and 3 story structures
each with 1 to 3 units The structures will be of wood
framed construction utilizing slab on grade and post and
beam foundation
Minor site filling generally about 1 foot or less is
planned over the building site
Up to about 20 feet of embankment fill is required for the
construction of the 56 foot wide access roadway into the site
off from Halawa Heights Road The access road will run
parallel to the adjacent state highway and down into the
townhouse and commercial areas
The 1 5 acre park site is presently planned to be located
in the western portion of the site where the underlying soft
deposit is thickest

SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is located in a lowlying area between
Halawa Heights Road and Foster Village Subdivision about
1200 feet northeast of Salt Lake Boulevard The proposed
Stadium Mall commercial development is located makai and
adjacent to the southwestern boundary of the project site
Most of the townhouse development site is relatively flat
and approximately 20 feet lower than Halawa Heights Raod
Along the project s southern and eastern boundaries the
ground surface slopes upward toward the existing Foster
Village Subdivision at slope ratios of about 2 horizontal
to 1 vertical
At the time of our field exploration the site was covered
with a moderate growth of weeds and brush
There is an old water well in the eastern portion of the site
which had been recently capped A recently installed 42
inch drain line is located along the northern boundary of
the site extending mauka from the Stadium Mall and runs
parallel to the Halawa Heights Road
PREVIOUS SITE GRADING
The project site was previously graded in May 1979

GEOLOGY
The general area in which the project site is located is
geologically mapped to consist of three distinct formations
The youngest formation present basically comprises the entire
relatively level portion of the site excluding any man
made fills placed upon it This type of formation usually
deposited in the lower elevations near the sea consists of
soft unconsolidated non calcareous deposits of sand silts
and clays in varying percentages These deposits are prin
cipally laid by streams and are referred to as younger
alluvium
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 844 20 P 6
An older formation is exposed in the surrounding higher
grounds primarily in the east and south sides This for
mation is mapped to be a consolidated gray lavender and
brown color bedded volcanic tuff or mudrock These cemented
ash layers are associated with the development of Aliamanu
Crater and Salt Lake Crater
The second older formation present at the site is exposed
on the upper natural side slopes that surround the lower
level area This appears to be underlying both the younger
alluvium and tuff formations This formation is referred
to as older alluvium consisting of a mottled brown to
red brown deeply weathered stiff conglomeration of clays
and silts sands gravel and cobbles
The soft lowlying townhouse area was probably initially
developed during a lower stand of the sea as stream erosion
downcut the older alluvium and tuff leaving a valley like
depression in this area With a subsequent higher stand of
the sea above present sea level shallow water deposits of
clays silts and sands filled the downcut depression leaving
a flat floor remaining as sea level retreated to its present
level
This sequence of events and related formations were essentially
confirmed in the field from the types and depths of the soil

slopes and higher grounds

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that most of the site is
underlain by a relatively thick deposit of soft compressible
clays and silts
Based on our previous experience with the settlement gauge
monitoring program at the adjacent Stadium Mall project and
a review of the available soil data from the State Highway
Division files on the adjacent Halawa Heights roadway embank
ment fills it is our observation that the underlying soft
deposits have slow rates of settlement
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 844 20 P 9
Data from the two settlement gauges placed
during the previous site grading in this project site and
consolidation tests performed on the soil samples obtained
for this investigation indicate that some long term settle
ment is still occurring due to the weight of the previously
placed fill over the soft underlying deposits We estimate
that these remaining settlements could be on the order of
about 1 to 3 inches over a period of many years with the
larger settlement expected in the southwestern portion of
the site where the compressible soils are thickest The
existing stiff crust over the soft deposits is relatively
thick and therefore would act as a large mat to help reduce
the effect of differential settlement to the future struc
tures in this area Therefore large detrimental settlements
are not anticipated for the proposed light load wood framed
structures if additional fill can be avoided to be placed
in this building area
Additional settlement gauges are recommended to be installed
in the proposed structure areas to monitor the remaining
ground settlements in this area
Since any additional fill would cause additional settlement
of the compressible deposits additional fills should be
avoided and if absolutely required it should be minimized
to no more than 1 to 2 feet

New Access Road Fill
Because of the grade control requirement at the Halawa
Heights Road entrance large fill is required to construct
the access road down into the townhouse site It is esti
mated that up to about 20 feet of new fill is planned at
the section where the new road enters the project site near
Boring 3 The roadway fill tapers down to about 4 to 5
feet high near Boring 2 and down to 0 feet near Boring 1
Based on and 3 it appears that the soft
compressible deposits are thicker near Boring 1 where fill
is minimum and thins out toward Boring 3 where the fill
is highest
Accurate settlement predictions for the proposed road fill
are difficult due to the varying heights of fill and varia
ble subsoil conditions Our estimated primary settlements
due to the new fill are on the order to 6 inches Addi
tional long term secondary settlements should also be
anticipated
The time required for the estimated primary settlements to
occur are very difficult to predict because they depend on
many variable including the composition and structure of
the subsoils Based on previous experience in the area
it appears that several years would be required to obtain 903

consolidation of the subsoils Since it may not be practi
cable to wait several years for the anticipated settlement
to occur a surcharge fill is recommended over the access
road embankment to reduce the waiting period for the antici
pated settlements Based on previous experience near the
project site it is estimated that for a surcharge height
of 10 feet a 6 to 9 month surcharge period would be required
Settlement gauges should be installed during the new filling
to monitor the actual settlement rate to determine when the
surcharge may be removed and utility and road construction
could proceed
To minimize delay of future construction the filling and
surcharge should be started as soon as practicable
Existing 42 inch Drain Line

This drain line would be affected by the general ground
settlements caused by the new embankment fill
However it is our opinion that due to the relatively thick
natural soil crust below the fill and the gradual tapering
of the proposed fill large differential settlements of
the pipe are not anticipated It is estimated that

into the project site only minor fills about 1 foot thick
or less are planned over the remaining building sites

Spread or continuous footing foundations are recommended for
the remaining proposed townhouse structures
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f is recommended
for footings bearing on compacted fill or stiff natural
ground The above value is for total dead plus live loads
and may be increased by one third when temporary loads are
considered
On level areas the bottom of the building footings should be
embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the outside finished
grade On sloping areas the footings should be embedded an
additional amount to obtain a 5 foot minimum set back distance
measured from the outer edge of the footing out to the slope
face This additional embedment is necessary to help resist
footing movement due to future erosion and slope creep and
also required to develop the necessary bearing capacity of
the underlying supporting soils
Resistance to lateral loads may be provided by combination of
passive pressure and friction Passive pressure may be taken
as that exerted by a fluid of density equal to 300 p c f
Friction may be taken as normal dead load times a coefficient
of friction equal to 0 4
A combination of friction and passive pressure may be used
provided that passive pressure is reduced by one third
Surface water should be diverted away from the building foun
dations

Retaining Wall
Basement walls up to about 10 feet high are planned for
the development Well graded granular material such as
base course rock should be used as backfill material behind
the walls Impervious material should be used for the top
two feet of the backfill
The recommendations given under Foundations should be
followed for the design of the wall foundations
The walls should be designed to resist lateral earth pressures
as follows
Walls unrestrained at the 40 p c f equivalent
top level backfill fluid pressure
Walls restrained at the 55 p c f equivalent
top level backfill fluid pressure
Additional loads due to surcharge should be
included where applicable
A friction factor of 0 4 can be utilized to determine the
sliding resistance of the wall
Additionally a passive soil resistance of 300 p c f can be
used However the passive resistance should be reduced by
one third when combined with the frictional resistance
A subsurface drainage system should be used to prevent the
build up of hydrostatic pressure behind the walls

Slab on Grade
The field exploration disclosed that most of the site has
been filled with a minimum of 3 feet of mudrock and clayey
Silt derived from cutting the upper portions of the site
Laboratory ring swell tests performed on this fill material
indicate low swelling potential However the borings
encountered expansive clayey soil below the existing upper
new fill layers and therefore pockets of expansive clay
may be encountered in some of the slab excavation parti
cularly where minimal fill was placed close to cut fill
daylight area The slab subgrade areas should be inspec
ted by a soil engineer individually prior to base course
placement to detect clay pockets at or below subgrade level
If encountered the clay pockets should be removed a mini
mum depth of 3 feet below the subgrade level and replaced
with compacted select material
The slab subgrade should be kept moist and protected from
drying prior to concrete placement Subgrade should be com
pacted to a minimum of 90 of maximum dry density
It is recommended that slab on grade be placed over a 4 inch
layer of 3B Fine cushion layer
Slab on grade should be separated from foundations

Gentlemen
As requested we have performed an additional investigation
at the proposed site for the Jolly Roger Restaurant at the
Stadium Mall Development
We have monitored the previous site grading development work
including field inspection and compaction density testing
at the site We have also installed and monitored the settle
ment gauges at the project site in conjunction with the sur
charge program recommended by our office
W O 844 20 P 2
A previous soil report entitled Supplementary Investigation
Review and Analyses of Foundations Halawa
Village dated had been prepared for the
Stadium Mall commercial development
The purpose of this additional investigation was to delineate
the thickness of the underlying soft soil deposits and to
provide specific foundation recommendations for the proposed
one story restaurant building
SITE CONDITIONS
The proposed Stadium Mall development site is located
southeast of the Aloha Stadium in the makai or lower por
tion of Halawa The site is bounded by Salt Lake Boulevard
on the west Kahuapaahi Street on the north vacant land on
the east and a steep natural slope which extends up to
the existing Foster Village Subdivision on the south
The site for the proposed Jolly Roger restaurant is located
in the southwestern portion of the development site about
200 feet northeast of Salt Lake Boulevard and adjacent to the
existing steep slope
During the site grading for the development up to 5 feet
of fill was placed over the northwestern portion of the
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 844 20 P 3
building site However little to no fill was placed over
the southeastern portion of the building
The restaurant site was generally graded to about the pro
posed finish elevation of 18 5 feet It was noted during our
field trip in October 1980 that the proposed restaurant
building site has been covered with about 5 feet of recent
miscellaneous stockpiles consisting of asphaltic concrete
broken concrete boulders and soil
PROPOSED STRUCTURE
The proposed restaurant will have a floor area of about
4000 square feet
The proposed structure will be a relatively light one story
building of wood construction
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Field Exploration
Two borings were drilled at the northwestern corners of
the proposed building The borings were drilled with a truck
mounted drill rig utilizing continuous flight augers
The approximate locations of the drilled borings are presented
on the Site Plan Plate l
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 844 20 P 4
Subsurface Conditions
Based on the two borings the subsurface soil conditions can
be generalized as follows
1 New FILL 4 to 5 5 feet thick consisting of stiff
clayey silt and gravel
Z CLAY 4 to 7 feet thick below the fill
3 STIFF ALLUVIUM 5 feet thick consiting of silty
clay and clayey silt with gravel
4 SOFT DEPOSIT 23 to 33 feet thick consisting of silty
clay and clayey silts with sand and shells
5 STIFF ALLUVIUM below the soft deposit to the depths
of the borings
Groundwater level was encountered at 11 5 to 14 feét below the
existing ground surface
CONCLUSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration confirmed the presence of the soft
alluvial deposits below the proposed building site The two
borings disclosed that the soft layer is about 23 to 33 feet
thick in the northwestern side of the proposed building
Previous exploration indicates that the soft layer thins out
rapidly toward the southern portion of the site
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 844 20 P 5
The recent site grading at the restaurant building location
consisted of little to no fill along the southern portion
of the building and increasing up to about 5 feet of fill
placed along the northern portion where the underlying soft
deposit is thicker
Ground settlements are anticipated due to the weight of
the new fill over the underlying soft deposit Due to the
thicker soft deposit and higher fill in the northwestern
portion of the proposed building the ground settliments are
expected to increase toward the northwest direction
Initial estimates of the total anticipated primary settle
ments were on the order of about 6 iches for 5 feet of fill
over 30 feet of soft deposit in the northwestern portion of
the building site Minimal settlement was anticipated in
the southern portion of the building site where very little
fill was placed The fill has been in place for about 1
year now and observation of the project settlement gauges
as well as recent laboratory consolidation tests indicate
that about 80 to 95 of the total primary consolidation may
have occurred The existing miscellaneous stockpiles over
the site have also helped to create a surcharge effect over
the softer portion of the site This should have further
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 844 20 P 6
reduced some of the residual future settlements We estimate
that about 1 to 2 inches of additional primary and long term
settlements could be experienced in the northwestern portion
of the building site The new structure should be designed
to tolerate the anticipated areal settlements Additional
large settlements due to the new structural building loads
are not anticipated due to the thickness of the stiff crust
13 to 18 feet below the existing ground surface
The natural soil below the new fill layer is expansive brown
clay In the southern half of the building site where
minimal fill was placed this expansive clay may be encoun
tered at excavated foundation level The expansive clay if
encountered should be removed within 3 feet below slabs
foundations and 2 feet below pavements areas and replaced
with select non expansive material We recommend that a soil
engineer be consulted during the construction phase and to
monitor the actual soil conditions encountered at the slab
foundation and parking subgrade levels
Foundations
For the proposed light one story wood framed structure
shallow foundations bearing on the compacted fill or stiff
natural ground non clay deposit can be used
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 844 20 P 7
Due to the anticipated ground settlements a rigid waffle
type mat or post tensioned slab foundation or spread footing
foundations well tied together by grade beams is recommended
An allowable bearing value of 2000 psf is recommended for
foundations bearing on the above specified compacted ground
or fill
The bottoms of the foundations should be placed a minimum
of 18 inches below the outside finished grade
Slab on Grade
It is recommended that slab on grade be placed over a four
inch layer of base course compacted to 95 percent of its
maximum dry density
The existing expansive clay soil southern portion of building
should be removed 2 feet outside and 3 feet below the slab
and replaced with select non expansive compacted fill material
The subgrade should be kept moist and protected from drying
prior to concrete placement Subgrade should be compacted
to a minimum of 90 percent of maximum dry density
Slab on grade should be separated from foundations
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 844 20 P 8
In order to protect against moisture damage of the flooring
material a polyethylene moisture barrier should be incor
porated within floor slabs
Design Review
Plans for foundations and site grading should be forwarded
to the soils engineer for review and written comments prior
to the construction to see that the letter and spirit of
our recommendations is included in the project plans and
specifications
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that stripping excavations and filling
