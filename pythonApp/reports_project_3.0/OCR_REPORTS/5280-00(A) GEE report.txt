2 1 Regional Geology
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lavas from
two shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated
to be middle to late Pliocene in age and Koolau Volcano is estimated to be late
Pliocene to early Pleistocene in age After a long period of volcanic inactivity during
which time erosion incised deep valleys into the Koolau Shield volcanic activity
returned with a series of lava flows followed by cinder and tuff cone formations These
series are referred to as the Honolulu Volcanic Series
During the Pleistocene Epoch Ice Age sea levels fluctuated in response to the
cycles of continental glaciation As the glaciers grew and advanced less water was
available to fill the oceanic basins such that sea levels fell below the present stands of
the sea When the glaciers melted and receded an excess of water became available
such that the sea levels rose to above the present sea level
The processes of erosion and deposition were affected by these glacio eustatic
sea level fluctuations When the sea level was low the erosional base level was
correspondingly lower and valleys were carved to depths below the present sea level
When the sea level was high the erosional base level was raised such that sediments
accumulated at higher elevations
In the mountainous regions of Hawaii the erosional processes are dominated by
detachment of soil and rock masses from the valley walls and are transported
downslope toward the axis of a valley primarily by gravity as colluvium Once these
materials reach the stream in the central portion of a valley alluvial processes become
dominant and the sediments are transported and deposited as alluvium
In general stream flows in Hawaii are intermittent and flashy such that the
stream flows transmit large volumes of water for very short durations Because of this
phenomenon the transport of sediments is intermittent and the bulk of the stream s
hydraulic load consists of a poorly sorted mixture of boulders cobbles gravel sands
Hawaii California

and fines When the erosional base levels change these sediment loads are left as
deposits
When deposits are left in place for long periods of time chemical processes
begin to alter the materials simultaneously causing a breakdown or weathering of the
material Chemical processes also cause induration or cementation of the
coarse grained portion of the sediment into a poorly consolidated sedimentary rock or
conglomerate Simultaneously erosion continues in the areas above the valley floors
and upstream in headwaters This continued erosion generates material that is
transported downslope covering the older alluvial deposits
Depending on the local base level and rate of transport these newer sediments
are generally transient in terms of geologic time In addition their consistency and
density are generally less than those of the older partially consolidated deposits

3 4 Utility Trenches
Based on the preliminary drawings provided we anticipate that the utilities for the
highway intersection improvement project will consist of the installation of a 24 inch
drain line In general good construction practices should be utilized for the installation
and backfilling of the trenches for the new utilities at the site The method and
equipment to be used for trench excavation should be determined by the contractor
subject to practical limits and safety considerations In addition the excavations should
comply with the applicable federal state and local safety requirements
Based on the results of our field exploration it appears that conventional
excavation methods may generally be utilized for utility line excavation at the site The
upper clayey soils encountered at the site are generally stiff to very stiff However
some of the utility trench excavations in the vicinity of to may
encounter hard basalt rock formation as described above which may pose some
excavation difficulties during the trench excavation
In addition it is anticipated that utilities extending deeper than 5 feet below the
existing ground pavement surface may encounter the soft to very soft recent alluvial
deposits as described above Where soft and or loose soils are encountered at the pipe
invert elevation we recommend that a subgrade stabilization layer consisting of
24 inches of No 2 Rock ASTM C 33 No 4 gradation wrapped in a non woven filter
fabric Mirafi 180N or equivalent be provided below the bedding layer for uniform
support for the new utility line The stabilization layer should also extend beyond the
sides of the pipe a minimum width of one fourth the outside diameter of the pipe or
12 inches whichever is greater
In general we recommend that granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of
No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation be used for support under the pipes

3 5 Corrosion Protection
Three sets of pH and minimum resistivity tests were performed to evaluate the
corrosivity of the on site clayey soils generally encountered at the project site The
results of these tests are presented on Plate B 3 of this report The minimum resistivity
values of the soil samples tested range between 3 400 and 4 800 ohm cm which
correspond to a Corrosion Rating of 3 corrosive based on the guidelines provided by
the City County of Honolulu Board of Water Supply Therefore we recommend that
all metallic substructures in contact with the soils be properly designed to be protected
against potential corrosion
