fire hydrant C 49 to the end of the existing 6 inch waterline near fire hydrant C 59 in the

the site of the project entitled Kamehameha Highway Haleiwa Water System
Improvements Part I The findings are presented in our report entitled Kamehameha
Highway Haleiwa Water System Improvements Part Haleiwa Oahu Hawaii dated
The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on
the Project Location Map Plate 1 Based on the information provided we understand
that it is proposed to construct new 8 inch and 4 inch water mains and appurtenances
along Kamehameha Highway for water system improvement The water system
improvement is shown on the Site Plans 1 through 4 Plates 2 1 through 2 4
Based on the plan and profile drawing provided the pipe inverts are not known

report

FIELD SURVEY
Geophysical survey equipment i e resistance meter was mobilized to the site on
for the field survey The survey was performed through
Our field work was performed under sunny and shower conditions
with generally dry to wet surface conditions
One hundred forty eight soil resistivity surveys were conducted at a maximum of
300 foot intervals at 37 locations designated as R 51 through R 87 along the shoulder of
the streets and the highway using the in Pin Method and a Nilsson Model
400 resistance meter manufactured by Nilsson Electrical Laboratory Inc The method
utilizes four surface probes or pins electrodes to evaluate the average resistivity an
indication of corrosivity of the underlying soil mass The average resistivity values of the
soil mass extending from the existing ground surface to the test depth was assessed by
using this method at the approximate survey locations shown on the Site Plans 1

The soil resistivity surveys were performed at the existing ground surface along the
shoulder of the existing roads of the proposed 8 inch and 4 inch water mains The
average soil resistance along the proposed alignment was evaluated at each survey
location with the electrodes aligned at equal spacing of generally 3 to 9 feet at the existing
ground surface The depth of penetration of a resistivity sounding is a function of the
electrode spacing Since the depth of the water main invert was not available at the time
of our field exploration a spacing of 3 to 9 feet was used for the electrode spacing for
each soil resistivity survey location
The resistance meter Nilsson Model 400 impresses a voltage between the outer
electrodes causing an electrical current to flow between them The voltage drop between
the inner electrodes is then measured by a sensitive built in voltmeter As a result the
soil resistance in ohms can be found directly on the dial The soil resistivity in ohm cm
which is directly related to both resistance and spacing can then be computed The
evaluated soil resistances the computed resistivity values and the corresponding
corrosion ratings are presented on Plates 3 1 through 3 7 of this report The data for the
test location R 18 surveyed in the Part of Kamehameha Highway project was also
presented for continuity of the two projects Field Resistivity Testing Summaries are
presented on Plates 4 1 through 4 5 It should be noted that the soil resistivity may be
influenced by temperature weather conditions existing underground facilities and other
factors
FINDINGS AND DISCUSSIONS
Resistivity is generally recognized as one of the most significant soil characteristics
with regard to the corrosivity of the soil to buried metallic objects In general the lower
the resistivity the greater the potential for corrosion of the buried metallic structure
Conversely the higher the resistivity the less likely the soil will contribute to corrosion of
metallic objects
On the basis of the field resistivity test results a majority of the test locations
indicate the subsurface soils along the proposed water main of Kamehameha Highway
within the project site have corrosion ratings of 3 to 5 This indicates the subsurface soils
to have corrosive to mildly corrosive potential Isolated resistivity measurements
indicating corrosion ratings of 2 were noted
