PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions at the project site in order to develop a generalized soils data set for the
formulation of geotechnical engineering design recommendations pertinent to the site
grading for the proposed residential development
The work was done in general accordance with our proposal of November 15
1990
The scope of our work included the drilling and sampling of seven 7 borings to
depths ranging between 11 0 and 21 5 feet below the existing ground surface Also
included were laboratory testing of selected samples obtained during the field exploration
engineering evaluation of the field and laboratory data the development of design
recommendations and report preparation
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration and laboratory testing are presented
in the appendices of this report
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site encompasses approximately twelve acres and is situated to the
north of the north fork of the Wailua River in the Kawaihau district of Wailua on the island
of Kauai Hawaii The site is bounded on the south by Kuamoo Road to the west by a
residential development and to the east by a lowlying area with an active stream The site
generally slopes to the north and is defined by plateau which has been marked by
substantial erosion creating a number of drainageways Existing elevations range from
430 feet above Mean Sea Level MSL along Kuamoo Road to 340 feet above MSL along
the northeast grading limits The site is sparsely vegetated by guava trees and grasses

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
The project site is situated at the base of the Makaleha Mountains and was created
by the Koloa Volcanic Series Lava flows of nepheline basalt melilite nepheline basalt
olive basalt and basanite basalt erupted from a large number of vents scattered over the
eastern portion of the island Most of the soils in the project area are residual soils
derived from the in situ weathering of these basic igneous rocks Thin beds of ash and
tuffaceous soil are intercalated with the flows
The surface soils consist of musigy 780 to dark reddish brown firm to friable silty
Clay The subsoils are dark red to dark reddish brown firm silty clay The substratum
is soft weathered basic igneous rock

Subsurface Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by drilling and sampling
seven 7 borings to depths ranging between 11 0 and 21 5 feet below the existing
ground surface The approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2
In general the borings encountered stiff clayey silts and silty clays to the maximum
depths drilled Colors of the soils ranged from browns to reds with frequent mottled
areas resulting from the deep chemical weathering which has occurred at the project site
A soft ground area was encountered in the vicinity delineated as Soft Ground on
the Site Plan Plate 2
In the Soft Ground area south eastern portion of the site Boring Nos 3 and 7
encountered up to 10 feet of soft soils The ground surface was marked with standing
water An open unsupported cavity had been bored through the native soils in the
existing ridge bordering the north limits of this area to drain the run off from a number of
drainageways converging from the upper portions of the site and surrounding areas
The logs of the drilled borings are presented in Appendix A of this report
Groundwater
In the soft areas the groundwater level was noted at or near the ground surface
or a few inches below the surface layer
Outside the soft areas groundwater was encountered in several of the borings and
varied between elevations 403 feet above MSL and 336 feet above MSL The variable
groundwater condition indicates that the groundwater originates from springs or seepage

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
It is our opinion that the proposed project is feasible from a geotechnical
engineering stand point provided that our recommendations are followed The wet soil
conditions and high to very high average annual rainfall at the project site will require
special construction methodology
A series of subdrains are recommended to intercept and divert groundwater
Essentially these subdrains will be located in existing drainageways
Site Preparation
In general the project site is covered with a moderately heavy growth of
vegetation Therefore moderate clearing and grubbing is anticipated during mass
grading
The site should be cleared of vegetation debris and other deleterious material In
areas to receive fill the ground should be scarified to a minimum depth of six 6 inches
aerated and recompacted to a minimum of 85 density per ASTM Test Method D 1557
78

Subdrain System
A subdrain system should be constructed to drain possible ground water seepage
from the proposed fill areas In the southern portion of the site these drains
should be placed in the bottom of the existing drainageways prior to the fill
placement The subdrains should consist of a four 4 inch perforated PVC pipe
encased in 3B Fine gravel and surrounded by a filter fabric In the soft ground
area and in the northern portion of the site the subdrains should be placed a
minimum of three 3 feet below the existing ground surface
For the recommended locations of the subdrains refer to the Site Plan Plate 2
For a schematic detail of the subdrain refer to Plate 3
Fill Material and Compaction Requirements
Generally the on site soils should be suitable for use as engineered fill provided
the high in situ moisture contents can be substantially lowered to achieve 85
compaction Since the project site is located in a high rainfall area the on site materials
have very high in situ moisture contents of about 15 to 30 percent above optimum
Therefore significant drying of excavated materials will be required prior to their use as
fill and or backfill
Due to the high in situ moisture contents of the on site soils and the high amounts
of rainfall occurring in the area it is our opinion that the compaction requirement for this
residential project may be reduced from the standard of 90 to a minimum of 85
maximum dry density as established by ASTM D 1557 78 Even with this reduced 85
minimum compaction requirement we believe substantial moisture reduction would still
be required along with the use of light compaction construction equipments The
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2610 00 Page 7
required aerating to lower the soil moisture and more intense compaction effort would
generally reduce the rate of fill placement at this site
Based on our findings from laboratory testing of on site soils a shrinkage factor
of about 15 to 25 percent due to the differences between the natural and compacted soil
densities may be considered for on site cut and fill quantities
If additional or drier imported materials are required the material should generally
be non expansive able to be compacted into a dense stable mass and have a minimum
California Bearing Ratio of 15 when tested in the laboratory in accordance with ASTM D
1883 The material should be tested and approved by the project geotechnical engineer
prior to transportation to the site
Fill Placement
All fills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 10 inches in loose level
thickness Fill placement should commence at the lowest point and continue upward in
level compacted layers
In areas to receive fill where the existing ground surface is steeper than five
horizontal to one vertical 5H 1V the new fill should be keyed and benched into the
existing slope to aid in the proper bonding of the new fill into the in situ soils see Plate
4
Settlements
Outside of the soft area the subsoils are generally stiff clayey silt with lenses of
softer soils Due to the non uniform subsoil conditions settlement predictions in this type
of soil material are usually difficult and based on empirical methods The estimated
GEOLABS HAWAIl
W O 2610 00 Page 8
settlement for 10 feet of fill is on the order of two 2 inches It is also estimated that it
will take about one 1 month to occur
In the soft ground area up to 15 feet of fill is planned The estimated settlement
from the weight of the new fill is on the order of six 6 inches Based on the
consolidation data it is estimated that 90 of the primary settlement would occur in about
three 8 months
Settlement gauges should be installed prior to fill placement to monitor the
settlement rate during the placement of fill and determine when building and utility
construction can proceed
Field Instrumentation
The installation of a minimum of three 3 settlement gauges in the soft ground
area are recommended prior to filling to monitor the actual amount and rate of fill ground
settlements and to determine when utility and house construction can commence
Settlement gauges could consist of conventional settlement platforms see Plate
5 which are optically monitored by the surveyor s level readings The surveyed
settlement readings should be submitted to the project geotechnical engineer for
interpretation The reference bench mark elevation should be located in the upper stable
ground away from the settlement areas

Foundation Support
The exact locations of the houses and design details were not available at this time
General guidelines for the house foundation design are presented below These
guidelines should be reviewed for each lot as house details become available to see if
modifications are necessary
For the design of residential structure footings an allowable bearing capacity of
2000 p s f may be utilized for footings bearing on the firm in situ residual soils or on fills
compacted in accordance with the above recommendations This value may be
increased by one third when temporary wind and seismic loads are considered In
general the footings should be embedded a minimum of 12 inches below the lowest
adjacent grade
If any soft or weak areas are encountered at the bottom of the footing excavation
they should be remedied by over excavation of the soft and or weak materials down to
very stiff natural alluvial soils and backfilling of the resultant void with fill material
compacted in accordance with the above recommendations

In areas where residential structures are to be constructed near the steep side
slopes of the drainageways the foundations should be set back sufficiently to bear below
an imaginary line of one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V projected upward from the
base of the slope
Retaining Walls
A retaining wall is proposed in conjunction with the site grading and roadway The
wall will support fills to be placed along the existing steep hillside slope adjacent to the
stream along the east grading limits
The wall footing should be embedded to a depth such that it will bear below an
imaginary line of one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V projected upward from the base
of the slope or set back a minimum of eight 8 feet from the face of the slope at the
footing elevation whichever is more
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f on compacted fill or natural soil may be
used for the design of the retaining wall foundations
Well graded granular material such as base course rock sand or equivalent
should be used as backfill material directly behind the retaining walls This would reduce
the compactive effort required for the backfill and reduce the build up of additional lateral
pressures on the wails
The backfill material should be placed in eight 8 inch maximum lifts and
compacted to at least 85 of its maximum dry density as established by ASTM Test
Method D 1557 78

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during site grading construction of the buttress fill and
foundation phases of the work This is to observe compliance with the design concepts
specifications or recommendations and to allow design changes in the event that
subsurface conditions differ from that anticipated prior to start of construction The
recommendations given in this report are contingent upon such observations
lf actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during construction are different
from those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made
