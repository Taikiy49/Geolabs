In general the project involves construction of a new parking structure as part of
the Bachelor Enlisted Quarters BEQ housing project at Marine Corps Base Hawaii at
Kaneohe Bay on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The new four story parking structure will be
located east of Harris Avenue near the intersection with Mokapu Drive The project site
currently consists of a large parking lot located in the area of a former landfill
In accordance with the project design requirements a static pile load test was
conducted on a dedicated augered cast in place ACIP pile on March 7 and 8 2013 for
the Bachelor Enlisted Quarters Parking Structure project This letter report presents the
results of our field observations and the test data obtained from the static pile load test
The augered cast in place pile for the load test was installed by Hawaiian Dredging
Construction Company and Condon Johnson Associates a Joint Venture HDCC CJA
The pile load test was conducted on an 18 inch diameter augered cast in place pile
with a length of approximately 65 3 feet relative to the existing ground surface To better
understand the load transfer mechanism at the pile soil interface the load test pile was
instrumented with nine vibrating wire embedment strain gauges Model No VCE 4200
manufactured by Geokon The embedment strain gauges were installed in the load test
pile at the following depths below the existing ground surface

The load test pile was located at the northwestern portion of the parking structure
project site Two 18 inch ACIP reaction piles with lengths of approximately 65 1 and
66 5 feet below the ground surface were installed on for the pile load
test program The test pile was installed on using a crane mounted drill
rig equipped with automatic monitoring equipment to provide the drilling depth drilling rate
auger torque stroke count etc on a near continuous basis The approximate locations of
the reaction piles and load test pile are shown on the ACIP Test Pile Location Plan
Plate 1 Installation records for the reaction piles and load test pile are presented in
Appendix A
The load test pile was drilled to a depth of about 65 3 feet below the existing ground
surface using a continuous flight auger and subsequently grouted from the bottom and up
After sound quality grout based on visual observation appeared at the top of the pile a
reinforcing cage was inserted into the grouted hole In addition the six embedment strain
gauges were installed onto a single threaded bar and inserted into the grouted hole at
about the center of the pile The depths at which the embedment strain gauges were
installed are presented in the table above
The piling subcontractor HDCC CJA provided the load testing equipment
including a reaction beam reference beams a hydraulic jack a hydraulic pump and other
accessories Based on the calibration data provided by the subcontractor an equation
relating to the applied load kips and hydraulic pump pressure psi was generated and
used during the load testing The strain gauge information and calibration sheets are
presented on Plates B 1 through B 4 of Appendix B
As requested Geolabs provided the data acquisition system including a
multiplexer a data logger and data acquisition program to collect the strain gauge
readings during the load testing The strain gauge readings were further used in the
analyses and evaluation of the static pile load test In addition displacements at the top of
the load test pile were measured and recorded by Geolabs
The test pile was loaded in general accordance with the Quick Load Test Method
ASTM D 1143 and modified by our office to suit the subsurface conditions at the project
site The design load of the pile was 300 kips and the maximum test load on the pile was
planned at 787 5 kips In general the test pile was loaded to the maximum test load of

The readings and the load displacement curve of the static load test are presented
on Plates 2 and 3 respectively Based on the shape of the curve of the compressive load
test it is our opinion that the load test pile is capable of sustaining the maximum test load
of 787 5 kips and the rate of the pile head movement generally was stable without signs of
appreciable creep
The six embedment strain gauges in the load test pile were connected using a
multiplexer to a Geokon Micro 10 data acquisition system to record the strains in the
six embedment strain gauges during the compressive pile load test Analyses were
conducted on reducing the data from the embedment strain gauges for evaluating the load
transfer between the ACIP pile and the surrounding soils The interpreted load transfer
curves in the load test pile are presented on Plate 4 of this letter report for reference
Based on the load test results it is our opinion that the parking structure may be
supported by ACIP piles with a diameter of 18 inches and lengths of approximately 50 and
60 feet long below the bottom of the pile cap elevation to achieve the allowable
compressive load capacities of about 200 and 300 kips respectively In addition it is our
opinion that installation of the production ACIP piles may proceed accordingly for the
project
