PROJECT DESCRIPTION
The Keola La i high rise residential building project is located at 600 Queen Street
48 008 in the Kaka ako area of Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The
property is approximately trapezoidal in shape and encompasses approximately
117 340 square feet in land area The project site is bounded in a clockwise direction by
South Street Kawaiahao Street Emily Street and Queen Street The layout of the new
residential development is shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
The property was the site of the former Queen Emmalani Tower project As part of
the Queen Emmalani Tower project we understand that driven pile foundations consisting
of over 1 300 piles were installed in about 1992 and 1993 at the project site for support of
the structures planned for the original development The driven piles consisted of
16 5 inch octagonal precast prestressed concrete piles designed based on an allowable
compressive load capacity of 70 and 100 tons per pile for the parking structure and tower
structure respectively Subsequent to the pile installation program development of the
Queen Emmalani Tower project was halted and the property was converted into an open
parking lot
The current project involves the development of the property under new
ownership with a revised design layout of the proposed tower and parking structure In
general the new development will consist of a 42 story high rise building providing
approximately 350 residential units The new high rise building tower structure will have
37 levels of residential floors constructed over a five level base parking structure with
commercial spaces at the ground floor level The new high rise building will have a
footprint of approximately 62 by 228 feet superimposed over the five level base parking
structure with a footprint encompassing the majority of the project site
Due to the revised layout of the project development a total of 246 new augered
cast in place ACIP piles were installed for support of the new structures planned for the
project The existing and new pilings where appropriate were incorporated into a mat
foundation for support of the new tower structure The ACIP piles have a diameter of
18 inches and length of approximately 40 and 50 feet to achieve an allowable compressive
load capacity of about 200 and 300 kips 100 and 150 tons per pile respectively As part
of the construction requirements a static load test was conducted on an instrumented
ACIP pile to evaluate the pile foundation design

AUGERED CAST IN PLACE PILE INSTALLATION
The scope of work discussed in the following sections herein includes
observation of the ACIP pile foundation installation for the Keola La i project The
General Contractor for the project is NORDIC PCL A Hawaiian Joint Venture and the
ACIP pile installation subcontractor is Morris Shea Bridge Company Inc
Test Pile Installation
A test pile program which consisted of installing eight ACIP test piles was
conducted for the project on The ACIP test piles included ACIP Pile
AP40 153 The locations of the test piles are shown on the ACIP Pile Location Plan
Plate 2 The purpose of the test pile installation program was to evaluate the adequacy of
the contractor s proposed equipment and installation methods The ACIP test piles were
considered satisfactory based on our field observations and load test therefore the test
piles were incorporated as part of the foundations for the project
Static Pile Load Test
In order to evaluate the adequacy of the recommended design lengths of the
ACIP pile foundation a static pile load test was conducted on a dedicated ACIP pile on
December 2 and 3 2005 The pile load test was conducted on an 18 inch diameter ACIP
pile with a length of approximately 51 feet relative to the existing ground surface To better
understand the load transfer mechanism at the pile soil interface the load test pile was
instrumented with six vibrating wire embedment strain gages installed at various depths in
GEOLABS INC
Hawaii California
A B Kaka ako LLC Page 5
W O 5630 10
the load test pile The load test results indicated that the load test pile generally behaved
like a friction pile with nearly 100 percent of the maximum test load approximately
750 kips being resisted by adhesion between the ACIP pile and the surrounding soils It
should be noted that a large portion of the frictional resistance of the pile about 250 kips
out of 750 kips was being provided by the top 10 feet of the load test pile
Based on our field observations and the static pile load test results we confirmed
that an embedment length of 50 feet below the existing ground surface would be sufficient
to achieve the design compressive load capacity of 150 tons per ACIP pile with a factor of
safety of at least 2 0 Detailed discussions and the static pile load test results are provided
in the report by Geolabs Inc entitled Static Pile Load Test Compression Keola La i
600 Queen Street Honolulu Oahu Hawaii dated

The production ACIP pile installation program consisting of installing 238 augered
cast in place piles 18 inch diameter was conducted from through

automatic monitoring equipment to provide the grout flow rate batch number pressure
etc on a near continuous basis During the pile installation operation logs were
maintained for each ACIP pile which included information pertaining to pile number date
installed depth of pile grout volume used grout return depth etc The locations of the
production ACIP piles installed are shown on the attached ACIP Pile Location Plan Plate

piles have been installed to sufficient depths and or resistance to provide the design load
capacity of 200 kips 100 tons or 300 kips 150 tons per pile with a safety factor of 2 0
