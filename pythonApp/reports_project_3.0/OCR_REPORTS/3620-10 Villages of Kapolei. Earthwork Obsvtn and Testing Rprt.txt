EARTHWORK OPERATION
The project site is located within the existing Villages of Kapolei development in the
District of Ewa on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project site is generally bounded by
Kapolei Parkway to the north Kapolei Intermediate School to the east and the future
Makai Villages 7 8 to the west and south
The roadway and utility construction for the subject portion of Roads A and AA
alignments were performed by Albert C Kobayashi Inc The earthwork operation
consisted of clearing of the existing ground over excavation of the surficial expansive
clays preparation of pavement subgrades placement and compaction of select borrow
and base course and utility trench excavation and backfill
Following the clearing operation the existing ground was generally over excavated
to approximately 20 5 inches below the finish grades to provide the recommended
pavement sections The over excavated subgrades were generally scarified to a minimum

During the earthwork operation samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor
and the test results are summarized on Plate 1 of this report
Periodic field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations to
confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures and soil conditions
were noted A total of 135 field density tests including retests of failed areas were
performed using the nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM Test Designation
D 2922 The results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 2 1
through 2 8 of this report These tests were taken on the prepared ground surfaces and
on constructed engineered fills and backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were
estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes and construction plans
These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied
by the methods used Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the
specified minimum compaction was obtained and or approved by our field representative
based on visual observation and hand probing
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the earthwork operations during the roadway and
utility construction for the above project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period
covered by this report were performed in general accordance with the intent of the project
plans and specifications and our geotechnical engineering recommendations presented in
the referenced report dated August 12 1996 and subsequent recommendations
LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork was
performed without our observation However the level of earthwork observation and
testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some of the geotechnical
risks to the project It should be recognized that there are variations in the accuracy and
