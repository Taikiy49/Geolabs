DRILLED SHAFT OBSERVATION REPORT 
REHABILITATION OF BUILDINGS AND YARD AREAS AT PIERS 34/35 
LIGHT POLE FOUNDATION 
HONOLULU, OAHU, HAWAII 
Dear Mr. Luke: 
As requested, Geolabs, Inc. has provided geotechnical engineering services 
during the drilled shaft foundation installation for the new light pole structures at the 
above project site. This letter report summarizes our geotechnical observations 
performed during the drilled shaft installation for the above light pole structures. In 
general, the purpose of our geotechnical engineering services was to observe the drilled 
shaft foundation installation for general conformance with the project plans and 
specifications. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The project site is located at Piers 34 and 35 at Honolulu Harbor on the Island of 
Oahu, Hawaii. The project involves relocating the University of Hawaii Marine Center to 
the existing Hawaii Stevedores’ Building. In addition, the project includes the installation 
of seven new light pole structures supported by drilled shaft foundation. The project 
location and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map, Plate 1. 
A total of seven, 48-inch diameter cast-in-place concrete drilled shafts were 
installed to support new light pole structures required for the project. A minimum drilled 
shaft length of 14 feet below the finished asphaltic concrete pavement was specified. In 
addition, construction of a trial shaft was performed prior to the installation of the seven 
production shafts. The approximate locations of the trial shaft and production drilled 
shafts for the light pole structures are shown on the Site Plan, Plate 2. 
DRILLED SHAFT INSTALLATION

production shafts and one trial shaft with 48-inch diameter were installed between

is provided on the Drilled Shaft Installation Summary, Plate 3. In general, a Geolabs 
representative observed the shaft excavation, placement of the steel reinforcements, 
and concrete placement operations during the drilled shaft installation.   
The shafts were excavated using an EDT-7 drill mounted on a Caterpillar Model 
345 track-mounted excavator with 48-inch and 42-inch digging buckets; 48-inch and 
42-inch core barrels; and a 42-inch cleanout bucket. Temporary casing with a diameter 
of 48 inches was utilized to case the entire length of the drilled shafts during the shaft 
construction. The drilled shafts were excavated to elevations of about -8 feet Mean Sea 
Level (MSL). 
After the shaft holes were drilled to their designated tip elevations, the reinforcing 
bar (rebar) cages were placed into the drilled holes. Rebar cage spacers were used to 
provide the clearance associated with a minimum concrete cover of 3 inches between 
the sidewalls of the drilled hole and the sides of the rebar cage. 
The shaft concrete was placed after placement of the rebar cage using a 
4-inch OD steel pipe by tremie methods. Shaft concrete with a minimum 28-day 
compressive strength of 5,000 pounds per square inch (psi) was used. During concrete 
placement, the bottom of the tremie pipe was immersed in at least 5 feet of concrete, as 
required in the specifications. 
For one of the drilled shafts, LP 15, the measured concrete slump was 
6.75 inches, which was below the minimum concrete slump of 7 inches in the project 
specifications. Based on our observation of the actual concrete volume installed in the 
shaft, the slightly low concrete slump for this shaft was considered satisfactory. 
Please note that two shafts (LP 13 and LP 14) were relocated about 6 feet 
mauka (northeast) of their original location due to underground obstructions that 
appeared to be existing drilled shafts. The location of the top of all the production shafts 
should be measured using optical survey equipment to verify the shaft position is within 
the tolerance in the project specifications. The locations of the relocated shafts (LP 13 
and LP 14) should be verified by the electrical engineer. 
Based on our field observations, we believe that the drilled shafts for the light 
pole foundations have been installed in general conformance with the project plans and 
specifications and within the limits of generally accepted construction practices.
