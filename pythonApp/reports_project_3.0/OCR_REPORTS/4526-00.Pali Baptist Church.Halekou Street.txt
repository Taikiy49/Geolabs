PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located on the existing Pali View Baptist Church property on
Halekou Street near the intersection of Halekou Street and Kamehameha Highway in the
Kanehoe area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii Based on the information provided we
understand that it is proposed to construct a singie story building parking areas and other
structures on the existing Pali View Baptist Church property The design for the proposed
single story structure includes a sanctuary with a pulpit and an attached lanai In addition
the existing floor slab of the sanctuary will be elevated to the same level as the adjacent
building floors
Detailed information pertaining to the structural loads of the new addition was not
available at the time this report was prepared However we understand that the sanctuary
will include a pulpit with an elevated floor supported on post footings The sanctuary and
lanai will be of slab on grade construction with perimeter CMU walls Based on the existing
topography and current design concept minimal site grading work is anticipated for the
project construction

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lavas from two
shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be
middie to iate Pliocene in age and forms the bulk of the western third of the island The
younger shield Koolau is estimated to be of late Pliocene to early Pleistocene Ice Age and
forms the majority of the eastern two thirds of the island Waianae became extinct while
Koolau was still active and its eastern flank was partially buried below Koolau lavas banking
against its eastern flank
GEOLABS INC
W O 4526 00 Page 4
The project area is located on the southern flank of the Koolau shield and composed
of basaltic rock built by the extrusion of the lavas of the Koolau Volcanic Series These rocks
are generally characterized by flows of jointed dense vesicular basalt with interbedded thin
clinker layers In situ chemical weathering of the Koolau lava flows has occurred for the last
1 to 2 million years The weathering process has formed a mantle of residual soils which
grade to saprolite with depth In general saprolite is comprised mainly of silty and clayey
material and is typical of the tropical weathering of volcanic rocks The saprolite grades to
basaltic rock formation with depth
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located on the existing Pali View Baptist Church property on
Halekou Street in Kanehoe on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project site is accessible
through a paved driveway and gate on Halekou Street which is situated approximately 100
to 200 feet west of the intersection of Halekou Street and Kamehameha Highway
The existing Pali View Baptist Church is a single story U shaped structure with floor
areas for chapel classrooms and other rooms Based on our field observations it appears

structure faces north towards the existing parking lot Existing residential houses currently
surround the property with Halekou Street running along the north end of the property
Based on our field observations and the site plan provided the project site is
relatively level with an existing ground elevation of approximately 280 feet Mean Sea Level

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field exploration the project site is generally underlain by soft to very
stiff to hard sandy clays extending to the maximum depth explored of approximately 16 5 feet
below the existing ground surface Based on the subsoil conditions encountered at the
project site we believe that a shallow foundation system consisting of spread and or
continuous footings bearing on the recompacted in situ stiff soils may be used to support the
proposed new addition
Based on our laboratory tests the near surface clayey soils exhibit slight to moderate
shrink swell potential when subjected to fluctuations in the soil moisture contents To reduce
the potential for distress to the future lightly loaded slabs on grade resulting from the swelling
of the near surface soils we recommend that the on site clayey soils be capped with a
minimum of 12 inches of non expansive select granular fill capping fill material below the
slab cushion In general the subgrade soils should be moisture conditioned to at least
2 percent above the optimum moisture content and recompacted to a minimum of 90 percent
relative compaction The slab subgrade should be kept moist and free of shrinkage cracks
prior to placement of capping fill cushion fill and or slab concrete Our geotechnical
GEOLABS INC
W O 4526 00 Page 6
engineering recommendations pertaining to the design of foundations and site preparation
are further discussed in the following sections of this report
Foundations
Based on the generally stiff subsoil conditions encountered at the site we believe that
shallow spread and or continuous footings may be used for support of the proposed new
addition An allowable bearing pressure of up to 3 000 pounds per square foot psf may be
used for the design of shallow foundations bearing on the recompacted stiff clayey soils or
engineered fills needed to achieve the final design grades This bearing value is for dead
plus live loads and may be increased by one third 1 3 for transient loads such as those
caused by wind or seismic forces
For footings bearing on the stiff in situ soils or new engineered fills the bottom of the
footing excavations should be recompacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction to
provide a relatively firm and smooth bearing surface prior to placement of reinforcing steel
and concrete If soft and or loose materials are encountered at the bottom of the footing
excavations the materials should be over excavated to expose the underlying firm materials
The over excavation may be backfilled with on site soils compacted to a minimum of 90
percent relative compaction or the footing bottom may be extended down to the underlying
competent material
Lateral loads acting on the structures may be resisted by frictional resistance
developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil and by passive earth
pressure acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system A coefficient of
friction of 0 3 may be used for footings bearing on the in situ stiff soils or engineered fills
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be estimated by using an equivalent fluid
pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth pcf This assumes that the soil
around footings is well compacted The passive pressure should be reduced for foundations
located on slopes Unless covered by pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the
upper 12 inches of soil should be neglected
GEOLABS INC
W O 4526 00 Page 7
In general footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest
adjacent finish grades Foundations that are located next to utility trenches or easements
should be embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane
extending upward from the bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the
utility lines This requirement is necessary to avoid surcharging adjacent below grade
structures with additional structural loads and to reduce the potential for foundation
settlement
We estimate that footing settlements under the anticipated design loads for footings
bearing on the recompacted in situ stiff soils or engineered fills as recommended herein to
be less than 1 inch total with differential settlements between adjacent footings on the order
of 0 5 inches
We recommend that footing excavations be observed by a representative from
Geolabs Inc prior to placement of reinforcing steel and concrete to confirm the foundation
bearing conditions and the required embedment depths
Slabs On Grade
Based on our field exploration the project site is generally underlain by near surface
clayey soils with slight to moderate expansion potential when subjected to moisture
fluctuations We recommend that the subgrade soils be properly prepared and capped with
at least 12 inches of non expansive select granular fill capping fill material below the slab
cushion to reduce potential future distress to the lightly loaded slabs on grade resulting from
shrinking and swelling of the clayey soils due to changes in the moisture content The
capping fill should also extend beyond the new slab on grade footprint a minimum of
24 inches
The non expansive select granular capping fill should be placed as soon as possible
after the subgrade soils are prepared in accordance with the recommendations presented
in the Site Preparation section of this report The prepared capping fill and the underlying
subgrade soils should be wetted and kept moist prior to final placement of concrete
Where shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of the subgrade we recommend that
GEOLABS INC
W O 4526 00 Page 8
the soils be thoroughly moistened to close the cracks Saturation and subsequent yielding
of the exposed subgrade due to inclement weather and poor drainage may require
over excavation of the soft areas and replacement with well compacted engineered fill
In general we recommend that a minimum 4 inch thick cushion layer consisting of
No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation or basaitic termite barrier BTB be placed
below the slabs to provide more uniform support of the slabs and to serve as a capillary
moisture break To reduce moisture infiltration and subsequent damage to floor coverings
an impervious moisture vapor barrier such as a plastic membrane is recommended on top
of the No 3B Fine gravel Two inches of moist sand may be considered above the plastic
membrane to provide protection of the membrane and to aid in curing of the slab concrete
Provisions of the 2 inches of moist sand above the vapor membrane will be left up to the
discretion of the project structural engineer Flexible floor coverings such as carpet or sheet
vinyl should be considered since they can better mask minor slab cracking
The thickened edges of slab adjacent to unpaved areas should be embedded at
least 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade Areas adjacent to the slab should
be backfilled tightly against the slab with low expansion impervious soil and graded to divert
water away from the slab to reduce the potential for water ponding around the foundations
Site Preparation
Grading plans were not available at the time this report was prepared However we
anticipate minor grading with cuts and fills on the order of less than 2 feet may be expected
for the proposed project Site grading operations should be observed by a representative
from Geolabs Inc It is important that a representative from our office observe whether the
exposed soil conditions during excavation are similar to those encountered in our
exploration The following recommendations are intended to provide guidelines for the
design of site grading work
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared Debris rubbish and other unsuitable materials should be removed and
GEOLABS INC
W O 4526 00 Page 9
disposed of off site Slabs and foundations of the existing structures to be demolished
should be completely removed Soft weak or yielding areas disclosed during clearing
operations or over excavations resulting from the demolition should be backfilled with
well compacted engineered fill Existing utilities to be abandoned should be removed and
the resulting excavation should be properly backfilled with well compacted engineered fill
Utilities to be abandoned in place under the proposed structures should be backfilled by
pumping lean concrete under low pressure
After clearing and demolition the areas within the future slab on grade limits should
be over excavated where necessary to provide the minimum 12 inch thick capping layer of
non expansive select granular fill material below the slab subgrade level i e bottom of the
cushion fill layer No 3B Fine gravel or BTB The non expansive select granular fill material
should extend a minimum of 2 feet beyond the perimeter of the proposed slab on grade
areas The over excavated subgrades should then be scarified to a minimum depth of
10 inches properly moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Relative
compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the
maximum dry density of the same soil determined in accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 1557 91 Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight
corresponding to the maximum dry density
Based on our field exploration we believe that the excavated on site soils may be
re used as a source of general fill material up to the bottom of the non expansive capping
fill layer provided that they are moisture conditioned and compacted as recommended
herein In general the on site clayey material to be used as general fill should be
moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture placed in level lifts
not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent
relative compaction
GEOLABS INC
W O 4526 00 Page 10
The non expansive capping fill material recommended below slabs on grades should
consist of well graded granular materials less than 3 inches in maximum dimension The
material should have a laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR value of 20 or more and
should have a maximum swell of less than 1 percent when tested in accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1883 In addition it should contain between 10 and 30 percent
passing the No 200 sieve Imported fill materials should be tested by Geolabs Inc for
conformance with our recommendations prior to delivery to the project site for its intended
use Imported materials should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture
content and compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction
Drainage
The finished grades outside the building addition should be sloped to shed water away
from foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also advised that gutter
systems be installed around the building and that the discharge be diverted away from the
foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near the foundations and
slabs should also be avoided Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have
concrete bottoms and drains to reduce the potential for water infiltration into the subsoils
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and maintained to drain surface
runoff away from the slabs and foundations
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the above
foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation and
subsequent loss of strength and or heaving of the near surface silty and clayey soils The
foundation excavations should be properly backfilled against the footings immediately after
setting of the concrete to reduce the potential for water infiltration

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling
three borings to depths of about 16 5 feet below the existing ground surface at the
approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were drilled using truck
mounted drilling equipment equipped with hollow stem augers
The materials encountered in the boring were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer geologist who monitored the drilling operations on
a near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by testing
in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representations of the materials
encountered are provided on the Log of Boring Plate A 1
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 3550 84 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a
3 inch OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches In
addition some samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils
by driving a 2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same hammer and drop
The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an 18 inch
drive are shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Boring at the appropriate
sample depths
Pocket penetrometer tests were performed on selected soil layers in the field The
pocket penetrometer tests provide an indication of the unconfined compressive strength of
the sample Results of the pocket penetrometer tests are summarized on the Log of Borings
at the appropriate sample depths

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content ASTM D 2216 and unit weight ASTM D 2937 determinations
were performed on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil
properties The results of these tests are presented on the Log of Borings at the appropriate
sample depths
Two Atterberg Limits tests ASTM D 4318 was performed on selected samples of
the soils to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits and to aid in soil classification Results of
this test are summarized on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depth Graphic
presentation of the test results is provided on Plate B 1
One laboratory California Bearing Ratio Test ASTM D 1883 was performed on a
bulk sample of the near surface soils to evaluate the pavement support characteristics of
the soils The test result is presented on Plate B 2
Two one inch ring swell tests were performed on selected soil samples to evaluate
the swelling potential of the near surface soils The results of the tests are summarized on
Plate B 3
