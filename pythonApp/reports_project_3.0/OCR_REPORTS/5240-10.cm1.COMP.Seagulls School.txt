This report summarizes our earthwork observation and testing services performed
during the site grading operations, foundation excavation, pavement construction, and
utility installation for the project from September 2009 through January 2010 in accordance
with the scope of services outlined in our fee proposal dated September 1, 2009.
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is along Alii Nui Drive in Ewa on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. The
school consists of an administration building and three classroom buildings along with
parking areas and a play lot. Site grading involved filling up to about 2-feet thick. A site
plan is shown on Plate 1.
EARTHWORK OPERATIONS
Jayar Construction, Inc. was the grading contractor for the project. The site grading
consisted of clearing, footing excavation, subgrade preparation, placement and
compaction of fills and backfills, pavement construction, and utility trench excavation and
backfill.

During the site grading operations, samples of the on-site materials utilized as fill
and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing. The
maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory
in general accordance with ASTM D 1557 (Modified Proctor), and the test results are
summarized on Plate 2.
The fill and backfill materials were moisture-conditioned to near the optimum
moisture content, and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density
as determined in accordance with ASTM D 1577. In the driveway and parking areas, the
base course fill material was compacted to a minimum of 95 percent of the maximum dry
density. Due to the high moisture content of the in-situ soils, the compaction requirement
of the pavement subgrade was reduced to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry
density. It should be noted that majority of the pavement subgrade areas were compacted
to a minimum 95 percent of the maximum dry density.
During the site grading and utility backfilling operations, periodic field density tests
were performed in general accordance with ASTM D 6938 (Nuclear Gauge Method).
Results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 3.1 through 3.9 of
this report. These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on
constructed fills and backfills at the site. Test locations and elevations were estimated by
pacing and interpolating from on-site survey stakes. These locations and elevations should
be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used. Areas assessed
to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained.
Our services consisted of part-time field observation of the contractor's fill
placement, subgrade preparation, and compaction procedures and confirmation of
subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above grading operations.
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests, it is our opinion that the site grading and utility installation work for the
above-referenced project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this
report were performed in conformance with the general intent of the recommendations in
the following references.

LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site. Therefore, some of the earthwork may
have been performed without our observation. However, the level of earthwork
observation and testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some
of the geotechnical risks to the project. It should be recognized that there are variations
in the accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the
earthwork construction and materials.

geotechnical engineering practice; no warranty is expressed or implied. Provision of
construction monitoring by an engineer is not insurance, nor does it guarantee
