It is proposed to construct a new athletic facility to support the needs of the

for baseball and football track usage Due to space limitations on the campus it is
proposed to situate the football field track on a structural deck to allow utilization of the
area below the field for school bus maintenance and other purposes
Columns and continuous bearing walls are anticipated for the support of the
structural deck It is anticipated that the column and wall loads of the proposed structure
will be moderate to heavy

The project site is located in the Kamehameha Heights area of Honolulu Oahu

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
The island of Oahu was built up during the late Pliocene and early Pleistocene by
the activities of two basaltic shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau Koolau is the
younger of the two and reached the end of its main shield building phase about 2 million
years ago The project site is located on the southern flank of Koolau
Koolau was built by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from northwest to southeast
trending rift zones These lava flows are interbedded pahoehoe and aa flows with
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2418 00 Page 5
pahoehoe being the dominant form The project site is situated on Kamehameha Heights
which is an erosional remnant ridge above Kalihi and Puunui
Pahoehoe is characterized by a smooth ropy or billowy surface and an internal
structure of generally vesicular rock Cavities are commonly encountered in pahoehoe
lavas These cavities were formed when the rock was still molten during its deposition
and represent both lava tubes intraflow cavities and interflow cavities such as blisters
and pockets
Aa is characterized by a rough spiny surface of clinker and has a core of dense
rock Generally there is another layer of clinker at the base of the flow Cavities are
occasionally found in aa flows
Lava tubes are formed when liquid lava drains from the cooling flow leaving a
hollow tube like structure which may extend for a large distance longitudinally along the
flow Interflow cavities are generally smaller in horizontal extent
Basalts are considered to be highly permeable rock and transmit water quite
readily in both the horizontal and vertical directions Water is transmitted through the
porous rock matrix along joints fractures and interflow contacts through cavities and
along clinker layers The permeability of the clinker and cavities is very high therefore
these serve as the major water transmission features
In situ weathering of the basaltic lava flows at the project site has generated a
mantle of residual soils and saprolites over the underlying basaltic lava rock Saprolite
is a form of residual soil that retains the structure of the original rock mass from which it
was derived

Soil Conditions
In general the drilled borings encountered approximately seven 7 to twenty five
25 feet of very stiff clayey silts overlying weathered in situ basalt formation These stiff
soils are generally residual and saprolite soils derived from the in place chemical
weathering of the parent basalt rock formation Some of the stiff soils represent man
made fills placed during the development of the campus area
W O 2418 00 Page 7
The underlying basalt formation was generally in a highly to moderately weathered
state Basalt in this stage usually has good foundation support characteristics but is
generally easier to excavate than unweathered rock
Groundwater was not encountered in the drilled borings during our field exploration
nor was seepage noted in our subsequent monitoring of the piezometers installed in
Boring and 5 However seepage is frequently encountered in excavations in
rock depending upon weather conditions and site specific geological factors Therefore
a potential seepage or transient perched groundwater could be encountered during future
construction
DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based upon our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering analyses it
is our opinion that the proposed athletic facilities project is feasible from a geotechnical
engineering point of view It is our opinion that depending upon the depth to the
underlying basalt formation and the magnitude of the structural loads either drilled pier
or spread footing foundations may be utilized to support the proposed site improvements
Site Grading
Details of the proposed sitework were not available for the present preliminary
geotechnical engineering exploration However we anticipate that the site grading for the
proposed athletic facilities will be predominantly excavation to create level playing fields
and for the proposed lower levels It is also assumed that some minor fill placement and
some minor amounts of external grading work will be required The following discussions
are given to provide preliminary input for site grading design
W O 2418 00 Page 8
Prior to the commencement of any earthwork all areas within the grading limits
should be cleared of all vegetation and deleterious materials The resulting grub spoil
material and demolition debris should be disposed of properly off site Organic soils
and or topsoil may be stockpiled for use in landscaping However caution should be
exercised to avoid contamination of fill and or backfill material with the topsoil
Any soft weak or yielding areas encountered during the clearing operations which
are not scheduled for structure excavation should be over excavated to firm ground and
the resulting excavation should be backfilled with well compacted engineered fill
Generally the on site soils should be suitable for use as engineered fill or backfills
if additional imported material is required the material should conform to the requirements
stipulated in Section 30 of the Standard Specifications for Public Works Construction
Select Borrow for Subbase Course dated September 1986
All fills and backfills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding eight 8 inches
in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density as
established by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Foundation Support
We anticipate that most of the column and wall loads generated by the proposed
athletic facility will be relatively heavy This exploration was conducted as a preliminary
effort to provide data for initial site excavation and foundation design input Structural
details were not available at the time this report was prepared We recommend additional
exploration be conducted when structural details are available and the actual location
and grades of the proposed site improvements had been established However we are
GEOLABS HAWAII

providing the following discussions on foundation support to provide preliminary design
guidelines and to provide information for budget estimation
Based on the conditions encountered in our exploration it is our opinion that either
spread footing or drilled pier foundations may be used to support the structural loads
both vertical and lateral generated by the proposed athletic facility Primary consider
ations in the selection of the foundation support system will be the depth to the underlying
basalt formation and the magnitude of the structural loads It is most likely that the final
design will incorporate a combination of the two 2 foundation systems
Generally our borings encountered competent basalt formation at relatively shallow
depths below the existing ground surface in the borings situated along the lowermost
edge and the uppermost corner of the proposed facility The depths to basalt in the
borings drilled on the existing Mawaena Field were somewhat greater however relatively
competent residual soil was encountered at relatively shallow depths in these borings
Therefore it appears that spread footing foundations bearing on in situ basalt could
be used to support the structural deck spanning over the area currently occupied by the
existing bus yard and canoe shop The foundation system for the portion of the deck
structure along the westerly portion of Mawaena Field would depend upon the structural
loads i e spread footing foundations could be utilized for light to moderate loads
whereas drilled piers would probably be required for heavy structural loads
For preliminary design purposes we recommend that an allowable bearing
capacity of 4 000 p s f be utilized for the design of spread footings bearing on residual
soils An allowable bearing capacity of 6 000 p s f may be utilized for the preliminary
design of either drilled piers or spread footing foundations bearing on in situ weathered

basalt formation These allowable bearing values may be increased by one third when
temporary wind and seismic loads are considered
Exterior footings in level areas should be embedded a minimum of two 2 feet
below the lowest adjacent grade For foundations situated in or adjacent to sloping
areas additional embedment would be required There should be sufficient embedment
to provide a minimum of about ten 10 feet of horizontal setback from the toe of the
footing to the nearest adjacent slope face
Cavity Probing
Cavities and other voids are commonly encountered in basalt formations To
reduce the possibility of foundation settlements resulting from the collapse of such voids
we recommend that a program of probing and grouting the footing locations be
considered when preparing specifications and engineering estimates for the project
For preliminary purposes it should be assumed that the probes would be drilled
at each column location and on 10 foot centers for line footings The probe holes should
be a minimum of three 3 inches in diameter and should extend a minimum of ten 10
feet below the footing subgrade The holes should be backfilled with sand cement grout
injected at low pressures
If a cavity or void is suspected at a footing location additional probes should be
drilled to attempt to delineate the extent of the suspected void
In order to avoid drilling the probes inside the footing excavations which would
increase drilling costs it is suggested that the probing and grouting work be performed
after site clearing but prior to the commencement of excavation work
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2418 00 Page 11
It should be further assumed that the probing and grouting operations would be
observed by the geotechnical engineer to see that the above recommendations are
properly implemented and to evaluate other solutions to backfill potential voids
Excavation
This exploration was conducted as a preliminary effort to provide data for initial
site excavation and foundation design input Details such as the limits and depths of
future excavation were not available at the time this report was prepared We
recommend additional exploration be conducted when these details are available to
provide more information for the development of design and construction recommenda
tions However we are providing the following discussions to provide preliminary design
guidelines and to provide information for budget estimation
Based on our exploration it is our opinion that conventional methods may be used
for excavation on the project If properly designed open cut construction will most likely
be the most economical method Based on our exploration a temporary cut slopes of
one and one half horizontal to one vertical in soil and one horizontal to one
vertical 1H 1V in weathered rock formation may be utilized for the excavation sidewalls
It is Our opinion that generally conventional excavation and ripping may be utilized
for the excavation work However since a large portion of the excavation will occur in in
situ volcanic formation it is possible that some areas of very hard excavation ripping may
be encountered and the use of hoerams or the limited use of explosives if permissible
may be considered to expedite the progress of the excavation work The excavation
method within applicable safety regulations and practices should be left to the discretion
of the contractor

Previous experience with excavations in basalt formations indicate zones of loose
material and zones of seepage are sometimes encountered Additional field exploration
is recommended to better delineate subsurface conditions at the project site with respect
to these concerns In the event that loose zones and or seepage are encountered in the
field during construction it is our recommendation that the project geotechnical engineer
be consulted for additional recommendations
If the size of the excavation is a major consideration it may be possible to make
an open cut excavation with near vertical sidewalls However the boring data and other
previous experiences with similar formations indicate that while such excavations will
frequently stand unsupported for short term durations there is a tendency for excavation
sidewalls to unravel when exposed Therefore it is recommended that the excavation
sidewalls be stabilized to reduce the potential for sloughing or collapse
The actual design of shoring and or bracing should be left to the contractor
However it should be noted that there is basalt formation at relatively shallow depths in
the project area Conventional sheetpiles will not be able to penetrate this formation
sufficiently to provide an adequate toe in penetration or embedment of the sheetpiles at
the base of the excavation to develop sufficient lateral support Therefore soldier piles
and lagging or a tie back system may be required
Consideration should also be given to stabilization by pregrouting of the rock
formation prior to excavation or by the application of a binder to the sidewall faces
immediately after excavation Pregrouting of the excavation line with a weak portland
cement or chemical grout could cement or bind any loose or fractured zones into a
relatively competent mass which could stand temporarily as vertical sidewalls This work
could be performed concurrently with the foundation probing and grouting work

Slabs On Grade
It is anticipated that the ground floors of the proposed athletic facility will be
concrete slabs on grade Concrete floor slabs subject to vehicular traffic are considered
rigid pavements and will be addressed in a subsequent section of this report
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2418 00 Page 15
Our laboratory testing indicates that the on site clayey silt soils have a moderate
to high swell potential when saturated under a load roughly equivalent to a concrete floor
slab and cushion This swelling potential is more pronounced for these on site soils when
they have been remolded i e excavated and then recompacted To reduce the potential
for future damage to floor slabs resulting from the swelling of the underlying soils we
recommend that conventional slabs and their cushions may be supported by a minimum
of eighteen 18 inches of select non expansive material moisture conditioned to
near optimum moisture content and compacted to 90 relative dry density as determined
by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 A four 4 inch layer of 3B Fine gravel should be
provided below the slabs as cushion fill
The above recommended select material may be eliminated where the slab
subgrade is immediately underlain by in situ basalt formation In this case the slab and
cushion may be supported directly by the basalt
In order to prevent future moisture infiltration and subsequent damage to floor
coverings an impervious moisture membrane may be considered below the slabs
provided an inch of moist fine sand is placed above the plastic membrane to help
concrete curing

Rock Coring
Recovery REC is used as a subjective guide to the interpretation of the relative
quality of rock masses Recovery is defined as the actual length of material recovered
from a coring attempt vs the length of the core attempt e g if 3 7 feet of material is
recovered from a 5 0 foot core run the recovery would be 74 and would be shown on
the Boring Logs as REC 74
The Rock Quality Designation RQD is also a subjective guide to the relative
quality of rock masses RQD is defined as the percentage of the core run that is sound
material that is in excess of four 4 inches in length without any discontinuities
discounting any drilling induced fractures or breaks if 2 5 feet of sound material is
