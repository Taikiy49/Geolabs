INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our preliminary geotechnical engineering
exploration performed to aid in evaluating the nature of the subsurface conditions at the
project site This preliminary exploration was conducted to obtain information to aid in
the development of a master plan for the site utilization and to provide information
pertinent to the feasibility of developing the site for residential and recreational purposes
and to provide preliminary information pertaining to the design of proposed site grading
and infrastructure installation Our exploration was conducted in general accordance with
our proposal to P V T dated

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
The project site is situated in the Lualualei Valley at the base of Puu Heleakala a
high ridge which extends along the eastern limits of the proposed development The
climate along the west side of the ridge which makes up a portion of the Waianae range
is typified by little annual rainfall
The Lualualei valley was formed during the Tertiary Period by the lower and middle
members of the Waianae Volcanic Series Erosion has since removed most of the
western slope of the Waianae shield and exposed the internal structure of the volcano
This erosion formed the large broad Lualualei Valley which is choked with enormous
accumulations of alluvium and colluvium The older consolidated alluvium of the western
portion of the Waianae Range appears to be graded to various elevations reflecting higher
stands of the sea during the Pleistocene Epoch These alluvial deposits are largely a
result of stream deposits and to a lesser extent mud flows Towards the mouth of the
valley the non marine alluvium grades to marine deposits of stratified clay with lenticular
beds of sand and gravel
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2732 00 Page 8
Eustatic fluctuations of sea level in the Hawaiian islands have caused changes in
the shoreline in the past The alluvial deposits in the valley and abutting the Waianae
range grade in part to former high sea levels and is being slowly removed by present day
stream erosion Weathering of the upper portions of the ridges has led to the
accumulation of the deposits of colluvium found at their bases
The soils underlying the proposed developmental site range from Mokuleia clay
consisting of recent alluvium of high plasticity overlying coral deposits and consolidated
terrestrial sediments In addition Lualualei stony clay occurs in the alluvial fans adjacent
to drainageways and is very plastic and often contains gypsum crystals

FINDINGS AND DISCUSSIONS
General
Deposits of expansive clay soils are frequently found at the bases of ridges and
steep slopes in Hawaii These clay soils have low shear strength values and are often
subject to downslope creep movement if the soils become saturated or have surcharge
loads placed on them Such downslope creep movements have resulted in significant
property damage at locations in eastern Oahu At all of these sites the subdivision
developments were underlain by thick deposits of clay soils on sloping terrain and
portions of the clay soils were left in place during the initial development of the sites
Over time the effects of grading and wetting of the clay soils resulted in creep movement
of the soils Generally in sloping terrains all adobe clays should be removed prior to
new site grading and all surface construction
In level areas expansive clay soils present a risk to development in terms of the
potential distresses to foundations pavements and other site improvements as a result
of the low strength parameters and swelling potential of the clays Expansive soils when
wetted from a dry and compacted state frequently have sufficient swelling pressure to lift
and crack foundations and slabs The saturated clay soils also have poor pavement
support characteristics which can contribute to the rapid deterioration of pavements
unless a sufficiently thick and strong pavement section is utilized with careful attention
also given to provision of adequate subdrainage and supportive subgrades
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2732 00 Page 11
Generally the potential risks of development of expansive clay soils on level ground
may be minimized by proper grading design by stabilization of the soils and by proper
foundation and pavement design
Area 1
In Area 1 with the exception of a small portion of the area east of Lualualei Naval
Road in the general vicinity of test pits nos 21 through 24 and along the upper eastern
boundaries of the project site east of Lualualei Naval Road this area appears to be
underlain by substantial depths of clay soils which exhibit signs of instability
The clays which were encountered in the Area 1 portion of the site are highly
expansive Addition of moisture to the in situ soils would very likely cause the soils to
expand considerably and subsequent drying would cause the soils to shrink Our
laboratory testing for swell potential shows the swell potential of the on site clays to range
from 14 to 41 Repeated shrink swell cycles can result in a form of soil creep known
as solifluction This is caused by water entering the surface cracks and causing the
internal soils to swell thereby wedging the soil mass in a downslope direction
In addition our exploration and laboratory testing indicates that in situ moisture
contents of the clay soils are close to or in excess of the plastic limits of the soils i e the
moisture content at which a soil begins to behave as a plastic substance When the soil s
moisture contents are at or above their plastic limits they are prone to deformation and
plastic creep Our subsurface exploration indicates that there are zones of soils in Area
1 which exhibit indications of previous or current creeping of the soils down the hillside
These zones of slippage were encountered in most areas to the bottom limits of the clay
soils from the test pit observations
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2732 00 Page 12
The placement of additional fills and construction of permanent steep cut slopes
along with the installation of utilities roadways and surface structures within and upon the
Clay soils in this area would entail a very high degree of risk of future landslide
movements Due to the high shrink swell potential and low shear strength potential of the
adobe clay it is our opinion that there is a strong potential for future lateral ground
movement of the clay soils when wetted and or placed under a large surcharge load
It is our opinion that the development of the areas underlain by substantial depths
of clay is not practical from a geotechnical point of view short of removal of the materials
or the use of elaborate subsurface drainage interception shoring and stabilization fil
buttressing systems
It is our further opinion that it is not practicable to utilize this area for residential or
structural development Unless the clay soils are completely removed the potential
downslope creep of the soils would damage surface structures roadways and utility
installations Consideration was given to the use of drilled piers or caissons to support
proposed structures however it is our opinion that the interaction between the piers and
the creeping soils would most likely result in shear failure of the piers
It is possible that it may be feasible to develop Area 1 as a golf course if certain
risks of future ground movement and high requirements to mitigate possible maintenance
damage is acceptable There is a potential of causing distresses to Lualualei Naval Road
as a result of grading in Area 1 It is our opinion that further detailed exploration and
analyses will be required to assess the potential of distress to the existing road and to
develop a means for predicting the behavior of soils under the influences of the proposed
grading
