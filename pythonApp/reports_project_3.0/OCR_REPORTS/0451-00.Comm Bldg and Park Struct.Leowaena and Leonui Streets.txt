I INTRODUCTION
General
This report presents the results of our pre purchase
feasibility level investigation to determine the sub
surface soil and foundation conditions for the project
The purpose of the investigation was to provide subsurface
information necessary to determine the feasibility of
constructing a light commercial building and parking lot
on the subject site The study included limited subsurface
borings laboratory testing and engineering analyses The
report summarizes our work on the project and presents the
findings with regards to the site development requirements
Project Descriptions
The proposed project is located on the corner of Leowaena
Street and Leonui Street Waipahu Oahu Hawaii The site
is approximately 150 feet wide along Leowaena Street and
450 feet long along Leonui Street Total relief on the
site is estimated to be on the order of 20 feet northeast
corner being the lowest and southwest corner being the
highest
W O 451 00
It is proposed to construct a bakery a warehouse and a
freezer on the site About 105000 square feet area lot
on the eastern portion will be left for future planning
The parking and driveway areas would be on the northern
and western areas The structural details of buildings are
not yet available
II FIELD EXPLORATION
Three subsurface borings ranging from 17 to 23 feet deep were
completed on under the supervision of an engi
neer from our firm The borings were excavated with rotary
drilling equipment having continuous flight solid stem augers
The boring locations are shown on the site sketch Plate 1 and
the boring logs are presented in Appendix A
In all borings Standard Penetration Tests were taken at selected
depths The Standard Penetration Test consists of driving a
2 inch 0 D spoon sampler 18 inches with a 140 pound hammer
free falling a distance of 30 inches The number of blows
required to drive the sampler the last 12 inches is termed the
Standard Penetration Resistance N and is an approximate
measure of the relative density or consistency of a soil These
resistance values are plotted on the boring logs Samples ob
tained were visually classified in the field and representative

material was placed in air tight containers and returned to
our laboratory for testing
III SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
A Geology
The Pearl Harbor embayment was formed by the banking of the
Koolau Mountain lavas against the older Waianae Range The
Waipahu peninsula was probably initially formed as a divide
between two ancient branches of the present Panakauahi
and Waikele Streams The region has undergone a number of
periods of emergence and submergence which have intermixed
marine bay muds with non marine alluvial clastic debris
Late Pleistocene Age volcanism also blanketed the area with
volcanic ash During periods of emergence subaerial weather
ing produced the characteristic red to brown soil profiles
present in the subsurface
B Soils
Limited subsurface exploration indicates that the surface
layer of soil which is about 9 to 17 feet thick consists
of dense silt and fine sand with a trace of clay Soil
below this to the extent of depths drilled consists of very
stiff silty clay with a trace of fine sand

IV CONCLUSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the results of this feasibility level field investiga
tion limited laboratory testing and engineering analyses
the following preliminary conclusions and recommendations are
presented
A Due to existing topography it is anticipated that considerable
grading would be required to obtain a level pad for all
buildings Under cutting and recompaction of cut areas
1 to 2 feet below the foundation level may be required
if the daylight between cut and fill passes through the
building This is to reduce the possibility of differential
settlement
B Site is presently covered with moderate brush vegetation
which will have to be cleared
C It should be feasible to support the proposed structure on
shallow spread footings provided that the foundation loads
are not very high
4
W O 451 00
D The surface soil consisting of silty fine sand with a
trace of clay is not considered to be significantly
expansive
E Construction of the proposed buildings on the site is
considered to be feasible from a geology and soils engi
neering standpoint
F Detailed recommendations for allowable bearing values
anticipated settlements pavement design etc will be
covered in the design level investigation when the grading
and structural details of the proposed buildings are
available
G For the design level investigation it would be required
to drill one or two borings obtain ring samples and
perform detailed laboratory testing and engineering
analysis to develop the foundation design parameters

Laboratory Testing
LABORATORY TESTING
A laboratory testing program was developed to establish the
engineering properties of the soils and rocks encountered at
is
the project site The tests where applicable to the subject
project are included in this Appendix
1 Classification Tests
a
c
Visual Classification All soil samples obtained from
the borings were brought to our laboratory where they
were visually reclassified to confirm or modify the
field classification prior to finalizing the Logs of
Borings
Water Content Determinations In addition to visual
classification typical samples were tested for natural
water content as an aid in soil classification and in
evaluating soil properties The water content values
are based upon the dry weight of the soil
Atterberg Limits Atterberg limit tests were performed
on selected clay and silt samples obtained from the
borings for the purposes of identification and correla
tion of soils Standard procedures ASTM D 424 for the
plastic limit and ASTM D 423 for the liquid limit were
used in the performance of these tests
1
2
d
Grain Size Analysis Sieve analysis were performed on
granular soils For fine grained soils the hydrometer
test was performed to determine the distribution of
the grain sizes beyond the No 200 sieve
Shear Strength Tests
a
b
Unconfined Compression Tests Intact core and split
spoon soil samples which apparently exhibited high
plasticity were tested for unconfined strength Length
to diameter ratio of 2 was maintained throughout the
tests to avoid scatter of test results and to facilitate
evaluation of shear strength values for design purposes
Direct Shear Test In general soil samples were tested
for shear strength using a 2 41 I D direct shear box
Each set consisted of at least two tests each test run
under different normal loads The results are plotted
as Normal Stress vs Shear Strength with a failure envelope
drawn to determine values of cohesion c and angle of
frictional resistance
Consolidation Test Consolidation tests were performed on
selected undisturbed ring samples of the compressible
soils to provide basic data for making settlement calculations
Porous stones were placed on the top and bottom of the samples
to allow drainage Vertical loads were applied in increments
with each load increment being allowed to consolidate prior
2
to adding the next increment Measurements of the time
and consolidation were obtained during each load increment
and rebound was measured during the unloading portion
Consolidation test results are plotted in terms of percent
settlement versus applied loads
Swell Test The ring sample was placed between porous
stones and a 60 psf load was applied to the sample for a
24 hour period The difference between the initial and
final sample heights is the amount of swell which is expressed
as a percentage of the initial height of the sample
Maximum Density Test Samples of soils are compacted ina
mold of given size with a 10 pound rammer dropped 18 inches
to determine the relationship between moisture content and
density of the soil At least three trial points at dif
ferent moisture content are run to determine the maximum
density and optimum moisture content The test is run in
accordance with ASTM D 1557 70
