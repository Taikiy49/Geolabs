Soil Engineering Report
Proposed Residence
4127 Sierra Drive
Honolulu Oahu Hawaii
In accordance with your request we have made a study of the near
surface soil conditions at the above referenced lot in order to provide
foundation recommendations for the proposed residence The study
consisted of drilling two shallow borings performing laboratory testing
and reviewing the preliminary house foundation plans This report
summarizes our findings and recommendations
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
Based on the preliminary house plans the proposed residence will be
a three story structure approximately 70 by 80 feet in plan dimension
with a basement level and two upper levels The general layout of the
house foundation is shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
W O 1490 00 Page 2
Concrete slab on grade is planned for the driveway garage the
entire basement level and portions of the main floor level of the house
The house will be of wood frame construction with CMU walls planned for
the lower level
To accommodate the basement level CMU retaining walls generally less
than 10 feet high are planned under the front portion of the main floor
level for grade separation
SITE CONDITIONS
The subject lot is on the southern side of Sierra Drive east of the
intersection with Manini Way The existing lot grade is a little lower
than Sierra Drive with a relatively flat pad area covering most of the
site The ground surface slopes down to the adjacent properties to the
south and east
At the time of our field exploration the house construction had
commenced and the lower level CMU walls were in place
SUBSURFACE SOIL CONDITIONS
Two borings were drilled within the lot to depths of about 10 feet

In general the borings encountered very stiff reddish brown clayey

No groundwater was encountered at the time of our field exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The stiff reddish brown clayey silt soil and weathered rock
encountered generally can provide adequate foundation support for the
proposed house
Lot Grading
Prior to construction the area to receive any fill should be cleared
of vegetation and debris
The upper surface soils at the site are loose Prior to filling or
foundation placement the existing ground should be scarified moisture
conditioned and compacted as specified below
All fills and backfills should be placed in horizontal lifts not
exceeding 8 inches in compacted thickness and compacted to a minimum of
90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1490 00 Page 4
Fills placed on existing slopes should start from the bottom up
and where the slope is steeper than 5 1 H V it should be keyed and
benched to bond the new fill to the existing slope
All imported fill materials should be tested and approved by the
soils engineer prior to being used in the site
The site should be so graded to provide drainage of surface runoff
away from all foundations
House Foundations
Spread or continuous footing foundation bearing on the stiff in situ
soil may be used to support the proposed structure
Interior footings should be embedded a minimum of 12 inches below the
lowest adjacent finished grade Perimeter wall footings should be
embedded a minimum of 18 inches
For post and wall footings located on the existing slope they should
be embedded further down below the slope face at least 2 5 feet or
sufficiently down enough to obtain a 6 foot minimum horizontal set back
distance measured from the outer edge of the footing out to the slope
face This additional embedment depth is required to help resist the
downhill slope creep movement and to develop the required confinement
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1490 00 Page 5
An allowable bearing value of 3 500 p s f may be used for footings
on the stiff on site soil All footing excavation should be examined and
approved by the soils engineer
Surface water should be diverted away from foundations
Slab On Grade
Concrete slab on grade is planned for the driveway garage and
lanai The thickened edge of the slab should be embedded a minimum of 8
inches below the lowest adjacent finish grade
To reduce future swelling potential of the underlying soils we
recommend that the in place clayey silt below the concrete slabs be kept
moist
A 4 inch layer of 3B Fine gravel cushion fill should be provided
below the slab In order to protect against future moisture
infiltration an impervious moisture barrier may be incorporated below
the slab
Retaining Walls
Retaining walls generally less than 10 feet high may be required
around the proposed residence to provide grade separation The
guidelines for the retaining wall designs are listed below

An allowable bearing value of up to 3 500 p s f is recommended

A passive earth resistance of 300 p s f per foot of depth may

Drainage
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance
of the above recommended foundations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of
strength The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled tight
against the wall or slab footing inmediately after setting of the
concrete to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales should be provided as scon as possible and maintained
to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundations

Design Review
Plans and specifications for foundations site grading and utility
construction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and
comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the lot grading footing excavations and
backfill operations be observed by the soils engineer during construction
to see that adequate compaction has been obtained and that the subsurface
conditions are consistent with the findings of our investigation
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above inspections
