SECTION 1 0 GENERAL
1 1 Introduction
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the C 17 Road Restoration project located in
Hickam Air Force Base on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity
of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering
recommendations resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and
engineering analyses These recommendations are intended for planning and design of
pavements The findings and recommendations presented herein are subject to the
limitations noted at the end of this report
1 2 Project Considerations
The project site is located within Hickam Air Force Base on the Island of Oahu
Hawaii Construction of the C 17 beddown project has resulted in an increase in traffic
volume and load The increased traffic volume and load have caused distresses to the
pavements that are part of the C 17 beddown haul routes The pavement distresses
include rutting cracking and some potholes
The increased traffic volume and load have affected eight areas of the existing
pavements Five areas Areas 3 5 and 4 are in fair to good condition and new
asphalt concrete overlay is planned in these areas The existing pavements have shown
significant distresses in three areas Areas 5 6 and 7 These areas are Engine Test
Road Area 5 Kuntz Avenue between Kuntz Gate and Luke Drive Area 6 and
Fort Kamehameha Road between Vickers Avenue and Seaman Avenue Area 7
A geotechnical engineering exploration was performed at Areas 5 6 and 7 to
determine whether the pavement distresses occurring along these areas are a result of
either structural failure of the pavement section or subgrade failure

2 1 Regional Geology
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lavas from
two shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older Waianae Volcano is estimated to
be middle to late Pliocene in age and the younger Koolau Volcano is estimated to be
late Pliocene to early Pleistocene in age After a long period of volcanic inactivity during
which time erosion incised deep valleys into the Koolau Shield volcanic activity
returned with a series of lava flows followed by cinder and tuff cone formations These
series are referred to as the Honolulu Volcanic Series
The underlying geologic site conditions are the result of a complex series of
depositional and erosional events due to glacio eustatic sea level changes and
post erosional volcanic activities during the Pleistocene Epoch The Koolau Volcanic
Shield was built by thinly bedded basaltic lava flows during the early Pleistocene Epoch
Following the cessation of activity of the Koolau Volcano alluvial soil materials
were deposited in an estuarine and deltaic environment in the Pearl Harbor area The
sea level fluctuated several times receding about 90 meters below and rising about
30 meters above the present sea level
During the Recent Epoch as the sea level rose to its current stand the area was
covered with additional sediments consisting primarily of soft and loose lagoonal marine
deposits Land development and reclamation projects in the past 40 to 60 years have
added man made fills of both documented and undocumented placement bringing the
site to its present configuration

SECTION 3 DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The pavement analyses were performed in general accordance with the Army s
pavement design software Pavement Transportation Computer Assisted Structural
Engineering PCASE
The subgrade soils under the pavement area should be scarified to a depth of
about 200 mm moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture and compacted to
at least 95 percent relative compaction Aggregate base and subbase course materials
should consist of crushed basaltic aggregates and should conform to the requirements
in 01FA The aggregate base and subbase course materials should be
compacted to a minimum of 100 percent relative compaction Relative compaction
refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry
density as determined by ASTMD 1557 Optimum moisture is the water content
percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
In general paved areas should be sloped and drainage gradients should be
maintained to carry surface water off the pavements Surface water ponding should not
be allowed on the site during or after construction In general the primary source of free
water in the pavement section is surface water infiltration through cracks and joints in
the pavement A major source of infiltrated moisture is at the longitudinal
pavement shoulder interface Water also enters the pavement section from shallow
ditches To reduce water infiltration into the pavement structure all pavement joints and
cracks should be sealed to reduce the potential for appreciable water infiltration into the
pavement section and underlying subgrade soils In addition longitudinal edge drains
should be considered along the roadway alignment with relatively flat gradients

course material
Recycled hot mix asphalt consists of removing the existing asphaltic concrete
crushing the asphaltic concrete to meet the maximum particle size requirements mixing

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling
eight borings extending to depths ranging from about 1 5 to 2 1 meters below the existing
ground surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plans Plates 2 1 through
2 3 The borings were drilled using truck mounted drilling equipment equipped with
continuous flight augers
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who observed the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed by visual observation
and testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified
Soil Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representation of the materials
encountered is presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 8
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in
general accordance with ASTM D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving
a 75 mm OD Modified California sampler with a 63 5 kilogram hammer falling 760 mm
In addition some samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance
with ASTM D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a
50 mm OD standard penetration sampler using the same hammer and drop The blow
counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third 300 mm of an 450 mm drive
are shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate
sample depths
