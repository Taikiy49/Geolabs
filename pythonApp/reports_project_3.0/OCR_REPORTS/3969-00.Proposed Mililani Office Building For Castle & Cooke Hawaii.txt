INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Mililani Office Building for Castle Cooke Hawaii in the
Mililani area on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the
project site are shown on the Project Location Map Piate 1
This report summarizes our _ findings and geotechnical engineering
recommendations resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering
analyses The recommendations presented herein are intended for design of
foundations site grading and trench backfill only The findings and recommendations
presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a one story structure approximately 75 feet long by
40 feet wide in plan dimension as an annex addition to the existing Mililani Office Building
of Castle Cooke Hawaii Based on the grading plan transmitted to us and the proposed
building pad elevation at 766 feet Mean Sea Level minor site grading consisting of cut
__and fills on the order of 1 to 2 feet is anticipated

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from two shield
volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be middle
to late Pliocene in age and forms the bulk of the western third of the island The younger
shield Koolau is estimated to be Late Pliocene to early Pleistocene in age and forms the
majority of the eastern two thirds of the island Waianae became extinct while Koolau
was still active and its eastern flank was partially buried below Koolau lavas banking
against its eastern flank These banked or ponded lavas formed a broad plateau referred
to as the Schofield Plateau on which the project site is located
The project area is composed of basaltic rock built by the extrusion of the lavas of
the Koolau Volcanic Series These rocks are generally characterized by flows of jointed
dense vesicular basalt with inter bedded thin clinker layers In situ chemical weathering
of the Koolau lava flows has occurred for the last 1 to 2 million years The weathering
process has formed a mantle of residual soils which grade to saprolite with depth

In general saprolite is composed mainly of silty material and is typical of the tropical
weathering of volcanic rocks The saprolite grades to basaltic rock formation with depth
EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The proposed project site is located adjacent to the intersection of Ainamakua

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Our field exploration at the subject site generally encountered very stiff to hard
residual soils consisting of reddish brown to brown silty clays to the maximum depth
explored of 16 5 feet below the existing ground surface Based on our field exploration
and laboratory testing we believe that a shallow foundation system consisting of spread
and or continuous footings bearing on the recompacted near surface soils and or new
engineered fills may be used to support the proposed building We recommend that
footing excavations be observed by a representative of Geolabs Hawaii to verify that the
exposed footing subgrade conditions are similar to those encountered in our field
exploration
Our laboratory tests indicate that the in situ silty clays exhibit low to moderate
potential for shrink swell when subjected to moisture fluctuation To reduce the potential
for structural distress to the future slabs on grade resulting from the potential
be supported on at least 12 inches of non expansive select granular fill materials
Detailed discussion of these items and our geotechnical engineering design
recommendations are presented in the following sections of this report
Building Foundations
Based on the current design concept and the generally competent subsoil
conditions encountered at the site we believe that shallow spread and or continuous
footings may be used for support of the proposed office building An allowable bearing
pressure of up to 3 500 pounds per square foot psf may be used for the design of
foundations bearing on the recompacted in situ soils or engineered fills needed to
achieve the final design grades This bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may
be increased by one third 1 3 for transient loads such as those caused by wind or
seismic forces In general footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below
the lowest adjacent finish grade
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3969 00 Page 6
if foundations are located next to utility trenches or easements they should be
embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane extending
upward from the bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility
lines This requirement is necessary to avoid surcharging adjacent below grade
structures with additional structural loads and to reduce the potential for foundation
settlement
For footings bearing on the in situ soils or new engineered fills the bottom of the
footing excavations should be recompacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction prior to placement of reinforcing steel or concrete Relative compaction
refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry
density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 91 test
procedures Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight
corresponding to the maximum dry density
If soft and or loose materials are encountered at the bottom of footing excavations
they should be over excavated to expose the underlying firm materials
The over excavation should be backfilled with select granular material compacted to a
minimum of 90 percent relative compaction or the footing bottom may be extended down
to the underlying competent material
Lateral loads acting on the building may be resisted by frictional resistance
developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil and by passive
earth pressure acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system
A coefficient of friction of 0 35 may be used for footings bearing on the recompacied
in situ soils or new engineered fills Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be
estimated using an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of
depth pcf This assumes that the soil around footings is well compacted The passive
pressure should be reduced for foundations located on slopes Unless covered by
pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the upper 12 inches of soil should be
neglected

We estimate that footing settlements under the anticipated design loads for
footings bearing on the recompacted in situ soils or new engineered fills as
recommended herein to be less than one inch total with differential settlements on the
order of one half inch
We recommend that footing excavations be observed by a representative of
Geolabs Hawaii prior to placement of reinforcing steel or concrete to confirm the

Slabs On Grade
We anticipate that concrete slabs on grade will be used for the proposed office
building Our laboratory tests indicate that the silty clays encountered in our borings
exhibit low to moderate potential for swelling when subjected to moisture fluctuations
To reduce the potential for structural distress of the slabs on grade resulting from the
swelling of the silty clay soils we recommend that the subgrade soils underneath the slab
areas be properly prepared and capped with at least 12 inches of non expansive select
granular fill material below the slab cushion The capping fill should also extend beyond
the building perimeter a minimum of 24 inches
The non expansive select capping fill should be placed as soon as possible after
the subgrade soils are prepared in accordance with the recommendations presented in
the Site Preparation section of this report The prepared capping fill and the underlying
subgrade soils should be wetted and kept moist prior to final placement of concrete
Where shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of the subgrade we recommend that
the soils be thoroughly moistened to close all cracks Saturation and subsequent yielding
of the exposed subgrade due to inclement weather and poor drainage may require
over excavation of the soft areas and replacement with well compacted engineered fill at
no additional cost to the Owner
We recommend that a minimum 4 inch thick cushion layer consisting of
free draining materials such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation be
placed below the slab to provide more uniform support of the slab To reduce the

potential for future moisture infiltration and subsequent damage to floor coverings an
impervious moisture vapor barrier such as a plastic membrane is recommended on
top of the cushion layer Two inches of moist fine sand may be considered above the

slab concrete The thickened edges of slab adjacent to any unpaved area should be

Exterior concrete walkways may be required for the proposed project
We recommend that concrete walkways be supported on 12 inches of granular soils
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Control joints should be
provided at intervals equal to the width of the walkways and at right angle intersections
Site Preparation
We envision that minimal site grading work will be required for the proposed
project construction In general the following guidelines should be used for site grading
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be thoroughly
cleared Vegetation concrete planter walls debris rubbish and other unsuitable
materials within the grading limit should be removed and disposed of properly off site
Soft and yielding areas encountered during clearing below areas designated to receive fill
or future improvements should be over excavated to expose firm or dense material and
the resulting excavation should be backfilled with well compacted engineered fill
The excavated soft and or organic soils should be properly disposed of off site Existing
utilities to be abandoned should be removed and the resulting excavation should be
properly backfilled with granular fill material placed in 6 to 8 inch loose lifts and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
After clearing and demolition areas beneath future slabs should be
over excavated where necessary to provide the minimum 12 inch thick capping layer of
non expansive select granular fill material below the slab subgrade level i e bottom of

Prior to placement of select capping fill the subgrade soils should be scarified to a

In areas where the finished grades will be subjected to vehicular traffic the compaction

Utility Trench
We envision that new utility lines may be required for the project to connect to the
main lines In general a granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel
ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation is recommended under the pipes Free draining granular
materials such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation should also be
used for the trench backfill up to about 12 inches above the pipes to provide adequate
support around the pipes and to reduce the compaction effort of the backfill thus
reducing the possibility of damaging the pipes It is critical to use this free draining
material to reduce the potential for formation of voids below the haunches of pipes and to
provide adequate support for the sides of the pipes Improper trench backfill could result
in settlement of the backfill and damage to the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipes to
the top of the subgrade or finished grade may consist of the on site granular soils or
select fill material The backfill should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum
moisture placed in maximum 8 inch level loose lifts and mechanically compacted to not
less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for future ground
subsidence Where trenches are below pavement areas the upper 2 feet of the trench
backfill below the pavement subgrade should be compacted to at least 95 percent relative
compaction
Drainage
The finished grades outside the proposed building should be sloped to shed water
away from foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding Excessive landscape
watering near the foundations and slabs should also be avoided Planters next to

foundations should be avoided or have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water
infiltration into the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil
saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations
should be properly backfilled against the walls or slab footings immediately after setting of
the concrete to reduce water infiltration Drainage swales should be provided as soon as
possible and should be maintained to drain surface run off away from the slab and footing
foundations

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part
upon information obtained from field borings Variations of subsoil conditions between
the borings may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become
evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be
necessary to reevaluate the recommendations provided in this report
The locations of the field borings indicated in this report were measured by taping
from the property corners of the adjacent building Elevations of the field borings were
estimated from the Grading Plan dated and transmitted by Pacific Asia
Design Group Inc on The physical locations and elevations of the
borings should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used
The stratification lines shown on the Logs of Borings depict the approximate
boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual transition Water
level data from the borings were measured at the time of our field exploration These
data have been reviewed and interpretations made in the formulation of this report
However it must be noted that fluctuation may occur due to variation in rainfall
temperature and other factors

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture contents ASTM D 2216 were performed on retrieved soil samples as an
aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties The results of these tests are
presented on the Log of Borings
Three 1 inch ring swell tests were performed on natural samples to evaluate the
swelling potential of the near surface soils The test results are presented on Plate B 1
One Atterberg Limits test ASTM D4318 was performed on a selected sample of
the residual soils to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits and to aid in soil classification
Graphic representation of the test results is provided on Plate B 2
