PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Pi Kai Subdivision project site is in the Anahola area of Kawaihau
in the eastern coast of the Island of Kauai Hawaii The proposed development generally
consists of subdividing the site into approximately 193 single family residential lots under
three phases of construction The mass grading and infrastructure installation work for
Phase I of the development was completed in 2008 Phase 1 of the project included the
development of 80 lots with the installation of new utilities including water and drain lines
as well as construction of new roadways The Phase 2 site is located at
Laekuaehu Street The subdivision layout is shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
Geotechnical engineering exploration for the entire project site was performed in
1995 A geotechnical engineering report was submitted by Geolabs Hawaii dated
entitled Geotechnical Engineering Exploration Anahola Residence

SITE GRADING
Earthworks Pacific performed the site grading utility installation and pavement
construction Earthwork generally consisted of cuts up to 12 feet deep and fills up to
14 feet high Prior to filling the existing ground surface was cleared and grubbed of
vegetation and deleterious material then proof rolled with a compactor
Material generated from the cuts consisting of silty clay and clayey silt were used
in the fills During the site grading operations samples of the fill materials utilized as fill
were obtained and transported to our office for testing The maximum dry densities and
optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general accordance with
ASTM D1557 Modified Proctor and the test results are summarized on Plate 2
The fill materials were moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture content
and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as determined in
accordance with ASTM D1557 The base course and select borrow under the pavements
were brought to about optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative density
During the site grading operations periodic field density tests were performed in
general accordance with ASTM D6938 Nuclear Gauge Method Compaction test results
are summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 35 Test locations and elevations were estimated
by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and elevations
should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas
assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was
obtained and or approved by our field representative based on visual observation and
hand probing
Our services consisted of full time and part time field observation of the contractor s
fill placement subgrade preparation and compaction procedures and confirmation of
subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above grading operations
Based on our field representative s observations and the referenced test results it
is our opinion the site grading and utility installation work for the project observed and
tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report were performed in conformance
with the general intent of the project plans and specifications and our geotechnical
engineering report
