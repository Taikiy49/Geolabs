PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The proposed Ho’opili Development project is to the south of Interstate Route H-1 
Highway and west of Fort Weaver Road in the Ewa area on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. The 
Ho`opili development encompasses approximately 1,500 acres. The project site is gently 
sloping and is currently used for agricultural cultivation. 
The SOHO Plantation Community Center is located at the southern portion of the 
development near southwest corner of Road “4” and Road “J”, as shown on the Project 
Location Map, Plate 1, and the Site Plan, Plate 2. The new community center site 
encompasses approximately 2 acres consisting of a multi-purpose space, conference 
room facility, swimming pool, playground, and parking lots. 
The site grading consisted of minor cuts and fills of generally less than 5 feet. 
Goodfellow Bros. (GBI) performed the earthwork operations for the project. 
SITE GRADING 
At the on-set of earthwork, areas within the contract grading limits were generally 
cleared and grubbed of vegetation and deleterious materials. Materials generated from 
the clearing operations were disposed of off-site. Following the clearing and grubbing
