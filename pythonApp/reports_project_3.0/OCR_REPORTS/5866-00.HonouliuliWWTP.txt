This letter report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations performed
during the foundation probing and grouting operations for the Honouliuli Wastewater
Treatment Plant New Solids Handling Facilities project between August 20, 2007 and
January 20, 2009.
The project site is located within the Honouliuli Wastewater Treatment Plant
situated adjacent to Geiger Road in the District of Ewa on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii, as
shown on the Project Location Map, Plate 1. The project includes the construction of the
following:
Three (3) Anaerobic Digesters approximately 90 feet in diameter
Digester Control Building
Switchgear Building
Two (2) Odor Control System structures

Because of potential subsurface cavities and/or voids within the underlying coral
formation, a probing and grouting program to locate and grout potential cavities beneath
foundations was specified for the project. Parsons Construction Group surveyed and
marked the probe hole locations in the field prior to the probing operations. The probe
holes were spaced at about 10-foot on center along the structure foundations. The total
number of scheduled probe holes was as follows:
Location No. of Scheduled Probe Holes
Anaerobic Digester No. 1 98
Anaerobic Digester No. 2 } 98 |

For Anaerobic Digester No. 1, one probe hole (Probe Number AD1-99) was added
because of an existing electrical vault structure below the foundation. The Switchgear
Building was relocated subsequent to encountering an underground electrical line. Prior to
the building relocation, 12 of the initial probe holes for the Switchgear Building were drilled.
The approximate locations and identification of the scheduled probe holes for the above
structures are shown on the Probe Hole Identification Plans, Plate 2.1 to 2.7.
The probing and grouting work for the project was performed after the excavation
for the structure foundations. Geolabs performed the probing work. The probe holes were
drilled with track-mounted and truck-mounted drill rigs equipped with continuous augers.
The probe holes drilled were about 4 inches in diameter and extended to about 10 to 13
feet below the existing ground surface, at least 10 feet below the bottom of footing
elevations. A summary of our foundation probe drilling and grouting observations is
provided on Plates 3.1 through 3.19.
The materials encountered during the probe drilling operations generally consisted
of fill materials underlain by tannish brown and grayish white coralline materials. After
completion of the probe drilling operations, the probe holes were filled with a one-part sand
and one-part cement grout. For the scheduled probe holes that generally had grout
intakes of more than four times the theoretical probe hole volume, fine aggregate masonry
grout (three-parts sand and one-part cement) was subsequently used to fill the probe

A total of 234 additional probe holes were drilled due to excessive amount of grout
intake in the probe holes. The approximate locations of the additional probe holes are
shown on the Probe Hole Identification Plan, Plate 2. The additional probe holes were
assigned an alphabet after the probe hole number (i.e. AD1-69B). Fine aggregate
masonry grout (three-parts sand and one-part cement) was generally used to fill the
additional probe holes drilled.
In summary, the foundation probing and grouting operations have been conducted
in general conformance with the intent of the project specifications. Based on our
observations, it is our professional opinion that the relatively small voids and/or cavities
encountered at the structure foundation locations have been grouted and that the
subgrade area should provide the recommended foundation support for the structure
foundation.
