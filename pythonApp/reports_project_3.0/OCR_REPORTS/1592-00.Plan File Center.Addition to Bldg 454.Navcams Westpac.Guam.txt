This report presents the results of our soils investigation
performed for the proposed plan file center an addition to Building 454
on Guam The general location of the project site and its vicinity is
shown on the Project Location Map Plate l
PROJECT CONSIDERATION
The project proposes to construct an addition along the northern
side of the existing Building 454 The building addition is anticipated
to be used as a plan file center
W O 1592 00 Page 2
The proposed structure approximately 41 by 160 feet in plan will
be of CMU wall and concrete roof framing construction A 6 inch concrete
on grade slab on a 4 inch cushion fill is planned for the proposed
building
It is our understanding that the building addition will span over
existing buried cables which will not be relocated The cables will be
placed in underground box structures for future maintenance accessi
bility and the slab will be designed to support metal posts carrying the
elevated flooring loads
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of the investigation was to evaluate the subsurface
soil conditions at the proposed site to provide soil data for the design
of the foundation and to determine the bearing and settlement charac
teristics of the soil underlying the proposed facility
The scope of our investigation which was performed in general
accordance with our proposal of included drilling and
sampling two 2 borings engineering analysis utilizing the current
field data and soil report preparation

GENERAL SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed site is adjacent to the northern side of the existing
Building 454 Classic Wizard at the United States Naval Communication
Station in Guan
The site is open and unoccupied on the surface in an area measured
at about 41 by 160 feet An existing fence to the north of the site will
be relocated further away from the proposed building
Ground level is generally flat and ground cover consists of clayey
silt soil with short grass
SUBSOIL CONDITIONS
Two borings to depths of 15 5 to 16 5 feet below the ground surface
were drilled Both borings encountered a thin top soil layer of medium
stiff reddish brown clayey silt with grass roots and coral gravel
underlain by hard white coralline limestone grading from dense sand to
massive boulder size
Groundwater was not encountered in the drilled borings at the time of our
field exploration

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATION
General
Our findings indicate that the site is predominantly mantled with a
thin layer of clayey silt top soil underlain by hard coralline limestone
Spread Footing Foundation
Based on the anticipated soil conditions and the light building
load we believe that the proposed building may be supported by shallow
spread or continuous wall footing bearing directly on the hard coralline
limestone rock or compacted structural fill extending down to the
limestone rock All limestone rock below footings should be probed or
drilled to determine the presence of cavities or soft coral deposits
within the upper 5 foot below the footing bottom
Footings should be placed a minimum of 12 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grade Uplift wind blast or other lateral loading
condition may further increase the embedment of the footing foundation
We recommend that the proposed new footings adjacent to Building 454
should be placed at the same level to match the existing footings located
along the northern perimeter of Building 454
An allowable bearing capacity of 4 500 p s f may be used for the
foundation design The bearing capacity value is for total dead plus
live load only and may be increased by one third for short duration wind

loading Settlements under the design loading are anticipated to be
negligible if the foundation is constructed according to the above
design criteria
Resistance to lateral loads for the proposed building can be
obtained by passive pressure and frictional resistance A frictional
coefficient of 0 4 may be used between the bottom of the footing and hard
coral or compacted structural fill In addition an equivalent fluid
pressure of 300 p s f per foot of depth may be used to determine the
passive resistance
Site Grading
Due to the relatively flat ground minimal site grading is
anticipated for the site development The finished grade level is not
available at this stage
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed All existing
vegetation debris and other unsuitable materials should be removed and
disposed of properly off site in order to prevent contamination of the
excavated material
Relatively small excavation up to about 1 2 feet into the limestone
rock is anticipated for placement of the footings The excavated area

should be clean and free from debris and other unsuitable materials
before concrete is placed
The areas which are to receive pavement should be scarified toa
depth of at least 6 inches below the subgrade elevation moisture
conditioned to within 3 percentage points of the optimum and recompacted
to at least 90 ASTM D 1557 78 maximum density
Loose spots encountered at the footing or subgrade level should be
removed and replaced with compacted on site clayey silt soil with coral
gravel or suitable imported material
The on site silty coral fragments may be used as a source of
backfill material If required new imported fill material should
consist of non expansive select granular materials less than 3 inches in
maximum size with less than 20 passing the 200 sieve
Slab On Grade
Based on our findings we believe that the proposed 6 inch thick
slab on grade on a 4 inch cushion fill is adequate for the proposed
duilding provided no light vehicular traffic will be permitted
The base course should consist of crushed rock aggregate compacted
to a dry density of at least 95 of the ASTM D 1557 78 maximum

Existing Buried Cables
It is our opinion that the underground box structures used to
protect the existing buried cables should be designed to resist some
lateral earth pressure depending on the finished depths of the boxes
into the ground The dimensions of the box structures are not available
at this time
For preliminary design purpose the following earth pressure
distributions may be used
Earth Pressure At Rest Condition Above Water Level
Equivalent fluid pressure per foot of depth
Clayey Silt Fill
Coral sand and gravel
All abandoned utility trenches should be backfilled with compacted
select fill materials as described in the provision of Site Grading to
obtain at least 90 ASTM D 1557 78 maximum density
Design Review
Plans and specifications for site grading foundation pavement and
box design should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and
written comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the grubbing site grading foundation and
box excavation backfilling and subgrade preparations be observed by the
soils engineer during construction to see that adequate compaction has
been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the
findings of our investigation
If conditions encountered during construction are different from
those presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made
