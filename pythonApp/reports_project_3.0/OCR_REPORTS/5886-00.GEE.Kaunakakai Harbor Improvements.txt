SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located at Kaunakakai Wharf in Kaunakakai Harbor near the
town of Kaunakakai on the Island of Molokai Hawaii The existing harbor is approximately
one half mile south of Highway 450 and can be accessed through Kaunakakai Place
which is paved by asphaltic concrete
The wharf is trapezoidal shaped The deck length is approximately 687 feet and
the deck width is about 230 feet and 400 feet
The new comfort station will be located at the northern end of the harbor wharf over
the existing ferry shelter where ferry services are currently provided The new wastewater
lift station will be installed near the comfort station inside a security fence where
Young Brothers cargo services are located
The wharf has reinforced concrete decks and extensions built between the later
1920 s and the early 1950 s The existing concrete deck is approximately 687 feet long
Based on the information provided the concrete deck is currently supported by 18 inch
square piles The portion currently proposed for upgrading is between Gridlines A and F
and Bay Nos 13 and 16 which occupied a total area of approximately 41 feet by 33 feet
Based on the available topographic map the wharf surface is relatively level with
an elevation of approximately 7 feet Mean Sea Level MSL

Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork areas within the contract grading limits should be
cleared and grubbed thoroughly Our boring at the new comfort station
encountered concrete at about 2 feet below the ground surface We
recommend completely removing any concrete encountered during the site
preparation Vegetation debris deleterious material and other unsuitable
materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site or stockpiled
in a designated area to reduce the potential for contamination of the
excavated materials
Soft and yielding areas encountered during clearing and grubbing should be
over excavated to expose firm natural material and the resulting excavation
should be backfilled with well compacted engineered fill The excavated soft
soils should be properly disposed off site and or used in landscaping areas
where appropriate Contract documents should include additive and
deductive unit prices for over excavation and engineered fill placement to
account for variations in the over excavation quantities
After clearing and grubbing the existing ground should be scarified to a
depth of 8 inches and moisture conditioned to about 2 percent above the
optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction For pavement subgrades the minimum relative compaction
should be 95 percent Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density
of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same
soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 Optimum moisture is the
water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry
density
Where shrinkage cracks are observed after compaction of the subgrade we
recommend preparing the subgrade soil again as recommended above
Saturation and subsequent yielding of the exposed subgrade due to
inclement weather and poor drainage may require over excavation of the
soft areas and replacement with well compacted fill The excavated soft soils
should be disposed properly off site and or used on landscaping areas
where appropriate

The surface and subsurface drainage at the site are critical We believe that the
finished grades adjacent to the structures should be sloped to shed water away
from the foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also advised to

Lateral Load Resistance
In general lateral load resistance of the piles is a function of the stiffness of the
surrounding soil the stiffness of the pile allowable deflection at the top of pile and
induced moment in the pile Based on the structural information provided we
understand that approximately 12 new concrete piles will be installed in a 3 by
4 format to support the new concrete deck
The lateral load analyses for single pile were performed using the program
LPile plus for Windows which is a microcomputer adaptation of a finite difference
laterally loaded pile program originally developed at the University of Texas at
Austin The program solves for deflection and bending moment along a pile under
lateral loads as a function of depth The analysis was carried out with the use of
non linear p y curves to represent soil moduli The lateral deflection was then
computed using the appropriate soil moduli at various depths
In addition to LPile plus program a Group 3D program was also utilized to evaluate
the lateral deflection and reactions of the pile groups The program was developed
to compute the distribution of loads vertical lateral and overturning moment in up
to three orthogonal axes from the pile cap to piles arranged in a group The
program solves the non linear response of each pile under combined loadings and
assures compatibility of geometry and equilibrium of forces between the applied
external loads and the reactions of each pile head

Foundation Settlements
Settlements of the driven pile foundations will primarily result from elastic
compression of the pile member and subgrade response We estimate the total
settlement of the pile foundations to be less than 0 5 inches with differential
settlements not exceeding about one half of the estimated total settlement We
believe that these settlements are essentially elastic and should occur as the loads
are applied
Pile Hammer Selection
The selection of the pile hammer for use in driving the pile foundations will be of
critical consideration The piles should be driven with a hammer capable of
delivering energies in the range of about 40 000 to 60 000 foot pounds in the field
The hammer should be equipped with an energy level control such that driving
within the soft and or loose soils may be controlled at a lower energy to reduce the
potential for tensile stress development which could damage or cause cracking of
the piles
Selection of driving equipment to be used should take into consideration the
matching of the pile hammer with the weight of the piles to be driven This will
result in the pile hammer efficiently delivering the required energy to the top of the
pile and also reduce the potential for damage to the piles Geolabs should review
the pile driving equipment proposed to evaluate whether the equipment is capable
of installing the piles to the recommended capacity The contractor should forward
pile driving equipment data forms to Geolabs prior to construction for evaluation
Geolabs should be provided the opportunity to review the submittal and to assess
the driveability of the piles with the use of the selected hammer

In general each pile should be driven continuously without interruption A pile may
be rejected or its design capacity may be reduced when the driving resistance is
interrupted for more than 4 hours and the pile cannot be driven to the required
depth and or meet the pile driving criteria During soft driving i e when the pile tip

controlled such that significant tensile stresses do not develop within the pile
Because variations in soil strength characteristics are likely to exist in the bearing
soils the supporting capacity of each pile must be confirmed in the field by our
representative during pile driving It should be noted that the pile design was
developed from our pile analysis using the field exploration data Therefore a test
pile program should be carried out to confirm our preliminary pile design and to
establish pile driving criteria It should be noted that the production pile driving
criteria may be revised based on the results of the test pile program
Test Pile Program
We recommend undertaking a test pile program which includes at least one static
load test to fulfill the following objectives
1
To examine the driveability of the piles with the use of the selected
hammer
2
To estimate the production pile lengths
3
To assess the test pile driving criteria which may be modified for use as
the production pile driving criteria
4
To confirm the recommended predrilling depths at the pile locations
To achieve these objectives we recommend that the test pile program consist of a
minimum of two piles for the project The test piles may be incorporated into the
actual foundation system upon the evaluation of the load test results When the
final foundation plans are available Geolabs should be consulted for selection of
the test pile locations

The test piles should be cast about 10 feet longer than the pile lengths
recommended above to suit the test pile program Due to the lengths of the test
piles we anticipate that the test piles will likely need to be spliced In addition the
test pile program should provide flexibility to allow for addition or relocation of the
test piles as may be deemed necessary by our office to suit the conditions
encountered during the test pile driving Re striking of the test piles should be
required as part of the test pile program
We recommend performing one pile load test at the project site The pile load test
should be performed in general accordance with ASTM D 1143 loading the test
pile to at least 200 percent of the design load In addition the static load test should
be conducted no earlier than ten days after the test pile has been driven to allow
some set up of the soils surrounding the test pile
It should be noted that the project site is underlain by complex subsurface
conditions Therefore a Geolabs representative should be present during the pile
driving operations to observe the actual driving behavior and to further evaluate the
field performance It should be noted that the pile design and preliminary pile driving
criteria provided herein were developed from our pile analyses using the field
exploration data Therefore our observation of the pile driving operations is
necessary to confirm our design assumptions and should be designated a Special
Inspection item in accordance with Section 1701 of the Uniform Building Code
