PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a pavilion or visitor center for the
display and marketing of products produced by Kilauea Agronomics
Incorporated in The proposed visitor center will
consist of four 4 single story wooden framed structures with
slab on grade floors The current phase of development is for the
construction of two of these buildings
EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is located at the end of Kuawa Road to the west of
the town of Kilauea on the island of Kauai Hawaii The proposed display
pavilion site is situated in a commercial guava orchard on land that was
formerly used for the cultivation of sugar cane

Prior to our site visit the site had been graded by the placement of
about one and a half to two feet of fill for the pads of the two Phase I
buildings The fill material is a reddish brown clayey silt which had
been imported from off site The surface of the pads appeared to be well
compacted and evidence indicated that a relatively heavy vibratory
compactor had been used to apply the compactive effort
TEST PIT EXCAVATION
A total of three 3 test pits were excavated at the project site to
make observations of the subgrade conditions These test pits were
excavated adjacent to but outside of the building lines using a
rubber tired backhoe provided by Kilauea Agronomics Inc Upon completion
of the observations the pits were left open to be backfilled with
compacted soil by the earthwork contractor
In general the test pits encountered very stiff to hard clayey silts
to the maximum depths excavated The upper 1 5 feet of the building pads
was imported compacted fill consisting of hard reddish brown clayey silt
Below the fill material was a layer between 0 6 and 1 5 feet in
thickness of very stiff brown clayey silt with organics representing the
original topsoil of the site Underlying this original topsoil was stiff
to very stiff brown mottled clayey silt with weakly cemented seams
representing the lateritic subsoils commonly encountered in northern
Kauai

DISCUSSTONS
Based on the field and laboratory data the soils encountered in the
test pits are of a very stiff to hard consistency and form a dense stable
mass The laboratory tests also indicate that the on site and imported
soils appear to have low expansion potential Therefore we believe that
adequate bearing to support the lightly loaded single story structures has
been attained
During our site visit it was learned that the pads may be extended
laterally and that the pad elevations may be raised If this is the case
the fill being placed to extend the pads should be keyed into the existing
fill to bond the two fills together The imported material currently
being used appears to be suitable for use as fill
The fill material should be moisture conditioned to near optimum
moisture content placed in horizontal lifts of no more than eight 8
inches in loose thickness and compacted to 90 of its maximum dry density
as established by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the
earthwork and foundation phases of the work This is to observe
compliance with the design concepts specifications or recommendations and
to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ
from that anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations
given in this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as test
pits Variations of conditions between the field data points may occur
and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident until
construction If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to
re evaluate the recommendations given in this report
The location of the field data points were approximately determined
by estimation from Sheet A3 of the plans Display Pavilion for Kilauea
Agronomics by Fox Hawaii dated September 28
1988 The physical location of the field data points should be considered
accurate only to the degree implied by the method used
