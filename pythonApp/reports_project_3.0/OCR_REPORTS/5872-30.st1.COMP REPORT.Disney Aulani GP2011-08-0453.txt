This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering services performed during 
the construction for Aulani, A Disney Resort and Spa project on the Island of Oahu. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
Aulani, A Disney Resort & Spa (formerly Disney’s Grand Hawaiian Resort & Spa) 
project site is within the existing Ko Olina Resort development in the District of Ewa on 
the Island of Oahu, Hawaii (as shown on the Project Location Map, Plate 1). The project 
site encompasses two parcels of land (Lot Nos. 13 and 15) fronting Lagoon No. 1 of the 
Ko Olina Resort. The project site is also adjacent to the existing JW Marriott Ihilani 
Resort and Spa (to the northwest). The two parcels are to the southwest of Ali`inui 
Drive, which is the main entry road into the resort.  A General Site Plan is provided on 
Plate 2. 
EARTHWORK OPERATION 
Jayar Construction Co., Inc. performed the earthwork for the project.  Hawaiian 
Dredging Construction Company was the general contractor for the project. Our 
observation and testing services during the construction of the project occurred between 
April 2010 and July 2012.

After clearing and grubbing, areas to be filled and subgrades in cuts were generally 
scarified to a depth of about 8 inches, moisture conditioned, and compacted to a minimum 
of 90 percent of the maximum dry density determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557 
(Modified Proctor).  The compaction requirement for the finished subgrades in areas 
subjected to vehicular traffic was increased to a minimum of 95 percent relative 
compaction. 
Fill and backfill materials generally consisted of excavated materials from the 
Aulani project site and some imported materials.  In general, fill materials consisted of 
materials less than 3 inches in maximum particle size.  Fills and backfills required to raise 
the grades were generally placed in level lifts of about 8 inches in loose thickness, 
moisture-conditioned and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction.  
The compaction requirement for the fill materials required under the pavement areas was 
increased to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction. 
Subgrades in the cut areas generally were scarified and recompacted to obtain a 
minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as determined in accordance with 
ASTM D 1557. 
During the earthwork operations, samples of the on-site and imported materials 
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing. 
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our 
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D 1557 (Modified Proctor), and the test 
results are summarized on the attached Plate 3. 
Field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations on an 
as-need basis to confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures 
and soil conditions were noted. A total of 1,127 field density tests were performed using 
the nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM D 6938. Compaction test results 
are summarized on Plates 4.1 through 4.70.  These tests were performed on the 
prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills. Test locations and 
elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on-site survey stakes and 
topographic features. These locations and elevations should be considered accurate 
only to the degree implied by the methods used. Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory 
were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained. 
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the 
referenced tests, it is our opinion that the earthwork operations during the construction 
of Aulani, A Disney Resort and Spa project observed and tested by Geolabs in the 
period covered by this report were performed in general accordance with the intent of 
the project plans and specifications and our geotechnical engineering recommendations 
presented in the referenced report dated September 30, 2008 and subsequent 
recommendations.
