SECTION 1 0 GENERAL
1 1 Introduction
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the Closure of Waiau Ponds project located within the existing
HECO Waiau Power Plant on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and
vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings from our field exploration and presents our
geotechnical recommendations derived from our analyses pertaining to the design of
site grading only Foundation recommendations for the possible construction of
structures in the future are beyond the scope of our services and are not included in this
report The findings and recommendations presented herein are subject to the
limitations noted at the end of this report
1 2 Project Considerations
The project site is located within the existing HECO Waiau Power Plant located
on the Island of Oahu Hawaii Based on the information provided we understand that
closure of the abandoned wastewater treatment ponds within the power plant is desired
The existing Waiau Ponds have earth berms of up to about 8 feet in height
Details of the site grading for the project was not available at the time this report was
prepared However we understand that it is desired to balance the cuts and fills for the
site grading of the project site to minimize the need for imported fills for the project In
general we anticipate that closure of the abandoned ponds will require areal fill
placement of up to about 3 feet in thickness
After closure of the ponds by filling the site we understand that the project site
will be used as storage area and new structures are not currently planned A paved
roadway will be constructed at the eastern portion of the project site to provide access
to the vehicle washing area In addition a gravel access road will be constructed
around the perimeter of the project site

2 1 Regional Geology
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lava from
two shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae Volcano is
estimated to be middle to late Pliocene in age and Koolau Volcano is estimated to be
late Pliocene to early Pleistocene in age As volcanic activity in the Waianae Volcano
ceased lava flows from Koolau Volcano banked against the eroded eastern slope of
Waianae Volcano forming a broad plateau now known as the Schofield Plateau The
project site is located on the southwestern flank of the Waianae Volcano
The project site is located on the south central portion of the coastal plain of
Oahu The geologic conditions underlying the Pearl Harbor lochs were the result of a
complex series of depositional and erosional events due to glacio eustatic sea level
changes and post erosional volcanic activities during the Pleistocene Epoch
The Koolau Volcanic Shield was built during the early Pleistocene Epoch by
thinly bedded basaltic lava flows Following the cessation of activity of the Koolau
Volcano alluvial materials were deposited in an estuarine and deltaic environment in
the area of Pearl Harbor The sea level fluctuated several times receding perhaps
300 feet below and rising about 100 feet above the present sea level As the Island of
Oahu sank coral reef grew around the shorelines keeping pace with the subsidence of
the island
During the Recent Epoch as sea level rose to its current stand the Pearl Harbor
lochs were covered with additional sediments consisting primarily of cohesive soils with
localized granular soils over the underlying alluvium Land development and
reclamation projects in the past 40 to 60 years have added man made fills along the
shoreline bringing the site and the surrounding area to its present configuration

SECTION 3 DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
3 1 Ground Settlements
As indicated previously new areal fills of up to about 3 feet above the existing
ground level may be required to achieve the design grades for the project Due to the
presence of soft recent alluvium at the project site ground settlements likely will occur
as a result of the placement of new fills at the project site
In general the borings drilled at the project site indicate that highly compressible
alluvial soil deposits are present at about 1 to 4 feet below the existing ground surface
at the bottoms of the existing ponds The borings indicate that the thickness of the
compressible soil deposits range from about 17 to 24 feet Based on our laboratory test
results the soft soil deposits may be considered normally consolidated and the soft soil
deposits would consolidate quite readily when subjected to structural loads It should
be noted that due to variations in the subsurface profile across the project site it is very
difficult to predict accurately the total settlements and therefore differential settlements
across the project site may occur
Based on these conditions we anticipate that the placement of about 3 feet of
new areal fills at the project site may induce ground settlements on the order of about
4 to 6 inches Based on our engineering analyses and previous experience we believe
that about 80 percent of the estimated ground settlements should occur in a period of
about 10 to 12 months after the fills are placed It should be noted that residual
settlements on the order of 1 to 2 inches still may occur over a long period Therefore
the new fills should be placed as early as possible to allow the filled ground to come to
equilibrium as early as practical
We understand that new structures are not planned at the project site However
in order to reduce the impact of the appreciable ground settlements caused by
consolidation of the soft soil deposits under the new fill loads we recommend that an
additional geotechnical study be conducted if and when the construction of new
structures and or improvements is desired at the project site In addition the
construction of pavements around the pond closure areas should be delayed as long as
possible until after a large portion of the settlements has occurred

Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork areas within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed Vegetation debris deleterious materials and
other unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site to
reduce the potential for contamination of the excavated materials
After clearing and grubbing finished subgrades in cut areas and areas designated
to receive fills should be scarified to a depth of approximately 8 inches
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a
minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the
in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density
of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557
test procedures Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight
corresponding to the maximum dry density

Where shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of the subgrades we

Imported fill materials if required should consist of non expansive select granular

Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
In general fill materials should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in

should be increased to 95 percent relative compaction for the finished subgrades of

moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the
maximum dry density Compaction of the fills should be accomplished using
sheepsfoot rollers vibratory rollers or other types of acceptable compaction
equipment
The filling operations should start at the lowest point and continue up in level

Cut and Fill Slopes
In general cut slopes exposing the soil materials encountered in the borings and
permanent fill slopes constructed of the on site soils may be designed with a slope
inclination of two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V or flatter Fills placed on slopes
steeper than 5H 1V should be keyed and benched into the existing slope to provide
stability of the new fill against sliding
Furthermore construction of earth berms interceptor ditches and the use of
geotextile fabrics over the fill slope face should be considered to reduce the
potential for significant erosion thus enhancing the long term stability of the fill
slopes Appropriate slope planting or other forms of erosion control measures to
reduce the potential for significant erosion of the exposed slopes including a
permanent irrigation system should be implemented as soon as possible after the
finished slope faces are completed
Excavation
Based on our observations the existing earth berms around the perimeter of the
existing ponds generally consist of medium stiff to very stiff sandy and clayey silts
It is anticipated that the near surface materials consisting of soil materials may be
readily excavated with normal heavy excavation equipment such as excavating
with a backhoe excavator
The above discussions regarding the excavation of the materials are based on our

proposing to bid on this project be encouraged to examine the site conditions and

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by drilling and sampling
five borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 5 The borings were advanced to the
maximum depths of about 25 6 to 35 5 feet below the existing ground surface The
borings were drilled using a truck mounted drill rig equipped with continuous flight auger
tools The approximate locations of the borings drilled for the project are shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representations of the materials
encountered are presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 5
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained from the borings drilled in
general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of
Soils by driving a 3 inch OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling
30 inches In addition some samples were obtained from the borings drilled in general
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel
Sampling of Soils by driving a 2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same
hammer and drop The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third
6 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of
Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Pocket Penetrometer tests were performed on selected cohesive soil samples
retrieved in the field The pocket penetrometer test provides an indication of the
unconfined compressive strength of the soil sample Results of the pocket
penetrometer tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample
depths

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture Content ASTM D 2216 and Unit Weight ASTM D 2937 determinations
were performed on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of
soil properties The results of these tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the
appropriate sample depths
Three Atterberg Limits tests ASTM D 4318 were performed on selected
samples of the soils to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits and to aid in soil
classification The results of the tests are summarized on the Logs of Borings at the
appropriate sample depths Graphic presentation of the test results is provided on Plate
B 1
One Consolidation test with time rates of consolidation ASTM D 2435 was
performed on the potentially compressible soils encountered in the borings to evaluate the
compressibility characteristics of the materials encountered Results of the consolidation
test are presented on Plate B 2

APPENDIX C
Percolation Testing
APPENDIX C
Percolation Testing
As part of our field exploration program we conducted two percolation tests in the
borings drilled using the falling head test method In general the borings drilled had a
diameter of about 4 inches and extended to depths of about 10 feet below the ground level
at the time of the test Water was filled in the boreholes and was allowed to percolate into
the ground for the falling head test method The water levels and corresponding times
were recorded for the calculations of subsurface permeability The results of the
percolation tests conducted in the borings drilled are presented on Plates C 1 and C 2
