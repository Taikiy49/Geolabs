Project Considerations
Maui Linen Inc is in the process of installing two new disposal wells In
accordance with the Hawaii Department of Health administrative rule for Underground
Injection Control UIC it is required to have a geologist maintain logs for this work
Logging Procedure and Scope of Work
We provided geological logging services for the wells in a manner appropriate to
the well construction method and in general accordance with good professional practice
and principles The logging of the wells was based on visual and textural examination
of samples of cuttings collected at about 5 foot intervals during the drilling The
samples were delivered to our laboratory for examination and logging
For logging cutting samples were removed from the sample bags and spread in
a laboratory sample tray Initial visual observation was conducted to evaluate the origin
of the material e g alluvium basalt formation or coralline formation Closer
examination was made to attempt to differentiate specifics about the material e g
pahoehoe a a clinker or a a core If possible further description of the formation was
provided A written description was made for each cutting sample and the descriptions
were compiled for longer depth intervals

Report
The well logs are presented as verbal descriptions on the attached Injection Well
Logs Plates 1 and 2 In general the wells at the site penetrated basaltic lava flows of
the Honomanu Volcanic Series
Our observations of the samples indicated that the lava flows consisted primarily
of thickly bedded a a flows with associated clinker No unusual geological or
hydrological conditions appeared to be associated with the samples examined
