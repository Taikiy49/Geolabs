PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Waimanalo Reservoir is located approximately 2 500 feet southwest of the
Waikupanaha and Wahailua Street intersection in the town of Waimanalo on the Island of
Oahu Hawaii The reservoir is about 1 400 feet from the foot of the Koolau Mountain
range on its windward side as shown on Project Location Map Plate 1
The objective of the compaction testing performed by Geolabs is to provide a field
determination of the relative moisture and density of the exposed on site soils during
construction of the reservoir improvements
SITE GRADING
The grading works consisted of clearing the downhill slopes along the outer face of
the reservoir embankment and grubbing to a depth of 1 foot and restoration to the original
grade with drop rock The exposed on site soils after grubbing consisted primarily of
clayey silts
At the on set of grading the areas that received rockfill were cleared and grubbed
to about 1 foot deep and compacted Prior to placement of the drop rock slope armor
Geolabs performed field compaction tests on the prepared surface along the crest and the
grubbed outer face of the north and west facing slopes of the reservoir embankment

A roller wheel attached to a Hitachi 450 excavator rig and Jumping Jack mechanical

methods used Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified
minimum compaction was obtained and or approved by our field representative
