INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our soil engineering
investigation for the proposed residential development
Golf Course Subdivision Unit Hawaii
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 890 00 P 2
The site is located at the south side of Kealahou Street
adjacent to the existing Hawaii Kai Golf Course as shown on
the Site Plan Plate 1
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed subdivision development would consist of
approximately 62 lots for single family dwellings The
structures would be of relatively light wood frame construc
tion Access roadways from Kealahou Street would be about
44 feet wide Garage and driveways would be provided with
each dwelling
The entire site has been previously graded during which
existing fill material was placed More grading is antici
pated for the proposed residential development Because of
the expansive soil and near surface rock conditions it is
our understanding that a minimum of 3 feet of non expansive
imported fill would be placed over the entire site or under
the structural areas Finish floor elevations are not final
ized at this time Structural loads are expected to be
relatively light
SITE CONDITIONS
Surface Conditions
The project site is located between a portion of Hawaii Kai
Golf Course and Kealahou Street and near the Golf Course
Subdivision No 3 The site is relatively flat with existing
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 890 00 P 3
ground surface elevations ranging from approximately 50 feet
MSL on the northwestern corner to about 30 feet MSL on
the southeastern corner of the property
Grass and shrubs cover the entire area of the site An
existing 30 inch reinforced concrete pipe from Kealahou
Street to the Golf Course bisects the site
Subsurface Exploration
To explore the subsurface conditions at the site a total
of 22 test pits were excavated with a 980C backhoe The
test pit depths ranged from one foot to ten feet below the
existing ground surface In addition seismic refraction
Surveys were used to supplement the field exploration and
help to delineate the thickness of the upper soil stratum
and to explore the hardness of the underlying rock The
locations of the test pits and seismic Surveys are shown on
the Site Plan Plate 1 Detailed description of the test
pit logs and laboratory test results are given in the Appendix
Based on the test pits and field observations the subsurface
conditions at the site could be described as follows
Gray vesicular basalt was encountered underlying
shallow surface soil layers at depths varying from
1 0 to 4 0 feet below the existing ground surface
The upper portion of the basalt formation is

of surface materials varied from 0 0 to as much as

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on the results of our test pit excavation seismic
refraction survey data laboratory testing and engineering
analysis the site is considered suitable for the planned
development provided that our recommendations presented in
this report are implemented
Much of the site appears to have been graded previously
probably during the Hawaii Kai Golf Course construction
Surface materials on the eastern half of the Site does not
appear to have significant amount of recent fill However
on the western portion of the site the thickness of the
existing fill varies from 2 to 6 feet
The existing fill in the western half of the site is loose
and consists of expansive adobe clays This clayey fill
material as is is not suitable for foundation support
The expansive fill material should be removed for a depth
of 3 feet below the foundations and pavements and replaced
with select non expansive soil Alternately the expansive
fill material can be capped with a 3 foot layer of select
non expansive soil import borrow
It is our understanding that it was decided in our October
12 1979 meeting that the grading concept would try to
incorporate the 3 0 feet capping or filling over the entire
project site Satisfactory foundation support could be
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 890 00 P 7
provided by this grading approach provided that at least
3 0 feet of select import fill low to non expansive soils
are placed under all the building and roadway areas Post
and beam type of foundation scheme was also considered as
a possible alternative for the proposed light residential
structures on the western half of the site This type of
foundation scheme appears to be relatively costly as com
pared to the other alternatives presented herewith since
select import fill would still be required under the slab
on grade driveways and roadway pavements over the expan
Sive fill subgrade material We would be happy to provide
more detailed recommendations should post and beam construc
tion be desired Specific recommendations for grading and
structural design and construction are presented in the sub
sequent sections of this report
Site Preparation and Grading
Prior to receiving any fill the site should be stripped to
remove all the vegetation and miscellaneous debris The
upper soil crust consisting of Organic material may be wasted
or stockpiled for possible future landscaping use All the
fill areas exposed should then be scarified to a minimum
depth of 6 inches brought to near optimum moisture and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry
density per ASTD D 1557 70 modified Proctor test method
prior to any fill placement Localized soft pockets if
encountered during site preparations should be excavated
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 890 00 P 8
and backfilled with compacted select material
Imported fill material should be tested and approved by the
soils engineer prior to importation Imported fill shall
consist of a low to non expansive material less than 1 03
swell according to the CBR swell test method Fill soils
should be placed in maximum 10 inch thick loose horizontal
lifts and each layer compacted to 90 relative compaction
using ASTM D 1557 70 modified Proctor as a standard We
recommend that during site grading for this project con
tinuous inspection and frequent compaction density tests
be performed under the observation of the soils engineer to
ensure that the specified compaction is obtained during the
placement of the fill
The excavated clayey adobe fill soils which exhibit expan
Sive characteristics should not be used as fill under or behind
the structural areas Adequate drainage should be provided and
maintained at all times to keep the water away from the site
during earthwork operations
The new fill should be placed in accordance with the standard
specifications as described in Appendix D of this report

The existing site surface loose fill consisting of
potentially expansive clayey adobe material is not
considered suitable for foundation support The

imported fill material could be granular and

that as much as 3 feet of fill is being contemplated

Rippability of Rock
Based on the information obtained from test pit excavation
and seismic refraction survey data it appears that the
upper few feet of rock is highly weathered and could be ex
cavated with conventional earth moving construction equip
ments Excavation below a few feet of rock formation would
require pneumatic type jack hammers Hard ripping or
blasting of rock may be required for the areas in rock where
underground utility lines are deeper
GEOLABS HAWAH
W G 890 00 P 12
We wish to point out that by filling the area with imported
fill we would raise the grade thereby minimizing ripping
and blasting in rock for utility lines and swimming pool
excavation in rock
Floor Slab Design
Floor slab could be supported on grade either on on site
non expansive material on the eastern half or on a minimum
of 3 feet of granular compacted fill import fill over the
existing expansive fill on the western half of the site
A 4 inch layer of granular material such as 3B fine rock
should be provided under all the slabs to act as a capillary
break In addition a moisture proof membrane should be
placed over the granular material to act as a vapor barrier
The membrane should be covered by one inch of sand to pro
tect it during construction and to aid in curing of the con
crete Expansion joints should be provided at regular inter
vals for all the floor slabs including the garage floor and
driveway to minimize cracking of the slabs
Asphaltic Concrete Pavement
Based on using compacted granular imported fill material as
subgrade for roadway areas the following pavement section
is recommended
2 inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 inch Base Course
8 inch Total Pavement Thickness

where existing fill clayey soils are highly expansive The
following pavement is recommended for the above condition
2 inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 inch Base Course
18 inch Select Borrow
26 inch Total Pavement Thickness
All subgrade soils should be compacted to a minimum of
95 percent of maximum density
CBR tests should be performed on the proposed fill material
and at subgrade soils during construction and the design
sections can be revised based on these tests

GOLF COURSE SUBDIVISION UNIT 4
The work under this section includes Clearing and grubbing
preparation of fill areas placement and compaction of fill
and removal and backfilling of underground structures These
specifications are of a general nature and may be superseded
by specific requirements or recommendations contained within
the soils report
I Clearing
All areas within contract limit lines shall be cleared of
trash debris organic matter and all other deleterious
materials These materials shall be removed and disposed
offsite
Il Preparation of Fill Areas
A Loose surface soils shall be removed to expose compe
tent natural soils Localized soft pockets shall be
undercut and backfilled with compacted select mate
rials
B All surfaces shall be inspected and approved by the
soils engineer prior to the placement of any fill
C After approval the surface shall be scarified to a
depth of six inches brought to near optimum moisture
content and compacted as herein specified

Fill Materials
Material for fill shall consist of on site soils or
approved import material Import material shall be
tested and approved prior to transportation to the site
Fill material shall contain less than 5 percent organic
matter and other deleterious material and shall not
contain rocks or lumps in excess of eight inches 8
in diameter
Placement and Compaction of Fill
A
The fill shall be placed in horizontal layers which
when compacted will not exceed nine inches 9 in
thickness Each layer shall be spread uniformly and
blade mixed to attain uniformity of material and
water content within each layer

Rock fragments or boulders Jess than eight inches
8 in diameter may be utilized provided they are
not concentrated in any one area and that there is
sufficient fine grained material to fill the voids
between the rocks
Rocks greater than eight inches 8 in diameter
shall be removed from the fill unless specifically
approved for placement in the fill by the soils
engineer
All fill shall be compacted to a minimum of 90
percent of the maximum dry density established by
ASTM Test Designation D 1557 unless otherwise
specified in the soils report
The compaction equipment may be sheepsfoot vibra
tory or pneumatic tire rollers suited to the soil
type and capable of producing the specified results
in an expedient and efficient manner The equipment
shall be in good working condition fully ballasted
and self cleaning
The compaction shall be accomplished in a systematic
and uniform manner over the entire fill area to pro
duce a uniformly compacted fill
