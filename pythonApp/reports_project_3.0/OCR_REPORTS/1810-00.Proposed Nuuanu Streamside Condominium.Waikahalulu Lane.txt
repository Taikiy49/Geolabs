INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of a soils engineering evaluation
performed for the proposed Nuuanu Streamside Condominium Complex at
Waikahalulu Lane on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location of
the site and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
Based on the preliminary plans provided to us the proposed
development will consist of four two to three story buildings with a
combination of asphalt and grasscrete parking driveway areas

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this study was to provide recommendations for site
grading building foundations and pavement design for the subject
development The recommendations are based on the soil information
obtained from borings drilled for this exploration
The study consisted of drilling 1 deep percolation test boring and
9 shallow borings Also hand auger probing was done in 3 areas
inaccessible by truck to delineate the extent of the surface clay The
approximate locations of the percolation test boring soil borings and
probed areas are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Soil sampling and percolation testing was performed by one of our
engineers The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests
which were utilized in the engineering analysis to formulate our
recommendations are presented in the appendices of this report
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations

EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is located in the Punchbowl District of Honolulu
This approximately rectangular shaped 28 401 square foot lot is fronted
by Waikahalulu Street to the west and just east of the Liliuokalani
Gardens and Nuuanu Stream
At the time of the investigation the site was occupied by four
cottages and two carports
The site is fairly level except for the southern most corner which
slopes to the west at a gradient less than 5 1 H V and the lower
garden located along the western boundary which is about 4 feet lower
than the adjacent ground surface It was necessary to investigate the
soils in these areas by probing with a hand auger rather than drilling
from a truck mounted rig
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
Geologically the site is quite complex It is located on the
lower reaches of the junction of Nuuanu and Pauoa Valleys which were
formed by the deep erosion of the Koolau Ridge The period of erosion
was followed by valley filling with alluvial deposits then covered by
the Honolulu Volcanic Series whose episodes were spread out over a
considerable period of time
The basalt which was encountered at about 5 foot depth may be
associated with the Luakaha Vent an earlier Nuuanu Flow of the Honolulu
Volcanic Series This unit may probably be many tens of feet thick
The alluvial soil layer above this was deposited by streams during a
cessation of volcanic activity

It was our understanding that utilization of on site drainage wells
for the surface runoff disposal was being considered and it was
therefore desirable to evaluate the hydraulic conductivity of the
subsurface soils and basalt
Constant and falling head percolation tests were performed in the
deep boring when the bottom of the hole was 22 feet and again at 26 5
feet Results of these tests and the subsurface conditions encountered
are presented on the Percolation Test Boring Log and Percolation Test
Results Plates A 10 and A 11 respectively
In general the percolation test boring encountered a 4 foot thick
layer of impervious clay overlying basalt The basalt formation
continued to a depth of at least 26 5 feet From 4 to 20 5 feet and
again from 26 feet to the bottom of the hole the basalt is vesicular
with moderately to widely spaced fractures The Rock Quality
Designations ROD in these zones were 95 to 98 A clinker zone was
encountered from 20 5 to 26 feet which had an RQD of 27 The clinker
consisted of vesicular basalt fragments with surfaces which are
moderately to strongly weathered Some fragments were coated with clay
and a clay layer was encountered from 22 to 22 5 feet The permeability
of the clinker zone was observed to be higher than the overlying tight
basalt yet it is our belief that the presence of the clay prevents the
clinker layer from providing the high permeability desired of an
efficient drain well
Two types of percolation tests constant and falling head tests
were performed in this 4 inch diameter boring When the bottom of the
hole was at a 22 foot depth just into the top of the clinker zone the
first set of tests were run The entire 5 foot thick clinker zone was
tested by the second set of tests which were performed when the bottom

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration disclosed that the lot is underlain by gray
to brown adobe clay The laboratory test results and previous
experience indicate that this material is highly to critically
expansive Therefore the foundations will require special design and
construction procedures in order to reduce the shrink swell effect of
the subsoils
Foundations
Spread or continuous footing foundations may be used to support the
proposed sturctures
In order to reduce the effects of the expansive soil we recommend
that the expansive clays be over excavated down to a depth of 3 feet
below the bottom of the individual footings or to rock formation and
then replaced with non expansive borrow soils such as crusher waste
select borrow or coral The replaced soils should be compacted to a
minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78 The
footings can then be placed on the compacted non expansive soil

Retaining Walls
There are low CRM retaining walls along the southern boundary of
the site The structural capacity of these walls are questionable and
therefore should not be depended on New walls may be required for the
development
It is our understanding that short retaining walls generally less
than 5 feet high may be required along the southeastern portion of the
site The guidelines for retaining wall design are listed below
1 In order to reduce the expansive effects of the existing clay
soils on the wall we recommend that the expansive adobe
clays if encountered be over excavated down for a depth of 2
feet below the bottom of the footing or to rock formation and
then replaced with non expansive borrow soils such as crusher
waste select borrow or coral compacted to a minimum of 90 of
its maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78 The
over excavation and replacement should extend a minimum
distance of 12 inches beyond the toe of the footing if
possible

The retaining wall footings should be embedded a minimum depth
of 12 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade
Therefore the total depth of the above over excavation and

An allowable bearing value of up to 2500 p s f is recommended

A base friction factor of 0 4 for soil can be utilized to

passive soil resistance of 250 p c f can be utilized if the
soils are confined by slab or pavement
To minimize excessive swelling pressure that may be exerted
laterally on the retaining wall the expansive adobe clay
should be over excavated at least 4 feet behind the wall or
equal to the height of the wall whichever is smaller
Retaining wall backfill should consist of granular materials
such as select borrow or crusher waste This type of backfill
would improve drainage reduce the compactive effort required
for the wall backfill and reduce lateral pressure on the
walls Impervious soil such as on site clay soils should he
used only for the top one foot of backfill to reduce seepage of
surface run off behind the walls
A subsurface drainage system subdrains or weepholes should be
utilized behind the walls Subdrains should be properly
constructed to drain water away from the wall

Site Grading
Minimal site grading is planned for most of the site Fills up to
6 feet are anticipated in the southern corner of the site
Fills and backfills within 3 feet of the finished grade should be
selected non expansive material generally less than 6 inches in size
placed in maximm 8 inch loose lifts and compacted to 90 of its maximum
dry density established by ASTM D 1557 78 The on site clay soil which
is expansive should not be used for fill material below structures
In the southern most corner of the site the ground surface slopes
in this area to increase the stability of the embankment The fills
should be keyed and benched into the existing slope as shown in the
General Grading Recommendations Plate 6

Some amount of boulders which were encountered during our
investigation should be anticipated during construction
Prior to filling the area should be cleared of vegetation and
debris
Drainage
The finished lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings
should be shaped to shed water away from foundations and to avoid
ponding condition Also it is advised that gutter systems be installed
around the condominiums and that gutter water be diverted far away from
foundations and walkways Excessive landscape watering near
foundations should also be avoided
Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper
performance of the above recommended foundations since ponded water
could cause excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving
or loss of strength The foundation excavations should be properly
backfilled tight against the slab footing immediately after setting of
the concrete to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained
to drain all surface run off away from the slab foundation
Pavements and Parking Areas
Based on our field observations and laboratory results of the site
materials the preliminary pavement design is as follows
2 Inch Asphalt Concrete
6 Inch Base Course
18 Inch Select Non Expansive Borrow
26 Inch Toal Pavement Thickness

Additional soil testing specifically CBR testing should be
performed on the actual subgrade soil samples during construction and
the above design section can be revised accordingly
Due to the presence of expansive soils grasscrete may not be
suitable for the site since it would allow water to penetrate down to
the existing expansive soils resulting in swelling and softening of the
subsoils
If the use of grasscrete is desired in the parking area provisions
should be made to prevent water saturation of the underlying expansive
soil This may be accomplished by the use of special subsurface
drainage layers and impermeable lining under the grasscrete
Design Review
Plans and specifications for foundation site grading and utility
construction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and
written comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included
CONSTRUCTION MONTTORING
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during
earthwork construction to monitor the contractor s grading operations
particulary fill placement
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be examined by a
soils engineer during construction to see that adequate bearing has been
obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the
findings of our exploration
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
