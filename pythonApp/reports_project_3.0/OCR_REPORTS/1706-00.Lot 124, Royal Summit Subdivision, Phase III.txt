Soil Engineering Report
Lot 124 Royal Summit Subdivision
Phase III
Aiea Oahu Hawaii
In accordance with your request we have made a study of the near
surface soil conditions at the above referenced lot in order to provide
foundation recommendations for the proposed residence The study
consisted of drilling three 3 shallow borings testing of the swelling
potential of the soils in our laboratory and reviewing of the subdivision
site grading and the preliminary house foundation plans This report
summarizes our findings and recommendations
Proposed Construction
The proposed residence will be a two story wooden structure with CMU
walls for the basement The house is approximately 60 by 100 feet in plan
dimension
Concrete slab is planned for the basement level garage and
driveway Post and beam construction is planned for the main floor level
of the house

The subject lot is on the northeastern side of Naukewai Place at the

Discussion and recommendations
General
The hard to very stiff reddish brown clayey silt encountered can
generally provide adequate foundation support for the proposed house
provided that some care is exercised during construction to minimize the
swelling potential of the soil
The field exploration and laboratory testing performed on the hard
clayey silt soil indicates that this material generally has low swelling
potential if maintained in a moist condition Therefore to minimize
effects due to soil swelling under on grade slabs we recommend that the
on site soil be capped with at least 12 inches of non expansive select
fill and that the subgrade soils underneath the select fill and slab be
moistened and protected from drying from the start of construction until
the slab concrete placement
House Foundations
Spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the proposed
residence An allowable bearing value of 2500 o s f may be used on the
natural soil or compacted fill

Individual footings on level areas should be embedded a minimum of
12 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade Continuous wall
footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the adjacent
finished grade
If located on a sloping area the footing should be embedded down an
additional amount to obtain a 6 foot minimum horizontal set back distance
measured from the outer edge of the footing out to the slope face This
additional embedment depth is required to help resist the downhill slope
creep movement and to develop the recommended bearing capacity of the
soil
Select non expansive soils should also be used as backfill behind
the retaining walls to minimize the additional pressure due to the
swelling potential of the natural soil

Slab On Grade
It should be noted that some of the on site clayey silt soils may be
moderately expansive if allowed to dry Therefore the on site soil
should be over excavated down to a depth of 12 inches below the slab
bottom thoroughly moistened and then replaced with non expansive imported
soils as previously discussed The thickened edge of the slab should be
embedded a minimum of 12 inches below the lowest adjacent finish grade
To further reduce future post construction swelling of the
underlying expansive soils we recommend that the in place clayey silt
below the upper capping fill pad be kept moist for one week prior to
concrete placement heavily soaked with water for the first two days and
sprinkled with water for the remainder of the week
A 4 inch layer of 3B Fine gravel cushion fill should be provided
below the slab In order to protect against future moisture infiltration
an impervious moisture membrane may be incorporated below the slab The
on grade slabs should be structurally separated from wall foundations

Concrete Driveway
Since the driveway can tolerate some amount of heaving the 12 inch
on site soil over excavation and replacement may be eliminated if some
small slab cracking is tolerable The subgrade soils should be maintained
in a moist condition and prevented from drying out prior to concrete
placement
Joints are recommended in the driveway slab at about 8 to 10 foot
intervals and at discontinuities in order to allow for some relative
movement of the concrete

Drainage
The finish lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings should
be shaped to shed water away from the foundations and to avoid ponding
condition Also it is advised that gutter systems should be installed
around the house and that gutter water should be diverted far away from
the foundations and walkways Excessive landscape watering near the
b should also be avoide
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils

These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance
of the above recomnended foundations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of
strength The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled tight
against the slab footing immediately after setting of the concrete to
prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained
to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundation
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the footing excavation and backfill
operations be observed by the soils engineer during construction to see
that adequate bearing has been obtained and that the subsurface conditions
are consistent with the findings of our subsurface investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above inspections
