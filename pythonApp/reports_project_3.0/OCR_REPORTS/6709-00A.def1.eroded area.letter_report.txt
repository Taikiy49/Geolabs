PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is situated in the lower portion of Papakolea in Honolulu on the Island
of Oahu Hawaii It is our understanding that your firm has been retained by the Department
of Hawaiian Home Lands to design a rehabilitation project for the sewers in the area
During the initial site visits for the project some issues were observed at and in the
vicinity of 2103 Tantalus Drive The first issue is an eroded area in the backyard of
2103 Tantalus Drive that is encroaching on the steep slope leading down to
Auwaiolimu Street Initial observations at the site tend to indicate that this erosion may be
related to an abandoned sewer lateral in the backyard
The second issue is distresses to a retaining wall immediately adjacent to the
Tantalus Drive overpass bridge crossing Auwaiolimu Street This wall has large open vertical
cracks and shows some signs of movement
The final issue related to the project is the steep near vertical slope between the
Papakolea subdivision area and Auwaiolimu Street This slope was cut in both
Punchbowl volcanic tuff and Koolau basalt The portion in the tuff formation appears to be
closely jointed and fragments spall from the slope face and fall to the right of way below
Since portions of the sewer lines run along the top of the slope there are concerns that
cut and cover trenching work could destabilize the slope

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Based on our limited field exploration hand dug test pits the subsurface
conditions at the site generally consist of about 2 feet of stiff clay soils overlying about
2 feet of stiff silty soil with volcanic tuff rock formation at a depth of about 4 feet below
the existing ground surface
The upper clay soil when allowed to dry forms gravel and sand sized clumps in
a relatively loose condition at the ground surface and extends to a depth of about
6 inches to 1 foot
We did not encounter groundwater in the test pits excavated at the time of our
field exploration The existing ground elevations at the project site are at an elevation of
over 200 feet above the highest basal groundwater in the area
DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the initial site visits it appeared that the erosion near the existing sewer
manhole at the rear of the property 2103 Tantalus Drive was related to an abandoned
sewer lateral exposed on the ground surface However hand excavation in the area
disclosed that the lateral was cut off several feet from the eroded area and the end of the
pipe was plugged with clay soil Further excavation in the eroded area did not indicate a
point of origin for the erosion However it was noted that the surface clay soils had dried
and formed gravel and sand sized clumps in a loose condition
Visual observations of the site indicate that this eroded area is the lowest point of
the property and the surface runoff flows through this area It is our opinion that during
heavy rainfall after a dry period the clumps do not rehydrate fast enough to bond into a
stable mass and are washed out
Our observations at the site indicate that if a good vegetative cover is maintained
on the native soil it will retain sufficient moisture to reduce the potential of such erosion
On this basis we recommend backfilling the eroded area with similar soil and covering it
with a permanent erosion control mat that allows vegetation to become established Since
the area is a non structural yard area only light compaction is required
During our work at the site it was noted that there were open holes at the
surface adjacent to the eroded area Excavation at those holes indicated that they lead
to the sewer lateral trench tying into the manhole It was found that the trench had been
backfilled with rubble material such as rocks broken masonry and concrete fragments
The holes were the result of piping of surface soil into the open rubble matrix This
should be remedied during the sewer system rehabilitation by removing the rubble and
properly backfilling the trench
