PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Honolulu Design Center project site is located between the Moana Pacific
development currently under construction and Kapiolani Boulevard in the Honolulu area
on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project site is bounded by Piikoi Street on the east and
Pensacola Street on the west as shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
Based on the information provided the proposed design center consists of a
three story building on the order of about 45 feet in height We envision the new design
center will be of concrete construction encompassing a total floor area of approximately
26 300 square feet on the ground level Based on the information provided by the project
structural engineer we understand the maximum load for the interior columns may be on
the order of about 405 kips per column
We envision that site grading work consisting of minor fills on the order of up to
about 2 feet thick will be required to achieve the design finished grades for the project In

EARTHWORK OPERATION
JJS Construction Inc performed the site grading operations utility installation and
pavement construction for the Honolulu Design Center project The site grading work
consisted of subgrade preparation placement and compaction of fills and backfills
pavement construction and utility trench excavation and backfill
Following the demolition and clearing operations the excavated subgrades
underneath future slab and pavement areas were scarified to a depth of about 8 inches
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and proof rolled with a
vibratory drum roller to a minimum of 90 and 95 percent relative compaction under the
building slab and pavement areas respectively of the maximum dry density as
determined in accordance with American Society of Testing and Material ASTM D 1557
Modified Proctor
Based on our field observations and hand probing with a pointed steel rod it
appeared that the bottom of the grease interceptor excavation was underlain by very soft
and or loose soils We recommended extending the excavation another 2 feet below the
bottom of the grease interceptor The over excavation was backfilled with 18 inches of
No 3B Fine gravel wrapped around by a layer of non woven geotextile fabric A 6 inch
bedding layer was then placed above the wrapped 18 inch gravel stabilization layer prior
to setting of the grease interceptor
During the site preparation work soft soil condition was also encountered at the
loading dock driveway area We recommended over excavating 12 inches of the in situ
soft soils at the driveway area The over excavation was lined with two layers of geotextile
fabric and then backfilled with 12 inches of No 2 rock prior to placing the 6 inches of base
course for the driveway
Fill and backfill materials required for the project were generally placed in
near level lifts brought to above the optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of
90 or 95 percent under the building slab and pavement areas respectively of the
maximum dry density determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor
During the site grading operations samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our

Field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations on an
as need basis to confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures
and soil conditions were noted A total of 133 field density tests including retests of
failed areas were performed using the nuclear probe method in accordance with
ASTM D 2922 and D 3017 Compaction test results are summarized on Plates 3 1
through 3 9 of this report These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces
and on constructed fills and backfills Test locations and elevations were estimated by
pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and elevations
should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas
assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction
was obtained
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the earthwork operations during the site grading
pavement and utility construction for the above project observed and tested by Geolabs
in the period covered by this report were performed in general accordance with the
intent of the project plans and specifications and our geotechnical engineering
recommendations presented in the referenced report dated and
subsequent recommendations
LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork may
have been performed without our observation However the level of earthwork
observation and testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some of
the geotechnical risks to the project It should be recognized that there are variations in the
accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the
earthwork construction and materials Our services were performed in accordance with
generally accepted standards of geotechnical engineering practice no warranty is
expressed or implied Provision of construction monitoring by an engineer is not insurance
nor does it guarantee construction of any type The contractor shall retain responsibility for
the quality of the work for adhering to the plans and specifications and for repairing
defects regardless of when they are found
