GEOTECHNICAL INVESTIGATION
In accordance with your request we have performed a geotechnical
investigation for the above referenced project The approximate location
of the proposed site and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location
Map Plate 1 Our work was performed in general accordance with our
proposal of May 29 1986
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
PROJECT CONSIDERATION
The proposed commercial development will consist of a 13 700
shop and on grade parking area around the structures The structures will
be light single story buildings

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our investigation was to explore the subsurface soil
and water conditions at the site to provide preliminary design parameters
for the foundation support of the structure particularly to evaluate if
there is an adequate surface crust to support a spread footing type of
foundation system for the proposed buildings
The scope of our investigation included drilling sampling and
probing in seven 7 borings to depths of 12 to 85 feet below the existing
ground surface Also included were laboratory testing of selected samples
obtained engineering analysis and evaluation of the field and laboratory
data and development of the foundation design recommendations
Detailed description of our field exploration and laboratory test
results are presented in the appendices of this report
SITE DESCREPTION
The project site is located in Kahaluu near the mouth of the
Kahaluu stream about 300 feet northwest of the junction of Kamehameha and
Kahekili Highways The site is flanked by the Kahaluu Fish Pond on the
east Kamehameha Highway on the south and the Kahaluu stream on the west
Presently the southern half of the site is occupied by a Chevron
gas station and the Kahaluu Drive In restaurant Part of the northwestern
quarter of the site is occupied by a soil stockpile about 10 feet high
The remaining area is generally flat The southern developed portion of
the site is paved with asphaltic concrete while the undeveloped portion of
the site is covered with trees and shrubs

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Our seven drilled borings indicate that a minimum of 30 to 55 feet
of highly compressible layers of lagoonal organic sandy silt and clayey
silt deposits exist below the upper 4 to 10 feet of surface fill crust at
the proposed development site Based on our laboratory soil testing and
study of old 1969 aerial photograph most of the existing surface fill
crust was probably placed at least 15 to 20 years ago and it appears that
the underlying soft clayey silt has settled due to the weight of this fill
and is generally normally consolidated Therefore provided that no new
fill is placed future settlement of the existing ground is expected to be
If new fi11 is placed within the site significant ground settlement
would occur due to compression of the soft deposits caused by the new fill
load Because of the low permeability of the soft clayey silt layers the
rate of ground settlement due to the new fill loads will be slow The
laboratory data indicates that it would take about 4 years for 90 of the
total primary settlement to take place
Therefore placement of additional fill within the project site
should be avoided if possible
Foundation Recommendations
Since the design of the proposed buildings have not yet been
finalized details of the structures and their loadings are unavailable
However it is our understanding that the individual column load for the
proposed building will be small less than 8 kips

Our subsurface exploration encountered a variable but generally
competent upper crust of fill at the anticipated foundation elevations for
the proposed buildings Shallow spread or continuous footing may be used
for foundation support of the structures
In general spread footings should be placed at a level as high as
possible to reduce the footing load applied to the underlying compressible
deposits It is recommended however that the footing bottom be embedded
at least 12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade Foundations should
not be placed below about elevation 3 feet or special design may be
required due to the close proximity of the soft subsoils and water level
An allowable net bearing capacity up to 1500 p s f may be used in
the footing design The bottoms of the footing excavations should be
recompacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density established by
ASTM D 1557 78
Estimated footing settlements under the design loading are
anticipated to be on the order of less than about 1 inch total and 0 5
inch differential It is probable that some footings would be underlain
by organic silt or clay lens beneath the fill For these footings we
believe that settlements would be slightly increased and occur slowly
Settlements
Based on our findings we believe that some ground settlements will
occur due to the building loads and in some areas the weight of the new
fill
In the proposed market and shops area located makai of the Chevron
gas station a portion of the building site has been subjected to
surcharge loads of approximately 5 feet of fill At the proposed retail
store area located southeast of the Kahaluu Drive In a portion of the
site has been surcharged with about 1 5 feet of fill

In the proposed market and shops location upon removal of the
10 feet high fill stockpile the soil beneath approximately half
of the site area will be over consolidated due to the weight of
the stockpile Consequently when the subsurface is subjected
to building load some differential settlement may occur
In order to minimize potential short and long term differential
settlements that may be detrimental to the building structures
we believe that a surcharge program should be considered for the
portions of the buildings situated outside of the existing
stockpile surcharge to initiate the ground settlements prior to
the construction of the new market and shops structures
The total height of the surcharge should be about 5 feet
Greater surcharge height is not recommended because of the
possibility of bearing or slope failure
The length of the surcharge period should be as long as
practicable because of the anticipated slow rate of settlement
see Plates 3A and 3B We recommend that a minimum 2 months
surcharge period be used Settlement gauges should be installed
in the area and periodic survey readings of the gauges should be
used to monitor the actual settlement The readings should be
taken by the project surveyor and transmitted to the soils
engineer in order that the actual settlement conditions can be
evaluated
In the proposed retail store area settlement due to the
placement of about one foot of fill over the existing
approximately 6 foot thick fill crust is expected to be
minimal However the settlement monitoring procedures
recommended above should be implemented

Site Preparation
No major fill is recommended since the additional weight of fill
will initiate ground settlements of the underlying very soft subsurface
soils conditions encountered in the proposed project site
It is recommended that the existing buildings foundations and
pavements be removed in order to provide a uniform subgrade support for
new on grade slabs and A C pavements We recommend that the upper 6
inches of the subgrade soil beneath the new foundation concrete slabs and
pavement should be scarified moisture conditioned and recompacted to 95
of its maximum density as established by ASTM D 1557 78 test method Any
loose or soft clayey soils encountered at subgrade level should be removed
and replaced with compacted select on site granular soils or with other
suitable imported select borrow material compacted to the above mentioned
standard
Also existing cesspools and fuel tanks should be removed and the
resulting excavations backfilled with granular material in maximum 10 inch
loose lifts to a minimum of 90 ASTM D 1557 78
The required fill about one foot maximum to raise the finished
floor elevation for the proposed retail store next to the existing Kahaluu
Drive In should be placed as early as possible in order to minimize
detrimental effects of future fill settlement on the proposed structure
Select on site granular materials and base course material removed
from the existing pavements could be re cycled for the new structural fill
usage New imported fill material should consist of non expansive select
granular materials less than 3 inches in maximum size with less than 20
passing the 200 sieve

Plans and specifications for site grading foundation and pavement

comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included

observed by the soils engineer during construction to see that adequate
compaction has been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are
consistent with the findings of our investingation
