The purpose of this soil engineering investigation was to
explore the subsurface soil and groundwater conditions at
the site to provide soil engineering data for use in
design and construction of the building foundations The
work was performed generally in accordance with our pro

SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed project site is located at the southwest
corner of the intersection of Meheula Parkway and Kamaio
Street in Mililani Oahu Hawaii
The entire site consists of an area of approximately 6
acres and is roughly square in plan configuration It is
bordered on the north by Kamaio Street on the east by

Meheula Parkway and on the west and south by existing
residential lots
Ground elevations throughout the site range from 594 feet
in the southeast corner to 605 feet in the north along
Kamaio Street The overall ground surface slopes down
very gently towards the southeast at a grade of less than
3 percent
A continuous drainage swale runs along the west and south
Sides of the site with a concrete box drain located in
the southeast corner The maximum difference in elevation
between the overall relatively level surface and the bottom
of the swale is approximately 9 to 10 feet located at the
concrete box drain
A concrete hollow tile wall about 6 to 7 feet in height
runs along the west property line A wood fence about 6
to 7 feet in height runs along the south property line
During the time of the investigation there were no struc
tures present on the site Vegetation consisted of a
Sparse growth of grass

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The project site is located within the Schofield Plateau
which is an area between the Waianae and Koolau mountain
range This area was principally formed by younger
basalt flows from the Koolau volcano banking against the
older Waianae range Through time weathering and erosion
formed thick residual and transported soil layers upon the
Koolau basalt flows covering any exposures of the under
lying rock

Subsurface Exploration
Five borings were drilled at the site to depths of 15
feet to 16 5 feet to explore the subsurface soil condi
tions and to obtain samples for laboratory testing
Based on the information obtained from these borings the
Site is generally underlain by a reddish brown and mottled
brown clayey soil throughout the explored depths There
was no noticeable trend in the arrangement of the soil
in comparing all of the borings
A boulderly layer was encountered in Boring No 1 from
approximately 11 feet to 15 feet
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings during
the field exploration
More detailed descriptions of the soils encountered in the
borings are presented in the Appendix
DISCUSSSTON AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration encountered very stiff to hard soils
at the projet site which could provide foundation support
for the proposed structures Laboratory soil tests indi
cated that the subsoils have low swell potential therefore
conventional slab on grade design can be utilized

Foundations
For the proposed structures spread or continuous footing
foundation are recommended
An allowable bearing value of up to 4000 p s f is recom
mended for footings bearing on the very stiff in situ soil
This value of bearing capacity is for total of dead plus
live loads and may be increased by 33 when temporary
lateral loads wind and seismic are considered
Based on the preliminary site grading plan foundations
on compacted fill are not anticipated
The bottoms of footings should be placed a minimum of 24
inches below the finish grade
Surface water should be diverted away from the building
foundations
Anticipated settlements are expected to be relatively
small on the order of less than half an inch
Site Grading
Most of the site will be cut from 1 to 5 feet Fill will
be limited to the area near the existing drainage swale
The site should be cleared of vegetation and debris prior
to filling

W O 1004 00 P 7
Fill and backfill should be placed in 10 inch loose lifts
compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density
established by ASTM D 1557
Where the existing ground is steeper than 5 horizontal to
1 vertical 5 1 benching and keying are required to
properly bond the new fill to the slope The filling
operation should start at the lowest point and continue
up in level compacted layers in accordance with the above
structural fill requirements
Two horizontal to 1 vertical 2 1 fill slopes are recom
mended
Retaining Wall
Retaining walls would be required along the southern boun
dary of the buildings where they extend into the exis
ting drainage swale
In order to reduce the amount of wall backfill required
later when the retaining walls are constructed we recom
mend that the site grading should incorporate a steeper
1 1 H V temporary fill slope ratio along the future
retaining wall area
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1004 00 P 8
The wall backfill should be compacted to a minimum of 90
percent compaction to reduce future settlement of the con
crete slab which will be placed over this backfill
Well graded granular material such as base course rock
or select borrow should be used to backfill the walls
Granular backfill is recommended since it requires less
compactive effort and therefore reduces the build up
of additional lateral pressure against the wall
Weepholes or a subsurface drainage system should be used
to minimize build up of hydrostatic pressures behind walls
The walls may be designed to resist lateral earth pres
sures equal to an equivalent fluid pressure of 40 p c f
Additional load due to surcharge loading should be included
where applicable
A friction factor of 0 4 can be utilized to determine the
Sliding resistance of the wall Additionally a passive
soil resistance of 350 p c f can be utilized The passive
resistance should be reduced by one third when combined
with the frictional resistance

CONSTRUCTIGN MONITORING
It is recommended that the soil engineer be present during
earthwork construction particularly the fill placement
to monitor the contractor s grading operations
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be
observed by a soil engineer during construction to see
that adequate bearing has been obtained and that the sub
surface conditions are consistent with the findings of
our subsurface investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive
tested and approved by the soil engineer prior to hauling
to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent
upon the above inspections

FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by
drilling 4 borings to a depth of 16 5 feet andl boring
to a depth of 15 feet below the existing ground surface
at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan
Plate 1 Truck mounted auger drilling equipment was used
to drill all of the borings
The soil encountered in the borings were classified by
visual and textural examination in the field by our engi
neering geologist who continuously monitored the drilling
operations The classifications were reviewed by visual
inspection and testing in the laboratory The soils were
classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A graphic presentation of the
soils is presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1l through
A 5
Soil samples were obtained by driving a standard penetration
sampler or a 2 4 inch I D split barrel sampler with a 140
pound hammer free falling a distance of 30 inches The
blow counts to drive the sampler the last 12 inches are
shown on the logs of borings at the appropriate sample depth
Bulk surface soil samples were obtained in the proposed
parking lot area using pick and shovel The approximate
locations are shown on the Site Plan Plate 1

selected samples as an aid in the classification and eva

are presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 5

near surface soil in the proposed parking area to evaluate
its suitability as pavement support The test results are
presented on Plate B 1l

surcharge load were performed on the near surface soil to
evaluate its swelling potential The test results are
summarized on Plate B 2
One moisture density test modified proctor was performed
on a bulk sample of the near surface soil to evaluate its

is presented on Plate B 3
Two direct shear tests were performed on selected samples
to evaluate its shear strength parameters The test results
are plotted on Plates B 4 and B 5
