Project Considerations
The project consists of reconstruction of Wilhelmina Rise PCC pavement from
the intersection with 13th Avenue to the intersection with Sierra Drive approximately
4 700 linear feet long Based on the information provided we understand that the
existing Wilhelmina Rise was constructed around late 1920 s and serves as the main
corridor to the Wilhelmina Rise residential areas
Based on the as built information provided we understand that the existing
roadway consists of two travel lanes about 20 feet wide and sidewalks on both sides
about 15 feet wide each within the 50 foot wide Right of Way ROW The existing
pavement consists of about 6 inches thick PCC pavement supported on the subgrade
It is desired to replace the existing aging PCC pavement Based on the current
design concept we understand that fiber reinforced concrete pavement is planned to

Existing Pavement and Subsurface Conditions
Our field exploration consisted of drilling and sampling six borings designated as
Boring Nos 1 through 6 extending to depths of about 4 to 5 feet below the ground
surface The boring locations are shown on the Site Plans Plates 2 1 through 2 3 Based
on the boring information the existing pavement section consisted of 5 5 to 7 inches of
concrete pavement overlying very stiff silty and clayey soils and weathered basalt rock As
mentioned previously the existing 6 inches thick concrete pavement was constructed
around late 1920 s
It should be noted that the depths of the weathered basalt rock encountered in the
borings varied from about 2 feet in the upper section Boring No 4 to 1 to 2 feet below the
ground surface in the lower section Boring Nos 5 and 6 while the rock was not
encountered in the remaining sections of Wilhelmina Rise Boring Nos 1 through 3
Ground water was not encountered in the borings
Laboratory tests were performed on selected soil samples collected during the field
exploration The results of the laboratory testing indicated that the equivalent R values
correlated from CBR values ranged from 37 to 63

In general the existing pavements along Wilhelmina Rise are in fair to moderate
conditions with localized punch failure and depression areas Longitudinal shrinkage
cracks were observed throughout most of the pavement sections Durability D cracks
were also observed in some of the areas due to aging and climate conditions Slippage
cracks were typically observed in most of the intersections especially in the areas where
the road gradient changed significantly from greater than 12 to near level such as
intersections to Sierra Drive
It should be noted that linear panel cracking and corner break were observed in
Pavement Section No 1 between 13th Avenue and Anuhea Street primarily along the
edge of the utility trench concrete patches where it was subjected to repeated traffic
loading We believe that the linear panel cracking and corner break may be due to poor
trench backfill and weak bonding strength at the interface of the PCC and trench concrete
patching suspected no rebar doweled into the existing PCC pavement section
We believe that the slippage cracks observed near the intersections may be due to
excessive seepage water collected near the bottom of the sloping roadway under the
pavements combined with down thrust traffic forces

Pavement Design and Recommendations
Design of the new pavement sections are primarily governed by the traffic
parameters consisting of average daily traffic ADT percent truck distribution design life
and annual traffic growth We understand that no traffic count and truck distribution
information is available for the project The following traffic parameters are assumed in our
pavement design We also assume that concrete has 600 psi rupture modulus and
pavement has doweled joints with no concrete shoulder
Subgrade Modulus K 150 pci
Average Daily Truck Traffic 2 way ADTT 40
Design Life 40 years
Annual Traffic Growth 0
Based on the above design parameters the thickness of the new PCC pavement
may be 8 inches considering a load safety factor of 1 1 and an axle load category of
3 Heavy It should be noted that the PCC pavement using Portland cement concrete is
based on PCA AASHTO Rigid Pavement Design Program version 2 10
As an alternative pavement analysis was also performed using the StreetPave
program from American Concrete Pavement Association Using the above design
parameters the thickness of the new PCC pavement may be 7 inches with 14 feet joint
spacing
It should be noted that the StreetPave program specifies traffic category such as
residential collector minor and major arterial to assign a typical axle load for
calculation of new pavement thickness This approach may result in under estimating
actual traffic force applied on the concrete pavement because acceleration and
deceleration of the trucks driving on more than 12 sloping ground can generate
additional forces in addition to the axle load To compensate for the additional force
generated from truck s acceleration and deceleration the traffic category was assume
as collector although Wilhelmina Rise is located within residential areas
We understand that fiber concrete reinforcing is proposed for the pavement
reconstruction to increase the durability and toughness of the concrete and to reduce
durability D and slippage cracking especially in the areas where the road gradient
changed significantly from greater than 12 to near level such as intersections to
Sierra Drive
Based on the steep roadway gradient greater than 12 we recommend
neglecting the aggregate base course to minimize excessive water collected near the

bottom of the sloping roadway Dowels for transverse joints are recommended to provide
resistance against down ward forces generated by vehicle s acceleration and deceleration
In addition a subdrain trench may be installed near the bottom of the sloping
roadway transversely crossing the entire road width The subdrain trench may be
minimum 12 inch wide and 24 inch deep below the pavement section as shown on
Typical Subdrain Details Plate 3 The subdrain trench should be backfilled with a
permeable material such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation wrapped
around by geotextile Mirafi 180N or equivalent A perforated pipe perforations down
should be placed near the bottom of the trench immediately around the permeable
material The perforated pipe should be day lighted to the catchment basin
Soft loose material may be encountered in the subgrade after demolishing the
existing concrete pavement We recommend complete removal of the soft loose subgrade
material or maximum 3 feet below the finished subgrade whichever is less The
over excavation should be backfilled with imported fill compacted to a minimum 95 percent
relative compaction and moisture conditioned to near the optimum moisture The backfill
should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness
Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a
percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with
ASTM D 1557 Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight
corresponding to the maximum dry density
In general the imported granular fill should consist of non expansive granular fill
such as crushed coral basalt or cinder sand The material should be well graded from
coarse to fine with particles no larger than 3 inches in largest dimension and should
contain between 15 and 30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve The material
should have a laboratory CBR value of 20 or more and should have a maximum swell of
less than 1 percent when tested in accordance with ASTM D 1883
