Foundation Probing and Grouting Report
Storage Tank

Dear Mr Eddy
In accordance with the project specifications we have completed the probing and
grouting observations for the Storage Tank for the above referenced project This
letter report summarizes our field observations made during the probing and grouting
operations performed at the subject site
The subject storage tank is part of the Koloa Poipu Water System project located in the
Poipu area on the Island of Kauai Hawaii as shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The project involves the construction of a storage tank and connecting pipeline
consisting of ductile iron pipe
The scope of our work discussed in this letter report includes observation of the probing
approximately 120 feet in diameter and 22 feet in height The storage tank is to be supported
by a perimeter wall footing and sixteen interior column footings As part of project
requirements the tank foundation was to be probed and grouted as part of the foundation
preparation The intent of the foundation probing and grouting operations is an attempt to
locate and grout potential subsurface cavities and or voids beneath the tank foundations
The storage tank foundation locations and probe hole locations were surveyed
and staked out by the sitework contractor Koga Engineering Construction Inc The probe
holes were marked along the perimeter footings spaced at about 10 foot on center and at the
center of the sixteen interior column footing locations At the time of the probing and grouting
operations the existing ground surface ranged between 2 feet and 2 feet from the footing
subgrade level The approximate locations of the probe holes for the storage tank are shown
on the Probe Hole Location Layout Plate 2
The probing operations were performed by The probe holes
were drilled with a track mounted drill rig equipped with an air track hammer The probe holes
drilled were about 3 5 inches in diameter and extended to depths of about 6 to 10 feet below

Materials encountered during the probe drilling operations generally consisted of
reddish gray to gray medium hard to hard basalt formations interbedded with layers of clinker
materials Based on our observations of the probing operations small cavities and or voids
were encountered in 35 of the 52 probe holes drilled A summary of our foundation probe
drilling and grouting observations is provided on Plates 3 1 and 3 2
Following the probing operations each probe hole was fitted with a 2 inch diameter
PVC pipe to maintain the integrity of the open hole After completion of the probe drilling
operations the probe holes were filled by injecting grout into the probe holes at an average
rate of about 0 16 cubic yards per minute The grouting operations were performed by
Thronas O Inc A total of about 16 cubic yards of grout was used to fill the 52 probe holes and
cavities encountered The maximum grout intake was located at Probe Hole No 8 below the
perimeter wall footing where a void was encountered between about 3 and 5 feet below the
surface
In summary the foundation probing and grouting operations have been conducted in
general conformance with the intent of the specifications and the geotechnical engineering
exploration report Based on our observations it is our professional opinion that the cavities
encountered at the storage tank location have been grouted and that the subgrade area
should provide the recommended foundation support for the storage tank structure
