INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the design of the proposed wastewater pump station in the Sand Island
Industrial Park The exploration was performed in accordance with our revised proposal
dated May 17 1991
The general location of the project site is shown on the Project Location Map
Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings resulting from our field exploration and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analyses based on the data
obtained from the field exploration and laboratory testing This report supplements and
confirms the discussions presented in our letter report dated except that
the proposed wet well invert elevation of 8 28 feet below Mean Lower Low Water has
been changed to 9 28 feet below Mean Lower Low Water MLLW and the
recommended high water table of 2 5 feet above MSL has been changed to 3 3 feet
above MLLW for uplift design
The recommendations presented in this report are intended for design input and
are not intended to form the basis for preparation of construction cost estimates or for
bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to demolish a metal building and a metal shed currently existing at
the site and to construct a new sewage pump station at the corner of Central Way Road
C and Grant Way Road F in the Sand Island Industrial Park It is our understanding
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2701 00 Page 3
that the proposed building will be approximately 28 by 56 feet in plan dimension including
the intake chamber and pump well
It is our understanding the existing ground elevation at the project site is
approximately 7 5 to 8 0 feet above Mean Lower Low Water MLLW The proposed
pump station building will be a single story masonry and concrete structure with finished
floor elevation at 8 5 feet above MLLW Therefore minimal site grading is anticipated
It is our understanding that the bottom elevation of the proposed wet well of the
sewage pump station will be at elevation 9 28 feet below MLLW Therefore a requirement
for relatively deep excavation extending below the groundwater table is anticipated
It is anticipated that concrete slab on grade construction will be provided for the
pump station building and asphaltic concrete pavement will be utilized for the access
roadway and parking area

of geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to the design and construction
of the proposed wastewater pump station

SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed pump station site is located at the southwestern corner of the
intersection of Central Way Road C and Grant Way Road F in the Sand Island
Industrial Park

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by drilling and sampling
two 2 borings in the vicinity of the proposed pump station The presence of an existing
metal building at the site precluded drilling at the actual location of the proposed
structure the borings were therefore located in adjacent streets as shown on the Site
Plan Plate 2 Both borings were advanced to depths of 40 5 feet below the existing
ground surface
In general the borings encountered a thin surface fill crust about four 4 feet
thick consisting of dense sand with coral gravel overlying loose sand and coral gravel
with lenses of loose clayey fine sand Very loose and soft silty fine sand with clay were
encountered between approximate depths of 16 to 30 feet below the existing ground
surface Medium dense to dense silty sand and coral gravel were encountered at
approximate depths of 30 0 to 40 5 feet the maximum drilled depth
The groundwater level was encountered in the drilled borings at depths of about
5 7 and 6 5 feet below the existing ground surface

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
It is our opinion that the proposed project is feasible from a geotechnical
engineering point of view Based on our field exploration and current project design it
is anticipated that the bottom of the wet well will encounter a stratum of very loose and
soft silty fine sand with clay Following excavation and replacement of 4 feet of this
material mat foundation may be used to support the structure The foundations and floor
slab of the station building will bear on compacted fill Spread footings or mat
foundations can be utilized to support this portion of the structure
Due to deep ground excavation anticipated both excavation shoring support and
dewatering will be required for the proposed construction
Detailed discussions are presented in the following sections
Foundation Excavation and Dewatering
The excavation for the proposed pump station and trenches for the connecting
sewer lines will encounter loose and soft soil conditions and groundwater Therefore it
is anticipated that shoring will be required to support the excavation sidewalls during
construction Generally the method and design of the shoring should be made the
responsibility of the contractor within practical limits and safety considerations It is our
opinion that continuous interlocking sheetpiles and or braced shoring should be
considered
Based on the groundwater data and the proposed lower floor elevation of the
pump station the average groundwater levels at the site may be about ten 10 feet
above the anticipated bottom excavation It is anticipated that relatively permeable loose
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2701 00 Page 7
and soft silty fine sand with clay will be encountered at and below the excavation bottom
Therefore moderate dewatering pumping flows will be required to dewater the
excavations to allow construction to be performed in the dry
It is our opinion that the definition of Dewatering in the contract documents should
be written to include all works or systems required to lower the natural groundwater table
and to exclude the water from the excavations to permit construction of the proposed
underground structures under safe and dry conditions These works or systems should
therefore include but not be limited to pumping only
Dewatering operations should be performed so that only clear water is pumped
Excessive pumping which removes soil fines may result in a quick condition or blowing
of the excavation bottom or sides
Based on the encountered subsoil conditions and our preliminary estimate the
sheeting used in the excavation sidewalls and trench supports should be driven to a
minimum embedment of ten 10 to fifteen 15 feet below the excavation bottom to
reduce the potential for heaving or piping of the sandy subgrade at the excavation
bottom We recommend that the excavation bottom be over excavated by a minimum of
four 4 feet and backfilled with 3B Fine gravel or ASTM C 33 No 67 gravel to provide
a cushion layer and a working platform for the construction of the foundations for the
bottom floor of the proposed pump station This cushion layer will also help to reduce
the future foundation settlements
Based on the groundwater level encountered in the drilled borings a groundwater
level of 3 3 feet above MLLW is recommended for the uplift design It is anticipated that
the pump station will be subject to a relatively large buoyant force due to submerged
condition of the structure It is our opinion that the pump station should be designed
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2701 00 Page 8
such that the buoyant force will be resisted entirely by the dead weight of pump station
structure Dewatering operations should be continuous until completion of the pump
station construction and backfill to prevent potential upward movement of the structure
during construction under uplift forces
Foundation Support
In order to provide uniform support for the proposed structure and to reduce the
magnitude of future foundation settlement we recommend that a minimum four 4 foot
thick cushion fill consisting of 3B Fine gravel or ASTM C 33 No 67 should be provided
below the mat foundations for the lower level of the pump station The cushion fill layer
should be tamped with small compaction equipment to densify the granular material
It is anticipated that the foundations of the engine generator room portion of the
pump station building will bear partially on existing fill and partially on backfill of the lower
levels of the structure In order to reduce differential settlements this portion of the pump
station building should be structurally separated from that portion of the pump station
which is supported by deep mat foundations The engine generator room foundations
should bear on a minimum of two 2 feet of select non expansive material compacted to
a minimum of 90 maximum dry density as established by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
to provide uniform foundation supports
For the design of spread or mat footing foundations bearing on compacted fill an
allowable bearing capacity of 3000 p s f may be utilized This value may be increased
by one third when temporary wind or seismic loads are considered
Perimeter foundations should be embedded a minimum of 12 inches below the
lowest adjacent outside grade
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2701 00 Page 9
Estimated settlements for foundations bearing on compacted fill are less than one
1 inch total and one half 1 2 inch differential
Slabs On Grade
It is our understanding that the ground floor of the engine generator room will be
concrete slab on grade It is anticipated that this portion of the building will bear partially
on existing fill and partially on backfill of the lower levels of the pump stations We
recommend that this portion of the pump station building should be structurally separated
from the pump station supported by deep mat foundations
A 4 inch layer of 3B Fine gravel or ASTM C 33 No 67 should be provided below
the concrete slab on grade as cushion fill Underlying the cushion fill a minimum of two
2 feet of select non expansive material compacted to a minimum of 90 maximum dry
density in accordance with the ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 is recommended in order
to provide uniform foundation support The slab subgrade should be kept moist prior to
placement of concrete
In order to prevent future moisture infiltration and subsequent damage to floor
coverings generating equipment or control equipment an impervious moisture membrane
is recommended below the slabs

Sewer Line Excavation and Backfill
It is our opinion that excavation trenches for the connecting sewer lines in the
vicinity of the pump station will encounter subsoil and groundwater conditions similar to
those described for the pump station excavation
It is anticipated that shoring will be required to support the excavation trench
sidewalls during construction Generally the method and design of the shoring should
be made the responsibility of the contractor It is also anticipated that it will be necessary
to dewater the excavations to allow construction to be performed in the dry Again
selection of the dewatering methods and equipment should be at the option of the
contractor
To reduce the potential of ground subsidence or settlements deep areal
drawdowns of the groundwater table should not be permitted In the vicinity of existing
structures recharging of the water may be required to maintain groundwater levels at or
near the existing levels
Dewatering operations should be performed so that only clear water is pumped
Excessive pumping which removes soil fines may result in a quick condition or blowing
of the excavation bottom or sides
Generally the excavation and backfill operations for construction of the connecting
sewer lines should conform to the requirements stipulated in Section 11 of the Standard
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2701 00 Page 12
Specifications for Public Works Construction Trench Excavation and Backfill dated
September 1986 It is our opinion that the excavated material may be used as backfill
between one 1 foot above the pipe and two 2 feet below finish grade Backfill should
be placed in level horizontal lifts of loose thickness not exceeding ten 10 inches and
compacted to a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density established by ASTM
D 1557 78
The upper two 2 feet of backfill below finished grade in roadways and other
paved areas should consist of select non expansive material compacted to a minimum
of 95 of the maximum dry density established by ASTM D 1557 78
Site Grading
It is our understanding that the finished floor elevation of the proposed pump
station building will be at elevation 8 5 feet above MLLW Based on the available
topographic survey map existing ground elevations at the project site range from about
7 to 8 feet above MLLW Therefore minimal site grading is anticipated
Prior to the commencement of any earthwork all areas within the grading limits
should be cleared and grubbed of all vegetation demolition debris rubbish and other
deleterious materials The resulting grubbed and spoil material should be disposed of
properly off site Any soft weak or yielding areas disclosed during the clearing operations
should be over excavated to firm ground and the resulting excavation should be backfilled
properly with compacted fill
Fills and or structural backfills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding ten 10
inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 maximum dry density as
established by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 For below grade structures it is
recommended that a clean granular material such as 3B Fine or ASTM C 33 No 67
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2701 00 Page 13
gravel be utilized as backfill to reduce the amount of compactive effort required to attain
90 compaction
Pavements
It is anticipated that asphaltic concrete pavements will be used for access
roadways to the proposed pump station For preliminary design purposes the following
pavement section is recommended
2 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 Inch Base Course
8 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
on Compacted Moist Subgrade
We recommend CBR and density tests and or field observations be performed on
the actual subgrade soils encountered during construction to verify the adequacy of the
above section The recommended section is based on the assumptions of light traffic
and that adequate drainage will be provided in the paved areas
Design Review
Drawings and specifications for the proposed construction should be forwarded
to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comment prior to construction This
review is needed to determine adherence to the earthwork and _ foundation
recommendations given herein If this review is not made the geotechnical engineer
cannot assume responsibility for misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during construction of the earthwork and foundation
phases of the work This is to observe compliance with the design concepts
specifications or recommendations and to allow design changes in the event that
GEOLABS HAWAII

subsurface conditions differ from that anticipated at the time of preparation of this report
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and approved by
the geotechnical engineer prior to delivery to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during construction are different
from those assumed or considered in this report appropriate modifications to the design
should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from our drilled borings Variations of conditions between the drilled
borings may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident
until construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to
reevaluate the recommendations given in this report
The location of the field borings are approximate having been determined by
taping from reference points shown on the available topographic survey map not
referenced Elevations of the borings were determined by estimation from information
presented on the same survey map The physical locations and elevations of the borings
should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the field borings depict
the approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual
transition

Water level data from the field borings were collected at the times given on the
graphic representations These data have been reviewed and interpretations made in the
formulation of this report However it must be noted that fluctuation may occur due to
variations in rainfall tides temperature and other factors
