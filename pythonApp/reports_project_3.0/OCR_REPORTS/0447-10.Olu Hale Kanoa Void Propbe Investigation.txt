As requested we drilled five borings on April 21 and 22 1975
at the subject site to delineate the extent of the backfilled
cavity encountered at the mauka Diamond Head corner of the
building
Probes P 1 through P 4 were drilled at the mauka Diamond Head
corner of the structure where a large cavity was uncovered
The locations af these four probes are shown on the enclosed
Site Plan Probe P 5 was drilled at Column Line 4 A
The probes were extended to depths of 15 to 20 feet below the
present graded ground surface level of approximately elevation
23 feet A detailed description of the materials encountered
in the five probe holes is attached
Probes P 1 through P 4 indicate that the loosely backfilled
cavity is a localized condition The extent of the cavity is
delineated by the present excavation and is confined to
between Column Lines E and F and 1 and 2
The cavity or void extended approximately seven feet below the
present ground surface to elevation 16 feet where a one foot
thick compressible black adobe clay layer was encountered
W a 1975
To avoid large differential settlements this clayey layer
should be removed and the footing extended to the underlying
light brown cinders or to the light brown coral
The enclosed Section A A along Column Line 1 summarizes the
depth of excavation to reach the coral or cinder bearing
materials The excavations should be backfilled with lean
concrete to the proposed foundation bottom elevation of 21 5
feet
Prior to placement of the lean concrete the bottom of all
footing excavations should be inspected to assure that the
competent bearing has been reached and that the loose materials
have been removed
Probe P 5 drilled at Column Line A 4 indicated dense coral
at a depth of approximately 4 feet below the existing ground
Surface
The above information and recommendations were given to
during our field meeting this morning If you
have any questions please feel free to call
