This progress report summarizes our work efforts and the field instrumentation
monitoring results for the Mona Street Stabilization Aina Haina Slide Zone Remediation

general accordance with the scope of services outlined in our fee proposal dated
March 21 2006
This report provides information for the lateral earth movements subsurface
water levels detected to date during the instrument monitoring effort and geotechnical
recommendations for remediation of the on going landslide We included general
information of our instrumentation program in our progress report dated
The geotechnical exploration results were presented in our report
entitled Revised Subsurface Investigation Program Report Mona Street Stabilization
Aina Haina Slide Zone Remediation Honolulu Oahu Hawaii dated
This report is the tenth semi annual progress report covering the 60 month
instrumentation monitoring period which officially commenced in January 2002

inclinometer was installed in this boring to better delineate slide limits Locations for B 1

PAVEMENT DEFORMATION MAPPING
Ground surface observations were recorded in our previous reports
Approximately 1 to 2 inch wide pavement cracks on the surface of the roadway were
observed between 1189 and 1199 Mona Street from May 2002 to September 2002
No significant continuous cracking development was recorded from September 2002 to
January 2003
Pavement resurfacing was performed in localized areas of Mona Street during
January 2003 to August 2003 Therefore a majority of the cracks previously recorded
was patched with asphaltic concrete No significant continuous cracking development
was recorded from January 2004 to August 2004
Our pavement deformation mapping resumed in Year 2005 During this period a
majority of the cracks recorded prior to the pavement resurfacing was again observed
on the resurfaced pavement with some extended new cracks as shown on the revised
Crack Location Plan Plate 1 2 For view comparison a series of photograph taken during
the monitoring period is presented on the Plate Nos 8 through 13
The crack observations indicate that the lateral earth movement at the project site
is active To reduce the potential for significant surface water infiltration into the
subsurface the cracks offset and gaps should be patched with asphaltic concrete
FINDING AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Inclinometer Nos I 3 and 4 indicated some shallow depth subsurface ground
movement at their respective instrument locations Inclinometer Nos I 1 and 1 2
currently do not indicate significant subsurface ground lateral movement We believe
that these Inclinometers may be located behind the sliding wedge zone Our piezometer
data indicates that subsurface water seepage occurred at the project site Based on our
pavement deformation mapping we believe that lateral near surface earth movement at
the site is active

Based on the interpretation from Inclinometer No I 3 it appears that the sliding
zone may be relatively shallow approximately 11 to 15 feet below the ground surface in
the area near Boring No 3 Inclinometer No I 4 is between Inclinometer Nos I 2 and
I 3 It appears that the slide zone near Boring No 4 may be slightly deeper than
Inclinometer No 3 at a depth of about 20 feet below the ground surface
Based on the results of instrumentation monitoring and slope stability analyses
we believe that the existing shallow active sliding zone may be located on the down
slope side of Mona Street from 1169 to 1217 Mona Street The head of the sliding
wedge may be near the edge of the pavement on the down slope side of Mona Street
while the toe of the sliding wedge is in the middle of the slope
Based on the data collected in the previous five year monitoring period we
believe that the existing active sliding zone is relatively well defined Therefore we
recommend terminating the current monitoring program and beginning to design
remediation of the sliding hazard along Mona Street We believe that the following
design scheme may be utilized for the project Details of the design schemes should be
developed during the design phase
Drilled Shaft Wall
Drilled shaft wall may be installed and extended to the depth of approximately
50 feet Depending on the location of the drilled shaft wall a tieback system may be
necessary to provide additional lateral support A subsurface drainage system will also
be required to intercept subsurface seepage
