Soil Investigation
Proposed Mezzanine Addition
429 Waiakamilo Road
Honolulu Oahu Hawaii
In accordance with your request and our proposal of February 19
1985 we have performed a soil investigation for the above referenced
project The approximate location of the proposed addition is shown on
the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
Purpose and Scope
The purpose of this investigation was to explore the subsurface soil
conditions at the new addition area to provide soil data for the design
of the foundation of the proposed 20 by 15 feet mezzanine structure
The scope of investigation included drilling and sampling two 2
borings to depths of 20 and 22 feet at the two ends of the addition area
and engineering evaluation of the field data and report preparation

Detailed description of our field exploration and logs are presented
in Appendix A
Proposed Construction
It is our understanding that the proposed 20 by 15 feet mezzanine
structure will be supported by six columns carrying a vertical load of
50 kips per column Underneath the mezzanine structure a 1000 gallon
juice storage tank will be installed on a new concrete pad
Site Description
The site is located on the Ewa side of Kapalama drainage canal
adjacent to the new Gem warehouse It is bounded to the east north and
south by an existing chain link fence an office warehouse structure and
a refrigeration unit
The headroom available at the site is limited to approximately 22
feet due to the roof overhanging out from the adjacent warehouse A
cesspool is located at the Ewa end of the addition area The entire area
is covered by concrete with thickness varying from 3 to 6 inches
Subsurface Conditions
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and
sampling two 2 borings to depths of 20 to 22 feet below the existing
ground surface The approximate locations of the borings are shown on
the Site Plan Plate 2
Boring No 1 located on the Diamond Head side encountered 3 5 of
fill material below which lay 4 feet of soft organic silty to clayey
material Underneath the organic soil lay approximately 6 feet of loose
coral gravel and loose silty to clayey sand Hard coral was then
encountered from about 14 feet to the full depth of the borings drilled

Discussion and Recommendations
General
In general the site is underlain by a layer of loose compressible
material over competent coral formation For shallow spread footing
foundation immediate and perhaps future settlement are anticipated
Deep foundation on the other hand has to be extended to the underlying
coral formation located at 13 5 to 16 feet below ground surface
For the construction of shallow spread footing an appropriate
approach would be to excavate the underlying loose fill and organic
material and replace them with controlled compacted backfill However
the close proximity of the warehouse structure and the high water level
have rendered this method impractical because excavation of loose
material underwater would likely cause collapse or cave in of soil
underneath the foundation of the warehouse
For deep foundation the lack of headroom and maneuverable space for
conventional pile driving equipment has prevented the use of driven piles
as foundation Also it would be uneconomical to use driven piles for
small scale projects

Realizing the subsurface condition at the site and the construction
difficulties as mentioned above the following alternative recommendations
are presented
Foundation
Shallow Spread Footing
With the provision for future relevelling of the mezzanine
structure shallow spread footing can be used as foundation support
The spread footing should be placed at a level as high as possible
to reduce the footing load applied over the underlying compressible
deposits It is recomaended however that the footing bottom be
embedded at least 12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade
Recompaction of the top 12 inches of the bearing soil to achieve
90 of maximum dry density as established by ASIM D 1557 78 method
prior to the construction of footing is recommended
We believe the footings located next to the existing warehouse
should be placed as far away as possible from the warehouse in order
to reduce disturbance to its foundation soil To achieve this we
recommend that some degree of cantilevering on the warehouse side of
the mezzanine structure be allowed
For design of the spread footing foundation a maximum net allowable
soil bearing pressure of 1000 p s f could be used Settlements
under this maximum allowable pressure is estimated to be about
of the settlement will occur soon after the load is applied
Differential settlements on the order of three fourths 3 4 of an
inch is expected The Ewa footing should be located away from the
existing cesspool to minimize excessive footing settlement due to
questionable cesspool backfill

It is recommended that the actual settlement of the columns at both
ends of the addition be monitored so that appropriate adjustments
could be made at a later date Provisions should be made so that
these columns could be adjusted as deemed necessary
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive
earth pressure developed against the buried vertical faces of the
foundation system and by friction resistance developed between the
bottom of the foundations and the bearing soil
Resistance due to passive pressure may be calculated using an
equivalent fluid pressure of 250 p c f This assumes that the soil
around the foundation is well compacted The frictional resistance
may be calculated by using a coefficient of 0 35 The above
resistance values are maximum values and do not contain a factor of
safety
Bored Pile Foundation
Bored piles socketed onto the coral formation can be used as
foundation support For the design of bored pile foundation a
bearing capacity of 10 000 p s f can be used for the underlying
hard coral A minimum penetration of 12 inches into the coral
formation is recommended for all the piles
A brief construction procedure for the installation of the bored
pile foundation is presented as follows
1 The 2 5 to 3 0 foot diameter holes should be drilled to the
designated depth below the ground surface To prevent caving in
of the holes bentonite or other drilling mud should be used
If found necessary temporary casing may be required

The fruit juice tank to be supported on a ground Slab should be

Design Review
Plans and specifications for foundation should be forwarded to the
soils engineer for review and written comments prior to construction to
see that the intent of our recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
We strongly recommend that the footing excavations and or the bore
pile drilling be inspected by the soils engineer prior to placement of
concrete or reinforcing steel to check that adequate bearing has been
obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the
findings of our investigation
I conditions encountered during construction are different from
those presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above inspections

Investigation Limitations
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations
made in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation
engineering principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all
other warranties either expressed or implied
The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are based
on our site reconnaissance and soil information from boring and laboratory
test data obtained from this soil investigation
Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encountered and cannot be
fully determined by reconnaissance and taking soil samples Unforeseen
conditions such as pockets of organic soils trash fill or expansive
soil may occur in localized areas which may require additional probing or
corrections in the field Therefore additional expenditures may be
needed during construction to attain a properly constructed project
Some contingency fund is thus recommended to accommodate these possible
costs
This report has been prepared in order to assist the engineer in the
foundation design of the proposed construction
