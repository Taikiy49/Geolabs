SITE GRADING
The Kekuilani Gardens is part of the Kapolei Village 4 development in the District
of Ewa on the island of Oahu Hawaii The project site has been previously mass graded
as part of the Villages of Kapolei mass grading work by Oahu Construction Company with
our firm providing the grading and compaction testing services A general layout of the
project site is provided on the Site Plan Plate 1

The fine grading operations and roadway and utility construction for Kekuilani
Gardens under the current scope of work were performed by Oahu Construction
Company The site grading consisted of clearing and grubbing placement and
compaction of engineered fills and utility trench excavation and backfill
Following the clearing and grubbing operations the exposed subgrades within the
building footprints were scarified to a depth of about 10 inches moisture conditioned to
between 2 and 5 percent above the optimum moisture content and recompacted to a
minimum of 85 percent relative compaction Fill materials were placed in near level lifts
brought to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of
90 percent relative compaction The upper 12 inches below the building pad subgrades
generally consist of 3 inch minus non expansive coralline materials compacted to a
minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place
dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same
soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 91 test procedures Optimum moisture
is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry
density
Due to the grade differences between the existing terrain and the design finished
subgrades over excavation was performed within the pavement areas in order to provide
the 12 inch select borrow and 6 inch base course layers The over excavated subgrades
were then prepared in accordance with the procedures described above prior to capping
with select borrow and base course materials
During the site grading operations samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor
and the test results are summarized on Plate 2 of this report
Periodic field density tests were taken during the fill and backfill operations to
confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures and soil conditions
were noted A total of 167 field density tests were performed using the nuclear probe
method in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 2922 and D 3017 The results of
the compaction tests taken are summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 11 of this report
These tests were taken on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed engineered
fills and backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and
interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed to

LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork was
performed without our observation However the level of earthwork observation and
testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some of the geotechnical
risks to the project It should be recognized that there are variations in the accuracy and
statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the earthwork
construction and materials Our services were performed in accordance with generally
accepted standards of geotechnical engineering practice no warranty is expressed or
implied Provision of construction monitoring by an engineer is not insurance nor does it
guarantee construction of any type The contractor shall retain responsibility for the
quality of the work for adhering to the plans and specifications and for repairing defects _
