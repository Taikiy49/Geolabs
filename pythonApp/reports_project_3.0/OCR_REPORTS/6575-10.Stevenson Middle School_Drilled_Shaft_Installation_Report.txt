Dear Mr Ayers
In accordance with our service contract with your office we observed the static load
test conducted and the production drilled shaft operations for the deep foundations
supporting the Stevenson Middle School Multipurpose Educational Facilities project This
letter report summarizes our geotechnical observations during installation of the drilled
shaft foundations for the project
Geolabs provided geotechnical engineering services for the project and the
findings and recommendations are presented in the referenced report The purpose of our
geotechnical observations was to monitor the geotechnical engineering aspects of the
drilled shaft foundation installation for conformance with the intent of the recommendations
presented in our report In addition we provided Special Inspection services as required
by the City and Country of Honolulu by observing the drilled shaft foundation installation
for general conformance with the project plans and specifications
PROJECT DESCRIPTION
The Multipurpose Educational Facilities project site is within the existing Stevenson
Middle School campus in Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project consists of
constructing a new two story classroom building with about 14 460 square feet of usable
interior space The general vicinity of the project site is shown on the Project Location
Map Plate 1

Based on the geotechnical engineering report referenced for the project we
recommended supporting the new building structure on a deep foundation system
consisting of 24 inch diameter concrete drilled shafts with allowable compressive load
capacities of 70 to 140 kips per shaft The drilled shafts would derive support primarily
from skin friction within the weathered volcanic tuff and cinder deposits present at the site
The typical drilled shaft lengths would range from 15 feet for the 70 kip capacity shafts to
30 feet for the 140 kip capacity shafts
The general contractor for the project was T Iida Contracting Ltd and the drilled
shaft installation subcontractor was Hawaiian Dredging Construction Company HDCC
The following sections provide descriptions of the load test shaft construction and
discussions on the production drilled shaft installation
LOAD TEST SHAFT
As required in the project specifications a dedicated 24 inch diameter load test
shaft was installed at the project site The load test shaft was excavated by the drilled shaft
subcontractor on Temporary casing was not installed for the load test shaft
The load test shaft extended to a depth of about 24 5 feet below the existing ground
surface The drilled shaft subcontractor lowered the reinforcing cage into the drilled hole
and placed concrete in the load test shaft on
After the load test shaft was installed and cured a load test was performed by
Geolabs in general accordance with ASTM D1143 on August 5 2013 The load test shaft
was loaded to a maximum test load of about 180 kips Based on the load test results
drilled shaft lengths of 15 feet for the 70 kip capacity shafts to 30 feet for 140 kip capacity
shafts were adequate to support the design loads with a safety factor of at least 2
PRODUCTION DRILLED SHAFTS
The drilled shaft subcontractor installed 66 production drilled shafts from
August 19 2013 through August 30 2013 The as built diameter and lengths of the
production drilled shafts installed for the project are summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 3
of this letter report In general Geolabs representatives observed the drilled shaft
excavation reinforcing cage placement and concrete placement operations during
installation of the production drilled shafts
In general the production drilled shafts were excavated using a SoilMec R 825 drill
rig equipped with 24 inch diameter auger and clean out bucket similar to the load test
shaft Because the drilled holes stayed open without significant caving in of the sidewalls
polymer slurry and temporary casing were not used during excavation of the production
drilled shafts After excavation of the production shafts to their designated tip elevations
the reinforcing cages were lowered into the drilled holes to the appropriate elevations with
the use of a crane After the reinforcing cage was installed in the drilled hole concrete was
placed through a 4 inch diameter steel tremie pipe starting from the bottom of the hole
During concrete placement we observed that at least 5 feet of concrete head was
