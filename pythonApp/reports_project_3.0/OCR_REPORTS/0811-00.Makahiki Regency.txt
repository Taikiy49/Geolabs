INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this investigation is to evaluate the sub
surface conditions at the site and to provide recommenda
tions for the design and construction of the foundations
of the proposed structure
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations

SITE DESCRIPTION
The site for the proposed building is located east of the
intersection of Waiola Street and Makahiki Way It is bordered
on the west by Makahiki Way on the north and south by resi
dential structures and on the east by Moiliili Park formerly
Honolulu Stadium Access to the site is by Makahiki Way off
of South King Street
The site consists of three adjacent residential lots presently
occupied by 1 and 2 story residential structures and large
trees and shrubs In general the site is flat and slopes
toward the south The ground elevation ranges from about
5 to 7 feet above sea level
PROPOSED DEVELOPMENT
The proposed highrise building will be a 27 story reinforced
concrete structure The lower six floors will be approximately
78 by 126 feet in plan and will be used for parking The
tower structure will be approximately 42 by 97 feet in plan
and will be located on the central and northern side of the
building
Maximum column loads up to about 1540 kips are anticipated

Geology
The project site is located on the Honolulu coastal plain
which consists of a thick series of interbedded marine
and nonmarine sediments capping the Koolau Basalt The
near subsurface structure at the site consists of a broad
elevated coral reef about 30 feet thick partly covered
by nonmarine alluvium and directly overlying another
extensive nonmarine alluvium These conditions are
considered to be the result of a complex series of
Pleistocene sea level changes The present reef plat
form which stands approximately 5 feet above sea level
is apparently a wave cut terrace formed by wave erosion
of an older reef which originally stood over 25 feet above
sea level
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 811 00 P
This widespread reef structure is known locally as the
Waimanalo Reef or the plus 25 foot reef The Waimanalo
Reef directly overlies a basically nonmarine deposit of
sediment which formed during a period of time when the
sea level was 40 feet lower than present
Although commonly referred to as coral reefs Hawaii reef
structure are predominately calcareous algae skeletons
which support a small percentage of coral growth The
reef environment is varied and complex but consists
basically of zones of growing coral and algae deposits
of coarse coral debris on the forereef and deposits of
sand silts and clays on the backreef or lagoon With
changes of sea level these basically calcareous deposits
usually become cemented or even recrystallized by the
action of percolating fresh water Most of the coral
reef as typically seen along Hawaii shores consists of
beachrock cemented sand or beds of cemented sand and
coral algal gravel known as reefrock All reefs also
have channels of deeper water leading through them
Normally these channels are opposite the mouths of
rivers and are typically filled with nonmarine sediments

Soil Conditions
Based on the borings the site appears to be underlain by
a surface layer about 1 to 6 feet thick of brown clayey
silt alluvium and fill over a coral reef formation to
about 40 feet The coral reef formation consisted mostly
of very dense coral algal reefrock near the ground surface
grading to cemented sand at greater depths
A possible stream deposit consisting mostly of gravel was
encountered in Boring No 5 above the coral formation at
11 feet A pocket of stiff clayey silt alluvium to 15
foot depths was encountered over the coral in Boring No 1
Very stiff clayey silt alluvium was encountered below
the coral formation at about 40 foot depths See Genera
lized Cross Section A A Plate 2
Water was encountered in the borings at 4 to 7 foot depths
during the field exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Foundations
The field exploration disclosed that the site is underlain
by a coral reef formation at shallow depths see Generalized
Cross Section A A Plate 2 Dense coral was encountered
below most of the site except for a few pockets along the
perimeter of the building Boring and 5

Boring No 1 encountered a layer of stiff clayey silt
alluvium over dense coral at about 15 feet Boring
Nos 2 and 5 did not encounter dense sand and coral until
about 15 to 20 foot depths
Shallow foundations are recommended for most of the building
where very dense coral was encountered at shallow depths
Pile foundations are recommended where dense coral is not
encountered near the surface
Additional shallow borings are recommended around the
perimeter of the building to delineate where pile founda
tions are required Due to the inaccessibility of much of
the area around the existing residences the additional
borings should be made as soon as possible after the site
is cleared
Spread Footings
Where the dense coral reef formation is encountered at
shallow depths spread or continuous footing foundations
are recommended The footings should be placed a minimum
of 1 foot into the dense coral and at least 2 feet below
the adjacent floor level An allowable bearing value of
5000 p s f is recommended for footings resting on the
dense coral reef formation
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P 7
The bearing value may be increased by one third when con
sidering temporary loads such as wind or seismic loads
Estimated settlements for the footings are on the order
of about one half of an inch total and one fourth inch
differential
Due to the natural variability of coral formations the
footing excavations should be observed by the soils engineer
to see that adequate bearing material is encountered
Pile Foundations
Pile foundations are recommended where the dense coral reef
formation is not encountered at shallow depths The extent
of the pile foundations will be delineated after the addi
tional probings are drilled at the site For preliminary
design purposes the columns along line A between lines
1 and 5 and along lines 9 to 11 between lines D and H
should be supported on piles
Design recommendations for the pile foundations are given
below
1 Twelve inch square precast concrete piles are
recommended
2 The piles should extend to the dense coral formation
at about 15 to 25 foot depths or to the hard clayey
silt layer encountered at about 40 feet
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Q 811 00 P 8
3 The piles should be driven with a hammer delivering
about 20 000 foot pounds of energy The piles
should be driven to a final resistance of 50 blows
per foot for the last foot but not over driven to
more than 10 blows per inch or fraction of an inch
4 A design pile capacity of 55 tons may be used
5 It is recommended that the pile driving be done
under the guidance of a soils engineer to see
that proper penetration is achieved
Site Grading
A few feet of fill is planned to raise the site
The site should be cleared of all vegetation debris
topsoil and old foundations prior to filling
Select granular material should be used for fill and
footing backfill material The fill and backfill should
be placed in 8 inch maximum horizontal loose lifts and
compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density
established by ASTM Test Method D 1557
Slab On Grade
Slab on grade should be placed over a 4 inch layer of base
in size The subgrade should be kept moist prior to pouring
the concrete

Lateral Resistance
To resist lateral loads a coefficient of friction of 0 50
may be used at the base of the foundations Additionally
a passive soil resistance of 300 p c f may also be used
Dewatering
An elevator pit about 10 feet deep is planned along column
line 1 between column lines E and G The water levels were
encountered at about 5 to 7 foot depths in the borings in
this area
Dewatering will be required for the elevator pit foundation
excavation It may also be required for some of the footing
foundations if the dense coral formation is encountered near
the water level
The quantity of water pumped through coral formations varies
considerably and depends on the characteristics of the coral
structure
For shallow excavations perimeter drains can be used to
lower the water level For deeper excavations wellpoint
or deep wells are recommended
The dewatering operations should be performed without
pumping out the soil fines creating a quick condition
or softening the bottom of excavation

Existing Cesspools
Existing cesspools may be located on the site Where exist
ing cesspools are located below or adjacent to footings and
Slabs the sludge should be removed and the cesspool filled
with compacted granular material or concrete The cesspool
walls within 5 feet of the finish grade should be removed
Design Review
The plans for foundations site grading and utility construc
tion should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review
and comments prior to construction to see that the intent
of our recommendations is included in the project plans
and specifications
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the site filling footing excavations
backfill operations and pile driving operations be observed
by a soils engineer during construction to see that adequate
bearing has been obtained and that the subsurface conditions
are consistent with the findings of our subsurface investi
gation
Any imported material required should be non expansive
tested and approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling
to the site

APPENDIX A
FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored
by drilling 11 borings to depths of 11 to 55 feet below
the existing ground surface at the approximate locations
shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
The soils encountered in the borings were classified
by visual and textural examination in the field by our
engineer who continuously monitored the drilling operations
The classifications were reviewed by visual inspection and
testing in the laboratory The soils were classified in
general conformance with the Unified Soil Classification
System A graphic presentation of the soils is presented
on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 14
In the borings soil samples were obtained by driving
split barrel sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30
inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the last
12 inches are shown on the boring logs at the appropriate
depths
