FIELD EXPLORATION
The scope of our work at the site was to investigate the sub
surface soil and rock conditions by drilling two borings to
evaluate the subsurface soil conditions and the necessity of
pile foundations to support the treatment plant No detailed
plans of the treatment plant structures are available at this
time
To explore the conditions at the site one test boring was
drilled utilizing truck mounted rotary wash drilling equipment

SITE CONDITIONS
The site is located in a large depression between the Haiku range
and low lying hills on the makai side This depressed area is
acting as a sedimentation basin for deposition from the higher
elevations The surface area is covered with a thick grass cover
and heavy root system and contains some scattered bushes and trees
Boring 2 which is at a lower elevation than Boring 1 had standing
water on the surface
SOIL CONDITIONS
The ground surface is immediately underlain in Boring 1 by 25 feet
thick soft swampy materials which extends to about 65 feet depth
in Boring 2 Underlying the soft swampy materials is a stiffer clayey

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The upper soil conditions encountered at this site preclude the
use of conventional spread or continuous footings for foundation
support of heavy structures and structures that cannot withstand
relatively excessive settlements
Future site grading involving filling this low lying site will
initiate the consolidation of underlying soft swampy soils and
areal settlement will occur
Future site filling should therefore be constructed as soon as
possible to allow the soft swampy soils to consolidate which will
reduce down drag forces on piles and ultimate areal settlements
after construction has begun
Generally the treatment plant structures should be founded on either
friction piles bearing in the stiff clayey silt layer or end bearing
piles driven to basalt rock

It appears that the basalt is located nearer to the surface in
the vicinity of Boring 1 and the rock surface elevation appears
to be sloping down toward Boring 2
Boring 1 indicated the possibility of shorter pile lengths due
to the 30 foot layer of stiff clayey silt material For end
bearing piles in basalt near this boring location the piles
will have to penetrate through the stiff layer In Boring 2
where the swampy material is much thicker and where the stiff
clayey layer is only 5 feet higher capacity end bearing piles
in rock may be considered
ADDITIONAL STUDIES
Additional borings will be required in a more detailed investigation
after the location and structural details of the proposed treatment
plant are more clearly defined
The more detailed investigation should determine the basalt rock
surface which may be irregular throughout the site The future
studies should also concern itself with making recommendations
for placing of fill over the soft yielding swamp deposits A
surcharging or time loading sequence may be required for structural
fills
Specific design parameters such as loading per pile pile lengths
bearing capacities and a pavement design etc should be provided
