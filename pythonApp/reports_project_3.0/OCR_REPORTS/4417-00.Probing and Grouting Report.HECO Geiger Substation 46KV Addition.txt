Probing and Grouting Report
HECO Geiger Substation 46KV Addition
Dear Mr Shimabukuro
This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations performed during
the probing and grouting operations for the HECO Geiger Substation 46KV Addition on
The subject site is situated within the existing Honouliuli Wastewater Treatment Plant
in the District of Ewa on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the
project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The subject site is approximately 59 by 59 feet in plan dimension The new substation
will include the construction of a Transformer Pad 12 by 15 feet Switchgear Pad 9 7 by
20 feet Battery Cabinet Pad 3 2 by 3 5 feet overhead steel frame structure supported by
concrete piers and an asphalt driveway Based on the grading plans provided the
topography at the subject site is relatively level with ground elevation of 39 Mean Sea Level
MSL Minimal fills are planned
Previously a field exploration was performed by Geolabs Inc for the Secondary
Effluent Reuse Facility at Honouliuli Wasterwater Treatment Plant Loose zones and voids in
the underlying coral formation were encountered The purpose of the probing and grouting
work was to reduce the potential for loss of foundation support resulting from collapse of
cavities or compression of loose zones below foundations and structures
The probing and grouting work was performed by Geolabs Inc A truck mounted drill
rig with continuous flight 4 inch diameter augers was used for the probing work The probe
holies extended about 10 feet below the existing grade elevation and were backfilled with
cement slurry grout injected at low to moderate pressures A field engineer from Geolabs Inc
observed the probing and grouting work
The probing and grouting operations were conducted at the locations of the
Switchgear Pad and the Transformer Pad The locations of these two structures are shown
on the Site Plan Plate Street Honolulu Hawaii 96819

A total of 10 probe holes each 10 feet deep with an aggregate length of about 100
lineal feet were drilled Approximately 78 gallons of cement slurry grout was utilized to fill the
probe holes The approximate locations of the probes are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The field probing and grouting records are shown on the Probe Logs Plates 3 through 12
For the asphalt driveway proof rolling the finish subgrade with a heavy equipment
was performed to assist in detecting and possibly collapsing near surface cavities voids
and loose zones The proof rolling operation was performed on The
finish subgrade within the project site was proof rolled a minimum of 6 passes using a
10 ton vibratory smooth drum roller A field engineer from Geolabs Inc observed the
proof rolling work Based on our observations no visible cavities or loose spots were noted
near the surface during proof rolling
Limitations
Elevations of the probes were estimated from the elevations shown on the Grading
Plans dated by Hawaiian Electric Co Inc The elevations of the probes
should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used
