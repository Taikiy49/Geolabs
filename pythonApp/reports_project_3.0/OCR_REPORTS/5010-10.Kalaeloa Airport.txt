1 1 Introduction
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
conducted for the Runway 4R Localizer project located within the existing Kalaeloa
Airport in the District of Ewa on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and
vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings and geotechnical engineering
recommendations derived from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering
analyses for the project The recommendations presented herein are intended for the
design of foundations for the localizer antenna array and equipment shelter at the
relocated site only The findings and recommendations presented herein are subject to
the limitations noted at the end of this report

2 1 Regional Geology
The Island of Oahu is composed largely of the weathered remnants of two extinct
shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older shield volcano Waianae forms the
bulk of the western third of the island while the younger shield volcano Koolau forms
the majority of the eastern two thirds of the island As volcanic activity in Waianae
ceased lava flows from Koolau banked against its eroded eastern slope forming a
broad plateau now known as the Schofield Plateau or Central Oahu
The forces of weathering and erosion attacked this plateau generating
sediments which were transported to the coast In the vicinity of the project site and
further south these alluvial sediments accumulated and interbedded with marine
sediments and coral algal reef formations to form a sedimentary wedge ranging in
thickness from zero feet in the area of the Interstate Route H 1 Highway to over
1 000 feet at Ewa Beach This wedge forms the Ewa Plain and serves as the confining
formation or caprock over the artesian basal aquifers of Southern Oahu Deposition
of these sediments has continued from earlier geologic time through the present
During the Pleistocene Epoch sea levels fluctuated in response to the cycles of
continental glaciation As the glaciers grew and advanced less water was available to
fill the oceanic basins such that sea levels fell below the present stands of the sea
When the glaciers melted and receded an excess of water became available such that
the sea levels rose to above the present sea level
The higher sea level stands caused the formation of deltas and fans of
accumulated terrigenous sediments in the heads of old bays accumulated reef deposits
at correspondingly higher elevations and deposited lagoonal marine sediments in the
quiet waters protected by fringing reefs The lower sea stands caused streams to carve
valleys in the sediments and reef deposits Subaerial exposure of the sediments and
calcareous materials caused consolidation of the soft deltaic materials and lagoonal
deposits and induration of the calcareous reef materials Land development and

2 3 Subsurface Conditions
The subsurface conditions at the relocated localizer site were explored by drilling
and sampling two borings designated as Boring Nos 101 and 102 extending to depths
of about 20 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate locations of the
drilled borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2

from the existing ground surface The coral formation extended to the bottom of the
borings at about 20 feet below the existing ground surface The coral formation
generally appeared to be slightly to highly weathered and medium hard to hard in
nature It should be noted that the strength characteristics of coral formation are highly
variable The depositional nature of the formation may create localized areas of hard
crystalline coral intermixed with soft severely fractured detritus It should be noted that
coral formation is frequently cavernous or contain pockets of voids Although cavities

3 2 Foundation Probing
Our experience suggests that cavities and or voids are commonly encountered in
coral formations Based on our previous field exploration small voids were present in
one of the borings drilled at the originally proposed localizer site Although cavities
were not encountered in our drilled borings at the relocated localizer site the potential
for the presence of cavities and or voids exists at the project site In order to reduce the
potential for structural damage due to collapse of the cavities below foundations a
program of cavity probing and grouting is often conducted at the foundation locations
during construction
For estimating purposes we recommend that probe holes be drilled at 10 foot
intervals along the perimeter thickened edge footings at the relocated localizer site The
probe holes should be at least 3 inches in diameter and should extend to at least 10 feet
below the planned bottom of foundation elevation If cavities and or voids are
encountered or suspected during the probing operation additional probe holes should
be drilled at closer spacings to help delineate the vertical and lateral extent of the cavity
and or void The probe holes and cavities discovered should be backfilled with
Controlled Low Strength Material CLSM or sand cement grout injected at low to
moderate pressures CLSM or sand cement grout should have a slump of about 6 to
9 inches
Because of the potential for encountering cavities and or voids at the site we
recommend that unit prices for additional probing and grouting be obtained during
bidding In addition the probe drill should be available on site until the probing and
grouting operation is complete The contractor should also be made aware that a

3 5 Utility Trench
We envision that new electrical duct lines will be required to service the proposed
new facility In general granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of clean sand or S4C is
recommended under the electrical duct lines Free draining granular materials such as
clean sand or S4C should also be used for the initial trench backfill up to about
6 inches above the duct lines to provide adequate support around the pipes and to
reduce the compaction effort of the backfill thus reducing the possibility of damaging
the pipes It is critical to use this free draining material to reduce the potential for
formation of voids below the haunches of pipes and to provide adequate support for the
sides of the pipes Improper backfill material and placement procedures could result in
settlement of the backfill and damage to the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 6 inches above the duct
lines to the top of the subgrade or finished grade may consist of on site soils
or imported select granular fill material The backfill should be placed in maximum
8 inch level loose lifts moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content
and mechanically compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce
the potential for appreciable future ground subsidence Where trenches are below
pavement areas the upper 3 feet of the trench backfill below the pavement grade
should be compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Relative
compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the
maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 1557 Optimum moisture is the water content corresponding to the
maximum dry density

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the relocated localizer site were explored by drilling
and sampling two borings designated as Boring Nos 101 and 102 extending to depths
of about 20 feet below the existing ground surface The borings were drilled using a
truck mounted drill rig equipped with continuous flight augers The approximate
locations of the borings drilled are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who observed the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed by visual
observation and testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance
with the Unified Soil Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic
representations of the materials encountered are presented on the Logs of Borings
Plates A 1 and A 2
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by
driving a 2 inch OD standard penetration sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling
30 inches The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches
of an 18 inch drive are shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Borings at
the appropriate sample depths

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture Content ASTM D 2216 and Unit Weight ASTM D 2937
determinations were performed on selected samples as an aid in the classification and
evaluation of soil properties The results of these tests are presented on the Logs of
Borings at the appropriate sample depths
One Sieve Analysis test ASTM C 117 and C 136 was performed on a selected
soil sample to evaluate the gradation characteristics of the soils and to aid in soil
classification Graphic presentations of the grain size distribution of the soils are
provided on Plate B 1
