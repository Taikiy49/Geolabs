INTRODUCTION
This report presents our recommendations for the foundation
design and construction along with percolation testing results
for cesspool design of the proposed radio station towers and
related facilities site
Preliminary design recommendations were provided verbally to
you on December 5 and 8 1980
Subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling a
total of three 3 borings each to 21 5 feet deep below the
existing ground surface The approximate boring locations are
shown on the Site Plan Plate 1 and the description of the
materials encountered in the borings is presented on the Boring
Logs Plates A 1 through A 3

Various laboratory tests were conducted on selected samples
Laboratory test results are presented on Plates A 4 through

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed KAIM Radio Station will include two towers
290 feet high one story tuning house related slab on
grade construction and a cesspool
GENERAL SITE DESCRIPTION
The general area for the proposed radio station towers and
related facilities will be located northeast of Kaluakoi
Road in Molokai Hawaii Portion of Tax Map Key
Parcel 19
The site slopes gently with the ground surface elevations
ranging from about 400 on the west to about 434 on the east
The area is presently covered with relatively thin brush
and Keawe trees No major structures exist at the site
GENERAL SOIL CONDITIONS
Based on the information obtained from borings the site is
generally underlain with stiff to hard reddish brown brown
and gray clayey silt material down to the depth of borings
No groundwater was encountered in our drilled borings during
the field exploration

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the results of our field exploration laboratory
testing and the results of our engineering analysis the
site is suitable for the proposed structures
Specific recommendations for the design of foundations and
construction are presented below
A Foundation
For the proposed radio station towers and tuning station
spread footing foundations are recommended All footings
should be placed on undisturbed competent clayey silt
material underlying the site
An allowable bearing pressure of up to 4 000 pounds per
Square foot could be used to design the footings placed
on competent undisturbed clayey silt material as
described above
The tower footing should have a minimum embedment of
about 4 feet However the concrete anchor and the
tuning station footings could be placed a minimum of
3 feet and 12 inches below the existing grade respec
tively
For the above recommendations settlements are antici
pated to be minimal

We recommend that all footing excavations be inspected
by the soils engineer to confirm the conditions upon
which our recommendations are based Any loose areas
encountered during excavation should be removed and
replaced with concrete
B Lateral Earth Pressures
Concrete anchors will be used to resist the lateral
loads on the towers Two sets of anchors placed 95
and 175 feet from the tower will be placed at three
locations around each tower
Resistance to lateral loads may be provided by passive
pressure Passive pressure may be taken as that
exerted by a fluid of density equal to 400 p c f
The concrete anchors should be excavated neat and poured
directly against the natural ground Backfill over
the concrete anchor blocks of the tower should be com
pacted to 95 percent of maximum dry density established
by ASTM D 1557 standard
C Slab On Grade
It is recommended that slab on grade be placed over a
4 inch layer of base course compacted to 95 of its
maximum dry density

W O 998 00 P 5
The subgrade should be kept moist and protected from
drying prior to concrete placement Subgrade should
be compacted to a minimum of 90 of maximum dry
density
In order to protect against moisture damage of the
flooring material a polyethylene moisture barrier
should be incorporated within floor slabs
D Cesspool Design
Falling head percolation tests were performed in Boring
No 3
After completion of the bore hole drilling and sampling
the hole was filled with water and allowed to saturate
The hole was refilled with water to various depths and
the water allowed to percolate through the subsoils
The changes of water level drop with time was monitored
and recorded
The test data and test results are summarized on Plate 2
Based on the percolation test results it is our opinion
that the underlying soils at the site could provide
adequate percolation rates for a properly designed
private cesspool sewage disposal system Based on the cal
culated average percolation rate of 2 3 gal sq ft day it
is our opinion that a cesspool about 6 feet in diameter and
20 feet deep could be utilized

E Earthwork
Earthwork operations at the site will include footing
excavation backfilling and foundation inspection
during construction
The required foundation excavation would extend to depths
of approximately 4 0 feet below the existing ground
surface It is anticipated that conventional earth
moving equipment could be used to conduct all the ex
cavations for the foundations
The brown silty clay surface on site material could be
used Excavations should be protected from surface
runoff which may enter the excavations All loose
soils or mud should be removed from the bottom of
all footing excavations prior to concrete placement
Some minor backfilling operations would be required
around the footings
Fill and backfill should be placed in horizontal 10 inch
lifts and compacted to at least 95 percent of maximum
dry density within the limits of structures and 90
percent outside the structure areas as obtained using
ASTM D 1557 70 standard
