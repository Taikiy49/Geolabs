As requested Geolabs has provided geotechnical engineering observation and
consultation services during the micropile foundation installation for the Beachwalk
Wastewater Pump Station Improvements project in the Waikiki area on the Island of Oahu
Hawaii This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations performed
during the micropile installation for the above project
Geotechnical recommendations pertaining to the micropile foundations were
presented in our geotechnical engineering report entitled Geotechnical Engineering
Consultation Micropile Underpinning System Beachwalk Wastewater Pump Station
Waikiki Oahu Hawaii dated The purpose of our geotechnical
engineering observation and consultation services was to observe the micropile foundation
installation for general conformance with our recommendations
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is at the existing Beachwalk Wastewater Pump Station WWPS in
the Waikiki area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project location and general vicinity
are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
To reduce potential movements of the structure due to possible construction
activities in the adjoining areas in the future we understand that cased micropile
foundations will be used to underpin the existing wastewater pump station structure In
general the cased micropile foundation system consists of a small diameter usually less
than 12 inches drilled and grouted pile with steel reinforcing The micropile foundation
typically is constructed by drilling a hole with or without casing placing reinforcing steel in
the hole and grouting the hole Micropiles are desirable because they can be installed
readily in access restrictive environments and in numerous soil types and ground

the adequacy of the foundation support system
MICROPILE INSTALLATION
The production micropiles installed at the site consisted of the cased micropile
system The components of the cased micropile system include an unbonded length
permanent steel casing a bonded length at the bottom to provide vertical load resistance
primarily from friction between the grout and surrounding soils and a steel reinforcement
bar extending the full depth of the drilled hole
The micropiles were installed by drilling 5 5 inch OD steel casing using a Champion
40 DTH Hammer The steel casing was extended down to provide a minimum bond
length of 14 feet in the older alluvial deposits The steel casing was left in the drilled hole
and cleaned out by blowing air through the drill rods After the drilled hole was cleaned
out a single galvanized all thread steel reinforcement bar Grade 75 with a diameter of
1 0 inch was inserted into the drilled hole down to near the bottom of the hole The
micropile was grouted by tremie method until relatively clean grout was observed at the
top of the steel casing The grout consisted of a sand cement mixture The steel casing
was extracted to provide a minimum bond length of 14 feet

Production Micropiles
Structural Systems Inc performed the installation of the production micropiles A
total of 28 micropiles were installed from July 27 through August 31 2011 The
approximate locations of the 28 micropiles installed are shown on the Micropile Location
Plan Plate 2
The production micropiles generally extended to depths ranging from about 50 7 to
58 8 feet below the ground surface Based on our field observation it appears that the
production micropiles extended about 10 7 to 18 8 feet into the underlying older alluvial
deposits to develop an allowable compressive load capacity of 13 kips A Summary of
Micropile Installation is presented on Plate 3 for reference
