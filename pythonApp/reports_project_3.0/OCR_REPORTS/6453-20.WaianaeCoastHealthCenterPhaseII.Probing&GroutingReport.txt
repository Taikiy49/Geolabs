Probing and Grouting Program
A foundation probing and grouting program was implemented for the new building
structures planned for the WCCHC facility in accordance with the recommendations
presented in our geotechnical engineering exploration report dated March 30 2012 The
purpose of the probing and grouting operations was to reduce the potential for loss of
foundation support resulting from collapse of cavities or compression of loose zones below
the building structures
Hawaiian Dredging Construction Company Inc HDCC surveyed the footing
layout and marked the probe hole locations prior to the probing and grouting operations In
general the probe holes were located at the column footings and spaced at approximately
10 foot intervals along the wall footings It should be noted that probe holes were not
drilled for the lightly loaded thickened edge footings for the Emergency and Other Clinical
Services Building Building A 1 The approximate probe hole locations are shown on the
Probe Hole Identification Plans Plates 2 1 through 2 3
Geolabs Inc performed the probing work for the Phase II Development project
using a truck mounted drill rig equipped with continuous flight augers The probe holes
were about 4 inches in diameter and extended to depths of about 8 to 19 feet below the
existing ground surface corresponding to approximately 10 feet below the design footing
bottom elevations
The materials encountered during the probe drilling operations generally consisted
of fill materials over tannish white to tan coralline materials with a basaltic boulder layer
underlain by stiff clayey soils at greater depths After completion of the probe drilling
operations the probe holes were filled with a one part sand and one part cement grout
The grout used to fill the probe holes was supplied by Hawaiian Cement and the boom
pump was operated by American Standard Concrete Pumping Hawaii Inc
Our field representatives observed the probing and grouting operations and
recorded the probe time grout time and approximate grout take A total of 94 probe
