PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
Based on the information provided we understand that the Environmental
Protection Agency EPA has required that the County of Hawaii abandon all large
capacity cesspools from service Due to the imminent cesspool closure it is desired to
construct a new wastewater collection system to provide municipal wastewater services to
areas with large capacity cesspools
A geotechnical engineering exploration has been conducted by our office and our
findings and recommendations are presented in the report entitled Geotechnical
Engineering Exploration Wastewater Collection System for the Large Capacity Cesspool

We understand that a Ground Penetrating Radar GPR study was conducted by
Hamili Akina and Associates LLC to evaluate the potential presence for lava tubes and
cavities along the sewer collection system Based on the findings it appears that the

present before making drastic changes to the construction plans Therefore additional
borings were drilled in areas where the GPR survey detected anomolies which could
indicate cavities voids
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of the additional field exploration program is to evaluate the
subsurface conditions at selected locations where anomolies were encountered in the
GPR study to confirm the presence of cavities and or voids In order to accomplish this
we conducted an exploration program consisting of the following tasks and work efforts

obtain the necessary excavation permit and with the various utility
companies for toning of the boring locations
2 Provision of traffic control and safety devices at the boring locations during
our field exploration program
3 Mobilization and demobilization of a truck mounted drill rig water truck and
two operators to and from the project site

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
We explored the subsurface conditions along the proposed sewer line alignment at
Mamane Street by drilling and sampling seven additional borings designated as
Boring Nos 101 through 107 extending to depths of about 26 to 31 5 feet below the
existing pavement surface The approximate boring locations are shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2
Our additional borings drilled along the proposed sewer line alignment at
Mamane Street generally encountered medium stiff to very stiff clayey silts and medium
dense to dense silty sands extending to depths ranging from about 5 to 20 feet below the
existing pavement surface The upper clayey silts and silty sands were generally underlain
by medium dense to very dense clinkers and medium hard to hard basalt rock formation
extending to the maximum depth explored of approximately 31 5 feet below the existing
pavement surface
It should be noted that soft and or loose zones were encountered within
Boring No 105 from about 6 to 10 feet and Boring No 107 from about 10 to 13 feet
drilled for the project In addition although our drilling and sampling operations did not
encounter any cavity and or void in Boring No 102 there exists the possibility of potential
void between depths of about 5 and 15 feet due to the excessive amount of soil cuttings
required to backfill the borehole
In general the borings did not encounter cavities or voids where the GPR survey
detected anomalies
We did not encounter groundwater in the borings at the time of our field exploration
However groundwater levels can significantly vary depending on rainfall temperature
surface runoff groundwater withdrawal and other factors
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration methodology and the Logs of Borings
are presented on Plates A 14 through A 20 of Appendix A

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
We explored the subsurface conditions along the proposed sewer alignment at
Mamane Street by drilling and sampling seven additional borings designated as
Boring Nos 101 through 107 The borings extended to depths of about 26 to 31 5 feet
below the existing pavement surface The approximate boring locations are shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were drilled using a truck mounted drill rig equipped with
continuous flight augers and coring tools
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer who observed the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed by visual observation
and testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified
Soil Classification System as shown on the Soil Log Legend Plate A 0 1 Graphic
representations of the materials encountered are presented on the Logs of Borings Plates
A 1 through A 7
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained in general accordance with
ASTM D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 3 inch OD Modified
California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches In addition some samples
were obtained from the borings drilled in general accordance with ASTM D 1586
Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 2 inch OD standard
penetration sampler using the same hammer and drop The blow counts needed to drive
the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown as the
Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths The
penetration resistance shown on the logs of borings indicates the number of blows
required for the specific sampler type used The blow counts may need to be factored to
obtain the Standard Penetration Test SPT blow counts
Core samples of the rock materials encountered at the project site were obtained
by using diamond core drilling techniques in general accordance with ASTM Standard
Practice D 2113 Diamond Core Drilling for Site Investigation Core drilling is a rotary
drilling method that uses a hollow bit to cut into the rock formation The rock material left in
the hollow core of the bit is mechanically recovered for examination and description Rock
cores were described in general accordance with the Rock Description System as shown
on the Rock Log Legend Plate A 0 2 The Rock Description System is based on the
publication Suggested Methods for the Quantitative Description of Discontinuities in Rock
Masses by the International Society for Rock Mechanics March 1977

Recovery REC is used as a subjective guide to the interpretation of the relative
quality of rock masses Recovery is defined as the actual length of material recovered from
a coring attempt versus the length of the core attempt For example if 3 7 feet of material
is recovered from a 5 0 foot core run the recovery would be 74 percent and would be
shown on the Logs of Borings as REC 74
The Rock Quality Designation RQD is also a subjective guide to the relative
quality of rock masses RQD is defined as the percentage of the core run that is sound
material in excess of 4 inches in length without any discontinuities discounting any drilling
induced fractures or breaks If 2 5 feet of sound material is recovered from a 5 0 foot core
run the RQD would be 50 percent and would be shown on the Logs of Borings as
RQD 50 Generally the following is used to describe the relative quality of the rock
based on the Practical Handbook of Physical Properties of Rocks and Minerals
Rock Quality RQD

Excellent 90 100
The rippability or excavation characteristics of a rock mass is a function of the
relative hardness of the rock its relative quality brittleness and fissile characteristics
Dense basalt formations with high RQD values would be very difficult to rip and probably
would require more arduous methods of excavation

ROCK FRACTURE CHARACTERISTICS
The following terms describe general fracture spacing of a rock
Massive Greater than 24 inches apart
Slightly Fractured 12 to 24 inches apart
Moderately Fractured 6 to 12 inches apart
Closely Fractured 3 to 6 inches apart
Severely Fractured Less than 3 inches apart
DEGREE OF WEATHERING
The following terms describe the chemical weathering of a rock
Unweathered Rock shows no sign of discoloration or loss of strength
Slightly Weathered Slight discoloration inwards from open fractures
Moderately Weathered Discoloration throughout and noticeably weakened though not able to break by hand
Highly Weathered Most minerals decomposed with some corestones present in residual soil mass Can be broken by hand
Extremely Weathered Saprolite Mineral residue completely decomposed to soil but fabric and structure preserved
