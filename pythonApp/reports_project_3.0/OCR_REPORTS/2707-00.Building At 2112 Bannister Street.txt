INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed warehouse building located at 2112 and 2112A Bannister
Place in Kalihi Oahu Hawaii The general location of the project site is shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings resulting from our exploration and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analysis based on the data
obtained from the field exploration and laboratory testing These recommendations are
intended for design input and are not intended for construction estimation or bidding
purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located on the northern side of Bannister Street in Kalihi Oahu
Hawaii
The proposed project involves the construction of a two story warehouse building
at the project site It is our understanding that the ground floor will be of concrete
construction and the second floor of steel frame construction
Currently two 2 methods are proposed to maintain the slab elevation at or higher
than the 23 elevation required for flooding
One of the methods proposed consists of placing the new building slab on the
surface soils in the higher southern portion of the site and spanning over the lower
northern portion of the site with a structural slab supported by spread footings
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2707 00 Page 4
The other method consists of leveling the site by filling the lower northern portion
of the site with about 2 to 3 feet of fill The new building slab will bear on the fill in the
northern portion of the site and the surface soils in the southern portion of the site
An asphaltic concrete parking area and driveway and portland cement concrete
loading docks are anticipated
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions at the project site to develop a generalized soil data set for the formulation of
geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to the design of the foundation site
grading and pavement of the project
The work was done generally in accordance with our proposal of March 6 1991
The scope of work was limited to
1 Mobilization and demobilization of a truck mounted drill rig and
operators to and from the project site
2 Drilling and sampling of five 5 borings at the building site to
depths from 14 5 to 66 5 feet below the existing ground surface
3 Coordination of the field exploration and logging of the borings by
a Field Engineer Geologist from our firm
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2707 00 Page 5
4 Laboratory testing of selected samples obtained during the field
exploration to determine the classification and pertinent engineering
properties of the materials encountered
5 Analyses of the field and laboratory data for the formulation of
geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to the design of the
foundation site grading and pavement of the project
6 Preparation of six 6 copies of a formal report summarizing our work
on the project and presenting our findings and recommendations
7 Coordination of our overall work on the project and client design
team consultation by a Principal Engineer of our firm
8 Miscellaneous work efforts such as drafting word processing and
clerical support
Detailed description of our field exploration and laboratory testing are presented
in the appendices of this report
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located at 2112 and 2112A Bannister Place between Kalihi
Stream and Bannister Street in Kalihi on the island of Oahu
Presently the site is occupied with two 2 apartment buildings three 3 house
structures asphaltic concrete paved parking areas and an asphaltic concrete paved
driveway
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2707 00 Page 6
In general the site consists of two fairly level areas of different elevations that are
separated by a horizontal to 1 vertical slope approximately 5 feet in height at the
central portion of the site
The existing ground elevation is about 21 to 22 feet above Mean Sea Level at the
northern portion of the site and range from 26 to 30 feet above Mean Sea Level at the
southern portion of the site
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions were explored by drilling and sampling five 5 borings
to depths of 14 5 to 66 5 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate
locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2

layer about 0 to 5 feet in thickness consisting of medium stiff brown silty clay with gravel
underlain by an alluvial deposit consisting of medium stiff to very soft brown to dark gray
clayey silt and loose to dense brown and gray clayey silty gravel and sand with cobbles
and boulders to about 33 to 58 feet depths Below the alluvial layer a basalt rock
formation about 2 to 7 feet in thickness was encountered followed by an older alluvium
deposit consisting of very stiff brown and gray silty clay to 66 5 feet the maximum depth
drilled
A pavement section consisting of 2 inches of asphaltic concrete and 4 inches of
base course was encountered above the fill layer in Borings B 4 and B 5
In the southern portion of the site the borings generally encountered a surface fill
layer about 6 to 10 feet in thickness consisting of stiff to hard brown silty clay and

to loose tan brown to brown silty coral sand and gravel to 16 5 feet
Groundwater was encountered in borings at the northern portion of the site from
about 11 9 to 13 0 feet below the existing ground surface at the time of our exploration
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration and laboratory test results are

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
In the northern portion of the site the borings encountered about 0 to 5 feet of
medium stiff clayey silt with gravel fill over medium stiff to very soft and loose to very
dense alluvial deposits to about 33 0 to 58 0 feet depths These deposits were underlain
by a hard basalt formation to about 40 0 to 60 0 feet followed by an older alluvial deposit
to 66 5 feet the maximum depth drilled
In the southern portion of the site the borings encountered about 6 to 10 feet of
stiff to hard silty clay fill and medium dense to dense silty sand and gravel with boulders
fill over dense to loose silty coral sand and gravel to 16 5 feet depths
Currently it is proposed to place the building slab on the surface soils in the higher
southern portion of the site and to span the lower northern portion of the site with a
structural slab supported by spread footings
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2707 00 Page 8
For spread or continuous footing foundations bearing on the surface fill layer
estimated settlements for the anticipated column loads of 100 kips and wall loads of 3
kips per linear foot are on the order of 1 to 2 inches in the northern portion of the site and
less than one half of an inch in the southern portion of the site If practicable a lighter
type of structure should be considered to reduce the building settlements in the northern
portion of the site
If the settlements for the spread footing designs are not tolerable then pile
foundations may be considered
It is also proposed as an alternative to place the building slab across the site by
filing the lower northern portion of the site and cutting down the southern portion to
provide a level pad across the site
With the addition of 2 to 3 feet of fill in the northern portion the site estimated
settlements on the order of one 1 inch are anticipated The settlements may take place
over along period of time In order to accelerate the settlements preloading the northern
portion of the site with surcharge fill for about 3 months may be considered However
the resulting settlements may cause settlement to adjacent structures
If additional fill is placed in the northern portion of the site the construction of the
new sewer line should be delayed until sufficient settlement caused by the added load
has occurred
Our laboratory testing indicates that the stiff to hard silty clay surface fill soils in the
southern portion of the site have moderate expansion potential Therefore special
grading design will be required to reduce the heaving of the stiff to hard moderately
expansive subsoils Ideally about 12 inches of select borrow fill should be utilized

overlying the existing clayey soils at the future building slab on grade area Otherwise
overexcavation of 12 inches of the on site surface soils and replacement with select
material would be required
Site Grading
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
cleared of vegetation debris rubbish and other deleterious materials These materials
should be removed and disposed of properly off site
In the northern portion of the site with the addition of 2 to 3 feet of fill estimated
settlements on the order of about one 1 inch are anticipated These settlements may
cause excessive settlement to the adjacent warehouse structure
In the southern portion of the site due to the variable expansion potential of the
subsoils encountered near the anticipated finish subgrade level we recommend that the
areas below the building be overexcavated to a depth of at least twelve 12 inches below
the slab and foundation subgrade level and backfilled with select non expansive material
The bottoms of the overexcavation level should be scarified to a depth of six 6
inches moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture content and recompacted to
90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 Any loose or soft
spots encountered should be overexcavated down to the firm subsoils and then replaced
with compacted non expansive select material
Imported fill material to replace the overexcavated material should consist of non
expansive select granular materials less than three 3 inches in maximum size with less
than 20 passing the 200 sieve Any imported fill material should be tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to transportation to the site
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2707 00 Page 10
The replacement fill should be placed in maximum 10 inch thick loose level lifts and
compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method
D 1557 78
The site should be graded to drain water away from the foundations
Spread Footing Foundation
Spread or continuous footing foundation bearing on the surface fill layer was
evaluated for the proposed two 2 story warehouse building planned for the site
used for design in the northern and southern portion of the site respectively
For the estimated column loads of 100 kips and wall loads of 3 kips per linear foot
estimated settlements are on the order of 1 to 2 inches in the northern portion of the site
If practicable a lighter steel frame and or wood structure should be considered
in the northern portion of the site to reduce the building loads and the resulting
settlements It would be desirable to limit the maximum column load to about 10 kips to
limit the settlements to tolerable levels
Individual footings and continuous wall footings should be embedded a minimum
of 24 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade
Foundations located next to the new eight 8 inch sewer line should be embedded
below a 1H 1V imaginary plane extending upward from the bottom edge of the sewer
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2707 00 Page 11
trench or as deep as the planned invert elevations of the sewer line to reduce the
possibility of footing settlement due to insufficient compaction of the sewer trench backfill
Foundations located next to the existing retaining wall at Kalini Stream and adjacent
to the existing warehouse should be embedded below a 1H 1V imaginary plane extending
upward from the bottom edge of the retaining wall and warehouse footing
The bottom of all footing excavations should be observed by the geotechnical
engineer prior to placement of the reinforcement to determine if adequate bearing has
been obtained and that soft loose soils are removed prior to concrete placement
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive earth pressure
developed against the buried vertical faces of the foundation system and by frictional
resistance developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be calculated using an equivalent fluid
pressure of 300 p c f per foot of depth This assumes that the soil around the foundation
is well compacted A coefficient of friction of 0 4 may be used Unless covered by
pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the upper 18 inches of soil should be
neglected
Pile Foundations
If the estimated settlement presented above for spread footing design are not
tolerable then pile foundations may be considered
The piles would develop their load capacity primarily from end bearing on the
basalt and very dense alluvial layers encountered at depths of about 35 to 70 feet below
the existing ground surface Our analysis indicates that with the use of a 12 inch square
prestressed concrete pile an allowable design capacity of 50 tons may be used
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2707 00 Page 12
Pile settlements on the order of less than one half of an inch are anticipated
Pile Driving
We recommend that a pile driving hammer which can deliver a minimum of 32 000
foot pounds of energy be used for the pile driving The piles should be driven to
a resistance of about 40 blows per foot for the last foot of penetration or 10 blows
per inch
The driving process should be continuous without interruption for each pile A pile
may be rejected or its design capacity may be modified if the driving progress is
interrupted for more than four 4 hours
During easy driving when the pile tip is within soft loose soils the hammer stroke
or energy should be carefully controlled so that excessive tensile stresses are not
developed in the pile
The pile driving should be observed by a qualified geotechnical engineer to see
that the piles are driven to the sufficient depth or into competent bearing materials
Test Pile Program
A test pile program is recommended to aid in the development of a safe and
economical pile design for the proposed building
The test program should be undertaken to fulfill the following objectives
1 To more accurately determine the production pile length s
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2707 00 Page 13
2 To hep evaluate and modify if necessary the pile driving criteria
We recommend that about ten 10 percent of the total number of piles be
test driven These test piles may later be incorporated into the actual
foundations of the proposed building
Splice cans should be provided at the site during test pile driving to
expedite splicing if required Splice cans may be fabricated of high grade
steel one fourth 1 4 of an inch thick and should be at least 6 feet long
Surcharge of Building Site
Another alternative that may be considered to reduce the excessive differential
ground settlements is to preload the northern portion of the site with surcharge fill after
placement of the permanent fill to induce ground settlements prior to building
construction
The total height of the surcharge should be a minimum of about five 5 feet
For a five foot high surcharge our estimate of surcharge time would be on the
order of three 3 to four 4 months Estimated ground settlement due to the five foot
surcharge is on the order of 1 to 2 inches It should be noted that these settlements may
cause settlement to the adjacent warehouse structure
Settlement gauges should be installed in the area prior to surcharge fill placement
and periodic survey readings of the gauges should be used to monitor the actual
settlement The surveyed gauges will be utilized to verify the rate of settlements and to
help determine hen the surcharge fill can be removed

Retaining Walls
Retaining walls are anticipated for grade separation The guidelines for the
retaining wall designs are listed below
1 The following lateral earth pressures may be used
Walls unrestrained at top 40 p c f equivalent
active case fluid pressure
Walls restrained at top 55 p c f equivalent
at rest case fluid pressure
Additional load due to traffic or surcharge should be included where
applicable
An allowable bearing value of up to 3000 p s f is recommended for
foundations bearing on imported select borrow or on stiff on site soil
2 A passive earth resistance of 300 p s f per foot of depth may be
used for the wall footing on level areas The passive pressure must be
reduced for walls on or adjacent to slopes
3 A base friction factor of 0 4 may also be used to determine the
resistance of the wall against sliding
4 The wall foundations on relatively flat areas should be embedded a
minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade On sloping
areas the wall footing should be embedded an additional amount to provide
a minimum 10 foot horizontal set back distance measured from the outer
edge of the wall footing to the slope face
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2707 00 Page 15
5 The backfill behind the retaining wall should consist of non
expansive granular materials generally less than 3 inches in maximum size
with less than 20 passing the 200 sieve
6 A subsurface drainage system subdrains or weepholes should be
utilized behind the walls Subdrains should be properly constructed to drain
water away from the wall

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during construction of the foundation phases of the
work This is to observe compliance with the design concepts specifications or
recommendations and to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions
differ from those anticipated prior to the start of construction The recommendations
given in this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and approved by
the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed soil conditions encountered during construction are different from
those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to the design
should be made
