As requested we have performed a soil investigation at the
above referenced site to evaluate the subsurface soil condi
tions in order to provide recommendations for the design and
construction of the foundations for the proposed structures
Our investigation consisted of three borings and one test
pit at the locations of the proposed control building and
headwall
The results of the field exploration and laboratory tests
which were used in the engineering analysis to formulate our
conclusions and recommendations are presented in the appen
dix of this report

The project site is situated at the end of Malumalu Place
in Ahuimanu Valley at the edge of the existing subdivision
development
There is an existing dirt road extending from the end of
Malumalu Place into the site At present there is a one
story high chlorinator building along with another adjacent

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Subsurface Exploration
Three borings ranging in depth from 4 5 to 7 5 feet below
the existing ground surface were drilled in order to explore
the subsurface conditions and obtain samples for laboratory
testing
One boring was drilled at the location of the proposed drain
line headwall Two borings were drilled near the proposed
control building The depth of drilling was limited by the
many cobbles and boulders encountered therefore one test
pit was excavated with a backhoe near the control building
in order to evaluate the consistency of the soils
Geology
The project site is situated in an amphitheatre headed valley
eroded into the eastern flank of the Koolau shield volcano
The on site soils are alluvial and colluvial materials
derived from the weathering of basaltic igneous rocks and
consist of brown clayey silts with gravel cobbles and boul
ders
Soil Conditions
Based on the borings and test pit the site appears to be
underlain by alluvium generally consisting of gravel and
boulders with stiff clayey silt matrix

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration encountered stiff alluvial deposits
consisting mostly of gravel and cobbles with clayey silt
These soils could provide adequate foundation support for
the proposed structures
Foundations
For the proposed control building spread or continuous
footing foundations are recommended
An allowable bearing value of up to 3000 p s f is recom
mended for footings bearing on the stiff in situ soil
This value of bearing capacity is for a total of dead
plus live loads and may be increased by 33 when temporary
lateral loads wind and seismic are considered
An allowable bearing capacity of 2500 p s f is recommended
on properly compacted structural fill
A minimum footing width of 12 inches should be utilized
The bottom of all footings should be placed a minimum of 18
inches below the finished grade
Surface water should be diverted away from the building
foundations
Anticipated settlements are expected to be relatively small
on the order of less than half an inch

Site Grading
Relatively small cuts and fills are anticipated for the
access road construction
The site should be cleared of vegetation and debris prior
to filling The existing ground surface should then be mois
ture conditioned scarified and recompacted to obtain 90
relative density
There is an existing large mango tree in the proposed con
trol building location We anticipate that tree roots may
be quite extensive and should be removed below the building
site The resulting hole from the root excavation should
be backfilled in thin lifts with compacted on site mater
ial
Pill and backfill should be placed in 10 inch loose lifts
and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density
established by ASTM D 1557
Select on site material generally smaller than 8 inches in
size can be used for the fill
Recommendations for fill slope ratios are 2 1 H V and 145 1
H V for cut slopes

APPENDIX A
FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by
drilling 3 borings to depths of 4 5 to 7 5 feet below the
existing ground surface and one test pit to a 10 foot depth
at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
The soil encountered in the borings and test pit were
classified by visual and textural examination in the field
by our engineer who continuously monitored the drilling and
excavation operations The classifications were reviewed
by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory The soils
were classified in general accordance with the Unified Clas
sification System A graphic presentation of the soils is
presented on the Boring and Test Pit logs Plates A 1 through
A 4
In the borings soil samples were obtained by driving a
Standard penetration sampler with a 140 pound hammer free
falling a distance of 30 inches The blow counts to drive
the sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the logs of bor
ings at the appropriate sample depths
Bulk surface soil samples were obtained along the pro
posed road using pick and shovel The approximate locations
are shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
