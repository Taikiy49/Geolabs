SITE GRADING
The project site was graded for the development of an 18 hole golf course and
associated infrastructure installation The grading entailed the removal of deleterious
surface materials clearing and grubbing excavation of an effluent retention pond and
golf course lakes and cuts and fills of up to 10 feet in thickness The mass grading
work was performed by Oahu Construction Company Ltd A general layout of the
project site is presented on the Site Plan Plate 1
Based on the observation of our field representatives and tests conducted it is
our opinion that the grading was performed in general accordance with the intent of the
recommendations presented in the above referenced Geotechnical Engineering Report
dated Detailed observations and discussions are presented in the
following sections of this letter report

Soft Ground Stabilization
Subsequent to clearing and grubbing soft ground areas encountered in the
vicinity of Fairway 11 and 14 were stabilized by the placement of a
geotextile fabric and a minimum of 18 inches of No 3B Fine gravel Import structural
fills were then placed in controlled lift heights and compacted with a sheepsfoot roller
Sinkhole at Fairway No 18 Clubhouse
A sinkhole approximately 35 feet wide 40 feet long and 5 feet deep was
encountered in the vicinity between the clubhouse and Fairway No 18 during the mass
grading operations A total of 24 probe holes were drilled in selected locations to
delineate the vertical and lateral extent of the sinkhole encountered The delineated
sinkhole was later exposed by collapsing the roof of the sinkhole removing the
collapsed soils and backfilling with large boulders from the bottom of the excavation
The boulder fill was then grouted with concrete to create a relatively level reinforced
concrete pad at the top prior to capping with approximately 4 feet of compacted borrow
soils
Bridge Golf Cart Crossing Foundations
Soft soils were encountered at the footing subgrades of some of the golf cart
crossings The soft soils were over excavated to a depth of approximately 3 feet and
backfilled with about 12 inches of surge rocks A layer of geogrid Tensar UX1400
underlain by a layer of geotextile fabric Mirafi 140N were then placed on top of the
surge rock prior to backfilling with 4 inch minus rocks in compacted lifts to the footing
subgrade elevations
Fill Placement
During the mass grading operations selected samples of the imported soils
utilized as fill materials were obtained and returned to our laboratory for testing The
maximum dry density and optimum moisture content were determined in our laboratory
in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Results of our laboratory
tests are presented on Plate 2
Observation and testing of the earthwork in the field were performed by
representatives from our office to observe that the grading was performed in general
conformance with the intent of the Geotechnical Engineering Report and specifications
pertaining to the geotechnical aspects of the development In place density tests of the
fills were performed periodically in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation
D 1556 Sand Cone and D 2922 Nuclear Method Areas that did not meet the
compaction requirement were reworked by the contractor and approved by our field
