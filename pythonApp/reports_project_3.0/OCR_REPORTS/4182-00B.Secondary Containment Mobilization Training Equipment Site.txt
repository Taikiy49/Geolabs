for the proposed Secondary Containment for the Mobilization Training Equipment Site
MATES located in the District of Waiawa on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The site is
located within the Pearl City Industrial Center The general location and vicinity of the project
site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from two shield
volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be middle to
late Pliocene in age and forms the bulk of the western third of the island The younger
shield Koolau is estimated to be late Pliocene to early Pleistocene Ice Age and forms the
majority of the eastern two thirds of the island Waianae became extinct while Koolau was
GEOLABS INC
W O 4182 00 B Page 4
still active and its eastern flank was partially buried below Koolau lavas banking against its
eastern flank These banked or ponded lavas formed a broad plateau referred to as the
Schofield Plateau
The Schofield Plateau was formed when lavas from the Koolau Volcano ponded
against the already eroded slopes of the Waianae Voicano in the late Pleistocene Epoch
The lava flows on the plateau have undergone in situ weathering to depths of 50 to 100 feet
and are characterized by the red colors of the soil The deep narrow canyons such as
Waikakalaua Kipapa and Kaukonahua are the result of this in situ weathering
This in situ chemical weathering of the lava has formed a mantle of residual soils that
grade to saprolite with depth In general saprolite is composed mainly of silty material and
is typical of the tropical weathering of volcanic rocks Spheroidally weathered boulders are
typical in the Schofield Plateau The saprolite grades to basaltic rock formation with depth

Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed Vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable
materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site or stockpiled in a
designated area to reduce the potential for contamination of the excavated materials
Existing utilities to be abandoned should be removed and the resulting excavation
should be properly backfilled with fill material placed in 6 to 8 inch level loose lifts and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction However the upper
2 feet from finish subgrade of material should be compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative compaction Utilities to be abandoned in place under the
proposed slab should be backfilled by pumping lean concrete under low pressure
Soft and yielding areas encountered should be over excavated to expose firm natural
material and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with fill placed in 12 inch
maximum loose lifts moisture conditioned to at least the optimum moisture content
and compacted to at least 95 percent relative compaction ASTM D 1557 91 The
excavated soft soils should be disposed of properly off site and or used in landscape
areas where appropriate
Finished subgrades and areas designated to receive fills should be scarified to a
depth of 10 inches and moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture and
compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Relative compaction
refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum
dry density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM Test Designation
GEOLABS INC
W O 4182 00 B Page 7
D 1557 91 Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight
corresponding to the maximum dry density
Where shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of the subgrade we recommend
that the soil be thoroughly moistened to close all cracks or prepared again as
recommended above Saturation and subsequent yielding of the exposed subgrade
due to inclement weather and poor drainage may require over excavation of the soft
areas and replacement with fill placed in 12 inch maximum loose lifts
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to at
least 95 percent relative compaction The excavated soft soils should be disposed of
properly off site and or used in landscape areas where appropriate
Fill Material
Fills should consist of well graded low expansive materials less than 3 inches in
largest dimension The fill material should also be free of vegetation and deleterious
materials In general the on site clay material should be suitable for fill material
Imported fill should consist of non expansive select granular material such as crushed
coral mudrock basalt or cinder sand The material should be free of vegetation and
deleterious materials The material should be well graded from coarse to fine with no
particles larger than 3 inches in largest dimension and should contain between 10 and
30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve The material should have a laboratory
CBR value of 20 or more and should have a maximum swell of 1 percent or less when
tested in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1883 Imported fill materials
should be tested by Geolabs Inc for conformance with these recommendations prior
to delivery to the project site for its intended use
Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
Fills backfills and base course should be moisture conditioned to about 2 percent
above the optimum moisture placed in level lifts not exceeding 12 inches in loose
thickness and compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction
GEOLABS INC
W O 4182 00 B Page 8
Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a
percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance
with ASTM D 1557 91 test procedures Optimum moisture is the water content
percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
Because moisture conditioning and compaction of soils are critical elements of
earthwork observations and soil density tests should be performed by Geolabs Inc
during site grading to assist the Contractor in obtaining the required degree of
compaction and the proper moisture content Where compaction is less than
required additional compactive effort should be applied with adjustment of moisture
content as necessary to obtain the specified compaction It should be noted that the
moisture requirement of the subgrades about 2 percent above the optimum moisture
is an important requirement for the on site clay soils
Cut and Fill Slopes
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered in the borings we believe that cut
and fill slopes composed of the on site materials may be made at an inclination of
2H 1V or flatter
Fills placed on slopes steeper than 5H 1V which is the situation with the existing
2H 1V slope should be keyed and benched into the existing slope to provide stability
of the new fill against sliding The filling operations should start at the lowest point
and continue up in level horizontal compacted layers in accordance with the above
fill placement recommendations
Fill slopes should be constructed by overfilling and cutting back to the design slope
ratio to obtain a well compacted slope face Track rolling of slopes should not be
acceptable In the event that over cutting of a slope occurs keying and benching
requirements should be implemented instead of backfilling the slope to the design
grade with sliver fills Water should be diverted away from the tops of slopes and
GEOLABS INC

slope planting should be provided as soon as possible to reduce the potential for
erosion of the finished slopes
MSE Retaining Wall Design
Based on the generally competent subsoil conditions encountered at the site we
believe that shallow footings may be used for support of the proposed MSE retaining wall
An allowable bearing pressure of may be utilized for the design of the MSE
retaining wall bearing on 4 inches of base leveling material This bearing value is for dead
plus live loads and may be increased by one third 1 3 for transient loads such as those
caused by wind or seismic forces Footings should be embedded a minimum of 8 inches
below the lowest adjacent grade This roughly corresponds to one course of MSE blocks
Footings should be embedded deep enough to provide a minimum horizontal setback
distance of 8 feet measured from the outside edge of the footing to the slope face Footings
parallel to the direction of the slope should be constructed in stepped footings
If soft and or loose materials are encountered at the footing bottom below the backfill
or below the base leveling material they should be over excavated to expose the underlying
firm materials The over excavation should be backfilled with select granular material
compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction or the footing bottom may be
deepened to the underlying firm materials
The passive pressure should be neglected in the design because of the shallow
embedment In addition the following parameters should also be used in the MSE retaining
wall design
e Soil friction angle 27 degrees
e Wall friction value 2 3 of the soil friction angle
e Sliding coefficient on base leveling material 0 5

e Maximum wall height without geogrid reinforcement 4 feet for level backfill

The base leveling material shouid consist of select granular fill material as described
in the Site Grading section of this report The base leveling material should be
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum
of 90 percent relative compaction ASTM D 1557 91
The unit fill material within the MSE units should consist of free draining material with
less than 5 passing the No 200 sieve as the unit fill material serves as the drainage
system for the MSE retaining wall No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation may
be used as unit fill material
The backfill material behind the MSE retaining wall and between the geogrids should
consist of fill material as described in the Site Grading section of this report In general the

The upper 12 inches of backfill should consist of relatively impervious material such
as the on site clay material to reduce water infiltration behind the walls Surface drainage
should shed water away from the wall and not permit ponding
To avoid surcharging the MSE retaining wall the edge of the parking lot should be set

We recommend that footing excavations be observed by a representative of

Slabs On Grade
Our field exploration disclosed that the project site is generally underlain by
near surface clay with low swelling potential Previous experience in the facility indicates the
clay material to have an air dried ring swell value of 1 0 to 3 0 These ring swell samples
GEOLABS INC
W O 4182 00 B Page 11
were compacted at a moisture content of about 35 to 36 and to a dry density of about
70 to 82 pcf The samples were air dried to a moisture content of about 24 to 25 and
saturated to a moisture content of about 41 to 52
However to reduce the potential for structural distress resulting from the shrinking
and swelling of the subgrade soils we recommend that the subgrade soils be kept at or
above the optimum moisture content prior to final placement of concrete In addition the
area adjacent to the slabs should be backfilled tightly against the slab and graded to divert
water away from the slab to reduce the potential of water ponding around the slab
For the design of structural slabs a modulus of subgrade reaction of 200 pounds per
square inch per inch of deflection pci may be used for the compacted subgrade material
Based on the anticipated vehicle loads and the soil conditions we recommend a minimum
slab thickness of 10 5 inches Provisions should be made for proper load transfer across the
slab joints which are subjected to vehicular traffic The thickened edges of slabs adjacent
to any unpaved area should be embedded at least 12 inches below the lowest adjacent
grade
Subgrade soils should be prepared in accordance with the recommendations
presented in the Site Preparation section of this report

Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling
two borings designated as Boring Nos 1 and 2 to depths of about 6 5 feet below the
existing ground elevation at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The
borings were drilled using a truck mounted drill rig equipped with auger equipment
