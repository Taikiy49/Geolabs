Evaluation of Slope Failure
Lot 49 Ahuimanu Hills
Phase III
Kaneohe Oahu Hawaii
At your request we have performed a preliminary evaluation of the
slope failure at the rear of the above referenced lot
Our study consisted of drilling one boring at the top of slope next
to the slip area and 2 hand auger probes along the existing steep slope
which extends down to the stream
The boring and probings encountered medium stiff to stiff brown
clayey silt typical of the soils in the area It appears that the slip
may have been caused by loose cast over fill placed prior to the
subdivision construction or the build up of water pressure behind the
slope or both
W O 797 10 Page 2
For remedial work a reconstructed slope at a slope ratio of 2 1
H V may be used Guidelines for the slope reconstruction are as
follows
The site to be graded should be cleared of all vegetation soft
and other unsuitable materials
After the clearing operation any remaining loose overcast
material should be excavated
The on site material from the general subdivision area free
of any expansive or organic components may be reused as a
source of fill material
Fills placed on the existing slope should be keyed and benched
into the firm ground to properly bond the new fill to the
slope For grading details see Plate 1 Typical bench width
and height of 6 and 4 feet respectively should be followed
whenever appropriate Also a minimum key of 2 feet should be
used at the toe of the slope

4 The filling operation should start at the lowest point and

It appears that there may not be sufficient horizontal space to
reconstruct the entire slope at 2 1 H V and therefore a retaining
wall may be required It appears that the most efficient location for
the wall may be at the top of slope since this area is more accessible
for equipment Additionally granular material may be used for the wall
backfill thereby providing a drainage system for potential water
seepage Guidelines for the wall design are as follows
1 We recommend that the wall should bear on the properly
compacted fill An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f may
be used
2 We recommend that the wall be designed to resist a lateral
earth pressure of 40 pcf equivalent fluid pressure level
backfill
3 A friction factor of 0 35 between the concrete footing and soil
may be utilized to determine the sliding resistance of the
wall
W Page 4
4 A passive soil resistance of 300 p c f may be used where the
finished ground surface in front of the toe of the wall is
level for 8 feet minimum The passive soil resistance should
be ignored where the ground surface in front of the wall is 2 1
H V The footing embedment should be sufficiently deep to
provide a minimum of 8 feet horizontal set back distance which
is measured from the outer edge of the footing out toward the
slope face
5 A subsurface drainage system should be used to prevent the
build up of hydrostatic pressure behind the walls To minimize
damage to the newly constructed retaining wall due to excessive
compaction equipment vibration and to provide drainage we
recommend that gravel aggregate such as 3B Fine be used as
the backfill material up to about 2 feet below the finish
grade Impervious in situ clayey soil should be used for the
remaining portion of the upper wall backfill
6 Since the retaining wall will be placed within a fill slope
the fill slope shall be constructed by over filling beyond the
design slope face to assure adequate compaction prior to wall
construction
