INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our foundation investigation
for the proposed Kalakaua Gateway project to be situated near the
southeastern corner of Kalakaua Avenue and South King Street Honolulu
Oahu Hawaii The general location of the site is shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1
W O 1242 00 Page 2
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is our understanding that the proposed Kalakaua Gateway project
will consist of a 13 story office condominium building with a full
basement There will also be a 7 level parking structure Under part
of the 13 story tower will be parking garages that will also include 2
levels of basement parking A general layout of the structures is
presented on the Site Plan Plate 2
The basement level under the tower will be about 11 feet and the
lowest basement parking level will be about 17 feet below the existing
South King Street sidewalk level The structural system of the tower
will generally include heavily loaded perimeter columns and a heavy
central elevator core The maximum column loads are anticipated to
range up to 1930 kips per column under dead and live loads
The project will also include access bridges one on the south
side and one near King Street that crosses the existing Makiki Stream
channel
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this foundation investigation is to explore the
subsurface soil conditions at the site and to provide design recommen
dations for the foundations of the proposed high rise and parking
structures
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1242 00 Page 3
For this investigation three borings were drilled to depths
ranging from 50 to 150 feet totalling 300 linear feet Subsoil
samples obtained were subjected to a series of laboratory soil
testing Engineering analyses were made and our recommendations and
conclusions were developed based on these field and laboratory
findings
The approximate boring locations are shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2 and the boring logs are presented in Appendix A Detailed
information regarding the field exploration and laboratory testing are
also presented in the appendices
The scope of our work was performed in general accordance with
our proposal dated March 28 1983 This report summarizes our
findings and recommendations
SITE CONDITIONS
Surface Conditions
The project site consists of several smaller lots which were
consolidated into a large plot The existing structures include a
two story CMU masonry building a one story wooden structure a one
story fast food restaurant with kitchen and dining areas surrounded by
asphalt concrete paved parking areas and drive through lanes
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1242 00 Page 4
In general the site slopes very slightly from the northern side
along King Street toward the south The grade of the site varies from
about elevation 17 to 14 feet
Geology
The geology of the site region is the result of a complex series
of erosional and depositional events brought on by glacio eustatic sea
level fluctuations and post erosional volcanic activity during the
Pleistocene Subsequent to the cessation of activity of the Koolau
Volcano extensive accummulation of alluvium and interbedded coral
reefs and associated deposits formed a coastal plain on the eroded
southern flanks of the volcano
During periods of low sea levels streams cut deep channels into
the Pleistocene coastal plain These channels were then infilled with
sediments and cut again by subsequent cycles of rise and fall of the
S a
Subsurface Conditions
Our field exploration disclosed that the site is located on a
buried Pleistocene alluvial channel As a result interbedded
alluvial gravel sand silt and clay sediments underlie the site
usually occurring as thin layers and localized lenses The complex
vertical and lateral changes make accurate interpretation of borehole
data very difficult
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1242 00 Page 5
In general a layer of tan to brown stiff to hard sandy to
clayey silt underlies the top 10 to 20 feet of the site This
material appears to be thickest 20 feet at the southern and the
northeastern corner as revealed in our Borings 3 and 2 respectively
This material grades to medium stiff at about 15 feet deep It is
probable that localized patches of thin Silty sand and gravel fill
occur at the site surface under the paved and unpaved areas
From 20 to 45 feet below the ground surface the alluvial
sediments consist mostly of reddish brown loose to medium dense silty
fine to coarse cinder sand with horizontal lenses of gravelly medium
dense coarse cinder sand and soft to medium stiff sandy to clayey
silt The sands also frequently contain coral and basalt particles
Towards the downstream or the southern end of the site as revealed by
Boring 3 this mostly silty sand horizon gradually thins out and
grades to predominantly clayey to sandy silt
From about 45 to 80 feet below the existing ground surface the
sediments consist largely of brown medium stiff sandy to clayey silt
containing 1 to 5 foot thick localized lenses of medium dense Silty
and gravelly cinder sands At about 65 to 80 foot depth the silty
alluvium becones stiffer and is underlain by a 2 to 10 foot layer of
light brown medium dense silty fine to coarse coral and cinder sand
with coral gravels
GEOLABS HAWAII
A brown very stiff to hard clayey silt horizon was encountered
by all the deeper holes at about 80 to 90 foot depth This clayey
silt alluvium contains up to about 10 of rounded weathered basaltic
gravel On the eastern side of the Site as revealed by Boring DM 2
drilled by others this 80 to 90 foot depth clayey silt horizon
appears to extend down to a depth of about 140 feet containing
pockets of fine to medium sands
A white to tan medium dense to dense silty coralline sand and
gravel underlie the clayey silt to a depth of 167 feet where Boring
DM 2 was terminated
Along the western side of the site parallel to the existing
Makiki Stream channel the subsoil at depths between 90 to 150 feet
comprises mostly of light brown dense silty fine to coarse sand with
localized lenses and pockets of cemented sand coralline gravels and
stiff sandy to clayey silt
Groundwater
Groundwater levels were monitored throughout our field explora
tion The observed groundwater levels are presented at the appropriate
log of borings In general the groundwater level appears to fluctuate
somewhat at about Elevation 1 5 to 2 0 feet The fluctuations in the
water level readings may have been affected by the wash boring drilling
operations

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
As discussed previously the project site is located over a
buried ancient stream channel which incised deeply into the
predominantly coral reef coastal plain of southern Oahu The botton
of this stream deposit is at least 150 feet below the existing grade
the maximum depth explored Generally the subsurface soil profile
correlates well with the previous borings drilled by others
Our preliminary settlement estimates for a mat foundation
indicate relatively significant settlements on the order of 6 or 8
inches under a uniformed distributed load under the tower structure
In view of the variable soil profile and the relatively concentrated
column loads around the perimeter and heavy elevator core areas within
the structure settlements will also be uneven Therefore we
recommend that pile foundation be used to support the tower and the
parking structures
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1242 00 Page 8
Pile Foundations
Although no suitable end bearing stratum was identified in the
borings within a shallow depth we believe that friction piles may
be used for the foundation support of the tower and the parking
structures These friction piles will be relatively long therefore
inch octagonal prestressed precast concrete piles are recom
mended We believe that the most practical combination of pile length
and pile capacity would be to drive the piles to about 80 feet of
embedment below the pile cap to develop a design capacity of 100 tons
per pile For piles extending at least 50 feet below the pile cap an
uplift capacity of 20 tons per pile may be considered
In general the piles should be driven by a hammer delivering
not less than lbs of energy per blow If hard driving
conditions are encountered under such a hammer the piles may be
terminated if the penetration resistance is more than 10 blows per
inch provided that the piles have penetrated a depth of at least 60
feet below the existing grade or below Elevation 45 These preli
minary driving criteria are given only as a guide and should be
modified according to the actual pile hammer used and the actual
field conditions encountered
The piles should be spaced no closer than 4 feet on centers

Test Pile Program
ne ee a om ee cae a
It is anticipated that the pile lengths may vary throughout the
Site Therefore the estimated cast lengths for the production
pile should be developed following a test pile program The test
pile program should include the following
1 Test Driving A test driving should consist of driving at
least 6 test piles within the building and 5 test piles under
the garage areas prior to construction Four of the test
piles should be driven as close to the boring locations as
possible The purpose of the test pile driving would be to
determine more accurately the subsequent order lengths for
production piles and to evaluate the final production pile
driving criteria
The test piles should be driven with the same hammer to be
used in the production pile installation The test piles
should be a minimum of 100 feet long based on the assumption
that the mass excavation for basement is made The actual
driving criteria for the production piles should be based on
the results of the test pile driving and load test program
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1242 00 Page 10
2 Load Testing At least two test piles should be selected by
the soils engineer for load testing The piles to be load
tested should be selected following the evaluation of the
test driving The pile load testing should be performed in
accordance with ASTM D 1143 81 test method to an ultimate
test load of 200 tons We recommend that the total weight of
the reaction load for the load testing be a minimum of 230
tons
Settlement
We believe that the settlement of the building supported on deep
piles as recommended above would be less than one inch and the
differential settlement would be less than one half inch
Predrilling
It is anticipated that the basement will be excavated to the Slab
subgrade or perhaps 2 feet above the prevalent groundwater level
before pile driving Although pile driving will generate some
amount of ground vibration we believe that the majority of the
piles may be driven without predrilling provided that the ground
is excavated to at least Elevation 5 feet or lower Otherwise
predrilling will be required particularly at pile locations
within 15 feet of the existing adjacent structures to remain
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1242 00 Page 11
If required predrilling should consist of drilling 18 inch
diameter holes for the inch octagonal concrete piles and
drilled to a depth of Elevation 0 foot or the bottom of the pile
cap whichever is the highest Overdrilling is not recommended
Lateral Pile Capacities
el ee ee
Generally The vertical piles will provide some lateral
resistance to horizontal loads Because the top of the piles
will mostly be in loose to medium dense silty sand the allowable
lateral capacities should be limited to 5 kips per pile
Lateral Earth Pressures
ee
To resist the lateral loads the passive earth pressure around
the pile caps and tie beams may be used We recommend using
equivalent fluid pressure of 250 pounds per square foot per foot
of depth The backfill against the pile caps or tie beams should
be properly backfilled with select borrow materials compacted to
a dry density of at least 95 of the maximum density as
determined by ASTM D 1557 78 test method
If any of the pile caps extend below the water level the
excavation should be over excavated to one foot below the bottom
elevation of the pile cap and backfilled with a clean gravel
such as No 3 Fine crushed rock Clean gravel should also be used
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1242 00 Page 12
as backfill below the water table The purpose of clean gravel
is to facilitate dewatering during construction The on site
brown sandy silt or silty sand may be used as backfill above the
water level where the backfill material should be compacted to a
minimum of 95 of its maximum dry density
Mat Foundation
Assuming that the building loads would be distributed uniformly
under the mat the calculated total settlements could range from 6 to
8 inches under the tower structure Those under the garage structure
will be smaller perhaps as little as 2 inches or so
However because of relatively high concentrated column loads
settlements will be locally greater under the columns and thick mat
foundations may be required In addition the soil conditions under
the building generally are not uniform therefore there will be large
differential settlements
In view of the above mat foundation is not recommended
Retaining Walls and Basement Walls
For the design of basement walls we recommend that an active
earth pressure of 55 p s f per foot of depth be used This assumes
that the top of the walls are restrained by floor slabs
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Page 13
For free standing retaining walls the active earth pressures may
be reduced to 40 p s f per foot of depth The backfill behind the
retaining wall should consist of permeable material such as No 3
Fine crushed rock
A hydrostatic pressure should be included for high groundwater
level conditions The selection of a design high groundwater level
should be made after an evaluation was made on the impact of the
flooding of the adjacent Makiki Stream channel during heavy rain
Pile Foundations under Retaining Walls and Basement Walls
It is also recommended that pile foundation be used to support
the perimeter basement walls It is likely that the pile capacities
required under the retaining wall foundations would not be as high as
100 tons We recommend that the friction piles be embedded at least
50 feet below the pile cap in order to provide a design capacity of 40
tons per pile
For these shorter piles 12 inch square prestressed concrete
piles may be used Any predrilling if required should not extend
below the design pile cap bottom elevations This limitation is to
provide full soil to pile contact to realize good lateral pile
capacity as well as good embedment for vertical support The lateral
pile capacity should be limited to 3 5 kips per pile For 12 inch
Square piles the hammer energy should be limited to not more than
Ibs per blow The piles should be spaced at least 3 feet
on centers
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1242 00 Page 14
Because of limited lateral capacity of these piles passive
soil resistance on pile caps and foundation keys should also be
considered Passive soil pressures of 250 p s f per foot of depth
may be used where soils are confined by slabs
The pile installation criteria consist primarily of achieving
sufficient embedment of at least 50 feet for a 40 ton pile Asa
preliminary guide the pile may be Stopped at this embedment length if
the penetration resistance is at least 5 blows per foot or more
However the driving criteria should be evaluated and modified after a
test pile program The test pile program should consist of driving 7
to 9 piles along the perimeter of the property We believe that load
testing may be waived for 40 ton capacity piles
Batter piles may be considered if lateral forces are too great to
be handled by lateral pile capacities and passive earth pressure on
the pile caps Generally a batter of upto 3 on 1 vertical to
horizontal is recommended The design axial capacity the minimum
embedment and the installation criteria should be similar to the
40 ton piles discussed above

Basement Floor Slabs
A portion of the basement floor slab in the garage may be
situated below the groundwater level We recommend that clean gravel
such as No 3 Fine crushed rock be placed at least 8 inches below the
slab and then compacted Thus it will be necessary to lower the
groundwater sufficiently to allow over excavation for the clean gravel
cushion construction The clean gravel should be compacted to 90 of
the ASTM D 1557 78 maximum density
Where the basement floor slabs are located above water level we
recommend that a minimum of 6 inches of base course under the garage
floor slab and a minimum of 4 inches of base course under the other
areas be placed and then compacted to 95 of minimum density
The basement floor slab should be designed to foreseeable high
groundwater level Although the groundwater level observed during our
boring varied from about Elevation 1 5 to 2 0 feet a higher ground
water level should be anticipated Since the site is adjacent to
Makiki Stream channel potential effect to groundwater during flooding
should be evaluated in detail This aspect is beyond the scope of
services at this time It is recommended that this should be
evaluated
GEOLABS HAWAII
Although standpipe may be used to reduce the temporary high
water conditions their spacing as well as effectiveness should be
evaluated Perimeter sheetpiles may be installed to act as cutoff
walls to reduce the rise of the piezometric head of groundwater under
the basement slabs The effectiveness of these measures should be
studied further
Earthwork
Excavations up to about 16 feet deep are planned for the
proposed basement construction Shoring the excavation for basement
or pile cap excavation will depend on the type of bracing and shoring
system used by the contractor Therefore no specific design recom
mendations is provided herein
The contractor should be responsible for developing the necessary
shoring and dewatering system to protect the adjacent structures and
the sidewalks from collapsing loss of fines and or settlement In
particular ground settlement may develop in adjacent areas where the
ground water level is lowered by dewatering
Design Review
Plans for foundations site grading basement and retaining walls
and utility constructions should be forwarded to the soils engineer
for review and written comments prior to construction to see that the
intent of our recommendations is included in the project plans and
specifications

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the pile test program and the production
pile driving operations be monitored on a full time basis by a
qualified soils technician under the direction of a soils engineer
to verify that the piles have been driven in accordance with the
recommendations and to modifiy our recommendations should the soil
conditions encountered during pile driving are different from our
field exploration
Pile load tests to verify the Capacity of the piles should be
performed in accordance with the City County s requirements or ASTM
Test Designation D 1443 81 The test setup and loading of the pile
should be made the responsibility of the contractor The readings and
test reports for pile load test should be prepared by a registered
soils engineer

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored
by drilling 3 borings at the approximate locations shown
on the Site Plan Plate 2 A truck mounted drilling rig
was used to drill the borings
The soils encountered in the borings were classi
fied by visual and textural examination in the field by
our engineer who continuously monitored the drilling
operations The classifications were further reviewed by
visual inspection and testing in the laboratory The soils
were classified in general conformance with the Unified
Soil Classification System A graphic presentation of the
materials encountered is presented on the Boring Logs
In the borings soil samples were obtained by
driving a 2 inch standard split spoon sampler or a 2 4 inch
I D split barrel sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling
30 inches In addition a inch 0 D Denison sampler
was used The blow counts used to drive the samplers the
last 12 inches are shown on the log of borings at the
appropriate sample depths

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
me
Moisture content and unit weight determinations were
performed on selected samples as an aid in the classification
and evaluation of soil properties The results of these tests
are presented on the boring logs at the appropriate sample
depths
To further identify soil characteristics additional
tests were performed These include 5 Atterberg Limit tests
and 1 Sieve Analysis The results of these tests are presented
on Plates B 1 through B 2
Eight direct shear tests were performed on undisturbed
samples to evaluate the strength characteristics of the
subsurface materials The test results are presented on Plates
B 3 through B 10
Fourteen Unconfined Compression Tests were performed on
slightly disturbed and undisturbed samples to further evaluate
the soil strength characteristics The peak compressive strength
values for unconfined compression tests are summarized on Plate
B ll
Six Unconsolidated Undrained Triaxial Compression Tests
were performed on select undisturbed samples to evaluate the soil
strength characteristics of the soil Five triaxial compression
tests were performed using a multi stage procedure in which the
next higher confining pressure was applied after the sample
approached the peak compressive strength under the on going
confining pressure The test results are summarized on Plate
B 12
Seven Consolidation tests were performed on selected
undisturbed samples to evaluate the settlement characteristics of
the alluvial sediments under the site The test results are
presented on Plates B 13 through B 19
