Soil Engineering Investigation of
Distresses to Pool and Decking
4838 Sierra Drive
Honolulu Oahu Hawaii
This report presents the results of our soil engineering
investigation for the observed distresses to the swimming pool and
deck located at 4838 Sierra Drive Honolulu Oahu Hawaii The
general location of the site and its vicinity is shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this investigation was to explore the existing
soil conditions at the site to provide data for evaluating the
possible cause or causes of the observed distresses and to develop
recommendations for possible remedial measures for the distressed
structures In addition this investigation was performed to evaluate
the overall stability of the residential structure adjacent to the
distressed pool and deck
W O 1273 00 Page 2
The scope of our investigation was performed in general
accordance with our proposal dated and included visual
observations at the site the drilling of two exploratory borings and
two hand auger probes laboratory testing engineering analysis and
report preparation
The results of our field investigation and laboratory tests which
were used to formulate our conclusion and recommendations are
presented in the appendices of this report
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located in the Maunalani Heights area of
Honolulu on Sierra Drive to the approximate east of Maunalani
Hospital The site is roughly square in plan view and is bounded by
Sierra Drive to the east Maunalani Hospital to the west the hospital
driveway to the south and a neighboring residence to the north
Along the south property line there is a high concrete retaining
wall and along the east property line is a low stone retaining wall
The north and west boundaries of the lot are marked by low cut slopes
Situated on the site is a single story masonry residential
structure with a semi basement garage To the south of the
residential structure is a swimming pool with a concrete deck patio
area

OBSERVED DISTRESSES
The major distresses observed at the site were settlement and
cracking of the swimming pool deck adjacent sidewalks and masonry
walls Some minor hairline cracking was noted in the swimming pool
itself
Additionally some cracking of the masonry wall supporting the
wooden deck facing Sierra Drive was noted
No significant distresses were noted on the existing masonry
hollow tile residential structure However the concrete floor
slab on grade could not be visually examined due to the wall to wall
carpeting

Soil Conditions
In Boring 1 our exploration encountered 10 feet of man made fill
underlying natural soil materials The upper five feet of this fill
material consisted of very loose silty sand and the lower five feet
consisted of very stiff silty clay The underlying natural soil was a
hard clayey silt with some boulders derived from the in situ
weathering of basaltic bedrock
In Boring 2 our exploration encountered man made fill to the
maximum depth explored of 16 5 feet This fill consisted of alternate
layers of silty clay and clayey silt which is believed to be the
backfill of the large retaining wall along the neighboring Maunalani
Hospital driveway The upper 4 to 5 feet of fill may have been added
when the pool and decking was constructed and appears to be in a loose
state The lower portion of the fill appear to be well compacted and
range in consistency from very stiff to hard
Both hand auger probes encountered fill materials consisting of
stiff clayey silts and silty clays
Groundwater was not noted in any of the borings or probes at the
time of our field exploration

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field explorations revealed that the upper 4 to 5 feet of
fill material below the pool decking is probably in a loose or poorly
compacted state In addition laboratory tests indicated that the
materials encountered at depths which coincide with the swimming pool
subgrade are moderately to critically expansive
The combination of these two factors could result in settlement
of the pool decking and heaving of the swimming pool itself which
would cause distress consistent with those observed at the subject
site Specifically the edges of the decking slab and the adjacent
patio sidewalk appear to have settled and cracked whereas the
perimeter wall of the swimming pool is elevated slightly one eighth
to one fourth of an inch above the decking
Based on the results of our field explorations and laboratory
tests we feel there are two possible alternatives for remedial
measures for the pool and decking Both of these alternatives involve
a calculated risk since it is not known whether the structural
integrity of the swimming pool is sufficient to prevent water from
leaking to the expansive subgrade which would cause heaving of the
pool and could possibly cause distresses to the hospital driveway
retaining wall
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1273 00 Page 6
One remedial alternative would be to remove the concrete pool
decking and to replace it with a post and beam supported wooden
decking The foundations for this wooden decking should bear on the
very stiff soils encountered at about 5 feet below the existing deck
surf ace
A second more costly alternative would be to remove the
existing concrete decking and the underlying poorly compacted fill to
very stiff material The resulting excavation should then be
backfilled with select non expansive impermeable material compacted
to 90 of its maximum dry density as established by Test Method ASTM
D 1557 78 Following the placement of this backfill a new concrete
decking consisting of checker board sections could be constructed A
4 inch minimum concrete slab over 6 inches of compacted base course
rock are recommended
Since both of the above alternatives rely on the integrity of the
pool we recommend that a structural engineer be retained to examine
the swimming pool and determine if the pool is water tight or
recommend measures to make the pool water tight

With regards to the residential structure our field observations
indicate that the house bears mainly on cut into natural ground The
natural soils in the vicinity of the site are not known to be
susceptible to sliding or other forms of movement In view of the
fact and the lack of observed distress to the residential structure
it is our opinion that the house appear to be stable at this time

drilling 2 borings to depths of 15 8 and 16 5 feet below the

drilling equipment Additionally two shallow probes to the

the Site Plan Plate 2

visual and textural examination in the field by our engineer
who continuously monitored the drilling operations The classi
fications were further reviewed by visual inspection and testing
in the laboratory The soils were classified in general confor
mance with the Unified Soil Classification System A graphic
presentation of the materials encountered is presented on the

with a 140 pound hammer free falling 30 inches The blow
counts to drive the sampler the 12 inches are shown on the log
