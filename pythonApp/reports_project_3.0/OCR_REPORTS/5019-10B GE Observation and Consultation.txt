Regional Geology
The Island of Hawaii the largest island in the Hawaiian Archipelago covers an
area of approximately 4 000 square miles The island was formed by the activity of
five shield volcanoes which include the following Kohala which is long extinct Mauna
Kea which has had activity during recent geologic time Hualalai which last erupted in
1801 and Mauna Loa and Kilauea both of which are still active
The project site is situated on the western flank of Kohala Mountain which has
formed the northern portion of the island The project site is underlain by basaltic lava
flows of the Hawi Volcanic Series of Kohala Mountain which were deposited during the
Pleistocene Epoch Stearns and Macdonald 1946 The Hawi Volcanic Series is typically
composed of basaltic lava flows that are overlain by a relatively thin layer of palagonitized
ash locally referred to as Pahala Ash The volcanic ash soil at the project site differs
from Pahala Ash found in other portions of the Island of Hawaii in that the in situ moisture
content is generally lower and the soil exists in a dry friable state The soils at the project
site have been subjected to wind erosion that has resulted in the formation of a desert
pavement on the surface of the site This desert pavement is a surface layer of cobbles
and gravel which were left behind as the finer ash was blown away
The lava formation at the project site appears to be of a a and pahoehoe basalt
type flows which spread and ponded as they approached the ocean A a lava is typically
characterized by a porous rough and irregular flow surface resembling a jagged
accumulation of rock fragments including cobbles and boulders Typically a more dense
and layered lava rock material is contained within the lava flow core Pahoehoe lava is
characterized by a smooth rope like or billowy surface and an internal structure of
vesicular porous rock Cavities are commonly encountered in pahoehoe lavas These
cavities were formed when the lava was still in a molten state and represent both lava
tubes intra flow cavities and blisters and pockets inter flow cavities Lava tubes are
formed when molten lava drains from the cooling flow leaving a hollow tube like structure
which may extend for a large distance longitudinally along the flow Inter flow cavities are
generally smaller in horizontal extent

Based on observations during the mass grading operations the project site

Discussion and Recommendations
Site grading generally consisted of placement of up to about 12 to 15 feet of new
fills to achieve the design grades During the mass grading operations extensive
subgrade preparation procedures were employed within the limits of grading at the
commencement of the mass grading operations for the project to reduce the potential for
collapse of near surface cavities and or voids The intent of the subgrade preparation
procedures is to assist in detecting and collapsing near surface cavities and or voids
contained in the porous near surface materials It should be noted that the subgrade
preparation procedures employed for this project are consistent with the standard of
practice for similar type projects and similar subsurface conditions along the Kohala Coast
on the sland of Hawaii
In general the subgrade preparation in fill areas consisted of ripping the original
ground to a depth of about 3 feet or until refusal using a ripper tooth of a large bulldozer to
assist in detecting and collapsing near surface cavities and or voids contained in the
porous near surface materials After the fill surfaces have been ripped the ground
generally was leveled with the use of a large bulldozer working the surface for about four
passes to provide a relatively level surface After leveling of the rugged surfaces with the
bulldozer the fill subgrades generally were proof rolled with a sheepsfoot roller compactor
minimum 20 tons for about eight passes to help detect and collapse near surface cavities
and or voids
Fill materials consisting of some cobbles and boulders and structural fills were
placed in the fill areas of the project site Structural fills consisted of well graded granular
