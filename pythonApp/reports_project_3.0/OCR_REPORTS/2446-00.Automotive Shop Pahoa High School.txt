engineering exploration for the proposed Automotive Shop at Pahoa High

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface soils and rock conditions at the site of the proposed
Automotive Shop in order to provide data for the formulation of
geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to the design of the
proposed site grading and building foundations
The scope of our exploration consisted of the following tasks and
work efforts

GENERAL SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located in the Pahoa area of the District of Puna
on the island of Hawaii The site is situated at Pahoa High School to the
south of the town of Pahoa and includes a small parking lot three 3
portable classroom buildings and open grassed lawn area
The topography of the site is generally level with ground surface
elevations ranging between 682 and 687 feet above Mean Sea Level The
boundaries of the proposed Automotive Shop site are approximately
demarcated by the Agricultural garden plot to the west the cafetorium to
the northwest the Woodworking Shop to the north the School
Office Library to the east and the Pahoa Kalapana Road to the south
PROJECT CONSTDERATTIONS
It is proposed to develop the site for the construction of an
Automotive Shop Based upon the information provided to us it is our
understanding that the proposed shop building will be a single story steel
framed concrete and masonry building with on grade slab floors Estimated
colum loads are on the order of 25 kips and estimated wall loads are on
the order of 2 kips per lineal foot
Anticipated site grading will be minimal with cuts and fills on the
order of two 2 feet in depth or thickness
As part of the development it is also proposed to construct a
shallow leaching field for the disposal of sanitary wastes from a septic
tank system
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
The project site is underlain by basaltic lava flows of the Puna
Volcanic Series of Kilauea which were deposited during the Pleistocene and
Holocene Epochs Macdonald and Abbott 1970
GEOLABS HAWAII
my
W Page 4
The Puna series is typically composed of basaltic lava flows which
are locally mantled by a relatively thin layer of palagonitized ash
locally referred to as Pahala Ash
At and in the area of the project site the lava flows are in the A a
form which is characterized by a rough spiny or rubbly surface of
fragmental clinker underlain by a dense core of massive basalt which
frequently has a thinner less continuous layer of clinker below the
massive core
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by the drilling
and sampling of three 3 borings to depths ranging between 16 0 and 21 5
feet below the existing ground surface at the project site In addition
falling head and constant head percolation tests were conducted in one of
the drilled borings to provide infiltration data for the design of the
septic system leach field
In general our exploration encountered a very thin surface layer
less than one 1 foot thick of brown silt Pahala Ash intermixed with
gravel and cobble sized basalt fragments Clinker Below this surface
layer our borings encountered in situ coarse volcanic clinker to 12 to 15
feet below the existing ground surface Underlying the clinker our
borings encountered dense basalt to the maximum depths explored
Groundwater was not encountered in the drilled borings during our
exploration
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration along with the boring
logs and percolation test results are presented in Appendix A of this
report

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on our exploration and analyses it is our opinion that
development of the proposed Automotive Shop facility is feasible from a
geotechnical engineering point of view The clinker formation encountered
at shallow depths below the surface of the site should provide adequate
bearing for the proposed structure
Foundation Support
It is our opinion that relatively shallow spread or strip footing
foundations may be used to support the proposed shop structure It is
anticipated that foundations will bear on either in situ clinker or
compacted rockfill
An allowable bearing capacity of up to 3 000 p s f may be used for
the design of foundations bearing on dense rockfill clinker in level
areas This value may be increased by one third when temporary wind and
seismic loads are considered
In order to provide uniform support for the foundations bearing on
the somewhat variable clinker formation we recommend that footing
excavations be prepared by over excavating one 1 foot below subgrade
level and backfilling with base course placed in 8 inch loose horizontal
lifts and compacted to a minimm of 95 of its maximm dry density per
ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Foundations should be embedded a minimum of two 2 feet below the
lowest adjacent finished grade Foundations located on sloping areas
should have a minimum set back of eight 8 feet from the outside edge of
the footing to the adjacent slope face This may require additional
embedment to achieve the proper set back distance

Lateral Pressures
Lateral loads on the proposed structures may be resisted by a passive
resistance of up to 300 p s f per foot of depth of the buried vertical
foundation faces A frictional coefficient of 0 45 may be used to
calculate resistance to sliding
Site Grading
Prior to commencement of grading all areas within the project
grading limits should be cleared of all vegetation and deleterious
materials The resulting grub and spoil material should be disposed of
properly off site or stockpiled for use in landscaping
Ash soils and topsoil generally have relatively low strength
Therefore it is our recommendation that any ash soil or topsoil
encountered within the grading area be removed The stripped material may
be either stockpiled for use in landscaping or disposed of properly
off site
Soft or yielding areas encountered during clearing and grubbing below
any areas designated to receive fill or structural areas should ke
over excavation to firm natural material and the resulting depression
backfilled with well compacted engineered fill
In general the excavated clinker materials less than 12 inches in
size may be suitable as fill material The material if reduced to less
than six 6 inches in size should be adequate as a source of trench and
structural backfill if needed Imported material if required should
conform to the requirements stipulated in Section 30 of the Standard
Specifications for Public Works Construction Select Borrow for Subbase
Course dated September 1986
Generally fills required should be placed in level lifts not
exceeding twelve 12 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum
of 90 maximm dry density as established by ASTM D 1557 78 Trench and

Excavation
The field exploration and visual observations made at the site and in
its vicinity indicate that the site is most likely underlain by volcanic
clinker at relatively shallow depths It is anticipated that most of the
clinker materials may be conventionally excavated Some hard excavation
and or ripping may be encountered in localized well cemented areas or if
large lava balls boulders or ledges are encountered

Leachfield Design
Based on our field percolation testing the clinker formation
encountered at shallow depths below the project site has rapid
infiltration characteristics Based on Public Health Service Publication
No 526 Manual of Septic Tank Practice the leachfield may be designed
for the application of up to 5 0 gallons per day of primary effluent per
square foot of trench bottom
Using an estimated 800 gallons per day of sewage a twenty four inch
wide infiltration trench would require a minimm of 80 lineal feet of
trench State Health Department Administrative Rules require that
subsurface disposal systems provide a 100 per cent backup capability
Therefore a total of 160 lineal feet of trench would be required The
actual length of trench required for the Automotive Shop will depend on
the actual design flow
Disposal of hydrocarbon products such as oil grease and solvents
is generally considered environmentally unacceptable and may be a
violation of some environmental statutes Provision should be made in the
design of the septic system to preclude the disposal of such products
through the sanitary sewer system

Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comment
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this review
is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for
misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the
excavation and foundation phases of the work This is to observe
compliance with the design concepts specifications or recommendations and
to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ
from that anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations
given in this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by the
drilling and sampling of three 3 borings to depths ranging
between 16 0 and 21 5 feet below the existing ground surface at the
approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 Truck
mounted drilling equipment was utilized to drill the borings
Samples of the rock material encountered at the proposed
quarry site were obtained using diamond core drilling techniques
Core drilling is a rotary drilling method which uses a hollow bit
to cut into the rock formation The rock material left in the
annulus of the bit is mechanically recovered for examination
classification and testing The recovered core were logged by an
Engineer or Geologist from our firm Descriptions of the recovered
rock cores are presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A
3
Recovery REC is used as a subjective guide to the
interpretation of the relative quality of rock masses Recovery
is defined as the actual length of material recovered from a coring
attempt vs the length of the core attempt e g if 3 7 feet of
material is recovered from a 5 0 foot core run the recovery would
be 74 and would be shown on the Boring Logs as REC 74
The Rock Quality Designation RQD is also a subjective guide
to the relative quality of rock masses RQD is defined as the
percentage of the core run that is sound material that is in excess
of four 4 inches in length without any discontinuities
discounting any drilling induced fractures or breaks if 2 5 feet
of sound material is recovered from a 5 0 foot core run the RQD
would be 50 and would be shown on the Boring Logs as REC 50
Generally the following is used to describe the relative quality
based on the Practical Handbook of Physical Properties of Rocks
and Minerals
Rock Quality ROD
Very Poor 0 25
Poor 25 50
Fair 50 75
Good 75 90
Excellent 90 100
