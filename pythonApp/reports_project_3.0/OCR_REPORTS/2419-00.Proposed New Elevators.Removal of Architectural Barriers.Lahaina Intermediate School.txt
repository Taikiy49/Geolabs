INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed elevators and mechanical rooms located adjacent to the
existing Buildings C and D of Lahaina Intermediate School on the island of Maui
Hawaii The general location of the site is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The exploration was performed in general accordance with our proposal dated September
11 1989
This report summarizes the exploration findings and our geotechnical engineering
recommendations derived from our analyses based on the data obtained during the
exploration These recommendations are intended for design input and are not intended
for final construction estimation or bidding purposes
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions at the project site in order to develop a generalized soil rock data set for the
formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to the foundation
design of the project
The work was done in general accordance with our proposal dated September 11
1989 The scope of work consisted of the following
1 Engineering observation of the drilling and sampling of four 4 borings to
depths ranging between 18 5 and 24 3 feet below the existing ground
surface by a drilling firm under separate contract to your agency
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2419 00 Page 3
2 Reconnaissance of the project site and vicinity to make visual observations
of the surficial soil conditions
3 Review of available soils and geologic information pertinent to the project
site and its vicinity
4 Laboratory testing of selected samples obtained during the field exploration
to determine the engineering properties and classification of the materials
encountered
5 Engineering analyses of the field and laboratory data for the purpose of
developing foundation and slab on grade recommendations
6 Preparation of this report presenting our findings and design
recommendations
Detailed descriptions of the field exploration and laboratory testing are presented
in the appendices of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct one 1 new elevator each for the existing 2 story high
Buildings C and D at the Lahaina Intermediate School on the island of Maui Hawaii
It is our understanding that the proposed elevator is approximately 7 by 8 feet in
plan dimension and that the bottom of the elevator shaft will extend downward another
4 to 5 feet below the existing ground surface
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2419 00 Page 4
A one 1 story high mechanical room approximately 7 by 9 feet in plan dimension
will also be constructed next to each of the proposed elevators Concrete slab on grade
will be utilized for the proposed mechanical rooms
Minimal site grading involving cuts and fills is anticipated
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located inside the Lahaina Intermediate School which is located
about two 2 miles inland from the western coastline of West Maui It also lies about
Surrounding the school are Lahaina Luna Road to the south and vacant land with
California grass to the east and west An actively grazed cow pasture with fence is
located to the north of the school Large basalt boulders over three feet in diameters
were observed just inside the fence for the cows The boulders appear to have been a
result of previous construction activities and site grading at the school campus
The school buildings are located on terraces probably constructed from previous
cuts and fills site grading work In general the school site is located on a slope dipping
approximately to the west with ground elevations ranging from about 396 to 350 feet
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The project site is located on the edge of the alluvial fan created by the Kahoma
and Kanaha Streams which is underlain by the basaltic shield volcanoes of the Wailuku
Volcanic series making up the bulk of West Maui About one half miles to the northwest
is Puu Laina a cinder cone formed during the last eruption of West Maui Some consider
West Maui to be extinct since its last eruption occurred about 1 3 million years ago
W O 2419 00 Page 5
Subsoil Conditions
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by drilling and sampling
four 4 borings to depths ranging between 18 5 and 24 3 feet below the existing ground
surface The approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general the field exploration encountered approximately a four 4 to eight 8
foot thick fill layer which consists of reddish brown clayey silt overlying volcanic deposits
of cinder spatter clinker and basalt formation to the maximum depth of the drilled
borings which was 24 3 feet below the existing ground surface
Groundwater level was not encountered in the drilled borings during our field
exploration
Detailed description of the subsoil conditions are presented on the Boring Logs in
Appendix A
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration encountered approximately a four 4 to eight 8 foot thick
surface stiff reddish brown clayey silt fill layer overlying volcanic deposits consisting of
cinder spatter clinker and basalt formation to the maximum depth of the drilled borings
which was 24 3 feet below the existing ground surface
Laboratory tests performed on the clayey silt surface fill material indicate that this
material generally has low to moderate swelling potential depending on the initial moisture
content and density of the soil prior to subsequent saturation Therefore to reduce the
soil swelling potential under on grade slabs we recommend that the on site soil be
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2419 00 Page 6
capped with at least twelve 12 inches of non expansive select borrow cushion fill and
that the subgrade soils below the above select fill and slab be properly moistened and
protected from subsequent drying during construction until the completion of slab
concrete placement
It is our opinion that spread continuous footing foundations can be used to
support the proposed elevators and the accompanying mechanical rooms
Relatively easy ground excavation into the clayey silt fill material is anticipated for
the proposed elevator shaft excavation
Details of our recommendations are discussed in the following sections
Foundations
Based on our field exploration and subsequent geotechnical engineering analyses
we recommend that spread or continuous footing foundations be used to support the
proposed elevators and the accompanying mechanical rooms An allowable bearing
silt soils A minimum footing embedment of eighteen 18 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grade is also recommended The bearing value is for total dead plus
live loads and may be increased by one third when temporary loads wind and seismic
are considered
The bottom of all footing excavations should be moisture conditioned to near the
optimum moisture content and recompacted to obtain a minimum of 95 maximum dry
density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2419 00 Page 7
If soft loose clayey soil pockets were encountered at the bottom of the future
footing excavation due to inadequately compacted backfill it should be overexcavated
to stiff in situ soil and replaced with granular select borrow compacted to a minimum of
95 maximum dry density
If a footing is located next to a utility line it should extend to the bottom of the
utility trench to reduce footing settlement due to settlement of the trench backfill
Provided the above recommendations are followed estimated total footing
settlement under typical structural loads anticipated for the proposed elevators and
mechanical rooms should be less than the order of one half of an inch
Site Gradin
Minimal site grading with generally less than one 1 foot cut or fill is anticipated for
the proposed project
Prior to the commencement of any earthwork all areas within the grading limits
and proposed structures should be cleared and grubbed of all rubbish and deleterious
materials The resulting grub and spoil material should be disposed of properly off site
The fill and backfill should be placed in maximum 10 inch thick loose level lifts and
compacted to a minimum of 95 maximum dry density as established by ASTM Test
Method D 1557 78
Imported select borrow fill material should be non expansive with less than 20
fines passing the 200 sieve
W O 2419 00 Page 8
The finished grade should be shaped to shed water away from foundations and
to avoid ponding condition
Slab On Grade
It is our understanding that concrete slab on grade foundations will be utilized for
the proposed mechanical rooms
It should be noted that the on site clayey silt have low to moderately high swelling
potential if they are allowed to dry and then subsequently get saturated Therefore we
recommend that the on site soil should be over excavated down to a minimum depth of
twelve 12 inches below the slab bottom the exposed subgrade soils thoroughly
moistened and then immediately replaced with non expansive imported select borrow
soils compacted to about 90 maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
The thickened edge of the slab should be embedded a minimum of twelve 12
inches below the lowest outside adjacent finished grade
A four 4 inch layer of 3B Fine gravel cushion fill should be provided below the
slab In order to protect against future moisture infiltration an impervious moisture barrier
may be incorporated below the slab
Elevator Construction
It is our understanding that the proposed elevators and mechanical rooms are in
a preliminary design stage and have not been finalized Furthermore the anticipated
excavation depth for the proposed elevators is about 4 to 5 feet below the existing ground
surface
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2419 00 Page 9
As built drawings of the existing buildings should be obtained and the proposed
elevators and mechanical rooms should be designed to reduce disturbance damage to
the structural integrity of the existing footings or structures Underpinning of the existing
slab on grade and second floor lanai walkway slabs may be required depending on the
final layout design of the proposed elevators and mechanical rooms
If the proposed 1 story mechanical room is to be located adjacent to the new
elevators the elevators should be constructed first and the backfill of the elevator
excavation should be consisted of select granular fill material compacted to a minimum
of 95 of its maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 The backfill
operation should be carefully monitored to reduce possibility of future backfill settlement
and subsequent distress of the slab on grade foundations that may be placed on the
compacted backfill
Relatively easy excavation of the subsoils is anticipated The method and
equipment used for excavation and bracing of the excavation should be the choice of the
contractor within practical limits and safety considerations

Design Review
Final construction plans and specifications for the proposed construction should
be forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for updated review evaluation and written
comments prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to the
earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this review is not made the
geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for misinterpretation of the
recommendations

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that a geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during earthwork construction and footing excavation
to monitor the contractor s geotechnical operations This is to observe compliance with
the design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow design changes in
the event that subsurface conditions differ from that anticipated prior to start of
construction The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon such
observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and approved by
the soils engineer prior to its delivery to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during construction are different
from those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based upon
information obtained from the field data points such as borings Variations of conditions
between the borings may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not
become evident until construction If variations then appear evident it will be necessary
to re evaluate the recommendations given in this report
The locations of the borings were determined by taping from structures indicated
on the transmitted topographic survey map by Control Point Surveying and Engineering
Inc dated Elevations of the borings were approximately determined
by interpolation from elevation points on the topographic survey map The physical
GEOLABS HAWAII

location and elevation of the borings should be considered accurate only to the degree
implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the boring points depict
the approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual
transition The strata lines shown on profiles or cross sections are based upon
interpolation between borings and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
Water level data from the boring points were collected at the times given on the
graphic representations and or in the text of this report These data have been reviewed
and interpretations made in the formulation of this report However it must be noted that
fluctuation may occur due to variations in rainfall tides temperature and other factors
