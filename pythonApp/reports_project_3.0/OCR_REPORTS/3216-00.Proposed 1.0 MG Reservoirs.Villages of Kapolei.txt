INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed 1 0 Million Gallon MG Reservoirs project within the Villages
of Kapolei development in the District of Ewa on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general
location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings and geotechnical engineering
recommendations resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering
analyses These recommendations are intended for design input only The findings and
recommendations presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this
report

The project site is located within the Villages of Kapolei development in the District
of Ewa on the island of Oahu Hawaii It is proposed to construct two reservoirs
near the eastern perimeter of Kapolei Village 8 and west of the existing Kapolei Lower
Channel between Station 8 50 and Station 13 50 of the Navy Boundary Line In

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lavas from two
shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be
of middle to late Pliocene in age and Koolau is estimated to be of late Pliocene to early
Pleistocene in age As volcanic activity in the Waianae ceased lava flows from the
Koolau banked against its eroded eastern slope forming a broad plateau now known as
the Schofield Plateau
Physical and chemical weathering followed by erosion of this plateau generated
sediments which were transported to the coast In the vicinity of the project site and to
the south these sediments accumulated and interbedded with marine sediments and
coral algal reef formations to form a sedimentary wedge The thickness of the
sedimentary wedge ranges from zero in the area of the Interstate Route H 1 Highway to
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3216 00 Page 6
over 1 000 feet at Ewa Beach This wedge forms the Ewa Plain and serves as the
confining formation or caprock over the artesian basal aquifers of southern Oahu
Deposition of sediments has continued from earlier geologic time through the present
Agricultural development within the last 100 years and recent mass grading work
have brought the Villages of Kapolei area to its present form

Site Grading
Besides the relatively deep excavations required for the proposed reservoirs
minor site grading is anticipated for the project construction Based on the design
concept and our field exploration the major geotechnical considerations during the site
grading work consist mainly of the following
1 Excavation of the project site to achieve the design slope grade of
for the reservoir construction
2 Proof rolling of the subgrades to help detect and collapse potential
near surface cavities voids in the underlying coral formation
3 Placement of the base course cushion and anchorage of the underlining
and impervious liner for the reservoirs
4 Over excavation of the near surface expansive soils and replacement with
non expansive select granular fill materials under concrete slabs on grade
and pavement areas

thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris rubbish and other

fill The excavated soft and or organic soils should be properly disposed of off site

and pavement areas should be over excavated where necessary to provide the

pavement limits

Fill Materials
Select granular fill materials required for the non expansive capping layer under
the concrete slabs on grade and pavement areas should be moisture conditioned
to above the optimum moisture placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose
thickness and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
Based on our field exploration the on site coralline materials encountered at
greater depths may be re used as a source of non expansive capping fill material
under the concrete slabs on grade provided that they are processed to a relatively
well graded granular material with a maximum size of 3 inches Additional
imported fill materials if required should consist of non expansive select granular
material such as crushed coral mudrock basalt or cinder sand The material
should be well graded from coarse to fine with no particles larger than 3 inches in
largest dimension and should contain between 10 and 30 percent particles passing
the No 200 sieve The material should have a laboratory CBR value of 20 or
more and should have a maximum swell of less than 1 percent The on site soils
or imported fill materials should be tested by Geolabs Hawaii for conformance with
these recommendations prior to delivery to the project site for its intended use

Excavation
Based on the existing topography and the finish grade of the proposed reservoirs
excavations up to about 15 feet will be required for the project construction The
method and equipment to be used for excavation should be determined by the
contractor subject to practical limits and safety considerations The excavated
materials should not be stockpiled adjacent to the planned excavation within a
horizontal distance equal to the depth of the excavation
Generally the dense coralline materials encountered at greater depths during our
field exploration should be rippable by a large bulldozer or other heavy equipment
However due to the nature of coral formations some hard excavation such as
using hoerams and or chipping may be needed in localized areas Therefore
allowances should be made for the additional time and expense that may be
required by the hard excavation
The above discussions regarding the rippability of the coralline materials are based
on the field data from the borings drilled at the site We recommend that the
contractor working on this project examine the site conditions to make their own
interpretation

Impervious Liner and Underlining
We understand that an impervious liner will be provided for the reservoirs to
reduce water infiltration through the underlying permeable material It is our opinion that
the material type performance installation and protection details of the impervious liner
should be designed in accordance with the recommendations by the liner manufacturer
It is critical that the liner maintain its integrity to prevent saturation and build up of
seepage water pressures within the excavation
Our experience with similar liner installations indicates that there is a tendency for
the liners to become detached at the top of the water impoundment It appears that this
may be the result of inadequate anchoring of the top of the liner and insufficient flexibility
of the liner material This is of particular importance with the relatively steep design side
slopes of proposed for this project Therefore it is our recommendation that the
liner manufacturer be consulted regarding proper construction detailing and installation of
the liner with particular attention to proper anchoring to reduce the potential of liner failure
Effects of fluctuating water levels on the performance of the impervious liner near the top
anchoring should also be considered
Technical representatives of the manufacturers of the impervious liner should be
required to be on site at all times during the installation process to assure that proper
construction procedures and precautions are followed A sales representative of the
supplier s may not have the technical expertise to adequately perform this function
An underlining consisting of a heavy duty geotextile fabric will be required beneath
the impervious liner to reduce the potential for puncture of the impervious liner The use
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3216 00 Page 15
of a geotextile fabric is recommended in lieu of a sand cushion due to the ease of
installation and the increased puncture resistance of the impervious liner when combined
with a layer of geotextile underlining In addition a sand cushion is not recommended
because of the steep side slopes of proposed for the reservoirs
Building Foundation
Based on the generally competent subsoil conditions encountered at the project
site we believe that shallow spread and or continuous footings may be used for support
of the proposed control building An allowable bearing pressure of up to 3 000 pounds
per square foot p s f may be used for the design of foundations bearing on the
undisturbed in situ stiff silty clay soils This bearing value is for dead plus live loads and
may be increased by one third for transient loads such as those caused by wind or
seismic forces
In general footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest
adjacent finish grades For footings constructed near top of slopes or on sloping ground
the footings should be embedded deep enough to provide a minimum horizontal set back
distance of 8 feet measured from the outside edge of the footings to the slope face
For footings bearing on the undisturbed in situ stiff soils the bottom of the footing
excavations should be lightly compacted to provide a relatively firm and smooth bearing
surface prior to the placement of reinforcing steel or concrete If soft and or loose
materials are encountered at the bottom of footing excavations they should be over
excavated to expose the underlying firm materials The over excavation should be
backfilled with select granular material compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative

Slabs On Grade
Our field exploration disclosed that the project site is generally underlain by
near surface silty clay soils with swelling potential ranging from slight to moderate when
subjected to moisture fluctuations Unless floor slabs constructed above the moderately
expansive soils are properly designed there is a potential for future distress to the lightly
loaded slabs on grade resulting from shrinking and swelling of the moderately expansive
clayey soils due to changes in the moisture content To reduce the potential for structural
distress resulting from the swelling of the expansive subgrade soils we recommend that
the subgrade soils be properly prepared and capped with at least 12 inches of
non expansive select granular fill material placed in accordance with the
recommendations presented in the Site Grading section of this report
For building slabs not subjected to vehicular traffic we recommend that a minimum
4 inch thick layer of cushion fill consisting of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33
No 67 gradation be placed above the 12 inch non expansive select granular fill to
provide more uniform support of the slabs To reduce future moisture infiltration and
subsequent damage to floor coverings an impervious moisture vapor barrier such as a
plastic membrane is recommended on top of the gravel cushion layer Two inches of
moist fine sand should be placed above the plastic membrane to provide protection of the
membrane and to aid in curing of the slabs concrete
Where the slabs will be subjected to vehicular traffic or equipment operation a
6 inch layer of base course compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction is
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 3216 00 Page 18
recommended in lieu of the 4 inch thick cushion layer The 2 inch moist fine sand and
vapor barrier may also be omitted for slabs subjected to vehicular traffic The base
course should consist of crushed basalt aggregate compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative compaction The thickened edges of slabs adjacent to any unpaved
area should be embedded at least 18 inches below the lowest adjacent grade
Exterior concrete walkways may be required for the proposed project We
recommend that a minimum of 6 inches of select granular fill be provided below the

width of the walkways with expansion joints at right angle intersections
The area adjacent to the slabs should be backfilled tightly against the slabs with

pipes which could result in settlement of the backfill and damage to the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipes to
the top of the subgrade or finished grade should consist of on site soils or select granular
fill material The backfill should be moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the
optimum moisture placed in maximum 8 inch level loose lifts and mechanically
compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for
future ground subsidence Where trenches are below pavement areas the upper 2 feet
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 3216 00 Page 22
of the trench backfill below the pavement subgrade should be compacted to a minimum
of 95 percent relative compaction
Drainage
The finished grades outside the proposed control building should be sloped to
shed water away from foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also
advised that gutter systems be installed around the building and that the discharge be
diverted away from the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering
near the foundations and slabs should also be avoided Planters next to foundations
should be avoided or have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into
the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil
saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations
should be properly backfilled against the walls or slabs footings immediately after setting
of the concrete to reduce water infiltration
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and should be
maintained to drain all surface run off away from the slabs and footing foundations
