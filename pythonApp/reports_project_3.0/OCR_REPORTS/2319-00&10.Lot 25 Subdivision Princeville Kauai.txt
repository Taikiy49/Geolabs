INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the proposed Lot 25 Subdivision in
Princeville on the island of Kauai Hawaii The general location of
the project site is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings of our exploration and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analyses based
on the data obtained during the exploration These recommendations are
intended for design input and are not intended for construction
estimation or bidding purposes

It is proposed to subdivide the existing Lot 25 of Princeville asa
residential development No site grading plans are currently available
however we anticipate that site grading will involve the cutting of up
to about four 4 to ten 10 feet and the placement of up to about 20
feet of fill in the northern part of the site It is also our
anticipation that relatively deep excavation on the order of 25 to 30
feet may be required if sewerage pump stations are installed within the
subdivision
As part of the off site improvements in conjunction with the
project it is also proposed to construct a maintenance access road
between Ka Haku Road and Lot 25

SITE DESCRIPTION
lot 25 is situated in Princeville on the northern coast of the
island of Kauai Hawaii The project site is located to the north of
the existing Puamana subdivision on top of the bluffs overlooking Anini
Beach The general location of the site and its vicinity is shown on
the Project Location Map Plate 1
Formerly the site was used for the cultivation of pineapple and
then as a pasture for the grazing of cattle and horses Currently the
site is maintained as a well trimmed open field
Based on our field reconnaissance the available topographic map
and site plan it appears that the site slopes gently towards the north
and the existing ground elevation ranges from about 200 to 240 feet
above Mean Sea Level MSL At the northernmost end of the site beyond
the project limits the ground surface slopes down to the north forming
the bluffs overlooking the Anini Beach area These slopes grade from
relatively gentle at the top and bottom to near vertical in their
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The project site is situated on a broad gently sloping plateau
along the north shore of the island of Kauai This plateau was built
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O Page 5
during the late Pliocene and Pleistocene by the extrusion of basaltic
lavas and related pyroclastics from the Pooku vent of the Koloa Volcanic
Series
Most of the soils in the project area are residual soils derived
from the in situ weathering of these basic igneous rocks The residual
soils grade with depth to saprolite a form of residual soil which
retains the relict structures vesicles and joints of the parent rock
In former pasture areas the upper foot or so of soil has been
reworked as a result of the past agricultural activities and generally
has a high organic content
Soil Conditions
The subsurface conditions were explored by drilling and sampling
seven 7 borings to depths of 11 5 to 119 5 feet below the existing
ground surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2
In general the soil conditions encountered throughout the project
site appear relatively uniform Most of our drilled borings encountered
a surface layer between one 1 and three 3 feet thick of medium
stiff to stiff brown clayey silt topsoil
Below the topsoil layer the borings generally encountered stiff to
very stiff reddish brown to brown clayey silts varying in thickness
between about 9 0 and 13 0 feet These are residual soils that derived
from the in situ weathering of Koloa Basalts and are generally
structureless
Underlying the residual soils our borings encountered generally
medium stiff to stiff saprolite soils consisting of clayey silts and
silts having predominantly brown color mottled with black spots or
streaks Occasional soft pockets and or seams were encountered in the
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O Page 6
saprolite layers at depths generally less than 15 feet below the ground
surface The saprolites encountered were generally about 35 feet in
thickness and grade with depth to extremely weathered basalt at a depth
of about 45 to 50 feet below the ground surface
The extremely weathered basalts in turn grade with depth into less
weathered material Competent basalt was generally encountered about 95
feet below the existing surface
During our field exploration groundwater was not encountered in
the drilled borings However groundwater has been encountered in deep
borings in other areas of Princeville This groundwater is in a perched
condition and is generally the result of low permeability ash beds or
lenses within the Koloa Volcanic Series Therefore it may be possible
that isolated lenses of groundwater may be encountered in deep
excavations within the project site
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration encountered generally competent subsoils
within the project site However modified grading techniques are
required for the earthwork portion of the proposed site improvements due
to the relatively high in situ moisture content of the subsoils and the
relatively high rainfall occurring in the area
We recommend that shallow spread footing foundations be used to
support the proposed residential structures
Site Grading
The proposed development is in the conceptual stage and detailed
grading plans are not available However cuts up to about 10 feet in
depth and fills of up to about 20 feet in thickness are anticipated
along the northern boundary of the site

Fill Compaction
Since the project site is located in a high rainfall area most of
the subsoils within the top 15 feet had in situ moisture contents
of about 35 to 50 at the time of our exploration These moisture
contents are considerably higher than the anticipated optimm
moisture contents which range from about 32 to 36 for similar
soils in the Princeville area Therefore considerable drying of
the excavated materials would normally be required prior to their
use as fill materials
However in view of the relatively frequent rainfall that generally
occurs in the area throughout the year drying of large quantities
of soils might not be readily achieved during mass grading
operations To facilitate construction it is our opinion that the
normal compaction standard of 90 of its maximm dry density per
ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 for general site fills as used in drier
areas may be reduced to 85 for this project Our previous
experience in Princeville and other similar areas indicates that
85 compaction is generally adequate for site fills under roadway
and light building loads
Select fills within the upper six 6 inches below roadway or
footing subgrade should be compacted to a minimm of 90 of its
maximm dry density per ASIM Test Method D 1557 78
Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed Topsoil should be
stripped and stockpiled for later use in landscaping Any

The ground surface below pavements or footings should be scarified

Fill Placement
Based on the field exploration and laboratory test results it is
our opinion that the on site soils have low expansion potential and
may be used as a source of fill and backfill materials If
additional off site borrow is required the material should be
tested and approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling
to the site to see that it is suitable for the proposed
construction The material should generally be non expansive and
should not contain large percentages of boulders organic or
deleterious matters

It is noted that most of the on site soils within the top 15 feet
have dry densities in the range of about 65 to 88 p c f When they
are compacted to higher densities corresponding volume reduction
will occur For earthwork quantity estimate we believe a
relatively high shrinkage factor should be used A shrinkage
factor of 8 to 19 for densification alone may be considered when
the above modified densification criteria are followed A higher
shrinkage factor may result if the soils are further dried and
compacted to 90 or more of the maximm dry density These
shrinkage factor estimates do not include losses for grubbing
topsoil stripping or handling

Slopes

horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V or flatter Mid point slope

20 feet
Fill Settlement
It is our understanding that the proposed site grading may include
the placement of fills up to about 20 feet deep at the project
site Due to the reduced compaction level which will be used in
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O Page 10
the earthwork for this project we anticipate that newly placed
fills will continue to consolidate or settle after the completion
of the earthwork
To reduce the potential of settlement induced distresses to future
structures we recommend that settlement gauges be placed at the
surface of all fills greater than five 5 feet deep These gauges
should be surveyed for vertical movement and the readings
transmitted to the project geotechnical engineer for review and
analyses
It is our opinion that a minimm 3 month fill settlement period
should be provided prior to residential footing construction The
actual fill settlement gauge readings would provide additional
in put to verify when the final structural improvement can be
initiated Therefore construction of structures and or utilities
should be coordinated until the settlements have reached tolerable
rates
Steep Existing Slopes
The northermmost portion of the project site encroaches on the
steep existing slopes overlooking the Anini Beach area These slopes
grade from gentle at the top to near vertical in their mid points Our
exploration indicates that the upper portions of these slopes are
composed of soil materials i e residual soils and saprolite to
depths of about 50 feet below the existing ground surface
Over the years there have been several slides on these steep
existing slopes The majority of these slides may have been shallow
seated failures or skin tears involving only the slope surface to a
depth of a few feet However there appears to have been occasional
more deeply seated slides along zones of weakness
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O Page 11
Based on our analyses it is our opinion that a minimm structural
setback of 100 feet away from the tops of these steep slopes is
necessary to maintain an adequate factor of safety of 1 5 for future
residential structures See Plate 4
The area within the structural setback limits could be used for
non structural purposes such as golf course or park area It may be
necessary that special consideration could be given to re configure Lot
25 and the adjacent golf course to optimize land use between the
residential subdivision and the golf course
Subdrains
Based upon the proposed grading concept the major fill area will
be in a drainageway within subdivision Lot Nos 7 and 8 on the northern
portion of the site Due to the wet climate conditions and based upon
previous experience of encountering seepage in drainageways in the
Princeville area we recommend that subdrains be installed in the base
and side slope of this drainageway fill embankment to relieve any
seepage which may occur or accumulate along the interface between the
existing natural ground and the new fill See Plates 5 and 6 Such
accumulation of water could create a plane of weakness between the fill
and the existing subsoils potentially causing slippage or creep of the
fill The final location and quantity of subdrains should be determined
in the field by the project geotechnical engineer
Foundations
Based on the soil conditions we believe that shallow spread and
continuous footing foundations bearing on the recompacted in situ
subsoils or compacted structural fill may be used to support the
proposed residential structures
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O Page 12
An allowable bearing value of up to 2 500 p s f with a minimm
footing embedment of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent grade is
recommended The bearing value is for total dead plus live load only
and may be increased by one third 1 3 for short duration wind or
seismic loading
For buildings to be constructed on or adjacent to slopes except as
discussed above in Steep Existing Slopes the building should be
set back or the depth of the footing embedment extended so that there
is a minimm of ten 10 feet horizontal distance between the outer toe
of the footing and the outside slope face
If a footing foundation is located next to a utility trench the
footing should be extended to the bottom of the utility trench or be
designed to span over the trench to reduce footing settlement due to
settlement of the trench backfill
Based on the boring data estimated total footing settlements under
the design loads are anticipated to be on the order of less than one 1
inch total and one half 1 2 of an inch differential
Resistance to lateral loads on the proposed structure may be
provided by passive soil pressure and frictional resistance A
frictional coefficient of 0 4 may be used to compute the frictional
resistance between the bottoms of the footings and the recompacted
in situ soils In addition an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 p s f
per foot of depth may be used against the buried vertical faces of the
footings to determine the passive soil pressure

placed over a 4 inch layer of 3B Fine gravel course may be used for the
proposed residential structures The thickened edges of the on grade

To protect against moisture damage of the floor covering material

Retaining Walls
If a sewerage pump station or other buried structures are to be
installed as part of the site improvements the walls should be designed
as retaining walls under a restrained condition

Granular materials such as base course rock 3B Fine gravel sand

the build up of hydrostatic pressure behind the walls and to reduce the
potential of moisture migration through the walls to the interior of the
building
A friction factor of 0 4 between the concrete footing bottom and
soil may be utilized to determine the sliding resistance of the walls
Additionally a passive soil resistance of 300 p c f equivalent fluid
pressure may be used

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as
borings Variations of conditions between the field data points may
occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become
evident until construction If variations then appear evident it will
be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations given in this report
The locations of the field data points were determined by taping
from reference points shown on the Princeville Lot 25 Layout Concept
Plan by Belt Collins Associates dated Elevations of
the data points were determined by interpolation from contours and
elevation points on the same plan The physical location and elevation
of the field data points should be considered accurate only to the
degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown
on profiles or cross section are based upon interpolation between field
data points and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
Water level data from the field data points were collected at the
times given on the graphic representations and or in the text of this
report These data have been reviewed and interpretations made in the
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O Page 17
formulation of this report However it must be noted that fluctuation
in water level may occur due to variations in rainfall tides
temperature and other factors

Placement and Compaction of Fill
A
The fill shall be placed in horizontal layers which will not
exceed ten 10 inches in loose thickness Each layer shall be
spread uniformly and blade mixed to attain uniformity of
material and water content within each layer
Additional fill material shall not be placed on any fill layer
which has not been properly compacted Subsequent removal of
any unacceptable layer shall be done at the Contractor s
expense
Rock fragments or boulders less than twelve inches 12 in
diameter may be utilized provided they are not concentrated in
any one area and that there is sufficient fine grained material
to fill the voids between the rocks
Rocks greater than twelve inches 12 in diameter shall be
removed from the fill unless specifically approved for placement
in the fill by the Geotechnical Engineer
All fill up to 6 inches below the subgrade levels shall be
compacted to a minimm of 85 percent of the maximm dry density
established by ASTM Test Method unless otherwise
specified in the Geotechnical Engineer s Report 90 compaction
is required in the last 6 inches below the subgrade levels
Boulders encountered in excavations may be used in the deeper
fills provided that the following are followed
1 Boulders shall be spread out and must not be nested
together They should be placed so that compaction
equipment will be able to compact around them
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O Plate C 8
Appendix C Continued
2 No part of the boulder should encroach any closer to
finished subgrade than the boulder s largest dimension and
in no case be closer than 3 feet to finished subgrade
3 No boulder larger than 5 feet in its largest dimension
should be placed in the fill It may be necessary to reduce
the size of larger boulders or dispose of them off site
4 Care must be exercised to avoid placement of boulders in the
proposed utility alignments to prevent difficulty in the
later excavation of the utility trenches
The compaction equipment may be sheepsfoot vibratory or
pneumatic tire rollers suited to the soil type and capable of
producing the specified results in an expedient and efficient
manner The equipment shall be in good working condition fully
ballasted and self cleaning
The compaction shall be accomplished in a systematic and uniform
manner over the entire fill area to produce a uniformly
compacted fill
The Contractor shall be required to obtain the above specified
levels of compaction out to the finish slope of fill slopes
This may be achieved by either over building the slope and
cutting back to the compacted core or by direct compaction of
the slope face with suitable equipment
Fill placed against previously graded cut or fill slopes shall
be properly keyed through topsoil or loose slope material into
firm material and the transition stripped of loose material
prior to fill placement Fill shall be placed and compacted
from the low side up
