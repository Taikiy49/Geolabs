INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this investigation is to define subsurface
conditions and to provide recommendations for the design
and construction of the proposed Autocrafters of Barrington
complex located in Honolulu Oahu Hawaii This report
summarizes our findings and recommendations
SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed building site is located on Lagoon Drive and at
the intersection with Ualena Street just north of the Honolulu
International Airport
W
Page 2
The site is relatively level at about elevation 8 5 feet
The site seems to be recently graded and cleared of trees
and vegetation A small portable car rental office is
located on the makai entrance Piles of precast concrete
were placed along the north property line adjacent to the
Jet Burger Drive In A 1 story high concrete block wall
warehouse building is located along the east property line
about 10 feet away
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
The proposed autocrafters building will be a one story high
steel framed structure The building is about 155 by 80 feet
in plan area
The span of the steel frame will be about 130 feet wide CMU
walls are planned at the north and east sides of the building
while metal walls will be located at the south and west ends
Maximum column loads will be about 50 kips each
No major cuts and fills will be required for site grading A C
pavement is planned for the building interior pavement and the
outside parking area

Soil Conditions
The subsurface explorations disclosed that the site is blanketed
by approximately two to five feet of stiff miscellaneous man made
fills consisting of silty clay sand gravel and boulders
Beneath the fill a layer of medium stiff to stiff grayish brown
silty clay was encountered The clay layer is about 12 feet thick
at the north east corner of the property and thins out to less
than 2 feet toward the south and west directions
Dense coral formation was encountered below the clay layer down
to the full depth of all the borings
The groundwater level will be influenced by tital fluctuations
of the Keehi Lagoon The water level was encountered at 6 8 to
7 7 feet below the existing ground surface
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Because of the non uniform density of the surface fill layer
spread and wall footing foundation should either bear on com
pacted granular backfill or upon the stiff in situ clay layer
encountered 2 to 5 feet below the existing ground surface
The A C pavement can be supported on compacted on site subgrade
soils
W
Page 4
Foundation
To provide more uniform bearing conditions and to minimize
differential settlements tt ts recommended that compacted
granular backfill material should replace the surface mis
cellaneous fill below all the footings
The miscellaneous fill layer is estimated about 2 to 5 feet
in thickness After this material is removed under all the
footings the granular backfill material should be placed in
9 inch loose lifts and compacted to 95 compaction
Wall or spread footing foundation placed at minimum depth can
be designed for an allowable bearing pressure of 2 500 pounds
per square foot for footing bearing upon the compacted granular
fill layer or on the in situ stiff clay layer
Based on the above bearing pressure the calculated total settle
ment would be about 1 inches for the columns The estimated
differential settlement would be approximately 3 4 of an inch
To reduce distress by foundation settlements the CMU wall at
the north and east sides of the structure should be separated
by vertical joints or the structural footing foundation should
be designed for this settlement condition
The bottom of footing and structural backfill will vary in depth
in order to reach the undisturbed in situ clay layer Therefore
the contract documents should provide clauses for excavation and
structural backfill quantity adjustments in the field

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It ts recommended that the foundatton excavations for the
butlding be inspected by the soils engineer prior to the
placement of concrete or reinforcement to assure that the
existing fill material is replaced with compacted granular
backfill and that adequate bearing has been obtained This
is to assure that the subsurface conditions are conststent
with the findings of our preliminary subsurface investigation
The utility trench and structural backfilling together with
other earthwork operations should be monitored by the soils
engineer
Construction inspection should include the necessary density
testing to assure that the proper degree of compaction is
consistently achieved
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon
the above inspection

APPENDIX A
FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling
5 borings utilizing truck mounted equipment The depths of all
the borings terminated at 10 to 16 feet below the existing ground
surface The locations of the borings are shown on the enclosed
Site Plan Plate 1
The soils were classified by visual and textural examination in
the field by our engineer who continuously supervised the drilling
operations The classifications were verified by visual inspection
and testing in the laboratory All soils were classified in accor
dance with the Unified Soil Classification System A graphic pre
sentation of the soils encountered is presented on the Boring Logs
Plates A 1 through A 5
Soil samples were obtained by driving either a 2 4 inch I D ring
sampler or a standard penetration sampler with a 140 pound hammer
free falling a distance of 30 inches The blow counts to drive
the sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the logs of borings
at the appropriate sample depth
