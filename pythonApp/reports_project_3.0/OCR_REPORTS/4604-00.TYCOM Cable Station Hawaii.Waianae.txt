REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The Island of Oahu is composed largely of the weathered remnants of two extinct
shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae forms the bulk of the
western third of the island while the younger shield volcano Koolau forms the majority of the
eastern two thirds of the island It is believed that Waianae became extinct while Koolau was
still active and its eastern flank is partially buried below Koolau lavas in Central Oahu
The project site is located on the western side of the Waianae Mountain Range
During the Pleistocene Epoch sea levels fluctuated in response to the cycles of continental
glaciation As the glaciers grew and advanced less water was available to fill the oceanic
basins such that sea levels fell below the present stands of the sea When the glaciers
melted and receded an excess of water became available such that the sea levels rose to
above the present sea level
The higher sea level stands caused the formation of deltas and fans of accumulated
terrigenous sediments in the heads of old bays accumulated reef deposits at
GEOLABS INC
W O 4604 00 Page 5
correspondingly higher elevations and deposited lagoonal marine sediments in the quiet
waters protected by fringing reefs The lower sea stands caused streams to carve valleys
in the sediments and reef deposits Subaerial exposure of the sediments and calcareous
materials caused consolidation of the soft deltaic materials and lagoonal deposits and
induration of the calcareous reef materials Land development and reclamation projects
within the last century have brought the project site to its present form

Foundations
As indicated previously the maximum column loads of the two story building structure
will be not more than 370 kips per column Based on the subsurface conditions encountered
at the site we recommend that the new cable station building be supported on a shallow
foundation system consisting of spread and or continuous footings An allowable bearing
pressure of up to 2 500 pounds per square foot psf may be used for design of the
foundations bearing on the recompacted on site soils or new compacted fills This bearing
such as those caused by wind or seismic forces
In general footings should have a minimum width of 18 inches In addition footings
for the new cable station building should embedded a minimum of 24 inches below the
lowest adjacent finished grade Foundations located next to utility trenches or easements
should be embedded below a 45 degree imaginary plane extending upward from the bottom
edge of the utility trench or should be extend to a depth as deep as the inverts of the utility
lines This requirement is necessary to avoid surcharging adjacent below grade structures
with additional structural loads and to reduce the potential for appreciable foundation
settlement

The bottom of the footing excavations should be compacted to a minimum of

If soft and or loose materials are encountered at the bottom of footing excavations the soft
and or loose materials should be removed to expose the underlying firm or dense
materials The over excavation should be backfilled with well compacted fill materials
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers

Slabs On Grade
Based on our laboratory test results the on site clayey and or silty soils exhibit a
slight to moderate potential for shrinking and swelling when subjected to moisture
fluctuations To reduce the potential for future distress to the lightly loaded slabs on grade
resulting from shrinking and swelling of the clayey soils we recommend that the subgrade
soils underneath the slab areas be properly prepared and capped with at least 12 inches
of non expansive select granular fill material The 12 inch thick non expansive select
granular fill capping fill should be placed below the slab cushion and also should extend
laterally beyond the slab on grade perimeter a minimum of 24 inches
The non expansive select granular fill should be placed as soon as possible after the
subgrade soils are prepared in accordance with the recommendations presented in the
Site Preparation subsection of this report The capping fill and the underlying subgrade
soils should be wetted and kept moist until the final placement of concrete Where shrinkage
cracks are noted after compaction of the subgrade we recommend that the soils be
thoroughly moistened to close the cracks or prepared again Saturation and subsequent
yielding of the exposed subgrade due to inclement weather and poor drainage may require
over excavation of the soft areas and replacement with well compacted fill
For the building slabs we recommend that a minimum 4 inch thick cushion layer
consisting of free draining materials such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67
gradation be provided below the slab above the capping fill to provide for uniform support
of the slab The open graded gravel cushion layer would also serve as a capillary moisture
break To reduce the potential for future moisture infiltration and subsequent damage to floor
coverings an impervious moisture barrier is recommended on top of the cushion layer Two
inches of moist fine sand may be considered above the moisture barrier to provide for
protection of the moisture barrier and to aid in curing of the slab concrete Provision of the
2 inches of moist fine sand above the moisture barrier is optional and will be left up to the
discretion of the project structural engineer
GEOLABS INC
W O 4604 00 Page 10
Where the slabs will be subjected to equipment operation and or vibrations we
recommend that a 6 inch layer of aggregate subbase compacted to a minimum of 95 percent
relative compaction be provided in lieu of the 4 inch thick cushion layer The 2 inch moist
fine sand layer and moisture barrier may also be omitted for these slabs The aggregate
subbase should consist of crushed basalt aggregate compacted to a minimum of 95 percent
relative compaction For the design of structural slabs a modulus of subgrade reaction of
250 pounds per square inch per inch of deflection pci may be used for the compacted
aggregate subbase
We envision that some exterior concrete walkways may be required for the proposed
project We recommend that concrete walkways be supported on at least 6 inches of
aggregate subbase or non expansive select granular fill The aggregate subbase or select
granular fill should be compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction To
reduce the potential for substantial shrinkage cracks in the slabs crack control joints should
be provided at intervals equal to the width of the walkways with expansion joints provided at
right angle intersections
The thickened edges of siabs adjacent to unpaved areas should be embedded at
least 12 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade Areas adjacent to the slab should
be backfilled tightly against the slab edges with the on site soils and graded to divert water
away from the slab to reduce the potential for water ponding around the slabs

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the proposed cable station site were explored by drilling
and sampling five borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 5 extending to depths of
about 21 5 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate locations of the borings
drilled are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were drilled using a truck mounted
drill rig equipped with continuous flight hollow stem augers
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by testing
in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representations of the materials
encountered in the borings are presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 5
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 3550 84 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils
by driving a 3 inch OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling
30 inches In addition some samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel
Sampling of Soils by driving a 2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same
hammer and drop The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third
6 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of
Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Pocket penetrometer tests were performed on selected cohesive soil samples
retrieved in the field The pocket penetrometer test provides an indication of the unconfined
compressive strength of the soil sample Results of the pocket penetrometer tests are
presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content ASTM D 2216 and unit weight ASTM D 2937 determinations were
performed on selected samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil
properties The results of these tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the
appropriate sample depths
Two one inch ring swell tests were performed on relatively undisturbed samples of the
soils to evaluate the swelling potential of the near surface soils The results of these tests
are summarized on Plate B 1
Two Atterberg Limits tests ASTM D 4318 were performed on selected samples of
the soils to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits and to aid in soil classification Results of
these tests are summarized on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Graphic presentation of the test results is provided on Plate B 2
One sieve analysis test ASTM C 136 ASTM D 1140 was performed on a selected
soil sample to evaluate the gradation characteristics of the soils and to aid in soil
classification Graphic presentation of the grain size distribution is provided on Plate B 3
One laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR test ASTM D 1883 was performed
on a bulk sample of the near surface soils to evaluate the pavement support characteristics
of the soils The results of the test are presented on Plate B 4
