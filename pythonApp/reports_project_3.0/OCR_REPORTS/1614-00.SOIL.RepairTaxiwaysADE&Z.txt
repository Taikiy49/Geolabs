Soil Engineering Investigation
Repair of Taxiways A D E Zz
Honolulu International Airport
Honolulu Oahu Hawaii
This report presents the results of our soil investigation for the
repair of Taxiways A D E Z at the Honolulu International
Airport The general location of the project site and vicinity are shown
on the Project Location Map Plate l
PURPOSE AND SCOPE OF SERVICES
The subject taxiways were constructed about 17 to 23 years ago in
1963 to 1969 Some of them were repaired with 1 to 5 inches overlay on
the bituminous surface course since then
W O 1614 00 Page 2
The existing pavements of the taxiways have experienced some
cracking and subsidence damage in various areas At some of the turns
where the airplanes change direction and induce large lateral stresses in
the pavement pushing shoving damage were observed in the bituminous
surface course
The purpose of our investigation was to explore the existing
pavement sections and subgrade soil to evaluate the condition of the
various pavement components to provide recommendations for the proposed
pavement repair
The scope of investigation included drilling and sampling seven 7
to 10 0 feet below the existing pavement surface Also
included were laboratory testing of the samples obtained engineering
evaluation of the field and laboratory data development of repair
recommendations and report preparation
Detailed description of our field exploration and laboratory test
results are presented in the appendices of this report
GEOLABS HAWAII
WO 1614 00 Page 3
SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed site is located to the south of the Diamond Head
Concourse at Honolulu International Airport All the taxiways included
in the investigation were paved with bituminous material The existing
surface condition ranged from good along Taxiway D to very poor along
Taxiway E Slight subsidence was observed in some areas
The general area is flat and drainage is directed to the shoulder
sections which are located on both sides of the taxiways
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions were explored by drilling and sampling 7
borings to depths of 5 0 to 10 0 feet below the existing ground surface
at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The borings disclosed varying pavement sections and subgrade
conditions at each of the taxiways The bituminous surface course
asphalt concrete varied in thickness from 3 inches to 10 inches and
the base course material ranged from very dense tannish white silty coral
sand with some gravel to poorly graded dense gray silty basalt gravel
with sand The subgrade consisted primarily of very dense tannish white
to grayish tan coral sand with coral gravel
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1614 00 Page 4
Borings 2 and 7 were drilled to a deeper depth of 10 feet at areas
where the pavement suffered slight subsidence and serious cracking
damage The borings disclosed that the subgrade in Boring 2 was
underlain with a thin layer of stiff clayey silt at the depths of about
5 5 to 6 5 feet and in Borings 7 a layer of medium dense grayish white
sandy silt at the depths of about 6 5 to 8 5 feet
Groundwater was encounterd only in Boring 2 at the depth of 8 3
feet during our field exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration disclosed that the bituminous surface course
of the subject taxiways are generally aged and deteriorated Extensive
cracking and or slight subsidence damage were noted at Taxiway E and
the western end of Taxiway A Taxiway Z appeared to be in
relatively stable condition while Taxiway D was just reconstructed in
February 1985 by placing a new 5 inch overly over the old bituminous
surface course
Our laboratory testing indicates that most of the base materials do
not comply completely with the Federal Aviation Administration FAA CBR
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1614 00 Page 5
and gradation requirements for base course while the subbase courses are
basically satisfactory except at the Boring 7 area where the subbase
material gradation was marginal
The combined effect of age and deterioration of the base subbase
material contribute to the pavement distress
Based on these findings we believe that the pavements of the
taxiways need to be repaired by replacing the surface course and possibly
base course and or subbase
The existing pavement conditions of each of the four taxiways will
be discussed more in detail in the following sections and the
corresponding repair measures will be recommended
Taxiway A
Surface Course
The existing bituminous surface course appears to be stable at the
eastern end but deteriorated at the western end with many
longitudinal cracks and slight subsidence

fines passing 200 mesh sieve than specified and therefore is

satisfactory

Based on the existing conditions and the 17 years in service of the
pavement we believe that the surface and the base courses of the
pavement need to be replaced

Turns
Pushing shoving of the bituminous surface course due to the
excessive lateral force caused by actions of turning aircraft traffic was
observed at turns of the taxiway

sufficient to resist the lateral stresses of the wheels of the turning
aircraft In order to resist the lateral stresses a concrete pavement
section may be utilized on the turn areas

Site Grading
Excavation up to about 2 feet into the existing pavements is
anticipated for the repair measures Where excavation is required all
existing pavements debris and other unsuitable materials should be
removed and disposed of properly off site in order to prevent
contamination of the excavated areas
Where the total component pavement section needs to be replaced
the subgrade subbase base should be scarified to a depth of at least 6
inches below the cleared ground surface moisture conditioned to optimum
moisture content and recompacted to 100 ASTM D 1557 78 maximum

base subbase material should comply with the FAA requirements for
base subbase material
Design Review
Plans and specifications for site grading and pavement repair
should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written comments
prior to construction to see that the intent of our recommendations is
included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the site grading subgrade and pavement
preparations be observed by the soils engineer during construction to see
that adequate compaction has been obtained and that the subsurface
conditions are consistent with the findings of our investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
