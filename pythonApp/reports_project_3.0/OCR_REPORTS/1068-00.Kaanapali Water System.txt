This report presents the results of our preliminary geologic
reconnaissance at the above referenced Site The work was
performed in general accordance with our proposal letters dated
August 17 1981 and
The purpose of this reconnaissance was to examine the general
surface soil conditions in the area in order to evaluate the
proposed pipeline construction from a soils engineering point
of view

The reconnaissance consisted of a review of available soils
and geologic maps and literature concerning the area b a
general geologic reconnaissance of the project area and the
proposed pipeline alignment c excavation of one test pit at
the location of proposed Constant Head Tank No 1 and d per
formance of seismic Surveys on the north and south sides of
Honokawai Gulch
PLANNED DEVELOPMENT
It is proposed to construct a 16 inch diameter water pipeline
to supply additional potable water to the Kaanapali Resort
area The project will also involve the construction of two
constant head tanks and a siphon structure crossing Honokawai
Gulch
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located in the Kahana Mahinahina and Moo
muku sections of the Lahaina district on the Island of Maui
It is bordered to the north by Pulepule Gulch and to the south
by the south fork of Honokowai Gulch and will generally follow
existing agricultural access roads
GEOLABS HAWAII
The terrain slopes generally to the west at approximately a 10
percent grade Drainage is provided by several gulches and
drainageways tending to the west with sidewalls having slopes
the alignment vary from about 730 feet to about 980 feet with
the invert of the Honokowai Gulch Siphon structure at 534 feet
The site is used primarily for agricultural purposes and is
planted in sugar cane and pineapple Small portions such as
the gulch sidewalls are not planted and the natural vegetation
consists of grasses haole koa java plum and eucalyptus
REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The project site is located on the western slopes of the West
Maui Mountains These mountains are the erosional remnants of
the original West Maui Volcano
During the late Tertiary Period a rapid succession of thin
bedded basalt lava flows of the Wailuku Volcanic Series built
the West Maui Volcano to a height of about 7000 feet above the
present sea level Following a brief period of quiesence vul
cCanism was renewed and large portions of the West Maui Volcano
were mantled by thick bedded andesitic lava flows of the Hono
lua Volcanic Series
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1068 00 Page 4
On the western coast of the West Maui Volcano the terrain gen
erally reflects the original surface of the volcano although
it is moderately dissected by youthful valleys Weathering and
erosion have only slightly modified the topography of the ridge
tops
Over most of the proposed alignment the surficial soils are
residual and weathered in place from lavas of the Wailuku Vol
canic Series On the ridge between Pulepule and Kahanaiki
Gulches the surficial soil is residually derived from the
younger Honolua Volcanic Series
Some minor amounts of alluvium and colluvium have accummulated
in the bottoms of the gulches but these deposits are generally
thin and overlie residual soil or weathered rock
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Visual examination of the project site and surrounding areas
indicate that subsurface conditions along the proposed align
ment are relatively uniform
GEOLABS HAWAIl
It is anticipated that through most of the alignment the
residually weathered brownish red and reddish brown clayey
silts near the ground surface will be very stiff and will range
from 3 to 10 feet thick This layer will grade into a layer of
very stiff mottled red gray brown colored saprolite between 4
and 15 feet thick Saprolite is a residual soil that retains
the structure of the parent rock and in this case is altered
by mechanical remolding into a brownish clayey silt with some
sand and gravel Boulders and isolated ledges of rock may be
encountered in both the surface residual soil and the under
lying saprolite and represent weathering resistant remnants of
the original basaltic rock
It is anticipated that such boulders will be most prevalent in
the portion of the alignment between wells P 5 and P 6
The saprolite layer will grade into weathered basalt and even
tually at a depth of about 40 to 60 feet it will grade to
relatively unweathered rock
In the Honokowai Gulch crossing the alignment will encounter
weathered basalt approximately 10 to 15 feet below the existing
intake structures and this will grade to relatively unweathered
rock with seams of weathered clinker at about 50 feet and
continue to about 20 feet from the bottom of the gulch At
this point the alignment will encounter a talus apron
overlying boulderly alluvium in the center of the gulch
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1068 00 Page 6
In the smaller gulches the alignment will encounter thin depo
sits generally less than 20 feet thick of talus and cobbly
alluvium at the bottoms of the gulches
At the time of our investigation no groundwater was encoun
tered in the excavated test pit nor was there any flow of water
noted in any of the gulches or drainageways although localized
seeps were noted on the walls of Honokowai Gulch

Based on our field reconnaissance of the project site our
general observations and recommendations are presented below
General
The field reconnaissance indicates that the Site is suitable
for the proposed pipeline construction
The competent residual soils volcanic rock and talus alluvial
materials should provide adequate bearing for the proposed
pipeline and related structures
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Page 7
Excavation
The estimated depth of excavation for the water line will be
about 5 to 10 feet Relatively easy digging is anticipated
through the upper stiff residual soil Some amount of boulders
or isolated rock ledges should be anticipated in the residual
soil formations
Due to the depth of the excavation anticipated shoring of the
trench walls is recommended to minimize caving in or sloughing
of the trench walls Design of the shoring should be the res
ponsibility of the contractor
If sufficient space is available the excavation may be cut at
a temporary slope of 1 1 H V in lieu of the above wall sup
ports
If practical the excavation should be made in short sections
and backfilled as soon as possible
Heavy equipment and stockpiles should be kept away from the
tops of the open trenches
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1068 00 Page 8
The ground adjacent to and near the top of the excavation
should be monitored continuously for cracks or dips and other
indications of movements and possible sloughing of the trench
walls This monitoring is particularly critical in the areas
where existing structures are adjacent to the trench excavation
Backfill
Trench backfill requirements will depend on the future develop
ment in the area For the existing agricultural use stringent
compaction of the backfill is not necessary However if
future development is planned proper backfilling is necessary
to prevent settlement of future structures over the trench
backfill
In general the trench backfill should be in accordance with
Section 11 of the Standard Specifications for Public Works Con
struction May 1975
Reaction Blocks
Reaction blocks are required in portions of the alignment to
resist lateral loads at the pipe bends The lateral soil
resistance that can be developed will depend upon the size and
depth of the reaction blocks For preliminary design purposes
a lateral bearing capacity of 2000 p s f may be used for reac
tion blocks in the stiff residual soil or saprolite
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 1068 00 Page 9
In order to develop the full lateral bearing capacity the
reaction blocks should have a minimum setback distance equal to
2 times the width of the block measured from the nearest adja
cent slope face
The concrete should be poured neat against the reaction block
excavation

Foundation
Spread or continuous footings may be used to support the
tank structure All footings should bear on undisturbed
very stiff residual clayey silt soils An allowable
bearing value of 3500 p s f may be used for footings
resting on the very stiff residual soils The footings
should be embedded a minimum of 12 inches below the
lowest adjacent finish grade
The ground surface around the footings should not be
excavated or allowed to erode If the footing is
located next to a utility trench it should extend down
to the same grade as the bottom of the utility trench
Surface water should be diverted away from the
foundations
The edge of the tank footing should be set back a mini
mum of 15 feet away from the edge of the nearest down
Slope face
Based on the test data and estimated loads it is our
opinion that minimal settlements less than 3 8 inch
are anticipated for the proposed structures

the above inspections
