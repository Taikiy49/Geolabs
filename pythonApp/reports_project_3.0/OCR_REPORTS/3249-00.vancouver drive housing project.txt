INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Vancouver Drive Housing Project located at Vancouver
Drive in the Manoa District on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and
vicinity of the site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering recommenda
tions resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering analyses
The recommendations presented herein are intended to provide design input for
foundations retaining walls site grading and pavement and may not be appropriate
to form the basis for preparation of construction cost estimates nor for construction
bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to develop a housing project at Vancouver Drive in the lower
Manoa area on the island of Oahu Hawaii Based on the information provided to us
the proposed housing project will involve the construction of a one story community
center administration building at the northeast corner of the project site and a 2 to
3 story apartment building with on grade parking under the rear portion of the building
as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 Construction of new driveway and utility lines are
also planned for the proposed project
GEOLABS HAWAII

Detailed grading plans for the project were not available at the time this report
was prepared Based on information provided to us we anticipate that cuts of less
than 3 feet and fills up to approximately 10 feet will be required to provide a raised level
building parking finish pad Retaining walls with maximum heights of approximately 15
feet are expected for grade separation
Our previous experience in the lower Manoa area suggests that the project site
may be underlain by stiff residual soils over decomposed basalt rock Special attention
was given to the swelling characteristics of the near surface soils and their potential
impact on the future floor slabs supported on grade

EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is located at Vancouver Drive in the District of Manoa on the
island of Oahu Hawaii The area is bounded by Vancouver Drive to the north Manoa
Valley Inn to the west a one story and a four story apartment buildings to the south
and deteriorated asphalt driveway to the east
It appears that the eastern portion of the project site is an abandoned residential
lot with old concrete slabs and broken CRM walls CMU and CRM walls approximately
2 to 3 feet high along the southern property line and concrete walls and wooden fence
along the western property line were also noticed during our field visit
The site is presently vacant and vegetated with mango trees and grasses
Based on our field exploration and a topographic map entitled Topographic Survey at
Vancouver Drive Housing Project by the Division of Land and Acquisition Department
of Public Works City and County of Honolulu dated the topography of
the site is relatively level with existing ground surface elevations ranging from about
113 to 132 feet Mean Sea Level MSL The site generally slopes toward the south
at a gradient of about ten horizontal to one vertical 10H 1V
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions at the subject site were explored by drilling and
sampling six borings to depths of about 21 feet below the existing ground surface
The approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general our exploration encountered residual soils consisting of very stiff to
hard silty clays to depths of approximately 2 to 6 feet below the existing ground
surface Below the residual soil layer our borings encountered very stiff to hard clayey

and silty soils with decomposed rock and extended to the maximum depth explored of
about 21 feet below the existing ground surface
The consistency of soils ranged from very stiff to hard and graded with highly
weathered gravel with increasing depth Very dense silty sand layers approximately

the ground surface respectively
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings at the time of our field

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field exploration and laboratory testing we believe that a shallow
foundation system bearing on the very stiff residual soils may be used to support the
proposed building structures In areas where the existing ground surface will be raised
significantly the shallow foundations may be supported on properly placed and
compacted structural fill
Laboratory tests performed on the near surface clayey soils indicate that the
materials generally have a moderate to high shrink swell potential when subjected to
moisture fluctuations Therefore we recommend that concrete slabs on grade be
GEOLABS HAWAIl
W O 3249 00 Page 7
supported on 24 inches of non expansive capping fill to reduce the potential effects of
the moderately expansive soils Where the slab will be supported on fill material the
thickness of the capping fill may be reduced after the evaluation of swell shrinkage
characteristics of fill material
We understand that new retaining structures will be required along the southern
property line to support new fills up to 10 feet high Where the existing retaining walls
will be kept in place we recommend that new footings be embedded at about the same
footing level of the existing retaining wall structure This requirement is necessary to
reduce the potential for surcharging adjacent existing retaining structures with additional
loads and thereby inducing settlement to the existing retaining wall

Foundations
Based on the information provided to us we anticipate that majority of the
buildings will be situated in areas with a raised grade Because of the generally very
stiff subsoil conditions encountered in our exploration we believe that a shallow
foundation system consisting of spread and or continuous footings may be used to
support the proposed new buildings
To minimize the potential of differential settlement associated with variable
thickness of fills to be placed in the building area all footing foundations should be
supported on the native stiff residual soil An allowable bearing pressure of up to

Retaining Walls
Because of the limited space for construction retaining structures are planned
to provide lateral support for the new fill We anticipate that retaining walls with heights
of up to about 15 feet will be constructed at the proposed site We understand that the
retaining walls are expected to be conventional concrete retaining walls General
guidelines for the design of the retaining wall structures are as follows
1 Foundations for retaining walls should be designed in general accordance
with the recommendations presented in the Foundations section of this
report
Where wall footings are to be located adjacent to the existing retaining
structures the footings should be embedded at about the same level of
the existing retaining walls If possible the existing retaining walls should
be demolished to make room for the new retaining walls This require
ment is necessary to reduce the potential for surcharging adjacent below
grade structures with additional structural loads and to reduce the
potential of foundation settlement
2 All retaining structures should be designed to resist the lateral earth
pressures due to adjacent soils and surcharge effects An active lateral
earth pressures of 40 p s f expressed in equivalent fluid pressure is
recommended for design of cantilever walls For at rest earth pressure
a value of 55 p s f equivalent fluid pressure per foot of depth may be
used

Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork all areas within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation existing concrete slabs existing
broken CRM walls debris and other unsuitable materials should be removed
and disposed of properly off site to reduce potential contamination of the
excavated materials
After clearing and grubbing the exposed subgrade should be scarified to a
minimum depth of 8 inches properly moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent
above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90
percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry
density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3249 00 Page 14
Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding
to the maximum dry density
Any soft weak or yielding areas disclosed during clearing operations should be
over excavated to expose firm ground and the resulting excavation should be
backfilled with engineered fill compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction Prior to placement of the backfill the over excavated subgrade
should be scarified to a depth of at least 8 inches moisture conditioned to at
least 2 percent above the optimum moisture content and compacted to at least
90 percent relative compaction The excavated soft and or organic soils should
be properly disposed of off site
If off site select material is required the imported fill material should be tested
and approved by Geolabs Hawaii prior to delivery to the site to confirm that it is
suitable for its intended purpose The requirement for imported fill material is
presented in the following subsection Fills and Backfills

The 24 inch non expansive capping select fill recommended below the floor
slabs should consist of imported granular material such as crushed coral basalt
gravel or cinder sand The material should be well graded from coarse to fine
with no particles larger than 3 inches in largest dimension The material should
have a laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR value of 15 or higher and a
swell potential of not more than one percent when tested in accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1883 It should contain less than 20 percent particles
passing the No 200 sieve All capping fill materials should be moisture

95 percent relative compaction All surfaces should be finished to create
smooth unyielding subgrades and should be kept moist until covered by
concrete slabs or pavements

backfill material It should be emphasized that the on site silty and clayey soils
should not be used within the minimum 2 foot non expansive capping fill layer
below slabs because of the moderately to highly expansive nature of the on site
soils However the on site silty and clayey soils may be used within the lower
portion of deeper fill located a minimum of 2 feet below the future slab subgrade
elevation The clayey soil should be properly moisture conditioned to at least 2
percent above the optimum moisture content placed in level lifts not exceeding
8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative

Drainage
The finished grades outside the buildings should be sloped to shed water away

subsoils

surface soils The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled against the

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content and unit weight determinations were performed on selected soil
samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties The results of
these tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Three one inch ring swell tests were performed on selected soil samples to
evaluate the swelling potential of the near surface soils The results are summarized
on Plate B 1
Two Atterberg Limits tests were performed on selected samples of the soils to
evaluate the liquid and plastic limits and to aid in soil classification Results of the
Atterberg Limits tests are presented on the logs of borings at the appropriate sample
depths A graphic presentation of the test results is presented on Plate B 2
Two unconfined compression tests were performed on selected samples of the
cohesive soils to evaluate the unconfined compressive strength of the material Results
of the unconfined compression tests are summarized on the logs of borings at the
appropriate sample depths
