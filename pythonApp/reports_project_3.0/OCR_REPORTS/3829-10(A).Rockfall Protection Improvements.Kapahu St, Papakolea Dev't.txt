2 1 Regional Geology
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lava from two
shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be
middle to late Pliocene in age and Koolau is estimated to be late Pliocene to early
Pleistocene in age After a long period of volcanic inactivity during which time erosion
incised deep valleys into the Koolau shield volcanic activity returned to the Island of Oahu
with a series of lava flows followed by cinder and tuff cone formations These most recent
volcanic series are referred to as the Honolulu Volcanic Series The Punchbowl crater
located to the west of the project site is one such extinct tuff and cinder cone
The project area is located on the southern flank of the Koolau Range and is
composed of basalt rock formation built by the extrusion of lavas of the Koolau Volcanic
Series These rocks are generally characterized by layered flows of jointed dense vesicular
basalt with interbedded clinker layers For the past 1 to 2 million years weathering of the
Koolau lava flows has occurred creating the valley and ridge topography visible today on the
southern side of the Koolau mountain range
In the mountainous regions of Hawaii the erosional processes are dominated by
mass wasting which is essentially the detachment of soil and rock masses from the valley
walls and transportation downslope toward the axis of a valley primarily by gravity and
deposited as colluvium Once these colluvial materials reach a stream typically in the central
portion of a valley alluvial processes become dominant and the sediments are transported
and deposited as alluvium
The project site is located on the southem lower hillside flank of a prominent ridgeline
situated to the northwest of the Tantalus Makiki area The ridgeline is bounded by Kanaha
Stream to the east and Pauoa Valley to the west The Kapahu Street project site is located
at the boundary of a gently sloping alluvial valley floor and a steep lower hillside flank of the
ridge mentioned earlier

SECTION 3 0 DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
As mentioned in our previous report the rockfall hazard at the site does not appear
to be related to a particular surface drainage problem but instead it appears to be the result
of natural weathering and mass wasting processes associated with the evolution of the steep
cut slope Based on conversation with some residents storm water runoff appears to be a
contributing mechanism that initiates many of the rockfall events however signs of previous
occurrence of concentrated storm water flow from the top of the slope were not observed
during our site visits Therefore we believe the rockfall is a naturally occurring hazard
induced as a result of the strongly fractured rock condition and steepness of the slope
Based on the anticipated need for a safe relatively low cost workable and easily
maintainable system for protection against rockfall we conclude that construction of a
semi rigid chain link barrier fence combined with slope grading is appropriate for this site
Our preliminary recommendation presented in the previous report considered the following
protective methods for use at the site
1 Building relocation and installation of a rigid concrete barrier
2 Installation of a flexible barrier fence
3 Installation of a semi rigid barrier fence
Subsequent evaluation of the existing site conditions has revealed that Method No 3
may be the most appropriate protective measure for this site Relocation setback of the
existing homes within the respective lots was considered to be less appropriate due to the
limited available space within some lots and the old age and weakened structural condition
of some of the buildings The installation of a flexible barrier fence was also considered less
appropriate due to the anticipated high cost of the specialized fence materials installation
of the fence anchors and the need for more frequent maintenance and clearing of debris
from the fence system to maintain operation

medium hard clinker seams The clinker seams may range from loose and friable to
compact consisting mainly of cemented cobble and gravel size materials It is our
opinion that the volcanic rock at the site may be excavated with normal heavy
excavation equipment such as large excavators where the rock is highly to severely
fractured However excavation of the dense blue rock materials will likely require the
use of hoerams or chipping
The above discussions regarding the rippability of the subsurface materials are based
on the field data from observations of exposed conditions at the site We recommend
that contractors proposing to bid on this project be encouraged to examine the site
conditions to make their own interpretation
Cut Slopes
We anticipate that the proposed cut slopes would generally expose the medium hard
to hard basalt and clinker rock visible on the existing exposed slope face In general
we believe that the planned cut slopes may be designed with a slope inclination of
Y2H 1V or flatter Considering the condition of the existing rock slope we believe that
slope benches would not be required for this project
