INTRODUCTION
The proposed Ala Moana Sewage Force Main extends from the
proposed pumping station near Fort Armstrong along the peri
meter of the Matson Container Yard across the Honolulu
Harbor entrance and along the length of Sand Island to the
new sewage treatment plant presently under construction
W 1975
This report summarizes the findings of a subsurface investi
gation to define soil and foundation conditions along the
proposed alignment crossing the Honolulu Harbor Channel
The investigations for the remaining sections of the force
main and for the pump station are covered by separate reports

Site Description
The pipe crossing begins near the makai end of Pier 1 at the
Matson Container Yard The ground surface drops relatively
steeply from approximately elev 7 to elev 45 within 80
feet of the shoreline This steep slope was created by
dredging of the access area to the Pier 1 extension
The bottom of the harbor channel is essentially level at
elev 45 Approximately 300 feet from the Sand Island
shoreline it begins to rise at a 30 percent slope to elev
10 feet A shallow water terrace then extends the remain
ing 150 feet to the shoreline
Underwater reconnaissance of the proposed alignment revealed
that the terrace and slopes are generally composed of sand
and shells with some silt and numerous coral ledges and out
crops The deeper portion in the vicinity of elevs 40 to
50 feet is blanketed by soft and loose sandy silt with some
clay
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The present Honolulu Harbor Channel is the result of both
construction and geological activities Initially the
W O
formations in the channel area were deeply bisected by fresh
water stream flows during periods of lower sea level
With a rise in the sea level the previously cut channel was
filled with reef debris and thick layers of non marine
alluvial water deposited clays and silts
Recent dredging operations deepened and widened the
channel entrance removing the alluvium and the coral forma
tions on both sides Part of the dredged material was
used to fill the Diamond Head end of Sand Island
Subsurface Explorations
The subsurface conditions along the proposed alignment were
investigated utilizing four test borings drilled at the
locations shown on the enclosed Boring Location Plan Plate 1
Borings 1 and 4 were drilled in areas underlain by coral Boring 2
was drilled within the center portion underlain by the deep
alluvial deposits Boring 3 was drilled in a transition
area between Borings 2 and 4
A detailed description of the field exploration methods and
accompanying laboratory testing is given in the appendix
along with the boring logs and laboratory test results
W O
Soil Conditions
The subsurface explorations and laboratory testing disclosed
two general types of subsurface conditions along the
proposed force main alignment crossing the Honolulu Harbor
entrance The enclosed Channel Profile Plate 2 illustrates
the findings of the subsurface explorations
On the terrace and slope areas coral was generally encoun
tered with layers of loose to medium dense sand This inter
layered sand and coral formation extended to the limit of
the explorations at elev 87
In Boring 4 the sand and ledge coral was encountered between
elevs 33 and 68 At elev 68 a cavity was encountered
which extended to approximately elev 81 where again the
white coral fragments and ledge coral was found
The coral formations are typical deposits for the Honolulu
Plain They generally consist of irregular masses of hard
ledge coral with interbedded lenses and layers of sand and
other fine grained coral debris Both open and sand filled
voids can be expected
The center section is underlain by very loose to medium dense
alluvial soils consisting of interbedded sand silt and clayey
W O
silt layers generally composed of non marine deposits The
clays and silts were eroded from the near by Koolau moun
tains and deposited by the streams flowing into Honolulu
Harbor The characteristic properties of this soils are low
density low strength and high compressibility
DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The coral and marine debris located on both flanks of the
Honolulu Harbor Channel will provide adequate support for
the proposed force main with normal bedding
The center channel section is underlain by loose alluvial
soils which will not provide adequate support Piles or
special bedding will be required in this area
Earth Pressures
The loads on buried conduits are related to the width of the
excavation and the unit weight and type of backfill material
Throughout the coral sections the trenches can be excavated
with steep sidewalls except in the upper loose sandy layers
In these areas the trench width will be held to a minimum
and it is anticipated that the on site coral or select
W O 484 40 f
granular fill would be utilized for the backfill The maxi
mum earth pressure on the pipe within the coral sections
would therefore be approximately ten kips per lineal foot
Within the alluvial deposits it will be difficult to main
tain side slopes steeper than 2 horizontal to 1 vertical It
is also anticipated that the predominate backfill material
will be the weak on site materials For these conditions the
maximum earth pressure is estimated to be approximately 15
kips per lineal foot
Pipe Support in Coral Areas
As previously mentioned the flanks of the channel are com
posed of coral deposits which should provide adequate support
for the proposed force main The upper portion of these
deposits contains pockets of loose to medium dense sand and
reef debris in addition to the hard coral
It is therefore recommended that the pipe in the coral areas
be supported upon a minimum two foot thickness of crushed
rock bedding The crushed rock will minimize differential
settlements and provide a cushion of backfill to eliminate
stress concentrations on the hard coral
W O
Excavations in Coral Areas
It is expected that the hard ledge coral deposits in the
lower portion of the trench excavations will require special
excavation methods such as percussion and or blasting tech
niques The upper unconsolidated silt sands and reef debris
can be excavated by clamshells dredges or similar devices
In the ledge coral unsupported slopes between 1 1 and verti
cal are expected to stand The sand and upper non cemented
materials should stand at slopes no steeper than 2 horizontal
to 1 vertical 2 1 Slopes in these materials will be
influenced by excavation methods bottom currents and wave
action which can readily erode the soils The time interval
between the trench excavation and the pipe placement should
be minimized to reduce the effects of these forces Slopes
as flat as 4 1 may be required in areas where there are
strong currents or wave action Temporary sheeting may be
advantageous in these areas near the existing shorelines
Pipe Support in Alluvial Deposits
The center portion of the force main through the harbor
channel will be bearing upon loose to medium dense alluvial
soils These soils consist of silts sands and coral frag
ments with some clay They have low shear strength and are
generally highly compressible
W O
Pile Foundations
If reinforced concrete pipes are utilized in the center
section they should be supported upon concrete piles The
piles would be designed as frictional piles obtaining their
capacity from the frictional resistance of the penetrated
soils
The enclosed Plate 3 presents the allowable pile capacities
for three pile sizes and varying pile lengths This capacity
is based upon an analysis of the shear strength of the allu
vial soils and must be verified by pile load tests The
driving characteristics should be established from the
driving records of the test piles The pile lengths may be
modified if hard layers are encountered before the design
depth is reached A design pile or piles can be selected
from this chart based upon the structural requirements of the
pipes The piles should have a minimum length of thirty
feet to adequately develop the pile resistance
The maximum pile length for the smaller piles may be limited
by splicings to obtain the design length and by stresses
within the piles during the construction operations It is
not anticipated that an 80 foot 10 inch square pile could be
utilized without excessive bending stresses during lifting
W O
With pile support the pipe must be backfilled with granular
material to a minimum of half the height to provide lateral
Support over the soft underlying soils
The pipe settlements with pile support should be less than 1
inch total and differential which should be within the
anticipated tolerances of the reinforced concrete pipe
Stabilized Bedding
Utilizing steel pipe which has stronger joint connections and
higher tolerance to differential settlements an alternative
pipe support consisting of stabilized bedding can be utilized
The steel pipe should be designed for maximum total settle
ments of 3 inches and differential settlements of 1 inches
The stabilized bedding would consist of a minimum of six feet
of compacted crushed rock beneath the pipe This material
should extend a minimum of five feet outside the width of the
pipe to reduce the bearing pressures upon the underlying
soils
This type of construction would eliminate the costs for the
concrete piles but would require deeper and wider excava
tions and higher material costs for the steel pipe
W O
Excavations in the Alluvial Deposits
A stability analysis of the bottom materials indicates
presence of adequate factor of safety at slopes of 2 hori
zontal to 1 vertical 2 1 This stability is based upon a
static condition without external shocks bottom currents or
wave action If disturbed by construction activities or
other external forces the materials may reach slopes as flat
as 4 to 1 The soils are saturated low density materials
which can liquify under external loadings or shock The con
tractor should be cautioned of the possibility of the low
slope stability of these materials
Pipe Protection
The force main should be protected by a blanket of compacted
granular materials to prevent damage from outside activities
such as dropped or dragged anchors
Previous force mains constructed in this area have been
damaged and displaced prior to the placement of the protec
tive covering
Seismic Activities
The pipe sections supported within the coral formations
should be relatively secure against seismic forces The
W O
center section within the alluvial deposits and the transi
tion zone between the two materials are the most sensitive
to seismic movements The unconsolidated alluvial deposits
can liquify upon strong seismic shock which would result in
temporary loss of pipe support It is not anticipated that
this liquifaction would occur except under the most severe
seismic shocks
It is anticipated that the stabilized mat or pile support
will provide adequate support for seismic forces equivalent
to Zones 1 or 2 A severe shock such as could occur in Zone
3 could damage the force main
The amplitude of the earth waves could produce an upward
movement and displacement of the concrete pipe from the piles
It is therefore recommended that a tension tie be provided to
connect the concrete pipe to the piles
It is not anticipated that the stabilized bedding would be
affected by this type of movement although additional stresses
would be placed upon the steel pipe
Inspections During Construction
Piles
It will be necessary to inspect the pile driving operations
to assure that the piles have developed adequate support for
W O
the proposed pipe The blows per foot required to drive the
piles should be monitored closely to prevent damage to the
piles if dense layers are encountered above the design depth
It is recommended that a minimum of three pile load tests be
undertaken to establish the final design capacity of the
piles and to establish the driving criteria
Coral Cavities
The trench excavations within the coral should also be
inspected to determine if cavities are present at or near the
invert level The void encountered in Boring 4 may be due to
undercutting of the coral ledge by fresh water flows A
probing program should be developed based upon the pipe
length and design to define the extent of this void and to
determine if additional voids are present within five feet of
the base of the trench excavations
If voids are encountered at or near the invert level they
should be filled with compacted granular fill or cement grout
It is anticipated that the concrete pipe would be capable of
spanning small void without excessive movements The struc
tural design of the pipe should be reviewed to determine its
maximum span
W O
Trench Excavations
The area through which the force main will be constructed is
composed of both coral and alluvial deposits However this
may not be consistent throughout the alignment Inspections
during the construction operations should be conducted to
determine if conditions are substantially different from
those encountered during the subsurface explorations
Pipe Bearing Conditions
The actual extent of the pipe bearing within the coral and
the alluvial deposits cannot be accurately defined based
upon the test borings It is therefore necessary to provide
within the construction specifications and documents pro
cedures for adjusting the location of the transitions between
the coral and the alluvial materials
Our review of the test boring information and the previous
construction of the near by force main indicates a rapid
change in subsurface conditions near the existing slopes
The estimated locations of the transition zones have been
Superimposed upon Plate 1 Please note that this is an
estimate to be utilized for the development of the construc
tion plans and that provisions must be made for modifying
the pipe construction based upon the subsurface conditions
uncovered during the construction operations

drilling platform The depths of the primary borings varied

A 4
