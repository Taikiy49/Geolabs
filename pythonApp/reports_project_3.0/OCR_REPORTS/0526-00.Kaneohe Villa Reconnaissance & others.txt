Gentlemen
This report presents the results of our geologic reconnaissance
of the 20 acre subject parcel that is located north of Mokapu
Saddle Road in Kaneohe Oahu
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is presently planned to construct 22 duplex units on the
lower slopes of the subject property The project is in the
preliminary design stage and maximum cuts on the order of 15
feet are anticipated on the upper units and maximum fills of
approximately 15 feet on the lower portion

SITE CONDITIONS
Geology
The island of Oahu was formed by two major volcanoes the
Waianae shield volcanoe and Koolau shield volcano Puu Papaa
Peak and the associated ridge line on which the site is
located is the caldera of the old Koolau shield volcanoe
Dikes are numerous throughout the area as are breccia associ
ated with caldera collapse The dikes and breccia are usually
more resistant to errosion compared to the thin lava flows
This errosional resistance ridge line has resulted after
extensive weathering of the less resistance surrounding lava
flows The warm climate and heavy rainfall at the project
area has caused a chemical weathering of the basalt rock
leaving residual soils of reddish brown to light brown clayey
silt Some exposed bedrock was noted in the upper end of the
steep gully
Surface
The site which consists basically of two associated ridge lines
and gully is heavily vegetated with trees and brush The
upper portion of the site has slopes up to 50 to 60 per cent
Page 3
W
levelling down to approximately 20 per cent at the lower por
tion of the slopes The area between the bottom of the slopes
and the Mokapu Saddle Road is fairly level and maybe composed
of fills from the road construction
A recent local landslide was found in the northern portion of
the property A possible ancient landslide was also observed
on the western slope of the steep gully just above the planned
residential development The shallow ancient landslide was
observed on aerial photographs approximately five years old
However the recent slide on the northern section of the prop
erty was not observed in these photos and is less than five
years old
Groundwater seepage was not observed during our reconnaissance
however the soils appeared to be in a wet condition and the
potential for underground seepage may exist

General

may be developed for the proposed duplex units The two slide
areas both the recent and the ancient slide that were

observed during our reconnaissance appears to be located above

The lower level portion of the site may be developed by normal

Slopes
The site except for the two noted slide areas appears to be in
a stable condition We cannot at this time fully evaluate the
landslide conditions but based on our knowledge of slopes and
residual soils it is our opinion that these landslides may
have been due to excessive water seepage in combination with
failure occurring along the fracture planes and joints inherited
from the parent basalt bedrock It is our opinion that these
slide areas can be investigated evaluated and a remedy
recommended to overcome the instability of these local areas
A proposal will be submitted outlining the investigations
necessary to evaluate the local and overall stability of the
existing slopes
Foundations
It is our opinion that the site soils can be used to support
the proposed structures The type of foundations will depend
on building design and foundation loads But based upon our

geologic reconnaissance we anticipate the use of shallow

The site soils are generally moderately expansive so therefore
footings and slabs on grade should be designed for expansive

Building pads should be so designed that foundation are seated
entirely in fill or cut to reduce differential settlements
We do not anticipate settlement problems with foundations seated

determined during the second phase investigation

Gentlemen
In accordance with your request we reviewed the preliminary plans
and profiles for Retaining Walls and 4 at the above project
This letter summarizes the recommended bearing capacities and earth
pressure design parameters for these walls
Figure 1 presents the recommended design loads and allowable bearing
pressures for the four walls assuming that on site soils are uti
lized for backfill of the retaining walls These pressures are
higher than normally anticipated due to the affects of the uphill
slope
In accordance with our meeting at your office we have
also included Figure 2 which presents the recommended earth pressure
design loads for the same walls assuming that selected granular
material is utilized for the backfill This granular material
should be a free draining material with a minimum friction angle of
32 degrees and less than 10 percent passing a No 200 sieve

Gentlemen
After a discussion with Mr Clarence Tanonaka we are pleased to
submit our revised proposal to perform a soil investigation for
the subject property
It is presently planned to develop the lower western portion of
a 20 acre parcel located north of Mokapu Saddle Road in Kaneohe
Oahu
Anticipated site grading will involve maximum fills and cuts on
the order of 15 feet Based on a site visit and review of the
general geology we believe the soil conditions at the site consist
of residual soils or colluvium that should be very uniform
Due to the time schedule for submission of your preliminary plans
we propose to investigate the project in two phases The first
phase will be limited to an evaluation study based on a site
and geologic reconnaissance The second phase will be supplemented
by drilling exploratory borings and obtaining on site soil samples
for testing and amalysis

Phase 1
Preliminary Soil Report for Planning Purposes
For the first phase a geologist or engineer from our firm will
study the surface features at the site Our geologic reconnaissance
will concern itself with the stability of existing slopes and
feasibility of the proposed development Based on the results of
our field reconnaissance we would provide you with a written
report presenting our evaluations for planning purposes
Phase II
Final Soil Investigation and Recommendations for Design Purposes
We believe a total of 4 to 5 borings will be necessary to supplement
the geologic reconnaissance study and to obtain on site subsurface
soil conditions
It is anticipated that portable drilling equipment will have to
be hand carried up the densely vegetated sloping terrain The
drilling operations will be continuously supervised by our engineer
who will also log the borings obtain undisturbed samples of the
encountered soil or rock and make detailed observations of the
subsurface site conditions with special emphasis on the slopes
and low lying area Laboratory tests will be performed on selected
samples to evaluate their strength compressibility and other
characteristics
Based on the results of our field investigation and laboratory
test data engineering analysis would be performed to evaluate
stability of the proposed cuts and fills and foundation requirements
for the 2 to 3 story duplex construction Requirements for grading
operations and particularly use of on site excavated material
as fill will also be evaluated Upon completion of our work the
results of our two investigations will be summarized in a final
written report to you along with supporting data
