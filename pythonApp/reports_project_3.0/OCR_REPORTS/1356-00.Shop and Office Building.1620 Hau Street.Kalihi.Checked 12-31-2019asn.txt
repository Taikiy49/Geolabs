SOILS INVESTIGATION
PROPOSED SHOP AND OFFICE BUILDING
1620 HAU STREET
This report presents the results of our soils investigation
performed for the proposed shop and office building at 1620 Hau Street
Oahu Hawaii The general location of the project site and its vicinity
is shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
The purpose of our study was to investigate the subsurface
conditions by drilling and sampling borings Initially only the front
portion of the site was explored because the existing structures limited
access to the other portion of the site After the structures were
demolished and the site cleared the remaining area was explored Data
obtained from borings and laboratory testing of soil samples were
utilized to provide recommendations for the design of the foundation of
the subject project
W O 1356 00 Page 2
Our work was performed in general accordance with the scope of
services outlined in our proposal dated
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
PROJECT CONSIDERATION
It is proposed to construct a two story shop and office building of
concrete masonry construction with concrete on grade slabs A concrete
driveway is also planned
The maximum foundation loadings are anticipated to be on the order
of 15 kips per column and 2 kips per linear foot for wall footings
The proposed layout of the building is shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2
SITE CONDITIONS
The site was previously occupied by wooden dwellings and a large
tree The front yard was paved with asphalt concrete and the western
side yard area was concreted and used as driveway and automobile
parking The ground was relatively level and generally appeared to be 1
to 2 feet lower than the sidewalk level

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Subsurface Exploration
Three borings and eleven probes were drilled and sampled to depths
between 2 0 and 15 0 feet below the ground surface to explore the
subsurface conditions at the approximate locations shown on the Site
before the existing structures were demolished and the remaining borings
and probes were drilled later Detailed descriptions of our field
exploration are presented in the appendix along with the boring logs and
laboratory test results
The borings and probes disclosed that the site is mantled by a thin
layer of stiff brown clayey silt overlying moderately hard tan white
cemented coral All probes indicated coral within a depth of 2 feet
except Probes P 2 and P 3 adjacent to Boring B 1 The clayey silt layer
generally varied from 0 5 to 2 0 feet thick and exhibited a low swelling
potential in our laboratory tests The coral consisted mainly of
cemented coral sand and gravel which is believed to be coral algal reef
detritus and extended to at least 15 feet below the ground the maximum
depth drilled Generally it is believed the coral extends to a
substantial depth in this area It should be noted that the coral
surface may contain occasional cavities As disclosed in Boring B 1
area the depth to coral rock varied from 2 to 5 feet within short
distances indicating that such a surface cavity may have been
encountered The cavity was probably filled with soft and very loose
grayish brown silty coral sand with some gravel

Groundwater was not encountered in the borings at the time of our
exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
It is anticipated that for drainage consideration fills up to 2
feet would be required to raise the grade of the site
Our findings indicate that the site is predominantly mantled with a
thin layer of stiff clayey silt underlain by coral
Spread Footing Foundation
For the proposed structure spread footing foundations bearing on
the underlying dense coral are recommended
An allowable soil bearing of 6000 p s f may be used for footing
bearing on coral The bearing may be increased by one third when
considering temporary wind or seismic loads A minimun footing size of
16 inches should be used
Non load bearing walls may be placed on new compacted fill or
recompacted on site soil These wall footings sheculd be embedded a
minimum of 12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade and be designed for
an allowable soil bearing pressure of 2500 pesef
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 1356 00 Page 5
It is anticipated that the total and differential foundation
settlements if constructed according to the above design criteria
should be negligible
We recommend that all footing excavations be inspected by a soils
engineer to confirm the conditions upon which our design recommendations
were based
It is anticipated that the perimeter walls would retain thin layers
of new 111 under the slabs and act as low retaining walls Therefore
the walls should be considered as restrained walls and be designed to
lateral earth pressures of 55 p s f per foot of depth below the top of
the interior slabs To resist this lateral loading a base friction
factor of 0 45 between the footing and the coral should be used
Passive earth pressures of 300 p s f per foot of depth may be
considered in further resisting lateral forces for interior footings
However the passive earth pressures on the perimeter footings that may
be available from adjacent lots should be ignored since future
construction activity may remove the available lateral support
Site Preparation
Prior to filling the site the existing tree stump and the roots in
excess of 1 5 inches in diameter should be grubbed and the resulting
excavation backfilled with compacted on site material The loosely
filled cavity suspected in the Boring B 1 area should be exposed prior to
being excavated and backfilled with compacted fill
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Page 6
We recommend that the entire site be scarified to a depth of at
least 6 inches moisture conditioned to within 3 percentage points of the
optimum and recompacted to at least 90 of the ASTM D 1557 78 maximum
density Any soft or yielding areas disclosed during this work should be
repaired by removing the soft soils and backfilling with compacted fill
as deemed necessary
Fill Construction
It is anticipated that imported fill will be required The fill
material should be non expansive soil such as coral cinder select
borrow or crusher waste and should not contain rock fragments larger
than 6 inches in size The material should be tested and approved by the
soils engineer before it is hauled to the site
All 111 and backfill material should be placed in horizontal lifts
not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a dry density
of at least 90 of the ASTM D 1557 78 maximum density
Cavity Probing and Grouting
It should be noted that the coral rock frequently contains filled
and unfilled voids or cavities After the site is cleared for building
construction the foundation area especially below the footings should
be probed for possible cavity or cavities
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1356 00 Page 7
It is recommended that a 3 inch diameter probe hole be drilled at
each columm location or on 8 foot centers along continuous wall footings
as appropriate The probe holes should be drilled to a minimum of 5 feet
into the coral rock The resulting probe holes should be backfilled with
neat cement grout mix injected at a low pressure
If cavities are encountered they should be grouted with cement or
concrete grout with a compressive strength of at least 2000 p S i For
small cavities with height of less than 6 inches measured at the probe
location cement grout should be used For cavities with height
exceeding 6 inches concrete grout may be used In such a case the
probe hole may need to be enlarged
If excessive grout take is noted filling should be temporary
suspended to allow the grout to set Thereafter the filling should then
be resumed The temporary suspension of filling is to limit the
excessive grout take
We recommend that the probing and grouting operations be monitored
by a soils engineer
On Grade Slab
On grade floor slab may be used for the proposed building after the
site has been scarified recompacted and filled The existing brown
clayey silt is low to moderately expansive Therefore the soil should
be kept moist to minimize its expansion potential
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Page 8
The on grade floor slab should be placed over a 4 inch cushion layer
of gravel size crushed rock material such as No 3 Fine rock to provide
uniform support A moisture barrier membrane should be placed under the
Slabs
The slab thickened edges exposed to any unpaved area should be
embedded at least 8 inches below the lowest adjacent grade
Concrete slabs are also planned for the driveway The base course
for the driveway slabs should consist of coral select borrow or crusher
waste of at least 4 inches thick compacted to a dry density of at least
95 of the ASTM D 1557 78 maximum
Design Review
Plans and specifications for site grading cavity probing and
grouting foundation and pavement should be forwarded to the soils
engineer for review and written comments prior to construction to see
that the intent of our recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the grubbing site preparation cavity
probing and grouting engineered fill placement and compaction
foundation excavation backfilling and subgrade preparation be observed
by the soils engineer during construction This is to see that
adequate compaction has been obtained and that the subsurface
conditions are consistent with the findings of our investigation
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1356 00 Page 9
If conditions encountered during construction are different from
those presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made
INVESTIGATION LIMITATIONS
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations
made in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation
engineering principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all
other warranties either expressed or implied
The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are
based on our site reconnaissance soil information from boring probing
and laboratory test data obtained from this preliminary soil
investigation
Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encountered and cannot
be fully determined by reconnaissance and borings These unforeseen
conditions such as pockets of organic soils trash fill or expansive
soils may occur in localized areas Therefore additional expenditures
may be needed during construction to attain a properly constructed
project Some contingency fund is thus recommended to accommodate
these possible costs
This report has been prepared in order to assist the engineers in
the preparation of the site grading plans and foundation design of this
project

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
ed
The subsurface conditions at the lot were explored by drilling 3
borings and 11 probes at the approximate locations shown on the Site
Plan Plate 2 A truck mounted drill rig was used to drill the borings
before the old dwelling was demolished The remainder of the borings
and the probes were drilled after demolition when the site was made
accessible to our truck mounted rig
The soils encountered were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by one of our engineers who continuously
monitored the excavating operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory The soils
encountered in the borings are presented in the Boring Logs Plates A 1
through A 3 The soils encountered in the probes and depths to the top
of the coral are presented on Plate A 4 All soils were classified in
accordance with the Unified Soil Classification System
In the borings soil samples were obtained by driving a 2 inch
standard split spoon or a 2 4 inch I D split barrel sampler with a
140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts used to drive the
samplers for 12 inches are shown on the boring logs at the appropriate
sample depths
