PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The proposed Ho’opili Development project is to the south of the Interstate Route 
H-1 Highway and west of Fort Weaver Road in the Ewa area on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii, 
as shown on the Project Location Map, Plate 1. The Ho`opili development encompasses 
approximately 1,500 acres. The project site is gently sloping and was previously used for 
agricultural cultivation. 
The off-site sewer improvements consisted of installing approximately 6,800 linear 
feet of sewer lines along future Roads “4”, “B”, “C”, “9” and “E” from the existing Farrington 
Highway to the Ho`opili Development Phase 2, as shown on the Site Plan, Plate 2. The 
sizes of the new sewer lines varied from 8 to 24 inches in diameter. 
The grading construction of the improvements generally consisted of trench 
excavations for sewer installation and backfills, cuts and fills, and access road subgrade 
preparations. Royal Contracting performed the earthwork operations for the project.

SITE GRADING 
At the onset of earthwork, areas within the contract grading limits were generally 
cleared and grubbed of vegetation and deleterious materials. Materials generated from 
the clearing operations were disposed of off-site. Following the clearing and grubbing 
operations, the areas to be cut were excavated. Finished grades were compacted using 
a sheepsfoot and/or smooth vibratory drum roller. 
Soft and yielding areas encountered during site grading were generally 
over-excavated to expose firm natural material, and the resulting excavation was backfilled 
with well-compacted engineering fill. 
The fill and backfill materials were moisture-conditioned to above the optimum 
moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density 
as determined in accordance with ASTM D1557. In the roadway areas, the subgrade was 
compacted to a minimum 95 percent of the maximum dry density with moisture content 
of 2 percent or greater above the optimum moisture content. 
During the site grading operations, samples of the materials utilized as fill were 
obtained and transported to our laboratory for testing.  The maximum dry densities and 
optimum moisture contents were established in general accordance with ASTM D1557 
(Modified Proctor), and the test results are summarized on Plate 3. 
Periodic in-situ moisture and density tests were conducted in general accordance 
with ASTM D6938 (Nuclear Gauge Method), to determine the adequacy of compaction of 
fills and backfills for infrastructure and utility installations.  
The test results are summarized on Plates 4.1 through 4.31. These tests were 
performed on moisture-conditioned and compacted lifts of fills and backfills.  Test 
locations and elevations were estimated using on-site survey stakes for reference.  These 
locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the 
methods used. Areas deemed unsatisfactory were reworked until the measured relative 
compaction conformed to or exceeded the minimum specified for the project. 
This report summarizes our field observations and compaction testing 
services during the site grading work for the period covering August 30, 2019 through 
May 20, 2021. 
Based on our field representative’s observations and the referenced test results, it 
is our opinion that the site grading for Ho’opili Development – IMX Off-Site Sewer 
Improvements project, observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this 
report, were performed in accordance with the general intent of the recommendations in 
the following references.
