This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations and testing
services performed during site grading for the Manele Bay Resort Drainage Improvements
Debris Basin and Culvert C6 2 from to August 2 2007
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project involved constructing drainage improvements to handle storm run off
and associated debris loads The drainage improvements included Debris Basin DW6
and associated Culvert C6 2 Earthwork for the project primarily consisted of excavating
the drainageway channel and sediment basins and excavating and backfilling the
concrete box culvert
The project location and general vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map
Plate 1 The grading plan for the site is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services for
the site grading operations
SITE GRADING AND UTILITY INSTALLATION
Geolabs observed the site grading from April 2007 to August 2007 The site grading
work consisted of clearing and grubbing excavation of the debris basin and placement
and compaction of backfills around the box culvert and culvert entrance Royal Contracting
Company performed the site grading and backfilling work During the site grading
operations samples of the on site and imported materials utilized as fill and backfill
materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing The maximum dry
densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general
accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test results are summarized
on Plate 3

Fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as
determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557 In the roadway areas the fill and backfill
materials within 2 feet of the finished subgrade were compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative density
During the site grading and utility backfilling operations periodic field density tests
were performed in general accordance with ASTM D 2922 Nuclear Gauge Method
Compaction tests results are summarized on Plates 4 1 through 4 3 These tests were
performed on the prepared over excavated surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills
Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site
survey stakes These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the
degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked
until the specified minimum compaction was obtained and or approved by our field
representative based on visual observation and hand probing
Our services consisted of part time field observation of the contractor s fill
placement subgrade preparation and compaction procedures and confirmation of
subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above grading operations
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion the site grading and utility installation work for the
drainage improvements project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by
this report were performed in conformance with the general intent of the
recommendations in the following references and the approved plans and specifications
1 Report by Geolabs Inc entitled Manele Bay Resort Drainage
Improvements Phase 1 Manele Island of Lanai Hawaii dated
