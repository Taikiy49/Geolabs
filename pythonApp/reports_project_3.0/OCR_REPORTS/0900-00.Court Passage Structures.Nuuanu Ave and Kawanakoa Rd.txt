INTRODUCTION
This report presents our recommendations for the foundation
design and construction of the proposed one story court
passage structures Subsurface conditions at the site
were explored by drilling a total of three borings ranging
in depth from 6 5 to 17 0 feet The approximate location
of the borings is shown on the Site Plan Plate 1 and the
description of the materials encountered in the borings
is presented on the Boring Logs Plates 2 through 4
Selected samples were subject to various tests in our
laboratory and the test results are presented on Plates
5 and 6

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed one story court passage structures would
consist of light weight concrete construction Elevations
at the site ranges from 206 5 on east side to 199 0 feet
on west side Finish floor elevation is planned at 204 0
feet Therefore cuts as much as 2 5 feet and fill as much
as 5 0 feet would be required

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Foundation Support
Based on the results of our field investigation laboratory
test results and engineering analysis the site is considered
Suitable for the proposed construction Foundation support
could be achieved by placing spread footings or continuous
wall footings either on undisturbed reddish brown clayey
Silt or highly decomposed basalt formation underlying the
surface fill materials
Footings could be designed using an allowable bearing pressure
infrequently applied wind and seismic loads The footings
should have a minimum width of 12 inches and be embedded
a minimum depth of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent
grade
GEOLABS HAWAII
Church of Perfect Liberty P 4
For foundations designed as recommended herein the anti
Cipated settlements would be relatively small on the
order of about three eighth of an inch
Slab On Grade
Low to moderate expansive soils were encountered during
our exploration The near surface clayey fill material
indicated low expansion potential of less than one per
cent while the natural reddish brown clayey silt under
lying the surface fill materials is moderately expansive
with about four percent Swelling during the laboratory
testing
We recommend that a 4 inch layer of granular material
such as No 3B Fine Rock be provided under the slab
To further protect the slab from ground moisture and
dampness infiltration an impermeable moisture barrier
may be incorporated under the slab
Earthwork
Earthwork operations at the site will include excavation
backfilling and foundation inspection during construction
The required foundation excavation would extend to depths
of approximately 1 0 to 3 5 feet below the existing ground
surface It is anticipated that conventional earthmoving
equipments could be used to conduct all the excavations
for the foundations
GEOLABS HAWAII
Church of Perfect Liberty P 5
Excavated surface fill material is suitable and can be
re used as a source of controlled compacted fill and
backfill The underlying reddish brown clayey silt
being moderately expansive is not recommended to be
used as fill under the slab area However this
material could be stockpiled for landscaping purposes
if required Excavations should be protected from
surface runoff which may enter the excavations All
loose soils or mud should be removed from the bottom
of all footing excavations prior to concrete placement
Backfilling
Some minor backfilling operations would be required
around the footings and under the floor slabs
It is our opinion that excavated on site materials
except for the reddish brown clayey silt within the
Slab area may be re used as backfill material provided
it is free from debris vegetation and deleterious
material Fill and backfill should be placed in hori
zontal 10 inch lifts and compacted to at least 95 per
cent of maximum dry density within the limits of structures
and 90 percent outside the structure areas as obtained
using ASTM D 1557 70 modified Proctor standard

Foundation Inspection
It is recommended that all the footing excavations and
filling operations for slab on grade be inspected by the
soils engineer to verify adequate compaction and that
the subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings
of our subsurface investigation
Our present recommendations are contingent upon the above
inspection and testing
