EARTHWORK OPERATIONS
Royal Contracting Company Ltd and Hunt Building Company Ltd performed the
fine grading operations site retaining wall construction site and house pad utility
installation and carport driveway subdivision roadway pavement construction for the
project development The grading operations consisted of clearing and grubbing of
existing vegetation subgrade preparation placement and compaction of fills and backfills
pavement construction and utility trench excavation and backfill
Following the clearing and grubbing operations the subgrades were generally
moisture conditioned to about 2 percent above the optimum moisture content and
recompacted to at least 95 percent of the maximum dry density as determined in
accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor
During placement of the general fill the fill materials were generally placed in
near level lifts brought to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a
minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density determined in accordance with
ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor The base course cushion under the AC pavement was
generally placed in near level lifts brought to above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to a minimum of 95 percent of the maximum dry density determined in
accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor
New utility lines were installed to serve the new development In general a bedding
layer consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel was provided below the piping and
manholes for uniform support Free draining granular materials consisting of No 3B Fine
gravel was also used for the trench backfill up to about 12 inches above the pipes to
provide adequate support around the pipes and to reduce the compaction effort on the
initial trench backfill The trench backfill was placed in 8 inch level loose lifts and
mechanically compacted to no less than 90 percent relative compaction Where trenches
are below pavement areas the upper 2 feet of the trench backfill below the pavement
subgrade was compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
During the trench excavation for the 8 inch sewer line in the vicinity of
Station 3 50 to Station 4 00 along Road B a coral ledge was encountered at about
7 feet below the existing ground surface Some voids and dry powdery coralline materials
were observed below the coral ledge Since it is impractical to backfill the voids
underneath the coral ledge with compacted materials a cavity probing and grouting
program was implemented alongside the afore mentioned stationing to delineate the
