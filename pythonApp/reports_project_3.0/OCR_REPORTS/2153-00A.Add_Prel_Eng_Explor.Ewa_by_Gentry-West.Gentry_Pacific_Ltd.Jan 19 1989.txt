INTRODUCTION
This addendum report presents the results of our additional soil
exploration performed for the subject project This exploration was a
continuation of the work performed in our Preliminary Soil Engineering
Exploration W O 2153 00 report dated
The purpose of our additional exploration was to provide more
detailed subsurface soil data for use in the design of the site grading
house foundations and road pavement for the proposed development
The scope of our work included the drilling and sampling of seven 7
additional borings to depths between 16 5 and 26 5 feet below the existing
ground surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate
2 Also included were laboratory testing of selected samples obtained
development of design recommendations and report preparation
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration and laboratory testing
are presented in the appendices of this report

Foundations
The field exploration disclosed that the site is underlain by a
surface layer of medium stiff to hard alluvial clayey silts and silty
clays overlying loose to dense hard coralline deposits The thickness of
the upper surface clayey layer was found to vary from twelve 12 feet in
the northern portion of the site to about four 4 feet in the southern
portions The laboratory tests performed indicate that the alluvial soils
are primarily clays with low to moderate swelling potential with some
areas exhibiting high swelling potential
Laboratory swell test values on one inch thick air dried clay samples
ranged from 2 6 to 15 when saturated under a surcharge load of 55
p s f loading conditions which are equivalent to a _ conventional
slab on grade Visually it is not readily possible to distinguish
between the low and high expansive clays Such critical differentiation
may be verified by further laboratory testing
The underlying coralline material has low expansion potential and is
considered an excellent source of fill and foundation support Therefore
in cut areas where the upper clay soils are removed and fill areas where
the upper two 2 feet of f1i11 consists of coralline material
conventional foundation systems may be used
Where the house foundations will bear on the surface clay soils
special subgrade preparation is recommended to reduce the effect of the
swelling soils The subgrade preparation would consist of removal and
replacement of some of the surface clay soils below the houses and
driveways
In order to minimize the costs for the over excavation and
replacement every effort should be made to utilize the coralline material
for fill within two 2 feet of finished grade

The expansion of the moderately expansive on site subgrade soils may
be reduced by capping it with a minimm of twelve 12 inches of
non expansive select material The capping should extend at least
three 3 feet beyond the perimeter of the proposed structure
The select material for the non expansive capping layer should be
low expansion granular material such as crushed coral The material
should be less than 3 inch in size with a laboratory CBR swell value
not exceeding 1 and CBR value of 20 or more It should not contain
large percentages of boulders organics or other deleterious
material

The select material should be examined and tested by the geotechnical
engineer prior to its use at the project site
Prior to the placement of the capping fill the bottom of the
excavation should be scarified to a depth of twelve 12 inches
moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture content and
recompacted to 85 of its maximm dry density determined by ASTM
D 1557 78
The capping fill should be placed in horizontal lifts moisture
conditioned to slightly above optimm moisture content and compacted
within a range of 85 to 90 of its maximm dry density as determined
by ASTM D 1557 78
The capping fill should be placed as soon as possible after the
scarification and recompaction of the existing soil This will
create a cap of moist soil above the potentially expansive soils and
thereby reduce the drying of the underlying clayey subgrade soils
The reduction in drying will reduce the potential swelling of the
expansive clay soils
Conventional slab on grade construction may then be utilized for the
proposed structures on the above recommended 12 inch thick capping
select fill
The slab subgrade areas should be wetted and kept moist prior to
concrete placement Moisture barriers and capillary breaks should be
incorporated below the slab to prevent damage to the floor coverings
by moisture infiltration
The thickened edges of the slabs should extend a minimm of twelve
12 inches below the lowest adjacent outside grade

Pavement Design
Based on the results of our field exploration previous experience
and laboratory test results the recommended pavement section is presented
below
2 Inch Asphaltic concrete
6 inch Base Course
10 Inch Select Borrow Sub base
18 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
Note Select borrow sub base course may be omitted where coralline
sand and gravel are present or a minimum of two 2 feet of
select import soil is used below the road subgrade
Select borrow should also be incorporated below sidewalks and
driveways to reduce potential heaving

The base course should be crushed rock or other granular material
capable of developing a field CBR value of 85 or more and conforming with
Section 31 Standard Specifications for Public Works construction The
select borrow should be crusher run mudrock coral sand or cinder with
less than 15 passing the 200 sieve and compacted to 95 of its maximum
density The top six 6 inches of the subgrade soil should be scarified
and recompacted wet of optimm moisture to 95 compaction The subgrade
soil should not be allowed to become dry and to develop shrinkage cracks
prior to the completion of the pavement placement
The subgrade and pavement sections should be shaped to drain
subsurface water away from the pavement into suitable drain outlets We
recommend that weepholes be installed in the catch basins at the base
course level in order to allow drainage of accumulated subsurface water

Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comment
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to

is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for
misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during site grading and
construction of the foundation phases of the work This is to observe
compliance with the design concepts specifications or recommendations and
to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ
from that anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations
given in this report are contingent upon such observations

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
upon information obtained from the borings Variations of conditions
between the borings may occur and the nature and extent of these
variations may not become evident until construction If variations then
appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations
given in this report
The locations of the borings were determined by taping from
structures indicated on the topographic survey Elevations of the borings
were approximately determined by interpolation from elevation points on
the topographic survey map The physical location and elevation of the
borings should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the
method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
boring points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types and
as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross sections are based upon interpolation between borings
and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
Water level data from the boring points were collected at the times
given on the graphic representations and or in the text of this report
These data have been reviewed and interpretations made in the formulation
of this report However it must be noted that fluctuation may occur due
to variations in rainfall tides temperature and other factors

samples of the near surface soils to determine maximum dry density
optimum moisture content and suitability for pavement support The test
results are summarized on Plate B 2
