Dear Ms Walker
In an effort to find an economical method to reduce dust and erosion of the
existing dirt roads it is our understanding that a thin layer of coral will be placed over
the dirt road surface The performance of the coral topping will be monitored to
evaluate its suitability for future use
Project Considerations
Currently Hale Olono Road is unpaved and thus dust control and erosion are a
concern To reduce these concerns it is proposed to place a compacted layer of coral
select borrow material over the existing roadway
Existing Site Conditions
Currently the road is unpaved Based on available maps it appears that the road
traverses some steep slopes Based on prevailing conditions of nearby areas it is likely
that the road traverses through tall grass kiawe trees brush and weeds
Subsurface Conditions
Based on the Soil Survey conducted by the United States Department of
Agriculture Soil Conservation Service the on site surface soils generally consist of dark
red brown silty clay soil MH CL underlain by soft weathered rock Closer inland the soil
generally has a moderate swell potential Further toward the sea the soil generally has a
moderate to high swell potential Near the sea the soil contains more boulders and
cobbles than soil material and contains some exposed rock outcrops

Site Gradin
Items of grading that are addressed in the following sections include 1 Site
Preparation 2 Fills and Backfills and 3 Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
Site grading operations should be observed by a_ representative of
Geolabs Hawaii It is important that a representative from our office observe the grading
operations to evaluate whether undesirable materials are encountered during the site
preparation or filling process
Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork if vegetation debris rubbish or other unsuitable
materials are encountered at the original ground surface it should be removed and
disposed of properly off site
Soft and yielding areas should be over excavated to expose firm material and the
resulting excavation should be backfilled with properly compacted fill material
The excavated soft soils should be disposed of properly off site
Prior to filling the subgrade should be moistened and proof rolled until a firm
surface is achieved that does not weave or creep under the weight of the compactor
It should be noted that it is likely that the surface soils are very dry due to windy
conditions therefore much watering may be necessary
Fills and Backfills
The coral material should be well graded from coarse to fine with no particles
larger than 3 inches in largest dimension and should contain between 10 and 25 percent
particles passing the No 200 sieve The material should also have no more than
35 percent particles passing the No 40 sieve The material should have a laboratory
CBR value of 20 or more and should have a maximum swell value of one percent or less
The silty coral gravel from the Grace Pacific Quarry appears to meet these requirements
Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
Fills should be moisture conditioned to at least 3 percent above optimum moisture
placed in level lifts to achieve a 6 inch compacted thickness and compacted to at least
95 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of
soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established
in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91
After compaction is achieved watering of the compacted surface should be
conducted to encourage cementing action Note that in areas that are rocky a
compacted lift thickness in excess of 6 inches may be necessary to level out high and low
