As of this date the above referenced lots have been constructed
to final grade and generally compacted adequately The compaction

The Increment II portion of the site generally consisted of
three separate relatively deep drainageways which connected
near the northern portion of the site The general grading con
cept consisted of cutting the upper ridge areas adjacent to the
drainageways and filling two of the lower drainageway areas to
form relatively fiat terraced lot grades The third drainage
way between Phase I and Phase II was left ungraded
Moderately deep swampy soil conditions were encountered in the
lower reaches of the drainageways Buttress fills extending
through the soft swampy soils were utilized to increase the
factor of safety against slope movement of the high embankment
fills over the soft soils The remainder of the swamp area was
stabilized with a synthetic filter fabric and gravel subdrainage
system The upper fill material consisted of on site material
from the upper cut areas
To reduce future settlement a surcharge fill was placed above

RECOMMENDATIONS
Building Foundations
Foundations and slabs should be setback a minimum of 6 feet
away from the top of cut or fill slopes to reduce the detrimen
tal effects of slope creep and erosion
Footings located on slopes should penctrate down to firm soil
in order to develop a minimum horizontal setback distance of
6 feet measured from the outer edge of the footing foundation
out to the slope face Individual footings on level areas should
be embedded a minimum of 8 inches below the ground surface All
footing backfill should be properly compacted to obtain 85
compaction and graded to drain water away from the footing The
soils engineer should inspect the bottom of all footing excava
tions prior to concrete placement

Slab On Grade
Slab on grade construction can be utilized on the cut and fill
lots for the remainder of the lots in Increment iI provided
the slabs can be setback at least 6 feet from the tops of slopes
The on site soil has low to moderate expansion potential Con
crete floor slab should be reinforced with a minimum of
6 x 6 8 x 8 welded wire fabric and underlain by a 4 inch thick
base course layer Slabs which will have moisture sensitive
floor covering shculd be protected by a zolyethyiene vapor
barrier
Twelve inch deep perimeter footings are recommended to act as
a cut off wall against the future subgrade moisture changes due
to drying and wetting around the slab

Off Site Drainage Ditch
Field observations after heavy rains indicated that surface
water appears to enter the project site in the vicinty of Lot
21 We have recommended previously that a berm or drainage
ditch be constructed to divert this surface water away from
the lots to reduce future runoff and possible erosion of the
lots
Individual Lot Drainage
Some amount of water ponding was noted on Lots 7 to
and 20 These lots should be fine graded to drain water away
It is our understanding that drainage swales will be constructed
later by the house contractor It is imperative that all the
lots be graded or drainage swales provided to drain water away
from the proposed house to prevent saturation and softening of
the subgrade soils
