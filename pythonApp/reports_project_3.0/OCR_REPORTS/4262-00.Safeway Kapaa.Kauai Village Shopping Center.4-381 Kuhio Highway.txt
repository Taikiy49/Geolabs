Preliminary Assessment
Based upon the limited available information we believe that the floor damage is
due to slight frost heaving and settling within Aisle No 3 We believe that the floor
damage is also due to an increase in moisture content near the floor elevation The
increase in moisture likely led to the adhesion problems with the floor coverings
especially with the weaker bonding environmentally friendly adhesives used today
Damage due to frost heaving may occur when the following three conditions are
satisfied
1 Presence of a frost susceptible soil
2 Presence of freezing temperatures
3 Presence of nearby supply of water
Generally silts and silty sands are considered as frost susceptible soils while
clays clean sands and clean gravels are considered as non frost susceptible soils
The movement of water within these soils is controlled by the following three
mechanisms

The on site soils are generally a silty sand material and therefore may be
considered as frost susceptible Although ice lenses were not encountered in the
excavations minor frost heaving can occur due to expansion of ice within the pore
water spaces and without an accumulation of ice lenses
The possible sources of water are the water table about 5 to 7 feet below grade
and the water from the drain pipes The absence of a capillary break material such as
No 3B Fine material that is typically found beneath slabs on grade may have
contributed to the migration of water to the concrete slab The location of the wire
fabric near the bottom of the slab instead of at the middle of the slab likely limited the
crack resistance of the slab from upheaval
Damage due to settlement may occur if the frozen water melts or if the drain
water from the pipes leaks as the water could soften the soil However based on the
continual use of the freezers it is unlikely that frozen water would melt Water leaking
from drain pipes may be a possible cause and may explain why only the front area is
experiencing distress
Settlement due to other distress mechanisms is unlikely due to relatively light
loads conditions that have remained relatively unchanged since prior to the last
renovation a relatively thick concrete slab and fairly adequate subsurface material
Also if settlement was the cause of the distress then the cracks should have been
centered around the freezer refrigerator units on both sides
Preliminary Recommendations
Based on the available information we believe that the damage is mostly caused
by adhesion problems with the floor coverings to the concrete with slight frost heaving
Some of the damage may be due to settlement from soil softening from increased
moisture however since settlement of sandy materials is relatively rapid and since the
loading is not changing additional large settlement is not anticipated
We do not perceive the damage as catastrophic or rapidly progressing
therefore we believe that a less aggressive solution should be attempted first We
recommend that the cracks be sealed properly with a sealant followed by a cement
wash of the slab surface Epoxy adhesive or some adhesive with high bond strength
