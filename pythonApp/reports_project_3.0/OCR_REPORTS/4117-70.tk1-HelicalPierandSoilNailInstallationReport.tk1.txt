PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is at the existing recreation center within Asing Community Park in
the Ewa area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project location and general vicinity are
shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 We understand that various types of
distresses have been observed at the existing facility as described below
Separation of the building walls from the ceiling at the top
Vertical cracking at the corners of the building walls
Separation of the building walls from the interior patio slabs and exterior
sidewalk slabs
Differential settlement between the building walls and the patio slabs
A geotechnical engineering report entitled Geotechnical Engineering Exploration
Recreation Building Distress Evaluation Asing Community Park
dated has been prepared by our office to present our findings
discussions and recommendations with respect to the observed building distresses based
on our document review site reconnaissance field exploration laboratory testing and

During the project construction trial installation of a helical anchor by the
subcontractor indicated that the anchor could only be advanced to a vertical depth of
approximately 8 feet due to the very stiff hard consistency of the soils Due to the limited
embedment depth observed for the trial anchor it was decided to replace the helical
tieback anchor system with injection anchors in order to provide the design tensile load
capacity In addition the load test program was revised to one compression load test on a
sacrificial helical pier and one tension load test on a sacrificial injection anchor No proof
test will be conducted for the project due to the concern for potential damage to the
existing building walls during testing

INJECTION ANCHOR INSTALLATION
As previously indicated the helical tieback anchors were replaced with injection
anchors due to the stiff soil conditions encountered at the site which limited the
penetration depth of the tieback anchors The injection anchor was composed of
galvanized R38 hollow core bar with a 3 5 inch drill bit The injection anchors were drilled
to a length of about 16 feet at inclinations of about 15 degrees from horizontal The grout
used for the injection anchor generally consisted of one sack of cement and five gallons of
water with a water cement ratio of about 0 44
Tension Load Test
In accordance with the project specifications and subsequent modifications a
sacrificial injection anchor designated as LT IA was installed by Structural Systems Inc
on August 17 2011 using a Beretta T59 drill rig The load test injection anchor is located
in the open area near Gridline No 1 as shown on the Foundation Location Plan Plate 2
Based on our field observations the load test injection anchor was installed to a length of
about 16 feet below the ground surface
A tension load test was conducted on the load test injection anchor on
