In accordance with our fee proposal dated January 10 we have conducted a
geotechnical engineering exploration for the proposed Athletic Field Maintenance Storage
Building at University of Hawaii at Manoa in Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii This
report summarizes our work on the project and presents our findings and geotechnical
engineering recommendations derived from our analyses for the subject project
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located between an existing football drill exercise area and a
comfort room at University of Hawaii at Manoa in Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii
The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map
Plate 1
Based on the information provided we understand that the project will involve the
construction of a pre engineered building with metal siding and masonry wall around the
perimeter for protection against damage Site grading will consist of about 2 feet of fill over
the building site

of drilling and sampling four borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 4 extending to a
depth of about 16 5 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate locations of the
drilled borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2

In general our field exploration disclosed that the project site is underlain by a surface
fill layer consisting of medium stiff to very stiff brown silty clay with gravel of approximately
3 to 8 feet thick Below the surface fill layer our borings generally encountered medium dense
coralline silty sand extending to the maximum depth drilled
Shallow groundwater levels were encountered in the drilled borings at depths of about
6 to 10 feet below the existing ground surface at the time of our field exploration It should be
noted that groundwater levels are subject to change due to seasonal precipitation
temperature groundwater withdrawal and other factors
Detailed descriptions of the subsurface materials encountered from our field exploration
are presented on the Boring Log Legend and Logs of Borings Plates 3 through 7 of this report
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the subsurface soil conditions encountered at the project site we believe
that shallow spread and continuous footings bearing on properly compacted fill may be
used to support the proposed maintenance storage building planned at the site
Detailed discussion of these items and our geotechnical engineering design
recommendations are presented in the following sections of this report
Foundations
Based on the anticipated subsurface soil conditions at the project site we believe that
shallow spread and continuous footing foundations may be used for support of the proposed
maintenance storage building An allowable bearing pressure of 2 500 pounds per square foot
psf may be used for the foundations bearing on properly compacted fill The bearing value
those caused by wind or seismic forces
In order to provide a uniform supporting layer for the structure we recommend that a
minimum of 2 feet of structural fill be provided below the building Portions of the building area
may require over excavation of the existing fill in order to provide this minimum 2 foot layer
In general the bottom of footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches
below the lowest adjacent finished grades If foundations are located next to utility
trenches they should be embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V

Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by frictional resistance developed
between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil and by passive earth pressure
acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system A coefficient of friction of 0 35
may be used for footings bearing on the compacted structural fills Resistance due to passive
earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 pounds per square
foot per foot of depth pcf This assumes that the soils around the footings are well
compacted Unless covered by pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the upper
12 inches of soil should be neglected
Site Grading
Prior to placement of the structural fill material the existing grade should be cleared
grubbed and scarified to a minimum depth of 8 inches moisture conditioned to above the
optimum moisture content and recompacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction
Soft and yielding areas encountered should be over excavated to expose firm natural
material and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with fill placed in 12 inch maximum
loose lifts moisture conditioned to at least the optimum moisture content and compacted to
at least 95 percent relative compaction ASTM D 1557 91 The excavated fill soils should be
disposed of properly off site and or used in landscape areas where appropriate
The compacted structural fill should consist of non expansive select granular material
such as crushed basalt or coral The material should be well graded from coarse to fine with
no particles larger than 3 inches in largest dimension and should contain between 10 and
30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve The material should have a laboratory
CBR value of 20 or more and should have a maximum swell of less than 1 percent
Compacted structural fill should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum
moisture content placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the
in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same
soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 91 test procedures Optimum moisture is
the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
Slab On Grade
The area adjacent to the slabs should be backfilled tightly against the slab and graded
to divert water away from the slab to reduce the potential of water ponding around the slab
For the design of structural slabs a modulus of subgrade reaction of 200 pounds per
square inch per inch of deflection pci may be used for the compacted subgrade material
Based on the anticipated vehicle loads and the soil conditions we recommend a minimum
slab thickness of 5 inches Provisions should be made for proper load transfer across the slab

Utilities
It is anticipated that some utilities may be constructed for the proposed project The
methods and equipment to be used for excavation should be determined by the contractor
subject to practical limits and safety considerations The excavation should comply with
all applicable local state and federal safety requirements Trench shoring design and
installation should be the responsibility of the contractor Trench shoring and bracing
should conform to the appropriate health and safety requirements
In general for support of the utility lines we recommend that a granular bedding
consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation be used under the
pipes The initial backfill up to about 1 foot above the pipes should consist of free draining
backfills such as No 3B Fine gravel to reduce the potential for damaging the pipes from
compaction of the backfill It is critical that a free draining granular material be used to reduce
the potential for formation of voids below the haunches of pipes and to provide adequate
support for the sides of the pipes The use of on site soils as backfill immediately around utility
pipes is not recommended
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 1 foot above the pipes to the
finished subgrade should consist of approved on site soils or select granular material The
backfill material should be moisture conditioned to at least the optimum moisture content
placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum
of 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for future ground subsidence The
upper 2 feet of the trench backfill below the pavement subgrade should be compacted to not
less than 95 percent relative compaction Mechanical compaction equipment should be used
to compact the materials at the project site Water tamping jetting or ponding should not be
used to compact the backfill material

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that Geolabs be retained to provide geotechnical engineering
services during construction of the proposed project The items of construction monitoring that
are critical requiring Special Inspection include observation of footing subgrade
over excavation and preparation and placement and compaction of the structural fill materials
Other aspects of earthwork construction should also be observed by a representative from
Geolabs This is to observe compliance with the intent of the design concepts specifications
and or recommendations and to expedite suggestions for design changes that may be
required in the event that subsurface conditions differ from those anticipated at the time this
report was prepared The recommendations provided in this report are contingent upon such
observations
If actual exposed subsurface conditions encountered during construction differ from
those assumed or considered in this report Geolabs should be contacted to review and or
revise the geotechnical engineering recommendations presented herein
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from the field borings Variations of subsoil conditions between and
beyond the field borings may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not
become evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident Geolabs
should be contacted to re evaluate the recommendations presented in this report
The boring locations in the field were determined by taping from the site layout plan
transmitted to our office by Architects Pacific Inc on The physical locations of
the field borings should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the method
used
The stratification lines shown on the graphic representations of the borings depict the
approximate boundaries between the soil types and as such may denote a gradual transition
Water level data from the borings were measured at the times shown on the graphic
representations and or in the text of this report These data have been reviewed and
interpretations made in the formulation of this report However it must be noted that
fluctuation may occur due to tidal fluctuation variation in rainfall temperatures and other
factors
