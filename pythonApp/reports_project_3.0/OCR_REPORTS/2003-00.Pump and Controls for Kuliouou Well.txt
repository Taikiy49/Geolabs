SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located off the end of Kalaau Place in Kuliouou
Valley Honolulu Oahu Hawaii The general location of the site and its
vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
Presently there is a natural stream running southward across the
middle portion of the site and a dirt road extends from the end of Kalaau
Place to near the stream crossing Due to the dense vegetation and many
boulders along the stream course the remainder of the site was
inaccessible to vehicular traffic until a trail was cut by a track mounted
backhoe during the field exploration
The ground surface at the site generally slopes toward the central
axis of the stream The existing ground elevations of the site vary from
about 219 to 240 feet above Mean Sea Level

Regional Geology
The island of Oahu is composed of two 2 shield volcanoes Waianae
and Koolau built by the extrusion of basaltic lavas during the late
Pliocene and Pleistocene epochs Preliminary reconnaissance of the site
and its vicinity indicates that the area is underlain by transported soils
derived from the weathering of Koolau lavas
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2003 00 Page 4
Dk No 52
Kuliouou Valley is typical of the steeply sided valleys on the
leeward side of the eastern portion of the Koolau Range Erosion of the
windward slopes of the Koolau range has truncated the headwaters of the
valley removing the source of the streams which originally carved the
valley After the initial period of erosion alluvium and colluvium
accumulated in the floor of the valley followed by the formation and or
deposition of a surficial clay soil derived from the chemical weathering
of the basic igneous rocks forming the Koolau Range
Soil Conditions
Based on our field exploration the subsurface soil types along the
access road and the well site may be generalized into three groups as
follows
1 Adobe Clay A dark brown to gray soil characterized by a very
high shrink swell potential and low shear strength It is not
considered suitable for use as structural fill material or for
foundation support
2 Colluvium A weakly to moderately cemented conglomerate of
boulders and cobbles with a matrix of grayish brown to brown
Clay silty clay or silty sand between the larger clasts The
colluvium represents material which has been eroded from the
adjacent upper valleys and transported by gravity to the lower
slopes and valley floor This colluvial material generally has
good strength both in place and as fill although it often
exhibits a moderate shrink swell potential when excavated and
placed as fill It is generally considered Suitable for
foundation support and as a source of fill material if provisions
are made to limit the swelling potential of the clayey soil

W O 2003 00 Page 5
Dk No 52
3 River Deposits Recent river deposits were encountered mainly in
the lower areas along the existing drainageways These deposits
primarily consisted of medium stiff or medium dense clayey silt
sand and gravel
Groundwater was not encountered in the test pits during the time of
the exploration but observations following heavy periods of rain indicate
that the stream water level could rise significantly

General
The field exploration disclosed that the project site is underlain by
a thin layer of adobe clay or silty clay over a colluviun alluvium
river deposits
From a soil engineering point of view the field exploration and
laboratory test results indicate that the site is suitable for the
proposed construction however some areas may require special design
considerations and construction procedures
Site Grading
Relatively small cuts and fill are anticipated for the construction
of the initial portion of the access road up to the stream crossing
Fills up to about 8 feet high are planned to raise the existing well
site above flood level for the construction of a one story control
building and its adjacent portion of the access road
Ground Preparation
The site should be cleared of vegetation debris and soft materials
Prior to site filling the existing ground should be scarified toa
minimum of six 6 inches and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its
maximum dry density established by ASTM D 1557 78 test method
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2003 00 Page 6
Dk No 52
Large surface boulders should be collected and hauled off the site
Fill Material
The amount of materials generated from the cut areas of the project
are very limited Based on the field exploration and laboratory test
results most of the soils on site in general exhibit moderate to
high swelling potential These materials should be used in the
deeper portions of the fill a minimum 3 feet below finished grade
It appears that most of the required embankment fill material to the
control building and roadway will be imported from off site
The imported material should generally be select granular material
with low expansion potential should not contain more than 20 fines
passing through the No 200 sieve and should not contain organic or
deleterious material and particles larger than 3 inches in size
Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
Fill and backfill should be placed in 10 inch loose lifts and
compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density established
by ASTM D 1557 78 The embankment fill placement should start at the
lower portion of the fill area and progress upward in level lifts to
provide adequate compaction of fill slopes the slope should first be
over filled with compacted material and then trimmed back to the
design grade The amount of overfilling required should be a minimum
of 2 feet
Fills placed where the existing ground is steeper than 5 horizontal
to 1 vertical should be keyed and benched into the existing firm
ground to properly bond the new fill to the slope refer to Plate 3
for details

Settlements
Small settlements approximately 1 inch or less are anticipated for
most of the fill areas where medium stiff to stiff subsoils are
present as indicated by our test pits
Due to the limited depth that could be excavated by a backhoe 15
feet the deep subsoil conditions could not be explored Therefore
there is a remote possibility that soft soils may be present at
deeper depths greater than 15 feet below the site In order to
further detect this condition we recommend that settlement gauges be
installed near the finished grade see Plate 4 to monitor the ground
settlements if any The settlement gauges should be read by the
surveyor at least once a week Building foundation construction
should be delayed until the settlement gauges indicate negligible
fill settlement We estimate that this waiting period would be a
minimum of about one month
Foundations
For the proposed control building spread or continuous footing
foundations are recommended
An allowable bearing value of up to 2500 p s f is recommended for
footings bearing on the above specified compacted fill This value of
bearing capacity is for a total of dead plus live loads and may be
increased by 33 when temporary lateral loads wind and seismic are
considered
A minimum footing width of 12 inches should be utilized The bottom
of footings should be set back a minimum of 10 feet away from the top of
fill embankment

below the finished grade
Surface water should be diverted away from the building foundations
Anticipated settlements are expected to be relatively small probably
on the order of less than half of an inch
Slab On Grade
Slabs should be placed over a 4 inch cushion layer of gravel such as
3B Fine gravel
The thickened edge around the slab on grade should extend a minimum
of eight 8 inches below the adjacent ground surface
To protect against moisture damage of the flooring material a

Retaining Wall
A CRM headwall is planned for the proposed 6 inch blow off line
from the well
An allowable bearing value of 3000 p s f can be used on the stiff
in situ soil
Well graded granular material such as base course rock 3B Fine
gravel sand or an equivalent should be used as backfill material
directly behind the walls This would reduce the compactive effort
required for the backfill and reduce the build up of additional lateral
pressures on the walls Impervious on site silty material should be used
for the top 2 feet of the backfill
We recommend that the walls be designed to resist the following
lateral earth pressures
Level Backfill 35 p c f equivalent fluid pressure
active condition
A friction factor of 0 4 between concrete and soil can be utilized to
determine the sliding resistance of the wall
Additionally a passive soil resistance of 250 for soil can be used
A subsurface drainage system should be used to prevent the build up
of hydrostatic pressure behind the walls
Concrete Interceptor Ditch
A concrete interceptor ditch is planned along the existing slope
behind the proposed control building
It is anticipated that a thin surface adobe clay layer over rock or
boulder colluvium could be encountered along the proposed interceptor
ditch location

To reduce heaving and cracking of the concrete interceptor ditch due
to the expansive adobe clay the ditch should extend through the surface
adobe soils
The interceptor ditch walls should be poured directly against the
excavation side walls to minimize future water seepage below the ditch
which could cause slope instability The ground surface next to the ditch
should be shaped to drain surface water into the ditch

onstruction Observation
