INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Rental Housing Project at 727 Hausten Street in the Moiliili
area of Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the
project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings from our field exploration and laboratory
testing and presents our design level geotechnical engineering recommendations
derived from our analyses for the proposed project These recommendations are
intended for design input only and may not be appropriate to form the basis for
construction cost estimates nor for construction bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed project site is located at 727 Hausten Street in the Moiliili area of
Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii It is proposed to develop the subject site with
a three story apartment building with the ground floor level parking consisting of asphalt
concrete pavement Concrete slabs on grade will be required for a ground level
apartment unit located at the rear of the proposed building Based on the information
provided by the project architect we understand that the proposed building will be of
concrete construction up to the second floor with wood or metal stud and light framing
above the second floor level According to the plans provided the proposed finish
grade at the rear of the subject lot will be approximately 2 feet higher than that at the
entrance area for site grading and drainage purposes Therefore only minor cuts and
fills are anticipated for the site grading
Based on the information provided by the project structural engineer maximum
column loads are estimated to be on the order of 87 kips per column with wall loads
of approximately 6 4 kips per lineal foot

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions at the project site to develop an idealized soil rock data set for the
formulation of geotechnical engineering design recommendations pertinent to the
design of the foundation site grading and pavement for the proposed apartment
building project The work was performed in general accordance with our fee proposal
dated March 11th Our scope of work included the following tasks and work efforts
1 Review of available soils and geologic information pertinent to the project
site and its vicinity
2 Mobilization and demobilization of a truck mounted drill rig and operators
to and from the project site
3 Drilling and sampling of four borings to a depth of about 26 feet below
the existing ground surface for a total footage of approximately 106 lineal
feet of exploration
4 Coordination of the field exploration and logging of the drilled borings by
a field engineer of our firm
5 Laboratory testing of selected samples obtained during the field explora
tion to evaluate the classification and engineering properties of the
materials encountered
6 Analyses of the field and laboratory data for the purpose of developing
geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to the design of
building foundation and pavement section for the proposed project

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling four borings to a
depth of about 26 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate locations
of these borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 4 are shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2
In general our field exploration encountered a surficial fill crust approximately
1 to 3 feet thick consisting mainly of stiff clayey silts The clayey silts were underlain
by compressible deposits of soft silty clays and loose silty sands to depths of
approximately 4 to 6 feet below the existing ground surface Dense coral deposits
were then encountered extending to the maximum depth drilled of approximately 26
feet below the existing ground surface
Groundwater was encountered at depths ranging from about 4 to 4 feet below
the existing ground surface at the time of our field exploration These depths generally
correspond to elevations of approximately 0 2 to 0 6 feet Mean Sea Level MSL
Groundwater levels are expected to vary with seasonal rainfall and time of year

presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 4 of Appendix A Laboratory
testing was performed on selected samples obtained and the test results are presented
in Appendix B

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field exploration the project site is underlain by 2 to 5 feet of
compressible deposits consisting of soft silty clays and loose silty sands below 1 to 3
feet of stiff clayey silt fill crust
Based on our exploration and the anticipated maximum column loads of 87 kips
we believe that the thin upper fill crust and the underlying compressible deposits would
not provide adequate foundation support for the proposed building without appreciable
settlements under the new loads In order to reduce the appreciable settlements we
recommend that the soft loose compressible deposits at the footing locations be over
excavated and replaced with structural fill Generally the depth of over excavation
would be on the order of about 5 feet below the existing ground surface
Laboratory tests performed on the near surface clayey soils indicate that the
material generally has a moderate shrink swell potential when subjected to moisture
fluctuations Therefore for future slabs on grade and asphalt concrete pavements we
recommend that the expansive clayey soils be capped with a minimum of 12 inches of
non expansive select granular material to reduce the potential for distress due to
swelling of the moderately expansive near surface soils Detailed discussions and
recommendations pertaining to the design of foundation slab on grade pavement site
preparation and other geotechnical aspects of the project are presented in the following
sections of this report
Foundations
It is our opinion that shallow spread and or continuous footings may be used to
support the proposed apartment building provided that the soft loose soils at footing
locations are over excavated and replaced with structural fill An allowable bearing
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3315 00 Page 7
pressure of 2 500 pounds per square foot p s f may be used for the design of footings
bearing on structural fill constructed as described below This bearing value is for dead
plus live loads and may be increased by one third for transient loads such as those
caused by wind or seismic forces
In order to reduce the potential for appreciable settlements due to the
compressible subgrade soils we recommend that the soft loose soils at the proposed
footing locations be removed to expose firm materials Based on our exploration the
depth of the over excavations would be approximately 5 feet below the existing ground
surface Additional over excavation may be required at localized areas to remove the
soft loose soils The depth of over excavation may be reduced if competent materials
are encountered before the indicated depth The bottom of the over excavation should
extend laterally a minimum of 18 inches beyond the edge of the footings A schematic
section showing the approximate extent of the over excavation is presented on Plate 3
The soft loese soils resulting from the over excavation should not be reused as backfill
material and should be disposed of properly off site
We anticipate that over excavation to the depths indicated will encounter the
groundwater level which was encountered between 4 and 4 feet below the surface
To facilitate placement of structural fill below the groundwater level we recommend that
the over excavation be lined with a layer of geotextile fabric Mirafi 140N or equivalent
and backfilled with No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation to about
12 inches above the groundwater level The geotextile fabric should wrap around the
gravel backfill to reduce the potential for migration of fines into the gravels Care
should be exercised by the contractor to avoid damaging the filter fabric during
placement Structural fill consisting of select granular material should then be placed
on the gravel backfill in level horizontal lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness
and compacted to not less than 95 percent relative compaction Relative compaction

refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry
density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 91 test
procedures Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight
corresponding to the maximum dry density A schematic section showing the footing
excavation and backfill details is presented on Plate 3
Individual footings and continuous wall footings bearing on the structural fill
prepared as described above should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the
lowest adjacent finish grade If foundations are located next to utility trenches or
easements they should be embedded below a 45 degree imaginary plane extending
upward from the bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility
lines
We estimate the footing settlements under the anticipated design loads to be on
the order of 1 inch total with differential settlements of inch provided that the
structural fill is placed as recommended above
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive earth pressure
acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system and by frictional
resistance developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing material
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid
pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth p c f This assumes that
the soil around footings is well compacted Unless covered by pavements or slabs the
passive resistance in the upper 18 inches of soil should be neglected A coefficient of
friction of 0 35 may be used for foundations bearing on the structural fill material

Slab On Grade
We understand that concrete slabs on grade are planned for the ground level
apartment unit located at the rear of the proposed project Our field exploration
disclosed that the project site is underlain by near surface clayey silts which are
considered to be moderately expansive when subjected to moisture fluctuations
Unless floor slabs supported by these soils are properly designed there is a potential
for future distress to the lightly loaded slabs on grade resulting from the shrinking and
swelling of these clayey soils due to changes in the moisture content To reduce the
potential for structural distress resulting from the swelling of the moderately expansive
subgrade soils we recommend that the subgrade soils be properly prepared and
capped with a minimum of 12 inches of non expansive select granular material for
support of slabs on grade The non expansive capping fill should extend laterally at
least 2 feet beyond the perimeter of the proposed structure
The non expansive capping fill should be placed as soon as possible after
preparation of the existing clayey subgrade soils Subgrade soils should be prepared
in accordance with the recommendations presented in the Site Preparation section
of this report The new capping fill and the underlying subgrade soil should be kept
moist prior to final placement of concrete The capping fill would reduce the potential
moisture loss from the prepared moist subgrade soils This reduction in moisture loss
should reduce the potential swelling of the moderately expansive clayey soils
We recommend that a minimum 4 inch thick layer of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM
C 33 No 67 gradation cushion layer be placed below the slab to provide uniform
support for the slab To reduce future moisture infiltration and subsequent damage to
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3315 00 Page 10
the floor coverings a moisture vapor barrier such as a plastic membrane is recom
mended on top of the gravel cushion layer Two inches of moist fine sand should be
placed above the plastic membrane to provide protection for the membrane and to aid
in curing of the slab concrete The thickened edges of slabs adjacent to any unpaved
area should be embedded at least 12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade
Exterior concrete walkways may be required for the proposed project We
recommend that a minimum 4 inch thick cushion layer of No 3B Fine gravel be
provided below the concrete slab Construction joints should be provided at intervals
equal to the width of the walkways with expansion joints at right angle intersections
Site Preparation
Based on the existing topography and the proposed finish grades minimal site
grading work is anticipated for the project construction The weight of new fills will
initiate long term ground settlement due to compression of the soft subsoils Therefore
any site filling that may be required should be initiated as soon as possible and should
be limited to a maximum height of about 1 to 2 feet above the existing ground surface
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable
materials should be removed and disposed of off site
Slabs and foundations of the existing structures that are to be demolished should
be completely removed Any over excavation resulting from the demolition should be
backfilled with compacted granular fill material Existing utilities to be abandoned
should be removed and the resulting excavation should be properly backfilled with
select granular fill material placed in 6 to 8 inch loose lifts and compacted Utilities that
are to be abandoned in place under the proposed structure should be backfilled by
pumping lean concrete under low pressure
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3315 00 Page 11
After demolition clearing and grubbing all areas beneath future slabs and
pavement areas should be over excavated to a depth of at least 12 inches below the
bottom of the No 3B Fine gravel cushion layer or the base course layer and replaced
with compacted non expansive select granular fill material If zones of soft loose or
saturated soils are encountered at the over excavated subgrade level deeper over
excavation may be required to expose the underlying firm material The resulting over
excavation should be backfilled with compacted non expansive select granular
material
The over excavated subgrade surface should be scarified to a minimum depth
of 8 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above optimum moisture content
and compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction
refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry
density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 91 test
procedures Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight
corresponding to the maximum dry density
Non expansive select granular material needed for the 12 inch capping layer
should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness moisture
conditioned and compacted to not less than 95 percent relative compaction under slab
on grade and paved areas
Structural fill material required under the footing locations and the non expansive
select granular material required under the slabs and pavement areas should consist
of imported granular material such as select borrow crushed coral or basalt gravel
The material should be well graded from coarse to fine with no particles greater than
3 inches in largest dimension The material should have a laboratory California Bearing
Ratio CBR value of 20 or higher and a swell potential of not more than 1 percent
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3315 00 Page 12
when tested in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1883 It should contain less
than 20 percent passing the No 200 sieve Any imported fill material should be tested
and approved by Geolabs Hawaii prior to delivery to the project site for its intended
use
Pavement Design
We understand that asphalt concrete pavement will be required for the ground
floor level parking at the proposed apartment building We anticipate the vehicle
loading for this project will consist primarily of passenger vehicles and light pick up
trucks We have made our preliminary pavement design assuming the pavement
subgrade soil will consist of at least 12 inches of imported non expansive select
granular fill material The select granular fill material under the pavement areas should
be compacted to 95 percent relative compaction The subgrade soils below the select
granular fill material should be prepared in accordance with the recommendations
presented in the Site Preparation section of this report Based on the above
assumptions we recommend the following pavement section be used for preliminary
design purposes

Utility Trench
We envision that new utility lines may be required for the project A granular
bedding consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation
is recommended under the pipes For pipes with inverts near the underlying soft clays
or loose sand deposits an additional 18 inches of No 3B Fine gravel wrapped in filter
fabric Mirafi 140N or equivalent should be provided below the bedding layer to provide
more uniform support of the pipe Self compacting granular materials such as No 3B
Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation should also be used for the trench backfill
up to about 12 inches above the pipes to provide adequate support around the pipes
and to reduce the compaction effort of the backfill thus reducing the possibility of
damaging the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipes
to the top of the subgrade or finished grade should consist of select granular material
The backfill should be placed in about 8 inch loose lifts and mechanically compacted
to not less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for future ground
subsidence Where trenches are below pavement areas the upper 2 feet of the trench
backfill below the pavement subgrade should be compacted to not less than 95 percent
relative compaction

Drainage
The finished grades outside the building should be sloped to shed water away
from foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding Also it is advised that gutter
systems be installed around the building and that the discharge be diverted away from
the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near the
foundations and slabs should also be avoided Planters next to foundations should be
avoided or have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the
subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil
saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations
should be properly backfilled against the walls or slab footings immediately after setting
of the concrete to reduce water infiltration
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and should be
maintained to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundations

30 inches The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the last 12 inches of an
18 inch 24 inch drive are shown on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample
depths

Laboratory Testing

samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties The results of
these tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Two 1 inch ring swell tests were performed on selected samples to evaluate the
swelling potential of the subsoils The test results are summarized on Plate B 1
One laboratory California Bearing Ratio Test ASTM Test Method D 1883 87
was performed on the near surface clayey silt to evaluate the pavement support
characteristics of the soils The test results are presented on Plate B 2
