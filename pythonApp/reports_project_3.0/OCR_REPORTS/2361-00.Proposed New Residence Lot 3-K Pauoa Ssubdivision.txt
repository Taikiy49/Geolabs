INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
evaluation performed for the proposed residence at Lot 3 K Pauoa
Subdivision Pauoa Valley on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general
location of the site and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location
Map Plate 1 The exploration was performed in general accordance with
our proposal dated
This report summarizes the findings from the exploration and
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analysis based
on the data obtained These recommendations are intended for design input
and are not intended for construction estimation or bidding purposes

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
Based on the plans provided to us the proposed new residence will be
a two story structure approximately 40 feet by 62 feet in plan
dimensions Concrete on grade slab is planned for the entire first floor
level
Site grading involving minor cuts and fills up to about four 4 feet
are anticipated
Retaining walls are planned to raise various portions of the proposed
house to finish floor elevations ranging from 276 to 282 feet above Mean
Sea Level

The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface conditions at the project site in order to develop a
generalized soil rock data set for the formulation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertinent to site grading and foundation
design of the project
The work was done in general accordance with our proposal dated
The scope of work was limited to

below the existing ground surface

visual observations of the surficial soil conditions
3 review of available soils and geologic information pertinent to
the project site and its vicinity
4 laboratory testing of selected samples obtained during the field
exploration to determine the engineering properties and
classification of the materials encountered

SITE DESCRIPTION
The subject lot is located at the northern portion of the Pauoa
Subdivision which is at the eastern end of Pauoa Road The lot is
rectangular shaped with a frontage of 50 feet and a depth of 105 feet
The topography of the site slopes downward from Booth Road with an
average gradient of approximately 16 percent
The current ground elevations range from about 270 feet to 287 feet
along Booth Road
There is an existing drainage swale along the lower boundary of the
lot

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The stiff on site soil encountered can generally provide adequate
foundation support for the proposed house provided that some care is
exercised during construction to reduce the swelling potential of the
soil as recommended herein
The field exploration and laboratory tests performed on the near
surface stiff on site silty clay indicate that this material generally has
low to moderate swelling potential depending on the initial moisture
content of the soil prior to subsequent saturation Therefore to reduce
the effects due to soil swelling under on grade slabs we recommend that
the on site soil be capped with at least 12 inches of non expansive select
fill and that the subgrade soils underneath the select fill and slab be
moistened and protected from drying from the start of construction until
the slab concrete placement
Lot Grading
The fills and backfills within the top 12 inches minimm below the
on grade slabs should consist of non expansive soils such as select
borrow crusher waste or coral Where minimal fill or cut is anticipated
the on site soil should be over excavated to provide a 12 inch minimm
thickness of non expansive select fill capping underneath the on grade
Slabs

All imported fill material should be tested and approved by the
geotechnical engineer prior to transportation to the site
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading

properly off site Any loose or soft spots encountered at fill areas

House Foundations
Spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the proposed
residence An allowable bearing value of 2000 p s f may he used for
footings bearing on the compacted fill soil or stiff natural ground
Individual footings and continuous wall footings should be embedded a
minimum of 12 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade
Slab On Grade
It should be noted that the on site silty clay soils may he
moderately expansive if allowed to dry then saturated Therefore the
on site soil should be over excavated down to a depth of 12 inches below
the slab bottom thoroughly moistened and then replaced with
non expansive imported soils as previously discussed The thickened edge
of the slab should be embedded a minimum of 12 inches below the lowest

movement of the concrete

Drainage
The finish lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings
should be shaped to shed water away from foundations and to avoid
ponding condition Also it is advised that gutter systems be
installed around the house and that gutter water should be diverted far
away from the foundations and driveway Excessive landscape watering
near foundations and slab should also be avoided
Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the foundation subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance
of the above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of
strength The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled tight
against the wall or slab footing inmediately after setting of the concrete
to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and maintained
to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundations

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field borings Variations of
conditions between the borings may occur and the nature and extent of
these variations may not become evident until construction If variations
then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations given in this report
The locations of the field borings were approximately determined by
taping from structures indicated on the Topographic Survey Map by ParEn
Inc dated August 2 1988 Elevations of the borings were approximately
detemnined by interpolation from elevation points on the same plan The
physical location and elevation of the field data points should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
borings depict the approximate boundaries between soil types and as
such may denote gradual transition The strata lines shown on profiles
or cross sections are based upon interpolation between borings and may not
represent actual subsurface conditions

Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling two
2 borings to a depth of 10 feet below the existing ground surface at the
approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 A truck mounted
drill rig was used to drill the borings
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by visual and
textural examination in the field by our engineer who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory All soils
were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A summary of the materials encountered are
presented in the Boring Logs Plates A 1 and A 2
Soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving a 2 inch
a 140 pound hammer free falling 30 inches The blow counts to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the Boring Logs at the appropriate
sample depths
