Dewatering
Dewatering appears feasible provided sheet piles are
driven into dense and firm materials Two feet of well
grade granular filter material will be needed as a working
base over soft subsoils
Sheet Piling
Steel sheet piling is preferred over concrete piling because
of its better penetrating ability for embedment into dense
coral sand which will be required for toe stability Sheet
pile driving through mud should be easy and vibration will
be minimum
Sheet pile lengths will generally be longer on the makai
portion between Ala Wai Canal and Fern Street For esti
mating purposes pile tip elevations varying between 30 to
40 feet should be used for this section Estimated sheet
pile tip elevations may be estimated to Elevation 20 feet
between Fern and Philip Streets For the remaining portion
mauka of Philip Street where the box invert will be above

Open Cut Excavation
The maximum height of open cut excavation without sheet
ing and bracing should be 5 feet in stiff soil condition
mauka of Philip Street and is generally not recommended
in the area makai of Philip Street and in the area where
soft mud is encountered near the water level Construc
tion bracing would be necessary for applicable safety
regulations
Bridge at Kapiolani Boulevard Crossing
Pile foundations will be required for this bridge A 50
ton per pile capacity can be anticipated for a 16 octagonal
prestressed concrete pile Generally pile tip elevations
will vary between Elevation 30 to 40 foot depths
A pile driving hammer delivering a minimum rated energy
of 15 000 foot pounds is recommended A blow count of 60
blows per foot for two consecutive feet of penetration
into the dense coral sand material will be able to develop
pile capacity

Gentlemen
In accordance with our recent telephone conversation with Mr Paul
Taniguchi enclosed are the revised earth pressure diagrams
The diagrams have been revised to present the earth pressure
distributions shown by the dotted lines resulting from equal water
levels on each side of the sheeting This pressure distribution
applies to the construction condition where the inside of the sheet
ing would not be dewatered
The strut and embedment forces have not been revised on the drawing
The reduction of the lateral pressures will produce a corresponding
reduction in both the strut force and the embedment force It is
recommended that a three foot minimum sheeting penetration be main
tained
Uniform surcharges placed adjacent to the sheeting will produce a
uniform lateral pressure distribution equivalent to the surcharge
loading multiplied by a coefficient of lateral earth pressure k
equal to 0 41 This pressure distribution will be uniform and will
apply for the full length of the sheeting
The passive resistance for the sheeting will not be influenced by
an increase in the water level on the inside or surcharge loadings
on theoutside of the sheeting
