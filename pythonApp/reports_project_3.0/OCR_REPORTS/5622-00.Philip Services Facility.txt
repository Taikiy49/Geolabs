SECTION 1 0 GENERAL
1 1 Introduction
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for Parcel 1 of the Street project located in Campbell
Industrial Park in the Kapolei area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location
and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings and geotechnical engineering
recommendations derived from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering
analyses for the development project The recommendations presented herein are
intended for the design of tank foundations The findings and recommendations
presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report
1 2 Project Considerations
Based on the information provided the project site is used for the transfer of bulk
petroleum products and other fluids At present there are several aboveground storage
tanks within containment areas surrounded by concrete walls It is proposed to replace
six existing horizontal tanks with larger capacity vertical tanks Details of the tanks were
not available at the time this report was prepared however it is anticipated that the
tanks will be supported by ring foundations or as encapsulated tanks on slabs on grade
It is anticipated that site grading will not be done
1 3 Purpose and Scope
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the surface and
subsurface conditions at the site to develop an idealized soil rock data set and to
formulate preliminary geotechnical engineering recommendations to assist in the
planning of the site preparation and mass grading The work was performed in general
accordance with our fee proposal dated The scope of work for this
exploration included the following tasks and work efforts

2 1 Regional Geology
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lavas from
two shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older Waianae Volcano is estimated to
be middle to late Pliocene in age and the younger Koolau Volcano is estimated to be
late Pliocene to early Pleistocene in age
The project site is situated on the Ewa Coastal Plain which extends seaward off
the southeastern flank of Waianae and the southwestern flank of Koolau This plain was
formed by the accumulation of a thick wedge of sediments and coral reef formations on
the flanks of the two volcanoes
During the Pleistocene Epoch sea levels fluctuated in response to the cycles of
continental glaciation As the glaciers grew and advanced less water was available to
fill the oceanic basins such that sea levels fell below the present stands of the sea
When the glaciers melted and receded an excess of water became available such that
the sea levels rose to above the present sea level
The higher sea level stands caused the formation of reef deposits at
correspondingly higher elevations Subaerial exposure of the sediments and calcareous
materials caused consolidation of the lagoonal deposits and induration of the
calcareous reef materials
2 2 Site Description
The project site is located in Campbell Industrial Park in the Kapolei area on the
Island of Oahu Hawaii The project site is an industrial lot located at the eastern half of
the industrial park at the southwestern side of the intersection of Komohana and Kauhi
Streets as shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
At the time of our field exploration the project site was a developed industrial
parcel that was being used for a variety of petroleum related and waste management
services The site is currently occupied by several aboveground storage tanks and

SECTION 3 0 DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Our field exploration disclosed that the project site is generally underlain by an
approximately 6 inch thick concrete slab over a layer of silty gravel fill or reworked coral
material about 3 feet thick overlying coral formation Groundwater was not encountered
in the borings drilled at the time of our field exploration
It is anticipated that the existing horizontal tanks and their containments will be
demolished for the installation of the new vertical tanks In addition it is anticipated that
a new containment area will be constructed with concrete walls and a concrete floor
Furthermore it is anticipated that site grading will not be done
For design of the foundations for the new tanks and containment walls an
allowable bearing pressure of up to 4 000 pounds per square foot psf may be used for
footings bearing on the existing concrete slab compacted on site materials placed in
accordance with the recommendations presented herein or coral formation
Voids and cavities are commonly encountered in coral formations Although
voids or cavities were not encountered in our exploration it is our opinion that due to
the nature of the site development a program of cavity probing and grouting along
subgrade areas and in major foundation systems should be considered
Detailed discussions and recommendations for design of foundations site

3 1 Foundations
For design of the foundations for the proposed vertical tanks and containment
walls an allowable bearing pressure of up to may be used for footings bearing
on recompacted on site soils prepared in accordance with the recommendations
presented herein or on coral formation The above recommended bearing value is for
those caused by wind or seismic forces

SECTION 3 DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grade The bottoms of foundation excavations should be scarified to a
depth of 12 inches below finished grade moisture conditioned to about 2 percent above
the optimum moisture and recompacted to at least 95 percent relative compaction
Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage
of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D
1557 Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by weight corresponding to
the maximum dry density We wish to emphasize that moisture conditioning is an
integral part of the recommendations provided herein Compaction should be
accomplished by sheepsfoot rollers vibratory rollers or other types of acceptable
compaction equipment
Footings located adjacent to slopes or on slopes should be embedded to a
sufficient depth to provide a minimum horizontal setback distance of at least 6 feet
measured from the outside edge of the footing to the face of the slope Foundations
located next to utility trenches or easements should be embedded below a 45 degree
imaginary plane extending upward from the bottom edge of the utility trench or the
footings should be extended to a depth as deep as the inverts of the utility lines
If the foundations are designed and constructed as recommended herein we
estimate that footing settlements will be less than 1 inch Differential settlements
between adjacent footings should be on the order of about 0 5 inches or less
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by a combination of
frictional resistance between the base of the foundation and the bearing materials and
by passive earth pressure developed against the near vertical faces of the embedded
portion of the footings A coefficient of friction of 0 40 may be used for footings bearing
on the on site materials Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be calculated
using an equivalent fluid pressure of 350 pounds per square foot per foot of depth pcf
for footings embedded in the structural fill or coralline materials The passive earth
pressure value assumes that the concrete for the footings is cast neat against on site
materials Otherwise the soils around the footings should be well compacted minimum

3 2 Slabs On Grade
Slabs on grade should be supported by a minimum of 6 inches of base course
material to provide uniform support for the slabs The base course should consist of
crushed basaltic aggregates compacted to a minimum of 95percent relative
compaction For the design of structural slabs supported on 6 inches of base course a
modulus of subgrade reaction of about 200 pounds per square inch per inch of
deflection pci may be used A minimum slab thickness of 6 inches should be used In
addition provisions should be made for proper load transfer across slab joints that will
be subjected to vehicular traffic
The thickened edges of any slabs adjacent to unpaved areas should be
embedded at least 12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade It should be emphasized
that the areas adjacent to the slabs should be backfilled tightly against the edges of the
slabs with relatively impervious soils well graded on site granular soils These areas
also should be graded to divert water away from the slabs and to reduce the potential
for water ponding around the slab edges
3 3 Cavity Probing and Grouting
Voids and cavities are commonly encountered in coral formations Although no
voids or cavities were encountered in our exploration it is our opinion that due to the
nature of the site development a program of cavity probing and grouting along
subgrade areas and in major foundation systems should be considered Probing and
grouting essentially consists of drilling an array of probe holes to depths of about 10 feet
below the finish grade In general we recommend that the probes be spaced at about
10 foot centers across the containment area and along major foundations Cement
grout should be pumped at low pressure in the drilled probe holes in an effort to
stabilize the buried voids encountered by the probe holes against potential collapse
Due to the granular nature of the coral formation underlying the site and the associated

tremie pipes be washed to the bottom of each hole and that the grout be placed as the

3 4 Basic Utility Installation
We anticipate that utility installation for the proposed project may consist of
electric telecommunications and product piping lines for the new tanks In general
good construction practices should be utilized for the installation and backfilling of the
trenches for the new utilities The method and equipment to be used for trench
excavation should be determined by the contractor subject to practical limits and safety
considerations In addition the excavations should comply with the applicable federal
state and local safety requirements The contractor should be responsible for trench
shoring design and installation
Based on our field exploration results it appears that some of the utility trench
excavations will likely encounter the coral formation as described in the logs of borings
which may pose some excavation difficulties during the trench excavation In addition
the contractor should anticipate significant over break of the sides of the trench
excavations considering the nature of the materials at the site This over break condition
may be minimized by the use of trenching machines
In general a granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel
ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation is recommended below the pipes Free draining
granular materials such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation should
be used for the trench backfill up to about 12 inches above the pipes to provide
adequate support around the pipes and to reduce compaction of the backfill thus
reducing the possibility of damaging the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill should consist of granular material
generally less than 3 inches in maximum size compacted to at least 95 percent relative
compaction Where the utility line crosses beneath the footings the utility pipes should
be concrete jacketed

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by drilling and
sampling six borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 6 extending to depths
ranging from about 15 0 to 16 3 feet below the existing ground surface The
approximate locations of the borings drilled are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The
borings were drilled using truck mounted rotary drilling equipment equipped with
continuous flight augers
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who observed the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed by visual
observation and testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance
with the Unified Soil Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic
representations of the materials encountered are presented on the Logs of Borings
Plates A 1 through A 6
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in
general accordance with ASTM D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving
a 3 inch OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches In
addition some samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance
with ASTM D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a
2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same hammer and drop The blow
counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an 18 inch drive
are shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate
sample depths
