Use of Excavated On site Material
Kahekili Highway Widening Phase Il
Station 52 00 to Station 74 00
In accordance with your request we have evaluated the possible use of the
excavated on site materials as fill and backfill materials at the above referenced project
The following is a summary of our recommendations on the use of the excavated on site
materials as fill and backfill materials
In general stiff to very stiff silty clays and clayey silts saprolites were encountered
at the subject site Based on the results of our field exploration and laboratory tests the
on site materials generated from excavations at the site may be reused as a source of fill
and backfill materials in the non structural areas provided they contain no lumps greater
than 3 inches in largest dimension However the soft and wet on site silty and clayey
soils such as the soft silts and clays generated from the removal operations in Valley No
1 should not be used as fill or backfill materials The excavated soft soils should be
disposed of off site to avoid contaminating the other usable excavated materials
In general we believe that the non structural areas of the subject site where the
excavated on site soils may be used include the following
Since the MSERW will be designed with a lateral earth pressure due to fine
grained soil 45 p c f equivalent fluid pressure the excavated on site
materials may be used as backfill behind the limits of the MSERW select
granular fill volume
If the conventional concrete retaining wall was designed with a lateral earth
pressure due to fine grained soil 45 p c f equivalent fluid pressure the
excavated on site materials may be used as backfill behind the drainage
material of conventional concrete retaining walls the plans currently specifies
select granular

Generally the fill and backfill materials should be placed in level lifts not exceeding
8 inches in loose thickness moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content
and compacted to the relative compaction stipulated in the plans and specifications for the
project Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a
percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil deter mined in accordance with
AASHTO T 180 Test Methods it should be emphasized that the wet method of sample
preparation Hawaii Test Method should be used in determining the maximum
dry density
Based on the results of our exploration some of the on site soils have high natural
moisture contents As a result some difficulty in achieving the specified compaction
based on maximum dry density should be expected if use of the excavated on site soils
is desired Therefore special attention should be given to Section 203 02 B 2 of the
Special Provisions of the subject project for testing and approval of the on site soils with
high natural moisture contents
