Redesign of Paihi Bridge Replacement Scour at Abutment No 1
COPY TO File Original
This memorandum provides a discussion on the scour area along the north side of
Paihi Bridge in the Hana area on the Island of Maui Hawaii
We understand that ControlPoint Surveying Inc has taken measurements of the
cavity below the existing bridge at the north side of the stream Based on their
measurements the basalt ledge is about 19 feet thick top of ledge is at approximately
201 feet MSL and the bottom of ledge is at about 182 feet MSL The area where scour
has occurred and is occurring is below the basalt ledge and extends down to the bottom
of the stream at an elevation of approximately 153 feet MSL The stream sidewalls
expose clinker materials The clinker has scoured approximately 17 feet laterally near the
stream bed from the face of the basalt ledge It appears that the lateral distance
decreases at higher elevations and also upstream Because of the scour occurring at the
north side of the stream it was recommended to redesign the replacement bridge
Based on the information provided the redesign will consist of setting back
Abutment No 1 located at the north side of the stream about 10 feet from the original
design location which moves the bridge foundations further away from the scour area
Further setback could not be achieved due to the presence of the mauka side cut slope
nearby
Other methods to reduce scour potential or loss of foundation support due to scour
consists of diverting the flow of the stream away from the north side stream bank lining
the sidewalls of the stream with concrete or extending the drilled shaft foundations below
the streambed and into the basalt formation Please note that diverting the stream flow
and or lining the sidewalls of the stream with concrete would require permits to work in the
stream In addition extending the drilled shaft foundations beyond a length of 30 feet
would require a larger drill rig which would be difficult to mobilize to the site due to its
remote location and numerous light capacity bridges along Hana Highway
We believe the potential for loss of foundation support would be reduced
significantly by setting back the abutment 10 feet original abutment location In addition
using a drilled shaft foundation would distribute the loads throughout the basalt ledge
reducing the potential for the basalt ledge instability Failure of this ledge would result in a
loss of foundation support It should be noted that the replacement bridge redesign will not
permanently protect the replacement bridge from a loss of foundation support due to scour
