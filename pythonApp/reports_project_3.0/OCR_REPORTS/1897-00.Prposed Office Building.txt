INTRODUCTION
This report represents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the design of the proposed office building at
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our study was to explore the subsurface soil
conditions at the proposed building site in order to provide
recommendations for use in the design of the site grading and
foundations of the proposed facility

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is situated in Lihue Kauai The general
location of the site and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location
Map Plate i
The site is located adjacent to Haleko Road near its intersection
with Rice Street The site is nearly level with a slight slope towards
the south The site is currently vacant and appears to have been used
formerly as a residential lot The existing ground surface elevation
of the site varies from approximately 170 to 202 above Mean Sea Level
PROJECT CONSTDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a two story office building on the
site The proposed building is L shaped with legs approximately 80
and 95 feet in length Each leg will be approximately 30 feet in
width A parking area with approximately 32 parking spaces is also
proposed

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
The island of Kauai is composed of a single basalt shield volcano
built by the extrusion of the lavas of the Waimea Canyon Volcanic
Series during the late Pliocene more than 2 3 million years before
present Following the cessation of this main shield building phase
there was renewed volcanic activity with the extrusion of the basaltic
lavas of the post erosional Koloa Volcanic Series and the concurrent
deposition of the alluvial sediments of the Palikea Formation our
exploration of the site and its vicinity indicates that the area is
underlain by thick soil layers derived from the in situ weathering of
alluvium
Subsurface Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by
drilling and sampling four 4 borings to depths ranging between 18 0
and 21 5 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate
locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general the borings encountered a surface layer of fill
composed of reddish brown and brown clayey silts approximately 4 5
feet in thickness This fill appears to exhibit varying degrees of
compaction and the soils range in consistency from stiff to hard
Below the fill the borings encountered moist very stiff reddish brown
and brown clayey silts with some sand and gravel weathered alluvium
to depths between 14 0 and 18 0 feet below the existing ground
surface Underlying this very stiff stratum the soil materials
encountered were similar except that the moisture content increased and
the soil consistency decreased to stiff

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
It is our opinion that the proposed project is feasible provided
that our recommendations are followed However the variable nature of
the upper fill encountered on the site as is makes its capability to
provide direct spread footing foundation support questionable We
recommend that spread or strip footings bear on the underlying very
stiff alluvial soils located about two to four feet below existing
grade
The bottom of all footing excavations should be inspected by the
geotechnical engineer prior to the placement of the reinforcement to
determine if adequate bearing has been obtained and that loose soils
are removed prior to concrete placement
It is our opinion that for footings designed and constructed in
accordance with the above criteria the potential total and
differential settlements should be less than one half inch
Site Grading
AS previously stated the exact details of the proposed site
grading are not currently available However it is assumed that some
minor amounts of grading work will be required
Prior to the commencement of any earthwork all areas within the
grading limits should be cleared of all vegetation and deleterious
materials The resulting grub and spoil material should be disposed of
properly off site or stockpiled for use in landscaping Any soft
weak or yielding areas disclosed during the clearing operations should
be over excavated to firm ground and the resulting excavation should be
backfilled with well compacted engineered fill

Generally the on site soils should be suitable for use as
engineered fill If additional imported material is required the
material should conform to the requirements stipulated in Section 30 of
the Standard Specifications for Public Works Construction Select
Borrow for Subbase Course dated September 1986
All fills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding eight 8
inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimm of 90 of its
maximum dry density as established by ASTM D 1557 78 Test Method
Cesspools
There may be buried abandoned cesspools on the project site The
available county and state records should be researched to
determine their locations Any cesspool found on the site should
be opened cleaned of loose soft materials and backfilled with
granular material such as 3 Fine to two 2 feet below finished
subgrade Above this level the cesspool excavation should be
filled and compacted as specified above
Cut areas underlying structures or pavements should be excavated
to rough finish subgrade and compacted to 90 of its maximum dry
density as specified above
Foundation Support
For the design of spread or strip footings an allowable bearing
capacity of 2500 p s f may be utilized for footings bearing on the
very stiff alluvial soil This value may be increased by one third
when temporary wind and seismic loads are considered The footings
should be embedded a minimum of two 2 feet below the lowest adjacent
grade and bear on the undisturbed very stiff alluvial soil

5 The wall foundations on relatively flat areas should be
embedded a minimum of 24 inches below the lowest adjacent
grade For sloping ground the footing should extend to
obtain a minimum 8 foot set back from the outside edge of the
footing measured horizontally to the slope face
6 A subsurface drainage system subdrains and or weephole

excavation and workspace behind the new wall does not affect
the footing of any adjacent existing structure
Recommendations for underpinning and temporary shoring are

exploration and study
Slabs On Grade
It is anticipated that the floors of the proposed building will be
concrete slabs on grade The slab and its cushion may be supported by
the upper fill provided the subgrade is first scarified to a depth of
ten 10 inches moisture conditioned to the wet side of optimim
moisture content and recompacted to 90 relative dry density as
determined by ASTM D 1557 78 Test Method A 4 inch layer of 3B Fine
gravel should be provided below the slabs as cushion fill
To minimize future subgrade soils from heaving due to subsequent
saturation we recommend that the subgrade soils should be kept
continuously moist by water sprinkling prior to concrete placement

damage to the floor coverings an impervious moisture membrane may be
considered below the slabs provided an inch of moist fine sand is

Pavements
Asphaltic concrete pavements are planned for the driveways and
parking area We recommend that the A C pavement section consist of
the following

6 Inches Base Course
8 Inches Total Pavement Thickness

performed on the actual subgrade soils encountered during construction
and that the above section be revised if necessary The recommended
section considers medium traffic and assumes that adequate drainage
will be provided in the paved areas Weepholes should be provided in
the parking area drainage inlet structures in order to relieve the
seepage of water which may accumulate in the subgrade
Design Review
Plans and specifications for foundations site grading and utility
construction should be forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for
review and written comments prior to construction to see that the
intent of our recommendations is included

to 21 5 feet below the existing ground surface at the

reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory The soils
were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A graphic presentation of the soils encountered
is presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 4

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content and unit weight determinations were performed on
selected samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil
properties The results of these tests are presented on the Logs of
Borings Plates A 1 through A 4 at the appropriate sample depth
One Atterberg Limits test was performed on a selected sample to
examine the nature of the soil The result of the test is shown on the
Summary of Laboratory Test Results Plate B 1
Two swell tests one natural and one remolded were performed on
selected samples to evaluate the swell potential of the on site soils
The results of these tests are shown on the Summary of Laboratory Test
Results Plate B 1
One laboratory compaction test and one CBR test were performed on
a sample of the near surface soil The results of these tests are
shown on the Summary of Laboratory Test Results Plate B 1
One direct shear test was performed on an undisturbed sample to
evaluate the strength characteristics of the in situ soil The test
result is presented on Plate B 2
