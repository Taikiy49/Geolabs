for the proposed Chang Residence project located at 279 Kaiali i Place in the Hawaii Loa
Ridge area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project

intended for the design of foundations retaining walls site grading and pavements only

the end of this report

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from two shield
volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be middle to
late Pliocene in age and forms the bulk of the western third of the island The younger
shield Koolau is estimated to be late Pliocene to early Pleistocene Ice Age in age and
forms the majority of the eastern two thirds of the island Waianae became extinct while
Koolau was still active and its eastern flank was partially buried below Koolau lavas banking
against its eastern flank These banked or ponded lavas formed a broad plateau referred
to as the Schofield Plateau Both Waianae and Koolau Ranges have lost most of their
original shield outlines and are now long narrow ridges shaped largely by erosion
The project area is located on the southeastern flank of Koolau and is composed of
basaltic rock built by the extrusion of lava of the Koolau Volcanic Series These rocks are
generally characterized by flows of jointed dense vesicular basalt with interbedded thin
clinker layers In situ chemical weathering of the Koolau lava flows has occurred for the last
1 to 2 million years The weathering process has formed a mantle of residual soils which
grade to saprolite with depth In general saprolite is composed mainly of silty material and
is typical of the tropical weathering of volcanic rocks The saprolite grades to basaltic rock
formation with depth

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Our field exploration indicates that the site is generally underlain by medium hard to
hard basalt rock formation from near the existing ground surface extending to the maximum
depth explored of about 22 5 feet below the existing ground surface Based on the
competent subsurface conditions encountered at the project site we recommend that a
shallow foundation system consisting of spread and or continuous footings be used for
support of the proposed residence Due to the potential for presence of cavities and or voids
as encountered in our borings we recommend that a program of probing and grouting the
foundation locations be implemented during construction to reduce the potential for loss of
foundation support

residence will generally be constructed on a cut condition made into the sloping terrain of the
lot Due to the depth of the proposed cut we anticipate that an extensive retaining wall
system will be necessary for the grade separation and foundation support needed to provide
a level pad for the proposed residence In general we anticipate that a retaining wall system
adjacent to the walls of the residence will serve as walls retaining the relatively deep
excavation made in the basalt rock formation In addition a series of retaining walls on the
south and west sides of the residence will be constructed to retain the fills anticipated in
these areas

Foundations
As previously mentioned the proposed residence will consist of a two story house
with a one level basement Based on the subsurface conditions encountered during our
exploration we recommend that a shallow foundation system consisting of spread and or
continuous footings be used to support the proposed residence An allowable bearing
pressure of up to 4 000 pounds per square foot psf may be used for the design of
foundations bearing on the medium hard to hard basalt rock formation encountered in our
borings This allowable bearing pressure is for dead plus live loads and may be increased
by 50 percent for transient loads such as those caused by temporary wind and or seismic
forces
Footings should be embedded a minimum depth of 18 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grade Due to the sloping terrain at the project site we recommend that
the foundations be embedded deep enough to obtain a minimum 6 foot setback distance
measured horizontally from the outside edge of the footing to the face of the slope The
6 foot horizontal setback distance may be neglected for footings bearing directly on basalt
rock formation Continuous footings running parallel to the direction of the slope should
be constructed in stepped footings
If foundations are located next to utility trenches or easements they should be
embedded below a 1H 1V imaginary plane extending upward from the bottom edge of the
utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility lines This requirement is necessary to
avoid surcharging adjacent below grade structures with additional structural loads and to
reduce the potential for foundation settlement Speciai attention should be given to this
requirement for structures located within close proximity to the sewer line easement located
at the western portion of the site
Due to the potential for presence of cavities and or voids as encountered in our
borings we recommend that a program of probing and grouting the foundation locations be
implemented during construction Probing and grouting requirements are further discussed
in the following Foundation Probing and Grouting section

Foundation Probing and Grouting
Based on our experience in the area and the highly vesicular nature of the basalt rock
encountered cavities and or voids may be present in the basalt rock formation as
encountered in the borings To reduce the potential for loss of foundation support resulting
from collapse of cavities and or voids below foundations we recommend that a program of
cavity probing and grouting be conducted during construction
For estimating purposes we recommend that probe holes be drilled at each isolated
spread footing location and at 10 foot on centers for continuous footings The probe holes
should be at least 3 inches in diameter and should extend to at least 8 feet below the
planned bottom of foundation If cavities and or voids are encountered or suspected during
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 4249 00 Page 9
the probing operation additional probe holes should be drilled at closer spacing to help
delineate the vertical and lateral extent of the cavity and or void
The probe holes and cavities discovered should be backfilled with sand cement grout
injected at low to moderate pressures A typical sand cement grout should have a slump of
about 6 to 9 inches As an alternative to sand cement grout controlled low strength material
CLSM or flowable fill may be used to fill the cavities and or voids provided that it may be
pumped into the probe holes
Because of the potential for encountering large cavities and or voids at the site we
recommend that unit prices for additional probing and grouting be obtained during bidding
In addition the probe drill should be available on site until the probing and grouting
operations are completed The contractor should also be made aware that a longer lag time
between probing grouting operations and foundation construction might be required in the
construction schedule
A representative of Geolabs Hawaii should observe the probing and grouting
operations to evaluate the extent of cavities if encountered The probing and grouting
operations should be designated as a Special Inspection item during construction
Slabs On Grade
In general we envision that the slab subgrades for the residence will consist of the
excavated basalt rock formation For more uniform support of the interior slabs we
recommend that the concrete slabs on grade be supported on a minimum 4 inch cushion
fill layer The cushion fill layer should consist of open graded gravels such as No 3B Fine
gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation which would also serve as a capillary moisture
break
For slabs on grade with moisture sensitive surfacing we recommend that an
impermeable membrane be placed over the cushion fill layer to reduce the potential for
migration of moisture vapor through the concrete slab Two inches of moist fine sand may
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 4249 00 Page 10
be considered on top of the plastic membrane to provide protection of the membrane and
to aid in curing of the slab concrete Flexible floor coverings such as carpet or sheet vinyl
should be considered since they can better mask minor slab cracking
Because the garage floor slab will be subjected to vehicular traffic we recommend
that the garage floor slab be supported on 6 inches of select borrow subbase in lieu of the
4 inch cushion fill and 2 inch fine sand The select borrow subbase material should be
compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction We envision that the exterior
flatwork concrete slabs on the south side of the residence will be subjected to vehicular
traffic Therefore we recommend that these slabs be supported by 6 inches of select borrow
subbase compacted to at least 95 percent relative compaction

Drainage
Retaining structures should be well drained to reduce the build up of hydrostatic
pressures A typical drainage system would consist of a 12 inch wide zone of
permeable material such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation
immediately around a perforated pipe perforations down at the base of the retaining
structure discharging to an appropriate outlet or weepholes As an alternative a
prefabricated drainage product such as MiraDrain or EnkaDrain may be used
instead of the drainage material The prefabricated drainage product should also be
hydraulically connected to a perforated pipe at the base of the retaining structures
Backfill behind the permeable drainage zone should consist of granular fill materials
less than 3 inches in maximum dimension Unless covered by concrete slabs the
upper 12 inches of backfill should consist of relatively impervious material to reduce
the potential for water infiltration behind the retaining structures

Site Grading
Based on the schematic plans provided cuts up to about 28 feet deep and fills on the
order of about 10 feet thick are anticipated for the site grading The deep cuts anticipated
at the site will encounter medium hard to hard basalt rock formation near the existing ground
surface Considering the relatively small volume of rock excavation at the site we envision
that it would not be economically feasible to process the excavated rock as fill materials
maximum particle size of 3 inches or less Therefore we envision that the excavated rock
materials would not be re used as fill materials unless they are processed to well graded
materials with a maximum particle size of 3 inches In addition it should be noted that the
natural soil materials in the Hawaii Loa Ridge area are generally expansive and should not
be used as fill materials directly below the residence and improvements planned at the site
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed Vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable materials
should be removed and disposed of properly off site Soft and yielding areas encountered
during clearing and grubbing should be over excavated to expose firm material and the
resulting excavation should be backfilled with well compacted fill
After clearing grubbing and excavation to the subgrade elevations areas beneath
future slabs and pavement areas should be proof rolled with a vibratory drum roller a
minimum of six passes to assist in detecting and collapsing near surface cavities Loose or
soft spots encountered at the subgrade levels should be removed and replaced with
well compacted fill
As previously indicated the excavated rocky materials may only be used as fill or
backfill materials provided that they are processed and reduced to a relatively well graded
granular material with a maximum particle size of about 3 inches Fill and backfill materials
to be used as fill material up to the slab subgrade level should consist of well graded granular
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 4249 00 Page 14
materials less than 3 inches in largest dimension In general we anticipate that the fills and
backfills needed at the site will likely consist of imported materials Imported materials should
be well graded from coarse to fine with no particles greater than 3 inches in largest
dimension The material should have a laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR value of
20 or higher and a swell potential of 1 percent or less when tested in accordance with ASTM

compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the
maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 91 test
procedures Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight
corresponding to the maximum dry density

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling two
borings extending to depths of about 10 and 22 5 feet below the existing ground surface at
the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were drilled using
portable drilling equipment equipped with coring tools
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually in the laboratory
Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil Classification System as
shown on Plate A Graphic representations of the materials encountered are provided on
the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 and A 2
Core samples of rock materials encountered at the site were obtained using diamond
core drilling techniques in general accordance with ASTM Standard Practice D 2113 83
Diamond Core Drilling for Site Investigation Core drilling is a rotary drilling method which
uses a hollow bit to cut into the rock formation The rock material left in the hollow core of
the bit is mechanically recovered for examination and description
Recovery REC is used as a subjective guide to the interpretation of the relative
quality of rock masses Recovery is defined as the actual length of material recovered
from a coring attempt versus the length of the core attempt For example if 3 7 feet of
material is recovered from a 5 0 foot core run the recovery would be 74 percent and would
be shown on the Boring Logs as REC 74
The Rock Quality Designation RQD is also a subjective guide to the relative quality
of rock masses RQD is defined as the percentage of the core run that is sound material
in excess of 4 inches in length without any discontinuities discounting any drilling induced
fractures or breaks If 2 5 feet of sound material is recovered from a 5 0 foot core run the
RQD would be 50 percent and would be shown on the Logs of Borings as RQD 50
Generally the following is used to describe the relative quality of the rock based on the
