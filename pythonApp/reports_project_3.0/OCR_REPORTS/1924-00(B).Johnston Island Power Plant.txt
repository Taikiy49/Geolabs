SUMMARY OF RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration and visual observations at
the site indicate that the generator foundation
blocks were constructed as designed and appear
competent The borings indicate that the
foundation blocks bear on a hard coral layer which
could provide adequate support for the generators
The borings drilled between the generator
foundation blocks encountered about 10 feet of
loose sand underlain by the coral formation
Therefore if new generator foundations are placed
in these areas overexcavation and recompaction of
the loose sand fill partially under water would
be required
Based on the above it appears more economical to
reuse the existing generator foundation blocks to
support the new generators
The text of this report should be referred to for
detailed and special design recommendations
INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
evaluation performed for the proposed rehabilitation of the Johnston
Island Power Plant
The general location of the project site is indicated on the Area
Map Plate 1 and the Project Location Map Plate 2

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this geotechnical engineering evaluation was to
explore the soil conditions below the existing generator foundations to
evaluate if rehabilitation of the foundations is feasible Previous
inspections by others concluded that a major factor contributing to the
malfunctioning of the generators is degradation of the foundation blocks
due to the machinery vibrations which in turn have resulted in increased
vibration of the generators and subsequent bearing and crankshaft
failures
The work was done in general accordance with our proposal dated
to the Department of the Navy Pacific Division Naval
Facilities Engineering Command with the exception that the field
exploration and evaluation of a new power plant was deleted The scope of
work was limited to
1 A site visit by the project manager to make visual observations
of the site conditions and to obtain pertinent information on the
generator foundations and performance from discussions with site
personnel and research of available plans
2 Drilling and sampling seven 7 borings with portable drilling
equipment to depths of 5 7 to 21 0 feet below the existing
concrete slab or generator foundation surface
3 Review of available soil and geologic information pertinent to
the project site and its vicinity
4 Laboratory testing of selected samples obtained from the field
exploration to determine the engineering properties and
classification of the materials encountered
5 Engineering analyses of the field and laboratory data for
formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations

6 Preparation of this report summarizing our work and presenting

PROJECT CONSTDERATTONS
The existing Johnston Island Power Plant will be rehabilitated to
enable it to provide continued service and to meet the increased demand on
the island
The power plant was constructed in 1964 In the past several years
the generators were reported to have been increasingly unreliable
resulting in shutting down of some of the generators and limiting the use
of others Therefore it is proposed to replace the existing generators
foundations and switch gears for the facility
The existing generators are supported by large concrete foundation
blocks which extend to depths of 8 to 10 feet The new generators will be
supported by much smaller foundation blocks and will incorporate base
isolators to minimize vibration to the foundation soils
The construction of a new structure will be considered in the overall
study however the foundation design for a structure is not included in
our present scope of work
SITE CONDITIONS
The existing power plant is situated on the northeastern side of
Johnston Island just north of the inactive East taxiway The existing
building is approximately 97 feet by 180 feet in plan dimensions and
consists of a rigid steel frame structure with cement asbestos siding

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Johnston Island was originally an atoll encompassing approximately 60
acres above the water Subsequent dredge and fill operations have
enlarged the island to approximately 626 acres The existing power plant
site is situated on an area which was on the seaward edge of the original
atoll
Three 3 of the borings Boring and 7 were drilled
through the generator foundation blocks The borings found the concrete
to be 10 5 to 11 5 feet thick The foundation blocks were underlain by a
dense coral limestone formation
The four 4 remaining borings were drilled in the area between the
generator foundation blocks to evaluate the subsoil conditions for
possible support of the new generator foundations The borings
encountered 5 0 to 7 5 inches of concrete slab underlain by loose
coralline sands to depths of about 9 to 12 feet and overlying dense coral
limestone formation
Groundwater level was encountered in the borings at depths of about 6
to 8 feet

DISCUSSTON AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Visual observations of the existing generator foundations did not
disclose obvious signs of distress or movement Some small cracks were
noted at the contact between the concrete blocks and the levelling grout
However this is a common occurrence when different materials are placed
adjacent to each other and at different times Cores of the grout taken
last year by others were observed and found to indicate that the grout was
competent Discussions with the contractor s surveyor indicated that a
recent level survey indicated that the foundations were level to the limit
of accuracy of the surveying instmments
The boring data indicate that the generator foundation blocks were
constructed on the dense coral formation in general accordance with the
design drawings It is our opinion that the dense coral formation is
competent and a suitable bearing stratum for the foundations
Based on the above discussion it is our opinion that the existing
generator foundation blocks do not appear to be the cause of the reported
generator problems

Reuse Existing Foundations
In light of the findings from the field exploration it appears that
one alternative for the new generator foundations would be to reuse the
existing foundations with the necessary modifications to accommodate the
new equipment This method would be relatively simple and cost effective
New Foundation Between Existing Generators
The existing generators are located in line with the colums for the
building thereby increasing the difficulty of removing the generators for
repair or replacement Therefore an alternative being considered under
the current study is to site the new generators between the existing
generator locations to simplify future removal of the new generators
through the walls of the building
The borings indicate that the area between the generators is
underlain by loose sand backfill which extends below the groundwater table
to a total depth of about 10 feet The existing loose sands are
susceptible to settlement due to loading particularly vibration loads
Therefore prior to construction of the new foundations the loose sands
should be overexcavated down to the dense coral formation approximately
10 feet and backfilled in thin lifts with proper compaction to achieve a
minimum of 95 of the maximum dry density according to ASTM Test Method
D 1557 78
Since the loose sand backfill extends below the water table
dewatering of the excavations would be required prior to replacement of
the backfill material The existing sands and coral formation are
generally permeable and could yield large quantities of water The
project dewatering operations should include adequate filtering to prevent
the pumping out of the finer materials which would result in settlement of
adjacent structures

W O 1924 00 B Page 7
Sheeting and bracing of the excavation walls would be important to
prevent lateral movements of the adjacent ground into the excavation
which would result in settlements of adjacent structures The sheeting
and bracing should comply with all applicable safety requirements
Following overexcavation and recompaction of the loose sands an
allowable bearing value of 3 500 p s f may be used in the design of the
new generator foundations
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comment
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this review
is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for
misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during the backfilling and
foundation construction phases of the project This is to observe
compliance with the design concepts specifications or recommendations and
to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ
from that anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations
given in this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the project
site
If actual rock soil conditions encountered during construction are
different from those assumed or considered in this report then
appropriate modifications to the design should be made

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
upon information obtained from the borings Variations of conditions
between the borings may occur and the nature and extent of these
variations may not become evident until construction If variations then
appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations
given in this report
The locations of the borings were determined by taping from
structures indicated on the Foundation and Floor Plan of the project site
prepared by Elevations of the borings were
approximately determined by interpolation from the floor elevation on the
plan The physical location and elevation of the borings should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
boring points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types and
as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross sections are based upon interpolation between borings
and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
Water level data from the borings were collected at the times given
on the graphic representations and or in the text of this report These
data have been reviewed and interpretations made in the formulation of
this report However it must be noted that fluctuation may occur due to
variations in rainfall tides temperature and other factors

Field Exploration

The subsurface conditions at the sites were explored by drilling
seven 7 borings to depths of 5 7 to 21 0 feet below the existing ground
surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 3 A
portable drill rig mobilized from Honolulu was used to drill the borings
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by visual and
textural examination in the field by our engineer who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory All soils
were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A summary of the materials encountered are
presented in the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 7
Core samples of the coral formation were obtained by using a rotary
wash NX core barrel The core recovery REC and rock quality designation
RQD are presented on the Boring Logs
Soil samples were obtained by driving a standard penetration split
spoon sampler with a 140 pound hammer free falling a distance of 30
inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the last 12 inches are shown
on the Boring Logs at the appropriate sample depths
