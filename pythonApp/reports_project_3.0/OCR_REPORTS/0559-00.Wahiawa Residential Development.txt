This report presents the results of a geological field recon
naissance at the subject site The purpose of the study was
to evaluate the geological and shallow soil conditions the sta
bility of existing slopes at the site and to present preliminary
recommendations for site development
SITE LOCATION
The proposed 22 6 acre development site is in Wahiawa Oahu
Hawaii The site s existing slopes are fairly steeply eroded
by one of the branch tributaries of the South Fork stream which
eventually flows into Wahiawa Reservoir This steep sided gully
traverses the center portion of the property in a NE to SW direc
tion dividing the site
The property is covered with grass and a moderately heavy growth
of shrubs on the upper region The stream valley is covered with
W O 559 00 Page 1974
very dense fern forest and tall eucalyptus trees On the western
portion of the site a dirt road leads to an existing house and
garage Cultivated grass lawn and ornamental plants and trees
surround the buildings
The most notable surface features of the site are 1 the deep
gully that runs NE to SW near the center of the property 2 the
heavy growth of vegetation and 3 the upper flat area
GEOLOGY
The site is underlain by decomposed basalt of the Koolau Volcanic
Series of Pliocene Age This series which is more than 3 100
feet thick consists of flows of pahoehoe and aa lava ranging
from 10 to 80 feet in thickness This highly weathered basalt
saprolite which includes all decomposed rock other than the
ferruginous bauxite and residual soils are rich in iron thus
produces shades of red and reddish brown
Saprolite is a soft decomposed rock in which nearly all of the
original minerals have been replaced by secondary minerals but
with most of the original basaltic texture and structure pre
served Much of the rock at the site area has been so thoroughly
weathered that soil and clay textures replace the original basaltic
textures in some places lumps and chunks of saprolite persist in
the soil horizons At the bottom of the gully dark grayish brown
to dark reddish brown rock indicates lesser degrees of weathering
and increasing hardness
W O 559 00 Page 1974
It appears that this highly weathered basalt can be ripped with
conventional grading equipment but as cut depths increase signi
ficantly harder basalt may be encountered
GROUNDWATER
The site area lies within the Schofield Plateau between the Waianae
and the Koolau Ranges The plateau is underlain by a groundwater
reservoir in lavas of the Koolau Volcanic Series The reservoir
has an area of about 15 square miles All natural groundwater
outflow is by subsurface percolation In the site area the flow
is southward into the southern Oahu area The water table ranges
in altitudes from 187 to 300 feet and may decline in steps to the
south
SOIL CONDITIONS
The majority of the natural areas are covered with vegetation and
contains up to six inches of organic top soil Underlying the top
soil and exposed in some slopes are mottled red and brown clayey
silts Laboratory tests reveal the soil to be moderately expansive
In its natural state it is very stiff to extremely stiff The
existing frame structures upon this natural soil appear to be
stable No major fills were observed on the site Loose debris
were found on some of the slopes where apparently some minor earth
movement have occured
W O 559 00 Page 1974
The locations of these movements and the investigation sites are
described in the Appendix A and illustrated on the Site Plan

Boulders and fallen trees on the slopes indicate some unstable
conditions during high discharge periods Limited evidence of
slope erosion and gullying are present but generally the slopes
are well protected by thick layers of vegetation Slope runoff
should be directed away from the footings of proposed structures
and the cut slopes should be protected against erosion
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on our field investigation it is our opinion that the site
is suitable for the proposed construction of townhouse Since
final plans are not available at this time more detailed studies
will be required at a later date
Foundations
It is our opinion that the on site soils can be used to support
the proposed structures The type of foundation will depend upon
the building design and foundation loads but based on our geolo
gical reconnaissance we anticipate the use of spread footings
The footing and slabs on grade should be designed for expansive
soils
W O 559 00 Page 1974
We do not anticipate settlement problems with foundations bearing
in natural ground
Slopes
The site except for the loose debris slopes and some erosion gul
lies along the stream channel is Stable Present plans call for
the construction of 5 per cent slope terraces fill in the eastern
portion of the site Special attention should be given to divert
runoff from this area and to the construction of the 36 inch and
24 inch drain pipes We recommended further investigation in this
proposed fill area
Most of the slopes are well protected by thick layers of vegetation
and we did not foresee any potential slide area Some of the slopes
which have little or no vegetation covers exhibit some slope insta
bility Vertical displacements of up to three feet were observed
Fallen trees and boulders on the creek bottom show high erosional
capability during high discharge periods Where buildings will be
placed at the top of natural slopes additional investigation will
be necessary to evaluate the stability of the slopes
Future Studies
It is our opinion that a minimum of eight test borings will be
necessary to evaluate the overall geologic and soil conditions and
to provide recommendations for detailed foundation and slope designs
