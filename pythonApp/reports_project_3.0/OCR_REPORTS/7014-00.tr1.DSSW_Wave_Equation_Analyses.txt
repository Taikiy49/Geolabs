PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The proposed design-build project is located at Polaris Point within the Naval Base 
in Apra Harbor, Guam. The project consists of construction of a Dehumidified Supply 
Storage Warehouse facility. The new warehouse building will be supported on a deep 
foundation system consisting of 406-mm square precast, prestressed concrete piles. 
The project site is generally level with existing ground elevations ranging from 
about Elevation +3.0 to +3.3 meters Mean Sea Level (MSL). The allowable compressive 
load capacity of the pile is 670 kN, which requires extending the concrete pile to a depth 
of about 26 meters below the ground surface. To assist in setting the piles, pre-drilling is 
allowed to a maximum depth of 6 meters below the ground surface.

HAMMER AND DRIVING SYSTEM 
Healy Tibbitts Builders, Inc. (pile driving subcontractor) proposed to use a 
Junttan HHK 5A hydraulic impact hammer for the pile driving operations. Based on the pile 
driving equipment data form provided, the Junttan HHK 5A hammer has a pile hammer 
weight of 5,000 kg and a maximum stroke of 1.2 meters. The maximum rated energy of the 
proposed Junttan HHK 5A hammer is 58,900 N-meters at the maximum stroke height. 
A 90% hammer efficiency was assumed and used in the wave equation analysis. 
A pile cushion consisting of 230 mm of plywood cushion block is proposed for the 
pile driving operations. The plywood cushion block generally has a cross section area of 
approximately 0.22 square meters for the 406-mm square concrete piles. Schematic 
details of the driving system are presented on the Pile and Driving Equipment Data Form 
in the Appendix (Page 1). 
PILE DETAILS 
The piles consist of 406-mm square precast, prestressed concrete piles. The 
proposed cast length of the 406-mm square piles is 27.5 meters for the test piles 
(1.5 meters longer than the estimated pile length of 26 meters). 
We understand the piles will be cast from concrete with minimum 7-day and 28-day 
unconfined compressive strengths of 34.5 and 41.4 MPa, respectively, as shown on the 
Precast Pile Details in the Appendix (Page 2). The design effective prestress level of the 
406-mm square pile is computed to be approximately 8.5 MPa (1,237 psi). Therefore, the 
maximum allowable stress in compression in the 406-mm square precast, prestressed 
concrete pile during pile driving (minimum 7-day cure) is about 20.8 MPa (3,013 psi), while 
the maximum allowable stress in tension is approximately 8.5 MPa (1,237 psi).

The wave equation analyses were performed using the computer program, 
GRLWEAP (Wave Equation Analysis of Pile Foundations), which is an extension of the 
WEAP87 program developed under the sponsorship of the Federal Highway 
Administration.  
Wave equation analyses were performed based on the pile driving system 
proposed by Healy Tibbitts Builders, Inc. and the subsurface conditions presented in the 
referenced geotechnical engineering report. The design pile foundation and subsurface 
profile are summarized below.
