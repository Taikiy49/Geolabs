INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Ewa Il Elementary School located within the Ewa by Gentry
development in the District of Ewa on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location
and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings from our field exploration and laboratory
testing and presents our geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our
analyses for the proposed project These recommendations are intended for design
input only and are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from two shield
volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be of
the middle to late Pliocene in age and forms the bulk of the western third of the island
The younger shield Koolau is estimated to be of the late Pliocene to early Pleistocene
Ice Age in age and forms the majority of the eastern two thirds of the island
Waianae became extinct while Koolau was still active and its eastern flank was partially
buried below Koolau lavas banking against its eastern flank These banked or ponded
lavas formed a broad plateau referred to as the Schofield Plateau or Central Oahu
The forces of weathering and erosion attacked this plateau generating sediments
which were transported to the Ewa Coastal Plain At the vicinity of the project site and
further south these alluvial sediments accumulated along with and interbedded with
marine sediments and coral agal reef formations to form a sedimentary wedge ranging
in thickness from zero feet in the area of H 1 Freeway to over 1 000 feet at Ewa Beach
This wedge forms the Ewa Plain and serves as the confining formation or caprock
over the artesian basal aquifers of southern Oahu Deposition of these sediments has
continued from earlier geologic time during the 25 foot Waimanalo stand of the sea
to present

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field exploration the project site appears to be underlain primarily
by surficial fill materials and dense coral algal reef formation interbedded with severely
jointed coral detritus deposits Our laboratory swell test results indicate that the surficial
fill materials generally exhibit slight shrink swell potential when subjected to fluctuations
in the moisture content
Based on the site and grading plan cuts are not anticipated at the site New fills
on the order of up to about 6 feet would be needed to provide level building pads for
the proposed construction Therefore most of the proposed improvements and
buildings will be constructed on the new fills needed to raise the grades at the site

Based on our field exploration and laboratory test results we believe that the
new compacted fills and on site soils encountered near the existing ground surface
would provide adequate foundation support for the new building structures In general
we believe that shallow footings and slab on grade foundations bearing on the
compacted new fills and or on site soils may be used to support the new elementary
school

At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable
materials should be removed and properly disposed of off site
After demolition clearing and grubbing slab or footing subgrades and areas to
receive fills should be scarified to a minimum depth of 8 inches moisture conditioned
to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to not less than 90 percent
relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil
expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established
in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91 Optimum moisture is the water
content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density

Any soft weak or yielding areas disclosed during clearing operations should be
over excavated to expose firm ground and the resulting excavation should be filled with
engineered fill moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture and compacted to
a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction The excavated soft and or organic soils
should be properly disposed of off site
Where shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction we recommend that the soil
be thoroughly moistened to close all cracks Saturation and subsequent yielding of the
exposed subgrade due to inclement weather and poor drainage may require over
excavation of the soft areas and replacement with well compacted engineered fill
Since import fills will likely be needed for the project construction we recommend
that import fills consist of granular material such as crushed coral basalt gravel or
cinder sand The material should be well graded with no particles greater than 3 inches
in largest dimension Import fill should have a laboratory CBR value of 12 or higher
and a swell of less than 1 percent when tested in accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 1883 The material should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches
in loose thickness and compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction The
upper 2 feet of fill material below the proposed building pad elevation and pavements
should be compacted to not less than 95 percent relative compaction
Our laboratory swell test results indicate that the surficial fill materials which
consist of clayey silty soils generally exhibit slight shrink swell potential when subjected
to moisture fluctuations Therefore if needed the on site silty clayey soils may also be
used as fill materials provided that they are placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches
in loose thickness moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum
moisture content and compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction

Foundations
Based on the information provided we understand that column loads on the
order of about 80 kips and wall loads on the order of about 6 kips per lineal foot are
anticipated Because of the generally competent subsoil conditions encountered at the
site we believe that shallow spread and or continuous footings and slab on grade
foundations may be used to support the proposed elementary school structures
An allowable bearing pressure of 3 500 p s f may be used for design of footings
bearing on the import fill and or on site soils encountered near the existing ground
surface This bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by one
third for transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic forces The
footings should be embedded a minimum depth of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent
finish grade
We estimate the footing settlements under the anticipated design loads for
footings bearing on the compacted import fill or dense in situ soils to be less than
1 inch total and inch differential
If foundations are located next to utility trenches or easements they should be
embedded below a 1H 1V imaginary plane extending upward from the bottom edge of
the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility lines
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3386 00 Page 11
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive earth pressure
acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system and by frictional
resistance developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fiuid
pressure of 350 pounds per square foot per foot of depth p c f This assumes that
the soil around footings is well compacted or undisturbed Unless covered by
pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the upper 12 inches of soil should be
neglected A coefficient of friction of 0 35 may be used for footings bearing on the
compacted import fill and or dense in situ soils
It is recommended that the bottom of all footing excavations be compacted to a
minimum of 95 percent relative compaction prior to placement of reinforcing steel and
concrete Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as
a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance
with procedures described in ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91
If soft and or loose materials are encountered at the bottom of footing
excavations they should be over excavated to expose firm materials The over
excavation should either be backfilled with engineered fill material compacted to a
minimum of 95 percent relative compaction or the footing bottom be extended down to
firm soil
Footing excavations should be observed by a representative of Geolabs Hawaii
prior to the placement of reinforcing steel or concrete to confirm foundation bearing
conditions and the embedment depths

Slab On Grade
We anticipate that concrete slabs on grades will be used for the buildings at the
proposed elementary school In accordance with the requirements of D A G S
Technical Memorandum No 61 dated a basaltic termite barrier BTB
will need to be incorporated into the design of new facilities Therefore concrete floor
slabs should be supported directly on a 6 inch layer of compacted BTB above the
prepared subgrade to serve as both a slab cushion and a termite barrier
For slabs on grade with moisture sensitive surfacing we recommend that an
impermeable membrane be placed over the compacted BTB to reduce potential
migration of moisture vapor through the concrete slab In order to promote more
uniform curing of the slab concrete and to provide protection of the vapor membrane
we recommend that a 2 inch layer of BTB be placed above the membrane prior to
placing the slab concrete
Where concrete slabs will be subjected to vehicular traffic such as driveways
and loading areas a 6 inch layer of base course aggregate is recommended below the
concrete slab to serve as a slab cushion in lieu of the basaltic termite barrier The
base course supporting the concrete slab should consist of crushed basalt aggregates
compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
For structural slabs bearing on compacted base course a modulus of subgrade
reaction of 250 pounds per cubic inch p c i may be used We recommend that the
concrete slab be designed by a structural engineer for the intended loads and should
be reinforced as a structural slab to accommodate some amount of differential
movements The thickened edges of slabs adjacent to any unpaved area should be
embedded at least 12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade

Utility Trench
We envision that new utility lines will be required for the project In general a
granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67
gradation is recommended under the pipes Free draining granular materials such as
No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation should also be used for the trench
backfill up to about 1 foot above the pipes to provide adequate support around the
pipes and to reduce the compaction effort of the backfill thus reducing the possibility
of damaging the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 1 foot above the pipes to
the top of the subgrade or finished grade may consist of on site material with no

particles greater than 6 inches in maximum size The backfill should be placed in
maximum 8 inch level loose lifts and mechanically compacted to not less than

Where trenches are below pavement areas the upper 2 feet of the trench backfill below
the pavement subgrade should be compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative
compaction
Drainage
The finish grades outside the building should be sloped to shed water away from
foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also advised that gutter
systems be installed around the building and that the discharge be diverted away from
the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near foundations
and slabs should also be avoided Planters next to foundations should be avoided or
have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil
saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations
should be properly backfilled against the walls or slab footings immediately after setting
of the concrete to reduce water infiltration
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and should be main
tained to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundations

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
eg
Moisture content and unit weight determinations were performed on selected soil
samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties The results of
these tests are presented on the logs of borings at the appropriate sample depths
Five one inch Ring Swell tests were performed on relatively undisturbed and
remolded samples of the near surface soils to evaluate the swelling potential of the
soils The results of these tests are summarized on Plate B 1
Two Atterberg Limits tests were performed on selected soil samples to evaluate
their liquid and plastic limits and to aid in soil classification A summary of the test
results is presented on Plate B 2
Three Sieve Analysis tests were performed on selected soil samples to evaluate
the gradation characteristics of the soils The test results are presented on Plate B 3
One Compaction test ASTM D 1557 91 was performed on a selected bulk
sample of the near surface soils to evaluate the moisture content and dry density
relationship Graphic presentation of the test results is provided on Plate B 4
One California Bearing Ratio CBR test was performed on a bulk sample
collected for pavement design The test results are presented on Plate B 5
