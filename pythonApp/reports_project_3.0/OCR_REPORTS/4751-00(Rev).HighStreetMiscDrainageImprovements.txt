In accordance with our proposal dated we have performed a
geotechnical engineering exploration for the High Street Miscellaneous Drainage
Improvements project in the vicinity of Main Street to Keanu Street in the Wailuku area on
the Island of Maui Hawaii This revised letter report summarizes the findings from our
field exploration at the project site These findings are intended for design input only and
are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report Work on site was performed
on 2001
Field Exploration
The project site is located along High Street between Kolo Place and Kahookele
Street in Wailuku on the Island of Maui Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the
project site is shown on the attached Project Location Map Plate 1
Two borings were drilled on High Street at the approximate locations designated by
Department of Transportation DOT personnel Boring No 1 was drilled at approximately
Station 295 45 offset o s 5 feet left and Boring No 2 was drilled at approximately Sta
298 75 o s 7 feet right Offsets were reckoned from the existing solid double yellow
center lines on the street The locations of the drilled borings are shown on the enclosed
Site Plan Plate 2
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling the borings utilizing
truck mounted drilling equipment The borings were drilled to depths of approximately
25 4 and 26 5 feet for Boring Nos 1 and 2 respectively Soil samples were obtained by

Subsurface Conditions
The two borings were drilled on High Street which is covered with a 4 inch thick
asphaltic concrete pavement Below the pavement Boring B 1 encountered silty sands
with gravel to a depth of 11 5 feet followed by stiff to hard silty clay to the total depth
explored of 25 4 feet Boring B 2 encountered a thin surficial layer of base course material
overlying silty clay with sand and gravel underlain by a layer of silty sand to a depth of
8 feet From 8 feet extending to the total depth explored of 26 5 feet hard clayey silt and
silty clay with sand and gravel were encountered
Detailed descriptions of the materials encountered in the borings are presented on
the Logs of Borings Plates 4 and 5
Laboratory Testing
Moisture Content determination AASHTO T 27 and gradation analyses AASHTO
T 265 were performed on samples selected by DOT as an aid in the soil classification and
evaluating soil properties A summary of the moisture content tests is summarized on
Plate 8 The results of the moisture content tests are shown on the logs of borings at the
appropriate sample depths Gradation analysis results are shown on Plate 9
Limitations
The purpose of our substrata investigation was to obtain a general overview of
the subsurface conditions at the High Street Miscellaneous Drainage Improvements
project in the vicinity of Main Street to Keanu Street site
It should be noted that variations of subsoil conditions between and beyond the
field borings may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become
evident until construction is underway
