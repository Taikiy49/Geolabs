SUMMARY OF FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
New pavements will be required for the Wilson Tunnel Improvements project along
Likelike Highway Because the project site is located in a high rainfall environment we
believe that a permeable drainage layer will be required for the pavement design in
accordance with the Pavement Design Manual by the Materials Testing and Research
Branch dated March 2002 In addition it is our opinion that asphaltic concrete pavements
may not provide adequate skid resistance for the steep roadway grade at the tunnels
Therefore we have only considered a rigid pavement structural section with a permeable
drainage layer in our analyses for the project
Based on our analyses we recommend that a rigid pavement section consisting of
10 5 inches of PCC placed on 6 inches of UTPB over 6 inches of ASB be used for the
design of pavements for the Wilson Tunnel Improvements project along Likelike Highway
The pavement surface should be sloped and drainage gradients should be
maintained to carry surface water off the pavement to appropriate drainage structures To
collect infiltrated water into the pavement structural section we recommend that a
pavement edge drain system hydraulically connected to the untreated permeable base
be provided along the edges of the new pavement areas
The text of this pavement justification report should be referred to for detailed
discussion and specific recommendations for design of pavements

2 1 Regional Geology
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from two main shield
volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older shield volcano Waianae Volcano is
estimated to be middle to late Pliocene in age and forms the bulk of the western third of
the island The younger shield volcano Koolau Volcano is estimated to be late Pliocene
to early Pleistocene Ice Age in age and forms the majority of the eastern two thirds of the
island The Waianae Volcano became extinct while the Koolau Volcano remained active
Therefore the older Waianae Volcano s eastern flank was partially buried below the
younger Koolau Volcano lavas that banked against the Waianae s eastern flank These
banked and ponded lava flows formed a broad upland plateau referred to as the Schofield
Plateau of Central Oahu
During the evolutionary history of the Island of Oahu fluctuation of the ocean s sea
level occurred as a result of the worldwide advance and retreat of the great continental
glaciers These sea level changes occurred more substantially during the Pleistocene
Epoch and had some effect on the geologic evolution of the Island of Oahu The erosional
baseline of terrestrial streams was affected and caused local submergence and
emergence of the coastal island landforms with respect to the level of the sea In addition
to sea level fluctuations the entire island mass experienced settlement as the weight of
the volcanic deposits depressed the oceanic crust beneath the island Together the sea
level fluctuations and land mass adjustments have imparted some complexity to the
geologic evolution of the Island of Oahu
The end of volcanic activity associated with the Koolau Volcano was followed by a
long period of landmass erosion whereby deep stream valleys were carved into the dome
shaped Koolau Shield Volcano The deeper valley floors were filled with thick
accumulations of alluvial sediment and colluvial landslide material as the island eroded
and slowly depressed beneath the weight of the new landmass

Stream erosion landslide activity and ocean wave attack are believed to have
contributed to the formation of the steep and elongated located along
the windward side of the Island of Oahu The existing elongated spine of the Koolau
Mountains represents the erosional remnant of the primary volcanic rift axis of the Koolau
Shield Volcano The width of the Koolau Pali which separates the valley heads located
on either side of the Pali windward and leeward has been slowly narrowing in response
to the erosional retreat of the heads of the opposing windward and leeward valleys The
erosional retreat of the Koolau Pali is most apparent at the heads of Kalihi and Nuuanu
Valleys The erosion occurring on either side of the Koolau Pali is contributing to the
formation of narrow wind gaps of reduced mountain height and thickness located at the
heads of these valleys The erosional gaps in the Koolau Pali are logical places for
trans Koolau routes such as the Likelike and Pali Highways as tunnel lengths are shorter
Approximately 850 000 to years ago some limited volcanic activity
resumed with the eruption of the Honolulu Volcanic Series from secondary vents scattered
across the eastern portion of the Island of Oahu The eruptions occurred along the
established secondary rift zones of the Koolau Volcano Some of the most notable
landmarks located on the Island of Oahu such as Salt Lake Koko Head and Diamond
Head Craters were created by the eruption of the Honolulu Volcanic Series The craters
are composed of hardened cinder and volcanic ash tuff and some basaltic lava flows
The eruption of the Honolulu Volcanic Series continued over a period of several hundred
thousand years which ended approximately 30 000 years ago
2 2 Climate Conditions
The Island of Oahu is located in the tropics in the Pacific Ocean The Island of
Oahu is characterized by mild temperatures throughout the year moderate humidity
persistent northeasterly trade winds significant differences in rainfall within short
distances and infrequent severe storms
The Island of Oahu is considered semitropical rather than tropical where the
temperature of the surrounding waters is about 10 degrees lower than that of some other
W O INC Page 5
Hawaii California

regions of equal latitude The temperatures on the Island of Oahu typically range between
52 degrees and 90 degrees Fahrenheit The lowest temperature varies with altitude
Typically there is an average decrease of about 4 degrees in the mean temperature with
each increase of 1 000 feet in altitude
Trade winds northeasterly winds are persistent on the Island of Oahu throughout
most of the year Wind velocities at Honolulu average about 10 miles per hour mph
However Kona winds southerly winds occasionally may interrupt the trade winds but for
no more than a few days at a time The Kona winds tend to be associated with high
humidity The relative humidity may reach 95 percent on days where Kona winds are
present
The rainfall on the Island of Oahu may vary from as low as 6 inches per year in the
leeward lowland areas to as high as 250 inches per year over the upper mountain slopes
and crests on the windward side of the island The leeward areas obtain their rainfall
mainly from a few winter storms Therefore the rainfall is usually seasonal and the
summers are dry In the wetter regions the rainfall comes from both winter storms and
trade wind showers The rainfall varies widely from year to year In regions of high
rainfall there is no well marked rainy season and droughts may occur in either the winter
or summer Rainfall at the project site may occur on a near daily basis The average
annual rainfall at the project site ranges between 100 and 200 inches per year The
precipitation at the project site may be considered very high

constructed for the tunnel improvements project Because the project site is located in a
heavy rainfall environment we recommend that a permeable drainage layer be
