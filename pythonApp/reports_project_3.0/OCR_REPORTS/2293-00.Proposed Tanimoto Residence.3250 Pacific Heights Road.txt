INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the proposed Tanimoto residence at 3250 Pacific
Heights Road in Honolulu Oahu Hawaii The general location of the site
and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The
exploration was performed in general accordance with our proposal dated
March 30 1989
W O 2293 00 Page 2
This report summarizes our findings from the exploration and
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analysis based
on the data obtained during our exploration These recommendations are
intended for design input and are not intended for construction estimation
or bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSTDERATTIONS
Based on the plans provided to us the proposed new residence will be
a two story structure approximately 50 by 140 feet in plan dimensions
with a two level basement at the front of the residence It is
anticipated that the residence will be of wood and masonry construction
with slabs on grade for the lower level floors A 82 by 14 by 4 feet deep
swimming pool is planned at the southwest portion of the site
Site grading involving cuts up to 13 feet southern portion and
minimal fills is anticipated

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling of four
4 borings to depths ranging from 19 2 to 20 0 feet below the existing
ground surface The approximate locations of the borings are shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2
In general the borings at the project site encountered a surface
fill layer of stiff to very stiff dark brown silty clays with traces of
sand approximately 2 0 to 4 0 feet in thickness Below this surface
layer the borings encountered stiff to hard mottled reddish brown and
gray silty clays ranging in thickness between 5 5 to 8 0 feet Underlying
this stratum stiff to hard mottled gray and brownish gray clayey silt
were encountered to the maximm depths drilled These clayey silts are
saprolite soils which are soils derived from the in situ chemical
weathering of rock and retain some or all of the original structures of
the parent rock
The surface fill layer was not encountered in Boring No 2
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings at the time of our
field exploration

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATTONS
General
The stiff underlying on site soils encountered can generally provide
adequate foundation support for the proposed house provided that some care
is exercised during construction to reduce the swelling potential of the
soil as recommended herein
The field exploration and laboratory tests performed on the stiff
on site silty clay indicate that this material generally has low to
moderate swelling potential depending on the initial moisture content of
the soil prior to subsequent saturation Therefore to reduce the effects
due to soil swelling we recommend that the on site silty clay soils below
on grade slabs be capped with at least twelve 12 inches of non expansive
select fill and that the subgrade soils below the select fill and slab be
moistened and protected from drying during construction until the slab
concrete placement and backfill
Tot Grading
The subgrade soil and backfills within the top twelve 12 inches
below the on grade slabs should consist of non expansive soils such as
select borrow crusher waste or coral In the basement cut where low
swelling clayey silt soils are anticipated below the on grade slab the
twelve inch 12 over excavation and replacement may be omitted
All imported fill material should be tested for suitability by the
project geotechnical engineer prior to transportation to the site
W O 2293 00 Page 6
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limits should be cleared of vegetation debris rubbish and other
deleterious materials These materials should be removed and disposed of
properly off site Any loose or soft spots encountered at the subgrade
areas should be over excavated down to the firm subsoils and then replaced
with compacted non expansive select material
The fill should be placed in maximum eight inch 8 thick loose
level lifts and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximm dry density
per ASTM D 1557 78
House Foundation
Spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the proposed
residence An allowable bearing value of 2 500 p s f may be used for
footings bearing on the compacted natural soil or fill
Individual or wall footings on level ground should be embedded a
minimum of eighteen 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade
Slabs On Grade
It should be noted that the on site silty clay soils may be
moderately expansive if allowed to dry and then saturated Therefore the
on site silty clay soils should be over excavated down to a depth of
twelve 12 inches below the slab bottom the subgrade should then be
thoroughly moistened and then immediately replaced with moistened
non expansive imported soils as previously discussed The thickened edge
of the slab should be embedded a minimm of twelve 12 inches below the
lowest adjacent finished grade
To further reduce future post construction swelling of the underlying
expansive soils we recommend that the in place silty clay below the upper
capping fill pad be kept moist during construction and prior to concrete
placement
W O 2293 00 Page 7
In the deep cuts extending into the low swelling saprolite soils the
twelve inch 12 capping fill pad may be omitted
A 4 inch layer of 3 Fine gravel cushion fill should be provided
below the slab In order to protect against future moisture infiltration
an impervious moisture barrier may be incorporated below the slab

Swimming Pool Design
Swimming pool design and construction are generally the
responsibility of the swimming pool builder This contractor is also
generally responsible for quality control and performance of the completed
pool
Since low to moderately expansive soil was encountered non expansive
select fill material is recommended under the perimeter walkways and slab
areas The pool walls and bottom should be properly designed to resist
possible swelling pressures due to future saturation of the exposed
subgrade soils We recommend that the swimming pool contractor should
seek design guidance from qualified consultants to help them to properly
design and to monitor the proposed pool construction
Concrete Driveway
Since the driveway can tolerate some amount of heaving the
twelve inch 12 on site soil over excavation and replacement may be
eliminated if some amount of small slab cracking are tolerable However
the subgrade soils should be maintained in a moist condition and prevented
from drying out prior to concrete placement
W O 2293 00 Page 10
Joints are also recommended in the driveway slab at about 8 to
10 foot intervals and at discontinuities in order to allow for some
relative movement of the concrete due to shrinkage of the concrete
uneven stress concentrations and or soil movement
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comments
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this review
is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for
misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during the site grading and
construction of the foundations This is to observe compliance with the
design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow design
changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from that
anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in
this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field borings Variations of
conditions between the field data points may occur and the nature and
W Page 11
extent of these variations may not become evident until construction If
variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations given in this report
The location of the field borings were approximately determined by
taping from structures indicated on the plans provided Elevations of the
data points were approximately determined by interpolation from elevation
points on the plans The physical location and elevation of the field
data points should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by
the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross sections are based upon interpolation between field data
points and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
This report has been prepared for the exclusive use of the project
designers for specific application to the site grading and foundation
design of the project in accordance with generally accepted geotechnical
engineering principles and practices No other warranty expressed or
implied is made
This report has been prepared solely for the purpose of assisting the
architect engineer in the preliminary design evaluation of the proposed
project Therefore this report may not contain sufficient data or the
proper information for use in contract bid estimation A contractor
wishing to bid on this project is urged to retain a competent geotechnical
engineer to assist in the interpretation of this report and or in the

purposes
