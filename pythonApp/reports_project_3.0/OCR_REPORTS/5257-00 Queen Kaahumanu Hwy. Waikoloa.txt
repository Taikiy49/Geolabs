SUMMARY OF FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
New pavements will be required for the Queen Kaahumanu Highway and Mauna
Lani Drive Intersection Improvements project Because the project will involve less than
4 lane miles of new pavements a detailed economic comparison involving a rigid
pavement section and flexible pavement sections will not be required for the highway
intersection improvements project
Based on our site reconnaissance experience in the project area and the current
design concept we envision that the new pavements will be constructed either on 6 inch
minus granular fill materials in the fill areas or on the in situ basalt rock formation in the
cut areas We believe that the 6 inch minus granular fill materials should provide
adequate drainage for the new pavements due to the generally permeable nature and
elevated grades of the embankment conditions For new pavements constructed on the
in situ vesicular porous basalt rock formation in the cut areas we believe that the
potential for accumulation of water would be minimal and should not adversely affect the
performance of the pavement structure Therefore we believe that the inclusion of an
Untreated Permeable Base UTPB layer for subdrainage purposes would not be
necessary for the subject project
Based on the subsurface conditions anticipated at the project site the traffic data
provided and our preliminary cost comparisons we recommend that the pavement
section for the Queen Kaahumanu Highway and Mauna Lani Drive Intersection
Improvements project consist of 4 inches of AC placed on 6 inches of ACB If needed a
leveling course consisting of free draining materials conforming to AASHTO M43
Size No 67 gradation may be provided on the prepared pavement subgrade prior to the
placement of the ACB The leveling course should be proof rolled to a dense and
unyielding condition prior to placement of the ACB
The text of this report should be referred to for detailed discussion and specific
recommendations for pavement design

1 1 Introduction
This pavement justification report presents the results of our analyses for the
design of new pavements for the Queen Kaahumanu Highway and Mauna Lani Drive
Intersection Improvements project located in the Waikoloa area in the District of South
Kohala on the Island of Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are
shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings from our literature research and site
reconnaissance and presents our geotechnical recommendations derived from our
analyses for the design of new pavements only The findings and recommendations
presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report
1 2 Project Considerations
The project site is located at the intersection of Queen Kaahumanu Highway and
Mauna Lani Drive in the Waikoloa area in the District of South Kohala on the Island of
Hawaii Based on the information provided we understand that it is proposed to widen
the northbound lanes mauka side of the existing Queen Kaahumanu Highway for

vehicular access to the future planned development along Mauna Lani Drive

about Station 1285 60 as shown on the Site Plans Plates 2 1 through 2 3 In general
the existing highway will be widened to three lanes along the mauka side of the
highway A new intersection will be constructed at about Station 1275 00 to provide
vehicular access to the planned future development along the mauka bound
Mauna Lani Drive
Based on the preliminary roadway plans provided we envision that the roadway
construction will involve minimal fills on the order of about 1 to 2 feet thick and cuts of

SECTION 2 0 SITE GEOLOGY
The Island of Hawaii the largest in the Hawaiian Archipelago covers an area of
approximately 4 000 square miles The island was formed by the activity of five shield
volcanoes which include the following Kohala which is long extinct Mauna Kea
which has had activity during recent geologic time Hualalai which last erupted in 1801
and Mauna Loa and Kilauea both of which are still active
The project site is situated on the northwestern flank of Mauna Kea Mountain
which makes up the west central portion of the island The lava formation encountered
at the site appears to consist of both a a and pahoehoe type flows which spread and
ponded as they approached the ocean A a lava is typically characterized by a porous
rough and irregular flow surface resembling a jagged accumulation of rock fragments
including cobbles and boulders Pahoehoe is characterized by a smooth rope like or
billowy surface and an internal structure of vesicular porous rock
Cavities are commonly encountered in basaltic lavas especially pahoehoe type
lava These cavities are formed when the lava is still in a molten state and they
represent both lava tubes intra flow cavities and blisters and pockets inter flow
cavities Lava tubes are formed when molten lava drains from the cooling flow leaving
a hollow tube like structure which may extend for a large distance longitudinally along
the flow Blisters and pockets are generally smaller in horizontal extent
Basalt is considered to be a relatively permeable rock and can transmit water
quite readily in both the horizontal and vertical directions In general water is
transmitted through the porous rock matrix along joints fractures and _ inter flow
contacts cavities and along clinker layers The permeability of the clinker and cavities
is high therefore they serve as the primary water transmission features

3 3 Design Subgrade Conditions
Based on our site reconnaissance experience in the area and the current
design concept we anticipate the pavement subgrade soils likely will consist of
compacted 6 inch minus granular fill materials imported and or in situ basalt rock
formation for the proposed highway intersection improvements project Based on our
experience with similar subgrade materials we believe that the 6 inch minus granular fill
materials and the in situ basalt rock formation would exhibit a Resistance R value
equivalent to that of an aggregate subbase material Therefore a design R value of 60
was adopted in our pavement analyses for the subgrade materials

Preparation Below Pavement Section
Based on the preliminary plans and profiles we understand that minimal fills on
the order of about 1 to 2 feet thick and cuts of up to about 6 to 8 feet deep will be
needed for the proposed highway intersection improvement construction In general
the area within the contract grading limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed at
the on set of earthwork Vegetation debris deleterious materials and other unsuitable
materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site or disposed of in a
designated area to reduce the potential for contamination of the excavated materials
Soft and yielding areas encountered during clearing and grubbing below areas
designated to receive fill or future improvements should be over excavated to expose
dense materials The resulting excavation should be backfilled with well compacted
granular fills The excavated soft and or organic soils should be properly disposed of
off site
After clearing and grubbing the future pavement areas should be excavated
where necessary to the pavement subgrade level i e bottom of the asphalt concrete
base layer In areas where the pavement subgrades expose dense clinker materials or
basalt rock formation the subgrades should be proof rolled with a minimum 15 ton
vibratory roller for a minimum of eight passes to help detect and collapse near surface
cavities Loose areas or cavities disclosed during clearing and proof rolling operations
should be backfilled with well compacted granular fill materials The proof rolling
operation also should be performed at the finished subgrades in cut areas The
vibratory drum roller should travel at a speed of about 200 to 300 feet per minute Due
to the potential for presence of cavities lava tubes blisters pockets etc and soft
and or wet soils contract documents should include additive and deductive unit prices
for over excavation and structural fill placement to account for variations in the
over excavation and backfill quantities

3 6 Fill Materials
As mentioned previously we have assumed that the recommended pavement
section will be underlain by structural fill materials 6 inch minus granular fills in the fill
areas For this project we recommend that the upper 2 feet of fill below the pavement
areas located above a fill section should consist of structural fill materials 6 inch minus
granular fills In general structural fill materials should consist of well graded granular
materials generally less than 6 inches in maximum dimension with sufficient fines to
prevent the occurrence of voids in the compacted mass
Imported fill material where required should consist of non expansive granular
material such as crushed basalt or cinder sand The material should be well graded
from coarse to fine with no particles greater than 6 inches in largest dimension The
material should have a CBR value of 25 or more and should have a maximum swell
value of 1 percent or less In addition the material should have a minimum R value of
60 or more Imported fill materials should be tested for conformance with these
recommendations prior to delivery to the project site for the intended use
3 7 Compaction Requirements

large rock fragments and or cobbles Instead a testing program to evaluate the number
of passes by a compactor needed to achieve the desired level of compaction should be
conducted at the start of the grading phase of the project under the observation of a
representative from Geolabs Based on this testing program the number of passes
may then be used as the field criterion for adequate compaction For preliminary

3 8 Pavement Drainage
One of the primary distress mechanisms in pavement structures is pumping due
to saturation of the aggregate subbase and or subgrade soils The basalt rock
formation in this area generally consists of vesicular porous pahoehoe lava The
basalt rock formation in this area of the Island of Hawaii drains well and may be
considered to be highly permeable Considering the permeable nature of the subgrade
materials granular fill materials or basalt rock pavement drainage for this project is not
as critical as in other areas As a minimum the pavement surface should be sloped
and drainage gradients should be maintained to carry surface water off the pavement to

site during or after construction Where landscaping is planned adjacent to the

Subdrains should be provided in areas where seepage is observed during and or
