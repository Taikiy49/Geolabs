In accordance with our proposal of May 25 2001 we conducted a geotechnical
engineering exploration at the project site Our preliminary findings and recommendations
were presented in our letter of August 16 2001 This letter report presents our updated
findings and recommendations based on the results of the recent monitoring period
Project Considerations
The scope of our work involved an assessment of the possible causes of
settlement that had occurred on the existing five story building at 1634 Nuuanu Avenue
located in Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the
project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
Two specific areas of concern were identified The first is a CMU non load bearing
wall located on the first level of the parking area facing Nuuanu Avenue This wall
appeared to have settled and had separated from the adjacent structure The second and
more significant area of concern is the separation and apparent settlement of the southern
portion of the building Large cracks are visible in the railing and stairway between the
southern and northern portions of the building It was suspected that these signs of
distress may be due to seepage poor backfill or subgrade preparation before the building
was constructed or poor shrink swell characteristic of the underlying ground surface
Field Exploration
Two borings were drilled at the site The approximate locations of the borings are
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Boring No 1 was drilled near the loading area on the western side of the building
This boring was drilled to a depth of about 27 5 feet below the existing pavement level
The boring encountered stiff silty clay to a depth of about 6 5 feet over about 1 5 feet of
boulders underlain by hard basalt rock formation to the full depth of the boring
Groundwater was not encountered in the boring at the time of the field exploration

Findings and Recommendations
First Floor CMU Wall
The settlement of this wall may be due to saturation and softening of the soils
below the wall footing It is our understanding that this wall will be replaced We
recommend that the new wall footing be underlain by a 2 foot thick layer of
structural fill The structural fill should consist of granular material less than
3 inches in size and with 10 to 30 percent fines The material should also have a
CBR greater than 20 and swell less than 1 percent when tested in accordance with
ASTM D 1883 The structural fill should be compacted to a minimum of 90 percent
of the maximum dry density in accordance with ASTM D 1557
We also recommend that large trees be kept away from the wall to reduce the
potential for root growth under the wall which could cause lifting of this light
structure
Settlement of Structure
The boring data indicates that the site is underlain by about 8 feet of fill and
alluvium silty clay over basalt rock formation The building drawings indicate that
the footing bottoms were established at about 3 to 4 feet below the pavement
level Therefore the footings appear to bear on the stiff upper soils not on the
basalt rock
It appears that some of the footings in the southern portion of the building may be
settling due to compression of the soil layer The settlement may be arrested or
reduced considerably by underpinning the affected footings with piers or pipe piles
extending down to the rock formation The costs for underpinning will vary
depending on the structural loads number of piers needed accessibility for
construction equipment and number of footings affected We estimate that the
underpinning may cost a minimum of about per footing In order to get
a better understanding of the repair costs we recommend the following

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from the field borings Variations of subsoil conditions between and
beyond the field borings may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not
become evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident Geolabs
should be contacted to re evaluate the recommendations presented in this report
The boring locations in the field were determined by taping from the Building Plot
Plan undated The physical locations of the field borings should be considered accurate
only to the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown on the graphic representations of the borings depict
the approximate boundaries between the soil types and as such may denote a gradual
transition Water level data from the borings were measured at the times shown on the
graphic representations and or in the text of this report These data have been reviewed
and interpretations made in the formulation of this report However it must be noted that
fluctuation may occur due to tidal fluctuation variation in rainfall temperatures and other
factors
