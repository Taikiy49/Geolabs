Project Consideration
Ihe HOST NEIH Final Environment Impact statement 1985 and Draft
supplemental Environmental Impact Statement for Alternative Methods of
Seawater Return Flow Disposal at NE1H 1986 proposed that a water quality
monitoring program be implemented to obtain factual data on the effects of
on land ocean water disposal on the quality of groundwater and natural
ecosystems present in offshore coastal waters and if necessacy to assist
in the implementation of mitigating measures
Monitoring of on land disposal of cold seawater return flow and of
mariculture waste is particularly important because preservation of the
integrity of the cold and warm ocean water resources is fundamental for
the continued growth and success of the existing and future HOST and NE1H
projects
If the water is degraded the projects will no longer have the
unique resource necessacy to attract the energy and mariculture activities
important to their success

The on land monitoring program would be implemented in three phases
The first phase would include a preliminary study to identify the
monitoring parameters locate the on land monitoring stations and develop
groundw ater quality monitoring well design aiid pecification guidelines
The second phase of the monitoring program would include drilling and
installation of the on land monitoring wells and establishment of the
baseline conditions for future groundwater quality evaluation
The third phase would be the implementation of long ten n water
quality and biota monitoring program as recommended in the Environmental
Impact Statements
