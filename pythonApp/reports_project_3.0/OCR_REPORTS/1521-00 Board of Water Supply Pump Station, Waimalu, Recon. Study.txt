Temporary Repair of Cut Slope Near The

In accordance with your request and our proposal of February 13
1985 we have made a reconnaissance study and intermittently observed
temporary slope repairs on the above captioned project
General Site Description
The project site consits of a steep cut bank with a slope ratio
varying from about 1 2 to 1 1 H V located adjacent to the residence at
Place and the Board of Water Supply Pump Station The cut
slope is about 40 feet high and is composed of basaltic lava flows in
varying degrees of weathering The upper 12 to 15 feet is a blocky more
massive AA basalt that has undergone some concentric onion skin

weathering with some silty soils along the fracture planes The lower
30 feet of the slope is a more coherent mass that appears to be a highly
weathered cemented clinker and basaltic rock
Purpose and Scope
The purpose of this geological reconnaissance was to observe the
surface condition of the existing rock slope to delineate areas where the
fractured rock mass was more susceptible to imminent rockfalls or
boulders rolling down slope towards the house located near the toe of the
slope Critical loose rock or boulders would then be hand barred and
scaled from the slope where deemed feasible
To accomplish this temporary repair work a work crew from Okada
Trucking Co Ltd began removing previous accumulations of rockfalls and
boulders from the toe area next to the house on and
then installed steel posts and erected an 8 feet high cyclone barrier
fence with reinforced wire cables They finished this phase of work on
about
Observations and Conclusions
Based on visual cbservations of the rock slope it appears that the
upper 12 to 15 feet of the rock slope was the most likely area where

fallen boulders had been originated from and rolled down towards the
house below
In the period from February 28th through March lst 1985 our
geologist assisted Okada s field crew in the scaling of loose rocks and
boulders by hand barring and shoveling Over 100 cubic yards of rocky
materials were loosened and removed and caught by the fallout area and
barrier fence during this scaling portion of the work
Following the scaling operations Okada s crew removed the rock soil
mass at the toe of slope that was retained by the barrier fence rebuilt
the damaged fence poured a concrete slab and patched the A C pavement
and did miscellaneous painting They completed this work on March 15
1985 According to an estimate by Okada a total of over 200 cubic yards
of rock soil were removed from the site during this contract
Rockfalls are a general phenomena occurring on many rock slopes in
the Islands Various methods of boulder barriers or catchments can be
used to reduce the hazard of rockfall that may affect property and lives
ef people located downslope
It should be understood that the above reconnaissance study
installing a barrier fence and removal of visible loose rock is an
emergency measure only and does not guarantee complete stability of the
rock slope from future rockfalls

Other methods may be considered later for a more permanent solution
for the rockfall problem after studies and investigations are done by a
qualified engineering consultant Some of the possible future improve
ments or solutions that may be used as a guide are as follows
1 Consider the future purchase of the property at
Place or move the structure to a more suitable location away
from the toe of a steep rock slope
2 Construct an 8 to 10 feet wide bench about 12 to 15 feet below
the top of the steep slope and reslope the upper cut section to
alsl H V or flatter slope The bench should be located in
the reddish brown weathered rock mass An upper check fence may
also be located along this bench
3 A slope covering wire mesh with a check fence located on the
Slope to dissipate the kinetic energy of rolling boulders
At the present because of the fairly large amount of rock and soil
that has been removed installing a fence and cleaning the toe of slope
above the house it appears that the property is reasonably safe for the
time being However it should be understood that an inherent risk of
falling boulders could still exist for the unstable rock slope

The state of the art of soil engineering has not advanced to a point
that a complete solution is available for this area It is our opinion
that no matter what one does there is always the risk of boulders
rolling down the slope Whatever measure is selected it only reduces
the risks It should be further understood that no guarantee against
future falling boulders could be made due to changes in soil and rock
conditions with time weathering erosion earthquake and construction
vibration and improvements and construction activities at and around
the site
