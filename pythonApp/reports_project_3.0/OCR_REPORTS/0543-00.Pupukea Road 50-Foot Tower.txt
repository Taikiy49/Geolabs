This report summarizes the findings of the subsurface exploration
at the subject site and presents recommendations for the design
and construction of the foundations to support the proposed tower
at Pupukea Road Oahu Hawaii
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is presently planned to construct a tower approximately 50 feet in
height to be supported upon a mat foundation approximately 9 5 feet
square located 5 feet below the existing ground surface
SITE CONDITIONS
The proposed site is located on the florth shore of Oahu approximately
three miles from the Waimea Fire Station following Pupukea Road The
previously cleared site is situated on a small ridge approximately
150 south of Pupukea Road
Geologically the site is part of the eroded Koolau basalt shield It
is situated on the end of the shield and is therefore subject to
greater erosion and weathering The typical weathered basaltic soils
are found which are predominately reddish brown clayey silt residual
soil derived from the in place weathering of the underlying bedrock
Pacific Tower Service 2 August 30 1974
FIELD INVESTIGATION
To explore the subsurface conditions at the site a test boring was
drilled at approximately the center of the proposed tower site uti
lizing truck mounted rotary auger equipment The location of the
boring is shown on the enclosed Site Plan and Boring Location
Plate l
The soils were classified by visual and textural examination in the
field by our geologist who continuously supervised the drilling
operations The field classifications were supplemented by visual
inspection and testing in the laboratory All soils were classified
in accordance with the Unified Soil Classification Systems A des
cription of the soils encountered and a graphical representation is
presented on the Boring Log Plate A 1l
The soil samples were obtained by driving either a 2 4 I D dia
meter or a standard penetration sampler with 140 pound hammer free
falling a distance of 30 inches The blow counts to drive the
sampler the last twelve inches are shown on the Boring Log at the
appropriate sample depth
To provide data for evaluating the strength characteristics of the
encountered soils a direct shear test was performed on the sample
obtained from Boring 1 between 7 0 and 8 5 feet The test results
are shown on Plate A 2
Moisture and density determinations and Atterberg Limits were per
formed on selected samples to aid in the classification of the soils
The results of these test are presented on the Boring Log at the
appropriate sample location
The test boring indicates that the site is underlain to 13 feet the
depth of the exploration by very stiff residual soil consisting of
clayey silt with traces of gravel near the surface This material
is highly pre consolidated and can provide adequate support for
spread or mat foundations No groundwater was encountered in the
boring
DISCUSSIONS RECOMMENDATIONS
The site is suitable for the proposed tower which can be supported
upon spread or mat foundations bearing within the residual soil
It is recommended that the tower foundations be designed for a maxi
mum allowable contact bearing pressure of 8 000 pounds per square
foot The bearing value can be increased by 1 3 for wind and seis
mic loadings The settlements are estimated to be 1 2 inch total
and 1 4 inch differential
Pacific Tower Service 3 August 30 1974
It is recommended that the spread or mat foundations be excavated
and poured neat without forming to assure firm lateral contact with
the natural soils A uniform passive pressure of 300 pounds per
square foot can than be utilized for the lateral resistance A
frictional factor of 0 4 should be utilized for the contact between
the foundations and the underlying soils
The on site soils have moisture contents in excess of 40 Drying
of these soils will be required if they are utilized as compacted
backfill The on site soils have an estimated maximum modified
proctor dry density of approximately 115 pounds per cubic foot and
an optimum moisture content of approximately 20
