In accordance with your request we have made a study of the near
surface soil conditions at the above referenced lot in order to provide
foundation recommendations for the proposed residence The study
consisted of sampling the near surface soils by means of excavating test
pits laboratory testing of the swelling potential of the soils and a
review of the preliminary house plans This report summarizes our
findings and recommendations
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
The proposed residence will be a two story structure approximately
45 by 70 feet in plan dimensions A semi basement level with CMU walls up
to 8 feet high are planned
Concrete on grade slab is planned for the semi basement level garage
and driveway areas

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The stiff reddish brown clayey silt encountered can generally provide
adequate foundation support for the proposed house provided that some care
is exercised during construction to minimize the swelling potential of the
soil
The field exploration and laboratory tests performed on the stiff
on site clayey silt soil indicate that this material generally has low to
moderate swelling potential depending on the initial moisture content of
the soil prior to subsequent saturation Therefore to reduce effects due
to soil swelling under on grade slabs we recommend that the on site soil
be capped with at least 12 inches of non expansive select fill and that
the subgrade soils underneath the select fill and slab be moistened and
protected from drying from the start of construction until the slab
concrete placement
Select non expansive soils should also be used as backfill behind the
retaining walls to reduce the swelling potential of the natural soil
lot Grading
The fills and backfills within the top 12 inches minimum below the
on grade slabs should consist of non expansive soil such as select
borrow crusher waste or coral Where cut or minimal fill is anticipated
such as the garage driveway and the house areas the on site soil should
be over excavated to provide a 12 inch minimm thickness of non expansive
select fill capping underneath the on grade slabs

House Foundations
Spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the proposed
residence An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f may be used on the
natural soil or compacted fill
Individual footings on level areas should be embedded a minimum of 12
inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade Continuous wall footings
should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the adjacent finished
grade

If located on a sloping area the footing should be embedded down an
additional amount to obtain a 6 foot minimum horizontal set back distance
measured from the outer edge of the footing out to the slope face This
additional embedment depth is required to help resist the downhill slope
creep movement and to develop the recommended bearing capacity of the
soil
The northern and southeastern portions of the house are located close
to the proposed CRM retaining wall To reduce the possibility of footing
settlement due to possible settlement of backfill behind the retaining
wall and or lateral wall movement we recommend that the footings in these
areas be embedded below a H V plane extending upward from the
bottom edge of the retaining wall Alternatively pipe piles could be
used to support these portions of the house
The pipe piles should consist of minimum 6 inch diameter drilled
holes with 3 to 4 inch diameter steel pipes inserted and grouted into the
holes
An allowable capacity of 5 kips per pipe pile may be used for a
10 foot long section The capacity may be increased by 1 kip for every 2
feet of additional depth
Slab On Grade
It should be noted that the on site clayey silt soils may be
moderately expansive if allowed to dry Therefore the on site soil
should be over excavated down to a depth of 12 inches below the slab
bottom thoroughly moistened and then replaced with non expansive imported
soils as previously discussed The thickened edge of the slab should be
embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finish grade

To further reduce future post construction swelling of the underlying
expansive soils we recommend that the in place clayey silt below the
upper capping fill pad be kept moist for one week prior to concrete
placement heavily soaked with water for the first two days and sprinkled
remainder of the week
A 4 inch layer of 3B Fine gravel cushion fill should be provided
below the slab In order to protect against future moisture infiltration
an impervious moisture barrier may be incorporated below the slab

4 The wall foundations on relatively flat areas should be embedded

5 The wall foundations on existing sloping ground areas should be

Drainage
The finish lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings should
be shaped to shed water away from foundations and to avoid ponding
condition Also it is advised that gutter systems be installed around
the house and that gutter water should be diverted far away from the
foundations and walkways Excessive landscape watering near foundations
and slab should also be avoided
Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils

Concrete Driveway
Since the driveway can tolerate some amount of heaving the 12 inch
on site soil over excavation and replacement may be eliminated if some
amount of small slab cracking are tolerable The subgrade soils should be
maintained in a moist condition and prevented from drying out prior to
concrete placement
Joints are recommended in the driveway slab at about 8 to 10 foot
intervals and at discontinuities in order to allow for some relative
movement of the concrete
Design Review
Final plans and specifications for foundations site grading and

and comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the footing excavations and backfill operation
be observed by the soils engineer during construction to see that adequate
bearing has been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are
consistent with the findings of our study

LIMITATIONS
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations
made in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation engineering
principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all other
warranties either expressed or implied
The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are based
on our site reconnaissance soil information from test pits and laboratory
test data obtained from this soil exploration
