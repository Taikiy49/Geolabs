INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this study was to review and evaluate the
revised site grading and foundation plans for the Puu Alii
Phase II development in Kaneohe Oahu Hawaii
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P 2
Soil and foundation investigations were performed on the
entire Puu Alii PUD development site by
in 1972 and by Geolabs Hawaii in 1975 and the following
reports were issued
1 Preliminary Soils Geology Investigation
Puu Alii Residential PUD
Heeia Oahu Hawaii dated
W
2 Soil Foundation Investigation
Proposed Lilipuna Hills PUD
Heeia Oahu Hawaii dated
W
The site grading for the project was started in about June
1976 and substantially completed by June 1977 Structural
fill varying from 5 feet to 22 feet was placed during the
site grading operations
Townhouse units are under construction in the upper Phase I
portion of the development
A new site grading and building layout design for the lower
Phase II portion of the development has been developed
SCOPE OF WORK
This study consisted of a review and evaluation of the new
site grading and building layout designs based on the soil
data obtained from the previous soil reports and observations
from a recent visit to the project site
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P 3
The grading plan for Puu Alii Phase II by Gray Rhee
Associates Inc dated was used in our
study
The design of the swimming pool is not included in the
scope of this report
PLANNED DEVELOPMENT
The new grading concept consists of cuts and fills generally
less than 10 feet to create level pads for the proposed
parking areas and townhouse structures
The townhouse structures will be three story wood buildings
with up to 6 units per building
SITE AND SOIL CONDITIONS
The Phase II portion of the development is located on the
lower northwestern portion of the total development site
It is bounded by Lilipuna Road on the north Kaneohe Bay
Shopping Center on the west and the existing Puu Alii town
house development on the south and east
The previous soil investigations disclosed stiff residual
material in the upper portions of the site and alluvial
deposits in the lower areas The alluvial deposits were
generally stiff Subsequently the site was cleared and
graded to its present level Generally between 5 and 22
feet of fill was placed in the lower areas
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P 4
Three stockpiles of soil were noted during a visit to the
site in December 1978 Laboratory tests were performed
on the stockpiled material the test results are presented
in the appendix
Except for the stockpiles and remaining trees the site
was clear
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on a review of the previous explorations the proposed
regrading of the site appears feasible from a soils engineer
ing standpoint
Special foundation designs are recommended for the buildings
close to the edge of slopes particularly Building Nos 1
2 and 3 in the uppermost part of the development
Fills up to about 6 feet high are planned along the northern
and western boundaries for the parking areas
Site Grading
In general the proposed site regrading consists of filling
the perimeter areas of the site and cutting the central
portion
The site to be graded should be cleared of vegetation
debris and other deleterious materials
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P 5
Prior to filling the stockpiles should be removed and the
existing ground should be scarified to a minimum of 6 inches
and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density
established by ASTM D 1557 Test method
Fills should be placed in 9 inch maximum horizontal lifts
compacted to the above specified density
The stockpiled soil at the site can be used as a source of
borrow material Any additional off site borrow required
should be tested and approved by the soil engineer prior
to transportation to the site The material should be
non expansive and should not contain large percentages of
boulders organic or deleterious material
Fills placed where the existing ground is steeper than
5 horizontal to 1 vertical should be keyed and benched
into the existing firm ground to properly bond the new
fill to the slope The filling operation should start
at the lowest point and continue up in level compacted
layers See Plate 2 for details
Slopes
The following slope ratios are recommended for the project
Fill slopes 2 horizontal to 1 vertical
Cut slopes in previously placed fill 2 horizontal
to 1 vertical
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P 6
Cut slopes in in situ residual soil 1 horizontal
to 1 vertical
Water should be diverted away from the tops of slopes and
slope planting utilized to reduce erosion
Drainage benches should be provided for slopes having a
vertical height greater than 20 feet
Foundation
The proposed townhouse structures will be three story wood
structures up to about 200 feet long
Lower Area
The lower portion of the development will be relatively
flat with slope heights less than 3 feet Spread or con
tinuous footing foundations are recommended for this area
The bottom of the footings should be a minimum of 18 inches
below the finish grade
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f is recommended
for the footings on the existing ground or compacted fill
Footings located next to utility trenches should extend to
the bottom of the trench
Surface water should be diverted away from the building
foundations
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P
Upper Area Building 3
The buildings in the upper portion of the development will
be located near or over the tops of fill slopes about 10
to 18 feet high
To resist the downhill creep movement of the near surface
soil and to develop the full bearing capacity of the sub
soil the footings and slab perimeter footings close to
the top of slope should extend down a sufficient depth to
obtain a minimum horizontal set back distance of 10 feet
from the slope face
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f can be used on
the compacted fill
An alternative foundation system would be to use caisson
foundations with a structural slab or wood floor
For a 12 inch diameter caisson 10 feet deep an allowable
load of 20 kips can be developed
The remaining building foundations located 10 feet or more
away from the top of fill slope can be designed as in the
lower area
Retaining Walls
Retaining walls up to about 5 feet high may be used along
the portion of Building 3 on or along the top
of slope
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P
Retaining walls may also be used along the upper parking
area
The recommendations given under foundations for the upper
area Building 3 should be followed for the
design of the wall foundations
For lateral earth pressures the following design values
are recommended
Walls unrestrained at the top 45 p c f equivalent
fluid pressure
Walls restrained at the top 60 p c f equivalent
fluid pressure
Additional load due to traffic or sloping surcharge
should be included where applicable
The walls should be backfilled with well graded granular
material such as base course rock
Weepholes or a subsurface drainage system should be used
to prevent the buildup of hydrostatic pressure behind the
walls
A friction factor of 0 35 can be utilized to determine the
sliding resistance of the wall foundation
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P 9
Slab On Grade
For slabs on grade particularly those located along the
water pond areas a capillary break cushion layer consisting
of 4 inches of 3B Fine rock aggregate should be used below
the slab
The subgrade should be moistened just prior to pouring of
the concrete
Roadway Parking Areas
Asphaltic concrete pavements are planned for the roadway
and parking areas
Based on the laboratory CBR test performed for the previous
investigation the following preliminary pavement section
is recommended
2 inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 inch Base Course
6 inch Select Borrow
14 inch Total Pavement Thickness
Additional CBR tests should be performed on the actual sub
grade soil samples at the time of the roadway construction
and the above design section can be revised accordingly if
other subgrade soil conditions are encountered
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P
Design Review
The plans for foundations site grading and utility con
struction should be forwarded to the soil engineer for
review and comments prior to construction to see that
the intent of our recommendations is included in the
project plans and specifications
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the soil engineer be present
during earthwork construction particularly the fill
placement to monitor the contractor s grading opera
tions
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be
observed by a soil engineer during construction to see
that adequate bearing has been obtained and that the
subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings
of our subsurface investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive
tested and approved by the soil engineer prior to hauling
to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent
upon the above inspections
