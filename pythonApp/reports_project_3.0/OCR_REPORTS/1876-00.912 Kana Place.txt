SUMMARY OF RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our boring and laboratory test data
the findings generally indicate that most of
the project site is underlain by an upper
layer of uncompacted fill consisting
primarily of expansive clay and boulders and
a lower layer of highly expansive adobe clay
which dips down slope towards the northwest
Weathered basalt occurs below these two upper
layers
It is our opinion that both the upper existing
on site fill and the lower expansive adobe
clay layer as is are unsuitable for direct
support of future foundations and structures
We recommend both the existing on site fill
and the adobe clay should be removed and
the weathered slightly weathered basalt
beneath the adobe clay layer should be used
to support the future structure loads
Alternatively select fill may be placed on
the project site after the existing fill and
adobe clay are removed Spread footing
foundations may then be supported by properly
compacted select borrow fill
INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of a soil engineering evaluation
performed for the subject project

The single house lot approximately 5 739 square feet is located on
the eastern rim of Kaimuki Crater about 3 000 feet north mauka of
Diamond Head The front portion of the site is relatively level but
approximately half of the rear lot consists of a steep 1H 1V slope 30

At this time the proposed location and dimensions of the building
have not yet been established However it is our understanding that the
future building may be a two story residential structure with
approximately 1 400 square feet of floor area and that column loads will
be light
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our evaluation was to explore the subsurface soil and
rock conditions at the site to
a determine the thickness of the upper surficial boulder and silty
clay material
b evaluate the shrink and swell potential of the surface and
near surface soils
c evaluate the overall slope stability of the project site and
d evaluate the general subsurface soil rock conditions in order to
provide recommendations for site grading retaining wall house
foundation and driveway designs

The scope of our exploration included drilling and sampling two 2
borings to depths of 11 and 18 5 feet below the existing ground surface
Also included were laboratory testing of selected samples obtained
engineering analysis and evaluation of the field and laboratory data
development of the preliminary design recommendations and report
preparation
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration and laboratory test
results are presented in the appendices of this report
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions were explored by drilling and sampling two
2 borings to about 11 0 to 18 5 feet below the existing ground surface
at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
Based upon the boring and laboratory test data the findings
generally indicate that the subsurface conditions at the project site
consist of an upper layer of boulders mixed with moderately to highly
expansive stiff to very stiff silty clay fill On site observation
revealed that locally voids may exist within the existing placed
uncompacted fill Beneath this upper fill layer is another 2 to 3 foot
thick layer of highly expansive stiff to very stiff adobe clay which is
dipping down slope towards the west Beneath the adobe clay is the
extremely weathered very stiff silty clay to hard slightly weathered
basalt
Groundwater was not encountered in the drilled borings during the
course of our field exploration However rises in groundwater levels may
occur during and after periods of heavy rainfall

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on our field exploration and laboratory test results it is our
opinion from a soil engineering point of view that the site is suitable
for construction of a single family residence provided special design
considerations and construction procedures are followed
The subsurface exploration revealed that most of the project site is
underlain by an upper layer of uncompacted fill consisting primarily of
expansive clay and boulders and a lower layer of highly expansive adobe
clay that may progressively creep downslope with time Beneath these two
soil layers is the extremely weathered very stiff sandy silt and silty
clay and slightly weathered basalt
It is our opinion that the existing on site fill and the underlying
adobe clay should be removed and that the extremely to slightly
weathered basalt beneath the expansive clay layer be used to support the
future building loads Alternatively new fill may be placed and
compacted on the site after the existing fill and adobe clay material
are removed In this case spread footing foundations may then bear upon
properly compacted select borrow fill material
Foundation
Spread footing foundations may be used on this site to support a
2 story single family house provided that the special site grading and
foundation support procedures suggested below are followed To reduce
potential excessive settlements due to probable presence of voids or loose
pockets in the existing fill and to minimize the shrink swell potential
and downslope creep of the adobe clay soils on building foundations we
recommend the following footing foundation excavation and construction
procedures

Spread Footing on Weathered or Unweathered Rock
After the existing on site uncompacted fill and adobe clay is
removed the footing foundations may be supported by extremely to
Slightly weathered basalt Select borrow such as crusher waste
should be used as a source of backfill compacted in 8 inch loose
lifts to obtain a dry density of at least 95 of its ASTM D 1557 78
maximum
An allowable bearing value of 3000 p s f may be used for footings
bearing on very stiff sandy silt or silty clay weathered basalt or
hard basalt The allowable bearing value may be increased by
one third for temporary wind and seismic load
A coefficient of friction of 0 4 can be utilized to determine sliding
resistance of the structure and foundation
The bottoms of the footing excavations in stiff silty clay or sandy
silt extremely weathered basalt should be recompacted to a minimum
of 95 of its maximm dry density established by ASTM D 1557 78
Footings should be embedded a minimum of about 15 inches below the
lowest adjacent finished grade on level area away from the slope
face Footings located on or near the top of slope should be
embedded sufficient depth to obtain a minimm 5 foot set back
measured horizontally out to the slope face
Estimated footing settlements under the design loading are
anticipated to be on the order of less than about one half of an inch
total and one fourth of an inch differential It is probable that
some footings would be found on hard slightly weathered to fresh
basalt For these footings we believe that settlements would be
negligible

Spread Footing on Compacted Fill
Where site grading fill may be required at the building site shallow
spread footings may be used for foundation support of the single
family residence structure provided that the special site grading
procedures suggested below are properly followed
All previously placed fill and adobe clay at the sloping portion of
the site and at the future building location should be removed down
to weathered basalt and replaced by compacted off site select borrow
fill Off site select borrow fill material such as crusher waste
should be used to re construct the new fill embankment area The
fill should be placed in horizontal lifts from the bottom up to key
and bench the fill into the existing weathered rock surface see
details on Plate 2 Sketch of Keying and Benching typical fill on
natural slope The fill should be compacted in 8 inch loose lifts
to obtain a dry density of at least 95 of its ASTM D 1557 78 maximum
density
A 2 1 H V slope ratio is recommended for the above replacement fill
slope
A coefficient of friction of 0 4 can be utilized to determine sliding
resistance of the structure and foundation
It is recommended that the footings on level area away from the slope
face be embedded at least 15 inches below the lowest adjacent grade
into the above compacted fill
For footings on the 2 1 H V replacement fill slope additional
footing embedment is needed to obtain a minimm 7 foot set back
horizontally out to the slope face

Site Grading
It is our understanding that a site grading plan for the project site
has not yet been established We strongly recommend that future site
grading design and plans for the project site should be reviewed by the
soil engineer to evaluate the stability of the existing hillside and that
the proposed structures will not be adversely affected by the site
grading
Existing Filled Areas
Existing loose boulder fill and the underlying Adobe clay materials
previously placed in the vicinity of the sloping portion of the site
should be removed Where future garage and driveways are located in
the level portion of the site existing loose boulder fill materials
placed should also be removed If required the new replacement fill
should consists of select borrow properly placed and compacted
Site Preparation
The site to be graded should be cleared of all vegetation debris
previous fill Adobe clay and soft materials
Prior to backfilling and construction of the select fill embankment
if required the subgrade should then be scarified moisture
conditioned and recompacted to 90 relative density

Two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V slope ratio is recommended for
future replacement fill slopes
Fill slopes should be constructed by overfilling past the design fill
slope and then cutting back
Surface water run off should be diverted away from the tops and toes
of slopes and slope planting utilized to reduce erosion Slope
benches 8 foot wide are recommended for slope heights in excess of
20 feet
Fill Material
Based on the field exploration and laboratory test results the
on site expansive fill and clay soils are not suitable for use as
fill
Off site borrow should be tested and approved by the soils engineer
prior to transportation to the site Imported fill material should
consist of non expansive select granular materials less than 3 inches
in maximum size with less than 20 passing the 200 sieve
All fill material should be placed in horizontal lifts not exceeding
eight 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a dry density of
at least 90 of its ASTM D density

preliminary access and service road pavement section is recommended for
expansive clay subgrade condition

The select borrow may be eliminated if the top 2 feet below the
subgrade is fairly non expansive bouldery material
Additional CBR tests and or visual observations should be performed
on the actual subgrade soil samples during construction and the above
design section can be revised if necessary The recommended section
considers light traffic and that good drainage will be provided adjacent
to paved areas

Design Review
Plans for site grading and foundation construction should be

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during earthwork
construction particularly slope cuts and fill placement to monitor the
contractor s grading operations
It is also recommended that the foundation excavations be observed by
a soils engineer during construction to see that adequate bearing has been
obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the
findings of our exploration
Any import material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
If conditions encountered during construction are different from
those presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made
