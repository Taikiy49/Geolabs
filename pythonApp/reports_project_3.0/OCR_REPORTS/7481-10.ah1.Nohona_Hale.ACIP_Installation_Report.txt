PROJECT DESCRIPTION 
In general, the Nohona Hale project involves the construction of a new 14-story 
tower with 111 living units set atop a 2-level podium. We understand the 2-level podium 
will house the lobby, living room, community spaces, and management offices. The site is 
generally bounded by commercial buildings and located along the north side of Cooke 
Street between Queen Street and Halekauwila Street. The project location and general 
vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map, Plate 1. 
Based on the geotechnical engineering report referenced above, we recommended 
supporting the new residential building on a deep foundation system consisting of 18 and 
24-inch diameter ACIP piles with a length ranging from about 18 to 78 feet long to achieve 
allowable compressive load capacities of 100 to 1,000 kips (50 to 500 tons) per pile. The 
ACIP piles would extend through the near-surface fills and lagoonal deposits and derive 
support primarily from skin friction within coral ledges and/or coralline detritus materials 
encountered below the lagoonal deposits with design pile lengths of about 18 to 50 feet for 
the 18-inch diameter ACIP piles and about 50 to 78 feet for the 24-inch diameter ACIP 
piles. Our recommendations pertaining to the ACIP pile capacities are presented in the 
following table.

To better understand the load transfer mechanism at the pile-soil interface, the load

test load on test pile TP-1 was 1,482 kips and a combined side friction and end bearing

specified maximum test load without failure, meeting the project specifications.  Based on 
our field observations and the results of the Osterberg Method load test, it is our opinion 
that the recommended pile lengths would be sufficient to achieve the design pile load 
capacities with a minimum factor safety of 2.0. 
PRODUCTION PILE INSTALLATION 
The general contractor for the project is Swinerton Builders and the ACIP pile 
installation subcontractor was Kelikai, Inc. The production ACIP pile installation program, 
consisting of installing 46 18-inch and 14 24-inch diameter ACIP piles, was conducted 
between June 18 and 29, 2018 under the near-continuous (full-time) observations by our 
field engineers. A Bauer BG 28 drill rig equipped with a continuous flight hollow stem 
auger was used to install the production piles. Automatic monitoring equipment was 
mounted on both drilling and pumping equipment with the intent of providing auger depth, 
grout volume, grout pressure, etc., on a near-continuous basis. 
During the pile installation operation, logs were maintained for each ACIP pile, 
which included information pertaining to pile number, date installed, depth of pile, grout 
volume used, grout return depth, etc. The locations of the production ACIP piles installed 
are shown on the ACIP Pile Identification Plan, Plate 2. The 18 and 24-inch diameter ACIP 
pile installation summaries are presented by pile number on Plates 3.1 through 3.3 of this
