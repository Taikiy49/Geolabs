2 1 Regional Geology
The Island of Hawaii is the largest island in the Hawaiian Archipelago and covers
an area of approximately 4 000 square miles The island was formed by the activity of
the following five shield volcanoes Kohala long extinct Mauna Kea activity during
recent geologic time Hualalai last erupted in 1801 1803 and Mauna Loa and
Kilauea both still active
The project site is situated on the southwestern flank of Mauna Loa Mountain
which makes up the south central portion of the island The lava formation encountered
at the site appears to be of both a a and pahoehoe type flows which spread and
ponded as they approached the ocean A a lava typically is characterized by a porous
rough and irregular flow surface resembling a jagged accumulation of rock fragments
cobbles and boulders Pahoehoe lava is characterized by a smooth rope like or billowy
surface and an internal structure of vesicular porous rock
Cavities are commonly encountered in basaltic lavas especially in pahoehoe
type lava Cavities form when the lava is in a molten state and represent both lava tubes
intra flow cavities and blisters and pockets inter flow cavities Lava tubes form when
molten lava drains from the cooling flow leaving a hollow tube like structure which may
extend for a large longitudinal distance along the flow Blisters and pockets are
generally smaller in horizontal extent
Basalt is considered to be relatively permeable and can transmit water quite
readily in both the horizontal and vertical directions In general water is transmitted
through the porous rock matrix along joints fractures and inter flow contacts cavities
and along clinker layers Clinker and cavities serve as the primary water transmission
features because of their high permeability

2 4 Earthquakes and Seismicity
In general earthquakes that occur throughout the world are caused by shifts in
the tectonic plates In contrast earthquake activity in Hawaii is primarily linked to
volcanic activity Therefore earthquake activity in Hawaii generally occurs before or
during volcanic eruptions In addition earthquakes may result from the underground
movement of magma that comes close to the surface but does not erupt The Island of
Hawaii experiences thousands of earthquakes each year but most are so small that
only sensitive instruments can detect the tremors However some of the earthquakes
are strong enough to be felt and a few cause minor to moderate damage
In general earthquakes associated with volcanic activity are most common on
the Island of Hawaii Earthquakes directly associated with the movement of magma are
concentrated beneath the active Kilauea and Mauna Loa Volcanoes on the Island of
Hawaii Because the majority of the earthquakes in Hawaii over 90 percent are related
to volcanic activity the risk of seismic activity and degree of ground shaking diminishes
with increased distance from the southern portion of the Island of Hawaii The Island of
Hawaii has experienced numerous earthquakes greater than Magnitude 6 M6 Based

2 5 Site Specific Response Spectrum Analysis
As requested we performed a seismic hazard analysis and site response
evaluation with respect to seismic design considerations for the proposed project The
purpose of our seismic hazard analysis and site response evaluation is to establish
appropriate design elastic response spectra taking into consideration the proximity of
the site to seismic sources regional seismicity and site specific subsurface conditions
The structural engineer utilizes a response spectrum to perform an analysis of the
structure given the dynamic loading conditions and the ground motions at the
foundation elevations
The response spectrum is a graphical representation relating to the maximum
response of a single degree of freedom damped elastic system with different
fundamental periods to dynamic forces The anticipated earthquake ground motions and
dynamic response of the subsurface materials are represented by site specific elastic
response spectra
Design Ground Motion
Based on the 1997 Uniform Building Code UBC the Island of Hawaii is within
Seismic Zone 4 with an associated peak ground acceleration of 0 40g
Alternatively site specific design ground motions may be developed based on a
site specific probabilistic seismic hazard analysis PSHA or determined based on
seismic hazard maps In any case the site specific design ground motions should
be based on a 10 percent probability of exceedance in 50 years This level of

3 4 Slabs On Grade

control building For the interior building slabs not subjected to vehicular traffic or
sustained vibrations we recommend providing a minimum 4 inch thick layer of cushion

break Where slabs are subjected to vehicular traffic or sustained vibrations such as

moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a
minimum of 95 percent relative compaction

cushion layer Flexible floor coverings should be considered above the floor slab
because they can better mask minor slab cracking

the existing ground surface to achieve the design subgrades Structural fill
materials should consist of well graded granular materials 6 inches in largest
dimension with sufficient fines to prevent the occurrence of voids in the compacted
mass The excavated on site materials may be re used as a source of structural fill
materials provided that the materials are processed to meet the maximum particle
size and gradation requirements Based on the limited height and extent of fill
required for the project materials larger than 6 inches should not be used as a
source of fill for the proposed project
lf the excavated on site materials do not contain sufficient fines to produce a
fragments or boulders should be considered to provide the required gradation and
particle sizes to develop a well graded material Imported material if necessary
should be observed and or tested by Geolabs or a qualified geotechnical engineer
for its suitability prior to being transported to the site for the intended use
Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
Structural fill materials should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 12 inches in
loose thickness moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to at least 95 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to
the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry
density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 Optimum
moisture content is the water content percentage by weight corresponding to the
maximum dry density Compaction should be accomplished by sheepsfoot rollers
vibratory rollers or other types of acceptable compaction equipment

Conventional compaction testing is generally not practical in fills composed of rocks
and or cobbles Instead the contractor should conduct a testing program under the
observation of a Geolabs representative or a qualified geotechnical engineer to
evaluate the number of passes needed by a compactor to achieve the desired level
of compaction at the start of the grading phase of the project Based on this testing
program the number of passes may then be used as the field criterion for adequate
compaction
For preliminary estimating purposes compaction may be accomplished by using a
large vibratory drum roller having a static weight of at least 20 tons traveling at a
speed of about 300 feet per minute The roller should pass over the fill surface at
least eight times for structural fill
Excavation
Based on our field exploration the project site is generally underlain by interbedded
layers of basalt formation and a a clinker materials We anticipate the severely
fractured basalt and a a clinker materials may be excavated with normal heavy
excavation equipment such as excavators and ripped with large bulldozers
However excavations in the dense basalt formations may require the use of
hoe rams or chipping
The above discussions regarding the rippability of the surface materials are based
on our visual observation of the existing basalt formation and experience with
similar type subsurface conditions We recommend encouraging contractors to
examine the site conditions and boring data to make their own interpretation

3 6 Pipelines
We understand new water pipelines will be installed for development of the
project We anticipate that most of the pipeline trenches will be excavated in the a a
clinker and or dense basalt formation encountered in the borings In general we
recommend providing a granular bedding layer consisting of 6 inches of open graded
gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation below the pipes for uniform support of the pipes
Free draining granular materials such as open graded gravel ASTM C 33
No 67 gradation should be used for the initial trench backfill up to about 12 inches
above the pipes It is critical to use this free draining material to reduce the potential for
formation of voids below the haunches of pipes and to provide adequate support for the
sides of the pipes Improper backfill material around the pipes and improper placement
of the backfill could result in backfill settlement and pipe damage
As an alternative the granular bedding and the initial trench backfill up to about
12 inches above the top of the pipes may consist of 1 inch minus well graded granular
materials aggregate base course Where used the 1 inch minus well graded granular
bedding and the initial trench backfill should be moisture conditioned and compacted to
no less than 90 percent relative compaction

Core samples of the rock formations encountered at the site were obtained using
diamond core drilling techniques in general accordance with ASTM D 2113 Diamond

hollow bit to cut into the rock formation The material left in the hollow core of the bit is
mechanically recovered for examination and description

material is recovered from a 5 0 foot core run the recovery would be 74 percent and
would be shown on the Logs of Borings as REC 74
