INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of a geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Palm Beach Estates located along Kamehmeha Highway
between Kahikole Place and Ahinalu Place in the Kaipapau District of the island of Oahu
Hawaii The general location of the site and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering recommenda
tions resulting from the field exploration laboratory testing and engineering analyses

These recommendations are intended for preliminary design input and may not be
appropriate to form the basis for preparation of construction cost estimates or for
construction bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to build six 6 single family houses in the project site
It is anticipated that portland cement concrete paving will be used to provide
access and parking for light vehicular traffic

GENERAL SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located in the town of Hauula in the Kaipapau District on the
island of Oahu The site is bounded on the northeast by the Pacific Ocean and on the
southwest by Kamehameha Highway Existing residences off Kahikole Place and Ahinalu
Place bound the property in the northwest and southeast directions respectively
The site is essentially flat with the existing ground ranging from approximately six
and one half 6 5 to eight 8 feet above Mean Sea Level MSL Embankments of up
to approximately two 2 feet in height may be required
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling six 6 borings to
depths of twenty six and one half 26 5 feet below the existing ground surface The
approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general the borings disclosed that the site is underlain by a surface layer
approximately seven and one half 7 5 to sixteen 16 feet thick of loose to medium
dense coral beach sand overlying medium stiff to stiff silty clay to a depth of 26 5 feet
the maximum drilled depth
At Boring No 3 only a layer of grayish brown silty gravel with sand approximately
one 1 foot thick overlying a highly decomposed boulder formation between depths of

encountered above the medium stiff to stiff clay formation
Groundwater was encountered in the drilled borings at depths of approximately six

exploration Due to the proximity of the site to the bay it is anticipated that the ground
water level will fluctuate in response to seasonal tides and storm surges
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
It is our opinion that the proposed project is feasible from a geotechnical
engineering point of view provided that the recommendations presented below are
followed
Foundations
Shallow spread and continuous footing foundations may be used to support the
proposed structures
An allowable bearing value of 2 000 p s f can be used for footings bearing on the
compacted in situ sand or compacted engineered embankment A footing embedment
of twelve 12 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade is recommended
For the residential structures proposed for this site it is anticipated that footing
loads and settlements for the project will be smail
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2923 00 Page 6
It is recommended that the bottom of all footing excavations should be compacted
to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as determined in accordance
with procedures described in ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
If loose sand lenses or pockets are encountered at the bottoms of footing
excavations they should be over excavated to dense in situ sand The resulting
depression should then be backfilled with select granular material compacted to a
minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as determined in accordance with
procedures described in ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
If a footing is located next to a utility line the utility trench backfill should be
carefully monitored and adequately compacted to reduce the potential for footing
settlement due to consolidation of the trench backfill
Surface water should be diverted away from the foundations to avoid ponding of
surface runoff water that may adversely affect the bearing capacity of the soils below the
foundations
At the time of preparing this report the maximum foundation design load had not
been determined Therefore no settlement estimations were made in this report
Slab on Grade
The ground floors of the proposed single family houses are anticipated to be
concrete slab on grade construction It is also anticipated that the slab will be supported
on the new compacted fill layer
A four inch thick concrete slab on top of a minimum four inch thick cushion layer
of No 3B Fine gravel or ASTM C 33 No 67 gravel is recommended In order to protect
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2923 00 Page 7
against moisture infiltration a plastic vapor barrier should be incorporated below the slab
One 1 inch of moist fine sand may be placed above the plastic membrane to help curing
of the concrete
The thickened edges of the concrete slab should be embedded a minimum of
twelve 12 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade
Concrete Driveway
It is anticipated that concrete pavements may be provided for vehicular access and
parking for the proposed residential development
Based on the assumption that anticipated traffic will be composed of light
passenger vehicles and that the subgrade soils will consist of select non expansive
import fill materials the following pavement section is recommended
6 Inch Concrete
4 Inch Base Course 95 Compaction
10 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
on Compacted Subgrade
Joints are recommended at about eight 8 to ten 10 foot intervals and at
discontinuities in order to control shrinkage and contraction cracks and to allow for some
relative movement of the concrete slabs The length to width ratios of the slabs should
not exceed one and one quarter 1 25 to one 1 Slabs exceeding this ratio or which
are otherwise not rectangular in shape should be provided with welded wire mesh
reinforcement
Site Grading
Prior to construction the site should be cleared and grubbed Any deleterious
materials such as vegetation debris construction rubble and organic matter encountered
during grubbing and scarifying should be removed and properly disposed of off site
GEOLABS HAWAIl
W O 2923 00 Page 8
The proposed building and pavement areas should be scarified to a depth of six
6 inches after site clearing and grubbing The scarified subgrade should be thoroughly
moisture conditioned and recompacted to obtain a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum
dry density as determined in accordance with procedures described in ASTM Test Method
D 1557 78 Any soft or loose areas should be over excavated to firm in situ material and
the resultant depression backfilled with select imported non expansive material properly
compacted as indicated above To minimize pore water pressure build up in sandy
subsoils causing possible soil weakening use of heavy vibratory compaction
equipment should be avoided
Based on information currently provided embankment fills of up to two 2 feet
may be anticipated Material for embankment construction should consist of select
non expansive imported material with a maximum particle size less than three 3
inches and with less than twenty 20 percent passing the No 200 sieve The
laboratory CBR Swelling Test value should not exceed one percent and a CBR value
of 12 or more Fill slopes should be no steeper than two horizontal to one vertical
2H 1V
The fills and backfills should be placed in loose horizontal lifts not exceeding ten
10 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the
maximum dry density as determined in accordance with procedures described in
ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
It is estimated that fill ground settlements due to the new fill embankments
should be minimum probably on the order of less than one inch It is also our
opinion that the fill ground settlements will probably occur within the first two 2 to
three 3 months after the site is built up We recommend that about six 6 settlement

to surface structural construction
Any imported fill and backfill material that may be required should be tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to delivery to the site
The site should be graded to provide drainage of surface runoff away from all
foundations
Drainage
The finished lot grade outside the footings slabs and pavements should be
shaped to shed water away from foundations to avoid ponding conditions Also it is
recommended that a gutter system be installed around the residence and that the
gutter run off water be diverted as far away from the foundations and pavements as
feasible Excessive landscape watering near the foundations should also be avoided
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have concrete bottoms
and drains to prevent water from entering the foundation and slab subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
above recommended foundation construction since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The
foundation excavations should be properly backfilled tight against the footings and
slabs immediately after setting of the concrete to prevent ponding conditions
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and maintained to
drain all surface run off away from the foundations and slabs

Design Review
Drawings and specifications for site grading utility and foundation construction
should be forwarded to the project geotechnical engineer for review and written
Comments prior to advertisement for bids This review is needed to determine
adherence to the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this
review is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot be considered to be responsible
for misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the site grading preparation fill placement and fill
compaction be monitored during construction by the project geotechnical engineer
Subsequent residential footing excavations and backfilling should also be observed by
a competent geotechnical engineer during construction to see that adequate bearing
has been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings
of our exploration The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above observations
The settlement gauges should be installed prior to fill placements Optical
survey on the installed gauges at intervals should be forwarded to the project
geotechnical engineer for fill settlement observation and to verify that fill settlements
have fully occurred and that surface structure construction can commence
if actual subsurface conditions encountered during construction are different
from those considered during preparation of this report then appropriate modifications
to the design should be made
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and approved
by the soils engineer prior to delivery to the site

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based upon
information obtained from the borings Variations of conditions between the borings
may occur and the nature and extent of theses variations may not become evident
until construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to
reevaluate the recommendations given in this report
The locations of the borings are approximate having been determined by
taping from existing structures indicated on a preliminary Grading and Utility Plan
dated prepared by Gray Hong Bills Associates Inc Elevations of
the borings were estimated from contours and spot elevations shown on the same
drawing The physical locations and elevations of the borings should be considered
accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the borings depict
the approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual
transition
Water level data from the borings were collected at the times given on the
graphic representations and or in the text of this report These data have been
reviewed and interpretations made in the formulation of this report However it must
be noted that fluctuation may occur due to variations in rainfall temperature and other
factors

the drilling operations The classifications were further reviewed by visual observation
and testing in the laboratory All soils were classified in general accordance with the
Unified Soil Classification System A summary of the materials encountered are
presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 6
