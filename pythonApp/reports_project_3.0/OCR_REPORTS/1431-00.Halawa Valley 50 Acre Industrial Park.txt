INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our soil engineering
investigation performed for the subject project The purpose of this
investigation was to explore the subsurface soil and groundwater
conditions to provide soil data for use in the design and construction of
the industrial subdivision the access roadway and the proposed drainage
channel The approximate location of the project site and its vicinity
is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests which we
used in the engineering analysis to formulate our conclusions and
recommendations are presented in the appendices of this report

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed plan is to clear and grade the site for a light
industrial park development A final layout scheme was not available for
the subdivision drainage channel and roadway at the time of our
investigation
The proposed grading plan was also not available However
preliminary discussions with the project civil engineer indicates that
only minor cuts are planned for the upper area and terraced fills up to
10 to 20 feet thick are planned for the lower area to produce a gentle
overall slope from the existing Quarry Road down towards the southern
project boundary along South Halawa Stream
The proposed development consists of the following
1 Rough grading for a 50 acre industrial subdivision with
approximately 66 lots covering an area of about 1200 by 1800
feet in plan Only minor cuts are contemplated near the
northerly boundary but terraced fills up to 10 to 20 feet high
are planned along the middle and lower portions to create
fairly level pads for the lots

SITE DESCRIPTION
Industrial Subdivision
The proposed industrial subdivision is located westerly of Halawa
Jail between Quarry Access Road and South Halawa Stream The topography
along the higher land portion next to the Quarry Road is a relatively
flat terrace 5 to 7 percent gradient that slopes down towards South
Halawa Stream The site is covered with stockpiled materials and
construction equipment Portions of this area are presently covered with
haole koa trees The ground elevations in this area vary from about 250
to 150 feet
From the higher terrace the ground slopes down more steeply for 20
to 30 feet to a lower area at the southwest side of the site This lower
area is relatively flat with ground elevations ranging from 125 to 145
feet and in places was covered with a very thick growth of haole koa
trees during our exploration

There is an easement for a sewer line along the southerly westerly
and a portion of the easterly boundaries of the subdivision site
An old trash or rubbish dump appears to be located generally in the
vicinity of the area between 4 and 6
Drainage Channel
The proposed drainage channel will extend from the Quarry Access
roadway crossing at the South Halawa Stream Tributary near Halawa Jail
and follow along this drainageway in a southerly direction a distance of
about 1400 feet drop in elevation from about 250 to 160 feet to its
intersection with South Halawa Stream then runs in a westerly direction
along South Halawa Stream for a distance of about 1300 feet drop in
elevation from about 160 to 120 feet and terminate at about the
southwest corner of the industrial subdivision where it will connect to
the existing lined channel
The proposed channel generally parallels the existing sewer
easement and crosses it at about 200 feet north of the intersection of
the Tributary and South Halawa Streams

2000 feet and terminates near Halawa Jail

alignment A 42 inch waterline crosses under the roadway between Boring

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
The site is located in the lower portion of Halawa Valley which has
been brought to its present form by a complex series of geologic events
The original Halawa Valley was a youthful valley cut into the Koolau Dome
during a lower stand of the sea Several subsequent stages of
alluviation have filled the valley floor with fluvial deposits
At an intermediate stage between two series of alluvial deposition
pyroclastic material such as cinder was laid across the mouth of the
valley by members of the Honolulu Volcanic Series These deposits of
volcanic material most likely originating from Aliamanu and Salt Lake
Craters caused a diversion of Halawa Stream to the west
Stream erosion and diversion of the course of Halawa Stream has
shaped the area to its present topography
During the last century man has been an important force in shaping
Halawa Valley to its present form with the placement of extensive fills
over much of the older alluvial deposits

Soil Conditions
Our test borings encountered basically competent fill and alluvial
materials consisting of silty clay clayey silt sandy silt and boulders
along the proposed roadway and concrete channel alignments
The test borings in the industrial subdivision generally
encountered suitable soil except for some pockets of clayey soils which
may be expansive noted in the surface layer of Boring and 4
Also some rubbish stockpiles were noted in the vicinity between Boring
and 6
The following soil conditions were encountered in the borings
Higher Ground above elevation 145
Boring and 16 drilled in these areas
encountered 5 to 10 feet of dense silty gravels and cobbles over
about 6 to 10 feet of hard or stiff clayey silt to the depths
drilled Boring and 4 encountered 10 to 16 feet of stiff
clay in the surface layer over alluvium
Lower Ground below elevation 145
Boring 13 and 14 drilled in the lower areas generally
below elevation 145 feet encountered dense gravels with boulders
and cobbles mixed with alluvial deposits consisting of Silty and
gravelly clays to the depths drilled
Boulderly materials may be encountered in the lower areas along the
stream channels and may present excavation problems and limit the
use of these materials as backfill

Groundwater Level
Groundwater was encountered at 11 to 20 foot depths in Boring Nos
3 8 and 9 The remaining borings did not encounter groundwater It
appears that the observed groundwater level in Boring No 3 may reflect a
localized seepage condition from other sources
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration encountered relatively competent subsurface
conditions at the site From a soils engineering point of view the site
is feasible for the proposed industrial park development site grading and
improvements provided our recommendations are followed
Industrial Subdivision Site Grading
The preliminary grading plan for the industrial subdivision was not
available at the time of our investigation but it is contemplated that
the major portion of the grading will be fills up to 10 to 20 feet thick
in the lower areas and only minor cuts in the upper areas Import fill
will be required for the proposed grading
Site Preparation
Prior to filling the site should first be cleared of vegetation
debris rubbish and other unsuitable materials The area to
receive fill should then be scarified and recompacted to 90 of its
maximum density as established by ASTM D 1557 78 test method
Stockpiles of rubbish and or fill material were noted in the area
of Boring and 4 and may be present in other areas After
clearing and grubbing operations field inspections should be made
to attempt to locate and remove unsuitable stockpiles
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1431 00 Page 8
Fill Placement
Fills should be placed in maximum 10 inch horizontal loose lifts
and compacted to obtain 90 of the above specified maximum density
For roadway areas the subgrade level should be compacted to a
minimum of 95 of its maximum dry density
Expansive clayey soils used in fills should be moisture conditioned
to above optimum moisture content prior to compaction and placed a
minimum of 3 feet below the finished grade to reduce the swelling
potential of the soils
Fills placed on slopes having grades steeper than 5 1 H V should
be properly keyed and benched into firm existing ground see Plate
3 for details
Fill Material
In general the on site soils are suitable for use in the fill
Excavated on site material generally less than 6 inches in size
may be used as a source of fill material
Imported fill material should generally be low to non expansive
soils and submitted to the project soils engineer for laboratory
testing and approval prior to its intended delivery to the project
site

Drainage Channel
A concrete lined rectangular drainage channel approximately 10 to
15 feet deep and 2700 feet long is planned to carry the flow from the
Tributary Stream into South Halawa Stream then along South Halawa Stream
to the existing lined channel near the abandoned H 3 highway segment
Sewer Crossing
The existing sewer line crossing at South Halawa Stream tributary
will probably be below the invert of the new channel A concrete
jacket should be designed to protect the sewer line Stiff
alluvial deposits consisting of clayey silt cobbles and boulders
are anticipated near the stream bottom A six inch bedding layer
of 3B Fine gravel is recommended below the concrete jacket to
provide uniform support for a working platform and to facilitate
dewatering if required
Excavation
The drainage channel alignment will generally follow the existing
unlined stream alignment Relatively easy digging is anticipated
through the alluvial soils although many cobbles and boulders were
encountered in some areas
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1431 00 Page 12
Although the streams were dry during our explorations provisions
should be made to divert the water flowing during wet periods to
allow the construction to proceed in the dry
Due to the depth of the excavation anticipated shoring of the
excavation slopes is recommended to minimize caving in of the
granular soil slopes
The method and equipment used for excavation should be the choice
of the contractor within practical limits
If sufficient space is available the excavation may be cut back at
a temporary slope of 1 1 H V in lieu of the above wall supports
Where the channel extends next to the existing sewer easement or
any other existing structures shoring of the slopes are
recommended to minimize damage of the existing structures
Heavy equipment and stockpiles should be kept away from the tops of
the open excavations
The ground adjacent to and near the top of the excavation should be
continuously monitored for cracks or dips and other indications of
movement and possible sloughing of the excavation Slopes Such
monitoring is particularly critical in the areas where existing
structures are adjacent to the excavation
Backfill
Since there is a good possibility that the area adjacent to the
proposed channel alignment may be developed in the future it is
important that the channel walls be properly backfilled and
compacted
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1431 00 Page 13
Backfilling operations should be inspected by a qualified
technician and density tests taken to verify that adequate
compaction has been obtained
Most of the on site granular soils less than 6 inches in size
from the channel excavation can be re used as the backfill behind
the structures to minimize the compactive effort required and
subsequent build up of additional lateral pressures
The backfill should be placed in lifts and mechnically compacted to
a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density as established by ASTM
D 1557 78
In order to provide uniform support for the channel slab a 6 inch
cushion of granular material such as 3 Fine or base course rock
is recommended

Sliding resistance of the structure Additionally a passive soil
resistance of 300 p c f may be included
Weepholes should be used through the walls to prevent the build up
of hydrostatic pressures behind the walls

Bearing Capacity and Settlement
The very stiff to hard alluvial and fill soils encountered through
the alignment have good bearing capacity Since the net increase
in load on the subsoils caused by the channel construction will be
small the resulting settlements should also be relatively small if
the drainage channel is properly constructed
For the dense alluvial soils an allowable bearing value of 4000
p s f may be used for the design of the channel
Dewatering
Groundwater may occur above the invert elevation in some portions
of the channel during construction depending on the time of year
and weather conditions
The method of dewatering should be left open to the contractor
provided adequate stability of the excavation is maintained
Dewatering operation should be done without pumping out the soil
fines i e pumping clear water only It should be coordinated
with the excavation support system so that the Stability of the
excavation is not jeopardized The operation should be carried out
without creating a quick condition or softening the bottom of the
excavation
Utility Trenches
Normal excavation is anticipated in the very stiff to hard alluvial
materials encountered at the site
Proper backfilling of the trenches is important to minimize
subsequent ground surface settlement along the utility alignments All
backfill should be placed in 10 inch loose lifts and compacted to 90 of
its maximum density as determined by ASTM D 1557 78

Design Review
Plans and specifications for site grading roadway and drainage
channel construction should be reviewed by the soils engineer prior to
construction to see that the intent of our recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the filling subgrade preparation and
backfilling operations be observed by the soils engineer during
construction to see that adequate compaction has been obtained and that
the subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of our
investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
If conditions encountered during construction are different from
those presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made

Laboratory Testing
ee red
Moisture content and unit weight determinations were
performed on selected samples as an aid in the classification and
evaluation of soil properties The results of these tests are
presented on the logs of boring at the appropriate sample depths
To further identify soil characteristics four Atter
berg Limit determinations and four sieve analyses tests were
performed The test results are summarized on Plates B 1 and
B 2
Three laboratory CBR tests and one compaction test were
performed on bulk samples of the on site surface soils to
evaluate their pavement subgrade support characteristics The
test results are summarized on Plate B 2
Four sets of direct shear tests were performed on
undisturbed samples to evaluate the strength characteristics of
near surface soils The test results are presented on Plates
B 3 through B 6
Two consolidation tests were performed on selected
undisturbed samples to evaluate the settlement characteristics
of the subsoil The test results are presented on Plates B 7
Five one inch ring swell tests were performed on
undisturbed and remolded air dried samples to evaluate the
swelling potential of the soils in both normal and worse case
conditions The test results are summarized on Plate B 9

The work under this section includes clearing and grubbing
preparation of fill areas placement and compaction of fill
and removal and backfilling of underground structures These
specifications are of a general nature and may be superseded by
specific requirements or recommendations within the soils
report
I Clearing
All areas within contract limit lines shall be cleared
of trash debris organic matter and all other dele
terious materials These materials shall be removed and
disposed off site
II Preparation of Fill Areas
A Loose surface soils shall be removed to expose com
petent natural soils Localized soft pockets shall be
undercut and backfilled with compacted select materials
W O 1431 00
Appendix C Plate C 2
B All surfaces shall be inspected and approved by the
soils engineer prior to the placement of any fill
C After approval the surface shall be scarified to a
depth of six 6 inches brought to near optimum
moisture content and compacted as herein specified
D Where the existing surface exceeds a slope of 5
horizontal to 1 vertical 5 1 the ground surface shall
be keyed and benched into competent soil to bond the
fill to the existing ground surface
E Subsurface drains may be required as directed by the
soils engineer where springs or zones of seepage are
encountered
III Fill Materials
Material for fill shall consist of on site soils or
approved import material Import material shall be
tested and approved a minimum of seven 7 working days
prior to transportation to the site Fill material
shall contain less than 5 percent organic matter and
other deleterious material and shall not contain rocks
or lumps in excess of six inches 6 in diameter
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1431 00
Appendix C Plate C 3
IV Placement and Compaction of Fill
A The 111 shall be placed in horizontal loose layers
not to exceed ten inches 10 in thickness Each layer
shall be spread uniformly and blade mixed to attain
uniformity of material and water content within each
layer
B Rock fragments or boulders less than six inches 6
in diameter may be utilized provided they are not
concentrated in any one area and that there is suffi
cient fine grained material to fill the voids between
the rocks
C Rocks greater than six inches 6 in diameter shall
be removed from the fill unless specifically approved
for placement in the fill by the soils engineer
D All fill shall be compacted to a minimum of 90 per
cent of the maximum dry density established by ASTM Test
Designation D 1557 unless otherwise specified in the
soils report
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1431 00
Appendix C Plate C 4
E The compaction equipment may be sheepsfoot vibra
tory or pneumatic tire rollers suited to the soil type
and capable of producing the specified results in an
expedient and efficient manner The equipment shall be
in good working condition fully ballasted and self
cleaning
F The compaction shall be accomplished in a systematic
and uniform manner over the entire fill area to produce
a uniformly compacted fill
G Fill material placed in an unsatisfactory condition
and not in accordance with these specifications shall
not be approved and shall be removed or reworked until
the required density is obtained
H The soils engineer shall be provided unrestricted
access to the fill to perform the necessary inspections
and testing
V Removal and Backfilling of Underground Structures
Underground structures such as cesspools cisterns
septic tanks wells pipe lines etc shall be
demolished and removed as directed by the soils
engineer Backfilling of the excavations shall be in
accordance with these specifications
