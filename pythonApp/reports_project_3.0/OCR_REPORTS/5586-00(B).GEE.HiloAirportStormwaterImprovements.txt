1 1 Introduction
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the Installation of Washrack at Hilo International Airport
Project No AH1080 02 project located on the Island of Hawaii The general location
and project vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering
recommendations resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and
engineering analyses These recommendations are intended for design of foundations
slabs on grade site grading and pavements only The findings and recommendations
presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report
1 2 Project Considerations
The project site is located at the Hilo International Airport on the Island of Hawaii
We understand the project involves installation of a washrack system for prevention of
wastewater discharges to comply with Hawaii Department of Health rules and
regulations A vehicle washrack is planned near the east end of the airport terminal
and the approximate location is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Fuel trucks ARFF vehicles and small passenger vehicles will utilize the
washrack The vehicle washrack will consist of a Portland cement concrete pavement
having a plan dimension of approximately 100 feet square An Oil Water separator is
planned on the southeast side and new sewer drainage and water lines are also
planned
Based on the available plans the site grading work will consist of cuts and fill on
the order of about 2 feet or less to achieve the design grades

2 1 Regional Geology
Hawaii the largest island of the Hawaiian Archipelago covers an area of
approximately 4 000 square miles This island was formed by the activity of five shield
volcanoes Kohala long extinct Mauna Kea active during recent geologic time Hualalai
last erupted in 1801 and Mauna Loa and Kilauea both active
The project site is located on the southeastern flank of the Mauna Kea Shield
Volcano which has been built up by successive accumulation of basaltic lava flows and
pyroclastic materials The near surface lava flows are from the Mauna Loa Volcano
overlying the Mauna Kea Shield The lava flows of the Mauna Kea Shield are believed to
be Pliocene to Pleistocene in age and belong to the upper member of the Hamakua
Volcanic Series The near surface lava flows of the Mauna Loa Shield may be on the
order of only 750 to 1 500 years old
The encountered lava formation appears to be of a a type flow which spread
downslope from vents further up on the mountain flank A a lavas are typically
characterized by a porous rough and irregular flow surface resembling a jagged
accumulation of rock fragments including cobbles and boulders Typically a more dense
and layered lava rock material is contained within the lava flow core
Cavities such as lava tubes or other void space are commonly encountered buried
within a a lavas When molten lava created structures by means of flow and ponding
many of these cavities were formed The cavities represent both lava tubes intra flow
cavities and blisters and pockets inter flow cavities Lava tubes are formed when molten
lava drains from the cooling flow leaving a hollow tube like structure which may extend
for a large distance longitudinally along the flow Inter flow cavities are generally smaller in
horizontal extent
The a a basalt lava rock and clinkers at the site are generally considered to be
relatively permeable rock and may transmit water readily in both horizontal and vertical

2 4 Earthquakes and Seismicity
In general earthquakes that occur throughout the world are solely caused by
shifts in the tectonic plates In contrast earthquake activity in Hawaii is primarily linked
to volcanic activity Therefore earthquake activity in Hawaii generally occurs before or
during volcanic eruptions In addition earthquakes may result from the underground
movement of magma that comes close to the surface but does not erupt The Island of
Hawaii experiences thousands of earthquakes each year but most are so small that
only sensitive instruments can detect the small tremors However some of the
earthquakes are strong enough to be felt and a few cause minor to moderate damage
In general earthquakes associated with volcanic activity are most common on
the Island of Hawaii Earthquakes that are directly associated with the movement of
magma are concentrated beneath the active Kilauea and Mauna Loa Volcanoes on the
Island of Hawaii Because the majority of the earthquakes in Hawaii over 90 percent
are related to volcanic activity the risk of seismic activity and degree of ground shaking
diminishes with increased distance from the southern portion of the Island of Hawaii
The Island of Hawaii has experienced numerous earthquakes greater than

3 2 Site Grading
Based on the existing topography we anticipate that minimal site grading work
will be required to achieve the design finished grades In general areas within the
contract grading limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed at the on set of
earthwork Vegetation debris deleterious material and other unsuitable materials

3 5 Underground Utilities
We anticipate newly planned utility line trenches will be excavated in the hard
basalt rock and medium dense to dense clinker encountered in the borings In general
granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67
gradation is recommended below the pipes Free draining granular materials such as
No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation should be used for the initial trench
backfill up to about 12 inches above the pipes It is critical that free draining granular
materials be used to provide adequate support around the pipes and to reduce the
compaction effort of the backfill
As an alternative the granular bedding and the initial trench backfill up to about
12 inches above the top of the pipes may consist of 1 inch minus well graded granular
materials aggregate base course Where used the 1 inch minus well graded granular
bedding and the initial trench backfill should be moisture conditioned and compacted to
not less than 90 percent relative compaction Improper placement of the initial trench
backfill could result in settlement of the backfill and damage to the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from a level of 12 inches above the pipes
to the top of the subgrade or finished grade may consist of excavated on site soils
having a maximum particle size of 6 inches The backfill should be
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content placed in maximum
8 inch level loose lifts and mechanically compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction to reduce the potential for future ground subsidence Where trenches are
below pavement areas the upper 3 feet of the trench backfill below the pavement
surface should be compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction

Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in

Core samples of the coral and volcanic tuff formations encountered at the site
were obtained using diamond core drilling techniques in general accordance with
ASTM D 2113 Diamond Core Drilling for Site Investigation Core drilling is a rotary
drilling method which uses a hollow bit to cut into the rock formation The material left in
the hollow core of the bit is mechanically recovered for examination and classification
Recovery REC is used as a subjective guide to the interpretation of the relative
quality of rock masses Recovery is defined as the actual length of material recovered
from a coring attempt versus the length of the core attempt For example if 4 feet of
material is recovered from a 5 foot core run the recovery would be 80 percent and
would be shown on the Logs of Borings as REC 80
The Rock Quality Designation RQD is also a subjective guide to the relative
quality of rock masses RQD is defined as the percentage of the core run that is sound
material in excess of 4 inches in length without any discontinuities discounting any
drilling induced fractures or breaks If 2 5 feet of sound material is recovered from a
5 foot core run the RQD would be 50 percent and would be shown on the Logs of
Borings as RQD 50 Generally the following is used to describe the relative quality
