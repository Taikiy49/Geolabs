INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this investigation was to define subsurface
conditions and to provide recommendations for the design and
construction of the proposed building additions at the Maui
Memorial Hospital This report summarizes our findings and
recommendations
Site Description
The proposed first increment development site is located at
the southern end of the Maui Memorial Hospital building complex
7
Page 2
W O 599 00
The hospital complex is situated on a relatively gentle slope
above the Wailuku Health Center site The site slopes down
makai toward the Kaahumanu Avenue at about 6 to 10 grade
The ground surface at the upper and lower A C parking areas
drops at a 1 1 slope ratio However there was no visible
sign of slope movements or instability in these slope areas
at the time of our field reconnaissance
The proposed building additions will be located immediately
east of the existing main structure which is presently grassed
An A C driveway runs through the building site Both sides
of the driveway are used as unpaved parking area
The existing covered walkway slopes down from elevation 156 at
the kitchen facility to elevation 142 at the medical II area
Proposed Construction
The proposed development for the lst increment consists of
two building additions an L shaped 5 story wing and a 2 story
laboratory radiology building The existing covered walkway
will be leveled to a finish elevation of 145
For the proposed 2 story building addition the northern portion
of the structure will be constructed at grade while the southern
portion will be elevated to match the existing floors
Cut and fill will be required within the new building site A
maximum of 12 feet of backfill will be necessary at the northern
corner of the 5 story wing An 8 feet cut will be required at
the western corner of the 2 story addition immediately adjacent to

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
Consolidated dunes occupy the central Maui region stretching
from Kahului for 8 miles across the Isthmus The dunes reach
the heights of 200 feet near the North Coast The dune sand
consists of mainly coral shells and foraminifera with some
basaltic sand The dune sands are stratified by different
degrees of consolidation The lowest sand is the hardest and
it is cemented to a firm rock called eolianite The eolianite
is believed to have been formed when the sea stood about 60 feet
lower than the present elevation Above this eolianite generally
there is a brownish red residual soil layer two to six feet thick
Overlying the residual soil are dune sand deposits that are weakly
cemented All dune sands are permeable when lie above zone of
saturation except along the North Coast near Wailuku where some
springs are found
Subsurface Explorations
The subsurface exploration for the proposed building sites con
sisted of drilling eleven test borings The borings were drilled
at the locations shown on the enclosed Boring Locations Plate l
The boring elevations were surveyed with a hand level based on the
finished floor elevation of 156 5 feet at the first floor of the
existing 4 story south wing building A detailed description of
the field exploration methods and accompanying laboratory testing

ratory test results
Soil Conditions
The exploration disclosed essentially uniform soil conditions
within the proposed building areas Light brown medium to fine
silty sand was encountered in all borings at ground surface to
the full depth of borings
In general the density of the soil increases with depths from
medium to very dense in all borings A loose to medium density
sand layer was noted at boring and 9 located next to
the existing building This looser layer appeared to be disturbed
on site material used to backfill the previous footing excavations
Groundwater was not noted in the bore holes at the time of field
exploration
DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The subsurface conditions encountered during the exploratory
program were relatively good from a soils and foundation en
gineering viewpoints Spread and wall footings bearing on the
undisturbed in situ silty sand will provide adequate support for
the proposed construction without excessive settlement New
footings located adjacent to the existing structures should
extend below the loose backfill into firm soils
Page 5
W O 599 00
Foundation
Spread and line footing foundation may be used for the proposed
building Foundation bearing in the in situ brown silty sand
can be designed for an allowable bearing pressure of 4 000 pounds
per square foot Generally the bottom of footings should be a
minimum of 36 inches below the finished ground surface
For the portions of the footing foundation located next to the
existing building the footing should be lowered to the elevation
similar to the existing building foundation Section A A on Plate 2
illustrated the present footing elevations
If the proposed footings have to be placed at an elevation lower
than the adjacent existing foundation they should be located
outside the 45 degree stress zone under the existing spread footing
foundations This will avoid having to underpin the adjacent footings
during construction However if a basement wall is required immedi
ately adjacent to the existing main building sheet piling will stiil
be required to protect the slab on grade and footings of the adjacent
structure
Where existing or abandoned utility lines are located under the
foundation footings should either be stepped down or be designed
to bridge over the loose backfill areas
Settlements
It is estimated that total settlements for the structures should be
less than 3 4 inch with a differential settlement of less than 1 2
inch
Page 06
W O 599 00
To reduce distress by foundation settlements slab on grade
should be separated by control joints from columns and walls
and away from direct footing contact In addition expansion
joints should be utilized between existing and proposed structures
and between the 5 story and 2 story additions
Excavations
Footing excavation in dune sand can stand vertically without
sheeting for a depth of about 4 feet Beyond that depth un
supported excavation should be sloped back at a 1l horizontal
to l vertical slope ratio To avoid settlement of the adjacent
slab on grade and wall units between footings supported on grade
beams it will be necessary to use sheet piling or shoring to
support the excavations immediately against the existing structures
Floor Slab
Slab on grade can be utilized without special provisions for
expansive soil Slab on grade with floor covering should be
protected by a plastic vapor barrier Groundwater was not en
countered within a depth of 35 feet below the existing ground
surface therefore it is not necessary to install a coarse
granular base under the slab
It is recommended that the subgrade be compacted to a minimum of
90 of maximum dry density established by ASTM Designation D 1557
72T
Page 7
W O 599 00
Retaining Wall
Retaining wall should be designed to resist active lateral loads
equivalent to a fluid pressure of 40 pcf if on site sandy soils
are utilized as backfill The backfill should be compacted between
90 to 95 maximum density Over compaction behind the wall can
increase the lateral earth pressure above the design value
A friction factor of 0 45 can be utilized for determining sliding
resistance of the retaining wall foundations
Weep holes or a subsurface drainage system should be incorporated
to prevent the build up of excessive hydrostatic pressure behind
the wall
If building foundations and slab loading are placed within 10 feet
of the top of the retaining wall the earth pressures should be
revised accordingly If these conditions occur they should be
evaluated on an individual basis to establish the total design
lateral earth pressures
Earthwork and Compaction Requirements
Footing excavation backfills should be mechanically compacted in
12 inch lifts to a minimum of 90 maximum density within the slab
areas Flooding should not be allowed as a substitute mean of
compacting backfill within the building slab areas
All areas to receive fill should be undercut to remove the surface
vegetation and any previously placed topsoil fills The ground
surface should then be keyed and benched to properly bond the newly
Page 8
W O 599 00
placed fill to the existing soil Prior to the placement of any
new fill the upper six inches of the existing ground surface
should be scarified brought to near optimum moisture content
and compacted to a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density
established by ASTM Test D 1557 72T Additional fills should be
placed in horizontal lifts and compacted to the specified density
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the foundation excavations be inspected
prior to the placement of the concrete or reinforcing to assure
that adequate bearing has been obtained and that the subsurface
conditions are consistent with the findings of our subsurface
investigation
As previously discussed the backfilling and earthwork operations
should be inspected on a full time basis This inspection should
include the necessary testing to assure that the proper degree of
compaction is consistently achieved Excessive compaction of the
backfill behind the retaining wall can increase the lateral earth
pressures above the design values
To minimize differential settlement concrete slab and first floor
wall supported on grade should be delayed as much as possible and
preferably after the upper floors are completed
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above inspections

FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling
11 borings utilizing truck mounted equipment The depths of all
the borings terminated at 24 5 feet to 35 0 feet below the exis
ting ground surface The locations of the borings are shown on
the enclosed Site Plan and Boring Location Plate l
The soils were classified by visual and textural examination in
the field by our engineer who continuously supervised the drilling
operations The classifications were verified by visual inspection
and testing in the laboratory All soils were classified in accor
dance with the Unifield Soil Classification System A graphic
presentation of the soils encountered is presented on the Boring
Logs Plate A 1 through A 1l
Soil samples were obtained by driving either a 2 4 inch I D ring
samplers or a standard penetration sampler with a 140 pound hammer
free falling a distance of 30 inches The blow counts to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the logs of borings at the
appropriate sample depth

LABORATORY TESTING
Moisture content determinations was made on all the samples as
an aid in the soil classification and in evaluating soil proper
ties The results of these tests are presented on the Log of
Borings at the appropriate sample location
Three sieve analyses were conducted on the samples at different
elevation to determine the distribution of the grain sizes The
test results are summarized on Plate A 12
A California Bearing Ratio CBR test and a modified proctor
compaction test were performed on a surface sample of the on
site soil to evaluate the suitability of the soil as fill and
pavement support The results are given on Plate A 13 and A 14
A total of two direct shear tests were performed on selected
samples to evaluate the strength characteristics of the en
countered soils The test results are shown on Plate A 15
