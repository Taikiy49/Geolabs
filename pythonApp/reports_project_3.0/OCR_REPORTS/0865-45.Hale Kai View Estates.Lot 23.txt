As requested we have made a study of the near surface soil
conditions below the above referenced lot The purpose of
the study was to evaluate the suitability of the near surface
soils as foundation support for the proposed house The study
consisted of three 3 borings drilled within the proposed
building site laboratory soil tests and a review of the
grading and compaction test record on the building site This
report summarizes our observations and recommendations

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that the lot is underlain by
grayish brown clay and gravel fill The laboratory test
results on this material generally indicated that they have
moderate to high expansion potential Therefore for Lot 23
the slab on grade and foundation construction would require
special design procedures in order to reduce the shrink swell
effect of the subsoils
House Foundation
For this lot post and beam construction is highly desirable
and recommended due to the sloping terrain and expansive soil
conditions The post and beam construction generally results
in lower foundation costs and the individual footings can be
readily adjusted should the need occur Where utilized
concrete slab on grade should also be properly designed for
expansive soil conditions
For post and beam construction we recommend that the expansive
clays be overexcavated down for a depth of 3 feet below the
bottom of the individual post footings and then replaced with
non expansive borrow soils such as crusher waste select
borrow or coral compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum
dry density per ASTM D 1557 78 The spread footings can then
be placed on the compacted non expansive soil
GEOLABS HAWAII
Alternatively the individual post footing can be extended a
minimum depth of 36 inches below the finished grade and back
filled with on site soil compacted to a minimum of 90 of its
maximum dry density The footings should be poured in place
and the excavation backfilled and compacted to provide an
impervious layer over the footing
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f can be used for
footings bearing on the stiff natural ground or compacted fill
If a footing is located next to a utility line it should
extend to the bottom of the utility trench to reduce footing
settlement due to settlement of the trench backfill
Surface water should be diverted away from the foundations
Retaining Wall Foundation
Retaining walls up to 5 feet high are planned between the
portions of the house on slab on grade and the portion on
post and beam
In order to reduce the expansive effects of the existing clay
soils on the wall we recommend that the expansive clays be
overexcavated down for a depth of 24 inches below the bottom of
the footing and then replaced with non expansive borrow soils
such as crusher waste or coral compacted to a minimum of 90
of its maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78
The overexcavation and replacement should extend a minimum
distance of 12 inches beyond the footing width
The wall footing should be embedded a minimum depth of 12
inches below the outside finished grade Therefore the total
depth of the above overexcavation and replacement would be 3 0
feet below the outside finished grade
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f can be used
Off site non expansive soil should be used as backfill over
the wall footing and behind the wall
The walls should be designed to resist lateral soil pressure of
35 p c f equivalent fluid pressure
A coefficient of friction of 0 4 between the bottom of the
footing and compacted soil can be used to determine the lateral
resistance of the wall
A subsurface drainage system should be utilized behind the wall
to prevent the build up of hydrostatic pressure

Slab On Grade
Concrete slab on grade is planned for the front portion of the
house It appears that this area would require fill up to 5
feet in order to obtain sufficient level area Select non
expansive borrow soils should be used for fills The on site
clay soils are expansive and are unsuitable as slab subgrade
soils
In order to reduce the expansive effects of the clay the
expansive clays should be overexcavated down for a depth of 3
feet below the slab subgrade and then replaced with non
expansive borrow soils such as select borrow crusher waste or
coral compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density
per ASTM D 1557 78 Except where retaining walls are used
this overexcavation and replacement should be extended for a
distance of 3 feet beyond the perimeter of the slab
A 4 inch layer of base course should be provided below the slab
and compacted to 90 of its maximum dry density In order to
protect against moisture infiltration a polyethylene moisture
barrier may be incorporated below the slab The membrane
should be protected by a thin layer of sand to aid in the
concrete curing

Concrete Driveway and Sidewalks
Expansive clay soils were encountered along the proposed drive
way Since the driveway can tolerate some amount of heaving
the clay overexcavation and replacement can be reduced to 2
feet below the driveway subgrade level Where 2 or more feet
of fill is planned select non expansive borrow material should
be used to cap over the expansive clay Where less than 2 feet
of fill is planned overexcavation and replacement of the
expansive clay with select non expansive material is required
The above requirements should be followed for a minimum distance
of 3 feet beyond the driveway
Since a relatively long driveway is planned joints are recom
mended at about 8 to 10 foot intervals in order to allow for
some relative movement of the concrete
For future sidewalks capping of the clay or overexcavation and
replacement similar to the driveway are recommended to reduce
heaving caused by the expansive clay soil
