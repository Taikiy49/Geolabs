This report summarizes the results of our geotechnical engineering services
performed during the earthwork and grading operations at the above referenced project
from August 1989 to December 1991 The subject site is located in Aiea Gulch between
Aiea Heights and Halawa Heights approximately one half mile mauka northeast of the
Aiea Sugar Mill in the district of Ewa on the island of Oahu Hawaii
SITE GRADING AND FILL PLACEMENT
The mass grading work was performed by Pan Pacific Construction Inc The
mass grading work generally consisted of cuts and fills on the order of up to 30 feet The
on site fill materials generally consisted of dark brown silty clay and excavated basalt
rock and previously stockpiled reddish brown silty clay
Prior to fill placement the existing vegetation was removed and the exposed
subgrades were scarified and brought to near optimum moisture content and
recompacted to 90 percent of the maximum dry density as determined in accordance with

Proctor The new fill material was then placed in near level lifts and compacted to a
minimum of 90 percent of its maximum dry density
Much of the fill material was generated from excavation in the basalt rock
formation In general the excavated rock material is screened to obtain fill material that
is less than 12 inches in largest dimension Large boulders with less than five feet 5

finish grade

ADDITIONAL RECOMMENDATIONS
Slopes at Lot Nos 85 and 86
The slopes at Lot Nos 85 and 86 appear to be overly steep estimated at
or steeper and these slopes have become eroded Observations of the soils
exposed in the eroded areas indicated the face of the slope consists of loose reddish
brown clayey silt
According to the grading plan the slopes appear to be located beyond the
grading limit However we recommend that loose material be removed and the slope
restored to the proper ratios of two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V
Individual lot owners at Lot Nos 85 and 86 should be advised to obtain profes
sional engineering consultation in the planning design and construction of any future
improvements
Cut Slope Protection at Lot Nos 164 through 167 and 175
The cut slope behind Lot Nos 164 through 167 and 175 has a horizon of
volcanic clinker exposed near the base of the 4H 1V cut slope It is our opinion that
this weathered clinker horizon is stable temporarily However it is difficult to predict
the long term behavior of this slope There is a possibility that this slope could ravel
or it may continue to stand up reasonably well over a long period There is a potential
that if a loss of this material occurs the upper basalt ledge would lose support and
GEOLABS HAWAII
Aiea Ventures Page 5

possibly fail Such a failure could cause damage to the residential structures below or
cause injury to the occupants
At Lot No 175 two 2 lava tubes of about three feet 8 wide were noted filled
partially with clayey soil For long term stability of the cut slope it is recommended
that these tubes be cleaned and capped with a riprap facing in general accordance
with our letter dated
It is our Own opinion that in the sake of prudence efforts should be made to
protect the steep cut slope at these lots Should the developer or owner elect to
institute additional measures to protect the steep cut slope face it is our opinion that
the soil horizon should be protected by a riprap or shotcrete facing constructed in
general accordance with our letter dated
Should the developer or owner decide not to make remedial efforts to protect
the slope it is his obligation to disclose the potential hazard to any future buyers and
accept the liability for any potential damages which may be caused by ravelling of the
slope We also recommend that gutter systems be installed on the roofs of these
homes and that water be diverted away from the cut slope and the house foundations
Cut Slopes at Lot Nos 101 and 102

behind Lot Nos 101 and 102 in our letter dated We believe that
