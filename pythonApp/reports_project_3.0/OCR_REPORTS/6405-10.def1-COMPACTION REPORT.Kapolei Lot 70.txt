PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project involves the mass grading of the Kapolei Lot 70 site adjacent to the
intersection of Kapolei Parkway and Fort Barrette Road in Kapolei on the Island of Oahu
Hawaii
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services for
the grading operations and infrastructure installation for the project site
SITE GRADING
The mass grading operations consisted of clearing and grubbing the existing
ground surface and placement of up to about 10 feet of fill to bring the site surface
elevation to between about 56 to 62 feet above Mean Sea Level On site clayey soils
were used for the lower portion of the fill in particular the detention basin in the southern
portion of the site Select non expansive granular materials imported from West Kapolei
were used as capping fill up to about 5 feet below finished grade Delta Construction
Corporation performed the site grading work Samples of the fill materials were obtained
and transported to our soils laboratory for testing The maximum dry densities and
optimum moisture contents were established in general accordance with ASTM D 1557
Modified Proctor The optimum moisture contents and corresponding maximum dry

The clayey fill materials used in the deeper portion of the fill were
moisture conditioned and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry

During the site grading operations periodic field density tests were performed in
general accordance with ASTM D 6938 Nuclear Gauge Method Compaction test results
are summarized on Plates 4 1 through 4 7 Areas that were determined to be

Our services consisted of part time field observation of the contractor s subgrade
preparation fill placement and compaction procedures and confirmation of subgrade soil
conditions exposed during the above grading operations
Based on our partial field observations and the referenced test results it is our
