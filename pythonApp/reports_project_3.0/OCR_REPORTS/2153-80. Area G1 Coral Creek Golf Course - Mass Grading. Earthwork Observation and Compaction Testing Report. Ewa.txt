Earthwork Observation and Compaction Testing Report
Coral Creek Golf Course Mass Grading
Area G1 of Ewa by Gentry West
Dear Mr Young
This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations performed
during the mass grading operations for the Coral Creek Golf Course Area G1 of Ewa by
Gentry West from August 19 1993 through
The subject site is located on the eastern side of Kapolei Parkway west of Fort
Weaver Road in Ewa on the island of Oahu Hawaii The site is also bounded by the
Arbors multi family development to the north and the Area 6 single family development to
the south
The mass grading work for Area G1 was performed by RHS Lee Inc The mass
grading work consisted of grading of the proposed golf course along with the slopes
between the golf course and the adjacent subdivisions to the north and south The mass
grading work generally consisted of small cut and fill heights of about 5 to 10 feet with
some isolated areas up to 15 to 25 feet in height The on site fill materials was generated
from Area G1 and Area G2 and from the Area 6 single family development to the south
The available fill materials generally consisted of silty coral sand silty coral gravel and
sandy clay
Prior to fill placement the existing vegetation was removed and the exposed
subgrades were scarified to a depth of approximately 6 inches 6 brought to near
optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum
dry density as determined in accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557 The fill
material was then placed in level lifts and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the
maximum dry density

During the fill placement operations periodic field density tests were performed in
general accordance with the American Society of Testing and Materials ASTM Test
Designation D 2922 Nuclear Gauge Method Results of the compaction tests are
summarized on Plates 4 1 through 4 5 of this report
During the grading period various on site soils were tested prior to use in the field
The maximum dry density and optimum moisture content relationships were established
in our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified
Proctor and the test results are summarized on Plate 3 of this report
Our services consisted of full time and part time field observation of the
contractor s fill placement and compaction procedures and confirmation of subgrade soil
conditions exposed during the above grading operations
Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from
on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should be considered accurate
only to the degree implied by the methods used Where low test results were noted the
areas was reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained
Field observations by our field technician during the mass grading work for the
project indicate that this work was performed in accordance with the general intent of the
recommendations in the following references
