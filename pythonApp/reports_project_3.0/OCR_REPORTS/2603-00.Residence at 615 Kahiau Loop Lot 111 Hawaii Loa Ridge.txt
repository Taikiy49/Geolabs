SUMMARY OF RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration encountered a thin surface
layer about one 1 to three 3 feet thick of
silty clay overlying weathered basalt formation
Laboratory testing and previous experience
have demonstrated that the surface clayey soil
has moderate to high swelling potential
particularly when remolded or recompacted
Special foundation design is recommended to
reduce the effects of the expansive soil
To utilize a slab on grade foundation the
recommended site improvement technique is to
place a minimum three 3 foot thick capping
layer of non expansive material below the
foundation
To utilize a spread or continuous footing
foundation the recommended site improvement
technique is to place the footing on three 3
feet of non expansive material or to embed the
footing a minimum of three 3 feet below the
finished grade
The recommended three 3 feet of non
expansive capping material or footing
embedment can be reduced if weathered basalt
formation is encountered at depths less than
three 3 feet below the finished grade
The text of this report should be referred to for
detailed and special design recommendations

INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of a geotechnical engineering evaluation performed
for the proposed residence at Lot Ridge Phase IV on the island of
Oahu Hawaii The general location of the site and its vicinity is shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is our understanding that the proposed house will consist of a two 2 story
residence and a swimming pool At the time of this report preparation the project design
had not been finalized and construction plans were not available
Concrete slab on grade are anticipated for the proposed residence and retaining
walls will be constructed along various parts of the proposed residence to provide grade
separations
A swimming pool is also planned at the southwestern corner of the proposed
residence
Site grading involving cuts and fills up to about 10 feet is anticipated
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this study was to provide recommendations for the house
foundations and site grading design of the proposed construction The recommendations
are based on the soil information obtained from borings drilled for this exploration

W O 2603 00 Page 3
The study consisted of drilling and sampling four 4 borings laboratory testing
engineering analyses and report preparation The approximate locations of the borings
are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests which were utilized in the
engineering analysis to formulate our recommendations are presented in the appendices
of this report
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is a flag lot located at 615 Kahiau Loop in the Hawaii Loa Ridge
District of Honolulu This approximately rectangular 23 400 square foot lot can be
accessed from Kahiau Loop with an existing concrete driveway located at the
northeastern corner of the lot
The topography of the site slopes downward towards the southwest with gradients
ranging from about four horizontal to one vertical 4H 1V to two horizontal to one vertical
2H 1V The current ground elevations range from about 566 to 494 feet above Mean
Sea Level MSL
At the time of our field exploration the site was vacant and mostly covered with
grasses and trees except for the northern portion of the lot which had been cleared in the
vicinity of the existing concrete driveway

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The exploration program consisted of drilling four 4 borings to depths of 11 5 to
20 7 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate locations of the borings are
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general the borings disclosed that the site is underlain by a thin surface layer
about one 1 to three 3 feet thick of expansive clayey soil overlying weathered basalt
rock formation
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings during our field exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration disclosed that the lot is underlain by a thin surface layer of
reddish brown expansive clay The laboratory test results and previous experience
indicate that this material is moderately to highly expansive Therefore the foundation will
require special design and construction procedures in order to reduce the shrink swell
effects of the subsoils
Foundation
A spread or continuous footing foundation may be used to support the proposed
structure
In order to reduce the effects of the expansive soil we recommend that the
expansive clays be over excavated down to a depth of three 3 feet or to weathered
basalt formation below the ground surface whichever is less The recommended over
excavation should be performed below each individual footing and then replaced with
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2603 00 Page 5
non expansive borrow soils such as crusher waste select borrow or coral The replaced
soils should be compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density determined
in accordance with ASTM D 1557 78 The footings can then be placed on the compacted
non expansive soil for details refer to the Schematic Section on Plate 3
Alternatively the footings may be embedded a minimum of three 3 feet below the
lowest adjacent grade or to weathered rock formation for details refer to the Schematic
Section on Plate 3
An allowable bearing value of 3000 p s f can be used for footings bearing on
compacted fill in situ soil or weathered basalt formation
If a footing is located next to a utility line it should extend to the bottom of the
utility trench to reduce footing settlement due to settlement of the trench backfill
Surface water should be diverted away from the foundation to avoid ponding of
surface run off water that may affect the expansive soils around the foundation
If a footing is located on or adjacent to sloping ground it should be embedded
deeper to provide an eight 8 foot minimum horizontal set back distance measured from
the toe of the footing to the sloping ground surface
Slab On Grade
In order to reduce the expansive effects of the soils we recommend that a
minimum three 3 foot thick capping layer of non expansive material be placed below the
slab on grade foundation The thickness of the capping layer may be reduced where
weathered rock formation is encountered
GEOLABS HAWAIH
W O 2603 00 Page 6
The expansive clay below the slab should be removed for a minimum depth of
three 3 feet below the outside finished grade and replaced with non expansive off site
borrow materials such as select borrow crusher waste or coral compacted to a
minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density determined in accordance with ASTM D
1557 78 The underlying clay soil remaining should be moistened by water sprinkling and
then maintained in a moist condition prior to placement of non expansive capping
material
A four 4 inch layer of 3 Fine cushion fill should be provided below the slab In
order to protect against moisture infiltration a vapor barrier may be incorporated below
the slab
The thickened edge slab footings can bear on the compacted non expansive fill
The thickened edges should be embedded a minimum of 12 inches below the lowest
adjacent grade for details refer to the Schematic Section on Plate 4
Concrete Driveway
It is our understanding that it is proposed to demolish the existing concrete
driveway and to construct a new concrete driveway Recommendations for the three 3
foot non expansive capping layer for the concrete slab on grade should be followed in the
concrete driveway design However since the driveway could tolerate some amount of
swelling the non expansive capping thickness could be reduced to two 2 feet to reduce
costs if some slab cracking is tolerable The subgrade soils should be kept moist prior
to concrete placement
A minimum 12 inch deep thickened edge is recommended for the driveways
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2603 00 Page 7
Joints are recommended at about 8 to 10 foot intervals and at discontinuities in
order to allow for some relative movements of the concrete
Retaining Wall
It is our understanding that concrete retaining walls are to be used for grade
separation The guidelines for retaining wall design are listed below
1 For lateral earth pressures the following can be used
Walls unrestrained at the top 35 p s f equivalent fluid pressure
active condition level backfill per foot of depth
Walls restrained at the top 55 p s f equivalent fluid pressure
at rest condition level backfill per foot of depth
An allowable bearing value of up to 3000 p s f is recommended for
foundations bearing on the weathered rock formation or compacted fill
2 A passive earth resistance of 300 p s f per foot of depth may be used for
the wall footing on level areas The passive pressure must be reduced for
walls on slopes
3 A base friction factor of 0 4 may also be used to determine the resistance
against sliding of the wall
4 The wall foundations on relatively flat areas should be embedded a
minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade
5 The wall foundations on or adjacent to sloping ground should be embedded
deeper to provide an eight 8 foot minimum horizontal set back distance
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2603 00 Page 8
measured from the toe of the wall footing to the existing sloping ground
surface
6 The wall foundations located next to sewer drainage easements should be
embedded below an imaginary plane of one and one half horizontal to one
vertical 0V extending upward from the bottom edge of the utility
trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility lines
7 Backfills directly behind the retaining wall should consist of granular non
expansive material such as select borrow crusher waste or 3B Fine
gravel The top 12 inches of backfill should consist of non expansive
impervious material to reduce water infiltration behind the wall
8 A subsurface drainage system subdrains or weepholes should be utilized
behind the walls Subdrains should be properly constructed to drain water
away from the wall foundations
Swimming Pool
For the swimming pool wall design a lateral earth pressure of 55 p s f equivalent
fluid pressure per foot of depth may be used for walls restrained at the top at rest
condition with level backfill
We recommend that the backfill behind the pool wall should consist of granular
material such as 3B Fine gravel to facilitate compaction
Foundation located in the vicinity of the swimming pool should be embedded below
an imaginary plane of one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V extending upward from the

bottom edge of the pool or the swimming pool wall should be designed for additional
lateral earth pressure induced by the adjacent foundation loads
Lot Grading
The fills and backfills within the top 36 inches minimum below the on grade slabs
should consist of non expansive soil such as select borrow crusher waste or coral
All imported fill materials should be tested and approved by the soils engineer prior
to being used at the site
Prior to construction the area to receive fill should be cleared of vegetation and
debris
The fills should be placed in loose horizontal lifts not exceeding 10 inches thick and
compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density determined in accordance
with ASTM D 1557 78
Any fills to be placed on existing slopes having ratios steeper than five horizontal
to one vertical 5H 1V should be keyed and benched into firm in situ soil or weathered
basalt formation to lessen potential instability of the new fill see Plate 5 The filling
operations should start at the lowest point and continue up in level horizontal compacted
layers in accordance with the above recommendations for fill placement
Two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V cut and fill slopes are recommended
The site should be graded to provide drainage of surface runoff away from all
foundations

Drainage
The finished lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings should be shaped
to shed water away from foundations and to avoid ponding condition Also it is advised
that a gutter system be installed around the residence and that gutter water be diverted
far away from the house foundation and driveway Excessive landscape watering near
foundations should also be avoided
Planters next to the foundation should be avoided or have concrete bottoms and
drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the above
recommended foundations since ponded water could cause excessive subsurface soil
saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavation should
be properly backfilled tight against the slab footing immediately after setting of the
concrete to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained to drain
all surface run off away from the slab foundation
Design Review
Plans and specifications for foundation site grading and utility construction should
be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written comments prior to construction
to see that the intent of our recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the footing excavations and backfilling be observed by a
soils engineer during construction to see that adequate bearing has been obtained and
that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of our exploration

Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and approved by
the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the above
observations
LIMITATIONS
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations made in
accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation engineering principles and
practices This warranty is in lieu of all other warranties either expressed or implied
The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are based on our site
reconnaissance and soil information from boring and laboratory test data obtained from
this soil evaluation
Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encountered and cannot be fully
determined by reconnaissance and borings These unforeseen conditions such as

preparation of the foundation design of this project

Classification System A summary of the materials encountered are presented on the
Boring Logs Plates A 1 to A 4
