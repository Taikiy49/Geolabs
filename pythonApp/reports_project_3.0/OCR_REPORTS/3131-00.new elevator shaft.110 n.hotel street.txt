INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed new elevator shaft structure at 110 North Hotel Street in the
Chinatown area of downtown Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general
location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings resulting from our field exploration and
laboratory testing and presents our design level geotechnical engineering
recommendations derived from our analyses for the proposed project These
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3131 00 Page 2
recommendations are intended for design input only and may not be appropriate to form
the basis for construction cost estimates nor for construction bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located at 110 North Hotel Street in the Chinatown area of
downtown Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii The proposed new elevator structure
will be installed inside the existing building at the location shown on the Site Plan Plate
2
On the basis of information provided to our office it is our understanding that the
new elevator shaft will be approximately six feet 6 by eight feet 8 in plan dimension
Below the existing ground floor level the elevator will have a four foot 4 deep
mechanical equipment pit of the same plan dimension We also understand that the shaft
pit of the new hydraulic elevator will be eighteen inches 18 in diameter and will extend
to a depth of about 28 feet below the existing ground floor slab
Further details regarding the proposed new elevator construction were not available
at the time this report was prepared

subsurface conditions at the project site and to develop an idealized subsurface data set
for the formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to the design
and installation of the proposed new elevator pit shaft Our work was done in general
accordance with our proposal dated August 11 1993 to Alger Foundation Our scope
of work consisted of the following

SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed new elevator shaft will be located inside the former Eagle Foods
Restaurant at 110 North Hotel Street in the Chinatown area of downtown Honolulu on the
island of Oahu
Based on our field reconnaissance and the information provided to us we
understand that the existing building is an abandoned three story structure with a partial
basement on the Ewa side Concrete masonry walls and slab on grade concrete floors
are utilized for the existing building construction with a floor area of approximately 2 700
square feet in plan dimension The proposed new elevator will be located on the
Diamond Head side at approximately the central portion of the building

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Our field exploration indicates that the site is underlain by a surficial layer of silty
sand material over dense coral deposits at shallow depths Based on the competent
subsoil conditions encountered at the project site it is our opinion that conventional
shallow foundations bearing on the dense coral deposits may be used for support of the
proposed elevator structure Detailed recommendations are presented in the following
subsections
Foundation Support
Based on the information provided to us we understand that the proposed new
elevator structure will consist of concrete masonry block walls Due to the proximity of
the new elevator structure to the existing basement walls we understand that the elevator
footings will be lowered to approximately the same elevation as the adjacent basement
floor to avoid surcharging the basement walls Therefore excavation into the dense coral
deposits is anticipated for the elevator foundation construction work It is our opinion that
shallow foundations bearing on the dense coral deposits may be used for support of the
proposed new elevator structure
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3131 00 Page 6
An allowable bearing pressure of 2 500 pounds per square foot p s f may be
used for design of the elevator s shallow footing foundations bearing on the dense coral
deposits encountered at shallow depths This bearing value is for dead plus live loads
and may be increased by one third 14 for transient loads such as those caused by wind
and or seismic forces
We estimate the footing settlements under the anticipated design loads for footings
bearing on the dense coral deposits to be less than 1 inch total and inch differential
Footings located adjacent to underground utility trenches or easements should be
extended to a depth below the projection of an imaginary one horizontal to one vertical
1H 1V plane which extends up from the bottom edge of the utility trench or from the
invert of the pipeline within the utility easement
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive earth pressure
acting against vertical or near vertical faces of the foundation system and by frictional
resistance between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil A passive pressure
developed by an equivalent fluid weighing 300 pounds per cubic foot p c f may be
used Passive resistance should be neglected in the upper foot unless confined by a slab
or paved surface A coefficient of friction of 0 30 may be used between the footings and
the supporting subgrade
The footing subgrade should be scarified moisture conditioned to about two
percent 2 above optimum moisture and recompacted to a minimum of 90 percent of
its relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil
expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in
accordance with procedures described in ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91 If soft or
loose pockets are encountered at the bottom of the footing excavations the pockets
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3131 00 Page 7
should be over excavated to competent in situ material and replaced with select granular
fill compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction
Select granular fill material required to backfill the loose pockets and to raise the
ground surface to the bottom of the mechanical pit floor slab should be placed in level
lifts not exceeding eight inches 8 in loose lift thickness and compacted to not less than
90 percent relative compaction
The select granular fill material should consist of imported granular material such
as crushed coral basalt gravel or cinder sand The material should be well graded from
coarse to fine with no particles greater than three inches 3 in largest dimension The
material should have a laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR value of 20 or higher
and a swell potential of not more than one percent 1 when tested in accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1883 It should contain less than 20 percent passing the No
200 sieve It is our opinion that the excavated on site silty sand material may be used as
backfill material provided that the above requirements are satisfied Any imported fill
material should be tested and approved for its intended use by Geolabs Hawaii prior to
delivery to the project site
Footing excavations should be observed by a representative of Geolabs Hawaii
prior to the placement of reinforcing steel or concrete to evaluate whether an adequate
bearing surface has been exposed and if loose soils have been removed prior to concrete
placement
Slab on Grade
We anticipate that the interior floor for the mechanical pit will consist of concrete
slab on grade bearing on the compacted granular fill material The loads imposed by the
planned mechanical equipment are unknown at the time this report was written However
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3131 00 Page 8
we recommend that the interior floor slab of the mechanical pit be well reinforced and
thickened in the appropriate locations for the support of any planned machinery
A minimum six inch 6 thick layer of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67
gradation should be placed over the compacted granular fill to serve as a slab cushion
and capillary break A moisture barrier overlying the cushion fill should be incorporated
below the concrete slab to reduce the potential of infiltration of moisture One inch 1
of moist fine sand may be placed over the moisture barrier membrane to aid in curing of
the concrete slab
Elevator Shaft Pit Installation
It is our understanding that the shaft pit for the proposed new elevator will be
approximately eighteen inches 18 in diameter and will extend approximately 28 feet
deep below the existing ground floor slab We anticipate that the shaft pit would be
constructed in the manner of a drilled pier Due to the anticipated groundwater table at
deeper depths and the potential presence of loose coral pockets below the water table
casing of the drilled pier may be required during the augering operation We recommend
that a qualified contractor be utilized who is skilled and experienced in the construction
of the elevator pit shaft
Lateral Earth Loads
Since the footing foundations will be lowered to approximately the same elevation
of the adjacent basement floor and the elevator shaft pit will be about 28 feet deep which
should extend below the groundwater level anticipated at the project site Therefore
lateral earth pressure and groundwater pressure are important design considerations for
the proposed elevator structure

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling of one
1 boring to a depth of 16 5 feet below the existing ground floor slab at the approximate
location shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The boring was drilled using portable drilling
equipment
The material encountered in the boring were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who monitored the drilling operations on a near
continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed by visual inspection and
testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A graphic presentation of the materials encountered are presented
on the Boring Log Plate A 1
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled boring in general accordance with
ASTM Test Method D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by
driving a standard penetration sampler with a 140 pound hammer free falling 30
inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the
Boring Log at the appropriate sample depths
Core samples of rock materials encountered at the site were obtained using
diamond core drilling techniques in general accordance with ASTM Standard Practice D
2113 83 Diamond Core Drilling for Site Investigation Core drilling is a rotary drilling
method which uses a hollow bit to cut into the rock formation The rock material left in
the hollow core of the bit is mechanically recovered for examination and description
Recovery REC is used as a subjective guide to the interpretation of the relative
quality of rock masses Recovery is defined as the actual length of material recovered
from a coring attempt vs the length of the core attempt e g if 3 7 feet of material is
recovered from a 5 0 foot core run the recovery would be 74 and would be shown on
the Boring Logs as REC 74
The Rock Quality Designation RQD is also a subjective guide to the relative
quality of rock masses RQD is defined as the percentage of the core run that is sound
material in excess of 4 inches in length without any discontinuities discounting any drilling
induced fractures or breaks If 2 5 feet of sound material is recovered from a 5 0 foot core
run the RQD would be 50 and would be shown on the Boring Logs as RQD 50
Generally the following is used to describe the relative quality of the rock based on the
Practical Handbook of Physical Properties of Rocks and Minerals
