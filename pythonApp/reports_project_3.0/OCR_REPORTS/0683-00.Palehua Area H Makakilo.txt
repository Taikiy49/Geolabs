SUMMARY OF RECOMMENDATIONS
The site is suitable for the proposed residential
development
The on site gray to brown silty clay is moderately
expansive when it is used as fill material
Slab on grade construction can be utilized provided
it is designed for the expansive soil conditions
Properly compacted on site clayey soils will provide
CBR values of 5 to 23 percent
INTRODUCTION
This report summarizes the findings of a subsurface investigation
at the subject site and provides recommendations for the design
and construction of the proposed cluster single family housing
development
SITE DESCRIPTION
The subject project site is located on the upper Makakilo Drive
in the Palehua Subdivision complex in Makakilo Oahu Hawaii
The site is bounded on the west by Makakilo Drive between stations
105 45 and 123 25 and on the south by the existing Makakilo Reser
voir No 3 A gully runs along the north boundary and parallel to
a dirt road along the top of the eastern boundary ravine
Palehua Area H 2
The surface slopes down in an easterly direction toward the ravine
along the east boundary There is generally a slight slope of
5 to 8 percent in the center portion of the site At the north
and the south ends the ground slopes increase to about 9 percent
The project ground elevations vary from 956 feet in the west to
about 902 feet in the east
At present the site is moderately vegetated with California grass
bushes and small Keawe trees The stretch of Makakilo Drive which
borders the project has been rough graded but is not paved Ad
ditional widening and improvement of this main roadway are antici
pated in the future along with this project development A separate
soil report has been prepared to cover this portion of the Makakilo
Drive extension
The upper 70 feet of the ravine slope at the eastern boundary
is as steep as 1 horizontal to 1 vertical At the time of this
investigation no signs of instability or movement is noted
PROJECT CONSIDERATION
The proposed cluster housing development consists of about 107
single family houses with the necessary roadway system branching
out from Makakilo Drive Grading plans are not available at the
time of this investigation Anticipated site grading will involve
cutting the upper area and filling the lower area
Palehua Area H 3
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Subsurface Exploration
Subsurface conditions were explored by drilling six test borings
with truck mounted drilling equipment The field exploration was
supplemented by digging nine test pits with a backhoe The borings
and test pit locations are shown on the Site Plan Plate 1 A
detailed description of the field exploration methods and accom
panying laboratory testing is given in the Appendix along with
the Log of Borings and Test Pit Logs
Soil Conditions
The subsurface exploration disclosed that over the entire site
there is a surface layer of reddish brown clayey silt which varies
from 1 0 to 6 5 feet in thickness below surface elevations Beneath
the surface soil layer ts a highly weathered basalt formation The
rock formation is found as close as 1 foot from the surface in the
borings drilled along the dirt road located at the top of the eastern
ravine
In boring nos 4 through 6 and test pit location 7 9
a grayish brown to brown silty clay layer 2 5 to 8 0 feet thick
was encountered in the higher elevations between the surface soil
and the rock formation
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The subsurface conditions indicated that the proposed development
Palehua Area H 4
by means of cut and fill is feasible from a soils engineering
viewpoint
The reddish brown clayey silt surface soil has low expansion po
tenttal and can be used as a source of fill or for select borrow
material The underlying gray to brown silty clay has moderate
expansion potential when it is remolded as fill soil Slab on
grade bearing on this clayey material should be designed for this
medium expansion potential condition
Site Grading
All areas to receive fill should be undercut to remove the surface
vegetation
Prior to the placement of compacted fill the existing ground
surface should be cleared of debris vegetation boulders and
the upper six inches of the ground surface should be scarified
brought to near optimum moisture content and compacted to a mini
mum of 90 of its maximum dry density established by ASTM D1557
Additional fill should be placed in 9 inch loose lifts and compacted
to the above specified density
To minimize site grading into the underlying rock formation the
amount of cutting should be limited to about 8 to 10 feet deep in
the upper western section of the project At the lower eastern
portion of the site the underlying hard basalt rock level was
Palehua Area H 5
about 3 5 feet below the existing ground surface along the top
of the ravine slope
The highly weathered basalt rock below the soil layers has decom
posed to a very hard silty soil formation and conventional exca
vation equipment could be utilized
At the time of our investigation the surface and the underlying
soils had in place moisture contents below their optimum moisture
content Dust control measures would be required of the contrac
tor in order to minimize construction dust problems
The underlying gray to brown silty clay soil exibited moderate
expansion when remolded The laboratory CBR test indicated that
it had a CBR value of about 4 5 along with a 5 2 swell upon
saturation
The upper reddish brown clayey silt surface soil has low expansion
potential and a high CBR value and thus can be utilized as a source
of borrow fill for the roadway and slab on grade construction In
the lowlying fill area this surface soil could be utilized as a
non expansive capping soil above the moderately expansive silty
clay This would require the contractor to separate and to stock
pile the better on site soil for later select borrow uses A mini
mum of 2 feet of reddish brown clayey silt soil would be required
within the building area if conventional slab on grade construction
is utilized for the low expansion potential condition
Palehua Area H 6
Slopes
Cut slopes constructed in the on site soils can be designed at
a maximum slope ratio of 1 horizontal to 1 vertical 1 1
Fill slopes should be designed at a 2 horizontal to 1 vertical
2 1 slope ratio
The on site soil its highly erosive and all slopes should be planted
as soon as practicable after construction Runoff over the slope
bank should be minimized
Slope Stability
The borings drilled along the top bank of the on site ravine along
the northern boundary indicated weathered rock formation at shallow
depths There was no sign of slope movement or instability on this
ravine slope along the project boundary Fill over the slope along
the gulch boundary should be avoided
Utility Trench Excavation
Sections of shallow trench excavation into the stiff in situ soil
formations may be left open for short term construction period with
out sloughing of the vertical sidewalls
Weathered rock formations can be expected for deep excavations in
the western portion and for shallower excavations in the east
Therefore a rock clause could be included in the contract do
cuments
Palehua Area nye 7
Foundation
Spread and wall footing foundations may be utilized For the
light structural loading anticipated at this project an allow
able bearing pressure of 2 000 p s f may be used for the footing
bearing on the on site soil or upon the compacted fill
For footing bearing on the weathered rock the bearing value can
be increased to 4 000 p s f if required
For footing bearing on the remolded grayish brown silty clay fill
layer the exterior footing should bear a minimum of 18 inches below
the finish grade For slab on grade footing details refer to Plates
C 3 and C 4 Foundation for Medium Expansion Potential Exterior
Footing and Foundation for Medium Expansion Potential Interior
Footing respectively
Slab On Grade
If slab on grade construction is to be used it should be designed
for low to moderately expansive soils For construction details in
these conditions refer to Plates C 1 through C 4
The subgrade soil should be compacted wet of its optimum moisture
content to a minimum of 90 of its dry density A 6 inch layer of
base course should be provided above the subgrade and compacted to
95 of its maximum dry density Commercially produced quarry waste
or other similar granular material may be used
Palehua Area H 8
To protect against moisture damage of the flooring material a
polyethylene moisture barrier should be placed just below the
concrete slab
Roadway Pavement
Representative on site soil samples were brought back to our labo

moderately high CBR value and low expansion potential for the upper
reddish brown clayey silt soil For the grayish brown to brown
silty clay found in the higher elevations of the site a low CBR
value and moderate expansion was recorded The test results are
summarized in Table 1 Summary of Laboratory Test Results

APPENDIX A
FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by
drilling 6 borings utilizing truck mounted drilling equipment
and 9 test pits dug by a backhoe The borings were terminated
at 4 0 to 13 0 feet below the existing ground surface The
test pits were dug down to a depth of 5 5 to 11 5 feet below
the existing ground elevation The locations of the borings
and test pits are shown on the enclosed Site Plan Plate 1
During the subsurface exploration two representative
samples of the surface or near surface soils were taken at
two boring locations for use in the evaluation of their charac
teristics as a source of borrow fill
The soils were classified by visual and textural examination
in the field by our engineer who monitored the drilling and test
pit digging operattons The classifications were verified by
visual inspection and testing tn the laboratory All soils were
classified in accordance with the Unified Soil Classification
System A graphic presentation of the soils is presented on
the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 7 Test pit findings are
recorded in Test Pit Logs Plate A 8
In the boring samples were obtained by driving either a
standard penetration or a 2 4 inch I D split barrel sampler with
a 140 pound hammer free falling a distance of 30 inches The blow
counts to drive the sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the
logs of borings at the appropriate sample depth
