Our field exploration generally encountered a surface fill layer ranging from about
1 to 6 feet thick The fill layer consisted of medium dense to very dense sandy gravel
hard sandy clay and stiff to hard clayey silt The fill layer was underlain by soft to hard
basalt rock formation with clinker layers extending to about 52 feet the maximum depth
explored In four of the borings drilled in Part A of the project residual soils and saprolite
extending to 4 5 to 9 feet deep was encountered below the surface fill layer In addition
volcanic tuff and cemented cinder deposit from about 1 to 2 5 feet thick was encountered
within the basalt rock formation in four of the borings We did not encounter groundwater
in the borings drilled at the time of our field exploration
Site grading generally consists of cuts up to about 35 feet in depth and fills up to
about 15 feet in height The large cut area is located at the excavation for the
Lahainaluna Road Grade Separation Structure The location of the large fill area is along

In general we anticipate the excavations will mainly encounter fill residual soil
saprolite clinker and basalt rock with the majority of the excavation encountering the
underlying basalt rock formation The excavated basalt rock material may be used as a
source of general fill and backfill materials provided that the material contains no rock
fragments greater than 6 inches and contains less than 30 percent passing the No 200
sieve Select granular fill materials should be used to a depth of 2 feet from finished
subgrade Select granular fill materials should be well graded from coarse to fine with
particles no larger than 3 inches in largest dimension have a laboratory CBR value of 20
or more with a maximum swell value of 1 percent or less and contains between 10 and
30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve
Fill slopes should be designed with a maximum inclination of two horizontal to
one vertical 2H 1V or flatter Cut slopes in the basalt rock formation may be designed
with inclinations of up to 1H 1V or flatter
The Lahainaluna Road Grade Separation Structure will consist of abutment
structures supported by spread footings bearing on hard basalt rock formation The
design of the abutment structures will take advantage of the high bearing capacity and low
lateral earth pressures of the underlying basalt rock formation

2 1 Regional Geology
The Island of Maui was built by two major volcanoes the older West Maui
Tertiary Epoch and the more recent East Maui also known as Haleakala Pleistocene
Epoch The Isthmus of Maui is a narrow gently sloping plain located between these
two volcanoes The project site is located on the lower western slopes of the older West
Maui Volcano
The older West Maui Volcano is generally comprised of three lava flows The
lower unit was formed during the Pliocene to early Pleistocene Epoch and is identified
as the Wailuku Volcanic Series The lavas of the Wailuku Volcanic Series generally
consist of thinly bedded a a and pahoehoe lavas Overlying this unit are lavas of the
Honolua Volcanic Series late Pleistocene Epoch that consist of alkalic lavas and stiff
trachyte
Volcanic inactivity occurred during the Middle Pleistocene Epoch resulting in
weathering of the lavas carving of canyons and deposition of materials During the late
Pleistocene Epoch renewal of volcanic activity occurred near the southern coast with
lava flows of the Lahaina Volcanic Series The lavas of the Lahaina Volcanic Series
generally consist of olivine rich basalt and nepheline basanite

Our field exploration generally encountered surface fill residual soils and
saprolite underlain by soft to hard basalt rock formation with clinker layers extending to

Based on the results of the field exploration we believe that the
Lahainaluna Road Grade Separation structure may be supported on_ shallow
foundations bearing on the basalt rock formation Based on our experience in the area

Type Soil Profile A seismic coefficient of 1 0 may be used for seismic analysis for the
bridges and other structures

Prior to filling the existing ground exposing soils should be scarified to a depth of
about 8 inches where possible moisture conditioned to above the optimum
moisture and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Where
shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of the subgrade we recommend
thoroughly moistening the soil to close the cracks or preparing the subgrade again
Saturation and subsequent yielding of the exposed subgrade due to inclement
weather and poor drainage may require over excavation of the soft areas and
replacement with well compacted fills The excavated soft soils may be used as fill
provided that they meet fill material requirements Relative compaction refers to the
in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density
of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557
Where scarification of the subgrade is not possible especially subgrades exposing
basalt formation we recommend proof rolling the subgrades with a large vibratory
drum roller minimum 15 tons static weight for a minimum of eight passes to help
detect and collapse near surface cavities and or voids The vibratory drum roller
should be operated at a speed of about 300 feet per minute Yielding areas loose
areas or cavities disclosed during the clearing and proof rolling operations should
be over excavated and backfilled with compacted fill materials The depth of
over excavation should extend until dense underlying materials are exposed and
should be evaluated by our field representative

3 5 Anchored Wall System
In general the anchored wall system consists of a concrete wall stem and footing
with permanent ground anchors drilled into the underlying basalt rock formation The
advantage of this retaining wall system is that it reduces the required footing width
reduces the amount of excavation required for wall construction and shortens the
duration of wall construction compared to conventional cantilever and drilled shaft
retaining walls
In general the permanent ground anchor system is constructed by drilling a
borehole installing a steel tendon or reinforcing bar into the borehole and grouting the
borehole The basic components of a grouted permanent ground anchor consist of the
anchorage and the bonded and unbonded lengths of the steel tendon or reinforcing bar
In general the unbonded length of the ground anchor is the portion of the steel tendon
or reinforcing bar that does not have direct contact with the grout allowing the tendon
or reinforcing bar to elongate elastically transferring the resisting force from the
structure to the bonded length The bonded length of the ground anchor is the portion of
the steel tendon or reinforcing bar that is directly bonded to the grout allowing it to
transfer the applied load into the ground We recommend that the borehole have a
minimum diameter of 5 inches
In general the permanent ground anchors would derive support principally from
the bond between the grout and the basalt rock formation anticipated at the project site
The bond stress which is the pullout resistance per unit area of the grout soil interface
contact is variable depending on the type of ground and grout the overburden stress
and the construction procedures used in installing the permanent ground anchors For a
rough estimate an average bond stress of 8 kips per square foot ksf may be used to
size the ground anchors embedded in the basalt formation The bond stress used in the
design will need to be confirmed during construction

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions were explored by drilling and sampling 35 borings
designated as Boring Nos 101 through 135 advanced to maximum depths of about
27 to 101 5 feet below the existing ground surface The borings were drilled using a
truck mounted drill rig equipped with continuous flight auger and coring tools The
approximate boring locations are shown on the Site Plans Plates 3 1 through 3 4
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer or geologist who monitored the drilling
operations on a near continuous basis Soils were classified in general conformance
with the Unified Scil Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic
representations of the materials encountered are presented on the Logs of Borings
Plates A 1 through A 35
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained from the borings drilled in
general accordance with ASTM D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving
a 3 inch OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches In
addition some samples were obtained from the borings drilled in general accordance
with ASTM D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a
2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same hammer and drop The blow
counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an 18 inch drive
are shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate
sample depths
Core samples of rock materials encountered at the site were obtained using
diamond core drilling techniques in general accordance with ASTM D 2113 Diamond
Core Drilling for Site Investigation Core drilling is a rotary drilling method that uses a
hollow bit to cut into the rock formation The rock material left in the hollow core of the
bit is mechanically recovered for examination and description
Recovery REC is used as a subjective guide to the interpretation of the relative
quality of rock masses Recovery is defined as the actual length of material recovered
from a coring attempt versus the length of the core attempt For example if 3 7 feet of
material is recovered from a 5 0 foot core run the recovery would be 74 percent and
would be shown on the Logs of Borings as REC 74

The Rock Quality Designation RQD is also a subjective guide to the relative
quality of rock masses RQD is defined as the percentage of the core run that is sound
material in excess of 4 inches in length without discontinuities discounting
drilling induced fractures or breaks If 2 5 feet of sound material is recovered from a
5 0 foot core run the RQD would be 50 percent and would be shown on the Logs of
Borings as RQD 50 Generally the following is used to describe the relative quality of
the rock based on the Practical Handbook of Physical Properties of Rocks and

The following terms describe the chemical weathering of a rock

Extremely Weathered Saprolite Mineral residue completely decomposed to soil but fabric and structure preserved

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture Content ASTM D 2216 and Unit Weight ASTM D 2937
determinations were performed on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification
and evaluation of soil properties The test results are presented on the Logs of Borings
at the appropriate sample depths
One Atterberg Limits test ASTM D 4318 was performed on a selected soil
sample to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits and to aid in soil classification The test
results are summarized on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depth
Graphic presentation of the test results is provided on Plate B 1
One Direct Shear test ASTM D 3080 was performed on a selected soil sample
to evaluate the shear strength characteristics Direct shear test results are presented on
Plate B 2
Three Unconsolidated Undrained Triaxial Compression tests ASTM D 2850
were performed on selected in situ soil samples to evaluate the undrained shear
strengths of the soils The approximate in situ effective overburden pressures were
used as the applied confining pressures for the relatively undisturbed soil samples
The test results and the stress strain curves are presented on Plates B 3 through B 5
Thirty Seven Unconfined Compression tests ASTM D 2938 were performed on
rock core samples to evaluate the unconfined compressive strength of the underlying
boulder and rock formation The test results are presented on the Logs of Borings at the
appropriate sample depth
Ten laboratory California Bearing Ratio tests ASTM D 1883 were performed on
bulk samples of the near surface soils to evaluate the pavement support characteristics
of the soils The samples were remolded to near the optimum moisture content The
test results are presented on Plates B 6 through B 15
