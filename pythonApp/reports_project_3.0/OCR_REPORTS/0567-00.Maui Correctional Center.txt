On August 1 1975 I met with and
Ted Suzuki of Sato and Kuniyoshi Inc Structural Engineers
to review the proposed foundation system for the above
captioned project This letter summarizes the supplemental
foundation recommendations developed during this meeting
In our previous report we recommended that the foundations
be designed for maximum allowable contact bearing pressures
of 6 000 pounds per square foot and that the foundations
should penetrate the upper 3 foot layer of loose and medium
dense sand and bear upon the lower medium to dense sand
Soils and Foundation Investigation Proposed Maui Intake Center
Community Correctional Center Waiale Drive
W 2 August 4 1975
The presently proposed foundation system includes numerous
small lightly loaded interior foundations in addition to the
more heavily loaded columns and exterior walls These
foundations will require numerous excavations to penetrate
the upper three foot layer of loose sand
To avoid this excavation problem the upper loose sand should
be compacted and the foundations supported directly upon the
compacted sand and designed for maximum bearing pressures of
2 000 pounds per square foot
The upper soils can be removed and replaced with on site
soils placed in six inch maximum lifts and compacted to 95
percent of the maximum density as established by ASTM Test
1557
In lieu of the undercutting and backfilling the sand can be
compacted in place with ten passes of a large vibratory com
pactor capable of delivering a minimum of 30 000 pounds of
force such as a Hyster 610A or equivalent The water con
tent of the soils to be compacted should be adjusted to near
optimum moisture content The compaction operations should
be inspected by a soils engineer to assure uniform coverage
and adequate compaction results
To minimize differential settlements the foundations includ
ing the heavily loaded columns near the center of the struc
ture should be designed for maximum contact bearing pressures
of 2 000 pounds per square foot The foundations for the
heavy column loads should extend to the surface of the dense
materials These are the only foundations that require
penetration through the upper compacted soils
The interior lightly loaded foundations can be founded upon
thicken slabs or floor slabs designed for the additional
loading The interior heavily loaded foundations designed
for 2 000 pounds per square foot and extending to bear upon
the lower denser materials can be constructed as an integral
part of the floor slabs
Within the proposed east wing the western wall will bear at
approximately the existing grade while the eastern wall will
be bearing upon approximately four feet of compacted fill
Similar foundation bearing conditions should be provided to
prevent distresses due to differential settlements
W 3 August 4 1975
This can be accomplished by undercutting the building area
and a five foot perimeter strip to elevation 240 and back
filling with compacted fill to the proposed foundation
bearing level The foundations for the east wing would
therefore be bearing uniformly upon approximately four feet
of compacted fill
The on site soils can be utilized for fill but may require
some crushing or breaking to eliminate large size fragments
This crushing operation can generally be accomplished with
the earth moving equipment
If you have any questions concerning our findings of conclu
sions or need additional clarification of our foundation
recommendations please do not hesitate to contact us
Respectfully submitted

SUMMARY OF RECOMMENDATIONS
The site is generally underlain by cemented sand which will
provide adequate support for spread and line foundations
The foundations should be designed for maximum allowable
contact bearing pressures of 6 000 pounds per square foot
The foundations should penetrate the upper layer of loose
and medium dense sand and bear upon the medium to dense
sand encountered at a depth of approximately three feet
below the existing ground surfaces
Retaining walls should be designed for an equivalent fluid
pressure of 30 pounds per square foot and a frictional
factor of 0 55 if the on site soils are used for backfill
INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this investigation was to define subsurface
conditions in the proposed construction area and to provide
recommendations for the design and construction of the founda
tions to support the proposed structures and for the ancilliary
earthwork
W Page 1975
Site Description
The site for the proposed Maui Intake Service Center is adja
cent to the existing Community Correction Center on Waiale
Drive approximately 1 2 mile south of the business section of
Wailuku Maui The proposed building site includes portions
of the existing parking lot the Administration Building the
Gift Shop and the mound area south of the parking lot
A large portion of the site is essentially level at approximately
elevation 246 A circular mound approximately 100 foot in dia
meter and 10 feet high is located at the southeastern corner of
the proposed structure At the rear of the existing wood frame
Administration Building the ground surface drops to approxi
mately elevation 234 in two terraces separated by stone retain
ing walls A nearly vertical slope is also located southeast
of the proposed building area in the vicinity of the existing
garage
The ground surface varies between elevation 234 at the western
end of the proposed building and elevation 254 at the crest of
the mound
Several areas of cemented dune sand are exposed around the base
of the circular mound Here vertical cuts up to six feet are
present without indications of instability or ravelling
W Page 1975
It is our understanding that the proposed development will con
sist of a one story concrete masonry building with bearing walls
and a concrete roof The anticipated first floor elevation of
244 5 will necessitate cuts of 2 to 10 feet and shallow fills
of less than 10 feet for the proposed slopes in the vicinity of
the present retaining walls and vertical cuts A series of low
retaining walls will also be utilized at the base of the pro
posed slopes
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The site is situated in an area of consolidated fine grain cross
bedded dunes composed of calcareous sand blown inland from ancient
beaches during the Pleistocene Epoch Except for surface distur
bances the dune sands are generally lightly cemented and of uni
form grain size
Subsurface Explorations
Subsurface conditions at the site were explored utilizing four
test borings drilled at the locations shown on the enclosed Site
Plan Plate 1 A detailed description of the field exploration
methods and accompanying laboratory testing is given in the
Appendix along with the boring logs and laboratory test results
W O 567 00 Page 1975
Soil Conditions
The investigation disclosed that the site is underlain to the
limit of the explorations by lightly cemented fine grain sand
The upper three feet of the sand is generally loose to medium
dense The lower portion varied from medium dense to very
dense No groundwater was encountered during the subsurface
explorations
Visual observations of the ground surface and the vertical cuts
in the vicinity of the circular mound indicates that the mound
is composed of similar cemented sands Some cracks and void
areas were noted along with surface evidence of graves or
other shallow excavations
DISCUSSIONS RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The subsurface explorations and laboratory testing disclosed
that the site is underlain to the limit of the explorations
by a relatively dense sand which will provide excellent
support for the proposed structures Except for the surface
materials which are generally loose the sand has high densi
ties and high shear strengths
W Page 1975
Foundations
The foundations should by pass the loose surface sand and
bear upon the underlying lightly cemented dune sand encountered
at a depth of three feet below the existing ground surfaces
Foundations at this level can be désigned for maximum allowable contact
bearing pressures of 6 000 pounds per square foot without detri
mental settlements The foundations should have a minimum width of
18 inches For the above loadings it is estimated that maxi
mum and differential settlements would not exceed 1 4 inch
The allowable bearing can be increased by 1 3 when considering
seismic and wind loadings
Floor Slabs
Slab on grade construction can be readily utilized at the site
All disturbed subgrades should be compacted to amminimum of 90
per cent of the maximum density to assure uniform support Due
to the granular nature of the on site soils it is not anticipated
that capillary fills will be required All slabs with moisture
sensitive floor coverings should be protected by plastic vapor
barriers
Retaining Walls
It is recommended that the retaining walls be designed for an equi
valent fluid pressure of 30 pounds per cubic foot and a frictional
factor of 0 55 if the on site soils are used as backfill The
W Page 1975
backfill should be compacted to between 90 and 95 per cent of
the maximum dry density established by American Society for
Testing Materials ASTM Test Designatéon D 1557 727
The proposed site grading generally entails shallow cuts and
fills to obtain the proposed finish grades
Prior to placement of any fills all organic soil old founda
tions or other deleterious materials should be removed and the
upper six inches of the existing ground surface scarified The
scarified surface should be brought to near optimum moisture
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 per cent of the maxi
mum dénsity Additional fills should be placed in horizontal
lifts and compacted to 90 per cent of the maximum density The
fill slopes should be constructed no steeper than one and one half
horizontal to one vertical 1 to 1
Cuts within the natural cemented sand can be made at essentially
vertical slopes providing no structures or structural loadings
are placed at the top of the slope Where structural loads will
be placed at the top of the slope it is recommended that cuts
be made no steeper than 1 horizontal to 1 vertical 1 to 1
It is anticipated that the cemented sand can be excavated with
standard earth moving equipment and blasting or jack hammering
will not be required for the general excavations Some limited
W Page 1975
areas of hard materials may be encountered and could require
special excavations methods in confined areas such as founda
tion or utility trenches
Generally the foundations will be bearing approximately three
feet below the existing ground surface In the vicinity of the
slopes along the eastern portion of the proposed site it will
be necessary to step the foundations so that all foundations will
be bearing upon undisturbed cemented sand This may require ex
cavations in excess of three feet in the vicinity of the exist
ing stone retaining walls It appears that the retaining walls
were backfilled with on site granular soil Due to the limited
access to these areas the densities and thicknessess of the back
fill could not be verified during the subsurface explorations
The actual bearing levels in this area must therefore be esta
blished during construction
The on site soil can be utilized for fill but may require some
crushing or breaking to eliminate large size fragments This
crushing operation can generally be accomplished with the earth
moving equipment
Paving
Based upon the classification of the on site soils and our pre
vious experience in this area the following pavement sections
should be considered in the preliminary design
W Page 1975
2 in Asphaltic Concrete
6 in Base Course
8 in Total Pavement Thickness
In areas subject to frequent truck traffic the asphalt concrete
should be increased to a thickness of 2 inches The base
course should be crushed rock or other granular materials with
a CBR greater than 85
The final pavement section should be based upon the actual sub
grade CBR measured during construction
Construction Monitoring Plan Review
It is recommended that the proposed foundation design be reviewed
by our office prior to finalization to verify that the design is
in accordance with our recommendations
Earthwork utilized for structural support retaining wall back
fill or earth slopes should be inspected to assure that the recom
mended degree of compaction is consistently achieved
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon such
inspections

FIELD EXPLORATIONS
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drill
ing four borings utilizing truck mounted equipment The
borings extended to a depth of 20 feet below the existing
ground surface The locations of the borings are shown on the
enclosed Site Plan Plate l
The soils were classified by visual and textural examination
in the field by our engineer who continuously supervised the
drilling operations The classifications were verified by
visual inspection and testing in the laboratory All soils
were classified in accordance with the Unified Soil Classifi
cation System A graphic representation of the soils encoun
tered is presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 4
Soil samples were obtained by driving either a 2 4 I D or a
standard penetration sampler with a 140 pound hammer free
falling a distance of 30 inches The blow counts to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the log of borings at
the appropriate sample depth
