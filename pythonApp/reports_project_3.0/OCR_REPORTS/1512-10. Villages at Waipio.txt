From May 23 1985 to we inspected the earthwork
and grading operations at the above referenced site on a full time basis
and from to the present our inspections were on an
intermittent basis It is our opinion that as of this date the above
referenced project except for Road L was constructed to final grade
and compacted adequately The density test results for the in place
fills are indicated in the attached Density Test Summary A site plan of
the subdivision is also presented on Plate l
This final report summarizes our inspection and compaction tests
performed at the above lots

The grading operations at the above referenced project consisted of
cuts and fills up to 7 feet thick The fill materials used conisted of
on site reddish brown clayey silt
During the fill placement operations periodic field density tests
were done in accordance with the American Society for Testing and
Material ASTM Test Designation D 1556 Sand Cone Method Based on the
field observations and compaction tests we believe that the fill areas
have been compacted adequately
The pavement section along most of Road L has not been installed
yet It is an understanding that this work will be completed later
During the grading period samples of the on site soils were tested
prior to being used in the fill The maximum soil density and optimum
moisture content were established in our laboratory in accordance with

Recommendations
During the final grading operations one inch ring swell tests
were performed on the finish grade soils at various locations The
ring swell test results are included in the attached One Inch Ring
Swell Test Summary and indicate that the soils generally have low
expansion potential Based on the test results the following are our
recommendations
Foundations
For the proposed multi family wooden structures spread footing
foundations or slab on grade may be used Slabs should be setback from
the tops of the slopes a minimum distance of 5 feet Post footings
located close to the tops of slopes should be embedded a sufficient depth
to obtain a minimum horizontal set back distance of 5 feet from the slope
face Post footings away from the tops of slopes should be placed a
minimum of 12 inches below the finish grade
Surface water should be diverted away fron building foundations
Slab on grade should be placed over a 4 inch layer of cushion
material such as No 3 fine gravel The thickened edge around the
slab on grade should extend a minimum of 12 inches below the adjacent
ground surface

To protect against moisture damage of the flooring material a
polyethylene moisture barrier should be considered
The slab subgrade soils should be kept moist and not be allowed to
dry out prior to concrete placement
Soil Engineering Inspection Services
We recommend that the soils engineer should test and approve all
additional imported fill material inspect the backfill placement and
recompaction and footing excavations prior to concrete placement
Site Regrading
Subsequent to completion of lot grading utility trenches within
the lot pad should be properly backfilled and compacted under the
observation of a soils engineer
Without our knowledge and written approval this office does not
assume any responsibility for any alterations made to slopes or pads on
the subject lots subsequent to the issuance of this report
