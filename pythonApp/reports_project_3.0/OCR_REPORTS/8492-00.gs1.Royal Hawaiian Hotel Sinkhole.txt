This technical memorandum presents our geotechnical recommendations for the 
repair of the sinkhole for the Royal Hawaiian Resort project in Honolulu on the Island of 
Oahu, Hawaii. The geotechnical recommendations presented herein are based on 
observations made during our site visits to the project site.  
Project Considerations 
The project site is located at the Royal Hawaiian Resort in Waikiki on the Island of 
Oahu, Hawaii. We understand that a sinkhole has formed behind an existing retaining wall 
at the Mailani Tower. We understand that the sinkhole may have been caused by a 
leaking irrigation line below the retaining wall. In addition, we understand that it is desired 
to develop the probable cause of the sinkhole and recommend remedial measures. 
Site Visits Observations 
A site visit was made on May 20, 2022 to observe the excavation made on the 
upper retaining wall area. An excavation about 6 feet in depth was exposed adjacent to an 
existing retaining wall. Exposed pipes were observed in the excavation. One of the pipes 
extended down to the bottom of the existing wall footing and appeared to run below the 
footing. We understand that this pipe is made of copper and is used for irrigation. A void 
was noted below the footing in the vicinity of the copper pipe. The extent of the void was 
not known. Based on discussions with the maintenance personnel, we understand that 
there has been a loss of water which could potentially be from the copper irrigation pipe. 
Maintenance personnel indicated that they saw water gushing to the surface at 
two locations near the wall. In addition, a large void was noted about 2 feet below the 
existing ground surface during the excavation. Photographs of the upper wall excavation 
are presented on Plate 1. It was recommended that the lower footing area of the existing 
wall be excavated to observe the extent of the voids below the footing. 
A second site visit was made on June 1, 2022 to observe the excavation made at 
the lower retaining wall area. An excavation about 4 to 5 feet deep was made to expose 
the irrigation line below the existing wall footing. At the time of our site visit, we understood

Potential Cause of the Sinkhole and Recommended Remedial Measures 
Based on our observations and discussions with maintenance personnel, we 
believe that the potential cause of the sinkhole is due to the leakage of the existing copper 
irrigation pipe that runs below the wall footing. 
We understand that a new irrigation line has been installed. Therefore, we 
recommend that the old irrigation pipe that extends below the wall footing be removed. 
Loose/soft materials at the bottom of the excavation pits and opening below the footing 
should be removed down to stiff/dense material. The roots and organic material in the 
opening below the footing should also be removed. 
Due to the existing piping in the excavation pits and narrow opening below the wall 
footing, we recommend that the excavation pits and narrow openings below the wall 
footing be backfilled with Controlled Low Strength Material (CLSM) or lean concrete with 
high slump (7 to 9 inches) up to at least above the bottom of the existing wall footing. After 
the CLSM and lean concrete sets, the excavated silty/clayey sandy soils may be used as 
backfill material. The backfill soil material should be placed in lifts and compacted to a 
stiff/dense condition. Should backfilling with the excavated soil is not desirable, the CLSM 
or lean concrete can be placed up to the finished grade.
