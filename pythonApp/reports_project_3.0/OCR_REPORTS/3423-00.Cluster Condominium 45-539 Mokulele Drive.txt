REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lavas from
two shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated
to be of the middle to late Pliocene in age and Koolau is estimated to be of the late
Pliocene to early Pleistocene in age After a long period of volcanic inactivity during
which erosion incised deep valleys into the Koolau shield volcanic activity returned with
a series of lava flows followed by cinder and tuff cone formations These series are
referred to as the Honolulu Volcanic Series
During the Pleistocene Epoch Ice Age sea levels fluctuated in response to the
cycles of continental glaciation As the glaciers grew and advanced less water was
available to fill the ocean basins such that sea levels fell below the present stands of
the sea When the glaciers melted and receded an excess of water became available
such that the sea levels rose to above the present sea
The processes of erosion and deposition were affected by these glacioeustatic
sea level fluctuations When the sea level was low the erosional base level was
correspondingly lower and valleys were carved to depths below the present sea level
When the sea level was high the erosional base level was raised such that sediments
accumulated at higher elevations
In the mountainous regions of Hawaii the erosional processes are dominated by
detachment of soil and rock masses from the valley walls and are transported
downslope toward the axis of a valley primarily by gravity as colluvium Once these
materials reach the stream in the central portion of a valley alluvial processes become
dominant and the sediments are transported and deposited as alluvium In general
stream flows in Hawaii are intermittent and flashy such that the stream flows transmit
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3423 00 Page 6
large volumes of water for very short durations Because of this transport of sediments
is intermittent and the bulk of the stream s hydraulic load consists of a poorly sorted
mixture of boulders cobbles gravel sands and fines When the erosional base levels
change these sediment loads are left as deposits
When deposits are left in place for long periods of time chemical processes
begin to alter the materials simultaneously causing a breakdown or weathering of the
material Chemical processes also cause induration or cementation of the coarse
grained portion of the sediment into a poorly consolidated sedimentary rock or
conglomerate Simultaneously erosion continues in the areas above the valley floors
and upstream in headwaters This continued erosion generates material which is
transported downslope covering the older alluvial deposits Depending on the local
base level and rate of transport these newer sediments are generally transient in terms
of geologic time In addition their consistency and density are generally less than
those of the older partially consolidated deposits

Natural Hillside Stability
Based on our field observations the east side of the project site is bounded by
a near vertical slope on the order of about 30 to 40 feet high A detailed
reconnaissance could not be conducted on the existing slope conditions due to the
thick vegetation cover on the slope face and limited accessibility at the project site
However based on our limited field observations made at the site it appears that signs
of erosion landslides and slumps were not evident on the slope face Based on these
observations the existing steep hillside appears to be relatively stable however the
steep slopes should be protected against erosion to reduce the potential for localized
slope instability and sloughing
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3423 00 Page 18
It should be noted that there is a potential for slope instability due to the steep
slopes at the site The potential for slope instability at the site is hard to predict and
is dependent on weather conditions local soil variabilities and hydrogeologic settings
Therefore although our current reconnaissance of the hillside area above the
development site did not disclose obvious signs of potential instability there exists a
potential for a debris flow to occur in the event of very intense and or prolonged periods
of rainfall This is an inherent risk associated with any hillside development
In light of the above there are some precautions which could help to reduce the
risk of damage to the proposed subdivision development It is our opinion that the
existing steep hillside with its established vegetation should not be disturbed during the
future site grading and house construction work However periodic maintenance may
be required for large trees that are leaning outward from the slope face and hanging
over the project site There is always a potential that these trees may pull the soil
loose with its root system due to the gravitational pull under its own weight

The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling
seven borings to depths ranging from about 8 to 18 feet below the existing ground
surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings

augers

Soil Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representation of the
materials encountered are presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 7

Plate B 3

support characteristics of the soils The test results are presented on Plate B 2

typical clayey silt soils The results are summarized on Plate B 2
