PROJECT DESCRIPTION
In general the FY11 MILCON P 858 BEQ Parking Structure project site is located
east of Harris Avenue near the intersection with Mokapu Drive at Kaneohe Bay on the
Island of Oahu Hawaii The property is approximately rectangular in shape and consisted
of a large parking lot located in the area of a former landfill The layout of the new parking
structure is shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
Based on the information provided we understand the project consists of a 4 level
parking structure with a finished floor elevation of about 12 8 feet Mean Sea Level MSL
The new building structure is supported on a deep foundation system consisting of 18 inch
diameter augered cast in place piles with an allowable compressive load capacity of up to
300 kips 150 tons per pile The project consisted of 5 indicator piles test piles and
103 production piles for a total of 108 ACIP piles for the foundation support of the parking
structure
Hawaiian Dredging Construction Company Inc HDCC was the general contractor
for the project and Hawaiian Dredging Construction Company
Associates Inc Joint Venture HDCC CJA was the ACIP pile installation subcontractor
ACIP PILE LOAD TEST PROGRAM
In order to evaluate the adequacy of the design length of the ACIP pile foundation
a load test pile program was implemented The load test pile program included
the installation and observation of two reaction piles and one instrumented load test
pile LT A static pile load test was conducted on the instrumented ACIP pile LT on
March 7 and 8 2013 The pile load test was conducted on an 18 inch diameter ACIP pile
with a length of approximately 65 3 feet relative to the existing ground surface The
identification of the load test and reaction piles is shown on the ACIP Pile Location Plan
Plate 2
The static pile load test results are summarized in the report entitled ACIP Pile
Load Test Report FY11 MILCON P 858 Bachelor Enlisted Quarters Parking Structure
Marine Corps Base Hawaii Kaneohe Bay Oahu Hawaii dated Based on
our field observations and the results of the static pile load test we confirmed the design
embedment lengths of approximately 50 and 60 feet long below the bottom of pile caps to
achieve the design compressive load capacity of 200 and 300 kips respectively per ACIP
pile with a factor of safety of at least 2 0

An indicator pile also known as a probe pile or test pile program which consisted
of installing 5 ACIP piles was conducted for the project on February 27 and 28 2013 The
indicator piles were selected from the future production pile locations and are shown on
the ACIP Pile Location Plan Plate 2 The purpose of the indicator pile program was to

PRODUCTION ACIP PILE INSTALLATION
The production ACIP pile installation program consisting of installing 103 augered
cast in place piles 18 inch diameter was conducted from March 13 through 29 and on
under the near continuous full time observations by a Geolabs field
representative HDCC CJA used the same drilling grouting equipment utilized for the
indicator or test pile program to install the production piles Automatic monitoring
equipment was mounted on the drilling pumping equipment with the intent of providing
auger depth grout flow rate grout pressure etc on a near continuous basis
During the pile installation operation logs were maintained for each ACIP pile
which included information pertaining to pile number date installed depth of pile grout
volume used grout return depth etc The identifications of the production ACIP piles
installed are shown on the ACIP Pile Location Plan Plate 3 The production ACIP pile
installation summary is presented by pile number on the attached Plates 3 1 through 3 4
Based on our observation of the equipment performance and subsurface conditions
encountered it is our professional opinion that the production ACIP piles have been
installed to sufficient depths and or resistance to provide the design load capacity of
200 and 300 kips respectively per pile with a safety factor of 2 0
LIMITATIONS

occurring simultaneously on a construction site even when our representatives are on the
project site on a full time basis Our observation and consultation services were performed

warranty is expressed or implied Provision of construction monitoring by an engineer is
not insurance nor does it guarantee construction of any type The contractor shall retain
