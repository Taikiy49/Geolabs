DRILLED SHAFT INSTALLATION
Five production shafts designated as BOL 1 through BOL 5 were installed from
August 21 through at the locations shown on the Drilled Shaft Location
Plan Plate 2 The as built diameter and lengths of the production drilled shafts installed for
the project are summarized on the Drilled Shaft Installation Summary Plate 3 of this letter
report A Geolabs representative observed the drilled shaft excavations reinforcing cage
placements and concrete operations during the installation of the production drilled shafts
The exception was the drilled shaft at Bollard 2 which was excavated prior to our
involvement on the project
In general the production drilled shafts were excavated using a SoilMec R 625
rotary drill rig equipped with an 81 inch diameter auger 72 inch diameter auger 72 inch
diameter digging bucket and 60 inch clean out bucket Temporary steel casings with an
outside diameter of 77 inches were installed to depths of about 15 to 31 feet below the
existing ground surface in the production drilled shafts After excavation of the production
shafts to their designated tip elevations the reinforcing cages were lowered into the drilled
holes to the appropriate elevations with the use of a crane
Due to the presence of groundwater within the drilled shaft holes concrete was
placed in the drilled holes using the tremie method After the reinforcing cage was installed
in the drilled hole concrete was placed through a 4 inch diameter steel tremie pipe starting
near the bottom of the hole During concrete placement we observed that at least 5 feet of
concrete head was maintained above the bottom of the tremie pipe
Concrete with a slump ranging from about 7 to 9 inches was used for the drilled
shafts Construction Engineering Labs monitored the concrete used for the drilled shafts
including the performance of slump tests and compression tests
As with most foundation installation projects some problems did arise during
construction After placing concrete for Bollard 5 and removal of the temporary casing it
was observed that the steel rebar cage had dropped approximately 32 inches below the
plan elevation HDCC attempted to raise the cage using a crane but was unsuccessful
