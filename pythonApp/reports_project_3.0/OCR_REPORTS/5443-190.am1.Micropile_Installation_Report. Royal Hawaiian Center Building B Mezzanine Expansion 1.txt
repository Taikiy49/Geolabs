As requested Geolabs Inc provided geotechnical engineering observation and
consultation services during the production micropile foundation installation work for the
above project This letter report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations
during the installation of production micropile foundations for the new Building B Room
Expansion for the Royal Hawaiian Center project in Waikiki on the Island of Oahu Hawaii
The project location and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
Geotechnical recommendations for the design of micropile foundations were presented in
our report dated as referenced above
This letter report presents our field observations and the results of load testing
performed for the production micropiles and our findings and recommendations for the
micropile foundation for the project
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located at Building B of the Royal Hawaiian Center in Waikiki
on the island of Oahu Hawaii Based on the contract documents provided we
understand that the two column footings for the new expansion are to be supported by
5 5 inch diameter micropiles with a design load capacity of 30 kips A layout of the
existing floor plan and proposed foundations are presented on the Micropile Location
Plan Plate 2

Due to the potential for excessive differential settlement between the extension
supported on a pile foundation and on spread footings anticipated at the project site we
recommended that the new foundations be supported on a deep foundation system
consisting of micropiles
MICROPILE INSTALLATION AND TESTING
Geotechnical recommendations for the micropile foundations were presented in our
referenced geotechnical engineering exploration report dated The
purpose of our geotechnical engineering observation and consultation services was to
observe the micropile foundation installation and testing for general conformance with our
recommendations
Based on the anticipated subsurface conditions at the site the micropiles were
specified to extend through the loose lagoonal deposits and derive load bearing support
from the underlying medium dense to dense coralline deposits
Kelikai Inc installed a total of two production micropiles from April 23 through
26 2021 at the approximate locations shown on the Micropile Location Plan Plate 2 The
micropile locations were provided by Kelikai Inc Details of the micropile installation are
presented in the Micropile Records in Appendix A
In general the micropiles were installed using a drill rig equipped
casing to the specified depth of the micropile to create a drilled hole of at least 5 5 inches
in diameter The steel casing was advanced to the specified depth to facilitate the
micropile installation
After the drilled holes were extended to the specified depth and cleaned out the
micropiles were initially grouted Grout was batched on site utilizing a colloidal high shear
and high speed mixer equipped with a grout pump The grout mix
consisted of one 94 pound sack of Portland Cement Type I and or II 87 pounds of
masonry sand and 5 gallons of water
The grout was pumped into the cased hole through a PVC tremie pipe starting from
the bottom of the cased hole After clean grout appeared at the top of the cased hole a
reinforcing bar with centralizers was placed in the cased hole
The temporary steel casing was removed one section at a time and regrouted as
necessary to maintain the grout level near the top of the casing This process continued
until the bottom of the steel casing reached the top of the bond zone The permanent
casing was then plunged to a final depth of approximately 3 feet into the bearing layer

proof test was conducted on one production micropile The proof test was conducted on

Micropile No 1 on May 5 2021 The micropile was loaded to about 39 9 kips 133 percent
of design load The maximum test load of about 39 9 kips was held for 10 minutes to
evaluate creep effects Subsequently the micropile was unloaded to zero load in about
four equal load decrements
At the maximum test load the creep movement during the 10 minute hold for
Micropile No 1 was about 0 025 inches which is less than 0 04 inches typically allowed
for the micropile foundation system At the maximum test load the vertical displacement of
Micropile No 1 was about 0 19 inches In general the proof tested production micropile
met the acceptance criteria specified in the project requirements The results of the proof
test are presented in Appendix B
FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field observations of the micropile installation and micropile proof load
tests performed by the subcontractor it is our professional opinion that the production
micropiles for the project have been installed to provide the design load capacity of 30 kips
with tolerable movements
