Preliminary Geotechnical Engineering Assessment
Slope Erosion Along Moanalua Loop
The Salvation Army Leeward Corps Community Center
Aiea Oahu Hawaii
Dear Lt Smith
This letter presents our preliminary findings for the stabilization of the steep rock
slope along Moanalua Loop fronting your facility
A site reconnaissance was made to evaluate the reported soil erosion and boulder
falls from the steep rock slope The topography along the property boundary fronting
Moanalua Loop consists of a near vertical slope comprised of moderately weathered
basalt flow The upper portions of the basalt flow consist of relatively hard fractured
blue rock while weathered clinker is exposed in some of the lower portions of the slope
Loose boulders were noted at the bottom of the slope These boulders have apparently
fallen from the slope face or areas immediately above the slope It was reported that
some of the slope material occasionally detaches from the slope particularly during heavy
rains
It appears that the boulder falls and slope detachments are due to two primary
causes First portions of the clinker layer have eroded undercutting the overlying hard
basalt As the upper basalt loses support from the clinker layer below tension cracks
develop in the rock formation and blocks of rock fall off the slope Second the rock
formation is highly fractured with large fractures an inch in size or more As the formation
weathers and the cracks widen instability may occur The falling boulders resulting from
these two mechanisms are hazards to pedestrians at the toe of the slope and to vehicles
on Moanalua Loop
One method to stabilize the slope would be to install a concrete facing over the
entire slope This would keep the rock slope material intact and prevent boulders from
falling The concrete facing would require a footing at the bottom of the slope for support
as shown on the attached sketch The topographic survey indicates that the footing
would conflict with the existing pavement in some areas and may create a driving hazard

A second alternative would be the installation of a rockfall protection netting over
the slope The netting consist of metal wire mesh anchored at the top of slope and draped
over the slope face to restrain loose rock blocks and allow them to be deposited at the toe
of the slope This method requires periodic maintenance to remove the loose materials
that will deposit at the bottom of the slope Additionally some of the very large rock
blocks on the slope face may be too large to be restrained by the netting
In light of the limitations of the alternatives and potential effect on the roadway we
recommend that you discuss the situation with the owners of Moanalua Loop in hopes of
working out remedial work that would benefit both parties
