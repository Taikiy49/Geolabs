This letter presents a summary of the anticipated areas with soft soils and the possible
implications on construction of the proposed Kaumualii Highway Widening project The
purpose of this letter is to identify locations along the proposed alignment that may be
underlain by soft soil deposits to point out anticipated concerns from a geotechnical
engineering point of view and to provide possible mitigation measures that may need to be
evaluated further during the design phase
Project Considerations
Based on the available preliminary plans provided by ParEn Inc on
we understand that the proposed Kaumualii Highway Widening project
will involve the construction of two bridges and about 45 box and pipe culverts along the
alignment We understand that the new Huleia and Lihue Mill Bridges will be constructed
adjacent to the existing bridges We also understand that Bridge 7G and 8A
will be demolished and replaced with concrete box culverts In general the existing pipe and
box culverts along the highway will be extended to accommodate the widened highway
and or removed and replaced with a larger sized culvert
Most existing culverts and bridges are located at stream and gully crossings Because
the culverts need to be located at the bottoms of these streams and gullies many existing
culverts are buried under a substantial amount of fill Therefore installation of new box and
pipe culverts will involve deep excavations and backfills In addition soft soil deposits
typically underlie areas near these streams and gullies Therefore considerations should be
given to the impact of constructing the box and pipe culverts on soft soils with relatively deep
fills

Concerns and Implications
As with most structures placed with a substantial amount of fill over soft soil
conditions the proposed new culvert structures and extensions will be placed over soft
soils with a substantial amount of fill Therefore several geotechnical considerations will
need to be addressed in the design and construction of these structures These
geotechnical engineering considerations which are discussed in the subsequent
subsections include the following
ground settlement
differential ground settlements
slope stability and
high groundwater table
Ground Settlement
Ground settlements may be anticipated when new fills are placed over the existing
ground to raise the site to the proposed finished elevations Because deep fills are planned
at areas likely underlain by soft soils significant ground settlements may occur at these
locations In addition concrete box and or pipe culverts are often present at many of these
potential soft ground areas requiring deep fills Ground settlement caused by addition of the
new fills will likely have adverse effects on the existing and new culverts differential
settlements Due to the large anticipated ground settlements special considerations and
mitigation measures should be taken into account by implementing proper construction
sequence and methods at these locations
Differential Ground Settlements
We understand that the new lanes will generally be located on either the north or
south side of the existing highway Placement of new fills over soft soils will likely cause
differential ground settlements across the width of the highway In addition fill heights will
generally be greater near the center of the streams or gullies due to the nature of these
topographic features Therefore ground settlement will likely be greater along the centerline
of stream and gully crossings where the new fill heights are the greatest
We also understand that the existing Kaumualii Highway will remain open during
construction of the new widened highway Therefore construction of new culverts across

Slope Stability
Based on the available information new fill heights of up to about 45 feet are planned
over potential soft soil areas along the highway Due to the large anticipated fill heights and
presence of soft soils stability of embankment slopes will need to be further evaluated during
the design phase In order to evaluate the stability of slopes detailed subsurface conditions
will need to be obtained at each potential soft soil location Subsurface information such as
thickness consistency and strength properties of the soft soils and underlying materials will
need to be obtained to perform detailed slope stability analyses
Based on the results of the slope stability analyses appropriate embankment slope
inclinations will be provided It should be noted that the soft soil areas with substantial fill
heights will likely require staged construction subgrade improvements and or flatter fill
slope inclinations Therefore the Right of Way limitations should be considered if use of
flatter slope inclination is desired
High Groundwater Table
As mentioned earlier most of the potential soft soil areas are located at the stream
and gully crossings Running streams were observed at many of these locations at the time
of our site reconnaissance Although surface water was not observed at other locations at
the time of our site reconnaissance high groundwater table should be anticipated at most
soft soil areas During installation of the new box and pipe culverts groundwater will likely
be encountered at many of the soft soil areas Therefore dewatering may be required during
construction of culverts depending on the type of soft soil mitigation measures that are
implemented If dewatering is required costs associated with obtaining National Pollution
Discharge Elimination System NPDES permits should be anticipated
Mitigation Measures
In order to provide some indication as to the possible mitigation measures to address
the geotechnical engineering considerations for the proposed highway widening the
following subsections discuss the four possible alternative mitigation measures These four
mitigations measures include the following
remove the soft soils and replace with select fills
staged construction
stone columns and
tunneling

Remove and Replace
To mitigate the effects of settlement and slope stability issues the soft soils under the
culverts and embankment areas may be removed and replaced with compacted engineered
fills However in areas underlain by substantial thickness of soft soils over excavation and
replacement may not be cost efficient Therefore it is critical to obtain in depth subsurface
information during the design phase in order to evaluate the cost effectiveness of this
proposed mitigation measure In addition the deep excavation will likely encounter
groundwater and or require the use of extensive shoring systems The remove and replace
method is generally practical and economical in areas containing limited extent of soft soils
Staged Construction
Due to slope stability issues we envision that placement of the deep fills greater than
15 feet thick at one time will likely not be feasible at the soft soil areas Therefore staged
construction should be implemented when placing substantial new fills on soft soils Staged
Construction generally involves placement of fill in multiple phases to allow the soft soils to
consolidate and gain strength prior to placement of additional fills and construction of various
structures Each phase of fill placement is followed by a settlement monitoring period which
may generally take about 3 to 12 months After the embankment is constructed to the design
finished grade the embankment may be excavated to install the culverts where necessary
Based on the available information from the preliminary investigation we estimate that
only about 10 to 15 feet of fill may be placed at one time at the soft soil locations It should
be noted that the design of staged construction would depend on many factors such as the
thickness consistency and engineering properties of the soft soil deposits Therefore
detailed subsurface exploration should be conducted during the design phase of this project
We anticipate that implementation of staged construction will likely be necessary
even if subgrade improvement techniques such as remove and replace and or stone
columns are utilized Because monitoring of ground settlement takes time consideration
should be given to construction scheduling at locations where new fills are required over soft
ground In addition installation of permanent culverts after completion of the fill placement
and settlement monitoring will likely require the use of a shoring system for deep
excavations
Stone Columns
Stone columns are a type of soft ground stabilization method that involves the
replacement of soft soils with coarse gravel which is compacted by vibration The size and
spacing of the stone columns are typically designed based on the subsurface conditions and
the anticipated loading Installation of stone columns will generally decrease the anticipated
ground settlements and increase the bearing capacity of the soil Furthermore stone
columns are ideal for rehabilitation of soft cohesive soils such as clays and silts encountered
and observed in our preliminary field exploration program for this project We envision that

Tunneling
Installation of the permanent culverts by tunneling may be considered in lieu of
excavation and backfilling after staged construction of the embankment fills In areas
requiring deep excavations shoring and or dewatering installation of the permanent culverts
by tunneling may be preferable and possibly more economical However tunneling may
not be practical and cost efficient for installation of large box culverts Therefore
consideration for use of tunneling may be limited to the pipe culverts Further analysis should
be performed during the design phase of this project for these possible mitigation measures
