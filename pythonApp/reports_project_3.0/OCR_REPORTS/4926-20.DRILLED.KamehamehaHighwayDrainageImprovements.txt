PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project is a design build project which includes the replacement of Kii Bridge
on Kamehameha Highway spanning Ohia Stream and the replacement of an existing
drainage culvert crossing Kamehameha Highway near Kahuku Hospital in the District of
Koolauloa on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project
site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1

Based on the available information the new bridge structure will be 55 feet wide
and about 80 feet in length The new bridge structure will be of concrete construction and
will be a single span bridge supported by the abutments at both ends The new culvert
structure also will be 55 feet wide and about 80 feet in length The culvert structure will
include four cells with openings of about 19 feet by 7 feet In addition the culvert structure
also included wingwalls of about 20 feet in length extending beyond the end cells Based
on the information provided the strength limit state axial loads at the bridge and culvert
structures will be about 595 and 230 kips respectively In general the strength limit state
loading governed the design lengths of the drilled shafts for the project
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered at the site and the structural
loading a deep foundation system consisting of 36 inch and 24 inch diameter
cast in place concrete drilled shafts were utilized for foundation support of the bridge and
culvert structures respectively Based on the results of the drilled shaft load tests we
recommended a revised shaft tip elevation of 75 feet Mean Sea Level MSL for the
bridge foundation shafts For the culvert foundation shafts we revised the shaft tip
elevations to 47 feet MSL Abutment 1 and Wall 1 49 feet MSL Walls 2 and 3 and
38 feet MSL Abutment 2 based on the load test results The approximate locations of
the drilled shafts for the bridge and culvert structures are shown on the Drilled Shaft
Identification Plans Plates 2 and 3
As mentioned previously the strength limit state loading governed the design
lengths of the drilled shafts for the project The drilled shafts were designed to derive
vertical support primarily from skin friction The end bearing component of the drilled
shafts was discounted in the design due to difficulties associated with obtaining a clean
bottom below the groundwater level during construction
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
At the replacement Kii Bridge site the borings generally encountered a surface fill
layer ranging in thickness from about 4 5 to 9 feet The fill layer consisted of medium stiff
to very stiff silty clays with sands and gravel and dense sands and gravel The surface fill
layer was underlain by alluvial deposits extending to depths of about 69 to 70 feet below
the existing ground surface The alluvial deposits generally consisted of soft to hard clays
and silts Below the alluvial deposits an older alluvial deposit was encountered extending
to depths of about 90 5 to 96 5 feet The older alluvial deposit generally was composed of
dense to very dense extremely weathered basaltic gravel cobbles and boulders The
older alluvial deposit was underlain by a basalt formation extending to the maximum depth
explored of about 102 feet below the existing ground surface
At the new culvert site the borings generally encountered a surface fill layer
consisting of medium dense to dense silty sands and gravel and basaltic and coralline
cobbles The fill layer ranged from about 7 to 9 feet thick The fill layer was underlain by a
lagoonal deposit extending to depths of about 14 to 27 feet below the existing ground
surface The lagoonal deposit generally consisted of very soft silty clays with shell

DRILLED SHAFT INSTALLATION
The installation of the load test and production shafts was performed by Malcolm
Drilling At the culvert site a 24 inch diameter load test shaft designated as TS 2 was
installed between March 30 and 31 2004 One 36 inch diameter load test shaft
designated as TS 1 was installed at the bridge site between April 1 and 5 2004 Due to
severe cave ins during the rebar cage installation Shaft TS 1 was abandoned and a
replacement load test shaft designated as TS 1A was constructed between
April 6 and 7 2004 Polymer slurry was used in the construction of the load test and
production shafts The approximate locations of the drilled shafts are shown on the Drilled
Shaft Identification Plans Plates 2 and 3
Based on the load test shaft installation program which also served as trial shafts
the drilled shaft subcontractor generally demonstrated that the proposed excavation
methods and equipment were acceptable for installing the drilled shaft foundations in
general accordance with the project specifications Based on the results of the
instrumented load tests performed the production drilled shaft lengths were determined
Ten 36 inch diameter production drilled shafts designated as BS 1 through BS 10
were installed at the Kii Bridge site between April 19 and May 5 2004 At the culvert site
installed between May 6 and 19 2004 The approximate locations of the drilled shafts are
shown on the Drilled Shaft Identification Plans Plates 2 and 3 In general a
representative from Geolabs observed the shaft excavation placement of the steel
reinforcements and concrete placement operations during the installation of the
production drilled shafts

The production shafts were excavated using an track mounted drill rig
equipped with 24 30 and 42 inch diameter augers 24 and 36 inch drilling buckets
36 inch diameter core barrel and 22 inch and 34 inch cleanout buckets Temporary
casings with diameters of 48 42 and 29 inches were utilized to case the upper portions of
the drilled shafts during the shaft construction The production shafts were excavated to
elevations ranging from about 75 to 80 4 feet MSL and 38 to 51 5 feet MSL for the
bridge and culvert shafts respectively
After the shaft holes were drilled to their designated tip elevations the reinforcing
bar rebar cages were placed into the drilled holes Rebar cage spacers were used to
provide the clearance associated with a minimum concrete cover of 3 5 inches between
the sidewalls of the drilled hole and the sides of the rebar cage
The shaft concrete was placed after placement of the rebar cage using a
3 5 inch OD steel pipe by tremie methods During concrete placement the bottom of the
tremie pipe was immersed in at least 5 feet of concrete as required in the specifications
In the shaft excavation where temporary casing was installed a minimum of 5 feet of
concrete was maintained above the bottom of the casing during casing removal and
concrete placement
It should be noted that concrete with time differences of more than 90 minutes
between the batch time and placement time was encountered for some of the shafts For
the drilled shaft concrete design mix a significant amount of retarders were used to extend
the time for the concrete to be workable The temperature of the concrete was monitored
during concrete placement and the temperature generally did not exceed 90 degrees
Fahrenheit before placement commenced We understand that the concrete cylinder
breaks were well above the design concrete strength Based on the above we believe
that the concrete placed for these shafts are satisfactory
During construction of culvert shaft CS 6 concrete with slump of about 5 to
6 inches estimated by visual observation was used during the shaft construction Based
on the project specifications the concrete slump requirement is 8 inches plus or minus
1 inch for concrete placed underwater To verify the shaft integrity Shaft CS 6 was cored
on using HQ coring tools Based on the results of the coring we believe that
the integrity of Shaft CS 6 is satisfactory and meets the minimum strength requirements
Based on our field observations we believe that the drilled shafts for the Kii Bridge
and Hospital Culvert have been installed in general conformance with the project plans
and specifications and within the limits of generally accepted construction practices The
location of the top of the shafts should be measured using optical survey equipment to
verify the shaft position is within the tolerance in the project specifications Based on our
observations of the drilled shaft installation it is our professional opinion that the drilled
shafts have been installed to sufficient depths to provide the design load capacity
