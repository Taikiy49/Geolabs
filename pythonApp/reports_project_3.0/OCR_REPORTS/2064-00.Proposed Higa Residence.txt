the above referenced project The purpose of our exploration was to
evaluate the suitability of the near surface soils as foundation support
for the proposed construction The study consisted of drilling and
sampling two 2 borings within the proposed building site performing
laboratory soil tests and engineering analysis This report summarizes
our findings and recommendations

EXISTING SITE CONDITION
The subject lot is located in the southwestern corner of the
subdivision development on Ala Puumalu Street and about 150 feet east of
Ala Hananui Street The ground surface slopes downward towards the south
at about 7 percent gradient The lot is mildly vegetated with small
bushes throughout
GEOLOGY
The project site is located near the southeastern flank of the
Aliamanu vent and on the northeastern flank of the Salt Lake vent complex
of the Honolulu Volcanic Series
As the molten magma ascended through these vents it reacted with the
groundwater generating steam This steam caused explosive eruptions which
deposited low cones of ash material that have consolidated to form tuff
locally referred to as mudrock
The Aliamanu tuff is an older water laid ash deposit that was laid
dewn when the sea level was higher than the present The Aliamanu
eruptive event deposited its material over alluvium originating from
Halawa Stream

After this phase of volcanic activity ceased sedimentary deposits
accumulated over the Aliamanu tuff before the next phase began Then the
Salt Lake eruptions began when the sea was at about 40 to 60 feet below
its present level The Salt Lake eruptive event deposited its material
over earlier tuffs from the older Aliamanu events
The water that accumulated in the crater was believed to originate
from depressional springs and rain water During periods of little
rainfall the shallow ponded water was reduced by evaporation and over the
years the mineral salt content of the ponded water increased
Recently man has developed the crater floor by filling and grading
the area for residential and golf course purposes
The subject residential lot is most likely near the old edge of the
lake shoreline where previous fill placement was done prior to 1972
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions at the subject lot were explored by the
drilling and sampling of two 2 borings to depths of 14 5 to 19 0 feet
below the existing ground surface The approximate locations of the
borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
In general the lot is underlain by a thin surface layer about 1
foot thick of compacted fill consisting of dense silty tuff sand and
gravel Between the bottom of the fill and the bottom of our borings
tuff mudrock formation was encountered
Groundwater was not encountered during our exploration

House Foundation
Spread footing foundation construction is anticipated for this
project To support the proposed house we believe that shallow spread
footing foundation bearing on compacted structural fill material or hard
tuff formation mudrock be used
An allowable bearing pressure of up to 3000 p s f can be used for
the compacted structural fill material and 4000 p s f for hard tuff
formation mudrock All footings should be embedded a minimum of 12
inches below the lowest adjacent outside grade For footings located on
sloping ground sufficient footing embedment should be provided to obtain
a 5 foot minimum horizontal set back distance between the toe of the
footing and the nearest adjacent Slope face
Settlement under the design loading is anticipated to be small and
for footings bearing on the compacted existing and new structural fill it
is estimated to be on the order of less than one half of an inch total and
one fourth of an inch differential For footings on the undisturbed
in situ hard tuff negligible settlement is anticipated
Slab On Grade
It is recommended that slab on grade be placed over a 4 inch layer of
base course compacted to 95 of its maximum dry density as determined by
ASTM D 1557 78
The subgrade should be kept moist and protected from drying prior to
concrete placement Subgrade should be compacted to a minimum of 90 of
maximum dry density In order to protect against moisture damage of the

Site Grading
The existing terrain at the site generally slopes down towards the
west at about seven percent gradient The grading work will consist of
about 2 feet of cut and about 4 5 feet of fill
The area to be filled should be cleared of vegetation prior to the
commencement of fill placement All excavated areas for footings should
be thoroughly recompacted or loose material removed prior to placement of
reinforcement in the footing
For fill and backfill materials we fell that the existing on site
fill soils or excavated mudrock broken down to 6 inch minus sizes which
are generally non expansive may be used
Fill and backfill should be placed in maximum 8 inch lifts and
compacted to 90 of its maximum dry density as established by ASTM
D 1557 78
The finished grade outside the slabs pavements and foundations
should be shaped to avoid ponding conditions and to shed water away from
the structure

Design Review
Plans and specifications for foundations site grading and utility
construction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and
written comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the footing excavations be examined by a soils
engineer during construction to see that adequate bearing has been
obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the
findings of our investigation
If imported material are used they should be non expansive tested
and approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site

LIMITATIONS
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations
made in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation engineering
principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all other
warranties either expressed or implied
The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are based
on our site reconnaissance soil information from boring and laboratory
test data obtained from this soil investigation
Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encountered and cannot be
fully determined by taking soil samples Unforeseen conditions such as

construction to attain a properly constructed project Some contingency
fund is thus recommended to accommodate these possible costs
