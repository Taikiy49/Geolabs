PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Mehana at Kapolei development site is bordered by a drainage easement and
Roosevelt Avenue to the south Ft Barrette Road to the east and the future James
Campbell Estate residential development to the north and west Pulewa at Mehana
Parcel 3 is located at the southeastern portion of Mehana It is bordered by Roads A B
N and M on the north south east and west respectively It encompasses approximately
6 9 acres as shown on the Phasing Plan Plate 1
Our monitoring services during construction consisted of moisture and density
testing to determine adequacy of compaction of fills and backfills for utility installations
including compaction testing for building pads garages walkways and driveways The
Site Grading Plans for Pulewa at Mehana Parcel 3 are shown on Plates 2 1 and 2 2
This report summarizes our field observations and compaction testing services for
the site grading operations and infrastructure installation for the Pulewa at Mehana
Parcel 3 project

MASS GRADING
Parcel 3 encompasses the southern portion of Mehana at Kapolei Phase I a
previously mass graded portion of the Mehana at Kapolei Development The mass
grading for Phase I consisted entirely of filling the site up to about 12 feet Various
imported off site materials were used to fill up to about 4 feet below finished grade The
upper 4 feet of fill consisted primarily of crushed coral structural fill
The fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to above optimum
moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry
density as determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557 In the roadway areas the fill
and backfill materials within 2 feet of the finished subgrade were compacted to a
minimum of 95 percent relative density
SITE GRADING
In conducting our construction monitoring services during the site grading and
utility backfilling operations for Parcel 3 samples of the materials utilized as fill were
obtained and transported to our laboratory for testing The maximum dry densities and
optimum moisture contents were established in general accordance with ASTM D 1557
Modified Proctor and the test results are summarized on Plate 3
Periodic field density tests were performed in general accordance with
ASTM D 6938 Nuclear Gauge Method The test results are summarized on
Plates 4 1 through 4 33 These tests were performed on prepared surfaces of
constructed fills and backfills Test locations and elevations were estimated using on
site survey stakes for reference These locations and elevations should be considered
accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed to be
unsatisfactory were reworked until the measured relative compaction conformed to or
exceeded the minimum specified for the project
Our services consisted of full time and part time field observation of the
contractor s Delta Construction Corporation subgrade preparation fill and backfill
placement and compaction procedures and confirmation of subgrade soil conditions
exposed during the above site grading operations

HOUSE CONSTRUCTION CONSIDERATIONS
The finished grades adjacent to the structures should be sloped to shed water
away from the foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also advised to
install gutter systems around the house structures to collect and discharge water away
from the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering within 3 feet of
the foundations and slabs should be avoided Gravel strips adjacent to slabs and
foundations should be avoided Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have
concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
building foundations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation and
subsequent loss of strength of the near surface soils The foundation excavations
should be properly backfilled against the footings immediately after setting of the
concrete to reduce water infiltration Drainage swales should be provided as soon as
possible and should be maintained to drain surface run off away from foundations
Landscaping of the house lots by the homeowners is recommended to reduce the
potential for erosion and for altering the drainage patterns
