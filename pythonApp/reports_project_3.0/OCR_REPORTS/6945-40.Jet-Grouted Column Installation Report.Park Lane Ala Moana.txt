Dear Mr. Pollock: 
In accordance with the Owner and Consultant Agreement entered into on 
August 14, 2014 with AMX Partners, LLC, Geolabs has observed the installation of jet-
grouted columns in support of the Park Lane Ala Moana project in Honolulu on the Island 
of Oahu, Hawaii. This letter report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observation 
services performed during the installation of the jet-grouted columns at the project site. 
The project location and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map, Plate 1.   
Project Considerations 
Ala Moana Center is located at 1450 Ala Moana Boulevard in Honolulu on the 
Island of Oahu, Hawaii. The shopping center is bounded by Kapiolani Boulevard and Kona 
Street to the north, Mahukona Street and Atkinson Drive to the east, Ala Moana Boulevard 
to the south, and Piikoi Street to the west. 
The project involves expansion of the makai (southern) portion of the center, 
generally bounded by Neiman Marcus to the east, Ala Moana Boulevard to the south, 
Piikoi Street to the west, and the existing mall and Ewa Mall Expansion (currently under 
construction) to the north. It is proposed to demolish the existing parking deck in this area 
and construct a three-level parking structure for the center below a multi-level residential 
condominium structure with additional parking for the residents.  
The subsurface conditions at the project site generally consist of surface fill 
materials overlying soft and/or loose lagoonal deposits extending to depths of about 
30 to 37 feet below the existing pavement surface. Coral ledges of variable thickness 
and hardness are generally encountered below the highly compressible lagoonal 
deposits across the project site.

New utility lines (i.e., water, sewer, and drain lines) and utility line connections 
adjacent to the project site are required for the development. The bottom of some of these 
utility lines and below-grade structures are near or within the soft/loose lagoonal deposits 
encountered below a depth of about 4 to 5 feet below the existing ground surface. To 
reduce the potential for appreciable settlement of these piping and structures, we 
recommended utilizing jet-grouted columns to support selected utility lines and below-
grade structures. The jet-grouted column foundations would derive vertical support 
primarily from bearing on the hard coral ledges and/or dense coralline detritus materials 
and from skin friction along the sides of the jet-grouted column foundations with the 
surrounding soils. 
Jet-Grouted Column Installation 
The jet-grouted columns were installed at the project site from September 23, 2014 
through November 17, 2014. Frank Coluccio Construction Company (FCCC) is the 
subcontractor installing the jet-grouted columns at the project site while Jayar Construction 
Company (Jayar) provided the jet-grouted column locations before installation 
commenced.  
In general, FCCC was required to install 36-inch diameter jet-grouted columns with 
a 7-day unconfined compressive strength of at least 200 pounds per square inch (psi) and 
a 28-day unconfined compressive strength of at least 500 psi. To install the jet-grouted 
columns, FCCC used a SOILMEC SM-405 jetting rig to drill down to various depths below 
the existing ground surface. Grout was then pumped using a high pressure pump attached 
to the jetting rig. The horizontal jets of cement grout slurry cut and mixed the surrounding 
in-situ materials with the neat cement slurry grout and a soil-cement column was formed 
as the drill bit was slowly rotated and withdrawn from the drilled hole.   
A total of 55 jet-grouted columns were installed at the project site for foundation 
support of several below-grade structures consisting of manhole structures, oil-sand 
separators (OSS), and hydrodynamic separators (HDS). The locations of the jet-grouted 
columns are shown on the Jet-Grouted Column Location Plan, Plates 2.1 through 2.6. A 
summary of the jet-grouted columns installed for the project is provided on Plates 3.1 and 
3.2.
