PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The Kainani Villas - Phase 2 project site is located in the northern portion of Lot E 
of Kukui’ula Development Company’s U/AA Subdivision in Koloa on the Island of Kauai, 
Hawaii. The Phase 2 development consists of subdividing the northern portion of Lot E 
into residential Lot Nos. 2 through 5 and construction of the extension of Road “B” to the 
previously mentioned house lots. In addition, underground site utilities were installed to 
support the above-mentioned house lots. The site is generally bounded by Lot F to the 
north, Ala Pualeikukui to the east, Kainani Place to the west, and the intersection of 
Ala Pualeikukui and the Kainani Villas Phase 1 project within Lot 1 to the south. The 
project location and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map, Plate 1. A 
layout of the project site is shown on the Site Plan, Plate 2. 
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services 
pertaining to the building grading, and roadway and utility installation for the Kainani 
Phase 2 project site.

BUILDING GRADING AND UTILITY INSTALLATION 
We observed the roadway grading and utility installation work for the Kainani Villas 
Phase 2 project from July 23, 2020 to August 20, 2020. The roadway grading work and 
utility installation consisted of clearing and grubbing, and placement and compaction of fills 
up to about 5 feet thick to achieve the finished grades. The roadway grading and utility 
installation work were performed by Earthworks Pacific Inc. During the grading operations, 
we obtained samples of the on-site and imported materials utilized as fill and backfill 
materials and transported them to our laboratory for testing. The maximum dry densities 
and optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general accordance 
with ASTM D1557 (Modified Proctor), and the test results are summarized on Plate 3. 
The fill and backfill materials were moisture-conditioned to above the optimum 
moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density 
as determined in accordance with ASTM D1557. The finished subgrade and aggregate 
base course materials for the roadways were moisture-conditioned to above optimum 
moisture and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction. 
During the roadway grading and utility installation work, periodic field density tests 
were performed in general accordance with ASTM D6938 (Nuclear Gauge Method). 
Results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 4.1 and 4.2 of this 
report. These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed 
fills and backfills at the site. Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and 
interpolating from on-site survey stakes. These locations and elevations should be 
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used. Areas assessed as 
unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained and/or 
approved by our field representative based on visual observation and hand probing. 
Our services consisted of part-time field observation of the contractor’s fill 
placement, subgrade preparation, and compaction procedures and confirmation of 
subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above grading operations. 
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the 
referenced tests, it is our opinion that the building grading, and utility and roadway 
installation work for the above project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period 
covered by this report were performed in conformance with the general intent of the
