In accordance with your request we have conducted a preliminary geophysical
survey for the above referenced project This report summarizes the findings and
recommendations derived from our field survey and analyses
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
Based on the information provided to us the site is a demolished Gasco plant
It is anticipated that various objects such as footings pipes and tanks were left
underground during demolition We understand that a new development is planned at
the project site It is desired to attempt to locate obstacles which may not have been
removed during demolition to reduce the potential for future construction problems

GENERAL SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located at the intersection of Pacific Street and Iwilei Road in
Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii The subject site is a demolished lIwilei Gasco
site Previously one underground storage tank was removed from the easterly side of
the building It was suspected that another tank may be present on the southerly or
easterly side of the building The project site is presently vacant and the topography
of the site is relatively level One half of the site was used as a temporary parking area
at the time of our field survey
FIELD EXPLORATION
A grid patten was generated on the plan and then transferred to the field on the
northwest portion of the site for the test survey The grid lines were spaced at about
20 feet apart
A preliminary geophysical survey was conducted using a ground penetrating
radar GPR system along the generated grid lines at the subject site on
The GPR system was manufactured by Geophysical Survey
Systems Inc consisting of an SIR System 3 control unit with a 500 Megahertz antenna
set
Ground penetrating radar utilizes impulse radar technology to obtain real time
profiles of the subsurface materials A radar frequency pulse is transmitted into the
subsurface and when it encounters an interface between two materials with differing
electrical properties it is reflected back to a receiver on the surface In a relatively
homogeneous medium this reflection is noted as an anomaly

The reflected pulse is intercepted by the receiver and then electronically
processed and printed on a graphic recorder producing a record similar to a marine
depth recorder chart As the transmitter and receiver antenna array is moved across
the ground surface a real time graphic record of the subsurface is generated Markers
can be inserted into the data print to reference distances as the array is moved
providing a means to physically locate anomalies
Ground penetrating radar is generally considered most applicable to the location
of subsurface voids and buried structures Depth of penetration and data resolution are
dependent upon soil type moisture content electrical properties antenna frequency
and power
The use of ground penetrating radar is limited by the presence of conductive
materials within the profile being surveyed This is particularly true if the conductive
materials are in the upper or surface portions of the subsurface profile Conductive
materials attenuate the electromagnetic signal and interfere with penetration of the
radar pulses This is particularly true for the subject site due to the various
underground obstacles and shallow groundwater table
FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATION
The preliminary surveys conducted with ground penetrating radar at the site
indicated some anomalies at the northwest portion of the site It should be noted that
the site contains various underground structures including footings slabs pipe and
tanks etc The complicated site conditions tended to obscure the data and make
accurate interpretation difficult Based on the available plans field geophysical data
and our analyses it is our opinion that these underground anomalies are possibly the
pipes and tanks left from previous demolition
Based on the preliminary survey results we suggested that a number of test pits
be excavated along the survey line to correlate the GPR survey results The
preliminary survey results and our suggestion were discussed in a meeting We
understand that this suggestion was not practical due to environmental issues at the
project site Therefore based on the test survey results the subsurface soil conditions
and the limitations of the GPR system it was agreed with the client that no further field
work be performed This outcome was recognized at the commencement of the
project
