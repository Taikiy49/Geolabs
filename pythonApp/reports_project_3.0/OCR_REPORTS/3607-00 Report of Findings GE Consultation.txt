In accordance with your request and our proposal of April 9th we have reviewed
the geotechnical engineering report by another geotechnical engineer for your house
and made a site visit to observe the present conditions This letter presents our
findings and recommendations
Background Information
It is our understanding that the subject residence was constructed in 1980 and
you have lived there since about October 1986 Movements of the retaining wall at the
southeast corner of the lot occurred resulting in some damage to portions of the house
near this wall Based on the recommendations of another geotechnical engineer
repairs to the retaining wall were made in 1994 and 1995 These repairs included the
installation of Chance anchors to increase the stability of the wall Since the completion
of the wall repairs the ground movements in the vicinity of the wall appear to have
slowed considerably Vertical subdrains were recommended behind the retaining wall
however due to the difficulty in constructing these drains this portion of the repair have
not been completed yet
Findings
A site visit was made by the undersigned on May 8th to observe the present
conditions and obtain additional information regarding the construction and history of
the residence A copy of the site and grading plan was obtained as well as pictures of

the repair work done to the retaining wall Based on these sources of information the
following are our findings and recommendations
1 The grading plan indicates that most of the lot was cut to provide level
area for the house The exception was the southeast corner where the
subject retaining wall was installed with about 2 to 8 feet of fill behind it for
a distance of about 10 to 20 feet
2 The retaining wall movements may have been caused by one or more of
the following a insufficient footing embedment b insufficient size of
wall c poor backfill compaction and c buildup of subsurface water
behind the wall
3 The installation of the wall repairs appears to have stabilized the wall
movements The separation crack between the wall and concrete slab
should be monitored on a long term basis to confirm the stability of the
retaining wall
4 A subdrain system should be installed to intercept subsurface water
behind the retaining wall Since the vertical drains are very difficult to
install and may have limited range an alternative may be to install a
subdrain trench further back behind the wall approximately 15 feet The
surface between the subdrain and wall should be covered with concrete or
an impervious liner to prevent surface water from infiltrating behind the
wall
Bi Future movements of the retaining wall or settlement of the backfill behind
the wall could cause movements and damage to the house Therefore it
may be prudent to underpin portions of the house close to the wall with
Atlas Piers or a similar system
