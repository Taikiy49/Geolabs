PROJECT DESCRIPTION 
The project is located on Kalaeloa Boulevard towards Malakole Street in the 
Kapolei area on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. The project location and general vicinity are 
shown on the Project Location Map, Plate 1.  The project involves the replacement of 
an existing power pole with new custom steel pole, designated as P20, at the approximate 
location shown on the Drilled Shaft Location Plan, Plate 2. 
The scope of our work discussed in this letter report includes observation of the 
drilled shaft foundation construction for New HECO P20 Power Pole project only.  Royal 
Contracting Co, Ltd. was the general contractor for the project and also installed the drilled 
shaft foundation support system. 
The new pole is supported on deep foundation consisting of cast-in-place 
concrete drilled shaft with 60 inches diameter and 22 feet length. The design top of 
foundation elevation is set at approximately +20.93 feet Mean Sea Level (MSL) with the 
existing ground level elevation of about +21.07 MSL. 
Based on our previous subsurface exploration, we anticipated encountering surface 
fill layer extending to a depth of about 5 feet below the existing ground surface overlying 
coral formation consisting of medium dense to very dense silty sands and gravel. Based 
on the drilled boring, petroleum hydrocarbon products (black oil) are anticipated at depths 
of about 20 to 26 feet below the existing ground surface.  
DRILLED SHAFT INSTALLATION 
Based on the anticipated subsurface conditions and foundation demand, a deep 
foundation system consisting of cast-in-place concrete drilled shaft embedded into the 
underlying fill material and coral formation was utilized for foundation support of the power 
pole structure. 
The cast-in-place concrete drilled shaft was designed to derive vertical and lateral 
support primarily from skin friction between the concrete drilled shaft and the coral 
formation. The cast-in-place concrete drilled shafts should have a nominal diameter of 
60 inches and a length of 22 feet.  
The drilled shaft contractor installed the drilled shaft on August 26 and 27, 2016 at 
the location shown on the Drilled Shaft Location Plan, Plate 2. The as-built diameter and 
the length of the drilled shaft installed for the project are summarized on Plate 3 of this 
report. A representative from Geolabs observed the drilled shaft excavation, placement of 
reinforcing cage, and concrete operations on a near-continuous (full-time) basis during the 
installation of the drilled shaft. 
In general, the drilled shaft was excavated using a combination of a CAT 330C 
excavator with an Auger Torque drill head attachment. Tooling used for the 60-inch

Drilling operations generally encountered about 7 feet of fill material consisting of 
medium dense silty coralline sands and gravel. At the depth of about 7 feet, an abandoned 
18-inch diameter drain line was encountered. After breaking through the pipe with a hoe 
ram, coral formation consisting of dense to very dense silty coralline sands was 
encountered to a depth of about 20.5 feet below the existing ground surface. Older 
alluvium consisting of stiff silty clay was encountered underlying the coral formation to a 
depth of about 21.5 feet below the existing ground surface. Groundwater was observed 
almost at the same elevation as the alluvial soil. Drilling was terminated at 21.5 feet below 
ground surface. The total length of the shaft at this point was about 22.8 feet. 
It should be noted that the contractor experienced difficulties placing the auger in 
the beginning so they used other tools (e.g., hoe ram and backhoe) for the excavation. 
The presence of the drain line also added to the difficulty of drilling which increased the 
diameter of the upper 7 feet of the shaft to about 6 to 7 feet in diameter.  
After excavation of the drilled shaft to the designated tip elevation, the 21.7-foot 
reinforcing cage was lowered into the drilled hole with the use of a crane. The tip of the 
shaft was measured at 20.9 feet below ground surface resulting in about 22.2 feet total 
length for the shaft. Concrete was then placed through a 4-inch rubber tremie pipe starting 
from the bottom. During concrete placement, we observed that at least 5 feet of concrete 
head was maintained above the bottom of the tremie pipe up to the depth of 5 feet below 
the ground surface and then it reduced to about 2 feet below concrete level. The final 
2 feet of the shaft was poured using the concrete truck chute. 
Concrete with slump ranging from 3 to 5 inches was used for the drilled shaft. 
Consolidated Engineering Laboratories monitored the concrete used for the drilled shaft, 
including the performance of slump tests and concrete sampling. 
Based on the observations of our field representative, it is our professional opinion 
that the drilled shaft supporting the New HECO P20 Power Pole was installed in general 
accordance with the intent of the project plans and specifications and the geotechnical 
recommendations presented in our geotechnical engineering report for the project and 
subsequent recommendations.
