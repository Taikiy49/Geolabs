Dear Mr. Dinsmore: 
This report summarizes our earthwork observation and testing services performed 
during the site grading operations for the TJ Maxx project at Kapolei Commons from 
January 29 through December 24, 2014. 
EARTHWORK OPERATIONS 
The TJ Maxx project site is located within the Kapolei Commons development in 
the Kapolei area in the District of Ewa on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii.  The project site is 
situated on the Ewa side of the existing Sports Authority building at the northwestern 
corner of the retail development.  A layout of the new retail store is shown on the Site Plan, 
Plate 1. 
Royal Contracting Company performed the site grading operations during the 
building pad and infrastructure installation for the project. The site grading work consisted 
of clearing and grubbing, demolition, footing excavation, subgrade preparation, placement 
and compaction of fills and backfills, pavement construction, and utility trench excavation 
and backfill. 
Following the clearing, grubbing, and demolition operations, the asphalt concrete 
and base course at the existing parking lot within the new TJ Maxx building pad were 
removed to expose the underlying coralline capping fill materials. The existing coral

The in-situ clayey soils along the Service Road and new pavement areas were 
over-excavated and replaced with 18 inches of non-expansive select granular materials 
prior to the placement of the aggregate base course.  Soft and pumping subgrade 
materials observed between about Sta. 0+75 and Sta. 1+00 along the west lane of the 
Service Road were over-excavated and replaced with non-expansive select granular 
material compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction. 
During placement of the non-expansive select granular fill layer, the fill materials 
were generally placed in near-level lifts, brought to above the optimum moisture content, 
and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density determined in 
accordance with ASTM D1557 (Modified Proctor). The base course under the AC 
pavements were generally placed in near-level lifts, brought to above the optimum 
moisture content, and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent of the maximum dry density 
determined in accordance with ASTM D1557 (Modified Proctor).  During the foundation 
excavation and preparation, the excavated footing bottom was generally recompacted to a 
minimum of 90 percent relative compaction. 
New utility connections were installed to serve the new TJ Maxx retail development. 
In general, a bedding layer consisting of 6 inches of No. 3B Fine gravel was provided 
below the piping and manholes for uniform support. Free-draining granular materials 
consisting of No. 3B Fine gravel was also used for the trench backfill up to about 12 inches 
above the pipes to provide adequate support around the pipes and to reduce the 
compaction effort on the initial trench backfill. The trench backfill was placed in 8-inch level 
loose lifts and mechanically compacted to no less than 90 percent relative compaction. 
Where trenches are below pavement areas, the upper 3 feet of the trench backfill below 
the pavement grade was compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction. 
During the site grading operations, samples of the on-site and imported materials 
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing. 
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our 
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D1557 (Modified Proctor), and the test 
results are summarized on Plate 2 of this report. 
Field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations on an 
as-need basis to confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures 
and soil conditions were noted. A total of 265 field density tests, including retest of failed

in the accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor 
earthwork construction and materials.
