This letter report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations during the
probing operation at the 0 6 Million Gallon MG water tank site for the Kulani Correctional
Facility Water System Improvements project on March 7 2001
Project Considerations
The project site is located within the existing Kulani Correctional Facility in the
District of South Hilo on the Island of Hawaii Based on the information provided we
understand that a new water tank will be constructed as part of the Kulani
Correctional Facility Water System Improvements project The general layout of the new
water tank is shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
Based on the information provided the new water tank will be of glass clad
construction with a concrete floor slab The water tank measuring approximately 70 feet in
diameter and 24 feet in height will be located on relatively level ground to the northeast of
the existing water tank and pump station The finish floor elevation for the new water tank
will be at approximately 5 315 feet Mean Sea Level MSL with a minimum 6 inch thick
reinforced concrete slab

Based on our field exploration and experience in the vicinity of the site cavities
and or voids may be present in the basaltic lava flows encountered at the project site To
reduce the potential for loss of foundation support resulting from collapse of the cavities
below foundations we recommended that a program of cavity probing and grouting at the
foundation locations be conducted during construction
Observation of Probing Operation
The purpose of the probing and grouting operations was to reduce the potential for
loss of foundation support resulting from collapse of cavities or compression of loose zones
below the new water tank The probing operation was performed in general conformance
with the recommendations presented in our report for the project dated
However we understand that the probing procedures have been modified as follows
1 Probe holes will be 3 inches in diameter and extend to a depth of about
10 feet below the tank subgrade
2 Probe holes will be spaced approximately 17 to 18 feet apart resulting in a
total of about 20 probe holes within the tank footprint
The probe hole locations were marked by Isemoto Contracting Co Ltd prior to the
probing operation In general the probe holes within the footprint of the new water tank
were drilled to a depth of about 10 feet below the water tank subgrade elevation The
probe holes about 3 inches in diameter were drilled by Isemoto Contracting Co Ltd
using a pressurized air percussion hammer on a track drill rig Following the probing
operation a 2 inch diameter PVC pipe was inserted into each of the probe holes to
maintain the integrity of the open hole and to reduce the potential for cave in prior to the
grouting operation
Based on our field observations soft loose zones were encountered at Probe Hole
No 9 between depths of about 5 and 10 feet below the existing ground surface Two
additional probe holes Probe Hole Nos 9A and 9B drilled in its vicinity have also
encountered similar soft loose zones at about the same depths Based on our field
observations and evaluation we believe that the soft loose zones encountered in these
probe holes probably consisted of loose clinker materials instead of a cavity and or void
Therefore it is our opinion that additional probe holes would not be required and the
grouting operation may proceed at these locations A total of 22 probe holes with an
aggregate length of about 220 lineal feet were drilled The approximate locations of the
probe holes are shown on the Probe Hole Location Plan Plate2 Based on the
information provided we understand that a representative from the D A G S Inspection
Branch will observe the grouting operation of the probe holes
