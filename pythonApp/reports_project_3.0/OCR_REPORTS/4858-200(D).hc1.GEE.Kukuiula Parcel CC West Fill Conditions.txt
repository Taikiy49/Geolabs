REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The Island of Kauai is composed of a single basalt shield volcano built by the
extrusion of lava of the Waimea Canyon Volcanic Series during the late Pliocene Epoch
more than 2 5 million years ago Following the cessation of this main shield building
phase renewed volcanic activity occurred with the extrusion of basaltic lava of the
post erosional Koloa Volcanic Series and the concurrent deposition of the alluvial
sediments of the Palikea Formation

Rocks of the Koloa Volcanic Series cover most of the eastern half of the Island of
Kauai These rocks generally are characterized as thick flows of dense basalt extruded
from groups of vents aligned in north south trends in various locales Associated with the
vents are pyroclastic materials that usually form low cinder cones at the vent
The basaltic rock built by the extrusion of lavas of the Koloa Volcanic Series
generally is characterized by flows of jointed dense vesicular basalt with interbedded thin
clinker layers The weathering process has formed a mantle of residual soils which grade
to saprolite with increased depth In general saprolite is composed of mainly silty material
and is typical of the tropical weathering of volcanic rocks The saprolite grades to basaltic
rock formation less weathered with increased depth
Agricultural development within the last 100 years and recent mass grading work
have brought the area to its present form
EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is located within the Kukui ula Development Company s Parcel CC
West Subdivision in Koloa on the Island of Kauai Hawaii The project site is currently
landscaped with grasses Based on our field observations and the project grading plan
the residential Lot Nos F to H and J to L have been mass graded to finish lot elevations
of about 56 to 70 feet Mean Sea Level MSL The lots generally slope upward in a
northerly direction The elevation difference between adjacent lots ranged from about 2
to 4 feet The project site is bounded by the existing golf course Hole Nos 7 and 9 on
the northern and southwestern sides respectively
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by drilling and sampling
nine borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 9 extending to depths ranging from
about 10 to 15 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate boring locations
are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Our borings generally encountered a surface fill layer about 5 to 15 feet thick
underlain by residual soils extending to the maximum explored depth of about 15 feet below
the existing ground surface The surface fill consisted of stiff to hard silty clay and clayey
silt with traces of sand The residual soil was composed of very stiff to hard silty clay and
clayey silt with traces of sand
Groundwater was not encountered in the drilled borings at the time of our field
exploration It should be noted that the groundwater level will fluctuate based upon rainfall
surface water runoff seasonal precipitation and other factors

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field exploration the surface fill thicknesses of Lot Nos F to H and
J to L varied from about 5 to 15 feet below the existing ground surface The surface fill
thickness was the shallowest at Lot Nos H and J with fill thickness of about 5 to 6 feet
The surface fill thicknesses were deeper at Lot Nos F G K and L with fill thicknesses
ranged from about 10 to 15 feet
The surface fill material encountered in our borings exhibited stiff to hard
consistency The typical optimum moisture content of the on site silty clay clayey silt soils
at the Kukui ula Development generally ranges from about 27 to 29 percent Based on
our laboratory testing the moisture contents of the fill material ranged from about 24 3 to
44 6 percent The fill moisture contents below 27 percent were generally encountered
within the top 4 feet below the existing ground surface in some of the borings
Modified consolidation tests were performed on selected soil samples to evaluate
the collapsibility swell potential of the fill material The conventional consolidation test is
modified to compress the in situ soil sample under a surcharge load equal to approximately
the over burden earth pressure The soil sample is allowed to settle until it has stabilized
under the surcharge load Water is then added to the soil sample to evaluate its collapse or
swell potential in a saturated condition Based on the modified consolidation test results the
fill materials exhibit a low collapsibility potential Results of the modified consolidation tests
are presented on Plates B 4 and B 5 in Appendix B
Based on the above and the stiff to hard consistency of the existing fill materials
we believe that the existing fills underlying Lot Nos F to H and J to L should provide
competent shallow foundation support for the future house structures to be constructed
on these lots The individual lot buyers should retain a qualified geotechnical engineer to
evaluate the soil conditions and provide specific recommendations for foundations
retaining walls site grading and pavements

Field Exploration
Our field exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling nine borings
designated as Boring Nos 1 through 9 extending to depths of about 10 to 15 feet below the
existing ground surface The borings were drilled using a truck mounted drill rig equipped
with continuous flight augers
Our geologist classified the materials encountered in the borings by visual and
textural examination in the field in general accordance with ASTM D2488 Standard Practice
for Description and Identification of Soils and monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous full time basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and
by testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general accordance with ASTM D2487
Standard Practice for Classification of Soils for Engineering Purposes Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on the Soil Log Legend Plate A 0 1 Deviations made to
the soil classification in accordance with ASTM D2487 are described on the Soil
Classification Log Key Plate A 0 2 Graphic representations of the materials encountered
are presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 9
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained from the borings drilled in
general accordance with ASTM D3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a
3 inch OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches In
addition some samples were obtained from the borings drilled in general accordance with
ASTM D1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 2 inch OD
standard penetration sampler using the same hammer and drop The blow counts needed
to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown as the
Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths The
penetration resistance shown on the Logs of Borings indicates the number of blows required
for the specific sampler type used The blow counts may need to be factored to obtain the
Standard Penetration Test SPT blow counts
Pocket penetrometer tests were performed on selected cohesive soil samples
retrieved in the field The pocket penetrometer test provides an indication of the unconfined
compressive strength of the sample Pocket penetrometer test results are summarized on
the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths

Laboratory Tests
Moisture Content ASTM D2216 and Unit Weight ASTM D2937 determinations
were performed on selected samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil
properties The test results are presented on the Log of Boring at the appropriate sample
depths
Six Atterberg Limits tests ASTM D4318 were performed on selected soil samples
to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits The test results are summarized on the Logs of
Borings at the appropriate sample depths Graphic presentation of the test results is
provided on Plate B 1
One Sieve Analysis test ASTM D6913 was performed on a selected soil sample to
evaluate the gradation characteristics of the soils and to aid in soil classification Graphic
presentation of the grain size distributions is provided on Plate B 2
Four one inch Ring Swell tests were performed on remolded and relatively
undisturbed samples to evaluate the swelling potential of the near surface soils The test
results are summarized on Plate B 3
Two modified consolidation tests were performed selected soil samples The
conventional consolidation tests were modified to evaluate the compressibility
characteristics of the in situ soil samples under surcharge load equal to approximately the
over burden earth pressure The soil sample is allowed to settle until it has stabilized under
a surcharge load Water is then added to the soil sample to evaluate its collapse or swell
potential in a saturated condition The test results are presented on Plates B 4 and B 5
