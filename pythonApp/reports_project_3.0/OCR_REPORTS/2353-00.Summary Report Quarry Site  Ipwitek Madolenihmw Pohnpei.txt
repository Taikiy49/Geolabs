PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our work on the project was to obtain samples of the
rock materials encountered at the site and to provide laboratory testing
pertaining to the potential development of the subject site as a quarry
site The general location of the site and its vicinity is shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1
The scope of our exploration consisted of the following tasks and
work efforts
1 Mobilization of a portable drill rig with operator to the project
site from our office in Saipan
2 Drilling of four 4 borings at the project site to obtain NX
size core samples of the underlying rock material for laboratory
testing
3 Securing bulk samples of near surface soils at locations directed
by a representative of the Pohnpei Transportation Authority
4 Review of available geological data pertinent to the project site
and its vicinity
5 Laboratory testing of the rock cores and bulk samples to provide
data pertaining to their suitability for use as base course and
select borrow fill material

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Circumferential Road Paving Project is the upgrade of existing
roads and construction of new roads to provide a 40 foot roadway
connecting the five 5 municipalities of the island of Pohnpei in the
Federated States of Micronesia The first phase of this project is the
upgrade of the four 4 miles of gravel road from the bridge at Dau Sokele
to the vicinity of Alamoar in the northeastern portion of the island
As part of this first phase it is desired to develop a quarry site
to provide raw material for the manufacture of base course and aggregate
A potential site in the vicinity of the village of Ipwitek was selected by
the staff of the Pohnpei Transportation Authority This site has been
referred to as the Swingley Site

SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed quarry site is located in the vicinity of the village of
Ipwitek in Madolenihmw Municipality on the island of Pohnpei The
proposed quarry site is situated to the south of the existing gravel
Circumferential Road and is a ridge between two intermittent streams
The topography of the site is generally steep and slopes down towards
the south at an average of about four horizontal to one vertical 4H 1V
The interior of the site is relatively rugged and is traversed by several
intermittent drainage courses
The site is lushly vegetated with Kelew
breadfruit bananas vines and other tropical vegetation There are also
three 3 existing Pohnpeian residences on the site
A topographic survey plan of the site was not available at the time
of our exploration however it is estimated that ground surface
elevations at the site range from about 10 to 300 feet above Mean Sea
Level
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
There is little available published information of the geology of the
island of Pohnpei In general the origin of the island appears to be
similar to that of other high islands in the Pacific e g the Hawaiian
Archipelago Samoa and Truk
The island was built by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from the
vents of one or more shield volcanoes It appears that the island went
through a cycle of the early extrusion of primitive olivine basalts
followed by a sustained period of erosion Subsequent to this erosional
period volcanic activity was renewed with the extrusion of more siliceous
alkalic basalts
GEOLABS MICRONESIA

At Ipwitek our exploration encountered a well consolidated alluvial
conglomerate which has been intruded by basaltic dikes This conglomerate
appears to date to the erosional period between the two main stages of
volcanic activity
DISCUSSION AND FINDINGS
In general our exploration encountered material at relatively
shallow depths below the existing surface at the proposed quarry site
which could potentially be exploited as a source of aggregate fill Based
on the data obtained from the borings and from seismic refraction surveys
presented in an earlier report the material should generally be rippable
with a large bulldozer such as a Caterpillar D 9 However the
conglomerate observed in outcrops on the site was relatively massive and
some blasting may be needed to expedite excavation of the material
Blasting may also be required in the deepest cut since the hardness of the
overburden appears to increase with depth
We anticipate that the material will experience a volume increase
when excavated and crushed to yield base course and or aggregate fill
For preliminary estimating purposes a factor of 1 1 may be used to
estimate the volume of material yielded by the excavation and processing
of a unit volume of rock from the quarry site
Laboratory Testing
Laboratory tests were performed on five 5 bulk samples obtained at
locations directed by a representative of Pohnpei Transportation Authority
and on rock cores obtained from our borings The rock cores were crushed
to aggregate size for the testing
A total of five 5 bulk samples were tested Generally the soils
could be grouped into two 2 types

Samples S 1 through S 4 inclusive may be described as tannish brown
silty clays with some gravel These soils showed relatively good CBR
test ASIM Test Method D 1883 values generally ranging from about
7 5 to 11 Sample S 2 showed a value of 21 which was higher than
anticipated for this type of soil The swell values measured in the
CBR tests ranged from less than 1 0 to 4 2 percent Generally these
clay soils should not be used within two 2 feet of the proposed
roadway subgrade
Sample S 5 is a grayish tan brown silty coral sand and gravel
mixture This material showed a high CBR value of 35 when tested in
accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1883 It is our opinion that this
material would provide an excellent subbase course for use below the
base course and surface course of an asphalt pavement section The
coral material would also be a good source of select fill material
for use in the underwater fills along the shore or in marsh areas
Rock Core Samples
Laboratory tests were performed on rock core samples from Borings 3
and 4 These borings encountered massive dark gray to black
conglomerate composed of basaltic gravel and cobbles in a matrix of
basaltic sand The core samples were broken to aggregate size for
the testing
Los Angeles Abrasion Tests showed an abrasion wear of about 17
percent after 500 revolutions The standards used by the local
Public Works agencies in Hawaii allow a maximum abrasion wear of 50
percent for aggregate base course Based on the L A Abrasion Test
the conglomerate is suitable for use as base course material
Sulfate Soundness testing in accordance with ASIM Test Method C 88 76
showed losses on the order of 39 to 48 percent Generally this high
range is not acceptable for aggregate materials However it appears

that the saturated sulfate solution used in the test chemically
attacks the binding cement of the conglomerate causing the rock to
revert to being a gravelly sand Sulfate soundness tests are
generally performed on base course materials to evaluate the
resistance of the material to cyclical freezing and thawing
Therefore for the climatic conditions anticipated in Pohnpei it is
our opinion that sulfate soundness of pavement materials is not
considered to be of great importance and more reliability should be
given to the results of the abrasion tests
However it should be understood that while the conglomerate is
strong and shows good agrasion resistance the rock is not Grade A
basalt and that the binding cement is still subject to breakdown due
to chemical or other means It is believed that there may be
sufficient chemical activity in the marshes mangroves and tidal
flats along the alignment to limit the use of the conglomerate as
underwater surge rock or boulder fill in these areas It appears
that it is preferable to crush the conglomerate to aggregate base
course size and use it as select fill or base course in a confined
condition

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by the
drilling and sampling of four 4 borings to depths ranging between
26 5 and 40 0 feet below the existing ground surface at the
approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 Portable
hand carried drilling equipment was utilized to drill the borings
This equipment was mobilized to Pohnpei from our Saipan office
Samples of the rock material encountered at the proposed
quarry site were obtained using diamond core drilling techniques
Core drilling is a rotary drilling method which uses a hollow bit
to cut into the rock formation The rock material left in the
annulus of the bit is mechanically recovered for examination
classification and testing The recovered core were logged by an
Engineer or Geologist from our firm Descriptions of the recovered
rock cores are presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 1 through
A 4 2
Recovery REC is used as a_ subjective guide to the
interpretation of the relative quality of rock masses Recovery
is defined as the actual length of material recovered from a coring
attempt vs the length of the core attempt e g if 3 7 feet of
material is recovered from a 5 0 foot core run the recovery would
be 74 and would be shown on the Boring Logs as REC 74
The Rock Quality Designation RQD is also a subjective guide
to the relative quality of rock masses RQD is defined as the
percentage of the core run that is sound material that is in excess
of four 4 inches in length without any discontinuities
discounting any drilling induced fractures or breaks if 2 5 feet
of sound material is recovered from a 5 0 foot core run the RQD
would be 50 and would be shown on the Boring Logs as REC 50
Generally the following is used to describe the relative quality
based on the Practical Handbook of Physical Properties of Rocks
and Minerals
