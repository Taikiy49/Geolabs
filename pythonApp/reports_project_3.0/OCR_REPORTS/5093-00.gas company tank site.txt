In accordance with our fee proposal dated we have recently

on the project and presents our geotechnical engineering recommendations pertaining

Project Considerations
The proposed project involves the installation of a Liquefied Petroleum Gas
propane Storage Facility at Kawaihae Harbor on the Island of Hawaii The site is located
on the eastern side of the cargo expansion area and at the northeastern side of the liquid
bulk terminals The storage facility will include fifteen 70 000 gallon horizontal pressure
vessels installed in a phased manner to meet market demands The site will be filled
about 5 feet to raise it above the flood level
Site Conditions
The project site is located along Kawaihae Road at the southeastern end of the
Kawaihae Harbor facility Access to the site is via a dirt road from Kawaihae Road Other
dirt roads cross the interior of the site The northeastern portion of the site is flat and clear
and is currently being used as a material storage yard and parking area The ground
surface in this area is approximately 6 to 8 feet above Mean Sea Level The remainder of
the site is undulating with a moderate growth of trees A drainage ditch crosses the site
from east to west

Subsurface Conditions
The subsoil conditions in the flat portions of the site were explored by excavating
and sampling four test pits ranging in depths from about 8 to 9 5 feet below the existing
ground surface Additionally two shallow hand dug test pits were made in areas not
accessible to the backhoe to obtain near surface soil samples The approximate locations
of the test pits are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Our field exploration generally encountered about 4 feet of fill consisting of medium
dense silty sand and sandy silt over variable fill consisting of reddish to dark brown clayey
silt with boulders cobbles tree branches and other organic material The fills were
underlain by boulders at about 8 to 9 5 feet below the ground surface the full depth of the
test pits Groundwater was encountered at about 6 5 to 8 5 feet below the existing ground
surface Groundwater levels will vary with tides rainfall and other factors
Detailed descriptions of the materials encountered in the test pits are presented on
the Logs of Test Pits Plates A 1 and A 2 Results of the laboratory tests performed on
selected soils samples are presented on Plate B 1
Discussion and Recommendations
The field exploration indicated that most of the area below the LPG tank site is
underlain by miscellaneous fill which are marginal for support of the new tanks In
particular the existing fill layer encountered below about 4 foot depth was observed to
be soft and contain boulders cobbles and organic materials Over excavation and
re compaction of the existing fill material is recommended
Detailed discussion of these items and our geotechnical engineering design
recommendations are presented in the following sections of this report
Site Grading
The preliminary grading plan indicates that the mass grading work will generally
consist of cuts and fills less than 5 feet high Items of grading that are addressed in the
following subsections include 1 Site Preparation 2 Fills and Backfills

Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork areas within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed Vegetation debris deleterious materials old
foundations and other unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of
properly off site or stockpiled in a designated area to reduce the potential for
contamination of the excavated materials
Stockpiles of soil and construction debris were noted within the project site The
construction debris and deleterious material within the stockpiles should be
removed and disposed of properly off site
The area within the proposed tank storage should be excavated to a depth of
5 feet below the existing ground surface The materials excavated should be
screened of boulders and deleterious material and stockpiled for later use as
backfill The bottom of the excavation should be proof rolled with a 10 ton roller
Soft and yielding areas encountered during the proof rolling should be
over excavated to expose firm material and the resulting excavation should be
backfilled with well compacted engineered fill The excavated soft soils may be
used as fill provided that it meets the requirements for fill material
Prior to filling the existing ground should be scarified to a depth of 10 inches and
moisture conditioned to about 2 percent above the optimum moisture and
compacted to a minimum of 90percent relative compaction Relative
compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of
the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1557
Fills and Backfills
The general fill material should consist of low expansive materials less than
6 inches in largest dimension with a California Bearing Ratio CBR swell of less
than 1 percent The fill material should also be free of vegetation and deleterious
materials The fill material below 5 feet from the finished subgrade may consist
of larger sized material 12 inches in largest dimension provided that the
material is well graded In general the on site silty clay clayey silt materials
should be suitable for fill material provided the materials larger than 6 inches are
removed
Structural fill and imported fill should consist of non expansive granular material
such as crushed coral mudrock basalt or cinder sand The material should be
well graded from coarse to fine with no particles larger than 3 inches in largest
dimension and should contain between 10 and 30 percent particles passing the
No 200 sieve The material should have a laboratory CBR value of 20 or more

Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
Fills and backfills should be moisture conditioned to about 2 percent above the
optimum moisture placed in level lifts not exceeding 10 inches in loose
thickness and compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction
Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a
percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in
accordance with ASTM D 1557 test procedures Optimum moisture is the water
content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
Compaction should be accomplished with sheepsfoot rollers vibratory rollers
multiple wheel pneumatic tired rollers or other types of acceptable compaction
equipment
Because moisture conditioning and compaction of the clayey subgrade soils are
critical elements of earthwork observations and soil density tests should be
performed by Geolabs Inc during site grading to assist the contractor in
obtaining the required degree of compaction and the proper moisture content
Where compaction is less than required additional compactive effort should be
applied with adjustment of moisture content as necessary to obtain the specified
compaction It should be noted that the moisture requirement of the fills and
subgrades about 2 percent above the optimum moisture is an important
requirement for the use of the on site clayey soils
Fill Slopes
Fill slopes should be designed with a slope inclination of two horizontal to
one vertical 2H 1V or flatter Fills placed on slopes steeper than 5H 1V such
as at the gullies should be keyed and benched into the existing slope to provide
stability of the new fill against sliding The filling operations should start at the
lowest point and continue up in level horizontal compacted layers in accordance
with the above fill placement recommendations
Fill slopes in excess of 5 feet in height should be constructed by overfilling and
cutting back to the design slope ratio to obtain a well compacted slope face
Track rolling of slopes in excess of 5 feet in height should not be accepted In
the event that over cutting of a slope occurs keying and benching requirements
should be implemented instead of backfilling the slope to the design grade with

Limitations
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part
upon information obtained from the field test pits Variations of conditions between and
beyond the test pits may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not
become evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will
be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations presented in this report
The boring locations in the field were determined by taping from structures
indicated on the Topographic Survey Map prepared by Control Point Surveying Inc
dated The elevations of the borings were estimated from the same
plan The locations and elevations of the test pits should be considered accurate only
to the degree implied by the methods used
The stratification breaks shown on graphic representations of the test pits depict
the approximate boundaries between soil rock types and as such may denote a
gradual transition Water level data from the borings were measured at the times
shown on the graphic representations and or presented in the text of this report These
data have been reviewed and interpretations made in the formulation of this report
However it must be noted that fluctuation may occur due to variation in seasonal
rainfall and other factors
