This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations and testing
services performed during the mass grading of Mehana at Kapolei Phase 4 project from
May 12 2014 to May 6 2015 in accordance with our fee proposal dated May 6 2014
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Mehana at Kapolei development Mehana is a mixed use residential
subdivision complex bounded by Renton Road to the south Ft Barrette Road to the east
and James Campbell Estate residential development to the north and west Phase 4 is a
30 acre parcel located at the mid western portion of Mehana It is bordered by Wakea
Street on the east and Kama aha Boulevard to the west Phase 4 is divided into 2 grading
areas namely Phase 4A and 4B which are located south and north of the Road A
alignment respectively as shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
Our monitoring services during mass grading consisted of field observations
including surface probing and field moisture and density determinations to confirm the
adequacy of compaction and competency of prepared subgrade fills and backfills within
the limits of grading as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
MASS GRADING
The mass grading of Mehana at Kapolei Phase 4 consisted of clearing and
grubbing subgrade preparations cut and fill operation with cuts on the order of up to
about 6 feet and fills of up to 7 feet On site cut clayey silt and coralline materials were
used as fill to raise the lower portions of the site to design grade

The fill materials were moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture content
placed and spread in level loose lifts not exceeding 10 inches in thickness and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density MDD as
determined in accordance with ASTM D1557 Samples of the materials utilized as fill
were obtained and transported to our laboratory for testing The maximum dry densities
and optimum moisture contents were established in general accordance with ASTM
D1557 Modified Proctor The test results are summarized on Plate 3
Periodic field moisture content and density tests were performed on compacted
subgrade and fills in general accordance with ASTM D6938 Nuclear Gauge Method
The test results are summarized on Plates 4 1 through 4 6 Test locations and
elevations were referenced in the field from on site survey stakes delineating a 100 foot
interval grid pattern as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 These locations and
elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods
used Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the measured relative
compaction conformed to or exceeded the minimum specified for the project
Our services consisted of full time and part time field observation of the
contractor s Royal Contracting Company Ltd subgrade preparation fill and backfill
placement and compaction procedures and confirmation of subgrade soil conditions
exposed during the above site grading operations
Based on our field representative s observations and the referenced test results
it is our opinion that the mass grading for Phase 4 of the Mehana at Kapolei
Development project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this

HOUSE CONSTRUCTION CONSIDERATIONS
The finished grades adjacent to the structures should be sloped to shed water
away from the foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also advised to
install gutter systems around the house structures to collect and discharge water away
from the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering within 3 feet of
the foundations and slabs should be avoided Planters next to foundations should be
avoided or have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the
subsoils
D R Horton Schuler Division

These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
building foundations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation and
subsequent loss of strength of the near surface soils The foundation excavations
should be properly backfilled against the footings immediately after setting of the
concrete to reduce water infiltration Drainage swales should be provided as soon as
possible and should be maintained to drain surface run off away from foundations
Landscaping of the house lots by the homeowners is recommended to reduce the
potential for erosion and for altering the drainage patterns
