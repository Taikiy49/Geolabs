As requested we have made a study of the near surface soil conditions
below the above referenced lot The purpose of the study was to eva
luate the suitability of the near surface soils as foundation support
for the proposed house The investigation consisted of excavating
three 3 test pits within the proposed building site perf orming
laboratory soil tests and reviewing the grading and compaction test
record on the building site This report summarizes our findings and
recommendations
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed single family residence will consist of a two story
wood framed structure Due to the sloping terrain at the site a
split level design will be utilized

EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
Lot 34 is located in the northwestern portion of the subdivision
development on the southwestern side of Kahena Street The lot is
bounded by Kahena Street on the northeast subdivision lots on the
north and south and the natural valley side slope on the west An
existing concrete drainage ditch is located on the southwestern
upper portion of the lot The lot generally slopes upward from
Kahena Street toward the southwest at about 32 to 39 percent gradient
The elevation of the lot generally varies from about 254 to 310 feet
SUBDIVISION LOT GRADING
Prior to the recent subdivision site grading operation the eastern
front portion of Lot 34 was covered with a large stockpile of soil
and boulders left over from a previous grading operation in the area
During that grading operation the stockpile was removed down to a
depth of 3 feet below the design finished grade and replaced with
compacted available on site soils The available on site soil con
sisted basically of silty clay and clayey silt soils which had been
mixed with some adobe clay pockets from the previous stockpile
The remaining western portion of the lot was outside the limits of
that grading work and was not graded
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our findings indicate that the proposed house is mantled by a layer of
grayish brown expansive clay of about 3 feet thick in the driveway
1 foot thick in the proposed house area TP 3 The expansive clay
surface layer is underlain by generally non expansive mottled orange
and brown weathered conglomerate rock consisting of cemented silty
sand with gravel and cobble deposit along with occasional boulders
All particles appear to have been highly weathered except the layer
particles
The remaining portion of the lot behind the house area is underlain by
natural colluvial deposits transported from the upper hillside area
These deposits are typically heterogenous and vary from stiff clayey
silt low expansion potential to stiff clay high expansion
potential

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that the lot is mantled by a layer of
grayish adobe clay about 0 5 to 3 0 feet thick over weathered rock
The laboratory test results on the adobe clay generally indicated
that it has high expansion potential Therefore for Lot 34 the
foundations should extend through the surface adobe clay layer and
bear on the weathered rock Concrete slab and driveways should bear
on a minimum of 3 and 2 feet respectively of select non expansive
soil or on weathered conglomerate rock
Foundation
All footings should extend a minimum of 12 inches into the weathered
rock formation An allowable bearing value of 4000 p s f may be used
on the weathered conglomerate rock

Slab On Grade
Concrete slab on grade is planned for the garage and storage areas
The preliminary plans indicate that the rear portion of the garage
area will be in cut of upto 5 feet Therefore it appears that the
weathered conglomerate rock would be encountered in the rear portion
of the garage excavation while adobe clay would underlie in the
storage area at the front portion where little or no cuts are planned
It should be noted that the adobe clay soils are highly expansive
and are not suitable as subgrade soils under on grade slab Where
encountered the adobe clays should be over excavated down to
weathered conglomerate rock or for a depth of at least 3 feet below
the slab bottom and then replaced with non expansive soils such as
select borrow crusher waste or coral compacted to a minimum of 90
of its maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78 test method This
over excavation and replacement should also be extended for a distance
of 1 foot beyond the perimeter of the slab
A 4 inch layer of base course should be provided below the slab and
compacted to 90 of its maximum dry density In order to protect
against moisture infiltration a moisture barrier may be incorporated
below the slab

Concrete Driveway and Sidewalks
A layer of more than 3 5 feet of expansive soils was encountered in
the driveway area Since the driveway can tolerate some amount of
heaving the adobe clay over excavation and replacement can be
reduced to a minimum of 2 feet below the driveway slab bottom The
over excavation should be backfilled with select non expansive borrow
material to cap over the expansive clay
In the fill area where less than 2 feet of fill is planned over
excavation and replacement of the expansive clay with select non
expansive material is required to provide at least 2 feet of fill
cap The above capping requirements should be followed for a minimum
distance of 1 foot beyond the driveway edges
Joints are recommended for the driveway at about 8 to 10 foot intervals
in order to allow for some relative movement of the concrete
For future sidewalks capping of the clay with over excavation and
replacement similar to the driveway are recommended to reduce heaving
caused by the expansive clay soil
