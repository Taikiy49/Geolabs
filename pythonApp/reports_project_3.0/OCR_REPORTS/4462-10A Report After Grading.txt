PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Halekuai Center project is located at 565 Farrington Highway at the corner of
Kapouka Place in Kapolei on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project involved the
construction of a two story retail office building with a footprint of approximately
20 000 square feet In addition a one story Chevron gasoline station convenience store
building and a car wash facility were also constructed as part of the site development
Based on the grading plans and our field observations site grading work for the project
construction generally consisted of minor cuts and fills to achieve the design finished
grades for the project New retaining walls were constructed to provide grade separation
along the perimeter of the site In addition new asphaltic concrete paved driveway and
parking areas were constructed to serve the new facility The layout of the new
construction is shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
Based on the laboratory swell test results the stiff clayey fill materials at the site
may be classified as moderately to highly expansive when subjected to fluctuations in
the moisture content As an alternate to the conventional removal and replacement
alternative chemical stabilization using the Electrochemical Soil Stabilizer EcSS 3000
was used to stabilize the expansive soils within the footprint of the two story retail office
building Halekuai Center Geotechnical engineering observations were performed by

EARTHWORK OPERATIONS AND BUILDING CONSTRUCTION
The earthwork operations for the Halekuai Center site were performed by Jayar
Construction Inc In general the site grading operations consisted of clearing and
grubbing and placement and compaction of fills Based on the grading plans and our field
observations minor cuts and fills were required to achieve the finished grades for the
project In general the on site soils generated from the cut areas were used in fill areas to
raise the existing ground to the finished grades
Samples of the on site and imported materials utilized as fill and backfill materials
were obtained and transported to our laboratory for testing The maximum dry densities
and optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general accordance
with American Society of Testing and Material ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified
Proctor The test results are summarized on Plate 2 of this report
Following clearing and grubbing operations the site was graded to near the
finished subgrade elevations for the building The finished subgrades within the Halekuai
Center footprint were stabilized by using Electrochemical Soil Stabilizer EcSS 3000 as
discussed above Detailed findings from our post treatment evaluation program should be
referred to the afore mentioned report dated The ground within the
Chevron site was generally over excavated where necessary to approximately 18 inches
below the finished subgrades in the building areas to provide the recommended 18 inch
thick non expansive select granular fill layer The over excavated subgrades were
moisture conditioned to about 2 percent above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to at least 90 percent of the maximum dry density as determined in
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor
During placement of the non expansive select granular fill layer the fill materials
were generally placed in near level lifts brought to above the optimum moisture
content and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent of the maximum dry density
determined in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor
During the foundation excavation and preparation for both the Halekuai Center and

Utility trench backfills for the project were generally placed in near level lifts

Periodic field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations
to confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures and soil

performed are summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 15 of this report These tests were
performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills at the
site
Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from

should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas
assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction
was obtained and or approved by our field representative based on visual observation
and hand probing with a pointed steel rod
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the

for the project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report were
performed in general conformance with the intent of the project plans and specifications
