PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of the soil engineering investigation was to
explore the subsurface soil and groundwater conditions at
the site to provide data for use in the design of the site
grading for the proposed condominium development

W O 966 00 P 2
The foundation design recommendations for the proposed
high rise and parking facilities will be contained in a
separate foundation engineering investigation report
The results of our field exploration and laboratory
tests which were used in the engineering analysis
that formulated our conclusion and recommendations
are presented in the appendix of this report
The approximate boring locations are shown on the Site
Plans Plates and the boring logs are presented in
Appendix A Information regarding the field exploration
and laboratory testing are also presented in the appendices
Boring data from a previous preliminary soil report by
Walter Lum Associates dated was also
used in our study
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations

SITE CONDITIONS
The proposed site is an arc shaped strip of land covering
about 18 5 acres located adjacent to and north upslope
of the Middle Loch Shoreline It is bounded on the west
and north by Waipio Point Access Road and Waipahu High
School respectively
A dirt road with several utility easements is situated
along the southern waterfront boundary of the site
bordering Middle Loch Additional lowlying farm lands
are located east of the project site A jet fuel line
crosses through the central upper portion of the Site
below an existing dirt road

CONCLUSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration and laboratory tests indicate rela
tively competent soil conditions in the upper proposed
cut areas of the site Normal site grading methods can
be used in this area
Soft soil conditions and high water table were encountered
in the lower portions of the site near Pearl Harbor
Special stabilization of the soft ground would be required
prior to site filling Ground settlement due to the
weight of the new fill should be anticipated in this area
Surcharging is recommended in this area to decrease the
waiting time for the settlements to occur and future
differential settlements The approximate extent of the
soft areas requiring stabilization is estimated and shown
on the Site Plan Plates 2 3

Site Preparation
The site should be cleared of vegetation trash
debris and other deleterious material In areas
to receive fill the ground should be scarified
to a minimum depth of 6 inches brought to near
optimum moisture content and compacted to a mini
mum of 90 density
Prior to site clearance and surface vegetation
removal in this wet ground off site drainage
water should be diverted where possible around
the lowlying swamp area to permit the existing
soft area to develop a firmer soil crust The
existing drain culverts should be cleared to
improve drainage of the surface water out of the
Site
The existing artesian water wells should be properly
capped and sealed

Fill Placement
After initial site preparation the fill should be
placed in maximum 10 inch lifts compacted to a mini

Fills placed over the soft marshy areas should be
placed at slow rates to avoid squeezing or failure
in the soft subsoils An initial estimated rate of
1 feet per day is recommended The actual rate
should be determined in the field based on the settle
ment gauge readings
The on site cut material from the upper areas are sui

Relocation of Jet Fuel Line
The existing Navy jet fuel line runs across the middle
of the project site Prior to site grading in the upper
area this fuel line will be relocated along the lower
project boundary near the north side of the existing
waterfront dirt road and utility corridor bordering
Pearl Harbor
The present grading plan indicates that 2 to 4 feet of
new fill is planned along this relocated line area to
raise the finish grade to elevation 5 to 7 feet Several
inches of settlement are anticipated due to the weight
of this new fill over the soft soils which are indicated
in Borings 27 and 37 as well as previous borings drilled
along this lower area extending down to 8 to 16 feet
below the existing ground surface Therefore it is
recommended that the fill within about 20 feet of the
new pipe in this area be filled first and be allowed to
settle prior to the pipe construction
GEOLABS HAWATTI
W O 966 00 P 19
Settlement gauges are recommended along the pipe align
ment to monitor the settlement rate and to determine
when pipe construction can begin It is anticipated
that about 3 to 6 months would be required before the
settlement rates reduce to tolerable levels to allow
construction of the pipeline
Surcharge fill is not recommended over this pipe align
ment since it may cause additional settlement and squee
zing of the soft subsoils and possible damage to the
adjacent existing utility lines including a major Stan
dard Oil line
In order to provide uniform bearing support for the pipe
and working platform over the soft subsoils we recommend
that the relocated jet fuel line be placed over a cushion
bedding layer consisting of 24 inches of 3B Fine gravel
over Mirafi 140 filter fabric or equivalent see Plate7
Existing Utility Corridor
Several major underground utility lines are located in

road bordering Pearl Harbor Our borings indicated
that this area is underlain by thick deposits of soft
subsoils Therefore it is important that additional
fill should be avoided in this lowlying area to minimize
additional ground settlement and subsequent damage of
the underlying utility lines

New Culvert Structures

any new fill over the utility corridor alignment should
be kept to an absolute minimum to minimize excessive
future settlement of the area Based on the boring data
it appears that the soft material in these areas may
extend down to about elevation 4 feet

soil as a potential source of fill material two ASTM D 1557 tests

undisturbed samples to evaluate the strength characteristics of the

APPENDIX A
FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored
by drilling 38 borings to depths of 21 5 to 105 feet below
the existing ground surface at the approximate locations
shown on the Site Plans Plates
The scope of the drilling work was reduced from our
original proposal However to avoid confusion the ori
ginal boring numbers were retained therefore some of the
intermediate boring numbers were not drilled at this time
The soils encountered in the borings were classified
by visual and textural examination in the field by our
engineering geologist who continuously monitored the dril
ling operations The classifications were reviewed by visual
inspection and testing in the laboratory The soils were
classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A graphic presentation of the soils
is presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 68
In the borings soil samples were obtained by driving
split barrel sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30
inches or a thin wall tube pushed with hydraulic pressure
The blow counts to drive the sampler the last 12 inches
are shown on the boring logs at the appropriate depths
