Renovation Work for Mall Slab at

In accordance with your request and our proposal of October 28
1985 we have performed a geotechnical investigation for the above
referenced project The approximate location of the site is shown on the
Project Location Map Plate l
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
W O 1625 00 Page 2
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this investigation was to explore the subsoil
condition below the existing damaged concrete mall area to evaluate the
possible cause of the slab damage and provide recommendations for
remedial work
The scope of the investigation included drilling and sampling seven
7 borings testing of the samples oodtained engineering evaluation of
the field and laboratory data development of design recommendations and
report preparation
Detailed description of our field exploration log of borings and
laboratory test results are presented in the appendices of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATION
The existing concrete paved mall area which is about 25 feet wide
by 500 feet suffers visible cracking damage in the slab due to some
degree of ground movement One major crack appears at approximately one
third from the north end of the mall with some minor cracks more or less
running along the longitudinal direction of the mall It is our
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 1625 00 Page 3
understanding that the cracking of the mall concrete slab began soon
after the construction of the pavement which started in 1969
It also appears that ground subsidence may have occurred near the
drain line along the mall length
It is also our understanding that several attempts had been made to
patch the damaged concrete slab in the past
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is a result of the reclamation of the former Salt
Lake See Plate 1 It is believed that site filling was done about 16
years ago in at least two stages At present it is an open and level
area paved by 5 inch thick concrete pavement and surrounded by two story
classroom library and administration buildings On the south side of
the mall there is a large A C paved parking area See Plate 2
The finished floor elevations are at 2 and 22 2 feet
based on old plan data of 1969 for the west north and east side
buildings respectively The finished floor elevation is at 26 5 feet
for the mall slab as well as the Administration Building
The paved mall is generally level The ground at the edge of the
mall slab slopes up toward the western buildings at 1 to 5 gradients

and slopes down toward the eastern building at 0 to 5 gradients At
locations there are vertical retaining walls separating the mall slab
from the eastern classroom buildings The building and mall are
connected by either sloping walkways or stairways There are five 5
local drain inlets located near the western edge along the length of the
mall which is adjacent to either a grass lawn or a concrete sidewalk
The condition of the mall slab is fair However there are some
major crack patterns See Plate 2
Substantial subsidence of the ground surface may have occurred as
indicated by the uneven appearance of the concrete slabs located near the
northern end of the mall A portion of the slab was noticed to be at a
heaved position at the time of the field exploration causing a crack
across the width of the mall at approximately one third from the
northern end of the mall
SUBSURFACE CONDITION
The subsurface conditions of the damaged concrete slab were
explored by drilling and sampling to a depth of 3 5 to 8 0 feet below the
existing ground surface
Seven 7 borings to depths of 3 5 to 8 0 feet were drilled The
approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The subsoil conditions at the site as indicated by our borings
GEOLABS HAWAIii
W O 1625 00 Page 6
generally consist of clayey soils which are potentially expansive when
wetted Structures resting on these clays CH soils may be subjected to
shrink swell movements
Although most of the slab cracking may be attributed to heaving of
the clayey soils it appears that the apparent movements of the adjacent
buildings have also resulted in some slab cracking
Damages to the mall slab in the southern half of the mall is
believed to be caused by settlement of the underlying soil under the
Administration Building which was rested on 2 to 4 feet of fill
We believe damages to the mall slab in the northern part may be
related to possible subsidence of footings of the Classroom Building c
facing the mall
We suggest that investigation of the structural and foundation
conditions in the Administration Building and Classroom Building C be
conducted to evaluate and to stabilize the building movements prior to
final repair of the mall slab

Causes and Recommended Methods of Remedy
Causes of Damage to the Mall Slab
Most of the observed slab cracking appeared to be a result of
heaving of the lightly loaded concrete slab and sidewalk in the
mall area caused by heaving of the underlying clayey material below
the upper 1 5 to 2 0 feet thick select sandy fill layer which was
placed below the concrete slab Laboratory tests indicated that
the swelling potential of these clayey soils might be classified as
moderate to high To simulate the existing concrete slab and 1 5
to 2 0 feet of non expansive sandy soil presently overlying the
clay laboratory swell tests were conducted with surcharge loads of
220 p s f These test results indicated that there may still be a
potential for some additional heaving A comparison of the in situ
moisture content 25 to 40 with the saturated moisture content
measured in the swell tests 40 to 45 also indicate a potential
for some additional heaving due to continued moisture infiltration
and wetting
Clayey soils obtained from Boring 3 experienced 12 to 18 percent
swelling while the others experienced anywhere from 0 to 6 percent
swelling See Plate B 4 in Appendix
GEOLABS HAWAIli
W O 1625 00 Page 8
Some of the observed cracks may be attributted to movement of the
adjacent buildings We believe that the longitudinal cracks on the
mall slab near the Administration Building could be caused by the
movement of foundation material located underneath and around the
footing and the wall
This movement could be a result of creeping of the foundation
material which was initiated perhaps in the wet season by
absorption of water resulting in a combination of swelling and
settlement We do not have sufficient information on the
foundation construction and soil conditions under the
Administration Building to determine the cause of movement
However judging from the cracking of the walls and movements of
the slabs in the Administration Building and in the mall slab it
leads us to believe that this movement could affect the nearby mall
slab and therefore a soil investigation should be conducted to
further evaluate this problem
The movement of footings under the classroom Building C perhaps
create an entirely different problem since Building C is located
at higher elevation Subsidence of the footings in this building
is perhaps due to the poor drainage resulting in uneven
settlement This uneven settlement causes rotation and twisting
effects to the mall slabs not to mention its own footings and
GEOLABS HAWAIii
W O 1625 00 Page 9
columns Unless the drainage is improved and the footing
subsidence conditions have been investigated and corrected we
believe the correctional measures applied to the mall slab at this
time could be only temporarily effective
Method of Remedy
A A NN hm mame
To prevent further deterioration of the existing damage conditions
immediate correction should be carried out
1 In order to reduce the heaving of the underlying clay soil
remedial work should concentrate on improving the drainage
around the mall area to minimize infiltration and saturation
of the underlying clay Drainage improvements should consist
of new concrete surface ditches and subdrains designed to
intercept and drain away surface and shallow subsurface water
Surface runoff from the mall area should be collected and
drained to a suitable discharge point Water should not be
allowed to pond immediately adjacent to the slab areas
Investigation of surface drainage water collection and removal
from the mall surface and for interception and collection of
water flowing from adjacent areas should be made If
plugged cleaning up the plugged drains or additional
provision for drainage should be considered
GEOLABS HAWAIIi
W O 1625 00 Page 10
2
In order to minimize the build up of water beneath the
pavement and subsequent softening of the subgrade soils a
subdrainage system at the subgrade level is recommended around
the mall slab area to intercept and drain subsurface water A
sketch of a typical subdrainage system is shown on Plate 3
In the sample plate an example of grating drainage is also
given to supplement the current surface water collection on
the mall slab
Additional reduction of the heaving of the soil may be
achieved by complete removal of the mall slab and construction
of a thicker layer of non expansive soil over the expansive
clay to increase the surcharge pressure over the clay The
laboratory swell tests indicate that a 3 to 4 foot thick layer
of non expansive soil may have enough weight to resist the
swelling pressures of the clay soil However due to the
large expense involved in the complete removal of the mall
slab as well as the anticipated construction difficulty due to
the many existing underground utilities this may not be a
practical solution
An alternate procedure may be to install the recommended
drainage systems and utilize only partial removal of the mall
slab with the understanding that some additional movement may
GEOLABS HAWAii
Page 11
occur The new slab should be reinforced with wire mesh and
be dowelled into the existing slab The original subgrade
should not be allowed to dry out before placing the new slab
We feel that daily sprinkling of water may be helpful in
maintaining the present moist condition
Any existing or new cracks should be immediately filled with a
flexible sealant to minimize the infiltration of water into
the subsoils A maintenance program should be planned and
executed to monitor and to seal off any new cracks that might
occur in the future
3 We believe that a practical overall remedy to the mall slab
distress should include investigation into the cause s of
structural damage as well as measures to provide additional
support to the Administration Building and Classroom Building
C to eliminate their effect on the slab performance
Design Review
Plans and specifications for site grading and additional drainage
system should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written
comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our recon
mendations is included
GEOLABS HAWAIi

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the excavation backfilling pavement
placing and grading operations be observed by the soils engineer during
construction to see that adequate compaction has been obtained and that
the subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of our
investigation and that the drainage pattern will help the subsurface
soils at the mall from absorbing the water
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
If conditions encountered during construction are different from
those presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made

Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling
seven 7 borings at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2 A truck mounted drill rig was used to drill the borings on the
level area of the site
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by visual and
textural examination in the field by our engineer who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory The soils
were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A graphic presentation of the materials
encountered is presented on the Log of Borings Plates A 1 through A 7
The soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving a 2 inch
standard split spoon sampler or a 2 4 inch I D split barrel sampler with
a 140 pound hammer free falling 30 inches The blow counts to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the log of borings at the
appropriate sample depths
