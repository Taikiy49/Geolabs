REMARKS It was brought to our attention that unstable fill materials were exposed
recently at the project site The area in question is located along the mauka
side of the northbound lanes between approximately and
We understand the fill materials were placed previously as part of the initial
construction of the existing highway Based on our observations the
existing fill is up to about 12 feet in vertical height Several cracks were
observed along the top of the slopes indicating the unstable or poorly
compacted nature of the existing fill materials The cracks observed were
up to about 8 feet laterally behind the existing top of slope The exposed
surface of the fill slope also appeared to be loose based on visual
observations We understand that an existing water line is present in this
area with an invert elevation of about 3 feet below the existing ground
surface
Based on our observations we recommend the unstable fill materials be
removed and backfilled with compacted structural fill Due to the presence
of the existing water line care should be exercised so as not to damage or
disturb the water line In general the over excavation should extend past
the limits of the cracked ground surface In addition the over excavation
below the water line invert elevation should stay about 4 feet laterally away
from the center of the existing water line A typical sketch of the
recommended remedial measure is provided on the following page
The actual limits of over excavation should be evaluated in the field based
on the exposed subsurface conditions The over excavation and backfill
operations should be conducted in presence of a Geolabs representative
