INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Administration Building and Library located within the
existing Kanoelani Elementary School near the Waipio area in the District of Ewa on
the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are
shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings from our field exploration and laboratory
testing and presents our geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our
analyses for the proposed project These recommendations are intended for design
input only and are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed project site is located within the existing Kanoelani Elementary
School off Oli Loop in the Waipio area on the island of Oahu Hawaii The proposed
project site is bounded by a parking lot and a dining facility to the north an office
building to the east Building F and two portable trailers to the south and landscaped
area to the west
It is proposed to construct a one story administration building and a library on the
approximately acre vacant lot We understand that the library will be located to the
east of the proposed administration building as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 Based
on the current design concept the buildings will consist generally of concrete and
masonry bearing walls with concrete slab on grade construction We understand that
column loads on the order of about 10 kips and wall loads on the order of about 3 kips
per lineal foot are anticipated
The topographic survey indicates that the existing ground elevations of the
project site range from about 356 feet on the east to 360 feet Mean Sea Level MSL

on the west The proposed finish floor elevations are planned at 357 5 feet MSL for
the library and 360 8 feet MSL for the administration building
Detailed grading plans for the project were not available at the time this report
was prepared In general we envision that site grading will consist of minor cuts and
fils on the order of about 2 feet to provide level building pads for the proposed
construction
Based on our previous experience in the area the project site may be underlain

slabs supported on grade

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from two shield
volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be of
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3449 00 Page 5
the middle to late Pliocene Age and forms the bulk of the western third of the island
The younger shield Koolau is estimated to be of the late Pliocene to early Pleistocene
Ice Age and forms the majority of the eastern two thirds of the island Waianae
became extinct while Koolau was still active and its eastern flank was partially buried
below Koolau lavas banking against its eastern flank These banked or ponded lavas
formed a broad plateau referred to as the Schofield Plateau on which the project site
is located
The project area is composed of basaltic rock built by extrusion of the lavas of
the Koolau Volcanic Series In situ chemical weathering of the Koolau lava flows has
occurred for the last 1 to 2 million years The weathering process has formed a mantle
of residual soils which grade to saprolite with depth In general saprolite is composed
mainly of silty material and is typical of the tropical weathering of volcanic rocks The
saprolite grades to basaltic rock formation with increasing depth

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field exploration the proposed project site is underlain by a
surficial fill layer of about 2 feet thick in some areas and very stiff to very hard residual
soils extending to the maximum depths drilled of about 16 to 21 feet below the
existing ground surface Based on our field exploration and laboratory testing we
believe that a shallow foundation system bearing on the stiff near surface fills and or
very stiff to very hard residual soils may be used to support the proposed administration
building and library structure
Our laboratory test results indicate that the near surface soils generally exhibit
a slight to moderate shrink swell potential when subjected to fluctuations in the moisture
content Therefore we recommend that slabs on grades be supported on a minimum
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3449 00 Page 7
of 6 inches of non expansive fill material to reduce the effects of the shrink swell
potential of the near surface soils In addition special attention should be given to the
subgrade preparation requirements presented in the Slab On Grade section of this
report for proper moisture conditioning and compaction of the near surface soils
Based on the near level topography at the site we anticipate only minor site
grading consisting of cuts and fills on the order of 2 feet or less would be needed to
provide level building pads for the proposed construction In addition shallow
excavations and subsequent backfilling are also anticipated for the utility trenches
Detailed geotechnical engineering recommendations pertaining to the foundation
design of the proposed structures and other geotechnical aspects of the project are
further discussed tn the following sections of this report
Foundations
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered in our exploration we believe
that a shallow foundation system consisting of spread and or continuous footings may
be used to support the proposed administration building and library structure An
allowable bearing pressure of up to 3 000 p s f may be used for the design of footings
bearing on the stiff near surface fills and or residual soils This allowable bearing
pressure is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third 14 for transient
loads such as temporary wind and or seismic forces Footings should be embedded
a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade We estimate that
the total footing settlements under the anticipated design loads to be on the order of
less than 1 inch with differential settlements of approximately inch
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by frictional resistance
between the base of the spread footing and the bearing materials and by passive earth
pressure developed against the near vertical faces of the embedded portion of footings
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3449 00 Page 8
A coefficient of friction of 0 35 may be used between the base of the footings and the
supporting materials Resistance due to passive pressure may be calculated using an
equivalent fluid pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth p c f for
footings embedded in the fills and or residual soils These values assume that the
concrete for footings is placed directly against the footing excavations Unless covered
by pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the upper 12 inches below finished
grade should be neglected
Where footings are to be located adjacent to below grade structures or utilities
the footings should extend to a depth below an imaginary 45 degree plane projected
upward from the bottom of the below grade structure or utility This requirement is
necessary to avoid surcharging adjacent below grade structures with additional
structural loads and to reduce the potential for foundation settlement
Footing excavations should not be allowed to dry before placing concrete If
shrinkage cracks appear in the footing excavations the soil should be thoroughly
moistened to close all cracks and recompacted to provide a firm base prior to concrete
placement
Footing excavations should be observed by a representative of Geolabs Hawaii
prior to the placement of reinforcing steel or concrete to confirm the competency of the
bearing materials and embedment depths If unsuitable materials are encountered in
the footing excavations these materials should be over excavated to expose firm soils
and be replaced with compacted engineered fill
Slab On Grade
We anticipate that concrete slabs on grades will be used for the proposed
buildings at the Kanoelani Elementary School The near surface soils observed at the
proposed project site consist of clayey and silty soils which exhibit slight to moderate
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 3449 00 Page 9
expansion potential when subjected to fluctuations in the moisture content Unless floor
slabs supported by these soils are properly designed there is a potential for future
distress to lightly loaded slabs on grade resulting from the shrinking and swelling of
these soils due to changes in the moisture content
To reduce the potential of structural distress resulting from the swelling of the
slightly to moderately expansive on site soils we recommend that a minimum of
6 inches of non expansive capping fill material be provided below slabs on grade The
non expansive capping fill should extend a minimum of 2 feet laterally beyond the limits
of the proposed building structure
The non expansive capping fill should provide a capping layer of moist soil above
the underlying potentially expansive subgrade soil thereby reducing the loss of
moisture from the clayey or silty subgrade soils In our opinion the reduction in
potential drying followed by subsequent wetting of the subgrade soils should reduce the
distress on the slab due to potential heaving of the underlying expansive clayey soils
when wetted
The non expansive capping fill material should consist of imported granular
materials such as crushed coral basalt gravel or cinder sand The material should be
well graded with no particles greater than 3 inches in size and should have a
laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR value of 12 or higher and a swell value not
exceeding 1 percent when prepared above the optimum moisture The material should
also contain between 10 and 30 percent passing the No 200 sieve
In general we recommend that the capping fill material be placed in level lifts
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to not
less than 90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place
dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3449 00 Page 10
soil determined in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91 Optimum
moisture is the water content percentage by weight corresponding to the maximum
dry density
The non expansive capping fill should be placed as soon as possible after the
scarification and compaction of the underlying subgrade soils The subgrade should
be scarified to a minimum depth of 8 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent
above the optimum moisture content and compacted to at least 90 percent relative
compaction Soil subgrades should be kept moist prior to placement of capping fill
and or concrete If shrinkage cracks appear in the subgrades the soil should be
thoroughly moistened to close all cracks and recompacted prior to capping fill or
concrete placement
In accordance with the requirements of D A G S Technical Memorandum No 61
dated a basaltic termite barrier BTB will need to be incorporated into
the design of new facilities Therefore concrete floor slabs should be supported on a
4 inch layer of compacted BTB above the compacted non expansive capping fill layer
to serve as both a slab cushion and a termite barrier Due to the cohesionless nature
of the BTB we recommend that the BTB be moisture conditioned and compacted with
a vibratory plate or similar equipment
For slabs on grade with moisture sensitive surfacing we recommend that an
impermeable membrane be placed over the compacted BIB to reduce potential
migration of moisture vapor through the concrete slab In order to promote more
uniform curing of the slab concrete and to provide protection of the vapor membrane
we recommend that a 2 inch layer of BTB be placed above the membrane prior to
placing the slab concrete
Where concrete slabs will be subjected to vehicular traffic such as driveways
and loading areas a 6 inch layer of base course aggregate is recommended below the
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3449 00 Page 11
concrete slab to serve as a slab cushion in lieu of the basaltic termite barrier The
base course supporting the concrete slab should consist of crushed basalt aggregates
compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
For structural slabs bearing on compacted base course a modulus of subgrade
reaction of 250 pounds per cubic inch p c i may be used We recommend that the
concrete slab be designed by a structural engineer for the intended loads and should
be reinforced as a structural slab to accommodate some amount of differential
movements The thickened edges of slabs adjacent to any unpaved area should be
embedded at least 12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade
Exterior concrete walkways and access ramps may be required for the proposed
project We recommend that concrete slabs for the walkways and access ramps bear
4 inches of select borrow or 3B Fine gravel ASTM C33 No 67 gradation Where
select borrow is used the select borrow should be compacted to at least 90 percent
relative compaction The on site subgrade soils should be moisture conditioned to at
least 2 percent above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of
90 percent relative compaction Construction joints should be provided at intervals
equal to the width of the walkways with expansion joints at right angle intersections
Site Grading
Final grading plans were not available at the time this report was prepared
However we anticipate minor site grading with cuts and fills on the order of 2 feet
relative to the existing ground surface may be expected for the proposed construction
Site grading operations should be observed by a representative of Geolabs
Hawaii It is important that a representative from our office observe whether the
exposed soil conditions during excavation are similar to those encountered in our
exploration The following recommendations are intended to provide guidelines for the
design of site grading work
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3449 00 Page 12
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable
materials should be removed and properly disposed of off site
After demolition clearing and grubbing slab or footing subgrades and areas to
receive fills should be scarified to a minimum depth of 8 inches moisture conditioned
to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture content and compacted to not less
than 90 percent relative compaction For subgrades of improvements subjected to
vehicular traffic the compaction requirement should be increased to a minimum of 95
percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of
soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil
established in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91 Optimum moisture
is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry
density
Any soft weak or yielding areas disclosed during clearing operations should be
over excavated to expose firm ground and the resulting excavation should be filled with
engineered fill moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture and compacted to
a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Prior to placement of the backfill the
over excavated subgrade should be scarified to a depth of at least 8 inches moisture
conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture content and compacted
to at least 90 percent relative compaction The excavated soft and or organic soils
should be properly disposed of off site
Where shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction we recommend that the soil
be thoroughly moistened to close all cracks Otherwise the subgrades will need to be
scarified again and prepared as recommended above Saturation and subsequent
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3449 00 Page 13
yielding of the exposed subgrade due to inclement weather and poor drainage may
require over excavation of the soft areas and replacement with well compacted
engineered fill
The on site clayey and silty soils generated from the excavation may be reused
as a source of fill and backfill material except for the 6 inch non expansive fill
recommended below slabs on grade The on site silty and clayey soils should be
properly moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture
content placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted
to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
In general permanent fill slopes constructed of the compacted on site or import
fill materials may be designed with a slope inclination of two horizontal to one vertical
2H 1V or flatter The 2H 1V slope inclination may also be used for cut slope
conditions Fills placed on slopes steeper than 5H 1V should be keyed and benched
Where import fill materials are required we recommend that the import fills
consist of non expansive fill material The non expansive fill material should be a well
graded granular material with no particles greater than 3 inches in largest dimension
Import fill should have a laboratory CBR value of 12 or higher and a swell of less than
1 percent when tested in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1883 The
material should also contain between 10 and 30 percent passing the No 200 sieve
The material should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness
and compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction The upper 2 feet of
fill material below the pavements should be compacted to not less than 95 percent
relative compaction

Utility Trench
We envision that new utility lines would be required for the proposed project In
general for support of the utility lines we recommend that a granular bedding
consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation be used
under the pipes The backfill up to about 1 foot above the pipes should consist of free
draining granular materials such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation
to provide adequate support around the pipes and to reduce the compaction effort of
the backfill thus reducing the possibility of damaging the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 1 foot above the pipes to
the top of the subgrade or finished grade may consist of on site material with no
particles greater than 3 inches in maximum size The backfill should be moisture
conditioned to above the optinum moisture content placed in maximum 8 inch level
loose lifts and mechanically compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction
to reduce the potential for future ground subsidence Where trenches are below
pavement areas the upper 2 feet of the trench backfill below the pavement subgrade
should be compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
Drainage
The finish grades outside the buildings should be sloped to shed water away
from foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also advised that gutter
systems be installed around the building and that the discharge be diverted away from
the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near foundations
and slabs should also be avoided Planters next to foundations should be avoided or
have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3449 00 Page 15
saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations
should be properly backfilled against the walls or slab footings immediately after setting
of the concrete to reduce water infiltration
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and should be
maintained to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundations

borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 5 at the approximate locations shown

by driving a 3 inch O D Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling
30 inches The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the last 12 inches of an 18
inch drive are shown on the logs of borings at the appropriate sample depths

Laboratory Testing

samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties The results of
these tests are presented on the logs of borings at the appropriate sample depths
Four one inch Ring Swell tests were performed on relatively undisturbed and
remolded samples of the near surface soils to evaluate the swelling potential of the
soils The results of these tests are summarized on Plate B 1
Two Atterberg Limits tests were performed on selected soil samples to evaluate
their liquid and plastic limits and to aid in soil classification A summary of the test
results is presented on Plate B 2
