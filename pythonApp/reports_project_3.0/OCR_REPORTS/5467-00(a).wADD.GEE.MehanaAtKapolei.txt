2 1 Regional Geology
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lavas from
two shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated
to be middle to late Pliocene in age and Koolau is estimated to be late Pliocene to early
Pleistocene in age
The project site is situated on the Ewa Coastal Plain which extends seaward off
the southeastern flank of Waianae and the southwestern flank of Koolau This plain
was formed by the accumulation of a thick wedge of sediments and coral reef
formations on the flanks of the two volcanoes
During the Pleistocene Epoch sea levels fluctuated in response to the cycles of
continental glaciation As the glaciers grew and advanced less water was available to
fill the oceanic basins such that sea levels fell below the present stands of the sea
When the glaciers melted and receded an excess of water became available such that
the sea levels rose to above the present sea level
The higher sea level stands caused the formation of reef deposits at
correspondingly higher elevations Subaerial exposure of the sediments and
calcareous materials caused consolidation of the lagoonal deposits and induration of
the calcareous reef materials

other unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site or

Soft and yielding areas encountered during clearing and grubbing should be
over excavated to expose firm natural material and the resulting excavation should
be backfilled with well compacted engineered fill The excavated soft soils may be
used as fill provided that it meets the requirements for fill material
Keying and Benching
The ground surface at the project site is nearly level however there may be some
localized areas with steeper slopes Therefore some keying and benching may be
required for earthwork at the site These keys and benches should be firmly seated
into existing ground
At the toe of fills on ground where the existing slope is steeper than five horizontal
to one vertical 5H 1V a keyway should be cut down into the in situ material This
key should be a minimum of 2 feet in depth at the toe of the fill and should be a
minimum of 8 feet in width extending back under the toe of the fill The floor of the
key should be sloped back under the base of the fill at about 10H 1V to bond the fill
into the existing slope
As fill placement progresses up the existing slope benches should be cut into the
existing slope to bond the mass of the fill to the existing ground These benches
should be a minimum of 3 feet in height and approximately 10 feet in width A
schematic section illustrating the keying and benching recommendations is shown
on Plate 3
Based on the site conditions encountered we recommend that prior to the
placement of fill the keys and benches be proof rolled with vibratory sheepsfoot

Fills and Backfills
General fills below 3 feet from finished mass grade level may consist of soil and
rock materials less than 6 inches in size with a CBR value of 8 or more and with a
maximum swell of less than 2 percent when tested in accordance with
ASTM D 1883 Structural fill material should be used for the top 3 feet of the mass
grading fill and consist of non expansive granular material such as crushed coral
basalt or cinder sand The material should be well graded from coarse to fine with
no particles larger than 3 inches in largest dimension and should contain between
10 and 30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve The material should have a
laboratory CBR value of 20 or more and should have a maximum swell of less than
1 percent when tested in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1883
Imported fill materials should be tested by Geolabs for conformance with these
recommendations prior to delivery to the project site for its intended use The
thickness of the structural fill layer should be a minimum of 3 feet Imported fill
materials should be observed and or tested by Geolabs for their suitability prior to
being transported to the site for the intended use
Base course and select borrow subbase material should consist of crushed basalt

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture Content ASTM D 2216 and Unit Weight ASTM D 2937
determinations were performed on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification
and evaluation of soil properties The results of these tests are presented on the Logs
of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Two Atterberg Limits tests ASTM D 4318 were performed on selected soil
samples to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits and to aid in soil classification Results
of the tests are summarized on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depth
Graphic presentation of the test results is provided on Plate B 1
Four Sieve Analysis tests C136 were performed on
selected soil samples to evaluate the gradation characteristics of the soils and to aid in
soil classification Graphic presentation of the grain size distribution is provided on
Plate B 2
Two laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR tests ASTM D 1883 were
performed on bulk samples of the near surface soils to evaluate the pavement support
characteristics of the soils The results of the test are presented on Plates B 3 and B 4
Five one inch Ring Swell tests were performed on natural and remolded samples
of the soils to evaluate the swelling potential of the near surface soils The test results
are presented on Plate B 5
