PURPOSE AND SCOPE OF SERVICES
The purpose of our soil engineering investigation was to explore
the subsurface conditions at the proposed reservoir site and the access
road culvert crossing in order to provide recommendations for use in
the design of the foundations for the proposed structures The general
location of the project area is shown on the Project Location Map
Plate l
The scope of work was performed in general accordance with our
proposal dated
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests which
we used in the engineering analysis to formulate our conclusions and
recommendations are presented in the appendices of this report
The approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site
Plan Plate 2 The log of borings and information regarding the
field exploration and laboratory testing are also presented in the
appendices of this report
GEOLABS HAWAII
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed project consists of the following
1 A 0 5 M G concrete reservoir structure approximately
74 feet in diameter and 20 feet high Cuts into the
hillside from about 10 feet eastern side up to about
40 feet western side are planned to create a level
pad area for the reservoir
2 A 36 inch reinforced concrete pipe RCP culvert
crossing is planned where the access roadway traverses
the existing drainageway
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located at the base of the Koolau Mountain
Range in the Ahuimanu District The
proposed development is approximately one mile southwest mauka of
the terminal end of Halemanu Street within the Ahuimanu Hills Phase
2 Subdivision
The site is bounded by the proposed Ahuimanu Hillis Phase III
Subdivision to the northeast with the Koolau Mountain Range forming
the remainder of the boundaries
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 1222 00 Page 3
Being located on a ridge the topography of this site is rather
steep with ridge faces sloping toward the north and south at slope
ratios of 3 1 and 2 1 H V respectively The ground surface
elevations at the tank site ranges from approximately 484 to 510
feet
An erosional drainageway is located at the toe of the northern
ridge face and varies from 3 to 10 feet deep and about 5 feet wide
Numerous boulders up to 5 feet in diameter were noted during our
investigation in this drainageway area
The site is currently densely vegetated with a thick growth of
grass fern lauhala mango and plum trees
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The geology of the Koolaupoko area is of an old age nature charac
terized by rolling landscape of ridges and valleys formed in weathered
alluvium and colluvium at the base of the Koolau Mountain Range
The small ridges are erosional remnants of a large deposit of mainly
colluvial material derived from the erosion of the windward flank of the
Koolau Range Valleys cut by stream erosion tend to be filled with
alluvial sands gravels and some amount of boulders In their lower
reaches recent alluvium consisting of clays and silts has covered a
majority of the older sand and gravel in the valley botton
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1222 00 Page 4
Soil Conditions
Relatively uniform soil conditions were encountered in our inves
tigation at the proposed tank site Approximately 6 to 12 inches of
decomposed organic matter was removed during trail clearing prior to
our test borings The borings encountered medium stiff brown clayey
Silt with decomposed gravel to depths of ten feet overlying a stiff
orange brown mottled clayey silt soil to the depth of the borings
drilled
At the proposed culvert crossing area which appears to be an
older drainageway approximately 6 5 feet of stiff brown clayey silt
was encountered overlying a dense boulder layer to the depth of the
borings
Groundwater was not encountered in any of the borings during the
time of our investigation
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The subsurface exploration generally indicates that most of the
site is underlain by stiff alluvial colluvial deposits which could
provide adequate foundation support for the proposed structures

0 5 M G Reservoir Foundation
The test borings disclosed that the proposed site is underlain
by a stiff brown clayey silt which could provide adequate foundation
support for the proposed reservoir
Foundations
Spread and continuous wall footings bearing on the in situ
soil is recommended for the reservoir An allowable
bearing value of 2500 p s f may be used for footings
bearing on these soils
The allowable bearing value may be increased by one third
for temporary wind and seismic loads
Footings should be embedded a minimum of 24 inches below
the lowest adjacent finished grade Footings located next
to utility trenches should extend down to the bottom of
the utility trench and bear on stiff undisturbed soil or
the trench backfill should be well compacted to minimize
footing settlement
The bottom of all footing excavations and trench backfill
should be compacted to a minimum of 85 of its maximum dry
density as established by ASTM D 1557 78 prior to concrete
placement

filling with the on site soil

Site Grading
In general the site grading will consist of cutting the ridge
about 10 to 40 feet at the reservoir site and a small amount of fill
and cut for the access roadway and culvert crossing
Due to a combination of high annual rainfall and dense vegetation
the natural moisture content of the on site soils are generally high
43 to 77 Therefore some drying out of the excavated material may
be required to attain the recommended compaction criteria
Site Preparation
The site to be graded should be cleared of all vegetation
organic matter debris and soft material Prior to filling
or footing excavation the existing ground should be scarified
to a minimum of 6 inches and recompacted to a minimum of 85
of its maximum dry density established by ASTM D 1557 78 test
method

Fill Placement Compaction Requirements

It is anticipated that no filling will be required at the
tank site Some filling for the access roadway may be
necessary
Where the existing ground is steeper than 5 horizontal to 1
vertical fill placed should be keyed and benched into the
existing firm ground to properly bond the new fill to the
Slope For details see Plate 3 This detail is essential
for the stability of the fills particularly any large
fills The filling operation should start at the lowest
point and continue up in level compacted layers
Since the project site is located in a high rainfall area
the on site subsoils have in place moisture contents about 10
to 40 percent above its optimum moisture content Therefore
it may not be practicable to dry the excavated soil to near
the optimum moisture content Due to the high in situ
moisture content and since the proposed fills are low it is
our opinion that the normal 90 compaction standard used in
other drier areas should be modified to 85 for this project

For the proposed reservoir it is anticipated that the
existing ridge to the west of the tank will be cut about 40
feet Cut slopes up to H V may be used with slope
benches provided at 25 foot maximum vertical height intervals
Near the finished grade the slopes should be cut with
extreme care by the contractor A backhoe should be used to
provide the final finishing of the cut slope surface Over
cutting beyond the design slope should be avoided since
backfilling and recompacting would create a very loose
surface layer which would be easily eroded
Surface water from areas above the slope should be diverted
away and prevented from flowing over the Slope face The
slope should be planted as soon as practical to prevent
unravelling due to erosion

Asphaltic concrete pavements are planned for the project
roads

Design Review
Plans for the site grading and foundation should be forwarded to
the soils engineer for review and written comments prior to construc
tion to see that the intent of our recommendations is included in the
project plans and specifications
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during
earthwork construction particularly the fill placement slope cuts
and to monitor the contractor s grading operations
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be observed
by a soils engineer during construction to see that adequate bearing
has been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent
with the findings of our investigation
