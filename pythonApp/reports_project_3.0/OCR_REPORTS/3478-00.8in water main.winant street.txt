PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located along Winant Street in the Kalihi area of the District
of Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii as shown on the Project Location Map
Plate 1 It is proposed to install roughly 700 lineal feet of new 8 inch water main and
appurtenances adjacent to the existing utilities Based on the preliminary project plans
and profiles we understand that the new water main will be installed at approximately
4 to 5 feet below the existing ground level The ground surface elevations in the
project area range from about 20 to 35 feet Mean Sea Level MSL Soil resistivity
surveys utilizing the in Pin Method were performed to evaluate the
corrosion rating of the near surface soils within the depths of the proposed water line

FIELD SURVEY
Geophysical survey equipment i e resistance meter was mobilized to the site
on November 1st and 9th for the field survey Our field work was performed in sunny
conditions with generally dry surface conditions
Fifteen soil resistivity tests were conducted with intervals ranging from about 50
to 150 feet at five locations at the project site using the in Pin Method
and a resistance meter Strata Scout R 40C manufactured by SoilTest Inc The
closer test intervals are intended to confirm the relatively low corrosion ratings The
resistance meter is capable of transmitting 120 volts and 24 watts of output into the
ground The method utilizes four surface probes or pins electrodes to evaluate the

average resistivity an indication of corrosivity of the underlying soil mass The

The soil resistivity tests were performed at the existing ground surface on the
exposed sidewalk and shoulder areas along the proposed water line alignment The
average soil resistance was evaluated at each test location with the electrodes aligned
at equal spacings of generally 3 to 5 feet at the existing ground surface along the
shoulder and sidewalk areas
The resistance meter impresses a voltage between the outer electrodes causing
an electrical current to flow between them The voltage drop between the inner
electrodes is then measured by a sensitive built in voltmeter As a result the soil
resistance in Ohms can be found directly on the dial The soil resistivity in ohm cm
which is directly related to both resistance and spacing can then be computed The
evaluated soil resistances the computed resistivities and the corresponding corrosion
ratings are presented on Plate 4 of this report However it should be noted that the
soil resistivity may be influenced by the existing utility lines near the test locations
temperature weather conditions and other factors
Resistivity is generally recognized as one of the most significant soil character
istics with regard to the corrosivity of the soil to buried metallic objects In general the
lower the resistivity the greater the potential for corrosion of the buried metallic
structure Conversely the higher the resistivity the less likely the soil will contribute
to corrosion of metallic objects
LIMITATIONS
The evaluations presented in this report are based upon information obtained
from field data points by means of geophysical surveys Variations of soil conditions
between the field data points may occur and the nature and extent of such variations
may not become evident until a later point in time If variations then appear evident
it will be necessary to reevaluate the ratings provided in this report
Locations of the data presented in this report are approximate having been
estimated from features and reference points shown on the preliminary construction
plans and profiles of the proposed water line project Thus locations of the data
