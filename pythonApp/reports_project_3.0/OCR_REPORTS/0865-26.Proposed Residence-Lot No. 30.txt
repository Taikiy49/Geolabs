Dear Mr Ono
As requested we have made a study of the near surface soil
conditions below the above referenced lot The purpose of
the study was to evaluate the suitablility of near surface
soils as foundation support for the proposed house The
study consisted of 4 hand auger probes within the proposed
building site laboratory soil tests and a review of the
grading and compaction test record on the building site
This letter summarizes our observations and recommendations
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The preliminary house plans for the proposed single family
residence were not available during our soil investigation
Preliminary discussions with you indicate that a 2 to 3 story
wood framed structure utilizing concrete slab on grade for
the garage and first floor level would be constructed A
CMU wall is planned for the rear house wall

Existing Site Conditions
Lot No 30 is located on the northwestern portion of the
subdivision development near the end of Kahena Street
The lot is bounded by Kahena Street on the east subdivi
sion lots on the north and south and the natural valley
side slope on the west An existing concrete drainage
ditch is located on the western upper portion of the lot
The front 60 feet of the lot generally slopes upward from
Kahena Street toward the west at about 5 1 H V ratios
while the remaining portion of the lot slopes upward at
ratios of about 2 1 H V The elevation of the lot gen
erally varies from about 269 to 314 feet
Subdivision Lot Grading
In general most of the front portion 50 to 60 feet from
Kahena Street of the lot was cut down about 2 to 4 feet
A few feet of fill was placed over the front corner south
eastern of the lot The remainder of the lot upper half
was outside the project limits of grading and was not graded

Subsurface Conditions
A review of the grading compaction test record and the site
reconnaissance indicates that the front portion of the site
60 feet from Kahena Street is underlain by a surface
layer of grayish brown expansive clay about 1 0 to 4 0 or
more feet thick over weathered rock
Detailed descriptions of the soils encountered in the hand
auger probings are presented on the attached Logs of Hand Probes
Plates A 1 and A 2 The approximate locations of the probings
are shown on the attached Site Plan Plate 1
Laboratory soil tests on the near surface material indicate
that the soil can be classified as clay CH according to
the Unified Soil Classification System Laboratory one inch
ring swell tests indicate that the soil has a high shrink
swell potential The laboratory test results are summarized
on Plate B l
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that the lot is underlain by
a layer of grayish brown highly to critically expansive clay
about 1 0 to 4 0 or more feet thick over weathered rock
Foundations should be embedded a minimum of 3 feet or extend
through the surface clay layer and bear on the dense rock for
mation Concrete slab should bear on a minimum of 3 feet of

Foundations
In order to resist the expansive effects of the clay soil
individual spread footings for the house should be embedded
a minimum of 3 feet below the finished ground surface or
should extend through the surface expansive clay layer and
bear on the underlying weathered rock formation An allow
able bearing value of 3 000 p s f can be used on the stiff
natural ground or weathered rock
If a footing is located next to a utility line the footing
should extend to the bottom of the utility trench to reduce
footing settlement due to the settlement of the trench back
fill
Surface water should be diverted away from the foundations
This is especially important in the rear portion of the house
to reduce ponding of surface run off water and possible sat
uration and séttlement or heaving of the foundation soils
Slab On Grade
Concrete slab on grade would probably be used for the garage
It appears that the rear portion of the garage area would be
cut in order to obtain sufficient level area for the garage

Therefore it appears that weathered rock may be encountered
in the rear cut portion of the garage excavation Most of
the garage will encounter the expansive clay soil The expan
sive clay soils are highly expansive and are unsuitable as
slab subgrade soils Where encountered the expansive clays
should be overexcavated down to weathered rock or for a maxi
mum depth of 3 feet below the slab subgrade and replaced with
non expansive borrow soils compacted to a minimum of 90 of
its maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 This overexcavation
and replacement should be extended for a distance of 3 feet
beyond the perimeter of the slab
A 4 inch layer of base course should be provided below the
slab and compacted to 90 of its maximum dry density In
order to protect against moisture infiltration a ployethy
lene moisture barrier may be incorporated below the slab
The membrane should be protected by a thin layer of sand to
aid in concrete curing
Concrete Driveway and Sidewalks
The expansive clay soils were also encountered in the front
portion of the lot driveway area Since the driveways can
tolerate some amount of heaving the clay overexcavation and
replacement recommended for the garage slab can be reduced
to 2 feet below the finished ground level Where 2 or more
feet of fill is planned select non expansive borrow material

If a relatively long driveway is planned joints are recom

Site Grading
Additional site grading beyond the future house area is not
anticipated at this time Due to the relatively steep terrain
in the rear half of the lot any future grading should be done
with care and under the guidance of a soils engineer
Fill and backfill material should be select non expansive
off site borrow material If available some of the weathered
rock excavated material smaller than 8 inches could be used
in the fill if it is not contaminated with the Adobe clay
The Adobe clay soil should not be used for fill material

The area to be filled should be cleared of vegetation and
debris prior to filling
Fill and backfill should be placed in maximum 8 inch lifts
and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density

Design Review
The plans for site grading and foundations should be forwarded
to the soils engineer for review and comments prior to con
struction to see that the intent of our recommendations is
included in the project plans and specifications
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during
earthwork construction particularly the fill placement
slope cuts and to monitor the contractor s grading operations
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be observed
by a soils engineer during construction to see that adequate
bearing has been obtained and that the subsurface conditions
are consistent with the findings of our subsurface investiga
tion
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested
and approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the
site
