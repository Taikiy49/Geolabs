As requested we have made a study of the near surface soil
conditions below the above referenced lot The purpose of the study
was to evaluate the suitability of the near surface soils as
foundation support for the proposed house The study consisted of
excavating four 4 test pits within the proposed building site
performing laboratory soil tests and reviewing the original
subdivision grading plan This report summarizes our findings and
recommendations
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed single family residence consists of a one story
wood structure approximately 44 by 68 feet in plan dimension Based
on the preliminary plans provided to us the house will be set back
about 15 feet from Kahena Street
W O 865 70 Page 2
Post and beam construction is planned for the house with
concrete on grade slab for the garage floor and driveway
EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
Lot 20 is located in the northern portion of the subdivision
development on the northeastern side of Kahena Street It is bounded
by Kahena Street on the west subdivision lots on the northeast and
south An existing 10 foot wide sewer easement is located about 90
feet from and parallel to Kahena Street The lot generally slopes
downward from Kahena Street towards the east at about 12 percent
gradient to the concrete lined drainage channel to the back of the
lot The existing elevation of the lot generally varies from about
231 to 202 feet
SUBDIVISION LOT GRADING
Prior to the recent subdivision site grading operation most of
Lot 20 was covered with a large stockpile of soil and boulders left
over from previous grading operations in the adjacent development area
During the recent subdivision site grading operation the
stockpile was removed down to a depth of 6 feet below the design
finished grade and replaced with compacted available on site soils
which consisted of silty clay and clayey silt soils that had been
mixed with expansive adobe clay from the previous stockpile

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
A review of the site grading plan and the findings of the test
pits at this lot indicate that Lot 20 is underlain by a layer of
brownish gray expansive adobe clay of at least 8 feet thick
Detailed descriptions of the soils encountered in the test pits
are presented on the attached Test Pit Logs Plates A 1 through A 4
The approximate locations of the test pits are shown on the attached
Site Plan Plate 1
Laboratory soil tests on the near surface material indicate
that the soil can be classified as clay CH according to the Unified
Soil Classification System Laboratory one inch ring swell tests
indicate that the soil has a moderate to high shrink swell potential
The laboratory test results are summarized on Plate B 1l
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that the lot is underlain by
brownish gray clay fill Laboratory test results on this material
generally indicate that it has a moderate to high expansion
potential Therefore for Lot 20 the slab on grade and foundation
construction would require special design procedures in order to
reduce the shrink swell effect of the subsoils

House Foundation
For this lot post and beam construction would be highly
desirable and recommended due to the sloping terrain and expansive
soil conditions The post and beam construction generally results in
lower foundation costs and the individual footings can be readily
adjusted should the need occur Where utilized concrete
slab on grade should also be designed for expansive soil conditions
For post and beam construction we recommend that the expansive
clays be over excavated down for a depth of 3 feet below the finished
grade and then replaced with non expansive borrow soils such as
crusher waste select borrow or coral compacted to a minimum of 90
of its maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78 The spread footings
can then be placed on the compacted non expansive soil
Alternatively the individual post footing can be extended a
minimum depth of 36 inches below the finished site grade in the firm
undisturbed on site soil The footings should be poured in place and
the excavation backfilled with compacted on site soil to provide an
impervious layer over the footings refer to the Schematic Section on
Plate 2 for details
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f can be used for
footings bearing on the stiff on site soil or compacted fill The
bottom of the footing excavation should be neat and free of loose
soils or debris
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 865 70 Page 5
If a footing is located next to a utility line it should
extend to the bottom level of the utility trench to reduce footing
settlement due to settlement of the trench backfill
The ground surface should be shaped to divert surface water
away from foundations
Slab On Grade
Concrete slab on grade is planned for the garage area To
reduce the effects of soil heaving on the on grade garage slabs the
expansive on site soil should be over excavated down to a depth of 3
feet below the slab bottom and then replaced with non expansive
borrow soils such as select borrow crusher waste or coral The
over excavation and backfilling should extend a minimum of 3 feet
beyond the edge of the slabs refer to the Schematic Section on Plate
3 for details
The non expansive borrow soil should be compacted to a minimum
of 90 of its maximum dry density as per ASTM D 1557 78 Similar
over excavation and replacement of the expansive adobe clay soils
are recommended for the lanai slabs if constructed on grade
To further reduce future post construction swelling of the
underlying expansive soils we recommend that the adobe clay below
the capping fill pad be kept moist

A 4 inch layer of base course should be provided below the slab
and compacted to 90 of its maximum dry density In order to protect
against moisture infiltration an impervious moisture barrier may be
incorporated below the slab
The slabs should be independent and free to move from the walls
Concrete Driveway and Sidewalks
Since the driveway can tolerate some amount of heaving the
on site adobe clay over excavation and replacement may be reduced to
2 feet minimum below the bottom of the driveway slab After moisture
conditioning of the exposed adobe clay it should then be
immediately backfilled with a select non expansive borrow material
which would act as a capping over the underlying adobe clay
Where more than 2 feet of fill is planned select non expansive
borrow material should be used to cap over the expansive clay Where
less than 2 feet of new fill is planned over excavation and
replacement of the adobe clay with select non expansive material is
recommended to provide a minimum 2 feet of capping The above capping
requirements should be followed for a minimum distance of 1 foot
beyond the driveway edge
Joints are recommended in the driveway slab at about 8 to
10 foot intervals and at discontinuities in order to allow for some
relative movement of the concrete
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 865 70 Page 7
For future sidewalks capping of the clay by means of the
over excavation and replacement method similar to the driveway is
recommended to reduce heaving caused by the expansive clay soil
Over excavation beyond the edge of the walkway may not be necessary
Site Grading
The area to be filled should be cleared of vegetation and
debris prior to filling
Fill and backfill should be placed in maximum 8 inch lifts and
compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density established
by ASTM D 1557 78
Where required two horizontal to one vertical 2 1 vertical
cut and fill slope ratios are recommended for slope height less than 5
feet
Drainage
The finished lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings
should be shaped to shed water away from the foundations and to avoid
ponding condition Also it is advised that gutter systems be
installed around the house and that gutter water be diverted far away
from the foundations and walkways Excessive landscape watering near
the foundations and slab should also be avoided
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 865 70 Page 8
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have
concrete bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the
subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper
performance of the above recommended foundations since ponded water
could cause excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent
heaving or loss of strength The site drainage should be maintained
throughout the life of the house
The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled tight
against slab footing immediately after setting of the concrete to
prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and
Maintained to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing
foundation
Design Review
Plans and specifications for foundations site grading and
utility construction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for
review and written comments prior to construction to see that the
intent of our recommendations is included
GEOLABS HAWAII

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during
earthwork construction particularly the fill placement slope cuts
and to monitor the contractor s grading operations
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be examined
by a soils engineer during construction to see that adequate bearing
has been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent
with the finding of our investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested
and approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon
the above inspections
