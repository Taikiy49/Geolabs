SECTION 1 0 GENERAL
1 1 Introduction
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Lanai Senior Center project in Lanai City on the Island of
Lanai Hawaii The project location and general vicinity are shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering
recommendations resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and
engineering analyses These recommendations are intended to assist in the design of
site grading foundations driveway pavements utilities excavation and fill placement
only The findings and recommendations presented herein are subject to the limitations
noted at the end of this report
1 2 Project Considerations
The proposed project consists of developing a new Senior Center to replace the
existing facility in Lanai City on the Island of Lanai Hawaii The site is located at the
northwest corner of Dole Park and is bordered by Fraser Avenue Seventh Street and
Gay Street
Based on the available information the new center will be a single story
wood framed building supported by post and pier foundations Asphalt concrete
pavement is anticipated for the parking areas We anticipate that the project will involve
minor site grading and infrastructure installation including driveways parking areas
sidewalks and utilities
1 3 Purpose and Scope
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the surface and
subsurface conditions at the site to develop a soil rock data set to formulate
geotechnical engineering recommendations for the proposed senior dormitory
residential development Our work was performed in general accordance with our fee

Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork areas within the contract grading limits should be
cleared and grubbed thoroughly Vegetation debris deleterious material and other
unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed properly off site or stockpiled
in a designated area to reduce the potential for contaminating the excavated
materials
Soft and yielding areas encountered during clearing and grubbing should be
over excavated to expose firm natural material and the resulting excavation should
be backfilled with well compacted engineered fill The excavated soft soils may be
used as fill provided that they meet fill material requirements
Prior to filling the existing ground should be scarified to a depth of 10 inches
moisture conditioned to about 2 percent above the optimum moisture and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction For pavement
subgrades the minimum relative compaction should be 95 percent Where
shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of the subgrade we recommend
thoroughly moistening the soil to close cracks or re preparing as recommended
above Saturation and subsequent yielding of the exposed subgrade due to
inclement weather and poor drainage may require over excavation of the soft areas
and replacement with well compacted engineered fill The excavated soft soils may
be used as fill provided that they meet fill material requirement Relative compaction
refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum
dry density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557
Fills and Backfills
General fill material should consist of low expansive materials less than 3 inches in
largest dimension with a CBR swell of less than 1 percent The fill material should
also be free of vegetation and deleterious materials Our laboratory test results
indicated a low to moderate swell potential for the on site silty clay material

Fills and backfills should be moisture conditioned to about 2 percent above the

below structural or pavement subgrade level should be compacted to at least
95 percent relative compaction
Base course and select borrow subbase materials should be moisture conditioned
to above the optimum moisture placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose
thickness and compacted to a minimum of 95percent relative compaction
Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a
percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance
with ASTM D 1557 Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry
weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
Compaction should be accomplished by sheepsfoot rollers vibratory rollers
multiple wheel pneumatic tired rollers or other types of acceptable compaction
equipment

3 2 Building Foundations
To provide conformance of the new center with respect to the historic nature of the
surrounding area it is desired to construct the new center using post and pier foundations
supporting wooden floors
Shallow spread and or continuous footings may be used to support the proposed
structure provided they are embedded a minimum of 24 inches below the finished
grade to reduce the effects of the expansive soils
An allowable bearing pressure of 3 000 pounds per square foot psf may be
utilized for the design of residential building foundations bearing on properly compacted fill
or stiff on site silty clay This bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may be
increased by one third 1 3 for transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic
forces Footings should have a minimum width of 12 inches and should be embedded a
minimum of 24 inches below the existing grade Footings constructed near tops of slopes
or on sloping ground should be embedded deep enough to provide a minimum horizontal
setback distance of 5 feet measured from the outside edge of the footings to the slope
face

We estimate that settlements for footings bearing on properly compacted fill or
on site medium stiff to hard silty clay as recommended herein to be less than 1 inch total
Differential settlement is estimated to be less than one half 2 inch
Soft and or loose materials encountered at the bottom of footing excavations should

3 6 Drainage
The finished grades adjacent to the dormitory structure should be sloped at
2 to 5 to shed water away from the foundations and to reduce the potential for
ponding It is also advised to install gutter systems around the future structures and to
divert the discharge away from the foundation and pavement areas Excessive

excavations should be properly backfilled against the footings immediately after setting
of the concrete to reduce water infiltration Drainage swales should be provided as soon
as possible and should be maintained to drain surface run off away from walls and
foundations

APPENDIX A

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
We explored the subsurface conditions at the site by the drilling and sampling
three borings each to a depth of approximately 21 5 feet below the existing ground
surface The borings were drilled with a truck mounted drill rig equipped with continuous
flight rotary auger tools at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Our geologist classified the materials encountered in the borings by visual and
textural examination in the field and observed the field exploration operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed by visual
observation and testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance
with the Unified Soil Classification System Logs of the materials encountered are
presented on Plates A 1 through A 3
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM D 3550 and D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving
with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts needed to drive the sampler
the second and third 6 inches of an 18 to 24 inch drive are shown on the Logs of Borings
at the appropriate sample depths
Pocket penetrometer tests were performed on selected cohesive soil samples
retrieved in the field The pocket penetrometer test provides an indication of the
unconfined compressive strength of the soil sample Pocket penetrometer test results
are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths

One Direct Shear test ASTM D 3080 was performed on a selected sample to
evaluate the shear strength characteristics of the materials tested Direct shear test
results are presented on Plate B 3

resistivity ASTM G 57 were performed on selected soil samples obtained from our
field exploration The test results are summarized on Plate B 4
