Project Considerations
The project site is along Naniloa Loop near the existing Hawaii Campus of Brigham
Young University in the Laie area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The Na Hale Kumu
Faculty Housing Area A project consists of demolishing the existing Buildings 1 through 8
and constructing eight new townhouse buildings The new buildings will be two story
structures As part of the project development a storm water runoff disposal system will
be required for drainage purpose Therefore it was desired to perform field infiltration
testing on the in situ soils at three locations for the design of Low Impact Development
LID detention systems
Subsurface Conditions
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by drilling and
sampling three exploratory borings designated as Boring through 50G 3
extending down to depths of about 10 1 to 12 feet below the existing ground surface The
approximate boring locations are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2

Based on the borings drilled the subsurface conditions at the site generally consist
of surface fills underlain by beach deposits and coralline deposits Surface fills generally
consist of stiff clayey silts of about 1 to 2 feet in thickness Fill materials were underlain
by beach deposits consisting of medium dense silty sands extending down to about 5 to
6 feet below the ground surface Beach deposits were generally underlain by medium
hard to hard coralline sandstone and loose to medium dense coralline detritus extending
down to the maximum depth explored of about 12 feet below the ground surface
In Boring No 50G 3 the beach deposits were underlain by loose silty sand
lagoonal deposits extending down to about 9 5 feet below the ground surface The
lagoonal deposits were underlain by stiff to very stiff silty clay alluvium extending down to
the maximum depth explored of about 12 feet below the ground surface
We encountered groundwater at depths of about 4 to 6 3 feet below the existing
ground surface in our borings at the time of our field exploration Groundwater levels
encountered generally correspond to elevation of about 0 2 to 0 7 feet Mean Sea Level
MSL Due to the proximity of the project site to the Pacific Ocean groundwater levels
likely will change depending on tidal fluctuations storm surge conditions seasonal
precipitation surface water runoff and other factors
Field Infiltration Testing
Infiltration tests were conducted in the three borings drilled at the site to evaluate
the infiltration characteristics of the subsurface materials The tests were performed in
general accordance with the procedures in Appendix D of the State of Maryland
Department of the Environment Stormwater Design Manual Volumes I and II rev
2009 These procedures are consistent with the other states procedures and generally
may be considered an industry standard
The field infiltration tests were performed by augering a boring to a selected test
depth of about 2 to 4 feet below the existing ground surface Upon reaching the test depth
a 4 inch inside diameter PVC solid casing was set to the bottom of the drilled hole to allow
infiltration only through the soil exposed at the bottom of the boring A falling head
infiltration test was performed to determine the average infiltration rate of the underlying
subsurface materials The test consists of four trials of filling the casing with about
24 inches of water and taking periodic readings over a 1 hour trial period or until the hole
drains completely The infiltration rates are then calculated based on the results of the
fourth and last trial Results of the final infiltration rates are presented in the table below
Details of our field infiltration tests are presented in Appendix C

Limitations
The geotechnical field and laboratory data submitted herein is based upon
information obtained from the borings Variations of the subsurface conditions between
and beyond the exploration points may occur and the nature and extent of these
variations may not become evident until additional exploration and or construction is
underway
The field boring locations indicated in this report is approximate having been
estimated by taping from visible features shown on the Architectural Site Plan transmitted
by Design Partners Inc on Boring elevations were estimated based
on interpolation between the spot elevations shown on the same plan The physical
location and elevation of the borings should be considered accurate only to the degree
implied by the methods used
The stratification lines shown on the graphic representations of the borings depict
the approximate boundaries between the soil and or rock types and as such may denote
a gradual transition Water level data presented in the text herein were based on
measurements recorded in the borings at the times shown on the graphic representations
of the borings However it must be noted that fluctuation may occur due to variation in
rainfall seepage tide temperature and other factors

Field Exploration
We explored the subsurface conditions at the project site by drilling and sampling
three boreholes designated as Borings No 50G 1 through 50G 3 The borings extended
to depths of about 10 1 to 12 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate
boring locations are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were drilled using a
truck mounted drill rig equipped with continuous flight augers
Our geologist classified the materials encountered in the borings by visual and
textural examination in the field in general accordance with ASTM D2488 Standard
Practice for Description and Identification of Soils and monitored the drilling operations
on a near continuous full time basis These classifications were further reviewed visually
and by testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general accordance with
ASTM D2487 Standard Practice for Classification of Soils for Engineering Purposes
Unified Soil Classification System as shown on the Soil Log Legend Plate A 0 1
Deviations made to the soil classification in accordance with ASTM D2487 are described in
the Soil Classification Log Key Plate A 0 2 Rocky materials were described in general
accordance with the Rock Description System as shown on the Rock Log Legend
Plate A 0 3 The Rock Description System is based on the publication Suggested Methods
for the Quantitative Description of Discontinuities in Rock Masses by the International
Society for Rock Mechanics March 1977 Graphic representations of the materials
encountered are presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 3
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained in general accordance with
ASTM D3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 3 inch OD Modified
California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches In addition some samples
were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with ASTM D1586
Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 2 inch OD standard
penetration sampler using the same hammer and drop The blow counts needed to drive
the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown as the
Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths The
penetration resistance shown on the Logs of Borings indicates the number of blows
required for the specific sampler type used The blow counts may need to be factored to
obtain the Standard Penetration Test SPT blow counts
