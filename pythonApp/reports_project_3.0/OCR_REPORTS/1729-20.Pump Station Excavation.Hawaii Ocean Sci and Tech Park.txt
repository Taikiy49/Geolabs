INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this report is to summarize and recapitulate the
conditions encountered and the events occurring during the construction of
the dewatering system i e cofferdam for the Cold and Warm Water Pump
Station at the Hawaii Ocean Science and Technology HOST Park site in
Keahole Hawaii see Project Location Map Plate 1 The report is
intended to document the geotechnical explorations performed both prior to
and during construction and to summarize events related to the
construction activities In addition this report attempts to provide a
representation of the actual subsurface conditions encountered by the
excavation
INITIAL EXPLORATION
In July 1986 the initial subsurface exploration for the proposed
HOST Park and Seacoast Test Facility Upgrade was performed by C W
Associates Hawaii This initial exploration consisted of
the drilling and sampling of seven 7 borings throughout the project
site These borings were drilled to obtain samples of the subsurface
materials for examination and testing in order to develop engineering
recommendations for the design and construction of the proposed project

ADDITIONAL EXPLORATIONS
Subsequent to the initial exploration construction contract was
awarded and construction work begun On six 6 track
drill test holes were drilled by Kiewit Pacific within the revised
approximately 35 by 65 foot Cold and Warm Water Pump Station site The
track drill logs suggested that loose white sand may be present from
about 23 feet below the existing ground surface to the bottom of the
test holes which terminated at about 36 foot depth
To construct the proposed pump station an approximately 28 foot
deep 20 feet below Mean Sea Level excavation and a dewatering scheme
were planned The presence of loose sand at the bottom of the proposed
excavation would significantly affect the dewatering scheme and possibly
the foundation design of the pump station
Based upon the above it was proposed to drill an additional five
5 borings within the revised pump station site to verify the presence
of the loose white sand These borings were drilled in March 1987 to
depths of 31 5 to 45 0 feet below the ground surface at the approximate
locations shown on Site Plan 2 Plate 3
Rock coring using both triple and double tube core barrels and
soil sampling using a 2 4 inch I D split barrel sampler SPT were
carried out The Logs of Borings are presented in Appendix A Plates
A 1 through A 7

moderately jointed basalt to about 30 feet below the existing ground
surface Below approximately 30 foot depth a clinker zone was
encountered which consisted primarily of slightly cemented to uncemented

tubes 1 to 5 feet thick were encountered within this clinker zone

Commencement of Over Excavation
At this point in time Kiewit Pacific had submitted a new dewatering
and construction plan which called for the erection of a cofferdam with a
gravity seal consisting of tremie placed concrete in its bottom This
new plan called for an additional 15 feet of excavation depth to
elevation 35 feet below Mean Sea Level the construction of a sheetpile
cofferdam and the placement of a 16 foot thick plug of concrete in the
bottom of the cofferdam
The cofferdam would form a water tight box in which the pump station
could be constructed The concrete plug would prevent water from entering
the bottom of the cofferdam and would be of sufficient thickness and
weight to resist the buoyant forces which would tend to float the
cofferdam

SUMMARY OF OBSERVATIONS
After careful review and comparison of the data obtained from the
additional borings observations made during excavation and during the
underwater survey of materials encountered it is our opinion that the
subsurface conditions encountered at the HOST Park Pump Station site in
general appear to be consistent with the initial boring drilled at the
site Some variations are noted in the interface between the upper hard
basalt and the underlying clinker layer however these variations are in
our opinion within the normal limits which could be anticipated in the
geologic environment existent at the site
