This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering services performed during
construction of the off site sewer improvements for the Waiakea Center project from May
through December 1996 in general accordance with our service contract
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed Waiakea Center project site is located at the corner of the
intersection of Kanoelehua Avenue and East Makaala Street in Hilo on the island of
Hawaii The project development consists of five one story retail stores and parking lots
with approximately 1 200 parking stalls As part of the project development off site
improvements consisting of a new 12 inch sewer line roadway widening and traffic
signal improvements were also proposed
The off site sewer improvements project consists of construction of a new 12 inch
sewer line with a length of approximately 4 900 lineal feet The new sewer line begins at
the intersection of Laukapu and Leilani Streets and runs along

OFF SITE SEWER CONSTRUCTION
The off site sewer improvements were performed by Sonomura Contracting
Company In general the off site sewer improvements consisted of excavating the sewer
trenches to the design elevations placing the 6 inch thick crushed rock cradle installing
the 12 inch PVC sewer line backfilling and compacting the sewer line trenches up to the
finished grades and leakage and mandrel testing of the sewer line
The sewer line installation was constructed in small sections In general a section
of about 50 to 100 feet of sewer trench was excavated followed by installation of the
sewer pipe and then backfilled on the same day Based on our field observations basalt
gravel cobble and basait rock formation were generally encountered during sewer trench
excavation The materials from the trench excavation were hauled to an on site crusher
where they were crushed and processed to generate trench backfill In general the
trench backfill materials generated consisted of well graded granular materials less than
6 inches in largest dimension
Following excavation of the sewer trenches to the design grades a minimum of
6 inches of crushed rock cradle ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation was placed at the bottom
of the trench as bedding material The 12 inch PVC sewer pipe was then placed on the
crushed rock cradle For the first lift of trench backfill free draining clean granular
material consisting of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C33 No 67 gradation was used
extending from the bedding material to about 12 inches above the top of the pipe
The backfill material from about 12 inches above the sewer pipes to the finish
subgrade consisted of on site 6 inch minus trench backfill materials The backfill
materials were generally placed in near level lifts of less than 12 inches in loose
thickness brought to above the optimum moisture and compacted to the specified
degree of compaction by using a vibratory drum roller Generally conventional
compaction testing is not practicable in backfills composed of rock boulders and or
cobbles Therefore for the trench backfill consisting of 6 inch minus materials the
degree of compaction was based on visual observations since the over sized nature of
the backfill material precluded field density testing The tops of the trenches were
finished on a daily basis with a temporary asphalt concrete cold patch

In accordance with the County of Hawaii Standard Specifications the sewer pipes
were tested for leakage and deflection for every segment The leakage of the installed
sewer pipes was tested using either the air test method or the infiltration method water
method and the pipe deflection was tested by the mandrel test A summary of the
leakage and mandrel tests taken for each segment along the sewer line is provided on
Plate 2
Following the leakage and mandrel tests for pipe leakage and deflection the
Contractor removed the temporary asphalt concrete patch and backfill materials down to
the finish subgrade level The finish subgrade level was re compacted prior to placement
of the base course material After compaction of the subgrade base course was placed
on the prepared finished subgrade
Samples of the base course material used at the site were obtained and
transported to our office for laboratory testing The maximum dry densities and optimum
moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test results are summarized
on Plate 3 of this report Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil
expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557
Field density tests were taken on the base course materials during the sewer
trench construction and pavement construction operations to confirm our field
observations as variations in construction procedures and soil conditions were noted
The field density tests were taken to determine the relative compaction of the fills placed
for conformance with the specified minimum compaction A total of 72 field density tests
were performed using the nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM Test
Designations D 2922 and D 3017 The results of the compaction tests taken during the
site grading operations are summarized on Plates 4 1 through 4 6 of this report These
tests were taken on the prepared ground surfaces and the finished base course at the
site Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from
on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only
to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were
reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
leakage mandrel and field density tests it is our opinion that the off site sewer
improvements for the Waiakea Center project observed and tested by Geolabs Hawaii
were performed in general accordance with the intent of the County of Hawaii Standard
Specifications and the project plans and specifications

LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is very difficult to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the construction may
have been performed without our observation However the level of the field observation
and testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some of the
geotechnical risks to the project It should be recognized that there are variations in the
accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the
earthwork construction and materials Our services were performed in accordance with
generally accepted standards of geotechnical engineering practice no warranty is
expressed or implied Provision of construction monitoring by an engineer is not
insurance nor does it guarantee construction of any type The contractor shall retain
responsibility for the quality of the work for adhering to the plans and specifications and
for repairing defects regardless of when they are found
