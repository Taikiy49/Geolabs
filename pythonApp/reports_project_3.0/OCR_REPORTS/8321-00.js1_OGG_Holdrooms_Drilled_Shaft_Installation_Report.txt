Dear Mr. Wong: 
As requested, Geolabs observed the installation and testing of the drilled shaft 
foundations for the Holdroom and Gate Improvements project at Kahului Airport on the 
Island of Maui, Hawaii. This letter report summarizes our geotechnical observations made 
during the drilled shaft foundation installation. The project location and general vicinity 
are shown on the Project Location Map, Plate 1. 
Geolabs provided geotechnical consultation services for the Holdroom and Gate 
Improvements project in general accordance with the requirements outlined in the project 
specifications (Section 02465 – Drilled Shafts). The purpose of our geotechnical 
observations was to monitor the geotechnical aspects of the drilled shaft foundation 
installation for conformance with the plans and specifications, as part of the “Special 
Inspection” in general accordance with Item 1704 of the 2012 International Building Code. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The improvements project is located at Kahului Airport on the Island of Maui. In 
general, the project involves the construction of a new Holdroom AB (by expanding a 
passenger concourse between existing Holdrooms A and B). As part of the project, three 
new passenger boarding bridges (PBB) will be installed at Gates 1, 3, and 7. The new 
passenger boarding bridges will be supported on deep foundations consisting of 
cast-in-place concrete drilled shafts. Drilled shafts with a diameter of 24 inches situated 
in groups of 3 to 4 drilled shafts per pile cap with design lengths of 15 and 20 feet below 
the bottom of pile cap were planned for the project. 
The scope of our work discussed in this letter report includes observation of the 
drilled shaft foundation construction for the passenger boarding bridges at Gates 1, 3,

bridges at Gates 1, 3, and 7. One sacrificial, pre-production drilled shaft (a 24-inch

for the project. It should be noted that Drilled Shaft No. 13 (located at Gate 7) was deleted 
due to an existing footing obstructing the drilled shaft location (refer to RFI No. 216.1 for 
further details). A summary of the drilled shafts installed is provided in the following table.

The purpose of the trial shaft was to demonstrate the adequacy of the proposed 
construction methods and equipment planned for use on the production shafts. The 
sacrificial, pre-production trial shaft was installed between April 11 and 13, 2022. The 
production drilled shafts for the project were installed between April 20 and April 29, 2022, 
and between September 13 and 30, 2022. The approximate drilled shaft locations and 
identification designations are shown on the Drilled Shaft Location Plan, Plates 2.1 and 
2.2.  
The drilling and concrete placement operations were conducted under the 
near-continuous observation of a Geolabs field representative. During the drilled shaft 
construction, logs were maintained for each drilled shaft. The logs included information 
such as the shaft number, size and length of the shaft, soil conditions encountered during 
excavation, and anomalies encountered (if any) during concrete placement. Summaries 
of the drilled shaft installation are presented on the Drilled Shaft Installation Summary, 
Plate 3. 
The trial and production drilled shafts for the project were installed by Foundations 
International, Inc. utilizing an EDT-5 ExcaDrill mounted on a CAT 325D Excavator 
equipped with a 24-inch diameter core barrel, digging bucket, and clean-out bucket. The 
production drilled shafts were excavated down to tip elevations ranging from +24.97 to 
+11.7 feet Mean Sea Level (MSL). Upon drilling to the tip elevations, the soil spoils were

After each drilled shaft was excavated to the tip elevation and cleaned out, the 
drilling contractor placed the rebar cage into the excavated shaft to the design elevation. 
Concrete was placed into the excavated shafts using the tremie method. The shaft 
concrete was provided by Hawaiian Cement with a design compressive strength of 
4,500 psi. 
It should be noted that an approximately 18-inch diameter abandoned cast iron 
utility line was encountered at about 13 feet below the ground surface at Drilled Shaft 
Nos. 2 and 3 (Gate 1). In order to successfully perform the concrete pour without concrete 
flowing into the open pipe, the contractor submitted an RFI (RFI No. 0227.1) requesting 
the insertion of a Sonotube to block the exposed pipe opening. The contractor proposed 
securing an approximately 5.5-foot-long section of Sonotube at the exposed pipe opening 
to prevent concrete from flowing into the exposed pipe opening. Because the addition of 
the Sonotube would reduce the skin friction contribution within the zone it was placed, we 
recommended increasing the length of Drill Shaft Nos. 2 and 3 from 20 to 24 feet. 
Concrete Integrity Coring 
In accordance with the project specifications, concrete integrity coring was 
performed on Drilled Shaft Nos. 7, 15, and 17 by Geolabs, Inc. utilizing a truck-mounted 
drill rig and coring tools on May 3, 2022 (Shaft No. 17) and October 10, 2022 (Shaft Nos. 
7 and 15). Concrete coring extended between about 14 and 18 feet below the top of 
concrete level (planned concrete cutoff elevation at time of coring). Based on our visual 
observations, the recovered cores appeared to be of good quality concrete. Therefore, 
we recommended acceptance of the cored drilled shafts based on concrete integrity 
coring results. Detailed findings from the concrete integrity coring can be found in two 
technical memoranda dated May 9, 2022 and May 12, 2023. 
RECOMMENDATIONS 
Based on our field observations, we believe the production drilled shafts for the 
Holdroom and Gate Improvements project has been installed in substantial conformance 
with the project plans and specifications and within the limits of generally accepted 
construction practices. Based on our observations of the drilled shaft installation and 
integrity coring results, it is our professional opinion that the drilled shafts observed by 
Geolabs have been installed to sufficient depths to provide the design load capacity.
