PROJECT DESCRIPTION 
In general, the Sky Ala Moana project involved the construction of new 41-story 
high-rise twin towers with parking structures. The site is generally bounded by Makaloa 
Street to the North, Kapiolani Boulevard to the south, a mixed-used building to the east, 
and a vacant lot to the west. The project location and general vicinity are shown on the 
Project Location Map, Plate 1. 
Based on the geotechnical engineering report referenced above, we recommended 
supporting the high-rise and parking structures on a deep foundation system consisting of 
18- and 24-inch diameter ACIP piles with allowable compressive load capacities of 500 
and 1,200 kips per pile, respectively. The ACIP piles would extend through the 
near-surface fills and lagoonal deposits and derive resistance primarily from skin friction 
within coral ledges and/or coralline detritus materials encountered below the lagoonal 
deposits with design pile lengths of about 65 and 110 feet for the 18- and 24-inch diameter 
ACIP piles, respectively.  
STATIC PILE LOAD TEST 
In order to evaluate the adequacy of the recommended design length of the ACIP 
pile foundation, two bi-directional static pile load tests were conducted on sacrificial ACIP 
piles, designated as TP-1, for the 18-inch diameter ACIP pile on July 17, 2020, and TP-2 
for the 24-inch diameter ACIP pile on July 9, 2020. To evaluate the load transfer 
mechanism at the pile-soil interface, the load test piles were instrumented with vibrating 
wire “sister bar” embedment strain gages installed at various depths along the length of 
the load test piles. The test piles were installed to depths of about 70 and 110 feet below 
the existing ground surface for the 18-inch and 24-inch diameter test piles, respectively. 
The locations of the bi-directional static load test piles are shown on the ACIP Pile 
Identification Plan, Plate 2. 
A Manitowoc 4100W Crawler Crane equipped with a Bauer/Berminghammer ACIP 
BG46 drill, equipped with automatic monitoring equipment, and a continuous flight 
hollow-stem auger were used to install the load test piles. A trailer-mounted hydraulic 
concrete pump with an average stroke volume of about 0.065 cubic yards per stroke was 
used to pump the grout into the pile system. The load test piles were loaded in general 
accordance with Procedure A Quick Test of ASTM D8169M, Deep Foundations Under 
Bi-Directional Static Axial Compressive Load. Detailed static pile load test results are 
referred to in the following reports by Applied Foundation Testing. 
 “Final Report of AFT-Cell, Bi-Directional Static Load Testing, 18-inch Test Pile, Sky 
Ala Moana, Oahu, Hawaii, AFT Project No. 520034,” dated July 22, 2020 
 “Final Report of AFT-Cell, Bi-Directional Static Load Testing, 24-inch Test Pile, Sky 
Ala Moana, Oahu, Hawaii, AFT Project No. 520034,” dated July 22, 2020

In general, the piles were loaded to the maximum loads of 1,907 and 4,388 kips for 
the 18- and 24-inch diameter load test piles, respectively. Based on our field observations 
and the results of the static pile load test for the 18-inch diameter ACIP pile, it is our opinion 
that the 18-inch diameter ACIP pile drilled to the design tip elevation would be sufficient to 
achieve the specified design allowable compressive load capacity of 500 kips per pile with a 
factor of safety of at least 2.0. Based on our field observations and the results of the static 
pile load test for the 24-inch diameter ACIP pile, we believe that the 24-inch diameter ACIP 
test pile is capable of supporting the specified maximum test load without failure, which is 
substantially exceeding the design allowable compressive load capacity of 1,200 kips 
meeting the project specification. Based on the favorable load test results, we 
recommended the ACIP pile length be reduced from 110 feet to 105 feet for the 24-inch 
diameter ACIP production piles, while the length of 65 feet for the 18-inch diameter ACIP 
piles remained as-is. 
PRODUCTION PILE INSTALLATION 
The general contractor for the project was Albert C. Kobayashi, Inc., and the ACIP 
pile subcontractor was Hawaiian Dredging Construction Company, Inc. (HDCC). The 
production ACIP pile installation, consisting of 125 18-inch and 220 24-inch diameter ACIP 
piles, was conducted from July 8 through September 30, 2020, under the near-continuous 
observations by our field engineers. A Manitowoc 4100W Crawler Crane equipped with a 
Bauer/Berminghammer ACIP BG46 drill and a continuous flight hollow-stem auger and a 
Bauer BG-28 drill rig equipped with a hollow-stem auger were used to install the 
production piles. Automatic monitoring equipment was mounted on both drilling/pumping 
equipment with the intent of providing auger depth, grout volume, grout pressure, etc., on 
a near-continuous basis. 
During the pile installation operation, logs were maintained for each ACIP pile, 
which included information pertaining to pile number, date installed, depth of pile, grout 
volume used, grout return depth, etc. The locations of the production ACIP piles installed 
are shown on the ACIP Pile Identification Plan, Plate 2. The 18- and 24-inch diameter 
ACIP pile installation summaries are presented by pile number on Plates 3.1 through 3.11 
of this letter report. The 18-inch diameter ACIP piles installed for the project ranged in 
lengths from about 65.0 to 65.8 feet, whereas the 24-inch diameter ACIP piles ranged in 
lengths from about 105.0 to 106.6 feet.  
Based on our observation of the equipment performance and subsurface conditions 
encountered, it is our professional opinion that the production 18- and 24-inch diameter 
ACIP piles have been installed to sufficient depths and/or resistance to provide the design 
load capacities of 500 kips per pile for the 18-inch diameter ACIP piles and 1,200 kips per 
pile for the 24-inch diameter ACIP piles, with a minimum safety factor of 2.0.
