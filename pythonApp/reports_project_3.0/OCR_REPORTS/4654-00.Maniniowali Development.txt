Regional Geology
The Island of Hawaii the largest in the Hawaiian Archipelago covers an area of
approximately 4 000 square miles The island was formed by the activity of five shield
volcanoes These are Kohala which is long extinct Mauna Kea which has had activity during
recent geologic time Hualalai which last erupted in 1801 and Mauna Loa and Kilauea both
of which are still active
The project site is situated on the northwestern flank of Hualalai Mountain which
makes up the west central portion of the island The lava formation encountered at the site
appears to be of both a a and pahoehoe type flows which spread and ponded as they
approached the ocean A a lava is typically characterized by a porous rough and irregular
flow surface resembling a jagged accumulation of rock fragments including cobbles and
boulders Pahoehoe is characterized by a smooth rope like or billowy surface and an internal
structure of vesicular porous rock
Cavities are commonly encountered in basaltic lavas especially pahoehoe lava These
cavities were formed when the lava was still in a molten state and represent both lava tubes
intra flow cavities and blisters and pockets inter flow cavities Lava tubes are formed when
molten lava drains from the cooling flow leaving a hollow tube like structure which may extend
for a large distance longitudinally along the flow Blisters and pockets are generally smaller
in horizontal extent
Basalt is considered to be a relatively permeable rock and can transmit water quite
readily in both the horizontal and vertical directions In general water is transmitted through
the porous rock matrix along joints fractures and inter flow contacts cavities and along
clinker layers The permeability of the clinker and cavities is high therefore they serve as the
major water transmission features

Site Description
As previously mentioned the project site is located to the southwest of the existing
Kukio Bay Beach Club in the District of North Kona on the Island of Hawaii Based on the
available plans the project site encompasses an area of approximately 388 acres The project
site extends from Queen Kaahumanu Highway along the eastern boundary to about 1 000 feet
east of the Pacific Ocean shoreline The Kuili cinder mound is located to the south of the
project limits
Based on our site reconnaissance the project site is generally underlain by pahoehoe
and a a lavas Numerous cavities collapsed lava tube features and loosely scattered cobbles
and boulders were observed at the site An access road traverses across the center of the
project site from Queen Kaahumanu Highway to the Kua Bay shoreline The access road was
generally very rough and difficult to drive on without four wheel drive vehicles A cinder mound
of approximately 8 1 acres in area is located at the northern portion of the project site
The vegetation at the project site is relatively light consisting of wild grasses and kiawe
trees The vegetation is light to moderate near the cinder mound located at the northern
portion of the project site Based on the available maps and our observations the project site
is generally sloping down slightly from south to north with ground surface elevations ranging
from about 42 to 265 feet Mean Sea Level MSL
Subsurface Conditions
As mentioned previously a field exploration consisting of drilling and sampling borings
was not conducted for this due diligence study Instead research and review of available
borings and test pits in house information in the vicinity of the project site was conducted to
evaluate the subsurface conditions at the project site
In general a large portion of the project site is underlain by pahoehoe and a a lavas
which is exposed at the ground surface In general the pahoehoe lavas at the site appeared
to consist of medium hard basalt formation near the ground surface However we believe that
dense basalt formation blue rock is present at greater depths A a materials generally consist
of loose boulder and cobble sized basalt fragments A a materials generally breakdown into
sands and gravel when excavated leveled with large bulldozers and or proof rolled
It should be noted that numerous cavities and collapsed lava tube features were
observed at the site Lava tubes and cavities are commonly encountered in basalt formations
in the vicinity of the project site Therefore the presence of lava tubes and cavities should be
anticipated at this project site
As mentioned previously a cinder mound is located at the northern portion of the
project site Cinder materials generally consist of loose silty sands and gravel It should be
noted that the cinder contained a significant amount of ash material based on our visual

Lava Tubes and Cavities
Based on our experience in the area lava tubes and cavities are commonly
encountered in basalt formations in the vicinity of the project site Lava tubes and cavities may
vary significantly in size from small blisters to large lava tubes of 20 feet in diameter or more
in general the majority of small cavities are limited in lateral extent However the potential
for long lava tubes that may traverse across the entire project site exists Therefore special
attention should be given to the site preparation of the ground surface prior to placement of
fills and construction of improvements on these types of materials

Site Preparation
Due to the potential for presence of lava tubes and cavities special attention should be
given to the site preparation aspect of the proposed construction Proof rolling of the
subgrades will be necessary to assist in detecting and possibly collapsing near surface lava
tubes and cavities The proof rolling process may be conducted using a combination of large
vibratory drum rollers and Caterpillar D 10 bulldozers tn addition the proof rolling operations
should be observed by a qualified geotechnical engineer to evaluate the potential for the
presence of lava tubes and or cavities near the existing ground surface
In general soft loose weak or yielding areas or cavities disclosed during site
preparation operations should be over excavated to expose firm and or dense ground The
resulting excavation should be backfilled with compacted structural fill materials It should be
noted that backfilling of lava tubes may be extensive due to the nature of these formations
Therefore contract documents for the mass grading should include additive and deductive unit
prices for over excavating the lava tube features and backfilling with compacted fill to account
for variations in the over excavation and backfill quantities
Detailed grading plans were not available at the time this letter report was prepared
However we envision that the mass grading will consist of significant cuts and fills of up to
about 20 feet in thickness We understand that the cinder mound located at the northern
portion of the project site will be preserved In general excavated on site materials may be
used as a source of fill materials provided that they are processed to meet the gradation
requirements of the various fill types recommended for grading of the site In general
structural fills should consist of well graded granular materials less than 6 inches in maximum
dimension with sufficient fines to prevent the occurrence of voids in the compacted mass
General fills should consist of well graded granular materials the majority of which are less
than 12 inches in size with an absolute maximum dimension of 18 inches
Boulders i e rock fragments larger than 12 inches in maximum size but less than
3 feet in largest dimension encountered during site grading may be used in rock fills provided
that several provisions are followed In general boulders should be placed at least 4 feet
below the finished subgrades and at least 6 feet laterally away from slope faces In addition
rock fills should be properly choked off at the surface to reduce the potential for migration of
fines through the voids between boulders
Foundations
We envision that the proposed structures at the site will range from one to three stories
Based on the subsurface conditions anticipated at the project site we believe that the
proposed structures may be supported on a shallow foundation system consisting of spread
and or continuous footings As mentioned previously lava tubes and cavities may be present
in the basaltic lava flows at the project site To reduce the potential for loss of foundation
