Foundation Engineering Investigation
Replace Canopy Building 662
U S Naval Submarine Base
Pearl Harbor Oahu Hawaii
Special Project
In accordance with our proposal of we have
performed a soil and foundation engineering investigation at the above
referenced site
The purpose of the exploration was to explore the subsurface
conditions at the site to provide soil data for the use in the design
of foundations for the proposed replacement canopy
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests which
we used in the engineering analysis to formulate our conclusions and
recommendations are presented in the appendices of this report
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
SITE DESCRIPTION
The site is located in the U S Naval Submarine Base at Pearl
Harbor on the island of Oahu Hawaii The project site is a semi open
courtyard adjacent to the Lockwood Hall Unaccompanied Officer
Personnel Housing Building 662
This courtyard has a concrete slab on grade and is partially
covered with a metal canopy which is somewhat aged and damaged The
courtyard is bounded by Building 662 to the north and east south
Waterfront Street to the south and Moore Street to the west
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is our understanding that the existing metal canopy will be
removed and replaced with an enlarged concrete canopy Column loads
of up to 60 kips are anticipated
It is anticipated that the replacement canopy will be constructed
to match the existing facilities and that site grading will not be
required

Three borings were drilled around the perimeter of the proposed
building and one boring in the courtyard to depths between 15 5 feet
and 26 5 feet below the existing ground surface

Soil Conditions
Generally two different subsoil conditions were encountered at
the site
The western two thirds of the site is underlain by 1 to 5 feet of
clayey silt fill and decomposed tuff clayey gravel over hard
weathered tuff to about 15 feet deep The tuff is underlain by stiff
alluvial soils
The eastern one third of the site is underlain by about 5 feet of
clayey silt fill over loose sand and gravel estuarine lagoonal
deposits to about 20 feet deep underlain by stiff alluvial soils

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
In general the field exploration disclosed that the site is
underlain by relatively competent materials
Foundations
Shallow spread footing foundations may be used to support the
proposed canopy structure
In the western two thirds of the site the footings may bear on
the hard tuff formation encountered at 1 to 5 foot depth An
allowable bearing value of 5000 p s f may be used on the dense tuff
Alternatively the footings could bear on the stiff upper fill
material using a lower allowable bearing value of 3000 p s f
Anticipated settlements are estimated to be on the order of one fourth
of an inch total and differential for footings on the fill and minimal
for footings on the tuff
In the eastern portion of the site the footing may bear on the
stiff fill material Due to the loose underlying soil a lower
allowable bearing value of 2000 p s f is recommended Anticipated
settlements due to the foundation loads are estimated to be on the
order of one half of an inch total and differential
In general the footings should be embedded a minimum of 24
inches below the lowest adjacent grade

The bottom of the footing excavations in fill should be compacted
to 95 of its maximum dry density as established by ASTM D 1557 78
Footings next to the existing building should be excavated with
care to avoid disturbing the existing foundations
Lateral Resistance
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive
earth pressure developed against the buried vertical faces of the
foundation system and by frictional resistance developed between the
bottom of the foundations and the bearing soil
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be calculated using
an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 p c f This assumes that the
granular soil around the foundations is well compacted
The frictional resistance may be calculated by using a
coefficient of friction of 0 4
The above resistance values are maximum values and do not contain
a factor of safety

APPENDIX A
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by
drilling 5 to 26 5 feet below the existing ground
surface at the approximate locations shown on Site Plan
Plate 2 Truck mounted and portable drilling equipments were
used to drill the borings
The soils encountered in the borings and probings were
classified by visual and textural examination in the field by
our engineer who continously monitored the drilling operations
The classifications were further reviewed by visual inspection
and testing in the laboratory The soils were classified in
general conformance with the Unified Soil Classification System
A graphic presentation of the materials encountered is pre
sented on the Log of Borings Plates A l through A 4
Soil samples were obtained from the boring by driving
either a standard penetration sampler or a 2 4 inch 0 D split
barrel sampler with a 140 pound hammer free falling at a
height of 30 inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the
last 12 inches are shown on the logs of borings at the appro
priate sample depths
