SITE DESCRIPTION
The site is located on the extreme northern shore of Kauai
Hawaii The site is bounded on the south by Kuhio Highway
on the north by residential housing and the Princeville
Golf Course on the east by the Princeville Shopping Center
and Hanalei Plantation Road on the west
The ground surface is relatively flat with surveyed ele
vations ranging from 230 to 290 feet above sea level
There is a natural drainageway running along the north
central portion of the property The head of the drainage
way extends into the proposed shopping center expansion and
apartment sites This portion of the drainageway is about
10 to 20 feet deep with side slopes up to about 1 1 H V
Much of the portion of the drainageway extending into the
site was grubbed at the time of our field exploration
Surface water was not noted in the drainageway at the time
of our field work
There is a steep near vertical cut bank up to 20 feet high
adjacent to the northern boundary of Lot 24 A warehouse
structure is located at the bottom of this steep cut
An existing 30 inch drain pipe under Ka Haku Road empties
into the eastern portion of the site This drainage water
is carried through an open ditch near the northwestern bound
ary of the site into the existing golf course Six to

Most of the site is covered with grass shrubs and large
trees An existing tree nursery occupies most of the cen

GEOLOGY
The site is underlain at depth by Pleistocene Ice Age
Kola volcanic series basalt These basalt flows were de
posited late in the geologic history of Kauai and are rela
tively fresh and hard near the existing coastline After
the lava flows were deposited intermittant eruptions of
volcanic ash mixed with silt sand and gravel washed down
from the mountains The lava flows and overlying sediments
are now being weathered in place
The upper five 5 to ten 10 feet of soil consists of
residually in place weathered slope wash silt sand and
gravel The weathering has reduced the slope wash material
to red orange and brown silty clay to clayey silt contain
ing highly weathered basalt volcanic ash sand and gravel
At depths ranging from roughly three 3 to eight 8 feet
the soil grades downward into extremely weathered basalt
saprolite Saprolite is bedrock weathered to a soil like
consistancy but retaining relic rock and mineral texture
The saprolite at this site is often soft and wet in places
at depths of about three 3

found at the top of extremely weathered bedrock
SOIL CONDITIONS
In general the soils encountered in the borings consisted
of stiff brown to reddish brown clayey silt with weathered
gravel A layer of soft to medium stiff wet clayey silt
soil was encountered at depths of 3 to 8 feet in the bor
ings located in the proposed future apartment and shopping
center expansion sites 14 17 G 18
The site is in a high rainfall area and the subsurface soils
were generally moist Laboratory test indicate that these
soils have high in place moisture contents and low expansion
potential
Groundwater levels were not encountered in the borings during
the field exploration
CONCLUSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed generally competent residual
soils below most of the proposed site From a soils engin
eering point of view the site is suitable for the proposed
development
Conventional site grading methods could be used for the pro
ject site development
Generally shallow spread footing foundation systems could
be used to support the single family residences and light

one to two story structures Medium to soft soil conditions
were encountered at 3 to 8 foot depths in the upper borings
drilled in the central portion future apartment and shopping
center expansion of the project site Some settlement might
be expected The effect of this soft layer on the proposed
structures in this area would depend on the amount of new
fill and the imposed structural loading condition Additional
settlement studies would be required after site grading and
structural plans became available It is our present opinion
that shallow spread footing foundations could still be utilized
in this area
Site Grading
A preliminary grading plan was not available at the time of
our investigation However only relatively minor site cutting
is anticipated Most of the site filling would probably be
concentrated in the existing drainageway area in the central
portion of the site Up to about 15 feet of fill is antici
pated in the drainageway To fill up a low lying area in the
northern portion of Lot 23 up to about 10 feet of fill may
be required
The site should be cleared of vegetation surface debris and
other deleterious material In areas to receive fill the
ground should be scarified to a minimum depth of 6 inches
brought to near optimum moisture content and compacted to a

Since the project site is located in a high rainfall area
the on site subsoils have in place moisture contents from
40 to 70 percent The optimum moisture content is about
30 percent Therefore some amount of drying of the exca
vated material will be reqired before using them as fill
material in the embankment
Because of the high in situ moisture contents of the on
site borrow material and the site being located in a high
rainfall area it is our opinion that the normal 90 compac
tion standard used in other drier areas should be modified
to 85 for this project
Laboratory shear strength tests on compacted samples indi
cate that 85 compaction will be adequate for the roadway
embankment fill and light building loads anticipated
Fill Placement
Based on the field exploration and laboratory test results
in general the on site materials are suiteable for use in
compacted fill and backfill The laboratory swell tests
indicated that the on site soils in general have low

swelling potential therefore after site preparations the
fill should be placed in about 10 inch lifts compacted to a
minimum of 85 of the above specified maximum dry density
It is recommended that if additional off site borrow is
required it should be tested and approved by the soils engin
eer prior to transportation to the site to assure that it is
suitable for the proposed construction The material should
generally be non expansive and should not contain large per
centages of boulders organic or deleterious matter
Where the existing ground is steeper than 5 horizontal to
1 vertical 5 1 benching and keying are required to properly
bond the new fill to the slope The filling operation should
start at the lowest point and continue up in level compacted
layers in accordance with the above structural fill require
ments For contruction details refer to Plate 2
Slopes
Two horizontal to 1 vertical 2 1 fill slopes are recommended
Cut slope ratios up to 1 horizontal to 1 vertical 1 1
can be used for cuts into the very stiff residual soil
For cut and fill slopes greater than 20 feet in vertical
height 8 foot drainage benches should be placed at mid
height of the slope to reduce the amount of runoff over the
slopes

Surface water should be diverted away from the top of the
slope and slope planting utilized as soon as practicable
to reduce erosion
Subdrains
To reduce piping and seepage pressure build up subdrains
should be placed in the bottom of the drainageways prior
to filling
The subdrains should be placed in a herringbone pattern
For preliminary design purposes the laterals can be spaced
at 100 foot intervals The actual locations of the subdrain
will be determined in the field depending on the site condi
tions For the subdrain detail see Plate 3
Foundations
Generally stiff residual soils were encountered in the
eastern future apartment site and western single family
subdivision portions of the site For the anticipated
light residential structures spread footing foundation
can be used
In the central future apartment and shopping center expan
sion area soft to medium soil conditions were encountered
at about 3 B 8 to 8 B 18 feet below the existing ground
surface Spread footing foundation could also be utilized
for the proposed relatively light structures in this area

however the allowable bearing capacity and the anticipated
settlement of the structures would depend on the additional
load imposed on the soft soil by the new structures This
additional load would depend on the amount of fill placed
and structural loading of the new structures Since this
data is unavailable at the time recommended bearing capac
ity and anticipated settlements will have to be determined
when more definite plans are available In general site
grading involving cut should be avoided and it would be
desirable to place as much new fill as possible in order to
keep the underlying soft layer a minimum of about 8 to 10
feet below the finished grade
For the eastern future apartment site and western single
family subdivision portions of the project site the general
foundation design guidelines are presented in the following
sections
For the proposed light residential structures spread foot
ing foundations or slab on grade may be used To minimize
the effects of slope creep and erosion slabs should be set
back from the tops of slopes a minimum distance of 6 feet
Also post footings located close to the tops of slopes
should be embedded a sufficient depth to obtain a minimum
horizontal set back distance of 6 feet from the slope face
Post footings 6 feet or more away from the tops of slopes
should be placed 12 inches below the finish grade

For the light loads anticipated an allowable bearing
pressure of 2500 p s f can be used for footings resting
on the stiff residual soil or compacted fill
If a footing is located next to a utility trench the
footing should extend to the bottom of the utility trench
or be designed to span over the trench to reduce footing
settlement due to settlement of the trench backfill
Slab on grade should be placed over a 4 inch layer of cap
illary break material such as 3B Fine aggregate to prevent
infiltration of moisture into the floor slab
The thickened edge around the slab on grade should extend
a minimum of 8 inches below the adjacent ground surface
The slab subgrade soils should be kept moist and not be
allowed to dry our prior to concrete placement
The ground surface should be graded to drain water away
from the structure and foundations
Retaining Walls
Retaining walls may be required in localized areas For re
taining walls less than 6 feet high the following wall design
parimeters may be used
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f is recommended for
the walls resting on the stiff natural ground or compacted fill

Well graded granular material such as base course rock
sandor equivalent should be used as backfill material
directly behind the walls This would reduce the compac
tive effort required for the backfill and reduce the build
up of additional lateral pressures on the walls Imper
vious on site silty material should be used for the top
2 feet of the backfill
We recommend that the walls should be designed to resist
lateral earth pressures as follows
Level backfill k 40pcf equivalent fluid
active condition pressure
Additional loads due to surcharge should be
included where applicable
A friction factor of 0 4 between concrete and soil can be
utilized to determine the sliding resistance of the wall
Additionally a passive soil resistance of 300 for soil can
be used However the passive resistance should be reduced
by one third when combined with the frictional resistance
A subsurface drainage system should be used to prevent the
build up of hydrostatic pressure behind the walls

Asphalitic concrete pavement is planned for the development
roadways and parking areas

of the soils encountered is presented on the Boring Logs
Plates A 1 through A 23

APPENDIX B
LABORATORY TESTING
Moisture content and unit weight determination were
performed on selected samples as an aid in the classification
and evaluation of soil properties The results of these tests
are presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 23
Three swell tests on one inch ring samples of natural
samples were performed to evaluate the expansion potential
of the soils The test results are summarized on Plate B 1
Two CBR tests were performed on the near surface soils
to evaluate the suitability of the soil as pavement support
The results are presented on Plate B 2
Two direct shear tests were performed on selected
samples to evaluate the strength characteristics of the soils
The test results are plotted on Plate B 3
One moisture density determinations ASTM D 1557 was
performed on a bulk sample of the near surface soils to eval
uate its compaction characteristics The test result is
summarized on Plate B 2
Two consolidation tests were performed on undisturbed
samples of the underlying soils to evaluate its consolidation
characteristics The test results are presented on Plates
B 4 and B 5
