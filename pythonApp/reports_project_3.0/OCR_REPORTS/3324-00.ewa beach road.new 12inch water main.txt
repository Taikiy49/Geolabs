PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site Ewa Beach Road is located to the south of Fort Weaver Road
along the southern Ewa Beach shoreline of Oahu Hawaii as shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1 It is proposed to construct a new 12 inch water main and
associated appurtenances atthe site The proposed water main will be approximately
4 300 feet long Ground surface elevations along the relatively flat site range from
about 4 to 5 feet Mean Sea Level MSL
It is anticipated that brackish groundwater will be encountered during excavation
for the new water main Based on current design concepts dewatering of the
excavation along the pipeline alignment will likely be needed to facilitate the
construction
It is intended that the water from the dewatering operations will be discharged
to the City and County storm drain system Therefore a permit under the National

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our work on this phase of the project was to provide the drilling
monitoring well installation and groundwater sampling and testing services necessary
to obtain groundwater samples for the preparation of the NPDES permit application
Our scope of work generally consisted of the following tasks and work efforts
1 Procurement of excavation permits and utility clearances as well as field
toning for underground utilities
2 Mobilization and demobilization of a truck mounted drill rig and operators
to and from the project site
3 Coordination of traffic control during the field exploration
4 Drilling and installation of four groundwater monitoring wells to depths of
about 10 feet below the existing ground surface
5 Logging of the borings and supervision of the monitoring well installation
by a Geologist from our firm
6 Development of the four monitoring wells
7 Collection of groundwater samples from the wells by a Geologist from our
firm approximately 6 days following development of the monitoring wells
The chain of custody documentation and transportation of the water
samples to the laboratory were also performed by our Geologist
8 Preparation of a brief report summarizing our work on the project and
presenting our findings

Field Exploration
The drilling and well installations were conducted on September 28th and 29th
The monitoring wells were developed on September 29th and the water samples were
collected and transported to the analytical laboratory on October 6th
Drilling of the monitoring wells were accomplished using a truck mounted drill rig
augers and associated drilling and sampling tools were steam cleaned and washed with
a Liquinox detergent solution prior to the drilling of each borehole and sampling event
Subsurface Conditions
The four monitoring wells were installed in the existing paved roadway near the
water main alignment Monitoring Well No is located at about Station 3 50
and Monitoring Well No is located at about Station 14 25 Monitoring Well
No is located at about Station 24 70 and Monitoring Well No is
located at about Station 34 75 The approximate locations of the four monitoring wells
are presented on the Site Plan Plates 2 through 6
Subsurface materials were examined and classified in general accordance with
ASTM Standard Practice D 2488 84 by a Geologist from our firm Soil samples were
obtained from the drilled borings in accordance with ASTM Standard Method
D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving either a
inches
In general the borings encountered surface asphalt concrete pavement of
approximately of 2 to 5 inches followed by a thin layer of dense coralline sandy
gravel fill ranging in thickness between about 6 inches and 2 feet Beneath the thin fill
layer the borings generally encountered dense coral and limestone formation to the
maximum depth explored of about 10 feet below the existing ground surface

Groundwater Conditions
Shallow groundwater conditions were encountered during the drilling and
monitoring well installations The measured groundwater levels ranged in depth from
about 3 to 4 feet below the existing paved roadway surface This corresponds to a
mean groundwater table at about 1 3 feet MSL
Due to the close proximity of the shoreline and the Pacific Ocean it is likely that
the groundwater levels measured in the borings during our field exploration will be
affected by tidal fluctuation of the sea level In addition the groundwater level at the
project site may be influenced by seasonal precipitation and storm surge conditions
Monitoring Well Installation
After drilling to a depth of about 10 feet below the ground surface the monitoring
well casing was lowered into the borehole through the annulus of the hollow stem
augers The well casing consisted of 2 inch O D Schedule 40 flush threaded PVC
threaded PVC bottom cap made up the lower portion of the well Approximately 3 feet
of blank 2 inch O D Schedule 40 flush threaded PVC pipe composed the upper portion
of the well The blank well casing extended upward to within about 6 inches of the
ground surface and was capped with a padlocked expansion plug The well was
completed at the surface with a traffic rated well cover
The well was backfilled with a No 3 filter sand pack consisting of clean
uniformly graded kiln dried Monterey Sands which should be chemically stable within
the subsurface environment to reduce potential sorption from the groundwater A 1 foot
thick bentonite seal was placed above the screened section and hydrated to seal the
monitoring well The traffic rated well cover was then cemented in place The
bentonite seal was placed to avoid contact with the underlying groundwater table which
is approximately 3 to 4 feet below the existing ground surface A sketch showing the
typical monitoring well detail for the project is presented on Plate 7
The groundwater samples from the monitoring wells were collected on
October 6th approximately 6 days following well development Each well was purged
by bailing a minimum of three well casing volumes from the well prior to the collection
of the samples Groundwater samples were collected and transported to an analytical
laboratory for analyses Results of the laboratory tests quality control data and the
chain of custody forms are presented on Plates B 1 through B 6 of Appendix B
