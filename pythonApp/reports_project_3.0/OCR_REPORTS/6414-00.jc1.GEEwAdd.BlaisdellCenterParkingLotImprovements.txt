pavement Over the years several pavement areas have experienced continual
settlement We performed three explorations in 2001 2007 and 2010 to evaluate

recommendations were presented in our reports entitled Geotechnical Engineering
Exploration Blaisdell Center Pavement Distress Evaluation Neal Blaisdell Center

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lavas from the
Waianae and Koolau shield volcanoes The older Waianae Volcano is estimated to be
middle to late Pliocene in age and forms the majority of the western one third of the island
The younger Koolau Volcano is estimated to be late Pliocene to early Pleistocene
Ice Age in age and forms the bulk of the eastern two thirds of the island After a long
period of volcanic inactivity during which time erosion incised deep valleys into the

Koolau Shield volcanic activity returned with a series of lava flows followed by cinder and
tuff cone formations These series are referred to as the Honolulu Volcanic Series
The project site is on the southern flank of the Koolau Volcano and on the coastal
plain of Southern Oahu Therefore the geomorphology and subsurface conditions in the
area are directly related to the glacio eustatic fluctuations of the sea level during the
Pleistocene Epoch Ice Age and the genesis of the Honolulu Coastal Plain The coastal
plain was built on the eroded flanks of the Koolau Volcano which forms the eastern
two thirds of the Island of Oahu The coastal plain was built by extensive accumulation of
alluvium derived from erosion of the volcano interbedded with coral reefs and associated
deposits
During the Pleistocene Epoch Ice Age sea levels fluctuated in response to the
cycles of continental glaciation Most of the coastal plains were developed during the
Pleistocene Epoch when the sea levels fluctuated significantly As the glaciers grew and
advanced less water was available to fill the oceanic basins such that sea levels fell below
the present stands of the sea When the glaciers melted and receded an excess of water
became available such that the sea levels rose to above its present level
The processes of erosion and deposition were affected by these glacio eustatic sea
level fluctuations When the sea level was low the erosional base level was
correspondingly lower and valleys were carved to depths below the present sea level
When the sea level was high the erosional base level was raised such that sediments
accumulated at higher elevations
The subsurface conditions in the Ala Moana Kaka ako area are dominated by fossil
Pleistocene Age coral algal reef deposits and related deposits which are intercalated with
terrigenous sediments laid during the higher stands of the sea Therefore the project site
is generally underlain by deposits consisting of calcareous sediments and lagoonal
deposits A surface layer of man made fill was placed over these deposits for the
development of the Ala Moana Kaka ako area within the last century
FIELD EXPLORATION
We explored the subsurface conditions at the project site by drilling and sampling
five borings designated as Boring Nos 101 through 105 The borings extended to
depths ranging from about 15 to 17 feet below the existing ground surface using a
truck mounted drill rig equipped with continuous flight hollow stem augers The boring
locations are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Our geologists classified the materials encountered in the borings by visual and
textural examination in the field in general accordance with ASTM D 2488 Standard
Practice for Description and Identification of Soils and monitored the drilling operations on
a near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by
testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general accordance with ASTM D 2487
Standard Practice for Classification of Soils for Engineering Purposes Unified Soil

LABORATORY TESTS
Samples recovered from our field exploration were visually examined and classified
in our laboratory in Honolulu Hawaii Selected samples were tested to evaluate the
physical characteristics and engineering properties of the soils encountered The
laboratory testing was formulated with the emphasis on evaluating the moisture density
plasticity gradation shear strength and compressibility of the soils encountered
Moisture Content and Dry Density tests were performed on selected soil samples
to aid in defining the interface between the various soil layers encountered and
correlating the layers between the borings The moisture content tests were performed
in general accordance with ASTM D 2216 The dry density tests were performed in
general accordance with ASTM D 2937 The test results are presented on the Logs of
Borings at the appropriate sample depths in Appendix A
Two Atterberg Limits tests were performed on selected soil samples to evaluate the
liquid limit LL plastic limit PL and plasticity index PI as an aid in classifying the soils
The Atterberg Limits tests were performed in general accordance with ASTM D 4318 The
test results are summarized on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths in
Appendix A Graphic presentation of the test results are provided on Plate B 1 of
Appendix B
To provide information on the particle size distribution of the soils encountered
five Sieve Analysis tests were performed to evaluate the gradation characteristics and
to aid in soil classification The tests were performed in accordance with ASTM C 136
and D 422 Graphic presentation of the test results are provided on Plate B 2 of
Appendix B

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our field exploration consisted of drilling and sampling five borings designated as
Boring Nos 101 through 105 extending to depths of approximately 15 to 17 feet below the
existing ground surface The boring locations are shown on the Site Plans Plate 2
Based on the subsurface materials encountered in Boring No 101 near the
northeastern end of the facility the subsurface conditions consist of about 3 feet of dense
sandy fills and about 5 feet of medium dense sandy volcanic cinder overlying dense
weathered coralline sandstone at about 8 to 9 feet below the ground surface We
encountered groundwater at a depth of about 11 feet below the ground surface at the time
of our field exploration
Boring No 102 was drilled along the driveway between the parking structure and
facility loading area The subsurface conditions consist of about 2 feet of medium dense
sandy fills and about 7 feet of medium dense sandy volcanic cinder overlying dense
weathered coralline sandstone at about 9 to 10 feet below the ground surface We
encountered groundwater at a depth of about 10 feet below the ground surface at the time
of our field exploration
Boring No 103 was drilled adjacent to the facility loading area next to the exhibition
hall The boring encountered about 6 feet of medium dense sandy and stiff clayey fills over
about 8 feet of loose sandy volcanic cinder Loose sandy alluvium was encountered below
the cinder to 17 feet depth of the boring We encountered groundwater at depth of about
6 5 feet below the ground surface at the time of our field exploration
Boring Nos 104 and 105 were drilled in the on grade parking areas between Neal
Blaisdell Arena and Kapiolani Boulevard The borings extended to a depth of about 17 feet
below the ground surface The subsurface conditions consisted of 3 to 4 feet of sandy
and or clayey fills overlying loose lagoonal deposits with a pocket of medium dense sandy
volcanic cinder We encountered groundwater at depth of about 6 feet below the ground
surface at the time of our field exploration

FINDINGS AND DISCUSSIONS
Based on our field observation and the subsurface conditions encountered in the
borings we believe that pavement distresses including depression settlement alligator
and or block cracking rutting and concrete curb buckling may be caused by a
combination of soft loose compressible soil and aging asphalt concrete pavement with
localized tree roots
Based on the existing pavement conditions observed and subsurface conditions
encountered in the borings the project site may be divided into the following
three sub areas as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 and repaired by reconstruction and or
rehabilitation
Area 101
2 inch AC removal and replacement with 2 inch new AC pavement
Area 102
3 foot excavation and replacement with Geogrid reinforced fill and
new pavement section of 4 inch AC over 6 inch aggregate base
course
Area 103
2 foot excavation and replacement with Geogrid reinforced fill and
new pavement section of 2 inch AC over 6 inch aggregate base
course

Fill and Compaction
The aggregate select borrow should generally confirm to Section 30 of the
Standard Specifications for Public Works Construction September 1986 The aggregate
select borrow should be placed no more than 12 inches in loose lift and
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content The fill should be
compacted to a minimum 95 percent relative compaction ASTM D 1557
Due to potential subsurface variation the limits and depths of the excavation should
be modified during construction based on observation by a Geolabs representative
Geolabs should be retained for geotechnical engineering services during
construction The critical items of construction monitoring which require Special
Inspection include observation of the subgrade preparation excavations and fill and
backfill placement A Geolabs representative should also monitor other aspects of
earthwork construction to observe compliance with the intent of the design concepts
specifications or recommendations and to expedite suggestions for design changes
that may be required in the event that subsurface conditions differ from those
anticipated at the time this report was prepared The recommendations provided herein
are contingent upon such observations If the actual soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this report then
appropriate design modifications should be made
