The proposed highrise apartment
building can be supported on piles

40 to 55 feet below the existing
ground Predrilling for the piles
would be required through the existing

INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our soil and foundation
investigation for the proposed 27 story apartment building
The scope of this report is to evaluate the subsurface con
ditions at the site and to provide recommendations for the
design and construction of the foundations of the proposed
structure

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed highrise building will be a 27 story reinforced
concrete structure approximately 70 by 75 feet in plan
Half of the parking area will be on grade while the other
half will utilize a semi basement level
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located in the Salt Lake area of
Honolulu between Ala Ilima and Likini Streets A terrace
approximately 5 feet high running in an east west direction
divides the site The surface elevation on the upper portion
ranges from about 30 to 34 feet above sea level with the
drainage directed southward towards Ala Ilima Street The
surface elevation of the lower portion ranges from about
18 to 29 feet and slopes uniformly northward toward Likini
Street at a 5 gradient There are no existing structures
on the site At the time of the investigation the site
was covered with low grass and sparse trees and shrubs

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The project site is located in Salt Lake Crater a volcanic
ash and cinder cone which formed during the Honolulu Series
of late volcanic eruptions along with the adjacent Alia
manu crater The Salt Lake cone rests on an old brown
soil which can be seen in a series of road cuts through
its outer flanks
During the formation of the cone the ash and cinder frag
ments were welded together by heat forming a hard rock or
welded tuff This welded tuff is referred to locally as
mudrock Because the tuff is hard and fairly impervious
the crater formed a lake in its center and was partially
filled with sediment from the erosion of tuff and ashy
soil along the upper slopes of the crater and from depo
sition of carbonates and salts during periods of dessication
and possibly by calcareous algae The carbonate minerals
are mixed withthe land derived debris in many places
beneath the site forming unconsolidated sands silts and
clays
Later marshes and swampy areas formed along the perimeter
of the lake Finally a 12 to 25 foot layer of man made
fill consisting of sand and tuff boulders was placed over
the previous lake and shoreline deposits

Subsurface Exploration
Two new borings were drilled at the site to depths of
60 to 65 feet below the existing ground surface to
explore the subsurface conditions and to obtain samples
for laboratory testing Boring logs from a previous

logs and laboratory test results
Soil Conditions
Based on an analysis of the test borings and soil samples
the site is immediately underlain by a brown to gray man made
boulder fill mudrock fill varying in thickness from about
12 to 22 feet below the existing surface The fill contains
many cobbles and large boulders of Salt Lake tuff ina
matrix of silty sand Generally split spoon blow counts
obtained in the fill material are relatively high 40 50
blows foot due to encountering dense boulders It is
possible that there are soft areas present in the fill
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P 5
Underlying the man made fill is a 25 to 35 foot layer of
layered lake deposits of sands silts and clays In
general these lake deposits are more clayey on top and
become more sandy and gravelly with depth This deposit
consists of basically land derived sediment of weathered
tuff mixed with varying amount of carbonate sands and
clays Located beneath the lake deposits at a depth of
40 to 55 feet below the existing ground is the Salt Lake
tuff formation which ranges from a very hard dense rock
to deposits of black sand The tuff formation is at least
17 feet thick in the southern section of the site as indi
cated in Boring No 2
Groundwater was encountered in the borings at 18 to 20
foot depths as shown on the boring logs Plates A 1
through A 4
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Foundations
Our investigation indicates that the site is underlain at
the near surface by a fairly competent layer of mudrock
fill However due to the high column loads and the
presence of a thick compressible layer of lake sediments
below the mudrock fill the highrise structure should be
supported on a pile foundation bearing on the underlying
Salt Lake tuff mudrock The parking structure can be
supported by shallow foundations bearing on the mudrock fill
GEOLABS HAWAII
W
Tower Structure
Pile foundations are recommended for the highrise
tower structure
Predrilling through the upper mudrock fill about
20 to 25 feet thick will be required prior to pile
driving The predrilled holes should be about 18
inches in diameter The piles should be placed
and driven immediately after the hole is drilled
Design recommendations for the pile foundations
are given below
1 Sixteen and one half 16 inch octagonal
prestressed concrete piles are recommended
2 The piles should extend down to the hard
volcanic tuff mudrock formation Based
on the borings estimated pile lengths of
45 to 55 feet are anticipated
3 The piles should be driven with a hammer
delivering about 30 000 foot pounds of
energy The piles should be driven to a
final resistance of 100 blows per foot for
the last foot but not overdriven to more
than 15 blows per inch or fraction of an
inch
GEOLABS HAWAII
W
4 Test piles should be driven prior to ordering
production piles in order to establish pile
driving criteria and order lengths A static
load test should be performed on a test pile
to confirm the design load capacity of the
pile
5 An allowable design load capacity of 100 tons
per pile is recommended
6 The piles should not be spaced closer than
3 diameters
7 It is recommended that the pile driving be done
under the guidance of a soils engineer to see
that proper pile penetration is achieved
Parking Structure
Spread or continuous footing foundations are
recommended for the two story parking structure
The footings should be placed a minimum of 24
inches below the finish grade
An allowable bearing value of 3000 p s f is
recommended for the footings bearing on the
dense mudrock fill
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P 8
The bottom of the footing excavation should be
tamped prior to pouring concrete
For the above recommendations estimated settle
ments of less than one half inch total and one
fourth inch differential are anticipated
The ramp structure between the parking structure
and the tower should be designed to tolerate
some differential movement
Uplift Resistance
An allowable uplift resistance of 10 tons per pile is
recommended This assumes that the pile will be driven
a minimum of 20 feet below the predrilled hole and splices
will not be utilized above this length
Lateral Resistance
To resist lateral loads an allowable resistance value
of 1 kip per pile is recommended
Basement Wall
Basement walls are planned for portions of the parking
structure
Well graded granular material such as base course rock
should be used as backfill material behind the walls
Impervious material should be used for the top 2 feet of
the backfill
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P 9
The recommendations given under the parking structure
spread footing foundations should be followed for the
design of the wall foundations
For lateral earth pressures the basement walls should
be designed to resist an equivalent fluid pressure of
60 p c f Additional load due to traffic should be
included where applicable
A friction factor of 0 45 can be utilized to determine
the sliding resistance of the wall foundation Addi
tionally a passive soil resistance of 350 p c f may
be used
A subsurface drainage system should be used to prevent
the build up of hydrostatic pressure behind the walls
Site Grading
A few feet of fill is planned to raise the site Several
feet of cut will be required for the lower parking area
The site should be cleared of all existing vegetation
and debris
Select on site granular material should be used for fill
and footing backfill material The fill and backfill should
be placed in 8 inch maximum horizontal loose lifts and com
pacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density estab
lished by ASTM Test Method D 1557
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P
Slab On Grade
Slab on grade should be placed over a 4 inch layer of
base course generally less than 3 4 inch and greater
than 1 4 inch in size The subgrade should be kept
moist prior to pouring the concrete
Driveway and Parking Area
For the A C driveway and parking areas the following
preliminary pavement design is recommended
2 inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 inch Base Course
8 inch Total Pavement Thickness
CBR tests should be performed on the actual subgrade
soil samples at the time of construction and the above
design section can be revised accordingly
The plans for foundations site grading and utility
construction should be forwarded to the soils engineer
for review and comments prior to construction to see
that the intent of our recommendations is included ir
the project plans and specifications
GEOLABS HAWAII

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the pile predrilling and driving
footing excavations and backfill operations be observed
by a soils engineer during construction to see that
adequate bearing has been obtained and that the subsur
face conditions are consistent with the findings of our
subsurface investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive
tested and approved by the soils engineer prior to
hauling to the site

APPENDIX A
FIELD EXPLORATION
ee
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored
by drilling two borings using trailer mounted portable
rotary drilling equipment with continuous flight solid
stem 4 inch auger The borings were drilled to depths of
60 to 65 feet below the existing ground surface Previous
borings ranging in depth from 50 to 60 feet are included
in Appendix C
The soils encountered in the borings were classified
by visual and textural examination in the field by our
geologist who continuously monitored the drilling opera
tions The classifications were reviewed by visual inspec
tion and testing in the laboratory The soils were classified
in general conformance with the Unified Soil Classification
System A graphic presentation of the soils is presented
on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 4
In the borings soil samples were obtained by driving
split barrel sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30
inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the last
12 inches are shown on the boring logs at the appropriate
depths
