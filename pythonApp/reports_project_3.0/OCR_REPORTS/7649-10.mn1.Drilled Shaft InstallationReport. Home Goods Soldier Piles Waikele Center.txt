As requested, Geolabs, Inc. provided geotechnical engineering observation 
services during the drilled shaft construction in support of the soldier pile installation for 
lateral support of the existing retaining wall at the above project. This letter report 
summarizes the results of our field observations during the drilled shaft construction 
work.  The project location and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map, 
Plate 1. 
In accordance with our fee proposal dated November 6, 2017 and Agreement for 
Professional Services entered into on November 7, 2017 with AAT Waikele Center LLC, 
Geolabs observed the drilled shaft construction in support of the installation of 
40 soldier piles.  
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The project site is located at the existing Waikele Center in the Waipahu area on 
the Island of Oahu, Hawaii.  We understand that it is desired to  provide lateral support 
for the existing Concrete Masonry Unit (CMU) retaining wall adjacent to Lumiaina 
Street, which is now free standing on the downhill (Makai) side after partial demolition of 
the former K-Mart Store building. A total of 40 soldier piles, consisting of 24 and 30-inch 
diameter drilled shafts extending to depths of about 20 and 22 feet below the existing 
grade, respectively, were designed to provide the necessary lateral load resistance 
along the exposed face of the existing retaining wall.

DRILLED SHAFT CONSTRUCTION FOR SOLDIER PILE INSTALLATION 
Maryl Group is the general contractor and Foundations International of Hawaii 
conducted the drilled shaft construction for the project.  In general, the Contractor 
installed a total of 40 drilled shafts, designated as Shaft Nos. 1 through 40, from 
November 8 through December 4, 2018 at the approximate locations shown on the Site 
Plan/Drilled Shaft Location Plan, Plate 2. Details of the drilled shaft installation are 
presented in the Drilled Shaft Installation Records in Appendix A and summarized on 
Plates 3.1 and 3.2. 
Two sets of drilled shafts were constructed for the soldier pile installation. The 
first set, including Shaft Nos. 1 through 22, consisted of drilling 30-inch diameter holes 
to a minimum depth of at least 22.25 feet to receive a 1’2”x1’2.5” steel H-section soldier 
pile. The second set, including Shaft Nos. 23 to 40, consisted of drilling 24-inch 
diameter holes to a minimum depth of 20.25 feet to receive a 1’2”x10” steel H-section 
soldier pile. 
In general, the drilled shafts were installed using a Watson track-mounted drilling 
rig equipped with 24 and 30-inch diameter tools advancing to the required minimum 
depths of at least 22.25 and 20.25 feet below the existing grade for Shaft Nos. 1 
through 22 and Shaft Nos. 23 through 40, respectively. 
After the drilled holes were extended to the required depths and cleaned out, a 
Geolabs representative inspected the bottom of the excavated shafts to determine that 
they are relatively clean and free of excessive debris from the drilling operation. Loose 
materials accumulated at the bottom of the drilled shafts were cleaned out with the 
appropriate tools.   
Upon completion of the drilling of the shaft holes and prior to concreting, the 
pre-fabricated, galvanized steel soldier H-pile sections were lowered to the design 
depths, aligned, and secured in-place with bolts and temporary clamps. 
Concrete was supplied and delivered on-site by Island Ready Mix Concrete. The 
concrete supplied generally had a slump of 7 +/- 1” and a 28-day design compressive 
strength of 3,000 pounds per square inch (psi). The concrete was pumped into the hole 
starting from the bottom through a 4-inch diameter PVC tremie pipe until sound 
concrete appeared at the top of the shaft hole. 
Based on our field observations, it is our professional opinion that the drilled 
shafts for the project have been installed to the required diameter and sufficient depths 
in accordance with the plans provided to achieve the intended design lateral load 
capacity.
