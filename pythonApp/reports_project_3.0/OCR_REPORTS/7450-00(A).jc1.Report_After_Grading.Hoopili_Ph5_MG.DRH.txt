PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The proposed Ho’opili Development project is to the south of the Interstate Route 
H-1 Highway and west of Fort Weaver Road in the Ewa area on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii, 
as shown on the Project Location Map, Plate 1. The Ho`opili development encompasses 
approximately 1,500 acres. The project site is gently sloping and was previously used for 
agricultural cultivation. 
The Phase 5 area is located at the west-central portion of the development, northeast 
and northwest of the Phases 2 and 4 development sites, respectively. The component of 
the Phase 5 project encompassed approximately 42 acres for multi-family structures, 
community development, and infrastructure development for roadways and underground 
utilities through the site in generally north-south and east-west directions. The 
development of the Phase 5 includes Parcels 18, 19, 26 & 27, one private community 
center and one private community garden, as shown on the Site Plan, Plate 2. 
The mass grading consisted of minor fills and cuts of up to about 6 feet deep, to 
achieve the design finished grades. Royal Contracting Construction Company performed 
the earthwork operations for the project.

SITE GRADING 
At the onset of earthwork, areas within the contract grading limits were generally 
cleared and grubbed of vegetation and deleterious materials. Materials generated from 
the clearing operations were disposed of off-site. Following the clearing and grubbing 
operations, the upper 12 inches of cultivated soils below the existing ground surface were 
removed and disposed of off-site or used in landscaped areas. After removal of the upper 
12 inches of cultivated soils, a 2-foot thick rework zone was generated by elevating the 
in-situ soil moisture content to about 2 to 4 percent above the optimum moisture content 
and compacting to about 85 to 90 percent of the maximum dry density as determined in 
accordance with ASTM D1557. Compaction effort was established using a sheepsfoot 
and/or smooth vibratory drum roller. 
Soft and yielding areas encountered during site grading were generally 
over-excavated to expose firm natural material, and the resulting excavation was backfilled 
with well-compacted engineering fill. 
The fill and backfill materials were moisture-conditioned to above the optimum 
moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density 
as determined in accordance with ASTM D1557. In the roadway areas, the subgrade was 
capped by 18 inches of select granular fills and compacted to a minimum 95 percent of 
the maximum dry density. 
During the site grading operations, samples of the materials utilized as fill were 
obtained and transported to our laboratory for testing.  The maximum dry densities and 
optimum moisture contents were established in general accordance with ASTM D1557 
(Modified Proctor), and the test results are summarized on Plate 3. 
Periodic in-situ moisture and density tests were conducted in general accordance 
with ASTM D6938 (Nuclear Gauge Method), to determine the adequacy of compaction of 
fills and backfills during the mass grading operation.  
The test results are summarized on Plates 4. These tests were performed on 
moisture-conditioned and compacted lifts of fills and backfills. Test locations and 
elevations were estimated using on-site survey stakes for reference. These locations and 
elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods 
used. Areas deemed unsatisfactory were reworked until the measured relative 
compaction conformed to or exceeded the minimum specified for the project. 
This report summarizes our field observations and compaction testing 
services during the site grading work for the period covering January 14 through 
November 25, 2019.
