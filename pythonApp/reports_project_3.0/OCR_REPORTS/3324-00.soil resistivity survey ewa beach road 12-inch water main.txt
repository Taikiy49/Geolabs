SUMMARY REPORT
SOIL RESISTIVITY SURVEY
EWA BEACH ROAD 12 INCH WATER MAIN
EWA BEACH OAHU HAWAII
In accordance with your request Geolabs Hawaii has completed a soil resistivity
survey for a new 12 inch water main along Ewa Beach Road in the District of Ewa on
the island of Oahu Hawaii The approximate location of the site is shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1 Our work was performed in general accordance with
our proposal dated This report presents the results of our soil
resistivity survey and summarizes our work on this project
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project area Ewa Beach Road is located to the south of Fort Weaver Road
along the southern Ewa Beach shoreline of Oahu as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
It is proposed to construct a new 12 inch water main of approximately 4 300 lineal feet
beneath Ewa Beach Road from about Station 0 20 to Station 41 98 adjacent to
existing underground utilities It is our understanding that a 12 inch ductile iron or PVC
pipe is planned for the water main at the subject area In general the average invert
of the proposed water main is anticipated to be between 4 and 5 feet below the existing
ground surface with some localized dips to about 6 to 8 feet below the existing ground
surface Soil resistivity surveys utilizing the in Pin Method were used to
evaluate the corrosion rating of the near surface soils within the levels of the proposed
water main

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our survey was to obtain an overview of the electrical resistivity
of the subsurface soils at the site Our work on this project was performed in general
conformance with ASTM Test Designation G 57 78 and with the requirements provided
in the Water System External Corrosion Control Standards Volume 3 dated 1991
and subsequent amendments and additions The scope of our work consisted of the
following work efforts
1 Performance of 48 in situ soil resistivity tests at 14 locations along Ewa
Beach Road These locations generally correspond to the 300 foot
spacing maximum required in the Water System External Corrosion
Control Standards Volume 3 1991
2 Coordination of the field work by an Engineer from our firm
3 Preparation of six copies of this letter report summarizing our work on the
project
4 Quality assurance of our work and client design team consultation by a
Principal Engineer of our firm
5 Miscellaneous work efforts such as drafting word processing and clerical
support
FIELD EXPLORATION
Geophysical survey equipment i e electrical resistance meter was mobilized
to the site on September 20th and 23rd and our field work was performed in sunny
conditions with generally dry surface conditions
Forty eight separate soil resistivity surveys were conducted at 14 locations as
shown on the Site Plans Plates 2 through 6 using the Wenner 4 Pin Method and a
resistance meter Strata Scout R 40C manufactured by SoilTest Inc The resistance
meter has a rated output of 120 volts and 24 watts
The Wenner 4 Pin Method utilizes four copper surface pins electrodes driven
into the ground surface at calculated spacings An electrical current is applied to the
electrodes to evaluate the average resistivity of the underlying soils beneath the pin

layout The average electrical resistivity of the soil mass extending from the ground
surface to the planned invert elevations of the water main were assessed by using this
method at the test locations shown on the Site Plans Plates 2 through 6
The soil resistivity tests were performed at the existing ground surface on the
exposed roadway shoulder adjacent to the asphalt pavement at each of the test
locations as shown on the Site Plans The four electrodes were placed at equal
spacings of generally 3 to 6 feet between pins Among the variable electrode spacings
used for each survey one spacing may have more significance than the other two
allowing greater contribution to the measured soil resistivity from the soil mass nearer
to the proposed pipeline invert elevation The more significant spacing and therefore
more representative resistivity data for the soil located near the pipeline invert is
marked with an asterisk on the resistivity test results provided on Plates 7 1 through
7 3
Based on the undated plan and profile received by our office on September 12th
the water main alignment between Station 0 00 and about Station 2 35 will be located
near the center of Ewa Beach Road The water main alignment between about
Stations 2 35 and 39 55 will be located on the northern side of Ewa Beach Road The
water main alignment between about Stations 39 55 and 42 00 will be located on the
eastern side of Ewa Beach Road In general the soil resistivity surveys were
conducted at selected locations along the shoulders of the road closest to the new
water line alignment where open ground could be found
The resistance meter R 40C induces a voltage between the outer electrodes
causing an electrical current to flow between them The voltage drop between the inner
electrodes is then measured by a sensitive built in voltmeter As a result the soil
resistance in Ohms can be read directly from the dial The soil resistivity in Ohm
cm which is directly related to both resistance and electrode spacing can then be
computed The evaluated soil resistance the computed resistivities and the
corresponding corrosion ratings are presented on Plates 7 1 through 7 3
Resistivity is generally recognized as one of the most significant soil
characteristics with regard to the corrosivity of the soil In general the lower the
resistivity the greater the potential for corrosion of buried metallic structures
Conversely the higher the resistivity the less likely the soil will contribute to corrosion

LIMITATIONS
The evaluations presented in this report are based upon information obtained
from field data points by means of geophysical surveys Variations of soil conditions
between the field data points may occur and the nature and extent of such variations
may not become evident until a later point in time If variations then appear evident
it will be necessary to reevaluate the ratings provided in this report
Locations and elevations of the data presented in this report are approximate
having been estimated from features and reference points shown on the undated
construction plan prepared by Okahara Associates Inc Elevations of the survey
locations were interpolated between the elevation points shown on the same plan The
locations and elevations of the data presented should be considered accurate only to
the degree implied by the methods used
