In accordance with the project specifications one of the three compressive pile load
tests was conducted on a selected test pile designated as TP 8 for the MCON Project
P 123 Berthing Wharves project between December 1 and 3 2000 This letter report
presents the results of our field observations and the test data obtained during the pile load
test
The pile load test was conducted on a 20 inch square precast prestressed
concrete pile Based on the project specifications Test Pile TP 8 was instrumented with
12 vibrating wire embedment strain gages Model No VCE 4200 manufactured by Geokon
The embedment strain gages were installed in the load test pile at the following depths below
the existing deck surface with an elevation of approximately 8 2 feet MLLW A schematic
sketch showing the locations of the embedment strain gages is provided on Plate 1

Test Pile TP 8 located at Bent 20 and Line A was driven on The
test pile was driven using a Delmag D 36 hammer delivering a maximum energy of
113 160 Joules 83 820 foot pounds During the test pile driving a record of the blow counts
and field observations of the driving behavior was maintained by a project engineer
Geotechnical QC Specialist from our office The test pile driving record is provided based
on metric units Plate 2 1 and English units Plate 2 2
Test Pile TP 8 was driven to a depth of about 28 6 meters 93 8 feet below the
existing deck surface The test pile was driven to depth when approximately 3 meters
9 9 feet of the pile was left above the existing deck surface Therefore the total length of
the load test pile is 31 6 meters 103 7 feet measured from the top to the tip of the test pile
The 12 embedment strain gages were connected using a multiplexer to a Geokon Micro 10
datalogger to record the strains in the 12 embedment strain gages during the compressive
pile load test
The test pile was loaded in general accordance with the Standard Loading Procedure
of ASTM Test Designation D 1143 The design load of the pile was 300 kips with a design
factor of safety of 2 25 Therefore the maximum test load on the pile was planned at
675 kips In general the test pile was loaded to the maximum test load of 675 kips in 75 kip
load increments Each load increment was held for at least 15 minutes or until the rate of pile
head movement was generally stable After loading the test pile to the maximum test load
of 675 kips the maximum test load was held for 24 hours as specified Subsequently the
test load was unloaded in approximately four equal decrements and was held at each
decrement for at least 15 minutes The load test readings and the load displacement curve
are presented on Plates 3 and 4 of this report The results of the compressive pile load test
are summarized as follows

the gage yet apart from this it experiences the same
