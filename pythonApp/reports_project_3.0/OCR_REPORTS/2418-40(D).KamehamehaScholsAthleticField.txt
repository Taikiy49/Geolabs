This letter report summarizes our geotechnical observations and earthwork testing
services performed during the site grading and utility installation for the Multi Purpose
Athletic Field project at Kamehameha Schools Our work was performed in general
accordance with the services outlined in our fee proposal dated
In general this letter report presents our observations and the results of the tests
performed on the earthwork for the project between and
The general contractor for the project was A C Kobayashi Inc and the earthwork
subcontractor for the project was Koga Engineering and Construction Inc
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The multi purpose athletic field project site is located within Kamehameha Schools
in the Kapalama Heights area of Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The
approximate location of the project site is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The multi purpose athletic field is located at the former football field location and to the
west of the baseball field Mauwaena Field
One of the major items of construction for the project is the grading for the athletic
field The site grading for the project consisted of cuts of up to about 40 feet deep and fills
on the order of about 35 feet thick The excavation and embankment quantities for the
project consisted of approximately 98 500 and 52 000 cubic yards respectively The
grading plan for the project is shown on the Site Plan Plates 2 and 3

SITE GRADING AND UTILITY INSTALLATION
The site grading and utility installation work for the Multi Purpose Athletic Field
project was observed by Geolabs Inc between and In
general the site was cleared and grubbed prior to The site grading work
consisted of the placement and compaction of fills on the order of about 35 feet thick in
order to achieve the finished grades for the project The site grading and utility installation
work were performed by Koga Engineering and Construction Inc
During the site grading and backfilling operations samples of the on site and
imported materials utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to
our office for testing The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were
established in our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557
Modified Proctor and the test results are summarized on Plate 4 It should be noted that
some of the excavated on site basalt rock was crushed and screened for use as select
granular fill material for the project
In general the fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to above
optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum
dry density as determined in accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557 In the new
roadway areas the fill and backfill materials within 2 feet of the finished subgrade were
compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative density
During the site grading and utility backfilling operations periodic field density tests
were performed in general accordance with ASTM Test Designations D 2922 and D 3017
Nuclear Gauge Method The results of the compaction tests performed on the fills and
subgrades are summarized on Plates 5 1 through 5 57 of this report These tests were
generally performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills
at the site A total of 950 field density tests including retests of failed areas were
performed in support of the site grading and utility installation for the project
Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from the
available on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should be considered
accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed to be
unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained and or
approved by our field representative based on visual observation and hand probing
Some unforeseen subsurface conditions were encountered during the earthwork
operations for the project It should be noted that subsurface water was encountered
during the site grading at the Field Maintenance and North Meeting Room area
Therefore a subdrain system was installed below the floor slab and connected to a
drainage manhole structure for proper discharge
In addition several surface depressions were noted on the paved track at or near
an irrigation line traversing the track The surface depressions above the irrigation line
