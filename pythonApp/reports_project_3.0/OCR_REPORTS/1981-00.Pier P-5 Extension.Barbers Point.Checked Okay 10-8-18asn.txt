evaluation performed for the proposed Pier P 5 Extension in Barbers Point

on the Project Location Map Plate 1

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to provide an additional 600 linear feet of berthing
facility on the eastern side of the originally proposed Pier P 5 which
will extend approximately 60 feet offshore and 1000 linear feet along the
shore in Barbers Point Harbor basin
Pile foundations for the pier will be embedded in previously dredged
slopes in coralline deposits with a slope angle of approximately 40
degrees The anticipated lateral load for both in land and offshore
directions is about 6 kips per linear foot of pier length
Site preparation involving cuts along the shore is anticipated since
the finished deck elevation is 9 0 feet above mean sea level MSL and the
existing ground elevation is about 12 to 14 feet MSL
A riprap wall is planned along the slope for wave erosion protection
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our evaluation was to explore the subsurface
conditions at the site in order to provide pertinent soil data for
foundation design of the proposed extension Of critical concern would be
the following
1 The characteristics of the underlying coralline deposits and the
suitable pile embedment depth
2 The lateral load capacity of piles adjacent to the dredged
slopes
3 The anticipated pile driving characteristics

SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is located at the southeastern side of the Barbers
Point Harbor basin Materials dredged during construction of the harbor
were stockpiled along the northeastern side of the basin
The on land portion of the pier extension site is generaly level with
elevations ranging from 12 to 14 feet above Mean Sea Level Near the
ground surface the coral is very hard but occasional cavities were
noticed When approaching water level however the deposits become less
dense and gravelly Along the bank the previously dredged slope is steep
with a slope ratio of about 1 1 H V
A dry dock owned by Marisco Company was situated in the basin about
40 feet offshore in the pier extension area The dry dock was moved off
site on for repair

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration encountered predominantly medium dense reef
detritus with occasional hard coral layers however no distinct
end bearing layers for piles were located in the drilled borings Thus
friction piles are recommended to support the pier load
Based on a review of the Standard Penetration Test data N values of
the previous borings in the general area for Pier P 5 and its extension
it appears that the underlying coralline deposits at the current pier
extension site are not as competent as those encountered within the
original Pier P 5 area We believe longer friction pile embedment depths
would be needed at the pier extension section in order to obtain the same
vertical pile load capacity
It is our understanding that 20 inch octagonal prestressed concrete
piles have been selected for this project because of the large bending
moment anticipated for the offshore piles Since inch octagonal
concrete piles are still the most commonly used pile size in Hawaii we
have considered the design pile capacities for both the 20 inch and the
inch piles in this report

Site Preparation
Based on the plans supplied to us the proposed finished deck
elevation is 9 0 feet above MSL and the proposed elevation of on land pile
caps is Since the existing on shore ground elevations are at
about 12 to 14 feet cutting the top of existing dredged slope would be
required
At the on set of earthwork the on land areas within the pier
development area should first be excavated down to future subgrade
elevation Within a distance of 20 feet behind the pier s end wall any
coral cavities encountered below the finish subgrade in the area should be
cleared and backfilled with lean concrete
The on site coral material broken to less than 3 inch diameter sizes
may be used as backfill material if required
Pile Foundation
Friction piles embedded in the in situ medium dense reef detritus are
recommended since our current field exploration did not locate a distinct
and sufficiently thick end bearing layer
Vertical Pile Capacity
The piles would develop their vertical load capacity primarily from
frictional resistance between the pile and the surrounding reef
detritus and partially from pile tip embedment
Based on current boring data the friction piles should be driven
into the coral formation to obtain a minimum 45 foot embedment For
the preliminary pile length estimate we recommend an average total
depth of about 45 feet for the on land piles and up to 90 feet below
water surface MLLW for the offshore piles However we believe a
better estimate of the total pile length required may be obtained
after the test pile progran

The piles should be driven with a hammer delivering a minimum energy
of about 39 000 foot pounds If a pile cannot be driven to the
previously specified depth the pile driving may be terminated when

the following criteria

20 inches octagonal prestressed piles or
b 60 blows for the last foot or 20 blows for the last inch for
inch octagonal prestressed piles

Lateral Pile Capacity
The lateral loads on piles are due to the berthing and mooring forces
from ships Of critical concern would be the condition when the
lateral loads on the piles are directed away from shore The lateral
resistance of offshore piles should be neglected in the design and
the offshore piles should be designed appropriately to resist the
anticipated bending moment developed from the unsupported pile length
down to the final harbor dredge depth of minus 45 feet Based on our
recent analysis it appears that the same lateral pile capacity for
Phase I area may also be used for the proposed pier extension site
Detailed descriptions of our lateral load analysis of the on land
piles are presented in Appendix D

Test Pile Program
ee
A test pile program should be undertaken to fulfill the following
objectives
1 To examine the drivability of piles in the erratic coralline
deposits with the use of one or a few selected hammers
2 To verify in the field the final predrilling spudding depths and
final production pile casting lengths
3 To examine if a driving shoe is required
4 To study the potential damages to the piles due to tensile
stresses developing during pile driving particularly for driving
the longer piles in deep water
5 To help evaluate and modify if necessary the production pile
driving criteria
To achieve the above objectives we recommend that a minimum of 30 to
40 piles be test driven first to determine the drivability of the
piles These test piles may be later incorporated into the actual
foundation
As a part of the test pile program two on land pile load tests
close to our boring locations are recommended The two test piles
should be load tested to twice the allowable pile load in accordance
with the ASTM D 1143 test procedure

Riprap Wall
Based on the plans supplied to us a boulder riprap lining is planned
along the slope for wave erosion control Sufficient cuts into the
previously dredged slope are anticipated for the riprap placement
We recommend that a layer of filter fabric be placed underneath the
boulder riprap see Plate 3 The fabric is meant to prevent the coral
fines from being eroded away through the voids between the boulder
riprap The proper sizing of the boulder riprap for the design and wave
action is not within our scope of work

Dewatering
According to the available plans the end wall foundations which
also serve as the pile caps for on land piles will be submerged under
water level MLLW Therefore construction dewatering may be required
for the pile cap foundation If dewatering is not feasible an
alternative is to use tremie concrete for the foundations
Slope Stability
It is our understanding that the harbor will be dredged to depth of
minus 45 feet in the future but the date of future dredging is unknown at
this time and probably will be after the pier construction Thus the
upper portion of the existing dredged slope may retain its present 38 to
40 degrees slope angle with the horizontal
It is our opinion that there is no imminent slope instability concern
at the site However during the service life of the pier unravelling of
loose surficial materials may occur locally due to waves generated by
berthing ships Therefore diving inspection of the slope should be
included as part of the pier maintenance work The results of the diving
inspection should be examined in the future to evaluate the extent and
amount of the unravelling and possible remedial works if required

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as borings
and probes Variations of conditions between the field data points may
occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become
evident until construction If variations then appear evident it will be
necessary to re evaluate the recommendations given in this report
The location of the field data points were determined by taping from
structures indicated on the topographic survey map dated February 16
1988 provided by Department of Transportation Harbors Division
Elevations of the data points were determined by interpolation from
elevation points on the topographic survey may The physical location and
elevation of the field data points should be considered accurate only to
the degree implied by the method used

APPENDIX Cc
Wave Equation Analysis of Pile Driving
stmt eo et AY a es
The wave equation analysis of pile driving was performed using
Program WEAPG which is a microcomputer adaption of the program WEAP
originally written by Goble and Associates for the Federal Highway
Administration to solve pile drivability problems
The program models hammer pile soil system by representing the pile
and driving system by a series of masses and springs The soil is modeled
by a spring and a dashpot attached to each mass The program output
provides dynamic resistance and maximum compressive and tensile stresses
in the piles as a function of hammer blowcount
The primary objectives of the analysis are
1 to analyze the driving stresses in the pile
2 to determine the suitable driving system to be used for the pile
driving and
3 to predict the ultimate dynamic capacity of the pile
DELMAG D 22 and D 30 The relationships of ultimate dynamic capacity and
hammer blowcount commonly known as the bearing graph are presented in
Plates C 1 and C 3 together with input information The maximum
compressive and tensile stresses are presented in Plates C 2 and C 4
A typical computer print out is presented on Plates C 5 through C 11

APPENDIX oD
Lateral Load Analysis of Piles
The lateral load analysis was performed using Program PILEDG which is
a microcomputer adaption of a finite difference laterally loaded pile
program originally developed at the University of Texas at Austin The
program solves for deflection and bending moment along a pile under
lateral loads as a function of depth
The analysis was carried out with the use of internally generated
non linear P Y curves to represent soil moduli The lateral deflection
was then computed using appropriate soil moduli at various depths The
analysis was performed for laterally loaded on land piles adjacent to the
existing dredged harbor slope
Since the soil modulus varies with the deflection experienced the
computerized analysis first assumes a modulus value then calculates the
pile deflection based on the assumed modulus The calculated deflection
is then used to determine the modulus from the non linear P Y curves The
calculated modulus and the assumed modulus are then compared The
procedure is repeated until the newly assumed and calculated moduli
converge
The results of the analysis are summarized in Plate D l for 20 and
16 5 inch piles
