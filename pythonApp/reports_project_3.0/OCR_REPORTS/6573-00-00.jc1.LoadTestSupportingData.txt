The following contains load test supporting data including calibration records a
testing arrangement diagram and the loading sequence as required by the contract
documents for the above project
Specification Section 20 requires performing static load test on a selected
test pile at least 7 days after the completion of initial pile driving The static pile load test
will be conducted in compression to at least 225 percent of the allowable design load in
accordance with ASTM D 1143 1143M using standard loading procedure
The following supporting data supports the contract requirements
1
A 500 ton Geokon load cell Serial Number equipped with a
GK 501 readout box will be used for monitoring the test loads The load cell
was calibrated on August 10 2011 by Geokon as shown on attached Load
Cell Calibration Report
2
We respectfully request a variance for the requirements of the hydraulic jack
calibration Because of the higher accuracy of the load cell equipped with
vibrating wire strain gauges we propose using the load cell as a primary
measure for the test loads Hydraulic jack pressure will not be recorded as a
test load measure versus a verification to ensure load transfer
3
Two Humboldt dial gauges Model No 46304CJ HUM Serial Numbers
112274540 and 112274535 will be used to measure the pile head axial

We propose using a single 150 ton hydraulic jack equipped with an
electronic pump to provide test loads The load test apparatus includes a
spherical bearing load cell hydraulic jack and two dial gauges as shown

will be installed on opposite side of the test pile The reference beams and
wire lines will be supported independently

increment of 20 kips as shown on the attached Static Conventional Pile
Load Test Loading Sequence Each load increment will be held until the rate
of movement less than 0 01 inch per hour with a maximum of 2 hours
holding The maximum of test load will be maintained for at least 12 hours
while the rate of movement is less than 0 01 inch per hour otherwise it will
be held for 24 hours
