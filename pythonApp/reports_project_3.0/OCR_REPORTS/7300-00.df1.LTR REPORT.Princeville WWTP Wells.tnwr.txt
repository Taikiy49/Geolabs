Dear Mr. Nance: 
As requested, we have provided geological logging services for Injection Well 
Nos. 1 and 2 for the Princeville Wastewater Treatment Plant Project. 
Project Considerations 
Treatment of domestic wastewater at the Princeville Wastewater Treatment Plant 
will generate effluent which will be placed into new disposal wells for disposal.  The 
wells were drilled and installed by Oasis Water Systems, Inc.  In accordance with the 
Hawaii Department of Health administrative rule for Underground Injection Control 
(UIC), it is required to have a geologist maintain logs for this work. 
Logging Procedure and Scope of Work 
We provided geological logging services for the wells in a manner appropriate to 
the well construction method used and in general accordance with normal professional 
practice and principles. The logging of the wells was based on visual and textural 
examination of samples of cuttings collected at about 5-foot intervals during the drilling. 
The samples were delivered to our laboratory for examination and logging. 
For logging, cutting samples were removed from the sample bags and spread in 
a laboratory sample tray.  Initial visual observation was conducted to evaluate the origin 
of the material (e.g., alluvium, basalt formation or coralline formation). A closer 
examination was made to attempt to differentiate specifics about the material 
(e.g. pahoehoe, a`a clinker or a`a core). If possible, further description of the formation 
was provided. A written description was made for each cutting sample and the 
descriptions were compiled for longer depth intervals.

Report
The well logs are presented as subsurface descriptions with depth on the
attached Injection Well Logs, Plates 1 and 2. In general, both wells at the site
penetrated basaltic lava flows of the Koloa Volcanic Series.
Our observations of the samples indicated that the lava flows consisted of
alternating layers of thickly bedded a’a flows with associated clinker intercalated with
layers of volcanic tuff, ash and cinder. No unusual geological or hydrological conditions
appeared to be associated with the samples examined.
Our work was performed within the agreed upon scope of work and in
