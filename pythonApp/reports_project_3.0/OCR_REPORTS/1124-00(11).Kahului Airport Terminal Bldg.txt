INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of an additional geotechnical
investigation performed for the proposed new terminal building at
Kahului Airport The general location of the
project site and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map
Plate l
This report also incorporates some of the findings from the soil
engineering investigations performed during 1982 for the Landside and
Airside Improvements and the previously proposed terminal building
Subsequent to the abovementioned investigations the concept for
the layout of the terminal structure had changed Significantly One of
the major changes is the replacement of the pier holdrooms by a linear
holdroom system The site development phasing and construction
sequences of the various areas within the new terminal complex has also
changed from the previous concept What was considered to be the last
phase within the high fill area along Hemaloa Street is now the Phase I
area under the present revised construction sequences In light of
these changes additional geotechnical investigation was performed

PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
It is our understanding that the new terminal building complex
consists of the following main structures
1 Four 50 by 350 foot holdrooms
2 A central building with restaurant facilities
3 A ticketing check in building
4 A baggage claim building which is remodelled from the existing
commuter terminal
5 A ground transportation building
6 A cooling tower structure
The proposed layout of these structures is presented on the Site
Plan Plate 2
The central building and all the holdrooms are connected by
elevated walkways supported by columns The majority of columns will be
spaced at 20 feet spans in the direction of the shorter northwesterly
axes of the terminal building while column spacing may vary up to 50
feet in the direction of the longer northeasterly axes The
anticipated foundation loads for the holdroom and central building will
range from 150 to 560 kips per column while that for the ticketing
check in building is on the order of 100 to 360 kips per column
Loading for the cooling tower was not available at the time of this
report preparation
The proposed Airside site grading plan prepared by R T Tanaka
Engineers Inc dated indicates that fills will be
placed from about 1 foot on the northeastern end of the central building
up to about 22 feet along the property line parallel to Hemaloa Street

The purpose of our additional investigation was to evaluate the
subsurface soil conditions under the new locations of the holdrooms and
the cooling tower to provide pertinent soil data for the site grading
and foundation design of the proposed buildings In light of the change
in construction sequence of the project particular attention was
focused on an up dated evaluation of the settlement characteristics of
the natural soil underlying Holdroom A location where large amount of
fill would be placed
The scope of the investigation which was performed in general
accordance with our proposal dated August 28 1985 includes review of
existing geotechnical data drilling and sampling of seven 7 borings
laboratory testings of soil samples obtained engineering evaluation of
field and laboratory data and report preparation

SITE CONDITION
The project site is located in the southwestern portion of the
existing Kahului Airport Complex
Most of the project site is paved and presently used for aircraft
traffic ranging from light single engine planes to larger jet aircraft
used by the inter island air carriers In front of the existing
passenger terminal is a Portland cement concrete apron which measures
approximately 1120 feet long and 180 feet wide The remaining paved
areas are covered with asphaltic concrete pavement Several drainlines
and sewer lines were noted below the existing pavement area
There are several other existing structures in the area including
the existing commuter airline terminal building air traffic control
tower electrical building and various paved vehicle parking areas
The existing aircraft pavement area is generally level From the
southern end of this pavement area the ground surface drops 10 to 15
feet at a relatively steep slope of about H V towards Hemaloa
Street Most of the area between the edge of the aircraft pavement and
Hemaloa Street is unimproved and densely vegetated by Haole Koa Kiawe
and other dry land flora This area is also known to be underlain by
various cable lines used by the FAA and the NOAA
Hemaloa Street is relatively level and extends from Keolani Place
for approximately 1800 feet to Haleakala Highway The existing roadway
pavement is about 20 feet wide
The ground surface elevations at the site vary from 20 to 42 feet
The lowest elevations are in the southern area along Hemaloa Street and
the highest are in the vicinity of the existing control tower

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The project site is located on the north shore of the island of
Maui on the northwest flank of Haleakala Volcano
The bulk of the Haleakala shield was built during the Pleistocene
by thin bedded basaltic lava flows of the Honomanu Volcanic Series
During the later part of Pleistocene the characteristics of the lavas
changed to very hard thickly bedded flows of andesitic composition
These lavas have been grouped as the Kula Volcanic Series
The Kula lava flows buried most of the Honomanu rocks throughout
East Maui and in some areas buried erosional products i e alluviun
derived from both Honomanu and early Kula rocks At the project site
most of the area is underlain by what appears to be a single thick flow
of Kula lava that overlies alluvial soils In some areas along
Kalialinui Gulch however recent alluvial soils were found to be
underlain by channel deposits It appears that there was no deposition
of Kula lavas in this area which resulted in the cutting of the gulch
into softer alluvial soils It is felt that Kalialinui Stream would not
have had sufficient erosive power to cut through the hard dense Kula
rock
In the seaward end of the Kalialinui Gulch area deposits of dune
and beach sand were encountered These calcareous sands were probably
deposited during glacioeustatic fluctuations of sea level in the
Pleistocene
Also encountered within the project site were varying amounts of
man made i11 which may have resulted from grading operations during the
construction of the existing airport facilities surrounding roads and
other improvements

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
A large portion of the project site including the building
locations for Holdrooms B C D t the Central Building and the
Ticketing Check In Building is underlain by medium stiff to stiff
residual soil overlying hard basalt andesite rock at shallow depths
For the foundation design of the abovementioned buildings we believe
that shallow spread footing bearing either on the compacted select
import structural fill recompacted in situ residual soil or the
underlying hard rock can be used
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O
I D 1737 Page 8
Towards the southwestern end of the project site where Holdroom A
and the proposed cooling tower will be constructed very large quantity
of 111 with thickness varying from 5 to 22 feet will be placed Due to
the heterogeneity of the alluvial soils underlying this part of the
site predictions of ground settlement due to the varying thickness of
additional fill are very difficult Also according to the current
phasing scheme which calls for an early construction of Holdroom AN we
believe that a significant amount of fill settlement could occur after
the construction of the building foundation has started The combined
condition of a tight construction schedule and the difficult to predict
fill settlement lead to our belief that for Holdroom A which spans
over fill with large difference in thickness pile foundation bearing on
underlying basalt may be more appropriate
If the construction of the cooling tower can be delayed until the
majority of the fill settlement has occurred it is our opinion that
spread footing bearing on compacted fill may be used
Detailed discussions of the spread footing and pile foundation are
presented in the following sections
Building Foundation Holdroom A
Holdroom A will be constructed on compacted import fill
material The grading plan for the new terminal building indicates that
the northeastern and southwestern end of the holdroom will be underlain
by 5 and 22 feet of fill respectively While the large quantity of fill
is anticipated to accumulate high total areal settlement the great
difference in fill thickness will cause substantial amount of
differential settlement within the building
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O

Based on the laboratory consolidation tests on various types of
alluvial and residual materials encountered underneath Holdroom A
location the total areal settlement due to site grading is estimated to
be on the order of 3 to 7 inches As much as 2 inches of differential
settlement is expected within a 50 foot span across the existing slope
located approximately underneath the mid section of the proposed
Holdroom A The soils are generally silty and sandy Therefore 90
of the settlement would occur within about 6 months after the completion
of the filling operation
It should be understood that due to the heterogeneity of the
alluvial soils and the large range of column loading 150 to 560 kips
designed for the holdrooms settlement predictions are very difficult
Although it is to our best knowledge that the estimates presented above
are appropriate it is possible that greater values for total settlement
and time for its completion could be resulted Therefore it is our
opinion that Holdroom A should be supported by pile foundation bearing
on the underlying basalt rock
Pile Foundation
Relatively short piles 20 to 30 feet extending through the
additional fill and the in situ soil deposits and bearing on the
hard basalt rock are recommended
Where fill is placed over a compressible soil layer the latter
consolidates and moves downwards relative to the pile The
downward movement results in negative skin friction forces
generally known as DOWNDRAG force on the pile shaft For the
piles supporting Holdroom A calculations indicate that a
16 5 inch octagonal prestressed concrete pile may be designed for
75 ton allowable pile capacity after downdrag force is taken into
account

Pile Driving
The drivability of pile at Holdroom A location is significantly
affected by the type of fill material used for the site grading
In general granular material especially those which are boulderly
and gravelly should be avoided in area where piles will be
driven If the on site residual soil is used care should be taken
to separate any large boulder and cobbles from the soil matrix
In addition to the careful selection of additional site filling
material we believe that pre drilling through the new and old fill
may be required to ensure the installation of the pile foundation
on the underlying rock A test pile program should be undertaken
to further investigate the predrilling requirement
The piles should be driven with a hammer with maximum rated energy
of at least 24 000 foot pounds The piles must be driven to the
bedrock layer which is located at about 20 to 30 feet below the
finished grade

Spread Footing Foundation
For foundation support of the above buildings we believe that
shallow spread footing bearing on either the compacted select
import structural fill recompacted in situ residual soil or the
underlying basalt rock can be used
Because the fill source or sources are yet to be determined the
final quality of the fill may vary depending on the availability of
materials To establish a framework for the analysis and design of
the spread footing foundation we have based our recommendations on
three different types of material that are readily available in the
area
A summary of recommendations for spread footing foundation which
includes the allowable bearing capacity minimum footing embedment
coefficient of friction between footing and bearing soil lateral
pressure and compaction requirement below the footings is shown on
Plate C 1 in the appendix It should be understood that the design
recommendations shown may need to be modified in the future
according to the actual materials and procedures used
In addition to the recommendation shown on Plate C 1 we would
recommend that all the footings within a single building be placed
on similar bearing material e g rock on site residual soil or
structural fill This will help to reduce the amount of
differential settlement within each building

Estimated Settlement For Spread Footing
Minimal settlements are anticipated for footings bearing on the
basalt rock However with the selected compacted import
structural fill or recompacted on site residual soil total
settlements under the design range of loadings may be anticipated
to range from 0 5 to 1 5 inches
Assuming that the adjacent footings are placed on similar material
then differential settlement over a 20 foot span is estimated to be
on the order of one half to three fourths of an inch
Building Foundation Cooling Tower
After mass grading the proposed cooling tower will be underlain by
approximately 28 feet of fill existing and additional overlying layers
of residual and alluvial deposits The areal settlement due to the
additional new fill is anticipated to be on the order of 6 to 8 inches
Therefore it is recommended that the construction of the cooling tower
be delayed as much as possible A minimum of five settlement gauges
should be installed at the corner and center of the location of this
structure prior to fill placement in order to determine when
construction of footing foundation can be initiated
Spread Footing Foundation
Provided that sufficient time 6 to 12 months is available for the
majority of fill settlement to occur we believe that the cooling
tower can be supported on spread footing foundation bearing on the
compacted import fill material Recommendations regarding the
design of the spread footing foundation are shown on Plate C 1 in
the appendix

Estimated Settlement for Spread Foot ing
At the time this report was prepared the type of structure and
magnitude of loading for the cooling tower were still under
consideration Therefore the settlement analysis for the cooling
tower was performed based on the assumption of an arbitrary 6 by
6 foot square footing under a pressure of 3000 p s f Refinement
to the settlement estimate presented below should be performed when
typical loading values are available
Assuming that the on site residual soil is used as fill material
then settlements under the assumed structural loading is estimated
to be on the order of one inch total and one half of an inch
differential over a distance of 20 feet
Since the cooling tower will be constructed close to the proposed
Slope along the boundary of the airport complex it is recommended that
all footings along this slope be embedded a sufficient depth to obtain a
minimum horizontal set back distance of 15 feet away from the slope face
Lateral Resistance of Spread Footing
Lateral loads acting on all the spread footing recommended for the
new terminal buildings may be resisted by passive earth pressure
developed against the buried vertical faces of the foundation system and
by friction resistance developed between the bottom of the foundation
and the bearing soil
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be calculated using an
equivalent fluid pressure shown on Plate C 1 The design values assume
that the soil around the foundation is well compacted The frictional
resistance may be calculated by using the coefficient of friction also
shown on Plate C 1

Spread Footing over Existing Utility Installation
Some of the recommended spread footings for the proposed new main
terminal building may span over the existing utility installation which
as we understood will be abandoned Both the size of the utility
trench with respect to that of the footing and the degree of compaction
of the trench backfill material may have an impact on the spread footing
For those abandoned utility trenches which are located within the
proposed new terminal building we would recommend that the top 3 feet
of the trench backfill material be excavated moisture conditioned and
recompacted in loose layers of no more than 8 inches The backfill
should be compacted to 95 of the maximum density as established by ASTM
D 1557 78 test method
On Grade Slab
Slabs supported on grade may be used based on the assumption that
the fill material will be non expansive select borrow The expansion
potential of all fill materials should be evaluated by the soils
engineer prior to acceptance and use in the fill construction The
range of expansion potential of the reddish brown residual soil fill
would be low Usually the damage potential from this residual soil
expansion may be reduced by combination of placing a 6 inch thick
cushion layer of base course rock pre moistening the subgrade soils and
providing thickened edges as cut off walls to reduce soil moisture
fluctuation However specific details should be developed after an
evaluation of the fill source is made
If the Waikapu select borrow or the calcareous dune sand are used
as fill material then a minimum of 4 inches of rock cushion should
generally be adequate under the slab Vapor barriers are recommended
under the floor slabs in office or storage areas

Slope
A 2 1 H V i11 slope will be constructed along the southwestern
boundary of the airport complex A slope stability analysis was carried
out for the design slope The results of the analysis indicated that
with the use of either one of the three recommended fill materials shown

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content unit weight and Atterberg Limit determinations
were performed on selected samples as an aid in the classification and
evaluation of soil properties The results of these tests are presented
on the Log of Borings at the appropriate sample depths The Atterberg
Limits are summarized on Plate B l
One sieve analysis test was performed on samples of the underlying
alluvial deposit to evaluate its grain size distribution The test
results are presented on Plate B 1
Three one inch ring swell tests were performed on remolded and
undisturbed samples to evaluate the swelling potential of the soils in
both natural and compacted conditions The test results are summarized
on Plate B 2
Two direct shear tests were performed on undisturbed samples to
evaluate the strength characteristics of the natural soil The test
results are presented on Plates B 3 and B 4
Three consolidation tests were performed on undisturbed and
recompacted samples to evaluate settlement characteristics The test
results are presented on Plates B 5 through B 7
