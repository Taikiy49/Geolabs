In accordance with our proposal dated August ist 1996 we have completed the
foundation probing and grouting services for the subject project This report summarizes
our field observations during the probing and grouting operations performed at the subject
site
The project site is located at the intersection of Hanua and Kuhela Streets in the
Barbers Point area of the District of Ewa on the island of Oahu Hawaii as shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1 The project consists of the construction of nine new fuel
tanks with capacities ranging from 5 000 to 68 000 barrels per tank The new fuel tanks
will range in size from about 41 to 92 feet in diameter with heights of about 24 to 60 feet
The approximate locations of the fuel tanks are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 As
required by the project specifications each of the tank footprints will need to be probed
and grouted as part of the foundation preparation The intent of the foundation probing
and grouting operation is an attempt to locate and grout potential subsurface cavities
and or voids beneath the tank foundations

The tank locations were surveyed and the probe hole locations were marked by
our field engineers prior to the probing operations The probe holes were marked along
the perimeter footings spaced at about 10 foot on center Additional probe holes were
marked based on 10 feet on center starting at the center of the tank and radiating out to
the perimeter in about 15 foot radial increments The approximate locations of the probe
holes at each tank location are shown on the Probe Hole Location Layout Plates 3
through 7
The probe holes were drilled with a truck mounted drill rig using 4 inch solid stem
augers The probe holes drilled were about 4 inches in diameter and extended to at least
12 feet below the existing ground elevation Upon drilling all scheduled 438 probe holes
a possible cavity was encountered at Hole No 13 of Tank No T 103 which required
additional probe holes In addition several of the probe holes around Probe Hole No 13
also showed signs of a possible cavity Therefore three additional probe holes
designated as Probe Hole Nos 51 through 53 were drilled around Probe Hole No 13 in
an attempt to delineate the lateral extent of the cavity and to facilitate grouting of the
cavity The approximate locations of the additional probe holes at Tank No T 103 are
shown on the Probe Hole Location Layout Plate 4 A total of 441 probe holes including
the three additional probes were drilled at the nine tank locations of the project site The
following is a summary of the probe holes drilled at the project site
