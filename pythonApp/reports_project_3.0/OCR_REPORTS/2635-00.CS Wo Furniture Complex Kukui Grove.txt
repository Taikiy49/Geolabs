INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed C S Wo Sons Furniture Complex at Lot No 4 in the Kukui
Grove Commercial Village West in Lihue on the island of Kauai Hawaii The general
location of the site and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings of the exploration and our geotechnical
engineering recommendations derived from our analyses based on the data obtained
during the exploration These recommendations are intended for design input and are
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2635 00 Page 2
not intended for construction estimation or bidding purposes This report was prepared
solely for Lot No 4 of the Kukui Grove Commercial Village West The findings and
recommendations presented herein should not be considered applicable to any other lot
in the same vicinity
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a single story furniture complex on the project site
Based on the information provided to us the building will consist of a showroom and
warehouse storage area encompassing a total area of ft It is anticipated that
the structure will be steel framed with exterior CMU walls Concrete slab on grade will be
utilized for the building floor Construction of new utility lines and asphalt pavements for
the entrance driveway and parking areas are anticipated
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions at the project site in order to develop a generalized soil rock data set for the
formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to site regrading and
the development of pavement and foundation designs for the project
The work was done in general accordance with our proposal dated December 21
1990 The scope of work consisted of the following
1 Drilling and sampling four 4 borings to depths of 16 5 feet below the
existing ground elevation and obtaining subsurface samples
2 Review of available soils and geologic information pertinent to the project
site and its vicinity

3 Laboratory testing of selected surface and subsurface samples obtained
during the field exploration to determine the engineering properties and
classification of the materials encountered
4 Engineering analyses of the field and laboratory data for the purpose of
developing site regrading pavement and _ foundation design
recommendations
5 Preparation of this report presenting our findings and design
recommendations
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration and laboratory testing are presented
in the appendices of this report
SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed site is located in the Kukui Grove Commercial Village West in Lihue
Kauai Hawaii and encompasses approximately ft of area The site was
graded and developed in the recent past It appears that the grading consisted primarily
of cuts with the possibility of some fill placement along the eastern limits The site is
generally level albeit gently sloping to the east with elevations ranging from 289 feet
above MSL in the southwestern corner to 281 feet in the northeastern corner Access to
the lot is planned from the southwest corner off of Pahee Street
Along the property boundaries the lot is bounded to the north by Kaumualii
Highway and separated from the highway by a concrete lined drainage ditch To the east
and west are paved access roadways The southern limits are juxtaposed to a similarly

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration generally encountered stiff to very stiff soils below the lot it
is Our opinion that these on site soils may provide adequate bearing support for the
proposed structures and pavement
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2635 00 Page 5
Site Regrading
Site regrading if any is anticipated to be minor
Prior to any future fill placement structural and pavement subgrade areas should
be scarified to a depth of six 6 inches moisture conditioned and recompacted to 90
of its maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 Any loose or soft spots
encountered should be over excavated down to the firm subsoils and then replaced with
compacted non expansive select material
Imported fill material to replace the over excavated material should consist of select
non expansive materials less than three 3 inches in maximum size with a CBR greater
than 10 Any imported fill material should be tested and approved by the geotechnical
engineer prior to transportation to the site
The replacement fill snould be placed in maximum 10 inch thick loose level lifts and
compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method
D 1557 78
The site should be graded to drain water away from the building foundations
Foundations
Spread or continuous wall footings bearing on the stiff in situ ground may be used
for the proposed structures The subgrades of all footings should be scarified and
recompacted to 90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 if they
are loosened during excavation An allowable bearing value of 3000 p s f may be used
for footings bearing on the on site or select fill material which has been recompacted
The bearing value is for total dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third
GEOLABS HAWAIl
W O 2635 00 Page 6
when temporary wind and seismic loads are considered Estimated settlements are on
the order of less than one half of an inch total and one fourth of an inch differential
Individual footings and continuous wail footings should be embedded a minimum
of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade
Foundations located next to the existing slope along the east property limits should
be embedded to such a depth that the bottom of the footing is set back a minimum of
eight 8 feet from the face of the slope at that elevation
The bottom of all footing excavations should be observed by the geotechnical
engineer prior to placement of the reinforcement to determine if adequate bearing has
been obtained and to see that loose soils are removed prior to concrete placement
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive earth pressure
developed against the buried vertical faces of the foundation system and by frictional
resistance developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be calculated using an equivalent fluid
pressure of 300 p c f per foot of depth This assumes that the soil around the foundation
is well compacted Unless covered by pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the
upper 18 inches of soil should be neglected A coefficient of friction of 0 4 may also be
used in the determination of the lateral resistance of the structure

Utility Trench
New utility lines are anticipated for the project A granular bedding consisting of
six 6 inches of ASTM C 33 No 67 gravel 3B Fine is recommended under the pipes
Granular materials should be used for the trench backfill to reduce the compactive
effort required The backfill up to about one foot above the pipes should be lightly
tamped to reduce the possibility of damaging the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level one foot above the pipes to
the subgrades or finished grade should be placed in maximum 10 inch thick horizontal
lifts mechanically compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM
Test Method D 1557 78 to reduce future ground subsidence
Drainage
The finished grade outside the building should be shaped to shed water away from
foundations and to avoid ponding condition Also it is advised that gutter systems be
installed around the building and the discharge be diverted away from the foundation and
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2635 00 Page 9
pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near foundations and slabs should also
be avoided
Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have concrete bottoms and
drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause excessive
subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation
excavations should be properly backfilled tight against the wall or slab footing immediately
after setting of the concrete to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and maintained to drain
all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundations
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be forwarded to the
geotechnical engineer for review and written comments prior to construction This review
is needed to determine adherence to the earthwork and foundation recommendations
given herein If this review is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume
responsibility for misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during any site regrading and construction of the
foundation This is to observe compliance with the design concepts specifications or
recommendations and to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2635 00 Page 10
differ from that anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in
this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and approved by
the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If the actual exposed soil conditions encountered during construction are different
from those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based upon
information obtained from the borings Variations of conditions between the borings may
occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident until
construction If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations given in this report
The locations of the field borings were approximately determined by taping from
Staked property lines observed in the field The data points were approximately estimated
by interpolation from elevation points on the Grading Plan furnished by Wong Sueda
Associates Inc undated The physical location and elevation of the field data points
should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the field data points
depict the approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a
gradual transition

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling four 4 borings
to depths of 16 5 feet below the existing ground surface and obtaining samples at the
approximate locations shown on the Site plan Plate 2 A truck mounted rotary drill
rig was used to drill the borings
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who continuously monitored the drilling
operations The classifications were further reviewed by visual inspection and testing
in the laboratory The soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified
Soil Classification System A graphic presentation of the materials encountered are
presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 4
Soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving a 2 inch standard split
spoon sampler or a 2 4 inch D split barrel sampler with a 140 pound hammer free
falling 30 inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the last 12 inches are shown
on the Boring Logs at the appropriate sample depths
