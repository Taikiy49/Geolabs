This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations and testing services 
performed during site grading, utility installation, and foundation preparation for the Honouliuli 
Wastewater Treatment Plant New Solids Handling Facilities project from August 14, 2007 to 
November 6, 2009 in accordance with our fee proposal dated August 7, 2007. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The project site is within the Honouliuli Wastewater Treatment Plant in the Ewa 
District on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii.  The project includes the construction of three 
anaerobic digesters, a digester control building, a switchgear building, and odor control 
structures.  In addition, the installation of new utility lines is included in the project.  The 
location of the project site is shown on the Project Location Map, Plate 1 and the new building 
structures are shown on the Site Plan, Plate 2. 
The project includes excavations up to about 12 feet in depth for the new structures.  In 
addition, the installation of numerous utility lines was included in the project.   
A probing and grouting program was implemented for the above building structures 
because of the potential presence of underground cavities.  The results of the probing and 
grouting program were provided in our report entitled “Foundation Probing and Grouting 
Report, Honouliuli Wastewater Treatment Plant New Solids Handling Facilities, Ewa, Oahu, 
Hawaii” dated January 23, 2009. 
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services pertaining 
to the site grading and backfill operations for the project site.

SITE GRADING AND UTILITY INSTALLATION 
The site grading and utility installation work for the Honouliuli Wastewater Treatment 
Plant New Solids Handling Facilities project was observed by Geolabs, Inc. from 
August 14, 2007 to November 6, 2009.  The site grading and utility installation consisted of 
clearing and grubbing, excavation of up to about 12 feet in depth, and placement and 
compaction of fills and backfills to achieve the design finished grades for the project.  Parsons 
Construction Group – Pacific Division performed the site grading and utility installation work.  
During the site grading and backfilling operations, samples of the on-site and imported 
materials utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our laboratory 
for testing.  The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in 
our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D 1557 (Modified Proctor), and the test 
results are summarized on Plate 3. 
The fill and backfill materials were moisture-conditioned to above optimum moisture 
content, and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as 
determined in accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557.  In the pavement areas, the fill and 
backfill materials within 2 feet of the finished subgrade were compacted to a minimum of 
95 percent relative compaction. 
During the site grading and backfilling operations, periodic field density tests were 
performed in general accordance with ASTM D 6938 (Nuclear Gauge Method).  Results of the 
compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 4.1 through 4.19 of this report.  These 
tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills at 
the site.  Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on-site 
survey stakes.  These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the 
degree implied by the methods used.  Areas assessed as unsatisfactory were reworked until 
the specified minimum compaction was obtained and/or approved by our field representative 
based on visual observation and hand probing. 
Underground cavities were encountered southeast of Digester No. 1.  The cavities were 
exposed and backfilled with compacted on-site coralline materials.  For Odor Control Building 
No. 1, a localized area of soft subgrade material was encountered.  The soft material was 
removed and backfilled with compacted on-site coralline material. 
Our services consisted of part-time field observation of the contractor’s fill and backfill 
placement, subgrade preparation, and compaction procedures during the above grading 
operations. 
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the referenced 
tests, it is our opinion that the site grading and backfill installation work for the Honouliuli 
Wastewater Treatment Plant New Solids Handling Facilities project, observed and tested by 
Geolabs in the period covered by this report, was performed in conformance with the general 
intent of the following references.
