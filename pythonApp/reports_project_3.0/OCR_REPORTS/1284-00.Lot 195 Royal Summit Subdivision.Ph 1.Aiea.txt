Dear Mr Hirouji
In accordance with your request we have made a study of the near
surface soil conditions at the above referenced lot in order to
provide foundation recommendations for the proposed residence The
study consisted of drilling three shallow borings laboratory testing
of the swelling potential of the soils and reviewing of the
subdivision site grading and preliminary house foundation plans This
report summarizes our findings and recommendations
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
Based on the preliminary house plans the proposed residence will be a
multi level structure approximately 41 by 72 feet in plan dimensions
Concrete on grade slab is planned for the garage and the house

The subject lot is located on the eastern side of Papaleali i Street

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Laboratory tests disclosed that the existing near surface soils
generally have low swelling potential However previous experience
indicates that the soils could exhibit moderate swelling potential if
allowed to dry out excessively and then saturated Therefore some
special foundation design and subgrade preparation are recommended in
order to provide suitable foundation support for the proposed
residence
House Foundations
Spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the proposed
residence An allowable bearing value of 3000 p s may be used on
the residual soil
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1284 00 Page 4
Spread footings on level areas should be embedded a minimum of 12
inches below the adjacent finished grade Continuous wall footings
should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the adjacent finished
grade If located on a sloping area the footing should be embedded
down an additional amount to obtain a 5 foot minimum horizontal
set back distance measured from the outer edge of the footing out to
the slope face This additional embedment depth is required to help
resist the downhill slope creep movement and to develop the
recommended bearing capacity of the soil
Slab On Grade
Concrete slab on grade bearing on the in situ soil is planned for the
garage driveway and the house Due to the low to moderate swelling
potential of the natural soil it is recommended that a deeper 18 inch
perimeter footing embedment be utilized to provide a barrier against
future water intrusion under the slab area
To further minimize the swelling potential of the soil special
subgrade preparation with lime saturation is recommended The general
procedure listed hereunder should be followed
1 Hydrated lime should be placed on the subgrade soils and the footing
excavations prior to water saturating of the subgrade soil We
believe that about ten to fifteen 50 1b bags of hydrated lime would
be adequate to cover these areas
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1284 00 Page 5
2 The slab subgrade soils should be water saturated by constant
sprinkling at least 2 days prior to concrete placement in order to
reduce the swelling potential of the underlying subsoil It is
important that the soils engineer should be notified to inspect the
above subgrade preparation and check the moisture contents of the
underlying soils prior to pouring of the slab and perimeter footing
3 The bottom of the finished subgrade and footing excavations should
be neat and free of loose soils It is anticipated that some very
soft silt and lime mud will be deposited at the footing after
Saturation These soft mud should be removed just prior to concrete
placement
4 The concreting of footing and slab should be coordinated in such a
way that the time period between the initial excavation and the
final concrete placement should be kept to a minimum in order to
prevent excessive subgrade drying
5 The above recommendations for saturating the subgrade with hydrated
lime should also be followed for the sidewalk driveway and patio
Slabs
It is recommended that on grade slabs be placed over a 4 inch layer of
well graded aggregate base course and separated from wall foundations

a moisture barrier is deemed necessary to protect the floor covering

Site Grading
Based on the field exploration and laboratory test results in
general the on site soil are suitable for use in the structural fill
if properly prepared The soils should be compacted above the optimum
moisture content to minimize the swelling potential
Boulders larger than 6 inches in diameter should not be used in the
fill
If required imported fill should be select non expansive soil such
as crusher waste select borrow or coral
GEOLABS HAWAIi
The fills should be placed in horizontal lifts not exceeding 6 inches
in compacted thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its
maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78
The soils at finished subgrade should be prepared as described above
to minimize the swelling potential
Site grading should provide drainage of surface runoff away from all
foundation
Drainage
The finish lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings should be
shaped to shed water away from the foundations and to avoid ponding
condition Also it is advised that gutter systems should be
installed around the house and that gutter water should be diverted
far away from the foundations and walkways Excessive landscape
watering near the foundations and slab should also be avoided
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance
of the above recommended foundations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of
strength

The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled tight against
slab footing immediately after setting of the concrete to prevent
ponding condition
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained to
drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundation
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the footing excavations and backfill operations
be observed by the soils engineer during construction to see that
adequate bearing has been obtained and that the subsurface conditions
are consistent with the findings of our subsurface investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the above
inspections
