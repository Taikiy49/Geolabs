PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The proposed project is located within the Waialua Sugar Mill property in Waialua on 
the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. A Project Location Map and Site Plan are shown on Plates 1 
and 2, respectively. 
The project consists of grading and capping two contaminated areas with 6 inches 
of aggregate base course and 2 inches of asphaltic concrete. The northern most area is 
identified as Site No. 1. We understand that this area is contaminated with pesticides. The 
area is currently covered with tall grass and trees. In addition, a surface layer of sawdust 
was noted during our site visit. The southern-most area is identified as Site No. 2. We 
understand that this area is contaminated with arsenic. The area is currently covered with 
grass. 
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services for 
the site grading at the project site.

SITE GRADING 
We observed the site grading work for the Waialua Sugar Mill AC Cap project from 
August 8, 2018 to September 7, 2018. The site grading work consisted of clearing and 
grubbing, and placement and compaction of fills on the order of about 1 foot thick to 
achieve the finished grades for the project. Paradigm Construction LLC performed the site 
grading work. During the site grading operations, we obtained samples of the imported 
materials utilized as fill materials and transported them to our laboratory for testing. The 
maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory 
in general accordance with ASTM D1557 (Modified Proctor), and the test results are 
summarized on Plate 3. 
The fill materials were moisture-conditioned to above the optimum moisture content 
and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as determined in 
accordance with ASTM D1557. The aggregate base course fill material was compacted to 
a minimum of 95 percent relative density. 
During the site grading operations, periodic field density tests were performed in 
general accordance with ASTM D6938 (Nuclear Gauge Method). Results of the 
compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 4.1 and 4.2 of this report. These 
tests were performed on constructed fills at the site. Test locations and elevations were 
estimated by pacing and interpolating from on-site survey stakes. These locations and 
elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used. 
Areas assessed as unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction 
was obtained and/or approved by our field representative based on visual observation and 
hand probing. 
Our services consisted of part-time field observation of the contractor’s fill 
placement, subgrade preparation, and compaction procedures and confirmation of 
subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above grading operations. 
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the 
referenced tests, it is our opinion that the site grading work for the above-referenced 
project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report were 
performed in conformance with the general intent of the recommendations in the following
