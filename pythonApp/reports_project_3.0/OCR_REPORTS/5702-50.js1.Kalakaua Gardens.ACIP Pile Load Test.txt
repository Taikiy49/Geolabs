In general the Kalakaua Gardens project involves construction of a new senior
housing project at 1723 Kalakaua Avenue in Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The
project site extends from Kalakaua Avenue to Kalauikalani Way In accordance with the
project design requirements a static compressive pile load test was conducted on a
dedicated augered cast in place ACIP pile on September 24 and 25 2014 This letter
report presents the results of our engineering analysis field observations and test data
obtained from the static compressive pile load test The project location and general
vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
Previously we provided geotechnical engineering recommendations for the ACIP
foundation design and our recommendations were presented in the technical
memorandum entitled Kalakaua Gardens ACIP Pile Recommendations dated
The pile load test was conducted on an 18 inch diameter ACIP pile with a length of
approximately 72 feet relative to the existing ground surface The load test pile was
installed by Hawaiian Dredging Construction Company HDCC using a crane mounted
drill rig equipped with automatic monitoring equipment to provide the drilling depth drilling
rate auger torque stroke count etc on a near continuous basis HDCC used continuous
flight augers to drill the pile As the augers were removed the hole was grouted from the
bottom upwards After sound quality grout based on visual observation appeared at the
top of the pile a reinforcing cage was inserted into the grouted hole The approximate
location of the load test pile is shown on the ACIP Test Pile Location Plan Plate 2 The
load test pile installation record is presented on Plate 3
To provide support for the load test two 18 inch ACIP reaction piles approximately
75 5 feet long were installed in similar fashion as the load test pile with 3 inch diameter
center bars in lieu of a rebar cage The reaction piles and load test pile were completed

compressive load test The embedment strain gages were installed in the load test pile at

The piling subcontractor HDCC provided load testing equipment including a
reaction beam reference beams and other accessories Geolabs Inc provided the load
cell hydraulic jack and hydraulic pump Based on the load cell calibration data an
equation relating the applied load kips to the loadcell reading voltage was generated
and used during the load testing
As requested Geolabs provided the data acquisition system including a
multiplexer a data logger and data acquisition program to collect the strain gage readings
during the load testing The strain gage readings were further used in the analyses and
evaluation of the static pile load test In addition displacements at the top of the load test
pile were measured and recorded by Geolabs
The load test pile was loaded and reloaded in general accordance with the Quick
Load Test Method ASTM D1143 and modified by our office to suit the subsurface
conditions at the project site In general the test pile was loaded to about 675 kips in
approximately 37 5 kip load increments Each load increment was held for at least
15 minutes or until the rate of the pile head movement generally was stable In addition
the static load test was held for approximately 1 5 hours at the 300 kip load increment and
4 hours at the 450 kip and 600 kip load increments to evaluate the effects of creep After
loading the load test pile was unloaded in approximately five equal decrements and held
