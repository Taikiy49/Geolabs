In accordance with our fee proposal dated we performed a
geotechnical engineering exploration for the Relocation of Drainage Ditch Molokai
Airport project on the Island of Molokai Hawaii This letter report summarizes our findings
and presents our recommendations for the design and construction of the new drainage
ditch The approximate location is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is on the south side of the existing NE SW Runway at the Molokai
Airport on the Island of Molokai Hawaii Based on the information provided we
understand that the existing drainage ditch is about 110 feet from the existing runway To
comply with regulations Part 139 the drainage ditch must be relocated beyond the
required clear zone from the existing runway
The existing drainage ditch is about 1 920 linear feet with one portion parallel to the
existing runway and the other portion perpendicular to the runway Based on the
information provided the parallel portion of the ditch will be relocated farther away from
the runway and the perpendicular portion will be replaced with a box culvert structure
We excavated five test pits to evaluate the subsurface conditions at the project site
to formulate geotechnical engineering recommendations for the new drainage ditch
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our field exploration program consisted of excavating five test pits each extending
to depths of approximately 5 to 6 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate
test pit locations are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The materials encountered in the test pits were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who monitored the excavation operations on a
near continuous basis Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil

FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Our field observations indicated that the project site is generally flat and underlain
by residual soils Based on the information provided we understand that the construction
will involve relocating one portion of the drainage ditch and replacing the other portion
with a box culvert structure
New Drainage Ditch Channel
We understand that the existing drainage ditch will be relocated away from the
existing runway Based on the information provided we believe that the new drainage
ditch channel will be unlined and have a trapezoid shaped cross section Based on the
test pit information we anticipate that the cut slopes for the channel will be composed
mainly of residual soils Based on our analysis we recommend using channel slopes with
an inclination of two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V or flatter
Box Culvert Structure
We understand that a box culvert drainage structure may be used for the project In
general we anticipate that shallow foundations bearing on the residual soils encountered
at the project site may be utilized for support of the reinforced concrete box culvert
structures Based on the field exploration results we believe that an allowable bearing
value of up to 3 000 pounds per cubic foot pcf may be used for the culvert structure
bearing on the coralline deposits
An 8 inch gravel cushion layer should be provided between the bottom of the box
culvert and the underlying foundation soils to provide more uniform bearing support The
gravel cushion layer should consist of No 3 Fine gravel AASHTO M43 Size No 67
The culvert walls should be designed to resist the lateral earth pressures due to the
adjacent soils and surcharge effects Based on our field exploration data and laboratory
test results we recommend designing the new culvert structures to resist the following
lateral earth pressures at rest conditions expressed in equivalent fluid pressures from the
adjacent soils The following table presents a summary of the recommended lateral earth
pressures

Back Filling the Existing Ditch
Based on our field observation the existing drainage ditch is relatively shallow and
flat We envision that the existing ditch may be abandoned and backfilled after relocation
In general the excavated on site residual soils may be reused as a source of
general fill material up to the finish grade The reused on site soils and imported general fill
materials if needed should consist of materials with particle sizes of 6 inches or less in
maximum dimension and should be free of deleterious materials The material should
have a laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR value no less than that of the on site
materials and a swell potential of not more than that of the on site materials when tested in
accordance with ASTM D 1883 It should be noted that the general fill requirements
presented herein are intended as guidelines only and may be modified based on our
additional laboratory testing and field observations of the available fill materials

General fill materials should be moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
Geolabs should be retained to provide geotechnical engineering services during
construction of the proposed project The critical items of construction monitoring that
require Special Inspection include observation of the foundation excavation Other
aspects of earthwork construction should also be observed by a Geolabs representative
This is to observe compliance with the intent of the design concepts specifications and or
recommendations and to expedite suggestions for design changes that may be required
in the event that subsurface conditions differ from those anticipated at the time this report
was prepared The recommendations provided herein are contingent upon such
observations
If actual exposed subsurface conditions encountered during construction differ
from those assumed or considered in this report Geolabs should be contacted to review
and or revise the geotechnical engineering recommendations presented herein
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted herein are based in part upon
information obtained from test pits excavated at the site Variations of subsurface
conditions from those described in this report may occur and the nature and extent of
these variations may not become evident until construction is underway If variations then
appear evident Geolabs should be contacted to re evaluate the recommendations
presented herein
