Evaluation of the Stockpile Material
Interstate Route H 3
North Halawa Valley Highway Unit II
North Halawa
In accordance with your request a site visit was made on November 28th to
evaluate and sample the stockpile material at the stockpile site The stockpile site is
located near the Hawaiian Cement Upper Quarry area as shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1 Based on our observations of the material at the stockpile site
three stockpiles were identified at the site designated as Stockpile Nos 1 through 3
The approximate location of Stockpile Nos 1 through 3 are shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2 At the time of our site visit we noted that a bulldozer was working on the
material at Stockpile No 1
Samples of the stockpile material were retrieved from the three stockpiles at the
site and transported to our office Moisture content test was performed on each sample
to evaluate the in situ moisture content of the stockpile material Results of the in situ
moisture content are summarized on Plate 3 An Atterberg Limits test was performed
on the sample from Stockpile No 2 to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits and to aid
in soil classification The test results of the Atterberg Limits test is summarized on
Plate 3 Graphic presentation of the Atterberg Limits test is provided on Plate 4 Three
Modified Proctor tests were performed in general accordance with AASHTO T 180 test
procedure on the samples of the stockpile materials to evaluate the moisture and dry
density relationship Results of the Modified Proctor tests are also summarized on
Plate 3 and graphically presented on Plates 2 and 5 3

Based on our laboratory test results the in situ moisture content of the stockpile
materials are about 10 percent above the optimum moisture content In order to
achieve the relative compaction levels of at least 90 percent or 95 percent we
recommend that the stockpile materials at the site be further aerated dried to near the
optimum moisture content prior to placement to achieve the required compaction
