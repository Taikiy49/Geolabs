In general the Building A Common Areas and Tenant Expansion project involves
improvements at Building A of the Royal Hawaiian Center in the Waikiki area of Honolulu
on the Island of Oahu Hawaii In accordance with the project design requirements a static
tension micropile load test was conducted on a pre production micropile on
August 26 2016 This letter report presents the results of our engineering analysis field
observations and test data obtained from the static tension micropile load test The project
location and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
Previously we provided geotechnical engineering consultation for the micropile
foundation design and our recommendations were presented in the revised letter report
entitled Geotechnical Engineering Consultation Building A Common Areas and Tenant
Expansion Royal Hawaiian Center Waikiki Oahu Hawaii dated March 17 2016
However due to the limited work area at the common area site the equipment to install
the recommended 7 5 inch diameter micropile would not be able to fit within the work area
Therefore the micropile diameter was revised to 7 inches to allow smaller equipment to be
used for the micropile installation In addition the unbonded and bonded lengths of the
micropiles were revised to 40 and 36 feet respectively for a total micropile length of
76 feet
The micropile load test was conducted on a 7 inch diameter micropile with a length
of approximately 76 5 feet relative to the existing ground surface The load test pile was
installed on August 18 and 19 2016 by using a Casagrande drill rig
equipped with a 7 inch down the hole hammer advancing a 7 inch OD Outside Diameter
steel casing to the full depth of the micropile to effectively create a drilled hole of at least
7 inches

After the drilled hole was extended to the specified depth of approximately 76 5 feet
36 2 feet of embedment into the bearing materials and cleaned out a No 11 all thread
reinforcing bar Grade 150 was placed in the cased hole with centralizers Grout was
batched on site utilizing a grout mixer equipped with a grout pump
The grout mix consisted of 94 pounds of Portland Cement Type I II 87 pounds of
masonry sand and 5 gallons of water
The grout was placed in the cased hole through a PVC tremie pipe starting from the
bottom of the cased hole After sound grout appeared at the top of the cased hole the
steel casing was removed one section 4 9 foot long at a time until the bottom of the steel
casing approached the tip of the permanent casing elevation or the specified top of the
grout bulb zone
Positive grout take was maintained during the grouting operation to reduce the
potential for necking of the micropile grout bulb The actual grout volume placed in
pre production micropile was about 211 of the theoretical grout volume Installation
records for the load test pile are presented in Appendix A
Kelikai provided the load testing equipment including a hydraulic jack hydraulic
pump load cell reaction beam reference beams and other accessories Based on the
calibration data provided by Kelikai an equation relating the applied load kips to the load
cell was generated and used during the load testing The displacements at the top of the
load test pile were measured and recorded by Geolabs
The load test pile was loaded and unloaded in general accordance with Standard
Test Method for Individual Piles Under Static Axial Tensile Load ASTM D3689 and
modified by our office to suit the subsurface conditions at the project site In general the
test pile was loaded to about 190 kips in approximately 25 kip load increments Each load
increment was held for at least 4 minutes or until the rate of the pile head movement
generally was stable In addition the static load test was held for approximately 4 hours at
the maximum test load of 190 kips to evaluate the effects of creep After loading the load
test pile was unloaded in approximately four equal decrements and held for at least
4 minutes at each decrement

The readings and the load displacement curves of the static tension micropile load
test are presented on Plates 2 and 3 respectively Based on our field observations and the
static tension micropile load test conducted the design parameters used for the micropile
foundation system are satisfactory Therefore each production 7 inch diameter micropile
would need to have total and bonded lengths of approximately 76 and 36 feet
respectively in order to develop an allowable compressible load capacity of 100 kips
