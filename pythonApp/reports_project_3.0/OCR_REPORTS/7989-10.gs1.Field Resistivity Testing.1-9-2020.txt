This memorandum provides the results of the field resistivity testing performed for 
the above project.  The field resistivity testing was performed on January 6, 2020 at the 
project site. 
Geophysical survey equipment (i.e. ground resistance testing equipment) was 
mobilized to the project site for the field resistivity survey.  Field resistivity tests were 
conducted at four selected locations, designated as Line R-1 through Line R-4, using the 
in-situ Wenner 4-Pin Method and a Nilsson Model 400 ground resistance testing 
equipment.  The method utilizes four surface probes or pins (electrodes) to evaluate the 
average resistivity of the underlying mass.  The average resistivity values of the 
subsurface mass, extending from the existing ground surface to the test depth, was 
assessed by using this method along the four test reference lines shown on the Resistivity 
Survey Plan, Plate 1.  Results of the resistivity surveys are presented on Plates 2.1 and 
2.2. 
The in-situ resistivity surveys were performed at the existing ground surface along 
the reference lines shown on the Resistivity Survey Plan.  The field resistivity tests were 
performed at various electrode spacing ranging from 5 to 100 feet.  The average soil 
resistance along the proposed alignments was evaluated at each survey location with the 
electrodes aligned at equal spacing as shown on Plates 2.1 and 2.2.  The depth of 
penetration of a resistivity sounding is a function of the electrode spacing.   
The ground resistance testing equipment (Nilsson Model 400) provides a voltage 
between the outer electrodes, causing an electrical current to flow between them.  The 
voltage drop between the inner electrodes is then measured by a sensitive, built-in 
voltmeter.  As a result, the soil resistance (in ohms) is obtained from the voltmeter dial 
reading.  The soil resistivity (in ohm-cm), which is directly related to both resistance and 
spacing, can then be computed.   
On the basis of the field resistivity test results, the test results for the subsurface 
soils within the project site have in-situ resistivity values ranging from about 6,492 to 
39,258 ohm-cm.  The pin spacing, evaluated soil resistances, and computed resistivity 
values are presented on Plates 2.1 and 2.2.  At the time of the field resistivity survey, the
