EARTHWORK OBSERVATION AND COMPACTION TESTING REPORT
TRAFFIC SIGNALS AT KAPOLEI PARKWAY AND KEAUNUI DRIVE
EWA BY GENTRY WEST
Dear Mr Chun
This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations and testing
services performed during trench backfilling for the Traffic Signals at Kapolei Parkway and
Keaunui Drive project from June 28 to in accordance with our fee proposal
dated
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is at the intersection of Kapolei Parkway and Keaunui Drive in the
Ewa District on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project involves the installation of new
electrical lines A Project Location Map and a Site Plan are shown on Plates 1 and 2
respectively
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services for the
trench backfilling operations during electrical line installation
SITE GRADING
We observed the trench backfilling work for the Traffic Signals at Kapolei Parkway
and Keaunui Drive project from June 28 to The backfilling work consisted of
the placement and compaction of the aggregate select borrow material and placement of
Controlled Low Strength Material CLSM for trench backfill Jack Endo Electric Inc
performed the trench backfilling work We obtained a sample of the imported aggregate
select borrow material utilized as fill material and transported the sample to our laboratory
for testing The maximum dry density and optimum moisture content were established in
our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test
results are summarized on Plate 3

The aggregate select borrow material was moisture conditioned to near optimum
moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density
as determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557
During the backfilling operations a field density test was performed in general
accordance with ASTM D 6938 Nuclear Gauge Method The compaction test results are
summarized on the attached Plate 4 The test was performed on the constructed backfill at
the site Test location and elevation were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site
survey stakes The location and elevation should be considered accurate only to the degree
implied by the methods used Areas assessed as unsatisfactory were reworked until the
specified minimum compaction was obtained and or approved by our field representative
based on visual observation and hand probing
Our services consisted of part time field observation of the contractor s backfill
placement subgrade preparation and compaction procedures and confirmation of
subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above grading operations
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the trench backfilling work for the above project
observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report was performed in
conformance with the general intent of the recommendations in the following references
1
Chapter 14 of the Revised Ordinances of Honolulu 1990 as amended
2
Report by Geolabs Inc entitled Preliminary Geotechnical Engineering
Exploration Laulani Development Area dated February 2
2004
LIMITATIONS
Geolabs assumes no responsibility for any alterations made to slopes or finished
grades on the above project subsequent to our observation and testing period without our
knowledge and written acknowledgment
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site therefore some work may have occurred
without our observation However we believe that the level of earthwork observation and
testing services provided is reasonable in reducing the geotechnical risks to the project It
should be recognized that there are variations in the accuracy and statistical repeatability of
the tests and methods used to monitor the earthwork construction and materials Our
services were performed in accordance with generally accepted standards of geotechnical
engineering practice no warranty is expressed or implied Provision of construction
monitoring by an engineer is not insurance nor does it guarantee construction of any type
The contractor shall retain responsibility for the quality of the work for adhering to the plans
and specifications and for repairing defects regardless of when they are found
