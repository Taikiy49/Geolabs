As requested seven borings were drilled at the project site last week Currently
laboratory testing on the soil samples obtained is in progress This letter presents a
summary of the subsurface conditions encountered in our field exploration and
preliminary geotechnical engineering recommendations for the subject project
Subsurface Conditions
Our field exploration generally encountered surificial fills consisting of medium
dense sandy gravels and clayey gravels from 1 5 to 4 feet below the existing ground
surface The fills were underlain by a relatively thick deposit of medium stiff adobe clay
to about 11 feet below the ground surface Below the adobe clay hard basalt formation
was encountered and extended to the maximum depth explored of approximately 20 feet
below the ground surface
Groundwater was encountered in two of the seven borings at 6 and 9 feet below
the existing ground surface The presence of nearly impervious clayey soils made
measurement of groundwater difficult due to the very long response time necessary
Although groundwater was not encountered in the remaining five borings groundwater
may be present from influence of the nearby stream It should be noted that groundwater
levels are subject to change due to seasonal precipitation run off and other factors

Building Foundations
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered in our exploration we believe
that a shallow foundation system consisting of spread and or continuous footings may be
used to support the proposed structure
An allowable bearing pressure of up to 3 000 p s f may be used for the design of
footings bearing on the medium stiff near surface clayey soils This allowable bearing
pressure is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third for transient loads
such as temporary wind and or seismic forces
The on site adobe clay is generally known to have a high shrink swell potential
when subjected to moisture fluctuations In order to reduce the effects of the expansive
clay soils resulting from moisture fluctuations we recommend that the footing bottoms be
embedded a minimum of 3 feet below the lowest adjacent finish grade
Slabs On Grade
The project site is generally underlain by a deposit of adobe clay soils which are
generally known to be highly expansive when subjected to moisture fluctuations Unless
concrete slabs supported by these soils are properly designed there is a potential for
future distress to the lightly loaded slab on grade construction resulting from the shrinking
and swelling of these soils due to changes in moisture content To reduce the potential
for structural distress resulting from the swelling of the on site expansive soils we
recommend that the expansive clay be removed and replaced with non expansive select
capping fill material to a minimum depth of 36 inches below the bottom of the slab
cushion The capping fill should also extend a minimum of 12 inches beyond the house
slab perimeter
We recommend that a minimum 4 inch thick layer of No 3B Fine gravel
ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation be incorporated below the concrete slab as a cushion
layer To reduce future moisture infiltration and subsequent damage to the floor
coverings an impervious moisture vapor barrier membrane is recommended on top of the
gravel cushion layer Two inches of moist fine sand may be placed above the membrane
to provide protection for the membrane and to aid in curing of the slab concrete
Exterior concrete sidewalks around the building may be required for the proposed
project If sidewalks are planned around the building the capping fill required under the
slab should also extend to the outside edge of the sidewalk slabs The 36 inch capping
layer recommended under the building slab may be reduced to 18 inches for the
sidewalks if some amount of slab cracking can be tolerated Contraction joints should be

Pavement
Asphaltic concrete pavement is anticipated for the new parking and driveway
areas We anticipate that the vehicle loading for the access roadway of this project will
consist primarily of passenger vehicles We have made our preliminary pavement design
assuming the pavement subgrade will consist of the adobe clay soils encountered in our
borings Based on the above assumptions we recommend the following pavement
section be used for preliminary design purposes
Flexible Pavement Section
2 0 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 0 Inch Base Course 95 Percent Relative Compaction
18 0 Inch Subbase 95 Percent Relative Compaction
26 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness on Compacted Subgrade
