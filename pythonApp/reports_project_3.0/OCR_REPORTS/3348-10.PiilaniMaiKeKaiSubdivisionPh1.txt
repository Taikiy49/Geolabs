PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Kai Subdivision project site is in the Anahola area of Kawaihau
in the eastern coast of the Island of Kauai Hawaii The terrain in the area slopes gently
down toward the east and the ocean The site was previously used by Lihue Plantation
Company for sugar cane cultivation
Based on the information provided it is proposed to develop the site for residential
use by the Department of Hawaiian Home Lands The proposed development will
generally consist of subdividing the site into approximately 193 single family residential
lots under three phases of construction Phase 1 will include developing 80 lots and
installing new utilities including water and drain lines as well as constructing new
roadways In addition we understand that site grading and pavement construction work
pertaining to the Kuhio Highway Improvements will be included under this phase of work

EARTHWORK OPERATIONS
Delta Construction Corporation performed the site grading operations pavement
construction and utility installation The site grading consisted of clearing and grubbing
subgrade preparation placement and compaction of fills and backfills pavement
construction and utility trench excavation and backfill
Following the clearing and grubbing operations the over excavated subgrades or
areas designated to receive fills were generally moisture conditioned to about 2 percent
above the optimum moisture content and compacted to at least 90 percent of the
maximum dry density as determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor
During placement of the general fill the fill materials were generally placed in
near level lifts brought to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a
minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density determined in accordance with ASTM
D 1557 Modified Proctor The base course cushion under the AC pavement was
generally placed in near level lifts brought to above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to a minimum of 95 percent of the maximum dry density determined in
accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor
New utility lines were installed to serve the new development In general a bedding
layer consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel was provided below the piping and
manholes for uniform support Free draining granular materials consisting of No 3B Fine
gravel was also used for the trench backfill up to about 12 inches above the pipes to
provide adequate support around the pipes and to reduce the compaction effort on the
initial trench backfill The trench backfill was placed in 8 inch level loose lifts and
mechanically compacted to no less than 90 percent relative compaction Where trenches
are below pavement areas the upper 2 feet of the trench backfill below the pavement
subgrade was compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
During the site grading operations samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test
results are summarized on Plate 2 of this report
Field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations on an
as need basis to confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures
and soil conditions were noted A total of 1 229 field density tests including retests of
failed areas were performed using the nuclear probe method in accordance with
ASTM D 2922 and D 3017 Results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on
Plates 3 1 through 3 59 of this report These tests were performed on the prepared ground
surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills at the site Test locations and elevations
were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations

LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork may
have been performed without our observation However the level of earthwork
observation and testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some
of the geotechnical risks to the project It should be recognized that there are variations
in the accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the
earthwork construction and materials
