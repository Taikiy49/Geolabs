This letter report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations during the 
probing and grouting operations for the Information Technology Center project at the 
University of Hawaii at Manoa from February 29 to March 19, 2012. The probing and 
grouting observation work was performed in general accordance with our fee proposal 
dated July 11, 2011. 
Project Considerations 
The new Information Technology Center project at the University of Hawaii at 
Manoa is in the District of Honolulu on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. It is bounded by Bilger 
Hall to the north, the Bilger Hall Addition to the west, the Physical Sciences Building to the 
east, and Correa Road to the south. The new building will be seven stories high with a 
footprint of up to 12,500 square feet at the Data Center level. It will house the 
system-wide data and telecommunications infrastructure, IT public services facilities, and 
administrative and staff office spaces.

Probing and Grouting Program 
A foundation probing and grouting program was implemented for the new 
Information Technology Center project.  Pacific Ground Systems, Inc. (PGS) performed 
the probing and grouting operations in general conformance with the recommendations 
presented in our report dated April 21, 2008 for the project. The purpose of the probing 
and grouting operations was to reduce the potential for loss of foundation support resulting 
from collapse of cavities or compression of loose zones below the building structure. 
DCK Pacific Construction, LLC surveyed the footing layout and marked the probe 
hole locations prior to the probing and grouting operations. In general, the probe holes 
were located within the spread and column footings at spacing no greater then 10 feet 
apart.  The approximate probe hole locations are shown on the Probe Hole Location Plan, 
Plate 1.  In general, the probe holes at the new building structure were drilled to depths 
ranging from about 8 to 25 feet below the existing ground surface, corresponding to 
approximately 10 feet below the design footing bottom elevations. 
The probing and grouting operations were accomplished using hollow drill rods with 
3 or 3.5-inch diameter drill bits with side discharge holes for water and grout discharge.  
Each probe hole was advanced by rotary percussion using a TEI 300HT drill mounted on 
a Komatsu PC75 excavator or a Cassagrande C6 hydraulic crawler drill.  Water was 
injected continuously through the hollow rods while advancing to the target probe depth.  
Evidence of cavities/voids was observed by the loss of water circulation with rapid 
receding of water into the drill hole.  For the probe hole where cavities/voids were not 
encountered, the probe hole was tremie grouted through the drill rod while the rod was 
being retracted.  The grout used for these probe holes generally consisted of one sack of 
cement and 7.5 gallons of water with a water cement ratio of about 0.70. 
For the probe hole where cavities/voids were encountered or suspected, a 1.5-inch 
diameter PVC pipe was inserted into the probe hole to maintain the integrity of the 
open-hole and to reduce the potential for cave-in prior to the grouting operation.  The 
probe hole was subsequently backfilled with grout batched by PGS on-site and injected at 
low pressures.  Additional probe holes were also drilled in the proximity to better delineate 
the lateral extent of these potential voids and/or cavities. Several probe holes did not 
return grout during the initial grouting, even though there was water return during drilling. 
For these probe hole locations that did not return grout during the initial grout application, 
PGS placed a 1.5-inch PVC pipe in the probe hole and made subsequent grout 
applications until the grout level was above the footing bottom elevation. 
Our field representative observed the probing and grouting operations and recorded 
the probe time, grout time, and approximate grout take.  A total of 135 probe holes with 
an aggregate length of about 2,244 lineal feet below the existing ground surface were 
drilled for the new building structure as summarized in the following table.  The probing 
and grouting summary is provided on Plates 2.1 through 2.12.
