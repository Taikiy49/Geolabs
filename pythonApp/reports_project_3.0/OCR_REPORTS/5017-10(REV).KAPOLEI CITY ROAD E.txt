This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering services performed during
the pavement and utility construction for Kapolei City Road E project from

EARTHWORK OPERATION
The project site is off Kamokila Boulevard across the Kapolei Entertainment Center
in the Kapolei area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The new roadway designated as
Road E measures approximately 60 feet wide and 410 feet long as shown on the
Site Plan Plate 1 Based on the plan and profile drawings provided fills of up to about
5 feet thick were placed to achieve the finished grades for the project construction In
addition new 12 inch waterline and 18 inch drain line connections were also installed as
part of the roadway construction
The roadway and utility construction for the Road E alignment were performed by
Royal Contracting Company The earthwork operation consisted of clearing the existing
ground over excavation of the surficial expansive clays preparation of pavement
subgrades placement and compaction of select borrow and base course and utility trench
excavation and backfill
Following the clearing operation the existing ground was generally over excavated
to approximately 26 5 inches below the finish grades to provide the recommended
pavement section The over excavated subgrades were generally scarified to a minimum

depth of 8 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture
content and recompacted to a minimum of 95 percent of the maximum dry density as

Utility trench backfills were generally placed in near level lifts brought to about
2 percent above the optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the
maximum dry density determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor
Select borrow and base course were generally placed in near level lifts brought to above
the optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent of the maximum dry
density
During the earthwork operation samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test
results are summarized on Plate 2
Periodic field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations to
confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures and soil conditions
were noted A total of 164 field density tests including retests of failed areas were
performed using the nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM D 2922 The
compaction test results performed are summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 11 These
tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed engineered fills
and backfills Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating
from on site survey stakes and construction plans These locations and elevations should
be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed
to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained
and or approved by our field representative based on visual observation and hand probing
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the earthwork operations during the roadway and
utility construction for the above project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period
covered by this report were performed in general accordance with the intent of the project
plans and specifications and our geotechnical engineering recommendations presented in
the referenced report dated March 17 2003 and subsequent recommendations
LIMITATIONS

occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork may
have been performed without our observation However the level of earthwork
observation and testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some of
the geotechnical risks to the project It should be recognized that there are variations in the
accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the
