Dear Mr Leong
In accordance with your request we have evaluated the soil conditions for the
planned trench excavations for the Hale Moku Housing Replacement project at the
above referenced project site This letter summarizes our findings and presents our
recommendations pertinent to the shoring requirements of the planned trench
excavations at the subject site
Based on the information provided the anticipated utility trench excavations are
generally on the order of about 6 to 7 feet deep for the water lines and drain lines
Excavations for the sewer lines are generally deeper with a maximum depth of about
17 feet below the existing ground surface In addition the trench excavations are
anticipated to be left open for about one week and up to a maximum of two weeks
Based on the information provided in the soils report entitled Preliminary
Geotechnical Assessment PGE Job No 8024 021 for Hale Moku Housing Replacement
Contract No N62742 96 R 0026 dated by Pacific Geotechnical
Engineers Inc we understand that the subsurface conditions at the project site generally
consisted of a relatively thin layer of fill on the surface underlain by a silt and clay stratum
of completely weathered volcanic rock Volcanic tuff rock was encountered below the
surface fills or silt and clay stratum at depths ranging from about 2 to 5 5 feet below the
existing grades except in Boring No 1 where it was found at a depth of approximately
17 feet Laboratory tests performed on selected intact cores of tuff rock indicated the
unconfined compressive strength of the rock cores ranged from 2 440 to 6 310 pounds
per square inch psi

Based on the soil boring information the unconfined compressive strength test
results provided and our engineering analyses we believe that the planned trench
excavations in the volcanic tuff rock encountered below the surficial fill layer may be left
unsupported at a near vertical excavation for up to a maximum period of about 2 weeks
A minimum 12 inch wide bench measured horizontally from the exposed mudrock face
should be provided at the interface of the upper fill layer and underlying mudrock The
depth of the bench will vary depending on the actual depth to the mudrock layer The use
of steel plates with Safe T Shores should be considered for excavation shoring in areas
where volcanic tuff rock are not exposed at shallow depths
In order to reduce the potential for adjacent ground movements we recommend
that the following measures and precautions be observed and implemented during the
project construction
e The upper portion of the excavation exposing the silt and clay stratum should
be sloped back at a slope inclination of three fourth horizontal to one vertical
or flatter The excavated soil may be used to provide an earth berm
around the perimeter of the excavation to reduce the potential for surface water
run off from entering the excavation
e Excavated soils should not be stockpiled within a horizontal distance closer
than the depth of the excavation measured from the edge of the excavation
e Heavy equipment and or vibrating equipment should not be allowed to operate
frequently near the edges of the planned excavation
e We understand that the excavation will need to be backfilled up to the existing
ground surface Care should be exercised such that vibrations resulting from
the backfilling operations are kept to a minimum
The contractor or his her representative should be required to be continuously
present on site during the subgrade preparation and backfilling operation of the
excavation Changing conditions which may affect the stability of the excavation such
as development of shrinkage and tension cracks at the ground surface and or on the
exposed sidewalls should be immediately brought to the attention of Geolabs Hawaii for
comments and further evaluation
It is recommended that Geolabs Hawaii be retained to provide periodic
observations during the project construction to observe compliance with the
recommendations presented herein and to expedite suggestions for changes that may be
required in the event that subsurface conditions differ from those observed at the time this
letter was prepared The recommendations provided in this letter are contingent upon
such observations

In accordance with your request we have conducted two site visits on June 29
and July 21 to observe the on going trench excavations for the Hale Moku Housing
Replacement project at the above referenced project site This letter summarizes our
field observations and findings resulting from our site visits conducted for the trench
excavations at the subject site
A site visit was made on June 29 to observe the water line excavations parallel to
Center Drive off Kamehameha Highway Based on our field observations the exposed
trench walls generally consisted of near vertical cuts into approximately 3 feet of surficial
fill soils overlying mudrock at shallow depths An 18 to 24 inch wide bench measured
horizontally from the exposed mudrock face was provided at the interface of the upper fill
layer and underlying tuff formation
A second site visit was made on July 21 to observe the sewer line excavations
between and 32 along Sewer Main C within the project site Based on our
field observations some portions of the trench excavations exposed surficial fill soils
down to a depth of about 5 feet The exposed trench walls in these areas generally
consisted of vertical cuts for the underlying mudrock formation Excavations in the
surficial fill soils generally consisted of 2 foot thick of near vertical cuts at the top followed
by a 2 foot wide bench The lower portion of the fill soils was generally sloped back at a
slope inclination of three fourths horizontal to one vertical Pocket
