engineering exploration conducted for the retaining wall planned for the proposed Malalo

of Oahu Hawaii The project location and general vicinity are shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1 The findings presented herein are subject to the limitations
noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The site for the P E Athletics Redevelopment Malalo Field Parking Structure
project is located at the Kapalama Campus of Kamehameha Schools in the Honolulu area
on the Island of Oahu Hawaii We understand the project will involve construction of a
new parking structure at Malalo Field
As part of the project a retaining wall is planned along the existing slope at the
north side of the parking structure The retaining wall will provide grade separation
between Bishop Drive and the parking structure Based on the information provided the
retaining wall will range from about 10 to 23 feet high The distance between the retaining
wall and the parking structure wall is about 3 5 feet Two borings were drilled along Bishop
Drive to determine the subsurface conditions along the proposed retaining wall alignment

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
We explored the subsurface conditions near the proposed retaining wall by drilling
and sampling two borings designated as Boring Nos 9 and 10 extending to depths of
about 30 5 and 40 0 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate boring
locations are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2

The borings generally encountered 4 to 5 inches of asphaltic concrete underlain by
fill materials to depths of about 2 to 2 5 feet below the existing ground surface The fill
materials consisted of medium dense sandy gravel and stiff silty clay Basalt formation
with occasional layers of breccia was encountered below the fill materials and extended to
the maximum depths explored of about 30 5 and 40 0 feet below the existing ground
surface The basalt formation was generally highly weathered and soft near the ground
surface and graded to moderately to slightly weathered and medium hard with depth
We did not encounter groundwater in the borings at the time of our field exploration
However groundwater levels can significantly vary depending on rainfall temperature
surface runoff groundwater withdrawal and other factors

variation in rainfall temperature and other factors

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
Our field exploration consisted of drilling and sampling two borings designated as
Boring Nos 9 and 10 extending to depths of about 30 5 and 40 0 feet below the existing
ground surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 We drilled
the borings using a truck mounted drill rig equipped with continuous flight augers and
rotary coring tools
Our engineer classified the materials encountered in the borings by visual and
textural examination in the field and monitored the drilling operations on a near continuous
basis Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil Classification
System as shown on Plate A Graphic representations of the materials encountered are
provided on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 and A 2
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained in general accordance with
ASTM D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 3 inch OD Modified
California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches In addition some samples
were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with ASTM D 1586
Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 2 inch OD standard
penetration sampler using the same hammer and drop The blow counts needed to drive
the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown as the
Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Core samples of the rock formations encountered at the project site were obtained
using diamond core drilling techniques in general accordance with ASTM D 2113
Diamond Core Drilling for Site Investigation Core drilling is a rotary drilling method that
uses a hollow bit to cut into the rock formation The material left in the hollow core of the
bit is mechanically recovered for examination and classification
Recovery REC is used as a subjective guide to the interpretation of the relative
quality of rock masses Recovery is defined as the actual length of material recovered
from a coring attempt versus the length of the core attempt For example if 4 feet of
material is recovered from a 5 foot core run the recovery would be 80 percent and would
be shown on the Logs of Borings as REC 80
The Rock Quality Designation RQD is also a subjective guide to the relative
quality of rock masses RQD is defined as the percentage of the core run that is sound
material in excess of 4 inches in length without any discontinuities discounting any
drilling induced fractures or breaks If 2 5 feet of sound material is recovered from a
5 foot core run the RQD would be 50 percent and would be shown on the Logs of

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture Content ASTM D 2216 and Unit Weight determinations ASTM D 2937

summarized on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depth and on Plate B 1

rock core samples to evaluate the unconfined compressive strength of the rock formation
encountered Unconfined compression test results are summarized on the Logs of Borings
at the appropriate sample depths and on Plate B 2
