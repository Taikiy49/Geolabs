INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of a soils and foundation
investigation for the proposed cafetorium at Maunawili Elemen
tary School in Kailua Oahu Hawaii The study consisted of
subsurface borings laboratory testing and engineering analyses
as outlined in our proposal dated Based on
the results of our engineering studies conclusionszand recom
mendations pertinent to site development are presented
PROJECT DESCRIPTION
Thesproperty is located at the Maunawili Elementary School Kailua
Oahu Hawaii The building site is approximately 120 feet long
by 60 feet wide and its location is indicated on the attached
site plan Plate 1 The site drains towards the north with an
estimated relief being on the order of 3 feet The site is
covered with short lawn grass
The column loads are expected to be on the order of 120 kips
while the wall loads are expected to be on thecorder of 3 1 kips
per lineal foot There will be a concrete floor slab on grade
FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface exploration consisted of three 3 borings ranging
in depth from 11 0 to 37 3 feet The drilling was accomplished
using truck mounted rotary drilling equipment with 4 inch
Page 2
W O 448 00
diameter solid continuous flight auger The borings were
continuously inspected by an engineer from our firm The drill
ing contractor was Continental Drilling Co
The approximate boring locations are shown on Plate 1 and the
boring logs are presented in Appendix A
In all inch I D ring samples or Standard Penetra
tion Test samples were taken at selected depth intervals The
Standard Penetration Test samples were taken by driving a split
spoon sampler for 18 inches with a 140 pound hammer free falling
a distance of 30 inches The number of blows required to drive
the sampler the last 12 inches is an approximate measure of the
relative density or consistency of a soil Number of blows to
drive the ring sampler for 12 inches were converted to equivalent
SPT blow count These resistance values are plotted on the
boring logs Samples obtained were visually classified in the
field and representative material was placed in air tight con
tainers and returned to our laboratory for inspection and testing
GEOLOGY AND SOIL CONDITIONS
The site is located on alluvial material derived from the weath
ering erosion and deposition of debris derived from the surround
ing Koolau Volcanic Range basalts and overlies Honolulu Volcanic
Series basalt to depths of approximately 19 to 32 feet The
Page 3
W O 448 00
basalt is weathered to a depth of approximately 4 5 feet on the
building site The overlying alluvial debris consists of clayey
SILT with occasional trace of fine sand and fine gravel The
upper surface layer of clayey SILT which is about 9 to 10 feet
thick is generally very stiff whereas soil below 10 feet is
generally soft to stiff The material in the upper surface
layer is not considered to be significantly expansive
GROUNDWATER
Standing water was encountered in Boring No 1 at 33 5 feet It
is believed that this water represents a perched water table as
the rock in this hole is deeper and no ground water was en
countered above the rock in Boring No 3

Approved fill material as described in item B 3 above
should be placed in maximum 9 inch lifts and compacted
to 90 relative compaction using ASTM D 1557 70 as a
standard All lifts must be placed horizontally

Concrete Floor Slab On Grade
The surface one foot of soil in the building pad should be
scarified watered and compacted to a minimum of 90 of
the maximum dry density A moisture barrier consisting of
a plastic membrane should be provided under the slab areas

Unconfined Compression Tests Intact core and split
spoon soil samples which apparently exhibited high
plasticity were tested for unconfined strength Length
to diameter ratio of 2 was maintained throughout the
tests to avoid scatter of test results and to facilitate
evaluation of shear strength values for design purposes
Direct Shear Test In general soil samples were tested
for shear strength using a 2 41 I D direct shear box
Each set consisted of at least two tests each test run
under different normal loads The results are plotted
as Normal Stress vs Shear Strength with a failure envelope
drawn to determine values of cohesion c and angle of
frictional resistance 9
Consolidation Test Consolidation tests were performed on
selected undisturbed ring samples of the compressible
soils to provide basic data for making settlement calculations
Porous stones were placed on the top and bottom of the samples
to allow drainage Vertical loads were applied in increments
with each load increment being allowed to consolidate prior
2
to adding the next increment Measurements of the time
and consolidation were obtained during each load increment
and rebound was measured during the unloading portion
Consolidation test results are plotted in terms of Percent
settlement versus applied loads
Swell Test The ring sample was placed between porous
stones and a 60 psf load was applied to the sample for a
24 hour period The difference between the initial and
final sample heights is the amount of swell which is expressed
as a percentage of the initial height of the sample
Maximum Density Test Samples of soils are compacted ina
mold of given size with a 10 pound rammer dropped 18 inches
to determine the relationship between moisture content and
density of the soil At least three trial points at dif
ferent moisture content are run to determine the maximum
density and optimum moisture content The test is run in
accordance with ASTM D 1557 70
