our analyses for the subject project
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located within the existing Melemanu Neighborhood Park in the
Mililani area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project
site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 Based on the information provided it
is proposed to install a new security lighting system along the perimeter of the park
premises as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The new light poles will be of concrete construction approximately 16 5 feet in
height about 300 pounds in weight and 6 inches diameter at the base and tapering to
3 5 inches at the top The following information pertaining to the foundation demand of
the new light poles was provided for our analysis by the project structural engineer
e Vertical Load per pole 300 pounds
e Lateral Load at the base per pole 250 pounds
Overturning Moment at the base per pole 2 630 pound feet
Our experience in the area suggests that the project site may be underlain by stiff
silty clays over weathered basalt rock formation at greater depths Special attention was
given to the lateral load supporting characteristics of the subsoils at the project site

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our field exploration program for the security lighting system consisted of drilling
and sampling three borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 3 extending to depths of
about 21 5 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate locations of the
borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general our field exploration disclosed that the project site is generally underlain
by very stiff to hard silty clays and clayey silts extending to the maximum depth explored of
approximately 21 5 feet below the existing ground surface Detailed descriptions of the
subsurface materials encountered from our field exploration are presented on the Logs of
Borings Plates A 1 through A 3 of this report
Groundwater was not encountered in the drilled borings at the time of our field
exploration However it should be noted that groundwater levels are subject to fluctuation
due to seasonal precipitation groundwater withdrawal and or injection and other factors
occurring off site
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Our field exploration indicated that the project site is generally underlain by very stiff
to hard silty clays and clayey silts extending to the maximum depth explored of
approximately 21 5 feet below the existing ground surface Based on the subsurface
conditions encountered and the foundation demand provided by the project structural
engineer we believe that a standard light pole foundation design consisting of 22 inch
square round drilled shaft sections extending to a depth of 5 feet below the design finished
grade may be utilized for the security lighting system
The light pole foundation should consist of a 22 inch square section extending to a
depth of 1 foot below the finished grade followed by a 22 inch round section extending to
a depth of 5 feet below the finished grade Our lateral load analyses indicate that the
estimated deflection at the top of the light pole foundation should be on the order of about
one fourth 4 inch It is our opinion that the project structural engineer should be
consulted to provide additional inputs on the adequacy of the proposed standard light pole
foundation design
The performance of the drilled shafts will depend significantly upon the contractor s
method of construction construction procedures and workmanship Therefore the
recommendations and general guidelines presented in this report should be followed by
the contractor during the foundation construction work

Construction Considerations
Drilled shafts are desirable for the light pole foundations because of the
significant increase in lateral and uplift load capacities when compared to shallow
foundations However the performance of the drilled shafts will depend significantly
upon the contractor s method of construction and construction procedures
The load bearing capacities of drilled shafts depend to a large extent on the
contact between the drilled shafts and the surrounding soils Therefore proper
construction techniques are important The contractor should exercise care in drilling the
shaft holes and placing concrete into the holes Drilling by methods utilizing drilling fluids
is not recommended A low shrink concrete mix with high slump 6 to 9 inch range
should be used to provide close contact between the drilled shafts and the surrounding
soils Concrete should be placed in a suitable manner to reduce the potential for
segregation of the aggregates from the concrete mix If precast concrete pole bases are
used the gap between the pole base and the surrounding soils should be backfilled with
concrete or lean concrete A representative from Geolabs should be present to observe
the installation of drilled shafts during construction to confirm the subsoil conditions and
embedment depths
Utility Trench
We envision that new electrical duct lines will be required to service the proposed
new lighting system In general granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of clean sand or
S4C is recommended under the duct lines Free draining granular materials such as
clean sand or S4C should also be used for the initial trench backfill up to about 6 inches
above the duct lines to provide adequate support around the pipes and to reduce the
compaction effort of the backfill thus reducing the possibility of damaging the pipes It is
critical to use this free draining material to reduce the potential for formation of voids below
the haunches of pipes and to provide adequate support for the sides of the pipes
Improper backfill material and placement procedures could result in settlement of the
backfill and damage to the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 6 inches above the duct lines
to the top of the subgrade or finished grade may consist of on site soils or imported select
granular fill material The backfill should be placed in maximum 6 inch level loose lifts
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and mechanically
compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for
future ground subsidence Where trenches are below pavement areas the upper 3 feet of
the trench backfill below the pavement grade should be compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of
soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Optimum moisture is the water content
corresponding to the maximum dry density

Design Review
Final drawings and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to Geolabs for review and written comments prior to advertisement for bids

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that Geolabs be retained to provide geotechnical engineering
services during construction of the proposed project The items of construction monitoring
that are critical requiring Special Inspection include observation of the drilled shaft
installation Other aspects of earthwork construction should also be observed by a
representative from Geolabs This is to observe compliance with the intent of the design
concepts specifications and or recommendations and to expedite suggestions for design
changes that may be required in the event that subsurface conditions differ from those
anticipated at the time this report was prepared The recommendations provided in this
report are contingent upon such observations
If actual exposed subsurface conditions encountered during construction differ
from those assumed or considered in this report Geolabs should be contacted to review
and or revise the geotechnical engineering recommendations presented herein
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from the field borings Variations of subsoil conditions between and
beyond the field borings may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not
become evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident Geolabs
should be contacted to re evaluate the recommendations presented in this report
The locations of the field borings indicated in this report are approximate having
been estimated by taping from the features shown on the Electrical Site Partial Plans
transmitted by Ronald N S Ho Associates Inc Elevations of the field borings were
interpolated from the elevation points shown on the Topographic Survey Worksheet
prepared by Engineers Surveyors Hawaii Inc The physical locations and elevations of
the field borings should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods
used
