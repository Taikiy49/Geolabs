ACIP PILE LOAD TEST REPORT
MARY SAVIO MEDICAL PLAZA AT NEWTOWN
Dear Mr Kaplan
In general the project involves construction of a new four story medical office
building along the southern side and a four story parking garage with seven levels of
parking at the northeastern side of the property adjacent to Waimalu Stream The project
site currently consists of a three story structure with a gross floor area of about 61 690
square feet On grade asphalt concrete paved parking is around the existing building with
the majority of the parking stalls at the front and rear of the building
In accordance with the project design requirements two static pile load tests were
conducted on a dedicated augered cast in place ACIP pile on April 29 and May 1 2013
This letter report presents the results of our field observations and the test data obtained
from the static pile load test Hawaiian Dredging Construction Company HDCC installed
the augered cast in place piles for the load test
The pile load tests were conducted on an 18 inch diameter augered cast in place
pile with a length of approximately 100 feet relative to the existing ground surface To
better understand the load transfer mechanism at the pile soil interface each load test pile
was instrumented with seven vibrating wire embedment strain gauges Model 4200
manufactured by Geokon The embedment strain gauges were installed in the load test
pile at the following depths below the existing ground surface

The load test piles were at the front of the existing Mary Savio Medical Plaza
Zone A and at the rear of the existing building Zone D Two 18 inch ACIP reaction piles
with lengths of approximately 100 feet below the ground surface were installed for each
test pile Test Piles 1 and 2 were installed on April 22 and 23 2013 respectively using
a crane mounted drill rig equipped with automatic monitoring equipment to provide the
drilling depth drilling rate auger torque stroke count etc on a near continuous basis
The approximate locations of the load test piles are shown on the ACIP Test Pile Location
Plan Plate 1 Installation records for load test pile are presented in Appendix A
The load test piles were both drilled to a depth of about 100 feet below the existing
ground surface using a continuous flight auger and subsequently grouted from the bottom
up After sound quality grout based on visual observation appeared at the top of the pile
a reinforcing cage was inserted into the grouted hole In addition the seven embedment
strain gauges were installed onto a single threaded bar and inserted into the grouted hole
at about the center of the pile The depths at which the embedment strain gauges were
installed are presented in the table above
The piling subcontractor HDCC provided the load testing equipment including a
reaction beam reference beams a hydraulic jack a hydraulic pump and other
accessories Based on the calibration data provided by the subcontractor an equation
relating the applied load kips and hydraulic pump pressure psi was generated and used
during the load testing The strain gauge information and calibration sheets are presented
on Plates B 1 through B 4 of Appendix B
Geolabs provided the data acquisition system including a multiplexer a
data logger and data acquisition program to collect the strain gauge readings during the
load testing The strain gauge readings were further used in the analyses and evaluation
of the static pile load test In addition Geolabs measured and recorded displacements at
the top of the load test pile
The test pile was loaded in general accordance with the Quick Load Test Method
ASTM D 1143 and modified by our office to suit the subsurface conditions at the project
site The design loads of the piles were 140 kips and 320 kips for Test Piles 1 and 2
respectively The maximum test loads on the pile were planned at 560 kips and 800 kips
for test piles 1 and 2 respectively In general the test piles were loaded to the
maximum test load in 12 5 kip load increments Each load increment was held for at least
15 minutes or until the rate of the pile head movement generally was stable
