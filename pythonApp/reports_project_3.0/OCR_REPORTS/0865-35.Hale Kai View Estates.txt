As requested we have made a study of the near surface soil
conditions below the above referenced lot The purpose of the
study was to evaluate the suitability of the near surface soils
as foundation support for the proposed house The study con
sisted of four 4 hand auger probes within the proposed build
ing site laboratory soil tests and a review of the grading
and compaction test record on the building site This report
summarizes our observations and recommendations

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS

brown expansive clay fill to the full depth of the compacted
on site soil which was more 3 feet deep The hand auger probes

gravelly colluvial deposits may be encountered below the
surface clay layer at about 6 to 7 foot depths
The remaining portion of the lot rear portion of the house is
underlain by natural colluvial deposits transported from the
upper hillside area These deposits are typically heterogenous
and vary from clayey silt low expansion potential to clay
high expansion potential

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that most of lot is underlain
by more than 3 feet of grayish brown clay fill The laboratory
test results on this material generally indicated that they
have high expansion potential Therefore for Lot 47 the
slab on grade and foundation construction would require special
design procedures in order to reduce the shrink swell effect of
the subsoils
Foundation
For this sloping lot post and beam construction is highly
desirable and recommended due to the sloping terrain and
expansive soil conditions The post and beam construction

Slab On Grade
Concrete slab on grade is planned for the garage and lanai It
appears that the rear portion of the garage area would require
cut up to about 7 5 feet in order to obtain sufficient level
area for the garage Therefore better less expansive material
may be encountered in the rear portion of the garage excava
tion The front portion of the garage will have less cut and
would encounter the expansive clay The on site clay soils are
expansive and are unsuitable as slab subgrade soils In order
to reduce the expansive effects of the clay where encountered
the expansive clays should be overexcavated down to a depth of
3 feet below the slab subgrade and then replaced with non
expansive borrow soils such as select borrow crusher waste or
coral compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density
per ASTM D 1557 Similar overexcavation and replacement of the
expansive clay soils are recommended for the lanai
A 4 inch layer of base course should be provided below the slab
and compacted to 90 of its maximum dry density In order to
protect against moisture infiltration a polyethylene moisture
barrier may be incorporated below the slab The membrane
should be protected by a thin layer of sand to aid in the
concrete curing

Concrete Driveway and Sidewalks
A layer of expansive soils more than 3 feet thick was encoun
tered in the front portion of the lot driveway area Since
the driveways can tolerate some amount of heaving the clay
overexcavation and replacement can be reduced to 2 feet below
the driveway subgrade level Where 2 or more feet of fill is
planned select non expansive borrow material should be used to
cap over the adobe clay Where less than 2 feet of fill is
planned overexcavation and replacement of the adobe clay
with select non expansive material is required The above
capping requirements should be followed for a minimum distance
of 3 feet beyond the driveway
For the long driveway planned joints are recommended at about
8 to 10 foot intervals and at discontinuities in order to allow
for some relative movement of the concrete
For future sidewalks capping of the clay or overexcavation and
replacement similar to the driveway is recommended to reduce
heaving caused by the expansive clay soil
