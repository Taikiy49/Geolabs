INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Mililani Middle School site in the Mililani area on the island of
Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings from our field exploration and laboratory
testing and presents our geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our
analyses of the proposed project These recommendations are intended for the design of
foundations site grading and pavements only The findings and recommendations
presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from two shield
volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be of
middle to late Pliocene in age and forms the bulk of the western third of the island The
younger shield Koolau is estimated to be of late Pliocene to early Pleistocene Ice Age
and forms the majority of the eastern two thirds of the island Waianae became extinct
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3542 10 Page 7
while Koolau was still active and its eastern flank was partially buried below Koolau lavas
banking against its eastern flank These banked or ponded lavas formed a broad plateau
referred to as the Schofield Plateau on which the project site is located
The project area is composed of basaltic rock built by the extrusion of lavas of the
Koolau Volcanic Series These rocks are generally characterized by flows of jointed
dense vesicular basalt interbedded with thin clinker layers In situ chemical weathering of
the Koolau lava flows has occurred for the last 1 to 2 million years The weathering
process has formed a mantle of residual soils which grade to saprolite with depth In
general saprolite is composed mainly of silty material and is typical of the tropical
weathering of volcanic rocks The saprolite grades to basaltic rock formation with depth

DISC ION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Our field exploration generally encountered residual soils and fill materials
consisting of stiff to very stiff silty clays and clayey silts throughout the depths of the
borings drilled at the subject site Based on the existing topography and current grading
concept we anticipate that some of the proposed new buildings will straddle across cut
and fill areas with fills on the order of about 5 feet thick and cuts up to about 13 feet deep
To reduce the effects of potential differential movements between the cut and fill
subgrades we recommend that concrete slabs on grade be supported on at least
12 inches of select granular fill for more uniform support In addition because the in situ
clayey soils exhibit a slight to moderate shrink swell potential when subjected to moisture
fluctuations the 12 inch select granular fill under the building slabs will also serve to
reduce the potential for future distress to the lightly loaded slab on grade resulting from
shrinking and swelling of the slightly to moderately expansive soils
Based on the generally stiff subsoil conditions native soils and fills encountered in
our borings we believe that shallow foundations consisting of spread and or continuous

Foundations

encountered at the site we believe that shallow spread and or continuous footings may
be used for support of the proposed new building construction An allowable bearing
pressure of up to 3 000 p s f may be used for the design of foundations bearing on the
undisturbed in situ stiff clayey soils or engineered fills needed to achieve the final design
grades This bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third
for transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic forces

As previously indicated we recommend that the foundations for the buildings
straddling across the in filled gully and in situ ground be structurally connected with tie
beams to reduce the effects of potential differential movements This recommendation
will be applicable to three of the structures at the site the Exploratorium House A and
House B structures
In general footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest
adjacent finish grades for the one story buildings Footing embedment should be
increased to a minimum of 24 inches for the two story Exploratorium building For
footings constructed near top of slopes or on sloping ground the footings should be
embedded deep enough to provide a minimum horizontal set back distance of 8 feet
measured from the outside edge of the footings to the slope face Footings located
adjacent to planned retaining walls should be embedded deep enough to avoid
surcharging the retaining wall foundations or the retaining walls should be designed to
resist the additional structural loads
If foundations are located next to utility trenches or easements they should be
embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane extending
upward from the bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility
lines This requirement is necessary to avoid surcharging adjacent below grade
structures with additional structural loads and to reduce the potential for foundation
settlement Therefore special attention should be given to the footing embedment or
building set back of the Physical Education building which is located in the vicinity of the
recently installed 84 inch drainline at the west side of the campus
For footings bearing on the undisturbed in situ stiff soils the bottom of the footing
excavations should be lightly compacted to provide a relatively firm and smooth bearing

Slabs On Grade
Based on the existing topography and the current grading concept the future
slabs on grade for the proposed facilities will likely straddle across stiff in situ soils and
engineered fill materials In order to provide more uniform support for the concrete slabs
on grade we recommend that concrete slabs on grade be supported on at least
12 inches of compacted non expansive select granular fill materials placed in accordance
with the recommendations presented in the Site Grading section of this report In
addition because the in situ clayey soils exhibit a slight to moderate shrink swell potential
when subjected to moisture fluctuations the 12 inch non expansive select granular fill
under the building slabs will also serve to reduce the potential for future distress to the
lightly loaded slabs on grade resulting from shrinking and swelling of the slightly to
moderately expansive soils
In accordance with the requirements of D A G S Technical Memorandum No 61
dated a basaltic termite barrier BTB will need to be incorporated into the
slab on grade design of new facilities Therefore for the interior building slabs not
subjected to vehicular traffic we recommend that a minimum 4 inch thick layer of cushion
fill consisting of BTB be placed below the slabs to serve as a termite barrier and to
provide more uniform support of the slabs To reduce future moisture infiltration and
subsequent damage to floor coverings an impervious moisture vapor barrier such as a
plastic membrane is recommended on top of the BTB cushion layer Two inches of
moist fine sand should be placed above the plastic membrane to provide protection of the
membrane and to aid in curing of the slab concrete Flexible floor coverings such as
carpet or sheet vinyl should be considered since they can better mask any minor slab
cracking
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3542 10 Page 14
Where the slabs will be subjected to vehicular traffic or equipment operation such
as driveways and parking aprons a 6 inch layer of base course compacted to a minimum
of 95 percent relative compaction is recommended in lieu of the 4 inch thick BTB cushion
layer The 2 inch moist fine sand and vapor barrier may also be omitted for these slabs
The base course should consist of crushed basalt aggregate compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative compaction For the design of structural slabs a modulus of subgrade
reaction of about 250 pounds per square inch per inch of deflection p c i may be used
for the compacted base course Where slabs are intended to function as rigid
pavements a minimum slab thickness of 6 inches may be used for preliminary design
purposes Provisions should be made for proper load transfer across the slab joints
which are subjected to vehicular traffic The thickened edges of slabs adjacent to any
unpaved area should be embedded at least 12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade
Exterior concrete walkways may be required for the proposed project We
recommend that concrete walkways be supported on 6 inches of non expansive select
granular fill or select borrow subbase The select granular fill or select borrow subbase
should be compacted to a least 90 percent relative compaction Construction joints
should be provided at intervals equal to the width of the walkways with expansion joints at
right angle intersections
The area adjacent to the slabs should be backfilled tightly against the slab with
non expansive impervious soil and graded to divert water away from the slab to reduce
the potential for water ponding around the slabs and foundations

anticipate that fills on the order of about 5 feet and cuts up to about 13 feet deep relative
to the existing grades will be required to create level building pads for construction of the
proposed facilities
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable
materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site Soft and yielding areas
encountered during clearing and grubbing below any areas designated to receive fill
should be over excavated to expose firm material and the resulting excavation should be
backfilled with well compacted engineered fill The excavated soft and or organic soils
should be properly disposed of off site
Soft and wet soils may be encountered at the ground surface in the vicinity of the
silting basin observed at the subject site We recommend that these soft and wet soils be
removed where encountered to expose the underlying firm soils prior to the placement

of fill materials Contract documents should include additive and deductive unit prices for

After clearing and grubbing the areas within the proposed building limits should be
over excavated where necessary to provide the minimum 12 inch thick capping layer of
non expansive select granular fill material below the slab subgrade level i e bottom of
slab cushion The select granular fill should extend a minimum of 2 feet beyond the
perimeter of the proposed building structures
The over excavated subgrades or areas designated to receive fills should be
scarified to a depth of about 8 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above
the optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage
of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM
D 1557 91 test procedures Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry
weight corresponding to the maximum dry density Where shrinkage cracks are noted
after compaction of the subgrade we recommend that the soil be re scarified and
prepared as recommended above or be thoroughly moistened to close all cracks
Saturation and subsequent yielding of the exposed subgrade due to inclement weather
and poor drainage may require over excavation of the soft areas and replacement with
well compacted engineered fill at no additional cost to the owner
The on site clayey soils generated from the cut areas may be used as a source of
fill material up to the bottom of the non expansive capping layer The on site silty and
clayey soils should not be used as the select granular fill material recommended below
slabs on grades Fills and backfills should be moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent

The 12 inch non expansive capping layer should consist of non expansive select

Utility Trench
We envision that new utility lines will be required for this project In general a
granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67
gradation is recommended under the pipes Free draining granular materials such as
No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation should also be used for the trench
backfill up to about 12 inches above the pipes to provide adequate support around the
pipes and to reduce the compaction effort of the backfill thus reducing the possibility of
damaging the pipes It is critical to use a free draining material for the initial backfill to
reduce the potential for formation of voids below the haunches of pipes and to provide
adequate support for the sides of the pipes If the haunches of the pipes are not properly
backfilled it could result in settlement of the backfill and damage to the pipes
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3542 10 Page 23
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipes to
the top of the subgrade or finished grade may consist of on site soils or select fill material
The backfill should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content
placed in maximum 8 inch level loose lifts and mechanically compacted to not less than
90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for future ground subsidence
Where trenches are below pavement areas the upper 2 feet of the trench backfill below
the pavement subgrade should be compacted to at least 95 percent relative compaction
Drainage
The finished grades outside the buildings should be sloped to shed water away
from foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also advised that gutter
systems be installed around the buildings and that the discharge be diverted away from
the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near the foundations
and slabs should also be avoided Planters next to foundations should be avoided or
have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil
saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of strength of the on site soils The foundation
excavations should be properly backfilled against the walls or slab footings immediately
after setting of the concrete to reduce water infiltration Drainage swales should also be
provided as soon as possible and should be maintained to drain all surface run off away
from the slab and footing foundations

Twenty five 1 inch ring swell tests were performed on selected samples to
evaluate the swelling potential of the subsoils The test results are summarized on Plates
C 1 through C 5
Ten Atterberg Limits tests ASTM D 4318 were performed on selected samples of
the soils to evaluate their liquid and plastic limits and to aid in soil classification The test
results are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths Graphic
presentation of the test results is provided on Plate C 6
Five unconfined compression tests ASTM D 2166 were performed on selected
samples of the in situ clay soils to evaluate the unconfined compressive strength of the
material Results of the tests are summarized on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate
sample depths
Four laboratory California Bearing Ratio Tests ASTM D 1883 87 were performed
on bulk samples of the near surface soils to evaluate the pavement support
characteristics of the soils The test results are presented on Plates C 7 through C 10
Four Modified Proctor compaction tests ASTM D 1557 91 were performed on
bulk samples of the near surface soils to evaluate the dry density and moisture content
relationships The test results are presented on Plates C 11 through C 14
