INTRODUCTION
This report represents the results of our geotechnical engineering
explorations performed for the feasibility and preliminary design of the
proposed Kukuiula Marina in Hawaii
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface conditions at the project site to develop a generalized
soils rock data set for the formulation of geotechnical engineering
recommendations pertinent to the preliminary design of the proposed marina
development
The work was done generally in accordance with our proposal of March
23 1988
The scope of our work included the drilling and sampling of three 3
borings to depths ranging from 19 5 to 28 5 feet below the existing ground
surface Also included were laboratory testing of selected samples
obtained during the field exploration engineering evaluation of the field
and laboratory data the development of preliminary design recommendations
and report preparation
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration and laboratory testing
are presented in the appendices of this report
SITE DESCRIPTION
The general project site is located on the south coast of the island
of Kauai to the west of Poipu at Kukuiula Bay The limits of the proposed
marina have not yet been firmly established The general location of the
site and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
GEOLABS HAWAII

The topographic relief of the proposed marina area is relatively
gentle with elevations ranging from sea level at the shoreline to about 16
feet above Mean Sea Level in the furthest inland portions of the site
At present the southern portion of the site is used for residential
purposes and is occupied by between five and ten houses The northeastern
portion of the project area is vacant land with a moderate vegetative
cover and numerous piles of refuse and debris The northwestern part of
proposed marina is currently used for the cultivation of sugar cane
Along the western boundary of the site an unnamed stream flows in a
generally southerly direction to its mouth in Kukuiula Bay In the
eastern side of the area several surface exposures of basaltic rock were
observed
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The current scheme is to develop the project site into a marina for
the commercial mooring of small craft It is anticipated that the marina
will be constructed by excavating dredging portions of the area to a depth
of about 10 feet below Mean Sea Level
Due to the preliminary nature of our exploration being performed as
part of the planning and feasibility studies neither the proposed site
grading the configuration of the marina basin nor the design of the
marina walls were available at the time this report was prepared
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
The island of Kauai was built during the middle to late Pliocene by
lava flows from a single basaltic shield volcano The rocks of this main
shield building stage are known as the Waimea Canyon Volcanic Series or
Waimea Basalts
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2035 00 Page 4
Dk No 54
After the end of the main shield building phase there was a long
period of erosion which was followed by a renewal of volcanic activity
This renewed activity deposited lava flows over the Waimea Basalts and
their weathering products These younger rocks are known as the Koloa
Volcanic Series or Koloa Basalts
The rocks in the vicinity of the project site have been mapped as
Koloa Basalts and surface exposures of basaltic rock were observed in the
eastern portion of the site during our field exploration However basalt
was encountered at the surface in only one of the three 3 borings
drilled during our exploration and this occurred as a relatively thin
ledge overlying alluvial soils The remaining two 2 borings encountered
alluvial soils believed to originate from the unnamed stream along the
western boundary of the site
Substrata Conditions
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by
drilling and sampling three 3 borings to depths ranging from 19 5 to
28 5 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate locations of
the borings are shown on the Boring Location Plan Plate 2
Boring 1 encountered a surface layer of fill or tilled soil
consisting of stiff reddish brown silty clay with traces of sand and
gravel extending from the existing surface to a depth of about 4 0 feet
Below this surficial fill the boring encountered alluvial soils which may
be generalized as stiff to very stiff brown silty clays with traces of
sand and gravel to the maximum depth explored in this boring of 21 5 feet
Boring 2 encountered a similar surficial fill or tilled zone about
3 0 feet in thickness which was underlain by medium stiff to very stiff
brown silty clays Alluvium to a depth of about 17 5 feet At this
depth medium hard gray basalt was penetrated to the maximum depth
explored in this boring of 19 5 feet

Field Exploration
Groundwater Conditions
In general the exploratory borings encountered groundwater at depths
ranging from about 7 5 to 15 5 feet below the existing ground surface
The groundwater is probably locally recharged by base flow from the nearby
stream and discharges at the adjacent coastline downgradient Due to the
proximity of the stream and the ocean it is anticipated that the
groundwater levels will fluctuate in response to tides storm surge
conditions high stream stages and other external influences
DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
It is our opinion that the proposed marina project is feasible from a
geotechnical engineering point of view However the preliminary nature
of our investigation and the degree of variation in the subsurface
conditions encountered in the borings preclude the formulation of detailed
design recommendations Therefore the recommendations presented below
should be considered preliminary and of a general nature
Site Grading
It is anticipated that the site grading for the proposed marina will
involve excavation dredging of a marina basin to an elevation of about 10
feet below Mean Sea Level 10 MLW
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2035 00 Page 6
Dk No 54
Based upon the borings drilled for our exploration it is our opinion
that there are two separate sets of excavation dredging conditions at the
project site To the west of the approximate boundary shown on the Boring
Location Plan Plate 2 it is anticipated that conventional
excavation dredging techniques may be utilized for the most part
However some hard excavation may be encountered in the deepest part of
the cut dredge area where rock formation may be encountered
To the east of the approximate boundary shown on Plate 2 it is
anticipated that hard excavation techniques such as hoerams blasting and
similar methods may be needed to remove the near surface basalt ledge
Easier excavation dredging may be encountered in areas where the ledge is
underlain by soil materials
To reduce the potential siltation of the near shore waters in
Kukuiula Bay it is recommended that the excavation dredging of the marina
commence in the inshore portions and progress out towards the ocean In
this way a berm could be maintained between the excavation and the ocean
Marina Walls
Plans for the configuration of the marina walls were not available at
the time this report was prepared It is anticipated that the walls will
either be cut slopes or bulkheads
For cut slopes a slope ratio of 2 horizontal to 1 vertical is
recommended Due to the fine grained nature of the cohesive soils
encountered below the waterline it will be necessary to protect these
slopes from erosion For slope shoreline protection we recommend the use
of a riprap armor underlain by a geotextile such as Mirafi Poly Filter
or similar to reduce losses of the fine grained soils due to piping
surge action etc

recommended that additional exploration be performed to provide data for

Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comment
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the recommendations given herein If this review is not made the
geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for misinterpretation
of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the
excavation phases of the work This is to observe compliance with the
design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow design
changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from that
anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in
this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as
borings probes test pits and or observation wells Variations of
conditions between the field data points may occur and the nature and
extent of these variations may not become evident until construction If
variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations given in this report
The location of the field data points were approximately determined
by taping from reference points shown on the Boring Location Plan
prepared by R M Towill Corporation Elevations of the data points were
approximately determined by estimation from the same plan The physical
location and elevation of the field data points should be considered
accurate only to the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross sections are based upon interpolation between field
data points and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
Water level data from the field data points was collected at the
times given on the graphic representations and or in the text of this
report This data has been reviewed and interpretations made in the
formulation of this report However it must be noted that fluctuation
May occur due to variations in rainfall tides temperature and other
factors
