Slab On Grade
According to the present construction drawing slab on grade
was not designed for the expansive soil condition Therefore
the adobe soil under the 4 inch base course should be over
excavated a minimum of 20 inches and replaced with non expansive
granular structural backfill
The structural backfill material preferably should be quarry
waste material Before the placement of the structural backfill
the adobe subgrade material should be kept moist with water
The structural backfill should then be placed in 6 inch layers
compacted to 90 of maximum density
The over excavation should extend 1 foot beyond the perimeter
of the concrete slab and extend a full 20 inch depth below the
subgrade elevation

SCOPE OF EXPLORATION
The purpose of this exploration was to determine general soil conditions
for the design of foundation for a two story swimming pool structure
at Waianae High School Waianae Oahu Hawaii
This report includes field explorations laboratory tests and general
foundation design recommendations for a two story structure
PRELIMINARY FIELD EXPLORATION AND LABORATORY TESTS
Two auger borings were made by Continental Drilling Company to depths
of about 20 ft Standard penetration tests were made using a standard
split spoon sampler driven with a 140 1b hammer falling 30 inches
Laboratory tests included natural water content Atterberg limits
expansion and CBR
SOIL CLASSIFICATION SYSTEM
Soil samples were visually observed and subjected to appropriate tests
in the laboratory Based on visual observations and laboratory tests
the soil descriptions given on the boring logs are generally made in
accordance with the Unified Soil Classification System
GENERAL SITE CONDITIONS
The site for the proposed swimming pool is located at the southwestern
portion of Waianae High School grounds The site is presently a grassed
lawn surrounded by teachers cottages The site is generally level with
a slight slope down towards the south Underground utility lines cross
the site
INTERPRETATION OF SOIL CONDITIONS
From the field exploration and laboratory test results the soils
encountered in the borings may be generally described as follows
A surface fill layer about 5 ft of stiff brown clays
with coral fragments underlain by white tan coral and
silty sand to about 20 ft the depths drilled
At the time of the field exploration water was noted in the borings
at about 6 to depths
For more detailed descriptions of the soils encountered in the drill
holes see the attached boring logs
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The proposed plan is to construct a swimming pool 75 ft long by 60 ft
wide surrounded by a deck including toilet shower and storage facilities
Site Grading
Site grading will involve fills of about 5 ft thick at the shallow
end of the pool and about 6 ft of cut under the deep end of the
pool
Below the pool clay soils should be removed down to coral or to
about 4 ft below the bottom of the pool and the excavations should
be backfilled with compacted select soils Before placing any
fills on existing clayey soil that is dry or desiccated the clay
should be removed and replaced or scarified moistened and
recompacted Care should be taken that clay soils under the ei
or structures are not allowed to dry out and crack Clay soil that
is dried or cracked should be scarified moistened and recompacted
For structures around the pool the existing surface clays should
be removed down to coral or to about 3 ft below the bottoms of
footings grade beams slab on ground and walkway slabs and the
excavations should be backfilled with compacted select soils
Fills should be constructed with select soils
Select soils may be approved on site or borrow soils The select
soils may be crushed coral or granular material generally less than
in sizes It should be fairly well graded from coarse to
fine The plasticity index of this material should be less than
15 and the material should generally be non expansive
Fills should be constructed in thin lifts and compacted in 6 in
layers to a relative density of at least 90 of AASHO T 180 57
density
g
Soft pockets and loose material encountered at the bottoms of
excavations should be removed and the excavation should be
backfilled with compacted select material
The site should be graded to drain water away from the
building site
Footing Foundations
Spread or continuous footings on compacted select backfill
material or on coral may be used
The bottoms of footing excavations should be compacted before
pouring of concrete
Bearing values of about 4000 p s f may be used for footings
on coral and 2500 p s f for compacted select materials
Minimum footing widths of about 3 ft should be used for
individual spread footings and about 16 in for continuous
wall footings
Block walls should be supported on deep concrete grade beam
type foundation to bridge over undetected soft spots and
minimize differential settlements
Existing Cesspools
If cesspools or cavities are encountered within the building
site they should be located Sludge should be removed from
the bottom of cesspools Cavities should be exposed and loose
sediments removed from the bottom
The holes should then be backfilled with well graded granular
material The material should be placed in thin layers and
rammed into place or compacted with vibratory equipment The
top 5 ft of fill should be compacted to 90 of AASHO T 180 57
density Foundations should be designed to bridge over these
areas
Slabs on Ground
Slabs on ground should be placed after the superstructure is
completed Slabs on ground should be separated from grade
beams walls and columns when practicable
For concrete slab on ground a base course of 4 in of well
size is recommended
The subgrade should be compacted and shaped to a level surface
or to drain if practicable and preferably be placed at an
elevation that is above the ground around the outside of the
building
Unforeseen Conditions
Unforeseen or undetected conditions such as soft spots or seepage
water may occur in localized areas and will have to be adjusted
and corrected in the field as they are detected

LIMITATIONS

regular The boring logs indicate the approximate subsurface soil

made at the times designated on the logs and may not represent conditions

may change with the passage of time and construction methods or improve
ments at the site
During construction should subsurface conditions much different from
those in the borings be observed encountered or otherwise indicated
