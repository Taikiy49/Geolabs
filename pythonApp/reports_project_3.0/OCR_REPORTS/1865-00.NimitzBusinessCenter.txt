This report presents the results of our geotechnical evaluation
performed for the proposed Nimitz Business Center development
The proposed Nimitz Business Center is located at 1130 North Nimitz
Highway in Honolulu Hawaii The general location of the site and its
vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this geotechnical evaluation was to explore the
subsurface soil and water conditions underlying the existing warehouse to
provide pertinent soil parameters for use in the design of the foundation
of the proposed development
The scope of the work performed in this evaluation was in general
accordance with our proposal dated except that the number of
borings were reduced due to budget restraints It included drilling four
4 borings to depths of 26 feet laboratory testing engineering
evaluation of the field and laboratory findings and report preparation
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
PROJECT DESCRIPTION
The existing warehouse structure is 680 feet long and 60 feet wide
It is proposed to develop the Nimitz Business Center by adding a
second floor level to the existing warehouse as well as additions to the
north and south ends of the warehouse A 2 story parking structure is
also planned mauka of the existing warehouse The completed Nimitz
Business Center will be a mixed use complex including industrial retail
and office facilities

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The project site is underlain by a 10 to 12 foot thick compressible
layer of loose clayey sand harbor sediment
Settlements are anticipated due to the additional weight of the new
building loads Allowance for settlement due to the structural and live
loads should be incorporated into the foundation design
Foundation Design
Spread or continuous footing foundation may be used to support the
loads of the proposed construction provided some settlement is tolerable
We believe that a maximm net allowable soil bearing pressure of 3000
p s f may be used for the design
Moderately high structural loads are anticipated for the
construction For the proposed column loads of 90 kips and wall loads of
4 kips per linear foot estimated settlements are on the order of 2
inches If practicable efforts should be made to reduce the building
loads
In general the footings should be placed at a level as high as
possible to reduce the footing load applied over the underlying
compressible deposits The footings should be raised so that the tops are
flush with the finished grade
The new column footing should be located away from the existing
footing to reduce settlements of the existing structure
Settlement
Due to the variation in soil characteristics in the Ewa Diamond Head
directions we anticipate that the settlement experienced may also vary
Generally less settlement may occur and within a shorter waiting time for
the Diamond Head area which is underlain by a thicker and stiffer crust
than the Ewa side of the site

It is anticipated that the majority of the settlement will occur in
about 6 months after the new structural loads are applied
Periodic optical survey reading settlement markers installed on the
top of footings should be used to monitor the actual settlement The
readings should be taken by a surveyor and transmitted to the soils
engineer to determine the actual settlement rate prior to finishing the
construction of the second floor
New fills should be avoided or at least minimized since these
additional loads would cause additional settlement
Pavements
Because of the projected settlement flexible A C pavement should be
considered whenever appropriate For pavements subject to light truck
traffic the following pavement section may be considered
Flexible Pavement
2 Inch Asphalt Concrete
6 Inch Base Course 95 Compaction
8 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
Design Review
Plans and specifications for foundation and pavement should be
forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written comments prior to
construction to see that the intent of our recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
We recommend that the footing excavations be inspected by the soils
engineer prior to placement of concrete or reinforcing steel to check that
adequate bearing has been obtained
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site

LIMITATIONS
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations
made in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation engineering
principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all other
warranties either expressed or implied
The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are based
on our site reconnaissance and soil information from boring and laboratory
test data obtained from this soil exploration
Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encountered and cannot be
fully determined by taking soils samples Unforeseen conditions such as
pockets of organic soils trash fill or expansive soils may occur in
localized areas and may require additional probing or corrections in the
field Therefore additional expenditures may be needed during
construction to attain a properly constructed project Some contingency
fund is thus recommended to accommodate these possible costs

Field Exploration

4 borings to depths of 26 feet below the existing ground surface at the
approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 A truck mounted
drill rig was used to drill the borings

textural examination in the field by our engineer who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory All soils
were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A summary of the materials encountered are
presented in the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 4
Soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving a 2 inch
a 140 pound hammer free falling 30 inches or a 3 inch diameter thin wall
tube hydraulically pushed The blow counts or the hydraulic pressure to
drive the sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the Logs of Borings at
the appropriate sample depths
