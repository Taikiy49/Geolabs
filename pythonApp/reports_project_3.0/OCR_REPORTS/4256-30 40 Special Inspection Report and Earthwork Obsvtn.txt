Dear Mr Sumida
This report summarizes our earthwork observation and testing services performed
during the site grading foundation excavation utility line installation and pavement
construction for the Kaluanui townhouse project The earthwork observation and testing
services for the project was performed from through in
accordance with our fee proposals of 2000
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located on the west side of Hawaii Kai Drive at the intersection
with Onohi Street in the Hawaii Kai area of east Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii
The subject parcel is approximately 2 acres in size and is situated at the base of the
western hillside The site is generally covered with a relatively heavy growth of trees and
grasses
The project involved the development of the subject site into a residential housing
project with six townhouse buildings for a total of 28 housing units Access roads and
underground utilities were also installed to serve the new residential development
A schematic site plan of the development is shown on the Site Plan Plate 1

Fills up to about 6 feet thick along Hawaii Kai Drive and cuts up to about 15 feet
deep into the existing western hillside were required to create a relatively level area for
the residential development Retaining walls were constructed along Hawaii Kai Drive
and within the upper level buildings to provide the necessary grade separation for the
subject development
EARTHWORK OPERATIONS AND BUILDING CONSTRUCTION
The earthwork operations for the Kaluanui development site were performed by
Royal Contracting Company Ltd In general the majority of the earthwork operations
involved clearing and grubbing removal of the expansive adobe soils and replacement
with 2 and 3 feet of non expansive select granular fill materials below the pavement and
building footprints respectively
Samples of the on site and imported materials utilized as fill and backfill materials
were obtained and transported to our laboratory for testing The maximum dry densities
and optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general accordance
with American Society of Testing and Material ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified
Proctor The test results are summarized on Plate 2 of this report
Following clearing and grubbing operations the existing ground was generally
over excavated to approximately 3 feet below the finished subgrades in the building areas
to provide the recommended 3 foot thick non expansive select granular fill layer The
over excavated subgrades were moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the
optimum moisture content and compacted to at least 85 to 90 percent of the maximum dry
density as determined in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified
Proctor The pavement areas were generally over excavated to approximately 2 feet
below the bottom of the base course layer to provide the recommended 2 foot thick
non expansive select granular fill layer The over excavated subgrades were moisture
conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture content and compacted to at
least 95 percent relative compaction
During placement of the non expansive select granular fill layer the fill materials
were generally placed in near level lifts brought to above the optimum moisture content
and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density determined in
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor
Utility trench backfills were generally placed in near level lifts brought to above the
optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry
density determined in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor
Aggregate base course was generally placed in near level lifts brought to above the
optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent of the maximum dry
density

Periodic field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations to
confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures and soil conditions
were noted A total of 206 field density tests including retests of failed areas were
performed using the nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM Test Designations
D 2922 and D 3017 The results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on
Plates 3 1 through 3 11 of this report These tests were performed on the prepared
ground surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills at the site
Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from
on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only
to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were
reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained and or approved by our
field representative based on visual observation and hand probing
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our professional opinion that the earthwork operations during the site
grading foundation excavation utility line installation and pavement construction for the
project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report were
performed in general conformance with the intent of the project plans and specifications
and our geotechnical engineering recommendations presented in the referenced report
dated and subsequent recommendations
LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork may
have been performed without our observation However the level of earthwork
observation and testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some of
the geotechnical risks to the project
It should be recognized that there are variations in the accuracy and statistical
repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the earthwork construction and
materials Our services were performed in accordance with generally accepted standards
of geotechnical engineering practice no warranty is expressed or implied Provision of
construction monitoring by an engineer is not insurance nor does it guarantee
construction of any type The contractor shall retain responsibility for the quality of the
work for adhering to the plans and specifications and for repairing defects regardless of
when they are found
