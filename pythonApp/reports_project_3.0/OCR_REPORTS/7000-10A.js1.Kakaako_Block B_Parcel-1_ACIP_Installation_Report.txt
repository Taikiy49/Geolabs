PROJECT DESCRIPTION 
The project site is located at 400 Keawe Street in the Kakaako area of Honolulu. 
The project site is bounded by Auahi Street to the south and Keawe Street to the east. In 
general, the Kakaako Block “B”, Parcel B-1 project involves construction of a new 
multi-story building. 
The new multi-story building structure is supported on a deep foundation system 
consisting of 18-inch diameter augered cast-in-place piles with allowable compressive load 
capacities ranging from 150 kips (75 tons) to 400 kips (200 tons) per pile. The project 
consisted of 5 test piles and 132 production piles for a total of 137 ACIP piles for the 
foundation support of the building structure. Hawaiian Dredging Construction Company, 
Inc. (HDCC) was the general and ACIP pile installation contractor for the project. 
AUGERED CAST-IN-PLACE PILE INSTALLATION 
Geotechnical recommendations for the ACIP pile foundations were presented in the 
afore-mentioned Geotechnical Engineering Exploration Report dated June 13, 2014. The 
purpose of our geotechnical engineering observation and consultation services was to 
observe the ACIP pile foundation installation for general conformance with our 
recommendations. 
The ACIP pile installation program for the multi-level structure consisted of 
installation of 5 test piles, performing one static compressive load test on a selected test 
pile, and installation of 132 production piles.  Detailed discussions are provided in the 
following subsections of this report. 
ACIP Test Pile Program 
A test pile program, which consisted of installing 5 ACIP piles at various depths and 
locations, was conducted for the project on February 23 and 24, 2015.  The test piles were 
selected from the planned production pile locations as shown on the ACIP Pile Location 
Plan, Plate 2. The purpose of the test pile program was to evaluate the subsurface 
conditions across the project site with respect to the selected ACIP equipment and the 
methods of ACIP pile installation.  
A summary of the test piles and our field observations are included in the ACIP Pile 
Installation Summary, Plates 3.1 through 3.5 of this report for reference. Based on the 
results of the test pile program, the selected ACIP equipment and the methods of ACIP 
pile installation are capable of installing the ACIP piles to the required design depths. 
ACIP Static Compressive Pile Load Test 
In order to evaluate the adequacy of the design length of the ACIP pile foundation 
and to confirm the ACIP pile design parameters, one static compressive pile load test was 
performed on a selected test pile (Test Pile No. 57). The static compressive pile load test 
was conducted on one 18-inch diameter ACIP pile with a length of approximately 60.7 feet

Production ACIP Pile Installation 
The production ACIP pile installation program, consisting of installing 132 augered 
cast-in-place piles (18-inch diameter), was conducted from March 5 through 18, 2015 
under the near-continuous (full-time) observations by a Geolabs field representative. 
HDCC used the same drilling/grouting equipment utilized for the test pile program to install 
the production piles. Automatic monitoring equipment was mounted on the 
drilling/pumping equipment with the intent of providing auger depth, grout flow rate, grout 
pressure, etc., on a near-continuous basis  
During the pile installation operation, logs were maintained for each ACIP pile, 
which included information pertaining to pile number, date installed, depth of pile, grout 
volume used, grout return depth, etc. The identifications of the production ACIP piles 
installed are shown on the ACIP Pile Location Plans, Plates 2.1 and 2.2. The production 
ACIP pile installation summary is presented by pile number on the attached 
Plates 3.1 through 3.5. 
A Geolabs field representative maintained a log of all grout used for the ACIP pile 
installation. Based on the accumulated grout volume data, an overall grout factor of 217% 
was calculated for the project. The grout factor is defined as the total grout ordered and 
delivered minus the non-payable grout volume, divided by the theoretical grout volume. 
Non-payable grout volume is defined as grout that was wasted due to contractor issues. 
Based on our observation of the equipment performance and subsurface conditions 
encountered, it is our professional opinion that the production ACIP piles have been 
installed to sufficient depths and/or resistance to provide the design compressive load 
capacity.
