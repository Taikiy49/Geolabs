Gentlemen
From to we have inspected the
earthwork and grading operations on an intermittent basis on the
subject site It is our opinion that as of this date the above
referenced lots were constructed to final grade and compacted
adequately The density test results for the in place fills are
indicated in the attached Density Test Summary
This final report summarizes our inspection and compaction tests
performed at the above lots
The grading operations at the above referenced lots consisted of cut
generally up to 40 feet on the northern portion of the site and fill
up to 15 feet on the southeastern portion of the site

Page 2
The soils encountered at the cut pad finished grade generally
consisted of medium dense basalt Some localized areas were noted
where the basalt had been overexcavated 6 to 12 inches and backfilled
with compacted on site sandy soil Field observations indicated that
those soils could provide adequate support for the proposed single
family residences
During the fill placement operations periodic field density tests
were done in accordance with the American Society for Testing and
Material ASTM Test Designation D 1556 Sand Cone Method It is
believed that the fill lots have been compacted adequately
During the grading period samples of the on site soil was tested
prior to being used in the fill The maximum soil density and optimum
moisture content was established in our laboratory in accordance with
the ASTM D 1557 test designation and the test results are as follows

RECOMMENDAT IONS
House Foundations
It is our understanding that the subdivision will be sold as lots
only with the individual lot purchasers providing their own house
design and construction Therefore details of the future house
design siting and site grading required are not available at this
time We recommend that the future lot purchasers be advised to
obtain services of a qualified soils engineer to help them design the
future construction and provide inspection services during the
construction of foundations
Future Construction and Site Grading
Future building additions patio slabs swimming pools and retaining
walls should be designed by a qualified soils engineer with considera
tion for the effects of the underlying soil conditions at each lot
Subsequent to completion of lot grading utility trenches within the
lot pad should be properly backfilled and compacted under the observa
tion of a qualified soils technician
This office assumes no responsibility for any alterations made to
slopes or pads on the subject lots subsequent to the issuance of this
report without our knowledge and written approval
