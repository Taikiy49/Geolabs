INTRODUCTION
This report summarized the findings of our subsurface inves
tigation and presents our recommendations for the foundation
design and construction of the proposed 7 story structure
Subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling
a total of four 4 borings ranging in depth from 18 0 to
36 5 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate
boring locations are shown on the Site Plan Plate 1 and
the description of the materials encountered in the borings
is presented on the Borings Logs Plates A 1 through A 4
Various laboratory tests were conducted on selected samples

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed 7 story high concrete structure including a
basement parking is planned for this development Basement
parking slab is planned at an elevation of about 32 5
feet C C Datum
The site is relatively flat with surface elevations ranging
from about 37 7 feet on the south side to 41 7 feet on the
north
The proposed structure will measure 154 x 36 in plan
dimensions Maximum column loads are anticipated to be about
350 kips dead plus live loads
The basement parking would require deep excavations as much
as 8 0 feet below the existing ground surface It is our
present understanding that the excavation for the proposed
basement would require vertical cuts along the northeastern
and southwestern property line boundaries

SOIL CONDITIONS
Based on the information obtained from the borings the site
is generally underlain by the following materials
Fill
The surface fill material consists of moderately expansive
clayey materials ranging in depths from 3 0 feet in Boring
Nos 3 and 4 to 6 0 feet in Boring No 2 from the
existing ground surface The surface fill material being
expansive should not be used for structural backfill
Clayey Silt with Sand
Underlying the surface fill materials brown clayey silt
with sand was encountered extending from 22 5 feet in Boring
No 1 to 30 0 feet in Boring No 2 A thin layer 1 5 feet

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on the results of our field exploration Laboratory
testing and the results of our engineering analysis the
site could be developed as planned
The surface fill consists of expansive clayey materials and
should not be re used for footing backfill or foundation
Support Fairly competent materials consisting of clayey
silt with sand were encountered underlying the surface fill
below the planned basement elevation of 32 5 feet which
could provide the desired foundation support

For the proposed 7 story structure spread or line footings
bearing on the undisturbed clayey silt formation could be
used
Deep excavations up to about 8 feet will be required to attain
the proposed basement elevation The proposed basement ex
cavation along the northeastern and southwestern property
boundaries would be cut at a near vertical slope and should
be handled with extreme care
Additional recommendations for the design of foundations
and construction are presented in the following paragraphs
Foundation
For the proposed structure spread or strip footing founda
tions are recommended All footings should be founded on
undisturbed competent clayey silt material underlying the site
below the basement elevation The upper 4 0 to 6 0 feet of
fill consists of expansive clay material and should not be
re used for footing or wall backfill
An allowable bearing pressure of 4 500 pounds per square
foot could be used to design the footings placed on compe
tent sandy silt material as described above A one third
increase may be used to allow for infrequently applied wind
and seismic loads

The tower footings should have a minimum width of 12 inches
and be embedded a minimum of 36 inches below the lowest
adjacent grade on the above recommended clayey silt layer
For the perimeter retaining wall footing the footing embed
ment can be reduced to 18 inches if a lower bearing value of
3000 p s f is required
For the above recommendations it is estimated that the
total and differential settlements will not exceed one
quarter of an inch
We recommend that all footing excavations be inspected by
the soils engineer to confirm the conditions upon which our
recommendations are based Any loose areas encountered
during excavation should be removed and replaced with either
concrete or granular material compacted to a minimum of 95
percent maximum density The bottom of footing excavations
should be tamped to obtain 90 minimum compaction density
Earthwork
Earthwork operations at the site will include excavation
backfilling and foundation inspection during construction
The proposed basement excavation would extend to depths of
approximately 8 0 feet below the existing ground surface

It is anticipated that conventional earthmoving equipments
could be used to conduct all the excavations for the basement
and foundations
The upper brown clayey silt surface fill being moderately to
highly expansive is not recommended as backfill under the
basement parking slab behind the basement walls and around
the footings However this material could be stockpiled
for landscaping purposes if required Excavations should
be protected from surface runoff which may enter the exca
vations All loose soils or mud should be removed from the
bottom of all footing excavations prior to concrete placement
Due to the silty nature of the material and the presence of
thin layers of brown volcanic cinders within the upper 10
feet the basement excavation walls may unravel with time
when cut at a near vertical slope or it may collapse if the
large excavation is left unprotected for a long period of
time Therefore the vertical excavation required for base
ment wall construction along the northeastern and southwestern
property boundaries should be handled with extreme care

Backfilling
Some minor backfilling operations would be required around
the footings and behind the basement walls Granular non
expansive on site material could be used for backfilling
around footings However the upper surface clayey material
is potentially expansive and should not be used for back
filling
Fill and backfill should be placed in horizontal 10 inch
lifts and compacted to at least 95 percent of maximum dry
density within the limits of structures and 90 percent out
side the structure areas as obtained using ASTM D 1557 70
modified Proctor standard
Basement Parking
Slab on grade may be used for basement parking
Slab on grade should be placed over a 4 inch cushion layer
of granular rock gravel material such as 3B Fine rock
to act as a capillary break Proof rolling of the subgrade
material is recommended prior to gravel placement

The slab on grade should be separated from walls and foun

Because of the moderately expansive surface soil the base
course layer for the on grade parking ramp should be in
creased to 6 inch minimum All subgrade soil should be
kept moist prior to concrete placement
Design of Basement Walls
Basement walls below grade will be subject to horizontal
earth pressure imposed by granular backfill material It
is recommended that the lateral earth pressures induced
on basement walls that are restrained from deflection be
designed for an equivalent fluid pressure of 45 pounds per
cubic foot and 35 pounds per cubic foot for walls that are
free to deflect
Resistance to lateral loads may be provided by a combination
of passive pressure and friction Passive pressure may be
taken as that exerted by a fluid of density equal to 350
pounds per cubic foot Friction may be taken as normal dead
load times a coefficient of friction factor equal to 0 45
A combination of friction and passive pressure may be used
provided that passive pressure is reduced by one third

Subsurface drainage system or weepholes should be provided

walls
Foundation Inspection
It is recommended that all the footing excavations and
filling operations be inspected by the soils engineer to
verify adequate compaction and that the subsurface condi
tions are consistent with the findings of our subsurface
investigation
Our present recommendations are contingent upon the above
inspection and testing
