This letter report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observation services
performed during the tieback anchor installation and testing for the Lower Campus Quarry
Wall Stabilization project located at the University of Hawaii at Manoa on the Island of

conducted on the tieback anchors from through
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is at the existing lower campus quarry wall within the University of
Hawaii UHM Manoa campus in Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project
location and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
A geotechnical engineering exploration was performed by Geolabs Inc and our
recommendations were presented in the report entitled Geotechnical Engineering
Exploration Lower Campus Quarry Wall Stabilization University of Hawaii at Manoa
Project No Honolulu Oahu Hawaii dated The
purpose of our geotechnical engineering services was to observe the installation and
testing of the tieback anchors for general conformance with the project plans specifications
and our recommendations
As part of the quarry wall stabilization a shotcrete tieback anchor retaining wall was
designed to mitigate the existing damaged Concrete Rubble Masonry CRM wall and to
reduce the potential for undermining of the concrete sidewalk behind the Law School
building The tieback anchor system was designed to provide resistance to the lateral
earth pressures and lateral loads imposed from the surcharge loading on the concrete
sidewalk The tieback anchor length was specified to be 35 feet including 25 feet of

PRE PRODUCTION TIEBACK ANCHOR INSTALLATION AND TESTING
Pre production tieback anchor installation and testing were performed by
Prometheus Construction The approximate location of the pre production tieback anchor
is shown on the Anchor Location Plan Plate 2 Installation and testing of the
pre production tieback anchor progressed from December 4 through
It should be noted that the pre production anchor initially selected and designated
as Pre Production 1 shown on the Plate 2 was drilled and abandoned in place due to
construction difficulties The drilled hole was backfilled with Portland cement grout
Alternate pre production anchor was specified and designated as Pre Production 2 as
also shown on the Plate 2
A 3 inch thick shotcrete wall was constructed in front of the damaged CRM wall
prior to the anchor installation This shotcrete wall was constructed to provide a level
working surface for drilling and testing of the tieback anchors
Pre Production Tieback Anchor Installation
A Geolabs representative observed the drilling and grouting operations during
construction of the pre production tieback anchor The pre production tieback anchor was
drilled using a TEI HEM550 Rock Drill mounted on a 4 x 7 foot platform The TE HEM550
drill rig was equipped with a down the hole hammer and a 6 inch Numa Super Jaws button
bit
The anchor hole was drilled to a depth of about 37 feet from the front face of the
shotcrete wall with an inclination of about 20 degrees from perpendicular to the face of the
shotcrete wall 6 Inch OD temporary steel casing was installed to about 34 foot depth to
reduce the potential for drilled hole caving in The drilled hole was extended about 1 foot
beyond the tieback anchor tendon required by the project specifications
After the hole was drilled to the specified depth the anchor tendon was inserted
into the drilled hole Centralizers were placed along the anchor at 10 foot intervals

A inch diameter high density plastic grouting tube was installed along the anchor
tendon Grouting began progressively from the bottom to top through the grouting tube
until fresh grout appeared at the top of the hole Most of the grouting tube was removed
after the completion of the grouting operation while the portion that could not be removed
was left in place with grout in fill The temporary casing was withdrawn during the grouting
process
The volume of grout placed within the anchor hole was about 7 1 cubic feet ft
approximately 1 4 times the theoretical volume of the drilled hole A log of the
pre production tieback anchor containing our observations and other pertinent information
recorded by our representative is provided in Appendix A Pre Production Tieback Anchor
Installation and Testing Results
Based on our observations for the pre production tieback anchor installation the
contractor generally demonstrated that the proposed drilling and grouting methods were
acceptable for installing the tieback anchors in general accordance with the project
specifications
Pre Production Tieback Anchor Testing
In accordance with the project specifications a performance and extended creep
test was conducted on the pre production tieback anchor The pre production tieback
anchor was loaded to the maximum test load about 58 5 kips of 150 percent of the
design load 39 kips The anchor head movements for the pre production anchor at each
load increment up to the maximum test load were measured and recorded
Based on the observation the pre production tieback anchor subjected to
performance and extended creep test met the acceptance criteria specified in the project
specifications consisting of the following
The tieback anchor displacement shall be greater than 80 percent of the
theoretical elastic elongation of the unbonded length
The tieback anchor displacement shall be less than the theoretical elastic
elongation of the unbonded length and half of the bonded length
The creep per log cycle shall be less than 0 040 inch between the 1 and
10 minute readings and 0 080 inch between the 6 and 60 minute readings
Based on the review of the pre production tieback anchor test performed by the
contractor it is our opinion that the pre production tieback anchor installed provides
sufficient resistance with tolerable movements The results of the pre production tieback
anchor performance and extended creep testing is presented in Appendix A

Production Tieback Anchor Installation
A Geolabs representative was present to observe the drilling and grouting
operations for the production tieback anchors The production tieback anchors were drilled
and grouted in the same manner as the pre production tieback anchor
The volumes of grout placed in the drilled holes ranged between about 8 0 and
18 3 cubic feet ft approximately 1 1 to 2 2 times the theoretical volume of the drilled
holes Logs of the individual tieback anchors containing our observations and other
pertinent information recorded by our representative for each tieback anchor installed are
provided in Appendix B Production Tieback Anchor Installation Records
It should be noted that the inclination of seven production tieback anchors Anchors
No 1 through 7 were modified due to the presence of underground utilities The
inclination of Anchors No 1 through 4 was modified from 20 to about 40 degrees and
Anchors No 5 through 7 was modified from 20 to about 30 degrees In addition the length
of Anchor No 7 was increased from 36 to about 41 feet based on the revision made by the
structural engineer
Based on the review and evaluation we believe that the specified production
tieback anchors observed by Geolabs representative have been installed in general
conformance with the project plans and specifications and within the limits of generally
accepted construction practices
Production Tieback Anchor Testing
In accordance with the project specifications a minimum of the first two and
ten percent of the remaining tiebacks installed should be performance tested Therefore a
total of four performance tests including two extended creep tests were conducted among
the installed production tieback anchors Production tieback anchors 2 and 8 were
subjected to performance and extended creep tests while production tieback anchors
5 and 13 were performance tested The remaining tieback anchors total of 19 tieback
anchors were proof tested
In general the production tieback anchors were loaded to the maximum test load
about 58 5 kips of 150 percent of the design load 39 kips The anchor head movements
for the tieback anchors at each load increment up to the maximum test load were
measured and recorded

Permanent Tieback Anchor Locking Off
In accordance with the project specifications and plans a lock off load of 31 kips
should be applied to the production tieback anchors after performance or proof tests The
actual lock off load should be not less than and not more than 5 percent of the specified
lock off load It should be noted that the required lock off load for Anchor No 7 was
increased from 31 kips to 35 kips based on the revision made by the structural engineer
All production tieback anchors were locked off between February 4 and 5 2010 A
summary of production tieback anchor lock off results are presented on Plates 3 1 and
3 2 Based on review and evaluation the production tieback anchors were locked off
within the specified range
LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site therefore some of the work for the
production tieback anchor installation and testing may have occurred without our direct
observation However we believe that the level of geotechnical observation provided is
reasonable in reducing the geotechnical risks to the project
Our services were performed in accordance with generally accepted standards of
geotechnical engineering practice no warranty is expressed or implied Provision of
construction monitoring by an engineer is not insurance nor does it guarantee
construction of any type The contractor shall retain responsibility for the quality of their
work for adhering to the plans and specifications and for repairing defects regardless of
when they are found
