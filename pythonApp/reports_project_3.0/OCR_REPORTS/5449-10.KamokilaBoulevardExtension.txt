Dear Mr Arakaki
This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering services performed during
the roadway pavement box culvert open drainage channel and utility construction for the
Kamokila Boulevard Extension project from May 21 2007 through in
general accordance with the scope of services outlined in our fee proposal dated
EARTHWORK OPERATION
Based on the information provided it is proposed to extend the southern side of the
existing Kamokila Boulevard in the Kapolei area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The
proposed roadway extension alignment will traverse approximately 590 feet to the south
from the intersection of Kamokila Boulevard and Kapolei Parkway towards the old railroad
tracks
The new roadway extension for Kamokila Boulevard measuring approximately
590 feet long will consist of a 30 foot wide travelway with a 10 foot wide sidewalk and a
10 foot wide landscape area on each side separated by a 10 or 20 foot wide median along
certain stationing Based on the grading plans provided cuts and fills on the order of about

1 to 2 feet thick will be required to achieve the finished grades for the new roadway
extension In addition new utilities such as water drain and sewer lines are planned for
the roadway extension project
As part of the project construction a new double cell concrete box culvert
measuring approximately 10 8 feet deep and 35 5 feet wide in outside dimensions will be
installed alongside the railroad tracks for a total length of about 943 feet The invert of the
box culvert is set at about 28 feet below the design finish grades Based on the information
provided the box culvert will be connected to an open concrete drainage channel for
about 1 500 lineal feet The layout of the roadway extension box culvert and open
concrete drainage channel alignments is shown on the Site Plans Plates 1 1 and 1 2
Delta Construction Company is the general contractor who also performed the
earthwork for the project The earthwork operation consisted of clearing and grubbing of
the existing ground over excavation and general fill placement preparation of pavement
subgrade placement and compaction of aggregate subbase and cushion fill and utility
trench and box culvert excavation and backfill
Following the clearing and grubbing operation the existing ground was generally
over excavated to approximately 18 inches below the finished pavement grades to
provide the recommended pavement structural section Prior to the placement of the
aggregate subbase the pavement subgrade was scarified to a depth of about 8 inches
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture and recompacted to a minimum of
95 percent of the maximum dry density determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557
Modified Proctor
Imported base course used as a source of aggregate subbase in the pavement
structural section was moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Coralline materials generated
from the box culvert excavation was tested and approved as a source of cushion fill
material underneath the box culvert and open drainage channel structures The coralline
materials were moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Utility trench backfills were
generally placed in near level lifts brought to about 2 percent above the optimum
moisture and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
During the earthwork operation samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test
results are summarized on the attached Plate 2
Periodic field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations to
confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures and soil conditions
were noted A total of 359 field density tests including retests of failed areas were
performed using the nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM D 2922 The results
