INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our soil investigation
performed for the sheet pile repair at Wharf S 1A U S Navy Project No
R1 84 at the Pear Harbor Naval Submarine Base Pearl Harbor Oahu
Hawaii The approximate location of the project site is showm on the
Project Location Map Plate 1
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The existing steel sheet pile bulkhead of Wharf S 1A is badly
deteriorated at and below the waterline The existing tie back and dead
man system is below grade and under Bldg 1588 and could not be
inspected However it has been assumed that the system is in the same
condition as the sheet pile bulkhead

It is proposed to install a new steel sheet pile bulkhead with a
new partial tie back system at Wharf S 1A No tie backs will be
installed for the portion in front of Building 1588 Instead a concrete
wale will be added on the inboard side of the new sheet pile to tie the
sections together The top of the new sheet pile bulkhead to elevation
will be encased in concrete and cathodic pretection will be
provided to increase the life of Wharf S 1A
SCOPE OF SERVICES
The purpose of the investigation is to explore the subsurface
conditions below the site to provide pertinent soil parameters for the
design of the new sheet pile wall anchor system and new asphaltic
pavement structure
The scope of work included drilling and sampling five 5 borings
laboratory testings of samples obtained engineering evaluation of the
field and laboratory data development of design recommendations and
report preparation
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration leg of borings and
laboratory test results are presented in the appendices of this report

SITE CONDITION
Wharf S 1A is situated in the northeast corner of Quarry Loch at
Subbase Pearl Harbor It is used by Subbase to berth small service
crafts necessary to support the requirements of the activities The
Wharf has three straight sections of anchored sheet pile with lengths of
approximately 25 41 and 50 feet respectively The area behind the
25 foot long section is occupied by Building 1588 while that behind the
other two sections is occupied by a flat asphaltic concrete paved zone
which is presently used as a parking lot The pavement appears to be in
good condition
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The geclogic conditions underlying the site are the results of a
complex series of deposition and erosional events brought on by
glaciceustatic sea level changes and post erosional volcanic activities
during the Pleistocene Following the cessation of activity of the
Koolau Volcano alluvial materials were deposited in a deltaic or
estuarine environment in the area of Pearl Harbor
The sea level fluctuated several times receding perhaps 300 feet
below and rising about 95 feet above the present sea level
Concurrent with a rise in sea level during late Pleistocene era
eruption of the Salt Lake Makelapa member of the Honolulu Volcanic Series
GEOLABS HAWAIil
W G 1514 00 Page 4
mantled the older alluvium with thick deposits of volcanic ash which
consolidated to form volcanic tuff locally called mudrock layers
Subsequent erosion dissected and thinned those wantles of tuff
During the Recent epoch as sea level rose to its current stand
the area was covered with soft and loose lagoonal marine deposits Those
deposits were subsequently covered by man made fill placed during the
middle portion of this century for land reclamation projects during the
construction of the existing Naval Submarine Base

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATION
General
Our borings disclosed the presence of a thick layer of soft and
leose lagoonal deposits underlying the preject site Therefore it is
anticipated that larger size sheet pile with deeper penetration would be
required especially for the section in front of Building 1588 where no
tieback will be installed

The existing asphaltic pavement appears to be in good condition
As information regarding future traffic condition at the project site is
not available it is our opinion that the existing pavement section
Gesign can be used for future new pavement
Sheet Pile Design Pressure
For the design of the bulkhead sheet piles at the proposed site
we recommend that the pressure Giagrams presented on Plates 3 and 4 be
used As there is no anticipated site filling or development at the area
behind the sheet pile wall the recommended pressure does not include any
surcharge loads The recommended pressure alsc ignored contribution due
to the existing sheet pile structure which is believed to be in a
deteriorated condition and therefore may not provide long term
support The design pressures were developed based on the boring 3 soil
conditions which were found to be more critical
The underwater ground profile in front of the sheet pile wall is
not well defined However based on the Location and Layout Plan for
Quay Wall Repairs dated May 1952 for the vicinity of the project site
a 3 1 H V slope which starts from the point where the mudline meets the
sheet pile and levels off at Elevation 65 feet Navy Datum was
adopted

For tieback force design we recommend that the free earth support
condition be considered The free earth method assumes that the piling
is rigid and may rotate at the anchor reod level with failure occurring
by a rotation about the anchor rod Passive pressure develops in the
soil in front of the piling and active pressure behind After the
theorectical embedment is computed the embedment value may be increased
by 20 to 40 percent to provide a factor of safety in the design
Sheet Pile Bulkhead Construction
Based on our field explorations we believe that the new sheet
piles could be driven through the lagoonal deposit without difficulty
However hard driving may be encountered when sheet piles are to be
driven inte the volcanic tuff in the vicinity of Boring 3 at elevation
4 feet Boring 4 encountered fill consisting of mudrock tuff cobbles
at a depth of 31 to 41 feet below the pavement level Hard driving may
be experienced through this type of material however since the new
sheet pile wall will be constructed on the outboard side of the existing
sheet pile wall this backfill material is probably not present
Because of the poor subsoil condition the depth of sheet pile
penetration will be high Preliminary evaluation indicates that the
anchored sheet piles should penetrate to a minimum depth of 49 feet below
the top of the sheet pile wall or to about elevation of 58 feet For the
cantilever sheet pile section in front of Building 1588 the required
minimum penetration is 77 feet cr to elevation of 30 feet

Deadman Anchor Tieback System
A deadman anchor tieback system will be used for the western
section of the sheet pile wall For deadman anchor design our
recommended design pressure diagram is shown on Plate 5 We believe that
the face of the deadman should be at least 35 feet away from the sheet
pile Generally this horizontal distance will place the passive earth
pressure wedge of the deadman anchor outside the active wedge zone of the
sheet pile
We recommend that the top of the deadman be embedded at least 2
feet below the street grade Some provisions should be made to limit any
future trenching operation in front of the deadman anchor tc avoid loss
of passive resisting earth pressures
The backfill around the deadman should be compacted to a minimum
of 90 of its maximum dry density based on ASTM D 1557 78 in order to
develop the design passive resistance
It is anticipated that the bottom of the deadman anchor excavation
will extend below the prevailing groundwater level and that dewatering
may be necessary In addition any soft or loose soil encountered at the
bottom of the excavations should be removed to a minimum depth of 18
inches and the over excavation backfilled with compacted crushed rock
such as No 3B Fine gravel

Pavement Design
New asphaltic concrete pavement is planned at the project site
As the traffic condition is not well defined at this time we believe
that the existing pavement section which appears to have performed
satisfactorily can be used Therefore the following preliminary
pavement design is recommended
2 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 Inch Base Course 95 Compaction
8 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
The above recommendation assumes a subgrade CBR of 8 and light
traffic condition
Backfill
Select granular material should be used for backfill behind the
sheet piles below the pavement and around the deadman anchor All fills
should be compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density
established by ASTM D 1557 78
For underwater fills clean granular material such as 3B Fine
ASTM C 33 No 67 is reccnmended

Plans and specifications for the proposed bulkhead repair should
be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written comments prior

Construction Monitoring

investigation
If conditions encountered during construction are different from
those presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made
