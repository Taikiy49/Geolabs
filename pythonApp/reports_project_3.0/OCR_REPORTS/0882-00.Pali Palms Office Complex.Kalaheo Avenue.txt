INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our soil engineering
investigation for the proposed Pali Palms Office Complex to
be constructed in Kaneohe The site is designated by tax map
Mokapu Road intersection Location of the site with respect
to the surrounding area is shown on plot plan Plate 1
The proposed office complex consists of a total of three 4 story
reinforced concrete buildings Column loads will vary and are
anticipated to average about 520 kips Basement parking would
be provided under the building and would consist of asphalt
concrete Basement Parking elevation is tentatively planned

The purpose of this soil engineering investigation was to
explore the subsurface soil and groundwater conditions at
the site and to provide data for use in design and con
struction of the building foundations
Specifically the scope of this investigation included the
following
1 To determine the subsurface soils and groundwater
conditions at the site to the depths which will be
significantly influenced by the proposed construction
2 To evaluate by the performance of the laboratory
tests the physical properties of the soils that
underlie the site which will influence foundation
design and construction
3 To evaluate foundation conditions for the proposed
structure including
a Assimilation interpretation and correlation
of field and laboratory data
b Development of engineering properties of
foundation materials for use in design
c Recommendation of appropriate foundation type
including design criteria and settlement esti

SITE DESCRIPTION _
The project site is located in Kaneohe Oahu Hawaii at the
intersection of Mokapu Boulevard and Kalaheo Avenue The
site covers an area of approximately three acres and is
bordered by Mokapu Boulevard to the west Kalaheo Avenue
to the south Kawainui Canal to the east and private
residential land to the north
At the time of the soil investigation existing structures
consisted of two 2 story wood framed and masonary buildings
several small metal sheds a swimming pool A C roadways
and gravel parking areas The site is landscaped with
numerous large trees and open grassy areas

GEOLOGY AND SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is situated geologically along the coast
line at the northern edge of Kailua Bay Along shoreline
areas the subsurface formations are typically variable
and discontinuous Variable conditions are basically a
result of different environmental conditions caused by
changing sea levels and stream courses Locally the
project site appears to have gone through periods as a
deep water embayment beach and back reef environment
Based on the test borings the site is surficially covered
by a thin 1 to 2 foot layer of man made fill of brown
clayey top soils or coral sand and gravel Underlying
the fill silty carbonate sands grading to clean tan
carbonate beach sands were encountered to depths of 7 to
12 feet Beneath the sand a thick heterogeneous deposit
of clayey sands and gravels typical of a nearshore bay
deposit were encountered to a depth of 89 feet This
layer contains large detrital coralline reefrock fragments
with discontinuous cemented layers at various depths

General
To minimize building settlement shallow pile foundation
system should be used to support high structural loading
Close proximity of basement asphaltic concrete parking
floor to the underlying groundwater would require special
considerations in design of basement parking Specific
recommendations for design and construction are presented
in the subsequent sections of this report

Foundations
During the course of our soil investigation based on the
boring data and laboratory test results two alternate
foundation recommendations are presented
in some of the borings drilled indicated the presence
of soft clayey zones resulting in low soil bearing
Capacity of about 2 000 pounds per square foot
Large magnitudes of total settlement are anticipated
if large spread footings were used Our estimate
indicated that total settlement of about three inches
and differential settlement of one half of the total
settlement should be anticipated
To minimize large magnitudes of settlement we recommend
that a pile foundation system be selected for the
proposed construction
pile foundation system consisting of 12 inch square
concrete piles be used to transfer column and wall
loads to the underlying sandy soils It is estimated
that short piles of about 15 to 25 foot lengths
installed into the cemented or dense coral sand
formation could be designed to carry loads up to
65 tons each live and dead

P 7
Boring 6A 7 and 7A indicated that
cemented coral sand formation may not be present
and piles are anticipated to be driven deeper
before reaching adequate resistance We recommend
that design pile capacity in the area designated
on Plot Plan Plate 1 should not exceed 40 tons
The following guidelines should be used for design
and construction of pile foundation
a Based on present boring information we
anticipate that the pile lengths should
vary between 15 to 25 feet for most of the
piles However the piles located in the
general area designated on the Plot Plan
Plate 1 are expected to be longer varying
in length between 45 to 60 feet
We recommend that a total of 15 test piles
be installed to better estimate final
production pile lengths for this project
Cast length for test piles should be ordered
as follows
1 Eleven piles of 20 foot length
2 Four piles of 60 foot length

Piles should be driven with a hammer delivering
about 26 000 foot pounds of energy The same
type of hammer should be used for production
pile driving as used for the test pile installa
tion
After the test piles are installed one pile
should be load tested to two times the design
loads of 65 tons in order to further verify
the competency of the bearing stratum
The 65 ton test piles should be driven to about
80 blows for the last foot or 8 blows per inch
for the last three inches For the 40 ton test
piles the driving criteria is 60 blows per foot
for the last foot or 6 blows per inch for the
last three inches is recommended The final
pile driving criteria may be modified after
the completion of test pile installation and
load test program
The piles should be spaced no closer than 3 times
the pile widths
Lateral pile capacity of 2 tons each may be considered
for design purposes

Lateral Loads
Basement walls below grade will be subject to horizontal
earth pressure imposed by granular backfill material It
is recommended that the lateral earth pressures induced
on basement walls that are restrained from deflection
be designed for an equivalent fluid pressure of 50 pounds
per cubic foot and 35 pounds per cubic foot for walls
that are free to deflect
Resistance to lateral loads may be provided by a combination
of passive pressure and friction Passive pressure may be
taken as that exerted by a fluid of density equal to 300
pounds per cubic foot Friction may be taken as normal
dead load times a coefficient of friction factor equal to
0 45 A combination of friction passive pressure and
lateral load per pile may be used provided that passive
pressure is reduced by one third
Excavation will be required to attain the proposed parking
elevation of 3 0 feet MSL Excavated on site sandy
material provided it is free from debris could be stock
piled and reused as a source of fill and granular backfill
around pile caps behind basement walls and as subbase
material under A C pavement

P 10
Fill should be placed in layers not exceeding 9 inch lifts
and compacted to 95 percent of the maximum dry density as
determined by the ASTM D 1557 Test Designation
Tentative basement A C parking would be at elevation 3 0
feet MSL It is anticipated that seasonal high tides may
cause occasional infilteration of groundwater close to the
bottom of the A C pavement and possibly causing pavement
damage and resulting in higher maintenance costs
Infilteration of groundwater could be minimized by providing
sumps at the building basement to collect water and pumping
it out during high tides Pumps could be installed with
automatic triggering devices
If A C pavement is used for the basement parking the
following pavement section could be considered
2 inch Asphaltic Concrete
4 inch Base Course 95 Compaction
6 inch Subbase consisting of 3B
Fine Rock Aggregate to provide
capillary break

Considering the possibility of high future costs to main
tain A C pavement it may be advisable to consider the
use of concrete slab for the basement parking Concrete
Slab if used should be designed to sustain uplift
pressure caused during high tides A 4 inch layer of
granular material such as 3B Fine Rock should be
provided under the slab to provide a Capillary break
On Grade Parking
The entire subgrade under A C parking area should be scarified
moisture conditioned and then recompacted to obtain uniform
subgrade condition prior to receiving structural fill and
base course layer
If the subgrade is bearing on select on site sandy soil the
pavement design may be as follows
2 inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 inch Base Course
8 inch Total Pavement Thickness
If clayey pockets are detected within 2 feet below the A C
surface during subgrade preparation they should be
excavated and replaced with compacted on site sand fill

APPENDIX A
FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by
drilling 14 borings using truck mounted rotary drilling equip
ment with continuous flight solid state stem 4 inch auger
The borings were drilled to depths ranging from 14 5 to 89 0
feet Below the existing ground surface undisturbed and dis
turbed samples were obtained and penetration tests were
performed
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by
visual and textural examination in the field by our engineers
who continuously monitored the drilling operations The
classifications were reviewed by visual inspection and testing
in the laboratory The soils were Classified in general
conformance with the unified soil Classification system A
graphic presentation of the soils is presented on the boring
logs Plates A 1 through A 14
In the borings soil samples were obtained by driving
either a split spoon sampler or a 2 4 inch I D split barrel
ring sampler Samplers were driven using a standard 140
pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts used to drive
the sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the boring logs
at the appropriate depths
