SOILS INVESTIGATION
PROPOSED AIRCRAFT PARKING APRON
HICKAM AIR FORCE BASE OAHU HAWAII
This report presents the results of our soils investigation for
the proposed aircraft parking apron at Hickam Air Force Base Oahu
Hawaii The investigation was performed in general accordance with
our proposal dated and consisted of the drilling and
sampling of 8 borings at the subject site visual observations and
laboratory testing
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located at the Fort Kamehameha Military
Reservation within the Hickam Air Force Base complex in Honolulu
Oahu Hawaii The general location of the site and its vicinity are
shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
W O 1261 00 Page 2
The site is bounded by Taxiway M to the north Harbor Drive to
the south Kumumau Canal to the west and the reef runway taxilane to
the east
An existing building is located along the southern boundary and
approximately midway into the site A large concrete slab and old
asphaltic concrete pavement section are located at the southwest and
southeastern corners respectively Overhead electrical lines about 20
feet high pass through the entire site with poles spaced about 100
feet on center There is a drainage ditch extending through the
southeastern portion of the site
In general the site is relatively flat and slopes gently toward
the southeast Ground surface elevations vary from zero 0 at the
drainage ditch to about 7 5 feet next to the Taxiway M
Ground cover consisted of thick Keawe bushes with tall grasses
and weeds
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to clear and grade the site for the construction
of a parking apron to provide six parking spaces for F 4 Phantom
aircrafts The parking apron may also be used by F 16 aircrafts It
is anticipated that the site grading will require minimal amount of
fill and that rigid concrete pavements will be utilized

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The project site is situated over an extensive coral algal reef
which forms the seaward margin of the Honolulu Ewa coastal plan As
urban and military base development progressed during the
early and mid twentieth century many lowlying areas were filled to
provide usuable land area The subject site is one of these reclaimed
areas
Subsur f ace Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by the
drilling of eight 8 borings at the approximate locations shown on
the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were drilled utilizing
truck mounted rotary hydraulic drilling equipment Detailed
description of the field exploration and laboratory testing along
with the boring logs and laboratory test results are presented in the
appendices of this report
The locations of the borings were based on the preliminary apron
layout Subsequent to the field exploration the apron location was
shifted approximately 100 feet towards the east It is our opinion
that the borings drilled can still provide adequate coverage and are
representative of the subsurface conditions

Soil Conditions
Our borings encountered a surface layer of man made fill rang ing
from 1 5 to 5 5 feet in thickness This fill consisted generally of
tan coral sands with coral gravel The fill appeared to be moderately
to well compacted with consistencies ranging from medium dense to
dense
Below the surficial fill material except in Borings 4 and 5 the
exploration encountered a layer of medium stiff brown clayey silt
which ranged from 1 5 to 3 0 feet in thickness This material was not
encountered in Borings 4 and 5
At a depth of approximately 5 feet below the existing surface
our borings encountered a stratum of natural coral sands These sands
ranged from silty sands to gravelly sands with consistencies varying
between medium dense and very loose to loose
At approximately 16 feet below the existing surface the borings
encountered medium hard coral formation which extended to the maximun
depth drilled of 20 5 feet below the surface
Groundwater was encountered in the drilled borings at depths
varying between 3 to 5 8 feet below the existing ground elevations
More detailed groundwater information are listed in the boring logs in
the appendices of this report

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Our field exploration disclosed that the site is underlain by
basically competent coral sand materials It is our opinion that the
subject site is suitable for the proposed parking apron
Site Grading
It is our understanding that the proposed site grading will
require minimal amount of fill
Ground Preparation
Ce ed
Prior to the commencement of earthwork all areas within the
grading limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed
including the demolition of all existing structures slabs and
existing pavements The resulting debris and grubbed material
should be disposed of properly off site Soft mud encountered at
the bottom of the drainage ditch should be removed
All areas to receive fill should be scarified to a depth of 6
inches moisture conditioned to optimum moisture and compacted to
95 maximum dry density as established by ASTM D 1557 78 Any
soft weak or pumping areas disclosed during these operations
should be over excavated to firm material and the resulting
depression backfilled with select non expansive material
compacted to 6 inch lifts to the above specified density

Fill Material

Fill material should consist of select non expansive material
with a minimum CBR value of 45 Fill should be placed in 6 inch
thick horizontal lifts and compacted to 95 maximum dry density
as established by ASTM D 1557 78
Underwater fills drainage ditch area should consist of granular
material with less than about 20 passing the 200 sieve fines
Compaction Requirement
a
For the on site coral material and imported granular soil we
recommend the following compaction requirement
Top 6 inches of subgrade 100 per ASTM D 1557 78
Remaining depth below subgrade 95 per ASIM D 1557 78

Generally minimal amount of fill is planned for the site
therefore minimal settlements are anticipated
In the existing drainage ditch fills up to about 5 feet thick
may be required If the soft mud in the bottom of the ditch is
cleaned out prior to filling settlements are anticipated to be
small and should occur rapidly

Aircraft Parking Apron Pavement
The first phase of the apron construction involves providing six
parking spaces for F 4 Phantom aircrafts
The new pavements will be constructed of Portland cement concrete
Subgrade Support for Pavement Design
tet eee
It is anticipated that the pavement finished elevation will vary
from about 7 5 feet next to the existing taxiway and slope down
to about elevation 4 feet at the southern edge of the apron
Low fill generally less than 1 5 feet are anticipated for most
of the area except in the existing drainage ditch where fills up
to 5 feet are required
The pavement design will be governed by the near surface coral
fill and new fill material Seven laboratory CBR tests were
performed on the near surface coral fill soil to determine its
CBR value when compacted to near 100 density The CBR values
varied from 28 to 50 with an average of 44
GEOLABS HAWAII
In general coral materials often produce a high CBR test value
under static test loading condition However the coral material
may have a tendency to break down under repeated dynamic
loading Therefore it is recommended that a design CBR value
equal to 75 of the laboratory average should be used This
corresponds to a design CBR of 33 which correlates to a subgrade
modulus k of 360 p s i inch
Aircraft Traffic
It is our understanding that the parking apron will be used
primarily for F 4 Phantom jets However F 16 aircrafts may also
use the facility
