Proposed 3 Lot Subdivision
Alani Drive
Manoa Oahu Hawaii
In accordance with your request we have performed a preliminary
geologic reconnaissance of the above referenced site The purpose of our
study was to examine the surface and near surface conditions at the site
in order to evaluate the feasibility of the proposed three lot
subdivision This report summarizes our preliminary findings and
recommendations
General Site Description
The subject lot covers about 33 500 square feet and is situated on
the western upslope side of Alani Drive in Manoa Valley The existing
ground surface slopes upward from Alani Drive towards existing residences
along Woodlawn Drive Along the northern boundary of the site there is a
drainageway which leads to a culvert crossing of Alani Drive This
drainageway has side slopes of approximately 3 horizontal to 1 vertical
and is approximately 30 feet in width Most of this existing drainage
course is densely overgrown with weeds small trees and bamboo The
remainder of the site is gently sloping towards Alani Drive at about 10

vertical fronting the street There is an existing wood frame house in

fruit trees on the site
Field Exploration
The field exploration consisted of geologic reconnaissance of the
site and its vicinity and the drilling of four 4 shallow hand auger
probes to depths between 2 and 7 feet below the existing ground surface
The on site reconnaissance indicated that the bottom of the drainageway
has pockets of soft soils and organic soils which are underlain at shallow
depths about 2 5 feet by boulders and cobbles Running water was noted
in most of the drainageway at the time of our field work and groundwater
was encountered in Probe P 2 at a depth of 3 5 feet below the ground
surface Our field work also indicated that the remainder of the site
the lot area is underlain by stiff to very stiff brown clayey silts to
the maximum depths drilled in the hand auger probes
The locations of the hand auger probes are indicated on the attached
Site Plan The probing logs are attached for your reference
Preliminary Findings and Recommendations
The field exploration disclosed that the surface and near surface of
the drainageway area is underlain by wet to saturated soft clayey and
organic soils These soils are generally weak and are not capable of
providing support for high embankments Special site grading
stabilization or removal of these soils may be required

The field exploration also disclosed that the surface and
near surface of the lot area is underlain by stiff clayey silt soils with
a low to moderate expansion potential Special foundation design may be
required to resist or reduce potential foundation and or slab movements
Due to the limited amount of soil data obtained from this preliminary
reconnaissance it is recommended that additional exploration be
undertaken to obtain more definitive data
Site Grading
In general the site grading will consist of cutting Lots A and C to
generate fill material for a proposed driveway which will overlie a
culvert structure in the existing drainageway It is anticipated that
cuts will be up to 10 feet deep in Lot A and up to 4 feet deep in Iot c
Fills of up to 16 feet thick will be required for the driveway grading
Due to a combination of the high annual rainfall and dense
vegetation the in situ moisture contents of the on site soils are
generally high 45 to 77 Therefore some arying out of the excavated
material may be required to attain the proper moisture content for
adequate compaction Alternatively drier off site material may be
imported for use as fill
Site Preparation
Prior to the commencement of earthwork the area within the grading
limits should be cleared and grubbed of all vegetation organic
material debris and other deleterious material These materials
should be removed from the the site to prevent contamination of the
fill material being generated from the cut areas and disposed of
properly off site in accordance with the appropriate ordinances
regulations and statutes

Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
Fill material should be moisture conditioned to near optimum moisture
content placed in horizontal lifts not exceeding eight inches 8
in loose thickness and compacted to 90 maximum dry density as
determined by ASTM D 1557 78 Slopes should be over filled at a
steeper ratio and then cut back to the 2H 1V finish slope ratio
Fills to be placed on existing slopes steeper than 5H 1V should be
keyed and benched into the existing slopes to resist and reduce
potential movement of the fill

It is anticipated that the site grading will require cuts of up to
approximately 12 feet in depth cut slopes of 2 horizontal to 1
vertical may be used Slope benches should be provided at maximum
vertical height intervals of about 20 feet if the final grading
plans will require cuts in excess of 20 feet
Near the finished grade of the slope faces cutting should be done
with extreme care Overcutting beyond the design slope ratio should
be avoided as attempts to backfill and recompact overcut areas would
most likely result in loose areas which would be subject to later
damage by erosion If boulders or small ledges of rock are
encountered near the design slope it may be desirable to carefully

cut or break these materials to match the design slope rather than
risking damage to the slope by loosening the soils adjacent to the
boulders or ledges by hard excavation
Surface run off from areas above the slope should be diverted away
from the cut slope bank and prevented from flowing across the slope
face The slope should be planted or otherwise protected as soon
as possible to minimize damage and unravelling by erosion
Due to the relatively high rainfall in the vicinity of the project
site and the nature of the soils encountered in the probes it is
possible that zones of seepage or intermittent seepage may be
encountered If so the geotechnical engineer should be consulted
for recommendations concerning the treatment of the seepage
Additional Exploration
Due to the preliminary nature of our field work and the sampling
technique used it was not possible to obtain all of the necessary soil
samples or data required for the development of recommendations for
foundation design potential settlements potential soil movement and or
pavement design Therefore it is recommended that additional exploration
be performed to obtain the data for these analyses
Design Review
Plans and specifications for foundations site grading and utility
construction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and
written comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
additional exploration
