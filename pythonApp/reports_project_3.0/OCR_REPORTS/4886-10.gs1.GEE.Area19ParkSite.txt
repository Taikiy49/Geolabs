PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is within the Ewa by Gentry East Development area on the Island of
Oahu Hawaii The project site is east of Fort Weaver Road south of Iroquois Point Road
and west of Keaunui Drive
We understand the portion of the park south of the existing wastewater pump
station will be developed and includes construction of a new comfort station and parking
lot to accommodate about 81 parking stalls In addition we anticipate that utility lines
will be installed The project site is relatively level and covered with light brush and a
tree In addition an existing soil stockpile and drain pipes were observed near the
comfort station location
We understand that grading plans for the project are not available at this time
Based on the fairly level topography at the site we anticipate that minimal grading with
cuts and fills up to about 2 feet will be required

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the results of our exploration the project site is generally underlain by fill
alluvium and coral formation Groundwater was not encountered in the test pits at the time
of our field exploration
The new comfort station building may be supported by shallow spread and or
continuous footings bearing on the stiffidense on site soils or compacted fill
Slabs on grade should be properly designed for the moderately expansive clayey soils
encountered at the project site Detailed discussion of these items and our pavement
design and site grading recommendations are presented in the following sections
Building Foundations
Based on the anticipated subsurface soil conditions we believe that shallow spread
and or continuous footings may be used to support the new comfort station structure An
allowable bearing pressure of up to 3 500 pounds per square foot psf may be used for
the design of shallow foundations bearing on the stiff dense on site soils or properly
compacted structural fill This bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may be
increased by one third 1 3 for transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic
forces
The bottom of the footing excavations should be scarified to a depth of at least
8 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture and
recompacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction to provide a relatively firm and
smooth bearing surface prior to placement of reinforcing steel or concrete Relative
compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the
maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557
Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the
maximum dry density
Soft and or loose materials encountered at the bottom of the footing excavations
should be over excavated to expose the underlying firm materials The over excavation
may be backfilled with the on site soils compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction or the footing bottom may be extended down to the underlying competent
GEOLABS INC
Hawaii California
Gentry Homes Ltd Page 4
W O 4886 10
materials For footings bearing on new compacted fills the bottom of the footing
excavations should also be recompacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
prior to the placement of reinforcing steel or concrete
In general the footing bottoms should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below
the lowest adjacent finished grades Footings should have a minimum width of 12 inches
Footings constructed near tops of slopes or on sloping ground should be embedded deep
enough to provide a minimum horizontal setback distance of 8 feet measured from the
outside edge of the footings to the face of the slope
Foundations located next to utility trenches or easements should be embedded
below a 45 degree imaginary plane extending upward from the bottom edge of the utility
trench or as deep as the utility line inverts This requirement is necessary to avoid
surcharging adjacent below grade structures with additional structural loads and to reduce
the potential for appreciable foundation settlement
If the foundations are designed and constructed in accordance with our
recommendations we estimate that total settlements of footings supported on the
recompacted stiff dense on site soils or new compacted fills may be on the order of about
1 inch We estimate that the differential settlements between adjacent foundations to be on
the order of about 0 5 inch
Lateral loads acting on the building structure may be resisted by friction developed
between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil and by passive earth pressure
acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system A coefficient of friction of
0 35 may be used for footings bearing on the stiff dense on site soils or new compacted
fills Resistance to lateral loads due to passive earth pressure may be estimated using an
equivalent fluid pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth pcf This
assumes that the soils around the footings are well compacted Unless covered by
pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the upper 12 inches of soil should be
neglected In addition the passive pressure for foundations located on slopes should be
reduced
A Geolabs representative should observe footing excavations prior to placement of
reinforcing steel and concrete to confirm the foundation bearing conditions and the
required embedment depths
Slab On Grade Foundations
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered we believe that slabs on grade
with thickened edge footings may be used to support the comfort station structure planned
for the project Our exploration indicates that the project site is generally underlain by
moderately expansive clayey soils near the existing ground surface Unless slab on grade
foundations supported by these expansive soils are properly designed there is a potential
GEOLABS INC
Hawaii California
Gentry Homes Ltd Page 5
W O 4886 10
for future distress to these structures resulting from wetting and subsequent swelling of the
expansive clayey soils
To reduce the potential for structural distress resulting from the swelling of the
on site expansive soils we recommend over excavating the comfort station area to allow
capping with at least 24 inches of non expansive select granular fill material The
perimeter thickened edges of the slabs should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches
below the lowest adjacent exterior grade The embedment of the interior thickened edge
footings may be reduced to a minimum depth of 12 inches below the adjacent finished
grade The base of the thickened edge should have a minimum width of 12 inches
The concrete slab should be reinforced with reinforcing steel bars The project
structural engineer should be consulted for design details of the thickened edge which is
intended to function both as a moisture barrier and as a perimeter bearing wall footing
Prior to placement of the non expansive select granular fill material the clayey
subgrade should be scarified to a minimum depth of 8 inches moisture conditioned to at
least 2 percent above the optimum moisture content and compacted to at least 90 percent
relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil
expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in
accordance with ASTM D 1557 Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by
dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
The slab subgrades including the select granular fill should be kept moist until the
placement of cushion fill and concrete The select granular fill material should provide a
capping layer of moist soil above the underlying potentially expansive subgrade soil
thereby reducing the loss of moisture from the clayey subgrade soils In our opinion the
reduction in potential drying and wetting of the subgrade soils should reduce the impact of
shrink swell effects on the slab due to potential heaving of the underlying expansive clayey
soils when wetted
We recommend incorporating a minimum 4 inch layer of cushion fill below the
concrete slab The cushion fill should consist of free draining materials such as
No 3 Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation which would also serve as a capillary
moisture break The slab subgrade should be thoroughly wetted prior to concrete
placement to allow the select granular fill layer to remain in a moist condition To reduce
the potential for future moisture infiltration and subsequent damage to floor coverings an
impervious moisture barrier is recommended on top of the cushion layer It is also
recommended for the interior wall design to incorporate some flexibility to accommodate
small amounts of possible ground movements
Areas adjacent to the slabs should be backfilled tightly against the slab edges with
relatively impervious soil and graded to divert water away from the slab to reduce the
potential for water ponding around the foundations A Geolabs representative should

Pavement Design
We anticipate that an asphaltic concrete pavement will be used for the planned
parking area In general we anticipate that the planned parking area will have a
low volume of vehicular traffic with light trucks Based on the above we recommend using
the following preliminary pavement design
Flexible Pavement Section
2 0 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 0 Inch Aggregate Base Course 95 Percent Relative Compaction
8 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness on Moist Compacted Subgrade
The subgrade soils under the pavement areas should be scarified to a minimum
depth of 8 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture and compacted to
at least 95 percent relative compaction CBR tests and or field observations should be
performed on the actual subgrade soils during construction to confirm that the above
design section is adequate The aggregate base course should consist of crushed basaltic
aggregate compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
In general paved areas should be sloped and drainage gradients should be
maintained to carry surface water off the pavements Surface water ponding should not be
allowed on site during or after construction Where concrete curbs are used to isolate
landscaping in or adjacent to the pavement areas we recommend extending the curbs a
minimum of 2 inches into the subgrade soil to reduce the potential for migration of
excessive landscape water into the pavement section
Site Grading
We anticipate that the site grading work will generally consist of minor cuts and fills
of up to about 2 feet A Geolabs representative should monitor the site grading operations
to observe whether undesirable materials are encountered during the excavation process
and to confirm whether the exposed soil rock conditions are similar to those encountered
in our field exploration
At the on set of earthwork areas within the contract grading limits should be
cleared and grubbed thoroughly Vegetation debris deleterious material and other
unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed properly off site or stockpiled in a
designated area to reduce the potential for contaminating the excavated materials
Soft and yielding areas encountered during clearing and grubbing should be
over excavated to expose firm natural material and the resulting excavation should be

backfilled with well compacted engineered fill The excavated soft soils may be used as
fill provided that it meets the requirements for fill material
Prior to filling the existing ground should be scarified to a depth of 8 inches
moisture conditioned to about 2 percent above the optimum moisture and compacted to a
minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Where shrinkage cracks are observed after
compaction of the subgrade we recommend thoroughly moistening the soil to close
cracks or preparing again as recommended above Saturation and subsequent yielding of
the exposed subgrade due to inclement weather and poor drainage may require
over excavation of the soft areas and replacement with well compacted engineered fill
The excavated soft soils may be used as fill provided that it meets the requirements for fill
material Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a
percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with
ASTM D 1557
The fill material should consist of low expansive materials less than 3 inches in
largest dimension with a CBR swell of less than 1 percent The fill material should also be
free of vegetation and deleterious materials In general the on site silty gravel materials
should be suitable for fill material
Select granular fill and imported fill should consist of non expansive granular
material such as crushed coral basalt or cinder sand The material should be well graded
from coarse to fine with particles no larger than 3 inches in largest dimension and should
contain between 10 and 30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve The material
should have a laboratory CBR value of 20 or more and should have a maximum swell of
less than 1 percent when tested in accordance with ASTM D 1883 Geolabs should test
imported fill materials for conformance with these recommendations prior to delivery to the
project site for its intended use
Fills and backfills should be moisture conditioned to about 2 percent above the
optimum moisture placed in level lifts not exceeding 12 inches in loose thickness and
compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the
in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the
same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 Optimum moisture is the water
content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
Compaction should be accomplished by sheepsfoot rollers vibratory rollers
multiple wheel pneumatic tired rollers or other types of acceptable compaction equipment
Fill slopes should be designed with a slope inclination of two horizontal to one
vertical 2H 1V or flatter Fills placed on slopes steeper than 5H 1V should be keyed and
benched into the existing slope to provide stability of the new fill against sliding The filling
operations should start at the lowest point and continue up in level horizontal compacted
layers in accordance with the above fill placement recommendations Water should be

Utility Trench
It is anticipated that the utilities for the comfort station will consist primarily of water
sewer and drain lines In general good construction practices should be utilized for the
installation and backfilling of the trenches for the proposed utilities The contractor should
determine the method and equipment to be used for excavation subject to practical limits
and safety considerations The excavation should comply with all applicable local state
and federal safety requirements The contractor should be responsible for trench shoring
design and installation Trench shoring and bracing should conform to the appropriate
health and safety requirements
In general we recommend using granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of
No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation to support the utility lines The initial
trench backfill up to about 12 inches above the pipes should consist of free draining
backfills such as No 3B Fine gravel to reduce the potential for pipe damage from
compaction of the backfill It is critical to use a free draining granular material to reduce the
potential for formation of voids below the haunches of pipes and to provide adequate
support for the sides of the pipes The use of on site clayey soils as backfill around the
utility pipes is not recommended and should not be allowed
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipes to
the finished subgrade may consist of approved on site soils or select granular fill material
The backfill material should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture
content placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a
minimum of 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for future ground
subsidence The upper 3 feet of the trench backfill below the pavement grade should be
compacted to no less than 95 percent relative compaction Mechanical compaction
equipment should be used to compact the backfill material considering the clayey nature
of the on site soils
Drainage
The finished grades outside the building structure should be sloped to shed water
away from the foundations and slabs and to reduce the potential for ponding Surface
water ponding should not be allowed on the site during or after construction It is highly
recommended to install gutter systems around the buildings and to divert discharge away
from the foundation areas Excessive landscape watering near the foundations and slabs
within 3 feet of the building perimeter should be avoided Planters next to foundations
should also be avoided or have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce the potential for
water infiltration into the subsoils

to reduce the potential for water infiltration In addition drainage swales should be
provided as soon as possible and should be maintained to drain surface water runoff away
from the foundations and slabs

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture Content ASTM D 2216 and Unit Weight determinations ASTM D 2937
were performed on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of
soil properties The test results are presented on the Logs of Test Pits at the appropriate
sample depths
Seven one inch ring swell tests were performed on selected samples to evaluate
the swelling potential of the soils under surcharge pressures These tests were run on
remolded samples Swell test results are presented on Plate B 1
One Atterberg Limits test ASTM D 4318 was performed on a selected soil
samples to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits The test results are presented on the
Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depth Graphic presentation of the test results
is provided on Plate B 2
Two laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR tests ASTM D 1883 were
performed on bulk samples of the near surface soils to evaluate the pavement support
characteristics of the soils The samples were remolded to near the optimum moisture
content of the soils Compaction of the samples was performed by dropping a 10 pound
hammer from a height of 18 inches The samples were compacted in five equal layers
with each layer receiving 56 blows of energy The samples were soaked in water prior to
the penetration test The results of the tests are presented on Plates B 3 and B 4
