PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed plan is to grade the site for a mlti family townhouse
condominium development The general site plan indicates that fourteen
14 residential structures with access roads and parking areas are
proposed A culvert or bridge structure is proposed in the western
portion of the site for the crossing by the main access road of an
intermittent drainageway
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located in the Mauna Lani Resort development area
in the district of South Kohala on the island of Hawaii The site is an
irregular polygon and is bounded to the north by Mauna Lani Drive and the
Tennis Club complex to the east and south by golf course and to the west
by undeveloped land
The terrain of the project site is rugged consisting of hillocks and
hollows characteristic of volcanic lavas in the pahoehoe and a a forms
see Photo 1 The ground surface elevations at the site range from about
20 to 50 feet above Mean Sea Level with local vertical relief as great as
15 to 20 feet

t
There are innumerable large cracks in the surface of the volcanic
rock formation at the site Some of these cracks are up to about three
3 feet in width and extend to depths of as much as 10 feet
Many cavities in the volcanic rock formation open to the ground
surface were observed at the site These cavities ranged from small
blisters or bubbles less than a foot in size to cavernous lava tubes with
heights up to about 10 feet extending 20 feet or more back from the
opening see Photos 2 and 3
Details of the proposed site grading were not available at the time

anticipate that cuts and fills on the order of ten 10 feet in depth or
thickness will be required to create level pads for the construction of
the proposed townhouses
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
Hawaii the largest island of the Hawaiian Archipelago covers an
area of approximately 4000 square miles The island was formed by the
activity of five 5 shield volcanoes These are Kohala which is long
extinct Mauna Kea which has had activity during recent geologic times
Hualalai which last erupted in 1801 and Mauna oa and Kilauea both
still active
The site is located on the northwestern flank of Mauna Loa mountain
which makes up the central and southern part of the island consists
largely of the prehistoric member of the Kau Volcanic Series These
volcanics are the assemblage of Recent and Latest Pleistocene a a and
pahoehoe basalts laid down by the volcano The Kau lavas form a thin
veneer on the lower slopes of Mauna Loa
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2329 00 Page 5
The lava formation encountered at the site appears to be a pahochoe
flow which spread and ponded as it approached the ocean As the flow
cooled fissures opened in its surface and some of the molten rock inside
the flow was extruded onto the surface as a a lava with a very coarse
clinker surface
As the molten lava from the interior was extruded the pahoehoe
surface subsided This subsidence resulted in an intense jointing of the
surface
The extrusion of material from the interior of the flow also resulted
in cavities or voids below the surface of the pahoehoe crust The voids
observed at the site ranged from less than one 1 foot to about ten 10
feet in height The voids observed at the site range in horizontal extent
from less than one 1 foot to large caves about 20 feet in width and
extending several tens of feet back under the ground surface
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field explorations disclosed that the site is underlain by
volcanic materials consisting of unconsolidated clinker and friable
pahoehoe lava rock formation From a geotechnical point of view the site
is suitable for the proposed condominium development provided that our
recommendations are followed
The volcanic materials should provide adequate bearing for the
proposed structures related roads and parking areas ann
We anticipate that the excavated clinker and friable pahoehoe will
probably experience some shrinkage during handling and compaction
Based on previous experience in the vicinity of the project site a
shrinkage factor of 0 5 to 0 7 may be considered for the various
excavated rock materials
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2329 00 Page 6
The excavated rock material is expected to break down to a size range
of one 1 to twelve 12 inches during handling and compaction
Cavities
Our reconnaissance and exploration revealed that there are a
significant number of cavities and voids open to the existing ground
surface at the project site This along with observations of adjacent
cuts tend to indicate that there are more voids in the subsurface which
are not exposed or open
Our reconnaissance also indicates that the near surface rock
materials are brittle and friable Voids in such material generally would
be easily collapsed under heavy loads
Since the proposed condominium buildings will impose light structural
loads on the footing subgrades it is our opinion that proofrolling a
minimum of ten 10 passes with a heavy bulldozer such as a D9 would be
sufficient to detect and collapse most of the near surface voids below
deep fill areas i e fills greater than five 5 feet thick Collapsed
voids should be backfilled with rock fill or imported select granular
material placed as recommended in the Fill Placement section of this
report
Several of the open cavities observed at the project site were large
enough to be potentially hazardous to heavy equipment operations
driving These large cavities should be accurately located and collapsed
without driving the equipment over them such as by ripping at their outer
limits or by hoeram
Caution should always be exercised when operating heavy equipment at
the site
To detect and fill voids or cavities in the subgrade of foundations
located in shallow fill and cut areas we recommend that a program of
probing and grouting be implemented Probes should be drilled at each
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2329 00 Page 7
column footing location amd on five 5 foot centers below strip footings
to a depth of ten 10 feet below the bottom of the foundation
The probes should be backfilled with a mortar like grout
The probing and grouting should be performed under the observation of
the project geotechnical engineer so that the program may be modified if
necessary
Site Grading
We anticipate that the major site grading work would consist of cuts
and fills up to five 5 to ten 10 feet deep or thick over most of the
building sites Some imported fill may be required for the proposed
grading
Site Preparation
Prior to filling the area to receive fill should either be scarified
to a depth of 12 inches and recompacted with a heavy dozer or roller
to obtain a dense choking surface layer or a choking gravel fill
should be placed over the clinker surface within fill areas We
believe several passes would be required to provide a dense and
compacted surface layer Any loose areas or voids disclosed during
site preparation should be repaired by removing the loose materials
and or collapsing the void then backfilling with suitable compacted
on site materials
Fill Material
Generally the on site small clinker material less than 12 inches in
size will be suitable for fill Clinkers or large rock fragments in
excess of 12 inches in size should be crushed down to smaller sizes
or disposed of in deep fill section in accordance with the procedures
recommended under the subsequent section Boulder Disposal

Imported fill material required for the embankment fill is assumed to
be similar to clinker or other well graded granular rocky materials
The maximum rock size should generally be less than 12 inches
Samples of any imported material should be submitted to the project
geotechnical engineer for determination of its suitability prior to
being transported to the site
If the excavated rock material does not contain sufficient fines to
produce a well graded material off site cinders or soil may be mixed
into the excavated rocky material to increase the percentage of fines
to provide a better graded material for compaction and increase the
stability of the fills
Boulder Disposal
Boulders i e rock fragments larger than 12 inches in size
encountered during site grading and in excavations may be used in the
deeper fills provided that the following recommendations are
followed
1 Boulders should be spread out and must not be nested together
They should be placed so that compaction equipment will be able
to compact around them
2 No part of the boulder should encroach any closer to finished
subgrade than the boulder s largest dimension and in no case be
closer than three 3 feet to finished subgrade
3 No boulder larger than three 3 feet in its largest dimension
should be placed in the fill It may be necessary to reduce the
size of larger boulders or dispose of them off site
4 Care must be exercised to avoid placement of boulders in the
proposed utility alignments to prevent difficulty in the later
excavation of the utility trenches

Fill Placement
Embankment fills should be placed in horizontal loose lifts up to 18
inches thick and compacted to 90 of its maximm density per ASIM
Test Method D 1557 78
Conventional compaction testing is generally not practicable in
excavated volcanic rock material due to the large particle size
Instead a testing program consisting of visual observations and
test strips to determine the lift thickness and mmmber of passes of
a compactor required to achieve the desired compaction should be
conducted at the start of the project The number of passes may then
be used as the criteria for compaction
Excavation
The field exploration disclosed that the site is underlain by friable
pahoehoe and unconsolidated clinker
It is anticipated that the pahoehoe and clinker may be excavated with
normal heavy excavating methods i e large bulldozer and or large
backhoe Some hard digging requiring the use of a hoeram may be
required if large accretionary lava balls boulders or ledges are
encountered
The above discussions regarding the rippability of the surface
materials are based on our visual observation of the existing lava
formations and cuts in the area and field data from the seismic
refraction surveys performed at the site
We recommend that all prospective contractors bidding on this site
should examine these cuts the site conditions and the seismic
refraction data to make their own interpretation

Slopes
In general two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V fill slopes are
reconmended
It appears that most of the cut slopes will be in varying mterials
mostly clinker and some dense rock An average overall design cut
recommended for cuts less than 20 feet high Steeper cut slopes may

Foundations
We believe the on site rocky ground and compacted granular fill
resulting from the above site grading will provide suitable foundation
support for the proposed buildings
Spread and continuous line footing foundations may be used for the
proposed structures
To provide uniform support for all foundations we recommend that
foundation locations in cut areas be ripped or scarified to 12 inches
below footing subgrade and then compacted to a minimm of 90 of the
maximm dry density per ASIM Test Method D 1557 78
All exterior footing bottoms should be embedded a minimm of 16
inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade The minimum embedment
for interior footings may be reduced to 12 inches below the finished
grade
For foundations bearing on compacted rock fill an allowable net soil
bearing pressure of up to 4000 p s f may be used This value may be
increased by one third when temporary wind or seismic loads are
considered
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2329 00 Page 11
Slabs and spread footing foundations located close to the tops of
slopes should have sufficient embedment to obtain a minimm horizontal
set back distance of ten 10 feet measured from the outer edge of the
footing to the slope face
The ground surface should be shaped to divert surface runoff away
from building foundations and slabs
It is anticipated that the footings if constructed in accordance
with the above recommendations will have negligible settlements
Slabs On Grade
Based on the non expansive slab subgrade conditions anticipated at
this site on grade slab should be placed over a 4 inch layer of base
course compacted to 90 of its maximm density as established by ASIM Test
Method D 1557 78 The thickened edge around the on grade slab should
extend a minimum of 12 inches below the lowest adjacent finished ground
surface
Depending on the structure of the underlying rock formation a
choking layer consisting of well graded rock materials may be required
between the underlying rugged rock surface and the upper cushion fill or
concrete slab The choking layer could be eliminated by the substitution
of one layer of Mirafi 180N over the rock surface
To reduce potential differential settlement where the slab is partly
on cut in rock and partly on fill the cut portions should be
over excavated about one foot and then recompacted to match the adjacent
subgrade density
Utilities
It is anticipated that most of the trenching will be done in the
friable pahoehoe or unconsolidated clinker material However some
boulders or areas of dense basalt may be encountered especially in deep
trenches

Asphalt concrete pavements may be used for the roadways and parking

A
Clearing and grubbing shall be performed and debris shall be

be removed to expose competent natural soils Localized soft
pockets shall be undercut and backfilled with compacted select
materials

Engineer prior to the placement of any fill
After approval the surface shall be scarified to a depth of six
inches brought to near optimum moisture content and compacted
as herein specified

Placement and Compaction of Fill
A
The fill shall be placed in horizontal layers which when
compacted will not exceed twelve inches 12 in thickness
Each layer shall be spread uniformly and blade mixed to attain
uniformity of material and water content within each layer
Additional fill material shall not be placed on any fill layer
which has not been properly compacted Subsequent removal of
any unacceptable layer shall be done at the Contractor s
expense
Rock fragments or boulders less than twelve inches 12 in
diameter may be utilized provided they are not concentrated in
any one area and that there is sufficient fine grained material
to fill the voids between the rocks
Rocks greater than eight inches 8 in diameter shall be
removed from the fill unless specifically approved for placement
in the fill by the Geotechnical Engineer
All fill shall be compacted to a minimm of 90 percent of the
maximum dry density established by ASIM Test Method D 1557 78
unless otherwise specified in the Geotechnical Engineer s
Report
Boulders encountered in excavations may be used in the deeper
fills provided that the following are followed
1 Boulders shall be spread out and must not be nested
together They should be placed so that compaction
equipment will be able to compact around them

2 No part of the boulder should enroach any closer to finished
subgrade than the boulder s largest dimension and in no
case be closer than 3 feet to finished subgrade
3 No boulder larger than 5 feet in its largest dimension
should be placed in the fill It may be necessary to reduce
the size of larger boulders or dispose of them off site
4 Care must be exercised to avoid placement of boulders in the
proposed utility alignments to prevent difficulty in the
later excavation of the utility trenches
Conventional compaction testing is generally not practicable in
excavated volcanic rock material due to the large particle
size Instead a testing program consisting of visual
observations and test strips to determine the lift thickness and
number of passes of a compactor required to achieve the desired
compaction should be conducted at the start of the project
The number of passes can then be used as the criteria for
compaction
The compaction equipment may be sheepsfoot vibratory or
pneumatic tire rollers suited to the soil type and capable of
producing the specified results in an expedient and efficient
manner The equipment shall be in good working condition fully
ballasted and self cleaning
The compaction shall be accomplished in a systematic and uniform
manner over the entire fill area to produce a uniformly
compacted fill
