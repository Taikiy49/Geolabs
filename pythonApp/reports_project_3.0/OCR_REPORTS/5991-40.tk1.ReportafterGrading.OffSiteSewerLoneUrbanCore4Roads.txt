Dear Mr. Higham: 
This report summarizes our earthwork observation and testing services performed 
during the grading and backfilling work for the New Off-Site Sewer Line at the Kapolei City 
Urban Core 4 Roads project from November 8, 2011 through January 27, 2012. 
EARTHWORK OPERATIONS 
Delta Construction Corporation performed the site grading operations and sewer 
line installation for the project. The site grading work consisted of subgrade preparation, 
placement and compaction of fills and backfills, and utility trench excavation and backfill. 
During the earthwork operations, the subgrades in areas designated to receive fills  
were scarified to a depth of about 8 inches, moisture-conditioned to above the optimum 
moisture content, and recompacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry 
density determined in accordance with American Society of Testing and Material (ASTM) 
D 1557 (Modified Proctor). 
Fill and backfill materials required for the project generally were placed in near-level 
lifts, brought to above the optimum moisture, and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent

During the site grading operations, samples of the on-site and imported materials 
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing. 
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our 
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D 1557 (Modified Proctor), and the test 
results are summarized on Plate 1. 
Field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations on an 
as-need basis to confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures 
and soil conditions were noted. A total of 52 field density tests, including retest of failed 
area, were performed using the nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM D 6938. 
Results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 2.1 through 2.3 of 
this report. These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on 
constructed fills and backfills at the site. Test locations and elevations were estimated by 
pacing and interpolating from on-site survey stakes. These locations and elevations should 
be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used. Areas assessed 
to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained. 
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the 
referenced tests, it is our opinion that the earthwork operations during the site grading and 
sewer line installation for the above project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period 
covered by this report were performed in general accordance with the intent of the project 
plans and specifications and our geotechnical recommendations presented in the 
referenced report dated January 25, 2008 and subsequent recommendations. 
LIMITATIONS 
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities 
occurring simultaneously on a construction site. Therefore, some of the earthwork were 
performed without our observation. However, the level of earthwork observation and 
testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some of the 
geotechnical risks to the project. It should be recognized that there are variations in the 
accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor 
earthwork construction and materials.  
Our services were performed in accordance with generally accepted standards of 
geotechnical engineering practice; no warranty is expressed or implied. Provision of 
construction monitoring by an engineer is not insurance, nor does it guarantee 
construction of any type. The contractor shall retain responsibility for the quality of the 
work, for adhering to the plans and specifications, and for repairing defects regardless of 
when they are found.
