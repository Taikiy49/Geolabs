INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration performed
for the Kailua Chapel project located in the Kailua area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The
general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering recommendations
resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering analyses These
recommendations are intended for the design of foundations retaining walls site preparation
and pavements only The findings and recommendations presented herein are subject to the
limitations noted at the end of this report

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lavas from two
shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be
middle to late Pliocene in age and Koolau is estimated to be late Pliocene to early Pleistocene
in age When the building of the Waianae and Koolau volcanoes came to an end only a slight
embayment existed on the southern coast of Oahu
During the Pleistocene Epoch Ice Age sea levels fluctuated in response to the cycles
of continental glaciation As the glaciers grew and advanced less water was available to fill
the oceanic basins such that sea levels fell below the present stands of the sea When the
glaciers melted and receded an excess of water became available such that the sea levels
rose to above the present sea level
The higher sea level stands caused the formation of deltas and fans of accumulated
terrigenous sediments in the heads of old bays and lagoonal marine sediments in the quiet
waters protected by fringing reefs The lower sea stands caused streams to carve valleys
through the sediments Subaerial exposure of the sediments caused consolidation of the soft
deltaic materials and lagoonal deposits Land development and reclamation projects within
the last century have brought the project site to its present form

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field exploration the project site is generally underlain by very stiff to hard
clayey soils extending to the maximum depth explored of about 21 5 feet below the existing
GEOLABS INC
W O 4549 00 Page 7
ground surface Based on the subsoil conditions encountered at the project site we believe
that shallow foundations consisting of spread and or continuous footings bearing on the
recompacted in situ stiff soils or engineered fill materials may be used to support the proposed
new chapel building
Based on our laboratory tests the near surface clayey soils exhibit moderately high
shrink swell potential when subjected to fluctuations in the soil moisture contents To reduce
the potential for distress to the future lightly loaded slabs on grade resulting from the swelling
of the near surface soils we recommend that the on site clayey soils be capped with a
minimum of 18 inches of non expansive select granular fill capping fill material below all slab
areas In general the subgrade soils should be compacted to provide a relatively firm and
smooth bearing surface prior to the placement of fill material Use of the vibratory action of
a compactor should be carefully controlled to avoid inducting a pumping subgrade condition
The slab subgrade should be kept moist and free of shrinkage cracks prior to placement of
capping fill cushion fill and or slab concrete Our geotechnical engineering recommendations
pertaining to the design of foundations retaining walls site preparation and pavements are
further discussed in the following sections of this report
Building Foundations
Based on the generally stiff subsoil conditions encountered at the site we believe that
shallow spread and or continuous footings may be used for support of the proposed new
construction An allowable bearing pressure of up to 3 000 pounds per square foot psf may
be used for the design of shallow foundations bearing on the recompacted stiff clayey soils or
engineered fills needed to achieve the final design grades This bearing value is for
caused by wind or seismic forces
For footings bearing on the in situ stiff clayey soils or compacted fill material the bottom
of footing excavations should be recompacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
prior to placement of reinforcing steel and concrete If soft and or loose materials are
GEOLABS INC
W O 4549 00 Page 8
encountered at the bottom of footing excavations the material should be over excavated to
expose the underlying firm materials The resulting over excavation should be backfilled with
select granular materials compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction In lieu
of backfilling the over excavation with compacted granular fill the footing bottom may be
extended down to the underlying competent material
It should be emphasized that if the footing subgrades are saturated or softened after
the visual observations probing or compaction testing due to inclement weather the footing
subgrades may need to be over excavated to expose firm ground and backfilled with
compacted fill As an alternative to backfilling with compacted fill the bottom of footing may
be extended deeper to bear on the firm over excavated subgrade
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by frictional resistance developed
between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil and by passive earth pressure
acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system A coefficient of friction of 0 3
may be used for footings bearing on the in situ stiff soils or engineered fills Resistance due
to passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 pounds
per square foot per foot of depth pcf This assumes that the soil around footings is
well compacted The passive pressure should be reduced for foundations located on slopes
Unless covered by pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the upper 12 inches of soil
should be neglected
In general footings should be embedded a minimum of 24 inches below the lowest
adjacent finish grades For footings constructed near tops of slopes or on sloping ground the
footings should be embedded deep enough to provide a minimum horizontal set back distance
of 8 feet measured from the outside edge of the footings to the slope face Footings located
adjacent to planned retaining walls should be embedded deep enough to avoid surcharging
the retaining wall foundations or the retaining walls should be designed to resist the additional
structural loads

W O 4549 00 Page 9
If foundations are located next to utility trenches or easements they should be
embedded below a 1H 1V imaginary plane extending upward from the bottom edge of the
utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility lines This requirement is necessary to
avoid surcharging adjacent below grade structures with additional structural loads and to
reduce the potential for foundation settlement
We estimate that footing settlements under the anticipated design loads for footings
bearing on the recompacted in situ stiff soils or engineered fills as recommended herein to be
less than 1 inch total with differential settlements on the order of 0 5 inches
We recommend that footing excavations be observed by a representative from Geolabs

conditions and the required embedment depths
Slabs On Grade
Based on our field exploration the project site is generally underlain by near surface
clayey soils with moderately high expansion potential when subjected to moisture fluctuations
To reduce the potential for future distress to the lightly loaded slabs on grade resulting from
shrinking and swelling of the clayey soils due to changes in the moisture content we
recommend that the subgrade soils be properly prepared and capped with at least 18 inches
of non expansive select granular fill capping fill material below the slab cushion The capping
fill should also extend beyond the new slab on grade footprint a minimum of 24 inches
The non expansive select granular capping fill should be placed as soon as possible
after the subgrade soils are prepared in accordance with the recommendations presented in
the Site Preparation section of this report The prepared capping fill and the underlying
subgrade soils should be wetted and kept moist prior to the final placement of concrete
Where shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of the subgrade we recommend that the
soils be thoroughly moistened to close the cracks Saturation and subsequent yielding of the
exposed subgrade due to inclement weather and poor drainage may require over excavation
of the soft areas and replacement with well compacted engineered fill
GEOLABS INC
W O 4549 00 Page 10
For interior building slabs not subjected to vehicular traffic we recommend that a
minimum 4 inch thick layer of cushion fill consisting of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67
gradation be placed below the slabs to serve as a capillary break and to provide more uniform
support of the slabs To reduce moisture infiltration and subsequent damage to floor
coverings an impervious moisture vapor barrier such as a_ plastic membrane is
recommended on top of the No 3B Fine gravel Two inches of moist fine sand may be
considered above the plastic membrane to provide protection of the membrane and to aid in
curing of the slab concrete Provisions of the 2 inches of moist fine sand above the vapor
membrane will be left up to the discretion of the project structural engineer Flexible floor
coverings such as carpet or sheet vinyl should be considered since they can better mask
minor slab cracking
Based on the preliminary plan provided we understand that four mechanical equipment
pads are also planned to serve the new chapel building Since the concrete slab on grade of
the mechanical equipment pads will be subjected to equipment operation a 6 inch layer of
base course is recommended in lieu of the normal gravel cushion and vapor barrier between
the slab concrete and the underlying capping fill material The base course should consist of
crushed basalt aggregate compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction For
the design of structural slabs a modulus of subgrade reaction of 250 pounds per cubic inch
pci may be used for the compacted base course
Exterior concrete walkways may be required for the proposed project We recommend
that concrete walkways be supported on 12 inches of non expansive select granular fill or
select borrow subbase The select granular fill or select borrow subbase should be compacted
to at least 90 percent relative compaction Control joints should be provided at intervals equal
to the width of the walkways with expansion joints at right angle intersections
The thickened edges of slab adjacent to unpaved areas should be embedded at
least 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade Areas adjacent to the slab should
be backfilled tightly against the slab with on site soil and graded to divert water away from the
slab to reduce the potential for water ponding around the foundations

Site Preparation
Based on the existing topography and the anticipated design finish floor elevation
we envision that the site grading work for the proposed project will generally consist of
minor fills of less than about 2 feet thick and cuts of up to about 5 feet deep
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared Debris rubbish and other unsuitable materials should be removed and
disposed of off site Soft and yielding areas encountered during clearing below areas
designated to receive fill or future improvements should be over excavated to expose firm
material and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with well compacted engineered
fill The excavated soft and or organic soils should be properly disposed of off site
Contract documents should include additive and deductive unit prices for over excavation
and engineered fill placement to account for variations in the over excavation quantities
Slabs and foundations of the existing structures and pavements to be demolished
should be completely removed Existing utilities to be abandoned should be removed and the
resulting excavation should be properly backfilled with select granular fill material
moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture content placed in 6 to 8 inch level loose lifts
and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Utilities to be abandoned in
place under the proposed structure should be backfilled by pumping lean concrete under low
pressure
After clearing and demolition the areas within the future slab on grade and pavement
limits should be scarified to a depth of about 8 inches moisture conditioned to at least
2 percent above the optimum moisture content and recompacted to a minimum of 90 and
95 percent relative compaction respectively Relative compaction refers to the in place dry
density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil

established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 91 test procedures Optimum moisture is the
water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
If the subgrade soils are pumping during subgrade preparation the pumping subgrade
may be stabilized by cement treatment As a guide one sack of cement may be used for
approximately 25 square feet of subgrade area Alternatively the subgrade may be
proof rolled and a woven geotextile fabric such as Mirafi 500X or equivalent should be placed
on the subgrade prior to placement of subsequent lifts of fill
The on site clayey soils generated from the cut areas may be reused as a source of
general fill material provided that they are free of vegetation deleterious materials and rock
fragments greater than 6 inches in maximum dimension The 18 inch non expansive capping

be well graded from coarse to fine with no particles larger than 3 inches in largest dimension
and should contain between 10 and 30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve The
material should have a laboratory CBR value of 20 or more and should have a maximum swell
of less than 1 percent

Drainage
The finished grades outside the building should be sloped to shed water away from

drains to reduce the potential for water infiltration into the subsoils Drainage swales should
be provided as soon as possible and maintained to drain surface run off away from the slabs

excavations should be properly backfilled against the footings immediately after setting of the

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling
seven borings to depths ranging from about 6 5 to 21 5 feet below the existing ground surface
Two bulk samples designated as Bulk 1 and Bulk 2 of the near surface materials were
obtained to evaluate the pavement support characteristics of the near surface soils The
approximate locations of the drilled borings and bulk samples are shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2 The borings were drilled using truck mounted drilling equipment equipped with
continuous flight augers
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by testing in
the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil Classification
System as shown on Plate A Graphic representations of the materials encountered are
provided on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 7
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 3550 84 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 3 inch
OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches In addition
some samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with ASTM
Test Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving
a 2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same hammer and drop The blow
counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an 18 inch drive are
shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample
depths

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content ASTM D 2216 and unit weight ASTM D 2937 determinations

Borings at the appropriate sample depth Graphic presentations of the test results are
provided on Plate B 2

selected samples of the near surface clayey soils to evaluate the pavement support
characteristics of the soils The test results are presented on Plates B 3 and B 4
Two Modified Proctor compaction tests ASTM D 1557 were performed on bulk
samples of the near surface soils to evaluate the dry density and moisture content
relationships The test results are presented on Plates B 5 and B 6
