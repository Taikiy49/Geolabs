Dear Mr Prentice
In accordance with our fee proposal dated May 1 2001 we have recently
completed the installation of pile jacket instrumentation for the Admiral Clarey Bridge
project This preliminary report summarizes our work pertaining to the installation of pile
jacket instrumentation and initial instrumentation readings for the subject project
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Admiral Clarey Bridge is located within Pearl Harbor and crosses the harbor
from the Rainbow Marina Restaurant to Ford Island The general location and vicinity of
the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The bridge structure over
water is supported on 24 inch precast prestressed concrete piles The piles were
constructed with epoxy coated spirals and silica fume admixture Subsequent to
completion of the bridge spalling of several of the concrete piles was noted We
understand that the silica fume may be the cause of the spalling
The remedial work for the spalled piles includes the installation of a reinforced
concrete jacket on 21 existing piles Three of the concrete jackets will be instrumented to
monitor stresses in the jacket reinforcement caused by additional expansion of the original
pile concrete from the silica fume
INSTRUMENTATION INSTALLATION MONITORING PROGRAM
The instrumentation program consists of the installation of twenty four spot
weldable vibrating wire strain gages in the concrete jacket of three selected piles For
each pile there are two instrumented zones One zone is located near the water level and
the second zone is located above the mudline In each zone the installation of
three horizontal strain gages and one vertical strain gage were planned

INSTALLATION OF INSTRUMENTATION
The 24 spot weldable vibrating wire strain gages were supplied by Geokon Inc and
consisted of Model VK 4150 strain gages Subsequent to receiving the strain gages the
instruments were checked for damage that may have occurred during shipping Initial
readings were taken using a Geokon Model GK 403 readout unit and are presented on
Plate 6 The calibration data for the vibrating wire strain gages and the readout unit are
provided in the Appendix
On May 11 and strain gages were installed on supplemental
horizontal stirrups and six strain gages were installed on supplemental vertical bars The
supplemental stirrups and bars consisted of 5 and 8 epoxy coated bars respectively A
power grinder was used to grind down a flat smooth area on the stirrups and bars for
strain gage placement The strain gages were spot welded onto the prepared surface
After welding the gages onto the stirrups and bars a cyanoacrylate adhesive and
waterproofing compound were applied over the mounting tab areas for corrosion
protection In addition a cover plate was mounted over the strain gages and secured to
the stirrups and bars with plastic locking straps The cover plate reduces the potential for
damage to the gage and cable The cover plate was then covered with waterproofing
electrical tape and adhesive sealant Strain gage readings were taken subsequent to
installation of the gages onto the stirrups and bars The measured readings are presented
on Plate 6
Pile 12N 1 and 22 7 were selected for instrumentation installation within
the concrete pile jacket The pile jacket instrumentation plan for these piles is shown on
Plates 2 through 4 The date of installation of the gages to the pile jacket and the date of
concrete placement for the jacket for the above piles are summarized on Plate 5
For Pile No 7 1 vertical strain gage SGV 1 was damaged during concrete form
removal The cable for gage SGV 1 was spliced and repaired on using
a 3M cable splice kit
Horizontal strain gages SGH 10 and SGH 12 for Pile No 12N 1 may have been
damaged during assembly of the reinforcing steel for the pile jacket A large change in the
reading was measured after the assembly of the reinforcing cage
For Pile No 22 7 horizontal strain gage SGH 13 appears to have been
over stressed and damaged during storage after installing the gage onto the supplemental
stirrup Strain gage SGH 18 was used to replace gage SGH 13 Therefore only two
horizontal strain gages SGH 16 and SGH 17 were used in Zone 2 During installation of
