SOIL ENGINEERING REPORT
LOT 40 ROYAL SUMMIT SUBDIVISION PHASE III
In accordance with your request we have made a study of the near
surface soil conditions at the above referenced lot in order to provide
foundation recommendations for the proposed residence The study
consisted of drilling three shallow borings testing the swelling
potential of the soils in our laboratory and reviewing the subdivision
site grading and the preliminary house foundation plans This report
summarizes our findings and recommendations
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
The proposed residence will be a two story wood framed structure
approximately 40 by 80 feet in plan dimension with a two car garage
Concrete slab on grade is planned for the driveway garage and the
entire front lower floor level of the house

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The stiff to very stiff reddish brown clayey silt to silty clay
encountered can generally provide adequate foundation support for the
proposed house provided that some care is exercised during construction
to reduce the swelling potential of the soil
Laboratory tests performed on the stiff to very stiff silty clay
soil indicate that this material generally has moderate to high swelling
potential depending on the initial moisture content prior to subsequent
saturation The results of the laboratory one inch ring swell tests are
summarized on Plate B 1 Therefore to reduce effects due to soil
swelling under on grade slabs we recommend that the on site soil be
capped with at least 24 inches of non expansive select off site fill
and that the subgrade soils underneath the select fill and slab be
moistened and protected from drying from the start of construction until
the slab concrete placement
House Foundation
In order to reduce the expansive effects of the soils we recommend
that a minimm 2 foot capping layer of non expansive material be placed
below the slab on grade foundations

The expansive clay below and for a horizontal distance of 3 feet

A 4 inch cushion layer of 3B Fine gravel should be provided below
the slab In order to protect against moisture infiltration a vapor
barrier may be incorporated below the slab

Site Grading
Fills and backfills within 2 feet of the finished grade should be
select non expansive material generally less than 3 inches in size
placed in maximum 8 inch loose lifts and compacted to 90 of its maximum
dry density established by ASTM D 1557 78 The on site clay soil should
not be used for fill material below structures
Prior to filling the area should be cleared of vegetation and
debris

Drainage
The finished lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings
should be shaped to shed water away from foundations to avoid ponding
condition Also it is advised that gutter systems be installed around
d that gutter water be diverted far away from the
foundations and walkways Excessive landscape watering near the
foundations and slab should also be avoided
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper
performance of the above recommended foundations since ponded water
could cause excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving
or loss of strength The foundation excavations should be properly
backfilled tight against the slab footing immediately after setting of
the concrete to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained
to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundation
Design Review
Plans and specifications for foundation site grading and utility
construction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and
written comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included

earthwork construction to monitor the contractor s grading operations
particularly fill placement

The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are based

Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encountered and cannot be
fully determined by reconnaissance and borings These unforeseen

costs
