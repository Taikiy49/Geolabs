As requested we have made a study of the near surface soil conditions
beneath the above referenced lot The purpose of the study was to
evaluate the suitability of the near surface soils as foundation
support for the proposed structure The study consisted of site
reconnaissance three exploratory borings within the proposed building
site and laboratory testing of selected samples This report sum
Marizes our observations and recommendations

preliminary plans provided to us the house will be set back about 12
feet from Nu Place

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
A total of three exploratory borings from 6 5 to 11 0 feet deep were
drilled through the near surface soils to investigate the subsurface
conditions at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate
1
The field exploration disclosed that the lot is underlain with about 6
to 12 inches of undesirable fill consisting of boulders decaying
wood large roots and other organic matter Directly beneath this
layer to the full depth of our borings is a brownish gray clay with
some boulders
Based on the laboratory test results this soil can be classified as a
fat clay CH according to the Unified Soil Classification System
Laboratory one inch ring swell test indicate that the soil generally
has a very high shrink swell potential
A detailed description of the soils encountered and laboratory testing
are presented in the appendix of this report

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that the site is underlain by stiff
expansive clay soils which shrink and swell with moisture changes In
addition the existing topography of the site provides poor drainage
Therefore the site grading slab on grade and house foundations would
require special design considerations and procedures in order to mini
mize the effects of these expansive subsoils
House Foundations
One method to minimize the shrink swell effects of the clay soils
would be to raise the area below the house and for a distance of 10
feet beyond the house perimeter about 3 feet to provide a non
expansive capping layer select borrow over the expansive clay This
would also provide better surface drainage away from the house and
reduce possible water infiltration under the foundations House
foundations and slab on grade could then be utilized on the 3 foot
select borrow capping layer
The fill should consist of non expansive select material placed in
8 inch loose lifts and compacted at optimum moisture content to a
minimum of 95 of its maximum dry density as established by ASTM
D 1557 78 With this 3 foot capping fill method the continuous wall
footings may be embedded a minimum of 12 inches below the finished
ground surface

W O 1185 00 Page 5
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f can be used for footings
bearing on the compacted select material
Slab on grade should be placed over a 4 inch layer of base course
compacted to 95 of its maximum dry density To protect against
moisture damage of the flooring material a polyethylene moisture
barrier should be considered
Alternate House Foundation
Alternatively a thinner capping layer in conjunction with a special
foundation design could be used
A minimum of 16 inches of select non expansive soil should be placed
below the slab on grade and 4 inch cushion layer Prior to placing
the fill hydrated lime approximately ft should be
applied over the expansive subgrade soils The subgrade should then
be saturated for about 2 or 3 days prior to concrete placement
In order to reduce the expansive effects of the existing clay soils on
the footings we recommend that the expansive clays be overexcavated
down for a depth of 2 feet below the bottom of the footing Hydrated
lime should be applied to the bottom of the footing excavation
Approximately 50 pounds of lime for every 15 lineal feet of excavation
is recommended

The excavation should then be moistened and filled with select non
expansive soil compacted to 95 of its maximum dry density The wall
footings can bear on the compacted fill using an allowable bearing
value of 2500 p s f
To provide drainage and to protect foundations from runoff infiltra
tion exterior wall foundations should be backfilled or capped with 2
feet of select non expansive material above the top of footing The
capping material should extend a minimum of 2 feet away from the wall
and should be shaped to slope away from the foundation A sketch of
the recommended foundation system is shown on Plate 2
The bottom of all footings should be free of loose soils and inspected
by the soils engineer prior to concrete placement
Lateral Loads
Resistance to lateral loads for the two story residence can be obtained
by passive pressure and frictional resistance
A frictional coefficient of 0 3 may be used between the bottom of
footing and compacted select fill In addition an equivalent fluid
pressure of 250 p c f may be used to determine the passive resistance

Concrete Driveway and Sidewalks
For concrete driveway and sidewalk recommendations provided for
the 3 foot capping layer for the concrete slab on grade should be
followed However since the driveways and sidewalks could tolerate
some amount of swelling the non expansive capping thickness could be
reduced to 2 feet All subgrade should be kept moist prior to concrete
placement
Site Grading
The existing terrain at the site generally slopes down at about 9 1
H V grade Prior to construction the area should be cleared of
vegetation and debris
Fills and backfills should be select non expansive material generally
less than 6 inches in size placed in maximum 6 inch loose lifts and
compacted to 95 of its maximum dry density established by ASTM
D 1557 78 The on site clay soil should not be used for fill material
below structures and behind retaining walls
Some amount of boulders which were encountered during our investigation
should be anticipated during construction
Cut and fill slope ratios of 2 1 H V are recommended

The finished grade outside the slabs and pavements should be shaped to
shed water away from the foundations and to avoid ponding condition

Design Review
Plans for site grading should be forwarded to the soils engineer for
review and written comments prior to construction to see that the
intent of our recommendations is included in the project plans and
specifications
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the filling operations be observed by the soils
engineer during construction This is to see that adequate compaction
has been obtained and the subsurface conditions are consistent with
the findings of our subsurface investigation The recommendations
given in this report are contingent upon the above inspections
