DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Bridge Foundation
Our subsurface exploration generally encountered a 18 0 to 29 0 foot
thick crust of medium dense to dense silty sand and cemented sand deposits
over very soft loose compressible soils We believe the upper sand and
cemented sand crust can provide adequate foundation bearing support for
the bridge abutments utilizing shallow spread or continuous wall footings
provided some densification of the near surface soils is achieved
Water table was encountered at about 2 5 and 5 0 feet depths which
corresponds to an elevation of about 1 5 and 0 feet respectively above
Mean Sea level MSL We anticipate that the normal water level may be
encountered up to about elevation 2 feet above MSL
The bottoms of the footing excavations should be recompacted to a
minimm of 95 of its maximm dry density established by ASTM D 1557 78 in
order to attempt to densify the soils directly below the footing
The footings should be setback from the top of the stream bank Slope
so the bottom of the footings are below a 1 5 horizontal to 1 vertical
imaginary plane extending upward from the bottom of the stream
slope
An allowable bearing value of 2 500 p s f may be used for the
footing design Estimated settlements for the relatively low bridge loads
10 kips per lin ft are on the order of less than three fourths 3 4
of an inch total

Retaining Wall
Granular backfill material such as select borrow or sand should be
used behind the abutments Weepholes or subdrains should be provided to
reduce the build up of hydrostatic pressure behind the walls
The recommended lateral earth pressures are as follows
Walls unrestrained at top 35 p c f equivalent fluid pressure
active case
Wall restrained at top 50 p c f equivalent fluid pressure
at rest case
Additional load due to traffic or surcharge should be included where
applicable
A coefficient of friction of 0 4 may be used between the sandy soil
and the bottom of the footing Passive soil resistance should be
neglected on the stream side of the footing
Erosion Protection
The bridge footings will be constructed at a low elevation which is
susceptible to periodic flooding Therefore it is our opinion that some
amount of erosion protection should be provided
A 4 inch thick concrete lining reinforced with welded wire fabric
installed in areas susceptible to periodic flooding could provide
resistance to scouring A 6 inch layer of base course is recommended
below the lining to provide a filter layer between the lining and
underlying soil
The lining should also incorporate cut off walls along the ends in
order to minimize scouring below the lining The cut off walls should
extend a minimum of 3 feet below the water level and ground surface

Site Grading
Minor grading work is anticipated
Site Preparation
Prior to placing the compacted fill the site should first be cleared
of vegetation rubbish debris and other unsuitable materials
Clearing and grubbing should consist of removing brush and trees
above the ground surface Roots larger than about 1 inch should also
be removed
Fill Material
Excavated on site material free of any organic and other expansive
component may be used as a source of fill material All imported
fill material should consist of select non expansive silty sandy or
granular materials less than 3 inches in size
Fill Placement
All fill materials should be placed in maximum of 10 inch horizontal
loose lifts and compacted to a minimm of 90 of the specified
maximum density
Design Review
Plans and specifications for foundations site grading and pavement
design should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written
comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the
sitework excavation and foundation construction phases of the work This
is to observe compliance with the design concepts specifications or
recommendations and to allow design changes in the event that subsurface
conditions differ from that anticipated prior to start of construction
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon such
observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed soil conditions encountered during construction are
different from those assumed or considered in this report then
appropriate modifications to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as borings
and samples Variations of condition between the field data points may
occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become
evident until construction If variations then appear evident it will be
necessary to re evaluate the recommendations given in this report
The locations of the field data points were approximately determined
by taping from reference points along property boundaries shown on the
topographic plan by Wilson Okamoto Associates Elevations of the
borings were estimated from the same plan The physical locations of the
field data points should be considered accurate only to the degree implied
by the method used

Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and
probing two 2 borings to depths of 35 to 38 feet below the ground
surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 A
truck mounted drill rig was used to drill the borings
Soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving a 2 inch
standard penetration sampler or a 2 4 inch I D split barrel sampler with
a 140 pound hammer free falling a distance of 30 inches The blow counts
to drive the sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the boring logs at
the appropriate sample depths
Materials encountered in the field explorations were classified by
visual and textural examination in the field by our engineers The
classifications were further reviewed by visual inspection and testing in
the laboratory All soils were classified in general conformance with the
Unified Soil Classification System A graphic presentation of the
materials encountered is presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 1 to
A 2 1
