SUMMARY OF RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration encountered a surfacial
layer of stiff clay over basalt near Stillman
Iane and alluvium deposits near stream at the
lot Laboratory testing and previous experience
have demonstrated that the clay has high swelling
potential when saturated from a dry condition
Special foundation and retaining wall design are
thus recommended to reduce the swelling effects
of the expansive soil
To utilize a slab on grade foundation it is
recommended that the expansive clay material be
removed a minimum of 3 feet below the concrete
slab foundation or down to the underlying
alluvium and replaced with non expansive soil
To utilize a spread or continuous footing
foundation it is recommended that the clay be
over excavated 3 feet below the bottom of the
individual footings or to the in situ alluviun
The over excavation should then be replaced with
compacted non expansive borrow soils for
foundation support
The text of this report should be referred to for
detailed and special design recommendations

performed for the proposed residence at 1645 Stillman Lane on the island

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to demolish the existing house and then replace it
with a new 3 story residence Foundation loads are anticipated to be
moderate to light and are typical for residential structures
Based on the preliminary plans supplied to us site grading
involving cuts and fills up to about 13 feet is anticipated Retaining
walls are planned for grade separation
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our evaluation was to explore the subsurface
conditions at the site to provide soil data for the foundation design and
site grading of the proposed residence The recommendations are based on
the soil information obtained from borings drilled for this exploration
Our scope of work included drilling and sampling three 3 borings
to depths ranging from 12 7 to 27 5 feet below the existing ground
surface The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests
which were utilized in the engineering analyses to formulate our
recommendations are presented in the appendices of this report
EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is located in the Nuuanu Area of Honolulu Hawaii
This approximately rectangular shaped 5 392 square foot lot is situated
between Stillman Lane to the west and Puehuehu Stream to the east

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
Geologically the site is located on the lower reaches of the
junction of Nuuanu and Pauoa Valleys which were formed by the deep
erosion of the Koolau Ridge The period of erosion was followed by
valley filling with alluvial deposits then covered by the Honolulu
Volcanic Series whose episodes were spread out over a considerable period
of time
Soil Conditions
The field exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling
three 3 borings to depths of 12 7 to 27 5 feet below the ground
surface The borings were drilled using portable hand carried drilling
equipment The locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration disclosed that the lot is underlain by
brownish gray expansive clay The laboratory test results and previous
experience indicate that this material is highly expansive Therefore
the house foundation will require special design and construction
procedures in order to reduce the shrink swell effect of the clay
subsoils
Foundation
Spread or continuous footing foundation may be used to support the
proposed structure To reduce the effects of the near surface expansive
soil we recommend that the expansive clays be over excavated down to a
minimum depth of 3 feet below the bottom of the individual spread
footings or to the in situ alluvium deposits The footing excavations
should then be backfilled with non expansive borrow soils such as crusher
waste select borrow or coral The backfill should be compacted in
6 inch lifts to a minimm of 90 of its maximm ary density per ASTM
D 1557 78 For details refer to the over excavation method shown on the
Schematic Section on Plate 3
Alternatively the footings may be embedded a minimm of 3 feet or
bearing on the in situ alluvium below the lowest adjacent grade for
details refer to the Schematic Section on Plate 3
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f and a minimm footing
embedment of 12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade may be used for
foundation design
If a footing is located next to a utility line it should be
extended to the bottom of the utility trench to reduce footing settlement
due to settlement of the trench backfill

Surface water should be diverted away from the foundation This is
to avoid ponding of surface run off water that may affect the expansive
soils around the foundation
Slab On Grade
To reduce the swelling effects of the underlying expansive clay
soils we recommend that a minimm 3 foot thick capping layer of
non expansive select material be placed below the slab on grade
foundation
The expansive clay below the slab should be removed for a minimm
depth of three 3 feet below finished grade or until the in situ
alluvium deposits are encountered The removal should also extend beyond
the slab perimeter about 3 feet The excavation should then be replaced
with non expansive off site materials such as select borrow crusher
waste or coral compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximm dry density
ber ASIM D 1557 78 The underlying remaining clay should be moistened
and then maintained in a moist condition
A 4 inch layer of 3B Fine cushion fill should be provided below the
slab In order to protect against moisture infiltration a vapor barrier
may be considered below the slab
The thickened edge slab footings can bear on the compacted
non expansive fill The thickened edges should be embedded a minimum of
12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade for details refer to the
Schematic Section on Plate 4

Site Grading
Cuts and fills up to about 13 feet are planned for the Stillman Lane
side of the building to create a level area for slab on grade
construction
It is our understanding that old retaining walls may be present
inside the current house for grade separation The structural capacity
of these old walls are questionable and therefore should not be
depended on New walls are required for the development
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed Any unsuitable
materials such as old footings old retaining walls vegetation and
rubbish should be removed and disposed of property off site
GEOLABS HAWAII

After clearing and grubbing the areas to receive fill should be
scarified to a depth of at least 6 inches moisture conditioned and
recompacted to 90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78
Fills and backfills within 3 feet of the finished grade should
consist of selected non expansive material generally less than 6 inches
in size placed in maximum 8 inch loose lifts and compacted to 90 of its
maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78
The on site expansive clay may be used for landscaping but should
not be used for fill material below structures All imported fill
material should be non expansive and should be tested and approved by the
soil engineer prior to use in the site
The site should be graded to provide drainage of surface run off
away from all foundations
Drainage
The finished lot grade outside the Slab pavement and footings
should be shaped to shed water away from foundations and to avoid ponding
condition Also it is advised that a gutter system be installed around
the residence and that gutter water be diverted far away from the house
foundation and driveway Excessive landscape watering near foundations
should also be avoided
Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance
of the above recommended foundations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of
strength The foundation excavation should be properly backfilled tight
against the slab footing immediately after setting of the concrete to
prevent ponding condition

changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from that
anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in
this report are contingent upon such observations

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as
borings probes test pits and or cbservation wells Variations of
conditions between the field data points may occur and the nature and
extent of these variations may not become evident until construction If
variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations given in this report
The location of the field data points were determined by taping from
structures indicated on the topographic survey Elevations of the data
points were approximately determined by interpolation from elevation
points on the topographic survey map The physical location and
elevation of the field data points should be considered accurate only to
the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition
