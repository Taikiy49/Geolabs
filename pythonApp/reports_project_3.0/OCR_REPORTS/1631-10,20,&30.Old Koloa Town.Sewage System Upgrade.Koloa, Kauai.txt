AND TESTING
OLD KOLOA TOWN
In accordance with your request we have performed additional
drilling and injection well testing for the above referenced project
The approximate location of the project site and its vicinity is shown on
the Project Location Map Plate 1 Our work was performed in general
accordance with our proposals of 15th and November
17 1986
This report summarizes our findings and installation details of the
wells
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this study and testing was to assist in site
selection design and installation of the effluent disposal wells for the
proposed wastewater treatment plant

The scope of our exploration included
1 Installation of PVC pipe casing and performance of a twelve hour
water injection test in a previously drilled 167 feet deep
borehole B 2
2 Drilling installing of Pvc pipe casing and testing of three
additional injection wells The boreholes B 1 B 3 B 4 were
initially drilled to 80 foot depths
3 Deepening of the three previously installed wells B 1 B 3
B 4 to 150 feet and performance of a twelve hour minimum water
injection tests in the above modified and redeveloped wells
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration testing and
injection well designs are presented in the appendices of this report

Soil Rock Conditions
The permeability of basalt formations depends largely on the
thickness of the uncemented clinker layers porous weathered rock zones
and the vesicularity and fracture spacings of the rock mass
The test wells drilled at the site encountered extremely weathered
to fresh basaltic rock formations below the upper 12 to 15 feet layer of
alluvial brown clayey silt In general the lava formations encountered
at the site appeared to be permeable and capable of transmitting clear
water from injection wells
Substantially thicker clinker and fractured rock zones were
encountered in the injection well B 2 which are reflected by the
significantly higher injection rate 72 gpm as compared to the other
three wells 38 to 45 gpm
Groundwater was encountered during our field exploration In
general a high water table of approximately 12 feet and a low of 15 feet
below the existing ground surface was recorded in the 4 injection wells
Detailed boring logs of the soils and rocks encountered during
drilling of the wells are presented in Appendix A
Injection Well Installation
A total of four injection wells were installed In two of the four
installed in the upper 20 feet The lower 20 to 30 feet of those drilled
holes were cased with slotted 4 inch PVC pipes In the remaining two
wells B 2 and B 4 blank 4 inch PVC casing was installed in the upper
14 to 15 feet and slotted 4 inch PVC casing was used to case the holes
from this level to 30 foot deep The core drilled holes were left open
from 30 foot depth down to the bottom of the holes The four wells were
developed by airlifting using an air compressor

SUMMARY AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The permeability of the basaltic lava depends largely on the
presence of interstitial spaces in clinker layers vesicles lava tubes
fractured and porous weathered rock zones The four test wells drilled
at the site encountered unweathered to extremely weathered basaltic rock
formations underlying the upper 12 to 15 feet of alluvial clayey silt
In general the lava flows encountered are fairly permeable
Water injection tests carried out in each of the four installed
wells yielded injection rates of 38 gpm B B B 3
and 39 gpm B 4 Based on these test data it is our opinion that the
installed wells can satisfy the required injection rate of 83 gpm
average flow and a peak factor of 6 plus 100 back up under
the testing conditions summarized in Appendix B
The frequencies of clogging and rate of reduction in the injection
capacity of the wells will depend on the quality of the effluent
Typically effluents injected into disposal wells contain suspended
solids micro organisms and other matter which may reduce the potential
permeability of the receiving rock formation Here in Hawaii there have
been numerous failures of injection wells These failures have been
attributed to numerous causes e g lack of routine maintenance
accumulation of solids bulking and formation caving

In view of this past history of failures provisions should be made
in the design of the facility for a long term maintenance program and
built in capability for well redevelopment to extend the useful life of
the wells Provision should also be incorporated in the development
scheme for additional injection wells to be installed as the original
wells reach the end of their effective service life

Coservations and Recommendations
The test well Boring No 2 which was drilled at the site
encountered basaltic rock formations below the upper 14 foot
unconsolidated soil deposit In general lava rock formations can be
considered permeable and transmit clear water for injection wells
However the permeability also depends on the amount of loose clinker
rock layers lava tubes weathered rock zones and to some extent the
vesicularity and fracture pattern of the rock itself
The trial tests performed below the lower 116 5 foot depth yielded an
injection capacity of less than 10 gallons per minute GPM below the
70 5 foot depth less than 25 GPM and at 19 feet a slightly higher
capacity of about 56 GPM
Based on our percolation test results and visual inspection of rock
cores it is our opinion that underground disposal of treated effluent
could be accomplised at the site by utilizing a series of fairly shallow
wells to about 80 feet each We propose to case the upper 20 feet with
4 inch solid PVC pipe and then install slotted 4 inch screen from 20 to
30 feet Below this would be about a 4 inch open hole to 80 feet
Percolation testing should be performed at each well drilled to
determine the total injection capacity for the design of an adequate
injection well system
