INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical investigation
performed for the proposed dolphin and seawall repairs at Ferry Slips
A and B at Ford Island Pearl Harbor Oahu Hawaii The general
location of the site and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location
Map Plate 1
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of the investigation was to explore the subsurface soil
conditions at the site to provide soil data for the design of the new
structures
The scope of our investigation included field reconnaissance the
drilling of six 6 borings laboratory testing of selected samples
engineering analysis and report preparation

The results of our field investigation and laboratory tests which
we used in the engineering analysis to formulate our conclusions and
recommendations are presented in the appendices of this report
SITE CONDITIONS
Ferry Slips A and B are situated on the southeastern coast of
Ford Island The slips are adjacent to each other with Slip A being
north of Slip B
Ferry Slip A consists of steel sheet pile seawalls and dolphins
on beth sides of the ferry loading ramp The seawalls on the northern
side have deteriorated and numerous areas are reported to have corroded
completely through This is evidenced by settlement of the backfill
behind the walls caused by washing out of the lower backfill areas The
existing dolphins at the site consist of both sheet pile and timber
pile The timber pile dolphins are visibly deteriorated and presently
being replaced The sheet pile dolphins are also reported by PWC to
have extensive corrosion damage Soundings taken during the field
exploration indicate the depth of water in the Slip is about 12 5 to 17
feet
Ferry Slip B consists of steel sheet pile seawalls with a
concrete cap The subject Dolphin No 2 has a pentagon shape and is
located between the ramp and the western seawall The steel sheet piles
are reported to have extensive corrosion damage Soundings taken during
the field exploration indicate the depth of water in the slip is about
13 feet

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The project site is located on the eastern side of Ford Island in
Pearl Harbor on the South Central Coastal Plain of Oahu
A complex series of erosional and depositional events resulting
from glacio eustatic sea level changes and post erosional volcanic
activities during the Pleistocene resulted in the accummulation of
alluvial deposits in a lagoonal deltaic environment during higher stands
of the sea
Ford Island is a remnant of the hard reef rock that once covered a
larger portion of the embayment and subsequently was carved away in many
places by streams during lower stands of the sea
During the Recent epoch the sea has adjusted to its present level
and additional sediments have continued to accummulate along the lochs
and channels of the harbor Land developments in the past 40 to 60
years have added man made fills along the shoreline

feet Only Boring No 3 encountered stiff alluvial soils at about

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration disclosed that the project site is underlain
by loose lagoonal deposits predominantly clayey coral sand and gravel
to depths in excess of 85 feet from the ground surface Soft clays bay
mud sediments of 2 5 to 9 feet thick were encountered below the water
level in and 5S
Sheet Pile Seawall Ferry Slip A
The portion of the existing seawall to be replaced is approximately
78 feet long Based on the construction drawings for the slip Y D
Drawing No 672136 it appears that the seawall was constructed in 1955
and consisted of Z 32 steel sheet piles driven to a total depth of 40
feet to approximately Elevation 68 feet U S Navy Datum The
drawings indicate that the tieback anchor System consists of 4 Z 32
sheet piles 7 feet wide and 10 feet long extending from about
Elevation 104 5 to 94 5 feet The tie rods are spaced about 12 feet
apart
It is our understanding that the initial repair was to leave some
of the old sheet piles in place However the proposed cathodic
protection system recommended would require that the old sheet piles be
removed

Soundings in front of the existing sheet pile wall indicate a water
depth of about 13 feet The depth of water increases to about 17 5

pile wall is about 21 feet above the mudline with a slightly
sloping ground in front of the wall

Backfill
oe
Granular material such as coral sand and gravel should be
utilized for the backfill above water behind the sheet pile wall
The material should meet the requirements for GM GP or
GW classification according to the Unified Soil Classification
System The backfill should be compacted to a minimum of 90 of
the maximum dry density established by ASTM D 1557 78
Underwater fill should consist of clean gravelly material with less
than 10 fines passing the No 200 sieve and capable to attain a
stable structure with minimal compactive effort Granular
material such as 3B Fine ASTM C 33 67 is recommended for
underwater fill
During backfilling every effort should be made to avoid trapping
additional soft mud behind the sheet pile wall since it would
greatly increase the lateral pressures on the sheet pile wall
Anchor Design
The sheet pile anchorage system will develop its resistance through
passive pressure in the silty sandy backfill material In order
for the anchorage system to be effective the anchor must be
located a sufficient distance behind the wall such that the
resisting anchor passive soil wedge does not intersect the active
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1334 00 Page 9
soil wedge behind the wall Based on this requirement it appears
that the anchor system for the wall should be situated a minimum
distance of about 30 feet behind the wall
In order to develop a full passive soil resistance the area in
front of the anchor system must be properly compacted and not be
allowed to loosen Future excavation in front of the anchor should
be avoided Utility trench excavations in this area should be well
compacted
Assuming compacted granular soil is used around the anchor the
soil parameters ultimate values presented on Plate 5 may be used
for the anchor design
The construction drawings indicate that the existing sheet pile
anchor system is situated about 30 feet behind the wall The
passive soil wedge from the lower 4 feet of the sheet pile anchor
intersects the active soil wedge and therefore this portion
should be neglected when determining the anchor resistance
Preliminary calculations indicate that if the existing anchor
system is still intact it could provide sufficient resistance
depending on the actual depth of sheeting driven Therefore the
condition of the sheet pile anchor system should be checked if
practicable to evaluate whether the anchor could be used

Dolphin Replacement
The existing sheet pile Dolphin No 4 at Ferry Slip A is
approximately 8 by 9 feet in plan dimension Construction drawings Y
D Nos 672136 and 672138 indicate that the dolphin was constructed in
about 1955 and consisted of MZ 32 steel sheet Piles approximately 50
feet long and extended from Elevation 108 to 58 feet Navy datun
The existing sheet pile Dolphin No 2 at Ferry Slip B has a
pentagon shape with a maximum side dimension of 30 feet As built
construction drawings NAVFAC Drawing no 1192657 indicate that the
sheet pile dolphin was driven around a previous concrete pile dolphin in
about 1969 It is our understanding that the sheet piles were driven to
their full length which was 70 to 80 feet and extended downward from
Elevation 110 to 40 to 30 feet Navy datum
Due to space requirements and the proposed cathodic protection
System the existing sheet piles will have to be removed
It is unclear what type of backfill was used to fill the dolphins
however the drawings indicate that some amount of cobbles and boulders
might have been used Therefore if the existing sheet piles are
removed first allowing the backfill to spill out some amount of
difficulty may be encountered during driving of the new sheet pile
sections through the cobbles and boulders Where practicable the new
sheet piles should be driven first prior to removing the old sheet piles

Design Review
Plans and specifications for remedial constructions should be
forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written comments prior to
construction to see that the intent of our recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that all sheet pile installation deadman anchor
installation and backfilling should be observed by the soils engineer
during construction This is to see that adequate penetration and
preparation have been made and that the subsurface conditions are
consistent with the findings of our investigation If conditions
encountered during construction are different from those presented in
this report appropriate modifications to the design should be needed

Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the two sites were explored by
drilling four 4 land borings and two 2 offshore borings at the
approximate locations shown on the Site Plans Plates 2 and 3 A
truck mounted drilling rig was used to drill the land borings The
offshore borings were drilled by a trailer mounted rig placed on a raft
The soils encountered were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by one of our engineers who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory The soils
were classified in accordance with the Unified Soil Classification
System A graphic presentation of the materials encountered is
In the borings soil samples were obtained by driving a 2 inch
standard split spoon sampler or a 2 4 inch I D split barrel sampler
with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches In addition a 3 0 inch
0O D Shelby sampler was used The blow counts used to drive the
samplers for 12 inches are shown on the boring logs at the appropriate
sample depths

APPENDIX _ B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content and unit weight determinations were performed on
selected samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil
properties The results of these tests are presented on the boring logs
at the appropriate sample depths
To further identify soil characteristics 7 sieve analysis test were
performed The results are presented on Plates B 1 through B 5
Eight direct shear tests were performed on undisturbed samples to
evaluate the strength characteristics of underlying soils The test
Tesults are presented on Plates B 6 through B 13
Two consolidated undrained triaxial compression tests were performed
on select undisturbed samples to evaluate the strength characteristics
of the soil The test results are summarized on Plates B 14 and B 15
Two consolidation tests were performed on selected undisturbed
samples to evaluate the settlement characteristics of the soft
sediment The test results are presented on Plate B 16 and B 17
