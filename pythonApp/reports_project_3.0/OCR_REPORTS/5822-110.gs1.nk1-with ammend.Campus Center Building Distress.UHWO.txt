Dear Ms Hara
In accordance with our fee proposal dated this letter report
presents the results of the additional floor level survey performed for the Additional Floor
Level Survey Campus Center Building Distress project in the Ewa area on the Island of
Oahu Hawaii
This report summarizes our findings and presents our geotechnical engineering
recommendations resulting from the additional floor level survey performed for the project
The findings and recommendations presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at
the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The University of Hawaii West Oahu UHWO Campus covers approximately
103 5 acres and is south and east of Farrington Highway and west of North South Road
in the District of Ewa on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project and general vicinity are
shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The mass and site grading for the project were completed in August 2012 The
existing clayey subgrade soil was scarified and moisture conditioned to at least
2 percent above the optimum water content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent
compaction prior to fill placement A minimum of 24 inches of non expansive select
granular fill material was placed below the concrete slabs on grade
The Campus Center consists of an L shaped two story building with a high attic
located near the center portion of the campus as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The
building is supported on shallow spread and continuous footings and concrete
slabs on grade

Based on available drawings there are two 4 inch sewer lines that extend
beneath the Campus Center South Wing building from the west side of the building
One of the sewer lines is located near the center of the South Wing and the other sewer
line is located near the south end of the South Wing building In the southern portion of
the South Wing building runoff water from the roof is piped down using rain gutter pipe
to the existing ground surface At the existing ground surface the rain gutter pipe is
placed inside a collector pipe with gravel The collector pipe is connected to a drain
pipe that runs perpendicular with the building and drains into a ditch area about 50 feet
from the building
The Campus Center building was completed in 2012 The signs of distress were
noted in about 2016
Building distress was noted at the southern portion of the Campus Center South
Wing building Based on initial observations it was recommended to perform the
distress study in two phases The first phase would consist of reviewing project
drawings and performing a site reconnaissance and floor level survey The results of
the first phase are contained in our report entitled Campus Center Building Distress
Phase 1 Study University of Hawaii West Oahu Campus dated
Based on the results of the Phase 1 study we understand that it was
decided to extend the distress observation period We understand that performing an
additional floor level survey was desired at this time This report presents the results of
the additional floor level survey and compares the differences in floor level elevations
from the previous study The necessity of the Phase 2 program was also re evaluated
based on our findings
OBSERVED DISTRESSES
The initial reported distresses were located at the southern portion of the South
Wing of the Campus Center Building The observed distresses were generally located
above and on the sides of door openings along the corners and interior of the walls at the
ceiling corners and between the cabinet and upper baseboard The distresses were
noted on the first and second floor levels of the building We understand that some of the
office doors are difficult to close In addition distress was noted at the building exterior at
the southwest corner of the building
The observed distresses during the additional floor level survey consisted of
separations ranging in width from hairline to about 3 8 inch wide These separations were
observed primarily around door frames between adjoining gypsum boards ceiling
bulkheads and drywall column interfaces Hairline separations were noted in and between
CMU blocks near the southwest corner of the building at multiple locations Photographs
taken of the observed distresses are presented on Plates 5 1 through 5 30 and Plates 6 1
through 6 34 for the first and second floor levels respectively Pictures taken from the first
floor level survey were compared with pictures taken from this more recent study where
applicable The locations of the photographs are provided on the First Floor Level Survey
Plans and Second Floor Level Survey Plans Plates 3 1 through 3 4

FLOOR LEVEL SURVEY
The initial floor level survey was performed on March 22 2016 on the first and
second floor levels of the southern portion of the Campus Center South Wing building
The additional floor level survey was performed approximately 10 months later on
The floor level survey equipment consists of a pressurized hydrostatic
altimeter that utilizes a pressurized system that measures changes in elevation between a
determined datum zero location and a point of interest The zero location was selected
at Gridline 16B between Gridlines J and K where building distresses were not observed
The initial first floor level survey readings ranged from 0 5 to 1 7 inches The
0 5 inch readings were measured along Gridline N between Gridlines 12B and 13B The
1 7 inch readings were measured between Gridlines L and M and between Gridlines 11B
and 12B Our additional floor level survey measured readings ranging from 0 9 to
1 7 inches in approximately the same locations These results indicate that the
difference in floor level readings generally ranged from about 0 1 to 0 5 inch The results
of the initial first floor survey and additional first floor survey recently taken are presented
on Plates 3 1 and 3 3 respectively Cross sections of the initial first floor survey and
additional first floor survey are presented on the Plates 4 1 and 4 2
The initial second floor level survey readings ranged from 0 2 to 1 1 inches The
0 2 inch reading was measured at Gridline M between Gridlines 12B and 13B at the
southern corner of the building The 0 9 to 1 1 readings were measured between
Gridlines G and H and between Gridlines 11B and 13B along Gridline L between
Gridlines 14B and 15B and at Gridline M and Gridline 11B Our additional floor level
survey generally measured readings ranging from 0 7 to 0 8 inches The 0 7 readings
were measured between Gridlines M and N and between Gridlines 12B and 13B The
0 8 readings were measured between Gridlines G and H and between Gridlines 11B to
13B and along Gridline L between Gridlines 14B and 15B The difference in floor level
readings between the initial readings and the additional readings generally ranged from
about 0 2 to 0 5 inch The results of the initial second floor survey and additional second
floor survey are presented on Plates 3 2 and 3 4 respectively Cross sections of the initial
second floor survey and additional second floor survey are presented Plates 4 3 and 4 4
FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The results of the initial and additional floor level surveys performed indicates that
the difference in floor level readings generally ranged from about 0 2 to 0 5 inch for the
first and second floor levels of the building The difference in the majority of the readings
were small at less than 0 25 inch It should be noted that the equipment used for the floor
level surveys has an accuracy of about 0 25 inch
The separations above and on the sides of door openings along the corners and
interior of the walls at the ceiling corners and between the cabinet and upper baseboard
noted during our initial observations on March 22 2016 were about the same or slightly
