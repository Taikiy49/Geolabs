INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the Proposed 2 Story Commercial Building in the Moiliili district of
Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project
site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings from our field exploration and laboratory
testing and presents our design level geotechnical engineering recommendations
derived from our analyses for the proposed project These recommendations are
intended for design input only and may not be appropriate to form the basis for
construction cost estimates nor for construction bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed project site is located at 1106 Hoawa Lane in the Moiliili district
of Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii The subject lot is approximately square in
shape with an area of approximately 9 600 square feet The site is currently occupied
by a 2 story single family dwelling at the center and a 2 story apartment building at the
northeast corner of the lot It is proposed to demolish the existing structures and
construct a new 2 story commercial building with first level parking and second level
offices
Based on the information provided to us by the project structural engineer
maximum column loads are estimated to be on the order of 150 kips per column with
wall loads of approximately 6 kips per lineal foot Based on our discussions with the
project architect minimal site grading work is anticipated for the project construction
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3275 00 Page 3
Our previous experience in the area suggested that the site may be underlain
by a thin surficial fill crust over coral deposits at shallow depths Special attention was
given to the swelling potential of the near surface soils and the densities of the
underlying coral deposits
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions at the project site to develop an idealized soil data set for the formulation of
geotechnical engineering design recommendations pertinent to the design of the
foundation site preparation and pavement section for the proposed 2 story commercial
building project The work was done in general accordance with our fee proposal dated
April 5th Our scope of work included the following tasks and work efforts
1 Review of available soils and geologic information pertinent to the project
site and its vicinity
2 Mobilization and demobilization of a truck mounted drill rig portable
drilling equipment and operators to and from the project site
3 Drilling and sampling of four borings to depths ranging from about 19 5 to
21 5 feet below the existing ground surface for a total footage of
approximately 82 lineal feet of exploration
4 Coordination of the field exploration and logging of the drilled borings by
a field engineer geologist of our firm

exploration to evaluate the classification and engineering properties of the

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is a corner lot located at 1106 Hoawa Lane to the northwestern
side of the intersection of Hoawa Lane and Young Street in the Moiliili district of
Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii The subject lot is bounded by Young Street
to the south Hoawa Lane to the east a 2 story commercial building with elevated on
grade parking to the north and a single family residential lot to the west
GEOLABS HAWAII

The project site is approximately square in shape with an area of approximately
9 600 square feet and is bounded by a 4 foot high chain linked fence The center area
of the project site is currently occupied by a 2 story single family dwelling on post and
beam foundations The northeast corner of the lot is occupied by a 2 story apartment
building measuring 20 by 50 feet in plan dimensions of brick masonry and plastic
sheeting construction The topography of the project site is relatively level with a thin
cover of vegetation and loose gravel at the ground surface
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling four borings to depths
ranging from about 19 5 to 21 5 feet below the existing ground surface The
approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general our field exploration disclosed that the subject site is generally
underlain by a surface layer of fill material approximately 2 to 3 feet in thickness
consisting of very stiff clayey silt soils This was underlain by coralline deposits
consisting of dense cemented sand and sandy coral gravels extending to the maximum
depths explored
The groundwater levels in the borings were measured at different times during
our field exploration and were encountered at depths of approximately 10 to 11 feet
below the existing ground surface Groundwater levels are expected to vary with
seasonal rainfall and time of year

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Our field exploration indicates that the project site is generally underlain by a
relatively thin layer of fill material over dense coralline deposits extending to the
maximum depths explored Based on the subsoil conditions encountered at the project
site and the anticipated heavy structural loadings it is our opinion that shallow
foundations consisting of spread and or continuous footings extending through the
upper clayey soils and bearing on the underlying dense coralline deposits may be used
to support the proposed 2 story commercial building New footings to be located
adjacent to the existing building at the north side of the subject lot should extend
downward at least to the same depth as the existing building footings to avoid
surcharging the existing foundations
Laboratory tests performed on the near surface clayey soils indicate that the
material generally has moderate shrink swell potential when subjected to moisture
fluctuations Therefore we recommend that the on grade parking pavement section
for the subject project be supported on 6 inches of select granular fill material Special
provisions and construction procedures should also be observed and implemented
during the pavement subgrade preparation to reduce the potential effects of the
expansive surface soils Detailed recommendations are presented in the following
subsections
Foundations
Due to the moderately heavy structural loads anticipated for the subject project
shallow foundations consisting of spread and or continuous footings extending through
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3275 00 Page 7
the upper clayey soils and bearing on the underlying dense coralline deposits are
recommended to support the proposed 2 story commercial building
An allowable bearing pressure of up to 4 000 p s f may be used for the design
of footing foundations bearing on the dense coralline deposits encountered at shallow
depths This bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by one
third 4 for transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic forces
The footings should be embedded a minimum of 24 inches below the lowest
adjacent finish grade Depending on the depths of the dense coralline deposits deeper
excavations may be required at localized areas to provide a competent bearing surface
for the footing foundations Wall footings should have a minimum width of 24 inches
New footings to be located adjacent to the existing structure at the north side of
the subject lot should be lowered to the same depth as the existing building footings
or deeper to avoid surcharging the existing foundations The actual depths of the
existing footings should be confirmed in the field during construction by the project
geotechnical engineer
If foundations are located next to utility trenches or easements they should be
embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane extending
upward from the bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility
lines
Loose materials encountered at the footing subgrades resulting from the
excavation operations should be removed and the subgrades recompacted prior to the
placement of reinforcing steel or concrete
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3275 00 Page 8
We estimate the footing settlements under the anticipated design loads for
footings bearing on the dense coralline deposits to be less than 1 inch total and inch
differential
Lateral loads acting on the structures may be resisted by passive earth pressure
acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system and by frictional
resistance developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid
pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth p c f This assumes that
the soil around footings is well compacted Unless covered by pavements or slabs the
passive resistance in the upper 12 inches of soil should be neglected A coefficient of
friction of 0 35 may be used for foundations bearing on the dense in situ soils
We recommend that foundation excavations be observed by a representative of
Geolabs Hawaii prior to the placement of reinforcing steel or concrete to confirm
adequate foundation bearing conditions and the required footing embedment depths
Pavement Design
We understand that slab on grade parking is planned at the ground floor level
of the proposed project Our field exploration disclosed that the project site is underlain
by a surficial fill layer of clayey soils which are considered to be moderately expansive
when subjected to moisture fluctuations Unless concrete slabs supported by these
soils are properly designed there is a potential for future distress to the lightly loaded
slab on grade construction resulting from the shrinking and swelling of these clayey
soils due to changes in the moisture content To reduce the potential for structural
distress resulting from the swelling of the on site moderately expansive subgrade soils
we recommend that the rigid concrete pavement section be supported on 6 inches of
select granular fill material Special provisions and construction procedures should also
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3275 00 Page 9
be observed and implemented during the pavement subgrade preparation to reduce the
potential effects of the near surface expansive clayey soils This is further discussed
in details under the Site Preparation section of this report
We anticipate the vehicle loading for this project will consist primarily of
passenger vehicles and light pick up trucks We have made our preliminary pavement
design assuming the pavement subgrade soils will consist of 6 inches of select granular
fill underlain by the stiffidense on site soils Based on the above assumptions we
recommend the following pavement section be used for preliminary design purposes
Rigid Pavement
5 0 Inch Portland Cement Concrete
6 0 Inch Aggregate Base Course 95 Percent Relative Compaction
11 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness on 6 inches of Select Granular Fill
Over Moist Compacted Subgrade
The base course should consist of crushed basalt aggregate compacted to not
less than 95 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place
dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same
soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 91 test procedures CBR and
density tests should be performed on the actual subgrade soils encountered during
construction to confirm the adequacy of the above section Provisions should be made
for proper load transfer across the slab joints which are subjected to vehicular traffic
Paved areas should be sloped and drainage gradients maintained to carry all
surface water off the site Surface water ponding should not be allowed anywhere on
the site during or after construction Where concrete curbs are used to isolate
landscaping in or adjacent to the pavement areas we recommend the curbs extend a
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3275 00 Page 10
minimum of 2 inches into the subgrade soil below the base course aggregate to
reduce migration of landscape water into the pavement section Alternatively a
subdrain system could be constructed to collect excessive water from landscaping
irrigation For long term performance we recommend a subdrain system be
constructed adjacent to paved landscape areas
Exterior concrete walkways may be required for the proposed project We
recommend that a minimum 4 inch thick cushion layer of No 3B Fine gravel be
provided below the concrete slab Construction joints should be provided at intervals
equal to the width of the walkways with expansion joints at right angle intersections
Site Preparation
Based on the existing topography and the proposed finish floor elevation site
grading work for the subject project will mainly involve the over excavation of the
surface clayey soils and replacement with select granular fill material underneath the
pavement areas
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable
materials should be removed and disposed of off site
Slabs and foundations of the existing structures that are to be demolished should
be completely removed Any over excavation resulting from the demolition should be
backfilled with compacted granular fill material Existing utilities to be abandoned
should be removed and the resulting excavation should be properly backfilled with 6
to 8 inch thick horizontal lifts of compacted select granular fill material Utilities that are
to be abandoned in place under the proposed structure should be backfilled by
pumping lean concrete under low pressure
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3275 00 Page 11
After demolition clearing and grubbing al areas beneath future slabs and
pavement areas should be over excavated to a depth of at least 6 inches below the
bottom of the base course layer and replaced with select granular fill material Loose
and or soft spots encountered during the over excavation process should be removed
and replaced with compacted select granular fill material The clayey material resulting
from the over excavation should be removed and disposed of off site
The over excavated subgrade surface should be moisture conditioned to at least
2 percent above optimum moisture content and compacted to at least 90 percent
relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil
expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established
in accordance with ASTM D 1557 91 test procedures
Select granular fill material needed for the 6 inch capping layer should be
compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Select granular fill material
should consist of select non expansive granular material such as crushed coral basalt
gravel or cinder sand The material should be well graded from coarse to fine with no
particles greater than 3 inches in largest dimension The material should have a
laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR value of 20 or higher and a swell potential
of not more than 1 percent when tested in accordance with ASTM Test Designation
D 1883 It should contain less than 20 percent passing the No 200 sieve Any
imported fill material should be tested and approved by Geolabs Hawaii prior to delivery
to the project site for its intended use
We recommend that the project gectechnical engineer Geolabs Hawaii be
retained to provide the field observation and monitoring services during the site
preparation and fill placement at the subject project in order to observe compliance with
our recommendations and if necessary to provide additional consultation

Utility Trench
We envision that new utility lines may be required for the project A granular
bedding consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation
is recommended under the pipes Self compacting granular materials such as No 3B
Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation should also be used for the trench backfill
up to about one foot above the pipes to provide adequate support around the pipes and
to reduce the compaction effort of the backfill thus reducing the possibility of damaging
the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level one foot above the pipes
to the top of the subgrade or finished grade should consist of select granular material
The backfill should be placed in about 10 inch loose lifts and mechanically compacted
to not less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for future ground
subsidence Where trenches are below pavement areas the upper 2 feet of the trench
backfill below the pavement subgrade should be compacted to 95 percent relative
compaction
Drainage
The finished grades outside the building should be sloped to shed water away
from foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding Also it is advised that gutter
systems be installed around the building and that the discharge be diverted away from
the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near the found
ations and slabs should also be avoided Planters next to foundations should be
avoided or have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the
subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil
GEOLABS HAWAII

saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations
should be properly backfilled against the walls or slab footings immediately after setting
of the concrete to reduce water infiltration
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and should be
maintained to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundations

Preliminary and final drawings and specifications for the proposed 2 story

Any imported material required should be tested by Geolabs Hawaii for con

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part
upon information obtained from field borings Variations of subsoil conditions between
the borings may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become
evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be
necessary to re evaluate the recommendations provided in this report
The location of the field borings indicated in this report are approximate having
been estimated by taping from the features shown on the Ground Level Floor Plan
prepared by dated April 12th The physical locations of the borings
should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used
The stratification lines shown on graphic representations of the borings depict
the approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual
transition
Water level data from the borings were measured at the times given on the
graphic representations and or in the text of this report These data have been
reviewed and interpretations made in the formulation of this report However it must
be noted that fluctuation may occur due to variation in rainfall tides temperature and
other factors

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content and unit weight determinations were performed on selected soil
samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties The results of
