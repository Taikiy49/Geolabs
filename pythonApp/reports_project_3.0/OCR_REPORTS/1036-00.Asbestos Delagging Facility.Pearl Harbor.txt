Gentlemen
As requested we have performed a soil investigation at the
above referenced site to evaluate the subsurface soil condi
tions in order to provide recommendations for the design and
construction of the foundations of the proposed structure
Our investigation consisted of four borings drilled in the
proposed location and visual observations of the site
The results of our field exploration and laboratory testing
which were used in the engineering analyses to formulate our
conclusions and recommendations are presented inthe appendix
of this report

P 2
The project site is situated in the Pearl Harbor Naval
Shipyard near the repair basins between Pearl Harbor
Channel and the southeastern Loch
There is an existing concrete slab approximately 111 feet
by 220 feet in plan It is our understanding that the concrete
Slab was part of a one story mess hall building which had once
occupied the area The existing slab was covered with stock
piles of scrap metal and equipment
Around the site there are also asphaltic concrete pavement
and warehouse buildings
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed building will be a one story prefabricated struc
ture approximately 70 by 120 feet in plan dimension
The new building will be situated mostly over the eastern
portion of the existing concrete slab It is anticipated
that the existing concrete slab will be retained and over
laid with additional concrete
Individual column loads of about 11 kips are anticipated
for the structure CMU walls will be utilized around the
work area The anticipated wall loading is about 2 kips
per foot

GENERAL SOIL CONDITIONS
The existing stockpiles of scrap metal and equipment over
the site limited the accessibility for our drilling rig
Boring Nos 1 and 2

of the proposed building and the following conditions were
encountered

2 Base Course 13 to 16 inches thick coral gravel

from subgrade level to about 10 feet deep
4 Sand and gravel medium density from below the
tuff to the depth of the feet

2 Brown clayey silt with tuff fragments soft to

10 feet deep appears to be fill
3 Coral sand dense

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration encountered very different soil condi
tions along the eastern and western areas of the proposed
building
For the very light prefabricated structure anticipated spread
or continuous footing foundation can be used Special footing
excavation preparation will be required near the eastern portion
of the building where soft to medium soils were encountered
Foundations
Dense weathered tuff was encountered in this area B 1 and
B 2 Spread or continuous footing foundation bearing on
the dense tuff can be used An allowable bearing value of
4000 p s f can be utilized A minimum footing width of
18 inches is recommended
Footings should have a minimum embedment of 18 inches
below the finished grade and 4 inches into the dense tuff
Footing settlements are anticipated to be minimum for foun
dations bearing in the dense tuff

Surface water should be diverted away from building foun
dations
To resist lateral loads a coefficient of friction of
0 5 can be used along with a passive soil resistance of
350 p c f
Soft to medium soils were encountered in this area B 3
and B 4 therefore special footing preparation is
required to reduce footing settlement
The footing excavation should be overexcavated a minimum
of 12 inches below the design footing bottom The bottom
of the excavation should be tamped first and then backfilled
with the excavated material The backfill should be compacted
in 6 inch lifts to a minimum of 90 of the maximum density
determined by ASTM D 1557
An allowable bearing value of 2000 p s f can be used
on the above specific recompacted backfill
The footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches
below the finished grade
Estimated settlements for the above footings are on the
order of half an inch

To resist lateral loads a coefficient of friction of

footing is recompacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum

2 times the footing embedment then a passive soil

Based on the limited number of test borings drilled it

Some special provisions should be made in the contract

Concrete Slab On Grade
It is anticipated that the existing concrete slab will remain
as part of the new building
A 10 ton fork lift loading is anticipated
Where dense natural tuff formation is encountered the exis
ting pavement should provide adequate support
Where soft subgrade soils are encountered differential move
ment may occur These soft areas are not believed to be exten
sive however sufficient boring data is not available to
adequately delineate the extent of the affected area

The following alternatives are suggested
1 Delineate poor subgrade area by additional borings
2 Remove existing slab over the section which has poor
subgrade soil and then recompact the underlying loose
fill for a depth of about 2 feet below the slab New
slab for this area can be poured after the above sub
grade preparation
3 Utilize slab as is with provision for future repairs
If distress occurs later in a certain portion of the
slab replace distressed areas only
4 Overlay existing concrete slab with additional concrete
thickness to strengthen the existing slab
Design Review
The plans for foundations site grading and utility construc
tion should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review
and comments prior to construction to see that the intent
of our recommendations is included in the project plans
and specifications

and recompaction be observed by a soils engineer during construc
tion to see that adequate bearing has been obtained and that
the subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings
of our subsurface investigation

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored
by drilling 4 borings to depths of 10 to 16 5 feet below
the existing ground surface at the approximate locations
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 Truck mounted auger
drilling equipment was used to drill all of the borings
The soil encountered in the borings were classified
by visual and textural examination in the field by our
engineer who continuously monitored the drilling operations
The classifications were reviewed by visual inspection and
testing in the laboratory The soils were classified in
general conformance with the Unified Soil Classification
System A graphic presentation of the soils is presented
on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 4
Soil samples were obtained by driving a standard pene
tration sampler with a 140 pound hammer free falling a dis
tance of 30 inches The blow counts to drive the sampler
the last 12 inches are shown on the logs of borings at the
appropriate sample depth
