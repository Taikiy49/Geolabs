In accordance with Amendment 14 to the Revised Statement of
Architect Engineer Services for Soils Investigation Services Pacific
Division Naval Facilities Engineering Command we have performed field
and laboratory testing on the existing PCC pavement base course and
subbase course at the project site This report presents our test
results
PURPOSE AND SCOPE OF WORK
The purpose of our work was to provide field and laboratory data on
the condition of the existing PCC pavement base course and subbase course
layers at the above referenced site Our services included the following

to obtain 106 samples for testing the compressive strength of the

GENERAL SITE DESCRIPTION
The subject pavement areas are situated on the eastern side of
Taxiway 15 33 The test areas were separated into five 5 areas Nos 1
through 5 as shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
CONCRETE CORE SAMPLES
A total of 106 core samples were taken from the existing PCC pavement
at locations specified by PACDIV The locations of the core samples are
shown on Plates 2 and 3
The cores were drilled with a portable Mobile Minuteman drill rig
which was mobilized to Midway via MAC flight from Honolulu The cores
were obtained with 3 875 inch and 6 inch diameter diamond coring bits
It was originally planned to test the core samples on Midway
However due to mechanical breakdown of the concrete saw on the island
the core samples were sent back to Honolulu for preparation and testing
The cores were obtained and tested in accordance with ASTM Test Methods
C 42 and C 617 The results of the compressive strength tests are
presented on Plates 4 through 6

LABORATORY TESTING
Bulk samples of the typical material used for the base course and
subbase course layers below the existing pavement were obtained and
delivered to our office by the Midway
Two 2 sieve analysis ASTM D 1557 78 were performed on each of the
two 2 samples to determine the grain size distribution of the samples
The four 4 tests confirmed visual observations that the two 2
materials were very similar and the base course could not be distinguished
from the subbase course The results of the sieve analyses are presented
on Plates 7 through 9
Since the bulk samples were very similar the two 2 samples were
combined for the laboratory CBR tests ASTM D 1557 78 three 3
laboratory CBR tests each at a different moisture and density were
performed on the combined samples The results of the laboratory CBR
tests are presented on Plates 10 through 12
A proctor test ASTM D 1557 was also performed on the combined
sample to determine the maximm dry density and optimm moisture content
for reference with the field in place density tests The results of the
test are presented on Plate 13
FIELD DENSITY TESTING
Three 3 field density tests were performed on the exposed surface
of the base course subbase course adjacent to the existing pavement in
order to evaluate the compaction of the layer The tests were performed
by the sand cone method ASTM D 1556 The test results are presented on
Plate 14 and the locations of the tests are indicated on Plates 2 and 3
