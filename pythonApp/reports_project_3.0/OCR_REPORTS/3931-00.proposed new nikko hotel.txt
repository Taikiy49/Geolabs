Project Considerations
The project site is located at the existing Nikko Palau Hotel in Koror in the Republic
of Palau Based on the information provided we understand that it is desired to demolish
the existing hotel and construct a new 300 room plus hotel at the same site The new
hotel will generally consist of one to two story structures near the shoreline and up to
about five story high buildings near the existing hotel structure higher elevations
Supporting facilities such as pier structures access roads utilities and a new man made
beach are also planned to serve the new hotel
Based on our field reconnaissance the project area appears to consist of
mangrove swamp deposits along the shoreline and stiff saprolite in the upper slope area
The existing slopes are very steep including some areas with near vertical slopes Key
geotechnical engineering considerations for the proposed hotel development would

Regional Geology
The islands of Palau are a part of Micronesia located in the western portion of the
north Pacific Ocean It lies approximately 885 kilometers to the east of Mindanao
Philippines and about 1 300 kilometers to the southwest of Guam The Palau Islands
consist of a small elongated arc shaped island chain in the Western Caroline Islands
which trend generally north to south extending from Latitude 8 degrees 12 minutes North
to Latitude 6 degrees 53 minutes North and from Longitude 134 degrees 8 minutes East
to Longitude 134 degrees 44 minutes East The major islands comprising the Republic
of Palau generally trend in a north south direction and consist of Babeldaob Koror
Peleliu and Angaur
The Philippine Sea and the Pacific Ocean Basin are separated by a series of
elongated submarine ridge structures The Palau Islands are situated at the exposed
crest of the southwestern most submarine ridge of the area The Palau ridge is part of
the Palau Kyushu ridge which extends from Kyushu Japan southward towards Palau
where a complementary trench structure forms to the southeast of Palau These ridge
and trench structures constitute the Palau Arc The eastern slope of the Palau ridge
slopes steeply to the Palau trench which plunges to about 8 200 meters at about
48 kilometers east of the north end of the Palau Islands The west flank of the ridge
slopes gradually to a depth of about 5 500 meters at about 400 kilometers to the west
The andesite line which marks the margin of the western Pacific Basin has
been positioned along the eastern edge of the Palau trench This demarcation
separates the region of predominantly andesitic volcanism to the west from the Pacific
Basin region of basaltic volcanism The area is also a major structural and
physiographic boundary between the two regions
The structure of the Palau Islands reflects their situation as the exposed crest of
the Palau ridge The island s elongated form has resulted from volcanic emissions and
deposition of pyroclastic materials along the arcuate ridge structure Due to the varied
locations of the volcanic sources and the amount of emissions the successive
accumulation of pyroclastic materials was highly irregular in occurrence In addition
most of the eruptions were subaqueous resulting in submarine landslides and slumps
and contributing to the deformed and highly irregular character of the depositional
surfaces

Subsurface Conditions
The preliminary field exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling five
borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 5 extending to depths between 1 2 and
6 7 meters below the existing ground surface The approximate locations of the borings
are shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
It should be noted that a total of 4 borings each to a depth of about 9 to
12 meters below the existing ground surface were originally planned for the preliminary
study of this project However we understand that the drilled borings were generally
terminated at shallow depths and did not penetrate through the boulders and or rock
formation underlying the surficial soil deposits In addition off shore borings were not
performed along the proposed pier structure during this preliminary field exploration
program Therefore an additional field exploration program with deeper borings should
be conducted for detailed design of the proposed hotel development
In general the limited field exploration disclosed that the upper slope of the project
site is generally underlain by a surficial deposit of colluvium and or saprolite consisting of
stiff to very stiff clayey silts extending to the maximum depth explored of approximately
6 7 meters below the existing ground surface Hard ledges or boulders were encountered
within the soil horizon at various depths in some of the drilled borings performed at the
subject site All the borings were terminated upon encountering refusal to the hammer
and did not penetrate through the underlying boulders and or rock formation Due to the
relatively shallow borings drilled at the project site a detailed soil rock profile could not be
obtained to evaluate the subsurface conditions at greater depths However we believe
that the surficial colluvium and or saprolite soil is generally underlain by hard rock
formation at greater depths Soft mangrove swamp deposits are anticipated along the
shoreline area at the lower portion of the project site
Groundwater was not encountered in the shallow borings at the time of the field
exploration However it should be noted that groundwater levels are subject to change
due to rainfall time of year seasonal precipitation run off storm surge conditions and
other factors
Descriptions of the materials encountered from the limited field exploration are
presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 5 Laboratory testing was
performed on selected samples obtained and the test results are presented on
Plates B 1 through B 4

Discussion and Recommendations
Based on the limited information obtained from the preliminary field exploration
program we believe that the proposed development is feasible from a geotechnical
engineering point of view provided that the concerns and recommendations presented in
this letter report are addressed
Foundations
Based on the limited field exploration conducted for the proposed development we
anticipate that the project site is generally underlain by stiff colluvium and or saprolite soils
overlying hard rock formation at greater depths In general we believe that shallow
foundations consisting of spread and or continuous footings bearing on the stiff saprolite
soils may be used for support of the proposed five story buildings planned at higher
elevations An allowable bearing pressure on the order of about 120 to 150 kilopascals
kPa may be used for preliminary design of foundations bearing on the stiff saprolite
soils These bearing values are for dead plus live loads and may be increased by
one third for transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic forces
Based on the information provided we understand that one to two story structures
are planned along the shoreline area of the project site Depending on the final design
layout our field reconnaissance suggest that the proposed structures may be underlain
by soft mangrove swamp deposits observed along the lower grounds of the project site
Because of the soft soil deposits significant ground settlements may occur under the
future building loads Therefore special measures should be implemented to reduce the
potential for future building distress in these soft ground areas These measures may
include the implementation of a surcharge program at the building locations or the use of
a deep foundation support system for the proposed structures
Driven pile foundation is anticipated for the foundation support of the pier structure
planned for the proposed hotel development and structures located over water We
believe that off shore piles consisting of 500 mm octagonal or square precast
prestressed concrete piles may be used for foundation support of the proposed pier
structure An allowable compressive load capacity of may be used for
the preliminary design of the off shore pile foundation bearing on the hard rock formation
anticipated at greater depths Lengths of the driven piles are unknown at this time due to
the limited subsurface information however we speculate the depth to rock formation to
be about 30 meters below the water surface
Retaining Structures
Due to the existing steep terrain at the project site we anticipate that a significant
number of retaining structures will be required to provide grade separation along the
sloping grounds at the project site Consideration may be given to soil nailing and
tied back retaining wall systems in cut conditions to reduce construction time and cost

Earthwork
Due to the high in situ moisture contents of the on site soils substantial aeration of
the high moisture soils will be required in order to achieve the normal 90 percent
compaction requirement and may not be feasible due to the year round high rainfall
experienced at the project site As a result the compaction requirements for general fill
placement will have to be reduced in order to facilitate earthwork construction using the
high moisture tropical soils in this wet environment Based on our previous experience
with similar soils we believe that a compaction requirement of a minimum of 85 percent
relative compaction may be used for this project Depending on the layout of the
proposed development it should be noted that some imported soils will be necessary for
use as structural fill material below foundations and adjacent to retaining wall structures
Based on the subsurface conditions anticipated at the project site we believe that
a slope inclination of one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V may be used for the
preliminary design of the planned cut slopes We believe that a cut slope inclination

Since the project site is located in a high rainfall environment we wish to
emphasize that drainage features such as interceptor ditches subdrains road swales
and longitudinal edge drains are critical to the stability of the cut and fill slopes and
performance of the retaining and pavement structures Excessive surface run off over
the slope face may cause erosion of the exposed soils thus potentially jeopardizing the
long term stability and performance of the cut and fill slopes Accumulation of water
behind the retaining structures will result in the build up of hydrostatic pressures and
may cause structural distresses and potential instability Infiltration of water below the
pavement structural section will soften the silty and clayey subgrade soils and cause
premature failure of the pavement structure Therefore it is our opinion that special
attention should be given to the design and implementation of these drainage features
at the project site
In addition to the construction of earth berms interceptor ditches and slope
planting other erosion control measures such as conventional grouted rubble paving
and concrete lining may be considered for the subject project especially in localized
areas where concentration of surface water runoff is evident In addition flexible
geosynthetic matting may offer a viable solution for slope erosion control problems by
promoting mulching reducing run off velocity and establishing temporary and
permanent erosion control on the slope face

Limitations

based in part upon information obtained from limited field borings Variations of subsoil
conditions between the borings may occur and the nature and extent of these variations
may not become evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident
it will be necessary to reevaluate the recommendations provided in this report
