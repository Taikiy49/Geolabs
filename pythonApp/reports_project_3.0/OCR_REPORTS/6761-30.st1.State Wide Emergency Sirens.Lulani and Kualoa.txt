deflection at the top of shaft, and induced moment in the shaft. The lateral load analyses 
were performed using the program LPILE-plus for Windows. The following table

Drilled Shaft Construction Considerations 
The subsurface materials may consist of granular soils with little cohesion.  In 
addition, shallow groundwater conditions should be anticipated at the project sites.  
Therefore, caving-in and/or sloughing of these materials may occur during the drilling 
operations. To reduce the potential for caving-in of the drilled holes, temporary casing of 
the drilled holes likely be required during the drilled shaft installation and/or the use of 
drilling fluids (polymer slurry) may be necessary to maintain the integrity of the drilled hole 
during drilled shaft installation.  The contractor should select drilled shaft installation 
methods which will maintain the integrity of the surrounding pavements and shoreline 
revetments. 
Care should be exercised during removal of the temporary casing, if used, to 
reduce the potential for "necking" of the drilled shaft. Therefore, a minimum 5-foot head of 
concrete above the bottom of the casing or adequate concrete head to counter the 
hydrostatic pressures due to the shallow groundwater conditions should be maintained 
during removal of the casing. The shallow groundwater conditions at the project sites may 
pose construction difficulties because proper observation of the sides and bottoms of the 
drilled shaft may not be possible. 
Because of the shallow groundwater conditions anticipated within the depths of the 
drilled shaft excavations, concrete placement by tremie methods will be required during 
drilled shaft construction. The concrete should be placed in a suitable manner by 
displacing the water in an upward fashion from the bottom of the drilled hole. A low-shrink 
concrete mix with high slump (7 to 9-inch slump range) should be used to provide close 
contact between the drilled shafts and the surrounding soils. The concrete should be 
placed in a suitable manner to reduce the potential for segregation of the aggregates from 
the concrete mix.

Due to the proximity of the drilled shafts to the ocean, the contractor will need to
provide appropriate erosion control and BMP should be exercised. The contractor should
also procure necessary permits from various agencies to conduct work near the ocean.
It is imperative that a Geolabs representative or a qualified geotechnical engineer is
present at the project site to observe the drilling and installation of drilled shafts during
construction and should be designated a “Special Inspection” item in accordance with
Section 1704 of the International Building Code (2006 Edition).
