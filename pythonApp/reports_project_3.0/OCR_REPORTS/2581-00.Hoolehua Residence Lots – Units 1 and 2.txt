INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of a geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the subject sites in accordance with our fee proposal dated May 7 1990
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface soil
conditions at the proposed project sites in order to provide data for the formulation of
geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to the proposed lot and roadway

grading design of the general house foundations roadway pavement sections and
infrastructure installation
The scope of our work consisted of the following tasks and work efforts

The excavation and sampling of four test pits at the project site for Unit 1
and four test pits at the project site for Unit 2
Detailed site reconnaissance coordination of the field work and logging of
the test pits by a geologist from our firm
Engineering analysis of the exploration data for the formulation of geotech

Preparation of this report presenting our findings and recommendations

At the time of this report grading plans were not available for the proposed

also included the collection of soils data for the use in the design of foundations for
residential structures
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
The project sites are located on the Hoolehua Plain between the West and East
Molokai Domes The Hoolehua Plain or isthmus of Molokai is composed of lava flows
from the east Molokai Volcano banked against the older West Molokai Volcano Most of
the plain is covered with ten to thirty feet of lateritic soil
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2581 00 Page 4
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the sites were explored by the excavation of a total
of eight test pits to a maximum depth of seven feet below the existing ground surface
The test pits were excavated at locations determined by our geologist to provide soils
data representative of the sites in general The test pits were logged in detail and soil
samples collected and returned to our laboratory for analysis
Our exploration revealed a surface layer approximately one to four feet thick of
moderately stiff to hard clayey silts Below this surface layer our test pits encountered
hard to very hard clayey silts with inclusions of moderately to highly weathered rock
Groundwater was not encountered in the test pits during our exploration
DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on our exploration and analysis it is our opinion that the proposed
development of the project sites is feasible from a geotechnical engineering point of view
Site Grading
Prior to the commencement of any earthwork all areas within the grading limits
should be cleared of all vegetation and deleterious materials The resulting grub and spoil
material should be disposed of properly off site or stockpiled for use in landscaping Any
soft weak or yielding areas disclosed during the clearing operations within the portions
of the grading limits designated to receive fill should be over excavated to firm ground
and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with well compacted engineered fill
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2581 00 Page 5
Generally the on site soils should be suitable for use as engineered fill However
swell tests performed by our laboratory indicate that some of the surface soils are
moderately expansive when wetted therefore it is recommended that the surface soils
under the proposed roadway be scarified to a depth of twelve inches moistened and
recompacted to a minimum of 90 compaction
If additional imported materials are required the material should generally be non
expansive able to be compacted in a dense stable mass and have a minimum California
Bearing Ratio of 15 when tested in the laboratory in accordance with ASTM D 1883
All fills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding ten inches 10 in loose
thickness Fill placement should commence at the lowest point and continue upward in
level compacted layers The general fills should be compacted to a minimum of 90 of
the maximum dry density as established by ASTM D 1557 78
In areas to receive fill where the existing ground surface is steeper than five
horizontal to one vertical 6H 1V the new fill should be keyed and benched into the
existing slope to aid in the proper bonding of the new fill into the in situ soils
Slopes
Two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V cut and fill slopes are recommended for the
sites
Fill slopes should be constructed by overfilling past the design slope then cutting
back
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2581 00 Page 6
Water should be diverted away from the tops of slopes and slope planting utilized
to reduce erosion
Foundation Support
For the design of residential structure footings an allowable bearing capacity of
2000 p s f may be utilized for footings bearing on in situ soils or on fills compacted in
accordance with the above recommendations This value may be increased by one third
when temporary wind and seismic loads are considered The footings should be
embedded a minimum of twelve inches 12 below the lowest adjacent grade
If any soft or weak areas are encountered at the bottom of the footing excavation
they should be remedied by over excavation of the soft and or weak materials down to
very stiff natural alluvial soils and backfilling of the resultant void with fill material
compacted in accordance with the above recommendations Lots should be graded to
divert water away from foundations
Pavements
CBR testing was performed on samples of the soils collected at project sites to
determine the pavement section for the proposed roadways Based upon our laboratory
analysis we recommend the following design
2 Inches Asphaltic Concrete
6 Inches Base Course
8 Inches Total pavement Thickness
on a Compacted Moist Subgrade
The subgrade and base course should be compacted to a minimum of 95 of its
maximum dry density as established by ASTM D 1557 78
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2581 00 Page 7
CBR and density tests and or field observations should be performed on the actual
subgrade soils encountered during construction and that the above section be revised
if necessary The recommended section considers medium traffic and assumes that
adequate drainage will be provided in the paved areas Weepholes should be provided
at the base course level in the drainage inlet structures in order to relieve the seepage of
water which may accumulate in the subgrade
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be forwarded to the
geotechnical engineer for review and written comments prior to construction This review
is needed to determine adherence to the earthwork and foundation recommendations
given herein If this review is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume
responsibility for misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during the site grading and construction of the
foundations This is to observe compliance with the design concepts specifications or
recommendations and to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions
differ from that anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in
this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and approved by
the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the sites

If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during construction are different
from those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from field borings Variations of conditions between the field data
points may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident
until construction If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations given in this report
The location of the field borings were approximately determined by taping from
structures indicated on the transmitted topographic survey map Elevations of the data
points were estimated by interpolation from elevation points on the same topographic
map provided The physical locations and elevations of the field data points should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the field data points
depict the approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a
gradual transition The strata lines shown on profiles or cross sections are based upon
interpolation between field data points and may not represent actual subsurface
conditions

with generally accepted geotechnical engineering principles and practices No other
