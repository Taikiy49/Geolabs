Dear Mr. Maulit: 
The pavement structural section for the Kokomo Road Reconstruction, Phase 2 
project was based on the recommendations presented in the above-referenced report 
prepared by our office. The recommendations for the pavement structural section 
consisted of 3.0 inches of Asphaltic Concrete (AC) over 4.5 inches of Asphalt Concrete 
Base (ACB) on 6.0 inches of Aggregate Base (AB) over a layer of reinforcing geogrid 
(Tensar TriAx Grid, TX7) on a layer of permeable separator (Mirafi 140N) over compacted 
subgrade. Results of the pavement design analyses are contained in the 
above-referenced Pavement Justification Report, dated May 29, 2014, for the project 
prepared by Geolabs, Inc. 
Because of a water main traversing part of the travel lanes at shallow depths of 
about 14 to 20 inches below the existing grades, it is not practical to install the 13.5-inch 
thick pavement structural section without encountering and/or potentially damaging the 
shallow utility line during construction.  Given the above considerations for shallow utility 
lines, a thinner pavement structural section will be required to reduce the potential for

In addition, we understand that prior to the request for our evaluation, a portion of 
the existing waterline had already been encountered by the Contractor and a field call 
was made to reduce the excavation depth to 6 inches from top of pavement grade, as 
well as a lateral offset of 4 feet from the waterline location, which resulted in a reduced 
total pavement thickness from 13.5 inches to 6 inches for a distance of approximately 
4 to 5 feet from the edge of pavement.  Asphalt Concrete Base (ACB) material was then 
paved for a thickness of 3 inches, leaving a remaining 3 inches of Mix V pavement to 
reach the finish grade elevations. Subsequent to the completion of this work, the ACB 
material has exhibited signs of pavement slippage and/or “shoving” in certain areas.  
Based on our discussions with the County of Maui and the Contractor, we believe 
for the remaining roadway reconstruction that a pavement structural section consisting 
of about 7.5 inches of asphaltic concrete pavement (3.0 inches of Mix V AC sandwiched 
by a layer of GlasPave placed over 4.5 inches of ACB) would be a reasonable 
compromise given the shallow utility lines. The width of the reduced pavement structural 
section is on the order of about 5 feet from the edge of pavements.  This reduced 
pavement structural section (from 13.5 inches down to 7.5 inches of total pavement 
thickness) is a significant reduction in the pavement structural section and the 
corresponding pavement design life. 
Based on the reduced pavement structural section of 7.5 inches (total pavement 
thickness), the pavement design life was reduced from 20 years to about 10 years when 
calculated based on the State of Hawaii Department of Transportation method of 
asphaltic concrete pavement design. The State of Hawaii Department of Transportation 
method of asphaltic concrete pavement design is the same method used in the 
Pavement Justification Report for the project. 
Due to the irregular surface elevations of the current ACB roadway surface, we 
recommend that a leveling course of 1.0 to 1.5 inches of Mix V AC be placed over the 
4.5-inch of ACB first, followed by a layer of GlasPave, and then completed with a final 
layer of 1.5 to 2.0 inches of Mix V AC at the top. A product specifications and installation 
guide of the GlasPave pavement reinforcement system is provided with this letter for 
information purpose only. The Contractor should follow the installation procedures and 
requirements from the manufacturer of the specific pavement reinforcement system 
used for the project.  
Due to the reduced pavement structural section, we recommend that a more 
diligent inspection program should be implemented to review the conditions of the 
performance of the reduced pavement structural section over time to maintain the 
integrity of the pavement.  If needed, a more vigorous maintenance program would be 
implemented based on the results of the inspection program.

GlasPave 25 (WGP25)
GlasPave 25 is a fiber glass reinforcement grid coated in a modified polymer with a
polyester substrate adhered to the mesh. Carbon black is one of the components that
make up the protective coating of the GlasPave 25. It is used to provide resistance to
ultraviolet aging. Carbon black is widely used in the geosynthetics industry for anti-
ultraviolet aging, because black has higher light absorption, which can effectively
prevent the coating from the sun and light oxidation degradation. GlasPave 25 is
resistant to biological and chemical elements normally found in paving environments.

have been derived from quality control testing performed by a GAI-LAP accredited

The GlasPave® Waterproofing Paving Mat is an 
unique combination of fiberglass mesh embedded 
between two high performance polyester mats.  
GlasPave delivers the highest performance for the 
given tensile strengths (25 kN or 50 kN) in the 
market compared to any other paving interlayer. The 
distinctive design is optimized for easy installation 
and to achieve maximum performance.
Installation Procedures for GlasPave® Paving Mat
A Hybrid Geosynthetic Waterproofing Paving Mat
Image 1: Interlayer laydown equipment.
INSTALLATION STEPS
1.	
The GlasPave Paving Mat should be stored indoors 
and protected from the water and the environment.
2.	
GlasPave can be installed on an existing, milled or 
new bound pavement surface.
3.	
Surfaces must be free from dirt and loose stone prior 
to spraying a tack coat and installing the GlasPave 
Paving Mat.
4.	
Crack with widths greater than 1/4 in. (6 mm) must be 
filled with an approved crack sealant and all potholes 
must be repaired.
5.	
If installing GlasPave on a milled surface, the texture 
depth (distance between the top of ridges and 
trough) should not be greater than 1/2 in. (12.5 mm). If 
texture depths exceed ½ in. (12.5 mm) please consult 
a manufacturer’s representative.   
6.	
On concrete pavement surfaces, a level course is 
recommended prior to the placement of GlasPave.
7.	
The tack coat must be applied uniformly and provide 
full coverage. Apply tack the full width of the 
GlasPave material plus 6 in. (150 mm) to ensure full 
coverage
8.	
Use a hot applied tack coat. PG 64 (-10 to -34) is 
recommended. When ambient temperatures are 
above 90°, PG 70 (-10 to -34) is recommended.
9.	
The recommended application rates are:
a.	New asphalt: 0.15 gal/yd² (0.70 L/m²)
b.	Existing asphalt surface: 0.17 gal/yd² (0.80 L/m²)
c.	Milled surfaces: 0.18 gal/yd² (0.90 L/m²)
10.	 Surface and ambient temperatures during 
installation shall be 50° F (10° C) and rising, to allow 
adequate liquid tack to hold the GlasPave in place.
11.	
GlasPave can be applied by interlayer laydown 
equipment (see image 1) or a distributor truck with a 
fabric applicator attached to the back (see image 2).
Image 2: Distributor truck with fabric applicator attached to the back.
12.	 All adjacent GlasPave material should be 
overlapped (see image 3).
a.	Longitudinal (direction of traffic): 1 in. to 2 in. 
(25 mm to 50 mm)
b.	Transverse (90° to traffic): 2 in. to 4 in. (50 
mm to 100 mm)
13.	 All overlaps shall be shingled in the direction of 
the paving.
14.	 Use a pneumatic tire roller on top of the 
installed GlasPave to bond the GlasPave to the 
tack coat (see image 4).
15.	
Any wrinkles greater than 1 in. (25 mm) should 
be slit and lapped in the direction of paving (see 
image 5).
16.	 If any tack coat bleeds through the GlasPave or 
is left exposed, then small quantities of loose 
asphalt mix can be spread on the areas that are 
bleeding.
17.	
The asphalt overlay or chip seal should be 
installed the same day as the GlasPave.
18.	 All paving machine types can run on the 
installed GlasPave (see image 6). However, all 
turns by pavers, rollers, or asphalt delivery 
trucks should be made gradually.
19.	 Non-construction traffic should be kept off the 
GlasPave unless approved by the engineer and 
the manufacturer’s representative.
