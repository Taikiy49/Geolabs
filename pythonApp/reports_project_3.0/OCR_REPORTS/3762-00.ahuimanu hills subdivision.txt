In accordance with our proposal dated March 12th we have performed a
geotechnical engineering exploration at Lot No 53 of the Ahuimanu Hills Subdivision
Phase 6 development in the Kaneohe area on the island of Oahu Hawaii This letter
report summarizes the findings from our field exploration and laboratory testing and
presents our geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analyses of
the proposed project These recommendations are intended for design input only and are
subject to the limitations noted at the end of this letter report

conditions at the project site to develop an idealized soil rock data set for the formulation

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our field exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling three borings
designated as Boring Nos 1 through 3 to depths ranging from about 2 5 to 31 5 feet
below the existing ground surface The approximate locations of the borings are shown
on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general our field exploration encountered fill soils consisting of stiff to very stiff
silty clays and clayey silts extending to a depth of approximately 20 feet below the
existing ground surface This was generally underlain by alluvial deposits of medium stiff
to stiff silty clays with highly weathered basalt gravel extending to the maximum depth
explored of 31 5 feet below the existing ground surface
Groundwater was encountered in the drilled borings at depths ranging from about
20 to 22 5 feet below the existing ground surface It should be noted that groundwater
levels are subject to change due to seasonal precipitation and other factors
Detailed descriptions of the materials encountered in our field exploration are
presented on the Logs of Borings Plates 3 through 5 Laboratory testing was performed
on selected samples obtained and the test results are presented on Plate 6
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Our field exploration at the subject site generally encountered stiff to very stiff silty
clay and clayey silt soils extending to the maximum depth explored of approximately
31 5 feet below the existing ground surface Based on the relatively competent subsoil
conditions encountered in our borings and the anticipated light structural loads it is our
opinion that shallow foundations bearing on the lightly recompacted surface fill soils may
be used for support of the slab on grade portion of the new residence Our laboratory
swell test results indicate that the near surface fill soils exhibit slight to low swelling and
shrinkage potential when subjected to moisture fluctuations Therefore it is our opinion
that concrete slabs may be constructed on grade provided that the subgrade is prepared
in accordance with the recommendations presented in the Slabs On Grade section of
this report For the portion of house structure located adjacent to and over the existing
sloping ground on the north side of the property we recommend that post footings

Shallow Foundations
Based on the generally competent subsoil conditions encountered at the site we
believe that shallow spread and or continuous footings may be used for support of the
slab on grade portion of the proposed new residence An allowable bearing pressure of
up to 2 000 pounds per square foot p s f may be used for the design of footing
foundations bearing on the recompacted near surface fill soils This bearing value is for
dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third for transient loads such as those
caused by wind or seismic forces
The bottom of the footing excavations should be lightly recompacted to provide a
relatively firm and smooth bearing surface prior to placement of reinforcing steel If soft
and or loose materials are encountered at the bottom of footing excavations they should
be over excavated to expose the underlying firm materials The over excavation should
be backfilled with select granular material compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction or the footing bottom may be extended down to the underlying competent
material
The bottom of the footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below
the lowest adjacent finish grades If foundations are located next to utility trenches or
easements they should be embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V
imaginary plane extending upward from the bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep
as the inverts of the utility lines This requirement is necessary to avoid surcharging
adjacent below grade structures with additional structural loads and to reduce the
potential for foundation settlement
We estimate that footing settlements under the anticipated design loads for
footings bearing on the lightly recompacted near surface fill soils as recommended herein
to be less than 1 inch total with differential settlements on the order of inch
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive earth pressure
acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system and by frictional resistance
developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil Resistance due to
passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid pressure of
300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth p c f This assumes that the soil around
footings is relatively undisturbed or well compacted Unless covered by pavements or
slabs the passive resistance in the upper 12 inches of soil should be neglected A
coefficient of friction of 0 3 may be used for footings bearing on the recompacted
near surface fill soils We recommend that footing excavations be observed by a

Slabs On Grade
We anticipate that concrete slabs on grade will be used for part of the proposed
new residence Our laboratory tests indicate that the surficial fill soils exhibit slight to low
potential for swelling when subjected to moisture fluctuations To reduce the potential for
structural distress of the slabs on grade resulting from the swelling of the near surface fill
soils we recommend that the subgrade soils underneath the slab areas be scarified to a
minimum depth of about 8 inches moisture conditioned to between 2 and 5 percent
above the optimum moisture and recompacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a
percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with
ASTM D 1557 91 test procedures Optimum moisture is the water content percentage
by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
The slab subgrades should be kept moist prior to placement of concrete Where
shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of the subgrade we recommend that the
soils be thoroughly moistened to close all cracks Saturation and subsequent yielding of
the exposed subgrade due to inclement weather and poor drainage may require over
excavation of the soft areas and replacement with well compacted engineered fill
We recommend that a minimum 4 inch thick cushion layer consisting of No 3B
Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation or basalt termite barrier be placed below the
slab to provide more uniform support of the slab and to serve as a capillary moisture
break To reduce future moisture infiltration and subsequent damage to floor coverings
an impervious moisture vapor barrier such as a plastic membrane is recommended on
top of the gravel cushion layer Two inches of moist fine sand should be placed above
the plastic membrane to provide protection of the membrane and to aid in curing of the
slab concrete Flexible floor coverings such as carpet or sheet vinyl should be
considered since they can better mask minor slab cracking
Where the slab is subjected to vehicular traffic such as the garage slab and
driveway area a 6 inch layer of base course compacted to 95 percent relative
compaction is recommended in lieu of the No 3B Fine gravel cushion layer The 2 inch
moist fine sand and vapor barrier may be omitted below the garage and driveway slabs
Exterior concrete walkways may be required for the proposed project We
recommend that concrete walkways be supported on at least 6 inches of select borrow or
non expansive select granular fill material The select borrow or non expansive select
granular fill material should be compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction The subgrade soils underneath the sidewalk slabs should be prepared in

Drilled Shaft Foundations
We understand that post and beam construction will be used to support the portion
of house structure over the existing sloping ground on the north side of the property In
general for footings constructed near the top of slopes or on sloping ground the footings
should be embedded deep enough to provide a minimum horizontal set back distance of
8 feet measured from the outside edge of the footings to the slope face Based on the
existing terrain at the project site we anticipate that excavations on the order of about
5 to 6 feet below the sloping ground will be required for the construction of these deeper
footings Since the project site is located at a high rainfall area our past experience
indicated that there exists a potential for slope erosion under adverse weather conditions
Therefore we recommend these deeper footings be replaced with near surface post
footings supported by cast in place drilled piers embedded in the underlying stiff to very
stiff fill and alluvial soils
The drilled shaft foundations should consist of minimum 12 inch auger holes with a
4 inch diameter steel pipe installed at the center of the drilled holes The drilled piers
should extend to a minimum depth of 25 feet below the bottom elevations of the post
footings The drilled piers may then be backfilled in place by pumping concrete via the
interior of the metal pipes and eventually filling up the annular space between the drilled
holes and the pipe piles Based on our field exploration and engineering analyses an
allowable compressive load capacity of up to 20 kips may be used for the design of drilled
shaft foundations with a diameter of 12 inches and an embedded length of 25 feet This
value is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third for transient loads
such as those caused by wind or seismic forces A minimum center to center spacing of
3 times the shaft diameter is recommended to avoid reduction in compressive load
capacity due to group action
Total foundation settlements are estimated to be on the order of less than 1 inch
Differential settlements between shafts are estimated to be on the order of inch We
believe that these settlements would be elastic and should occur as the loads are applied
The pipe pile should be embedded a minimum of 4 inches at the top to a post
footing The bottom of the post footing should be embedded at about 12 inches below
the lowest adjacent finish grade Construction joints should be provided between the

Construction Considerations
The performance of drilled shafts will depend significantly upon the contractor s
method of construction and construction procedures As a result we recommend that a
representative of Geolabs Hawaii be present to observe the installation of drilled shafts
during construction In our opinion the following may have a significant impact on the
effectiveness and cost of the drilled shaft foundations
Cobbles and Boulders
Based on our field exploration results and our experience in the area cobbles and
possibly boulders may be encountered during the installation of the drilled shaft
foundations Drilling through the cobbles and boulders is a slow process for any
type of deep foundation To reduce the potential for the drilled holes to cave in
temporary casing of the drilled holes may be required during drilled shaft
construction Therefore unit prices for casing should be obtained during bidding
Care should be exercised during removal of the temporary casing to reduce the
potential for necking of the drilled shaft Therefore a minimum 5 foot head of
concrete should be maintained above the bottom of the casing during removal
Workmanship
The load carrying capacities of drilled shafts depend to a large extent on the
contact between the drilled shafts and the surrounding soils Therefore proper
construction techniques are important The contractor should exercise care in
drilling the shaft holes and in placing concrete into the holes
Drilling by methods utilizing drilling fluids is generally not recommended A
low shrink concrete mix with high slump 6 to 9 inch slumps should be used to
provide close contact between the drilled shafts and the surrounding soils The
concrete should be placed in a suitable manner to reduce segregation of the
aggregates from the concrete mix A representative of Geolabs Hawaii should be
present to observe the installation of drilled shafts during construction
Site Preparation
We envision that minimal site grading work is anticipated for the project
construction In general the following guidelines should be used for site grading At the
on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be thoroughly

should be removed and disposed of properly off site Soft and yielding areas
encountered during clearing and grubbing below any areas designated to receive fill
should be over excavated to expose firm natural material and the resulting excavation
should be backfilled with well compacted engineered fill The excavated soft and or
organic soils should be properly disposed of off site
After clearing and grubbing all areas at grade or to receive fill should be scarified
to a minimum depth of about 8 inches moisture conditioned to between 2 and 5 percent
above the optimum moisture and recompacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction Where shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of the subgrade we
recommend that the soil be thoroughly moistened to close all cracks Saturation and
subsequent yielding of the exposed subgrade due to inclement weather and poor
drainage may require over excavation of the soft areas and replacement with well
compacted engineered fill Contract documents should include additive and deductive
unit prices for over excavation and engineered fill placement to account for variations in
the over excavation quantities
Imported fill materials if required should consist of non expansive granular
material such as crushed coral mudrock basalt or cinder sand The material should be
well graded from coarse to fine with no particles larger than 3 inches in largest dimension
and should contain between 10 and 30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve The
material should have a laboratory CBR value of 20 or more and should have a maximum
swell of less than 1 percent Imported fill material should be tested by Geolabs Hawaii for
conformance with these recommendations prior to delivery to the project site for its
intended use Select granular fill materials should be moisture conditioned to above the
optimum moisture placed in level lifts not exceeding 10 inches in loose thickness and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
Site grading operations should be observed by a_ representative of
Geolabs Hawaii It is important that a representative from our office observe the site
grading to evaluate whether any undesirable materials are encountered during the
excavation and scarification process and whether the exposed soil rock conditions are
similar to those encountered in our field exploration
Utility Trench
We envision that new utility lines may be required for the project A granular
bedding consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation is
recommended under the pipes Free draining granular materials such as No 3B Fine
gravel should also be used for the trench backfill up to about 12 inches above the pipes to
provide adequate support around the pipes and to reduce the compaction effort of the
backfill thus reducing the possibility of damaging the pipes It is critical to use this free

draining material to reduce the potential of formation of voids below the haunches of
pipes and to provide adequate support for the sides of the pipes Improper trench backfill
could result in settlement of the backfill and damage to the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipes to
the top of the subgrade or finished grade may consist of on site soils or select fill material
The backfill should be placed in maximum 8 inch level loose lifts and mechanically
compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for
future ground subsidence Where trenches are below pavement areas the upper 2 feet
of the trench backfill below the pavement subgrade should be compacted to 95 percent
relative compaction
Drainage
The finished grades outside the new residence should be sloped to shed water
away from foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding Also it is advised that
gutter systems be installed around the building and that the discharge be diverted away
from the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near the
foundations and slabs should also be avoided Planters next to foundations should be
avoided or have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil
saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations
should be properly backfilled against the walls or slab footings immediately after setting of
the concrete to reduce water infiltration Drainage swales should be provided as soon as
possible and should be maintained to drain all surface run off away from the slab and
footing foundations

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part
upon information obtained from field borings Variations of subsoil conditions between
the borings may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become
evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be
necessary to reevaluate the recommendations provided in this report
The locations of the field borings indicated in this report are approximate having
been estimated by taping from the property lines shown on the Site Layout Plan
transmitted to our office on March 7th by Hawaiian Home Builders Elevations of the field
borings were estimated by interpolation from the contour lines shown on the same plan
The physical locations and elevations of the borings should be considered accurate only
to the degree implied by the methods used
The stratification lines shown on graphic representations of the borings depict the
approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual
transition Water level data from the borings were measured at the times shown on the
graphic representations and or in the text of this report These data have been reviewed
and interpretations made in the formulation of this report However it must be noted that
fluctuation may occur due to variation in rainfall tides temperature and other factors
