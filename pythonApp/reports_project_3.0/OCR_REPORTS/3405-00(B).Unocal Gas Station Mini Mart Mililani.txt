INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed UNOCAL Gas Station Mini Mart project at the Mililani
Mauka Center on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the
project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering recommenda
tions resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering analyses
These recommendations are intended for the design of foundation site grading and
excavation only The findings and recommendations presented herein are subject to
the limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
We understand that it is proposed to construct a new gas station mini mart
located at the Mililani Mauka Center on the island of Oahu Hawaii Based on the
information provided to us the site is triangular in shape and will have an area of
approximately 22 417 square feet We understand that the proposed new station will
consist of a one story food mart several pump islands and two underground fuel
storage tanks
Detailed grading plans for the project were not available at the time this report
was prepared We understand that the finished floor elevation for the proposed
structures will be at approximately 767 4 feet Mean Sea Level MSL Therefore we
anticipate that minor cuts and fills of less than 3 feet relative to the existing ground
surface may be needed to provide level pad for the proposed structures and parking
areas

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from two shield
volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be of
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 3405 00 Page 5
middle to late Pliocene in age and forms the bulk of the western third of the island The
younger shield Koolau is estimated to be of late Pliocene to early Pleistocene Ice
Age and forms the majority of the eastern two thirds of the island Waianae became
extinct while Koolau was still active and its eastern flank was partially buried below
Koolau lavas banking against its eastern flank These banked or ponded lavas formed
a broad plateau referred to as the Schofield Plateau on which the project site is
located
The project area is composed of basaltic rock built by the extrusion of the lavas
of the Koolau Volcanic Series These rocks are generally characterized by flows of
jointed dense vesicular basalt with interbedded thin clinker layers In situ chemical
weathering of the Koolau lava flows has occurred for the last 1 to 2 million years The
weathering process has formed a mantle of residual soils which grade to saprolite with
depth In general saprolite is composed mainly of silty material and is typical of the
tropical weathering of volcanic rocks The saprolite grades to basaltic rock formation
with depth

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsoil conditions at the subject site were explored by drilling and sampling
four borings to depths of about 16 to 21 feet below the existing ground surface The
approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Generally our field exploration encountered residual soils consisting of very stiff
to hard silty clays and clayey silts to the maximum depths explored of about 16 to
21 feet below the existing ground surface The residual soils grade to saprolite highly
weathered rock at depths of about 14 and 16 feet below the ground surface in Boring
Nos 2 and 4 respectively
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings at the time of our field
exploration It should be noted that groundwater levels are subject to change due to
rainfall time of year tidal fluctuations and other factors

on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 4 of Appendix A Results of the

Foundations
The proposed gas station mini mart facility will consist of a mini mart several
pump islands and two underground fuel storage tanks Based on the subsurface
conditions encountered in our exploration we believe that a shallow foundation system
consisting of spread and or continuous footings may be used to support the proposed
structures
An allowable bearing pressure of up to 3 000 p s f may be used for the design
of footings bearing on the very stiff to hard near surface residual soils This allowable
bearing pressure is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third 1 for
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3405 00 Page 8
transient loads such as temporary wind and or seismic forces Footings should be
embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade We
estimate that the total footing settlements under the anticipated design loads to be on
the order of less than 1 inch with differential settlements of approximately inch
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by frictional resistance
between the base of the spread footing and the bearing materials and by passive earth
pressure developed against the near vertical faces of the embedded portion of footings
A coefficient of friction of 0 35 may be used between the base of the footings and the
supporting materials Resistance due to passive pressure may be calculated using an
equivalent fluid pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth p c f for
footings embedded in the residual soils These values assume that the concrete for
footings is placed directly against the footing excavations Unless covered by
pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the upper 12 inches below finished grade
should be neglecied
Uplift loads especially for the canopy of the pump islands may be resisted by
a combination of the following 1 dead weight of the structure 2 weight of backfill
material above the footing where applicable and 3 side shear between the sides of
the footing and surrounding soil Contribution to uplift resistance due to the weight of
the backfill above the footing may be estimated using a unit weight of 105 p c f For
side shear between the sides of the footings and the adjacent soils a shear value of
150 p s f may be used to resist uplift loads
Where footings are to be located adjacent to below grade structures or utilities
the footings should extend to a depth below an imaginary 45 degree plane projected
upward from the bottom of the below grade structure or utility This requirement is
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3405 00 Page 9
necessary to avoid surcharging adjacent below grade structures with additional
structural loads and to reduce the potential for foundation settlement
Footing excavations should not be allowed to dry before placing concrete If
shrinkage cracks appear in the footing excavations the soil should be thoroughly
moistened to close all cracks and recompacted to provide a firm base prior to concrete
placement
Footing excavations should be observed by a representative of Geolabs Hawaii
prior to the placement of reinforcing steel or concrete to confirm the competency of the
bearing materials and embedment depths If unsuitable materials are encountered in
the footing excavations these materials should be over excavated to firm soils and
replaced with compacted engineered fill
Slabs On Grade
We anticipate that concrete slabs on grade will be used for the proposed
structures such as the mini mart and pump islands The near surface soils encoun
tered at the proposed site consist of clayey residual soils which exhibit a slight
expansion potential Therefore we recommend that the subgrade be properly prepared
and kept moist prior to placement of cushion fill and slab concrete The subgrades for
slabs on grades should be scarified to a depth of at least 8 inches moisture
conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture and compacted to not
less than 90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place
dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same
soil determined in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91 Optimum
moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the
maximum dry density
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 3405 00 Page 10
For more uniform support of interior slabs mini mart we recommend that the
concrete slab on grade be supported on a minimum 4 inch cushion fill The cushion
fill layer should consist of open graded gravels such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM
C 33 No 67 gradation which will also serve as a capillary moisture break The slab
cushion fill should be placed as soon as possible after the scarification and recompac
tion of the existing subgrade soils as recommended above For slabs on grade with
moisture sensitive surfacing we recommend that an impermeable membrane be placed
over the cushion fill to reduce potential migration of moisture vapor through the
concrete slab In order to promote more uniform curing of the slab concrete and to
provide protection of the vapor membrane we recommend that a 2 inch layer of moist
fine sand be placed above the membrane prior to placing the slab concrete The sand
should be moistened slightly prior to placing concrete
Where concrete slabs will be subjected to vehicular traffic such as the pump
islands we recommend that the slab be supported on a 6 inch layer of base course
compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction in lieu of the 4 inch cushion
fill layer and 2 inch sand The base course should consist of crushed basalt aggregate
The subgrade should also be scarified to a minimum depth of 8 inches moisture
conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture content and compacted
to not less than 95 percent relative compaction For design of structural slabs a
modulus of subgrade reaction of 250 pounds per cubic inch p c i may be used for the
compacted base course The thickened edges of slabs adjacent to any unpaved area
should be embedded at least 12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade
Site Grading
Final grading plans were not available to us at the time this report was prepared
However we anticipate grading with cuts and fills on the order of 3 feet and excavation
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3405 00 Page 11
and backfill of up to 15 feet relative to the existing ground surface may be expected for
the proposed development
Site grading operations should be observed by a representative of Geolabs
Hawaii It is important that a representative from our office observe whether the
exposed soil conditions during excavation are similar to those encountered in our
exploration The following recommendations are intended to provide guidelines for the
design of site grading work
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable
materials should be removed and disposed of off site After clearing and grubbing the
exposed subgrade should be scarified to a minimum depth of 8 inches properly mois
ture conditioned to at ieast 2 percent above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction For subgrades of
improvements subjected to vehicular traffic such as the pump islands and pavements
the compaction requirement should be increased to a minimum of 95 percent relative
compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed
as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil determined in accordance
with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91 Optimum moisture is the water content
percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
Any soft weak or yielding areas disclosed during clearing operations should be
over excavated to expose firm ground and the resulting excavation should be backfilled
with engineered fill compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Prior
to placement of the backfill the over excavated subgrade should be scarified to a depth
of at least 8 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3405 00 Page 12
moisture content and compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction The
excavated soft and or organic soils should be properly disposed of off site
The on site clayey and silty soils generated from the excavation may be reused
as a source of fill and backfill material The on site silty and clayey soils should be
properly moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture
content placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted
to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
Permanent fill slopes may be designed with a slope inclination of two horizontal
to one vertical 2H 1V or flatter Fills placed on slopes steeper than 5H 1V should be
keyed and benched
if imported fill materials are required the imported fill and or backfill material
should consist of well graded granular materials of less than 3 inches in maximum
dimension The material should have a laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR value
of 15 or more and should have a maximum swell of less than 1 percent when tested
in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1883 In addition it should contain
between 10 and 30 percent passing the No 200 sieve Imported fill material should be
tested by Geolabs Hawaii for conformance with our recommendations prior to delivery
to the project site for its intended use Imported materials should be brought to above
the optimum moisture content and compacted to not less than 90 percent relative
compaction
Excavation
In order to install the underground fuel storage tanks we anticipate that
excavations on the order of up to 10 to 15 feet below the existing ground surface will
be required for the project construction In general the method and equipment to be
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3405 00 Page 13
used for excavation should be determined by the contractor subject to practical limits
and safety considerations In general our field exploration encountered very stiff to
hard residual soils Therefore we anticipate that conventional excavation techniques
may be used for the proposed excavation with occasional hard layers
Based on our field exploration the project site was underlain by very stiff to hard
residual soils Therefore it is our opinion that an open cut may be used for the
excavation of the proposed underground fuel storage tanks provided construction space
is available A slope inclination ratio of 1H 1V or flatter may be used for the temporary
excavation in the stiff residual soils
If the open cut method is not practical for the proposed excavation we
recommend that a temporary shoring system be used for the proposed excavation
The temporary shoring support should conform to the requirements of local state and
federal regulations Appropriate loads due to surcharge equipment traffic or sloping
ground conditions adjacent to the excavations should be added to the design earth
pressures Stockpiling of excavated on site and or imported materials within a
horizontal distance equal to the depth of the excavation from the edge of the excavation
should be avoided
Pavements
Flexible pavements are anticipated for the driveway and parking lot areas of the
proposed project We anticipate the vehicle loading for this project will consist primarily
of passenger vehicles light pick up trucks and occasional heavy trucks We have
made our preliminary pavement designs assuming the pavement subgrade soil will be
similar to the clayey soils encountered in the borings with a minimum California Bearing
Ratio CBR of 10 and swell of less than 1 percent Based on the above assumptions

purposes
Asphaltic Concrete Pavement
2 0 Inch Asphalt Concrete
6 0 Inch Basalt Base Course 95 percent relative compaction
6 0 Inch Basalt Subbase Course 95 percent relative compaction
14 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness on a Moist Compacted Subgrade
The pavement subgrade soils should be scarified to a depth of least 8 inches
moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optinum moisture content and
compacted to not less than 95 percent relative compaction If pumping of the subgrade
occurs during construction the subgrade should be proof rolled to a minimum of
90 percent relative compaction After proof rolling a layer of filter fabric such as Mirafi
500X or equivalent should be placed on the subgrade prior to placement of the
subbase and base course The subbase and base course should consist of crushed
basalt aggregate compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
Laboratory CBR tests should be performed on samples of the actual subgrade
soils encountered during construction to confirm the above recommended design
section The recommended pavement section assume that good drainage will be
provided for areas adjacent to the pavements
Paved areas should be sloped and drainage gradients maintained to carry all
surface water off the site Surface water ponding should not be allowed anywhere on
the site during or after construction Where concrete curbs are used to isolate
landscaping in or adjacent to the pavement areas we recommend that the curbs
extend a minimum of 2 inches into the subgrade below the aggregate base course to

Utility Trench
We envision that utility lines would be required for the proposed project In
general for support of the utility lines we recommend that a granular bedding
consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation be used
under the pipes Where soft and or loose soil deposits are encountered near the pipe
invert elevations we recommend that the utility pipe be supported on an additional
18 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation wrapped in a geotextile
fabric Mirafi 140N or equivalent The backfill up to about 1 foot above the pipes
should consist of free draining backfills such as No 3B Fine gravel to reduce the
potential for damaging the pipes from compaction of the backfill
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 1 foot above the pipes to
the top of the subgrade or finished grade should consist of select granular material or
the approved on site soils The backfill should be moisture conditioned to above the
optimum moisture content placed in maximum 8 inch level loose lifts and mechanically
compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for
future ground subsidence Where trenches are below pavement areas the upper 2 feet
of the trench backfill below the pavement subgrade should be compacted to not less
than 95 percent relative compaction
Drainage
The finished grades outside the buildings should be sloped to shed water away
from foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding Also it is advised that gutter
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3405 00 Page 16
systems be installed around the buildings and that the discharge be diverted away from
the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near the
foundations and slabs should also be avoided Planters next to foundations should be
avoided or have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the
subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil
saturation and subsequent heaving because of the swelling potential of the near
surface residual soils The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled against
the slab footings immediately after setting of the concrete to reduce water infiltration
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and maintained to drain
all surface run off away from the slabs and footing foundations

summarized on the Logs of Boring at the appropriate sample depth Graphic presenta

near surface soil to evaluate the strength characteristics for pavement subgrade
support Results of the CBR test are presented on Plate B 3
