SUMMARY OF FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that the project site is underlain
by a competent coralline deposit in the lower flat northern
areas and blanketed by a layer of highly expansive adobe
clay in the southern sloping areas The surface layer of
adobe clay was about 0 to 4 feet in thickness over an older
alluvium colluvium deposit in the upper slope areas and
increased in thickness to about 2 to 31 feet over a coralline
deposit in the lower slope areas
Because of the shrink swell potential and possibility of
downslope creep movements of these adobe soils the
recommended grading concept for the site is to remove the
adobe clay soils above a 10 horizontal to 1 vertical 10H 1V
slope and placement of the adobe Clay in deep fills in the
lower flat areas The adobe clay should be capped with a
minimum of 3 feet of non expansive material The on site
coralline soils may be used as this capping fill
In the southeastern portion of the site where the adobe clay
soils extend up slope beyond the property limits special
stabilization methods will be required to limit the down slope
creep of the adobe clay soils For the development of this
area additional exploration is recommended
The text of this report should be referred to for detailed and
special design recommendations

GENERAL SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located at the base of Puu Mailiili in the Waianae district on the
leeward coast of the island of Oahu Hawaii Most of the site was formerly used as a
coral limestone quarry The remainder of the site may have previously been used as
pasture but is now covered with dryland vegetation such as kiawe trees haole koa and
grass
The site slopes upwards in the southerly direction with slopes generally ranging
from 15 horizontal to 1 vertical 15H 1V in the lower flat areas 5H 1V slopes in the lower
slope areas and 2H 1V slopes in the upper slope areas The existing ground elevations
ranged from about 30 to 230 feet above Mean Sea Level
The upper and lower slope areas of the site are mantled with highly expansive
adobe clay soils Previous experience with similar areas in the Waianae district indicate
that some of these adobe slopes may have adverse mass stability conditions with a
potential for development of downward movement known as creep

CONCLUSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Our exploration disclosed that the lower northern areas of the project site are underlain
by competent coralline soils Near the lower slope areas in the southern portion of the
site a surface layer of expansive adobe clay about two 2 to thirty one 31 feet in thickness
was encountered above the coralline soils In the upper slope areas in the southern portion
of the site the surface adobe clay layer is thinner less than four 4 feet in thickness
and is underlain by an older alluvium colluvium deposit and basalt
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2294 10 Page 6
Slickensided zones were noted in some of the test pits indicating shear creep movement
of the deposit
These deep sloping adobe clay deposits have high shrink swell potential an extreme
tendency for downhill creep movement and even a possibility for landslide movement
particularly when steepened by large cuts
Because of the shrink swell potential and possible downslope creep movement
of these soils the recommended general grading concept for long term stability is to remove
all the adobe clay soils above a ten 10 horizontal to one 1 vertical 10H 1V slope line
and burying the adobe clay soils in the lower flat areas The adobe clay soils should
be capped with a minimum of three 3 feet of non expansive material The on site coralline
soils are considered to be suitable for fill within the upper three 3 feet of finished grade
In the southeastern portion of the site where the adobe clay extends up slope
beyond the property limits special stabilization methods such as use of a buttress fill or
retaining wall will be required to control the downhill creep of these soils This condition
was not anticipated when our previous proposals and investigations were made and there
is therefore insufficient data available upon which to base specific recommendations
regarding this facet of the design Additional exploration is recommended in this area for
the development of our stabilization recommendations

for the Phase II development The grading plan for Phase III was not available at the time

Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
cleared of trees and other vegetation debris rubbish and other deleterious
materials These materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site
In areas to receive fill the existing ground should be scarified to a depth of six 6
inches moisture conditioned to the wet side of optimum moisture content and
recompacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density as determined in
accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 Soft or yielding areas should be
over excavated and stabilized by backfilling with select material placed in ten 10
inch thick loose lifts compacted to 90 of its maximum dry density as determined
in accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Fill Material and Placement
The adobe clay soils to be removed from the upper slopes and placed in the
lower area of the site have moderate to high swelling potential depending on the
initial Compaction density and molding moisture content prior to saturation
Therefore in order to reduce the swelling potential of the soil the material should
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2294 10 Page 8
be placed at moisture contents above the optimum moisture content The adobe
clay soils should not be placed within three 3 feet of the finished grade
Materials used for fills placed within the upper three 3 feet of the embankments
should be select non expansive material generally less than three 3 inches in
maximum dimension with a plasticity index Pl as determined in accordance with
ASTM Test Method D 4318 84 not exceeding twenty 20 The on site coralline
soils are considered to be suitable for this purpose
If additional off site borrow soil is required it should be tested by the geotechnical
engineer to evaluate its suitability as fill for the project prior to its delivery to the
project site

Many surface boulders were noted around the site In addition a large quantity
of boulders will be generated from excavation operations Boulders at the surface
and in the excavations may be used in the deeper fills provided that the following
recommendations are followed
1 Boulders must not be nested together They should be placed so that
compaction equipment is able to compact around them
2 Large boulders should not be used within three 3 feet of the
finish grade elevation

Fill Placement and Compaction
The fill should be placed in maximum ten 10 inch thick loose level lifts and
compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density as determined in
accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 Each layer should be spread
uniformly and blade mixed to attain uniformity of the material and water content
Additional fill material should not be placed on any fill layer which has
not been properly compacted
Cut and fill slopes of two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V may be used This is
based on the assumption that the fill slope will be constructed of select material
Steeper cut slope ratios of up to 1H 1V may be used in weathered basalt formation
and up to in stiff colluvium
Fill slopes should be constructed by overfilling then cutting back to the
design slope to obtain a well compacted slope face
For cut and fill slopes greater than twenty 20 feet in vertical height an eight 8
foot wide drainage bench should be placed at the mid height of the slope

Where the existing ground is steeper than five horizontal to one vertical
5H 1V keying and benching are required to properly bond the new fill to the
slope The filling operations should start at the lowest point and continue up in
level compacted layers as recommended above Construction details are shown
on Plate 3
Water should be diverted away from the tops of slopes and slope planting utilized
to minimize erosion
Foundations
It is our opinion that shallow spread or strip footing foundations bearing on the on site
coralline soils or compacted fill may be used to support the proposed houses and
industrial structures Thickened edge slabs on grade may be considered to support the
lightly loaded single family residential structures
General guidelines for foundation design are as follows
1 Footings on level areas should be embedded a minimum of eight 8 inches
below the lowest adjacent finished grade
2 Footings on sloping areas should be embedded an additional amount to
obtain a five 5 foot minimum horizontal set back distance The set back
distance is the horizontal distance measured from the outer edge of the
footing out to the slope face For a two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V
slope ratio a minimum of two and one half 2 5 feet of embedment would
be required at the lower side of the footing This additional embedment
depth is required to mobilize the full bearing Capacity of the soil To
eliminate difficulty of backfilling the excavation we recommend that concrete
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2294 10 Page 11
be used to construct the deep pier footings with column straps buried in the
concrete backfilled excavation Refer to Plate 5 for details
3 An allowable bearing capacity of 2 500 p s f may be used for footings
bearing on the in site coralline soils or on properly compacted fill
4 The bottom of all foundation excavations should be cleared of loose material
and bear on firm soil The excavations should be observed by a
geotechnical engineer to see that adequate bearing soil has been obtained
5 The ground surface should be shaped to drain water away from the
foundations
6 It is recommended that the thickened edges of slabs on grade should
extend a minimum of eight 8 inches below the lowest adjacent exterior
grade The slab should be a minimum of four 4 inches thick and should
be supported by a four 4 inch thick cushion of No 3B Fine gravel or
ASTM C 33 No 67 A moisture barrier membrane should be incorporated
between the slab and cushion to protect the interior floor covering from
moisture infiltration It is recommended that isolation joints be provided
between the slabs on grade and the wall foundations to allow for some
relative movement between the slabs and walls

Boulder Protection
The upper hillside lots may be subject to boulders rolling down the hillside
The falling boulders are a general phenomena occurring in all ridge slopes on
Oahu Various methods of boulder barriers or catchment can be used to reduce the
hazard of falling boulders For this site various methods of boulder prevention may be
appropriate Some methods that may be considered include but are not necessarily
limited to the following
1 A boulder catchment area about ten 10 to fifteen 15 feet wide with or
without a fence or wall could be utilized near the property boundary to
catch rolling boulders An interceptor ditch along the upper boundaries of
the site could be used in this capacity
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2294 10 Page 14
2 Boulder sweeps along the upper boundaries of the site could be performed
periodically to identify and remove loose boulders that appear likely to roll
down the slopes
3 The natural trees and vegetation along the upper portions of the site should
be retained to act as a natural barrier against movement of the boulders
and to reduce erosion
4 Where practicable the structures should be setback away from the toe of
hillside slopes to create an additional boulder catchment area
It must be pointed out that whatever methods are used an inherent risk of falling
boulders could still exist for these hillside lots
The state of the art of soil engineering practice has not advanced to a point that
a solution is available for this area It is our opinion that no matter what one does there
is always the risk of boulders rolling down the hill if not from areas near the house from
areas higher up on the slopes Whatever measure is chosen it only reduces the risk
It should be pointed out that no guarantee against future falling boulders can be made
due to changes in soil and rock conditions with time weathering erosion earthquake and
construction vibration and improvements and construction activities at and around the
site
The owners of these hillside lots should be made aware of the aforementioned
limitations and cautioned against future improvements in the upper areas of the lots which
may disturb the stability of the existing boulders

A
