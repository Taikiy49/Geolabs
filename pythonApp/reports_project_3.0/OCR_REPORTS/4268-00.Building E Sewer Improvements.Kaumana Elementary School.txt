In accordance with our proposal dated we have performed a
geotechnical engineering exploration and field percolation testing program within the existing
Kaumana Elementary School within the District of South Hilo on the Island of Hawaii This
letter report summarizes the findings from our field exploration and percolation testing at the
proposed leach field and alternative leach field sites and presents our findings and
discussions for the proposed project These findings and discussions are intended for design
input only and are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this letter report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located within the existing Kaumana Elementary School in the
Kaumana area of Hilo on the Island of Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the
project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
Based on the information provided we understand that sewer improvements
consisting of a septic tank and a leach field are planned for the existing Administration
Building known as Building E Therefore it was desired to conduct field percolation tests at
the proposed leach field and alternative leach field locations to obtain data pertaining to the
preliminary design of the sewage disposal system The approximate locations of the
proposed septic tank and leach fields are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 We understand
that the sewage system from Building E is currently being collected by a cesspool located
to the north of Building E The cesspool has experienced overflowing in the past probably
due to a high groundwater table and low permeability subsurface materials

conditions at the proposed septic tanks and leach field locations and to perform field
percolation tests The subsurface information obtained was used to evaluate the subsoil
infiltration characteristics for the formulation of preliminary recommendations pertinent to
the design of the disposal components of the on site sewage disposal system The work
was performed in general accordance with our fee proposal dated Our
scope of work included the following tasks and work efforts

project site and its vicinity

leach field locations

from our firm

EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is located within the existing Kaumana Elementary School in the
Kaumana area of Hilo on the Island of Hawaii The proposed project site is bounded by
Building E to the south the cafeteria to the west portable buildings to the east and an open
play field to the north as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Based on our site reconnaissance the project site is currently an open area covered
with lawn grass An existing cesspool is located just north of Building E Vehicular access
onto the project site is not possible due to covered walkways surrounding the site However
at the time of our exploration the roofs of the covered walkways were removed between the
portable buildings and Building E to allow vehicular access onto the site The project site
gently slopes downward from north to south Based on the site plan provided the existing
ground surface ranges in elevation from about 1 022 to about 1 026 feet Mean Sea
Level MSL
FIELD EXPLORATION
Our field exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling four borings
designated as Boring Nos 1 through 4 extending to depths of about 11 to 15 feet below the
existing ground surface The borings were drilled using a truck mounted drill rig equipped
with continuous flight augers Boring Nos 1 and 2 were drilled at the proposed leach field
location and Boring Nos 3 and 4 were drilled at the proposed alternate leach field location
The approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by testing
in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on the Boring Log Legend Plate 3 Graphic representations
of the materials encountered are provided on the Logs of Borings Plates 4 through 7
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by
driving a 2 inch OD standard penetration sampler or a 3 inch OD Modified California sampler

Pocket penetrometer and torvane shear tests were performed on selected cohesive

FIELD PERCOLATION TESTING
Field percolation tests were performed in the borings in general accordance with the
Wastewater Systems and Chapter 10 of the Ten States Standards To evaluate the subsoil
profile and permeability of the subsurface soils for wastewater disposal the percolation tests
were performed by drilling 4 0 inch diameter boreholes extending to depths ranging from
about 11 to 15 feet below the existing ground surface The percolation tests were performed
in the volcanic ash material and the basalt rock formation encountered
Approximately one to two inches of gravel were then placed at the bottom of the
boreholes to protect the bottom of the holes from scouring and sediments During the test
the holes were carefully filled with water to a minimum height of 6 inches above the gravel
pack The time intervals and water drops were then recorded to provide data upon which
calculation of the percolation rates were based The results of the individual percolation tests
are presented on Plates 8 through 11 of this report
LABORATORY TESTING
The following laboratory tests were performed on selected soil samples obtained from
the field exploration to aid in classifying the materials encountered
Moisture content ASTM D 2216 and unit weight ASTM D 2937 determinations were
performed on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil
properties The results of these tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the
appropriate sample depths
One Atterberg Limits test ASTM D 4318 was performed on a selected sample of the
surficial soils to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits and to aid in soil classification Results
of this test are summarized on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths and
presented graphically on Plate 12

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
In general our field exploration encountered a layer of volcanic ash deposits
consisting of medium stiff to very soft clayey silts extending to depths of about 7 to 10 feet
below the existing ground surface The volcanic ash deposits were underlain by extremely
to moderately weathered basalt rock formation extending to the maximum depths explored
of about 11 to 15 feet below the existing ground surface Groundwater was not
encountered in the drilled borings at the time of our field exploration However
groundwater levels can fluctuate depending on factors such as seasonal rainfall
groundwater withdrawal and or injection and other factors Detailed descriptions of the
materials encountered in our field exploration are presented on the Logs of Borings Plates 4
through 7
FINDINGS AND DISCUSSIONS
We understand that on site sewage disposal by the septic tank and leach field method
is planned for Building E within the existing Kaumana Elementary School site Therefore we
have conducted field percolation tests to obtain data pertaining to the preliminary design of
the sewage disposal system A percolation test was performed at each of the four borings
drilled The approximate locations of the field percolation tests are shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2 In addition selected soil samples obtained from the field exploration were tested in
the laboratory to evaluate the moisture density and plasticity as an aid in classifying the
materials encountered
Based on the percolation tests conducted the percolation rate at the proposed leach
field location performed at depths of about 11 5 to 15 feet below the existing ground surface
was greater than 60 minutes per inch The percolation rates at the proposed alternate leach
field location performed at depths of about 11 to 15 feet below the existing ground surface
ranges from about 1 minute per inch in Boring No 3 to about 28 minutes per inch in Boring
No 4 Results of the individual percolation tests are presented on Plates 8 through 11 of this
report The percolation tests were performed in the volcanic ash material and the basalt rock
formation encountered
Based on the Manual of Septic Tank Practice prepared by the U S Department of
Health Education and Welfare proposed leach field locations with percolation rates
exceeding 60 minutes per inch would not be suitable for the siting of the absorption system
Based on the percolation test results obtained at the proposed leach field location in
Boring Nos 1 and 2 we believe that the subsoils in this area are highly impermeable and
may not be suitable for the siting of the sewage disposal system

Based on the results we believe that the basalt rock encountered at depths of about
7 to 9 feet below the existing ground surface at the proposed alternate leach field location
may be suitable for the siting of the sewage disposal system It should be emphasized that
the percolation rate of the basalt rock formation at the site may be highly variable due to the
presence or absence of significant voids the fracturing of the rock formation and the
presence of embedded clinker seams Due to the variability of the subsoil conditions the
absorption capacity of the disposal system should be confirmed by additional percolation
tests during construction
It should also be noted that the existing volcanic ash materials overlying the basalt
rock formation are soft and are sensitive to vibrations Therefore excavation for the leach
field should consider the presence of the soft volcanic ash materials which may collapse into
the excavation during construction Therefore proper shoring of the leach field excavation
will likely be required during construction
In addition based on the guidelines presented in the State of Hawaii Department of
absorption trenches should not be closer than 2 feet to the seasonal high groundwater level
As previously mentioned groundwater was not encountered in our borings at the time of our
field exploration However groundwater levels can fluctuate depending on factors such as
seasonal rainfall groundwater withdrawal and or injection and other factors
