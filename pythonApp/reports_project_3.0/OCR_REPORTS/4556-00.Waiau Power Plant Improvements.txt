INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration performed
for the proposed Waiau Power Plant Improvements project in the Pearl City area on the
Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical recommendations derived from
our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering analyses These recommendations
are intended for the design of retaining structures and site preparation only The findings and
recommendations presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this
report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS

planned for the existing power plant

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lava from two
shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be
middle to late Pliocene in age and Koolau is estimated to be late Pliocene to early
Pleistocene in age As the volcanic activity in Waianae ceased lava flows from Koolau
banked against its eroded eastern slope forming a broad plateau now known as the
Schofield Plateau The project site is located on the southwestern flank of the Waianae
Volcano
Physical and chemical weathering followed by erosion of Schofield Plateau generated
sediments which were transported to the coast In the vicinity of the project site and to the
south these sediments accumulated and are interbedded with marine sediments and
coral algal reef formations to form a sedimentary wedge The thickness of the sedimentary
wedge ranges from zero in the area of the Interstate Route H 1 Highway to over 1 000 feet
at Ewa Beach This wedge forms the Ewa Plain and serves as the confining formation or
caprock over the artesian basal aquifers of Southern Oahu Deposition of sediments has
continued from earlier geologic time through the present
During the Pleistocene Epoch sea levels fluctuated in response to the cycles of
continental glaciation As the glaciers grew and advanced less water was available to fill the
oceanic basins such that sea levels fell below the present stands of the sea When the
glaciers melted and receded an excess of water became available such that the sea levels
rose to above the present sea level
The processes of erosion and deposition caused the top of the basaltic lava flows to
be highly weathered and mantled by residual soils During the Pleistocene Epoch the Kaena
stand of the sea level at approximately 90 feet Mean Sea Level a delta of silt sand and
gravel built out into the embayment near the project site Agricultural land development and
reclaimation projects within the last century have brought the project site to its present form

EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The existing Waiau Power Plant is located along Kamehameha Highway in the Pearl
City area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The power plant was divided into two parts north
and south by a utility corridor right way travelling from west to east Generally the project
site is located in the southern portion of the power plant and bordered by the utility corridor
a stream channel lagoon and the ocean
The proposed Timber Pile Renovation site is located at the southwest portion of the
power plant The existing timber piles line the side of a channel lagoon and extend from
northeast to southwest towards the ocean as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The existing
timber piles serve as a retaining wall along the existing channel lagoon Based on our site
observation the tops of the timber piles are located about 1 to 3 feet above the existing
ground surface The water level in the channel was measured about 3 feet below the ground
surface during our site visit The bottom of the channel varied from 4 to 6 feet below the
ground surface An unpaved roadway was observed along the timber pile wall Due to the
deteriorated condition of the timber pile wall the roadway immediately adjacent to the timber
pile wall experienced erosion and localized stability failure
The proposed Intake Discharge Sheet Pile Separator site is located at the
southeastern portion of the power plant The existing sheet pile separators consist of two
walls extending from land into Pearl Harbor as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The existing
intake discharge sheet pile separators were constructed in 1980 Based on information
provided the existing sheet piles are generally about 30 feet in length and the top 6 feet of
the sheet piles are coated with epoxy for corrosion protection The sheet pile wall along the
easter side is about 1 300 feet long and in fair condition with rusty sections observed above
the water Large holes were not observed The sheet pile wall located west of the long sheet
pile wall is about 300 feet long Large holes were observed on the sheet piles above the
water level which indicate that significant deterioration and corrosion have occurred
Our subsurface exploration for the Sheet Pile Separators was limited to the area
adjacent to the existing sheet pile walls along the shoreline The area adjacent to the short

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by drilling and sampling
seven borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 7 extending to depths of about 28 5 to
51 5 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate locations of the borings drilled
are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Our field exploration in the area of the existing timber pile retaining wall consisted of
drilling four borings designated as B 1 through B 4 to a maximum depth of 51 5 feet below
the existing ground surface Generally our borings encountered about 3 to 5 feet of fill
consisting of stiff clayey and silty soils The surficial fill was generally underlain by very soft
harbor mud consisting of silty and clayey soils with organic content to a depth of about 40
feet Some very loose silty gravel was encountered near the bottom of Boring No 1 underlain
by soft silty clay to the maximum depth drilled of 51 5 feet below the existing ground surface
In addition some very stiff clayey silt and dense silty gravel were encountered in the bottoms
of and 4 respectively Groundwater was encountered in the borings at about
2 to 5 feet below the existing ground surface during our field exploration
Our field exploration in the area of the existing intake discharge sheet pile separators
consisted of drilling three borings one boring near the short sheet pile separator and
two borings near the long sheet pile separator designated as through 7 to
the maximum depth of 33 feet below the existing ground surface Boring No 5 which is
adjacent to the short sheet pile separator encountered about 2 feet of soft sandy clay fill
overlying very loose to loose harbor mud consisting of clayey gravel with organic contents
and silty gravel to a depth of about 25 5 feet below the existing ground surface Boulders
were encountered to the maximum depth drilled of 33 feet below the existing ground surface
Groundwater was encountered in the boring at about 6 inches below the ground surface
during our field exploration

The mudline along the east and west sides of the short sheet pile wall may range from

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The timber pile wall renovation site is generally underlain by a thin surficial fill layer
overlying very soft to soft harbor mud materials The short sheet pile separator site is
generally underlain by a thin surficial fill layer overlying very loose harbor mud material and
GEOLABS INC
W O 4556 00 Page 8
some boulders at greater depths The long sheet pile separator site is generally underlain
by a thin surficial fill layer overlying very soft or very loose harbor mud materials with some
boulders and stiff materials at greater depths
Differential mudline elevations were noted on two sides of the existing sheet pile
separators by the jet probing operation conducted by Sea Engineering Inc Low resistance
was encountered up to 20 feet below the mudline elevations along the long sheet pile
separator However greater resistance was encountered at 8 to 10 feet below the mudline
elevations along the short sheet pile separator
A steel sheet pile wall system is planned to replace the existing timber pile wall and
two intake discharge separators It is our opinion that a cantilevered steel sheet pile wall may
be used to replace the existing timber piles to resist lateral earth pressure due to the in situ
soils and to retain the roadway behind the existing channel Due to differential mudline
elevations on the two sides of the existing sheet pile separators and consideration of the
possible storm condition new sheet pile separators may be designed as cantilevered
retaining walls to resist lateral earth pressure and lateral pressure due to unbalanced water
elevations during storm conditions

Timber Pile Renovation
A sheet pile wall will be used to replace the existing timber pile retaining wall and to
provide lateral support for the existing soils Design of the sheet pile wall will need to
consider both overturning stability and overall slope stability Geotechnical design
parameters to evaluate these stability conditions are presented in the following subsections
of this report The overturning stability will need to be evaluated by the design engineer We
have evaluated the overall slope stability for the proposed sheet pile wall and the results are
presented in a subsequent subsection of the report

Static Lateral Earth Pressures
The sheet pile wall should be designed to resist the lateral earth pressures due to the
adjacent soils and surcharge effects We recommend that the sheet pile wall be
designed for lateral earth pressures due to the in situ soils which include the surface
fill and harbor mud materials Based on the current design concept the existing
timber piles will remain and the new sheet pile wall will be installed behind the timber
piles We envision that a portion of the surface fill adjacent to the timber piles may
need to be removed and backfilled with compacted granular material Based on the
borings the very soft harbor mud materials extend to approximately 32 feet MLLW
near the wall locations Based on these conditions the recommended lateral earth
pressures for design of the sheet pile wall expressed as an equivalent fluid pressure
in pounds per square foot per foot of depth pcf are provided in the following table
RECOMMENDED LATERAL EARTH PRESSURES
SHEET PILE WALL
Active earth pressure above water level 35 pounds per cubic foot
Active earth pressure below water level 14 pounds per cubic foot
Passive earth pressure below water level 75 pounds per cubic foot
It should be noted that the lateral earth pressures provided above do not include
hydrostatic pressure due to water An apparent lateral earth pressure diagram is
presented on Plate 3 to illustrate the above recommendations for design of the sheet
pile wall
Based on information provided we understand that light traffic loading may be applied
on the roadway behind the sheet pile wall Generally surcharge stresses due to areal
surcharges line loads and point loads within a horizontal distance equal to the depth
of the sheet pile should be considered in the design For uniform surcharge stresses
imposed on the loaded side of the sheet pile a rectangular distribution with uniform
pressure equal to 40 percent of the vertical surcharge pressure acting within 10 feet
from the top of the wall may be used in the design Additional analysis during design
may be needed to evaluate the surcharge effects of point loads and line loads

Unbalanced Hydraulic Pressures
For waterfront structures such as sheet pile walls maximum lateral earth pressures
may be expected when the tide is at its lowest level A receding tide may cause
higher water levels behind the sheet pile than in front of it depending on the type of
soils behind the sheet piles The high water level behind the sheet pile wall will
introduce an additional hydrostatic load For the type of soils encountered at the
project site an estimated hydrostatic pressure based on the maximum tide difference
may be considered for the sheet pile design
Overall Slope Stability
We have evaluated the overall slope stability of the proposed sheet pile wall Slope
stability analyses consisting of two dimensional slope cross sections using Bishop s
simplified method of limit equilibrium analysis were performed on the sheet pile wall
structure Based on our analyses the factors of safety for the overall slope stability
of the sheet pile wall are at least 1 5 which is the minimum normally recommended
Corrosion Protection
Two pH and two minimum resistivity tests were performed to evaluate the corrosivity
of the underlying soils The results of these tests are presented on Plate B 3 of
Appendix B The minimum resistivity values of the soil samples tested range from
190 to 350 ohm cm which are indicative of an extremely corrosive environment to
steel and metals in contact with the soils Therefore we recommend that
substructures including other steel sheet piles and other metallic substructures in
contact with the soils be properly designed to protect against potential corrosion A
corrosion engineer should be consulted for detailed recommendations on corrosion
protection We understand that a concrete cap will be cast over the exposed sheet
pile wall to reduce the potential for corrosion to the exposed portions of the sheet
piles

Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
Based on the current design concept we anticipate that minimal site grading work will
be required for the proposed construction As mentioned previously surface fill materials
adjacent to the existing timber piles were eroded Some localized slope stability failure was
observed behind the timber pile wall alignment These erosive loose surface fill materials
may need to be removed and replaced with properly compacted granular fill
Site grading operations should be observed by a representative from Geolabs It is
important that a representative from our office be present to observe the site preparation to
evaluate whether undesirable materials are encountered during the excavation and filling
process
In general we recommend that the loose material behind the timber piles be
completely removed to expose firm or dense material and the resulting excavation be
backfilled with well compacted fill Contract documents should include additive and deductive
unit prices for over excavation and compacted fill placement to account for variation in the
over excavation quantities Select granular fill material should be placed in level lifts not
exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture
content and compacted to between 90 and 95 percent relative compaction
Over compaction of the backfill would exert additional lateral earth pressures on the walls
and should be avoided Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil
expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil determined in
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91 Optimum moisture is the water content
percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density

Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling
seven borings extending to depths of about 28 5 to 51 5 feet below the existing ground
surface The approximate locations of the borings drilled are shown on the Site Plan Plate
2 The borings were drilled using a truck mounted drill rig equipped with continuous flight
solid stem augers
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by testing
in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representations of the materials
encountered are presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 4
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 3550 84 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 3 inch

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content ASTM D 2216 and unit weight ASTM D 2937 determinations were
performed on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil
properties The results of these tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the
appropriate sample depths
Three Atterberg Limits tests ASTM D 4318 were performed on selected samples of
the soils to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits and to aid in soil classification The results
of these tests are summarized on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depth
Graphic representation of the test results is provided on Plate B 1
One sieve analysis ASTM C 117 ASTM C 136 was performed on a selected
sample of the soils to evaluate the gradation characteristics of the soils and to aid in soil
classification Graphic presentations of the grain size distribution are provided on Plate B 2
Two pH ASTM G 51 and two minimum resistivity tests ASTM G 57 were performed
on selected soil samples obtained from the areas along the proposed sheet pile walls and
wall separators to evaluate the corrosivity of the soils The results of these tests are
summarized on Plate B 3
