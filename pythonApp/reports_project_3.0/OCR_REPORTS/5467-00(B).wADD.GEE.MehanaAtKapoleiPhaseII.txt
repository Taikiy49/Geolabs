SECTION 2 0 SITE CHARACTERIZATION
2 1 Regional Geology
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lavas from
two shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated
to be middle to late Pliocene in age and Koolau is estimated to be late Pliocene to early
Pleistocene in age
The project site is situated on the Ewa Coastal Plain which extends seaward off
the southeastern flank of Waianae and the southwestern flank of Koolau This plain
was formed by the accumulation of a thick wedge of sediments and coral reef
formations on the flanks of the two volcanoes
During the Pleistocene Epoch sea levels fluctuated in response to the cycles of
continental glaciation As the glaciers grew and advanced less water was available to
fill the oceanic basins such that sea levels fell below the present stands of the sea
When the glaciers melted and receded an excess of water became available such that
the sea levels rose to above the present sea level
The higher sea level stands caused the formation of reef deposits at
correspondingly higher elevations Subaerial exposure of the sediments and
calcareous materials caused consolidation of the lagoonal deposits and induration of
the calcareous reef materials
The soils and rocks underlying the project site were described by H T Stearns
and K N Vaksik as presented below

2 2 Site Description
The Mehana at Kapolei project site is located to the south of the City of Kapolei
in the Kapolei area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project site Lot Nos 2 and 3 is
former sugar cane plantation land and is situated to the west of Fort Barrette Road and
to the north of Kalaeloa formerly Naval Air Station Barbers Point as shown on the
Project Location Plan Plate 1
At the time of our field exploration the project site was vacant land with a light to
heavy cover of dry land vegetation such as keawe haole koa and grasses Based on
our field observations and the current USGS topographic map most of the project site
generally slopes down toward the southeast with existing ground surface elevations
ranging from about 50 feet Mean Sea Level MSL along the southern boundary to
about 60 feet MSL along the existing Waimanalo Road at the northern side of the
Mehana project site

3 1 Site Grading
Items of grading that are addressed in the following subsections include 1 Site
Preparation 2 Keying and Benching 3 Fills and Backfills 4 Fill Placement and

Site grading operations should be observed by a representative from Geolabs It

Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork areas within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed Vegetation debris deleterious material and
other unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site or
stockpiled in a designated area to reduce the potential for contamination of the
excavated materials
Soft and yielding areas encountered during clearing grubbing and over excavation
work should be over excavated to expose firm natural material and the resulting
excavation should be backfilled with well compacted engineered fill The excavated
soft soils may be used as fill provided that it meets the requirements for fill
material
Keying and Benching
The ground surface at the project site is nearly level however there may be some
localized areas with steeper slopes Therefore this section is provided to give
guidance in design and construction of grading for those areas Any keys and
benches required should be firmly seated into existing ground

key should be a minimum of 2 feet in depth at the toe of the fill and should be a

Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
Fills and backfills should be moisture conditioned to about 2 percent above the
optimum moisture placed in level lifts not exceeding 10 inches in loose thickness
and compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction
refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum
dry density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557
Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by weight corresponding to the
maximum dry density We wish to emphasize that moisture conditioning of the fill
materials is an integral part of the fill placement recommendations provided herein
Compaction should be accomplished by sheepsfoot rollers vibratory rollers or
other types of acceptable compaction equipment
Base course and select borrow subbase materials in roads driveways and parking
areas should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture placed in
level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum
of 95 percent relative compaction
Because moisture conditioning and compaction of the subgrade soils are critical
elements of earthwork observations and soil density tests should be performed by
Geolabs during site grading to assist the contractor in obtaining the required degree

3 3 Slabs On Grade
Slabs on grade should bear on a minimum of 4 feet of structural fill For interior
building slabs not subjected to vehicular traffic we recommend that a minimum 4 inch
thick layer of cushion fill consisting of open graded gravel ASTM C 33 No 67
gradation be provided below the slabs The open graded gravel cushion fill would
serve as a Capillary moisture break and would provide for uniform support of the slabs
To reduce the potential for future moisture infiltration through the slab and subsequent
damage to floor coverings an impervious moisture barrier is recommended on top of
the open graded gravel cushion layer It also is recommended that interior walls be
designed to incorporate some flexibility to accommodate a small amount of possible
ground movements
Where slabs will be subjected to vehicular traffic such as garage slabs we
recommend that a 6 inch layer of aggregate base course be provided below the slabs

crushed basaltic aggregates compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture Content ASTM D 2216 and Unit Weight ASTM D 2937
determinations were performed on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification
and evaluation of soil properties The results of these tests are presented on the Logs
of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Three Atterberg Limits tests ASTM D 4318 were performed on selected soil
samples to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits and to aid in soil classification Results
of the tests are summarized on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depth
Graphic presentation of the test results is provided on Plate B 1
Four Sieve Analysis tests C136 were performed on
selected soil samples to evaluate the gradation characteristics of the soils and to aid in
soil classification Graphic presentation of the grain size distribution is provided on
Plate B 2
Three laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR tests ASTM D 1883 were
performed on bulk samples of the near surface soils to evaluate the pavement support
characteristics of the soils The results of the test are presented on Plates B 3 thru B 5
Five one inch Ring Swell tests were performed on natural and remolded samples
of the soils to evaluate the swelling potential of the near surface soils The test results
are presented on Plate B 6
