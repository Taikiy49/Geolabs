PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located along the makai side of Queen Kaahumanu Highway
in the Kohanaiki area on the Island of Hawaii Based on the information provided the
project site encompasses an area of about 500 acres
We understand that a new luxury residential subdivision will be constructed at the
project site In general the proposed development includes the construction of a golf
course over 300 residential lots and other related structures Details for the proposed
development were not available at the time this letter was prepared
SURFACE AND SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Based on our site reconnaissance the project site is generally underlain by
pahoehoe lava flows at the ground surface The project site is generally sloping down
from Queen Kaahumanu Highway towards the Pacific Ocean Based on the topographic
survey map available the existing ground surface elevation ranges from about 10 feet
Mean Sea Level MSL near the shoreline to about 80 feet MSL at Queen Kaahumanu
Highway Vegetation at the project site is generally sparse with some wild grass growth
through the crevices of lava rock
In general we envision that the project site is underlain by dense pahoehoe basalt
formation with interbedded layers of clinker materials In general the dense basalt
formations at the project site may be hard to very hard and relatively unweathered to
moderately weathered It should be noted that cavities and or voids are commonly
present in the basaltic lava flows in the vicinity Therefore the presence of cavities and or
voids should be anticipated during construction of the project

MASS GRADING
Based on the preliminary mass grading plans we anticipate that mass grading for
the proposed construction at the project site will involve excavations and embankments of
up to about 15 to 20 feet in height Site preparation of the existing ground surface prior to
the start of filling operations at the project site is a critical element of the earthwork
operations Therefore special attention should be given to the discussion and
recommendations pertaining to the subgrade preparation at the project site Items of site
grading that are addressed in the subsequent subsections include the following

excavation and scarification process and whether the exposed soil conditions are similar
to those encountered in our field exploration
Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork areas within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed Vegetation debris deleterious materials and other
unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site to reduce the
potential for contamination of the excavated materials to be used as embankment fill
materials We wish to point out that caution should be exercised by the contractor while
clearing and grading near collapsed lava tube features and or cavities which may
represent potential lava tubes or cavities that may further collapse under the load of heavy
equipment
In general we recommend that the fill areas of the project site be ripped to a depth
of about 3 feet below the existing ground surface at the commencement of grading The
purpose of ripping the fill subgrades is to assist in detecting and collapsing cavities and or
voids contained in these porous near surface materials As a minimum ripping of the
ground surface should extend at least 3 feet laterally beyond the fill areas

After the fill surfaces have been ripped to a depth of about 3 feet below the ground
surface the ground should be leveled with the use of a Caterpillar D 10 bulldozer or
similar size bulldozer working the surface a minimum of four passes to provide a relatively
level surface After leveling of the rugged surfaces with a bulldozer we recommend that
the fill subgrades be proof rolled with a large vibratory drum roller minimum 20 tons static
weight for a minimum of eight passes to help detect and collapse near surface cavities
and or voids The vibratory drum roller should be operated at a speed of about 300 feet
per minute Yielding areas loose areas or cavities disclosed during the clearing and
proof rolling operations should be over excavated and backfilled with compacted fill
materials The depth of over excavation should be extended until dense underlying
materials are exposed and should be evaluated in the field by a representative from
Geolabs
As for the finished subgrades in cut areas we recommend that the cut subgrades
be proof rolled with a minimum 20 ton static weight vibratory drum roller for a minimum of
eight passes to help detect and collapse near surface cavities and or voids The vibratory
drum roller also should be operated at a speed of about 300 feet per minute The
requirement for ripping the ground surface by a depth of about 3 feet in fill areas may be
omitted for the cut subgrades In addition proof rolling of the subgrade with a Caterpillar
D 10 bulldozer for a minimum of four passes may be omitted in the cut subgrades
provided that the finished cut subgrades are relatively level to operate the vibratory drum
roller
The proof rolling operations should be performed in the presence of a
representative from Geolabs Yielding areas loose areas or cavities disclosed during
clearing and proof rolling operations should be over excavated and backfilled with
compacted fill materials Contract documents should include additive and deductive unit
prices for over excavation of the collapsed lava tube features and other features and
backfilling with compacted fill to account for variations in the over excavation and backfill
quantities
Based on our past experience and site reconnaissance we envision that the
existing ground surface will be lower in elevation when compared to the existing ground
elevations after the site preparation operations at the project site Lava tubes and or
cavities commonly are encountered in the pahoehoe lava flows at the ground surface
Due to the porous nature of the near surface materials the existing ground surface
could be lowered in elevation by about 1 to 2 feet in localized areas after site
preparation For preliminary planning and quantity estimation purposes we believe that
the existing ground surface could be lowered by approximately 6 to 18 inches on the
average from the original ground level following site preparation operations Therefore
estimation of earthwork quantities should take into consideration the anticipated
lowering of the existing ground surface

Fills and Backfillis
In general we recommend that the fill areas of the project site be capped with a
minimum 2 foot thick layer of structural fill material to serve as a choking layer for the
larger sized general fill materials below Structural fill materials should consist of
well graded granular materials generally less than 6 inches in maximum dimension with
sufficient fines to prevent the occurrence of voids in the compacted mass In general the
excavated materials may be re used as a source of structural fill materials provided that
the materials are processed to meet the maximum particle size and gradation
requirements presented above Where appropriate such as within utility corridors the
thickness of the structural fill layer may be increased to a minimum of 1 foot below the
invert elevation of the utilities to facilitate future construction of the utilities
Fill and backfill materials below the minimum 2 foot thick structural fill layer may
consist of general fill materials General fill and backfill materials should consist of
well graded granular materials the majority of which are less than 12 inches in size with
an absolute maximum dimension of 18 inches Materials between 12 and 18 inches in
particle size should be fimited to about 15 percent or less of the total volume of general fill
materials The excavated on site materials if less than 18 inches in maximum dimension
may be used as a source of general fill or backfill materials except for trench backfill
provided that they are processed to meet the above gradation requirements for general fill
If the excavated materials do not contain sufficient fines to produce a well graded material
off site borrow or on site rock crushing of larger rock fragments or boulders should be
considered to provide the required gradation and particle sizes to develop a well graded
material
Where over cuiting of the finished cut subgrades occur during the mass grading
operations backfilling of the over cut area should be treated as fill areas Therefore the
above recommendations pertaining to fill materials should apply to the over cut areas and
the fill materials required for backfilling will depend on the depth of over cut below the
finished subgrade
Imported fill materials where required should be free of organics and deleterious
materials and should be suitable for the intended use based on the recommendations for
fill materials provided in this report structural fill general fill and or boulder rock fill In
addition imported fill materials should be observed and or tested by Geolabs prior to being
transported to the site for the intended use
Excavated on site materials consisting of boulders less than 5 feet in maximum
dimension may be used as rock fill provided that the top of the rock fill layer is placed at
least 4 feet below the finished subgrades In general the placement of boulders in fills
should be performed in accordance with the procedures recommended in the following

Boulder Disposal
Boulders i e rock fragments larger than 12 inches in maximum size but less than
5 feet in largest dimension encountered during site grading may be used in rock fills
provided that the following recommendations are followed
1 Boulders less than 5 feet in largest dimension may be utilized as rock fills
provided that the top of the rock fill layer is placed at least 4 feet below the
finished subgrades Boulders larger than 5 feet in maximum dimension
should not be used as rock fill unless reduced in size
2 Boulders greater than 18 inches in largest dimension should not be
exposed at the fill slope face Boulder placement should be setback a
lateral distance of at least 6 feet from the edge of the fill slope face
3 Care must be exercised to avoid placement of boulders in the proposed
utility line alignment roadways and within the depths of the proposed
utility lines to reduce the potential for encountering the boulders during
excavation for the utility trenches
Compaction of the boulders in the rock fills should be performed in accordance with
the recommendations presented in the following Fill Placement and Compaction
Requirements subsection It should be noted that disposal of boulders in the general fill
material will require that the entire fill mass be compacted and properly choked off at the
surface and each layer of rock fill to reduce the potential for migration of fines through the
voids between boulders
Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
Structural fill materials should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 12 inches in
loose thickness moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture and compacted to
at least 95 percent relative compaction General fills and backfills should be placed in
level lifts not exceeding 18 inches in loose thickness moisture conditioned to above the
optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of
the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 1557 Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by weight
corresponding to the maximum dry density Compaction should be accomplished by
sheepsfoot rollers vibratory rollers or other types of acceptable compaction equipment
Rock fills should be placed in lifts not exceeding the maximum dimension of the
majority of the rocks or 5 feet whichever is less Placement should be by haul trucks
traversing previously placed lifts and dumping at the edge of the currently placed lift
Spreading of the rock fill should be by a large bulldozer to facilitate seating of the rocks
The rock fill should be watered heavily during placement by water trucks traversing in front

Compaction equipment such as a Caterpillar D 10 bulldozer or similar heavy compaction
equipment providing suitable energy to achieve the required compaction should be
utilized The number of passes usually six to eight passes to be made to achieve proper
compaction should be determined at the on set of site grading by the performance of a trial
compaction test or by plate bearing tests Subsequent layers of rock fill above the
previously placed rock fill should provide a choking layer of smaller sized boulder materials
to fill in the voids between the boulders of the preceding rock fill layer
Conventional compaction testing generally is not practical in fills composed of
rocks boulders and or cobbles Instead a testing program to evaluate the number of
passes by a compactor needed to achieve the desired level of compaction should be
conducted at the start of the grading phase of the project under the observation of a
representative from Geolabs Based on this testing program the number of passes may
then be used as the field criterion for adequate compaction For preliminary estimating
purposes compaction may be accomplished by using a large vibratory drum roller having
a static weight of at least 20 tons traveling at a speed of about 300 feet per minute The
roller should pass over the fill surface at least eight times for structural fill and six times for
general fill For general fill a Caterpillar D 10 bulldozer or similar size equipment may be
used in lieu of the vibratory drum roller
Excavation
The project site is generally underlain by dense basalt formations pahoehoe lavas
ranging fram medium hard to very hard It is anticipated that the basalt formation near the
ground surface may be readily excavated with normal heavy excavation equipment such
as excavators and ripping with large bulldozers However some of the deeper
excavations in the dense pahoehoe lava may require the use of hoerams chipping or
blasting
The above discussions regarding the rippability of the surface materials are based
on our visual observation of the existing basalt formation and field data from the borings
performed at the project site We recommend that contractors be encouraged to examine
the site conditions and the subsurface data to make their own interpretation
Cut and Fill Slopes
Based on available subsurface information it appears that most of the cut slopes
would expose pahoehoe basalt formations encountered at the project site In general we
believe that the cut slopes planned at the site may be designed with a slope inclination of
one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V or flatter However due to the presence of clinker
layers the individual cut slopes should be re evaluated during construction In areas
where Clinker layers unstable and or friable materials are exposed at the cut slope faces
the cut slopes should be flattened to 2H 1V or flatter Therefore provisions to flatten the
cut slopes and or construct retaining walls should be considered in the design of the

We understand that it is desired to excavate some of the cut areas with a

be provided for fill slopes greater than 10 feet in vertical height placed on existing ground
steeper than 5H 1V The fill slope face should be finished to a relatively smooth and
well compacted surface The fill slope face should be free of large boulders greater than
18 inches in largest dimension and should be free of voids which would allow erosion
and migration of fines to occur In addition slope planting or other means of slope
protection should be provided as soon as possible to reduce the potential for significant
erosion of the finished slopes
PAVEMENTS
We anticipate that asphaltic concrete pavements will be used for the roadways
planned at the project site In general we anticipate that the vehicle loading for the
proposed roadways of this project would consist primarily of passenger vehicles light
trucks and occasional heavy trucks Therefore we have assumed generally light to
medium traffic loading conditions for pavement design purposes On this basis we
recommend that the following preliminary pavement design be used for this project
Flexible Pavement Section
2 0 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 0 Inch Aggregate Base Course 95 Percent Relative Compaction
8 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness on Moist Compacted Subgrade
The subgrade under the pavement areas should be scarified to a minimum depth of
8 inches where possible moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture and
compacted to at least 95 percent relative compaction Where roadway subgrades consist
of dense basalt formation the roadway subgrades should be proof rolled to aid in
detecting and collapsing near surface cavities Proof rolling operations should be
performed with a large vibratory drum roller minimum 20 tons static weight for a minimum
