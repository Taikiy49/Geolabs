Pile Driving Report
PCAS Services
Repair Wharf W5
Naval Magazine
In general accordance with the scope of services outlined in our
proposal dated we have observed the predrilling and the
pile driving of the test piles and production piles of the proposed fender
system at Wharf W5
A Vulcan 01 pile hammer was used for both the test and production
driving of the eighteen inch square precast and prestressed concrete
fender piles A total of forty four 44 piles which included three 3
test piles and forty one 41 production piles were driven to the general
depths of about 51 5 to 61 0 feet below the design cut off elevation of
about 108 9 feet U S Navy Datum However the pile at Line No 56 could
not be driven to the required depth due to obstruction by an unknown
object objects buried in the subsoils The driving of this pile will be
attempted again later when the predrilled hole is cleared
The pile locations were generally predrilled to a depth of about 54
to 58 feet prior to pile driving except at Line Nos 56 and 58 where the
predrilling was stopped at shallower depths due to total or partial
obstruction of the drilling
The locations of all the piles are shown on the attached Pile
Location Plan
Based on our observations we believe that both the production and
test piles have been driven to sufficient depths and or resistance to
support the weight of the piles and provide the design lateral capacity
