INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed H 1 Kawela Bay Hotel at the Kuilima Development at Kahuku
on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location of the site and its vicinity is shown
on the Project Location Map Piate 1

This report summarizes the findings of the exploration and our geotechnical
engineering recommendations derived from our analyses based on the data obtained
during the exploration These recommendations are intended for design input and are
not intended for construction estimation or bidding purposes
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions at the project site in order to determine pertinent physical properties of the soil
and rock at the site for the formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations
pertinent to site grading and foundation design of the project
The work was done in general accordance with our proposal dated November 15
1988 The scope of work consisted of the following
1 The drilling and sampling of thirteen 13 borings to depths ranging from
40 5 to 75 0 feet below the existing ground surface
2 Reconnaissance of the project site and vicinity to make visual observations
of the surficial soil conditions
3 Review of available soils and geologic information pertinent to the project
site and its vicinity
4 Laboratory testing of selected samples obtained during the field exploration
to determine the classification and engineering properties of the materials
encountered

5 Engineering analyses of the field and laboratory data for the purpose of
developing site grading and foundation design recommendations
6 Preparation of this report presenting our findings and design
recommendations
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration and laboratory testing are presented
in the appendices of this report

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located on the northern tip of Oahu adjacent to the existing
Turtle Bay Hilton resort and the H 2 Kawela Bay Hotel presently under construction
The site is bounded by Kamehameha Highway to the south and the Pacific Ocean
to the north
The topography of the site is generally flat with ground elevations ranging from 8
to 11 feet above MSL
The area is moderately to heavily vegetated with trees large shrubs and grasses
There are existing residential structures along the shoreline at the northern part of
the site
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
The island of Oahu was built during the late Pliocene to early Pleistocene by two
basaltic shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau Waianae is the older of the two and
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2333 00 Page 5
forms the bulk of the western half of the island The younger Koolau forms the eastern
half of the island extending from Kahuku to Koko Head
After the main shield building phase of the two volcanoes the island began to
subside in response to tectonic stresses imposed by the loads of the mountain masses
Concurrently reefs began to grow on the flanks of the subsiding volcanoes forming
fringing and barrier reefs of coral algal limestone
During the middle and late Pleistocene or Ice Age sea levels fluctuated in response
to the advance and retreat of the continental glaciers As the glaciers grew and
advanced there was less water to fill the ocean basins so sea levels fell to below the
current level When the glaciers melted and retreated more water was available and sea
levels rose often to levels above the present stand of the sea
The geologic conditions at the project site are a direct result of these glacioeustatic
sea level changes during the Pleistocene

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration indicates that the proposed development is feasible from a
geotechnical engineering point of view Different foundation systems may be used to
support the proposed structures depending on the anticipated loads
Site Grading
It is our understanding that the proposed site grading for the project consists of
up to about 9 feet of fill to raise the site above the tsunami and flood heights to elevations
of about 17 5 feet
GEOLABS HAWAII

Settlement
Since significant site filling is planned in the project area settlements on the order
of 1 to 3 inches are anticipated due to the compression of the soft and loose soil
from the weight of 9 feet of new fill
It is very difficult to precisely predict the actual time required for the above fill
settlements to occur since it depends on minute characteristics of the soil structure
and actual thickness of the compressible layer We estimate that 95 of the total
settlements will have occurred within two 2 months of the fill completion
Because of the anticipated ground settlements we recommend that the site
preparation and embankment construction for this area be given first priority to
minimize delaying the commencement of the building construction
Settlement Monitoring
We recommend that a minimum of six 6 settlement gauges be installed within the
project area prior to filling to monitor the actual amount and rate of subsidence
and to determine when the construction of buildings and utilities can commence
The surveyed settlement readings should be submitted to the project geotechnical
engineer for interpretation
Details of the settlement platforms are presented on Plate 3 Since the gauges are
highly susceptible to damage by the contractor s equipment during filling operation
extreme caution is required in the operation of equipment in their vicinity and the
platforms must be conspicuously flagged in the field

Site Preparation
Prior to the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should
be cleared of trees and other vegetation debris rubbish and other deleterious
materials These materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site
Top soil should be segregated and stockpiled for future landscaping uses if
appropriate
The area should be thoroughly grubbed to a minimum depth of 6 inches and all
roots removed from the site
The fill areas should then be scarified to a depth of six 6 inches moisture
conditioned and recompacted to a minimum of 90 of ASTM D 1557 78 maximum
dry density
Fill Material and Placement
It is anticipated that off site borrow soils will be required for fill materials The
quality of the fill materials should be evaluated by the geotechnical engineer prior
to transport to the site
In general fill material should have low expansion potential be less than 6 inches
in size with less than 5 organic materials and capable of being compacted into
a dense stable mass Materials used for fills placed within the top three 3 feet
of finished grade should be non expansive with no individual particles exceeding
three 3 inches in maximum dimension and a plasticity index Pl per ASTM Test
Method D 4318 84 not exceeding 20
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2333 00 Page 9
In order to minimize later difficulties during pile driving boulders larger than 6
inches should not be used in the structural fill areas
The fill should be placed in loose level lifts with a maximum thickness of 10 inches
and compacted to a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density per ASTM Test
Method D 1557 78 Each layer should be spread uniformly and blade mixed to
attain uniformity of the material and water content
Additional fill material should not be placed on any fill layer which has not been
properly compacted
Slopes
Cut and fill slopes of 2 1 H V are recommended
Fill slopes should be constructed by overfilling then cutting back to the design
slope to obtain a well compacted slope face
Surface water should be diverted away from the tops of the slopes and slope
planting utilized as soon as possible to minimize erosion
Building Foundations
Based on the results of our field exploration a pile foundation system appears to
be the most feasible means to transmit the heavy multi level structural loads to the
underlying basalt formation For medium to low rise buildings generally less than four
4 stories high a spread or continuous footing foundation system may be used to
support the lighter structural ioads

Shallow Foundations
The field exploration generally encountered a surface layer 20 feet thick of
medium dense sand overlying alluvial clay or coral reef detritus
Up to nine 9 feet of embankment will be placed over the project site Spread or
continuous footing foundations bearing on this compacted fill may be used to
support the low rise buildings generally less than 4 story high
An allowable bearing value of up to 3000 p s f with a minimum footing embedment
of 18 inches is recommended
For estimated column loads of up to 50 kips and wall loads of up to 12 kips per
foot anticipated settlements are on the order of less than one inch total and
one half of an inch differential For higher column loads of up to 300 kips and wall
loads of up to 32 kips per foot estimated total settlements are on the order of one
1 to two 2 inches

Test Pile Program
A test pile program should be undertaken to fulfill the following objectives
1 To more accurately delineate the boundaries where changes of subsoil
conditions occur
2 To provide a better estimate of the final production pile casting lengths to
reduce the lengths of unused piles
3 To study the potential damages to the piles due to tensile stresses developed
during the early stages of pile driving particularly for driving the piles through
the fill and into the loose silty sands
4 To help evaluate and modify if necessary the production pile driving criteria
To achieve the above objectives we recommend that a minimum of 10 of the
total number of piles be test driven first to determine the actual pile lengths
especially in areas where subsoil conditions are anticipated to change rapidly
These test piles may be later incorporated into the actual foundations
As part of the test pile program two 2 pile load tests are recommended The
two 2 test piles should be load tested to twice the allowable pile load in
accordance with the ASTM D 1143 test method
The test piles should be driven with the same hammer which is used for
production pile driving We recommend that the test pile program be conducted
under close and continuous observation of the project geotechnical engineer who
GEOLABS HAWAII

will coordinate field pile driving work determine when piles may be stopped and
provide guidance should additional measures be required due to unforeseen
driving conditions
Slab On Grade
Slab on grade construction may be used for the development The on grade floor
slabs should be placed over a 4 inch thick cushion layer of clean gravel material generally
between a quarter to three quarters of an inch in size to provide uniform support and a
capillary break Below the cushion layer the subgrade soil should consist of a minimum
2 feet of non expansive select material A moisture barrier membrane is recommended
between the slab and the cushion layer to reduce the possibility of moisture damage to
the floor coverings
The thickened edges of the on grade slabs should be embedded a minimum of 12
inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade The bottom of all thickened edge
excavations should be wetted and kept moist until concrete placement The backfill
against the concrete slab should be adequately compacted and graded to shed water
away from the slab perimeter

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from borings Variations of conditions between the field data points
may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident until
construction If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations given in this report
The location and elevation of the borings were determined by estimation from the
topographic map by Engineer Surveyors Hawaii Inc The physical location and elevation
of the borings should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the method
used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the borings depict the
approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual
transition The strata lines shown on profiles or cross sections are based upon
interpolation between field data points and may not represent actual subsurface
conditions
