PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The Lima Ola Subdivision Phase 1 project encompasses about 50 acres in 
Ele’ele on the island of Kaua’i, Hawai’i. The Lima Ola Subdivision Phase 1 project is 
generally bound by Kapaa Reservoir to the north, Halewili Road to the south, 
agricultural land to the east, and Kaumuali’i Highway to the west. Additionally, off-site 
improvements were made to the intersection of Kaumuali’i Highway and Laulea Street. 
A Project Location Map is shown on Plate 1. Site plans for the overall site work, 
roadway and utility work, and off-site intersection improvements are shown on 
Plates 2.1 through 2.3, respectively. 
The development consists of grading and installation of new parcels and 
stormwater runoff detention basins on 50 acres of the project site. The development site 
was broken into eight increments, numbered 1 through 8. On Increments 1 through 6, 
the 941 feet extension of Mahea Road and construction of 2,658 feet of new access 
roads to the new house lots consisted of flexible asphaltic pavements. Other site

SITE GRADING AND UTILITY AND ROADWAY INSTALLATION 
We observed the mass and pad grading, underground utility construction, and 
roadway construction of Lima Ola Subdivision Phase 1 and the Kaumuali’i Highway and 
Laulea Street (South) Intersection Improvements projects from November 7, 2020 through 
November 12, 2021. The mass and site grading work consisted of clearing and grubbing, 
and placement and compaction of fills up to about 12 feet thick to achieve the finished 
grades. Prior to filling, the existing ground surface was cleared and grubbed of vegetation 
and deleterious material, then proof-rolled with a compactor. Earthworks Pacific, Inc. 
performed the mass and pad grading, and utility and roadway construction work. 
During the site grading operations, samples of the materials utilized as fill were 
obtained and transported to our office for testing. The maximum dry densities and 
optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general accordance with 
ASTM D1557 (Modified Proctor), and the test results are summarized on Plates 3 and 5.  
The fill materials were moisture-conditioned to near optimum moisture content and 
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density, as determined in 
accordance with ASTM D1557. In the roadway areas, the fill and backfill materials at the 
finished subgrade were compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction.  
During the mass and pad grading, and utility and roadway construction operations, 
periodic field density tests were performed in general accordance with ASTM D6938 
(Nuclear Gauge Method). Compaction test results for the overall site work, roadway, and 
utility work are summarized on Plates 4.1 through 4.41. Compaction test results for the 
off-site intersection improvements at the intersection of Kaumuali’i Highway and Laulea 
Street are summarized on Plates 6.1 through 6.4. These tests were performed on the
