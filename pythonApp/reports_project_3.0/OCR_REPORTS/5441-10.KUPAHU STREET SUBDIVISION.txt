PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is in the Waialua area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The
roadway project extends Kupahu Street between Nauahi Street and Goodale Avenue
Extension for a distance of approximately 200 linear feet A Project Location Map and a
Site Plan are shown on Plates 1 and 2 respectively
The new roadway consists of two 12 foot wide traffic lanes in each direction with
is about 44 feet wide The site grading work involved cuts and fills from about 1 to 3 feet
in height with finished roadway grades ranging from about 31 6 to 32 6 feet Mean
Sea Level MSL
A water line extension and laterals were also constructed along the new
roadway The water line consisted of 4 and 8 inch Polyvinyl Chloride PVC pipes
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services for
the site grading operations and infrastructure installation

SITE GRADING AND UTILITY INSTALLATION
We observed the site grading and utility installation work for the Kupahu Street
Subdivision project from to May 22 2008 The site grading work
consisted of clearing and grubbing and placement and compaction of fills on the order
of about 1 to 3 feet thick to achieve the design finished grades Ron s Construction
Corporation performed the site grading and utility installation work During the site
grading operations we obtained samples of the on site and imported materials utilized
as fill and backfill materials and transported them to our laboratory for testing The
maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test
results are summarized on Plate 3
The fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to above optimum
moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry
density as determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557 In the roadway areas the fill
and backfill materials within 2 feet of the finished subgrade were compacted to a
minimum of 95 percent relative density
During the site grading and utility backfilling operations periodic field density
tests were performed in general accordance with ASTM D 2922 Nuclear Gauge
Method The compaction test results are summarized on the attached
Plates 4 1 and 4 2 These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and
on constructed fills and backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were
estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and
elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods
used Areas assessed as unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum
compaction was obtained and or approved by our field representative based on visual
observation and hand probing
Our services consisted of part time field observation of the contractor s fill
placement subgrade preparation and compaction procedures and confirmation of
subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above grading operations
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the site grading and utility installation work for the
above referenced project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this
report were performed in conformance with the general intent of the recommendations
