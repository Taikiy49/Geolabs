design and construction of the site grading and foundations
for the proposed single family residential development

SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed development is located along the western side
of Salt Lake Boulevard and is bordered by Makalapa Access
Road on the north the H 1 Interstate Highway viaduct on
the west and Makalapa Elementary School on the southeast
The upper southern three fourths of the site is gently
sloping towards the north and is covered with a light to
moderately heavy growth of shrubs The lower northern
portion of the site is covered with a heavy growth of Haole
Koa A small stream traverses this lower area trending
generally in an east west direction The division between
the upper and lower portions of the site is delineated by
a bank varying in steepness between 4 1 and 3 1 H V

Portions of the site are covered with piles of rubbish
There are several existing utility easements crossing
through the site There is a 30 foot wide energy corridor
easement along the western side of the property A 40 foot
wide Navy utility tunnel easement and a 10 foot wide water
easement extends in an east west direction under the south
ern corner of the site There is also an existing 10 foot
wide sewer easement which crosses through the western portion
of the site generally in a north south direction
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed plan is to clear and grade the 4 acre site for
a single family residential development with the necessary
access roadways through the site
The preliminary available grading plan indicates that the
southern three fourths 3 4 of the site will be cut up to
10 feet while the northern one fourth 4 will be filled
up to 10 feet to create a total of 27 terraced lots
In addition a 5 5x10 foot concrete box culvert structure
is proposed to realign the existing stream flow
Most of the existing sewer line will be realigned under the
proposed subdivision access roads

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The project site is located on the western portion of the
Salt Lake Aliamanu complex of the Honolulu Volcanic ser
ies This complex consists of several post erosional vol
canic vents
As the molten magma ascended through these vents it reacted
with the high groundwater in this area This interaction
created explosive eruptions depositing low cones of consol
idated ash material This consolidated volcanic ash tuff
is locally refered to as mudrock
In the lower portion of the site the tuff has been eroded
away and exposed aliuvial deposits consisting of clayey silts
and silty clays

Soil Conditions
The field exploration disclosed two relatively different
subsurface soil conditions on the site In the upper area
competent tuff mudrock was encountered at very shallow
depths less than 1 foot In the lower area a very stiff
alluvial deposit consisting of about 8 to 9 feet of silty
clay over clayey silt to the depths drilled was encountered
Groundwater was encountered in Boring No 7 at 19 feet below
the existing ground surface
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration encountered relatively competent sub
surface soil conditions at the site From a soils engineer
ing point of view the site is feasible for the proposed
development provided our recommendations are followed
Site Grading
The preliminary grading plan provided for our study indicates
that the grading in the upper portion of the site will be
cuts up to 10 feet or less The grade will be raised in the
lower portion utilizing fills up to 10 feet in thickness
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1039 00
P 6
The excavated on site tuff material generally less than
8 inches in size may be used for these fills
Prior to filling the site should first be cleared of vege
tation debris and other unsuitable materials It should
then be scarified and recompacted to 90 of its maximum dry
density established by ASTM D 1557 test method In the case
where the base of the fill will rest on competent tuff
these scarifying and recompaction operations may be omitted
Fills should be placed in about 10 inch maximum horizontal
loose lifts and compacted to obtain 90 of the above speci
fied maximum density
Fills placed on slopes having grades steeper than 5 1 H V
should be keyed and benched into firm existing ground
If required imported fill material should be non expansive
and tested by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
Very stiff alluvial soils were encountered in the lower por
tion of the site therefore it is our opinion that major
settlement problems are not anticipated for fills over this
area
Most of the existing stream will be realigned and the aban
doned section will be filled To reduce underground erosion

Utility Trenches
Tuff formation was encountered at or near the surface ele
vation throughout the upper portion of the site Therefore
moderate to hard excavation is anticipated and some special
excavating techniques such as hoerams may be necessary to
excavate the utility trenches The hard excavation will be
particularly important for the relocated sewer line where
trench depths of up to 30 feet are anticipated
Proper backfilling of the trenches is important to minimize
surface ground settlement along the utility alignments

On site material generally less than 6 inches in size may
be utilized as backfill All backfill should be compacted
to 90 of its maximum density as determined by ASTM D 1557
Slopes
Slope ratios of 2 1 H V can be used for fill slopes util
izing the on site materials and select imported materials
Cut slopes of 1 1 H V are recommended in competent tuff
mudrock
For cut and fill slopes greater than 20 feet in vertical
height 8 foot drainage benches should be placed at mid
height of the slope
Surface water should be diverted away from the top of the
slope and slope planting utilized to reduce erosion
Foundations
The field exploration disclosed that the site is underlain
by a tuff formation mudrock near or at the existing surface
in the upper areas and very stiff alluvial deposits in the
lower area
For the proposed light residential structures spread foot
ing foundations or slab on grade may be used Slabs should
be set back from the tops of the slopes a minimum distance

P 9
of 5 feet Post footings located close to the tops of slopes
should be embedded a sufficient depth to obtain a minimum
horizontal set back distance of 5 feet from the slope face
Post footings away from the tops of slopes should be placed
a mimimum of 12 inches below the finish grade
For the light loads anticipated an allowable bearing pres
sure of 2500 p s f can be used for footings resting on the
tuff or compacted fill
There are several existing utility lines below the site
Foundations place over these lines will be affected by the
existing trench backfill Therefore the competency of these
trench backfills should be investigated during the site grad
ing operations to determine if some overexcavation and recom
paction of the backfill is required
Surface water should be diverted away from building founda
tions
Slab on grade should be placed over a 4 inch layer of base
course compacted to 95 of its maximum dry density The
thickened edge around the slab on grade should extend a mini
mum of 8 inches below the adjacent ground surface
To protect against moisture damage of the flooring material
a polyethylene moisture barrier should be considered

Box Culvert and Drainage Channel Wall Foundation
A 5 5 by 10 foot wide box culvert approximately 165 feet
long is planned in the lower portion of the site to chan
nelize the flow from the existing stream The box culvert
will extend from near Salt Lake Boulevard up to the exist
ing 30 foot wide energy corridor easement along the western
boundary of the side approximately 165 feet An open chan
nel section is planned for the remaining portion through
the energy corridor
The bottom of the box culvert will be placed close to the
existing ground elevation next to the existing stream The
borings encountered very stiff alluvial soils in this area
The bottom of the open channel walls will bear on the exist
ing stream floor level Field observations indicate that
stiff alluvial soils will probably be encountered at shallow
depths
An allowable bearing value of 3500 p s f can be utilized
in the very stiff alluvial soils

Weepholes should be provided through the channel walls to
reduce the build up of hydrostatic pressure behind the walls

STANDARD GRADING SPECIFICATIONS
The work under this section includes clearing and grubbing
preparation of fill areas placement and compaction of fill
and removal and backfilling of underground structures These
specifications are of a general nature and may be superseded
by specific requirements or recommendations contained within
the soils report
I
Il
Clearing
All areas within contract limit lines shall be cleared of
trash debris organic matter and all other deleterious
materials These materials shall be removed and disposed
offsite
Preparation of Fill Areas
A
Loose surface soils shall be removed to expose compe
tent natural soils Localized soft pockets shall be
undercut and backfilled with compacted select mate
rials
All surfaces shall be inspected and approved by the
soils engineer prior to the placement of any fill
After approval the surface shall be scarified to a
depth of six inches brought to near optimum moisture
content and compacted as herein specified unless the
base of the fill will rest on competent tuff
IV
2
D Where the existing surface exceeds a slope of 5 1
horizontal to vertical the ground surface shall
be keyed and benched into competent soil to bond
the fill to the existing ground surface
E Subsurface drains may be required as directed by the
soils engineer where springs or zones of seepage are
encountered
Fill Materials
Material for fill shall consist of on site soils or
approved import material Import material shall be sub
mitted to the soils engineer for testing and approval a
minimum of 5 working days prior to its proposed delivery
to the site Fill material shall contain less than 5
organic matter and other deleterious material and shall
not contain rocks of lumps in excess of eight inches 8
in diameter
Placement and Compaction of Fill
A The 111 shall be placed in horizontal layers which
when compacted will not exceed nine inches 9 in
thickness Each layer shall be spread uniformly and
blade mixed to attain uniformity of material and
water content within each layer
3
Rock fragments or boulders less than eight inches
8 in diameter may be utilized provided they are
not concentrated in any one area and that there is
sufficient fine grained material to fill the voids
between the rocks
Rocks greater than eight inches 8 in diameter
shall be removed from the fill unless specifically
approved for placement in the fill by the soils
engineer
All f111 shall be compacted to a minimum of 90
percent of the maximum dry density established by
ASTM Test Designation D 1557 unless otherwise
specified in the soils report
The compaction equipment may be sheepsfoot vibra
tory or pneumatic tire rollers suited to the soil
type and capable of producing the specified results
in an expedient and efficient manner The equipment
shall be in good working condition fully ballasted
and self cleaning
The compaction shall be accomplished in a systematic
and uniform manner over the entire fill area to pro
duce a uniformly compacted fill
VI
4
G Fill material placed in an unsatisfactory condition
and not in accordance with these specifications
shall not be approved and shall be removed or reworked
until the required density is obtained
H The soils engineers shall be provided unrestricted
access to the fill to perform the compaction tests
Removal and Backfilling of Underground Structures
Underground structures such as cesspools cisterns
septic tanks wells pipe lines etc shall be removed
as directed by the soils engineer Backfilling of the
excavations shall be in accordance with these specifi
cations
seasonal Requirements
A No fill shall be placed during unfavorable weather
conditions When interruption of work is due to
heavy rain the previous fill surface shall be
approved before resumption of earthmoving operations
B The contractor shall maintain the surface of the fill
so that it will be free draining and will not pond
surface water
