Dear Mr Beesley
This report summarizes our earthwork observation and testing services performed
by Okland Construction for the Na Hale Kumu Area A Townhouse project on the Island of
Oahu Hawaii
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project consisted of demolishing the existing Buildings 1 through 8 and
constructing eight new townhouse buildings The new buildings are two story structures
The general vicinity of the project site is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
EARTHWORK OPERATION
Okland Construction was the general contractor for the project covered by this report
Our observation and testing services during the site work performed by Jayar Construction
Inc for the project occurred in March 2019 through July 2020
CRSA

Hawaii California
Fill and backfill materials generally consisted of excavated materials from the project
site and some imported materials In general fill materials consisted of materials less than
3 inches in maximum particle size Fills and backfills required to raise the grades were
generally placed in level lifts of about 8 inches in loose thickness moisture conditioned and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction The compaction requirement
for the last lift of fills was increased to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction under
areas subjected to vehicular traffic
During the earthwork operations samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D1557 Modified Proctor and the test results
are summarized on the attached Plate 2
Field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations on an
as need basis to confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures
and soil conditions were noted A total of 217 field density tests were performed using the
nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM D6938 Compaction test results are
summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 9 These tests were performed on the prepared
ground surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills Test locations and elevations were
estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes and topographic
features These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the
degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked
until the specified minimum compaction was obtained
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the earthwork operations during construction of the
Na Hale Kumu Area A Townhouse project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period
covered by this report were performed in general accordance with the intent of the project
plans and specifications and our geotechnical engineering recommendations presented in
the referenced report dated and subsequent recommendations
LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities occurring
simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork may have been
performed without our observation However the level of earthwork observation and testing
services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some of the geotechnical risks to
the project It should be recognized that there are variations in the accuracy and statistical
repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the earthwork construction and
materials Our services were performed in accordance with generally accepted standards
of geotechnical engineering practice no warranty is expressed or implied Provision of
construction monitoring by an engineer is not insurance nor does it guarantee construction
of any type The contractor shall retain responsibility for the quality of the work for adhering
to the plans and specifications and for repairing defects regardless of when they are found
