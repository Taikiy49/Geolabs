PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our work on the project was to obtain an overview of
the subsurface conditions at the currently proposed quarry site by
geophysical exploration methods in order to provide data pertinent to the
development of the subject site as a potential quarry site The general
location of the site and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location
Map Plate 1
The scope of our exploration consisted of the following tasks and work
efforts
1 Mobilization of a refraction seismograph with operator to the
project site from our office in Honolulu
2 Performance of four 4 seismic refraction surveys on the project
site to obtain geophysical data relating to the thickness of
overburden and density of the rock occurring at relatively shallow
depths below the proposed quarry site
3 Coordination of the field exploration and reconnaissance of the
proposed quarry site and vicinity by a Project Geologist from our
firn
4 Reduction and analyses of the geophysical field data for the
formulation of geotechnical engineering interpretations pertinent
to the proposed quarry site development

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Circumferential Road Paving Project is the upgrade of existing
roads and construction of new roads to provide a 40 foot paved roadway
connecting the five 5 municipalities of the island of Pohnpei in the
Federated States of Micronesia The first phase of this project is the
upgrade of the four 4 miles of gravel road from the bridge at Dau Sokele
to the vicinity of Alamoar in the northeastern portion of the island
As part of this first phase it is desired to develop a quarry site to
provide raw material for the manufacture of base course and aggregate A
potential site in the vicinity of the village of Ipwitek was selected by
the staff of the Pohnpei Transportation Authority This site has been
referred to as the Swingley Site

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located in the vicinity of the village of Ipwitek
in Madolenihmw Municipality on the island of Pohnpei The proposed quarry
site is situated to the south of the existing gravel Circumferential Road
and is a ridge between two intermittent streams
The topography of the site is generally steep and slopes down towards
the south at an average of about four horizontal to one vertical 4H 1V
The interior of the site is relatively rugged and is traversed by several
intermittent drainage courses
The site is lushly vegetated with Kelew
breadfruit bananas vines and other tropical vegetation There are also
three 3 existing Pohnpeian residences on the site
A topographic survey plan of the site was not available at the time of
our exploration however it is estimated that ground surface elevations
at the site range from about 10 to 300 feet above Mean Sea Level
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
There is little available published information of the geology of the
island of Pohnpei In general the origin of the island appears to be
similar to that of other high islands in the Pacific e g the Hawaiian
Archipelago Samoa and Truk
The island was built by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from the vents
of one or more shield volcanoes It appears that the island went through
a cycle of the early extrusion of primitive olivine basalts followed by a
sustained period of erosion Subsequent to this erosional period
volcanic activity was renewed with the extrusion of more siliceous alkalic
basalts

At Ipwitek our exploration and reconnaissance encountered a
well consolidated alluvial conglomerate which has been intruded by
basaltic dikes Conglomerate is a rock consisting of rounded waterworn
sand and gravel cemented into a competent rock mass This conglomerate
appears to date to the erosional period between the two main stages of
volcanic activity
It is believed that overburden pressure fram overlying basalt which
have now been eroded away and hydrothermal action from the intruded dikes
have contributed to the cementation of the conglomerate
Soil Rock Conditions
Our geophysical exploration encountered subsurface materials with
seismic velocities ranging from about 1 500 to 14 000 feet per second
Based on our reconnaissance of the site and our interpretation of the
seismic data the following table may be used to estimate the types of
materials encountered below the site
Velocity feet per second Material
Less than 3 000 Soil Overburden
3 000 to 5 000 Weathered Conglomerate
Over 5 000 Very Hard Conglomerate
or Dense Basalt
In general it appears that the thickness of the overburden increases
in an upslope direction i e the depth to the rock material increases
with distance from the existing road
DISCUSSION AND INTERPRETATION
In general our exploration encountered rocky material at relatively
shallow depths below the existing surface at the proposed quarry site
which could potentially be exploited as a source of aggregate Based on

the data obtained from the seismic refraction surveys the rocky material
should generally be marginally rippable to unrippable with a large
bulldozer such as a Caterpillar D 9
The conglomerate observed in outcrops on the site was relatively
massive and blasting will be needed to expedite excavation of the
material Heavy blasting may be required in the deepest cut areas since
the density or hardness of the subsurface rock appears to increase with
depth
Based on visual examination of the rocky material exposed in surface
outcrops at the currently proposed quarry site it appears that the
gravel and larger sized particles of the conglomerate are sufficiently
sound to yield aggregate and or base course The sand sized matrix
appears to be somewhat softer and may not have sufficient abrasion
resistance However the geophysical surveys indicate that the material
does become hard with depth
We anticipate that the rock material will experience a volume increase
when excavated and crushed to yield base course and or aggregate For
preliminary estimating purposes a factor of 1 1 may be used to estimate
the volume of material yielded by the excavation and processing of a unit
volume of rock from the quarry site
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and interpretations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as
refraction seismography surveys Variations of conditions between the
field data points may occur and the nature and extent of these
variations may not become evident until excavation If variations then
appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the interpretations
given in this report

The locations of the field data points were approximately determined
by compass and pace from structures and other field reference points
Elevations were approximately determined by estimation from the
Topographic Map of the Island of Ponape North Half by the U S
Geological Survey dated 1983 The physical location and elevations
referenced in this report should be considered accurate only to the
degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross sections are based upon interpolation between field
data points and may not represent actual subsurface conditions

Transportation Authority for specific application to the proposed quarry
site Swingley Site in the vicinity of the village of Ipwitek
Madolenihmw Pohnpei Federated States of Micronesia in accordance with
generally accepted geotechnical engineering principles and practices No
other warranty expressed or implied is made
The owner client should be aware that unanticipated soil conditions
are commonly encountered Unforeseen soil conditions such as perched
groundwater soft deposits hard layers or cavities may occur in
localized areas and may required additional probing or corrections in the
field which may result in construction delays to attain a properly
constructed project Therefore sufficient contingency fund is thus
recommended to accommodate these possible extra costs
