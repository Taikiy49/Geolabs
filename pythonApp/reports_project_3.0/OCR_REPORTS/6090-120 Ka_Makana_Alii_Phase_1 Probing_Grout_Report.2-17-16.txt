Probing and Grouting Operations
A foundation probing and grouting program was implemented for the Building M1
project The location of the building is shown on the Site Plan Plate 1 Pacific Ground
Systems Inc performed the probing and grouting operations in general conformance with
the recommendations presented in our report dated The purpose of the
probing and grouting operations was to reduce the potential for loss of foundation support
resulting from collapse of cavities or compression of loose zones below the building
structure
Nordic PCL Construction Inc surveyed the footing layout and marked the probe
hole locations prior to the probing and grouting operations In general the probe holes
were located at the column footings one probe hole for every footing and continuous strip
footings one probe hole for about every 10 feet of footing length The approximate probe
hole locations are shown on the Probe Hole Location Plan Plate 2 In general the probe
holes at the new building structure were drilled to a depth of about 12 5 to 14 3 feet below
the existing ground surface corresponding to approximately 10 feet below the design
footing bottom elevations
The probing and grouting operations were accomplished using hollow drill rods with
3 inch diameter drill bits with side discharge holes for water and grout discharge Each
probe hole was advanced by rotary percussion using a TEI 300HT drill mounted on a
Komatsu PC75 excavator Water was injected continuously through the hollow rods while
advancing to the target probe depth Evidence of cavities voids was observed by the loss
of water circulation with rapid receding of water into the drill hole For the probe hole
where cavities voids were not encountered the probe hole was tremie grouted through the
drill rod while the rod was being retracted The grout used for these probe holes generally
consisted of one sack of cement mixed with 7 5 gallons of water with a water cement ratio
of about 0 70
Several probe holes did not return grout during the initial grouting even though
there was water return during drilling For these probe hole locations that did not return
grout during the initial grout application PGS placed a 1 5 inch PVC pipe in the probe hole
to maintain the integrity of the open hole and to reduce the potential for cave in prior to
additional grout application Additional probe holes were also drilled in the proximity of
these probe holes to better delineate the lateral extent of the potential voids and or
cavities These probe holes were subsequently backfilled with either cement grout or
sand cement grout batched by PGS on site and injected at low pressures until the grout
level was above the footing bottom elevation
Our field representative observed the probing and grouting operations and recorded
the probe time grout time and approximate grout take A total of 100 probe holes with
an aggregate length of about lineal feet below the existing ground surface were
