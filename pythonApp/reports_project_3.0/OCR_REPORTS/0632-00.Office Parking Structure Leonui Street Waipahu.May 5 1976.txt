SUMMARY OF RECOMMENDATIONS
The subsurface exploration disclosed medium
to very loose density sandy surface soil under
lain by soft organic silt below the proposed
footing elevation
Spread or continuous wall footings of limited
size can be designed for a maximum bearing
pressure of 2 000 pounds per square foot For
higher structural loading pile foundation should
be considered
INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this investigation is to define the soil con
ditions at the proposed building location and to verify the
allowable soil bearing pressure at the excavated footing
location
SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed project site is located on Leonui Street Waipahu
Oahu Hawaii The northern side of the property is next to the
service road to Farrington Highway The total development site
is about 251 x 151 feet The proposed building is located at the
southern end of the property approximately 125 feet wide by 75
feet deep

At the time of this boring investigation the project site had
been cut down to its rough grade At the proposed building
location trenches for the retaining wall foundation had been
excavated down to elevation 20 feet and slab subgrade area
was at about elevation 21 feet Unbraced vertical cut about
4 to 5 feet high was made at the existing northern bank for
retaining wall construction At the southern ends of the
retaining wall 1 to 2 feet high cut existed
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
The proposed building is a 3 story slab on grade reinforced
concrete structure Office is planned to occupy the lower
two stories and the top floor will be used as parking area
Retaining walls will be required at the north east and west
sides of the building in order to have the ground floor lowered
to Leonui Street level

Subsurface Condition
The subsurface exploration disclosed that the already graded
building site is blanketed with about ten to seventeen feet
of medium to very loose density silty and sandy soil layers
Two to eight feet of dark gray soft organic silt and soft
brown clay mixed with organic silt layers were found beneath
the sandy layer Stiff to hard brown clayey silt was noted
underlying the organic layer at a depth about seventeen to
twenty one feet below the ground surface
At the southern end of the proposed building location expansive
clay pockets were noted at the ground surface
Groundwater was observed in the borings at about elevation 14
DISCUSSION RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The subsurface exploration indicated that the building site is
blanketed by a sandy silt soil layer of medium density at the
bottom of footing excavation The density of this surface crust
graded to loose condition near the water level Below this
soft organic silts and loose sand layers were encountered down
to a depth of about 20 feet where stiff clayey silt was located
Spread and wall footings bearing on the medium density sandy
silt layer at the existing ground surface will provide adequate
support for 2 story high lightly loaded structure For heavily
loaded structure which can not take too much differential settle
ment pile foundation may be considered

Foundation
For the on site soils spread and line footing foundation
up to 10 feet wide can provide an allowable bearing pressure
of 2 000 pounds per square foot without excessive settiements
For large mat foundation exceeding 10 foot width this aiiow
able bearing pressure should be reduced to one half or 1 000
pounds per square foot
This reduction is due to the fact that vertical pressure
at the underlying loose strata is a function of the widti
footing Therefore in order to have a permissible vei
in the lower loose strata without causing excessive setiiciic
proposed footing size should be iimited not to exceed 1 feet fo
spread footing and 6 foot width for wall footing

To reduce foundation structural loading the heavier live
load parking deck could be placed on the ground floor while
the lighter live load office can be placed above the ground
second and third floors
Settlements
Based on the above allowable bearing pressure and footing
Sizes the calculated total settlement for the structure
should be less than one inch with a differential settlement
of less than 3 4 of an inch
To reduce distress by foundation settlements slab on grade
should be separated by control joints from columns and walls
and away from direct footing contact
Slab On Grade
The clayey surface soil at the southern end of the proposed
building location is highly expansive when it is compacted
and remolded For slab on grade the clayey soil encountered
below the base course layer should be undercut and replaced
by a minimum of 18 inches of non expansive compacted backfill
The backfill material can be selected sandy soil obtained from
Other part of the site The backfill should be placed in 6 inch
compacted layers to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density
established by ASIM Designation D 1557 721 Prior to placing
this granular backfill the subgrade soil should be scarified
to a minimum of 6 inches wetted to above its optimum moisture

Groundwater was noted at a depth about 6 feet below the existing
ground surface and it is necessary to install a 4 inch layer of
capillary break base course under the slab
Slab on grade with floor coverings should be protected by a
plastic vapor barrier The plastic sheet should be covered
by a l inch layer of sand to help concrete slab curing and
to protect the plastic membrane from puncturing
Retaining Wall
Retaining walls should be designed to resist active lateral
loads equivalent to a fluid pressure of 40 pcf if on site
sandy soils are utilized as backfill The backfill should be
compacted between 90 to 95 maximum density Over compaction
behind the wall can increase the lateral earth pressure above
the design value
A friction factor of 0 45 can be utilized for determining sliding
resistence of the retaining wall foundation
A subsurface drainage system should be incorporated to prevent
the build up of excessive hydrostatic pressure behind the wall
Earthwork
Site grading will involve cutting down about two feet of the
expansive surface clay soils at the southern end of building
location and replacing it with non expansive sandy backfill
under the slab area

The surface soils at the graded building area is loose and
have a non uniform density The upper six inches of the
existing ground surface should be scarified brought to
near optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum
of 90 of its maximum dry density Ramps should be provided
from the slab area down into the foundation trenches in order
to allow the compactor to work in the footing area
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the foundation excavations be inspected
prior to the placement of the concrete or reinforcing to assure
that adequate bearing has been obtained and that the subsurface
conditions are consistent with the findings of our subsurface
investigation
To minimize differential settlement concrete slabs and first
floor walls supported on grade should be delayed as much as
possible preferably after the upper floors are completed
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon
the above inspections

APPENDIX A 1
FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by
drilling 4 borings utilizing truck mounted equipment
The depths of all the borings terminated at 18 5 to 21 0
feet below the existing ground surface The locations of
the borings are shown on the enclosed Site Plan Plate l
The soils were classified by visual and textural examination
in the field by our engineer who continuously supervised the
drilling operations The classifications were verified by
visual inspection and testing in the laboratory All soils
were Classified in accordance with the Unified Soil Classi
fication System A graphic presentation of the soils encoun
tered is presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 4
Soil samples were obtained by driving either a 2 4 inch 1 D
ring samplers or a standard penetration sampler with a 140
pound hammer free falling a distance of 30 inches The blow
counts to drive the sampler the last 12 inchés are shown on
the logs of borings at the appropriate sample depth

APPENDIX A 2Z
LABORATORY TESTING
Moisture content determinations and Atterberg Limits were
made on selected samples as an aid in the soil classification
and in evaluating soil properties The results of these tests
are presented on the Log of Borings at the appropriate sample
location
Three swell tests of one inch thick ring sample were conducted
on near surface samples for measuring the swell potential The
swell test results are summarized on Plate B l
A modified proctor compaction test was performed
on a surface sample of the on site soil to evaluate the suitabi
lity of the soil as fill and pavement support The results are
given on Plate B 2
Direct shear tests were performed on selected samples to evaluate
the strength characteristics of the encountered soils The test
results are shown on Plate B 3
In addition Torvane Shear tests were conducted on undisturbed
ring samples The test results are shown on the boring logs
at the appropriate sample location
To determine the consolidation characteristic of the on site
soil a total of two consolidation tests were performed The
consolidation curves are shown on Plates B 4 and B 5
