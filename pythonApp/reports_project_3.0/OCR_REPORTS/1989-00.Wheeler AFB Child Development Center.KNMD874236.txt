INTRODUCTION
This report represents the results of our soil and foundation
exploration performed for the site grading and foundation design of the
proposed Wheeler Child Development Center at Wheeler Air Force Base Oahu
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to examine the subsurface soil
conditions at the site in order to obtain data for the formulation of
engineering recommendations pertinent to the design of the site grading
and foundations of the proposed structure

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is situated in Wheeler Air Force Base near Wahiawa
Oahu Hawaii The general location of the site and its vicinity is shown
on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The site is at the southwest corner of Wright Avenue and Warhawk
Street and is bounded by the two streets and Buildings 822 and 824 The
site is relatively level and is currently occupied in part by the existing
Building 823

It is currently proposed to demolish the existing Building 823 and to
construct a one story masonry structure having plan dimensions of
approximately 100 by 150 feet on the site The proposed structure will be

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
The project site is located in the central portion of the island of
Oahu on what is known as the Schofield Plateau The island of Oahu was
formed by two basaltic shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau Waianae was
the older of the two and became dormant first The Schofield Plateau was
formed when the lavas of Koolau banked and ponded against the eroded
slopes of Waianae
Since deposition the lavas forming the plateau have been subjected
to chemical weathering which has resulted in the formation of deep
residual soils and saprolites on the surface of the plateau
Subsurface Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by
drilling and sampling three 3 borings to depths ranging between 16 0 and
21 0 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate locations of
the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general the borings encountered residual soils consisting of very
stiff to hard reddish brown and brownish red clayey silts to the maximum
depths drilled A thin approximately 1 5 feet thick layer of fill was
encountered at the surface in Boring 1 Groundwater was not encountered
in the drilled borings at the time of our exploration However
laboratory testing indicates that the on site soils have high in situ
moisture contents

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The subsurface exploration indicates that generally competent
foundation bearing soils underlie the site
The very stiff to hard residual soils should provide adequate support
without excessive settlement The laboratory swell tests indicate that
the on site soils have low to moderate expansion potential
Slabs on grade may be utilized
It is our opinion that the proposed project is feasible provided our
recommendations are followed
Foundation Support
It is our opinion that shallow spread or continuous wall footings
bearing on the in situ very stiff to hard clayey silt residual soils may
be used to support the proposed structure
An allowable bearing value of 3500 p s f may be utilized for
foundations bearing on undisturbed very stiff natural soils Footings
should be embedded a minimum of 24 inches below the lowest adjacent finish
grade If any foundations bear on the new structural fill the allowable
bearing value should be reduced to 2500 p s f
Settlements for foundations bearing on the very stiff residual soils
or compacted structural fill are anticipated to be on the order of less
than one half of an inch total and one quarter of an inch differential
Site Grading
The details of the site grading are not currently available
However it is assumed that some minor amounts of grading work will be
required

Prior to the commencement of any earthwork all areas within the
grading limits should be cleared of all vegetation and deleterious
materials The resulting grub and spoil material should be disposed of
properly off site or stockpiled for use in landscaping Any soft weak
or yielding areas disclosed during the clearing operations should be
over excavated to firm ground and the resulting excavation should be
backfilled with well compacted engineered fill
Generally the on site clayey silt soils are suitable for use as
engineered fill If additional imported material is required the
material should be non expansive granular select borrow less than 3 inch
in size and capable of forming a dense stable mass when compacted The
material should be tested by the geotechnical engineer to determine that
its physical characteristics are suitable for the intended use prior to
its transport to the project site
All fills and structural backfills should be placed in level lifts
not exceeding eight 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a
minimum of 95 of the maximum dry density established by ASTM Test Method
D 1557 78
Slabs on Grade
It is anticipated that the floors of the proposed building will be
concrete slabs on grade Due to the high in situ moisture contents of the
on site soils it is recommended that a 6 inch layer of 3B Fine gravel be
provided below the slabs as cushion fill and also as a moisture capillary
break
In order to prevent future moisture infiltration and subsequent
damage to the floor coverings an impervious moisture membrane should be
incorporated below the slabs An inch of moist fine sand should be placed
above the membrane to help concrete curing

The slab subgrade should be kept moist and protected from drying
prior to concrete placement
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comment
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this review
is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for
misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the site
grading foundation excavation and on grade slab preparation phases of the
work This is to observe compliance with the design concepts
specifications or recommendations and to allow design changes in the event
that subsurface conditions differ from that anticipated prior to start of
construction The recommendations given in this report are contingent
upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
