Princeville Golf and Tennis Center Phase 1
Clubhouse Building Excavation and Foundation Construction
As requested we have been observing and testing the earthwork at the
above referenced project Recently excavation of the clubhouse building
has commenced and at near basement slab subgrade some areas of soft and
wet soils have been encountered Representatives of Goodfellow Brothers
and Rovens Tanaka have requested that Geolabs Hawaii submit
recommendations to address those areas of soft saturated soils at footing
and slab subgrades
As the excavation for footings proceeds a representative from
Geolabs Hawaii will be in attendance to evaluate the competency of the
bearing soils encountered at specific locations In the event that the
exposed bearing soils are determined to be incompetent it is recommended
that the area be over excavated to an additional two feet in depth lined
with a Mirafi type fabric and backfilled with two feet of 3B fine or
similar gravel
It is our opinion that the slab subgrade should be cleared of soft or
loose spoils prior to the introduction of cushion material It is not
necessary to over excavate in these areas unless extraordinary conditions
necessitate such a procedure We recommend that a self compacting
material such as 3B fine gravel be used as the cushion material

Recently we have discussed the difficulty in achieving proper
compaction of placed fills excavated from both the clubhouse building and
borrow areas adjacent to the project site As revealed in our
above referenced report the moisture contents of the native soils in this
area ranging from 40 to 60 per cent are considerably higher than the
ASTM Test Method D moisture contents which range from 32
to 36 per cent We advised that modified grading techniques would be
required due to the moisture content of the subsoils to be used as fill
and the relatively high rainfall in the area
It is our opinion that the on site soils are suitable as structural
fill as long as they are dried first to a lower moisture content and the
fill lift thickness be controlled to allow for achieving a minimm of its
85 maximum dry density per ASIM Test Method D 1557 78 During our
attendance at the project site it was noted that spreading of soils
excavated from both borrow sites upon previously placed fills which were
not adequately dried and compacted was performed Our understanding with
the contractor was that at some time in the future these areas would
require re working over excavation or complete removal prior to the
addition of further lifts of new fill so that proper compaction of
underlying soils would be realized It is important that the previously
placed fills must be demonstrated to be competent before further fill
placement could resume

Modified Procedures for Structural Fill Placement
Princeville Golf Clubhouse
As requested we have been involved in the observation and testing of
mass grading procedures and structural fill placement at the
above referenced project site Due to the wet weather conditions and high
in situ moisture content of the soils being utilized for the earthwork
construction much difficulty has been encountered in the attempt to meet
the minimm specifications as set forth by Geolabs Hawaii in regards to
satisfactory placement of structural fills In light of this situation
we have been requested to review the existing specifications in an attempt
to accommodate the continued performance of earthwork associated with the
building construction
It is our opinion that the following revised recommendations entail a
risk for settlement of placed fills where they may be utilized with
subsequent potential for deleterious effects upon structures associated
with these placed fills We wish to emphasize that this risk is to be
assumed by Princeville Development Corporation in as much as we are
proposing the revised recommendations at your request to expedite the
completion of the project
In specific regards to the backfill to be placed in the closure strip
outside of the basement walls we advise that the soils should be placed
in lifts not to exceed twelve inches in depth and compacted with a
sheepsfoot type roller The duration of compaction should be determined
on site by the representative of the soils engineer but for estimating
purposes we can initially assume eight to ten passes per lift The soils
to be utilized should be as dry as possible and the work should not be
performed in wet weather conditions
