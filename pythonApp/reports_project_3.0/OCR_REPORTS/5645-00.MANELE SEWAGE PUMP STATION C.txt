SECTION 1 0 GENERAL
1 1 Introduction
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the Manele Sewage Pump Station C project within the Manele District
on the Island of Lanai Hawaii The project location and general vicinity are shown on
the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings from our field exploration and presents our
geotechnical recommendations derived from our analyses These recommendations are
intended for the design of foundations retaining structures slabs on grade and site
preparation only The findings and recommendations presented herein are subject to
the limitations noted at the end of this report
1 2 Project Considerations
The project involves the construction of the new Sewage Pump Station C In
addition the project also involves the installation of a new force main from the new
sewage pump station C to the existing Manele WWTP and a new dual 6 inch force
main rerouted to the new sewage pump station C We anticipate that the proposed
pump station will consist of a generator building an underground structure to house
pumps and a concrete pad to support equipment
Based on the site grading plan provided the existing grades at the project site
vary from about 222 to 246 feet Mean Sea Level MSL We anticipate that site
grading consisting of cuts and fills up to 4 feet will be needed to achieve the design
grades Deep excavation and backfill may be required for the new underground
structure installation
1 3 Purpose and Scope
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the surface and
subsurface conditions at the project site to develop a soil rock data set to formulate
geotechnical recommendations for the design of foundations retaining structures and
site preparation Our work was performed in general accordance with our fee proposal

SECTION 2 0 SITE CHARACTERIZATION
2 1 Existing Site Conditions
The project site is within the Manele District on the Island of Lanai Hawaii The
project site is about 360 feet north of Hulopoe Street and approximately 1 200 feet
north of the existing Manele WWTP as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Based on the field observation and topographic map the project site slopes
down from northeast to southwest with existing ground elevations ranging from about
Elevation 222 to 246 feet MSL
2 2 Subsurface Conditions
We explored the subsurface conditions by drilling and sampling one test boring
designated as Boring No 1 extending to a depth of about 35 feet below the ground
surface The approximate boring location is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Our boring indicates that the project site is generally underlain by a thin layer of
medium stiff silty clay to a depth of about 2 feet below the ground surface The surface
materials were generally underlain by basalt formation with clinker extending to the
maximum depth explored of about 35 feet below the ground level
We did not encounter groundwater in the boring at the time of our field
exploration However it should be noted that groundwater levels are expected to vary
with seasonal rainfall storm surge conditions time of year and other factors
Detailed descriptions of the materials encountered and water levels observed are
presented on the Log of Boring in Appendix A Results of the laboratory tests performed
on selected soil samples are presented in Appendix B

3 2 Excavation
Our field exploration disclosed that the project site is generally underlain by
basalt rock at shallow depths below the existing ground surface In general the basalt
rock may be excavated with normal heavy excavation equipment such as ripping with
large bulldozers However deeper cuts will require the use of hoerams or chipping The
above discussions regarding the rippability of the surface materials are based on our
visual observation of the existing rock formations and field data from the boring drilled
Contractors proposing to bid on this project should be encouraged to examine the site
conditions and the boring data to make their own interpretation
A shoring system may be required during the excavation operation
The adequacy and safety of the shoring installation should be made the sole
responsibility of the contractor His her representative who should be required to be
continuously present on site during excavation and construction work will have the best
opportunity to promptly observe changing conditions during construction such as
unforeseen subsurface soil conditions loose zones inappropriate construction
sequence or techniques etc which may adversely affect shoring stability

Retaining structures should be well drained to reduce the potential for build up of
hydrostatic pressures A typical drainage system for site retaining walls would consist of
1 cubic foot of permeable material such as open graded gravel ASTM C 33 No 67

3 6 Slabs On Grade
As previously mentioned the project site is generally underlain by a layer of
clayey materials of about 2 feet thick To reduce the potential for future structural
distress resulting from swelling of the subgrade soils we recommend properly preparing
the slab subgrades and capping with select granular fill
In general we recommend providing a minimum of 6 inches of structural fill
material below the building slab areas The structural fill should also extend beyond the
building perimeter a minimum of 24inches The structural fill should be
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a
minimum of 90 percent relative compaction The structural fill should be well graded
from coarse to fine with particles no larger than 3 inches in largest dimension and
should contain between 10 to 30 percent fines passing the No 200 sieve The material
should have a laboratory CBR of 20 or more and a maximum swell of 1 percent

3 7 Underground Utility Lines
We envision that new utility lines may be needed In general we recommend
providing granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of open graded gravel ASTM C 33
No 67 gradation below the pipes Free draining granular materials such as open
graded gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation should also be used for the initial trench
backfill up to about 12 inches above the pipes to provide adequate support around the
pipes It is critical to use this free draining material to reduce the potential for formation
of voids below the haunches of pipes and to provide adequate support for the sides of
the pipes Improper trench backfill could result in backfill settlement and pipe damage
The upper portion of the trench backfill from a level of 12 inches above the pipes
to the top of the subgrade or finished grade may consist of the on site clayey materials
or imported granular materials The backfill should be moisture conditioned to above the
optimum moisture placed in about 8 inch loose lifts and mechanically compacted to no
less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for appreciable future

3 8 Drainage
The finished grades outside the sewage pump station should be sloped to shed
water away from foundations and slabs to reduce the potential for ponding It is also
advised to install gutter systems around the building structures and to divert discharge
away from the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near the
foundations and slabs should also be avoided Planters next to foundations should be
avoided or have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce the potential for significant
water infiltration into the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil
saturation and subsequent loss of strength The foundation excavations should be
backfilled properly against the walls or slab edges immediately after the concrete have
set to reduce the potential for appreciable water infiltration into the subsurface
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and should be maintained to
drain surface water runoff away from the foundations and slabs

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
We explored the subsurface conditions at the project site by drilling and sampling
one boring designated as Boring No 1 extending to a depth of about 35 feet below the
existing ground surface The approximate boring location is shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2 The boring was drilled using a truck mounted drill rig equipped with continuous
flight augers
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our field geologist who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified
Soil Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representations of the
materials encountered are presented on the Log of Boring Plate A 1
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained from the boring in general
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel
Sampling of Soils by driving a 2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same
hammer and drop The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third
6 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of
Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Core samples of the rock formation and or boulder materials encountered at the
site were obtained using diamond core drilling techniques in general accordance with
ASTM Standard Practice D 2113 Diamond Core Drilling for Site Investigation Core
drilling is a rotary drilling method that uses a hollow bit to cut into the rock formation
The material left in the hollow core of the bit is mechanically recovered for examination
and classification
Recovery REC is used as a subjective guide to the interpretation of the relative
quality of rock masses Recovery is defined as the actual length of material recovered
from a coring attempt versus the length of the core attempt For example if 4 feet of
material is recovered from a 5 foot core run the recovery would be 80 percent and
would be shown on the Logs of Borings as REC 80

The Rock Quality Designation RQD is also a subjective guide to the relative
quality of rock masses RQD is defined as the percentage of the core run that is sound
material in excess of 4 inches in length without any discontinuities discounting any
drilling induced fractures or breaks If 2 5 feet of sound material is recovered from a
5 foot core run the RQD would be 50 percent and would be shown on the Logs of
Borings as RQD 50 Generally the following is used to describe the relative quality
of the rock based on the Practical Handbook of Physical Properties of Rocks and
Minerals
Very Poor
Poor 25 50
Fair 50 75
Good 75 90
Excellent 90 100
The rippability of a rock mass is a function of the relative hardness of the rock its
relative quality brittleness and fissile characteristics A dense rock formation with a
high RQD value would be very difficult to rip and would probably require more arduous
methods of excavation
