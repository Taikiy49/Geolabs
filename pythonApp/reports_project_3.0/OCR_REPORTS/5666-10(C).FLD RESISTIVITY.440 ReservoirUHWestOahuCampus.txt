was conducted along the transmission main alignment for the 440 Reservoir University of

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The site is on the northern side of the H 1 Freeway and northeast of Palehua Road
in the Kapolei area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii We understand that it is planned to
construct two 2 0 Million Gallon MG water reservoir tanks on the lower eastern slopes of
Puu Kapu ai a cinder cone on the southeastern flank of Waianae Volcano A transmission
main and appurtenances are also planned for the project
The transmission main will be a 24 inch diameter pipe line The transmission main
traverses downslope through agricultural lands in a south to southwesterly direction from
the reservoir site After traversing through agricultural lands the transmission main
crosses below Kaloi Gulch The transmission main traverses southeasterly on
Palehua Road and crosses the H 1 Freeway It should be noted that the transmission
main crossing the H 1 Freeway will be installed as part of the North South Road project
therefore the transmission main crossing the H 1 Freeway is not part of our scope of work

Volume 3 1991 The scope of our work consisted of the following tasks and work efforts

FIELD RESISTIVITY TESTING
Geophysical survey equipment i e resistance meter was mobilized to the site on
for the field survey The geophysical survey was completed on
In general our field work was performed in overcast conditions with
generally dry to damp surface conditions
Fifty seven soil resistivity surveys were conducted at a maximum of 300 foot intervals
at 19 locations designated as R 1 through R 19 along the transmission main alignment
The soil resistivity surveys were performed by using the in Pin Method and an
AEMC 4610 Ground Resistance Tester The method utilizes four surface probes or pins
electrodes to evaluate the average resistivity an indication of corrosivity of the underlying
soil mass The average resistivity values of the soil mass extending from the existing ground
surface to the test depth was assessed by using this method at the approximate survey
locations are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The soil resistivity surveys were performed at the existing ground surface along the
proposed alignment of the transmission main The average soil resistance along the
proposed alignment was evaluated at each survey location with the electrodes aligned at
equal spacing of generally 4 to 10 feet The depth of penetration of a resistivity sounding is a
function of the electrode spacing

The resistance meter AEMC 4610 Ground Resistance Tester impresses a voltage
between the outer electrodes causing an electrical current to flow between them The
voltage drop between the inner electrodes is then measured by a sensitive built in voltmeter
As a result the soil resistance in ohms can be found directly on the dial The soil resistivity
in ohm cm which is directly related to both resistance and spacing can then be computed
The evaluated soil resistances the computed resistivity values and the corresponding
corrosion ratings are presented on Plates 3 1 through 3 3 of this report Graphical
presentation of the soil resistivity values along the proposed transmission main alignment is
provided on Plate 4 It should be noted that the soil resistivity at the site might be influenced
by existing underground structures temperature weather conditions and other factors
FINDINGS AND DISCUSSIONS
Resistivity is generally recognized as one of the most significant soil characteristics
with regard to the corrosivity of the soil to buried metallic objects In general the lower the
resistivity the greater the potential for corrosion of the buried metallic structure Conversely
the higher the resistivity the less likely the soil will contribute to corrosion to metallic objects
Based on the resistivity values and the corrosion ratings of the subsurface materials at
the site the subsurface materials at the site may be classified as moderately corrosive to
very corrosive for buried metallic structures In general the following Corrosion Ratings may
