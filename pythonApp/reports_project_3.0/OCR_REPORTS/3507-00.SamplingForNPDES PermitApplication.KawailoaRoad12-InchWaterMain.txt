PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Kawailoa Road project site is located in the Kailua Beach Park area of Lanikai
on the windward side of the island of Oahu Hawaii as shown on the Project Location Map
Plate 1 It is proposed to construct a new 12 inch water main and associated
appurtenances at the site The proposed water main will be approximately 1 400 feet in
length Ground surface elevations along the relatively flat site range from about 5 to 11
feet Mean Sea Level MSL
Based on the preliminary plans for the project the pipeline invert will generally be
located between elevation 1 and 4 feet MSL Therefore it is anticipated that brackish
groundwater may be encountered during excavation for the new water main Based on the
current design concept dewatering of the excavation along the pipeline alignment will likely
be needed to facilitate the construction
It is intended that the water from the dewatering operations will be discharged to the
City and County storm drain system Therefore a permit under the National Pollution
Discharge Elimination System NPDES will be required for this discharge In order to
obtain general baseline groundwater data for the preparation of the permit application it
is desired to sample and test the groundwater underlying the subject site

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our work on this phase of the project was to provide the drilling
monitoring well installation and groundwater sampling and testing services necessary to
obtain groundwater quality baseline data for the preparation of the NPDES permit
application Our scope of work generally consisted of the following tasks and work efforts
1 Procurement of excavation permits and utility clearances as well as field
toning for underground utilities
2 Mobilization and demobilization of a truck mounted drill rig and operators to
and from the project site
3 Coordination of traffic contro during the field exploration
4 Drilling and installation of one groundwater monitoring well to a depth of
about 26 5 feet below the existing ground surface
5 Logging of the boring and supervision of the monitoring well installation by
a Geologist from our firm
6 Development of the monitoring well
7 Collection of groundwater samples from the well by a Geologist from our firm
approximately 16 days following development of the monitoring well The
chain of custody documentation and transportation of the water samples to
the laboratory were also performed by our Geologist
8 Preparation of a brief report summarizing our work on the project and
presenting our findings
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is situated near the Kailua Beach shoreline area on the windward
side of the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are
shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The proposed water main will be located
beneath Kawailoa Road in an existing residential area fronting Kailua Beach Park The
Kailua Beach shoreline and Pacific Ocean is located approximately 600 to 800 feet to the
north of the project area The project site is also bounded by Kaelepulu Stream which is
located to the northwest at about Station Stream terminates at the Pacific
Ocean along the Kailua Beach shoreline

Field Exploration
The drilling well installation and well development was conducted on December 18
1995 The water samples were collected and transported to the analytical laboratory on
January 3rd approximately 16 days after the well was installed
Drilling of the monitoring well was accomplished using a truck mounted drill rig
and associated drilling and sampling tools were washed by steam cleaning prior to the
drilling of the borehole In addition the sampling tools were washed with a Liquinox
detergent solution prior to each soil sampling event
Subsurface Conditions
The monitoring well was installed on the unpaved makai shoulder of Kawailoa Road
near Station 7 50 of the new water main alignment The approximate location of the
monitoring well designated as MW 1 is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Subsurface materials were examined and classified in general accordance with
ASTM Standard Practice D 2488 84 by a Geologist from our firm Soil samples were
obtained from the drilled boring in accordance with ASTM Standard Method D 1586 84
sampler with a 140 pound hammer free falling 30 inches
In general the boring encountered medium dense to dense coralline sand with
some cemented gravel extending to a depth of about 26 5 feet below the ground surface
Prior to the collection of the water samples on January 3rd the groundwater level was
measured at a depth of about 5 9 feet below the surface at 1155 hours Based on the
estimated ground surface elevation of about 7 feet MSL the groundwater table
corresponded to an elevation of about 1 1 feet MSL
Detailed descriptions of the soil materials encountered in the monitoring well are
presented on the Log of Boring Plate A 1 of Appendix A
Groundwater Conditions
Shallow groundwater conditions were encountered during the drilling and monitoring
well installation Sixteen days following the monitoring well installation the groundwater

Monitoring Well Installation
After drilling to a depth of about 26 5 feet below the ground surface the monitoring
well casing was lowered into the borehole through the annulus of the hollow stem augers
The well casing consisted of 2 inch O D Schedule 40 flush threaded PVC pipes Twenty
bottom cap made up the lower portion of the well Approximately 3 feet of blank 2 inch
O D Schedule 40 flush threaded PVC pipe comprised the upper portion of the well The
blank well casing extended upward to within about 6 inches of the ground surface and was
capped with a padilocked expansion plug The well was completed at the surface with a
traffic rated flush mounted well cover
The well was backfilled with a No 3 filter sand pack consisting of clean uniformly
graded kiln dried Monterey Sands which should be chemically stable within the
subsurface environment to reduce potential sorption from the groundwater An
approximately 1 foot thick bentonite seal was placed above the screened section and
hydrated to seal the monitoring well An approximately 7 foot thick cement grout seal was
placed above the bentonite layer and the traffic rated well cover was then cemenied in
place The bentonite seal was placed to avoid contact with the underlying groundwater
table which is estimated to be approximately 5 to 6 feet below the existing ground surface
A sketch showing the monitoring well detail for the project is presented on Plate 3
The groundwater samples from the monitoring well was collected on January 3rd
approximately 16 days following well development The static groundwater table was
measured and recorded prior to the well being purged by bailing a minimum of three well
casing volumes from the well prior to the collection of the samples Groundwater samples
were collected and transported in a chilled condition to an analytical laboratory for
analyses Results of the laboratory tests quality control data and the chain of custody
forms are presented on Plates B 1 through B 4 of Appendix B
In general the test results indicate the reporting limit which refers to the detection
limit of the laboratory equipment The reporting limit is dependent on the testing equipment

LIMITATIONS
The discussions submitted in this report are based in part upon information obtained
from the field monitoring well Variations of conditions beyond the monitoring well may
occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident until
construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to
reevaluate the variations
The location of the field monitoring well is approximate having been estimated by
taping from references indicated on the preliminary undated drawings received from Belt
Collins Hawaii on The elevation of the monitoring well is also
approximate having been estimated by interpolation from elevation points on the same
drawings The physical location and elevation of the monitoring wells should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the field monitoring well
depict the approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual
transition Water level data from the field monitoring well were collected at the time shown
on the graphic representations and or in the text of this report These data have been
reviewed and interpretations made in the preparation of this report However it must be
noted that fluctuation may occur due to variations in tides rainfall temperature and other
factors
