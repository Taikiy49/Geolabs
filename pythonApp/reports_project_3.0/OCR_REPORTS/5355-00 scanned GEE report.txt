PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our geotechnical engineering exploration was to obtain an overview
of the surface and subsurface conditions at the project site to develop a soil rock data set
for the formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations pertaining to the
proposed Keystone wall To accomplish this we conducted an exploration program
consisting of the following tasks and work efforts
1 Mobilization and demobilization of a truck mounted drill rig and operators to
the project site and back
2 Drilling and sampling of three borings to depths of approximately 29 5 to
41 feet below the existing ground surface
3 Coordination of the field exploration and logging of the borings by a field
geologist from our firm
4 Laboratory testing of selected samples obtained during the field exploration
as an aid in classifying the materials encountered and evaluating their
engineering properties
5 Analyses of the field and laboratory data for the formulation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertaining to the proposed retaining wall
6 Preparation of this letter report summarizing our work on the project and
presenting our findings and recommendations
7 Coordination of our overall work on the project by a project engineer from
our firm
8 Quality assurance of our work and client design team consultation by a
principal engineer from our firm
9 Miscellaneous work efforts such as drafting word processing and clerical
support
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration and Logs of Borings are presented on
Plates 4 through 6 Laboratory testing was performed on selected soil samples obtained

FIELD EXPLORATION
Our field exploration consisted of drilling and sampling three borings designated as
Boring Nos 1 through 3 extending to depths of about 29 5 to 41 feet below the existing
ground surface The borings were drilled using a truck mounted drill rig equipped with
continuous flight solid stem augers and coring equipments The approximate locations of
the borings drilled at the site are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on the Boring Log Legend Plate 3 Graphic
representations of the materials encountered are provided on the Logs of Borings
Plates 4 through 6
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained from the borings drilled in
general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of
Soils by driving a 3 inch OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling
30 inches In addition some samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel
Sampling of Soils by driving a 2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same
hammer and drop The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third
6 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of
Borings at the appropriate sample depths

We understand that it is proposed to build a new retaining wall at the Pinch Point
along the Kawela Camp Road for the Kawela Camp Road Improvements project Based
on the current grading concept walls retaining a fill condition ranging between about 2 and
10 feet high will be required for the project In addition the retaining wall will need to retain
the fill materials that are supporting the pavement structures Based on these conditions
we recommend the use of a segmental retaining wall system such as a Keystone
retaining wall system Detailed discussion of these items and our geotechnical
engineering recommendations for the retaining wall design are presented in the following
sections of this report

Keystone Retaining Wall
The Keystone retaining wall system is a composite wall system which utilizes high
density polyethylene or other reinforcing elements to reinforce the backfill zone and
improve the shear strength of the reinforced soil zone This composite system essentially
forms a gravity wall structure with an ability to tolerate significant total and differential
settlements In addition Keystone retaining walls are also desirable due to the flexibility of
the wall ease of construction high load carrying capacity and economy
Design of the retaining wall system will need to take into consideration both the
external and internal stability of the structure In evaluating external stability the retaining
wall must satisfy four stability conditions 1 bearing failure 2 translational sliding
3 overturning stability and 4 overall slope stability Geotechnical design parameters to
evaluate these stability conditions are presented in the following subsections Some of the
geotechnical parameters necessary in evaluating the internal stability of the retaining wall
are presented in the Reinforced Fill and Backfill Materials subsection
Keystone Retaining Wall Foundations
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered at the project site we
recommend that an allowable bearing pressure of up to 3 000 pounds per square
foot psf be used for design of the retaining wall bearing on the on site soils This
transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic forces
As stated previously approximately 4 feet of fill material was encountered in our
borings Due to the uncertainty of the uniformity and consistency of the subsurface
fill layer we recommend that the base of the wall will be placed a minimum of
2 feet below the existing ground surface to bypass most of the fill Therefore the
retaining wall should generally be embedded a minimum of 24 inches below the
lowest adjacent finished grade In addition the footing should be extended deeper
to obtain a minimum 8 foot setback distance measured horizontally from the
outside edge of the footing to the face of the slope for sloping ground conditions
The wall subgrades should be compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative
compaction to provide a firm and unyielding base Soft and or loose soils
encountered at the wall subgrades should be over excavated to a minimum of
24 inches The over excavation should also extend a minimum of 24 inches
laterally beyond the front face of the walls The resulting over excavation should
be backfilled with select borrow
Lateral loads acting on the structures may be resisted by frictional resistance
developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil and by
passive earth pressure acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation
system A coefficient of friction of 0 3 may be used for the retaining wall
foundation bearing on the on site soils Resistance due to passive earth pressure

Overall Slope Stability
We have evaluated the overall slope stability of the wall structures planned for the
project Based on our analyses the factor of safety for short term and long term
stability of the Keystone retaining wall is at least 1 5 which is the minimum factor
of safety normally recommended
Based on our slope stability and external stability analyses a minimum base width
to wall height ratio of 0 7 is recommended for the retaining wall structures planned
at the project site
Reinforced Fill and Backfill Materials
We recommend that the reinforced fill material for the retaining wall consist of
imported select granular materials The material should be well graded from
coarse to fine with no particles larger than 3 inches in largest dimension The
material should also contain less than 15 percent particles passing the
No 200 sieve The material should have a CBR value of 25 or higher and a swell
potential of 1 percent or less when tested in accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 1883
In addition the reinforced fill material should have an angle of internal friction of at
least 34 degrees when tested by the standard Direct Shear test ASTM D 3080
The sample to be tested should be compacted to 90 percent relative compaction at
moisture contents above the optimum
Reinforced fill materials should be placed in level loose lifts not exceeding 8 inches
in loose thickness and be compacted to at least 90 percent of the maximum dry
density established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 Test Method at moisture
contents above the optimum
Site Grading
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed Vegetation debris deleterious material and other
unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site Soft and or
yielding areas encountered during clearing and grubbing below areas designated to
receive fill should be over excavated to expose firm material and the resulting excavation
should be backfilled with well compacted fills The excavated soft and or organic soils
should be properly disposed of off site and or used in landscape areas where appropriate

The over excavated subgrades finished subgrades and areas designated to
receive fills should then be scarified to a minimum depth of 8 inches moisture conditioned
to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of
90 percent relative compaction However areas under the future retaining wall should be
compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to

drainage may require over excavation of the soft areas and replacement with
well compacted fill The need for over excavation due to soft subgrade soil conditions
should be evaluated by a representative from Geolabs in the field
Fill Materials
General fill materials required to raise the existing ground surface to the proposed
finished subgrades may consist of the excavated on site soils provided that they
are free of vegetation deleterious materials and rock fragments greater than
3 inches in maximum dimension
Imported fill materials should consist of non expansive select granular material
such as crushed coral or basalt The material should be well graded from coarse to
fine with no particles larger than 3 inches in largest dimension The material should
also contain between 10 and 30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve The
material should have a laboratory CBR value of 20 or more and should have a
maximum swell of 1 percent or less when tested in accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 1883
Imported fill materials should be tested by Geolabs for conformance with these
recommendations prior to delivery to the project site for the intended use

optimum moisture placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness
and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction However the fill
materials within the retaining wall footprint should be moisture conditioned to at
least 2 percent above the optimum moisture placed in level lifts not exceeding
8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative
compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil
