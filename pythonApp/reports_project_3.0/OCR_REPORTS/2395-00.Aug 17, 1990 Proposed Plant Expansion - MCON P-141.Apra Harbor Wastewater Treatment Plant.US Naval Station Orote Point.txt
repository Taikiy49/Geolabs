INTRODUCTION
A report presenting the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed and our geotechnical recommendations for the proposed expansion of the
existing Apra Harbor Wastewater Treatment Plant in Guam Mariana Islands was
previously issued on

It is proposed to expand the existing Apra Harbor Wastewater Treatment Plant in
phases to meet the growing needs at the U S Naval Station Orote Point on the island of
Guam Mariana Islands

Biotower
The proposed biotower will be a cylindrical tank approximately 68 feet in inside
diameter and about 20 feet high above the proposed finished grade at 17 0 feet elevation

four 4 to 6 feet of fill is required at the structure site

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The project site is located on the western central portion of the island of Guam the
largest and southernmost of the Mariana Islands The approximate location of the project
site is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The foundation of the island is composed of volcanic rocks including pyroclastic
materials which were extruded during early to mid Tertiary time Volcanic activity ended
during the Oligocene epoch
Following the cessation of the volcanic activity extensive coral algal reef deposits
were laid down on the island from the Miocene through Pleistocene epochs
Tectonic uplift has raised these old reef deposits and subsequent marine activity
created lagoonal environments where fine grained sediments accumulated During the
recent century development of the military facility has shaped the existing land by grading
and filling
Subsoil Conditions
The subsurface soil conditions at the proposed plant expansion site were explored
by drilling and sampling four 4 borings to depths of 61 5 to 66 5 feet below the existing
ground surface The approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2
The exploration encountered a thin surface fill crust four 4 to five 5 feet thick
of medium dense to dense coral sand and gravel over soft to medium stiff clays and
loose to medium dense sand and gravel deposits predominantly of lagoonal origin Coral
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2395 00 Page 10
reef detritus and formation were encountered below the lagoonal deposits at
approximately 15 feet below the existing ground
The lagoonal deposits particularly the clay layer are highly compressible under
structure loads Generally the lagoonal layer is about seven 7 to ten 10 feet thick
The clay layer has a variable thickness and appears to be thickest at the Boring No 4
which is the location of the proposed biotower
The gravelly reef detritus underlying the lagoonal deposits has generally medium
to high density The reef formation below the detritus layer also has generally medium
to high density It appears that the formation grades locally denser at approximately 55
to 60 feet below the existing ground indicating possible cementation in the coral growth
Groundwater Level
During our field exploration the highest groundwater level was measured in Boring
No 2 at the approximate depth of 8 5 feet below the existing ground surface or at the
approximate elevation of 2 3 feet MSL
The lowest groundwater level was measured in Boring No 1 at the approximate
depth of 10 5 feet below the existing ground surface corresponding to the approximate
elevation of 1 6 feet MSL
Due to the proximity to Apra Harbor and the Pacific Ocean it is likely that the
groundwater levels measured in the borings during our field exploration may not reflect
the highest possible levels and the groundwater level at the project site will be influenced
by tidal fluctuations and storm surge conditions

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on the configurations and embedment depths of the proposed structures
and the operating wastewater loads we believe that mat and footing foundation supports
may be used
It is Our Opinion that an adequately thick mat foundation or tremie concrete pad
should be used to increase the weight of the deeply submerged new clarifier to offset the
groundwater buoyant forces
We recommend that the sites of the new biotower and the new sludge thickener
be surcharged first to reduce the potential of significant ground settlements under the
weight of the new structures and or the site fill required
To reduce ground settlement due to the weight of new fills we recommend that
a raised structural floor be used in lieu of site filling to support the proposed chlorination
building on the thin fill crust
Sheetpile walls should be installed along the portions of the two 2 existing sewers
adjacent to the biotower site to reduce settlement due to the site filling and surcharging
at the biotower site
Excavations are anticipated for the construction of the new embedded structures
and the new sewer lines We recommend that continuous and interlocking steel sheetpile
walls be used to support the excavations Lateral bracings may also be needed to
support the sheetpiles for braced excavations such as in sewer trenches

In addition an adequate efficient dewatering system will be required to maintain
the safety and the stability of the excavations below the groundwater level
Mat Foundations Below Unsurcharged Ground
Except for the proposed biotower and thickener a significant volume of subsoils
will be excavated at the sites of the other proposed structures We recommend that mat
foundation support be used for these deeply embedded structures to take advantage of
the reduction in earth loading on the underlying subsoils resulting from the excavations
Since these underground structures will be embedded to various depths below
grade and thus may or may not be submerged below groundwater level two types of mat
foundation may be used
Type Mat
Based on our foundation analyses the new structures which includes the biotower
pump station the secondary facilities building and the solids contactor and
reaerator will generally not be embedded below groundwater table As a result
these structures will not be subject to significant buoyant forces and will impose
only minimal bearing stresses on the underlying subsoils due to the relief of
significant earth overburden pressure by excavation
We recommend that these new structures be supported by mat foundations of
adequate thickness and strength to resist structural loadings such as punching
shear bearing stress etc from columns walls and equipment as deemed
appropriate by the project structural engineer
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2395 00 Page 13
Type Il Mat
The tank floor of the proposed secondary clarifier will be submerged to 2 0 to
7 75 feet elevations below groundwater level The bottom of the mat foundation
below the tank floor will be submerged even deeper below groundwater
Therefore significant buoyant uplift forces will act upon the structure especially
when the tank is empty and under high tide or storm surge conditions
We recommend that an adequately thick mat foundation be used to increase the
weight of the proposed tank structure to resist the buoyant forces Based on our
estimate a mat foundation in excess of 4 feet thick may be needed A thinner mat
may be considered provided the combined total dead weight of the structure and
the mat foundation is sufficient to resist the buoyant uplift forces
Alternatively an adequately thick tremie concrete pad may be poured initially to
Stabilize the excavation bottom prior to the construction of the clarifier tank The
tank may then be supported by a thinner mat foundation which is attached to the
tremie concrete pad
It is Our opinion that the actual thicknesses of the mat foundation and the concrete
pad if needed for the proposed clarifier tank should be determined by the project
structural engineer who can more accurately estimate the weight of the structure
and the buoyant uplift forces based on the design structural configuration of the
tank

Subgrade Preparation
Various foundation subgrade conditions may be encountered at the different
bottom elevations of the embedded structures The bottom elevations of some of
the structures may also vary such as the proposed biotower pump station
For these reasons it appears impractical to predict the specific foundation
subgrade which may be encountered below every locations at the proposed
Structure sites based on the boring data Therefore the following subgrade
preparation recommendations are provided only as a general guideline
Mat foundation subgrades in sand and gravel subsoils above groundwater level
should be recompacted to 90 of the maximum dry density per ASTM Test
Method D 1557 78 prior to foundation construction Below groundwater level a
minimum 1 foot thick cushion of ASTM C 33 No 67 gravel is recommended on the
sand and or gravel subgrade
The one foot thick gravel cushion on sand and or gravel subgrade may be
deleted if a tremie concrete pad is poured on the excavation bottom below
groundwater level such as for the proposed clarifier tank
If wet clays and or fine grained subsoils are encountered in the foundation
subgrade a 2 foot thick working platform of ASTM C 33 No 67 gravel should be
placed below the mat foundation The thickness of the working platform may be
reduced to 1 foot if the clay and or fine grained soils can be dried and
recompacted

Other Design Considerations
Based on the subsoil conditions of relatively thin soft loose soils overlying coral
reef formation it appears that a site coefficient S of 1 5 may be used for the
seismic design of the mat foundations based on the recommendations for different
subsoil profiles presented in Uniform Building Code Table No 23 J 1988 Edition
Settlements of the embedded structures on mat foundations when they are
properly constructed are estimated to be minimal since most of the anticipated
design loadings will be compensated by the relief of overburden pressure due to
earth removal from the excavations
Resistance to lateral loads acting on the new structures may be obtained by
passive earth pressure and frictional resistance developed against the structures
An equivalent fluid pressure of 240 p s f per foot of depth may be used to
determine the passive earth pressure developed against the vertical faces of the
embedded portions of the new structures above groundwater table A frictional
coefficient of 0 4 may be used to compute the frictional resistance developed
between the mat foundation and the sand and gravel subgrade Where clay
subgrade is encountered a reduced frictional coefficient of 0 25 may be used

Excavation Method
Easy excavation of the subsoils is anticipated within the design depths of the
proposed structures The method and equipment used for excavation should be
the choice of the contractor within practical limits and safety considerations
The width of the sewer trench excavations should be kept to a minimum to reduce
the potential of long term settlements of the utility lines due to vertical earth
pressures from the trench backfill
In order to reduce the potential of ground movement or subsidence which may
damage existing adjacent structures excavated material should not be stockpiled
within 15 feet of the excavations

Even with good construction procedure some movement of the sheetpile walls

Dewatering
Dewatering of the deep excavations for the proposed structure and sewerline
construction will be necessary where the existing groundwater level is close to or above
the excavation bottom Therefore we recommend that dewatering provisions be included
in the contract documents for the construction of the proposed structures including but
not limited to the secondary facilities building and the secondary clarifier
Large quantities of water may need to be dewatered from the excavations below
groundwater level since the underlying subsoils may consist of highly permeable coral
formation and loose to medium sand and gravel of coral detritus and or lagoonal origin
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2395 00 Page 26
Coral formation is generally structurally heterogeneous and can contain hard ledges soft
zones and or voids which are capable of transmitting large volumes of water
The granular subsoils are relatively permeable and may be susceptible to piping
and quick conditions Therefore dewatering operations should be done without
pumping out soil fines bumping clear water only and should be coordinated with the
excavation support so that the excavation is not jeopardized The dewatering operations
should be carried out without creating a quick condition or softening at the bottom of
the excavation
Dewatering Method
The method and equipment used for the dewatering operations should be the
choice of the contractor within practical limits and safety considerations
It is Our Opinion that the definition of Dewatering in the contract documents
should mean any works or systems required to lower the natural groundwater table
and to exclude it from the excavations to permit construction of the proposed
structures and sewers under safe and dry conditions These works or systems
should therefore include but not be limited to pumping only
It should also be realized that the dewatering system chosen will have an effect on
the penetration depth of sheeting used
We suggest that the following three 3 basic selection criteria be considered in
determining a suitable method of dewatering
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2395 00 Page 27
a The method should impose the least disturbance or damage to the existing
site improvements
b The method should maintain adequate excavation stability and provide safe
working conditions
C The method should be sufficiently flexible to allow modification to
accommodate various ground conditions
In general it appears that sump pumping inside the adequately supported
excavations may be used if the sheeting is driven to sufficient depths to prevent
piping and heaving at the bottom of the excavations
Alternatively dewatering by means of a well point or deep well system along the
outside of the excavations could be used This method could reduce the required
sheetpile penetration but should be done with care as the resulting areal
depression of the groundwater table caused by using the well point method could
induce consolidation of subsoils and subsequent ground settlements below the
existing adjacent structures Therefore recharging by injecting water back into the
subsoils to balance the natural groundwater level and minimize areal drawdown
should be considered in areas near the existing adjacent structures
The risk of causing settlements to the adjacent structures by using the well point
method will generally be higher than using the sump pumping method

Backfill
The trench excavations should be backfilled as soon as the new sewer pipes are
properly bedded and installed
The first zone of backfill extends from the bedding material to at least 12 inches
above the top of the pipes Select free draining well graded granular material is
recommended for the first zone of backfill to minimize the compaction effort
required and resultant stresses in the pipes The granular material should be
well graded less than one 1 inch in size and have no more than 10 passing
No 200 sieve Compaction by water ponding or jetting may be allowed provided
the granular material is self draining
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2395 00 Page 30
The trench backfill from 12 inches above the top of the pipes to within 2 feet of the
finished road grade may consist of the excavated on site soils except clays and
organic materials The backfill should be mechanically compacted to a minimum
90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
The trench backfill from within two 2 feet of the finished road grade to subgrade
level should consist of select borrow compacted to a minimum 95 of its
maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Precautions should be taken to prevent the flotation of pipelines before and during
trench backfilling since large quantities of groundwater and or surface runoff
during rainstorm may enter into the trench excavations in a short period of time
The uplift buoyant forces due to sudden groundwater influx and or runoff
accumulation may lift empty or even partially filled sewer pipes
If practicable the trenches should be kept free of water during backfilling _ If
backfilling under water is necessary granular material such as base course rock
or ASTM C 33 No 67 gravel which will be self compacting under water should be
utilized to reduce the potential of excessive ground settlement of the trench backfill
in the future

Pipe Bedding
Distribution of bearing stresses against the bottom of a sewer pipe has a great
impact upon the load carrying capacity of the pipe itself Pipe bedding is
therefore an important design consideration
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2395 00 Page 31
Due to the prevailing high groundwater level and the soft loose subsoil conditions
we recommend that a two foot thick crushed rock cradle be used to stabilize the
bottom of the utility trenches and provide a working platform for the pipe
installation The rock cradle material should consist of clean granular material
such as ASTM C 33 No 67 gravel and should be brought up to within six 6
inches of the invert grade or to the bottom of any concrete jacket or cradle No
mechanical compaction is required for placing the generally self compacting ASTM
C 33 No 67 gravel as the rock cradle material
Where dense sand and gravel subsoils are encountered at the bedding subgrade
above groundwater level the two foot rock cradle cushion below the pipe bedding
may be deleted
We also recommend that clean granular material such as ASTM C 33 No 67
gravel be used as the bedding material for the new utility A minimum of six 6
inches of bedding material should be placed below the new pipes The granular
bedding should extend beyond the sides of the pipes for a minimum distance of
one fourth the outside pipe diameter and also half way up both sides of the pipes
