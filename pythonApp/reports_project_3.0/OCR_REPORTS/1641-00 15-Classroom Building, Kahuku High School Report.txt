Project Consideration
It is proposed to construct a 15 classroom building namely
Building Y on the existing campus of Kahuku High School located in
Kahuku district on the island of Oahu Hawaii
W O 1641 00 I D 1906 Page 2
The proposed building will consist of two three story reinforced
concrete structures each approximately 42 by 92 feet in plan
Separated by a proposed courtyard the two structures will be connected
by walkways along the eastern end wall
Anticipated maximum column loads for each structure will be 170 to
250 kips with wall load of about 7 kips per lineal foot
Fill up to about 4 feet is anticipated for the proposed
construction
A general layout of the proposed construction is shown on the Site
Plan Plate 2
Purpose and Scope
The purpose of this investigation was to explore the subsurface
soil and water conditions at the site to obtain pertinent soil data for
the design of the foundation for the proposed structures
The main objectives were to explore the thickness and competency of
the upper crust and the underlying soil deposit for spread footing
design and to investigate the effect upon the new building foundation
due to any loose underlying layers cavities below
The scope of our investigation included drilling and sampling eight
0 to 22 5 feet below the existing ground surface Also
included were laboratory testing of the samples obtained engineering
evaluation of the field and laboratory data development of design
recommendations and report preparation
Detailed description of our field exploration and laboratory test
results are presented in the appendices of this report

Site Description
The proposed site is located on the existing campus of Kahuku High
School on Kamehameha Highway in Kahuku
The proposed structures are primarily located in a courtyard
surrounded by the existing buildings L D W H and two portable
classrooms Building L will be demolished and the two portable
classrooms will be relocated to provide space for the proposed
construction
Presently the courtyard is planted with sparse grass lawn and
several big trees Several concrete benches are also placed there for
recreational use
Due to the existing surrounding buildings utilities trees and
concrete benches all the borings except Borings 2 B and 6 B could
only be drilled at accessible locations and as close as possible to
their originally planned locations
Subsurface Conditions
The subsurface conditions were explored by drilling and sampling 8
borings to depths of 19 0 to 22 5 feet below the existing ground surface
at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general all borings encountered dense to very dense tan coral
gravel with sand and silt at shallow depths below the upper 2 0 to
5 5 foot thick stiff to very stiff brown clayey silt layer Loose
lenses were located at depths of about 11 to 14 feet in Boring 4 B and
at 5 to 7 feet and 10 to 11 5 feet in Boring 5 B In Boring 3 B a
6 foot high cavity was noted however the extent of which would have to
be delineated with further probing during construction

Discussion and Recommendations
It is our understanding that the existing ground at the proposed
site will be raised with about 3 to 5 feet of fill to the proposed
finished grades of about elevation 12 5 to 14 0 feet Our laboratory
tests indicate that the on site brown clayey silt is moderately to
highly expansive and is not suitable for use as a source of site filling
and footing utility backfill materials within the building areas We
recommend that imported non expansive select borrow such as crusher
waste be used as fill and backfill materials Spread or continuous
footing bearing directly on the non expansive structural fill may be
used to support the proposed structures
To ensure a firm uniform support below footings we recommend that
the clayey silt subgrade soil below the structural fill at and along the
new footing locations be scarified and recompacted prior to site filling
After scarification and recompaction of the footing subgrade in the
clayey silt the non expansive structural fill should then be placed
prior to footing excavation We recommend that the non expansive fill
Material be provided below the proposed new structures and pavement
areas to a distance of 1 foot away from the perimeters of the structural
and pavement areas
However the cavity in Boring 3 B and the loose lenses of the
gravelly soil in Borings 4 B and 5 B indicate that cavity and or loose
lens may also exist in the underlying soils across the whole site
These may induce excessive settlement or cause the footing to collapse
if they are present at shallow depths below the bottom of the footing

W O 1641 00 I D 1906 Page 5
Therefore we believe that the site should be probed during construction
at and along footing locations to delineate any possible cavity loose
lens existence Once delineated the cavity lens should be backfilled
with low pressure grout to ensure sufficient support to the foundations
above
Foundation Support
Based on the anticipated soil conditions site grading and design
loading we believe that an allowable bearing capacity of 3000 p s f
may be used for spread or continuous footing bearing on compacted
structural fill
Footing should be placed a minimum of 24 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grade Uplift wind or other lateral loading
condition may further increase the embedment of the footing foundation
The bearing capacity value is for total dead plus live load only
and may be increased by one third for short duration wind loading
Estimated settlements under the design loading are anticipated to be on
the order of less than one inch total and one half of an inch
differential
Resistance to lateral loads for the proposed structures can be
obtained by passive pressure and frictional resistance A frictional
coefficient of 0 4 may be used between the bottom of the footing and the
compacted structural fill In addition an equivalent fluid pressure of
300 ps f per foot of depth may be used to determine the passive
resistance
Cavity
In our exploration a 6 foot high cavity was encountered in the
dense coral layer in Boring 3 B which was drilled near the western edge
of the proposed building site The cavity was encountered from a depth
of 15 0 to 21 0 feet
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1641 00 I D 1906 Page 6
Cavities are formed in coral formation due to the presence of fresh
water streams during the growth stages of the coral which prevent the
growth of the coral Therefore it is likely that the cavity may be
part of an old stream and therefore may extend horizontally through
the site rather than being an isolated occurrence
Probing
To reduce the possibility of footing settlement due to possible
large cavities or loose lenses encountered near the footing bottom
elevation in the underlying coral formation drilled probes are
recommended at each colum location below every footing bottom and at
15 foot centers for continuous wall footings The probe holes should be
drilled with a 4 inch diameter auger drill and extend below the bottom
of the footing to a depth equals to two times the footing width or a
minimum of 10 feet
If a cavity or loose lens is suspected below a footing additional
probe holes should be made to delineate the horizontal extent and
thickness of the cavity or loose lens Voids greater than 6 inches in
diameter and loose lens thicker than 1 foot should be grouted as
recommended by the soils engineer The probing and grouting operations
should be monitored by the soils engineer to see that the aforementioned
recommendations are carried out properly and to evaluate other solutions
to properly backfill potential voids and densify the loose soil which
may occur near the footing elevations

Grouting
The following methods should be used for grouting
Grouting Cavity
The cavity should be grouted with a sand cement water mixture until
the cavity is completely filled up The low pressure grout should
be injected intermittently through drilled hole s into the cavity
in smail volume of about one fourth of the estimated size of the
cavity or maximum and a lapse time of at least 1 hour
should be allowed between successive grout injection The hardened
grout mixture is a bulb of strong and relatively incompressible
material which should fill up the whole cavity
Grouting Loose Lens
Loose lens should be grouted continuously with the same
low pressure sand cement water mixture which acts as a radial
hydraulic jack that compresses and densifies the soil underneath
the bottom of the footing The hardened grout mixture is a bulb of
strong and relatively imcompressible material
Both methods are relatively expensive but the degree of disturbance
caused to the surrounding area will be minimal
Site Grading
Fills up to about 4 feet are anticipated for the site grading of
the proposed construction The on site brown clayey silt is moderately
to highly expansive and is not suitable for use as a source of site
filling and footing utility backfill material within the building areas
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1641 00 I D 1906 Page 8
We recommend that imported non expansive select borrow such as
crusher waste or non expansive select granular materials less than 3
inches in maximum size with less than 20 passing the 200 sieve should
be used as fill and backfill materials
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limit should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed Any unsuitable
materials such as debris rubbish and vegetation should be removed and
disposed of properly off site
Prior to site filling the clayey silt subgrade soil below the
non expansive structural fill at and along each footing location should
be scarified to a depth of at least 6 inches below the subgrade
elevation moisture conditioned to optimum moisture content and
recompacted to a minimun of 95 of its ASTM D 1557 78 maximum dry
density
Loose spots encountered at subgrade level should be removed and
replaced with imported fill material compacted to a minimum of 90 of
its ASTM D 1557 78 maximum dry density
After scarification and recompaction of the footing subgrade the
structural fill should be placed prior to footing excavation in
horizontal lifts not exceeding 6 inches in compacted thickness and
compacted to a minimum of 95 of its ASTM D 1557 78 maximum dry density
The site should be graded to provide drainage of surface runoff
away from the buildings
Slab On Grade Pavement
Concrete on grade slab is anticipated for the proposed structures
and concrete pavements are planned for the walkways of the proposed
structures and major area of the courtyard where the pavement will have
a rock salt finish
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1641 00 I D 1906 Page 9
We recommend that concrete slab be placed on a 4 inch layer of 3B
Fine gravel cushion fill for both on grade slab and walkway pavement
placed on compacted structural fill
In order to protect against future moisture infiltration an
impervious moisture membrane may be incorporated below the
slab pavement The on grade slabs should be structurally separated from
wall foundations
Drywell
It is our understanding that a 2 foot diameter drywell will be
installed in the central planter within the proposed courtyard We
recommend that this drywell should extend beyond the originally planned
3 foot depth below invert of the 4 inch pipe to at least 2 feet into the
underlying coral layer One inch diameter weep holes should be provided
in this drywell at locations adjacent to the bottom of the new
non expansive select fill layer This may help to relieve excessive
trapped moisture in the fill layer and minimize the seepage of water
into the underlying expansive clayey silt
Design Review
Plans and specifications for probing and grouting site grading
foundation and pavement design should be forwarded to the soils engineer
for review and written comments prior to construction to see that the
intent of our recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the probing and grouting site grading and
footing excavation be observed by the soils engineer during construction
to see that adequate compaction has been obtained and that the
subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of our
investigation

Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site for use
If conditions encountered are different from those presented in
this report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
Investigation Limitations
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations
made in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation
engineering principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all
other warranties either expressed or implied
The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are based
on our site reconnaissance soil information from boring and laboratory
test data obtained from this soil investigation
Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encountered and cannot
be fully determined by taking soil samples Unforeseen conditions may

Laboratory Testing
Moisture content and unit weight determinations were
performed on selected samples as an aid in the classification
and evaluation of soil properties The results of these tests
are presented on the Boring Logs at the appropriate sample
depths
Six one inch ring swell tests were performed on undisturbed
and remolded samples to evaluate the swelling potential of the soils
in both natural and compacted conditions The test results are
summarized on Plate B 1
Two Atterbert Limits were performed on selected samples of
the soils to evaluate liquid and plastic limits The test results
are shown on Plate B 2
Two sieve analysis tests were performed on selected samples
of the soils to evaluate the grain size distribution The test
results are presented on Plate B 2
One laboratory CBR test was performed on a bulk sample to
evaluate the subgrade support characteristics of the soil for
pavement design The test result is summarized on Plate B 2
One direct shear test was performed on a select undisturbed
sample to evaluate the shear strength parameter of the soil The
test result is plotted on Plate B
