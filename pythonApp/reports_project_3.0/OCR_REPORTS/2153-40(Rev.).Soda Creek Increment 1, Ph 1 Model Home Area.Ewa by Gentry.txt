This revised letter report summarizes our findings and preliminary geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertaining to the house pad grading and pavement
design at the above referenced project site which was also known as the Sun Terra
Model Complex The work was conducted in general accordance with your transmittal
letter dated April 7th and our fee proposal dated April 19th
The scope of work was limited to a review of the available soils information
including the Soils Investigation Report prepared by Soils International dated April 22
1985 and the Construction Observation and Testing Final Report prepared by Soils
International dated a site reconnaissance to observe the existing
conditions sampling of the proposed road subgrade soils and our experience within
the Ewa by Gentry development No subsurface investigation test borings or test pits
were included in the scope of our design consultation services The recommendations
presented in this letter are contingent upon confirming the assumed subsurface
conditions during construction

Grading
At the on set of earthwork all areas within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris rubbish concrete slabs
crushed asphalt and other unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of
properly off site to reduce potential contamination of the excavated materials
After clearing and grubbing the exposed subgrade should be scarified to a
minimum depth of 8 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the
optimum moisture and compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction The
compaction requirements for subgrades of pavements and driveways should be
increased to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers
to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry
density of the same soil determined in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D
1557 91 Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight
corresponding to the maximum dry density
Any soft weak or yielding areas disclosed during clearing operations should be
over excavated to expose firm ground and the resulting excavation should be filled with
engineered fill moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture and compacted to
at least 90 percent relative compaction All excavated soft and or organic soils should
be properly disposed of off site Saturation and subsequent yielding of the exposed
subgrade due to inclement weather and poor drainage may require over excavation of
the soft areas and replacement with well compacted engineered fill
In general the on site materials may be used as fill materials at the house pad
area provided that they are moisture conditioned at least 2 percent above the optimum
moisture placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted
to at least 90 percent relative compaction Any boulders or cobbles larger than

Drainage
The on site surficial soils are susceptible to erosion As a result water should
be diverted away from the tops of slopes and adequate slope planting should be done
as soon as possible to reduce the potential for erosion Drainage swales should be
provided as soon as possible and maintained to drain all surface run off away from the
slab footing foundations and the edges of slopes
The finish grades outside the houses should be sloped to shed water away from
foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding Also it is advised that gutter
systems be installed around the house and that the discharge be diverted away from
the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near the
foundations and slabs should also be avoided Planters next to foundations should be
avoided or have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the
subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
foundations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation and subsequent
loss of strength or heaving of the near surface soils The foundation excavations
should be properly backfilled against the walls or slab footings immediately after setting
of the concrete to reduce water infiltration

needed to raise the existing grades We understand that excavated materials
generated during the construction of the proposed roadway will be used as fill materials
for the house pads Based on the laboratory test results the near surface soils
encountered near the proposed road subgrade at the site consist of silty clays which
exhibit a slight expansion potential when subjected to moisture fluctuations This
expansion potential will likely be increased if there is a longer lag time more than
2 days between completion of the house pad and placement of concrete
Therefore to reduce the potential for structural distress resulting from swelling
of the on site soils we recommend that a minimum of 6 inches of non expansive
capping fill material be provided below slabs on grade The non expansive capping fill
should extend at least 2 feet beyond the limits of the proposed structure
The non expansive fili material should provide a capping layer of moist soil
above the underlying potentially expansive subgrade soil thereby reducing the loss of
moisture from the clayey subgrade soils In our opinion the reduction in potential
drying and wetting of the subgrade soils should reduce the distress on the slab due to
potential heaving of the underlying expansive clayey soils when wetted
In general non expansive capping fill should consist of granular materials such
as crushed coral basalt crusher waste or cinder The non expansive capping fill
should be well graded with no particles greater than 3 inches in maximum dimension
and should have a laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR value exceeding 25 when
prepared near the optimum moisture The non expansive capping fill should be
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture and compacted to not less than
95 percent relative compaction The non expansive fill should be placed as soon as
possible after the compaction of the underlying clayey fill materials Soil subgrades
should be kept moist prior to placement of non expansive capping fill and or concrete
If shrinkage cracks appear in the subgrade the soil should be thoroughly moistened
to close all cracks and recompacted prior to capping fill or concrete placement
For more uniform support we recommend that interior slabs on grade be
supported on a 4 inch thick cushion fill layer consisting of open graded gravels such
as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation The gravel cushion layer should
be placed above the non expansive capping fill and would serve as a capillary break
To reduce moisture infiltration through the slab a plastic vapor membrane may be
considered above the cushion layer below the slab A 2 inch thick moist fine sand layer

Pavement
Based on the current design concept asphaltic concrete pavements are planned
for the roadway and portland cement concrete pavements are planned for the house
driveway In general we anticipate the vehicle loading for this project will consist
primarily of passenger vehicles with occasional light trucks The preliminary pavement
design is based on a minimum CBR value of 12 or more with swells of less than
1 percent based on our laboratory testing of the proposed road subgrade soils
As discussed earlier previous grading records indicate that 14 inches of select
borrow were placed near the east end of Pohahawai Place Therefore we recommend
that additional tests be performed to confirm or revise the preliminary pavement
sections if the exposed subgrade soil conditions are different from those assumed in
our preliminary design Based on the above assumptions the following pavement
section may be used for preliminary design purposes
