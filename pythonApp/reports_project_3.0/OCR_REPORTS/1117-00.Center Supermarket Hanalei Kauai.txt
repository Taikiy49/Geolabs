In accordance with our proposal of March 18 1982 we have per
formed a soil and foundation investigation at the above refer
enced site
The purpose of the soil engineering investigation was to
explore the subsurface soil and groundwater conditions at the
site to provide data for use in the design of the site grading
foundations and roadways for the proposed supermarket develop
ment
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests
which were used in the engineering analysis that formulated
our conclusions and recommendations are presented in the
appendix of this report
The approximate boring locations are shown on the Boring Loca
tion Plan Plate 1 and the boring logs are presented in the
appendix Detailed information regarding the field exploration
and laboratory testing are also presented in the appendices
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
SITE DESCRIPTION
The site is located in Hanalei on the extreme north shore of
Kauai Hawaii The site is bounded by Kuhio Highway on the
south the existing Princeville Shopping Center on the east and
open areas presently used as a plant nursery on the north and
west
The area is generally level except for a small narrow drain
ageway about 4 feet deep extending along the northern boun
dary of the proposed supermarket The southeastern portion of
the area is presently occupied by a portion of the shopping
center parking while the remaining area is vacant and covered
with grass
The ground surface elevation generally varies from about 260 to
220 feet above sea level

PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
The proposed building will consist of a one story supermarket
approximately 134 by 144 feet in plan with a 2 story office and
retail building attached around the eastern half of the super
market The proposed structures will have block walls and
concrete slab on grade for the first floor level with wood
construction for the second floor level Anticipated column
loads are 45 kips for the supermarket and 5 kips per foot for
the two story wall footings
A cold storage unit will be utilized in the supermarket
Driveways and parking areas are planned around the building
Minimal grading is anticipated below the proposed building Up
to about 6 feet of fill may be required to fill the existing
gully area

Soil Conditions
In general the soils encountered in the borings consisted of
reddish brown to brown clayey silt with weathered gravel The
soils were moist and generally stiff near the surface however
in Borings 2 and 4 they became wet and soft at depths of about
8 feet down to 18 to 23 feet
Groundwater level was only encountered in Boring No 4 at a
depth of 16 8 feet This may be a perched water table
CONCLUSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that the site is underlain by
generally stiff residual soil near the surface However in
some of the areas the soils become wet and soft at depths of
about 8 feet down to as much as 23 feet deep
Shallow spread footing foundation can be used to support the
proposed building with some allowance for settlement due to the
soft underlying soil layer

Foundation
Shallow spread footing foundation may be used for the proposed
one and two story structure
Due to the soft soil layer encountered at about 8 foot depth a
low allowable bearing value of 2000 p s f is recommended The
footings should be placed as high as practicable above the soft
layer and a minimum of 18 inches below the finished grade
Settlements are anticipated due to the additional weight of new
fill and foundation loads on the underlying soft soil How
ever minimal site grading is anticipated therefore settle
ments will be limited to those caused by the foundation loads
Anticipated settlements due to the foundation loads are on the
order of one inch total and half an inch differential
If a footing is located next to a utility trench it should be
extended to the bottom of the utility trench or be designed to
span over the trench to reduce footing settlement due to
settlement of the trench backfill
Slab on grade should be placed over a 4 inch layer of capillary
break material such as 3B Fine aggregate to prevent infil
tration of moisture into the floor slab by Capillary action

The thickened edge around the slab on grade should extend a
minimum of 9 inches below the adjacent ground surface
The slab subgrade soils should be kept moist and not be allowed
to dry out prior to concrete placement
The ground surface should be graded to drain water away from
the structure and foundations
Retaining and Basement Walls
Short retaining or basement type walls may be required in the
proposed structure
An allowable bearing value of 2000 p s f is recommended for
the wall footings bearing into the stiff natural ground
Well graded granular material such as base course rock sand
or equivalent should be used as backfill material directly
behind the walls This would reduce the compactive effort
required for the backfill and reduce the build up of additional
lateral pressures on the walls

We recommend that the walls should be designed to resist
lateral earth pressures as follows
Unrestrained at top equivalent
active condition fluid pressure
Restrained at top equivalent
at rest condition fluid pressure
Additional loads due to surcharge should be included
where applicable
A friction factor of 0 4 between concrete and soil can be
utilized to determine the sliding resistance of the wall
Additionally a passive soil resistance of 300 for soil can be
used However the passive resistance should be reduced by
one third when combined with the frictional resistance
A subsurface drainage system should be used to prevent the
build up of hydrostatic pressure behind the walls
Cold Storage Unit
A cold storage unit is planned for the supermarket The freez
ing temperatures developed by the cold storage unit could
initiate frost action in the subsoils
GEOLABS HAWAII
Frost action occurs when there is a combination of freezing
temperature high water level or available water source and
subgrade soils which have high capillary rise potential When
the surface of the soil is below freezing temperature upward
moisture migration from the high perched water level can occur
due to capillary action and condensation The water will then
collect and freeze to form lenses of ice in the upper part of
the ground which will displace the soil and cause heaving The
formation of the ice lenses can continue indefinitely as long
as suitable conditions exist
The on site soils consists of silts and clays which generally
have limited potential for frost action due to its low permea
bility However the on site soils have high water contents
which could provide a source of water and be susceptible to
freezing and subsequent heaving below the cold storage unit
In order to reduce the possibility of frost action below the
new cold storage unit a capillary break layer is recommended
below the unit to prevent upward migration of water and to act
as a drain for accumulated condensation from the freezer unit
GEOLABS HAWAII

A clean gravel such as 3B Fine gravel is recommended for the
capillary break material The thickness of the capillary break
layer would depend on the effectiveness of the insulation and
depth of frost penetration Generally a minimum thickness of
2 feet is recommended
Site Grading
Although minimal site grading is anticipated for the proposed
building area fills up to about 6 feet are planned in the
narrow drainageway along the northern side of the building to
create a level parking area In order to minimize footing
settlements due to the weight of future site filling we
recommend that this fill be placed as soon as practicable prior
to foundation construction
The site should be cleared of vegetation surface debris and
other deleterious material In areas to receive fill the
ground should be scarified to a minimum depth of 6 inches
brought to near optimum moisture content and then compacted to
a minimum of 85 density per ASTM D 1557 70 test method modi
fied to compact the soil Samples at a wet to dry molding mois
ture condition

After site preparation the fill should be placed in about
10 inch lifts compacted to a minimum of 85 of the above
specified maximum dry density With the high moisture content
of the fill soil in the area the 85 compaction requirement is
recommended due to anticipated difficulties in achieving 90
compaction
It is recommended that if additional off site borrow is
required it should be tested and approved by the soils
engineer prior to transportation to the site to assure that it
is suitable for the proposed construction The material should
generally be non expansive and not contain large percentages of
boulders organic or deleterious matter
Pavement for Access Roads and Parking Areas
Asphaltic concrete pavement is planned for the development
roadways and parking areas

site material the preliminary pavement design for the

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by
drilling 4 borings to depths of 16 5 to 26 5 feet below the
existing ground surface at the approximate locations shown on
the Boring Location Plan Plate 1 Truck mounted drilling
equipment was used to drill all of the borings
The soil encountered in the borings were classified by
visual and textural examination in the field by our engineering
geologist who continuously monitored the drilling operations
The classifications were reviewed by visual inspection and
testing in the laboratory The soils were classified in
general conformance with the Unified Soil Classification
System A graphic presentation of the soils is presented on
the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 4
In the borings soil samples were obtained by driving a
sampler with a 140 pound hammer free falling a distance of 30
inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the last 12
inches are shown on the logs of borings at the appropriate
sample depths
