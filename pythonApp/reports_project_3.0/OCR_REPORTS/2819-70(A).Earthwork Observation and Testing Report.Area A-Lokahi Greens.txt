SITE PREPARATION
The project site is located at Area A within the Ewa Villages development in the
District of Ewa on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project involved the partial
development of the subject parcel into approximately 96 single family housing units out
of a total of 154 units with floor plans of about 1 200 to 1 600 square feet The
96 housing units of this project are shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
The general contractor for this project is Shioi Construction Inc and the earthwork
operations were performed by C J Peterson Services The earthwork operations
generally consisted of clearing and grubbing over excavation of expansive clays and
shrinkage cracks under the building perimeter walkway and driveway areas and
placement and compaction of non expansive select granular fill material under the new
improvements as

During the earthwork operations samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor
and the test results are summarized on Plate 2 of this report
Following the clearing and grubbing operations the areas within the building and
driveway limits were generally over excavated a minimum of 2 feet below the existing
ground surface to remove the zone with shrinkage cracks Additional over excavations
were performed in localized areas to provide the recommended 3 foot thick non
expansive select granular capping fill under the building slab cushion The non expansive
select capping fill was also placed approximately 3 feet beyond the building footprint i e
to the outside edge of the perimeter walkway The over excavated subgrades were
generally moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture content
and compacted to at least 90 percent of the maximum dry density as determined in
accordance with American Society of Testing and Material ASTM Test Designation D
1557 Modified Proctor
A minimum of 3 feet of non expansive select granular capping fill material was
placed under the building slab cushion and the perimeter walkway During placement of
the non expansive select granular capping fill layer the fill materials were generally
placed in near level lifts brought to above the optimum moisture and compacted to a
minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density determined in accordance with ASTM
Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor The capping fill underneath the driveway
slabs was reduced to a minimum of 2 feet thick and was compacted to at least 95 percent
relative compaction
Periodic field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations to
confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures and soil conditions
were noted A total of 393 field density tests were performed using the nuclear probe
method in accordance with ASTM Test Designations D 2922 D 3017 The results of
the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 11 of this report
These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on compacted fills and
backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and
interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the earthwork operations during the subgrade
preparation foundation excavation and driveway construction for the above referenced
project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report were
performed in general accordance with the intent of the project plans and specifications
and our geotechnical engineering recommendations presented in the referenced report
dated and subsequent addendum dated August 5 1998

LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork may
have occurred without our observation However the level of earthwork observation and
testing services provided appear to be reasonable in reducing some of the geotechnical
risks to the project It should be recognized that there are variations in the accuracy and
statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the earthwork
construction and materials Our services were performed in accordance with generally
accepted standards of geotechnical engineering practice no warranty is expressed or
implied Provision of construction monitoring by an engineer is not insurance nor does it
guarantee construction of any type The contractor shall retain responsibility for the
quality of the work for adhering to the plans and specifications and for repairing defects
regardless of when they are found
