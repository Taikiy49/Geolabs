PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
In general the Ala Moana Center Ewa Expansion project involves expansion of the
western Ewa end of the Ala Moana Center in Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii
The project area was formerly occupied by Sears department store and mall parking and
will be converted to four levels of retail development
The new mall structure is supported on a deep foundation system consisting of
nine hundred and nineteen 919 18 inch diameter augered cast in place ACIP piles and
one hundred inch diameter micropiles The layout of the new construction is
shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
Site grading work generally consisted of cuts and fills on the order of less than
about 2 feet thick The project will also include the installation of new utility connection
backfilling around pile caps pavement construction among others The scope of work
discussed in this letter report includes observation and compaction testing during the
earthwork and infrastructure installation for the Ala Moana Center Ewa Expansion project

EARTHWORK OPERATIONS
Hawaiian Dredging Construction Company Inc HDCC is the general contractor
for the project Jayar Construction Inc performed the site grading operations pavement
construction and utility line installation The site grading work consisted of subgrade
preparation placement and compaction of fills and backfills pavement construction and
utility trench excavation and backfill
Following the demolition clearing and foundation installation operations the
excavated subgrades underneath future slab and pavement areas were scarified to a
depth of about 8 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and
proof rolled with a minimum 10 ton vibratory drum roller to obtain a minimum of 90 and
95 percent relative compaction under the building slab and pavement areas respectively
of the maximum dry density determined in accordance with American Society of Testing
and Material ASTM D1557 Modified Proctor
Fill and backfill materials required for the project generally were placed in near level
lifts brought to above the optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of 90 or
95 percent under the building slab and pavement areas respectively of the maximum dry
density determined in accordance with ASTM D1557 Modified Proctor
New utility lines i e water sewer and drain lines and utility line connections
adjacent to the project site are required for the development The bottom of some of these
utility lines and below grade structures are near or within the soft loose lagoonal deposits
encountered below a depth of about 4 to 5 feet below the existing ground surface To
reduce the potential for appreciable settlement of these piping a subgrade stabilization
layer consisting of 24 to 32 inches of No 2 Rock ASTM C33 No 4 gradation wrapped in
a non woven filter fabric Mirafi 140N was installed below the bedding layer for uniform
support
In addition it was necessary to support some of the more sensitive utility lines
specifically gravity sewer lines and heavy below grade structures on jet grouted column
foundations The jet grouted column foundations would derive vertical support primarily
from bearing on the hard coral ledges and or dense coralline detritus materials and from
skin friction along the sides of the jet grouted column foundations with the surrounding
soils Detailed discussions and field observations during the jet grouted column
installation work are presented in our report entitled Jet Grout Column Installation Report
Ala Moana Center Ewa Expansion Honolulu Oahu Hawaii dated
transmitted under separate cover
Dewatering pits excavated for the project construction were generally backfilled in
accordance with the following procedures The bottom of the dewatering pit was first
stabilized with approximately 18 inches thick of 6 inch surge rock to provide a working
platform for the subsequent backfilling work An 18 inch thick choking stabilization layer of
No 2 rock wrapped around with non woven geotextile fabric was then placed above the
surge rock layer and extended slightly above the groundwater table prior to backfilling with
the on site coralline materials compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction

It should be noted that conventional compaction testing is not applicable to the 6 inch
surge rock and No 2 rock due to the nature of the materials especially they were placed
below the groundwater table The surge rock was generally packed down by pounding
them using the bucket of the excavator and the No 2 rock layer was densified by tracking
over them using the excavator
During the site grading operations samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D1557 Modified Proctor and the test
results are summarized on Plate 2
Field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations on an
as need basis to confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures
and soil conditions were noted A total of 1 719 field density tests including retests of
failed areas were performed using the nuclear probe method in accordance with
ASTM D6938 Results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 3 1
through 3 124 of this report These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces
and on constructed fills and backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were
estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and
elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used
Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum
compaction was obtained
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the earthwork operations during the site grading
pavement and utility line construction for the above project observed and tested by
Geolabs in the period covered by this report were performed in general accordance with
the intent of the project plans and specifications and our geotechnical recommendations
presented in the above referenced geotechnical engineering report
and subsequent recommendations
LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork were
performed without our observation However the level of earthwork observation and
testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some of the
geotechnical risks to the project It should be recognized that there are variations in the
accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor
earthwork construction and materials
