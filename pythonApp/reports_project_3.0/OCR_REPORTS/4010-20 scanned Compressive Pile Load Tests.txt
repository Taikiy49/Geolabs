The readings and the load displacement curve of the static load tests are presented
on Plates 3 and 4 for Pile No and on Plates 5 and 6 for Pile No
Based on the shape of the curve of the compressive load test it appears that the total pile
head displacement of the 20 inch octagonal concrete slightly exceeded the Davisson
failure criteria at the maximum test load of 640 kips which is two times the design load of
the pile However the load test piles were capable of sustaining the maximum test load
for at least 12 hours and the rate of the pile head movement was generally stable at the
maximum test load without signs of appreciable creep
It should be noted that the total pile head displacement of the piles at the design
load of 320 kips pile design capacity was observed to be about 0 35 inches or less which
is less than the 0 5 inch total pile settlement estimated for the project Therefore we
believe that the 20 inch octagonal concrete piles Pile Nos 57 and 121 are capable of
supporting a design load of 320 kips with an adequate factor of safety and with reasonable
settlement characteristics compatible with the design of the project
The eight embedment strain gages in each of the load test piles were connected
using a multiplexer to a Geokon Micro 10 datalogger to record the strains in the eight
embedment strain gages during the compressive pile load test Analyses were conducted
on reducing the data from the embedment strain gages for evaluating the load transfer
between the concrete pile and the surrounding soils The interpreted loads at each
embedment strain gage are summarized on Plate 7 The interpreted load transfer curves
in the load test piles are shown on Plates 8 1 and 8 2 for Pile Nos 57 and 121
respectively
Based on these load transfer curves it appears that the two load test piles behaved
as friction piles in the static load test because the majority 85 percent of the maximum
test load was resisted by adhesion between the pile shaft and the surrounding soils
Based on our load transfer curves it appears that less than 100 kips 15 percent of the
maximum test load 640 kips was transferred to the tip of the load test pile

The Models 4210 and VCE 4202
Vibrating Wire strain Gages are designed for direct em
bedment in concrete The VCE 4200 is six inches long
and is commonly used for strain measurements in found
ations piles bridges dams containment vessels tunnel
liners etc The VCE 4210 is 10 inches long and is design
ed for use in mass concrete with coarse aggregates It is
extra rugged to resist bending and has large flanges to
provide greater engagement area The VCE 4202 is
designed for laboratory use and or where there are space
limitations
The gages may be positioned either by means of wires
wrapped around the body or by attachment to a rosette
which will hold the gage ata predetermined orientation in
the concrete One type of rosette is shown overleali
Strains are measured using the vibrating wire principle
A length of steel wire is tensioned between two end
blocks that are embedded directly tn concrete Deform
ations i e strain changes of the concrete mass will
cause the two end blocks to move relative to one another
thus altering the tension in the steel wire The tension is
measured by plucking the wire and measuring ils resonant
frequency of vibration using an electromagnetic coil
The Model GK 403 Readout Box used in conjunction
with the Vibrating Wire Strain Gage will provide the
necessary voltage pulses to pluck the wire and will
convert the resulting frequency reading directly into strain
units by means of an internal microprocessor The strain
reading is displayed on a 5 digit LCD
The advantage of the vibrating wire strain gage over
more cotiventional electrical resistance or semiconductor
types lies mainly in the use of a frequency rather than a
voltage as the output signal from the strain gage Fre
quencies may be transmitted over long cable lengths with
out appreciable degradation caused by variations in cable
resistance contact resistance or leakage to ground
Vibrating wire gages also have excellent long term zero
stability For measurement of dynamic strains up to 100
Hz autoresonant versions are available
Where no stress strain gages are required a sheet steel
container is available which is lined with styrofoam This
container isolates the section of conerete around the gage
from the rest of the concrete so that no stresses can fall on
the gage vet apart from this if experiences the same
environmental conditions as adjacent gages and thus pro
vides a measure of effects due to temperature moisture
autogenous growth etc
The strain gages are provided with thermistors encap
sulated in the plucking coil The thermistor enables
lemperatures to be measured Over voltage protection can
be provided in the gage and this can be supplemented by
gas tube surge arrestors at the terminal box location
Installation procedures are fully explained in manuals
supplied with the gages
