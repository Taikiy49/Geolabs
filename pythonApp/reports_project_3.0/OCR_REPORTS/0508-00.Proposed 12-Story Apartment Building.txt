INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our foundation investigation
for the proposed apartment building located at Kalo Place in
Moiliili Honolulu Hawaii The purposes of this investigation
were to explore the subsurface conditions at the site and to
provide data for use in design and construction of foundations
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is presently planned to construct a 12 story building of
reinforced concrete construction The building will be approxi
mately 30 by 160 feet in plan dimension The lower two floors

will contain perimeter parking Maximum vertical column dead
plus real live loads for the main building will be on the order
of 1000 kips and on the order of 50 kips for the parking structure
Minimal site grading operations will be required to prepare the
site for construction
SITE CONDITIONS
Surface
The site which is approximately 205 feet long and varies in
width from 106 to approximately 190 feet is located on the
south side of Kalo Place At the time of our investigation
the site was occupied by numerous wooden frame buildings
The site is fairly level and the present grade is below the
adjacent street
Subsurface Condition
Geologically the site is underlain by a coral reef formation
that has been intersected by numerous fresh water stream flows
These cavities were eventually filled with coral debris Below
the reef are decomposed silts from the volcanic flows of the
Honolulu Volcanic Series
In 6rder to explore the subsurface conditions at the site four
borings were drilled The locations of these borings are shown
on the Site Plan Plate 1 A more detailed description of the

The site is immediately underlain by a soft clayey silt fill
that varies in thickness from 1 to 5 feet Below the fill coral
reef formation was encountered to depths of 25 to 30 feet below
the existing ground surface This coral reef formation is
generally very dense and hard but contains many soft and open
areas These cavities may be filled with loose coral debris
Due to the high variability of reef formations it would be very
difficult to predict the location of these voids and loosely
filled cavities which may be due to freshwater stream deposits
cutting through the reef formation when sea level was at a
lower elevation
Found beneath the coral formation is a very stiff to hard clayey
silt layer that appears to be highly decomposed basalt formation
This material was encountered to the limits of our borings which
extended to approximately 76 feet below the existing ground surface
Residual decomposed basalt generally becomes coarse and contains
more rock particles closer to bedrock as was encountered in the
lower 20 feet of Boring 5 It is anticipated that bedrock wétl
be over 100 feet below the existing ground surface
Groundwater was encountered in all our borings at depths of 6
to 7 feet below the existing ground surface

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on our investigation the proposed structure may be
supported by a combination of spread footings and pile foundations
Spread footings may be used over dense coral formation Each
footing will have to be probed to determine the presence of
cavities or soft coral deposits beneath the individual footings
Where soft or loose materials are encountered pile foundations
will have to be used
Foundations
1 Spread Footings
It is our opinion that where the reef formation is sufficiently
dense the structure may be supported on spread footings Footings
may be designed for a soil bearing value of 5000 pounds per square
foot for dead plus real live loads and should be bottomed a minimum
of two feet below final adjacent grade All footings should penetrate
the soft upper fill material and into the coral formation A one
third increase in the bearing value may be used when considering
wind or seismic loads Maximum anticipated settlements for spread
footings on dense coral will be less than one fourth of an inch
2 Pile Foundations
Where soft conditions are encountered it is our opinion that a
pile foundation be used for that particular footing We recommend
Page 5
W O 508 00
that a 12 inch square or 16 inch octagonal precast concrete pile
be used Piles may be driven into dense coral if the soft coral
or cavities are confined to the upper portion of the coral formation
or into the very stiff clayey silt located about 25 feet below the
existing grade
Piles should be designed for loads of 60 tons each
Based on the present subsurface information the pile lengths may
vary between 30 and 40 feet Generally the shorter pile length
shall apply if the lower coral formation is dense and free of
cavities below pile tips elevation The driven pile may have
sufficient blow counts in the coral material to attain the
desired pile capacity without penetrating into the clayey silts
Otherwise longer piles may have to be driven into the clayey
silt soil for friction and end bearing support
The piles should be spaced no closer than 3 times the pile diameter
otherwise group action may reduce the pile capacities
Test piles should be driven at the site to determine order lengths
for this project We will be glad to assist you in determining
the test pile locations
We would recommend that all pile driving on this project be done
under the observation of a soils engineer

We anticipate that settlement on a driven pile would be less
than one fourth inch This does not assume group action It
is recommended that pile cap designs be submitted to the soils
engineer to evaluate the effect of group action on the pile
capacities and settlement
Lateral Pressures
Lateral resistance to wind or seismic forces can be developed
by passive soil pressures acting against pile caps and footings
and by soil friction The passive resistance of natural undisturbed
soil and compacted fill may be assumed to be equal to the pressure
developed by a fluid with a density of 250 pounds per cubic foot
The existing fill is not recommended for developing passive
pressures due to its soft condition A coefficient of 0 30
may be used between foundations and supporting soils
It is is determined that additional lateral resistance is
required in shallow footings or piles we will be available
for consultation
Slabs on grade
The existing fill is slightly expansive and in a relatively
soft condition It is therefore recommended that the upper
18 inches of subgrade be removed beneath slabs and if possible
replaced with a non expansive soil and recompacted to a minimum

of 90 of maximum density Slabs should be placed on a minimum
of four inches of granular type material
Site Preparation
After all debris and structures have been removed from the
site the ground surface should be stripped and cleared of all
deleterious material Consideration should be given to removing
a minimum of 18 inches of the upper soft existing fill and re
compacting it to at least 90 of maximum density in slab and
pavement areas If any additional fill is required this
should also be compacted to 90 of maximum density

APPENDIX
FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface soil conditions at the site were explored by
drilling four test borings utilizing truck mounted rotary
auger equipment The depths of the borings ranged from 27
to 76 feet below the existing ground surface The locations
of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
The soils and coral were classified by visual and textural
examinations in the field by our field engineer who continu
ously supervised the drilling operations The classifications
were verified by inspection and testing in the laboratory
All soils were classified in accordance with the Unified Soil
Classification System The description of coral is based on
local geologic terminology Graphical representations of the
soils and coral encountered are presented on the Boring Logs
Plate A 1A through A 1D Undisturbed soil samples were obtained
by driving a 2 4 inch diameter samplet with a 140 pound hammer
free falling a distance of 30 inches Standard Penetration
Test SPT were also taken at selected depths The blow counts
to drive the sampler were converted to SPT blow counts and these
along with the SPT blow counts are shown on the Log of Borings
at the appropriate sample depth
