PURPOSE AND SCOPE
This report presents the results of our subsurface investi
gation performed at the site of the proposed cart storage
building at the Mid Pacific Country Club
The purpose of this investigation was to explore the subsur
face soil and groundwater conditions at the project site in
order to provide soil data for use in the design of the
foundations for the proposed structure
The work was done in general accordance with our proposal
of August 6 1981
Our study consisted of 6 borings within the accessible por
tions of the proposed golf cart storage building site plus
one boring at the site of the proposed pro shop laboratory
testing and field observations at the site The results of
our field exploration and laboratory tests which were used
in the engineering analysis that formulated our conclusions
and recommendations are presented in the appendix of this
report
The approximate locations of the borings are shown on the
Boring Location Plan Plate 1 and the boring logs are pre
sented in Apppendix A

SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed structures are located on the southwestern side
of the existing clubhouse building The existing pro shop
and cart storage shed is situated between the Ist and 10th
tee areas The existing structure is about 48 feet by 53
feet in plan
The proposed cart storage building will be situated adjacent
to the western and southern end of the existing pro shop
structure This area is presently used as the ist tee
The northern half of the proposed building site slopes down
Most of the southern half of the building site is gently
sloping The ground surface adjacent to the southern end
of the building slopes down toward the south at a slope ratio
of about 1 1 H V The area is covered with concrete path
ways and grass
The proposed new pro shop is located adjacent to the southern
portion of the clubhouse This area is flat and covered with
grass
PROPOSED DEVELOPMENT
The proposed plan is to construct a new cart storage building
around the existing pro shop building The existing structure

Soil Conditions
The field exploration disclosed that the proposed cart stor
age building site is underlain by 1 to 10 feet of fill over
medium to dense dune sand deposits
The fill appears to be relatively thin in the northern and
eastern portions of the building site The fill thickness
increases in the southwestern direction Boring 7
The fill material encountered in the borings was heterogene
ous and varied from sandy silt to clay and from stiff to
loose
The underlying dune sand deposit was generally medium to
dense
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings
Boring No 4 indicated that the proposed pro shop site is
underlain by dense dune sand deposits to the depth drilled
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that the site for the new
golf cart storage building is underlain by a fill layer of
varying thickness The fill was found to be heterogeneous
with varying degrees of competency Special foundation prep
aration is recommended to provide more uniform foundation

Competent dune sand deposits were encountered at the proposed

Foundation
Fill varying from 1 to 10 feet thick was encountered below
this building site The fill appears to be thickest in the
southwestern portion of the site Boring 7
The density of the fill was also found to be highly vari
able It is our opinion that footings placed directly on
this fill material would be subject to large differential
settlements We believe that special foundation prepara
tion would be required to minimize settlements
Several other foundation alternatives such as complete
excavation and recompaction of the entire fill layer or
caisson foundations extending through the fill have been
considered However it is our opinion that partial removal
and replacement of a portion of the fill layer directly
below the footings could be considered an adequate solution
In order to provide more uniform support for the footings
we recommend that the fill material below the bottom of

Retaining Walls
Retaining walls will be utilized on the northern southern
and possibly the western sides of the proposed golf cart
storage building
Recommendations given under Foundations should be followed
for the design of the wall footings
Well graded granular material such as base course rock or
on site sandy material should be used as backfill material
behind the walls A subsurface drainage system should be
utilized behind the walls to prevent the build up of hydro
static pressure

The walls should be designed to resist lateral earth pressures
as follows
Walls unrestrained at top 35 p c f equivalent
active case fluid pressure
Walls restrained at top 50 p c f equivalent
at rest case fluid pressure
A coefficient of friction of 0 45 between the footing and
soil can be used Additionally a passive soil resistance
of 300 p c f can be used
Slab on Grade
Slab on grade should be placed over a 4 inch cushion layer
of gravel such as 3B Fine gravel to act as a capillary
break
The slab on grade should be separated from walls and founda
tions
Site Grading
Up to about 6 feet of fill is planned on the western side of
the golf cart storage building Cuts up to about 10 feet
are anticipated on the northern side of the building
The site should be cleared of vegetation debris and old
slabs prior to filling The existing ground surface should
be proofrolled to detect near surface soft pockets If
encountered the soft pockets should be overexcavated down

The on site sand and some of the Silty or sandy fill material
excavated from below the footings can be used as fill and
backfill material The soils should be tested and approved
by the soils engineer prior to placement in the fill The
fills should be placed in 10 inch maximum loose lifts and
compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density
Where the existing ground is steeper than 5 1 H V bench

point and continue up in level compacted layers in accordance
with the above structural fill requirements
Fill slopes of 2 1 H V are recommended
