Bellows AFS Storage
Tank Removal and Closure
Bellows Air Force Station
Waimanalo Oahu Hawaii
As requested we observed the earthwork operation at the bulkhead area water
treatment area and water sedimentation area during tank removal and closure at Bellows
Air Force Station The excavation of the side slope and bulkhead for the underground
storage tank involved trimming the base of a hill to expose an existing underground
storage tunnel which was protected by a massive concrete bulkhead A trench
approximately 8 to 10 feet wide and 12 feet below the existing vent pipe on the concrete
bulkhead was also excavated to remove the existing distribution line from the bulkhead
extending approximately 100 feet to the east
An area of approximately 50 feet square in dimension was excavated and leveled
at approximately 100 feet to the north of the bulkhead to serve as a water treatment area
A sedimentation pond was also excavated to a depth of approximately 3 feet below the
existing ground surface at approximately 200 feet away from the water treatment area
and along the edge of the existing runway
After removal of the pipeline and water treatment facilities the slope on the hill was
restored and the excavations were backfilled by ESP personnel with on site materials
Backfill materials were placed in 8 inch loose layers A test layer was compacted with
several passes of ESP s on site equipment and tested for consistency Field density
tests were performed using a Troxler moisture density gauge in accordance with
ASTM D 2922 91 Test Methods for Density of Soil and Soil Aggregate in Place by
Nuclear Methods The number of passes to attain 85 of the maximum dry density
determined through Test Method for Laboratory Compaction
Characteristics of Soil Using Modified Effort was recorded The number of passes were
used as a guide in ESP s backfill and compaction of the succeeding lifts
A summary of field density tests on the test layers are shown on Plate 1
