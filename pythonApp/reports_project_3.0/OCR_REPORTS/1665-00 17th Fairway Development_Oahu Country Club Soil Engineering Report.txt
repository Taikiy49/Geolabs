Soil Engineering Investigation
Proposed 17th Fairway Development
Oahu Country Club
Honolulu Oahu Hawaii
In accordance with your request we have made a study of the near
surface soil conditions at the site in order to provide preliminary data
for use in the design of the site grading work for the proposed golf
course fairway and 17th green construction The location of the project
site and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The scope of our investigation included the excavation of thirteen
13 test pits geologic observations of the existing site conditions
laboratory testing and engineering analysis
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests which we
used in the engineering analysis to formulate our conclusions and
recommendations are presented in the appendices of this report
Project Considerations
The proposed fairway and 17th green development generally consists of
clearing and grading a strip of land about 1600 feet long by 300 feet wide
for construction of a new fairway The site is located on a sloping
hillside along the western side of Nuuanu Valley adjacent to the existing
golf course fairways
Preliminary grading plans prepared by Belt Collins and Associates
indicate that the proposed site grading consists of cuts and fills varying
from 10 feet up to 40 feet high
The topography of this site is a fairly steep natural hillside that
slopes down towards the southeast at about 3 1 to 5 1 H V
Many large boulders are scattered over the existing slope
The ground elevations at the site range from approximately 380 to 530
feet above MSL
The site is currently heavily vegetated with a growth of grasses
bushes amd scattered trees In some areas trees were tilted ina
down slope direction This may indicate that surficial downhill movement
creep may have taken place in certain parts of the slope

Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by excavating 13
test pits to depths of 6 to 24 feet below the existing ground surface at
the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 Soil samples
obtained from the test pits were selectively tested in our laboratory to
further examine their geotechnical engineering characteristics
Subsurface Conditions
Geology
The site is located on the southern flank of the elongated Koolau
Volcanic Range which forms the southeastern portion of Oahu Hawaii The
range was formed during the Tertiary Period from pahoehoe and Maa
basalt lava flows from a rift zone roughly paralleling the existing
mountain trends During the late Tertiary and Pleistocene Ice Age deep
valleys including Nuuanu Valley were eroded by streams
During higher stands of the sea level in the Pleistocene the valleys
were infilled with sediments alluviated grading to the high sea level
stands Underlying these sediments are the older volcanic lava rock
deposits
The site lies on a gently sloping upland slope that consists of
coalesced fans of alluvium and colluvial sediments derived from the
salient volcanic cliffs of the Koolau Range

Discussion and Recommendations
General
Our findings disclosed that the site is underlain by thick 3 to more
than 20 feet deposits of Adobe clay soils that are highly expansive and
weak Near the lower part of the slope along the existing cart path the
Adobe clay was found to be thicker than 20 feet the maximum depth that
could be excavated with the backhoe The thickness of the Adobe clay
generally decreases in the upslope direction to about 3 to 8 feet near the
proposed limit of grading
Because the existing ground is fairly steep varying from about 3 1 to
5 1 H V the construction of cut slopes and embankments must be carefully
designed and controlled to reduce the possibility of large slope
movements
We believe that the existing inclined ground surface is slowly
creeping downhill especially where the ground surface is steeper than 4 1
H V The potential for movement increases with the steepness of the
ground surface
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1665 00 Page 6
For the fairway construction it is desired to create a gently sloping
pad against the hillside at an elevation above the present 17th fairway
level
The proposed grading scheme consists of cutting the soils in the upper
portion and filling the lower portion of the site Due to the poor
quality of the existing soils slope stability considerations are a
critical factor in the grading design
Ideally the Adobe clay soils should be removed and replaced with
select off site material or a large buttress fill extending through the
Adobe clay should be constructed at the toe of slope to stabilize the
slope However in order to reduce costs it is desired to balance the
cut and fill quantities to minimize haul costs This concept will then
require the utilization of the excavated weak clay soils and boulders in
the new embankment sections and steepening some existing upper slope areas
by cutting to generate the required embankment fill materials Based on
this scheme the calculated factor of safety for the fill slope is less
than 1 0 indicating that slope failure in the embankment fill is likely
to occur
In order to increase the factor of safety against slope failure
several design considerations or options such as the construction of a
rock buttress or revetment at the toe of the embankment section within the
steeper 2 1 lower slope to help retain the upper 5 1 compacted clay slope

Slope Stability Evaluation
Based on laboratory test results past experience on the engineering
behavior of similar clay soils and a generalized surface and subsurface
profile our slope stability analysis indicates the following
A Due to the lower shear strength behavior of the clay soils when
wet and the relatively steep 11 to 18 degreees natural ground slope
we believe the near surface soils are susceptible to the phenomenon of
slow downhill creep
GEOLABS HAWAII

B Along the most critical section A A under saturated condition
the proposed fairway grading using predominantly Adobe clay backfill
would have an unsatisfactory factor of safety 0 9 against an overall
slope failure
C Along the most critical section A A our slope stability
analysis indicates that construction of a rockfill buttress at the toe
of the clay embankment along with adequate surface and subsurface
drainage provisions would improve the overall stability of the new
embankment fill with a computed minimum facter of safety of about 1 2
This factor of safety is somewhat lower than normally used
however it is our opinion that the safety factor of 1 2 may be
adequate for the golf course fairway development with the
understanding that some downhill creep movements would occur
D Where the vertical height of the fill slope is less than 35 feet
a flatter slope ratio of 3 1 H V may be used to maintain the factor
of safety against slope failure of 1 2 Downhill slope creep
movements should be anticipated on the slope
E A cut slope ratio of 2 1 H V is recommended for slopes cut in
soil and firm weathered basalt rock Local thin layers of Adobe
clay soil may still remain overlying the underlying basalt rock or the
less expansive clay MH soils and would be prone to creep downslope

Several special construction procedures should be considered
A It would be prudent to carry out the cut and fill operations
during the dry season
B Where deep about 20 feet excavation is required staged cut and

C All excavation should be properly backfilled as soon as

Drainage
Proper drainage is essential for the satisfactory performance of the
embankment fills as excessive water will weaken the clay soils
We believe the interceptor ditch should be installed at the toe of the
proposed cut slope to minimize bearing directly on the existing
surface Adobe clay layer
This ditch would intercept most of the surface runoff above the fill
enbankment and carry the flow to a suitable outlet in the lower area
of the development
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1665 00 Page 11
Should Adobe clay still remain below the ditch bottom during
excavation for this interceptor ditch a 12 inch of over excavation
should be made and replaced with base course type gravel The Adobe
clay subgrade below the gravel layer should be kept moist to minimize
excessive ditch damage due to subsequent ground heave
As it is difficult to intercept all infiltrating water from the clay
embankment we recommend that the proposed clay fill have layers of
six 6 inch thick gravel placed at about 10 foot vertical intervals
on a subgrade sloped to drain into the rockfill buttress below These
drainage blankets would be connected to a 6 inch blanket of chimney
drain placed on the benched existing slope
A chimney drain about 12 inches wide and consisting of 3B Fine
gravel should be utilized along the benching between the embankment
and natural ground to intercept subsurface seepage Subdrains should
be provided to allow drainage from the bottom of the chimney drain
The toe area of the rockfill buttress should be graded such that
groundwater intercepted by the subsurface drainage blankets can be
diverted to a suitable outlet in the lower area of the development
A pipe drain is recommended along the toe of the rockfill buttress to
intercept seepage water infiltrating through the buttress

Site Grading
In addition to the standard grading specifications provided in
Appendix C the following special considerations are recommended
A
Our soil investigation reveals that a portion of the on site
excavation will consist of silty clays which are low to moderately
expansive In general we believe that if the clay embankment are
capped with at least 18 inches of this less expansive clay soil
the potential for future excessive swelling and shrinkage at the
surface of the proposed fairway can be reduced
Cobbles and boulders removed from the existing ground surface and
cut slope section may be used to construct the rockfill buttress
Large boulders in excess of 5 foot in diameter should be reduced
to smaller sizes
For the type II buttress a choking fill layer consisting of
mainly cobble size broken up rock fragments with gravel size
granular materials 1 2 to 8 inch sizes should be placed between
the capping top soil layer and the underlying boulder size rock
fill

C A cut slope ratio of 2 1 H V is recommended for slope cuts in
soil and rock As minor amount of rock excavation is anticipated
no differential slope ratio is given
D Clay fill slopes above the rock buttress toe fill should be
placed at an overall 5 1 H V or flatter slope ratio
F Proper type of cut and fill slope planting should be provided as
to minimize slope erosion

STANDARD GRADING SPECIFICATIONS
OAHU COUNTRY CLUB
PROPOSED 17TH FAIRWAY DEVELOPMENT
W O 1986
The work under this section includes clearing and grubbing
preparation of cut and fill areas and placement and compaction of fill
These specifications are of a general nature and may be superseded by
specific requirements or recommendations within the soils report
I Clearing
All areas within contract limit lines shall be cleared of all
vegetation debris organic matter and all other deleterious
materials These materials shall be removed and disposed
off site The on site granular cobbles and boulders shall be saved
for constructing the buttress fill
II Preparation of Fill Areas
A Loose surface soils shall be recompacted to match the density
of the natural soil Localized soft pockets shall be undercut
and backfilled with compacted select materials
B All surfaces shall be inspected and approved by the soils
engineer prior to the placement of any fill
GEOLABS HAWAII
STANDARD GRADING SPECIFICATIONS 2
IIT
C After approval the surface shall be scarified to a depth of six
inches 6 brought to near optimum moisture content and
compacted as herein specified
D Where the existing surface exceeds a slope of 5 horizontal to 1
vertical 5H 1V the ground surface shall be keyed and benched
into competent soil to bond the fill to the existing ground
surface
E Subsurface drains are required as shown on the plans and as
directed by the soils engineer where springs or zones of seepage
are encountered
Fill Materials
Material for fill shall consist of on site soils or approved import
material Import material shall be tested and approved a minimum of
seven 7 working days prior to transportation to the site Fill
material shall contain less than 5 percent organic matter and other
deleterious material and shall not contain rocks or lumps in excess
of six inches 6 in diameter
GEOLABS HAWAII
STANDARD GRADING SPECIFICATIONS 3
IV Placement and Compaction of Fill
A
The fill shall be placed in horizontal loose layers not to
exceed ten inches 10 in thickness Each layer shall be
spread uniformly and blade mixed to attain uniformity of
material and water content within each layer
Rock fragments or boulders less than six inches 6 in diameter
may be utilized provided they are not concentrated in any one
area and that there is sufficient fine grained material to fill
the voids between the rocks
Rocks greater than six inches 6 in diameter shall be used in
the buttress fills
All fill shall be compacted to a minimum of 85 percent of the
maximum dry density established by ASTM Test Designation D 1557
unless otherwise specified in the soils report
The compaction equipment may be sheepsfoot vibratory or
pneumatic tire rollers suited to the soil type and capable of
producing the specified results in an expedient and efficient
manner The equipment shall be in good working condition fully
ballasted and self cleaning
GEOLABS HAWAII
STANDARD GRADING SPECIFICATIONS 4
F
The compaction shall be accomplished in a systematic and uniform
manner over the entire fill area to produce a uniformly
compacted fill
Fill material placed in an unsatisfactory condition and not in
accordance with these specifications shall not be approved and
shall be removed or reworked until the required density is
obtained
The soils engineer shall be provided unrestricted access to the
fill to perform the necessary inspections and testing
VI Removal and Backfilling of Underground Structures
Underground structures such as cesspool cisterns septic tanks
wells pipe lines etc shall be demolished and removed as directed
by the soils engineer Backfilling of the excavations shall be in
accordance with these specifications
