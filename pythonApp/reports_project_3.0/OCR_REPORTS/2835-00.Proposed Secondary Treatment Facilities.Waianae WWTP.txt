SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The island of Oahu is composed largely of the weathered remnants of two extinct
shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae forms the bulk of
the western third of the island while the younger shield Koolau forms the majority of the
eastern two thirds of the island Waianae became extinct while Koolau was still active and
its eastern flank is partially buried below Koolau lavas in central Oahu
The Waianae and Koolau volcanic shields were built during the late Pilocene and
early Pleistocene by thin bedded basaltic lava flows The main shield building activities
ceased approximately 5 million years ago Evidence from drilled wells indicates
that the island of Oahu has subsided as much as 1200 feet since the cessation of this
early volcanic activity Macdonald and Abbott 1970
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2835 00 Page 8
During that period of subsidence coral algal reefs began to grow on the southern
coast of Oahu forming broad bays with barrier reefs across its mouth The growth of the
reefs essentially kept pace with the rate of subsidence A series of lagoons formed
behind the barrier reefs and both terrigenous and marine sediments accumulated in the
lagoons Macdonaid and Abbott 1970
During the Pleistocene Ice Age there were many sea level changes as a result
of widespread glaciation in the continental areas of the world As the great continental
glaciers accumulated the level of the ocean fell since there was less water available to
fill the oceanic basins Conversely as the glaciers receded or melted global sea levels
rose because more water was available The land mass of Oahu remained essentially
stable during these changes and the fluctuations were eustatic in nature These glacio
eustatic fluctuations resulted in stands of the sea which were both higher and lower
relative to present sea level on Oahu
The higher sea level stands caused the accumulation of deltas and fans of
terrigenous sediments in the heads of the old bays accumulation of reef deposits at
corresponding higher elevations and lagoonal marine sediments in the quiet waters
protected by fringing reefs
The concurrent growth of reefs and lagoonal sediments may have also resulted in
deposits of coral algal limestone and maris within the predominantly lagoonal sedimentary
units

The field exploration generally encountered coral formation throughout the entire

be drilled at each column location and at 15 foot centers along continuous or mat
foundations

probe hole up to the top If large cavities greater than one foot in height are
encountered during the probe drilling or if grout takes exceeds four 4 times the
theoretical volume of the probe hole a mortar like grout 1 sand to 1 cement should be
injected instead of the neat cement grout If the grout intake exceeds one half cubic yard
it should be stopped and the grout allowed to set prior to continuation of the remaining
grouting backfilling operations

Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling of
twenty six 26 borings to depths ranging between 3 7 to 77 0 feet below the existing
ground surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings
were drilled using truck mounted equipment
The material encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who continuously monitored the drilling
operations These classifications were further reviewed by visual inspection and testing
in the laboratory All soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A graphic presentation of the materials encountered are presented
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Method D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by
driving a standard penetration sampler or 3 O D Modified California sampler with a
140 pound hammer free falling 30 inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the
last 12 inches are shown on the Boring Logs at the appropriate sample depths
Core samples of rock materials encountered at the site were obtained using
diamond core drilling techniques in general accordance with ASTM Standard Practice D
2113 83 Diamond Core Drilling for Site Investigation Core drilling is a rotary drilling
method which uses a hollow bit to cut into the rock formation The rock material left in
the hollow core of the bit is mechanically recovered for examination and description
Recovery REC is used as a subjective guide to the interpretation of the relative
quality of rock masses Recovery is defined as the actual length of material recovered
from a coring attempt vs the length of the core attempt e g if 3 7 feet of material is
recovered from a 5 0 foot core run the recovery would be 74 and would be shown on
the Boring Logs as REC 74
The Rock Quality Designation RQD is also a subjective guide to the relative
quality of rock masses RQD is defined as the percentage of the core run that is sound
material in excess of 4 inches in length without any discontinuities discounting any drilling
_ induced fractures or breaks If 2 5 feet of sound material is recovered from a 5 0 foot core
run the RQD would be 50 and would be shown on the Boring Logs as RQD 50
Generally the following is used to describe the relative quality of the rock based on the
Practical Handbook of Physical Properties of Rocks and Minerals

APPENDIX C
Field Permeability Testing
Six 6 permeability tests were performed in three 3 selected borings during our
field exploration in order to evaluate the in situ permeability of the subsoil Both constant
and falling head test methods were utilized The tests were performed by advancing a
four inch 4 diameter solid casing with flushed bottom to the desired depths
The constant head test was performed by pumping water into the cased borehole
until water surface reached and remained at equilibrium for a sufficient period of time
The depth to the water level and the rate of pumping were then measured
The falling head tests were performed by measuring the rate of water level drap
after filling up the casing with water
The test data and the corresponding calculated permeabilities of the subsoils are
presented on Plates C 1 through C 6
