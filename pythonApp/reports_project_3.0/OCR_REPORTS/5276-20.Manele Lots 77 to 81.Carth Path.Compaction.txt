This letter report summarizes our geotechnical observations and earthwork testing
services for Lots 77 to 81 Retaining Wall and Cart Path Reconstruction Our work was
performed in general accordance with the services outlined in our fee proposal dated
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
High retaining walls were constructed along the rear of Lots 77 to 81 in order to
create level building pads along Hulopoe Drive Mechanically stabilized earth retaining
walls i e Anchor Block walls were used A two tier wall system was used and consisted
of a lower wall about 10 to 12 feet high and an upper wall about 10 to 12 feet high The
upper wall is situated about 20 to 30 feet behind the lower wall
Prior to the retaining wall construction and backfill the previously placed fill was
removed down to natural ground and the buried clay soils were removed down to rock
formation The rock formation was ripped to increase the vertical permeability in order to
eliminate the originally planned subdrains Structural fill consisting of crushed basalt was
used for the wall and general backfill Geogrids Miragrid were installed behind the walls
to reinforce the backfill
MASS GRADING AND WALL CONSTRUCTION
The grading work and retaining wall construction were performed by Lanai Builders
Inc During the grading and backfilling operations samples of the on site and imported
materials utilized as fill and backfill were obtained and transported to our office for testing
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor
