Pleistocene in age After a long period of volcanic inactivity during which time erosion

During the Pleistocene Epoch ice Age sea levels fluctuated in response to the
cycles of continental glaciation As the glaciers grew and advanced less water was
available to fill the ocean basins such that sea levels fell below the present stands of the
sea When the glaciers melted and receded an excess of water became available such
that the sea levels rose to above the present sea level
The processes of erosion and deposition were affected by these glacioeustatic sea
level fluctuations When the sea level was low the erosional base level was
correspondingly lower and valleys were carved to depths below the present sea level
When the sea level was high the erosional base level was raised such that sediments
accumulated at higher elevations

SITE DESCRIPTION GEOLOGY
The subject parcel is located on Kuala Street in the Pearl City area on the Island of
Oahu Hawaii A concrete lined drainage channel located along the eastern perimeter
bounds the rectangular shaped parcel A three story concrete masonry apartment building
and chain link fence exists along the northern perimeter of the parcel The site is vegetated
in patches with tall grass and some areas of brush shrub growth along the property
boundaries
The subject parcel is generally flat at the interior with some gentle to moderate
slopes trending down toward the drainage channel at the eastern perimeter of the site
Some concrete pilings were observed stockpiled at the east central portion of the site In
addition some sporadic patches of asphalt were observed on the ground surface and in
one location some metal rebar with plastic sheathing was observed protruding from the
ground surface Based on these observations we believe that the potential exists for some
isolated debris construction materials to be buried at the central portion of the site
Furthermore some thin gravel sized aggregate was observed spread on the ground
surface at the central portion of the site The aggregate may have been placed on the
ground surface to facilitate previous vehicular traffic and parking at the site

In general the ground surface was observed to consist of light reddish brown
clayey and silty saprolitic soils with some embedded hard basalt boulders estimated to
range in size from 2 to about 6 feet in dimension Most of the ground surface appears to
reflect the native soil condition with some areas having received fills to achieve the
generally flat existing ground surface condition It appears that some corraline materials
mixed with clay soil were placed as fill material at the northwestern half of the project site
The fill material is of unknown depth and lateral extent and appears to be generally firm
with a compacted surface appearance The soils observed at the ground surface were ina
dry condition and appear to have a generally low shrink swell capacity Ponded water was
not observed at the site and based on the existing topography and relief of the ground
surface stormwater runoff would appear to sheetflow across the site
Based on our review of available geologic and geotechnical data pertaining to the
vicinity of the project site the site may be generally underlain by clayey and silty soils with
sand and friable gravel that represent residual and saprolitic soils These in situ soils are
completely weathered products of the parent basalt rock formation which is believed to
exist at greater depth below the existing ground surface In general the saprolitic soils are
anticipated to grade at depth with highly weathered basalt rock Furthermore due to
natural weathering characteristics hard generally round shape basalt boulders may be
embedded within the saprolitic soils as evidenced by the surface protrusions of the
boulders at the site As mentioned previously some fills may have been placed at the site
to achieve the existing flattened ground surface topography
PRELIMINARY FINDINGS
Based on our review of available information and our brief site visit we believe that
there are no geologic or geotechnical engineering conditions or hazards that would restrict
or preclude prudent development of the subject parcel It is our professional opinion that
the on site soils and rock units are capable of moderate structural loading from low to
mid rise structures with proper foundation design
It is likely that the existing near surface fills at the site were not properly placed and
compacted These uncontrolled fills should be over excavated and properly compacted or
removed from the site
