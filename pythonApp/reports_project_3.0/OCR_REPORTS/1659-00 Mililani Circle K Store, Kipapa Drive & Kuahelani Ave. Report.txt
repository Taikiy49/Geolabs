In accordance with your request we have made a study of the near
surface soil conditions at the above referenced property in order to
provide foundation recommendations for the proposed renovation and
addition The general location is shown on the Project Location Map
Plate 1
The study consisted of drilling and sampling four shallow borings
performing laboratory testing and to determine the characteristics of
the materials This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
Proposed Construction
Based on the preliminary store plans the proposed renovation and
addition will be a one story CMU structure approximately 40 by 60 feet
in plan dimensions with an 8 by 32 feet rubbish bin and equipment
enclosure area

Site Conditions
The subject site is located on the northeastern corner of the
junction at Kipapa Drive and Kuahelani Avenue in Mililani Town on the
island of Oahu Hawaii
Currently the site is in use as a gas station with A C pavement
The western half of the building is now being used as a store and office
and the eastern half which was originally design as a garage is now
used for storage of merchandises The eastern area is 6 to 8 inches
lower than the store and several cracks were noted generally running in
north south directions
A relatively level pad about 11 feet wide and 32 feet long with
similar crack pattern is adjacent to the back portion of the building
The pad slopes upward at about 4 1 H V to a second level pad at the
western portion of the rear side of the site
The existing ground is generally level and slopes generally towards
the south and west at gradients of about 1 to 3

Discussion and Recommendations
General
The proposed one story addition will be connected to the northern
side of the existing gas station building Existing utilities and
previously buried cesspools could be located in the proposed addition
area The locations of underground utility lines should be verified
The stiff reddish brown clayey silt soil encountered can provide
adequate support for the proposed foundation loadings
Laboratory swelling tests performed on the clayey silt soils
indicate that this material generally has low to moderate swelling
potential depending on the moisture content of the soil prior to
concrete placement
Therefore to minimize effects due to the soil swelling under
on grade slabs we recommend that the on site soil be capped with 12
inches of granular select borrow fill and that the underlying subgrade
soils should be kept moist and protected from drying prior to the
placement of the upper select borrow
Because the surface soils are generally fairly impervious the
surface drainage should be carefully designed to drain water
particularly away from structures

Site Regrading
Some site regrading is planned for the proposed addition area and
the new slab in front of the store
Because the site is fairly level at the addition area positive
drainage away from the proposed structures should be provided during and
after construction
Grading work should be done in general conformance with Chapter 23
of the Revised Ordinances of Honolulu 1978 as amended and the
following additional guidelines
1 The project site where the new slabs are located should be
cleared and grubbed Surface vegetation topsoil
miscellaneous debris and stockpiles should be cleared and
removed prior to site grading
2 Topsoil and stockpiled soils should be stripped to stiff
natural ground before placing of fills Loose surface soils at
finish grades should be scarified and recompacted
3 The site should be proofrolled Localized loose soft pockets
or hard spots encountered during site preparation should be
excavated and replaced with select on site material compacted
in thin lifts
4 Provisions should be included to drain the site during and
after site preparation

It is anticipated that the entire building including the addition
will be of slab on grade construction and supported by continuous CMU or
CRM walls footings or individual spread footings

General guidelines for preliminary foundation design considerations
are as follows
1
Spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the proposed
building
Footings should be placed on existing stiff ground or on

Soft spots or pockets of loose material encountered in footing
excavations should be excavated and replaced with select
well graded granular material
Select granular materials should be 3 inch maximum size
non expansive with a plasticity index generally tess than 25
Bearing values for a given soil usually vary with size and
depth of footing Allowable bearing values of about 2500
p s f may be used for footings on stiff natural ground or on
compacted fill
Interior footings on level areas should be embedded a minimum
of 8 inches below the finished grade Perimeter wall footings
should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the adjacent
finished grade
Footings located next to utility trenches should extend down to
the bottom of the utility trench or be designed to span over
the trench

Slab On Grade
If practicable concrete slabs on ground should be placed after the
superstructure is constructed and should be separated from grade beams
walls and columns
It should be noted that some of the on site clayey silt soils are
moderately expansive when dried and then subsequently saturated
Therefore the on site soil should be over excavated down to a depth of
12 inches below the stab bottom thoroughly moistened and then replaced
with 8 inches of non expansive select borrow and a 4 inch layer of 3B
Fine gravel cushion fill The thickened edge of the stab should be
embedded a minimum of 10 inches below the lowest adjacent finish grade
To further reduce future post construction swelling of the
underlying soils we recommend that the in place subgrade soil below the
upper capping fill pad be kept moist prior to select borrow fill
placement
A 4 inch layer of 3B fine gravel cushion fill should be provided
below the slab In order to protect against future moisture
infiltration an impervious moisture membrane may be incorporated below
the stab The on grade slabs should be structurally separated from wall
foundations
If soft pockets are encountered they should be removed to a depth
of 2 feet below the bottom of the slab and replaced with fairly
well graded granular material

Joints and Connection Details
Some differential movements are to be expected between the building
elements due to slightly expansive surface
Connections between the existing and the new structures should be
detailed to allow some vertical movements and future adjustments or
maintenance and the connections should be delayed as long as
practicable
Sidewalks entry slabs etc to the building should be detailed to
allow for some rotation and yet maintain a smooth transition from the
entry slab to the building
Utilities
Utilities and other underground structures or obstructions are
anticipated The locations of underground utility lines should be
verified It may be necessary to move an existing line or adjust
structural details to accommodate the utility Should an unanticipated
condition or buried structure be encountered during construction each
condition should be evaluated and the design modified if necessary
New utility lines should be designed with flexible joints
particularly where lines are connected to structures
Drainage
We recommend that planters next to the foundations should be
avoided or have concrete bottoms and drains to prevent water from
entering the subsoils

The finish lot grade outside the building walls should be shaped to
shed water away from the walls to avoid ponding condition It is also
advised that gutter systems should be maintained properly around the
building and that gutter water diverted far away from the walls
These drainage requirements are essential for the _ proper
performance of the above recommended foundations since ponded water
could cause excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving
or loss of strength The foundation excavations should be properly
backfilled tight against the wall or slab footing immediately after
setting of the concrete to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales must be provided and maintained to drain all
surface run off away from the slab and footing foundation
Design Review
Plans and specifications for lot regrading foundation and utility
construction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and
written comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the lot regrading footing excavations and
backfill operations be observed by the soils engineer during
construction to see that adequate compaction has been obtained and that
the subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of our
investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
If conditions encountered during construction are different from
those presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made

Investigation Limitations
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations
made in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation
engineering principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all
other warranties either expressed or implied
The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are based
on our site reconnaissance soil information from boring and laboratory
test data obtained from this soil investigation
Because of the variability of soil deposits site improvements
designs and construction techniques conditions may be encountered that
cannot be foreseen with even the most exhaustive studies of site and
project conditions These unforeseen conditions should be recognized
when encountered and then evaluated so that the design or the
construction methods may be modified accordingly if necessary
Unforeseen or undetected conditions such as soft spots existing
utility trenches structure foundations voids or cavities boulders
expansive soil pockets or seepage water etc may occur in localized
areas and will have to be adjusted and corrected in the field as they
are detected Some contingency fund is thus recommended to accommodate
these possible costs

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling four
4 borings to depths of 10 0 to 15 0 feet below the existing ground
surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 A
truck mounted drill rig was used to drill the borings
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by visual and
textural examination in the field by our engineer who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory All soils
were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A summary of the materials encountered are
presented in the Log of Borings Plates A 1 through A 4
Soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving a 2 inch
standard split spoon sampler with a 140 pound hammer free falling 30
inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the last 12 inches are
shown on the Log of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
