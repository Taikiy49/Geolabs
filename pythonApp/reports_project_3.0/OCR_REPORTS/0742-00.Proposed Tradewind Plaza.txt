INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our review and analysis
of the proposed Tradewind Plaza project located in Waikiki
Honolulu Hawaii
Between the period of April 1973 and May 1977 concrete piles
have been driven at the subject site Based on a review of
the pile driving reports prepared by Engineering Equipment
Co Ltd a total of 71 twelve inch square piles had been
driven by the contractor with piles penetration varying
between 40 and 174 feet below the existing ground surface
elevations
The previous piles driven by Engineering Equipments Co Ltd
were driven at the locations shown on the previous structural
plan prepared by T Y
The boring locations and the driven piles are shown on the
new Basement Plan prepared by Jo Paul Rognstad and Associates
Inc current Project Architect and Structural Engineer
PROPOSED TRADEWIND PLAZA 1978
PROJECT CONSIDERATION
The proposed development will consist of a 12 story reinforced
concrete tower and a basement level
The highrise structure will be approximately 74 feet by 74 feet
in plan dimensions and will be supported on pile foundation
The basement perimeter wall will not be on piles and will bear
on line footing upon the in situ subgrade soil The finish
basement slab elevation will be at about elevation 2 feet
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Based on the two boring logs by Nat Whiton Drilling the site
is blanketed with about 5 feet of fill varying from loose to
firm sand and clay mixture
Below the surface fill crust is a thin 0 7 to 1 4 feet coral
crust underlain with about 30 feet of very soft and loose sand
silt and clayey deposits
Coral layers of varying consistency were noted below the exist
ing ground surface extending from a depth of about 40 feet down
to 60 feet the depth of borings
Water levels were noted at 3 6 to 3 8 feet below the ground
surface and probably subjected to tidal fluctuations For
details see the enclosed boring logs

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Pile Foundation
Based on a review of the existing pile driving data the
boring logs and our previous experience in the Waikiki
area it is our opinion that the existing twelve inch
square piles driven by Engineering Equipment Co Ltd may
be used to support the proposed highrise structure at the
project site A design pile capacity of 60 tons may be
used
Due to the variable thickness and densities of the under
lytng coral deposits pile length could vary considerably
at various locations
Where the underlying coral layer is dense the pile will
be end bearing on the coral formation
Where the pile penetrates below the upper coral crust it
may have to be a friction pile driven into the medium dense
coral sand layers
At either of the above case the new piles should develop a
final driving resistance of 7 blows per inch for the last
3 inches utilizing a Link Belt 520 driving hammer equiva
lent rated driving energy of 26 000 foot pounds
We recommend that a few of the existing piles be driven to
check their final set and to verify present capacity

Spread Footing Foundation
The present structural plan indicated that the perimeter
basement retaining wall will not be supported on piles
Continuous line footing about 2 feet wide is proposed
Since the highrise structure ts on piles some differ
ential settlements will occur between the pile supported
and spread footing foundations
To minimize settlement on the continuous wall footing
low bearing value of 600 p s f should be used Some
allowance should be made to protect the water proofing
membrane under the basement slab particularly along the
perimeter wall and at the pile cap areas
Soft mud or very loose material encountered below the
bottom of the wall footing should be removed and replaced
with compacted granular backfill down the thin coral crust
encountered at depths of 6 to 7 feet below the existing
ground surface
Uplift Resistance
Allowable uplift resistance of 5 kips per pile may be used
for single and group piles This value is relatively low
due to the fact that some piles have limited penetration
into the competent materials and low strength of the upper
lagoonal deposits

Lateral Capacity
The loose lagoonal matertals also limtts the lateral pile
capacity It ts recommended that a value of one kips per
pile be utilized for long term lateral loadings
Groundwater
Due to the high groundwater levels basement or elevator
shafts extending below approximately elevation 2 should
be designed to resist hydrostatic uplift pressures
Grading
Excavatton backfill around the pile cap and behind the base
ment retaining wall should be adequately compacted to at
least 90 of the maximum density established in accordance
with ASTM D 1557 designation
The on site coral sand is acceptable as backfill provided
that the organic clay and other deleterious materials are
removed
Full time inspection should be performed to verify the
degree of compaction when the fill will be utilized for
structural support
Construction Inspection
It is recommended that the existing driven in place pile
locations be verify by the contractor prior to driving of
the additional piles required to complete this project
