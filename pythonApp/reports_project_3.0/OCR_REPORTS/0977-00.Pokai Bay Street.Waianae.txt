Gentlemen
At your request we have performed a soil investigation at
the western portion of the above referenced lot
The western portion of the lot was filled with a few feet of
excavated material from the construction of the adjacent new
concrete drainage channel The fill placement was not moni
tored by a soils engineer and compaction testing was not per
formed during the grading operation
The purpose of our investigation was to evaluate the condition
of the recently placed fill from a compaction point of view
in order to satisfy requirements to obtain a grading permit
for the recent fill The soil data from our investigation
W O 977 00 P 2
would also be used to evaluate the planned future improvements
on the lot which include either a paved parking area or a
Single family residential development
SITE CONDITIONS
The subject lot is generally flag shaped The larger rec
tangular shaped portion is bordered by Farrington Highway to
the east Lualualei Homestead Road on the north and an
existing concrete drainage channel on the south The stem
portion of the lot which was the area under investigation
extends along the concrete drainage channel from the larger
portion of the lot to Pokai Bay Street
The lot is relatively flat and about 1 to 3 feet higher than
the adjacent existing houses on the north
The site was vacant at the time of our investigation
Subsurface Exploration
To explore the shallow soil conditions below the Site a
series of 5 test pits were excavated utilizing a hoptoe Den
Sity tests were taken in the exposed fill material encountered
in the test pits Additional laboratory soil tests were per
formed on the fill and underlying natural soil to evaluate
its suitability as foundation support
The location of the test pits are shown on the Site Plan

Subsurface Conditions
The test pits indicate that the recent fill at the Site is
about 1 0 to 3 0 feet thick and consists of light brown to
gray sandy silt with coral gravel Most of the fill was loose
to medium density
The soils below the fill consisted of soft to medium consis
tency gray brown clay to the bottom of the test pits It
appears that these soils were deposited by the former drainage
way which extended through this area prior to the construction
of the concrete drainage channel
Test Pit No 5 which is located on the northern portion of
the site encountered a light gray silty clay with coral frag
ments to a depth of 2 0 feet fill Below this was a soft
brown clay with roots and branches to a depth of 3 0 feet
Below this is concrete rubble with maximum size of 2 0 feet
to a depth of 4 0 feet depth of test pit
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Existing Fill
Based on the test pit data it appears that the area has been
covered with about 1 to 3 feet of fill The upper fill
material consisted of a light brown sandy silt with coral
gravel Compaction tests taken in the pits indicated unsatis
factory compaction of this upper fill material
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 977 00 P 4
We had recommended that this upper fill material should be
moistened to near optimum moisture content and recompacted to
a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density established by
ASTM D 1557 prior to preparation of an as built grading plan
Subsequently the area was compacted and additional compaction
tests were taken on the near surface fill material The addi
tional compaction tests indicated sufficient compaction of
this new fill The compaction test results are presented on
Place C l
Future Construction
The test pits encountered a soft to medium Silty clay layer
below the upper fill This soil has a low bearing capacity
and would be subject to settlements from additional fill or
future structure loads
Low allowable bearing values of about 1500 psf should be uti
lized for light weight structures over these soils The
magnitude of anticipated settlements of the structures would
depend on the type of structure and total column loading
Additional analysis would be required when more detailed
information is available
It is our understanding that the site may be also used for an
asphaltic concrete pavement area
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 977 00 P 5
The site should be proof rolled with a heavy roller to detect
soft areas When encountered the soft areas should be
removed and replaced with select non expansive material for
a maximum depth of 2 feet below the base course level The
subgrade should be shaped to prevent ponding and drain water
away After removal of any soft pockets the pavement section
would consist of 2 inch asphaltic concrete over 6 inch base
course
