Geotechnical Engineering Report
New Administration Building
Kailua Wastewater Treatment Plant Modifications
Phase 1
Kailua
Subsequent to our previous geotechnical exploration in 1987 the
proposed New Administration Building has been relocated The new facility
site is to the southeast of the existing Influent Pump Station of the
treatment plant
In general accordance with the scope of our geotechnical engineering
exploration two 5 and RB 6 have been drilled and sampled
at the new building site to depths of about 31 5 to 36 5 feet below the
existing ground surface at the approximate locations shown on the attached
Site Plan Plate 1 Selected soil samples from the borings were tested to
determine their general engineering properties The boring logs and the
laboratory test results are also attached
Subsoil Conditions
The borings at the new building site indicate that the subsoil
conditions generally consist of an at least 7 5 foot thick crust of stiff
to very stiff clays underlain by interbedded lagoonal deposits alluvium
and coral reef detritus which have generally variable consistency ranging
from very loose to dense and stiff to very stiff

During the field exploration groundwater level was measured in the
borings at approximately 6 8 feet below the existing ground surface
Foundation
The near surface soils exhibit low to moderate swelling potential
depending on the moisture content of the soils The laboratory tests
indicate that if the near surface soils can be maintained in a moist
condition greater than 35 the swelling potential is generally low
Since the new building site will be raised about one foot 1 above
the existing ground it appears that the anticipated fill thickness in
addition to the moistening of the underlying expansive clays would be
sufficient to reduce the potential subsoil swelling effect on the new
structure
Therefore we believe that shallow spread or continuous footing
foundations bearing on either the compacted recompacted fill layers or the
recompacted subsoils may be used for the new structure A minimum footing
embedment of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finish grade should be
used
We recommend that an allowable bearing capacity of 2500 p s f be
used for the footing foundation design The allowable bearing value is
for total dead and live loads only and may be increased by one third for
seismic wind and other short duration loading considerations The
footing foundation settlements under the anticipated design loadings are
differential
Resistance to lateral loads on the structure may be provided by
frictional resistance and passive earth pressure acting on the structure
A frictional coefficient of 0 4 may be used to compute the frictional
resistance between the bottom of the footing foundations and the

Site Grading
Based on the available finish grades it is our understanding that
the new building site will be raised by about one foot 1 Due to the
interbedded soft loose subsoils it is our opinion that new fills should
be placed at the site as early as possible to allow most of the induced
ground settlements to occur prior to building construction
Prior to site filling the existing ground surface should be kept
moist and prevented from drying to reduce the potential swelling of the
underlying clay subsoils
For other pertinent site grading discussion and recommendations our
previous geotechnical engineering report W O 1895 00 dated September
23 1987 should be referred to
Slab On Grade
Concrete slab on grade is anticipated for the new building Based on
the anticipated total capping fill thickness over the underlying expansive
clay within the new building the following concrete slab section may be
used
4 Inch Concrete Slab
4 Inch Base Course
8 Inch Total Thickness
The above design section may need to be revised if the design
subgrade level is lowered closer to expansive clay

Parking Pavement
Asphaltic concrete pavement is anticipated for the proposed adjacent
parking area which will be built on the existing grade Based on the
subsoil conditions the new pavement subgrade within two 2 feet of the
finish elevations will consist of the expansive clay
Therefore we recommend that the existing ground at the proposed
parking area be over excavated to at least 24 inches below the finish
elevation of the new pavement and backfilled with compacted non expansive
select fill The following preliminary pavement section may be used on
the compacted non expansive backfill
2 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 Inch Base Course 95 Compaction
8 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
Laboratory CBR tests should be performed on the actual subgrade soil
samples during construction and the above design section may be revised
if necessary The recommended pavement section considers medium traffic
conditions and assumes that good drainage will be provided for areas
adjacent to the pavements
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comment
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this review
is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for
misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring

provide geotechnical engineering services during site grading and
foundation construction of the project This is to observe compliance

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
upon information obtained from borings Variations of conditions between
the borings may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may
not become evident until construction If variations then appear evident
it will be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations given in this
report
The locations of the borings were determined by taping from the
existing structures indicated on the grading plan dated November 18
1988 of the project site prepared by R M Towill Corporation Elevations
of the borings were approximately determined by interpolation from
elevation points or contours on the plan The physical location and
elevation of the borings should be considered accurate only to the degree
implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
borings depict the approximate boundaries between soil types and as
such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on profiles
or cross sections are based upon interpolation between borings and may not
represent actual subsurface conditions
