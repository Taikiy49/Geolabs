Gentlemen
From to we have inspected the
earthwork and grading operations performed by Rego Trucking at
the above referenced project This report summarizes our
inspection and compaction tests performed at the above
referenced site
The grading operations at the above site consisted of cuts up
to 18 feet and fills up to about 6 feet The soils encountered
at the cut pad finished grade consisted of very stiff reddish
brown and brown clayey silt Field observations and laboratory
tests indicated that those soils could provide adequate bearing
support for the proposed single family residences
During the fill placement operations periodic field density
tests were done in accordance with the American Society for
Testing and Materials ASTM Test Designation D 1556 Sand
Cone Method A summary of the compaction test results is
attached Based on the compaction test results and our field
observation it is our opinion that the fill lots have been
compacted adequately
During the grading period the on site soil was tested prior to
being used in the fill The maximum soil density and optimum
moisture contents were established in our laboratory in accor
dance with ASTM D 1557 test designation and the results are as

RECOMMENDATIONS
House Foundations
In general spread footing foundation or slab on grade may be
used for the residences
To minimize the effects of slope creep and erosion slabs
should be set back away from the tops of slopes a minimum
distance of 5 feet Post footings located close to the tops of
slopes should be embedded a sufficient depth to obtain a mini
mum horizontal set back distance of 5 feet from the slope
face Post footings which will be located a minimum distance
of 5 feet away from the tops of slopes should be embedded a
minimum of 12 inches below the finished grade
Slab on grade should be placed over a 4 inch layer of base
course compacted to 95 of its maximum dry density
It is critical that the subgrade should be kept moist and
protected from drying prior to concrete placement Subgrade
should be compacted to a minimum of 85 of its maximum dry
density
Site Regrading
Subsequent to completion of lot grading utility trenches
within the lot pads should be properly backfilled and compacted
under the observations of a soils engineer
This office assumes no responsibility for any alterations made
to slope or pads on the subject lots subsequent to the issuance
of this report without our knowledge and written approval
