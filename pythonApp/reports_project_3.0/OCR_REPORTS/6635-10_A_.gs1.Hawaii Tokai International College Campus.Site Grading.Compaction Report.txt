Dear Mr. Hastings: 
This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations and testing 
services performed during mass and site grading and utility installation for the Hawaii 
Tokai International College Campus project from July 26, 2013 to January 21, 2015 in 
accordance with our revised fee proposal dated October 31, 2013. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The Hawaii Tokai International College Campus covers approximately 6 acres off 
Farrington Highway in the Ewa District on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii.  New building 
structures and supporting infrastructure installation are planned for the new campus 
development.  The location of the project site is shown on the Project Location Map, 
Plate 1 and the Site Plans, Plates 2.1 and 2.2. 
The new campus will include the following construction. 
1. 
One-story administration building with a floor area of 7,000 square feet. 
2. 
One-story multi-purpose building with a floor area of 6,600 square feet. 
3. 
Two-story classroom building with a total floor area of 29,616 square feet. 
4. 
Two-story student housing building with a total floor area of 35,128 square 
feet. 
5. 
One-story maintenance and storage building. 
6. 
Asphaltic concrete paved parking lots and service areas. 
The infrastructure for the development included the installation of new underground 
utilities, such as water, sewer, and drain lines.  Based on the grading plans, fills up to 
about 2 to 3 feet were planned below the buildings for the project.  Deeper fills are 
required along the perimeter berm and landscape areas.

MASS AND SITE GRADING, AND UTILITY INSTALLATION 
The mass and site grading, and utility installation work for the Hawaii Tokai 
International College Campus project was observed by Geolabs, Inc. from July 26, 2013 to 
January 21, 2015.  The mass and site grading, and utility installation consisted of clearing 
and grubbing, fills of up to about 2 to 3 feet in depth, and placement and compaction of fills 
and backfills to achieve the design finished grades for the project.  Deeper fills were 
placed along the perimeter berm and landscaped areas.  Delta Construction performed the 
mass grading and utility installation work. During the mass and site grading, and backfilling 
operations, samples of the on-site and imported materials utilized as fill and backfill 
materials were obtained and transported to our laboratory for testing.  The maximum dry 
densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general 
accordance with ASTM D1557 (Modified Proctor), and the test results are summarized on 
Plate 3. 
The fill and backfill materials were moisture-conditioned to above optimum moisture 
content, and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as 
determined in accordance with ASTM D1557.  In the pavement areas, the fill and backfill 
materials within 2 feet of the finished subgrade were compacted to a minimum of 
95 percent relative compaction. 
During the site grading and backfilling operations, periodic field density tests were 
performed in general accordance with ASTM D6938 (Nuclear Gauge Method).  Results of 
the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 4.1 through 4.31 of this report.  
These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and 
backfills at the site.  Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and 
interpolating from on-site survey stakes. These locations and elevations should be 
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used.  Areas assessed as 
unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained and/or 
approved by our field representative based on visual observation and hand probing. 
Our services consisted of full-time and part-time field observation of the contractor’s 
fill and backfill placement, subgrade preparation, and compaction procedures during the 
above grading operations. 
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the 
referenced tests, it is our opinion that the mass and site grading, and backfill installation 
work for the Hawaii Tokai International College Campus project, observed and tested by 
Geolabs in the period covered by this report, was performed in conformance with the 
general intent of the following references.
