INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed McLaughlin Residence in the Hanalei area on the Island of
Kauai Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings based on the field exploration and laboratory
testing and presents our geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our
analyses for the proposed residence These recommendations are intended for the
design of house foundations and site grading only and are subject to the limitations noted
at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located at 5142 Weke Road in the Hanalei area on the Island of
Kauai Hawaii Based on the information provided it is proposed to construct a new
house at the project site
It is our understanding that the existing house has experienced some distress
apparently due to foundation settlements The existing house will be demolished and
replaced with a new residence within the property We understand that the new
residence will be constructed about 3 feet above the existing ground utilizing post and
beam construction
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our field exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions at the site to develop a soil data set for the formulation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertinent to the design of house foundations and site
grading To accomplish this we conducted an exploration program generally consisting
of the following tasks and work efforts

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The Island of Kauai is composed of a single basalt shield volcano built by the
extrusion of the lavas of the Waimea Canyon Volcanic Series during the late Pliocene
more than 2 3 million years before present Following the cessation of this main shield
building phase there was renewed volcanic activity with the extrusion of the basaltic
lavas of the post erosional Koloa Series and the concurrent deposition of the alluvial

sediments of the Palikea Formation Subsequently the project area was covered with
beach and dune sand
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located at 5142 Weke Road in the Hanalei area on the Island of
Kauai Hawaii The subject property is located on the northwestern side of Weke Road
next to Hanalei Bay
Based on the site reconnaissance and topographic map provided the site is
generally flat with ground elevations ranging from about 11 feet to 14 feet Mean Sea
Level MSL The property is located in a special flood hazard area inundated by
100 year flood zones AE and VE with Base flood elevation of 13 feet

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field exploration the subsurface conditions at the project site
generally consist of fine sand extending from the surface to a depth of approximately
21 5 feet below the existing ground surface the maximum depth drilled The sand soil
was generally in damp to moist condition and graded from loose to medium dense near
the surface to dense with depth
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered at the project site shallow
spread or wall foundations may be used to support the new house An allowable bearing
pressure of up to may be used for the design of house foundations
Foundations should be embedded a minimum of 24 inches below the outside finished
grade in order to reduce the potential for erosion of the sand under the footings
Detailed discussion of these items and our geotechnical engineering design
recommendations are presented in the following sections of this report
House Foundations
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered at the project site we
recommend that shallow spread or wall foundations be used to support the new
residence An allowable bearing pressure of up to may be used for the design
of footings bearing on the soils This bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may be
increased by one third 1 3 for transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic
forces Foundations should be embedded a minimum of 24 inches below the outside
finished grade in order to reduce the potential for erosion of the sand under the footings
Where foundations are located next to retaining structures utility trenches and or
easements they should be embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V
GEOLABS INC
W O 4310 00 Page 6
imaginary plane extending upward from the bottom edge of the retaining structure
foundations utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility lines This requirement is
necessary to reduce the potential of surcharging adjacent below grade structures with
additional structural loads and to reduce the potential for foundation settlement
If soft and or loose materials are encountered at the bottom of footing
excavations they should be over excavated to expose firm materials The
over excavation should either be backfilled with granular material compacted to a
minimum of 95 percent relative compaction or the footing bottom should be extended
down to underlying competent material Relative compaction refers to the in place dry
density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil
established in accordance with procedures described in ASTM Test Designation
D 1557 91
If the foundations are designed in accordance with our recommendations we
estimate total settlements to be less than 1 inch with differential settlements on the order
of inch
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by frictional resistance
developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil and by passive
earth pressure acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system A
coefficient of friction of 0 4 may be used for footings bearing on soils Resistance due to
passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid pressure of
300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth pcf This assumes that the soil around
footings is well compacted Unless covered by pavements or slabs the passive
resistance in the upper 12 inches of soil should be neglected Surface water should be
diverted away from foundations

Site Grading
Based on the existing topography and the proposed finished grade we anticipate
that minimal site grading may be needed Items of grading that are addressed in the
following subsections include the following 1 Site Preparation 2 Fills and Backfills
and 3 Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
Site grading operations should be observed by a representative of Geolabs Inc It
is important that a representative from our office observe the site grading operations to
evaluate whether undesirable materials are encountered during the excavation and
scarification process and whether the exposed soil rock conditions are similar to those
encountered in our field exploration
Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork areas within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed Previous house foundations vegetation
debris rubbish and other unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed
of properly off site
After house demolition the ground surface should be proof rolled and compacted
with a heavy vibratory roller in an attempt to densify the near surface sand soils
prior to house construction In areas to receive fill the subgrade should be
scarified to a minimum depth of 8 inches moisture conditioned to at least
2 percent above the optimum moisture content and compacted to at least
95 percent relative compaction The excavated materials may be reused as a
source of fill materials provided that the materials meet the gradation requirements
presented in the Fills and Backfills subsection of this report Loose and or soft
GEOLABS INC
W O 4310 00 Page 8
spots encountered at the excavated subgrade levels should be removed and
replaced with granular fill materials compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative
compaction
Fills and Backfills
In general we believe that the excavated on site soils may be reused as a source
of general fill material at the site provided that the materials are free of vegetation
deleterious materials clay lumps and rock fragments greater than 3 inches in
maximum dimension In addition the fill material should be moisture conditioned
and compacted as recommended in the following subsection entitled Fill
Placement and Compaction Requirements
Imported material should be well graded granular material with no particles greater
than 3 inches in largest dimension with less than 25 percent passing the No 200
sieve Imported material should be observed and or tested by Geolabs Inc for its
suitability prior to being transported to the site
Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
In general fills and backfills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 10 inches
in loose thickness moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum
moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
Where fills are placed under pavements the upper 3 feet of fill below the
pavement grade should be compacted to not less than 95 percent relative
compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil
expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density as determined by
ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91 Optimum moisture is the water content
percentage by weight corresponding to the maximum dry density

Carport Slabs and Concrete Driveways
We anticipate that concrete slabs on grade will be used for the carport slab and
concrete driveway The subgrades for slabs on grades should be scarified to a depth of
at least 8 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture
and compacted to not less than 95 percent relative compaction Relative compaction
refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry
density of the same soil determined in accordance with ASTM Test Designation
D 1557 91 Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight
corresponding to the maximum dry density
A 6 inch layer of select borrow subbase over compacted moist subgrade is
recommended for more uniform support The select borrow subbase should consist of
crushed basalt aggregate compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
We recommend that the concrete slab be designed for the intended loads For design of
structural slabs a modulus of subgrade reaction of 250 pounds per cubic inch pci may
be used for the compacted select borrow subbase Provisions should be made for proper
load transfer across the slab joints subjected to vehicular traffic The subgrade soils
beneath the garage and driveway slabs should be prepared in accordance with the
recommendations presented in the Site Preparation subsection of this report
Utility Lines
We envision that new utility lines i e water lines will be required for development
of the residence It is anticipated that most of the trenches for utilities will be excavated in
the sandy soils Shoring will be required for utility trench deeper than 3 feet In general a
granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67
gradation is recommended below the pipes Free draining granular materials such as
No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation should be used for the trench backfill
up to about 12 inches above the pipes to provide adequate support around the pipes and
to reduce compaction of the backfill thus reducing the potential of damaging the pipes
GEOLABS INC
W O 4310 00 Page 10
The upper portion of the trench backfill should consist of granular material
generally less than 6 inches in maximum size as recommended in the Fills and Backfills
subsection compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction Where trenches will be
located in paved areas the upper 3 feet of the trench backfill below the pavement grade
should be compacted to not less than 95 percent relative compaction
Drainage
The finished grades outside the residential structure should be sloped to shed
water away from foundations and slabs and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also
recommended that gutter systems be installed around the buildings and that the
discharge be diverted away from the foundation and slab areas Excessive landscape
watering near the foundations and slabs should be avoided Planters next to foundations
should also be avoided or have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce the potential for
water infiltration into the subsoils
The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled against the walls or slab
edges immediately after setting of the concrete to reduce the potential for water
infiltration In addition drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and
should be maintained to drain surface run off away from the foundations and slabs

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that Geolabs Inc be retained to provide geotechnical
engineering services during construction of the proposed project The items of
construction monitoring that are critical requiring Special Inspection include observation

are different from those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate
modifications to the design should be made
