INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our preliminary geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the site located in the Hawaii Kai area of eastern
Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project
site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and presents our preliminary geotechnical
engineering evaluation and recommendations resulting from our limited field exploration
laboratory testing and engineering analyses The discussion and recommendations
presented herein are intended for preliminary assessment of the project site only The
findings and recommendations presented herein are subject to the additional services
and limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located north of Hawaii Kai Drive between the intersection of
Keahole Place and Wailua Street in the Hawaii Kai area of eastern Honolulu on the
island of Oahu Hawaii The site is generally bounded by Hawaii Kai Drive to the south
and southeast Kaluanui Road to the north and northeast and vacant land to the west as
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Based on the information provided we understand that the southern part of the
project site formerly contained a linear shaped low pond area with water and soft soils
measuring approximately 940 feet long The approximate location and extent of this
former low pond area has been interpreted and shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
According to a previous soil study conducted by Walter Lum Associates Inc in 1967 it
was recommended that the low pond area be stabilized with a subdrain system and
gravel backfill up to an elevation of about 3 feet Mean Sea Level MSL
Based on our field observations and the preliminary concept plan dated
provided it appears that the former low pond area has been filled to

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu is composed largely of the weathered remnants of two extinct
shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae forms the bulk of
the western third of the island while the younger shield Koolau forms the majority of the
eastern two thirds of the island Waianae became extinct while Koolau was still active
and its eastern flank is partially buried below Koolau lava in central Oahu
The project site is located at the southeastern end of Koolau and its
geomorphology and subsurface conditions are directly related to glacio eustatic
fluctuations of sea level during the Pleistocene Epoch The base of the stratigraphic
section in this area is Koolau Basalt
The Koolau volcanic shield was built during the late Pliocene and early Pleistocene
Epoch by thin bedded basaltic lava flows The main shield building activities ceased
approximately 2 5 million years ago Evidence from drilled wells indicates that the island
of Oahu has subsided as much as 1 200 feet since the cessation of this early volcanic
activity Macdonald and Abbott 1970
During the Pleistocene Epoch Ice Age there were many sea level changes as a
result of widespread glaciation in the continental areas of the world As the great
continental glaciers accumulated the level of the ocean fell since there was less water
available to fill the oceanic basins Conversely as the glaciers receded or melted global
sea levels rose because more water was available The land mass of Oahu remained
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 4192 00 Page 6
essentially stable during these changes and the fluctuations were eustatic in nature
These glacio eustatic fluctuations resulted in stands of the sea which were both higher
and lower relative to present sea level of Oahu
The higher sea level stands caused the accumulation of terrigenous sediments in
the heads of the old bays accumulation of reef deposits at corresponding higher
elevations and lagoon marine sediments in the quiet waters protected by fringing reefs
The lower sea stands caused streams to carve valleys in the volcanic dome of Koolau
During the late Pleistocene Epoch volcanic activity was renewed on Koolau with
eruptions of the Honolulu Volcanic Series In the vicinity of the site eruptions along the
Koko Fissure deposited pyroclastic materials consisting of tuff and cinder in portions of
the valleys cut into the southeastern end of Koolau
The site is situated at the mouth of Hahaione Valley and was formerly part of
Kuapa Pond a shallow estuary formed at the confluence of Hahaione Kamilonui and
Kamiloiki Valleys Land reclamation efforts filled portions of this estuary to create new
land for development
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is situated to the northwest of the intersection of Hawaii Kai Drive
and Keahole Street in the Hawaii Kai area of eastern Honolulu on the island of Oahu
Hawaii The site is generally bounded by Hawaii Kai Drive to the south and southeast
Kaluanui Road to the north and northeast and vacant land to the west as shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2
The site is currently vacant land and is mostly densely vegetated by kiawe trees
and grasses with a few unimproved access roads The site is at the end of an existing
canyon along the base of Kaluanui Ridge The project site is generally flat along Hawaii
Kai Drive except for a drainage ditch that ends near the intersection of Hawaii Kai drive
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 4192 00 Page 7
and Wailua Street The ditch crosses the central portion of the site and generally
separates the level topography in the southern portion of the site from the raised and
hummocky topography in the northern portion
The northern portion of the site contains fill piles and rows of boulders mostly 3 to
6 feet in diameter with some up to 10 feet across The northwest side of the site slopes
up to the rocky slope of the canyon The northeast boundary contains the embankment
fill for Kaluanui Road Based on the available preliminary concept plan the existing
elevation range from about 7 feet MSL in the south to about 75 feet in the north
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our preliminary field exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling
three borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 3 extending to depths ranging from
about 15 5 to 24 feet below the existing ground surface along the level grounds adjacent
to Hawaii Kai Drive In addition a total of six test pits were excavated at selected
locations at the higher ground area to depths ranging between about 5 to 9 feet below the
existing ground surface The approximate locations of the drilled borings and excavated
test pits are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general our borings at the former low pond area indicate that the area has been
backfilled with medium dense to dense silty and gravelly sands up to an elevation of
about 3 feet MSL The upper portion of the fills extending from about elevation 3 feet
MSL to the existing grades generally consisted of medium dense to dense clayey sand
and gravel and very stiff to hard sandy and silty clay soils Below the fill materials our
drilled borings generally encountered in situ soils consisting of varying amounts of stiff
silty clay underlain by dense cemented silty sands extending to the maximum depth
explored of about 24 feet below the existing ground surface
The excavated test pits at the higher grounds of the project site generally
encountered up to about 6 feet of fill materiais ranging from soft to stiff sandy and silty
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 4192 00 Page 8
clays to medium dense to dense clayey and gravelly sands with a mixture of gravel
cobbles and boulders The surficial fill materials were generally underlain by in situ soils
consisting of dense to very dense cemented silty sands and volcanic tuff deposits
extending to the maximum depth explored of about 9 feet below the existing ground
surface
Based on the Soil Survey of Islands of Molokai and Lanai
State of Hawaii by the United States Department of Agriculture the predominant soil type
in the project area is the Koko series silt loam KSB as shown on the Soil Unit Map
Plate 3 This soil type is described as dark reddish brown or dark brown silt loam loam
or clay loam that has subangular blocky structure The substratum consists of cinders
and tuff at a depth of approximately 3 to 6 feet below the top of a typical soil profile The
substratum appears to correspond to the dense cemented silty sand and tuff deposits
encountered in our field exploration performed at the project site
Groundwater was not encountered in the shallow test pits excavated at the higher
grounds on the north side of the project site Along the flat area adjacent to Hawaii Kai
Drive our drilled borings encountered groundwater levels at depths of about 6 5 to
7 7 feet below the existing ground surface at the time of our field exploration These
depths generally correspond to elevations of approximately 1 6 to 2 0 feet MSL Due
to the proximity of the project site to the Kuapa Pond and Pacific Ocean it is likely the
water levels measured in the borings during our field exploration were affected by tidal
influences In addition the water levels at the project site may be influenced by seasonal
precipitation run off storm surge conditions and other factors

PRELIMINARY FINDINGS AND DISCUSSION
Based on the limited information obtained from the preliminary field exploration
program we believe that future development of the subject site is feasible from a
geotechnical engineering point of view provided that the concerns and
recommendations presented in this report are properly addressed and followed during
the future site development
Our drilled borings along the southern portion of the project site adjacent to
Hawaii Kai Drive indicate that the former low pond area has been properly backfilled with
granular materials up to an elevation of about 3 feet MSL The remaining fill materials
up to the existing grades appear to consist of low to moderate expansion soils placed with
some amount of compaction effort Additional laboratory sweil tests should be conducted
for the design and construction of future slabs on grade planned at the project site
Based on the limited borings drilled for the preliminary geotechnical engineering
exploration the original soft mud present at the former low pond area appears to have
been removed prior to the pond filling operation It is our opinion that the thickness areal
extent and conditions of these soft mud deposits should be further delineated and
evaluated to assess its potential impacts on the future development planned at the site
Based on our field observations and the test pits excavated at the higher grounds
it appears that the northern portion of the project site was generally overlain by various
stockpiles dredged materials from Kuapa Pond and undocumented fills construction
debris from unknown sources at the existing ground surface It is our opinion that these
uncompacted fill materials should be accurately delineated and further evaluated for
recompaction and or removal during the design and construction of future development
planned at the project site
Special attention should be given to the potential presence of soft soil deposits and
boulder accumulation aiong the drainageway traversing the project site Loose boulders

Former Low Pond Area
Based on the limited field exploration conducted at the former low pond area the
level ground immediately to the north of Hawaii Kai Drive it appears that the majority of
the low pond area has been properly backfilled in accordance with the recommendations
presented in the afore mentioned soils report prepared by Walter Lum Associates Inc
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered in the drilled borings it appears
that the lower portion of the former pond area up to elevation of about 3 feet MSL has
been properly backfilled using granular materials consisting predominantly of silty and
gravelly sands The remaining backfill of the former low pond area up to the existing
grades generally consisted of clayey sand and gravel and sandy and silty clay soils
Based on the penetration resistance observed during our field exploration it appears that
the backfill materials have been placed with some amount of compaction effort with the
consistency of fill materials generally ranging from medium dense to dense or very stiff to
hard Substantial zones of soft and or loose backfills were not encountered in the borings
drilled for this preliminary field exploration
Laboratory swell tests were performed on selected soil samples obtained from our
field exploration to evaluate the swelling potential of the near surface fill materials in this
area Based on our laboratory swell tests the near surface soils appear to be of low to
moderate expansion when subjected to fluctuations in moisture content In order to
reduce the potential for distress to future slabs on grade resulting from swelling of the
near surface expansive soils we believe that concrete slabs on grade planned at the
project site may need to be supported on a non expansive select granular capping layer
In addition proper slab subgrade preparation including _ scarification

Stockpile Undocumented Fills
Based on the preliminary soil report prepared by Walter Lum Associates Inc and
the topographic survey map provided it appears that stockpiles resulting from the
dredging operation of Kuapa Pond have been placed at the higher ground on the north
side of the project site In addition our field reconnaissance indicates that undocumented
fills from unknown sources have been dumped at various locations across the project site
Based on our test pit excavations conducted at the project site it appears that the
existing stockpiles and undocumented fills generally consisted of variable materials
ranging from soft to stiff sandy and silty clays to medium dense to dense clayey and
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 4192 00 Page 12
gravelly sands with a mixture of gravel cobbles and boulders Based on our visual
observations and the pocket penetrometer tests performed the materials appear to have
been dumped in place with no apparent compaction efforts
A primary concern regarding the stockpiles and undocumented fills is the potential
existence of loose and or soft under compacted fill materials that may encroach within the
future development planned at the project site Future improvements bearing on these
uncompacted fills may result in potential distress such as foundation settlement and
slab wall cracking due to the compression of these uncompacted fills under the new
loads Therefore it is our opinion that these uncompacted fill materials should be
accurately delineated and further evaluated during the design of future development
planned at the project site Special earthwork and grading requirements should be
observed and implemented during construction at the higher ground areas Soft and or
loose materials exposed during the clearing and grubbing operations should be further
evaluated for removal and or recompaction
Other Earthwork Considerations
Based on our field observations and the preliminary concept plan provided it
appears that a drainageway and or ditch traverse the project site from the northwest to
the southeast Special attention should be given to the conditions along the bottom of the
drainageway for potential presence of soft soil deposits and accumulation of cobbles and
boulders It is our opinion that these soft soil deposits and loose boulders if encountered
should be removed during the earthwork operation of the future development
Our field observations indicate that significant amount of boulders and rocks are
present in various locations across the project site Some of these rocks exist as isolated
boulders while others are nested together as a rock berm It is our opinion that loose
boulders on sloping ground should be removed to reduce the potential for rolling boulder
or rockfall hazard to the future development Rock berms should also be removed and

Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the former low pond area were explored by drilling
and sampling three borings to depths ranging from about 15 5 to 24 feet below the
existing ground surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The
borings were drilled using truck mounted drilling equipment equipped with continuous
flight augers
In addition the subsurface conditions in the higher grounds of the project site were
explored by excavating and sampling six test pits to depths ranging from about 5 to 9 feet
below the existing ground surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2 The test pits were excavated using a rubber tired backhoe
The materials encountered in the borings and test pits were classified by visual
and textural examination in the field by our engineer who monitored the drilling and
excavation operations on a near continuous basis These classifications were further
reviewed visually and by testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general
conformance with the Unified Soil Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic
representations of the materials encountered are provided on the Logs of Borings
Plates A 1 through A 3 Logs of the materials encountered in the test pits are presented
on the Logs of Test Pits Plates A 4 through A 6
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils
by driving a 2 0 inch OD standard penetration sampler or a 3 inch OD Modified California
sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts needed to drive
the sampler the last 12 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown on the Logs of Borings at
the appropriate sample depths
Pocket penetrometer tests were performed on selected soil layers in the field The
pocket penetrometer test provide an indication of the unconfined compressive strength of
the sample Results of the pocket penetrometer tests are summarized on the Logs of
Borings and Test Pits at the appropriate sample depths

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content and unit weight determinations were performed on selected soil
samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties The results of
these tests are presented on the Logs of Borings and Test Pits at the appropriate sample
depths
Two one inch ring swell tests were performed on selected soil samples to evaluate
the swelling potential of the near surface soils The results of the tests are summarized
on Plate B 1
Two Atterberg Limits tests ASTM D 4318 were performed on selected soil
samples to evaluate their liquid and plastic limits and to aid in soil classification The test
results are presented on the Logs of Borings and Test Pits at the appropriate sample
depths Graphic presentation of the test results is provided on Plate B 2
Three sieve analysis tests ASTM C 136 were performed on selected samples of
the soils to evaluate the gradation characteristics of the soils and to aid in soil
classification Graphic presentation of the grain size distribution is presented on
Plate B 3
One residual direct shear test ASTM D 3080 was performed on a selected
sample of clayey soils to evaluate the residual shear strength of the material tested
Results of the residual direct shear test are presented on Plate B 4
