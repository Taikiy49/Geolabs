GEOLOGY
The sewage pump station site and the force main alignment are located
on the northeastern side of the elongated Koolau Volcanic Range The
sites are believed to be in the throat or caldera of this large vol
cano where much of original basaltic rock has been hydrothermally
altered After the cessation of the original mountain building
volcanic activities the land had been deeply weathered and eroded
The old erosion products consist mainly of clay and silt termed
older alluvium were redeposited along the mountain flanks Several
subsequent rise and fall of sea level further altered landscape and
the Kaneohe Bay was formed Recent rise in sea level produced embay
ments and swamps in the Kahaluu Kaneohe Kailua areas
The original Ahuimanu Stream ran along much of the present Kahekili
Highway from Ahuimanu to the Kahaluu area and joined Kahaluu Stream at
about the middle of the force main alignment The streams deposited
thick soft swamp sediments in drowned valleys
Construction of the new Kahekili Highway in 1972 filled most of the
original stream and swamp land along its alignment and relocated the
stream to the west of the present highway

SITE AND SOIL CONDITIONS
Sewage Pumping Station
The sewage pumping station site is relatively flat with elevation
varying from 6 to 7 feet There were three medium sized trees and a
mound of earth toward the northern portion of the site The remaining
ground was covered with moderately dense grass at the time of our
exploration
The subsurface conditions were explored by drilling 4 borings varying
from 22 5 feet to 48 0 feet deep at the pump station site The appro
Ximate location of these borings are shown on Plate 2
In general the subsoils consist of stiff surface fill over soft
lagoonal sediments underlain by stiff silty older alluvium and dense
basalt
The surface fill is 6 to 7 feet thick and consists of stiff brown
clayey silt with some sand and gravel In general the fill becomes
softer near the bottom The soft lagoonal sediments below the fill
vary from zero feet in Boring 3 to 14 5 feet thick in Boring 4
consisting mainly of soft gray silty Clay and loose gray silty sand
with occassional shell fragments Underlying the soft lagoonal
sediments is an older alluvial deposit consisting of stiff brown to
orange brown color silt with decomposed sand and gravel

W O 1155 00 Page 6
In Boring 2 dense and hard large boulders were encountered in the
older alluvium This may indicate that a buried rock cliff may exist
nearby toward Boring 3 where no soft lagoonal sediment was found in
the boring
Underlying the older alluvium is dark gray dense basalt rock In
general the rock core samples recovered from our boring indicate the
basalt rock is predominantly hard dense and massive but some had
become softer altered by hydrothermal action while still retaining
dense appearance The rock core samples also indicate that these
massive basalt flows have been intruded by dikes In some areas
contact metamorphism has altered the country rock basalt flows into
a softer soil like material saprolite In general the basalt rock
may be described as hard dense basalt with intervening soft
decomposed layers

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
FOR SEWAGE PUMP STATION
General
The proposed sewage pump station will require a substantial excavation
below groundwater level through soft lagoonal sediment and probably
some boulders Adequate shoring and careful dewatering work will be
required by the contractor during construction
For the design of the pump station facilities the assumed construction
sequence is that the ground will first be excavated building structure
constructed and backfilled the connecting inbound gravity line and
outbound sewer force main will then be trenched and tied to the com
pleted structure
Foundation Excavation and Dewatering
The excavation for pump station and connecting sewer lines should be
shored The appropriate method of excavation and shoring should be at
the contractor s option Dewatering or lowering of groundwater may be
required for excavation However deep well drawdown should be care
fully evaluated by the contractor to avoid subsidence problems asso
ciated with lowering of the water level
A deep drawdown of water level outside the excavation area may cause
ground settlements in the surrounding area which could cause damages
to existing structures such as those in the gasoline service station
and buried lines in the near vicinity
GEOLABS HAWAIl
W O 1155 00 Page 10
It appears that sumping within the sheetpiled water tight enclosure
should be considered
If excessive drawdowns are occurring in the surrounding area pumping
may need to be suspended immediately and or recharging of groundwater
outside the sheetpile may need to be implemented immediately
Foundation Support
It is anticipated that the foundation of the pump station will be a
thick mat slab probably bottomed at Elevations 16 to 17 feet The
bottom of foundation excavation should be dewatered so that concrete
can be placed in a dry condition
We recommend that an allowable soil bearing pressure of 3000 p s f be
used based on the foundation slab bearing on the underlying brown to
orange brown stiff silt or soft rock Where soft lagoonal sediment
such as soft dark gray clayey silt and loose gray Silty sand are
encountered at the bottom of the mat excavation they should be
removed completely and backfilled with compacted crushed rock or other
suitable material as appropriate
It is probable that hard rock may be encountered at the bottom of mat
excavation in limited areas Where encountered the rock at the
bottom of footing should be overexcavated another 8 inches below the
footing and backfilled with compacted crushed rock
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1155 00 Page 11
The foundation backfill material should be a coarse grained clean
crushed rock product such as No 3 Fine rock meeting ASTM C 33 No
67 gradation The backfill placement should be performed when the
excavation is dewatered to a relatively dry condition The founda
tion backfill under the pump station structure should be placed in
horizontal lifts not exceeding 9 loose inches and compacted to at
least 95 of the maximum dry density determined in accordance with the
ASTM D 1557 78 test procedure
It is noted that the intake structure will be higher at about Eleva
tion 7 feet where soft swamp sediments are likely to be encountered
We recommend that the intake structure be designed as an integral part
of the entire structure without independent foundation Otherwise
significant differential settlement may result
The 1 000 gallon tank may be supported on shallow footings founded on
the new compacted fill or the existing fill The existing fill should
be recompacted to 95 of the ASTM D 1557 78 maximum density at least 6
inches below the bottom of the footing and 6 inches horizontally
beyond the edges of the footing We believe that the settlement under

Settlements
Little or no settlement is anticipated on the pump station bearing on
the undisturbed stiff silt or soft rock
However it is our understanding that perhaps up to feet of new
fill will be required outside the station structure to raise the site
grade from about Elevation 6 5 feet to a finished grade of Elevations
8 5 to 9 5 feet It is estimated that the new fill will probably
induce a ground settlement on the order of 2 to 4 inches with larger
settlements occurring on the northern and eastern sides of the station
The ground settlements will probably continue for another several
months after the fill placement Although some ground settlements are
also anticipated on the front or southern side of the pump station
structure we believe that these settlements will probably be small
and occur quickly after the initial fill placement
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1155 00 Page 14
Because of the above ground settlement outside the pump station
structure due to site filling we strongly recommend that site
filling if required should be done first and prior to pump station
constructions so that sufficient time be given for majority of fill
settlement to occur
It is also recommended that the subsequent gravity and force main
lines to and from the substation as well as other utility lines be
placed last when the site has first been filled the pump station
structure completed and properly backfilled All utility line
connections at the station should have flexible connections to
accommodate these ground settlements The above construction sequence
is recommended to alleviate potential problems that may be caused by
excessive ground settlement Otherwise the site should be preloaded
for at least six weeks to induce the majority of settlement before
pipe installation It is anticipated that there may still be some
residual settlements after the surcharge or after the construction
Thus the underground lines should be constructed in short sections
with flexible joints to accommodate these long term ground settlement
It is anticipated that the settlements will be greater toward the pump
station and will tend to cause more positive flow toward the station
for gravity lines

Ground Settlement Due to Highway Fill
Because of the soft underlying swampy deposits a 5 foot high sur
charge fill had been utilized in the early 1970 s prior to the A C
roadway pavement construction along the section of Kahekili Highway
between Kahaluu and Ahuimanu Sewage Treatment Plant It is believed
that this surcharging fill has accelerated the ground settlement
process It is assumed that the primary consolidation of the soft
soils under the highway fill load has now been completed or nearly
completed However slow but relatively small additional ground
settlement could also continue due to secondary compression of the
thick soft swamp sediment under the highway embankment fill We
estimate that the magnitude of this secondary compression will be
small probably less than 3 inches It is believed that this
magnitude of settlement should be acceptable for a force main
Differential settlements can be anticipated in areas where the
original highway alignment changes from cut in rock or stiff older
alluvium to fill over swamp areas The differential settlements may
range up to 2 inches in 40 feet We estimate that the locations of
anticipated differential settlement areas might be at force main

made available to us The piles were 16 inch octagonal prestressed
concrete piles driven by a single acting hammer with a rated energy of
lbs per blow According to those data the production

For the force main bridge foundation we recommend that 12 inch square
prestressed concrete piles be used These piles should be driven to
about Elevation 30 and derive frictional soil resistance as well as
some end bearing for a total pile capacity up to 40 tons per pile

Generally for pile design capacity exceeding 40 tons a static pile
load test is required by the government agencies In view of the past
experience on the existing pile foundations at the highway bridge
crossing it may be possible to obtain a waiver on pile load test

Based on the available pile driving data piles may need to reach

pile lengths on the order of 90 to 95 feet
For 12 inch square piles splicing will be required to reach such
depths and would be less cost effective Although inch

feet they would be more difficult to handle and install
For cost comparison purpose the maximum pile capacity should be

inch octagonal piles penetrating to Elevation 50 feet and
