INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for Lot 76 at the Halawa Business Park Oahu
Hawaii The approximate location of the project site is shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1
PURPOSE AND SCOPE OF WORK
A geotechnical engineering exploration was previously conducted by
our firm for the Halawa Business Park development Subsequently
additional site grading was planned for Lot 76 of the development
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface conditions at the project site to provide pertinent soil data
such as shear strength compressibility swelling potential and soil
classification for the site grading of Lot 76

The scope of the exploration was performed in general accordance with
our proposal dated and included a review of available
geologic literature and previous borings in the area field
reconnaissance the drilling and sampling of four 4 borings laboratory
soil testing engineering analysis of the field and laboratory data and
report preparation
The boring logs and laboratory test data are presented in the
appendices of this report
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located on the western end of the Halawa Business
Park and it is bounded by Halawa Quarry Road on the northwest The lot is
triangular in shape and includes approximately 51 300 square feet of area
The project site was covered with piles of construction waste and
soil during our exploration There is a CRM retaining wall and a
temporary dirt road to provide access from Halawa Quarry Road on the
eastern side of the lot
PROJECT CONSIDERATION
The proposed plan is to clear and grade the site to create a
generally level area
Based on the proposed grading plan cut and fills up to about 10 feet
are anticipated to create a fairly level pad for this site
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
The site is located in the lower portion of Halawa Valley which has
been brought to its present form by a complex series of geologic events
The original Halawa Valley was a youthful valley cut into the Koolau Dome
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2155 00 Page 3
during a lower stand of the sea Several subsequent stages of alluviation
have filled the valley floor with fluvial deposits
At an intermediate stage between two series of alluvial deposition
pyroclastic material such as cinder was laid across the mouth of the
valley by members of the Honolulu Volcanic Series These deposits of
volcanic material most likely originating from Aliamanu and Salt Lake
Craters caused a diversion of Halawa Stream to the west
Stream erosion and diversion of the course of Halawa Stream has
shaped the area to its present topography
During the last century man has been an important force in shaping
Halawa Valley to its present form with the placement of extensive fills
over much of the older alluvial deposits

Four 4 borings were drilled at the site to depths of 6 0 to 26 5
feet below the existing ground surface to explore the subsurface

Subsoil Conditions
Our recent borings encountered basically competent fill and alluvial
materials consisting of silty gravel clayey silt sandy silt silty clay
and boulders in this project site
The borings indicate that the site is underlain by a fill layer of
dense silty gravel with a thickness of about 3 to 5 feet Below this
fill a very stiff to hard clayey silt or silty clay alluvial layer was

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration encountered relatively competent subsoil
conditions at the site
Site Grading
The preliminary grading plan for this project site indicates that the
site will be graded into a fairly flat pad with a 2 percent gradient down
towards the Animal Quarantine Station The major grading work will
involve fills up to 10 feet on the eastern and southeastern portions of
the lot
Site Preparation
Prior to grading the site should first be cleared of vegetation
debris rubbish and other unsuitable materials The area to receive
fill should then be scarified and recompacted to 90 of its maximum
density as established by ASTM D 1557 78 test method
Fill Material and Compaction Requirements
Generally the on site soils except rubbish highly expansive soil
and boulders which are larger than 12 inches in size may be suitable
for use in the fill Imported fill material should be submitted to
the project soils engineer for laboratory testing and approval prior
to its intended delivery to the project site
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2155 00 Page 5
Fills should be placed in maximum 10 inch horizontal loose lifts and
compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density
Fills placed on slopes having grades steeper than 5 1 H V should be
properly keyed and benched into firm existing ground see Plate 3 for
details
A slope ratio of 2 horizontal to 1 vertical is recommended for cut
and fill slopes
Fill slopes should be constructed by over filling past the design
fill slope and then cutting back
Generally benches should be provided for slopes having a vertical
height in excess of 20 feet
In general surface runoff water should be diverted as much as
feasible away from the top of the slope Slope planting should he
incorporated as soon as practicable to reduce erosion on fill slopes
as well as alluvial soil exposed on the cut slope surface
Foundation Support
Additional exploration and testing should be performed for the
foundation design of the proposed structures when detailed plans are
available
Design Revi
Plans and specifications for the proposed project site should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comments
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the recommendations given herein If this review is not made the
geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for misinterpretation
of the reconmendations

Construction Observation
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during the site grading
construction of the lot This is to allow the project geotechnical
engineer to observe compliance with the design concepts specifications or
recommendations and to allow design changes in the event that subsurface
conditions differ from that anticipated prior to start of construction
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon such
observations
Any imported backfill material required should be select granular
materials tested and approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to
hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
upon information obtained from the borings Variations of conditions
between the borings may occur and the nature and extent of these
variations may not become evident until construction If variations then
appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations
given in this report
The locations of the borings were determined by taping from
structures indicated on the Grading Plan dated
provided by Community Planning Incorporated Elevations of the borings
were approximately determined by interpolation from elevation points on
the topographic survey map The physical location and elevation of the
borings should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the
method used
