Soil Investigation
Model House II Lot 54
Mauna Olu Subdivision Phase 1
Makaha Oahu Hawaii
In accordance with your request we have made a study of the near
surface soil conditions at the above referenced lot to provide foundation
recommendations for the proposed model house construction The general
location of the lot is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
Our study consisted of drilling three shallow borings laboratory
testing of the swelling potential of the soils and reviewing of the
subdivision site grading and preliminary house foundation plans
Detailed description of our field exploration and logs of borings along
with the laboratory testing are presented in the appendix
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations

PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
It is our understanding that the proposed model house will be a
split level and roughly L shaped structure with a partly covered lanai
area A swimming pool approximtely 24 by 48 feet in plan dimension is
planned to the south of the model house Concrete on grade slab is
planned for the house lanai swimming decks and the garage A
relatively long driveway will be constructed The proposed layout of the
model house is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
CMU retaining walls are anticipated to provide grade separation
between the different levels
Fills up to 20 feet thick and cuts up to 3 feet deep are planned
for the proposed construction
SITE CONDITIONS
The lot is located on the northeastern corner of the intersection of
Roads F and A in the Mauna Olu Subdivision Makaha Oahu Hawaii
The lot generally slopes down from the northeast to the southwest at
a 5 1 H V grade
SUBSURFACE SOIL CONDITIONS
Three borings were drilled on the lot to depths ranging from 8 0 to
10 0 feet below the existing grade at the approximate locations shown on
the Site Plan Plate 2

The borings encountered about 1 to 2 feet of stiff low to
moderately expansive dark brown clayey silt with gravel and cobbles
overlying dense gray silty sandy gravel and cobbles Borings 1 and 2
encountered 1 5 to 2 feet thick pockets of gravel and cobbles relatively
free of silts and sands
Our field exploration and review of the site grading plans indicate

of Road A
No groundwater was encountered at the time of our field exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration disclosed that the site is Situated on a talus
deposit on the lower slopes of Makaha Valley Typically the subsurface
material consisted basically of dense gray gravel cobbles and boulders
with a silty clay matrix between the rock particles The silty clay is
more predominant in the upper few feet below the ground surface
Laboratory soil tests indicate that the Silty clay matrix material is low
to moderately expansive Generally conventional grading can be used at
this site Some special subgrade preparation are recommended in order to
provide suitable foundation support for the proposed model house

Site Grading
The preliminary plans indicate that cuts will be less than 3 feet
and fills may range up to 20 feet for the proposed model house
construction Thus a significant amount of imported fill will be
required
Many boulders are anticipated to be generated from the on site
cutting or in the borrow excavation for imported fill material within the
subdivision
Prior to filling the model house site should be cleared of
vegetation and debris then scarified moisture conditioned and
recompacted to 90 of its maximum dry density established by ASTM
D 1557 78 Due to the abundance of boulders in the natural deposit
conventional compaction testing may not be practical Instead a testing
program should be first conducted at the start of the project to
determine the required number of passes by the contractor s compactor to
achieve the desired compaction The number of passes can then be used as
the criteria for compaction We recommend that an experienced soils
technician be utilized to observe the filling to verify that proper type
of fill material is used and that compaction on the fill is compacted to
the above specified density
The on site material generally less than 6 inches in size can be
used for the proposed fills In general boulders in excess of 6 inches
should be crushed to minus 6 inch size prior to being used in the fill
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Page 5
Imported materials for general fill should be of the Similar quality
as the on site material or better Boulders or rock fragments larger
than 6 inches should not be included
Fills should be placed in 10 inch maximum horizontal lifts compacted
to the above specified density Sufficient water should be incorporated
in the fill material to maintain the moisture content within S percentage
points wet of the optimum during compaction After the fill is placed
the subgrade soil should be kept moist to reduce swelling potential
Fills placed on slopes having grades steeper than 5 horizontal to 1
vertical should be keyed and benched into the existing firm ground as
shown on Plate 3
Slope ratios of 2 horizontal to 1 vertical can be used for cut or
fill slopes
House Foundations
Spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the proposed
model house construction The footings should be in firm natural ground
or compacted soil
Generally spread and wall footings on level areas should be
embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished
grade The interior footings protected by on grade slabs on all sides
may be embedded a minimum of 12 inches below the adjacent finished grade
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Page 6
An allowable net soil bearing value of 3000 p s f may be used with
one third increase allowed for wind or seismic loading
If footings are located on a sloping area the footing should be
embedded an additional amount to obtain a 5 foot minimum horizontal
set back distance measured from the outer edge of the footing out to the
Slope face This additional embedment depth is required to help resist
the downhill slope creep movement and to develop the recommended bearing
Capacity of the soil
Slab On Grade
Concrete slab on grade is planned for the house and the swimming
pool constructions It is recommended that a minimun 12 inch thickened
edge embedment be utilized to provide a protective barrier against future
water intrusion under the house and covered lanai slab area
To minimize the swelling potential of the soil the subgrade should
be kept moist from the start of construction to the concrete placement
It is recommended that on grade slabs be placed over a 4 inch layer
of crushed aggregate base course and separated from wall foundations
A vapor barrier membrane may be considered over the base course
layer if a moisture barrier is deemed necessary to protect the floor
covering

Retaining Walls
It is our understanding that retaining walls up to 10 feet high are
planned throughout the house and around the swimming pool to provide
grade separation The guidelines listed below for wall design should
be followed
1 For lateral earth pressures the following can be used
Walls unrestrained at the top 35 p s f equivalent
active condition level fluid pressure
backfill per foot of depth
Wall restrained at the top 55 p s f equivalent
at rest condition fluid pressure
level backfill per foot of depth
2 An allowable bearing value of up to 3000 p s f is recommended for
foundations bearing on the stiff soil
53 A passive earth resistance of 250 p s f per foot of depth may be
used for the wall footing on level areas The passive pressure
does not apply where the ground Slopes down in front of the wall
4 A base friction factor of 0 4 can be used against sliding of the
wall
The retaining wall foundations on relatively flat areas less than
5 horizontal to 1 vertical should be embedded a minimum of 18
inches below the outside finished grade

Wall foundations situated on slopes should be embedded an
additional amount to maintain a minimum 8 foot horizontal set back
distance measured from the outside slope face to the edge of the
wall footing
6 A subsurface drainage system subdrains or weepholes should be
utilized behind the walls Subdrains should be properly
constructed to drain freely by gravity onto unobstructed areas
Swimming Pool
For the planned swimming pool a lateral earth pressure of 55
p s f equivalent fluid pressure per foot of depth should be used for
walls restrained at the top at rest condition with level backfill
Typically the swelling potential of the on site soil increases
with decreasing moisture content therefore the swimming pool
excavation should be kept moist all the time
Drainage
The finish lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings should
be shaped to shed water away from the foundations and to avoid ponding
condition Also it is advised that gutter systems should be installed
around the house and that gutter water should be diverted far away from
the foundations and walkways Excessive landscape watering near the
foundations and slab should also be avoided
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 945 50 Page 9
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent excess water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper
performance of the above recommended foundations and on grade slab
since ponded water could cause excessive subsurface soil saturation and
subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations
should be properly backfilled tight against footing immediately after
setting of the concrete to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained
to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundation
Road Pavement
Asphalt concrete pavement may be used for the long driveway It is
anticipated that the driveway will mostly be in cut in the boulderly
talus deposit and the subgrade Support characteristic will vary
considerably To provide a more uniform subgrade support the subgrade
should be scarified to a depth of 6 inches moisture conditioned and
recompacted to 90 of the ASTM D 1557 78 maximum density Oversized
boulders should be crushed or removed during scarification so that a
uniform subgrade surface will result

Based on our field observations and previous experience the
following preliminary pavement design section is recommended
2 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 Inch Base Course
8 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
The recommended section considers medium traffic and that good
drainage will be provided adjacent to paved areas
Design Review
Plans and specifications for site grading and house foundation
should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written
comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the site grading benching filling footing
excavation and slab subgrade moistening operations be observed by the
Soils engineer during construction to see that adequate compaction has
been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with
the findings of our investigation The soils engineer s observation
should also include tests to record compaction of the fill
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above inspections

Field Exploration
a ee ee
The subsurface conditions at the lot were explored by drilling 3
test borings at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2 A truck mounted drill rig using 4 inch diameter continuous
flight auger was used to advance the borings
The soils encountered were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by one of our engineers who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory A graphic
presentation of the materials encountered in the borings are presented
in the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 3 All soils were classified
in accordance with the Unified Soil Classification System
In the borings soil samples were obtained by driving a 2 inch
Standard split spoon sampler or a 2 4 inch I D split barrel sampler
with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts used to
drive the sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the log of borings at
the appropriate sample depths
