SUMMARY OF RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration encountered stiff silty clay
soils beneath the site Laboratory testing and
previous experience have demonstrated that this
material has low to moderately high swelling
potential when saturated from a dry condition
Special foundation design is recommended to reduce
the effects of the low to moderately expansive
soil
To utilize a slab on grade foundation the
recommended site improvement technique is to place
a minimum 1 foot thick capping layer of
non expansive granular material below the
foundation
We recommend that spread or continuous footing
foundations be embedded a minimum of 18 inches
below the finished grade
The text of this report should be referred to for
detailed and special design recommendations

evaluation performed for the proposed new additions for the Leeward

general location of the site is shown on the Project Location Map Plate

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
Based on the preliminary plans provided to us the proposed
addition will consist of three buildings The dimensions of the major
addition which will be located on the northeastern side of the existing
building will be approximately 96 by 150 feet in plan
The secondary new addition will be located on the eastern side of
the existing building and will be approximately 40 by 85 feet in plan
dimension
The third proposed addition will be located in the northern garden
area at a distance of 40 feet from the existing building and will be
about 25 by 50 feet in plan dimension
Concrete slab on grade is planned for the building floors and
walkways
Based on the preliminary design drawings the floor elevation of
the new additions will match the existing building thus some minor site
grading is anticipated

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located on the north side of Jade Street in
Makaha Valley Oahu Hawaii Most of the site is occupied by an
existing nursing home facility which consists of the nursing home
building two asphaltic concrete parking areas and a well maintained
lawn
The site is bounded by Jade Street to the south and by vacant land
to the other sides
Most of the proposed additions will be to the east of the existing
nursing home building partially in the lawn area and partially in vacant
land which appears to have been previously used for farming This
vacant area is covered with tall grasses and occasional thick growths of
haole koa
The topographic relief of the site is generally low with a slight
Slope to west However in the vacant land to the east of the existing
building there is a windrow of soil and a large pile of boulders which
stand approximately 5 and 10 feet respectively above the surrounding
areas
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The exploration program consisted of drilling seven borings to
depths of 9 to 17 3 feet below the existing ground All borings were
drilled with a truck mounted drilling rig at the approximate locations
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general the site is covered with a 2 5 to 7 5 foot thick layer
of reddish brown silty clay underlain by alluvium consisting of dense
sand gravel cobbles and boulders The surface soils were found to be
very stiff and somewhat dry at the time of our exploration except in
Borings B 1 and B 2 which are located in the lawn area

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration disclosed that the lot is underlain by
reddish brown silty clay The laboratory test results indicate that
this material has low to moderately high expansion potential
Therefore the foundation will require special design and construction
procedures in order to reduce the shrink swell effects of the near
surface soils when wetted
Lot Grading
It appears that the site grading will involve a maximum of 4 foot
cut and 2 foot fill
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limit should be cleared of vegetation debris rubbish and other
deleterious materials These materials should be removed and disposed
of properly off site Any loose or soft spots encountered at fill areas
should be over excavated down to firm subsoils and then replaced with
compacted non expansive off site select material The fill areas
should then be scarified to a depth of six 6 inches moisture
conditioned and recompacted to 90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM
D 1557 78
The fills and backfills within the top 12 inches minimum below
the on grade slabs should consist of non expansive soils such as select
borrow crusher waste or coral to reduce the effects due to soil
swelling Where cut is anticipated such as the western portion of the
major new addition the on site soil should be over excavated to provide
a 12 inch minimum thickness of non expansive select fill capping
underneath the on grade slabs
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2059 00 Page 6
The on site cut soil may be used for fill if they are placed below
12 inches from the slab bottom and compacted under a moist condition of
about 5 7 above optimum moisture content
All imported fill material should be tested and approved by the
soils engineer prior to transportation to the site
The fill should be placed in maximum 10 inch thick loose level
lifts and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density per
ASTM D 1557 78
Foundation
A spread or continuous footing foundation may be used to support
the proposed structures
In order to reduce the effects of the expansive soil we recommend
that the expansive clays be over excavated down to a depth of 12 inches
below the bottom of the individual footings and then replaced with
non expansive borrow soils such as crusher waste select borrow or
coral The replaced soils should be compacted to a minimum of 90 of
its maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78 The footings can then be
placed on the compacted non expansive soil
Alternatively the footings may be embedded a minimum of 18 inches
below the lowest adjacent grade and the backfill should be compacted to
a minimum 90 of its maximum dry density
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f can be used for footings
bearing on compacted fill or in situ soil
If a footing is located next to a utility line it should extend to
the bottom of the utility trench to reduce footing settlement due to
settlement of the trench backfill
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2059 00 Page 7
Surface water should be diverted away from the foundation to avoid
ponding of surface run off water that may affect the expansive soils
around the foundation
Slab on Grade
In order to reduce the expansive effects of the soils we recommend
that a minimum 1 foot thick capping layer of non expansive material be
placed below the slab on grade foundation
The expansive clay below the slab should be removed for a minimum
depth of one foot below outside finished grade and replaced with
non expansive off site borrow materials such as select borrow crusher
waste or coral compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry
density per ASTM D 1557 78 The underlying silty clay soil remaining
should be wetted by water sprinkling and then maintained in a moist
condition prior to concrete placement
A 4 inch layer of 3 Fine cushion fill should be provided below the
slab In order to protect against capillary moisture infiltration a
vapor barrier may be incorporated below the slab
The thickened edge slab footings should either bear on the
compacted non expansive fill with a minimum 12 inch footing embedment or
the thickened edges should be embedded a minimum of eighteen inches
18 below the lowest adjacent grade for details of this alternate
refer to Schematic Section Plate 3
Drainage
The finished lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings
should be shaped to shed water away from foundations and to avoid
ponding condition Also it is advised that a gutter system be
installed around the building and that gutter water be diverted far away
from the building foundation and walkway Excessive landscape watering
near foundations should also be avoided
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2059 00 Page 8
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper
performance of the above recommended foundations since ponded water
could cause excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving
or loss of strength The foundation excavation should be properly
backfilled tight against the slab footing immediately after setting of
the concrete to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained
to drain all surface run off away from the slab foundation
Design Review
Plans and specifications for foundation site grading and utility
construction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and
written comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the footing excavations be observed by a
soils engineer during construction to see that adequate bearing has been
obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the
findings of our exploration
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above observations

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as
borings probes test pits and or observation wells Variations of
conditions between the field data points may occur and the nature and
extent of these variations may not become evident until construction If
variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations given in this report
The location of the field data points were determined by taping from
the existing structures indicated on the Topographic Survey Worksheet of
Lots and 820 undated prepared by Imata Associates
Inc Elevations of the data points were determined by the same map
The physical location and elevation of the field data points should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross section are based upon interpolation between field data
points and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
Water level data from the field data points was collected at the
times given on the graphic representations and or in the text of this
report This data has been reviewed and interpretations made in the
formulation of this report However it must be noted that fluctuation
may occur due to variations in rainfall tides temperature and other
factors
This report has been prepared for the exclusive use of Leeward
Nursing Home for specific application to the foundation design of the
project in accordance with generally accepted geotechnical engineering
principles and practices No other warranty expressed or implied is
