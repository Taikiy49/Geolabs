In accordance with your request we have performed a soil and
foundation investigation at the above referenced site Our work was
done in general accordance with our proposal of
The purpose of the investigation is to explore the subsurface
conditions at the site to provide soil data for use in the foundation
design of the proposed structures
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests which we
used in the engineering analysis to formulate our conclusion and
recommendations are presented in the appendices of this report
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The storm damage restoration project consists of the construction
of the following six buildings at various locations on the base to
replace structures damaged by Hurricane Iwa
Building A Fire Station
A one story structure approximately 65 by 85 feet in plan
dimension is planned The structure will be constructed of reinforced
concrete with open web steel joist roof framing concrete floor and roof
Building B Proposed Public Works Storage Building
A one story structure approximately 50 by 110 feet in plan
dimension is planned It will be constructed of CMU walls asphaltic
concrete flooring and built up roofing Maximum column loads up to 36
kips and wall loads of 1 8 kips foot are anticipated Anticipated
vehicle traffic will include 4000 1lb capacity forklift trucks
Building C Brigade Service Support Group Warehouse
A one story warehouse approximately 80 by 238 feet in plan
dimension is planned The structure will be of pre engineered metal
construction with masonry and metal walls metal roof and concrete
floor Maximum column loads are anticipated to be about 35 kips
Anticipated vehicle traffic inside the warehouse will include 4000 1b
and 6000 1b capacity forklift trucks
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1333 00 Page 3
Building D Electronic Communications Maintenance Shop
A one story structure approximately 70 by 138 feet in plan
dimension is planned The structure will be of pre engineered metal
construction with concrete slab on grade The building will include
office space work areas and vehicle parking and repair areas
Building E Proposed First Radio Battalion Storage Building
A one story structure approximately 40 by 100 feet in plan
dimension is planned The building will be of pre engineered metal
construction with concrete slab on grade Maximum column loads of about
35 kips are anticipated Anticipated vehicle traffic will include
6000 1b capacity forklift trucks
Building F Auto Maintenance Shop
A one story structure approximately 30 by 100 feet is planned
The building will be of pre engineered metal construction with CMU and
metal walls metal roof and concrete slab on grade Maintenance of
heavy equipment will be performed within this building Heavy vehicle
traffic including capacity forklift trucks is anticipated

Site Grading
Up to 4 feet of fill may be required at Buildings D E and F In
general the other sites will require fills of less than two feet
Demolition of existing structures are required at Buildings A B and C
Site Preparation
Existing concrete slabs foundations and asphaltic concrete
pavements below the structures should be removed
Vegetation organic or other deleterious material should be removed
The existing ground should be compacted to a minimum of 95 of its
maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78
Fill Material
Imported material will be required for the fill The material to
be imported should be non expansive free of organic matter and
should form a dense stable mass when compacted to the required
densities
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1333 00 Page 17
In general the structural fill material should be well graded
granular material less than 3 inches in size with less than 20
passing a 200 sieve The soil should also provide a minimum CBR
value of 15
Fill Placement
Fills should be placed in horizontal lifts not exceeding 6 inches
in compacted thickness and compacted to a minimum of 95 of its
maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78
The site should be so graded to provide drainage of surface runoff
away from all foundations
Cesspools
Cesspools may be required for Buildings D E and F Based on the
coral sand and gravel layer encountered in 9 and 10
drilled in these areas it appears that cesspool disposal is feasible
Design Review
Plans and specifications for site grading and foundation
construction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and
written comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Page 18
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the site grading spread footing
excavations and subgrade preparation for slabs and pavements be
observed by the soils engineer during construction This is to see
that adequate compaction has been obtained and that the subsurface
conditions are consistent with the findings of our investigation and
that densification is attained
If conditions encountered are different from those presented in
this report then appropriate modifications to the design should be
made

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
ed
Moisture content and unit weight determinations were performed
on selected samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of
soil properties The results of these tests are presented on the
boring logs at the appropriate sample depths
To further identify soil characteristics five mechanical sieve
analysis tests were performed The test results are presented on
Plates B 1l
Four laboratory CBR tests were performed on bulk samples to
evaluate the subgrade support characteristics of the soils for
pavement design The test results are summarized on Plate B 2
Four direct shear tests were performed on selected undisturbed
samples to evaluate the shear strength parameters of the subsoils
The test results are presented on Plates B 3 through B 6
Seven one inch ring swell tests were performed on undisturbed
and remolded samples to evaluate the swelling potential of the soils
The test results are summarized on Plate B 7
