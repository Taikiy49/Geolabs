Dear Mr. Siegrist: 
This letter report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations performed 
during the foundation probing and grouting operations for Hangar 1790 as part of the ASBP 
C-130J Facilities Upgrades project between October 24 and November 3, 2023. 
The project site is located within the U.S. Coast Guard Station at Barbers Point in the 
Ewa District on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii.  The project location and general vicinity are 
shown on the Project Location Map, Plate 1.  
Due to potential subsurface cavities and/or voids within the underlying coral 
formation, a probing and grouting program to locate and grout potential cavities beneath the 
foundations was specified for the project. The probe hole locations were surveyed and 
marked in the field by the general contractor (The Whiting-Turner Contracting Company) 
prior to the probing operations. The probe holes were spaced at about 7 to 10 feet on center 
along the building’s continuous footings. Probing and grouting were conducted at the 
existing grade conditions generally about +15 feet Mean Sea Level (MSL). 
The probing and grouting work was performed by Kelikai, Inc. The probe holes were 
drilled with a Bretta T24 drill rig equipped with a 4-inch tricone rock drill bit. The probe holes 
were about 4-inch diameter and extended to about 13 feet below the existing ground 
surface. The probe holes generally extended to a depth of at least 10 feet below the bottom 
of footing elevations. A summary of our foundation probe drilling and grouting observations 
is provided on Plates 3.1 through 3.3. The materials encountered during the probe drilling 
operations generally consisted of tan coralline materials. 
After completion of the probe drilling operations, the drilling rods were removed, and 
the probe hole was filled using a PVC tremie pipe with a grout mix consisting of 20 gallons 
of water to four bags of cement and 350 pounds of sand. A Chemgrout CG500 mixer

equipment was used to mix and pump grout through PVC tremie into the probe holes.
Additional probe holes were drilled and grouted around the probe holes with grout intakes
greater than six times the theoretical volume.
A total of 42 probe holes were drilled and grouted for the Hangar 1790 extension.
Grout overage ranged from about 141 to 3,680 percent of the theoretical hole volume. It
should be noted that concrete slabs were encountered at probe locations P-4 and P-11C
at depths of about 2 to 3 feet below the existing ground surface. The contractor removed
the concrete slabs at those two locations, and the probes were drilled to the design tip
elevation. A Probe Hole Identification Plan is presented on Plate 2.
In summary, the foundation probing and grouting operations for the project have
been conducted in general conformance with the intent of the project requirements.
Based on our observations, it is our professional opinion that the voids and/or cavities
encountered at the structure foundation locations have been grouted and that the treated
subgrade area should provide the recommended foundation support for the new
proposed building foundations.
We appreciate the opportunity to be of service to you on this project. If you have
questions or need additional information, please contact our office.
