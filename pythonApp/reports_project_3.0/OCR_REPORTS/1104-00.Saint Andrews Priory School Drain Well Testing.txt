This report summarizes the findings of our test borings and
percolation testing and presents our recommendations for the
design of a disposal system for the ponded surface water
The purpose of the investigation was to evaluate the subsurface
conditions in areas of surface ponding in order to formulate
design recommendations for drain well disposal
The investigation consisted of drilling two 2 borings in the
areas of surface ponding visual observations at the site and
percolation testing of the underlying soil strata

SITE DESCRIPTION
The area of study is located on the grounds of St Andrew s
Priory School at Queen Emma Square in Honolulu The site is
occupied by the existing school buildings and there are nume
rous underground utilities within the school grounds
The surface ponding of water occurs during rainstorms and
appears to be the result of poor site drainage and low per
meability surface soils
REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The site is located on the Honolulu Coastal Plain which is com
posed of interbedded alluvial marine coral and volcanic
black sand deposits of Pleistocene and Recent age
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our test borings encountered a surface layer approximately 3
feet thick of brown clayey silt Below this surface layer
black volcanic cinder sand was encountered to a depth of about
10 feet and was underlain by alluvial deposits of brown silty
sand with gravel to depths of 14 5 and 23 feet in Borings 1 and
2 respectively Below this the borings penetrated decomposed
coral materials to 30 feet the maximum depth drilled

Percolation Tests
Falling head percolation tests were performed in the two
drilled holes
After completion of the bore hole drilling each hole was
filled with water and allowed to saturate Each bore hole was
refilled with water and the water allowed to percolate through
the subsoil The drop of water level was monitored and re
corded
A constant head percolation test was performed in Boring 1
however the results of this test were not used for analysis as
we feel that the water was flowing out through a cavity encoun
tered between 27 and 28 feet We feel that this cavity in the
coral material is not typical of the local subsurface condi
tions

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our observations at the site it appears that the sur
face ponding of water at the school ground is the result of
poor site drainage combined with low permeability surface soils
which do not allow the rainwater to percolate rapidly into the
underlying sandy soils
Our test borings and percolation tests indicate that the sub
surface soils are low to moderately permeable and can provide
limited disposal of some surface water by means of drain wells
Drain Well Design
Our field observations and percolation test results indicate
that the black sand alluvium and coral subsoils below the site
have significant amounts of fines which reduce the permeability
of the deposits Our test data indicate rather slow percola
tion rates Therefore it appears that the drain wells must be
designed with a substantial storage Capacity to collect the
runoff and allow extended periods of time for percolation of
the accumulated water through the soils
