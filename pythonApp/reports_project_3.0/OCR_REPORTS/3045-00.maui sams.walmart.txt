This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Maui Sam s Wholesale Club Wal Mart located at the
intersection of Haleakala Highway and Dairy Road near the Kahului Airport on the island
of Maui Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the site are shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering recommenda
tions resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering analyses

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed project consists of the construction of a new wholesale warehouse
facility in Kahului on the island of Maui Hawaii We understand the proposed warehouse
facilities will include construction of an approximately 128 000 square foot building with
a below grade loading dock area for trucks and a paved parking lot with 1 007 stalls as
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The proposed building superstructure will consist of a combination of load bearing
concrete block walls and steel columns supporting the roof with steel joist girders and bar
joists The finished floor elevation is planned at 14 feet Mean Sea Level MSL
The truck loading ramp area is anticipated to be a below grade ramp The height
of retaining wall associated with this structure is not known at this time We understand
that standard duty flexible pavements are planned for the parking lot areas and special
heavy duty flexible and or rigid pavements are planned for the truck drive areas
Based on information provided by Wal Mart maximum column loads on the order
of 145 kips including live loads are anticipated for the proposed warehouse structure
Maximum column uplift loads are about 30 kips The maximum uniform load on
continuous footings is six 6 kips per lineal foot The estimated maximum uniform floor
slab load is 250 pounds per square foot p s f with a maximum concentrated floor slab
load of 16 kips

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The borings drilled for this exploration generally encountered dry to moist sandy
and clayey silt fill soils down to depths of about two 2 to eight feet 8 across the site
These fill soils generally were encountered in a matrix containing abundant gravel rocks
cobbles and boulders The consistency of the silts ranged from loose and dry near the
surface to very stiff and moist with increasing depths We believe that past uses for the
site included cultivation of sugar cane Cane fields are commonly ripped and turned to
depths of up to five feet 5 which results in a surficial soil profile that may appear to be
fill The engineering properties of such disturbed native soils are those of undocumented
fill
The surficial fill soils were generally underlain by alluvial sandy soils residual silty
soils and basalt in a moderately to highly weathered state The alluvial sands are
generally medium dense to very dense containing abundant gravel at greater depth The
residual silts are derived from the underlying basalt formation and graded from medium
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3045 00 Page 7
stiff to hard Although weathered the basalt formation encountered by our exploration
should be able to provide relatively good foundation support characteristics and should
be generally easier to excavate than unweathered rock
Groundwater was encountered in the borings drilled at the time of our field
exploration from depths ranging from about 7 to 15 feet below the existing ground
surface corresponding to elevations of 1 to 7 feet MSL The higher groundwater level
encountered may be a perched groundwater condition We encountered groundwater
in only 3 of the 20 test pits excavated for this exploration at depths ranging from about
6 to 12 feet below the ground surface It should be noted that groundwater depths are
subject to change due to rainfall time of year and tidal fluctuations

Foundations
The proposed new warehouse facility will be a one story structure situated on a
relatively level site with stockpile mounds Based on the subsurface conditions
encountered in our exploration we believe that a composite foundation system may be
used to support the large structural loads generated by the proposed warehouse facility
The composite system should consist of spread footings for a significant portion of the
proposed warehouse and either deepened footings drilled piers or driven precast
prestressed concrete piles supporting the structural slab over the drainage channel

Drilled Pier Foundations
As an alternative to spread footings for support of the structural slab over the
drainage channel we recommend that drilled piers caisson be considered for
foundation support The drilled pier foundations will derive their load supporting
capacity primarily from friction between the pier shaft and surrounding residual
soils and alluvial sands We recommend that minimum 16 inch diameter drilled
piers be used Allowable axial load capacities for the drilled piers may be
estimated using a frictional resistance of 500 p s f in the compacted engineered
fill soils and underlying residual and alluvial soils The drilled piers should be
spaced a minimum of three 3 diameters center to center to avoid reduction in
axial load capacity
We anticipate that some sloughing and significant subsurface groundwater will be
encountered during drilling of the piers Therefore unit prices for casing and
dewatering should be obtained during bidding Isolated hard horizons or boulders
may be encountered in pier drilling operations Therefore unit prices for rock
drilling should also be obtained during bidding and should be available as a
contingency during construction
Installation of drilled piers should be observed by a representative of Geolabs
Hawaii to confirm that the depth of piers are sufficient and that the piers are
sufficiently cleaned out at the bottoms Since the axial load capacity will be derived
from frictional resistance between the pier shaft and the in situ soils extra care
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3045 00 Page 12
should be exercised in drilling of the pier holes selection of the concrete mix
design and placement of concrete
Prompt placement of concrete in the drilled pier shafts is recommended to reduce
the potential of caving in of the holes Pier shafts should not be left open
overnight Pier shafts should be thoroughly cleaned and free of loose material
prior to placement of concrete The concrete mix should be designed to reduce
shrinkage in order to maintain frictional contact between the concrete of the piers
and the surrounding in situ soils Tne concrete should be placed in a manner that
will reduce segregation of the aggregates and will reduce the potential formation
of rock pockets cold joints and other discontinuities
Lateral load resistance of drilled piers is a function of the stiffness of the
surrounding soil the stiffness of the pier length and diameter of the pier and
allowable deflection at the top of the pier Resistance due to passive pressure may
be calculated using an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per
foot of depth p c f for the drilled piers
Driven Pile Foundations
As an alternative to spread footings or drilled piers for support of the structural slab
over the drainage channel we considered driven precast prestressed concrete
piles Because of the high frequency of cobbles and boulders encountered during
our exploration and the possibility of displacement of the adjacent concrete
channel walls predrilling of the upper 10 to 12 feet below existing grade would be
prudent for driven piles at this location Frictional supporting capacity from the
upper 10 feet would then be neglected in design computations likely resulting in
pile tip elevations well below the depth of our exploration Therefore driven piles

Lateral Earth Pressures
All retaining structures should be designed to resist the lateral earth pressures due
to adjacent soils and surcharge effects We understand that Wal Mart desires at
rest pressures The recommended at rest lateral earth pressure for design
expressed in equivalent fluid pressure is 50 p c f for an assumed level backfill
The value provided above is for on site soils or imported granular soils Generally
walls whose tops are not free to deflect beyond about 0 5 percent of the wall
height or are restrained should be designed for the at rest condition This lateral
earth pressure does not include hydrostatic pressures that might be caused by
groundwater trapped behind the walls
Retaining walls should be well drained to reduce the build up of hydrostatic
pressures A typical drainage system would consist of a one foot 1 to two foot
2 wide zone of permeable material No 3B Fine gravel or ASTM C 33 No 67
gradation immediately adjacent to the wall with a perforated pipe perforations
down at the base of the wall discharging to a discharge facility A filter fabric
such as Mirafi 140N or equivalent should be placed between the drainage material
and the compacted backfill material As an alternative a prefabricated drainage
product such as MiraDrain or EnkaDrain may be used instead of the drainage
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3045 00 Page 17
material with filter fabric The prefabricated drainage product should also
hydraulically connected to a perforated pipe at the base of the wall
Surcharge stresses due to areal surcharges line loads and point loads within a
horizontal distance equal to the height of the wall should be considered in the
design For uniform surcharge stresses imposed on the loaded side of the wall
a rectangular distribution with uniform pressure equal to 45 percent of the
surcharge pressure acting on the entire height of the wall which is restrained at
the top may be used in design Additional analyses during design may be needed
to evaluate the surcharge effects of point loads and line loads
