This report presents the results of our Soils Engineering and
Geologic Investigation for the proposed Maunawili Sewer Line
Section 2 located in The purpose of
this study was to determine the general subsurface conditions
and develop pertinent soil and foundation recommendations for

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed sewer line with a length of approximately 7600 feet
begins on the south side of Kalanianaole Highway near its inter
section with Maunawili Road and extends southward to the existing
sewage treatment plant on Maunawili Road
The sewer line will consist of reinforced concrete pipe varying
from 24 to 18 inches in diameter to be placed between 8 and 20
feet below the existing ground surface The last short section
between Stations 72 17 and 76 19 will consist of a 10 inch diameter
vitrified clay pipe The sewer invert varies from Elevation 12
feet at Station 0 00 to Elevation 84 feet at the termination of the
project at Station 76 19 The proposed sewer line connects into
the existing sewer line at the northwest corner of the treatment
plant on Maunawili Road
Throughout the lower reaches the proposed alignment essentially
parallels Maunawili Stream along its eastern bank At approxi
mately Station 52 30 the first stream cross ng is encountered
Four subsequent crossings of the stream or its tributaries occurs
from Stations 52 30 to the last crossing at approximately Station

Geology
The sewer line is located on the northeast side of the elongated
Koolau Volcanic Range which forms the southeast portion of Oahu
After the formation of the Koolau Volcanic Range and the asso
ciated Kailua Volcanic Series in the Kaneohe area the surface
soils were deeply dissected by stream erosion Subsequent emer
gence and submergence of the area with concomitant changes of
the sea level resulted in the area of the Maunawili flood plain
changing from a lagoonal area located at the mouth of steep sided
valleys to a swamp area during periods of lower sea level
The flood plain was subsequently dissected by the meandering
Maunawili Stream which deposited layers of coarse gravel and
cobble outwash from the nearby mountains The oscillating sea

level resulted in an alternating sequence of gravel layers
interspersed between layers of silty clay and organic clayey
silt swamp deposits These changes in the environment in
which the soils were deposited in conjunction with the meander
ing stream have produced highly variable subsurface conditions

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Poor subsurface conditions were encountered along the proposed
alignment except in the upper Mauka section near Borings 14
through 17 Throughout the Makai section soft organic soils
extended from the surface to below the proposed sewer line
invert In the Center section the organic materials were
underlain at approximately the sewer line invert by a layer of
river gravel cobble and boulders with a silty clay matrix
Throughout these areas the proposed inverts are generally be
low the groundwater level and will require dewatering In the
Mauka section boulders or decomposed basalt were encountered
above the invert elevations and blasting or other special excas
vatt on methods may be required
Earth Pressure Loads on Pipe
Loads on buried conduits are influenced by the width of the
trench the unit weight the backfill material and the fric
tional resistance between the backfill materials and the trench
walls For calculating the vertical loads on the pipes a unit
weight of 120 PCF and a coefficient of friction of 0 25 is
recommended Earth forces upon the pipe increase rapidly with
the width of the trench Therefore the trench width Bd
should be kept to a minimum The enclosed Plate 2 indicates
three alternative trench types

W
Bearing Capacities and Settlement
Throughout the lower portion of the site the existing soils
have very low bearing capacities due to their high moisture
contents and low wet strengths The sewer line construction
will not materially increase the vertical loading upon these
soils if additional fills are not placed upon the surface
The resulting net loading will be essentially zero thereby
producing negligible vertical settlements Future development
of the area adjoining the sewer line could produce differen
tial settlements if additional loads are placed upon the
compressible materials below the sewer line
Pipe Bedding
Distribution of the stresses against the bottom of a pipe has
a great effect upon the load supporting capacity of the pipe
Pipe bedding the contact between the foundation and the pipe
is therefore an important design consideration
Due to the soft conditions throughout the lower Makai portion
and the river gravel formation encountered in the Center sec
tion it is recommended that a two foot thick crushed rock
cradle be utilized for the proposed sewer line This material
should be well compacted and brought up to within six inches
of the invert grade or to the bottom of any concrete jacket or
cradle
Page 10
W
A minimum of six inches of compacted granular bedding material
should be placed upon the crushed rock cradle and the pipe
placed on a flat trench bottom The granular bedding should
extend beyond the pipe sides a minimum width of one fourth the
outside pipe diameter and should extend halfway up the pipe
sides The remainder of the trench backfill should be in accor
dance with the City and County Standard Specifications for
Public Works Construction Section 11 3 The recommended bed
ding method is illustrated on the enclosed Plate 3
In the Mauka section the same pipe bedding should be utilized
but in this area a firm trench bottom is expected and the two
foot rock cradle can be reduced to a one foot thickness
Sewer Construction
The method and equipment used for excavation should be the
choice of the Contractor within practical limits Depths of
excavations in this operation will be from approximately 8
feet to in excess of 20 feet Hard ripping or special exca
vating methods for hard materials are not anticipated except
in the vicinity of Borings 15 through 17 where boulders and
weathered rock were encountered The remainder of the align
ment should be through relatively easily excavated soil with
low shear strengths Some difficulties can be expected in
removing the lower materials in the Center section where
boulders and large gravel may be encountered The excavated
Page 11
W
materials should not be stockpiled within 10 feet of the trench
or a distance equal to one half the depth of the excavation
whichever is greater
Sheeting and bracing to prevent a cave in of the trench walls is
recommended throughout the project Due to the low strength and
high moisture content of the predominant soils at the site spe
cial care must be exercised to prevent a failure of the trench
walls or upheaval of the trench bottom due to the excavating and
dewatering methods
The following general soil parameters are recommended for the
design of the sheeting and bracing They do not consider the
location of the groundwater level which will substantially
affect the lateral pressures Groundwater pressures must be
included after the dewatering method is selected since the
dewatering may alter the groundwater levels significantly

Backfill
The backfilling should be performed in accordance with the
City and County Standard Specifications for Public Works Con
struction The onsite soils while suitable as backfill above
the pipe are presently above the optimum moisture content and
will generally require drying to obtain the specified levels
of compaction Due to the isolated nature of the project and
providing that the area will not be utilized in the future for
dwellings or other structures sensitive to settlement a cost
Savings can be obtained by permitting the Contractor to utilize
Page 13
W
the onsite soils with minimal compactive effort Settlements
will occur within the trench due to subsequent consolidation
of these materials These settlements can be corrected some
what by overfilling the trench area approximately one foot
to compensate for the settlements that will occur following
the placement of the backfill
In the areas where the proposed sewer line will cross paved
areas or areas subject to erosion the backfilling should be
made with selected soil compacted to a minimum of 90 percent
of the maximum dry density established by ASTM D 1557 72
Properly compacted soil will reduce the settlements of the
trench backfill and greatly reduce the susceptibility to ero
sion Along the stream beds it is recommended that the trench
excavations be rip rapped to prevent the removal of the sur
face materials by stream erosion

surface conditions along the proposed alignment Variations
in the subsurface condition can be expected due to the changing
geologic environment during the deposition of the soils The
Soils Engineer should be consulted if subsurface conditions
materially different than those disclosed by the boring are
uncovered The recommendations can then be reviewed and revised
if necessary

APPENDIX
FIELD EXPLORATIONS
The subsurface soil conditions at the site were explored by
drilling seventeen test borings The borings were drilled
utilizing truck mounted rotary auger equipment with rock core
attachments and portable auger equipment for the sites with
limited access The depths of the borings ranged from 5 to
20 feet the total boring program entailed 267 5 feet of soil
drilling and five feet of rock coring The boring locations
are shown on the enclosed Site Plan and Boring Locations in
the text of this report
The soil and rocks encountered in the subsurface explorations
were classified by visual examination in the field by our
geologist who continuously supervised the drilling operations
The classifications were verified by inspection and testing
in the laboratory and are based upon the Unified Soil Classi
fication System Descriptions of the rocks are based upon
local geological terminology
The soil samples were obtained by driving a 2 4 inch diameter
or a standard penetration sampler with a 140 pound hammer
free falling a distance of 30 inches The blow counts to
drive the sampler the last 12 inches and the percent recovery
of the rock cores are shown on the enclosed Log of Borings
Plates A 1 to A 17 at the appropriate sample depths
