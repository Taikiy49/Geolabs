Based on the field exploration conducted along the proposed roadway alignment a
surface fill layer about 1 5 to 2 5 feet thick was encountered in the borings The surface fill
layer was generally underlain by hard to very hard basalt formation extending to the
maximum depths explored of about 15 feet below the existing ground surface A void was
encountered at about 3 5 feet deep in one of the borings The excavated test pits
generally encountered basalt rock formation and basaltic cobbles and gravels from the
ground surface to about 3 feet deep We did not encounter groundwater in the drilled
borings and test pits at the time of our field exploration
In general the project site is underlain by a a and pahoehoe lava flows Due to the
potential of encountering lava tubes and or cavities within the pahoehoe lava we
recommend ripping the subgrades of fill areas to a depth of at least 3 feet below the
existing ground surface to detect and collapse near surface cavities and or voids After
the fill surfaces have been ripped to a depth of at least 3 feet below the ground surface
the ground should be proof rolled with a Caterpillar D 10 bulldozer or similar size bulldozer
a minimum of four passes to provide a relatively level surface After leveling of the rugged
surfaces with a bulldozer we recommend proof rolling the fill subgrades with a large
vibratory drum roller minimum 15 tons for a minimum of eight passes to help detect and
collapse near surface cavities and or voids Yielding areas loose areas or cavities
disclosed during clearing and proof rolling operations should be over excavated and
backfilled with compacted fill materials
Cut slopes planned at the site will likely expose basalt rock formation at the slope
face In general a slope inclination of 1H 1V or flatter may be used for design of cut
slopes For permanent fill slopes constructed of granular fill materials a slope inclination
of or flatter may be used in design of the slopes
In general we recommend capping the top of the fill embankment with a minimum
2 foot thick layer of structural fill In addition we also recommend providing a minimum of
2 feet of general fill materials below the upper structural fill layer before rock fills zones for
boulder disposal within the fill embankment are used to construct the roadway
embankment In general boulders less than 5 feet in diameter may be used in rock fills
provided that the top of the rock fill layer is placed at least 4 feet below the finished

Regional Geology
The Island of Hawaii is the largest island of the Hawaiian Archipelago and covers
an area of approximately 4 000 square miles This island was formed by the eruptive
activity of five major shield volcanoes Kohala long extinct Mauna Kea some activity
during recent geologic time Hualalai last erupted in 1801 1803 and Mauna Loa
and Kilauea both active
The project site is located on the western flank of Hualalai Shield Volcano which
had been built up by the successive accumulation of pahoehoe and a a lava flows
originating from the northwestern rift zone At the project site the basalts encountered
were relatively fresh and unweathered and are estimated to be of an age on the order
of a few hundreds of years or less The lavas exposed at and near the project site were
mainly of the pahoehoe form with some clinker areas
Cavities or voids are encountered commonly in pahoehoe lava Cavities form
when the lava was still in a molten state and represent both lava tubes intra flow
cavities and inter flow cavities blisters and pockets Lava tubes form when molten
lava drains from the cooling flow leaving a hollow tube like structure which may extend
for a large distance longitudinally along the flow Inter flow cavities are generally smaller
in horizontal extent

SECTION 3 DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field exploration the project site is generally underlain by hard to
very hard basalt rock formation We did not encounter groundwater in the borings and
test pits at the time of our field exploration
Based on the plans and profiles provided we understand that cuts up to about
4 feet and fills up to about 18 feet high will be required for the proposed roadways For
permanent fill slopes a slope inclination of one and one half horizontal to one vertical
or flatter may be used in the design based on a fill embankment constructed
of structural fill and general fill as recommended herein
In general the project site is underlain by pahoehoe lava flows with signs of lava
tube features and or cavities As a result of these apparent signs of lava tube features
and or cavities we recommend giving special attention to the site preparation
recommendations prior to commencement of fill placement at the site In general we
recommend ripping the subgrades of fill areas to a minimum depth of 3 feet below the
existing ground surface to assist in detecting and collapsing cavities and or voids at the
commencement of grading After the fill surfaces have been ripped to a depth of at
least 3 feet below the ground surface the ground should be leveled with the use of a
Caterpillar D 10 bulldozer or similar size bulldozer working the surface a minimum of
four passes to provide a relatively level surface After leveling the rugged surfaces with
a bulldozer we recommend proof rolling the fill subgrades with a large vibratory drum
roller minimum 20 tons for a minimum of six passes to help detect and collapse the
near surface cavities and or voids
Yielding areas loose areas or cavities disclosed during clearing and proof rolling
operations should be over excavated and backfilled with compacted fill materials The
proof rolling operation should also be performed at the finished subgrades in cut areas
In the finished subgrades in cut areas we recommend proof rolling the cut subgrades
with a minimum 20 ton vibratory drum roller for a minimum of six passes to help detect
and collapse near surface cavities and or voids

Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork areas within the contract grading limits should be
cleared and grubbed thoroughly Vegetation debris deleterious materials and
other unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site to
reduce the potential for contamination of the excavated materials to be used as
embankment fill materials It should be noted that the contractor should exercise
care while clearing and grading at the site since potential lava tubes or cavities may
collapse under the load of heavy construction equipment
Based on our site observations some collapsible pahoehoe lavas may be present
at the ground surface at the project site These near surface materials are
relatively porous and will compress when subjected to structural and vehicular
loads Therefore we recommend ripping the fill areas of the project site to a
minimum depth of 3 feet below the existing ground surface to assist in detecting
and collapsing cavities and or voids at the commencement of grading
After the fill surfaces have been ripped to a depth of at least 3 feet below the
ground surface the ground should be leveled with the use of a Caterpillar D 10
bulldozer or similar size bulldozer working the surface a minimum of four passes to
provide a relatively level surface After leveling the rugged surfaces with a
bulldozer we recommend proof rolling the fill subgrades with a large vibratory drum
roller minimum 20 tons for a minimum of six passes to help detect and collapse
near surface cavities and or voids The vibratory drum roller should be operated at
a speed of about 300 feet per minute Yielding areas loose areas or cavities
disclosed during the clearing and proof rolling operations should be over excavated
and backfilled with compacted fill materials The depth of over excavation should
be extended until dense underlying materials are exposed and should be evaluated
in the field by a Geolabs representative
We recommend proof rolling the finished subgrades in cut areas with a minimum
20 ton vibratory drum roller for a minimum of six passes to help detect and collapse
near surface cavities and or voids The vibratory drum roller should also be
operated at a speed of about 300 feet per minute The requirement for ripping the

Cut and Fill Slopes
Based on our field observation and exploration volcanic rock formation with
occasional clinker layers and some loose cobbles boulders and clinkers were
observed near the ground surface Generally pahoehoe lava flows were observed
on the ground surface at the project site with a a lava flows in some localized areas
Therefore we anticipate that voids and cavities will likely exist in the basalt
formation characteristic of the pahoehoe lava flows
Based on the available information we anticipate that most of the cut slopes would
expose basalt rock with fractures In general we believe that the cut slopes
planned at the site may be designed with a slope inclination of 1H 1V or flatter
However we anticipate that clinker zones and or voids may be encountered at the
cut slope face In the event that clinker zones are exposed at the cut slope face
the cut slope may need to be flattened Large cavities that may be exposed on the
cut slope face should be backfilled and or grouted if necessary A Geolabs
engineer or geologist should observe and map the cut slopes during construction to
evaluate whether cut slopes need to be flattened or further recommendations need
to be implemented We believe that the permanent cut slopes in basalt formation
do not need benches for cut slopes up to 30 feet in vertical height
Permanent fill slopes constructed of the general fill and structural fill materials
recommended herein may be designed with a slope inclination of or flatter
In addition fills placed on slopes steeper than 5H 1V should be keyed and
benched The fill slope face should be finished to a relatively smooth and
well compacted surface The fill slope face should be free of large boulders
greater than 18 inches in largest dimension and should be free of voids which
would allow erosion and migration of fines to occur In addition slope planting or
other means of slope protection should be provided as soon as possible to reduce
the potential for erosion of the finished slopes

Pipe Culverts
Based on the plans provided we understand that 36 inches in diameter and
larger pipe culverts traversing the proposed roadways will be installed within the fill
areas Based on our field exploration we anticipate that the proposed culvert structures
would be underlain by hard to very hard basalt rock To provide for uniform support of
the pipe culverts we recommend supporting the pipe culverts on a minimum of 6 inches
of bedding material consisting of free draining granular materials such as
AASHTO M 43 No 67 gradation material or equivalent
The initial zone of backfill material extending from the bedding up to about
12 inches above the pipes should consist of open graded material such as
AASHTO M 43 No 67 gradation material It is critical to use free draining granular
materials to reduce the potential for formation of voids below the haunches of pipes and
to provide adequate support for the sides of the pipes Improper backfill around the pipe
culverts could result in backfill settlement and pipe damage

pipes to the top of the subgrade or finished grade should consist of granular materials
generally less than 6 inches in maximum size structural fill The backfill material
should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content placed in
maximum 12 inch level loose lifts and mechanically compacted to not less than
90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for future ground subsidence

compaction

Percolation Tests
The
permeability
of
the
in situ
soils
was
evaluated
by
performing
two Constant Head Permeability test and one Falling Head Permeability test at a
selected depth within the borings The tests were performed at a depth of about 15 feet
below the existing ground surface
The Constant Head Permeability tests were performed by pumping water into the
borehole until the water surface stabilized and remained at equilibrium for a sufficient
period of time The water height and rate of pumping were then measured to provide
data upon which to base the calculation of subsoil permeability The field permeability
test results are presented on Plates D 1 and D 2
The Falling Head Permeability test was performed by pumping water into the
borehole up to a reference level where the pumping of water was stopped The drop in
water height with time was then measured and used in the calculation of subsoil
permeability The field permeability test results are presented on Plate D 3
