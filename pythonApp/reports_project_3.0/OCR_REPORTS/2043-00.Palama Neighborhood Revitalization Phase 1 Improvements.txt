used in the engineering analysis to formulate our conclusions and
recommendations are presented in the appendix of this report

SITE DESCRIPTION
The entire project encompasses an area generally bounded by Kokea
Street North School Street and the H 1 Freeway The Phase 1 portion of
the project is situated at the southern end of the project site south of
Palama Street
The Phase 1 improvements include Momolio Street Panalaau Street and
Sing Icy Lane These streets are generally narrow fairly flat and
presently paved with asphaltic concrete
The existing asphaltic concrete pavements are in fair to poor
condition
PROJECT CONS IDERATTONS
The proposed improvements consist of the following
1 New pavement for Panalaau Street and Sing Loy Lane according to
City and County standards
2 New pavement for Momolio Street
3 Relocation or improvement of existing utility lines including
sewer water and drain under roadway areas

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Based on the boring data it appears that below the pavement the
near surface soils at the site can be generalized into two basic types
1 Alluvium stiff alluvial soils consisting of mottled dark brown
to brown clay with many boulders These clay soils are expansive
and are generally encountered in the upper 5 feet below the
pavement level These soils are generally geologically mapped as
Younger Alluvium
2 Basalt dense rock formation was encountered below the surface
clay
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings during the field
exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration encountered stiff alluvial soils over rock
formation below the existing roadway pavements
The near surface alluvial soils have high swelling potential am
therefore relatively thick pavement sections are recommended
In general hard excavation is anticipated for the deeper portions of
the utility trenches where rock formation may be encountered
The existing roadway pavements have deteriorated and in general are
in poor condition In order to provide uniform support for the new
pavement sections the existing pavements should be removed down to the
new pavement section level and reconstructed

Earth Pressure Loads on Pipe
Loads on buried conduits are influenced by the width of the trench
the size of the pipe the unit weight of backfill material and the
frictional resistance between the backfill material and the trench
side walls To compute the vertical loads on the pipes a soil unit
weight of 120 pcf and a coefficient of friction of 0 25 is
recommended Earth forces upon the pipe increase rapidly with the
width of the trench Therefore the width of the trench should be
kept to a minimm
Bearing Capacity and Settlement
The underlying stiff alluvium and rock formation has good bearing
capacity Therefore minimal settlements are anticipated for
structures supported on this layer
The new utility line construction in general will not materially
increase the vertical loading upon these underlying soils if no
additional fills are placed upon the existing ground surface The
net difference in loading between the weights of the excavated
material and backfill will be essentially small Therefore
significant settlements are not anticipated if proper construction
techniques particularly for shoring are utilized

Pipe Bedding
The stress distribution against the bottom of a pipe has a great
effect upon the load supporting capacity of the pipe Therefore the
pipe bedding is an important design consideration
For pipes bearing on the rock formation at the invert level a
minimm of six inches of compacted granular bedding material should
be placed below the pipe The granular bedding should extend beyond
the pipe sides a minimm width of one fourth the outside pipe
diameter and should extend half way up the pipe sides The bedding
material should consist of clean granular material such as 3B Fine
gravel
Trench Excavation
The anticipated depth of excavation is 6 to 8 feet The method and
equipment used for excavation should be the choice of the contractor
within practical limits
Relatively easy excavation is anticipated for the soils above the
rock formation Hard excavation is anticipated in the rock layer
The excavation should be provided with proper bracing in accordance
with the anticipated loading conditions traffic and construction
materials stockpile and equipment amd to satisfy the applicable
safety requirements The design and safety of the trench supports
should be the responsibility of the contractor
The ground adjacent to and near the top of the excavation should be
continuously monitored for cracks or dips and other indications of
movements and possible sloughing of the trench walls Such
monitoring is particularly critical in areas where existing
structures and utilities are adjacent to the trench excavation

Backfill
In general the trench backfill should be in accordance with the
Standard Specifications for Public Works Construction September
1986
Granular material such as sand should be used for the backfill up
to about one foot above the pipe to reduce the compactive effort
required and possible damage to the pipe
Most of the excavated material will consist of clay soils and
boulders which may not be suitable for reuse as backfill
Therefore imported borrow material may be required Backfill should
be mechanically compacted to a minimm of 90 of its maximm dry
density established by ASIM D 1557 Below roadway areas the
backfill within 2 feet of the finished grade should consist of select
borrow and be compacted to 95 of its maximum dry density
If practicable the trenches should be kept free of water during
backfilling
Manhole Structures
The manhole structures could bear on the dense rock formation or
stiff alluvium Structures supported on the underlying dense rock or
stiff alluvium may be designed with an allowable bearing value of up to
anticipated for these structures
The lateral earth pressure acting on the underground structures will
depend on the type of backfill used the extent of the backfill material
and the compactive effort required on the material behind the structure
walls Granular material is recommended for the backfill adjacent to the
structures to reduce the compactive effort required and to minimize the
build up of additional lateral earth pressures

Construction Observation
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during roadway grading
construction backfill of utility excavations and foundation phases of the
work This is to observe compliance with the design concepts
specifications or recommendations and to allow design changes in the event

construction The recommendations given in this report are contingent
upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and

If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
