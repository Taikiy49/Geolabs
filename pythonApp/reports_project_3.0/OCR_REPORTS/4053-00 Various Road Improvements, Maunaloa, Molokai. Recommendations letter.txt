Recommendations for Coral Select Borrow Capping
Various Road Improvements
Dear Mr Edwards
In an effort to find an economical method to reduce dust and erosion of the
existing dirt roads a thin layer of coral has been placed over some of the dirt roads in
the various areas A site visit was made on August 18 to observe the condition of
some of the older coral surfacing and the Hale Olono Road coral surfacing currently
being constructed This letter presents some guideline recommendations for the
maintenance of older roads and the construction of future roads
Hale Olono Road
The coral surfacing over Hale Olono Road was nearly completed General
guidelines for this construction were presented in our letter of Our recent
observations indicate that most of the coral surface along the road seems to be
developing a light cementation which would help to reduce dust and erosion We
recommend that the contractor continue to spray water over the surface to facilitate
additional cementation During our initial site visit in the morning the thickness of the
coral was noted to be less than the specified 6 inches in isolated areas It is important
that the coral thickness be close to 6 inches to take advantage of the strength of the coral
material During a follow up visit in the afternoon we noted the contractor was filling some
of the thin spots with additional coral Overall the coral resurfacing appeared to be
progressing satisfactorily
Maintenance of Roads
Some of the older coral roads such as the road to Kaupoa Camp were also
observed We noted many areas where the ride was very bumpy and dusty In these
deficient areas the coral surface did not exhibit cementation and often appeared to be
very thin or did not contain sufficient finer material in the gradation General guidelines for
road maintenance are presented as follows

Future Coral Roads
Future coral road construction should require that the contractor box out the road
section to provide the minimum 6 inch thickness Generally this means that the existing
grade along the road would be excavated about 6 inches and leveled Additionally the
edges of the road section should be cut to nearly vertical This would assure that the
coral section is a minimum of 6 inches thick and would help to maintain the natural
surface drainage Where the road crosses drainage ditches soil cement or concrete
surfacing may be required to reduce erosion
We noted deficient areas along the older roads where only the larger coral pieces
remained It appeared that the finer materials were eroded away This may be due to
poor gradation of the coral fill The coral should be tested prior to shipping to verify that
the material is well graded and can be compacted properly
It should be remembered that the coral surface has a limited life and cannot resist
significant erosive forces without a more permanent topping i e concrete or asphalt
