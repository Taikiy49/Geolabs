INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration performed
for the proposed Makaha 242 Reservoir No 2 Disposal Grading Plan adjacent to Kili Drive
in Waianae on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project
site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical recommendations based on our
review and analyses of the site conditions the grading plan and the subsurface information
obtained for this project The geotechnical recommendations are intended for the design of
the site preparation grading and infrastructure for the subject project only The findings and
recommendations presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this
report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Makaha 242 Reservoir No 2 Disposal Grading Plan site is located to the north
of Kili Drive in Waianae on the Island of Oahu Hawaii as shown on the Project Location
Map Plate 1 Goodfellow Brothers Inc GBI will be excavating approximately
105 000 bank cubic yards or an estimated 130 000 loose cubic yards of soil and rock from
an adjacent property to create a level cut area for the construction of the new Makaha 242
Reservoir No 2 It is proposed to use the project site for the disposal of the excavated
materials as engineered fill which may be later developed for residential use It is estimated
that approximately 10 acres of land area will be required for disposal of the excavated
materials
GEOLABS INC
W O 4320 00 Page 3
The project site is as much as 4 feet lower than Kili Drive Development of the site
for residential use would require fill placement to reduce or eliminate this grade separation
The current grading scheme for the disposal site involves the placement of fill up to
about 10 feet in thickness The lower portions of the fill embankment would consist of 3 foot
minus materials The upper 2 foot capping layer would consist of 6 inch minus material to
allow for future residential commercial development on the site The fill embankment will
serve as a buttress to aid in stabilization of the colluvial slopes to the north of the proposed
disposal site
It is anticipated that additional capping material will be required for future
development If the development plans for the area change the disposal site fill
embankment could be exploited as a source of fill material for other areas
Further details of the proposed site grading and development were not available at
the time this report was prepared

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu is composed largely of the weathered remnants of two extinct
shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The project site is located in a valley that has
been deeply incised into the western flank of the Waianae shield The subsoils at the site
consist of intercalated colluvial alluvial and marine sedimentary materials filling the floor
of the valley The base of the stratigraphic section in this area is the basalts of the
Waianae Volcanic Series
The surface soils at the site consist of dark brown silty clays and clayey silts which
grade with depth to bouldery alluvium These soils have been classified by the U S
Department of Agriculture as Pulehu stony clay loam PuB with areas of Stony Land rST
and Haleiwa silty clay HeA The soil series are formed in alluvium and colluvium derived
from basic igneous rock Permeability of these soils is moderately slow runoff is medium
and erosion hazard is moderate
The surface of the site is exposed soil with a relatively thin vegetative cover
Infiltration into these clayey soils is slow so the majority of runoff generated at the site
moves as sheetflow to North Makaha Stream which forms the western boundary of the site

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located on the northern side of Kili Drive near the former Sheraton
Makaha Valley Resort in Waianae on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project is situated
adjacent to and to the west of the existing Makaha 242 Reservoir and accompanying pump
station The project site is bounded by existing settlement basins referred to as the ponds
and North Makaha Stream an intermittent stream to the west Kili Drive and undeveloped
land to the south the existing reservoir and pump station to the east and lower south facing
slopes of Puu Keaau to the north The site and its immediate surroundings are shown on the
General Site Plan Plate 2
The project site is underlain primarily by interbedded alluvial and colluvial soils with
a thin mantle of alluvium exposed at the ground surface Based on our site reconnaissance
and test pit exploration it appears that portions of the site have been partially graded and
that an area in the south central portion adjacent to Kili Drive was previously used as a
boulder disposal site
At the time of our exploration the site was vacant with no developed infrastructure
The surface of the site was thinly vegetated with haole koa kiawe and dry grasses
Scattered piles of refuse and some abandoned vehicles were observed on the site
There is an existing drain inlet near the eastern boundary of the site on the northern
side of Kili Drive This drain inlet discharges to the surface of the site During our field
exploration it was also noted that there is a shallow drainage swale along Kili Drive in the
western portion of the site
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our subsurface exploration program consisted of excavating and sampling 24 test
pits designated as Test Pit Nos 1 through 24 extending to depths of about 4 to 10 feet
below the existing ground surface The approximate locations of the borings and test pits
are shown on the General Site Plan Plate 2
GEOLABS INC
W O 4320 00 Page 6
In general the test pits encountered a thin surface layer of desiccated clay soil at the
surface This graded with depth to a stiff to very stiff moist dark brown clay with varying
amounts of boulders and gravel This surface clay locally referred to as Adobe is
expansive and has relatively poor engineering characteristics unless properly managed
during site preparation and grading work The surface layer of Adobe generally extended
to a depth of about 5 feet below the existing ground surface at the locations explored by test
pit excavation
Underlying the surface clay soils our exploration encountered relatively competent
alluvial soils generally consisting of dense and weakly cemented gravelly sands with some
cobbles and boulders These alluvial materials extended to the maximum depth explored
of 10 feet below the existing ground surface
Groundwater was not encountered in the test pits at the time of the field exploration
However groundwater levels can fluctuate depending on seasonal precipitation groundwater
withdrawal and or injection and other factors Given the alluvial nature of the deeper
subsoils encountered at the site there is a potential for encountering ephemeral ground
water in gravel beds and lenses following periods of rainfall
For detailed descriptions of the subsurface conditions encountered please refer to
the Logs of Test Pits presented on Plates A 1 through A 8 of Appendix A
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field exploration the project site is generally underlain by a surface layer
of stiff to very stiff expansive clay underlain by gravelly and bouldery alluvial materials
These materials are generally competent and should provide adequate support for the
proposed fill placement if the recommendations presented herein are followed
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered and the grading planned at the site
the principal geotechnical engineering considerations for the disposal site development
GEOLABS INC
W O 4320 00 Page 7
include the site preparation of the expansive clay soils and the placement of coarse rock fill
at the site
In order to provide a firm competent subgrade for the proposed rock fill embankment
we recommend that the upper expansive clay soils be scarified moisture conditioned and
recompacted Loose boulders encountered at the surface and produced during the
scarification operation should be stockpiled for later incorporation into the rock fill
embankment Further discussion of the site preparation is provided in a subsequent section
of this report Yielding areas loose areas or cavities disclosed during the site preparation
operations should be also over excavated and backfilled with compacted fill material
We also recommend that the top of the fill embankment be capped with a minimum
of 2 feet of structural fill materials 6 inch minus granular materials to provide a firm base on
which to construct future improvements Below the structural fill layer we believe that the
fill section may consist of general fili or rock fill materials Considering the relative thickness
of the fills planned about 10 to 15 feet thick we believe that rock fragments and boulders
up to 3 feet in maximum dimension may be disposed of in the deeper fills provided that the
recommendations pertaining to rock fill placement described in this report are implemented
in the field during grading of the site
Detailed discussion of these items and our geotechnical engineering design
recommendations are presented in the following sections of this report
Site Grading
Based on the current available plans for the subject project grading for the proposed
disposal site development will involve the placement of fill on the order of about 10 to 15 feet
at the site It is understood that the fill material will be generated from excavation work for
the construction of a reservoir at an adjacent site It is further understood that the excavated
materials will consist primarily of rock and rock fragments and that the material would be
suitable for use as general fill materials provided that the materials are processed to meet
the gradation requirements recommended in this report In addition the materials may

Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork areas within the project grading limits should be thoroughly
cleared and grubbed Vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable materials
should be removed and disposed of properly off site to reduce the potential for
contamination of the excavated materials
In order to provide a firm competent subgrade for the future fill embankment we
recommend that the upper clay soils be scarified to a depth of about 2 feet moisture
conditioned to about 3 percent above optimum moisture content and recompacted to
between 85 and 90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the
in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of
the same soil established in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Loose
boulders encountered on the surface of the site or generated during the scarification
and recompaction operations should be stockpiled for incorporation into the rock fill
embankment

The scarification and recompaction operations should be performed in the presence
of a representative from Geolabs Inc Yielding areas loose areas or cavities
disclosed during clearing and proof rolling operations should be backfilled with
compacted fill materials
Fills and Backfills
As previously mentioned we recommend that the fill embankment be capped with
2 feet of structural fill material to serve as a choking layer over the larger sized rock
fill below and also to provide a working zone for future construction The capping fill
should consist of well graded granular materials less than 6 inches in maximum
dimension with sufficient fines to prevent the occurrence of voids in the compacted
mass
General rock fill materials below the 2 foot structural capping fill layer may consist of
well graded granular materials of which the majority portion is less than 12 inches in
size with an absolute maximum dimension of 3 feet
Boulders or rock fragments from the reservoir excavation work less than 3 feet in
largest dimension may be utilized as rock fill material provided that the top of the rock
fill layer is placed at least 2 feet below the finished subgrades Boulders or rock
fragments larger than 3 feet in maximum dimension should not be used as rock fill
unless reduced in size to 3 feet or less in maximum dimension
Boulders or rock fragments greater than 18 inches in largest dimension should not
be exposed at the fill slope face Boulders or rock fragments larger than 18 inches
in maximum dimension should be setback a lateral distance of at least 2 feet from the
edge of the fill slope face
Compaction of the boulders and rock fragments in the rock fills should be performed
in accordance with the recommendations presented in the Fill Placement and
Compaction Requirements subsection of this report It should be noted that fill
GEOLABS INC
W O 4320 00 Page 10
embankment construction will require that the entire fill mass be compacted and
properly choked off at the surface to reduce the potential for migration of fines
through the voids between boulders
Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
Capping fill materials 6 inch minus fills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding
12 inches in loose thickness moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture
and compacted to at least 95 percent relative compaction Rock fills should be placed
in level lifts not exceeding 36 inches in loose thickness moisture conditioned to above
the optinum moisture and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed
as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Optimum moisture is the water
content percentage by weight corresponding to the maximum dry density We wish
to emphasize that moisture conditioning of the fill materials is an integral part of the
fill placement recommendations provided herein Compaction of the capping fill
should be accomplished by sheepsfoot rollers vibratory rollers or other types of
acceptable compaction equipment
Rock fill placement should be by haul trucks traversing previously placed lifts and
dumping at the edge of the currently placed lift Spreading of each layer of the rock
fill should be performed by a bulldozer to facilitate seating of the rock The rock fill
should be watered heavily during placement by water trucks traversing in front of the
current rock lift face and spraying water continuously during placement Compaction
equipment such as a Caterpillar D 9 bulldozer or similar heavy compaction
equipment providing suitable energy to achieve the required densification should be
utilized to compact the rock fill
Conventional compaction testing is generally not practicable in fills composed of
rocks boulders and or cobbles Instead a testing program to evaluate the number
of passes by a compactor needed to achieve the desired level of compaction should
GEOLABS INC
W O 4320 00 Page 11
be conducted at the start of the grading phase of the project under the observation
of a representative from Geolabs Inc Based on this testing program the number of
passes may then be used as the field criterion for adequate compaction
Excavation
Based on our field exploration at the site the project site is generally underlain by stiff
to very stiff clays underlain by gravelly and bouldery alluvial materials It is anticipated
that the near surface materials unconsolidated materials may be readily excavated
with normal heavy excavation equipment such as backhoes and ripping with large
bulldozers However excavations encountering isolated large boulder may require
the use of hoerams or chipping
The above discussions regarding the excavation characteristics of the surface
materials are based on our visual observation of the existing materials encountered
and field data from the test pits excavated at the site We recommend that
contractors be encouraged to examine the site conditions and the test pit data to
make their own interpretation
Cut and Fill Slopes
Based on the current grading scheme cut slopes are not planned for the proposed
project However if the grading scheme changes and cut slopes are required cut
slopes may be designed with a slope inclination of two horizontal to one vertical
2H 1V or flatter A representative from Geolabs Inc should observe cut slopes
during construction to evaluate whether the exposed cut slope materials are
consistent with our assumptions
Permanent fill slopes constructed of the rock fill and capping fill materials constructed
as recommended herein may be designed with a slope inclination of 2H 1V or flatter
Fills placed on slopes steeper than 5H 1V should be keyed and benched The fill
slope face should be finished to provide a relatively smooth and well compacted face
The fill slope face should be free of large boulders greater than 18 inches in largest

dimension and should be free of voids which would allow erosion and migration of
fines to occur In addition slope planting or other means of slope protection should
be provided as soon as possible to reduce the potential for erosion of the finished
slopes
Future Underground Utilities
As previously recommended placement of boulders within the utility trench corridors
and within the anticipated depths of the utility lines should be avoided to reduce the problems
associated with excavating boulders from within a utility trench
It should be noted that the rock fill materials recommended for the mass grading
operations consist generally of 12 inch minus and may include boulders or rock fragments
up to 3 feet in maximum dimension When these larger sized materials are excavated from
the utility trenches and are re used as trench backfill materials the over sized materials will
need to be screened to provide only 6 inch minus materials for the trench backfilling
operations As an alternative selective grading may be performed during the grading
operations to provide only 6 inch minus structural fill materials within the future drain line
corridors
Surface Drainage
The project site is located in a relatively arid area of Oahu Rainfall in this region
generally occurs in large events on an infrequent basis
It is recommended that site grading be designed to efficiently manage large short term
runoff without discharging to existing improvements such as Kili Drive The site grading
should also divert water from the faces of fill slopes to reduce the potential of destabilizing
the slopes
In addition the site is situated at the base of relatively broad and steep slopes along
the southern face of Puu Keaau Infiltration of runoff from these slopes into the new fill could
result in piping and loss of stability of the fill subgrade It is recommended that an interceptor
GEOLABS INC
W O 4320 00 Page 13
wall be installed at the interface between the new fill and existing slopes to reduce infiltration
of surface runoff into the fill
Boulder Catchment
As discussed above the site is situated at the base of the relatively steep slopes
of Puu Keaau There is a potential that through natural processes of erosion boulders
and other earth materials could fall from the slopes onto the site Future development
plans should include a boulder catchment are at the toe of slope to reduce the potential for
damage from boulders that may fall into the site from the adjacent slopes
