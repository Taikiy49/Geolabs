PROJECT CONSIDERATION
It is proposed to construct a one story warehouse with a mezzanine
over part of the building It will be a steel frame structure with

concrete masonry walls and concrete on grade slabs Asphaltic concrete
pavement will be utilized along the northern portion of the site for the
on grade parking areas along with a concrete apron for truck traffic
The proposed layout of the building and the parking structure is
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed site is located in an open area at the junction of
Puuhale and Kahai Streets and occupies about one fifth of that area
The area is generally flat and presently paved with asphaltic
concrete There are no adjacent structures within a distance of
approximately 100 feet
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Subsurface Exploration
The subsurface conditions were explored by drilling and sampling 5
borings to depths of 10 to 16 5 feet below the existing ground surface
In addition two pits were hand dug to recover near surface scil samples

Soil Conditions
The borings disclosed that the site was mantled by a thin layer of
medium to stiff brown clayey silt overlying a thicker layer of moderately
hard tan white coral The upper portion of the coral layer was cemented
but graded to medium dense below the water level to at least 16 5 feet
below the ground the maximum depth drilled
The depth to the top of the coral layer varied from approximately
1 5 feet in the northern portion of the site to about 4 5 feet in the
southern portion
Groundwater level was monitored during our exploration and was
found to be quite stable The cbhserved water levels varied from 8 feet 2
inches to 8 feet 10 inches below the ground surface
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATION
General
Our findings indicate that the site is predominantly mantled with a
thin layer of clayey silt underlain by hard coral
Spread Footing Foundation
For the proposed structure spread footing foundations bearing on
GFEOLARS HAWAITI
W G 1566 00 Page 4
the underlying moderately hard coral layer are recommended
An allowable soil bearing of 5000 p s f may be used for spread
footing bearing on competent in situ coral The bearing may be increased
by one third when considering temporary wind or seismic loads A minimum
footing size of 18 inches should be used
Non lead bearing walls may be placed on the thickened edges of
on grade concrete slabs These thickened edges should be embedded a
minimum of 12 inches below the lowest ad jacent grade and be designed for
an allowable soil bearing pressure of 2500 p s f
It is anticipated that the total and differential foundation
settlements if constructed according to the above design criteria
should be negligible
We recommend that all footing excavations be inspected by a soils
engineer to confirm the subsurface soil conditions upon which our design
recommendations are based
Site Preparation
Prior to placing the on grade concrete slabs and the concrete
apron the existing asphaltic concrete pavement at these areas should be
removed and the resulting excavation could be backfilled with compacted
on site material
W C 1566 00 Page 5
The existing brown clayey silt may be used for backfill Our
laboratory test results indicate that this silty material generally has
low to moderate expansion potential
We recommend that the subgrade soils be scarified first and then
moisture conditioned to within 3 percentage points of the optimum and
recompacted to at least 90 of the ASTM D 1557 78 maximum density
Pavements
Asphalt concrete pavement is planned for the parking areas while
concrete pavements are planned for the warehouse and apron The
thickness of the pavement section required depends on the traffic loads
anticipated Based on the laboratory CER test results the following
