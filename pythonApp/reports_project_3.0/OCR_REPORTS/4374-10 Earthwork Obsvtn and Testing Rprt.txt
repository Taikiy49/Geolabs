PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located at Lot No 32 also referenced as Lot No 8027 on the
southwest side of Lauwiliwili Avenue one lot southeast of the intersection with Kalaeloa
Boulevard within the Kapolei Business Park in the Kapolei area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii
The approximately square shaped 4 acre site along with the other lots within the business
park was previously mass graded and is bounded by vacant lots to the northwest southwest
and southeast as shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
The project involved the construction of a one story processing plant building of
approximately 77 000 square feet in floor space Shallow foundations consisting of spread
and continuous footings will be used to support the interior column and perimeter wall loads
of the proposed structure Infrastructure such as water sewer drainage and electrical lines
will also be installed to serve the new facility
Based on the information provided the new Palama Meats facility will be surrounded
by driveways and parking areas to accommodate approximately 80 passenger vehicles

SITE PREPARATION
As previously mentioned the site grading work consisted of placement and compaction
of fills on the order of about 3 feet thick or less to achieve the design finished grades for the
project The earthwork operations and foundation excavations were performed by Jayar
Construction Inc
Observations during our geotechnical engineering exploration indicated that abundant
shrinkage cracks existed across the project site Shrinkage cracks located adjacent to the
building foundations are potentially detrimental since water can readily infiltrate through the
crack opening and soften the subsurface soils around the building foundations thus affecting
their performance Therefore special subgrade preparation procedures were followed during
the site grading operations for the project development
After clearing and grubbing the exposed subgrades were proof rolled with a 10 ton
vibratory drum roller a minimum of six passes to assist in detecting and collapsing
near surface voids and loose zones created by the shrinkage cracks The exposed subgrades
were proof rolled without moisture conditioning of the subgrade soils at the pre existing
moisture contents Loose areas disclosed during the proof rolling operation were backfilled
with general fill material compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction The
proof rolling operations were conducted under the observation of a representative from
Geolabs
Subsequent to the proof rolling operations the subgrades were scarified to a depth of
about 12 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density determined in accordance
with American Society of Testing and Material ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified
Proctor
Following the subgrade preparation imported coralline materials were placed as
general fill at the project site Quarry produced product meeting the specification requirements
was used for the 24 inch thick non expansive select granular fill layer required below the
building and sidewalk slabs
During the site grading operations samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing The

Periodic field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations to
confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures and soil conditions were
noted A total of 435 field density tests including retests of failed areas were performed using
the nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM Test Designations D 2922 and D 3017
The results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 22 of
this report
The tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and
backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating
from on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should be considered accurate
only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were
reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained and or approved by our field
representative based on visual observation and hand probing

LIMITATIONS

simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork may have been
performed without our observation However the level of earthwork observation and testing
services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some of the geotechnical risks to the
project It should be recognized that there are variations in the accuracy and statistical
repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the earthwork construction and
