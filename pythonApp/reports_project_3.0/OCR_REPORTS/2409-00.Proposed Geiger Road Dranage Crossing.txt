INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the proposed Geiger Road drainage crossing in
Ewa on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location of the site and
its vicinity is shown on the Project Iocation Map Plate 1 The
exploration was performed in general accordance with our proposal dated
This report summarizes the findings of the exploration and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analyses based
on the data obtained during the exploration These recommendations are
intended for design input and are not intended for construction estimation
or bidding purposes

PROJECT CONSTDERATTIONS
It is proposed to construct a drainage culvert structure crossing at
the subject site as part of a golf course development project The
structure would consist of sixteen 12 6 by 7 11 metal pipe arch
culverts extending 250 feet long
According to the plans the lowest invert elevation of the proposed
culverts is MSL which is about one foot above the existing
84 inch sewer line Potential differential settlements along the length
of the culverts and the imposed load on the existing sewer line are of
major concern of the project
Based on the plans provided to us numerous existing utility lines
including an 84 inch diameter concrete sewer pipe telephone fuel and
water lines run along the Geiger Road Temporary rerouting of traffic and
relocation of the existing utility lines are anticipated during
construction of the project Also about two 2 to three 3 feet of
fill is anticipated to raise the existing road grade at the project site
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface conditions at the project site in order to develop a
generalized soil rock data set for the formation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertinent to the design of the proposed
drainage crossing project
The work was done in general accordance with our proposal to Hawaii
Pacific Engineers Inc dated The scope of work
consisted of the following
1 Drilling and sampling five 5 borings to depths of 20 0 to 21 5
feet below the existing ground surface

SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed project site is located on Geiger Road between Fort
Weaver Road and Hanson Road near Barbers Point Naval Air Station in Ewa
on the island of Oahu Hawaii
Geiger Road is a two lane traffic roadway with asphaltic concrete
pavement To the north of the road the general area was under mass
grading for a golf course development The southern part of the road was
an existing sugarcane field at the time of our exploration The terrain
of the site is generally level with elevations ranging from 32 to 34 feet
above Mean Sea Level From the shoulders of the road the terrain slopes
down toward the north and south

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling five 5
borings to depths of 20 0 to 21 5 feet below the existing ground
surface The approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site
Plan Plate 2
Based on the borings the subsoils at the site consist of up to about
two 2 feet of silty clay and gravel overlying relatively competent
coral algal formation to the maximum depths drilled
Groundwater level was not encountered in the borings at the time of
our field exploration

DISCUSSTON AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration generally encountered relatively competent
coral algal formation at and below the proposed invert level It is our
opinion that the on site soils may provide adequate support for the
proposed culvert structure with minimal settlements
Since underground utility lines including an 84 inch sewer pipe are
anticipated at the site it is the contractor s responsibility to locate
and protect all the existing utility lines through the construction period
Site Grading
In general the site grading would consist of excavation into the site
for the construction of the culvert structure backfill around the
culverts and grading of the temporary detour around the construction site

Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limits should be cleared of vegetation debris rubbish and other
deleterious materials These materials should be removed and
disposed of properly off site
The fill areas for the temporary detour road should be scarified to a
depth of six 6 inches moisture conditioned and recompacted to 90
of its maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 Any loose
or soft spots encountered should be over excavated and then replaced
with compacted non expansive select material
Excavation
Relatively hard excavation i e hoe ram or hard ripping is
anticipated for excavation into the underlying hard coral algal
formation
Fill Material
The on site excavated coral materials may be used as general fill and
backfill material provided that they are processed to three 3 inch
minus sizes
If required imported materials should consist of non expansive
select granular materials generally less than 3 inches in maximm
size with less than 20 passing the 200 sieve such as select
borrow cinder sand coral and crusher waste All imported
materials should be tested and approved by the geotechnical engineer
prior to transportation to the site

The applicability of the excavated coral materials for backfill
around the metal culverts would depend on other considerations such
as the design service life of the metal culverts Detailed
discussions on trench backfill around the culverts are presented in
the Section Culvert Structure
Fill Placement
The fill should be placed in maximum ten 10 inch thick loose level
lifts and compacted to a minimm of 90 of its maximm dry density
per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
A slope ratio of two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V may be used
for fill slopes
Existing Utility Lines
Numerous underground utility lines including telephone water fuel
and an 84 inch sewer pipe are anticipated
Based on the plans provided to us it appears that the 84 inch
reinforced concrete pipe is located along the southern boundary of Geiger
Road and below the proposed invert of the new culverts The rest of the
existing lines are along the northern part of the road and are located
above the invert of the new culverts The contractor should be
responsible for locating the existing utility lines and protect them
during the construction period
It is our understanding that the intended construction sequence is to
first install the culverts at the southern half of Geiger Road then
relocate the utility lines in the northern portion of the road to the
southern portion and then complete the culverts construction at the
northern side of the road

Continuous Concrete Cradle
kt Sk i ne ERS te es
Based on the available information the top of the existing 84 inch
concrete sewer pipe is at approximately elevation 21 0 feet MSL The
invert elevations of the culverts however are only one 1 to two
2 feet higher than the existing sewer pipe
Due to the proximity of the proposed culvert structure and the
existing sewer pipe we recommend that a continuous concrete cradle
be constructed on top of the sewer pipe before the installation of
the new culverts This would require over excavation below the
invert elevations of the culverts to expose the underlying pipe for
construction of the above concrete cradle We believe the concrete
cradle should help to uniformly distribute the overlying loads from
the culvert structure over the existing in situ sewer pipe
To avoid adverse point load conditions the 84 inch sewer pipe should
be exposed for a minimum of five 5 feet across the pipe axis This
would provide sufficient uniform contact surface between the concrete
cradle and the sewer pipe
Culvert Structure
Sixteen pipe arch culverts will be constructed for the drainage
crossing General guidelines for the culvert construction are as follows
Trench Backfill
The backfill up to about one foot above the culverts should consist
of select well draining self compacting granular materials with
inch such as ASTM C 33 No 67 gravel 3 Fine The backfill should
be lightly tamped to reduce the possibility of damaging the culverts

It is our understanding that whether the excavated coral materials
will be selected for use as backfill directly over the metal culverts
would depend on other considerations such as the design service life
of the metal culverts Based on the laboratory results the coral
material generally has a pH value of 10 and a resistivity value of
about 5 200 ohm cm These values may be used as a guide to evaluate
the service life design criteria and final selection of the type of
culverts to be used
If the excavated on site coral materials are selected as backfill
over of the culverts they should be broken down and processed to the
above recommended grain sizes Otherwise imported granular
materials such as 3 Fine gravel are recommended for use as backfill
directly over the culverts
The upper portion of the backfill from the level one 1 foot above
the culverts to the finish grade should consist of general fill
materials as recommended under the Section Site Grading The fill
should be placed generally in maximum ten 10 inch thick loose level
lifts and mechanically compacted to a minimm of 90 of its maximum
dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 to protect against
potential future ground subsidence
Cushion
A 12 inch cushion layer of gravel such as 3B Fine is recommended
below the culvert structure to provide drainage and uniform cushion
support

culverts An allowable bearing value of up to 3000 p s f may be

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during site grading and
construction of the culvert structure This is to observe compliance with
the design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow design
changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from that
anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in
this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field borings Variations of
conditions between the field data points may occur and the nature and
extent of these variations may not become evident until construction If
variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations given in this report
The locations of the field borings were approximately determined by
taping from objects indicated on the topographic map by Belt Collins
Associates Elevations of the data points were interpreted from the same
map The physical locations of the field data points should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the method used
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2409 00 Page 11
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross sections are based upon interpolation between field
data points and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
Water level data from the field data points was collected at the
times given on the graphic representations and or in the text of this
report This data has been reviewed and interpretations made in the
formulation of this report However it must be noted that fluctuation
may occur due to variation in rainfall tides temperature and other
factors
