GEOTECHNICAL ENGINEERING EVALUATION 
MANELE DESALINATION FACILITY – SOURCE WELL NO. 1 
ISLAND OF LANAI, HAWAII 
Dear Mr. Fukumitsu: 
This letter report presents our preliminary assessment of the proposed site grading 
at the Manele Desalination Facility – Source Well No. 1 project on the Island of Lanai, 
Hawaii. The project location and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map, 
Plate 1. 
This letter report summarizes our geotechnical engineering recommendations 
resulting from our site reconnaissance and previous experience in the vicinity. These 
recommendations are intended for the site preparation only. The findings and 
recommendations presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this 
letter report. 
Project Considerations 
The project involves development of a desalination plant and distribution system in 
the area above the existing Manele resort development and golf course. The desalination 
facility will be located west of the existing sewage treatment facility. Eventually, four source 
wells and three disposal wells plus sedimentation basin will be constructed. This report 
only covers Source Well No. 1. The Source Well No. 1 site is about 90 feet by 145 feet in 
plan. The well will be adjacent to existing roads in the area.  Site grading involving 
primarily cuts will be needed to create level areas for the well development. 
Purpose and Scope 
The purpose of the geotechnical evaluation is to obtain an overview of the 
surface and subsurface conditions to develop a soil and/or rock data set to formulate 
geotechnical recommendations for the site grading construction of the proposed source 
well facility. Our assessment is based on a site reconnaissance and previous 
subsurface information in the project vicinity.

Site Description 
The project is located in the southwestern portion of Lanai, mauka of the existing 
Four Seasons Resort in Manele. The well site is on the upslope side of the existing 
construction road about 2,400 feet west from intersection of Manele Road and Kapihaa 
Place. The ground surface slopes down toward the southeast at a gradient of about 
20 percent. The site is covered with dryland shrubs and trees. 
Subsurface Condition 
Based on our site reconnaissance and previous experience in the vicinity, the 
anticipated subsurface conditions at Source Well No. 1 site consist of a thin surface soil 
layer over weathered basalt rock formation.  The surface soil is typically less than 2 feet 
thick and comprised of clayey and silty soils.  The basalt formations generally consist of 
moderately weathered, medium hard basalt rock with clinker layers. 
Discussion and Recommendations 
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered at the site, we recommend 
designing the cut slope in rock formation with an inclination of one horizontal to one 
vertical (1H:1V) without a slope bench. Fill slope may be utilized with an inclination of 
1.5H:1V or flatter. 
We anticipate that site grading will primarily involve cuts up to 20 feet to achieve the 
finished elevation of the new facility.  Minor filling may be required in isolated areas.  In 
general, a Geolabs representative should monitor the site preparation to confirm whether 
the exposed soil conditions during excavation are similar to those encountered in our field 
exploration.  The following recommendations are intended to provide guidelines for the 
design of site preparation work. 
At the on-set of earthwork, the area within the contract grading limits should be 
cleared and grubbed thoroughly. Vegetation, debris, deleterious material, and other 
unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed off-site.  
In general, the on-site materials generated from the excavations may be re-used as 
a source of general fill and backfill materials, provided that the materials are screened of 
over-sized materials (greater than 3 inches in maximum dimension) and are free of 
deleterious materials.  Where used as fill and backfill materials, the on-site soils should be 
properly moisture-conditioned to above the optimum moisture content, placed in level lifts 
not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness, and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent 
relative compaction. 
Based on our site reconnaissance and previous exploration in the vicinity, the 
project site is generally underlain by dense basalt formations ranging from hard to very 
hard.  It is anticipated that the basalt formation near the ground surface may be readily 
excavated with normal heavy excavation equipment, such as excavators and ripping with

The above discussions regarding the rippability of the surface materials are based

Based on the anticipated subsurface conditions at Source Well No. 1, we believe 
that the cut slopes planned at the site may be designed with a slope inclination of one 
horizontal to one vertical (1H:1V) or flatter.  However, due to the presence of clinker layers, 
the individual cut slopes should be re-evaluated during construction.  In areas where loose 
clinker layers, unstable, and/or friable materials are exposed at the cut slope faces, the cut 
slopes may need to be flattened.  Therefore, provisions to flatten the cut slopes and/or 
construct retaining walls should be considered in the design of the project. A Geolabs 
representative should observe and map the cut slopes during construction to evaluate 
whether the exposed cut slope materials are consistent with our assumptions. 
Slope bench may be omitted due to the competent basalt rock formation 
anticipated at Source Well No. 1 site.  It should be noted that the slope bench should be 
avoided if the cut slope may have potential rockfall dislodged from upper hill. 
Permanent fill slopes constructed of the general fill and structural fill materials 
recommended herein may be designed with a slope inclination of 1.5H:1V or flatter.  In 
addition, fills placed on slopes steeper than 5H:1V should be benched.  A keyway should 
be provided for fill slopes greater than 10 feet in vertical height placed on existing ground 
steeper than 5H:1V.  The fill slope face should be finished to a relatively smooth and 
well-compacted surface. 
Geolabs should be retained to provide geotechnical engineering services during 
construction.  The following are critical items of construction monitoring that require 
"Special Inspection": 
Observation of fill placement and compaction 
Observation of waterline trench excavation and compaction 
A Geolabs representative should also monitor other aspects of earthwork 
construction to confirm compliance with the intent of the design concepts, specifications, 
and/or recommendations, and to expedite suggestions for design changes that may be 
required in the event that subsurface conditions differ from those anticipated at the time 
this report was prepared.  The recommendations presented herein are contingent upon 
such observations.  If the actual exposed soil/rock conditions encountered during 
construction differ from those assumed or considered in this report, Geolabs should be 
contacted to review and/or revise the geotechnical engineering recommendations 
presented herein.

Limitations 
The analyses and recommendations submitted herein are based in part upon 
information obtained from our site reconnaissance and previous experience in the project 
vicinity. Variations of subsoil conditions may occur, and the nature and extent of these 
variations may not become evident until construction is underway.  If variations then 
appear evident, Geolabs should be contacted to re-evaluate the recommendations 
presented herein. 
This report has been prepared for the exclusive use of Tom Nance Water Resource 
Engineering for specific application to the Manele Desalination Facility – Source & 
Disposal Wells project in accordance with generally accepted geotechnical engineering 
principles and practices.  No warranty is expressed or implied. 
This report has been prepared solely for the purpose of assisting the design 
engineers in the design of the proposed project.  Therefore, this report may not contain 
sufficient data or the proper information to serve as the basis for construction cost 
estimates nor for bidding purposes.  A contractor wishing to bid on this project should 
retain a competent geotechnical engineer to assist in the interpretation of this report and/or 
in the performance of additional site specific exploration for bid estimating purposes. 
The owner/client should be aware that unanticipated soil conditions are commonly 
encountered.  Unforeseen soil conditions, such as perched groundwater, soft deposits, 
hard layers or cavities, may occur in localized areas and may require additional probing or 
corrections in the field (which may result in construction delays) to attain a properly 
constructed project.  Therefore, a sufficient contingency fund is recommended to 
accommodate these possible extra costs. 
This geotechnical engineering evaluation conducted at the project site was not 
intended to investigate the potential presence of hazardous materials and/or 
archeological site existing at the site.  It should be noted that the equipment, 
techniques, and personnel used to conduct a geo-environmental exploration differ 
substantially from those applied in geotechnical engineering.
