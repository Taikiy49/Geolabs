Supplemental Study
Proposed Regrading for Lots 1A thru 6B
Palisades View Lots Subdivision
Hawaii
As requested we have performed a supplemental study at the site
for the proposed slope regrading at Lots 1A thru 6B along Auhuhu Street
The study consisted of field reconnaissance and hand augering five 5
probes along the upper slopes of the lots
The existing lots have relatively steep grades varying from about
additional space for house construction The regrading would consist of
substantial cuts into the hillside
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Five hand auger probes were drilled along the upper slopes to
depths of 5 5 to 10 0 feet depths The approximate locations of the
probes are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
W Page 2
The probes encountered very stiff residual soil and saprolite over
highly weathered rock near the bottom of the probes
Groundwater was not encountered in the probes during the field
exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Cut_Slopes
For cut slopes into the existing stiff residual soil slope ratios
up to 1 5 horizontal to 1 vertical may be used Care should be exercised
when cutting to the final slope surface to avoid over cutting since it is
not possible to properly replace and compact soil on such a steep slope
Water should be diverted away from the tops of slopes and slope
planting utilized to reduce erosion
Slope benches should be provided for exposed slopes having a
vertical height greater than 25 feet
House Foundations
It is anticipated that basement walls would be required for all of
the houses and scme post footings Lots 2A 2D and 3A 3B may be situated
on sloping ground

Due to the sloping terrain at this site deeper pier type
foundations will be necessary to minimize erosion and subsequent loss of
bearing support for the post footing foundation
General guidelines for house foundation design are as follows
1 Footings on level areas should be embedded a minimum of 12
inches below the adjacent finished grade to resist erosion
and the moderately expansive effects of the near surface
soils
2 Footings on sloping areas should be embedded down an addi
tional amount to obtain a 5 foot minimum horizontal set back
distance The set back distance is the horizontal distance
measured from the cuter edge of the footing out to the slope
embedment at the lower side would be required This
additional embedment depth is required to help resist
downhill slope creep and to develop the recommended bearing
capacity of the soil To eliminate difficulty of backfilling
the excavation we reconmend that concrete be used to
construct the 3 foot deep pier footing with the colum strap
buried in the concrete backfilled excavation Refer to
Figures 3 and 4 for details

3 An allowable bearing value of 3000 psf on the stiff natural
soil is recommended for the anticipated light loads
4 The bottom of all foundation excavations should be cleaned of
loose material and bear on firm soil The excavations should
be observed by a soils engineer to see that adequate bearing
soil has been obtained
5 The ground surface should be shaped to drain water away from
the foundations On Lots 2A 2B and 3A 3B a cutoff wall
would be required at the rear wall of the house to divert
water away from the foundation
It is recommended that a 12 inch embedment be utilized for
the slab thickened edge to reduce future water intrusion
under the house slab area
It is recommended that slab on grade be placed over a 4 inch
layer of aggregate cushion fill such as No 3 Fine rock and
separated from wall foundations to allow for some relative
movements of the slab and walls
