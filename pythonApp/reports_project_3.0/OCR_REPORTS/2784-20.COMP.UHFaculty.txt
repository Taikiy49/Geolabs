Dear Mr Ahana
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering services
performed during the earthwork and grading operations for the above referenced project
from September 1992 through May 1994 The subject site is located at the toe of the
East Manoa Hillside in Manoa Valley on the island of Oahu Hawaii The site is
bounded to the north by Woodlawn Drive to the east by Kalawao Street and to the
west by Manoa Stream The site is divided into three phases Phases Il and Ill as
shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
In general our services during the site work of the project include periodic
observation and testing of fill placement full time observation of the installation of wick
drain and installation and monitoring of piezometers and settlement gauges Our work
was conducted in general accordance with our fee proposal dated and
additional fee request dated August 5 1993
Site Grading
The site grading work at the above referenced site was performed by Okada
Trucking Company Ltd The grading work generally consisted of clearing and grubbing
preparing the subgrade and placing up to about 6 feet of new fills

Prior to fill placement the existing vegetation was removed and the exposed
subgrades were generally scarified to a depth of approximately 6 inches moisture
conditioned to near the optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90
percent of the maximum dry density determined in accordance with American Society
of Testing and Materials ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor New fill
materials consisting of imported non expansive select borrow were moisture
conditioned to near the optimum moisture content placed in near level lifts and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of its maximum dry density for general fill For
structural fills within roadways prisms the new fills including base course were
compacted to not less than 95 percent relative compaction
In general in areas where there are at least 3 feet of new fills planned within the
building pad the contractor placed the select borrow fill from the existing grade up to
the finish subgrade of building areas Where there was less than 3 feet of fill within the
building pad the subgrade was over excavated to provide the minimum 3 feet of non
expansive select borrow fill below the building subgrade level
During the building construction the building contractor encountered
approximately 2 feet of select borrow fill in lieu of 3 feet at the location of Building DH
1 As a result we drilled six short borings in the area to evaluate the thickness of the
select borrow capping fill in this area We also conducted laboratory tests on the
clayey subgrade soils to evaluate the swelling potential of the clayey soils under a
surcharge pressure equal to about 2 feet of fill Based on the six short borings and
laboratory tests we believe that the minimum 2 feet of select borrow fill at this location
would be adequate to resist the potential swelling of the clayey subgrade soils Our
recommendations for this condition were documented in our letter dated December 9
1993

report
During the fill placement operations periodic field density tests were taken in

Wick Drains and Surcharging Program
The site is underlain by a relatively stiff clayey crust overlying soft lagoonal
deposits Due to the load of new fills planned and the future buildings in the Phase III
the underlying soft clay layer would be subjected to potential settlement due to the
surcharge loading As a result a program consisting of wick drains and surcharge fills
was recommended to accelerate the settlement the soft soil deposits under the
surcharge loading The wick drain and surcharge program was only limited to the
Phase Ill portion of the project
In general after mass grading of Phase III with the exception of a small section
in Phase III A Highway Construction Company Ltd installed wick drains to depths of
approximately 25 to 45 feet below the ground surface The wick drain installation
proceeded from February through June of 1993 Approximately 153 932 lineal feet of
wick drains were installed in the Phase Ill portion of the project to accelerate the
dissipation of excess pore water pressure within the underlying soft soil deposit and the
ground surface settlement
A surcharge load consisting of about 8 feet of uncompacted soil was placed in
the generai area of Phase Ill B portion of the project between March and September
1993 About 4 feet of surcharge fill was placed in the general area of Phase III A
portion of the project from August through December 1993

Limitations

some work may have occurred without our observation However we believe that the
level of earthwork observation and testing services provided is reasonable in reducing
the geotechnical risks to the project It should also be recognized that there are
variations in the accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to
monitor the earthwork construction and materials
