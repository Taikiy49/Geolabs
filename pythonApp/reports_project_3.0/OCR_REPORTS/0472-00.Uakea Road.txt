At your request this firm has performed a visual site inspection
on May 9 1973 for the above referenced property to determine
the feasibility of constructing a single family residence on the
same This report outlines the investigation and findings along
with preliminary recommendations
For the field investigation the subject site was visually
reviewed by Mr H M Patel from this office along with
Mr M B Hewett from Hana Ranch Boundary locations of the site
were discussed with of Tryck Nyman and Hayes Inc
Land Surveyors who prepared the preliminary topographic map attached

The swamp ancient fish pond area was randomly probed at
about 10 locations by trying to push a 6 foot long 1 inch
diameter pointed steel rod by hand This gave an indication of
the minimum thickness of the very soft surficial organic soil
In addition to this two holes were hand excavated using a
shovel to depths varying from 2 feet 6 inches to 2 feet 9 inches
Logs of these holes are attached with this report Based on
this it is estimated that the minimum thickness of the very soft
surficial organic soil layer is on the order of 2 feet 6 inches
to 3 feet in major portion of the swamp area and is on the order
of 4 feet to 5 feet or more in the western portion of the swamp
Groundwater in the test holes was encountered at a depth of
2 feet 6 inches apparently after breaking through a thin hard
crust and rose to depths of about 9 inches which is an elevation
of about 7 feet 6 inches This indicates that the water encoun
tered in the test holes is probably not due to the presence of
nearby sea but is fed from underground sources and is under
artesian head of about two feet The ground in the swamp area
feels to be very soft and spongy when walked over The swamp
area is covered with about 3 feet tall grass
Various locations more or less in order of merit are discussed
hereunder for the feasibility of constructing a single family
residence at this site It is our understanding that the proposed

Northern Area of Site Area 1
The area to the north of the swamp and south of A C paved
approach road may provide sufficient area to construct a
single family residence Presently this area is covered with
heavy vegetation bushes and trees so site clearing and
grubbing of tree roots would be required The ground in
this area appears to be relatively firm with many boulders
or rock outcropping on the surface If this area is
selected we do not anticipate any major soil or foundation
problems however subsurface exploration would be required
to confirm this opinion and develop foundation requirements
when the details of proposed residence is available The
structure can probably be founded on continuous footings

Area West of Swamp and East of High Cliff Area 3
This area will have to be explored before soil and foundation
recommendations may be developed as it is difficult to pre
dict soil condition in this area The structures in this
area should be placed away from the cliffs say 20 feet
because of the danger of loose rock chunks or boulders falling
off the cliff which are very steep and high The vegetation
in this area is short bushes and few trees The ground in
this area is about 2 to 3 feet higher than the swamp
Swamp Area Area 4
The swamp is in the center of the property and has enough
level area to construct a single family residente If the
swamp was absent this area might have been the obvious
choice for the home site However presence of the swamp
and shallow ground water which is believed to be fed by
sources other than nearby sea and is under artesian pressure
