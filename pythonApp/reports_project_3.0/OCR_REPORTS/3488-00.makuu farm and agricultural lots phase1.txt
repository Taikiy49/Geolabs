SITE GRADING
The Makuu Farm and Agricultural Lots Phase 1 project is located in the Makuu
area within the District of Puna on the island of Hawaii The project site is approximately
bounded by Waikahekahe Iki to the north Keonepoko Nui to the east and south and
Kea au Pahoa Road to the west The general location and vicinity of the project site are
shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
As part of the Makuu Farm and Agricultural Lots development the project site was
graded for the construction of approximately 7 miles of new roadways to serve
approximately 177 farm and agricultural lots as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 In
addition the project also involved the installation of approximately 138 drywells and
approximately 43 000 lineal feet of ductile iron water pipes Earthwork construction of the

Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork areas within the contract grading limits were cleared
and grubbed Vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable materials were
removed and disposed of to reduce the potential for contamination of the excavated
materials
In areas of cut the existing ground was ripped with a Caterpillar D 11 bulldozer
down to the finished subgrade levels The excavated materials were hauled to the
on site crusher plant for processing and stockpile The over excavated subgrade was
then tracked with the bulldozer and or a sheepsfoot compactor to help detect and
collapse near surface cavities as well as to provide a relatively level area for the fine
grading operation Loose areas or cavities disclosed during clearing and proof rolling
operations were over excavated to expose firm materials and backfilled with compacted
fill A vibratory drum roller was used during the fine grading operations to proof roll the
pavement subgrade prior to the placement of the base course layer
In areas that received fill the subgrade was generally proof rolled with a
Caterpillar D 11 bulldozer or similar heavy equipment minimum 4 passes to help
detect and collapse near surface cavities Fill material was then used to raise the
existing ground to the finished subgrade elevations
During the site grading operations several pockets of soft soils with vegetation
and tree roots were observed along the left hand side of Road E 2 from approximately
Stations 4 00 to 5 00 These soft and or contaminated materials were removed to
expose the underlying dense basalt rock formation The resulting over excavation was
backfilled with 6 inch minus material in compacted lifts prior to the placement of the
base course layer

The roadway shoulder and swale areas were graded and proof rolled using a
vibratory drum roller Heavy rains in November 1996 have eroded some of the
compacted shoulder and swale areas prior to asphalt concrete paving and
hydromulching These areas were subsequently reworked and observed by our field
representative Erosion control measures such as asphalt concrete paving and or
hydromulching should be established as early as possible for the compacted shoulder
and swale areas to reduce the potential for future erosion and loss of base course and
compacted fill materials The establishment of these erosion control measures are
critical to the satisfactory performance of the shoulder and swale areas of the
constructed roadways
Fill Placement and Compaction Testing
Materials used in fill areas generally consisted of well graded granular materials
less than 6 inches in largest dimension were obtained by processing the excavated
materials generated in cut areas using the on site crusher plant Conventional
compaction testing is generally not practicable in fills composed of rocks boulders
and or cobbles For fills consisting of these materials the fills were placed in near level
lifts and compacted to the specified degree of compaction by tracking using a Caterpillar
D 11 bulldozer or similar equipment The degree of compaction was based on visual
observations since the over sized nature of the fill material precluded field density testing
of the fill materials
Base course material was placed in level lifts of approximately 6 inches in loose
thickness moisture conditioned and compacted to at least 95 percent relative
compaction using a vibratory drum roller Relative compaction refers to the in place dry
density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil
established in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Optimum moisture is
the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry
density
During the site grading operations samples of the materials intended for use as
water line trench backfill and base course materials were retrieved from the stockpiles at
the on site crusher plant and transported to our office in Honolulu for testing The
maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in general
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test results
are summarized on Plate 3 of this report

Field density tests were taken on the compacted water line trench backfill and
base course material for conformance with the specified minimum compaction prior to
paving with asphalt concrete A total of 172 field density tests were performed using the
nuclear probe method in general accordance with ASTM Test Designations D 2922 and
D 3017 The results of the compaction tests taken during construction of the subject
project are summarized on Plates 4 1 through 4 6 of this report Test locations were
estimated by interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the method used
Water Lines
Installation of the water lines generally included excavation in hard basalt rock
formation followed by backfilling the trench excavation after placement of the utility line
In general after placement of pipes in the trenches free draining 1 inch minus granular
materials obtained by crushing and processing the on site excavated basalt rock were
used for the trench backfill The backfill was generally placed in about 8 inch lifts and
compacted with a jumping jack and or vibratory rammax

Based on the project construction plans a provision for a 6 inch thick aggregate
subbase course to be determined by the geotechnical engineer was included in the
pavement section for the subject project as shown below
Pavement Section Specified in Construction Plans
2 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
4 Inch Aggregate Base Course
6 Inch Select Borrow Subbase As Required by Geotechnical Engineer
12 Inch Total Pavement Thickness over Subgrade
In general the purpose of the subbase course required in the County of Hawaii
Standard is to serve as a foundation or stabilizing course such that the subsequent base
and surfacing may be constructed on top of an inferior subgrade soil The standard
pavement section does not take into consideration good quality subgrade conditions
Based on the actual exposed pavement subgrade conditions observed by our field
personnel the subgrade conditions along the roadway alignments at the Makuu Farm
Agricultural Lots Phase 1 site consisted of compacted rock fill material or basalt rock

EVALUATIONS
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the earthwork operations observed and tested by
Geolabs Hawaii for the pavement construction and infrastructure installation of the Makuu
Farm and Agricultural Lots Phase 1 project in the period covered by this report were
performed in general accordance with the intent of the plans and specifications and the
recommendations presented in the correspondences prepared by our office
LIMITATIONS
This office assumes no responsibility for any alterations made to slopes or finished
grades on the subject project subsequent to our observation and testing period without
our knowledge and written acknowledgment
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site therefore some work may have occurred
without our observation However we believe that the level of earthwork observation and
testing services provided is reasonable in reducing the geotechnical risks to the project It
should be recognized that there are variations in the accuracy and statistical repeatability
of the tests and methods used to monitor the earthwork construction and materials Our
services were performed in accordance with generally accepted standards of
geotechnical engineering practice no warranty is expressed or implied Provision of
construction monitoring by an engineer is not insurance nor does it guarantee
construction of any type The contractor shall retain responsibility for the quality of the
work for adhering to the plans and specifications and for repairing defects regardless of
when they are found
