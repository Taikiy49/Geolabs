In accordance with our discussions in the meeting on April 10th we have
performed a laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR test on the on site soils to
reevaluate the pavement subgrade characteristics at the above referenced project site
One sample consisting of reddish brown clayey silt was retrieved from a stockpile of
on site material at the project site Based on conversations with the general contractor
the on site stockpile material are to be used as fill material at the parking and driveway
areas
Based on the results of a Modified Proctor test performed by others the CBR test
sample was prepared with a moisture content of about 2 percent above the optimum
moisture content and tested in general accordance with ASTM D 1883 Based on the
results of our CBR test the on site stockpile sample tested has a CBR value of about
13 with a maximum expansion value of about 2 9 percent The results of our laboratory
CBR test are presented on the attached Plate 1
Based on the results of our laboratory CBR test it is our opinion that the original
pavement design of 2 inch Asphalt Concrete over 6 inch Basalt Base Course over
6 inch Basalt Subbase Course may be maintained provided that the on site stockpile
material will be the subgrade soils below the pavement structure Additionally based on
the CBR test performed we believe that our recommendation for slab on grade
