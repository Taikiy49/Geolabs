INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Overflow Wet Well for Sewage Pump Station located in
Schofield Barracks in the District of Wahiawa on the island of Oahu Hawaii
general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3302 00 Page 2
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering
recommendations resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and
engineering analyses The recommendations presented herein are intended for design
input of foundation retaining wall site grading and excavation and may not be
appropriate to form the basis for the preparation of construction cost estimates nor for
construction bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
We understand that it is proposed to install an overflow wet well at the Schofield
Barracks Wastewater Treatment Plant in the District of Wahiawa on the island of Oahu
Hawaii Based on the information provided to us the proposed wet well will be a
rectangular shaped tank structure partially embedded in an existing slope with an
inclination of about one and three quarters horizontal to one vertical 1 H 1V The
tank will be approximately 30 feet long 10 feet wide and about 16 feet high as shown
on the Site Plan Plate 2
Detailed grading plans for the project were not available at the time this report
was prepared Based on available information we anticipate that cut and backfill of up
to 15 feet relative to the existing ground surface will be required for the proposed
construction
Our previous experience in the area suggest that the project site may be
underlain by residual soils consisting of stiff clayey silt material Special attention was
given to the design parameters for the embedded tank walls and the stability of the
existing slope with the proposed construction

EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The proposed overflow wet well project is located within Schofield Barracks in
the District of Wahiawa on the island of Oahu Hawaii The project site is bound by an
asphalt pavement to the north an existing pump station to the northeast and an open
area with trees and bushes to the west The proposed tank structure will be situated
near the top of an existing slope bank as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Based on our field observation and a review of the topographic map provided by
R M Towill Corporation undated it appears that the existing bank slopes downward
to the south with a maximum vertical drop of approximately 50 feet The existing slope
has an inclination of approximately 1 H 1V for the upslope area and about 2H 1V for
the downslope area The slope surface is heavily vegetated with grasses and trees at
the time of our field exploration
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsoil conditions at the subject site were explored by drilling and sampling
one boring to a depth of about 31 feet below the existing ground surface The
approximate location of the boring is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general our exploration encountered stiff to very stiff clayey silt to a depth of
about 10 feet below the existing ground surface Underlying this surface layer our
boring generally encountered very stiff clayey silt with decomposed rock extending to
the maximum depth explored of about 31 feet below the existing ground surface

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
in general our field exploration indicates that the proposed project site is
underlain by very stiff clayey silts to the maximum depth drilled of about 31 feet below
the existing ground surface Based on the results of our field exploration and laboratory
testing we believe that a shallow foundation system bearing on the very stiff native
soils may be used to support the proposed tank structure
We understand that the proposed tank structure will be partially embedded in the
existing slope bank Therefore the tank walls should be designed as retaining walls
to resist lateral earth pressures imposed by the adjacent soils and surcharge effects
We recommend that the wall footings be embedded deep enough to provide a minimum
horizontal setback distance of at least 10 feet measured from the edge of the wall
footings to the face of the slope to reduce surcharging the existing slope bank
We anticipate that cut and backfill of up to 15 feet will be required for
construction of the new tank structure The temporary excavation should conform to
the requirements of local state and federal regulations
We have evaluated the overall stability of the existing slope with the proposed
construction Based on the laboratory test data and our engineering analyses the
factors of safety for short term and long term stabilities of the existing slope with the
GEOLABS HAWAII

recommended

Foundations
Based on the information provided to us we anticipate that the proposed tank
structure will bear on the native soils Because of the generally competent subsoil
conditions encountered in our exploration we believe that a shallow foundation system
may be used to support the proposed tank structure
An allowable bearing pressure of up to 2 500 p s f may be used for the design
of footing foundations bearing on the very stiff native soils or properly placed
compacted fill This allowable bearing pressure is for dead plus live loads and may be
increased by one third for transient loadings such as temporary wind and or seismic
forces
A6 inch gravel layer such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation
is recommended below the footing slab to provide uniform bearing support and or as
a working platform for construction For the design of structural slabs a modulus of
subgrade reaction of 150 pounds per cubic inch p c i may be used for the compacted
subgrade
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by frictional resistance
between the base of the footing and the bearing materials and by passive earth
pressure developed against the near vertical faces of the embedded portion of the tank
walls A coefficient of friction of 0 30 may be used between the base of the footing and
the supporting materials Resistance due to passive pressure for footings located on
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3302 00 Page 7
the slope may be calculated using an equivalent fluid pressure of 130 pounds per
square foot per foot of depth p c f This assumes that the gap between the
excavated face and the downslope tank wall is properly backfilled and compacted
Unless covered by pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the upper 12 inches
below finished grade should be neglected
Footing excavations should be kept moist prior to concrete placement If
shrinkage cracks appear in the footing excavations the soil should be thoroughly
moistened to close all cracks and recompacted prior to concrete placement
Footing excavations should be observed by a representative of Geolabs Hawaii
prior to the placement of reinforcing steel or concrete to confirm the competency of the
bearing material and the embedment depths If unsuitable materials are encountered
in the footing excavations these materials should be over excavated to firm soils and
replaced with compacted engineered fill

resist lateral earth pressure due to adjacent soils and surcharge effects We
understand that the retaining walls are expected to be conventional reinforced concrete
retaining walls

The surcharge effect due to traffic areal surcharges line loads and point loads
within a horizontal distance equal to the height of the wall should be considered in the
design For uniform surcharge stresses imposed on the loaded side of the wall a
rectangular distribution with uniform pressure equal to 35 and 50 percent of the
surcharge pressure acting on the entire height of the wall may be used for active and
at rest conditions respectively
The recommended lateral earth pressures above do not include hydrostatic
pressures that may be caused by groundwater trapped behind the walls Therefore
retaining walls should be well drained to reduce the build up of hydrostatic pressures
A typical drainage system would consist of a 1 foot to 2 foot wide zone of permeable
material No 3B Fine gravel or ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation immediately adjacent
to the wall with a perforated pipe perforations down along the base of the wall The
perforated pipe should be routed toward the slope face to discharge potential
groundwater trapped behind the walls The drain pipe should be covered with No 3B
Fine gravel and wrapped around by a layer of filter fabric to protect the perforated pipes
from clogging with fines The drainage material should extend from the base of the wall
to a height within 2 feet below finish grade The remaining backfill should consist of an

impervious capping of select on site silty soil to reduce water infiltration behind the
walls
Site Grading
Final grading plans were not available to us at the time this report was prepared
However we anticipate cuts and backfills of up to 15 feet relative to the existing ground
surface will be required for the project construction Site grading operations should be
observed by a representative of Geolabs Hawaii It is important that a representative
from our office observe whether any undesirable materials are encountered during the
excavation and scarification process and whether the exposed soil conditions are
similar to those encountered in our exploration
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable
materials should be removed and disposed of off site
After clearing and grubbing areas within the proposed tank structure should be
excavated to the finish subgrade The exposed subgrade should then be scarified to
a depth of at least 8 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the
optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed
as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in
accordance with ASTM D 1557 91 test procedures Loose or soft spots encountered
during the scarification process should be removed and replaced with compacted select
granular fill material
The on site silty soils generated from the excavations should not be reused as
a source of backfill material around the tank walls in order to reduce the lateral earth
pressure acting on the tank walls Imported fill and or backfill material if needed
should be granular materials of less than 3 inches in maximum dimension The
GEOLABS HAWAIl
W O 3302 00 Page 10
material should have a laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR value of 15 or more
and should have a maximum swell of less than 1 percent when tested in accordance
with ASTM Test Designation D 1883 In addition it should contain less than 25 percent
passing the No 200 sieve Imported fill material should be tested by Geolabs Hawaii
for conformance with our recommendations prior to delivery to the project site for its
intended use To reduce additional loading on the walls of the proposed tank
stockpiling of excavated on site and imported fill materials adjacent to the tank
excavations should be avoided
All fills and backfills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in
loose thickness moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Fill placement should
commence at the lowest point and continue upward in level compacted lifts In areas
to receive fill where the existing ground surface is steeper than five horizontal to one
vertical 5H 1V the new fill should be keyed and benched into the existing slope to
bond the new fill into the in situ soils Permanent cut and fill slopes should be designed
for an inclination of not steeper than two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V All fill
slopes should be over built and cut back to the design grade to provide a well
compacted slope face

Construction Precaution and Monitoring
The selected excavation shoring system must comply with all applicable safety
requirements It is recommended that a qualified geotechnical engineer be
retained by the contractor to design and evaluate the shoring system
The adequacy and safety of the shoring installation should be the sole
responsibility of the contractor His representative who should be required to
be continuously present on site during excavation and construction work will
have the best opportunity to promptly observe any changed conditions during
construction such as unforeseen subsoils unexpectedly high groundwater table
inappropriate construction sequences and or techniques all of which may affect
shoring stability
However it should be noted that some movement of the shoring system and the
adjacent ground may still occur due to changes in earth stresses during
excavation The magnitude of movement is difficult to accurately estimate due
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3302 00 Page 12
to the complexity of the stress changes The magnitude depends greatly upon
workmanship such as how quickly and tightly the shoring and bracing supports
are installed and also on the subsoil conditions the size of the excavation and
the rate of excavation
Therefore it is important to realize that the excavation shoring should be
installed properly and as early as practical if necessary and that the adjacent
ground continuously monitored for cracks dips and or other indications of
movements with instruments such as settlement markers and inclinometer
Such monitoring is particularly critical in areas adjacent to existing structures and
utilities
We strongly recommend that the contractor retain a competent engineering
consultant to design and to coordinate the implementation of the excavation
support system where necessary
Utility Trench
We envision that new utility lines may be required for the project A granular
bedding consisting of 6 inches of select borrow or No 3B Fine gravel is recommended
under the pipes Self compacting granular materials such as No 3B Fine gravel
ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation should be used for the trench backfill up to about 12
inches above the pipes to provide adequate support around the pipes and to reduce
compaction of the backfill thus reducing the potential for damaging the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level one foot above the pipes
to the top of the subgrade or finished grade should consist of select granular material
or the on site soils The backfill should be moisture conditioned to within 2 percent of
the optimum moisture content placed in maximum 8 inch level loose lifts and mechani
cally compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential
for future ground subsidence Where trenches are below pavement areas the upper
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3302 00 Page 13
2 feet of the trench backfill below the pavement subgrade should be compacted to 95
percent relative compaction
