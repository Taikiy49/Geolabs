SECTION 1 PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
This geotechnical data report presents the results of our field exploration and
laboratory testing performed for the Kalaeloa Parcel 12B site in Kalaeloa the former
Naval Air Station Barbers Point in the Honouliuli area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii
The project location and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map
Plate 1
The field exploration program conducted for the afore mentioned design build
project consisted of drilling and sampling six borings designated as Boring Nos 1
through 6 extending to depths ranging from about 21 5 to 54 5 feet below the existing
ground surface
In addition two bulk samples of the near surface soils were collected for
moisture density relations and California Bearing Ratio CBR tests The approximate
test boring and bulk sample locations are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Laboratory tests were conducted on selected soil samples to assist in the
classification and evaluation of engineering properties The test results are presented in
Appendix B

3 1 Regional Geology
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lavas from
the Waianae and Koolau shield volcanoes The older Waianae volcano is estimated to
be middle to late Pliocene in age and Koolau is estimated to be late Pliocene to early
Pleistocene in age
The project site is situated on the Ewa Coastal Plain which extends seaward off
the southeastern flank of Waianae and the southwestern flank of Koolau This plain
was formed by the accumulation of a thick wedge of sediments and coral reef
formations on the flanks of the two volcanoes
During the Pleistocene Epoch sea levels fluctuated in response to the cycles of
continental glaciation As the glaciers grew and advanced less water was available to
fill the oceanic basins such that sea levels fell below the present stands of the sea
When the glaciers melted and receded an excess of water became available such that
the sea levels rose to above the present sea level
The higher sea level stands caused the formation of reef deposits at
correspondingly higher elevations Subaerial exposure of the sediments and
calcareous materials caused consolidation of the lagoonal deposits and induration of
the calcareous reef materials

The project site is in Kalaeloa also known as the former Naval Air Station
Barbers Point in the Honouliuli area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project site is
currently unused land near the old main gate of the air station at the southern terminus
of Fort Barrette Road and its intersection with Roosevelt Avenue as shown on the
Project Location Plan Plate 1
The site encompasses approximately 10 acres and is bounded to the north by
Roosevelt Avenue to west by Bataan Avenue to the south by the remnants of an old

SECTION 4 GEOTECHNICAL EXPLORATION PROGRAM
The geotechnical exploration program conducted for the Kalaeloa Parcel 12B site
generally consisted of the following field and laboratory work components A brief
description of each work component is presented below Detailed discussions findings
and test results of the various components are provided in the respective appendices
herein
4 1 Test Boring Exploration
Soil sampling methods and field testing are described in more detail in the
introduction to Appendix A The following discussion provides an overview of the
methods and procedures
Typically the test borings were advanced with 4 inch diameter continuous flight
augers HQ wireline coring was used in selected locations to obtain samples of the
harder coralline formation Sampling of the soils was performed using the modified
California sampler ASTM D 3550 and the Standard Penetration Test SPT split spoon
sampler ASTM D 1586
In situ testing of the subsurface materials in the borings included the SPT which
was performed in accordance with ASTM D 1586 SPT sampling provides a measure of
the resistance or consistency of a soil and is reported as the uncorrected N value which
is defined as the number of blows required to drive a standard split spoon sampler
18 inches with a 140 pound hammer after 6 inches of initial seating The penetration
resistance of the modified California sampler was recorded in a similar manner and the
modified California sampler blows per 12 inches can be approximately related to the
SPT N value through correlation

SECTION 5 DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Our field exploration disclosed that the project site is generally underlain by silty
and sandy gravels interbedded coral reef formation and weakly cemented reef
detritus Groundwater was not encountered in the borings drilled at the time of our field
exploration
Based on the information provided it is proposed to develop the site for a four to
five story building with a total area of about 150 000 square feet Building design will be
by the successful design build contractor therefore detailed information for the proposed
project was not available at the time this report was prepared However it is anticipated
that the project will involve building foundations minimal site grading and some
infrastructure installation
For the preliminary geotechnical assessment of the building foundations it
appears that shallow spread and or continuous footing foundations may be used
Voids and cavities are commonly encountered in coral formations Although no
voids or cavities were encountered by our exploration it is our opinion that due to the
nature of the site development a program of cavity probing and grouting along building
subgrade areas and in major foundation systems should be implemented
5 1 Additional Exploration
This exploration was performed on a preliminary basis to provide an_ initial
overview of subsurface conditions at the site As such the findings and
recommendations presented herein are not sufficient for final design and construction
Without information pertaining to the details of this development it was not possible to
determine if other design elements such as retaining walls basements deep
excavations drainage or deep fills would be required Therefore we recommend
conducting a more extensive subsurface exploration when more detailed plans are
available

APPENDIX A
APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by drilling and
sampling six borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 6 extending to depths
ranging from about 21 5 to 54 5 feet below the existing ground surface The
approximate boring locations are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were
drilled using truck mounted rotary drill equipment equipped with continuous flight
augers
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who observed the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed by visual
observation and testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance
with the Unified Soil Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic
representations of the materials encountered are presented on the Logs of Borings
Plates A 1 through A 6
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in
general accordance with ASTM D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving
a 3 inch OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches In
addition some samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance
with ASTM D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a
2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same hammer and drop The blow
counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an 18 inch drive
are shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate
sample depths

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Tests
Moisture Content ASTM D 2216 and Unit Weight ASTM D 2937
determinations were performed on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification
and evaluation of soil properties The test results are presented on the Logs of Borings
at the appropriate sample depths
Three Atterberg Limits tests ASTM D 4318 were performed on selected soil
samples to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits and to aid in soil classification Test
results are summarized on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depth
Graphic presentation of the test results is provided on Plate B 1
Eleven Sieve Analysis tests C136 were performed on
selected soil samples to evaluate the gradation characteristics of the soils and to aid in
soil classification Graphic presentation of the grain size distribution is provided on
Plates B 2 through B 4
One Direct Shear test ASTM D 3080 were performed on selected samples to
evaluate the shear strength characteristics of the materials tested The test results are
presented on Plate B 5
Two laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR tests ASTM D 1883 were
performed on bulk samples of the near surface soils to evaluate the pavement support
characteristics of the soils The results of the test are presented on Plates B 6 and B 7
