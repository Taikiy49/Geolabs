In accordance with our fee proposal dated June 22, 2021, we have performed 
geotechnical engineering consultation services consisting of the field and laboratory 
resistivity testing for the proposed new HECO substation facility within the Ho`opili 
subdivision on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. The field in-situ resistivity survey was performed 
on August 4, 2021. This report presents the results of the field and laboratory resistivity 
testing performed for the above project. 
Design of metallic substructures, such as electrical conduits or other metallic 
piping, should consider the effects of the corrosive environment on the substructure. 
Resistivity is generally recognized as one of the most significant soil characteristics 
regarding the corrosivity of the soil to buried metallic objects. In general, the lower the 
resistivity, the greater the potential for corrosion of the buried metallic structure. 
Conversely, the higher the resistivity, the less likely the soil will contribute to the corrosion 
of metallic objects. Items of corrosion potential that are addressed in the report are 
presented in the following sub-sections. 
FIELD IN-SITU RESISTIVITY SURVEY 
Geophysical survey equipment (i.e., ground resistance testing equipment) was 
mobilized to the project site for the field in-situ resistivity survey. Field resistivity tests were 
conducted at two perpendicular line locations, designated as R-1 and R-2, using the 
in-situ Wenner 4-Pin Method and an AEMC Model 4630 resistance meter manufactured 
by AEMC Instruments, at 2, 4, 8, 15, and 30-foot spacing in general accordance with 
ASTM G57 testing method. The average resistivity values of the subsurface mass, 
extending from the existing ground surface to the test depth, were assessed by using this 
method along the two test locations shown on the Site Plan, Plate 1. Results of the field 
resistivity surveys are presented on Plates 2.1 and 2.2. 
The method utilizes four surface probes or pins (electrodes) to evaluate the 
average resistivity (an indication of corrosivity) of the underlying soil mass. The field

Based on the results of our field in-situ resistivity surveys, the subsurface soils at 
the project site generally exhibited resistivity values ranging from about 600 to

corrosive) using the Board of Water Supply (BWS) guidelines. Special attention should 
be given to the corrosion engineer when using the resistivity results provided in this report. 
LABORATORY THERMAL RESISTIVITY TESTING 
We envision that electrical duct lines will be installed underground for the new 
substation project. Geotherm USA, our subcontracting laboratory, conducted laboratory 
soil thermal resistivity testing to evaluate the thermal characteristics of the in-situ 
near-surface soils encountered at the site. Two bulk samples were collected at the 
designated locations as shown on the Site Plan, Plate 1, and were remolded to 
approximately 90 percent relative density according to ASTM D1557, Standard Test 
Methods for Laboratory Compaction Characteristics of Soil Using Modified Effort. Detailed 
discussions and test results are provided in the report entitled “Thermal Analysis of Native 
Soil Samples, HECO Kulanihakoi Substation – Ewa, Oahu, HI (Project No. 8336.00),” 
dated August 20, 2021, as included in Plates 3.1 through 3.3.
