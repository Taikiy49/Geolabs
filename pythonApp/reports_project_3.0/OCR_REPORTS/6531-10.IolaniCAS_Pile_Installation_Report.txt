In accordance with our contract with Iolani School we observed performance of the
static load test test pile installation and production pile operations for the augered cast in
place ACIP pile foundations for the Iolani School Center for Applied Studies project We
also observed installation of the micropile foundations at the project site This letter report
summarizes our geotechnical observations during the installation of the ACIP pile and
micropile foundations for the Iolani School Center for Applied Studies project
Geolabs provided geotechnical engineering consultation for the Iolani School
Center for Applied Studies project and the findings and recommendations are presented
in the referenced report The purpose of our geotechnical observations was to monitor the
geotechnical engineering aspects of the pile foundation installation for conformance with
the intent of the recommendations presented in our report In addition we provided
Special Inspection services as required by the City and Country of Honolulu by
observing the ACIP pile and micropile foundation installation for general conformance with
the project plans and specifications
PROJECT DESCRIPTION
In general the Center for Applied Studies CAS Building project site is within the
existing Iolani School campus in Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project
consists of constructing a new classroom building Based on the information provided the

CAS will consist of a four story building with a footprint of about 160 feet by 90 feet The
general project vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
Based on the geotechnical engineering report referenced above for the project we
recommended supporting the new building structure on a deep foundation system
consisting of 18 inch augered cast in place piles with allowable compressive load
capacities of 160 and 240 kips per pile Most of the ACIP piles would extend through the
soft and or loose lagoonal deposits near the surface and derive support primarily from
skin friction and end bearing within the alluvium coral ledges and or coralline detritus
materials encountered below the lagoonal deposit The typical ACIP pile lengths would be
about 50 and 65 feet for the 160 and 240 kip capacity piles respectively
The general contractor for the CAS project was Nordic PCL Construction Inc
NPCL and the ACIP pile installation subcontractor was Hawaiian Dredging Construction
Company Associates Inc Joint Venture HDCC CJA
STATIC PILE LOAD TEST
In order to evaluate the adequacy of the recommended design length of the
ACIP pile foundation a static pile load test was conducted on a dedicated ACIP pile on
August 9 and 10 2012 The pile load test was conducted on an 18 inch diameter ACIP
pile with a length of approximately 70 feet relative to the existing ground surface To better
understand the load transfer mechanism at the pile soil interface the load test pile was
instrumented with nine vibrating wire embedment strain gages installed at various depths
along the length of the load test pile
A Manitowoc 12000 series crane with crane mounted hollow stem auger powered
by an APE 75C Hydraulic pump was used to install the test piles A hydraulic concrete
pump with an average stroke volume of about 0 044 cubic yard per stroke was used to
pump the grout into the pile system The test pile was loaded in general accordance with
ASTM D 1143 Quick Load Test Method The maximum test load on the pile was planned
at 480 kips
Based on the static pile load test results it appeared the load test pile behaved like
a friction pile with nearly 100 percent of the maximum test load being resisted by adhesion
between the ACIP pile and the surrounding soils Based on our field observations and the
results of the static pile load test we confirmed the design embedment lengths would be
sufficient to achieve the design compressive load capacities of 160 and 240 kips per pile
for the 50 and 65 foot deep piles respectively with a factor of safety of at least 2 0

PRODUCTION PILE INSTALLATION
The production ACIP pile installation program consisting of installing 61 ACIP piles
18 inch diameter was conducted from August 13 2012 through August 28 2012 under
the near continuous full time observations by a field engineer from our office HDCC CJA
used the same drilling grouting equipment utilized for the test pile program to install the
production piles Automatic monitoring equipment was mounted on the drilling pumping
equipment with the intent of providing auger depth grout flow rate grout pressure etc on
a near continuous basis However the equipment was not functioning reliably throughout
the project and manual monitoring was conducted by our field engineer for the piles
installed During the pile installation operation logs were maintained for each ACIP pile
which included information pertaining to pile number date installed depth of pile grout
volume used grout return depth etc The locations of the production ACIP piles installed
are shown on the Foundation Location Plans Plates 2 1 through 2 3 The production ACIP
pile installation summary is presented by pile number on Plates 3 1 through 3 6 of this
letter report
Two pile locations were abandoned due to excessive grout loss within the soil
strata NPCL arranged micropiles to be installed at the abandoned pile locations The
micropile subcontractor Structural System Inc SSI installed two micropiles with a
5 5 inch outside diameter permanent casing extending down to about 65 feet below the
ground surface The total lengths of the micropiles were about 119 1 and 119 7 feet below
the ground surface An air powered mixer was used to mix and pump and place neat
cement grout for the micropiles A summary of the micropiles installed is presented on
Plate 4 of this letter report
Based on our observation of the equipment performance and subsurface conditions
encountered it is our professional opinion that the production ACIP piles and the
micropiles have been installed to sufficient depths and or resistance to provide the design
load capacities of 160 and 240 kips per pile with a safety factor of 2 0
