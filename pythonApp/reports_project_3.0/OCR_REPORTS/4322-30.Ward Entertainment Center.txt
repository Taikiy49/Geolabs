PILE LOAD TEST REPORT
WARD ENTERTAINMENT CENTER
Dear Mr Bumgarner
In accordance with the project specification pile load tests were conducted on two
selected test piles designated as Pile Nos 256 and 112 for the Ward Entertainment
Center project on May 17 and 24 2000 respectively This report presents the results of
our field observations during the pile load test program
The piles tested were 16 5 inch octagonal precast prestressed reinforced
concrete piles Test Pile No 112 is located at column designation 2 4 and row
designation E 2 driven on May 23 2000 Test Pile No 256 is located at column
designation 5 5 and row designation A1 driven on May 15 2000 The test piles were
driven with a hammer delivering a maximum energy of 44 23 kip feet
During the test pile driving logs of the blow counts were recorded by an engineer from
our office The test pile driving records are provided on Plates 1 1 and 1 2
The test piles were loaded in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation
D 1143 Quick Load Test Method as modified in the Specifications for the project The
maximum test load on the test pile was planned at about 300 kips In general the load
tests were loaded to the maximum test load of 240 and 300 kips in 15 kip load
increments for Test Pile Nos 112 and 256 Each load increment was held for at least
5 minutes or until the rate of settlement was generally stable For Test Pile No 112
the 150 kip load increment was held for 60 minutes and the 225 kip and 240 kip load
increments were held for 120 minutes For Test Pile No 256 the 150 kip load
increment was held for 60 minutes and the 300 kip load increment was held for
240 minutes After loading the test piles to the maximum test loads the test loads were
unloaded in approximately four equal decrements and held for at least 5 minutes at
each decrement The results of the load tests are presented on Plates through
and are summarized as follows
