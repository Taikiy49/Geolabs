GEOTECHNICAL ENGINEERING EVALUATION 
MANELE DESALINATION FACILITY – SEDIMENTATION BASIN 
ISLAND OF LANAI, HAWAII 
Dear Mr. Fukumitsu: 
This letter report presents our preliminary assessment of the proposed site grading 
at the Manele Desalination Facility – Sedimentation Basin project on the Island of Lanai, 
Hawaii. The project location and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map, 
Plate 1. 
This letter report summarizes our geotechnical engineering recommendations 
resulting from our site reconnaissance and previous experience in the vicinity. These 
recommendations are intended for the site preparation only. The findings and 
recommendations presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this 
letter report. 
Project Considerations 
The project involves development of a desalination plant and distribution system in 
the area above the existing Manele resort development and golf course. The desalination 
facility will be west of the existing sewage treatment facility. Four source wells and three 
disposal wells plus sedimentation basin will be constructed. This report only covers the 
design of the proposed Sedimentation Basin. The Sedimentation Basin site is about 
150 feet by 270 feet in plan dimension. The basin will be adjacent to an existing paved 
concrete asphalt road in the area.  Site grading involving cuts and fills on the order of 
15 and 10 feet, respectively, will be needed to create level areas for the desalinization 
facility development. 
Purpose and Scope 
The purpose of our geotechnical engineering evaluation is to obtain an overview 
of the surface and subsurface conditions to develop a soil and/or rock data set to 
formulate geotechnical recommendations for the earthwork and foundation design of the

Site Description 
The project is in the southwestern portion of Lanai, mauka of the existing Four 
Seasons Resort in Manele. The sedimentation basin site is along the western side of a 
paved construction access road extending from the west end of Kapihaa Place, as shown 
on the Site Plan, Plate 2. Locations of the disposal wells are also shown on Plate 2 for 
reference. The ground surface slopes down toward the southeast at a gradient of about 10 
percent. The site is covered with dryland shrubs and trees. 
Based on the topographic map provided, elevations of the ground surface at the 
sedimentation basin site range from approximately +450 to +460 feet Mean Sea Level 
(MSL). 
Subsurface Condition 
The anticipated subsurface conditions consist of a thin surface soil layer over basalt 
rock formation with localized limestone pockets. The surface soil is normally on the order 
of 1 to 2 feet thick and comprised of moderately expansive silty and clayey soils. The 
basalt formations generally consist of moderately weathered, moderately hard basalt rock 
with clinker layers. 
Discussion and Recommendations 
We recommend designing the cut slope in rock formation with an inclination of 
one horizontal to one vertical (1H:1V) without a slope bench. Fill slope may be designed 
with an inclination of 1.5H:1V or flatter. Detailed discussions and recommendations for site 
preparation are presented in the following sections. 
Site Preparation 
We anticipate that site grading will involve up to 15 feet of cuts and 10 feet of fills, to 
achieve the finished elevation of the sedimentation basin. In general, a Geolabs 
representative should monitor the site preparation to confirm whether the exposed 
soil/rock conditions during excavation are similar to those anticipated and/or encountered 
in our field exploration.  The following recommendations are intended to provide guidelines 
for the design of site preparation work. 
At the on-set of earthwork, the area within the contract grading limits should be 
cleared and grubbed thoroughly. Vegetation, debris, deleterious material, and other 
unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed off-site. 
In general, the on-site materials generated from the excavations may be re-used as 
a source of general fill and backfill materials, provided that the materials are screened of 
over-sized materials (greater than 6 inches in maximum dimension) and are free of

Excavation 
Based on our site reconnaissance and previous exploration in the vicinity, the 
project site is generally underlain by dense basalt formations ranging from hard to very 
hard.  It is anticipated that the basalt formation near the ground surface may be readily 
excavated with normal heavy excavation equipment, such as excavators and ripping with 
large bulldozers.  However, some of the deeper excavations in the dense pahoehoe lava 
may require the use of hoerams or chipping. 
The above discussions regarding the rippability of the surface materials are based 
on our visual observation of the existing basalt formation and field data from the previous 
borings performed in the project site vicinity.  We recommend encouraging contractors to 
examine the site conditions and the subsurface data to make their own interpretation. 
Cut and Fill Slopes 
We believe that the cut slopes planned at the site may be designed with a slope 
inclination of one horizontal to one vertical (1H:1V) or flatter.  However, due to the 
presence of clinker layers, the individual cut slopes should be re-evaluated during 
construction.  In areas where loose clinker layers, unstable, and/or friable materials are 
exposed at the cut slope faces, the cut slopes may need to be flattened.  Therefore, 
provisions to flatten the cut slopes and/or slope stabilization alternatives should be 
considered in the design of the project.  A representative from Geolabs should observe 
and map the cut slopes during construction to evaluate whether the exposed cut slope 
materials are consistent with our assumptions. 
Slope bench may be omitted due to the anticipated basalt rock formation at the site.  
It should be noted that slope benches are generally avoided in rock cut slopes to prevent 
creating a “launching pad” for rocks that may be dislodged from upper hill. 
Permanent fill slopes constructed of the general fill and structural fill materials 
recommended herein may be designed with a slope inclination of 1.5H:1V or flatter.  In 
addition, fills placed on slopes steeper than 5H:1V should be benched.  A keyway should 
be provided for fill slopes greater than 10 feet in vertical height placed on existing ground 
steeper than 5H:1V.  The fill slope face should be finished to a relatively smooth and 
well-compacted surface. 
Areas to receive fill should be scarified to a depth of 12 inches, moisture 
conditioned to about 2 percent above the optimum moisture and compacted to at least 
90 percent relative compaction.  Relative compaction refers to the in-place dry density of 
soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in

Pavement Design 
We understand that the access roadway will be paved with asphalt concrete.  The 
access roadway will serve as access during construction and maintenance during 
operation of the desalination facility.  Therefore, we envision that the access roadway will 
be subject to relatively light traffic volume.  Based on the anticipated subsurface 
conditions, the following flexible pavement section may be used for preliminary design and 
budget purposes. 
Asphaltic Concrete Pavement 
2.0-Inch Asphaltic Concrete 
6.0-Inch Aggregate Base Course (95 Percent Relative Compaction)  
8.0-Inch Total Pavement Thickness on a Moist Compacted Subgrade 
The subgrade soil under the pavement area should be moisture-conditioned to 
about 2 percent above the optimum moisture, and compacted to at least 95 percent 
relative compaction.  The subgrade should be kept moist prior to placement of select 
borrow subbase and base course.  Base course and select borrow subbase materials 
(if needed) should consist of crushed basalt aggregate compacted to not less than 
95 percent relative compaction.  CBR and density tests should be performed on the actual 
subgrade soils encountered during construction to confirm the adequacy of the above 
section. 
Aggregate base course and select borrow subbase materials should meet 
the material requirements for Base Course and Subbase Course as specified in 
Sections 30 and 31, respectively, of the Standard Specifications for Public Works 
Construction, Department of Public Works, County of Maui, September 1986.  Imported fill 
material should be tested for conformance with these recommendations prior to delivery to 
the project site for its intended use. 
Paved areas should be sloped, and drainage gradients should be maintained to 
carry surface water off the site.  Surface water ponding should not be allowed on the site 
during or after construction.

Limitations 
The analyses and recommendations submitted herein are based in part upon 
information obtained from our site reconnaissance and previous experience in the project 
vicinity. Variations of subsoil conditions may occur, and the nature and extent of these 
variations may not become evident until construction is underway.  If variations then 
appear evident, Geolabs should be contacted to re-evaluate the recommendations 
presented herein.
