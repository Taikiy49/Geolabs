PROJECT DESCRIPTION 
The project site is located at 615 Keawe Street in Honolulu on the Island of 
Oahu, Hawaii. The project involves the development of a new 42-story high-rise building 
providing 328 residential units. The project also will include a parking structure with 
eight parking levels on the makai side of the residential tower. The project location and 
general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map, Plate 1. 
Based on the geotechnical engineering report referenced above for the project, 
we recommended supporting the Ililani project on a deep foundation system consisting 
of 24-inch diameter augered cast-in-place (ACIP) piles with allowable compressive load 
capacities ranging from 200 to 1,200 kips (design load) per pile. The ACIP piles would 
extend through the fill material, loose to medium dense silty sands and gravel, medium 
hard to hard coral formation, and loose to dense sandy and gravelly coralline detritus 
with design pile lengths ranging from about 30 to 75 feet below the bottom of pile cap 
elevation.  
BI-DIRECTIONAL STATIC PILE LOAD TEST 
In order to evaluate the adequacy of the recommended design lengths of the 
ACIP pile foundation, a bi-directional static pile load test was conducted on one 24-inch 
ACIP load test pile, designated as Test Pile No. TP-1R1. To better understand the load 
transfer mechanism at the pile-soil interface, the load test pile was instrumented with 
vibrating wire embedment strain gages installed at various depths along the length of 
the load test pile. The load test pile was installed to a depth of about 93 feet below the 
existing ground surface. The location of the bi-directional static load test pile is shown 
on the ACIP Pile Location Plan, Plates 2.1 and 2.2. 
A Manitowoc 12000 Crawler Crane equipped with an APE Model 80BB Top Drive 
Auger, equipped with a continuous flight hollow-stem auger and automatic monitoring 
equipment was used to install the load test pile. A trailer-mounted hydraulic concrete 
pump with an average stroke volume of about 0.059 cubic yards per stroke was used to 
pump the grout into the pile. The load test pile was loaded in general accordance with 
ASTM D8169M “Deep Foundations Under Bi-Directional Static Axial Compressive 
Load” Procedure A Quick Test. The bi-directional static pile load test results are 
summarized in the report entitled “Bi-Directional Static Load Test Report, Ililani Tower, 
Keawe & Halekauwila Parcels” dated November 19, 2019 by GRL Engineers, Inc. 
Based on the static pile load test results, the 24-inch diameter load test pile was 
loaded to a maximum test load of 3,155 kips. Based on the favorable results of the 
static load test conducted on the ACIP load test pile, we recommended a reduction in 
the length of the 24-inch diameter ACIP production piles as provided in the following 
table.

ACIP PRODUCTION PILE INSTALLATION 
The General Contractor for the project and the deep foundation installation 
subcontractor was Hawaiian Dredging Construction Company. The production pile 
installation program, consisted of installing 169 24-inch diameter ACIP piles, was 
conducted from May 10 through June 3, 2022, under the full-time observation by our 
field engineer. A Bauer BG28 drill rig equipped with a hollow-stem auger was used to 
install the production piles. Automatic monitoring equipment was mounted on both 
drilling/pumping equipment with the intent of providing auger depth, grout volume, grout 
pressure, etc. on a near-continuous basis. 
During the pile installation operation, logs were maintained for each ACIP pile, 
which included information pertaining to pile number, date installed, depth of pile, grout 
volume used, grout return depth, etc. The locations of the production ACIP piles 
installed are shown on the ACIP Pile Location Plan (Plates 2.1 and 2.2). The production 
ACIP pile installation summary is presented by pile numbers on Plates 3.1 through 3.5 
of this letter report. 
Based on our observations of the equipment performance and subsurface 
conditions encountered, it is our professional opinion that the production ACIP piles 
have been installed to sufficient depths and/or resistance to provide the design load 
capacities of 200 through 1,200 kips per pile with a factor of safety of 2.

reinforcing cage was lowered into the drilled hole to the appropriate elevations with the 
use of a crane. After the reinforcing cage was installed in the drilled hole, concrete was 
placed through a 4-inch steel tremie pipe starting from the bottom. We did observe that 
at least 10 feet of concrete head was maintained above the bottom of the tremie pipe 
during concrete placement.

that the drilled shafts have been installed to sufficient depths to provide the allowable
