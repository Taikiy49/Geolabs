INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the proposed Kahale Mua Subdivision in the town
of Maunaloa in the Kaluakoi District of the island of Molokai Hawaii
The general location of the site and its vicinity is showm on the Project
Location Map Plate 1 The exploration was performed in general
accordance with our proposal dated
This report summarizes the findings of the exploration and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analyses based
on the data obtained during the exploration These recommendations are
intended for design input and are not intended for construction estimation
or bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct twenty six 26 two story wood framed
residences at the project site Post and beam foundations are anticipated
for most of the residential structures

will be performed to adjust the existing site grade prior to building

northwest corner and encompasses an area of approximately two acres

The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface conditions at the project site in order to develop a
generalized soil rock data set for the formation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertinent to site grading pavement and
foundation design of the project

STTE DESCRIPTION
The proposed project site is located in the Kaluakoi district of the
island of Molokai Hawaii
The site slopes down from the southeast to the northwest with a
slight depression in the central portion The surface soils consist of a
dry reddish brown clayey silt
The area is presently vacant and overgrown with grasses and shrubs
Clusters of pine trees exist along the south and west edges of the area
The site is bounded on the north and west sides by traffickable roadways
and along the south edge by a road which is overgrown and no longer in
use
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The exploration program consisted of the drilling and sampling of
five 5 borings to depths of sixteen 16 feet below the existing ground
surface with a truck mounted drill rig The location of the borings are
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general the field exploration encountered residual lateritic
soils with highly to moderately weathered gravel to the maximm depths
explored The soils can be classified as CI ML according to the Unified
Soil Classification System These soils are generally reddish brown in
color and exhibit low expansion potential

GEOLOGY
Molokai was formed by the junction of two separate volcanoes Later
either a eustatic rise in sea level or submergence of the island formed
the area between the two mountains the present Hoolehua Plain
The island subsequently emerged although with several intervening
periods of partial submergence and emergence Subaerial erosion of the
emerging East and West Molokai Volcanoes and subsequent deposition of
the clastic debris as coalescing alluvial fans formed the present
configuration of the Hoolehua Plain Late Pleistocene to Recent
weathering has produced a residual lateritic soil overlying highly
weathered basalt rock formations underlying the site
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration generally encountered relatively competent
subsoils near the surface It is our opinion that shallow spread or
continuous wall footings may be used to support the proposed buildings
Site Grading
Cuts and fills in the range of six 6 feet are anticipated
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limits should be cleared of vegetation and other deleterious materials
These materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2460 00 Page 5
The areas to be filled should then be scarified to a depth of six 6
inches moisture conditioned and recompacted to a minimum of 90 of its
maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 Any loose or soft
spots encountered should be over excavated and then replaced with select
fill materials compacted to a minimum of 90 compaction as recommended
above
Based on the field exploration results the on site near surface
soils may be used as fill material If required imported fill material
should consist of non expansive select granular materials generally less
than three 3 inches in maximm size with less than 20 passing the 200
sieve
The fill should be placed in maximum nine 9 inch thick loose level
lifts and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density per
ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
In general for cut and fill slopes slope ratios of two horizontal
to one vertical 2H 1V or flatter are recommended for the site grading
design
Water should be diverted away from the tops of slopes and slope
planting utilized to reduce potential erosion and or instability of the
finished slopes
The site should be graded to drain water away from the foundations
House Foundations
Post and beam footings may be used for the proposed project An
allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f with a minimum footing embedment of
12 inches may be used for footings bearing on the very stiff natural soils
or compacted fill material
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2460 00 Page 6
The bearing value is for total dead plus live loads and may be
increased by one third when temporary wind and seismic loads are
considered Estimated settlements are on the order of less than one half
of an inch total and one fourth of an inch differential
If foundations are located next to sewer drainage easements they
should be embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V
imaginary plane extending upward from the bottom edge of the utility
trenches or as deep as the inverts of the utility lines
The bottom of all footing excavations should be cleared of loose
soils prior to placement of the reinforcement The footing excavation
should be observed by the project geotechnical engineer to determine if
adequate bearing has been obtained prior to concrete placement
Tateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive
earth pressure developed against the buried vertical faces of the
foundation system and by frictional resistance developed between the
bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be calculated using an
equivalent fluid pressure of 300 p c f This assumes that the soil around
the foundation is well compacted A coefficient of friction of 0 35 may
be used Unless covered by pavements or slabs the passive resistance in
the upper 18 inches of soil should be neglected
Slab On Grade
For concrete slab on grade construction a four 4 inch layer of 3B
Fine gravel should be provided below the slabs as cushion fill The slab
subgrade should be kept moist prior to concrete placement
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2460 00 Page 7
In order to protect against future moisture infiltration an
impervious moisture barrier is recommended below the slabs One 1 inch
of moist fine sand may be placed above the plastic membrane to help
concrete curing
Pavement Section
It is anticipated that asphaltic concrete pavement may be used for
driveways and parking areas The following preliminary pavement section
may be used
2 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 Inch Base Course
8 Inch Total Pavement Thickness on Moist
Compacted Non Expansive Select Subgrade
We recommend that CBR and density tests should be performed on the
actual subgrade soils encountered during construction and the above
section may be revised if necessary The recommended section considers
medium traffic and assumes that adequate drainage will be provided in the
paved areas
Utility Trench
New utility lines are anticipated for the project A granular
bedding consisting of 6 inches of ASIM C 33 No 67 gravel 3 Fine is
recommended under the pipes
Granular materials should be used for the trench backfill The
backfill up to about one foot above the pipes should be lightly tamped to
reduce the possibility of damaging the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level one 1 foot
above the pipes to the subgrade or finished grade should be placed in
maximum nine 9 inch horizontal lift mechanically compacted to a minimum
of 90 of its maximm dry density per ASIM Test Method D 1557 78 to
protect against potential future ground subsidence

Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comments
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this review
is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for
misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during earth work and
construction of the foundations This is to observe compliance with the
design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow design
changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from that
anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in
this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field borings Variations of
conditions between the field data points may occur and the nature and
extent of these variations may not become evident until construction If
variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations given in this report

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling five
5 borings to depths ranging from 10 5 to 16 5 feet below the existing
ground surface with a truck mounted drill rig The locations of the
borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by visual and
textural examination in the field by our geologist who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory The soils
were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A graphic presentation of the materials
encountered is presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 5
Soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving a standard
penetration sampler with a 140 pound hammer free falling at a height of
thirty 30 inches The blow counts to drive the sample the last twelve
12 inches are shown on the Boring Logs at the appropriate sample depths
