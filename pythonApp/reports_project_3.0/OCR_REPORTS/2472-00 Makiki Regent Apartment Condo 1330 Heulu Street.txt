INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the proposed new apartment condominium at 1330
Heulu Street in the Makiki district on the island of Oahu Hawaii The
general location of the site is shown on the Project Location Map Plate
1 The exploration was performed in general accordance with our proposal
dated

W O 2472 00 Page 2
This report summarizes the findings of the exploration and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analyses based
on the data obtained during the exploration These recommendations are
intended for design input and are not intended for final construction
estimation or bidding purposes
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface conditions at the project site in order to develop a
generalized soil rock data set for the formulation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertinent to the foundation design of the
project
The work was done in general accordance with our proposal dated
The scope of work consisted of the following
1 The drilling and sampling of four 4 borings to depths between
16 5 and 39 0 feet below the existing ground surface
2 Reconnaissance of the project site and vicinity to make visual
observations of the surficial soil conditions
3 Review of available soils and geologic information pertinent to
the project site and its vicinity
4 Laboratory testing of selected samples obtained during the field
exploration to determine the engineering properties and
Classification of the materials encountered
5 Engineering analyses of the field and laboratory data for the
purpose of developing foundation and pavement recommendations
6 Preparation of this report presenting our findings and design
recommendations

PROJECT CONSTDERATTONS
It is proposed to demolish the existing 1 story wood houses on the
project site and construct a new residential condominium structure
identified as the Makiki Regent
It is our understanding that the proposed residential condominium
will be a ten 10 story structure approximately 44 by 76 feet in plan
dimension A full basement parking level is also planned for the proposed
development Anticipated maximum excavation depth for the proposed
basement parking is up to about nine 9 feet below the existing ground
surface Minimal site grading is planned for future finished grade in
areas surrounding the basement parking
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located at 1330 Heulu Street between Kewalo and
Keeaumoku Streets in the Makiki district on the island of Oahu Hawaii
The project site is approximately square and consist of two 2 lots
with the following Tax Map Key 10 11
The makai side of the project side is next to the existing sidewalk
of the Heulu Street
The mauka side of the site is bounded by existing approximately 4 to
7 foot high CRM wall
The majority of the Ewa side property line is bounded by existing
perimeter tile wall and about 25 foot long section of this property line
near the northern corner is bounded by an existing CRM wall

There are existing houses and apartment buildings placed on the lots

The site is generally level with ground elevations ranging from

original wooden framed houses were removed and some of the resulting grub
and spoil material were observed
SUBSOTL CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by
drilling and sampling four 4 borings to depths ranging between 16 5 to
39 0 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate locations of
the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general the field exploration encountered volcanic ash and cinder
deposits consisting of stiff sandy clayey silt to dense silty fine sand to
the maximum depth of the drilled borings which was 39 0 feet below the
existing ground surface The volcanic deposits graded with more dark gray
to black cinder sand at depths below 15 feet
Groundwater levels were not encountered during our field exploration
Detailed description of the subsoil conditions are presented on the
Boring Logs in Appendix A
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration encountered volcanic ash and cinder deposits
consisting of stiff clayey silt to dense silty fine sand to the maximm
depth of the drilled borings which was 39 0 feet below the existing
ground surface

It is our opinion that spread and continuous footing foundations can
be used to support the proposed condominium building with a 1 level
basement parking
Relatively easy ground excavation into the volcanic ash cinder
deposits is anticipated for the proposed basement parking excavation
However due to the anticipated deep basement and footing excavations with
depths of up to about 9 feet we recommend that an adequately designed and
constructed excavation support system such as timber shoring or steel
sheeting should be provided by the selected project contractor per OSHA
safety requirements to protect the safety of the construction workers and
to prevent possible caving in or lateral movements of adjacent ground
which may jeopardize the integrity of the existing adjacent structures
Details of our recommendations are discussed in the following
sections
Foundations
Based on our field exploration and subsequent geotechnical
engineering analyses we recommend that spread and continuous footing
foundations be used to support the proposed 10 story high condominium
structure with a 1 level basement parking An allowable bearing capacity
volcanic ash cinder deposits consisting of dense silty fine sands to stiff
sandy clayey silts A minimm footing embedment of 24 inches below the
lowest adjacent finished grade is also recommended The bearing value is
for dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third when temporary
loads wind and seismic are considered
The bottom of all footing excavations should be recompacted to obtain
a minimm of 95 maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
If soft loose soil pockets were encountered at the bottom of the
future footing excavation it should be over excavated to stiff in situ
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2472 00 Page 6
soil and replaced with granular select borrow compacted to a minimum of
95 maximum dry density
If a footing is located next to a utility line it should extend to
the bottom of the utility trench to reduce footing settlement due to
settlement of the trench backfill
Provided the above recommendations are followed estimated total
footing settlement under a column load up to 710 kips is less than the
order of one 1 inch
Site Grading
Minimal site grading with generally less than one 1 foot cut or
fill is anticipated for the proposed project
Prior to the commencement of earthwork all areas within the grading
limits and basement parking structure should be cleared and grubbed of all
rubbish and deleterious materials old foundations and asphaltic concrete
pavements The resulting grub and spoil material should be disposed of
properly off site
Select on site material such as the excavated silty sand from
basement excavation could be stockpiled and re used as select fill or
subsequent footing and basement backfill material
The fill and backfill should be placed in maximum 10 inch thick loose
level lifts and compacted to a minimm of 95 maximum dry density as
established by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Imported select fill material should be non expansive and have less
than 20 fines passing the 200 sieve
The finished grade should be shaped to shed water away from
foundations and to avoid ponding condition

Basement Excavation
Based on the current design concept a large and deep excavation
extending down to a maximm depth of about 9 feet below the existing
ground surface will be required for the proposed basement parking level
construction
It is imperative that the deep excavation be adequately supported
during construction to minimize the potential of adjacent ground loss or
lateral movements which may jeopardize the safety of the construction
workers and or the structural integrity of the existing adjacent
residential structures We recommend that a thorough study of the deep
excavation support system be required from the selected contractor and
that qualified consultants be retained by the contractor to help him
evaluate the excavation support design s before construction and also to
monitor the actual installation of the basement excavation support system
during construction
Relatively easy excavation of the subsoils is anticipated The
method and equipment used for excavation should be the choice of the
contractor within practical limits and safety considerations
In order to reduce the potential of ground movement or subsidence
excavated material should not be stockpiled within 15 feet on top of the
excavation
It is our opinion that continuous interlocking steel sheetpiles or
timber shoring may be used to support the anticipated deep excavation
Sloping ground excavation when space is available and or construction of
the basement wall in small sections may also be considered as means to
minimize possible ground trench failure in the basement wall excavation
Major dewatering is not anticipated for the proposed basement
excavation since groundwater levels were not encountered in our drilled
borings during our field exploration

subjected to relatively high lateral earth loads since the embedded
perimeter walls will be non yielding members at rest condition

Well graded granular material such as base course rock cinder sand
or equivalent should be used as backfill material directly behind the
basement walls This would minimize the compaction effort required for
the backfill and reduce the build up of additional lateral pressures on
the walls
A subsurface drainage system should also be utilized behind the walls
to reduce the build up of hydrostatic water pressure Subdrains should be
properly constructed to drain water away from the walls
Lateral Resistance
Resistance to lateral loads such as short duration wind loads acting
on the new superstructure may be obtained by passive earth pressure
developed against the perimeter basement retaining walls of the proposed
building A passive earth pressure of 300 p s f per foot of depth may be
used In addition a base friction factor of 0 4 may also be used to
determine the resistance against sliding

Design Review
Final construction plans and specifications for the proposed
construction should be forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for updated
review evaluation and written comments prior to construction This
review is needed to determine adherence to the earthwork and foundation
recommendations given herein If this review is not made the
geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for misinterpretation
of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during earthwork construction
basement and footing excavation to monitor the contractor s geotechnical
operations This is to observe compliance with the design concepts
specifications or recommendations and to allow design changes in the event
that subsurface conditions differ from that anticipated prior to start of
construction The recommendations given in this report are contingent
upon such observations

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
upon information obtained from the field data points such as borings
Variations of conditions between the borings may occur and the nature
and extent of these variations may not become evident until construction
If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations given in this report
The locations of the borings were determined by taping from
structures indicated on the transmitted topographic survey undated
Elevations of the borings were approximately determined by interpolation
from elevation points on the topographic survey map The physical
location and elevation of the borings should be considered accurate only
to the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
boring points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types and
as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross sections are based upon interpolation between borings
and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
Water level data from the boring points were collected at the times
given on the graphic representations and or in the text of this report
These data have been reviewed and interpretations made in the formulation
of this report However it must be noted that fluctuation may occur due
to variations in rainfall tides temperature and other factors

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by
drilling four 4 borings to depths of 16 5 to 39 0 feet below the
existing ground surface at the approximate locations shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2 A truck mounted rig was used to drill the
borings
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by visual
and textural examination in the field by our geologist or engineer
who continuously monitored the drilling operations The
classifications were further reviewed by visual observation and
testing in the laboratory All soils were classified in general
conformance with the Unified Soil Classification System A summary
of the materials encountered are presented on the Boring Logs
Soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving a 2
sampler with a 140 pound hammer free falling 30 inches The blow
counts to drive the sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the
Boring Logs at the appropriate sample depths
