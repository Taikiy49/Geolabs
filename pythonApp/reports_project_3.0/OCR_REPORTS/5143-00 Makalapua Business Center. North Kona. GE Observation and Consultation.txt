In accordance with your request we have provided geotechnical engineering
services during the mass grading operations at the Makalapua Business Center located
in the Kailua Kona area in the District of North Kona on the Island of Hawaii This letter
report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observation and consultation services
performed for the Improvements to Service Road project The discussions presented
herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this letter report
Project Considerations
The project site is located in the Kailua Kona area on the western coast of the
Island of Hawaii In general the project site is located between Luhia Street and
Kuakini Highway The project consisted of constructing a new service road between
Luhia Street and Kuakini Highway In addition eleven new lots were mass graded along
the eastern side of the new service road Installation of underground utility lines and
drywells were completed at the project site In general the mass grading for the project
consisted of cuts and fills of up to about 6 to 8 feet in height
The contractor for the earthwork operations mass grading at the site was Isemoto
Contracting Company Ltd Based on our records the earthwork operations
for the Makalapua Business Center were conducted between October 2003 and
December 2004
Subsurface Conditions
As mentioned previously the Makalapua Business Center project site is located in
the Kailua Kona area in the District of North Kona on the Island of Hawaii In general the
original topography of the site prior to site grading activities consisted of irregular
or hummocky lava flow terrain with frequent topographic depressions separated by
adjacent higher relief mounds and low ridge features

Based on observations during the mass grading operations the project site
generally is underlain by medium hard to hard basalt formation with interbedded layers of
clinker materials It should be noted that lava tubes cavities and blisters commonly are
encountered in basaltic lava flows in the vicinity of the project site In addition some lava
tube features were observed at the site during the mass grading operations It should be
noted that near surface lava tubes detected within the limits of grading for the mass
grading operations generally were collapsed and the loose materials from within the
collapsed lava tube were removed The resulting excavation was backfilled with
compacted fill materials placed in general accordance with the recommendations outlined
in the earthwork specifications for the project
Discussion and Recommendations
The project site generally was mass graded to finished pad elevations ranging
from about 22 to 50 feet Mean Sea Level MSL Mass grading generally consisted of
placement of up to about 6 to 8 feet of new fills to achieve the design grades A Site Plan
of the project is provided as Plate 1
During the mass grading operations extensive subgrade preparation procedures
were employed within the limits of grading at the commencement of the mass grading
operations for the project to reduce the potential for collapse of near surface cavities
and or voids within the lots The intent of the subgrade preparation procedures is to assist
in detecting and collapsing near surface cavities and or voids contained in the porous
near surface materials It should be noted that the subgrade preparation procedures
employed for this project are consistent with the standard of practice for similar type
projects and similar subsurface conditions in the Kailua Kona area on the Island of Hawaii
In general the subgrade preparation in fill areas consisted of ripping the original
ground to a depth of about 3 feet or until refusal using a ripper tooth of a large bulldozer to
assist in detecting and collapsing near surface cavities and or voids contained in the
porous near surface materials After the fill surfaces have been ripped the ground
generally was leveled with the use of a large bulldozer working the surface for about four
passes to provide a relatively level surface After leveling of the rugged surfaces with the
bulldozer the fill subgrades generally were proof rolled with a sheepsfoot roller compactor
minimum 20 tons for about eight passes to help detect and collapse near surface cavities
and or voids
Fill materials consisting of some cobbles and boulders and structural fills were
placed in the fill areas of the project site Structural fills consisted of well graded granular
materials generally smaller than 6 inches in dimension In general the fill materials within
the top 2 feet of the lots within the pad areas consisted of structural fill materials It
should be noted that general fills consisting of cobbles and boulders of about 2 feet in
dimension generally were placed at least about 2 feet below the finished grades within the
pad areas in fill areas and choked off with finer sized materials In addition boulders of
about 3 to 4 feet in dimension generally were placed at least about 4 feet below the

During the site grading and backfilling operations samples of the on site and
imported materials utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to
our office for testing The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were
established in our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557
Modified Proctor and the test results are summarized on Plate 2
In general the fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to above
optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum
dry density as determined in accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557 In the roadway
areas the last lift of fill and backfill materials were generally compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative density
During the site grading operations periodic field density tests were performed in
general accordance with ASTM Test Designations D 2922 and D 3017 Nuclear Gauge
Method The results of the compaction tests performed on the fills and subgrades are
summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 3 of this report These tests were generally
performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills at the
site A total of 61 field density tests including retests of failed areas were performed in
support of the site grading and utility installation for the project
Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from the
available on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should be considered
accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed as
unsatisfactory were generally reworked until the specified minimum compaction was
obtained and or confirmed by our field representative based on visual observation and
hand probing
Subgrades in the cut areas generally were proof rolled with a large bulldozer
minimum 20 tons for about eight passes to help detect and collapse near surface cavities
and or voids The presence of native materials such as basalt formation and a a basalt
should be anticipated at or near the existing pad elevation in the cut areas
Based on the observations of our field representatives it is our professional
opinion that the earthwork operations during the grading for the above project observed
by Geolabs in the period covered in this report were performed in general conformance
with the intent of the project plans and specifications
It should be noted that the above statement of professional opinion is based on the
grades existing at the completion of the grading phase of the project in January 2005
Geolabs assumes no responsibility for any alterations made to the slopes or finished
grades on the above project subsequent to our observation and testing period without our
knowledge and written acknowledgment

Limitations
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork was
performed without our observation However the level of earthwork observation and
testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some of the geotechnical
risks to the project It should be recognized that there are variations in the accuracy and
statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the earthwork
construction and materials
