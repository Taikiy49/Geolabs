Project Considerations
The project site is located at 1181 Kapiolani Boulevard in Honolulu on the Island of
Oahu, Hawaii as shown on the Project Location Map, Plate 1. The parcel is at the
southwest corner of the intersection between Kapiolani Boulevard and Pensacola Street
(TMK: 2-3-04: 02). A three-story building structure will be constructed.
The building structure will have plan dimensions of about 135 feet long by 40 feet
wide. The building structure will abut the existing building located on the west side of the
project site. The majority of the lower level of the building structure will consist of space for
vehicular parking and a loading zone. A lobby will be located on the north side of the lower
level. The second level will be a light manufacturing area, and the third level will be a retail
area with an open lanai. An elevator and two stairwells will be provided for access to the
upper levels on the north side of the building structure. In addition, a driveway is planned
at the Pensacola Street side of the project site.
The building structure will be supported on a drilled shaft foundation system
consisting of 24-inch and 36-inch diameter shafts. A total of fourteen 24-inch diameter
shafts and three 36-inch diameter shafts will support the building structure.
The scope of our work discussed in this letter report includes observation of the
drilled shaft foundation construction for The Wedding Ring Shop. The general contractor
for the project was Swinerton Builders, and the drilled shaft installation subcontractor
was Foundations International, Inc.
Summary of the Drilled Shaft Construction
The following subsections of this letter report provide brief discussions on the load
test shaft installed and production drilled shafts constructed.
Load Test Shaft
A 24-inch diameter load test shaft was installed by the drilled shaft subcontractor
(Foundations International, Inc.). The load test shaft excavation was conducted on May 27
and May 28, 2005, and concrete for the load test shaft was placed on May 28, 2005. The
load test shaft was installed at a production drilled shaft location (Shaft No. 12).
During the load test shaft excavation, the drilled shaft contractor installed a 30-inch
OD (outside diameter) temporary casing to an elevation of about -21 feet Mean Sea Level
(MSL). After excavation, a full-length steel reinforcing cage was inserted into the drilled
hole. In addition, an Osterberg Load Cell (O-Cell) was installed at an elevation of about
-30 feet MSL. A log of the load test shaft (Shaft No. 12) installed and the materials
encountered during the shaft excavation is presented on Plates A-12.1 and A-12.2.

A few days after installation of the load test shaft, a load test was performed by
LOADTEST, INC. Based on the load test results, we believe that the drilled shafts planned
to support the building structure should be able to provide the design load capacities.
Production Shaft Installation
After the load test, the drilled shaft contractor began production shaft installation on
June 13, 2005 and completed production shaft installation on June 20, 2005. The as-built
dimensions of the production shafts are summarized on Plate 3.
Geolabs representatives observed the excavation, placement of steel reinforcing
cage, and concrete operations during installation of the production shafts. Temporary
casing was used during excavation of the production drilled shafts. After excavation of the
production shafts to their designated tip elevations, the steel reinforcing cages were placed
into the drilled holes. The majority of the steel reinforcing cage lengths did not extend to
the full depth of the drilled holes per the structural drawings. The lengths of the steel
reinforcing cages ranged between 10 and 25 feet long. The steel reinforcing cages for
Shaft Nos. 1 and 2 extended down the full length of the shaft.
Due to the presence of groundwater within the drilled shaft excavations, concrete
was placed into the drilled holes using the tremie method. In general, concrete with slump
ranging from 7 to 9 inches was used for the drilled shafts. Logs of the individual drilled
shafts and the materials encountered during the drilled shaft excavation are presented on
Plates A-1.1 through A-17.2.
Based on our field representatives observations, it is our professional opinion that
the seventeen drilled shafts supporting the building structure were installed in general
accordance with the intent of the project plans and specifications and the geotechnical
recommendations presented in our geotechnical engineering report for the project dated
January 26, 2004 and addendum letter dated May 19, 2005. In addition, it is our
professional opinion that the drilled shafts have been installed to sufficient depths to
provide the design load capacities.
Limitations
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site. Our observation and consultation services
were performed in accordance with generally accepted standards of geotechnical
engineering practice; no warranty is expressed or implied. Provision of construction
monitoring by an engineer is not insurance, nor does it guarantee construction of any type.
The contractor shall retain primary responsibility for the quality of the work, for adhering to
plans and specifications, and for repairing defects regardless of when they are found.
