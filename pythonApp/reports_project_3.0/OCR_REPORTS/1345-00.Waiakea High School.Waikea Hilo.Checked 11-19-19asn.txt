INTRODUCTION
The purpose of our investigation was to evaluate the subsurface
soil conditions at the proposed site in order to provide recommendations
for the design and construction of the proposed gymnasium The general
location of the project site and its vicinity is shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1
The scope of our investigation included a field reconnaissance
drilling and sampling of seven 7 exploratory borings laboratory
testing of selected samples and reviewing of existing grading plans
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests which were
used in the engineering analysis that formulated our conclusions and
recommendations are presented in the appendices of this report

SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed site is situated within the Waiakea High School campus
located at the intersection of Kapiolani and Kawili Streets in Hilo on
the island of Hawaii The site is bordered by Kawili Street on the
north private residential homes on the east portable classrooms to the
west and school play fields to the east
Generally the site slopes gently down from the existing portable
classroons towards the existing play fields to the east and slopes more
steeply to a lowlying area to the north The relief of the ground
surface ranges up to about 20 feet
Presently the site is vacant with ground cover consisting of
well maintained grass lawn
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to grade the site to provide areas for a
construction of a gymnasium about 120 by 170 feet and a paved
automobile parking area about 120 by 240 feet in plan dimensions The
proposed layout is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The preliminary grading plan available at the time of our
investigation indicated that site grading which consist of embankment
fill up to 18 feet high would be required for the proposed structure
Cuts generally less than 2 feet deep are planned at the upper southern
area for the new A C parking area

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
The site is located on the eastern flank of the Mauna Loa Shield
Volcano which has been built up by deposition of basaltic lava flows
There are generally two types of flows identified as pahoehoe and ata
lava flows
The pahoehoe flows are characterized by a smooth ropy or billowy
surface and a very porous or vesicular interior The a a flows are
characterized by a rough broken surface of sharp clinker material and a
dense inner core
The lavas on the site are of Recent and Pleistocene origin
belonging to the Ka u volcanic series of the Mauna Loa Volcano The
lava flows were subsequently covered with volcanic ash deposits
Subsurface Exploration
Seven borings were drilled at the project site to explore the
subsurface conditions and to obtain samples The borings were drilled
to depths ranging from 10 0 to 20 0 feet below the existing ground
surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Detailed information on the field exploration and laboratory
testing is covered in the appendix along with the boring logs and
laboratory test results

Soil Conditions
The borings encountered up to 6 feet of brown silt weathered
volcanic ash and cinder overlying medium dense clinker layer and
moderately to closely jointed gray vesicular basalt rock a a lava to
the full depth of the borings The upper weathered volcanic ash soil
exhibited high in situ moisture contents and moderate compressibility
and 5 encountered up to 1 5 feet of fill overlying the
volcanic ash and clinker In Boring 6 the basalt clinker layer was
relatively thick and extended to 20 feet below the existing ground
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings during the time of
our field exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration disclosed relatively competent subsurface
conditions at the site From a geotechnical engineering point of view
the site is suitable for the proposed gymnasium and parking area
construction provided our recommendations are followed

Site Grading
The preliminary grading plan indicated that the major site grading
work would consist of embankment fills up to 18 feet thick over the
lowlying area Thus a substantial amount of imported 111 will be
required for the proposed grading Some cuts and fills of generally
less than 2 feet would be required in the automobile parking area
Site Preparation
Prior to filling the site should first be cleared of vegetation
debris and other unsuitable materials The area to receive fill
should then be scarified to a depth of 8 inches and recompacted to
90 of its maximum density as established by ASTM D 1557 78 test
method Any soft or loose areas disclosed during site preparation
should be repaired by removing the soft or loose materials and
backfilling with compacted suitable on site material In
particular the high moisture content weathered ash if encountered
under the new embankment slope should be removed and replaced with
compacted on site granular fill
Fill Material
Generally the on site sand and small clinker material would be
suitable for fill if free of vegetation and over sized particles
Clinkers or rock fragments in excess of 8 inches in size should be
crushed down to smaller sizes or removed from the fill material
The on site silt of weathered ash should not be re used in the
embankment fill It may be stockpiled as topsoil for planting
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1345 00 Page 6
Imported fill material required for the embankment fill should be
select borrow or other well graded granular material The maximum
rock size should be less than 8 inches The fill materials should
be tested and approved by the soils engineer prior to being
transported to the project site
Fill Placement
Embankment fills should be placed in horizontal loose lifts up to
12 inches thick and compacted to 90 of the above specified maximum
density Conventional compaction testing may not be practicable in
rock fill material Instead a testing program should be conducted
at the start of the project to determine the minimum required
number of passes by the contractor s compactor to achieve the
desired compaction The number of passes could then be used as the
criteria for compaction
Fills placed against slopes having grades steeper than 5 1 H V
should be keyed and benched to properly bond the new fill into firm
existing ground
To assure adequate compaction of fill slopes the Slopes should
first be over filled with compacted material and then trimmed back
to the design grade
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Page 7
Cut or fill slopes should not be steeper than 2 1 H V
Intermediate benches may not be required for fill slopes up to 20
feet in vertical height For fill slopes greater than 20 feet but
less than 30 feet an intermediate bench of 8 feet wide should be
placed at the mid height of the slope
Embankment Fill Settlement
Some settlements are anticipated to occur under the high embankment
fill I select borrow or granular fill materials are used to construct
the embankment ground settlements would occur relatively fast and
should be completed within the construction period
Surface runoff water should be diverted away from the top of the
Slope Slope planting should be utilized as soon as practicable to
reduce erosion of fill slope
Foundations
We believe that the compacted granular fill resulting from the
above site grading will provide suitable foundation support for the
proposed gymnasium
Spread and wall footing foundations may be used for the proposed
structure
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1345 00 Page 8
All exterior footing bottom should be embedded a minimum of 18
inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade The minimum embedment
for interior footings may be reduced to 12 inches below the finished
grade
For the footing bearing on the compacted fill an allowable net
soil bearing pressure of 2500 p s f may be used This value may be
increased by one third when temporary wind or seismic loads are
considered
Spread foundations located close to the tops of the fill slopes
should have sufficient embedment to obtain a minimum horizontal set back
distance of 10 feet measured from the outer edge of the footing to the
slope face
The ground surface should be shaped to divert surface runoff away
from building foundations and slabs
It is anticipated that the footings if constructed in accordance
with the above recommendations may have settlement of up to one half of
an inch total with differential settlement less than one fourth of an
inch between columns

On Grade Slab
Based on a non expansive slab subgrade condition anticipated at
this site on grade slab should be placed over a 4 inch layer of base
course compacted to 90 of its maximum density as established by ASTM
D 1557 78 The thickened edge around the on grade slab should extend a
minimum of 8 inches below the lowest adjacent finished ground surface
To protect against moisture damage of the flooring material a
polyethylene moisture barrier should be considered
Pavement
Asphalt concrete pavements may be used for the automobile parking
area In general the on site silt sand and clinker materials will
provide an excellent subgrade support when evenly mixed and compacted
Assuming that either the on site soils or imported select granular fills
with CBR greater than 12 are used in the pavement subgrade we
recommend that the following preliminary pavement design be used for
cost estimate purposes
2 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 Inch Base Course
8 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
on Compacted Soil
The subgrade soil under the pavement area should be compacted to at
least 95 of the ASTM D 1557 78 maximum density to a depth of 6 inches
below the bottom of the base course
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1345 00 Page 10
Additional CBR tests should be performed on the actual subgrade
soil during construction and the above design section can be revised if
necessary The recommended section considers medium traffic and that
good drainage will be provided for adjacent to paved areas
Design Review
Plans and specifications for foundation site grading and utility
construction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and
written comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that fill placement be controlled and compaction
tested to assure 90 compaction throughout the new embankment
construction Prior to filling continuous inspection should be
provided to monitor the removal of loose fill ash materials and the
keying and benching operations Subdrains may be required if seepage
water is encountered on the existing slope surface during site clearing
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be observed by
the soils engineer during construction to see that adequate bearing has
been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with
the findings of our investigation

Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to its delivery to the site
If conditions encountered are different from those presented in
this report then appropriate modifications to the design should be
made
