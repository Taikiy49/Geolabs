GEOTECHNICAL ENGINEERING INVESTIGATION
FAMILY HOUSING SPECIAL PROJECT HC4 85
PROPOSED FURNITURE WAREHOUSE
PACIFIC MISSILE RANGE FACILITY
BARKING SANDS KAUAI HAWAII
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
investigation performed for the proposed furniture warehouse at the
Pacific Missile Range Facility at Barking Sands on the island of Kauai
Hawaii The general location of the project site and its vicinity are
shown on the Project Location Map Plate l
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of the investigation was to evaluate the subsurface
soil conditions at the proposed site to determine the bearing and
settlement characteristics of the underlying soil in order to provide soil
data for the design of the foundation for the proposed warehouse
W O 1687 00 Page 2
The scope of our investigtion which was performed in general
accordance with our proposal of included drilling and
sampling two 2 borings laboratory soil testing engineering analysis
utilizing the field and laboratory data and report preparation
Detailed description of our field exploration laboratory testing
and boring logs are presented in Appendix A The approximate locations of
the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
PROJECT CONSIDERATION
The proposed furniture warehouse consists of a 42 by 50 foot
building Total load on each interior column is approximately 15 kips and
continuous wall load is approximately 2 kips per lineal foot
Fill up to about 2 feet is anticipated to raise the existing grade
level of about 15 feet MSL to the proposed finished grade of about 17 67
feet MSL for the building

GENERAL SITE CONDITIONS
The proposed site is located approximately 140 feet to the west of
North Sidewinder Road and in the vicinity of Building 1256 at the Pacific
The site is heavily overgrown with trees and other smaller
vegetations which made most of the site inaccessible The northeastern
portion is currently occupied by a small berm of beach sand which was
probably placed during past construction
Ground level at the site is sloping gently from the berm towards
the center Ground cover consists mainly of dry vegetation and beach
sand
Subsoil Condition
Two borings were drilled to depths of 15 5 to 20 0 feet below the
ground surface Due to limited access at the site both borings were
drilled at the northeastern margin of the lot It is our opinion that
these borings are representative of the soil conditions in the remainder
of the site Both borings encountered a thin crust of medium dense
locally cemented fine to medium beach sand with gravel at the depths of
about 0 5 to 1 5 feet The subsoil then graded to very dense with some
loose pockets to the depth of the borings

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATION
Foundation
Based on the anticipated soil conditions proposed finished grades
and design load we believe that shallow spread or continuous wall
footings bearing on the upper in situ reconpacted fine to medium beach
sand or compacted structural fill may be used
For footings bearing on sand or structural fill we recommend they
should be placed a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished
grade Uplift wind blast or other lateral loading condition may further
increase the embedment of the footing foundation
The botton of the footing excavation should be recompacted to a
minimum of 95 of its maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78
An allowable bearing capacity of 2 000 p s f may be used for the
foundation design The bearing capacity value is for total dead plus live
load only and may be increased by one third for short duration wind
loading Settlements under the design loading are anticipated to be on
the order of less than one half of an inch total and one fourth of an inch
differential
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1687 00 Page 5
Resistance to lateral loads for the proposed building can be
obtained by passive pressure and frictional resistance A frictional
coefficient of 0 4 may be used between the bottom of the footing and the
recompacted dense soil In addition an equivalent fluid pressure of 300
p s f per foot of depth may be used to determine the passive soil
resistance
Site Grading
It is our understanding that up to about 2 feet of fill is
anticipated for the site development
At the on set of earthwork the area within the building grading
limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed Any unsuitable
materials such as vegetation debris and rubbish should be removed and
disposed of properly off site in order to prevent contamination of the
excavated material
After clearing and grubbing the areas to receive fill should be
scarified at least 6 inches below the cleared grade moisture conditioned
and recompacted to 95 of its maximum density as established by ASTM
D 1557 78 test method Any loose spots encountered should be recompacted
or replaced with select on site soil or with suitable imported material
compacted to the above mentioned standard
GEOLABS HAWAII
wW O 1687 00 Page 6
The areas to receive pavement in the in situ beach sand should
be scarified to a depth of at least 6 inches below the subgrade elevation
moisture conditioned to optimum moisture content and recompacted to a
minimum of 95 of ASTM D 1557 78 to obtain a firm subgrade
The on site sandy soil may be used as fill material If required
new imported fill material should consist of non expansive select granular
materials less than 3 inches in maximum size with less than 20 passing
the 200 sieve
Structural fill should be placed in 8 inch thick compacted
horizontal lifts and compacted to 95 ASTM D 1557 78 maximum density
Warehouse Slab On Grade
It is our understanding that concrete slab on grade subject to
heavy truck 12 kip wheel load and forklift traffic will be used for the
proposed building
The PCA design method for City streets incorporating 15 trips per
day was used for the design Assuming that the fill soil will be similar
to the on site sand and can provide a CBR value of 15 and that the
conerete can develop a flexural strength of 650 p s i at 28 days the
cecommended pavement section is as follows
5 5 Inch Portland Cement Concrete
6 0 Inch Base Course
Ti 5 Inch Total Pavement Thickness

The slab thickened edges exposed to any unpaved area should be
embedded at least 18 inches below the lowest adjacent grade
Pavement
Asphaltic concrete pavement is anticipated for the access road
adjacent to the warehouse Utilizing the pavement design method in DM 5 4
with a design index of 3 the recommended pavement section is as follows
2 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 Inch Base Course
8 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
Design Review
Plans and specifications for site grading and foundations should
be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written comments prior
to construction to see that the intent of our recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the site grading subgrade preparation and
footing excavations be observed by the soils engineer during construction
to see that adequate compaction has been obtained and that the subsurface
conditions are consistent with the findings of our investigation

INVESTIGATION LIMITATIONS
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations
made in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation engineering
principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all other
warranties either expressed or implied
The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are
based on our site reconnaissance soil information from boring data and
laboratory tests obtained from this soil investigation
Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encountered and cannot
be fully determined by taking soil samples Unforeseen conditions such
as expansive soil and soft spots may occur in localized areas and may
require additional probing or corrections in the field Therefore
additional expenditures may be needed during construction to attain a
properly constructed project Some contingency fund is thus recommended
to accomodate these possible costs
This report has been prepared in order to assist the engineer in
the preparation of site grading foundation and pavement design of the
proposed construction

The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling two
2 borings to depths of 15 5 to 20 0 feet below the existing ground
surface A truck mounted drill rig was used to drill the borings
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by visual and
textural examination in the field by our engineer who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory All soils
were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A summary of the materials encountered are
presented in the Log of Borings Plates A l and A 2
Soil samples were obtained fron the borings by driving a 2 inch
a 140 pound hammer free falling 30 inches The blow counts to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the Log of Borings at the
appropriate sample depths
Moisture content and density determinations were performed on
selected samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil
properties The results of these tests are presented on the Log of
Borings at the appropriate sample depths
