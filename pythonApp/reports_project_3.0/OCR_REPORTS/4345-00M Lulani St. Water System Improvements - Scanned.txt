PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located along Lulani Street Lulani Place and Kamehameha
Highway in the Kahaluu area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity
of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 Based on the information
provided we understand that it is proposed to construct new 12 inch 8 inch and 4 inch
water mains and appurtenances along these roadways and highway for water system
improvements The water system improvement areas are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Based on the plan and profile drawing provided the pipe inverts are not known at
this time although they are expected to be about 3 feet below ground surface In order to
evaluate the need for corrosion control protection field resistivity testing was conducted
along the water main alignment in general accordance with the Water System External
Corrosion Control Standards Volume 3 1991 Detailed discussions and results of our
corrosion tests are presented in the following sections of this letter report

3 1991 The scope of our work consisted of the following tasks and work efforts

FIELD SURVEY
Geophysical survey equipment i e resistance meter was mobilized to the project site
on for the field survey Our field work was performed through
under clear and sunny conditions with generally dry to moist surface
conditions
One hundred four soil resistivity surveys were conducted at a maximum of 300 foot
intervals at 26 locations designated as R 1 through R 26 along the shoulder of the streets
and highway using the in Pin Method and a Nilsson Model 400 resistance
meter manufactured by Nilsson Electrical Laboratory Inc The method utilizes four surface
probes or pins electrodes to evaluate the average resistivity an indication of corrosivity of
the underlying soil mass The average resistivity values of the soil mass extending from the
existing ground surface to the test depth was assessed by using this method at the
approximate survey locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 Results of the resistivity
surveys are presented on Plates 3 1 through 3 5

The soil resistivity surveys were performed at the existing ground surface along the
shoulder of the existing roadways and highway of the proposed water mains The average
soil resistance along the proposed alignment was evaluated at each survey location with the
electrodes aligned at equal spacing of generally 3 to 9 feet at the existing ground surface
The depih of penetration of a resistivity sounding is a function of the electrode spacing
Since the depth of the water main invert was not available at the time of our field exploration
spacings of 3 to 9 feet were used for the electrode spacing for each soil resistivity survey
location
The resistance meter Nilsson Model 400 impresses a voltage between the outer
electrodes causing an electrical current to flow between them The voltage drop between
the inner electrodes is then measured by a sensitive built in voltmeter As a result the soil
resistance in ohms can be found directly on the dial The soil resistivity in ohm cm which
is directly related to both resistance and spacing can then be computed The evaluated soil
resistances the computed resistivity values and the corresponding corrosion ratings are
presented on Plates 3 1 through 3 5 of this report Field Resistivity Testing Summaries are
presented on Plates 4 1 through 4 4 It should be noted that the soil resistivity may be
influenced by temperature weather conditions existing underground facilities and other
factors
FINDINGS AND DISCUSSIONS
Resistivity is generally recognized as one of the most significant soil characteristics
with regard to the corrosivity of the soil to buried metallic objects In general the lower the
resistivity the greater the potential for corrosion of the buried metallic structure Conversely
the higher the resistivity the less likely the soil will contribute to corrosion of metallic objects
On the basis of the field resistivity test results a majority of the tests indicate the
subsurface soils along the proposed water mains within the project site have corrosion
ratings of 1 to 2 This indicates the subsurface soils have extremely corrosive to very
corrosive potential Eleven resistivity measurements indicating corrosion ratings of 3 and 4
were noted
LIMITATIONS
The evaluations presented in this report are based upon information obtained from
field data points by means of geophysical surveys Variation of soil conditions between and
beyond the field data points may occur and the nature and extent of such variations may not
become evident until a later point in time If variations then appear evident it will be
necessary to reevaluate the ratings provided in this report
