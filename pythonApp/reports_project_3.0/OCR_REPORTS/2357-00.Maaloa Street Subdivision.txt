exploration performed for the proposed Maaloa Street Subdivision in the
Inalualei District of Waianae on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general
location of the site and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location
Map Plate 1 The exploration was performed in general accordance with
our proposal dated

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to grade the project site for the development of nine
9 individual residential lots With the exception of the removal of the
stockpile of materials in the central portion of the site minor grading
with fills to a maximum of two feet in depth are anticipated to
accommodate the existing site grade for drainage purposes

The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface conditions at the project site in order to develop a
generalized soil rock data set for the formulation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertinent to site grading pavement and
foundation design of the project
The work was done in general accordance with our proposal to Mr
William Blaisdell dated The scope of work consisted of the
following

observations of the surficial soil conditions

the project site and its vicinity

SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed project site is located along the north side of Maaloa
Street east of Farrington Highway in the Iualualei District in Waianae on
the island of Oahu Hawaii
The site is presently vacant and generally level with exception of a
stockpile of silty coral gravel in the western portion of the site It is
understood that this coral gravel is to be utilized as fill where needed
during the grading process

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration generally encountered brown clayey soils
immediately below the surface layer of coral gravel The borrow clayey
soils exhibited slightly to moderately expansive potential when wetted
It is our opinion that where a minimum of twelve 12 inches of select
non expansive borrow materials are placed as fill over the existing
surface soils conventional spread or continuous wall footings embedded
to a depth of 12 inches below the nearest adjacent subgrade may be used
to support the proposed buildings Where slab on grade building
foundations are to be constructed on the existing subgrade soils we
recommend that the expansive soils be removed to a depth of 24 inches
below the slab and replaced with compacted non expansive materials
Our observation of the grading operations and subsequent viewing of
utility excavations and shallow test pits excavated in areas which did not
receive fill indicate that expansive subsoils which might affect future
structures are located in the southwest corner of the site It is our
recommendation that they be removed and replaced with compacted select
borrow
At the on set of earthwork the contract grading limits should be
cleared of vegetation and other deleterious materials These materials
should be removed and disposed of properly off site

W O 2357 00 Page 5
The areas should then be scarified to a depth of six 6 inches
moisture conditioned and recompacted to a minimm of 90 of its maximm
dry density per ASIM Test Method D 1557 78 Any loose or soft spots
encountered should be over excavated and then replaced with select fill
materials compacted to a minimum of 90 compaction as recommended above
Based on the field exploration results the on site sandy coral
gravel near surface soils may be used as fill material If required
imported fill material should consist of non expansive select granular
materials generally less than three 3 inches in maximum size with less
than 20 passing the 200 sieve such as select borrow cinder sand coral
and crusher waste All imported fill material should be tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to transportation to the site
The fill should be placed in maximm nine 9 inch thick loose level
lifts and compacted to a minimm of 90 of its maximum dry density per
ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
The site should be graded to drain water away from the foundations
Foundations
Spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the proposed
project An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f with a minimum footing
embedment of 12 inches may be used for footings bearing on the stiff
natural soils or compacted fill material
The bearing value is for total dead plus live loads and may be
increased by one third when temporary wind and seismic loads are
considered Estimated settlements are on the order of less than one half
of an inch total and one fourth of an inch differential
If foundations are located next to sewer drainage easements they
should be embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V
imaginary plane extending upward from the bottom edge of the utility
trenches or as deep as the inverts of the utility lines

The bottom of all footing excavations should be scarified to a depth
of six 6 inches and recompacted to a minimm of 95 of its maximm dry
density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 prior to placement of the
reinforcement The footing excavation should be observed by the project
geotechnical engineer to determine if competent soils and adequate bearing
has been obtained prior to concrete placement
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive
earth pressure developed against the buried vertical faces of the
foundation system and by frictional resistance developed between the
bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be calculated using an
equivalent fluid pressure of 300 p c f This assumes that the soil around
the foundation is well compacted A coefficient of friction of 0 40 may
be used Unless covered by pavements or slabs the passive resistance in
the upper 12 inches of soil should be neglected
Slab On Grade
Where concrete slab on grade construction is anticipated for the
floor of the structures the moderately expansive brown clayey silty soils
Should be removed below the slabs to a depth of 24 inches and replaced
with compacted non expansive borrow materials Where cohesive soils are
used for the above replacement the upper four inches below the slab
should consist of a granular cushion layer such as 3B Fine gravel The
slab subgrade should be kept moist prior to concrete placement
In order to protect against future moisture infiltration an
impervious moisture barrier is recommended below the slabs One 1 inch
of moist fine sand may be placed above the plastic membrane to help
concrete curing

Pavement Section
Asphaltic concrete pavement is ideal for driveway construction use
because it is more flexible to accommodate swelling potential of the
subgrade soils The following preliminary pavement section may be used
2 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 Inch Base Course
6 Inch Select Borrow
14 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
on Moist Compacted Subgrade
If concrete pavement is used in the driveway area the amount of
select borrow should be increased to a thickness of about 12 inches
Since the subsurface soils is variable CBR and density tests should be
performed on the actual subgrade soils encountered at each lot during
construction and the above section should be verified if necessary The
recommended section considers medium traffic and assumes that adequate
drainage will be provided in the paved areas
Utility Trench
New utility lines are anticipated for the project A granular
bedding consisting of 6 inches of ASIM C 33 No 67 gravel 3B Fine is
recommended under the pipes
Granular materials should be used for the trench backfill The
backfill up to about 1 foot above the pipes should be lightly tamped to
reduce the possibility of damaging the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 1 foot above
the pipes to the subgrade or finished grade should be placed in maximm
9 inch horizontal lift mechanically compacted to a minimum of 90 of its
maximm dry density per ASIM Test Method D 1557 78 to protect against
potential future ground subsidence

Design Review
We strongly recommend that a copy of this report be forwarded to
prospective homebuilders Individual homebuilders should consult with a
Geotechnical Engineer prior to construction so that plans and
specifications for the proposed individual lot construction can be
reviewed and written comments made prior to construction This review is
needed to determine adherence to the earthwork and foundation
recommendations given herein If this review is not made the
geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for misinterpretation
of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during earthwork and
construction of the foundations This is to observe compliance with the
design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow design
changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from that
anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in
this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained froh test pit excavations Variations
of conditions between the field data points may occur and the nature and
extent of these variations may not become evident until construction If
variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations given in this report

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Selected soils samples collected during the excavation of the test
pits at the project site were submitted for laboratory testing to aid in
the determination of the engineering properties and classification of the
materials encountered during the field exploration
A sieve analysis was performed on three samples collected at varying
depths during the subsurface exploration to determine the gradation of the
in place materials
A CBR test was performed on a representative surface sample to aid in
the formulation of a pavement design section for paved surfaces
Three one inch ring swell tests were performed to aid in the
determination of the expansion potential of selected soils encountered
during the exploration
The results of the laboratory testing are presented on Plates B 1 and
B 2 attached to this report
