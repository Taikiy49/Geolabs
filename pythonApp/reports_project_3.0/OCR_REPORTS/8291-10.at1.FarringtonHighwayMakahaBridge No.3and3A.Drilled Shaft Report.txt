PROJECT DESCRIPTION 
The Farrington Highway Makaha Bridge Nos. 3 and 3A project consists of 
two Acrow bridge structures spanning across Makaha Stream and West Makaha Stream 
on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. Based on the information provided, we understand that the 
new foundations for the new Acrow bridges will consist of reinforced concrete abutments, 
each supported on two cast-in-place concrete drilled shaft foundations. Additionally, we 
understand that Bridge Nos. 3 and 3A will be 42 feet wide by 130 feet and 100 feet long 
each.  
 A total of eight drilled shafts, four shafts for each bridge with 48-inch diameter will 
be constructed for the project. The drilled shaft foundations would derive support 
principally from adhesion between the sides of the drilled shaft and the coralline detritus, 
alluvium, and coral formation encountered in the borings.  The contribution from end 
bearing of the drilled shafts was discounted in our analyses due to difficulties associated 
with cleaning the shaft bottom. The project also required the performance of 
two bi-directional load tests to evaluate and confirm the drilled shaft foundation design. 
The scope of our work discussed in this letter report includes observation of the 
drilled shaft foundation construction for the Farrington Highway Makaha Bridge Nos. 3 
and 3A project only. Grace Pacific, LLC was the general contractor for the project and 
CMZ of Hawaii, Inc. was the subcontractor that installed the drilled shaft foundation 
support system. 
DRILLED SHAFT INSTALLATION 
As part of the project requirement, a total of two sacrificial load test shafts and eight 
production shafts were installed for the Farrington Highway Makaha Bridge Nos 3 and 3A 
project. The following subsections provide descriptions of the load test shaft construction 
and discussions on the production drilled shaft installation. 
Load Test Shafts 
The first 48-inch diameter load test shaft, designated as Bridge 3 Test Shaft, was 
installed between March 24 and March 29, 2022 down to a tip elevation of -80.6 feet Mean 
Sea Level (MSL) on the southwest side of Bridge No. 3. The test shaft was constructed 
using a Bauer BG 33 track-mounted drill rig. The purpose of the test shaft was to examine 
the adequacy of the methods and equipment proposed by the contractor to install the 
drilled shafts into the existing subsurface soil/rock deposits and to confirm the design 
assumptions and/or modify the estimated design tip elevations of the production drilled 
shafts.  
After the load test shaft was installed and cured for at least seven days, AFT 
performed the load test on April 14, 2022. The 48-inch diameter load test shaft was 
loaded to a maximum sustained bi-directional load of approximately 3,030 kips. Details

The second 48-inch diameter load test shaft, designated as Bridge 3A Test Shaft, 
was installed between April 7 and 8, 2022 down to a tip elevation of -59.1 feet MSL on the 
northwest side of Bridge No. 3A. The test shaft was to confirm the design assumptions 
and/or modify the estimated design tip elevations of the production drilled shafts. 
After the load test shaft was installed and cured for at least seven days, AFT 
performed the load test on April 16, 2022. The 48-inch diameter load test shaft was 
loaded to a maximum sustained bi-directional load of approximately 1,706 kips. Details 
of the load test program were presented in a report dated April 28, 2022, prepared by 
AFT. 
Based on the results of the load test shaft program, the drilled shaft subcontractor 
generally demonstrated that the proposed excavation methods and equipment were 
acceptable for installing the production drilled shaft foundations in general accordance with 
the project specifications. Based on the results of the instrumented load tests performed, 
the production drilled shaft lengths were determined. 
Production Drilled Shafts 
The drilled shaft contractor installed a total of eight production shafts at the 
locations shown on the Foundation Plans, Plates 2.1 and 2.2. The contractor installed 
four drilled shafts from May 27 through July 21, 2022 on the west side of Bridge Nos. 3 
and 3A. After about a two-month delay due to earthwork and utility relocation, the 
contractor resumed the shaft installation process and installed the remaining four drilled 
shafts on the east side of Bridge Nos. 3 and 3A from October 24 through 
December 5, 2022. A summary of the shafts installed for the project is presented on 
Plate 3 of this report. Representatives from Geolabs, Inc. observed the drilled shaft 
excavation, placement of reinforcing cage, and concrete operations during the 
installation of the production drilled shafts. 
In general, the production drilled shafts were excavated using a Bauer BG 33 
track-mounted drill rig. Excavation tools used for the 48-inch diameter drilled shafts 
included 48-inch diameter auger, digging bucket, core barrel, and clean-out bucket.  
During excavation, the following generalized soil conditions were encountered at 
the location of each bridge location: 
Bridge No. 3: About 10 feet of silty sand fill was underlain by interbedded 
layers of coralline detritus and alluvium deposits to a depth of about 
78 feet below the existing ground surface. The coralline detritus generally 
consisted of loose to dense silty sand and the alluvium layer consisted of 
medium stiff to stiff sandy/clayey silt. Cobbles and boulders were also 
encountered within the alluvium layer. It should be noted that coral

After excavation of the production shafts to their designated tip elevations, the 
reinforcing cages were lowered into the drilled holes to the appropriate elevations with 
the use of a crane. After the reinforcing cage was installed in the drilled hole, concrete 
was placed in the drilled shafts within 24 hours from substantial completion of the 
excavation. Concrete was placed through a 5-inch diameter steel tremie pipe starting 
from the bottom. During concrete placement, we observed that at least 10 feet of 
concrete head was maintained above the bottom of the tremie pipe. Concrete 
placement continued until a minimum of 4 feet of overpour was achieved. Concrete with 
slump ranging from 7.5 to 9 inches was used for the drilled shafts. Slump tests and 
compression tests of concrete were performed by others. Drilled shaft installation 
records of test and production shafts are shown on Plates 4.1.1 through 4.10.5. 
Based on our observations, it is our professional opinion that the production 
drilled shafts supporting the bridge structures at Farrington Highway Makaha Bridge 
Nos. 3 and 3A project were installed in general accordance with the intent of the project 
plans and specifications, the geotechnical recommendations presented in the above-
referenced geotechnical engineering report (September 3, 2021) for the project, and 
subsequent recommendations. In addition, it is our professional opinion that the drilled 
shafts have been installed to sufficient depths to provide the allowable design 
compressive load capacities.
