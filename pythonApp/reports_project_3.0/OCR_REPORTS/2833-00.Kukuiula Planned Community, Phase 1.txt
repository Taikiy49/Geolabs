The field exploration disclosed that the alignment of the
proposed water line stream crossing is generally underlain by
a very soft alluvial deposit at the southern side of the stream
and a stiff to very stiff alluvial deposit at the northern side of
the stream
Groundwater was encountered at elevations above the
proposed water line invert elevations along the stream
crossing alignment
To reduce the distress to the new water line due to the
settlement and differential settlement of the underlying very
soft and stiff alluvial deposits we recommend that the water
line spanning the stream crossing be provided with a

minimum of six 6 inches of granular bedding material is
recommended

groundwater levels we recommend that the excavated
trenches be properly supported with adequately designed

INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the design of the proposed sixteen inch water line stream crossing project
located at Kukuiula on the island of Kauai Hawaii The exploration was performed in
accordance with our proposal dated
The general location of the project site is shown on the Project Location Map
Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings resulting from our exploration and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analysis based on the data
obtained from the field exploration and laboratory testing These recommendations are
intended for design input and are not intended to serve as the basis for preparation of
construction cost estimates or for bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located near the southern coast of the island of Kauai to the
north and slightly west of the town of Koloa An underground stream crossing is planned
along Omao Road below the Loko Reservoir for the proposed sixteen inch water line
The proposed water line will carry water from a proposed well west of the stream crossing
to the planned Kukuiula Planned Community Phase 1 development approximately one
and one half 112 miles to the south The project site is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The invert of the proposed sixteen inch water line ranges from 222 to 223 feet
above Mean Sea Level MSL or at depths of about six 6 feet below the existing ground
surface at the approaches of the stream crossing At its lowest point below the existing

distance of approximately thirty five 35 feet

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
It is Our opinion that the proposed water line stream crossing may be constructed
along the proposed alignment provided our geotechnical engineering recommendations
as presented in this report are followed
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2833 00 Page 6
Poor to good soil conditions were encountered along the project site On the
southern side of the stream a very soft alluvial deposit was encountered at the proposed
invert elevations while on the northern side of the stream a stiff to very stiff alluvial deposit
was encountered at the proposed invert elevations
To reduce the distress to the new water line due to the settlement and differential
settlement of the underlying very soft and stiff deposits we recommend that the new
water line spanning the stream crossing be provided with a reinforced concrete jacket
The groundwater levels were above the proposed invert elevations at the stream
crossing Therefore dewatering will be required for the trench excavation and
construction of the concrete jacket
Earth Pressure Loads on Pipe
Loads on buried conduits are influenced by the width of the trench the size of the
pipes the unit weight of backfill material and the frictional resistance between the backfill
material and the trench walls To calculate the vertical loads on the buried water pipes
an average unit weight of 120 p c f for backfill material and a coefficient of friction of 0 25
are recommended Earth forces upon the pipe increase rapidly with the width of the
trench and therefore the width of the water line trench should be kept to a minimum
Settlements
Primary settlement of the water line structures is normally caused by the difference
in unit weight between the lighter excavated original earth and the heavier new backfill
over the structures The net increase in loading will cause primary consolidation or
settlement of the underlying subsoils below the trench invert Based on our estimate
primary settlement up to two 2 inches is anticipated along the portions of the proposed
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2833 00 Page 7
water line alignment underlain by the very soft alluvial deposit encountered at the stream
crossing site
Due to the organic content of the very soft alluvial deposits additional long term
settlement on the order of up to half an inch may also result from secondary consolidation
of these deposits over the expected life of the new water line structures
Differential settlement between the portions of the proposed alignments spanning
between subsoils of different competency or density i e very soft clay and stiff clay
should also be an important design consideration
The above estimates assume proper construction procedures and good
workmanship Additional settlement could occur as a result of improper trench support
or dewatering procedures
In order to reduce the distress to the new water line due to the settlement and
differential settlement of the underlying very soft and stiff deposits we recommend that
the water line spanning the stream crossing be provided with a reinforced concrete jacket
To provide uniform support below the concrete jacket a minimum of six 6 inches of
granular bedding material consisting of clean granular material such as 3B Fine gravel
or ASTM C 33 No 67 is recommended
Stockpiling of excavated material adjacent to trench excavations should be strictly
prohibited in order to reduce the potential of adjacent ground movement or settlements
Trench Excavation
Based on the plans provided to us the proposed invert depths vary from about
five 5 to nine 9 feet below the existing ground surface along the proposed alignment
at the stream crossing

Based on the subsoil conditions trench excavation should pose no untoward
difficulty with conventional excavation equipment Determination of the method and
equipment used for excavation should be made by the contractor within practical limits
and safety considerations
Due to the load considerations discussed in the preceding sections the width of
the water line trench excavations should be kept as narrow as feasible to minimize the
amount of ground settlement
Trench Support
It is Our opinion that the trench walls in the very loose alluvial deposits with shallow
groundwater level conditions will require continuous sheetpiling support It is important
that adequate interlocking steel sheetpiling be installed prior to the excavation and
maintained tight against the trench walls with proper bracing to reduce lateral movements
of the adjacent ground into the trench excavation
The steel sheetpiling should be driven properly with a suitable hammer to a depth
sufficient to maintain the stability of the trench bottom and to reduce the possibility of
bottom heaving and of piping through the subsoil or quick conditions which may be
created by upward seepage pressure during dewatering Based on our estimate the
sheeting should be driven to a minimum penetration depth of five 5 feet below the
trench bottom for this purpose A factor of safety of 1 5 is assumed for determining the
design sheetpile penetration
The required depths of penetration are also affected by the method selected for
dewatering operations The above recommended sheetpile penetration depth is based
on the assumption that there is no drawdown of the groundwater table outside of the
sheeting i e pumping is done within the sheeting The use of a deep well system
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2833 00 Page 9
outside the excavation and sheeting could reduce the required sheetpile embedment
However this is not recommended since it would result in lowering the natural
groundwater level in the surrounding area possibly resulting in ground settlement
The sheetpiling and bracing systems used throughout the project must comply with
all applicable safety regulations and requirements The adequacy and safety of the
sheetpiling and bracing installations should be made the responsibility of the contractor
His representative who should be required to be continually present at the site will have
the opportunity for observing changing conditions which might affect the trench stability
during construction
For calculating earth loads in the struts of the trench bracing the apparent
pressure diagram presented on Plate 4 Apparent Pressure Diagram may be used as
a guide The pressure diagram does not include a factor of safety of 1 5 which should
be incorporated in the final calculations
Appropriate loads due to surcharge traffic or sloping ground conditions adjacent
to the trench excavations should also be added to the recommended earth pressures for
design purposes
Although good construction procedures are followed some movement of the
trench support bracing system may be anticipated due to changes in earth stresses
during excavation Movements depend greatly on workmanship e g how quickly and
tightly the support is installed It also depends on the types of subsoils the depth and
width of excavation and the type of support In view of the complexity of the problem it
is difficult to accurately predict the amount of movement to be anticipated of a bracing
system and the adjacent ground Therefore it is important to realize that the trench
support should be installed as early as practicable

The ground adjacent to the trench excavations should be continuously monitored
for cracks or dips and other indications of movements and possible sloughing of the
trench walls
Dewatering
Dewatering will be necessary for the proposed alignment below the existing stream
for the concrete jacket construction and trench backfill The dewatering operations
should be coordinated with the trench shoring support so that the stability of the
excavations is not jeopardized The operations should be carried out without softening
the bottom of the excavations
Relatively large quantities of groundwater may have to be extracted from the
excavations since the natural groundwater table may be as high as five 5 feet above the
deepest excavation bottom
Method of Dewatering
The method and equipment to be used for the excavation dewatering should be
determined by the contractor subject to practical limits and safety considerations
It is Our Opinion that the definition of Dewatering in the contract documents
should be written to include all works or systems required to lower the natural
groundwater table and to exclude the water from the excavations to permit
construction of the proposed water line structures under safe and dry conditions
These works or systems should therefore include but not be limited to pumping
only
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2833 00 Page 11
We suggest that the following three 3 basic criteria be considered in selecting a
suitable method of dewatering
1 The method should result in the least disturbance or damage to the
adjacent existing structures
2 The method should maintain stability of and also provide safe and dry
working conditions in the excavations
3 The method should be sufficiently flexible to allow modifications to
accommodate various ground conditions
Based on these criteria it appears that sump pumping inside the excavations may
be used if the steel sheetpile shoring is driven sufficiently deep to prevent piping
and or heaving of the excavation bottoms
Dewatering by means of a well point system along the outside of the excavations
is generally not recommended even though this method could reduce the required
sheetpile penetration The resultant areal depression of the natural groundwater
table could induce consolidation of subsoils resulting in potential ground
settlements affecting the existing proximate roadways and structures
If however the use of a well point system is elected recharging by timely injection
of water back into the subsoils should be considered to balance the groundwater
table and minimize areal drawdown in areas near the existing roadways and
structures We believe however that the risk of causing settlements to adjacent
development will generally be higher by using the well point method than using the
sump pumping method

Construction Precautions
The potential impact of the dewatering system selected on depressing the natural
groundwater table must be carefully evaluated by the contractor prior to
dewatering It is recommended that a qualified geotechnical engineer be retained
by the contractor to design and evaluate the dewatering system used
The impact and safety of the dewatering operations should be made the sole
responsibility of the contractor His qualified representative who should be
required to be continuously present at the site will have the best opportunity to
promptly observe the effects of dewatering during construction and to implement
as soon as possible necessary precautionary or remedial measures including but
not limited to stopping or slowing down the dewatering operations
Where encountered at the excavation bottoms permeable granular subsoils may
be susceptible to piping and quick conditions Therefore the project dewatering
operations should be done without pumping out soil fines pumping clear water
only and should be coordinated with the shoring installation so that the
excavation stability is not jeopardized The dewatering operations should be
carried out without creating a quick condition or softening of the excavation
bottoms
Backfill
In general the trench backfill snould be constructed in accordance with the
Standard Specifications for Public Works Construction of September 1986
The first zone of backfill extends from the bedding material to at least twelve 12
inches above the top of the water line pipes Select free draining well graded granular
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2833 00 Page 13
material is recommended for the first zone of backfill to minimize the compaction effort
required and resulting stresses to the pipe The granular material should be well graded
less than one inch in size and have no more than ten 10 percent passing No 200 sieve
Compaction by water ponding or jetting may be allowed provided the granular material
is self draining
The trench backfill from twelve 12 inches above the top of the pipes to the
existing ground elevation may consist of the excavated on site soils without organic
material The backfill should be placed in thin lifts with each lift mechanically compacted
to a minimum 90 percent of its maximum dry density as determined in accordance with
procedures described in ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
A typical section of the pipe trench is presented on Plate 3 Typical Trench
Details
Precautions should be taken against the occurrence of pipeline flotation before and
during trench backfilling due to possible influx of large quantities of groundwater into the
trenches The uplift buoyant forces due to groundwater influx may lift empty or even
partially filled water pipes
If practicable the trenches should be kept free of water during backfilling Should
backfilling under water be necessary granular material such as 3B Fine gravel or ASTM
C33 No 67 should be utilized to minimize to potential of excessive settlement of the
trench backfill in the future

Laboratory Testing
Laboratory testing was performed on selected samples obtained during our field
exploration A laboratory testing program was developed to identify appropriate tests to
aid in determining pertinent engineering properties of the soil and or rock materials
sampled
Classification Tests
Frequently the classification of a material provides a correlative index to certain
engineering properties of the material
Moisture Content ASTM Test Method D 2216 90 and Unit Weight Determinations
are used as an aid to soil classification and to aid in determining certain engineering
properties The moisture content expressed as a percentage of the dry weight of the
tested sample is shown at the appropriate sample depths on the Boring Logs
Atterberg Limits tests ASTM Test Method D 4318 84 are used to determine the
plasticity of the fine grained portion of soils The Plasticity Index of the material is used
to classify the soil under the Unified Soil Classification System The results of these tests
are shown on the appropriate sample depths of the Boring Logs and are summarized on
Plate B 1
Physical Property Tests
Tests are performed to develop a correlative index of certain physical parameters
of asoil These physical parameters may be used in engineering analyses pertaining to
the performance or suitability of a soil
Consolidation tests ASTM Test Method D 2435 80 are used to evaluate the
consolidation characteristics of the soil The result of the test is summarized on Plate B 2
