SLOPE STABILITY EVALUATION 
3045 WAILANI ROAD

Dear Mr. & Ms. Imanaka: 
As requested, we performed slope stability analyses for an existing two-story 
house, located at 3045 Wailani Road in the vicinity of Pacific Heights area. Based on 
the information provided, we understand that the house was originally built in 1921 and 
renovated through 2004 as a wood frame and masonry detached single-family dwelling 
with a separated garage. 
We understand that the house structure had experienced numerous distresses in 
2010, including drywall cracks, frame misalignment, garage floor buckling, etc. The 
house has undergone repairs, such as installing micropiles and tieback anchors to 
strengthen the house structure in 2011 and 2015. Notable water was seeping through a 
CRM retaining wall between Wailani Road and the house driveway. Water leakage was 
reported in 2015 and repair was made by BWS near an existing fire hydrant adjacent to 
the CRM wall, where notable water seeped through. 
This report summarizes the results of the slope stability evaluation and provides 
our engineering opinions pertaining to effects of ground water saturating the house 
foundation subgrade. The soil profile was established based on the Geoanalytical 
Report by Weidig Geoanalysts, dated July 19, 2010. A sensitivity analysis was 
performed, to evaluate the in-situ soil shear strength in relation to factor of safety of the 
slope stability under a static uniform distributed load of 250 psf. 
Slope stability analysis was performed using the computer program Phase 2, 
version 6 from RocScience, Inc. The model for the slope stability analyses was 
established using the two dimensional (2D) finite element stress analysis method to 
compute the factor of safety of the critical slip circle. Results of the slope stability 
analyses are summarized in the table below.

It should be noted that it is generally acceptable for a factor of safety greater than 
1.3 to be considered sufficient for slope stabilization under the static loading condition. 
Based on the results of the slope stability analyses, we believe that the saturated 
ground condition would have a significant impact on reducing the factor of safety of 
slope stability. Given the saturated ground condition reported by the contractor during 
2011 construction, the water line leakage would cause ground saturation and adversely 
impact the slope stability of the house foundation, inducing house structure distress 
and/or failure.    
LIMITATIONS 
The analyses and recommendations submitted herein are based in part upon 
information obtained from available report and/or construction drawings and experience in 
the project vicinity.  Variations of subsoil conditions may occur, and the nature and extent 
of these variations may not become evident until construction is underway.  If variations 
then appear evident, Geolabs should be contacted to re-evaluate the recommendations 
presented herein.

This report has been prepared solely for the purpose of assisting the design 
engineers in the conceptual evaluation of the proposed property. Therefore, this report

preparation of construction cost estimates. 
The owner/client should be aware that unanticipated soil conditions are commonly 
encountered.  Unforeseen soil conditions, such as perched groundwater, soft deposits, 
hard layers or cavities, may occur in localized areas and may require additional probing or

constructed project.  Therefore, a sufficient contingency fund is recommended to 
accommodate these possible extra costs.
