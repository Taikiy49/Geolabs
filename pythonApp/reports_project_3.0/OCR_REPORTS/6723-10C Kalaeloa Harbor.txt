At your request, Geolabs, Inc. provided geotechnical engineering observation and
consultation services during the drilled shaft foundation installation for the new monopole
at the above project. This letter report summarizes our geotechnical engineering
observations during the installation of the drilled shaft foundations for the SPSP Maritime
Wireless Network System project located in the Barber’s Point area on the Island of Oahu,
Hawaii.
The purpose of our geotechnical engineering observation during construction was
to provide engineering consultation and assist in problem resolutions during the
construction of the drilled shaft foundation.
Project Considerations
The project consists of constructing new communication monopoles at various
locations throughout the State of Hawaii. As part of the project, a new monopole of about
80 feet in height will be constructed at Kalaeloa Harbor.
Based on the project plans, the new monopole was designed to be supported on a
deep foundation system consisting of a 72-inch diameter cast-in-place concrete drilled

Summary of Drilled Shaft Installation
Installation of test shafts and load testing were not required by the contract
documents for this project. The drilled shaft contractor installed the drilled shaft on
June 18, 2013 and June 19, 2013. The as-built diameters and the lengths of the
production drilled shafts installed for the project are summarized on Plate 1.1 of this letter
report. Detailed installation logs for the production shafts are provided on Plates 1.2
through 1.4. Representatives from Geolabs observed the drilled shaft excavation,
placement of reinforcing cage, and concrete operations during the installation of the drilled
shaft.
In general, the drilled shaft was excavated using a Casagrande drill rig equipped
with 72-inch diameter augers and 60-inch diameter clean-out bucket. No drilling slurry was
utilized for the production shafts. However, 72- and 78-inch diameter temporary casings
were used during excavation of the drilled shaft to avoid the potential cave-in condition of
the lagoonal deposits near the ground surface and also to provide a safer working
environment. The drilled shaft was generally extended to about 35.8 feet below the
existing ground surface.
After excavation of the shaft to its designated tip elevation, the reinforcing cage was
lowered into the drilled hole to the appropriate elevation using a crane. After the reinforcing
cage was installed in the drilled hole, concrete was placed and pumped through a 4-inch
|.D. steel tremie pipe starting from near the bottom of the excavation. During concrete
placement, we observed that at least 5 feet of concrete head was maintained above the
bottom of the tremie pipe.
The concrete for the shaft was supplied by Hawaiian Cement. A special design
concrete mix was utilized for the production shafts, which maintains a high slump
(7 to 9 inches) throughout the concrete pour. Log of the drilled shaft installed and the
materials encountered in the drilled shaft during shaft excavation are presented on the
attached Drilled Shaft Record.
Crosshole sonic logging (CSL) testing was conducted on the completed drilled shaft
to evaluate the integrity of the drilled shaft. The details of the CSL testing and test results
are provided on Plates 2 through 4.8.
