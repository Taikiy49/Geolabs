Dear Mr Settepani
As requested this letter provides our recommendations for the planned cut slopes
located at the northern portion of the subject site Our recommendations are based on our
evaluation of the proposed cut slope inclination and a review of our geotechnical engineering
report prepared for the project on August 18 2000
We understand that new cut slopes are proposed to construct a detention basin at the
existing Waimanalo Gulch facility The Site Plan drawing which shows the approximate
location of the subject cut slope area is attached as Plate 1
Based on a review of the existing site conditions we believe that the proposed cut slopes
to be located within the gulch encompassed by the lower elevation gulch side slopes shown on
Plate 1 should encounter weathered basalt rock formation consisting of alternating layers of
dense basalt rock and medium hard basalt clinker We believe that a proposed cut slope
inclination of one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V is suitable for the various basalt rock
materials that may be encountered A steeper cut slope inclination of up to may be
used outside of the portions of the detention basin in contact with water and where competent
basalt is encountered If alluvium or colluvium is encountered the slope inclinations should be
flattened to 2H 1V We recommend that the excavations be monitored by an engineer or
geologist to confirm the suitability of the actual materials encountered
We appreciate the opportunity to be of continued service to you on this project If you
have questions or need additional information please contact our office
