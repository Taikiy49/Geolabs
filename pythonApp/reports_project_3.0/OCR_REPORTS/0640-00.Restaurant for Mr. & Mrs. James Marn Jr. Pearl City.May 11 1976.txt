This nreport presents the results of our test pit and soil
testing performed at the subject restaurant development site
Field Investigation
Two test pits were dug at the subject restaurant site at the
two ends of the proposed restaurant building location The
subsurface soils encountered were logged and visually indenti
fied in the field Representative soil samples were obtained
from the test pits and were brought back to the laboratory for
additional laboratory classification and testing
Proposed Development
The proposed restaurant site is located at Pearl City makai
of the intersection of Kam Highway and Lehua Avenue The
Pearl City Tavern is located Ewa across Lehua Avenue
The proposed construction consists of a one story restaurant
building walkway and A C pavement parking
It is anticipated that slab on grade with perimeter load bearing
footing will be utilized
Subsurface Soil Conditions
The two test pits generally indicated light brown to mottled
brown gray clayey subgrade soils which has high swelling poten
tial The clayey material generally is stiff and it will provide
adequate support for light building loads anticipated from this
development

Discussion Recommendations
For the proposed l story restaurant construction allowable
bearing capacity of 2 kips per square foot may be used Be
cause of the expansion potential of the subgrade soils the
proposed perimeter footing should extend a minimum of 2 feet
below the top of slab elevation in order to minimize moisture
migration In order to curtail the expansion potential of the
site clay soil and to avoid over excavation and replacement
with off site select borrow lime stabilization are recommended
The stabilized area will include the building slab walkway
and A C pavement
If the 8 inch layer of the subgrade soil is not lime stabilized
the non expansive select borrow backfill layer would amount to
18 inches thick
Detailed procedures for lime stabilization are included in the
Lime Stabilization Specifications
Slab on grade should have a minimum of 4 inch base course under
lain with lime stabilized subgrade For moisture sensitive flooring
plastic sheet should be provided below the concrete slab for mois
ture protection
Because of the limited field exploration involved in this project
we recommend that the soil engineer should inspect all footing
excavations prior to the placement of steel and concrete The
subgrade lime stabilization water curing preparation and com
paction should be monitored by a soil engineer to assure that
proper procedures are carried out by the contractor Adequate
compaction tests should be obtained to be sure that the stabilized
soil are properly compacted prior to the placement of base course
and pavement It is also very important that the subgrade be
compacted wet of optimum moisture content and that the base
course layer is placed as soon as proper subgrade moisture has
been obtained
The above recommendations are contingent upon such construction
inspections and control
If you have other questions concerning the above please feel
free to contact us

The areas to be lime stabilized within each lot shall be

2 Lime Stabilization
50 pound sack of hydrated lime manufactured locally by
Gaspro or equivalent shall be used Hach 50 pound sack
of lime shall be used for every 16 square feet of the area
to be stabilized This amounts to spreading one bag of
lime at every four feet interval covering the entire area
The lime together with a 8 inch minimum thick of the surface
soil layer shall be thoroughly mixed by the proper pulverising
equipments Sufficient numbers of passes shall be made until
the lime are well blend in with the underlying 8 inch layer
of surface soils Initially in the field samples of the
soil lime mix shall be obtained after several trial run to
determine if adequate mixing are obtained
LIME STABILIZATION SPECIFICATIONS cont d
After a uniform lime content is obtained the soil
engineer shall give approval to the contractor to
flood the soil lime mix with water Water soaking
and curing the lime mix shall last a minimum of 48
hours and until the lime has substantially reacted
with the on site soil During this 48 hour period
the soil lime mix shall not be allowed to dry below
its saturated water content Periodic sampling will
be made by the soil engineer to assure that the lime
is adequately water cured in the field Sprinkler
system shall be used to provide continuous supply of
moisture in the mix
At the end of 48 hours the stabilized and cured soil
shall be allowed to dry and to lower its moisture con
tent to 2 above optimum At this time the soil can
be compacted to a minimum of 80 of its maximum density but
no more than 85 compaction Final compaction shall be
carried out with pneumatic or smooth wheeled rollers used
in conjunction with graders to bring the stabilized soil
to final grade Light water application on the mix shall
be made to keep the moisture 2 above optimum
LIME STABILIZATION SPECIFICATIONS cont d
Immediately after the necessary compaction density is
obtained on the lime stabilized sub base quary waste
base course and the sand layer with polyethylene shall
be placed as soon as possible to prevent further drying
It is very important to keep the stabilized area moist
at all time during construction
Equipment
The soil engineer shall determine the type of equipment
which will attain the specified results in the most ef
ficient manner Pulveriser or stabilizer may be used
in the test section to obtain the proper mixing in the
most expedient manner Compaction equipment used shall
be in good working condition and fully ballasted Any
unsatisfactory equipment shall be rejected by the soil
engineer and said equipment shall be replaced by the
contractor and approved by the soil engineer Unsatis
factory work shall be reworked until satisfactory results
are obtained
