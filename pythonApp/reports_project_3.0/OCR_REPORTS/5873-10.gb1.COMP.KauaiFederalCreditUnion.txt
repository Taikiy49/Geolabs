This report summarizes our earthwork observation and testing services performed 
during the site grading operations, foundation excavation, pavement construction, and 
utility installation for the Kauai Community Federal Credit Union project from June 3, 2009 
through September 2, 2010 in accordance with the scope of services outlined in our fee 
proposal dated May 18, 2009. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The site for the Kauai Community Federal Credit Union project is in the Lihue area 
on the Island of Kauai, Hawaii. The site is between Kalepa Street and Pahe’e Street within 
the Kukui Grove Village. The project involves the construction of a new building and 
parking at the site. The new building is at the south side of the site.  
The northern portion of the site will consist of an on-grade parking area. This 
parking area is referred to as the upper parking level. The upper parking level elevation is 
at approximately +288 feet MSL.    
EARTHWORK OPERATIONS 
RCM, Inc. was the general contractor for the Kauai Community Federal Credit 
Union project. Earthworks Pacific, Inc. performed the site grading operations, foundation 
excavation, pavement construction, and utility installation. The site grading consisted of 
clearing and grubbing, footing excavation, subgrade preparation, placement and

compaction of fills and backfills, pavement construction, and utility trench excavation and

During the site grading operations, samples of the on-site and imported materials 
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our laboratory for 
testing. The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in 
our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D 1557 (Modified Proctor), and the test 
results are summarized on Plate 2. 
The fill and backfill materials were moisture-conditioned to near the optimum 
moisture content, and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density 
as determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557.  For pavement areas, the subgrades 
and base course fill materials were compacted to a minimum of 95 percent of the 
maximum dry density.  
During the site grading and utility backfilling operations, periodic field density tests 
were performed in general accordance with ASTM D 6938 (Nuclear Gauge Method). 
Results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 3.1 through 3.18 of 
this report. These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on 
constructed fills and backfills at the site. Test locations and elevations were estimated by 
pacing and interpolating from on-site survey stakes. These locations and elevations should  
be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used. Areas assessed 
to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained. 
Our services consisted of part-time field observation of the contractor’s fill 
placement, subgrade preparation, and compaction procedures and confirmation of 
subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above grading operations. 
Based on the observations of our field representatives, it is our professional opinion 
that the site work operations for the above-mentioned site observed and tested by 
Geolabs in the period covered in this report were performed in general conformance with 
the intent of the project plans and specifications and our geotechnical engineering 
recommendations presented in the referenced reports and subsequent recommendations. 
Geolabs assumes no responsibility for any alterations made to the slopes or finished 
grades on the above project subsequent to our observation and testing period without our 
knowledge and written acknowledgment. 
LIMITATIONS 
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities 
occurring simultaneously on a construction site. Therefore, some of the earthwork may 
have been performed without our observation. However, the level of earthwork 
observation and testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some 
of the geotechnical risks to the project. It should be recognized that there are variations 
in the accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the 
earthwork construction and materials.
