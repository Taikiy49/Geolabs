Dear Mr. Sales: 
In accordance with the contract entered into with The Howard Hughes Corporation, 
Geolabs observed the installation of the jet grout test section and production columns for 
the Auahi Street 18-Inch Sewer Line project in the Kakaako area of Honolulu on the Island 
of Oahu, Hawaii. This letter report summarizes our geotechnical observations during the jet 
grout column installation for the project. 
The purpose of our geotechnical observations was to monitor the geotechnical 
engineering aspects of the jet grout column installation for conformance with the intent of 
the project plans and specifications. 
PROJECT DESCRIPTION 
The project involves the installation of a new 18-inch diameter sewer line along 
Auahi Street in the Kakaako area of Honolulu on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. Over 500 
feet of the new PVC sewer line will be installed for the project. 
The off-site gravity sewer line was designed to be supported by jet-grouted columns 
following the strict guidelines by the City and County of Honolulu, where appreciable 
settlement of the sewer line is not tolerable.  The project required primary jet-grouted support 
columns with a minimum diameter of 36 inches installed at a maximum 10-foot 
center-to-center spacing along the centerline of the new sewer line.  The primary jet grout 
columns are designed to be installed at least 1 foot into the coral formation/coralline detritus, 
located at about -19 feet Mean Sea Level (MSL). In addition, secondary continuous jet-
grouted cradle columns were required from about 6 inches below the sewer pipe inverts 
extending down at least 5 feet between the primary jet grout columns.

JET GROUT TEST SECTION 
As part of the project requirements, one sacrificial jet grout test section was installed 
adjacent to Auahi Street to the south, near SMH B. The test section was installed by 
Schnabel Foundation Company (Schnabel), on December 11, 2019.  
The test section consisted of installing three jet grout columns, designated as T-1 
through T-3, consisting of 5-foot diameter jet grout columns.  The test columns were installed 
to elevations of about -22 to -14 feet MSL. 
After 7 days, the test section was excavated for physical inspection of the installed 
test columns.  Based on our observations, test columns T-1 through T-3 had diameters of 
at least 5 feet.  Following the test section installation, the contractor decided to utilize 5-foot 
diameter jet grout columns for the production jet grout columns (primary support and 
secondary cradle columns). 
A continuous coring of the test column was conducted using a drill rig equipped with 
triple-tube core barrel.  Unconfined compression tests were conducted by others on the 
samples of soil-crete retrieved from the test column. 
PRODUCTION JET GROUT COLUMN INSTALLATION 
The production jet grout column installation program consisted of installing primary 
jet grout columns and secondary cradle columns along the new sewer line alignment. In 
addition, the contractor installed jet grout columns along the sides of the planned trench for 
dewatering and shoring support.  The production jet grout column installation, including 
shoring support columns, was conducted from January 6, 2020 to July 14, 2020 under the 
near-continuous (full-time) observations by a field representative from our office. Schnabel 
used a Comacchio MC8D crawler drill rig equipped with Jean Lutz Dialog JTC jet grout 
measuring system to install the jet grout columns. 
In general, the 5-foot diameter primary jet grout columns were installed along the 
centerline of the new sewer line at a maximum center-to-center spacing of 10 feet.  Five-foot 
diameter jet grout columns were also utilized for the secondary cradle columns.  The cradle 
columns were also installed along the centerline of the new sewer line at a center-to-center 
spacing of about 4 feet.   During the jet grout column installation operation, logs were 
maintained for each jet grout column, which included information pertaining to column 
number, date installed, depths of installation, grout volume used, grout withdrawal rate, etc.  
The records of the jet grout columns installed are presented on Plates 1.1 through 1.127 of 
this letter report. 
Continuous coring of production jet grout columns was conducted at six locations 
using a drill rig equipped with triple-tube core barrel.  Unconfined compression tests were 
conducted by others on the samples of soil-crete retrieved from the corings.
