INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of a soil investigation for
the proposed Fort Shafter Sewer Replacement Off Site Gravity
Sewer Line B located in Fort The
purpose of this study is to determine the general subsurface
conditions and develop pertinent soil data and engineering
recommendations for sewer design and construction cost estimate

PROJECT CONSIDERATION
The new gravity sewer line is to replace a section of the
existing Tripler General Hospital gravity sewer line mauka
of Fort Shafter Sewer Pump Station in Fort Shafter Oahu
Hawaii The sewer line replacement under this study consists
of the following two schemes
Scheme_
Scheme 1 is only to replace the section of the sewer makai
of the Moanalua Freeway to the existing Fort Shafter Sewer
Pump Station The length of the new sewer is approximately
2 160 feet with sewer inverts varying from elevation 5 7 to
7 3 feet The replacement sewer will be a 27 inch diameter
pipe and will generally be placed about 15 to 20 feet below
the ground surface The highland east of the Hawaiian Tele
phone Company base yard will be excavated to approximately
elevation 13 before the new sewer construction The mass
grading east of the Telephone Company site will therefore
involve a maximum cut height of approximately 20 feet
Scheme 2
In order to raise the sewer invert and to minimize deep
dewatering and trenching in soft ground conditions the
alternate scheme is to extend the replacement sewer mauka

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The area between the Moanalua Garden and the Shafter Flat
is located on the southwest flank of the Koolau Volcanic
domes It is composed of basaltic lava flows of early
Pleistocene age The basalt is overlain by a complex
interlayered system of terrestrial and marine sedimentary
deposits During the late Pleistocene age many sea level
shifts caused by renewed volcanism resulted in local swamp
conditions which was later superimposed by more recent
deposits of terrestrial detritus of sand gravel silt and
clay eroded from the older rock of the area
Along the sewer line alignment the following soil conditions
exist

The large boulders from the alluvial deposits indicate high

complex geological process had existed in the area

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Poor subsurface conditions are encountered along the proposed
alignment at the upper section inside the Moanalua Garden and
the middle section mauka of the Hawaiian Telephone Company
base yard along Moanalua Freeway
Based on the old construction plan of the existing gravity
sewer the present Telephone Company base yard site was once
taro patches and fish pond Later land fill and structural
improvements in this area have caused this portion of the
sewer line to settle Recent survey of the sewer manholes
located in this area indicated more than 2 5 feet of settle
The existing sewer line mauka of the Freeway in the Moanalua
Garden is also underlain by thick compressible soils However
site grading improvement in this area has been very limited
after the construction of the existing sewer Therefore a
much lesser amount of sewer settlement has occurred in this
area compared to the Hawaiian Telephone Company site The
laboratory consolidation test results indicate the present
settlement rate of the existing sewer should have reached its
secondary consolidation phase This means that the settlement
has practically ceased Based on the soils engineering view
point if this mauka section of the existing sewer is still in
good structural condition this portion of the sewer could
remain The anticipated future settlement of this section of

Loads on buried conduits are influenced by the width of the
trench the size of the pipe the unit weight of backfill
material and the friction resistance between the backfill
material and the trench wall To calculate the vertical
loads on the pipe a unit weight for backfill material of
125 p c f and a coefficient of friction of 0 25 are recom
mended Earth forces upon the pipe increase rapidly with
the width of the trench therefore the width Bd of the
trench should be kept to minimum The enclosed Plate 2
indicates three alternative types of trench
Additional traffic load should be considered on the sewer
pipe located in the Telephone Company site where the align
ment is within the driveway area

wet strengths The sewer line construction in general will not

additional fills are not placed upon the existing ground surface
The resulting net loading between the weight of excavated material
and backfill will be essentially small Based on the laboratory

Pipe Bedding
Distribution of the stresses against the bottom of a pipe has
a great effect upon the load supporting capacity of the pipe
GEOLABS HAWAII
W 15
Pipe bedding the contact between the foundation and the pipe
is therefore an important design consideration
Due to the high groundwater level close to or above the pro
posed sewer invert and the very soft condition throughout the
upper and middle sections it is recommended that a two foot
thick crushed rock cradle be utilized to stabilize the bottom
of the trench along this portion of the sewer line The rock
cradle material should be well compacted and brought up to
within six inches of the invert grade or to the bottom of any
concrete jacket or cradle
We recommend Class B First Class Bedding for the proposed
sewer The sewer pipe should be bedded in a minimum of 6
inches of compacted granular material placed on a flat trench
bottom The granular bedding should extend beyond the sides
of the pipe a minimum width of one fourth the outside pipe
diameter and should extend halfway up the sides of the pipe
The recommended bedding detail is illustrated on the enclosed
Plate 3
In the lower section the same pipe bedding should be utilized
But in this area a firm trench bottom is expected and the two
foot thick rock cradle can be reduced to a one foot thickness

Settlements adjacent to open cuts cannot be predicted
since they depend on accidental features of the subsoil
and construction methods The settlements may be caused
by erratic loss of ground associated with seepage or
runs of cohesionless material or by strains in the
soil mass associated with stress relief caused by
removal of the excavated material
Settlements of adjacent ground can be minimized by
preventing erratic loss of ground and by introducing
points of support for the side walls of the bracing
system as soon as practicable and at appropriate and
close vertical spacings If these steps are taken
and the workmanship is good the settlements will be
negligible

Dewatering
As previously stated groundwater was encountered at
or above the proposed invert level throughout the pro
ject site The method of dewatering should be left
Open to the Contractor provided adequate excavation
stability is maintained The project is well separated
from nearby structures and special protection for adjoining
structures will generally not be required except for the
middle section inside the Telephone Company base yard where
slab on grade structure is about 15 to 20 feet away from
the center of the proposed sewer To prevent erratic
loss of soils that may cause settlement adjacent to
the open cut the depth of sheet piling in this section
should be designed to prevent any piping to occur at
the bottom of the trench excavation It is recommended
that the required penetration for sheeting should be
designed for loose sand condition of infinite depth
Backfilling should proceed as soon as possible after
trench made joints or other structure gains sufficient
strength to withstand earth loads without damage
The first zone of backfill extends from the bedding
material to at least 24 inches above top of pipe or
structure The intermediate zone containing the
major volume of the fill extends to the pavement
subgrade or finished grading materials
GEOLABS HAWAII
W 21
The first zone should consist of selected free draining
well graded granular material placed by hand or by
suitable equipment in such manner as not to disturb
the pipe and compacted to a density consistent with
design assumptions The granular material minimum
90 compaction is brought above the pipe to insure
high density backfill with minimum compactive effort
Material for the intermediate zone is not critical as
long as the material is capable of compaction resulting
in a reasonably small number of voids We recommend
this material be compacted to 90 compaction
Since the middle section is at a base yard and driveway
area the upper zone should be constructed to the proper
elevation for receiving paving and base course under
conditions matching design assumption for the subgrade
Before and during the backfilling of a trench precautions
should be taken against the floatation of pipelines due
to the entry of large quantities of water into the trench
causing an uplift on the empty or partly filled pipeline
The on site black organic clays and silts are presently
above the optimum moisture and will generally require
drying to obtain the specified levels of compaction for
the Hawaiian Telephone Company base yard section of the

APPENDIX B
LABORATORY TESTING
Moisture content determinations were made on all the
samples as an aid in the soil classification and in evaluating
soil properties Unit weights were run on selected samples
to further aid in the evaluation of the soil properties The
results of these tests are presented on the Logs of Borings
at the appropriate sample location
Four consolidation tests were performed on undisturbed
sample to determine the consolidation characteristic of the
organic silt layer The consolidation curves are shown on
Plates B 1 through B 4
Two grain size analysis tests were conducted on the
on site soil to evaluate the grain size characteristics of
the sandy and gravelly material The results of these tests
are presented on Plate B 5 Table 1
Nine direct shear tests were performed on selected soil
samples to evaluate the strength characteristics of the soils
encountered the test results are shown on Plates B 6 through
B 8
