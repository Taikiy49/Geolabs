PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Mehana at Kapolei development site is bordered by a drainage easement and
Roosevelt Road to the south Ft Barrette Road to the east and future James Campbell
Estate residential development to the north and west Phase 1 is in the northeastern
portion of the project site and encompasses approximately 43 acres as shown on the
Site Plan Plate 1 1
The mass grading consisted of fills up to about 12 feet The grading plans for the
site are shown on the Site Plans Plates 1 2 thru 1 4
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services for
the site grading operations and infrastructure installation for the project site
SITE GRADING
The mass grading consisted entirely of filling the site up to about 12 feet Various
imported off site materials were used to fill up to about 4 feet below finished grade The
upper 4 feet of fill consisted primarily of crushed coral structural fill At the onset of grading
the area was cleared and grubbed prior to filling Royal Contracting Company Ltd
performed the site grading backfilling work During the site grading operations samples of
the imported materials utilized as fill materials were obtained and transported to our office
for testing The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established
in our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test
results are summarized on Plate 2

The fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as
determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557 In the roadway areas the fill and backfill
materials within 2 feet of the finished subgrade were compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative density
During the site grading and utility backfilling operations periodic field density tests
were performed in general accordance with ASTM D 2922 Nuclear Gauge Method
Compaction test results are summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 58 These tests were
performed on the prepared over excavated surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills
Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site
survey stakes These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the
degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked
until the specified minimum compaction was obtained and or approved by our field
representative based on visual observation and hand probing
Our services consisted of full time and part time field observation of the contractor s
fill placement subgrade preparation and compaction procedures and confirmation of
subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above grading operations
Based on our field representative observations and the referenced test results it is
our opinion the site grading and utility installation work for the Mehana at Kapolei Mass
Grading Phase 1 project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this
report were performed in conformance with the general intent of the recommendations in
the following references

HOUSE CONSTRUCTION CONSIDERATIONS
The finished grades adjacent to the structures should be sloped to shed water away
from the foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also advised to install
gutter systems around the house structures and to divert discharge away from the
foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering within 3 feet of the
foundations and slabs should be avoided Gravel strips adjacent to slabs and foundations
should be avoided Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
building foundations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation and
subsequent loss of strength of the near surface soils The foundation excavations should

altering the drainage patterns
