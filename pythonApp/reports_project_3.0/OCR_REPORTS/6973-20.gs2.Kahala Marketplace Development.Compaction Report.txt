Dear Mr. Char: 
This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations and testing 
services performed during the excavation and backfilling of contaminated material 
excavations for the Kahala Marketplace Development project from January 7, 2019 to 
June 8, 2020 in accordance with our fee proposal dated July 9, 2018. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The project site is located on the mauka side of Waialae Avenue, east of Hunakai 
Street, and south of Keanu Street in Honolulu on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. The project 
generally involves the construction of one to two-story structures, retail spaces, parking stalls 
and driveways. The location of the project site is shown on the Project Location Map, Plate 1, 
and on the Site Plan, Plate 2. 
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services 
pertaining to the backfilling of the excavations for the project site. 
BACKFILLING OF EXCAVATIONS 
The excavation and backfilling of the excavations for the contaminated materials for the 
Kahala Marketplace Development project were observed by Geolabs, Inc. from 
January 7, 2019 to June 8, 2020. The excavation and backfilling work were performed by 
Royal Contracting Company Ltd. who was the subcontractor for the prime contractor, Allied 
Builders System. During the backfilling operations, samples of the on-site and imported 
materials utilized as backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing. 
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our

The backfill materials were moisture-conditioned to above the optimum moisture 
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as 
determined in accordance with ASTM Test Method D1557. 
During the backfilling operations, periodic field density tests were performed in general 
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D2922 (Nuclear Gauge Method).  Results of the 
compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 4.1 and 4.2 of this report.  These tests 
were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed backfills at the site.  
Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on-site survey 
stakes.  These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree 
implied by the methods used.  Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the 
specified minimum compaction was obtained and/or approved by our field representative 
based on visual observation and hand probing. 
Our services consisted of part-time field observation of the contractor’s backfill 
placement, subgrade preparation, and compaction procedures during the above grading 
operations. 
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the referenced 
tests, it is our opinion that the backfill installation for the contaminated material excavations for 
the Kahala Marketplace Development project, observed and tested by Geolabs in the period 
covered by this report, were performed in conformance with the general intent of the following

LIMITATIONS 
Geolabs assumes no responsibility for alterations made to slopes or finished grades on 
the subject project subsequent to our observation and testing period without our knowledge 
and written acknowledgment.

observation.  However, we believe that the level of earthwork observation and testing services 
provided is reasonable in reducing the geotechnical risks to the project. It should be 
recognized that there are variations in the accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests 
and methods used to monitor the earthwork construction and materials. Our services were 
performed in accordance with generally accepted standards of geotechnical engineering

engineer is not insurance, nor does it guarantee construction of any type.  The contractor shall 
retain responsibility for the quality of the work, for adhering to the plans and specifications, 
and for repairing defects regardless of when they are found.
