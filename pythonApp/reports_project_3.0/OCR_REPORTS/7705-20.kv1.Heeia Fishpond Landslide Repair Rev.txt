HE’EIA FISHPOND LANDSLIDE REPAIR

Dear Mr. Harada: 
This letter report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations 
performed during the soil nail installation and testing for the He’eia Fishpond Landslide 
Repair project, located in Kaneohe on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. The project location 
and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map, Plate 1 and Site Plan, Plate 2.  
This letter report presents our field observations and the results of the soil nail 
installation and testing performed for the project from December 1, 2022 through 
January 13, 2023. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
He’eia Fishpond is located along the coast of Kaneohe, with He’eia State Park to 
the north and King Intermediate School to the south. The entrance to the fishpond is 
located on Ipuka Street at Paepae o He’eia. The landslide is located on a steep, heavily 
vegetated natural slope between Paepae o He’eia office and the nearby residential 
houses.  
It was desired to provide geotechnical observation and consultation services 
during the soil nailed slope construction. The purpose of our geotechnical engineering 
services was to observe the installation and testing of the soil nails for general 
conformance with the project plans and specifications. 
SOIL NAIL INSTALLATION AND TESTING 
Verification and production soil nail installation was performed by HI-TECH 
Rockfall Construction, Inc. One pre-production soil nail, identified as TP1, was installed 
for the project. In addition, a total of 45 production soil nails were installed at the project 
Wilson Okamoto Corporation

site. Approximate locations of the installed soil nails are illustrated on the Soil Nail 
Location Plan, Plate 3. 
A Geolabs representative observed the drilling and grouting operations during the 
installation of the soil nails (anchors). The soil nails were drilled using a Caterpillar mini 
excavator equipped with a TEI Rock Drill and a 4-inch diameter down-the-hole hammer 
bit. 
After the drilled holes were extended to the specified lengths, soil cuttings were 
removed from the boreholes using pressurized air. A threaded epoxy-coated No. 10 
reinforcing bar encased in MCP corrugated sheathing and equipped with centralizers was 
then inserted into the hole. In general, the centralizers were placed at about 2 feet from 
the top and 2 feet from the bottom of the reinforcing bar. 
The grout was batched on-site utilizing a colloidal high-shear and high-speed grout 
mixer equipped with a grout pump. Grouting began progressively from the bottom to the 
top of the drilled hole through a tremie tube until fresh grout appeared at the top of the 
hole. The soil nail installation and testing records are summarized in the Summary of Soil 
Nail Installation and Testing, Plates 4.1 through 4.3.  
Prior to the production soil nail installation, one verification test was performed on 
a sacrificial, pre-production soil nail, designated as TP1, in accordance with the project 
documents. Proof tests were selected and performed on five production soil nails, 
designated as Soil Nails C3R3, C6R3, C3R4, C5R5, and C9R5. In general, the 
verification test anchor and the proof test anchors were tested to about 200 percent and 
150 percent of the design pull-out capacity, respectively. The soil nail head movement at 
each load increment up to the maximum test load was measured and recorded. Details 
of verification and proof testing conducted on the selected soil nail anchors are presented 
in Appendices A and B, respectively. 
Based on our field observations, we believe that the observed soil nail installation 
for the soil nailed slope for the He’eia Fishpond Landslide Repair project have been 
installed in general conformance with the project plans and specifications and within the 
limits of generally accepted construction practices. Based on our observations of the soil 
nail installation, it is our professional opinion that the soil nails observed in this report 
have been installed to sufficient depths to provide the design load capacity. 
LIMITATIONS 
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities 
occurring simultaneously on a construction site; therefore, some of the work for the soil 
nail installation and testing may have occurred without our direct observation.
