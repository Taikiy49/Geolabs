INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this investigation was to evaluate the sub
surface conditions at the site to provide recommendations
for the design and construction of the foundations of the
proposed structure
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P 2
SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed site is located between Kalewa Street and Lele
Street about 200 feet east of Lagoon Drive
The site is bordered by a one story structure to the south
and an empty lot on the north
The area is quite level and covered with sparse grass and
weeds
The site was vacant at the time of the field exploration
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
The proposed building will be a one story steel framed
structure with a slab on grade
A C parking areas are planned for the eastern and southern
portions of the site
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Subsurface Exploration
Five borings were drilled at the site to depths of 10 to
15 feet below the existing ground surface to explore the
subsurface conditions and to obtain samples for laboratory
testing The approximate locations of the borings are
shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P 3
Detailed information on the field exploration and labora
tory testing is covered in the appendices along with the
boring logs and laboratory test results
Soil Conditions
Based on the borings the site appears to be underlain by
a surface layer about 3 to 6 feet thick of fill material
consisting of silty clay gravel and sand Below this a
layer of fine sand and coral was encountered down to about
8 feet A layer of soft clay was encountered at about 8 to
10 foot depths over silty sand and coral to 16 5 feet the
maximum depth drilled
The water level was encountered at about 7 to 7 5 foot depths
in the borings
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed fairly competent bearing
soil at the site
Due to the variable nature of the upper fill layer proof
rolling of the building site is recommended to create a
uniform bearing layer for the proposed structure
The proofrolling should be performed with a 10 ton vibra
tory roller A minimum of 6 passes iS recommended
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P 4
Foundations
After proofrolling the building site spread or continuous
footing foundations are recommended An allowable bearing
value of 2 500 p s f is recommended for footings resting
on the stiff on site soils
The bottoms of footings should be placed about 2 feet below
the finish grade
The bottom of all footing excavations should be tamped to
obtain 95 compaction prior to the pouring of concrete
Surface water should be diverted away from the building
foundations
For the above recommendations it is estimated that settle
tial
If a footing is located next to a utility trench the
bottom of footing should extend to the bottom of the
trench
Slab On Grade
Slab on grade should be placed over a 4 inch layer of
base course generally less than 3 4 inch and larger than
1 4 inch in size The subgrade should be recompacted prior
to placing the base course
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P 5
To protect against moisture damage of the flooring material
a polyethylene moisture barrier should be used
Parking Area
An asphaltic concrete parking area is planned for the pro
ject
Based on the laboratory CBR tests on the near surface coral
sand samples the preliminary pavement design is as follows
2 inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 inch Base Course
8 inch Total Pavement Thickness
Additional CBR tests should be performed if different materials
are encountered at the subgrade level
The subgrade should be recompacted to obtain 95 of maximum
dry density established by ASTM D 1557 prior to placing the
base course layer
Design Review
The plans for foundations site grading and utility con
struction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for
review and comments prior to construction to see that
the intent of our recommendations is included in the
project plans and specifications
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P 6
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during
the proofrolling operations
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be
observed by a soils engineer during construction to see
that adequate bearing has been obtained and that the sub
surface conditions are consistent with the findings of our
subsurface investigation
Any imported structural fill material required should be
non expansive tested and approved by the soils engineer
prior to hauling to the site

APPENDIX _ A
FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored
by drilling 5 borings to depths of 10 to 16 5 feet below
the existing ground surface The borings were drilled with
a truck mounted drill rig The approximate locations of the
borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
The soils encountered in the borings were classified
by visual and textural examination in the field by our geo
logist who continuously monitored the drilling operations
The classifications were reviewed by visual inspection and
testing in the laboratory The soils were classified in
general conformance with the Unified Soil Classification
System A graphic presentation of the soils is presented
on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 5
In the borings soil samples were obtained by driving
split barrel sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30
inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the last
12 inches are shown on the boring logs at the appropriate
depths

LABORATORY TESTING
Moisture content sive analyses and unit weight
determinations were performed on selected samples as an
aid in the classification and evaluation of the soil pro
perties The results of the moisture content and unit
weight determinations are presented on the boring logs at
the appropriate sample depths The results of the sieve
analyses are presented on Table 1 Plate B 1l

the shear strength of the underlying soils The test
results are presented on Plate B 2 and B 3

from the soft clay layer to evaluate the consolidation
properties of the layer The consolidation curve is
presented on Plate B 4
Two laboratory CBR tests were performed on the near
surface soil to evaluate its suitability as pavement support
The test results are presented on Table 1 Plate B 1l
