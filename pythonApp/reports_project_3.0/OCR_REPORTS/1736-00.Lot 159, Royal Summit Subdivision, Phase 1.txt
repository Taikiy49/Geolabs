In accordance with your request we have made a study of the near
surface soil conditions at the above referenced lot in order to provide
foundation recommendations for the proposed residence The study
consisted of drilling three shallow borings performing laboratory
testing and reviewing the subdivision site grading and preliminary house
foundation plans This report summarizes our findings and
recommendations

SUBSURFACE SOTL CONDITIONS
Three borings were drilled within Lot 159 to depths ranging from
5 0 to 10 0 feet below the existing grade at the approximate locations
shown on the Site Plan Plate l
In general the borings encountered very stiff reddish brown clayey
silt to the full depth of our borings A review of the mass grading
plan for Royal Summit Phase 1 dated prepared by
Community Planning Inc indicated that lot 159 received up to 10 feet
of compacted fills over the natural grade
No groundwater was encountered at the time of our field exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The very stiff reddish brown clayey silt soil encountered will
generally be suitable for foundation support of the proposed house
provided that some care is exercised during construction Retaining
wall and structure footings located on and near slope area would require
special footing embedment
Laboratory tests performed on the very stiff clayey silt soil
indicates that this material generally has low swelling potential if
the moisture of the soil is maintained over 30 Based on the findings
from our field exploration the present moisture content is quite dry
ranging from 22 to 27 Thus the swelling potential could increase to
a moderate range Therefore to minimize the swelling potential the
subgrade soil should be moistened and protected from drying from the
start of construction until final concrete placement
Select non expansive soils such as select borrow crusher waste or
coral should be used for fill below the slab and backfill behind the
retaining walls to minimize the swelling potential of the natural soil

House Foundations
Spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the proposed
residence An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f may be used on the
compacted soil
Interior footings on level areas should be embedded a minimum of 12
inches below the finished grade Perimeter wall footings should
embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the adjacent finished grade to
provide a barrier against future moisture infiltration under the slab
If located on a sloping area the footing should be embedded down
an additional amount to obtain a 8 foot minimum horizontal set back
distance measured from the outer edge of the footing out to the slope
face This additional embedment depth is required to help resist the
downhill slope creep movement and to develop the recommended bearing
capacity of the soil
The north and northwestern sections of the house partial basement
are located about 1 0 to 3 5 feet from the existing sewer easements To
minimize the possibility of footing settlement due to settlement of the
trench backfill or due to sewer trench excavation at a later date for
repair purposes we recommend that the footings in this area be
the easement
Slab On Grade
The laboratory swell tests indicate that the on site soils have low
swelling potential if they are maintained at or above the optimum
moisture content 30 During the field exploration the near surface
soils were found to be drier than optimum moisture content To reduce
the soil swelling potential the subgrade soils should be wetted kept
moist and not allowed to dry out from the start of construction until
the placement of concrete This would require daily sprinkling of the
subgrade soils

It is recommended that slabs on grade be placed over a 6 inch layer
of well graded base course aggregate as a cushion layer and should be

A plastic membrane should be placed over the cushion layer if a
moisture barrier is deemed necessary to protect the floor covering
Lot Grading
Structural fills and backfills should consist of non expansive
soil such as select borrow or crusher waste and be placed in maximum
8 inch loose lifts and then compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum
dry density established by ASTM D 1557 78 The on site soils in the
Royal Summit area generally have low to moderate swelling potential and
would require special preparation prior to use as structural fill
Therefore the on site soil should only be used for yard fill
Prior to construction the area to receive any fill should be
cleared of vegetation and debris
The fills should be placed in horizontal lifts not exceeding 6
inches in compacted thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its
maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78
The site should be so graded to provide drainage of surface runoff
away from all foundations

Drainage
The finish lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings should
be shaped to shed water away from the foundations and to avoid ponding
conditions Also it is advised that gutter systems should be installed
around the house and that gutter water should be diverted far away from
the foundations and walkways Excessive landscape watering near the
foundations and slab should also be avoided
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper
performance of the above recommended foundations since ponded water
could cause excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving
or loss of soil strength The foundation excavations should be properly
backfilled tight against the wall or slab footing immediately after
setting of the concrete to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained
to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundation
