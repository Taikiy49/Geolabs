In accordance with our fee proposal dated we have observed the
cavity probe drilling and grouting operations for the above referenced project from
until This letter report summarizes our field observations
pertaining to the probing and grouting operations performed at the subject site
The project involves the construction of a two story Special Services Building at a
relocated site as part of the Kona Hospital Renovation and Expansion Phase II project
The intent of the cavity probe drilling and grouting operations is to attempt to locate and
fill subsurface cavities beneath the foundation of the proposed building
The cavity probe drilling was performed by Isemoto Contracting Co Ltd using an
air track rotary drill rig with 3 inch diameter bit A total of 106 probe holes and
approximately 1 203 lineal feet of probe drilling was performed during the cavity probe
drilling operation In general the probe holes were drilled at proposed column footing
locations and or spaced at about 10 feet on centers along continuous footings Probe
holes were drilled to depths ranging from 8 to 17 feet which correspond to a depth of

Materials encountered during the probe drilling generally consisted of volcanic ash
and clinker materials overlying hard basalt rock formation at greater depths Based on
our observation of the probing operations it appeared that cavities and or voids were
encountered at Probe Hole and 95 Additional probe holes were drilled at
closer spacing at these locations to delineate the vertical and lateral extent of the cavities
The drilled probe holes were subsequently backfilled with approximately 37 5 cubic yards
of sand cement grout mixes During grouting operations each probe hole was grouted
from the desired depth upwards with low to moderate injection Grouting operations were
performed to reduce the potential loss of footing bearing capacity due to cavities and
provide additional support to the footings Results of our cavity probe drilling and grouting
observations are summarized on Plates A 1 through A 4 of this report
In summary the cavity probe drilling and grouting operations were conducted in
general conformance with the intent of the specifications and our geotechnical
engineering report Based on our observations we anticipate that the footing subgrade
should provide suitable foundation support for the proposed building
