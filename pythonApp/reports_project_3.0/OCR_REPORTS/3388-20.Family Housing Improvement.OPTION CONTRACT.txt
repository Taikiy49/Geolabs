INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed family housing improvement project within Hickam Air Force
Base in Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of
the site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering recommenda
tions resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering analyses
These recommendations are intended for the design of residential housing foundations
site preparation and pavements The findings and recommendations presented herein
are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The geologic conditions underlying the site are the result of a complex series of
deposition and erosional events brought on by glacio eustatic sea level changes and
post erosional volcanic activities during the Pleistocene Epoch Following the cessation
of activity of the Koolau Volcano alluvial materials were deposited in a deltaic or
estuarine environment in the area of Pearl Harbor
The sea level fluctuated several times receding perhaps 300 feet below and
rising about 95 feet above the present sea level Concurrent with a rise in sea level
during the late Pleistocene Epoch eruption of the Salt Lake Makalapa member of the
Honolulu Volcanic Series mantled the older alluvium with thick deposits of volcanic ash
which consolidated to form volcanic tuff locally called mudrock layers Subsequent
erosion dissected and thinned those mantles of tuff
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3388 20 Page 6
During the recent epoch as sea level rose to its current stand the area was
covered with soft and loose lagoonal marine deposits These deposits were
subsequently covered by man made fill placed during the middle portion of this century
for land reclamation projects during construction of the existing Hickam Air Force Base

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our field exploration consisted of drilling and sampling nine borings designated
as Boring Nos 1 through 9 at the proposed project site to depths ranging from about
8 to 16 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate locations of the
borings are shown on the Site Plans Plates 2 and 3
Our field exploration generally encountered a surficial layer of fill material
approximately 3 to 6 feet thick consisting of stiff clayey soils and medium dense coral
sands and gravels Except for Boring No 7 volcanic tuff formation locally called
mudrock was encountered below the surficial fill layer at depths ranging from about
3 to 6 feet below the existing ground surface in all of the borings drilled and extended

surface In Boring No 7 the surficial fill layer was underlain by a coralline deposit of

of about 16 feet below the existing ground surface
Groundwater was not encountered in the drilled borings at the time of our field

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Our field exploration indicates that the project site is generally underlain by a
surficial fill layer of stiff clayey soils and medium dense coralline materials Based on
the subsoil conditions encountered at the project site it is our opinion that a shallow
foundation system bearing on the stiff medium dense near surface fills may be used to
provide support for the proposed residential additions However the near surface
clayey soils exhibit highly variable shrink swell characteristics ranging from slight to very
high expansion potential when subjected to fluctuations in the soil moisture contents
As a result we recommend that the near surface soils be capped with a minimum of
12 inches of non expansive select capping fill material below all building slabs within
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3388 20 Page 8
Areas A C and D The non expansive capping fill should be increased to a minimum
of 24 inches for slabs on grade located within Area B In addition special attention
should be given to the recommended procedures for subgrade preparation of slabs and
pavements to reduce the potential shrink swell effects of the near surface soils Our
laboratory test results indicate that highly expansive near surface soils were
encountered within Area B Therefore additional near surface soils may be obtained
in Area B to perform swell tests to delineate the areas that are underlain by the highly
expansive clayey soils in an attempt to reduce the cost of over excavation and
replacement
Our geotechnical engineering recommendations pertaining to the design of

Foundations
Based on the generally competent subsoil conditions encountered at the site we
believe that shallow spread and or continuous footings may be used to support the
proposed new residential expansion An allowable bearing pressure of 2 500 p s f may
be used for design of footing foundations bearing on the stiff fmedium dense soils
encountered at shallow depths This bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may
be increased by one third for transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic
forces
Footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest
adjacent finish grade If foundations are located next to utility trenches or easements
they should be embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary
plane extending upward from the bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep as the
inverts of the utility lines We estimate the footing settlements under the anticipated
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 3388 20 Page 9
design loads for footings bearing on the stiff fmedium dense soils to be less than 1 inch
total and inch differential
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive earth pressure
acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system and by frictional
resistance developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid
pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth p c f This assumes that
the soil around the footings is well compacted or undisturbed Unless covered by
pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the upper 12 inches of soil should be
neglected A coefficient of friction of 0 3 may be used for footings bearing on the
stifffmedium dense near surface soils
It is recommended that the bottom of all footings bear on the stiff medium dense
near surface soils If soft and or loose materials are encountered at the bottom of
footing excavations they should be over excavated to expose firm materials The over
excavation should either be backfilled with granular material compacted to a minimum
of 90 percent relative compaction or the footing bottom be extended down to the
underlying competent material Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density
of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil
established in accordance with procedures described in ASTM Test Designation
D 1557 91
We recommend that footing excavations be observed by a representative of
Geolabs Hawaii prior to the placement of reinforcing steel or concrete to confirm the
foundation bearing conditions and the required embedment depths

Slab On Grade
Our field exploration disclosed that the project site is generally underlain by near
surface clayey silt and silty clay soils with swelling potential ranging from slight to high
when subjected to moisture fluctuations Unless floor slabs constructed above the
highly expansive soils are properly designed there is a potential for future distress to
the lightly loaded slabs on grade resulting from the shrinking and swelling of the
expansive clayey soils due to changes in the moisture content To reduce the potential
for structural distress resulting from the swelling of the expansive subgrade soils we
recommend that the subgrade soils be properly prepared and capped with a layer of
non expansive select granular fill material for support of slabs on grade The non
expansive capping fill should extend laterally at least 2 feet beyond the perimeter of the
proposed structures and pavement areas The upper 6 inches of capping fill outside
the limits of the house structure should consist of non expansive impervious material
to reduce water infiltration into the underlying select granular fill material as shown on
Plate 4
Based on the laboratory swell test results it is our opinion that all slabs on
grades within Areas A C and D should be supported on a minimum of 12 inches of
imported non expansive select granular fill material For slabs on grade located within
Area B it is our opinion that the non expansive capping fill should be increased to a
minimum of 24 inches
The non expansive capping fill should be placed as soon as possible after
scarification moisture conditioning and compaction of the underlying clayey subgrade
soils Subgrade soils should be prepared in accordance with the recommendations
presented in the Site Preparation section of this report The non expansive capping
fill and the underlying subgrade soil should be kept moist prior to final placement of
concrete The non expansive select granular fill material should provide a capping
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3388 20 Page 11
layer of moist soil above the underlying potentially expansive subgrade soil thereby
reducing the loss of moisture from the clayey subgrade soils We believe that the
reduction in potential drying and wetting of the subgrade soils should reduce the impact
on the slab due to potential heaving of the underlying expansive clayey soils when
wetted
To reduce the potential for drying of the subgrade soils and to reduce the costs
of form construction we recommend that the slab edges be cast neat against the
soils The slab edges should incorporate a sufficient amount of top and bottom
longitudinal steel reinforcement The top and bottom steel reinforcement bars should
be connected by stir ups The ends of the stir ups should extend into the floor slab
area and should be tied to the welded wire mesh A structural engineer should be
consulted for design details of this thickened edge which is intended to function both
as a moisture barrier and as a perimeter wall footing The welded mesh in the slab on
grade should be embedded at the proper height in the concrete to provide adequate
slab reinforcement Blocks or saddles may be used to raise the wire mesh A stiff
grade of wire mesh is thus recommended A typical detail showing the slab on grade
design on expansive soil is presented on Plate 4
For more uniform support of the slab we recommend that a minimum 4 inch
thick layer of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation cushion layer be
placed below the slab To reduce the potential for future moisture infiltration and
subsequent damage to the floor coverings a moisture vapor barrier such as a plastic
membrane is recommended on top of the gravel cushion layer Two inches of moist
fine sand should be placed above the plastic membrane to provide protection of the
membrane and to aid in curing of the slab concrete The thickened edges of slabs
adjacent to any unpaved area should be embedded at least 12 inches below the lowest
adjacent grade
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 3388 20 Page 12
Flexible floor coverings such as carpet or sheet vinyl should be considered
since they can better mask any minor slab cracking It is also recommended that
interior walls be designed to incorporate some flexibility to accommodate small amount
of possible ground movements
Where the slab will be subjected to vehicular traffic such as the driveways and
garage slabs a 6 inch layer of base course is recommended in lieu of the 4 inch
cushion fill layer vapor barrier and 2 inch sand The base course should consist of
crushed basalt aggregate compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
The 12 to 24 inch capping layer recommended above for slabs on grade construction
should also be provided for driveways and garage slabs
Exterior concrete walkways may be required for the proposed project We
recommend that a minimum 4 inch thick cushion layer of No 3B Fine gravel be
provided below the concrete slab Construction joints should be provided at intervals
equal to the width of the walkways with expansion joints at right angle intersections
Site Preparation
Based on the existing topography and the proposed finish grades minimal site
grading work is anticipated for the project construction At the on set of earthwork the
area within the contract grading limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed All
vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable materials should be removed and
disposed of off site
Slabs and foundations of the existing structures that are to be demolished should
be completely removed Any over excavation resulting from the demolition should be
backfilled with compacted granular fill material Existing utilities to be abandoned
should be removed and the resulting excavation should be properly backfilled with
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3388 20 Page 13
select granular fill material placed in 8 inch loose lifts and compacted Utilities that are
to be abandoned in place under the proposed structure should be backfilled by
pumping lean concrete under low pressure
After demolition clearing and grubbing all areas beneath future slabs and
pavement areas should be over excavated to a depth of 12 inches Areas A C and D
and 24 inches Area B below the bottom of the gravel cushion layer or the base course
layer and replaced with compacted non expansive select granular fill material If zones
of soft loose or saturated soils are encountered at the over excavated subgrade level
deeper over excavation may be required to expose the underlying firm material The
resulting over excavation should be backfilled with compacted non expansive select
granular material
The over excavated subgrade surface should be scarified to a minimum depth
of 8 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above optimum moisture content
and compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction
refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry
density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 91 test
procedures Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight
corresponding to the maximum dry density
Where shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of the subgrade we
recommend that the soil be thoroughly moistened to close all cracks or scarified
moisture conditioned and recompacted Saturation and subsequent yielding of the
exposed subgrade due to inclement weather and poor drainage may require over
excavation of the soft areas and replacement with well compacted engineered fill
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3388 20 Page 14
Non expansive select granular fill material required for the capping layer should
be placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness moisture conditioned
and compacted to not less than 95 percent relative compaction under slab on grade
and paved areas
Non expansive select granular material required under the slabs and pavement
areas should consist of imported granular material such as select borrow crushed coral
or basalt gravel The material should be well graded from coarse to fine with no
particles greater than 3 inches in largest dimension The material should have a
laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR value of 20 or higher and a swell potential
of not more than 1 percent when tested in accordance with ASTM Test Designation
D 1883 It should also contain less than 20 percent passing the No 200 sieve Any
imported fill material should be tested and approved by Geolabs Hawaii prior to delivery
to the project site for its intended use

Utility Trench
We envision that new utility lines may be required for the project A granular
bedding consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation
is recommended under the pipes Free draining granular materials such as No 3B
Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation should also be used for the trench backfill
up to about 12 inches above the pipes to provide adequate support around the pipes
and to reduce the compaction effort of the backfill thus reducing the possibility of
damaging the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipes
to the top of the subgrade or finished grade should consist of the on site soils or select
granular material The backfill should be placed in about 8 inch loose lifts and
mechanically compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the
potential for future ground subsidence Where trenches are below pavement areas the
upper 2 feet of the trench backfill below the pavement subgrade should be compacted
to not less than 95 percent relative compaction
Drainage
The finished grades outside the buildings should be sloped to shed water away
from foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding Also it is advised that gutter
systems be installed around the building additions and that the discharge be diverted
away from the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3388 20 Page 17
the foundations and slabs should also be avoided Planters next to foundations should
be avoided or have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the
subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil
saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations
should be properly backfilled against the walls or slab footings immediately after setting
of the concrete to reduce water infiltration Drainage swales should be provided as
soon as possible and should be maintained to drain all surface run off away from the
slab and foundations

Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling nine
borings to depths between 8 and 16 feet below the existing ground surface at the
approximate locations shown on the Site Plans Plates 2 and 3 The borings were
drilled using a drill rig equipped with continuous flight augers
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our field geologist who observed the drilling operations on
a near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed by visual
observation and testing in the laboratory All soils were classified in general
conformance with the Unified Soil Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic
representation of the materials encountered is presented on the Logs of Borings Plates
A 1 through A 9
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils
Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow
counts needed to drive the sampler the last 12 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown
on the logs of borings at the appropriate sample depths
