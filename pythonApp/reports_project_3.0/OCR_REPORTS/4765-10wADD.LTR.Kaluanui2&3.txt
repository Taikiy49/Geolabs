This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering services performed during
the mass grading operations building pad preparation utility installation and pavement
construction for the Kaluanui Multi Family also known as Nanea Kai development from
May 16 2002 through March 9 2004 in general accordance with our fee proposal dated
May 17 2002 and our additional budget requests dated April 22 and
EARTHWORK OPERATION
The project site is located north of Hawaii Kai Drive between about Keahole Place
and Wailua Street in the Hawaii Kai area of eastern Honolulu on the Island of Oahu
Hawaii The project site is generally bounded by the United States Post Office Hawaii
Kai Station to the northeast Hawaii Kai Drive along the south and southeast the Hale Alii
at Hawaii Kai senior living development to the west and undeveloped land to the north as
shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
The multi family housing development project consists of six buildings Buildings
1 through 6 comprising a total of 87 units The buildings are three stories high and
constructed of light gauge metal framing with wood trusses and asphalt shingle roofing
Buildings 3 through 6 are located on relatively level grounds alongside Hawaii Kai Drive
Minimal site grading work was performed for the construction of the new buildings in this

The site grading work and infrastructure installation were performed by Royal
Contracting Company The earthwork operations consisted of clearing and grubbing of
the existing ground over excavation of the surface expansive clays preparation of slab
and pavement subgrades placement and compaction of non expansive capping fill select
borrow and base course and utility trench excavation and backfill It should be noted that
field observations of the building foundation excavation and preparation work were
performed by others and were specifically excluded from our scope of work for this project
Following the clearing and grubbing operations the existing ground at Buildings 3
through 6 was generally over excavated where necessary to provide 12 inches of
non expansive select granular fill material capping fill at the building pad footprints and
extending 2 feet beyond The 12 inch non expansive select granular capping fill was
increased to 3 feet for Buildings 1 and 2 due to the presence of highly expansive adobe
clay soils encountered at the slab subgrade level In general the over excavated
subgrades were moisture conditioned to about 2 percent above the optimum moisture
content and compacted to at least 90 percent of the maximum dry density During
placement of the non expansive select granular fill layer the fill materials were generally
placed in near level lifts brought to above the optimum moisture content and compacted
to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density The maximum dry density of the
materials referred to in this report were determined in accordance with American Society
of Testing and Material ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor
The existing ground at the motor courts for Buildings 3 through 6 was generally
over excavated where necessary to provide 6 inches of base course over 12 inches of
non expansive select granular fill material The motor court slabs for Buildings 1 and 2
were constructed directly over the 3 foot non expansive select granular capping layer In
addition the access road was generally over excavated where necessary to
approximately 6 to 36 inches below the base course layer for placement of the select
borrow subbase material in accordance with our recommendations The over excavated
subgrades at the pavement areas were moisture conditioned to about 2 percent above the
optimum moisture content and compacted to at least 95 percent relative compaction The
select borrow subbase and base course were generally placed in near level lifts brought
to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent
relative compaction
Based on our field observations it appeared that expansive soils such as
adobe clays were encountered at the footing subgrade elevation along some portion of
the CRM walls planned at the site Where highly expansive adobe clays gray color
were encountered at the footing subgrade elevation the adobe clays were removed a
mininum of 2 feet and replaced with compacted granular materials The

New sewer water and drain lines as well as catch basins and manholes were
installed to serve the new residential development In general a bedding layer
consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel was provided below the piping and
manholes for uniform support Based on our field observations soft and wet soils were
encountered below the bedding layer along the utility lines and manhole structures at
the project site A stabilization layer consisting of 18 to 24 inches of No 3B Fine gravel
wrapped in a non woven filter fabric Mirafi 18ON was provided below the bedding layer
for more uniform support Free draining granular materials consisting of No 3B Fine
gravel was also used for the trench backfill up to about 12 inches above the pipes to
provide adequate support around the pipes and to reduce the compaction effort on the
initial trench backfill The trench backfill was placed in 8 inch level loose lifts and
mechanically compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction Where
trenches are below pavement areas the upper 3 feet of the trench backfill below the
pavement grade was compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
During the earthwork operation samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor
and the test results are summarized on Plates 2 1 and 2 2 of this report
Periodic field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations to
confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures and soil conditions
were noted A total of 456 field density tests including retests of failed areas were
performed using the Nuclear Probe Method in accordance with ASTM Test Designation
D 2922 The results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 3 1
through 3 25 of this report These tests were taken on the prepared ground surfaces and
on constructed engineered fills and backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were
estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes and consiruction plans
These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied
by the methods used Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the
specified minimum compaction was obtained
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the earthwork operations for the above project
observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report were performed in
general accordance with the intent of the project plans and specifications our geotechnical
engineering recommendations presented in the referenced report dated
