In accordance with your request we have prepared this letter report to summarize
our work on the project and present our findings and geotechnical recommendations for

Project Considerations
The project site is located to the north of the intersection between Kahuna Road
and Kawaihau Road in the Kapaa area on the Island of Kauai Hawaii The general
location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
Based on the information provided the project consists of the construction of the pump
control building connecting pipelines and an access road We understand that the control
building will be a one story structure consisting of concrete masonry units CMU The
access road will consist of both asphaltic and Portland cement concrete pavements
We understand that high moisture soils were encountered during the trench
excavation and subsequent backfilling operations at the project site Because aeration of
the high moisture soils was not practical in the high rainfall environment at the site
compaction of the trench backfills to the specified requirement was very difficult Several
compaction tests initial and re test were performed on the trench backfill with high in situ
moisture contents Based on the compaction test results the compaction requirement of
90 and 95 percent relative compaction was difficult to attain in the majority of the
compaction tests due to the high in situ moisture contents of the trench backfill
We understand that some alternative methods for compaction requirements and or
increase in the pavement structural section based on the on site soils with high in situ
moisture contents may be considered for the project

Purpose and Scope
The purpose of our site reconnaissance was to obtain an overview of the surface
and the shallow subsurface conditions at the project site The shallow subsurface
information obtained was used to formulate geotechnical recommendations pertaining to
the re design of pavements and modification of compaction requirements for the subject
project Our work was performed in general accordance with our fee proposal dated
Our scope of work included the following tasks and work efforts
1 Review of the construction plans provided by Okahara Associates Inc
2 Reconnaissance of the project site by an engineer from our firm
3 Laboratory testing of selected bulk samples obtained during the site
reconnaissance to evaluate the engineering properties of the on site soils
4 Analyses of the field and laboratory data for the formulation of geotechnical
recommendations pertaining to the compaction of the wet soils encountered
at the project site
5 Preparation of a letter report summarizing our work on the project and
presenting our findings and recommendations
6 Quality assurance of our work and client design team consultation by a
principal engineer from our firm
Site Reconnaissance
A site reconnaissance of the project site was performed on by an
engineer from our office The purpose of the site reconnaissance was to observe the
conditions of the on site soils along the access road The weather conditions during the
site visit were generally sunny with some passing light showers
During our site reconnaissance it was observed that the new control building and
retaining structures had already been constructed The near surface soils along the new
access road and near the control building consisted of brown to reddish brown silty clays
with varying amounts of basaltic cobbles and gravel The near surface silty clays were
generally stiff in consistency The near surface silty clays were generally in a very moist to
wet condition At the time of our site reconnaissance we observed some low spots with
standing water along the access road Basaltic gravel was observed along the access
road We understand that basaltic gravel was placed to provide a more stable base for the
construction vehicular traffic

Laboratory Testing
Soil samples of the near surface silty clay soils obtained along the new access road
during our site reconnaissance of the project site were tested in the laboratory with an
emphasis on evaluating the moisture and pavement support characteristics The following
laboratory tests were performed on the near surface silty clay soils obtained from our site
reconnaissance
e Moisture content was performed in accordance with ASTM D 2216 on the
soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties
One laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR test was performed in
accordance with ASTM D 1883 on the soil samples to evaluate the
pavement support characteristics of the soils The results of the test are
presented on Plate 2
One Modified Proctor compaction test was performed in accordance with
ASTM D 1557 on the bulk soil sample to determine the dry density and
moisture content relationship Results of the test are presented on Plate 3
The results of our laboratory tests indicate that the near surface silty clay soils
along the access road have in situ moisture contents of about 47 percent The CBR test
was performed on the near surface silty clay soils molded to near the in situ moisture
content 47 percent to determine the pavement support characteristics of the silty clay
soils Based on the results of the CBR test the CBR value of the silty clay soils with the
molding moisture near the in situ moisture content is 3 1 with an expansion value of only
0 04 percent
Discussions and Recommendations
As mentioned previously wet soil conditions were encountered during the trench
excavation and subsequent backfilling operations at the project site Based on the
compaction test results the specified compaction requirement of 90 and 95 percent
relative compaction was difficult to attain in the majority of the compaction tests due to the
wet soil conditions of the trench backfill and the high rainfall environment at the site
During our site reconnaissance stiff silty clays in a very moist to wet condition were
observed along the new access road and around the control building area
Our laboratory tests indicate that the near surface silty clay soils have in situ
moisture contents of about 47 percent Because of the difficulty in obtaining the specified
compaction requirements for trench backfill and pavement subgrade preparation due to
the wet soil conditions we believe that modifications to the compaction requirements and
re design of the pavement structural sections is warranted Detailed discussion of our
geotechnical recommendations for the re design of the access road pavements and

Pavement Design
We anticipate that the vehicle loading for the pavement areas will consist
primarily of passenger vehicles and light utility trucks We have made our preliminary
pavement design assuming the pavement subgrade soil will be similar to the clayey
soils encountered during our site reconnaissance and represented by the CBR test
Based on the above assumptions we recommend that the following pavement sections
be used for design purposes
Asphaltic Concrete Pavement
2 0 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 0 Inch Aggregate Base Course 95 Percent Relative Compaction
8 0 Inch Select Borrow Subbase 95 Percent Relative Compaction
6 0 Inch Select Borrow Subbase 90 Percent Relative Compaction
22 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness on Compacted Subgrade
Portland Cement Concrete Pavement
6 0 Inch Portland Cement Concrete
6 0 Inch Aggregate Base Course 95 Percent Relative Compaction
6 0 Inch Select Borrow Subbase 95 Percent Relative Compaction
6 0 Inch Select Borrow Subbase 90 Percent Relative Compaction
24 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness on Compacted Subgrade
Due to the high rainfall environment and the high in situ moisture contents of the
existing on site soils scarification of the subgrade soils at the project site is not
recommended and the current compaction requirement of 95 percent relative compaction
should be reduced Based on our laboratory test results and engineering analyses the
subgrade soils under the pavement areas should be proof rolled to provide a subgrade
with a minimum of 85 percent relative compaction Based on the available compaction
tests performed we believe that the 85 percent relative compaction may be achieved with
some difficulty
Because of the reduced subgrade compaction requirement and the high in situ
moisture content we believe that it would be difficult to obtain a compaction requirement of
at least 95 percent for the first layer of the select borrow subbase placed over the
pavement subgrades Therefore we recommend that the first layer of select borrow
subbase placed above the pavement subgrades be compacted to a minimum of
90 percent relative compaction The subsequent layers of aggregate subbase course
should be compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction

The aggregate base course and select borrow subbase should consist of crushed

actual subgrade soils at the in situ moisture content during construction to confirm that the
above design section is adequate In addition the recommended pavement section
assumes that good drainage will be provided for areas adjacent to the pavements
Paved areas should be sloped and drainage gradients should be maintained to
carry surface water off the site Surface water ponding should not be allowed on the site
during or after construction Where concrete curbs are used to isolate landscaping in or
adjacent to the pavement areas we recommend that the curbs be extended a minimum of
2 inches below the subgrade soils to reduce the potential for migration of excessive water
into the pavement section Alternatively a subdrain system is recommended to collect the
excess water from landscaping irrigation For long term performance we believe that a
subdrain system should be considered adjacent to paved landscaped areas
Utility Trench Backfill
As mentioned previously the contractor has experienced difficulties in obtaining the
specified compaction requirements for the trench backfill due to the wet soil conditions
Therefore we believe that the upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 1 foot
above the pipes to the finished subgrade may consist of the on site soils compacted to a
lesser degree The backfill material should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum
moisture content placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and
compacted to a minimum of 85 percent relative compaction
It should be noted that the reduced compaction requirement for the trench backfill
materials would result in some additional settlement of the trench backfill Therefore we
believe that the reduced compaction requirement for the trench backfill materials should be
limited to a total trench depth of 10 feet or less to reduce the potential for appreciable
ground subsidence due to the lower compaction trench backfill materials For the deeper
trenches we believe that the compaction requirement for the lower portion of the trench
backfill should be increased to 90 percent relative compaction In order to achieve the
higher compaction requirement imported backfill materials may be required Geolabs
should be contacted to provide supplemental recommendations for this condition if
appropriate
