This report presents the results of our pavement coring and logging services in
support of the PAVER Implementation Project for various U S Army installations on the
island of Oahu Hawaii
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our work on the project was to verify the thickness of existing
pavement sections at selected locations on the various installations These data will be
used by others in analyses of pavement performance and life
The scope of our work consisted of the following tasks and work efforts

FIELD EXPLORATION
The general locations of the core samples were selected by Kennedy Jenks
Consultants or their subcontractor Austin Research Engineers The general sample
locations are shown on the Site Plans Plates 1 through 9
The core drilling operations commenced on at Tripler Army
Medical Center and concluded on March 3 1993 at Fort Shafter There was a brief hiatus
in the field work while right of entry to aircraft operational areas at Wheeler Army Airfield

was resolved with U S Army Support Command Hawaii personnel and while the locations
for core sampling on Fort Shafter were being selected
The actual core locations were selected in the field by our Field Engineer or
Geologist as typical of the pavement surface conditions in the selected area Sample
locations were dimensionally tied to fixed features in the field such as building corners
electric poles or manholes These data were recorded on sketches of the locations and
are presented on Plates A 1 through A 75 All test locations were checked for
underground utilities using a pipe and cable locator prior to drilling Upon completion of
the drilling and sampling the core holes were patched with portland cement concrete
The cores were drilled using a truck mounted Mobile Drill model B 34 drilling rig
advancing a four inch 4 diameter diamond pavement coring bit The recovered cores
were measured for thickness Where multiple layers or overlays were observed the
thickness of the individual layers were recorded The bit was advanced to subgrade level
and the thickness of the base course was measured using a metal measuring tape Ifa
layer of subbase course was observed this was also measured and recorded The field
data is presented on Plates A 1 through A 75 The results are also tabulated and
summarized on Plates B 1 through B 5
