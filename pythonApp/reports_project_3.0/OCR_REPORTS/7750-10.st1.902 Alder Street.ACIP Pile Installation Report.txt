In accordance with our contract agreement, Geolabs observed the performance of 
one bi-directional load test, test pile installation, and production pile installation for the 
augered cast-in-place (ACIP) pile foundations for the 902 Alder Street project in Honolulu 
on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. This letter report summarizes our geotechnical observations 
during the ACIP pile foundation installation for the project. 
Geolabs provided geotechnical engineering consultation for the 902 Alder Street 
project, and the findings and recommendations are presented in the referenced 
geotechnical engineering report. The purpose of our geotechnical observations was to 
monitor the geotechnical engineering aspects of the pile foundation installation for 
conformance with the intent of the recommendations presented in our report. In addition, we 
provided “Special Inspection” services (as required by the City and County of Honolulu) by 
observing the ACIP pile foundation installation for general conformance with the project 
plans and specifications.

BI-DIRECTIONAL METHOD LOAD TEST 
As part of the project specifications, one sacrificial load test pile, designated as LT-1, 
was installed at the approximate location indicated on the ACIP Pile Identification Plan, 
Plate 1. LT-1 consisted of an 18-inch diameter ACIP pile. The load test pile was installed by 
HDCC on November 12, 2020. The load test pile was installed with a bi-directional load cell 
and embedment strain gauge instrumentation. 
A drill rig with a continuous hollow stem auger was utilized to install the ACIP test 
pile. A hydraulic concrete pump was used to pump the grout into the pile system. The 
18-inch diameter load test pile was extended to a depth of about 63 feet below the existing 
ground surface. 
After the load test pile was installed and cured for about seven days, GRL Engineers, 
Inc. performed the load test on November 19, 2020. The load test pile was loaded in general 
accordance with ASTM D1143, Quick Load Test Method to a maximum sustained 
bi-directional load of approximately 744 kips. Complete details of the load test program are 
presented in the Bi-Directional Static Load Test Report, dated November 27, 2020, 
submitted by GRL Engineers, Inc. 
Based on the results of the load tests conducted, we recommended utilizing the ACIP 
pile lengths shown in the above table for the production ACIP piles to support the allowable 
compressive loads (Design Capacities).

PRODUCTION PILE INSTALLATION 
The production ACIP pile installation program, consisting of installing 174 ACIP piles 
(including four-tower crane piles and one production pile that was replaced with a tower 
crane pile), was conducted from November 30, 2020 through December 19, 2020 under 
the near-continuous (full-time) observations by a field engineer from our office. Hawaiian 
Dredging Construction Company utilized a drill rig with continuous hollow stem augers to 
install the production piles. Automatic monitoring equipment was mounted on the 
drilling/pumping equipment with the intent of providing auger depth, grout flow rate, grout 
pressure, etc., on a near-continuous basis. 
During the pile installation operation, logs were maintained for each ACIP pile, which 
included information pertaining to pile number, date installed, depth of pile, grout volume 
used, grout return depth, etc. The locations of the production ACIP piles installed are shown 
on the ACIP Pile Numbering Plans, Plates 1.1 and 1.2. The production ACIP pile installation 
summary is presented on Plates 2.1 through 2.4 of this letter report. 
As with most foundation installation projects, some problems did arise during 
construction. The associated problems and the corresponding resolutions are presented in 
the “Remarks” column of the ACIP Pile Installation Summary. 
Based on our observation of the equipment performance and subsurface conditions 
encountered, it is our professional opinion that the production ACIP piles have been installed 
to sufficient depths and/or resistance to provide the allowable design load capacities.
