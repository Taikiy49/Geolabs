Dear Mr. Sato: 
This letter report summarizes our geotechnical observation services performed 
during the installation and testing of the soil nails for the Emergency Repair of Kahekili 
Highway Culvert at MP 8.4 project located in the Kahakuloa area on the Island of Maui, 
Hawaii. 
In general, this letter report presents our observations and the results of the soil 
nail installation and testing performed on the soil nails for the project from March 23 to 
May 8, 2020. Prometheus Construction was the general contractor and specialty 
subcontractor responsible for the installation and testing of the soil nails. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The Emergency Repair of Kahekili Highway Culvert at MP 8.4 project is located on 
Kahekili Highway at Mile Post (MP) 8.4 in the Kahakuloa area on the Island of Maui, 
Hawaii. A small landslide at MP 8.4 resulted in the partial undermining of the existing 
24-inch diameter pipe culvert that crosses under Kahekili Highway. To prevent further 
damage to the pipe culvert and instability to the existing CRM retaining wall and Kahekili 
Highway, a new soil nail retaining wall was designed to strengthen the failing slope 
surrounding the existing pipe culvert. 
The purpose of our geotechnical engineering services during construction of the 
project was to observe the construction and testing of the soil nails for general 
conformance with the project plans and specifications.

SOIL NAIL INSTALLATION AND TESTING 
As part of the project requirements, a reinforced shotcrete soil nail retaining wall 
system was designed, consisting of 13 individual reinforcing bars grouted into drilled holes 
to strengthen and stabilize the existing CRM wall. In addition, one pre-production test nail, 
designated as TN-1, was installed and tested prior to the production construction to examine 
the construction method and verify the soil nail pull-out resistance. A structural shotcrete 
layer was specified to be placed on the face of the existing CRM wall, in addition to an 
architectural (sculpture) shotcrete layer for aesthetic appearance. 
A 2-inch thick fiber reinforced shotcrete facing was constructed in front of the existing 
CRM wall prior to the soil nail installation. This fiber reinforced shotcrete facing was placed 
to provide a level working surface for drilling and testing of the soil nails. 
Pre-production and production soil nail installation and testing were performed by 
Prometheus Construction. The approximate locations of the soil nails and numbering 
system are shown on the Soil Nail Location Plan, Plate 1. Installation and testing of the soil 
nails were progressed from March 23 through April 17, 2020, with sub-sequenced sculpture 
shotcrete placement and final inspection walk-through. A summary of the soil nail installation 
and testing is presented on Plate 2. 
In addition, our daily field reports documenting the construction activities are also 
included in the Appendix for reference. 
Soil Nail Installation 
A Geolabs representative observed the drilling and grouting operations during 
construction of the soil nails. The soil nails were drilled using a TEI Platform Rock Drill 
mounted on a Komatsu PC 210 LC excavator. The TEI drill rig was equipped with a down 
the hole hammer and an 8-inch underreammer percussion bit. 
The pre-production soil nail, designated as TN-1, was installed to a depth of about 
35 feet on March 25, 2020. The production soil nail holes were drilled to depths of about 26 
to 27 feet from the front face of the initial fiber reinforced shotcrete facing with an inclination 
of about 20 degrees from perpendicular to the shotcrete facing. An 8-inch Outside Diameter 
(OD) temporary steel casing was installed to a depth of about 10 feet behind the existing 
CRM wall to maintain the stability of the drilled hole. After the hole was drilled to the specified 
depth, the prefabricated soil nail bar with the required double-corrosion protection was 
inserted into the drilled hole. Centralizers were placed along the soil nail at 10-foot intervals. 
A 3/4-inch diameter high-density plastic grouting tube was inserted into the drilled 
hole along the soil nail bar. Grouting began progressively from the bottom to top through the 
grouting tube until fresh grout appeared at the top of the borehole. The grouting tube was 
removed after the completion of the grouting operation. The temporary casing was 
withdrawn during the grouting process.

Pre-Production Soil Nail Testing 
In accordance with the project specifications, a verification test was performed on the 
pre-production test nail No. TN-1 on March 31, 2020. In general, the pre-production test nail 
was capable of maintaining at the maximum test load of about 75 kips, equivalent to about 
375 percent of the design load (20 kips), without pullout failure and excessive creeping 
movement. The head movements for the pre-production test nail at each load increment up 
to the maximum test load were measured and recorded. Results of the pre-production 
verification testing, including Load versus Displacement and the Creep Movement versus 
Time curves, are presented on Plates 4.1 through 4.5. 
Production Soil Nail Testing 
In accordance with the project specifications, the production soil nails are required to 
load tested to 150 percent of the design load (20 kips) and held for 60 minutes for verification 
testing and 10 or 60 minutes depending on creep movement for proof testing. In addition, 
the verification testing soil nail shall be continuously loaded to the maximum testing load of 
200 percent of the design load. The criteria for acceptance of the soil nails consist of the 
following: 
For the verification testing, a total creep movement of less than 0.08 inches 
per log cycle of time between the 6 and 60-minute readings is measured 
during creep testing and the creep rate is linear or decreasing throughout 
the creep test load hold period. 
For the proof testing, a total creep movement of less than 0.04 inches is 
measured between the 1 and 10 minute readings or a total creep movement 
of less than 0.08 inches is measured between the 6 and 60-minute readings 
and the creep rate is linear or decreasing throughout the creep test load 
hold period. 
Total measured movement at the maximum test load exceeds 80 percent 
of the theoretical elastic elongation of the test nail unbonded length. 
Pullout failure does not occur at the maximum test load. 
The proof testing was performed on the first installed soil nail (Soil Nail No. 1) on 
April 6, 2020. The soil nail was able to maintain at the maximum testing load of about 48 
kips without pullout failure and excessive creeping movement. Results of the proof testing, 
including Load versus Displacement and the Creep Movement versus Time curves, are 
presented on Plates 5.1 through 5.3.
