PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this soil and foundation investigation is to
explore the soil and groundwater conditions at the site in order to
provide data for use in the design of the foundations for the proposed
bridge structure Our work was performed in general accordance with
our proposal of
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests which
we used in the engineering analysis to formulate our conclusions and
recommendations are presented in the appendix of this report
The approximate boring locations are shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2 and the boring logs are presented in Appendix A
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
As part of the Kahului Airport Terminal Complex Expansion
Kalialinui Gulch will be realigned A new bridge structure will be
required where Keolani Place crosses the gulch to accommodate the
proposed realigned gulch and improvements to Keolani Place The
bridge structure will also accommodate the proposed additional access
lane from Keolani Place into Hemaloa Street
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O Page 3
The new bridge structure will be approximately 100 to 150 feet
wide by 100 feet long in plan dimension
Anticipated abutment dead loads are about 25 kips per foot
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located at the intersection of Keolani Place
and Hemaloa Street about one quarter mile southwest of the existing
Kahului Airport Terminal Complex The project area is bordered by
Hemaloa Street to the northeast Hertz Rent A Car base yard to the
northwest and undeveloped land parcels form the remaining boundaries
An existing drainageway crosses the site in an approximate
southeast to northwest direction The drainageway which is concrete
lined for a distance of 260 feet at the approach to Keolani Place is
about 8 feet deep and 40 feet wide
The existing 35 feet wide concrete bridge at Keolani Place spans
the drainageway a distance of about 40 feet
The project site is currently vacant with ground cover consisting
mainly of overgrown grass and other vegetation The area adjacent to
the southeast bridge abutment consists of lawn grass and many coconut
trees
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O Page 4
Several existing buried utility lines including a 36 inch storm
drain a telephone cable and two water lines measuring 8 and 16 inches
traverse the project site
The topography generally slopes down towards the north with
ground surface elevations varying from 10 to 20 feet
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions disclosed in our borings consisted of a
stiff brown silt overlying loose sand and gravel estuarine deposit
to a depth of about 35 to 40 feet Directly beneath this layer a
moderate to densely packed boulder layer with a soil matrix of varying
thickness were encountered Dense gray vesicular basalt was
encountered ranging from 51 to 77 feet below the existing ground
surface to the full depth of our borings
Generalized cross sections of the soil profiles are presented on
Plate 3
The brown silt appears to be a transported ash generally grading
from stiff near the surface to soft near the groundwater level at a
depth of about 15 feet
The estuarine deposit below the silt included a thin
approximately 2 feet black organic silt layer over a silty coral
sand with coral and basalt gravels
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O Page 5
Below about 35 to 40 feet a dense boulder and soil layer was
encountered varying from 15 to 40 feet thick This layer ranged to a
depth of 50 feet in Boring 1 to as deep as 75 feet in Boring 2 This
boulder layer was transported and deposited at a time when Kalialinui
Stream flowed with a higher velocity
Underlying the alluvial formation gray vesicular basalt was
encountered The consistency of the basalt layer varied from highly
weathered and fractured in and 5 to very dense and
massive in Borings 1 and 2 This basalt layer extended to the full
depth of our borings
Groundwater was encountered in all of the borings and are shown
on the log of Borings Generally the groundwater level ranged from
14 5 to 15 5 feet below the existing ground surface
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Bridge Foundation
The field exploration disclosed that the site is underlain by
about 20 feet of soft to medium silt over about 15 to 20 feet of loose
sand Due to these soft and loose upper soils pile foundations are
recommended for the proposed bridge structure Ideally piles driven
down to the basalt formation at about 50 to 75 feet deep could provide
high pile capacity However it appears that the piles could not be
driven through the thick boulder layer down to the basalt without
costly predrilling Therefore lower capacity piles bearing in the
boulder layer are recommended for the bridge structure
GEOLABS HAWAII
General guidelines for the pile foundations are presented below
1
concrete piles are recommended due to the anticipated hard
driving into the boulder layer
Pile capacity in boulder layers are difficult to evaluate due
to the random arrangement of the boulders and variable
thicknesses of the matrix soil For piles installed into the
underlying boulder layer an allowable pile capacity of 50
tons per pile may be used
Based on the present subsurface soil data from the borings
it is anticipated that the pile lengths would vary from about
40 to 60 feet below the existing ground surface Variable
pile lengths are anticipated due to the erratic nature of the
stream boulder deposits
The piles should be driven with a hammer delivering a minimun
of 30 000 foot pounds energy The same type of hammer should
be used for the production pile driving as the one used for
the test pile installation
The piles should be driven to a final driving resistance of
about 15 blows per inch for the last two inches and about 50
blows for the last foot
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O Page 7
5
We recommend that test piles be first installed to estimate
the final production pile casting lengths for the project
The lengths of these test piles should be about 60 feet
After the test piles are installed one should be load tested
to two times the design load in order to further verify the
competency of the bearing stratun
The final pile driving criteria may be modified after the
completion of the test pile installation and the load test
program
The piles should be spaced a minimum of about 3 times the
pile widths
The lateral pile capacity of these piles would be about 2 5
kips each In addition a passive soil resistance of 250
p c f against the pile caps and grade beams could be used
where there is sufficient level area 2 times the height in
front of the structure
Settlement of about one half inch are anticipated for the
piles bearing in the dense boulder layer

Lateral Soil Pressures
The abutment walls will be subject to lateral earth pressures
An equivalent fluid pressure of 35 p c f active case is
recommended Additional load due to traffic or surcharge should be
included where applicable
Granular backfill material should be used and weepholes provided
through the abutment walls to reduce the build up of hydrostatic
pressure behind the walls
Site Grading
The existing Keolani Place road grades wil be raised about 4 to 5
feet
Select non expansive soil generally less than 6 inches in size
should be used for the fill material The quality of the fill
material should reflect the subgrade design assumptions for the road
pavement design section
The fill material should be properly moisture conditioned and
placed in 9 inch maximum lifts compacted to obtain a minimum of 90
for cohesive soil or 95 for granular material of its maximum dry
density established by ASTM D 1557 78
GEOLABS HAWAII
Generally ground settlements would be anticipated from the
weight of the new fill on the soft and loose underlying soils
However part of the increase in load would be off set by the proposed
excavation of the channel Estimated settlements are on the order of
1 to 2 inches Due to the granular nature of the subsoils it is
anticipated that the settlements would occur rapidly The fills
should be placed as soon as practicable preferably prior to pile
driving in order to reduce negative skin friction and allow to the
settlements to occur prior to road paving
Design Review
The plans for foundations site grading and utility construction
should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written
comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included in the project plans and specifications
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
The pile driving operations should be monitored on a full time
basis by a qualified soil technician under the direction of a soil
engineer to verify that the piles have been driven in accordance with
our recommendations
It is recommended that the excavation and backfill operations be
observed by a soils engineer during construction to see that adequate
bearing has been obtained and the soil conditions are consistent with

encountered during construction be different from our field

APPENDIX A
FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored
by drilling 5 borings utilizing truck mounted equipment The
depths of the borings were terminated at 65 to 90 feet below
the existing ground surface The approximate locations of the
borings are shown on the enclosed Site Plan Plate 2
The soils were classified by visual and textural
examinations in the field by our engineer who continuously
monitored the drilling Operations The classifications were
verified by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory
The soils were classified in accordance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A graphic presentation of the soils
encountered is presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 1
through A 5 2
Soil samples were obtained by driving either a 2 4 inch
I D ring sampler or a standard penetration sampler with a
140 pound hammer free falling a distance of 30 inches or a
Dennison sampler The blow counts to drive the sampler the
last 12 inches are shown on the Logs of Borings at the appro
priate sample depths
Rock samples were obtained by a double tube NWG
size core barrel attached with a diamond coring bit
