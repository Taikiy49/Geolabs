INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of a foundation investigation per
formed at Kolekole Pass on the island of Oahu Hawaii The purpose of
the investigation was to explore the subsurface conditions at the site
to provide soil data for the foundation design of a new microwave
antenna to replace the former unit which collapsed during the recent
Hurricane Iwa
The scope of the investigation included the drilling of two borings
field observations at the site laboratory testing and engineering
analysis based on the soil data obtained The work was done in
general conformance with our proposal dated

This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project is located in the central Waianae Mountains at the border
between the Waianae and Wahiawa Districts of the island of Oahu The
site is located in the southern side of Kolekole Pass on the ridge
which forms the divide between Lualualei Valley and Waianae Uka The
general location of the project site is shown on the Project Location
Map Plate 1
The ground surface at the antenna site is relatively level however
there are steep slopes of about 2 1 H V immediately to the northeast
and southwest The ground surface elevation is on the order of 1700
feet and a recent topographic survey using an assumed datum of 500
feet at the finished floor of the adjacent building shows a variation
in elevation at the proposed antenna site of up to 3 feet
The site is currently vegetated with a cover of grass and weeds The
surrounding area is densely vegetated with guava eucalyptus and other
trees Access to the site is provided by a paved road from the
Kolekole Pass guard house Adjacent to the antenna site is a one
story concrete building that was formerly used as a repeater station

PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
It is proposed to construct a new four legged tower approximately 90
feet high to replace the former tripod tower which collapsed The
new tower will be located near the same centerpoint as the former
unit The tower will be used to support two microwave dish antennae
approximately 15 feet in diameter It is our understanding that two
additional antennae are planned in the future The new and previous
antenna locations are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Minimal site grading is anticipated
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The Waianae Mountains are the eroded remnants of the extinct Waianae
Shield Volcano This volcano dome was built during Tertiary time by
the extrusion of basaltic lavas from three principal rift zones
Erosion has removed most of the western half of the shield The
project site is at the head of an eastward draining valley which has
been partially truncated by the headward advance of Lualualei Valley
The surficial soils are the weathering products of older alluvium
deposited prior to the capture of the headwater of the valley

Subsurface Exploration
Two borings were drilled at the site to depths of 21 5 feet below the
existing ground surface to explore the subsurface conditions and to
obtain samples for laboratory testing The approximate locations of
the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Soil Conditions
The borings encountered very stiff to hard alluvial clayey silts to
depths between 15 5 to 18 5 feet Below the alluvial soils the
borings encountered stiff residual silts to the maximun depth drilled
of 21 5 feet
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings during the field
exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that the antenna site is underlain by
very stiff to hard clayey silts alluvial soils which can provide
adequate support for the antenna and tower structure
The new tower structure will be situated over the previous tower
location The footings for the previous tower are still in place at
the site The actual size of the footing is unknown however they
are believed to be on the order of 10 by 10 feet in plan and approxi
mately 10 feet deep It appears that portions if not all of the
existing footings will have to be removed to permit construction of
the new antenna tower foundations
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1208 00 Page 5
After removal of the existing foundations the excavation should be
backfilled with on site soil compacted to a minimum of 90 of its
maximum dry density established by ASTM D 1557 78 test method Any
additional backfill material should consist of granular soil such as
sand coral or select borrow compacted to a minimum of 95 of its
maximum dry density Backfill around the new foundations should also
be compacted as specified above
Foundations
Foundations for the proposed tower could consist of deep footings or
drilled pier type foundations Based on the field exploration and
laboratory test results we recommend the following soil parameters be
assigned to the in situ soil
1 Angle of Internal Friction g 28 Degrees
2 Cohesion C 200 p s f
3 Unit Weight fw 115 p c f
Vertical Load
It is estimated that a spread footing about 10 feet square or a
9 foot diameter pier both 13 feet deep may be required for each
tower leg support
Based on the above an allowable bearing value of 4000 p s f on
the stiff in situ soil may be used Minimal settlement is antici
pated from the tower loads

Lateral Load
The tower will be subject to lateral loads primarily due to wind
and possibly seismic activity An active soil pressure against the
foundations of 40 p c f equivalent fluid pressure may be used
In order to resist the lateral loads a passive soil pressure of
200 p c f equivalent fluid pressure may be used on level areas
_ away from the edge of the slope Passive resistance should be
neglected for the footing next to the steep slope on the south
western side of the site In addition a coefficient of friction
of 0 35 between the foundation and soil may be used
Significant uplift loads are anticipated on the tower The uplift
forces may be resisted by the weight of the structure foundations
and any soil over the foundations Additionally an adhesion value
of 200 p s f between the concrete and soil around the perimeter of
the foundation may be used
Design Review
Plans for foundation and backfilling should be forwarded to the soils
engineer for review and written comments prior to construction to see
that the intent of our recommendations is included in the project
plans and specifications

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during back
filling to monitor the contractor s grading operations It is also
recommended that the footing excavations be observed by the soils
engineer during construction to see that adequate bearing has been
obtained and the subsurface conditions are consistent with the
findings of our investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the above
inspections

APPENDIX A
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by
drilling 2 borings to 21 5 feet below the existing ground surface
at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Truck mounted drilling equipment was used to drill the borings
The material encountered in the borings were classified
by visual and textural examination in the field by our
engineering geologist who continously monitored the drilling
operations The classifications were further reviewed by
visual inspection and testing in the laboratory The soils
were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A graphic presentation of the materials
encountered is presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A l
and A 2
Soil samples were obtained from the boring by driving
a standard penetration sampler or a 2 4 inch I D split barrel
sampler with a 140 pound hammer free falling at a height of
30 inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the last 12
inches are shown on the logs of borings at the appropriate
sample depths
