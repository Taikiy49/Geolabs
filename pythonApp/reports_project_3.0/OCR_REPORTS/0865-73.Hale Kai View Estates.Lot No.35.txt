As requested we have made a study of the near surface soil conditions at the
above referenced lot The purpose of the study was to evaluate the near
surface soils conditions for foundation support of the proposed house The
investigation consisted of drilling three 3 shallow borings within the
proposed building site laboratory testing of the swelling potential of the
soils reviewing the subdivision site grading and the preliminary house
foundation plans This report summarizes our findings and recommendations

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that the lot is underlain by a grayish brown
adobe clay The laboratory test results on this material generally indicates
that they have high expansion potential Therefore for Lot 35 the
slab on grade and post footing foundation construction would require special
design procedures in order to minimize the detrimental shrink swell efects of
the in situ soils
House foundation
On grade slab construction will be used for the driveway garage and partial
basement with post and beam construction under the rear portion of the upper
floor level

Slab on grade
The in situ clay soils have a high swell potential and are
therefore generally unsuitable as slab subgrade soils
In order to reduce the expansive effects of the clay they should
be over excavated down for a depth of 3 feet below the slab
subgrade and then replaced with non expansive soils such as
select borrow crusher waste or coral This over excavation and
replacement should also be extended for a horizontal distance of 3
feet beyond the building foundations
Fills should be placed in maximum 6 inch loose lifts generally be
less than 6 inches in diameter and compacted to a minimum of 90
of its maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78
For slab on grade bearing on the non expansive fill an allowable
bearing capacity of 3000 p s f may be used in addition we also
recommend that the perimeter footings have a minimum embeddment of
8 inches measured from the lowest adjacent grade to the bottom of
the footing
It is recommended that slab on grade be placed over a 4 inch layer
of well graded base course aggregate and should be separated from
wall foundations

Post _and beam construction
Due to the expansive clay soils the moderate sloping terrain the
effects of down hill creep and to develop the full bearing
capacity we recommend that for post and beam construction the
footings be embedded a distance of 3 feet measured from the
lowest adjacent grade to the bottom of the footing The footing
should be poured in place and the excavation should be backfilled
and compacted with impervious material
An allowable bearing value of 3000 p s f can be used for footings
bearing on the stiff undisturbed soil The bottom of the footing
excavation should be neat and free of soil or debris
If a footing is located next to a utility trench it should extend
to the bottom of the utility trench to reduce the possibility of
footing settlement due to poor trench backfill
The ground surface should be shaped to divert runoff away from all
foundations

Retaining Wall Foundations
CMU retaining walls up to 10 feet high are planned in the middle portion of the
house
To reduce the expansive effects of the existing clay soils on the wall we
recommend that the expansive clays be over excavated down for a depth of 24
inches below the bottom of footings and then replaced with non expansive borrow
soils compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM
D 1557 78
The wall footings should be embedded a minimum of 12 inches below the outside
finished grade Therefore the total depth of the over excavation and
replacement would be 3 feet below the outside finished grade
Off site non expansive soil such as 3B fine or crusher waste should be used
as backfill behind the wall for a horizontal distance equal to the retaining
wall height measured from the wall face
Impervious soil such as on site clay soils should be used for the top one 1
foot of backfill to reduce seepage of surface run off into the backfill behind
the walls

Concrete Driveway and Sidewalks
Expansive clay soils were encountered along the proposed driveway area Since
the driveway can tolerate some amount of heaving the clay over excavation and
replacement can be reduced to a minimum of 2 feet below the driveway subgrade
level Where 2 or more feet of fill is planned select non expansive borrow
material should be used to cap over the expansive clay Where less than 2 feet
of fill is planned over excavation and replacement of the expansive clay with
select non expansive material is required The above requirements should be
followed for a minimum distance of 3 feet beyond the edge of the driveway
Joints are recommended for the driveway at about 8 to 10 foot intervals in
order to allow for some relative movement of the concrete
For future sidewalks capping of the clay with over excavation and replacement
similar to the driveway are recommended to reduce heaving caused by the
expansive clay soil
Site Grading
In order to accomodate the basement level the existing slope will have cuts up
to 7 feet deep Fills generally less than 1 foot are anticipated for lot 35
Prior to construction the area to recieve any fill should be cleared of
vegetation and debris

Structural fills and backfills should consist of non expansive soil such as
select borrow or crusher waste and be placed in maximum 8 inch loose lifts and
then compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density established by
ASTM D 1557 78
The ground surface should be shaped to drain water away from the foundations
Two horizontal to one vertical 2 1 cut and fill slopes are recommended
Drainage
The finish lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings should be shaped
to shed water away from the foundations and to avoid ponding condition Also
it is advised that gutter systems should be installed around the house and that
gutter water should be diverted far away from the foundations and walkways
Excessive landscape watering near the foundations and slabs should also be
avoided
Planters and garden courts next to the foundations should be avoided or have
concrete bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
above recommended foundations since ponded water could cause excessive
subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The
foundation excavations should be properly backfilled tight against slab

Plans for foundations site grading and utility construction should be

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during earthwork
construction to monitor the contractor s grading operations particularly the
fill placement and slope cuts
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be obsrved by a soils
engineer during construction to see that adequate bearing has been obtained and
that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of our
subsurface investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and approved by
the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
