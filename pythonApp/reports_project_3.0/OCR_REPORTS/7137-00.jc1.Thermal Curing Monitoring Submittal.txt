Dear Mr. Loo: 
The following contains our thermal concrete curing temperature monitoring results 
for the above project. Specification Section 31 63 30 (Drilled Concrete Shaft 
Foundations) requires that drilled shafts measuring 5 feet in diameter or greater be 
considered mass concrete and subject to the requirement of Section 03 70 00 (Mass 
Concrete).  Specification Section 03 70 00 requires monitoring and controlling the 
internal temperature and temperature differential of mass concrete during the heat 
dissipation period.  It requires using the selected mix design, casting procedures and 
material, to control the maximum concrete curing temperature not exceeding 160°F 
(Fahrenheit) with temperature differential less than 35°F. 
Based on the information provided, we understand that the approved 
4,500 pounds per square inch (psi) concrete mix design (dated October 18, 2014) 
contains 540 pounds of cement per cubic yard with 25 percent of Class F Fly Ash for 
drilled shaft construction of the above project.  The length of the drilled shafts varies 
from about 72 to 82 feet with an 8-foot diameter of lower shaft and a 10-foot or 9-foot 
diameter of upper shaft. 
The concrete behavior was previously evaluated using the program 
ConcreteWorks, originally developed by Texas Department of Transportation (TxDOT), 
a non-linear regression analysis to model the heat of hydration development induced by 
activation energy of concrete cementitious component.  We modeled the ground 
temperature to be about 78°F with concrete placement temperature ranging from 70°F 
to 80°F.  The maximum temperature in the specified drilled shaft was predicted to be 
approximately 145°F to 157°F with the maximum temperature differential about 23°F to 
27°F.

Three thermistor probes (Geokon Model 3800) were installed in the middle of the 
drilled shafts (one near the center and two at opposite sides of the rebar cage), to 
measure concrete temperature development between the interior core center and 
exterior surface.  The concrete temperature measurement began upon completion of 
concrete placement and recorded continuously by Geokon LC-2 Datalogger.  The 
measurement terminated after the temperature near the shaft center was about 130°F, 
within 50°F of the ambient temperature (about 80°F), or for a maximum of two weeks.  
Detailed temperature measurements are presented on Plates 1.1 through 4.2 and also 
summarized in the table below.
