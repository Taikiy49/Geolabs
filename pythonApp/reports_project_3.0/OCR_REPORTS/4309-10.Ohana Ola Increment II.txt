PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions at the site to develop a soil rock data set for the formulation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertaining to the proposed aerobic system for the project
To accomplish this we conducted an exploration program consisting of the following tasks
and work efforts
1 Mobilization and demobilization of a truck mounted drill rig and
two operators to and from the project site
2 Drilling and sampling of four boreholes to depths of approximately 17 3 to
18 5 feet below the existing ground surface for the proposed project
3 Coordination of the field exploration logging of the borings and perfomance
of percolation tests by a field engineer geologist from our firm
4 Laboratory testing of selected samples obtained during the field exploration
as an aid in classifying the materials encountered and evaluating their
engineering properties
5 Analyses of the field and laboratory data for the formulation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertaining to the proposed project
6 Preparation of this report summarizing our work on the project and
presenting our findings and recommendations
7 Coordination of our overall work on the project by a project engineer from
our firm
8 Quality assurance of our work and client design team consultation by a
principal engineer from our firm
9 Miscellaneous work efforts such as drafting word processing and clerical
support

SUBSURFACE CONDITION
In general the borings encountered various alluvial materials consisting of clay silt
sand and gravel extending to the maximum depths explored of about 17 3 to 18 5 feet
below the existing ground surface
At the time of our field exploration groundwater was not encountered in the borings
drilled at the project site However it should be noted that groundwater levels may vary
significantly depending on seasonal precipitation groundwater withdrawal and other
factors

Permeability Tests of DOH Method
Four field percolation tests were performed in four boreholes identified as B 1
B 2 B 3 and B 6 at the project site The percolation tests were performed in general
accordance with the State of Hawaii Department of Health s Administrative Rules
Standards to evaluate the permeability of the subsurface soils for wastewater disposal
The tests were performed in 4 inch diameter boreholes drilled to depths of about 17 3 to
18 5 feet below the existing ground surface Approximately 1 inch of gravel was then
placed at the bottom of the borehole to protect the bottom of the hole from scouring and
sediments During testing the hole was carefully filled with water to a depth of about
6 inches above the gravel and refilled as necessary The time intervals and water drops

LIMITATIONS
The findings and discussion submitted in this report are based upon information
obtained from limited field borings and percolation tests Variations of subsurface
conditions between and beyond the field borings may occur and the nature and extent of
these variations may not become evident until construction is underway If variations then
appear evident it will be necessary to reevaluate the findings and discussion provided in
this report
The locations of the field borings indicated in this report are approximate having
been taped from features shown on the Percolation Test Location Plan provided by
Luersen Architects on The physical locations of the borings should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used
The stratification lines shown on the graphic representations of the borings depict
the approximate boundaries between soil and or rock types and as such may denote a
gradual transition Groundwater was not encountered in the borings drilled at the time of
our field exploration However it should be noted that groundwater levels may vary
depending on seasonal precipitation groundwater withdrawal and other factors
This report has been prepared for the exclusive use of Alternative Structures
International O Kahumana Kahumana Farm Community and their
consultants for specific application to the proposed Ohana Ola O Kahumana Increment
project located at Waianae on the Island of Oahu Hawaii in accordance with generally
accepted geotechnical engineering principles and practices No warranty is expressed or
implied
This data report has been prepared solely for the purpose of assisting the engineer
in the planning and preliminary design of the proposed project Therefore this report may
not contain sufficient data or the proper information for use in preparation of final design
drawings construction cost estimates o or other uses A contractor wishing to bid on this
project is urged to retain a competent geotechnical engineer to assist in the interpretation
of this report and or in the performance of additional site specific expioration for detailed
design and bid estimating purposes
The owner client should be aware that unanticipated subsurface conditions are
commonly encountered Unforeseen subsurface conditions such as _ perched
groundwater soft deposits hard layers or cavities may occur in localized areas and may
require additional probing or corrections in the field which may result in construction
delays to attain a properly constructed project Therefore a sufficient contingency fund is
recommended to accommodate these possible extra costs
