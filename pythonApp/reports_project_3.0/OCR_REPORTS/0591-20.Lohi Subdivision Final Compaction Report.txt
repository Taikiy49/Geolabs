From August 1976 to September 1977 periodic inspection and
density tests were made at the subject site This report
summarizes our inspections and testings As of to date all
the building pads had been filled and compacted adequately
up to about final grade
The lowlying portion of the site is a former marsh area sta
bilized with Mirafi fabric and subdrainage system The
upper fill material consisted of off site borrow mudrock
and sand mixture The upper area are cut lots consisting of
on site brown silty clay soil

RECOMMENDATIONS
Building Foundations
For light residential structures post and beam construction
is recommended
Post footing foundation should bottom at a mintmum depth of
12 inches below the adjacent ground surface and bear on stiff
in situ clay soil or compacted fill material
Bearing value of 1 000 pounds per square foot may be used
Because of the downhill creep effect of soils on a slope
building should generally be placed about 10 feet from the
tops of slopes Next to the drainage channel about 15 feet
setback is recommended
Driveway and Garage
For the lots at the upper area lots 2 through 11 the brown
silty clay subgrade soil is moderately expansive For concrete
driveway and garage slab construction a 4 inch layer of base
course rock should be provided above moist subgrade soil
In the lower area lots 1 and 12 through 28 on site brown
silty clay soil was used as topsoil capping over the underlying
imported mudrock fill layer For the above mentioned slab con
struction a 4 inch layer of gravel base course should be pro
vided The 4 inch gravel base course layer may be eliminated
if the entire slab can be lowered and bear on compacted mudrock
fill layer
