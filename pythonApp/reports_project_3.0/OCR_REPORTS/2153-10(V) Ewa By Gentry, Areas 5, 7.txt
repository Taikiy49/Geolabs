Geotechnical Engineering Recommendations
Proposed Mechanically Stabilized Earth Retaining Walls
Areas 5 7
Ewa by Gentry
This letter report presents our geotechnical engineering recommendations for the
proposed mechanically stabilized earth retaining walls MSERWs at Areas 5 and 7 of
the Ewa by Gentry development in the district of Ewa on the island of Oahu Hawaii
The general location and vicinity of the project sites are shown on the Project Location
Map Plate 1
This letter report summarizes our geotechnical engineering recommendations
resulting from our laboratory testing and engineering analyses The recommendations
presented herein are intended for design input of the MSERWs and may not be
appropriate to form the basis for preparation of construction cost estimates nor for
construction bidding purposes

Discussion and Recommendations
Based on our test pit exploration for Areas 5 and 7 which are presented in our
letters to Gentry Hawaii dated July 16 and August 5 1993 the MSERWs will bear on
medium dense sandy coral gravels and stiff silty clays Therefore an internal friction
angle of 25 degrees with no cohesion may be used in the design of the underlying
bearing materials An allowable bearing pressure of 2 500 pounds per square foot
p s f may be used for MSERWs bearing on the medium dense sandy coral gravels
and or stiff silty clays
For select borrow material compacted to 95 percent relative compaction our
laboratory test results indicated that an internal friction angle of 41 degrees may be
obtained However due to potential variations in construction process we recommend
that a friction angle of 35 degrees with no cohesion be used in the design When the
select borrow material is compacted to 95 percent relative compaction near the
optimum moisture a wet density of approximately 146 pounds per cubic foot p c f

Global Stability
Stability analyses of the proposed MSERWSs were performed using the computer
program STABRG by Geosoft The program evaluates the factors of safety of circular
slip surfaces by using both the Ordinary Method of Slices and the Bishop s Modified
Method
Our slope stability analyses were based on the data obtained from test pit
exploration of the site Based on our analyses the global stability of the proposed
MSERWs for short term and long term stabilities has factors of safety greater than 1 5

Limitations
The analyses and recommendations presented in this report are based in part
upon information obtained from test pits Variations of subsoil conditions between the
test pits may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become
evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident Geolabs
Hawaii should be contacted to reevaluate the recommendations presented in this
report
