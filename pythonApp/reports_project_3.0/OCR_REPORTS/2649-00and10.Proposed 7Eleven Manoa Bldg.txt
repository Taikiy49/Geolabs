INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed 7 Eleven Manoa Building in Manoa on the island of Oahu
Hawaii The general project area is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings of our field exploration and presents our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from analyses of the data obtained
during the exploration and laboratory testing These recommendations are intended for
design input and are not intended for construction cost estimation for bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to demolish an existing 7 Eleven store a gas pump island and three
3 underground fuel tanks to permit construction of an elevated office building with
provision for on grade parking at the project site
Three 3 alternate floor plans have been considered for the new office building
It is our Current understanding that Floor Plan No 2 may be selected The configuration
of this alternate is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 Neither column nor wall loadings for
any of the three 3 floor plans were available at the time of preparation of this report
The proposed Floor Plan No 2 includes an 8 000 square foot elevated single story
Office above an on grade parking area paved with asphaltic concrete Landscaping
comprising trees and shrubs are planned in front and on both sides of the new building

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions at the site to develop a generalized soil rock data set for the formulation of
geotechnical engineering recommendations pertaining to the proposed building
development
Geotechnical and environmental engineering evaluation pertaining to the removal
of the gas pump island and underground fuel tanks is not included in our current scope
of work
The work for this building project was done in general accordance with our two 2
fee proposals to 7 Eleven Hawaii Inc dated February 13 and March 21 1991 The scope
of work comprised
1 Mobilization and demobilization of a truck mounted drill rig portable drilling
equipment and drill crews to and from the project site
2 Drilling and sampling of three 3 initial borings to depths of about 46 5 to
72 5 feet below the existing ground surface The last 12 5 feet of the
deepest boring was accomplished by continuous penetration with no
samples retrieved Two 2 additional borings were subsequently drilled and
sampled to depths of 21 5 feet below the existing ground surface
3 Coordination of the field exploration and logging of the borings by a Field
Geologist and a Field Engineer of our firm

4 Laboratory testing of selected samples as an aid in classifying the subsoils
encountered and determining their pertinent engineering properties
5 Engineering analyses of field and laboratory data obtained to formulate our
geotechnical engineering recommendations
6 Preparation of this report summarizing our work on the project and
presenting our findings and recommendations
7 Coordination of our overall work on the project and consultation with
representative of the client and the designers by a Principal Engineer of our
firm

SITE DESCRIPTION
The site is located on the southeasterly side of East Manoa Road in the vicinity
of its intersection with Lowrey Avenue in the district of Manoa on the island of Oahu
Hawaii
At the time of our field exploration it was occupied by a one story 7 Eleven store
building located at the back of the site A concrete sidewalk and a gas pump island were
located between the store building and East Manoa Road

W O Page 5
An undated preliminary site plan of the existing structures provided to us by W M
Dean Alcon Associates on March 7 1991 indicated that three 3 existing underground
fuel tanks are located underneath the open parking stalls directly in front of the store
Surface elevations of the proposed site were not available at the time of
preparation of this report The existing ground at the site appears however to be
generally flat and is mostly paved with asphaltic concrete A narrow unpaved area
provides a planted set back behind the building Planting areas are also located at the
corners of the site and in front of the gas pump island The lot is bounded on all sides
except at Manoa Road by fences and a guard rail
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Subsoil Conditions
The subsoil conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling a total of
five 5 borings three 3 initial borings to depths of about 46 5 to 72 5 feet below the
existing ground surface and two 2 additional borings to depths of 21 5 feet below the
existing ground surface The approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site
Plan Plate 2
The field exploration encountered a surface crust of generally competent subsoils
consisting of medium dense to very dense sand and gravel and medium stiff to very stiff
Clay and clayey silt The crust varies from about 13 0 to 14 5 feet thick in Boring Nos 2
3 and 4 to about 8 0 to 10 5 feet thick in Boring Nos 1 and 5 In general the crust
thickness appears to decrease towards the easterly corner of the project site
A thick deposit of moderately to highly compressible alluvium and marshy subsoil
was encountered below the surface crust to at least 45 feet below the existing ground
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O Page 6
surface The compressible sediments range from very loose to medium dense sand and
gravel to very soft to medium stiff clayey silt In and 3 between the
depths of approximately 25 to 45 feet organic materials including visually identifiable
wood chips were encountered
Stiff to hard alluvial clay and silt were encountered below the compressible
sediments to the maximum drilled depths of 66 5 to 72 5 feet in Boring Nos 1 and 3
A very dense boulder layer was also encountered between the approximate depths
of 33 and 39 feet within the compressible sediments in Boring No 2 It appears that this
dense boulder layer is localized
Goundwater Conditions
Groundwater levels were measured at various depths in the borings ranging from
approximately 7 0 to 9 5 feet below the existing ground surface
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
If the maximum column and load bearing wall loads can be limited to one hundred
100 kips per column and ten 10 kips per foot of wall we believe that shallow
foundations may be used to support the entire proposed building on the generally
competent crust of subsoils above the compressible sediments
This concept envisions construction of the easterly wall and the southerly stairwell
walls as load bearing walls on footings to distribute the loading over the thinner crust in
this area thereby reducing potential settlements The remaining western portion of the
building may be supported on shallow spread footings and continuous wall footings
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O Page 7
A combination of pile support and shallow footings is not recommended due to
potential differential settlement between the two different foundation systems
if loading heavier than 100 kips per column are anticipated deep piles penetrating
into the stiff to hard older alluvium below the compressible sediments may be used to
derive adequate foundation support capacity Predrilling at the western end of the
proposed building is required prior to pile driving due to the localized dense boulder layer
Footing Foundations
Based on our analysis we believe that shallow spread and continuous load bearing
wall footings may be used to support the entire proposed building provided that the
maximum building loads are limited to 100 kips per column and 10 kips per foot of wall
and that load bearing wall construction for the western end of the building and stairwell
is acceptable
If this concept is adopted we recommend that both the spread and continuous
footings be embedded a minimum of 18 inches but not more than 24 inches below the
lowest adjacent finished grade The maximum footing depth is established in order to
reduce imposition of high loadings on the underlying compressible subsoils
The excavated footing subgrades should be scarified and recompacted to a
minimum of 95 of the maximum dry density as determined by ASTM Test Method
D 1557 78
Provided the above design guidelines are followed we believe that an allowable
bearing capacity of up to 2 500 p s f may be used for the footing design The bearing
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O Page 8
value may be increased by up to one third 1 3 for consideration of seismic loads wind
loads and other short term loadings
Up to one 1 inch of total footing settiement and one half 1 2 of an inch of
differential footing settlement are anticipated under the indicated maximum design
loadings
Resistance to lateral loading on the structure may be obtained by passive earth
pressure and frictional resistance on the footing foundations We recommend that an
equivalent fluid pressure of 300 p s f per foot of depth be used to determine the passive
earth pressure developed against the embedded footings A frictional coefficient of 0 3
may be used to compute the frictional resistance developed between the footings and the
recompacted footing subgrade
If load bearing wall construction at the western end of the proposed building is not
acceptable due to aesthetic or other considerations this shallow footing concept should
not be adopted
It is also considered that a combination of shallow footings and pile supported
foundations would result in differential settlements of unacceptable magnitude and is
therefore not considered to be a viable option
Pile Foundations
If column loadings exceeding 100 kips per column are anticipated we recommend
that pile foundation be used to support the entire proposed building in order to reduce
the building settlements to tolerable limits The piles may develop their vertical load
bearing capacity by penetrating a minimum of 70 feet below the existing ground surface
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O Page 9
into the stiff to hard older alluvial clay and silt below the moderately to highly compressible
sediments
Twelve 12 inch square precast and prestressed concrete piles are
recommended Based on our pile analysis the minimum 70 foot long piles may derive
a vertical allowable capacity of 40 tons per pile A total pile settlement including pile shaft
elastic deformation of up to half 1 2 of an inch is anticipated under the 40 ton pile
design load
Due to the localized dense boulder layer encountered in Boring No 2 it is
anticipated that a requirement for predrilling of the pile locations at the western end of the
proposed building will be found necessary This predrilling should extend to a minimum
depth of forty 40 feet below the existing ground surface
The effect of this predrilling has been considered in the analysis performed for
preliminary determination of pile length requirements
A pile hammer capable of delivering a minimum of 24 000 foot pounds of energy
during final driving should be used to drive the piles The piles should be driven to a
minimum depth of seventy 70 feet below the existing ground surface Also required is
a minimum driving resistance of 36 blows per foot for the last foot or 3 blows per inch for
the last 6 inches of pile penetration Driving to depths in excess of seventy 70 feet will
be required if necessary to attain the specified driving resistance
The pile driving should be observed by the project geotechnical engineer to see
that the foundation piles are driven to the required depth and penetration resistance
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O Page 10
Each pile should be driven continuously without interruption A pile may be
rejected or its design capacity may be reduced when the driving process is interrupted
for more than four 4 hours and the pile cannot be driven to the minimum required depth
During soft driving i e when the pile tip is within soft or loose soils the hammer
stroke or energy should be carefully controlled so that excessive tensile stress is not
developed in the pile
A test pile program prior to production pile driving is recommended to provide pile
driving data to verify our design assumptions and also to fulfill the following objectives
1 To modify if necessary the pile driving criteria for use during production
driving
2 To determine the casting length required for production piles more
accurately
We recommend that a minimum of six 6 test piles be driven at selected locations
within the site prior to production pile driving These test piles may be incorporated into
the actual pile foundation system
The test piles should be driven with the same pile hammer which will be used for
the subsequent production pile driving The test pile program including the selection of
test pile locations should be conducted under the observation of the project geotechnical
engineer

Elevator Machinery Pit
An elevator is planned at the main stairwell in the center of the building It is
anticipated that the lifting winches etc will be located in a machinery pit rather than being
roof mounted
The depth of the pit is anticipated to be no more than six 6 feet
The walls of the machinery pit should be designed as retaining walls restrained at
the top The following design parameters are recommended
1 Lateral earth pressure 60 p s f equivalent fluid pressure per foot of depth
This does not include any allowance for traffic or other transitory live loads
that may be imposed adjacent to the walls
2 An allowable bearing capacity up to 1 500 p s f for retaining wall
foundations bearing on recompacted natural soils is recommended
3 A passive earth pressure resistance of 300 p s f per foot depth may be
used for wall footings
4 A base friction factor of 0 3 may be used to determine the resistance of the
walls against sliding
Due to the absence of ground water and the competence of the materials
encountered within the anticipated depth of the pit it is anticipated that the requirement
for and design of shoring for the excavation will be as prescribed by applicable governing
regulations
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O Page 12
The final determination of the requirement for shoring and the design there of if
found to be required should be made the sole responsibility of the contractor who should
retain the services of qualified professional geotechnical and structural engineers for this
purpose
Review of the site plan and borings indicates that it should be feasible at the
option of the contractor to construct the elevator machinery pit in an open unshored
Backfill behind the retaining walls for either type of excavation open or shored
should be select imported material to reduce the amount of compactive effort required
The backfill material should be compacted to a maximum of 95 of the maximum dry
density as determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Site Grading
Since the project site is generally flat and the new building will be an on grade
structure only minor site grading is anticipated to be required for the project
At the on set of earthwork the project area within the contract grading limits should
be thoroughly cleared and grubbed All existing vegetation debris rubbish old structures
and other unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site
After clearing and grubbing areas at grade or designated to receive fill should be
scarified to a depth of at least 6 inches and recompacted to a minimum of 90 of the
maximum dry density as determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O Page 13
New pavement and or slab subgrades should also be scarified to a depth of at
least 6 inches and recompacted to a minimum of 95 of the maximum dry density
determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Soft or loose spots encountered within the cleared grade or pavement subgrade
level should be removed and replaced with select imported backfill material compacted
to a minimum of 95 of its maximum dry density as determined by ASTM Test Method
D 1557 78
The excavated on site granular soils may be re used as a source of fill and or
backfill materials If required new imported materials should consist of non expansive
select granular materials less than 3 inches in maximum size with less than 20 passing
the 200 sieve All imported materials should be tested and approved for their intended
purpose by the project geotechnical engineer prior to transport to the project site
Fill and backfill materials should be placed in horizontal lifts of 9 inch maximum
loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density as
determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Parking and Access Pavements
Asphaltic concrete pavement is anticipated for the proposed parking and access
areas on the existing grade to serve the new building
In general the existing pavement subgrade consists of an approximately two 2
to three 3 foot thick layer of medium dense to very dense sand and gravel Based on
the characteristics of the anticipated granular subgrade and light weight and volume of
traffic we believe that the following asphaltic concrete pavement section may be used for
preliminary design

2 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 Inch Base Course 95 Compaction
8 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
lf concrete pavement is required at the parking and or loading zones we believe
that the following slab section may be used for preliminary design
6 Inch Portland Cement Concrete
6 Inch Base Course 95 Compaction
12 Inch Total Slab Thickness
However if the existing granular pavement subgrade will be removed by lowering
of the grade such that the new pavement will be placed on the underlying clay soils
laboratory CBR and swell tests should be performed on the actual subgrade soil s
exposed during construction to verify the adequacy of the above design sections These
preliminary pavement designs assume that good drainage will be provided in the paved
areas as well as the adjacent areas

information obtained from field borings Variations of subsoil conditions between the
borings may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident

re evaluate the recommendations given in this report
The locations of the field borings are approximate having been determined by
taping from existing structures and or objects indicated on an undated preliminary site
plan of the existing structures by W M Dean Alcon Associates Inc The physical
locations of the borings should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the
method used

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content and unit weight determinations were performed on selected
soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties The
results of these tests are presented on the Boring Logs at the appropriate sample
depths
Two 2 one inch ring swell tests were performed on selected undisturbed
samples to evaluate the swelling potential of the existing near surface soils The test
results are summarized on Plate B 1
Two 2 consolidation tests were performed on selected samples of the
subsoils to evaluate their compressibility The test results are plotted on Plates B 2
and B 3
