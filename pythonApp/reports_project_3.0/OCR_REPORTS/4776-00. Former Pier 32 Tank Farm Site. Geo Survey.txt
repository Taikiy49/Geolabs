GEOPHYSICAL SURVEY
FORMER PIER 32 TANK FARM SITE
Dear Mr Fraim
We recenily completed a geophysical survey of the Former Tank Farm Pier 32
project site located at Honolulu Harbor on the Island of Oahu Hawaii This letter report
summarizes the work performed by Geolabs Inc and presents our interpretation of the
geophysical survey data obtained at the project site
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is situated in the vicinity of Pier 32 at Honolulu Harbor on the Island
of Oahu as generally shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 Based on the
information provided the former tank farm site was used as a bulk terminal for the storage
and trans shipment of asphalt The majority of the facilities consisted of above ground
storage tanks and pipelines Based on the information provided it is suspected that
possibly one or two underground storage tanks may have been utilized at the site These
tanks were reportedly closed in place
We understand that the site is presently undergoing an environmental investigation
In support of the environmental investigation it is desired to obtain some information
pertaining to the location of possible buried structures abandoned utility lines and
underground storage tanks which may remain buried at the site It was desired to utilize
non intrusive geophysical methods in an attempt to scan the shallow subsurface and
identify the location of possible buried features such as tanks or pipelines that may remain
at the site
SITE DESCRIPTION
In general the project site consists of a rectangular shaped land area with
dimensions of approximately 370 feet by about 120 feet The limits of the project site and
survey area are bounded by the existing walls located at the northern and western
perimeter of the site and the various roadway and utility easements located along the
southwestern and southeastern perimeter of the site as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2

GEOPHYSICAL SURVEY
A geophysical survey of the site was conducted using electromagnetic toning
instruments and ground penetrating radar in an attempt to map the approximate location of
anomalous features that may be buried in the shallow subsurface A grid pattern of survey
traverse lines was established at the site to serve as the basis for locating and mapping
the electrical and magnetic anomalies detected at the site
The toning survey was conducted utilizing both a Goldak inductive pipe and cable
locating device in addition to a magnetic locating device Both
instruments were utilized in an effort to detect the potential presence of buried metal
objects and conductive objects located in the shallow subsurface at depths of less than
about 2 to 4 feet below the existing ground surface Objects detected by this method may
include metallic construction debris pipelines electrical conduits and other objects that
contain electrically conductive or ferrous components
The ground penetrating radar survey was conducted using Geophysical Survey
Systems Inc SIR 3 radar profiling recorder and a 500 MHz transceiver antenna Ground
Penetrating Radar GPR is an electromagnetic impulse radar technology that uses high
frequency radio waves to obtain a real time profile of potential differences in subsurface
materials encountered along a survey traverse GPR works by transmitting a radar
frequency pulse signal into the subsurface via a transmitting antenna that is moved
steadily along the ground surface The pulse signal passes through and reflects off of
subsurface features that may have a difference in electrical properties The reflected
pulse signal is received by a receiving antenna located at the ground surface which is
moved in unison with the transmitting antenna The reflected signals are processed by
a recording instrument that displays the pattern of electrical signals received
The recording instrument generates a continuous 2 dimensional profile of the
reflected signals obtained by the ground surface traverse of the antenna system
Anomalous electrical signal signatures may be encountered and are shown on the
profile with respect to their vertical and lateral location along the survey traverse The
anomalous signature may correspond to variations in the electrical properties between a

DISCUSSION
The toning survey was conducted along the 37 survey lines located within the
project site limits as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The survey lines are identified as
Line Nos 1 through 37 The survey lines are positioned at 10 feet on center intervals
The shallow depth magnetic anomalies detected at the site were mapped as either spot
anomalies or elongated segments consisting of multiple anomalies or continuous length
anomalies The mapped anomalies are believed to represent randomly scattered metallic
objects such as construction debris or remaining portions of ferrous utility lines In general
the size of the mapped anomaly may provide a rough indication as to the relative size or
depth of the buried object The anomalies mapped as spot anomalies are believed to
represent objects that may be of relatively small size such as isolated metallic fragments
The larger magnetic anomalies mapped at the site may represent larger size objects such
as utility pipelines or concrete containing reinforcing bars
Based on the data obtained by the toning survey the more significant magnetic
anomalies appear to be located at the southeastern half of the project site It is generally
not possible to identify what kind of feature the magnetic anomaly may represent without
excavating the area The approximate location of the magnetic anomalies detected at the
site is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The ground penetrating radar survey was conducted along the bi directional survey
grid pattern shown on the Site Plan Plate 3 The survey lines are positioned at 10 feet
on center intervals The penetration depth of the radar pulse signal was calculated based
on the instrument calibration with respect to the anticipated on site soil conditions and
known depths of utility drain lines which were scanned at the site The recorded depth of
penetration is believed to be about 7 to 8 feet below the existing ground surface
Based on our interpretation of the recorded anomalies detected by GPR there
appears to be a wide scatter of anomalies detected across the project site Most of the
anomalies detected appear to be randomly distributed and may represent localized buried
objects that are anomalous to the prevailing soil conditions The anomalies could possibly
represent pipe and concrete fragments including boulders and other objects dissimilar to
the surrounding soil material However some significant radar anomalies were detected
along the northern perimeter of the site located between Survey Line Nos 17 through 26
The pattern of anomaly distribution and the relatively distinct character of the radar
signature suggest that the anomalies may possibly be related to a buried structure that
substantially differs in composition from the surrounding soils

shallower depths Typically as the radar signal passes through this highly conductive
horizon the return signal begins to degrade with increasing depth due to dispersion in the
highly conductive medium Potential target features located below a salt water table or in
highly conductive clayey soils may not be located by GPR

LIMITATIONS
The analyses submitted in this report are based upon information obtained from the
geophysical survey and site reconnaissance conducted at the project site The findings
and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon information
obtained from the interpretation of toning output and ground penetrating radar record
Variations of subsurface conditions across the project site are likely to occur and the
nature and extent of these variations may not become evident until confirmed by other
means If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the findings
provided in this report
The location of the mapped ground penetrating radar anomalies indicated in this
report are approximate having been estimated by measuring from existing features shown
on the available site map provided by Edward K Noda and Associates Inc The physical
location of the marked anomalies should be considered accurate only to the degree
implied by the methods used
The ground penetrating radar data have been reviewed and interpretations of the
radar signatures were made in the formulation of this report However it must be noted
that geophysical surveys such as ground penetrating radar are subject to interpretation of
the data by the operator s to form judgmental conclusions The geophysical survey
conducted for this project was performed in conformance with generally accepted protocol
for this type of exploration
