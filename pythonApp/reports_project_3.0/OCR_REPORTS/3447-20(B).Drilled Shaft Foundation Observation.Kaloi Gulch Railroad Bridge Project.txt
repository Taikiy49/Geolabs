In accordance with the project specifications Geolabs Inc has provided geotechnical
engineering consultation services in support of the drilled shaft foundation installation for the
above referenced project This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering
observations during the drilled shaft installation for the bridge foundations at the subject site
A geotechnical engineering exploration was conducted for the project by Geolabs and
the results were presented in our report entitled Geotechnical Engineering Exploration Kaloi
Gulch Railroad Bridge Job No 33 98 dated In
addition a trial shaft program with an instrumented load test was conducted at the beginning
of the drilled shaft foundation installation for the bridge The results of the trial shaft and load
test program were presented in our report entitled Trial Shaft and Load Test Program Kaloi
Gulch Railroad Bridge Project dated
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Kaloi Gulch Railroad Bridge project is located adjacent to the existing Coral Creek
Golf Course and next to the Kula Lei Subdivision in the District of Ewa on the Island of Oahu
Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1
The railroad bridge project site is situated along the existing railroad tracks and
Chevron oil pipelines located to the south of Renton Road Based on the project plans the
new bridge will be approximately 159 feet long from abutment to abutment about 28 feet
wide and about 18 feet above the bottom of the new Kaloi Gulch Channel floor The bridge
will be a three span bridge supported at each end by the abutments and by two intermediate
piers located within the new open channel A deep foundation system consisting of a series
of drilled shafts was designed to support the bridge The approximate locations of the drilled
shafts for the bridge foundation are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2

Based on the available information the structural loading of the bridge at each
abutment is approximately 385 kips compression load and the structural loading at each
intermediate pier is about 1 100 kips compression load We understand that the existing
Chevron oil pipelines would be relocated and supported on the drilled shafts of the bridge
Each drilled shaft for the bridge foundation was designed based on ultimate compression
load capacities of about 800 and 1 970 kips for the abutment and intermediate pier
structures respectively
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Based on the borings drilled for the project in 1995 and 1998 the project site is
underlain primarily by medium dense silty sands and silty clayey coral gravel below some
near surface clayey materials Groundwater was encountered in the borings in 1998 at
depths of about 40 and 40 5 feet below the existing ground surface at the time of the
field exploration The groundwater levels measured in 1998 corresponded to about
Elevations 2 to 2 5 feet Mean Sea Level MSL Due to the proximity of the project site to
the Pacific Ocean and the permeable subsurface soils at the site the groundwater levels
might be affected by tidal fluctuations In addition groundwater levels are also subject to
change due to seasonal precipitation storm surge conditions and other factors
PRODUCTION DRILLED SHAFT INSTALLATION
The trial shaft and the production drilled shafts were installed by Foundation
International Inc Based on the trial shaft program the drilling subcontractor demonsirated
that the proposed excavation method and equipment were acceptable for installing the drilled
shaft foundations in general accordance with the project specifications Based on the
instrumented load test results the lengths of the production drilled shafts were reduced by
1 5 and 4 feet for the abutments and the intermediate piers respectively
A total of 12 drilled shafts three shafts for each of the two abutments and two
intermediate piers were installed between August 12 and The
approximate locations of the drilled shafts installed are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The
installation operations were conducted under the near continuous observation of an engineer
from our office
The production drilled shafts were excavated using Watson 3000 track mounted
and Watson 3200 truck mounted drill rigs The bottoms of the drilled shafts were
excavated to about the revised tip elevations of 7 0 and 56 0 feet MSL for the abutments
and intermediate piers respectively During the production shaft excavation it was noted
that the sidewalls of the drilled shaft hole held up relatively well without the need for
temporary casing of the drilled shaft hole Therefore the production shaft excavations
proceeded without using a temporary casing for drilled hole stabilization The logs of the
production drilled shaft construction and materials encountered are provided in the Appendix
A summary of the installed shafts is presented on Plate 3

A degradable drilling fluid flocculant Quick Floc was used to stabilize the drilled hole
below the groundwater table After the shaft was excavated to the tip elevation the
reinforcing bar rebar cage was placed into the drilled hole using a crane The rebar cage
for the intermediate pier shafts consisted of two sections of rebar cages connected together
with a mechanical splice The rebar cage for the abutment shafts consisted of one
continuous section Drilled shaft spacers were used to provide the minimum 3 inches of
concrete cover over the rebar cage
After the rebar cage was installed concrete was placed through a 4 inch tremie steel
pipe starting from the bottom During concrete placement we observed that at least 5 feet
of concrete head was maintained above the bottom of the tremie pipe In order to provide
good quality concrete at the top of the drilled shaft additional concrete was placed in the
drilled shaft to allow some over pouring of the concrete at the top of the drilled shaft
Concrete was over poured until sound concrete was evident at the top of the drilled shaft
Based on our field observations we believe that the drilled shafts for this project have
been installed in general conformance with the project plans and specifications and within
the limits of generally accepted construction practices Based on our observations of the
materials encountered during the shaft excavation it is our professional opinion that the
drilled shafts have been installed to sufficient depths to provide the design vertical ultimate
compression load capacities
