PURPOSE AND SCOPE
A previous soil investigation consisting of a series of
shallow test pits excavated around the general site area was
performed by our office in November 1978 The test pits en
countered competent coral sand subsoils at the site Perco
lation tests were performed in three of the dug test pits
which extended down to about 5 foot depths where groundwater
was encountered Previously the percolation tests in these
shallow depth pits indicated quite satisfactory percolation
rates for typical single family one cesspool use However

The purpose of the additional percolation testing was to
provide in situ seepage characteristics of the sandy soils
below the natural water level and to evaluate the adequacy
of these porous layers below the water level for cesspool
percolations
This report summarizes our recent percolation test results
analysis and the resulting conclusions regarding the perco
lation characteristics of the project site subsoils

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The geology of the Kekaha area is typical of the shoreline
deposits of the soutwest coast of Kauai The entire project
site is located on dune sand and marine derived coral beach
sand deposits
These surficial sand deposits were formed on a relatively
flat coastal plain as relative sea level lowered in this
area Percolating fresh water combined with tidal move
ment of the brackish groundwater caused partial cementation
of the sand at and near present mean sea level
Soil Condition
The previous test pits and our recent additional drilling for
the percolation test holes indicated that the site is under
lain by dune and coral sand deposits The underlying poorly
graded sand deposits consist of fine dune sand above the
water level and grades to coarser size medium coral sand
below the water level

PERCOLATION TESTING
Four percolation holes and testing were performed at various
locations at the proposed development area The approximate
locations of the percolation tests are shown on Site Plan
Plate l
The test holes were drilled with a 6 inch diameter auger to
depths of 7 to 11 5 feet To similate future cesspool ring
installation below the water level into the sand layers a
3 or 4 inch diameter PVC casing was then placed into the
drilled hole to prevent caving in the sand The lower por
tion of casing below the water table was perforated while
the upper portion above the water table was not perforated
Water was introduced into the casing from the top and the
change in water level with time was observed and recorded
If the water level could be maintained to stabilize with a
head at a certain level during the test percolation rate
equal to inflow rate a constant head test was then perfor
med If the inflow rate exceeded the percolation rate the
casing was filled up by pumping more water and a falling
head test would be performed by measuring the rate of water
level dropping

CONCLUSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The recent percolation test results generally confirm our
previous belief and observations that good percolation rates
can be obtained in the medium size poorly graded coral sand
deposits below the natural high water level at this low cost
single family housing project site where minimum site fill is
desired to minimize costly hauling in of import fill material
Constant head tests performed in drilled holes indicated very
good percolation rates At Hole Nos 4 and 1 locations

inflow rates of 4 0 and which amount to 5 760 and
12 384 gallons per day were dissipated in the test holes
with a head build up of 4 5 to 2 feet respectively
The falling head tests performed in Hole No 2 also indicated
a very high percolation or dissipation rate of 0 33 minutes
per inch FHA calculation method A lower and still adequate
percolation rate of 5 minites per inch was obtained for Hole
No 3 However the lower percolation rates obtained in
Hole No 3 were a result of the smaller dissipation area in
the hole caused by washing in of the surrounding sand into
the casing This condition can be remedied in the final
cesspool design by the placement of a filter gravel blanket
at the outside and bottom of the cesspool
It is our observation that the percolation test results indi
cate more than adequate percolation rates for the subsurface
coral sand deposits below the water table and it should be
included as part of the area for the cesspool Based on the
test results it is our opinion that this seepage surface
area below the high water level should be included to develop
the additional percolation rate

caving in of the surrounding sandy soils
