INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the Lanai VORTAC Main Antenna Tower located on the Island of Lanai
Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1

This report summarizes the findings and _ geotechnical engineering
recommendations resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering
analyses These geotechnical engineering recommendations are intended for the design
of foundations and site preparation only The findings and recommendations presented
herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located within the Lanai VORTAC main antenna tower site
approximately 2 miles south of the Lanai Airport on the island of Lanai Hawaii It is
proposed to construct a 20 foot high self supporting tower for the main antenna adjacent
to the existing equipment building Based on the information provided we anticipate that
the antenna tower will be relatively light in weight but will be subjected to significant uplift
forces and moment at the foundation level Minimal site grading work is anticipated for
the project construction

recommendations pertinent to the foundation design and site preparation for the new
antenna The work was done in general accordance with our proposal dated

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located within the existing Lanai VORTAC site approximately
2 miles south of the Lanai Airport Access to the site is through dirt roads on rolling hills
and cattle grazing lands within the immediate vicinity of the facility Ground elevation at
the site varies from 1 252 feet above Mean Sea Level MSL at the VORTAC area on the
northern side to 1 235 feet above MSL at the building area and slopes down sharply
towards the ocean on the southern side

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
On the basis of our geotechnical engineering exploration we believe that the
proposed antenna tower may be supported on a mat foundation bearing on the in situ stiff
soils encountered at shallow depths We estimate the total foundation settlements under
the anticipated light structural loads to be less than 1 inch with differential settlements
between the center and the corner of the mat to be on the order of inch Detailed
discussion of our geotechnical engineering design recommendations are presented in the
following sections of this report
Mat Foundations
Based on the generally competent subsoil conditions encountered at the site we
believe that a mat foundation bearing on the in situ stiff soils may be used to support the
proposed antenna tower structure An allowable bearing pressure of up to 3 500 pounds
per square foot p s f may be used for design of shallow mat foundations bearing on the
in situ stiff silty clay soils This bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may be
increased by one third 1 3 for transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic
forces For mat foundation design a modulus of subgrade reaction of 150 pounds per
square inch per inch of deflection p c i may be used for the mat foundation bearing on
the in situ stiff soils The subgrade modulus is the modulus of vertical subgrade reaction
for a 1 foot square test plate
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 4012 00 Page 5
The bottom of the mat foundation should be embedded a minimum of 2 feet below
the lowest adjacent finish grade Deeper embedment may be required to provide the
required capacity against uplift and moment reactions at the base of the tower
Resistance to uplift loads may be provided by the dead weight of the structure and the
mat foundation Additional resistance to uplift loads may be provided by the weight of the
backfill material placed above the footings if applicable A unit weight of 110 pounds per
cubic foot p c f may be used for the on site compacted silty clay materials
The bottom of the mat footing excavation should be firm and unyielding prior to
placement of reinforcing steel or concrete If soft and or loose materials are encountered
at the bottom of the footing excavation they should be over excavated to expose the
underlying firm materials The resulting over excavation should be backfilled with the
on site soil placed and compacted in accordance with the recommendations presented in
the Site Preparation section of this report or the footing bottom may be extended down
to the underlying competent material
If foundations are located next to utility trenches or easements they should be
embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane extending
upward from the bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility
lines
We estimate the total foundation settlements under the anticipated light structural
loads to be less than 1 inch with differential settlements between the center and the
corner of the mat to be on the order of inch
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive earth pressure
acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system and by frictional resistance
and adhesion developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid
pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth p c f This assumes that the
soils around the mat are well compacted or undisturbed Where needed a shear key

Site Preparation
Based on the proposed location of the antenna tower minimal site grading work is
anticipated for the project construction
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed Debris unsuitable materials and existing surface
pavements that are to be demolished should be removed and disposed of off site
Areas beneath the future tower mat should be over excavated to the future
foundation subgrade level If zones of soft loose or saturated soils are encountered at
the over excavated subgrade level deeper over excavation may be required to expose
firm material
Due to the relatively high natural moisture contents the mat subgrade should be
proof rolled with a smooth drum roller in lieu of scarification and recompaction In
addition heavy or rubber tired equipment are not recommended to reduce the potential
for pumping subgrade conditions
Generally the on site silty soils should be suitable for use as engineered fill
materials provided that they are properly moisture conditioned and compacted It should
be noted that the on site soils have moderate to high in situ moisture contents
Therefore it may be difficult to achieve the required compaction without some aeration of
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 4012 00 Page 7
the on site soils If imported material is required the material should conform to the
requirements for Select Borrow for Subbase Course stipulated in Section 30 of the
Standard Specifications for Public Works Construction dated September 1986 Any
imported fill material should be tested and approved for its intended use by our firm prior
to delivery to the project site
Fills and backfills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose
thickness moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above optimum moisture content
and compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to
the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of
the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 91 test procedures
Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the
maximum dry density
Site grading operations should be observed by a representative of
Geolabs Hawaii It is important that a representative from our office observe the site
grading operation to evaluate whether undesirable materials are encountered during the
excavation and scarification process and whether the exposed soil rock conditions are
similar to those encountered in our exploration
Drainage
The finished grades outside the antenna tower should be sloped to shed water
away from foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding Excessive landscape
watering near the foundations should also be avoided Drainage swales should be
provided as soon as possible and should be maintained to drain all surface run off away
from the foundation slab
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil
saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations
should be properly backfilled against the foundation slab immediately after setting of the
concrete to reduce water infiltration

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that Geolabs Hawaii be retained to provide geotechnical
engineering services during the site preparation and construction of the foundation This
is to observe compliance with the design concepts specifications or recommendations
and to expedite suggestions for design changes that may be required in the event that
subsurface conditions differ from those anticipated at the time this report was prepared
The recommendations provided in this report are contingent upon such observations
If actual exposed subsurface conditions encountered during construction are
different from those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications
to the design should be made

evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be
necessary to reevaluate the recommendations provided in this report
