and sampling services conducted for the proposed Kaiwakiloumoku Hawaiian Cultural

The project location and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The findings presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
We understand that a one story building and one level covered parking structure
are planned at the site The site is located at the Chapel parking lot and the
undeveloped area north of the parking lot Based on the topographic survey map the
undeveloped area is located on sloping ground
Because the project is on sloping ground relatively high cut slopes are required to
level the site for the new building structure We understand that the proposed finished
floor elevation will be set at approximately 545 0 feet Mean Sea Level MSL Based on
the proposed finished floor elevation the cut slopes may range from about 25 to 40 feet
high Our previous borings indicated that the cut slopes would expose medium hard to
hard basalt rock formation Due to the proximity of the project site to existing structures
there is a concern of damage if blasting is used during the site excavation Therefore
three additional borings were drilled along the cut slope area to further investigate the
possibility of blasting as a means of excavating the high cut slope

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our work was to provide geotechnical drilling and sampling services
to obtain additional subsurface information on the basalt rock formation underlying the
proposed slope cut In order to accomplish this we conducted a program generally
consisting of the following tasks and work efforts
1 Site clearing and set up of a wooden drill platform at the boring locations
2 Mobilization and demobilization of a portable drill rig and two operators to
and from the project site
3 Drilling and sampling of three borings extending to depths of about 44 5 to
45 0 feet below the existing ground surface
4 Coordination of the field exploration stakeout of boring locations and
logging of borings by our field engineer or geologist
5 Laboratory testing of selected samples obtained during the field exploration
as an aid in classifying the materials and evaluating their engineering
properties
6 Preparation of a data report summarizing our work on the project and
presenting our findings
7 Coordination of our overall work on the project by our engineer
8 Quality assurance of our work and client design team consultation by our
principal engineer
9 Miscellaneous work efforts such as drafting word processing and clerical
support
Detailed description of the field exploration methodology and the Logs of Borings
are presented in Appendix A Results of the laboratory tests are presented in Appendix B
Photographs of the rock core samples are presented in Appendix C
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
We explored the subsurface conditions along the proposed cut slope by drilling and
sampling three borings designated as Boring Nos 101 through 103 extending to depths
of about 44 5 to 45 0 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate boring
locations are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2

The borings generally encountered a surface layer of soil consisting of stiff clayey
silt extending to depths of about 3 to 5 feet below the existing ground surface Basalt rock
formation was encountered below the surface soils The basalt rock formation extended to
the maximum depths explored of about 44 5 to 45 0 feet below the existing ground
surface
The basalt rock formation was generally hard with some soft to medium hard zones
encountered in Boring No 103 The fracturing of the basalt formation generally ranged
from close to moderate with some areas being massive Voids and clinker zones were
also encountered in the drilled borings
We did not encounter groundwater in the borings at the time of our field exploration
However groundwater levels can significantly vary depending on rainfall temperature
surface runoff groundwater withdrawal and other factors

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
Our field exploration consisted of drilling and sampling three borings designated as
Boring Nos 101 through 103 extending to depths of about 44 5 to 45 0 feet below the
existing ground surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 We
drilled the borings using a portable drill rig equipped with NQ wireline coring tools
Our geologist classified the materials encountered in the borings by visual and
textural examination in the field and monitored the drilling operations on a near continuous
basis Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil Classification
System as shown on Plate A Graphic representations of the materials encountered are
provided on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 3
Core samples of the rock formations encountered at the project site were obtained
using diamond core drilling techniques in general accordance with ASTM D 2113
Diamond Core Drilling for Site Investigation Core drilling is a rotary drilling method that
uses a hollow bit to cut into the rock formation The material left in the hollow core of the
bit is mechanically recovered for examination and classification
Recovery REC is used as a subjective guide to the interpretation of the relative
quality of rock masses Recovery is defined as the actual length of material recovered
from a coring attempt versus the length of the core attempt For example if 4 feet of
material is recovered from a 5 foot core run the recovery would be 80 percent and would
be shown on the Logs of Borings as REC 80
The Rock Quality Designation RQD is also a subjective guide to the relative
quality of rock masses RQD is defined as the percentage of the core run that is sound
material in excess of 4 inches in length without any discontinuities discounting any
drilling induced fractures or breaks If 2 5 feet of sound material is recovered from a
5 foot core run the RQD would be 50 percent and would be shown on the Logs of
Borings as RQD 50 Generally the following is used to describe the relative quality
of the rock based on the Practical Handbook of Physical Properties of Rocks and
Minerals
