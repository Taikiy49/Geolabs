PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the near surface soil
conditions at the project site in order to develop an idealized subsurface data set for the
formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to the design of
pavement site grading and utility construction for the project
The work was done in general accordance with our proposal dated
March 22 1993 The scope of our work on the project consisted of excavation and
sampling of shallow test pits along the alignment of the proposed collector roads
laboratory testing engineering analysis and report preparation
The results of our exploration and laboratory tests are presented in the appendices
of this report The approximate locations of the test pits are shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2

The project site is located on a relatively flat area within the multi family develop
ment of Kapolei Village 6 At the time of our exploration a 15 foot deep sedimentation
basin exists between and of the proposed Collector Road C
Based on our site reconnaissance approximately 2 to 3 feet of soft sediments have
accumulated at the base of the sedimentation basin
An approximately 14 feet deep temporary drainage ditch was noted between Sta

covered with a layer of geotextile and inclinations of the banks of the ditch were at
approximately 2 horizontal to 1 vertical The temporary drainage ditch extends to the
existing 10 foot by 6 foot box culvert below the existing Ewa Parkway

RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the test pits and laboratory test results the project site is underlain by
a surficial layer of expansive silty clay Our geotechnical engineering recommendations
pertaining to the design of collector road pavements site grading and utility construction
are presented in the following sections
Pavement Design
Asphaltic concrete pavements are planned for the collector roads The anticipated
subgrade soils for the proposed roadways consist of the near surface silty clays
encountered in our test pits Based on our current field exploration and laboratory tests
the above anticipated subgrade soil appears to have a CBR value of about 2 with
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3192 00 Page 5
expansion values of about 2 to 3 percent Considering the medium traffic anticipated for
the proposed roadways we recommend the following preliminary pavement designs for
budgeting purposes
2 5 Inches Asphaltic Concrete
6 0 Inches Base Course 95 percent relative compaction
18 0 Inches Select Borrow 95 percent relative compaction
26 5 Inches Total Pavement Thickness
on a Moist Compacted Subgrade
A minimum of 6 inches of select borrow should be incorporated below concrete
walkways wheelchair ramps and passages to reduce the potential of heaving The select
borrow should be compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
All subgrades should be thoroughly moistened and kept moist until covered with
select borrow and or base course
We recommend that CBR and density tests be performed on the actual subgrade
soils encountered during construction to confirm the adequacy of the above sections
The recommended sections also assume that adequate drainage will be provided for the
paved areas Weep holes should be provided in drainage inlet structures to relieve water
which may accumulate in the subgrade and cause pumping failure of the pavement The
weep holes should be installed at both base course and subbase course elevations
Site Grading
1 Prior to commencement of earthwork the site should be thoroughly cleared and
grubbed All existing surface vegetation geotextiles old pavements and
deleterious materials within the limits of the proposed collector roads should be
removed and disposed of off site The soft sediments at the bottom of the
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3192 00 Page 6
sedimentation basin and temporary drainage ditch should be completely removed
to expose firm soils
2 Any zones of wet saturated or yielding areas encountered during the clearing
process should be over excavated to expose firm ground and the resulting
excavations should be backfilled with well compacted engineered fill This should
be evaluated by the project geotechnical engineer Contract documents should
include additive and deductive unit prices for over excavation and engineered fill
placement to account for variations in the over excavation quantities
3 Areas at grade or designated to receive fill should be scarified to a depth of 10
inches moisture conditioned to between 2 to 5 percent above the optimum
moisture content and compacted to 90 percent relative compaction Relative
compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of
the maximum dry density as determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 91
Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding
to the maximum dry density
4 All fill and backfill material should be evaluated by the project geotechnical
engineer prior to use Because the site grading consists only of minor cuts we
anticipate that import fill material would be needed for this project Import fill
material should consist of non expansive select granular material Select fill
material should contain no perishable substances organic materials or rocks
greater than 3 inches in greatest dimension with less than 15 percent passing the
No 200 sieve with laboratory CBR swell value not exceeding 1 percent and CBR
value of 20 or more

5 Generally all select fill and backfill material should be placed in 10 inch loose level
lifts and compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction unless
specified otherwise
All fills required to backfill the sedimentation basin and temporary drainage ditch
should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to not less than 95 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential
for settlement of new fills In areas where future improvements such as houses are
planned above the new fills on site excavated clayey soils may be used to backfill
the lower portion of the sedimentation basin and temporary drainage ditch up to
2 feet below the finished grade elevation The remaining upper 2 feet of backfill
should consist of non expansive select fill material
In roadway areas the upper 2 feet of fill below the finished grade should be
compacted to not less than 95 percent relative compaction The road and
sidewalk subgrade should be moisture conditioned to between 2 and 5 percent
above the optimum moisture content and compacted to 85 to 90 percent relative
compaction Due to the expansive potential of the subgrade soil over compaction
of the subgrade soil is not recommended
The project site is relatively flat however there may be localized areas of relatively
steep slopes To properly bond new fill to the existing ground we recommend
that the existing slope be keyed and benched as shown on Plate 3 where the
existing slope is steeper than 5 horizontal to 1 vertical

Utility Trench
It is anticipated that the utilities for the roadway construction will consist primarily
of water drainage and sewer In general good construction practices should be utilized
for the installation and backfilling of the trench for the proposed utilities
Based on the results of our field exploration it appears that in general
conventional excavation methods may be utilized for utility line trenching at the site The
subsurface clayey soil materials encountered at the site are generally medium stiff to very
hard However areas of loose materials may be encountered Trench shoring design
and installation should be the responsibility of the contractor Trench shoring and bracing
should conform to the appropriate health and safety requirements
Utilities should be placed on a 6 inch cushion of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33
No 67 gradation Self compacting granular backfill such as No 3B Fine gravel should
be used around the pipe and extend 12 inches above the top of the pipe It is critical that
a self compacting material be used to reduce the potential of formation of voids below the
haunches of pipes and to provide adequate support for the sides of the pipes The use
of on site clayey soils as backfill around utility pipes is not recommended
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3192 00 Page 9
Trench backfills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 10 inches in loose
thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction In roadway
and structural areas the upper 2 feet of backfill below finished grade should be
compacted to a minimum of 95 percent
Mechanical compaction equipment should be used to compact the materials at the
project site Water tamping jetting or ponding may not be used to compact the on site
clayey soils
Box Culvert
Based on our field exploration laboratory testing and previous exploration data
from Kapolei Village 6 Multi Family Project we believe that an allowable bearing pressure
of up to 2 500 pounds per square foot p s f may be used for the design of the box
culvert and inlet structure bearing on a minimum of 12 inches of No 3B Fine gravel
placed on the in situ hard clayey soil The allowable bearing pressure is for dead plus
live loads and may be increased by one third for transient loadings such as temporary
seismic forces
A friction factor of 0 35 may be used to evaluate the sliding resistance between the
box culvert and the cushion fill A passive soil resistance of 300 p s f per foot of depth
may be used
The walls of the culvert structure should be designed for lateral earth pressure
expressed in equivalent earth pressure for a restrained wall or an unyielding condition
The recommended lateral earth pressure expressed in equivalent fluid pressure for the
culvert structure is 55 p c f Surcharge stresses on the box culvert walls should be taken
as a rectangular distribution with a uniform pressure equal to 50 percent of the vertical
surcharge pressure
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3192 00 Page 10
We also recommend a drainage system consisting of a minimum 1 foot wide zone
of permeable material No 3B Fine gravel or ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation immediately
adjacent to the wall with a perforated pipe perforations down at the base of the wall
discharging to a discharge facility or weep holes A filter fabric such as Mirafi 140N or
equivalent should be placed between the drainage material and the compacted backfill
material The drainage material should extend from the base of the wall to a height within
2 feet below finish grade The remaining backfill should consist of an impervious capping
of select non expansive soil
As an alternative a prefabricated drainage product such as MiraDrain or
EnkaDrain may be used instead of the drainage material with filter fabric The
prefabricated drainage product should also hydraulically connect to a perforated pipe at
the base of the wall
To reduce possible future ground subsidence the fill should be placed in level
loose lifts not exceeding 10 inches in thickness and mechanically compacted to a
minimum of 90 percent of its maximum dry density as determined in accordance with
procedures described in ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91 The top 2 feet of backfill
should consist of non expansive impervious material to reduce infiltration of surface water
behind the wall

Construction Observation
It is recommended that a qualified geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during the project construction to observe the site
grading and culvert construction and backfill of the project This is to observe compliance
with the intent of the design concepts specifications and or recommendations and to
allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from those anticipated
during design
lf actual exposed soil conditions encountered during construction are different from
those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to the design
should be made by a qualified geotechnical engineer

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
Three test pits were excavated to depth of about 5 feet from the existing ground
surface A small hoptoe was used to excavate the test pits The approximate test pit
locations are shown on the Site
The materials encountered in the test pits were examined and classified in general
accordance with ASTM Standard Practice D 2488 84 Description and Identification of
Soils Visual Manual Procedure by our engineer who observed the excavation
operations The classifications were further reviewed by visual observations and testing
in the laboratory All soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A summary of the materials encountered are presented in the Test
Pit Logs Plates A 1 and A 2
The subsoil data from our exploration for the Kapolei Village 6 Multi Family Housing
Project are also presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 3 through A 5 for ease of
reference
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Method D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by
California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts needed
to drive the sampler the last 12 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown on the boring logs
at the appropriate sample depths
The materials encountered in the borings were examined and classified in general
accordance with ASTM Standard Practice D 2488 84 Description and Identification of
Soils Visual Manual Procedure by a geologist from our firm The logs of the borings
are shown on Plates A 3 through A 5
