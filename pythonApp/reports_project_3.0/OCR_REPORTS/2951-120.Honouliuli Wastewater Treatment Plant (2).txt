INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration performed
for the proposed 700 000 gallon reverse osmosis RO water reservoir for the existing
Honouliuli Wastewater Treatment Plant WWTP in the District of Ewa on the Island of
Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering recommendations
resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering analyses for the
700 000 gallon water reservoir These recommendations are intended for the design of
foundations and site grading only and are not intended for the basis of preparation of cost
estimates or for bidding purposes The findings and recommendations presented herein are
subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a 700 000 gallon RO stainless steel water reservoir tank
approximately 70 feet in diameter and 25 feet in height with a bolted 316 stainless steel
floor will be supported on a ring wall foundation The tank will include a 12 foot high
aluminum dome roof In addition a pump station will be provided which will consist of two
pumps and foundations supporting the pumps The pumps will be covered under an
open wall shed roof building
The finish floor elevation for the new water reservoir will closely match the existing
ground surface which is at approximately 18 1 feet Mean Sea Level MSL based on the
project plans provided Therefore we anticipate that minor site grading consisting of cuts
for embedding the footings will be needed for the construction of the proposed reservoir
and appurtenant structures
Based on information provided the anticipated total load on the tank with a 24 52 foot
high shell including the load of water and footing will be approximately 6 000 kips The
GEOLABS INC

reinforced concrete ring wall foundation will be approximately 15 inches wide and 18 inches
deep

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lavas from two
shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be
middle to late Pliocene in age and Koolau is estimated to be late Pliocene to early
Pleistocene in age As volcanic activity in Waianae ceased lava flows from Koolau banked
against its eroded eastern slope forming a broad plateau now known as the Schofield
Plateau or Central Oahu
The forces of weathering and erosion attacked this plateau generating sediments
which were transported to the coast In the vicinity of the project site and further south these
alluvial sediments accumulated and interbedded with marine sediments and coral algal reef
formations to form a sedimentary wedge ranging in thickness from zero feet in the area of
H 1 Freeway to over 1 000 feet at Ewa Beach This wedge forms the Ewa Plain and serves
as the confining formation or caprock over the artesian basal aquifers of southern Oahu
Deposition of these sediments has continued from earlier geologic time through the present
During the Pleistocene Epoch Ice Age sea levels fluctuated in response to the
growth and retreat of the great continental glaciers As the glaciers advanced less water
was available to fill the ocean basins and sea levels fell below the current sea level As the
glaciers melted and retreated more water became available and sea levels rose to and often
above the current level When the stand of the sea was high and prolonged marine deposits
accumulated at elevations above the current sea level
At the project site the near surface materials are primarily coral algal deposits which
are composed of medium hard limestone with an open lattice like structure It is believed
that these coral deposits were laid during the Kaena 95 feet MSL stand of the sea and
subsequently eroded to near their current elevation during the Waimanalo 25 feet MSL
stand of the sea The coral formation has also been subjected to subaerial weathering
Coral aigal materials are chemically composed of calcium carbonate During
subaerial weathering slightly acidic rain and groundwater can slowly attack the carbonate
GEOLABS INC
W O 2951 120 Page 5
dissolving the weaker portions of the formation This creates solution cavities ranging from
small pores to large caverns
EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The proposed RO storage tank will be built separately from the existing Honouliuli
Wastewater Treatment Plant facility in the District of Ewa on the Island of Oahu Hawaii
The proposed RO storage tank will be located in Campbell Industrial Park in the District of
Ewa on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project site is bounded by
refinery facility to the south gravel paved access road parallel to a drainage channel to the
east and an open area with small trees and light vegetation owned by James Campbell
Trust Estate to the north and west
Based on the plans provided the elevations on the project site range between 17 0
and 20 7 feet MSL The presence of soil piles and depressions on the project site accounts
for the difference in the surface elevations Prior to geotechnical exploration the site was
cleared of vegetation and the piles of soil and shallow depressions were leveled The site
is currently relatively flat and estimated to be at an average elevation of approximately
18 feet MSL

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field explorations the subsurface conditions at the project site generally
consist of relatively competent coral materials throughout the depths explored except at the
southeastern quadrant of the reservoir site where voids and cavities were encountered The
major geotechnical considerations for the project are the quality and uniformity of the coral
formation at the location of the proposed structure
Based on the results of our explorations we believe that the proposed structures may
be supported on shallow ring wall foundation bearing on the medium dense to very dense
coral materials or properly compacted select granular fill material
Due to the potential presence of cavities voids and very loose to loose zones in the
coral formation a foundation probing and grouting program was implemented simultaneously
with our geotechnical engineering exploration The purpose of the probing program is to
reduce the potential for loss of foundation support resulting from collapse of the cavities or
compression of loose zones below foundations and structures
Soft and or loose materials that are encountered at the bottom of foundation
excavations should be over excavated to expose the underlying firm materials or to a
maximum depth of 4 feet below the bottom of footing elevation The over excavation should
GEOLABS INC
W O 2951 120 Page 7
be backfilled with base course material compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction
For pavements and slabs on grade we recommend that the subgrade be proof rolled
with a minimum 10 ton vibratory drum roller or a Caterpillar D 8 bulldozer or similar heavy
excavation equipment a minimum of 6 passes to assist in detecting the presence of
near surface cavities and loose zones
Detailed discussions and recommendations for design of foundations retaining
structures site grading slabs on grade and other geotechnical aspects of the project are

Reservoir Foundations
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered at the project site we believe that
the underlying medium dense to dense coral formation would provide suitable foundation
support for the proposed reverse osmosis water reservoir structure It is our opinion that
spread and or continuous footings with concrete slab on grade foundations may be used
to support the proposed reservoir structure
For foundations bearing on the dense coral formation or compacted base course
layer an allowable bearing pressure of up to may be used for design This
bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third for transient
loads such as those caused by wind or seismic forces
Prior to placement of the base course layer the footing and slab subgrades should
be scarified to a depth of about 12 inches moisture conditioned to above optimum
moisture content and recompacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
Footings should be embedded a minimum of 12 inches below the lowest adjacent
finished grade Foundations located next to utility trenches or easements should be
embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane extending
GEOLABS INC
W O 2951 120 Page 8
upward from the bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility
lines
lf the footings are designed in accordance with recommendations presented herein
we estimate total settlement on the order of inch with differential settlements on the order
of inch
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by frictional resistance
developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil and by passive earth
pressure acting against the near vertical faces of the embedded portion of footings
Resistance due to friction may be estimated using a coefficient of friction of 0 35 for
foundations bearing directly on the on site medium dense to very dense coral materials or
properly compacted base course material Resistance due to passive earth pressure may
be estimated using an equivalent fluid pressure of 350 pounds per square foot per foot of
depth pcf for footings embedded in the compacted base course or coral formation Unless
covered by pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the upper 12 inches below the
finished grade should be neglected
We recommend that foundation excavations be observed by a representative of
Geolabs Inc prior to placement of reinforcing steel and concrete to confirm the foundation
bearing conditions and the required embedment depths
Foundation Probing
To reduce the potential for loss of foundation support resulting from collapse of the
cavities or compression of loose zones below foundations and structures a separate
program of probing and grouting was conducted in December 2000 Based on our field
exploration cavities and or loose zones were present in the coral formations encountered
at the project site
A total of 26 probeholes were drilled to depths of approximately 10 feet below the
planned bottom of the footings Twenty four of the probeholes were drilled below the
GEOLABS INC

proposed water reservoir and the remainder was drilled below the proposed pump station
The probeholes were drilled using 4 inch diameter continuous flight augers Cavities voids
were encountered at the southeastern quadrant of the proposed reservoir Probeholes and
cavities were backfilled with sand cement grout injected at low to moderate pressures The
results of the probing and grouting program were presented on a separate report by our
office entitled Probing and Grouting for the Proposed 700 000 Gallon RO Storage Tank

Site Grading
Based on the proposed finish floor elevation and the existing grades at the reservoir
site we anticipate that site grading work for the proposed project will consist of minor cuts
and fills within the limits of the reservoir structure to achieve the design finish grades
At the on set of earthwork the areas within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed Vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable materials
should be removed and disposed of properly off site Soft and yielding areas encountered
during clearing and grubbing below areas designated to receive fill should be over excavated
to expose firm natural material and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with
well compacted engineered fill The excavated soft and or organic soils should be properly
disposed of off site or used in landscaping areas if appropriate
The on site clayey soils generated from the cut areas should not be used as fill and
backfill materials below the reservoir footprint They should be disposed of properly off site
or used in landscaping areas if appropriate Aggregate base course material should meet
the material requirements for Aggregate for Untreated Base Section 703 06 as specified
in the Hawaii Standard Specifications for Road Bridge and Public Works Construction
1994 Imported fill material if required should consist of non expansive imported granular
material such as crushed coral basalt or cinder sand The material should be well graded
from coarse to fine with no particles larger than 3 inches in largest dimension and should
contain between 10 and 30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve The material should
have a laboratory CBR value of 20 or more and should have a maximum swell of less than
GEOLABS INC
W O 2951 120 Page 10
1 percent Imported fill material should be tested by Geolabs Inc for conformance with
these recommendations prior to delivery to the project site for its intended use
Base course and imported granular fill materials should be moisture conditioned to
above the optimum moisture placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness
and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers
to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of
the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 971 test procedures Optimum
moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry
density Compaction should be accomplished by sheepsfoot rollers vibratory rollers or other
types of acceptable compaction equipment Water tamping jetting or ponding should not
be used to compact the fill materials
Site grading operations should be observed by a representative of Geolabs Inc It
is important that a representative from our office observe the site grading operation to
evaluate whether undesirable materials are encountered during the scarification and
excavation process and whether the exposed soil rock conditions are similar to those
encountered in our field exploration
Drainage
The finished grades outside the reservoir structure should be sloped to shed water
away from the foundations and slabs and to reduce the potential for ponding Excessive
landscape watering near the foundations and slabs should also be avoided These drainage
requirements are essential for the proper performance of the above foundation
recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation and
subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations should be properly
backfilled against the walls or slab footings immediately after setting of the concrete to
reduce water infiltration In addition drainage swales should be provided as soon as
possible and should be maintained to drain all surface run off away from slabs and
foundations

Utility Lines
We envision that new utility lines will be required for development of this project The
methods and equipment to be used for excavation should be determined by the contractor
subject to practical limits and safety considerations The excavation should comply with all
applicable local state and federal safety requirements Trench shoring design and
installation should be the responsibility of the contractor Trench shoring and bracing should
conform to the appropriate health and safety requirements
For support of the utility lines a granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine
gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation is recommended under the pipes Free draining
granular materials such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation should also
be used for the trench backfill up to about 12 inches above the pipes to provide adequate
support around the pipes and to reduce the potential for damaging the pipes from
compaction of the backfill It is critical that free draining granular materials be used to reduce
the potential of formation of voids below the haunches of pipes and to provide adequate
support for the sides of the pipes The use of on site soils as backfill immediately around
utility pipes is not recommended
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipes to the
top of the subgrade or finished grade should consist of on site soils or select granular
material generally less than 3 inches in maximum size The backfill material should be
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture placed in maximum 8 inch level loose
lifts and mechanically compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce
the potential for future ground subsidence Where trenches are below pavement areas the
upper 2 feet of the trench backfill below the pavement subgrade should be compacted to at
least 95 percent relative compaction Where the utility line crosses beneath the footings the
utility pipes should be concrete jacketed
Since competent coral formation was encountered at shallow depths during our field
explorations the trench work for utility installation may involve excavation in the coral
formation and may require hard ripping or hoerams Care must be exercised by the
GEOLABS INC

contractor to avoid over ripping which would disrupt the structure of the coral formation
resulting in a potential loss of bearing strength for footings in its vicinity
