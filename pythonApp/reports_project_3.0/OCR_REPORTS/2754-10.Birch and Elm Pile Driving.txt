production pile driving operations for the referenced project on 28 29

6 7 and 10 were driven without predrilling The remaining production piles were driven

reference

Test Pile Driving Report
The Birch and Elm Condominium
Honolulu Oahu Hawaii
In accordance with our proposal dated we have observed the
test pile driving on December 9 and 10 1992 at the above referenced project site
A Vulcan No 1 air steam pile hammer was used to drive the test piles at the
locations shown on Plate 1 The piles were 12 inch square precast and prestressed
concrete piles which were cast to 45 feet length Based on the information provided by
the pile driving contractor it is our understanding that the pile hammer was capable of
delivering a rated energy of about 15 000 foot pounds One copy of the test pile driving
records are attached for your reference
A total of eight 8 test piles were driven to approximately 8 5 to 20 feet below the
design cut off elevations High blow counts which were in excess of 120 blows per foot
were recorded between 8 5 to 10 0 feet at pile numbers 39 and 47 during the test pile
driving operation The remaining test piles were embedded between 16 5 to 19 5 feet
below the existing ground
Static Pile Load Test
In accordance with the project specifications a static pile load test was performed
on pile number 39 The pile load test results were presented in a separate report to the
pile driving contractor Hawaiian Dredging and Construction Company A copy of the
report is enclosed for your reference
Based on the results from the load test we recommend that pile numbers 39 and
47 be retapped and driven to the ground If the two 2 piles can be driven further it

Additional Boring Exploration
Additional boring exploration was made on in an attempt to
explore the subsurface soil conditions adjacent to pile numbers 39 and 47 In general
data obtained from our exploration indicated the presence of a thin layer of coral detritus
formation from about 8 5 to 12 0 feet MSL It is our opinion that piles embedded in this
thin coral formation would not develop sufficient capacity
Predrilling
Based on the boring data and pile load test results predrilling through the thin
coral formation at about 8 5 to 12 0 feet MSL is recommended for each pile We
recommend a minimum predrill diameter of 17 inch and a predrill depth of 12 feet below
the existing ground surface After installation of piles in the predilled holes each pile
location should be backfilled with clean fine sand up to the existing ground prior to
driving After the driving clean fine sand should be backfilled up to the pile cap elevation
Production Pile
Based on the boring data test pile observations and pile load test results a
production pile length of 25 feet may be used The pile may develop the allowable pile
capacity by end bearing on the coral formation
The production pile driving may be terminated when it has been driven a minimum
of 17 feet and a minimum driving resistance of 120 blows per foot

Pile Load Test Report
Birch and Elm Condominium
Honolulu Oahu Hawaii
This report presents the results of a pile load test performed on December 11
1992 at the project site
The test pile number 39 was loaded in accordance with the standard loading
procedure of ASTM Test Method D 1443 in 10 ton increments Excessive settlement
occurred when the loading was increased to 50 ton Further attempts to increase the
loading resulted in increasing pile settlement Due to excessive settlement the load test
was stopped and the test results are as follows
Pile No 39
Location Grid Lines D 2 5
Driven Depth Feet 10 5 below ground
Tip Elevation Feet 2 8 Mean Sea Level
Design Load Tons 40
Test Load Tons 80
Total Settlement Inch 2 3 plus
Based on the test results it is our opinion that the test pile cannot support its
design capacity of 40 tons since the pile settled excessively before reaching twice the
design load
