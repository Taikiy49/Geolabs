This report summarizes our earthwork observation and testing services performed
during the site grading operations for the Pearl City Gateway project from May 19 2008
through August 20 2008 in accordance with the consultant agreement dated
May 19 2008
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project is situated on about 13 5 acres identified as Manana Parcel B at the
intersection of Kuala Street and Acacia Road in the Manana area of Pearl City on the
Island of Oahu Hawaii The development will consist of several buildings with a total
planned footprint area of approximately 150 000 square feet In addition an asphaltic
concrete paved parking area for over 680 parking stalls and other related infrastructures
also are planned The building structures will be a combination of concrete masonry shear
walls and steel columns supporting roof loads by means of steel joist girders and steel bar
joists
Based on the existing topography and the finished floor elevation for the future
Shops Pad the grading consisted of fills from 0 to 3 feet in height to achieve the design
grades Imported non expansive select granular fill was used to fill the top 12 inches of the
building pads

EARTHWORK OPERATIONS
Royal Contracting Company performed the site grading operations pavement
construction and utility installation The site grading consisted of clearing and grubbing
subgrade preparation placement and compaction of fills and backfills pavement
construction and utility trench excavation and backfill
Following the clearing and grubbing operations the existing ground was generally
over excavated where necessary to approximately 12 inches below the slab cushion
within the building limits to provide the recommended 12 inch thick non expansive select
granular fill layer The over excavated subgrades were moisture conditioned to above the
optimum moisture content and compacted to at least 90 percent of the maximum dry
density as determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor
During placement of the non expansive select granular fill layer the fill materials
were generally placed in near level lifts brought to above the optimum moisture content
and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density determined in
accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor
During the site grading operations we obtained samples of the on site and
imported materials utilized as fill and backfill materials and transported them to our
laboratory for testing The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were
established in our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor
and the test results are summarized on Plate 2 of this report
Field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations to confirm
our field observations A total of 213 field density tests including retests of failed areas
were performed using the nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM D 2922 and
D 3017 Results of the compaction tests performed for the entire project are summarized
on the attached Plates 3 1 through 3 11 These tests were performed on the prepared
ground surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills at the site Test locations and
elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes These
locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the
methods used Areas assessed as unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified
minimum compaction was obtained
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the earthwork operations during the site grading of
the future Shops Pad observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report
were performed in general accordance with the intent of the project plans and
specifications and our geotechnical engineering recommendations

LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork may
have been performed without our observation However the level of earthwork
observation and testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some
of the geotechnical risks to the project It should be recognized that there are variations
in the accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the
earthwork construction and materials
Our services were performed in accordance with generally accepted standards of
geotechnical engineering practice no warranty is expressed or implied Provision of
construction monitoring by an engineer is not insurance nor does it guarantee
construction of any type The contractor shall retain responsibility for the quality of the
work for adhering to the plans and specifications and for repairing defects regardless of
when they are found
