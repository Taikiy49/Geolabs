PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The new GSA Office Building project site is at 91 1300 Enterprise Avenue
Parcel 12B in the former Naval Air Station Barbers Point in the Kapolei area of the
District of Ewa on the Island of Oahu Hawaii It is approximately bounded by Franklin D
Roosevelt Avenue to the north Enterprise Avenue to the east and open land along the
south and west sides The project site is approximately rectangular in shape
encompassing approximately 10 acres and presently consists of vacant land with
moderate vegetation toward the western portion of the site We understand the new GSA
Office Building project plan calls for the development of the following structures A layout
of the new facility is shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
A four story office building on the east side of the site
A one story link annex on the west side of the site

EARTHWORK OPERATIONS
The general contractor for the project is Archer Western Charles Pankow Builders
Joint Venture JJS Construction Ltd performed the site grading operations foundation
excavation pavement construction and utility installation The site grading consisted of
clearing and grubbing footing excavation subgrade preparation placement and
compaction of fills and backfills pavement construction and utility trench excavation and
backfill
Following the clearing and grubbing operations the existing ground was generally
over excavated where necessary to the subgrade elevations within the building and
pavement limits The coral formation exposed at the over excavated subgrades was then
proof rolled with a minimum 10 ton sheepsfoot and or vibratory drum rollers to detect the
presence of potential near surface cavities sinkholes or loose zones New fills required
for the project generally consisted of the excavated in situ coralline materials or imported
non expansive select granular materials The excavated in situ coralline materials were
generally screened of the over sized materials and or processed to a maximum particle
size of 3 inches or less
During placement of the non expansive select granular fill layer the fill materials
were generally placed in near level lifts brought to above the optimum moisture content
and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent of the maximum dry density determined in
accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor The base course and aggregate
subbase under the AC pavement was generally placed in near level lifts brought to above
the optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent of the
maximum dry density determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor
During the foundation excavation and preparation the excavated footing bottom was
generally recompacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
New utility connections were installed to serve the new development In general a
bedding layer consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel was provided below the piping
and manholes for uniform support Free draining granular materials consisting of
No 3B Fine gravel was also used for the trench backfill up to about 12 inches above the
pipes to provide adequate support around the pipes and to reduce the compaction effort
on the initial trench backfill The trench backfill was placed in 8 inch level loose lifts and
mechanically compacted to no less than 90 percent relative compaction Where trenches
are below pavement areas the upper 3 feet of the trench backfill below the pavement
grade was compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
During the site grading operations samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our

Several cavities were encountered in the subsurface during the project
construction A cavity approximately 18 inches in diameter and 5 feet deep was
encountered at the bottom of the sewer line which is about 11 feet from the existing
grade Due to the substantial depth of the cavity from the existing grade we
recommended that the cavity be filled with flowable fill CLSM or lean concrete A second
cavity approximately 12 to 15 inches in diameter and about 12 feet deep was exposed at
the existing ground surface located at about 15 feet outside from the northeastern corner
of the Office Building We recommended that the cavity be filled with flowable fill CLSM
or lean concrete Another cavity was encountered at inlet C4 which is approximately 4 to
5 feet wide and about 4 deep Due to the limited depth of the cavity we recommended
removing the ground cover and loose materials to expose the bottom of the cavity The
resulting excavation was backfilled with on site coralline material placed in 10 inch loose
lifts and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
Field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations on an
as need basis to confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures
and soil conditions were noted A total of 667 field density tests including retests of failed
areas were performed using the nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM D 6938
Results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 40 of
this report These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on
constructed fills and backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by
pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should
be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed
as unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained
