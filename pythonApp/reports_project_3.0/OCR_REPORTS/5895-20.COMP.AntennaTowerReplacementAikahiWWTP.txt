EARTHWORK OBSERVATION AND COMPACTION TESTING REPORT
ANTENNA TOWER REPLACEMENT AT AIKAHI WWTP SITE
Dear Mr Crain
This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations and testing
services performed during site grading and utility construction for the Aikahi Antenna
Tower Replacement project from January 5 to May 1 2009 in accordance with our fee
proposal dated
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The existing antenna facility is within the Aikahi Kailua WWTP site on the Island
of Oahu Hawaii The project will replace the existing three leg 100 foot antenna with a
new four leg 120 foot antenna tower The new tower will be designed to withstand a
maximum wind speed of 155 mph Additionally the one story CMU building will be
renovated and expanded
Based on the existing topography we anticipate that fills and cuts on the order of
about 1 feet or less would be required to achieve the design grades
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services for
the site grading operations and utility installation
SITE GRADING AND UTILITY INSTALLATION
We observed the site grading and trench backfill work for the Aikahi Antenna
Tower Replacement project from January 5 to May 1 2009 The site grading work
consisted of clearing and grubbing and placement and compaction of fills on the order
of about 1 foot thick to achieve the design finished grades Summit Construction Inc
performed the site grading and utility installation work During the site grading
operations laboratory testing results were also provided by Summit Construction Inc

The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in general
accordance with ASTM D 1557 and the results are summarized on Plate 3
At the sidewalk area around the existing building and the building extension the
fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture content
and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as determined
in accordance with ASTM D 1557 In the Antenna tower pad and utility trench areas the
fill and backfill materials were compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative density
During the site grading and utility backfilling operations periodic field density
tests were performed in general accordance with ASTM D 6938 Nuclear Gauge
Method The compaction test results are summarized on the attached Plate 4 These
tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and
backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and
interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed
as unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained
and or approved by our field representative based on visual observation and hand
probing
Our services consisted of intermittent field observation of the contractor s fill
placement subgrade preparation and compaction procedures and confirmation of
subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above grading operations
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the site grading and trench backfill work for the
above project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report were
performed in conformance with the general intent of the recommendations in the
following references
