Gentlemen
This letter presents the results of our review and analysis
of the proposed Waialae Shopping Center development located
in Kaimuki Honolulu Hawaii
The purpose of this report was to provide preliminary foun
dation design recommendations for the proposed development
The basis of our soil evaluation consists of six 15 foot
deep borings drilled at the site in April 1978 The purpose
of drilling the borings at that time was to obtain a general
subsurface site condition for the design of the shopping
center development and to locate the underlying basalt layer
No laboratory tests were made on the soil and rock samples

PLANNED DEVELOPMENT
The present plan dated August 31 1978 indicated a 3 story
high reinforced concrete structure approximately 390 by 90
feet in plan dimension to be located in the northern front
portion of the site near Waialae Avenue A 2 story parking
structure will be located on the rear portion of the site
The lower levels of the shopping center and parking struc
tures will be constructed below grade
Relatively high column loads are anticipated for the shopping
center structure
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located on Waialae Avenue between 4th
and Sth Avenues in Kaimuki Oahu Hawaii
The site is relatively flat sloping gently to the north
mauka at about a 6 percent gradient The surveyed eleva
tions range from about 90 to 112 feet
At the time of the field exploration the site contained
several l story wooden structures and was covered with
numerous large trees Numerous loose boulders and several
rock outcrops were noted at the site

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Subsurface Exploration
Six test borings were drilled at the site to a depth of
15 feet below the existing ground surface using 4 inch
continuous flight auger and BX core barrel with a diamond
bit The approximate locations of the borings are shown
on the Site Plan Plate 1
The subsurface conditions were explored by obtaining BX
core samples standard split spoon samples or 2 4 inch
I D split barrel samples as shown on the boring logs
Plates A 1 through A 5
Geology
The project site is located at the western Ewa edge of
a small shield volcano of the post erosional Honolulu
Series The volcano consists of a series of lava flows
of olivine basalt that covered the Kaimuki area and
relocated Palolo Stream to the west From a geologic
analysis of the area it is estimated that the basalt
in this area is 30 to 40 feet thick resting on an allu
vial soil layer at depth Lava flows of this nature are
laid down over a long period of time so that layers of
soil or alluvium may accumulate randomly in the dense
basalt

SOIL CONDITIONS
Based on the test borings the site is underlain predo
minantly by dense hard basalt to the extent of the
borings 15 feet Several thin stiff to hard clayey
soil layers were encountered in the upper 8 feet in
Borings 1 and 5 Dense decomposed rock was encountered
in the upper 8 feet of Borings 4 and 6 Underlying the
top 8 feet of the site is generally a massive dense basalt
formation of unknown thickness
ENGINEERING RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the results of the previously drilled boring data
the following recommendations are presented
A Foundations
For the proposed parking and shopping center structures
all spread or continuous footings should be designed
to bear on the underlying basalt Based on the present
data it is our opinion that an allowable bearing pres
sure of 12 000 p s f may be used on fractured basalt
Footing excavation should be examined for faults joints
or local soft areas due to weathering The excavations
should be relatively free of large surface irregularities
and should be cleaned out carefully

To reduce the possibility of footing settlement due to
possible near surface cavities clay layer or lava
tube in the underlying lava rock we recommend to
probe footing excavations with a jack hammer at
frequent intervals or at each column location
The probe hole should have a minimum diameter of
2 inches and extend below the bottom of the footing
a depth equals to 2 times the footing width or a
minimum of 5 feet The probe holes should be grouted
from the bottom of the hole using low pressures
If a cavity is suspected below a footing additional
probe holes should be made to delineate the horizontal
extent of the cavity The probing and grouting opera
tions should be monitored by a soils engineer
Settlements
For the above recommendations total and differential
settlements for the proposed structures will be
negligible
Retaining Walls
The recommendations given under foundations for the
structures should be followed for the design of the
retaining wall footings

For lateral earth pressures the following design
values are recommended
Walls unrestrained at the top 35 p c f equivalent
fluid pressure
Walls restrained at the top 45 p c f equivalent
fluid pressure
The walls should be backfilled with granular material
such as base course rock sand or cinder
Weepholes or a subsurface drainage system should be used
to prevent the build up of hydrostatic pressure behind
the walls
For resistance to lateral loads the following criteria
may be used
Coefficient of friction in basalt 0 7
Coefficient of friction in soil 0 4
Passive pressure in basalt 6000 p s f
Passive pressure in soil 300 p s f

and parking areas should be compacted in 8 inch
lifts to 90 of the laboratory maximum density
using ASTM test method D 1557 as a standard

ADDITIONAL SOIL ENGINEERING SERVICES
It is recommended that the footing excavation and probing
be observed by a soils engineer during construction to see
that adequate bearing has been obtained and that the sub
surface conditions are consistent with the findings of
the drilled test borings If unanticipated conditions
are encountered we would be able to modify our recommen
dations as appropriate
Any imported material required should be non expansive and
should be tested and approved in writing by the soils engin
eer prior to use on site

mendations made in accordance with generally accepted soil
and foundation engineering principles and practices This
warranty is in lieu of all other warranties either expressed
or implied

are based on our site reconnaissance and soil information
from existing boring data
