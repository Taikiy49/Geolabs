INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our soil and foundation
investigation performed for the proposed classroom addition at Laie
Elementary School located in Laie Oahu Hawaii
The general location of the project site and its vicinity is shown on
the Project Location Map Plate 1

PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
The proposed addition will be a two story concrete classroom
structure approximately 30 by 95 feet in plan Maximum column loads of
75 kips and wall loads of 4 kips per foot are anticipated for the
structure
An elevator system is planned at the northeastern corner of the
addition To install the system a 4 feet excavation is required
Maximum loading of 11 8 kips due to the system is anticipated
No grading work is planned for the site
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our investigation was to evaluate the subsurface
conditions at the site to develop soil parameters for the design of the
foundations for the proposed structure
The scope of our investigation included supervising the drilling and
sampling of two 2 borings performed by a drilling contractor labora
tory testing of samples obtained engineering evaluation of the field and
laboratory data development of design recommendations and report
preparation

Detailed descriptions of the field exploration log of borings and
laboratory test results are presented in the appendices of this report
SITE DESCRIPTION
The Laie Elementary School is located on Iosepa Street about 500
feet west of Kamehameha Highway
The site for the proposed addition is located in the northern portion
of the school grounds adjacent to an existing two story classroom
building constructed in 1982 This existing building appeared to be in
good condition with only a few minor cracks which appear on the concrete
sidewalk
At present the site is occupied by two portable classroom
buildings The ground is in general level and the area to the north
east and west of the portable classrooms is covered by grass An AC
paved driveway forms the south boundary of the site
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The project site is located in the lowlying coastal area around the
northeastern portion of Oahu
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1483 00 Page 4
The area is underlain by alluvial deposits derived from the
weathering of upper coral formations and dune sand blown inland from the
beach areas The loose nature of the subsoils and the soft clay noted in
some of the samples indicate that the subsoils were deposited under water
and not subjected to substantial surcharge loading
Subsoil Condition
The subsoil conditions at the site were explored by drilling and
sampling two 2 borings to a depth of 30 feet The drilling contractor
for this job was MAS Drilling Company The approximate boring locations
are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general the site is underlain by about 18 inches of medium dense
silty sand Underneath the silty sand lies a 12 to 15 foot thick layer
of coral sand and gravel with consistency varying from medium dense at
the near surface elevation to very dense near the bottom of the layer
Underneath the dense sand Boring 1 encountered a layer of lagoonal
Geposit composed mainly of 4 feet of soft to medium soft sandy clay
overlying about 5 feet of loose coral sand In Boring 2 only traces of
clayey material was noted in the medium dense coral sand
From depths of 22 feet downward both borings encountered dense to
medium dense gravelly coral sand to their final depth of 30 feet In
general the density of the soil increased with increasing depth

Groundwater level was monitored during the exploration and was found
to vary according to tidal condition A high of 5 6 feet and a low level
of 6 feet from the ground surface were recorded
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATTON
The field exploration disclosed competent sandy subsoils in the near
surface areas at the site
Recompaction of the building site by the use of a vibratory roller is
recommended to densify the near surface soil and to create a uniform
bearing layer for the proposed structure
Foundation Supports
The borings indicated that the site was underlain by medium dense to
very dense coral sand and gravel between feet and 13 feet from the
surface Therefore we believe that shallow spread or continuous wall
footing foundation bearing on this in situ material may be used to
support the proposed classroom addition
An allowable bearing value of up to 3000 p s f is recommended for
footing resting on the dense coral sand
The bottoms of the footings should be placed at least 2 feet below
the finish grade Also the bottom of the footing excavation should be
recompacted to achieve at least 90 of the maximum dry density
established by the ASTM D 1557 78 method

Settlement
Two types of settlements namely immediate and consolidation
settlements are anticipated For both the spread and wall footings
an estimate of immediate settlement due to the initial application of
loading from the building structure is about half an inch Consolida
tion settlement may occur when the underlying layer of soft sandy clay is
compressed Total settlement estimated to be between half of an inch to
an inch may take about 4 to 6 months to complete Final differential
settlement after the completion of the consolidation settlement is
anticipated to be on the order of about 3 4 inch
Lateral Resistance
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive
earth pressure developed against the buried vertical faces of the
foundation system and by friction resistance developed between the bottom
of the foundations and the bearing soil
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be calculated using an
equivalent fluid pressure of 300 p c f This assumes that the soil
around the foundation is well compacted The frictional resistance may
be calculated by using a coefficient of friction of 0 4 The above
resistance values are maximum values and do not contain a factor of
safety

Slab On Grade
Slab on grade should be placed over a 4 inch layer of base course
generally less than 3 4 inch and larger than 1 4 inch in size The
subgrade should be recompacted prior to placing the base course To
protect against moisture damage of the flooring material a polyethylene
moisture barrier should be used
Elevator
Boring 2 indicated that the concrete platform for the elevator system
will bear on the in situ dense coral sand above the water table An
allowable bearing capacity of 3000 p s f on the dense sand can be used
The bottom of the excavation should be thoroughly tamped prior to
placement of reinforcing steel
Settlement due to the anticipated loading of 11 8 kips from the
elevator system is estimated to be less than 1 4 inch and is expected to
eccur on initial application of loading
Site Grading
We understand that no major site cutting or filling is planned
Site Preparation
Prior to any earthwork the site should be cleared of all

vegetation debris and old foundations and sidewalks The
existing ground should then be scarified to a depth of at least
six inches moisture conditioned and recompacted by using a
vibratory roller to achieve a minimum of 90 relative compaction

Backfill Material
Select on site sandy material can be reused as structural
fills Any imported fill material if required should be
granular such as coral or tuff free of organic material
non expansive and generally less than 6 inches in size
Design Review
We recommend that plans and specifications for foundation
construction be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written
comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our recom
mendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that footing excavation and backfilling operations
be observed by the soils engineer during construction to see that ade
quate compaction has been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are
consistent with the findings of our investigation The recommendations
given in this report are contingent upon the above inspections

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling
2 borings to a depth of 30 feet below the existing ground surface at the
approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 Due to the
existing portable classrooms the borings could not be drilled within the
proposed building site However they were placed as close as
practicable The drilling operation was carried out by MAS Drilling
Company A truck mounted equipment was used to drill the borings
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by visual
and textural examination in the field by our engineer who continuously
supervised the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory The soils
were Classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil Classifi
cation System A graphic presentation of the materials encountered is
presented on the Log of Borings Plates A 1 and A 2
Soil samples were obtained from the boring by driving a standard
penetration sampler or a 2 4 inch I D split barrel sampler with a 140
pound hammer free falling at a height of 30 inches The blow counts to
drive the sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the Log of Borings at
the appropriate sample depths

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content and unit weight determinations were performed
on selected samples to aid in the classification and evaluation of soil
properties The results of these tests are presented on the Log of
Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Three sieve analyses were performed on the upper fill soil to
determine the grain size distribution of the soil The test results are
summarized on Plate B 1
One Atterberg limit test was performed on the sandy clay
material to determine its change of consistency with change of water
content The test result is summarized on Plate B 1
Two direct shear tests were performed on undisturbed samples to
evaluate the strength characteristics of the fill soil The test results
are summarized on Plates B 2 and B 3
