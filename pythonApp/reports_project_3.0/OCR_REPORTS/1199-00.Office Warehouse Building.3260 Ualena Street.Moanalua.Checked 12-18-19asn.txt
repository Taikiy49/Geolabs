INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our foundation investigation for
the proposed fourth floor addition at the existing office warehouse
Oahu Hawaii The vicinity of the project site is shown on the
Project Location Plan Plate 1
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The general layout of the existing building is shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2 which is based on a set of copies of original drawings dated
reduced to about half size for our reference
w Page 2
We understand that the proposed fourth floor addition will cover fron
Lines B to E and from Lines 9 to 12 over the existing 3 story office
structure No previous soils information was available at the time of
our investigation The available drawings indicate that all footings
to bear on firm and undisturbed soil with a minimum bearing capacity
of 4000 p s f and that Where bearing capacity does not fullfill
these minimum requirements the Contractor shall notify the Engineer
who will revise the elevations and or design of footings Because
there was no indication of final depths or sizes of footings it was
not clear whether any changes had been made
It is our understanding that no additional foundation work is
considered for the proposed fourth floor addition at this time and
that the existing footings will need to carry the added loading
Thus the conditions of the existing footings need to be evaluated
We further understand that the proposed addition will use relatively
light weight materials to reduce the added loading
PURPOSE AND SCOPE OF SERVICE
The purpose of our investigation was to explore the subsurface
conditions and expose selected existing footings to examine the
foundation conditions to provide our geotechnical recommendations
W O 1199 00 Page 3
Three borings were drilled to depths ranging from 24 0 feet to 30 5
feet Subsoil samples were obtained from the borings for visual
examination and laboratory testing We further excavated 3 test pits
and partially exposed 3 footings to examine the depths sizes and the
thickness of footings exposed in the test pits The locations of our
borings and test pits are shown on Plate 2 The sketches of footings
exposed in the test pits are presented on Plate 3 Description of our
field exploration and test pit examinations the boring and test pit
logs laboratory testing and test results are presented in the
appendices Our findings are summarized and recommendations presented
in this report
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions disclosed by our borings consist of about 6
inches of pavement section about 2 feet of stiff clay and silt fill
and about 2 feet of stiff to very stiff gray brown expansive silty
clay underlain by weathered volcanic tuff at about 5 foot depth The
tuff is about 8 feet thick weathered and moderately hard The tuff
is underlain by gray brown very stiff clayey silt about 6 feet thick
and coral deposit to 30 5 feet The maximum depth explored A large
cavity was encountered in Boring 1 and also in Boring 2 near the top
of coral deposit These cavities are probably localized and not
interconnected No cavity was found in Boring 3
W O 1199 00 Page 4
Groundwater was encountered at about 17 feet below the ground The
actual depth measured are shown on the log of borings

FOUNDATION SUPPORT
Generally the weathered volcanic tuff formation at a depth of about 5
feet would provide excellent foundation support We believe that an
allowable soil bearing pressure of at least 7 000 p s f may be used
if footings are founded in this volcanic tuff Settlement would be
negligible in this case This allowable bearing in tuff is well in
excess of the 4 000 p s f bearing pressure stipulated in the drawings
of the original building If footings were founded on stiff to very
stiff silty clay just above the volcanic tuff the allowable bearing
settlement to be anticipated under the added load Such a condition
is possibly encountered in Test Pit 4 as discussed later in the
report
Although cavities were disclosed in the coral deposits at about
20 foot depths in our borings we believe that they are discontinuous
and localized Furthermore we believe that the cavities are limited
W O 1199 00 Page 6
in the coral deposits only and are sufficiently deep below the
existing footings so that these cavities will not Significantly affect
the footings We believe that no further action will be required for
these cavities at this time
As for the conditions of existing footings examined in our test pits
the large footing at Column D 11 appears to have the plan dimensions
slightly larger than those shown on the set of drawings However the
edge thickness of concrete footing was measured to be only 12 inches
while the drawings called for 24 inches The materials under the edge
of footing were found to consist of very stiff gray silty clay for
about 12 inches underlain by volcanic tuff
The above condition is somewhat puzzling as the wall footings examined
in Test Pits 5 and 6 were apparently placed deep enough to rest
directly on the competent tuff and were of sufficient thicknesses in
general conformance with the dimensions shown on the drawings It
would appear that the large footing would also be of the thickness
called for in the original drawings It is possible that the footing
might have been excavated to tuff having the thickness called for in
the drawings but edges may have sloughed at the footing excavation
bottom and become thinner
W O 1199 00 Page 7
We believe that the above recommended follow up footing thickness
inspection work can be made during construction although it would be
preferable to examine it prior to finalizing the design
Design and Construction Review
Plans and specifications for the fourth floor addition should be
forwarded to the soils engineer for review and comments prior to
construction to see that the intent of our recommendations is included
in the project plans and specifications
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the test pit excavations and backfilling
operations or test coring operations be observed by the soils engineer
during construction This is to see that the subsurface conditions
are consistent with the findings of our subsurface investigation and
that adequate compaction has been obtained
INVESTIGATION LIMITATIONS
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations made
in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation engineering
principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all other
warranties either expressed or implied

The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are based on
our site reconnaissance soil information from boring and test pit and
laboratory tests obtained from this soil investigation Our
professional opinions and recommendations are addressed to the
proposed fourth floor addition only but not to the existing

Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encountered and cannot be
fully determined by reconnaissance and soil borings These unforeseen

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling
0 to 30 5 feet below the existing ground surface
and excavating 3 test pits to expose the existing footings at
the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Truck imounted equipment was used to drill the borings and
excavate test pits
The soils rock encountered in the borings were classified by
visual and textural examination in the field by one of our
engineers who continuously monitored the drilling operations
The classifications were further reviewed by visual inspection
and testing in the laboratory The soils were classified in
general conformance with the Unified Soil Classification
System A graphic presentation of the materials encountered is
presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 3
Soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving a
hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts to drive the sampler
12 inches are shown on the log of borings at the appropriate
sample depths
