INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the Upgrade of Underground Fuel Storage Tank project at the Wahiawa
Wastewater Treatment Plant located in the District of Wahiawa on the island of Oahu
Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering recom
mendations resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering
analyses These recommendations are intended for design input of site grading
excavation and backfill only The findings and recommendations presented herein are
subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
We understand that it is proposed to improve the existing underground fuel
storage tank system at the Wahiawa Wastewater Treatment Plant in the District of
Wahiawa on the island of Oahu Hawaii Based on the latest information provided we
understand that the proposed project will consist of improvements to the existing tank
only which include replacement of the pipings and connections The approximate
location of the existing tank is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Detailed information for the project was not available at the time this report was
prepared Based on the available information we understand that the existing
underground fuel storage tank has plan dimensions of approximately 20 feet long by
8 feet wide and is embedded about 3 to 4 feet below the existing ground surface In
order to improve the existing underground fuel storage tank system we envision that

be required

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lavas from
two shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated
to be of the middle to late Pliocene in age and forms the bulk of the western third of the
island The younger shield Koolau is estimated to be of late Pliocene to early
Pleistocene Ice Age and forms the majority of the eastern two thirds of the island
Waianae became extinct while Koolau was still active and its eastern flank was
partially buried below Koolau lavas banking against its eastern flank These banked
or ponded lavas formed a broad plateau referred to as the Schofield Plateau on which
the project site is located
The project area is composed of basaltic rock built by the extrusion of the lavas
of the Koolau Volcanic Series These rocks are generally characterized by flows of
jointed dense vesicular basalt with interbedded thin clinker layers In situ chemical
GEOLABS HAWAIi

weathering of the Koolau lava flows has occurred for the last 1 to 2 million years The
weathering process has formed a mantle of residual soils which grade to saprolite with
depth In general saprolite is composed mainly of silty material and is typical of the
tropical weathering of volcanic rocks The saprolite grades to basaltic rock formation
with depth

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
In general our field exploration indicates that the proposed project site is
underlain by hard residual soils consisting silty clays and clayey silts extending to the
maximum depths drilled of about 36 feet below the existing ground surface
Groundwater was encountered only in Boring No 1 at a depth of about 30 feet below
the ground surface at the time of our field exploration
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 3364 00 E Page 7
We anticipate that the existing tank structure is embedded about 3 to 4 feet
below the existing ground surface Therefore we envision that an excavation on the
order of about 5 to 6 feet below the ground surface will likely be required for the
proposed improvements to the existing underground fuel storage tank system
Based on the soil conditions encountered in our borings we believe that an open
cut may be used for the proposed excavation with a slope inclination of H 1V or flatter
if construction space is available If construction space is limited shoring of the
excavation during construction may be needed The on site soils generated from the
excavation may be reused as a source of backfill material The excavation should be
properly backfilled with compacted on site soils and or imported materials
Detailed discussions and recommendations for design of site grading excavation
and backfill and other geotechnical aspects of the project are presented in the following
sections of this report
Site Grading
Details of site grading were not available at the time this report was prepared
However we anticipate an excavation of up to about 5 to 6 feet relative to the existing
ground surface and subsequent backfilling will be required for the project construction
Site grading operations should be observed by a representative of Geolabs Hawaii
The following recommendations are intended to provide guidelines for the design of site
grading work
1 At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits
should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris
rubbish and other unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed
of off site
GEOLABS HAWAN
W O 3364 00 E Page 8
2 The on site soils generated from the excavation may be reused as a
source of fill and backfill material provided that they are free of particles
greater than 3 inches in largest dimension
3 Imported fill and or backfill material should consist of well graded granular
materials of less than 3 inches in maximum dimension The material
should have a laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR value of 15 or
more and should have a maximum swell of less than 1 percent when
tested in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1883 In addition
it should contain between 10 and 30 percent passing the No 200 sieve
Imported fill material should be tested by Geolabs Hawaii for conformance
with our recommendations prior to delivery to the project site for its
intended use
4 All fills and backfills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches
in loose thickness moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as
a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil determined in
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91 Optimum moisture
is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the
maximum dry density Fill placement should commence at the lowest
point and continue upward in level compacted lifts
Excavation
In order to improve the existing underground fuel storage tank system we
anticipate that an excavation on the order of about 5 to 6 feet below the existing ground
surface will likely be required for the project construction In general the method and
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 3364 00 E Page 9
equipment to be used for excavation should be determined by the contractor subject
to practical limits and safety considerations In general our field exploration
encountered hard residual soils and fills consisting of silty clays and clayey silts
Therefore we anticipate that conventional excavation techniques may be used for the
proposed excavation
Based on the hard residual soils encountered at the project site we anticipate
that an open cut may be used for the proposed excavation if construction space is
available A slope inclination ratio of H 1V or flatter may be used for the temporary
cut in the hard residual soils If the open cut method is not practical for the proposed
excavation a temporary shoring system should be used for the proposed excavation
to protect personnel within the excavation The temporary shoring support should
conform to the requirements of local state and federal regulations Appropriate loads
due to surcharge equipment traffic or sloping ground conditions adjacent to the
excavations should be added to the design earth pressures Stockpiling of excavated
on site and or imported materials within a horizontal distance equal to the depth of the
excavation from the edge of the excavation should be avoided
Pavements
We envision that new flexible pavements may be needed for the proposed
project The new pavements should match the existing pavement sections or use the
following pavement section for preliminary design
Asphaltic Concrete Pavement
2 0 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 0 Inch Aggregate Base Course 95 percent relative compaction
8 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness on a Moist Compacted Subgrade

The pavement subgrade soils should be scarified to a depth of least 8 inches
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to not
less than 95 percent relative compaction The base course should consist of crushed
basalt aggregates compacted to not less than 95 percent relative compaction
Paved areas should be sloped and drainage gradients maintained to carry all
surface water off the site The recommended pavement section assumes that good
drainage will be provided for areas adjacent to the pavements Surface water ponding
should not be allowed anywhere on the site during or after construction
Utility Trench
We envision that new utility lines will be required for the proposed project In
general for support of the utility lines we recommend that a granular bedding
consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation be used
under the pipes The backfill up to about 1 foot above the pipes should consist of free
draining backfills such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation to
reduce the potential for damaging the pipes from compaction of the backfill
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 1 foot above the pipes to
the top of the subgrade or finished grade should consist of select granular material or
the on site soils Where encountered soft and or wet excavated soils should not be
used as trench backfill The backfill should be moisture conditioned to above the
optimum moisture content placed in maximum 8 inch level loose lifts and mechanically
compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for
future ground subsidence Where trenches are below pavement areas the upper 2 feet
of the trench backfill below the pavement subgrade should be compacted to not less
than 95 percent relative compaction

Plate B 3

support Results of the CBR test are presented on Plate B 4
