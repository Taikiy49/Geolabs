As regested we have performed a soil investigation and a pavement
design for the above site Our work was done in general accordance with
our verbal proposal of the 6th
The purpose of the investigation is to explore the subsurface
conditions at the site to provide soil data for use in the pavement
design
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests which we
used in the engineering analysis to formulate our conclusion and
recommendations are presented in the appendices of this report
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
Approximately 13 000 square yards of asphaltic concrete pavement
will be placed adjacent to Building X 31 of the Public Works Center The
completed area will be used as an open storage facility Anticipated
vehicle traffic will include ton capacity forklift trucks operating
at a moderate frequency
SITE DESCRIPTION
At the time our exploration work was carried out about two thirds
of the site were stockpiled with lumber In general the whole area is
flat with only very slight dipping towards the west Most vegetation
had been cleared and no utility line was encountered during the entire
operation
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
A total of 5 test pits were excavated at the site to depths of 1 0
to 2 0 feet at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate
1 Detailed descriptions of the soils encountered are presented on the
Test Pit Logs Plates A 1 and A 2 One laboratory CBR and one standard
compaction tests were carried out on representative samples obtained and
the results are presented on Plate B 1
Based on the results of the test pits most of the site is covered
by 1 to 1 5 feet of fill material which consists mainly of medium dense
sandy gravel with some silt Underneath the fill lies the medium dense
to dense volcanic tuff or mudrock No water was encountered at the
time of the exploration of those shallow pits

Site Grading
Site Preparation
At the onset of earthwork the area to receive pavement should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All existing vegetation
structures debris rubbish and any other unsuitable materials
should be removed and disposed of properly off site
Any soft or clayey subgrade soils encountered during the clearing
operations should also be removed and replaced by backfilling with
well compacted select fill
The subgrade soils should then be proof rolled by rollers to
achieve a uniform density which is at least 95 of the maximum dry
density as establish by ASTM D 1557 78 test method
Fill Material
Large amount of fill is not anticipated However if required
imported fill material should be tested and approved by the soils
engineer prior to its delivery to the project site In general
the 111 material should be non expansive less than 3 inches in
size and form a dense stable soil mass when compacted

The pavement design was based on a minimum subgrade CBR value of 26
for the storage area Therefore material placed at the finished
subgrade should match this CBR value and CBR swell values should be
less than 0 5 when compacted to 95 of the maximum dry density
Otherwise the above pavement design should be revised
Fill Placement
All iii material should be moisture conditioned prior to
compaction Moisture content should be within 3 percent of the
optimum
Design Review
Plans and specifications for site grading should be forwarded to
the soils engineer for review and written comments prior to construction
to see that the intent of our recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that excavation and placement of fill be observed
by the soils engineer during construction to see that adequate compaction
has been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with
the findings of our investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
If conditions encountered during construction are different from
those presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made

INVESTIGATION LIMITATIONS
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations
made in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation
engineering principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all
other warranties either expressed or implied
The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are based
on our site reconnaissance soil information from test pit and laboratory
test data obtained from this soil investigation
Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encountered and cannot
be fully determined by taking soil samples Unforeseen conditions such
as expansive soil and soft spots may occur in localized areas and may
require additional probing or corrections in the field Therefore
additional expenditures may be needed during construction to attain a
properly constructed project Some contingency fund is thus recommended
to accommodate these possible costs
