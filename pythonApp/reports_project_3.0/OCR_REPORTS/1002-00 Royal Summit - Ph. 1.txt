From to March 25 1982 we have inspected the
earthwork and grading operations at the above referenced site
This final report summarizes our inspection and compaction
tests performed at the above lots It should be noted that
Lots 112 through 118 were observed by another soil consultant
and are not included in this report
It is our opinion that as of this date the above referenced
lots are constructed to final grade and compacted adequately
The density test results for the in place fills are indicated
in the attached Density Test Summary A site plan indicating
the location and number of the lots is also attached
Community Planning Inc
W O 1002 00
The grading operations consisted of cuts and fills generally
less than 20 feet Prior to fill placement the existing
vegetation was removed and the exposed ground was scarified and
recompacted The fill was then placed in horizontal lifts and
compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density
During the fill placement operations periodic field density
tests were made in accordance with American Society for Testing
and Materials ASTM test designation D 1556 Sand Cone
method Based on the compaction test results and field
observations it is our opinion that the fill lots have been
compacted adequately
During the grading period samples of the on site soils were
tested prior to being used in the fill The maximum soil
density and optimum moisture content was established in our
laboratory in accordance with the ASTM D 1557 78 test
designation and the test results are as follows

During the site grading isolated pockets of expansive soil
were encountered These soils were removed within 3 feet of
finished grade and used in the deeper fill areas
Laboratory ring swell tests which were periodically performed
on the on site soils to evaluate their swelling potential
indicated that the soils generally have low swell potential at
or above optimum moisture content However if allowed to dry
some of the soils could become moderately expansive
RECOMMENDATIONS
House Foundations
Ft 1sour understanding that the subdivision lots ea be sold
as is with the individual lot purchasers providing their own
house design and construction Therefore details of the
future house design siting and site grading required are not
available at this time
In addition it should be noted that pockets of expansive soil
may have gone undetected below the cut lots and future site
grading may expose expansive soils which were buried a minimum
of 3 feet below the fill lots Therefore we recommend that
the future lot purchasers be advised to obtain the services of
a qualified soils engineer to aid in the design of future
construction and to provide inspection during the foundation
construction

Future Construction and Site Grading
Future building additions patio slabs swimming pools and
retaining walls should be designed by a qualified soils
engineer with consideration for the effects of the underlying
soil conditions at each lot
Subsequent to completion of lot grading utility trenches
within the lot pad should be properly backfilled and compacted
under the observation of a qualified soils technician
Without our knowledge and written approval this office assumes
no responsibility for any alterations made to slopes or pads on
the subject lots subsequent to the issuance of this report
We strongly suggest that all of the above referenced
recommendations and restrictions be made available to all
future lot and home purchasers of this subdivision so that
they would be advised to include the consultation of a
qualified professional in the planning design and construction
of any improvements Home owners should also be advised not to
block or alter surface drainage and to provide adequate
drainage provisions for gutters and planters to prevent ponding
of surface water especially next to foundations
