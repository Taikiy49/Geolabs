Dear Mr Postacchini
In accordance with your request we have provided geotechnical engineering
observation and consultation services during the drilled shaft foundation installation for the
CRU Vertical Exchanger project located within the Tesoro Hawaii Refinery in the Campbell
Industrial Park area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii This report summarizes our
geotechnical engineering observations performed during the drilled shaft foundation
installation for the above referenced project
A geotechnical engineering exploration was performed by Geolabs Inc and our
recommendations were presented in the report entitled Geotechnical Engineering
Exploration CRU Vertical Exchanger Tesoro Hawaii Refinery Campbell Industrial Park
Oahu Hawaii dated The purpose of our geotechnical engineering services
was to observe the drilled shaft foundation installation for general conformance with the
project plans and specifications
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located within the existing Tesoro Hawaii Refinery site in the
Campbell Industrial Park area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii A new CRU Vertical
Exchanger is planned at the project site The new vertical exchanger will have a diameter
ranging from about 4 7 to 6 feet and will be approximately 80 feet in height We
understand that the vertical exchanger will have an operating weight of about
275 200 pounds Based on the information provided the bottom of the foundation cap will
be set at about 4 feet below the existing pavement elevation
Based on the structural loads we recommended that a deep foundation system
consisting of cast in place concrete drilled shafts be used to support the new vertical
exchanger The cast in place concrete drilled shafts would derive vertical support
primarily from skin friction between the concrete shaft and the surrounding subsurface
materials Based on the force and moment demands provided by the project structural

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Based on the borings drilled at the site the project site is underlain by a thin layer of
surface fill materials overlying coral formation extending to the maximum depth explored of
about 51 feet below the existing ground surface The surface fills generally consisted of
dense silty gravel with sands extending down to about 1 5 feet below the ground surface
The coral formation encountered was generally medium hard to hard and ranged from
slightly to moderately weathered The coral formation encountered graded to severely
fractured from about 25 feet extending to the maximum depth explored of about 51 feet
Boring No 1
Groundwater was encountered at about 17 feet below the ground surface in the
borings drilled at the time of our field exploration It should be noted that the groundwater
level will fluctuate with tidal changes rainfall and other factors
DRILLED SHAFT FOUNDATION INSTALLATION
The installation of the drilled shafts was performed by Foundation International Inc
Four drilled shafts designated as Nos 1 through 4 were installed from July 24 through 26
2001 The approximate locations of the four drilled shafts are shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2 In general a representative from Geolabs observed the drilling installation and
concreting process during the construction of these drilled shafts Due to the limited
_ number of drilled shafts for this project a trial shaft and or a load test was not specified in
the contract documents for this project
The drilled shafts were drilled using a track mounted drill rig equipped with a
36 inch diameter core barrel and bucket auger The drilled shafts were drilled to depths
ranging from about 23 5 to 24 3 feet below the ground surface The drilled shafts
generally extended to a depth of at least 22 feet below the drilled shaft cap elevation Due
to the shallow groundwater conditions at the site concrete placement in the drilled shafts
was conducted using tremie methods The individual drilled shaft records are presented
Based on our field observations we believe that the four drilled shafts for the new
CRU vertical exchanger have been installed in general conformance with the project plans
and specifications and within the limits of generally accepted construction practices
Based on our observations of the installation it is our professional opinion that the drilled
shafts have been installed to the required diameter and depth to provide the ultimate load
capacity of 650 kips
