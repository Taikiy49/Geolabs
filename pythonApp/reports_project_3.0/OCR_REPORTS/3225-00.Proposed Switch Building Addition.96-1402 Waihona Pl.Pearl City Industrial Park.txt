INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Switch Building Addition in Pearl City on the island of Oahu
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 3225 00 Page 2
Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings from our field exploration and laboratory
testing and presents our design level geotechnical engineering recommendations derived
from our analyses for the proposed project These recommendations are intended for
design input only and may not be appropriate to form the basis for construction cost
estimates nor for construction bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed project site is located at Place within the Pearl City
Industrial Park on the island of Oahu Hawaii It is proposed to construct a new Switch
Building by the northeastern side of the existing Earth Station Building Due to potential
site flooding concern on the two sides of the property we understand that it is proposed
to raise the new building by placing approximately 3 feet of new fill at the project site
The proposed addition will be a two story structure covering 6 000 to 8 000 square
feet per floor We understand that the new building will be a concrete structure with slab
on grade construction Based on the information provided to us by the project structural
engineer maximum column loads are estimated to be on the order of 400 kips per
column with wall loads of approximately 12 kips per lineal foot
Our previous project experience in the area indicated that the project site may be
underlain by a man made fill up to 20 feet thick consisting of reddish brown clayey silt
material Particular attention was given to the condition of the existing fills previously
placed to develop the Pearl City Industrial Park project site
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 3225 00 Page 3
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions at the project site to develop an idealized soil data set for the formulation of
geotechnical engineering design recommendations pertinent to the design of the building
foundation and pavement section for the subject project The work was done in general
accordance with our revised fee proposal dated Our scope of work
included the following tasks and work efforts
1 Review of available soils and geologic information pertinent to the project
site and its vicinity
2 Mobilization and demobilization of a truck mounted drill rig and operators
to and from the project site
3 Drilling and sampling of six borings to depths ranging from 5 5 to 25 5 feet
below the existing ground surface for a total footage of 93 5 lineal feet of
exploration
4 Coordination of the field exploration and logging of the drilled borings by a
field engineer of our firm
5 Laboratory testing of selected samples obtained during the field exploration
to evaluate the classification and engineering properties of the materials
encountered
6 Analyses of the field and laboratory data for the purpose of developing
geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to the design of
building foundation and pavement section for the proposed project
GEOLABS HAWAII

7 Preparation of this report summarizing our work on the project and
presenting our findings and geotechnical engineering design recom
mendations
8 Coordination of our overall work on the project by a project manager from
our firm
9 Quality assurance of our work and client design team consultation by a
principal engineer of our firm
10 Miscellaneous work efforts such as drafting word processing and clerical
support
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration and Boring Logs are presented in

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located at Place within the Pearl City Industrial
Park on the island of Oahu Hawaii The site had previously been filled over a gully in the
mid 1970 s for the site grading of the industrial park subdivision
The project site is currently occupied by a microwave tower an 18 meter dish
antenna a one story Earth Station Building and a parking lot Most of the area to be
occupied by the new Switch Building is an open lawn except the area adjacent to the
existing building where transformers underground utilities and asphalt concrete pavement
are located
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 3225 00 Page 5
The existing ground surface at the proposed building location is relatively level with
the ground surface elevations on the order of 84 to 86 feet Mean Sea Level MSL
A concrete lined ditch is located on the northern side of the site at the toe of the existing
fill slope
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The project site is located in the Waiawa Stream valley which has been cut into the
southwestern flank of the Koolau Mountains The bottom of this valley had been filled
with alluvium deposited by Waiawa Stream In recent times portions of the valley have
been graded and filled by man
Our field exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling six borings to
depths ranging from 5 5 to 25 5 feet below the existing ground surface In general our
exploration encountered a man made fill up to 20 feet thick consisting of generally well
compacted clayey silt material Groundwater was not encountered in the drilled borings
at the time of our field exploration Groundwater levels are expected to vary with seasonal
rainfall and time of year
For more detailed descriptions of the materials encountered please refer to the
Boring Logs presented on Plates A 1 through A 6
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Our field exploration indicates that the site is underlain by well compacted clayey
silt fill material which extends to the depths explored Based on the subsoil conditions
encountered at the project site it is our opinion that conventional shallow foundations
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 3225 00 Page 6
consisting of spread and or continuous footings may be used for support of the
proposed Switch Building Addition
Laboratory tests performed on the on site clayey silt soils indicate that the material
generally has a moderate shrink swell potential when subjected to moisture fluctuations
Therefore we recommend that concrete slabs on grade should not be placed directly
over this clayey subgrade soil The concrete slab should be supported directly on a layer
of capping fill consisting of non expansive select borrow to reduce the potential effects
of the moderately high expansive surface soils Detailed recommendations are presented
in the following subsections
Foundations
Based on the generally stiff and dense subsoil conditions encountered at the site
and the anticipated structural loadings we believe that shallow spread and or continuous
footings may be used to support the proposed new addition bearing on the recompacted
clayey silt fill material or the required new engineered fill
An allowable bearing pressure of 3 000 pounds per square foot p s f may be
used for design of spread footing foundations bearing on the recompacted in situ fill soils
encountered at the project site or on proposed engineered fill This bearing value is for
dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third for transient loads such as
those caused by wind or seismic forces
The footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest
adjacent finish grade Footings adjacent to the existing Earth Station Building should
extend at least to the same depth as the existing building footings to avoid surcharging
the existing foundations The actual depths of the existing footings should be confirmed
in the field during construction by the project geotechnical engineer
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 3225 00 Page 7
Due to the potential flooding concern of the existing surface water drainage system
located along two sides of the property we understand that it is proposed to elevate the
new building by placing approximately 3 feet of new fill at the project site We
recommend that the new addition be set back a minimum of 5 feet away from the edge
of the new fill embankment Footings located within the set back area along the edge of
the new fill slope should be embedded deep enough to obtain a minimum 8 foot set back
distance measured horizontally from the outside edge of the footing out to the slope face
lf foundations are located next to utility trenches or easements they should be
embedded below a 1H 1V imaginary plane extending upward from the bottom edge of
the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility lines
We estimate the footing settlements under the anticipated design loads for spread
footings bearing on the dense in situ fill soils or the new fill to be less than inch total
and inch differential
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive earth pressure
acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system and by frictional resistance
developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil Resistance due
to passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid pressure of 300
pounds per square foot per foot of depth p c f This assumes that the soil around
footings is well compacted or undisturbed The passive pressure should be reduced for
foundations located on slopes Unless covered by pavements or slabs the passive
resistance in the upper 12 inches of soil should be neglected A coefficient of friction of
0 3 may be used for footings bearing on the recompacted in situ silty soils or engineered
fill
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 3225 00 Page 8
Depending on the quality control and workmanship the existing man made fill
encountered at the site may not be uniformly placed and adequately compacted
Therefore we recommend that the subgrades of all footings be scarified to a depth of 8
inches moisture conditioned to about 2 percent above optimum moisture content and
recompacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers
to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density
of the same soil established in accordance with procedures described in ASTM Test
Designation D 1557 91 If soft loose pockets are encountered at the bottom of the
footing excavations they should be over excavated to expose the underlying firm
materials The over excavation should either be backfilled with granular material
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction or the footing bottom be
extended down to the underiying firm material
We recommend that footing excavations be observed by a representative of
Geolabs Hawaii prior to the placement of reinforcing steel or concrete to confirm adequate
foundation bearing conditions and the required embedment depths
Slab On Grade
The project site is underlain by a man made fill of clayey silt soils which are
considered to be moderately expansive when subjected to moisture fluctuations Unless
floor slabs supported by these soils are properly designed there is a potential for future
distress to the lightly loaded slab on grade construction resulting from the shrinking and
swelling of these clayey soils due to changes in the moisture content To reduce the
potential for structural distress resulting from the swelling of the on site moderately
expansive subgrade soils we recommend that the concrete slab on grade be supported
on 18 inches of non expansive select granular fill material The non expansive select fill
should extend at least 2 feet beyond the perimeter of the proposed structure
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3225 00 Page 9
We understand that approximately 3 feet of fill will be required to raise the existing
ground to the proposed building slab subgrade elevation Therefore the new fill may
serve as the capping fill as recommended above provided the followed recommendations
are properly followed
The new fill material should be placed as soon as possible after the scarification
and recompaction of the existing clayey subgrade soils The prepared new fill and the
underlying subgrade soil should be wetted and kept moist prior to final placement of
concrete Subgrade soils should be prepared in accordance with the recommendations
presented in the Site Grading section of this report The new fill would serve as a
capping fill layer of moist soil over the underlying potentially expansive subgrade soils
and this capping fill would also reduce the potential moisture loss from the prepared moist
subgrade soils This reduction in moisture loss should reduce the potential swelling of
the moderately expansive clayey soils Compaction requirements for the subgrade soils
and new fill are discussed in the Site Grading section of this report
We recommend that a minimum 4 inch thick layer of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM
C 33 No 67 gradation cushion layer be installed below the slab to provide uniform
support for the slab To reduce future moisture infiltration and subsequent damage to the
floor coverings an impervious moisture vapor barrier such as a plastic membrane is
recommended on top of the gravel cushion layer Two inches of moist fine sand may be
placed above the plastic membrane to provide protection for the membrane and to aid
in curing of the slab concrete The thickened edges of slabs adjacent to any unpaved
area should be embedded at least 12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade
Where the slab is subjected to vehicular traffic such as the docking area a 6 inch
layer of base course compacted to 95 percent relative compaction is recommended in lieu
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 3225 00 Page 10
of the No 3B Fine gravel capillary break layer Provisions should be made for proper
load transfer across the slab joints which are subjected to vehicular traffic
Exterior concrete walkways may be required for the proposed project We
recommend that a minimum 4 inch thick cushion layer of No 3B Fine gravel be provided
below the concrete slab Construction joints should be provided at intervals equal to the
width of the walkways with expansion joints at right angle intersections

Site Grading
Due to the concern for the existing surface water drainage system on two sides
of the property we understand that it is proposed to elevate the new building by placing
approximately 3 feet of fill at the project site
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable
materials should be removed and disposed of off site
Slabs and foundations of the existing structures that are to be demolished should
be completely removed Any over excavation resulting from the demolition should be
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 3225 00 Page 13
backfilled with compacted granular fill material Existing utilities to be abandoned should
be removed and the resulting excavation should be properly backfilled with horizontal lifts
of compacted select granular fill material Utilities that are to be abandoned in place
should be backfilled by pumping lean concrete under low pressure
After demolition clearing and grubbing all areas to receive fill should be scarified
to a depth of 8 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above optimum
moisture content and compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction Select
granular fill material should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 10 inches in loose
thickness and compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction Two horizontal
to one vertical 2H 1V fill slopes are recommended
Select granular fill material should consist of imported granular material such as
crushed coral basalt gravel or cinder sand The material should be well graded from
coarse to fine with no particles greater than 3 inches in largest dimension The material
should have a laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR value of 20 or higher and a swell
potential of not more than 1 percent when tested in accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 1883 It should contain less than 20 percent passing the No 200 sieve
Any imported fill material should be tested and approved by Geolabs Hawaii prior to
delivery to the project site for its intended use

and concrete aprons for the loading dock may be required to serve the new structure

vehicles and delivery trucks We have made our preliminary pavement designs assuming
the pavement subgrade soil will consist of at least 18 inches of imported select granular
fill material The imported select granular fill material under the pavement areas should

Utility Trench
We envision that new utility lines may be required for the project A granular
bedding consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation is
recommended under the pipes Self compacting granular materials such as No 3B Fine
gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation should also be used for the trench backfill up to
about one foot above the pipes to provide adequate support around the pipes and to
reduce the compaction effort of the backfill thus reducing the possibility of damaging the
pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level one foot above the pipes to
the top of the subgrade or finished grade should consist of select granular material The
backfill should be placed in maximum 10 inch level loose lifts and mechanically
compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for
future ground subsidence Where trenches are below pavement areas the upper 2 feet
of the trench backfill below the pavement subgrade should be compacted to 95 percent
relative compaction
Drainage
The finished grades outside the building should be sloped to shed water away from
foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding Also it is advised that gutter
systems be installed around the building and that the discharge be diverted away from
the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near the foundations
GEOLABS HAWAIi
W O 3225 00 Page 16
and slabs should also be avoided Planters next to foundations should be avoided or
have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the above
foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation
and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations should be
properly backfilled against the walls or slab footings immediately after setting of the
concrete to reduce water infiltration
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and should be
maintained to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundations

transition
Water level data from the borings were measured at the times given on the graphic

fluctuation may occur due to variation in rainfall tides temperature and other factors
