In accordance with the scope of services outlined in our fee proposal dated April 12, 
2022, we observed the drilled shaft foundation installation work for the New 138kV Double 
Dead End Steel Pole project located at the CEIP Substation in the Kapolei area on the Island 
of Oahu, Hawaii. The project location and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location 
Map, Plate 1. 
Geolabs, Inc. provided geotechnical engineering consultation services for the project. 
The purpose of our geotechnical observations was to monitor the geotechnical engineering 
aspects of the drilled shaft foundation installation for conformance with the plans and 
specifications and to provide subsequent recommendations as appropriate. 
PROJECT DESCRIPTION 
The project is located within the existing CEIP Substation off Kalaeloa Boulevard in 
the Kapolei area on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. The project consists of the installation of a 
new 138kV double dead end steel pole. Borehole drilling at the new power pole location was 
specifically not included in our scope of services during the geotechnical engineering study. 
Therefore, it is important to verify the actual subsurface conditions with the estimated 
subsurface conditions used in the study. 
The scope of our work discussed in this letter report includes observation of the drilled 
shaft foundation construction for the new 138kV double dead end steel pole. Mana 
Construction was the contractor for the project and was responsible for the fabrication and 
installation of the reinforcing cage and concrete placement. Foundation International Inc. 
was the drilling subcontractor.

DRILLED SHAFT INSTALLATION 
Based on the anticipated subsurface conditions and foundation demand, a deep 
foundation system consisting of cast-in-place concrete drilled shaft embedded into the 
underlying coralline material was utilized for foundation support of the new 138kV double 
dead end steel pole. 
The drilling subcontractor installed one drilled shaft from May 11, 2022 through 
May 16, 2022 at the location shown on the Site Plan, Plate 2. A representative from Geolabs, 
Inc. observed the drilled shaft excavation, placement of reinforcing cage, and concrete 
operation on a near-continuous (full-time) basis during the installation of the production 
drilled shaft. The drilled shaft was excavated by the subcontractor utilizing a CAT 512 
excavator with a Watson D7 drill attachment. Tooling used for the 84-inch diameter shafts 
included a 60-inch auger and clean-out bucket, 84-inch core barrel, and 60-inch digging 
bucket with a 1-foot reamer attachment on both sides of the bucket. The drilled shaft was 
excavated by the subcontractor by initially developing a 60-inch pilot hole with the 60-inch 
auger below the design tip depth. The pilot hole was then expanded to an 84-inch drilled 
shaft using the 84-inch core barrel and 60-inch digging bucket with the reamer attachment. 
Groundwater was not encountered during the excavation. 
After excavating the drilled shaft to the designated tip elevation, the reinforcing cage 
was lowered into the drilled hole to the appropriate elevation by the General Contractor using 
a Telehandler. The General Contractor creates the forms and setup form a day prior to 
concrete placement. Due to concrete placement scheduled for two days after excavation of 
the drilled shaft had met the designated tip elevation and had been cleaned out, the 
contractor was notified that since the time frame between the completion of the excavation 
and the concrete placement will exceed the recommended 24 hours, the shaft will need to 
be inspected prior to the concrete placement for borehole side integrity and a clean bottom. 
During concrete placement, concrete was dropped through a chute that extended 
down the hole. Using a 15-foot Oztec Backpack Concrete Vibrator, Model BP-50A, the 
contractor vibrated the concrete as it reached near the surface. Concrete with a measured 
slump of 8.25 inches and with a temperature of 84 degrees was used for the drilled shaft. 
Drilled shaft installation records are presented on Plates 3.1 through 3.4. 
Based on our field observations of the drilled shaft installation, we believe that the 
drilled shaft for the new double dead end steel pole has been installed in general accordance 
with the intent of the project plans, specifications, the geotechnical recommendations 
presented in our geotechnical engineering report dated August 6, 2021 and within the limits 
of generally accepted construction practices. In addition, it is our professional opinion that 
the drilled shaft has been installed to sufficient depth to provide the design load capacity.
