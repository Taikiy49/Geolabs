PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is on the north and south sides of Kamehameha Highway between
Kaahumanu Street and the Interstate Route H 1 crossing with Kamehameha Highway in
the Waiau area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project consists of the development of
the DAR for the rehabilitation reconstruction of approximately 10 700 linear feet of 6 to
12 inch sewer lines and 72 manhole structures The existing sewer lines and manhole
structures are on the following roadways Kuleana Place Kaulike Drive Kauhihau Place
Hila Place Kaluamoi Drive Kaluamoi Place and Kamehameha Highway The project site
is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The existing sewer lines consist of 6 to 12 inch vitrified clay pipe VCP and cast
iron pipe CIP The sewer lines were installed between 1952 and 2005 with the majority
of the sewer lines installed in 1957 The pipe invert elevation range from about
Elevation 35 feet Mean Sea Level MSL to 6 7 feet MSL The pipe depths range from
about 3 3 to 19 2 feet below the existing ground surface We understand that about
4 800 linear feet of existing sewer lines will be reconstructed The new sewer lines will
have the same size pipe or upsized to a larger pipe The remaining sewer lines will be
rehabilitated using Cured In Place Pipe CIPP method A summary of the sewer line
rehabilitation reconstruction is presented on Plate 3

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from two shield
volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older Waianae Volcano is estimated to be middle to
late Pliocene in age and forms the bulk of the western one third of the island The younger
Koolau Volcano is estimated to be late Pliocene to early Pleistocene Ice Age in age and
forms the majority of the eastern two thirds of the island Waianae became extinct while
Koolau was still active and its eastern flank was partially buried below Koolau lavas
banking against its eastern flank forming a broad plateau now known as the Schofield
Plateau
During the Pleistocene Epoch sea levels fluctuated in response to the cycles of
continental glaciation The higher sea level stands caused the formation of deltas and
fans of accumulated terrigenous sediments in the heads of old bays accumulated reef
deposits at correspondingly higher elevations and deposited lagoonal marine sediments
in the quiet waters protected by fringing reefs The lower sea stands caused streams to
carve valleys in the sediments and reef deposits Subaerial exposure of the sediments
and calcareous materials caused consolidation of the lagoonal deposits and induration of
the calcareous reef materials
Based on geologic maps the project site is generally underlain by
unconsolidated non calcareous deposits consolidated deposits and basalt lavas The
majority of the project site is underlain by unconsolidated non calcareous deposits The
upper portion of the site is underlain by consolidated deposits and basalt lavas The
unconsolidated non calcareous deposits are mainly composed of recent alluvial
deposits This deposit generally consists of compressible material The consolidated
deposits consist of older alluvium with stiffer characteristics compared to the recent
alluvium The site is also underlain by basalt rock formation of the Koolau Volcanic
Series The geologic map of the project site is shown on the Geology Plan Plate 5

Soft Ground Conditions
Areas underlain by recent alluvium are susceptible to consolidation and settlement
over time as man made fills are placed over these soft ground areas Based on our
literature research soft soils are likely to be encountered within a major portion of the
project site A thorough field exploration should be performed at these locations
suspected to be underlain by soft soil deposits to determine their lateral extent thickness
and consolidation characteristics under the existing and new loading conditions These
soft soil areas should be further evaluated to provide recommendations for design and
construction of new sewer lines to maintain line and grade over these areas
Areas with soft ground conditions may be susceptible to liquefaction during seismic
events or construction activities Soil liquefaction is a condition where saturated
cohesionless soils near the ground surface undergo a substantial loss of strength due to
the build up of excess pore water pressures resulting from cyclic stress applications
induced by earthquakes In this process when the loose saturated sand and or gravel
deposit is subjected to vibration such as during an earthquake the soil tends to densify
and decrease in volume causing an increase in pore water pressure If drainage is unable
to occur rapidly enough to dissipate the build up of pore water pressure the effective
stress internal strength of the soil is reduced Under sustained vibrations the pore water
pressure build up could equal the overburden pressure essentially reducing the soil shear
strength to zero and causing it to behave as a viscous fluid During liquefaction the soil
acquires a mobility sufficient to permit both horizontal and vertical movements and if not
confined will result in significant deformations Soils most susceptible to liquefaction are
loose uniformly graded fine grained sands and loose silts with little cohesion
For conditions where the soft soil layer is relatively thin below the pipe the soft soil
layer is typically removed and replaced with granular material to provide a stable base for
the new pipe Where the soft soil layer is relatively thick below the pipe a deep foundation
system may be required to support new sewer lines and to reduce the potential for
excessive settlement to the sewer line Deep foundation systems are discussed below
High Groundwater Levels
Based on the existing pipe invert elevations we anticipate that high groundwater
levels are present at the project site For portions of the sewer line along Kamehameha
Highway and Kaluamoi Place the existing pipe invert elevations range from about 3 feet
to 6 7 feet MSL In addition artesian springs are located in close proximity of the project
site These artesian springs will produce water at higher elevations and are typically used
in the area for farming watercress
The high groundwater levels will require the installation of temporary shoring and
the dewatering of the excavation for sewer line installation Typically interlocking steel
sheet piling is used to reduce the amount of groundwater entering into the excavation and

Deep Foundations
Deep foundation systems may provide support for the new sewer lines underlain by
thick soft soil deposit Deep foundation systems include driven concrete piles micropiles
and jet grouted columns
Driven piles consist of precast prestressed concrete members with diameter or
width from 12 inches and larger Generally the concrete piles are driven through the soft
soil layer and into the underlying stiff dense bearing layer The driven piles derive vertical
support from end bearing on the stiff dense underlying soil and from skin friction along the
sides of the piles
With the use of driven piles the construction of concrete beam or cradle is required
to connect the piles and provide support for the new sewer pipes The pipe support or
cradles are typically cast in place concrete structures
Larger construction equipment is typically used for driven pile installation
Therefore narrow residential roadways with overhead utility lines may create significant
problems for pile installation In addition a significant amount of vibration may be
generated during pile driving These vibrations may damage adjacent structures and may
induce liquefaction of the underlying soft deposits
In general a micropile foundation system consists of a small diameter usually less
than 12 inches drilled and grouted pile with steel reinforcing The micropile foundation
typically is constructed by drilling a borehole placing steel casing and reinforcing steel in
the hole and grouting the borehole Micropiles are desirable because they can be
installed readily in access restrictive environments and in numerous soil types and ground
conditions The equipment used for micropile installation is smaller in size as compared to
equipment required for driven pile installation In addition installation of the micropiles
generally causes minimal disturbance to adjacent structures the adjacent soils and the
environment

Similar to driven piles the construction of concrete beam or cradle is required to
connect the micropiles and to provide support for the new sewer pipes
Jet grouting is a technique utilizing a special drill bit and injection monitor with radial
horizontal nozzles to produce stabilized soil cement columns The jet grouting technique
is a process that produces soil cement columns by pumping neat cement grout slurry
through horizontal jets injected at high pressures The horizontal jets of cement grout
slurry cuts and mixes the surrounding in situ materials with the neat cement slurry grout as
the drill bit is slowly rotated and withdrawn to form a soil cement column
The jet grouted columns can provide support for the new sewer line from potential
future settlements within soft ground conditions The jet grouted columns would derive
vertical support primarily from bearing on the stiff dense underlying soils and from skin
friction along the sides of the jet grouted columns
The jet grouted columns may be used to reduce the amount of groundwater
entering into the excavation and therefore reduce the amount of dewatering required
The size of equipment used for jet grouting is typically similar to equipment used for
micropile installation
Trenchless Rehabilitation Methods
Trenchless rehabilitation consists of reusing the existing pipe or pipe alignment and
includes pipe bursting slip lining and Cured In Place Pipe CIPP methods For the slip
lining and CIPP methods the inside diameter of the pipe will be reduced Although the
CIPP method reduces the inside diameter of the pipe the friction between the pipe and
the liquid is reduced and may not significantly reduce the flow capacity of the pipe We
understand that the majority of the existing sewer lines will be rehabilitated by CIPP
methods
Pipe bursting consist of winching a pneumatic powered bursting tool through an old
section of pipe breaking and cracking the old pipe as it progresses A new pipe section is
pulled into place behind the bursting tool replacing the old pipe section Pipe bursting can
replace existing cast iron pipe vitrified clay pipe asbestos pipe ductile iron pipe and mild
steel pipe Various types of pipe may be installed using pipe bursting and includes high
density polyethylene HDPE vitrified clay and polyvinyl chloride PVC pipe Since the
new pipe section will follow the alignment of the old pipe section this method of pipe
installation should not be used if the existing pipe alignment is out of the required line and
grade Pipe bursting have the capability to install a larger size pipe with higher flow
capacity than the existing pipe
During pipe bursting the soil surrounding the bursting tool will be compressed
Damage to existing utilities in close proximity of the bursting tool may occur for existing
