EARTHWORK OBSERVATION AND TESTING REPORT
PALM VILLA INCREMENT 2
EWA BY GENTRY
We have previously submitted a preliminary report entitled Compaction Report
Mass Grading Palm Villa Increment Hawaii dated
This report presents the results of our geotechnical services performed during the
earthwork and grading operations for the above referenced project from May 1989
through June 1991
The subject site is located within the Ewa Gentry Development off Fort Weaver
Road in the district of Ewa on the island of Oahu Hawaii In general the fill area is
designated as the western half of F 2 of the mass grading plans for the development
The grading operations at the above referenced site consisted of cuts up to 4 feet
and fills up to 5 feet The mass grading work was performed by RHS Lee Inc and the
utility construction was performed by Oahu Construction The mass grading work
consisted of scarifying and compacting the existing subgrade prior to the placement of

fills The available on site fill materials generally consisted of sandy coral gravel dark
brown silty clay mixed with coral gravel material and coralline silty sand and gravel
Prior to fill placement the existing vegetation was removed and the exposed
subgrades were scarified to a depth of approximately 6 inches brought to near optimum
moisture and compacted to 90 percent of the maximum dry density as determined in
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor The fill material was
then placed in near level lifts and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of its maximum
dry density
During the fill placement operations periodic field density tests were done in
general accordance with the American Society for Testing and Material ASTM Test
Designation D 1556 Sand Cone Method and D 2922 Nuclear Gauge Method Results
of the compaction tests are summarized in the attachments of this report
During the grading period various on site and borrow soils were tested prior to use
in the filed The maximum dry density and optimum moisture content relationships were
established in our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557
Modified Proctor and the test results are also summarized in the attachment of this
report
Our services consisted of periodic field observation of the contractor s fill
placement and compaction procedures and observation of subgrade soil conditions
exposed during the above grading operations
Field density test locations were selected to confirm field observations as variations
in construction procedures and soil conditions were noted Test locations and elevations
were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations
