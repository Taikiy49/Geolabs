Prior to the pile driving activity Hawaiian Dredging Construction Company the pile
driving contractor submitted a Request For Information RFI for casting all production
piles to 154 feet long without additional cost to the State to minimize construction delay
Upon the approval of the RFI twelve concrete piles including one test pile were cast in
two 77 foot segments splicing for a total length of 154 feet and barged from Oahu to
Molokai
A wave equation report was performed by Dan Brown Associates dated
entitled as WEAP Analyses of Proposed Piles for Harbor
Improvements Kaunakakai Harbor Ferry System Molokai HI The report concluded that
the proposed single action diesel hammer is sufficient to install the
specified 20 inch octagonal concrete pile mobilizing 400 kip ultimate pile resistance to
satisfy the design criteria
Test pile Pile No 10 was selected and driven to a depth of about 143 5 feet below
existing concrete dock equivalent to Elevation about 135 8 feet Mean Sea Level MSL
Subsequently a static compressive load test was conducted to a maximum test load of
about 400 kips and held for about 60 minutes without excessive creep movement

It should be noted that Pile No 11 was initially cast in two 77 foot segments One of
the segments was damaged during storage A diagonal crack was observed at a depth of
about 25 feet and extended about 5 feet long Upon approval this damaged 77 foot
segment was saw cut into three sections including two sections 20 foot and 47 foot
without cracks and one section 10 foot with cracks Subsequently two good sections
were structurally spliced utilizing four rebar dowels with grout and an 8 foot long steel
splice can A spliced 67 foot long segment was then placed at the bottom portion of Pile
No 11
It is our opinion that the piles for this project have been installed in general
conformance with the project requirements and within the limits of generally accepted
construction practices Based on our observations of hammer performance and pile driving
behavior and the static compressive pile load test results it is our professional opinion
that the piles have been driven to sufficient depths and or resistance to provide the design
vertical compressive load capacity
