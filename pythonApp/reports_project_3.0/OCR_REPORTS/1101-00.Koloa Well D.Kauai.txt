PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our investigation was to evaluate the condition
of the existing 111 slope which was suspected of being con
structed by inadequate fill placement procedures and to deter
mine possible remedial measures to stabilize the questionable
fill
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests
which were used in the engineering analysis that formulated
our conclusions and recommendations are presented in the
appendix of this report
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is situated southeast of Koloa town east of
McBryde Sugar Mill
The well D site is situated on the lower Slopes of a hill
Side at about elevation 220 to 230 feet
The up slope portion of the area has been cut about 6 to 8
feet exposing weathered rock formation below a thin approxi
mately 1 foot surface layer of silty clay slope wash mater
ial Rocky material was exposed in the recently graded level
cut area within about 10 to 15 feet from the toe of the cut
slope
The existing fil1 slope surface was loose and irregular and is

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration encountered about 5 feet of loose fill
material with roots branches and boulders along the top of
the existing slope It appears that the upper fill was pushed
over the sloping site without adequate keying benching and
compaction Since the fill was not properly constructed it
would be very susceptible to later downhill movements and
settlements which would adversely affect the proposed roadway
In addition to the improper filling the natural soil below the
fill consists of about 2 to 3 feet of adobe clay These
adobe clay are expansive soils which will become very weak
when saturated and are subject to slope creep and slides

It is our opinion that the loose fill and underlying adobe
clay layer are very susceptible to downhill slope movements
which would affect the performance of the proposed pavement
area In order to stabilize the fill two alternate designs
are presented below
Alternate 1 Reconstruction of the fill slope
The loose fill and adobe clay should be removed down
to the rock formation The i11 should then be recon
structed with proper keying and benching into the rocky
material The new fill should be placed in 10 inch
horizontal lifts and then compacted to a minimum of 90
of its maximum dry density established by ASTM D 1557
for details of fill slope reconstruction see Plate 3
The adobe clay should not be reused in the fill The
upper non adobe loose fill material can be reused if
the boulders branches and large pieces of roots are
removed
Off site borrow material will be required to complete
the fill slope Borrow material should be non expansive
soil generally less than 6 inches in size

Alternate 2 Rock Buttress Wall
To avoid the removal and reconstruction of fill slope a
rock buttress wall could be constructed along the edge
of the pavement area to provide lateral support for the
loose fill and adobe clay soils below the pavement
area see Plate 4
The rock buttress should extend into the underlying rock
formation anticipated at about 8 feet below the exist
ing ground surface The buttress should be a minimum of
5 feet wide The general construction sequence would be
as follows
1 Excavate down a minimum of 1 foot into
rock formation
2 Place a layer of concrete at the bottom
of the excavation then place boulders
with sufficient concrete between to pro
vide a well cemented structure

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the stripping excavations keying
benching and filling operations be observed by the soils
engineer during construction This is to see that adequate
compaction has been obtained and the subsurface conditions
are consistent with the findings of our subsurface investi
gation The soils engineer s observations should also
include density tests to record compaction of the fill The
recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above inspections
