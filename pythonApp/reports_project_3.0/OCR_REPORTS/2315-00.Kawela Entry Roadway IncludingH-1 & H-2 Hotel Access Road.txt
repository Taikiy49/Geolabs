This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
PROJECT DESCRIPTION
The project site includes an area along Kamehameha Highway and a new
entry road from the highway to the H 1 and H 2 Hotel sites A new bridge
will be constructed on Kamehameha Highway where the proposed Kawela Stream
realignment crosses the highway

The proposed roadway construction will consist of the widening of
about 1800 feet of Kamehameha construction of a concrete bridge
approximately 65 by 80 feet at the eastern end of this highway improvement
and construction of about 1200 feet of new entry roads to the proposed H 1
and H 2 Hotel sites
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface soil and water level conditions at the site in order to provide
data for the formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations for
roadway embankment construction pavement thickness design and
preliminary bridge foundation design
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests which we
used in the engineering analysis to formulate our conclusions and
recommendations are presented in the appendices of this report
The scope of work performed in this exploration was in general
accordance with our proposal dated Our scope included
drilling and sampling a total of nine 9 borings to depths varying from
19 5 to 75 feet below the existing ground surface laboratory testing
engineering evaluation of the field and laboratory data and report
preparation
STTE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located on the North Shore of Oahu along
Kamehameha Highway between Kuilima Drive and Hanopu Street
In general the topography is flat with ground elevations ranging
from 7 to 10 feet above Mean Sea Level MSL

H 1 and H 2 Hotel sites A concrete bridge is planned for the widening
of Kamehameha Highway crossing over the proposed West Main Drain

Site Grading
Fills up to 2 feet are planned for the improvement and widening of
Kamehameha Highway and fills up to 6 feet are anticipated for the
designated access road B to the H 1 and H 2 Hotel sites
The site should be cleared of all ground surface vegetation debris
and existing structures
Any local soft spots and unsuitable soft materials encountered within
the construction area should be removed and replaced with compacted
fill
Following the clearing and grubbing all areas designated to receive
fill should be scarified to a depth of six 6 inches and recompacted
to a minimm of 90 of its maximum dry density per ASIM Test Method
D 1557 78 The moisture content of the soil showld be near the
optimm moisture content
For fill areas over Kamehameha Highway the existing A C pavement
should be scarified six 6 inch maximm aggregate size and
recompacted to the requirements in the previous paragraph

Slopes
Two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V fill slopes are recommended
for fills constructed from the proposed borrow soils
Fill slopes should be constructed by overfilling past the design fill
slope and then cutting back to the above finish slope ratio
Water should be diverted away from the top of constructed slopes and
slope planting utilized as soon as practicable to reduce erosion
Slope benches are generally recommended for slope heights in excess
of about 20 feet
Settlements
Ground settlements on the order of 1 to 2 inches are anticipated for
the embankment fill placed over the underlying loose sand encountered
in the vicinity of Road B
We recommend that settlement gauges be installed at about 200 foot
intervals in the above roadway areas to monitor the fill ground
settlements For details of the settlement gauge refer to Plate 3
The settlement gauges should be read by the project surveyor on a
weekly basis and the readings transmitted to the geotechnical
engineer for review Construction of permanent facilities such as
sewer and water lines drainage structures and pavement should be
delayed until the settlement gauges indicate that majority of the
fill settlements has occurred or the remaining settlements are
tolerable We estimate that the fill settlement waiting period would
be brief probably on the order of about one month

Earth Pressure Loads on Pipe
Toads on buried conduits are influenced by the width of the trench
the size of the pipe the unit weight of backfill material and the
frictional resistance between the backfill material and the trench

side walls To compute the vertical loads on the pipes an average
unit weight for backfill material of 120 p c f and a coefficient of
friction of 0 25 are recommended Earth forces upon the pipe
increase rapidly with the width of the trench Therefore the width
of the trench should be kept to a minimum
Bearing Capacity and Settlement
The underlying layers of beach and marine deposits have good to poor
bearing capacity depending on where the invert elevations of the
sewerline will be located
The medium dense beach layer encountered generally has good bearing
capacity The loose coral and soft sandy silts generally have poor
bearing capacity and this would require recompaction at the trench
bottom and or special bedding layer An adequately designed pipe
bedding layer should provide the necessary stress distribution and to
reduce the stress imposed on the underlying poor soil conditions
The new sewerline construction in general will not materially
increase the vertical loading upon these underlying soils The net
difference in loading between the weights of the excavated material
and the subsequent sewer pipe and backfill will be essentially small
Assuming utility construction is delayed until fill ground
settlements have occurred first significant settlements due to
sewerline construction are not anticipated if proper construction
techniques are utilized
Pipe Bedding
The stress distribution against the bottom of a pipe has a great
effect upon the load supporting capacity of the pipe Therefore the
pipe bedding is an important design consideration

For pipes bearing on the medium dense beach or alluvial deposits a
minimum of 6 inches of compacted granular bedding material should be
placed below the pipe The granular bedding should extend beyond the
pipe sides a minimm width of one fourth the outside pipe diameter
and it also should extend half way up the pipe sides The bedding
material should consist of clean granular material such as 3B Fine
gravel
For pipes on the loose coral and soft sandy silts an additional
18 inch layer of clear granular material should be provided below the
utility pipe and bedding layer to provide more uniform support for
the pipe
Backfill
In general the trench backfill should be in accordance with the
Standard Specifications for Public Works Construction September
1986
The first zone of backfill extends from the bedding mterial to at
least 12 inches above the top of the pipe The first zone should
consist of select free draining well graded granular material placed
by hand or by suitable equipment so that the backfill material is in
contact with the entire periphery of the pipe and in such manner as
not to disturb the pipe Granular material is recommended to provide
a relatively high density backfill with minimm compactive effort
Compaction of this portion of the backfill by ponding and jetting may
be permitted provided the granular backfill material is
self draining The material should be well graded less than 3
inches in size and with no more than 10 passing the No 200 sieve

Joints and Connections
Due to the various soil conditions which will be crossed by the pipe
flexible pipe joints are recommended to allow for some amount of
differential settlement These flexible connections are particularly
important in the loose sand areas
Kamehameha Highway Bridge
A concrete bridge approximately 65 feet wide by 180 feet long is
planned for the crossing of Kamehameha Highway over the proposed West Main
Drain The borings at the bridge site encountered stratified deposits
consisting of alternating layers of alluvial clayey silt and coralline
sand and gravel The upper 20 to 25 feet of soil was generally loose to
medium stiff but graded to stiff or dense at deeper depths

Method D 1557 to provide uniform support An allowable bearing value

tolerable then pile foundations may be considered
The piles would develop their load capacity from end bearing in the

the existing ground surface Our analysis indicates that with the
use of a 16 5 inch octagonal prestressed concrete pile an allowable
design capacity of 100 tons may be used

Pile Driving
We recommend that a pile driving hammer which can deliver a
minimm of 32000 foot pounds of energy be used for the pile
Q riving The piles should be driven to a resistance of about 100
blows per foot for the last foot of penetration or 10 blows per
inch for the last 3 inches
The driving process should be continuous without interruption for
each pile A pile may be rejected or its design capacity may be
modified if the driving process is interrupted for more than four
4 hours
During easy driving when the pile tip is within loose soils
the hammer stroke or energy should be carefully controlled so
that excessive tensile stresses are not developed in the pile
The pile driving should be observed by a qualified geotechnical
engineer to see that the piles are driven to the sufficient depth
or into competent bearing materials

UIMTTATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field borings Variations of
conditions between the field data points may cccur and the nature and
extend of these variations may not become evident until construction If
variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations given in this report
The locations of the field borings were approximately determined by
taping from structures indicated on the topographic survey provided by EDP
Hawaii Elevations of the data points were approximately estimated by
interpolation from elevation points on the same map The physical
location and elevation of the field data points should be considered
accurate only to the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross sections are based upon interpolation between field data
points and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
Water level data from the field data points were collected at the
times given on the graphic representations and or in the text of this
report These data have been reviewed and interpretations made in the
formulation of this report However it must be noted that fluctuation
may occur due to variations in rainfall tides temperature and other
factors
