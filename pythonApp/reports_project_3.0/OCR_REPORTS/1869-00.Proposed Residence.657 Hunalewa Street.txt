The field exploration encountered stiff clay
soils beneath the site Laboratory testing and
previous experience have demonstrated that this
material has high swelling potential
Special foundation design is recommended to
reduce the effects of the expansive soil
To utilize a slab on grade foundation the
recommended site improvement technique is to
place a minimum 3 foot capping layer of
non expansive material below the foundation
To utilize a spread or continuous footing
foundation the recommended site improvement
technique is to place the footing on 3 feet of
non expansive material or to embed the footing
a minimum of 3 feet below the finished grade

INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of a soils engineering evaluation
performed for the proposed residence at 657 Hunalewa Street on the
island of Oahu Hawaii The general location of the site and its
vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
Based on the preliminary plans provided to us the proposed
development will consist of a two story residence The ground level of
the residence will consist of a garage with the living areas primarily
on the second floor
A concrete slab on grade is planned for the garage Building
foundation loadings are anticipated to be moderate to light and typical
for two story wooden structures
Minimal site grading is anticipated
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this study was to provide recommendations for the
building foundation for the subject house The recommendations are
based on the soil information obtained from borings drilled for this
exploration
The study consisted of drilling 3 borings The approximate
locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Soil sampling was performed by one of our engineers The results
of our field exploration and laboratory tests which were utilized in
the engineering analysis to formulate our recommendations are presented
in the appendices of this report

This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is located in the Kaimuki District of Honolulu
This approximately rectangular shaped 5 740 square foot lot is fronted
by Hunalewa Street to the east
At the time of the investigation the site was fairly level
undeveloped and covered in knee deep grass and weeds
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The investigation program consisted of drilling two borings B 1
and B 2 to depths of 13 0 feet A third boring B 3 was drilled to a
depth of 8 0 feet All borings were drilled with a truck mounted
drilling rig The locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2
In general the site is underlain by expansive clay soils
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings at the time of the
drilling investigation
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration disclosed that the lot is underlain by brown
expansive clay The laboratory test results and previous experience
indicate that this material is highly expansive Therefore the
foundation will require special design and construction procedures in
order to reduce the shrink swell effect of the subsoils

Foundation
A spread or continuous footing foundation may be used to support
the proposed structure
In order to reduce the effects of the expansive soil we recommend
that the expansive clays be over excavated down to a depth of 3 feet
below the bottom of the individual footings and then replaced with
non expansive borrow soils such as crusher waste select borrow or
coral The replaced soils should be compacted to a minimm of 90 of
its maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78 The footings can then be
placed on the compacted non expansive soil for details refer to
Schematic Section Plate 3
Alternatively the footings may be embedded a minimum of 3 feet
below the lowest adjacent grade for details refer to the Schematic
Section Plate 3
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f can be used for footings
bearing on compacted fill or in situ soil
If a footing is located next to a utility line it should extend to
the bottom of the utility trench to reduce footing settlement due to
settlement of the trench backfill
Surface water should be diverted away from the foundation This is
to avoid ponding of surface run off water that may affect the expansive
soils around the foundation
Slab on Grade
In order to reduce the expansive effects of the soils we recommend
that a minimum 3 foot thick capping layer of non expansive material be
placed below the slab on grade foundation

The expansive clay below the slab should be removed for a minimum
depth of three 3 feet below outside finished grade and replaced with
non expansive off site borrow materials such as select borrow crusher
waste or coral compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximm dry
density per ASIM D 1557 78 The underlying clay soil remaining should
first be covered with about 1 4 inch of hydrated lime wetted by water
sprinkling and then maintained in a moist condition
A 4 inch layer of 3 Fine cushion fill should be provided below the
slab In order to protect against moisture infiltration a vapor
barrier may be incorporated below the slab
The thickened edge slab footings can bear on the compacted
non expansive fill The thickened edges should be embedded a minimm of
12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade for details refer to
Schematic Section Plate 4
Concrete Driveway
For the concrete driveway recommendations provided for the 3 foot
capping layer for the concrete slab on grade should be followed
However since the driveway could tolerate some amount of swelling the
non expansive capping thickness could be reduced to 2 feet to reduce
costs if some cracking is tolerable The subgrade soils should be kept
moist and covered with lime prior to concrete placement
A minimum 12 inch thick thickened edge is recommended for the
driveways
Joints are recommended at about 8 to 10 foot intervals and at
discontinuities in order to allow for some relative movements of the
concrete

Drainage
The finished lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings
should be shaped to shed water away from foundations and to avoid
ponding condition Also it is advised that a gutter system be
installed around the residence and that gutter water be diverted far
away from the house foundation and driveway Excessive landscape
watering near foundations should also be avoided
Planters next to the foundation should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper
performance of the above recommended foundations since ponded water
could cause excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving
or loss of strength The foundation excavation should be properly
backfilled tight against the slab footing immediately after setting of
the concrete to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained
to drain all surface run off away from the slab foundation
Design Review
Plans and specifications for foundation site grading and utility
construction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and
written comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included
CONSTRUCTION MONTTORING
It is recommended that the footing excavations be observed by a
soils engineer during construction to see that adequate bearing has been
obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the
findings of our exploration

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling three
3 test to 13 0 feet below the existing ground surface at
the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 A
truck mounted drill rig using 4 inch diameter continuous flight auger
was used to drill the borings
The soils encountered were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by one of our engineers who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory A graphic
presentation of the materials encountered in the borings are presented in
the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 3 All soils were classified in
accordance with the Unified Soil Classification System
In the borings soil samples were obtained by driving a 2 inch
standard split spoon sampler or a 2 4 inch I D split barrel sampler with
a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts used to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the Logs of Borings at the
appropriate sample depths
