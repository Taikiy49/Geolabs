Project Considerations
The project site is located on Aoloa Place in Kailua on the island of Oahu Hawaii
Several units at the property have experienced cracks in the walls and floors over a
period of several years It is suspected that the damage may be due to settlement of the
building foundations We understand that a settlement monitoring program was
implemented for selected columns at the area of distress starting on March 28 1996 An
evaluation of the existing building condition and recommendations pertaining to possible
stabilization methods were desired

Regional Geology
The island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lavas from two
shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be
middle to late Pliocene in age and Koolau is estimated to be late Pliocene to early
Pleistocene in age When the building of the Waianae and Koolau volcanoes came to an
end only a slight embayment existed on the southern coast of Oahu
During the Pleistocene Epoch Ice Age sea levels fluctuated in response to the
cycles of continental glaciation As the glaciers grew and advanced less water was
available to fill the oceanic basins such that sea levels fell below the present stands of the
sea When the glaciers melted and receded an excess of water became available such
that the sea levels rose to above the present sea level
The higher sea level stands caused the formation of deltas and fans of
accumulated terrigenous sediments in the heads of old bays and lagoonal marine
sediments in the quiet waters protected by fringing reefs The lower sea stands caused
streams to carve valleys through the sediments Subaerial exposure of the sediments
caused consolidation of the soft deltaic materials and lagoonal deposits Land
development and reclamation projects within the last century have brought the project site
to its present form
Existing Site Conditions
The project site is located in Kailua on the island of Oahu Hawaii The building
complex is located to the east of Aoloa Place as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The Poinciana Manor was built in early to mid 1970 s The existing building is a
four story timber structure with first level parking area Above the parking area the
second level is comprised of 7 5 inch thick post tensioned concrete flat plate The
12 inch diameter concrete columns support the upper three levels and are founded on
spread footings
During our site visit significant cracks in the walls and floors were observed in
some of the units In addition several noticeable distortions on door and window frames
were observed Based on discussions with residents of the units the majority of the
visual distress has occurred within the past several years The damages appear to be
concentrated around the southwestern corner of the apartment complex The affected
units include Unit 457 and several other units in the vicinity

Subsurface Conditions
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by drilling and
sampling one boring designated as Boring No 1 to a depth of about 30 2 feet below the
existing ground surface The approximate location of the boring is shown on the Site
Plan Plate 2
In general our field exploration encountered fill to about 3 feet below the existing
ground surface consisting of dense clayey sand The fill was underlain by stiff to very
stiff silty clay with organic materials to about 9 feet below the existing ground surface
Below this layer lagoonal deposit was encountered to the maximum depth explored of
30 2 feet below the existing ground surface The lagoonal deposit generally consisted of
loose coral gravel and sand
Groundwater level was encountered in Boring No 1 at 9 1 feet below the ground
surface during our field exploration It should be noted that the water levels at the project
site may be influenced by seasonal precipitation tides storm surge conditions and other
factors
Detailed descriptions of the materials encountered in the boring are presented on

soil samples are presented in Appendix B

Evaluation of Existing Conditions
Based on a site visit discussions with the resident manager and residents our
field exploration and our past experience in the vicinity of the project site an evaluation
of existing conditions was made It is our opinion that the building distress is caused by
settlement of the structure Several possible explanations for the ground settlement are
described in the following subsections
Long Term Ground Settlement
Settlement may result from structural column loads and new fill loads Whenever
new loads are introduced ground settlement could occur due to compression of
the loose soils under the site The amount of total settlement and the time for a
majority of the settlement to occur will vary depending on the subsurface
conditions Generally the majority of the total settlement will occur within the first
several years However long term settlement could continue for many years at
diminishing rates
We estimate that footing settlements due to the structural loads would be on the
order of 1 to 2 inches An additional 1 to 2 inches of settlement could occur due to
2 to 3 feet of fill placed to achieve the present grades Long term settlements
would usually be on the order of 10 to 20 percent of the total settlement
Structures made of timber exhibit relatively high flexibility compared to other
materials such as concrete which tends to be more brittle This combined with the
strength and flexibility of the post tensioned slab may explain why signs of distress
became visible only several years ago while most of the ground settlement due to
the structural loading should have occurred within the first several years after
construction Because of its high flexible strength wood structures can withstand
relatively high levels of distress without visual symptoms It is possible that
continuing long term settlements finally reached a tolerable limit for the timber
structure and started showing signs of distress several years ago

Areal Dewatering
Decline in groundwater table may induce ground settlements in affected areas
When the groundwater level drops the soil is no longer floating in water Thus it
effectively increases the load on the underlying soil and subsequently may cause
ground settlement
Areal dewatering refers to decline of groundwater level in a large general area
This process may take years to develop and has significant effects on the natural
environment Changes in the natural topography may affect drainage patterns of
the Kawainui Swamp and nearby areas Alteration of drainage patterns may
cause significant and complex changes in the area including lowering of the
groundwater table
Upon review of available information it appears that areal dewatering is an unlikely
major cause of recent problems at the Poinciana Manor Based on our field
exploration and past experience in the project vicinity we did not find evidence of
significant areal dewatering The present groundwater level appears fairly
consistent with past groundwater levels in the area Furthermore areal dewatering
should cause settlement problems in surrounding areas as well as the subject
site Because the distress is concentrated in a small area we believe that areal
dewatering is an unlikely cause of settlements at Poinciana Manor
Settlement Caused by the Adjacent Development
We understand that construction of the Hokulani In Kailua housing development
began around 1990 The Hokulani In Kailua development is located adjacent to
the problem areas at Poinciana Manor across the street on Aoloa Place The
adjacent development consisted of two to three story wood framed buildings
In general adjacent development could cause ground settlement to neighboring
structures due to several factors including introduction of new loads vibration
during construction and dewatering of groundwater levels in the vicinity of the site
New loads including fill and column loads are introduced whenever a new
structure is built Influence from adjacent loads can travel both laterally and
vertically and affect nearby structures In this case the loading from timber
structures and new fills may be relatively small More importantly the lateral

distance between the problem areas at Poinciana Manor and the closest
Hokulani In Kailua building is over 80 feet Given the lateral extent between the
two structures we believe that influence from the adjacent development on the
Poinciana Manor is negligible
Vibration is commonly induced by various operations during construction As with
other loading scenarios vibration can travel laterally and affect structures in its
vicinity Subsurface conditions consisting of loose granular materials such as
sands and gravels are most susceptible to settlement under vibratory loading
However the distance separating the two properties makes vibration from
adjacent development an unlikely source of settlement at Poinciana Manor In
addition vibration from construction would have only been temporary If this was
the cause of ground settlement at Poinciana Manor distress problems would have
terminated after construction
As explained in the Areal Dewatering subsection dewatering or lowering of the
water level may cause settlements to adjacent structures In general dewatering
is commonly used during construction to temporarily lower the groundwater table
This allows machines to excavate below groundwater table and install utility lines
storage tanks etc without having to work under water Influence from
groundwater drawdown could extend laterally to nearby areas but given the
relatively far distance between the two properties it is unlikely that dewatering
during construction of the adjacent development caused the settlement at
Poinciana Manor In addition dewatering during construction is also only a
temporary influence Continuing settlements should not occur after construction of
the adjacent development
Foundation Underpinning
As discussed earlier continuous distress at the southwestern corner of the
Poinciana Manor is evident from visual signs at several units and settlement monitoring
program conducted by Hawaii Land Consultants It is our opinion that foundation
settlement is the primary cause of distress at the subject site Generally we anticipate
that the settlements may decrease over time If it is desired to stabilize the building
foundations to mitigate future settlement problems we recommend that underpinning of
the shallow foundation be considered
There are several different methods to underpin the foundations Some of the
common types that are used include helical anchors and steel pipe piles In general the
design and execution of underpinning system are the responsibility of the contractor and
therefore are outside the scope of this report The contractor should determine the
method and equipment to be used subject to practical limits and safety considerations

In general the underpinning system should extend vertically down to a firm
bearing layer Based on our boring the loose lagoonal deposits were encountered to the
maximum depth explored of about 30 2 feet below the existing ground surface The
underpinning system should be installed to resist the structural loads by side friction
and or end bearing For preliminary estimating proposes we assume that each affected
footing would require two helical anchor supports each to a depth of 50 feet
