In accordance with your request we have performed a geotechnical
engineering evaluation at the above referenced site Our work was done in
general accordance with our proposal of
The purpose of the evaluation was to explore the subsurface
conditions at the site to provide soil data for use in the foundation
design of the proposed antenna
The results of our field exploration which we used in the
engineering analysis to formulate our conclusions and recommendations are
presented in the appendices of this report
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
PROJECT DESCRIPTION
It is proposed to construct a new communication antenna in the
eastern portion of the new Kailua Police Department on the island of
Hawaii

SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is located in the eastern portion of the New Kailua
Police Department on the island of Hawaii The ground surface at the site
was generally flat and surface conditions consisted of a boulder fill
However site grading operations were being conducted at the time of our
exploration therefore surface conditions may be subject to change A
2 foot high fence wall lies approximately 10 feet north of the site
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
One 1 boring was drilled and sampled at the site to a depth of 13
feet below the existing ground level with a truck mounted drilling rig
The approximate boring location is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Based on our field exploration the site is underlain by about 2 feet
of cobbles boulders fill over slightly weathered moderately to severely
jointed basalt
No groundwater was encountered during our exploration

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Tower Foundations
It is our understanding that spread footing foundations have been
selected for the antenna support Based on the preliminary design
drawings supplied to us 4 by 4 foot footings embedded 6 feet below the
ground surface are planned for each leg
For footings bearing on the underlying basalt the following soil
parameters may be used for the foundation design or to check the adequacy
of the preliminary design
a Active Soil Pressure 30 p c f
b Passive Soil Pressure 370 p c f
c Internal Friction Angle 40 degrees
d Bearing Value 6000 p s f
e Soil Unit Weight 140 p c f
f Friction coefficient 0 5
Between in situ basalt and concrete
Excavation and Backfilling
Excavation and backfilling would be required for the construction of
the tower foundations The bottom of the excavations should be cleared of
loose material prior to the placement of concrete
Based on our field exploration we believe that the excavation into
the underlying basalt may require special excavating procedures such as
hoeram to make the proposed cuts However the actual rippability of the
basalt formation should be determined by the contractor on site
Excavated on site basalt broken down to 6 inch minus size may be
used as a source of fill material If required all imported fill
material should consist of selected non expansive silty sandy or granular

Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comments
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earth work and foundation recommendations given herein If this
review is not made the geotechnical engineer can assume no responsibility
for misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Observation
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during the excavation and
construction phases of the foundation work This is to observe compliance
with the design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow
design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from that
anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in
this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive granular
material tested and approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to
hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as borings
and observations Variations of conditions between the field data points
may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become
evident until construction If variations then appear evident it will be
necessary to re evaluate the recommendations given in this report
The location of the field data points were determined by tape
measurement on the site with reference to the preliminary site plan
provided The physical location of the field data points should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between rock types
and as such may denote a denote a gradual transition The strata lines
shown on profiles or cross section are based upon interpolation between
field data points and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
This report has been prepared for the exclusive use of the project
designers for specific application to the foundation design of the project
in accordance with generally accepted geotechnical engineering principles
and practices No other warranty expressed or implied is made
This report has been prepared solely for the purpose of assisting the
architect engineer in the preliminary design evaluation of the proposed
project Therefore this report may not contain sufficient data or the
proper information for use in contract bid estimation A contractor
wishing to bid on this project is urged to retain a competent geotechnical
engineer to assist in the interpretation of this report and or in the
performance of additional site specific exploration for bid estimating
purposes
