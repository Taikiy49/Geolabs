In accordance with our revised fee proposal dated August 9, 2018, Geolabs 
observed the probing and grouting operations for the Kihei High School project located in 
Kihei on the Island of Maui, Hawaii. This letter report summarizes our geotechnical 
observations during the probing and grouting operations for the project. 
The new Kihei High School structures will be supported on shallow foundations 
consisting of spread and continuous strip footings. To reduce the potential for loss of 
foundation support resulting from the collapse of cavities or compression of loose zones 
below the building structure, a probing and grouting program consisting of a total of 766 
4-inch diameter probe holes was implemented. Probing and grouting was performed for 
spread and continuous strip footings for Buildings A, B, C, D, and H, Retaining Walls B 
and F, Well No. 1, and the Pedestrian Bridge. 
Geolabs provided geotechnical engineering consultation for the Kihei High School 
project, and the findings and recommendations are presented in the referenced report. 
The purpose of our geotechnical observations was to monitor the geotechnical 
engineering aspects of the probing and grouting operations for conformance with the intent 
of the recommendations presented in our report. In addition, we provided “Special 
Inspection” services (as stipulated in Section 1704 of the 2012 International Building Code)

PROJECT DESCRIPTION 
The project consists of the development of a new public high school in the town of 
Kihei on the Island of Maui, Hawaii. The project site encompasses approximately 70 acres 
of undeveloped land east of Piilani Highway, between Kulanihakoi and Waipuilani 
Gulches, as shown on the Project Location Map, Plate 1.  
As indicated in the referenced report, cavities and/or voids are commonly present in 
the basaltic lava flows and were encountered in several of the borings drilled at the project 
site.  To reduce the potential for loss of foundation support resulting from the collapse of 
cavities below foundations, Geolabs recommend implementing a program of cavity 
probing and grouting of the new structure foundations.  In accordance with this 
recommendation, probing and grouting was performed for spread and continuous strip 
footings for Buildings A, B, C, D, and H, Retaining Walls B and F, Well No. 1, and the 
Pedestrian Bridge. 
PROBING AND GROUTING OPERATIONS 
The probing and grouting operation was conducted over a time span from 
November 16, 2020 through January 29, 2021 under the near-continuous (full-time) 
observation by our field engineer. In general, the probe holes were located along the 
continuous strip footings (one probe hole at 10 feet on-center) and at each isolated spread 
footing location (one probe hole per 50 square feet). The approximate probe hole locations 
are shown on the Probe Hole Location Plans, Plates 2.1 through 2.9.  In general, the 
probe holes at the new structure foundations were drilled to depths between about 10 to 
21 feet below the existing ground surface, corresponding to approximately 10 feet below 
the design bottom of footing elevations. 
The probing and grouting operations were accomplished by Goodfellow Bros. 
utilizing a TD 55 Air Track Drill mounted on a SANDVIK DP1500I rock drill equipped with a 
4-inch diameter Downhole hammer. Pressurized air continuously flowed through the 
hollow rods while advancing to the target probe depth. Evidence of cavities/voids was 
observed by advancement rate and drilling intensity during the probing operation.  After 
completion of the probe drilling operation, the probe holes were tremie grouted through a 
tremie pipe.  The grout mix used for the project consisted of a fine grout mix provided by 
Hawaiian Cement. 
Our field representative observed the probing and grouting operations and recorded 
the probe time, drilling intensity, and approximate grout intake.  A total of 766 probe holes 
with an aggregate length of about 9,387 lineal feet below the existing ground surface 
were drilled for the nine structures, as summarized in the following table and the Probing 
and Grouting Summary, Plates 3.1 through 3.33.
