Construction Monitoring
Our part time observation of the construction activity at the subject site was
performed between May 15 2002 and August 14 2002 In general the construction
activities that were monitored included the rock and colluvial slope excavation trimming
site grading of the proposed buffer zone and the initial layout of the proposed fence
alignment
Based on our observation of the construction activities we believe that most of
the rockfall protection improvements were constructed in general accordance with the
nlans and specifications for the project However we understand that construction of
some portions of the proposed fence line have not been completed at this time in
accordance with the construction plans
Based on our monitoring of the construction activity there were some minor
revisions to the construction plan that were noted in our previous field reports that were
transmitted to your office during construction In addition to the work prescribed in the
construction plan the following is a summary of the significant plan revisions and
additional work items based on our site observations and discussions with the

Summary
The construction of the rockfall protection buffer zone and the heavy duty chain
link fence should reduce the potential hazard associated with possible falling rock at the
rear of the existing homes on Kapahu Street It should be noted that the occurrence of
rockfall is difficult to predict as it is a naturally occurrir y hazard associated with slope
erosion that is dependent on a variety of environmental factors The rockfall protection
system constructed at the project site serves to reduce the potential rockfall hazard by
the provision of an area buffer zone with fence for the catchment of potential rockfall
debris
In summary we believe that the project was constructed in general conformance
with the plans and specifications and the improvements should increase the safety of
the site from the perspective of potential rockfall It must be noted that rockfali events
may continue at the site due to the weathered condition of the rock slope Changes in
the slope condition due to erosion surface runoff and the passage of time should be
anticipated to occur The changes may affect rockfall activity at the site and the
residents should be informed that the potential for some rockfall remains
Therefore the residents are advised to refrain from the construction of structures
and limit the occupancy of the buffer zone areas Furthermore maintenance of the
buffer zone to keep the area free of soil and rock accumulations is strongly
recommended to preserve the function of the protective system The accumulation of
soil rock talus along the slope toe may reduce the width of the flat buffer zone area and
limit the effectiveness of the buffer zone to dissipate rockfall energy Damage to the
chain link fencing should be repaired in a timely manner to maintain the integrity of the
protective system
