PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of the geotechnical reconnaissance was to provide general
soil data in the area to aid in the site grading design of the
development Of particular concern would be to identify the general
occurrence of expansive clay and or soft deposits that need to be removed
and hard material that may be difficult to excavate as these would affect
the development costs

SITE_ DESCRIPTION
The site is located at the eastern end of Pauoa Road in Pauoa Valley
on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The site is bounded by the Pauoa Stream on
the east and Booth Road on the north The slopes next to the stream are
generally steep and covered with some surface boulders
At present the site is heavily vegetated with a thick growth of
grass and trees and is inaccessible to vehicle traffic The ground
surface in the southern half of the site is generally flat and slopes up

SUBSOTL CONDITION
Six 6 probing holes were hand augered to depths of 1 to 3 feet
below the existing ground surface at the approximate locations shown on
the Site Plan Plate 1 Dense gravelly and or boulderly soil conditions
were encountered at shallow depths and prevented penetration of the
probing holes to deeper depths
The soil conditions disclosed by our field reconnaissance consisted
of about 1 foot of brown to dark brown silty clay overlying clayey silt
with sand and gravel which may be a result of recent fill and or alluvial
deposits Further site exploration is needed in order to develop a more
accurate soil profile
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Our preliminary site reconnaissance encountered soil conditions
consisting of about 1 foot of silty clay overlying clayey silt with sand
and gravel Conventional site grading procedures are anticipated for
earthwork in this area
Subsequent to site clearance and prior to site grading borings
should be drilled to confirm the subsurface data from this preliminary
site reconnaissance and to explore possible critical areas
Recommendations based on this initial phase of soil engineering study
are presented in the following sections

Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limit should be thoroughly cleared Any unsuitable materials such
as previous fill rubbish soft material and vegetation should be
removed and disposed of properly off site
Loose spots encountered at subgrade level should be removed and
replaced with imported fill material compacted to a minimum of 90 of
its ASTM D 1557 78 maximum dry density
Based on our preliminary site reconnaissance and laboratory test
results the site is covered with a surficial layer of moderately to
highly expansive clay Depending on the thickness of the adobe
clay layer different slope ratios are recommended
Presently minimal cut or fill slopes are planned If required we
recommend a 3 1 H V slope ratio or flatter for proposed cuts in the
clay area Slope ratio of up to 2 1 H V is recommended for cut and
fill slopes in areas not covered by the adobe clay
Fill slopes should be constructed by overfilling past the design fill
slope and then cutting back
Surface water run off should be diverted away from the tops and toes
of cut and fill slopes and slope planting utilized to reduce
erosion

Fill Material
All imported fill material should consist of select non expansive
soil such as select borrow crusher waste or coral and should be
tested and approved by the soils engineer prior to being used in the
site
The fills should be placed in horizontal lifts not exceeding 8 inches
in compacted thickness and compacted to a minimm of 90 of its
maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78

House Foundations
The laboratory test results indicate that the on site clay soil
generally has moderate to high swelling potential depending on the initial
moisture content prior to subsequent saturation Therefore special
procedures for the house foundation design such as deep footings
subgrade saturation or capping with non expansive soil may be required
It is our understanding that the lots will be sold to individual
owners who will construct their own houses
A more detailed geotechnical engineering evaluation should be
conducted at each lot to provide detailed design criteria for house
foundations

Retaining Walls
Retaining walls of about 1 to 8 5 feet high are planned for the
proposed lots next to the Pauoa Stream The guidelines for preliminary
retaining wall design are listed below
1
For lateral earth pressures the following can be used
Walls unrestrained at the top 35 p s f equivalent
active condition level fluid pressure
backfill per foot of depth
Wall restrained at the top 55 p s f equivalent
at rest condition level fluid pressure
backfill per foot of depth
An allowable bearing value of up to 3000 p s f is recommended
for foundation bearing on the stiff on site soil
A passive earth resistance of 300 p s f per foot of depth may be
used for the wall footing on level areas The passive pressure
must reduced for walls on slopes next to the stream
A base friction of 0 4 may also be used to determine the
resistance against sliding of the wall
The wall foundations on relatively flat areas should be embedded
a minimim of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade
The wall foundations on existing sloping ground areas should be
embedded deeper to provide with an 8 foot minimum horizontal
set back distance from the toe of the wall to the sloping ground
surface
Backfills directly behind the retaining wall should consist of
granular non expansive material such as select borrow crusher
waste or 3B Fine gravel The top 12 inches of backfill should
consist of non expansive impervious material to reduce water
infiltration behind the wall

Access Road Pavement
Based on a CBR of 7 1 and previous experience in the area the
following preliminary pavement design section is recommended for the
access roadway extending from the end of Pauoa Road
2 0 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 0 Inch Base Course 95 Compaction
12 0 Inch Select Borrow
20 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
The select borrow may be reduced or eliminated if the top 2 feet
below the subgrade is fairly non expansive bouldery material
Additional CBR tests and or visual observations should be performed
on the actual subgrade soil samples during construction and the above
design section can be revised if necessary The recommended section
considers light traffic and that good drainage will be provided adjacent
to paved areas
Additional Exploration
An additional exploration utilizing borings or test pits should be
conducted prior to site grading to confirm the findings of our
reconnaissance study
