Gentlemen
This is to confirm our recent telephone conversation concerning the
utilization of a 54 diameter concrete pipe culvert in lieu of the
previously proposed box culvert at the subject project
The settlements at the center of the proposed fill were estimated
to approach five inches The transition between the point of zero
settlement and the point of maximum settlement will be gradual and
should not subject the concrete pipe to differential movement suffi
cient to cause cracking or distresses
A one foot granular bedding should be utilized to provide uniform
support In addition to the granular base it is recommended that
the lower half of the concrete pipe be backfilled with sand which
can be readily compacted by ponding Coarse grained granular ma
terial or the on site soils could be utilized as backfill but would
require hand compaction in a confined space Granular material or
sand can be utilized for the remaining backfill to cover the pipe
Should you have any questions concerning our recommendations please
do not hesitate to contact us

This report contains our recommendations based on a soil investi
gation for the proposed subdivision located south of Haiku Road in
Heeia Oahu Hawaii
The scope of our work at the site was to investigate the subsurface
soil conditions and to evaluate the earthwork requirements for a
proposed roadway fill and drainage structure along Kaahala tributary
stream
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
It is presently planned to subdivide the existing parcel into five
multi family residential lots A road approximately 1100 feet long

SITE CONDITIONS
Surface
The proposed development site is located immediately south of
Haiku Road Two tributaries of Kaahala stream form the southerly
boundary and traverses the center portion of the property Slopes
along the southerly tributary and the southerly bank of the center
tributary stream are steep approaching a 50 percent grade The
northerly bank of the center tributary have slopes of approximately
15 percent Between the tributaries the site is essentially level
with a slight slope to the east The ground surface elevations of
the site varies from elevation 116 feet at the stream invert to
approximately 168 feet in the southwesterly corner of the site
The site is densely vegetated with the upper portions covered with
tall elephant grass The stream bottom and banks have heavy
growth of thickets and trees The stream beds were dry at the
time of our investigation and no standing water was observed
Representative samples were sent to our laboratory for additional

Subsurface Conditions
Geologically the site is underlain by deeply weathered basalts
from the flows of the Koolau Volcanic Range These volcanic flows
were subsequently dissected by stream erosion which deposited
recent alluvium along the existing stream beds
The subsurface investigations revealed that the upper portion of
the site is underlain by a stiff reddish brown clayey silt which
appears to be a highly decomposed basalt residual soil This
material was encountered to the depths and 3

to a depth of 20 feet below the existing stream bed Groundwater
was not encountered in any of the borings during the period of the
subsurface explorations

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The alluvial soils located adjacent to the stream bed are moderately
compressible and will consolidate beneath the embankment loadings
The 20 feet of embankment is equivalent to a uniform load of
approximately 2000 psf upon the underlying compressible soils
The geometry of the fill embankment will vary the loadings from
zero at the abutments where little fill will be placed to a maxi
mum of 2000 psf in the center of the valley beneath the thickest
portion of the fill In addition to this differential loadings
there will also be a loading differential along the length of the
box culvert Beneath the outer slopes of the embankment the
loading will be essentially zero and will increase to the 2000 psf
uniform load near the center of the embankment
Based on the consolidation test results and the subsurface condi
tions established by the test borings it is estimated that the maximum
settlement will approach approximately five inches at the center
of the embankment where the new fill will be the thickest Essen
tially zero settlement is expected at the upper and lower ends of
the box culvert and at the transition from the natural soil onto
the fill
Page 6
W O 529 00
Box Culvert Design
It is recommended that the culvert be designed to resist earth
pressures equal to an equivalent fluid pressure of 45 pcf To
utilize this value it must be verified that the compaction of
each side of the culvert is adequate to prevent settlements along
the sides which could greatly increase the earth pressures due to
arching within the fill
A minimum of an eight inch granular base course extending one foot
outside the limits of the culvert should be provided as bedding
for the concrete The culvert should be designed to withstand
the differential settlements of approximately five inches from
each end to the center of the culvert It is estimated that these
settlements will occur slowly and there will be a gradual transi
tion between the areas that do not settle to the area of maximum
settlement Based upon a gradual transition between the different
areas the estimated five inch differential settlement appears to
be tolerable for the structure
To minimize the differential settlements it is recommended that
the initial five feet of fill be placed and compacted prior to
the construction of the culvert The area for the culvert can
then be excavated thru the compacted fill This will permit some
Page 7
W O 529 00
initial settlements due to the fill loads to occur prior to the
culvert construction Sufficient lateral clearance should be
provided to permit proper compaction of the culvert backfill
Site Preparation and Earthwork
All areas to receive fill should first be cleared of all delete
rious and organic materials and the surface scarified a minimum
depth of 12 inches Localized soft areas should be removed at
this time The scarified surface should then be brought back to
near optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum density
of 90 percent of the maximum dry density determined by ASTM Test
Designation D 1557 72 The fill should then be placed in hori
zontal lifts not exceeding six inches in thickness and compacted
to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum density Where the
natural ground surface is steeper than 5 horizontal to 1 vertical
the fill material should be keyed and benched into the firm
residual soil or as approved by the soils engineer
It appears that the onsite soils can be utilized for the fill
construction providing the moisture contents are properly controlled
by drying or watering to obtain adequate compaction Generally
the on site borrow material has an in place moisture content which
is above its optimum moisture

The grading operations and soil placement should be under the
direct supervision of the soils engineer to assure that proper
undercutting is accomplished and that the desired fill densities
are consistently achieved
Based upon the above construction recommendations a stability
analysis was performed assuming that the embankment would be con
structed with the on site soils at the proposed two horizontal to
one vertical 2 1 slopes This stability analysis resulted in
factors of safety varying from 1 5 to 2 9 which is more than
adequate for the proposed construction

Soil Bearing Pressures
Due to the soft to medium stiff consistency of the soils adjacent
to the stream bed it is recommended that the culvert end walls be
designed for maximum contact bearing pressures of 2000 pounds psf
Utilizing this bearing pressure the anticipated settlement is
estimated to be less than one half inch
Additional subsurface investigations will be required to develop
recommendations for the design and construction of foundations
for major structures such as multi story buildings The additional
studies would provide recommendations for maximum foundation bear
ing pressures required slope set backs and foundation bearing
levels The available information indicates that the existing
slopes are stable in their natural condition The effects of
foundation or additional fills must be evaluated on an individual
basis

Gentlemen
As requested by Mr Watson Lee we are pleased to submit this
proposal to perform a soils investigation for the proposed
subdivision off Haiku Road in Heeia Oahu Hawaii
It is presently planned to develop the site into five commer
cial subdivision lots The site is located on the sloping
banks of Keaahala Tributary Streams and is bordered on the
north by Haiku Road
To develop the necessary access roadway to the subdivision
embankment fill above the bottom of the tributary stream will
be required A box culvert and drainage structures will be
covered by approximately 16 feet of roadway fill material
It is planned to place 10 feet of fills on the north and 4
feet of cut is anticipated on the south bank of the stream
Based on a site visit by our geologist a review of the
general geology and the grading plan we believe the soil
conditions at the site consist of colluvium or residual soils
that are moderately uniform The banks along the stream are
sloping down toward the proposed culvert and will have to be
investigated for stability We would evaluate the subsurface
soil conditions at the stream bottom and prepare recommenda
tions regarding the placement of the roadway will and stability
of drainage structures
To accomplish the purposes of this study we propose to investi
gate the subsurface conditions en the site by drilling four
borings two at the stream bed and one at each bank 10 to 20
