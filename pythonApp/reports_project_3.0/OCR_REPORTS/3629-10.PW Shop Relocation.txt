INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed PW Shop Storage Relocation project located within the
Barbers Point Naval Air Station in the District of Ewa on the island of Oahu Hawaii The
general location and vicinity of the site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3629 10 Page 2
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering recom
mendations resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering
analyses These recommendations are intended for design input only and are subject to
the limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located near the junction of Enterprise and Shangrila Streets
within the Barbers Point Naval Air Station on the island of Oahu Hawaii Based on the
information provided it is proposed to construct an addition to the existing Building No 90
for use as PW Shop storage
Based on the current design concept the proposed addition will be a one story
structure with plan dimensions of approximately 60 by 75 feet We understand that the
new addition will be constructed immediately adjacent to the southern side of the existing
Building No 90 We also understand that the new structure will be constructed of
masonry walls with on grade concrete floor slabs
Based on a finish floor elevation of 41 5 feet Mean Sea Level MSL we
anticipate that site grading for the proposed project site will only involve minor cuts and
fills of less than one foot relative to the existing ground surface for foundation and slab
subgrade preparation

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lavas from two
shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be
middle to late Pliocene in age and Koolau is estimated to be late Pliocene to early
Pleistocene in age As volcanic activity in the Waianae ceased lava flows from the
Koolau banked against its eroded eastern slope forming a broad plateau now known as
the Schofield Plateau
Physical and chemical weathering followed by erosion of this plateau generated
sediments which were transported to the coast In the vicinity of the project site and to
the south these sediments accumulated and interbedded with marine sediments and
coraValga l reef formations to form a sedimentary wedge The thickness of the
sedimentary wedge ranges from zero in the area of the Interstate Route H 1 Highway to
over 1 000 feet at Ewa Beach This wedge forms the Ewa Plain and serves as the
confining formation or caprock over the artesian basal aquifers of southern Oahu
Deposition of sediments has continued from earlier geologic time through the present

DISCUSSION AND PRELIMINARY RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field exploration the proposed project site is underlain by loose to
very dense silty sands and sandy coral gravels below the asphalt pavement extending to
about 21 5 feet below the existing ground surface Based on the subsoils encountered at
the site we believe that a shallow foundation system consisting of spread and or
continuous footings bearing on the recompacted in situ soils and or compacted
engineered fill may be used for support of the proposed structure Detailed discussions
and geotechnical engineering design recommendations pertaining to the proposed
project are presented in the following sections of this report
Foundations
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered in our exploration we believe
that a shallow foundation system may be used to support the proposed one story
structure An allowable bearing pressure of 2 000 pounds per square foot p s f may be
used for design of isolated and continuous footings bearing on the recompacted in situ
soils and or compacted engineered fill This allowable bearing pressure is for dead plus
live loads and may be increased by one third 1 3 for transient loads such as wind and or
seismic forces In general footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3629 10 Page 7
the lowest adjacent finish grades For the portion of the new addition adjacent to the
existing Building No 90 footings for the new addition should extend to the same
elevation as the bottom of footings for the existing building to reduce the potential
settlements due to surcharge effects The excavations for the new footings adjacent to
the existing building should not extend below the existing building foundations
If foundations are located next to utility trenches or easements they should be
embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane extending
upward from the bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility
lines This requirement is necessary to avoid surcharging adjacent below grade
structures with additional structural loads and to reduce the potential for foundation
settlement
We estimate that footing settlements under the anticipated design loads for
footings bearing on the recompacted in situ soils or new engineered fills as
recommended herein to be less than one inch total with differential settlements on the
order of one half inch
For footings bearing on the in situ soils or new engineered fills the bottom of the
footing excavations should be recompacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative
compaction prior to the placement of reinforcing steel or concrete If soft and or loose
materials are encountered at the bottom of footing excavations they should be over
excavated to expose the underlying firm dense materials The over excavation should be
backfilled with select granular material compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative
compaction We recommend that the contract documents include additive and deductive
unit price for over excavation and engineered fill placement in the event that loose soft
subgrade soils are encountered during footing excavation
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3629 10 Page 8
In general lateral loads acting on the structures may be resisted by passive earth
pressure acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system and by frictional
resistance developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil A
coefficient of friction of 0 3 may be used for footings bearing on the recompacted in situ
soils or new engineered fills Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be estimated
using an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth p c f
This assumes that the soil around the footings is well compacted Unless covered by
pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the upper 12 inches of soil should be
neglected
Footing excavations should be observed by a representative of Geolabs Hawaii
prior to placement of reinforcing steel or concrete to confirm the foundation bearing
conditions and the required embedment depths
Slabs On Grade
Based on the current design concept we anticipate that concrete slabs on grade
will be used for the proposed structure We understand that the majority of the interior
slabs may be subjected to vehicular traffic such as forklift Where the concrete slabs will
be subjected to vehicular traffic we recommend that a 6 inch layer of compacted base
course be used below the slab to provide more uniform support of the slab Prior to
placement of base course the subgrade for slabs on grade should be scarified to a depth
of at least 8 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to not less than 95 percent relative compaction The base course should
consist of crushed basalt aggregate compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative
compaction For the design of structural slabs a modulus of subgrade reaction of about
250 pounds per cubic inch p c i may be used for the compacted base course A slab
thickness of 6 inches is recommended for preliminary design purposes for slabs
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3629 10 Page 9
subjected to vehicular traffic Provisions should be made for proper load transfer across
the slab joints which are subjected to vehicular traffic The thickened edges of slabs
adjacent to any unpaved areas should be embedded at least 12 inches below the lowest
adjacent grade
For interior building slabs not subjected to vehicular traffic a minimum 4 inch thick
layer of cushion fill consisting of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation may
be placed below the slab in lieu of the 6 inch compacted base course To reduce future
moisture infiltration and subsequent damage to floor coverings an impervious moisture
vapor barrier such as a plastic membrane is recommended on top of the gravel cushion
layer Two inches of moist fine sand should be placed above the plastic membrane to
provide protection of the membrane and to aid in curing of the slab concrete Flexible
floor coverings such as carpet or sheet vinyl should be considered since they can better
mask any minor slab cracking
Exterior concrete walkways may be required for the proposed project We
recommend that a minimum 6 inch thick layer of select granular material or select borrow
subbase be provided below the concrete walkway slab Construction joints should be
provided at intervals equal to the width of the walkways with expansion joints at right
angle intersections
The area adjacent to the slabs should be backfilled tightly against the slab with
non expansive impervious soil and graded to divert water away from the slab to reduce
the potential of water ponding around the foundation

Site Grading
We envision that site grading for the proposed project will only involve minor cuts
and fills of about one foot relative to the existing ground surface to attain a level ground
for the proposed building structure Site grading operations should be observed by a
representative of Geolabs Hawaii It is important that a representative from our office
observe the site grading to evaluate whether undesirable materials are encountered
during site grading and whether the exposed soil conditions are similar to those
encountered in our field exploration
At the on set of earthwork the existing pavement within the contract grading limits
should be completely removed All asphalt concrete debris rubbish and other unsuitable
materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site
Any soft weak or yielding areas disclosed during demolition and clearing
operations should be over excavated to expose firm dense ground and the resulting
excavation should be backfilled with engineered fill moisture conditioned to above the
optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Prior
to placement of the backfill material the over excavated subgrade should be scarified to a
depth of at least 8 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content
and compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction The excavated soft loose soils
should be properly disposed of off site
Following the demolition and clearing operation the subgrades should be scarified
to a minimum depth of 8 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture
and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction The compaction
requirement for pavement and slab subgrade should be increased to a minimum of
95 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3629 10 Page 11
soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil determined
in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91 Optimum moisture is the water
content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
In general the excavated on site soils may be reused as a source of general fill
materials provided that they contain no lumps or fragments greater than 3 inches in
maximum dimension If imported fill materials are required as general fill and backfill
materials the imported fill material should consist of non expansive material such as
crushed coral mudrock basalt or cinder sand The material should be well graded from
coarse to fine with no particles larger than 3 inches in maximum dimension The material
should have a laboratory CBR value of 12 or more and should have a maximum swell of
1 percent or less when tested in accordance with ASTM Test Designation 1883
Imported fill material should be tested by Geolabs Hawaii for conformance with our
recommendations prior to delivery to the project site for its intended use
Generally the fill and backfill materials should be placed in level lifts not exceeding
8 inches in loose thickness moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content
and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction The fill and backfill
material within the upper 2 feet below the finish subgrade in pavement areas should be
compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction

Utility Trench
We envision that new utility lines will be required for the proposed project In
general a granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33
No 67 gradation is recommended under the pipes Free draining granular materials
such as No 3B Fine gravel should also be used for the initial trench backfill up to about
12 inches above the pipes to provide adequate support around the pipes and to reduce
the compaction effort of the backfill thus reducing the possibility of damaging the
pipes It is critical that the free draining material be used to reduce the potential for
formation of voids below the haunches of pipes and to provide adequate support around
the sides of the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipes to
the top of the subgrade or finished grade may consist of the on site soils or select fill
material The backfill should be moisture conditioned to above the optinum moisture
placed in maximum 8 inch level loose lifts and mechanically compacted to not less than
90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for future ground subsidence
Where trenches are below pavement areas the upper 2 feet of the trench backfill below
the pavement subgrade should be compacted to at least 95 percent relative compaction
Drainage
The finished grades outside the building should be sloped to shed water away
from foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also advised that gutter
systems be installed around the building and that the discharge be diverted away from
the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near the foundations
and slabs should also be avoided Planters next to foundations should be avoided or
have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the subsoils
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3629 10 Page 14
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
foundations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation and subsequent
loss of strength of the near surface soils The foundation excavations should be properly
backfilled against the walls or slab footings immediately after setting of the concrete to
reduce water infiltration

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content ASTM D 2216 and unit weight determinations were performed
on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties
The results of these tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample
depths
Two sieve analysis tests ASTM D 422 were performed on selected samples of
the soils to evaluate the gradation characteristics of the soils and to aid in soil
classification Graphic presentation of the test results is provided on Plate B 1
Two California Bearing Ratio CBR tests ASTM Test Method D 1883 were
performed on cuttings of the near surface soils to evaluate the soil strength for preliminary
pavement design Results of the CBR tests are presented on Plates B 2 and B 3
