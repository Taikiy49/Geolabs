Project Background
The project site is located at the Ewa Villages Subdivision B Phase 4A area of the
Ewa Villages Development in the District of Ewa on the Island of Oahu Hawaii Based on the
plans provided Subdivision B is divided into 126 lots covering an area of approximately
19 65 acres We understand that the area has been previously mass graded by the placement
of approximately 5 to 8 feet of fill materials The roadway construction and_ utility
improvements for the subdivision were completed about 4 to 5 years ago however the
individual properties are partially developed at this time The mass grading and infrastructure
installation work were performed by Oahu Construction Company Ltd with Pacific Rim
Consulting and Inspection Corporation performing the field compaction testing

The subject subdivision was served by an extension of the existing Park Row
designated as Road A as well as four secondary streets designated as Road B through
Road E as shown on the Site Plan Plate 1 Based on visual observations conducted at
the site a significant amount of pavement distress was observed aiong the majority of the
paved roadways especially along Road B and Road C
The distress observed generally consisted of longitudinal cracking in the asphaltic
concrete pavements settlement of the pavement surface especially around some of the
manhole structures and sagging of portions of the concrete rolled curb and gutter
Therefore it was decided to proceed with the reconstruction of the existing roadways at the
subject site
Earthwork Observations
The earthwork operations for the roadway reconstruction work were performed by
Jayar Construction Inc During the earthwork operations the existing pavements along
Roads A through E were excavated down to about 31 inches below the design finished
grades The over excavated subgrade was then proof rolled to obtain a minimum of
90 percent relative compaction A single smooth drum vibratory roller a
CASE 252 tandem smooth drum vibratory roller a Hamm 2420 roller a roller
a rammax and a jumping jack were used to provide the compaction efforts for this project
Subsequent to the preparation of the over excavated subgrade a layer of
geogrid was then placed directly over the compacted subgrade with a
minimum 18 inch overlap between adjacent rolls of the geogrid Following the placement
of the geogrid the over excavated roadway was brought up to the finished grade with
12 inches of recycled base course and subbase 10 inches of imported new aggregate
subbase 7 inches of imported new aggregate base course and topped with 2 inches of
asphaltic concrete Both the imported and recycled aggregate base course and subbase
were compacted to a minimum of 95 percent of the maximum dry density as determined in
accordance with American Society of Testing and Material ASTM Test Designation D 1557
Modified Proctor
Samples of the on site and imported aggregate base course and subbase materials
were obtained and transported to our office for laboratory testing The maximum dry densities
and optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test resuits are summarized on
Plate 2 of this report
Periodic field density tests were performed during the roadway reconstruction to
confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures and soil conditions were
noted A total of 392 field density tests including retests of failed areas were performed using
the nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM Test Designations D 2922 and D 3017

Limitations
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe activities occurring
simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork may have occurred
without our observation However the level of earthwork observation and testing services
provided appear to be reasonable in reducing some of the geotechnical risks to the project
It should be recognized that there are variations in the accuracy and statistical
repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor earthwork construction and materials
Our services were performed in accordance with generally accepted standards of geotechnical
