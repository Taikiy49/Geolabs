In accordance with our proposal we have completed
an investigation of the coral cavities beneath the major footing
locations of the proposed 12 story apartment structure This
letter summarizes our findings and conclusions

During May and June 1974 the subsurface soil conditions at the
site were explored by drilling 24 test borings utilizing truck
mounted rotary equipment The locations of the borings are
shown on the Boring Location Plan Plate 1 The drilling opera
tions were supervised on a full time basis by a field engineer
who classified by visual and textural examination the soils and
coral encountered The classifications were verified by inspec
tions and testings in the laboratory Standard penetration test
SPT were also taken at selected depths The SPT blow counts
are shown on the attached Boring Logs Plates A 1 thru A 22 at
the appropriate sample depth
To provide data for evaluating the strength characteristics of
the soils encountered three direct shear tests were performed
on selected ring samples obtained from Boring C 2A2 The
results of these tests are shown on Plates A 23 thru A 25
DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Pile Foundations
Due to the predominance of cavities within the coral formation
in the area of the major foundations between Column Rows 1 and
7 it is recommended that these foundations be supported upon
concrete piles bearing within the clayey silt below the coral
formation In order to bypass the upper 25 feet of the dense coral
ING
Page 3
W O 508 10
it will be necessary to predrill for the piles It is estimated
that a 12 inch square concrete pile with a length of 45 feet
will provide a design capacity of 40 tons per pile
The piles should be spaced no closer than three pile diameters
otherwise group action may reduce the pile capacities It is
normally recommended that three test piles be driven to esta
blish the order lengths for the precast piles It is our under
standing from Nordic Construction Company that it may be more
economical to order piles having a length of 45 feet and cut
off the excess This would eliminate the construction costs
due to the delay time and equipment costs resulting from driving
the test piles then waiting for pile deliveries It is our
opinion that the required pile lengths will not exceed 45 feet and
will be in the range of 35 to 45 feet
It is recommended that the pile driving operations closely
follow the predrilling to minimize the in flow of loose materials
into the predrilled hole Some difficulties can be expected
in the lower portions where the loose sands are encountered
The predrilled holes should have a diameter in excess of the
pile diagonal measurement to assure that the piles do not bind
within the coral formation

It is anticipated that the piles can be driven to the desired
20 feet penetration without exceeding 100 blow per foot driving
resistance for the last foot with a hammer delivering a minimum
of 25 000 foot pounds ofenergy The actual driving criteria
may be established by a driving test prior to construction or
by the soils engineer during the initial stages of the founda
tion construction The recommended pile capacity of 40 tons
per pile is based upon attaining a minimum penetration of 20 feet
and a final driving resistance in excess of 80 blows per foot
for the last two feet The driving criteria may be modified
during construction if excessive resistance is encountered
The anticipated settlement on the driven piles is estimated to
be less than 4 inch assuming that group action does not take
place The pile cap designs should be submitted to the soils
engineer to evaluate the effects of group action on the pile
Capacities and settlements It is also recommended that all pile
driving be done under the supervision of a soils engineer to
assure that proper penetration is being achieved
Spread Foundations
The borings in the area of Column Rows 7 thru 11 encountered
medium dense to dense coral sand without voids Foundations in

this area can be designed as spread foundations with contact
bearing pressures of 5000 pounds per square foot for dead plus
real live loads The spread foundations should bypass the upper
soft fill and bear upon the coral material A minimum foundation
depth of two feet below the final adjacent grade should be main
tained Maximum anticipated settlements for spread foundations
bearing upon the coral is estimated to be less than one half of
an inch The estimated maximum differential settlement between
the spread foundations and the pile foundations is therefore
one fourth of an inch
The foundation at Boring D 6 can be designed as either a pile
foundation or a spread foundation since no voids were encountered
at this location The void locations and recommended foundation
types are summarized on Plate 1
