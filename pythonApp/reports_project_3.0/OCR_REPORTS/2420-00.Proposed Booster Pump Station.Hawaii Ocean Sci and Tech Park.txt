INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the proposed booster pump station in the Hawaii
Ocean Science and Technology H O S T Park at Keahole Point on the
island of Hawaii Hawaii The general location of the project site and
its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
GEOLABS HAWAII

This report summarizes the findings of our exploration and presents
our geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from analyses of the
data obtained during the exploration These recommendations are intended
for design input and are not intended for construction estimation or
bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The original concept of the proposed construction was to build a
booster pump station to raise the pressures of two cold water supply
systems the 12 inch and 18 inch NEIH Natural Energy Laboratory of
Hawaii pipes and the Ocean Farms pipes to provide standby flows to
water pipe system failed for any reason
Currently it is proposed to construct the booster pump station also
to raise the pressures of all cold water flows to the upper elevations at
H O S T Park
The proposed booster pump station will measure approximately 36 feet
by 39 feet in plan and 12 feet in height and will be a double well
structure constructed of reinforced concrete The smaller wet well will
hold cold water inflow which will be pumped away at higher pressures by
flygt pumps installed in the adjoining dry well
The concrete structure will be embedded approximately 7 to 8 feet
below the existing site grades or to 2 feet elevation above Mean Sea
level MSL The dry well will be accessible through a stairway covered
above ground by a small 7 foot high building which will become the station
entrance

A 30 feet by 36 feet parking area is planned on the western side of
the pump station A 12 foot wide access road will connect the new parking
area to the future H O S T Park subdivision road
Minimal site grading is anticipated for the proposed construction

geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to the foundation site

the Department of Accounting and General Services D A G S Division of

by an Engineer from our firm
4 Laboratory visual observation of the rock core samples obtained
5 Engineering analyses of the field and laboratory data for the
purpose of developing foundation site grading and pavement
design recommendations

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located in the Hawaii Ocean Science and
Technology Park at Keahole Point on the island of Hawaii Hawaii It is
accessible via the Main Road of the Park and the future H O S T Park
subdivision road
The site is generally level with ground elevations at approximately
9 to 10 feet MSL elevations
The existing ground is primarily lava rock
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by
drilling and sampling five 5 borings to depths of 16 to 21 feet below
the existing ground surface The approximate locations of the borings are
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Hard vesicular to dense basalt lava flow was encountered throughout
the site during our exploration The volcanic rock formation is slightly
to moderately weathered massive to slightly moderately jointed and
extended to the maximum depth explored of 21 feet below the existing
ground surface

The basaltic rock formation encountered is in the pahoehoe form as
characterized by the ropy hummocky surface of the rock Pahoehoe lava is
prone to having subsurface voids or cavities which were created when the
lava flow was deposited Voids were encountered at various depths in
Boring and 4
Groundwater levels were measured at about 9 to 10 feet below the
existing ground surface corresponding to about Mean Sea Level during our
field exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration encountered hard lava flows at the project
site Hard excavation is anticipated for the proposed pump station
construction
Excavation
Excavation into the lava rock formation may require hard ripping with
hoerams To expedite excavation properly controlled blasting may be used
to increase construction efficiency Care must be exercised to avoid
over ripping or over shooting which may disrupt the structure of the rock
formation The method s of excavation and or blasting should be the
choice of the contractor within practical limits and safety
considerations
The rock formation is generally hard and may be excavated to a slope
of approximately Horizontal Vertical for the pump station
construction Some excavation support may be required to prevent loose
rock fragments on the slope from falling into the excavation
The actual extent of shoring required should be determined by the
actual conditions of the excavation walls after ripping and or blasting
As a result the contractor should be made solely responsible for the
safety and adequacy of the design and construction of the shoring system
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2420 00 Page 6
Foundation
The basalt rock formation at the pump station site generally has good
bearing capacity The total weight of the new embedded structure
including its equipment and water loads will be lighter than the rock
excavated at the site Therefore the resulting net loading on the
underlying rock formation will be small
It is also anticipated that the new structure can be constructed
mostly in a ary condition since the bottom will be at 2 feet elevation
above Mean Sea Level
We recommend that a thin mat or footing foundation be used to support
the new pump station structure An allowable bearing capacity of 4000
p s f may be used for the foundation design
The above bearing value is for total dead plus live loads only It
may be increased by up to one third 1 3 for wind seismic and other
short duration loading considerations
Foundation settlements of the new structure should be negligible if
the above design recommendations are properly followed and proper
construction techniques are used
Resistance to lateral loads acting on the proposed structure may be
provided by passive earth pressure and frictional resistance We
recommend that an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 p s f per foot of
depth be used to calculate the passive earth resistance developed against
the buried walls of the pump station structure A frictional coefficient
of 0 4 may be used to calculate the frictional resistance developed
between the foundation bottom and the rock subgrade

lateral Earth Toads
Generally walls of embedded structures are subject to lateral earth
loads exerted by the surrounding soils The magnitude of the lateral load
will depend on the strength of the surrounding soils Since the in situ
rock formation has high strength minimal lateral loads should be imposed
on the pump station theoretically However the actual earth loads would
depend on the disturbance of the rock face after excavation blasting and
on the extent of backfill behind the structure walls
For design purpose we have assumed a large excavation with
substantial backfill behind the walls It is anticipated that the
underground walls will be relatively non yielding and therefore should
be designed for at rest lateral earth loads
We recommend that a lateral earth pressure of 45 p s f per foot
depth of wall be used for design This lateral earth load does not
include any hydrostatic load since the proposed structure will be
constructed two 2 feet above Mean Sea Level
Heavy storm and or tsunami conditions could cause a temporary high
groundwater level A higher lateral earth load of 90 p s f per foot
depth of wall including hydrostatic pressure may be used for this design
condition
Backfill
Backfill around the pump station will be required after the new
structure is constructed We recommend that well graded granular material
generally less than 6 inches in size and with less than 15 passing the
200 sieve be used as the backfill material
The backfill material should be compacted in level horizontal lifts
not exceeding 12 inches in loose thickness to a dry density of at least
90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78

Site Grading
Based on the anticipated finished grade of the proposed structure
minor site filling may be required around the pump station and for the
proposed parking area and access road to the pump station
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris
rubbish and other unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed
properly off site in order to prevent contamination of the excavated
material
The excavated rock material if reduced to less than 6 inches in
size should be suitable as fill and or backfill material If imported
fill material is required the material should be a select granular
material less than 6 inches in maximum size with less than 15 passing the
No 200 sieve
Fill should be placed in level horizontal lifts not exceeding 12
inches in loose thickness and then compacted to a minimm of 95 of its
maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
The site grading should be designed to divert surface water away from
the proposed structure
Pavement Section
Based on the anticipated rock or compacted fill subgrade conditions
the following asphaltic concrete pavement section may be utilized for the
preliminary design of the access road and parking areas
2 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 Inch Base Course 95 Compaction
8 Inch Total Pavement Thickness

Laboratory CBR tests should be performed on the actual subgrade soils
during construction and the above design section may be revised if
necessary The preliminary recommended section considers light traffic
and is based upon the assumption that good drainage will be provided
adjacent to the paved areas
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the project geotechnical engineer for review and written
comments prior to construction This review is needed to determine
adherence to the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If
this review is not made the geotechnical engineer can not assume
responsibility for misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Observation
It is recommended that the project geotechnical engineer be retained
to provide geotechnical engineering services during the earthwork and
foundation construction phases of the work This is to observe compliance
with the design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow
design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from that
anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in
this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from borings Variations of conditions
between the borings may occur and the nature and extent of these

The locations of the borings were approximately determined by scaling
from the proposed site layout plan based on a photo map dated February 9
1989 Elevations of the borings were determined by interpolation from
elevation contours on the plan The physical locations and elevations of
the borings should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by
the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
borings depict the approximate boundaries between soil types and as such
may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on profiles or
cross section are based upon interpolation between borings and may not
represent actual subsurface conditions
