SUMMARY OF RECOMMENDATIONS
Our field exploration disclosed that the project
site is underlain by rock formation at shallow
depths However due to the steep terrain at the
site varying amounts of cut and fills are
required to reach the design finished elevation
In order to reduce differential settlements to the
tank structure we recommend that either 1 all
of the tank foundations extend to rock formation
and a structural slab system be incorporated or
2 the upper areas be over excavated to provide a
minimm of 5 feet of structural fill under the
tank with the tank foundations bearing on the
structural fill
The text of this report should be referred to for
detailed recommendations

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is situated in Waimea on the island of Kauai The
general location of the site is shown on the Project Location Map Plate
1
The tank site is located on the lower side of Waimea Canyon Drive at
an elevation of about 180 feet above MSL The ground surface slopes down
from the edge of Waimea Canyon Drive at a ratio of about 2 1 H V Visual
observation indicates that this steeper area is composed of fill from the
construction of the road The remainder of the site slopes down toward
the south at a ratio of about 4 1 H V The existing ground is presently
covered with haole koa and loose surface boulders
PROJECT CONSTDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a 0 5 million gallon capacity reinforced
concrete tank structure on the site The tank will be approximately 70
feet in diameter and 23 feet in height
The details of the proposed site grading were not known at the time
of our exploration However it is anticipated that up to 8 feet of cut
and 20 feet of fill will be required to create a level pad at an elevation
of 180 feet for the tank and access road

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
The island of Kauai is composed of a single basalt shield volcano
built by extrusion of the lavas of the Waimea Canyon Volcanic Series
during the Late Pliocene more than 2 3 million years before present
Preliminary reconnaissance of the site and its vicinity indicates that the
area is underlain at shallow depth by Waimea lavas
Subsurface Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by
drilling and sampling four 4 borings to depths of 20 to 21 feet below
the existing ground surface The approximate locations of the borings are
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Based on the field exploration it appears that the portion of the
site adjacent to Waimea Canyon Drive may be underlain by up to 8 feet of
fill over basalt rock formation The remaining areas are underlain by
basalt rock formation The basalt formation is weathered but generally
hard and competent A generalized subsurface cross section through the
tank site is illustrated on Plate 3
Groundwater levels were not encountered at the time of our field
exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration indicated that the proposed tank site is
underlain by weathered rock formation at shallow depths However due to
the relatively steep sloping grade at the site and the need to maintain a
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2107 00 Page 4
Dk No 502
finished grade of 180 feet varying amounts of cuts and fills will he
required to develop the site Cuts up to 8 feet are required for the
higher areas of the site while fills up to 16 feet are required for the
lower portions This condition is illustrated on Plate 3
The differences in bearing materials and variable fill thickness
below the proposed tank will induce differential settlement to the tank
structure
Two alternatives to reduce differential settlement are presented
below
Alternative 1 Foundations Bearing on Rock
The tank foundations including the perimeter and individual spread
footings could be extended through the fill and bear on the underlying
rock formation A structural slab system should also be used to reduce
differential settlements to the slab Due to the thickness of the fill
it appears that it would be more expedient to construct the foundation
walls and footings prior to backfilling around them instead of filling
the site first and then excavating for the foundations
An allowable bearing value of 6000 p s f may be used for the design
of foundations bearing directly on the in situ hard weathered rock This
value may be increased by one third when temporary wind and seismic loads
are considered
In order to resist lateral loads an equivalent fluid pressure of 300
p s f per foot of depth may be used A friction factor of 0 5 may be
utilized to determine the sliding resistance between the foundations and
rock formation
Based upon the boring data and the anticipated tank loads negligible
settlements are anticipated

To reduce the possibility of footing settlement due to the possible
cavities or lava tubes in the underlying lava rock the footing excavation
should be probed at approximately 10 feet on centers The probe holes
should have a minimum diameter of 2 inches and extend below the bottom of
footing a depth of 6 feet The probe holes should be grouted from the
bottom of the hole using low pressures If a cavity is suspected below
the footing additional probe holes should be made to delineate the
horizontal extent of the cavity The probing and grouting operations
should be monitored by the soils engineer or the project inspector to see
that these recommendations are carried out properly and to locate any
potential voids near the footing excavations
Alternative 2 Foundations Bearing on Fill
In order to reduce the differential settlement the upper portions of
the tank site should be over excavated for a minimum depth of five 5
feet below the finished grade and backfilled with compacted select
material In this way the entire tank site would bear on a minimm of
five 5 feet of compacted fill Some differential settlements would
still occur due to the difference in compressibility between the different
fill height i e 5 feet versus 16 feet however if the fill is properly
constructed and monitored we believe the differential settlements will
probably be small
In this method proper benching of the new fill and compaction of
each fill lift to the required minimum density is critically important to
the performance of the tank structure since excessive vertical or lateral
movements due to poor fill construction would affect the tank
An allowable bearing value up to 3000 p s f may be used for the
design of foundations bearing on properly compacted fill This value may
be increased by one third when temporary winds and seismic loads are
considered

Exterior wall footings should be embedded a minimm of 18 inches

first prior to foundation construction could be compacted to 95 maximum

Site Grading
In general the site grading will consist of cutting about 8 feet
into the upper areas at the tank site and filling up to 18 feet along the
lower areas
Due to the relatively steep slope at the site and high fills
required the proper placement and compaction of the fills are of critical
importance

Site Preparation
Prior the the commencement of earthwork the area within the grading
limits should be cleared and grubbed of all vegetation organic
material debris and other deleterious material
Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
The steep grade and near surface rock condition necessitate the
cutting of deep benches into the natural rock in order to provide
suitable bonding and stability of the new fill This requirement is
schematically illustrated on Plate 4
Fills should be moisture conditioned to near optimum moisture
content placed in horizontal lifts not exceeding eight inches 8
in loose thickness and compacted to a minimm of 95 maximm dry
density as determined by ASTM D 1557 78 Slopes should be
over filled at a steeper ratio and then cut back to the 2H 1V finish
Slope ratio
Fill Material
Most of the excavated material at the site will consist of cobbles
and boulders which are too large for use in the structural fills
Therefore imported fills are required We recommend that a
well graded granular material such as select borrow be used as
structural fill A surface layer of topsoil may be required for
landscaping purposes
It is anticipated that the site grading for the reservoir structure
will require fills up to approximately 20 feet in height Fill slope
ratios of 2 horizontal to 1 vertical may be used Slope benches

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the
foundation phases of the work This is to observe compliance with the
design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow design
changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from that
anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in
this report are contingent upon such observations
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
upon information obtained from the borings Variations of conditions
between the borings may occur and the nature and extent of these
variations may not become evident until construction If variations then
appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations
given in this report
The locations of the borings were determined by taping from
structures indicated on the topographic survey Elevations of the borings
were approximately determined by interpolation from elevation points on
the topographic survey map The physical location and elevation of the
borings should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the
method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
boring points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types and
as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross sections are based upon interpolation between borings
and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
