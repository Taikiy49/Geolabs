PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project involves the repair and improvements to the existing bike path along
Pearl Harbor from Waipio Point Access Road to Lehua Elementary School in the Waiawa
district on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The proposed improvements include grading of the
bike path shoulders repairs to the asphalt concrete pavement restriping of the bike path
installation of project regulatory and interpretive signs planting of grass and temporary
irrigation The general location of the project is shown on the Project Location Map
Plate 1
Minimal site filling is anticipated for the project construction Grading is generally
limited to clearing and leveling areas adjacent to the existing bike path
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our geotechnical reconnaissance survey was to obtain an overview
of the existing surface conditions at the site to develop a generalized soil data set for the
formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations pertaining to the design of the
grading work for the trail improvements To accomplish this we conducted an exploration
program consisting of the following tasks and work efforts

ANTICIPATED SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Based on a review of the geology our experience in the area and the field
reconnaissance performed at the site we anticipate that the subsoils underlying most of
the trail alignment consists of a thin surface layer of fill over thick deposits of soft harbor
mud Groundwater levels are anticipated at shallow depths
DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Minimal site filling is anticipated for the project except adjacent to the existing
sewage pump station where 1 to 2 feet of fill is planned For the remaining alignment the
grading work will consist of clearing and leveling the area next to the existing bike path
Site Preparation
In general the area within the contract grading limits should be thoroughly cleared
and grubbed at the on set of earthwork Vegetation debris deleterious material and
other unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site Soft and
yielding areas encountered during clearing below areas designated to receive fill or future
improvements should be over excavated to expose firm and or stiff material and the
resulting excavation should be backfilled with well compacted engineered fill The
excavated soft and or organic soils should be properly disposed of off site
After clearing grubbing and demolition areas should be excavated where
necessary to the finished grade level then scarified to a depth of about 8 inches
moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to a minimum of 85 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to
the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of
the same soil determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557 Optimum moisture is the
water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density

If the subgrade soils are pumping during subgrade preparation the pumping

Fills and Backfills
General fill materials required to raise the existing ground surface to the proposed
finished grades may consist of the excavated on site soils provided that they are free of
vegetation deleterious materials and rock fragments greater than 6 inches in maximum
dimension If required imported borrow should consist of material less than 3 inches in
largest dimension with a laboratory CBR value of 8 or more and should have a maximum
swell of 1 percent or less when tested in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1883
Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
General fill materials should be moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the
optimum moisture placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and
compacted to a minimum of 85 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers
to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density
of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 test procedures Optimum
moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum
dry density Compaction should be accomplished by sheepsfoot rollers multiple wheel
pneumatic tired rollers or other types of acceptable compaction equipment Water
tamping jetting or ponding should not be allowed for compaction of the fills
Fills placed on slopes steeper than five horizontal to one vertical 56H 1V should be
keyed and benched into the existing slope to provide stability of the new fill against sliding
The filling operations should start at the lowest point and continue up in level horizontal
compacted layers in accordance with the above fill placement recommendations Fill
slopes should be constructed by overfilling and cutting back to the design slope ratio to
obtain a well compacted slope face
Pavement Repair
Deteriorated portions of the bike path pavement will be repaired The repair will
consist of replacement of the 2 inches of asphaltic concrete surface course and 7 inches of
base course
Due to the anticipated high moisture contents of the in situ soils the pavement
subgrades should be compacted to a minimum of 85 percent relative compaction The
aggregate base course should consist of crushed basalt aggregate compacted to a
minimum of 95 percent relative compaction CBR and field density tests should be
performed on the actual subgrade soils encountered during construction to confirm the
adequacy of the above sections

Pavement Drainage
One of the primary distress mechanisms in pavement structures is pumping due to
saturation of the base course subbase and or subgrade soils Therefore the pavement
surface should be sloped and drainage gradients maintained to carry surface water off the
pavement to appropriate drainage structures The grade adjacent to the pavements
should be sloped to prevent ponding adjacent to the pavement section
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from visual observations Variations of subsoil conditions below the
surface may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident
until construction is underway If variations then appear evident Geolabs should be
contacted to re evaluate the recommendations presented in this report
