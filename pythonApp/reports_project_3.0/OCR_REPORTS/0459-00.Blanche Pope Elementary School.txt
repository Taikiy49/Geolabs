INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of a soils and foundation
investigation for the proposed multi purpose buildings at
the Blanche Pope Elementary School in Waimanalo Oahu Hawaii
The study consisted of subsurface borings laboratory testing
and engineering analyses as outlined in our proposal dated
Based on the results of our engineering
studies conclusions and recommendations pertinent to site
development are presented The plans dated December 1972
by Michael T Suzuki Associates were used during the study
PROJECT DESCRIPTION
The school property is located adjacent to Huli Street in
Waimanalo Oahu Hawaii The immediate building site is
irregularly L shaped and its location within the school
premises is indicated on the attached site plan Plate l
The site is relatively flat and is presently covered with
lawn grass
The proposed structure will be part one story and part two
stories in height and will be constructed of concrete The
maximum column loads are expected to be on the order of 70
kips while the maximum wall loads are expected to be on the

floor slab on grade
FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface exploration consisted of four 4 borings
ranging in depth from 20 to 25 feet The drilling was ac
complished using truck mounted rotary drilling equipment
with 9 inch diameter hollow continuous flight auger The
borings were continuously inspected by an engineer from our
firm The drilling contractor was Northwest Exploration
Services Inc
The approximate boring locations are shown on Plate 1 and
the boring logs are presented in Appendix A
In all inch I D ring samples or Standard
Penetration Test samples were taken at selected depth in
tervals The Standard Penetration Test samples were taken
by driving a 2 0 inch O D split spoon sampler for 18
inches with a 140 pound hammer free falling a distance of
30 inches The number of blows required to drive the sampler
the last 12 inches is an approximate measure of the relative
density or consistency of a soil The number of blows to

GEOLOGY AND SOIL CONDITIONS
A Geology
The proposed building site is located on recent alluvium
which overlies older coral algal reefs lithified beach
and dune sands
The Island of Oahu was formed throughout the Late
Tertiary Period starting approximately 3 5 million years
ago During the late Pliocene and Pleistocene Epochs
shallow water coral algal reefs covered the Waimanalo
area Fragmental debris derived from the reefs was
washed onto the prehistoric beaches and blown into dunes
These dunes and beach sands were later lithified cemented
into rock by downward percolating rainwater mixed with
Page 4
W O 459 00
March 16 1973
organic debris These weak organic acids dissolved some
of the calcium carbonate from the sands and redeposited
the carbonate material in the intersticies of the under
lying sands cementing them into rock Later continued
erosion of the Koolau Mountains supplied clay sized
material to the lowlying coastal swamps that once covered
the building site
Minor fill has been deposited over these clays to a
maximum depth of 2 feet in the proposed building area

Preparation of Ground
If any local soft spots are encountered in any areas
to receive fill they should be removed and replaced
with compacted structural fill Areas to receive fill
should first be scarified to a depth of 9 inches and
should be watered and recompacted according to the
requirements for structural fills
Fill Material
Onsite soils with the exception of deleterious ma
terial above can be utilized for compacted structural
fills if desired However when recompacted to the
requirements for structural fills the onsite materials
are very expansive and the recommendations contained
later under Expansive Soil are necessary
Imported fill material if planned should be tested
and approved by the soils engineer prior to importation
Imported fill shall be of a granular nonexpansive
nature and should meet the following specifications

Finish Grade
To provide uniform slab support the upper one foot
of soils beneath the finish subgrade over the entire
building pad should be compacted to a minimum of 90
of the maximum dry density
Depending upon the final grade of the structure either
one foot of compacted fill or rework of the onsite
surficial materials to obtain the minimum of one foot
compacted thickness beneath slabs will meet the
above recommendation

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
LABORATORY TESTING
A laboratory testing program was developed to establish the
engineering properties of the soils and rocks encountered at
the project site The tests where applicable to the subject
project are included in this Appendix
1 Classification Tests
a
b
Visual Classification All soil samples obtained from
the borings were brought to our laboratory where they
were visually reclassified to confirm or modify the
field classification prior to finalizing the Logs of
Borings
Water Content Determinations In addition to visual
classification typical samples were tested for natural
water content as an aid in soil classification and in
evaluating soil properties The water content values
are based upon the dry weight of the soil
Atterberg Limits Atterberg limit tests were performed
on selected clay and silt samples obtained from the
borings for the purposes of identification and correla
tion of soils Standard procedures ASTM D 424 for the
plastic limit and ASTM D 423 for the liquid limit were
used in the performance of these tests
1
d Grain Size Analysis Sieve analysis were performed an
granular soils For fine grained soils the hydrometer
test was performed to determine the distribution of
the grain sizes beyond the No 200 sieve
2 Shear Strength Tests
a Unconfined Compression Tests Intact core and split
spoon soil samples which apparently exhibited high
plasticity were tested for unconfined strength Length
to diameter ratio of 2 was maintained throughout the
tests to avoid scatter of test results and to facilitate
evaluation of shear strength values for design purposes
b Direct Shear Test In general soil samples were tested
for shear strength using a 2 41 I D direct shear box
Each set consisted of at least two tests each test run
under different normal loads The results are plotted
as Normal Stress vs Shear Strength with a failure envelope
drawn to determine values of cohesion c and angle of
frictional resistance
3 Consolidation Test Consolidation tests were performed on
selected undisturbed ring samples of the compressible
soils to provide basic data for making settlement calculations
Porous stones were placed on the top and bottom of the samples
to allow drainage Vertical loads were applied in increments
with each load increment being allowed to consolidate prior
2
to adding the next increment Measurements of the time
and consolidation were obtained during each load increment
and rebound was measured during the unloading portion
Consolidation test results are plotted in terms of percent
settlement versus applied loads
Swell Test The ring sample was placed between porous
stones and a 60 psf load was applied to the sample for a
24 hour period The difference between the initial and
final sample heights is the amount of swell which is expressed
as a percentage of the initial height of the sample
Maximum Density Test Samples of soils are compacted in a
mold of given size with a 10 pound rammer dropped 18 inches
to determine the relationship between moisture content and
density of the soil At least three trial points at dif
ferent moisture content are run to determine the maximum
density and optimum moisture content The test is run in
accordance with ASTM D 1557 70
