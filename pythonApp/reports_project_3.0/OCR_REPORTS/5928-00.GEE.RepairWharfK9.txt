EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is at Wharf K9 at the Fleet and Industrial Supply Center within the
Pearl Harbor Naval Station on the Island of Oahu Hawaii It is bounded by Wharf K8 to
the southwest Wharf K10 to the northeast Building 1900 to the east and open waters to
the west
Wharf K9 was originally constructed in the 1940s The wharf was constructed of an
approximately 10 inch thick concrete deck slab supported on approximately 2 5 foot deep
cap beams and 20 inch square concrete piles Based on the information provided the top
of deck elevation is at approximately 108 feet USND The middle one third of the wharf
deck between Bents 51 and 74 was lowered to approximately 104 feet USND The total
length of Wharf K9 is about 360 linear feet
A precast concrete sheet pile bulkhead wall was constructed along the landside of
Wharf K9 Along the waterside of the sheet pile bulkhead wall a concrete curtain wall
quay wall was constructed to connect the deck slab and the top of the concrete sheet pile
bulkhead wall
The existing timber fender piles to be demolished and removed are along the west
side of Wharf K9 Based on our field observations the existing timber fender pile system
appears to be in relatively poor condition with many of the fender piles broken or missing
The water level during our field exploration ranged from about 3 5 to 7 3 feet below the
existing pier deck pavement surface
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
We explored the subsurface conditions by drilling and sampling seven test borings
designated as Boring Nos 101 through 107 extending to depths of about 48 5 to 132 feet
below the existing pier deck pavement surface In addition two probes were performed
designated as Probe Nos 1 and 2 extending to depths of about 94 and 110 feet below the
existing pier deck surface The approximate boring and probe locations are shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2
Based on our borings along the existing wharf the borings performed by Pacific
Geotechnical Engineers Inc and the borings shown on the as built drawings provided
the mudline along the existing wharf at the project site is at about 70 feet USND
In general the subsurface conditions between Bents 48 and 78 are underlain by
relatively thick harbor deposits overlying very stiff to hard alluvium extending to the
maximum depth drilled of approximately 132 feet below the existing concrete deck The
harbor deposits encountered were generally very soft and weak The thickness of the
harbor deposits ranges from approximately 30 feet to about 70 feet below the mudline
elevation An idealized soil profile presenting the subsurface conditions encountered in our

FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
In general the subsurface conditions between Bents 48 and 78 are underlain by
relatively thick and soft harbor deposits overlying very stiff to hard alluvium extending to
the maximum depth drilled of approximately 132 feet below the existing concrete deck
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered and the structural loading
provided we believe that different pile tip elevations varying from 15 to 30 feet USND
will be required to provide the necessary lateral load capacity and bearing resistance for
the fender piles
The 20 inch square concrete replacement piles for the existing broken missing pile
at should be driven to an estimated pile tip elevation of about 60 feet
USND to bear on the relatively hard volcanic tuff formation mudrock in order to develop a
design compressive load capacity of 30 tons 60 kips per pile
Due to the potential presence for boulders and volcanic tuff formation in the shallow
subsurface the proposed fender and replacement pile locations as well as the sheet pile
wall alignment should be predrilled to the design tip elevations to facilitate the pile driving
and installation Detailed discussions and specific fender pile design recommendations
and other items of the design build project are presented in the following sections herein
Concrete Fender Piles
We understand that it is proposed to replace the existing timber pile fender system
with a 24 inch square precast prestressed concrete pile fender system In general the
concrete fender pile system will absorb the energy generated by vessel berthing velocities
Therefore the lateral load capacity of the 24 inch square precast prestressed concrete
fender piles is the main design consideration of the fender pile system
The lateral load analyses were performed using the program LPILE plus for
Windows which is a microcomputer adaptation of a finite difference laterally loaded pile
program originally developed at the University of Texas at Austin The program solves for
deflection and bending moment along a pile under lateral loads as a function of depth The
analysis was carried out with the use of non linear p y curves to represent soil moduli

The lateral deflection was then computed using the appropriate soil moduli at various
depths
Based on the information provided by the project structural engineer the lateral
load imposed on a single pile is approximately 21 5 kips acting at about 3 feet below the
top of pile We assume that the foam filled fenders will hang 3 feet below the top of pile
when reacting with the fender piles A distributed load of 21 5 kips per lineal foot applied
between 2 5 feet and 3 5 feet below the top of the fender pile was used in the lateral load
analyses
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered at the project site and the
structural loading provided we believe that five different pile tip elevations will be required
to provide the necessary lateral load capacity and bearing resistance for the fender piles

of the borings within the anticipated depths of the fender pile system Therefore we
recommend predrilling at each fender pile location to facilitate the installation of the
concrete fender piles The fender pile locations should be predrilled to near to about
6 inches above the estimated pile tip elevation provided previously In general we
recommend limiting the predrilling equipment diameter to the diagonal dimension of the
square piles Geolabs should confirm and or modify the depth and or extent of predrilling
based on field observations and or the subsurface conditions encountered during
construction In addition the harbor bottom should be inspected for broken timber fender
pile stubs debris and or other obstructions prior to installation of the concrete fender piles
Wharf Pile Replacement
As part of the project scope of work we understand that the existing
broken missing pile at about will be replaced by installing two new
20 inch square concrete piles adjacent to the original location of the missing pile
According to the information provided by the project structural engineer each
replacement pile will be subjected to a service load requirement dead plus live loads
of up to about 30 tons 60 kips

Based on the subsurface conditions encountered in Boring Nos 105 and 106
drilled in the vicinity of the broken missing pile we believe that the replacement piles
may develop the required compressive load capacity by end bearing onto the volcanic
tuff formation mudrock anticipated at a depth of about 50 feet below the existing deck
surface corresponding to an estimated pile tip elevation of about 60 feet USND
Due to the relatively low compressive load capacity required and the limited
number of piles involved we believe that a test pile program may be omitted for the
project However we recommend using a longer cast length of 60 feet to account for
the potential variation in the mudrock surface elevation As previously mentioned dense
mudrock boulders were encountered in some of the borings within the depths of the
replacement pile installation Therefore we recommend predrilling at each replacement
pile location to facilitate the installation of the concrete piles through the dense mudrock
boulder zone
Concrete Sheet Pile Bulkhead Repair
Based on the information provided there are gaps along the joints between the
existing concrete sheet piles at the bulkhead The original project scope of work proposed
to seal off the gaps in the existing concrete sheet pile bulkhead by constructing a soil mix
type wall using soil mixing jet grouting from an approximate Elevation of 98 feet
USND to the tip of the existing concrete sheet pile 74 feet USND and a reinforced
concrete wall from grade to approximate Elevation 98 feet USND immediately behind
the existing concrete bulkhead We understand that the design build team has
submitted a Value Engineering Change Proposal VECP to substitute the soil mix type
wall with a steel sheet pile wall
Because of the potential for presence of boulders and the volcanic tuff formation
within the anticipated depths of the steel sheet piling we also recommend predrilling to
the full depth of the sheet pile along the sheet pile wall alignment to facilitate the
installation of the sheet piling through the obstruction and rock formation zones

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
Our field exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling seven test borings
designated as Boring Nos 101 through 107 extending to depths of about 48 5 to 132 feet
below the existing pier deck pavement surface In addition two probes designated as
Probe Nos 1 and 2 were advanced by continuous probing with blunt point penetration to
depths of about 94 and 110 feet below the existing pier deck surface The approximate
boring and probe locations are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 We drilled the borings
using a truck mounted drill rig equipped with solid stem augers and coring tools
Our geologist classified the materials encountered in the borings by visual and
textural examination in the field and monitored the drilling operations on a near continuous
basis Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil Classification
System as shown on the Log Legend Plate A Graphic representations of the materials
encountered are provided on the Logs of Borings and Probes Plates A 1 through A 9
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained from the borings in general
accordance with ASTM D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 3 inch
outside diameter OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling
30 inches In addition we obtained some samples from the drilled borings in general
accordance with ASTM D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by
driving a 2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same hammer and drop The
blow counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an 18 inch drive
are shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate
sample depths
Core samples of rock and or hard materials encountered at the site were obtained
using diamond core drilling techniques in general accordance with ASTM Standard
Practice D 2113 Diamond Core Drilling for Site Investigation Core drilling is a rotary
drilling method which uses a hollow bit to cut into the rock formation The material left in
the hollow core of the bit is mechanically recovered for examination and classification
Recovery REC is used as a subjective guide to the interpretation of the relative
quality of rock masses Recovery is defined as the actual length of material recovered from
a coring attempt versus the length of the core attempt For example if 4 feet of material is
recovered from a 5 foot core run the recovery would be 80 percent and would be shown
on the Logs of Borings as REC 80
The Rock Quality Designation RQD is also a subjective guide to the relative
quality of rock masses RQD is defined as the percentage of the core run that is sound
material in excess of 4 inches in length without any discontinuities discounting any
drilling induced fractures or breaks If 2 5 feet of sound material is recovered from a
5 foot core run the RQD would be 50 percent and would be shown on the Logs of

Laboratory Testing
Moisture Content ASTM D 2216 and Unit Weight ASTM D 2937
determinations were performed on selected samples as an aid in the classification and
evaluation of soil properties The test results are presented on the Logs of Borings at
the appropriate sample depths
One Atterberg Limits test ASTM D 4318 was performed on a selected soil sample
to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits The test results are summarized on the Logs of
Borings at the appropriate sample depth Graphic presentations test results are provided
on Plate B 1
One Sieve Analysis test ASTM C 117 C 136 was performed on a selected soil
sample to evaluate the gradation characteristics of the soils and to aid in soil classification
Graphic presentation of the grain size distribution is provided on Plate B 2
To evaluate the unconfined compressive strength of the in situ clayey soils
six Unconfined Compression tests ASTM D 2166 were performed on selected in situ
samples Individual stress strain curves of the unconfined compression tests are
presented on Plates B 3 through B 8
