This report summarizes our earthwork observation and testing services performed
during the grading operations foundation excavation utility installation and pavement
construction for the Dillingham Self Storage project from through
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is at 935 Dillingham Boulevard in the Honolulu area on the Island of
Oahu Hawaii A new multi story storage building was constructed at the site The
structure is supported on precast concrete pile foundations Site grading was limited to fills
of about 1 foot or less to achieve the design finished grades Installation of infrastructure
including sewer water and electricity and asphalt concrete pavement were also included
EARTHWORK OPERATION
Jayar Construction Company performed the site grading operations utility
installation and pavement construction The site grading consisted of clearing and
grubbing subgrade preparation placement and compaction of fills and backfills pavement
construction and utility trench excavation and backfill

Fill and backfill materials required for the project were generally placed in
near level lifts brought to above the optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of
90 percent of the maximum dry density determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557
Modified Proctor
New sewer water and drain lines were installed to serve the new development
The trench backfill was placed in maximum 10 inch level loose lifts and mechanically
compacted to no less than 90 percent relative compaction
The excavated subgrades underneath some of the pavement areas were wet and
soft therefore thicker select borrow and or geotextiles were installed to strengthen the
pavement section
During the site grading operations samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our laboratory for
testing The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in
our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test
results are summarized on the attached Plate 1
Field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations on an
as needed basis to confirm our field observations as variations in construction
procedures and soil conditions were noted A total of 71 field density tests including
retests of failed areas were performed using the nuclear probe method in accordance
with ASTM D 2922 and D 3017 Compaction test results are summarized on Plates 2 1
through 2 7 These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on
constructed fills and backfills Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing
and interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed
to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was
obtained
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the earthwork operations during the site grading
foundation excavation pavement and utility construction for the above project observed
and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report were performed in general
accordance with the intent of the project plans and specifications and our geotechnical
engineering recommendations presented in the referenced report dated
and subsequent recommendations
LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork may
have been performed without our observation However the level of earthwork
