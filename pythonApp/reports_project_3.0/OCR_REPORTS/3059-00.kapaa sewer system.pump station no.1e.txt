INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our preliminary environmental site
characterization of the vicinity of the underground storage tank previously located at the
Kapaa Sewer Pump Station No 1 E facility in Kapaa on the island of Kauai Hawaii The
tank was removed as part of a pump station upgrade project The general project area
is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is an existing County of Kauai wastewater pump station facility
To provide emergency power for the pumping station there is a standby diesel generator
at the site Fuel for this generator had been stored in a 1 000 gallon capacity steel
underground tank According to Highway Construction Co Ltd this tank was removed
in November of 1991 to make way for a new emergency generator building which has
been constructed over the area where the tank was buried
The limited scope of our exploration and report is intended to determine the
potential presence of subsurface contamination at the locations drilled and sampled for
this exploration by petroleum products which may have been released from the former
underground storage tank
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions in the vicinity of the former underground storage tank location with respect to
GEOLABS HAWAII

The information obtained was intended to provide a preliminary site specific soil
data set to aid in determining the potential presence of petroleum contaminants in the
soils within the immediate vicinity of the removed underground storage tank location

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located on the southwest corner of the intersection of Haleilio
Road and Kuhio Highway near the town of Kapaa in the district of Kawaihau on the island
of Kauai Hawaii
The site is bounded by a locked chain link fence which encloses the County pump
station property Topography across the site is generally level with the exception of an
excavated trench to the southeast of the new generator building The site is vegetated
with grass except for the area immediately surrounding the new generator building and
driveway entrance No ponding of surface water or evidence of petroleum residue was
observed at the ground surface during the time of our field exploration
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3059 00 Page 5
At the time of our exploration there were two 2 emergency generator buildings
located on the property The newly constructed generator building eastern was built at
the location from which the former underground storage tank was removed Prior to its
removal in November of 1991 the underground fuel storage tank supplied the original
emergency generator building Since this time a portable above ground fuel storage tank
has been parked several feet to the north of the old generator building to supply fuel in
the absence of an underground tank
The Pump Station No 1 E is situated below ground to the northeast of the new
generator building Man hole covers and piping mark the entrance to this underground
pump station
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Boring No 1 located adjacent to the underground pump station encountered
approximately seven feet 7 of clayey silt with sand overlying very loose sand extending
to the maximum depth drilled approximately 22 5 feet Due to the close proximity of the
underground pump station this material is interpreted to be backfill of the former tank pit
Boring Nos 2 and 3 encountered approximately two 2 to five feet 5 of sandy
fill material overlying saprolitic clayey silts and fine sands which then grade to highly
weathered basalt rock at approximately twenty feet 20 below the ground surface
Groundwater Conditions
At the time of our exploration groundwater was encountered in all three 3 borings
at depths ranging from 10 5 to 11 feet below the existing ground surface Due to the
close proximity of the ocean groundwater levels may be affected by tidal changes or
storm surges

FIELD EXPLORATION
Sample Collection Activities
The subsurface conditions in the vicinity of the removed storage tank were
explored by the drilling and sampling of three 3 borings to depths ranging between 21 5
and 22 5 feet below the existing ground surface The borings were drilled using a truck
mounted rotary hydraulic drill rig advancing hollow stem augers Samples were obtained
from the borings in general accordance with ASTM Standard Method D 1586 84
California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts to drive
the sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the boring logs at the appropriate sample
depths
Samples recovered from the split barrel sampler were examined and classified in
general accordance with ASTM Standard Practice D 2488 84 Description and
Identification of Soils Visual Manual Procedure by a geologist from our firm The logs
of the borings are shown on Plates A 1 through A 3
Upon return to our laboratory headspace sampling of selected samples was
conducted by using an H Nu PI 101 photo ionization detector PID The PID was
calibrated using an isobutylene standard The readings obtained are shown on the boring
logs at the appropriate sample depth
All sampling equipment was cleaned and decontaminated prior to each sampling
event by washing with a Liquinox detergent solution and rinsing with deionized water
Drilling tools such as the hollow stem augers were decontaminated by steam cleaning
prior to the drilling of each boring and prior to leaving the site
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3059 00 Page 7
Soil samples were contained in six inch 6 long stainless steel sampler liners
Select undisturbed samples were sealed with teflon tape and plastic end caps labelled
and chilled for shipment via Federal Express to a laboratory qualified under the U S
Environmental Protection Agency Contract Laboratory Program The analytical results are
presented in Appendix C of this report
When possible water samples were collected utilizing a decontaminated PVC bailer
and immediately transferred to jars containing a hydrochloric acid preservative These
samples were sealed labelled and chilled for transport to the laboratory for analyses
FINDINGS AND DISCUSSION
General
Our field exploration indicates that the site is underlain by several feet of man made
fill Below this fill our exploration encountered stiff to very stiff medium dense in situ
weathered soils to the maximum depths explored for this site assessment The boring
locations were selected to collect soils data from the area surrounding the former
underground storage tank site and to provide background data from an area hydraulically
upgradient of the removed tank location

Hydrocarbon contamination in Boring No 1 was not detected by either the
laboratory testing or field screening Laboratory analyses of samples from Boring No 2
show some low level hydrocarbon contamination However some of the hydrocarbons
detected in the analyses tested as a hydrocarbon which is heavier than diesel and
therefore may not be quantified as diesel Samples analyzed from Boring No 3 showed
substantial levels of hydrocarbons quantified as diesel
Boring No 3 had been drilled at a location believed to be hydraulically upgradient
of the former underground tank site to obtain background data for comparison with data
retrieved from the immediate vicinity of the underground tank Interestingly this
background boring encountered hydrocarbon contamination Based on our
understanding of the hydrology of the site it is our opinion that this contamination is not
directly related to the former underground storage tank
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3059 00 Page 9
During our field investigation it was noted that a temporary fuel storage tank was
parked several feet to the south of Boring No 3 The tank appeared to be in good
condition with no overt signs of leakage
Based on our exploration and our understanding of the hydrologic conditions at
the site it appears that the contamination may be the a result of spillage from the
temporary storage tank Discussions with Highway Construction revealed that the tank
is owned by Highway Construction and was placed at the site as part of their contract for
work on the sewer pump station The tank was provided in a filled condition Following
placement at the site Highway Construction provided no further filling or servicing on the
tank
In the weeks following Hurricane Iniki and during the subsequent power outage
the tank was filled approximately twice daily by County of Kauai and other emergency
response personnel The spill or spills may have occurred during this time
It is our understanding that Highway Construction Company would be responsible
for the grouting of the three 3 borings drilled for the site characterization In addition
the cuttings stockpiled at the site should be disposed of by a licensed treatment disposal
contractor
Additional Testing
Our findings are based on the drilling and sampling of three 3 shallow borings in
the vicinity of the removed tank Because of the detection and confirmation of subsurface
contamination in Boring Nos 2 and 3 it is recommended that additional borings and
monitoring wells be installed to attempt to determine the potential source of the
contamination and to attempt to delineate the extent of contamination Although there is
no direct evidence that there was any release of diesel fuel from the underground tank
GEOLABS HAWAII

or related piping system the additional borings would aid in determining which tank may
have been the source
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from field data points such as drilled borings and grab samples
Variations of conditions may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not
become evident until further exploration is conducted If variations then appear evident
it will be necessary to re evaluate the findings presented in this report
