Existing Structure and Site History
The Arizona Memorial Shoreside Visitors Center is located within the Pearl
Harbor Naval Station in Honolulu Hawaii The site is situated on reclaimed land at the
mouth of Halawa Stream where it enters Pearl Harbor The general project site and
vicinity is shown on Plate 1 Previous reports indicate that the site was filled at various
times between about 1927 to 1945 to raise the area to about 2 to 3 feet above sea level
The existing facility was constructed in about 1979 to 1980 Additional fills
ranging from about 1 to 7 feet were placed to raise the site to near its present level
The Visitors Center consists of three buildings around a common square shaped
courtyard area The buildings are one story concrete structures with a basement crawl
space below The building foundations consist of shallow spread and wall footings that
were detailed to allow periodic re leveling to compensate for the anticipated long term
ground settlement It is our understanding that the building has been releveled four
times since its construction and settlement experienced to date has greatly exceeded
the initial projections Additionally the re leveling system may be near its capacity and
the complex can no longer be jacked to a level condition

The total settlements recorded to date are about 1 0 to 2 5 feet The re leveling

The initial concept of the visitor center expansion involved the design of a
light weight structure to reduce the additional loads and corresponding settlement from
compression of the very soft subsoils However any increase of loads will cause

Several alternatives to reduce or mitigate the settlement issues for the buildings
were evaluated
Pile Foundations for Expansion
Deep pile foundations could be used to support the expansion structure to
minimize the future settlements The piles would probably consist of 16 5 inch octagonal
pre cast pre stressed concrete and have to extend to depths of about 200 feet in order
to carry the structural loads and resist the down drag forces caused by the on going
settlement The piles would derive support from frictional resistance in the stiff alluvial
soils below the very soft estuarine deposits since suitable end bearing formations are
not present within practical depths at the site Besides the high cost the pile driving
operations are very noisy and would require closing of the visitor center during
installation Additionally the piles would have to extend through the existing structural
slab above the crawl space The pile foundations would mitigate the settlement of the
expansion structure however differential settlements between the new and existing
structures as well as connections to utilities would be a serious concern
Micro Pile Support for Expansion
Drilled micro piles with diameters of about 8 to 10 inches were evaluated The
micro piles would have lower capacity more piles and deeper depths than the pre cast
concrete piles The advantages of micro piles are the smaller installation equipment
that can fit in restricted access areas However the existing crawl space may be too
small for the deep micro piles required for this project Additionally micro piles of this
depth have not been installed in Hawaii The micro pile support system would have the
same differential settlement concerns as the pre cast piles system

Deep Foundation Support for Entire Building
Deep foundations including pre cast concrete piles and micro piles could be
used to support the existing and new structures to minimize total and differential
settlement between the buildings Due to the very limited access this alternative would
be extremely expensive and would require closing of the facility during installation of the
new foundations It should be noted that there would still be differential settlement
concerns between the buildings and utilities since the utilities would continue to settle
with the ground
Soil Improvement of the Soft Soils
There are several methods to increase the strength of poor soils below a site
including grouting jet grouting and soil mixing These methods have been used
recently in Hawaii and involve injection of materials primarily cement to increase the
strength of soft soils Due to the very limited access for equipment and deep depth of
soft soils these methods would probably be economically infeasible for this project
Compensating Foundation System
Ground settlements can be reduced by reducing the loads placed on the soft
soils One method to do this is to replace normal soil with lightweight materials such as
expanded polystyrene known as EPS or Geofoam Since normal soil weighs about
120 pounds per cubic foot and Geofoam weighs about 2 pounds per cubic foot
incorporation of the Geofoam into the fill can greatly reduce the fill loads However due
to the limited accessible fill areas and proximity to the water level buoyancy must be
accounted for this method would not be effective in mitigating the settlements
New Visitor Center
As discussed above the site and building conditions greatly limit foundation
alternatives for support of expansion structures at the visitor center The foundation
alternatives are costly and would disrupt daily operations at the center Additionally the
condition and performance of the existing structures due to the past and projected
settlements are a serious concern for the effective life of the structures Therefore
considerations for construction of a new visitor center may be appropriate Foundation
design of a new structure is not within the scope of our work however some pertinent
guidelines are presented below
A new visitor center within the current property limits would probably encounter a
similar soil profile e g soft soil deposits However the thickness of the soft soil will
vary and would have a significant effect on the cost of foundations therefore site
selection is critical A deep foundation system would probably be required for the new
building
