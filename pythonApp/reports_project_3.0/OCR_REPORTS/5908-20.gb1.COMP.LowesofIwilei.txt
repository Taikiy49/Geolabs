EARTHWORK OBSERVATION AND COMPACTION TESTING REPORT
LOWE S OF IWILEI ON SITE
GRADING PERMIT NO 0171
Dear Mr Manion
This letter report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observation and testing
services performed during the earthwork construction of the on site portion for the Lowe s
of Iwilei project in Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii
In general this letter report presents our observations and testing performed
during the earthwork construction of the site work for the project from May 3 2010 to
Robinson Construction Co is the general contractor for the project and
Bobby K Construction is the subcontractor responsible for earthwork construction
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is in the Iwilei area of Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The
on site portion 13 010 of the project is bounded by Home Depot to the north
Pacific Street to the east and Nimitz Highway to the south and west
It is planned to construct Lowe s 117k square building prototype with a garden
center The maximum dimensions of the building structure are approximately 300 feet
wide by 398 feet long The ground improvement below the shallow footings consists of
over excavating to near the groundwater level replacing with surge rock to about
12 inches above the groundwater level placing Mirafi HP270 above the surge rock and
placing compacted structural fill up to the bottom of footing elevation
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services
pertaining to the earthwork construction for the on site portion of the Lowe s of Iwilei
project only

EARTHWORK CONSTRUCTION
Geolabs Inc observed the on site earthwork construction for the Lowe s of Iwilei
project from May 3 2010 to The on site earthwork construction generally
consisted of compaction of the existing subgrade placement and compaction of fills to
achieve the finished subgrades shallow foundation subgrade preparation compaction of
the finished subgrades placement and compaction of aggregate base course Robinson
Construction Co and Bobby K Construction performed the on site earthwork operations
The footing subgrade preparation consisted of over excavating the footing
subgrades to about 6 inches below the groundwater level which correspond to an average
depth of about 6 feet below the ground surface Surge rock or No 2 Rock ASTM C 33
No 4 gradation was placed into the over excavation and worked into the bottom of the
over excavation with the excavator bucket and rolled with a wheel compactor attached to
the excavator The surge rock or No 2 Rock was placed to about 12 to 18 inches above
the groundwater level A Mirafi HP270 woven geotextile fabric was placed above the
surge rock or No 2 Rock The remaining over excavation was backfilled with structural fill
materials compacted to a minimum of 92 percent relative compaction up to the current
ground surface After completion of footing subgrade preparation the footing locations
were excavated and the footing subgrades were compacted to a minimum of 95 percent
relative compaction The building pad subgrade was compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative compaction
Samples of the on site materials utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained
and transported to our office for testing The maximum dry densities and optimum
moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general accordance with
ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test results are summarized on Plate 2
The fill and backfill materials were generally moisture conditioned to near the
optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 92 and 95 percent of the
maximum dry density as determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557
During the earthwork operations periodic field density tests were performed in
general accordance with ASTM D 6938 Nuclear Gauge Method The compaction test
results are summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 78 of this report These tests were
performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills Test
locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey
stakes These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree
implied by the methods used Areas assessed as unsatisfactory were generally reworked
until the specified minimum compaction was obtained
Our services consisted of part time field observation of the contractor s fill and
backfill placement subgrade preparation and compaction procedures and confirmation of
subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above earthwork operations
