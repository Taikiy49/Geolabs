PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our survey was to obtain an overview of the electrical resistivity of
the subsurface soils at the site Our work on this project was performed in general
conformance with ASTM Test Designation G 57 78 and with the requirements provided
in the Water System External Corrosion Control Standards Volume 3 dated 1991 and
subsequent amendments and additions The scope of our work consisted of the
following work efforts
1 Mobilization Demobilization of equipment and engineers to the site
2 Performance of in situ soil resistivity tests by Engineers from our firm at 20
selected locations across the Kuhio Homes site These locations generally
correspond to the 300 foot spacing maximum required in the Water
System External Corrosion Control Standards Volume 3 dated 1991

FIELD EXPLORATION AND LABORATORY TESTING
Geophysical survey equipment i e resistance meter was mobilized to the site on
March 16th Our field work was performed in sunny conditions with generally dry surface
conditions
A total of 60 in situ soil resistivity surveys were conducted at 20 selected locations
across the Kuhio Homes Site using the Wenner 4 Pin Method and a resistance meter
Strata Scout R 40C manufactured by SoilTest Inc with 120 volts and 24 watts output
into the ground This method utilizes four surface probes electrodes to evaluate the
average resistivity an indication of corrosivity of the underlying soil mass The average
soil resistance was evaluated at each test location with the electrodes aligned at equal
spacings of generally 2 to 4 feet based on the assumption that the inverts of the new
waterline will be approximately 3 feet below the existing ground surface

Among these spacings used one spacing may have more significance than the
others for allowing more contribution to the evaluated soil resistivity from the closest mass
of soil to the proposed pipe invert Such spacings are flagged with an asterisk on
Plates 3 1 through 3 4
The resistance meter R 40C impresses a voltage between the outer electrodes
causing an electrical current to flow between them The voltage drop between the inner
electrodes is then measured by a sensitive built in voltmeter As a result soil resistance
Ohm can be found directly on the dial The soil resistivity Ohm cm which is directly
related to both resistance and spacing can then be computed The evaluated soil
resistances the computed resistivities and the corresponding corrosion rating are
presented on Plates 3 1 through 3 4 of this report
Five Soil Box Resistivity Tests were performed in our laboratory on selected soil
samples retrieved at depths ranging from 3 to 5 feet below the ground surface Sample
testing was completed utilizing a Nilson model 400 Soil Resistivity Meter Results of the
resistivity testing as well as the corresponding corrosion rating are presented on Plate 3 5
of this report
Resistivity is generally recognized as one of the most significant soil characteristic
with regard to corrosivity of the soil In general the lower the resistivity the greater the
potential for corrosion of buried metallic structures Conversely the higher the resistivity
the less likely the soil will contribute to corrosion

LIMITATIONS
The evaluations presented in this report are based upon information obtained from
field data points by the means of geophysical surveys and laboratory testing Variations
of soil conditions between the field data points may occur and the nature and extent of
such variations may not become evident until the new waterlines are installed
Locations and elevations of the data presented in this report are approximate
having been scaled from the topographic map provided to us by your office on
March 17th All elevations were rounded to the nearest foot to emphasize that they
may not be accurate enough for other purposes Elevations and locations of the
presented data should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods
used
