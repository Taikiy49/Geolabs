SUMMARY OF RECOMMENDATIONS
The soils underlying the triangular shaped golf
course site can be summarized by two general
sets of subsurface conditions
In the northwest corner of the site the soils
may be generalized as consisting of a surface
layer approximately 5 feet thick of stiff silty
clay with gravel overlying basalt boulders with
some silty clay to a depth of 17 feet
In the northeastern and southern corners of the
site the soils may be generalized as consisting
of a surface layer approximately 6 feet thick of
firm silty sand crust overlying a 15 to 20 foot
thick layer of loose clayey sand and gravel
In general cast in place pier foundations do
not appear to be feasible due to adverse
subsurface conditions
The proposed baffles may be supported on mat
foundations designed to adequate resistance for
the design lateral loading on a 50 foot high
timber baffle pole
The text of this report should be referred to
for detailed and special design recommendations

PROJECT CONSIDERATION
The proposed baffles will consist of poles about 50 feet high
supporting screens or mesh between them to catch errant golf balls The
design lateral loads to which the poles will be subjected have been
determined by the project structural engineers to be 116 foot kips
The locations of the proposed baffles are indicated on the Site Plan
Plate 2
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to examine the subsurface
conditions at the site to provide soil data for use in the foundation
design of the proposed baffles
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests used in
the engineering analysis to formulate our conclusions and recommendations
are presented in the appendices of this report
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations

golf course is located in the Waialae district of Honolulu on the island
of Oahu Hawaii The triangular shaped golf course is bounded in the

the southwest by Kealaolu Avenue

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration disclosed that the site is underlain by two
general sets of subsurface conditions
In the northwestern corner of the site the borings encountered a
surface layer of stiff silty clay underlain by basalt boulders with some
silty clay without encountering groundwater to the maximum depths
penetrated below ground surface 17 5 feet The drilling of piers
through the basalt boulders would be very difficult Therefore it is our
opinion that mat foundations bearing on properly compacted backfill over
the boulder stratum would be a more economical solution
In the northeastern and southern corners of the site the borings
encountered an upper crust of stiff to medium dense silty clay and silty
sands and gravels These upper soils were encountered from the surface to
depths of approximately 5 to 6 feet and were generally of a stiff to
medium dense consistency Loose lagoonal deposits of clayey sand and
gravel extended below the upper crust to depths of 20 feet It is our
opinion that temporary casing and tremie concrete placement would be
required to construct drilled pier foundations in these soil and high
water level conditions
As an alternative we recommend that shallow mat foundations placed
above the groundwater level could be utilized to provide a more economical
foundation
Pier Foundations
Due to the high lateral loading requirement 116 foot kips bending
moment for the proposed baffle structures 24 inch diameter 15 to
20 foot long drilled piers could be used as foundation support Lateral
resistance of the drilled pier may be analyzed by using the appropriate
soil modulus values at various depths below the ground surface The
vertical loading capacities for the drilled pier would be derived mainly
from the frictional resistance between the shaft of the pier and the
in situ soil

Mat Foundation
A mat footing foundation with sufficient dead weight may be used to
resist the lateral loadings on each baffle and to provide vertical bearing
support to each baffle A preliminary analysis indicates that a mat
foundation of dimensions of 8 feet square by 4 feet thick appears to be
adequate in resisting the given design moment Details of the footing
design considerations are as follow
Northwestern Corner
In this area it appears that the mat footing may bear on the stiff
brown silty clay with some gravel or on the underlying boulder
layers
If boulders are encountered at the bottoms of required footing
excavation a minimum 12 to 18 inches of over excavation is
recommended and replaced with compacted choking layers The
choking layer above the boulders should consist of well graded
granular material cobbles and small size boulders well packed to
fill the voids between the exposed boulders grading to gravel and
base course size rock at the foundation bottom see Plate 3
An allowable bearing value up to 3000 p s f may be used on this
dense gravel and choking fill backfill The above value may be
increased by 1 3 for wind and seismic loading
A sliding friction coefficient of 0 45 may be used

Northeastern and Southern Corners
In the northeastern and southern corners of the site it is
anticipated that the mat footing bottom will be close to the
underlying loose sand deposit and probably near the groundwater
level To develop a firm stabilized bearing surface we recommend
a 12 inch over excavation and replacement with a 3B Fine gravel
cushion below the footing bottom if a loose sandy soil condition is
encountered at the bottom of footing excavation
An allowable bearing capacity up to 3000 p s f may be used for the
footings bearing on the 3B Fine gravel cushion and it can be
increased by 1 3 for wind and seismic loading
A sliding friction coefficient of 0 4 may be used between the
footing bottom and 3B Fine gravel cushion layer
Settlements
Settlement of the footing and baffle pole is estimated to be on the
order of one half to one inch total
Iateral Resistance
The footing should be embedded sufficiently deep below the lowest
adjacent grade to develop the necessary lateral resistance for the
design over turning moment
Properly compacted footing backfill is critical to develop the
required lateral resistance Minimm 95 relative compaction density
should be obtained per ASTM D 1557 78 test method on footing backfill
An equivalent fluid pressure of 300 p s f per foot of footing
depth may be used to determine the passive resistance We recommend the
upper 8 inches of top soil be ignored

Construction Monitoring
We recommend that the drilling of the pier holes be observed by the

recommended that all footing excavations be inspected by the soils
engineer to check that adequate bearing and compaction have been
obtained The observation by the soils engineer is also to check that

If conditions encountered are different from those presented in
this report then appropriate modifications to the design should be
made

APPENDIX Cc
Laterally Loaded Pier Analysis
Because of the anticipated large lateral force and moment acting on
the pier a laterally loaded free head pier analysis was performed The
analysis was carried out by using a finite difference program PILEDG
which was derived from the program COM624
The program adopts the P Y Curve approach and has the capability to
generate internally P Y curves given the soil data Since the lateral
soil modulus varies with the deflection experienced the computerized
analysis first assumes a modulus value then calculates the pile
deflection based on the assumed modulus The calculated deflection is
then used to determine the modulus from the P Y curves The calculated
modulus and the assumed modulus are then compared The procedure is
repeated until the new assumed and calculated moduli are essentially the
same
A summary of maximum lateral deflections of the pier under different
boundary forces and moment conditions is shown on Plate C 1
