SUMMARY OF FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
New pavements will be constructed as part of the Route Bridge
Replacement project Based on our field exploration we anticipate the majority of the
pavement subgrade soils for the new pavements will likely consist of clayey gravel and
silty clays Due to the absence of roadway drainage structures a permeable drainage
layer was not incorporated into the pavement design for the new roadway and approaches
to the replacement bridge structure
Based on the estimated truck traffic the CBR Value of the subgrade soils and the
economic analyses performed we recommend utilizing a rigid pavement structural section
for the new pavements for the roadway and approaches to the replacement bridge
structure The recommended pavement structural section consisting of 10 inches of PCC
placed over 6 inches of ASB should be used for the pavement design for the new Route
Bridge Replacement project
One of the primary distress mechanisms in pavement structures is pumping due to
saturation of the subbase and or subgrade soils Therefore special attention should be
given to the surface drainage of the pavements As a minimum the pavement surface
should be sloped adequately and drainage gradients should be maintained to carry
surface water off the pavement to appropriate drainage systems
The text of this pavement justification report should be referred to for detailed
discussion and specific recommendations for pavement design

SECTION 1 0 GENERAL
1 1 Introduction
This pavement justification report presents the results of our design analyses
performed for the new pavements for the Route Bridge Replacement
project located in the Faga alu area on the Island of Tutuila American Samoa The
project location and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings from our field exploration and presents our
geotechnical recommendations derived from our pavement design analyses for the
pavements only The findings and recommendations presented herein are subject to the
limitations noted at the end of this report
1 2 Project Considerations
The project site is located in the Village of Faga alu on the island of Tutuila
American Samoa The project involves the bridge replacement of a stream crossing and
roadway reconstruction on both sides of the stream crossing The existing bridge
structure has a span of about 15 feet measured from abutment to abutment and is
about 12 feet wide The existing road is a two lane roadway and is paved with asphaltic
concrete The roadway has undergone significant distress and deterioration of the
surface pavements is evident The roadway is used primarily by trucks traveling to and
from the nearby quarry
Based on information provided we understand that the new bridge structure will
be about 20 feet wide by 30 feet long The new bridge structure will be a reinforced
concrete box culvert structure In addition the project will involve the construction of
approximately 1 500 linear feet of roadway on both sides of the existing bridge crossing
Therefore this pavement justification report was prepared in support of the design of
the new pavements for the project
It should be noted that a separate geotechnical engineering report will be
prepared in support of the design of the bridge foundations and other structural

2 1 Regional Geology
The Island of Tutuila was built during the Pliocene and early Pleistocene Epoch
by the extrusion of basaltic lavas and pyroclastic materials from five volcanoes Stearns
1944 Following the cessation of the main shield building activity there was a long
period of erosion accompanied by the growth of fringing coral reefs During the late
Quaternary volcanism was renewed by eruptions of lava and pyroclastic materials at
several vents on the south side of the island forming the Tafuna Leone Plain
The main mountain mass of Tutuila was divided into seven formations Stearns
1944 The rocks are thought to be of Pliocene Epoch consisting of thinly bedded to
massive flows of basalt andesitic basalt and andesite interbedded with cinder cones
and tuff beds with thrachytic dikes plugs and crater fills The rock units were erupted
from as many as five eruptive centers but the principal volcano was near the middle of
the island Pago Pago Harbor is believed to occupy part of the collapsed caldera of that
principal volcano Erosion along a major fault along the caldera wall formed the
near vertical cliff on the northeast side of the harbor
Prior to the eruption of the Leone Volcanics beach and lagoonal deposits
accumulated on and behind a submerged barrier reef at the present site of the Leone
Plain These deposits were later buried under several hundred feet of volcanic
materials These older beach and lagoonal deposits partially consists of sands and
coralline gravel
Three tuff cones and a thick sequence of lava flows form the plain between
Tafuna and Leone The rock is thought to be of Holocene Epoch possibly only a few
thousand years old as evidenced by geologic relationships in the area The lava flows
are mainly thinly bedded basalt formations with interbeds of tuff and breccia

2 2 Site Description
The project site is in the Village of Faga alu located west of the Pago Pago
Harbor entrance on the south shore of the Island of Tutuila As part of the project the
approaches to the new bridge structure will be reconstructed Reconstruction of the
approaches to the new bridge structure generally will start from about 30 feet from the
east side and 70 feet on the west side of the new bridge structure as shown on the
Site Plans Plates 2 1 and 2 2 In addition partial roadway reconstruction of about
1 300 linear feet in length will be performed
It appears that the existing pavement has experienced distress throughout the
roadway alignment Numerous scattered potholes with cracked and depressed areas
were noted throughout the entire roadway alignment During our field exploration it was
observed that this portion of the roadway experiences occasional passage of heavy
trucks carrying quarry products
The existing asphaltic concrete roadway generally slopes down toward the east
at about thirty horizontal to one vertical 30H 1V inclination Based on the topographic
map provided the elevations of the existing roadway grade range from about 36 to
75 feet Mean Sea Level MSL
2 3 Subsurface Conditions
The subsurface conditions along the roadway alignment were explored by
excavating three test pits designated as Test Pit Nos 1 through 3 extending to depths

Probing Nos 1 and 2 were extended to depths of about 25 feet below the ground level
at the replacement bridge location The approximate test pit and probehole locations are
shown on the Site Plans Plates 2 1 and 2 2

3 1 Methodology of Pavement Design
Two types of pavement structural sections flexible and rigid pavements were
considered in the pavement analyses The flexible pavement sections presented herein
generally were determined based on the methodology described in Chapter 3 of the
revised Pavement Design Manual dated March 2002 The Pavement Design Manual
was prepared by the State of Hawaii Department of Transportation Highways Division
Material Testing and Research Branch The pavement design methodology is based on
the Hveem Stabilometer method developed and used by the California Department of
Transportation Caltrans
The design procedures for the rigid pavement sections generally are based on
the design procedures described in the Portland Cement Association Thickness Design
for Concrete Highway and Street Pavements

Preparation Below Pavement Section
We anticipate the majority of the new roadway pavements will be constructed at
or near the existing grades or in a slight cut condition In general the area within the
contract grading limits should be cleared and grubbed thoroughly at the on set of
earthwork Vegetation debris deleterious materials and other unsuitable materials
should be removed and disposed properly off site or disposed in a designated area to
reduce the potential for contamination of the excavated materials
Soft and yielding areas encountered during clearing and grubbing below areas
designated to receive fill or future improvements should be over excavated to expose
stiff and or dense materials The resulting excavation should be backfilled with
aggregate subbase materials The excavated soft and or organic soils should be
properly disposed off site
After clearing and grubbing the future pavement areas should be excavated
where necessary to the pavement subgrade level i e bottom of the aggregate
subbase course layer The pavement subgrade soils should be scarified to a depth of at
least 8 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to not less than 95 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers
to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry
density of the same soil determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557 Optimum
moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum
dry density
If the subgrade soils are pumping during subgrade preparation the pumping
subgrade may be stabilized by cement treatment to reduce the potential for pumping
subgrade conditions As a guide stabilization by cement treatment may consist of one

3 6 Fill Materials
In general the excavated on site materials with particles no larger than 3 inches
may be re used as a source of general fill material Imported fill materials if required
should consist of non expansive select granular material such as crushed coralline or
basaltic materials The materials should be well graded from coarse to fine with particles
no larger than 3 inches in largest dimension and should contain between 10 and
30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve The materials should have a laboratory
CBR value of 20 or more and should have a maximum swell of 1 percent or less when
tested in accordance with ASTM D 1883 Imported fill materials should be tested for
conformance with these recommendations prior to delivery to the project site for the
intended use
3 7 Compaction Requirements

703 Aggregate of the project specifications The aggregate subbase should be

3 8 Pavement Drainage
One of the primary distress mechanisms in pavement structures is pumping due
to saturation of the base subbase and or subgrade soils It should be noted that the
clayey subgrade soils are susceptible to softening and loss of strength upon saturation
Therefore special attention should be given to the surface drainage of the pavements
As a minimum the pavement surface should be sloped adequately and drainage
gradients should be maintained to carry surface water off the pavement to appropriate
drainage systems Surface water ponding should not be allowed on the site during or
after construction
