In accordance with our proposal of we have conducted
a study of the near surface soil conditions at the above referenced site
The study consisted of field reconnaissance two borings and
laboratory testing of the subsoils for foundation support This report
sumiarizes our observations and recommendations
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
A one story control building approximately 65 feet long and 40
feet wide is proposed on the level area adjacent and northerly of the
existing visitor center building The location of the proposed relocated
structure is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located in the Hawaiian Electric Company Power
Plant which is situated on the Makai side of Kamehameha Highway in Waiau
Oahu The general project location is shown on Project Location Map
Plate 1
The proposed structure is located on the Ewa side at the entrance
roadway into the plant There is a one story visitor center building
adjacent to the southerly side of the proposed structure
The site is a grassed area with scattered palm pine and lauhala
trees
There are also numerous underground utilities including electrical
water and telephone lines

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The tield exploration encountered hard residual soil layers over
moderately hard basalt rock formation Spread footing foundations may be
used to support the proposed building The existing soils are expansive
subject to heaving when wetted and therefore a minimum of 12 inches
of imported non expansive select fill should be placed below the on grade
Slab
Site Grading
Prior to grading all deleterious materials such as vegetation and
debris should be removed and disposed of properly off site
If the site is to be filled the existing ground below the proposed
structure should be scarified to a depth of 6 inches moisture
conditioned to near optimum moisture content and then compacted according
to the requirements for structural fill
Fill material to be imported to the site should be free of organic
material non expansive and generally less than 6 inches in size The
fill should be placed in horizontal lifts not exceeding 6 inches in
compacted thickness and compacted to a minimum of 95 of its maximum dry
density as established by ASTM D 1557 78
Site grading should provide drainage of surface runoff away from
all foundations
Building Foundation Design
Light structural loading is anticipated Therefore spread or
continuous footing foundations can be used to support the proposed
structure
Footings should be placed a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest
adjacent grade and should bear on the stiff to hard existing ground
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 1088 10 Page 4
An allowable bearing value of 3500 p s f may be used for
foundations bearing on the existing ground The bearing capacity value
is for total dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third for
short duration wind and or seismic loading
The bottom of all footings should be compacted to a minimum of 95
of its maximum dry density as established by ASTM D 1557 78 and
inspected by the soils engineer prior to concrete placement
If a footing is situated next to a utility trench it should be
extended to the bottom of the trench or the trench backfill must be well
compacted to prevent footing settlement due to settlement of the trench
backfill
Slab On Grade
Concrete on grade slab should be placed over 4 inches of
well graded aggregate base course A polyethylene vapor barrier over 4
inches of 3 Fine gravel may be substituted for the base course should a
capillary break be deemed necessary to protect the floor covering
Due to the expansive nature of the existing near surface soils the
on grade slab and the 4 inch base course should be placed on a minimum 12
inches of imported non expansive select fill such as crusher waste
coral or mudrock The subgrade soils below the non expansive fill should
be kept moist to minimize the swelling potential
Design Review
We recommend that plans and specifications for grading foundation
and utility construction be forwarded to the soils engineer for review
and written comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included

Construction Monitoring
We recommend that the filling footing excavation and backfilling
operations be observed by the soils engineer during construction to see
that adequate compaction has been obtained and that the subsurface
conditions are consistent with the findings of our investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
If conditions encountered during construction are different from
those presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made

engineering principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all

The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are based

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by
drilling 5 to 15 7 feet below the existing ground
surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2 Truck mounted equipment was used to drill the borings
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by
visual and textural examination in the field by our engineer who
continuously monitored the drilling operations The classifi
cations were further reviewed by visual inspection and testing in
the laboratory The soils were classified in general conformance
with the Unified Soil Classification System A graphic present
ation of the materials encountered is presented on the Log of
Borings Plates A l and A 2
Soil samples were obtained from the boring by driving a
standard penetration sampler or a 2 4 inch I D split barrel
sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow
counts to drive the sampler 12 inches are shown on the Log of
Borings at the appropriate sample depths Rock core samples were
obtained with an NX double tube core barrel
