PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface conditions at the project site to develop a generalized soils
data set for the formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations
pertinent to the site grading and foundation design and construction of
the proposed facility
The work was done generally in accordance with our revised proposal
of
The scope of our work included the drilling and sampling of ten 10
borings to depths ranging from 5 5 to 16 5 feet below the existing ground
surface Also included were laboratory testing of selected samples
obtained during the field exploration engineering evaluation of the field
and laboratory data development of design recommendations and report
preparation
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration and laboratory testing
are presented in the appendices of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to clear and grade the site for the construction of a
desalinization plant This plant if constructed will be a pilot
demonstration project to evaluate the feasibility of developing brackish
aquifers as a resource by treating the water to potable quality
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1998 00 Page 3
dk duke
The plant will be a single story metal framed structure with
slab on grade floors The plant will be served by asphaltic concrete
access roads and parking lots
It is anticipated that the site grading for the proposed plant and
access roads will involve shallow cuts of about one to two feet in depth
and fills up to about seven feet in thickness
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located on the eastern side of Kalaeloa Boulevard
between the main entrance to Campbell Industrial Park and H 1 Freeway in
The site in plan view is an irregular shaped polygon
bounded by an existing 60 foot wide drainage channel to the north and
east and by vacant land to the south and west
The existing ground surface elevations at the site range from
approximately 48 to 60 feet above Mean Sea Level In general the site
slopes gently towards the southwest However there is a low area in the
northeastern portion of the site
The site was formerly used for the cultivation of sugar cane but
cultivation has been suspended and the site and nearby fields are now
fallow Currently most of the site is a vacant field covered with tall
grasses and bushes
In the northern portion of the site an area was cleared for the
recent drilling of the brackish water supply wells on the site

Regional Geology
The island of Oahu was built by two basaltic shield volcanoes
Waianae and Koolau Waianae is the older of the two and reached the end
of its activity before Koolau

As volcanic activity on the island of Oahu drew to a close the
island began to subside slowly While the island was sinking fringing
coral algal reefs began to grow on the seaward margins of the island
During the Pleistocene or Ice Age global sea levels rose and fell
in response to the great continental glaciers As the glaciers grew the
global sea level fell below the existing level and as the glaciers melted
and retreated the sea level rose to heights above the current stand of
the sea
locally the fringing coral algal reefs continued to grow during the
high stands of the sea leaving fossil reefs which are now exposed above
sea level At the project site and in its vicinity there are exposures
of such fossil reefs which most likely grew during the Kaena 95 stand
of the sea Interbedded with these coral algal materials are alluvial
sediments which were transported from the nearby Waianae Range
During historic time the project site was used for agricultural
purposes This use most likely resulted in some minor modification of the
terrain such as tilling and grading of the fields

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on the subsoil conditions it is our opinion that the proposed
structure may be supported by shallow spread and or continuous footing
foundations However laboratory testing indicates that the near surface
alluvial soils have moderate to critical shrink swell potential
Therefore special design and construction procedures will have to be
utilized to reduce the potential of foundation and pavement distresses
resulting from shrink swell movements of the subsoils
Site Grading
It is anticipated that shallow cuts one to two feet deep and fills
up to seven feet in thickness will be required to level the site for the
development of the proposed desalinization plant
The following recommendations are specific to the anticipated site
grading
1 Prior to the commencement of any earthwork all areas within the
grading limits should be cleared and grubbed of all vegetation
debris rubbish and deleterious materials The resulting grub
and spoil material should be disposed of properly off site Any
soft weak or yielding areas disclosed during the clearing
operations should be overexcavated to firm ground and the
resulting excavations should be backfilled with well compacted
engineered fill

Subsequent to clearing and grubbing the areas to receive fill
within the grading limits should be scarified to a depth of 12
inches moisture conditioned to near optimum moisture content
proofrolled and recompacted to 90 maximum dry density as
established by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 Any rubble or
boulders encountered during the scarification which exceed six
6 inches in maximum dimension should be removed and discarded
Any soft weak or yielding areas encountered during the
proofrolling and recompaction operations should be overexcavated
and backfilled with select material placed in eight inch thick
loose lifts compacted to 90 maximum ary density per ASTM
D 1557 78
The on site expansive soils are not suitable for use as
structural fills or backfills Imported fill material should be
non expansive capable of forming a dense stable mass when
compacted and have a minimum California Bearing Ratio CBR of 15
when tested in accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1883
All structural fills and backfills should be placed in level
lifts not exceeding eight 8 to nine 9 inches in loose
thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 maximum dry density
as established by ASTM Test Method D 1557 73
The site should be graded to provide drainage of surface run off
away from all foundations and pavements
Foundations
AS previously discussed the surface alluvial soils at the site
exhibit moderate to critical shrink swell potentials To reduce the
potential of future foundation movements resulting from the
shrinking swelling of the on site soils we recommend that the on site
alluvial soils be capped by a minimum of three 3 feet of non expansive
select material compacted to a minimum of 90 maximum dry density as
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1998 00 Page 7
Dk No 52
established by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 The select material should be
less than three 3 inches in size non expansive and with less than 25
passing a 200 sieve
In order to provide uniform support for the foundations we recommend
that this three feet thick capping layer of select material be used
throughout the building area and for a distance of three 3 feet beyond
the building In areas where the site will be filled a minimum of three
3 feet the capping layer can be provided by the new fill However
overexcavation and replacement of the expansive soils will be required in
cut areas
To further reduce the swelling potential of the alluvial soils we
recommend that the foundation and slab subgrades be thoroughly saturated
prior to concrete placement
For foundation subgrades prepared in accordance with the above
recommendations an allowable bearing capacity of 2 500 p s f may be
utilized for the design of the foundations This value may be increased
by one third when temporary wind or seismic loads are considered
Foundations should be embedded a minimum of twelve 12 inches below
the lowest adjacent outside grade Interior footings should be embedded a
minimum of twelve 12 inches below the lowest adjacent finish grade
Estimated settlements for foundations bearing on the compacted select
capping layer are believed to be less than one half of an inch total and
one quarter of an inch differential
Resistance to lateral loads may be obtained by passive soil pressure
and frictional resistance A coefficient of friction of 0 4 may be used
to compute the frictional resistance between the bottoms of the footings
and the recompacted subgrade An equivalent fluid pressure of 300 p s f
per foot of depth of the buried vertical footing faces may be used to
calculate the passive soil resistance

Slabs On Grade
It is anticipated that the floor s of the proposed structure will be
concrete slabs on grade The slabs and their cushions should be supported
by a minimum 3 foot thick capping layer of select material as recommended
above
The thickened edge slab footings should bear on the non expansive
select material The thickened edges should be embedded a minimum of 12
inches below the lowest adjacent grade
A 4 inch layer of 3B Fine crushed rock should be provided below the
slabs as cushion fill If light forklifts are to be operated in the
desalinization plant six 6 inches of compacted base course should be
used in lieu of the 3B Fine rock The slab subgrade should be kept moist
prior to concrete placement
In order to prevent future moisture infiltration and subsequent
damage to floor coverings desalinization equipment or control equipment
an impervious moisture membrane is recommended below the Slabs
Pavements
Based on our field exploration and laboratory testing the following
preliminary pavement design may be used for the access roads and parking
lots
2 Inches Asphaltic Concrete
6 Inches Base Course
18 Inches Select Borrow
26 Inches Total Pavement Thickness
The select borrow can be omitted where there is a minimum of eighteen
18 inches of select fill below the road subgrade
CBR tests should be performed on the actual subgrade soils
encountered during construction and the above design section may be
revised accordingly

Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comment
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this review
is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for
misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the
earthwork and foundation phases of the work This is to observe
compliance with the design concepts specifications or recommendations and
to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ
from that anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations
given in this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made

recommendations given in this report
