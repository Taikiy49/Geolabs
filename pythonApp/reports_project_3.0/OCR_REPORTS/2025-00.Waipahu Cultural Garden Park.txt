evaluation performed for the proposed plantation village of the Waipahu
Cultural Garden Park in Waipahu on the island of Oahu Hawaii
general location of the project site and its vicinity are shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
Waipahu Cultural Garden Park is a theme park dedicated to the
cultural history of Waipahu and Hawaiian sugar plantations The park has
been developed primarily by volunteer and pro bono efforts
It is currently proposed to grade an area on the Honolulu side of the
park for the construction of an exhibit consisting of a village of
restored or reconstructed examples of plantation housing The initial
grading concept consisted of cutting the irregular slope between Waipahu
Street and the exhibit area to a slope ratio of 2H 1V and grading the
exhibit area to slope towards the taro patches at about 5 percent The
new grading concept will involve minimizing the proposed slope cut and
relocating the exhibit area upslope to accommodate the anticipated soft
subsoil conditions However new grading plans are still in the
development stage
The plantation housing will be one story wooden structures of post
and beam construction
A 10 foot wide walkway is planned to serve visitors of the plantation
village

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is in the Waipahu Cultural Garden Park located to
the south of Waipahu Street in a low area created by the drainage of
Waikele and Kapakahi Streams It is bounded to the east by Takenaka
Store to the west by an existing A c road adjacent to a one story
building and to the south by taro patches of the park
The site was formerly a plantation camp and portions are occupied by
abandoned structures or the remnants of old structures The existing
ground elevations vary from about 36 to 11 feet above Mean Sea Level
MSL sloping from Waipahu Street towards the south at about 3H 1V
gradient to almost level in the taro patches
Most of the site is moderately to densely vegetated The ground
surface at some areas in the lowlying portion was observed to be soft and
muddy probably due to the high groundwater There is a coral reef
outcrop forming a terrace at about 60 feet south of Waipahu Street The
outcrop is about 3 feet thick at the elevation of about 30 feet Mean Sea
Level along a distance of about 70 feet parallel to the street
Occasional springs from groundwater seepage probably due to artesian
pressure are indicated throughout the lowlying area and the taro patches
on the USGS topographic map of the project site and its vicinity

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The original development concept included some structures on the
lowlying soft ground area Due to the presence of soft swamp deposits and
high groundwater level we recommended that the original grading concept
be modified to relocate the structures on the higher areas to achieve a
more stable and economical site grading and foundation design
Subgrade stabilization is required for portions of the proposed
10 foot wide walkway which is maintained in the lower swampy area
Grading Concept Modification
The original grading concept included cutting the higher area along
Waipahu Street to a 2H 1V slope to provide a level area for the plantation
houses For this scheme the stability of the cut slope is questionable
due to the possible presence of the soft swamp deposits in the vicinity of
its toe Plate 3 Additionally this would increase development costs
due to the relatively large earthwork quantities involved
Some of the proposed plantation housing structures were originally
sited at the lowlying area with soft swampy subsoils Special
stabilization procedures and costly foundation support would be required
for the new structures in this area
In order to reduce costs and provide a more stable foundation
support we recommend that the original grading concept be modified
It is our opinion that the new grading concept should include minimal
site grading of the higher slope area maintain a moderate sloping
terrain and relocation of the proposed plantation structures upslope
Generally the slope area above elevation 20 feet Mean Sea Level may have
thinner swampy subsoils and lower groundwater level and therefore is
considered competent for shallow footing foundation support of the new
structures

Site Grading
The new grading plans are still in the development stage However
in accordance with the above recommended new grading concept minimal cuts
are anticipated for the higher slope area
Some small site filling may be required to raise the grade at
selected areas in the lowlying portion of the site especially where the
existing surface soil conditions are wet and soft yielding and where the
groundwater level is close to the ground surface Site filling should be
minimized to reduce ground settlements caused by compression of the soft
soils from the additional fill weight
At the onset of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed Any unsuitable
materials such as debris rubbish vegetation and other deleterious
materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site
The area to receive fill should be scarified to a minimum depth of 6
inches below the cleared grade level moisture conditioned and recompacted
to a minimum of 85 of its maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method
D 1557 78
The ground surface below footings should be scarified to a minimum
depth of 6 inches below subgrade level moisture conditioned and
recompacted to a minimum of 95 of its maximum dry density per ASTM Test
Method D 1557 78
Any loose soft or yielding areas spots encountered at the pavement
or footing subgrade level should be over excavated down to in situ firm
ground and replaced with in situ or imported backfill compacted in 8 inch
loose lifts to a minimum of 85 of its ASTM D 1557 78 maximum dry density

Foundation
Based on the anticipated soil conditions and light foundation loads
we believe that shallow spread and continuous footing foundations bearing
on the medium stiff to stiff in situ surface fills or alluvial subsoils
may be used to support the proposed wooden structures relocated upslope
An allowable bearing value of up to 1500 p s f with a minimum
footing embedment of 12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade is
recommended The bearing value is for total dead plus live load only and
may be increased by one third for short duration wind or seismic loading
Based on the available limited boring data especially in the higher
area estimated total primary footing settlement under the design loads
are anticipated to be on the order or less than one 1 inch total and
one half 1 2 of an inch differential However due to the uncertainty
in thickness and extent and the organic nature of the swampy subsoils in
the slope area it is anticipated that additional primary and secondary
settlements may occur Therefore provisions should be made to allow
relevelling of the wooden structures on posts and beams should significant
footing settlements occur in the future
Resistance to lateral loads on the proposed buildings may be provided
by passive soil pressure and frictional resistance A frictional
coefficient of 0 4 may be used to compute the frictional resistance

10 Foot Wide Walkway
It is our understanding that the proposed walkway will be subjected
to very light loading from pedestrians and occasional light service
vehicles However in light of the probable muddy subgrade and high
groundwater conditions due to artesian pressure we believe that a layer
of geofabric such as Mirafi 140 overlain by 12 inches of 3B Fine should
be used to stabilize the new walkway in the lower swampy area
Provided these subgrade stabilization measures are used the
following pavement section may be used for the new walkway
2 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 Inch Base Course

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as borings
and probes Variations of conditions between the field data points may
occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become
evident until construction If variations then appear evident it will be
necessary to re evaluate the recommendations given in this report
The location of the field data points were determined by taping from
structures and trees indicated on the topographic survey map dated
provided by Park Engineering Elevations
of the data points were determined by interpolation from elevation points
on the topographic survey map The physical location and elevation of the
field data points should be considered accurate only to the degree implied
by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross sections are based upon interpolation between field data
points and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
