This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations and testing

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Ewa by Gentry Area 40 site is on the western side of Fort Weaver Road
south of Geiger Road in the Ewa District on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project site
is bounded by Area 12 to the north Fort Weaver Road to the east future Keaunui Drive
to the south and Kapolei Parkway to the west The Area 40 site is divided into three
increments with Phases 1D and 1E at the southern portion of the site adjacent to
Keaunui Drive A Project Location Map and Site Plans are shown on Plates 1 and 2 1
and 2 2 respectively
The project involves mass and site grading 43 house pads for a residential
development consisting of house lots and related access roads The site grading work
involved fills up to about 10 feet thick In addition the project includes utility and roadway
construction
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services for
the mass and pad grading and utility and roadway construction at the project site

MASS AND PAD GRADING AND UTILITY ROADWAY CONSTRUCTION
We observed the mass and pad grading and utility and roadway construction work
for the Area 40 Phases 1D and 1E project located adjacent to Keaunui Drive from
January 7 to The mass and site grading work consisted of clearing and
grubbing and placement and compaction of fills on the order of up to about 10 feet thick to
achieve the finished grades for the project In addition soft subgrade soils were removed
and backfilled with compacted fill materials Royal Contracting Co Ltd performed the
mass and site grading and utility and roadway construction work During the mass and site
grading operations samples of the on site and imported materials utilized as fill and
backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing The maximum dry
densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general
accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test results are summarized on
Plates 3 1 and 3 2
The fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to above the optimum
moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density
as determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557 In the roadway areas the fill and backfill
materials within 2 feet of the finished subgrade were compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative density
During the mass and site grading and utility and roadway construction operations
periodic field density tests were performed in general accordance with ASTM D 6938
Nuclear Gauge Method Results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on
Plates 4 1 through 4 28 herein These tests were performed on the prepared ground
surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills at the site Test locations and elevations
were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations
and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods
used Areas assessed as unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum
compaction was obtained and or approved by our field representative based on visual
observation and hand probing
Soft clayey subgrade soils were encountered in the northern portion of the project
site The soft subgrade soils were removed and backfilled with compacted fill materials
Our services consisted of part time field observation of the contractor s fill
placement subgrade preparation and compaction procedures and confirmation of
subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above grading operations
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the mass and site grading and utility and roadway
construction work for the above project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period
covered by this report were performed in conformance with the general intent of the
recommendations in the following references
