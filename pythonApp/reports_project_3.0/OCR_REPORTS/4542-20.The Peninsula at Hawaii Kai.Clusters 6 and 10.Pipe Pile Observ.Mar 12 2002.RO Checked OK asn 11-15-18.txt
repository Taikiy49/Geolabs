PIPE PILE OBSERVATION REPORT
CLUSTERS 6 AND 10
THE PENINSULA AT HAWAII KAI
Dear Mr Reigels
In accordance with your request Geolabs has provided geotechnical engineering
observation and consultation services during the pipe pile foundation installation for the
Peninsula at Hawaii Kai project located in the Hawaii Kai area on the Island of Oahu
Hawaii This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations performed
during the pipe pile installation for the homes at Clusters 6 and 10 of the above project
A geotechnical engineering exploration was performed by Geolabs Inc and our
recommendations were presented in the report entitled Geotechnical Engineering
Exploration Mass Grading and Subdivision Development The Peninsula at Hawaii Kai
Honolulu Oahu Hawaii dated March 5 2001 The purpose of our geotechnical
engineering services was to observe the pipe pile foundation installation for general
conformance with the project plans and specifications
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located along both sides of Wailua Street west of the intersection
with Lunalilo Home Road in the Hawaii Kai area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii as shown
on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The majority of the site is a peninsula constructed of
man made materials extending out into Kuapa Pond The total project area is about
42 65 acres
The proposed development consists of a mix of single family townhouse and
condominium units The single family units are anticipated to be one and two story
structures while the multi family units may be housed in two to four story structures In
addition roadways and utility lines will also be constructed for this project

Based on our borings the project site is generally underlain by surface fills over
loose lagoonal deposits cinder sands and volcanic tuff Due to the presence of loose
lagoonal deposits which may be susceptible to liquefaction under seismic loading a
30 foot building set back distance from the seawall was established for the project site
Because Cluster 6 and 10 houses along the eastern seawall in Parcel A encroach the
30 foot set back distance a pipe pile foundation system was recommended in order to
increase the stability of the houses in the event that lateral ground movements occur
during a seismic event
Based on the relatively light loads provided by the structural engineer we
recommended that the pipe pile foundations consist of grouted 6 inch diameter pipe piles
extending to the volcanic tuff formation In order to provide adequate cover for corrosion
protection the 6 inch diameter pipe piles would be inserted in a drilled column of at least
12 inches in diameter
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Based on our field exploration the project site is generally underlain by surface fills
over lagoonal deposits cinder sands and volcanic tuff Our borings generally
encountered surface fills extending to about 5 5 to 11 feet below the ground surface In
general the surface fills consist of medium dense to very dense silty sands with gravel
The surface fills were generally underlain by lagoonal deposits extending to about 13 to
22 feet below the ground surface The lagoonal deposits generally consisted of very loose
to loose silty sands Our borings encountered dense to very dense cinder sands
underlying the lagoonal deposits extending to about 20 to 35 feet below the ground
surface Relatively competent volcanic tuff was generally encountered below the cinder
sands to about 50 8 feet below the ground surface
Groundwater levels were observed at elevations between 3 3 feet and 0 7 feet
Mean Sea Level during our field exploration Due to the proximity of the project site to the
Kuapa Pond it is anticipated that the groundwater level will fluctuate with tidal changes
rainfall and other factors
PIPE PILE FOUNDATION INSTALLATION
The installation of the production shafts was performed by Foundation International
Inc A total of 13 pipe piles designated as DS 1 through DS 13 were installed between
February 19 and 20 2002 The approximate locations of the 13 pipe piles are shown on
the Site Plan Plate 2 A representative from Geolabs observed the drilling pipe pile
installation and concreting process during the construction of these pipe piles Due to the
limited number of pipe piles and relatively low structural loads provided for this project a
test pile and or a load test was not specified in the contract documents

The pipe piles were drilled using a track mounted drill rig equipped with a 12 inch
diameter auger The pipe piles generally extended at least 2 feet below volcanic tuff
formation or dense materials encountered during the drilling operation The pipe piles
were drilled to depths ranging between about 20 and 21 feet below the ground surface
Due to the soft subsurface condition and relatively shallow groundwater table the drilled
holes were cased with a 14 inch outside diameter temporary casing The soil in the cased
holes was generally cleaned using a clean out bucket The 6 inch diameter steel pipes
were installed after the holes have been cleaned out Due to the shallow groundwater
table the concreting of the pipe piles were conducted using tremmie methods The Pipe
Pile Summary is presented on Plate 3 Individual pipe pile records are presented on
Based on our field observations we believe that the 13 pipe piles installed for the
Cluster 6 and 10 houses have been installed in general conformance with the project
plans and specifications and within the limits of generally accepted construction practices
Based on our observations of the pipe pile installation it is our opinion that the pipe piles
have been installed to the required diameter and depth to provide the design load
capacity
