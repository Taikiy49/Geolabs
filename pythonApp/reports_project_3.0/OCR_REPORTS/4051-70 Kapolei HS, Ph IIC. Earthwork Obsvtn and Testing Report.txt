PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Kapolei High School project site is located within the existing Villages of
Kapolei development in the District of Ewa on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The high school
site is situated approximately at the southwest corner of the Makai Villages 7 8
residential development site The high school site encompasses an area of approximately
40 acres and is bounded to the north by Kapolei Parkway to the east by the future
Makai Village 8 to the south by the Kapolei Lower Channel and to the west by
Fort Barrette Road
As part of the high school master development plan a new athletic field bleachers
and Building M locker shower are planned at the southwestern corner of the premises
under the Phase IIC increment of the project The layout of the new construction is shown
on the Site Plan Plate 1
Based on the construction plans provided for the project site grading work will
generally consist of minimal cuts and fills of up to about 3 feet thick The scope of work
discussed in this letter report includes observation and compaction testing during the

EARTHWORK OPERATIONS
The site grading operations foundation excavations utility installation and
pavement construction for the Kapolei High School Phase IIC project were performed by
Koga Engineering Construction Inc The site grading work consisted of subgrade
preparation placement and compaction of fills and backfills footing excavation and
preparation pavement construction and utility trench excavation and backfill
Following the demolition and clearing operations the excavated subgrades
underneath the future football field bleachers building slabs and pavement areas were
scarified to a depth of about 8 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the
optimum moisture content and proof rolled with a minimum 10 ton vibratory drum roller to
a minimum of 90 or 95 percent relative compaction of the maximum dry density as
determined in accordance with American Society of Testing and Material ASTM D 1557
Modified Proctor
The existing ground at Building M was generally over excavated where necessary
to approximately 12 inches below the finished subgrade in the building area to provide the
recommended 12 inches thick non expansive select granular fill layer During placement
of the non expansive select granular fill layer the fill materials were generally placed in
near level lifts brought to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a
minimum of 95 percent of the maximum dry density determined in accordance with
ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor During the foundation excavation and preparation the
footing subgrades were compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction prior
to the placement of reinforcing steel and concrete
Fill and backfill materials required for the project were generally placed in near level
lifts brought to above the optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of 90 or
95 percent of the maximum dry density determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557
Modified Proctor
During the site grading operations samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test
results are summarized on Plates 2 1 and 2 2 of this report
Field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations on an
as need basis to confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures
and soil conditions were noted A total of 578 field density tests including retests of failed
areas were performed using the nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM D 2922
and D 3017 Results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 3 1

LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork may
have been performed without our observation However the level of earthwork
observation and testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some
of the geotechnical risks to the project It should be recognized that there are variations
in the accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the
earthwork construction and materials
