PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed project site is located along Farrington Highway in the Makaha area on
the Island of Oahu Hawaii as shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The project
generally consists of installing 8 12 and 24 inch diameter water mains from about Lawaia
Street to Jade Street We understand that the pipe materials may consist of Ductile Iron Dl
Concrete Cylinder CC Class 250 PVC or other pipe materials Based on the information
provided the project will consist of approximately 11 700 lineal feet of water mains and
appurtenances The 24 inch water main will have inverts ranging from about 5 to 9 feet below
the existing ground surface and the 8 inch and 12 inch water mains will have inverts ranging
from about 4 to 8 feet below the existing ground surface Deeper inverts are anticipated where
the water mains traverse existing utility lines and box culverts
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our study was to obtain an overview of the corrosion characteristics of
the subsurface soils along the proposed 8 12 and 24 inch water mains planned at the project
site Our work was performed in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation G 57 95
and the requirements provided in the Water System External Corrosion Control Standards
Volume 3 1991 The scope of our work consisted of the following tasks and work efforts

FIELD SURVEY
Geophysical survey equipment i e resistance meter was mobilized to the site on
for the field survey The geophysical survey was completed on
In general our field work was performed in sunny conditions with generally
dry surface conditions
Ninety three soil resistivity surveys were conducted at a maximum of 300 foot intervals
at thirty one locations designated as R 1 through R 31 along the shoulder areas of Farrington
Highway The soil resistivity surveys were performed by using the in Pin
Method and a resistance meter Nilsson Model 400 manufactured by Nilsson Electrical
Laboratory Inc The method utilizes four surface probes or pins electrodes to evaluate the
average resistivity an indication of corrosivity of the underlying soil mass The average
resistivity values of the soil mass extending from the existing ground surface to the test depth
were assessed by using this method at the approximate survey locations shown on the
General Site Plan Plate 2 Enlarged site plans of the project site are shown on Plates 3 1
through 3 8 The results of the resistivity survey are presented on Plates 4 1 through 4 8

The soil resistivity surveys were performed at the existing ground surface along the
proposed alignment of the 8 12 and 24 inch water mains The average soil resistance along
the proposed alignment was evaluated at each survey location with the electrodes aligned at
equal spacing of generally 3 to 7 feet at the existing ground surface The depth of penetration
of a resistivity sounding is a function of the electrode spacing The electrode spacing was
spaced at about 3 to 7 feet for each soil resistivity survey location It should be noted that
some underground utility lines were observed within the shoulder areas near some survey
locations
In general the resistance meter Nilsson Model 400 impresses a voltage between the
outer electrodes causing an electrical current to flow between them The voltage drop
between the inner electrodes is then measured by a sensitive built in voltmeter As a result
the soil resistance in ohms can be found directly on the dial The soil resistivity in ohm cm
which is directly related to both resistance and spacing can then be computed
The evaluated soil resistances the computed resistivity values and the corresponding
corrosion ratings are presented on Plates 4 1 through 4 8 of this report Graphic presentation
of the soil resistivity values and the corrosion ratings along the proposed water main alignment
are provided on Plates 5 1 through 5 3 It should be noted that the soil resistivity at the site
might be influenced by existing underground structures temperature weather conditions and
other factors
LABORATORY TESTING
A laboratory corrosion test program was also conducted for the subsurface materials
along the new water mains planned along this portion of Farrington Highway Selected soil
samples retrieved from our field exploration program were tested to determine the minimum
resistivity values using the Soil Box Method in general accordance with ASTM Test
Designation G 57 95
In addition laboratory tests were performed on the subsurface materials along the
proposed water main alignments to evaluate the corrosion characteristics of the soils to
concrete Selected soil samples retrieved from our field exploration program were tested by
Environmental Laboratory of the Pacific to determine the pH value and the chloride and sulfate
contents The pH value was determined in general accordance with EPA SW9045B test

FINDINGS AND DISCUSSIONS
Resistivity is generally recognized as one of the most significant soil characteristics with
regard to the corrosivity of the soil to buried metallic objects In general the lower the
resistivity the greater the potential for corrosion of the buried metallic structure Conversely
the higher the resistivity the less likely the soil will contribute to corrosion of metallic objects
In general our test results indicate that the subsurface materials at the 8 12 and
24 inch water mains generally have corrosion ratings of 4 or 5 Corrosion ratings of 2 or 3
were noted in localized areas along the water main alignments In addition we also noted that
a corrosion rating of 1 was observed between about Station 40 24 and Station 46 24 for the
12 inch water main
The method used to control the corrosion of underground concrete pipelines and
structures is dependent on the pH and the chloride and sulfate contents of the soil For
underground concrete pipelines or structures the laboratory test results indicate that the
subsurface materials do not have a Corrosion Rating of 1 Therefore concrete cylinder pipes
may be considered as an option for this portion of the project
LIMITATIONS
The evaluations presented in this report are based upon information obtained from field
borings and field data points by means of geophysical surveys and laboratory testing
Variation of soil conditions between and beyond the field borings and data points may occur
and the nature and extent of such variations may not become evident until a later point in time
If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the ratings provided in this
report
Locations of the field borings presented in this report are approximate having been
estimated by taping from features shown on the general site plan transmitted by
R M Towill Corporation on Elevations of the field borings are based on
interpolating between the elevation points shown on the same plan Field resistivity survey
locations are approximate having been estimated by taping from features shown on the Tax

between the elevation points shown on the preliminary site plan Therefore the locations and
elevations of the data presented should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by
the methods used
