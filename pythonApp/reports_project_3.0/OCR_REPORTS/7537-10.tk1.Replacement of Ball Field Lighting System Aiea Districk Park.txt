system at Aiea District Park in the Aiea area of Honolulu on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii.

observation services for the Replacement of Ball Field Lighting System project, and the 
findings, recommendations and observations are presented in the above-referenced 
reports. The purpose of our non-destructive integrity testing services was to evaluate the 
concrete integrity of the six drilled shafts installed to support the light pole structures for the 
above project.

PROJECT DESCRIPTION 
The project site is within the existing Aiea District Park in the Aiea area on the 
Island of Oahu, Hawaii.  The park is bordered by Aiea Heights Drive to the east, Ulune 
Street to the west, and Aiea High School to the north.  In general, the project involves the 
reconstruction of the existing ball field lighting system. We understand that a total of six 
new light poles are planned for the project. The new light poles are supported on deep 
foundations consisting of cast-in-place concrete drilled shafts. Drilled shafts with 
diameters of 60 inches and lengths ranging from about 13.1 to 21 feet were used to 
support the new light poles. 
The scope of our work discussed in this letter report includes the performance of 
CSL testing on the six drilled shafts located at various locations along the perimeter of the 
ball field, designated as Shaft Nos. P-1 through P-6, installed for the above project. Paul’s 
Electrical Contracting, LLC was the general contractor for the project and also installed 
the drilled shaft foundation support system. 
In accordance with the project specification Section 02200 – Site Work, Paragraph 
3.05 – Crosshole Sonic Logging (CSL) Testing, we performed the CSL testing from 
June 26 through July 8, 2019 for the designated drilled shafts installed for the above 
project. The drilling operation and concrete placement for the six drilled shafts are 
summarized in the following table.

CROSSHOLE SONIC LOGGING TESTING 
In general, the CSL testing was conducted in accordance with ASTM D6760 using 
the CHUM CSL Testing equipment manufactured by Piletest. Crosshole Sonic Logging 
(CSL) tests were performed between the possible access tube combinations in the drilled 
shaft (four tubes resulting in six profiles).  The northern most access tube was designated 
as Tube No. 1, and subsequent tubes were numbered in a clockwise direction.  
The steel access tubes were extended approximately  5.6, 4.5, 5.8, 4.9, 3.1 and 
4.5 feet for Shaft Nos. P-1, P-2, P-3, P-4, P-5, and P-6, respectively, above the designed 
top of the drilled shaft concrete for access considerations. We understand that potable 
water was used to fill the access tubes after concrete placement. Based on our 
observation in the field, the water levels within the access tubes were generally above the 
top of the drilled shaft concrete at the time of testing. 
The CSL test results are based on the percentage decrease in velocity as 
correlated to the following Concrete Condition Rating Criteria (CCRC).  Deviations from 
the following values are generally used for determining the Concrete Condition Rating.  
The CCRC is summarized in the table below.

As listed in the table above, increases in First Arrival Times (FATs) of 10% or less 
may be considered insignificant.  Increases in FATs greater than 10% are considered 
failure to meet the specified “Good” Concrete Condition Rating, and coring may be 
required to intercept the suspected or defective zones. 
The average apparent sonic wave velocity can be determined from the CSL test 
results by dividing the average tube distances by the average first arrival times for each 
tube combination in a drilled shaft. It should be noted that the average apparent sonic 
wave velocity computed in this manner is not an absolute number and should be used with 
caution because the actual tube distances below the top of the drilled shaft are not known 
accurately. 
FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS 
A summary of the CSL test results, including the tested concrete length, computed 
apparent sonic wave velocity, and comments on the concrete condition are presented on 
Plates 3.1 through 3.6.  The CSL test results for the drilled shafts tested are summarized 
on Plates 4.1 through 4.6.  The individual CSL test plots for each tested tube combination 
(profile) and a plan showing the tube locations are presented on Plates 5.1 through 5.12. 
Based on the CSL test results, the increased first arrival times (FATs) were 
generally less than 10%, corresponding calculated wave velocity reduction was less than 
10%, and the wave energy was relatively uniform in all the profiles for Shaft Nos. P-1 
through P-6 with the following exception in Shaft No. P-2. It should be noted that changes 
in the relative energy can sometimes indicate defects depending on the degree of the 
reduction and the associated increase in FAT.

The average apparent sonic wave velocities for Drilled Shaft No. P-1 ranged from 
approximately 13,300 to 15,900 feet per second. Based on the results of the CSL test and 
FAT increases of less than 10%, we believe that concrete integrity of Shaft No. P-1 within 
the testing depth meets a Concrete Condition Rating of “Good” in accordance with the 
above CCRC. Because no significant defect/anomaly is indicated by the CSL test, we 
recommend acceptance of Shaft No. P-1 within the testing depth. 
The average apparent sonic wave velocities for Drilled Shaft No. P-2 ranged from 
approximately 13,000 to 14,000 feet per second. Based on the results of the CSL test and 
FAT increases of 18% in Profile 3-4, we believe that concrete integrity of Shaft No. P-2 
within the testing depth meets a Concrete Condition Rating of “Questionable” in 
accordance with the above CCRC. Because the minor contamination was observed in 
only one profile and less than 4 inches in length, we recommend acceptance of Shaft 
No. P-2 within the testing depth. 
The average apparent sonic wave velocities for Drilled Shaft No. P-3 ranged from 
approximately 13,200 to 14,600 feet per second. Based on the results of the CSL test and 
FAT increases of less than 10%, we believe that concrete integrity of Shaft No. P-3 within 
the testing depth meets a Concrete Condition Rating of “Good” in accordance with the 
above CCRC. Because no significant defect/anomaly is indicated by the CSL test, we 
recommend acceptance of Shaft No. P-3 within the testing depth. 
The average apparent sonic wave velocities for Drilled Shaft No. P-4 ranged from 
approximately 13,400 to 14,800 feet per second. Based on the results of the CSL test and 
FAT increases of less than 10%, we believe that concrete integrity of Shaft No. P-4 within 
the testing depth meets a Concrete Condition Rating of “Good” in accordance with the 
above CCRC. Because no significant defect/anomaly is indicated by the CSL test, we 
recommend acceptance of Shaft No. P-4 within the testing depth. 
The average apparent sonic wave velocities for Drilled Shaft No. P-5 ranged from 
approximately 11,100 to 13,500 feet per second. Based on the results of the CSL test and 
FAT increases of less than 10%, we believe that concrete integrity of Shaft No. P-5 within 
the testing depth meets a Concrete Condition Rating of “Good” in accordance with the 
above CCRC. Because no significant defect/anomaly is indicated by the CSL test, we 
recommend acceptance of Shaft No. P-5 within the testing depth. 
The average apparent sonic wave velocities for Drilled Shaft No. P-6 ranged from 
approximately 12,600 to 14,500 feet per second. Based on the results of the CSL test and
