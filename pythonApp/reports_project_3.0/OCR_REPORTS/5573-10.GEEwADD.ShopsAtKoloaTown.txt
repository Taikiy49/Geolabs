In accordance with our fee proposal dated we have conducted a
geotechnical engineering exploration at the proposed alternate borrow site for The Shops
at Koloa Town project in the Koloa area on the Island of Kauai Hawaii The project
location and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings and geotechnical engineering
recommendations resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering
analyses These recommendations are intended to assist in evaluating the suitability of the
material within the borrow site for fill materials The findings and recommendations
presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Shops at Koloa Town project is in the vicinity of the northeast corner of the
intersection of Maluhia and Koloa Roads on the Island of Kauai Hawaii It is our
understanding that imported fill material will be required to raise the site above the design
flood level Therefore an alternate nearby borrow site has been designated for this fill
The borrow site is south of Koloa Bypass Road at the intersection with Maluhia
Road

geotechnical engineering recommendations for the suitability of the soil materials at the
borrow site Our work was performed in general accordance with our fee proposal dated
The scope of our work included the following tasks and work efforts

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The Island of Kauai is composed of a single basalt shield volcano built by the
extrusion of lava of the Waimea Canyon Volcanic Series during the late Pliocene Epoch
more than 2 million years before present Following the cessation of this main shield
building phase renewed volcanic activity occurred with the extrusion of basaltic lava of the
post erosional Koloa Volcanic Series and the concurrent deposition of the alluvial
sediments of the Palikea Formation The project site is on the southern flank of the shield
volcano
Rocks of the Koloa Volcanic Series are generally characterized as thick flows of
dense basalt extruded from groups of vents aligned in north south trends in various
locales Associated with the vents are pyroclastic materials which usually form low cinder
cones at the vent Rocks of the Koloa Volcanic Series cover most of the eastern half of
the Island of Kauai
During the Pleistocene Epoch many sea level changes occurred as a result of
widespread glaciation in the continental areas of the world As the great continental
glaciers advanced and accumulated the level of the ocean fell since there was less water

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
We explored the subsurface conditions at the project site by excavating five test
pits designated as Test Pit Nos 1 through 5 to depths of about 5 to 7 feet below the
existing ground surface The approximate test pit locations are shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2 The test pits were excavated using a Komatsu WB140 backhoe provided by the
client
In general our test pits excavated at the site generally encountered very stiff clayey
silt that extended to depths of about 5 to 7 feet below the existing ground surface The
clayey silt was generally in a moist condition It should be noted that roots were
encountered within the top 1 foot of the test pit excavations
We did not encounter groundwater in the test pits at the time of our field
exploration It should be noted that groundwater levels at the project site may fluctuate
depending on seasonal precipitation and other factors Detailed descriptions of the
materials encountered in the test pits are presented on the Logs of Test Pits Plates A 1
and A 2 in Appendix A Results of the laboratory tests performed on selected soil samples
are presented in Appendix B
DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The proposed borrow site is underlain alluvial deposits We did not encounter
groundwater in the test pits at the time of our field exploration Based on the subsurface
conditions encountered and the laboratory tests performed on the soil samples obtained
during our field exploration we believe that the clayey silt soils encountered between Test
Pit Nos 1 and 3 may be suitable for fill material at The Shops at Koloa Town project
Based on our laboratory tests the soils encountered between Test Pit Nos 1 and 3
have California Bearing Ratio CBR values between 16 and 23 and CBR swell values of
about 0 1 percent The ring swell test values range between 2 7 and 3 9 percent The
laboratory test results indicate that the clayey silt soils have a low potential for expansion
provided that the soils are kept moist prior to placement of concrete for the slabs on grade
The fill materials should be free of deleterious materials vegetation and screened of
particles greater than 3 inches in largest dimension It should be noted that roots were

The fill materials should be moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Five one inch Ring Swell tests were performed on remolded soil samples to
evaluate the swelling potential of the near surface soils The test results are presented on
Plate B 1
Three Atterberg Limits tests ASTM D 4318 were performed on selected soil
samples to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits The test results are presented on
Plate B 2
Five laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR tests ASTM D 1883 were
performed on bulk soil samples to evaluate the engineering characteristics of the soils
The samples were remolded to near the optimum moisture content of the soils
Compaction of the samples was performed by dropping a 10 pound hammer from a height
of 18 inches The samples were compacted in five equal layers with each layer receiving
56 blows of energy The samples were soaked in water prior to the penetration test The
test results are presented on Plates B 3 through B 7
