SUMMARY
The predominant soils are alluvial and colluvial
deposits which will provide adequate support for
the proposed structures Caisson foundations will
be required in the low lying areas where softer
recent alluvium was encountered
The existing fill at the south end of the proposed
high rise structure contains organic and other
deleterious materials and must be removed from the
proposed construction area
INTRODUCTION
This report summarizes the findings of the subsurface investigations
conducted at the subject site to evaluate subsurface conditions
and to establish recommendations for the design and construction of
the foundations to support the proposed structures and for the an
cillary earthwork
W O 564 00 Page 1974
PROJECT CONSIDERATION
The proposed development will consist of three structures a park
apartment The high rise apartment and swimming pool will be lo
cated along the eastern third of the site The northern portion
of this building will be constructed at elevation 154 feet
essentially the existing grade The southern section which in
cludes the swimming and recreational areas has proposed finish
elevations varying from elevations 140 to 151 and will be placed
upon sloping terrain The maximum anticipated column loads are
approximately 1300 kips Shear wall loadings of up to 200 kips
per lineal foot are anticipated
A separate 5 story low rise apartment with a finish floor level
of approximately elevation 132 is planned along the southern pro
perty boundary The northwestern third of the site will contain
a two level parking structure with the lower level at elevation
147 Excavations of up to eleven feet will be required for the
portion adjacent to Haiku Road
SITE CONDITIONS
Surface The proposed development is adjacent to the Kulana Nani
Phase II Development presently under construction It is immedi
ately south of Haiku Road approximately 1 4 mile east of its inter
section with Kahekili Road The area near Haiku Road is essentially
level with scattered fruit and ornamental trees Several dwellings
W O 564 00 Page 1974
have been razed leaving isolated concrete floor slabs The rear
third of the site dropes at approximately a 15 per cent slope
to Keaahala Tributary Stream which traverses the site on the south
border
The ground surface varies from elevation 122 at the southeastern
corner to elevation 162 at the extreme northwestern corner The
Slope areas are heavily vegetated with vines and trees The flat
areas are generally clear
An area of dumped fill exists at the southern end of the proposed
high rise structure This fill includes concrete asphalt and
Other building rubble along with abandoned automobiles and mis
celleaneous junk The extent of this area is outlined by the steep
slope and contour elevation 150 shown on the enclosed Site Plan
Plate l
Subsurface Exploration
The site is underlain by deeply weathered basalt flows of the Koolau
Volcanic Range which were subsequently dissected by stream erosion
Recent alluvium was then deposited along the existing stream beds
Seven borings were drilled at the locations shown on the enclosed
Plate 1 to delineate subsurface conditions A geologist or engineer
from our office supervised the field investigations and drilling
operations obtained samples of the subsoils and observed the general
site conditions Soil samples were obtained by driving a standard
penetration or ring sampler with a 140 pound hammer free falling a
W O 564 00 Page 1974
distance of 30 inches The blows required to drive the sampler
the last 12 inches were recorded and are shown on the enclosed
boring logs Plates A 1 thru A 7
Laboratory tests were performed on selected samples to assist in
the classification and to measure the physical parameters These
testing included moisture contents Atterburg limits consolidation
expansiveness and direct shear tests The moisture content and
Atterburg limits test results are shown on the boring logs at the
appropriate sample locations The results of the consolidation
and direct shear tests are shown on Plates A 8 thru A 15
Subsurface Conditions
The subsurface explorations disclosed three general soil conditions
beneath the proposed site The most prevalent soil is a thick layer
of alluvium and colluvium which generally blankets the site The
soils are brown and reddish brown clayey silt varying from stiff to
extremely stiff in consistency These soils were encountered in the
upper level area adjacent to Haiku Road at Borings 6 and 7 on the
slope area in Borings 1 and 2 and beneath the fill in Borings 4 and
5 The soil has a relatively high water content but has sufficient
strength to provide moderate support without excessive settlements
Slopes within this material are stable with no indications of creep
movements
The soils have a low expansive potential at their present high
moisture content They will dry and crack if exposed during con
struction and should be protected from loss of moisture
W O 564 00 Page 1974
The alluvium and colluvium are blanketed in the vicinity of Borings
4 and 5 by a thick layer of fill varying from 7 feet at Borings 5
to 19 feet at Boring 4 The fill is predominately a silty clay or
clayey silt varying from stiff to extremely stiff in consistency
It contained areas of boulders concrete asphalt wood fragments
and organic materials The surface materials consist of large ba
i
salt boulders abandoned vehicles and miscellaneous junk with some
Organic materials and limited soil
The apparent extent of the fill is shown on the Site Plan by the
steeply sloped area between elevation 128 and elevation 150 and
by the mound portion above elevation 152 The fill underlies the
southern third of the proposed high rise structure including the
swimming pool and deck areas It also extends to the northeastern
corner of the low rise structure
Apparently the fill was end dumped with little or no site pre
paration or compaction Test borings encountered organic materials
which will not provide adequate slope stability or foundation sup
port This material should be removed from the proposed construc
tion and slope areas
Boring 3 encountered what is apparently recent alluvium in the
low lying area adjacent to the stream This soil is a clayey silt
with pebbles gravel and traces of sand It varies from medium to
stiff in consistency and extended to a depth of approximately 23
feet It cannot provide adequate support for even lightly loaded
structures

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS _
General
The predominant soil at the site the alluvium and colluvium de
posits will provide adequate support for the proposed structures
Special foundations will be required in low lying areas where the
softer recent alluvium was encountered The existing dumped fill
must be removed prior to construction and the foundations placed
on the underlying competent soils
A summary of the subsurface explorations is given on page 7
Detailed classification logs of the soils and rock encountered
are appended
Foundations
Foundations bearing on the older alluvium and colluvium can be
designed as spread foundations for maximum contact bearing pres
sures of The soils are not sensitive to impact load
ings The bearing value can therefore be increased to 6 000
psf when considering seismic and wind loadings The
allowable bearing pressure applies to the parking garage the por
tion of the high rise structure above elevation 152 and the western
five bays of the low rise structure All foundations should bear a
minimum of three feet below the adjacent final grade
The two easterly bearing walls of the low rise structure tra
verse the portion of the site underlain by the recent alluvium

Floor Slabs
Expansive soils were not encountered during the subsurface explora
tions An expansion test on a sample of the near surface soil from Boring
2 resulted in a 2 6 per cent expansion with a surcharge of 60 psf
This is generally considered to be a low expansion potential
Slab on grade construction can therefore be utilized without special
provisions for expansive soils It is recommended that the subgrade
be protected from drying during the construction operations The
soils within this areas are susceptible to drying and cracking if
exposed for extended periods Drying of the soils during construc
tion would increase the expansion potential
Due to the high moisture content slabs on grade should be provided
with protective plastic vapor barriers Due to the potential for
high groundwater levels it is recommeded that the floor slabs with
in the low rise area be provided with a four inch granular base to
prevent the accumulation of capillary moisture
Retaining Walls
It appears that low retaining walls will be utilized throughout the
project as part of the up hill foundations Where the walls are
less than twelve feet in height they should be designed for an
W O 564 00 Page 1974
equivalent fluid pressure of 40 pounds per cubic foot if the on
site soils are utilized as backfill The backfill should be com
pacted to between 90 and 95 per cent of the maximum density esta
blished by American Society for Testing and Materials ASTM Test
Designation D 1557 72 A frictional factor of 0 50 should be uti
lized to determine the sliding resistance of the retaining wall
foundations The existing fill on the site should not be utilized
as backfill behind the retaining walls Retaining wall in excess
of twelve feet in height should be reviewed individually
The site is situated in a high rainfall area and it is recommended
that the retaining wall construction include a subsurface drain and
a minimum one foot thickness of granular material adjacent to the
retaining wall to prevent the buildup of hydrostatic pressures dur
ing periods of high percipitation
Earthwork and Grading
The proposed site grading will entail cuts of up to twelve feet for
the uphill portions of the parking structure and the low rise build
ing The high rise structure will be constructed essentially at
grade for the northern two thirds and with excavations of approxi
mately ten feet in the lower section These excavations do not
include the removal of the existing fill
It is anticipated that new fills varying from 3 to 10 feet in thick
ness will be placed between the parking structure and the low rise
building
W Page 1974
The alluvial and colluvial soils at the site are acceptable as fill
Our previous experience in the area indicates that the optimum
moisture for the on site soils are approximately 40 per cent The
Samples obtained from the test borings generally had natural mois
ture contents exceeding 50 per cent It will therefore be neces
sary to dry the on site soils to achieve proper compaction The
site is located in an area of frequent rainfalls and the drying
Operations could be difficult during all but the dry seasons
It would be advantageous to perform the filling operations during
dry summer months if possible and utilize a borrow material less
sensitive to moisture for backfilling in critical locations
such as adjacent to retaining walls and beneath floor slabs If
granular fill such as quarry waste is utilized for backfill adja
cent to the retaining walls the design lateral earth pressures
can be reduced to 35 pounds per cubic foot
Where fill will be placed upon existing slopes exceeding five
horizontal to one vertical 5 1 the ground surface should be
keyed and benched to properly bond the newly placed fill with the
existing soil Prior to the placement of any fill the exposed
surface should be inspected to insure that all organic and dele
terious materials have been removed The proposed fill areas
should also be inspected for areas of seepage or springs Where
these water conditions are encountered subsurface drains may be
required to prevent saturation of the fill
W O 564 00 Page 1974
Upon approval of the undercut surface it should be scarified to
a depth of six inches brought to near optimum moisture content
and compacted to a minimum of 90 per cent of the maximum density
The additional fill should than be placed in horizontal lifts and
compacted to the same density
Where the fill exceeds four feet in height or where it will be used
for structural support or behind retaining walls the compaction
operation should in inspected on a full time basis to assure that
the recommended degree of compaction is consistently achieved
The final fill slope should not exceed two horizontal to one verti
The alluvial and colluvial soils can be cut at essen
tially vertical slopes for short term construction conditions Per
manent cut slopes should be no steeper than 1 horizontal to 1
vertical 1 1
Paving
Based upon the City County of Honolulu Design Standards for Flex
ible Pavements the following pavement sections should be considered
for preliminary planning
2 in asphaltic concrete pavement
6 in base course
6 in selected borrow
12 in borrow
26 in total pavement thickness

Final pavement sections should be determined during the grading
operations by testing the actual subgrade materials and establish
ing expansion and CBR values
The base course should be crushed rock or other granular material
with a CRB of 85 and conforming with Section 31 2 Standard Speci
fications for Public Work Construction or equivalent Select
borrow subbase should be crusher run waste mudrock coral sand
or cinders conforming to Section 30 of the above specification
Design Review Construction Monitoring
Due to the preliminary stage of the site planning at the time of
our evaluation the grading foundation and retaining wall plans
and details for the structures should be forwarded to the soils
engineer for review and comments prior to finalizing the designs
The grading operations should be inspected on a full time basis
This includes the preparation of the areas to receive fill the
fill placement and compaction backfilling around retaining struc
tures and within buildings and the removal of the existing un
suitable fill
All foundation excavations and slab areas should be inspected prior
to the concrete placement to assure that the subsurface conditions
are consistent with the findings of our investigation

APPENDIX
FIELD EXPLORATIONS
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling
seven borings utilizing truck mounted equipment The depths of
the borings varied from 25 0 to 10 0 feet below the existing
ground surface The locations of the borings are shown on the
enclosed Site Plan Plate 1
The soils were classified by visual and textural examination in
the field by our engineer or geologist who continuously super
vised the drilling operations The classifications were verified
by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory All soils
were classified in accordance with the Unified Soil Classifica
tion System A graphic presentation of the soils encountered is
presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 7
Soil samples were obtained by driving either a 2 4 inch I D or
a standard penetration sampler with a 140 pound hammer free
falling a distance of 30 inches The blow counts to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the log of borings at the
appropriate sample depth

on selected samples to aid in the classification of the soils
The results of these tests are presented on the Log of Borings at
the appropriate sample location
To provide data to evaluate the strength characteristics of the
encountered soils two direct shear tests were performed on
selected samples The test results are shown on Plates A 8 and
A 9
Six consolidation tests were performed on selected samples to
measure the soil response under various vertical loadings The
consolidation test results are graphically presented on Plates
A 10 through A 15

of this letter outlines the factors which

To take advantage of the present building regulations concerning
sprinkler system it was essential that our investigation and
analysis be completed in time to permit the structural engineer
to design the foundations for a December 31 submittal It was
therefore necessary to assign additional personnel to expedite
the surveying of the boring locations and the clearing of the
access roads to the borings The access roads permitted the
utilization of truck mounted drilling equipment which could com
plete the investigation in less time than the light portable
equipment normally utilized for difficult locations
As shown by our final report areas of compressible soils were
encountered which required a more extensive laboratory testing
program To meet the specified deadline it was necessary to
conduct the laboratory testing on an overtime basis to provide
the necessary soil parameters for settlement analysis
