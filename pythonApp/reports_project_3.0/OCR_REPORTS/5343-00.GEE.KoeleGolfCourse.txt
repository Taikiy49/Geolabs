SECTION 1 0 GENERAL
1 1 Introduction
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the ground settlement occurring at the 17 hole of the Koele Golf Course
on the Island of Lanai Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are
shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings and presents our geotechnical engineering
recommendations derived from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering
analyses These recommendations are intended for the design of repair or other
remedial actions only The findings and recommendations presented herein are subject
to the limitations noted at the end of this report
1 2 Project Considerations
The Koele Golf Course is a resort and championship golf course situated on the
northern side of Lanai City on the Island of Lanai Hawaii The project site is situated
along the 17 fairway of the course adjacent to a bunker and water feature as shown
on the Site Plan Plate 2
Based on the information provided a portion of the fairway has experienced
ground settlements on the order of about 12 to 18 inches from the original fairway
surface The impacted area is roughly elliptical in shape and is approximately 90 to
100 feet in length and about 60 feet in width Low scarps have developed along the
edges of the impacted area
1 3 Purpose and Scope
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions at the project site The subsurface information was utilized to develop an
idealized soil data set for the formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations
pertaining to the design of repair or remedial work to the portion of the fairway
experiencing ground settlements Our work was performed in general accordance with

2 1 Regional Geology
The Island of Lanai was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lava from a
single shield volcano The volcano is estimated to be late Pliocene to early Pleistocene
in age As the volcanic activity ceased the central portion subsided forming a low
basin now known as the Palawai Basin The project site is located on the northern side
of the basin
2 2 Existing Site Conditions
The Koele Golf Course is a resort and championship golf course situated on the
northern side of Lanai City on the Island of Lanai Hawaii The project site is situated
along the 17 fairway of the course adjacent to a bunker and water feature as shown
on the Site Plan Plate 2

ground settlements on the order of about 12 to 18 inches from the original fairway
surface The impacted area is roughly elliptical in shape and is approximately 90 to
100 feet in length and about 60 feet in width Low scarps have developed along the
edges of the impacted area
Based on our field observations the project site is covered with well maintained
grass used for the golf course fairway

3 1 Surface Repair and Continued Maintenance
In this alternate the area impacted by ground settlements would be repaired on
the surface and allowed to continue settling until it stabilizes Continued maintenance
by placement of additional material would be required
At the on set of earthwork areas within the grading limits should be thoroughly
cleared and grubbed The surface sod should be removed and disposed of properly
off site or preserved to revegetate the site after the repair
Soft and yielding areas encountered during clearing and grubbing within areas
designated to receive fill should be over excavated to expose firm natural material and
the resulting excavation should be backfilled with well compacted engineered fill The
excavated soft soils may be used as fill provided that it meets the requirements for fill
material
After removal of the surface sod the existing ground should be scarified to a
depth of 10 inches and moisture conditioned to about 2 percent above the optimum
moisture and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Saturation
and subsequent yielding of the exposed subgrade due to inclement weather and poor
drainage may require over excavation of the soft areas and replacement with
well compacted engineered fill The excavated soft soils may be used as fill provided
that it meets the requirements for fill material Relative compaction refers to the in place

The tube a manchette TAM sleeveport grout pipe method uses pipes
perforated at selected intervals with the perforations covered by a flexible sleeve Grout
is injected into the subsoils through these ports usually with a packer inside of the TAM
pipe The TAM method allows for repeated injections of grout into the subsoils until a
specified pressure is attained which is assumed to indicate adequate permeation and
stabilization of the subsoils For this project we recommend that TAM pipes be
installed in staggered rows with the pipes on 3 to 5 foot centers to inject neat cement
grout into the organic debris layer A refusal pressure of 150 to 175 pounds per square
inch is recommended Preliminary estimation with an assumed grout uptake of about
25 percent by volume indicates that about 300 cubic yards of neat cement grout would
be required
Upon completion of grout installation at the site the fairway surface could be
restored in general accordance with the recommendations presented in Subsection 3 1
Surface Repair and Continued Maintenance

Geolabs It is important that a representative from our office observe the grouting
operations to evaluate whether the work is properly performed to verify grout quantities

with ASTM Test Designation D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of
Soils by driving a 2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same hammer and
drop The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an
