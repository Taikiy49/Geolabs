GEOTECHNICAL ENGINEERING CONSULTATION REPORT
BUILDING M DISTRESS
THE RIDGE AT LAUNANI VALLEY
Gentlemen
This report presents the results of our consultation services concerning the building
foundation distress at Unit 104 of Building M at the Ridge at Launani Valley development
located in Waikakalau on the Island of Oahu Hawaii
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering recommendations
resulting from our review of previously performed field explorations and laboratory testing by
others The findings and recommendations presented herein are subject to the limitations
noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The subject multi family development was constructed in about 1993
Subsequently signs of distress including cracks in the interior walls and slabs were noted
in Unit 104 in the middle of Building M Masa Fujioka and Associates MFA the project
geotechnical engineer for the developer evaluated the condition and concluded that the
distress was related to expansion of the subgrade soils caused primarily by drainage
conditions in the building vicinity
The Association of Apartment Owners AOAO contracted Weidig Geoanalysts
WG who conducted a boring exploration around the affected building and concluded that
the distress was caused by settlement of the building due to insufficient compaction of the
fill WG presented their findings and recommendations in a report dated March 23 1999

MFA subsequently drilled four additional borings in the interior of Unit 104 from
October 20 to 25 1999 and obtained soil samples of the near surface soils supporting the
building
Since both geotechnical consultants could not agree on the cause and possible
repair alternatives it was desired to obtain an independent opinion of the probable cause s
of the distress to facilitate determination of possible remedial work for the building Due to
budget and time constraints the independent opinion would be based solely on existing
available data

subsurface information and building grading plans to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions at the project site This information was used to develop a generalized soil rock

causes s of the distress and possible remedial work that can be initiated Our work was
performed in general accordance with our proposal dated The scope of

LABORATORY TESTING
Laboratory testing was performed on samples obtained by MFA from borings inside the
affected unit Recommendations for the types of tests to be performed were obtained from
MFA and WG Generally the tests selected were to evaluate the compressibility and swell
potential of the soils Additionally the moisture content and density were determined to
evaluate the relative compaction of the soils A summary of the laboratory test results is
presented on Plates 2 thru 13
OBSERVATIONS DURING SITE VISITS
A site visit was made on to observe the condition inside the affected
unit A second visit was made on to conduct a level survey of the concrete
slab inside the unit During both visits the unit was vacant and cleared of most of the furniture
and wall coverings
Large cracks were noted in the concrete floor slab of the living room master bedroom
and second bedroom The cracks generally trended parallel to the bearing walls The cracks
appeared consistent with the previous descriptions by both MFA and WG
Horizontal cracks were noted in the second bedroom drywall facing the parking area
Our level survey indicated that the concrete floor slab seems to slope about
one half inch down from the center partition wall to each end wall of the unit This is consistent
with the survey by WG
The gutter downspouts were extended away from the building and connected to an
underground pipe drain system
FINDINGS
The cracking pattern and level survey indicate that differential movements have
occurred between the center partition wall and the two end walls of Unit 104 Several
possible causes of the movements are discussed below

Heaving of the Foundations by Swelling of the Soils
Many soils will expand when water is absorbed and shrink when dry Sources of
water may include landscape watering next to the foundations and leakage from utility lines
under the slab Swelling of the soils was identified as a likely cause of movement due to
the reported ponding of landscape and gutter discharge water next to the building
foundations
Laboratory swell testing of the samples obtained from the MFA borings inside the
unit indicate that the swell potential of the soils is low Also the crack pattern in the
concrete slab is not indicative of heaving movements Generally the heaving related
movements would be larger near the outside walls close to the source of water The lightly
loaded slabs would also be lifted much higher than the heavy wall foundations The level
survey indicates that the heavily loaded center partition wall is higher than the adjacent
concrete slabs For the above reasons it is our opinion that swelling of the soils is not a
primary cause of the observed movements
Bearing Capacity Failure Under Foundations
It has been speculated that the settlements are a result of localized bearing failure
of the foundations We did not observe any signs of bearing failure It is our opinion that
this is not a cause of the movements
Settlement Due to Poor Fill Compaction
The project grading plans indicate that the building site was filled from about 6 to 14
feet Some of the borings encountered thin layers of softer material leading to the
speculation that the fill was not compacted properly It should be noted that due to the
normal method of constructing fill in layers some variability in the fill stiffness is normal
Our review of the compaction tests performed during the fill placement and the densities
of the soil retrieved from the borings inside the unit did not indicate that poor fill
compaction was a primary cause of the observed movements If the fill was poorly
compacted we would expect that signs of distress would be noted in most of the building
units as the entire building is supported by fill
Settlement Due to Fill Softening by Saturation
Most fined grained soils not sand and gravel will lose some strength and increase
compressibility when saturated
A review of the compaction records from MFA indicate that the fill soils were
generally compacted at moisture contents of about 27 to 34 percent Tests on the samples
from the borings inside the unit indicate that the current soil moisture contents are about
34 to 47 percent This increase in moisture content could account for footing settlement
due to the increase in the soil compressibility

The possible sources of water include landscape gutter and seepage water which
were noted at the site The footing settlements would be expected to be higher under the
heavier three story portion of the building as observed
Hand probing with a one half inch steel rod below the wall footing exposed near the
front entrance indicated that the soils within about 6 inches below the footing bottom were
soft and wet
It is our opinion that saturation of the soils is a major cause of the observed
settlement
RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the building distress observed and the results of our review of the
available subsurface data it is our opinion that remedial work should include improvements
to the surface drainage around the building and structural support of selected portions of
the foundations
Drainage Improvements
We believe that the recent extension of the gutter drains into a pipe system will
greatly benefit the performance of the building foundations The ground surface adjacent
to the building should be shaped to drain water away from the foundations to reduce the
potential for surface water infiltration into the soils below the structure The site should be
observed periodically after heavy rains to confirm that ponding areas are not present
Structural Improvements
In order to greatly reduce future foundation movements structural support may be
provided under the three affected wall footings of Unit 104 The structural supports may
consist of Atlas piers extending to below the fill material and bearing into the natural soils
at depths of about 20 feet below the existing ground surface Generally the piers should
be spaced about 5 to 7 feet apart A structural engineer and or specialty contractor should
be consulted for the exact spacing of the piers
Installation of the Atlas piers will likely require over excavation of about 2 feet
laterally beyond the Atlas pier location to allow installation of the bracket and pipe pile
jacking assembly The excavation will also extend laterally about 1 foot beneath the
footing The piers should be jacked downward until a bearing capacity of 20 000 pounds
based on a 3 5 inch outside diameter steel pipe is obtained and to a minimum depth of
20 feet below the existing ground surface

Support for Remainder of Building
It is our understanding that the other units in the building have not experienced
movements similar to Unit 104 Therefore it is our opinion that structural support is
unnecessary for the remainder of the building at this time However it should be understood
that installation of the structural support under Unit 104 could create a hard spot if the
remainder of the building experiences future settlement It this occurs additional structural
support may be required for the remainder of the building
In order to reduce possible future differential settlement between the Atlas pier
supported unit and the remainder of the building it may be advisable to delay installation of
the Atlas piers for a period of 6 to 12 months During this waiting period level readings may
be taken at selected points around the building to determine if settlements are ongoing If the
level readings indicate negligible settlement the piers in Unit 104 may be installed with greater
confidence that differential settlement may not occur
Construction Observations
It is recommended that Geolabs be retained to provide geotechnical engineering
services during construction of the remedial work for the project The items of construction
monitoring that are critical and which require Special Inspection include observation of the
Atlas pier installation and subgrade preparation for the concrete slabs This is to observe
compliance with the intent of the design concepts specifications or recommendations and to
expedite suggestions for design changes that may be required in the event that subsurface
conditions differ from those anticipated at the time this report was prepared The
recommendations provided in this report are contingent upon such observations If the actual
soil conditions encountered during construction are different from those assumed or
considered in the report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
