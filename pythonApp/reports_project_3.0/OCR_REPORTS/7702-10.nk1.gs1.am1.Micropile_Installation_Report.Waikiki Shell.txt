As requested, Geolabs, Inc. provided geotechnical engineering observation and 
consultation services during the production micropile foundation installation work for the 
above project. This letter report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations 
during the installation of production micropile foundations for the new sound and lighting 
equipment for the Waikiki Shell Improvement project in Honolulu on the Island of Oahu, 
Hawaii. The project location and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map, 
Plate 1. 
This letter report presents our field observations and the results of load testing 
performed for the production micropiles, and our findings and recommendations for the 
micropile foundation for the project. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The Waikiki Shell facility is located in Honolulu on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. 
Based on the contract documents provided, we understand that the footings for the truss 
frame structures are to be supported by 5.5-inch diameter micropiles with a design load 
capacity of 29 kips. A layout of the existing floor plan and proposed foundations are

MICROPILE INSTALLATION AND TESTING 
Geotechnical recommendations for the micropile foundations were presented in our 
referenced letter report dated March 8, 2019, and addendum dated October 27, 2020. The 
purpose of our geotechnical engineering observation and consultation services was to 
observe the micropile foundation installation and testing for general conformance with our 
recommendations. 
Based on the anticipated subsurface conditions at the site, the micropiles were 
specified to extend through the very loose lagoonal deposits and derive load-bearing 
support from the underlying calcareous sandstone formation. 
Kelikai, Inc. installed a total of sixteen production micropiles from January 25 
through February 4, 2021 at the approximate locations shown on the Micropile Location 
Plan, Plate 2. The micropile locations were provided by Kelikai, Inc. Details of the micropile 
installation are presented in the Micropile Records in Appendix A. 
In general, the micropiles were installed using a Beretta T-24 drill rig equipped with 
a down-the-hole hammer and advanced with 5.5-inch OD (Outside Diameter) steel casing 
to the specified depth of the micropile to create a drilled hole of at least 5.5 inches in 
diameter. Advancing steel casing to the bottom of the specified depth was performed in 
order to facilitate the micropile installation.  
After the drilled holes were extended to the specified depth and cleaned out, the 
micropiles were initially grouted. Grout was batched on-site utilizing a colloidal high-shear 
and high-speed ChemGrout CG-500H mixer equipped with a grout pump. The grout mix 
consisted of one sack of 94-pound Portland Cement Type I and/or II, 87 pounds of 
masonry sand, and 5 gallons of water.  
The grout was pumped into the cased hole through a PVC tremie pipe starting from 
the bottom of the cased hole. After clean grout appeared at the top of the cased hole, a 
reinforcing bar with centralizers was placed in the cased hole. 
The temporary steel casing was removed one section at a time and re-grouted as 
necessary to maintain the grout level near the top of the casing. This process continued 
until the bottom of the steel casing reached the top of the bond zone. The permanent 
casing was then plunged to a final depth of approximately 3 feet into the bearing layer.  
It should be noted that Micropile No. C1 was not installed properly and at a 
4-degree from vertical inclination. Kelikai, Inc. successfully removed all seven steel 
casings and filled the hole with grout to the ground surface using about seven bags of 
cement. Micropile No. C1 was later reinstalled and identified as Micropile No. C-1R.

Micropile Testing 
In order to evaluate the load carrying capacities of the installed micropiles, two 
proof tests were conducted on two production micropiles. The proof tests were conducted 
on Micropile Nos. A-4 and C-1R on February 19, 2021. Both micropiles were loaded to a 
maximum test load of 43.5 kips (150 percent of the design load). The maximum test load 
of about 43.5 kips was help for 10 minutes to evaluate creep effects. Subsequently, both 
micropiles were unloaded to zero load in about five equal load decrements.  
At the maximum test load, the creep movement during the 10-minute hold for 
Micropile Nos. A-4 and C-1R were about 0.009 inches and 0.003 inches, respectively, 
which are less than 0.04 inches typically allowed for the micropile foundation system. At 
the maximum test load, the vertical displacement of Micropile Nos. A-4 and C-1R were 
about 0.11 and 0.049 inches, respectively. In general, the proof-tested production 
micropiles met the acceptance criteria specified in the project requirements. The results of 
the proof tests are presented in Appendix B. 
FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS 
Based on our field observations of the micropile installation and micropile proof load 
tests performed by the subcontractor, it is our professional opinion that the production 
micropiles for the project have been installed to provide the design load capacity of 29 kips 
with tolerable movements.  
LIMITATIONS
