INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering evaluation
performed for the proposed residence at Lot No 77 Waialae Iki Heights on the island
of Oahu Hawaii The exploration was performed in general accordance with our proposal
dated

This report summarizes our findings from the exploration and our geotechnical
engineering recommendations derived from our analysis based on the data obtained
during the exploration These recommendations are intended for design input and are
not intended for construction estimation or bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
Based on the plans provided to us the proposed new residence will be a two story
structure approximately 60 by 100 feet in plan dimensions Concrete on grade slab is
planned for garage and driveway areas A swimming pool with a surrounding deck
system will be enclosed on three sides of the house
Site grading is minimal with cuts of up to 5 feet in the pool and semi basement
areas and the remainder of the house structure located close to existing grade

SITE DESCRIPTION
The subject lot is located on Laukahi Street near its intersection with Oko a Street
in Waialae Iki Honolulu This trapezoid shaped lot encompasses approximately 9 000
square feet The topography of the site slopes downward from north to south with a
gradient of approximately six horizontal to one vertical 6GH 1V The current ground
elevations range from about 970 feet Southern portion to 990 feet northern portion
above Mean Sea Level Sewer and utility easements are located along the lower
southern lot boundary

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The stiff on site residual soils encountered below the existing ground surface can
generally provide adequate foundation support for the proposed house provided that
some care is exercised during construction to control the swelling potential of the soil as
discussed herein
The field exploration and laboratory tests performed on the stiff on site silty clay
indicate that this material generally has low to moderate swelling potential if it is allowed
to dry prior to subsequent saturation Therefore to reduce the effects due to soil swelling
under on grade slabs we recommend that the on site soil be capped with 12 inches of
non expansive select fill and that the subgrade soils underneath the select fill and slab
be moistened and protected from drying from the start of construction until the slab
concrete placement
Lot Grading
The fills and backfills within the top 12 inches minimum below the on grade slabs
and its cushion fill should consist of non expansive soils such as select borrow crusher
waste or coral Where cut or minimal fill is anticipated the on site soil should be
over excavated to provide a 12 inch minimum thickness of non expansive select fill
capping underneath the on grade slab s cushion fill
The on site cut soils may be used for fill if they are compacted at least 5 above
optimum moisture content and placed below 12 inches from the slab bottom and away
from retaining walls
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2778 00 Page 6
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
cleared of vegetation debris rubbish and other deleterious materials These materials
should be removed and disposed of properly off site Any loose or soft spots
encountered should be over excavated down to the firm subsoils and then replaced with
compacted non expansive select material
The fill material should consist of non expansive granular materials generally less
than three 3 inches in maximum size with less than 20 passing the 200 sieve All
imported fill material should be tested and approved by the soils engineer prior to
transportation to the site
The fill areas should then be scarified to a depth of six 6 inches moisture
conditioned and recompacted to 90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method
D 1557 78 Due to the sloping nature of the site we recommend that the fill should be
keyed and benched into the natural stiff ground
The fill should be placed in maximum 8 inch thick loose level lifts and compacted
to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
House Foundations
Spread or continuous wail footings may be used for the proposed residence An
allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f may be used for footings bearing on the natural soil
or compacted fill
Individual footings and continuous wall footings for the residence should be
embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2778 00 Page 7
If located on a sloping area the footing should be embedded down an additional
amount to obtain a 10 foot minimum horizontal set back distance measured from the
outer edge of the footing out to the slope face This additional embedment depth is
required to help resist the downhill slope creep movement and to develop the
recommended bearing capacity of the soil
The construction of the enclosed swimming pool may alter the footing
requirements Generally the swimming pool is constructed after foundation construction
of the house In this case the pool excavation may cause settlements of nearby shallow
footings To reduce this possibility the house footing near the pool should be embedded
below a two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V plane extending upward from the bottom
of the pool
Slab On Grade
It should be noted that the on site silty clay soils may be moderately expansive if
allowed to dry Therefore the on site soil should be over excavated down to a depth of
12 inches minimum below the slab cushion fill thoroughly moistened and then replaced
with non expansive imported soils as previously discussed The thickened edge of the
slab should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finish grade
To further reduce future post construction swelling of the underlying expansive
soils we recommend that the in place soils below the upper capping fill pad be kept
moist prior to concrete and cushion fill placement
A 4 inch layer of 3B Fine gravel cushion fill should be provided below the slab
In order to protect against future moisture infiltration an impervious moisture barrier may
be incorporated below the slab

Drainage
The finish lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings should be shaped to
shed water away from foundations and to avoid ponding condition Also it is advised that
gutter systems be installed around the house and gutter water be diverted far away from
the foundations and driveway Excessive landscape watering near foundations and slab
should also be avoided
Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have concrete bottoms and
drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the above
foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause excessive subsurface soil
saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations
should be properly backfilled tight against the wall or slab footing immediately after setting
of the concrete to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and maintained to drain
all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundations
Retaining Walls
Retaining walls may be required to provide different finish grades for the proposed
project The following design guidelines for retaining walls are recommended
1 For lateral earth pressures the following values may be used
Walls unrestrained at the top 35 p c f equivalent
active condition level fluid pressure
backfill and above groundwater

Walls restrained at the top 50 p c f equivalent
at rest condition level fluid pressure
backfill and above groundwater
2 A bearing value up to 3 500 p s f with a minimum footing embedment of 12
inches below the lowest adjacent finish grade may be used for design
3 For lateral resistance design a passive earth resistance of 300 p s f may
be used a base friction factor of 0 4 may also be used to determine the
resistance against sliding of the walls
4 Backfill behind the retaining walls should consist of granular non expansive
material such as select on site sandy material select borrow or No 3B Fine
gravel to minimize excessive compaction effort required The upper 12
inches of backfill should consist of non expansive impervious material to
reduce water infiltration behind the walls
5 A subsurface drainage system should be utilized behind the walls to protect
against build up of hydrostatic pressure

Concrete Driveway
Since the driveway can tolerate some amount of heaving the 12 inch on site soil
over excavation and replacement may be replaced with 6 inch thick base course layer if
some amount of small slab cracking are tolerable It is critical that the subgrade soils
should be maintained in a moist condition and prevented from drying out prior to base
course and concrete placement
Joints are recommended in the driveway slab at about 8 to 10 foot intervals and
at discontinuities in order to allow for some relative movements of the concrete
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be forwarded to the
geotechnical engineer for review and written comments prior to construction This review
is needed to determine adherence to the earthwork and foundation recommendations
given herein If this review is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume
responsibility for misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during site grading and construction of the foundation
This is to observe compliance with the design concepts specifications or
recommendations and to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions
differ from that anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in
this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and approved by
the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site

from those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from the borings Variations of conditions between the borings may
occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident until
construction If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations given in this report
The location of the borings were approximately determined by taping from
structures indicated on the Plot Plan provided by Custom Home Planners Elevations of
the data points were approximately determined by interpolation from elevation points on
the plans The physical location and elevation of the borings should be considered
accurate only to the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the borings depict the
approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual
transition The strata lines shown on profiles or cross sections are based upon
interpolation between field data points and may not represent actual subsurface
conditions

Therefore this report may not contain sufficient data or the proper information for use
