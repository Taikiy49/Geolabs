Dear Mr. Kaneshiro: 
This letter report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations performed 
during the foundation probing and grouting operations for the New Classroom Building at 
James Campbell High School project between April 16 and May 10, 2018. 
The project location is within the James Campbell High School complex in Ewa on the 
Island of Oahu, Hawaii, as shown on the Project Location Map, Plate 1. The project 
encompasses approximately 53,000 square feet with five, 2 and 3-story buildings as shown 
on the General Site Plan, Plate 2. 
Because of potential subsurface cavities and/or voids within the underlying coral 
formation, a probing and grouting program to locate and grout potential cavities beneath 
foundations was specified for the project. The probe hole locations were surveyed and 
marked in the field by the general contractor (Swinerton Builders) prior to the probing 
operations. The probe holes were spaced at about less than 10-foot on center along the 
structure foundations. The total number of scheduled probe holes for the five buildings are as 
follows:  
Building No. 1: 73 probe holes 
Building No. 2: 51 probe holes 
Building No. 3: 31 probe holes

The approximate locations and identification of the scheduled probe holes for this 
project are shown on the Probe Hole Identification Plans, Plates 3.1 through 3.5. 
The probing and grouting work was performed by Structural System Inc. The probe 
holes were drilled with a Casagrande C6 drill rig equipped with a 4-inch diameter bit. The 
actual probe holes drilled were about 4.5 inches in diameter and extended to about 12 to 
14 feet below the existing ground surface. The probe holes generally extended to a depth 
of at least 10 feet below the bottom of footing elevations. A summary of our foundation 
probe drilling and grouting observations is provided on Plates 4.1 through 4.12. 
The materials encountered during the probe drilling operations generally consisted of 
white to gray coralline materials.  After completion of the probe drilling operations, the probe 
holes were filled with 4,000 pounds per square inch cement grout mix. A Chem 500 hydraulic 
grouting equipment was used for the grouting.   
Additional probe holes were drilled adjacent to the scheduled probe holes with grout 
intakes greater than about 4 times the theoretical volume of the hole.  Of the 228 scheduled 
probe holes, 77 additional probe holes were drilled.  The total number of additional probe 
holes drilled for each building are as follows: 
Building No. 1: 23 holes 
Building No. 2: 31 holes 
Building No. 3: 4 holes 
Building No. 4: 0 holes 
Building No. 5: 19 holes 
In summary, the foundation probing and grouting operations for the project have 
been conducted in general conformance with the intent of the project documents. Based on 
our observations, it is our professional opinion that the voids and/or cavities encountered at 
the structure foundation locations have been grouted and that the subgrade area should 
provide the recommended foundation support for the new classroom building foundations.
