nor for construction bidding purposes

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lavas from
two shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated
to be of the middle to late Pliocene in age and Koolau is estimated to be of the late
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3409 00 Page 5
Pliocene to early Pleistocene in age After a long period of volcanic inactivity during
which erosion incised deep valleys into the Koolau shield volcanic activity returned with
a series of lava flows followed by cinder and tuff cone formations These series are
referred to as the Honolulu Volcanic Series
In the mountainous regions of Hawaii the erosional processes are dominated by
detachment of soil and rock masses from the valley walls and are transported
downslope toward the axis of a valley primarily by gravity as colluvium Once these
materials reach the stream in the central portion of a valley alluvial processes become
dominant and the sediments are transported and deposited as alluvium
in general stream flows in Hawaii are intermittent and flashy such that the
stream flows transmit large volumes of water for very short durations Because of this
transport of sediments is intermittent and the bulk of the stream s hydraulic load
consists of a poorly sorted mixture of boulders cobbles gravel sands and fines
When the erosional base levels change these sediment loads are left as deposits
The project site is located in Nuuanu Valley an amphitheater headed valley
carved by erosion into Koolau Basalts Filling the base of this valley is a thick
sequence of alluvium interbedded with deposits of lavas and pyroclastics of the
Honolulu Volcanic Series

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Our field exploration generally encountered alluvial deposits consisting of
medium stiff to stiff clayey silts with high moisture contents near the proposed design
finish subgrades of approximately 12 feet below the existing ground surface Based
on the relatively competent subsoil conditions encountered in our borings and the
anticipated light structural loads it is our opinion that shallow foundations bearing on
the undisturbed in situ stiff soils may be used for support of the proposed new
residence Our laboratory testing indicated that the on site soils exhibit slight to low
expansion potential when subjected to moisture fluctuations Therefore concrete slabs
on grade may be constructed on grade with proper subgrade preparation Because of
the relatively high moisture contents of the subsoils anticipated at the design subgrade
elevations special attention should be given to the subgrade preparation for this project
to reduce the potential for pumping subgrade conditions In addition we recommend
that Contract documents include additive and deductive unit prices for over excavation
and engineered fill placement in the event that soft and wet pumping subgrade soils are
encountered during the subgrade preparation work Detailed recommendations are
presented in the following sections of this report

Foundations
Based on the generally competent subsoil conditions encountered at the site we
believe that shallow spread and or continuous footings may be used for support of the
proposed new residence An allowable bearing pressure of up to 2 000 pounds per
square foot p s f may be used for the design of foundations bearing on the
undisturbed in situ medium stiff to stiff clayey silt soils This bearing value is for dead
plus live loads and may be increased by one third for transient loads such as those
caused by wind or seismic forces
in general footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the
lowest adjacent finish grades For footings constructed near top of slopes or on sloping
ground the footings should be embedded deep enough to provide a minimum
horizontal set back distance of 8 feet measured from the outside edge of the footings
to the slope face
The bottom of the footing excavations should be lightly compacted to provide a
relatively firm and smooth bearing surface prior to the placement of reinforcing steel or
concrete If soft and or loose materials are encountered at the bottom of footing
excavations they should be over excavated to expose the underlying firm materials
The over excavation should be backfilled with seléct granular material compacted to a
minimum of 90 percent relative compaction or the footing bottom may be extended
down to the underlying competent material Relative compaction refers to the in place
dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same
soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 91 test procedures Optimum
moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the
maximum dry density

Stab On Grade
We anticipate that concrete slabs on grade will be required for the project
construction Based on the laboratory test results the subsoils encountered at the site
W O 3409 00 Page 10
generally consist of silty clays and clayey silts which exhibit slight to low expansion
potential when subjected to fluctuations in moisture content Therefore it is our opinion
that concrete slabs on grade may be constructed on grade with proper subgrade
preparation Detailed recommendations are presented in the Site Preparation section
of this report
It should be noted that the on site soils have high natural moisture contents
Therefore the slab subgrades should be kept moist prior to placement of concrete to
reduce the potential for shrinkage of the subgrade soils To reduce the potential for
drying of the subgrade soils and to reduce the costs of form construction we
recommend that the slab edges be cast neat against the soils The slab edges
should incorporate a_ sufficient amount of top and bottom longitudinal steel
reinforcement The top and bottom steel reinforcement bars should be connected by
stir ups The ends of the stir ups should extend into the floor slab area and should be
tied to the welded wire mesh A structural engineer should be consulted for design
details of this thickened edge which is intended to function both as a moisture barrier
and as a perimeter wall footing
The welded wire mesh in the slab on grade should be embedded at the proper
height in the concrete to provide adequate reinforcement Blocks or saddles may be
used to raise the wire mesh A stiff grade or wire mesh is recommended
We recommend that a minimum 4 inch thick layer of cushion fill consisting of
No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation be placed below the slab to provide
more uniform support of the slab To reduce future moisture infiltration and subsequent
damage to floor coverings an impervious moisture vapor barrier such as a plastic
membrane is recommended on top of the gravel cushion layer Two inches of moist
W O 3409 00 Page 11
fine sand should be placed above the plastic membrane to provide protection of the
membrane and to aid in curing of the slab concrete
Flexible floor coverings such as carpet or sheet vinyl should be considered
since they can better mask any minor slab cracking It is also recommended that
interior walls be designed to incorporate some flexibility to accommodate small amount
of possible ground movements
Where the slab is subjected to vehicular traffic such as the garage slab a 6 inch
layer of base course compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction is
recommended in lieu of the 4 inch thick cushion layer The 2 inch moist fine sand and
vapor barrier may be omitted for the garage slabs
Exterior concrete walkways may be required for the proposed project We
recommend that a minimum 4 inch thick cushion layer of No 3B Fine gravel be
provided below the concrete walkway slab The subgrade soils underneath the
sidewalk slabs should be prepared in accordance with the recommendations presented
in the Site Preparation section of this report Construction joints should be provided
at intervals equal to the width of the walkways with expansion joints at right angle
intersections
The area adjacent to the slabs should be backfilled tightly against the slab with
non expansive impervious soil and graded to divert water away from the slab to reduce
the potential of water ponding around the foundations

Site Preparation
We understand that cuts on the order of about 12 feet will be required to reach
the design finish subgrades for the project construction At the on set of earthwork the
area within the contract grading limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed All
vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable materials should be removed and
disposed of off site
Slabs and foundations of the existing structures that are to be demolished should
be completely removed Any over excavation resulting from the demolition should be
backfilled with compacted select granular fill material Existing utilities to be abandoned
should be removed and the resulting excavation should be properly backfilled with
select granular fill material placed in 6 to 8 inch loose lifts and mechanically compacted
Utilities that are to be abandoned in place under the proposed structure should be
backfilled by pumping lean concrete under low pressure
After demolition clearing and grubbing the site should be excavated to the
proposed design finish subgrades Due to the high natural moisture contents the
design subgrade should be proof rolled with a smooth drum roller in lieu of scarification
and recompaction In addition heavy or rubber tired equipment are not recommended
to reduce the potential for pumping subgrade conditions Vibratory action of the

subgrade

Utility Trench
We envision that new utility connections may be required for the project A
granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33
No 67 gradation is recommended under the pipes Free draining granular materials
such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation should also be used for
the trench backfill up to about 12 inches above the pipes to provide adequate support
around the pipes and to reduce the compaction effort of the backfill thus reducing the
possibility of damaging the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipes
to the top of the subgrade or finished grade should consist of select granular material
The backfill should be placed in about 8 inch loose lifts and mechanically compacted
to not less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for future ground
subsidence The on site materials may be used as backfill materials however it may
be difficult to attain the required compaction due to the high in situ moisture contents
Where trenches are below pavement areas the upper 2 feet of the trench backfill below
the pavement subgrade should be compacted to not less than 95 percent relative
compaction

Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling two
borings to depths of approximately 28 and 31 feet below the existing ground surface
at the approximate locations shown on Plate 2 The borings were drilled using truck
mounted drilling equipment
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our field geologist who monitored the drilling operations on
a near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by
testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified
Soil Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representation of the
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils
by driving a 3 inch O D Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling
30 inches The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the last 12 inches of an
18 inch drive or the middle 12 inches of an 24 inch drive are shown on the Logs of
Borings at the appropriate sample depths

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content and unit weight determinations were performed on selected soil
samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties The results of
these tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Eight 1 inch ring swell tests were performed on selected samples to evaluate the
swelling potential of the subsoils The test results are summarized on Plates B 1 and
B 2
One Atterberg Limits test was performed on a selected sample of the soils to
evaluate the liquid and plastic limits The test results are shown on the Logs of Borings
at the appropriate sample depth and on Plate B 3

Material Specifications
Engineering fabric consisting of pavement reinforcing fabric shall conform to the
requirements in this section The fabric shall be placed in accordance with the
requirements in this section The engineering fabric shall be furnished in protective
covers capable of protecting the fabric from ultraviolet rays abrasion and water
The pavement reinforcing fabric shall be manufactured from high modulus
woven polypropylene material The fabric shall also conform in all respects to the
property requirements listed below
Average Grab Tensile Strength ASTM D pounds
Maximum Grab Tensile Elongation ASTM D percent
Average Burst Strength ASTM D psi
Trapezoid Tear Strength ASTM D pounds
Puncture Resistance ASTM D pounds
Roll Size and Weight Approximately 500 Square Yards weighing
approximately 210 pounds

Placement Specifications
The fabric should be laid in the direction of construction traffic Fabric rolls
should be laid out such that they overlap by a minimum of 24 inches at the ends and
sided of rolls Aggregate should be back dumped on the fabric after it has been pulled
taut in both directions over the subgrade Wheeled or tracked vehicles should not be
operated directly on the fabric
