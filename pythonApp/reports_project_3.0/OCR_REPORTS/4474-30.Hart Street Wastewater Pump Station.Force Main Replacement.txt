Project Considerations
We understand that the project consists of installing approximately 5 200 lineal feet of
new sewer force main between the existing Hart Street Wastewater Pump Station and the
Sand Island Wastewater Treatment Plant Microtunneling methods will be used to install about
2 000 lineal feet of 48 inch diameter force main from the Sand Island Wastewater Treatment
Plant to the south side of the Honolulu Harbor crossing It was desired to conduct a field
exploration program by drilling and sampling six borings along the microtunneling alignment
and at the new jacking and receiving pits in the Sand Island area of Honolulu on the Island of
Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1
We understand that the proposed sewer line invert is located approximately 25 feet
below the existing ground surface We envision that the sheet piles for the jacking and
receiving pits will extend to depths of about 15 to 20 feet below the invert of the sewer line
One boring each was drilled at Jacking Pit No 2 and Receiving Pit No 3 The remaining four
borings were drilled along the sewer line alignment between the jacking and receiving pits as
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2

Purpose and Scope
The purpose of our work on this project was to provide drilling and sampling services
for this microtunneling project In order to accomplish this we conducted a program generally
consisting of the following tasks and work efforts
1 Mobilization and demobilization of a truck mounted drill rig and two operators

Regional Geology
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lavas from two
shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be
middle to late Pliocene in age and Koolau is estimated to be late Pliocene to early Pleistocene
in age After a long period of volcanic inactivity during which time erosion incised deep valleys
into the Koolau Shield volcanic activity returned with a series of lava flows followed by cinder
and tuff cone formations These series are referred to as the Honolulu Volcanic Series
The project site is located on the man made Sand Island on Southern Oahu The
coastal plain was built on the eroded flanks of the Koolau Volcano which forms the eastern
two thirds of the Island of Oahu The coastal plain was built by extensive accumulation of
alluvium derived from erosion of the volcano interbedded with coral reefs and associated
deposits
During the Pleistocene Epoch Ice Age sea levels fluctuated in response to the cycles
of continental glaciation Most of the coastal plains were developed during the Pleistocene
Epoch when the sea levels fluctuated significantly As the glaciers grew and advanced less
water was available to fill the oceanic basins such that sea levels fell below the present stands
of the sea When the glaciers melted and receded an excess of water became available such
that the sea levels rose to above the present sea

The processes of erosion and deposition were affected by these glacio eustatic sea
level fluctuations When the sea level was low the erosional base level was correspondingly
lower and valleys were carved to depths below the present sea level When the sea level was
high the erosional base level was raised such that sediments accumulated at higher
elevations
The project site is generally underlain by deposits of lagoonal and calcareous materials
A surface layer of man made fill was placed over these deposits for the development of
Sand Island within the last century
Surface and Subsurface Conditions
The project site is located in the Sand Island area of Honolulu on the Island of Oahu
Hawaii The microtunneling alignment will start from the Sand Island Wastewater Treatment
Plant traverse Sand Island Parkway and extend into the Matson container terminal
The project site is generally level Based on the available information elevations of the
existing ground surface generally range from about 8 to 10 feet Mean Sea Level MSL At
the Sand Island Wastewater Treatment Plant area the ground surface generally consists of
open grass lawn areas The Matson container terminal area is generally paved with asphaltic
concrete
Based on our drilled borings the project site is generally underlain by near surface fills
consisting of mixtures of silty sands and gravel extending to depths of about 4 to 11 feet below
the existing ground surface The relative density of the near surface fill is likely to range from
loose to dense in localized areas Our borings generally encountered lagoonal deposits
consisting of very loose to medium dense silty sands and gravel underlying the surface fills
extending to depths of about 22 to 32 feet below the existing ground surface corresponding
to about Elevation 12 to 23 feet MSL The lagoonal deposits are generally underlain by
medium hard to very hard coral reef formation extending to the maximum depth explored of
about 47 3 feet below the existing ground surface
Groundwater was generally encountered at about 6 5 to 9 7 feet below the existing
ground surface at the time of our field exploration The groundwater levels measured
correspond to elevations of approximately 1 4 to 3 1 feet MSL It should be noted that
groundwater levels are expected to vary with tidal fluctuations seasonal rainfall time of the
year and other factors

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by drilling and sampling
six borings designated as Boring Nos 101 through 106 extending to depths of about 31 to
47 3 feet below the existing ground surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site
Plan Plate 2 The borings were drilled using a truck mounted drill rig equipped with
continuous flight augers and rotary wash methods
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by testing in
the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil Classification
System as shown on Plate A Graphic representations of the materials encountered are
presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 6
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 3550 84 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 3 inch
OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30inches In addition some
samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 2 inch
OD standard penetrometer sampler using the same hammer and drop The blow counts
needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown as
the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Torvane shear tests were performed on selected cohesive soil samples soft to medium
stiff samples only The torvane shear test provides a quick estimate of the undrained shear
strength of the soil Results of the torvane shear tests are summarized on the Logs of Borings
at the appropriate sample depths
