As requested we have made a study of the near surface soil
conditions below the above referenced lot The purpose of the study
was to evaluate the suitability of the near surface soils as foundation
support for the proposed house The study consisted of three 3
borings drilled within the proposed building site laboratory soil
tests and a review of the grading and compaction test record on the
building site This report summarizes our observations and
recommendations
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed single family residence consists of a two story wood
structure approximately 40 by 56 feet in plan dimension A partial
basement level is planned for the rear portion of the house Based on
the preliminary plans provided to us the house will be set back about
19 feet from Kahena Street
Concrete slab on grade is planned for the garage driveway and
basement floor
The basement finished floor elevation is anticipated to be about
194 3 feet
CMU retaining walls are planned around the garage and the lower
partial basement
About 4 feet of cut is planned on the southeastern portion of the
basement area
EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
Lot 17 is located on Kahena Street in the eastern portion of the
subdivision development It is bounded by Kahena Street on the west
subdivision lots on the north south and east An existing concrete
drainage channel is located along the lower northeastern boundary of
the lot
The lot generally slopes downward from Kahena Street toward the
east at about 15 percent gradient The elevation of the lot varies
from 181 to 210 feet The existing rectangular concrete drainage
channel is about 10 feet deep

SUBDIVISION LOT GRADING
Prior to the recent project site grading operation Lot 17 was
covered with a large stockpile of soil and boulders left over from
previous grading operations in the area
During that grading operation the stockpile was removed down to a
depth of 4 feet below the design finished grade and replaced with com
pacted available on site soils The available on site soil consisted
basically of silty clay and clayey silt soils which has been mixed with
some adobe clay pockets from the previous stockpile
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
A review of the grading and compaction test record and a site
reconnaissance indicate that Lot 17 is underlain by grayish brown
expansive clay to the depth of the borings which was about 6 5 feet
Detailed description of the soils encountered in borings are
presented on the attached Log of Borings Plates A 1 through A 3 The
approximate locations of the borings are shown on the attached Site
Plan Plate l
Laboratory soil tests on the near surface material indicated that
the soil can be classified as clay CH according to the Unified Soil
Classification System Laboratory one inch ring swell tests indicated
that the soil has a moderate to high shrink swell potential The
laboratory test results are summarized on Plate B 1

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that most of the lot is underlain
by grayish brown clay The laboratory test results on this material
indicated that they have high expansion potential Therefore for Lot
17 the slab on grade and foundation construction would require special
design procedures in order to reduce the shrink swell effect of the
subsoils
House Foundation
Slab on grade is planned for the garage and semi basement floor
level The on site clay soils are expansive and unsuitable as slab
subgrade soils In order to reduce the expansive effects of the soils
level should be over excavated and replaced with non expansive borrow
soils such as select borrow crusher waste or coral compacted to a
minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78 The
over excavation should extend for a minimum distance of 3 feet beyond
the slabs
A 4 inch layer of base course should be provided below the slab
and compacted to 90 of its maximum dry density In order to protect
against moisture infiltration a polyethylene moisture barrier may be
incorporated below the slab The membrane should be protected by a
thin layer of sand to aid in the concrete curing
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Page 5
The thickened edge slab footings can bear on the compacted
non expansive fill The thickened edges should be embedded a minimum
of 8 inches below the lowest adjacent grade
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f may be used on the
compacted fill
Surface water should be diverted away from foundations
For post and beam construction we recommend that the expansive
clays be over excavated down for a depth of 3 feet below the bottom of
the individual post footings and then replaced with non expansive
borrow soils such as crusher waste select borrow or coral compacted
to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78 The
spread footings can then be placed on the compacted non expansive soil
Alternatively the individual spread footings can be embedded a
minimum of 3 feet below the finished ground surface
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f can be used for footings
bearing on the compacted select material or stiff existing ground
Basement Retaining Wall Foundation
Basement retaining walls up to 4 feet high are planned in the
southeastern portion of the house

In order to reduce the expansive effects of the existing clay
soils on the wall we recommend that the expansive clays be
overexcavated down for a depth of 2 feet below the bottom of the
footing and then replaced with non expansive borrow soils such as
crusher waste or coral compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum
dry density per ASTM D 1557 78 The overexcavation and replacement
should extend a minimum distance of 12 inches beyond the footing width
The wall footings should be embedded a minimum depth of 12 inches
below the outside finished grade Therefore the total depth of the
above overexcavation and replacement would be 3 feet below the outside
finished grade
An allowable bearing value of up to 2500 p s f can be used
In order to reduce the build up of high lateral earth pressures on
the wall due to the existing clay soils off site non expansive soil
should be used as backfill for a minimum distance equal to the height
of the wall behind the wall

Concrete Driveway and Sidewalks
Expansive clay soils were encountered in the driveway area Since
the driveway can tolerate some amount of heaving the clay over excavation
and replacement with non expansive soil can be reduced to 2 feet below the
driveway subgrade level Where 2 or more feet of fill is planned select
non expansive borrow material should be used to cap over the expansive
clay
Where less than 2 feet of fill is planned over excavation and
replacement of the expansive clay with select non expansive material is
required This requirement should be followed for a minimum distance of 3
feet beyond the driveway
Since a relatively long driveway is planned joints are
recommended at about 8 to 10 foot intervals in order to allow for some
relative movement of the concrete

For future sidewalks capping of the clay or over excavation and
replacement similar to the driveway design is recommended to reduce
heaving caused by the expansive clay soil
Site Grading
The existing terrain at the site generally slopes down at about 15
percent grade or flatter
Up to 4 feet of cut in the basement and 3 feet of fill in the
garage are anticipated
The area to be filled should be cleared of vegetation and debris
prior to filling
Fill and backfill materials should be select non expansive borrow
material placed in maximum 8 inch lifts and compacted to a minimum of
90 of its maximum dry density established by ASTM D 1557 78 The
on site clay soil should not be used for fill material below structures
and behind retaining walls
Drainage
The finished lot grade outside the slabs pavements and
foundations should be shaped to avoid ponding conditions and to shed
water away from the structures In addition it is advised that gutter
systems be installed around the house and that gutter water should be
diverted far away from the foundations and walkways Excessive
landscape watering near the foundations and slab should also be avoided
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 865 76 Page 9
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have
concrete bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper
performance of the above referenced foundations since ponded water
could cause excessive subsurface soil saturation and resulting in
heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations should be
properly backfilled tight against the wall or slab footing immediately
after setting of the concrete to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and
maintained to drain all surface runoff away from the slab and footing
foundation
Design Review
Plans and specifications for foundations site grading and utility
construction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and
written comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the footing excavations and backfill
operations be observed by a soils engineer during construction to see
that bearing has been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are
consistent with the finding of our investigation

approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
