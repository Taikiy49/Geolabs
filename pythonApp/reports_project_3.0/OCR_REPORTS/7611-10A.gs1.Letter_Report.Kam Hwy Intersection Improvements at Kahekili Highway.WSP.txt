Project Considerations

Kahekili Highway in Kahaluu on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. We understand that 
corrosivity testing per the current Board of Water Supply External Corrosion Standards 
(2021) is desired near two locations at the project site where the existing waterline 
crosses new drain lines.

Subsurface Conditions 
We explored the subsurface conditions at the project site by drilling and sampling 
two borings, designated as Boring Nos. 101 and 102, extending to a depth of about 
6.5 feet below the existing ground surface. The approximate boring locations are shown 
on the Site Plan, Plate 2. 
Our borings generally encountered alluvial deposits extending to the maximum 
depth explored of 6.5 feet below the existing ground surface. One of the borings 
encountered a 4-inch thick asphaltic concrete layer above the alluvium. The alluvium 
generally consisted of very stiff to stiff silty clay and clayey silt and loose to very loose 
silty/clayey gravel.  
We did not encounter groundwater in the drilled borings at the time of our field 
exploration. However, it should be noted that groundwater levels are subject to change 
due to rainfall, time of year, seasonal precipitation, surface water runoff, and other factors.

Corrosion Potential 
Laboratory corrosion tests, including pH, minimum resistivity, chloride content, 
sulfate content, and bicarbonate, were performed on selected samples obtained during 
our field exploration to evaluate the corrosivity of the near-surface soils at the project site. 
The test results are summarized and presented in Appendix B. Detailed results of the pH 
testing (EPA SW9045), Chloride Content (EPA 300.0), and Sulfate Content (EPA 300.0) 
performed by TestAmerica Laboratories, Inc. and Bicarbonate performed by Enthalpy 
Analytical are presented in Appendix C. 
Based on the updated version of the Board of Water Supply, City and County of 
Honolulu Water System External Corrosion Control Standards dated July 28, 2021, there 
are two soil corrosivity categories. Category A (Moderately to Severely Corrosive) applies 
to soil meeting any one of the following conditions. Corrosion Category B (Negligibly to 
Mildly Corrosive) is applicable when none of the following conditions are met. The 
updated version of the standards is effective as of the publishing date for Oahu only. 
• Resistivity ≤ 5,000 ohm-cm 
• pH ≤ 6.5 
• Water-soluble chloride concentration ≥ 500 ppm 
• Water-soluble sulfate concentration ≥ 1,000 ppm 
• Within fluctuating saltwater table 
Resistivity is generally recognized as one of the most significant soil characteristics 
regarding the corrosivity of the soil to buried metallic objects. In general, the lower the 
resistivity, the greater the potential for corrosion of the buried metallic structure. 
Conversely, the higher the resistivity, the less likely the soil will contribute to the corrosion 
of metallic objects. Results of the laboratory testing indicate that the on-site soils have  
resistivity values of about 1,600 and 2,400 ohm-cm with a pH value of 7.91. Therefore, 
the on-site near-surface soils may be considered as Corrosion Category A (Moderately 
to Severely Corrosive) based on the updated Board of Water Supply External Corrosion 
Control Standards dated July 28, 2021. 
Chloride content and sulfate content testing were performed by TestAmerica 
Laboratories, Inc. to evaluate the corrosivity of the on-site soils encountered. Based on 
the chloride and sulfate content tests performed on the on-site soils, the test values are 
about 12 and 26 parts per million (ppm) for chloride content and 34 and 100 ppm for 
sulfate content. In addition, bicarbonate testing was performed by Enthalpy Analytical with 
test values of 860 and 990 ppm.

Limitations 
The analyses and recommendations submitted herein are based, in part, upon 
information obtained from our field borings. Variations of subsoil conditions between and 
beyond the field borings may occur, and the nature and extent of these variations may 
not become evident until construction is underway. If variations then appear evident, it 
will be necessary to re-evaluate the recommendations provided herein. 
The field boring locations indicated in this report were located using the Conceptual 
Layout Plan transmitted by WSP on March 17, 2020. The elevations of the borings were 
estimated by using the same drawing referenced above. The locations and elevations of 
the field borings should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods 
used. 
The stratification breaks shown on the graphic representations of the borings 
depict the approximate boundaries between soil types and, as such, may denote a 
gradual transition. Water level data from the borings were measured at the times shown 
on the graphic representations and/or presented in the text of this report. This data has 
been reviewed and interpretations made in the formulation of this report. However, it 
should be noted that groundwater levels can fluctuate depending on surface water runoff, 
storm surge conditions, seasonal precipitation, perched groundwater, and other factors.
