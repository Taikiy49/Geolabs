SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is situated between Bravo Road and H Avenue
within the Naval Ammunition Depot which is located along the southern
shores of the West Loch branch of Pearl Harbor Hawaii
The topography at the site is relatively flat with a slight
gradient down toward the south
The area is presently overgrown with grass and haole koa Two
concrete slabs were noted within the site
The existing ground elevations vary from about 18 to 19 feet above

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project consists of construction of a one story guided missile
maintenance facility of reinforced concrete construction The facility
will be designed to withstand blast overpressure
Also included will be access roads and utilities Fills of up to 3
feet are anticipated at the building site
The general layout of the proposed construction is shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2

Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling
three 3 borings to depths of 15 feet below the existing ground surface
at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 Soil
samples obtained from the borings were selectively tested in our
laboratory to further examine their geotechnical engineering
characteristics Detailed descriptions of our field exploration and

Soil Conditions
The borings encountered a surface layer generally less than 2 feet
thick of stiff fill or alluvium over coral formation The thickness of
the surface layer was found to be about 8 feet thick at Boring No 2

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
In general our borings encountered competent silty subsoils and a
near surface coral formation at the site which can provide adequate
foundation support for the proposed one story structure and pavements
Foundation Design
Spread or continuous footing foundation bearing on the dense
in situ coral formation or stiff alluvium or compacted fill may be used
to support the proposed building
An allowable bearing value of up to 4000 p s f may be used on the
in situ dense coral material and the very stiff alluvium and up to 2500
p s f on the compacted fill The bearing value is for total dead plus
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1599 00 Page 5
live loads and may be increased by one third for temporary wind seismic
or blasting loads
The footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the
lowest adjacent grade
Any loose pockets encountered at the bottom of the footing
excavation should be recompacted to a minimum of 95 of its maximum dry
density established by ASTM D 1557 78 If a footing is situated adjacent
to a utility trench the trench backfill must be well compacted to
prevent potential footing settlement due to settlement of loose trench
backfill
Minimal footing settlements are anticipated for the design loads if
the above recommendations are followed
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive
earth pressure developed against the buried vertical faces of the
foundation system and by frictional resistance developed between the
bottom of the foundations and the bearing soil Resistance due to
passive earth pressure may be calculated using an equivalent fluid
pressure of 300 p s f This assumes that the soil around the foundation
GEOLABS HAWAII

is well compacted The frictional resistance may be calculated by using
a coefficient of friction of 0 4 between the foundation concrete and
coral formation The above resistance values are maximum values and do
not contain a factor of safety

Site Grading
Site Preparation
Prior to grading all deleterious materials such as vegetation
debris unsuitable fill old foundations and organically
contaminated soils should be removed and disposed of properly
off site Following the clearing the ground surface should be
scarified to a depth of 6 inches moisture conditioned to optimum
moisture content and recompacted to a minimum of 95 of its maximum
dry density as established by ASTM D 1557 78
Any soft weak or pumping areas disclosed during the recompaction
operations should be excavated to fim ground and backfilled with
select on site material or non expansive import fill material
compacted to the above specified density
Structural Fill Material and Placement
et IN A MN ho mS i ts
The on site coral sandy and gravel soils are suitable for
structural fill material Imported fill material should be
non expansive soil free of organic matter and form a dense stable
soil mass when compacted to the required density Generally the
particle size should be less than 3 inches in size The pavement
designs presented in this report were based on a subgrade CBR value
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1599 00 Page 8
of 15 and therefore the material placed within the top 3 feet of
the fill under the pavements should have a soaked CBR of at least
15 per ASTM D 1883 73 when compacted to 95 of the maximum
densities determined according to ASTM D 1557 78 The total
expansion of sample during the soaking for CBR test should be less
than 1
The fills should be placed in horizontal lifts not exceeding 8
inches in compacted thickness and compacted to a minimum of 95 of
its maximum dry density
Design Review
Plans and specifications for site grading foundation and pavement
design should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written
comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our recom
mendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the site clearing and grubbing fill
placement and compaction foundation excavation backfilling and subgrade
preparation be observed by the soils engineer during construction to see
that adequate compaction has been obtained and that the subsurface
conditions are consistent with the findings of our investigation
