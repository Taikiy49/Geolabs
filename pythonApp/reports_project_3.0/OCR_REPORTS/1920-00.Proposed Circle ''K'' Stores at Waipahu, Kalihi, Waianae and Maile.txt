In accordance wtih your request we have performed additional
probings at the above referenced projects in order to evaluate if the
previous underground storage tank excavation areas were properly
backfilled The exploration consisted of drilling two to three shallow
borings with close interval sampling at each of the previous underground
storage tank areas obtaining representative soil samples for visual
evaluation and the recording of blow counts for use to determine the
consistency of the backfill soils
Our work was performed in general accordance with our proposal of
SOIL CONDITIONS
The borings drilled at the locations of the previous underground
storage tank excavations primarily encountered medium dense to dense silty
gravels and silty sands with loose zones encountered locally The brown
silty gravel is probably an off site borrow

Detailed descriptions of the materials encountered in the borings
are shown on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 9 The approximate
locations of the borings are shown on four 4 Site Plans Plates 1
through Plate 4
SUMMARY OF FINDINGS
The borings encountered generally relatively competent subsurface
backfill soil conditions at the locations of the four 4 previous
underground storage tank excavations Localized loose zones were
encountered in some of the borings at the lower portions of the tank
backfill It is our opinion that they should not significantly impact the
proposed new A C pavements and lightly loaded structures
Based on the above findings and anticipated construction we believe
that the project sites can be developed as planned
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that subgrade preparations footing excavations
and backfill operations be observed by the soil engineer during
construction to see that adequate compactions have been obtained and that
the subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of our boring
exploration

made in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation engineering
principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all other

The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are based
on our site reconnaissance and soil information from boring and laboratory
test data obtained from this study
Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encountered and cannot be
fully determined by taking soil samples Unforeseen conditions such as
expansive soil and soft spots may occur in localized areas and require
additional probing or corrections in the field Therefore additional
expenditures may be needed during construction to attain a properly
constructed project Some contingency fund is thus recommended to
accommodate these possible costs
This report has been prepared in order to assist the
architect engineer in the evaluation of previous underground storage tank
backfill conditions at the above referenced sites
