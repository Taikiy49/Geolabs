As requested we provided geotechnical engineering services during the mass
grading operations at Kukuinui Subdivision Increments Il located in the Kalaoa area
in the District of North Kona Hawaii This letter report summarizes our geotechnical
engineering observation and consultation services performed The discussions presented
herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this letter report
Project Considerations
The Kukuinui Subdivision Increments II development site is located in the
Kalaoa area in the District of North Kona on the Island of Hawaii The project site
encompasses an area of about 10 2 acres The project consisted of constructing
34 residential lots for the Increments II portions of the project New roadways and
underground utility lines were also constructed for the Increment portion New roadways
for the Increment portion were graded to the finish subgrade elevations Installation of
underground utility lines for Increment Il portion is not included in this report
Approximately 23 780 cubic yards of cuts and 21 860 cubic yards of fills were required for
the Increments II earthwork operations
The contractor for the earthwork operations mass grading was
Willocks Construction Corporation Based on our records the earthwork operations for
the Kukuinui Subdivision were conducted between May 2004 and February 2005
Regional Geology
The Island of Hawaii the largest in the Hawaiian Archipelago covers an area of
approximately 4 000 square miles The island was formed by the activity of five shield
volcanoes which include the following Kohala which is long extinct Mauna Kea which
has had activity during recent geologic time Hualalai which last erupted in 1801 and
Mauna Loa and Kilauea both of which are still active

The project site is situated on the western flank of Hualalai Mountain which makes
up the west central portion of the island The lava formation encountered at the site
appears to be predominantly of pahoehoe type flows with some a a lava which spread
and ponded as they approached the ocean Ava lava is typically characterized by a
porous rough and irregular flow surface resembling a jagged accumulation of rock
fragments including cobbles and boulders Pahoehoe is characterized by a smooth
rope like or billowy surface and an internal structure of vesicular porous rock
Cavities are commonly encountered in basaltic lavas especially the pahoehoe type
lava These cavities are formed when the lava is still ina molten state and they represent
both lava tubes intra flow cavities and blisters and pockets inter flow cavities Lava
tubes are formed when molten lava drains from the cooling flow leaving a hollow tube like
structure which may extend for a large distance longitudinally along the flow Blisters and
pockets are generally smaller in horizontal extent
Basalt is considered to be a relatively permeable rock and can transmit water quite
readily in both the horizontal and vertical directions In general water is transmitted
through the porous rock matrix along joints fractures and inter flow contacts cavities and
along clinker layers The permeability of the clinker and cavities is high therefore they
serve as the primary water transmission features
Subsurface Conditions
As mentioned previously the Kukuinui Subdivision development site is located in
the Kalaoa area in the District of North Kona on the Island of Hawaii In general the
original topography of the site prior to site grading activities consisted of irregular
or hummocky lava flow terrain with frequent topographic depressions separated by
adjacent higher relief mounds and low ridge features
Based on observations during the mass grading operations the project site is
generally underlain by medium hard to hard basalt formation with interbedded layers of
clinker materials It should be noted that lava tubes cavities and blisters are commonly
encountered in basaltic lava flows in the project vicinity In addition numerous lava tube
features were observed at the site during the mass grading operations It should be
noted that near surface lava tubes detected within the limits of grading for the mass
grading operations generally were collapsed and the loose materials from within the
collapsed lava tube were removed The resulting excavation was backfilled with
compacted fill in general accordance with the recommendations outlined in the earthwork
specifications
Discussion and Recommendations
The project site was generally mass graded to a finished pad elevation ranging
from about 816 to 924 feet Mean Sea Level MSL Mass grading generally consisted

of up to about 6 to 8 feet of cuts and fills to achieve the design grades An As Built
Erosion Control and Grading Plan is provided on Plate 1
During the mass grading operations extensive subgrade preparation procedures
were employed within the grading limits at the commencement of the mass grading
operations to reduce the potential for collapse of near surface cavities and or voids within
the lots The intent of the subgrade preparation procedures is to assist in detecting and
collapsing near surface cavities and or voids contained in the porous near surface
materials It should be noted that the subgrade preparation procedures employed for this
project are consistent with the standard of practice for similar type projects and similar
subsurface conditions along the Kohala Coast on the Island of Hawaii
In general the subgrade preparation in fill areas consisted of proof rolling with a
sheepsfoot roller compactor minimum 20 tons for about eight passes to help detect and
collapse near surface cavities and or voids Fill materials consisting of some cobbles and
boulders and structural fills were placed in the fill areas Structural fills consisted of well
graded granular materials generally smaller than 6 inches in dimension In general the
fill materials within the top 2 feet of the lots within the pad areas consisted of structural
fill materials It should be noted that general fills consisting of cobbles and boulders of up
to about 2 feet in dimension generally were placed at least about 2 feet below the finished
grades within the pad areas in fill areas and choked off with finer sized materials In
addition boulders of up to about 3 to 5 feet in dimension were generally placed at least
about 4 feet below the finished grades within the pad areas and choked off with
finer sized materials Boulders larger than 18 inches in dimension were generally
placed about 6 feet or more horizontally from the face of the fill slopes
During the site grading and backfilling operations samples of the on site and
imported materials utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to
our Office for testing The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were
established in our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified
Proctor and the test results are summarized on Plate 2
In general the fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to above
optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum
dry density as determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557 In the roadway areas the
last lift of fill and backfill materials were generally compacted to a minimum of 95 percent
relative density
During the site grading operations periodic field density tests were performed in
general accordance with ASTM D 2922 and ASTM D 3017 Nuclear Gauge Method
The results of the compaction tests performed on the fills and subgrades are summarized
on Plates 3 1 and 3 2 of this report These tests were generally performed on the
prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills at the site A total of 31
field density tests including retests of failed areas were performed in support of the site
grading and utility installation

unsatisfactory were generally reworked until the specified minimum compaction was
obtained and or approved by our field representative based on visual observation and
hand probing
Subgrades in the cut areas were generally proof rolled with a large bulldozer
minimum 20 tons for about eight passes to help detect and collapse near surface cavities
and or voids The presence of native materials such as basait formation and a a basalt
should be anticipated at or near the existing pad elevation in the cut areas
Based on the observations of our field representatives it is our professional
opinion that the earthwork operations during the mass grading of Increment and Il
portions as shown on the As Built Erosion Control and Grading Plan Plate 1 observed
by Geolabs in the period covered in this report were performed in general conformance
with the intent of the project plans and specifications
It should be noted that the statement of professional opinion is based on the
grades existing at the completion of the mass grading phase in
assumes no responsibility for any alterations made to the slopes or finished grades on the
project subsequent to our observation and testing period without our knowledge and
written acknowledgment
In addition further study and exploration of the subsurface conditions at the
individual lots should be conducted by a qualified geotechnical engineer during the
design of planned structures to evaluate the subsurface conditions for design of the
proposed improvements on the individual lots This work would be better defined when
the footprint of the new structures proposed within the lot is known
Limitations
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork may
have been performed without our observation However the level of earthwork
observation and testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some of
the geotechnical risks to the project It should be recognized that there are variations in
the accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the
earthwork construction and materials Our services were performed in accordance with
generally accepted standards of geotechnical engineering practice no warranty is
expressed or implied Provision of construction monitoring by an engineer is not
insurance nor does it guarantee construction of any type The contractor shall retain
