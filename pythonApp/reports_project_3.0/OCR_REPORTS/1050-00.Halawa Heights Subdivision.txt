PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this soil engineering investigation was to
explore the subsurface soil conditions at the site in order
to provide data for use in the design of the site grading
for the proposed subdivision development
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests
which were used in the engineering analysis that formulated
our conclusions and recommendations are presented in the
appendix of this report
W O 1050 00 P 2
The approximate boring locations are shown on the Site
Plan Plate 1 and the boring logs are presented in
Appendix A Detailed information regarding the field
exploration and laboratory testing are also presented
in the appendices
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located on the southern side of Halawa
Heights Road approximately 140 to 150 feet east of the
intersection with Ulune Street
The lot is triangular in shape with a northern boundary
along Halawa Heights Road approximately 500 feet long
Existing residences are located in the adjacent areas
north of Halawa Heights Road
The eastern lot boundary is about 450 feet long There is
an existing drainage channel approximately 6 to 10 feet
deep along this eastern boundary The northern 350 feet
of the channel next to the site is unlined with steep
side slopes while the remaining portions have a concrete
lining The adjacent property next to the eastern boundary
is presently vacant

W O 1050 00 P 3
The western boundary is about 480 feet long and is bordered
by a 25 foot wide energy corridor easement and the H 1 Inter
state Highway
The ground surface at the site Slopes upward toward the
east with a gradient of about 5 1 H V The surface
elevation varies from about 75 feet at the south western
border to 105 feet along the eastern boundary
Numerous basalt boulders ranging in size from 1 to 6 feet
in diameter were noted throughout the site with two 2
larger mounds found in the middle and southern tip of the
property
Stockpiles of rubbish and debris were also noted around
the site
The site is presently covered with haole koa and grass
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed plan is to clear and grade the site for a
single family residential development consisting of 18
lots and one access road into the site
The preliminary grading plan indicates that the eastern
and western portions of the site will be filled from about
2 to 5 feet while the central portion will be cut about
2 to 4 feet

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Seven borings were drilled at the site to depths of 6 5
to 16 feet below the existing ground surface to explore
the subsurface conditions and to obtain samples for labora
tory testing
Soil Conditions
Based on the borings the site appears to be underlain by
a layer of fill generally about 6 feet to more than 16
feet deep The fill generally consists of stiff clayey
Silt with cobbles and gravel The borings indicate that
generally the fill is stiff and well compacted
Stiff residual soil consisting of reddish brown clayey
Silt with decomposed rock was encountered below the upper
fill
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings

Site Grading
There are piles of rubbish and boulders existing on
the site Due to the relatively small fills anti
cipated boulder disposal in the proposed new fill
is not recommended Therefore prior to filling
the site should be cleared of boulders vegetation
rubbish and other deleterious material
In areas to receive fill the ground should first
be proof rolled with a heavy roller to detect soft
Spots in the existing fill If soft spots are
encountered they should be over excavated down
to firm ground and recompacted The ground should
then be scarified to a minimum depth of 6 inches
brought to near optimum moisture content and then
compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry
density established by ASTM D 1557 70 test method
After the above site preparation the fill should be
moistened to above optimum moisture content placed
in 10 inch lifts and compacted to obtain a minimum
90 of its maximum dry density established by the
above ASTM test designation
GEOLABS HAWAIl
W O 1050 00 P 6
Laboratory soil tests indicate that the on site soils
have low swelling potential therefore the material
generally less than 8 inch size can be used for the
fill
It appears that additional off site borrow is required
The material should be tested and approved by the soils
engineer prior to transportation to the site to assure
that it is suitable for the proposed construction The
material should generally be non expansive and should
not contain large percentage of boulders organic or
deleterious matter
Slopes
Cut and fill slopes generally less than 10 feet in
vertical height are anticipated
Cut and fill slope ratios of 2 1 H V are recommended
Fill slopes should be constructed by overfilling then
cutting back to the design slope to obtain a well com
pacted slope face
Surface water should be diverted away from the top of
Slope and slope planting utilized as soon as practi
cable to reduce erosion

Utility Trenches
A relatively deep sewer line as much as 14 feet deep is
planned along the proposed access road and extending across
the drainage channel to connect to an existing sewer line
Stiff compacted soil should be encountered in the upper
portion of the trench while decomposed rock is antici
pated near the bottom of the deeper trenches
Proper backfilling of all the trenches is important to
minimize surface ground settlement along the utility align
ments On site material generally less than 6 inches in
size may be utilized as backfill All backfill should
be compacted to 90 of its maximum dry density as deter
mined by ASTM D 1557 Most of the on site material is
fine grained and would not be suitable for compaction
by water jetting or ponding and therefore this would
require mechanical compaction
House Foundations
For the light residential structures spread footing foun
dations or slab on grade may be used To minimize the
effects of slope creep and erosion slabs should be set
back a minimum distance of 5 feet away from the tops of
Slopes Also post footings located close to the tops
of slopes should be embedded a sufficient depth to obtain
a minimum horizontal set back distance of 5 feet from the
slope face

For post footings located 5 feet or more away from the
tops of slopes a minimum footing embedment of 12 inches

Lots 17 and 18
These lots are situated next to the unlined
portion of the drainage channel The existing
side slopes along the portion of the channel
are about 10 to 12 feet high and at about 1 1
H V slope ratio This Slope ratio is steeper

for fill slopes In order to minimize the
detrimental effects of down hill Slope creep
adjacent to this steep Slope the following
alternatives can be utilized
1 The existing 1 1 H V Slope can be flattened
to a 2 1 H V slope Then the foundation
recommendations presented above can be
utilized
2 The existing slope could be left as is and
the future house and structure foundations
set back a minimum distance of 10 feet away
from the top of slope

Slab on grade should be placed over a 4 inch layer of
compacted base course The thickened edge around the
slab on grade should extend a minimum of 12 inches below

The slab subgrade soils should be kept moist and not be

The lots should be graded to drain water away from the
house foundations
Pavement
Asphaltic concrete pavements are planned for the project
Based on the present laboratory CBR test results of the
material near the roadway area the preliminary pavement
design is as follows

Additional CBR tests should be performed on the actual
subgrade soil samples during construction and the above
design section can be revised if necessary The recommended
section considers medium traffic and that good drainage will
be provided for adjacent to paved areas

It is recommended that the soils engineer be present
during earthwork construction to monitor the contractor s
grading operations
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be
observed by the soil engineer during construction to

subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of
our subsurface investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive
tested and approved by the soil engineer prior to hauling
to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent
upon the above inspections

STANDARD GRADING SPECIFICATIONS

The work under this section includes clearing and grubbing
preparation of fill areas placement and compaction of fill
and removal and backfilling of underground structures These
specifications are of a general nature and may be superseded
by specific requirements or recommendations contained within
the soils report
I Clearing
All areas within contract limit lines shall be cleared of
trash debris organic matter and all other deleterious
materials These materials shall be removed and disposed
offsite
If Preparation of Fill Areas
A Loose surface soils shall be removed to expose compe
tent natural soils Localized soft pockets shall be
undercut and backfilled with compacted select mate
rials
B All surfaces shall be inspected and approved by the
soils engineer prior to the placement of any fill
C After approval the surface shal be scarified to a
depth of six inches brought to near optimum moisture
content and compacted as herein specified
Ill
IV
2
D Where the existing surface exceeds a slope of 5
horizontal to 1 vertical 5 1 the ground surface
shall be keyed and benched into competent soil to
bond the fill to the existing ground surface
E Subsurface drains may be required as directed by the
soils engineer where springs or zones of seepage are
encountered
Fill Materials
Material for fill shal consist of on site soils or
approved import material Import material shall be
tested and approved prior to transportation to the site
Fill material shall contain less than 5 percent organic
matter and other deleterious material and shall not
contain rocks or lumps in excess of eight inches 8
in diameter
Placement and Compaction of Fill
A The fill shall be placed in horizontal layers which
when compacted will not exceed ten inches 10 in
thickness Each layer shall be spread uniformly and
blade mixed to attain uniformity of material and
water content within each layer
GEOLABS HAWAII
3
Rock fragments or boulders less than eight inches
8 in diameter may be utilized provided they are
not concentrated in any one area and that there is
sufficient fine grained material to fill the voids
between the rocks
Rocks greater than eight inches 8 in diameter
shall be removed from the fill unless specifically
approved for placement in the fill by the soils
engineer
All fill shall be compacted to a minimum of 90
percent of the maximum dry density established by
ASTM Test Designation D 1557 unless otherwise
specified in the soils report
The compaction equipment may be sheepsfoot vibra
tory or pneumatic tire rollers suited to the soil
type and capable of producing the specified results
in an expedient and efficient manner The equipment
shall be in good working condition fully ballasted
and self cleaning
The compaction shall be accomplished in a systematic
and uniform manner over the entire fill area to pro
duce a uniformly compacted fill

G Fill material placed in an unsatisfactory condition
and not in accordance with these specifications
shall not be approved and shal be removed or reworked
until the required density is obtained
H The soils engineers shall be provided unrestricted
access to the fill to perform the compaction tests
V Removal and Backfilling of Underground Structures
Underground structures such as cesspools cisterns
septic tanks wells pipe lines etc shall be removed
as directed by the soils engineer Backfilling of the
excavations shall be in accordance with these specifi
cations
VI Seasonal Requirements
A No fill shall be placed during unfavorable weather
conditions When interruption of work is due to
heavy rain the previous fill surface shall be
approved before resumption of earthmoving operations
B The contractor shall maintain the surface of the fill
so that it will be free draining and will not pond
surface water
