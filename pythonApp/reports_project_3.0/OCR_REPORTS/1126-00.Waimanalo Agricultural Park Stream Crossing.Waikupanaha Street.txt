SUMMARY OF RECOMMENDATIONS
RE NE EY
A bridge structure was initially considered however due
to poor subsoil conditions encountered very long fric
tion piles in excess of 100 feet would be required to
Support the structure In addition long batter piles
required would create potential pile driving difficul
ties
Subsequently it was decided to use a box culvert struc
ture for the stream crossing A 3 foot thick granular
cushion layer is recommended below the box culvert to
create a working platform Anticipated settlements for
the culvert and subsequent fill are on the order of 2 to
6 inches total
Construction of the utilities and road pavement above
the culvert structure should be delayed until settlement
gauges indicate that most of the settlements have
occurred
The text of this report should be referred to for
detailed and special design recommendations
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this soil and foundation investigation was to
explore the subsurface soil and groundwater conditions at the
site in order to provide data for use in the design of the
foundations of the proposed box culvert structure

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located on Waikupanaha Street about 140
feet west of the intersection with Kumuhau Street in Waimanalo
An existing narrow bridge about 14 feet wide and 30 feet long
crosses over a small stream approximately 15 to 18 feet below
Running water and many boulders were noted in the stream
The ground adjacent to the bridge Slopes steeply toward the
stream and is heavily vegetated with tall grass and trees
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 1126 00 Page 3
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
A new box culvert structure is planned to replace the existing
narrow bridge as part of the widening and improvement of
Waikupanaha Street for the Waimanalo Agricultural Park
The new structure will be about 30 feet wide 44 feet long and
12 feet high Approximately 5 feet of fill is anticipated over
the box culvert
Retaining walls 114 to 129 feet long are planned on both sides
of the road next to the box culvert structure
SUBSURFACE EXPLORATION
The existing bridge is very narrow and would require closing of
the road to allow drilling on the existing road In addition
the heavy vegetation cover and Steep terrain made the remainder
of the site inaccessible for conventional truck mounted dril
ling equipment therefore hand carried portable drilling
equipment was required for the borings
Since the number of borings was limited by budget restraints
one boring was drilled at each end of the structure location
with the understanding that additional borings may be required
if unusual conditions were encountered
GEOLABS HAWAIl
W O 1126 00 Page 4
The borings were drilled to depths of 80 feet in order to
explore the subsurface conditions and to obtain soil samples
for laboratory testing
Detailed information on the field exploration and laboratory
testing is covered in the appendix along with the log of
borings
SOIL CONDITIONS
The two borings encountered 5 to 10 feet of soft organic clayey
silt marsh deposit below the stream level underlain by allu
vial deposits down to 60 to 80 foot depth The alluvium con
sists of alternating layers of silty sand and clayey silt
ranging in consistency from loose to medium stiff Residual
soil may have been encountered at 60 foot depth in Boring No
Cobbles and boulders were noted in the alluvial deposits
The water levels in the borings were generally encountered at
about the stream level However when the borings penetrated
to about 35 to 45 foot depth the water level rose to the top
of the hole indicating an artesian head of about 8 to 10 feet

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The borings encountered poor subsurface soil conditions at the
bridge abutment locations A 5 to 10 foot thick layer of soft
marshy soil was encountered below the stream level underlain
by loose to medium alluvial and residual soils to 80 feet the
depth of the borings Also some of the gravelly or sandy
alluvial layers appear to have an excess hydrostatic head ar
tesian condition Due to these soil and water conditions
special foundation design is required for the structure
Bridge Structure
Originally a bridge structure was considered for the stream
crossing Due to the poor subsoil condition and absence of a
hard end bearing layer long friction piles would be required
to support the bridge abutments Due to the soft soil condi
tions and new abutment fill required the piles would be
affected by downdrag negative skin friction caused by settle
ment of the soft layer Also the artesian uplift pressure
would reduce the weight of the upper soils and thereby reduce
its frictional resistance Based on the above it was antici
pated that the piles would have to extend down to about 90 feet
below the stream level in order to develop an allowable single
pile capacity of 30 tons Since these are friction piles the
Single pile capacity would have to be further reduced due to
the effects of group action of the cluster
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1126 00 Page 6
Therefore the piles would have to extend an additional 20 to
30 feet 110 to 120 foot pile length in order to maintain a
net 30 ton design pile capacity for all of the piles in the
group
High lateral forces were also anticipated on the bridge abut
ments due to the 19 foot height of soil that they would
retain The lateral pile capacity available was small due to
the low strength of the soft soils Therefore batter piles
would be required to resist the lateral load Due to the
length required the batter piles from both abutments would
intersect creating additional pile driving difficulties
Due to the aforementioned factors it was decided to utilize a
box culvert structure instead of a bridge to cross the stream
Box Culvert
The concrete box culvert structure would be about 30 feet wide
12 feet high with about 5 feet of fill above
The borings encountered about 5 to 10 feet of soft clayey silts
below the stream level In order to provide a working platform
and more uniform support a minimum 3 foot thick cushion layer
of granular material such as 3B Fine should be provided
under the culvert structure
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1126 00 Page 7
An allowable bearing value of 1500 p s f may be used for the
culvert structure bearing on the above granular layer
Based on the anticipated total load of 2100 kips and the
culvert structure bearing on a 3 foot thick granular cushion
fill layer anticipated settlements are on the order of 2 to 6
inches total We anticipate that over half of the settlements
would occur during construction of the culvert and backfill
If these residual settlements are not tolerable the thickness
of the granular cushion layer may have to be increased to
further reduce subsequent settlements
Our settlement estimates are based on the results of the two
borings drilled We recommend that prior to placement of the
granular cushion additional probes be made with a drill or
backhoe to more accurately determine the variation in thickness
of the soft layer and to verify if additional thickness of
granular material is necessary to reduce total and differential
settlements
We recommend that settlement gauges be installed as soon as
practicable above the culvert structure to monitor the settle
ment rates The construction of utilities the inlet structure
and road pavement should be delayed until the settlement gauges
indicate tolerable rates of settlements
GEOLABS HAWAII

Holes should be incorporated throughout the slab to help
release hydrostatic pressure build up under the slab
The culvert walls can be designed for lateral soil pressures as
follows
a Walls unrestrained at the top active condition
35 p c f equivalent fluid pressure
b Walls restrained at the top at rest condition
50 p c f equivalent fluid pressure
Additional loads due to traffic should be included
where applicable
Retaining Wall Foundation
Retaining walls are anticipated on both sides of the road next
to the box culvert
Soft soil is anticipated below the footings approximately at
the level of the stream Therefore where the footing level is
within 3 feet of the stream level a minimum 3 foot thick gran
ular layer 3B Fine is recommended below the wall footing to
provide uniform support and a higher contact bearing pressure
Some differential settlement of the walls should be anticipated
due to the varying amounts of backfill required and the varying
thickness and depth of the soft layer

Backfill
A 12 inch thick layer of 3B Fine gravel should be utilized
behind the box culvert and retaining walls to act as a drainage
layer
Well graded granular material such as select borrow should be
used within 10 feet behind the walls to reduce the compactive
effort required and to obtain proper compaction and subsequent
build up of additional lateral pressures against the walls
The backfill should be in maximum 12 inch thick lifts and
compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density per
ASTM D 1557 78
Road Pavement
The pavement section required depends on the type of material
utilized for the road fill If the material from the agricul
tural subdivision site is used the following preliminary pave
ment design is recommended
Z inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 inch Base Course
6 inch Select Borrow
14 inch Total Pavement Thickness Over
a Prepared Subgrade
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1126 00 Page 11
The base course should be crushed rock or other granular mater
ial with a CBR of at least 85 and conforming with Section 31 2
Standard Specification for Public Work Construction or equiva
lent Non expansive select borrow should have a minimum CBR
value of 25 and consist of crusher run mudrock coral sand or
cinder material Both the base course and select borrow should
be compacted to 95 of its maximum density
The select borrow layer can be omitted if the fill material
utilized can provide a CBR greater than 12 with an expansion
less than 1 0
Additional CBR tests should be performed on the actual subgrade
soil samples at the time of roadway construction and the above
design section can be revised accordingly The recommended
section considers medium traffic and that good drainage will be
provided adjacent to paved areas

Plans for site grading and foundations should be forwarded
to the soils engineer for review and written comments prior
to construction to see that the intent of our recommenda
tions is included in the project plans and specifications

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during
subgrade preparation particularly the installation of the
granular working platform the footing excavations and
backfill placement to see that adequate bearing has been
obtained and the subsurface conditions are consistent with
the findings of our investigation
Any imported material required should generally be non
expansive tested and approved by the soils engineer prior
to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon
the above inspections

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by
drilling 2 borings to 80 0 feet below the existing ground
surface at the approximate locations shown on the Boring
Location Plan Plate 1 Portable hand carried drilling
equipment was used to drill the borings
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by
visual and textural examination in the field by our engineer or
geologist who continuously monitored the drilling operations
The classifications were further reviewed by visual inspection
and testing in the laboratory The soils were classified in
general conformance with the Unified Soil Classification
System A graphic presentation of the soils encountered is
presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A l and A 6
Soil samples were obtained from the boring by driving
split barrel sampler with a 140 pound hammer free falling at a
height of 30 inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the
last 12 inches are shown on the logs of borings at the
appropriate sample depths

Laboratory Testing
Moisture content unit weight and Atterberg Limit
determinations were performed on selected samples as an aid in
the classification and evaluation of soil properties The
results of these tests are presented on the logs of borings
Plates A 1 thru A 6 at the appropriate sample depth

samples to evaluate the strength characteristics of the natural
soil The torvane shear values are presented on the logs of
borings at the appropriate sample depths

underlying alluvial soils to aid in the classification of the
soils The test results are presented on Plate B l
One consolidation test was performed on an undisturbed
sample of the soft subsoils to evaluate the settlement charac
teristics of these soils The consolidation curve is presented
on Plate B 2
