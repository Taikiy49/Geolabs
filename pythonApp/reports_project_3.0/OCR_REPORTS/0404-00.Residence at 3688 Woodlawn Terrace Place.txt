to determine the existing slope conditions below the residence

present slope conditions the drilling and sampling of two holes
and an engineering evaluation of the site

FIELD EXPLORATION
The field investigation consisted of drilling and sampling of
two holes supervised by a geologist from our firm In both
borings either Standard Penetration test or 2 5 inch O D ring
Samples were taken at intervals not exceeding 5 feet The
Standard Penetration test consists of driving a 2 inch O D
split spoon sampler 18 inches The number of blows required
to drive the sampler the last 12 inches is termed the Standard
Penetration Resistance N and is an approximate measure of
the relative density or consistency of a soil These resistance
values are plotted on the boring logs

CONCLUSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Periodic sliding is apparent beneath the structure probably
occurring during periods of heavy rainfall The materials
encountered in our subsurface exploration program were quite
wet and soft This condition promotes general downhill
creep of the soils on steep slopes Bowing of tree trunks
below the residence is visual evidence of active creep
Alternating wetting and drying of the clayey soils exposed
in the near vertical slide scarp beneath the house also
produces periodic spalling and popout of soil blocks which
Slowly decreases stability of the structure by removing
supporting soils
Due to the sliding and creep the downhill side of the
residence is presently in an unstable condition and requires
remedial action to improve support

To achieve full structural support the wooden column
supports beneath the view deck and downhill side of the
residence should be underpinned to underlying firm bedrock
by end bearing caissons The caissons should penetrate
firm bedrock a minimum of 3 feet Deeper penetration may
be required by the structural engineer The minimum
required caisson depth at the location indicated is approxi
mately 35 feet
The allowable bearing value for firm bedrock on the subject
property is 5000 pounds per square foot Because the onsite
fill and soil have been subject to continued sliding and
creep particularly during periods of heavy rainfall the
underpinning caissons should be designed to withstand a
lateral creep load of 1000 pounds per linear foot of pene
tration through all fill soil and badly weathered rock
For lateral design an allowable lateral bearing of 1000
pounds per square foot per foot of depth in the firm bedrock
and an allowable coefficient of friction of 0 8 may be used
All caisson excavations should be inspected and approved
by the soils engineer or geologist prior to placement of
concrete

The near vertical scarp beneath the structure should be
