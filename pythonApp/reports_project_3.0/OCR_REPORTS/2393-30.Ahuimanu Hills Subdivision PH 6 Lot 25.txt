GEOTECHNICAL ENGINEERING EXPLORATION
AHUIMANU HILLS SUBDIVISION
PHASE 6 LOT 25
In accordance with our proposal dated February 10th we have performed a
geotechnical engineering exploration at Lot No 25 of the Ahuimanu Hills Subdivision
Phase 6 development in the Kaneohe area on the island of Oahu Hawaii This letter
report summarizes the findings from our field exploration and laboratory testing and
presents our geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analyses of
the proposed project These recommendations are intended for design input only and are
subject to the limitations noted at the end of this letter report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located at Lot No 25 within the existing Ahuimanu Hills
Subdivision Phase 6 development in the Kaneohe area on the island of Oahu Hawaii
The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location
Map Plate 1 The project site measuring approximately 10 400 square feet in area is
presently vacant and is accessible through a concrete driveway Based on the
information provided we understand that it is proposed to construct a two story single
family residence at the subject lot
Based on the existing topography and the proposed finish floor elevation minimal
site grading work will be required to attain the design finished grades for the proposed
construction Our previous experience in the area suggests that the project site may be
underlain by surficial fill soils overlying alluvial deposits at greater depths Special
attention was given to the thickness bearing capacity anticipated settlements and
swelling potential of the near surface soils and their effects on the foundation design of
the proposed structure

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our field exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling three borings
designated as Boring Nos 1 through 3 to depths ranging from about 13 5 to 21 5 feet
below the existing ground surface The approximate locations of the borings are shown
on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general our field exploration encountered a surficial layer of fill soils consisting
of stiff to very stiff silty clays extending to depths of approximately 5 to 7 feet below the
existing ground surface This was generally underlain by alluvial deposits of medium stiff
to stiff silty clays extending to the maximum depth explored of 21 5 feet below the existing
ground surface In Boring Nos 1 and 2 soft organic clayey silts and loose silty gravel on
the order of about 2 to 5 feet thick were encountered between the upper fill soils and the
underlying alluvial deposits
Groundwater was encountered in the drilled borings at depths ranging from about
7 5 to 10 8 feet below the existing ground surface It should be noted that groundwater
levels are subject to change due to seasonal precipitation and other factors
Detailed descriptions of the materials encountered in our field exploration are
presented on the Logs of Borings Plates 3 through 5 Laboratory testing was performed
on selected samples obtained and the test results are presented on Plate 6
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Our field exploration at the subject site generally encountered stiff to very stiff fill
soils extending to depths of approximately 5 to 7 feet below the existing ground surface
Based on the relatively competent subsoil conditions encountered in our borings and the
anticipated light structural loads it is our opinion that shallow foundations bearing on the
lightly recompacted surface fill soils may be used for support of the proposed new
residence Our laboratory swell test results indicate that the near surface fill soils exhibit
slight to low swelling and shrinkage potential when subjected to moisture fluctuations
Therefore it is our opinion that concrete slabs may be constructed on grade provided that
the subgrade is prepared in accordance with the recommendations presented in the
Slabs On Grade section of this report Detailed discussion and recommendations are
presented in the following sections of this report

House Foundations
Based on the generally competent subsoil conditions encountered at the site we
believe that shallow spread and or continuous footings may be used for support of the
proposed new residence An allowable bearing pressure of up to 2 000 pounds per
square foot p s f may be used for the design of footing foundations bearing on the
recompacted near surface fill soils This bearing value is for dead plus live loads and
may be increased by one third for transient loads such as those caused by wind or
seismic forces
The bottom of the footing excavations should be lightly recompacted to provide a
relatively firm and smooth bearing surface prior to placement of reinforcing steel If soft
and or loose materials are encountered at the bottom of footing excavations they should
be over excavated to expose the underlying firm materials The over excavation should
be backfilled with select granular material compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction or the footing bottom may be extended down to the underlying competent
material
In order to utilize the surficial fill crust the bottom of footings should be embedded
a minimum of 18 inches and up to a maximum of 24 inches below the lowest adjacent
finish grades If foundations are located next to utility trenches or easements they should
be embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane extending
upward from the bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility
lines This requirement is necessary to avoid surcharging adjacent below grade
structures with additional structural loads and to reduce the potential for foundation
settlement
We estimate that footing settlements under the anticipated design loads for
footings bearing on the lightly recompacted near surface fill soils as recommended herein
to be less than 1 inch total with differential settlements on the order of inch
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive earth pressure
acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system and by frictional resistance
developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil Resistance due to
passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid pressure of
300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth p c f This assumes that the soil around
footings is relatively undisturbed or well compacted Unless covered by pavements or
slabs the passive resistance in the upper 12 inches of soil should be neglected A
coefficient of friction of 0 3 may be used for footings bearing on the recompacted
near surface fill soils We recommend that footing excavations be observed by a

Slabs On Grade
We anticipate that concrete slabs on grade will be used for the proposed new
residence Our laboratory tests indicate that the surficial fill soils exhibit slight to low
potential for swelling when subjected to moisture fluctuations To reduce the potential for
structural distress of the slabs on grade resulting from the swelling of the near surface fill
soils we recommend that the subgrade soils underneath the slab areas be moisture
conditioned to between 2 and 5 percent above the optimum moisture and recompacted
to a minimum of 85 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the
in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the
same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 91 test procedures Optimum
moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum
dry density
The slab subgrades should be kept moist prior to placement of concrete Where
shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of the subgrade we recommend that the
soils be thoroughly moistened to close all cracks Saturation and subsequent yielding of
the exposed subgrade due to inclement weather and poor drainage may require over
excavation of the soft areas and replacement with well compacted engineered fill
We recommend that a minimum 4 inch thick cushion layer consisting of No 3B
Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation or basalt termite barrier be placed below the
slab to provide more uniform support of the slab and to serve as a capillary moisture
break To reduce future moisture infiltration and subsequent damage to floor coverings
an impervious moisture vapor barrier such as a plastic membrane is recommended on
top of the gravel cushion layer Two inches of moist fine sand should be placed above
the plastic membrane to provide protection of the membrane and to aid in curing of the
slab concrete Flexible floor coverings such as carpet or sheet vinyl should be
considered since they can better mask minor slab cracking
Where the slab is subjected to vehicular traffic such as the garage slab and
driveway area a 6 inch layer of base course compacted to 95 percent relative
compaction is recommended in lieu of the No 3B Fine gravel cushion layer The 2 inch
moist fine sand and vapor barrier below the garage and driveway slabs may be omitted
Exterior concrete walkways may be required for the proposed project We
recommend that concrete walkways be supported on at least 6 inches of select borrow or
non expansive select granular fill material The select borrow or non expansive select

Site Preparation
We envision that minimal site grading work is anticipated for the project
construction In general the following guidelines should be used for site grading At the
on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be thoroughly
Cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable materials
should be removed and disposed of properly off site Soft and yielding areas
encountered during clearing and grubbing below any areas designated to receive fill
should be over excavated to expose firm natural material and the resulting excavation
should be backfilled with well compacted engineered fill The excavated soft and or
organic soils should be properly disposed of off site
After clearing and grubbing all areas at grade or to receive fill should be moisture
conditioned to between 2 and 5 percent above the optimum moisture and recompacted
to a minimum of 85 percent relative compaction Where shrinkage cracks are noted after
compaction of the subgrade we recommend that the soil be thoroughly moistened to
close all cracks Saturation and subsequent yielding of the exposed subgrade due to
inclement weather and poor drainage may require over excavation of the soft areas and
replacement with well compacted engineered fill Contract documents should include
additive and deductive unit prices for over excavation and engineered fill placement to
account for variations in the over excavation quantities
Imported fill materials if required should consist of non expansive granular
material such as crushed coral mudrock basalt or cinder sand The material should be
well graded from coarse to fine with no particles larger than 3 inches in largest dimension
and should contain between 10 and 30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve The
material should have a laboratory CBR value of 20 or more and should have a maximum
swell of less than 1 percent Imported fill material should be tested by Geolabs Hawaii for
conformance with these recommendations prior to delivery to the project site for its
intended use Select granular fill materials should be moisture conditioned to above the

tility Trench
We envision that new utility lines may be required for the project A granular
bedding consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation is
recommended under the pipes For pipes with inverts near the underlying soft loose
soils an additional 18 inches of No 3B Fine gravel wrapped in a filter fabric Mirafi 140N
or equivalent should be provided below the bedding layer for more uniform support of the
pipes
Free draining granular materials such as No 3B Fine gravel should also be used
for the trench backfill up to about 12 inches above the pipes to provide adequate support
around the pipes and to reduce the compaction effort of the backfill thus reducing the
possibility of damaging the pipes It is critical to use this free draining material to reduce
the potential of formation of voids below the haunches of pipes and to provide adequate
support for the sides of the pipes Improper trench backfill could result in settlement of
the backfill and damage to the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipes to
the top of the subgrade or finished grade should consist of on site soils excluding the soft
organic clayey silts or select fill material The backfill should be placed in maximum
8 inch level loose lifts and mechanically compacted to not less than 90 percent relative
compaction to reduce the potential for future ground subsidence Where trenches are
below pavement areas the upper 2 feet of the trench backfill below the pavement
subgrade should be compacted to 95 percent relative compaction
Drainage
The finished grades outside the new residence should be sloped to shed water
away from foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding Also it is advised that
gutter systems be installed around the building and that the discharge be diverted away
from the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near the
foundations and slabs should also be avoided Planters next to foundations should be
avoided or have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the subsoils

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that Geolabs Hawaii be retained to provide geotechnical
engineering services during the construction of the proposed residential project The
items of construction monitoring that are critical requiring Special Inspection include
observation of footing excavation subgrade preparation fill placement and compaction
Other aspects of earthwork construction should also be observed by a representative
from Geolabs Hawaii This is to observe compliance with the design concepts
specifications or recommendations and to expedite suggestions for design changes that
may be required in the event that subsurface conditions differ from those anticipated at
the time this report was prepared The recommendations provided in this report are
contingent upon such observations If actual exposed subsurface conditions encountered
during construction are different from those assumed or considered in this report then
appropriate modifications to the design should be made
