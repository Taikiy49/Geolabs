In accordance with our fee proposal dated May 31 2006 we conducted
geotechnical engineering services for the Drainage Improvements project on
Kaamilo Street in the Aiea area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii This report summarizes
our work on the project and presents our findings and geotechnical engineering
recommendations derived from our reconnaissance and analyses
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project involves repair and improvements to the existing concrete rubble
masonry CRM drainage ditch at Kaamilo Street in the Aiea area on the Island of
Oahu Hawaii We understand that the CRM drainage ditch has sustained considerable
damage during the last major storm Therefore it is planned to repair the damaged
areas and improve the entire CRM drainage ditch The existing CRM lining will be
removed and replaced with a concrete lining
Due to the very limited access for drilling equipment borings have not been
drilled for the design of the repairs and improvements A site reconnaissance was
conducted to determine the near subsurface conditions at the site
REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from the Waianae
and Koolau shield volcanoes The older Waianae Volcano is estimated to be middle to
late Pliocene in age and forms the bulk of the western one third of the island The
younger Koolau Volcano is estimated to be late Pliocene to early Pleistocene Ice Age
in age and forms the majority of the eastern two thirds of the island The
Koolau Volcano reached the end of its main shield building phase about 2 million years
ago

The project site is on the southeastern flank of the Koolau Range and is
composed of basaltic rock built by the extrusion of lava from the Koolau Volcanic
Series These rocks are generally characterized by flows of jointed dense vesicular
basalt interbedded with thin clinker layers The lava flows have weathered in place and
are characterized by the red colors of the soil The brown soils are generally ancient
alluvium deposited over the surface
SITE RECONAISSANCE
The project site is on Kaamilo Street in the Aiea area on the Island of Oahu
Hawaii as shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 We conducted a site
reconnaissance on We observed two drainage ditches on
Kaamilo Street The eastern drainage ditch is a concrete lined ditch and the western
drainage ditch is a CRM lined drainage ditch The drainage ditches merge into a single
concrete lined drainage ditch about 230 feet downstream towards the south We
observed two considerably large damaged areas at the CRM lined drainage ditch The
location of the CRM lined drainage ditch is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The first damaged area is where the slope of the drainage ditch transitions from
a gentle to a very steep inclination about 110 feet from the south edge of Kaamilo
Street The lining of the drainage ditch and portions of the soils below the lining has
eroded The soils below the drainage ditch appear to be reddish brown clayey silt in a
very moist condition The consistency of the soils ranged between medium stiff to stiff
Soft areas possibly from runoff sediment were observed in localized areas The soil
eroded down to depths ranging from about 2 to 8 below the CRM lining In general the
sides are near vertical The west sidewall of the damaged area has an overhang due to
the bottom portion of the sidewalls having been eroded The west side has eroded
about 4 feet inwards A portion of a concrete jacketed pipe was exposed at the north
side of the damaged area
The second damaged area is about 70 feet down stream of the first damaged
area This area extends down to the merge with concrete lined drainage ditch The
lining of the drainage ditch and portions of the soils below the lining has eroded The
soils below the drainage ditch appear to be basaltic boulders and cobbles in a reddish
brown clayey silt matrix The east sidewall of the damaged area has an overhang
because the bottom portion of the sidewall has eroded We observed large amounts of
debris consisting of boulders wood pieces grass and tree roots within the second
damaged area

Drainage Ditch Lining
The sidewalls of the drainage ditch should be well drained to reduce the build up
of hydrostatic pressures A typical drainage system would consist of a 12 inch wide
zone of permeable material such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67
gradation placed directly around a perforated pipe perforations facing down at the
base of the wall discharging to an appropriate outlet or weepholes As an alternative a
prefabricated drainage product such as MiraDrain or EnkaDrain may be used instead
of the drainage material
We understand that the existing CRM drainage ditch lining will be removed and
replaced with a new concrete lining Based on the anticipated subsurface conditions
that may be encountered along the drainage ditch we recommend providing a bedding
layer below the concrete invert slab The bedding layer should consist of a minimum
6 inch thick layer of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation
The damaged areas should be backfilled with well compacted fill materials The
well compacted fill materials should be placed up to about 24 inches below the
drainage ditch subgrade Controlled low strength material CLSM should be used to fill
the area from the top of the well compacted fills up to the drainage ditch subgrade
bottom of the bedding layer
Since the drainage ditch will be lined with new concrete we recommend using a
slope inclination of up to one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V or flatter for the drainage
ditch sides The factor of safety for overall slope stability of the drainage ditch side
slopes in the long term condition is at least 1 5 which is the minimum factor of safety
normally recommended for long term conditions
Site Grading
Based on the available information the majority of the drainage ditch will be
constructed near the existing grade or in a cut condition The damaged areas will
require fills on the order of about 2 to 7 feet high The CRM lining within the contract
grading limits should be demolished and removed at the on set of earthwork
Deleterious material and other unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of
properly off site Soft and yielding areas encountered during clearing should be
over excavated to expose firm natural material The excavated soft and or organic soils
should be disposed properly off site
The finished subgrades and areas designated to receive fills should be scarified
to a minimum depth of 8 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the
optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as
a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance

with ASTM D 1557 Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight

Where shrinkage cracks are observed after compaction of the subgrade we
recommend preparing the subgrade again as recommended Saturation and
subsequent yielding of the exposed subgrade due to inclement weather and poor
drainage may require over excavation of the soft areas and replacement with
well compacted fill A Geolabs representative in the field should evaluate the need for
over excavation
General fill materials required to raise the existing ground surface to the
proposed finished subgrades may consist of the excavated on site soils provided that
they are free of vegetation deleterious materials and rock fragment greater than
3 inches in maximum dimension Imported general fill materials should consist of
materials with particle sizes of 3 inches or less in maximum dimension and should be
free of deleterious materials The material should have a laboratory CBR value of 12 or
higher and a swell potential of no more than 1 percent when tested in accordance with
ASTM D 1883 Geolabs should test the imported fill materials for conformance with
these recommendations prior to delivery to the project site for the intended use
General fill materials should be moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above
the optimum moisture placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness
and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Compaction should be
accomplished by sheepsfoot rollers vibratory rollers multiple wheel pneumatic tired
rollers or other types of acceptable compaction equipment Water tamping jetting or
ponding should not be allowed to compact the fill material for this project
Since site grading operations will occur in an existing drainage ditch there may
be surface water runoff within the drainage ditch during the site grading operations The
presence of surface water runoff within the existing drainage ditch may result in some
difficulties during compaction of the general fill material Due to the potential difficulties
that may occur when surface water runoff is present within the drainage ditch a
self compacting material such as CLSM may be used as an alternative to placing and
compacting general fill material
It is important that our office representative monitor site grading operations to
observe whether undesirable materials are encountered during the site preparation
operations and to confirm whether the exposed soil rock conditions are similar to those
encountered in our field exploration
Cut Slopes
Based on the subsurface conditions anticipated along the drainage ditch we
believe that cut slopes planned adjacent to the drainage ditch may be designed with a
slope inclination of 2H 1V or flatter

Excavation
Based on our reconnaissance we observed medium stiff to stiff clayey silt with
some basaltic boulders at the site It is anticipated that the near surface materials may
be readily excavated with normal heavy excavation equipment such as backhoes
However the excavations may encounter basaltic boulders which may require the use
of hoerams or chipping
The above discussions regarding the rippability of the surface materials are
based on our visual observation at the site Contractors should be encouraged to
examine the site conditions to make their own interpretation
Design Review
Final drawings and specifications for the proposed construction of the drainage
improvements should be forwarded to Geolabs for review and written comments prior to
bid advertisement This review is necessary to evaluate conformance of the plans and
specifications with the intent of the dredging recommendations provided herein If this
review is not made Geolabs cannot be responsible for misinterpretation of our
recommendations
Construction Monitoring
Geolabs should be retained to provide geotechnical engineering services during
construction This is to observe compliance with the intent of the design concepts
specifications and or recommendations and to expedite suggestions for design
changes that may be required in the event that subsurface conditions differ from those
anticipated at the time this report was prepared The recommendations provided herein
are contingent upon such observations
If actual exposed subsurface conditions encountered during construction differ
from those assumed or considered in this report Geolabs should be contacted to
review and or revise the geotechnical engineering recommendations presented herein
LIMITATIONS
The findings and recommendations submitted herein are based upon visual
observations made during our site visit Variations of subsurface conditions may occur
and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident until construction
is underway If variations then appear evident Geolabs Inc should be contacted to
re evaluate the recommendations presented herein
