At your request and as required by the State of Hawaii Department of
Transportation Highway Division Geolabs Inc provided geotechnical engineering
observation and consultation services during the drilled shaft foundation installation for the
above project This letter report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations
during the installation of the drilled shaft foundations for the North South Road Phase 1C
project located in the Ewa area on the island of Oahu Hawaii
Geolabs conducted a geotechnical engineering exploration for the North South
Road Phase 1C project and our recommendations are presented in the above report
The purpose of our geotechnical engineering observation during construction was to
provide engineering consultation and assist in problem resolutions during the construction
of the drilled shaft foundation In addition we provided Special Inspection services by
observing the drilled shaft foundation installation for general conformance with the project
plans and specifications
Project Considerations
The new North South Road project will involve construction of new roadways
between the proposed Kapolei Parkway to the south and the Interstate Route H 1
Highway to the north The Phase 1C portion of the project consists of constructing the
roadway in the inbound and outbound directions between North South Road

The new Interchange Bridge structures will be of concrete construction and will
consist of two parallel bridge structures for each direction of traffic on Interstate Route H 1
The single span bridge structures will be about 60 feet wide and about 165 feet long and
will be supported by abutment structures at each end of the bridge span A deep
foundation system consisting of 60 inch diameter cast in place concrete drilled shafts were
utilized for foundation support of the bridge structures
The scope of our work discussed in this letter report includes observation of the
drilled shaft foundation construction for the two bridge structures mentioned above only
The general contractor for the project is Kiewit Pacific and the drilled shaft installation
subcontractor is CMZ Hawaii
Subsurface Conditions
Our borings drilled near the new bridge structures generally encountered a surface
fill layer ranging from about 20 to 30 5 feet thick The fill layer generally consisted of
medium dense to very dense sandy gravel clayey silty sands with cobbles and boulders
and medium stiff to very hard silty clays with gravel and boulders The fill layer was
underlain by an alluvial deposit extending from about 20 to 91 feet deep The alluvial
deposit generally consisted of hard to very hard cobbles and boulders medium dense to
dense silty sands medium stiff to very hard clays and silts with gravel cobbles and
boulders and very stiff to very hard sandy silt Below the alluvial deposit very hard
residual soils weathered basalt and hard basalt formation extended to the maximum
explored depth of about 111 feet below the existing ground surface
We did not encounter groundwater in the borings at the time of our field exploration
However it should be noted that groundwater levels also may vary with seasonal rainfall
time of the year and other factors
Summary of Drilled Shaft Installation
To examine construction methods proposed by the contractor and to verify the
bearing stratigraphy of the drilled shaft foundations a test program consisting of installing
one trial shaft and installing testing two instrumented load test shafts were performed prior
to the production shaft installation Following completion of the load tests the drilled shaft
lengths for the bridge structure were revised based on the results of the instrumented load
tests
The following subsections provide descriptions of the trial shaft and load test shafts
construction and discussions on the production drilled shaft installation

Trial Shaft
A non reinforced 60 inch diameter Trial Shaft was installed at the designated
location shown on the Drilled Shaft Identification Plan Plate 2 The trial shaft was
excavated by the drilled shaft contractor from March 4 2008 to March 7 2008 The drilled
shaft contractor placed the concrete for the trial shaft on March 7 2008
The Trial Shaft was excavated using a drill rig equipped with 60 inch
diameter auger core barrel and clean out bucket The drilled shaft contractor used a
64 inch O D temporary steel casing installed to a depth of about 11 feet below the ground
surface The total length of the trial shaft was 95 feet A log of the trial shaft and the
materials encountered during shaft excavation are presented in the attached Drilled Shaft
Records After completion of the shaft excavation the drilled hole was backfilled with the
specified concrete using tremie method The temporary steel casing was retrieved prior to
completion of the concrete pour
Based on the results of the trial shaft installation the drilled shaft contractor
generally demonstrated that the proposed excavation methods and equipment were
acceptable for installing the specified drilled shaft foundations in general accordance with
the project specifications
Load Test Shafts
As part of the project specifications two dedicated 60 inch diameter Load Test
Shafts were installed at the project site after the completion of the Trial Shaft The Load
Test Shafts LT 1 and LT 2 were drilled at center median of the existing Interstate
Route H 1 Highway near the west abutments and outside the guardrail of the Interstate
Route H 1 Highway inbound lanes near the east abutments respectively The test shaft
locations were selected to be close to the future abutments in order to provide maximum
amount of information to refine the design of the production shafts The actual load test
shaft locations are shown on the Drilled Shaft Identification Plan Plate 2
The first Load Test Shaft LT 1 was excavated by the drilled shaft contractor from
March 16 2008 to March 24 2008 This load test shaft extended to a depth of about
63 2 feet below the existing ground surface The second Load Test Shaft LT 2 was
excavated from March 25 2008 to and extended to about 140 8 feet below
the existing ground surface Prior to the concreting of each Load Test Shaft the drilled
shaft contractor lowered the reinforcing cage with an Osterberg Load Cell and embedment
strain gage instrumentation into the drilled hole Concrete was placed in the Load Test
Shafts LT 1 and LT 2 on March 24 2008 and respectively
Due to the presence of cobbles and boulders at relatively shallow depth temporary
casings were installed for both load test shafts Temporary casing lengths of about 13 3
feet and 11 feet were used for LT 1 and LT 2 respectively A special adaptor was used
by the drill shaft contractor to twist the casing into the predrilled hole to the required depth
Logs of the materials encountered during load test shaft excavation and details of the load
test shaft construction are presented on the attached Drilled Shaft Records

After the load test shafts were installed and cured between 8 and 10 days load
tests were performed by Load Test Inc The load test shafts were loaded to maximum cell
pressure or until the load test shaft failed Complete details of the load test program are
presented in the reports prepared by Load Test Inc entitled Report On Drilled Shaft Load
Testing Osterberg Method LT 1 North South Road Kapolei HI dated
and Report On Drilled Shaft Load Testing Osterberg Method LT 2 North South Road
Kapolei HI dated
Based on the results of the load tests we recommend that the production shaft
lengths be reduced about 10 feet from the initially specified shaft length for all the bridge
abutment shafts except for the South Bridge Abutment 2 For the shafts at South Bridge
Abutment 2 location the shaft length was reduced about 13 feet from 95 feet to 82 feet
Production Drilled Shafts
The drilled shaft contractor installed 16 production shafts from through
The as built diameters and the lengths of the production drilled shafts
installed for the project are summarized on Plate 3 of this letter report Detailed installation
logs for the production shafts are attached to this letter report Representatives from
Geolabs observed the drilled shaft excavation placement of reinforcing cage and
concrete operations during the installation of the production drilled shafts
In general the production drilled shafts were excavated using a drill
rig equipped with 60 inch diameter auger core barrel and clean out bucket similar to the
trial and load test shafts Because groundwater was not encountered during the drilling
process no drilling slurry was utilized for the production shafts However temporary
casing was used during excavation of the production drilled shafts to avoid the potential
cave in condition of the cobbles and boulders near the ground surface and also to provide
a safer working environment After excavation of the production shafts to their designated
tip elevations the reinforcing cages with prefabricated cross hole sonic logging CSL
testing tubes were lowered into the drilled holes to the appropriate elevations using a FMC
Link Belt crane After the reinforcing cage was installed in the drilled hole concrete was
placed and pumped through a 4 inch I D steel tremie pipe starting from near the bottom of
the excavation During concrete placement we observed that at least 5 feet of concrete
head was maintained above the bottom of the tremie pipe
The concrete for the production shaft was supplied by Island Ready Mix
Concrete Inc A special design concrete mix was utilized for the production shafts which
maintains a high slump 7 to 9 inches throughout the concrete pour It should be noted
that the Hawaii Department of Transportation monitored the concrete used for the drilled
shafts including the performance of slump tests air content tests and compression tests
Logs of the individual drilled shafts installed and the materials encountered in each of the
drilled shafts during shaft excavation are presented on the attached Drilled Shaft Records
Based on the observations of our field representatives it is our professional opinion
that the production drilled shafts at the North South Road Phase 1C Project were installed
