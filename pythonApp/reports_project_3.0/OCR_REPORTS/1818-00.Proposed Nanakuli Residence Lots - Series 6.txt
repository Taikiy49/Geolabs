INTRODUCTION
The purpose of our exploration was to explore the subsurface soil
conditions at the site in order to provide pertinent soil data to aid in
the site grading slab on grade and house foundation designs
The scope of our investigation included drilling and sampling four
4 borings to depths of 11 to 14 feet below the existing ground
surface Also included were laboratory testing of selected samples
obtained engineering analysis and evaluation of the field and laboratory
data development of the preliminary design recommendations and report
preparation

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed development includes 38 single family residential lots
and approximately 1400 L F of access road Relatively small 2 to 6
feet cuts and fills are anticipated for the proposed site grading
At the present time the type and exact locations of structure are
unavailable
The general location of the access road and the residential lots are
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located in Nanakuli Valley along Nanaikala
Street and between Haleakala and Nanakuli Avenues The site is about 300
feet northwest of Nanakuli Steam and flanked by single family residences
on the mauka side and Nanakuli High School on the makai side
The ground elevations at this site vary from about 216 to 176 feet
above Mean Sea Level The existing ground surface generally slopes down
toward the south
The site is partially covered with grass and surface boulders up to
about 3 feet in diameter Two to 8 feet high fill mounds were noted in
several locations within the site

SUBSURFACE CONDTTIONS
The soil conditions at the site were explored by drilling four
borings 11 to 14 feet deep below the existing ground surface at the
approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The borings generally encountered a few inches to about 8 feet of
dark grayish brown to reddish brown stiff to very stiff clay adobe
over basalt cobbles and boulders that extend to the bottom of most
borings up to about 14 feet deep
At the Haleakala Avenue northern end of the site B 4 soft to
medium stiff gravelly adobe clay was encountered behind the existing
slope to depth of about 7 feet below the existing ground surface The
soft material may be previous fill generated by past road construction
adjacent to the site
DISCUSSTON AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed a surface layer of highly expansive
adobe clay underlain by dense bouldery alluvium at the proposed
residential development site Since only minor cuts and fills 6 feet or
less are planned and because light structure loads are anticipated the
main concern in designing the on grade slabs and building footing
foundations would be to adopt special procedures in order to reduce the
shrink swell effect of the subsoils
We suggest that wood framed post and beam structures should he
considered These would reduce the need for on grade slabs and
therefore the amount of over excavation and replacement with
non expansive borrow that are recommended to reduce the shrink swell
effect of the subsoil

Foundation Support
Spread footing foundation may be used for the proposed houses
provided special procedures are utilized to reduce the shrinkage swelling
effects of the clay soils
The adobe clay soils below the foundations have high volume change
potential and can shrink when dried or heave considerably if saturated in
the future Therefore we recommend that the expansive clays below the
foundations should either be removed or over excavated down to a minimum
depth of 3 feet below the bottom of the footings The over excavation
should also extend a minimum of 6 inches beyond the edge of the footings
The bottom 2 feet of the over excavation should be backfilled with
non expansive borrow materials such as select borrow crusher waste or
coral placed in horizontal lifts not exceeding 6 inches thick and
compacted to a minimm of 90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM
D 1557 78 The top 1 foot should be backfilled with impervious
non expansive soil compacted to the above density refer to Plate 3
Based on the anticipated soil conditions site grading and design
loading we believe that an allowable bearing capacity of 2500 p s f may
be used for spread footing bearing on compacted structural fill
The footing should be placed a minimm of 8 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grade
The use of individual spread footing is recommended since this may
allow special provisions to be incorporated under the colum for
periodic adjustment of the structural colums as the footings may settle
because of excessive shrinkage of the adobe clay upon drying of the
subsoils

Slab on Grade
The expansive on site clay soil under on grade slabs areas should be
over excavated to a depth of at least 3 feet below the slab bottom and
replaced with non expansive borrow soils in order to reduce the effects
of soil heaving on the slabs see Plate 4
Similar over excavation and replacement of the expansive clay soils
are recommended for driveways walkways and other slabs if required
To reduce future post construction swelling of the underlying
expansive soils we recommend that the on site soils below the
over excavation level be kept continuously moist prior to concrete
placement
A 4 inch layer of 3B Fine gravel should be provided below the
slab In order to protect against moisture infiltration an impervious
moisture barrier may be incorporated below the slab
Site Grading
In general the site grading at the proposed residential development
site will mainly consist of cutting the existing ground up to about 6
feet at the northern corner of the site and generally minor cuts and
fills 2 to 4 feet for the remaining areas of the site
Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limit should be thoroughly cleared Any unsuitable materials such
as previous fill rubbish soft material and vegetation should be
removed and disposed of properly off site
Loose spots encountered at subgrade level should be removed and
replaced with imported fill material compacted to a minimum of 90
of its ASTM D 1557 78 maximum dry density

Excavation
The field exploration indicates that the project site is underlain
at fairly shallow depths by hard basalt cobbles and boulders
Moderately hard ripping is anticipated in the boulder formation
Drainage
The finished grade outside the slabs and footings should be shaped
to shed water away from the foundations and to avoid ponding condition
We also suggest that gutter systems be installed around the
structures and that gutter water be diverted far away from the
foundations and walkways including the existing structures Excessive
landscape watering near the foundations and slabs should also be avoided
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper preformance
of the above mentioned foundations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of
strength

To prevent ponding condition the foundation excavations should be
properly backfilled tight against the footing immediately after setting
of the concrete
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained
to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundation
Access Road Pavement
Based on our field exploration and previous experience the
following preliminary access road pavement section is recommended for
adobe clay subgrade condition
2 0 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 0 Inch Base Course 95 Compaction
18 0 Inch Select Borrow
26 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
The select borrow may be eliminated if the top 2 feet below the
subgrade is fairly non expansive bouldery material
Additional CBR tests and or visual observations should be performed
on the actual subgrade soil samples during construction and the above
design section can be revised if necessary The recommended section
considers light traffic and that good drainage will be provided adjacent
to paved areas

Plans and specifications for site grading foundation and pavement
design should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written
comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendation is included

Construction Monitoring
It is recommend that the site grading and footing excavation he
observed by the soils engineer during construction to see that adequate
compaction has been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are
consistent with the findings of our investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site for use
If conditions encountered are different from those presented in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations
made in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation
engineering principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all
other warranties either expressed or implied
The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are based
on our site reconnaissance soil information from boring and laboratory
test data obtained for this study
Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encountered and cannot be
full determined by taking soils samples Unforeseen conditions such as
expansive soil and soft spots may occur in localized areas and require
additional probing or corrections in the field Therefore additional
expenditures may be needed during construction to attain a properly
constructed project Some contingency fund is thus recommended to
accommodate these possible costs
This report has been prepared in order to assist the engineer in the
preparation of site grading plans and foundation design of this project
