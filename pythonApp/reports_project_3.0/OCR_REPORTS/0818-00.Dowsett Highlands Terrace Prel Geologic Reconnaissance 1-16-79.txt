As requested we have conducted a preliminary geologic
reconnaissance at the above mentioned project site The
purpose of the reconnaissance was to observe the visible
surface features at the site to evaluate the proposed
development plans and delineate areas for more detailed
exploration
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located on the slope break at the base
of the Koolau Mountain Range in Nuuanu Valley Oahu Hawaii
W P 2
The site is bounded on the southeast by a spur of the Koolau
Range flanking Nuuanu Valley The north east and western
portions of the site are bounded by housing developments
similar to the proposed development
The site is generally very hilly with topographic relief of
over 260 feet Surveyed elevations range from about 480 to
740 feet The project site consists mainly of two moderately
sloping ridges Areas I II Plate 1 two steep about 450
to near vertical in places valleys about 30 and 50 feet
deep and a rugged and generally steep sided about 45 to
vertical rocky highland on the northeast The crests of
the ridges appear to have been graded and are unvegetated
or covered with dense stands of bamboo and Ohia fern The
valleys are heavily vegetated with trees and shrubs The
rocky highland is covered by pines eucalyptus and other
trees
Drainage from the site is generally eastward by a small
perennial stream which is subject to substantial flows
during heavy rains
Access to the site is primarily through Puu Paka Street
with more restricted access through Kamaaina Place
There are no known existing structures on the site

GEQLOGY
The topographically lower portions of the site are formed
by rounded ridges and valleys cut into weathered Older
Alluvium alluvium and colluvium at the base of the
Koolau Mountain Range
These small ridges are erosional remnants of extensive
deposits of mainly colluvial debris slope wash eroded
from the leeward flank of the Koolau Range Valleys
cut by stream erosion are filled mainly with boulders
and some sand and gravel in their lower reaches Recent
alluvium and colluvium consisting of clayey silts with
boulders are found on the ridges and slopes
Some organic material can be found in the lower portions
of the valleys
SITE DEVELOPMENT
From our site reconnaissance the project site has been
classified into four areas for discussion as shown on the
Site Plan Plate 1
It appears that this area can be developed by conventional
grading methods

Stiff subsoils are anticipated in this area however set
backs from the top of the drainageway slope will be required
due to the steepness and height of the existing slope De
tailed recommendations will be made after additional field
exploration is completed and a grading plan developed
Drainageways
The two drainageways will present special development problems
Filling the drainageways will require very high fills up to
50 to 60 feet Soft organic material in the bottom of the
drainageways will settle due to new fill loading or it
will have to be removed prior to filling Special provisions
will also be required to control the surface and subsurface
water conditions Development in the drainageways will be
difficult and should be done with extreme care
Rocky Highlands
The rocky highlands at the head of the large drainageway
consists mostly of fresh very hard basalt The very
steep topography here restricts access to this area
and the hard rock will probably require blasting for
development

Area II

Area I A heavy growth of vegetation limited surface infor
