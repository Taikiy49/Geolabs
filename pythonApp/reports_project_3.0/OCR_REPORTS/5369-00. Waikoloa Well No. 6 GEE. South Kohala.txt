SECTION 1 0 GENERAL
1 1 Introduction
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed access road and control building at the Waikoloa Well No 6
site located in Waikoloa in the District of South Kohala on the Island of Hawaii The
general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map
Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings from our field exploration and presents our
geotechnical recommendations derived from our analyses for the above project only
These recommendations are intended for site grading gravel road section and design
of foundations only The findings and recommendations presented herein are subject to
the limitations noted at the end of this report
1 2 Project Considerations
The project site is located in the vicinity of Well Nos 2 and 3 in the Waikoloa
area within the District of South Kohala on the Island of Hawaii The project consists of
constructing a gravel access road from Well No 2 to Well No 6 A control building will
also be constructed in the vicinity of Waikoloa Well No 6 site The Well No 6 site is
located approximately 1 300 feet above Mean Sea Level MSL
Based on a topographic survey map provided the existing grades at the project
site vary from about 1 200 to 1 300 feet MSL within the limits of grading In general
we envision that site grading will consist of minor cuts and fills to attain the desired
finished grades
1 3 Purpose and Scope
The purpose of our geotechnical engineering exploration was to obtain an
overview of the surface and subsurface conditions at the project site to develop a soil
and or rock data set for the formulation of geotechnical recommendations pertaining to
the design of the project To accomplish this we conducted a field exploration program
consisting of the following tasks and work efforts

2 1 Regional Geology
The Island of Hawaii was built by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from five shield
volcanoes Kohala Mauna Kea and Kilauea The oldest volcano
Kohala is estimated to be middle Pleistocene in age and forms the northern lobe of the
island The next younger shield Mauna Kea is estimated to be Pleistocene in age and
shows evidence of glaciation during the Ice Age The remaining shields have been
active in recent history
The site is situated in a saddle between Kohala and Mauna Kea where
Mauna Kea lava flows banked against the older Kohala rocks Based on available
geological information and geomorphological interpretation the site is located on Mauna
Kea rocks near the surface contact with Kohala
Following the cessation of activity in Kohala and Mauna Kea activity from the other
shields resulted in widespread aerial fall of ash over much of the island This ash has
weathered rapidly into a fine silty soil which is typified by low in situ densities and high field
moisture contents In some locales when the field moisture content is sufficiently high the
ash soil exhibits thixotropic properties i e the soil temporarily loses strength when
remolded and regains its strength after internal pore pressures dissipate In more arid
areas the ash soil exists in a dry friable state even though field moisture contents can
approach 100 percent of dry weight

SECTION 3 0 DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field exploration the project site is underlain by a thin surficial fill
layer of gravel materials underlain by clayey silt volcanic ash extending down to 2 5 to
19 feet below the existing ground surface
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered at the control building site we
believe that shallow foundations consisting of continuous footings may be used to
support the new control building planned at the site Based on our laboratory tests and
the clayey silt volcanic ash soils we recommend that the subgrade soils underneath
the footing and slab areas be properly prepared and capped with at least 6 inches of
compacted base course The aggregate base course should be compacted to a
minimum of 95 percent relative compaction The subgrade soils should be scarified to a
depth of about 12 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above optimum
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction The slab
subgrade should be kept moist and free of shrinkage cracks prior to placement of base
course and concrete slab
Along the access road alignment the near surface soils appear to have a
CBR value of about 2 5 percent and a swell value of 0 9 percent Based on the above
conditions and anticipated volume of traffic consisting primarily of maintenance trucks
with light usage we believe that a minimum of 12 inches of aggregate base should be
used for the unpaved road
Detailed discussion of these items and our geotechnical recommendations for
design are presented in the following sections of this report
3 1 Foundations
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered at the control building site we
envision that shallow foundations consisting of continuous footings may be used to
support the new control building An allowable bearing pressure of up to 3 000 pounds
per square foot psf may be used for design of shallow foundations bearing on the
compacted base course The bearing value provided is for dead plus live loads and
W O INC Page 6
Hawaii California
SECTION 3 DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
may be increased by 50 percent for transient loads such as those caused by wind or
seismic forces In addition we envision that the new control building will have a
concrete floor in the interior of the building In order to provide a uniform bearing
surface for the concrete floor we recommend that a 6 inch thick layer of compacted
base course be provided below the concrete floor
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by frictional resistance
developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil and by passive
earth pressure acting against the near vertical faces of the embedded portion of
footings Resistance due to friction may be estimated using a coefficient of friction of
0 40 for foundations bearing directly on the properly compacted base course material
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid
pressure of 350 pounds per square foot per foot of depth pcf for footings embedded in
the very stiff to hard clayey silt soil formation or compacted base course Unless
covered by pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the upper 12 inches below the
finished grade should be neglected
If foundations are located next to utility trenches or easements they should be
embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane extending
upward from the bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility
lines This requirement is necessary to avoid surcharging adjacent below grade
structures with additional structural loads and to reduce the potential for foundation
settlement
3 2 Site Grading
At the on set of earthwork the areas within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed Vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable
materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site Soft and yielding areas
encountered during clearing and grubbing below areas designated to receive fill should
be over excavated to expose firm natural material and the resulting excavation should
be backfilled with well compacted engineered fill The excavated soft and or organic
soils should be properly disposed of off site or used in landscaping areas if appropriate

The on site unsuitable materials generated from the cut areas should not be
used as fill and backfill materials They should be disposed of properly off site or used
in landscaping areas if appropriate Aggregate base course material should meet the
material requirements for Aggregate for Untreated Base Section 703 06 as specified
in the Hawaii Standard Specifications for Road Bridge and Public Works Construction
1994 Imported fill material if required should consist of non expansive imported
granular material such as crushed coral basalt or cinder sand The material should be
well graded from coarse to fine with no particles larger than 3 inches in largest
dimension and should contain between 10 and 30 percent particles passing the No 200
sieve The material should have a laboratory CBR value of 20 or more and should have
a maximum swell of less than 1 percent Imported fill material should be tested by
Geolabs Inc for conformance with these recommendations prior to delivery to the
project site for its intended use
Base course and imported granular fill materials should be moisture conditioned
to above the optimum moisture placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose
thickness and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Relative
compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the
maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557
test procedures Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight
corresponding to the maximum dry density Compaction should be accomplished by
sheepsfoot rollers vibratory rollers or other types of acceptable compaction equipment
Water tamping jetting or ponding should not be used to compact the fill materials
Site grading operations should be observed by a representative from
Geolabs Inc It is important that a representative from our office observe the site
grading operation to evaluate whether undesirable materials are encountered during the
scarification and excavation process and whether the exposed soil rock conditions are
similar to those encountered in our field exploration

3 3 Slab On Grade
Based on our field exploration we recommend that the subgrade soils below the
slab concrete be properly prepared and capped with a minimum 6 inch thick base
course layer The 6 inch thick compacted base course layer will also serve to provide
more uniform support for the slab The base course materials shall be compacted to a
minimum of 95 percent of the maximum dry density as determined by ASTM D 1557
Test Methods for Laboratory Compaction Characteristics of Soil Using Modified Effort
The in place density of the compacted base course shall be determined in accordance
with ASTM D 1556 Standard Test Methods for Density and Unit Weight of Soil in Place
by the Sand Cone Method or ASTM D 2922 Standard Test Methods for Density of Soil
and Soil Aggregate in Place by Nuclear Methods Shallow Depths The area adjacent
to the control building slab should be backfilled tightly against the slab with compacted
on site soils and graded to divert water away from the slab to reduce the potential for
water ponding around the slab and foundation
3 4 Concrete Pads
We envision that concrete pads and exterior flatwork would be constructed at the
project site In general we recommend that a minimum 4 inch thick cushion fill layer
consisting of open graded gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation be provided below the
concrete pad for uniform support As an alternative the concrete pad may be
constructed directly on the compacted structural fill subgrade in fill areas provided that
the subgrade is properly prepared and compacted To reduce the potential for
substantial shrinkage cracks in the concrete slabs crack control joints should be
provided at intervals equal to the width of the concrete pads with expansion joints at
right angle intersections
3 5 Pipelines
We envision that a water pipeline will be installed at the project site It is
anticipated that most of the trenches for the pipelines will be excavated in the stiff silty
clay encountered in the borings drilled for the project In general we recommend that a
granular bedding layer consisting of 6 inches of open graded gravel ASTM C 33
No 67 gradation be provided below the pipes for uniform support of the pipes

Free draining granular materials such as open graded gravel ASTM C 33
No 67 gradation should be used for the initial trench backfill up to about 12 inches
above the pipes It is critical to use this free draining material to reduce the potential for

granular bedding and the initial trench backfill should be moisture conditioned and
compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction

to the top of the subgrade or finished grade should consist of well graded granular
materials less than G6inches in maximum size The backfill should be
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture placed in about 12 inch loose lifts
and mechanically compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by drilling and
sampling six borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 6 The borings were
advanced to depths of approximately 10 to 32 feet below the existing ground surface
using a truck mounted drill rig equipped with augers and coring tools The approximate
locations of the borings drilled for the project are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our field engineer geologist who monitored the drilling
operations on a near continuous basis Soils were classified in general conformance
with the Unified Soil Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic
representations of the materials encountered are presented on the Logs of Borings
Plates A 1 through A 6
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained from the borings drilled in
general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling
of Soils using a 3 inch OD Modified California sampler and ASTM Test Designation
D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils using 2 inch OD standard
penetration sampler The samplers were driven by a140 pound hammer falling
30 inches The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches
of an 18 inch drive are shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Borings at
the appropriate sample depths
Core samples of the rock formations encountered at the site were obtained using
diamond core drilling techniques in general accordance with ASTM Standard Practice
D 2113 Diamond Core Drilling for Site Investigation Core drilling is a rotary drilling
method that uses a hollow bit to cut into the rock formation The material left in the
hollow core of the bit is mechanically recovered for examination and description

Laboratory Testing
Moisture Content ASTM D 2216 and Unit Weight ASTM D 2937
determinations were performed on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification
and evaluation of soil properties The results of these tests are presented on the
Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Two Sieve Analysis tests ASTM C 136 ASTM D 1140 were performed on
selected soil samples to evaluate the gradation characteristics of the soil and to aid in
soil classification Graphic presentation of the grain size distribution is provided on
Plate B 1
One laboratory California Bearing Ratio Test ASTM D 1883 was performed on
a bulk sample of the near surface soil materials to evaluate the pavement support
characteristics of the soil Results of the CBR test are presented on Plate B 2
Four one inch Ring Swell tests were performed on either natural or remolded
samples of the soil to evaluate the swelling potential of the near surface soils under
different surcharge pressures The results of these tests are summarized on Plate B 3
