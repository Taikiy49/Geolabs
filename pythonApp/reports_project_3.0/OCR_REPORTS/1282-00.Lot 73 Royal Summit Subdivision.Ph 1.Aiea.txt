Dear Mr Titcomb
In accordance with your request we have made a study of the near
surface soil conditions at the above referenced lot in order to
provide foundation recommendations for the proposed residence The
study consisted of drilling three shallow borings laboratory testing
of the swelling potential of the soils and reviewing of the subdivision
site grading and preliminary house foundation plans This report
summarizes our findings and recommendations
PROPOSED CONSTRUCT ION
Based on the preliminary house plans the proposed residence will be a
split level structure approximately 47 by 52 feet in plan dimensions
It is our understanding that concrete on grade slab is planned for the
garage and the house

The subject lot is located on the northwestern corner of Aupunimo i

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Laboratory swell tests on the surface material indicate that it
generally has low swelling potential if maintained at or above opt imum
moisture content However previous experience with this type of
material indicates that the swelling potential may increase if it is
allowed to dry out Therefore some special foundation design and
subgrade preparation are recommended
It is anticipated that 2 to 4 feet of fill will be required over most
of the lot
Ideally select non expansive soils such as select borrow crusher
waste or coral should be used for fill below 2 feet of the house
finished grade and for a distance of 3 feet beyond the slab
perimeter This would provide a competent bearing layer for the
foundations in this portion of the house
The on site stockpile of excess material from the Royal Summit
Subdivision grading work may be available for fill at a lower costs

Although this material generally has low to moderate swelling
potential it could have been contaminated with other expansive soil
during handling Therefore it is recommended that these soils be
only used in the yard areas and up to 2 feet below the slab subgrade
The remaining upper 2 feet should consist of select borrow
non expansive granular soil
The garage and driveway will probably bear on the in situ existing
soils The foundation and subgrade preparation recommendations are
listed in the following sections
Foundations on Imported Select Borrow
Foundations bearing on the minimum 2 foot cap of compacted
non expansive select borrow material may use an allowable bearing
value of 3000 p s f
Conventional on grade slab design may be used on the select material
To reduce cost the remaining fill below the upper 2 foot cap of
non expansive 111 could consist of the on site select fill from the
Royal Summit Subdivision grading The material should first be
approved by a qualified soils engineer prior to its being used as fill
GEOLABS HAWAII

Foundations in On Site Soil
Laboratory testing of the stiff on site material indicates that these
soils have a low to moderate swelling potential Therefore special
precautionary measures are recommended for foundations placed on the
existing soils
The recommended subgrade preparation prior to final concrete placement
as described under the SLAB ON GRADE section is also required
We further recommend that a deeper footing embedment of 12 inches
below the lowest adjacent finished grade for individual footings and
18 inches for perimeter slab footings be used for the stiff on site
soils An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f imay be used
The bottom of all foundation excavations should be cleaned of loose
material and bear on firm soil The excavations should be observed by
a soils engineer to see that adequate bearing soil has been obtained
The ground surface should be shaped to drain water away from the
foundations
Slab On Grade
Concrete on grade slab is planned for the garage driveway and the
entire house Where a minimum of 2 feet of imported non expansive
soil such as crusher waste is used for the fill conventional
on grade slab design with an 8 inch deep thickened edge may be used

Where on site soil is encountered at subgrade level a deeper 18 inch
perimeter footing embedment and special subgrade preparation with lime
saturation are recommended The general procedures listed hereunder
for lime saturation should be followed
1
Hydrated lime should be placed on subgrade soils and at the wall
footing excavations prior to water saturating of the subgrade
soil We believe that about ten 50 1b bags of hydrated lime would
be adequate to cover these areas
The subgrade soils should be water saturated with perforated
hoses by constant sprinkling for at least 2 days prior to concrete
placement to reduce the swelling potential of the underlying
subsoil It is important that the soils engineer be notified to
inspect the above subgrade preparation and check the moisture
contents of the underlying soils prior to pouring of the slab and
perimeter footing
The bottom of the finished subgrade and footing excavations should
be neat and free of loose soils It is anticipated that the very
soft silt and lime mud deposited at the footing after saturation
would be removed just prior to concrete placement

4 The concreting of footing and slab should be coordinated in such a
way that the time period between the initial excavation and the
final concrete placement should be kept to a minimum in order to
prevent excessive subgrade drying
5 The above recommendations for saturating the subgrade with hydrated
lime should also be followed for the sidewalk driveway and patio
Slabs
It is recommended that on grade slabs be placed over a 4 inch layer of
well graded aggregate base course and should be separated from wall
foundations
A plastic membrane should be placed over the base course layer if a
moisture barrier is deemed necessary to protect the floor covering
Retaining Walls
It is our understanding that low retaining walls are planned for the
proposed residence The guidelines for wall designs are listed below
1 For lateral earth pressures the following can be used
Walls unrestrained at the top 35 p s f equivalent
active condition level fluid pressure
backfill per foot of depth
Wall restrained at the top 45 p s f equivalent
at rest condition fluid pressure
level backfill per foot of depth

W O 1282 00 Page 8
2 An allowable bearing value of up to 3000 psf is
recommended for foundations bearing on imported select
borrow and 2500 p s f for the stiff on site soil
3 A passive earth resistance of 300 p s per foot of depth
may be used for the wall footing on level areas The
passive pressure does not apply for walls on slopes
4 A base friction factor of 0 4 can also be used against
sliding of the wall
5 The wall foundations on relatively flat areas should be
embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent
finished grade
6 A subsurface drainage system subdrains or weepholes should
be utilized behind the walls Any subdrain should be
properly constructed and drain freely by gravity onto lower
open space
Drainage
The finish lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings should be
shaped to shed water away from the foundations and to avoid ponding
condition Also it is advised that gutter systems should be
installed around the house and that gutter water should be diverted
far away from the foundations and walkways Excessive landscape
watering near the foundations and slab should also be avoided
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1282 00 Page 9
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance
of the above recommended foundations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of
strength The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled
tight against slab footing immediately after setting of the concrete
to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained to
drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundation
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the footing excavations and backfill operations
be observed by the soils engineer during construction to see that
adequate bearing has been obtained and that the subsurface conditions
are consistent with the findings of our subsurface investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the above
inspections

INVESTIGATION LIMITATIONS
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations made
in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation engineering
principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all other
warranties either expressed or implied
The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are based on
our site reconnaissance soil information from boring data and
laboratory tests obtained from this soil investigation
Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encountered and cannot be
fully determined by taking soil samples Unforeseen conditions such

properly constructed project Some contingency fund is thus
recommended to accommodate these possible costs
