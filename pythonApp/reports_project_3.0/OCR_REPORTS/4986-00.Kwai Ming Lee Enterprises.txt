In accordance with our fee proposal dated we have conducted a
geotechnical engineering exploration for the new office building for Kwai Ming Lee
Enterprises in the Kapahulu area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii This report summarizes
our work on the project and presents our findings and geotechnical engineering
recommendations derived from our analyses for the subject project
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located at 988 C Kapahulu Avenue 31 28 in
Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The new 2 story office building with a roof deck
and stair well case on the third floor will have a gross floor area of 7 573 square feet and
will replace an existing 2 story wooden building The first floor will mainly be a parking
garage The second floor will consist of general offices The new building will have
concrete floor slabs and CMU walls

The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions at the site to develop a soil rock data set for the formulation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertaining to the foundation design of the proposed office
building Our scope of work included the following tasks and work efforts

SITE CONDITIONS
The subject property is located off of Kapahulu Avenue southwest of the
intersection with Kaimuki Avenue The lot is rectangular in shape with a total area of about
6 742 square feet Access is via a private driveway along the northern property line The
lot is fairly level with an existing two story wooden structure situated on the rear or
southern portion of the lot A covered parking shed is located along the western portion of
the lot The parking areas in the front of the site are paved with asphaltic concrete There
is an existing 10 foot wide sewer easement along the rear property boundary

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the subsurface soil conditions encountered at the project site we
believe that shallow spread and continuous footings bearing on the near surface coral
formation or compacted fill may be used to support the proposed building
Detailed discussion of these items and our geotechnical engineering design
recommendations are presented in the following sections of this report
Foundations
Based on the anticipated subsurface soil conditions at the project site we believe
that shallow spread and continuous footing foundations may be used for support of the
proposed building An allowable bearing pressure of up to 3 000 pounds per square foot
psf may be used for the foundations bearing on the near surface coral formation or
properly compacted structural fill The bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may
be increased by one third 1 3 for transient loads such as those caused by wind or
seismic forces
In general the footings should be embedded a minimum of 24 inches below the
lowest adjacent finish grades If foundations are located next to utility trenches they
should be embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane
extending upward from the bottom edge of the utility
Because of the potential for presence of voids and or cavities in the coral formation
below the foundations a program of probing and grouting the foundation locations is

We estimate that footing settlements under the anticipated design loads for footings
bearing on the coral formation or compacted fill as recommended herein to be less than
one inch total with differential settlements on the order of inch
For footings bearing on compacted fill the bottom of the footing excavations should
be recompacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction prior to placement of
reinforcing steel or concrete For footings bearing on the dense coral the bottom of the
excavation should be clean and free of loose material If soft and or loose materials are
encountered during footing excavations they should be over excavated to expose the
underlying firm dense materials The over excavation should be backfilled with lean
concrete or select granular material compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative
compaction
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by frictional resistance
developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil and by passive earth
pressure acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system A coefficient of
friction of 0 4 may be used for footings bearing on the coral formation or compacted
structural fills Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be estimated using an
equivalent fluid pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth pcf This
assumes that the soils around the footings are well compacted Unless covered by
pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the upper 12 inches of soil should be
neglected
Foundation Probing
To reduce the potential for loss of foundation support resulting from collapse of
cavities and or voids below the foundations we recommend that a program of cavity
probing and grouting be conducted below the footings locations
For estimating purposes we recommend that probe holes be drilled at each spread
footing locations and on 15 foot centers for continuous footings The probe holes should
be at least 3 inches in diameter and should extend to at least 10 feet below the planned
bottom of footing If cavities and or voids are encountered or suspected during the probing
operation additional probe holes should be drilled at closer spacings to help delineate the
vertical and lateral extend of the cavity and or void Following the drilling of each probe
hole a 2 inch PVC pipe should be inserted into the probe holes to preserve to open hole
integrity for grouting A field engineer or geologist from Geolabs should be present to log
the probe holes and to determine the locations of additional probe holes needed
_ The probe holes and cavities discovered should be backfilled with sand cement
grout We recommend that a low strength sand cement grout mixture with an

Slab On Grade
Our field exploration indicates that the project site is generally underlain by
near surface clayey silt soils with low to moderate swelling potential wnen subjected to
moisture fluctuations Unless floor slabs constructed above these soils are properly
designed there is a potential for future distress to the lightly loaded slabs on grade
resulting from shrinking and swelling of these soils due to changes in the moisture content
To reduce the potential for structural distress resulting from the swelling of the subgrade
soils we recommend that the subgrade soils be properly prepared and capped with at
least 6 inches of non expansive select granular fill material The exterior area adjacent to
the slabs should be backfilled tightly against the slab and graded to divert water away from
the slab to reduce the potential of water ponding around the foundation
The select granular fill should be placed soon after moisture conditioning and
compaction of the clayey silt subgrade soils The prepared capping fill and the underlying
subgrade soils should be wetted and kept moist prior to final placement of concrete
Subgrade soils should be prepared in accordance with the recommendations presented in
the Site Grading section of this report Compaction requirements for the subgrade soils
and select granular fill are also discussed in the same section of this report
For the interior building slabs not subjected to vehicular traffic we recommend
that a minimum 4 inch thick layer of cushion fill consisting of No 3 Fine gravel
ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation or Basaltic Termite Barrier BTB be placed below the slab
to provide more uniform support of the slabs and to serve as a capillary break To reduce
the potential for future moisture infiltration and subsequent damage to floor coverings an
impervious moisture barrier is recommended on top of the gravel or BTB cushion layer
Flexible floor coverings such as carpet or sheet vinyl should be considered since they
can better mask minor slab cracking It is also recommended that interior walls be

For slabs on grade that will be subjected to vehicular traffic such as the garage

width of the walkways and at right angle intersections
The areas adjacent to the slabs should be backfilled tightly against the slabs and
graded to divert water away from the slabs to reduce the potential of water ponding around
the slab The thickened edges of slabs adjacent to unpaved areas should be embedded
at least 12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade
Site Grading
At the commencement of grading work areas within the grading limits should be
cleared grubbed and scarified to a minimum depth of 12 inches moisture conditioned to
above the optimum moisture content and recompacted to not less than 90 percent relative
compaction ASTM D 1557 Trees should be removed and any roots greater than
3 inches in diameter should be over excavated and be backfilled with fill placed in 12 inch
maximum loose lifts moisture conditioned to at least the optimum moisture content and
compacted to at least 95 percent relative compaction
Soft and yielding areas encountered should be over excavated to expose firm
natural material and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with fill placed in 12 inch
maximum loose lifts moisture conditioned to at least the optimum moisture content and
compacted to at least 95 percent relative compaction The excavated soils should be
disposed of properly off site and or used in landscape areas where appropriate

The compacted structural fill should consist of non expansive select granular
material such as crushed basalt or coral The material should be well graded from coarse
to fine with no particles larger than 3 inches in largest dimension and should contain
between 10 and 30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve The material should have
a laboratory CBR value of 20 or more and should have a swell of less than 1 percent
Compacted structural fill should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum
moisture content placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and
compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to
the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of
the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 test procedures Optimum
moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum
dry density
Utilities
In general for support of the utility lines we recommend that a granular bedding
consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation be used under
the pipes The initial backfill up to about 1 foot above the pipes should consist of
free draining backfills such as No 3B Fine gravel to reduce the potential for damaging
the pipes from compaction of the backfill It is critical that a free draining granular material
be used to reduce the potential for formation of voids below the haunches of pipes and to
provide adequate support for the sides of the pipes The use of on site soils as backfill
immediately around utility pipes is not recommended
For protected utility lines such as concrete jacketed electrical and
telecommunications lines the bedding requirements described above are not necessary if
the concrete jackets are cast neat in the utility trench
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 1 foot above the pipes to the
finished subgrade should consist of approved on site soils or select granular material The
backfill material should be moisture conditioned to at least the optimum moisture content
placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a
minimum of 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for future ground
subsidence The upper 2 feet of the trench backfill below the pavement subgrade should
be compacted to not less than 95 percent relative compaction Mechanical compaction
equipment should be used to compact the materials at the project site Water tamping
jetting or ponding should not be used to compact the backfill material

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from the field borings Variations of subsoil conditions between and
beyond the field borings may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not
become evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident Geolabs
should be contacted to re evaluate the recommendations presented in this report
The boring locations in the field were determined by taping from the site layout plan
transmitted to our office by Architects Pacific Ltd The physical locations of the field
borings should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown on the graphic representations of the borings depict
the approximate boundaries between the soil types and as such may denote a gradual
transition Water level data from the borings were measured at the times shown on the
graphic representations and or in the text of this report These data have been reviewed
and interpretations made in the formulation of this report However it must be noted that
fluctuation may occur due to tidal fluctuation variation in rainfall temperatures and other
factors

Office building project in accordance with generally accepted geotechnical engineering

This report has been prepared solely for the purpose of assisting the design
engineers and architect in the design evaluation of the proposed project Therefore this
report may not contain sufficient data or the proper information to serve as the basis for
preparation of construction cost estimates nor for bidding purposes A contractor wishing
to bid on this project should retain a competent geotechnical engineer to assist in the
interpretation of this report and or in the performance of additional site specific exploration

constructed project Therefore a sufficient contingency fund is recommended to
