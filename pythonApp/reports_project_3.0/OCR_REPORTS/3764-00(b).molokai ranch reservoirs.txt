In accordance with our proposal dated March 26 1997 we have been observing
the earthwork operation and performing tests on the materials for this reservoir project
since A previous report dated August 21 1998 was issued for the
earthwork observations for Reservoir No 2 This report summarizes the earthwork
observation services performed during the earthwork construction from March 5 1998 to
for the construction of the Reservoir No 1 and access roads
Background Information
The proposed reservoir project is located within Molokai Ranch in the District of
Kaunakakai on the Island of Molokai Hawaii as shown on the Project Location Map
Plate 1 The project consists of constructing two lined reservoir lakes for water
impoundment designated as Reservoir Nos 1 and 2 Each of the two proposed
reservoirs will have an estimated capacity of about 16 million gallons MG In addition
several access roads will be constructed for this project The grading plan and
approximate locations of the two reservoirs are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2

Based on our field exploration the project site is underlain by silty and clayey
residual soils overlying saprolite soils and highly weathered rock The earthwork
observed for the reservoir construction generally included excavations of up to about
45 feet in height fills of up to about 20 feet in height subgrade preparation and fill
placement and compaction
Earthwork Observation and Testing
The earthwork operations for the project were performed by Patterson
Construction and Trucking Inc At the on set of earthwork areas within the contract
grading limits were generally cleared and grubbed of vegetation and deleterious
materials Materials generated from the clearing operations were disposed of off site
Following the clearing and grubbing operations the areas to be cut were excavated
Finished grades were compacted using a smooth drum and or sheepsfoot vibratory roller
The fills and backfills needed for the earthwork generally consisted of on site
materials generated from the excavations The on site fill materials generally consisted of
silty and clayey soils with some gravel Samples of the fill and backfill materials utilized
during construction were obtained and transported to our office for laboratory testing The
maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory
in general accordance with American Society of Testing and Material ASTM Test
Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor The test results are summarized on Plate 3 of
this report
During the fill placement operations the fill materials were generally placed in thin
lifts and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density determined
in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor In general the
on site soils were in a very moist condition Periodic field density tests were generally
taken at every one half station about 50 foot intervals for each lift during the fill
placement and subgrade preparation operations to confirm the degree of compaction and
moisture content of the fill material achieved during the reservoir construction
A total of 197 field density tests were performed using the nuclear probe method in
general accordance with ASTM Test Designations D 2922 and D 3017 The results of the
compaction tests performed during the fill placement operations are summarized on
Plates 4 1 through 4 6 of this report These tests were performed on the compacted fills
at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from
on site survey stakes where available These locations and elevations should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed to
be unsatisfactory were generally reworked and in most cases retested until the specified
minimum compaction was obtained

Limitations
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore portions of the earthwork at
the site may have occurred without our observation It should be recognized that there
are variations in the accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used
to evaluate the earthwork construction and materials Our services were performed in
accordance with generally accepted standards of geotechnical engineering practice no
warranty is expressed or implied Provision of construction testing and monitoring by an
engineer is not insurance nor does it guarantee construction of any type The contractor
shall retain responsibility for the quality of the work for adhering to the plans and
specifications and for repairing defects regardless of when they are found
