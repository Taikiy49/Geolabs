Project Considerations
The project site designated as Tax Map Key 67 is adjacent to the existing
Nanaikapono Elementary School off Mano Avenue in Nanakuli in the leeward coast area
on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project site is a trapezoidal shaped parcel
encompassing approximately 1 61 acres
Based on the information provided we understand that a new NFL Youth
Education Town YET facility for the Boys Girls Club of Hawaii is planned We
understand that the new building will be a single story structure with perimeter Concrete
Masonry Unit CMU walls and isolated CMU and steel columns as well as fiberglass
shingle roofing and a concrete slab on grade floor
Based on the information provided by the project structural engineer we anticipate
maximum column loads on the order of about 46 kips and wall loads of about 1 7 kips per
lineal foot for the design of foundations for the building The design finished floor elevation

Probing and Grouting Operations
The probing and grouting operations were performed in general conformance with
the recommendations presented in our report for the project dated The
purpose of the probing and grouting operation was to reduce the potential for loss of
foundation support resulting from collapse of cavities or compression of loose zones below
the main building
Lindemann Construction Inc surveyed the footing layout and Geolabs marked the
probe hole locations prior to the probing operation In general the probe holes were
located at the center of the column footings and spaced at approximately 10 foot intervals
along the wall footings The approximate probe hole locations are shown on the Probe
Hole Location Plan Plate 1 In general the probe holes at the new building were drilled to
a depth of about 12 feet below the existing ground surface corresponding to
approximately 8 to 10 feet below the design footing bottom elevations
Geolabs drilled the probe holes about 4 inches in diameter using a truck mounted
drill rig equipped with continuous flight augers Following the probing operation a 2 inch
diameter PVC pipe was inserted into each of the probe holes to maintain the integrity of
the open hole and to reduce the potential for cave in prior to the grouting operation A total
of 60 probe holes with an aggregate length of about 720 lineal feet below the existing
ground surface were drilled for the project
The probe holes were backfilled with sand cement grout supplied by Ameron
injected at low pressures American Standard Concrete Pumping Hawaii Inc performed
the grouting work and a Geolabs representative observed the grouting operations Grout
quantities were estimated using the number of strokes to fill each probe hole compared to
the number of strokes to fill up a 5 gallon bucket The grouting operation generally
proceeded in the following manner
e Immediately prior to grouting of a particular hole the temporary PVC pipe
was removed A 2 inch diameter PVC grout pipe attached to the end of the
grouting hose was then inserted into the open hole and lowered to
approximately 2 feet from the bottom of the hole Grout injection then
commenced at the direction of our field representative who provided
direction as to when the injection was complete or whether additional
injection was required
e Each probe hole was grouted from the desired depth upward by low
pressure injection of grout through the PVC grout pipe which was retracted
