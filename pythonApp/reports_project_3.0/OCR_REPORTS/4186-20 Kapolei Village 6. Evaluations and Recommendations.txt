Dear Mr Blevins
In accordance with your request site visits were made to observe and evaluate the
house distress reported by the homeowners at Lot Nos 172 and 174 of the Kapolei Village
6 subdivision in the Kapolei area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The following
summarizes our field observations and findings during our site visits and presents our
discussions and preliminary recommendations with respect to the observed distresses at
the two lots The approximate locations of these two lots are shown on the Site Plan
Plate 1
Field Observations

A site visit was conducted on to observe and evaluate the house

of the drywalls non bearing walls

gap was on the order of approximately 1 inch
e Diagonal hairline cracks at the window corner and garage door frame
e The appearance of tension cracks along the top of the sloping backyard as
reported by the homeowner the cracks were not apparent or observed at
the time of our site visit

Discussions and Preliminary Recommendations
Based on our field observations and the information provided by the homeowners
at Lot Nos 172 and 174 it is our opinion that the distresses observed do not appear to be

a result of structural inadequacy of the house structures However it is apparent that both
residences have experienced some amount of ground movements due to the sloping
backyard condition at the lots Therefore we believe that some of the distresses observed
could be attributed to ground movements resulting from creeping of soil slow movement
of the soil due to drying and wetting of the soil and or erosion washing of soils caused by
improper drainage at the sloping backyard We also noted that some ponding of water
was observed in Lot No 174 Ponding of water within the lot would cause infiltration of
water into the subsurface causing the underling clayey soils to swell and heave
Therefore some corrective measures to provide for positive drainage away from the
house structure should be undertaken to improve the lot drainage of Lot No 174
Based on discussions with the homeowners we understand that some minor
movements and recurrence of the distresses are still being experienced at the above lots
In order to determine the appropriate remedial actions we recommend that a monitoring
program be implemented to determine if the ground movement causing the cracks
observed within the homes has stabilized or are still ongoing Crack gauges and or other
markers should be installed at selected locations by our office for this purpose The
gauges and or markers should be read on a monthly basis for a minimum period of 3 to
6 months to provide sufficient data for further evaluation and determination
Recommendations for appropriate remedial measures may then be finalized for
implementation at the end of the monitoring period
