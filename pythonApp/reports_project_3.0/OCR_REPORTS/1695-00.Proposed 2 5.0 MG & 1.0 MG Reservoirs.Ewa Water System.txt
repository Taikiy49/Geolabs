EWA WATER SYSTEM
In accordance with your request we have performed a soil and
foundation investigation at the above referenced site The scope of work
performed was in general accordance with our revised proposal dated April
25 1986
The purpose of our soil engineering investigation was to explore the
subsurface conditions at the two proposed sites in order to provide
recommendations for the design of the foundations for the three proposed
reservoirs The general location of the project area is shown on the
Project Location Map Plate l
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests which we
used in the engineering analysis to formulate our conclusions and
recommendations are presented in the appendices of this report
The approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site
Plans Plates 2 and 3 The log of borings and information regarding the
field exploration and laboratory testing are also presented in the
appendices of this report
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed project consists of the following
1 Two 5 0 M G concrete reservoirs each approxmiately 208 feet in
diameter and 20 feet high Only one reservoir will be
constructed at the present time The second structure will be
constructed at a future date Column loads of about 100 kips
and perimeter wall loads of about 5 3 kips per foot are
anticipated
Cuts into the hillside fron about 7 feet eastern side up to
about 20 feet western side are planned to create a level pad
area for the proposed initial reservoir Cuts from 20 feet up
to 27 feet are anticipated for the future reservoir
2 A 1 0 M G concrete reservoir approximately 100 feet in
diameter and 20 feet high Column loads of about 100 kips and
perimeter wall loads of about 2 2 kips per foot are anticipated
Cuts into the hillside fron 6 feet up to 10 feet are planned to
create a level pad area for the reservoir

SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed site for the two 5 0 M G reservoirs is located ina
cane field approximately 300 feet south of Honouliuli Stream and 200 feet
north of the H 1 Freeway Waipahu is about 2 miles east of the site
The topography at the 5 0 M G reservoirs site is generally flat
sloping slightly to the east The site is in recently harvested cane
fields that are accessible to truck mounted drilling equipment by means of
cane haul roads The ground elevations at the site generally vary from
about 210 to 235 feet above MSL
The 1 0 M G reservoir site is located about 4500 feet up slope
north of the 5 0 M G reservoirs and is also located along the edge of
a cane field that is accessible by cane haul roads The ground elevations
at this site vary from about 412 to 430 feet above MSL

cobbles and boulders at different depths 5 to 22 feet indicating that

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Initial 5 0 M G Reservoir
The borings disclosed that the reservoir site is situated over a
thick layer of boulders extending a minimum of 20 feet below the reservoir
finish elevation Based on the boring information the boulders appear to
be tightly packed with only a little soil matrix Therefore it is our
opinion that the reservoir may be constructed on the dense boulder layer
Foundation

therefore we recommend that the boulders be over excavated a
minimum of 2 feet below the footings and slab and replaced with
compacted select borrow to provide a uniform bearing layer A
polyethylene moisture barrier is recommended over the gravel fill to
minimize possible seepage below the reservoir tank
Spread and continuous wall footings bearing on the compacted gravel
is recommended for the reservoir An allowable bearing value of
3500 p s f may be used for footings bearing on these soils

For the proposed reservoir sites the hillsides behind the tanks

Several different soil conditions are anticipated in the cut

formation is anticipated at the future 5 0 M G reservoir site
Stiff residual soil is anticipated at the 1 0 M G reservoir site

Excavation
a a me i
The field exploration indicates that the two 5 0 M G reservoir
sites are underlain at fairly shallow depths by dense basalt and
cobbles and boulders
Relatively hard ripping is anticipated in the boulders and basalt
formation The more massive basalt in the future reservoir cut
slope will likely require special excavating procedures such as hoe
ram equipment or blasting to make the proposed cut Therefore we
recommend that the site grading for the future reservoir be
completed in conjunction with the initial reservoir site work to
minimize the possibility of damage to the reservoir structure caused
by excessive vibrations for the rock excavation
The above discussions regarding the rippability of the subsurface
materials are based on our visual observations of the existing rock
outcrops in the area and interpretation of the boring core data
We recommend that all prospective contractors bidding on this site
should examine the rock outcrops and the boring data and to make
their own interpretation
Relatively easy excavation is anticipated for the 1 0 M G reservoir
site

Design Review
Plans for the site grading and foundation construction should be
forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written comments prior to

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during
earthwork construction particularly slope cuts to monitor the
contractor s grading operations
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be observed by a
soils engineer during construction to see that adequate bearing has been
ootained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the
findings of our investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
If conditions encountered during construction are different from
those presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made
