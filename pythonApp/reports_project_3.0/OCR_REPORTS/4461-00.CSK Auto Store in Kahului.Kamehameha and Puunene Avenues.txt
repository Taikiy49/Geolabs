INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration performed
for the proposed CSK Auto Store in Kahului near the intersection between Kamehameha and
Pu unene Avenues in the Kahului area on the Island of Maui Hawaii The general location
and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering recommendations
derived from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering analyses The
recommendations presented herein are intended for the design of foundations
slabs on grade site preparation and pavements only The findings and recommendations
presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed project site is located near the southeast corner of the intersection
between Kamehameha and Pu unene Avenues in the Kahului area on the Island of Maui
Hawaii It is proposed to construct a new one story auto parts store with associated parking
areas around the building Based on the information provided the project site encompasses
an area of approximately 30 000 square feet which includes the new one story store and
parking areas as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The site is currently a temporary storage
area and asphaltic concrete paved parking area
The new store building will have a footprint of about 8 000 square feet Based on the
surface conditions at the site we anticipate that the finished floor of the new store building
will be at or near the existing grades Therefore we envision that only minor site grading
consisting of cuts and fills on the order of about 1 foot or less would be needed to achieve
the design finished grades Further details pertaining to the site grading were not available
at the time this report was prepared
Based on our experience in the vicinity of the project site we anticipate that the
subsoil conditions at the proposed project site may consist of sandy or silty near surface

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The Island of Maui is comprised of two major volcanoes the older West Maui Volcano
Tertiary Epoch and the more recent East Maui Volcano also known as Haleakala
Pleistocene Epoch The Isthmus of Maui is a narrow gently sloping plain located between
these two volcanoes The project site is located near the northern portion of the island or on
the lower northern slope of the East Maui Volcano Haleakala
Haleakala was generally built in three distinguishable series of lava flows The lower
unit the Honomanu Volcanic Series late Pliocene to early Pleistocene Epoch consists of
thinly bedded tholeiitic basaltic pahoehoe and a a lavas These lavas tend to be very
permeable and carry basal water Overlying this unit is the Kula Volcanic Series early
Pleistocene Epoch composed primarily of thicker alkalic a a flows which may contain many
inter stratified thin ash soil layers
A long quiescent period followed during which time canyons were carved in the
volcano It is believed that a few eruptions may have occurred during this erosion interval
in addition to erosion due to eustatic changes higher and lower stands of the sea level At
the end of the rest period copious flows erupted along the southwest and east rifts This is
the third and upper rock unit known as the Hana Volcanic Series Pleistocene Epoch The
lavas of the Hana Volcanic Series range in composition from ultra basic olivine porphyries
to non porphyritic alkalic basalts

EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The proposed project site is located near the southeast corner of Kamehameha and
Pu unene Avenues in the Kahului area on the Island of Maui Hawaii The site is adjacent
to the existing Maui Mall which is located to the southeast In general the site is relatively
level with a slight slope for drainage purposes A topographic survey was not available for
our use at the time this report was prepared
The site presently exists as a temporary storage area enclosed by chain link fences
The project site used to be a portion of the parking lot for the adjacent Maui Mall and is
paved with asphaltic concrete Several light poles were observed within the footprint of the
proposed store site In addition several landscape islands were noted in the parking lot
where the new store will be situated Based on the location of the light poles and landscape
islands we envision that some underground utility lines will likely traverse the site We
anticipate that some of these underground utility lines and the light poles will need to be
relocated prior to commencement of construction of the store building

The subsurface conditions at the proposed auto store site were explored by drilling
and sampling four test borings extending to depths of about 11 5 and 16 5 feet below the
existing ground surface The approximate locations of the test borings are shown on the

depths of about 3 to 5 feet below the existing ground surface The near surface granular fill
materials consist of medium dense silty gravel and sand Loose to medium dense sands
with silt and traces of shell fragments beach sands were encountered below the near
surface fills extending to the maximum depth explored of approximate 16 5 feet below the
existing ground surface

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
As previously mentioned our field exploration at the subject site generally
encountered medium dense granular fills overlying loose to medium dense sandy soils
beach sands The beach sands encountered extended to the maximum explored depth of
about 16 5 feet below the existing ground surface At the time of our field exploration
groundwater was encountered at about 4 4 to 8 6 feet below the ground surface
Based on our field exploration and laboratory tests we believe that a shallow
foundation system consisting of spread and or continuous footings may be used to support
the proposed auto store building Due to the presence of pockets of loose sands at the
anticipated foundation subgrade level we recommend that the building foundations be
supported on 12 inches of compacted fill Therefore we recommend that the granular soils
_below the bottom of the footings be over excavated by at least 12 inches and be replaced
as compacted fill Detailed discussion of these items and our geotechnical engineering
recommendations are presented in the following sections of this report
Foundations
Based on the subsurface soil conditions encountered in our borings we recommend
that shallow spread and or continuous footings bearing on 12 inches of compacted fills be
used to support the proposed store building An allowable bearing pressure of up to
2 500 pounds per square foot psf may be used for the design of foundations bearing on
12 inches of compacted fills This bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may be
GEOLABS INC
W O 4461 00 Page 7
increased by one third 1 3 for transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic
forces
In order to provide the 12 inches of compacted fill below the bottom of footings we
envision that the subgrade soils below the bottom of footings will need to be over excavated
and replaced as compacted fill It should be noted that the over excavation should extend
a minimum distance of 12 inches laterally beyond the edges of the footings After over
excavating the footing subgrade soils the exposed subgrades should be compacted to a
dense and unyielding condition prior to placement of the 12 inches of compacted fills below
the footings
The over excavated soils granular materials may be re used as a source of fill
materials for the compacted fills The 12 inch thick compacted fills should be moisture
conditioned to above the optimum moisture content placed in 8 inch maximum loose lifts
and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers
to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of
the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 91 test procedures Optimum
moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry
density
In general footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest
adjacent grade If foundations are located next to utility trenches or easements they should
be embedded below a 45 degree imaginary plane extending upward from the bottom edge
of the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility lines This requirement is necessary
to avoid surcharging adjacent below grade structures with additional structural loads and to
reduce the potential for appreciable foundation settlement
lf soft and or loose materials are encountered at the bottom of the footing
over excavations the soft and or loose materials should be removed to expose the
underlying firm materials The over excavation should be backfilled with the on site granular
GEOLABS INC
W O Page 8
materials compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction or the footing may be
extended down deeper to bear on the underlying competent material
If the footings are designed and constructed as recommended herein we estimate
that footing settlements under the anticipated design loads for footings bearing on 12 inches
of compacted fills to be less than 1 inch total Differential settlements between adjacent
footings supported on the compacted fills are estimated to be on the order of about
0 5 inches
Lateral loads acting on the building may be resisted by frictional resistance developed
between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil and by passive earth pressure
acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system A coefficient of friction of
0 35 may be used for footings bearing on the compacted fills Resistance due to passive
earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 pounds per
square foot per foot of depth pcf This assumes that the soils around the footings are well
compacted Unless covered by pavements or slabs the passive pressure resistance in the
upper 12 inches of soil should be neglected
We recommend that foundation material over excavation backfill and footing
excavations be observed by a representative from Geolabs prior to placement of reinforcing
steel and concrete to confirm the competency of the foundation bearing materials and the
required embedment depths
Slabs On Grade
We anticipate that concrete slabs on grade will be used for the proposed auto
store building In general we recommend that the subgrade soils below the concrete
slabs on grade be prepared in accordance with the recommendations presented in the Site
Preparation section of this report Where shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of
the subgrade we recommend that the soils be thoroughly moistened to close the cracks or
prepared again Saturation and subsequent yielding of the exposed subgrade due to
GEOLABS INC
W O 4461 00 Page 9
inclement weather and poor drainage may require over excavation of the soft areas and
replacement with well compacted fill
For interior slabs we recommend that a minimum 4 inch thick cushion layer consisting
of free draining materials such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation be
placed below the slab to provide for uniform support of the slab To reduce the potential for
future moisture infiltration and subsequent damage to floor coverings an impervious
moisture vapor barrier such as a plastic membrane is recommended on top of the cushion
layer Two inches of moist fine sand may be considered above the plastic membrane to
provide protection of the membrane and to aid in curing of the slab concrete Provision of
the 2 inch layer of moist fine sand above the vapor barrier should be left up to the discretion
of the project structural engineer
The thickened edges of slabs adjacent to unpaved areas should be embedded at
least 12 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade Areas adjacent to the slab should
be backfilled tightly against the slab with relatively impervious soils on site soils and graded
to divert water away from the slab to reduce the potential for water ponding around the
foundations
Site Preparation
Based on the surface conditions at the site we envision that minimal site grading work
will be required for the proposed project construction In general we recommend that the
following guidelines be used for the site grading work anticipated At the on set of earthwork
the area within the contract grading limits should be thoroughly cleared Vegetation debris
rubbish asphaltic concrete pavements concrete slabs and other unsuitable materials within
the grading limits should be removed and disposed of properly off site
Soft loose and yielding areas encountered during clearing below areas designated
to receive fill or future improvements should be over excavated to expose firm or dense
material and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with well compacted fill The
excavated soft loose and or organic soils should be properly disposed of off site Contract
GEOLABS INC
W O Page 10
documents should include additive and deductive unit prices for over excavation and
compacted fill placement to account for variation in the over excavation quantities
Existing utilities to be abandoned should be removed The resulting excavation
should be properly backfilled with on site fill materials moisture conditioned to above the
optimum moisture content placed in 6 to 8 inch loose lifts and compacted to a minimum of
90 percent relative compaction
The depth of the over excavation below the footings should extend to a depth of at
least 12 inches below the bottom of footing elevation to provide a layer of compacted fill
below the bottom of footings The lateral limits of the over excavation should also extend a
minimum of 12 inches beyond the edges of the footings Prior to placement of the 12 inch
thick compacted fills the subgrade soils should be lightly scarified and compacted to a dense
and unyielding condition The fill materials should be moisture conditioned to above the
optimum moisture content placed in 8 inch maximum loose lifts and compacted to a
minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
In areas to receive fills the subgrade soils should be scarified to a depth of about
8 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to
a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction The subgrades for areas subjected to
vehicular traffic such as pavement areas should be scarified to a depth of about 8 inches
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum
of 95 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of
soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in
accordance with ASTM D 1557 91 test procedures Optimum moisture is the water content
percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
Where shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of the subgrade we recommend
that the soil be thoroughly moistened to close the cracks or scarified and prepared again as
recommended above Saturation and subsequent yielding of the exposed subgrade due to
GEOLABS INC
W O 4461 00 Page 11
inclement weather and poor drainage may require over excavation of the loose soft areas
and replacement with well compacted fill at no additional cost to the owner
If imported fill materials are required for the project construction imported fill materials
should consist of non expansive granular material such as crushed coral basalt or cinder
sand The material should be well graded from coarse to fine with no particles larger than
3 inches in largest dimension In addition the material should contain between 10 and
30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve The material should have a laboratory CBR
value of 20 or more and should have a maximum swell of 1 percent or less Imported fill
materials should be tested by Geolabs for conformance with these recommendations prior
to delivery to the project site for the intended use
Site preparation should be observed by a representative from Geolabs It is important
that a representative from our office observe the site preparation operations to evaluate
whether undesirable materials are encountered during the excavation and scarification
process and whether the exposed soil rock conditions are similar to those encountered in our
field exploration
Corrosion Evaluation
One of the subsurface soil samples retrieved from our field exploration at the site was
subjected to chemical analyses for soluble chloride and sulfate concentrations The chemical
analyses were performed by Environmental Laboratory of the Pacific The results of these
tests are presented on Plate B 4 of Appendix B Minimum resistivity and pH analysis tests
were also conducted to evaluate the potential for corrosion to metallic substructures The
results of these tests are also presented on Plate B 4 of Appendix B Based on a minimum
resistivity of 1 400 ohm cm the on site soils tested may be considered corrosive to buried
metalliic substructures
Based on the test results the soluble chloride and sulfate concentrations in the soil
sample tested were below the limits considered to be corrosive to concrete structures as
shown on Plate B 4 Therefore the on site soils tested may be considered to be generally
GEOLABS INC
W O 4461 00 Page 12
non corrosive to reinforced concrete in contact with the soils However normal precautions
to reduce the potential effects of corrosion such as minimum concrete cover should be
observed for reinforced concrete substructures
Pavement Design
We understand that asphaltic concrete pavements will be required for the parking
areas around the proposed building In general we anticipate that the vehicle loading for the
proposed store will consist primarily of passenger vehicles pick up trucks and occasional
moderately loaded trucks Our laboratory tests performed on the near surface silty sands
encountered beneath the existing pavement appears to have a CBR value of 62 with a swell
value of less than 1 percent Considering the generally light to medium traffic anticipated for
the proposed project we recommend that the following pavement design be used for
preliminary budgeting purposes
Asphaltic Concrete Pavement
2 0 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 0 Inch Aggregate Base Course 95 Percent Relative Compaction
8 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness on Compacted Moist Subgrade
The subgrade soils for the new pavement areas should be scarified to a depth of
about 8 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to not less than 95 percent relative compaction The aggregate base course
should consist of crushed basalt aggregate compacted not less than 95 percent relative
compaction CBR and field density tests should be performed on the actual subgrade soils
encountered during construction to confirm the adequacy of the above section
In general paved areas should be sloped and drainage gradients should be
maintained to carry surface water off the site Surface water ponding should not be allowed
on the site during or after construction Where concrete curbs are used to isolate
landscaping in or adjacent to the pavement areas we recommend that the curbs be
extended a minimum of 2 inches into the subgrade soils below the aggregate base course
to reduce the potential for migration of landscape water into the pavement section

Altematively a subdrain system is recommended to collect the excess water from landscape
irrigation For long term performance we believe that a subdrain system should be
considered adjacent to the paved landscaped areas
Utility Trench
We envision that some new utility lines and utility connections may be required for the
proposed project In general granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel
ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation is recommended under the pipes for support Free draining
granular materials such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation should also
be used for the initial trench backfill up to about 12 inches above the pipes It is critical to use
this free draining material to reduce the potential for formation of voids below the haunches
of pipes and to provide adequate support for the sides of the pipes Improper trench backfill
could result in settlement of the backfill and damage to the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipes to the
top of the subgrade or finished grade may consist of the excavated on site soils The backfill
should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content placed in about
8 inch level loose lifts and mechanically compacted to not less than 90 percent relative
compaction to reduce the potential for appreciable future ground subsidence Where
trenches are below pavement areas the upper 3 feet of the trench backfill below the
pavement grade should be compacted to not less than 95 percent relative compaction
Drainage
The finished grades outside the building should be sloped to shed water away from
the foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also advised that gutter systems
be installed around the building and that the discharge be diverted away from the foundation
and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near the foundations and slabs should
be avoided Planters next to foundations should also be avoided or have concrete bottoms
and drains to reduce the potential for water infiltration into the subsoils
GEOLABS INC
W O 4461 00 Page 14
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the above
foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation and
subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations should be properly
backfilled against the walls or slab edges immediately after setting of the concrete to reduce
the potential for water infiltration
In addition drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and should be
maintained to drain surface water runoff away from the foundations and slabs

Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling four
borings extending to depths of about 11 5 to 16 5 feet below the existing ground surface at
the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were drilled using
a truck mounted drill rig equipped with continuous flight hollow stem augers
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer who monitored the drilling operations on a near
continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by testing in the
laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil Classification
System as shown on Plate A Graphic representations of the materials encountered are
presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 4
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 3550 84 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 3 inch
OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches In addition
some samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a
2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same hammer and drop The blow counts
needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown as
the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content ASTM D 2216 and unit weight ASTM D 2937 determinations were
performed on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil
properties The results of these tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the
appropriate sample depths
Two sieve analysis tests ASTM C 136 D 1140 were performed on selected
samples to determine the gradation characteristics of the soils and to aid in soil classification
Graphic presentation of the grain size distribution is provided on Plate B 1
One laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR test ASTM D 1883 was performed
on a bulk sample of the near surface soils to evaluate the pavement support characteristics
of the soils The results of the test are presented on Plate B 2
One Modified Proctor compaction test ASTM D 1557 was performed on a bulk
sample of the near surface soils to evaluate the moisture content and dry density
relationships The results of the test are presented on Plate B 3
One set of chemical analysis tests including Chlorides EPA Test Method 325 2 and
Sulfates EPA Test Method 375 2 was performed on a selected sample of the soils to
evaluate their corrosion potential The chemical analyses were performed by Environmental
Laboratory of the Pacific Results of the tests are summarized on Plate B 4
One set of corrosion evaluation tests including pH ASTM D 2976 and minimum
resistivity ASTM G 57 was performed on a selected sample of the soils Results of the
tests are summarized on Plate B 4
