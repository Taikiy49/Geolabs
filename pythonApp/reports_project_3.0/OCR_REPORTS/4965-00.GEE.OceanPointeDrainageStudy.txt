In accordance with our proposal dated we have performed a
geotechnical engineering exploration in support of the Ocean Pointe Drainage Study

intended for preliminary design only and are subject to the limitations noted at the end of
this letter report

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our geotechnical engineering exploration was to obtain an overview
of the surface and subsurface conditions at the site The subsurface information obtained
was utilized to develop a soil rock data set for the formulation of geotechnical engineering
recommendations pertaining to the preliminary design of disposal methods for storm water
runoff To accomplish this we conducted an exploration program consisting of the
following tasks and work efforts
1 Research and review of available in house soils and geologic information
pertaining to the project site and its vicinity
2 Mobilization and demobilization of a trailer mounted drill rig a water truck
and two operators to and from the project site
3 Drilling and sampling of three borings one boring at each site extending to
depths of about 48 to 58 feet below the existing ground surface
4 Coordination of the field exploration and logging of the borings by a field
engineer from our firm
5 Performing a total of nine permeability tests three permeability tests in each
boring at various depths to evaluate the infiltration characteristics of the
subsurface soils encountered at the site
6 Laboratory testing of selected samples obtained during the field exploration
as an aid in classifying the materials encountered and evaluating their
engineering properties
7 Analyses of field and laboratory data for the formulation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertaining to the preliminary design for the
project
8 Preparation of a letter report summarizing our work on the project and
presenting our findings and recommendations
9 Coordination of our overall work on the project by an engineer from our firm
10 Quality assurance of our work and client design team consultation by a
principal engineer from our firm
11 Miscellaneous work efforts such as drafting word processing and clerical
support

EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is located within the Ocean Pointe development in Ewa Beach in
the District of Ewa on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project site is bordered by Fort
Weaver Road to the east Papipi Road to the south and an existing channel to the west
as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Site No 1 is located to the northwest of the intersection between Fort Weaver Road
and Hanakahi Street Based on our observations and the available topographic survey
the existing ground surface at Area No 1 is relatively level with elevations ranging from
about 20 to 24 feet Mean Sea Level MSL At the time of our exploration several
pieces of heavy construction equipment were near the vicinity of Site No 1 In addition
the vegetation observed at the location was minimal
Site No 2 is located at the bottom of an existing basin Based on our observations
and the available topographic survey the existing ground surface is relatively level with
elevations ranging from about 2 to 3 feet MSL The bottom of the basin is about 15 feet
below the top of the basin Shrubs and grass were observed within the basin The
exposed bottom of the basin consisted of tannish white to white coral formation
Site No 3 is located at the northeast end of Papipi Drive Based on our
observations and the available topographic survey the existing ground surface is relatively
level with elevations ranging from about 5 to 8 feet MSL At the time of our exploration
trees shrubs and grass were observed Access to this area may be made through a
chain link fence gate located on Papipi Drive
FIELD EXPLORATION
Our field exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling three borings
designated as Boring Nos 1 through 3 extending to depths of about 48 to 58 feet below
the existing ground surface Boring Nos 1 through 3 were drilled at Site Nos 1 through 3
respectively The borings were drilled using a truck mounted drill rig equipped with
continuous flight solid stem augers and rotary coring tools The approximate locations of
the borings drilled at the site are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on the Boring Log Legend Plate 3 Graphic
representations of the materials encountered are provided on the Logs of Borings
Plates 4 through 6

LABORATORY TESTING
Selected soil samples from the field exploration were tested in the laboratory with
an emphasis on evaluating the moisture and gradation characteristics The following
laboratory tests were performed on selected soil samples obtained from the field
exploration
Moisture content ASTM D 2216 determinations were performed on selected
samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties The test results
are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Six sieve analysis tests were performed on selected samples of the soils to
evaluate the gradation characteristics and to aid in soil classification The tests were
performed in accordance with ASTM Test Designations C 117 and C 136 Graphic
presentation of the grain size distribution is provided on Plates 7 and 8
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our field exploration at Site No 1 generally encountered a surface fill layer which
consisted of medium dense silty sand of about 1 foot in thickness Underlying the surface
fill layer were medium hard moderately fractured coral formation and very dense coralline
sandstone formation extending to a depth of about 23 feet below the existing ground
surface The coral formation and coralline sandstone formation were underlain by soft to
medium hard severely fractured coral formation with pockets of silty clay extending to the
maximum depth explored of about 58 feet below the existing ground surface
Groundwater was encountered at depths ranging between about 19 5 and 19 8 feet below
the existing ground surface at the time of our exploration
Our field exploration at Site No 2 generally encountered soft severely fractured
coralline detritus extending to a depth of about 20 feet below the existing ground surface
Below the coralline detritus were medium hard coralline limestone formation soft to
medium hard coral and very dense coralline sandstone formation extending to the
maximum depth explored of about 48 feet below the existing ground surface The coral
and coralline limestone formations were generally severely fractured Groundwater was
encountered at depths ranging between about 1 and 1 2 feet below the existing ground
surface at the time of our exploration

FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the information provided we understand that it is planned to conduct a
drainage study at the Ocean Pointe development area This study includes the evaluation
of the infiltration characteristics of the subsurface materials to determine possible disposal
methods for storm water runoff The disposal methods may include seepage trenches that
will be excavated to about the groundwater level or drywells injection wells that may
extend to depths ranging between 20 and 40 feet MSL
Our field exploration at the three sites that are being considered generally
encountered a surface layer of fill material about 1 foot in thickness underlain by severely
fractured coral coralline limestone and sandstone and coralline detritus At the time of
our exploration the groundwater levels ranged between about 1 and 19 8 feet below the
existing ground surface Based on the permeability tests conducted in the borings the
subsurface materials encountered at the project site may be classified as moderately to
highly permeable Detailed discussions of our geotechnical engineering recommendations
for preliminary design of disposal methods for storm water runoff at the project site are
presented in the following sections of this report

LIMITATIONS
The findings and discussions submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from the field borings Variations of subsurface conditions between
and beyond the borings may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not
become evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be
necessary to re evaluate the findings and discussions provided in this letter report
The locations of the field borings indicated in this report are approximate having
been estimated by taping and pacing from the features shown on the site plan provided by
R M Towill Corporation on The approximate elevations of field
borings were estimated from the contour lines shown on the same plan and from
information provided by R M Towill Corporation The physical locations and elevations of
the borings should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods
used
The stratification lines shown on graphic representations of the borings depict the
approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual transition
As previously mentioned groundwater was encountered in our borings at the time of our
exploration However it must be noted that groundwater level changes may occur due to
tidal fluctuations seasonal precipitation surface runoff and other factors
