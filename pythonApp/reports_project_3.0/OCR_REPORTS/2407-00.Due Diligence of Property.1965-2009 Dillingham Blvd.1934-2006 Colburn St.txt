INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our preliminary geotechnical
engineering exploration performed at the subject properties on Dillingham
Boulevard and Colburn Street in the Kalihi Kai area of Honolulu on the
island of Oahu Hawaii The general location of the project site is shown
on the Project Location Map Plate 1

This report summarizes the findings of our field exploration and
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from analyses of the
exploration data obtained These recommendations are intended to provide
input to the due diligence exploration for the acquisition of a ground
lease of the subject properties and they are not intended for design
purpose
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to acquire the ground lease of six 6 contiguous
parcels of land for the construction of a warehouse or 3 story office
building development
The six 6 properties total an approximate land area of 28 950
square feet located between Dillingham Boulevard and Colburn Street in
Kalihi Kai

In general the two 2 drilled borings encountered competent
subsoils which consist of an approximately 4 foot thick fill crust of

In Boring No 1 which was drilled near Colburn Street on the makai

field exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on the available boring data it is our opinion that the
subsoils at the project site are generally competent to provide support
for shallow spread footing foundations for a warehouse or 3 story
structure The allowable bearing value for the footing design depends on
the final column loads and uniformity of the underlying subsoils within
the project site
laboratory tests generally indicate that the upper fill crust is
sandy but the underlying clay layers below the surface fill crust are
moderately to highly expansive when saturated This may create ground
heaving problem for surface pavements and on grade slabs if the overlying
fill crust is removed or absent at part of the project area
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2407 00 Page 5
Therefore we recommend that further field exploration should be
performed to verify the presence thickness and consistency of the surface
fill crust and also the depth to underlying expansive clay layers for
later design purpose
Site grading involving cuts below the existing ground surface is
generally not recommended since it will remove part of the existing fill
crust which is serving as a capping fill layer over the underlying
expansive subsoils
Geotechnical Site Evaluation
Foundation
From the foundation engineering point of view it is our opinion that
the project site is suitable for the proposed warehouse or office
building construction
Based on our engineering analysis of the preliminary boring data we
believe that shallow spread or continuous wall footing foundations
may be used to support the proposed structure
The footing foundations should be adequately embedded on the upper
fill crust yet placed above the underlying expensive clay layer The
footing bearing layer of generally loose to medium dense sandy fill
should be proof rolled or recompacted properly prior to footing
placement or construction
Site Grading
Minimal grading is anticipated for the proposed development due to
the existing level grade at the project site Generally site
cutting is not recommended when it involves substantial removal of
the generally sandy surface fill crust overlying the underlying
expansive clays encountered at about four 4 foot depth below the
existing ground
W O 2407 00 Page 6
If the fill crust is removed or not present in some of the project
area the site grading concept may need to be reviewed since the
underlying swelling clay layers may create ground heaving on the
surface slab and pavement when it is wetted or saturated
Additional Exploration
It is recommended that additional soil exploration with sufficient
number of borings to cover the development site should be performed to
verify the site soil conditions for later design use
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from two 2 short borings within an
accessible part of the site Variations of conditions between the borings
and at other areas may occur and the nature and extent of these
variations may not become evident until later exploration work or during
construction If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to
re evaluate the recommendations given in this report
The locations of the borings in the project site were determined by
taping from the existing structures or property lines as indicated on the
Site Plan of the project site derived from a copy of the tax map key TMK
provided by Hawaiian Host Candies The physical locations of the
borings should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the
method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
borings depict the approximate boundaries between soil types and as such
may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on any profiles
or cross sections are based upon interpolation between borings and may not
represent actual subsurface conditions
Water level data from the borings were collected at the times given
on the graphic representations and or in the text of this report These
