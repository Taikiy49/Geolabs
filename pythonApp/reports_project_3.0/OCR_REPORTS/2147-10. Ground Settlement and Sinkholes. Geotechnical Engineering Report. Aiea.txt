EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is located in the upper portion of Aiea Heights
between Puliki Place and Hoapono Place The upper portion of the lot
where the house is located is relatively flat The original house was
constructed of brick and wood on a slab on grade An extension enclosed
lanai consisting primarily of wood amd screen construction on a
slab on grade was noted adjacent to the downslope side of the house
From the edge of the extension the ground surface slopes down towards
Hoapono Place which is about 25 to 30 feet lower than the house There
are two sets of CRM retaining walls along this sloping area
Large cracks were noted in the ground in the area between the house
extension and the first 5 foot high retaining wall Visual observations
indicate that the ground in this area has experienced some settlement and
is presently starting to undermine the extension slab Some small amounts

FIELD EXPLORATION
Two 2 borings were drilled and sampled at the project site to
obtain subsurface soil data pertinent to the observed earth movements
Boring No 1 which was drilled closer to the house extension
encountered about six 6 feet of fill material consisting of reddish
brown clay overlying residual soils also consisting of reddish brown
clay The consistency of the fill material was in the range of soft to
medium stiff and generally indicates that the material was not compacted
By comparison the underlying residual natural soil had a consistency in
the range of very stiff
Boring No 2 drilled below the first retaining wall encountered a
thin layer of fill about 1 5 feet thick underlain by residual soils
The soils encountered in this boring had a very stiff consistency

Laboratory tests were performed on selected samples obtained from the
borings to aid in classifying the materials determining their
shrink swell potential and to obtain an estimate of the degree of
compaction The results of these tests are attached
DISCUSSIONS
Based on the field and laboratory data the upper fill soils
encountered in Boring No 1 are of a soft to medium stiff consistency and
may be prone to piping washing out through cracks or other openings in
the wall The laboratory tests indicate that the soils encountered have a
moderate expansion potential It appears that the main cause of the earth
movement and sinkholes is erosion of the uncompacted fill soils through
the retaining wall

Therefore we recommend that the poor quality fill be over excavated
and recompacted as shown on the attached Plate 4 Backfill should be
placed in horizontal lifts with a maximm thickness of eight 8 inches
and then be compacted to a minimm of 90 of its maximum dry density per
ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 The backfill immediately behind the retaining
wall should be a relatively granular non expansive select material to aid
in the drainage of the wall and to reduce the amount of compactive effort
required to attain the desired degree of compaction A filter fabric or
geotextile such as Mirafi 140N should be placed as shown on Plate 4 to
reduce the loss of soil fines by piping
The condition of the existing retaining wall is uncertain at this
time Although there are some cracks visible the wall appears intact
During the above repair work the condition of the wall should be
carefully observed and necessary repairs made i e reset and grout loose
rocks add weepholes etc
The amount of water flowing over this area should be reduced by the
installation of gutters and diversion of surface flow away from the area

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the
earthwork and foundation phases of the work This is to observe
compliance with the design concepts specifications or recommendations and
to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ
from that anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations
given in this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as visual
observations and borings Variations of conditions between the field
data points may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may
not become evident until construction If variations then appear
evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations given in
this report
The location of the field data points were approximately determined
by estimation The physical location of the field data points should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the method used

This report has been prepared solely for the purpose of assisting
the owner or engineer architect in the preliminary design evaluation of

geotechnical engineer to assist in the interpretation of this report
and or in the performance of additional site specific exploration for bid
estimating purposes
The owner client should be aware that unanticipated soil conditions
are commonly encountered Unforeseen soil conditions such as perched
groundwater soft deposits hard layers or cavities may occur in
localized areas and may required additional probing or corrections in the
field which may result in construction delays to attain a properly
constructed project Therefore sufficient contingency fund is thus
recommended to accommodate these possible extra costs

Field Exploration

sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the Boring Logs at the appropriate
sample depths
