As requested we have made a study of the near surface soil conditions below
the above referenced lot The purpose of the study was to evaluate the
suitability of the near surface soils as foundation support for the proposed
house The study consisted of three 3 borings drilled within the proposed
building site laboratory soil tests and a review of the grading and
compaction test record on the building site This report summarizes our
observations and recommendations
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS

structure Due to the sloping terrain at the site a lower level garage cut
into the hillside with an upper level living area are planned

EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
Lot 42 is located in the central portion of the subdivision development on the
western side of Kahena Street The lot is bounded by Kahena Street on the
northeast subdivision lots on the north and south and the natural valley side
slope on the west An existing concrete drainage ditch is located on the
southwestern upper portion of the lot The lot generally slopes upward from
Kahena Street toward the southwest at about 22 percent gradient The elevation
of the lot generally varies from about 206 to 280 feet

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
A review of the grading compaction test record and the site reconnaissance
indicate that Lot 42 is underlain by a layer of grayish brown expansive clay
more than 5 feet deep
The remaining upper portion of the lot beyond the house area is underlain by
natural colluvial deposits transported from the upper hillside area These
deposits are typically heterogenous and vary from clayey silt low expansion
potential to clay high expansion potential

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that the lot is underlain by a grayish brown
adobe clay The laboratory test results on this material generally indicates
that they have high expansion potential Therefore for Lot 42 the
slab on grade and footing foundation construction would require special design
procedures in order to minimize the detrimental shrink swell effects of the
in situ soils
House foundation
On grade slab construction will be used for the driveway garage and partial
basement with post and beam construction under the rear portion of the upper
floor level
Slab on grade
The in situ clay soils have a high swell potential and are
therefore unsuitable as slab subgrade soils
In order to reduce the expansive effects of the clay they should
be over excavated down for a depth of 3 feet below the slab
subgrade and then replaced with non expansive soils such as
select borrow crusher waste or coral This over excavation and
replacement should also be extended for a horizontal distance of 3
feet beyond the building foundations

Fills should generally be less than 6 inches in diameter placed in
maximum 6 inch loose lifts and compacted to a minimum of 902 of
its maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78
For slab on grade bearing on the non expansive fill an allowable
bearing capacity of 3000 p s f may be used In addition we also
recommend that the perimeter footings have a minimum embeddment of
8 inches measured from the lowest adjacent grade to the bottom of
the footing
It is recommended that slab on grade be placed over a 4 inch layer
of well graded base course aggregate and should be separated from
wall foundations
A plastic membrane should be placed over the base course layer if a
moisture barrier is deemed necessary to protect the floor covering
eee
Due to the expansive clay soils the sloping terrain the effects
of down hill creep and to develop the full bearing capacity we
recommend that for post and beam construction the footings be
embedded a depth of 3 feet measured from the lowest adjacent grade
to the bottom of the footing The footings should be poured in
place and the excavation should be backfilled and compacted with
impervious material

An allowable bearing value of 3000 p s f can be used for footings
bearing on the stiff undisturbed soil The bottom of the footing
excavation should be neat and free of loose soils or debris
If a footing is located next to a utility trench it should extend
to the bottom of the utility trench to reduce footing settlement
due to poor trench backfill
The ground surface should be shaped to divert runoff away from all
foundations
Concrete driveway and sidewalks
A layer of expansive soils more than 5 feet thick was encountered in the
driveway area Since the driveway can tolerate some amount of heaving the
clay overexcavation and replacement as described above can be reduced to 2 feet
below the driveway subgrade level Where 2 or more feet of fill is planned
select non expansive borrow material should be used to cap over the expansive
clay Where less than 2 feet of fill is planned overexcavation and
replacement of the expansive clay with select non expansive material is
required The above capping requirements should be followed for a minimum
distance of 3 feet beyond the driveway
Joints are recommended at about 8 to 10 foot intervals and at discontinuities
in the driveway in order to allow for some relative movement of the concrete

For future sidewalks capping of the clay or overexcavation and replacement
similar to the driveway is recommended to reduce heaving caused by the
expansive clay soil
Site grading
The existing terrain at the site generally slopes upward at about 22 percent
gradient
Site grading will consist mostly of cutting to provide sufficient level area
for the first floor level Minor filling is anticipated
The area to be filled should be cleared of vegetation and debris prior to
filling
Fills and backfills should be select non expansive borrow material placed in
maximum 8 inch lifts and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry
density established by ASTM D 1557 78 The on site clay soil should not be
used for fill material below structures and behind retaining walls
Cut and fill slope ratios of 2 horizontal to 1 vertical are recommended
The finished grade outside the slabs pavements and foundations should be
shaped to avoid ponding conditions and to shed water away from the structures

Construction monitoring
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during earthwork
construction to monitor the contractor s grading operations particularly the
fill placement and slope cuts
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be observed by a soils
engineer during construction to see that adequate bearing has been obtained and
that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of our
subsurface investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and approved by
the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the above
inspections
