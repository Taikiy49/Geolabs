INTRODUCTION
In accordance with your request we have performed a preliminary
geologic reconnaissance of the above referenced site This report
presents our preliminary findings and recommendations
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our reconnaissance was to make observations of the
general surface and shallow subsurface soil conditions at the project site
in order to obtain data to aid in evaluating the developability of the
site from a geotechnical engineering viewpoint
The reconnaissance consisted of the following tasks and work efforts
1
A review of available soils and geologic maps of the site and its
vicinity
A reveiw of available soils and geologic literature pertinent to
the site and its vicinity
A general geologic reconnaissance of the vicinity of the project
site
An on ground reconnaissance of the site performed by a Project
Geologist and a Field Assistant from our firm
Preparation of this report
Miscellaneous work efforts such as drafting and clerical support

GENERAL SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located in the Kokokahi area of Kaneohe on the
island of Oahu Hawaii The site is situated to the north of Kaneohe Bay
Drive and is bordered to the north east and west by raw undeveloped land
and by Kaneohe Bay Drive to the south The topography of the site is
rolling and generally drains towards Kawa Stream to the north of the
site Except for an existing residence in the northern portion of the
site and an asphalt driveway crossing the center of the site most of the
project site is densely vegetated with christmas berry java plum
brassia California grass and Job s Tears
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to develop the site into a 32 lot residential
subdivision The proposed development is still in the planning phase
therefore no detailed plans of the proposed subdivision grading are
available However it is desired to have some preliminary geotechnical
engineering input for use in land use and zoning proceedings
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Visual examination of the project site and its environs including
nearby road cuts leads to the conjecture that most of the subsurface
conditions underlying the site are relatively uniform
Based on the observations made during our preliminary reconnaissance
it appears that most of the site is underlain by stiff brownish red clayey
soils with relatively low expansion potential
Along the western boundary of the site our hand auger probe
encountered soft brownish gray to gray organic silt soils to the maximum
depth probed of ten 10 feet below the existing ground surface This
condition appears to be limited to the lowlying nearly level area to the
west of and below the existing residence

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our reconnaissance of the area our preliminary findings and
recommendations are presented below
General
Our reconnaissance indicates that the site is suitable for the
proposed development from a geotechnical engineering viewpoint
The stiff clayey silt soils observed throughout most of the project
site are generally competent and should provide adequate bearing for the
proposed development
Site Grading
In general it is our opinion that conventional grading methods may
be used for the site and roadway grading of the proposed development
Prior to commencement of grading surface vegetation and organic
matter should be cleared and grubbed The resulting spoils should be
disposed of properly off site or stockpiled for use in landscaping
The soft soils encountered along the westernmost boundary of the site
generally have relatively low strength Additional information regarding
the proposed site grading in this area and additional subsurface
exploration are needed before recommendations for grading and site work
for this small portion of the site can be developed

Soft or yielding areas encountered during clearing and grubbing
should be over excavated to firm natural material and the resulting
depression backfilled with well compacted fill
Any existing miscellaneous rubbish fill should be removed and wasted
off site
In general the excavated soil materials may be suitable as a source
of fill material Due to the wet climatic conditions in the vicinity of
the project site and the anticipated high in situ moisture contents of
native soils in the vicinity of the site the customary practice is to
reduce the compaction standard to 85 of the maximm dry density per ASIM
Test Method D 1557 78 as opposed to the 90 standard used in drier parts
of the island This reduced compaction level facilitates earthwork
progress by reducing the amount of aeration and handling required to bring
the soil to a lower more workable content
Generally fills should be placed in level lifts and compacted to a
minimum of 85 maximum dry density as established by ASTM D 1557 78 All
fills to be placed on slopes having ratios steeper than five horizontal to
one vertical 5H 1V should be keyed and benched into the underlying stiff
soils to lessen potential instability of the new fill The keys and
benches should not be based on loose soil
Excavation
It is anticipated that most of the soil materials at the site may be
conventionally excavated Some perched groundwater or seepage may be
encountered in excavations
Slopes
In general for the design of cut and fill slopes a ratio of two
horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V may be utilized Additional
exploration and analyses are required for the design of slopes in the
lowlying area along the westernmost boundary of the site

Additional Exploration
The findings and recommendations presented herein are based upon
visual observations made at the site and its vicinity during a preliminary
geologic reconnaissance We strongly recommend that we be allowed to
perform additional geotechnical exploration work to confirm the findings
of our reconnaissance This additional exploration should consist at a
minimm of a series of borings The recommendations presented herein are
contingent upon such additional exploration
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field observations Variations of
conditions may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may
not become evident until construction If variations then appear evident
it will be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations given in this
report
