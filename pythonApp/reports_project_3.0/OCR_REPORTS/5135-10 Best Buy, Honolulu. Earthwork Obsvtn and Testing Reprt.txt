PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located to the northeast of the intersection of Alakawa Street and
Nimitz Highway in the Iwilei area of Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The property
is approximately triangular in shape and encompasses approximately 3 67 acres in land
area The project site is located within the proximity of the existing Nimitz Center to the
west and the Home Depot and Costco stores across Alakawa Street
We understand that the new Best Buy retail store is a one story structure with
rooftop parking covering approximately 50 000 square feet in floor area In addition a new
parking lot and loading zones subjected to container truck traffic are planned to serve the
new retail development The layout of the new retail development is shown on the
Site Plan Plate 1
Based on the information provided the Best Buy retail store building will consist of
concrete tilt up panels with concrete slab on grade construction According to the project
structural engineer the typical interior column loads are estimated to be on the order of
about 250 kips per column with the wall loads on the order of about 7 kips per lineal foot
Due to the presence of soft silty clays and loose silty coralline sands and gravel

SETTLEMENT MONITORING PROGRAM
As previously mentioned new fills up to about 3 feet thick was required to raise the
existing ground surface at the project site to achieve the design finished floor elevation for
the new retail facility As previously indicated soft silty clays and loose silty coralline
sands and gravel were anticipated at depths of about 3 to 23 feet below the existing
ground surface Therefore some ground settlements would occur when the new fills of up
to about 3 feet thick were placed over the potentially compressible soils
A settlement monitoring program was established to evaluate the magnitude and
rate of the estimated settlements during the settlement waiting period prior to the
construction of the building structure To monitor the actual settlement rate five settlement
gauges were installed at selected locations across the building footprints on
October 13 and 15 2004 prior to the fill placement operations The settlement gauges
were read optically on a periodic basis by Jayar Construction Inc and the readings were
transmitted to our office for review
In general non expansive select granular fill material was used as a source of
structural fill materials to raise the existing ground to the finish grade of the building pad
The settlement waiting period commenced on and terminated on
Filled ground settlements up to about 2 5 inches were recorded
based on the settlement gauge readings provided by Jayar Construction Inc
EARTHWORK OPERATIONS
The site grading operations pavement and utility construction for the Best Buy
Honolulu project were performed by Jayar Construction Inc The site grading work
consisted of subgrade preparation placement and compaction of fills and backfills
pavement construction and utility trench excavation and backfill
Following the demolition and clearing operations the excavated subgrades
underneath future slab and pavement areas were scarified to a depth of about 8 inches
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and proof rolled with a
minimum 10 ton vibratory drum roller to a minimum of 90 and 95 percent relative
compaction under the building slab and pavement areas respectively of the maximum dry

Several abandoned underground utilities and structure were encountered during
the earthwork operations at the project site The 8 inch fire water line was capped and
grouted with flowable fill while the 2 5 inch water and gas lines were capped and
abandoned in place In addition the existing cesspool was backfilled with flowable fill up
to about 2 feet below the existing ground and capped with on site soils compacted to a
firm and unyielding surface
Soft soils were encountered to a depth of about 2 feet below the existing ground
surface at the loading dock area for the project Since the loading dock area will be
subjected to container truck traffic the soft soils were removed to a depth of 2 feet below
the existing ground surface A layer of geotextile fabric such as Mirafi 500X or equivalent
was placed at the bottom of the over excavation prior to backfilling with select borrow
material compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction The top of the select
borrow was compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction prior to the
placement of the 6 inch base course layer The amount of over excavation was extended
to about 17 to 20 feet out from the building wall For the retaining wall along the ramp the
soft soils were over excavated to a depth of 2 feet below the footing bottom and replaced
with select borrow wrapped around with a filter fabric such as Mirafi 180N or equivalent
The select borrow subbase was compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction
Fill and backfill materials required for the project were generally placed in near level
lifts brought to above the optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of 90 or
95 percent under the building slab and pavement areas respectively of the maximum dry
density determined in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor
During the site grading operations samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor
and the test results are summarized on Plate 2 of this report
Field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations on an
as need basis to confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures
and soil conditions were noted A total of 160 field density tests including retests of failed
areas were performed using the nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM Test
Designations D 2922 and D3017 Results of the compaction tests performed are
summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 11 of this report These tests were performed on the
prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills at the site Test locations
and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes
These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied
