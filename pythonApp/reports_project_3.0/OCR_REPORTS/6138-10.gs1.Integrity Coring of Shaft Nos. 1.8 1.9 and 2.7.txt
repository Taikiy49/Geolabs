This memorandum provides the results of the integrity coring performed on
Shaft Nos. 1.8, 1.9 and 2.7 for the above project. Installation of Shaft Nos. 1.8, 1.9 and
2.7 were completed on December 21, 2023, January 16, 2024, and January 25, 2024,
respectively. Based on the Crosshole Sonic Logging test results, three integrity coring
for Shaft No. 1.8 between Access Tube Nos. 1 and 2, 2 and 3, and 2 and 4, extending
to minimum depths of 5 and 10 feet below the drilled shaft cutoff elevation, was
recommended. In addition, one integrity coring for Shaft No. 1.9 between Access Tube
Nos. 3 and 4, extending to a minimum depth of 4 feet below the drilled shaft cutoff
elevation, was recommended. Furthermore, one integrity coring for Shaft No. 2.7
between Access Tube Nos. 1 and 4, extending to a minimum depth of 3 feet below the
drilled shaft cutoff elevation, was recommended. The design 28-day concrete
compressive strength for the drilled shaft is 4,500 pounds per square inch (psi).
Shaft Nos. 1.8, 1.9 and 2.7 were cored between March 4 and 7, 2024. The coring
was performed by Valley Well Drilling. We understand that the existing top of shaft
elevations are +4.92, +5.17 and +5.4 feet Mean Sea Level (MSL) for Shaft Nos. 1.8, 1.9
and 2.7, respectively. In addition, the shaft cutoff elevation is -0.52 feet MSL. Therefore,
the shaft cutoff elevations are about 5.42, 5.67 and 5.9 feet below the existing top of the
shaft concrete elevations for Shaft Nos. 1.8, 1.9 and 2.7, respectively.
Based on visual observations of the recovered cores, no significant defects such as
voids or soil intrusion were observed, with the exception of Shaft No. 1.8, Core 2-3, where
a small piece of concrete was missing from the side of the core and Shaft No. 1.9,
Core 3-4, where air voids up to about 0.5 inches were observed. The small piece of
concrete missing from the side of the core may have been caused by the sampling
procedure. The coring logs are presented on Plates A-1 through A-5. In addition,
photographs of the recovered concrete core samples are presented on Plates B-1 through
B-5.
We performed Uniaxial Compressive Strength tests on 16 recovered core samples
from Shaft Nos. 1.8, 1.9 and 2.7. The compressive strength tests were conducted on
March 13, 2024, corresponding to 83, 57 and 41 days after shaft concrete placement for
Shaft Nos. 1.8, 1.9, and 2.7, respectively.

Based on the compression test results, concrete compressive strength below the 
cutoff elevation of Core 1-2, 2-3 and 2-4 for Shaft No. 1.8 ranged from 3,610 to 5,440 psi. 
The average compressive strength for Core 1-2, 2-3 and 2-4 is 4,521 psi. The lowest 
compressive strength for Core 1-2, 2-3 and 2-4 was 3,610 psi. For Shaft No. 1.9, concrete 
compressive strength below the cutoff elevation of Core 3-4 ranged from 5,080 to 7,320 
psi. The average compressive strength for Core 3-4 is 6,400 psi. The lowest compressive 
strength for Core 3-4 was 5,080 psi. For Shaft No. 2.7, concrete compressive strength 
below the cutoff elevation of Core 1-4 ranged from 4,880 to 7,080 psi. The average 
compressive strength for Core 1-4 is 5,980 psi. The lowest compressive strength for Core 
1-4 was 4,880 psi. 
Based on ACI 318, the average compressive strength of cores should be greater 
than or equal to 85 percent of the design compressive strength. In addition, no 
compressive strength of the cores should be less than 75 percent of the design 
compressive strength. For the design compressive strength of 4,500 psi, 85 and 
75 percent of the design compressive strength is 3,825 psi and 3,375 psi, respectively. 
Based on the test results for Shaft No. 1.8, 1.9 and 2.7, the average compressive 
strength of the cores below the shaft cutoff elevation meets the minimum 3,825 psi 
requirement. The lowest break cores below the shaft cutoff elevation meet the minimum 
3,375 psi requirement. Therefore, the shaft concrete tested for Shaft Nos. 1.8, 1.9 and 2.7 
meets the project requirements.
