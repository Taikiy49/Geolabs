INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of a preliminary geotechnical
engineering exploration performed for the subject project
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1764 00 Page 2
Dk No 25
The proposed Royal Hawaiian Country Club in Maunawili includes a
private access road golf courses club house structures and a roadway
bridge This preliminary study covers only the clearing and grading for
a new private access road from near Kalanianacle Highway towards the
proposed mauka golf course
The purpose of this study was to carry out surface geologic
reconnaissance and to explore the subsurface soil and groundwater
conditions at the site to provide general subsurface soil data for site
grading design utility line excavation and recommendations for roadway
embankment and pavement design
The approximate location of the project is shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1
The results of our preliminary field exploration and laboratory
tests which we used in the engineering analysis to formulate our
recommendations are presented in the appendices of this report

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site consists of a narrow strip of land about 6000 feet
long located about 50 to 400 feet east of Maunawili Stream from Auloa
Road into Maunawili Valley and along the lower western foothill of
Olomana Ridge
In general the topography slopes down towards the stream channels
about 24 to 72 feet along the northern portion of the alignment to 160
feet above Mean Sea Level near the south end of the site
The area is generally covered with thick vegetation including Hao
and bamboo trees Many large surface boulders were visible particularly
between approximately Stations 9 00 to 23 00 of the road alignment
The project site is located in a high rainfall area and the ground
was wet during the field exploration period Groundwater seepage was
observed in a pasture area downslope of Stations 39 00 to 41400 during
the field exploration in November 1986
Tilted trees which may indicate the occurrence of surficial soil
creep were observed upslope of Stations 28 00 to 30 00 and slip scars
were observed on the southward facing natural slope approximately 600
feet east of Station 45 00

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Field Exploration
The preliminary field exploration consisted of surface geologic
reconnaissance five 5 drilled borings to depths of 10 to 65 feet and
six 6 auger probes hand driven to depths of 5 to 13 feet below the
existing ground surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site
Plans Plates 2 and 3
Piezometers were installed at Boring Nos 4 and 5 to monitor the
fluctuations in groundwater levels in this major cut slope area
Due to the presence of thick vegetation and the wet and muddy ground
conditions the terrain in much of the area was not accessible to dozer
and truck mounted drill rig Therefore a detailed field exploration
program was not considered practicable at this time It is our
understanding that additional borings and testing may be carried out if
required after the initial site grading and clearance
The borings and probes were located primarily in the major road cut
Slopes and fill embankment areas
Detailed descriptions of the soils encountered in the borings and
probings are shown on the Boring and Probing Logs in Appendix A
Soil Conditions
Based on the preliminary field exploration the geologic soil types
along the private access road alignment may be generalized into three
distinct groups as follows
Recent Alluvium Recent alluvial deposits were encountered mainly
in lower areas along existing drainageways This alluvium primarily
consisted of medium stiff to very soft brown clayey silt
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1764 00 Page 5
Dk No 25
Older Alluvium Weathered Colluvium Weathered older alluvial and
colluvial deposits were encountered mainly along the southern half
of the proposed access road The alluvium generally consisted of
medium stiff to stiff clayey silt to silty clay soils with some
cobbles and boulders
Colluvium is slope wash or previous landslide debris and locally
inter mixed with the older alluvium It generally consisted of
unweathered to extremely weathered basalt cobbles and boulders in a
medium stiff to stiff Clayey silt matrix Tocally soft and wet
zones were encountered within the deposits
Residual Soil Residual soil to extremely weathered rock derived
mainly from basaltic rocks were encountered in portions of the
site such as mauka of approximate Stations 11 00 to 21 00 The
weathered materials were primarily stiff Clayey silt with some
gravel
Groundwater was encountered in Boring Nos 1 and 2 at about 3 to 1 4
feet below the existing ground level at about elevation 23 feet and
also in Boring Nos 4 and 5 at about 33 4 and 63 5 feet below existing
ground level at about elevations 99 6 and 109 5 feet respectively
Rises in groundwater levels are anticipated during and after periods of
heavy rainfall
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on field exploration and laboratory tests performed and from a
soil engineering point of view the site is in general suitable for the
proposed access road development but some areas may require special
design considerations and construction procedures

The preliminary subsurface exploration generally revealed that most
of the proposed access road alignment is underlain primarily by clayey
silts which are stiff alluvium colluvium and residual soil
Some very soft soils and high water levels 1 4 to 3 0 feet below
existing ground were noted in one drainageway crossing Stations 8 00 to
10 50 A granular stabilization layer and a fill surcharge program is
recommended in this area to minimize potential excessive settlement and
to stabilize the soft ground prior to embankment and culvert
construction
Some major cut slopes 20 to 60 feet high are required along the
proposed road alignment Based on our present boring and probing data
it is anticipated that the proposed cut slopes will be excavated
primarily in weathered colluvium and extremely weathered basalt
Based on the laboratory determined direct shear strengths which
were measured after saturation and slope stability analysis performed
it is our opinion that cut Slopes less than 40 feet high may be designed
with a slope ratio of up to H V cuts higher than 40 feet should
utilize a slope ratio of 2 1 H V or less in order for the proposed
cuts to have a minimum overall factor of safety of 1 5 See Plate 6
The analysis assumes 8 foot wide benches every 20 feet of vertical slope
height and that excess water pressure will not be experienced in the
slope
Portions of the proposed roadway will be constructed by the
placement of fill embankments on the existing hillside In order to
reduce the destabilizing influence of excessive build up of pore water
pressure trench drains should be included in fill embankments that are
immediately downslope of large catchment areas See Plates 3 and 7 In
areas where the toe of the fill embankments are prone to scouring by
flood water toe protection by rockfill is recommended to maintain the
long term stability of the fill embankments

Boring Nos 1 and 2 indicate that soft to very soft alluvium
consisting mainly of clayey silts and some sand may be encountered
to at least 20 to 36 feet beneath the proposed culvert location
Upon placement of the proposed 8 foot high embankment fill along the
drainageway significant ground settlement will occur due to
compression of the soft deposits caused by the new fill load The
estimated total primary settlement due to the new fill is about 1 to
2 feet If the culvert is installed during filling this settlement
would damage the culvert Therefore we recommend that the
installation of the culvert be delayed until the embankment fill has
first been allowed to settle Because of the low permeability of

the soft clayey silt layers the rate of ground settlement due to
the new fill loads will be slow The laboratory test data indicates
that it may take about 3 years for 90 of the total primary
settlement to take place
In order to reduce the settlement waiting time we believe that a
surcharge program should be considered to accelerate the ground
settlements prior to the construction of the new culvert structures
The total height of the surcharge should be limited to about 5
feet Greater surcharge height is not recommended because of the
possibility of bearing or slope failure
The length of the surcharge period should be as long as practicable
because of the anticipated slow rate of settlement We estimate
that a 6 month minimum surcharge period may be required Settlement
gauges should be installed in the area and periodic survey readings
of the gauges should be used to monitor the actual settlement The
readings should be taken by the project surveyor and transmitted to
the soils engineer as soon as available in order that the actual
settlement conditions could be evaluated
During the surcharge period a section of the drainageway should be
left opened and prepared for use as a temporary drainage channel to
allow runoff to pass this area This remaining portion of the
embankment can be constructed after the surcharge program is
completed and the new culvert structures placed It appears that
there may not be sufficient time to adequately surcharge this area
therefore we believe significant short and long term ground
settlements will occur at this portion of the embankment and this
may require periodic maintenance and repairs of pavements

It is apparent that this portion of the roadway construction will
have a significant effect on the overall construction schedule and
therefore to expedite the embankment and culvert construction two
suggestions are presented below
1 Place surcharge as soon as possible prior to road
construction The drainageway area is presently accessible and
could be surcharged with loose or compacted fill in order to
allow the ground to start settling prior to commencement of the
mass grading work This would reduce the delay caused by the
settlement waiting period
2 Use imported granular material for embankment fill The on site
soil is wet and requires some drying prior to filling
Therefore even light rains at the site would stop the filling
operations The use of granular import borrow such as coral or
crusher waste materials would greatly reduce delays caused by
rains Additionally these materials would provide a stronger
embankment fill

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
a se ee es ec re ee ee ss Sn se as ee
Moisture content and unit weight determinations were performed on
selected samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil
properties The results of these tests are presented on the Boring Logs
at the appropriate sample depths
Fifteen Atterberg Limit tests were performed to aid in the
classification of the soils The test results are summarized on Plates
B 1 through B 3
Three California Bearing Ratio CBR tests were performed on samples
of the near surface soils to evaluate their suitability for road subgrade
support The test results are summarized on Plate B 4
Iwo Proctor tests were performed to determine the moisture content
verses dry density relationship of the compacted soil The test results
are summarized on Plates B 4 B 14 and B 15
Eight 8 direct shear tests were performed on selected undisturbed
and remolded minimum 85 compaction samples to evaluate the shear
strength parameters of the soil The test results are plotted on Plates
B 5 through B 13
Two 2 consolidation tests were performed on undisturbed
fine grained sample to evaluate the compressibility and settlement
characteristics The test results are presented on Plates B 16 and B 17
Six 6 one inch ring swell tests were performed on undisturbed and
remolded samples to evaluate the swelling potential of the soils in both
natural and compacted conditions The test results are summarized on
Plate B 18

Horizontal Slotted Drains
1
Horizontal slotted drains shall be installed where directed by the
engineer or owner The drains shall be sloped at 5 percent or as
directed by the engineer
The entrance to the pipe shall be tightly plugged with a rounded or
pointed extension which shall extend not more than 0 5 foot beyond the
end of the pipe
The horizontal holes shall be drilled with rotary drilling equipment
The drains shall be slotted 1 5 inch Schedule 80 polyvinyl chloride
pipe Pvc 2110 Slotted pipe shall be furnished in 10 foot lengths
ends of the pipe shall be machined to provide for a smooth wall slip
coupling Average configuration shall be 42 slots per row per foot
using 0 02 inch slots The slots shall consist of three 3 rows cut
circumferentially on the pipe on 120 degree centers commercially cut
at the factory
