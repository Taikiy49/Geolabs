Dear Mr Crawford
This letter report presents the results of our percolation tests performed in
support of the design of a leaching field for the waste disposal system at the Kukui ula
Temporary Sales Center in Koloa on the Island of Kauai Hawaii
Project Considerations
The project involves the construction of a leach field for the waste disposal system
for the proposed sales center Planned improvements will involve the construction of a
temporary sales center parking areas and access driveway
Percolation Test
One borehole was drilled to a depth of approximately 5 feet below the existing
ground surface using a truck mounted drill rig equipped with 4 inch diameter
continuous flight augers The borehole encountered soils consisting of stiff brown
clayey silt with traces of gravel overlying basalt cobbles and boulders in a clayey silt
matrix Groundwater was not encountered in the borehole at the time of our
exploration Field percolation test was performed in general accordance with the State
Wastewater Systems and Chapter 10 of the Ten State s Standards to evaluate the
permeability of the subsurface soils for disposal of wastewater effluent The test was
performed in a 4 inch diameter borehole with approximately 1 inch of gravel placed at
the bottom of the borehole to protect the bottom of the borehole from scouring and
sediments During testing the borehole was carefully filled with water to a depth of
about 6 inches above the gravel and refilled as necessary The time intervals and water
drops were then recorded to provide data upon which the calculation of the percolation
test was based
