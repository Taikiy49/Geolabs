Dear Mr. Waters: 
This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations and testing 
services performed during the roadway site grading and construction for the Dey Family 
Subdivision project from June 12, 2013 to November 18, 2016 in accordance with our fee 
proposals dated June 12, 2013 and November 19, 2013. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The project site is situated at the base of Mount Olomana in the Kailua area on the 
Island of Oahu, Hawaii. The project site is located along Old Kalanianaole Highway on the 
southern side of Kalanianaole Highway in the Kailua/Waimanalo area on the Island of 
Oahu, Hawaii.  The project site is bounded by Waimanalo Ridge towards the south and 
Mount Olomana towards the west.  A Project Location Map and Site Plan are shown on 
Plates 1 and 2, respectively.   
We understand that the project will involve subdividing the undeveloped property 
(TMK: 4-2-05: Por. 06) into 23 residential agricultural lots ranging in size from 2 to 8 acres. 
The initial site development will include site grading and installation of roadways. The new 
roadways will be about 2,440 linear feet in length with slopes varying from 1 to 12 percent. 
Fills up to about 15 feet and cuts up to about 20 feet are planned for the proposed 
development.  The larger fills are anticipated near the eastern portion of the new roadway 
and the deeper cuts are planned along the western portion of the new roadway.

ROADWAY SITE GRADING AND CONSTRUCTION 
We observed the roadway site grading construction work for the Dey Family 
Subdivision project from June 12, 2013 to November 18, 2016. The site grading work 
consisted of placement and compaction of fills on the order of up to about 15 feet thick and 
cuts up to about 20 feet to achieve the finished grades for the project. Goodfellow 
Brothers, Inc. performed the roadway site grading and construction work. During the site 
grading operations, we obtained samples of the on-site and imported materials utilized as 
fill and backfill materials and transported them to our laboratory for testing. The maximum 
dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general 
accordance with ASTM D1557 (Modified Proctor), and the test results are summarized on 
Plate 3. 
The fill and backfill materials were moisture-conditioned to above the optimum 
moisture content, and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density 
as determined in accordance with ASTM D1557. In the roadway areas, the fill and backfill 
materials within 2 feet of the finished subgrade were compacted to a minimum of 
95 percent relative density. 
During the roadway site grading and construction operations, periodic field density 
tests were performed in general accordance with ASTM D6938 (Nuclear Gauge Method). 
Results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 4.1 through 4.48 of 
this report. These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on 
constructed fills and backfills at the site. Test locations and elevations were estimated by 
pacing and interpolating from on-site survey stakes. These locations and elevations should 
be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used. Areas assessed 
as unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained 
and/or approved by our field representative based on visual observation and hand probing. 
Seepage water was observed along Road “A” during our site observations on 
July 28, 2016.  The installation of a subdrain system was recommended to intercept the 
observed seepage water.  The subdrain system was installed in November 2016. 
Our services consisted of part-time field observation of the contractor’s fill 
placement, subgrade preparation, and compaction procedures and confirmation of 
subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above grading operations.
