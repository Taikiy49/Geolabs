Dear Mr Oyama
This letter report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations services
performed during the micropile testing and production installation for the drain box top slab
foundations for the Interstate Route H 1 PM Contraflow Lane Phase 2 project located in the
Pearl City area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The deep foundation support system of the
four new drain box structures consists of sixteen 6 inch diameter micropiles Geolabs
observed the installation and proof testing of the production micropiles for the drain box top
slab foundations
In general this letter report presents the results of our field observations the test
data collected during the proof testing conducted on March 27 and 28 2018 and our
analyses and recommendations
PROJECT DESCRIPTION
In general the project involves the installation of an outbound PM contraflow lane
and widening of the westbound outbound side of the Pearl City Viaduct on Interstate Route
H 1 on the Island of Oahu Hawaii We understand the project also includes the installation
of new top slabs over existing drainage structures These drainage structures are located
along the outbound shoulder of Interstate Route H 1 at Station 522 35
526 66 and 531 81
The site is generally located along the outbound shoulder of the Interstate Route H 1
near Exit 8A for H 2 North to Mililani Wahiawa and North Shore The project location and
general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1

Based on our geotechnical engineering memorandum titled New Top Slabs over
Drain Boxes Micropile Foundation Option Interstate Route H 1 PM Contraflow Lane Phase
2 Pearl City Oahu Hawaii dated we recommended supporting the new
drain box structures on a deep foundation system consisting of 7 inch diameter micropiles
with Strength I Limit State and Extreme Event Limit State capacities of 60 and 109 kips per
micropile respectively The micropiles would extend through the near surface fills and
derive support primarily from skin friction within the underlying stiff clayey silty residual soils
with a design cased length and a design bond length of about 10 feet and 20 feet
respectively Based on the contractor s request to reduce the micropile diameter from 7
inches to 6 inches we recommended extending the micropile bond length from 20 feet to
24 feet
MICROPILE PROOF TEST PROGRAM
Proof Test Micropile Installation
The two production and 15 selected for proof testing were
installed by Hawaiian Dredging Construction Company between March 15 and 20 2018
utilizing a KLEMM 806D track mounted drill rig The drill rig was equipped with a downhole
hammer and a 6 inch diameter drill bit The approximate locations of the micropiles are
shown on the Micropile Identification Plan Plate 2 1
Production Micropile No 13 extended to a depth of about 37 4 feet below the ground
6 inch grout bulb as bond zone about 1 3 feet of the steel casing stick up above the ground
surface Production Micropile No 15 extended to a depth of about 36 3 feet below the
24 6 feet of 6 inch grout bulb as bond zone about 1 3 feet of the steel casing stick up above
the ground surface
Based on our field observations the two micropiles were installed by advancing the
steel casing to a depth of about 11 8 feet below the ground surface and further advancing
the drill bit to depths between about 36 3 and 37 4 feet below the ground surface The proof
test micropiles generally encountered fill materials and or residual soils to the maximum
depth drilled of about 37 4 feet below the ground surface Upon reaching the tip elevations
the cement grout was pumped via a tremie pipe from the bottom of the micropile to the top
of casing Prior to the grout placement all threaded reinforcing bars with
centralizers spaced at every 8 feet were installed to the full depth of the boreholes
Installation records of the two proof test micropiles are provided on Plates 3 1 through 4 2
Micropile Testing
To evaluate the micropile design capacity a total of two static tensile proof tests were
conducted on production and 15 for the project The tensile proof tests
were conducted in general accordance with U S Department of Transportation Federal
Highway Administration Micropile Design and Construction Guidelines Publication No
070 The allowable tensile load capacity was specified as 60 kips for the

micropiles A hydraulic jack system reaction against a reaction beam was used to apply the

A load cell was used to measure the loads applied to the micropiles The micropile

were placed on the left and right sides of the proof test micropiles
In general both tensile test micropiles were loaded to a maximum test load of
109 6 kips in about 7 kip load increments Each load increment was held for at least
5 minutes The maximum test load was held for at least 10 minutes at 109 6 kip load
increment about 1 82 times of the design allowable tensile load capacity to evaluate the
effects of creep The gross rates of settlement at the 109 6 kip load increment were less
than 0 02 inches per hour Subsequently the micropiles were unloaded in approximately
seven equal decrements and held for 2 minutes The results of the proof tests are presented
