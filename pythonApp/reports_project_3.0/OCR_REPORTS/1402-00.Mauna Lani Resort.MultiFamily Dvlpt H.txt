PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this investigation was to explore the subsurface
soil conditions at the site in order to provide data for use in the
design of site grading building foundation and pavement design for the
proposed multi family development The general location of the site and
its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1

W Page 2
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests which we
used in the engineering analysis to formulate our conclusions and
recommendations are presented in the appendices of this report The
approximate boring locations are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located about one mile southwest of the Mauna
Lani Hotel between Fairways 6 7 and 8 for the golf course
Kaniku Drive is located along the easterly entrance to the site and
Iliilinaehehe Bay and rocky slopes along the ocean shoreline form the
westerly boundary
The topography of the site located over an uneven lava flow
generally varies from an elevation of about 25 to 30 feet at the
northerly end to about 50 to 55 feet at the southerly end
The easterly and southerly portions of the site is a very rough
rocky area except for a dozed and compacted access road down the center
Much of the westerly portion of the development has been graded to a
fairly smooth surface that is covered with gravel to cobble size clinkers
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed plan is to grade the site for a multi family
condominium development The general site plan indicates that sixteen
16 2 and 3 story structures with access roads and parking areas are
anticipated

The proposed grading plan indicates that most of the buildings will
be located on shallow fills less than 10 feet thick The exceptions to
this were noted at Buildings A and C where the structures will be
located over a cut and fill section and Buildings D and E which
appear to be in cut
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
Hawaii the largest island of the Hawaiian Archipelago covers an
area of approximately 4000 square miles The island was formed by the
activity of 5 shield volcanoes These are Kohala which is long
extinct Mauna Kea which has had activity during recent geologic times
Hualalai which last erupted in 1801 and Mauna Loa and Kilauea both
Still active
The site is located on the northwestern flank of Mauna Loa
mountain which makes up the central and southern part of the island
consists largely of the prehistoric member of the Kau Volcanic Series
These volcanics are the assemblage of Recent and Latest Pleistocene Aa
and Pahoehoe basalts laid down by the volcano The Kau lavas form a thin
veneer on the lower slopes of Mauna Loa
The lava flow at the site is an Aa flow consisting of thick beds of
rough broken sharp clinker rock Locally the Aa basalt is called The
Kaniku Flow The clinker is extremely permeable
Soil Conditions
Eight borings were drilled at the site to depths of 12 5 to 20 feet
below the existing ground surface to delineate the subsurface geological
conditions The approximate locations of the borings are shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2

The field exploration disclosed that the site is covered with a
fairly thick unconsolidated clinker deposit that may vary from 10 to 20
feet or more thick The clinker consists of cobble and gravel sized
basaltic fragments Occasionally boulders are scattered through the
clinker layers Below the clinker layer there appears to be an Aa lava
flow which is composed of dense basalt It is anticipated that
individual flows may be up to 10 to 20 feet thick and underlain with
other clinker layers
The subsurface conditions throughout the site appear to be
relatively uniform
No water was encountered in the borings during the field
exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field explorations disclosed that the site is underlain by
unconsolidated clinker and lava rock formations From a geotechnical
point of view the site is suitable for the proposed condominium
development
The volcanic materials should provide adequate bearing for the
proposed structures related roads and parking areas
We anticipate that the excavated clinker will probably experience
shrinkage which may result in a shortage of material
From discussions with your construction coordinator Mr C W
Spitz a shrinkage factor of 0 5 to 0 7 may be considered for the various
rocky materials
The rock excavation is expected to break within a size range of 1
to 12 inches

Site Grading
The preliminary grading plan indicated that the iajor site grading
work would consist of fills up to 5 to 10 feet thick over most of the
building sites Thus some imported fill may be required for the
proposed grading Cut and fill generally less than 4 feet would be
required in the roads and parking areas
Site Preparation
Prior to filling the area to receive fill should either be
scarified to a depth of 12 inches and recompacted with a heavy
dozer or roller to obtain a dense choking surface layer or a
choking gravel fill be placed over the clinker surface We believe
several passes would be required to provide a dense and compacted
surface layer Any loose areas disclosed during site preparation
should be repaired by removing the loose materials and backfilling
with compacted suitable on site materials
Fill Material
Generally the on site small clinker material less than 12 inches
in size will be suitable for fill Clinkers or large rock
fragments in excess of 12 inches in size should be crushed down to
smaller sizes or disposed of in deep fill section in accordance
with the procedures recommended under the subsequent section
BOULDER DISPOSAL
Imported 111 material required for the embankment fill is assumed
to be similar to clinker or other well graded granular rocky
materials The maximum rock size should generally be less than 12
inches The fill materials should be tested and approved by the
soils engineer prior to being transported to the project site
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1402 00 Page 6
If the excavated rock material does not contain sufficient fines to
produce a well graded material off site cinders or soil may be
mixed into the excavated rocky material to increase the percentage
of fines to provide a better graded material for compaction and
increase the stability of the fills
The larger boulders used in the construction of fills should be
placed along the toe sections to be used for buttress fills or
revetments
Boulder Disposal
Boulders encountered in excavations may be used in the deeper fills
provided that the following recommendations are followed
1 Boulders should be spread out and must not be nested together
They should be placed so that compaction equipment will be able
to compact around then
2 No part of the boulder should enroach any closer to finished
subgrade than the boulder s largest dimension and in no case
be closer than 3 feet to finished subgrade
3 No boulder larger than 3 feet in its largest dimension should
be placed in the fill It may be necessary to reduce the size
of larger boulders or dispose of them off site
4 Care must be exercised to avoid placement of boulders in the
proposed utility alignments to prevent difficulty in the later
excavation of the utility trenches

Fill Placement
Embankment fills should be placed in horizontal loose lifts up to
18 inches thick and compacted to its maximum density
Conventional compaction testing is generally not practicable in
excavated volcanic rock material due to the large particle size
Instead a testing program consisting of visual observations and
test strips to determine the lift thickness and number of passes of
a compactor required to achieve the desired compaction should be
conducted at the start of the project The number of passes can
then be used as the criteria for compaction
Excavation
The field exploration disclosed that the site is underlain by
unconsolidated clinker and accretionary lava balls boulders
It is anticipated that the clinker may be excavated with normal
excavating methods Some hard digging may require the use of a hoe
ram if large accretionary lava balls boulders are encountered
The above discussions regarding the rippability of the surface
materials are based on our visual observations of the existing lava
formations and cuts in the area and field observation of the
drilling method adopted for the exploration
We recommend that all prospective contractors bidding on this site
should examine these cuts the boring data and to make their own
interpretation

In general two horizontal to one vertical fill slopes are

It appears that most of the cut slopes will be in varying

Foundations
We believe the on site rocky ground and compacted granular fill
resulting from the above site grading will provide suitable foundation
support for the proposed buildings
Spread and wall footing foundations may be used for the proposed
structures
All exterior footing bottoms should be embedded a minimum of 24
inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade The minimum embedment
for interior footings may be reduced to 18 inches below the finished
grade
For the footing bearing on the compacted clinker rock fill an
allowable net soil bearing pressure of 4000 p s f may be used The
recommended soil bearing could be increased for footing bearing on
natural clinker densely backfilled with a choking fill material We
believe if required an allowable bearing value up to 6000 p s could
be used These values may be increased by one third when temporary wind
or seismic loads are considered
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1402 00 Page 9
Slabs and spread foundations located close to the tops of the fill
slopes should have sufficient embedment to obtain a minimun horizontal
set back distance of 8 feet measured from the outer edge of the footing
to the slope face
The ground surface should be shaped to divert surface runoff away
from building foundations and slabs
It is anticipated that the footings if constructed in accordance
with the above recommendations will have hegligible settlements
On Grade Slab
Based on a non expansive slab subgrade condition anticipated at
this site on grade slab should be placed over a 4 inch layer of base
course compacted to 90 of its maximum density as established by ASTM
D 1557 78 The thickened edge around the on grade slab should extend a
minimum of 12 inches below the lowest adjacent finished ground surface
Depending on the structure of the underlying rock formation a
choking layer consisting of well graded rock materials may be required
between the underlying rugged rock surface and the upper cushion fill or
concrete slab
To reduce differential settlement where the slab is partly on cut
in rock and partly on fill the cut portions should be over excavated
about one foot and then recompacted to match the adjacent subgrade
density
Utilities
It is anticipated that most of the trenching will be done in the
unconsolidated clinker material However some boulders or areas of
dense basalt may be encountered especially in the deeper trenches
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1402 00 Page 10
Trench backfill should be granular material generally less than 6
inches in size and compacted by water and mechanical tamping to 90 of
its maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78 except for the top 2 feet
below finished road subgrade which should be compacted to 95 of its
maximum dry density
Pavement
Asphalt concrete pavements may be used for the roadways and parking
areas In general the on site smaller clinker materials will provide an
excellent subgrade support when evenly mixed and compacted Assuming
that either the on site clinker or imported select granular fills with
CBR greater than 25 are used in the pavement subgrade we recommend that
the following preliminary pavement design be used for cost estimate
purposes
2 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
4 I nch Base Course
6 Inch Total Pavement Thickness on
Compacted Clinker
The subgrade soil under the pavement area should be compacted to at
least 95 of the ASTM D 1557 78 maximum density to a depth of 6 inches
below the bottom of the base course
CBR tests and or field observations should be performed on the
actual subgrade soil samples during construction and the above design
sections can be revised if necessary The recommended sections consider
medium traffic and that good drainage will be provided for adjacent to
paved areas
Design Review
Plans and specifications for site grading and utility constructions
should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written comments
prior to construction to see that the intent of our recommendations is
included

Construction Monitoring
To a degree the performance of any project is dependent upon the
construction procedures and quality Hence observations of site work
and fill placement should be carried out by a soils engineer to permit
correlation between the preliminary data obtained during this exploration
and actual conditions encountered during construction
If conditions encountered during construction are different from
those presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made

Preparation of Fill Areas
A Any loose areas disclosed during site preparation
should be repaired by removing the loose materials and
backfilling with compacted suitable on site materials
B After approval the area to receive fill should
either be scarified to a depth of 12 inches and
recompacted by a heavy dozer or roller to obtain a dense
choking surface layer or a choking gravel fill be
placed over the clinker surface

Fill Materials
Material for fill shall consist of on site clinker
material or approved imported material Imported
material shall be tested and approved a minimum of seven
7 working days prior to transportation to the site
Fill material shall contain less than 5 percent organic
matter and other deleterious material and shall not
contain rocks or lumps in excess of twelve 12 inches
in diameter
If the excavated rock material does not contain
sufficient fines to produce a well graded material
imported cinders or soil may be mixed into the excavated
rocky material to increase the percentage of fines to
provide a better graded material for compaction and
increase the stability of the fills
Placement and Compaction of Fill
A The il1 shall be placed in horizontal loose layers
not to exceed eighteen 18 inches in thickness Each
layer shall be spread uniformly and blade mixed to
attain uniformity of material and water content within
each layer

Appendix B
B Rock fragments or boulders less than twelve 12
inches in diameter may be utilized provided they are not
concentrated in any one area and that there is suffi
cient fine grained material to fill the voids between
the rocks
C Boulders encountered in excavations may be used in
the deeper fills provided that the following are
followed
1 Boulders shall be spread out and must not be nested
together They should be placed so that compaction
equipment will be able to compact around them
2 No part of the boulder should enroach any closer to
finished subgrade than the boulder s largest
dimension and in no case be closer than 3 feet to
finished subgrade
3 No boulder larger than 3 feet in its largest
dimension should be placed in the fill It may be
necessary to reduce the size of larger boulders or
dispose of them off site

4 Care must be exercised to avoid placement of
boulders in the proposed utility alignments to
prevent difficulty in the later excavation of the
utility trenches
D Conventional compaction testing is generally not
practicable in excavated volcanic rock material due to
the large particle size Instead a testing program
