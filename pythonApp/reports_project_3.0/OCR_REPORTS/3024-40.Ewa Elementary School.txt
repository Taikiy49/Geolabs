New Eight Classroom Building
Ewa Elementary School
This letter report presents the results of our field percolation testing performed at
the above referenced project The general location and vicinity of the project site are
shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
Project Considerations
The project site is located at the corner of Renton and Cane Haul Roads in the
District of Ewa on the island of Oahu Hawaii A new eight classroom building is
proposed within the existing Ewa Elementary School grounds
We understand that the City and County sewer system would not be ready in time
for connection with the new sewerline from the subject project Therefore it is proposed
to evaluate the infiltration characteristics of the subsoils at the project site to determine

infiltration characteristics of the subsoils at the project cesspool sites to provide a
preliminary evaluation of the feasibility of using the existing cesspools for additional
sewage disposal

Subsurface Conditions
Our initial field work consisted of drilling and sampling two borings designated as
Boring Nos P 1 and P 2 near the existing cesspools at Buildings D and J to a preliminary
depth of approximately 21 feet below the existing ground surface Subsequently we
understood that only the cesspool adjacent to Building J would be used as a temporary
sewage disposal facility for the additional sewage from the new classroom building
Further study and field investigation also indicated that the existing cesspool adjacent to
Building J may be as deep as 35 to 40 feet below the existing ground surface Therefore
a third boring designated as Boring No P 3 was drilled adjacent to Boring No P 2 to

Percolation Testing
Boring Nos P 1 and P 2
After Boring Nos P 1 and P 2 borings were drilled to depths of 21 feet about one
inch of gravel No 3B Fine was placed on the bottom of the 4 inch drilled holes Water
was then added into the borings to presoak the soils at the bottom portion of the borings
for at least 4 hours The soils were allowed to swell for about 20 hours before
commencing the percolation tests

The subsurface percolation rates at the sites were tested by the falling head
method Water was added from the bottom of the borings up to approximately 6 inches
above the gravel layer before testing The changes in water level in the borings were
recorded with respect to time for about 4 hours The results of these tests are presented
on Plates 3 and 4
Based on the percolation test results it appears that the soils percolation rates at
both locations is relatively low Percolation rates on the order of 240 and 70 minutes per
inch were obtained for the clayey soil formation at a depth of about 20 feet below the
existing ground surface at Boring Nos P 1 and P 2 respectively
Boring No P 3
Further study and field investigation verified that the existing cesspool adjacent to
Building J may be as deep as 35 to 40 feet below the existing ground surface Therefore
a third boring designated as Boring No P 3 was drilled adjacent to Boring No P 2 to
a deeper depth for additional percolation testing
Our additional boring indicated that the area is underlain by a deposit of loose to
medium dense sandy coral gravels below the surficial silty clays at a depth of
approximately 24 feet below the existing ground surface This relatively permeable
deposit extends to the bottom of the boring at a depth of about 36 feet corresponding
to the estimated depth of the existing cesspool
Due to the granular nature of the subsoils encountered at the lower portion of
Boring No P 3 percolation testing was performed immediately after preliminary test runs
indicated that the subsoil is highly permeable Based on the percolation test results

presented on Plate 5 it appears that the percolation rate is relatively high at the lower

It should be pointed out that loss of infiltration capacity due to saturation and soil
clogging by combined physical chemical and biological factors may have occurred at
the existing cesspools The latter factor is the most important one since an organic mat
of several millimeters thick may form at the soil liquid interface Other factors such as
rainfall and maintenance effort etc may also affect the permeability of the underlying
granular soils to some extent Therefore the actual percolation rate of the existing
cesspool may vary significantly from the percolation test values presented above
Limitations
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report letter are based in
part upon information obtained from field data points Variations of conditions between
field data points may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not
become evident until the cesspool is subjected to the additional disposal capacity If
variations then appear evident it will be necessary to reevaluate the findings presented
in this report
