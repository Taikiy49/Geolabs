SOIL ENGINEERING REPORT
LOT 130 ROYAL SUMMIT SUBDIVISION PHASE IIT
In accordance with your request we have made a study of the near
surface soil conditions at the above referenced lot in order to provide
foundation recommendations for the proposed residence The study
consisted of drilling three shallow borings testing the swelling
potential of the soils in our laboratory and reviewing the subdivision
site grading and the preliminary house foundation plans This report
summarizes our findings and recommendations
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
The proposed residence will be a two story structure approximately
60 by 90 feet in plan dimension with a three car garage
Concrete on grade slab is planned for the driveway garage and the
entire floor level of the house

RECOMMENDATTONS
General
The stiff to very stiff reddish brown clayey silt encountered can
generally provide adequate foundation support for the proposed house
provided that some care is exercised during construction to reduce the
swelling potential of the soil
Laboratory tests performed on the stiff to very stiff clayey silt
soil indicate that this material generally has low to moderate swelling
potential depending on the initial moisture content prior to subsequent
saturation The results of the laboratory one inch ring swell tests are
summarized on Plate B 1 Therefore to reduce effects due to soil
swelling under on grade slabs we recommend that the on site soil be
capped with at least 12 inches of non expansive off site borrow fill
and that the subgrade soils underneath the select fill and slab be
moistened and protected from drying from the start of construction until
the slab concrete placement
House Foundations
Spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the proposed
residence An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f may be used on the
compacted fill
Individual footings on level areas should be embedded a minimum of
12 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade Continuous wall
footings should be embedded a minimm of 18 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grade

Lot Grading
The fills and backfills within the top 12 inches minimm below
the on grade slabs should consist of non expansive soil such as select
borrow crusher waste or coral The on site soil within 12 inches of
the bottom of the slabs should be over excavated to provide a 12 inch

capping
All imported fill materials should be tested and approved by the
soils engineer prior to being used in the site
Prior to construction the area to receive any fill should be
cleared of vegetation and debris Benching into the stiff on site soil
is required prior to keying the new fill lifts on ground steeper than
5 1 H V
The fills should be placed in horizontal lifts not exceeding 8
inches in compacted thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its
maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78
Two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V cut and fill slopes are
recommended
The site should be graded to provide drainage of surface runoff
away from all foundations

Slab On Grade
It should be noted that the on site clayey silt soils may be
expansive if allowed to dry Therefore the on site soil should be
over excavated down to a depth of 12 inches low the slab bottom
noistened and then replaced with non expansive imported
borrow fills as previously discussed The thickened edge of the slab
should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent
finished grade
To further reduce future post construction swelling of the
underlying expansive soils we recommend that the in place soil below
the upper capping select fill pad be kept moist for one week prior to
concrete placement heavily soaked with water for the first two days and
sprinkled with water for the remainder of the week
A 4 inch cushion layer of 3B Fine gravel should be provided below
the slab In order to protect against future moisture infiltration an
impervious moisture barrier may be incorporated below the slab

Drainage
The finished lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings
should be shaped to shed water away from foundations and to avoid
ponding condition Also it is advised that gutter systems be installed
around the house and that gutter water be diverted far away from the
foundations and walkways Excessive landscape watering near the
foundations and slab should also be avoided
Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper
functioning of the above recommended foundations since ponded water
could cause excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving
or loss of strength The foundation excavations should be properly
backfilled tight against the wall or slab footing immediately after
setting of the concrete to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and
maintained to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing
foundations
Design Review
Final plans and specifications for foundations site grading and
utility construction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for
review and comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the footing excavations and backfill
operations be observed by the soils engineer during construction to see
that adequate bearing has been obtained and that the subsurface
conditions are consistent with the findings of our study
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above inspections
LIMITATIONS
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations
made in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation
engineering principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all
other warranties either expressed or implied
The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are based
on our site reconnaissance soil information from boring and laboratory
test data obtained from this soil exploration
Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encountered and cannot
be fully determined by taking soil samples Unforeseen conditions such
as expansive soil and soft spots may occur in localized areas and may
require additional probing or corrections in the field Therefore
additional expenditures may be needed during construction to attain a
properly constructed project Some contingency fund is thus recommended
to accommodate these possible costs

engineer in the preparation of foundation plans for this residence
