Project Considerations 
The project site is located west of Kalaeloa Boulevard and adjacent to Malakole 
Street in Kapolei on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. We understand that the project consists of 
a planned retention basin at the project site. We understand that the retention basin invert 
will be about 5 feet below the existing ground surface. The project site is shown on the 
Site Plan, Plate 2. 
Based on our field observations, the project site is relatively flat, sloping gently 
downward from northeast to southwest at an average gradient of about 0.5 percent. A 
topographic survey map was not available at the time this report was prepared. Based on 
Google Earth Pro, the area just to the north of Malakole Street generally has ground 
elevations ranging from about +5 to +11 feet Mean Sea Level (MSL).

Subsurface Conditions 
Our field exploration consisted of drilling and sampling ten borings, designated as 
Boring Nos. 1 through 10, extending to a depth of about 8 feet below the existing ground 
surface. We also conducted field infiltration tests in each of the borings for a total of ten 
field infiltration tests.  The approximate boring locations are shown on the Site Plan, 
Plate 2. 
The borings generally encountered a surface fill layer of about 0.5 to 7 feet thick 
underlain by beach deposit, siltstone, and coral formation extending to a maximum 
explored depth of 8 feet below the existing ground surface. The surface fill layer was not 
encountered in two of the borings drilled.  The surface fill layer consisted of stiff to hard 
sandy/clayey silt and medium dense to very dense silty sand/gravel and sandy gravel.  
The beach deposit was composed of medium dense to very dense silty sand with some 
gravel.  The siltstone and coral formation were soft to hard. 
We encountered groundwater in the drilled borings at the depths of 3 to 8 feet 
below the ground surface. However, it should be noted groundwater levels are subject to 
change due to tides, rainfall, time of year, seasonal precipitation, surface water runoff, 
and other factors. 
Detailed descriptions of the field exploration methodology are presented in 
Appendix A. Descriptions and graphic representations of the materials encountered in the 
borings are presented on the Logs of Borings in Appendix A. Laboratory tests were 
performed on selected soil and rock samples and the test results are presented in 
Appendix B. 
Field Infiltration Testing 
The field infiltration testing was performed in general accordance with the procedures 
in Appendix D of the state of Maryland, Department of the Environment, “Stormwater Design 
Manual, Volumes I and II” (Rev. 2009). These procedures are consistent with other states’ 
procedures and generally may be considered an industry standard. 
The field infiltration tests were performed in the borings by augering boreholes to 
the selected test depths of about 2 to 8 feet below the existing ground surface.  The 
approximate boring locations are shown on the Site Plan, Plate 2.  It should be noted that 
Boring No. 9 was located in a low spot and groundwater was encountered in the boring 
at a depth of about 3 feet below the existing ground surface. Due to the shallow 
groundwater level encountered, an additional boring extending to about 2 feet below the 
existing ground surface was drilled to perform the infiltration testing at this location.

Upon reaching the test depth, a 4-inch diameter Polyvinyl Chloride (PVC) solid 
casing was set to the bottom of the drilled hole to allow infiltration only through the soil 
exposed at the bottom of the boring. Falling head infiltration tests were performed to 
determine the infiltration rates of the underlying subsurface materials. Each test consisted 
of four trials of filling the casing with 24 inches of water and taking periodic readings over 
a one-hour trial period. The infiltration rate is calculated based on the result of the fourth 
and last trial for each test location. The results of the infiltration tests are summarized in 
the following table for ease of reference.

appear evident, it will be necessary to re-evaluate the recommendations provided herein.
