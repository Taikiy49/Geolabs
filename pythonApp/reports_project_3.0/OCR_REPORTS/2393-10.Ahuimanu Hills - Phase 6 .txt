Geotechnical Engineering Report dated March 6 1990 Detailed observations and
discussions are presented in the following sections of this letter report
Stabilization of Soft Ground
Two soft ground areas adjacent to Ahuimanu Stream as shown on the attached
Site Plan Plate 1 were encountered during our field exploration performed for the
subject project During the mass grading operations a stabilization blanket consisting
of a gravel cushion over a layer of geotextile fabric with subdrains was installed at Soft
Ground Area 1 prior to the placement of fill material up to the design finish grades A
surcharge program was then implemented to accelerate the consolidation of the
underlying soft soils induced by the new fill loads The surcharge fill was removed after
the settlement readings suggested that most of the ground settlements have occurred
under the surcharge loads
in Soft Ground Area 2 a buttress fill was constructed under the new lower fill
slope to reduce the potential of slope instability during the fill embankment construction
A settlement monitoring program was also implemented to monitor the rate and
magnitude of the fill ground settlements
Subdrain Installation
During the grading operation subsurface seepage was encountered at various
locations across the project site Subdrains were installed and daylighted into drainage
structures or outside the grading limits for seepage relief The approximate locations
of the subdrains are shown on Plates 2 through 4
Fill Placement
During the mass grading operations representative samples of the on site and
imported soils utilized as fill materials were obtained and returned to our laboratory for
testing The maximum dry density and optimum moisture content were determined in
our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Results of
our laboratory tests are presented on Plate 5
Observation and testing of the earthwork in the field were performed by
representatives from our office to observe that the grading was performed in general
conformance with the intent of the Geotechnical Engineering Report and specifications
pertaining to the geotechnical aspects of the development Due to the high in situ
moisture contents of the on site soils and the high rainfall occurring in this area the
compaction requirement for this residential project has been reduced from the standard
of 90 percent to a minimum of 85 percent of the maximum dry density as established

HOUSE FOUNDATION AND CONSTRUCTION CONSIDERATIONS
We strongly recommend that a competent geotechnical engineer be retained by
each individual lot buyer to review the house foundation plans and specifications prior
to construction to determine adherence to the general foundation recommendations
provided in our Geotechnical Engineering Evaluation report Home owners should be
advised to extend foundation sufficiently down into competent in situ stable soil and not
to block or alter surface drainage Professional geotechnical engineering consultation
and observation should be obtained in the planning design and construction of any
future improvemenis
The services of a geotechnical engineer should also be utilized during the
foundation construction and regrading of future lots to observe that the soil conditions
are consistent with our previous findings and that the required footing set back and
embedment criteria are followed He should test and approve all additional imported
fill material observe the footing excavation soft soil removal backfill placement and
recompaction and footing preparation prior to concrete placement Our recommen
dations are contingent upon the above field monitoring and confirmation
We strongly recommend that the aforementioned recommendations and
restrictions be made available to all future lot and home purchasers of this subdivision
