This report presents the results of our subsurface exploration
for the proposed future development of coral limestone mining
operations at the subject site The investigation was performed in
general accordance with our proposal dated March 26 1981 and the
subsequent update of that proposal dated and consisted
of the drilling and sampling of six borings at the proposed quarry
site visual observations and laboratory testing
SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed quarry site is a knoll situated between Malaekahana
and Kahawainui Streams about three quarters of a mile to the west of
the town of Laie The location of the site and its vicinity is shown
on the Project Location Map Plate 1
W O 1259 00 Page 2
The site is currently used as pasture and the vegetative cover
consists of sparse to moderately dense grasses shrubs and low trees
The general surface elevations range from approximately 30 feet
to 150 feet above ean sea level
There have been some limited amounts of quarrying or borrow
operations previously performed on portions of the site
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
A review of available maps and literature indicated that area is
underlain by partly consolidated to well consolidated cemented dunes
of calcareous sand These dunes were created as a result of
glacio eustatic sea level fluctuations during the Pleistocene epoch
As sea level rose fringing reefs of coral and calcareous algae
grew around the shore of the island of Oahu When the level of the
sea fell often to below its current level these reef materials were
exposed and weathered The prevailing winds carried carbonate sands
to inland areas where they were deposited as dunes Rainwater
percolating through the calcareous sands has dissolved some of the
calcium carbonate in the sand and redeposited it to act as a cement to
bind the sand together into a sandstone In some areas the calcium
carbonate has been almost totally recrystalized by solution

Our field exploration confirmed that the site is underlain by
dune sands with a very thin 0 to 5 feet overburden of sandy or
clayey silts which appear to be of eolian wind blown origin The
dune sands varied from slightly to well cemented
Subsurface Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by the
drilling and sampling of six 6 borings at the approximate locations
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In addition six 6 surface samples were taken for testing and
evaluation of the near surface materials
Detailed descriptions of the field exploration and the laboratory
testing are presented in the appendices of this report
Soil Conditions
In general our borings encountered the following materials
1 Overburden medium stiff brown sandy or clayey silts
occurring in localized areas of the site up to 5 feet
thick These silts are most likely wind blown eolian soils
recently deposited by the strong prevailing trade winds from
the northeast

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on our findings the dune sand materials encountered on the
subject site are suitable for use as borrow materials and to an extent
suitable for use as quarried stone and aggregate
Excavation and Crushing
It is our opinion that most of the materials encountered on the
subject site should be rippable by a conventional dozer such as a
Caterpillar D 9 However better control of material for crushing may
be obtained by the use of rock excavation techniques such as blasting
or rock splitting
When crushed the moderately cemented or less grades of
sandstone will tend to split along bedding planes yielding irregular
flat fragments The well cemented grades of sandstone will tend to
crush into more regular aggregate
Material Suitability
It is our opinion that in general the material encountered at
the subject site could probably meet all City County specifications
except gradation for Borrow Sec 16 and Select Borrow Sec 30
Since the in situ material is basically a sandstone in nature it
is anticipated that the gradation can be adjusted as necessary when
the material is crushed and or processed at the crushing plant

The laboratory testing indicated that the on site material is
non expansive generally free of deleterious materials has good sand
equivalent values and fair to good resistance to abrasion
California Bearing Ratio tests performed on selected near surf ace
samples indicated CBR values of about 35 It is believed that higher
CBR values may be obtained from material produced by the processing of
well cemented sandstone
With respect to use as aggregate it is our opinion that the
on site materials are readily suited with processing to use as fine
aggregate for conrete and as filler sand for asphalt It is felt that
the better grades of sandstone i e moderately and well cemented
can be processed for use as coarse aggregate for concrete However
the use of this concrete should be limited to non structural
applications e g driveways patios Sidewalks etc due to the
relatively low compressive strength 2000 or less p s i of the
sandstone

The subsurface conditions at the site were explored
by drilling 0 to 70 0 feet below the existing
ground surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site
Plan Plate 2 Truck mounted equipment was used to drill the
borings
The soils and rock encountered in the borings were
classified by visual and textural examination in the field by
our geologist who continuously monitored the drilling operations
The classifications were further reviewed by visual inspection
and testing in the laboratory The soils were classified in
general conformance with the Unified Soil Classification System
A graphic presentation of the materials encountered is presented
Soil samples were obtained from the boring by
driving a standard penetration sampler or a 2 4 inch I D
split barrel sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches
The blow counts to drive the sampler the last 12 inches are
shown on the log of borings at the appropriate sample depths
Rock core samples were obtained by using a rotary
wash NWD4 series double tube core barrel The core recovery
and rock quality designation RQD are presented on the logs
of borings

APPENDIX B
Moisture content and unit weight determinations were
performed on selected samples as an aid in the classification and
evaluation of soil properties The results of these tests are
presented on the Boring Logs at the appropriate depths
Two 2 laboratory CBR tests per ASTM D 1883 were performed
on bulk samples of the on site materials to evaluate the CBR Swell
characteristics and subgrade Support potential of the proposed
borrow
Three Abrasion Tests per ASTM C 131
were performed to evaluate the resistance to abrasion in use as
aggregate
Three 3 sulphate soundness tests ASTM C 88 test method
were performed to evaluate the resistance of the on site material
against chemical attack in use as aggregate
Six 6 sand equivalent tests were performed to evaluate
the suitability of the on site material for use as borrow and
aggregate ASTM D 2419 test method
Three 3 unconfined compression specific gravity and
absorption tests were performed on selected core samples to
further aid in the evaluation of the compression strength related
to its potential use as aggregate
