This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the proposed Aircraft Washing Facility at the
Pacific Missile Range Facility in Barking Sands on the island of Kauai
Hawaii The general location of the project site is shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1
PROJECT CONSTDERATION
The Aircraft Washing Facility project primarily includes a 15 by
20 foot one story control building and an 85 foot square concrete aircraft
washrack
New asphaltic concrete pavement is planned to connect the washrack and
an existing asphaltic concrete paved area located to the south of the
washrack

A swale is also planned to divert runoff and wash water from the
washrack towards a low spot to the east of the pad location for percolation
into the ground
Minimal site grading is anticipated for the proposed facility
construction
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of the exploration was to evaluate the subsurface soil
conditions at the proposed site to provide soil data for foundation design
of the proposed control building the thickness design of the proposed
concrete washrack and new ashpaltic concrete pavement We were also
requested to evaluate the percolation values of the surface soil at a low
spot near the proposed facility
The scope of our exploration which was performed in general
accordance with our revised proposal of August 7 1986 included logging
and sampling two 2 backhoe excavated test pits laboratory soil testing
engineering analysis utilizing the field and laboratory data and report
preparation
Detailed description of the field exploration our field and
laboratory testing and test pit logs are presented in the appendices The
approximate locations of the test pits are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
SITE DESCRIPTION
The Aircraft Washing Facility will be located to the north of an
existing hangar and on the eastern side of one end of Kaiwa Road at the
Pacific Missile Range Facility in Barking Sands on the island of Kauai
Hawaii

The site is an open and unoccupied area approximately 110 by 110 feet
in plan Ground surface at the site is sloping gently from the eastern
edge of Kaiwa Road towards a low spot at about 100 feet east of the
proposed control building location
At the time of the field exploration it was observed that several
helicopters were parked on Kaiwa Road in the proximity of the proposed
washrack location The existing asphaltic concrete pavement of the road
was seriously cracked However an asphaltic concrete paved open area
connected with one end of Kaiwa Road and located to the south of the
proposed washrack location appeared to be in a good service condition
Ground cover consists of some dry grasses and fine to medium beach
sand with silt
SUBSOTL CONDITIONS
Two 2 test pits were excavated to a depth of about 9 feet below the
ground surface Both test pits encountered fine to medium grain sized
beach sand with traces of silt and gravel from the ground surface to the
bottom of the test pits Occasional slightly cemented sand seams were
observed in the upper 4 feet of Test Pit No 2 and a thin well cemented
sandstone layer was also observed at a depth of about 5 5 to 6 0 feet
Slightly cemented sand cobble and gravel sizes were encountered in both
pits In general it appeared that the sandy subsoil may be loose on the
ground surface and graded denser with depth to medium dense and dense
Groundwater was encountered at the bottom of Test Pit No 2 at a depth
of about 9 feet at the time of the field exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATION
Building Foundation
It is anticipated that the loads from the 15 by 20 foot one story
control building should be light
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1742 00 Page 4
Dk No 38
Based on these anticipated loading and soil conditions we believe
that the one story building may be supported by shallow spread or
continuous wall footings bearing on the recompacted in situ medium dense to
loose sand
Footings should be placed a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grade Uplift wind or other lateral loading condition
may further increase the embedment of the footing foundation
An allowable bearing capacity of 2 500 p s f may be used for the
footing foundation design The bearing capacity value is for total dead
plus live load only and may be increased by one third for short duration
wind loading Settlements under the design loading are anticipated to be
on the order of less than one half of an inch total and one fourth of an
inch differential
Resistance to lateral loads on the control building can be obtained by
passive earth pressure and frictional resistance A frictional coefficient
of 0 4 may be used to calculate the frictional resistance between the
bottom of the footing and the underlying recompacted sandy soil In
addition an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 p s f per foot of depth may
be used to determine the passive earth resistance against the buried
vertical faces of the footing
Building Slab On Grade
In the proposed control building where light floor loads are
anticipated a 4 inch concrete slab over 4 inches of well graded aggregate
base course is recommended A plastic vapor barrier over the base course
could be used should a capillary break be deemed necessary to protect the
floor covering

Concrete Washrack Pad
The design of the proposed concrete washrack pad was carried out in
accordance with NAVFAC Design Manual DM 21 Airfield Pavements June
1973 The design of the proposed asphaltic concrete access to the washrack
was carried out in accordance with Navy Design Manual DM 21 3 Flexible
Pavement Design for Airfields August 1978
Design Aircraft Data
It is our understanding that the proposed concrete washrack pad will
serve mostly helicopters with occasional visits of other fixed wing
aircrafts The design aircraft with the heaviest wheel load will be
the P 3 Patrol which has a design gear load of 68 000 pounds and tire
pressure of 170 p s i
Design Subgrade California Bearing Ratio CBR
CBR tests for laboratory compacted in situ soil were performed in
accordance with ASTM D 1883 test method to evaluate the support
characteristics of the subgrade material The results of these tests
are summarized in Appendix B
In general the subgrade material consists of fine to medium grained
sand with good drainage characteristics and very low compressibility
and expansion potential Based on our laboratory tests we believe
that a design subgrade CBR value of 22 can be used

Site Grading
Site grading including minimal cut for the washrack and some fill for
the control building is anticipated for the proposed construction
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed All existing vegetation
any old pavement debris rubbish and other unsuitable materials should be
removed and disposed of properly off site in order to prevent contamination
of the excavated material
After clearing the area to receive fill cut should be scarified at
least 6 inches below the cleared finished grades and recompacted to a
minimm of 95 of its maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78
For the control building foundation the footing excavation should be
kept moist and thoroughly tamped prior to concrete placement For the
concrete washrack asphaltic concrete pavement the subgrade should be
scarified to a depth of at least 6 inches moisture conditioned and
recompacted to 100 of its maximum dry density as established by ASTM
D 1557 78 test method in accordance with the Navy airfield pavement
subgrade compaction requirement

Loose spots encountered at the footing or pavement subgrade level
should be recompacted or removed and replaced with on site sandy soil
compacted to the specifications mentioned above
The on site sandy soil may be used as a source of fill and or backfill
material If required new imported fill material should consist of
non expansive select granular materials less than 3 inches in maximm size
with less than 20 passing the 200 sieve
Structural fill below the control building should be placed in maximm
8 inch thick compacted horizontal lifts and compacted to its 95 ASTM
D 1557 78 maximum dry density
Percolation Tests
It is our understanding that runoff and the wash water from rinsing
the aircrafts will be diverted via a new swale to drain at a shallow
drainage pit planned at a low spot to the east of the proposed aircraft
washing facility Two percolation tests were performed at the low area to
determine the percolation values of the surface soil Detailed information
on percolation test set up and results are presented in Appendix Cc
Based on the test results it appears that the surface sandy soil at
the low area may dissipate clean water at an average rate of about 7 cubic
feet per hour per square foot drainage area under an initial 2 5 foot water
head It should be noted that siltation of the surface soils will greatly
reduce these percolation rates therefore periodic maintenance is
essential to maintain good percolation rates in the soils
Design Review
Plans and specifications for site grading washrack pavement and
foundation design should be forwarded to the soil engineer for review and
written comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the site grading subgrade preparation footing
and washrack pavement excavations be observed by the soil engineer during
construction to see that adequate compaction has been obtained and that the
subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of our exploration
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soil engineer prior to hauling to the site for use
If the conditions encountered during construction are different from
those presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made
LIMITATIONS
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations made
in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation engineering
principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all other warranties
either expressed or implied
The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are based on
our site reconnaissance soil information from test pit data field
percolation tests and laboratory tests for this study
Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encountered and cannot be
fully determined by taking soil samples Unforeseen conditions such as
expansive soil and soft spots may occur in localized areas and may require
additional probing or corrections in the field Therefore additional
expenditures may be needed during construction to attain a properly
constructed project Some contingency fund is thus recommended to
accommodate these possible costs
This report has been prepared in order to assist the engineer in the
preparation of site grading foundation and pavement design of the proposed
construction
