This report presents the results of our boring exploration and
percolation tests performed at the proposed Missile Maintenance Facility
in the Naval Magazine at Lualualei in order to provide subsurface data
for use in the design of the septic tank with leaching field The
general location of the project site and its vicinity is shown on the
Project Location Map Plate l
Our work was performed in general accordance with our proposal of
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
A previous investigation was performed at the site for the
foundation and site grading design aspects of the project
Subsequently it was decided to utilize a septic tank and leaching field
disposal system in lieu of a pump station and sewer main as originally
planned
W O 1676 00 I D 1942 Page 2
At present an area located about 150 feet south of the proposed
missile maintenance structure is being considered for the leaching field
According to the information made available to us it is our
understanding that a leaching field with a dimension of about 30 by 140
feet in plan 4000 square feet is being proposed The capacity of the
leaching field is anticipated to be about 2700 gallons of sewage
effluent per day The invert for the drain field piping will be at
about elevation 15 feet or 2 to 3 feet below the existing ground
For the design and feasibility of leaching field construction the
subsurface soil and groundwater conditions at the project site were
investigated by drilling borings at four locations at the site
A series of percolation tests was then performed in the augered
borings to evaluate the permeability characteristics of the upper 3 0 to
6 0 foot soil layer The approximate locations of the borings are shown
on the Site Plan Plate 2
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is situated between Bravo Road and H Avenue
Within the Naval Ammunition Depot which is located along the southern
shores of the West Loch Branch of Pearl Harbor
The topography at the site is relatively flat with a slight
gradient down towards the south
The area is presently overgrown with grass and haole koa
The existing ground elevations vary from about 18 to 19 feet above

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling
four 4 borings to depths of 3 to 6 feet below the existing ground
surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Soil samples obtained from the borings were selectively tested in our
laboratory to further examine their geotechnical engineering
characteristics Detailed descriptions of our field exploration
laboratory and percolation test results are presented in the appendices
Soil Conditions
The borings encountered 3 to 6 feet of silty sand and coral gravel
with hard zones of coral
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings at the time of our
field exploration
Detailed description of the materials encountered are presented on
the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 4
Several laboratory mechanical sieve analyses were performed on the
soil samples recovered in the borings to examine their grain size
distribution The test results are presented on Plates B l and B 2
PERCOLATION TESTS
After completion of drilling the borings were filled with water to
saturate the soils then percolation tests were performed in the holes
The falling head percolation test procedure was used for the tests The
test data are presented on Plates C 1 through C 4

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field percolation test results it appears that the
percolation rates of the soil at the site are fair to good This is
further confirmed by visual examination of the soils encountered in the
borings and the laboratory grain size analysis results
The capacity of the leaching field would be governed by the
underlying sand coral deposits which have percolation rates that appear
to be adequate for disposal Based on the percolation test data
obtained in the drilled holes a drainage leaching field covering a
minimum area of about 4000 square feet may have a capacity of about 8000
gallons per day A figure of 2 gallons per square foot per day may be
used to obtain the capacity of smaller fields
It should be pointed out however that the loss of infiltrative
capacity and soil clogging can occur in the future by combined physical
chemical and biological factors The latter is the most important
factor since an organic mat of several millimeters thick may form at the
soil liquid interface Other factors such as rainfall rise in
groundwater level etc may also affect the permeability to some

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the proposed sites were explored by
drilling 4 borings at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2 A truck mounted drilling rig was used to drill the borings to
depths of 3 to 6 feet below the existing ground surface
The soil encountered in the borings were classified by visual and
textural examination in the field by one of our engineers who
continuously monitored the drilling operations The classifications were
further reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory The
soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A graphic presentation of the materials
encountered is presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 4
In the borings soil samples were obtained by driving a 2 inch
standard split spoon sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches
The blow counts used to drive the sampler the last 12 inches are shown
on the boring logs at the appropirate sample depths
