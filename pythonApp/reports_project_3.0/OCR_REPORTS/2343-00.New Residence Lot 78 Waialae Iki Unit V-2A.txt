DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The stiff on site natural soil encountered can generally provide
adequate foundation support for the proposed house provided that some care
is exercised during construction to reduce the swelling potential of the
soil as recommended herein
The field exploration and laboratory tests performed on the stiff
on site silty clay indicate that in its natural condition this material
generally has low to moderate swelling potential depending on the initial
moisture content of the soil prior to subsequent saturation Therefore
to reduce the effects due to soil swelling under on grade slabs we
recommend that the on site soil be capped with at least 18 inches of
non expansive select fill and that the subgrade soils underneath the
select fill and slab be moistened and protected from drying from the start
of construction until the slab concrete placement The laboratory tests
also indicate that the swelling potential of the soil increases when it is
remolded or recompacted Therefore the on site soil should not be used
as fill under structural areas
Iot_ Grading
The fills and backfills below the on grade slabs should consist of
non expansive soils such as select borrow crusher waste or coral Where
cut is anticipated the on site soil should be over excavated to provide

an 18 inch minimum thickness of non expansive select fill capping
underneath the on grade slabs
The on site cut soils should not be used for fill below structural
areas and behind retaining walls
All imported fill material should be tested and approved by the soils
engineer prior to transportation to the site
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limits should be cleared of vegetation debris rubbish and other
deleterious materials These materials should be removed and disposed of
properly off site Any loose or soft spots encountered at fill areas
should be over excavated down to the firm subsoils and then replaced with

The fill areas should then be scarified to a depth of six 6 inches

The fill should be placed in maximm 10 inch thick loose level lifts
and compacted to a minimm of 90 of its maximm dry density per ASTM
D 1557 78
House Foundations
Spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the proposed
residence An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f may be used for
footings bearing on the natural soil or compacted fill
Individual footings should be embedded a minimm of 12 inches below
the lowest adjacent finished grade Continuous wall footings should be
embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade

If located on a sloping area the footing should be embedded down an
additional amount to obtain a 6 foot minimm horizontal set back distance
measured from the outer edge of the footing out to the slope face This
additional embedment depth is required to help resist the downhill slope
creep movement and to develop the recommended bearing capacity of the
soil
Slab On Grade
It should be noted that the on site silty clay soils may be
moderately expansive if allowed to dry Therefore the on site soil
should be over excavated down to a depth of 18 inches below the slab
bottom thoroughly moistened and then replaced with non expansive
imported soils as previously discussed The thickened edge of the slab
should be embedded a minimum of 12 inches below the lowest adjacent
finished grade for details refer to the Schematic Section on Plate 3
To further reduce future post construction swelling of the underlying
expansive soils we recommend that the in place silty clay below the upper
capping fill pad be kept moist prior to concrete placement
A 4 inch layer of 3B Fine gravel cushion fill should be provided
below the slab In order to protect against future moisture infiltration
an impervious moisture barrier may be incorporated below the slab

Swimming Pool
It appears that most of the proposed swimming pool will be excavated
below the existing ground For the swimming pool wall design a lateral
earth pressure of 55 p s f equivalent fluid pressure per foot of depth
may be used for walls restrained at the top at rest condition with level
backfill
We recommend that the backfill behind the pool wall should consist of
non expansive granular material such as 3B Fine gravel select borrow or
crusher waste We also recommend that the swimming pool should be
designed by Structural Engineer as a rigid structure in light of close
vicinity to foundations of the proposed residence Additional lateral
earth pressure acting on the swimming pool wall induced by foundation
loads in close vicinity to the pool less than 8 feet away from the pool
should be considered in the swimming pool design
A subdrainage system is recommended to drain possible pool leakage
away from the house foundation
Drainage
The finish lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings should
be shaped to shed water away from foundations and to avoid ponding
condition Also it is advised that gutter systems be installed around
the house and that gutter water should be diverted far away from the
foundations and driveway Excessive landscape watering near foundations
and slab should also be avoided
Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils

These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance
of the above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of
strength The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled tight
against the wall or slab footing inmediately after setting of the concrete
to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and maintained
to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundations
Concrete Driveway
Since the driveway can tolerate some amount of heaving the 18 inch
on site soil over excavation and replacement may be reduced to 6 inches if
some amount of small slab cracking are tolerable The subgrade soils
should be maintained in a moist condition and prevented from drying out
prior to concrete placement
Joints are recommended in the driveway slab at about 8 to 10 foot
intervals and at discontinuities in order to allow for some relative
movement of the concrete

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field borings Variations of
conditions between the borings may occur and the nature and extent of
these variations may not become evident until construction If
variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations given in this report
The locations of the field borings were approximately determined by
taping from structures indicated on the Site Plan by Innovative
Architectural Designs Inc Elevations of the data points were
approximately determined by interpolation from elevation points on the
same plan The physical location and elevation of the field data points
should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the method
used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross sections are based upon interpolation between field
data points and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
