GEOTECHNICAL ENGINEERING EVALUATION
EXCAVATION OF SECONDARY CLARIFIER TANK NO 2
LIHUE WASTEWATER TREATMENT PLANT EXPANSION PHASE IV
This letter report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering evaluation
performed for the excavation of the Secondary Clarifier Tank No located
within the Lihue Wastewater Treatment Plant in Lihue on the island of Kauai Hawaii
PROJECT BACKGROUND
The project site is located adjacent to the southwest corner of Lihue Airport on
the island of Kauai Hawaii As part of the expansion program at the Lihue Wastewater
Treatment Plant it is proposed to increase the capacity of SC 2 by extending the depth
of the existing tank floor
The existing SC 2 consists of a circular 60 foot diameter tank with elevations
extending from about 125 feet Mean Sea Level MSL near the top of the perimeter
wail down to about 110 5 feet MSL near the bottom of the center of the existing tank
floor as shown on Plate 1 In general the existing tank floor slopes downward at an
inclination of approximately ten horizontal to one vertical 10H 1V radially toward the
center
Based on the construction plans the elevation of the existing tank floor is at
approximately 113 5 feet MSL near the perimeter and the proposed new tank floor
elevation will be at about 106 5 feet MSL Considering the approximately 1 feet of
slab and cushion thickness below the new tank floor an excavation depth on the order
of about 9 feet below the existing tank floor is anticipated at the perimeter of SC 2

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our consultation was to review the existing soil data presented
in the Foundation Investigation Report prepared by Dames Moore dated December
26 1990 and to obtain an overview of the anticipated subsurface conditions at the
SC 2 site to evaluate the stability of the proposed near vertical excavation slope at
SC 2 Our work was performed in general accordance with our revised fee proposal
dated February 21st Our scope of work included the following tasks and work efforts
1 Review of the construction plans and foundation investigation report
prepared by Dames Moore
2 Analyses of the excavation and evaluation of slope stabilization method
3 Preparation of this letter report summarizing our work on the project and
presenting our findings and recommendations
SUMMARY OF FOUNDATION INVESTIGATION REPORT
Based on the Dames Moore report the site is generally underlain by soils
derived from the decomposition of the Koloa Volcanic Series basalt rock The surface
soil generally grades to more rock like with depth to the maximum drilled depth of about
30 feet below the surface Groundwater was measured in four borings at about 17 to
25 feet below the surface corresponding to about 104 6 to 110 5 feet MSL
Based on laboratory tests the cohesion values of the tested samples retrieved
from various borings and depths generally exceed 1 250 pounds per square foot p s f
Based on our linear regression analysis of the direct shear test results the mean
cohesion of the samples may be about 475 p s f with an internal friction angle of about
52 degrees Considering the lower boundary value the internal friction angle may be
about 36 degrees Graphic presentation of the direct shear test results are shown on

EXCAVATION METHOD
Based on the current excavation concept we understand that the following
construction procedure will generally be used for the excavation of SC 2
1
A John Deere 690 excavator will be utilized to demolish the existing
concrete tank floor
The excavator will remove soil from a bench at the existing tank floor
The excavation will be advanced to the desired depth with a near vertical
slope inclination The spoils will be removed from within the tank by the
excavator onto the outside edge of SC 2 generally in the area where the
inside of SC 2 has not been excavated
A 25 000 pound rubber tired loader will remove the spoils from the outside
edge of SC 2
After the excavator has removed the existing tank floor slab the machine
will excavate to the bottom of the new tank floor
The remaining excavated spoil will be placed into skip boxes to be lifted
out of the tank by a 25 ton hydraulic crane
The near vertical cut slope face will be covered with a non woven fabric
such as Supac 4NP or equivalent Approximately six different concrete
placements will be needed for the construction of the new slab
Construction of the new perimeter wall will not take place until about three
weeks after initial excavation For the construction of the new perimeter
wall concrete will be placed directly against the near vertical cut face

long term stability Therefore it is our opinion that if the slope is excavated to near
vertical the slope may be considered stable without any shoring for a short duration
It should be noted that the presence of groundwater and surface water runoff will
significantly reduce the factors of safey indicated in our analysis Based on our
telephone conversation we understand that groundwaier was not encountered during
the excavation down to approximately 105 feet MSL at Clarifier Tank No 1
Therefore we have been advised to exclude groundwater conditions in our analysis for
the excavation in SC 2
Based on the boring data the underlying soils may be considered to be over
consolidated materials Therefore it is possible that during the excavation which
results in release of vertical soil pressure base heaving may be observed The
heaving will likely reduce the stability of the near vertical cut slope Therefore it is
prudent to limit the excavation width and reduce the overall open excavation exposure
time prior to the perimeter wall construction
In addition to the soil properties the stability of the near vertical cut slope in
SC 2 will also be greatly influenced by construction procedure and related environ
mental factors such as but not limited to drainage and rainfall In general we believe
that the proposed excavation procedures may be adopted provided that the following
recommendations are implemented
1 A steel plate should be placed on the edge of SC 2 to support the 25 ton
hydraulic crane and the CAT 235 excavator and to reduce and distribute
the load surcharge on the existing perimeter wail It should be noted that
vibration generated from the movement of the hydraulic crane and
excavator can cause instability of the excavated slope therefore use of
the crane and excavator near the top of SC 2 should be minimized

2 Based on the available laboratory test results the underlying subsoils
have very high in situ moisture contents Therefore when exposed to air
the exposed soils are likely to exhibit high shrink swell potential due to
fluctuations in the moisture content This will likely increase the sloughing
potential of the cut slope and potential instability to the slope To reduce
the potential of soil sloughing due to loss of moisture or erosion we
recommend that geotextile fabric such as Supac 4NP or equivalent be
secured or nailed onto the slope face immediately after the excavation
3 Excavation work is not recommended during downpour period to reduce
the erosion potential In addition the excavated slope should be
protected from saturation due to the rains and other sources of water
infiltration
4 Diversion ditches dikes or other suitable means should be used to
prevent surface water from entering the excavation Therefore adequate
drainage should be provided for the area adjacent to the excavation and
to prevent seepage water flowing through the excavated slope
5 Since the actual excavation depth below the existing ground is on the
order of about 20 feet we believe that it is prudent to expose only up to
a maximum width of about 40 feet for each near vertical cut section Ifa
larger exposed section of the near vertical cut is desired there is a higher
level of risk associated with the stability of the excavation since many
factors can influence the stability of the near vertical cut slope
6 Any loose material and or slough observed prior to concrete pour of the
wall should be removed and disposed
7 In general a rubber tired loader may be driven up to 2 feet from the edge
of the tank to remove spoils generated from the tank excavation on a
limited basis Other vehicular traffic should be diverted a minimum of
15 feet away from the outer edge of the tank prior to the completion of the
permanent wall With the use of a steel plate a 25 ton hydraulic crane
or CAT 235 excavator may be stationed at least 2 feet from the edge of
the tank however vibrations generated from the movements of these
machines can cause instability of the excavated slope

EXCAVATION OBSERVATION
We understand that full time observation by a representative from our office is
considered cost prohibitive Therefore daily inspection of excavations and the adjacent
areas should be made for evidence of a situation that may result in possible hazardous
working conditions As a minimum we recommend that periodic site visits be made
by a representative from our office especially after rain storms or other hazard
increasing occurrences to observe compliance with the intent of the recommendations
and to expedite suggestions for design changes that may be required in the event that
subsurface conditions differ from those considered in our analyses The recom
mendations presented in this report are contingent upon such observations
