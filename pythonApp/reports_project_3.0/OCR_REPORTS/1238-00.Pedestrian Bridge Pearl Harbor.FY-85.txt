In accordance with our proposal of we have
performed a foundation investigation at the above referenced site
The purpose of the foundation investigation was to explore the
subsurface conditions at the site to provide data for use in the
design of the foundations of the proposed pedestrian bridge
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests which
were used in the engineering analyses that formulated our conclusions
and recommendations are presented in the appendices of this report
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
SITE DESCRIPTION
The site is located in the U S Naval Submarine Base at the
intersection of North Road and Bole Street Pearl Harbor Oahu Hawaii
The area is currently used for vehicular traffic and parking
Most of the site is paved with asphaltic concrete
The site is quite level with surface elevation at approximately
13 feet above sea level or 113 feet based on U S Navy Datum MLLW
100
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a single span concrete pedestrian
bridge across North Road It is anticipated that the piers at each
end of the span will be supported by spread foundations on the level
area Additionally a shorter pier abutment will be required on the
east side to support the access ramp It is anticipated that the
abutment retaining wall will be 4 5 feet high located on the lower
portion of the existing slope adjacent to Building 1262
SUBSURFACE EXPLORATION
Field Exploration
Three borings were drilled at the site to depths of 18 0 and 31 5
feet below the existing ground surface Three additional test pits
were also hand excavated at the top of an existing slope bank where
the future ramp footing will be located north of Building 1262

Soil Conditions
Our borings encountered two unique sets of soil conditions In
Boring 1 after penetrating a 4 inch thick pavement section our
exploration encountered man made fill to a depth of 15 feet below the
existing ground surface This fill consists of medium stiff clayey
Silts and loose to medium dense sands which is believed to be trench
backfill for utilities
Below the fill Boring 1 encountered stiff to very stiff clayey
silt This clayey silt is an alluvial deposit and extends to the
maximum depth drilled of 31 5 feet
In Boring 2 where the western pier footing will be located our
exploration encountered 18 inches of topsoil underlain by 8 5 feet of
medium hard to hard weathered volcanic tuff Below the tuff stratum
our boring penetrated very stiff Clayey silt an alluvial deposit
which extends to the maximum depth explored of 18 0 feet
Subsequent to encountering utility trench backfill Boring 3 was
drilled about 60 feet east of Boring 1 where the original eastern pier
footing was planned At about a 3 foot depth volcanic tuff or
mudrock was encountered in Boring 3 below the upper road pavement and
select borrow fill

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
LENA LONG
Spread footing foundations may be used to support the proposed
pedestrian bridge footings provided that our recommendations are
Closely followed
In general the footings should be embedded a minimum of 24
inches below the lowest adjacent grade and bear on the underlying
brown weathered tuff formation
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1238 00 Page 5
At the proposed pier foundation location on the east side of
North Road where Boring 1 was drilling deep utility trench backfill
was encountered We recommend that this pier footing be relocated
toward the eastern direction where dense volcanic tuff formation was
encountered at a depth of about 3 feet below the road grade at the
Boring 3 location
For the eastern abutment wall footing to be located on the 8 foot
high slope our test pits hand excavated at the top of the slope
indicated weathered tuff formation at about 2 foot depth below the top
of the bank We anticipate that volcanic tuff formation should be
encountered at the proposed abutment wall footing bottom elevation of
116 5 feet A minimum horizontal set back distance of 5 feet should
be provided between the outer edge of the footing and the Slope face
For footing bearing on weathered tuff a design bearing value
of 4000 p s f may be used A Sliding friction factor of 0 45 is
recommended For passive earth pressure resistance against the side
of the pier footing on level ground an equivalent fluid density of
300 p c f may be used
Estimated settlements due to the foundation loads on the volcanic
tuff would be minimum and should be less than one fourth of an inch
total

Lateral Earth Pressures
For the 5 foot high abutment wall planned on the slope the
following lateral earth pressure can be used
Walls unrestrained at the top 40 p s f equivalent fluid
active condition level pressure per foot of depth
granular backfill
Wall restrained at the top 350 p s f equivalent fluid
at rest condition pressure per foot of depth
level backfill
Retaining wall backfill should consist of granular materials
such as select borrow or crusher waste Impervious soil such as
silty clay should be used for the top one foot of the backfill to
reduce seepage of surface runoff behind the walls
A properly designed subsurface drainage system should be utilized
behind the walls
Design Review
Plans for foundation site grading and utility construction
should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written
comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included in the project plans and specifications

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
EE EEMUURIENG
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during
earthwork for the abutment wall construction slope cuts and to
monitor the contractor s grading operations
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be examined
by a soils engineer during construction to see that adequate bearing
has been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent
with the findings of our investigation
Any imported backfill material required should be tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above inspections

Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encountered and cannot
be fully determined by reconnaissance and borings test pits These
unforeseen conditions such as pockets of organic soils trash fill
or expansive soils may occur in localized areas Therefore additional
expenditures may be needed during construction to attain a properly
constructed project Some contingency fund is thus recommended to
accommodate these possible costs
