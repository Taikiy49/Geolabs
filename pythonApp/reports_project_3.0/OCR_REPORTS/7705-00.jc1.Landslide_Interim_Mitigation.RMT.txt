GEOTECHNICAL ENGINEERING RECONNAISSANCE REPORT 
HEEIA FISH POND LANDSLIDE REPAIR 
KANEOHE, OAHU, HAWAII 
Dear Mr. Ring: 
This report presents our preliminary assessment of Heeia Fish Pond landslide and 
summarizes our geotechnical engineering recommendations resulting from our site 
reconnaissance. The findings and recommendations presented herein are subject to the 
limitations noted at the end of this report. 
Detailed recommendations will be included in our formal exploration evaluation 
report, pending our field exploration and slope stability analysis. The formal exploration 
report will be submitted upon completion of the above-mentioned engineering efforts. 
The project site is located at an existing residential neighborhood near the southern 
tip of the Heeia Fish Pond in the Kaneohe area on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii.  The project 
location and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map, Plate 1.  We 
understand that the existing residential subdivision was initially constructed in the late 60s 
and early 70s.  The rear slope behind the residential dwellings generally slopes down toward 
the lower fish pond property at a gradient between about one horizontal to one vertical 
(1H:1V) and 3H:1V. 
Based on the information provided and our site visit, we understand that a portion of 
the rear slope behind a residential dwelling has slid down towards the lower fish pond 
property after recent heavy rains.  The landslide has resulted in a scar face about 3 to 5 feet 
deep and approximately 20 to 25 feet high that is devoid of vegetation.  The landslide soil 
debris and a section of the damaged CRM retaining wall at the bottom of the failed slope 
had been removed at the time of our site visit. 
Based on our field observation, we believe the landslide remediation may consist of 
a mechanically stabilized earth (MSE) structure as a permanent stabilization solution. As an 
interim mitigation, we recommend installing an Anchored Geogrid System (AGS) consisting 
of Percussion Driven Earth Anchor (PDEA) with a layer of geogrid and turf reinforcing

Interim Mitigation – Anchored Geogrid System (AGS) 
The AGS is a slope treatment that involves the installation of geogrid panels 
anchored onto the slope face with a driven anchor tie-down system in an effort to prevent 
the near-surface soils from moving/falling. A layer of erosion control matting with hydro-
mulch seeding is usually placed between the slope surface and the geogrid. Considering it 
is an interim remediation and the permanent repair will be provided within 12 to 18 months 
of the design/bidding period, we recommend omitting the erosion control matting and the 
hydro-mulch seeding, so that minimal clearing and grubbing will be required when the 
construction of the permanent retaining structure is underway. A schematic cross-section of 
the interim slope repair is also presented on Plate 2. 
It should be noted that the slope repair limits and dimensions are approximate and 
subject to verification/modification during the construction. The topographic map shown on 
the site plan was referenced from the Topographic Survey Map by ControlPoint dated 
July 3, 2006. An updated topographic survey should be conducted prior to the construction, 
to verify the slope conditions and construction quantities. 
We recommend installing the anchors in a stagger pattern of 5 feet by 5 feet 
center-to-center along the slope surface with additional anchor at the lower spots to 
conform the irregular slope surface. Each anchor should be 2 feet long with a working 
load of about 200 pounds. We recommend locking all the anchors in-place to the working 
loads after installation to engage the geogrid panel tightly against the slope surface. 
Prior to installation of the AGS, we recommend removal and disposal of loose debris 
from the landslide slope failure. However, we do not recommend regrading the slope, to 
reduce potential risk of further destabilizing the existing steep slope. 
Based on the approximate slope interim mitigation limits and quantities, we estimate 
the construction cost to be on the order of magnitude of about $100,000. Details of the cost 
breakdown are presented on Plate 3. 
Limitations 
The analyses and recommendations submitted herein are based in part upon 
information obtained from our site reconnaissance in the project site. Variations of subsoil 
conditions may occur, and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident 
until construction is underway.  If variations then appear evident, Geolabs should be 
contacted to reevaluate the recommendations presented herein.
