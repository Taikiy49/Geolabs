This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations and testing 
services performed during site grading construction for the Wai Kai Trail Grading – 
Increment 1 project from July 2019 to February 2021 in accordance with our revised fee 
proposal dated February 13, 2019. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The proposed new trail project is located at the western end of the existing 
Wai Kai Lagoon at the Haseko development in Ewa Beach on the Island of Oahu, 
Hawaii. It is proposed to construct a 20 to 28-foot wide trail consisting of an 8-foot wide 
concrete fire lane and walkway, and a 12 to 20-foot wide crushed coral fire access lane. 
In addition, we understand that an 8-foot wide infiltration trench/swale will be 
constructed on the east side of the crushed coral fire access lane. The Project Location 
Map and Overall Site Plan and Site Plans are provided as Plates 1, and 2.1 through 2.4, 
respectively. 
Based on the grading plans, we anticipate site grading for the project will involve 
cuts up to about 3 feet deep and fills up to about 5 feet thick to achieve the design 
finished grades. 
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services for 
the site grading construction at the project site. 
SITE GRADING 
We observed the site grading work for the Wai Kai Trail Grading – Increment 1 
project from July 2019 to February 2021. The site grading work consisted of excavation up 
to about 3 feet in height, and placement and compaction of fills on the order of up to about

of the on-site and imported materials utilized as fill and backfill materials and transported 
them to our laboratory for testing. The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture

The fill and backfill materials were moisture-conditioned to above the optimum

During the site grading, periodic field density tests were performed in general 
accordance with ASTM D6938 (Nuclear Gauge Method). Results of the compaction tests 
performed are summarized on Plates 4.1 through 4.8 of this report. These tests were 
performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills at the 
site. Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on-site 
survey stakes. These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the 
degree implied by the methods used. Areas assessed as unsatisfactory were reworked 
until the specified minimum compaction was obtained and/or approved by our field 
representative based on visual observation and hand probing. 
Our services consisted of part-time field observation of the contractor’s fill 
placement, subgrade preparation, and compaction procedures and confirmation of 
subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above grading operations. 
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the 
referenced tests, it is our opinion that the site grading work for the above project observed 
and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report was performed in conformance
