Dear Mr Ikeda
We have prepared this addendum to update the geotechnical recommendations
presented in the referenced report prepared for the project
We understand that the new pavement will be constructed in phases Based on
the available plans some sections of the existing road will be left open to allow the flow
of vehicular traffic The closed sections of the roadway will be removed and new
pavements will be constructed After the new pavements are in place the adjacent
existing road will be removed to allow construction of the new pavements
As stated in the above referenced report a drainage layer will be required for the
new pavements The material for the drainage layer typically consists of open graded
gravel that requires confinement to remain in place
When the existing pavement adjacent to the newly constructed pavement which
consists of a drainage layer is removed there will be no confinement of the drainage
layer Because there will be no confinement we believe that the drainage layer would
not remain in place and will likely migrate to the open excavation where the existing
pavement was removed The migration of the drainage layer would cause undermining
of the new pavement which may result in pavement damage when vehicular traffic
traverses near the edge of the new pavement

To provide confinement for the drainage layer the permeable separator below
the drainage layer should wrap around the drainage layer at the end that is adjacent to
the existing pavement that will be removed in a subsequent phase The permeable
separator should wrap around the drainage layer and extend at least 24 inches at the
top of the drainage layer
Also in Subsection Design Pavement Section of the above referenced
report the 92 Percent Relative Specific Gravity following the 8 0 Inch Untreated
Permeable Base of Option 1 and Option 2 should be omitted
