Field Observations of Slope Bank
Poo Poo Bridge Replacement
County of Maui Hawaii
This letter summarizes our field observations of the existing slope bank which
were made during our site visit on The purpose of our site observations
was to provide an opinion of the present stability of the existing slope bank as related to
the proposed stream bank undermining repair and in light of the recent slope failure
adjacent to the bridge site
At the time of our visit the construction of the bridge structure was close to
completion The new bridge is a single span structure supported on spread footing
foundations bearing on basalt formation The bridge spans over a relatively narrow
gulch with steep side slopes approximately 30 to 40 feet high
The gulch side slopes consist of layered basalt lava flows comprised of very
dense basalt blue rock over weathered clinker near the present stream bottom The
upper 5 to 10 feet of the dense basalt exhibited columnar relatively close vertical
jointing Most of the remaining dense basalt exposed was massive with many random
joints and fractures on the exposed surface The clinker layer exposed near the stream
bottom was weathered and appeared to have been eroded by the stream The erosion
extended into the clinker layer for 5 feet or more on both sides of the gulch The plans
called for repair of this stream bank undermining by placement of cobbles and boulders
grouted in place to fill the undercut areas

The issue of concern is the present stability of the steep undercut slope as the
repair work will require that men and equipment work below the overhanging basalt
layer for an extended period of time The stability of the overhanging basalt layer
depends primarily on the extent of the fractures in the formation The inherent strength
of the basalt is very high however the strength along the fractures may be very low
Therefore if sufficient fractures are present along key failure planes instability could
result It is very difficult to evaluate the stability of the overhanging basait layer since
the fracture pattern is generally random
In light of the above uncertainties and particularly since a failure of a similar
overhanging basalt slope has already taken place near the bridge structure it is our
opinion that the present stability of the overhanging basalt layer above the proposed
repair areas is questionable We recommend that alternative methods to repair the
undermined areas be considered to avoid placing men and equipment under the
undermined areas One possible alternative would be to fill the eroded areas entirely
with concrete eliminating the boulders This may be accomplished by placing forms
along the finished face and pumping concrete behind the forms This would eliminate
the need for personnel working beneath the overhanging basalt and would provide an
equivalent or better repair to the undermining
