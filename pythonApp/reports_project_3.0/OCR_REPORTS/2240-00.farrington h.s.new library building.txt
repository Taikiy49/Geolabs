INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the proposed new library facility at Farrington
High School in the Kalihi area of Honolulu Oahu Hawaii
The general location of the site is shown on the Project Location
Map Plate 1
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
near surface subsoil conditions at the site to develop a generalized soil
and rock data set for the formulation of geotechnical engineering
recommendations pertinent to the design of the subject project
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located on North King Street at the intersection
of Kalihi Street in the Kalihi area of Honolulu Oahu Hawaii The site is
bounded by North King Street to the south Kalihi Street to the west and
the remainder of the Farrington High School campus to the north and east
The general location of the project site and its vicinity is shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1
The site is currently open space on the school campus
The topographic relief of the site is generally flat In the
northern end of the project site there is a low retaining wall that
provides about three 3 to four 4 feet of grade separation
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to demolish the existing retaining wall grade the
site and construct a new library on the site It is anticipated that the
site grading will be minimal and the library building will be a
single story masonry structure with a steel framed roof Estimated wall
loads are on the order of three 3 kips per linear foot and estimated
column loads are on the order of 80 kips
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The field exploration consisted of drilling eight 8 borings and two
2 auger probes at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2 The boring depths ranged between 13 0 and 15 0 feet and the
auger probe depths were 4 0 feet below the existing ground surface Our
field work indicated that in general the site is underlain by a thin
surface layer of stiff brown clay Adobe overlying basaltic rock The
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2240 00 Page 3
depth to rock encountered in the drilled borings and probes varies from
about 1 5 to 5 0 feet below the existing ground surface with an average
thickness of about 2 5 feet
Our laboratory tests and previous experience indicate that surface
clay soils have high swelling potential
Groundwater was neither encountered in the drilled borings nor in the
probes at the time of our exploration
The Boring and Probing Logs are presented in Appendix A of this
report
FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that the surface and near surface of
the site is underlain by stiff clay overlying moderately to highly
weathered basalt This clay soil has a high expansion potential Special
site grading is recommended to reduce potential foundation movements
Foundations
Based upon the boring and laboratory test data the on site brown
clay soils Adobe have moderate to high expansion potential In order
to reduce potential foundation and or building distresses we recommend
that this material be removed down to the rock formation below the
proposed building location for a minimum of three 3 feet beyond the
exterior building line The clay soils removed from within the building
area should be disposed of properly off site The over excavated area
should be backfilled with non expansive select material placed in maximum
10 inch loose horizontal lifts and compacted to a minimm of 90 of its
maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2240 00 Page 4
Foundations bearing on replacement fill placed in accordance with the
above recommendations may be designed for an allowable bearing capacity of
3000 pounds per square foot The footings should be embedded a minimm of
18 inches below the lowest adjacent exterior grade
Slabs On Grade
It is anticipated that the floors of the proposed building will be
concrete slabs on grade A 4 inch layer of 3B Fine gravel should be
provided below the slabs as cushion fill
To reduce future subgrade soils from heaving due to subsequent
saturation we recommend that the subgrade soils should be kept
continuously moist by water sprinkling prior to concrete placement
In order to prevent future moisture infiltration and subsequent
damage to the floor coverings an impervious moisture membrane may be
considered below the slabs
Site Grading
The exact details of the proposed site grading are not currently
available However it is assumed that some minor amounts of grading work
will be required
Prior to the commencement of any earthwork all areas within the
grading limits should be cleared of all vegetation and deleterious
materials The resulting grub and spoil material should be disposed of
properly off site or stockpiled for use in landscaping Any soft weak
or yielding areas disclosed during the clearing operations should be
over excavated to firm ground and the resulting excavation should be
backfilled with well compacted engineered fill
Imported fill material should be granular non expansive soil
generally less than three 3 inches in size and capable of forming a
dense stable mass when compacted

All fills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding ten 10
inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum

Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comment
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this review
is not made the geotechnical engineer camnot assume responsibility for
misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the
excavation and foundation phases of the work This is to observe
compliance with the design concepts specifications or recommendations and
to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ
from that anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations
given in this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as borings
and probes Variations of conditions between the field data points may

occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become
evident until construction If variations then appear evident it will
be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations given in this report
The locations of the field data points were approximately determined
by taping from reference points shown on the Boring Location Plan by
Architects dated January 1989 Elevations of the
data points were estimated from the same plan The physical location and
elevation of the field data points should be considered accurate only to
the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross sections are based upon interpolation between field
data points and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
Water level data from the field data points were collected at the
times given on the graphic representations and or in the text of this
report This data has been reviewed and interpretations made in the
formulation of this report However it must be noted that fluctuation
may occur due to variations in rainfall tides temperature and other
factors

Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling
eight 8 borings and two 2 probes to depths of 4 0 to 15 0 feet
below the existing ground surface at the approximate locations shown on
the Site Plan Plate 2 Truck mounted drilling equipment was used to
drill the borings and probings

textural examination in the field by our engineer who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory All soils
were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A summary of the materials encountered are
presented in the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 10
Soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving a 2 inch
with a 140 pound hammer free falling 30 inches The blow counts to
drive the sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the Boring Logs at
the appropriate sample depths
