PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located within the existing Kailua Regional Wastewater Treatment
Plant WWTP in the Kailua area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and
vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 Based on the field
exploration at the site the proposed UV Disinfection Facility site is generally underlain by
near surface fills followed by highly compressible soils extending to depths of over 60 feet
below the existing ground surface Basalt rock formation is generally present at depths of
about 100 feet below the existing ground surface

Based on the construction drawings it is proposed to expand the existing Kailua
VWTP with the construction of the UV Disinfection Facility The UV Disinfection Facility will
be supported on 12 inch square precast prestressed concrete piles The pile length
recommended for the facility was about 100 feet below the existing ground surface Due to
the limited number of piles a test pile program was not conducted for this project Therefore
the production piles were cast a minimum of 10 feet longer than the recommended pile
length to account for potential variation in the subsoil conditions at the subject site
Based on the plans provided we anticipate that the cut off elevation of the piles will
range from about 4 to 11 feet below the existing ground elevation The piles were designed
with an allowable compression load capacity of 70 tons 140 kips per pile We understand
that the piles will derive support from a combination of end bearing and skin friction or from
end bearing on the basalt rock formation underlying the site The pile locations are to be
predrilled to a minimum depth of 10 feet below the existing ground surface to reduce the
potential for vibration impacts to the existing structures
Hammer and Driving System
The pile driving subcontractor for this project was Lahaina Pier Pile Company Inc
A single acting diesel hammer was proposed for the pile driving operations
by Lahaina Pier Pile Company Based on the pile driving equipment data form provided
the hammer has a ram weight of 3 528 pounds and a maximum stroke of
maximum pump setting Pump Setting 4 Schematic details of the driving system are
presented on the Pile and Driving Equipment Data Form Plate 2 The hammer has a
minimum rated energy of 14 100 foot pounds corresponding to Pump Setting 1
Based on the subsurface conditions and the driving conditions anticipated a pile
cushion consisting of 9 inches of p ywood cushion blocks was planned for the pile driving
operations at the site The plywood cushion blocks generally have a cross section area of
approximately 324 square inches
Pile Details
As previously indicated the specified piles will be 12 inch square precast prestressed
concrete piles with lengths of about 110 feet The piles were designed with an allowable
compression load capacity of 70 tons 140 kips per pile The piles will be cast and driven
in two pieces 55 feet per segment connected with a 6 foot long splice can
We understand that the piles will be cast from concrete with a minimum 28 day
unconfined compressive strength of 6 000 pounds per square inch psi We understand that
the effective prestress level of the prestressed pile is approximately 900 psi Based on this
information the maximum allowable compressive stress in a precast prestressed concrete
pile during pile driving is about 4 200 psi The maximum allowable tensile stress developed
in a precast prestressed concrete pile during pile driving is approximately 900 psi

WAVE EQUATION ANALYSES
A wave equation analysis was performed based on the pile driving system proposed
by Lahaina Pier Pile Company and the subsurface conditions presented in the
Geotechnical Engineering Exploration Report The wave equation analysis was performed
using the computer program GRLWEAP Wave Equation Analysis of Pile Foundations
which is an extension of the WEAP87 program developed under the sponsorship of the
Federal Highway Administration
Pile Capacity
Based on our wave equation analyses we believe that the proposed pile driving
equipment consisting of the Delmag D 16 32 hammer is capable of driving the specified pile
section to the desired ultimate pile capacity of 280 kips Based on our analyses and the
subsurface conditions anticipated at the site we recommend that either of the following pile
driving criteria be used to develop the ultimate pile capacity of 280 kips based on the
110 foot long piles A graph showing the pile capacity versus the driving resistance is
provided on Plate 3 1
I The piles may be terminated when the last foot of pile driving achieves a
penetration resistance of at least 32 blows per foot
2 The piles may be terminated when the last 10 feet of pile driving achieves a
penetration resistance of at least 15 blows per foot when the pile has reached
a tip elevation of 100 feet MSL
Driving Stresses
The computed maximum compressive and tensile stresses in the pile during pile
driving are 3 030 and 620 psi respectively based on an ultimate pile capacity of 280 kips
A graph showing the computed maximum compressive and tensile stresses during pile
driving is provided on Plate 3 2
As previously indicated the maximum allowable compressive and tensile stresses
during pile driving for the specified pile section are 4 200 psi and 900 psi
respectively Based on our analyses the estimated compressive and tensile stresses
induced in the pile during the pile driving operations for the specified ultimate capacity of 280
kips are within the range specified for this project The range of stresses that is allowed
during pile driving has been shaded on Plate 3 2 for ease of reference

PILE DRIVING OBSERVATIONS
At the beginning of the foundation installation the pile locations were predrilled to a
minimum depth of 10 feet below the existing ground surface to reduce the potential vibration
impacts on the nearby existing structures The predrilling operation was performed by
using a large truck mounted drill rig
A Delmag single acting diesel hammer Model D16 32 mounted on the fixed leads
of a Manitowoc crane was used to drive the 12 inch square precast prestressed concrete
piles The hammer has a rated energy between 14 100 and 39 400 foot
pounds Based on our field observations the pile hammer generally delivered a driving
energy of approximately 30 000 to 34 000 foot pounds per blow during the majority of the pile
driving operations The hammer was equipped with an energy level such that driving within
the soft loose deposits may be achieved at a reduced energy level to minimize tensile stress
development within the pile which could damage the pile
Fifty seven 57 piles including two replacement piles were driven between August
26 and for the subject project The approximate locations of the piles
driven are shown on the Pile Location Plan Plate 4 The pile driving operations were
conducted under the near continuous observation of a field representative of our firm During
the pile driving operations logs were maintained for each pile The logs included information
pertaining to pile number date driven predrilled depth blow counts observations size and
length of the piles A Pile Driving Summary is presented by the pile number sequence on
Plates 5 1 and 5 2 Another Pile Driving Summary is presented chronologically by date on
Plates 6 1 and 6 2 The individual pile driving records are provided in the Appendix for
reference
During pile driving it was noted that the splice can for Pile No 49 was broken as the
second piece of the pile was placed and driven In addition it was also noted that Pile No
50 was broken during the pile driving operations Therefore two replacement piles
Pile Nos 49A and 50A were driven to replace Pile Nos 49 and 50 Pile No 49A was
slightly misaligned by about 6 inches to the north of the true alignment The locations of the
two replacement piles were coordinated with the project structural engineer
and are also shown on Plate 4
In addition it was noted that Pile Nos 22 and 38 were slightly out of plumb by about
significant reduction in the vertical and lateral load capacities of the piles
Based on our field observations we believe that the piles for this project have been
installed in general conformance with the project plans and specifications and within the limits
of generally accepted construction practices Based on our observations of the hammer
