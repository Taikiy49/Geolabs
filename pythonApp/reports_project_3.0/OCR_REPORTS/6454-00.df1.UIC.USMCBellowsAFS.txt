INTRODUCTION
In accordance with the request of from MCB Hawaii Environmental
Compliance and Protection Department Geolabs Inc conducted the performance testing
and annual inspection of the injection wells at Marine Corps Training Area Bellows in
Waimanalo on the Island of Oahu Hawaii This inspection is for the 2011 permit year
April 2010 to March 2011 This annual inspection and performance testing was
conducted on March 24 2011 in general accordance with our proposal to the MCB Hawaii
Environmental Compliance and Protection Department
This report summarizes the findings from the performance testing and inspection
This report presents discussions and recommendations regarding the injection wells at the
project site

SECTION 1 GENERAL
1 1
Project Considerations
The project site is situated at the US Marine Corps Training Area Bellows
USMCTAB in Waimanalo on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The site is used primarily as
a training area for military personnel The land area of the project site was formerly a
portion of Bellows Air Force Station until transfer of the property to the USMC Since
the station as a whole is not served by a central sewer system operation of the
individual facilities requires on site treatment and disposal of wastewater generated at
the buildings The permitted facility consists of a series of buildings that were formerly
used by the USAF as a communications facility Wastewater was generated only in
Buildings 700 and 701 Based on past use of the facility and capacity of the wastewater
system the facility is considered to be regulated under the Underground Injection
Control UIC program
The facility uses two seepage pits for the disposal of wastewater These
disposal structures are considered to be injection wells under the UIC program and are
operated under a permit issued by the Hawaii Department of Health DOH As a permit
condition DOH requires that injection wells be inspected on a regular basis by an
engineer or geologist and that a report on the current status of the wells be submitted to
DOH This report is intended to satisfy the requirement for annual inspection given in
the permit for Marine Corps Training Area Bellows and provide performance data for
each of the wells

SECTION 2 BACKGROUND
2 1
General Information
The USMC assumed control of the land area for the Marine Corps Training Area
Bellows which contains former Buildings 700 and 701 of Bellows Air Force Station
These buildings are served by a septic system with two seepage pits connected in
series to a septic tank The USMC has been granted a system permit by DOH to
operate the injection wells under the Underground Injection Control program In this
permit the facility is identified by Permit No UO 2209 and the wells are identified as
Well Nos 1 and 2 Table 1 provides a summary of the injection wells as listed in the
UIC permit
Based on information provided by the MCB Hawaii Environmental Compliance
and Protection Department the buildings have not been used on a regular basis since
control of the area was assumed from the USAF Review of the previous reports
prepared by EKNA Services Inc for the 2010 permit year indicates that the flow to the
wells has been estimated to be less than 1 000 gallons per day Since the buildings
have not been used regularly the current rate of wastewater disposal is minimal
The wastewater system for Well Nos 1 and 2 consist of a septic tank and the
two injection wells as the disposal component Flow through the system and for
injection of the effluent is by gravity
Building 701 was used as a generator building and wastewater generation in
Building 701 when it was in use was limited to a single stall restroom Building 701 is
no longer operational and the restroom is no longer functional Based on the previous
report by EKNA Services Inc Building 700 a former communications building has
been secure and inaccessible during every visit to the site including previous
inspections made for the USAF However their visual observation of the exterior of the
building indicates that there were most likely office spaces and living quarters in the
building

Field Observations
On March 24 2011 a visit was made to Marine Corps Training Area Bellows by
Mr Larson of MCB Hawaii Environmental Compliance and Protection Department and
Geolabs Inc to make observations of the operational condition and status of the wells
Following inspection and measurement performance testing was performed for each of
the wells Following are synopses of observations made and data collected during the
site visit
1
Buildings 700 and 701 are military use buildings situated on Marine Corps
Training Area Bellows The injection wells are connected in series and
are situated in an open area to the east of the buildings There are
four manhole covers at this location Based on field observations the
two manholes closest to the buildings are the openings to a septic tank
Therefore some treatment is given to the sewage prior to placement in
the wells and disposal when the system is in use
2
As previously discussed the facility has not been operational since the
property was transferred to the USMC Therefore the wastewater system
has been essentially inactive for at least six years
3
Observation of the interior of the septic tank indicated that both
compartments contained water with some sludge
4
The well heads consist of precast concrete cesspool lids topped with
Sanitary Sewer cast iron manholes Access to the wells is through the
manholes
5
No evidence of operational problems such as overflows were noted in
the cesspool during the site visit No significant environmental conditions
were noted in the vicinity of the well Based on the hydrogeological
conditions in the area it is believed that the injection interval of the wells is
dune sand Groundwater is believed to be saline and tidally influenced
6
The interiors of the wells were sounded and probed using 3 4 inch PVC
pipe to determine depth to fluid in the well approximate thickness of
sludge and total depth
7

Performance Testing
Concurrent with the inspection of the wells testing was conducted to evaluate
the performance of the wells This testing was performed by rapidly placing about

1 500 gallons of water into each well and measuring the change in head levels in a
modified slug test
A fire hose provided by MCB Hawaii Environmental Compliance and Protection
Department connected to a nearby fire hydrant was used to inject water to each of the
wells The water was introduced at a rate on the order of 62 gallons per minute The
rate and volume of injection was recorded using a GF Signet paddle wheel flow
sensor with a totalizing function
Head levels were measured during the introduction of water and as the water
level in the well dropped after placement of the water Readings were taken at an
interval of one minute during injection of water and during the initial infiltration period
The interval was subsequently increased as the rate of drop decreased The test was
continued until the rate of decrease in the water level became very small
Since the actual geometry of the wells is not available and the actual
characteristics of the receiving aquifer are not known the performance test data were
not subjected to rigorous analyses to estimate hydraulic conductivities or maximum
capacities The recovery graphs were reviewed on a subjective basis to estimate the
performance of the wells

SECTION 3 DISCUSSION AND FINDINGS
Information provided by MCB Hawaii Environmental Compliance and Protection
Department indicates that the wells have not been used since they were acquired from
the USAF Previous information indicated that they were used for the disposal of
wastewater at average estimated rates of about 900 gallons per day However the
wastewater system has been essentially inactive for at least six years
In general the injection wells appear to be in good operational condition The
field inspection did not reveal indications of failure excessive head build up or overflow
from either of the wells In general most of the system components appear to be in
similar condition as noted in the previous annual inspection conducted by EKNA
Services Inc
Based on Geolabs Inc s observations at the site it appears that there is a drain
line connecting the trench drains in Building 701 and Well No 1 It also appears that in
the past this drain line allowed for introduction of petroleum products into the well
Although the generator operations in the building have been discontinued it is our
opinion that the drain line should be isolated from the well to comply with the
requirements of the UIC permit
When considering the limited use of the septic system at the site and the costs
associated with maintaining the UIC permit e g inspection testing monitoring and
reporting it is our opinion that it may be more economical to close the wells and replace
them with a leachfield or similar shallow disposal component There is adequate space
at the site for the installation of a leachfield and there appears to be adequate depth to
ground water Therefore we recommend closing the wells and replacing them with a
leachfield
During the interim until funding can be procured for the well closure and
leachfield system installation it is recommended that the permit be granted inactive
status since the system is not in use This status should include a waiver of future
performance testing
