Dear Mr Toyota
This letter report presents the results of our percolation tests performed in
support of the design of the leaching field system at the Hawaii Kai Championship Golf
Course in Hawaii Kai on the Island of Oahu Hawaii
Project Considerations
The project involves replacing the existing cesspools for two restrooms at the
Hawaii Kai Championship Golf Course facility in Hawaii Kai on the Island of Oahu
Hawaii Planned improvements may involve constructing a shallow leach field or a deeper
seepage pit Two boreholes each were drilled in the vicinity of Comfort Station Nos 1
and 2
Percolation Test
A total of four boreholes were drilled to depths varying from 3 to 14 feet below
the existing ground surface using a Mobile B 80 drill rig equipped with 4 inch diameter
continuous flight augers Groundwater was not encountered in the boreholes at the
time of our exploration
The boreholes in the vicinity of Comfort Station No 1 were drilled to depths of
3 feet and 14 feet below the existing ground surface Materials encountered were silty
sand sandy silt soils The percolation rates were about 15 minute per inch
The boreholes in the vicinity of Comfort Station No 2 were drilled to depths of
3 feet and 10 feet below the existing ground surface Materials encountered were basalt
rock formation The upper portion encountered porous clinker zones with high percolation
rates and the lower portion encountered dense blue rock basalt which did not allow
percolation

Field percolation tests were performed in general accordance with the State of
Systems and Chapter 10 of the Ten State s Standards to evaluate the permeability of
the subsurface soils for disposal of wastewater effluent The tests were performed in a
4 inch diameter borehole with approximately 1 inch of gravel placed at the bottom of the
borehole to protect the bottom of the borehole from scouring and sediments During
testing the borehole was carefully filled with water to a depth of about 6 inches above
the gravel and refilled as necessary The time intervals and water drops were then
recorded to provide data upon which the calculation of the percolation tests was based
