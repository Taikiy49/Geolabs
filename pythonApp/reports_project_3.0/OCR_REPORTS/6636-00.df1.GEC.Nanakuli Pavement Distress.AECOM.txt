SECTION 3.  DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS 
Our field reconnaissance at the project site generally indicated that the existing 
pavements have experienced some distress.  Individual discussions for each street are 
presented below. 
Visual classification was performed in general accordance with the definitions 
described in the “Asphalt Distress Manual” dated June 1997 prepared by the 
Maintenance and Preservation Division (FL-P) of the Facilities Technology Laboratory 
(FL), U.S. Army Construction Engineering Research Laboratories (USACERL). Selected 
pavement distress definitions are presented in the following table. 
ASPHALT PAVEMENT DISTRESSES  
Block Cracking 
Block cracks are inter-connected cracks that divide the pavement 
into approximately rectangular pieces. 
Depression 
Depressions are localized pavement surface areas with elevations 
slightly lower than those of the surrounding pavement. 
Longitudinal 
Cracking 
Longitudinal cracks are cracks parallel to the pavement’s 
centerline or laydown direction. 
Patch 
Patch is an area of pavement that has been replaced with new 
material to repair the existing pavement. 
Pothole 
Potholes are small bowl-shaped (usually less than 30 inches in 
diameter) depressions in the pavement surface. 
Rutting 
A rut is a surface depression in the wheel paths. 
Weathering & 
Raveling 
Weathering and raveling are wearing away of the pavement 
surface due to a loss of asphalt or tar binder and dislodged 
aggregate particles. 
As described above, the existing pavements in the subdivisions generally appear 
to be worn and in need of an overlay or rehabilitation.  In general, we recommend cold 
planing and resurfacing the streets.  The following discuss the general condition of each 
of the individual streets: 
Haleakala Avenue (from Nanakuli Elementary School to the end at 
Nanakuli Avenue) - This street is generally in worn condition with some 
asphalt stripping and polishing of exposed aggregate.  Some utility cuts 
were observed to have evidence of settlement in the subgrade backfill.  
Minor cracking was observed in the pavement surface and was expressed 
as longitudinal cracking beginning to progress to block cracking.

For this street, we recommend cold-planing and overlaying the existing 
pavement surface with a new surface course of asphaltic concrete.  
Where utility cuts are present, we recommend overlaying the trench cuts 
and other patches with Tensar Glass Grid pavement reinforcing material, 
or similar, to reduce the potential of cracking of the new pavement  
surface.  The reinforcing material should be applied prior to placement of 
the new asphalt and extend a minimum of 5 feet beyond outer edges of 
the utility cut or patch. 
Nanakuli Avenue (from Lyman Ranch to Nanakuli Elementary School) - 
This street is generally in worn condition with some asphalt stripping and 
polishing of exposed aggregate.  Some utility cuts were observed to have 
evidence of settlement in the subgrade backfill.  Minor cracking was 
observed in the pavement surface and was expressed as longitudinal 
cracking beginning to progress to block cracking. 
For this street, we recommend cold-planing and overlaying the existing 
pavement surface with a new surface course of asphaltic concrete.  
Where utility cuts are present, we recommend overlaying the trench cuts 
and other patches with Tensar Glass Grid pavement reinforcing material, 
or similar, to reduce the potential of cracking of the new pavement  
surface.  The reinforcing material should be applied prior to placement of 
the new asphalt and extend a minimum of 5 feet beyond outer edges of 
the utility cut or patch. 
Naniahiahi Street - In general, this street shows signs of deterioration with 
worn pavement, low to moderate block cracking and edge raveling along 
its length.  We recommend cold-planing and repaving the surface. 
Naniahiahi Place - This cul-de-sac street is an extension of Naniahiahi 
Street and exhibits similar conditions.  There is also a concrete patched 
pothole on the outbound side near the intersection with Nanakuli Avenue.  
In addition to the cold-planing and resurfacing of this street, we 
recommend excavating the pothole and properly repairing by replacing the 
base course and repaving.  The repaired area should be reinforced using 
pavement reinforcing material as described above. 
Nanaikala Street - Of all the streets in the subdivision, Nanaikala Street 
appeared to be in the poorest condition.  The asphaltic concrete surface is 
worn and developing ruts.  Numerous trench cuts are present as well as 
some major potholes and depressions.  There is moderate to high severity 
block cracking and edge raveling along the street.  We recommend 
cold-planing and resurfacing the surface. The trench cuts should be 
reinforced as described herein.  Discussion on more severe distresses 
and their repairs are presented in Appendix A.

SECTION 4.  LIMITATIONS 
The analyses and recommendations submitted herein are based in part upon 
information obtained from the field observations and data review.  Variations of 
conditions may occur, and the nature and extent of these variations may not become 
evident until remedial construction is underway.  If variations then appear evident, 
Geolabs should be contacted to re-evaluate the recommendations presented in this 
report. 
This report has been prepared for the exclusive use of AECOM, their client, 
Department of Hawaiian Homelands and other project consultants for specific 
application to the Pavement Distresses at Nanakuli Homesteads Subdivisions in 
accordance with generally accepted geotechnical engineering principles and practices.  
No warranty is expressed or implied. 
This report has been prepared solely for the purpose of assisting the design 
engineers in the design of the proposed remedial work.  Therefore, this report may not 
contain sufficient data, or the proper information, to serve as the basis for preparation of 
construction cost estimates nor for bidding purposes.  A contractor wishing to bid on this 
project should retain a competent geotechnical engineer to assist in the interpretation of 
this report and/or in the performance of additional site specific exploration for bid 
estimating purposes. 
The owner/client should be aware that unanticipated soil conditions are 
commonly encountered.  Unforeseen soil conditions, such as perched groundwater, soft 
deposits, hard layers, or cavities, may occur in localized areas and may require 
additional probing or corrections in the field (which may result in construction delays) to 
attain a properly constructed project. Therefore, a sufficient contingency fund is 
recommended to accommodate these possible extra costs.

Description: 
High Severity Depression 
Location N007 shows a severe depression surrounding an utility manhole.  As 
shown in the photograph, the depression is about 17 feet by 13 feet in plan 
dimension.  The depression appears to have been patched multiple times. 
It is our opinion that this defect is the result of poor compaction around the manhole 
riser.  As such, it is believed that a shallow repair would not address the issue since 
the poorly compacted soils most likely extend to the bottom of the riser. 
Rather than excavating the full depth, we recommend excavating the area to a depth 
of about 3 feet below the bottom of base course level and filling the resulting 
excavation with controlled low strength material (CLSM) to the bottom of the base 
course. This would help bridge over the poorly compacted subsoils and reduce the 
potential for future subsidence.  After the placement of the CLSM and replacing the 
base course, a 5-foot wide strip of Tensar Glass Grid pavement reinforcing material, 
or similar, should be placed over the joint between the existing base course and the 
replacement base course to reduce the potential for reflection cracking. 
The replacement base course should be compacted to a minimum of 95 percent 
relative compaction.
