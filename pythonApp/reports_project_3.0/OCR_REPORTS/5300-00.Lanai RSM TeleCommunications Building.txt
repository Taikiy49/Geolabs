GEOTECHNICAL ENGINEERING EXPLORATION
LANAI RSM TELE COMMUNICATION BUILDING
ISLAND OF LANAI HAWAII
Dear Mr Kondo
In accordance with our fee proposal dated we have conducted a
geotechnical engineering exploration for the proposed Lanai RSM Tele Communication
Building within the Lanai Residence Lots subdivision on the Island of Lanai Hawaii This
report summarizes our work on the project and presents our findings and geotechnical
engineering recommendations derived from our analyses for the project
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located within the Department of Hawaiian Home Lands Lanai
Residence Lots subdivision adjacent to Lanai City on the Island of Lanai Hawaii The
general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map
Plate 1
At present the site is vacant undeveloped land formerly used for the cultivation of
pineapple The site is generally level and vegetated with weeds shrubs and bushes
typical of abandoned pineapple fields
Based on the information provided we understand that the project will involve the
construction of a one story pre engineered building with plan dimensions of
approximately 13 feet by 40 feet to be used by Sandwich Isles Communications to
provide tele communications service to the surrounding subdivision
It is anticipated that the structure will be supported by a slab on grade and will be
surrounded by concrete sidewalks Minor site grading is anticipated

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our field exploration program for the tele communications building project consisted
of drilling and sampling one 1 boring designated as Boring No 1 extending to a depth of
about 10 5 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate location of the drilled
boring is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The boring generally encountered residual soils consisting of stiff to very stiff
dark brown to brown silty clay to the maximum depth explored of 10 5 feet below the
existing ground surface Groundwater was not encountered in the boring drilled at the
time of our field exploration
Detailed descriptions of the materials encountered in the boring are presented on
the attached Log of Boring Plate A 1 of Appendix A Results of the laboratory tests
performed on selected soil samples are presented in Appendix B
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the subsurface soil conditions encountered at the project site we
believe that shallow spread and continuous footings bearing on properly compacted
structural fill may be used to support the proposed CEV building planned at the site
Detailed discussion of these items and our geotechnical engineering design
recommendations are presented in the following sections of this report
Foundations
Based on the anticipated subsurface soil conditions at the project site we believe
that shallow spread and continuous footing foundations including thickened edge slabs
may be used for support of the proposed building An allowable bearing pressure of
3 000 pounds per square foot psf may be used for the foundations bearing on properly
compacted structural fill This bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may be
increased by one third 1 3 for transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic
forces
Our exploration indicates that the silty clay soils underlying the site have a
moderate to high swelling potential In order to provide a uniform supporting layer of
non expansive material for the structure and the surrounding sidewalks we recommend
that a minimum of 30 inches of structural fill be provided below the building and extending
a minimum horizontal distance of 2 feet beyond the footprint of the building and sidewalks
Portions of the building area may require over excavation and replacement of the existing
surface soils in order to provide this minimum 30 inch layer

In general the bottom of footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches
below the lowest adjacent finished grades If foundations are located next to utility
trenches they should be embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V
imaginary plane extending upward from the bottom edge of the utility
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by frictional resistance
developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil and by passive earth
pressure acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system A coefficient of
friction of 0 4 may be used for footings bearing on the compacted structural fills
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid
pressure of 250 pounds per square foot per foot of depth pcf This assumes that the
soils around the footings are well compacted Unless covered by pavements or slabs the
passive resistance in the upper 12 inches of soil should be neglected
Active soil pressure on retaining walls and structures may be estimated at
35 pounds per square foot per foot of depth pcf
Site Grading
At the commencement of grading work areas within the grading limits should be
cleared grubbed and scarified to a minimum depth of 12 inches moisture conditioned to
above the optimum moisture content and recompacted to not less than 90 percent relative
compaction ASTM D 1557 91 Boulders encountered should be removed to a minimum
depth of 12 inches below the structural fill subgrade Vegetation should be removed and
any roots greater than 3 inches in diameter encountered should be over excavated and be
backfilled with fill placed in 12 inch maximum loose lifts moisture conditioned to at least
the optimum moisture content and compacted to at least 95 percent relative compaction
Soft and yielding areas encountered should be over excavated to expose firm
natural material and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with fill placed in 12 inch
maximum loose lifts moisture conditioned to at least the optimum moisture content and
compacted to at least 95 percent relative compaction The excavated soils should be
disposed of properly off site and or used in landscape areas where appropriate Boulders
or debris produced from the site grading operations should be segregated and disposed of
at an appropriate off site location
The compacted structural fill should consist of non expansive select granular
material such as crushed basalt or coral The material should be well graded from coarse
to fine with no particles larger than 3 inches in largest dimension and should contain
between 10 and 30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve The material should have
a laboratory CBR value of 20 or more and should have a maximum swell of less than
1 percent when tested in accordance with ASTM D 1883

Imported fill materials should be tested by Geolabs Inc for conformance with these
recommendations prior to delivery to the project site for its intended use
Compacted structural fill should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum
moisture content placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to
the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of
the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 test procedures Optimum
moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum
dry density
The non expansive select granular fill should be placed as soon as possible after
the subgrade soils are prepared The compacted structural fill and the underlying
subgrade soils should be wetted and kept moist prior to the final placement of concrete
Where shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of the subgrade we recommend
that the soils be thoroughly moistened to close the cracks Saturation and subsequent
yielding of the exposed subgrade due to inclement weather and poor drainage may
require over excavation of the soft areas and replacement with well compacted
engineered fill
Slab On Grade
For more uniform support of interior slabs we recommend that the concrete
slab on grade be supported on a minimum 4 inch layer of cushion fill The cushion fill
layer should consist of open graded gravels such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C33
No 67 gradation or basalt termite barrier BTB For slabs on grade with
moisture sensitive surfacing we recommend that an impervious moisture barrier be
placed over the cushion fill to reduce potential migration of moisture through the concrete
slab
The area adjacent to the slab should be backfilled tightly against the slab and
graded to divert water away from the slab to reduce the potential of water ponding around
the slab
The thickened edges of slabs adjacent to unpaved areas should be embedded at
least 12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade

In general for support of the utility lines we recommend that a granular bedding
consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation be used under
the pipes The initial backfill up to about 1 foot above the pipes should consist of
free draining backfills such as No 3B Fine gravel to reduce the potential for damaging
the pipes from compaction of the backfill It is critical that free draining granular material
be used to reduce the potential for formation of voids below the haunches of pipes and to
provide adequate support for the sides of the pipes The use of on site soils as backfill
immediately around utility pipes is not recommended
For protected utility lines such as concrete jacketed electrical and
telecommunications lines the bedding requirements described above are not necessary if
the concrete jackets are cast neat in the utility trench
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 1 foot above the pipes to the
finished subgrade should consist of approved on site soils or select granular material The
backfill material should be moisture conditioned to at least the optimum moisture content
placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a
minimum of 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for future ground
subsidence The upper 2 feet of the trench backfill below the pavement subgrade should
be compacted to not less than 95 percent relative compaction Mechanical compaction
equipment should be used to compact the materials at the project site Water tamping
jetting or ponding should not be used to compact the backfill material
Pavement
Paved areas such as an access road or driveway and parking areas may be
required for this project Based on the results of our exploration and laboratory testing
the following pavement section is recommended
2 0 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 0 Inch Aggregate Base Course 95 Relative Compaction
12 0 Inch inches Subbase Course 95 Relative Compaction
20 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness on Moist Compacted Subgrade
The subbase course should consist of non expansive select granular material
such as crushed basalt or coral The material should be well graded from coarse to fine
with no particles larger than 3 inches in largest dimension and should contain between
10 and 30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve The material should have a
laboratory CBR value of 20 or more and should have a maximum swell of less than
1 percent

Drainage
The finished grades adjacent to the structure should be sloped at 2 to 5 to shed
water away from the foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also advised
that gutter systems be installed around the structure and that the discharge be diverted
away from the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near the
foundations and slab should also be avoided Gravel strips adjacent to the slab and
foundation should be avoided Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have
concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the above
foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation
and subsequent loss of strength of the near surface soils The foundation excavations
should be properly backfilled against the footings immediately after setting of the concrete
to reduce water infiltration Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and
should be maintained to drain surface run off away from walls and foundations
Design Review
Final drawings and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to Geolabs for review and written comments prior to advertisement for bids
This review is necessary to evaluate conformance of the plans and specifications with the
intent of the earthwork and foundation recommendations provided herein If this review is

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that Geolabs be retained to provide geotechnical engineering
services during construction of the proposed project The items of construction monitoring
that are critical requiring Special Inspection include observation of footing subgrade
over excavation and preparation and placement and compaction of the structural fill
materials Other aspects of earthwork construction should also be observed by a
representative from Geolabs This is to observe compliance with the intent of the design
concepts specifications and or recommendations and to expedite suggestions for design
changes that may be required in the event that subsurface conditions differ from those
anticipated at the time this report was prepared The recommendations provided in this
report are contingent upon such observations
If actual exposed subsurface conditions encountered during construction differ
from those assumed or considered in this report Geolabs should be contacted to review
and or revise the geotechnical engineering recommendations presented herein

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from a single field boring Variations of subsoil conditions beyond the
field boring may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become
evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident Geolabs should
be contacted to re evaluate the recommendations presented in this report
The boring location in the field was determined by taping from reference points
shown on the Lanai Residence Lots Phase 2 Grading and Erosion Control Plan prepared
by Belt Collins Hawaii Ltd and dated November 2004 The physical location of the field
boring should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown on the graphic representation of the boring depict the
approximate boundaries between the soil types and as such may denote a gradual
transition Water level data from the boring were measured at the times shown on the
graphic representations and or in the text of this report These data have been reviewed
and interpretations made in the formulation of this report However it must be noted that
fluctuation may occur due to tidal fluctuation variation in rainfall temperatures and other
factors
This report has been prepared for the exclusive use of INK Architects and other
project subconsultants for specific application to the proposed tele communications
building project in accordance with generally accepted geotechnical engineering principles
and practices No warranty is expressed or implied
This report has been prepared solely for the purpose of assisting the design
engineers and architect in the preliminary design evaluation of the proposed project
Therefore this report may not contain sufficient data or the proper information to serve as
the basis for preparation of construction cost estimates nor for bidding purposes
A contractor wishing to bid on this project should retain a competent geotechnical engineer
to assist in the interpretation of this report and or in the performance of additional site
specific exploration for bid estimating purposes
The owner client should be aware that unanticipated soil conditions are commonly
encountered Unforeseen soil conditions such as perched groundwater soft deposits
hard layers or cavities may occur in localized areas and may require additional probing or
corrections in the field which may result in construction delays to attain a properly
constructed project Therefore a sufficient contingency fund is recommended to
accommodate these possible extra costs
