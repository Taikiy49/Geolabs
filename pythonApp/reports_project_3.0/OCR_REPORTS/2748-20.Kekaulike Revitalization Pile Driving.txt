Based on our geotechnical engineering exploration and the design concept most
of the competent near surface coral formation will be removed by the basement
excavation It appears that the underlying loose to medium dense coralline deposits
cannot provide adequate support for shallow foundations such as mat or spread
footings without inducing excessive settlements under the high structural loads of the
proposed structure Therefore deep pile foundations embedded in the basalt formation
or bouldery old alluvium was recommended for foundation support of the project A
deep foundation system consisting of 16 inch octagonal pre cast prestressed
concrete piles with a design vertical compressive load capacity of 100 tons per pile was
recommended for support of the structure
Test Pile Program
A test pile program which consisted of driving 24 piles at various locations
across the site was conducted from December 15 through 23 1994 The purpose of
the test pile program was to establish the driveability of the piles given the subsurface
conditions at the site driving equipment and methods of installation In addition the
program also provides information on the driving resistance and contours of the load
bearing stratum across the project site which was used to estimate the pile tip
elevations A pile load test was conducted on December 27 and
selected test pile to evaluate the pile driving criteria and to confirm the design bearing
capacity Results of the test pile program are presented in our Pile Load Test Report

Production Pile Driving Observations
All production pile locations were generally predrilled to a depth of about 20 feet
below the then existing ground surface prior to pile installation Obstructions such as
basalt boulders and coral ledges were encountered at several locations during
predrilling In most cases the auger was able to drill through these obstructions
A Delmag single acting diesel hammer Model D30 32 mounted on the fixed
leads of a Manitowac crane was used to drive the 16 inch octagonal pre cast
prestressed concrete piles The hammer has a rated energy between
35 400 and 69 900 foot pounds Based on our field observations the pile hammer was
generally delivering a driving energy of approximately 50 000 to 55 000 foot pounds
during the majority of the pile driving operations Due to the proximity of some of the
production piles to the existing structures lower pile hammer settings were used for
some of the piles in an attempt to reduce the potential adverse effects on the adjacent
structures due to vibrations generated during pile driving operations Results of our
vibration monitoring services performed during the production pile driving operation will
be summarized in a separate report and will be transmitted under separate cover
A total of 234 production piles were driven between May 9 and August 3 1995
for the subject project The pile driving operations were conducted under the near
continuous observation of a field representative of our firm During the pile driving
operation logs were maintained for each pile which included information pertaining to
pile number date driven predrilled depth hammer setting blow counts observations
size and length of the piles The locations of the production piles driven are shown on
the attached Pile Location Plans Plates 2 and 3 This included four additional piles
Pile 230A and 239A to replace the original piles which were driven
at the wrong locations During the course of pile driving operations four additional piles
Pile Nos 118 through 121 were driven for the structure and tower crane foundation
Original Pile Nos 118 through 123 163 through 167 were eliminated and replaced with
