SITE GRADING
The irrigation lake site is located approximately 4 000 feet south of Queen
Kaahumanu Highway on the mauka side of the Four Seasons Resort Hawaii at Hualalai
project in the District of North Kona on the island of Hawaii Access to the site is
provided via an unimproved gravel road which services an existing water tank and
pump station adjacent to the project site
The site was graded for the construction of a 2 0 M G irrigation lake with
associated irrigation lines connecting the head tank wells and the lake The original
ground surface elevations at the irrigation lake site prior to construction ranged from
about 455 to 485 feet Mean Sea Level MSL The design finish grades at the lake
bottom ranged from about 462 to 465 feet MSL with the top of lake embankment at

Removal of Unsuitable Materials
During the grading operation the surficial porous and loose clinker materials a a
flow encountered within the irrigation lake embankment area were removed to expose
the underlying dense basalt formation The exposed surface was proof rolled with a
vibratory smooth drum roller prior to placement of structural and choker fill materials
The reservoir embankment construction generally consisted of the placement of
12 inch minus materials compacted to at least 95 percent relative compaction During
the trench excavation of the effluent and drain line our field observations disclosed that
the upstream face of the reservoir embankment contained some over sized materials
exceeding 12 inches in maximum size with insufficient fines such that the over sized
materials were nested together In accordance with our recommendations and the
project specifications the upstream slope face materials were removed and replaced
with structural fill consisting of 6 inch minus materials by keying into the adjacent fills
with proper moisture conditioning and compaction
Placement of Choker Fill and Impervious Liner System
After preparation of the irrigation lake subgrades a 6 inch layer of choker fill
consisting of base course materials were piaced at the bottom and side slopes of the
irrigation lake to provide a relatively smooth and stable surface for the placement of the
impervious liner system
A 2 foot deep trench was excavated alongside the perimeter of the irrigation lake
for anchoring of the impervious liner system The trench was backfilled with base
course materials and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction with

Installation of Influent Effluent Lines
The installation of the influent effluent lines consisted of the placement of ductile
iron pipes at a depth of approximately 5 feet below the ground surface The excavated
trenches were generally backfilled with 1 inch minus materials compacted with a single
drum vibratory roller with moisture conditioning
Fill Placement
During the grading operations representative samples of the fill materials were
obtained and returned to our laboratory for testing The maximum dry density and
optimum moisture content were determined in our laboratory in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Results of our laboratory tests are presented on
Plate 2
Observation and testing of the earthwork in the field were performed by
representatives from our office to observe that the grading was performed in general
conformance with the intent of the Geotechnical Engineering Report and specifications
pertaining to the geotechnical engineering aspects of the project In place density tests
of the base course materials were performed periodically in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 2922 Nuclear Method The test results are presented on
the attached Summary of Field Density Test Results Plates 3 1 and 3 2

This office assumes no responsibility for any alterations made to slopes or
finished grades on the subject project subsequent to our observation and testing period
without our knowledge and written acknowledgement

in the accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor
the earthwork construction and materials Our services were performed in accordance
