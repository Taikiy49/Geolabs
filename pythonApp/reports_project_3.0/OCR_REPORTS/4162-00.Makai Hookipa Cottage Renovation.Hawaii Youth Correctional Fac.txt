INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Makai Hookipa Cottage Renovation project located within the
existing Hawaii Youth Correctional Facility in the Kailua area on the island of Oahu
Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering
recommendations resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering
analyses These recommendations are intended for the design of foundations and site
preparation only The findings and recommendations presented herein are subject to the
limitations noted at the end of this report

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lava from two
shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be
middle to late Pliocene in age and Koolau is estimated to be late Pliocene to early
Pleistocene in age After a long period of volcanic inactivity during which time erosion
incised deep valleys into the Koolau shield volcanic activity returned with a series of lava
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 4162 00 Page 4
flows followed by cinder and tuff cone formations These series are referred to as the
Honolulu Volcanic Series
During the Pleistocene Epoch Ice Age sea levels fluctuated in response to the
cycles of continental glaciation As the glaciers grew and advanced less water was
available to fill the oceanic basins such that sea levels fell below the present stands of the
sea When the glaciers melted and receded an excess of water became available such
that the sea levels rose to above the present sea level
The processes of erosion and deposition were affected by these glacioeustatic sea
level fluctuations When the sea level was low the erosional base level was
correspondingly lower and valleys were carved to depths below the present sea level
When the sea level was high the erosional base level was raised such that sediments
accumulated at higher elevations
In the mountainous regions of Hawaii the erosional processes are dominated by
detachment of soil and rock masses from the valley walls and are transported downslope
toward the axis of a valley primarily by gravity as colluvium Once these materials reach
the stream in the central portion of a valley alluvial processes become dominant and the
sediments are transported and deposited as alluvium
In general stream flows in Hawaii are intermittent and flashy such that the stream
flows transmit large volumes of water for very short durations Because of this transport
of sediments is intermittent and the bulk of the stream s hydraulic load consists of a
poorly sorted mixture of boulders cobbles gravel sands and fines When the erosional
base levels change these sediment loads are left as deposits
When deposits are left in place for long periods of time chemical processes begin
to alter the materials simultaneously causing a breakdown or weathering of the material
Chemical processes also cause induration or cementation of the coarse grained portion
of the sediment resulting in a poorly consolidated sedimentary rock or conglomerate
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 4162 00 Page 5
Simultaneously erosion continues in the areas above the valley floors and upstream in
headwaters This continued erosion generates material which is transported downslope
covering the older alluvial deposits
Depending on the local base level and rate of transport these newer sediments
are generally transient in terms of geologic time In addition their consistency and
density are generally less than those of the older partially consolidated deposits
SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed project site is located at the existing Makai Hookipa Cottage of the
Hawaii Youth Correctional Facility near the intersection of Ulupii Street and
Kalanianaole Highway in the Kailua area on the island of Oahu Hawaii The project
site can be accessed through a short driveway situated approximately 100 feet to the
southeast of the entrance to the Women s Community Correctional Center which is
currently being used for the Hawaii Youth Correctional Facility
The existing Makai Hookipa Cottage is a single story odd shaped structure with
floor areas for day room dining kitchen laundry living quarters and bathrooms
Based on our field observations it appears that the existing structure is supported on
post footings as well as on slabs on grade The area outside the building structure
generally consists of grass lawn with some picnic tables enclosed by chain link fences
An asphalt paved parking lot is located at the end of the short driveway outside the
fenced area
Based on our field observations and the site plan provided the project site is
relatively level with existing ground elevations ranging between about 197 and
200 feet Mean Sea Level MSL

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field exploration the project site is generally underlain by very stiff to
hard clayey soils extending to the maximum depth explored of about 21 5 feet below the
existing ground surface Based on the subsoil conditions encountered at the project site
we believe that shallow foundations consisting of spread and or continuous footings
bearing on the recompacted in situ stiff soils may be used to support the proposed new
addition
Based on our laboratory tests the near surface clayey soils exhibit moderate
shrink swell potential when subjected to fluctuations in the soil moisture contents To
reduce the potential for distress to the future lightly loaded slabs on grade resulting from
the swelling of the near surface soils we recommend that the on site clayey soils be
capped with a minimum of 18 inches of non expansive select granular fill capping fill
material below the slab cushion In general the subgrade soils should be
moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture content and
recompacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction The slab subgrade should
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 4162 00 Page 7
be kept moist and free of shrinkage cracks prior to placement of capping fill cushion fill
and or slab concrete Our geotechnical engineering recommendations pertaining to the
design of foundations and site preparation are further discussed in the following sections
of this report
Foundations
Based on the generally stiff subsoil conditions encountered at the site we believe
that shallow spread and or continuous footings may be used for support of the proposed
new addition An allowable bearing pressure of up to 3 000 pounds per square foot psf
may be used for the design of shallow foundations bearing on the recompacted stiff
clayey soils or engineered fills needed to achieve the final design grades This bearing
value is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third 1 3 for transient
loads such as those caused by wind or seismic forces
For footings bearing on the stiff in situ soils or new engineered fills the bottom of
the footing excavations should be recompacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction
to provide a relatively firm and smooth bearing surface prior to placement of reinforcing
steel and concrete If soft and or loose materials are encountered at the bottom of the
footing excavations they should be over excavated to expose the underlying firm
materials The over excavation may be backfilled with on site soils compacted to a
minimum of 90 percent relative compaction or the footing bottom may be extended down
to the underlying competent material
Lateral loads acting on the structures may be resisted by frictional resistance
developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil and by passive
earth pressure acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system A
coefficient of friction of 0 3 may be used for footings bearing on the in situ stiff soils or
engineered fills Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be estimated using an
equivalent fluid pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth pcf This
assumes that the soil around footings is well compacted The passive pressure should
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 4162 00 Page 8
be reduced for foundations located on slopes Unless covered by pavements or slabs
the passive resistance in the upper 12 inches of soil should be neglected
In general footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest
adjacent finish grades If foundations are located next to utility trenches or easements
they should be embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane
extending upward from the bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of
the utility lines This requirement is necessary to avoid surcharging adjacent below grade
structures with additional structural loads and to reduce the potential for foundation
settlement
We estimate that footing settlements under the anticipated design loads for
footings bearing on the recompacted in situ stiff soils or engineered fills as recommended
herein to be less than 1 inch total with differential settlements on the order of 0 5 inches
We recommend that footing excavations be observed by a representative of
Geolabs Hawaii prior to placement of reinforcing steel and concrete to confirm the
foundation bearing conditions and the required embedment depths
Slabs On Grade
Based on our field exploration the project site is generally underlain by
near surface clayey soils with moderate expansion potential when subjected to moisture
fluctuations To reduce the potential for future distress to the lightly loaded
slabs on grade resulting from shrinking and swelling of the clayey soils due to changes in
the moisture content we recommend that the subgrade soils be properly prepared and
capped with at least 18 inches of non expansive select granuiar fili capping fill material
below the slab cushion The capping fill should also extend beyond the new
slab on grade footprint a minimum of 24 inches
The non expansive select granular capping fill should be placed as soon as
possible after the subgrade soils are prepared in accordance with the recommendations
W O 4162 00 Page 9
presented in the Site Preparation section of this report The prepared capping fill and
the underlying subgrade soils should be wetted and kept moist prior to final placement of
concrete Where shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of the subgrade we
recommend that the soils be thoroughly moistened to close the cracks Saturation and
subsequent yielding of the exposed subgrade due to inclement weather and poor
drainage may require over excavation of the soft areas and replacement with
well compacted engineered fill
In general we recommend that a minimum 4 inch thick cushion layer consisting of
No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation or basaltic termite barrier BTB be
placed below the slabs to provide more uniform support of the slabs and to serve as a
capillary moisture break To reduce the potential for future moisture infiltration and
subsequent damage to floor coverings an impervious moisture vapor barrier such as a
plastic membrane is recommended on top of the gravel or BTB cushion layer Two
inches of moist fine sand or BTB may be considered above the plastic membrane to
provide protection of the membrane and to aid in curing of the slab concrete Flexible
floor coverings such as carpet or sheet vinyl should be considered since they can better
mask minor slab cracking
The thickened edges of slab adjacent to unpaved area should be embedded at
least 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade Areas adjacent to the slab
should be backfilled tightly against the slab with low expansion impervious soil and
graded to divert water away from the slab to reduce the potential for water ponding
around the foundations
Site Preparation
Grading plans were not available at the time this report was prepared However
we anticipate minor grading with cuts and fills on the order of less than 2 feet may be
expected for the proposed project Site grading operations should be observed by a
representative of Geolabs Hawaii It is important that a representative from our office
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 4162 00 Page 10
observe whether the exposed soil conditions during excavation are similar to those
encountered in our exploration The following recommendations are intended to
provide guidelines for the design of site grading work
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared Debris rubbish and other unsuitable materials should be removed
and disposed of off site Slabs and foundations of the existing structures to be
demolished should be completely removed Soft weak or yielding areas disclosed during
clearing operations or over excavations resulting from the demolition should be backfilled
with well compacted engineered fill Existing utiliies to be abandoned should be
removed and the resulting excavation should be properly backfilled with well compacted
engineered fill Utilities to be abandoned in place under the proposed structures should
be backfilled by pumping lean concrete under low pressure
After clearing and demolition the areas within the future slab on grade limits
should be over excavated where necessary to provide the minimum 18 inch thick
capping layer of non expansive select granular fill material below the slab subgrade level
i e bottom of the cushion fill layer No 3B Fine gravel or BTB The non expansive
select granular fill material should extend a minimum of 2 feet beyond the perimeter of the
proposed slab on grade areas The over excavated subgrades should then be scarified
to a minimum depth of 10 inches properly moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent
above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a
percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil determined in accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91 Optimum moisture is the water content percentage
by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
Based on our field exploration we believe that the excavated on site soils may
be re used as a source of general fill material up to the bottom of the non expansive
capping fill layer provided that they are moisture conditioned and compacted as
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 4162 00 Page 11
recommended herein In general the on site clayey material to be used as general fill
should be moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture
placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a
minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
The non expansive capping fill material recommended below slabs on grades
should consist of well graded granular materials less than 3 inches in maximum
dimension The material should have a laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR value of
20 or more and should have a maximum swell of less than 1 percent when tested in
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1883 In addition it should contain between
10 and 30 percent passing the No 200 sieve Imported fill materials should be tested by
Geolabs Hawaii for conformance with our recommendations prior to delivery to the project
site for its intended use Imported materiais should be moisture conditioned to above the
optimum moisture content and compacted to not less than 90 percent relative
compaction
Drainage
The finished grades outside the building addition should be sloped to shed water
away from foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also advised that
gutter systems be installed around the building and that the discharge be diverted away
from the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near the
foundations and slabs should also be avoided Planters next to foundations should be
avoided or have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce the potential for water infiltration
into the subsoils Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and
maintained to drain surface run off away from the slabs and foundations
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil
saturation and subsequent loss of strength and or heaving of the near surface silty and
clayey soils The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled against the
GEOLABS HAWAI

footings immediately after setting of the concrete to reduce the potential for water
infiltration
