ITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located in the Moiliili area of Honolulu
across Coyne Street from Varsity Theater s parking lot The
site s area covers approximately 1 acre and is bordered by
Coyne Kaialiu and South Beretania Streets on the northeast
northwest and southwest sides respectively The southeast
side is bordered by adjacent buildings and a service station
The existing building structures on the project site during
the time of the soil investigation consisted of the Tire
Recap Service s building and garage and two separate single
story concrete masonary buildings housing residences and
small shops An existing parking lot is located on the
northeastern portion of the site adjacent to Coyne Street
The relief of the project site is essentially level with no
outstanding natural surface features
There was no significant amount of vegetation present through
out the site except for a relatively small mango tree
SOIL CONDITIONS
The subsurface exploration disclosed that the site is mantled
by a 5 foot thick clayey gravel fill material Underlying
the upper layer soft to very soft alluvial deposits of
organic clays light tan clay and dark anes clayey Sit
with sand layers were encountered to depths of 28 to 50
feet

Geology
The project site is geologically situated in an area that
borders between a Quaternary limestone and recent alluvium
as indicated on the Honolulu Quadrangle Geological Survey
Map
Within the surrounding Moiliili area the subsurface forma
tions are typically variable and discontinuous as experienced
from previous projects Some areas are underlain with ancient
coral or coral related formations relatively near the surface
while other areas possess thick deposits of organic clays and
Silts begining near the surface These variable formations
are reflective of the different conditions that were present
throughout the time of the deposition conditions such as
changing sea levels and stream flow
Locally the project site appears to have been an area of
accumulation of organic clays silts and calcareous clays
in a swampy environment Historically the site is said to
have been a lily pond

The following pile foundation recommendations are based upon
presently available information and results of our previous
meeting and discussions
The soils engineer should be notified immediately for any
conditions encountered that are not covered in this report
and are contrary to the contents of this report to enable
him to review and if necessary revise the recommendations
in light of changed conditions

Pile Foundation Design
To minimize building settlement a pile foundation system
eonposed of 12 inch square concrete pile driven to the
underlying decomposed rock and rock layer should be capable
of supporting an allowable design load of 30 tons each
Our preliminary computation indicated that a negative skin
friction up to 30 tons could be developed around the pile
Shaft due to the settlement of the soft underlying materials
and therefore would produce additional down drag on the
driven pile
The pile should be driven down to obtain end bearing support
on the underlying basalt rock formation Based on our deep
boring B 5 drilled at this site basalt rock was encountered
at a depth of about 59 feet below the existing ground elevation
Based on the penetration data performed at the other borings
it is estimated that the rock or pile bearing layer could
vary considerably within the building site We recommend
that ten 60 foot long test piles be ordered and driven to

Based on the subsurface information the pile
length should vary between 30 and 60 feet
Splicing of piles should be avoided if practicable
The piles should be driven with a hammer delivering
a minimum energy of about 20 000 foot pounds
The piles should be driven through alluvial clays
and silty sand deposits and into the underlying
basalt rock formation or to about 60 blows per
foot for the last foot but not over driven to
more than 10 blows per inch or the last fraction
of an inch The final pile driving criteria may
be modified after the test piles are first driven
at the building site
The piles may be considered to be fixed at 5 foot
depths from the present grade
The piles should be spaced no closer than 3 times
the pile widths
