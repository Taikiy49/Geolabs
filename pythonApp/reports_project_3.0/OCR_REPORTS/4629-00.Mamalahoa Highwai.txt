Dear Mr Haymore
This letter report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations performed
during the foundation probing and grouting operations for the Emergency Replacement of
Kaalaala Siream Bridge project between September 4 and
The project site is located along Mamalahoa Highway Route 11 about 1 2 miles
north of the Town of Pahala in the District of Kau on the Island of Hawaii as shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1 The project consists of consiructing a replacement bridge
of approximately 43 feet in width and 105 feet in length The replacement bridge
abutments and two intermediate piers are supported on a shallow foundation system The
intent of the foundation probing and grouting operation is an attempt to locate and grout
potential subsurface cavities and or voids beneath the bridge abutment and pier
foundations
The bridge abutment and pier foundation locations and the probe hole locations
were surveyed and marked in the field by the general contractor Hawaiian Dredging
Construction Company prior to the probing operations The probe holes were spaced at
about 10 foot on center along the abutment and pier foundations A total of 20 scheduled
probe holes were located at the bridge abutment and pier foundation locations The

approximate locations and identification of the 20 scheduled probe holes for the bridge

The probing and grouting work for the replacement bridge was performed prior to
excavation for the bridge abutment and pier foundations The probing work was
performed by Isemoto Contracting Company The probe holes were drilled with a track
mounted drill rig equipped with an air percussion hammer The probe holes drilled were
about 3 inches in diameter and extended to about 17 to 30 feet below the existing ground
surface The probe holes drilled were extended to a depth of at least 10 feet below the
bottom of footing elevations A summary of our foundation probe drilling and grouting
observations is provided on Plates 3 1 and 3 2
The materials encountered during the probe drilling operations generally consisted
of fill materials underlain by gray and reddish brown medium hard to hard basalt
formations interbedded with layers of reddish brown clinker materials After completion of
the probe drilling operations the contractor filled the probe holes with a fine aggregate
masonry grout specified in the project specifications The grout used to fill the probe holes
was supplied by Jas W Glover Ltd The field probing and grouting records are provided
in Appendix A of this letter report
Additional probe holes were drilled adjacent to the scheduled probe holes that
generally had grout intakes of 0 5 cubic yards or greater Of the 20 scheduled probe
holes six probe holes three probe holes at Abutment No 1 one probe hole at Pier No 2
and two probe holes at Abutment No 2 had grout intakes greater than 0 5 cubic yards
Therefore twenty three additional probe holes were drilled for Abutment No 1 Pier No 2
and Abutment No 2 The approximate locations of the additional probe holes are shown
on the Probe Hole Identification Plan Plate 2 Approximately 14 4 cubic yards of fine
aggregate masonry grout was used to fill both the scheduled and additional probe holes
drilled for the bridge foundations
In summary the foundation probing and grouting operations for the project have
been conducted in general conformance with the intent of the project specifications and
our geotechnical engineering exploration report Based on our observations it is our
professional opinion that the relatively small voids and or cavities encountered at the
bridge abutment and pier foundation locations have been grouted and that the subgrade
area should provide the recommended foundation support for the bridge abutment and
pier foundation
