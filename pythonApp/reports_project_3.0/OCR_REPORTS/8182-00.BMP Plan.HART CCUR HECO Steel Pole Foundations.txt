BEST MANAGEMENT PRACTICES 
Best Management Practices (BMP) Plan serves to evaluate and address potential sources of 
sediment and other pollutants associated with the performance of the field geotechnical 
exploratory borings within the project site. It also provides controls that minimize and/or 
prevent pollutant discharges to surface and groundwaters. 
Any activities associated with the geotechnical exploratory drilling that requires BMP Plans are 
discussed below. These activities include borehole abandonment, dust control, inlet protection, 
material storage, fuel/oil spills, foreign object debris (FOD), litter, and solid waste. 
Exploratory Drilling 
A photograph of our typical Geotechnical Boring Set Up is shown on Plate 1. The exploratory a 
borings will consist of drilling a 4-inch diameter borehole through the existing Portland cement 
concrete or asphaltic concrete pavements. The borehole would then be advanced with a 4-inch 
diameter auger and/or rotary coring tools to depths of about 50 to 120 feet below the 
pavement surface for the new HECO steel pole foundation design. Potable water may be 
pumped into the borehole to serve as a drilling fluid and aid in drilling. Drill spoils generated 
generally will consist of soil and rock cuttings and any excess drilling fluid.  
Excess drill water that surfaces will be contained with a steel mud pan, and the water will be 
recirculated back into the borehole. Any remaining water at the end of the workday will be 
returned back into the borehole and/or placed in drums and transported to a storage area for 
future disposal at an acceptable disposal facility. Water generated by drilling activities will not 
be allowed into storm drains. All drilling fluid will be contained in the immediate drilling area 
and will not be allowed to sheet flow off the project site. 
A work isolation area will be set up around our drill rig and crew truck to contain any fluids in 
the event of a spill using geotextile Snake Bags and/or BioSocks. In the event of a spill or BMP 
failure, all drilling work will immediately stop to mitigate the situation. Details of the Snake Bag 
and BioSock are shown on Plates 2.1 and 2.2, and Plate 3, respectively. The site of excavations 
will be restored to its original condition as practical.  
Borehole Abandonment 
In general, the borings will be backfilled with cement grout up to the bottom of the existing 
pavement. Non-shrink grout will be used to patch the balance of the boring to the pavement 
surface. A black dye will be used to match the color of the concrete to the existing asphalt 
concrete pavement, where applicable.

Soil Cuttings Management 
We anticipate less than 0.4 cubic yards of soil drill cutting (waste material) to be generated during 
the drilling of each borehole. Generated drill cuttings will be placed in drums (or other acceptable 
receptacles) and transported to a storage area for future disposal at an acceptable disposal facility. 
Drill cuttings will be placed into drums or other acceptable receptacles at the end of each shift. In 
the event of rain, Snake Bags or BioSocks will be placed around the soil cuttings to mitigate 
potential runoff. Snake Bags or BioSocks will be monitored and maintained in good working order. 
Other soil material will be contained in buckets or in a small temporary plastic-lined basin. 
Petroleum-Contaminated Soil Management  
In the event soil or groundwater contamination is suspected to be encountered during the drilling 
operations based on sheen or olfactory observations, the field engineer or geologist will contact 
the Project Manager for further guidance and direction. 
Dust Control 
If there is a dust nuisance, a water hose will be available and used to provide wet suppression of 
exposed soil. Water used for dust control will be used only as necessary and applied to wet 
surfaces to minimize the potential for sediment runoff. 
Inlet Protection 
Geotechnical exploratory borings located adjacent to or near a drain inlet will require that those 
drain inlets be protected with geotextile Snake Bags and/or BioSocks during drilling operations. 
The geotextile Snake Bags and/or BioSocks will be monitored and maintained in good working 
order.  
Fuel/Oil Spills 
All on-site vehicles will be monitored for leaks and receive regular preventive maintenance in 
accordance with manufacturers’ recommendations to reduce potential leakage. In the event of 
leakage, absorbent pads will be available on-site to control and clean up the spill. As little water as 
possible will be used to clean up spills. All spilled and cleanup material will be properly disposed of 
off-site. 
Litter 
The work site will be kept clean and orderly. Covered bins will be provided on-site for proper 
collection of generated litter. The trash will be disposed of properly offsite at the end of each work 
day.

Solid Waste 
Solid waste (soil cuttings/drill spoils) should consist of less than 0.4 cubic yards of soil material, 
produced during drilling of the borehole, all of which will be placed in drums (or receptacles) and 
transported to a storage area for future disposal at an acceptable disposal facility. 
Contingency Plan 
Work will stop, and the BMP Plan will be fixed to mitigate the problem.
