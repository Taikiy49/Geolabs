This report presents the results of our visual geologic
and soil reconnaissance at the subject site located on
the lower southwestern slope of Hawaii Loa Ridge in
Aina Haina off Hoana Place adjacent to an existing
residential development at the base of the slope
PROJECT CONSIDERATION
It is presently planned to subdivide the 54 acre site
into 20 family housing development on the subject parcel
The preliminary development scheme is to locate the lots
around the perimeter of the site with an access roadway
extending from Hoana Place through the center of the site

Preliminary plan dated prepared by your office

in lieu of the existing drainage course
The project site is located on the lower southwestern slope
of Hawaii Loa Ridge off Hoana Place and east of Opihi above
the existing dwellings in The site is
presently heavily overgrown with trees shrubs and grasses
Located in the upper portion of the site there are abundant

are not easily visible as they are all covered by heavy

DISCUSSTON AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the results of our visual geologic reconnaissance
the site appears to be underlain by 4 to 10 feet of colluvium
and talus materials with abundant boulders over weathered to
Slightly weathered basaltic rock formation The site appears
to be feasible for the proposed residential development pro
vided special design and detailed soil engineering recommen
dations are implemented both during the design and construction
phases Although the soil engineering problems can be resolved
at the site the economics of implementing the special design
required should be investigated

Site Preparation and Grading
Prior to any grading operations the site should be cleared
and stripped of all vegetations and boulders Any cut
materials generated by the proposed access road construction
should be stockpiled and reused for filling the existing
dirt road below the proposed access road Some boulders
may be disposed within the existing dirt road and or lined
drainage ditch provided proper windrow methods with voids
between boulders are properly filled Any excess of boulders
not used should be properly disposed of
It appears that the lots below the proposed access road
should be filled with suitable soil materials by raising
some of the lot grades to create a buttressing effect for
the possible thick talus materials within the former drain
ageway area The lots above the proposed access road should
have minimal grading and if grading is necessary flat slopes
of about 3 1 H V may have to be used

above slope ratios should be verified by detailed soil
investigation during the design phase of the project
Boulder Barriers
Based on our site observation and as discussed under
Geologic and Soil Conditions section the boulders will
tend to fall from the existing steep rock Slopes at or
near the upper boundary of the site The falling boulders
are a general phenomena occurring in all ridge slopes in
Oahu The state of art of soil engineering todate has
not advanced to a stage of conclusive solutions for falling
boulders It can be only stated that with time boulders
will fall from ridges and cliffs in any slope
Various methods of boulder barriers or catchments can be
used The degree of safety of preventing falling boulder
hazards is a function of economics For this site various
methods of boulder prevention can be utilized such as
1 Erecting boulder catchment utilizing a
chain link fence at the proposed drainage
easement area
2 proper set back distance of all living
structures be maintained or
3 perform boulder sweep operation above
the existing rock slope or cliffs
