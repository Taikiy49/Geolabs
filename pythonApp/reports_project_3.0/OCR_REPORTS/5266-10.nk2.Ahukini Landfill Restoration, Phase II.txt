SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our field exploration consisted of drilling and sampling six borings designated as
below the existing ground surface
In general our borings encountered a surface fill layer landfill capping layer
ranging in thickness from about 2 to 4 feet consisting of medium stiff to very stiff clayey
silt Beneath the fill landfill material consisting of medium stiff to hard clayey sandy silt
and very loose to loose silty sand with some landfill debris was encountered to depths of
about 2 5 to 20 5 feet below the existing ground surface The thickness of the landfill
wedge generally increased from west to east about 2 5 to 4 feet thick in our inland borings
and 15 to 20 5 feet thick in our makai borings Beneath the landfill material residual soil
was encountered to depths of about 16 5 to 23 feet followed by basalt formation extending
to the maximum depth explored of about 50 5 feet below the existing ground surface The
residual soil generally consisted of stiff to hard clayey silt and the basalt formation was
generally severely to moderately fractured highly to moderately weathered and medium
hard to hard In two of our borings a layer of colluvium consisting of cobbles and boulders
was encountered above the basalt formation at depths of about 13 to 31 feet below the
existing ground surface
Groundwater was not encountered in our drilled borings at the time of our field
exploration However it should be noted that water levels may vary with tidal fluctuations
seasonal rainfall time of year and other environmental factors

SLOPE STABILITY ANALYSIS
Based on the 30 Submittal Ahukini Landfill Restoration Phase Il General Grading
Plan provided we understand that it is desired to place up to about 15 feet of fill on the
inland side of the Phase II landfill area The existing slope leading down towards the ocean
will be graded to a three horizontal to one vertical 3H 1V slope inclination down to an
elevation of about 48 feet Mean Sea Level MSL where the 10 foot wide Upper Bench will
be constructed From the Upper Bench down to an elevation of 23 feet MSL the slope face
will be cut down to the underlying basalt formation with a typical finished slope face
inclination of up to a maximum inclination of 1H 1V The remainder of the slope
will be protected with a boulder armoring system to mitigate future erosion
Two two dimensional cross sections of the grading improvements generally running
from west to east were used for our slope stability analysis The purpose of our analysis
was to evaluate the stability of the proposed grading improvements and new soil slopes
Our analyses were performed using the latest version of the software program Slide2 by
Rocscience Slide2 is a two dimensional finite element analysis program that can model
complex slope stability and seepage conditions The Bishop s Simplified Method Spencer s
Method and GLE Morgenstern Price Method were used to evaluate the stability grading
improvements and new soil slopes
Based on our analyses the factors of safety for overall global stability under static
conditions were greater than 1 5 for the grading improvements and new soil slopes In
addition the factors of safety for overall global stability under seismic conditions were
greater than the minimum recommended value of 1 1
SITE GRADING
Based on the information provided we understand that site grading work for the
project will consist of fill up to about 15 feet thick and cuts about 22 feet deep In addition
we understand that cut slopes up to about 40 feet in height are planned
Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed Vegetation debris deleterious materials and other
unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site or in a
designated area to reduce the potential for contamination of the excavated materials
Soft and yielding areas encountered during clearing and grubbing below areas
designated to receive fill and or future improvements should be over excavated to expose
firm material and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with well compacted fill
The excavated soft soils should not be reused as fill materials and should be properly
disposed of off site or in landscaped areas if appropriate

Fills and Backfills
In general the on site soils may be reused as a source of general fill material
provided they are free of vegetation deleterious materials and rock fragments greater
than 3 inches in maximum dimension The excavated landfill material should not be
reused as a source of general fill material Imported fill materials should consist non
expansive select granular fill material from crushed basalt or coral
Non expansive select granular fill material should be well graded from coarse to
fine with particles no larger than 3 inches in largest dimension and should contain
between 10 and 30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve The material should have
a laboratory CBR value of 20 or more and should have a maximum swell of less than
1 percent when tested in accordance with ASTM D1883
Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
General fill materials should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose
thickness moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture
content and compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction Non expansive select
granular fill materials should be placed in level lifts of about 8 inches in loose thickness
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture and compacted to at least
90 percent relative compaction Imported clayey material used for the landfill capping
layer should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness moisture
conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture content and compacted to
at least 85 percent relative compaction
Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a
percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with
ASTM D1557 Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by weight
corresponding to the maximum dry density
Compaction should be accomplished by sheepsfoot rollers vibratory rollers or
other types of acceptable compaction equipment Water tamping jetting or ponding
should not be allowed to compact the fills Where compaction is less than required

Cut and Fill Slopes
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered in the borings it is anticipated
that cut slopes would expose soils consisting of the surface fill landfill material colluvium
and basalt rock formation Cut slopes into the fill and landfill material may be cut at an
inclination of 3H 1V or flatter Cut slopes into the colluvium cobbles and boulders may
be cut at an inclination of 2H 1V or flatter and cut slopes into the basalt rock formation
may be cut at a typical inclination of up to a maximum inclination of 1H 1V
For the planned soil cut slopes at the project site slope benches are recommended
at maximum 20 foot vertical height intervals Fills placed on slopes steeper than 5H 1V
such as the clayey soil capping layer over the 3H 1V cut slope should be keyed and
benched into the existing slope to provide stability of the new fill against sliding The filling
operations should start at the lowest point and continue up in level horizontal compacted
layers in accordance with the above fill placement recommendations
Fill slopes in excess of 5 feet in height should be constructed by overfilling and
cutting back to the design slope ratio to obtain a well compacted slope face Track rolling
of slopes in excess of 5 feet in height should not be accepted In the event that over
cutting of a slope occurs keying and benching requirements should be implemented
instead of backfilling the slope to the design grade with sliver fills Water should be
diverted away from the tops of slopes and slope planting should be provided as soon as
possible to reduce the potential for erosion of the finished slopes
After grading erosion control matting such as North American Green RollMax Vmax
C350 or equivalent should be installed on the exposed soil slope areas The erosion control
matting should be pinned to the slope face per the manufacturers recommendations
Hydromulch should then be applied to the erosion control matting to promote the growth of
vegetation as quickly as possible
