Dear Mr. Maulit: 
The pavement structural section for the Kokomo Road Reconstruction, Phase 2 
project was based on the recommendations presented in the above-referenced report 
prepared by our office. The recommendations for the pavement structural section 
consisted of 3.0 inches of Asphaltic Concrete (AC) over 4.5 inches of Asphalt Concrete 
Base (ACB) on 6.0 inches of Aggregate Base (AB) over a layer of reinforcing geogrid 
(Tensar TriAx Grid, TX7) on a layer of permeable separator (Mirafi 140N) over compacted 
subgrade. Results of the pavement design analyses are contained in the 
above-referenced Pavement Justification Report dated May 29, 2014 for the project 
prepared by Geolabs, Inc. 
Because of a water main traversing part of the travel lanes at shallow depths of 
about 14 to 20 inches below the existing grades, it is not practical to install the 13.5-inch 
thick pavement structural section without encountering and/or potentially damaging the 
shallow utility line during construction.  Given the above considerations for shallow utility 
lines, a thinner pavement structural section will be required to reduce the potential for 
encountering shallow utility lines that may require relocation and/or repair during 
construction.

Based on our discussions with the County of Maui and the Contractor, we believe 
that a pavement structural section consisting of about 7.5 inches of asphaltic concrete 
pavement (3.0 inches of Mix V AC sandwiched by a layer of Glasgrid placed over 
4.5 inches of ACB) would be a reasonable compromise given the shallow utility lines. 
The width of the reduced pavement structural section is on the order of about 6 to 8 feet 
from the edge of pavements.  This reduced pavement structural section (from 
13.5 inches down to 7.5 inches of total pavement thickness) is a significant reduction in 
the pavement structural section and the corresponding pavement design life. 
Based on the reduced pavement structural section of 7.5 inches (total pavement 
thickness), the pavement design life was reduced from 20 years to about 10 years when 
calculated based on the State of Hawaii Department of Transportation method of 
asphaltic concrete pavement design.  The State of Hawaii Department of Transportation 
method of asphaltic concrete pavement design is the same method used in the 
Pavement Justification Report for the project. 
Due to the irregular surface elevations of the current ACB roadway surface, we 
recommend that a leveling course of 1.0 to 1.5 inches of Mix V AC be placed over the 
4.5-inch of ACB first, followed by a layer of Glasgrid, and then completed with a final 
layer of 1.5 to 2.0 inches of Mix V AC at the top. An installation guide of the Glasgrid 
pavement reinforcement system is provided with this letter for information purpose only. 
The Contractor should follow the installation procedures and requirements from the 
manufacturer of the specific glasgrid pavement reinforcement system used for the 
project.  
Due to the reduced pavement structural section, we recommend that a more 
diligent inspection program should be implemented to review the conditions of the 
performance of the reduced pavement structural section over time to maintain the 
integrity of the pavement.  If needed, a more vigorous maintenance program would be 
implemented based on the results of the inspection program.

This document is intended to provide guidance on the method 
for installing the GlasGrid® Pavement Reinforcement System

ROAD PREPARATION 
Complete all crack sealing, pothole filling, base repairs, leveling 
course installations, and any other necessary repairs. 
ROAD SURFACE CONDITION 
Surface must be: 
˴ Dry 
˴ Clean and dust-free 
˴ Between 5°C (40°F) and 60°C (140°F) 
If a tack coat is specified, it may be applied before or after the 
installation of GlasGrid, depending on site conditions. Tack must 
be completely cured prior to paving.
PRODUCT STORAGE 
In order to preserve adhesive properties GlasGrid products must 
be stored inside a dust-free environment and kept dry at the  
job site. 
TWO EASY-TO-PLACE METHODS 
Fabric Tractor Installation
The first, and more common approach, involves mechanical 
placement, typically with a fabric tractor that has been modified 
so that the GlasGrid material can be front-mounted (Figure 1). 
The tractor is typically used for full-width installations but can 
also be used for detail repairs that are sufficiently large. 
Fabric Tractor Procedure:
1.	 Load roll onto front of tractor. Always load the red-colored
end of the core on the same end each time to allow place‑ 
ment of GlasGrid product with the self-adhesive side down. 
2.	Roll with rubber tired roller or with specially designed fabric
tractor to activate the pressure sensitive adhesive. 
3.	If a tack coat is specified, apply tack coat and allow to cure.
4.	Pave.
Manual Placement Installation
An alternative installation method involves manual placement 
of the GlasGrid product (Figure 2). Although the product is 
physically placed by hand, it is highly recommended that the 
GlasGrid roll be mounted on the back of a truck or other vehicle 
to help maintain tension during placement. Manual installation 
is more commonly used for localized areas of road.
Manual Placement Procedure 
1.	 Load roll on an appropriate dispensing device on the back
of a pick-up truck. To ensure that the GlasGrid product  
will always unroll with the sticky side face down, load  
so that the red-colored end of the core is on the same  
side each time.
2.	Secure the roll on the dispensing device with locking
device. 
3.	Secure start of roll on pavement as the truck slowly moves
away. 
4.	For shorter pieces pull GlasGrid product off roller to desired
length. Cut with utility knife and lay by hand. 
5.	Roll only with rubber tired roller. Roller must be kept clean.
6.	If a tack coat is specified, apply tack coat and allow to cure.
7.	 Pave.

TEST FOR PROPER ADHESION 
The following procedure can be used to determine whether 
sufficient adhesion exists between the GlasGrid and the 
underlying asphalt:
˴ Cut a square-shaped sample of the 
GlasGrid material approximately  
1m² (1 sq yd) in size.
˴ Place the sample on the road surface to 
be paved and under the same conditions.
˴ Apply adequate vertical pressure to fully activate the 
pressure sensitive adhesive e.g., by use of a rubber tired 
roller or by other means.
˴ Insert the hook of a spring balance under the center of the 
GlasGrid sample (Figure 5).
˴ Pull the spring balance upward until the sample starts to 
pull loose, and record the gauge reading.
˴ In the event that 20 lbs (19 kg) or more force is required  
to pull the sample up from the road surface, sufficient 
adhesion has been achieved to support typical paving 
operations. Therefore the placement of GlasGrid may 
proceed. 
˴ In the event that the sample does not have sufficient 
adherence, identify the cleanliness or moisture issues 
present and resolve them before installing the rest of  
the GlasGrid material.
