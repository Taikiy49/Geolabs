Dear Mr Mercer
We recently completed the field exploration work for the Southern Portion of Golf
Course Lot 2 1A in the Hawaii Kai area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii This letter report
summarizes our work performed on the project and presents our geotechnical engineering
recommendations derived from our analyses for the subject project
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located at the southern portion of 010 001
Parcels 1 and 4 in the vicinity of Kealahou Street in the Queen s Gate area of Hawaii Kai on
the Island of Oahu Hawaii A subdivision development is planned in the northern portion of
the site however the southern portion is zoned for preservation and will not be developed It
is desired to fill the southern portion of the site utilizing imported fill as well as the existing
stockpiled material
SITE DESCRIPTION
The site is located within the Queen s Gate area in Hawaii Kai on the Island of Oahu
Hawaii It is designated by Tax Map Key 010 001 Parcel 1 Based on our site
reconnaissance and the topographic map provided the site is relatively open and slopes
down gradually from northwest to southeast at an inclination of about nine horizontal to one
vertical 9H 1V Steeper slopes of up to two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V are present
at both the western boundary and center portion of the site
The existing ground elevations range from about 25 feet Mean Seal Level MSL at
the southeastern end to about 105 feet MSL at the northwestern end of the site A large
soil stockpile approximately 200 feet by 330 feet in plan dimension and about 38 feet high
was observed at the northwestern portion of the site

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our field exploration at the project site consisted of excavating five test pits
designated as Test Pit Nos 1 through 5 extending to depths of about 7 to 13 feet below the
existing ground surface The approximate locations of the test pits are shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2
Our field exploration generally encountered loose to dense silty sand and gravel
extending to the maximum depths explored of about 7 to 13 feet below the existing ground
surface In the eastern portion of the site Test Pit and 5 very stiff to hard silty clay
of about 4 5 to 11 5 feet thick were encountered at depths of about 0 to 0 75 feet below the
existing ground surface Cobbles and boulders were also encountered in Test Pit No 5
Groundwater was not encountered in the test pits at the time of our exploration
However it should be noted that groundwater levels are subject to change due to tidal
fluctuations seasonal precipitation runoff and other factors
Descriptions of the materials encountered in our field exploration are presented on the
Logs of Test Pits Plates A 1 through A 3 of Appendix A Laboratory tests were performed
on selected soil samples and the results are presented in Appendix B
SITE GRADING
The site will remain undeveloped or possibly used as a park in the
future Therefore a lower compaction requirement of 85 percent instead of the normal
90 percent is appropriate The 85 percent compaction will still provide a relatively dense
fill and resistance to erosion
Site Preparation
In general the area within the contract grading limits should be thoroughly cleared
and grubbed at the on set of earthwork Vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable
materials should be removed and disposed of off site
The subgrade surface should be scarified to a minimum depth of 8 inches
moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to not less than 85 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to

Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
Compaction should be accomplished by sheepsfoot rollers vibratory rollers or other
types of acceptable compaction equipment General fill materials should be placed in level
lifts not exceeding 12 inches in loose thickness moisture conditioned to above the optimum
moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 85 percent relative compaction Relative
compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the
maximum dry density as determined by ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91 Optimum
moisture is the water content percentage by weight corresponding to the maximum dry
density
In general boulders placed within the general fill mass should be spread out and
should not be nested together The boulders should be spread out such that compaction
equipment may be able to compact finer general fill materials between and around the
boulders The general fill materials between and around the boulders should be
compacted to a minimum of 85 percent relative compaction In addition the boulder fill
mass should be heavily watered by water trucks during placement to facilitate seating of
the boulder in the fill mass
Existing soil stockpiles of approximately 15 to 38 feet in height are located in the
northern and eastern portions of the site The stockpile materials generally consist of clayey
sands with gravel cobbles and boulders In general we believe that the stockpile materials
may be incorporated into the new fills The upper two feet of the stockpile materials should
be removed and recompacted
Fill slopes may be constructed at slope ratios of 2H 1V or less For slope heights
greater than 25 feet an 8 foot wide bench should be placed at mid height
Limitations
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from our field exploration Variations of subsoil conditions between and
beyond the field test pits may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not
become evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be
necessary to re evaluate the recommendations provided in this report
