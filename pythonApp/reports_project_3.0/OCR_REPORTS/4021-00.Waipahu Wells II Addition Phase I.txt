REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from two shield
volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be middle to
late Pliocene in age and forms the bulk of the western one third of the island The younger
shield Koolau is estimated to be late Pliocene to early Pleistocene Ice Age in age and
forms the majority of the eastern two thirds of the island The Koolau Shield reached the end
of its main shield building phase about 2 million years ago
The Schofield Plateau was formed when lavas from the Koolau Shield Volcano
ponded against the already eroded slopes of Waianae Volcano in the late Pleistocene
Epoch The dips of the lava beds generally range from about 3 to 5 degrees The lava flows
on the plateau have weathered to depths of about 50 to 100 feet and are characterized by
the red colors of the soil The deep narrow canyons such as Waikakalaua Kipapa and
Kaukonahua are the result of this weathering The project site is located at the southern
slopes of Schofield Plateau

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Our field exploration at the project site generally encountered very stiff to hard residual
soils consisting of reddish brown clayey silts extending from the ground surface to the
maximum depths of our exploration Based on our field exploration and laboratory testing
we believe that a shallow foundation system consisting of spread and or continuous footings
bearing on the recompacted near surface soils and or compacted fills may be used to
support the proposed structures
At the proposed backwash tank site soft to medium stiff reddish brown clayey silts
were encountered from approximately 2 5 to 9 feet below the existing ground surface Based
on our field exploration and laboratory testing we believe that the thin upper fill materials and
the underlying soft to medium stiff materials would not provide adequate foundation support
for the proposed backwash tank without appreciable settlements under the new loads In
order to reduce the potential for settlements we recommend that the soft to medium stiff soil
deposits at the backwash tank location be over excavated and replaced with non expansive
select granular fill Generally the depth of the over excavation would be on the order of
about 3 feet below the lowest point of the bottom of the foundation
Our laboratory tests also indicated that the in situ clayey silts exhibit a slight potential
for shrinking and swelling when subjected to moisture fluctuations In order to reduce the
potential for structural distress to future slabs on grade resulting from the shrinking swelling
of the in situ clayey silts we recommend that concrete slabs on grade be supported on at

Slabs On Grade
We anticipate that concrete slabs on grade will be used for the proposed control
building expansion and GAC tanks Our laboratory tests indicate that the clayey silt
encountered in our borings exhibit a slight potential for swelling when subjected to moisture
fluctuations To reduce the potential for structural distress to the slabs on grade resulting
from swelling of the clayey silt soils we recommend that the subgrade soils underneath the
slab areas be properly prepared and capped with at least 6 inches of non expansive select
granular fill material below the slab cushion The non expansive select granular fill should
also extend beyond the building perimeter a minimum of 24 inches
The non expansive select granular fill should be placed as soon as possible after the
subgrade soils are prepared in accordance with the recommendations presented in the Site
Preparation subsection of this report The select granular fill and the underlying subgrade
soils should be wetted and kept moist prior to final placement of concrete Where shrinkage
cracks are noted after compaction of the subgrade we recommend that the soil be
thoroughly moistened to close the cracks Saturation and subsequent yielding of the
exposed subgrade due to inclement weather and poor drainage may require over excavation
of the soft areas and replacement with well compacted fill
We recommend that a minimum 4 inch thick cushion layer consisting of free draining
materials such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation be placed below the
slab to provide more uniform support of the slab To reduce the potential for future moisture
infiltration and subsequent damage to floor coverings an impervious moisture vapor barrier
such as a plastic membrane is recommended on top of the cushion layer Two inches of
moist fine sand may be considered above the plastic membrane to provide protection of the
membrane and to aid in curing of the slab concrete The thickened edges of slabs adjacent
to unpaved areas should be embedded at least 12 inches below the lowest adjacent finished

Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork areas within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed Vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable
materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site or stockpiled in a
designated area to reduce the potential for contamination of the excavated materials
Existing utilities to be abandoned should be removed and the resulting excavation
should be properly backfilled with excavated on site materials placed in 8 inch level
loose lifts moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture and compacted to
a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the
in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density
of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91
Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by weight corresponding to the
maximum dry density Utilities to be abandoned in place under the proposed
structures should be backfilled by pumping lean concrete under low pressure

Fills and Backfills
In general the excavated on site clayey silt soils may be re used as a source of
general fill material provided that they are free of vegetation deleterious materials
and rock fragments greater than 3 inches in maximum dimension The on site clayey
silt soils may not be used as the select granular fill material recommended below
slabs on grade and the backwash tank foundation
Select granular fill should consist of non expansive select granular material such as
crushed coral basalt gravel or cinder sand The material should be well graded from
coarse to fine with no particles larger than 3 inches in largest dimension and should
contain between 10 and 30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve The material
should have a laboratory CBR vaiue of 20 or more and should have a maximum swell
of less than 1 percent when tested in accordance with ASTM Test Designation
D 1883 Imported fill materials if required should conform to the requirements of
select granular fill material
Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
Fill and backfill materials should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose
thickness moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction The fill and backfill
material within the upper 3 feet below the finished grades in pavement areas should
be compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Relative compaction

Drainage
The finished grades outside the new backwash tank GAC tanks and control building
expansion should be sloped to shed water away from the foundations and to reduce the
potential for ponding It is also advised that gutter systems be installed around the building
and that the discharge be diverted away from the foundation and pavement areas
Excessive landscape watering near the foundations and slabs should also be avoided
Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have concrete bottoms and drains to
reduce the potential for water infiltration into the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the above
foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation and
subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations should be properly
backfilled against the walls or slab edges immediately after setting of the concrete to reduce
the potential for water infiltration Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible
and should be maintained to drain surface water run off away from the foundations and
slabs

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling three
borings extending to depths of about 6 5 to 15 feet below the existing ground surface at the
approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were drilled using a
truck mounted drill rig equipped with continuous flight solid stem augers
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by testing
in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representations of the materials
encountered are presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 3
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with ASTM
Test Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving
a 3 inch OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The
blow counts needed to drive the sampler the last 12 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown on
the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Pocket penetrometer tests were performed on selected cohesive soil samples in the
field The pocket penetrometer test provides an indication of the unconfined compressive
strength of the soil sample Results of the pocket penetrometer tests are summarized on the
Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
