INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration performed
for the proposed Sadoshima Residence project located at 6 Lumahai Street in the Koko
Head area of Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of
the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering recommendations
derived from our field exploration and engineering analyses These recommendations are
intended for the design of foundations retaining walls and site grading only The findings
and recommendations presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this
report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
We understand that it is proposed to demolish the existing house and construct a new
understand that the subject lot encompasses an area of approximately 38 819 square feet
Based on the available information the new residence will consist of a two to three story
concrete structure We understand that a retaining wall system will be constructed for grade
separation and foundation support and that a swimming pool will also be constructed within
the proposed residence as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Based on the site plan and the preliminary plans provided the residence will be
constructed on a cut condition made into the sloping terrain of the lot Due to the relatively
steep sloping ground conditions at the project site we anticipate that a retaining wall system
will be necessary for the grade separation and foundation support needed to provide a level
pad for the proposed residence
Based on the surface conditions at the project site and the preliminary plans we
anticipate that site grading consisting of up to about 15 feet of cuts will generally be
needed to achieve the design finished grades for the proposed project Further details
pertaining to the proposed residential construction were not available at the time this report
was prepared

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from two shield
volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be middle to
late Pliocene in age and forms the bulk of the western third of the island The younger
shield Koolau is estimated to be late Pliocene to early Pleistocene Ice Age in age and
forms the majority of the eastern two thirds of the island Waianae became extinct while
Koolau was still active and its eastern flank was partially buried below Koolau lavas banking
against its eastern flank These banked or ponded lavas formed a broad plateau referred
to as the Schofield Plateau Both Waianae and Koolau Ranges have lost most of their
original shield outlines and are now long narrow ridges shaped largely by erosion
The project area is located on the southeastern flank of Koko Crater of the Honolulu
Volcanic Series The subsoil conditions at the project site are generally formed by the
deposition of recent pyroclastic materials and lava flows from eruptions of the vents of
members of the Honolulu Volcanic Series

House Foundations
As previously mentioned the proposed residence will consist of a two to three story
concrete structure Based on the subsurface conditions encountered during our
exploration we recommend that a shallow foundation system consisting of spread and or
continuous footings be used to support the proposed residence An allowable bearing
pressure of up to 3 500 pounds per square foot psf may be used for the design of
foundations bearing on the re compacted on site sandy materials and or the weathered tuff
rock This allowable bearing pressure is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by
one third 1 3 for transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic forces
Footings should be embedded a minimum depth of 24 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grade Due to the sloping terrain at the project site we recommend that
he foundations be embedded deep enough to obtain a minimum 6 foot setback distance
measured horizontally from the outside edge of the footing to the face of the slope The
6 foot horizontal setback distance may be neglected for footings bearing directly on slightly
weathered tuff rock formation Continuous footings running parallel to the direction of the
slope should be constructed in stepped footings
Foundations located next to utility trenches or easements should be embedded below
a 1H 1V imaginary plane extending upward from the bottom edge of the utility trench or as
deep as the inverts of the utility lines This requirement is necessary to avoid surcharging
GEOLABS INC
W O 4550 00 Page 7
adjacent below grade structures with additional structural loads and to reduce the potential
for foundation settlement Special attention should be given to this requirement for structures
located within close proximity to the sewer line easement located at the northern portion of
the site
If the foundations are designed and constructed in accordance with our
recommendations we estimate that the total footing settlements under the anticipated design
loads to be less than 1 inch Differential settlements of the foundations are estimated to be
on the order of 0 5 inches
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by frictional resistance
developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil and by passive earth
pressure acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system A coefficient of
friction of 0 40 may be used for footings bearing on recompacted on site soils and or
weathered tuff rock Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be estimated using an
equivalent fluid pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth pcf This
assumes that the soils around the footings are well compacted Unless covered by
pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the upper 12 inches of soil should be
neglected
We recommend that footing excavations be observed by a representative from
Geolabs prior to placement of reinforcing steel or concrete to confirm the foundation bearing
conditions and the required embedment depths
Slabs On Grade
In general we envision that concrete slabs on grade will be used for the proposed
residence Based on the information from the drilled borings we recommend that the
subgrade be properly prepared and kept moist prior to placement of cushion fill and slab
concrete The subgrade for slabs on grades should be scarified to a depth of at least
8 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture and compacted to not less
than 90 percent relative compaction
GEOLABS INC
W O 4550 00 Page 8
We recommend that a cushion fill layer consisting of 4 inches of No 3B Fine gravel
ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation be provided below the slab for uniform support The
cushion fill would also serve as a capillary break A moisture membrane should be
incorporated above the capillary break to reduce the potential for damage to
moisture sensitive floor coverings by moisture infiltration It may be advisable to place a
2 inch layer of moist fine sand above the vapor membrane to protect the membrane and
to aid in curing of the concrete Provisions for the 2 inch layer of moist fine sand above the
vapor barrier should be left up to the discretion of the project structural engineer
The thickened edges of slabs adjacent to unpaved areas should be embedded at
least 12 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade Areas adjacent to the slab
should be backfilled tightly against the edges of the slab with on site soil and graded to
divert water away from the slab to reduce the potential for water ponding around the slab
edges
Swimming Pool Foundation
In general we believe that the swimming pool foundation may be designed in
accordance with the parameters and recommendations presented in the House
Foundations section of this report Because the swimming pool will be water retention
structure we envision that the foundation for the poo will likely consist of mat foundation
To provide a relatively uniform surface below the mat foundation we recommend
that the pool foundation be underlain by a layer of compacted cushion fill material The
cushion fill material may consist of 6 inches of select borrow subbase The cushion fill
material should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction The pool subgrade should
be firm and unyielding prior to construction of the pool foundation and or bottom slab

Site Grading
Based on the schematic plans provided cuts up to about 15 feet deep and minor fills
are anticipated for the site grading The deep cuts anticipated at the site will encounter
medium hard tuff rock formation near the existing ground surface Considering the relatively
small volume of rock excavation at the site we envision that it would not be economically
feasible to process the excavated rock as fill materials maximum particle size of 3 inches
or less Therefore we envision that the excavated rock materials would not be re used as
fill materials unless they are processed to well graded materials with a maximum particle size
of 3 inches
Following demolition of the existing house the area within the contract grading limits
should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed Vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable
materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site After clearing and grubbing
the exposed subgrade should be scarified to a minimum depth of 8 inches properly
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum
of 90 percent relative compaction For subgrades subjected to vehicular traffic such as
garage slab the compaction requirement should be increased to a minimum of 95 percent
relative compaction
Soft and yielding areas encountered during clearing and grubbing should be
over excavated to expose firm material and the resulting excavation should be backfilled
with well compacted fill
As previously indicated the excavated rocky materials may only be used as fill or
backfill materials provided that they are processed and reduced to a relatively well graded
granular material with a maximum particle size of about 3 inches Fill and backfill materials
to be used as fill material up to the slab subgrade level should consist of well graded granular
GEOLABS INC
W O 4550 00 Page 12
materials less than 3 inches in largest dimension Imported materials should be well graded
from coarse to fine with no particles greater than 3 inches in largest dimension The material
should have a laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR value of 20 or higher and a swell
potential of 1 percent or less when tested in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1883
and should also contain between 10 and 30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve
Imported fill materials should be observed and or tested by Geolabs for suitability prior to
being transported to the site
Fills and backfills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose
thickness moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to
a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction In areas where the finished grades will be
subjected to vehicular traffic the compaction requirement of the subgrade should be
increased to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the
in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the
same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 91 test procedures Optimum
moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry
density

We envision that new utility connetions i e water sewer and drain lines will be
required for the proposed residence It is anticipated that most of the trenches for utilities will
be excavated in basalt rock or the compacted fill In general granular bedding consisting of
6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation is recommended under the
pipes for support of the utility lines Free draining granular materials such as No 3B Fine
gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation should also be used for the initial trench backfill up
to about 12 inches above the pipes to provide adequate support around the pipes and to

reduce the compaction effort of the backfill in order to reduce the possibility of damaging the

voids below the haunches of pipes and to provide adequate support for the sides of the

The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipes to the

concrete jacketed

strength for footings in the vicinity

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling
five borings extending to depths of about 9 to 13 feet below the existing ground surface at
the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were drilled using
portable drilling equipment equipped with coring tools
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually in the laboratory
Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil Classification System as
shown on Plate A Graphic representations of the materials encountered are provided on
the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 thru A 5
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 3550 84 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 3 inch
OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches In addition
some samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a
2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same hammer and drop The blow counts
needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown as
the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Core samples of rock materials encountered at the site were obtained using diamond
core drilling techniques in general accordance with ASTM Standard Practice D 2113 83
Diamond Core Drilling for Site Investigation Core drilling is a rotary drilling method which
uses a hollow bit to cut into the rock formation The rock material left in the hollow core of
the bit is mechanically recovered for examination and description
Recovery REC is used as a subjective guide to the interpretation of the relative
quality of rock masses Recovery is defined as the actual length of material recovered
from a coring attempt versus the length of the core attempt For example if 3 7 feet of
material is recovered from a 5 0 foot core run the recovery would be 74 percent and would
be shown on the Boring Logs as REC 74
The Rock Quality Designation RQD is also a subjective guide to the relative quality
of rock masses RQD is defined as the percentage of the core run that is sound material
in excess of 4 inches in length without any discontinuities discounting any drilling induced
fractures or breaks If 2 5 feet of sound material is recovered from a 5 0 foot core run the
RQD would be 50 percent and would be shown on the Logs of Borings as RQD 50
Generally the following is used to describe the relative quality of the rock based on the
