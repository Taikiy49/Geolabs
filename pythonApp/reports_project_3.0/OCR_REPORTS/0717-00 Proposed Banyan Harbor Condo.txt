INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this investigation is to evaluate subsurface
conditions to provide soil data for the design and construc
tion of the proposed Banyan Harbor Condominium at Lihue Kauai
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations

SITE DESCRIPTION
The site is located generally on the sidehill of a ridge in
Nawiliwili Kauai The site is bordered by Nawiliwili Highway
to the south Wilcox Road to the southeast and Wapa and Lala
Roads to the north A cemetary and Kauai High School are
located west of the site
The southern portion of the site is generally flat and slopes
upward toward the north at about 2 to 10 percent gradients
The northern portion of the site is steeper with gradients
of 25 to 50 percent The northeastern portion of the site
is located on the top of the ridge and is generally flat
with gradients of about 10 percent over most of the area
An easement road in the hillside about 20 feet wide was
noted along the eastern boundary of the site
Most of the site is covered with trees and brush Several
abandoned houses and dirt roads were noted on the site
The elevation at the site generally varies from about 12 to
70 feet
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The present plan is to construct 12 3 story wood framed
townhouse structures with semi basements

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology 3
The area is essentially Koloa volcanic deposits of early
Pleistocene Epoch These lava flows of nepheline basalts
erupted from numerous vents Alluvium and calcareous sedi
ments are also present in the lower reaches
During the Pleistocene Epoch stream valleys and sea cliffs
were eroded to a base level of up to 100 feet below the exis
ting sea level Beaches of calcareous sand were formed and
blown inland to form dunes which were then covered by later
flows of basalt As the sea rose around the island the
valley mouths were alluviated
The lava flows of the Koloa Volcanic series are poorly to
moderately permeable and carry fresh or brackish water
The calcareous dunes are highly permeable and contain
brackish water

Soil Conditions
Based on the borings the site appears to be underlain by
two distinct soil types
In the lower southern portion of the site the subsoils consist
of 1 to 10 feet of stiff fill or alluvium over medium density
sand
In the upper portions of the site the subsoils appear to con
sist of a layer about 5 to 8 feet of medium clayey silt over
stiff silty clay with decomposed rock to 13 to 21 feet the
depths drilled
In the northeastern portion of the site on the top of the
ridge about 15 feet of decomposed and weathered rock over
fractured lava rock was encountered
Water was noted in Boring 7 in the lower area at 11 5 feet
below the existing ground surface
Groundwater was not noted in the other borings during the
field exploration

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration indicated that most of the upper area
is covered with a 5 to 8 feet layer of medium clayey silt
Field observations and laboratory tests of this material
indicate that this silty soil will be susceptible to erosion
and settlements due to wetting
Foundations should avoid bearing on this layer and slope
cuts should be maintained When encountered under pavement
areas this silty material should be scarified to a depth
of 2 feet and recompacted prior to filling
It appears that the lower area of the site is underlain by
medium sand at shallow depths To reduce settlements due to
this sand layer the building areas should be proofrolled with
a heavy vibratory compactor to densify the sand prior to filling

Fill slopes should not be constructed at slopes steeper
than s
Prior to filling the site should be cleared of all vege

Fills should be placed as soon as practicable to allow the
subsoils to adjust to new load
Structural fill should be placed in 9 inch loose horizontal
lifts and compacted to at least 90 of the maximum dry den
sity established by ASTM Test D 1557
Fills placed on slopes having grades steeper than 5 hori
zontal to 1 vertical should be keyed and benched into the
existing firm ground
To help prevent slope instability utility trenches should
be located away from the tops and bottoms of slopes

In the upper areas of the site where the medium clayey silt
layer was encountered the building foundations should extend
through this silty material and bear on the stiff residual
material encountered below at about 5 foot depths
The following foundation alternatives may be considered

below the upper brown silty material and

Retaining and Basement Walls
Retaining or basement type wall may be utilized for the
bottom floor of thezunits
Well graded granular material such as base course rock
should be used as backfill material behind the walls
The recommendations given under foundations should be
followed for the design of the wall foundations
For lateral earth pressures the following can be used
Walls unrestrained at the top 45 p c f equivalent
fluid pressure
Walls restrained at top 60 p c f equivalent
fluid pressure
Additional load due to traffic or sloping
surcharge should be included where applicable
A fraction factor of 0 4 can be utilized to determine the
sliding resistance of the wall foundation
A subsurface drainage system should be used to prevent the
build up of hydrostatic pressure behind the walls
Slab On Grade
Slab on grade may be utilized for the garage areas A 4 inch
layer of base course should be provided below the slab and
compacted to 95 of its maximum dry density

It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during
earthwork construction particularly the excavation and
replacement of the medium clayey silt layer and to monitor
the contractor s grading operations
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be
observed by 5 soils engineer during construction to assure
that adequate bearing has been obtained and that the sub
surface conditions are consistent with the findings of our
subsurface investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive and
should be tested and approved by the soils engineer prior
to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent
upon the above inspections
