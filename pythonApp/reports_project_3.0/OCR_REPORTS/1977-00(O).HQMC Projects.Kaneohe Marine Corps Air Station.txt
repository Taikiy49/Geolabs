INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the proposed Leadership Reaction Course and Gas
Chamber Facility in the Marine Corps Air Station in Kaneohe on the island
The general location of the project site is shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1

This report summarizes the findings of the exploration and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from analyses of the
exploration data obtained These recommendations are intended for design
input and not intended for construction estimation or bidding
PROJECT CONSTDERATIONS
It is proposed to build a one story CMU structure to house the
proposed Leadership Reaction Course and Gas Chamber Facility in Kaneohe
Marine Corps Air Station
The proposed structure will be approximately 80 feet by 180 feet in
plan It will consist of free standing 8 to 12 inch thick CMW wall
partitions and a partial prestressed hollow core roof with concrete
topping supported by CMU walls Maximum wall loads will be 2500 to 3000
pounds per linear foot
Concrete sSlab on grade as well as an elevated walkway are planned for
the project
Minor site fills of 1 to 2 feet will be used to grade the site for
drainage concerns
A parking area is also planned but is not included in our scope of
work

The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface conditions at the project site in order to develop a
generalized soil rock data set for the formilation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertinent to the site grading foundation and
slab design of the proposed structure

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located east of the south end of Harris Avenue in
the vicinity of Nu upia Ponds Wildlife Management Area in the Marine Corps
Air Station in Kaneohe on the island of Oahu Hawaii Buildings 3095
4077 and 4078 are also in the project vicinity

The site is presently overgrown with heavy vegetation including 4 to
5 feet tall California grass and Haole Koa trees
A trail was cleared to the boring locations during our field
exploration Based on the observation of the cleared ground it appears
that the site is relatively flat
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions at the new structure location were explored
by drilling two 2 borings to a depth of 26 5 feet below the existing
ground surface The approximate locations of these borings are shown on
the Site Plan Plate 2
In general the site is underlain by a thin crust of very stiff
clayey silt underlain by very dense to dense silty coral sand and gravel
The sand and gravel deposits grade looser with depth and have generally
loose density below the depth of 15 to 20 feet
Groundwater was encountered in the borings at depths of approximately
5 8 to 6 0 feet below the existing ground surface during our field
exploration
DISCUSSTON AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration disclosed competent near surface bearing
subsoils at the project site suitable for shallow foundation support We
recommend that an allowable footing bearing capacity of 3000 p s f may be
used for design
Minor ground subsidence due to the proposed site fills is
anticipated However the ground settlements should occur relatively
quickly due to the granular nature of the underlying subsoils Therefore
we believe that the site fills should be placed as early as possible prior
to the building construction

W O Page 5
Footing Foundation
Based on the competent subsoils encountered we believe that shallow
footing foundations may be used to support the proposed light weight
structure
An allowable bearing capacity of 3000 p s f may be used for the
footing foundation design This value is for total dead plus live loads
only and may be increased by up to one third 1 3 for short duration
wind or seismic load considerations
A minimum footing embedment of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent
finished grade is recommended
The bottoms of the footing excavations should be recompacted to at
least 95 of its maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Provided the above design guidelines are followed footing
settlements under the anticipated design loading are estimated to be less
differential
Resistance to lateral loads acting on the proposed structure may be
provided by passive soil pressure and frictional resistance We recommend
that an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 p s f per foot of depth be used
to calculate the passive soil pressure developed against the buried
vertical faces of the footing foundations and a frictional coefficient of
0 4 be used to calculate the frictional resistance developed between the
footing bottoms and the recompacted footing subgrade
Site Grading
Site fills of 1 to 2 feet are planned at the project site for
drainage concerns Based on our settlement estimate it appears that up
to one 1 inch of ground subsidence may occur under the weight of the
fills Therefore we recommend that the proposed site fills be placed as
early as possible prior to the building construction

W O Page 6
At the onset of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed Any unsuitable
materials such as rubbish vegetation and other deleterious materials
should be removed and disposed of properly off site
The area to receive fills or slab on grade should be scarified to a
minimum depth of 6 inches below the grubbed cleared grade or slab subgrade
level moisture conditioned and recompacted to a minimum of 95 of its
maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Any loose soft or yielding spots encountered at the cleared or slab
subgrade level should be over excavated down to in situ firm ground and
replaced with in situ or imported non expansive backfill compacted in
8 inch loose lifts to a minimum of 95 of its maximum dry density per ASTM
Test Method D 1557 78
New imported fill and backfill materials if needed should consist
of non expansive select granular materials less than 3 inches in maximm
size with less than 20 passing the 200 sieve
Slab On Grade
Depending on the finished grades the proposed slabs on grade may be
constructed on the recompacted in situ firm ground or on compacted
non expansive fill materials We believe that a four 4 inch concrete
Slab over four 4 inch base course may be used for design provided the
subgrade is compacted recompacted to at least 95 of its maximm dry
density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the project geotechnical engineer for review and written
comment prior to construction This review is needed to determine
adherence to the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein
If this review is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume
responsibility for misinterpretation of the recommendations

Construction Observation
It is recommended that the project geotechnical engineer be retained
to provide geotechnical engineering services during the site grading and
foundation construction phases of the project This is to observe
compliance with the design concepts specifications or recommendations and
to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ
from that anticipated prior to the start of construction The
recommendations given in this report are contingent upon such
observations
Imported fill material if required should consist of select
non expansive material such as select borrow coral or cinder and should
be tested for suitability as fill material by the project geotechnical
engineer prior to its delivery to the project site
If actual rock soil conditions encountered during construction are
different from those assumed or considered in this report then
appropriate modifications to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from borings Variations of conditions
between the borings may occur and the nature and extent of these
variations may not become evident until construction If variations then
appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations
given in this report
The locations of the borings in the project site were determined by
taping from the existing structures in the project vicinity as indicated
on the undated Site Plan of the project site provided by Department of the
Navy Elevations of the borings are unknown at this stage The physical
locations of the borings should be considered accurate only to the degree
implied by the methods used

borings depict the approximate boundaries between soil types and as such

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling two
2 borings to a depth of 26 5 feet below the existing ground surface at
the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 Truck mounted
equipment was used to drill the borings
The material encountered in the borings were classified by visual and
textural examination in the field by our geologist who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual observation and testing in the laboratory The soils
were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A graphic presentation of the materials
encountered is presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 and A 2
Soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving a standard
penetration sampler or a 2 4 inch I D split barrel sampler with a
140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts to drive the sampler
the last 12 inches are shown on the Boring Logs at the appropriate sample
depths
