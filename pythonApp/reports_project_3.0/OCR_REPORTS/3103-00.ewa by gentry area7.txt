Purpose and Scope

of the subsurface soils at the site Our work on this project was in general conformance
with ASTM Test Designation G 57 78 and with the requirements provided in the Water
System External Corrosion Control Standards Volume 3 dated 1991 and subsequent
amendments and additions

FIELD EXPLORATION
Geophysical survey equipment i e resistance meter was mobilized to the site on
Our field work was then performed on successive sunny days with daily
high temperatures of approximately 87 F and a generally dry surface condition Road B
at this area however was an exception for being muddy due to on going earthwork
Unusual ground surface conditions are foot noted on the plates
Forty nine 49 soil resistivity surveys were conducted at 17 locations in the above
referenced area using the Wenner four pin method and a resistance meter Strata Scout
R 40C manufactured by SoilTest Inc with 120 volts voltage output and 24 watts power
output into the ground This method utilizes four surface probes electrodes to evaluate
the average resistivity an indication of corrosivity of the underlying soil mass The
average soil resistance was evaluated at test locations with the electrodes aligned at equal
spacings of generally four 4 five 5 and six 6 feet Among the spacings used one
spacing or two may have more significance than the others for allowing more contribution
to the evaluated soil resistivity from the closest mass of soil to the proposed
pipe invert Such spacing s are flagged with one asterisk on the plates
The tests were generally performed at the stakes locations except for those at
Road M Most of the planned subgrade of Road M was below a hill The stake at Road M
was located on top of that hill at about 11 to 12 feet above the proposed pipe invert We
performed our resistivity survey at two other locations closer to the pipe invert Test

The resistance meter R 40C impresses a voltage between the outer electrodes
causing an electrical current to flow between them and the voltage drop between the
inner electrodes is then measured by a sensitive built in voltmeter Alternatively soil
resistance Ohm can be found directly on the dial The soil resistivity Ohm cm which
is directly related to both resistance and spacing can then be computed The evaluated
soil resistances the computed resistivities and the corresponding corrosion rating are
presented in Plates 3 1 through 3 3 of this report
Resistivity is generally recognized as one of the most significant soil characteristic
with regard to the corrosivity of the soil In general the lower the resistivity the greater
the potential for corrosion of buried metallic structures Conversely the higher the
resistivity the less likely the soil will contribute to corrosion
LIMITATIONS
The evaluations presented in this report are based upon information obtained from
field data points by the means of geophysical surveys Variations of soil conditions
between the field data points may occur and the nature and extent of such variations
may not become evident until a later point in time If variations then appear evident it
will be necessary to re evaluate the ratings provided in this report
Elevations of the data presented in this report are approximate having been
estimated from those marked on the stakes as existing surface elevations and scaled of
the construction plans prepared by Gentry Hawaii dated April 1993 as approximate pipe
inverts All elevations were rounded to the nearest 4 foot to emphasize that they may
