Introduction
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering analyses
performed for the Roadway Pavement Evaluation Pukalani Terrace Subdivision project
in the Makawao area on the Island of Maui Hawaii The project location and general
vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings from our field exploration and laboratory
testing and presents our geotechnical recommendations derived from our analyses for
the rehabilitation and reconstruction of the existing pavements only The findings and
recommendations presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this
report
1 2
Project Considerations
The project site is located within the Pukalani Terrace Subdivision in the
Makawao area on the Island of Maui Hawaii We understand that it is desired to
evaluate the existing pavement conditions along some of the roadways within the
subdivision in order to determine whether resurfacing and or reconstruction should be
performed to support a pavement life of 15 to 20 years The approximate limits of the
roadways under consideration for this project include the following
Aliiolani Street extending from Iolani Street to Elilani Street
Elilani Street extending from Iolani Street to Aliiolani Street
Hololani Street extending from Iolani Street to Hokulani Street
Olulani Street extending from Liliuokalani Street to Nalani Street
Liholani Street extending from Elilani Street to Aina Lani Drive
In general the existing pavements showing minor distresses and severity will be
rehabilitated with cold planing and an AC overlay Reconstruction of the existing
pavements will be performed for those areas where the observed distresses are likely
caused by failures in the underlying base subbase and or subgrade materials Special
attention was given to the potential presence of near surface soft and or loose soils

Regional Geology
The Island of Maui was built by two major volcanoes the older West Maui
Tertiary Epoch and the more recent East Maui also known as Haleakala Pleistocene
Epoch The Isthmus of Maui is a narrow gently sloping plain located between these
two volcanoes The project site is situated on the isthmus connecting the two volcanic
domes
The isthmus was built by the deposition of sediments over the underlying basalts
of the two volcanoes During the Pleistocene Epoch sea levels fell to stands lower than
present sea level exposing large areas of coralline sand on what was the ocean bottom
Wind action transported these coralline sands inland forming extensive areas of sand
dunes In some areas these dunes have been mantled by man made fill which was
apparently placed during the period that the area was used for the cultivation of sugar
cane
2 2
Existing Site Pavement Conditions
The existing roadways within the limits under consideration for this project are
generally two lane undivided roadways The approximate limits of the five roadways are
shown on the General Site Plan Plate 2
The shoulder areas beyond the edge of pavement generally consist of short cut
grass up to the edge of pavement and or sidewalks traffic signs and utility posts The
surface soils along the shoulder areas below the vegetation generally consist of dark
reddish brown clayey silts with sands as well as red silty cinder sands with gravel The
existing Liholani Street and Olulani Street roadway alignments generally slope down
from east to west Elilani Street and Aliiolani Street roadway alignments slope from
south to north and Hololani Street roadway alignment slopes from north to south
between the limits of the project
Our office conducted a site reconnaissance in September 2015 to collect general
information for the existing pavement conditions along the portions of the roadways
under consideration for this project Detailed descriptions of our field survey are

Subsurface Conditions
Our field exploration program consisted of excavating and sampling five test pits
designated as Test Pit Nos 1 through 5 at selected locations along the project roadway
alignments extending to depths of about 12 to 14 inches below the existing pavement
surface In addition six bulk samples of the subgrade soils designated as Bulk 1
through Bulk 6 were collected for laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR tests The
approximate test pit and bulk sample locations are shown on the General Site Plan
Plate 2 and on the Site Plans Plates 3 1 through 3 6
In general our excavated test pits for the roadway pavement evaluation project
encountered a pavement section consisting of approximately 1 5 to 4 5 inches of AC
over 3 to 10 inches of silty cinder sands with gravel possibly base course materials
Below the cinder sands some of the excavated test pits encountered clayey silts with
gravel extending to the maximum depth explored of approximately 14 inches below the
existing pavement surface
We did not encounter groundwater in the shallow excavated test pits at the time
of our field exploration It should be noted that water level may vary with seasonal
rainfall time of the year and other factors
Detailed descriptions of the materials encountered in the test pits are presented
on the Logs of Test Pits in Appendix A Results of the laboratory tests performed on
selected soil samples are presented in Appendix B

Rehabilitation of Existing Pavements
In general the type and severity of the distresses exhibited in the pavement
areas would determine whether rehabilitation or reconstruction of the existing
pavements should be performed Existing pavements with minor distresses are
generally rehabilitated with an AC overlay with cold planing Based on our evaluation it
is our opinion that pavement areas with minor distresses may be rehabilitated with an
AC overlay consisting of cold planing 2 inches of the existing pavements and replacing
with 2 inches of new AC overlay
In general the contractor should vacuum sweep the pavement areas
immediately after cold planing to remove gravel sized materials and dust not picked up
by the cold planing operation It should be noted that the vacuum sweep should not
preclude the dust control operation during cold planing In general the contractor
should begin paving no later than three working days after cold planing begins Finally
vacuum sweeping of cold planed surfaces should also be performed immediately prior
to paving

Design Pavement Section
As discussed in the Existing Site Pavement Conditions subsection herein some
of the observed distresses along the roadways are likely caused by failures in the
underlying base subbase and or subgrade materials In general these areas should be
reconstructed with a new pavement section in lieu of an AC overlay section In addition
these distressed areas should be repaired prior to the AC overlay work for the entire
roadway under consideration
Based on the anticipated traffic volume and the design subgrade conditions as
described above it is our opinion that the following flexible pavement design section
may be used for the pavement reconstruction areas in accordance with the guidelines
presented in the Methodology of Pavement Design subsection of this report

To enhance the pavement support capability of the subgrade materials
consideration may be given to placing a layer of reinforcing geogrid such as Tensar
TriAx Grid TX5 or equivalent on a layer of permeable separator Mirafi 180N or
equivalent below the new pavement structural section In general the triaxial geogrid
will interlock with the aggregate base course resulting in two benefits during initial
construction and for the life of the project 1 lateral confinement increasing the
modulus of the aggregate base course and 2 subgrade bearing capacity
enhancement Therefore we recommend that the incorporation of the reinforcing
geogrid and permeable separator below the new pavement structural section be
included as an additive bid item for the project
The exposed pavement subgrade materials should be proof rolled with a large
vibratory drum roller minimum 10 tons static weight a minimum of eight passes The
vibratory drum roller should be operated at a speed of about 300 feet per minute
A Geolabs representative should monitor the pavement subgrade preparation to
observe whether undesirable materials are encountered during the excavation and
scarification process and to confirm whether the exposed soil rock conditions are similar
to those encountered in our field exploration
Aggregate base course materials required for the project should consist of
crushed basaltic aggregates with a 1 5 inch maximum nominal size and should conform
to Subsection 703 06 of the State of Hawaii Standard Specifications for Road and
Bridge Construction 2005 Aggregate base material should be moisture conditioned to
above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent
relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil

Surface Drainage
One of the primary distress mechanisms in pavement structures is pumping due
to saturation of the subbase and base course and or subgrade soils Therefore the
pavement surface should be sloped to drain and drainage gradients should be
maintained to carry surface water off the pavement to appropriate drainage structures
Surface water ponding should not be allowed during or after construction Development
of good shoulder drainage along with a program to prevent obstructions in the drainage
structures is strongly recommended to reduce the potential for pavement deterioration
or premature failure of the pavements

Field Exploration
We explored the subsurface conditions along the roadway alignments by
excavating and sampling five test pits designated as Test Pit Nos 1 through 5 The test
pits extended to depths of about 12 to 14 inches below the existing pavement surface The
approximate test pit locations are shown on the Site Plans Plates 3 1 through 3 6 The
test pits were excavated using a CASE 580 backhoe
Our geologist classified the materials encountered in the test pits by visual and
textural examination in the field in general accordance with ASTM D2488 Standard
Practice for Description and Identification of Soils and monitored the excavation
operations on a near continuous full time basis These classifications were further
reviewed visually and by testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general
accordance with ASTM D2487 Standard Practice for Classification of Soils for
Engineering Purposes Unified Soil Classification System as shown on the Soil Log
Legend Plate A 0 1 Deviations made to the soil classification in accordance with
ASTM D2487 are described in the Soil Classification Log Key Plate A 0 2 Grab samples
were obtained from the excavated test pits at selected depths for further visual review and
laboratory testing Graphic representations of the materials encountered are presented on
the Logs of Test Pits Plates A 1 through A 5

Laboratory Tests
Moisture Content ASTM D2216 determinations were performed on selected soil
samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties The test results
are presented on the logs of test pits at the appropriate sample depths
Three Atterberg Limits tests ASTM D4318 were performed on selected soil

depths Graphic presentation of the test results is provided on Plate B 1

of the in situ materials to evaluate the gradation characteristics of the soils and to aid in
soil classification Graphic presentations of the test results are presented on Plate B 2
Six laboratory California Bearing Ratio tests ASTM D1883 were performed on
bulk samples of the in situ materials to evaluate their pavement support characteristics
The test results are presented on Plates B 3 through B 8
