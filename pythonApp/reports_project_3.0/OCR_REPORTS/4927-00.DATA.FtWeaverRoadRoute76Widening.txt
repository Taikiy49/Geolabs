This geotechnical field and laboratory data report presents the results of our
preliminary geotechnical engineering exploration conducted for the Fort Weaver Road
Route 76 Widening project between Aawa Street and Geiger Road in the District of Ewa
on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are
shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This data report presents the results of our preliminary field exploration and
laboratory testing of materials for the project The findings presented herein are intended
for preliminary site characterization in support of the preliminary design of the project only
The findings presented herein are subject to the additional exploration and limitations
noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed project is located along Fort Weaver Road Route 76 in the District
of Ewa on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project starts from approximately 1 000 feet
south of Laulaunui Road Station 123 80 and extends approximately 1 000 feet south of
Geiger Road Station 257 45 as shown on the General Site Plan Plate 2
The existing Fort Weaver Road Route 76 is a four lane expressway with 12 foot
lanes 6 to 10 foot wide shoulders and a 36 foot wide grass median An 8 foot wide
bicycle and pedestrian pathway is located along portions of the northbound side of
Fort Weaver Road The existing pavements along Fort Weaver Road consist of asphaltic
concrete and the pavements appeared to be in relatively good condition at the time of our
field exploration

The existing roadway generally slopes down gradually from the north to south with

Start of the project near the Honouliuli Stream Bridge rises to about 55 feet MSL at about
the Ewa Cane Haul Underpass Bridge and then slopes down to about 30 to 35 feet
MSL near Geiger Road
In general the proposed improvements project include widening Fort Weaver Road
from four to six through lanes at the Aawa Drive Renton Road and Geiger Road
intersections plus the straightaway section between Aawa Drive and Plantation Road We
understand that the areas near Kolowaka Drive will be widened under a separate contract
by others The proposed widening will consist of a new 12 foot through lane and 10 foot
shoulder in each travel direction In addition right turn deceleration lanes 12 feet wide
will be provided at the above intersections The new roadway widening project also will
require provision of two additional lanes one lane in each travel direction at the existing
Honouliuli Stream Bridge traversing Honouliuli Stream
In addition to widening of the roadway construction of retaining walls and or
steepened slopes may be required along portions of the widened roadway We also
understand that new roadway lighting will be installed in some areas A bike pedestrian
path also will be constructed along the roadway

FIELD EXPLORATION PROGRAM
The preliminary field exploration program for the project generally consisted of
limited test boring exploration and bulk sampling of the near surface soils for laboratory
testing The following subsections provide a description of the various components of our
preliminary field exploration program for the project
Test Boring Exploration
The subsurface conditions along the proposed Fort Weaver Road Route 76
Widening project were explored by advancing 13 borings to depths ranging from about
10 8 to 41 5 feet below the existing ground surface The borings drilled are identified as
Boring Nos 1 through 13 and the approximate locations of the borings drilled are shown
on the Site Plans Plates 3 1 through 3 7
The borings were sited at selected locations along the existing Fort Weaver Road
alignment In general the boring locations were distributed evenly along the roadway
alignment in order to obtain a general overview of the subsurface conditions throughout
the length of the roadway alignment The borings were advanced using a truck mounted
drill rig equipped with solid stem augers
Detailed description of our field exploration methodology and the Logs of Borings
are presented in Appendix A Results of the laboratory tests performed on selected soil
samples obtained from the borings drilled are presented in Appendix B

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by drilling and sampling
13 borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 13 The borings drilled were advanced to
depths of about 10 8 to 41 5 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate
locations of the borings drilled for the project are shown on the Site Plans Plates 3 1
through 3 7
The majority of the borings drilled generally encountered surface fills consisting of
dense sandy gravel underlain by alluvium In general the surface fills were less than
about 2 feet in thickness The alluvium encountered generally consisted of medium stiff to
hard silty clays and clayey silts Coralline detritus consisting of dense to very dense sands
and gravel were encountered below the alluvium in Boring Nos 8 and 13 at depths of
about 24 feet and 8 feet below the ground surface respectively
Boring Nos 1 through 4 generally were drilled near the Honouliuli Stream Bridge
crossing These borings generally encountered fills consisting of medium dense sands
and stiff to hard silty clays extending from the ground surface down to about 10 to 18 feet
below the ground surface The fill materials generally were underlain by soft and or loose
alluvial and lagoonal deposits The alluvial and lagoonal deposits generally consisted of
soft sandy and clayey silts and very loose silty sands The soft and or loose soil
deposits generally extended down to about 28 feet below the ground surface in
Boring Nos 1 and 4

and preliminary design of the project only therefore this report is not intended for use in

of the proposed Fort Weaver Road Route 76 Widening Aawa Street to Geiger Road

recommendations for final design

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored preliminarily by drilling
and sampling 13 borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 13 The borings were
advanced to maximum depth explored of approximately 41 5 feet below the existing
ground surface The borings were drilled using a truck mounted drill rig equipped with
continuous flight auger tools The approximate locations of the borings drilled for the
project are shown on the Site Plans Plates 3 1 through 3 7
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representations of the materials
encountered are presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 13
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained from the borings drilled in
general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of
Soils by driving a 3 inch OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling
30 inches In addition some samples were obtained from the borings drilled in general
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel
Sampling of Soils by driving a 2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same
hammer and drop The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third
6 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of
Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Pocket penetrometer tests were performed on selected cohesive soil samples
retrieved in the field The pocket penetrometer test provides an indication of the
unconfined compressive strength of the soil sample Results of the pocket penetrometer
tests performed on selected samples are presented on the Logs of Borings at the
appropriate sample depths

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture Content ASTM D 2216 and Unit Weight ASTM D 2937 determinations
were performed on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of
soil properties The results of these tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the
appropriate sample depths
Two Atterberg Limits tests ASTM D 4318 were performed on selected samples
of the soils to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits and to aid in soil classification The
results of the tests are summarized on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample
depths Graphic presentation of the test results is provided on Plate B 1
One Sieve Analysis test ASTM C 117 C 136 was performed on a selected
sample of the granular soils to evaluate the gradation characteristics of the soils and to
aid in soil classification Graphic presentation of the grain size distribution is provided
on Plate B 2
One Direct Shear test ASTM D 3080 was performed on a selected sample of the
soils to evaluate the shear strength characteristics The results of the direct shear test are
presented on Plate B 3
One Consolidation test with time rates of consolidation ASTM D 2435 was
performed on a sample of the potentially compressible soils to evaluate the compressibility
characteristics of the materials encountered Results of the consolidation test are
presented on Plate B 4
Four laboratory Resistance R Value tests AASHTO T 190 were performed on
selected bulk samples of the near surface soils to evaluate the pavement support
characteristics of the soils The test results are presented on Plates B 5 through B 8
Four laboratory California Bearing Ratio tests ASTM D 1883 also were performed
on bulk samples of the near surface soils to evaluate the pavement support characteristics
of the soils The samples tested were remolded to near the optimum moisture content
Results of the tests are presented on Plates B 9 through B 12
Four Modified Proctor compaction tests ASTM D 698B were performed on bulk
samples of the near surface soils to evaluate the dry density and moisture content
relationships The results of the tests are presented on Plates B 13 through B 16
