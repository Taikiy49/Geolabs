PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Ewa Villages Area D Drainage Improvements project involved the
installation of about 600 feet of concrete box drain from the proposed Franciscan Vistas
development to the intersection of Kapolei Parkway and the future extension of Park
Row The project site is northwest of Puanaeole Street and the abandoned railroad
tracks and north of the future Kapolei Parkway The box drain is about 6 feet by 8 feet
in section and the invert is about 11 feet below the existing ground
EARTHWORK OPERATIONS
Paradigm Construction Company was the grading contractor The grading
consisted of excavation for the culvert installation the backfilling around the completed
culvert
At the commencement of the earthwork operations the site was prepared by
clearing and grubbing of the surface vegetation and other deleterious materials These
materials were removed from the site

Trench excavation was accomplished using a LC track
excavator The box culvert was constructed using the cast in place method
Compaction of the trench bottom and of the backfill on the sides of the box was
accomplished using a sheepsfoot boom attachment on the excavator Compaction of
the upper part of the backfill above the box was done using a 10 ton sheepsfoot roller
and the excavator boom attachment
During the site grading operations samples of the on site materials utilized as fill
and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing The
maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory
in general accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test results are
summarized on Plate 2
The fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to near the optimum
moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry
density as determined in accordance with ASTM D 1577
During the site grading and utility backfilling operations periodic field density tests
were performed in general accordance with ASTM D 6938 Nuclear Gauge Method
Results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 6 of
this report These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on
constructed fills and backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by
pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should
be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed
to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained
Our services consisted of part time field observation of the contractor s fill
placement subgrade preparation and compaction procedures and confirmation of
subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above grading operations
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the site grading and utility installation work for the
above referenced project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this
report were performed in conformance with the general intent of the recommendations

LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork may
have been performed without our observation However the level of earthwork
observation and testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some of
the geotechnical risks to the project It should be recognized that there are variations in the
accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the
earthwork construction and materials Our services were performed in accordance with
generally accepted standards of geotechnical engineering practice no warranty is
expressed or implied Provision of construction monitoring by an engineer is not insurance
nor does it guarantee construction of any type The contractor shall retain responsibility for
the quality of the work for adhering to the plans and specifications and for repairing
defects regardless of when they are found
