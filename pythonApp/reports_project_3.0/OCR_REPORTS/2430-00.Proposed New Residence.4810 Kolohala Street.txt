INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the proposed new residence at 4810 Kolohala
Street on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location of the site
and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The
exploration was performed in general accordance with our proposal dated
This report summarizes the findings from the exploration and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from the analyses based
on the data obtained during the exploration These recommendations are
intended for design input and are not intended for construction estimation
or bidding purposes

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is anticipated that the proposed new residence will be a one to
two story structure with concrete slab on grade and spread footing
foundations Minimal site grading is anticipated for the project
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface conditions at the project site in order to develop a
generalized soil rock data set for the fommlation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertinent to the design of the foundation
pavements and site grading of the proposed new residence project
The work was done in general accordance with our proposal to Tanabe
Corporation dated The scope of work consisted of the
following
1 Drilling and sampling three 3 borings to depths of 15 0 to 15 8
feet below the existing ground surface
2 Reconnaissance of the project site and vicinity to make visual
observations of the surficial soil conditions
3 Review of available soils and geologic information pertinent to
the project site and its vicinity
4 laboratory testing of selected samples obtained during the field
exploration to determine the engineering properties and
Classification of the materials encountered
5 Engineering analyses of the field and laboratory data for the
formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent
to the proposed development

6 Preparation of this report presenting the findings and design
recommendations
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration and laboratory testing
are presented in the appendices of this report
SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed project site is located on 4810 Kolohala Street in the
southeastern part of Oahu Hawaii The lot is Closely rectangular in
shape and about 11 574 square feet in area Presently the site is
occupied by an existing one story residential structure The terrain of
the site is generally level
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling three 3
shallow borings with small portable equipment to depths of 15 0 to 15 8
feet below the existing ground surface The approximate locations of the
borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general the subsurface conditions encountered in the borings
consisted of the following units
1 A 4 5 to 5 0 foot thick surface layer of medium dense tan fine
sand
2 A 2 5 to 3 0 foot thick layer of loose to medium dense tan
medium to coarse sand
3 A 5 0 to 7 0 foot thick layer of very loose to loose light gray
silty clayey sands with coral gravels and
4 Dense tan silty sand with coral gravel to about 15 8 feet below
the existing ground surface the maximum depth drilled

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration generally encountered a 4 5 to 8 0 foot thick
crust of medium dense sand deposits over a layer of loose sand It is our
opinion that the upper sand crust can provide adequate foundation support
for the proposed one to two story residence provided additional site
filling is kept to a minimum
Foundations
Spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the proposed
residence An allowable bearing value of 2000 p s f may be used for
footings bearing on the upper sand crust or compacted fill The bearing
value is for total dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third
when temporary wind and or seismic loads are considered Estimated
settlements are on the order of less than one half of an inch total and
one fourth of an inch differential
Individual footing and continuous wall footings should be embedded a
minim of eighteen 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade
The bottom of excavated footing locations should be tamped to a dense fim
consistency

W O 2430 00 Page 5
If the foundations are located next to sewer drainage easements they
should be embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V
imaginary plane extending upward from the bottom edge of the utility
trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility lines Loose utility line
backfills adjacent to the new footings should be properly removed and
recompacted to a minimm of 90 relative density per ASTM Test Method
D 1557 78
The bottom of all footing excavations should be observed by the
geotechnical engineer prior to placement of the reinforcement to
determine if adequate bearing has been obtained and to see that loose
soils are removed prior to concrete placement
Iateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive
earth pressure developed against the buried vertical faces of the
foundation system and by frictional resistance developed between the
bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be calculated using an
equivalent fluid pressure of 300 p c f This assumes that the soil
around the foundation is well compacted A coefficient of friction of 0 3
May be used Unless covered by pavements or slabs the passive resistance
in the upper 18 inches of soil should be neglected
Slab On Grade
Concrete slab on grade construction is anticipated for the floor A
4 inch layer of 3B Fine gravel should be provided below the slabs as
cushion fill The slab subgrade should be kept moist prior to concrete
placement The thickened edge of the slab should be embedded a minimum of
18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade
In order to protect against future moisture infiltration and
Subsequent damage to floor coverings an impervious moisture barrier is
recommended below the slabs provided that an inch of moist fine sand is
placed above the plastic membrane to help concrete curing

Site Grading
Minimal site grading is anticipated
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limits should be cleared of vegetation debris rubbish old footings and
slabs and other deleterious materials These materials should be removed
and disposed of properly off site
The areas should then be scarified to a depth of six 6 inches
moisture conditioned and recompacted to 90 of its maximum dry density per
ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 Any loose or soft spots encountered should be
over excavated and then replaced with compacted non expansive select
material
Based on the field exploration and laboratory test results most of
the on site soils may be used as fill material If required imported
fill material should consist of non expansive select granular materials
less than 3 inches in maximm size with less than 20 passing the 200
sieve All imported fill material should be tested and approved by the
geotechnical engineer prior to transportation to the site
The fill should be placed in maximum 8 inch thick loose level lifts
and compacted to a minimm of 90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM Test
Method D 1557 78
The site should be graded to drain water away from the foundations

Existing Cesspools
Existing cesspools may be located at the site If encountered the
cesspool locations should be flagged in the field To reduce future
settlement of structures where existing cesspools are located the sludge
should be removed and filled with compacted granular backfill such as
broken up coral or cinder The cesspool walls within 5 feet of the
finished grade should be removed to prevent hard spots and subsequent
differential settlement at the finished grade
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should he
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comments
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this review
is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for
misinterpretation of the recommendations

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the
foundation phases of the work This is to observe compliance with the
design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow design
changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from that
anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in
this report are contingent upon such observations
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
upon information obtained from the borings Variations of conditions
between the borings may occur and the nature and extent of these
variations may not become evident until construction If variations then
appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations
given in this report
The locations of the field borings were approximately determined by
taping from structures indicated on the topographic plan by Control Point
Surveying Engineering Inc dated Elevations of the
data points were approximately determined by interpolation from elevation
points on the topographic plan The physical location and elevation of
the borings should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by
the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
boring points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types and
as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross sections are based upon interpolation between borings
and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
