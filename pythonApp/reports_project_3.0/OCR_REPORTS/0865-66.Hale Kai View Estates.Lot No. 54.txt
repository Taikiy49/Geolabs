Soil Investigation
Proposed Residence Lot No 54
Hale Kai View Estates
Hahaione Valley Oahu Hawaii
As requested we have made a study of the near surface soil
conditions below the above referenced lot The purpose of the study was
to evaluate the suitability of the near surface soils as foundation
support for the proposed house The study consisted of drilling three
3 borings within the proposed building site performing laboratory soil
tests and reviewing the grading and compaction test record for the lot
This report summarizes our observations and recommendations

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed single family residence consists of a one story wood
structure approximately 40 by 55 feet in plan dimension Based on the
preliminary plans provided to us the garage will be set back about 19
feet from Kahena Street
Concrete on grade slab is planned for the garage and driveway while
post and beam construction is planned for the remainder of the house
A short retaining wall is planned along the rear and northwestern
sides of the lot to provide grade separation between the adjacent house
lots
About 2 feet of fill is planned under the house and garage slab
EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
Lot 54 is located on Kahena Street in the southeastern portion of
the subdivision development about 350 feet north of Ainapo Street It
is bounded by Kahena Street on the east and subdivision lots on the
north south and west An existing 10 foot wide sewer easement is

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that most of the lot is underlain
by more than 5 feet of grayish brown clay fill The laboratory swelling
test results on this material indicate that they have high expansion
potential Therefore for Lot 54 the slab on grade and foundation
construction would require special design procedures in order to reduce
the shrink swell effect of the subsoils
House Foundation
Slab on grade is planned for the garage The on site clayey soils
are expansive and unsuitable as slab subgrade soils In order to reduce
the expansive effects of the soils the garage area should be over
excavated and backfilled to provide a 3 foot minimum capping layer
consisting of off site non expansive borrow material over the existing
expansive soils Also prior to the placement of the upper capping fill
layer the underlying expansive clay subgrade should be scarified
moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture content then compacted to
85 density prior to filling The 3 foot capping layer should extend
horizontally for a minimum distance of 3 feet beyond the slabs refer to
Schematic Section Plate 2 for details With the abovementioned
W Page 5
procedures slab on grade construction with an 8 inch deep perimeter
thickened edge footing can be utilized
Slab on grade should be placed over a 4 inch layer of base course
For the post and beam portion of the house we recommend that the
expansive clays be overexcavated down for a depth of 3 feet below the
bottom of the individual post footings and then replaced with non
expansive borrow soils such as crusher waste select borrow or coral
compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM D
1557 78 Refer to Schematic Section Plate 3 for details The spread
footings can then be placed on the compacted non expansive soil
Alternatively the individual post footings can be extended a minimum
depth of 36 inches below the finished ground surface The footings
should be poured in place and backfilled to form an impervious layer over
the foundations
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f can be used for footings
bearing on the stiff existing ground or compacted select material The
bottom of the footing excavation should be neat and free of loose soils
or debris
The finish grade outside the slab and pavements should be shaped to
shed water away from the foundations and to avoid ponding condition near

Retaining Wall Foundation
Retaining walls up to 3 feet high may be needed along the perimeter
of the house and the garage area
In order to reduce the expansive effects of the existing clay soils
on the wall we recommend that the expansive clays be overexcavated down
for a depth of 24 inches below the bottom of the footing and then
replaced with non expansive borrow soils such as crusher waste or coral
compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557
78 The overexcavation and replacement should extend a minimum distance
of 12 inches in front of the footing and beyond the footing width
The wall footing should be embedded a minimum depth of 12 inches

Concrete Driveway and Sidewalks
A layer of expansive soils more than 5 feet thick was encountered
along the proposed driveway Since the driveway can tolerate some amount
of heaving the capping layer may be reduced to 2 feet minimum below the
bottom of the driveway slab After moisture conditioning of the exposed
adobe clay it should then be immediately backfilled with a select non
expansive borrow material which would act as a capping over the
underlying adobe clay
Where more than 2 feet of fill is planned select non expansive
borrow material should be used to cap over the expansive clay Where
less than 2 feet of new fill is planned overexcavation and replacement
of the adobe clay with select non expansive material is recommended to
provide a minimum 2 feet of capping The above capping requirements
should be followed for a minimum distance of 1 foot beyond the driveway
edge

Joints are recommended in the driveway slab at about 10 foot
intervals and at discontinuities in order to allow for some relative
movement of the concrete
For future sidewalks capping of the clay or overexcavation and
replacement similar to the driveway is recommended to reduce heaving
caused by the expansive clay soil Overexcavation beyond the edge of the
walkway may not be necessary
Site Grading
The existing terrain at the site generally slopes down at about 5
grade or flatter
Minor grading is anticipated for most of the area below the house
The area to be filled should be cleared of vegetation and debris
prior to filling
Fill and backfill should be placed in maximum 8 inch lifts and
compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density established by
ASTM D 1557 78
The finished grade outside the slabs pavements and foundations
should be shaped to avoid ponding conditions and to shed water away from
the structures

Design Review
Plans and specifications for foundations site grading and utility
construction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during
earthwork construction particularly the fill placement slope cuts and
to monitor the contractor s grading operations
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be examined by
a soils engineer during construction to see that adequate bearing has
been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the
findings of our investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above inspections
