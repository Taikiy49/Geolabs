This letter report presents the results of the floor-level survey performed for the 
7-Eleven Kailua project in the Kailua area on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. 
This report summarizes our findings and presents our geotechnical engineering 
recommendations resulting from the floor-level survey performed for the project. The 
findings and recommendations presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at the 
end of the report. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The project site is located at the corner of Kailua Road and Uluoa Street in Kailua 
on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. The 7-Eleven store is located at the north end of the 
existing one-story building. The project location and general vicinity are shown on the 
Project Location Map, Plate 1. 
The 7-Eleven store is part of a single-story structure supported on a shallow 
foundation with concrete slabs-on-grade on the southern and western ends of the 
building. Distresses on the floor tiles were observed within the store, as well as in the 
front and the trash bin area on the west side of the store. We understand that it is 
desired to perform a floor-level survey study of the 7-Eleven store, which includes the 
walkway in front of the store and the loading area to the west of the building. The 
building layout of the store is shown on the Site Plan, Plate 2.

FLOOR-LEVEL SURVEY 
A floor-level survey was performed on June 13, 2023 for the project.  The floor level 
survey was performed inside the store in open areas, along the concrete sidewalk in front 
of the store, and at the loading zone and trash bin area at the north end of the store. The 
floor-level survey equipment used consisted of a pressurized hydrostatic altimeter that 
utilizes a pressurized system that measures changes in elevation between a determined 
datum (zero location) and a point of interest.  The zero-datum location was selected at the 
corner of the landscape planter located on the west end of the project site adjacent to the 
loading zone. 
The floor level survey readings ranged from -31.3 to +6.5 inches relative to the zero-
datum location. The readings ranged from -0.8 to +6.5 inches inside the store with the 
lowest reading of -0.8 inches at the north corner of the store and the highest reading of +6.5 
inches within the freezer area at the southeast end of the store. Along the outside walkway, 
the survey readings ranged from +2.6 to 0 inches. The readings along the loading zone and 
the trash bin area ranged from -6.8 to -31.3 inches. The significant difference in elevation 
from the datum and the loading zone was partly due to the grade drop from the landscape 
planter to the loading zone. The results of the floor level survey are presented on the Floor 
Level Survey Plan, Plate 4.

FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS 
Based on the as-built drawings provided, the zero-datum location is about 
Elevation 114.25 feet Mean Sea Level (MSL).  In addition, the finished floor elevation of the 
store is 114.5 feet MSL. Based on the floor level readings, it appears that the freezer area 
has risen about 2 inches.  In addition, the north end of the store has settled about 3 
inches. 
At the trash and loading zone area, the concrete slab and AC pavement have 
settled from about 5 to 19 inches. Significant cracks were noted on the trash area concrete 
slab. Distress was not noted on the existing AC pavement. Therefore, we suspect that the 
loading zone has been repaved. 
Wet and soft ground conditions were observed behind the store building area. We 
suspect that moisture may have gotten below the store slab, which may have caused the 
heave observed in the freezer area. 
Based on the floor level survey, it appears that significant settlement has occurred 
on the northern end of the store and at the trash and loading zone area. The measured 
settlement ranged from about 6 to 19 inches.  
The results of the floor-level survey performed generally indicate that there are 
differential movements throughout the surveyed area. As shown on the Floor Level Survey 
Plan, areas along the eastern and western perimeter of the structure appear to be lower 
relative to the middle of the store. This dip in relative elevations is more apparent within the 
back-office area of the store located on the northern corner of the structure.  
Based on the above, we recommend that the subgrade condition be explored at the 
southern end of the store where heave was measured and at the northern end of the store 
where significant settlement was measured. In addition, the subgrade condition near the 
middle of the store should be explored to compare the subgrade conditions between the 
heaving and settling subgrades. 
Outside of the building, we recommend that borings be performed within the trash 
and loading zone area. The borings will be used to evaluate the high settlement in this 
area. Due to the limited area available behind the building, we recommend that Dynamic 
Cone Penetrometer (DCP) testing be performed to evaluate the consistency of the 
subgrade soils.
