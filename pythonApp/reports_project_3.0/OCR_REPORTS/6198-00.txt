This letter report presents the results of our percolation tests performed for the
Schnitzer Steel Hawaii Corporation in the Kapolei area on the Island of Hawaii The project
location and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
Subsurface Conditions
Two borings designated as Boring Nos 1 and 2 each extending to a depth of
about 5 feet below the existing ground surface were drilled at the site to perform
percolation tests The approximate boring locations are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Based on our field exploration the project site generally is underlain by surface fill
underlain by coral formation A thin layer of silty sand fills of less than about 1 foot in
thickness were encountered in our borings The surface fills were generally underlain by
medium hard to hard coral formation extending to the maximum depth explored of about
5 feet below the ground surface It should be noted that voids and cavities are commonly
encountered in coral formations near the project site Therefore presence of voids and
cavities should be anticipated at the project site
We did not encounter groundwater in our borings at the time of our field exploration
However groundwater levels likely will change due to tidal fluctuations seasonal
precipitation surface water runoff and other factors

Percolation Tests
Two percolation tests one constant head test and one falling head test were
conducted in borings drilled to provide data for design of the proposed storm water
disposal systems planned at the project site In general the drilled borings had a diameter
of about 3 5 inches and extended to depths of about 5 feet below the ground level at the
time of the testing
A water pump on our drill rig was used to pump clear water into the borehole for the
constant head test method The flow rate was recorded by timing and calculating the
volume as a relatively constant flow rate was achieved
The falling head method was tested by filling the borehole with water and allowing
the water to percolate into the ground The water levels and corresponding times were
recorded for the calculation of subsurface permeability The head levels within the test
boring were monitored by an electric water level sounder during the test The percolation
test data is provided on Plate 3 The following table summarizes the percolation test
results
