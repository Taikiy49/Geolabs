Geolabs Inc performed probing and grouting services for the above project on
August 6 8 and 9 2005 The work was performed in general accordance with our fee
proposal dated August 4 2005 The results of the probing and grouting work are
presented in this letter report
Project Considerations
The project involves the construction of a new warehouse in the vicinity of the
intersection of Kalihi and Silva Streets in Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii It was
proposed to probe and grout the underlying ground surface along wall columns and or
center of columns to depths of 10 feet below the bottom of footing elevation 10 feet
below bottom of footing plus 2 feet assumed maximum thickness of footing
Drilling of Probeholes
A total of 68 probeholes were drilled below the proposed bottom of column and wall
footings for the proposed new AJ W Office and Warehouse Building in the vicinity of the
intersection of Kalihi and Silva Streets in Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The
probeholes were drilled at the center of the column footings and along the wall footings
A probehole was drilled at each column footing and between each column footing along
the wall footings Spacing of the probeholes along the wall footings varied from 8 feet to
9 feet and 3 inches The column and wall footing locations were staked on site by Quality
Design Build The probehole locations were staked on site by Geolabs Inc The location
of the probeholes are presented on the Probe Hole Location plan Plate 1

At the time of our probing work the wall footing at the north and east ends of the
building have been excavated by Quality Design Build to a depth of approximately
1 to 2 feet below the existing ground surface which is approximately the bottom of the
planned footing Where footings have been excavated to the desired depth or close to
the desired depth the probeholes were drilled to 10 feet below the proposed bottom of
the footing The remaining probeholes on the western and southern sides of the building
were drilled to total depths of approximately 12 feet below the existing ground surface
10 feet below the bottom of footing plus 2 feet assumed maximum thickness of footing
Probing
Probing was performed by Geolabs Inc on August using a
probeholes were advanced from the bottom of the footing or top of the existing ground to
depths of 10 feet below the footing elevation Immediately after probing 2 inch diameter
PVC pipe casings were inserted into the probehole to protect the annulus of the probehole
wall prior to grouting work Materials encountered were silty coralline sand and gravel with
occasional silt pockets
A representative from Geolabs Inc was present during the probing operation to
observe the rate of penetration of the augers to monitor the consistency of the underlying
ground and observe the possible presence of cavities No significant voids or cavities
were observed during the probing work
Grouting
Immediately after probing the probeholes were filled with sand cement grout on
August 8 and 9 2005 Grouting of the probeholes was performed using a grout pump
from American Standard Concrete Pumping Sand cement grout from Hawaiian Cement
applied at low pressure was used to fill up the probeholes All the probeholes were filled
with the theoretical quantities of grout Grout quantities were estimated based on the
number of strokes to fill each probehole The volume of grout per stroke was compared to
the number of strokes to fill up a 5 gallon bucket A total of 3 821 cubic yards of flowable
sand cement grout was used to backfill the probeholes No significant excess volume of
concrete was observed to fill any probehole and no significant voids or cavities were
observed during the grouting work
A representative from Geolabs Inc was present during the grouting operation to
observe the rate of penetration of the augers to monitor the consistency of the underlying
ground and the possible presence of cavities The volume of grout used to fill up each
borehole was also observed and recorded The results are presented on the attached
