In accordance with your request we have performed a
geotechnical investigation for the above subject project The
approximate location of the proposed site and its vicinity is
shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 Our work was
performed in general accordance with our proposal of May 21
1985
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
Project Consideration
lt is proposed to construct a new asphalt plant facility at
the site The new construction evaluated in our investigation
are as follows
1 an aggregate storage area with an underground tunnel for
an aggregate conveyor belt
I D 1634 Page 2
2 an asphalt storage tank area with 8 tanks each with weight
of 125 tons and
3 surge tanks area with anticipated loads of 150 tons per
tank
Purpose and Scope
The purpose of this investigation was to explore the soil
conditions at the site to obtain pertinent soil data for the
design of the structure foundations
The main objectives of the investigation was
1 to determine the settlement properties of the soft harbor
clays to evaluate the potential settlement of the
structures and
Z to determine if suitable bearing layers are present at
deeper depths for pile foundation design should the
anticipated ground and foundation settlement exceed
tolerable limits
The scope of investigation included drilling and sampling
three 5 to 130 feet below the existing ground
surface Also included were laboratory testing of the samples
obtained engineering evaluation of the field and laboratory
data development of design recommendations and report
preparation
Detailed description of our field exploration and
laboratory test results are presented in the appendices of this
report

existing ready mix batch plant and its conveyor system

Discussion and Recommendations
General
The proposed site is underlain by a layer of highly
compressible lagoonal harbor deposit varying in thickness from
approximately 30 to 60 feet This layer consists primarily of
loose coral sand or gravel in a clay matrix The soil below
changes to a thick layer of silty clay which is also moderately
compressible
Due to the soft ground conditions and the proximity of the
water table to the ground surface significant settlements are
anticipated due to the weight of the structures together with
their live loads
To induce ground settlement prior to foundation
construction we recommend that a surcharge fill be placed as
soon as possible
If sufficient surcharge fill can be placed over the site
it is our opinion that a large percentage of the anticipated
ground settlements primary settlements due to the weight of
the structures together with their live and dead loads could be
eliminated However some amount of secondary settlements may
still occur over the life of the structures
Ideally construction of the structures should be delayed
until most of the settlement have occurred Then mat
foundations may be used for the support of the structures with
allowance for some future settlements due to the structural
live and dead loads
GEOLABS HAWAII
I D 1634 Page 5
Alternatively if a surcharge program is not feasible a
friction pile foundation may be used The borings disclosed
that a suitable end bearing layer is not present for at least
130 feet below the site Therefore a friction pile system
would be required to support the entire loaded area to minimize
downdrag on the pile
It appears that 80 to 100 feet long 12 inch square piles
lay develop 40 tons of allowable capacity
Subsequent to the new construction additional fills or
surcharge loads should not be placed near the new structures as
this would cause additional settlements to mat and friction
pile foundations
Our recommendations are discussed in detail for each of
the three loaded areas and presented in the following sections
Aggregate Storage and Underground Conveyor
Significant settlement on the order of one to two feet
are anticipated due to the weight of the aggregate storage
This settlement would be detrimental to the underground tunnel
and tine conveyor system inside Pile foundations could be
utilized however they would be very costly Because of the
great depth to a suitable end bearing layer low capacity
friction pile system would be required Additionally the
piles would be required to support the entire aggregate storage
area to minimize ground settlement and the subsequent downdrag
loads on the pile
For the above reasons we believe a surcharge program
should be considered to initiate the ground settlements prior
to the construction of the new structure
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1550 00
I D 1634 Page 6
The total weight of surcharge should be about 15 feet over
the proposed storage area It should be placed in two
increments of about 7 5 feet with a one week rest period
between intervals to allow the soft ground to gain strength to
reduce the possibility of bearing or slope failure
The length of the surcharge period will depend on the
amount of surcharge used If this 15 foot high surcharge is
used our estimated surcharge time should be on the order of 1
to 2 months
Settlement gauges should be installed in the area and
periodic survey readings of the gauges should be used to
monitor the actual settlement The readings should be taken by
the project surveyor and transmitted to the soils engineer to
determine the actual surcharge time required
It should be understood that although a surcharge fill
might help to minimize primary settlement due to the aggregate
storage long term secondary settlements on the order of 1 to 2
inches may still occur
After completion of the surcharge program construction of
the underground tunnel containing the conveyor system may be
started
The tunnel would be constructed with a portion of its
depth under tne water table and possibly into the soft soil
layer We believe that a suitable bedding system should be
used underneath the tunnel to provide a working platform and
uniform support The bedding system should consist of a 2 foot
layer of gravel 3B Fine underlain by a filter fabric such as
MIRAFI 140 or TYPAR

The lateral earth pressure acting on the underground
Structures will depend on the type of backfill used the extent
of the backfill material and the compactive effort required on
the material behind the structure walls Granular material
such as the select on site sand is recommended for the
backfill adjacent to the structures in order to reduce the
compactive effort required and to minimize the build up of
additional lateral earth pressures
The tunnel should be designed to resist lateral earth
pressure plus applicable surcharge load from the aggregate
storage
We believe that a lateral earth pressure of 55 p s f per
linear foot of depth may be used for the design of the portion
of the tunnel above water and a lateral earth pressure of 25
p s per linear foot in excess of hydrostatic pressure may be
used for that below water The aggregate storage surcharge
load may be taken as a 520 p s f uniform pressure acting on
the full depth of the tunnel if an at rest earth pressure
condition is assumed
Asphalt Storage Tanks
With similar soft ground conditions significant
settlements are anticipated due to the weight of the tanks
We recommend that friction pile foundations be used to support
these structures
A mat foundation in conjunction with a surcharge program
was considered However the height of surcharge required
would be as high as 20 feet or more and the possibility of
bearing failure causing titling of the mat would be high For
this reason we believe that friction pile foundations should
be used instead
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1550 00
I D 1634 Page 8
The piles should be used to support the tanks in
conjunction with a structural slab to eliminate the surcharge
fill required and potential downdrag on the piles
The piles would develop their load capacity primarily from
frictional resistance or adhesion between the piles and the
medium stiff to stiff alluvial soil encountered below about
65 foot depth
Twelve inch square prestressed concrete piles are
recommended The piles should extend into the medium stiff
soil encountered at about 65 feet below the existing ground
surface and be driven to a total depth of 100 feet
The piles should be driven with a hammer delivering a
minimum of about 24 000 foot pounds of energy and to a
resistance of 3 blows per inch for the last 6 inches
For the above recommendations an allowable pile capacity
of 40 tons per pile may be used Pile settlements on the order
of less than one half of an inch are anticipated
The pile driving should be monitored by a geotechnical
engineer to see that they are driven into competent bearing
formation
To resist tne lateral loads the passive earth pressure
around the pile caps and tie beams may be used We recommend
an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 pounds per square foot of
depth The backfill against the pile caps or tie beams should
be properly backfilled with select materials compacted to a dry
density of at least 95 of the maximum density as determined by
ASTM D 1557 78 test method

It should be noted that the friction piles could lose some
of their load carrying capacity if the adjacent areas are
filled and thereby causing ground settlements and downdrag
loads on the piles
Surge Tank Structures
Significant settlements are anticipated under the load of
the surge tank structures These settlements could be
minimized if a surcharge program is completed prior to the
construction of the structure or if friction pile foundations
are used
We believe that a mat foundation in conjunction with a
10 foot surcharge fill may be used The fill should be placed
in two increments of about 5 feet with a one week rest period
between intervals and the estimated surcharge time would be on
the order of 1 to 2 months
It should be understood that all the other relevant
guidelines and precautions for using a surcharge program as
discussed in the section Aggregate Storage and Underground
Conveyor should be observed here
The mat foundation should be placed at the existing ground
level and a maximum net allowable soil bearing pressure of 1000
p s may be used for the design
Alternatively a friction pile foundation may be used
Twelve inch square prestressed concrete piles should be
used to support the structures The friction piles should
extend into the soft to medium stiff soil encountered at about
30 feet below the existing ground surface and be driven to a

total depth of 80 feet A shorter pile length may be used in
this area since Boring 3 indicated that the soft lagoonal
deposit is thinner at this location An allowable pile
capacity of 40 tons per pile may be used Pile settlements on
the order of less than one half of an inch are anticipated
It should be understood that all the other relevant
guidelines and precautions for using friction pile foundation
as discussed in the section Asphalt Storage Tanks should be

Design Review
Plans and specifications for surcharge mat foundations
and piles should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review
and written comments prior to construction to see that the
intent of our recommendations is included
The earthwork operation including excavation fill
placement and compaction and surcharge should be observed by
the soils engineer during construction to see that adequate
compaction has been obtained and that the subsurface conditions
are consistent with the findings of our investigation
We also recommend that the mat foundation excavations be
observed by the soils engineer prior to placement of concrete
or reinforcing steel to check that adequate bearing has been
obtained
Any imported material required should be non expansive
tested and approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to
the site

different from those presented in this report then appropriate
modifications to the design should be made
Investigation Limitations
Our services consist of professional opinions and
recommendations made in accordance with generally accepted soil
and foundation engineering principles and practices This
warranty is in lieu of all other warranties either expressed or
implied
The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report
are based on our site reconnaissance soil information from
boring and laboratory test data obtained from this soil
investigation
Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encountered and
cannot be fully determined by taking soil samples Unforeseen
conditions such as pockets of organic soils trash fill or
expansive soils may occur in localized areas and may require
additional probing or corrections in the field Therefore
additional expenditures may be needed during construction to
attain a properly constructed project Some contingency fund
is thus recommended to accommodate these possible costs
This report has been prepared in order to assist the
engineer in the preparation of site grading and foundation
plans of the proposed construction
