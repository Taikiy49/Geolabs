Dear Mr Tippets
This report summarizes our earthwork observation and testing services performed
during the construction of the Three Story Married Student Housing at Brigham Young
University Hawaii Campus on the Island of Oahu
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is within the existing Hawaii Campus of Brigham Young University
in the Laie area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project consisted of constructing a
new three story building About 4 to 5 feet of new fills were placed for the construction
of the project The general vicinity of the project site is shown on the Project Location
Map Plate 1
EARTHWORK OPERATION
Jayar Construction Co Inc performed the earthwork for the project Jacobsen
Construction was the general contractor for the project Our observation and testing
services during the construction of the project occurred between June 2012 to June 2013

After clearing and grubbing areas to be filled and subgrades in cuts were generally
scarified to a depth of about 8 inches moisture conditioned and compacted to a minimum
of 90 percent of the maximum dry density determined in accordance with ASTM D1557
Modified Proctor The compaction requirement for the finished subgrades in areas
subjected to vehicular traffic was increased to a minimum of 95 percent relative
compaction
Fill and backfill materials generally consisted of excavated materials from the
project site and some imported materials In general fill materials consisted of materials
less than 3 inches in maximum particle size Fills and backfills required to raise the grades
were generally placed in level lifts of about 8 inches in loose thickness
moisture conditioned and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
The compaction requirement for the fill materials required under the pavement areas was
increased to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
During the earthwork operations samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D1557 Modified Proctor and the test
results are summarized on the attached Plate 2
Field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations on an
as need basis to confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures
and soil conditions were noted A total of 66 field density tests were performed using the
nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM D6938 Compaction test results are
summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 5 These tests were performed on the prepared
ground surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills Test locations and elevations
were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes and topographic
features These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the
degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were
reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the earthwork operations during construction of
the Three Story Married Student Housing project observed and tested by Geolabs in the
period covered by this report were performed in general accordance with the intent of
the project plans and specifications and our geotechnical engineering recommendations
presented in the referenced report dated and subsequent
recommendations
LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork may
have been performed without our observation However the level of earthwork
observation and testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some of
