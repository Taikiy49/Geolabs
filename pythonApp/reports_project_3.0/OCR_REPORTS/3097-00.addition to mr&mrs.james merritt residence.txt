INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed addition to Mr and Residence in the
Diamond Head area of Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location of
the project site and its vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings resulting from our field exploration and
laboratory testing and it presents our geotechnical engineering recommendations derived
from our analyses based on the data obtained from the field exploration and laboratory
testing
These recommendations are intended for design input only and may not be
appropriate to form the basis for construction cost estimates or for construction bidding
purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The existing Merritt residence is located at 3653 Diamond Head Circle
20 11 on the western slope of Diamond Head in the district of Honolulu on the island of
Oahu We understand that an addition with plan dimensions of about 150 by 30 feet is
planned on the north and west sides of the existing residence as shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2
On the basis of information provided to our office the planned addition will be two
2 stories in height and will be located on a relatively flat area adjacent to the existing
structure We understand that a basement about ten feet 10 deep is also planned at
the northwest corner of the proposed building addition The proposed addition will have
a finish ground floor elevation of about 40 feet Mean Sea Level MSL

We anticipate that site grading will consist of minor cuts and fills on the order of
one foot 1 except for the excavation for the proposed basement construction

The purpose of our geotechnical exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface soil conditions at the project site and to develop an idealized subsurface data
set for the formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to
foundation design and site grading for the proposed addition
Our work was done in general accordance with our proposal dated May 24 1993
to Our scope of work consisted of the following
1 Reconnaissance of the project site and its vicinity to visually observe the
surface conditions
2 Mobilization and demobilization of truck mounted drilling equipment and
operators to and from the project site
3 Drilling and sampling three 3 borings to depths ranging from 16 5 to 20 6
feet below the existing ground surface
4 Coordination of the field exploration and logging of the borings by a
geologist of our firm
5 Laboratory testing of selected soil samples obtained during the field
exploration to evaluate the classification and engineering properties of the
materials encountered

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located at 3653 Diamond Head Circle on the western slope of
Diamond Head in the district of Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii The approximate
0 4 acre lot is currently occupied by the existing Merritt residence which consists of a
one story house located at the east side of the lot and a one story masonry concrete
structure at the south side A swimming pool is situated at the center of the lot as shown
on the Site Plan Plate 2
The existing ground surface of the lot is relatively flat and slopes downward gently
in a northwest direction with ground elevations varying from about 45 8 feet MSL at the
upper southeast corner to about 37 1 feet MSL at the lower northwest corner of the lot
Loose fill materials consisting of brown clayey silt with gravel were noted on the
ground surface near the northwest corner Several trees were also observed on the lot

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our field exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling three 3 borings
to depths ranging from 16 5 to 20 6 feet below the existing ground surface The
approximate locations of the three 3 borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on our field exploration and laboratory test results the near surface
materials encountered are of stiff to hard consistency which are generally suitable to
provide foundation support for the proposed residential addition It is our opinion that the
proposed structure may be supported on conventional shallow footing foundation system
bearing on the in situ stiff clayey silt soils
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3097 00 Page 6
Laboratory tests performed on the on site clayey soils indicate that the materials
generally have a moderate shrink swell potential which will exhibit heaving when
subjected to moisture infiltration from a low moisture content condition Therefore we
recommend that concrete slabs on grade be supported on a gravel cushion fill layer
overlying a one foot 1 minimum of non expansive granular select borrow capping fill to
reduce the swelling potential effects of the in situ moderately expansive near surface
clayey subgrade soils
Detailed recommendations addressing the above geotechnical considerations and
other geotechnical aspects of the proposed construction are discussed in the following
sections of this report
Site Grading
The proposed finish floor elevations for the new addition vary between 38 4 to
40 9 feet MSL Based on these finish floor elevations minor cuts and fills of up to one
foot 1 will be required to provide a level building pad for the proposed addition except
for the proposed basement construction excavation which is estimated to be about ten
feet 10 deep The following recommendations are presented to provide general
guidelines for the above site grading work
1 The area within the contract grading limits should be thoroughly cleared and
grubbed All vegetation and other unsuitable materials should be removed
and disposed of properly off site
2 Any loose or soft spots encountered during site grading should be over
excavated to expose firm ground and replaced with compacted non
expansive select material The material resulting from overexcavation
should be removed and properly disposed of off site
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3097 00 Page 7
3 Areas that are to receive new fills or select borrow capping fill should be
scarified to a depth of eight inches 8 moisture conditioned to about two
percent above optimum moisture content and compacted to about 90
percent relative compaction density Relative compaction density refers to
the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum
dry density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557
test procedures
4 All fill and backfill material should be tested for suitability for its intended use
by Geolabs Hawaii prior to its delivery to the site Only select borrow fill
should be used within the upper 12 inches below the finished subgrade in
building and slab areas The non expansive select fill material snould be
well graded from coarse to fine with no particles larger than three inches
3 in largest dimension The material should contain less than 20 percent
passing the No 200 sieve
5 All fill and backfill should be placed in loose lift not exceeding twelve inches
in thickness and compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction
6 Measures should be incorporated into the grading of the project site to reduce
the potential for erosion and to prevent concentration of ponding water around
slab structures The ground surface around structures should be sloped
away from structures
Foundations
Further details regarding the structure design were not available at the time this report
was prepared However in consideration of the generally stiff to hard subsoil conditions
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3097 00 Page 8
foundations bearing in situ soils or engineered fills may be used to support the proposed
structures
An allowable bearing capacity of up to 2 500 p s f may be used for the design of
residential footing foundation bearing on the in situ materials encountered near the
surface The bearing pressure provided is for dead plus live loads only and may be
increased by up to one third s for transient loads such as wind or seismic forces
The bottom of the footing excavations should be firm and unyielding If soft and or
loose soils are encountered at the bottom of the footing excavation they should be over
excavated to expose competent in situ material and replaced with select granular material
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction at moisture content between
two 2 percent and five 5 percent above the optinum moisture content Compaction
of the bottom of the footing excavation as recommended above may be necessary if the
bearing materials have been disturbed during the excavation
The footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest
adjacent finish grade
If foundations are located next to utility trenches or easements they should be
embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane extending
upward from the bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility
lines
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by a combination of passive
earth pressure exerted against near vertical foundation faces and soil friction between the
base of foundations and the supporting subgrade Passive earth pressure equal to an
equivalent fluid weighing 300 pounds per cubic foot p c f and a base friction factor of

resistance in the upper 12 inches should be neglected
If foundations are designed in accordance with our recommendations we estimate
total settlements to be less than one inch with differential settlements of less than 4 inch
Footing excavations should be observed by the project geotechnical engineer prior
to placement of reinforcing steel or concrete to allow verification of the bearing material
and the embedment depths
Slab On Grade
Concrete slab on grade construction is anticipated for the ground floor of the
proposed residence addition As previously indicated the on site materials exhibit
moderate expansion potential when subjected to changes in the moisture content To
reduce the potential for slab distress due to the moderately expansive materials we
recommend that the concrete slab be supported on 12 inches of non expansive select
borrow fill material The non expansive select borrow fill should extend at least two feet
2 beyond the perimeter of the proposed structures
The subgrade soils should be prepared in accordance with the recommendations
presented in the Site Grading section of this report The select borrow fill material
should be placed as soon as possible after the scarification and recompaction of the
subgrade soils The select borrow fill would serve as a layer of moist soil above the
underlying potentially expansive subgrade soils and would reduce the potential loss of
moisture from the subgrade soils The reduction in drying should reduce the potential
swelling shrinkage of the underlying moderately expansive soils
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3097 00 Page 10
The prepared capping fill should be wetted and kept moist prior to final placement
of cushion fill and slab concrete A minimum six inch 6 thick layer of No 3B Fine gravel
ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation should be placed over the prepared capping layer to
serve as a slab cushion and capillary break A moisture barrier overlying the cushion fill
should be incorporated below the concrete slab to reduce the potential of damage to
moisture sensitive floor coverings by infiltration of moisture It is advisable to place one
inch 1 of moist fine sand over the moisture barrier membrane to protect the membrane
and to aid in curing of concrete slab
Conventional slab on grade construction may be utilized for the proposed structure
constructed on the 12 inch non expansive capping fill However it is recommended that
flexible floor coverings such as carpet or sheet vinyl be utilized to mask any minor slab
cracking It is also recommended that interior walls be designed to incorporate some
flexibility to accommodate possible movements
Basement Construction
Based on the information provided to our office we anticipate excavations on the
order of about ten feet 10 below the existing ground surface will be required for the
basement construction
According to the materials generally encountered in our borings the excavation for
the basement should present no difficulty by utilizing conventional earthmoving
equipment The method and equipment to be used for excavation should be left to the
choice of the contractor within practical limits and safety considerations
Based on our field exploration it is our opinion that sloping back the sides of the
excavation may be required to provide stability during the project construction However
the space on the north side may be limited to excavate the basement to sufficiently flat
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3097 00 Page 11
and stable slopes on the north side of the basement The contractor may elect to utilize
vertical or near vertical excavations Shoring may be required for vertical or near vertical
excavations to ensure the stability of the walls of these excavations
The adequacy and safety of the shoring system if used must comply with all
applicable regulations and or safety requirements The design installation performance
and removal of the shoring system should be made the sole responsibility of the
contractor His qualified representative who should be required to be present on the site
during excavation and construction work will have the best opportunity to promptly
observe any unanticipated or changing conditions during construction such as
unforeseen subsoils unexpectedly high groundwater table inappropriate construction
sequences or techniques etc which may affect the stability of the excavation
It must also be noted that some movement of the adjacent ground may still occur
due to the changes in earth stresses during excavation The magnitude of movement is
difficult to accurately predict due to the complexity of the stress changes The magnitude
depends greatly upon workmanship and also on the subsoil conditions the size of the
excavation and the rate of excavation
Therefore it is important to realize that the excavation should be properly
performed and that the adjacent ground should be continuously monitored for cracks
dips and or other indications of movements Such monitoring is particularly critical in
areas adjacent to existing facilities and utilities
We recommend that the lateral active earth pressure assuming clayey silt material
equal to an equivalent fluid weighing 35 pounds per cubic foot p c f be used for the
basement excavation design

Utility Trench
We envision that new utility lines may be required for the project A granular
bedding consisting of six inches 6 of select borrow or ASTM C 33 NO 67 gradation
is recommended under the pipes Self compacting granular materials such as No 3B
Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation should be used for the trench backfill up to
about one foot 1 above the pipes to provide adequate support around the pipes and
to reduce compaction of the backfill thus reducing the potential for damaging the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level one foot 1 above the pipes
to the top of the subgrade or finished grade should consist of select granular material
The backfill should be placed in maximum ten inch 10 level loose lifts and mechanically
compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for
future ground subsidence

the approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual
transition
