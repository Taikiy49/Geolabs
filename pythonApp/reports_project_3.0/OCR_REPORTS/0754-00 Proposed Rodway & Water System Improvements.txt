INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this investigation was to evaluate the sub
surface conditions at the site to provide soil data for the
design and construction of the proposed reservoirs and access
roadway
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations

W P 2
SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed site is located in the upland area of Molokai
Access to the site is from Kalae Highway
The topography at the site is moderately rolling with drain
ageways noted throughout the area Water was not noted in
the drainageways during the field exploration The site
is presently used for cattle grazing
The upper reservoir site is located next to Kalae Highway
which is fairly flat with gradients less than 10 percent
down toward the south An existing reservoir is located
at the site The area is covered with grass and some large
Banyan and Eucalyptus trees The elevation generally varies
from about 1410 to 1420 feet
An existing dirt road provides access between the existing
upper and lower reservoirs The new road alignment generally
follows the existing dirt road The existing dirt road is
less than 10 feet wide and appears to have been constructed
by cutting into the gentle sidehill slopes Weathered and
highly decomposed rock was noted at various places in the
road surface and slope cuts Vegetation along the existing
road consisted mostly of low grass The elevation along
the road generally varies from about 1410 to 1010 feet
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Pies
The proposed lower reservoir site is located on the northern
side of the existing dirt road The proposed site is ona
sidehill that generally slopes down toward the existing road
at about 25 to 30 percent gradients Vegetation in the area
consisted of low grass and shrubs Two existing reservoirs
and a pump house were located about 100 feet west of the
proposed site The elevation at the site generally varies
from about 1015 to 1050 feet
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The construction of 2 reservoirs and a connecting access
road is planned for the site
The upper reservoir will be a 1 million gallon reinforced
concrete tank structure The structure will be about 20
feet high and about 70 feet in diameter The anticipated
column load is 76 kips and perimeter wall load is about
8 8 kips per foot The existing 2 million gallon reservoir
will be demolished and the new reservoir will be placed
mostly over the old reservoir site A roadway consisting
of coral base with oi will be constructed around the new
reservoir
The new lower reservoir will be a 0 1 million gallon rein
forced concrete tank structure Cuts up to about 30 feet
high with an intermediate bench will be required to create
a level pad for the structure

The structure will be about 16 feet high and 38 feet in
diameter A roadway consisting of coral base with oil
will be constructed around the new reservoir
The new road alignment will generally follow the existing
dirt road and be about 3600 feet long The road will have
a 12 foot pavement width Cuts generally less than 7 feet
and fills less than 2 feet are anticipated

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on the field exploration and laboratory test results
it is our opinion that the proposed reservoir and roadway
sites are suitable for the planned construction
Foundation
Upper Reservoir
Three borings were drilled at the initially proposed
site for the new 1 milion gallon reservoir

P36
After the field exploration was completed it was
decided to place the new reservoir over the existing
reservoir site about 200 feet north of the initially
proposed site
Field observations at the site indicate that the
existing reservoir site is probably underlain by
subsoils similar to that encountered at the initially
proposed site Also since the existing reservoir
is a 2 million gallon capacity structure as compared
to 1 million gallon capacity for the proposed struc
ture the increase in load on the subsoils due to the
new structure should be small Therefore additional
explorations may not be required at this time However
field observation should be made by a soils engineer
during construction of the reservoir foundations to
see that the soil conditions are as anticipated
The latest reservoir location indicates that most of the
tank will be located over the existing reservoir site
however a portion of the tank will be located outside
of the existing tank area If practical the new tank
should be located entirely over the existing tank site
to reduce possible differential settlements due to
different load histories of the subsoils

presented below
i
The footings should bear a minimum of 12 inches
below the adjacent finish grade The ground
surface around the footings should not be
excavated or allowed to erode
The bottom of all footing excavations should be

established by ASTM Test Method D 1557
If a footing is located next to a utility
trench it should extend down to the bottom
Of the utility trench
Slab on grade should bear entirely on the
stiff natural soil or entirely on compacted
structural fill
Surface water should be diverted away from the
reservoir foundations

Site Grading
The site should be cleared of all surface vegetation old
foundations and slabs and loose topsoil
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Be
Select on site material can be used for fill
The fill should be placed in horizontal loose lifts
of 10 inches and compacted to 90 of its maximum dry
density established by ASTM Test Method D 1557
Fills placed on slopes having grades steeper than
5 horizontal to 1 vertical should be keyed and benched
into the existing firm ground
Cut and fill slopes of 2 horizontal to 1 vertical are
recommended
Sliver fills should be avoided if practicable
Surface runoff should be diverted away from the tops
of slopes and slope planting should be utilized to
reduce slope erosion
Where the road subgrade will be partly on cut and partly
on fill the cut section should be overexcavated 1 foot
and recompacted to reduce differential movement between
the cut and fill sections
Roadway Pavements
Based on the laboratory CBR tests on the near surface
soils at the site and light traffic conditions the
following preliminary pavement design is recommended
for the access roadways

above design sections can be revised based on the additional
tests
The roadway subgrades should be compacted and shaped to drain

FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored
by drilling 5 borings at the site utilizing trailer mounted
and portable drilling equipment and by digging 8 test pits
with a backhoe The borings were drilled to depths of 14 5
to 26 5 feet The test pits were dug to depths of 3 to
9 5 feet The approximate locations of the borings and
test pits are shown on the enclosed Site Plan Plate 1
The soils were classified by visual and textural
examinations in the field by our engineer who continuously
monitored the drilling and test pit operations The classi
fications were reviewed by visual inspection and testing in
the laboratory All soils were classified in accordance
with the Unified Soil Classification System A graphic
presentation of the soils encountered in the borings is
presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 5
The test pit logs are presented on Plates A 6 and A 7
Soil samples were obtained by driving either a 2 4
inch I D ring samplers or a standard penetration sampler
with a 140 pound hammer free falling a distance of 30 inches
The blow counts to drive the sampler the last 12 inches are
shown on the logs of borings at the appropriate sample depths

LABORATORY TESTING
Moisture content Atterberg Limits and unit weight determinations

evaluation of soil properties The results of these tests are presented

sented on Plate B 3

surcharge load were conducted to get an indication of the swelling
potential of the near surface soils The results are presented on
Plate B 3

were performed on near surface samples to evaluate its suitability
as fill material The results are shown on Plates B 4 through B 6
