In accordance with your request, we conducted a geotechnical engineering 
evaluation in support of the Genset Trailer Seismic Anchors project at Waikoloa Well Site

report. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The project is located at Waikoloa Well Nos. 2, 6 and 7 in the Waikoloa area in the 
District of South Kohala on the Island of Hawaii. The project location and general vicinity 
are shown on the Project Location Map, Plate 1.  
Seismic anchors will be required for the existing emergency generator trailers at the 
three well sites.  Locations of the existing generator trailers at each well site are shown on 
the Site Plans, Plates 2.1 through 2.3. Based on the structural load demand, drilled shafts 
will be utilized to resist the uplift and lateral loads due to a potential seismic event. 
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS 
Subsurface conditions were evaluated based on past field explorations conducted 
in the vicinity of the emergency generator trailers. Based on the borings data, the well 
sites are generally underlain by volcanic ash with some clinker materials over basalt 
formation.  The thickness of the volcanic ash varied significantly across the three well 
sites.  At Well No. 7, the volcanic ash layer was generally less than 5 feet in thickness.  
At Well No. 2, volcanic ash extended down to about 10 feet below the ground surface.  
The thickness of the volcanic ash was over 19 feet at Well No. 6.  Volcanic ash materials

site corresponding to a Site Class C soil profile type based on Table 20.3-1 of ASCE 7-16.

DRILLED SHAFTS 
The existing emergency generator trailers at the three well sites will be restrained 
with a tie-down anchor system to resist potential seismic loading.  We recommend that 
cast-in-place concrete drilled shaft system be used for the support of the generator trailer 
seismic anchor system. 
In general, drilled shaft foundations are constructed by drilling a hole down into the 
bearing strata, placing reinforcing steel, and then pumping high slump concrete to fill up the 
hole. The result is a cast-in-place concrete drilled shaft for foundation support. Based on the 
subsurface conditions anticipated at the project site, we envision the drilled shaft foundations 
would derive vertical support primarily from skin friction between the drilled shaft and the 
surrounding materials. In general, the end-bearing component of the drilled shafts has been 
discounted in our analysis due to difficulties associated with obtaining a clean bottom during 
construction of the drilled shafts.

Lateral Load Resistance

deflection at the top of the shaft, and induced moment in the shaft. The lateral load analyses

Foundation Settlements 
Settlement of the drilled shaft foundation will result from elastic compression of the 
shaft and subgrade response of the foundation embedded in the subsurface soils. Total 
settlements of the 12- to 15-foot long drilled shafts are estimated to be on the order of less 
than 0.5 inches. We believe that a significant portion of the settlement is elastic and should 
occur as the loads are applied. 
Drilled Shaft Construction Considerations 
Based on the past field explorations conducted in the vicinity of the emergency 
generator trailers, volcanic ash materials consisting of stiff to very stiff clayey silts with 
various amounts of sands and gravels are present at the project site.  Due to the dry friable 
nature of the volcanic ash materials and the presence of granular sands and gravels,

caving-in and/or sloughing of these materials may occur during the drilling operations.  The 
contractor should select drilled shaft installation methods, such as the utilization of 
temporary casing, which will maintain the integrity of the shaft hole. 
Care should be exercised during the removal of the temporary casing, if used, to 
reduce the potential for "necking" of the drilled shaft. Therefore, a minimum 5-foot head of 
concrete above the bottom of the casing should be maintained during the removal of the 
casing. 
The drilled shafts will extend into hard to very hard basalt formation in some areas.  
Therefore, some difficult drilling should be anticipated for the installation of the drilled shaft 
foundations. 
It is imperative that a Geolabs representative be present at the project site to observe 
the drilling and installation of drilled shafts during construction and should be designated as 
a “Special Inspection” item in accordance with Section 1704 of the International Building 
Code (2018 Edition). 
LIMITATIONS 
The findings and recommendations submitted in this letter report are based, 
in part, on our experience in the vicinity of the project site and past field explorations 
conducted in the vicinity of the project site. Variations of subsurface conditions from those 
assumed herein may occur, and the nature and extent of these variations may not 
become evident until construction is underway. If variations then appear evident, Geolabs 
should be contacted to re-evaluate the recommendations presented in this letter report.
