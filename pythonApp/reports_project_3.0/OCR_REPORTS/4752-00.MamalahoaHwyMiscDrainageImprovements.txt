In accordance with our proposal dated we have performed a
geotechnical engineering exploration for the Mamalahoa Highway Miscellaneous
Drainage Improvements project in the vicinity of Hookena in the South Kona area on the
Island of Hawaii This letter report summarizes the findings from our field exploration at
the project site These findings are intended for design input only and are subject to
the limitations noted at the end of this report Work on site was performed on
2001
Field Exploration
The project site is located along Mamalahoa Highway in the vicinity of Hookena on
the Island of Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site is shown on the
attached Project Location Map Plate 1
Two borings were drilled along Mamalahoa Highway at the approximate locations
designated by Department of Transportation DOT personnel Boring No 1 was drilled at
approximately Station 421 56 offset o s 18 feet left and Boring No 2 was drilled at
approximately Station 484 68 o s 18 feet left Offsets were reckoned from the existing
solid double yellow center lines on the street The locations of the drilled borings are
shown on the attached Site Plans Plates 2 1 and 2 2
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling the borings utilizing
truck mounted drilling equipment The borings were drilled to depths of approximately
26 0 and 31 0 feet for Boring Nos 1 and 2 respectively Rock core samples

Field Permeability Tests
Field permeability tests were performed in each of the borings drilled to determine
values of in situ coefficient of permeability Constant head and falling head permeability
tests were attempted in each borehole at depths where permeable zones may be present
by adding a volume of water through a flowmeter at recorded time intervals Each
borehole was filled with water and allowed to saturate After initial saturation the borings
were refilled with water and the drops of water level with time were recorded The results
of the permeability tests are presented on Plates 6 7A and 7B
Subsurface Conditions
The two borings were drilled along Mamalahoa Highway which is covered with 2 to
3 inch thick asphaltic concrete pavement Below the pavement Boring No 1 encountered
silty basaltic gravel to a depth of 2 5 feet followed by vesicular and fractured basalt to the
total depth explored of 26 0 feet Boring No 2 encountered basaltic gravel with sand and
cobbles to a depth of 3 5 feet overlying vugular vesicular and fractured basalt to the total
depth explored of 31 0 feet with clinker layer laminated at 13 to 20 feet
Detailed descriptions of the materials encountered in the borings are presented on
the Logs of Borings Plates 4 and 5
Laboratory Testing
Gradation analyses AASHTO T 265 were performed on samples selected by DOT
as an aid in the soil classification and evaluating soil properties Gradation analysis results
are shown on Plate 8
