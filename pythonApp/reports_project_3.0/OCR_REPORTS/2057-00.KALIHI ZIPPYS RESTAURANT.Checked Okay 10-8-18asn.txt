INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our preliminary geotechnical
engineering evaluation performed for the proposed Kalihi Zippy s
Restaurant in Honolulu Hawaii
The General location of the site is shown on the Project Location
Map Plate 1

The purpose of our exploration was to obtain a preliminary overview
of the near surface subsoil conditions at the site to develop a
generalized soil and rock data set for the formulation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertinent to the design of the subject
project
This report summarizes our preliminary findings and recommendations
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located on North King Street between Mokauea
Street and Puuhale Road in the Kalihi area of Honolulu Oahu Hawaii The
site is bounded by North King Street to the north Puuhale Road to the
west a park to the south and Mokauea Street to the east
The site is currently used for commercial and single family
residential purposes
The topographic relief of the site is generally flat The exposed
surface soils are generally brown clay to clayey silts believed to be
fill material
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to demolish the existing structures grade the site
and construct a restaurant on the site It is anticipated that the
restaurant will be a lightly loaded wood frame structure with
slab on grade floors It is also planned to construct an asphaltic
concrete parking area in the makai part of the site

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The field exploration consisted of drilling three 3 borings and two
2 auger probes at the site The boring depths ranged between 9 5 and
15 0 feet and the auger probe depths were 2 5 and 3 5 feet below the
existing ground surface Our field work indicated that the site is
underlain by a thin surface layer of brown clayey silt over stiff brown
clay Adobe overlying basaltic rock The depth to rock encountered in
the drilled borings and probes varies from about 2 5 to 4 5 feet below the
existing ground surface
The laboratory tests indicate that surface fill layer has low to
moderate swelling potential while the underlying clay has high swelling
potential
Groundwater was neither encountered in the drilled borings nor the
probes at the time of our exploration
The locations of the borings are indicated on the attached Site
Plan The Boring and Probing Logs are attached for your reference
FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that the surface and near surface of
the site is underlain by stiff clay overlying moderately to highly
weathered basalt This clay soil has a high expansion potential Special
foundation design or site grading is recommended to resist or reduce
potential foundation movements
Foundations
Based upon the boring and laboratory test data the on site brown
clay soils Adobe have high expansion potential In order to resist or
reduce potential foundation and or building distresses we recommend that
this material be removed down to a depth of three 3 feet below the slab
GEOLABS HAWAII

subgrade or to the rock formation below the proposed building location for
a minimum of three 3 feet beyond the exterior building line refer to
Plate 3 The clay soils removed from within the building area should be
disposed of properly off site The overexcavated area should be
backfilled with non expansive select material placed in 8 inch loose
horizontal lifts and compacted to 90 maximum dry density as established
by ASTM D 1557 78
Foundations bearing on fill placed in accordance with the above
recommendations may be designed for an allowable bearing capacity of 3000
pounds per square foot The footings should be embedded a minimum of 12
inches below the lowest adjacent exterior grade
Alternatively if the site can be raised 2 to 3 feet with select
non expansive soil the overexcavation may be eliminated or reduced
Slabs On Grade
It is anticipated that the floors of the proposed building will be
concrete slabs on grade A 4 inch layer of 3B Fine gravel should be
provided below the slabs as cushion fill
To reduce future subgrade soils from heaving due to subsequent
saturation we recommend that the subgrade soils should be kept
continuously moist by water sprinkling prior to concrete placement
In order to prevent future moisture infiltration and subsequent
damage to the floor coverings an impervious moisture membrane may be
considered below the slabs
Site Grading
The exact details of the proposed site grading are not currently
available However it is assumed that some minor amounts of grading work
will be required

Prior to the commencement of any earthwork all areas within the
grading limits should be cleared of all vegetation and deleterious
materials The resulting grub and spoil material should be disposed of
properly off site or stockpiled for use in landscaping Any soft weak
or yielding areas disclosed during the clearing operations should be
over excavated to firm ground and the resulting excavation should be
backfilled with well compacted engineered fill
Imported fill material should be granular non expansive soil
generally less than 3 inches in size and capable of forming a dense stable
mass when compacted
All fills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding eight 8
inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum
dry density as established by ASTM D 1557 78 Test Method
Cesspools
There may be buried abandoned cesspools on the project site The
available county and state records should be researched to
determine their locations Any cesspool found on the site should
be opened cleaned of loose soft materials and backfilled with
granular material such as 3B Fine to two 2 feet below
finished subgrade Above this level the cesspool excavation
should be filled and compacted as specified above

Additional Exploration
Due to the present limited access to the site and the consequent
limited amount of information obtained during this exploration we
recommend that additional borings be drilled after the demolition of
the existing structures on the site These borings will serve to
verify the subsurface conditions encountered at the site and allow
revision of the recommendations given herein if appropriate
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comment
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this
review is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume
responsibility for misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the
excavation and foundation phases of the work This is to observe
compliance with the design concepts specifications or recommendations
and to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions
differ from that anticipated prior to start of construction The
recommendations given in this report are contingent upon such
observations

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as
borings probes test pits and or observation wells Variations of
conditions between the field data points may occur and the nature and
extent of these variations may not become evident until construction If
variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations given in this report
The location of the field data points were approximately determined
by estimation from the untitled site plan transmitted to us on April 29
1988 by Boss Corporation Architects AIA Elevations of the data points
were not available to us at the time of our exploration The physical
location and elevation of the field data points should be considered
accurate only to the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross sections are based upon interpolation between field
data points and may not represent actual subsurface conditions

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling three
3 borings and two 2 probes to depths of 2 5 to 15 0 feet below the
existing ground surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site
Plan Plate 2 Portable drilling equipment was used to drill the borings
and probings

textural examination in the field by our geologist who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory All soils
were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A summary of the materials encountered are
presented in the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 5
Soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving a 2 inch
a 140 pound hammer free falling 30 inches The blow counts to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the Boring Logs at the appropriate
sample depths
