In accordance with your request we have performed an additional
geotechnical engineering investigation for the above referenced project
The approximate location of the proposed site and its vicinity is shown on
the Project Location Map Plate 1 Our work was performed in general
accordance with our proposal of June 19th
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
PROJECT CONSIDERATION
It is proposed to construct a new 3 level concrete parking structure
over the existing parking lot at Whaler s Village in Kaanapali on the
island of Maui Hawaii

The proposed structure will be approximately 210 by 240 feet in plan
dimensions Relatively high column loads are anticipated with typical
interior column loads equal to 312 kips and typical exterior column loads
equal to 226 kips
Minimal site grading is anticipated for the proposed construction
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
Our previous investigation at the proposed site indicated that loose
pockets layers of sand may be present at depths of about 5 0 to 8 5 feet
in the areas of Borings 2 and 5 Therefore the purpose of this
investigation was to evaluate the thickness and lateral extent of possible
loose pockets layers of sand in the areas to see if special footing
preparations or considerations would be needed prior to the final
foundation design and construction stage of the project
The scope of this additional investigation included probing by
continuous penetration to a depth of about 10 feet below the existing
ground surface at 18 column locations in the areas of the previous Borings
2 and 5 engineering analysis utilizing the field data and report
preparation
Detailed description of our field exploartion and probing logs are
presented in Appendix A The approximate locations of the probings are
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2

GENERAL STTE CONDITIONS
The proposed site is located to the west of Kaanapali Parkway in the
existing parking lot located to the north of Building A of Whaler s
Village at Kaanapali
The 50 000 square foot site is currently used as a parking lot for
patrons of the shoping village The ground slopes slightly from the
southwest towards the northeast of the site
The 18 column locations in the areas of Borings 2 and 5 are located
primarily in the northwest quarter of the parking lot
Ground cover consists primarily of deteriorating asphaltic concrete
pavement with extensive cracks
Subsoil Condition
Eighteen probings were made at the 18 anticipated column locations
to depths of 9 0 to 10 0 feet below the existing ground surface in the
areas of Borings 2 and 5 All probings encountered a medium dense to
dense layer of beach sand which was cemented near the bottom of the
probe These subsoil conditions are in general agreement with the boring
data obtained from our previous investigation at the proposed site
However most of the probings did not encounter the thick loose soil
conditions which were indicated previously by Borings 2 and 5 Therefore
we believe that the loose zones in the sandy soil are only localized thin
loose pockets of sand

Generally 1 to 1 5 foot thick loose pockets of sand exist at

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATION
The probings encountered generally competent subsoil conditions for
footing foundation at the 18 column locations explored Only thin
localized loose pockets of sand were noted in some of the probings
Based on the soil conditions and anticipated column loads we
believe that the thin localized loose pockets of sand should not
significantly affect the stability of footing foundation bearing on the
sand layer due to the following reasons
1 The sand pockets are generally thin lenses about 1 to 1 5 feet
thick
2 Stress reduction effect at depths below the bottom of footing
may help to significantly reduce the bearing stress on the loose
pockets due to the footing foundation and thus reduce the
anticipated settlements
3 Probable sand arching effect may help to further reduce the
bearing stress on the loose pockets due to the footing
foundation
4 Most of the anticipated settlements due to footing foundation
loads on the loose pockets of sand are immediate settlements
which should occur quickly during construction and their
post construction effects should be minimal

Design Review
As mentioned in our soil report dated plans and
specifications for site grading pavement and foundation design should be
forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written comments prior to
construction to see that the intent of our recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that site grading subgrade preparation and
footing excavations be observed by the soils engineer during construction
to see that adequate compaction has been obtained and that the subsurface
conditions are consistent with the findings of our investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
If conditions encountered during construction are different from
those presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made
The recommendations presented in this report are contingent upon the
above inspections
