PRELIMINARY SOIL ENGINEERING REPORT
Tot A RIDGE PHASE I
In accordance with your request we have made a preliminary study of
the near surface soil conditions at the above referenced lot in the Hawaii
Ioa Ridge Subdivision in order to provide site grading and foundation
recommendations for the proposed residence The study consisted of a
seismic refraction survey and geologic reconnaissance of the project lot
and a review of the preliminary site plan and house plans and sections
This report summarizes our preliminary findings and recommendations
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
The proposed residence will be a one story structure over a lower
level garage and driveway with total plan area of about 3500 square
feet The preliminary plans indicate that site grading for the house will
include up to about 9 feet of fills and up to about 7 feet of cuts Site
grading around the house will include additional minor fills and cuts
Concrete slab on grade is anticipated for some of the house floor
areas the garage and the driveway A retaining wall is anticipated to
provide grade separation at about the middle portion of the house

EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The subject lot is located on the north side of Pu u ikena Place in
the Phase I development of the Hawaii Loa Ridge Subdivision in Honolulu
on the island of Oahu Hawaii

The existing elevation of the site generally varies from about 292 to

The topography is characterized by a sloping hillside that is
sparsely to moderately covered with grasses basaltic gravel cobbles and
boulders
Rock outcrops were observed over most of the building site
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The site is located on the southern flank of the elongated Koolau
Volcanic Range which forms the southeastern portion of Oahu Hawaii The
range was formed during the Tertiary Period of pahoehoe and aa basalt
lava flows from a rift zone roughly paralleling the existing mountain
trends

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The seismic survey and geological reconnaissance indicate that the
site is suitable for the proposed house construction
The near surface rock formation should provide adequate bearing for
the proposed structure and related utilities
Site Grading
Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation
debris rubbish and other unsuitable materials should be removed and
disposed properly off site in order to prevent contamination of the
excavated material
Loose soil pockets cobbles and boulders below the house and driveway
areas should be removed
Excavation
The field reconnaissance and seismic survey indicate that the site
may be underlain by basalt formations with occasional surface cobbles
and boulders
We believe that the excavation into the near surface weathered rock
for the proposed residence may be done by bulldozer and ripping
equipment

Fills should be placed in horizontal loose lifts up to 12 inches
thick and compacted to 90 of its maximm dry density per ASTM
D 1557 78
Conventional compaction testing may not be practicable in excavated
volcanic rock material due to the large particle size Instead a
testing program consisting of visual observations and test strips to
determine the lift thickness and number of passes of a compactor
required to achieve the desired compaction should be conducted at
the start of the project The number of passes can then be used as
the criteria for compaction
Foundations
We believe the on site rocky ground and compacted select granular
fill resulting from the above site grading can provide suitable foundation
support for the proposed building
Structural fill should be placed in accordance with the
recommendations under Site Grading and should be observed by a qualified
soil technician or engineer
Shallow spread and continuous wall footing foundations may be used
for the proposed structure
All exterior footing bottoms should be embedded a minimm of 18
inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade into compacted granular
fill or 6 inches into basalt rock formations The minimm embedment for
interior footings may be reduced to 12 inches on fill or 6 inches into
rock formations below the lowest adjacent finished grade

For the footings bearing on the compacted rock or granular fill an
allowable bearing pressure of 3000 p s f may be used An allowable
bearing value up to 5000 p s f may be used for footings bearings on hard
basalt formations These values may be increased by one third when
temporary wind or seismic loads are considered
It is anticipated that the footings if constructed in accordance
with the above recommendations will have negligible settlements
Resistance to lateral loads on the building can be obtained by
passive earth pressure and frictional resistance A frictional
coefficient of 0 4 may be used to calculate the friction between the
bottom of the footings and basalt compacted fill In addition an
equivalent fluid pressure of 350 p s f per foot of depth may be used to
determine the passive earth resistance against the buried vertical faces
of the footings
Concrete Slab On Grade
Based on the non expansive and competent slab subgrade conditions
anticipated at the site 4 inch concrete on grade slabs placed over a
4 inch layer of base course compacted to 95 of its maxima dry density
per ASIM D 1557 78 may be used The thickened edges of the on grade slabs
should extend a minimum of 12 inches below the lowest adjacent finished
ground surface or 6 inches into basalt rock formation
To reduce differential settlement where the slab is partly on cut in
rock formation and partly on fill the cut portions should be
over excavated about one foot and then recompacted to match the adjacent
subgrade density

Retaining Walls
It is our understanding that a retaining wall may be used for grade
separation for the proposed construction
Based on the anticipated soil conditions slightly weathered to
massive basalt rock formations may be encountered along the wall
location For the foundation of the retaining wall the footings should
be embedded a minimum of 1 foot into the basalt formations
An allowable bearing capacity of 5000 p s f may be utilized for wall
footing on hard basalt formations The value is for total dead plus live
loads and may be increased by one third when temporary wind and seismic
loads are considered
Clean granular materials such as the on site sand and gravel
materials or imported 3B Fine gravel should be used as backfill directly
behind the wall This would reduce the compactive effort required for the
backfill and the build up of additional lateral pressures on the wall
Non expansive impervious silty material should be used for the top 2 feet
of the backfill to reduce the potential of water infiltration behind the
wall

Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be

Construction Observation
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the
foundation phase of the work This is to observe compliance with the
design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow design
changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from that
anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in
this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site

LIMITATION
The recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from a seismic refraction survey Variations of
conditions away from the surveyed alignment may occur and the nature and
extent of these variations may not become evident until construction If
variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations given in this report
The location of the surveyed alignment was approximately determined
by tape measurement on the site with reference to the preliminary site
plan from Innovative Architecture Designs Inc The physical location of
the survey should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the
method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representation of the
subsoils along the surveyed alignment depict the interpreted boundaries
between rock zones with varying degree of weathering and as such may
denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on the profile or
cross section are based upon interpolation along the alignment and may not
represent actual subsurface conditions
