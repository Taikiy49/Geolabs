INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration performed
for the proposed CSK Auto Store in Waipahu located at Highway in the
Waipahu area in the District of Ewa on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and
vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering recommendations
derived from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering analyses These
recommendations are intended for the design of foundations retaining structures site
grading and pavements only The findings and recommendations presented herein are
subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
We understand that it is desired to construct a new CSK Auto Store in Waipahu at the
located at Highway in the Waipahu area in the District of Ewa on the
island of Oahu Hawaii The project site encompasses an area of approximately
45 000 square feet Based on the available information the new one story store building will
have a footprint of about 10 000 square feet We understand that some related
improvements such as parking stalls will also be constructed around the store building as
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 Due to the slightly sloping surface conditions at the project
site and the presence of a drainage canal on the eastern property line we anticipate that
some retaining walls may be necessary to provide for grade separation and a level building
pad for the store
Based on the existing site conditions we anticipate that grading at the site will likely
consist primarily of fills to achieve the design finished grades In general we envision that
fills on the order of about 4 to 8 feet or less with minor cuts would be needed to achieve
the design finished grades for the proposed project Further details pertaining to the
proposed store construction were not available at the time this report was prepared

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from two shield
volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be middle to
late Pliocene in age and forms the bulk of the western third of the island The younger
shield Koolau is estimated to be late Pliocene to early Pleistocene Ice Age in age and
forms the majority of the eastern two thirds of the island Waianae became extinct while
Koolau was still active and its eastern flank was partially buried below Koolau lavas banking
against its eastern flank These banked or ponded lavas formed a broad plateau referred
to as the Schofield Plateau Both Waianae and Koolau Ranges have lost most of their
original shield outlines and are now long narrow ridges shaped largely by erosion
The project site is located along the southern flank of the Koolau Mountain Range
The subsurface conditions at the project site are composed of terrigenous alluvial soils
deposited by continuous erosion of the volcanic dome The base of the stratigraphic section
in this area is Koolau Basalt Man made fills were placed during commercial development
in the area in recent times and brought the project site to its current conditions

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field exploration the proposed project site is generally underlain by a
thin layer of surficial fills consisting of dense clayey gravel with sand of about 1 5 feet in
thickness The surficial fills are generally underlain by stiff to very stiff silty clays extending
to the maximum depths drilled of about 31 5 feet below the existing ground surface Two of
the four borings Boring Nos 3 and 4 encountered pockets of soft to medium stiff silty clays
up to about 10 feet in thickness The soft to medium stiff silty clays were encountered
starting at depths of about 12 to 15 feet below the existing ground surface Groundwater was
encountered at about 16 5 to 18 feet below the existing ground surface
Based on our field exploration and laboratory testing we believe that a shallow
foundation system bearing on the stiff to very stiff near surface silty clays may be used to
support the proposed store building The on site silty clay soils exhibit a slight to moderate
potential for shrinking and swelling when subjected to moisture fluctuations To reduce the
potential for moderate ground movements resulting from the shrinking swelling of the silty
clay soils we recommend that the subgrade soils underneath the slab areas be properly
prepared and capped with at least 12 inches of non expansive select granular fill material
below the slab cushion
In addition we understand that a retaining wall involving the placement of fills will
be constructed along the existing concrete lined drainage canal It should be noted that
some ground settlements may occur in areas where fills will be placed since a portion of the
site is underlain by about 10 feet of soft to medium stiff soil deposits Therefore since the
retaining wall proposed along the concrete lined drainage canal will require some new fills
to achieve the design finished grades we anticipate that some ground settlements would
occur Therefore consideration should be given to the use of more flexible walls such as
a Mechanically Stabilized Earth MSE Retaining Wall system to better tolerate the
anticipated ground movements

Building Foundations
As previously mentioned the proposed auto parts store will consist of a one story
building with approximately 10 000 square feet of floor space Based on the subsurface
conditions encountered in our exploration we recommend that a shallow foundation
system consisting of spread and or continuous footings be used to support the proposed
structure An allowable bearing pressure of up to 3 000 pounds per square foot psf may
be used for the design of footings bearing on the stiff to very stiff silty clays encountered
in our borings This allowable bearing pressure is for dead plus live loads and may be
increased by one third 1 3 for transient loads such as those caused by temporary wind
and or seismic forces
In general footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grade Due to the slightly sloping ground conditions at the project site
we anticipate that a portion of the building footprint may need to be filled to provide a level
building pad Therefore we anticipate that the building walls may also function as retaining
structures In order to avoid surcharging the existing retaining walls at the site with structural
loads the new building foundations should be located far enough away from the wall to
provide a minimum horizontal set back distance equal to the depth of the retaining wall from
the back side of the wall As an alternative to the horizontal set back distance the footings
for the new building should be embedded below a 45 degree imaginary plane extending up
from the heel of the existing retaining wall footing
Where footings are located adjacent to other below grade structures or utilities the
footings should also extend to a depth below an imaginary 45 degree plane projected
upward from the bottom of the below grade structure or utility This requirement is necessary
to avoid surcharging adjacent below grade structures with additional structural loads and to
reduce the potential for foundation settlement

Slabs On Grade
We anticipate that concrete slabs on grade will be used for the proposed auto parts
store building The near surface soils encountered in our borings generally consist of silty
clays which exhibit a slight to moderate expansion potential when subjected to moisture
fluctuations Therefore to reduce the potential for structural distress to the lightly loaded
slabs we recommend that a minimum of 12 inches of non expansive select granular fill
material be provided below the concrete slab The non expansive fill should extend a
minimum of 2 feet laterally beyond the edges of the slab The clayey subgrades for
slabs on grades should be scarified to a depth of at least 8 inches moisture conditioned to
at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture and compacted to not less than 90 percent

relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed
as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil determined in accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91 Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by
dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
The non expansive select granular fill recommended below the floor slabs should
consist of imported granular material such as crushed coral basalt gravel or cinder sand
The material should be well graded from coarse to fine with no particles larger than 3 inches
in largest dimension It should also contain between 10 and 30 percent particles passing the
No 200 sieve The material should have a laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR value
of 20 or higher and a swell potential of 1 percent or less when tested in accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1883 The select granular fill materials should be
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content placed in 8 inch level loose
lifts and compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction The slab subgrades should
be kept moist until covered by the select granular fill
To provide more uniform support for the slabs we recommend that the concrete
slabs on grade be supported on a minimum 4 inch cushion fill over the 12 inches of
non expansive select granular fill The cushion fill layer should consist of open graded
gravels such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation which would also serve
as a capillary moisture break
For slabs on grade with moisture sensitive surfacing we recommend that an
impermeable membrane be placed over the cushion fill to reduce the potential for migration
of moisture vapor through the concrete slab In order to promote more uniform curing of the
slab concrete and to provide protection of the vapor membrane placement of a 2 inch layer
of moist fine sand may be considered above the membrane If the 2 inch sand layer is
specified the sand should be moistened slightly prior to placing the slab concrete

Mechanically Stabilized Earth MSE Retaining Wall
Based on the presence of some soft soil deposits at the site we recommend that the
retaining wall planned at the eastern property line of the project site consist of a Mechanically
Stabilized Earth MSE retaining wall such as a Keystone reinforced soil retaining wall
system We recommend that the MSE retaining wall be used to accommodate the potential
ground settlements due to the placement of fills behind the new retaining wall above the soft
soil deposits underlying the site The system combines modular concrete wall units with
geogrid reinforcement and compacted soil to result in a reinforced soil mass that supports
the earth pressure and surcharge loads This composite essentially forms a gravity wall
structure with an ability to tolerate significant total and differential settlements In addition
MSE retaining walls may be desirable due to the flexibility of the wall ease of construction
high load carrying capacity and economy
Design of the MSE retaining wall will need to take into consideration both external and
internal stabilities of the structure In evaluating external stability the MSE retaining wall
structure must satisfy four stability conditions 1 bearing failure 2 translational sliding
3 overturning stability and 4 overall slope stability Geotechnical design parameters to
evaluate these stability conditions are presented in the following subsections Some of the
geotechnical parameters necessary in evaluating the internal stability of the MSE retaining
wall are presented in the Reinforced Fill and Backfill Materials subsection below The
internal and external stabilities of the MSE retaining wall will need to be evaluated by the
design engineer We have evaluated the overall slope stability for the MSE retaining wali
and the results are summarized in the Overall Slope Stability subsection

Overall Slope Stability
We have evaluated the overall slope stability of the MSE retaining wall by performing
slope stability analyses of two dimensional slope cross sections using Bishop s
simplified method of limit equilibrium analysis Based on our analyses the factor of
safety for short term and long term stabilities of the MSE retaining wall is at least 1 5
which is the minimum factor of safety normally recommended
Based on our slope stability a minimum base width to wall height ratio of at least 0 7
is recommended for the MSE retaining wall with a level backfill condition We also
recommend that the base width be at least 8 feet to develop sufficient length for
overall stability

Reinforced Fill and Backfill Materials
We recommend that the reinforced fill material for the MSE retaining wall consist of
imported non expansive select granular fill material such as crushed coral basalt
gravel or cinder sand The material should be well graded from coarse to fine with

Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
General fills and backfills should be moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above
the optinum moisture content placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches loose
thickness and compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction The
compaction requirement should be increased to 95 percent relative compaction for
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 4215 00 A Page 17
the last lift of fills placed under the pavements Relative compaction refers to the
in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of
the same soil established in accordance with AASHTO T 180 ASTM D 1557 91
Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by weight corresponding to the
maximum dry density Compaction should be accomplished by sheepsfoot rollers
vibratory rollers multiple wheel pneumatic tired rollers or other similar types of
acceptable compaction equipment Water tamping jetting or ponding should not be
allowed to compact the fill
Cut and Fill Slopes
In general we believe that cut slopes exposing the on site materials may be
designed with a slope inclination of two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V or flatter
Permanent fill slopes constructed using the excavated on site soils or imported
materials may be designed with a slope inclination of 2H 1V or flatter Fills placed on
slopes steeper than 5H 1V should be keyed and benched into the existing slope to
provide stability of the new fill against sliding
The filling operations should start at the lowest point and continue up in level
horizontal compacted layers in accordance with the above fill placement
recommendations Fill slopes should be constructed by overfilling and cutting back
to the design slope ratio to obtain a well compacted slope face In the event that
over cutting of a slope occurs keying and benching requirements should be
implemented instead of backfilling the slope to the design grade with sliver fill In
addition slope planting should be provided as soon as possible to reduce the
potential for erosion of the finished slopes
Corrosion Evaluation
One of the subsurface soil samples retrieved from our field exploration at the site was
subjected to chemical analyses for soluble chloride and sulfate concentrations The chemical
analysis tests were performed by Environmental Laboratory of the Pacific The results of
these tests are presented on Plate B 5
W O 4215 00 A Page 18
Based on the test results the soluble chloride and sulfate concentrations in the soil
sample tested were below the limits considered to be corrosive to concrete substructures
Therefore the on site soils tested may be considered to be generally non corrosive to
reinforced concrete in contact with the soils However normal precautions to reduce the
potential effects of corrosion such as minimum concrete cover should be observed for
reinforced concrete substructures
Pavement Design
New flexible pavements are anticipated for the parking and driveway areas for the
proposed auto store project We anticipate that the vehicle loading for this project will consist
primarily of passenger vehicles light pick up trucks and occasional heavy trucks We have
made our preliminary pavement design assuming that the pavement subgrade soil will be
similar to the stiff silty clay soils encountered in the borings with a CBR value of at least
12 with a swell value of less than 3 percent ASTM D 1883 Based on the above
assumptions we recommend that the following pavement section be used for preliminary
design purposes
Asphaltic Concrete Pavement
2 Inch Asphalt Concrete
6 Inch Aggregate Base Course 95 Percent Relative Compaction
6 Inch Select Borrow Subbase 95 Percent Relative Compaction
14 Inch Total Pavement Thickness on a Moist Compacted Subgrade
The pavement subgrade soils should be scarified to a minimum depth of 8 inches
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to not less
than 95 percent relative compaction The select borrow subbase and aggregate base course
should consist of crushed basalt aggregate compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative
compaction
Laboratory CBR tests and field density tests should be performed on samples of the
actual subgrade soils encountered during construction to confirm the design section

recommended above In addition the recommended pavement section is based on the
assumption that good drainage will be provided for areas adjacent to the pavements
Paved areas should be sloped and drainage gradients should be maintained to carry
surface water off the site Surface water ponding should not be allowed on the site during
or after construction Where concrete curbs are used to isolate landscaping in or adjacent
to the pavement areas we recommend that the curbs extend a minimum of 2 inches into the
subgrade below the select borrow subbase to reduce the potential for migration of landscape
water into the pavement section
Utility Trench
We envision that some new utility lines and utility connections may be required for the
proposed project In general we recommend that granular bedding consisting of 6 inches
of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation be used under the pipes for support
of the utility lines Free draining granular materials such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33
No 67 gradation should also be used for the initial trench backfill up to about 12 inches
above the pipes to provide adequate support around the pipes and to reduce the compaction
effort of the backfill in order to reduce the possibility of damaging the pipes It is critical to
use this free draining material to reduce the potential for formation of voids below the
haunches of pipes and to provide adequate support for the sides of the pipes Improper
trench backfill could result in settlement of the backfill and damage to the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipes to the
top of the subgrade or finished grade may consist of the on site soils or select granular fill
material The backfill should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture
content placed in maximum 8 inch level loose lifts and mechanically compacted to not less
than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for future ground subsidence
Where trenches are below pavement areas the upper 3 feet of the trench backfill below the
pavement grade should be compacted to not less than 95 percent relative compaction

Drainage
The finished grades outside the building should be sloped to shed water away from
the foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also advised that gutter systems
be installed around the building and that the discharge be diverted away from the foundation
and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near the foundations and slabs should
also be avoided considering the slight to moderate shrink swell potential of the on site soils
Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have concrete bottoms and drains to
reduce the potential for water infiltration into the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the above
foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation and
subsequent loss of strength and or heaving of the near surface soils The foundation
excavations should be properly backfilled against the footings immediately after setting of the
concrete to reduce the potential for water infiltration In addition drainage swales should be
provided as soon as possible and maintained to drain surface water runoff away from the
foundations and slabs
