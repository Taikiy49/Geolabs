INTRODUCTION
The purpose of our exploration was to explore the subsurface soil
conditions at the site in order to provide pertinent soil data to aid in
the design of the site grading slab on grade and foundation for the
project
The scope of our investigation included drilling and sampling of two
2 borings to depths of 16 5 feet below the existing ground surface
Also included were laboratory testing of selected samples obtained
engineering analysis and evaluation of the field and laboratory data
development of the preliminary design recommendations and report
preparation

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a Circle K convenience facility at
this site The facility will consist of a single story 40 by 72 foot
store and a 24 by 40 foot pump island with two gasoline dispenser units
To supply the gas island an underground gasoline storage area
consisting of three 10 000 gallon tanks is planned for a 28 by 32 foot
area adjacent to the Circle K store The convenience facility will be
surrounded by an new asphaltic concrete A C parking pavement
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located on the southeastern corner of the
junction of School Street and Kamehameha IV Road in Kalihi Oahu Hawaii
The site measures approximately 160 by 160 feet in plan dimension
and slopes gently towards the south with ground elevations varying
between 115 and 121 feet above Mean Sea Level
An abandoned Texaco Service Station currently occupies the site
Most of the site is currently covered with A C pavement A small
one story building and two 2 concrete gas pump islands are also
situated on the project site

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration generally encountered only 1 5 feet of medium
dense silty gravel and sand fill select borrow underlain by highly
expansive adobe clay Since light loads are anticipated for the
proposed Circle K and pump island the main concern in designing the
on grade slabs and building foundations would be the effect due to the
proximity of the swelling adobe clay to the bottom on grade slab and
footings It is our opinion that special procedures to reduce the
shrink swell potential of the clayey subsoils should be considered
Ideally if the finished grade of the slab on grade and project site
grading can be raised by an extra one foot we believe there should be
sufficient capping thickness totalling 30 inches to stabilize the
foundation subgrade Otherwise over excavation below the upper select
fill to remove a sufficient amount of the underlying expansive clay would
be required first and replaced with non expansive select fill in order to
provide a minimum thickness of non expansive capping fill layer varying
between 2 to 3 feet above the underlying adobe clay soil
Foundation Support
Shallow spread or continuous wall footings bearing on the compacted
select backfill may be used to support the proposed structures along with
special procedures to reduce the shrinkage swelling effects of the adobe
clay soils

Our previous experience indicates that the adobe clay soils below
the foundations and the select borrow can have high volume charge
potential when wetted from a dry moisture content and it can also shrink
when dried in the future Therefore we recommend that the expansive
clays below the foundations should over excavated down to a minimum depth
of two 2 feet below the bottom of the footings The over excavation
should also extend a minimum of 6 inches beyond the edge of the footings
The bottom 2 feet of the over excavation should be backfilled with
non expansive borrow materials such as select borrow crusher waste or
coral placed in compacted horizontal lifts not exceeding 6 inches thick
and compacted to a minimum of of 85 and no more than 90 of its maximm
dry density per ASTM D 1557 78 The top 1 foot of the footing excavation
should be backfilled with impervious non expansive soil compacted to the
above density refer to Plate 2
The recommended minimum footing width is 12 inches with a minimum
embedment of 12 inches below the lowest adjacent outside grade An
allowable bearing value up to 2500 p s f may be used The bearing
capacity is for total dead plus live loads and may be increased by
one third for temporary wind and seismic loads
Site Grading
No major site grading is anticipated
At the on set of earthwork the area within the building grading
limit should be thoroughly cleared Any unsuitable materials such as
previous fill rubbish soft material and vegetation should be removed
and disposed of properly off site
Loose materials encountered at subgrade level should be removed and
replaced with imported select fill material compacted to a minimum of 90
of its ASTM D 1557 78 maximum dry density

The present poor condition of the existing asphaltic concrete
pavement requires that it be broken and disposed of properly off site or
if necessary reused in the fill
Slab On Grade
The expansive on site clay soil under on grade slabs area should be
over excavated to a depth of at least 36 inches below the slab bottom and
replaced with non expansive select borrow soils and a 4 inch gravel
cushion layer in order to reduce the effects of soil heaving on the slabs
see Plate 3 We also recommend that in order to further stabilize the
on site adobe subgrade soils below the over excavation level hydrated
lime should be placed at a rate of about one 1 lb of lime per square
foot over the clay soil water saturated and then be kept continuously
moist prior to concrete placement
If it is feasible we recommend that the slab on grade be placed
over a minimum 12 inch thick layer of non expansive select fill above the
existing A C pavement surface This would require that the finish site
grading be raised by a minimm one foot above the existing site
elevation To reduce the soil swelling potential the subgrade soils
should be thoroughly wetted kept moist and not allowed to dry from the
start of construction until the placement of concrete This would
require daily sprinkling of the subgrade soils
In both designs mentioned above a 4 inch layer of 3B Fine gravel
should be provided below the slab In order to protect against moisture
infiltration an impervious moisture barrier may be incorporated below
the slab
Underground Storage Tanks
The tank diameters as indicated on the Site Plan are 8 feet The
tank will therefore bear on the underlying expansive clay soils

It is our recommendation that a stabilization pad consisting of a
6 inch thick layer of 3B Fine gravel be placed underneath the storage

The bottom of the excavation should be kept moist continuously and
not allowed to dry out
It is anticipated that the excavation would penetrate through a
stiff adobe clay formation about 10 feet thick and the contractor

Pavement and Parking Areas
Based on the results of our field study the following preliminary
pavement design is recommended for expansive clay subgrade condition
2 Inch Asphalt Concrete
6 Inch Base Course Basalt Aggregate
18 Inch Select Borrow Above Moist Subgrade
26 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
CBR tests should be performed on the actual subgrade soil samples
during construction and the above design section should verified in the
field
Utilities
Existing utilities and previously buried cesspools should be located
and verified in the field It may be necessary to move an existing line
or adjust structural details to accommodate the utility Should an
unanticipated condition or buried structure be encountered during
construction each condition should be evaluated by the soils engineer
and the design modified if necessary

Plans and specifications for foundations and pavement design should

We recommend that all subgrade preparation footing and slab
