a two story structure approximately 40 by 60 feet in plan dimensions

Waialae Iki Honolulu This trapezoid shaped approximately 10 840 square

lot boundary The extent of utility trench excavation and backfill
condition is not known at this time

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The stiff on site residual soils encountered below the existing
ground surface can generally provide adequate foundation support for the
proposed house provided that some care is exercised during construction to
minimize the swelling potential of the soil as discussed herein
The field exploration and laboratory tests performed on the stiff
on site silty clay indicate that this material generally has moderately
high swelling potential if it is allowed to dry prior to subsequent
saturation Therefore to reduce the effects due to soil swelling under
on grade slabs we recommend that the on site soil be capped with at least
12 inches of non expansive select fill and that the subgrade soils
underneath the select fill and slab be moistened and protected from drying
from the start of construction until the slab concrete placement

Lot Grading
The fills and backfills within the top 12 inches minimm below the
on grade slabs and its cushion fill should consist of non expansive soils
such as select borrow crusher waste or coral Where cut or minimal fill
is anticipated the on site soil should be over excavated to provide a
12 inch minimum thickness of non expansive select fill capping underneath
the on grade slab s cushion fill
The on site cut soils may be used for fill if they are compacted at
least 5 above optimum moisture content and placed below 12 inches from
the slab bottom and away from retaining walls
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limits should be cleared of vegetation debris rubbish and other
deleterious materials These materials should be removed and disposed of
properly off site Any loose or soft spots encountered should be
over excavated down to the firm subsoils and then replaced with compacted
non expansive select material
The fill material should consist of non expansive granular materials
generally less than three 3 inches in maximum size with less than 20
passing the 200 sieve All imported fill material should be tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to transportation to the site
The fill areas should then be scarified to a depth of six 6 inches
moisture conditioned and recompacted to 90 of its maximum dry density per
ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 Due to the sloping nature of the site we
recommend that the total fill thickness and retaining wall backfill to
raise the finish grade be limited to three 3 feet maximm Also the
fill should be keyed and benched into the nature stiff ground
The fill should be placed in maximm 8 inch thick loose level lifts
and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density per ASIM Test
Method D 1557 78

House _ Foundations
Spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the proposed
residence An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f may be used for
footings bearing on the natural soil or compacted fill
Individual footings and continuous wall footings for the residence
and deck should be embedded a minimm of 24 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grade
If located on a sloping area the footing should be embedded down an
additional amount to obtain a 10 foot minimm horizontal set back
distance measured from the outer edge of the footing out to the slope
face This additional embedment depth is required to help resist the
downhill slope creep movement and to develop the recommended bearing
capacity of the soil
Slab On Grade
It should be noted that the on site silty clay soils may be
moderately expansive if allowed to dry Therefore the on site soil
should be over excavated down to a depth of 12 inches minimum below the
slab cushion fill thoroughly moistened and then replaced with
non expansive imported soils as previously discussed The thickened edge
of the slab should be embedded a minimm of 24 inches below the lowest
adjacent finish grade
To further reduce future post construction swelling of the underlying
expansive soils we recommend that the in place soils below the upper
capping fill pad be kept moist prior to concrete and cushion fill
placement
A 4 inch layer of 3B Fine gravel cushion fill should be provided
below the slab In order to protect against future moisture infiltration
an impervious moisture barrier may be incorporated below the slab

Drainage
The finish lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings should
be shaped to shed water away from foundations and to avoid ponding
condition Also it is advised that gutter systems be installed around
the house and gutter water be diverted far away from the foundations and
driveway Excessive landscape watering near foundations and slab should
also be avoided
Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance
of the above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of
strength The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled tight
against the wall or slab footing inmediately after setting of the concrete
to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and maintained
to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundations

Swimming Pool Design
Swimming pool design and construction are the responsibility of the
swimming pool builder This contractor is also generally responsible for
quality control and performance of the completed pool
Since expansive soil was encountered non expansive select fill
material is recommended behind the pool wall If necessary the
recommendations given in the Retaining Walls section of this report may
be given considerations in the pool design
If required the upper portion of the deep end of the swimming pool
should be a free standing pool without resorting to the use of fill or a
high retaining wall backfill to provide the lateral support
Concrete Driveway
Since the driveway can tolerate some amount of heaving the 12 inch
on site soil over excavation and replacement may be replaced with 8 inch
thick base course layer if some amount of small slab cracking are
tolerable It is critical that the subgrade soils should be maintained in
a moist condition and prevented from drying out prior to base course and
concrete placement
Joints are recommended in the driveway slab at about 8 to 10 foot
intervals and at discontinuities in order to allow for some relative
movements of the concrete
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be

prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this review
is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for
misinterpretation of the recommendations

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during site grading and
construction of the foundation This is to observe compliance with the
design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow design
changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from that
anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in
this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field borings Variations of
conditions between the field data points may occur and the nature and
extent of these variations may not become evident until construction If
variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations given in this report
The location of the field borings were approximately determined by
taping from structures indicated on the plans provided Elevations of
the data points were approximately determined by interpolation from
elevation points on the plans The physical location and elevation of
the field data points should be considered accurate only to the degree
implied by the method used
