PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Mehana at Kapolei development Mehana is a mixed residential subdivision
project bordered by Renton Road to the south Ft Barrette Road to the east the future
James Campbell Estate residential development to the north and the Ronald T Y Moon
Judiciary Complex to the west
Pohakala Parcel 8A development is a 12 unit multi family residential subdivision
within a 5 3 acre parcel located in the western area of the Mehana development Pohakala
is bordered on the south by Kakala Street and Kama aha Boulevard to the east as shown
on the Project Location Map Plate 1 and the Site Plan Plate 2
SITE GRADING
The mass grading for Mehana at Kapolei Pohakala Parcel 8A generally
consisted of clearing and grubbing removal of existing stockpiles and site grading for
minor cuts generally less than 5 feet and fills about 10 to 15 feet Royal Contracting
performed the earthwork operations for the project
At the onset of earthwork areas within the contract grading limits were generally
cleared and grubbed of vegetation and deleterious materials Materials generated from
the clearing operations were disposed of off site Following the clearing and grubbing
operations the areas to be cut were excavated Finished grades were compacted using
D R Horton Schuler Division
Page 2

a sheepsfoot and or smooth vibratory drum roller In general the site was capped with
3 feet of coralline materials
Soft and yielding areas encountered during clearing and grubbing were generally
over excavated to expose firm natural material and the resulting excavation was backfilled
with well compacted engineered fill
The fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to above optimum
moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density
as determined in accordance with ASTM D1557 In the roadway areas the fill and backfill
materials within 3 feet of the finished grade were compacted to a minimum 95 percent of
the maximum dry density Open non buildable or landscaping areas were compacted to
at least 80 percent of the maximum dry density
During the site grading operations samples of the materials utilized as fill were
obtained and transported to our laboratory for testing The maximum dry densities and
optimum moisture contents were established in general accordance with ASTM D1557
Modified Proctor and the test results are summarized on Plate 3
Periodic in situ moisture and density tests were conducted in general accordance
with ASTM D6938 Nuclear Gauge Method to determine the adequacy of compaction of
fills and backfills for infrastructure and utility installations including compaction of building
pads garages walkways and driveways
The test results are summarized on Plates 4 1 through 4 19 These tests were
performed on moisture conditioned and compacted lifts of fills and backfills Test locations
and elevations were estimated using on site survey stakes for reference These locations
and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods
used Areas deemed unsatisfactory were reworked until the measured relative
compaction conformed to or exceeded the minimum specified for the project
