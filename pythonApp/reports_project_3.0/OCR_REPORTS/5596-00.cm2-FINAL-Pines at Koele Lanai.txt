Project Considerations
The proposed Pines at Koele project consists of 15 two story wood framed
buildings located on the east side of Kukui Road in Lanai City Hawaii The site is vacant
and consists of relatively level ground covered with lawn
A geotechnical investigation was conducted by Island Geotechnical Engineering
Inc in September 2004 Based on the investigation results removal and replacement of
48 inches of the on site clay soils with structural fill was recommended
Geolabs Inc recently conducted a geotechnical exploration at the nearby Villas at
Koele project The removal and replacement recommendations for the Villas project was
substantially different from the Pines project Therefore Geolabs was requested to review
the subsurface data from the Pines project to provide alternative foundation
recommendations
Purpose and Scope
The purpose of our consultation was to review available subsurface information to
obtain an overview of the subsurface conditions and develop a soil rock data set to
formulate geotechnical engineering recommendations pertaining to the proposed
structures To accomplish this we conducted a preliminary exploration program consisting
of the following tasks and work efforts
1 Sampling of near surface soils by hand digging
2 Review of the subsurface information obtained by others at the site

Preliminary Findings and Recommendations
Our laboratory swell tests generally confirm the soils at the Pines project may be
more expansive than those found at the Villas project Generally the soils at the
Pines project may be classified as having moderate to high swelling potential depending
on the moisture content and density of the soil The swelling potential of the soil increases
with decreasing moisture content and increasing density
The swell tests performed by Island Geotechnical Engineering Inc included
air drying samples to 15 percent moisture content prior to saturation Our swell tests with
similar air drying produced similar swell tests results However due to the natural wetter
climate at the site we believe it may be more appropriate to test the soils without
air drying The swell tests without air drying running the tests at the natural moisture
content produce greatly reduced swell potential
Based on the test results our opinion is that the depth of over excavation and
replacement could be greatly reduced if the soils are maintained in a moist to wet
condition The following are recommended to maintain the moisture under the buildings
e Provide a 12 inch thick layer of structural fill under the slab This would
provide a capping layer over the in situ soils to help reduce surface drying
and maintain the moisture in the underlying clay soil
Maintain the subgrade soils in a wet condition prior to placement of the
12 inch structural fill layer Daily sprinkling may be required depending on
the rainfall conditions

Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork areas within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed Vegetation debris deleterious material and other
unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed properly off site or stockpiled
in a designated area to reduce the potential for contamination of excavated
materials
Soft and yielding areas encountered during clearing and grubbing should be
over excavated to expose firm natural material and the resulting excavation should
be backfilled with well compacted engineered fill The excavated soft soils may be
used as fill provided it meets fill material requirements
Prior to filling the existing ground should be scarified to a depth of 10 inches
moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction For pavement
subgrades the minimum relative compaction should be 95 percent Where
shrinkage cracks are observed after subgrade compaction we recommend the soil
be thoroughly moistened to close cracks or prepared again as recommended
above Saturation and subsequent yielding of the exposed subgrade due to
inclement weather and poor drainage may require over excavation of the soft areas
and replacement with well compacted engineered fill The excavated soft soils may
be used as fill provided it meets fill material requirements Relative compaction

Fills and Backfills
General fill material should consist of low expansive materials less than 3 inches in
largest dimension with a CBR swell of less than 1 percent The fill material should
also be free of vegetation and deleterious materials Our laboratory test results
indicated a low to moderate swell potential for the on site clayey silt material
Therefore most of the on site clayey material may generally be suitable for fill
material within 1 foot of finish subgrade below slabs on grade
Base course and select borrow subbase material should consist of crushed basalt
aggregate and should conform to the City and County of Honolulu Department of
Public Works Standard Specifications for Public Works Construction dated
September 1986
Structural fill should consist of non expansive granular material such as crushed
coral basalt or cinder sand The material should be well graded from coarse to fine
with no particles larger than 3 inches in largest dimension and should contain
between 10 and 30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve The material
should have a laboratory CBR value of 20 or more and should have a maximum
swell of less than 1 percent when tested in accordance with ASTM D 1883
Geolabs should test imported fill materials for conformance with these
recommendations prior to delivery to the project site for its intended use
Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
Fills and backfills should be moisture conditioned to about 2 percent above the
optimum moisture placed in level lifts not exceeding 12 inches in loose thickness
and compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction
Base course and select borrow subbase materials should be moisture conditioned
to above the optimum moisture placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose
thickness and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a
percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance
with ASTMD 1557 test procedures Optimum moisture is water content
percentage by dry weight corresponding to maximum dry density
Compaction should be accomplished by sheepsfoot rollers vibratory rollers
multiple wheel pneumatic tired rollers or other types of acceptable compaction
equipment
Because moisture conditioning and compaction of the clayey subgrade soils are
critical elements of earthwork Geolabs should perform observations and soil

Excavation
Based on our field exploration utility installation excavation may involve cuts into
the underlying fill and residual soil deposit consisting of clayey silts with some
gravel It is anticipated that fill and residual soils may be excavated with normal
heavy excavation equipment However isolated remnant boulders may be
encountered which may require over excavation for their removal or breaking to
extend the trench
The above discussions regarding surface material rippability are based on field data
obtained from the borings performed at the site We recommend contractors
bidding on this project be encouraged to examine the site conditions and soil data
to make their own interpretation
Cut and Fill Slopes
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered in the borings it appears that cut
slopes would expose soils consisting of medium stiff to hard clayey silts In general
we believe cut slopes into the soil material may be cut at an inclination of
two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V or flatter
Fill slopes should be designed with an inclination of 2H 1V or flatter Fills placed on
slopes steeper than 5H 1V should be keyed and benched into the existing slope to
provide stability of the new fill against sliding The filling operations should start at
the lowest point and continue up in level horizontal compacted layers in accordance
with the above fill placement recommendations
We recommend fill slopes in excess of 5 feet in height be constructed by overfilling
and cutting back to the design slope ratio to obtain a well compacted slope face
Track rolling of slopes in excess of 5 feet in height should not be accepted In the
event over cutting of a slope occurs keying and benching requirements should be
implemented instead of backfilling the slope to the design grade with sliver fills
Water should be diverted away from the tops of slopes and slope planting should
be provided as soon as possible to reduce the potential for erosion of the finished
slopes

If soft and or loose materials are encountered at the bottom of footing excavations

underlying firm materials
Foundations located next to utility trenches or easements should be embedded
below a 1H 1V imaginary plane extending upward from the bottom edge of the utility
trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility lines This requirement is necessary to avoid
surcharging adjacent below grade structures with additional structural loads and to reduce
the potential for foundation settlement
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive earth pressure
acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system and by frictional resistance
developed between the foundation bottom and the bearing soil Resistance due to passive
earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 psf This
assumes that the soil around the footings is relatively undisturbed or well compacted
Unless covered by pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the upper 12 inches of
soil should be neglected For footings bearing on properly compacted fill or on site stiff silty

Slabs On Grade
Based on our field exploration the project site is underlain by near surface
clayey soils with moderate to high expansion potential when subjected to moisture
fluctuations To reduce the potential for future distress to lightly loaded slabs on grade
resulting from shrinking and swelling of the clayey soils due to changes in the moisture
content we recommend subgrade soils below the buildings be properly prepared and
capped with at least 12 inches of structural fill material capping fill below the slab
cushion
The non expansive select granular capping fill should be placed as soon as
possible after the subgrade soils are prepared in accordance with the recommendations
presented in the Site Preparation subsection of this report The completed capping fill
and the underlying subgrade soils should be maintained at above optimum moisture
content prior to concrete placement Where shrinkage cracks are observed after
subgrade compaction we recommend soils be thoroughly moistened to close the
cracks Saturation and subsequent yielding of the exposed subgrade due to inclement
weather and poor drainage may require over excavation of the soft areas and
replacement with weil compacted engineered fill
In general we recommend a minimum 4 inch thick layer of cushion fill consisting
of No 3 Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation be placed below the slab to serve
as a capillary break and to provide more uniform support of the slab To reduce the
potential for moisture infiltration and subsequent damage to floor coverings an
impervious moisture barrier is recommended on top of the No 3 Fine gravel
The thickened edges of slab adjacent to unpaved areas should be embedded at
least 24 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade Areas adjacent to the slab
should be backfilled with on site clayey soil tightly against the slab and graded to divert
water away from the slabs in order to reduce the potential for water ponding around the
slabs and foundations

Utilities
In general for support of the utility lines we recommend granular bedding
consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation be used
under the pipes The initial backfill up to about 1 foot above the pipes should consist of
free draining backfills such as No 3B Fine gravel to reduce the potential for damaging
the pipes from compaction of the backfill It is critical that a free draining granular
material be used to reduce the potential for void formation below the haunches of pipes
and to provide adequate support for the sides of the pipes Use of on site soils as
backfill immediately around utility pipes is not recommended
The upper portion of the trench backfill from a level of 1 foot above the pipes to
the finish subgrade should consist of the approved on site silty clay soils or select
granular material The backfill material should be moisture conditioned to at least
2 percent above the optimum moisture content placed in level lifts not exceeding
8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction to reduce the potential for future ground subsidence The upper 2 feet of
trench backfill below the pavement subgrade should be compacted to not less than
95 percent relative compaction Mechanical compaction equipment should be used to
compact the materials Water tamping jetting or ponding should not be used to
compact the backfill material
