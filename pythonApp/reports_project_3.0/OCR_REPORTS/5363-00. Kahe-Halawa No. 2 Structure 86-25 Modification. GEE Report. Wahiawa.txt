1 1 Introduction
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the HECO Kahe Halawa 2 Structure 86 25 Modification project located
in the Waiawa area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of
the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings and recommendations resulting from our
field exploration laboratory testing and engineering analyses These recommendations
are intended for the design of new power pole foundations only The findings and
recommendations presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this
report
1 2 Project Considerations
The proposed project involves the installation of new power poles for the
modification of the existing Kahe Halawa 2 Structure 86 25 in the Waiawa
Pacific Palisades area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii Based on the information
provided the five existing power poles will be replaced with four new power poles
We understand that the 25 L and 86 L power poles will be 42 feet in height while
25 R and 86 R power poles will be 75 feet in height We also understand that the new
power poles will be self supporting with no guy wires for lateral support Due to the
moment demands resulting from wind loads we envision that a deep foundation system
will be required for support of the new power poles planned Because the project site is
located in a remote mountainous area the new power pole foundations will be
excavated by portable hand operated equipment

2 1 Regional Geology
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lava from two shield
volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older Waianae Volcano is estimated to be middle
to late Pliocene in age and forms the bulk of the western one third of the island The
younger Koolau Volcano is estimated to be late Pliocene to early Pleistocene in age
and forms the majority of the eastern two thirds of the island The Waianae Volcano
became extinct while Koolau Volcano was still active and its eastern flank was partially
buried below Koolau lava banking against its eastern flank These banked or ponded
lava formed a broad plateau referred to as the Schofield Plateau
The project site is located on the southwestern flank of Koolau mountain range
The project area is composed of basaltic rock built by the extrusion of lava of the Koolau
Volcanic Series These rocks are generally characterized by flows of jointed dense
vesicular basalt rock formation with inter bedded thin clinker layers In situ chemical
weathering of the Koolau lava flows has occurred for the last 1 to 2 million years The
weathering process has formed a mantle of residual soils which grade to saprolite with
depth In general saprolite is composed mainly of silty material and is typical of the
tropical weathering of volcanic rocks The saprolite grades to basaltic rock formation
with increasing depth

Uplift Load Resistance
In general uplift loads may be resisted by a combination of the dead weight of the
cast in place concrete shaft and by shear along the shaft surface and the adjacent
soils Considering that the shafts are designed based on adhesion between the
shaft and the surrounding soils we believe that an ultimate uplift capacity of
300 kips per shaft may be used in the design The uplift load capacity provided
includes the weight of the cast in place concrete shaft
The ultimate uplift load capacities provided above should be used for transient
loads only For sustained uplift loads acting on the foundations the ultimate uplift
load capacities should be reduced by a factor of safety of 2 0 The project structural
engineer should check the structural capacity of the shaft member in tension
Lateral Load Resistance
Lateral loads imposed on the power pole foundations may be resisted by the lateral
load capacity of the cast in place concrete shafts In general lateral load resistance
of the shafts is a function of the stiffness of the surrounding soil the stiffness of the
shaft allowable deflection at the top of shaft and induced moment in the shaft In
general we recommend that the shafts be spaced at a minimum of 2 5 times the
diameter of the shaft from center to center Therefore the effect of group action
was considered in our lateral load analyses by including an efficiency factor in the
direction of loading
The lateral load analyses were performed using the program LPILE plus for
Windows which is a microcomputer adaptation of a finite difference laterally
loaded cast in place concrete shaft program originally developed at the University
of Texas at Austin The program solves for deflection and bending moment along a
cast in place concrete shaft under lateral loads as a function of depth The analysis
was carried out with the use of non linear p y curves to represent soil moduli The
lateral deflection was then computed using the appropriate soil moduli at various
depths

In general the performance of cast in place concrete shafts depends significantly
upon the contractor s method of installation and construction procedures The
following conditions would have a significant effect on the effectiveness and cost of
the cast in place concrete shaft foundations
The load bearing capacities of shafts depend to a significant extent on the
frictional resistance between the shaft and the surrounding soils Therefore proper
construction techniques especially during the excavation operations are important

In general saprolite is a weathered product of native basalt formation Therefore

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by drilling and
sampling two borings to depths of approximately 41 5 feet below the existing ground
surface The borings were drilled using a skid mounted drill rig equipped with
continuous flight augers at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by
testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified
Soil Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representations of the
materials encountered are presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 and A 2
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained from the borings drilled in
general accordance with ASTM D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving
a 3 inch OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches In
addition some samples were obtained from the borings drilled in general accordance
with ASTM D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a
2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same hammer and drop The blow
counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an 18 inch drive
are shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Boring at the appropriate
sample depths
Pocket penetrometer tests were performed on selected cohesive soil samples
retrieved in the field The pocket penetrometer test provides an indication of the
unconfined compressive strength of the soil sample Results of the pocket penetrometer
tests performed on selected samples are presented on the Logs of Borings at the
appropriate sample depths

Laboratory Testing
Moisture Content ASTM D 2216 and Unit Weight ASTM D 2937
determinations were performed on selected soil samples to aid in the classification and
evaluation of soil properties The results of these tests are presented on the Logs of
Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Two Atterberg Limits tests ASTM D 4318 were performed on selected soil
samples to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits and to aid in soil classification The test
results are summarized on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Graphic presentations of the test results are provided on Plate B 1
One Unconfined Compression test ASTM D 2166 was performed on selected
soil sample to evaluate the unconfined compressive strength of the cohesive materials
encountered Results of the unconfined compression test are presented on Plate B 2
