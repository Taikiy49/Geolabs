INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the proposed Pahoa Elementary School at Pahoa
Puna on the island of Hawaii

The general location of the project site is shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1 The proposed layout of the new elementary school
is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
This report summarizes our findings from the exploration and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analyses based
on the data obtained during the exploration These recommendations are
intended for design input and are not intended for construction estimation
or bidding purposes

subsurface conditions at the proposed building areas and the sewage plant

site grading building foundations and the effluent disposal system

below the existing ground surface

in five 5 of the borings to obtain data pertinent to the soil
absorption characteristics of the on site soils

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located on the northerly side of the Kahakai
Boulevard in Pahoa Puna on the island of Hawaii
The site is bounded to the east by one 1 story residential
buildings and to the north and west by existing open field
Subsurface Conditions
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by
drilling and sampling twenty 20 borings to depths ranging between 10 0
and 15 0 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate
locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general the borings encountered relatively competent basaltic
rock formation to the maximum depths explored A very thin surface layer
of 0 to 0 5 foot thick of black organic silt was encountered overlying the
GEOLABS HAWAI

basalt formation The lava flow formation encountered at the project site
appears to have been in the pahoehoe form and resulted from different
flows with thickness varying from about 1 to 5 feet Flow contacts baked

Subsurface voids cavities lava tube were also encountered in some of
the borings drilled

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
It is our opinion that the proposed project is feasible from a
geotechnical engineering viewpoint The basalt rock formation encountered
at shallow depths below the existing ground surface of the project site
should provide adequate bearing for the proposed structures and related
site improvement work However due to the potential existence of
cavities within the volcanic rock we recommend that a program of probing
and grouting at foundation locations be conducted during construction
This is discussed in a later section of this report
Site Grading
Prior to the commencement of any earthwork all areas within the
grading limits should be cleared of vegetation and deleterious materials
The resulting grub and spoil material should be disposed of properly
off site or stockpiled for use in landscaping Any soft weak or
yielding areas disclosed during the clearing operations within the
portions of the grading limits designated to receive fill should be
over excavated to firm ground and the resulting excavation should be
backfilled with well compacted engineered fill
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2391 00 Page 6
The on site basalt rock material if reduced to generally less than 6
inches in size should be a source of adequate fill material Imported
fill material if required should consist of non expansive select
granular materials less than 3 inches in maximm size with less than 20
passing the 200 sieve
Any fills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding twelve 12
inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 maximm dry
density as established by ASTM Test Method D 1557 73 Structural
backfills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding eight 8 inches
in loose thickness and compacted to a minimm of 903 maximm dry density
as established by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Slopes in dense rock formation may be cut to a ratio of one half
horizontal to one vertical Slopes in fractured basalt may be
cut to a ratio of one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V Care should be
exercised to avoid overcutting of the slopes
Fill slopes should be placed at a ratio of two horizontal to one
vertical 2H 1V Fill slopes should be constructed by overfilling and
cutting back to the design slope
Excavation
Due to the nature of the rock formation encountered at the site it
is anticipated that some hard excavation e g hoerams and or chipping
will be required for cuts foundation structure excavations or trenches
that encounter dense rock formation
Shoring and or bracing of the excavations if any should conform to
the applicable safety requirements The design of the shoring bracing
should be left to the discretion of the contractor performing the work
However shallow excavations in dense rock formation normally can stand
unsupported for temporary construction purposes

Foundation Support
We recommend that the structure foundations should bear on the
in situ basalt or fill placed in accordance with the recommendations
presented herein For the design of foundations bearing on the in situ
basalt an allowable bearing capacity of 6 000 p s f may be utilized
For foundations bearing on fill composed of the on site excavated rock
less than 6 inches in maximum size or select borrow compacted to 90
maximum dry density as established by ASIM Test Method D 1557 78 an
allowable bearing capacity of 3 000 p s f may be utilized
These values may be increased by one third when temporary wind or
seismic loads are considered
Foundations should be embedded a minimm of eighteen 18 inches
below the lowest adjacent outside grade
If any soft or weak areas are encountered at the bottom of the
footing excavation they should be remedied by over excavation of the soft
and or weak materials down to in situ basalt and backfilling of the
resultant void with fill material compacted in accordance with the above
recommendations
Anticipated footing settlements would be negligible if the above
recommendations are followed

Cavity Probing
lava flows often contain tubes or cavities Several of the borings
encountered small cavities therefore to reduce the possibility of
footing settlement due to possible occurrence of cavities or loose lenses
near the footing bottom elevation drilled probes are recommended below
each column footing and at 15 foot centers for continuous wall footings
The probe holes should be a minimum of 3 inch in diameter and extend below
the bottom of the footing to a depth equal to two times the footing width
or a minimm of 10 feet The probe holes should be grouted with a
sand water cement mixture under low pressures
If a cavity or loose lens is suspected below a footing additional
probe holes should be made to delineate the horizontal extent and
thickness of the cavity or loose lens Then the cavity may be filled by
grouting through the probe holes Alternatively if a large cavity is
encountered near the ground surface it may be more economical to expose
the cavity and backfill with select fill The probing and grouting
operations should be monitored by the soils engineer to see that the
aforementioned recommendations are carried cut properly and to evaluate
other field solutions which may be required to properly backfill potential
voids and densify the loose soil which may occur near the footing
elevations
Slabs On Grade
It is anticipated that the floors of the proposed structures will be
concrete slabs on grade The slabs and cushion fill may be supported by
dense basalt or compacted fill A 4 inch layer of ASTM C 33 No 67
gravel should be provided below the slabs as cushion fill
In order to prevent future moisture infiltration and subsequent
damage to floor coverings an impervious moisture membrane is recommended
below the slabs

Sewage Treatment Plant
The first increment of the proposed Pahoa Elementary School will
include construction of an on site sewage treatment plant to handle
sanitary wastes generated at the new school It is our understanding that
the effluent disposal system of the proposed sewage treatment plant may
consist of a leaching field design or a seepag2 pit
Percolation tests were performed in Boring Nos 1 through 5 to obtain
data pertinent to the soil rock absorption characteristic of the on site
materials The test results are presented in Appendix B of this report
In general all of the borings encountered dense pahoehoe lava
flows The dense pahoehoe flows have very low permeability since the
vesicles and fractures are not continuous The exception is when
voids lava tubes are encountered then tremendous percolation rates are
observed Therefore it is apparent that the conventional method of
assigning a percolation rate gallons per square foot per day to the
formation to determine the required percolation area is not applicable to
this site The subsurface rock can accept the anticipated flow only if
cavities are encountered in the disposal trenches pits Therefore we
recommend that the disposal design should include provisions to extend the
trenches pits if sufficient cavities are not encountered during
construction It is advisable to perform field testing to determine the
capacity of the disposal system and evaluate possible effects downslope of
the site should the cavities daylight

Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comment

If this review is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the
foundation phases of the work This is to observe compliance with the
design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow design
changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from that
anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in
this report are contingent upon such observations
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
upon information obtained from the borings Variations of conditions
between the borings may occur and the nature and extent of these
variations may not become evident until construction If variations then
appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations
given in this report
The locations of the borings were determined by the drilling
contractor as specified in the drilling contract Elevations of the
borings were approximately determined by estimation from the site plan
prepared by Oda McCarty Architects Limited dated August 31 1989 The
physical location and elevation of the field data points should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
boring points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types and
as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross sections are based upon interpolation between borings
and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
Water level data from the boring points were collected at the times
given on the graphic representations and or in the text of this report
These data have been reviewed and interpretations made in the formulation
of this report However it must be noted that fluctuation may occur due
to variations in rainfall tides temperature and other factors
This report has been prepared for the exclusive use of D A G S and
their design consultants for specific application to the proposed Pahoa
Elementary School in accordance with generally accepted geotechnical
engineering principles and practices No other warranty expressed or
implied is made
