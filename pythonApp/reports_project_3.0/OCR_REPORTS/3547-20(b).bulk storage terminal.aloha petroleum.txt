Background Information
The project site is located at the intersection of Hanua and Kuhela Streets in the
Barbers Point area of the District of Ewa on the island of Oahu Hawaii as shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1 The project consists of the construction of nine new fuel
tanks with capacities ranging from 5 000 to 68 000 barrels per tank The new fuel tanks will
range in size from about 41 to 92 feet in diameter with heights of about 24 to 60 feet The
approximate locations of the fuel tanks are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The project site is generally underlain by coral formation at shallow depths Probing
and grouting of the tank footprints was required to detect potential cavities and or voids
below the tank foundations The probing and grouting operations were performed

Earthwork Observation and Testing
The earthwork operations for the project were performed by Pacific Hydrotech
Corporation At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits was
generally cleared and grubbed of vegetation and unsuitable materials Materials
generated from the clearing operations were disposed of properly Following the clearing
and grubbing operations the exposed subgrades at the tank locations were proof rolled a
minimum of six passes utilizing a vibratory sheepsfoot compactor about 12 tons to
detect near surface cavities and or voids Based on our observation of the subgrade
proof rolling operations significant cavities and or voids were not evident at the tank
subgrades
The fills and backfills needed for the earthwork generally consisted of on site
materials generated from the excavations and select fill material imported from Makakilo
Quarry Samples of the fill materials utilized as backfill materials were obtained and
transported to our office for laboratory testing The maximum dry densities and optimum
moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general accordance with
American Society of Testing and Material ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified
Proctor The test results are summarized on Plate 3 of this report
During the backfill operations the backfill materials were generally placed in
near level lifts brought to above the optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of
95 percent of the maximum dry density determined in accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor Periodic field density tests were taken during the
backfill operations to confirm the degree of compaction and moisture content of the
backfill material achieved during earthwork construction
A total of 123 field density tests were performed using the nuclear probe method in
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 2922 and D 3017 The results of the
compaction tests performed during the backfilling operations are summarized on
Plates 4 1 through 4 6 of this report These tests were performed on the compacted
backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and
interpolating from on site survey stakes where available These locations and elevations
should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas
assessed to be unsatisfactory such as soft saturated and or pumping soils were
generally removed and replaced with imported select fill compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative compaction or were reworked until the specified minimum compaction
was obtained

Limitation
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site In addition our services were performed
on an on call basis to test the backfills only Therefore a large portion of the earthwork at
the site occurred without our observation It should be recognized that there are
variations in the accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to
monitor the earthwork construction and materials Our services were performed in
accordance with generally accepted standards of geotechnical engineering practice no
warranty is expressed or implied Provision of construction testing and monitoring by an
engineer is not insurance nor does it guarantee construction of any type The contractor
shall retain responsibility for the quality of the work for adhering to the plans and
specifications and for repairing defects regardless of when they are found
