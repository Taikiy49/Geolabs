This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations performed during
the mass grading pad grading utility installation and road construction operations from

Project Considerations
The project site is located near the current end of Palahia Street in the Makakilo area
on the Island of Oahu Hawaii as shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The site is
bounded by Palahia Street to the north and east and Palailai Gulch to the south and west as
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 A geotechnical engineering exploration was performed by
Ernest K Hirata Associates Inc dated
The project consists of the construction of 128 single family homes and five
asphalt paved roads and associated utilities including water sewer electrical telephone
cable and storm drain lines Site grading involved cuts up to 15 feet deep and fills up to
30 feet high relative to the existing ground surface The tallest constructed slope has a slope
inclination of two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V and is about 60 feet in height with three
8 foot wide benches located at the 15 30 and 45 foot heights
Based on our field reconnaissance and laboratory testing we understand that the
surface soils at the site range from slightly to moderately expansive Based on our
laboratory swell test results we have categorized the on site soils into three general types
as described below
Type 1 Low expansion soils with a swell value of 0 to 3 9
Type 2 Moderate expansion soils with a swell value of 4 0 to 8 9
Type 3 High expansion soils with a swell value of over 9 0

Site Grading
The earthwork operations for the project were performed by Royal Contracting
Company At the on set of earthwork areas within the contract grading limits were generally
cleared and grubbed of vegetation and deleterious materials Materials generated from the
clearing operations were disposed of off site Following the clearing and grubbing operations
the areas to be cut were excavated Finish grades were compacted using a smooth drum
and or sheepsfoot vibratory roller
During construction representative soil samples were collected from near lot finished
grades to for swell testing Generally samples tested were remolded in 2 4 inch diameter by
1 inch high rings They were air dried overnight and then saturated for 24 hours under a
surcharge pressure of 110 psf Based on the testing results lots were categorized as
Types 1 2 or 3 and summarized on Plate 3 of this report It should be noted that Lot Nos 53
through 58 60 and 61 were classified as Type 3 originally Two feet of highly expansive
materials was removed However due to lack of low expansion soil material moderate
expansive materials were used to backfill the over excavation Therefore these lots were
finally categorized as Type 2
The fills and backfills needed for the earthwork generally consisted of imported low
expansion materials and on site materials generated from the excavations The on site fill
materials generally consisted of silty and clayey soils with some gravel The expansion of the
on site fill material ranged from low to high Samples of the fill and backfill materials utilized
during construction were obtained and transported to our office for laboratory testing The
maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in
general accordance with American Society of Testing and Material ASTM Test Designation
D 1557 Modified Proctor The test results are summarized on Plates 4 1 and 4 2 of this
report

In general fill materials consisting of 6 inch minus structural fills were placed for the fills
and utility trench backfills Field density tests were performed with the nuclear density gauge
ASTM Test Designations D 2922 and D 3017 During the fill and backfill operations the
materials were generally placed in near level lifts brought to near the optimum moisture and
compacted to obtain the specified degree of compaction based on visual observations and or
field density tests Periodic field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill
operations to confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures and soil
conditions were noted
Boulder fill was used in the deep fill areas at Lot Nos 16 through 22 and 121 through
123 In general boulder less than 18 inches in diameter generated during site grading was
used below 2 feet of the finished grade The boulders were spread out with finer material
placed and compacted to fill the spaces between boulders
A total of 617 field density tests including re tests of failed areas were performed using
the nuclear density gauge method in general accordance with ASTM Test Designations
D 2922 and D 3017 The results of the compaction tests performed during construction of the
subject project are summarized on Plates 5 1 through 5 23 of this report Test locations and
elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes where
available These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree
implied by the methods used
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the referenced
tests it is our professional opinion that the earthwork operations for the Westhills Phase 2
Subdivision project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report were
performed in general conformance with the intent of the plans and specifications and our
recommendations In general areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were re worked to
achieve the specified minimum compaction
Limitations
This office assumes no responsibility for any alterations made to slopes or finished
grades on the subject project subsequent to our observation and testing period without our
knowledge and written acknowledgment Lot owners should be advised not to block the
surface drainage and to obtain professional consultation in the planning design and
construction of any future improvements Lot owners should also be advised not to water
excessively adjacent to the house structure
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities occurring
simultaneously on a construction site therefore some work may have occurred without our
observation However we believe that the level of earthwork observation and testing services
provided is reasonable in reducing the geotechnical risks to the project It should be
recognized that there are variations in the accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and
methods used to monitor the earthwork construction and materials Our services were
