Twenty additional test pits were excavated in the head of
the valley area at the above referenced site The test pits
were concentrated in the large drainageway area to determine
the depth and lateral upslope extent of the adobe clay
deposit The approximate locations of the recent test pits
as well as the previously excavated test pits are presented
on the Test Pit Location Plan Detailed description of the
soils encountered are presented on the attached Test Pit
Logs
Based on the test pit data the following general conclu
sions regarding the soil conditions at and beyond the pro
ject lot boundaries are presented below
l The worst soil condition thickest adobe clay
deposit is in the large central drainageway area
The adobe clay was found to be more than 20 feet
deep the limit of the backhoe and extended to the
upper property boundary
2 On the eastern side the adobe clay thins out toward
Lot 201 where rock was encountered close to the surface
note all lot numbers refer to the construction plan
lot numbers
3 Less than about 5 feet of adobe clay over rock for
mation was encountered in the area north of Lot 95 and
Kealahou Street

4 Thinner Adobe clay deposit generally about 5 to 15
feet was encountered in the smaller drainageway area
behind Lots 93 94 and 95
The above results have been presented in previous meetings
with you and Gray Hong Associates Inc Subsequently
several alternate grading schemes by Gray Hong Associates
Inc have been evaluated
Based on our field exploration and discussions with you and
Gray Hong Associates Inc the following general conclu
sions regarding the site grading development have been pre
sented
1 Buttress fill stabilization is required in the adobe
clay areas if development is desired We believe
that the existing adobe hillside area is marginally
stable due to encountering slickensided adobe
A buttress fill is recommended to stabilize the lower
portion of the slope where the houses and utilities
are planned It is assumed in our analysis that the
area above the buttress fill will remain in its
natural state and that no construction is planned
in this upper area
2 In order to minimize the size of buttress fill required
and increase the stability of the adobe deposit
cuts into the adobe should be minimized and the lower
portion of the site should be raised as much as
practicable
Guidelines for the buttress fill are presented below
l The adobe clay should be removed below the buttress
fill area down to stiff colluvial or rocky material
and be replaced with select material compacted to a
minimum of 90 of ASTM D 1557
2 The excavated adobe clay should be used in the
deeper fill areas and be capped with a minimum of
3 feet of select material
3 Select material for the buttress could be the better
on site colluvium or weathered rock or borrow from
Kamehame Ridge Subdivision

4 The buttress fill should be constructed at 2 1 H V
slopes with benches at maximum 25 foot vertical height
intervals The buttress should be extended upslope
a sufficient distance to develop the recommended L
distance indicated on the attached sketch Plate 3
In the central drainageway area the adobe was
found to be thicker than 20 feet Assuming that the
adobe layer is 25 feet thick and extends upslope for
a distance of 1000 feet an L distance of 65 feet is
required to obtain a slope stability factor of safety
equals to 1 2
At Lots 93 95 the adobe is about 5 to 15 feet thick
and may extend upslope for about 200 feet For these
lots an L distance of 40 feet is required in order
to obtain a factor of safety equals to 1 5
5 Where rock formation is encountered at shallow depths
north of Lot 95 and Kealahou Street buttress fill
may not be required
6 A chimney drain about 2 feet wide and consisting of
3B Fine gravel should be utilized at the upper end
of the buttress excavation to intercept subsurface
seepage Subdrains should be provided to allow
drainage from the chimney drain see Plate 3
It should be noted that the backhoe could not penetrate
deeper than about 20 feet and therefore was unable to
determine the total thickness of the adobe clay in the
central portion of the large drainageway An adobe thick
ness of 25 feet has been assumed in our analysis We recom
mend that this portion of the buttress be excavated first to
check the actual thickness of the adobe clay
The above buttress fill design is based on residual shear
strength analysis with parameters obtained from laboratory
direct shear testing of the adobe clay soils The para
meters used are summarized as follows
a Angle of internal friction 14 degrees
b Cohesion 100 p s f
c Wet Unit Weight 110 p c f
d Import Fill Friction Angle 40 degrees
e Import Fill Wet Unit Weight 120 p c f

Investigation Limitations
The materials encountered on the project site are considered
representative of the total area however soil materials
may vary in characteristics between test pits Since our
investigation is based on a fraction of the site materials
selective laboratory testing and analyses the conclusions
and recommendations are professional opinions These
opinions have been derived in accordance with current
standards of practice and a warranty is not expressed nor
implied
