EARTHWORK OPERATION
The project site is located within the existing Pu ohala Elementary School at
Street in the Kaneohe area on the island of Oahu Hawaii A portion of the
hillside fronting Pu ohala Elementary School on the southwest side of the main parking lot
slipped after heavy rains on The landslide has resulted in a hillside
that was bare and stood at a near vertical slope inclination Subsequent vegetation
growth in the past two years has covered the majority of the slope face at the slipped
area The top and toe areas of the hillside have an elevation difference of up to about
35 feet It was subsequently decided to construct a keystone retaining wall along the
slipped area to stabilize the existing hillside from further potential slippage
The earthwork operation for the keystone retaining wall construction was
performed by HaRon Construction Inc The earthwork operation consisted of clearing
and grubbing over excavation and removal of loose soils and unsuitable materials
preparation of the keystone retaining wall foundations placement and compaction of
select borrow subbase below the reinforced fill zone and placement and compaction of
reinforced and retained fills behind the keystone retaining wall

During the earthwork operation samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor
and the test results are summarized on Plate 1 of this report
Following the clearing operations the existing ground was_ generally
over excavated to approximately 2 feet below the leveling mortar bed of the keystone
retaining wall to provide the 2 foot thick select borrow subbase under the reinforced fill
zone The over excavated subgrades were generally moisture conditioned to at least
2 percent above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of
95 percent of the maximum dry density as determined in accordance with American
Society of Testing and Material ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor
During placement of the reinforced and retained fill material the fill materials were
generally placed in near level lifts brought to above the optimum moisture and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density determined in
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor
Periodic field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations at
approximately 2 foot vertical interval to confirm our field observations as variations in
construction procedures and soil conditions were noted A total of 91 field density tests
including retests of failed areas were performed using the nuclear probe method in
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 2922 The results of the compaction tests
performed are summarized on Plates 2 1 through 2 5 of this report These tests were
performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed engineered fills and
backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by taping and
interpolating from on site survey stakes and construction plans These locations and
elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods
used Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum
compaction was obtained and or approved by our field representative based on visual
observation and hand probing
Based on the observations of our field representative and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the earthwork operation during the keystone
retaining wall construction at the above referenced site observed and tested by
Geolabs Hawaii in the period covered by this report were performed in general
accordance with the intent of the project plans and specifications and our geotechnical
engineering recommendations presented in the referenced report dated
and subsequent recommendations

LIMITATIONS

occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork may

risks to the project It should be recognized that there are variations in the accuracy and
