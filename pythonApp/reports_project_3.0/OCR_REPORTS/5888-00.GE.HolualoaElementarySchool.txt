Dear Mr Bumgardner
This letter report presents the results of the percolation test performed for the
new wastewater disposal system at the Holualoa Elementary School Our work was
performed in general accordance with the scope of services outlined in our fee proposal
dated May 8 2007
Project Considerations
Holualoa Elementary School is in the Holualoa area in the District of North Kona
on the Island of Hawaii We understand an injection well is planned for the restroom
facilities at the school We understand the State of Hawaii Department of Health
requires performance of a percolation test at the specific site to evaluate the suitability
of the site location for use of an individual wastewater system Therefore it was desired
to conduct field percolation testing at the leaching field location
Percolation Test
One field percolation test was performed at the proposed injection well area for
Holualoa Elementary School The field percolation test was performed in general
accordance with the State of Hawaii Department of Health s Administrative Rules
Standards to evaluate the permeability of the subsurface soils for disposal of
wastewater effluent
The percolation test was performed in an approximately 4 inch diameter hole of
about 11 5 feet deep Approximately 1 inch of gravel was then placed at the bottom of
the borehole to protect the bottom of the borehole from scouring and sediments During
testing the test hole was carefully filled with water to a depth of about 6 inches above
the gravel layer and refilled as necessary The time intervals and water drops were
recorded to provide data upon which the calculation of the percolation test was based
