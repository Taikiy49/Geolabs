PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of the geotechnical exploration conducted was to obtain an overview
of the surface and subsurface conditions at the project site The subsurface information
obtained was utilized to evaluate the vertical and lateral extents of the basalt rock
formation within the two large cuts The scope of our work for this exploration consisted of
drilling and sampling four test borings including rock core sampling and performing
thirty eight 38 test probes across the project area The scope of work also included
preparing this geotechnical field data report summarizing our work and presenting our
findings
Detailed description of the field exploration methodology and the Logs of Borings
are presented in Appendix A of this report Individual logs of the anticipated subsurface
materials based on the test probe drilling are presented in Appendix B

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the large cut from approximately Sta No 495 00 to
Sta No 505 00 of the Saddle Road PTA 1 Section Phase 1 project were explored by
drilling and sampling four borings designated as Boring Nos 201 through 204 The
borings extended to depths ranging from about 24 to 40 feet below the existing ground
surface Because the core barrel was jammed in Boring No 204 at a depth of about
24 feet below the ground surface an additional boring Boring No 205 was drilled at a
location 5 feet away from Boring No 204 to supplement the information in Boring No 204
The borings were drilled using a truck mounted drill rig equipped with continuous flight
augers and HQ wireline coring tools as appropriate The locations of the borings drilled
are summarized in the table in the text of this report
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representations of the materials
encountered are provided on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 5
Soil samples were obtained from the borings drilled in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by
driving a 2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same hammer and drop The
blow counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an 18 inch drive
are shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate
sample depths
Core samples of the rock formations encountered at the project site were obtained
using diamond core drilling techniques in general accordance with ASTM Standard
Practice D 2113 Diamond Core Drilling for Site Investigation Core drilling is a rotary
drilling method that uses a hollow bit to cut into the rock formation The material left in the
hollow core of the bit is mechanically recovered for examination and classification
Recovery REC is used as a subjective guide to the interpretation of the relative
quality of rock masses Recovery is defined as the actual length of material recovered
from a coring attempt versus the length of the core attempt For example if 4 feet of
material is recovered from a 5 foot core run the recovery would be 80 percent and would
be shown on the Logs of Borings as REC 80
The Rock Quality Designation RQD is also a subjective guide to the relative
quality of rock masses RQD is defined as the percentage of the core run that is sound
material in excess of 4 inches in length without any discontinuities discounting any
drilling induced fractures or breaks If 2 5 feet of sound material is recovered from a
