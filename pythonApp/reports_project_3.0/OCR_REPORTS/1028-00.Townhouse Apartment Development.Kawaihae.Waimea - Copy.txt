SUMMARY
The field exploration and laboratory
testing disclosed competent foundation
bearing soils at the project site
Shallow spread footing foundations can
be used for the proposed structures
An allowable soil bearing value up to
3500 p s f can be used for foundations
bearing on the stiff natural ground in
cut areas and 2500 p s f for compacted
fill
The text of this report should be
referred to for detailed and special
design recommendations
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this soil investigation was to evaluate the
subsurface soil conditions at the two sites in order to
develop soil data for site planning and design recommenda
tions for grading and foundation construction Percolation
tests were also made in three of the drilled holes to eval
uate the seepage characteristic of the underlying rock for
sewage disposal
The approximate boring locations are shown on the Site Plan
Plates 2 3 and the appropriate logs are presented in

explorations and laboratory testing are presented in the

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site consists of two 2 acre plus parcels located
on both sides of the existing Hawaii Prepatory Academy and
along the northern side of Kawaihae Road in Waimea The site
is relatively flat and drains down toward Kawaihae Road
At the time of our field exploration there were several houses
sheds and a Youth Center on the site Access into the site
is by small gravel roads and driveways
The area is covered with tall grass many large trees and
shrubs
The ground elevation at the sites varies from about 2645 to
2660 feet
PROPOSED DEVELOPMENT
Small clusters of 2 story wood framed residential structures
are planned for both parcels A basement parking level
approximately 5 feet below the existing ground is planned
below most of the building area Access roads from Kawaihae
Road along with the necessary parking areas will be required
for each parcel

Minor site grading is anticipated
SUBSURFACE EXPLORATION
Twelve 6 inch borings were made for each parcel ranging in
depths from 12 to 45 feet The borings were made by utiliz
ing a truck mounted rotary hydraulic drill rig Soil samples
were obtained from each boring for visual and laboratory
testing Two 2 bulk surface samples were also obtained
from the site for plasticity index CBR and other laboratory
tests
SOIL CONDITIONS
The borings encountered a surface soil layer about 3 5 to
10 feet thick consisting of stiff brown clayey silt This
material is derived from the drying and weathering of volcanic
ash
Below this upper layer was a stiff brown clayey silt and
weathered gravel layer extending to about 9 to 15 feet below
the ground surface This material consisted of a mixture of
the volcanic ash and weathered rock or clinker
Dense basalt lava formation was encountered at depths of 9 to
15 feet continuing down to the depths drilled

General
The field exploration disclosed basically competent founda
tion bearing soils at the proposed project site From a soil
engineering point of view the site is suitable for the pro
posed residential development
Shallow foundations can be used for the proposed structures
The near surface soils have low swelling potential and there
for slab on grade construction can also be utilized
Foundations
Spread or continuous footing foundations are recommended for
the structures on level or gently sloping areas
An allowable bearing value up to 3500 p s f is recommended
for footings bearing on stiff upper in situ silty soil or the
underlying gravel layer For footings bearing on compacted
structural fill a bearing value of 2500 p s f may be used to
design the footings The above values are for total dead
plus live loads and may be increased by one third when temp
orary loads are considered

on the order of less than half an inch

Site Grading
Relatively small cuts and fills are anticipated for the site
The site should be cleared of vegetation old foundations

PERCOLATION TESTING
A sewage treatment plant and injection well disposal system
is planned for the development
Three percolation tests were performed in and 7
to evaluate the suitability of the underlying rock formation
for injection well disposal
During the rock coring in these borings the wash water return
was monitored to detect porous zones which could provide suit
able percolation
The upper portions of the rock formation was found to be very
dense and appeared unsuitable for waste disposal However a
weathered and fractured zone was encountered at about 37 to
42 foot depths The borings continued and extended 2 to 3
feet into this fractured zone prior to percolation testing
The percolation testing consisted of pumping water from a
fire hydrant into the drilled holes and observing the increase
in water level or head in the bored hole Inflow rates of
200 g p m were utilized in Boring Nos 1 and 2
At Boring No 7 a longer distance to the fire hydrant required
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1028 00
P 9
extending the water hose across an existing street therefore
a smaller hose was used to reduce traffic interference This
smaller diameter hose limited the inflow rate to 50 g p m
In each hole the percolation test was run for an hour and the
inflow water was dissipated without an appreciable build up
of the existing water level
Based on our test results it is our opinion that the porous
fractured rock zone encountered below 37 to 42 foot depths
could provide suitable percolation for injection well disposal
However it should be noted that periodic flushing and surge
pumping inside the injection well is recommended to maintain
the effluent percolation

through A 15

sample depths

Nx core barrel The core recovery and rock quality designation
