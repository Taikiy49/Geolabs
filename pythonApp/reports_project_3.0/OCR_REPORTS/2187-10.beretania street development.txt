INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the proposed 12 story condominium at 1953 South
Beretania in the McCully area of metropolitan Honolulu on the island of
Oahu Hawaii The general location of the site and its vicinity is show
on the Project Location Map Plate 1 Our work on this project was
performed in general accordance with our proposal to Beta Development
Company dated and subsequent proposals for additional
borings to further delineate the extent location of the underlying buried
alluvial channel and field grouting to check the cement grout take at the
channel location
This report summarizes and presents our finding and recommendations
derived from analyses based upon data obtained during our explorations
The discussions and recommendations are intended for design input and may
not be adequate for use as a basis for construction estimation or bidding
purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It was proposed to develop the subject parcel as a high rise luxury
residential condominium The proposed development would have consisted of
a 12 story apartment building with one level of basement parking and a two
level underground parking structure The basement level floors would be
slabs on grade
No grading plans were available at the time that this report was
prepared However it is anticipated that site grading will be minimal
with the exception of the basement excavation

Regional Geology
The island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from
two shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau during the late Pliocene and
Pleistocene epochs After the main shield building phases of the two
volcanoes the island began to erode
Following an extended period of erosion volcanic activity was
renewed on Koolau These post erosional volcanics were comprised
predominantly of pyroclastic deposits e g ash and cinder
Concurrent with erosional period and the renewed volcanism sea level
fluctuated in response to the widespread glaciation of the Pleistocene
Ice Age As the great continental glaciers grew and advanced sea level
fell because there was less water available to fill the ocean basins
When the glaciers melted and retreated sea levels rose to or above the
current level
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2187 10 Page 5
When sea levels were low the land eroded at elevations below the
present sea level Rivers extended further to the sea and their bottoms
were cut to below current sea level
When the sea levels rose sediments accumulated at elevations higher
than the current base level Often drainage channels became choked and
filled with sediments or buried
The project site is situated on the Honolulu Coastal Plain which is
a thick wedge of interbedded alluvium marine deposits and volcanics built
on to the southern flank of Koolau Evidence indicates that the site and
its vicinity have been traversed by buried drainageways originating the
Manoa Makiki Pauoa area
In the early part of this century the vicinity of the project site
was low lying agricultural land As the area became urbanized the land
was graded to suit the needs of urban development

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on our field exploration and analyses it is our opinion that
the loose sand and gravel soils encountered in the alluvial channel that
appears to traverse the site are not adequate to support the foundation
loads of the proposed 12 story condominium building on either spread
footing foundations or on a mat foundation without inducing excessive
structural settlements
Generally it would appear that pile foundations are required to
support the proposed structure However since the poor subsoil
conditions appear to occur in a relatively restricted portion of the
project it is our opinion that these soils may be sufficiently improved
by injecting stiff mortar like cement grout so that the use of a mat
foundation would be feasible
Therefore we are presenting discussions and recommendations about
both pile foundations and the use of grouting as a method of subsoil
improvement
Alternately the development plan could be altered to a smaller
lighter structure with half or no basement Such a structure on the
order of five 5 to six 6 stories in height could take advantage of
the twenty or so feet of surface crust encountered at the site and utilize
spread footing foundations for support

Pile Foundations
Since stiff alluvial clay soils were encountered in all of the
drilled borings at a depth of about 40 feet below the existing ground
surface it is our opinion that pile foundations developing vertical
support from shaft adhesion in the stiff clay and partial end bearing
against the tip of the pile could be used to support the proposed
condominium building We recommend the use of sixteen and a half
inch octagonal prestressed concrete piles with a minimm tip
elevation of about 60 feet below the existing ground surface
Based on our field exploration and analyses it is our opinion that
such piles driven to a minimum tip elevation of sixty 60 feet below the
existing surface with a hammer delivering a minimm rated energy of at
least 24 000 foot pounds for a minimm of 40 blows per foot for the last
foot will develop an allowable total vertical capacity of sixty 60 tons
each Pile total settlements on the order of about one half of an inch
are estimated
To resist lateral loads on the proposed structures the passive earth
pressure around the pile caps and tie beams may also be used To utilize
the passive soil resistance the space around the pile caps or tie beams
should be properly backfilled with select material compacted to at least
95 of its maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 An
equivalent fluid pressure of 300 p s f per foot of depth of the
caps beams may be used to calculate the passive soil resistance
A test pile program should be implemented to fulfill the following
objectives
1 To examine the drivability of piles using one or more selected
hammers
2 To verify that predrilling through the surficial soil layers is
not required

Injection Grouting
Injection grouting is a method of ground modification that involves
the injection of cement or chemical grouts into the subsoils to improve
the in situ strength or other characteristics of the subsoils The
improvement is achieved either by permeation of the grout into the voids
between the soils particles resulting in a cementation of the soil mass or
by the displacement of soil particles by the grout to effectively compact
the soil mass
Based on our exploration it is our opinion that the very loose sand
soils encountered in the alluvial channel traversing the site could be
sufficiently improved by injection grouting to allow the use of a mat
foundation to support the proposed building However the preferred
method of grouting the type of soils encountered would be the method
referred to as compaction grouting In compaction grouting very stiff
mortar like grouts are injected into the subsoils at relatively high
pressures This injection creates a bulb of grout which displaces the
loose soil and densifies it The equipment required for this method is
not readily available in Hawaii
The normal local grouting technique is to inject cement slurries at
high pressures to simultaneously displace and cement the subsoils
At the project site we performed a series of cement slurry grout
injection tests in Boring Nos 3 and 12 to evaluate if this technique
would be an optimum method of injection grouting to utilize at the site
for the subsoil conditions encountered and the locally available
equipment
Boring Nos 3 and 12 were packed off at a depth of about 25 feet with
an open grout standpipe leading to the underlying loose sand and gravel
formation Approximately 3 1 cubic yards of a stiff neat cement grout
W C were placed during two 2 trial injections No substantial
increase in pumping pressure was noted during either trial
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2187 10 Page 10
Subsequent to each trial an additional probe was drilled next to
Boring No 12 to observe the effects of the grout injection Samples were
also taken from these probes for visual examination to see if the slurry
was penetrating the formation
The injection pressure observations the high grout takes and the
samples indicate that the neat cement slurry tended to penetrate the loose
subsoils rather than to displace then Therefore it appears that
compaction grouting is not feasible with the locally available grouting
equipment
It is our current opinion that to be sufficiently confident about
using grouting to densify the very loose saturated alluvial formation
injection of very stiff mortar like cement grout utilizing the
specialized mixing and pumping equipment would be needed
Mat Foundation
Based on our exploration and analyses we estimate that settlements
will be on the order of eleven 11 inches for the portion of a mat
foundation constructed over the buried channel without adequate grouting
of the loose buried channel subsoils It is also our opinion that the
settlements for the mat footing located away from the underlying channel
would be on the order of one half 1 2 to one 1 inch total
Therefore it does not appear feasible to support the proposed
12 story highrise with basement building on a continuous rigid mat
foundation without experiencing excessive differential settlements To
provide an allowable bearing value of 3 000 p s f for the design of the
mat foundation we believe costly ground modification improvements would
be required to further densify the very loose 20 foot thick buried stream
channel formation located about 20 to 40 feet below the ground surface

Alternate Development Scheme
It is our opinion that the site may be economically developed if the
development scheme is revised to a smaller lighter structure such as a

Site Grading
It is anticipated that the overall site grading for the project will
be minimal with the exception of the excavation required for the basement
parking area
Basement Excavation
Based on our exploration it is our opinion that conventional
excavation methods may be utilized for the basement excavation The
maximum depth of excavation should be limited to about 12 feet below
the existing ground surface due to the groundwater levels encountered
during our exploration
Due to the adjacent existing structures which are believed to be on
spread footing foundations near the existing ground surface it will
be necessary to either temporarily slope back the sidewalls and away
from the adjacent footings or to structurally shore the excavation
during construction The excavation sidewalls should be sloped back
a ratio of one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V or flatter with a
minimum setback of ten 10 feet between the top of the slope and the
property line Otherwise continuous sheeting driven sufficiently
deep below the basement excavation is recommended Due to the long
spans anticipated it may be necessary to use bracing to provide
lateral support to the sheeting

Prior to the commencement of any earthwork all areas within the
grading limits should be cleared of all vegetation and deleterious

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as
borings probes test pits and or observation wells Variations of
conditions between the field data points may occur and the nature and
extent of these variations may not become evident until construction If
variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations given in this report
The location of the field data points were approximately determined
by taping from the property corners Elevations of the data points were
not available at the time of our exploration The physical location and
elevation of the field data points should be considered accurate only to
the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross sections are based upon interpolation between field
data points and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
Water level data from the field data points was collected at the
times given on the graphic representations and or in the text of this
report This data has been reviewed and interpretations made in the
formulation of this report However it must be noted that fluctuation
may occur due to variations in rainfall tides temperature and other
factors

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content and unit weight determinations were performed on
selected soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of
soil properties The results of these tests are presented on the Boring
Logs at the appropriate sample depths
Four 4 one inch ring swell tests were performed on selected natural
and remolded samples to evaluate the swelling potential of the existing
near surface soils The results are summarized on Plate B 1
Four 4 sieve analysis were performed on selected soil samples to
determine their gradation characteristics The test results are
summarized on Plate B 2
Eight 8 direct shear tests were performed on selected soil samples
to evaluate their shear strength parameters The test results are
presented on Plates B 3 through B 10
Five 5 consolidation tests were performed on selected soil samples
to evaluate their compressibility The test results are plotted on Plates
B 11 thru B 15
