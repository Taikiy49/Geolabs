INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our soils engineering
investigation conducted for the proposed HECO Waiau Tunnel Source
Development at The general location of the site
is shown on Plate 1 Project Location Map
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of the subsurface investigation was to explore the soil
and groundwater conditions at the locations of the structures to
provide soil data for use in the foundation design
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1088 00 Page 2
The scope of our investigation included field reconnaissance the
drilling of 6 exploratory borings and laboratory testing of selected
soil samples Our work was done in general conformance with our
proposal dated March 20 1981
The results of our field exploration and laboratory testing which we
used in the engineering analysis to formulate our conclusions and
recommendations are presented in the appendices of this report
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
The existing water tunnel supplies cooling water for the power plant
operation It is planned to intercept and reuse some of the excess
water that presently exits into an open channel and drains off the
site In order to accomplish this several structures are required
for the operation including
1 One story L Shaped control building approximately 44
feet long with widths of 16 and 32 feet Fills up to
about 6 feet high are planned to create a level area for
the building
2 Pump Station approximately 13 by 26 feet in plan and
about 28 to 30 feet deep
3 Valve vault approximately 9 by 12 feet in plan and
approximately 17 feet deep
4 Diversion manhole approximately 6 by 6 feet in plan and
about 23 feet deep
The locations of the proposed structures are shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located in the Hawaiian Electric Company Power
Plant which is situated on the Makai side of Kamehameha Highway in
Waiau Oahu The general project location is shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1
The proposed structures are located near the entrance roadway into the
plant There are low CRM retaining walls and grassy areas adjacent to
the road
There are many existing structures in the area including a one story
visitor center building water tanks and overhead lines
There are also numerous underground utilities including a water
tunnel electrical water and telephone lines
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The project area is located on the southwest flank of the eroded
remnants of the Koolau Shield Volcano This volcanic massif was built
during the late Pliocene and early Pleistocene by thin bedded basaltic
lava flows
At the project site these basaltic flows are overlain by a thin
mantle of residual soil and alluvium or fill
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1088 00 Page 4
Field Exploration
A total of six 6 borings were drilled at the site to depths of 11 5
to 35 feet below the existing ground surface at the locations
indicated on the Site Plan Plate 2
Detailed descriptions of our field explorations and the log of
borings together with a brief description of the laboratory testing
and summary of test results are presented in the appendices of this
report
Soil Conditions
Boring Nos 1 through 4 which were drilled in the slightly lower
makai portion of the site encountered 9 to 13 5 feet of silty clay
alluvium and fill over basalt formation to the full depth of the
borings In the underlying rock formation a thin residual soil layer
was encountered at 23 to 30 foot depths indicating separate layers of
lava flows
Boring Nos 5 and 6 which were drilled in the upper portion of the
site encountered about 10 feet of stiff clayey silt fill and residual
soil over rock formation
Groundwater was encountered in the borings at elevations of 4 5 to 8
feet above MSL

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration encountered stiff fill layers or alluvium over
moderately hard basalt rock formation Relatively hard excavation is
anticipated for the proposed underground structures
Groundwater level will also be encountered in the excavation
Valve Vault Pump Station Diversion Manhole
Excavations ranging from 18 feet for the Valve Vault and Diversion
Manhole structures up to 30 feet at the Pump Station are planned
Based on the borings relatively hard excavation utilizing hoe ram
equipment is anticipated in the rock formation encountered at about 9
to 13 5 foot depths
Excavations should be done with care as there are many existing
underground utilities
Water level was encountered at elevation 4 5 to 8 feet in the
borings It should be noted that the water level may be affected by
artesian head and could vary Dewatering through deep sumps or
perimeter deep wells is anticipated for the excavations
For the foundation design of the structures an allowable bearing
value of 8000 p s f may be used on the dense basalt
GEOLABS HAWAII
Assuming at rest condition the following lateral soil pressures may
be used for the design of the structure walls
1 Upper Fill or Alluvium 50 p c f equivalent fluid pressure
2 Basalt Formation Above Water 20 p c f equivalent fluid
pressure
3 Basalt Formation Below Water 10 p c f equivalent fluid
pressure plus water pressure
As discussed above the water level may be affected by artesian head
If practicable the foundation walls should be poured directly against
the rock formation to seal off the artesian water
Control Building
Site Grading
Prior to grading all deleterious materials such as vegetation
and debris should be removed and disposed of properly off site
Prior to filling the existing ground below the proposed structure
should be scarified to a depth of 6 inches moisture conditioned
to near optimum moisture content and then compacted according to
the requirements for structural fill
The on site upper fill and alluvium from the underground structure
excavations is generally moderately expansive and should not be
used within 2 feet of the building finished grade The excavated
rock material generally less than 6 inches in size may be used for
fill
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1088 60 Page 7
It appears that additional off site borrow material may be
required for fill
The imported fill material should be free of organic material
non expansive and generally less than 6 inches in size The fill
should be placed in horizontal lifts not exceeding 6 inches in
compacted thickness and compacted to a minimum of 95 of its
maximum dry density as established by ASTM D 1557 78
The fills should be keyed and benched into the existing sloping
ground to properly bond the new fill to the natural soil
Site grading should provide drainage of surface runoff away from
all foundations
Foundation Design
Light structural loading is anticipated Therefore spread or
continuous footing foundations can be used to support the proposed
structure
Footings should be placed a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest
adjacent grade and should bear on the compacted fill
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f may be used for
foundations on the compacted fill The bearing capacity value is
for total dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third
for short duration wind and or seismic loading
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O
1088 00 Page 8
The bottom of all footings should be compacted to a minimum of 95
of its maximum dry density as established by ASTM D 1557 78 and
inspected by the soils engineer prior to concrete placement
If a footing is situated next to a utility trench it should be
extended to the bottom of the trench or the trench backfill must
be well compacted to prevent footing settlement due to settlement
of the trench backfill
Concrete slab on grade should be placed over 4 inches of
well graded aggregate base course A polyethylene vapor barrier
over 4 inches of 3B Fine gravel may be substituted for the base
course should a capillary break be deemed necessary to protect the
floor covering
Driveway
It is our understanding that a new asphaltic concrete driveway is
also planned For the light traffic anticipated and assuming
select fill material is used the recommended pavement section is
as follows
2 inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 inch Base Course
8 inch Total Pavement Thickness

Design Review
We recommend that plans and specifications for grading foundation and
utility construction be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and
written comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included in the project plans and specifications
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the filling footing excavation and backfilling
operations be observed by the soils engineer during construction
This is to see that adequate compaction has been obtained and the
subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of our
subsurface investigation The recommendations given in this report
are contingent upon the above inspections
