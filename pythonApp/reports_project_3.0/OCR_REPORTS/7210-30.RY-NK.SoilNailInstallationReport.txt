This letter report summarizes our geotechnical observation services performed 
during the installation and testing of the pre-production sacrificial and production 
grouted anchors for the Kamehameha Highway Access Improvements, Koa Ridge 
Development project located in the Waipio area on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. The 
project location and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map, Plate 1. 
In general, this letter report presents our observations and the results of the 
grouted anchor installation and testing performed on the anchors for the project from 
February 20, 2020 through May 11, 2020. Royal Contracting Co. Ltd. was the general 
contractor for the project, and Prometheus Construction was the specialty subcontractor 
responsible for installation and testing of the grouted soil/rock anchors. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The project is along Kamehameha Highway from the intersection of 
Kamehameha Highway and Ka Uka Boulevard and extends about 1,400 linear feet 
north from the intersection in the Waipio area on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. The project 
consists of roadway improvements along Kamehameha Highway to accommodate a 
new right-in-right-out intersection about 900 linear feet north of the intersection of 
Kamehameha Highway and Ka Uka Boulevard. A cut slope with a height up to 
approximately 30 feet relative to the existing ground surface is planned in the area north 
of the intersection of Kamehameha Highway and future Road “C”. Due to the steep 
finished slope condition and soil conditions encountered in our boring, the installation of 
an anchored wire mesh system was recommended between Sta. 19+00 and 
Sta. 20+00.

Geolabs, Inc. conducted a geotechnical engineering exploration of the project

GROUTED ANCHOR INSTALLATION AND TESTING 
The anchored wire mesh system consisted of 178 individual reinforcing bars 
grouted into drilled holes to secure the wire mesh and stabilize the existing slope. In 
addition, supplemental (short) anchors were installed where a smooth slope surface is 
not achieved in order to push the wire mesh against the ground to develop this smooth 
surface. Boundary ropes were used to pull the edge of the anchored wire mesh tight 
against the slope for aesthetic appearance. 
The grouted anchor installations, verification and proof tests were performed by 
Prometheus Construction. The approximate locations of the grouted soil/rock anchors are 
shown on the Soil Anchor Location Plan, Plate 2. Installation and testing of the anchors 
were conducted from February 20, 2020 to May 11, 2020. A summary of the anchor 
installations and testing are presented on Plates 3.1 through 3.7. 
Grouted Anchor Installation 
A Geolabs’ representative observed the drilling and grouting operations during 
construction of the soil/rock anchors. The anchors were drilled using a TEI Platform Rock 
Drill mounted on a CAT 315C excavator, TEI TD50 drill rig, and a TEI wagon. The TEI drill 
rig was equipped with a down-the-hole hammer and a 4-inch underreamer percussion bit. 
The anchor holes were drilled to depths of 22.0 to 23.5 feet from the front face of 
the slope facing with an inclination about 20 degrees from horizontal. After the hole was 
drilled to the specified depth, a hollow threaded galvanized bar was inserted into the drilled 
hole. Centralizers were placed along the threaded bar at 2-foot intervals. 
Eight additional anchor holes were drilled to depths of approximately 10.0 feet, 
serving as supplemental (short) anchors to provide a neat appearance for the anchored 
wire mesh system but serve no structural function. Four additional anchors, two by Anchor 
No. A-1 and two by Anchor No. A-18 were drilled with an inclination about 20 degrees from 
horizontal. The remaining four additional anchors, two by Anchor No. J-1 and two by 
Anchor No. J-18, were drilled with an inclination of approximately 20 degrees from 
horizontal. A 1.25-inch diameter Grade 75 solid threaded bar was installed in the 
supplemental anchors. 
A 1½-inch diameter high-density plastic grouting tube was inserted into the drilled 
hole along the anchor tendon. Grouting began progressively from the bottom to top 
through the grouting tube until fresh grout appeared at the top of the hole. The grouting

Grouted Anchor Testing 
In accordance with the project specifications, the verification soil/rock anchor was 
loaded to 200 percent of the design load (28.0 kips) and held for 60 minutes at 
150 percent of the design load (21.0 kips). Based on the project specifications, ten percent 
of the production anchors (18 anchors) were selected for proof testing. The production test 
anchors had at least 4 feet of unbonded length for testing. Proof test anchors were loaded 
to 150 percent of the design load (21.0 kips), and the load was maintained for 60 minutes 
to measure the creep movements. The criteria for acceptance of the grouted anchors 
consist of the following: 
For the verification tests, a total creep movement of less than 0.08 inches 
per log cycle of time between the 6 and 60-minute readings is measured 
during creep testing, and the creep rate is linear or decreasing throughout 
the creep test load hold period. 
For the proof tests, a total creep movement of less than 0.04 inches is 
measured between the 1 and 10-minute readings or a total creep 
movement of less than 0.08 inches is measured between the 6 and 
60-minute readings, and the creep rate is linear or decreasing throughout 
the creep test load hold period. 
Total measured movement at the maximum test load exceeds 80 percent 
of the theoretical elastic elongation of the test nail unbonded length. 
Pullout failure does not occur at the maximum test load.

In accordance with the project specifications, a verification test was performed on 
Soil Anchor No. T-1 on February 25, 2020. Soil Anchor No. T-1 was loaded to the 
maximum test load of approximately 28.0 kips (200 percent of the design load). The head 
movement for the soil anchor at each load increment up to the maximum test load was 
measured and recorded. After testing the soil anchor to 200 percent of the design load, the 
test anchor was unloaded in eight load decrements. The total measured head movement 
at 200 percent of the design load was about 0.165 inches. Results of the verification test 
including Load versus Displacement and the Creep Movement versus Time curves are 
presented on Plates 4.1 through 4.4. 
In accordance with the project specifications, proof tests on selected production 
anchors were performed on Soil Anchors A-12, A-16, B-8, B-15, C-1, D-4, D-17, E-7, F-10, 
F-14, G-2, G-7, H-2, H-12, I-5, I-16, J-1, and J-11 on May 6 to 8 and 11, 2020. Maximum 
total head movement measured was about 0.368 inches from Soil Anchor I-16. Results of 
the proof tests including Load versus Displacement and the Creep Movement versus Time 
curves are presented on Plates 5.A-12.1 through 5.J-11.3. 
Based on our observations, we believe that the soil/rock anchors observed by 
Geolabs have been installed in general conformance with the project plans and 
specifications and within the limits of generally accepted construction practices. Based on 
our observations of the grouted anchor tests performed by the contractor, it is our 
professional opinion that the anchors have been installed to provide sufficient load 
capacity with tolerable movements. 
LIMITATIONS 
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities 
occurring simultaneously on a construction site; therefore, some of the work for the 
grouted anchor installation and testing may have occurred without our direct 
observation.  However, we believe that the level of geotechnical observation provided is 
reasonable in reducing the geotechnical risks to the project.
