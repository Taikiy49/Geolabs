INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed sewer reconstruction project for Kaneohe Sewers Section
9 located on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location of the proposed sewer line
project is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings of our exploration and our geotechnical
engineering recommendations derived from our analyses of the data obtained during the
exploration These recommendations are intended for design input and are not intended
for preparation of construction estimates or bids
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to reconstruct the sewer lines for Kaneohe Sewers Section 9
The sewer reconstruction project is in an area bounded by Kamehameha Highway
Luluku Road and Ualani Place The proposed invert elevations will be between
approximately 128 and 213 feet MSL or at depths of about 5 to 10 feet below the
existing ground The new sewer pipes will have diameters ranging between 6 and 8
inches
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2613 00 B Page 3
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions along the proposed sewer alignments to develop a general soil data set for the
formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to the design and
construction of the new sewers
The work was done in accordance with our proposal dated
to the Department of Public Works City and County of Honolulu The scope of work was
limited to
1 Obtaining proper permits and utility clearances for our drilling operations
from the City and utility companies
2 Mobilization and demobilization of a truck mounted drill rig and a drill crew
of our firm to and from the boring locations
3 Coordination of street traffic with the aid of coning to block off working
areas at the boring locations along the streets
4 Drilling and sampling of four 4 borings along the proposed sewer
alignment to depths ranging from 13 5 to 21 5 feet below the existing
ground surface
5 Logging and sampling of the borings by a Field Engineer or Geologist of
our firm
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2613 00 B Page 4
6 Laboratory testing of selected samples obtained from the field exploration
to classify the subsoils encountered and to determine their pertinent
engineering properties
7 Engineering analyses of the field and laboratory data for the formulation of
geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to the design of the
proposed sewer line reconstruction
8 Preparation of this report summarizing our work on the project and
presenting our findings and recommendations
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration and iaboratory testing are presented
in the appendices of this report
GENERAL SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located between the Hawaiian Memorial Park Cemetery and the
Windward City Shopping Center on the western side of Kamehameha Highway which
forms the eastern boundary of the project area on the island of Oahu The general
project area is presently occupied mostly by commercial and residential buildings
The topography of the site generally slopes up towards the west with ground
elevations ranging from 120 to 290 feet above MSL
The western boundary of the project area is defined by irregular lot lines This
boundary is generally parallel to and approximately 300 feet east of Apapane Street for
the central third of its alignment or roughly 5000 feet Apapane Street in turn is roughly
parallel to and approximately one quarter mile east of Kamooalii Stream

The new sewer lines will be constructed along and traverse existing streets

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATION
General
It is Our opinion that the proposed Kaneohe Sewers may be constructed along the
proposed alignments provided our geotechnical engineering recommendations as
presented in this report are followed
Fair to poor soil conditions were encountered below the project site Groundwater
levels above the proposed invert elevations and soft to very soft clayey silt deposits were
encountered in the lowlying areas along the western boundary of the project site
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2613 00 B Page 6
Therefore proper dewatering trench shoring with interlocking steel sheet piling and
bracing will be needed in these areas to minimize lateral movement of ground with
resulting potential damage to existing facilities
Earth Pressure Loads on Pipe
Loads on buried conduits are influenced by the width of trench the size of pipes
the unit weight of backfill material and the frictional resistance between backfill material
and trench wall To calculate the vertical loads on the buried sewer pipes an average
unit weight of 120 p c f for backfill material and a coefficient of friction of 0 25 are
recommended Earth forces upon a pipe increase rapidly with the width of trench and
therefore the width of the sewer trenches should be kept to a minimum
Traffic loads on the buried pipes should also be considered since the proposed
sewer alignments are located partially within travelled ways
Settlements
Primary settlement of new sewer construction is normally caused by the difference
in the unit weight of the lighter excavated original earth and the heavier compacted backfill
material placed over the sewer structures The net increase in loading will cause
settlement of the underlying subsoils below the trench invert Based on our calculations
primary settlement of less than one half of an inch is anticipated for the project These
settlements will be generally limited to the sewer alignments underlain by softer materials
i in the vicinity of Boring Nos 18 and 20
The above estimates assume that proper construction procedures and good
workmanship will be provided Additional settlement could occur if improper trench
support or dewatering procedures are allowed
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2613 00 B Page 7
Therefore we believe that the proposed sewer lines should be designed with
sufficient flexibility to accommodate these possible settlement conditions
Alternately light weight backfill material such as cinder sand may be used to
reduce the difference in unit weights between the backfill and the in situ subsoils thereby
reducing the amount of settlement to be anticipated
Pipe Bedding
Distribution of bearing stresses against the bottom of a sewer pipe has a great
impact upon the load carrying capacity of the pipe itself Pipe bedding is therefore an
important design consideration
For pipes bearing on the medium stiff to very stiff silt and clay layer above the
ground water level a minimum of six 6 inches of compacted granular bedding material
should be placed below the pipe The granular bedding should extend beyond both pipe
sides a minimum width of one foot and should extend halfway up the pipe sides The
bedding material should consist of clean granular material such as 3B Fine gravel or
ASTM C 33 No 67
For pipes bearing on the soft to very soft clayey silt an additional 18 inch layer of
clean granular material should be provided below the pipe and the previously described
six 6 inch bedding layer to provide more uniform support for the pipe The approximate
area where soft to very soft clayey silt are anticipated near the invert elevations is shown
on Plate 2
The recommended bedding detail is illustrated on Plate 3

Trench Excavation
Based on the current design concept the anticipated trench excavation depths will
vary from about 5 to 15 feet below the existing ground surface along the proposed sewer
alignments
In consideration of the types of soils anticipated to be encountered the trench
excavation is anticipated to pose no untoward difficulties
Determination of the method and equipment used for excavation should be made
by the contractor within practical limits and safety considerations
Due to load considerations discussed in ihe preceding sections the width of the
sewer trench excavations should be kept as narrow as feasible to minimize the amount
of ground settlements
Trench Support
It is our opinion that the trench walls in the soft silt and clay deposits and shallow
groundwater level conditions will require continuous sheet piling support It is important
that adequate interlocking steel sheet piling be installed prior to the excavation and
maintained tight against the trench walls with proper bracing to minimize lateral
movements of the adjacent ground into the trench excavation which would result in
potential settlements of adjacent structures This is particularly critical since most if not
all of the existing adjacent structures may be supported on spread footings bearing on
the upper subsoils
The steel sheet piling should be driven properly with a suitable hammer to a depth
sufficient to assure the stability of the trench bottom and to reduce the possibility of
GEOLABS HAWAIl
W O 2613 00 B Page 9
bottom heaving or quick conditions which may be created by upward seepage pressure
during dewatering
Based on our estimate the sheet piling should be driven to a minimum penetration
depth of seven 7 feet below the stabilization layer or the trench bottom along the
proposed sewer alignments near the western boundary of the site from Awanene Place
to Huawaina Place to Ualani Place
A factor of safety of 1 5 is used in the determination of the above recommended
sheet pile penetration requirements
The required depths of sheet pile penetration will also be affected by the method
of dewatering The sheet pile penetration depth recommended above is based on the
assumption that there is no groundwater drawdown outside of the trench sides If a deep
well system is used it will probably create groundwater drawdown outside the sheet piles
which would allow reduction in the sheet pile penetration requirements However care
must be taken to prevent bottom heaving or piping when pumping from the deep well
system is stopped
The sheet piling and bracing system used must comply with all applicable safety
requirements The adequacy and safety of the sheet piling and bracing installation
operations should be made the responsibility of the contractor His representative who
should be required to be continually present on site would therefore have the opportunity
for observing changing conditions which might affect the trench stability during
construction
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2613 00 B Page 10
For calculating earth loads in the struts of the trench bracing the apparent
pressure diagram presented on Plate 4 may be used as a guide The pressure diagram
do not include a factor of safety of 1 5
Appropriate loads due to surcharge traffic or sloping ground conditions adjacent
to the trench excavations should be added to the recommended earth pressures for
design purposes
Even if good construction procedures are followed some movement of the trench
support bracing system may be anticipated because of changes in earth stresses during
excavation Movements depend greatly on workmanship e g how quickly and tightly
the support is instalied it also depends on the types of subsoils the depth and width of
excavation and the type of support In view of the complexity of the problem it is difficult
to accurately predict the amount of movement to be anticipated of a bracing system and
the adjacent ground Therefore it is important to realize that the trench support should
be installed as early as practicable and that measurements of vertical and horizontal
movements are required especially in areas where ground movements may result in
damage to the existing adjacent structures
The ground adjacent to the trench excavations should be continuously monitored
for cracks or dips and other indications of movements and possible sloughing of the
trench walls Such monitoring is particularly critical in areas adjacent to existing structures
and utilities
Dewatering
Dewatering will be necessary for the area near the western border of the site since
the groundwater level in this area is generally above the proposed sewer pipe inverts and
the subsoils immediately below trench bottom may consist mostly of soft clays and silts
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2613 00 B Page 11
The dewatering operations should be coordinated with the trench shoring support so that
the stability of the excavation is not jeopardized The operations should be carried out
without softening the bottom of the excavations
Determination of the method and equipment to be used for the dewatering
operations should be made by the contractor within practical limits and safety
considerations It should be realized that the dewatering system chosen will have an
effect on the penetration depth of sheet piling used
We suggest that the following three 3 basic selection criteria be considered in
determining a suitable method of dewatering
1 The method should impose the least disturbance or damage to the adjacent
existing development
2 The method should maintain adequate excavation stability and provide safe
working conditions
3 The method should be sufficiently flexible to allow modification to
accommodate various ground conditions
Dewatering by means of a well point system along the outside of the sheet piling
could be used This method could reduce the required sheet pile penetration
requirements However the resultant areal depression of the water table could induce
consolidation of subsoils or settlements below the adjacent existing structures with the
potential for extensive damage thereto Therefore recharging injecting water back into
the subsoils to balance the natural groundwater levels and minimize areal drawdown in
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2613 00 B Page 12
areas near adjacent existing structures may be required to reduce the risk of settlement
to these buildings if a well point system is selected for dewatering
Alternately it appears that sump pumping inside the trenches may be used if the
sheet piling is driven sufficiently deep to prevent piping and heaving of the bottom of the
trenches
Backfilling
The trench excavations should be backfilled as soon as the proposed sewer pipes
are installed with proper bedding support In general the trench backfill requirements
should follow the Standard Specifications for Public Works Construction City and County
of Honolulu September 1986
The first zone of backfill extends from the bedding material to at least 12 inches
above the top of the sewer pipes Select free draining well graded granular material is
recommended for the first zone of backfill to minimize the compaction effort required and
resulting stresses to the pipe The granular material should be well graded less than one
1 inch in size and have no more than 10 passing No 200 sieve Compaction by water
ponding or jetting may be allowed provided the granular material is self draining
The trench backfill from 12 inches above the top of the pipes to within two 2 feet
of the designed road surface elevation or of finish grade in unpaved areas may consist
of the excavated on site soils except organic clays and silts The backfill should be
placed in thin lifts with each lift mechanically compacted to a minimum 90 of its
maximum dry density as determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2613 00 B Page 13
In the paved areas the trench backfill up to a level within two 2 feet of the design
pavement surface elevation should consist of select borrow compacted to a minimum
95 of its maximum dry density as determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
A typical section of sewer trench is presented on Plate 3
Precautions should be taken against the occurrence of pipeline floatation before
and during trench backfilling due to possible influx of large quantities of groundwater into
the trenches The uplift buoyant forces due to groundwater influx may lift empty or even
partially filled sewer pipes
if practicable the trenches should be kept free of water during backfilling Shouid
backfilling under water be necessary clean granular material such as 3B Fine gravel or
ASTM C 33 No 67 should be utilized to minimize the potential of excessive settlement
of the trench backfill in the future
Joints and Connections
Due to the variable subsoil conditions along the proposed sewer alignments we
recommend that flexible pipe joints be used to connect the sewer pipes to allow for some
amount of differential settlement These flexible connections are particularly important in
the soft subsoil areas
Manhole Structures
Structures supported on the medium stiff to very stiff clays and silts may be
designed using an allowable bearing value up to 2000 p s f Minimal settlements are
anticipated for these structures
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2613 00 B Page 14
If it is necessary to place the manhole structures on the soft silty clayey soils an
18 inch layer of gravel e g 3B Fine gravel or ASTM C 33 No 67 should be provided
below the structure to develop uniform bearing support An allowable bearing value of
1500 p s f may be used on the 18 inch gravel layer The increase in load on the subsoils
due to the manhole structures is small Therefore large settlements are not anticipated
if proper shoring and dewatering techniques are utilized
The lateral earth pressure acting on the proposed underground manhole structures
will depend on the type of backfill to be used the extent of backfill and the compactive
effort on the backfill material around the structures Therefore granular material with no
more than 10 passing No 200 sieve is recommended for use as backfill around the
structures to reduce the compactive effort required and thus the build up of additional
lateral earth pressures We recommend that the new manholes be designed to resist the
following lateral earth pressure provided granular backfill is used
1 Above water level 50 p c f equivalent fluid pressure
2 Below water level 25 p c f equivalent fluid pressure plus
hydraulic pressure
The above values assume that the backfill around manhole structures is properly
placed and compacted Surcharge loading due to traffic or other applicable sources
should be added to the above values for design
Existing Utility Lines
Since the general project site is in a developed urban area it is anticipated that
many existing utility lines may be encountered during construction of the replacement
sewers
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2613 00 B Page 15
Therefore we recommend that all existing utility lines along the proposed sewer
alignments be located accurately prior to construction and that an adequate program be
devised to protect the existing utility lines during construction
Construction Monitoring
As the project will involve relatively deep open trenching in a well developed urban
area we believe that the importance of construction monitoring including field
observation and engineering supervision should be emphasized
It is our understanding that the extent of construction monitoring required may vary
with individual contractors However we believe that the following items of work should
be considered
Prior to Trench Excavation
1 A damage survey of all the existing buildings and structures adjacent to the
proposed sewer alignment should be conducted including photographs
and crack surveys prior to the commencement of construction
2 Inclinometer casings should be installed at about 75 foot intervals along the
proposed alignments in front of critical buildings or structures The
inclinometer casings should penetrate through soft loose subsoil layers into
underlying stiff material for at least 10 feet in order to establish a reference
for measurement of movements
3 Settlement markers should also be installed as much as possible in a
straight line pattern adjacent to the existing structures on both sides of the
proposed sewer lines We recommend that one 1 marker be installed at
approximately 30 to 50 foot intervals along the proposed sewer alignments
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2613 00 B Page 16
Sets of two 2 observation wells should be installed perpendicular to and
on each side of the proposed sewer alignments to allow observation of the
groundwater drawdown effect due to dewatering One 1 well of each set
should be placed as close to the existing adjacent structure as possible
The observation well sets should be spaced no more than 75 feet apart in
front of existing adjacent structures which are identified as critical
The driving of steel sheet piles prior to trench excavation should be
observed by a qualified geotechnical engineer to see that a suitable sheet
pile hammer is used and to record blow counts and depths of penetration
of the sheet piles in order to assure that adequate passive soil resistance
has been developed in front of the sheet piles
