INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this investigation is to evaluate the sub
surface conditions at the site and to provide recommenda
tions for the design and construction of the proposed bus
service facility located in Honolulu Oahu Hawaii
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
GEOLABS HAWAII
ALAPAI SERVICE FACILITY P 2
SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed bus facility site is located on the Ewa half
of the block bounded by King Ward Beretania and Alapai
Streets
In general the site is relatively level with a slight slope
from elevation 21 feet at the corner of Alapai and Beretania
Streets down to about elevation 9 feet close to the corner
of Alapai and King Streets
At present the whole area is covered with asphaltic concrete
pavement Two buildings for office and maintenance purposes
are located on the northern portion of the site The remain
der of the area is used as a parking lot for buses
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
The proposed plan consists of removing the existing structures
and constructing the following structures
1 A 3 story concrete maintenance building
approximately 140 feet by 265 feet in
plan Column loads up to about 400 kips
are anticipated
2 A 1 story concrete service building
approximately 80 feet by 150 feet in
plan
GEOLABS HAWAII
ALAPAI SERVICE FACILITY P 3
3 About 650 feet of concrete retaining walls
up to 13 feet high along the Beretania
Street boundary of the site
The remaining area will be used as a parking lot for buses
and cars
Cuts generally less than 1 foot and fills less than 1 5
feet are anticipated
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Subsurface Exploration
The subsurface exploration for the proposed site consisted
of drilling 14 borings at the approximate locations shown
on the enclosed Site Plan Plate 1 A detailed description
of the field exploration methods and accompanying laboratory
testing is given in the appendix along with the boring logs
and laboratory test results
Soil Conditions
The subsurface exploration disclosed that most of the site
is mantled bya layer about 0 5 to 3 feet thick of stiff
brown clayey silt and silty clay material Underlying the
upper layer black sand volcanic cinder varying from
loose to medium density was encountered The thickness
of this layer varies from 5 feet to 10 feet Beneath the
black sand dense to very dense light tan silty coral and
sand were encountered down to 21 5 feet the depth drilled

ALAPAI SERVICE FACILITY P 4
Water Levels
Groundwater was encountered at about elevation 1 foot to
2 feet MSL in the drill holes during this field explora
tion No heavy rainfall occurred during this period The
groundwater level could be affected by heavy rainfall or
tidal variations
DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Building Foundations
The field exploration indicated medium density black sand
below the building sites The black sand would be a com
petent bearing layer provided some additional compaction
is done The additional compaction may be accomplished
by proofrolling the building sites with a heavy roller
The building sites should be cleared of old pavements and
foundations and then proofrolled with a ten ton vibratory
roller capable of delivering a total impact force in excess
of 45000 foot pounds A minimum of 12 passes is recommended
For the proposed structures spread or continuous footing
foundations are recommended An allowable bearing value
of 4 500 p s f may be used for footings resting on the
black sand layer
The bottoms of footings should be a minimum of 2 feet below
the finish ground surface
GEOLABS HAWAII
ALAPAI SERVICE FACILITY P 5
If a footing is located next to a utility trench the footing
should extend to the bottom of the utility trench
The bottom of the footing excavation should be tamped prior
to pouring concrete
For the above recommendations it is estimated that settle
ments will not exceed 1 inch total and inch differential
Site Grading
Cuts and fills generally less than 1 5 feet are planned for
the project site
Pavements old foundations and debris should be removed prior
to site filling
Select on site material should be used for fill and backfill
material
Fill should be placed in horizontal loose lifts of about
8 inches and compacted to a minimum of 90 of the maximum
dry density established by ASTM Test Method D 1557
Slab On Grade
Slab on grade should be placed over a 4 inch layer of base
course compacted to 95 of its maximum dry density
Where the slab is located below the outside ground surface
a subdrainage system around the perimeter will help to reduce
seepage water

ALAPAI SERVICE FACILITY P 6
Retaining and Basement Walls
About 650 feet of retaining and basement walls up to
13 feet high is planned along the Beretania Street
boundary of the site Portions of the wall will be
used as basement walls for the proposed maintenance
and service buildings
Well graded granular material such as base course rock
or black sand should be used as backfill material behind
the walls Impervious material should be used for the
top 2 feet of the backfill
The recommendations given under foundations should be
followed for the design of the wall foundations
For lateral earth pressures the following can be used
Walls unrestrained at the top 45 p c f equivalent
fluid pressure
Walls restrained at top 60 p c f equivalent
fluid pressure
Additional load due to traffic or sloping
surcharge should be included where applicable
A fraction factor of 0 4 can be utilized to determine the
sliding resistance of the wall foundation
GEOLABS HAWAII
ALAPAI SERVICE FACILITY P 7
A subsurface drainage system should be used to prevent
the build up of hydrostatic pressure behind the walls
Due to the different loading conditions a joint or
separation should be placed between the retaining and
basement portions of the wall
Parking Area
A total of about 12 acres of A C pavement for the bus
parking area is planned
Cuts and fills generally less than1 5 feet are anticipated
for this area
CBR tests were performed on near surface samples of the
on site black sand and brown silty clay materials Based
on these tests the preliminary pavement design is as
follows
3 inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 inch Base Course
9 inch Total Pavement Thickness
Additional CBR tests should be performed at the actual sub
grade level during construction and if different materials
are encountered at subgrade level
The subgrade should be recompacted prior to placing the
base course layer
GEOLABS HAWAII
ALAPAI SERVICE FACILITY P 8
Weepholes should be placed at the subgrade levels through
the walls of catch basins in order to provide drainage of
the base course layer
Design Review
The plans for foundations site grading and utility con
struction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for
review and comments prior to construction to see that the
intent of our recommendations is included in the project
plans and specifications
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during
the parking lot grading to monitor the contractor s grading
operations
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be
observed by a soils engineer during construction to assure
that adequate bearing has been obtained and that the sub
surface conditions are consistent with the findings of our
subsurface investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive
tested and approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling
to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent
upon the above inspections

APPENDIX A
FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored
by drilling 14 borings to depths of 5 to 21 5 feet below
the existing ground surface The approximate locations
of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
During the subsurface exploration two bulk bag
samples of near surface soil were taken in the proposed
A C parking areas
The soils encountered in the borings were classified
by visual and textural examination in the field by our en
gineer who continuously monitored the drilling operations
The classifications were reviewed by visual inspection and
testing in the laboratory The soils were classified in
general conformance with the Unified Soil Classification
System A graphic presentation of the soils is presented
on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 14
In the borings soil samples were obtained by driving
split barrel sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30
inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the last
12 inches are shown on the boring logs at the appropriate
sample depths
