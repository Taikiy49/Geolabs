In general this letter report presents our observations and the results of the tests

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located in the intersection of Acacia Road and Kuala Street in Pearl
City on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The approximate location of the project site is shown on
the Project Location Map Plate 1 The property is bounded by Acacia Street to the
southwest the City and County of Honolulu Bus Barn to the north Kuala Street to the west
Board of Water Supply properties to the east and the U S Post Office to the south
One of the major items of construction for the project was the construction of
six upper Keystone Retaining Walls three lower Keystone Retaining Walls and one
Keystone Wall with CMU screen wall on the eastern western and southern portions of the
project site The walls consisted of masonry units Keystone Blocks geogrids Miragrid a
drainage system or along the slope behind the Keystone
Retaining wall and compacted select backfill materials Wall heights for the six upper
Keystone retaining walls range from about 5 to 31 feet the three lower Keystone Retaining
walls range from about 6 to 25 feet and the Keystone Retaining wall with CMU screen wall
range from about 2 to 11 feet The total length of the walls are approximately 2 162 linear
feet The location of the Keystone Retaining walls are shown on Plate 1

KEYSTONE RETAINING WALL CONSTRUCTION
The Keystone retaining wall construction work for the Wal Mart Store Manana project
was observed by Geolabs Inc from March 1 through August 16 2005 The site was cleared
and grubbed prior to March 1 2005 The grading work for the wall construction and the
Keystone wall construction work was performed by Goodfellow Brothers Inc During the
grading and backfilling operations samples of the on site and imported materials utilized as
fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing The
maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in
general accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test results are
summarized on Plates 2 1 and 2 2
In general the subgrade and leveling pad materials Select Borrow for the Keystone
retaining wall were moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture content and compacted
to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as determined in accordance with
ASTM Test Method D 1557 In addition the reinforced fill and retained fill materials were
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
During the leveling pad reinforced fill and retained fill construction periodic field
density tests were performed in general accordance with ASTM D 2922 and D 3017 Nuclear
Gauge Method The results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on
Plates 3 1 through 3 79 of this report These tests were performed on the prepared ground
surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills at the site
Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing hand level and interpolating
from on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should be considered accurate
only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were
reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained and or approved by our field
representative based on visual observation and hand probing
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our professional opinion the construction of the Keystone retaining wall
for the above project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report
was performed in conformance with the general intent of the recommendations in the
following references
