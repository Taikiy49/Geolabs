SECTION 1 0 GENERAL
1 1 Introduction
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the sland Shutters Inc Warehouse at Kapolei Industrial Park project
located in the Kapolei area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project location and
general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings from our field exploration and laboratory
testing and presents our geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our
analyses for the proposed project These recommendations are intended for the design
of building foundations slabs on grade pavement design and site preparation only
The findings and recommendations presented herein are subject to the limitations noted
at the end of this report
1 2 Project Considerations
The project consists of constructing a new warehouse building with an asphalt
concrete parking area in the vicinity of the intersection of Munu Street and Lauia Street
within the Kapolei Industrial Park in Kapolei on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The new
building will be approximately 100 feet by 204 feet in dimensions The proposed building
location is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 Structural details were not available at the
time this report was prepared
1 3 Purpose and Scope
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the surface and
subsurface conditions to develop a soil rock data set to formulate geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertaining to the design of building foundations
slabs on grade pavement design and site preparation for the proposed support
buildings In order to accomplish this we conducted an exploration program consisting
of the following tasks and work efforts

2 1 Regional Geology
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lava from the
Waianae and Koolau shield volcanoes The Waianae Volcano is estimated to be middle
to late Pliocene in age and Koolau Volcano is estimated to be late Pliocene to early
Pleistocene in age As the volcanic activity in Waianae Volcano ceased lava flows from
Koolau Volcano banked against its eroded eastern slope and formed a broad plateau
now known as the Schofield Plateau The project site is located on the southwestern
flank of the Waianae Volcano
Physical and chemical weathering followed by erosion of Schofield Plateau
generated sediments which were transported to the coast In the project vicinity and to
the south these sediments accumulated and are interbedded with marine sediments
and coral algal reef formations that formed a sedimentary wedge The thickness of the
sedimentary wedge ranges from 0 feet in the area of the Interstate Route H 1 Highway
to over 1 000 feet at Ewa Beach This wedge forms the Ewa Plain and serves as the
confining formation or caprock over the artesian basal aquifers of Southern Oahu
Deposition of sediments has continued from earlier geologic time through the present
During the Pleistocene Epoch sea levels fluctuated in response to the cycles of
continental glaciation As the glaciers grew and advanced less water was available to
fill the oceanic basins such that sea levels fell below the present stands of the sea
When the glaciers melted and receded an excess of water became available such that
the sea levels rose to above the present sea level
The higher sea level stands caused the formation of deltas and fans of
accumulated terrigenous sediments in the heads of old bays accumulated reef deposits
at correspondingly higher elevations and deposited lagoonal marine sediments in the
quiet waters protected by fringing reefs The project site is generally underlain by coral
formation

Our field exploration consisted of drilling and sampling five borings designated
as Boring Nos 1 through 5 Boring Nos 1 through 3 were drilled to the depth of

on the order of about 2 to 4 5 feet thick consisting of dense to very dense coralline silty

fill in Boring No 1 Below the surface soils coral formation was encountered and

localized areas of hard crystalline coral intermixed with soft severely fractured detritus
It should be noted that coral formation is frequently cavernous or contain pockets of
voids Although cavities were not encountered in our drilled borings the potential for the
presence of cavities and or voids exists
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings drilled at the time of our field

SECTION 3 0 DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Our field exploration generally encountered surface fills overlying coral formation
extending to the maximum depth explored of approximately 21 5 feet below the existing
ground surface Since cavities and or voids are commonly encountered in coral
formations we believe that the primary geotechnical considerations for the proposed
construction will include the following 1 probing and grouting for potential cavities
and or sinkholes beneath the building foundations and 2 proof rolling of future slab
subgrades to detect the presence of potential near surface cavities sinkholes or loose
zones
Based on the relatively competent subsoil conditions encountered in our borings
and the anticipated design structural loads it is our opinion that spread and or
continuous footings may be used to support the proposed buildings planned An
allowable bearing pressure of up to 3 000 pounds per square foot psf may be used to
design footing foundations bearing on the recompacted in situ soils or new compacted
fills
To reduce the potential for loss of foundation support resulting from collapse of
the cavities and or sinkholes below foundations we recommend implementing a
program of cavity probing and grouting at the foundation locations during construction
In addition we recommend proof rolling the excavated subgrades underneath future
slabs with a minimum 10 ton vibratory drum roller to detect the presence of
near surface cavities sinkholes or loose zones Special attention should be given to the
subgrade preparation requirements presented in the Site Preparation section of this
report to reduce the potential for future distress on the floor slabs Detailed discussions
and recommendations pertaining to these items are further presented in the following
sections

3 2 Foundation Probing
Our experience suggests that cavities and or voids are commonly encountered in
the coral formations underlying the project site To reduce the potential for loss of
foundation support resulting from collapse of the cavities and or sinkholes below
foundations we recommend implementing a program of cavity probing and grouting at
the foundation locations during construction
For estimating purposes we recommend drilling probe holes at each spread
footing location one probe hole for every 50 square feet of footing area and at 10 feet
on center along wall foundations The probe holes should be at least 3 inches in
diameter and should extend to at least 10 feet below the planned bottom of foundation
elevation If cavities and or voids are encountered or suspected during the probing
operation additional probe holes should be drilled at closer spacing to aid in delineating
the vertical and lateral extent of the cavity and or void The probe holes and cavities

3 3 Slabs On Grade
We anticipate that concrete slabs on grade will be used for the floors of the new
warehouse structure For the interior building slabs not subject to vehicular traffic we
recommend providing a minimum 4 inch thick layer of cushion fill below the slabs for
uniform support The cushion fill should consist of open graded gravel ASTM C 33
No 67 gradation and also would serve as a capillary moisture break Where slabs are
subject to vehicular traffic or sustained vibrations a minimum of 6 inches of compacted
aggregate base course should be provided below the slabs in lieu of the 4 inch thick
cushion fill layer The aggregate base course layer should be moisture conditioned to
above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent
relative compaction
To reduce the potential for excess moisture infiltration and subsequent damage
to floor coverings an impervious moisture barrier is recommended on top of the gravel
cushion layer Flexible floor coverings should be considered above the floor slab
because they can better mask minor slab cracking
We envision that exterior concrete walkways and exterior flatwork likely will be
required In general we recommend providing a minimum 4 inch thick cushion layer of

3 5 Site Grading
Based on the grading plan provided we anticipate that the majority of the site
preparation work for the project will consist of minor cuts and or fills on the order of
about 2 feet thick to achieve the design finish grades The project site may be underlain
by cavities and or sinkholes near the existing ground surface Therefore special
attention should be given to the subgrade preparation for the project development
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading timits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed Vegetation organic debris construction debris
deleterious material and other unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed
properly off site or in a designated area to reduce the potential for contamination of the
excavated materials with the cleared and grubbed spoils Soft and yielding areas
encountered during clearing and grubbing should be over excavated to expose firm
natural material and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with well compacted
fill The excavated soft and or organic soils should be properly disposed off site
The excavated subgrades underneath future slab areas should be scarified to a
depth of about 8 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content
and proof rolled with a minimum 10 ton vibratory drum roller to a minimum of 90 percent
relative compaction to detect the presence of potential near surface cavities sinkholes
or loose zones If the proof rolling operations detect a near surface cavity or loose zone
the area should be ripped open cleaned of debris and then backfilled with compacted
fill material or lean concrete
Fill materials required for the project construction should consist of
non expansive select granular material such as crushed coral and basalt The material
should be well graded from coarse to fine with particles no larger than 3 inches in
largest dimension and should contain between 10 and 30 percent particles passing the
No 200 sieve The material should have a laboratory CBR value of 20 or more and
should have a maximum swell of one percent or less when tested in accordance with
ASTM D 1883 The on site coralline materials should be suitable for use as
non expansive select granular fill material provided that they are processed to meet the

A granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33

3 7 Drainage
The finished grades outside the new buildings should be sloped to shed water
away from foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding Gutter systems should
be installed around the buildings and the discharge should be diverted away from the
foundation areas Excessive landscape watering near the foundations and slabs should
be avoided Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have concrete bottoms
and drains to reduce the potential for water infiltration into the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil
saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations
should be properly backfilled against the walls or slab footings immediately after setting
of the concrete to reduce the potential for water infiltration Drainage swales should be
provided as soon as possible and should be maintained to drain surface runoff away
from the slab and footing foundations

APPENDIX A

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by drilling and
sampling five borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 5 extending to the depths
of about 6 5 and 21 5 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate locations
of the borings drilled are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were drilled using
truck mounted drilling equipment equipped with continuous flight augers
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by
testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified
Soil Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representations of the
materials encountered are presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 5
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in
general accordance with ASTM D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving
a 3 inch OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches In
addition some samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance
with ASTM D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a
2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same hammer and drop The blow
counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an 18 inch drive
are shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate
sample depths
Pocket penetrometer tests were performed on selected cohesive soil samples in
the field The pocket penetrometer test provides an indication of the unconfined
compressive strength of the sample Results of the pocket penetrometer tests are
summarized are on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
