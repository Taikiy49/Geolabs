SECTION 1 0 GENERAL
1 1 Introduction
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed 24 000 gallon water tank within the existing Keakealani
Elementary School in the Puna area of the Island of Hawaii The general location and
vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering
recommendations resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering
analyses for the proposed new water tank project These recommendations are intended
for the design of foundations and site preparation only The findings and
recommendations presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this
report
1 2 Project Considerations
The project site is located within the existing Keakealani Elementary School in the
Puna area on the Island of Hawaii Currently there is one wooden tank and two steel
tanks located adjacent to an existing two story building The wooden tank will be retained
while the two steel tanks will be replaced with a new 24 000 gallon steel tank The new
tank will be about 17 feet in diameter and about 15 feet in height
1 3 Purpose and Scope
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the surface and
subsurface conditions at the project site to develop a generalized soil data set for the
formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations pertaining to the design of
foundations and site preparation for the proposed new water tank project Our work was
performed in general accordance with our revised fee proposal dated
The scope of work for this exploration included the following tasks and work efforts

SECTION 2 0 SITE CHARACTERIZATION
2 1 Site Description
The project site is located within the existing Keakealani Elementary School in the
Puna area of the Island of Hawaii as shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The
entrance to the school is from Haunani Avenue
The existing new water tank site is situated on the eastern side of an existing
two story building There is an existing elevated wooden non potable water tank about
12 feet in diameter adjacent to the building The two steel tanks are located adjacent to
the wooden tank and each are about 17 feet in diameter
The topography at the site is relatively flat with ground elevations of about 3 735 to
3 740 feet above Mean Sea Level MSL Most of the area is grassed with asphaltic
concrete pavement along the access driveways
2 2 Subsurface Conditions
Our field exploration consisted of drilling and sampling one boring designated as
Boring No 1 near the existing new water tank site extending to a depth of approximately
30 5 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate location of the boring is
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Our field exploration at the water tank site generally encountered a surficial fill layer
overlying saprolite soils consisting of stiff to very stiff clayey silts derived from the
weathering of volcanic rocks extending to the maximum depth explored of approximately
16 5 feet below the existing ground surface
Groundwater was not encountered in the drilled boring during our field exploration
However it should be noted that the groundwater level may vary significantly depending
on seasonal rainfall time of year and other factors

SECTION 3 0 DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field exploration the project site is generally underlain by stiff to very
stiff clayey silt soils extending to the maximum depth explored of about 16 5 feet below the
existing ground surface Based on the subsoil conditions encountered at the project site
we believe that shallow foundations consisting of a perimeter ring footing bearing on the
basalt formation may be used for support of the proposed new water tank
Our geotechnical engineering recommendations pertaining to the design of
foundations and site preparation are further discussed in the following sections of this
report
3 1 Water Tank Foundations
Based on the current design concept and the generally competent subsoil
conditions encountered at the site we believe that shallow foundations may be used to
support the proposed new water tank An allowable bearing pressure of up to
6 000 pounds per square foot psf may be used for the design of the perimeter ring
footing bearing on the basalt formation anticipated at the project site This bearing value is
as those caused by wind or seismic forces
The bottom of the footing excavations should be leveled and cleared of loose
material to provide a smooth bearing surface
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by frictional resistance
developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil and by passive earth
pressure acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system A coefficient of
friction of 0 4 may be used for foundations bearing on the basalt formation Resistance due
to passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid pressure of
300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth pcf This assumes that the soils around the
footings are well compacted The passive pressure should be reduced for foundations

Hawaii California
SECTION 3 DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
located on slopes Unless covered by pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the
upper 12 inches of soil should be neglected
In general footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grade For footings constructed near tops of slopes or on sloping
ground the footings should be embedded deep enough to provide a minimum horizontal
set back distance of 8 feet measured from the outside edge of the footings to the face of
the slope
If foundations are located next to utility trenches or easements they should be
embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane extending
upward from the bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility
lines This requirement is necessary to avoid surcharging adjacent below grade structures
with additional structural loads and to reduce the potential for foundation settlement
We estimate that foundation settlements under the anticipated design loads for
footings bearing on the recompacted in situ stiff soils as recommended herein to be less
than 1 inch total with differential settlements on the order of 0 5 inches
We recommend that foundation excavations be observed by a representative from
Geolabs prior to placement of reinforcing steel or concrete to confirm the foundation
bearing conditions and the required embedment depths
3 2 Foundation Probing
Voids and or cavities may be present in the basaltic lava flows at the project site
However since the new tank will be located over the old tank location it is our opinion
that the risk of cavity collapse may be very small and the normal cavity probing and
grouting procedure may be eliminated if some risk is acceptable
If it is desired to further reduce the potential for loss of foundation support resulting
from collapse of cavities below foundations a program of cavity probing and grouting
below the water tank foundation may be implemented

Hawaii California

For estimating purposes we recommend that probe holes be drilled at 10 feet on
centers below the perimeter footing In addition one probe hole should be drilled at the
center of the tank The probe holes should be at least 3 inches in diameter and should
extend to a depth of at least 10 feet below the planned bottom of the foundation
If cavities and or voids are encountered or suspected during the probing
operation additional probe holes should be drilled at closer spacings to help delineate
the vertical and lateral extent of the cavity and or void The probe holes and cavities
discovered should be backfilled with Controlled Low Strength Material CLSM with a
slump of about 6 to 9 inches The CLSM should be injected at low to moderate
pressures As an alternate to CLSM sand cement grout with a slump of about 6 to
9 inches may also be used
We recommend that unit prices for additional probing and grouting be obtained
during bidding In addition the probe drill should be available on site until the probing
and grouting operations are completed The contractor should also be made aware that
a longer lag time between probing grouting operations and foundation construction
might be required in the construction schedule
The probing and grouting program should be conducted under the observation of
a representative from Geolabs This would allow our firm to monitor the presence of
cavities and to allow additional recommendations to be made if excess grout take
and or changed conditions are observed
3 3 Slab On Grade
Based on anticipated competent subgrade conditions we recommend that the
concrete slab be supported by 4 inches of base course to provide more uniform support
for the tank slab The base course should consist of crushed basalt aggregate compacted
to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction For the design of structural slabs a
modulus of subgrade reaction of about 250 pounds per square inch per inch of deflection
pci may be used for the compacted base course The area adjacent to the tank slab
should be backfilled tightly against the slab with compacted on site soils and graded to

3 4 Site Preparation
Based on the existing topography and the current design concept we anticipate
that minimal site grading work will be required for construction of the new water tank At
the on set of earthwork the areas within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared Vegetation debris deleterious material old foundations and other
unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site Soft and
yielding areas encountered during clearing below areas designated to receive fill should
be over excavated to expose firm natural material and the resulting excavation should
be backfilled with well compacted general fill The excavated soft and or organic soils
should be properly disposed of off site or used in landscaping areas if appropriate
Slabs and foundations of the existing water tank to be demolished should be
completely removed Existing utilities to be abandoned should be removed and the
resulting excavation should be properly backfilled with select granular fill material
moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture content placed in 6 to 8 inch level loose
lifts and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Utilities to be
abandoned in place under the proposed tank structure should be backfilled by pumping
lean concrete under low pressure
Base course material should meet the material requirements for Aggregate for
Untreated Base Section 703 06 as specified in the Hawaii Standard Specifications for
Road Bridge and Public Works Construction 1994 Imported fill material if required
should consist of non expansive imported granular material such as crushed coral basalt
gravel or cinder sand The material should be well graded from coarse to fine with no
particles larger than 3 inches in largest dimension and should contain between 10 and
30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve The material should have a laboratory
CBR value of 20 or more and should have a maximum swell of less than 1 percent when
tested in accordance with ASTM D 1883 Imported fill material should be tested by

for the intended use
Base course and imported granular fill materials should be moisture conditioned to
above the optimum moisture placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose
thickness and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Relative
compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the
maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 test
procedures Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight
corresponding to the maximum dry density
3 5 Waterlines
We understand that new waterlines will be required for this project In general for
support of the waterlines we recommend that a granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of
1 inch minus granular fill material be used under the pipes The 1 inch minus granular fill
materials should also be used for the initial trench backfill up to about 12 inches above the
pipes to provide adequate support around the pipes The 1 inch minus granular fill
material should be compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction It is critical
that the 1 inch minus granular fill materials be well compacted to reduce the potential for
formation of voids below the haunches of pipes and to provide adequate support for the
sides of the pipes Improper backfill material and placement procedures could result in
settlement of the backfill and damage to the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipes to
the top of the subgrade or finished grade may consist of the on site soils or select granular
material The backfill should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture
placed in about 8 inch loose lifts and mechanically compacted to at least 90 percent
relative compaction Where trenches are located in paved areas the upper 3 feet of the
trench backfill below the pavement grade should be compacted to not less than 95 percent
relative compaction

3 6 Drainage
The finished grades outside the water tank structure should be sloped to shed
water away from the foundations and slabs and to reduce the potential for ponding
Excessive landscape watering near the foundations and slabs should also be avoided
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the above
foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation
and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations should be
properly backfilled against the walls or slab edges immediately after setting of the concrete
to reduce the potential for water infiltration In addition drainage swales should be
provided as soon as possible and should be maintained to drain surface water run off
away from foundations and slabs

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling one
boring designated as Boring No 1 extending to a depth of approximately 30 5 feet below
the existing ground surface at the approximate location shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The boring was drilled using truck mounted drilling equipment equipped with continuous
flight augers
The materials encountered in the boring were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who observed the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed by visual observation
and testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified
Soil Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representation of the materials
encountered is presented on the Log of Boring Plate A 1
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a
3 inch OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches In
addition some samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance
with ASTM Test Designation D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of
Soils by driving a 2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same hammer and
drop The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an
18 inch drive are shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Boring at the
appropriate sample depths
