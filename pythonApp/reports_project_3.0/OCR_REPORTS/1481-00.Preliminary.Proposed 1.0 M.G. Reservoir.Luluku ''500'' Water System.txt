PRELIMINARY SOIL ENGINEERING EVALUATION
PROPOSED 1 0 M G RESERVOIR
IULUKU 500 WATER SYSTEM
This report presents the results of our preliminary soil
engineering evaluation performed for the proposed reservoir site in
Kaneohe on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location of the
site is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
PURPOSE AND SCOPE OF SERVICES
The purpose of our preliminary evaluation was to explore the
shallow subsurface conditions at the reservoir site in order to
provide preliminary recommendations for use in the design of the
foundation for the proposed structure

A soil exploration program including three borings was
originally planned at the site However due to wet and muddy
conditions along the existing access road and right of entry
difficulties a drill rig could not be mobilized to the site at this
time
Subsequent to discussions with the the Board of Water Supply it
was decided to conduct a site reconnaissance with hand augered
probings at the tank site to provide preliminary soil data for use in
the site grading and foundation design of the project Borings will
be drilled when access is available at the commencement of the site
grading construction to verify the preliminary recommendations
Provisions will be made in the contract documents to allow the
necessary revisions to the plans
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests which
we used in the engineering analysis to formulate our conclusions and
recommendations are presented in the appendices of this report The
approximate locations of the probings are shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed project consists of a 1 0 M G concrete reservoir
structure approximately 100 feet in diameter and 22 feet high Cuts
into the hillside from 6 feet up to about 30 feet are planned to
provide a level pad area for the reservoir

Soil Conditions
The soil conditions at the project site were explored by
hand augering two probings to a depth of 10 feet
Based on the probings and general geology of the area the
reservoir site may be underlain by stiff brown to mottled brow
clayey silt Older Alluvium which has some gravel or decomposed
gravel at depths
No groundwater was encountered in the probings during the time of
our exploration but the soils have high in situ moisture contents
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The subsurface exploration at the reservoir site generally
encountered stiff soil conditions which appear suitable for support of
the reservoir construction
Reservoir Foundations
The probings disclosed that at the proposed finished grade the
site may be underlain by a stiff reddish brown to mottled orange
clayey silt which could provide adequate foundation support for the
proposed reservoir
An allowable bearing value of 3500 p s f may be used for the
foundation design
The allowable bearing value may be increased by one third for
temporary wind and seismic loads
Footings should be embedded a minimm of 18 inches below the
lowest adjacent finished grade Footings located next to utility
trenches should extend down to the bottom of the utility trench and
bear on stiff undisturbed soil or the trench backfill should be
well compacted to minimize footing settlement

The bottom of all footing excavations and trench backfill should
be compacted to a minimm of 90 of its maximum dry density as
established by ASTM D 1557 78 prior to concrete placement
The site grading should be designed to divert surface water away
from the reservoir foundations
Settlement
Based on the boring and laboratory test data and the anticipated
loads of the reservoir the estimated settlements would generally
be on the order of less than one inch total and one half of an
inch differential
Lateral Resistance
For passive earth pressure an equivalent fluid pressure of 300
p s f per foot of depth can be used A friction factor of 0 4
can be utilized to determine sliding resistance of the structure
and foundation
Site Grading
In general the site grading will consist of cutting the ridge
about 6 to 30 feet at the reservoir site and a small amount of fill
and cut for the access roadway
Due to a combination of high annual rainfall and dense
vegetation the natural moisture content of the on site soils are
generally high Therefore some drying out of the excavated material
would be required to attain the recommended compaction criteria
Alternatively drier off site material may be used for the fill

Site Preparation
The site to be graded should be cleared of all vegetation
organic matter debris and soft material
Fill Placement Compaction Requirements
It is anticipated that no filling will be required at the tank
site Some filling for the access roadway may be necessary
Where the existing ground is steeper than 5 horizontal to 1
vertical fill placed should be keyed and benched into the
existing firm ground to properly bond the new fill to the slope
for details see Plate 3 This detail is essential for the
stability of the fills particularly any large fills The
filling operation should start at the lowest point and continue
up in level compacted layers
Since the project site is located ina high rainfall area the
on site subsoils have high in place moisture contents
Therefore it may not be practicable to dry the excavated soil to
near the optimum moisture content Due to the high in situ
moisture content and since the proposed fills are low it is our
opinion that the normal 90 compaction standard used in other
drier areas should be modified to 85 for this project
Groundwater seepage may be encountered in localized areas
Provisions should be made for the installation of subdrains if
seepage zones are encountered Schematic details of the subdrain
are shown on Plate 4

It is anticipated that the existing ridge to the west of the
reservoir will be cut about 10 to 30 feet Cut slope ratios up
20 foot maximum vertical height intervals
Near the finished grade the slopes should be cut with extreme
care by the contractor A backhoe should be used to provide
the final finishing of the cut slope surface Overcutting
beyond the design slope should be avoided since backfilling and
recompacting would create a very loose surface layer which
could be easily eroded
Surface water from areas above the slope should be diverted
away and prevented from flowing over the slope face The slope
should be planted as soon as practical to prevent unravelling
due to erosion
Due to the high rainfall in the area it is not uncommon to
encounter seepage zones through gravelly layers exposed in the
cut slopes If encountered special provisions such as
horizontal drains or toe drains may be required to stabilize
the cut slopes A typical horizontal drain detail is show on
Plates 5 and 6

Design Review
Plans for the site grading and foundation should be forwarded to

Additional Exploration
Additional borings are required at the commencement of the site

CONSTRUCTION MONTTORING
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during
earthwork construction particularly the fill placement slope cuts
and to monitor the contractor s grading operations
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be observed
by a soils engineer during construction to see that adequate bearing
has been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent
with the findings of our investigation
Any imported material required should generally be non expansive
tested and approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the
site

LIMITATIONS
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations
made in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation
engineering principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all
other warranties either expressed or implied
The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are
based on our site reconnaissance soil information from boring data
and laboratory tests obtained from this soil exploration
Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encountered and cannot
be fully determined by reconnaissance and hand auger probing These
unforeseen conditions such as pockets of organic soils seepage
trash fill or expansive soils may occur in localized areas
Therefore additional expenditures may be needed during construction
to attain a properly constructed project Some contingency fund is
thus recommended to accommodate these possible costs
This report has been prepared in order to assist the engineer in
the preparation of the site grading and foundation design of this
project Thus it may not contain adequate information for other
purposes such as construction

Suggested Specifications for Horizontal Drains
Horizontal Slotted Drains
1
Horizontal slotted drains shall be installed where directed by the
engineer or owner The drains shall be sloped at 5 percent or as
directed by the engineer
The entrance to the pipe shall be tightly plugged with a rounded or
pointed extension which shall extend not more than 0 5 foot beyond the
end of the pipe
The horizontal holes shall be drilled with rotary drilling equipment
The drains shall be slotted 1 5 inch Schedule 80 polyvinyl chloride
pipe PVC 2110 Slotted pipe shall be furnished in 10 foot lengths
ends of the pipe shall be machined to provide for a smooth wall slip
coupling Average configuration shall be 42 slots per row per foot
using 0 02 inch slots The slots shall consist of three 3 rows cut
circumferentially on the pipe on 120 degree centers commercially cut
at the factory
