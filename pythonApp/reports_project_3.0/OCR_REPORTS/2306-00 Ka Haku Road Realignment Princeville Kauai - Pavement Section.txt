Ka Haku Road Pavement Repairs
At your request we have observed the reconstruction of the two test sections by
removal and reconstruction of two concrete cross bandings of the Ka Haku Road
pavement We had previously provided recommendations for repair of the cross
banding in a letter dated The two test sections were constructed
on Due to time constraints limited resources and the conditions
observed all the recommendations in the letter were not fully
implemented The following briefly describes the reconstruction of the two test
sections
Observations and Findings
Two test sections consisting of a 2 by 4 foot and a 2 by 8 foot section of the
concrete cross banding were removed and reconstructed on
These repaired test sections are located near the entrance to Princeville at the
intersection of Kuhio Highway and Ka Haku Road The two test sections were removed
by saw cutting the concrete cross banding and exposing the underlying base course
below the slab The existing concrete cross banding was observed to be between 5
to 6 inches thick The exposed base course was observed to be wet with a
concentration of fines near the surface of the base course layer It was also observed
at these test sections that a joint sealant was absent
From the exposed sections it was observed that the shear keys at the interface
between the cross banding and the adjacent concrete slab have been sheared off In
addition several of the tie bars connecting the cross banding to the adjacent slabs
have also been sheared off It was also observed that the tie bar was generally placed
above the shear keys and in some cases the tie bar was very close to the pavement

Test Section 2 by 4 foot
Based on our observations of the exposed conditions the base course in the 2
by 4 foot test section was removed to the subgrade level The base course was
measured to be about 8 inches thick and was underlain by a geotextile fabric The
geotextile fabric prevented us to observe and evaluate the subgrade soils however the
subgrade soils did not appear to be soft
For the reconstruction of the 2 by 4 foot test section the remaining tie bars were
cut off to facilitate placement of the new base course Removing the tie bars would
provide for a free floating section of concrete cross banding which would move
independently of the adjacent slab
A non woven filter fabric was placed in the 2 by 4 foot test section above the
geotextile fabric to encapsulate the new base course The purpose of the filter fabric
is to reduce the potential for migration of fines from the new base course New base
course was used to replace the removed base course and was compacted using a
jumping jack compactor The new base course appeared to be relatively well
compacted and the compacted thickness is about 7 inches The filter fabric was
lapped over the surface of the base course
Reinforcing steel consisting of 3 bars were placed at approximately 2 foot
centers to reinforce the new concrete cross banding The thickness of the new
concrete cross banding is about 7 inches A piece of felt was used to isolate the new
concrete banding from the adjacent slabs to provide a free floating section
Test Section 2 by 8 foot
The conditions observed at the top of the base course of the 2 by 8 foot test
section are similar to those observed at the 2 by 4 foot test section In general the
top of the underlying base course was wet and some of the tie bars were sheared off
Due to time constraints the base course in the 2 by 8 foot test section was not
removed Therefore the thickness of the base course was not determined at this test
section The top of the base course was tamped with a jumping jack compactor and
the remaining tie bar extending from the adjacent slabs were left in place A welded

Findings and Recommendations
In general we believe that the principal cause of the failures of the cross
bandings is the result of water infiltration into the base course layer Water infiltration
into the base course saturated the base course causing it to pump during vehicular
loading and subsequent loss of subgrade soil support The source of the water may
have been introduced into the pavement section from surface water infiltration through
the joints of the pavements infiltration through cracks seepage of water from the
adjacent areas and or other sources Lack of a joint sealant for the pavements may
have contributed significantly to the 2 foot wide concrete band failures observed
It has been about 6 months since the above referenced test sections were
constructed It is our understanding that the reconstructed cross banding sections have
performed relatively well up to this point in time and you would like to proceed with the
reconstruction of the damaged cross banding In general we believe that the following
guidelines be considered in the reconstruction of the damaged cross bandings
1 Place additional steel reinforcement in the cross banding sections to
provide a stronger structural pavement section to accommodate bending
moments on the individual repaired sections
2 Consult with a structural engineer to determine if the number and location
of tie bars which may have been reduced due to shearing off will be
adequate for the reconstruction The structural engineer should also be
consulted to provide a detail of tie bar connection to adjacent concrete
slabs for the reconstruction of the cross banding panels
o Keeping the base course layer dry unsaturated is important in allowing
the base course to function as intended We recognize that it would be
difficult to provide drainage below the pavement section during the
reconstruction effort however we suggest that seepage sub drainage be
considered within the base course layer
4 The use of a stabilized base course aggregate such as asphalt treated
base for support of the cross banding may be considered in lieu of the
normal type of base course material

Ka Haku Road Pavement Repairs
On June 15th we had a meeting with Belt Collins Hawaii to discuss the
recommendations for the Ka Haku Road pavement repairs presented in our letter dated
June 2nd Based on the meeting we have jointly agreed on the following findings and
repair procedures It should be noted that the repair procedure discussed herein is the
result of constraints presented by the conditions of the repair
Findings and Recommendations
In general we believe that the principal cause of the failures of the cross
bandings is the result of water infiltration into the base course layer It was brought to
our attention that the cross banding was constructed several months after the adjacent
slabs were constructed and the base course was compacted Therefore considering
the frequent and high rainfall at the project site the base course layer supporting the
cross banding was likely exposed to the elements and saturated prior to construction
of the cross banding Recompaction of the base course prior to construction of the
cross banding concrete was unlikely due to obstructions caused by the presence of the
tie bars from the adjacent slabs
As indicated in our previous letter water infiltration into the base course layer
Saturated the base course and caused it to pump during vehicular loading and
subsequent loss of support One of the principal sources of water is most likely from
water infiltration prior to concrete placement for the cross banding since the base
course was exposed to the elements After the cross banding was constructed
additional water may have been introduced into the pavement section from surface
