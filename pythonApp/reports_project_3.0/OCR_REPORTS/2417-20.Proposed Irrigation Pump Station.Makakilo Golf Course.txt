INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Irrigation Pump Station forming a part of the Makakilo Golf
Course development The project site is located on the eastern slope of Puu Makakilo
in the Ewa District of leeward Oahu Hawaii The general location of the project site is
shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering recommenda
tions resulting from the field exploration laboratory testing and engineering analyses
These recommendations are intended for foundation design input and may not be
appropriate for preparation of construction cost estimation or for construction bidding
purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a pump station on the southwesterly side of Lake 18
which will impound water for irrigation purposes for the Makakilo Golf Course
development
Drawings transmitted to us indicate that the proposed pump station will be a one
story concrete building approximately 37 feet by 15 feet in plan dimension A concrete
slab on grade with finish floor elevation at about 603 feet above Mean Sea Level MSL
is anticipated
A pre cast vault of 8 feet by 10 feet in plan dimension is planned for the wet well
of the proposed pump station The bottom of the wet well will be at elevation 585 feet
above MSL Therefore an excavation of about 20 feet deep will be required for the
proposed pump station

The purpose of our geotechnical engineering exploration was to obtain an overview
of the subsurface conditions at the project site to develop a soil rock data set for the
formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to the foundation
design of the proposed pump station
The scope of our exploration consisted of the following tasks and work efforts

site and its vicinity

of the surface soil conditions

below the existing ground surface
4 Laboratory testing of selected sample obtained during the field exploration
to determine the strength properties of the materials encountered
5 Engineering analyses of the field and laboratory data for the formulation of
geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to the fill embankment
design
6 Preparation of this report presenting our findings and recommendations

GENERAL SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located in an existing natural drainageway on the eastern slopes
of Puu Makakilo in the Ewa District on the island of Oahu Hawaii The pump station site
is adjacent to a proposed impoundment area to store water for irrigation purposes for the
Makakilo Golf Course development The general location of the site and its vicinity is
shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The project site is located on a relatively steep and rugged side slope of an existing
gully Boulders scattered randomly on the surface were observed Due to the
predominantly dry weather in the vicinity of the project site at the time of our work the
existing gully was dry The surface soils were also dry during our field exploration
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
The island of Oahu is composed of two 2 shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau
built up by the extrusion of basaltic lavas during the late Pliocene and Pleistocene epochs
Puu Makakilo is situated on the southeastern flank of Waianae along the southern rift
zone
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2417 20 Page 5
Puu Makakilo is a cinder and spatter cone that was built by one or more eruptive
events which produced large quantities of pyroclastic material such as cinder spatter and
ash In some areas welding and or hydrothermal action has fused or cemented the
pyroclastics into weak to strong rock
In addition to the pyroclastics the eruptions produced lava flows of very dense
basalt which are interbedded with the pyroclastic materials Structurally Puu Makakilo
is a heterogeneous mixture of interbedded cinder tuff and dense basalt The units are
discontinuous and difficult to differentiate on an areal basis in the field
In situ chemical weathering of the pyroclastics and basalts has produced a surface
layer of residual soil which mantles most of the site This residual soil grades rapidly to
the underlying volcanic materials

The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling one
1 boring to a depth of fifty one 51 feet below the existing ground surface The
approximate location of the drilled boring is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2

one foot thick overlying a basalt formation The upper twenty 20 feet of the basalt
formation consisted of medium hard highly to moderately weathered and highly jointed
basalt A medium hard to soft welded cinder zone was encountered at approximate
depths of 3 to 7 feet below the existing ground surface
Hard moderately weathered and moderately jointed basalt was encountered from
approximate depths of 20 feet to 51 feet the maximum drilled depth

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on our field exploration and geotechnical engineering analyses it is our
opinion that the construction of the proposed pump station is feasible from a geotechnical
engineering point of view The weathered basalt formation encountered at shallow depths
within the project site should provide adequate foundation supports
Detailed discussions and recommendations are presented in the following sections
Site Preparation
Prior to the commencement of any earthwork all areas within the project grading
limits should be cleared of any vegetation rubbish surface boulders and any deleterious
materials The resulting grubbed and spoil material should be disposed of properly
off site
Any soft weak or yielding areas disclosed during the clearing operations should
be over excavated to firm ground and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with
well compacted engineered fill
Fill Materials
The excavated on site basalt material if reduced to generally less than six 6
inches in size should be a source of general fill material The excavated basalt materials
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2417 20 Page 7
may not have sufficient fines to produce a well graded fill material Therefore some
mixing and handling of the excavated materials may be required
Imported fill materials if required should be tested by the geotechnical engineer
to determine if the fill materials are suitable for the intended use prior to hauling to the
project site
Fill Placement
Fills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding twelve 12 inches in loose
thickness The placed fills should then be compacted to a minimum of 90 of the maxi
mum dry density as determined in accordance with the ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Due to the dry and windy site conditions it is important that proper moisture
conditioning be performed for the fill materials in order to attain the recommended
compacted density It is also advisable to institute a dust control program during grading
operations
Slopes
Fill slopes should be designed at a ratio of two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V
Fills placed on existing slopes having ratios steeper than five horizontal to one
vertical 5H 1V should be keyed and benched into firm and dense in situ weathered
volcanic formations to lessen potential instability of the new fill Details of the benching
requirements are shown on Plate 3 attached to this report The filling operations should
Start at the lowest point and continue up in level horizontal compacted layers in
accordance with the above recommendations for fill placement
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2417 20 Page 8
Fill slopes should be constructed by overfilling and subsequent cutting back to the
final grades in order to provide a well compacted fill slope face
For cut slopes the following maximum slope ratios or flatter are recommended
a Residual Soil 2H 1V
b Cinder
Cc Dense Basalt 1H 1V
Water should be diverted away from the top of the slopes Slope planting should
also be utilized to reduce potential erosion and or instability of the finished slope
surfaces
Excavation
An excavation of about 20 feet deep is anticipated for the wet well of the proposed
pump station
Due to the nature of the basalt formation encountered at the site it is anticipated
that hard excavation utilizing hoerams chipping or blasting may be required Therefore
allowance should be made for the additional expense and time to perform the hard
excavation If blasting is considered care should be exercised to avoid overshooting
which could disrupt the basalt formation resulting in a loss of strength
The stability of the sides of the excavation should be made the responsibility of the
contractor All applicable safety regulations must be complied with
Excavations in relatively competent basalt formations similar to that found at the
project site can normally stand unsupported at relatively steep slopes approximately one
GEOLABS HAWAIi
W O 2417 20 Page 9
quarter horizontal to one vertical for temporary construction purposes The excavation
equipment and methods to be used should be selected by the contractor performing the
work
Foundations
An allowable bearing capacity of up to 8 000 p s f is recommended for the design
of footing foundations bearing on the in situ dense basalt or welded cinder formations
This value may be increased by one third when temporary wind or seismic loads are
considered
Footing foundations should be embedded a minimum of eighteen 18 inches
below the lowest adjacent outside grade
If any soft or weak areas are encountered at the bottom of the footing excavation
they should be over excavated to in situ dense basalt formation and the resulting
depression backfilled with compacted engineered fills
Negligible footing settlements are anticipated for foundations bearing on the in situ
dense basalt formation
Slab On Grade
It is anticipated that the floors of the proposed pump station will be constructed of
a concrete slab on grade The slab may be supported by the in situ dense basalt or
welded cinder formation A minimum four 4 inch thick cushion fill layer consisting of
3B Fine or ASTM C 33 No 67 gravel should be provided below the slab
If pockets seams of loose cinder or soft residual soils are encountered at the slab
subgrade they should be over excavated to the in situ dense basalt formation The
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2417 20 Page 10
resulting depressions should be backfilled with well compacted engineered fills The
exposed slab subgrade should be observed by the project geotechnical engineer prior
to placement of the cushion fill materials
Retaining Structures
According to the drawings provided to us it is our understanding that the
proposed wet well will be about 20 feet deep The external walls of the wet well should
be designed as retaining walls It is also our understanding that the westerly perimeter
wall of the proposed pump station building will retain approximately 2 5 feet of additional
backfills behind the wall The recommended guidelines for retaining wall design are listed
below
1 Lateral earth pressure for level backfill
Wall unrestrained at the top 35 p c f equivalent
active condition fluid pressure
Wall restrained at the top 50 p c f equivalent
at rest condition fluid pressure
2 An allowable bearing capacity of up to 8 000 p s f is recommended for
foundations bearing on the in situ dense basalt formation
3 A passive earth resistance of 5 000 p s f per foot of depth may be used for
foundations embedded in the in situ basalt formation
4 A base friction factor of 0 45 may also be used to determine the resistance
to sliding
5 A subsurface drainage system should be incorporated behind the walls to
reduce water pressure build up behind the walls

Backfill Placement
It is anticipated that backfilling will be required for below grade structures and for
trenches The maximum particle size of the backfill materials should not exceed three 3
inches Trench and structural backfills should be placed in loose level lifts not exceeding
eight 8 inches and compacted to a minimum of 90 maximum dry density as
determined by the ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 The upper two 2 feet of the backfills
below road subgrade level should be compacted to a minimum of 95 maximum dry
density
For below grade structures it is recommended that a clean granular material such
as 3B Fine or ASTM C 33 No 67 gravel be utilized as backfills to reduce the amount
of compactive effort required to attain the recommended 90 percent compaction The top
12 inches of backfill snould consist of non expansive impervious material to reduce water
infiltration hehind the wall

be provided in the paved areas

Design Review
Drawings and specifications for construction of the proposed pump station should
be forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comment prior to
construction This review is needed to determine adherence to the earthwork and
foundation recommendations given herein If this review is not made the geotechnical
engineer cannot assume responsibility for misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during site grading and foundation construction phases
of the work This is to observe compliance with the design concepts specifications or
recommendations and to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions
differ from those anticipated at the time of preparation of this report The
recommendations given in this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and approved by
the geotechnical engineer prior to delivery at the site
If actual exposed soil rock conditions encountered during construction are different
from those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling one 1 boring to
a depth of fifty one 51 feet below the existing ground surface at the approximate location
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 A truck mounted drill rig was used to drill the boring
The material encountered in the boring were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist engineer who continuously monitored the drilling
operations The classifications were further reviewed by visual inspection and testing in
the laboratory A graphic presentation of the materials encountered in the boring is
Samples were obtained using diamond core drilling techniques Core drilling is a
rotary drilling method which uses a hollow bit to cut into the rock formation The rock
material left in the annulus of the bit is mechanically recovered for examination
classification and testing The recovered core were logged by a geologist from our firm
Descriptions of the recovered rock cores are presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 1
and A 1 2
Recovery REC is used as a subjective guide to the interpretation of the relative
quality of rock masses Recovery is defined as the actual length of material recovered
from a coring attempt vs the length of the core attempt e g if 3 7 feet of material is
recovered from a 5 0 foot core run the recovery would be 74 and would be shown on
the Boring Logs as REC 74
The Rock Quality Designation RQD is also a subjective guide to the relative
quality of rock masses RQD is defined as the percentage of the core run that is sound
material that is in excess of four 4 inches in length without any discontinuities
discounting any drilling induced fractures or breaks if 2 5 feet of sound material is
recovered from a 5 0 foot core run the RQD would be 50 and would be shown on the
Boring Logs as REC 50 Generally the following is used to describe the relative rock
quality based on the Practical Handbook of Physical Properties of Rocks and Minerals
Rock Quality RQD
Very Poor 0 25
Poor 25 50
Fair 50 75
Good 75 90
Excellent 90 100
