This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations and testing
services performed during site work construction house grading and utility line
installation for the Parcel A portion of The Peninsula at Hawaii Kai project in general
accordance with our fee proposal dated May 7 2001 and additional budget request dated
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located along both sides of Wailua Street and west of the
intersection with Lunalilo Home Road in the Hawaii Kai area on the Island of Oahu
Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1 Most of the site is a peninsula constructed of man made
materials extending out into Kuapa Pond The total project area is about 42 65 acres
Parcel A is the southern peninsula portion of the project as shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services
pertaining to the site work construction house grading and utility line installation for the
Parcel A portion of The Peninsula at Hawaii Kai project
SITE GRADING AND UTILITY INSTALLATION
The site work construction house grading and utility line installation for the Parcel A
portion of The Peninsula at Hawaii Kai project were observed by Geolabs Inc from May
23 2001 to The site work construction consisted of clearing and
grubbing and placement and compaction of fills on the order of up to about 5 feet thick to
achieve the design finished grades for the project The site grading and utility installation

work were performed by Goodfellow Brothers Inc The house grading consisted of
footing excavation and subgrade preparation of foundations slabs on grade and

Samples of the on site and imported materials utilized as fill and backfill materials
were obtained and transported to our office for testing The maximum dry densities and
optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test results are summarized
on Plate 3
The fill and backfill materials were generally moisture conditioned to above
optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum
dry density as determined in accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557 In the roadway
areas the last lift of fill and backfill materials were generally compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative density
During the earthwork operations periodic field density tests were performed in
general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 2922 Nuclear Gauge Method
Results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 4 1 through 4 67 of
this report These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on
constructed fills and backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by
pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and elevations
should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas
assessed to be unsatisfactory were generally reworked until the specified minimum
compaction was obtained and or approved by our field representative based on visual
observation and hand probing
Our services consisted of part time field observation of the contractor s fill
placement subgrade preparation and compaction procedures and confirmation of
subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above grading operations
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the site grading and utility installation work for the
project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report were
performed in conformance with the general intent of the recommendations in the following
references

grades on the subject project subsequent to our observation and testing period without our
