SUMMARY OF RECOMMENDATTONS
Two general areas within the site and with very
different soil conditions were evaluated for
possible siting of the hospital complex
The lowlying portions of the site are underlain
by soft compressible soils to depths of up to 80
feet below the existing ground The soft ground
conditions have a significant effect on the
design of the site development and construction
costs Special ground stabilization is required
prior to filling over the soft soils
Additionally special procedures such as sand
drains and surcharge fills would be required
during the site grading in order to accelerate
the ground settlements caused by compression of
the soft soils due to the weight of the new
fills The soft soils also limit the height of
fill which can be placed due to stability
considerations and necessitate the use of pile
foundations to support the proposed structures
Generally the soft ground area may be utilized
if the height of fill can be minimized and there
is sufficient time to allow for the site
stabilization and ground settlement to occur
prior to building construction

W O 1551 00 Page 2
In light of the above considerations the upper
hillside area was evaluated as a possible site
for the hospital Generally competent soil
conditions are anticipated at the upper hillside
area however it appears that a very large
quantity of excavation would be required to
place the entire hospital complex within the
cut Additionally slope stabilization may be
required for the very large cut slope resulting
from the excavation Generally the upper
hillside area appears feasible from a geotech
nical engineering standpoint provided the large
excavation quantities can be accommodated in
the site grading
It appears that a third location for the
structures may be along the base of the hillside
and soft ground where lower volume of hillside
excavation is required and the thickness of the
soft layer is smaller The feasibility and

whether the hospital complex could be

The purpose of our investigation was to evaluate the subsurface
conditions at the site to provide pertinent soil data such as shear
strength compressibility CBR value swelling potential and soil
classification for the site grading and foundation design of the proposed
hospital complex
The scope of the investigation was performed in general accordance
with our revised proposal dated May 15 1985 and included a review of
available aerial photographs geologic literature and soil reports of the
general area field reconnaissance drilling of 11 borings laboratory
soils testing engineering analysis of the field and laboratory data and
report preparation
A seismic study as described in our proposal of March 27 1985
was also performed for the project site The results of the study will
be presented in a subsequent report

testing are presented in the appendices of the report

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The project site is located within the western Volcanic Intrusive
Province of Luzon and comprises Recent to Pliocene Quaternary Volcanic
deposits The E W Trending San Antonio Fracture zone is probably the
closest identified major Fault zone about 8 to 10 miles northerly of the
site
At the project site the volcanic deposits are primarily of
pyroclastic origin and consist of lithic tuffs volcanic breccia
agglomerate and volcanic mudflows lahar believed to have been
generated by explosive eruptions from Mount Natib Mount Santa Rita and
Mount Balakibok three of the ma jor volcanic centers which comprises the
Zzanbales Bataan Volcanic Highlands
Examination of the geomorphology of the area leads to the
conjecture that the site is located on a marine deltaic flat located at
the toe of rugged mountain terrain The lewlying area was cut during low
stands of the sea by streams originating in the mountainous areas to the
east of the site
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Page 10
Numerous glacial eustatic sea level variations caused the emergence
and submergence of the land mass that produced soil deposits at various
levels that can be identified at both above and below the present sea
level
The site investigation carried out revealed that the surface
materials in the lowlying portions of the study area are mainly of a
low energy depositional estuarine or lagoonal environment which would be
encountered in a sheltered bay area such as Subic Bay
Owing to the low energy estuarine or deltaic environment of
deposition these soft and highly compressible sediments would tend to be
poorly consolidated clays and silts containing traces of organic matter
underlain at depths probably 80 feet or more below MLIW by more
granular deposits of ash flow tuffs and nudflow debris from the eruption
of Mount Natib These deposits were probably contemporaneous with the
Pleistocene sea level changes

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Poor soil conditions were encountered in the lowlying portions of
the site The soft compressible deposits were found to be as thick as
80 feet along Binictican Drive and thinning out near the base of the
hillside area Originally the field investigation was planned only for
the lowlying area however during the field work it became apparent
that the soft ground condition would severely affect the design and
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1551 00 Page 16
construction of the project Therefore some of the borings planned for
the low area were drilled along the lower slopes of the hillside to
provide preliminary information to assist in siting the buildings
Preliminary design information for both the soft lowlying area and the
hillside are presented Additional field investigation is required when
the final building location is determined
The lewlying area with elevations of about 3 to 5 feet is subject
to flooding during rains see Photographs 5 and 6 Therefore it
appears that the general site will have to be raised for flooding and
drainage considerations Preliminary information from the project civil
engineer indicates that the site may have to be raised to about elevation
20 to 30 feet above MSL Since the existing low ground is presently at
about elevation 3 to 5 feet fills of 15 to 17 feet or more are
indicated Our embankment slope stability analysis indicates that the
fill over the soft ground should be minimized and kept to less than about
12 feet to minimize the possibility of Slope failure If more than 12
feet of fill mst be placed special ground strengthening or staged
loading must be considered
The weight of the new fill will cause considerable settlement
consolidation of the underlying soft campressible deposits Also we
believe significant long term secondary settlement can be anticipated
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1551 00 Page 17
Hence special soil improvement methods such as installation of vertical
sand drains and or precampression by surcharging fill will be required
In order to monitor the effect of the large site filling an instru
mentation program is recommended
The borings and visual observations indicate that the hillside
area is composed of volcanic tuff ash and agglomerates which may be
classified as soft rock
For the hillside area the most critical aspect of investigation is
the stability evaluation of the proposed high cut slopes which are
necessary to provide the needed level area to accommodate the siting of
the proposed hospital From the results of the site investigation we
believe two of the most important controlling factors affecting the slope
stability are the groundwater conditions and the earthquake forces
The required building foundation system will be governed by the
siting of the buildings Relatively long Pile foundation will be
required for the foundation support of the hospital buildings located on
the lowlying portions of the site However if the hillside area can be
used to accommodate the hospital building site then we believe that
spread footing foundation bearing on the volcanic tuff material may be
used

It is estimated that the clearing and grubbing of the surface
vegetation and organic material will average 12 inches over the
fill portion of the site
The existing surface soil in the lowlying areas is marshy and wet
and does not have sufficient strength to support heavy construction
equipment Therefore after clearing and grubbing a working
platform or drainage blanket should be established prior to the
major filling of the swamp area The working platform may consist
of a combination of a synthetic filter fabric and a 12 inch gravel
layer The filter fabric reduces the required gravel thickness and
eliminates the strict gradation requirements of the drainage
blanket since it will act as a filter medium to prevent gravel loss
into the soft ground and prevent the mud and fine soil particles
from contaminating the granular blanket material It also provides
tensile reinforcement to the gravel layer and permits earlier use
of heavy equipment on a minimum thickness of compacted aggregate
The filter fabric is usually rolled onto the soft ground by
laborers The gravel is then placed over the fabric by small
equipment and fill is added until a suitable platform for heavy
equipment is obtained
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1551 00 Page 20
A subdrainage system should be incorporated in the drainage blanket
to facilitate subsurface drainage and pore water dissipation which
will shorten the drainage path and thereby shorten the conscli
dation waiting period

the primary settlement

strength When most of the settlement is completed a second

Soil Improvement Program
In order to accelerate the settlements reduce the waiting time and
to compensate to the long term secondary settlement a soil
improvement program consisting of the use of the following
methods is recommended
1 Surcharging
The use of surcharge loads i e loads from temporary
stockpile of earth fill in excess of that to be applied by
the permanent fill can accelerate the settlement Plates C 4
through C show the effects of the surcharge on the percen
tage of total primary settlement occurring at the end of one
year We have assumed that 1 year would be the maximum
practical waiting time for the project
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1551 00 Page 24
The results summarized on Plates C 4 through C 6 indicate that
in order to make the surcharge program effective surcharge
fill thickness in excess of 15 feet is required However
because of the very low strength of the soft soil at the site
the thickness of fill and surcharge that can be placed over
the area without creating an embankment stability problem is
limited
Vertical Drains
Since the amount of surcharging is limited by the weak
strength of the soil the combined use of vertical drains and
surcharging can be considered The presence of the vertical
drain reduces the drainage path for the water squeezed out of
the soft soil as it compresses Hence it greatly accelerates
the rate of settlement The drain has to be extended from the
ground surface to the bottom of the soft layer A schematic
diagram of a typical vertical drain installation is shown on
Plate 6
Different types of vertical drains e g prefabricated and
sand drain are available for the project site However
because of the advantages in both cost and its strengthening
effect on the soft soil it is our opinion that sand drains
are preferrable to prefabricated drains e g plastic band
drain for this site
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1551 00 Page 25

Slope Stability Analysis
a I i ne ee
Slope stability analysis for the perimeter of the fill embankment
at the lowlying area and the high ground cut slope were performed
The analysis were carried out by using the computor STABR developed
by the Department of Civil Engineering at the University of California at
Berkeley The program calculates the factor of safety by using both the
Ordinary Method of Slices and the Bishop s Modified Method both of which
involve circular failure surface STABR can also perform seismic
analysis of the slope when appropriate seismic coefficients are input
For the analysis of the perimeter of the fill embankment various
earthquake conditions are considered The analysis studies the effect
of the fill and surcharge lacd on the factor of safety of various
embankments with different slope ratios Also the effects of stepped
grading with benches are analyzed The results of these analysis are
sunmarized on Plates D 1 through D 2
For the stability of the high ground cut slope a parametric study
with variation of the following factors is carried out
1 Shear Strength The strength of the scil which forms the
slope is assumed to have a cohesion value of 100 psf and a
friction angle varing between 35 to 45 degree
2 Groundwater Condition Study was made by varying the
groundwater level from the surface of the slope saturated
condition to a depth below the critical slip surface dry
condition
3 Earthquake Condition Earthquake coefficients of 0 0 no
earthquake and 0 25g with the design earthquake were used
in the study The seismic force is assumed to act at the
center of the slice
in the study
The results of the study are summarized on Plates D 3 through D 5
A typical output from the computer program STABR is shown on Plates_
