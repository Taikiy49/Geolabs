This report presents the results of our soils investigation at
the above project which is located at Waipio Oahu Hawaii This
investigation was performed in accordance with our proposal dated July
27 1983 The general vicinity of the project site is shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1
PROJECT CONSIDERATION
It is our understanding that the proposed construction will
consist of a one to two story building with CMU masonry walls and
on grade slabs Some site grading work will be required to prepare
the site There will be two columns loaded to 112 kips each The
loading for the remaining columns is anticipated to be about 10 kips
per column The bearing walls will generally carry 1 kip foot of
loading
SCOPE OF SERVICES
The purpose of this investigation is to explore the subsurface
soil conditions to provide design recommendations for the foundation
Support of the structure Four borings were drilled to depths ranging
from 16 0 to 16 5 feet below the existing ground surface at the
approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Disturbed and undisturbed soil samples were obtained and brought
back to our laboratory for further examination and testing of selected
samples The logs of borings are shown on Plates A 1 through A 4
The results of our laboratory tests are presented on Plates B 1 and
B 2
SITE CONDITIONS
The project site fronts Lumikula Street in the Waipio area in
central Oahu Most of the site is relatively level except for the
rear one third of the area where the ground slopes at about 3
horizontal to 1 vertical down to an existing concrete lined drainage
ditch about 15 feet below the level area Generally the front
one third area had been recently cleared and Supports only sparse to
medium grasses The remainder of the site is overgrown with 6 to 8
feet of Kiawe trees and dense grasses
Geologically the site is situated on the Leeward side of the
Koolau Mountain range where the basalt had been deeply weathered into
residual soils

The subsurface conditions disclosed in our borings consisted of

The fill material was the same type of soil as the underlying in situ
residual soil Our laboratory swell tests indicate that these soils
have low swelling potential if maintained near the optimum moisture
content
No groundwater was encountered in our borings at the time of our
field exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The very stiff to hard reddish brown clayey silt previously
placed as fill will generally be suitable for foundation and on grade
slab support of the proposed structure provided that the soils are not
allowed to dry out excessively prior to final concrete placement
It is anticipated that only minor amounts of new fill will be
required to support the slabs where the new building will be located
along the top of the existing slope

The on grade slabs may bear on existing fill soils The
foundation and subgrade preparation recommendations are listed in the
following sections
Foundation Support
Spread or continuous footing foundation may be used for the
structure We recommend a minimum footing embedment of 24 inches
below the lowest adjacent finished grade for the heavy column
footings and an embedment of 18 inches for perimeter slab footings
thickened edge An allowable soil bearing value of 3000 p s f may
be used
For any footing placed on or near a Slope the footing embedment
should be deepened so that there will be a minimum of 10 feet
horizontal set back distance between the outside edge of the footing
and the face of the slope
The bottom of all footing excavations should be cleaned of loose
material and bear on firm in situ soil We recommend that the footing
excavation be observed by a soils engineer to see that adequate
bearing has been obtained
The ground surface should be shaped to drain water away from the
foundations

Site Grading
Prior to filling the area to receive any fill building or
pavements should be cleared of vegetation debris and organic matter
and grubbed of tree roots larger than 1 inch in diameter
The subgrade below the slab cushion should be wetted or dried to
optimum moisture and recompacted to 90 of the maximum density
established by ASTM D 1557 78
Fills and backfills should consist of select on site reddish
brown clayey silt or imported non expansive soil such as select
borrow and should be placed in maximum 8 inch loose lifts and then
compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density The on site
brown to reddish brown clayey silt should be placed near optimum
moisture content in order to reduce its swelling potential Any fill
placed on the slope should be properly keyed and benched as shown on
Plate 3 I any imported soil is used for fill it should first be
tested and approved by a qualified soils engineer
On Grade Slab
Concrete on grade slabs are planned for the majority of the
building It is recommended that on grade slabs be placed over a
4 inch layer of No 3 Fine gravel base and be separated from wall
foundations

Retaining Walls
It is our understanding that retaining walls may be required for
the proposed construction The following guidelines are recommended
for the wall design

Wall restrained at the top 50 p s equivalent
at rest condition fluid pressure
level backfil11 per foot of depth
2 An allowable bearing value of up to 3000 p s f is recommended
for foundations bearing on the stiff on site soil
3 For the wall footing on level areas a passive earth resistance
of 300 p s f per foot of depth may be used
4 A base friction of 0 4 may also be used against sliding of the
wall

The wall foundations on relatively flat areas should be embedded
a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade
Any wall that is parallel and close to the top of the slopes
should be embedded an additional amount to maintain a minimum
10 foot horizontal set back distance measured from the outside
slope face to the edge of the wall footing
A subsurface drainage system subdrains or weepholes should be
utilized behind the retaining walls Any subsurface drainage
system should be properly designed to flow freely under gravity
and drain to lower open areas away from foundations
Granular material such as No 3B Fine gravel should be utilized
as a backfill material behind the new retaining walls to minimize
the compactive effort required and build up of lateral pressures
against the walls
Pavements

recommended below

Drainage
The finished grade of the lot outside the slab pavement and
footings should be shaped to shed water away from the foundations and
to avoid ponding condition Also it is advised that gutter systems
be installed around the house and that gutter water be diverted far
away from the foundations and walkways Excessive landscape watering
near the foundations and slab should also be avoided
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have
concrete bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the
subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper
performance of the above recommended foundations since ponded water
could cause excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent
heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations should be
properly backfilled tight against slab footing immediately after
setting of the concrete to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and
maintained to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing
foundation

Design Review
Plans and specifications for site grading and foundation for the
proposed construction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for
review and written comments prior to construction to see that the
intent of our recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the footing excavations backfilling
operation and subgrade preparation be observed by the soils engineer
during construction This is to see that adequate compaction has been
obtained and the subsurface conditions are consistent with the
findings of our investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested
and approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above inspections
