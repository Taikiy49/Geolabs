Building Foundations
Based on the anticipated subsurface soil conditions at the new building sites we
believe that shallow foundations consisting of spread and or continuous footings may be
used for support of the proposed lightly loaded one story buildings planned at the project
site An allowable bearing pressure on order of about 2 500 pounds per square foot psf
may be used for the foundation design of the proposed new single story steel framed
structures These bearing values are for dead plus live loads and may be increased by
one third for transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic forces
However due to the potential for presence of soft and or loose subsoils at shallow
depths as encountered in our field exploration a 2 foot thick layer of compacted fill
material should be provided below the bottom of footings for more uniform support The
over excavation for the compacted fill should extend beyond the edges of the footing a
minimum of 12 inches Prior to placement of the compacted fill the bottom of the footing
over excavations should be scarified to a minimum depth of 8 _ inches
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and recompacted to not
less than 90 percent relative compaction If soft and or loose soils are encountered at the
over excavated subgrade levels deeper over excavation may be required to expose the
underlying firm material Alternatively the bottom of the over excavations may be lined
with a layer of filter fabric such as Mirafi 180N or equivalent prior to placement of the
compacted fill
The compacted fill may consist of the excavated on site granular soils provided
that the largest rock fragments in the material is less than 3 inches in maximum
dimension and that the materials are free of vegetation and deleterious materials
Compacted fill should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content
placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a
minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place
dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same
soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 91 test procedures Optimum moisture
is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry
density
In general footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the
lowest adjacent finished grades Footings located adjacent to planned retaining walls
should be embedded deep enough to avoid surcharging the retaining wall foundations
If foundations are located next to utility trenches or easements they should be
embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane extending
upward from the bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility
lines This requirement is necessary to avoid surcharging adjacent below grade
structures with additional structural loads and to reduce the potential for foundation
settlement

Foundation Probing
Our previous experience and current field exploration at the project site indicate
that cavities and or voids are commonly encountered in coralline deposits To reduce the
potential for loss of foundation support resulting from collapse of the cavities below
foundations we recommend that a program of cavity probing and grouting at the
foundation locations be implemented during construction
For estimating purposes we recommend that probe holes be drilled at each
spread footing location one probehole for every 50 square feet of footing area and at
10 feet on center along wall foundations The probe holes should be at least 3 inches in
diameter and should extend to at least 10 feet below the planned bottom of foundation
elevation If cavities and or voids are encountered or suspected during the probing
operation additional probe holes should be drilled at closer spacing to aid in delineating
the vertical and lateral extent of the cavity and or void The probeholes and cavities
discovered should be backfilled with sand cement grout injected at low to moderate
pressures We recommend that a low strength sand cement grout with a slump range of
6 to 9 inches be utilized for the grouting operations In lieu of the sand cement grout fluid
lean concrete such as controlled low strength material flowable fill may also be used for
the grouting operation
Because of the potential for encountering cavities and or voids at the site we
recommend that unit prices for additional probing and grouting be obtained during
bidding In addition the probe drill should be available on site until the probing and
grouting operation is complete The contractor should also be made aware that a longer
lag time between probing grouting operations and foundation construction might be
required in the construction schedule

Slabs On Grade
Based on our field exploration results we anticipate that new concrete
slabs on grade for the proposed development will likely bear on coralline materials and
therefore we believe that conventional slab on grade design may be utilized However if
clayey soils or loose soils are encountered at the subgrade level these soils should be
over excavated and replaced with select granular fill materials compacted to a minimum
of 90 percent relative compaction
For more uniform support slabs on grade should be supported on a cushion layer
consisting of 4 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation The gravel
cushion layer would also serve as a capillary break To reduce moisture infiltration
through the slab and subsequent damage to floor coverings an impervious plastic
moisture vapor membrane is recommended on top of the gravel cushion layer
Consideration may be given to the placement of 2 inches of moist fine sand above the
plastic membrane to provide protection of the membrane and to aid in curing of the
concrete Flexible floor coverings such as carpet or sheet vinyl should be considered
since they can better mask minor slab cracking
For slabs subjected to vehicular traffic and or vibrations due to machinery we
recommend that the cushion fill consist of 6 inches of compacted select borrow subbase
materials in lieu of the 4 inch gravel cushion layer 2 inch moist fine sand layer and the
vapor barrier The select borrow subbase material should be compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative compaction For the design of structural slabs a modulus of subgrade
reaction of 250 pounds per square inch per inch of deflection pci may be used for the
compacted subbase material Provisions should be made for proper load transfer across
the slab joints which are subjected to vehicular traffic The thickened edges of slabs
adjacent to any unpaved area should be embedded at least 12 inches below the lowest
adjacent grade
For exterior concrete walkways we recommend that a minimum 4 inch thick
cushion layer consisting of select borrow subbase material or No 3B Fine gravel
ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation be provided below the concrete walkway slab
Construction joints should be provided at intervals equal to the width of the walkways with
expansion joints at right angle intersections
Areas adjacent to concrete slabs should be backfilled tightly against the slab and
graded to divert water away from the slab to reduce the potential for water ponding
around the foundation Subgrade soils should be prepared in accordance with the
recommendations presented in the Site Preparation section of this letter

Site Preparation
As previously mentioned our field exploration disclosed that the project site may
be underlain by loose subsoils and cavities near the existing ground surface Therefore
special attention should be given to the subgrade preparation for the project
development
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed Vegetation organic debris construction debris rubbish
old pavements and other unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of
properly off site or in a designated area to reduce the potential for contamination of the
excavated materials with the cleared and grubbed spoils Soft and yielding areas
encountered during clearing and grubbing should be over excavated to expose firm
natural material and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with well compacted fill
Slabs and foundations of the existing structures to be demolished should be
completely removed Over excavation resulting from the demolition should be backfilled
with compacted granular fill material Existing utilities to be abandoned should be
removed and the resulting excavation should be properly backfilled with select granular
fill material moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture content placed in 6 to 8 inch
level loose lifts and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Utilities
to be abandoned in place under the proposed structure should be backfilled by pumping
lean concrete under low pressure
The excavated subgrades underneath future slabs and pavements should be
proof rolled with a minimum 10 ton vibratory drum roller or similar heavy equipment a
minimum of 6 passes to detect the presence of potential near surface cavities or loose
zones If the proof rolling operations detect a near surface cavity or loose zone the
area should be ripped open cleaned of debris and then backfilled with compacted fill
material or lean concrete
In general the excavated on site granular soils may be re used as a source of fill
material provided that the largest rock fragment is less than 3 inches in maximum
dimension and that the materials are free of vegetation and deleterious materials Select
granular fill should consist of non expansive granular material such as crushed coral and
basalt The material should be well graded from coarse to fine with no particles larger
than 3 inches in largest dimension and should contain between 10 and 30 percent
particles passing the No 200 sieve The material should have a laboratory CBR value of
20 or more and should have a maximum swell of 1 percent or less when tested in
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1883 The on site coralline materials should
be suitable for use as select granular fill material provided that they are processed to
meet the above requirements Imported fill material should be tested for conformance
with these recommendations prior to delivery to the project site for its intended use

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The Island of Oahu is composed largely of the weathered remnants of two extinct
shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae forms the bulk of the
western third of the island while the younger shield Koolau forms the majority of the eastern
two thirds of the island It is believed that Waianae became extinct while Koolau was still
active and its eastern flank is partially buried below Koolau lavas in central Oahu
During the Pleistocene Epoch sea levels fluctuated in response to the cycles of
continental glaciation As the glaciers grew and advanced less water was available to fill the
oceanic basins such that sea levels fell below the present stands of the sea When the
glaciers melted and receded an excess of water became available such that the sea levels
rose to above the present sea level
The higher sea level stands caused the formation of deltas and fans of accumulated
terrigenous sediments in the heads of old bays accumulated reef deposits at
correspondingly higher elevations and lagoonal marine sediments in the quiet waters
protected by fringing reefs The lower sea stands caused streams to carve valleys in the
sediments and reef deposits Subaerial exposure of the sediments and calcareous materials
caused consolidation of the soft deltaic materials and lagoonal deposits and induration of the
calcareous reef materials Land development and reclamation projects within the last century
have brought the project site to its present form

moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content placed in level lifts not
exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a
percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with
ASTM D 1557 91 test procedures Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by
dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
In general footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grades Footings located adjacent to planned retaining walls should be
embedded deep enough to avoid surcharging the retaining wall foundations If foundations
are located next to utility trenches or easements they should be embedded below a
one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane extending upward from the bottom
edge of the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility lines This requirement is
necessary to avoid surcharging adjacent below grade structures with additional structural
loads and to reduce the potential for foundation settlement
Lateral loads acting on the structures may be resisted by frictional resistance
developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil and by passive earth
pressure acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system A coefficient of

Foundation Probing
Our previous experience and current field exploration at the project site indicate that
cavities and or voids are commonly encountered in coralline deposits To reduce the
potential for loss of foundation support resulting from collapse of the cavities below
foundations we recommend that a program of cavity probing and grouting at the foundation
locations be implemented during construction
For estimating purposes we recommend that probe holes be drilled at each spread
footing location one probe hole for every 50 square feet of footing area and at 10 feet on
center along wall foundations The probe holes should be at least 3 inches in diameter and
should extend to at least 10 feet below the planned bottom of foundation elevation If cavities
and or voids are encountered or suspected during the probing operation additional probe
holes should be drilled at closer spacing to aid in delineating the vertical and lateral extent
of the cavity and or void The probe holes and cavities discovered should be backfilled with
sand cement grout injected at low to moderate pressures We recommend that a low
strength sand cement grout with a slump range of 6 to 9 inches be utilized for the grouting
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 4270 00 Page 10
operations In lieu of the sand cement grout fluid lean concrete such as controlled low
strength material flowable fill may also be used for the grouting operation
Because of the potential for encountering cavities and or voids at the site we
recommend that unit prices for additional probing and grouting be obtained during bidding
In addition the probe drill should be available on site until the probing and grouting operation
is complete The contractor should also be made aware that a longer lag time between
probing grouting operations and foundation construction might be required in the construction
schedule
Slabs On Grade
Based on our field exploration results we anticipate that new concrete slabs on grade
for the proposed development will likely bear on coralline materials and therefore we believe
that conventional slab on grade design may be utilized However if clayey soils or loose
soils are encountered at the subgrade level these soils should be over excavated and
replaced with select granular fill materials compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction
For more uniform support slabs on grade should be supported on a cushion layer
consisting of 4 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation The gravel
cushion layer would also serve as a capillary break To reduce the potential for moisture
infiltration through the slab and subsequent damage to floor coverings an impervious plastic
moisture vapor membrane is recommended on top of the gravel cushion layer Consideration
may be given to the placement of 2 inches of moist fine sand above the plastic membrane
to provide protection of the membrane and to aid in curing of the concrete Flexible floor
coverings such as carpet or sheet vinyl should be considered since they can better mask
minor slab cracking
For slabs subjected to vehicular traffic and or vibrations due to machinery we
recommend that the cushion fill consist of 6 inches of compacted select borrow subbase
materials in lieu of the 4 inch gravel cushion layer 2 inch moist fine sand layer and the

vapor barrier The select borrow subbase material should be compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative compaction For the design of structural slabs a modulus of subgrade
reaction of 250 pounds per square inch per inch of deflection pci may be used for the
compacted subbase material Provisions should be made for proper load transfer across the
slab joints which are subjected to vehicular traffic The thickened edges of slabs adjacent

grade
For exterior concrete walkways we recommend that a minimum 4 inch thick cushion

Site Preparation
As previously mentioned our field exploration disclosed that the project site may be
underlain by loose subsoils and cavities near the existing ground surface Therefore special
attention should be given to the subgrade preparation for the project development
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed Vegetation organic debris construction debris rubbish
old pavements and other unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of properly
off site or in a designated area to reduce the potential for contamination of the excavated
materials with the cleared and grubbed spoils Soft and yielding areas encountered during
clearing and grubbing should be over excavated to expose firm natural material and the
resulting excavation should be backfilled with well compacted fill
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 4270 00 Page 12
Slabs and foundations of the existing structures to be demolished should be
completely removed Over excavation resulting from the demolition should be backfilled with
compacted granular fill material Existing utilities to be abandoned should be removed and
the resulting excavation should be properly backfilled with select granular fill material
moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture content placed in 6 to 8 inch level loose
lifts and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Utilities to be
abandoned in place under the proposed structure should be backfilled by pumping lean
concrete under low pressure
The excavated subgrades underneath future slabs and pavements should be
proof rolled with a minimum 10 ton vibratory drum roller or similar heavy equipment a
minimum of 6 passes to detect the presence of potential near surface cavities or loose
zones If the proof rolling operations detect a near surface cavity or loose zone the area
should be ripped open cleaned of debris and then backfilled with compacted fill material
or lean concrete
In general the excavated on site granular soils may be re used as a source of fill
material provided that the largest rock fragment is less than 3 inches in maximum dimension
and that the materials are free of vegetation and deleterious materials Select granular fill
should consist of non expansive granular material such as crushed coral and basalt The
material should be well graded from coarse to fine with no particles larger than 3 inches in
largest dimension and should contain between 10 and 30 percent particles passing the
No 200 sieve The material should have a laboratory CBR value of 20 or more and should
have a maximum swell of 1 percent or less when tested in accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 1883 The on site coralline materials should be suitable for use as select
granular fill material provided that they are processed to meet the above requirements
Imported fill material should be tested for conformance with these recommendations prior to
delivery to the project site for its intended use

Utility Trench
We envision that new utility connections may be required for the proposed project In
general for support of the utility lines we recommend that a granular bedding consisting of
6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation be used under the pipes
Free draining granular materials such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation
should also be used for the trench backfill up to about 12 inches above the pipes to provide
adequate support around the pipes and to reduce the compaction effort of the backfill thus
reducing the possibility of damaging the pipes It is critical to use this free draining material
to reduce the potential for formation of voids below the haunches of pipes and to provide
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 4270 00 Page 15
adequate support for the sides of the pipes Improper trench backfill could result in
settlement of the backfill and damage to the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipes to the
top of the subgrade or finished grade may consist of select fill material The backfill should
be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture placed in maximum 8 inch level
loose lifts and mechanically compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction to
reduce the potential for future ground subsidence Where trenches are below pavement
areas the upper 3 feet of the trench backfill below the pavement subgrade should be
compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction
Drainage
The finished grades outside the buildings should be sloped to shed water away from
foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also advised that gutter systems
be installed around the buildings and that the discharge be diverted away from the foundation
and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near the foundations and slabs should
also be avoided Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have concrete bottoms
and drains to reduce the potential for water infiltration into the subsoils Drainage swales
should be provided as soon as possible and maintained to drain surface run off away from
the slabs and foundations
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the above
foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation and
subsequent loss of strength and or heaving of the near surface clayey soils The foundation
excavations should be properly backfilled against the footings immediately after setting of the
concrete to reduce the potential for water infiltration

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content and unit weight determinations were performed on selected soil
samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties The results of these
tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Two sieve analysis tests ASTM C136 were performed on selected soil samples to
evaluate the gradation characteristics of the soils and to aid in soil classification Graphic
presentation of the test results are provided on Plate B 1
One laboratory California Bearing Ratio Test ASTM D 1883 was performed on the
near surface soils to evaluate the pavement support characteristics of the soils The test
results are presented on Plate B 2
One Modified Proctor compaction test ASTM D 1557 was performed on the typical
near surface soils encountered at the site The test results are presented on Plate B 3
