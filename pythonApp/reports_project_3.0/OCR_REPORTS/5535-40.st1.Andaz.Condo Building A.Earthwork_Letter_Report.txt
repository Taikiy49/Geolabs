In accordance with your request we conducted a geotechnical engineering
exploration in support of the mass grading design of Villa A within Andaz Maui at Wailea
Resort on the Island of Maui Hawaii This letter report presents our earthwork
recommendations for the development of the project only It should be noted that the
earthwork recommendations provided are subject to the limitations noted at the end of
this letter report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project consists of construction of a new Villa A within Andaz Maui at Wailea
Resort on the Island of Maui Hawaii Based on the information provided the new building
will be four stories with partial basement conditions The building will have a ground finish
floor elevation of 62 feet Mean Sea Level MSL
Based on the preliminary grading plan cuts of up to about 16 5 feet deep and fills
of up to about 6 feet high will be required to achieve the design finished grades New cart
paths and underground utility lines will also be installed for the project
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by drilling and sampling
three test borings designated as Boring Nos 301 through 303 extending down to about
21 to 31 feet below the existing ground surface Based on our borings the subsurface
conditions generally consist of fill materials underlain by hard basalt formation
Fill materials encountered generally consist of stiff to very stiff sandy silts with
some gravel extending down to about 4 to 5 feet below the ground surface Fill materials

Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork areas within the contract grading limits should be
cleared and grubbed thoroughly Vegetation debris deleterious materials old
foundations of the existing structures walkways and other unsuitable materials should be
removed and disposed of properly off site to reduce the potential for contamination of the
excavated materials
Soft and yielding areas encountered during clearing and grubbing below areas
designated to receive fill and or future improvements should be over excavated to expose
firm materials and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with well compacted fill
The excavated soft soils should not be reused as a source of fill materials and should be
disposed of properly off site and or used in landscape areas where appropriate

In general the subgrades in cut areas and areas designated to receive fills should
be scarified to a depth of about 8 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum
moisture content and recompacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of
the maximum dry density as determined by ASTM D1557 Optimum moisture is the water
content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
Where hard basalt formation is exposed the subgrades in cut areas and areas
designated to receive fills should be proof rolled with a large vibratory drum roller minimum
10 tons static weight for a minimum of eight passes to help detect and collapse near surface
cavities and or voids The vibratory drum roller should be operated at a speed of about
300 feet per minute about 3½ miles per hour
The site preparation operations should be performed in the presence of a Geolabs
representative Yielding areas loose areas or cavities disclosed during ripping and
proof rolling operations should be over excavated and backfilled with compacted fill
materials Contract documents should include additive and deductive unit prices for
over excavation of the collapsed lava tube features and other features and backfilled
with compacted fill to account for variations in the over excavation and backfill quantities
Fills and Backfills
Structural fill materials should consist of well graded granular materials generally
less than 6 inches in maximum dimension with sufficient fines to prevent the occurrence
of voids in the compacted mass In addition structural fill materials should contain less
than 30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve by weight In general the excavated
well graded granular on site materials may be reused as a source of structural fill
materials except excavated soft soils provided they are processed to meet the
maximum particle size and gradation requirements presented above
It should be noted that the excavated rock materials will need to be processed to
reduce the particle size of the materials to 6 inches or less in order for the excavated rock
materials to be reused as a source of structural fill materials
The maximum particle size of the fills and backfills should be limited to 3 inches or
less for fills and backfills to be placed in confined locations Confined locations are defined
as areas where the compaction equipment is limited in size to less than 2 tons in static
weight In addition if the contractor elects to use smaller sized compaction equipment
less than 2 tons in static weight the maximum particle size of the fill and backfill
materials also should be limited to 3 inches in dimension
Imported materials to be used as select granular fill should be non expansive
granular materials such as crushed coral or basalt The select granular fill should be
well graded from coarse to fine with no particles larger than 3 inches in largest dimension
The material should have a California Bearing Ratio CBR value of 20 or higher and a swell
potential of 1 percent or less when tested in accordance with ASTM D1883 The material

Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
In general fills and backfills should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum
moisture content placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and
compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction Compaction requirements for the
subgrades of improvements subjected to vehicular traffic such as the ground surface of
driveways cart paths and pavements should be increased to a minimum of 95 percent
relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed
as a percentage of the maximum dry density as determined by ASTM D1557 Optimum
moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry
density
Compaction of the fill materials should be accomplished by utilizing sheepsfoot
rollers vibratory rollers or other types of acceptable compaction equipment Water tamping
jetting or ponding should not be allowed to compact the fills in lieu of mechanical
compaction of the fill materials
Structural fill materials well graded granular fills less than 6 inch in maximum
dimension should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 12 inches in loose thickness
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to at least
95 percent relative compaction
Conventional compaction testing is generally not practical in fills composed of
rocks and or cobbles Instead a testing program should be conducted at the start of the
grading phase of the project under the observation of a Geolabs representative to
evaluate the number of passes needed to achieve the desired level of compaction Based
on this testing program the number of passes may then be used as the field criterion for
adequate compaction
For preliminary estimating purposes compaction may be accomplished by using
a large vibratory drum roller having a static weight of at least 20 tons traveling at a speed
of about 300 feet per minute about 3½ miles per hour The roller should pass over the
fill surface at least eight times for structural fill
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and earthwork recommendations submitted in this letter report are
based in part upon information obtained from the field borings and site reconnaissance
Variations of the subsurface conditions between and beyond the field borings may occur
and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident until construction is
underway If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations presented in this letter report
