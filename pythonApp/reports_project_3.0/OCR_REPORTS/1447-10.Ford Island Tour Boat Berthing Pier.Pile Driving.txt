INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our pile driving inspection at
the proposed tour boat berthing pier on Ford Island
The general location of the project site and its vicinity is shown
on the Project Location Map Plate 1
PURPOSE AND SCOPE OF WORK
A soil and foundation investigation was performed by our firm for
the proposed tour boat berthing piers A report of our findings and
recommendations was issued on
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1447 10 Page 2
Originally two piers about 7 feet wide by 80 feet long were
planned One boring was drilled at the shoreside end Boring 1 of the
eastern pier and one boring Boring 2 was drilled at the offshore end
of the western pier
The two borings encountered significantly different subsoil
conditions
In Boring 1 the mudline was encountered at 8 5 feet below the
concrete platform Loose to medium dense alluvial deposit consisting
mainly of gray clayey sand with some coral gravel was then encountered
down to a depth of 40 feet Below the alluvial deposit stiff to very
stiff silty clay was encountered to the full depth of this boring
In Boring 2 however the mudline was encountered at 36 5 feet
below MLLW The soft to very soft alluvial deposit consisting mainly of
loose clayey sand and some coral gravel was encountered down to
elevation 80 feet below MLLW Medium dense silty coral sand then
extended to a depth of 90 feet where the material became more gravelly
Stiff clayey silt was encountered at a depth of 98 feet and extended to
the end of the boring at 105 feet below MLLW
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1447 10 Page 3
Based on the boring data pile foundations deriving their support
from frictional resistance along the pile shaft were recommended to
accommodate the anticipated vertical load The required pile tip
elevations to develop 17 tons of vertical capacity were calculated to be
minus 60 feet and minus 110 feet below the water level for the shoreside
and seaside ends respectively Since boring data was not available for
the middle portions of the pier the variation of pile length was
assumed
Subsequent to our investigation it was decided to construct only
the eastern pier
The purpose of our pile driving inspection was
1 to monitor the pile driving operations to evaluate whether the
soil conditions were consistent with the findings of our
investigation and design assumptions
2 to advise the contracting officer of any unusual conditions
during driving and
3 to provide a report of findings which include the pile driving
logs and a summary of our observations and recommendations

SUMMARY OF OBSERVATIONS
1 The actual driven lengths of most of the piles were signifi
cantly shorter than the design lengths A summary of the
driven versus design pile lengths is presented on Plate 3
It should be noted that at the ends of the pier where the
borings were drilled the driven pile lengths were close to
the design lengths
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1447 10 Page 8
3
However in the remainder of the pier the pile lengths had to
be interpolated between the two very different boring
conditions The pile lengths were estimated conservatively
long since it is easier to cut off excess pile than to
splice an additional length
Based on the blow counts it appears that all of the piles
have been driven to a sufficient depth to develop the 17 ton
vertical pile capacity Also the piles have been driven to a
sufficient depth to develop the full lateral resistance of the
pile
During the pile driving there was a concern regarding the
location of the splice Due to the shorter driven pile
lengths the splices would not be embedded below the mudline
as deep as originally designed elevation 50 0 Discussion
with the structural engineer confirmed that the splice was
designed to develop fill moment and therefore it was not
essential that the splice be embedded deep below the mudline
During the driving all of the splices were embedded at some
depth below the mudline
