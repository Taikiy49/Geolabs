Dear Mr. Le:
This letter report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations during the
probing and grouting operations for the Ewa Beach Elementary School project in the
Ewa Beach area from August 23 to August 31, 2005. The probing and grouting work was
performed in general accordance with our fee proposal dated August 11, 2005.
Project Considerations
The project involves the construction of a one-story building at Ewa Beach
Elementary School in Ewa on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. The proposed building will
accommodate a total of six classrooms. The existing parking lot will also be
restriped/reconfigured as a part of the school improvement. The general location and
vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map, Plate 1.
Field exploration performed for the project revealed that the project site was
underlain by coral formation. Based on our engineering experience, voids and/or cavities
are commonly encountered in the coral formation. To reduce the potential for loss of
foundation support resulting from collapse of the voids and/or cavities below foundations,
probing and grouting was performed by the contractor to determine the potential presence
of voids and/or cavities prior to foundation construction.
Probing and Grouting Operations
The probing and grouting operations were performed in general conformance with
the recommendations presented in our report for the project dated August 11, 2004. The
purpose of the probing and grouting operation was to reduce the potential for loss of

The probe hole locations were marked by 57 Builders, Ltd. prior to the probing
operation. In general, the probe holes were located at the center of the column footings
and spaced at approximately 10-foot intervals along the wall footings. The approximate
locations of the probe holes are shown on the Probe Hole Identification Plan, Plate 2. In
general, the probe holes at the new building were drilled to depths about 12.5 feet below
the existing ground surface, corresponding to approximately 10.5 feet below the design
footing bottom elevations.
The probe holes, about 4 inches in diameter, were drilled by Leslie’s Drilling, Inc.
using a truck-mounted drill rig equipped with continuous flight augers. Following the
probing operation, a 2’4-inch diameter PVC pipe was inserted into each of the probe holes
to maintain the integrity of the open-hole and to reduce the potential for cave-in prior to the
grouting operation. A total of 96 probe holes with an aggregate length of about
1,200 lineal feet below the existing ground surface were drilled for the project.
The probe holes were backfilled with fine masonry grout supplied by Hawaiian
Cement and Ameron Hawaii. The grouting work was performed by Lestlie’s Drilling, Inc.
and observed by a field representative from Geolabs. Grout quantities were estimated
using the number of strokes to fill each probe hole compared to the number of strokes to
fill up a 5-gallon bucket. The grouting operation generally proceeded in the following
manner:
° immediately prior to grouting of a particular hole, the temporary PVC pipe
was removed. A 2-inch diameter steel tremie pipe attached to the end of the
grouting hose was then inserted into the open hole and lowered to the
bottom of the hole. Grout injection then commenced until the hole was filled.
° Each probe hole was grouted from bottom of the hole upward through the
tremie pipe, which was retracted from the probe hole as the grout filled the
hole. The grouting operation and the approximate grout take were observed
and recorded by our field representative.
Based on the field observation, it appeared that some holes had caved-in at the
time of grouting operation as the tremie pipe could not reach the drilled depth of about
12.5 feet below the existing ground surface.
In general, no significant voids and/or cavities were observed during the probing
operation. Approximately 13 cubic yards of fine masonry grout was utilized during the
grouting operation to fill up the 96 probe holes. The probing and grouting records are
provided on Plates A-1 through A-96. The probing and grouting summary is provided on
Plates B-1 through B-3.
