As requested we have made a study of the near surface soil conditions
below the above referenced lot The purpose of the study was to
evaluate the suitability of the near surface soils as foundation support
for the proposed house The investigation consisted of drilling three
borings within the proposed building site performing laboratory soil
tests and reviewing the grading and compaction test records on the
building site This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed single family residence will consist of a two story wood
structure approximately 34 by 61 feet in plan dimension Due to the
sloping terrain at the site the garage level will require cuts into the
Slope with the living areas situated above the garage

Based on the preliminary plans the house will be set back about 20 feet
from Kulani Street The house will be supported on posts or wall
footings Concrete slab on grade is planned for the garage floor
driveway and the rear patio Retaining walls of up to 8 feet high are
planned for the storage and garage areas Up to 10 feet of cut is
planned on the rear portion of the house
EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
Lot 72 is located in the southern portion of this subdivision
development near the middle of Kulani Street The lot is bounded by
Kulani Street on the northeast subdivision lots on the southeast and
northwest and the natural valley side slope on the west An existing
concrete drainage ditch is located on the southwestern upper portion
of the lot
The lot generally slopes upward from Kulani Street toward the west at
about 20 to 25 percent gradient The elevation of the lot generally
varies from about 150 to 208 feet

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our findings indicate that the front portion of the lot where most of
the house is planned is underlain by a layer of gray brown expansive
adobe clay about 2 feet thick over weathered vesicular basalt Rock
outcrops were noted on the existing surface in the rear portion of the
lot
Descriptions of the soils encountered in the borings are presented on
the attached logs Plates A 1 through A 3 The approximate locations of
the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
Laboratory soil tests on the near surface material indicate that the
surface soils belong to CH classification according to the Unified Soil
Classification System Laboratory one inch ring swell tests indicate
that the soil generally has a high shrink swell potential The
laboratory test results are summarized on Plate B 1
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that most of the lot is underlain by
a surface layer about 2 feet thick of highly expansive gray brown
adobe clay over weathered vesicular basalt Therefore the
slab on grade and foundation construction would require special
measures to reduce the shrink swell effect of the subsoils

Foundation
For this lot post and beam construction is highly desirable and
recommended due to the sloping terrain and expansive soil conditions
The post and beam construction would generally result in lower
foundation costs and the individual footings can be readily adjusted
should such a need occur in the future
For post and beam construction the individual post footings should
extend a minimum depth of 36 inches below the ground surface or 6
inches into the gray weathered rock material All footings should be
poured in place and the excavation backfilled with compacted fill to
provide an impervious layer over the footing
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f can be used for footings
bearing on the stiff undisturbed soil and 5000 p s f for footings in
weathered rock Design of perimeter thickened edge footings for
report
If a footing is located next to a utility line it should be extended
to the bottom of the utility trench
Surface water should be diverted away from the foundations This
is especially important in the rear portion of the house to reduce
ponding of surface run off water that may affect expansive soils
around the foundation

Slab On Grade
Concrete slab on grade is planned for the garage and patio It
appears that the rear portion of the garage and patio area would
require cuts of up to about 8 feet and 4 feet respectively It is
probable that the more competent gray weathered vesicular basalt rock
may be encountered in the rear portion of the garage and patio
excavation The front portion of the garage will have less cut and
may encounter the expansive gray adobe clay which is generally
unsuitable as slab subgrade soils
In order to reduce the expansive effects of the clay where
encountered they should be over excavated down to a depth of 3 feet
below the slab bottom or to weathered rock and then replaced with
non expansive borrow soils such as select borrow crusher waste or
coral The over excavation and backfilling should extend a minimum of
12 inches beyond the edge of the slabs
A 4 inch layer of base course should be provided below the slab and
compacted to 90 of its maximum dry density In order to protect
against moisture infiltration an impervious moisture barrier may be
incorporated below the slab
After the above removal and replacement the perimeter thickened edge
footings of the on grade slabs could then be embedded at least 12
inches below the lowest adjacent grade

Concrete Driveway and Sidewalks
It appears that expansive soils may be encountered in the driveway
area Since the driveway can tolerate some amount of heaving the
adobe clay over excavation and replacement may be reduced to 2 feet
minimum below the bottom of the driveway slab or to weathered rock
This 2 foot minimum capping under the driveway or walkway should be
used even if new fill is used to raise the grade Where less than 2
feet of new fill is planned over excavation and replacement of the
adobe clay with select non expansive material is recommended to
provide a minimum 2 feet of capping The above capping requirements
should be followed for a minimum distance of 1 foot beyond the
driveway edges
Joints are recommended in the driveway slab at about 8 to 10 foot
intervals and at discontinuities in order to allow for some relative
movement of the concrete
For future sidewalks capping of the clay or over excavation and
replacement similar to the driveway is recommended to reduce heaving
caused by the expansive clay soil Over excavation beyond the edge of
the walkway may not be necessary

Site Grading
The existing terrain at the site generally slopes down at about 20 to
25 percent gradient Up to 4 feet of cut is anticipated for the patio
area
Based on the field observations we anticipate that cuts deeper than
about 2 feet may encounter weathered rock formation
A cut slope ratio of 1 1 H V can be utilized in weathered rock and
3 1 H V is recommended for low slopes in the upper on site clayey
soil
The area to be filled should be cleared of vegetation and debris prior
to filling
Fills and backfills should be select non expansive borrow material
placed in maximum 8 inch loose lifts and compacted to a minimum of 90
of its maximum dry density established by ASTM D 1557 78 The on site
adobe clay soil should definitely not be used for fill material
below structures and behind retaining walls
The finished grade outside the slabs pavements and foundations
should be shaped to avoid ponding conditions and to shed water away
from the structures

Retaining Walls
CRM retaining walls are planned around the garage level to retain the
proposed cuts of up to about 8 feet
The retaining wall footings should be embedded a minimum depth of 18
inches below the outside finished grade or six inches into weathered
rock formation
Granular backfill such as select borrow or crusher waste should be
used for a distance behind the wall equal to the height of the wall
This would improve drainage reduce the compactive effort required for
the wall backfill and reduce lateral pressure behind the walls
Impervious soil such as on site clay soils should be used only for
the top one foot of backfill to reduce seepage of surface run off
behind the garage wall Clay backfill should not be used under the
house
A subsurface drainage system subdrain or weepholes should be
utilized behind the wall to prevent the build up of hydrostatic
pressure
An allowable bearing value of up to 5000 p s f may be used for
foundations bearing on the dense weathered rock

Design Review
Plans for foundations site grading and utility construction should
be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written comments
prior to construction to see that the intent of our recommendations is
included in the project plans and specifications
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during earthwork
construction particularly the fill placement slope cuts and to
monitor the contractor s grading operations
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be examined by a
soils engineer during construction to see that adequate bearing has
been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with
the findings of our investigation
