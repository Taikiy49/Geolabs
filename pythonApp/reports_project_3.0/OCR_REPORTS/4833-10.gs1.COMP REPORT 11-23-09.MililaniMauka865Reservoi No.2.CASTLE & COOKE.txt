PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located at the upper end of Konaku Street in the Mililani Mauka
Residential Development area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii A new 1 0 Million Gallon MG
reservoir will be constructed adjacent to an existing reservoir identified as BWS 865
Reservoir A Project Location Map and a Site Plan are shown on Plates 1 and 2
respectively
The new reservoir will be of reinforced concrete construction with a concrete floor
slab The reservoir measuring approximately 77 feet in diameter and 30 feet in height will
be located northeast of the existing reservoir and adjacent to an existing slope The finish
floor elevation for the new reservoir will be approximately at the existing ground surface
elevation of about 843 feet Mean Sea Level MSL with a minimum 6 inch thick reinforced
concrete slab
As part of the project construction connecting pipelines will also be required to
serve the new reservoir In addition a 10 foot wide asphalt concrete paved service road
will be provided between the existing Reservoir and the new Reservoir
and around the new reservoir structure

SITE GRADING AND UTILITY INSTALLATION
Geolabs Inc observed the site grading and utility installation work for the Mililani
Mauka 865 Reservoir No 2 project from to The site
grading work consisted of clearing and grubbing and placement and compaction of fills on
the order of about 1 to 2 feet thick and cuts on the order of 9 feet deep to achieve the design
finished grades for the project Parsons Construction Group performed the site grading and
utility installation work During the site grading operations samples of the on site and
imported materials utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our
office for testing The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were
established in our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor
and the test results are summarized on Plate 3
The fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as
determined in accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557 In the reservoir pad and
perimeter road areas the base course fill material was compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative density
During the site grading and utility backfilling operations periodic field density tests
were performed in general accordance with ASTM D 6938 Nuclear Gauge Method Results
of the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 4 1 through 4 5 of this report
These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and
backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating
from on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should be considered accurate
only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were
reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained and or approved by our field
representative based on visual observation and hand probing
The finished slopes near the northern side of the new reservoir are steeper than the
two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V inclination shown on the project drawings We
understand that these steeper slope areas were vegetated with grass by the contractor In
addition we understand that the Board of Water Supply has accepted the finished slope
condition
