This report summarizes our earthwork observation and testing services performed
during the earthwork operations of the Kamakana Villages Senior Rental Housing project
on the Island of Hawaii
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
Kamakana Villages is at the intersection of Ane Keohokalole Highway and
Manawalea Street in Kailua Kona on the Island of Hawaii The Senior Rental Housing
portion of the development is located on the makai side of Lot No 11 with an area of about
2 9 acres
A total of five residential buildings and a community center were constructed at the
site for the Senior Rental Housing portion of the project The residential buildings are
three stories and will provide about 85 one and two bedroom units A total of about 90 on
grade parking spaces was also provided for the project In addition site retaining walls
access roadways storm water disposal system and underground utility lines were required
for the project The general vicinity of the project site is shown on the Project Location Map

EARTHWORK OPERATION
The general contractor for the project was Unlimited Construction Services Inc The
earthwork operations site grading was conducted by E M Rivera Sons Inc Our
observation and testing services during the earthwork construction of the project occurred
between August 2016 to October 2017
During the mass grading operations extensive subgrade preparation procedures
were employed within the limits of grading at the commencement of the mass grading
operations for the project to reduce the potential for collapse of near surface cavities and or
voids within the lots The intent of the subgrade preparation procedures is to assist in
detecting and collapsing near surface cavities and or voids contained in the porous
near surface materials It should be noted that the subgrade preparation procedures
employed for this project are consistent with the standard of practice for similar type projects
and similar subsurface conditions on the Island of Hawaii
In general the subgrade preparation in fill areas consisted of ripping the original
ground to a depth of about 3 feet or until refusal using a ripper tooth of a large bulldozer to
assist in detecting and collapsing near surface cavities and or voids contained in the porous
near surface materials After the fill surfaces have been ripped the ground generally was
leveled with the use of Caterpillar D10 and or Komatsu 400 bulldozers working the surface
for about four passes to provide a relatively level surface After leveling of the rugged
surfaces with the bulldozer the fill subgrades generally were proof rolled with a Dynapac
vibratory roller compactor minimum 20 tons for about eight passes to help detect and
collapse near surface cavities and or voids
Fill materials consisting of processed on site materials including a a basalt and or
dense pahoehoe basalt were placed in the fill areas of the project site Structural fills
consisted of well graded granular materials generally smaller than 6 inches in dimension
In general the fill materials within the top 2 feet of the lots within the fill areas of the pad
consisted of structural fill materials General fills consisting of boulders of up to about
18 inches in dimension generally were placed about 2 feet below the finished grades
within the pad areas of the lots and choked off with finer sized materials Boulders of up
to about 3 feet in size were placed at least 4 feet below the finished grades within the pad
areas
Subgrades in cut areas generally were proof rolled with a Dynapac vibratory roller
compactor minimum 20 tons for about eight passes to help detect and collapse
near surface cavities and or voids It should be noted that some portions of the project
subgrades were graded in a cut condition Therefore the presence of basalt formation
should be anticipated at or near the existing pad elevation in these areas
During the earthwork operations samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our

Field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations on an
as need basis to confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures
and soil conditions were noted A total of 81 field density tests were performed using the
nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM D6938 Compaction test results are
summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 4 These tests were performed on the prepared
ground surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills Test locations and elevations were
estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes and topographic
features These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the
degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked
until the specified minimum compaction was obtained

LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities occurring
simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork may have been
performed without our observation However the level of earthwork observation and testing
services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some of the geotechnical risks to
the project It should be recognized that there are variations in the accuracy and statistical
repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the earthwork construction and
materials Our services were performed in accordance with generally accepted standards
of geotechnical engineering practice no warranty is expressed or implied Provision of
construction monitoring by an engineer is not insurance nor does it guarantee construction
of any type The contractor shall retain responsibility for the quality of the work for adhering
to the plans and specifications and for repairing defects regardless of when they are found
