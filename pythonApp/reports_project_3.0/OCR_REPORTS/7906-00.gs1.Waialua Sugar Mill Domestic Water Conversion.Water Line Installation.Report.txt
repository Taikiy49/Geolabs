This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations and testing 
services performed during waterline installation for the Waialua Sugar Mill Domestic Water 
Conversion project from October 17, 2019 to January 13, 2020 in accordance with our fee 
proposal dated March 6, 2019. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The project is located within the Waialua Sugar Mill property and extends to 
Goodale Avenue in Waialua on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. The installation of 
approximately 520 linear feet of waterline consisting of 8 and 12-inch PVC pipes, and 
4 and 8-inch ductile iron pipes are planned for the project. The waterline inverts ranged 
from about 3 to 5.5 feet below the existing ground surface. A Project Location Map and 
Site Plans are shown on Plates 1 and 2.1 through 2.3, respectively. 
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services for 
the waterline installation construction at the project site.

WATERLINE INSTALLATION 
We observed the waterline installation work for the Waialua Sugar Mill Domestic 
Water Conversion project from October 17, 2019 to January 13, 2020. The waterline 
installation work consisted of placement and compaction of trench backfills to achieve the 
finished grades for the project. Royal Contracting Company Ltd. performed the waterline 
installation work. During the waterline installation, we obtained samples of the on-site and 
imported materials utilized as backfill materials and transported them to our laboratory for 
testing. The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in 
our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D1557 (Modified Proctor), and the test 
results are summarized on Plate 3. 
The backfill materials were moisture-conditioned to above the optimum moisture 
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as 
determined in accordance with ASTM D1557. In the roadway areas, the backfill materials 
within 2 feet of the finished subgrade were compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative 
density. 
During the waterline installation work, periodic field density tests were performed in 
general accordance with ASTM D6938 (Nuclear Gauge Method). Results of the 
compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 4.1 through 4.3 of this report. 
These tests were performed on constructed trench backfills at the site. Test locations and 
elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on-site survey stakes. These 
locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the 
methods used. Areas assessed as unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified 
minimum compaction was obtained and/or approved by our field representative based on 
visual observation and hand probing. 
Our services consisted of part-time field observation of the contractor’s backfill 
placement, subgrade preparation, and compaction procedures and confirmation of 
subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above grading operations. 
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the 
referenced tests, it is our opinion that the waterline installation work for the above project 
observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report was performed in 
conformance with the general intent of the recommendations in the following references:
