PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions adjacent to the location of the planned antenna facility and to evaluate if the
near surface soil can provide a bearing capacity of not less than 3000 psf The field
exploration and laboratory testing data were used to develop an idealized soil data set to
evaluate bearing capacity

2 borings to depths ranging between 177 and 22 feet below the existing ground surface
at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were drilled

The material encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
observation in the field by our field engineer who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed by visual observations
and testing in the laboratory All soils were classified in general conformance with the
Unified Soil Classification System A graphic presentation of the materials encountered
are presented on the Boring Logs Plates 3 and 4
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Method D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by
Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow
counts to drive the sampler the last 12 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown on the
Boring Logs at the appropriate sample depths
Core samples of rock materials encountered at the site were obtained using
diamond core drilling techniques in general accordance with ASTM Standard Practice D
2113 83 Diamond Core Drilling for Site Investigation Core drilling is a rotary drilling
method that uses a hollow bit to cut into the rock formation The rock material left in the
hollow core of the bit is mechanically recovered for visual observation and description
Recovery REC is used as a subjective guide to the interpretation of the relative
quality of rock masses Recovery is defined as the actual length of material recovered
from a coring attempt versus the length of the core attempt for example if 3 7 feet of
material was recovered from a 5 0 foot core run the recovery would be 74 percent and
would be shown on the Boring Logs as REC 74

The Rock Quality Designation RQD is also a subjective guide to the relative
quality of rock masses RQD is defined as the percentage of the core run that is sound
material in excess of four inches 4 in length without any discontinuities discounting any
drilling induced fractures or breaks If 2 5 feet of sound material is recovered from a 5 0
foot core run the RQD would be 50 percent and would be shown on the Boring Logs as
RQD 50 Generally the following is used to describe the relative quality of the rock
based on the Practical Handbook of Physical Properties of Rocks and Minerals

The rippability of a rock mass is a function of the relative hardness of the rock its
relative quality brittleness and fissile characteristics A dense basalt with a high RQD
would be very difficult to rip and would probably require more arduous methods of
excavation

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
A near surface layer of fill material consisting of medium dense silty sand of
approximately one 1 to two foot 2 in thickness was encountered in the two 2
borings drilled A layer of light gray clayey silt was encountered below the surface fill
layer The clayey silt layer is medium stiff and is wet A layer of loose to medium dense
coral silty sand was encountered below the clayey silt in the two borings to depths of 14
and 17 feet below the surface A dense coral formation was encountered below the coral
silty sand to the maximum depths explored of 22 feet below the existing ground surface
For more detail descriptions of the materials encountered please refer to the Boring Logs
presented on Plates 3 and 4
GROUNDWATER LEVEL
Groundwater levels were encountered at depths of about three feet 3 below the
ground surface in the drilled borings at the time of our field exploration It should be
noted that groundwater levels can vary significantly depending on factors such as rainfall
tidal influences time of year and other factors
DISCUSSION AND EVALUATION
On the basis of our field exploration and laboratory testing the significant
geotechnical considerations at the location of the planned satellite antenna facility is the
presence of potentially compressible soils near the surface We understand that the plans
for the subject project requires that the foundation soils provide a bearing capacity of not

LIMITATIONS
The evaluations presented in this report are based in part upon information
obtained from field borings Variations of subsoil conditions between the borings may
occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident until
construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to
re evaluate the recommendations provided in this report
The locations of the field borings indicated in this report are approximate having
been estimated by taping from visible features shown on the plans provided by SIGCOM
Total Communications Elevations of the borings were estimated by interpolation from
the elevations shown on the same plan The physical locations and elevations of the
borings should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the borings depict the
approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual
