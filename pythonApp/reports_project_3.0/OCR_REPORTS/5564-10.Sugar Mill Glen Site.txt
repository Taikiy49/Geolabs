SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions were explored by drilling and sampling one boring
designated as Boring No 1 to a depth of about 11 5 feet below the existing ground
surface at the approximate location shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The boring generally encountered a surfacial fill layer of about 7 feet thick
consisting of medium stiff to very stiff clays and silts with sand and gravel The fill was
underlain by residual soils consisting of very stiff silty clay to the maximum depth explored
of about 11 5 feet below the existing ground surface Groundwater was not encountered in
the drilled boring at the time of our field exploration
It should be noted that groundwater levels at the project site may fluctuate
depending on tidal fluctuations seasonal precipitation and other factors Detailed
descriptions of the materials encountered in the boring are presented on the Log of Boring
Plate A 1 in Appendix A

FIELD PERCOLATION TESTING
A field permeability test was conducted at the proposed underground drainage
retention chamber site to evaluate the infiltration characteristics of the subsurface
materials encountered at the boring The permeability tests were performed in
Boring No 1 as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
We understand that the planned underground drainage retention chamber will be
installed at depths of about 8 to 12 feet below the ground surface Therefore the field
percolation test was performed at about 10 feet below the existing ground surface A
constant head permeability test was performed to determine the average hydraulic
conductivity of the underlying subsurface materials Based on our field exploration the
subsurface materials encountered at the site generally consist of fill overlying residual
soils The permeability test results are presented in Appendix B
The hydraulic conductivity k value is calculated as 0 002 feet per minute at the
test location and depth based on the constant head field permeability test results In
general the calculated k value for the test indicated that the subsurface soils encountered
at the boring location could be considered moderately permeable in terms of water
transmission
UNDERGROUND DRAINAGE RETENTION CHAMBER
Based on the provided information we understand that an underground drainage
retention chamber is planned for surface runoff disposal at the project site The typical
cross section detail of the retention chamber is shown on the Chamber
System Typical Cross Section Detail Plate 3 As previously mentioned one field
permeability test using the constant head method was performed in Boring No 1 at the
selected depth of 10 feet to evaluate the infiltration characteristics of the subsurface
materials at the site The test results indicate that the subsurface materials exhibit a
k value of about 2 0 x 10 feet per minute

APPENDIX B
Field Permeability Testing
The permeability of the in situ materials at the underground drainage retention
chamber site was evaluated by performing one constant head test in Boring No 1 The
test was performed at a depth of about 10 feet below the existing ground surface The
approximate boring location is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 Casing was not used for
drilling and testing
The constant head test was performed by pumping water into the borehole until the
water surface stabilized and remained at equilibrium for a period of time The water height
and the rate of pumping were then measured to provide data upon which to base the
calculation of the subsurface material permeability The field permeability test results are
presented on Plate B 1
