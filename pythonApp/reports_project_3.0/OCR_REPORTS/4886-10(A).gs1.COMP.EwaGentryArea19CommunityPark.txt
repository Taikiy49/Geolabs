PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is within the Ewa by Gentry East Development area in the District of
Ewa on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project site is east of Fort Weaver Road south of
Iroquois Point Road and west of Keaunui Drive A Project Location Map and Site Plans are
shown on Plate 1 and Plate 2 respectively
A new comfort station and parking lot to be located south of the existing wastewater
pump station are planned In addition the surrounding area will be graded for the future
soccer fields An existing drain line will be extended into the project site
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services for the
site grading and utility installation at the project site
SITE GRADING
Geolabs Inc observed the site grading and utility installation at the Ewa by Gentry
Area 19 Community Park project from April 5 to The site grading and utility
installation work consisted of the placement and compaction of fills on the order of up to about
2 feet thick and backfills up to about 8 feet thick to achieve the finished grades Paradigm
Construction LLC performed the site grading and utility installation work During the site
grading operations samples of the on site and imported materials utilized as fill materials were

obtained and transported to our laboratory for testing The maximum dry densities and
optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general accordance with
ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test results are summarized on Plate 3
The fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as
determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557 Within the future soccer field areas and other
open field areas the fill materials were compacted to a minimum of 85 percent relative
compaction In the roadway areas the fill materials within 2 feet of the finished subgrade were
compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative density
During the site grading and utility installation operations periodic field density tests
were performed in general accordance with ASTM D 6938 Nuclear Gauge Method Results
of the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 4 1 through 4 4 of this report
These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and
backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating
from on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should be considered accurate
only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were
reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained and or approved by our field
representative based on visual observation and hand probing
Our services consisted of part time field observation of the contractor s fill placement
subgrade preparation and compaction procedures and confirmation of subgrade soil
conditions exposed during the grading operations
