INTRODUCTION
This report presents our recommendations for the foundation
design of the porposed subject
The site is located approximately 12 miles north of Kaunakakai
on the right side of Maunaloa Highway The proposed project
site is on a gently sloping hillside
Subsurface conditions were explored by utilizing a backhoe
and digging seven 7 test pits to a depth of eight 8 feet
below the existing ground surface The approximate backhoe
dug location are shown on the site plan Plate 1 and the
descriptiotn of materials encountered in the test pits is
presented on the Test Pit logs
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed Seventh Day Adventist Church Complex will in
clude classroom and sanctuary buildings parking area and
access road Future expansion is not considered at this time
SITE DESCRIPTION
The site slopes gently with ground surface elevations which
range from about 60 feet on the south to 100 feet on the
north

GENERAL SOIL CONDITIONS
Based on the information obtained from the test pits
the site is generally underlain with a stiff to hard
reddish brown clayey silt and decomposed rock
Test Pit numbers 3 and 4 indicated and old rock fill
area starting at 4 feet However test pit numbers
3 and 4 are outside the general building area
No ground water was encountered in our test pits during
field exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the results of our field exploration the site is
suitable for the proposed construction
Foundation
For the proposed buildings spread footing foundations
are recommended All footings should be placed on undis
turbed competent clayey silt material enderlying the site
An allowable bearing pressure of up to 3 000 P S F could
be used to design footings placed on competent undisturbed
clayey silt material as described
For the above recommendations settlements are anticipated
to be minimal
It is recommended that all footing excavations be inspected
by the soils engineer to confirm the conditions upon which
our recommendations are based Any loose areas encountered
during excavation should be removed or recompacted to 95
relative density
Slab on Grade
According to the laboratory swell test results the on site
silty soil is low to non expansive Conventional slab on
grade design may be used It is recommended that slab on
grade be placed over with a 4 inch layer of base course com
pacted to 95 of its maximum dry density

The subgrade should be Kept moist and protected from
drying prior to concrete placement Subgrade should be
compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density
In order to protect against moisture damage of the flooring
material a Polyethylene moisture barrier should be incor
porated within floor slabs
Earthwork
Earthwork operation at the site will include cuts to 6 feett
minor filling footing excavation and foundation inspection
during construction It is anticipated that conventional
earth moving equipment could be used to conduct all the ex
cavation for foundations
The reddish brown clayey silt on site material could be
used for fill material
Excavations should be protected from water runoff which may
enter footing excavations All loose soils or mud should be
removed from the bottom of all footing excavations prior to
concrete placement
Fill should be placed in horizontal 10 inch lifts and com
pacted to at least 95 of its maximum dry density within the
limits of the building area and 90 outside the building area
as obtained using standard
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the footing excavations and recom
paction be observed by a soils engineer during construction
to see that adequate bearing has been obtained and that the
subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of
our subsurface investigation
