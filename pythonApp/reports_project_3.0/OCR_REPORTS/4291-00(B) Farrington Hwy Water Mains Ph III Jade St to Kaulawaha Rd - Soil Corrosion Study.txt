PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed project site is located along Farrington Highway in the Makaha area on
the Island of Oahu Hawaii as shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The project
generally consists of installing 8 inch and 24 inch diameter water lines from about Jade Street
to Kaulawaha Road We understand that the pipe materials may consist of Ductile Iron Dl
Concrete Cylinder CC Class 250 PVC or other pipe materials Based on the information
provided the project will consist of approximately 6 000 lineal feet of water mains and
appurtenances The 24 inch water main will have inverts ranging from about 4 to 7 feet below
the existing ground surface and the 8 inch water main will have inverts ranging from about
3 to 6 feet below the existing ground surface
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our study was to obtain an overview of the corrosion characteristics of
the subsurface soils along the proposed 8 and 24 inch water mains planned at the project site
Our work was performed in general conformance with ASTM Test Designation G 57 95 and
the requirements provided in the Water System External Corrosion Control Standards
Volume 3 1991 The scope of our work consisted of the following tasks and work efforts

FIELD SURVEY
Geophysical survey equipment i e resistance meter was mobilized to the site on
for the field survey The geophysical survey was completed on
In general our field work was performed in sunny conditions with
generally dry surface conditions
Seventy five soil resistivity surveys were conducted at a maximum of 300 foot intervals
at twenty five locations designated as R 1 through R 25 along the shoulder areas of
Farrington Highway The soil resistivity surveys were performed by using the in situ Wenner
4 Pin Method and a resistance meter Nilsson Model 400 manufactured by Nilsson Electrical
Laboratory Inc The method utilizes four surface probes or pins electrodes to evaluate the
average resistivity an indication of corrosivity of the underlying soil mass The average
resistivity values of the soil mass extending from the existing ground surface to the test depth
was assessed by using this method at the approximate survey locations are shown on the
General Site Plan Plate 2 Enlarged site plans of the project site are shown on Plates 3 1
through 3 4 The Results of the resistivity survey are presented on Plates 4 1 through 4 8
The soil resistivity surveys were performed at the existing ground surface along the
proposed alignment of the 8 and 24 inch water mains The average soil resistance along the

proposed alignment was evaluated at each survey location with the electrodes aligned at equal
spacing of generally 3 to 8 feet at the existing ground surface The depth of penetration of a
resistivity sounding is a function of the electrode spacing The electrode spacing was spaced
at about 3 to 8 feet for each soil resistivity survey location It should be noted that some
underground utility lines were observed within the shoulder areas near some survey locations
The resistance meter Nilsson Model 400 impresses a voltage between the outer
electrodes causing an electrical current to flow between them The voltage drop between the
inner electrodes is then measured by a sensitive built in voltmeter As a result the soil
resistance in ohms can be found directly on the dial The soil resistivity in ohm cm which
is directly related to both resistance and spacing can then be computed
The evaluated soil resistances the computed resistivity values and the corresponding
corrosion ratings are presented on Plates 4 1 through 4 8 of this report Graphical
presentation of the soil resistivity values and the corrosion ratings along the proposed water
main alignment are provided on Plates 5 1 and 5 2 It should be noted that the soil resistivity
at the site might be influenced by existing underground structures temperature weather
conditions and other factors
LABORATORY TESTING
In addition to a field resistivity survey laboratory soil analyses study were also
performed on the subsurface materials along the proposed water mains planned along this
portion of Farrington Highway Selected soil samples retrieved from our field exploration
program were tested by Environmental Laboratory of the Pacific to determine the pH value
and the chloride and sulfate contents The pH value was determined in general accordance
with EPA SW9045B test method The chloride and sulfate contents were determined in
general accordance with and test methods respectively Results of the
soil analyses pH chlorides and sulfates conducted on selected soil samples are
summarized on Plate 6 of this report
FINDINGS AND DISCUSSIONS
Resistivity is generally recognized as one of the most significant soil characteristics with
regard to the corrosivity of the soil to buried metallic objects In general the lower the
resistivity the greater the potential for corrosion of the buried metallic structure Conversely
the higher the resistivity the less likely the soil will contribute to corrosion to metallic objects
Based on the resistivity values and the corrosion ratings of the subsurface materials
at the site the subsurface materials at the site may be classified as corrosive to highly
corrosive for buried metallic structures In general it appears that the proposed water main
will likely need to be designed based on a Corrosion Rating of 1
The method used to control the corrosion of underground concrete pipelines and
structures is dependent on the pH and the chloride and sulfate contents found in the soil For

LIMITATIONS
The evaluations presented in this report are based upon information obtained from field
borings and field data points by means of geophysical surveys and laboratory testing
Variation of soil conditions between and beyond the field borings and data points may occur
and the nature and extent of such variations may not become evident until a later point in time
If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the ratings provided in this
report

Corporation on August 9 1999 Elevations of the field borings are based on interpolating
between the elevation points shown on the same plan Field resistivity survey locations are
approximate having been estimated by taping from features shown on the preliminary site

presented should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used
