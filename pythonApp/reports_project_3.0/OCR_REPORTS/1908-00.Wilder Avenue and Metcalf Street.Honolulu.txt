INTRODUCTION
The purpose of our exploration was to explore the subsurface
conditions at the site in order to provide pertinent soil data to aid in
the site grading and foundation design for the project
The scope of our evaluation included drilling and sampling of three
3 borings to depths of 11 feet below the existing ground surface Also
included were laboratory testing of selected samples obtained
engineering analysis and evaluation of the field and laboratory data
development of the preliminary design recommendations and report
preparation

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a Circle K convenience store at this
site The facility will consist of a single story 45 by 67 foot store
and a 24 by 36 foot pump island with two gasoline dispenser units To
supply the gas island an underground gasoline storage area consisting
of three 10 000 gallon tanks is planned for a 35 by 40 foot area
adjacent to the proposed store The convenience facility will be
surrounded by new asphaltic concrete A C paved parking area
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located on the northwestern corner of the
junction of Wilder Avenue and Metcalf Street in Honolulu Oahu Hawaii
The location and general vicinity of the site is shown on the Project
Tocation Map Plate 1
The site measures approximately 86 by 209 feet in plan dimension and
is generally level with ground elevation about 30 feet above Mean Sea
Level The site was previously occupied by a Chevron Station and
apartment buildings Most of the existing A C pavement at the site has
been demolished
A chain link fence is present along the northern and western
boundaries of the site

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATTONS
The field exploration generally encountered about 1 foot of gravel
fill pavement underlain by moderately expansive clay Since light loads
are anticipated from the proposed Circle K Store and pump island the
main concern in designing the on grade slabs and building foundations
would be the effect of the swelling clay
It is our opinion that Special procedures to reduce the shrink swell
potential of the clayey subsoils should be considered Ideally if the
finished grade of the slab on grade and project site grading can he
raised by at least one foot we believe there should be sufficient
capping thickness totalling 24 inches to stabilize the foundation
subgrade
If raising the current site grade is not practical an alternative
is to over excavate below the building to remove about 2 feet of the
underlying expansive clay and replace it with non expansive select fill
Shallow spread or continuous wall footings bearing on the compacted
select backfill may be used to Support the proposed structures along with
special procedures to reduce the shrinkage swelling effects of the
moderately expansive clay soils

Our laboratory tests and previous experience indicate that the clay
soils below the site have moderate volume change potential when wetted
from a dry moisture content and it can also shrink when dried in the
future Therefore we recommend that the clay soils be kept a minimm of
two 2 feet below the bottom of the footings and slabs This may be
achieved by either raising the site with non expansive fill or
overexcavation and replacement of the clay below the foundations The
over excavation should also extend a minimm of 6 inches beyond the edge
of the footings
The underlying clay soils should be kept in a moist condition at all
times and not allowed to dry out
The backfill should consist of non expansive borrow materials such
as select borrow crusher waste or coral placed in compacted horizontal
lifts not exceeding 6 inches thick and compacted to a minimum of 90 of
its maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78
The recommended minimum footing width is 12 inches with a minimum
embedment of 12 inches below the lowest adjacent outside grade An
allowable bearing value up to 2500 p s f may be used The bearing
capacity is for total dead plus live loads and may be increased by
one third for temporary wind and seismic loads
Site Grading
It is our understanding that the final site grading design has not
been completed yet In order to reduce the shrink swell potential of the
expansive clay we recommend that the current site grade be raised by at
least 1 foot
The imported fill material should be non expansive free of organic
and other deleterious matter and form a dense stable soil mass when
compacted to the required density Generally the soil particles should
be well graded in size with no individual particle larger than 6 inches
in maximum dimension

At the on set of earthwork the area within the grading limit should
be thoroughly cleared and grubbed including any required demolition
Any unsuitable materials such as previous fill remaining A c pavement
rubbish soft material and vegetation should be removed and disposed of
properly off site
After clearing and grubbing the areas to receive fill should be
scarified to a depth of 12 inches moisture conditioned to near optimum
moisture content and compacted to 90 of the maximum ary density as
established by ASTM D 1557 78 Any soft yielding or pumping areas noted
during the compaction process should be excavated to firm material and
backfilled with suitable material placed in 6 inch compacted lifts and
compacted to a minimum of 90 maximum dry density as recommended above
A slope ratio of 2 to 1 horizontal to vertical is recommended for
cut and fill slopes
The site should be graded to provide drainage for surface run off
away from all foundations
Backfill of Tank Excavation
It is assumed that the previous Chevron Station owner has excavated
the old underground storage tank s and has properly backfilled the
excavation However this has not yet been verified To prevent
potential ground settlement due to the possible improper backfill we
recommend that the old tank site be located and the quality of the
backfill be checked A proper backfill should consist of non expansive
select borrow material placed in 6 inch thick compacted level lifts and
compacted to 90 maximum dry density as established by ASTM D 1557 78
This preparation work should be performed prior to current project
construction
The expansive on site soil under on grade slabs area should be
over excavated to a depth of at least 24 inches below the slab bottom and
replaced with non expansive select borrow soils and a 4 inch gravel

New Underground Storage Tanks
It is planned to place three underground tanks approximately 10 000
gallons each in a 35 by 40 foot area adjacent to the Circle kK store
The tank will bear on the underlying clayey silt
Since the field exploration indicated that cobbles were encountered
the proposed tank site it is anticipated that cobbles may be encountered
at the bottom of excavation The bottom 6 inches of the excavation
should be scarified moist conditioned and recompacted to 90 of the
maximum dry density to support the tanks Particles in the bottom 6
inches of the excavation with dimension over 3 inches should be discarded
to provide uniform subgrade support
The tank backfill should consist of clean gravel such as 3B Fine
to reduce the compactive effort necessary around the tanks

Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comments
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this
review is not made the geotechnical engineer can not be responsible for
misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the
excavation and foundation phases of the work This is to observe
compliance with the design concepts specifications or recommendations
and to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as
borings probes test pits and or observation wells Variations of
conditions between the field data points may occur and the nature and
extent of these variations may not become evident until construction If
variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations given in this report
The location of the field data points were determined by taping from
structures indicated on the topographic survey Elevations of the data
points were approximately determined by interpolation from elevation
point on the topographic survey map The physical location and elevation
of the field data points should be considered accurate only to the degree
implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of field
data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types and as
such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross sections are based upon interpolation between field
data points and may not represent actual subsurface conditions

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration

three 3 test borings to depths of about 11 feet below the existing
ground surface The approximate locations of the borings are shown on
the Site Plan Plate 2 A truck mounted drill rig was used to drill the
borings

examination in the field by one of our engineers who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory A graphic
presentation of the soils encountered is presented on the Iogs of
Borings Plates A 1 through A 3 All soils were classified in
accordance with the Unified Soil Classification System
In the borings soil samples were obtained by driving a standard
penetration sampler or a 2 4 inch I D split barrel ring sampler with a
140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the Legs of Borings at the
appropriate sample depths
