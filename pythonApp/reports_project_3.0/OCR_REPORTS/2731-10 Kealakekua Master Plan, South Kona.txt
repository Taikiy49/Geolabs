In accordance with your request we made a site visit to examine and mark the
approximate location of Kikuya Crack on the subject site The site visit was made on
by Mr Clifford Temps of Kealakekua Development Lee
of R M Towill Corporation and of our firm
In our previous field work and in our report dated we had assumed
that the crack on the site is a relatively discrete feature such as a surface fracture After
making further visual observations at the site and discussing what features are referred to
as the crack we came to the conclusion that the crack is a zone of anomalous ground
conditions rather than a single discrete feature
During the course of the site visit this zone was walked and the approximate center
of the zone was flagged for later survey by R M Towill Corporation The attached Plate
1 shows the general location and extent of the anomalous zone
Based on our visual observations at the site Kikuya Crack is a zone of somewhat
depressed topography bounded by low scarps Within this zone there are small discrete
open surface cracks Many of these cracks are obscured by vegetation

Geomorphologically the zone is similar to a graben i e a downthrust block of rock
usually bounded by two or more faults There is a possibility that the zone could be a
graben related to Kealakekua Kaholo fault system which is located to the southwest of the
project site Although the zone has the appearance of a fault related structure no overt
signs of fault activity were noted in our field observations The zone crosses several dry laid
stone walls which appear to be stable and do not show any signs of movement It is
believed that these walls are over fifty years old
In our report dated we recommended that the crack area not be
used specifically as a vehicle for the disposal of surface runoff since the structure may be
fault related and fluids could lubricate the fault Our visual observations made at the site
on the site visit indicate that the crack zone as marked in the field is a
natural drainage course through the site Based on these new observations it is our opinion
that it would be acceptable to continue to allow natural drainage through the zone
However no new contributions should be made to the drainage of the crack zone i e
excess surface runoff that will result from site improvements should be intercepted and
handled by other means
There have also been discussions that structural improvements should not be
constructed over the crack These discussions were generally based on the assumption that
the crack is a single discrete feature
Based on discussions with Mr Temps of Kealakekua Development Co during the
site visit most of the crack zone is in the area planned for golf course use
However portions of the zone cross proposed home areas It is our opinion that a
disclosure statement should be provided to future purchasers stating that a possible fault is
located at the site and that site improvements may be at risk from earth movements
We hope that these comments are sufficient for your needs We appreciate the
opportunity to assist on this project Should you have questions or need any further
assistance please contact the undersigned or of our office
