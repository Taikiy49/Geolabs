This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering recommenda

analyses The recommendations presented herein are intended for design input and may
not be appropriate to form a basis for preparation of final construction cost estimates nor
for construction bidding purposes

Site Grading
Based on the available site plan the proposed finish floor elevation for the
residence is at 890 feet Mean Sea Level MSL Grading for the proposed residence
will consist of minor cuts and fills to provide a level building pad for the new residence
Additional cuts are also anticipated in the concrete driveway and garage area on the west
side of the new residence The following recommendations are presented to provide
general guidelines for the site grading work
1 Prior to commencement of earthwork the areas within the contract grading
limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris
and other deleterious materials should be removed and disposed of
properly off site
2 In areas to receive fill because of the high moisture content of the on site
soils the subgrade should be proof rolled to provide a firm and unyielding
subgrade surface if the subgrade soils have a moisture content in excess
of 5 percent above the optimum moisture content
If the subgrade soils have a moisture content less than 5 percent above the
optimum moisture content then the subgrade soils should be scarified to
a depth of 10 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the

This statement of work details requirements for a subcontractor performing vegetation
clearing trenching and drilling activities at two sites at the Ship Repair Facility SRF on
the Island of Guam in the Marianas Islands The two sites are the Area Behind the SRF
Fenceline Site and the Plating Shop Leach Field Site The location of the sites is shown
on Figure 1 The expected period of performance for the scope of services is between mid
March 1993 and mid June 1993
SITE DESCRIPTIONS
THE AREA BEHIND THE SRF FENCELINE SITE
The Area Behind the SRF Fenceline site is located on the Orote Peninsula at the western
edge of SRF This site is roughly oval in shape and covers an area of approximately
10 acres It is bounded by Sumay Cove to the northwest the SRF Fenceline to the east
the Naval Supply Depot to the southeast and the Naval Station to the southwest The Area
Behind the SRF Fenceline Site may have been used for the disposal of various wastes from
the SRF
The site is bisected by a paved back access road to SRF Paved roads border the
southwestern and southern edge while a dirt road surrounds the remainder of the site
Access to the interior of the site is limited by trees and thick vegetation The interior of the
site appears to consist of vegetation typical of that found in wetland areas Vegetation
clearing will be performed under this subcontract prior to the conduct of trenching and
drilling activities
THE PLATING SHOP LEACH FIELD SITE
The Plating Shop Leach Field Site is located directly east of the northeast corner of the
_Battery Plating Shop Building 2074 The leach field consists of three underground
approximately 20 foot long perforated PVC pipes connected via a concrete sump to two
floor drains located within Building 2074 Physical access to the site is good and available
by paved roads No clearing will be required at this site
EXPECTED SITE CONDITIONS

comprised predominantly of crushed coral and debris Below this lies an approximately
7 foot thick sandy to silty clay Depth to ground water varies from near the surface to
approximately 11 feet Topography may pose a problem in some instances as there is a
sharp dropoff of about 5 to 7 feet into the wetland like areas in the central portions of the
site

Filter pack consisting of sand or equivalent shall be installed in

Water may be added to the filter pack material to reduce possible complications

A 3 foot thick annular seal of bentonite consisting of granular bentonite in
shallow wells and 1 4 inch pellets in deep wells shall be placed on top of the

poured by hand into the annular space The quantity of bentonite required to
construct the seal will be determined by Ogden The bentonite seal will be
hydrated with water obtained from a specified on base source and will be of
known chemical quality The amount of water added will be in accordance with
the manufacturer s specifications A minimum of 15 minutes time will be given
to allow for adequate hydration of the bentonite The type of bentonite to be
used will be established by Ogden
A cement grout seal shall be placed in the annulus between the borehole wall and
the casing from the top of the bentonite seal to the ground surface For the deep
well the grout shall be placed through the augers or by tremie pipe For the
shallow wells the grout may be placed by hand The grout shall consist of a
Portland cement slurry containing 3 to 5 percent sodium bentonite or
equivalent
For the deep well the grout shall be applied in one continuous operation from
the bottom of the interval to be sealed to the ground surface and the augers shall
not be pulled above the level of the grout during injection
No further work shall be done on the well until the grout has firmly set
All borings not completed as monitor wells shall be filled with sodium bentonite
grout using either the hollow stem augers or a tremie pipe to ensure that the
borings are completely backfilled
