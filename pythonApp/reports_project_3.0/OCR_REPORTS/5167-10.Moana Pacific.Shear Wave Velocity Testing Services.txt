Project Considerations
Seismic Piezocone Penetration Testing SCPTu provides near continuous soil
profiling at the site in an effort to more closely analyze the seismic design considerations
for the project site The SCPTu method involves inserting a sensitive probe into the soil at
a constant speed Various sensors embedded in the probe continuously measure the tip
resistance side friction and induced pore pressures as the probe is systematically pushed
through the soil layers
In general subsurface explorations consisting of SCPTu yields more accurate
results for highly sensitive materials such as the loose granular soils at the site because
the data is obtained directly from the in situ soils Therefore SCPTu with seismic shear
wave velocity profiling is considered to be the industry standard for higher order analysis
such as for detailed seismic analyses especially in these subsurface conditions

Field Exploration
Our field exploration at the project site generally encountered dense sand of about
5 to 8 feet thick across the project site The surface fills were generally underlain by loose
and or soft lagoonal deposits coralline detritus and thin coral ledges extending to depths
of approximately 106 5 to 112 feet below the existing ground surface Below the highly
compressible lagoonal deposits our field exploration generally encountered layers of
dense silty sand stiff sandy silt and silty clay extending to the maximum depths of
exploration
Due to the nature of the subsurface conditions at the site it was desired to perform

In the piezocone penetration test the seismic piezocone penetrometer is
hydraulically pushed at a rate of approximately 20 mm second 5 mm second through
the subsurface soil The seismic piezocone consists of a 10 ton capacity steel electronic
subtraction cone with an apex angle of 60 degrees and an area of 1 55 square inches
10 square centimeters connected to a friction sleeve with an area of 23 25 square inches
150 square centimeters The tip resistance side friction resistance and pore pressure
induced immediately behind the cone tip designated as the Us parameter during
penetration are measured by pressure transducer and strain gages within the instrument
and are recorded versus the depth of penetration in approximately 2 inch 50 mm
intervals When the material encountered was too hard for further penetration by the
SCPTu equipment which might cause potential equipment damage the cone was
withdrawn Subsequently the hole was advanced by predrilling and using NQ coring
techniques to a depth of about 10 feet below the bottom of the previous cone sounding
The cone was then returned to the new hole bottom for continued sounding
The cone carries a uniaxial horizontal geophone to detect the arrival of a shear
wave propagated from the ground surface The seismic measurements are made when
the SCPTu has stopped and a shear wave is sent into the subsurface A shear wave is
generated at the surface by striking a loaded plank with a switched hammer The
propagation time of the wave from the hammer blow to the cone was measured at each
depth interval The vector difference of these depths divided by the time difference
provided the average shear wave velocity over the depth interval
Logs of the seismic cone penetration tests are presented on Plates 3 and 4 of this
letter report In general the plots include corrected cone tip resistance friction ratio
sleeve friction over tip resistance pore pressure differential pore pressure ratio shear
wave velocity soil behavior based on the R zone and interpreted soil description The
values of the tip resistance friction ratio and pore pressure may be correlated to soil
strength and classification The center of the friction sleeve is approximately 4 inches
100 mm behind the cone tip To calculate the friction ratio the average friction
resistance and tip resistance are compared at the same depth This involves an offset of
the friction resistance by the physical distance of 4 inches
Pore pressure is presented in terms of equivalent height of water head
Differential pore pressure ratio is calculated as a ratio of excess pore pressure to cone tip
resistance expressed as a percentage The excess pore pressure was calculated by
subtracting estimated equilibrium water pressure from the measured pore pressure The
equilibrium water pressure was estimated based on depth below water surface at the test
location and the density of water The soil behavior is determined using the average tip

Soil Profile Type
The shear wave velocities measured and the weighted average shear wave
velocity for the top 100 feet of the soil profile at the two locations are presented on Plates 5
and 6 The weighted average shear wave velocity was calculated based on the average
shear wave velocity method of the Uniform Building Code Section 1997
Edition
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered in our field exploration the two
weighted average shear wave velocities were measured at about 1 440 and 1 630 feet per
second Based on the shear wave velocity test results we believe that the project site
may be classified from a seismic analysis standpoint as being a Very Dense Soil and Soft
Rock site corresponding to an S soil profile type based on the Uniform Building Code
Table No 16 J 1997 Edition
Limitations
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part
upon information obtained from the cone penetration test probes It should be noted that
the cone penetration testing does not actually obtain a soil sample As a result it is not
possible to precisely identify exactly every type of soil encountered in the SCPTu probes
therefore the term SOIL BEHAVIOR TYPE is used on the logs In general the soil is
behaving like one soil type based on the SCPTu data It may not be precisely the soil
described on the logs but it is behaving as such Variations of subsurface soil conditions
and shear wave velocities between and beyond the test probes may occur and the nature
and extent of these variations may not become evident until construction is underway If
variations then appear evident it will be necessary to reevaluate the recommendations
provided in this letter report
The locations of the field cone penetration test indicated in this report are
approximate having been estimated by taping from the features shown on Site Plan and
Topographic Survey Maps transmitted by Architects Hawaii Limited on
Elevations of the probes were interpolated from the elevation points shown on the same
plan The physical locations and elevations of the cone penetration test probes should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the method used
