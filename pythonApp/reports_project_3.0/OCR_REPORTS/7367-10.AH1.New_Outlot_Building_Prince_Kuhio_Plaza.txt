Dear Mr. Gutierrez: 
This letter report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations during the 
probing and grouting operations for the New Outlot Building at Prince Kuhio Plaza on 
December 6 and 7, 2016. The probing and grouting observation work was performed in 
general accordance with our fee proposal dated December 2, 2016. 
Project Considerations 
The project site is in the northwest corner of the existing Prince Kuhio Plaza, near 
the intersection of Mamalahoa Highway and Makaala Street in Hilo on the Island of 
Hawaii. Prior to the construction of the new outlot building, an abandoned building 
occupied the project site previously operated by Hilo Hattie.  
Based on our field exploration and experience in the project area, cavities and/or 
voids are encountered commonly within the lava rock formation that characterize the 
subsurface at the project site. To reduce the potential for loss of foundation support 
resulting from the collapse of cavities below foundations, a cavity probing and grouting 
program was recommended prior to the footing construction. 
Based on available information, the existing ground surface surrounding the 
building pad was relatively level, with elevations ranging from about +73 to +74 feet Mean

Probing and Grouting Operations 
A foundation probing and grouting program was implemented for the New Outlot 
building project.  The location and general vicinity of the New Outlot building is shown on 
the Project Location Plan, Plate 1.  Isemoto Contracting Co., Ltd. performed the probing 
and grouting operations in general conformance with the recommendations presented in 
our report dated October 31, 2016 for the project. The purpose of the probing and grouting 
operations was to reduce the potential for loss of foundation support resulting from 
collapse of cavities or compression of loose zones below the building structure. 
A Geolabs, Inc. representative marked the probe hole locations prior to the probing 
and grouting operations. In general, the probe holes were located at the continuous strip 
footings (one probe hole at 10 feet on-center) and at each isolated spread footing location 
(one probe hole per 50 square feet). The approximate probe hole locations are shown on 
the Probe Hole Location Plan, Plate 2.  In general, the probe holes at the new building 
structure were drilled to a depth of about 10 to 11 feet below the existing ground surface, 
corresponding to approximately 10 feet below the design footing bottom elevations. 
The probing and grouting operations were accomplished using hollow drill rods with 
3.5-inch diameter drill bits with side discharge holes for water discharge.  Each probe hole 
was advanced by rotary percussion using a Gardner-Denver track drill.  Water was 
injected continuously through the hollow rods while advancing to the target probe depth.  
Evidence of cavities/voids was observed by advancement rate and drilling intensity during 
the probing.  After completion of the probe drilling operations, the probe hole was tremie 
grouted through the drill rod while the rod was being retracted.  The grout used for these 
probe holes consisted of a fine grout mix provided by Jas W. Glover, Ltd. 
Our field representative observed the probing and grouting operations and recorded 
the probe time, drilling intensity, and approximate grout take.  A total of 53 probe holes 
with an aggregate length of about 535 lineal feet below the existing ground surface were 
drilled for the building foundations as summarized in the following table and on Plates 3.1 
thru 3.3.

basalt rock formation. In general, we did not observe significant voids and/or cavities 
during the probing and grouting operations.
