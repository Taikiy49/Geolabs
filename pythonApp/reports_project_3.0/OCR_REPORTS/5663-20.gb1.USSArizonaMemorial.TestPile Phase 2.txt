In accordance with the project requirements a test pile program was undertaken for
the USS Arizona Memorial Visitor Center Replacement project at Pearl Harbor on the
Island of Oahu Hawaii between March 10 and March 15 2010 The test pile program
included driving seven test piles and performing dynamic load testing on all test piles This
report presents the results of our field observations during the test pile program and
recommendations for production pile lengths and driving of the production piles It should
be noted that the recommended production pile lengths contained herein are for
Buildings G H and J only
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is on the northern bank at the downstream end of
Halawa Stream in Pearl Harbor Due to the ongoing settlement of the existing visitor
center supported on shallow foundations a new USS Arizona Memorial Visitor Center
is planned at the site
The new visitor center will consist of several structures for orientation services
exhibits AV theaters retail areas restrooms and basic office space These structures
will consist of small one story buildings Exterior concrete flatwork or gravel walkways
connecting each structure will allow visitors to traverse the visitor center
Due to the ongoing consolidation at the site we recommended supporting the
new buildings and concrete slabs both interior and exterior on driven 16 5 inch
octagonal precast prestressed concrete piles with an allowable compressive load
capacity of up to 75 tons 150 kips per pile The ongoing consolidation of the estuarine
deposits would result in negative skin friction forces downdrag forces acting on the pile
shaft In order to achieve an allowable compressive load capacity of up to 75 tons for
the 16 5 inch octagonal concrete piles the piles would have to extend to depths of
about 200 feet below the existing ground surface to provide resistance to the downdrag

Hammer and Driving System
A diesel hammer was used for the pile driving operations by
Healy Tibbitts Builders Inc Based on the pile driving equipment data form provided
the hammer has a ram weight of 6 6 kips and a maximum stroke of
10 feet The maximum rated energy of the diesel hammer is about 66 1 kip feet when
set at Pump Setting 4 The hammer has a minimum rated energy of 33 7 kip feet when
set at Pump Setting 1
The hammer cushion consists of 3 layers of inch thick aluminum and 1 layer of
inch thick conbest for a total thickness of 2 inches Based on the subsurface
conditions and the driving conditions anticipated a pile cushion consisting of 6 inches of
plywood cushion blocks was proposed for the pile driving operations We understand
that the hammer cushion and plywood pile cushion blocks have cross sectional areas of
approximately 625 and 342 square inches respectively
Pile Details
The test piles generally consisted of 16 5 inch octagonal precast prestressed
concrete piles with lengths of about 203 and 210 feet The 210 foot long piles were
driven in three segments consisting of a 90 foot bottom segment a 65 foot middle
segment and a 55 foot top segment The 203 foot long pile Pile No 173 was driven
in three segments consisting of an 80 foot bottom segment a 60 foot middle segment
and a 63 foot top segment The pile segments were spliced with 6 foot long
non mechanical splice cans
TEST PILE PROGRAM
The test pile program was conducted between March 10 and 15 2010 The test
pile program generally consisted of driving seven test piles at various locations across
Buildings G H and J In addition the dynamic load testing of each test pile was
conducted by Foundation and Geotechnical Engineering LLC The purpose of the test
pile program was to establish the driveability of the piles to confirm the subsurface
conditions encountered in our borings and soil parameters used in the design and then
to provide the installation guidelines for the production pile program In addition the
test pile program also provides information on the driving resistance and contours of the
load bearing stratum across the project site which was used to estimate the lengths of
the production piles

It should be noted that a dense boulder and cobble layer was encountered in the
borings at depths of about 139 to 146 feet below the existing ground surface There is a
potential for the 200 foot long piles to encounter refusal on the boulder cobble layer
during driving of the middle segment Because of the potential for encountering refusal
during driving of the middle segment the tight spiral reinforcement spacing required in
the top segment should also be provided in the middle segment
Pile Driving Criteria
In general the diesel hammer set at Pump Setting 4 should be
used for the production pile installation Based on our engineering analyses and the test
pile program conducted for the project the following pile driving criteria may be used
during the production pile driving operations
1 Drive to the full depth of the production pile ordered 200 feet below the
existing ground surface
2 The pile driving operation may be terminated for the 200 foot long piles
when the pile driving resistance meets the following blow counts at a
minimum depth of about 140 feet below the existing ground surface
e Last 6 inches of penetration of at least 18 blows per inch
