This report summarizes our earthwork observation and testing services performed
during the site grading and foundation excavation of the Import Terminal for the Hawaiian
Cement Company at the Barbers Point Deep Draft Harbor from April 26 through
in accordance with our fee proposal dated May 4 2000
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The import terminal site is located to the northeast of Pier No 6 at the Barbers Point
Deep Draft Harbor on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The majority of the site is surrounded by
open land except towards the southwest which is abutted by the existing concrete
pavements of Pier No 6
The import terminal will consist of two monolithic concrete domes with a diameter of
about 145 feet and a height of about 77 feet Each dome will have an underground tunnel
located approximately 16 feet below the design finished grade We understand that the new
dome structures will generally have a finished floor elevation of about 22 7 feet Mean Sea
Level MSL The underground tunnel floor is set at an elevation of about 7 feet MSL In
addition we understand that the floor loading of the domes will be on the order of about
7 000 pounds per square foot psf The general layout of the dome structures and truck
loading area are shown on the Site Plan Plate 1

SITE PREPARATION AND EARTHWORK CONSTRUCTION
The existing ground at both dome locations was generally over excavated to provide
the 2 foot thick layer of compacted fill material below the dome slab subgrades For the truck
loading area the existing ground was generally excavated to about 6 inches below the
bottom of the concrete slab The over excavated subgrades were then proof rolled with a
10 ton vibratory sheepsfoot compactor a minimum of six passes to detect the presence of
near surface cavities Based on our field observations near surface cavities were not
evident during the proof rolling operations
The 2 foot thick compacted fills consisting of the on site coralline materials were
generally placed in near level lifts brought to above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to a minimum of 95 percent of the maximum dry density determined in
accordance with American Society of Testing and Material ASTM Test Designation D 1557
Modified Proctor During the foundation excavation and preparation the footing subgrades
were compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction prior to the placement of
reinforcing steel and concrete The earthwork operations and foundation excavations were
performed by Jayar Construction with Geolabs providing the field monitoring and compaction
testing services
Subsequent to the site preparation and fill placement operations a probing and
grouting program was conducted to reduce the potential for loss of foundation and floor slab
support resulting from collapse of cavities or compression of loose zones below the dome
structures and truck load area Detailed discussions and findings were presented in our
report entitled Foundation Probing and Grouting Report Import Terminal at Barbers Point
Hawaiian Cement Company Barbers Point Oahu Hawaii dated prepared for
the subject development
During the site grading operations samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor
and the test results are summarized on Plate 2 of this report
Periodic field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations to
confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures and soil conditions
were noted A total of 185 field density tests including retests of failed areas were
performed using the nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM Test Designations
D 2922 and D 3017 The results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on
Plates 3 1 through 3 9 of this report

The tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills
and backfills at the project site Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and
interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed to be
unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained and or
approved by our field representative based on visual observation and hand probing
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the earthwork operations during the site grading and
foundation excavation for the above referenced project observed and tested by Geolabs in

LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities occurring
simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork may have been
performed without our observation However the level of earthwork observation and testing
services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some of the geotechnical risks to the
project It should be recognized that there are variations in the accuracy and statistical
repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the earthwork construction and
materials
