GEOTECHNICAL ENGINEERING EXPLORATION
PORTABLES 1 THROUGH 6 SOIL SETTLEMENT STUDY
ALIAMANU ELEMENTARY SCHOOL
In accordance with our fee proposal dated we have completed
the geotechnical engineering exploration pertaining to the study of the settlement
of portable classrooms 1 through 6 at Aliamanu Elementary School for D A G S Job
No This letter report summarizes the work performed on the project and
presents our geotechnical findings and recommendations
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located within the Aliamanu Elementary School in the District of
Salt Lake in Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of
the project site are shown on the attached Project Location Map Plate 1
Based on information provided we understand that the portable classrooms at
Aliamanu Elementary School have experienced significant settlements on the order of
about 6 inches or more The portable buildings are wood framed structures supported on
precast concrete blocks Originally the precast blocks were placed directly on the ground
surface with no embedment
Presently almost all of the lower footing blocks have
subsided into the ground indicating settlements of about 6 inches or more Some of the
portable footings have settled differentially causing additional distress to the structures
We understand that other classroom buildings adjacent to the portable buildings
also experienced settlement distress associated with differential settlement It is desired to
evaluate the probable causes of the settlement for these portable buildings and provide a
conceptual design of remedial measures to raise the buildings to their origir ial elevations
A topographic map of the site was not available at the time of this report prepared
Based on the above information our subsurface exploration program concentrated
on the collapsibility and compressibility of the subsurface soils

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lava from two
shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be
middle to late Pliocene in age and Koolau is estimated to be late Pliocene to early
Pleistocene in age As the volcanic activity in Waianae ceased lava flows from Koolau
banked against its eroded eastern slope forming a broad plateau now known as the
Schofield Plateau
The project site is located on the south of the Aliamanu vent and on the
southwestern flank of the Salt Lake vent complex of the Honolulu Volcanic Series As the
molten magma ascended through these vents it reacted with the groundwater generating
steam This steam caused explosive eruptions which deposited low cones of ash material
that have consolidated to form tuff locally referred to as mudrock
The Aliamanu tuff is an older water laid ash deposit that was laid down when the
sea level was higher than the present level The Aliamanu eruptive event deposited its
material over alluvium originating from Halawa Stream
After this phase of volcanic activity ceased sedimentary deposits accumulated over
the Aliamanu tuff before the next phase of volcanic activity began Then the Salt Lake
eruptions began when the sea level was at about 40 to 60 feet below its present level
The Salt Lake eruptive event deposited its material over earlier tuffs from the older
Aliamanu events
The water that accumulated in the crater was believed to originate from
depressional springs and rainwater During periods of little rainfall the shallow ponded
water was reduced by evaporation and over the years the mineral salt content of the
ponded water increased resulting in the name Salt Lake
Recently man has
developed the crater floor by filling and grading the area for residential and golf course
purposes

In general the portable buildings consist of conventional wooden post and beam
construction supported on concrete blocks Portables 1 to 3 are supported on 20 wooden
post footings Portables 4 and 5 are supported on 35 wooden post footings and Portable 6
is supported on 25 wooden post footings
The building floor is approximately 6 to
12 inches above the ground
Based on our field observations the tops of the concrete blocks were generally
located near the existing ground surface Several concrete block footings appeared to
experience bearing failure The surface soil appeared dry and soft Shrinkage cracks up
to 1 inch wide were noted below the building floor The buildings are surrounded by lawn
grass A small void as well as wider shrinkage cracks were observed near Portable 6
Volcanic tuff rock formation was exposed on the existing ground in the areas near
Portable 1 Two drainline manholes were observed adjacent to Portables 4 and 5 which
indicated that a drain line system may traverse the site below the ground Metal pipelines
were also observed under the portable buildings
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by drilling and sampling
nine borings extending to depths of approximately 10 to 27 feet below the existing ground
surface Boring Nos 1 and 2 were drilled near Portable 6 The other borings designated
as Boring Nos 3 through 9 were drilled around Portables 1 through 5 The borings were
drilled using a truck mounted drill rig equipped with 4 inch solid stem auger The boring
locations are presented on the attached Site Plan Plate 2
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil

In general our borings indicated that the site was underlain by a relatively thin
surface fill layer consisting of soft to stiff reddish brown silty clay with some sand and
gravel over very stiff to hard clay deposit Weathered volcanic tuff rock formation was
encountered below the clay and extended to the depth of the borings
In the areas of Portables 1 through 5 the thickness of the surface fill and stiff clay
deposit increased from the east to west Portable 1 towards Portable 5 The weathered
volcanic tuff rock formation appeared to slope down from east to west The depths of the
top of the tuff rock varied from 0 5 to 14 5 feet below the ground surface
In the area of Portable 6 the clay deposit was relatively thicker and extended to a
depth of 23 5 feet below the ground surface
Groundwater was not encountered in the drilled borings at the time of our field
exploration However groundwater levels are expected to vary with seasonal rainfall
surface water runoff and time of year

FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the results of our field exploration and laboratory tests it appears that the
foundation settlement are a result of softening and collapse of the near surface soil directly
below the footings following saturation of the soil We recommend that the existing
concrete block footings be removed and replaced by embedded reinforced concrete
cast in place footings We recommend that soils below the portable buildings be removed
for a minimum depth of 3 feet and replaced with compacted select granular fill material
Building Foundation
Based on the information provided and our field observations the existing portable
buildings have experienced settlement distress
It appeared that the majority of the
footings for all six portable buildings had undergone similar settlement In general the
concrete block footings have subsided approximately 6 inches or more Some of the
footings have also experienced bearing failure Our boring information indicated that the
site is underlain by a thin layer of soft and dry surface fill consisting of silty clay with sand
and gravel
The surface fill was underlain by very stiff to hard clay over weathered
volcanic tuff rock formation extending to the maximum depth drilled of 27 feet below the
existing ground surface
It should be noted that the weathered tuff rock formation
was encountered at 6 to 12 inches below the ground surface in the area of
Portables 1 through 3
Our laboratory testing indicated that the stiff underlying clay is over consolidated
therefore on going consolidation settlement from this layer is unlikely
However our
laboratory testing indicated that soft and dry surface silty clay fill material has a potential to
collapse when the soil gets saturated
Based on the above information we believe that the concrete block footings
settlement may be induced primarily by the collapse of the surface soil Therefore we
recommend that the collapsible surface soil should be completely removed
The
underlying clay soils are stiff but have moderate to high swell potential Therefore we
recommend that a minimum 3 foot over excavation be conducted within the area of the
buildings as shown as Plate 3 The over excavation should be backfilled with compacted
select granular fill material The existing concrete block footings may be removed and
replaced with the new reinforced concrete footings The new footings should be designed
by a licensed structural engineer

An allowable bearing pressure of up to 2 500 pounds per square foot psf may be
used for the design of the new reinforced concrete footings bearing on the compacted
select granular fill material This bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may be
increased by one third 1 3 for transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic
forces We recommend that the footings be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the
lowest adjacent finished grade The existing portable buildings should be moved off the
site to allow for the over excavation and replacement We also recommend that the
wooden posts of the portable building be structurally tied with the new footings
The bottom of the over excavation and select granular backfill material should be
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction prior to placement of
reinforcing steel and concrete Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of
soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in
accordance with ASTM D 1557 test procedures Optimum moisture is the water content
percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density If foundations are
designed and constructed in accordance with our recommendations we estimate total
settlements to be less than 1 inch total with differential settlements on the order of about
0 5 inches
If foundations are located next to utility trenches or easements they should be
embedded below a 45 degree imaginary plane extending upward from the bottom edge of
the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility lines
This requirement is
necessary to avoid surcharging adjacent below grade structures with additional structural
loads and to reduce the potential for appreciable foundation settlement
Lateral loads acting on the structures may be resisted by frictional resistance
developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil and by passive earth
pressure acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system A coefficient of
friction of 0 35 may be used for footings bearing on the select granular fill materials
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid
pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth pct This assumes that the
soils around the footings are well compacted Unless covered by pavements or slabs the
passive resistance in the upper 12 inches of soil should be neglected
Drainage
The finished grades outside the building perimeter should be sloped to shed water
away from the foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also advised that
gutter systems be installed around the building and that the discharge be diverted away
from the foundations Excessive landscape watering near the foundations should also be
avoided Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have concrete bottoms and
drains to reduce the potential for water infiltration into the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the above
foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation

Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared Vegetation debris deleterious material asphalt concrete pavements
and other unsuitable materials within the grading limits should be removed and disposed
of properly off site
Soft and yielding areas encountered during clearing below areas designated to
receive fill or future improvements should be over excavated to expose firm or dense
material and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with well compacted fill The
excavated soft and or organic soils should be properly disposed of off site
Contract
documents should include additive and deductive unit prices for over excavation and
compacted fill placement to account for variation in the over excavation quantities
Existing underground utilities to be abandoned should be removed and the resulting
excavation should be properly backfilled with on site fill materials moisture conditioned to
above the optimum moisture content placed in 6 to 8 inch loose lifts and compacted to a
minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
The subgrade soils should be wetted and kept moist until placement of the concrete
Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of
the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557
test procedures
Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight
corresponding to the maximum dry density
Where shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of the subgrade we
recommend that the soil be thoroughly moistened to close the cracks or scarified and
prepared again as recommend above Saturation and subsequent yielding of the exposed
subgrade due to inclement weather and poor drainage may require over excavation of the
soft areas and replacement with well compacted fill at no additional cost to the owner
Select granular fill material should consist of non expansive granular material such
as crushed coral mudrock basalt or cinder sand The material should be well graded
from coarse to fine with no particles larger than 3 inches in largest dimension and should
contain between 20 and 30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve The material
should have a laboratory CBR value of 20 or more and should have a maximum swell of
1 percent or less
The select granular fill material should be tested by Geolabs for
conformance with these recommendations prior to delivery to the project site for the
intended use
