GEOLOGIC HISTORY
At the close of the Pleistocene Epoch Ice Age the area now
known as Enchanted Lakes was a bay on the windward coast of
Oahu Through the natural processes of sedimentation and
accretion of littoral beach sands the mouth of this bay was
narrowed until the area became a lagoon Further constriction
of the mouth created a pond with marshy fringes known as
Kaelepulu Pond Kraft in preparation

Natural sediments continued to accumulate in Kaelepulu Pond

POSSIBLE CAUSES FOR SETTLEMENT
Based on the information available to us our opinion regarding
possible causes for the settlements experienced in the Building
10 areas are summarized below There is also a possibility
that the settlements may be the result of a combination of the
plausible mechanisms
A Long Term Settlements due to Site Filling
The general project site is situated over an old lakebed
area which is underlain by a thick layer 45 of soft under
water deposits Fills up to about 25 feet thick were placed
over the soft soils Substantial ground settlements would
occur due to the weight of the new fill over the soft soils
Generally surcharging preloading the site with additional
stockpiles of soil is utilized to accelerate the settlements
and decrease the settlements under the new building However
we did not find evidence that surcharging was utilized for this
site

B LOWERING OF GROUNDWATER LEVEL
Conjecture has been advanced that a break or breaks in the
Keolu Drive Sewer System is depressing the groundwater level in
this area This depression of the piezometric surface may have
removed the bouyant forces supporting the fill soils and
thereby increased the effective overburden pressures and
increased the consolidation of the underlying soft sediments
While there is some evidence to support this idea there are
some data which conflicts with this theory
If the sewer line was the source of the water drain the water
level drawdown would be the deepest next to the drain and
therefore should result in the largest settlement next to the
drain While survey readings indicate that the sewer line and
Keolu Drive are also settling it appears that the focus of the
settlement is somewhat removed from the sewer line

CONCLUSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Our review of the available data indicates that the area may be
experiencing long term settlements due to the weight of the
previously placed fill over the soft lake deposits Evidence
of surcharging and settlement monitoring to reduce long term
settlements prior to house construction were not found in the
data available to us
Lowering of the ground water level due to breaks in the sewer
line could cause additional settlements however flow measure
ments by the Division of Waste Water Management appears to
contradict this theory
