PRELIMINARY FOUNDATION INVESTIGATION
PROPOSED COLD STORAGE WAREHOUSE ADDITION
MCON PROJECT P 110
U S NAVAL SUPPLY DEPOT GUAM
This report presents the results of our preliminary soils
investigation performed for the proposed Cold Storage Warehouse Addition
P 110 at the U S Naval Supply Depot Guam Mariana Islands The
general location of the site and its vicinity are shown on the Project
Location Map Plate l
PROJECT CONSIDERATION
It is proposed to construct a one story 25 640 square foot
reintorced concrete addition to the existing cold storage warehouse
The general layout of the project is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
W O 1435 00 Page 2
The design live load over the warehouse floor will be 450 p s f
with small forklift truck operations The cold storage floor slab
system will consist of a 6 inch concrete slab over 4 inches of
insulation on top of a 10 inch thick reinforced concrete structural Slab
which will be supported by piles The finished floor grade is to match
that of the existing warehouse which is at elevation 12 0 feet the
datum assumed for this project
A truck apron area consisting of concrete pavement with asphalt
concrete pavement transition areas will be constructed to the east of
the warehouse addition for truck loading
Site grading both cut and fill along with installation of
supporting utilities will be required for the sidewalk and concrete
truck apron areas
PURPOSE AND SCOPE OF SERVICES
The purpose of the soil investigation is to provide information
related to soils and foundation engineering for the above design The
scope of services rendered is in general conformance with our proposal
dated In general our services included field
exploration program consisting of a site visit by the Principal
Engineer drilling and sampling of 5 borings to a depth of 80 feet each
a laboratory testing program and an engineering evaluation of the field
and laboratory findings
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1435 00 Page 3
Detailed description of our field exploration laboratory testing
together with the logs of borings are presented in the appendices The
approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is near the eastern shore of the Inner Apra
Harbor Islands
The site is located on a relatively flat asphalt concrete paved
area at the northern end of the existing cold storage warehouse
building The general ground surface ranged from about elevation 11 5
feet along the western wharf X boundary to about elevation 8 5 feet
near the drain inlet catch basin located at the northeastern corner of
the existing cold storage building
The site drainage generally runs from the higher ground at the
western end towards the eastern portion of the site
Subsurface Soil Conditions
Below the A C pavement the site is underlain by an 8 to 10 foot
fill layer consisting of dense to medium dense coral sand and gravel
overlying soft and loose compressible lagoonal clay and loose sand
deposits of various thickness The compressible clay was about 40 feet
thick in Boring No 4 but was not encountered in Boring No 5
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1435 00 Page 4
The compressible lagoonal deposits were in turn underlain by medium
dense to dense coral sand and gravel deposits with partly cemented
pockets
A 6 5 foot cavity was encountered at a depth of about 72 feet in
Boring No 5
The groundwater levels were encountered in our borings and
measured as shown on the boring logs It appeared that the prevailing
groundwater level at the time of our borings was at about 7 to 9 feet
below the existing grade and may fluctuate due to tidal and other
environmental influences such as barometric pressure and rain run off
DISCUSSION RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Our preliminary borings reveal that the site is underlain by soft
and loose compressible lagoonal deposits of varying thickness
Furthermore it is anticipated that the requirement of both design
structural and floor load will be high Hence potential ground
settlements will probably contribute the major consideration for the
proposed foundation construction
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1435 00 Page 5
It is estimated that ground settlement from the design live load
plus the weight of the floor slab could induce ground settlements
ranging from one to six inches If 2 feet of fill is placed in addition
to the above loadings the ground settlement could increase by another 2
inches We believe the anticipated settlements will occur at various
rates i e very slow settlements in Boring No 4 area and relatively
fast settlement in Boring and 5 areas Therefore substantial
differential settlements can be resulted under such loading conditions
In view of the anticipated high settlement we believe that one of
the better solutions to the foundation problem will be for the proposed
cold storage structure to be supported entirely on piles Detailed
recommendations on pile foundation will be discussed in the later part
of the report
Several alternatives to avoid or minimize the scale of using pile
foundation had been presented in our meetings with the project team
Those alternatives which were considered to be not feasible for the
proposed scheme are briefly described below

Alternatives to Pile Foundation
A Compensated Foundation
The finished floor slabs could be constructed on grade about 5
feet below the existing grade to minimize settlements from the
live loads However it would appear that the floor grade
will be much lower than the existing adjoining warehouse
structure This has been considered unacceptable from an
operational point of view
B Light Weight Structure
8 om mm em ae cn
Sufficient amount of the existing fill may be removed and
replaced with a hollow lightweight structure for on grade
floor support It is estimated that 7 to 8 feet of fill
removal will be required and the excavation will probably
bottom in the soft lagoonal deposit The heavier building
wall and column load will still need to be supported on
separate pile foundations while the structural floor could be
eliminated
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Page 7
C Preloading
Preloading the site to minimize post construction settlements
have been used in other projects However preloading may
develop ground settlements and add downdrags to the foundation
piles in the existing adjacent cold storage warehouse
structure In addition it will require a very long period of
time to consolidate the soft lagoonal clay deposit
Pile Foundation
In view of the underlying thick soft compressible lagoonal
deposits it is recommended that all loads including the building and
floor loads should be supported by pile foundations No on grade slab
should be used to support the heavy floor loads
Our analysis shows that the pile foundation depends critically on
whether there is a need to place fill on top of the existing grade The
two cases one with new fill and the other with no fill were analysed
individually and are presented as follows
Pile Foundation with Fill
Filling is required at part of the site where existing grade is
lower than the future subgrade level The additional fill will
induce substantial ground settlements which in turn will create
large downdrag forces on both the new and the adjacent existing
timber composite piles To counteract this additional downdrag
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1435 00 Page 8
the new pile will need to penetrate deeper probably requiring
embedment greater than 80 feet the maximum depth originally
scheduled for this investigation In addition a large cavity was
encountered within the 80 foot borings drilled Based on the
recently available information a competent coral limestone
formation was encountered at a depth of about 110 feet below MLLW
level below the nearby Army Reserve Center site It would appear
that such a competent end bearing stratum could be encountered
within a reasonable depth of say 130 feet below grade
It is also noted that at the Army Reserve Center site thin
limestone ledges were also noted in some borings at various
depths Such thin ledges though not encountered at the project
site borings could pose additional difficulties for the
installation of long piles
Our current recommendation calls for the schedule of two 2
additional borings to a depth of 150 feet each One boring should
be located between Boring Nos 1 and 2 and the other boring
should be located close to Boring No 5 where a large cavity was
encountered Final recommendations for pile foundations with site
filling will be provided when these two additional borings are
completed

Pile Foundation Without Fill
a a Se a et ae ce is
If no new fill is placed at the site we believe downdrag force
will be minimum Although no competent end bearing stratum was
encountered it is our opinion that adequate support can be
developed from skin friction within the underlying medium dense to
dense coral sand and gravel deposit
For friction piles required we believe that 12 inch square
pre stressed concrete piles could be utilized For pile capacity
up to 50 tons each the calculated pile tip elevation should be
about elevation 70 feet in Boring Nos 1 and 4 and about 60
feet in Boring Nos 2 and 3 areas In Boring No 5 area where a
large cavity was detected below a depth of 72 feet additional
probing should be made to examine the extent of the cavity To
further delineate the horizontal and vertical extent of possible
cavity zones we recommend that a number of long test piles be
driven through the suspected cavity zones Assuming that the
cavity is localized and that the hard zone above the cavity could
not be penetrated we believe that friction pile capacity should
be reduced to about 30 tons each and the pile penetration be
Stopped at 55 feet below the finished grade
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1435 00 Page 10
Thus for preliminary budget estimate a design pile load capacity
of about 50 tons with an average pile penetration length of 80
feet could be used The final pile casting lengths should be
determined after the additional probing and test pile driving at
various locations have been carried out
The piles should be driven with a hammer capable of delivering at
least 15 000 foot pounds of energy per blow Heavy hammers such
as those with energies exceeding 30 000 foot pounds are not
recommended for this project It is recommended that the piles be
designed to a net maximum axial loading of 50 tons per pile and be
spaced at a minimum of feet on centers
A test pile program should be undertaken to examine the pile
driving and penetration behaviors and pile capacity We
recommend that at least ten piles be test driven for this
purpose These test piles can be incorporated into actual
foundations later Each test pile section should be 80 feet
long At least three extra sections and four splice cans should
be provided at the site during the test driving period Splice
cans may be fabricated with 1 4 inch thick high grade steel and
should be at least 4 feet long
GEOLABS HAWAII
WO 1435 00 Page ll
It is further recommended that at least one test pile be driven at
close vicinity to Boring Nos 1 through 4 to examine the pile
driving characteristics through sand and gravel pocket within the
lagoonal deposit Boring No 1 and partly cemented zone Boring
No 2 It is probable that other test piles may encounter high
driving resistance at shallow depths during the test driving The
test piles should penetrate through hard lenses in order to
examine the nature and complexity of these hard driving zones and
to determine if driving shoe or predrilling is necessary for
future remaining production pile installation At this time we
believe that the piles can probably be driven through the sand or
gravel pocket or the partly cemented zones
Based on the use of a pile driving hammer with 15 000 foot pounds
of rated energy to drive a 12 inch square concrete pile 80 feet
long with a net design load of 50 tons the recommended test pile
driving criteria is as follows
1 The driving process should be continuous without interruption
for each pile A pile may be rejected or its design capacity
be modified when the driving process is interrupted for more
than 4 hours
2 The piles should be embedded at least 30 feet below the
existing ground surface
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1435 00 Page 12
3 The pile driving may be terminated when the blow counts are
steadily increasing with depth and reach 10 blows per inch
for each of the last 6 inches or aggregated blow counts of
120 blows for the last foot
The pile driving criteria should be modified according to the
actual rated energy of the hammer used
Test pile should be driven with a hammer which is to be used also
for production pile driving The test pile program should be
conducted under close and continuous observation of a soils
engineer who will determine when piles may be stopped and to
provide guidance should additional measures be required due to
unforeseen driving conditions He should evaluate the final load
carrying pile capacity and pile embedment lengths based on the
results of the test pile driving and a pile load test
As a part of the test pile program a pile load test is
recommended The soils engineer should select one of the test
piles driven and load tested it to 120 tons in accordance with
the ASTM D 1143 test procedure

Pavements
It is our understanding that a concrete truck apron is planned in
front of the loading dock area to the east of the cold storage
warehouse addition To provide sufficient dock height and to allow for
new truck apron pavement section up to 3 feet of excavation below the
existing grade will be required Asphalt concrete pavements are
planned for transition zones on both northern and southern sides of the
truck apron area
The traffic loading is not well defined at this time For design
purpose we have selected a medium highway traffic load with Design
Index of 3 according to NAVFAC Design Manual 5 4 This traffic load
consists of 21 to 75 daily repetition of 18 kip equivalent axle load
Based on our borings the existing upper fill is coral sand and
gravel which will generally provide good subgrade support For the new
pavement construction it is recommended that the existing fill be
overexcavated down to at least 18 inches below the finished grade of
the new pavement The top 6 inches of soil below the overexcavated
bottom should be recompacted to a minimum of 95 relative compaction
per the ASTM D 1557 78 maximum density
Any soft or yielding spot disclosed at the bottom of this
overexcavation should be repaired by removing soft or yielding soils
and replaced with compacted approved on site coral sand and gravel
material
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 1435 00 Page 14
The excavated on site coral sand and gravel may be stockpiled to
be reused as backfill into the overexcavation provided that the
material is first tested and approved by a soils engineer
The backfill within 18 inches of the finished pavement grade
should be placed in lifts not exceeding 8 inches in compacted thickness
and compacted to a minimum of 95 relative compaction
It is anticipated that the coral sand and gravel be used as
subgrade soils Accordingly a relatively good subgrade support with a
minimum CBR value of 25 is assumed in the design For the new
pavements in the truck apron and transition areas the following
sections are recommended

Design Review
Plans and specifications for site grading and foundation
construction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and
written comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the pile installations truck apron
overexcavation and subgrade preparations for pavements be observed by
the soils engineer during construction This is to see that adequate
pile driving and or soil compaction have been obtained and that the
subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of our
investigation
If conditions encountered during construction are different from
those presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above inspections

five 81 5 foot borings below the existing ground surface at the
approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 A truck mounted

monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further

Classification System A graphic presentation of the materials
encountered is presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 1 through
A 5 3
Soil samples were obtained from the boring by driving a 2 inch
standard split spoon sampler or a 2 4 inch I D split barrel sampler
with a 140 pound hammer free falling 30 inches The blow counts to
drive the sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the boring logs at
the appropriate sample depths
