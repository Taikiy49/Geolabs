INTRODUCTION
This report represents the results of our geotechnical
engineering evaluation performed for the Hilo Environmental Health
Offices near the town of Hilo in the District of South Hilo on the
island of Hawaii The general location of the project site and
its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2467 00 Page 2
exploration was performed in general accordance with our proposal
dated
This report summarizes our findings from the exploration and
our geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our
analyses based on the data obtained during the exploration These
recommendations are intended for design input and are not intended
for construction estimation or bidding purposes
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of
the subsurface conditions at the project site in order to develop
a generalized soils data set for the formulation of engineering
design recommendations pertinent to the proposed development
The work was done generally in accordance with our proposal
of
The scope of work included the drilling and sampling of six
6 borings to depths ranging between 15 0 and 33 0 feet below the
existing ground surface Also included were engineering evaluation
of the field data the development of design recommendations and
report preparation
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration and laboratory
testing are presented in the appendices of this report
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is situated about 2 000 feet west of the
existing Hilo International Airport General Lyman Field in the
South Hilo District of the island of Hawaii The site is located
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2467 00 Page 3
on the south side of Kamehameha Avenue between the KKTei Restaurant
and Waiakea Recreation Center The general location of the site
and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The site measures 217 by 117 feet in plan dimensions and is
relatively level Ground elevations at the site vary between 10 0
to 14 0 feet above Mean Sea Level MSL
Presently the site is vacant
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to develop the site as the new State of Hawaii
Department of Health Environmental Programs offices for the island
of Hawaii
The preliminary plans indicate that the proposed office
building will be a single story structure of wood and masonry
construction with slab on grade floors
In addition the development will include two 2 parking
areas with a total of 25 stalls It is anticipated that asphaltic
concrete will be used for the parking area pavements
Minimal site grading is anticipated
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored
by drilling and sampling six 6 borings to depths ranging between
15 0 and 33 0 feet below the existing ground surface The
approximate locations are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2467 00 Page 4
In general the borings encountered a surface fill layer
about 3 5 to 11 5 feet thick consisting of loose to dense gray and
brown silty sand and gravel underlain by gray clinkers and basalt
rock to 33 0 feet the maximum depth drilled
An ash layer consisting of medium brown clayey silt with
traces of gravel was encountered from 1 0 to 3 5 foot depths in
Boring No B 6
Subsurface voids were encountered in Boring No 2
The logs of the drilled borings are presented in Appendix A
of this report
Groundwater levels were encountered in the drilled borings at
about 10 0 foot depth below the existing ground surface during our
exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Our field exploration generally encountered a surface fill
layer about 3 5 to 11 5 feet thick consisting of loose to dense
silty sand and gravel underlain by clinkers and basalt rock to 33 0
feet the maximum depth drilled
Because of the loose soils encountered near the estimated
finish grade we recommend that at least two 2 feet of loose
soils below the footings and on grade slabs be excavated back
filled and recompacted to a minimum of 90 maximum dry density as
established by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2467 00 Page 5
Subsurface voids were encountered in one of the borings Due
to the existence of subsurface voids and the potential existence
of additional cavities within the volcanic rock formation we
recommend that a program of probing and grouting at the foundation
locations be conducted during construction This is discussed in
a later section of this report
Site Grading
Prior to the commencement of any earthwork all areas within
the grading limits should be cleared of all vegetation and
deleterious materials The resulting grub and spoil material
should be disposed of properly off site or stockpiled for use in
landscaping Any soft weak or yielding areas disclosed during the
clearing operations within the portions of the grading limits
designated to receive fill should be over excavated to firm ground
and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with well
compacted engineered fill
It is our recommendation that the subgrade below the footings
and on grade slabs be over excavated a minimum of two 2 feet
backfilled and recompacted as stated previously because of the
loose soils encountered near the estimated finish grade
If required new imported fill material should consist of non
expansive select granular materials less than 3 inches in maximum
size with less than 20 passing the 200 sieve
Fills and backfills should be placed in level lifts not
exceeding ten 10 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a
minimum of 90 maximum dry density as established by ASTM Test
Method D 1557 78

Foundations
Spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the
proposed structure An allowable bearing value of 3 000 p s f may
be used for footings bearing on the compacted on site or select
fill material The bearing value is for total dead plus live loads
and may be increased by one third 1 3 when temporary wind and
seismic loads are considered Estimated settlements are on the
order of less than one half 1 2 of an inch total and one fourth
1 4 of an inch differential
Individual footings and continuous wall footing should be
embedded a minimum of 12 inches below the lowest adjacent finished
grade
Resistance to lateral loads on the building can be obtained
by passive earth pressure and frictional resistance A frictional
coefficient of 0 4 may be used to calculate the friction between
the bottom of the footings and the recompacted on site or borrow
soil In addition an equivalent fluid pressure of 350 p s f per
foot of depth may be used to determine the passive earth resistance
against the buried vertical faces of the footings The perimeters
of the footings should be properly compacted in order to provide
the recommended passive resistance Unless covered by pavements
or slabs the passive resistance in the upper 18 inches of soil
should be neglected
Cavity Probing
The field exploration encountered volcanic rock formation at
shallow depths below the project site Cavities or void were
encountered in Boring No 2 during our exploration Previous
experience indicates that such voids and cavities lava tubes are
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2467 00 Page 7
commonly found in volcanic formations Several substantial tubes
have been found in the South Hilo District
In order to reduce the possibility of a potential void
situated below a foundation causing settlement and or foundation
failure we recommend that the foundation subgrades be probed The
probes should consist of a 3 inch minimum diameter hole drilled to
a depth of 10 feet below the bottom of the footing Probes should
be drilled at each major footing location and on 10 foot centers
along continuous or strip foundations
The probe holes should be backfilled with a mortar like grout
1 sand to 1 cement injected at low to moderate pressures in an
upstage fashion i e from the bottom of the probe hole to the
top
To simplify the mobilization of the probing equipment the
probing and grouting operations may be performed prior to structure
and footing excavation
Slabs On Grade
It is anticipated that the floors of the proposed structure
will be concrete slabs on grade The on grade floor slab should
be placed over a 4 inch thick cushion layer of gravel size
material such as 3B Fine rock to provide uniform support
In order to prevent future moisture infiltration and subse
quent damage to floor coverings an impervious moisture membrane
is recommended below the slabs

Pavements
Asphaltic concrete pavements are anticipated for the parking
areas We recommend that the A C pavement section consist of the
following
2 Inches Asphaltic Concrete
6 Inches Base Course
8 Inches Total Pavement Thickness
on Compacted Moist Subgrade
The base course should be compacted to a maximum of 95
maximum dry density as established by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
CBR and density tests and or field observations should be
performed on the actual subgrade soils encountered during construc
tion to verify the adequacy of the above section The recommended
section considers medium traffic and assumes that adequate drainage
will be provided in the paved areas Weepholes should be provided
at the base course level in drainage inlet structures in order to
relieve water which may accumulate in the subgrade
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should
be forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written
comment prior to construction This review is needed to determine
adherence to the intent of the earthwork and foundation recommenda
tions given herein If this review is not made the geotechnical
engineer cannot assume responsibility for misinterpretation of the
recommendations
GEOLABS HAWAII

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained
to provide geotechnical engineering services during construction
of the excavation and foundation phases of work This is to
observe compliance with the design concepts specifications or
recommendations and to allow design changes in the event that
subsurface conditions differ from that anticipated prior to start
of construction The recommendations given in this report are
contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested
and approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the
site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be
made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are
based in part upon information obtained from field data points
such as borings Variations of conditions between the field data
points may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may
not become evident until construction If variations then appear
evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations
given in this report
The location of the field data points were approximately
determined by taping and estimation from reference points shown on
the plan prepared by Imata Associates Inc undated Elevations
of the data points were approximately determined by estimation from
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2467 00 Page 11
the same plan The physical locations and elevations of the field
data points should be considered accurate only to the degree
implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in the graphic representations
of the field data points depict the approximate boundaries between
soil types and as such may denote a gradual transition The
strata lines shown on profiles or cross sections are based upon
interpolation between field data points and may not represent
actual subsurface conditions
Water level data from the field data points was collected at
the times given on the graphic representations and or in the text
of this report This data has been reviewed and interpretations
made in the formulation of this report However it must be noted
that fluctuation may occur due to variations in rainfall tempera
ture and other factors
