IV General Guidelines for the Boring Depth Determination
Boring depth in cut areas should extend a minimum of 5 feet below the
bottom of the planned cut elevation
Boring depth in fill areas should generally be on the order of about 20 to
30 feet
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3595 00 Page 3
Boring depth in soft ground areas should extend a minimum of 10 feet
below the bottom of the soft soil deposits Soft soil is defined as
blowcounts of less than about 10 blows per foot
e Boring depth at bridge abutment locations should be adjusted in the field
depending on the subsoil conditions encountered The boring depth
should be a minimum of 40 feet
V General Guidelines for Bulk Sample Collection
e Surface vegetation and debris should be cleared before obtaining near
surface bulk samples Remove debris and large stones from the collected
bulk sample
e Bulk samples should be obtained when distinctly different soil types are
encountered along the alignment for each design package
A minimum of 2 CBR bags at least 75 percent filled should be obtained
at each sample location and clearly labeled with sample number and
location
A minimum of 10 bulk sample locations should be spread evenly along the
alignment for each design package
e Both the sample identification number and Station number where the bulk
sample was collected should be recorded separately for reference and
marked on each CBR bag with non erasable black marker pen in bold
lettering
A bulk sample one CBR bag full should be obtained at the stream bed
where a bridge crossing is planned for gradation tests pertaining to stream
scour analyses

Groundwater Measurements
Measure and record groundwater levels as soon as groundwater is encountered
and again at least twice per day once at the end of drilling following
groundwater stabilization and once before commencement of drilling the
following day in each boring Additional groundwater levels should be taken if
the measurements vary Keep borings covered and the surrounding area graded
to keep rainwater from pouring in Backfill all borings with soil cuttings following
the last groundwater measurement
General Guidelines for Rock Core Sampling
Rock coring should be performed when the subsoils grade to rock
formation In general begin coring when drive sampler cannot be
advanced following a maximum of 2 sampling events or when augers will
not penetrate Do not overstress auger drilling equipment especially
portable drilling machines
Rock coring may be terminated in planned fill areas when competent rock
formation is encountered for a minimum of 5 to 10 feet
Rock coring in cut areas should extend at least to the planned cut
elevation
Rock coring should be performed with 5 foot runs using water as the
drilling fluid with mud additive only where necessary to maintain hole
stability and cutting removal Shorter core runs are permissible if total
recovery is unsatisfactory

SPT samples should be taken between core runs to verify hardness of
saprolite and weathered rock materials
Rock cores should be placed in the core box with the beginning depth
starting at the upper left hand corner proceeding to the lower right hand
corner of the core box
Core runs should be separated with a block spacer and labeled with
boring number and depth Core should be packed securely with minimal
excess space as core box supply is limited
Core box interior cover and exterior front should be clearly marked with
permanent black marker showing the boring number and coring depth
contained therein
Each core box should be used for one boring only
Good quality field photographs should be taken of each full core box with
the boring number and coring depth information shown in the picture
Should take 2 photos per box to assure development
Restrictions to Field Explorations
No disturbance to unexploded ordnance Instructions presented in the
brochure entitled Ordnance Explosives Safety prepared by Brad
Lasater for the Palau Compact Road project should be followed and
observed
Do not allow runoff or drilling fluids or other waste products to enter
streams lakes bays or other bodies of water Bury sanitary wastes away
from sources of water
Debris such as damaged tools sample bags rings tubes core box
bottles and other litter must be removed from each drilling site following
drilling Do not litter roadways villages or storage areas
Disturbance to pre existing site conditions should be restored to its
original conditions as far as practical Do not alter any historic or
archeological features
All borings should be backfilled with cuttings or on site soils after
completion of the drilling sampling and groundwater level
measurements

Observe local customs and avoid divulging specific project information
Also avoid using foul or offensive language in the villages
Obey all instruction given by government authorities or village

Adhere to the requirements and regulations set forth in the earthwork

BACKGROUND
Citizens Band C B radio was selected for use on this project due to the lack of
communication facilities on Babeldaob VHF of other hand held radios do not have the
distance range that a CB radio has due to lower power and smaller antenna
Before installing and operating the radio please read and familiarize yourself with
the pamphlet provided with the radio unit Incorrect wiring or improper usage of the
radio could result in radio damage and loss
RADIO INSTALLATION
The radio antenna base and whip antenna comprise the 3 main pieces of
equipment For our use the radio should either be securely mounted in the vehicle or
be placed for daily removal from the vehicle for theft reasons
Exercise caution to provide for the following

ANTENNA INSTALLATION
The antenna base is a magnet mount and is packaged separate from the whip
Use the given allen wrench keep it in a safe place for future tuning to install the
whip Install the base of whip exactly 1 0 inch into the base mount and tighten
securely This should be the proper tuned setting further adjustment may be
necessary to optimize the transmitter power Once the whip is installed and tuned do
not remove it Store antenna at night for security in vehicle
RADIO USE
To keep all personnel on the same SSB channel we will use channel 36
with the switch set to USB UPPER SIDE BAND
Refer to the operations manual for information on how to use the radio Basically
SSB transmission gives you higher power but you must manually tune the clarifier
control to clarify voice to the appropriate sound

1 Scope
1 1 This test method covers the field vane test in soft
saturated cohesive soils Knowledge of the nature of the soil
in which each vane test is to be made is necessary for
assessment of the applicability and interpretation of the test
1 2 The values stated in inch pound units are to be
regarded as the standard The SI units given in parentheses
are for information only
1 3 This standard does not purport to address all of the
safety concerns if any associated with its use It is the
responsibility of the user of this standard to establish appro
priate safety and health practices and determine the applica
bility of regulatory limitations prior to use
2 Summary of Test Method
2 1 The vane shear test basically consists of placing a
four bladed vane in the undisturbed soil and rotating it from
the surface to determine the torsional force required to cause
a cylindrical surface to be sheared by the vane this force is
then converted to a unit shearing resistance of the cylindrical
surface It is of basic importance that the friction of the vane
tod and instrument be accounted for otherwise the friction
would be improperly recorded as soil strength Friction
measurements under no load conditions such as the use of a
blank stem in place of the vanes or a vane that allows some
free rotation of the rod prior to loading are satisfactory only
provided that the torque is applied by a balanced moment
that does not result in a side thrust As torsional forces
become greater during a test a side thrust in the instrument
will result in an increase in friction that is not accounted for
by initial no load readings Instruments involving side thrust
are not recommended The vane rod may be of sufficient
igidity that it does not twist under full load conditions
Otherwise a correction must be made for plotting torque
Totation curves

5 Procedure
5 1 In the case where a vane housing is used advance the
housing to a depth which is at least five vane housing
diameters less than the desired depth of the vane tip Where
no vane housing is used stop the hole in which the vane is
lowered at a depth such that the vane tip may penetrate
undisturbed soil for a depth of at least five times the diameter
of the hole
5 2 Advance the vane from the bottom of the hole or the
vane housing in a single thrust to the depth at which the test
is to be conducted Take precautions to make sure no torque
is applied to the torque rods during the thrust
5 3 With the vane in position apply the torque to the
vane at a rate which should not exceed 0 1 s This generally
requires a time to failure of from 2 to 5 min except in very
soft clays where the time to failure may be as much as 10 to
15 min In stiffer materials which reach failure at small
deformations it may be desirable to reduce the rate of
angular displacement so that a reasonable determination of
the stress strain properties can be obtained During the
rotation of the vane hold it at a fixed elevation Record the
maximum torque With apparatus with geared drives it is
desirable to record intermediate values of torque at intervals
of 15 s or at lesser frequency if conditions require
5 4 Following the determination of the maximum torque
rotate the vane rapidly through a minimum of 10 revolu
tions the determination of the remoulded strength should be
started immediately after completion of rapid rotation and in
all cases within min after the remoulding process

BACKGROUND
A number of important military towns were located on
Babeldoab Island during the Japanese occupation
A large amount of various types of ammunition is known to
have been stockpiled by the Japanese during their
occupation In preparation for a possible invasion by U S
forces the Japanese prepared several defenses on the
island These defenses included coastal defense batteries
mine emplacements along various beaches and mine
emplacements in the major roads Many of the mine
emplacements did not include fuzes The fuzes were
intended to be put in when an invasion of the island became
imminent
Although the U S did bomb several targets on Babeldoab
this island was not invaded during the war After the
Japanese Area Commander surrendered on 2 September
1945 the ammunition stockpiles gun emplacements and
mines were abandoned and left in place
CURRENT STATUS
Many of the stockpiles gun emplacements and mines
remain today It is probable that you will encounter
ordnance during your operations in Palau Much of the
ordnance is on the surface of the ground In many locations
Page D 3
simply parting the jungle canopy will expose stacks of
projectiles or bombs The existing road from Airai to
Ngardmau has mines buried close to the surface Some of
these are visible as you drive while others still remain
hidden
Most Japanese ordnance items were manufactured with
varying degrees of built in safety Often the safety was no
more than a shear wire which can be expected to have
rusted away after 50 years in the ground Safety forks
and safety pins can also be expected to have rusted away
In other words a lot of the OE may have NO safety devices
remaining These items may be extremely sensitive to
movement Most Japanese fuzes were manufactured with
Picric Acid as a major part of the gain booster Picric Acid
forms extremely sensitive explosive salts in contact with
steel The slightest friction through movement may be
enough to initiate the explosive
In contrast to metal which becomes weaker with age
explosives may become more unstable and more sensitive
The only thing that can be predicted about OE items is that
they are UNPREDICTABLE

PRECAUTIONS
U S Army Corps of Engineers policy on doing work in areas
contaminated with Ordnance Explosives OE is as follows
When an HTRW or construction project including survey
and design activities takes place in an area having potential

the purpose of avoiding ordnance This team will normally
consist of two people a UXO Supervisor and a UXO
Specialist or UXO Assistant POD recognizes that a
contractor UXO team may not be present for all contractor
teams performing work during the survey and design phases
of this project For any activities performed without the
presence of a UXO team you need to take the following

PURPOSE
This plan is to outline the general safety procedures for drilling operations The
plan includes but is not limited to safety practices and procedures for general daily
operations and drilling operations conducted at sites potentially contaminated with
hazardous toxic substances Due to the diverse nature of our operations procedures and
practices may be modified on an individual basis
Although it is the employer s responsibility to provide a safe working place it is the
employee s responsibility to comply with safety procedures and practices established by
the company Failure to comply will result in disciplinary action
HEALTH AND SAFETY PERSONNEL
The general Health and Safety Officer HSO is Francis Meyer Drilling Foreman
or in his absence The HSO will be responsible for the day to day health
and safety considerations and for maintaining an adequate inventory of health and safety
supplies Shortages of supplies and or special requirements should be reported to the
HSO sufficiently in advance to allow the need to be satisfied
During field work a member of the field crew either the Field Engineer Geologist
or the Driller will be the Site Safety Officer SSO The SSO will be responsible for
observing that the work is conducted in accordance with this plan and other applicable
safety rules and procedures including the use of common sense
In addition the SSO will be responsible for the following
Daily safety meeting before start of work
Monitoring field activities to ensure that work is performed in a safe manner
and to terminate work when unsafe conditions become evident
Making sure that the crew is familiar with the Site Safety Plan
Making sure that the field personnel wear the proper protective equipment
and maintain that equipment in a functional condition
Monitoring site conditions and evaluating potential hazards
GEOLABS HAWAII
General Site Safety Plan Page E 2
Monitoring work area and equipment cleanliness and hygiene and
decontamination procedures if required
Reporting any site injury or illness to the HSO
In the event that unsafe conditions exist or if an injury or accident occurs on site
the HSO and Project Manager should be notified as soon as possible
PERSONAL PROTECTION
Federal safety regulations call for four 4 different levels of personal protection
depending on the site conditions and relative risk or hazard to workers These levels are
1 Level D This is the lowest level of risk The personal protective equipment
required is a work uniform with safety equipment such as hardhats and
safety goggles as necessary
2 Level C This is the next level of risk where workers are potentially
exposed to toxic hazardous substances at levels which may exceed safe
occupational doses The personal protective equipment includes protective
clothing such as Tyvek coveralls and or splash suits and respiratory
protection
So Levels B and A These are higher levels of protection and are not to be
practiced by employees unless specifically instructed and prepared for
these situations In general these levels require full protective clothing and
supplied breathing air
Daily operations will be conducted at Level D i e work clothes sturdy work shoes
and hardhats It is strictly prohibited to wear loose ragged clothing and athletic shoes for
field work Working at levels A B or C are not anticipated
Employees will wear proper eye protection when engaged in activities where eye
hazards are presented including but not limited to chipping grinding welding chainsaw
operation mixing of chemicals including cement and drilling foam grouting and using
pressure washers steam cleaners
Employees will wear proper hearing protection in environments where noise
hazards are present including but not limited to pile driving operation of pneumatic
equipment and during transportation via military aircraft In general hearing protection
should be worn any time the noise levels are louder than normal conversation

HAZARD EVALUATION
Potential Physical Hazards
Field personnel should be cognizant of potential physical hazards associated with
use of heavy equipment and electrical equipment during field operations Field
personnel will wear appropriate foot head eye and hearing protection to minimize
physical hazards Field personnel should keep clear of heavy equipment which
may be operating on site
Boring and monitoring well locations should be cleared toned for underground
utilities prior to the start of drilling work
Physical exertion during hot weather conditions may result in heat exhaustion and
sunstroke Field personnel should drink fluids often

Purpose The purpose of this operating procedure is to provide guidelines for the safe
conduct of drilling operations with truck mounted and other engine powered drill rigs The
procedure addresses off road movement of drill rigs overhead and buried utilities use of
augers rotary and core drilling and other drilling operations and activities The
procedures and guidelines established herein meet the U S Army Corps of Engineers

Application These guidelines shall be applied for all Geolabs Hawaii projects in which

to all Geolabs Hawaii employees as well as employees of firms subcontracted by
Geolabs Hawaii to operate drill rigs

SAFETY GUIDELINES
Off Road Movement of Drill Rigs Before moving the rig the operator must do the
following
To the extent practical walk the planned route and inspect it for depressions gullies ruts
and other obstacles
Ensure that reverse signal alarms are functioning
Check the brakes of the truck carrier especially if the terrain along the route of travel is
rough or sloped
Discharge all passengers before moving onto steep or rough terrain
Engage the front axle on vehicles before traveling rough or steep terrain
Driving drill rigs along the sides of hills should be avoided however if side hill travel
becomes necessary the operator must conservatively evaluate the ability of the rig to
remain upright while on the hill Rigs will be equipped with seatbelts The possibility that
the presence of drilling tools on the rig may reduce the ability of the rigs to remain upright
by raising the center of gravity of the rig must be considered
Logs ditches road curbs and other long horizontal obstacles should normally be
approached and driven over squarely not at an angle
When close lateral or overhead clearance is encountered the driver of the rig should be
guided by another person on the ground
Loads on the drill rig and truck must be tied down while the truck is moving and the mast
must be fully in the lowered position

Safe Use of Augers
Never place hands or fingers under the bottom of an auger flight when hoisting the flight

ORDNANCE MONITORING
An unexploded ordnance UXO team shall locate magnetic anomaly free areas for soil
samples and borehole drilling If a preselected area indicates magnetic anomalies a new
sampling drilling site shall be chosen Boundaries of the cleared soil sampling or boring
sites shall be clearly marked by the UXO specialist The area shall be cleared squarely
with a side dimension equal to twice the length of the largest vehicle or piece of
equipment to be brought on site UXO personnel shall locate a magnetic anomaly free
site by using a magnetometer with downhole monitoring capabilities prior to drilling
equipment being transported to the proposed boring location UXO technicians shall start
the borehole with a hand auger At not more than a depth of 0 6 meters two feet the
hand auger shall be withdrawn and the magnetometer shall be lowered into the borehole
This procedure shall be used to insure that smaller items of UXO undetected from the
surface can be detected If no magnetic anomalies are found the procedure shall be
repeated at 0 6 meter two foot intervals to the maximum depth of the hand auger
GEOLABS HAWAII
Accident Prevention Plan Page F 9
generally no less than 1 8 meters six feet If the proposed boring site is still free of
magnetic anomalies the drilling equipment shall then be brought on site and utilized
Borehole monitoring with the magnetometer shall continue at 0 6 meter two foot
intervals until virgin soil is reached
Alternately the drill rig may auger down to about 0 6 meters two feet The auger would
then be extracted for magnetometer toning The magnetometer s sensitivity would dictate
the depth interval at which toning is performed however the more sensitive the
instrument the more interference would be generated by the drill rig Removing the
auger from the borehole could cause the walls of said borehole to collapse or sloughing
could occur The area would be toned for surface and shallow subsurface UXO If none
are detected the drill rig would auger slowly
