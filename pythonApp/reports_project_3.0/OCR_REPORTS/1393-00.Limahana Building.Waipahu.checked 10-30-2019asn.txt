Foundation Investigation
Proposed Limahana Building
Waipahu
In accordance with your request we have performed a soil and
foundation investigation for the above referenced project The
approximate location of the project site and its vicinity is shown on
the Project Location Map Plate 1 Our work was performed in general
accordance with our proposal of the 5th
The purpose of the investigation was to explore the subsurface
conditions at the site to provide soil data for use in the foundation
design of the proposed structures
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests which we
used in the engineering analysis to formulate our conclusions and
recommendations are presented in the appendices of this report
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed Limahana Building will be a two story steel framed
structure approximately 90 by 105 feet in plan dimensions with a 30
feet by 62 feet second level loading ramp Concrete on grade slabs are
planned for the building CMU walls are planned for the southern and
eastern exterior walls while the northern and most of the western walls
will be of glass construction An automobile parking area with asphalt
concrete pavement will be provided Minor site grading is anticipated
The general layout of the proposed construction is shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2
The anticipated foundation loadings are up to 4 6 kips per linear
foot under the exterior CMU walls and up to 120 kips per column under
the second level loading ramp columns Average column loadings in the
building will range from 70 to 90 kips per column
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located in a relatively level area on the
southern side of Farrington Highway in the town of Waipahu At the time
of our field exploration the site was occupied by a vacant single story
service station building with concrete on grade slab There were two
hydraulic lifts for servicing automobile inside the existing building

Most of the area outside the building was paved with ashpalt concrete
except a portion of the western side which is paved with concrete
There were buried fuel tanks on the western side of the site Two
drainage manholes were noted along the the highway The site was
bounded to the east by a vacant lot to the west by a second level truck
loading ramp with a CMU wall and to the south by a 4 to 5 foot high
retaining wall and a high building wall in the adjoining property
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The site is located in a detrital alluvial formation of the
Quaternary period identified as a valley flat that abuts the lower
Slope of the Koolau Volcano This older alluvium has generally
weathered to various colored soils with weathered basalt cobbles and
boulders The site soils consist mainly of stiff silts with vary ing
amounts of clays and sands These valley flats were frequently cut by
humerous streams and gullies of various sizes Man made cuts and fills
were since made in most of the general vicinity of the site for various
human activites
Soil Conditions
To explore the subsurface conditions three exploratory borings
were drilled and sampled to a depth of 16 5 feet below the existing
grades The approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site
Plan Plate 2
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1393 00 Page 4
Based on the borings drilled the subsurface soil conditions were
generally consistent under the paved areas The pavements generally
consisted of 2 inches of asphalt concrete and 4 to 6 inches of crushed
basalt rock base course Under the pavement reddish brow clays and
silts with some gray and brown mottling were encountered The clay and
silt soils were generally stiff to very stiff within the top 3 to 5
feet then graded to less stiff with depth to become soft to medium
stiff at depths ranging from 8 to 10 feet Below a depth of 10 feet
the soils again became stiff and extended to a depth of 16 5 feet the
maximum depth of our borings
Groundwater was not encountered in our borings
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Our borings indicate the site is mantled with stiff clay and silt
soils 3 to 5 feet thick over medium soils to about 10 foot depth then
underlain further by stiff soils Some amount of settlements are
anticipated due to compression of the medium stiff soils under the more
heavily loaded footings The magnitudes of foundation settlements are
anticipated to be relatively small under the recommended spread footing
foundation

The existing asphalt concrete pavement appeared to be in a fair to
good condition at the time of our exploration However the new
building construction will require most of the pavements to be removed
The fuel tanks on the western side of the site shall also be removed
Site Grading
For the proposed Limahana Building construction it is anticipated
that only a small amount of new fill will be required to grade the site
Any vegetation organic or other deleterious materials should be
removed from unpaved areas Part of the existing concrete slabs and
asphalt concrete pavement may need to be removed to accommodate the new
slab or cushion fill
Imported fill material should consist of select non expansive
granular material less than 3 inches in size with less than 20 passing
the 200 sieve The minimum CBR value should be 15 when compacted to
95 of the ASTM D 1557 78 maximum density
Fill should be placed in 6 inch thick horizontal lifts and
compacted to 90 of the maximum dry density Under the asphalt concrete
or Portland cement concrete slabs the top 6 inches of the fill below
the subgrade level should be compacted to a minimum of 953

Foundation Support
Spread footing foundations may be used for the proposed structure
provided some settlements can be tolerated The underlying moderately
compressible soils are only partially saturated and therefore the
settlements should occur quite rapidly
It is our understanding that potential settlements will be among
the major concerns for the planned exterior CMU walls design In view
of the moderate compressibility of the underlying medium stiff clays we
anticipate that some settlement should be expected
For the design wall foundation loading of 4 6 kips per linear foot
the anticipated footing settlements may range up to about one half of an
inch and differential settlements May range up to one fourth of an inch
over a distance of 10 feet The differential settlements can be
expected to be more pronounced near the end Segments of the long walls
Differential settlements will also be higher where loading vary
Significantly such as at the locations where large wall openings doors
or gates are located and the variation in soil compressibility
Therefore we recommend that the wall footings be designed as continuous
grade beams that can span over a distance of 10 feet

For the interior column loading of 120 kips per column the
anticipated spread footing settlement may Tange up to 1 inch For the
average 80 kip column loadings the anticipated settlements may be
slightly less than one half of an inch The differential settlements
may range up to one half of an inch between columns It is our
understanding that the above magnitudes of settlements will be
acceptable for the proposed construction
An allowable soil bearing value of up to 2500 p s f may be used
for footings bearing on the stiff silt A one third increase may be
allowed for soil bearing under wind or seismic loads All footings
should have a minimum dimension of 18 inches and the footing bottom
should be embedded at least 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished
grade
Any soft or weak soils encountered at the bottom of the foot ing
excavations should be removed to firm soils and backfilled with
compacted fill or lean concrete as appropriate The footing
excavations should be inspected by a soils engineer to verify that they
are located in suitable soil
Footings adjacent to any utility trench should be deepened so that
their bottom would be at or below the level of the adjacent trench
bottom

The wall footing along the southern rear boundary may need to be
located at or below the existing retaining wall footing if the retaining
wall is not constructed to accept the new building wall footings
For new retaining wall design the active earth pressures may be
calculated with an equivalent fluid weighing 35 p c f Floor surcharge
load if any may be converted to equivalent backfill heights at one
foot high for each 100 p s f of surcharge
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive
earth pressure developed against the buried vertical faces of the
foundation system and by frictional resistance developed between the
bottom of the foundations and the bearing soil
Resistance utilizing passive earth pressure may be calculated with
an equivalent fluid weighing 250 p c f This assumes that the soil
backfill around the foundations is well compacted Frictional
resistance may be calculated by using a friction factor of 0 4 The
above resistance values are maximum values and do not contain a factor
of safety

Plans and specifications for site grading and foundation
construction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the site grading spread footing
excavations subgrade preparations and cushion layer constructions for
Slabs and pavements be observed by the soils engineer during
construction to see that adequate compaction has been obtained and that
the subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of our
investigation

Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by
drilling 3 test borings at the approximate locations shown
on the Site Plan Plate 2 A truck mounted drill rig was
used to drill the borings
The soils encountered were classified by visual and
textural examination in the field by one of our engineers who
continuously monitored the drilling operations The classifi
cations were further reviewed by visual inspection and testing
in the laboratory A graphic presentation of the materials
encountered in the borings are presented in the Log of Borings
Plates A 1 through A 3 All soils were classified in accord
ance with the Unified Soil Classification System
In the borings soil samples were obtained by driving a
barrel sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches
The blow counts used to drive the sampler the last 12 inches
are shown on the boring logs at the appropriate sample depths

APPENDIX B
Moisture content determinations were performed on
selected samples as an aid in the classification and
evaluation of soil properties The results of these tests

presented on Plate B 1

natural soils The test results are presented on Plate B 2

remolded and undisturbed samples to evaluate the swelling
potential of the soils in the natural and compacted condition
The test results are summarized on Plate B 3
