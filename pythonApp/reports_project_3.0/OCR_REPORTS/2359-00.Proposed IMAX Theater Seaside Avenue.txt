Conceptually speaking the groundwater depression effect is similar

foundations Therefore we believe that the allowable vertical pile

attain additional capacity to withstand any possible downdrag that may

Based on our current estimate up to 15 tons of downdrag force may
develop if significant ground settlement occurs Therefore we believe
that the end bearing piles should be driven to a slightly higher
resistance of 110 blows per foot for the last foot of pile penetration or
9 blows per inch for the last six 6 inches of penetration to achieve an

Slab On Grade
It is also our urmderstanding that slab on grade will be bearing on
the proposed 3 foot site filling at the ground floor of the theatre
building We recommend that the following precautionary measures be
incorporated in the ground floor slab design and construction to reduce
the impact due to potential fi11 ground settlements
1 Sufficient flexible joints should be incorporated in the slabs
2 Concrete pour of the slabs should be delayed until the end of the
building construction or as much as possible
3 As much as feasible flexible floor cover material is more
preferable than rigid floor tiles such as Marble tiles are
recommended

installed at the fill area prior to site filling to monitor the rate of
ground settlement if any in order to determine more accurately the
proper time of slab placement

INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the proposed IMAX Theatre on Seaside Avenue in
Waikiki Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location of
the project site is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2359 00 Page 2
This report summarizes the findings of our field exploration and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from analyses of the data
obtained during the exploration The recommendations are intended for
design input and not intended for construction estimate or bidding
PROJECT CONSTDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct an elevated movie theatre with a seating
capacity of 436 seats and a 90 foot high roof Heavy columm loads are
anticipated for the proposed elevated structure
The ground floor of the proposed theatre will be occupied by a box
office retail stores a loading apron and also through driveways for the
existing parking structure located behind the new theatre structure It
is anticipated that asphaltic concrete will be used to pave the proposed
driveways and concrete slabs will be used for the proposed loading apron
Minimal grading is anticipated due to the existing level site
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface conditions at the project site in order to develop a
generalized soil rock data set for the formation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertinent to the design of site grading
foundations and pavements of the proposed theatre building
The work was done in general accordance with our proposal dated July
17 1989 to of Hawaii Film Venture Inc c o
Mitchell The scope of work was limited to
1 drilling and sampling three 3 borings to depths ranging fron
53 0 to 62 5 feet below the existing ground surface

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located between Waikiki Twins Theatre and Waikiki
Business Plaza on Seaside Avenue in the Waikiki district of Honolulu on
the island of Oahu Hawaii
The vacant lot is presently used as a toll parking area serving
patrons of the twins theatre and the nearby shopping plazas Except for
the parking attendant booth and several boundary planter areas the site
is generally level with asphalt concrete pavement

Ground elevations at the site average approximately 5 5 to 5 6 feet
above Mean Sea Level MSL
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
In general the borings encountered about 5 to 6 feet of medium dense
to dense sandy gravel fills and loose beach sand overlying approximately
26 to 28 feet of soft loose lagoonal deposits
The highly compressible lagoonal deposits are interbedded with a reef
detritus layer of variable density at the approximate depths of 8 to 9
feet below the existing ground In Boring No 2 the reef detritus
encountered was very dense while in Boring Nos 1 and 3 the reef detritus
encountered in the borings were generally in medium dense to dense
consistency
A localized len of medium dense reef detritus was also encountered
below the lagoonal deposits in Boring No 1
Underlying the lagoonal and detritus deposits is a thin layer of
dense to very dense silty sand with gravel or decomposed tuff encountered
at the depths of about 38 to 40 feet Below this is very dense to hard
coral formation at the depths of about 43 to 45 feet below the existing
ground
The site is mantled by a 2 inch asphaltic concrete pavement
Groundwater was also encountered in the borings at the depths of
approximately 11 to 13 feet below the existing ground surface during our
field exploration

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
It is our understanding that the overlying subsoils are highly
compressible under the anticipated high columm loads and therefore are
not suitable to support shallow spread footing foundation The hard coral
formation encountered at about 43 to 45 foot depths in the project site
is suitable for use as an end bearing layer to support the proposed pile
foundation
Therefore we recommend that pile foundations bearing on the
underlying hard coral formation be used to support the proposed IMAX
theatre
We also recommend that predrilling be used prior to pile driving to
penetrate a localized very dense area in the upper reef detritus layer and
also to reduce ground vibration caused by the pile driving
Minimal site grading is recommended for the proposed building and
on grade driveways to reduce the potential of causing significant ground
settlements and downdrag forces on the pile foundations
End Bearing Pile Foundation
Since a suitable bearing layer is present below the highly
compressible upper subsoils we recommend that end bearing pile
foundations be used to support the anticipated heavy column loads of the
proposed 90 foot high elevated structure The piles would develop their
vertical load capacity primarily from end bearing on the underlying hard
coral formation if they can penetrate through the 5 foot thick decomposed
tuff layer or if they can be bearing into the tuff formation We shall
verify the drivability through the tuff formation via the initial test
pile program

The end bearing piles should be driven to the hard coral formation
encountered at the approximate depths of about 43 to 45 feet below the

Provided no major site filling less than 1 foot high will be
placed an allowable vertical pile capacity of 100 tons per pile may be

one third for seismic and or wind load considerations
Pile settlements on the order of less than one half of an inch are

To resist lateral loads acting on the proposed structure the passive
earth pressure around the pile caps and tie beams may be used We
recommend that an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 p s f per foot of
depth of the caps beams be used to calculate the passive earth pressure
The backfill against the pile caps or tie beams should be properly
backfilled with select materials compacted to a density of at least 95 of
its maximum dry density as determined by the ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Pile Driving
The end bearing piles should be driven with a hammer capable of
delivering a minimm of about 24 000 foot pounds of energy and to a
resistance of 85 blows per foot for the last foot of pile penetration
or 7 blows per inch for the last six 6 inches of penetration

Each pile should be driven continuously without interruption A pile
may be rejected or its design capacity be reduced when the driving
process is interrupted for more than 4 hours
During soft driving when the pile tip is still within soft soil
the hammer stroke or energy should be carefully controlled so that
excessive tensile stress is not developed in the pile
The pile driving should be monitored by a qualified geotechnical
engineer to see that the piles are driven to the competent bearing
formation
Test Pile Program
A test pile program is recommended in order to achieve a safe and
economical pile foundation for the proposed theatre
The test pile program should be undertaken to fulfill the following
objectives
1 To determine more accurately the production pile lengths
2 To help evaluate and modify if necessary the pile driving
criteria
We recommend that about 10 percent of the total piles be test driven
at the site These test piles may later be incorporated into the
actual foundations of the proposed theatre
Splice cans should be provided at the site during the test pile
driving to expedite splicing if required Splice cans may be
fabricated with one fourth of an inch thick high grade steel and
should be at least 6 feet long

Predrilling
We also recommend that predrilling be used prior to pile driving to
penetrate localized very dense area in the upper reef detritus layer at
about 8 to 9 feet below the existing ground Generally speaking
predrilling may be needed for pile locations near Boring No 2 in which
the detritus deposits encountered had a very high density
However if ground vibration caused by the pile driving needs to be
reduced we believe that predrilling may be employed throughout the entire
project site We recommend that predrilling to a maximum depth of 15 feet
be used for piles in the vicinity of Boring No 2 and predrilling through
the fill crust to a minimm depth of 5 feet be considered for the other
areas of the project site
Site Grading
It is our understanding that the project grading plans are still in
the conceptual stage However minimal site grading is anticipated for
the proposed construction due to the existing flat grades at the project
site
It is our opinion that if site filling is needed it should be
minimized to less than 1 foot thick to reduce the potential of causing
significant ground settlements by compression of the underlying soft loose
lagoonal deposits under the additional fill weight
At the onset of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed Any unsuitable
materials such as debris rubbish vegetation old pavements and other
deleterious materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from borings Variations of conditions
between the borings may occur and the nature and extent of these
variations may not become evident until construction If variations then
appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations
given in this report
The locations of the borings were determined by taping from the
property boundaries and existing structures as indicated on the
topographic survey dated by Inc
Elevations of the borings were determined by interpolation between
elevation points on the plan The physical locations and elevations of
the borings should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by
the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
borings depict the approximate boundaries between soil types and as such
may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on any profiles
or cross section are based upon interpolation between borings and may not
represent actual subsurface conditions
