INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
Keaau Elementary and Intermediate School campus on the island of Hawaii The general
location of the project site is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings resulting from our exploration and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analysis based on the data
obtained from the field exploration and laboratory testing These recommendations are
intended for design input and are not intended for construction estimation or bidding
purposes

of volcanic ash layer overlying a medium dense to loose clinker deposit approximately 2 5

settlements and failure due to collapse of possible subsurface voids or cavities

Foundation Probing
The basaltic rock formation encountered at the site has a tendency to contain
tubes voids and cavities The field exploration encountered a void or cavity in the basalt
rock formation in Boring No 1
In order to reduce the potential loss of foundation support resulting from collapse
of void s below a footing foundation we recommend that a program of cavity probing
be implemented
Probes should be drilled at each individual footing location and ten 10 foot on
centers along the continuous footing location where the thickness of the underlying
compacted fill layer is less than two 2 times the footing width The probes should be
a minimum of three 3 inches in diameter and should be drilled to a minimum drilled
depth equal to two 2 times the footing width below the footing bottom
To backfill the probes and to aid in the detection of cavities the probes should
then be filled with neat cement grout injected at low to moderate pump pressures from
a grout pump If the volume of grout injected exceeds four 4 times the calculated
volume of the probe hole the grout mix should be adjusted to a one 1 part sand to one
1 part cement 1 1 mixture
The probing and grouting program should be conducted under the observation of
the project geotechnical engineer to monitor the presence of voids and to allow additional
recommendations to be made if changed ground conditions occur and or if large voids
are encountered

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling six 6 borings to
depths of 5 0 to 21 0 feet below the existing ground surface at the approximate locations
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were drilled by a truck mounted rotary drill
rig advancing continuous flight augers or rotary and core drilling tools as appropriate
The materials encountered in the borings were examined and classified in general
accordance with ASTM Standard Practice D 2488 84 Description and Identification of
Soils Visual Manual Procedure by a geologist from our firm The logs of the borings
are shown on Plates A 1 through A 6
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Standard Method D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils
sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the boring logs at the appropriate sample
depths
Core samples of soil and rock materials encountered at the site were obtained
using diamond core drilling techniques in general accordance with ASTM Standard
Practice D 2113 83 Diamond Core Drilling for Site Investigation Core drilling is a rotary
drilling method which uses a hollow bit to cut into the rock formation The rock material
left in the hollow stem of the bit is mechanically recovered for examination classification
and testing
Recovery REC is used as a subjective guide to interpretation of the relative quality
of rock masses Recovery is defined as the actual length of material recovered from a
coring attempt vs the length of the core attempt e g if 3 7 feet of material is recovered
from a 5 0 foot core run the recovery would be 74 percent and would be shown on the
Boring Logs as REC 74
The Rock Quality Designation RQD is also a subjective guide to the relative
quality of rock masses This designation is only applicable to cores retrieved from in situ
rock formations RQD is defined as the percentage of the core run that is sound material
in excess of four 4 inches in length without any discontinuities discounting any drilling
induced fractures or breaks if 2 5 feet of sound material is recovered from a 5 0 foot core
run the RQD would be 50 percent and would be shown on the Boring Logs as RQD
50 Generally the following is used to describe the relative quality based on the
Practical Handbook of Physical Properties of Rocks and Minerals

APPENDIX C
Laboratory Testing
Laboratory testing was performed on selected samples obtained during our field
exploration A laboratory testing program was developed to identify appropriate tests to
aid in determining pertinent engineering properties of the soil and or rock materials
sampled
Classification Tests
Frequently the classification of a material provides a correlative index to certain
engineering properties of the material
Moisture Content ASTM Test Method D 2216 80 and Unit Weight Determinations
are used as an aid to soil classification and to assist in determination of certain
engineering properties The moisture content expressed as a percentage of the dry
weight of the tested sample is shown at the appropriate sample depths on the Boring
Logs
