INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Rich Residence located within Kohala Ranch in the District of
South Kohala on the Island of Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site
are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings from our field exploration and laboratory
testing and presents our geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our
analyses for the proposed project These recommendations are intended for the design
of house foundations site grading and pavements only The findings and
recommendations presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this
report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed project site is located at Lot No 297 of the Kohala Ranch
development in South Kohala on the Island of Hawaii We understand that the subject lot
encompasses approximately 3 acres in area Based on the information provided it is
proposed to construct a new one story residence at the proposed site centering around
an existing tree We understand that the residence will be constructed with reinforced
concrete walls and steel framed roof In addition a two car garage and concrete
driveway areas are also planned Details regarding structural loads were not available at
the time this report was prepared However we understand that the reinforced concrete
walls will have a height of up to about 19 feet Therefore moderate wall loads may be
anticipated for this residence
Based on the information provided we anticipate that site grading consisting of
minor cuts and fills on the order of about 3 feet or less would be needed to achieve the
design grades for the proposed project We understand that a swimming pool is not
planned for this project at this time However if a swimming pool is desired in the

construction of a swimming pool
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our geotechnical engineering exploration was to obtain an
overview of the subsurface conditions at the site to develop a soil rock data set for the
formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to the proposed Rich
residence To accomplish this we conducted an exploration program consisting of the
following tasks and work efforts
1 Mobilization and demobilization of a truck mounted drill rig on the island of
Hawaii and two operators from Honolulu and back
2 Drilling and sampling of three borings to depths of about 15 to 16 feet below
the existing ground surface for one day of exploration
3 Coordination of the field exploration and logging of the borings by a
Geologist from our firm
4 Laboratory testing of selected samples obtained during the field exploration
as an aid in classifying the materials encountered and evaluating their
engineering properties
5 Analyses of the field and laboratory data for the formulation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertinent to the proposed project
6 Preparation of this formal report summarizing our work on the project and
presenting our findings and recommendations
7 Coordination of our overall work on the project by a Project Engineer from
our firm
8 Quality assurance of our work and client design team consultation by a
Principal Engineer of our firm
9 Miscellaneous work efforts such as drafting word processing and clerical
support

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Hawaii the largest in the Hawaiian Archipelago covers an area of
approximately 4 000 square miles The island was formed by the activity of five shield
volcanoes These are Kohala which is long extinct Mauna Kea which has had activity
during recent geologic times Hualalai which last erupted in 1801 and Mauna Loa and
Kilauea which are both still active
The project site is underlain by basaltic lava flows of the Hawi and Pulolu Volcanic
Series of Kohala which were deposited during the middle to late Pleistocene Epoch
Stearns and Macdonald 1946 These series are typically composed of basaltic and
andesitic lava flows which are mantled by a relatively thin layer of palagonitized ashy soil
similar to the ash soils locally referred to as Pahala Ash The ashy soils at the project site
differs from Pahala Ash found in other portions of the island of Hawaii in that the in situ
moisture content is generally lower and the soil exists in a dry friable state The soil
encountered at the project site may also be a product of in situ weathering of the lavas
rather than an air laid ash
The soils at the site have been subjected to wind erosion which has resulted in
the formation of a desert pavement on the surface of the site This desert pavement is a
surface layer of cobbles and gravel which were left behind as the finer ashy soils were
blown away
In general the majority of the lava flows underlying the ashy soils at the project
site are in a a form which is characterized by a rough spiny or rubbly surface of
fragmental clinkers Typically these a a flows are underlain by a dense core of massive
basalt which is frequently interbedded with thinner less continuous clinker layers

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located at Lot No 297 of the Kohala Ranch development in
South Kohala on the Island of Hawaii The subject property is located at the western end
of the Palila Place cul de sac and is bounded by Kohala Ranch Road to the west The lot
is triangular in shape and encompasses approximately 3 acres of land Our field
observation indicated that the site is presently vacant with a thin cover of vegetation The
vegetation primarily consisted of wild grasses growing over the dry ashy soils with some
scattered trees
Based on the site reconnaissance and topographic map provided the terrain at the
southern portion of the site generally slopes toward the southwest makai side at an
inclination of about three horizontal to one vertical 3H 1V The elevation in this area
ranges from about 700 to 760 feet Mean Sea Level MSL The northern portion of the
project site near the proposed residence is nearly level with ground surface elevations of
about 736 to 740 feet MSL

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field exploration the subsurface conditions at the project site
generally consist of ash deposits intermixed with varying amounts of medium dense
basalt gravels and cobbles extending from the surface to the maximum depth explored of
approximately 15 8 feet below the existing ground surface The ash materials generally
consist of dry sandy silt soils It should be noted that pockets of loose and or soft ash
soils of various thicknesses without significant amount of gravels and cobbles may be
present in localized areas throughout the project site
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered at the project site we believe
that a shallow foundation system consisting of spread and or continuous footings may be
used to support the proposed new residence An allowable bearing pressure of up to
3 000 pounds per square foot p s f may be used for the design of footings bearing on
structural fill or the recompacted basalt gravels and cobbles in an ashy soil matrix
encountered at or near the ground surface Pockets of ash soils without significant
amount of basalt gravels and cobbles where encountered within the limits of
improvements should be over excavated to expose dense materials and replaced with
structural fill The limits of improvements include the building footprint and driveway
areas The over excavation should remove the ash materials and expose the underlying
dense materials

House Foundations
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered at the project site we
recommend that shallow spread and or continuous footings be used to support the
proposed new residence An allowable bearing pressure of up to 3 000 pounds per
square foot p s f may be used for the design of footings bearing on structural fill or the
recompacted basalt gravels and cobbles in an ashy soil matrix encountered at shallow
depths This bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third
for transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic forces
In general footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest
adjacent finish grade For footings constructed near the tops of slopes or on slopes the
footings should be embedded deep enough such that the horizontal distance measured
from the outside edge at the base of the footing to the slope face is not less than 8 feet
Continuous foundations oriented parallel to the direction of the slope should be
constructed in stepped footings
Where foundations are located next to utility trenches or easements they should
be embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane extending
upward from the bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility
lines This requirement is necessary to avoid surcharging adjacent below grade
structures with additional structural loads and to reduce the potential for foundation
settlement
Our field exploration indicated that the subsurface soils are generally dry and
friable Therefore bottom of the footing excavations should be moisture conditioned to
above optimum moisture and recompacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction prior to the placement of reinforcing steel or concrete Relative compaction
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3943 00 Page 8
refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry
density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 91 test
procedures Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight
corresponding to the maximum dry density
As previously indicated pockets of ash soils of various thickness without
significant amounts of basalt gravels and cobbles may be present in localized areas at
various depths throughout the project site These soft and or loose ash pockets where
encountered should be over excavated and replaced with structural fill The
over excavation may be limited to areas within the project improvements such as limits of
building footprint driveway areas and utility trench locations The over excavation should
be extended until underlying dense materials are exposed The over excavation should
be backfilled with structural fill moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction or the bottom of
footing may be extended down to the underlying competent material
If the foundations are designed in accordance with our recommendations we
estimate total settlements to be less than 1 inch with differential settlements on the order
of inch
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by frictional resistance
developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil and by passive
earth pressure acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system A
coefficient of friction of 0 35 may be used for footings bearing on structural fill or
compacted on site soils Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be estimated
using an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth p c f
This assumes that the soil around footings is well compacted Unless covered by
pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the upper 12 inches of soil should be
neglected

Slabs On Grade
Concrete slabs on grade are anticipated for the proposed residence For the
interior house slabs which are not subjected to vehicular traffic we recommend that a
minimum 4 inch thick layer of cushion fill consisting of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33
No 67 gradation be placed below the slabs to provide more uniform support of the slabs
and to serve as a capillary break To reduce future moisture infiltration and subsequent
damage to floor coverings an impervious moisture vapor barrier such as a plastic
membrane is recommended on top of the gravel cushion layer Placement of 2 inches of
moist sand above the plastic membrane may be considered to provide protection of the
membrane and to aid in curing of the slab concrete
Exterior concrete walkways may be required for the proposed project In general
we recommend that a minimum 4 inch thick cushion layer of gravel be provided below the
concrete walkway slab Contraction joints should be provided at intervals equal to the
width of the walkways with expansion joints at right angle intersections
Garage Slab and Concrete Driveway
For slabs on grade that will be subjected to vehicular traffic such as the garage
slab and the driveway we recommend that a 6 inch layer of select borrow subbase be
provided below the slab in lieu of the cushion fill layer for more uniform support The
vapor barrier and the 2 inch moist sand layer may also be omitted for these slabs The
select borrow subbase should consist of crushed basalt aggregate compacted to a
minimum of 95 percent relative compaction We recommend that the concrete slab be
designed for the intended loads For design of structural slabs a modulus of subgrade
reaction of 250 pounds per cubic inch p c i may be used for the compacted select
borrow subbase Provisions should be made for proper load transfer across the slab joints

Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork all areas within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris rubbish and other
unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site Ash
soils generally have relatively low strength and if the in situ moisture contents
are high enough they exhibit thixotropic properties i e they lose significant
strength when remolded
Based on our field exploration it is likely that ash pockets of various thickness
may be present in localized areas throughout the project site Therefore we
recommend that the pockets of ash soils without significant amounts of basalt
gravels and cobbles where encountered be removed below finish subgrades of
structural and pavement areas The ash pockets should be over excavated to
GEOLABS HAWAII

expose underlying dense materials and be replaced with structural fill materials
It is recommended that a representative from Geolabs Hawaii be present to
observe the finished subgrades prior to placement of fills
After clearing and grubbing the areas within the proposed building and pavement
limits should be excavated where necessary to the design finish subgrades The
excavated materials except for the ash materials without substantial amounts of
gravels and cobbles may be reused as a source of fill materials provided that the
materials meet the gradation requirements presented in the Fills and Backfills
subsection of this report Loose and or soft spots encountered at the excavated
subgrade levels should be removed and replaced with structural fill materials
compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
Fills and Backfills
Fill and backfill materials to be used as general and structural fill up to the slab
foundation or pavement subgrade level should consist of well graded granular
materials less than 6 inches in largest dimension The on site cut materials may
be used as fill or backfill materials provided they are processed and reduced to a
relatively well graded select granular material with a maximum size of about
6 inches Any imported material should be well graded from coarse to fine with no
particles greater than 6 inches in largest dimension Any imported material should
be observed and or tested by Geolabs Hawaii for its suitability prior to being
transported to the site
Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
Fills and backfills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 12 inches in loose
thickness moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Relative compaction
refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3943 00 Page 12
maximum dry density as determined by ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91
Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by weight corresponding to
the maximum dry density
Conventional compaction testing is generally not practicable in fills composed of
rock boulders and or cobbles Instead a testing program to evaluate the number
of passes of a compactor needed to achieve the desired level of compaction
should be conducted at the start of the grading phase of the project under the
observation of a representative from Geolabs Hawaii On the basis of this testing
program the number of passes may then be used as the field criterion for
adequate compaction

Cut and Fill Slopes
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered in the borings it appears that
most of the cut slopes would expose the basalt gravels and cobbles in ashy soil
matrix In general we believe that cut slopes may be designed with a slope
inclination of 2H 1V or flatter
Permanent fill slopes should be designed with a slope inclination of 2H 1V or
flatter All fills to be placed on slopes with inclinations steeper than 5H 1V should
be keyed and benched into the existing slope to provide stability of the new fill
against sliding The filling operations should start at the lowest point and continue
up in level horizontal compacted layers in accordance with the above fill placement
recommendations Fill slopes should be constructed by overfilling and cutting back
to the design slope ratio to obtain a well compacted slope face Water should be
diverted away from the tops of slopes and slope planting should be provided as
soon as possible to reduce the potential for erosion of the finished slopes
Utility Lines
We envision that new utility lines i e water and sewer lines will be required for
development of this residence It is anticipated that most of the trenches for utilities will
be excavated in the surficial basalt gravels and cobbles in an ashy soil matrix In general
a granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67
gradation is recommended below the pipes Free draining granular materials such as
No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation should be used for the trench backfill
up to about 12 inches above the pipes to provide adequate support around the pipes and
to reduce compaction of the backfill thus reducing the possibility of damaging the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill should consist of granular material
generally less than 6 inches in maximum size as recommended in the Fills and Backfills
subsection compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction Where trenches will be
located in paved areas the upper 3 feet of the trench backfill below the pavement grade

line crosses beneath the footing within 2 feet of the bottom of footing elevation the utility

Drainage
The finished grades outside the residential structure should be sloped to shed
water away from foundations and slabs and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also
advised that gutter systems be installed around the buildings and that the discharge be
diverted away from the foundation and slab areas Excessive landscape watering near
the foundations and slabs should also be avoided Planters next to foundations should
be avoided or have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the
subsoils
The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled against the walls or slab
edges immediately after setting of the concrete to reduce water infiltration In addition
drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and should be maintained to
drain surface run off away from the foundations and slabs

reviewed and interpretations made in the formulation of this report However it must be
noted that fluctuation may occur due to variation in rainfall temperature and other factors

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling three
borings to depths of approximately 15 to 15 8 feet below the existing ground surface The
approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings
were drilled using truck mounted drilling equipment
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our Geologist who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representation of the materials
encountered are presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 3
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Spilt Barrel Sampling of Soils
California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts needed
to drive the sampler the last 12 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown on the logs of
borings at the appropriate sample depths

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content ASTM D 2216 and unit weight determinations were performed
on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties
The results of these tests are presented on the logs of borings at the appropriate sample
depths
One sieve analysis ASTM C 136 was performed on a selected sample of the
soils to evaluate the gradation characteristics of the soils and to aid in soil classification
Graphic representation of the grain size distribution is presented on Plate B 1
