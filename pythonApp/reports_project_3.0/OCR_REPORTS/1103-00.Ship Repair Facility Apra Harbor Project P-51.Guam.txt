As requested we have performed a soil investigation at the
above referenced site to evaluate the subsurface soil condi
tions in order to provide recommendations for the design and
construction of the foundations of the proposed structure
Our investigation consisted of five 5 borings drilled in the
proposed location of the building two 2 borings drilled in
the proposed marine railway alignment and visual observations
of the site
The results of our field exploration and laboratory testing
which were used in the engineering analysis to formulate our
conclusions and recommendations are presented in the appendix
of this report
W O 1103 00 Page 2
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project building site is located four hundred 400 feet
west of the edge of L Wharf between the Krause and Hartiu
Buildings The marine railway alignment is from the edge of
L Wharf west four hundred 400 feet between existing
Krause and Hartiu Buildings in Apra Harbor
The entire project site building and marine railway is in an
asphaltic concrete paved wharf area
The asphaltic concrete pavement is flat shows no signs of
cracking or heaving and is two and a half inches thick
There are repair buildings woodshop motor shop foundry and
other buildings around the site
PROJECT CONSIDERATION
The proposed building will be a one story concrete structure
approximately eight five 85 by fifty five 55 feet in plan
dimension The individual columns will Support a span of 55
feet and will be spaced at 20 feet on center A perimeter
masonry wall with interior precast panel walls is planned
Maximum column loads of 110 kips are anticipated

A rail track approximately 400 feet long is planned between
the existing pier and the proposed building for transport of
boats to the repair facility
The new building will be situated in the area of the present
materials storage yard It is anticipated that the existing
asphaltic concrete will be removed in the proposed building
site and marine railway alignment

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration encountered relatively uniform soil con
dition consisting of about 10 feet of coral sand and gravel
fill grading from dense near the surface to medium density with
depth over the natural coral sand and gravel formation The
GEOLABS HAWAIl
W O 1103 00 Page 5
upper 3 to 4 feet of the natural coral material below the
fill is generally loose to medium dense and grades to
medium dense with depth
Foundations
For the relatively light building loads anticipated shallow
continuous footing foundation bearing on the upper coral sand
fill layer can be used An allowable bearing value of 3 000
p s f can be utilized in the dense sand fill This value
bearing capacity is for total dead plus live loads and may be
increased by one third when temporary lateral loads wind or
seismic are considered A minimum footing width of 24 inches
is recommended
Footings should have a minimum embedment of 24 inches below the
finished grade It is recommended that the footings be placed
as high as practicable to reduce the additional footing load
imposed in the relatively loose layer encountered at about 10
to 14 foot depth
Prior to concrete placement the bottom of the footing excava
tion should be compacted with a vibratory roller to a minimum
of 95 of its maximum dry density established by ASTM D 1557

Estimated settlements are on the order of about one half inch
total and one quarter inch differential
Surface water should be diverted away from building foundations
Lateral Resistance
Lateral loads acting on the structure can be resisted by pas
sive earth pressure developed against the buried vertical faces
of the foundation system and by frictional resistance developed
between the bottom of the foundations and the bearing soil
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be calculated
using an equivalent fluid pressure of 350 p c f This assumes
that the granular soil around the foundations is well compacted
The frictional resistance may be calculated using a coefficient
of friction of 0 4
The above resistance values are maximum values and do not con
tain a factor of safety

Site Grading
Minimal site grading is anticipated
Well graded granular material such as the on site coral sand
should be used for backfilling around the foundations and
utility trenches
Backfill should be placed in 9 inch loose lifts compacted to a
minimum of 95 of its maximum dry density established by ASTM
D 1557
Rail Track
Generally competent soil conditions were encountered below the
proposed rail track alignment The subgrade soils can provide
adequate support for the relatively light boat loads antici
pated
Slab On Grade
Concrete slab on grade is planned for the proposed structure
Industrial loading including forklift traffic is anticipated
over the slab
A 6 inch thick concrete slab over a compacted sand and coral
subgrade is recommended

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by
drilling 7 borings to depths of 15 to 50 feet below the exis
ting ground surface at the approximate locations shown on the
Boring Location Plans Plates 1 and 2 Truck mounted auger
drilling equipment was used to drill all of the borings
The soil encountered in the borings were classified by
visual and textural examination in the field by our engineer
who continuously monitored the drilling operations The clas
sifications were reviewed by visual inspection and testing in
the laboratory The soils were classified in general confor
mance with the Unified Soil Classification System A graphic
presentation of the soils is presented on the Boring Logs
Plates A 1 through A 8
Soil samples were obtained by driving a standard penetra
tion sampler or a 2 4 inch diameter ring sampler with a 140
pound hammer free falling a distance of 30 inches The blow
counts to drive the sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the
logs of borings at the appropriate sample depth

Laboratory Testing
Moisture content and unit weight determinations were per
formed on selected samples as an aid in the classification and
evaluation of soil properties The results of these tests are
presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 8
Four sieve analysis tests were performed on samples of
the underlying sandy soils at various depths to evaluate the
grain size distribution of the soils The test results are
summarized on Plate B l
One CBR test was performed on a composite sample of the
upper fill material to evaluate its suitability for pavement
Support The test result is presented on Plate B l
One moisture density test modified proctor was per
formed on a composite sample of the upper fill material to
determine its maximum dry density The test result is pre
sented on Plate B l
Two direct shear tests were performed on undisturbed
samples of the upper sand fill to evaluate its strength para
meters The test results are presented on Plates B 2 and B 3
