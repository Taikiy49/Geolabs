INTRODUCTION
In accordance with the request of the Pono Kai Association of Apartment Owners we
have drilled installed and tested two 2 new injection wells at the subject site
The approximate location of the project site and its vicinity are shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1 Our work was performed in general accordance with our revised
proposal to the Resort dated March 30 1993
This report summarizes our findings from the drilling installation and testing of the new
injection wells at the project site
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
Pono Kai Resort is located in the Kapaa area on the eastern shore of the island of
Kauai Hawaii The site is in an unsewered area and utilizes on site treatment to handle
domestic sanitary wastes generated in the residential complex The wastewater system uses
injection wells for the disposal of the treated effluent
The existing treatment plant is a package aeration unit which has been in operation
for several years The existing Well C has lost most of its injection capacity because of
mechanical failure such as collapse of the well casing Therefore two 2 new wells have
been installed at the site for additional backup capacity
GEOLABS HAWAII

Department of Health Administrative Rules Chapter 11 23 require that an approval be
obtained prior to constructing new injection wells and that a permit be obtained to operate
the wells These are accomplished by submittal to the Department of Health of an
Underground Injection Control UIC permit application and an engineering report
documenting the construction and testing of the wells
An application was submitted to the State of Hawaii Department of Health for approval
to construct the two new injection wells A preliminary approval letter for construction was
received under UIC permit No UK 1306 from the Department of Health before the injection
well construction

GENERAL INFORMATION
The Pono Kai Resort is located on the southern side of the town of Kapaa on the
island of Kauai Hawaii The wells are situated on the parcel identified by Tax Map Key No
07 02 easterly of Kuhio Highway in a driveway running along the northern side
of the project The general topography of the site and the location of the wells are shown
on the Site Plan Plate 2
Available records show that at one point in time there was an industrial supply well
in the general vicinity of the site This well was drilled in 1928 for Hawaiian Canneries Co
a former occupant of the site The former cannery was demolished for the development of
the Pono Kai Resort It is assumed that this old well is now either abandoned or lost
There are no known active drinking water supply wells within one quarter mile of the
site Drinking water in this portion of the island of Kauai is obtained from wells tapping the
basal aquifers in Waimea Basalts and to a lesser extent aquifers in Koloa Basalts Neither
of these formations were encountered during the drilling of the injection wells

PHYSICAL CHARACTERISTICS
The site is located in the Kapaa area in the Kawaihau District on the eastern shore of
the island of Kauai Hawaii The climate of the Kapaa area is mild with an average annual
temperature of about 74 F and an average annual rainfall of about 50 inches per year
The site is situated on a coastal plain adjacent to the northeasterly portion of the Linue
Depression and east of the northern end of Nonou The topography of this coastal plain is
a very gentle slope down towards the east from the mountains to the ocean
Regional Geology
The island of Kauai is composed of a single basalt shield volcano built by the extrusion
of the lavas of the Waimea Canyon Volcanic Series during the late Pliocene more than 2 3
million years before present Following the cessation of this main shield building phase
there was renewed volcanic activity with the extrusion of the basaltic lavas of the
post erosional Koloa Volcanic Series and the concurrent deposition of the alluvial sediments
of the Palikea Formation
Rocks of the Koloa Volcanic Series cover most of the eastern half of the island of
Kauai These rocks are generally characterized as thick flows of dense basalt extruded from
groups of vents aligned in north south trends in various locales Associated with the vents
are pyroclastic material e g usually forming low cinder cones at the vent
During the Pleistocene there were many sea level changes as a result of widespread
glaciation in the continental areas of the world As the great continental glaciers
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3094 00 Page 6
accumulated the level of the ocean fell since there was less water available to fill the oceanic
basins Conversely as the glaciers receded or melted global sea levels rose because more
water was available The land mass of Kauai remained essentially stable during these
changes and the fluctuations were eustatic in nature These glacio eustatic fluctuations
resulted in stands of the sea which were both higher and lower relative to present sea level
on Kauai
The higher sea level stands caused the accumulation of deltas and fans of terrigenous
sediments in the heads of the old bays accumulation of reef deposits at corresponding
higher elevations and lagoonal marine sediments in the quiet waters protected by fringing
reefs
The subject site appears to be in an old amphitheater headed valley eroded into the
Waimea volcanics which was subsequently filled by Koloa rocks and Palikea Sediments The
geologic map by Macdonald Davis and Cox 1960 indicates that the site is underlain by
recent beach sands and noncalcareous sediments The log of the old industrial supply well
indicates that the beach sands extended to about 50 feet below the ground surface and then
the well penetrated approximately 100 feet of sediment before encountering basal basalt rock
formation
During historic time the project site was used for industrial purposes i e a cannery
This use most likely resulted in some minor modification of the terrain such as filling and
grading of the site
Subsurface and Foundation Conditions
Our field exploration consisted of drilling and sampling two borings to a depth of about
60 5 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate locations of the borings are
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2

In general the drilled borings encountered a thin surface layer consisting of clayey silt
with gravel overlying hard beach sand and loose sandy gravel materials to a depth of about
50 feet below the existing ground surface where alluvial sediments consisting of clayey silt
with sand and gravel were encountered to the maximum depth drilled Detailed descriptions
of the subsurface conditions encountered are presented on the Well Logs Plates A 1 1
through A 2 2
Groundwater was encountered in both wells at about 9 5 feet below the existing
ground surface at the time the wells were drilled
Since the injection wells are sited in an existing driveway and the foundation aspects
of the existing buildings were addressed in separate geotechnical engineering efforts
foundation considerations are not considered to be of significance for the wells themselves
Earthquake Conditions
The island of Kauai is designated in the 1985 Uniform Building Code as Seismic Zone
One Since no new structures will be directly associated with the wells seismic conditions
are not considered to be significant
Flood Problems
The existing site was engineered and graded for drainage There is an existing storm
drain system at the site and a catch basin inlet for this system Therefore inundation is not
considered to be of significance to the operation of the wells
Land Use
The site is an area which is designated for residential and resort use The site is
currently used for these purposes and the proposed wells will provide backup capacity for

the existing waste water disposal system Therefore construction of the injection wells at the
site is consistent with this use
SYSTEM OPERATION
It is proposed to use the new wells to provide backup capacity for the disposal of
treated domestic sewage Prior to disposal the sewage will be treated at the existing
wastewater treatment plant The plant is a package plant which is generally of extended
aeration design The plant is intended to provide secondary level treatment
The plant capacity is 68 000 gallons per day with an average flow on the order of 30
gallons per minute and a design peak flow of 180 gallons per minute
The complete injection system currently consists of four 4 wells A C D and E
arranged in an inline series of sump boxes The majority of injection is under gravity
pressure i e the head pressure within the individual sumps Existing Well C appears to
have lost most of its capacity because of the mechanical failure such as casing collapse
The newly constructed Wells F and G will provide backup capacity for the existing injection
system The sketch details of the system are shown on Plate 2
GEOHYDROLOGIC CONSIDERATIONS
The site is located at Latitude N and Longitude W The
ground surface elevation at the site at the time of well drilling and testing was approximately
91 feet above Mean Sea Level
The logs of the drilled injection wells are presented in Appendix A Generally the wells
encountered coralline sands and gravels overlying clayey silt alluvial sediments
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3094 00 Page 9
Water levels in the drilled wells were noted to fluctuate slightly in response to the tides
in the nearby ocean Tidal fluctuations and efficiency are estimated to be on the order of
about 25 percent
The shallow groundwater in the area is brackish water in a sedimentary caprock
aquifer Instrument readings taken in the new wells indicated that the conductivity of the
groundwater ranges from about 188 to about per centimeter The
caprock aquifer which serves as the injection aquifer is isolated from the underlying artesian
basalt aquifer by clayey soils and sediments with low permeability

When the new wells had been drilled and cased to their intended depths the wells
were developed by airlifting and tested by the injection of clear fresh water These tests
indicated that the injection formation has a coefficient of permeability K in the range of about
36 to 66 feet per day which corresponds to a capacity of about 80 to 120 gallons per
minute
The subsurface permeability conditions at the site were tested by the injection of clear
fresh water from a nearby fire hydrant at variable rates for a period of approximately 12 hours
per well

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and findings submitted in this report are based in part upon information
obtained from field data points such as drilled wells and pump tests Variations of conditions
between the field data points may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may
not become evident until operation of the injection wells If variations then appear evident
it will be necessary to re evaluate the findings presented in this report
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the field data points depict
the approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual
transition
Water level data from the field data points was collected at the times given on the
graphic representations and or in the text and appendices of this report This data has been
reviewed and interpretations made in the formulation of this report However it must be
noted that fluctuation may occur due to variations in rainfall tides temperature and other
factors
