PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from two shield
volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be
middle to late Pliocene in age and forms the bulk of the western third of the island The
younger shield volcano Koolau is estimated to be late Pliocene to early Pleistocene
Ice Age and forms the majority of the eastern two thirds of the island Waianae
became extinct while Koolau was still active and its eastern flank was partially buried
below Koolau lavas banking against its eastern flank
The project area is located on the southeastern flank of Koolau and is composed
of basaltic rock built by the extrusion of lavas of the Koolau Volcanic Series These
rocks are generally characterized by flows of jointed dense vesicular basalt with
interbedded thin clinker layers For the last 1 to 2 million years in situ chemical
weathering of the Koolau lava flows has occurred forming a mantle of residual soils
which grade to saprolite with depth In general saprolite is composed mainly of silty
material and is typical of the tropical weathering of volcanic rocks The saprolite grades
to basaltic rock formation with depth Colluvial deposits consisting of slope wash
materials transported down the steeper slopes to the more gradual slopes at the base of
the ridges have accumulated at the site Grading operations for the housing
developments in the area have brought the site to its present form

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
We understand that the sewer line is located in an area of on going slide
movements It is not yet possible to accurately define the boundaries and the rate of the
slide movement It should be noted that the earth movement may be composed of either
a single large mass of shifting soil or a series of smaller shifting soil masses moving at
varying rates with respect to each other Based on our observations the head scarp
upper portion of the landslide may be limited to the makai portion of Mona Street as
evident by the longitudinal cracks along Mona Street To reduce the potential for future
damage to the new sewer line we recommend that the new sewer line alignment along
Mona Street be placed away from the head scarp of the landslide along the mauka side
of Mona Street if possible
If this is not possible we recommend that remedial work to reduce the potential
for slide movements be implemented before construction of the sewer line commences
Detailed information regarding the rate of movement is not yet available however a
separate investigation is being performed by Geolabs Inc to determine the depth and
rate of movement of the landslide
Installation of the new pipe using trenchless methods and conventional open trench
method were evaluated for the project Based on our evaluation we believe that both
open trench and trenchless rehabilitation are viable options Detailed discussions of these
items and our geotechnical engineering recommendations are presented in the following
sections of this report
Open Trench Method
Conventional open trench method is a viable option and may be used for the
installation of the replacement pipe on a new alignment We envision that the invert
depths of the new sewer line will range from about 6 to 10 feet below the existing
ground surface Based on our field exploration groundwater was not encountered in
our borings However wet soil conditions were encountered in Boring Nos 2 and 3
The wet soil conditions were believed to be due to water seepage

Excavation Method
It is anticipated that conventional excavation techniques may be used for the
proposed excavations However it should be noted that existing abandoned
underground utilities and other below grade concrete structures may exist at the
site and would require heavy equipment excavation As previously mentioned
very dense cobbles and boulders in a soil matrix were encountered in our borings
between the depths of about 1 5 to 33 feet below the existing ground surface
Therefore excavation in localized hard areas encountering the very dense
cobbles and boulders may require the use of hoerams or chipping In general
the method and equipment to be used for excavation should be determined by
the contractor subject to practical limits and safety considerations The
excavated soils from the trench excavation should be stockpiled no closer than a
horizontal distance equal to the depth of the excavation from the edge of the
excavation to reduce the potential for ground movement
Excavation Support
As previously mentioned we envision that the new sewer line inverts will range
between about 6 to 10 feet below the existing ground surface Because the new
sewer line will be along the roadway sloping of the sides of the trenches may not
be possible due to the limited space Based on the relatively deep excavations
anticipated at localized areas shoring of the sides of the trenches will be
necessary to facilitate the proposed trench excavations and to protect personnel
working in the trench
Due to the potential presence of boulders within the trench excavation depths we
believe that it is not practical to use interlocking sheet piles to shore the trench
excavations Trench shields or other methods may be required The excavation
support and shoring system used must comply with all applicable safety
requirements It is recommended that a qualified geotechnical engineer be retained
by the contractor to design and evaluate the shoring system used The adequacy
and safety of the shoring installation should be made the sole responsibility of the
contractor
Because the site is in an area of potential earth movement good construction
procedures should be followed to reduce the potential for claims associated with
ground movements from adjacent property owners Even if good construction
procedures are followed some movement of the shoring system and the adjacent
ground may still occur due to changes in earth stresses during excavation Due to
the complexity of the stress changes it is difficult to estimate accurately the
magnitude of ground movement The magnitude of ground movement also
depends greatly upon workmanship such as how quickly and tightly the shoring
supports are installed the subsoil conditions the size of the excavation and the
rate of excavation Therefore it is important to realize that the excavation shoring

Groundwater
Based on our field exploration wet soil conditions were encountered in
Boring No 2 at depths of about 15 25 and 45 feet below the existing ground
surface and in Boring No 3 at 15 and 25 feet below the existing ground surface
We believe the wet soil conditions were from water seepage due to perched
groundwater The groundwater levels may fluctuate in response to changes in
rainfall conditions and infiltration of the surface water runoff Groundwater may be
encountered within the trench excavations Therefore dewatering of the
proposed trenches is not anticipated but may be necessary in some localized
areas to facilitate the proposed installation of the sewer lines
Backfill
In general trench backfill should be performed in accordance with the Standard
Specifications for Public Works Construction Department of Public Works City and
County of Honolulu September 1986 Free draining granular materials such as
No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation should be used for the trench
backfill up to about 12 inches above the pipes to provide adequate support around
the pipes and to reduce compaction of the backfill thus reducing the possibility of
damaging the pipes Compaction by water ponding or jetting should not be allowed
for this project Improper trench backfill could result in settlement of the backfill and
damage to the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipes to
the top of the subgrade or finished grade may consist of on site soils with particles
less than 6 inches in maximum dimension or select fill material It should be noted
that cobbles and boulders were encountered in our borings at shallow depths
Therefore boulders encountered in the trench excavations should not be used for
trench backfill
The backfill should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture
content placed in maximum 8 inch horizontal loose lifts and mechanically
compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential
for future ground subsidence Where trenches are located below pavement areas
the upper 3 feet of the trench backfill below the pavement grade should be
compacted to at least 95 percent relative compaction

Trenchless Methods
Two categories of trenchless methods are available rehabilitation and
conventional Trenchless rehabilitation consists of reusing the existing pipe or pipe
alignment and includes pipe bursting slip lining and Cured In Place Pipe CIPP methods
Conventional trenchless methods consist of installing a replacement pipe generally parallel
to the existing pipe that includes horizontal directional drilling and microtunneling
Slip Lining
Slip lining consists of inserting a new pipe of smaller diameter into the deteriorated
host pipe The new pipe is inserted either by pushing or pulling the pipe into the
deteriorated pipe The annular space between the existing pipe and the new pipe
is filled with grout
We believe that slip lining is a viable method provided that the host pipe does not
have excessive joint settlements severe misalignment and large deformations
Cured In Place Pipe CIPP
CIPP consist of inserting a liquid thermoset resin saturated material into the host
pipe The liquid thermoset resin saturated material is inserted into the host pipe by
hydrostatic or air inversion or by mechanically pulling with a winch and cable The
material is then heat cured in place
We believe that CIPP is a viable method provided that the host pipe does not have
large deformations and severe misalignment
Pipe Bursting
Pipe bursting consists of winching a pneumatic powered bursting tool through an
old section of pipe breaking and cracking the old pipe as it progresses A new pipe
section is pulled into place behind the bursting tool replacing the old pipe section
High Density Polyethylene HDPE pipe is typically used with pipe bursting
A conventional open trench method was used for installation of the existing 8 inch
sewer line The open trench method consists of excavating a trench wide enough
for the sewer line installation placing the sewer pipe and backfilling the open
trench with backfill material Because an increase in the pipe size is not planned
we believe that the soil surrounding the bursting tool will be the backfill material
During pipe bursting the soil surrounding the bursting tool will be compressed Ina
backfilled trench compaction of the backfill material will generally occur In
addition we believe that the soil matrix surrounding the boulders and cobbles will
be compressed during pipe bursting

Damage to existing utilities in close proximity of the bursting tool may occur for
existing utilities generally within 3 feet of the bursting tool Based on the existing
utility plan some utility lines are within this zone In these areas the utility
crossings may be exposed and the crossing utility moved further away from the
bursting tool during the pipe installation to reduce the potential for damage
For conditions where the bursting tool is close to the existing ground surface
surface heave may occur We anticipate that a 10 inch HDPE pipe will be required
to obtain an inside diameter of at least 8 inches For this HDPE pipe size
approximately 4 feet of ground cover should be provided to reduce the potential for
ground heave Based on the utility plans it appears that the existing pipe has more
than 4 feet of ground cover
It should be noted that the existing sewer alignment is located within an area of
on going slide movements Therefore some movements of the rehabilitated sewer
line should be anticipated
Excavation pits will be required to connect the laterals with the main sewer line
Electrofusion methods may be used to connect the HDPE pipes at the joints Since
the sewer line will probably need to keep active a bypassing system will probably
be required
In summary we believe that pipe bursting is a viable option for the proposed sewer
line rehabilitation provided that the concerns above are addressed However it
should be noted that the new pipe will follow the same alignment as the old pipe
and may be within the limits of the landslide
Horizontal Directional Drilling
This trenchless steerable installation method uses a multi axis drilling machine to
bore a small pilot hole The pilot hole is then reamed to a larger size to
accommodate the new pipe The new pipe is pulled back into the reamed hole by
the drilling machine
It is generally more difficult to maintain an accurate alignment with horizontal
directional drilling as compared to microtunneling or pipe bursting Since the sewer
line is a gravity type sewer line accurate alignment and grade are critical We
understand that consideration is being given to using the existing sewer line as the
pilot hole Because the drilling rod diameter is smaller than the inside diameter of
the existing pipe it would be difficult to maintain the center of the drill rod at the
center of the existing pipe Therefore there is a potential for deviation in grade

Based on our field exploration the subsurface conditions along Mona Street
consisted of colluvial deposits consisting of boulders and cobbles in a sand and
clay matrix extending to the maximum depths explored at the site The colluvial
deposits containing high gravel content about 50 percent or more and

caving in of the pilot and reamed holes The caved in materials will obstruct or
divert reaming tools or the pipe string during pipe pullback This will substantially
increase the pulling stresses on the pipe string and may impede pipe pulling or
damage the pipeline
Another consideration in the above method is that an entry pit of significant size will
be required to accommodate the drilling machine required for the size of pipe being
considered or large excavation pits will be required to tie in the installed sewer line
to the manhole structure
Because of the above reasons we believe that installation of the new sewer line
using horizontal directional drilling may not be a viable option
Microtunneling
Microtunneling is an installation method where a pipe is jacked behind a remotely
controlled steerable laser guided tunnel boring machine Entry and exit pits will be
required These shallow pits are generally rectangular in shape to accommodate
the length about three to four tunnel diameters and width about one tunnel
diameter of the tunnel boring machine the jacking frame reaction block and pipe
sections to be jacked into place During the microtunneling process the excavated
material is removed by using a mixture of water and slurry pumped through a pipe
for disposal balanced screw auger or a screw conveyor system
Microtunneling is performed with specialized equipment that is very costly and is
generally limited to the smallest pipe size of 18 inches
In microtunneling selecting the right machine cutter head for the ground condition
is critical Different types of cutter heads are used for various types of soil rock
Based on our field exploration colluvial deposits consisting of boulders and cobbles
in aclay and sand matrix was encountered at the project site We anticipate that
difficulty in steering may deflect off the cobbles or boulders and into clayey and
sandy soils In addition the cobbles and boulders within the drill path might halt
the advance of the drill bit reamer or pipeline during pullback
Based on the above assumptions microtunneling is not a viable option for this
project
