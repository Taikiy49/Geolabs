This report summarizes our earthwork observation and testing services performed
during the site grading operations foundation excavation pavement construction and
utility installation for the Queen Liliuokalani Children s Center project from
through in accordance with our fee proposal dated March 15 2010
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site designated by Tax Map Key Number 057 068 is a
rectangular parcel located at Highway between Aulani Place and
Kahau Street in the Lualualei area of Waianae on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The
subject parcel is bounded by existing residential homes to the northeast northwest
southeast and Farrington Highway to the southwest
Based on the information provided the project will consist of utility infrastructure
upgrades and realignment site improvements to parking lots and landscaping relocation
and renovation of four existing structures and construction of a new office building facility
and entry lobby The layout of the children s center development is shown on the
Site Plan Plate 1 The anticipated construction scope of work will include
Clearing and grubbing approximately 18 000 square feet
Removal and realignment of driveways and parking areas
Installation of underground utilities including sewer drain water electrical
telephone cable gas and irrigation

EARTHWORK OPERATIONS
The general contractor for the project is Constructors Hawaii Inc R H S Lee Inc
performed the site grading operations foundation excavation pavement construction and
utility installation The site grading consisted of clearing and grubbing footing excavation
subgrade preparation placement and compaction of fills and backfills pavement
construction and utility trench excavation and backfill
Following the clearing and grubbing operations the on site clayey soils were
generally over excavated where necessary to approximately 24inches below the slab
cushion within the building limits to provide the recommended 24 inch thick non expansive
select granular fill layer Where coralline materials are encountered prior to the 24 inch
depth the excavation was terminated at the top of the coralline deposits
The over excavated subgrade was generally scarified to a depth of about 6 inches
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and proof rolled with a
SAKAI single vibratory drum roller to help detect soft and or loose zones prior to
placement with non expansive select granular fill material
During placement of the non expansive select granular fill layer the fill materials
were generally placed in near level lifts brought to above the optimum moisture content
and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent of the maximum dry density determined in
accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor The base course and aggregate
subbase under the AC pavement was generally placed in near level lifts brought to above
the optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent of the
maximum dry density determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor
During the foundation excavation and preparation the excavated footing bottom was
generally recompacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
New utility connections were installed to serve the new development In general a
bedding layer consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel was provided below the piping
and manholes for uniform support Free draining granular materials consisting of
No 3B Fine gravel was also used for the trench backfill up to about 12 inches above the
pipes to provide adequate support around the pipes and to reduce the compaction effort
on the initial trench backfill The trench backfill was placed in 8 inch level loose lifts and
mechanically compacted to no less than 90 percent relative compaction Where trenches
are below pavement areas the upper 3 feet of the trench backfill below the pavement
grade was compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction

During the site grading operations samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test
results are summarized on Plate 2 of this report
Field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations on an
as need basis to confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures
and soil conditions were noted A total of 225 field density tests including retests of failed
areas were performed using the nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM D 6938
Results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 13 of
this report These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on
constructed fills and backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by
pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should
be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed
as unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the earthwork operations during the site grading
foundation excavation pavement and utility construction for the above project observed
and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report were performed in general
accordance with the intent of the project plans and specifications
LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork may
have been performed without our observation However the level of earthwork
observation and testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some
of the geotechnical risks to the project It should be recognized that there are variations
in the accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor
earthwork construction and materials
Our services were performed in accordance with generally accepted standards of
geotechnical engineering practice no warranty is expressed or implied Provision of
construction monitoring by an engineer is not insurance nor does it guarantee
construction of any type The contractor shall retain responsibility for the quality of the
work for adhering to the plans and specifications and for repairing defects regardless of
when they are found
