PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed mass grading project for the Makalapua Center Lot 4 site is
located between the existing K Mart and Liberty House retail stores in the Kailua Kona
area on the Island of Hawaii as shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The site
currently exists as vacant land that was partially graded as part of the K Mart store
development
Based on our review of the proposed mass grading pians for the lot we
understand that it is desired to grade the area to provide for an enhanced finish site
appearance The grading would include continuing the existing cut slope at the rear of
the K Mart store to connect with the cut slope at the back of the Liberty House store as
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The existing basalt rock bank between the existing
K Mart and Liberty House stores will be excavated to provide a level pad for future
development
The front of the Makalapua Center Lot 4 area will entail filling of the parcel
between the existing parking lots to match the adjacent grades and to provide for
surface drainage In general the proposed grades for the Makalapua Center Lot 4

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our geotechnical engineering reconnaissance of the site is to
obtain an overview of the surface conditions at the project site to develop a soil rock data
set for the formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to the
mass grading of the site In order to accomplish this we performed the following tasks
and work efforts
1 Review of available in house geotechnical information pertaining to the site
and vicinity including the geotechnical engineering exploration reports for
the K Mart and Liberty House store developments prepared by our office
2 Reconnaissance of the site by a Principal Engineer of our firm to observe
the existing ground conditions for earthwork considerations
3 Analyses of the field data for the formulation of geotechnical engineering
recommendations pertinent to the proposed mass grading project
4 Preparation of six copies of a letter report summarizing our work on the
project and presenting our findings and recommendations
5 Coordination of our overall work on the project by a Principal Engineer from
our firm
6 Quality assurance of our work and client design team consultation by a
Principal Engineer of our firm
7 Miscellaneous work efforts such as drafting word processing and clerical
support

Site Grading
Based on the information provided we anticipate the proposed site grading will
consist of cuts up to about 20 feet deep and fills up to about 10 feet thick In general we
recommend that fill areas be capped with 4 feet of choker fill material to reduce the
potential for migration of fines from the upper portion of the fill through the voids of the
compacted general fill Items of grading that are addressed in the following subsections
include the following 1 Site Preparation 2 Fill and Backfill Materials 3 Boulder
Disposal 4 Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements 5 Excavation and 6 Cut
and Fill Slopes
Site grading operations should be observed by a _ representative of
Geolabs Hawaii It is important that a representative from our office observe the site
grading operations to evaluate whether undesirable materials are encountered during the
excavation and proof rolling process and whether the exposed soil rock conditions are
similar to those anticipated
Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork areas within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed Vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable
materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site to reduce potential
contamination of the excavated materials The scattered loose boulders lying on
the existing ground surface should be removed to expose the underlying firm
and or dense ground The boulders may be used as fill material provided that they
are placed in accordance with the Boulder Disposal subsection of this report
Finished subgrades in cut areas and areas designated to receive fil should be
proof rolled with a Caterpillar D 9 bulldozer or similar heavy equipment minimum
6 passes to help detect and collapse near surface cavities Loose areas or
cavities disclosed during clearing operations should be backfilled with compacted
fill
Care should be exercised by the mass grading contractor when working in the
area designated as the Potential Lava Tube Area as indicated on the Site Plan
Plate 2 As previously indicated a lava tube structure is known to exist in this area
and will need to be collapsed and backfilled appropriately during the mass grading
operations

Fill and Backfill Materials
The fill and backfill materials to be used as general fill up to 4 feet below the
finished mass grading grades should consist of relatively well graded granular
materials less than 12 inches in largest dimension The on site cut material if less
than 12 inches in maximum dimension may be used as general fill or backfill
materials Boulders such as rock fragments greater than 12 inches in diameter
may also be used as fill material provided that they are placed at least 4 feet below
the finish subgrades In general placement of boulders in fills should be in
accordance with the procedures recommended in the Boulder Disposal
subsection of this letter report
Choker fill or structural fill should be used within the upper 4 feet of fill areas
below the finished mass grading grades Choker fill or structural fill should
consist of well graded granular material of 6 inches in maximum dimension with
sufficient fines to prevent the occurrence of voids in the compacted mass The
4 foot thick choker fill or structural fill material is recommended to facilitate
construction of the future underground utilities in the area
If the excavated rock material does not contain sufficient fines to produce a
well graded material off site cinders or non expansive soil may be mixed into the
excavated material to increase the percentage of fines to provide a well graded
material for compaction and to increase the stability of the fills If imported fill will
be required for this project it should conform to the requirements above and
should be observed and or tested by Geolabs Hawaii for its suitability prior to
being transported to the site for its intended use
Boulder Disposal
Boulders such as rock fragments larger than 12 inches in maximum size
encountered at the ground surface during site grading or imported may be
disposed in the general fill mass except within the 4 foot thick choker fill or
structural fill layer recommended as a cap above the general fill Boulders may be
used in the general fill provided that the following recommendations are followed
1 Boulders should be spread out and should not be nested together They
should be placed such that compaction equipment will be able to compact
between and around them
2 Boulders or portions of boulders should not encroach within 4 feet of the
finished mass grading grades

Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
In general fills and backfills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 12 inches
in loose thickness moisture conditioned and compacted to a minimum of
90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry
density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same
soil established in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91 The
finished grades of fill areas should be well graded free of voids not bony and
unyielding
Conventional compaction testing is generally not practicable in fills composed of
rocks boulders and or cobbles Instead a testing program to evaluate the
number of passes of a compactor needed to achieve the desired level of
compaction should be conducted at the start of the grading phase of the project
under the observation of a representative from Geolabs Hawaii Based on this
testing program the number of passes may then be used as the field criterion for
adequate compaction
Excavation
Based on our reconnaissance we anticipate that the site is underlain by slightly to
moderately fractured volcanic rock materials ranging from medium hard to very
hard It is anticipated that the volcanic rock may be excavated with normal heavy
excavation equipment such as ripping with large bulldozers where the rock is
near the existing ground surface However excavations into the deeper
formations may require the use of hoerams chipping or blasting It should be
noted that the K Mart and Liberty House buildings are located within proximity to
the proposed grading areas Therefore if blasting operations are to be
implemented by the contractor the contractor should evaluate the effects of
vibrations generated from the proposed blasting operations on the existing
buildings
The above discussions regarding the rippability of the surface materials are based
on our visual observation of the existing rock formation at the site We recommend

Cut and Fill Slopes
We understand that cut slopes up to about 20 feet high are planned for the
proposed mass grading project Based on our experience in the area we
anticipate that the cut slope would expose relatively dense basalt rock with some
fractures In general we believe that the cut slopes planned may be designed with
a slope inclination of one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V or flatter Since the cut
slopes will likely expose basalt rock formation we believe that slope benches may
be omitted for cut slopes up to 40 feet in vertical height
Based on the subsurface conditions anticipated at the site cavities and or clinker
zones may be encountered at the cut slope face In the event that clinker zones
are exposed at the cut slope face the cut slope may need io be flattened to a
slope inclination of Large cavities that may be exposed on the cut slope
face should be backfilled and or grouted if necessary A representative from
Geoiabs Hawaii should observe and map the cut slope during construction to
evaluate if the cut slope needs to be flattened and if the exposed materials are
consistent with our assumptions
In the event that over cutting of a cut slope occurs consideration should be given
to leaving the slope as is considering the relatively steep cut slopes
recommended Otherwise keying and benching the slope with cement treated
granular fills should be implemented instead of backfilling the slope to the design
grade with sliver fills
Permanent fill slopes constructed of the granular general fill and structural fill
choker fill materials may be designed with a slope inclination of 2H 1V or flatter
For fill slopes with vertical heights greater than 30 feet we recommend that a
minimum 8 foot wide bench be provided at the mid point of the slope to reduce the
potential for erosion due to surface water run off
Fills placed on slopes steeper than 5H 1V should be keyed and benched into the
existing slope to provide stability of the new fill against sliding The filling
operations should start at the lowest point and continue up in level horizontal
compacted layers in accordance with the above fill placement recommendations
Fill slopes should be constructed by overfilling and cutting back to the design slope
ratio to obtain a well compacted slope face
We recommend that the top of cut and fill slopes be rounded and that loose
materials be removed from the finished slopes In addition due to the nature of
the on site material anticipated at the project site water should be diverted away

Construction Observation
Construction observation of the mass grading work during construction is an
integral part of the geotechnical engineering recommendations provided herein The
items of construction monitoring that are critical requiring Special Inspection include
observation of the site preparation and fill placement and compaction Other aspects of
earthwork construction should also be observed by a representative from
Geolabs Hawaii This is to observe compliance with the intent of the design concepts
specifications or recommendations and to expedite suggestions for design changes that
may be required in the event that subsurface conditions differ from those considered in
this report The recommendations provided in this letter report are contingent upon such
observations
If actual exposed subsurface conditions encountered during construction are
different from those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications
to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this letter report are based in
part upon information obtained from our field reconnaissance Variations of conditions
between the field observation points may occur and the nature and extent of these
variations may not become evident until construction is underway If variations then
appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations provided in this
letter report
