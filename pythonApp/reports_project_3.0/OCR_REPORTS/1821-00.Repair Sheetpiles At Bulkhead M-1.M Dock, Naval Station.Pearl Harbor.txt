INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the repair of the sheetpile walls at Bulkhead
M 1 M Dock of the Naval Station at Pearl Harbor The approximate
location of the project site is shown on the Project Location Map
Plate 1
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our study was to evaluate the subsurface conditions
below the site to provide pertinent soil parameters for the design of
the proposed new sheetpile bulkhead at M Dock of the Naval Station at
Pearl Harbor on the island of Oahu Hawaii

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is our understanding that the existing sheetpile bulkhead M 1 of
the M Dock is badly deteriorated at and below the waterline
Therefore it is proposed to repair the deteriorated bulkhead by
installing a new sheetpile wall behind the existing sheetpile walls It
is anticipated that the top of the new sheetpile bulkhead would be
encased in concrete
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located at the beginning station of the M Dock
next to North Road and fronting Merry Loch inside the Naval Station at
Pearl Harbor The Merry Landing for the ferry transportation is located
to the southwest of the project site
At present the project site is paved with asphaltic concrete
pavement The pavement is generally flat and in good condition
However a surface crack running along the sheetpile wall direction was
noted at the A C pavement above the bulkhead location It is believed
that this is due to the deterioration of the sheetpile bulkhead and
subsequent soil movement

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface soil conditions at the project site were explored by
drilling and sampling 2 borings to depths of 77 0 and 51 5 feet below the
existing ground surface The approximate locations of these borings are
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The field exploration indicated that the soil conditions beneath the
existing A C pavement consist of medium dense to loose granular fill of
sandy gravel with silt to depths of about 16 to 20 feet below the
existing ground surface Lagoonal soil deposits consisting of loose
silty sand with gravel about 25 foot thick over soft clayey silt were
encountered beneath the granular fill to the depths of the borings
drilled
Groundwater was encountered in the borings The depths to the
groundwater level measured in the borings varied from 6 8 to 7 8 feet
below the existing ground surface
DISCUSSTON AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
In general our two drilled borings indicated that the on site
subsoil conditions consist of about 16 to 20 feet of medium dense to
loose granular fill over soft lagoonal soil deposits
The main consideration of this geotechnical engineering study was to
evaluate the pertinent soil parameters to be used in the new sheetpile
wall design Based on our field exploration and engineering analysis it
appears that the deteriorated sheetpile bulkhead can be repaired by
driving new sheetpiles behind the existing bulkhead

The new sheetpiles design length would depend on the height of
backfill material behind the sheetpiles and the sloping condition of the
mudline in front of the sheetpiles The existing deteriorated sheetpiles
in front of the proposed new sheetpiles would contribute some passive
resistance However due to the deteriorated condition of the
sheetpiles we recommend that the resistance of the existing sheetpiles
be neglected

Sheetpile Bulkhead Construction
Based on our field exploration we believe that in general the
proposed new sheetpiles could be driven through the upper granular fill
material and the soft lagoonal soil deposits without difficulty
However localized hard driving may be experienced if the sheetpiles
encounter cobbles and boulders in the fill materials
Backfill
Backfill material should consist of well graded granular material
generally less than 3 inches in size with less than about 25 passing the
200 sieve

The existing in situ backfill material of sandy gravel may be
reused for backfill behind the proposed new sheetpiles All backfills
should be compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximm dry density
established by ASTM D 1557 78 The top 12 inches should be compacted to
95 of its maximum dry density
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed sheetpile repair should be
forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written comments prior to
construction to see that the intent of our recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that all sheetpile installation be observed by the
soils engineer during construction to see that adequate penetration and
preparation have been made and that the subsurface conditions are
consistent with the findings of our geotechnical engineering study
If conditions encountered during construction are different from
those presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above evaluation

Field Exploration
se sm
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling
two 2 borings to depths of 77 0 and 51 5 feet below the existing
ground surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2 A truck mounted drill rig was used to drill the borings
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by visual
and textural examination in the field by our engineer who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory The soils
were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A graphic presentation of the materials
encountered is presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 1 through
An 2 2
Soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving a 2 inch
standard split spoon sampler or a 2 4 inch I D split barrel sampler
with a 140 pound hammer free falling 30 inches or by hydraulically
pushing a 3 inch diameter thin wall tube The blow counts to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches or the hydraulic pressure to push the sampler
the first 24 inches are shown on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate
sample depths
A rock core sample was obtained by coring with a NX core barrel
The percentage of core recovery REC and the rock quality designation
ROD for each run are shown on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate
depth
