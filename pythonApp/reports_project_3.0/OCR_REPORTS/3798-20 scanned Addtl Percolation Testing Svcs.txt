Dear Mr Awaya
In accordance with our proposal of we performed additional
percolation tests performed in the vicinity of the drainage detention basin at the
Waimanalo Kupuna Housing Project in Waimanalo Oahu Hawaii This letter presents
the results of these percolation tests
Percolation tests were performed in three shallow probeholes designated as
Probeholes P 101 through P 103 in the vicinity of the drainage detention basin location
The probeholes extended to depths of about 5 feet below the existing ground surface
The testing was performed in general accordance with the Auger Hole method All
three percolation tests were performed using the falling head method in which water is
added to fill an auger hole at a given level and then the fall of the water level is
observed with time The permeability is based on the assumption that a unit hydraulic
head gradient causes the flow through the bottom and sides of the hole The
probeholes were drilled using a truck mounted drill rig equipped with 4 inch diameter
auger equipment The results of the tests can be found on the attached Plates 1
through 3
The materials encountered in the probeholes were classified by visual and
textural examination in the field by our geologist who monitored the drilling operations
on a near continuous basis Soils were classified in general conformance with the
United Soil Classification System Materials encountered in all three probeholes
generally consisted of brown sandy silt to silty sand SM to white fine calcareous sand
SW
The percolation test results were generally consistent with our previous findings
We observed that all three percolation tests indicated moderate permeability values of
utilization in the design for storm water infiltration basins
