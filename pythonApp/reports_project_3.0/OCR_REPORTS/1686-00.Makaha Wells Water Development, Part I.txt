MAKAHA WELLS WATER DEVELOPMENT PART I

In accordance with your request we have made a preliminary geologic
reconnaissance of the near surface soil conditions at the upper well and
tank site No 4
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of the reconnaissance was to explore the subsurface soil
conditions within the site in order to provide recommendations for tank
retaining wall and building foundation pavement design and general
subsurface soil data for site grading Particular attention was directed
to the upper portion of the site where a retaining wall for the tank and
the building would be designed against the existing slope

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed project consists of the following
1 A 20 000 gallon steel reservoir structure approximately 20 feet
in diameter and 14 feet high Shallow cuts and fills of about 2
to 5 feet are planned to create a level pad area for the
reservoir
2 A one story control building approximately 25 by 30 feet in
plan dimension It will be located on a fill section about 30
feet west of the proposed new reservoir and constructed of
concrete and CMU walls

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site consists of a narrow strip of land about 200 by 60
feet located about 30 feet north of Makaha Stream along the stream
terrace in Makaha Valley and about 13 000 feet northerly of the Sheraton
Makaha Hotel
In general the topography slopes down towards the stream channel at
from about 1060 feet along the southern portion of site to 1090 feet
above Mean Sea Level near the northern end of the site The area is
partially covered with vegetation and small trees Access to the site is
by means of 4 wheel drive vehicles over improved roads that at times may
be impassable
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The project site is located in the upper reaches of Makaha Valley
which is essentially an amphitheater headed valley
After the Waianae Volcano ceased its activity it was deeply incised
by amphitheater headed valleys and partly filled with sediments during
Pliocene time and later when the glacio eustatic sea level changes during
the Pleistocene resulted in the accumulation of hundreds of feet of
marine and non marine sediments
The site lies on a gently sloping terrace between the stream channel
and toe of the steep hillside The terrace consists of coalesced fans of
alluvium and colluvial sediments derived from the salient volcanic cliffs
of the Waianae Range

Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the reservoir site was investigated by
attempting to hand auger with a small diameter auger to help delineate
the near surface soil conditions and to obtain small samples for testing
The hand auger method proved to be inadequate for penetration beyond
depths of 2 to 3 feet
Soil Conditions
From visual observations of soil rock exposures in the gully and cut
bank and the shallow depth augered it appears that most of the area is
covered with a fairly thick more than 30 feet surface soil layer of
older alluvium or colluvium The alluvium generally consists of stiff
clayey silt to silty clay soils with some cobbles and boulders
Colluvium is slope wash or previous landslide debris which may be
locally inter mixed with the older alluvium It generally consists of
unweathered to extremely weathered basalt cobbles and boulders in a
medium stiff to stiff clayey silt matrix
Additional information of the soils in the area visited was taken
from the Soil Survey of the Islands of Molokai and
Lanai State of Hawaii by the USDA Soil Conservation Service issued in
August 1972 Comments from this survey on the flatter area from toe of
slope to Makaha Stream and portion of steep slope are as follows
Pulehu Very Stony Clay Loam 0 to 12 slopes is a well drained soil
on alluvial fans and stream terraces They developed in alluvium
washed from igneous rock The soil has a moderate to low
shrink swell value and high shear strength May be subject to

GEOLOGIC ENGINEERING CONSIDERATIONS AND CONCLUSTONS
Considerations and Conclusions
Based on the limited data it appears that the majority of the
alluvial colluvial soils at the site could provide adequate support for
the proposed structures except where expansive clay soils may be
encountered In these areas site preparation particularly the
stripping of surface clayey soils should be considered
The excavated soil and rock materials should provide adequate fill
material for the embankments Expansive soils if encountered should be
placed a minimum of 3 feet below finished grade and away from sloping
areas
20 000 Gallon Reservoir Foundation
The study disclosed that the proposed site is underlain by stiff
brown clayey silt with cobbles which could provide adequate foundation
support for the proposed reservoir The proposed grading plan indicates
that the tank will be partly on fill and partly in cut
Foundation
Because the tank will be located partly in cut and partly on fill
the area in cut should be overexcavated 1 foot and backfilled The
backfill should be compacted to match the density of the new fill
The fill materials within the top 12 inches below the reservoir
on grade slab should consist of select non expansive on site soil
or off site borrow such as crusher waste material The structural
fill should be placed in 8 inch loose lifts and compacted to obtain
a minimum 95 of its maximum dry density established by ASTM
D 1557 78 test method

An allowable bearing value of 2000 p s f on the above specified
compacted fill is recommended for the anticipated tank loads
The allowable bearing value may be increased by one third for
temporary wind and seismic loads
Footing should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grade Footings located next to utility trenches
should extend down to the bottom of the utility trench and bear on
stiff undisturbed soil or structurally designed to span over the
well compacted trench backfill to minimize footing settlement
A 6 inch cushion layer of gravel such as 3B Fine is recommended
below the structure to provide drainage and uniform support
Prior to concrete placement the bottom of all footing excavations
and trench backfill should be compacted to a minimum of 95 of its
maximum dry density as established by ASTM D 1557 78
The site grading should be designed to divert surface water away
from the reservoir foundation
Based on our preliminary study and estimated tank loads anticipted
it is our opinion that settlements would generally be less than
one half of an inch total and one fourth of an inch differential

Retaining Wall
Retaining walls are required as part of the construction for the
site grading structures and roadway These walls will be designed
against the existing hillside slope and along the existing stream bank
We recommend that the retaining wall excavation and construction be
made in sections and backfilled as soon as practicable For the
temporary cut slope behind portions of the wall a gradient of up to
H V may be used
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f on compacted fill or
natural soil may be used
Wall footings along the stream bank should be embedded a minimum of
3 feet or a sufficient depth to obtain a minimum horizontal set back
distance of 10 feet as measured from the outer edge of the footing to the
stream edge For walls located on level ground the footings should be
embedded a minimum of 2 feet below the lowest adjacent grade

Well graded granular material such as base course rock sand or
equivalent should be used as backfill material directly behind the
retaining walls This would minimize the compactive effort required for
the backfill and reduce the build up of additional lateral pressures on
the walls
In general the granular backfill should be placed in an area within
a line drawn at a 45 degree angle from the heel of the retaining wall
structure
For the material outside the limit for granular backfill we believe
that the on site excavated material which consists of alluvial soil can
be used Notwithstanding the recommended use of on site material all
backfills used should be non expansive soil free of organic matter and
form a dense stable soil mass when compacted to the required density
Generally the particle size should be less than 6 inches in size
All the backfill material should be placed in 8 inch maximm lifts
and compacted to at least 90 of its maximm dry density as established
by the ASTM D 1557 78 method
To minimize the build up of hydrostatic pressures behind the walls
sufficient drainage should be provided We would recommend that the
drainage provision consist of the followings
Subdrain A 4 inch perforated pipe should be placed on the heel
directly behind the wall At a distance of no more than 50 feet
along the alignment of the wall the subdrain should daylight into
the drainage ditch in front of the wall
Drainage Ditch Drainage ditches should be provided immediately
behind the top and at the ground level in front of the wall These
ditches will direct the surface run off away from the wall

Site Grading
In general the site grading will consist of filling along the
existing terrace at locations shown on Plate 2 Very little cutting is
planned for the Well No 4 site
The site should be cleared of vegetation debris and soft
materials Prior to filling or footing excavation the existing ground
should be scarified to a minimum of 6 inches and compacted to a minimum
of 90 of its maximum dry density established by ASTM D 1557 78 test
method
Based on the field reconnaissance and laboratory test results most
of the on site soils are in general suitable for use as fill
Boulders less than 2 feet in diameter may be used in the
non structural fill provided they are not allowed to nest together and
the voids between boulders are filled
Large boulders in excess of 2 feet in diameter may be used to
construct riprap or buttress or should be reduced to smaller sizes
If additional off site borrow is required the material should be
tested and approved by the soils engineer prior to transportation to the
site The material should generally be of low expansion potential and
should not contain large percentages of boulders organic or deleterious
material

Since the project site is located in a fairly high rainfall area 50
to 75 inches year the on site subsoils may have in place moisture
contents above its optimm moisture content Therefore drying of the
excavated material may be required before using it as fill material
Any local soft spots and soft material encountered within the
construction area should be removed and replaced with compacted fill
Fills placed where the existing ground is steeper than 5 horizontal
to 1 vertical should be keyed and benched into the existing firm ground
to properly bond the new fill to the slope For details see Plate 3
This detail is essential for the stability of the fills particularly to
reduce the potential of sliding along the fill in situ ground contact
The filling operation should start at the lowest point and continue up in
level compacted layers
Proper drainage is essential for satisfactory performance of the
cuts and embankment fills as excessive water will weaken the clayey silt
soils

ADDITIONAL EXPLORATION
The recommendations presented in this report are presented as a
guide for planning purposes only Detailed subsurface investigations
including borings or test pits should be conducted for specific project
areas when access permits are obtained at a later date prior to the

Design Review

CONSTRUCTION MONT TORING
It is recommended that the excavation operations and backfilling of
the retaining walls be observed by the soils engineer during
construction It is also recommended that the footing excavations be
observed by the soils engineer during construction to see that adequate
bearing and set back have been obtained and that the subsurface
conditions are consistent with the findings of our investigation The
recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the above
inspections If conditions encountered during construction are different
from those presented in this report such as pockets of expansive soil
soft spots appropriate modifications to the design should be made in the
field by the project soils engineer

made in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation
