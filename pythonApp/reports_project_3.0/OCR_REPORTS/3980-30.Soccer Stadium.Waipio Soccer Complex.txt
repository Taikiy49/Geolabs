This letter report presents our recommendations for the proposed soccer stadium within the
partially graded Waipio Soccer Complex located in the Waipio Peninsula area in the District of
Waipahu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are
shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering recommendations for the design
associated with the proposed soccer stadium The findings and recommendations presented herein
are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a soccer stadium with bleachers on earthen berms near the
northwestern corner of the Waipio Soccer Complex The stadium site is bounded by mudponds and
the Waipahu Ash Landfill to the west The stadium is also bounded by the eastern half of the
Waipahu Incinerator Plant to the north
Retaining walls with sloping and level backfill conditions under free standing and restrained
conditions are planned for the bleachers Concrete steps and slabs are planned for the entire
footprint of the bleachers within the berms Construction of the berms will likely involve fills on the
order of about 30 to 40 feet in height and cuts on the order of about 10 to 20 feet in depth The
finished grade for the soccer stadium field is planned for about 18 to 20 feet Mean Sea
Level MSL
Two one story structures for restrooms and concession facilities are planned for the
southeastern entrance of the stadium These structures will be about 40 feet by 60 feet in plan view

EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The stadium site is relatively flat except for a linear ground depression situated in a
north south direction near the center of the proposed soccer stadium Ground elevations range from
about 20 feet MSL at the northeastern corner to about 36 feet MSL at the southwestern corner
of the proposed soccer stadium The site is generally covered with abandoned sugar cane growth
tall grass and weeds

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the relatively firm subsurface soils we believe areal settlements from the 30 to
40 foot high fills will be on the order of about 3 inches In addition we believe that the settlements
from compression of the 30 to 40 foot high fills under its own weight will also be on the order of
about 3 inches Therefore we recommend that surface monuments be established on the fills and
monitored on a weekly basis by a qualified professional surveyor until the rate of settlement of the
fill has subsided The readings should be transmitted to Geolabs Inc for review We anticipate that
the settlement will take about 2 months following the completion of mass grading
Based on the moderate expansion potential of the on site soils and anticipated steeply
sloping area at the soccer stadium bleachers we recommend that the subgrade soil be properly
prepared moisture conditioned and covered with at least 4 inches of shotcrete or other material
within 48 hours of proper moisture conditioning and compaction of the subgrades to prevent drying
of the soils below 2 above optimum moisture Fill materials should consist of approved import
material on site coralline or on site brown clayey soils The on site dark gray clayey soils should
not be used as fill
Based on the current plans we believe that it will be difficult to build the stadium bleachers
with a typical retaining wall and drainage system With the current configuration the retaining walls
will need to be constructed prior to the bleachers above the retaining walls Otherwise the
bleachers could become undermined when excavations for the retaining walls are made To reduce
the amount of excavation and backfill we recommend that the design of retaining walls with reverse
footings i e minimum wall heel dimension be considered We believe that the slab at the lower side
of the retaining wall may be made part of the reverse footing We also recommend that a
prefabricated drainage product such as MiraDrain or EnkaDrain be used in lieu of the drainage
material The prefabricated drainage product should also be hydraulically connected to a perforated
pipe at the base of the wall
Based on the current plans the slope beneath the bleachers will be about 1 5 horizontal to
1 vertical We believe this is feasible provided positive drainage including surface drains
is established to prevent excessive landscape water from percolating into the embankment fill

Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork areas within the contract grading limits should be thoroughly
cleared and grubbed Vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable materials should be
removed and disposed of properly off site or stockpiled in a designated area to reduce the potential
for contamination of the excavated materials If incinerator ash is encountered this material should
be removed and disposed of properly in accordance with the environmental consultant s
recommendations
Soft and yielding areas encountered during clearing and grubbing should be over excavated
to expose firm natural material and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with
well compacted engineered fill The excavated soft soils may be used as fill provided that it meets
the requirements for fill material
If zones of soft loose or saturated soils are encountered at the subgrade level deeper
excavation may be required to expose the underlying firm material The resulting excavation should
be backfilled with well compacted engineered fill The excavated soft soils may be used as fill
provided that it meets the requirements for fill material
Prior to filling the existing ground should be scarified to a depth of 10 inches and
moisture conditioned to about 2 percent above the optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum
of 90 percent relative compaction Where shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of the
subgrade we recommend that the soil be thoroughly moistened to close cracks or prepared again
as recommended above Saturation and subsequent yielding of the exposed subgrade due to
inclement weather and poor drainage may require over excavation of the soft areas and
replacement with well compacted engineered fill The excavated soft soils may be used as fill
provided that it meets the requirements for fill material Relative compaction refers to the in place
dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil
established in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91

Fills and Backfills
The fill material should consist of well graded low to moderately expansive materials less
than 3 inches in largest dimension with a CBR swell less than 2 percent The fill material should
also be free of vegetation and deleterious materials In general the on site brown clayey materials
and tannish white coralline materials should be suitable for fill material
Select granular fill and imported fill should consist of non expansive granular material such
as crushed coral mudrock basalt or cinder sand The material should be well graded from coarse
to fine with no particles larger than 3 inches in largest dimension and should contain between 10 and
30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve The material should have a laboratory CBR value
of 20 or more and should have a maximum swell of 1 percent or less when tested in accordance
with ASTM Test Designation D 1883 The on site coralline material is suitable for select granular
fill material Imported fill materials should be tested by Geolabs Inc for conformance with these
recommendations prior to delivery to the project site for its intended use
Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
Fills and backfills should be moisture conditioned to about 2 percent above the optimum
moisture placed in level lifts not exceeding 12 inches in loose thickness and compacted to at least
90 percent relative compaction
Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of
the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 91 test
procedures Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to
the maximum dry density
Compaction should be accomplished by sheepsfoot rollers vibratory rollers multiple wheel
pneumatic tired rollers or other types of acceptable compaction equipment
Because moisture conditioning and compaction of the clayey subgrade soils are critical
elements of earthwork observations and soil density tests should be performed by Geolabs Inc
during site grading to assist the contractor in obtaining the required degree of compaction and the
proper moisture content Where compaction is less than required additional compactive effort
should be applied with adjustment of moisture content as necessary to obtain the specified
compaction It should be noted that the moisture requirement of the fills and subgrades about
2 percent above the optimum moisture is an important requirement for the use of the on site clayey
soils

Cut and Fill Slopes
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered in the borings it appears that most of the
cut slopes would expose soils consisting of stiff clayey materials or very dense coralline materials
In general we believe that cut slopes into these soil materials may be made at an inclination of
2H 1V or flatter
Fill slopes should be designed with a slope inclination of 2H 1V or flatter Fills placed on
slopes steeper than 5H 1V should be keyed and benched into the existing slope to provide stability
of the new fill against sliding The filling operations should start at the lowest point and continue up
in level horizontal compacted layers in accordance with the above fill placement recommendations
Cut and fill slopes beneath the soccer stadium bleachers may be constructed at a slope
inclination of provided that the subgrade soil is properly prepared and prevented from
drying below the optinum moisture by covering the finished surface with shotcrete or other
coverings
Slope benches should be provided at maximum 35 foot height increments The slope
benches should be at least 8 feet in width
Fill slopes should be constructed by overfilling and cutting back to the design slope ratio to
obtain a well compacted slope face Track rolling of slopes should not be acceptable In the event
that over cutting of a slope occurs keying and benching requirements should be implemented
instead of backfilling the slope to the design grade with sliver fills Water should be diverted away
from the tops of slopes and slope planting should be provided as soon as possible to reduce the
potential for erosion of the finished slopes

SLABS ON GRADE
Based on the moderate expansion potential of the on site soils and anticipated steeply
sloping area at the soccer stadium bleachers we recommend that the subgrade soil be properly
prepared and kept moist above the optimum moisture content prior to concrete plalcement Proper
moisture conditioning involves keeping the soil at a moisture content of at least 2 percent above the
optimum moisture content Fill materials should consist of approved import material on site
coralline or on site brown clayey soils The on site dark gray clayey soils should not be used as fill
Exterior concrete slabs at locations other than the stadium bleachers should be constructed
with at least 12 inches of non expansive select granular fill below the concrete slab However for

exterior sidewalks the non expansive select granular fill thickness may be reduced to 6 inches The
select granular fill should be placed on properly compacted and moisture conditioned material that
has been kept moist Control joints should be provided at intervals equal to the width of the
sidewalks and at right angle intersections
In addition to the 12 inches of non expansive select granular fill interior concrete slabs for
the restroom and concession structures should include a minimum 4 inch thick layer of cushion fill
below the slab The cushion fill consisting of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation
or Basaltic Termite Barrier BTB should provide more uniform support of the slabs and should

damage to floor coverings an impervious moisture vapor barrier such as a plastic membrane is

or sheet vinyl should be considered since they can better mask minor slab cracking It is also
recommended that interior walls be designed to incorporate some flexibility to accommodate small
amount of possible ground movements
UTILITIES
It is anticipated that some utilities may be constructed for the proposed project The method
and equipment to be used for excavation should be determined by the contractor subject to
practical limits and safety considerations The excavation should comply with all applicable local
state and federal safety requirements Trench shoring design and installation should be the
responsibility of the contractor Trench shoring and bracing should conform to the appropriate health
and safety requirements
In general for support of the utility lines we recommend that a granular bedding consisting
of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation be used under the pipes The
initial backfill up to about 1 foot above the pipes should consist of free draining backfills such as
No 3B Fine gravel to reduce the potential for damaging the pipes from compaction of the backfill
It is critical that a free draining granular material be used to reduce the potential for formation of
voids below the haunches of pipes and to provide adequate support for the sides of the pipes The
use of on site soils as backfill immediately around utility pipes is not recommended
For utility excavations that encounter soft dark gray soils we recommend that the No 3B
Fine gravel bedding thickness be increased to 12 inches and wrapped with a geotextile fabric
AMOCO 4557 or similar The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 1 foot above the

DRAINAGE
The finished grades outside the proposed structures should be sloped to shed water away
from the slabs and foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding Excessive landscape
watering near the foundations and slabs should be avoided Planters and gravel strips next to
foundations and slabs should be avoided or have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce the
potential for water infiltration into the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the above
foundation and slab recomendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation and
subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled
against the walls or slabs immediately after setting the concrete to reduce the potential for water
infiltration In addition drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and should be
maintained to drain surface run off away from slabs and foundations

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from the field borings Variations of conditions between and beyond the field
borings may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident until
construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to reevaluate the
recommendations presented in this report

the Topographic Survey Map by ControlPoint Surveying Inc Elevations of the test borings were

map were then transferred to the Grading and Drainage Plan by Belt Collins Hawaii Ltd dated
December 1999
The stratification breaks implied by the written representations of the borings depict the
approximate boundaries between soil rock types and as such may denote a gradual transition
Water level data from the borings were measured at the time of the field exploration These data
have been reviewed and interpretations made in the formulation of this report However it must be
noted that fluctuation may occur due to variation in artesian conditions perched water conditions
tidal influences seasonal precipitation and storm surge conditions
This letter report has been prepared solely for the purpose of assisting the design engineers
and architect for the proposed project Therefore this report may not contain sufficient data or
proper information for use to form the basis for preparation of construction cost estimates or
contract bidding A contractor wishing to bid on this project should retain a competent geotechnical
engineer to assist in the interpretation of this report and or performance of site specific exploration

sufficient contingency fund is recommended to accommodate these possible extra costs
