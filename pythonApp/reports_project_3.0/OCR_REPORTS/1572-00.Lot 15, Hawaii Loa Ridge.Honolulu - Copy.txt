In accordance with your request we have made a study of the near
surface soil conditions at the above referenced lot in order to provide
site grading and foundation recommendations for the proposed residence
The study consisted of a seismographic survey and geologic reconnaissance
of the project area a review of the site plan and the preliminary house
foundation plan This report summarizes our observations and
reconmendations

GEOLOGY
The site is located on the southern flank of the elongated Koolau
Volcanic Range which forms the southeastern portion of Oahu Hawaii The
range was formed during the Tertiary Pericd of pahoehoe and aa
basalt lava flows from a rift zone roughly paralleling the existing
mountain trends

EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The subject Ict is on the northeastern corner of Pu u Ikena Drive
and Lelekepue Place in the Hawaii Loa Ridge Subdivision located in
Honolulu Oahu Hawaii
Lot 15 generally slopes downward towards the south at about 8 to
20 gradients with steeper slopes along the northeast and southeast
boundaries of the site
The existing elevation of the site generally varies from about 358
to 384 feet
The topography is characterized by a gently sloping hillside that
is sparsely covered with grasses and basaltic gravel and cobbles
Rock outcrops were noted along the southerly and easterly sides of
the building site
A grassy strip about 30 feet wide is located along the Pu u Ikena
Drive side cf the lot

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field reconnaissance of the area our general
observations and recommendations are presented below
General
The field engineering and geological reconnaissance indicates that
the site is suitable for the proposed house construction
The anticipated competent soil and rock materials should provide
adequate bearing for the proposed structure and related utilities

Fill Material and Compaction Requirements
In general the excavated rock material less than 12 inches in
size may be used as general fill material If the excavated rock
material does not contain sufficient fines to produce a well graded
material the surface soils may be mixed into the excavated rocky
material to increase the percentage of fines to provide a better
graded material for compaction and increase the stability of the
fills
For fills within 2 feet of finished grade below the structure and
for subsequent structural backfill within the house limits the
maximum size of particle in the fill should be reduced to 6 inches
to facilitate compaction
Large boulders should be wasted off the site

Fill Placement
an se RE He A He a
Fills should be placed in horizontal loose lifts up to 12 to 14
inches thick and compacted to its maximum density Excavated rock
fills may be placed in horizontal layers not to exceed 18 inches in
thickness
Conventional compaction testing is generally not practicable in
excavated volcanic rock material less than 12 inches in size due
to the large particle size Instead a testing program consisting
of visual observations and test strips to determine the lift
thickness and number of passes of a compactor required to achieve
the desired compaction should be conducted at the start of the
project The number of passes can then be used as the criteria for
compaction
Excavation
The field reconnaissance indicates that the site may be underlain
at fairly shallow depths by basalt formations with a thin layer of
silty soil mixed with cobbles and boulders
We believe that much of the excavation into rock for the proposed
facility may be done by bulldozer and ripping equipment

Foundations
We believe the on site rocky ground and compacted select granular
fill resulting from the above site grading can provide suitable
foundation support for the proposed building
Structural fill should be placed in accordance with the reccnmenda
tions under Site Grading and should be observed by a soil technician or
engineer
Spread and wall footing foundations may be used for the proposed
structure
All exterior footing bottoms should be embedded a minimum of 24
inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade or 6 inches into
weathered basalt rock or clinker formation The minimum embedment for

interior footings may be reduced to 18 inches on fill or 6 inches into

For the footings bearing on the compacted rock fill an allowable

On Grade Slab
Based on a non expansive slab subgrade condition anticipated at
this site on grade slab should be placed over a 4 inch layer of base
course compacted to 90 of its maximum density as established by ASTM
D 1557 78 The thickened edge around the on grade slab should extend a
minimum of 12 inches below the lowest adjacent finished ground surface

To reduce differential settlement where the slab is partly on cut
in rock and partly on fill the cut portions should be over excavated
about one foot and then recompacted to match the adjacent subgrade
density
Depending on the structure of the underlying rock formation
whether voids or pukas are encountered a choking layer consisting
of well graded rock materials may be required between the underlying
rugged rock surface and the upper cushion fill or concrete slab

Swimming Pool
It appears that the proposed swimming pool will be excavated below
the existing ground at the west side of the lot However the upper
portion of the pool wall will be backfill For the upper portion of the
swimming pool wall in fill or backfill conditions a lateral earth
pressure around the pool of 56 p s f equivalent fluid pressure per foot
of depth may be used for walls restrained at the top at rest condition
with level backfill Normal water pressure of 62 4 p c f would act on
the inside walls of the pool
We recommend that the backfill behind the pool wall should consist
of granular material such as 3B Fine gravel or the fill area be
over fill first with adequate compaction requirement during the initial
site grading and the pool be excavated subsequently to obtain a stable
sidewall for the pool construction
Retaining Walls
Several retaining walls are planned for the proposed house
Seismic refraction surveys were made near some of the proposed wall
locations Also rock outcrops were observed and based on these visual
observations consolidated clinker material or basalt rock is anticipated
at the wall locations For the foundations of the retaining walls the
footings should be embedded a minimum of 2 feet inte the consolidated
clinker material or 6 inches into dense basalt whichever occurs first

considered
