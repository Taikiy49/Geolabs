Dear Mr. Soong: 
This letter report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations during the 
probing and grouting operations for the Ho’opili Off-Site Water System from February 28 
through March 7, 2018. The probing and grouting observation work was performed in 
general accordance with the project specifications. 
Project Considerations 
The Ho’opili Development in the Ewa area on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii is a 
master-planned community encompassing approximately 1,500 acres of residential, 
commercial, industrial, roadway and utility infrastructure, and public school facility. The 
Ho’opili Development is generally located to the south of the Interstate Route H-1 Highway 
and west of Fort Weaver Road. 
Based on the information provided, the project involves the development of the 
440-Foot and 228-Foot Water System Improvements. Based on the subsurface conditions 
and grading construction anticipated at the site, the basalt formation will be presented near 
the bottom of the foundation subgrade. To reduce the potential for loss of foundation 
support resulting from the collapse of cavities or compression of loose zones below the 
foundation subgrade, a program of cavity probing and grouting was planned for the new 
440-Foot Reservoir foundation during construction.

Probing and Grouting Operations 
The location of the project is shown on the Project Location Map, Plate 1. Mort 
Drilling Services, Inc. performed the probing and grouting operations. 
Nan Inc. surveyed the footing layout and marked the probe hole locations prior to 
the probing and grouting operations. In general, the probe holes were located at the 
column footings (one probe hole for every footing), along the wall footing (one probe hole 
every 10 feet), and in an 11 Ft. 6 In. +/- grid throughout the tank floor. The approximate 
probe hole locations are shown on the Probe Hole Location Plan, Plate 2. In general, the 
probe holes at the new reservoir structure were drilled to a depth of about 13 feet below 
the subgrade elevation. 
The probing and grouting operations were accomplished using hollow drill rods with 
3-inch diameter drill bits with side discharge holes for water and grout injection. Each 
probe hole was advanced by rotary percussion using a TEI 300HT drill mounted on a 
Komatsu PC75 excavator. Water was injected continuously through the hollow rods while 
advancing to the target probe depth. Evidence of cavities/voids was observed by the loss 
of water circulation with a rapid receding of water into the drill hole. Upon reaching the 
target depth, the probe hole was tremie grouted through the drill rod while the rod was 
being retracted gradually. The grout used for the probe holes generally consisted of one 
sack of cement mixed with 8 gallons of water with a water cement ratio of about 0.70. 
For the probe hole locations that did not return grout during the initial grout 
application, the contractor placed a 1.5-inch PVC pipe in the probe hole to preserve the 
grouting access without being affected by a potential cave-in prior to additional grout 
application.  
Our field representative observed the probing and grouting operations and recorded 
the probe time, grout time, and approximate estimated grout take. A total of 168 probe 
holes with an aggregate length of about 2,189 lineal feet below the existing ground 
surface were drilled for the new reservoir as summarized in the following table and on 
Plate 3.1. Detailed information pertaining to the probing and grouting operations at each 
probe hole is provided on Plates 3.2 through 3.8.
