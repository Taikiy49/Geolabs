PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project is on a 2 229 acre parcel off Lauwiliwili Street within the Kapolei
Business Park Phase 1 development in the District of Ewa on the Island of Oahu
Hawaii The project entails constructing a two story facility with a footprint of about
30 000 square feet to accommodate retail training human services and administrative
uses and donations sorting and processing In addition parking and a loading zone will be
provided to serve the new facility
Based on the information provided we envision that the new building will be
constructed of pre cast concrete panels for the exterior walls second floor and roofing
with a concrete slab on grade floor at the ground level We understand that the finished
floor elevation for the new building is set at about 40 1 feet Mean Sea Level MSL
Based on the existing topography and the finished floor elevation of the new building we
envision that grading consisting of cuts and fills on the order of less than about 5 feet high
will be required to achieve the design grades A layout of the new facility is shown on the

EARTHWORK OPERATIONS
Constructors Hawaii Inc is the general contractor for the Goodwill Ohana Career
Learning Center project Jayar Construction Co Inc performed the site grading
operations foundation excavation pavement construction and utility installation The site
grading consisted of clearing and grubbing footing excavation subgrade proof rolling
placement and compaction of fills and backfills pavement construction and utility trench
excavation and backfill
Following the clearing and grubbing operations the existing ground was generally
over excavated where necessary to the subgrade elevations within the building and
pavement limits The coral formation exposed at the over excavated subgrades was then
proof rolled with a minimum 10 ton sheepsfoot and or vibratory drum rollers to detect the
presence of potential near surface cavities sinkholes or loose zones New fills required
for the project consisting generally of the excavated in situ coralline materials were
generally placed in near level lifts brought to above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density determined in
accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor
New sewer water and drain lines were installed to serve the new development In
general a bedding layer consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel was provided below
the piping and manholes for uniform support Free draining granular materials consisting
of No 3B Fine gravel was also used for the trench backfill up to about 12 inches above the
pipes to provide adequate support around the pipes and to reduce the compaction effort
on the initial trench backfill The trench backfill was placed in 8 inch level loose lifts and
mechanically compacted to no less than 90 percent relative compaction Where trenches
are below pavement areas the upper 3 feet of the trench backfill below the pavement
grade was compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
During the site grading operations sample of the on site soils and imported
materials utilized as fill materials were obtained and transported to our laboratory for
testing The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in
our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test
results are summarized on the attached Plate 2
Field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations on an
as need basis to confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures
and soil conditions were noted A total of 122 field density tests were performed using the
nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM D 6938 Results of the compaction tests
performed are summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 8 These tests were performed on the
prepared ground surfaces and on the constructed fills and backfills at the site Test
locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey

occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork may
have been performed without our observation However the level of earthwork
observation and testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some
of the geotechnical risks to the project It should be recognized that there are variations
in the accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the
earthwork construction and materials
