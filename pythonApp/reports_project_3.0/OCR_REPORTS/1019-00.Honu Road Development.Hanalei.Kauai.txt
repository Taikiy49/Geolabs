Gentlemen
As requested we have performed a soil investigation at the
above referenced project site to evaluate the subgrade soil
conditions and to provide design recommendations for site
grading of the proposed road realignment
Our investigation which was performed in accordance with
our proposal dated March 2 1981 consisted of four borings
drilled along the proposed new road alignment visual obser
vations of the site and laboratory soil testing
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests
which were used in the engineering analysis to formulate
our conclusions and recommendations are presented in the
appendices of the report
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations

SITE DISCRIPTION
The existing Hanalei Plantation Road extends from Kuhio Highway
generally in a westward direction The proposed realignment is
planned near the western end of the existing road
The existing road is about 15 feet wide and paved with asphaltic
concrete
The proposed realignment would shift a portion of the existing
road to the north and would be approximately 1400 feet long
The existing ground along the proposed alignment slopes down
toward the north The ground surface in the eastern and western
portions are relatively gently sloping with Slopes generally
less than 5 1 H V The central portion of the alignment is
Situated at the top of a large drainageway with steep sided
Slopes The ground surface along this portion of the alignment
are generally as steep as 2 1 The side slopes of the drainage
way adjacent to this portion of the alignment generally vary
The ground elevation along the alignment generally varies from
about 105 to 160 feet
The site is covered with grass and many mango and Hau trees

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration encountered stiff to hard soils at the
site which would provide adequate subgrade support for the pro
posed roadway fill and pavement structure
The central portion of the alignment approximately
to is situated adjacent to the very steep 1 1 H V
side slope of the natural drainageway In order to reduce pos
sible loss of support of the roadway by erosion and surface
slope creep the road pavement should be setback a minimum dis
tance of about 10 feet from the top of the slope

It is also very difficult to properly compact small fill on
sloping ground Sliver fills Therefore if practicable
these sliver fills should be avoided Instead retaining walls

Site Grading
The site should be cleared of vegetation surface
debris and other deleterious material In areas to
receive fill the ground should be scarified to a
minimum depth of 6 inches brought to near optimum
moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90
density
Fill Placement
The on site cut material from the upper areas are
suitable for use in compacted fill and backfill
After site preparation the fill should be placed in
maximum 10 inch lifts compacted to a minimum of 90
of its maximum dry density established by ASTM test
designation D 1557 70
The natural moisture contents of the in situ soils
vary from about 30 to 50 generally increasing with
depth as compared to about 30 for the optimum mois
ture content Therefore some amount of drying of the

Slopes
Two horizontal to 1 vertical 2 1 fill slopes are
recommended
Cut slope ratios up to 1 horizontal to 1 vertical
1 1 can be used for cuts into the very stiff resid

able to reduce erosion

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that stripping excavations benching and
filling operations be observed by the soils engineer during

the above inspections

APPENDIX A
FIELD_EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by
drilling 4 borings to depths of 21 5 feet below the existing
ground surface at the approximate locations shown on the
Site Plan Plate 1 Truck mounted drilling equipment was
used to drill the borings
The soils encountered in the borings were classified
by visual and textural examination in the field by our engin
eering geologist who continuously monitored the drilling oper
ations The classifications were reviewed by visual inspec
tion and testing in the laboratory The soils were classified
in general conformance with the Unified Soil Classification
System A graphic presentation of the soils is presented on
the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 4
In the borings soil samples were obtained by driving either
rel sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The
blow counts to drive the sampler the last 12 inches are shown
on the boring logs at the appropriate depths

APPENDIX B
LABORATORY TESTING
Moisture content unit weight and Atterberg Limit
determinations were performed on select samples as an aid
in the evaluation and classification of soil properties
The results of these tests are presented on the Boring Logs
at the appropriate sample depths
Torvane shear strength tests were performed on sel
ected samples The test results are presented on the Boring
Logs One direct shear was performed on a selected undis
turbed sample to evaluate the strength characteristics of
the subsoils This test result is plotted on Plate B 2
To obtain the moisture density relationship of the
upper on site soil as a possible source of fill material
one ASTM D 1557 test Modified Proctor curve was developed
The result is summarized on Plate B l
Three CBR tests were performed on bulk samples of the
on site upper material to evaluate the material as a source
of pavement support The tests are summarized on Plate B 1
