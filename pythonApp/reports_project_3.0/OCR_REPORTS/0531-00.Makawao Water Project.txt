This report presents the results of our soil investigation
at the proposed water tank site in
The purposes of our study were to investigate the subsurface
conditions at the tank site determine whether the proposed
foundation design was adequate and to provide recommenda
tions to complete the planned water tank construction
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed 1 0 million gallon reservoir structure located
on a graded site is entirely in cut The cut ranges from a
maximum of eleven feet to zero feet at its northeastern
section The reservoir will be of reinforced concrete con
struction 22 feet high and about 97 feet in diameter

SITE CONDITIONS
The proposed reservoir site is located makai of an asphalt
paved roadway running mauka from Makawao Avenue The site is
generally level and it has been cut from a gently sloping hill
side terrain The 11 foot high cut bank located on the south
western end of the site is at a 1 horizontal to 1 vertical
slope The cut slope daylights at contour Elevation 1684 feet
at the northeastern perimeter of the tank structure near its
washout location Boring 1
SOIL CONDITIONS
The southern end of the site is covered by approximately 2 to
3 feet of sandy silt material underlain with highly weathered
and decomposed basalt rock material The surface soil at the
existing cut area is generally dry and silty The exposed
surface soil is non expansive The three borings which were
drilled on the northeastern section of the tank site generally
indicates the surface soils are highly decomposed rock material
which consists mainly of sandy silts and rock fragments under
lain by highly weathered and decomposed basalt rock material
down to a depth of approximately 15 feet No groundwater was
observed during our field exploration

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
It is our opinion that the site is suitable for the planned
tank construction with columns and perimeter wall footings
being supported on shallow conventional spread footings
However due to the highly weathered nature of the upper sur
face soil near the northeastern end of the site and to main
tain a minimum ten foot clearance from edge of footing to
slope surface it is recommended that the portion of the
perimeter wall footing between Contour Elevation 1684 and 1686
be over excavated a minimum of two feet below the footings and
replaced with lean concrete or compacted with a well graded
granular material
Foundation
Except for the above mentioned section of the wall footing the
tank structure may be supported on spread and continuous foot
ings bottomed a minimum of 15 inches below final adjacent grade
Due to the erratic condition of the upper layer of the decom
posed and weathered rock formation and to the fact that the
northeastern section of the perimeter wall footing is located
at the daylight area between cut and natural sloping surface
it is our recommendation that this section of the perimeter
wall footing be over excavated below its present planned eleva
tion and replaced with lean concrete The over excavation

should extend a minimum of 2 feet below its planned footing
bottom elevation Elevation 1681 75 The approximate loca

feet wide
Additional Site Preparations
The pockets of clayey surface soils with traces of tree roots
should be removed at the time of the tank footing and slab
on grade excavations Because the onsite material is dry and
sandy water should be sprinkled over the entire tank site
to bring the surface soils to its optimum moisture content
before tamping or proof rolling Also some excavated materials
have been cast over the existing northeastern slope This silty
overcast material is loose and dry therefore it should be
removed Any additional required fill should be keyed into
original ground and compacted in layers to a minimum of 90
of laboratory maximum densities per ASTM D 1557 70
The onsite materials are generally silty and all slopes should
be provided erosion control measures such as slope planting
and interceptor ditches
