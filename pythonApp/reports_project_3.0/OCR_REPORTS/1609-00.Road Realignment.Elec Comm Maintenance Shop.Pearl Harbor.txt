In accordance with your request we have performed a geotechnical
engineering investigation for the subject project The approximate
location of the project site is shown on the Project Location Map
Plate 1 Our work was performed in a general accordance with our
proposal of August 6 1985
The purpose of the investigation was to explore the subsurface
conditions at the site to provide recommendations for use in the site
grading design of the roadway system and related facilities
W O 1609 00 Page 2
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests which we
used in the engineering analyses to formulate our conclusions and recom
mendations are presented in the appendices of this report
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located on the western edge of Hickam Air Force
Base between Vickers Avenue and the end of Bishop Point Road The ocean
shoreline is about 500 feet west of the site
The topography at the site is relatively flat The existing ground
elevations vary from about 5 to 8 feet above MSL
The proposed road realignment will be in front of an electronic
communications maintenance shop
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project is to construct a new roadway about 1 200 feet in
length to connect the existing road systems Minor grading work is
anticipated

Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling
four 4 borings to a depth of 10 feet below the existing ground surface
at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 Soil
samples obtained from the borings were selectively tested in our
laboratory to further examine their geotechnical engineering
characteristics Detailed descriptions of our field exploration and
laboratory test results are presented in the appendices
Soil Conditions
The borings encountered weathered coral formation consisting of
generally medium dense gravelly sand with silt above water table This
material grades to loose below the water table
Groundwater was encountered at about 4 5 to 6 5 feet below the
existing ground surface at the time of our field exploration
Detailed descriptions of the materials encountered are presented on
the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 4

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
In general our borings encountered competent sandy subsoils and
weathered coral formation at the site which can provide adequate support
for the proposed road pavements
Roadway Pavement Section
Asphaltic conerete pavements to accommodate some semi trailer truck
traffic are planned for the project
In general relatively competent subgrade soil conditions were
encountered at the proposed roadway locations Medium dense coral
gravelly sand was encountered at the ground surface of the site There
fore if properly prepared and compacted as specified in the Site
Grading section the in situ soils can provide suitable subgrade support
for the proposed road
The following parameters were used in the pavement thickness design
in accordance with the method in NAVFAC DM 5 4
Subgrade Design CBR 25
Design Index 5 Daily Equivalent Axle
Load 251 to 900
Base Course 6 Inch Minimum Thickness
compacted to 100 percent
of maximum dry density

Based on the above the recommended pavement section is as follows

6 Inch Base Course
8 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
Site Grading
Prior to grading all deleterious materials such as vegetation
debris unsuitable fill and organically contaminated soils should
be removed and disposed of properly off site Following the
clearing the ground surface should be scarified to a depth of 6
inches moisture conditioned to optimum moisture content and
recompacted to a minimum of 95 of its maximum dry density as
established by ASTM D 1557 78
Any soft weak or pumping areas disclosed during the recompaction
operations should be excavated to firm ground and backfilled with
select on site material or non expansive import fill material
compacted to the above specified density
Fill Material and Placement
The on site coral sand and gravel soils are suitable for structural
fill material Imported fill material should be non expansive
GEOLABS HAWAIH

soil free of organic matter and form a dense stable soil mass
when compacted to the required density Generally the particle
size should be less than 3 inches in size The pavement designs
presented in this report were based on a subgrade CBR value of 25
and therefore the material placed within the top 3 feet of the
fill under the pavements should have a soaked CBR of at least 25
per ASTM D 1883 73 when compacted to 95 of the maximum densities
determined according to ASTM D 1557 78 The total expansion of
sample during the soaking for CBR test should be less than 1
The fills should be placed in horizontal lifts not exceeding 8
inches in compacted thickness and compacted to a minimum of 95 of
its maximum dry density
Design Review
Plans and specifications for site grading foundation and pavement
design should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written
comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our recom
mendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the site clearing and grubbing fill
placement and compaction foundation excavation backfilling and subgrade
