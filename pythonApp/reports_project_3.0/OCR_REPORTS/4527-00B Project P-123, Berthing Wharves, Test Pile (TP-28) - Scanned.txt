In accordance with the project specifications one of the three compressive pile load
tests was conducted on a selected test pile designated as TP 28 for the MCON Project
P 123 Berthing Wharves project between December 7 and 9 2000 This letter report
presents the results of our field observations and the test data obtained during the pile load
test
The pile load test was conducted on a inch square precast prestressed
concrete pile Test Pile TP 28 is located at Bent 36 and between Lines C and D The test
pile was driven on using a Delmag D 36 hammer delivering a maximum
rated energy of 113 160 Joules 83 820 foot pounds During the test pile driving a record
of the blow counts and field observations of the driving behavior was maintained by a project
engineer Geotechnical QC Specialist from our office The test pile driving record is
provided based on metric units Plate 1 1 and English units Plate 1 2
Test Pile TP 28 was driven to a depth of about 22 7 meters 74 5 feet below the
existing deck surface The test pile was driven to a depth when approximately 14 6 meters
48 feet of the pile was left above the existing deck surface Approximately 13 4 meters
44 feet of the top of the test pile was saw cut in order to set up the load test frame over the
test pile Therefore the total length of the load test pile was 23 9 meters 78 5 feet
measured from the top to the tip of the test pile
The test pile was loaded in general accordance with the Standard Loading Procedure
of ASTM Test Designation D 1143 The design load of the pile was 300 kips with
a design factor of safety of 2 25 Therefore the maximum test load on the pile was planned
at 675 kips In general the test pile was loaded to the maximum test load of
675 Kips in kip load increments Each load increment was held for
at least 15 minutes or until the rate of pile head movement was generally stable After
loading the test pile to the maximum test load of 675 kips the maximum test load
was held for 24 hours as specified Subsequently the test load was unloaded in
