INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed improvements at the existing Heeia Corporation Yard in
Heeia Kaneohe on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location of the site and its
vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2647 00 Page 2
This report summarizes our findings resulting from the exploration and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analyses based on the data
obtained during the exploration and the results of laboratory tests These
recommendations are intended for design input and are not intended for construction
estimation and bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a new office building and storeroom complex for use
by the City and County of Honolulu Board of Water Supply at the existing Heeia
Corporation Yard in Heeia Kaneohe Oahu Hawaii Two schematic layouts for the
improvements have been proposed for consideration Scheme 1 includes a single
structure housing both the administrative and personnel support functions and the
storage area Scheme 2 includes a separate storage structure located approximately 30
feet west of the administrative structure The proposed improvements under both
schemes will cover approximately ft in floor area
Based on the information provided to us the buildings will be single story wood
or metal framed construction with exterior CMU walls Concrete slabs on grade will be
utilized for the floors Construction of new utility lines and asphalt pavements are included
in the proposed construction
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions at the project site in order to develop a generalized subsurface data set for the
formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to site grading
foundation and pavement design for the project

scope of work included the following

of surficial soil conditions and to select sites for borings

5 Engineering analyses of the field and laboratory data for the purpose of

SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed project site is situated adjacent to and north of the King Intermediate
School on the windward side of the island of Oahu Hawaii The site is part of the existing
Heeia Corporation Yard and is situated along the high side of an existing retaining wall
2 to five 5 feet high which provides grade separation between the Corporation Yard
and the King Intermediate School property
The site of the improvements is situated within a large open storage area and
covers incidentally part of the vehicular access there to The area is paved with asphalt
concrete except that the proposed storage structure in Scheme 2 is presently a grassed
lawn
The topography of the proposed site is generally level except that the storage
structure in Scheme 2 is sited on an area approximately three feet lower than the existing
elevation of the proposed administrative building site
An existing retaining wall which varies from two 2 to five 5 feet in height
provides grade separation between the proposed site and the adjacent King Intermediate
School property The topography is generally level on the low side of the retaining wall
except at the northeast end where the terrain starts to slope gently away from the
proposed site at about ten horizontal to one vertical 10H 1V

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration generally encountered stiff soils near the surface It is our
opinion that the stiff on site soils can provide adequate bearing support for the proposed
structures and pavements
Our laboratory testing indicates that these soils have variable expansion potential
ranging from low to moderate Therefore special grading procedures will be required to
reduce potential structural distress caused by swelling of the foundation soils
Site Grading
Minor site grading work is anticipated for the project
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2647 00 Page 6
Site grading work should include clearing and grubbing over excavation of the
areas below the building foundations slabs and pavements recompaction of the resulting
subgrade level and replacement of the excavated material with suitable granular material
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
cleared of vegetation debris rubbish and other deleterious materials These materials
should be removed and properly disposed of off site
Due to the expansion potential of the subsoils encountered near the anticipated
finish subgrade levels we recommend that the areas below the footings floor slabs and
pavements be over excavated to a depth of at least twelve 12 inches below the
proposed levels of the foundations floor slabs and pavement sections
When the over excavation reaches this indicated level all structural and pavement
areas should be scarified to a depth of six 6 inches moisture conditioned and
recompacted to 90 of the maximum dry density as determined by ASTM Test Method
D 1557 78 Any loose or soft spots encountered should be over excavated down to firm
material and backfilled with compacted non expansive select material
Imported fill material to replace the over excavated material should consist of
non expansive select granular material with no particles exceeding three 3 inches
maximum size and with less than 20 passing the 200 sieve All imported fill material
should be tested and approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to transportation to the
site
The replacement fill should be placed in maximum 10 inch thick loose level lifts and
compacted to a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density as determined by ASTM
Test Method D 1557 78

Foundations
Spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the proposed structure The
subgrades of all footings should be scarified and recompacted to a minimum of 90 of
its maximum dry density as determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 An allowable
bearing value of 2500 p s f may be used for footings bearing on the compacted select
fill material or on stiff natural ground The bearing value is for total dead plus live loads
and may be increased by one third when temporary wind and seismic loads are
considered Estimated settlements are anticipated to be on the order of less than
one half inch total and one fourth inch differential
Individual footings and continuous wall footings should be embedded a minimum
of 24 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade
If foundations are located next to utility trenches or easements they should be
embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane extending
upward from the bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility
lines
The bottom of all footing excavations should be observed by the geotechnical
engineer prior to placement of the reinforcement to determine if adequate bearing has
been obtained and that loose soils are removed prior to concrete placement
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive earth pressure
developed against the buried vertical faces of the foundation system and by frictional
resistance developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be calculated using an equivalent fluid
pressure of 300 p s f per foot of depth This assumes that the soil around the foundation
is well compacted A coefficient of friction of 0 4 may be used Unless covered by

pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the upper 18 inches of soil should be
neglected
Pavement Design
New asphaltic concrete pavement is indicated to be provided as a part of the new
improvements
For preliminary design purposes the following pavement section is recommended
2 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 Inch Base Course
6 Inch Select Borrow
14 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
on Compacted Subgrade
CBR and density tests should be performed on the actual subgrade soils
encountered during construction to verify the adequate of the above sections The
recommended section considers medium traffic and assumes that adequate drainage will
be provided in the paved areas
Utility Trench
New utility lines are anticipated for the project A granular bedding consisting of
six 6 inches of 3B Fine gravel or ASTM C 33 No 67 is recommended under the pipes
and up to an elevation of at least one half the pipe diameter
Granular materials should be used for the trench backfill The backfill up to about
one foot above the pipes should be lightly tamped to reduce the possibility of damaging
the pipes The upper six 6 inches to one 1 foot of the trench backfill may be
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2647 00 Page 9
composed of the clayey materials found on site to minimize infiltration of surface water
into the subgrade
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level one foot above the pipes to
the bottom of the pavement sections should be placed in maximum 10 inch horizontal
lifts mechanically compacted to a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density as
determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 to reduce future ground subsidence
Drainage
The finished grade outside the building should be shaped to shed water away from
foundations and to avoid ponding Also it is recommended that gutter systems be
installed around the buildings and that the discharge be diverted away from the
foundations and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near foundations and
slabs should also be avoided
Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have concrete bottoms and
drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the above
foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause excessive subsurface soil
saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations
should be properly backfilled tight against the wall or slab footings immediately after
setting of the concrete to prevent ponding
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and maintained to drain
all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundations

Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be forwarded to the
geotechnical engineer for review and written comments prior to construction This review
is needed to determine adherence to the earthwork and foundation recommendations
given herein If this review is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume
responsibility for misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Observation
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during site grading and construction of the foundations
This is to observe compliance with the design concepts specifications or
recommendations and to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions
differ from that anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in
this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and approved by
the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
lf actual exposed subsurface conditions encountered during construction are
different from those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications
to the design should be made
