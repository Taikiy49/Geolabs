In accordance with your request we have performed a soil and
foundation investigation at the above referenced site The scope of work
performed was in general accordance with our revised proposal dated April
25 1986
The purpose of our soil engineering investigation was to explore the
subsurface conditions at the two proposed sites in order to provide
recommendations for the design of the foundations for the three proposed
reservoirs The general location of the project area is shown on the
Project Location Map Plate l
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests which we
used in the engineering analysis to formulate our conclusions and
recommendations are presented in the appendices of this report

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed project consists of the following
1 Two 5 0 M G concrete reservoirs each approxmiately 208 feet in
diameter and 20 feet high Only one reservoir will be
constructed at the present time The second structure will be
constructed at a future date Column loads of about 100 kips
and perimeter wall loads of about 5 3 kips per foot are
anticipated
Cuts into the hillside from about 7 feet eastern side up to
about 20 feet western side are planned to create a level pad
area for the proposed initial reservoir Cuts from 20 feet up
to 27 feet are anticipated for the future reservoir
2 A 1 0 M G concrete reservoir approximately 100 feet in
diameter and 20 feet high Column loads of about 100 kips and
perimeter wall loads of about 2 2 kips per foot are anticipated
Cuts into the hillside from 6 feet up to 10 feet are planned to
create a level pad area for the reservoir

SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed site for the two 5 0 M G reservoirs is located in a
cane field approximately 300 feet south of Honouliuli Stream and 200 feet
north of the H 1 Freeway Waipahu is about 2 miles east of the site
The topography at the 5 0 M G reservoirs site is generally flat
sloping slightly to the east The site is in recently harvested cane
fields that are accessible to truck mounted drilling equipment by means of
cane haul roads The ground elevations at the site generally vary from
about 210 to 235 feet above MSL
The 1 0 M G reservoir site is located about 4500 feet up slope
north of the 5 0 M G reservoirs and is also located along the edge of
a cane field that is accessible by cane haul roads The ground elevations
at this site vary from about 412 to 430 feet above MSL
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Two 5 0 M G Reservoir Sites
The soil conditions at the site were explored by drilling and
sampling eight 8 borings to depths of 20 feet to 58 feet The borings
disclosed that the site is situated at the edge of a filled stream bed
and 3 at the initial reservoir site disclosed a 5 to 10 feet
thick surface layer of clayey silt alluvium over basalt boulders that
extend to the bottom of the borings up to 58 feet deep The boulders
appear to be packed tightly with only a little matrix soil between
boulders and 6 at the future reservoir site encountered
blue vesicular basalt formation beneath the layer of weathered basalt
cobbles and boulders at different depths 5 to 22 feet indicating that
the basalt formation slopes downwards in the eastern direction
Additional borings 7 and 8 were drilled in order to delineate the basalt

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Initial 5 0 M G Reservoir
The borings disclosed that the reservoir site is situated over a
thick layer of boulders extending a minimum of 20 feet below the reservoir
finish elevation Based on the boring information the boulders appear to
be tightly packed with only a little soil matrix Therefore it is our
opinion that the reservoir may be constructed on the dense boulder layer
Foundation
The boulder surface at the finished grade will be irregular and
therefore we recommend that the boulders be over excavated a
minimum of 2 feet below the footings and slab and replaced with
compacted select borrow to provide a uniform bearing layer A
polyethylene moisture barrier is recommended over the gravel fill to
minimize possible seepage below the reservoir tank
Spread and continuous wall footings bearing on the compacted gravel
is recommended for the reservoir An allowable bearing value of
3500 p s f may be used for footings bearing on these soils

The allowable bearing value may be increased by one third for
temporary wind and seismic loads
Footings should be embedded a minimum of 16 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grade Footings located next to utility trenches
should extend down to the bottom of the utility trench and bear on
stiff undistrubed soil or the trench backfill should be
well compacted to minimize footing settlement
The site grading should be designed to divert surface water away
from the reservoir foundations
Settlement
Based on the boring laboratory test data and estimated reservoir
loads the anticipated settlements are on the order of less than
one half of an inch total and one fourth of an inch differential
Lateral Resistance
For passive earth pressure an equivalent fluid pressure of 350
p s f per foot of depth can be used A coefficient of friction of
0 4 can be utilized to determine sliding resistance of the structure
and foundation
Future 5 0 M G Reservoir
The borings disclosed that at the present location the eastern edge
of the future reservoir will be underlain by boulders while the remainder
of the tank will bear on rock formation In order to prevent excessive
differential settlement we recommend that the future reservoir location
be moved towards the west so that the entire reservoir can bear on rock

Foundation
Spread and continuous wall footings bearing on the basalt rock is
recommended for the reservoir An allowable bearing value up to
6000 p s f may be used for footings bearing on the rock formation
The allowable bearing value may be increased by one third for
temporary wind and seismic loads
Footings should be embedded a minimum of 16 inches below the lowest

Footings located next to utility trenches should extend down to the

The site grading should be designed to divert surface water away

All trench backfill away from the reservoir foundation should be
properly compacted to obtain a minimum of 95 of the maximum dry
density per ASTM D 1557 78 test method
To reduce the possibility of footing settlement due to possible
cavities or lava tubes in the underlying lava rock the footing
excavation should be probed at approximately 10 feet on centers
The probe holes should have a minimum diameter of 2 inches and
extend below the bottom of footing a depth of 6 feet The probe

foundation support for the proposed reservoir

Site Grading
In general the site grading at the reservoir sites will consist of
cutting the hillsides from 6 to 30 feet for the 5 0 M G reservoir sites
and about 6 to 10 feet for the 1 0 M G reservoir site

Site Preparation
The site to be graded should be cleared of all vegetation organic
matter debris and soft material
Backfill
If required backfill should consist of select granular material
such as base course or select borrow The backfill should be
compacted to a minimum of 95 of its maximum dry density established
by ASTM D 1557 78
Slopes
For the proposed reservoir sites the hillsides behind the tanks
will be cut for a vertical height of about 6 to 30 feet
Several different soil conditions are anticipated in the cut
slopes Alluvium over boulders are anticipated at the initial 5 0
M G reservoir site while a layer of soil and boulders over rock
formation is anticipated at the future 5 0 M G reservoir site
Stiff residual soil is anticipated at the 1 0 M G reservoir site
The recommended cut slope ratios are as follows
Alluvium and boulders
Basalt rock formation H V
Stiff Residual Soil
Slope benches are recommended for slopes in excess of 20 feet in
vertical height

Excavation
The field exploration indicates that the two 5 0 M G reservoir
sites are underlain at fairly shallow depths by dense basalt and
cobbles and boulders
Relatively hard ripping is anticipated in the boulders and basalt
formation The more massive basalt in the future reservoir cut
slope will likely require special excavating procedures such as hoe
ram equipment or blasting to make the proposed cut Therefore we
recommend that the site grading for the future reservoir be
completed in conjunction with the initial reservoir site work to
minimize the possibility of damage to the reservoir structure caused
by excessive vibrations for the rock excavation
The above discussions regarding the rippability of the subsurface
materials are based on our visual observations of the existing rock
outcrops in the area and interpretation of the boring core data
We recommend that all prospective contractors bidding on this site
should examine the rock outcrops and the boring data and to make
their own interpretation
Relatively easy excavation is anticipated for the 1 0 M G reservoir
site
Service Road Pavements
For the light automobile traffic anticipated for the service roadway
around the perimeter of the tank the following pavement thickness may be
used based on the results of the borings and visual observations
2 Inch Asphaltic concrete
6 Inch Base Course
8 Inch Total Pavement Thickness

Design Review
Plans for the site grading and foundation construction should be
forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written comments prior to
construction to see that the intent of our recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during
earthwork construction particularly slope cuts to monitor the
contractor s grading operations
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be observed by a
soils engineer during construction to see that adequate bearing has been
obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the
findings of our investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
If conditions encountered during construction are different from
those presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made

Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling ten
10 borings to depths of 25 to 58 feet below the existing ground surface
at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plans Plates 2 and 3 A
truck mounted drill rig was used to drill the borings
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by visual and
textural examination in the field by our engineer who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory All soils
were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A summary of the materials encountered are
presented in the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 10 1
Soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving a 2 inch
a 140 pound hammer free falling 30 inches The blow counts to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the Boring Logs at the appropriate
sample depths

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content and unit weight determinations were performed on
selected samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil
properties The results of these tests are presented on the Boring Logs
at the appropriate sample depths
Two direct shear tests were performed on selected undisturbed
samples to evaluate the shear strength parameters of the soil The test
results are plotted on Plates B 1 and B 2
Two one inch ring swell tests were performed on undisturbed and
remolded samples to evaluate the swelling potential of the soils in both
natural and compacted conditions The test results are summarized on
Plate B 3
