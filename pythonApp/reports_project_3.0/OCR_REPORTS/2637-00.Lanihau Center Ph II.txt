PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface soil
and rock conditions at the site in order to provide subsurface data for use in the design
of the site grading building foundation and pavement design for the proposed
development The general location of the site and its vicinity is shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1
The scope of our exploration consisted of the following tasks and work efforts
1 Review of available soils geological and geotechnical engineering data and
literature pertinent to the project site and its vicinity
2 Reconnaissance of the project site and vicinity to make visual observations
of surficial soil conditions and select sites for borings
3 Mobilization and demobilization of a refraction seismograph with operators
to and from the project site
4 Performance of seismic refraction survey on the project site
5 Mobilization and demobilization of a truck mounted drill rig to and from the
project site
6 Drilling of four 4 borings of up to 32 feet in depth with a truck mounted
drilling rig

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to grade and develop the site for a commercial center The general
site plan indicates that three large buildings encompassing a total area of approximately
ft and associated retail shops storage and rental space encompassing an
area of approximately ft and up to four separate pads with a total area of
approximately ft are to be constructed In addition parking and infrastructure
facilities including a proposed Henry Street along the east property limits connecting
Kuakini Highway to the south with Queen Kaahumanu Highway to the north are
proposed
Details of the proposed site grading were not available at the time of our
exploration However based on the topography of the site we anticipate that cuts and
fills on the order of up to thirty 30 feet in depth or thickness will be required to create
level pads for the construction of the proposed buildings
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located in the town of Kailua Kona in the District of North Kona
on the island of Hawaii and is bounded to the west by the existing Lanihau Shopping
Center Phase and to the north by Queen Kaahumanu Highway The site is located in
raw undeveloped land with the exception of an area in the west and north central portions
of the site which have been cleared to serve as a staging area for adjacent development
In addition it appears that some uncontrolled fills from adjacent construction projects may
have been placed in portions of the project site
The site grades from north to south with elevations ranging from 180 feet above
Mean Sea Level M S L along the northern limits to 40 feet above Mean Sea Level

M S L along the southern limits The site is moderately vegetated with Keawe trees and
grasses
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
Hawaii the largest island of the Hawaiian Archipelago covers an area of
approximately 400 square miles The island was formed by the activity of five 5 shield
volcanoes These are Kohala which is long extinct Mauna Kea which has had activity
during recent geologic time Hualalai which last erupted in 1801 and Mauna Loa and
Kilauea both still active
The site is located on the western flank of Hualalai mountain which makes up the
west central part of the island The lava formation encountered at the site appears to be
a pahoehoe flow which spread and ponded as it approached the ocean
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATION
The field exploration disclosed that the site is underlain by volcanic materials
consisting of moderately to widely fractured moderately hard to very hard lava rock
formations with a few lenses of consolidated clinker From a geotechnical engineering
point of view the site is suitable for the proposed commercial development provided that
our recommendations are followed
The volcanic materials should provide adequate bearing for the proposed
structures related roads and parking areas
We anticipate that the excavated materials will probably experience negligible
shrinkage during handling and compaction
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2637 00 Page 6
The excavated rock material is likely to break down to sizes ranging from large
boulders in excess of five feet in diameter to small cobbles and gravel with few fines
Cavities
Our reconnaissance and exploration encountered a small number of voids not
exceeding eighteen inches in vertical height However the potential for larger and more
frequent occurrence of cavities is not uncommon in the type of rock formation
encountered at the project site It is our opinion that proofrolling with a minimum of ten
10 passes with a heavy bulldozer such as a D9 would be sufficient to detect and
collapse most of the near surface voids below deep fill areas i e fills greater that five 5
feet thick Collapsed voids should be backfilled with rock fill or imported select granular
material placed as recommended in the Fill Placement section of this report
To detect and fill voids or cavities in the subgrade of foundations located in shallow
fill and cut areas we recommend that a program of probing and grouting be
implemented Probes should be drilled to a minimum depth of ten 10 feet below the
bottom of the foundations at each column footing location and on ten 10 foot intervals
below strip footings
The probe holes and any voids discovered should be backfilled with a mortar like
The probing and grouting should be performed under the observation of the
project geotechnical engineer so that the program may be modified if necessary

Site Grading
We anticipate that the major site grading work would consist of cuts and fills of up
to twenty 20 to thirty 30 feet deep or thick over parts of the project site generally with
cuts along the northern portion and fills in the south portions
Site Preparation
Prior to filling the area to receive fill should be cleared of all deleterious materials
including surface vegetation and removed for the project site In areas to receive
fill the subgrade should be proofrolled with a D 9 or similar heavy equipment to
detect and collapse near surface voids Any loose areas or voids disclosed during
site preparation should be repaired by removing the loose materials and or
collapsing the void then backfilling with suitable compacted on site materials
Fill Material
Previously placed fill was noted in portions of the site We recommend that the
previously placed material be removed to original ground and recompacted as
specified in this report The approximate limits of the fill is shown on the Site Plan
Plate 1
Generally the on site material less than 18 inches in size will be suitable for fill
Large rock fragments in excess of 18 inches in size should be crushed down to
smaller sizes or disposed of in fill sections deeper than 5 feet in accordance with
the procedures recommended under the subsequent section Boulder Disposal
Imported fill material required for the embankment fill should be well graded
granular rocky materials The maximum rock size should generally be less than
18 inches Samples of any imported material should be submitted to the project
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2637 00 Page 8
geotechnical engineer for determination of its suitability prior to being transported
to the site
The upper two 2 feet of fill to finish subgrade should consist of well graded
granular material with sufficient fines to prevent the occurrence of voids in the
compacted mass If the excavated rock material does not contain sufficient fines
to produce a well graded material off site cinders or soil may be mixed into the
excavated rocky material to increase the percentage of fines to provide a better
graded material for compaction and increase the stability of the fills
Boulder Disposal
Boulders i e rock fragments larger than 18 inches in size encountered during site
grading and in excavations may be used in the deeper fills provided that the
following recommendations are followed
1 Boulders should be spread out and must not be nested together They
should be placed so that compaction equipment will be able to compact
between and around them
2 No part of the boulder should encroach any closer to the finish subgrade
elevation than the boulder s largest dimension and in no case be closer
than three 8 feet to finished subgrade
3 No boulder larger than three 8 feet in its largest dimension should be
placed in the fill It may be necessary to reduce the size of larger boulders
or dispose of them off site
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2637 00 Page 9
4 Care must be exercised to avoid placement of boulders in the proposed
utility alignment to prevent difficulty in the later excavation of the utility
trenches
Fill Placement
Embankment fills should be placed in horizontal loose lifts up to a maximum height
of 18 inches thick and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry
density dry as determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Conventional compaction testing is generally not practicable in excavated volcanic
rock material due to the large particle size Instead a testing program consisting
of visual observations and test strips to determine the lift thickness and number
of passes of a compactor required to achieve the desired compaction should be
conducted at the start of the project The number of passes may then be used as
the criteria for compaction
Excavation
The field exploration disclosed that the site is underlain by moderately to widely
fractured volcanic rock ranging from moderately hard to very hard Itis anticipated
that the volcanic rock may be excavated with normal heavy excavating methods
i ripping with large bulldozers and or large backhoes at near surface
elevations However deeper formations may require the use of hoerams chipping
or blasting

The above discussions regarding the rippability of the surface materials are based
on our visual observation of the existing lava formations and field data from the
borings performed at the site
We recommend that all contractors proposing to bid on this project be encouraged
to examine the site conditions and the boring data to make their own interpretation
In general two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V slopes are recommended for fill
construction
It appears that most of the cut slopes will be in dense rock An average overall
design cut slope of one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V is recommended for cuts
up to 40 feet high Steeper cut slopes may be provided on an individual basis
after we have an opportunity to review and examine the nature of the individual cut
areas
Foundations
We believe the on site rocky ground and compacted granular fill resulting from the
above site grading will provide suitable foundation support for the proposed buildings
Spread column and or continuous line footing foundations may be used for the
proposed structures
To provide uniform support for foundations bearing on both cut and fill we
recommend that the cut areas be ripped or scarified to 12 inches below footing subgrade
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2637 00 Page 11
and then compacted to a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density ASTM Test
Method D 1557 78
All exterior footing bottoms should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below
the lowest adjacent finished grade The minimum embedment for interior footings may
be reduced to 12 inches below the finished grade
For foundations bearing on compacted rock fill an allowable net soil bearing
pressure of up to 3000 p s f may be used The allowable bearing value may be
increased to 6000 p s f on hard rock formation These values may be increased by one
third when temporary wind or seismic loads are considered
Slabs and spread footing foundations located close to the tops of slopes should
have sufficient embedment to obtain a minimum horizontal set back distance of ten 10
feet measured from the outer edge of the footing to the slope face
The ground surface should be shaped to divert surface runoff away from the
building foundations and slabs
It is anticipated that the footings if constructed in accordance with the above
recommendations will have negligible settlements
Slabs On Grade
Based on the non expansive slab subgrade conditions anticipated at this site on
grade slabs should be placed over a 4 inch layer of base course compacted to 90 of
its maximum density as determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 The thickened
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2637 00 Page 12
edge around the on grade slab should extend a minimum of 12 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished ground surface
To reduce potential differential settlement where the slab is partly on cut in rock
and partly on fill the cut portions should be over excavated about one foot and then
recompacted to match the adjacent subgrade density
Utilities
It is anticipated that most of the trenching will be done in hard basalt formations
Trench backfill should be granular material generally less than six 6 inches in size
and compacted by water and mechanical tamping to 90 of its maximum dry density as
determined by ASTM Test Method D1557 78 except for the top two 2 feet below
finished road subgrade which should be compacted to 95 of its maximum dry density
Pavement
Asphalt concrete pavements may be used for the roadways and parking areas
In general the on site smaller clinker materials will provide excellent subgrade support
when evenly mixed and compacted Assuming that either this material or imported select
granular fill with CBR greater than 25 are used in the pavement subgrade we
recommend that the following preliminary pavement design be used for cost estimating
purposes
2 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
4 Inch Base Course
6 Inch Total Pavement Thickness on
Compacted Subgrade

The subgrade soil under the pavement area should be compacted to at least 95
of the maximum density to a depth of six 6 inches below the bottom
of the base course
CBR tests and or field observations should be performed on the actual subgrade
soil samples during construction to verify that the above design sections are adequate
The recommended sections consider medium traffic and that good drainage will be
provided adjacent to paved areas
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be forwarded to the
geotechnical engineer for review and written comment prior to construction This review
is needed to determine adherence to the intent of the earthwork recommendations given
herein If this review is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility
for misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during construction of the foundation phases of the
work This is to observe compliance with the design concepts specifications or
recommendations and to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions
differ from that anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in
this report are contingent upon such observations
If the actual exposed rock or soil conditions encountered during construction are
different from those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications
to the design should be made

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based upon
information obtained form the geophysical surveys borings and visual observations
Variations of conditions may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may
not become evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident it
will be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations given in this report
The locations of the borings were determined by taping and triangulation from
reference points indicated on the plans provided to us by Towill Shigeoka Associates
Inc dated The physical location of the surveys and borings should
be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the borings depict the
approximate boundaries between the rock characteristics and as such may denote a
gradual transition
Water level data from the boring points were collected at the times given on the
graphic representations and or in the text of this report These data have been reviewed
and interpretations made in the formulation of this report However it must be noted that
fluctuation may occur due to variation in rainfall tides temperature and other factors

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by the drilling of four 4
borings to depths of up to 32 feet in depth with a truck mounted drill rig The
approximate locations of the borings are shown on the enclosed Site Plan Plate 2 The
soil and rock were classified by visual and textural examinations in the field by our field
engineer who monitored the drilling operations The classifications were reviewed by
visual inspection in the laboratory All soil rock was classified in accordance with the
Unified Soil Classification System
A graphic presentation of the conditions encountered in the borings is presented
on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 4 Rock core samples were obtained by using
a rotary wash NQ core barrel The core recovery and Rock Quality Designation RQD
are presented in the Boring Logs
In addition seismic refraction surveys were conducted at various locations at the
project site in order to evaluate the range of seismic velocities in the rock formation which
can generally be correlated to the rock hardness However due to the nature of the rock
formation i e with varying lenses of clinker particularly in the near surface areas the
surveys did not produce data which we feel is presentable in the format of this report
The surveys were affected by a large degree of scattering which generally produces
velocities which are unreliable
A reconnaissance of the surface soil rock conditions at the site was conducted and
the observations were mapped by a geologist from our firm Approximate locations of
previously placed fills are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
