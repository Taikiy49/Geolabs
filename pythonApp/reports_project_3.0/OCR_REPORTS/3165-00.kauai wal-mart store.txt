INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Kauai Wal Mart store located at the intersection of Kuhio

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed project consists of construction of a new retail store facility in Linue
on the island of Kauai Hawaii We understand the proposed retail store facility will
include construction of an approximately 93 000 square foot building with a paved parking
lot of approximately 245 000 square feet with 683 stalls as shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2
The proposed building superstructure will consist of a combination of load bearing
concrete block walls and steel columns to support the roof with steel joist girders and bar
joists The finished floor elevation is planned at about 220 feet Mean Sea Level MSL
It is also proposed to construct a below grade loading dock area for truck loading
The truck loading ramp area is anticipated to be a below grade ramp The height of
retaining wall associated with this structure is not known at this time We understand that
flexible asphaltic concrete pavements are planned for the parking lot areas and heavy
duty flexible and or rigid pavements are planned for the truck drive areas

Based on information provided by Wal Mart maximum column loads on the order
of 140 kips for dead plus live loads are anticipated for the proposed structure Maximum
column uplift loads are on the order of about 30 kips The maximum uniform load on
continuous footings is 6 kips per lineal foot The estimated maximum uniform floor slab
load is 125 pounds per square foot p s f with a maximum concentrated floor slab load
of 5 kips
Detailed grading plans for the project were not available at the time this report was

EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The proposed retail store facility is located about 14 miles west of the Lihue Airport
on the northwest side of Lihue on the island of Kauai Hawaii The site for the proposed
facility is bounded by the proposed KCOM Corporation commercial development to the
north by Kuhio Highway to the west by an unpaved cane haul road to the south and
by existing sugar cane fields to the east An existing irrigation ditch of about 14 feet wide
traverses the southeast corner of the site partially within the southeast corner of the
proposed building
The site is presently heavily vegetated with trees and grasses Based on our field
exploration and a topographic map entitled Topographic Survey of KCOM WAL MART
Development dated by Esaki Surveying and Mapping Inc the
topography of the site is relatively level with existing ground surface elevations of
approximately 215 to 222 feet Mean Sea Level MSL The site generally slopes down
toward the center of the site
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsoil conditions at the subject site were explored by drilling and sampling
a total of 13 borings to depths of about 6 to 26 feet below the existing ground surface
The approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Based on the 13 borings planned for the field exploration at the site we generally
encountered residual soils consisting of reddish brown silty clays and clayey silts from the
ground surface to the maximum depths explored The consistency of residual soils
ranged from stiff to very stiff and should be capable of supporting a shallow foundation
system for the proposed structure The residual soils grade to saprolite partially to highly
GEOLABS HAWAII

weathered rock with increasing depth Our borings were advanced to a maximum depth
of 2612 feet below the existing ground surface
The near surface soils appear to be dry to damp and grades to moist with
increasing depth Based on laboratory tests the near surface soils appear to be
moderately expansive when subjected to changes in the moisture content
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings drilled at the time of our field
exploration It should be noted that groundwater levels are subject to change due to
rainfall time of year and tidal fluctuations

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
In general our field exploration indicates that the proposed project site is underlain
by a layer of very stiff to hard residual clayey soil to depths of 7 to 18 feet below the
existing ground surface Based on our field exploration and laboratory testing we believe
that a shallow foundation system bearing on the very stiff residual soils and or compacted
engineered fill may be used to support the proposed building structure
Laboratory tests performed on the near surface clayey soils indicate that the
materials generally have a moderate shrink swell potential when subjected to fluctuations
in the moisture content Therefore concrete slabs on grade should be supported on
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3165 00 Page 7
12 inches of non expansive capping fill to reduce the potential effects of the moderately
expansive near surface soils
Proper backfilling of the irrigation ditch located at the southeast corner of the
proposed building will be necessary to provide suitable foundation for the proposed
building The irrigation ditch should be over excavated to remove all soft and or loose
materials and backfilled with compacted engineered fill

design site grading pavements and other geotechnical aspects of the project are
presented in the following sections of this report
Foundations
The proposed retail store facility will consist of a one story structure situated on a
relatively level site Based on a finish floor elevation of 220 feet MSL portions of the
building foundations will bear on the stiff residual soils and portions on engineered fill
needed to raise the grades at the site Based on the subsurface conditions encountered
in our exploration we believe that a shallow foundation system may be used to support
the structural loads generated by the proposed retail store facility Spread footings
bearing on the native residual soils and or compacted engineered fill may be used to
support the proposed building structure
An allowable bearing pressure of up to 3 500 p s f may be used for design of
spread footings bearing on the residual soils and or compacted engineered fill needed
to raise the grades The allowable bearing pressure is for dead plus live loads and may
be increased by one third for transient loadings such as temporary wind and or
seismic forces Footings should be embedded a minimum of 24 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grade For the anticipated maximum column loads of up to 140 kips
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3165 00 Page 8
we estimate total settlements of footings bearing on the residual soils and or compacted
engineered fill to be on the order of less than 1 inch with differential settlements of about
Y inch
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by frictional resistance
between the base of the spread footing and the bearing materials and by passive earth
pressure developed against the near vertical faces of the embedded portion of footings
A coefficient of friction of 0 35 may be used between the base of the footings and the
supporting residual soils Resistance due to passive pressure may be calculated using
an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth p c f for
footings embedded in the residual soils and engineered fill These values assume that
the concrete for footings is placed directly against the footing excavations Unless
covered by pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the upper 12 inches below
finished grade should be neglected
Where footings will be located adjacent to below grade structures or utilities the
footings should extend to a depth below an imaginary 45 degree plane projected upward
from the bottom of the below grade structure or utility line trench This requirement is
necessary to avoid surcharging adjacent below grade structures with additional structural
loads and to reduce the potential of foundation settlement
It is important that a representative of Geolabs Hawaii observe the footing
excavations to evaluate the competency of the bearing materials and the embedment
depths If unsuitable materials are encountered in the footing excavations these materials
should be over excavated to firm soils and replaced with compacted engineered fill

Slabs On Grade
We anticipate concrete slabs on grade will be used for the proposed retail store
structure The near surface soils encountered at the proposed building site consist of
residual soils which exhibit moderate expansion potential Based on this we recommend
fill to reduce the potential effects of the moderately expansive near surface soils The
non expansive fill should extend a minimum of 3 feet laterally beyond the limits of the
proposed building Requirements for moisture conditioning and compaction of fill
materials are discussed in the Grading section
We anticipate that some portions of the slabs may be subjected to forklift traffic
and will therefore function as rigid pavements Where slabs will be subjected to
vehicular traffic a 6 inch layer of base course compacted to 95 percent relative
compaction is recommended above the non expansive fill for more uniform support of the
slab A modulus of subgrade reaction of 250 pounds per square inch per inch of
deflection p c i may be used for design of structural slabs
We understand that it is Wal Mart s general policy to eliminate the vapor membrane
and capillary break below slabs Although the vapor membrane and capillary break are
not absolutely necessary it may be prudent to provide a vapor membrane and capillary
break below the slab considering the wet environment of this semi tropical area If a
capillary break is desired below slabs that are not subjected to vehicular traffic we
recommend that a 4 inch layer of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation be
used The capillary break would also serve as a cushion fill to provide more uniform
support of the slab A vapor membrane should be incorporated above the capillary break
to reduce potential damage to moisture sensitive floor coverings by infiltration of moisture
It is recommended that a 2 inch layer of moist fine sand be placed above the vapor
membrane to protect the membrane and to aid in concrete curing
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3165 00 Page 10
The base course should consist of crushed basalt aggregate compacted to a
minimum of 95 percent relative compaction For design of structural slabs a modulus of
subgrade reaction of 250 pounds per cubic inch p c i may be used for the compacted
base course The thickened edges of slabs adjacent to any unpaved area should be
embedded at least 12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade
Retaining Walls
We anticipate that retaining walls ranging up to about 5 feet in height will be
constructed for the truck loading ramp areas of this project In general we understand
that the retaining walls planned will be conventional concrete retaining walls Foundations
for retaining walls should be designed in accordance with the recommendations
presented in the Foundations section of this report
All retaining structures should be designed to resist the lateral earth pressures due
to adjacent soils and surcharge effects We understand that Wal Mart desires at rest
pressures The recommended at rest lateral earth pressure for design expressed in
equivalent fluid pressure is 55 p c f for an assumed level backfill Generally walls which
tops are not free to deflect beyond about 0 5 percent of the wall height or are restrained
should be designed for the at rest condition
The value provided above is for on site soils or imported granular soils Backfills
behind retaining walls should be moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the
optimum moisture content and compacted to between 90 and 95 percent relative
compaction Over compaction of the wall backfill should be avoided This lateral earth
pressure does not include hydrostatic pressures that might be caused by groundwater
trapped behind the walls
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3165 00 Page 11
Retaining walls should be well drained to reduce the build up of hydrostatic
pressures A typical drainage system would consist of a 1 foot to 2 foot wide zone of
permeable material No 3B Fine gravel or ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation immediately
adjacent to the wall with a perforated pipe perforations down at the base of the wall
discharging to a discharge facility A filter fabric such as Mirafi 140N or equivalent
should be placed between the drainage material and the compacted backfill material As
an alternative a prefabricated drainage product such as MiraDrain or EnkaDrain may be
used instead of the drainage material with filter fabric Tne prefabricated drainage product
should also be hydraulically connected to a perforated pipe at the base of the wall
Surcharge stresses due to areal surcharges line loads and point loads within a
horizontal distance equal to the height of the wall should be considered in the design
For uniform surcharge stresses imposed on the loaded side of the wall a rectangular
distribution with uniform pressure equal to 50 percent of the vertical surcharge pressure
acting on the entire height of the wall which is restrained at the top may be used in
design Additional analyses during design may be needed to evaluate the surcharge
effects of point loads and line loads

Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork all areas within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris rubbish and other
unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site to reduce
potential contamination of the excavated materials
After clearing and grubbing the exposed subgrade should be scarified to a
minimum depth of 8 inches properly moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent
above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent
relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil
expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil
determined in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91 Optimum
moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the
maximum dry density
Any soft weak or yielding areas disclosed during clearing operations should be
over excavated to expose firm ground and the resulting excavation should be
backfilled with engineered fill compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction Prior to placement of the backfill the over excavated subgrade
should be scarified to a depth of at least 8 inches moisture conditioned to at least
2 percent above the optimum moisture content and compacted to at least 90
percent relative compaction The excavated soft and or organic soils should be
properly disposed of off site Contract documents should include additive and
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3165 00 Page 13
deductive unit prices for over excavation and engineered fill construction to
account for variations in the over excavation quantities
The existing irrigation ditch located at the southeast corner of the proposed
building footprint should be properly backfilled The irrigation ditch should be
cleaned out and the sides and bottom of the ditch should be over excavated to
expose firm soils The over excavated materials should not be used as on site fill
materials and should be disposed of off site The resulting excavation should be
backfilled with engineered fill materials moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent
above the optimum moisture content and compacted to at least 90 percent relative
compaction Engineered fill should be keyed into the sides of the irrigation ditch
to bond the new fills to the existing ground
If off site select material is required the imported fill material should be tested and
approved by Geolabs Hawaii prior to delivery to the site to confirm that it is suitable
for its intended purpose The requirement for imported fill material is presented in
the following subsection Fills and Backfills
Fills and Backfills
In general the on site materials may be used as engineered fill and backfill
materials except where non expansive select fill is recommended It should be
emphasized that the on site silty and clayey soils should not be used as non
expansive fill below slabs on grade because of the moderately expansive nature
of the on site soils However the on site silty and clayey soils may be used as fill
material in other areas provided that they are properly moisture conditioned and
compacted as recommended herein
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3165 00 Page 14
Where used as fill material the on site soils should be placed in level lifts not
exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent
above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent
relative Compaction For pavement subgrade a minimum compaction of 95
percent relative compaction is required Backfills behind retaining walls should be
compacted to between 90 and 95 percent relative compaction Over compaction
of the retaining wall backfill would exert additional lateral earth pressures on the
walls and should be avoided
As previously indicated a 12 inch non expansive select fill layer will be required
below the floor slab within the building footprint We anticipate that import fill
material would be needed to provide the recommended 12 inch non expansive
select fill The non expansive select fill material should be well graded from coarse
to fine with no particles larger than 3 inches in largest dimension and should
contain a maximum of 20 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve All select
fill materials should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture
content and compacted to 95 percent relative compaction
Any imported soil should be select fill Import materials should be tested and
approved by Geolabs Hawaii prior to delivery to the job site Imported soils having
low expansion potential should be brought to above the optimum moisture content
and compacted to not less than 95 percent relative compaction All surfaces
should be finished to create smooth unyielding subgrades and should be kept
moist until covered by concrete slabs or pavements

Utility Trench
We envision that new utility lines may be required for the project A granular
bedding consisting of 6 inches of select borrow or No 3B Fine gravel is recommended
under the pipes Self compacting granular materials such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM
C 33 No 67 gradation should be used for the trench backfill up to about 12 inches
above the pipes to provide adequate support around the pipes and to reduce compaction
of the backfill thus reducing the potential for damaging the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 1 foot above the pipes to the
top of the subgrade or finished grade should consist of select granular material or the on
site soils The backfill should be moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent of the
optimum moisture placed in maximum 8 inch level loose lifts and mechanically
GEOLABS HAWAII

compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for
future ground subsidence Where trenches are below pavement areas the upper 2 feet

Drainage
The finished grades outside the building should be sloped to shed water away from
foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding Also it is advised that gutter
systems be installed around the building and that the discharge be diverted away from
the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near the foundations
and slabs should also be avoided Planters next to foundations should be avoided or
have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the above
foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation
and subsequent heaving because of the swelling potential of the near surface soils The
foundation excavations should be properly backfilled against the walls or slab footings
immediately after setting of the concrete to reduce water infiltration
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and maintained to drain
all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundations
Percolation Testing
Two percolation tests were performed in Boring Nos 8 and 13 in the parking area
to evaluate the subsurface permeability conditions at the site The falling head method
was used for the percolation testing After the boring was drilled fresh water was injected
into the boring and the drop of the water level in the boring was measured at different
time intervals Results of the falling head percolation tests are presented in Appendix C

permeability k in the range of 1x10 to 3x10 inches per second which corresponds to

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content and unit weight determinations were performed on selected soil
samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties The results of
these tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Two Atterberg Limits tests were performed on selected samples of the soils to
evaluate their liquid and plastic limits and to aid in soil classification The test results are
summarized on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths Graphic
presentation of the test results is shown on Plate B 3
Six one inch ring swell tests were performed on selected samples to evaluate the
swelling potential of the subsoils Test results are summarized on Plate B 1
One sieve analysis was performed on a selected soil sample to evaluate the
gradation characteristics of the soil and to aid in soil classification The test results are
also summarized on Plate B 2 Graphic presentation of grain size distribution is provided
on Plate B 4
Two direct shear tests were performed on selected soil samples to evaluate the
shear strength of the material Results of the direct shear tests are presented on Plates
B 5 and B 6
Two sets of corrosion tests consisting of pH and minimum resistivity were
performed on the foundation soils to evaluate the corrosivity of the materials Results of
the pH and minimum resistivity values are presented on Plate B 2
One compaction test ASTM D 1557 91 was performed on a bulk sample of the
soil to evaluate the maximum dry density and optimum moisture content relationship The
test results are presented on Plate B 7
Two CBR tests were performed on bulk samples of the soils to evaluate their
strength characteristics for pavement subgrade support Results of the CBR tests are
presented on Plate B 2
