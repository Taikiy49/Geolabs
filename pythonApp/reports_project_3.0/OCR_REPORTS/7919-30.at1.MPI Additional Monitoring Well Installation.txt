This technical memorandum provides the boring log and photographs of the
additional monitoring well installed for the Mid-Pacific Institute Phase 1 project in Honolulu
on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii.
MONITORING WELL INSTALLATION
We understand that additional groundwater monitoring is desired in support of the
Mid-Pacific Institute Phase 1 project. In order to install the additional monitoring well, we
drilled a boring, designated as MW-201, at the location shown on the attached Site Plan,
Plate 1.
Boring MW-201 was drilled on March 22, 2024 to a depth of about 36 feet below
the existing ground surface. Our boring encountered a 3.5-foot-thick surface fill layer
underlain by alluvial soils extending to a maximum depth explored of about 36 feet below
the existing ground surface at the time of our drilling. We encountered ground water in
the drilled boring at a depth of about 8 feet, corresponding to an elevation of about
+99 feet Mean Sea Level (MSL). The log of our drilled boring is presented on Plate 2.
After completion of drilling, the borehole was converted into a monitoring well
consisting of a 2-inch perforated PVC pipe surrounded by pea gravel. The PVC pipe was
capped with a well plug at the top and the monitoring well was covered with an 8-inch
diameter well cover. Photos of the in-place well are presented on Plate 3. We understand
that the additional monitoring well will be monitored by others.
