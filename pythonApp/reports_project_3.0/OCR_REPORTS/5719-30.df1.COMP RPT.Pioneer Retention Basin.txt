Project Considerations
The existing Pioneer Research Facility is off Kunia Road in the Honouliuli area in
the District of Ewa on the Island of Oahu Hawaii It is situated adjacent to an existing
cane haul road accessible at the intersection of Kupuna Loop and Kunia Road near the
Royal Kunia Shopping Center The project location and general vicinity are shown on
the Project Location Map Plate 1 Site grading work for the construction generally
consisted of cuts of up to about 8 feet and fills of up to about 4 feet relative to the
existing grades The layout of the project is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services for
the earthwork site grading and utility construction at the project site

Earthwork Site Grading and Utility Construction
The earthwork construction at the Pioneer Research Facility consisted of the
excavation of a new retention basin embankment filling of the basin downstream berm
and utility construction The work was performed by Aloha Trucking During the site
grading operations samples of the on site and imported materials utilized as fill and
backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing The maximum
dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general
accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor The laboratory test results are
summarized on Plate 3
The fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to near optimum moisture
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as
determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557 In the roadway areas the fill and backfill
materials within 2 feet of the finished subgrade were compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative density In addition select borrow and base course materials below
the pavement areas were compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative density
During the work periodic field density tests were performed in general accordance
with ASTM D 6938 Nuclear Gauge Method Results of the compaction tests performed
are summarized on Plates 4 1 through 4 9 of this report These tests were performed on
the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills at the site Test
locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey
stakes These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree
implied by the methods used Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until
