SUMMARY OF FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Our subsurface exploration at the project site indicates that
the site is underlain by a thin surface layer of alluvium below
which clinker hard basalt and ash layers were encountered
at varying depths
Our findings indicate that the area north of the building as
Currently sited is underlain at shallow depths by soft ash soils
which are not suitable to sustain loadings which would result
from construction of embankments of any appreciable height
Fills over three feet in height should not be constructed in this
area No fills should be constructed with slopes steeper than
two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V
The siting of the project currently envisions locating the
building in the center of the site with parking on its south east
and west sides This siting is feasible from a geotechnical
point of view provided that the structural fill required to
support the floor slab is contained by retaining walls and that
other recommendations presented in this report are followed
Permeability tests performed at varying locations indicate that
the disposal of surface run off water is feasible providing that
dry wells are constructed in such a manner that the above
mentioned ash layer will not be adversely affected
The text of this report should be referred to for detailed and
special design recommendations

INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Cost U Less Store to be located on Kinoole Street in
downtown Hilo on the island of Hawaii The general location of the site and its vicinity
is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The exploration was performed in two phases Five 5 borings were initially
performed at the project site during the period of July 4 and 5 1991 to obtain an
overview of the subsurface conditions Review of the information obtained from the initial
exploration indicated that the subsurface conditions were of such nature that a second
more location specific exploration was necessary to further define the nature of the
materials underlying the northern lower portion of the site so that appropriate geotechniai
engineering recommendations could be formulated The second phase of the exploration
consisted of drilling five 5 more borings from July 15 through at locations
chosen to define in more detail the depth extent and engineering properties of a soft to
medium stiff compressible ash layer encountered in the northern lower portion of the
project site
The report summarizes our findings resulting from the field exploration and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations which were derived from analyses based on
the data obtained during the exploration and laboratory testing These recommendations
are intended for grading and foundation design input and are not intended to serve as
the basis for preparation of construction cost estimates or for construction bidding
purposes

Prior to site grading areas within the grading limits should be cleared of all
vegetation and deleterious material which should be properly disposed of off site Any
soft weak or yielding areas encountered during the clearing operations should be over
excavated to firm ground and the resulting depression backfilled with select granular fill
material compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density as determined by
ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Fills up to twelve 12 feet high and minimal cuts are anticipated at the project site
Imported fill material is therefore anticipated to be required Imported fill should consist
of non expansive select granular material less than three 3 inches in maximum size with
less than 20 passing the No 200 sieve Any imported fill material should be approved
by the project geotechnical engineer prior to delivery to the project site

thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density as determined

level lifts not exceeding eight 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum
of 90 of the maximum dry density as determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 The

compacted to a minimum of 95 of the maximum dry density

Foundation Design Considerations
It is our opinion that spread footing foundations bearing on the recompacted in situ
material or compacted imported fill material may be used
If footings will bear on two different types of materials such as in situ hard rock and
the unconsolidated clayey basalt cobbles we recommend that the footing subgrade in
rock be over excavated a minimum of twelve 12 inches and backfilled with select
granular material The select granular backfill should be compacted in maximum eight
8 inch loose level lifts to 95 of its maximum dry density as determined by ASTM Test
Method D 1557 78 This will lessen the potential for differential settlement across the
footing span due to its bearing on materials with different bearing capacities
In the event that the above mentioned conditions exist and a continuous footing
excavation encounters rock over a relatively long span i e more than ten 10 feet the
over excavation may be transitioned over a distance of five 5 feet from the twelve 12
inch depth as recommended above to no over excavation in the rock formation This
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O Page 11
would reduce the amount of over excavation while still providing acceptable support for
the footing foundation
An allowable bearing capacity of 2 500 p s f may be utilized for the footing design
This footing bearing value may be increased by up to one third for short term loadings
including wind and seismic loads The exterior footings should be embedded a minimum
of eighteen 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade The minimum
embedment for interior footings may be reduced to twelve 12 inches below finished
grade
Footings located next to utility trenches should extend down to an elevation equal
to or deeper than the bottom of the trench
Any soft or weak area encountered at the bottom of any footing excavation should

APPENDIX C
Field Permeability Testing
The in situ permeability of the on site soil was determined by performing one 1
falling head percolation test at Boring No 1 with a surface elevation of fifty one 51 feet
above MSL and a depth of sixteen 16 feet and two 2 constant head percolation tests
at Boring No 4 with surface elevation of thirty five 85 feet above MSL and B 5 with
surface elevation of thirty eight 38 feet above MSL both borings were drilled to depths
of twenty one 21 feet The approximate test locations are shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2
The test results for the falling head percolation test at Boring No B 1 are shown
on Plate C 1
The constant head percolation tests were determined by the introduction of water
into the borings at a rate of approximately fourteen 14 gallons per minute for a total
amount of five hundred 500 gallons At both test locations the water was absorbed by
the subsurface materials as fast as it was introduced into the borings
