INTRODUCTION
This report summarizes the findings of our subsurface investi
gation and presents recommendations for the design and
construction of the foundations to support the proposed struc
tures roadways and ancillary earthwork
W ee May 9 1975
Project Description
The proposed resort development will consist of 74 units
within nine 2 and 3 story woodframe slab on grade structures
A swimming 91 tennis courts parking area and other ameni
ties are inciuded
It is anticipated that the structures will utilize conven
tional spread and line foundations with loadings of less than
In the absence of a grading plan it is anticipated that the
site grading will involve the removal of the soil mounds and
filling of the low lying areas at the southeastern corner
The total grading will generally be minimal
SITE CONDITIONS
Site Description
The 3 7 acre site is essentially rectangular in shape with
plan dimensions of approximately 230 by 720 feet The site
is located approximately six miles north of Lihue between
Kuhio Highway and the ocean near Wailua River
It is bound on the north and east by Wailua River State Park
and on the south by Nehe Road A single land parcel separates
the property from the Leho Drive which is the site access from
Kuhio Highway
W 3 May 9 1975
The basic ground surface generally varies from elevation 30
at the nortnern corner to approximately elevation 12 at the
southeastern corner of the property Numerous earth mounds
are present throughout the site and extend to elevation 36
feet
The major portion of the site is overgrown with thick Cali
fornia grass and brush The southeastern portion is covered
with young koa trees
Numerous large boulders are evident at the surface and several
existing foundations are located in the low lying area at the
southeastern corner of the property
An area of wind blown beach sand blankets the surface along
the eastern property line nearest the ocean
Reportedly the mounds and low lying area were reworked and
graded in connection with the structure which previously
occupied the reinforced concrete foundations at the south
eastern corner of the property
Geology and General Soil Conditions
The soils at the site were derived from weathering of lava
fiows of Nepheline basalt deposited by the Koloa Volcanic
Series during the middle Pleistocene Epoch
W 4 May 9 1975
According to the Soil Conservation mapping the most prevalent
soil at the site is the Kalapa Series which ranges from dark
red to reddish brown silty clay and clay with a subangular
structure It varies from 40 to 50 inches in thickness and
is underlain by highly weathered rock
The soil is strongly acidic and has a severe to very severe
erosion potential It varies in permeability from moderate
to rapid and has a moderate to low shrink swell potential
with high shear strengths
Subsurface Explorations
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drill
ine nine test borings at the locations shown on the enclosed
Site Plan Plate 1 A detailed description of the explora
tion methods and accompanying laboratory testing is given in
the appendix along with the boring logs and laboratory test
results
Soil Conditions
The explorations disclosed that the site is underlain by
extremely stiff reddish brown silty clay varying from 4 to 10
feet in thickness Hard basaltic boulders were encountered
randomly throughout this upper layer
oe
W 5 May 9 1975
The upper silty clay is underlain by a gray brown silty clay
decomposed rock derived from the weathering of vesicular
basalt the structure of which remains partially intact
This lower layer contains more boulders which are harder and
less weathered It extends to a depth of 12 to 20 feet where
massive basalt rock was encountered The lower basalt con
tained interspersed clay seams Portions of the basalt is
highly fractured with open cracks as evidenced by the loss of
drilling fluid in Boring 4
No groundwater was encountered during the subsurface explora
tions
DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The subsurface explorations and laboratory testings show that
the site is suitable for the proposed development The struc
tures may be supported upon conventional line or spread founda
tions Minimal site preparation will be required to assure
uniformed bearing or to remove isolated boulders encountered
at or near the foundation bearing levels
Foundations
The proposed structures may be designed to bear upon spread or
line foundations designed for allowable contact bearing pres

adjacent grade For this loading the estimated settlements

The foundations may bear upon the existing soils or upon
structural fill compacted in accordance with the recommenda
tions given for the earthwork and grading in the following
paragraphs
It is anticipated that boulders will be encountered in the
excavations throughout the site Where the boulders occur at
or near the proposed foundation bearing level it will be
necessary to remove the boulders and backfill the area with
compacted on site soils
Since portions of the site have been previously excavated or
filled it is recommended that the proposed building areas be
proof rolled to delineate possible localized soft spots or
areas of uncompacted fill If encountered these areas should
be undercut to the limit of the unsuitable or loose materials
and backfilled with compacted on site soils
Slabs On Grade
Laboratory testing of selected samples of the on site soils
show that they are non expansive Slab on grade construction
can therefore be utilized without special provisions

It is recommended that the concrete slabs be provided with a
minimum of a four inch base course consisting of well graded
gravel Jess than 3 4 inch maximum size The subgrade should
be compacted and shaped to drain if practical
Plastic vapor barriers should be placed under concrete slabs
where moisture sensitive floor coverings are to be used
These barriers should be covered by a thin layer of sand approx
imately one inch thick to prevent puncture and to aid in the
concrete curing F
Site Grading and Earthwork
All areas to receive fill should be first stripped of surface
vegetation and miscellaneous debris including the removal of
the existing reinforced concrete foundations
Prior to the placement of any fill the exposed surface should
be proof rolled to delineate localized soft pockets or areas
of loose surface soils Thin deposits of surface soils can
be compacted in place Deep deposits must be undercut and
backfilled in layers in order to obtain a uniformed density
This proof rolling should be performed in all building park
ing and fill areas
The soil should be placed and compacted in accordance with the
enclosed typical specifications In general the soils should

be compacted to a minimum of 90 per cent of the maximum dry
density as established by American Society for Testing and
Materials ASTM Test Designation D 1557 72T
The on site soils can be utilized for the construction of the
site fills The moisture density determination made on a bulk
sample of the surface soils produced a maximum proctor density
of and an optimum water content of 31 3 per cent At
the time of our investigation the near surface soils had an
average moisture content of approximately 35 per cent and would
therefore be suitable as fill at these present moisture contents
Provisions should be made for either drying or wetting of the
soils dependent upon the weather conditions at the time of
construction
The test borings indicate numerous boulders within the soil
mounds and surface soils The large boulders greater than
eight inches diameter should not be placed in the compacted
fill areas The boulders can be incorporated into the fills
for the open areas not sensitive to settlements or disposed
off site
The loose wind blown sand deposits encountered at the surface
in the southeastern corner must be compacted before they can
be utilized to support foundations or additional fill

Unforeseen Conditions
Unforeseen or undetected conditions such as localized soft
spots groundwater seepage pockets of expansive soils or

Construction Monitoring
The foundation excavations should be inspected prior to the
placement of the reinforcing steel to verify that the condi

explorations and that adequate bearing has been obtained
The placement and compaction of all fills utilized for struc

settlements should be inspected on a full time basis to
assure that the specified degree of compaction is consistently
achieved

STANDARD GRADING SPECIFICATIONS
The work under this section includes clearing and grubbing
preparation of fill areas placement and compaction of fill
and removal and backfilling of underground structures These
specifications are of a general nature and may be superseded
by specific requirements or recommendations contained within
the soils report
I Clearing
ANI areas within contract limit lines shall be cleared of
trash debris organic matter and all other deleterious
materials These materials shall be removed and disposed
offsite
II Preparation of Fill Areas
A
Loose surface soils shall be removed to expose compe
tent natural soils Localized soft pockets shall be
undercut and backfilled with compacted select mate
rials
All surfaces shall be inspected and approved by the
soils engineer prior to the placement of any fill
After approval the surface shall be scarified to a
depth of six inches brought to near optimum moisture
content and compacted as herein specified
cq
IV
2
Where the existing surface exceeds a slope of 5
horizontal to 1 vertical 5 1 the ground surface
shall be keyed and benched into competent soil to
bond the fill to the existing ground surface
Subsurface drains may be required as directed by the
soils engineer where springs or zones of seepage are
encountered
Fill Materials
Material for fill shall consist of on site soils or
approved import material Import material shall be
tested and approved prior to transportation to the site
Fill material shall contain Jess than 5 percent organic
matter and other deleterious material and shall not
contain rocks or lumps in excess of eight inches 8
dn diameter
Placement and Compaction of Fill
A
The fill shall be placed in horizontal layers which
when compacted will not exceed six inches 6 in
thickness Each layer shall be spread uniformly and
blade mixed to attain uniformity of material and
water content within each layer
coy
3
Rock fragments or boulders less than eight inches
8 in diameter may be utilized provided they are
not concentrated in any one area and that there is
sufficient fine grained material to fill the voids
between the rocks
Rocks greater than eight inches 8 in diameter
shall be removed from the fill unless specifically
approved for placement in the fill by the soils
report
All fill shall be compacted to a minimum of 90
percent of the maximum dry density established by
ASTM Test Designation D 1557 72T unless otherwise
specified in the soils report
The compaction equipment may be sheepsfoot vibra
tory or pneumatic tire rollers suited to the soil
type and capable of producing the specified results
in an expedient and efficient manner The equipment
shall be in good working condition fully ballasted
and self cleaning
The compaction shall be accomplished in a systematic
and uniform manner over the entire fill area to pro
duce a uniformly compacted fill
VI
4
G Fill material placed in an unsatisfactory condition
and not in accordance with these specifications
shall not be approved and shall be removed or reworked
until the required density is obtained
H The soils engineers shall be provided unrestricted
access to the fill to perform the compaction tests
Removal and Backfilling of Underground Structures
Underground structures such as cesspools cisterns
septic tanks wells pipe lines etc shall be removed
as directed by the soils engineer Backfilling of the
excavations shall be in accordance with these specifi
cations
Seasonal Requirements
A No fill shall be placed during unfavorable weather
conditions When interruption of work is due to
heavy rain the previous fill surface shall be
approved before resumption of earthmoving operations
B The contractor shall maintain the surface of the fill
so that it will be free draining and will not pond
surface water
