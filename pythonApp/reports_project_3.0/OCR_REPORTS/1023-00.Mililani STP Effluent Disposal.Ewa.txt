GENERAL PROJECT DESCRIPTION
The proposed effluent disposal System will consist of a 30 36
inch diameter sewer lines extending from the Mililani Sewage
Treatment Plant to the Waipahu Sewage Pump Station The
project is separated into two Phases Phase I extends from
Mililani STP to the Ewa side of Waikele Gulch approximately
8 000 feet long Phase II extends from Waikele Gulch to
Waipahu SPS approximately 17 600 feet The invert of the
proposed sewer line varies from about 5 to 40 feet below the
existing ground surface Also included in this project is
a 30 inch diameter irrigation line approximately 3 500 feet
long

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Generally relatively competent soil conditions were encoun
tered along most of the mauka portion of the alignment above
the lowlying portions of Waipahu town In this upper area
stiff residual soils grading into decomposed rock were encoun
tered
Loose rubbish fill was encountered near to Sta
110 50 at the proposed sewer line invert level We recommend
that the sewer invert be lowered below the loose fill level
Deep deposits of soft organic clays and silts muck were
encountered in the lower end of the alignment below about
elevation 15 feet Adequate shoring and bracing are criti
cal in this area to minimize damage to the adjacent struc
tures due to the lateral ground movements or failure of the
trench walls High water level was also encountered in this
area and this will require careful dewatering
Based on the boring data the anticipated soil conditions at
the proposed sewer invert level are delineated and presented
on the Site Plans Plates 3
Excavation
Estimated depth of excavation will be about 5 to 42 feet
Relatively easy digging is anticipated through the upper
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1023 00
P 12
stiff residual soil Some amount of boulders should be
anticipated in the residual soil formations Harder dig
ging is anticipated in the deeper portions of the trenches
where dense weathered rock is encountered
Due to depth of the excavation anticipated shoring of the
trench walls are recommended to minimize caving in of the
trench walls Additional bracing may be required in the
existing loose rubbish fill areas
If sufficient space is available the excavation may be
cut back at a temporary slope of 1 1 H V in lieu of the
above wall supports
Soft Ground Conditions
Estimated depth of excavation will be about 10 to 18 feet
Easy digging is anticipated through the soft muck subsoils
Sheeting and bracing is required for this portion to pre
vent a cave in of the trench walls Due to the low strengths
and high water contents of the soils and the proximity to
existing structures special care must be exercised to pre
vent a failure of the trench walls or upheaval of the trench
bottom due to the excavation and dewatering operations
Sheet pile or other support system may be required to obtain
trench stability
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1023 00
P 13
If practical the excavation should be made in short sec
tions and backfilled as soon as possible
Heavy equipment and stockpiles should be kept away from
the tops of the open trenches
The ground adjacent to and near the top of the excavation
Should be continuously monitored for cracks or dips and
other indications of movements and possible sloughing of
the trench walls This monitoring is particularly criti
cal in the area where existing structures are adjacent to
the trench excavation
Dewatering
High groundwater levels were noted in the lower southern
portion of the alignment
The method of dewatering should be left open to the contractor
provided adequate stability of the excavation is maintained
The alluvial deposits encountered in the borings along the
alignment consisted mostly of organic clays and silts with
some silty sand
The project dewatering operation should be done without pump
ing out the soil fines pumping clear water only It should
be coordinated with the excavation support system so that the
stability of the excavation is not jeopardized The operations
should be carried out without creating a quick condition or
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1023 00
P 14
softening the bottom of the excavation
Another alternative to dewatering would be to place the pipe
in a wet trench condition
Pipe Bedding
The stress distribution against the bottom of a pipe has a
great effect upon the load Supporting capacity of the pipe
therefore the pipe bedding is an important design consider
ation
A minimum of ten inches of compacted granular bedding mater
ial should be placed below the pipe The granular bedding
should extend beyond the pipe sides a minimum width of one
fourth the outside pipe diameter and should extend halfway
up the pipe sides The bedding material should consist of
well graded granular material such as 3B Fine gravel
Subgrade Preparation
Where soft soil conditions are encountered in the trench bot
tom Waipahu SPS and lower Waipahu areas stabilization of
the soft soils is required prior to pipe installation To
provide uniform support and a working platform a 2 foot layer
of granular material such as 3B Fine gravel should be pro
vided below the sewer pipe and the bedding layer
From about to 110 50 Phase II the sewer pipe alignment
appears to extend through a loose fill deposit We recommend
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1023 00
P 15
that the pipe invert be lowered to extend below this loose
rubbish fill layer and bear on the older stiff fill or stiff
residual soil below Prior to the placement of the bedding
layer the bottom of the trench in this section should be com
pacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density per
ASTM D 1557 test designation
Backfill
In general the trench backfill should be in accordance with
the Standard Specifications for Public Works Construction
May 1975
Granular material such as sand should be used for the back
fill up to about one foot above the pipe to reduce the com
pactive effort required and possible damage to the pipe
Except for the soft organic clays and silt muck and rub
bish fill most of the excavated on site soil can be re used
as the upper portion of the trench backfill from above one
foot over the pipe to within 2 feet of finished grade Back
fill should be mechanically compacted to a minimum of 90 of
its maximum dry density established by ASTM D 1557 Below
roadway areas the backfill within 2 feet of finished grade
should consist of select borrow and be compacted to 95 of
its maximum dry density
If practicable the trenches should be kept free of water

during backfilling Should backfill under the water level
be necessary granular material such as base course rock
Should be utilized to minimize excessive ground settlement

Settlements
In the upper portions of the alignment where stiff soils
were encountered since the net increase in load on the sub
soils caused by the sewer construction will be small the
resulting settlements should also be relatively small if the
sewer line is properly constructed
In soft soil areas some amount of surface settlements may
occur due to disturbance of the subsoils dewatering opera
tions and increase in unit weight of the trench backfill
The amount of settlement to be experienced depends on the
contractor s construction methods
Joints and Connections
Due to the various soil conditions which will be crossed by
the pipe flexible pipe joints are recommended to allow for
some amount of differential settlement These flexible con
nections are particularly important in the soft ground areas
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1023 00
P 17
Reaction Blocks
Reaction blocks are required in the upper portions of the
alignment to resist lateral loads at the pipe bends The
lateral soil resistance that can be developed will depend
on the size and depth of the reaction blocks For prelimi
nary design purposes a lateral bearing capacity of 2000
p s f can be used for reaction blocks in the stiff resid
ual soil
The concrete should be poured neat against the reaction
block excavation
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
Field inspection and engineering observations during the
sewer construction are important to permit correlation
between the preliminary soil data obtained during this inves
tigation and the actual soil conditions encountered during
construction
Backfilling operations should be inspected by a soils engineer
and density tests should be taken to verify that adequate com
paction density has been obtained
