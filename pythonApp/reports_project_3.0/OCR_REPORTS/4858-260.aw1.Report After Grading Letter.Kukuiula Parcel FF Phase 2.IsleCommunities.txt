PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The planned Kukui ula Development is a resort community that encompasses
about 1 045 acres in Koloa on the south shore of Kauai and includes a hotel vacation
ownership commercial park golf course and residential uses The Parcel FF Lots 1 8
Subdivision project is part of the improvements for the Kukui ula Development
The Parcel FF Phase 2 is a subdivision located in the central portion of the
Kukui ula Resort Community overlooking the 1st hole of the Kukui ula Golf Course and to
the east of the Resort Core and Golf Club House The project consisted of constructing
four single story houses with separate carport structures walking paths underground
utilities and expanding the existing parking lot and driveway A Project Location Map
and Site Plan are shown on Plates 1 and 2 respectively
Site grading generally consisted of cuts and fills up to about 3 feet thick and
excavations up to 5 feet deep for underground utilities On site soils generated from the
cut areas were the primary source of fill material for the project Imported granular fill for
the building pads and pavements were imported from Kaua i Aggregate and Rock
Quarry The project also included installation of new infrastructure including roadways
drainage sewer water and other utilities

SITE GRADING
We observed the proof rolling mass and pad grading underground utility
construction and parking lot construction work for the Kukui ula Parcel FF Phase 2
project from through The mass and site grading work
consisted of clearing and grubbing minor cuts and placement and compaction of fills up to
about 3 feet thick to achieve the finished grades performed the
mass and pad grading and utility and parking lot construction work
Prior to fill placement the building pads for the project were proof rolled utilizing a
10 ton vibratory compactor where no near surface voids soft areas or cavities were
found During the site grading operations samples of the materials utilized as fill were
obtained and transported to our office for testing The maximum dry densities and
optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general accordance with
ASTM D1557 Modified Proctor and the test results are summarized on Plate 3
The fill materials were moisture conditioned to near optimum moisture content and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as determined in
accordance with ASTM D1557 In the parking areas the fill and backfill materials at the
finished subgrade were compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
During the mass and pad grading and utility and parking lot construction
operations periodic field density tests were performed in general accordance with
ASTM D6938 Nuclear Gauge Method Compaction test results are summarized on
Plates 4 1 through 4 8 These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and
on constructed fills and backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated
by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and elevations
should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas
assessed as unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was
obtained and or approved by our field representative based on visual observation and
hand probing
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the site grading utility and parking lot installation
work for the above project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this
report were performed in conformance with the general intent of the recommendations in
the following references
