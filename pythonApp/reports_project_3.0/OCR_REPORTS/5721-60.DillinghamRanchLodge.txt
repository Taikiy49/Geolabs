Mokuleia area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project consists of mass grading the

plans cuts and fills on the order of about 2 to 3 feet are planned for the proposed mass
grading project

underground drain lines are also planned for the mass grading project

Regional Geology
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lavas from the
Waianae and Koolau shield volcanoes The older Waianae Volcano is estimated to be
middle to late Pliocene in age and forms the bulk of the western one third of the island
The younger Koolau Volcano is estimated to be late Pliocene to early Pleistocene Ice
Age in age and forms the majority of the eastern two thirds of the island
The project site is situated on a narrow coastal plain and pediment which was
formed by the accumulation of a relatively thick wedge of colluvium alluvium and marine
sediments along the base of an extensive wave cut cliff on the northern flank of the
Waianae Volcano Geological conditions at the site and in its immediate vicinity can
generally be delineated by the location and slope of the various portions of the site In the
area close to Farrington Highway where the slope is relatively level the shallow
subsurface conditions generally consist of marine sediments dune sands and recent
alluvium The soils in this area generally have poor engineering characteristics e g soft
soil conditions and high ground water table
As the slope steepens to the inland side of the site the subsurface conditions
become dominated by older alluvial materials that have accumulated in alluvial fans
originating from major drainageways draining to the north from the Waianae Range
These alluvial materials consist of interbedded clays silts gravels and some areas of
boulder conglomerate In some areas the alluvium has weathered to a residual type soil
and the underlying conglomerates have weathered to a saprolitic state The soils in these
areas are generally more competent however they exhibit moderate to high shrink swell
potentials
Further inland as the property approaches the base of the Waianae Range the
geology grades to an accumulation of talus and colluvium at the base of the wave cut cliff
This interbedded talus and colluvium generally consists of angular boulders and cobbles
with a matrix of highly expansive clay The large number of boulders and cobbles create
grading difficulties and the highly expansive clay is poorly suited for most engineering
uses

Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork areas within the contract grading limits should be
cleared and grubbed thoroughly Vegetation debris deleterious material and
other unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed properly off site or

Soft and yielding areas encountered during clearing and grubbing should be
over excavated to expose firm natural material and the resulting excavation
should be backfilled with engineered fill
Areas to receive fill and finished subgrades in cut areas should be scarified to a
minimum depth of 12 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the
optinum moisture and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction The compaction requirement should be increased to 95 percent
relative compaction in areas subject to vehicular traffic Relative compaction refers
to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry
density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557
The compacted subgrade should be kept moist until new fill is placed over it If the
subgrade is allowed to dry and shrinkage cracks are observed after compaction of
the subgrade we recommend scarifying the soils again and preparing the
subgrade as recommended above Saturation and subsequent yielding of the
exposed subgrade due to inclement weather and poor drainage may require
over excavation of the soft areas and replacement with engineered fill
Because moisture conditioning and compaction of the subgrade soils are critical
elements of earthwork Geolabs should perform observations and soil density tests
during site grading to assist the contractor in obtaining the required degree of
compaction and the proper moisture content Where compaction is less than
required additional compactive effort should be applied with adjustment of
moisture content as necessary to obtain the specified compaction It should be
noted that the moisture requirement about 2 percent above the optimum
moisture is an important requirement for the use of the on site clayey soils
Fills and Backfills
The excavated on site soils may be used as general fill materials provided that
these soils are placed at least 10 feet away from new and existing structures In
addition the excavated on site soils should be free of vegetation deleterious
materials and clay lumps and rock fragments greater than 3 inches in maximum
dimension It should be noted that the general fill requirements presented herein
are intended as guidelines only and may be modified based on additional
laboratory testing and field observations on the available fill materials conducted
by Geolabs General fills may be placed over the clay soils provided that they are
scarified moisture conditioned and recompacted as recommended in the
Site Preparation subsection herein

Imported general fill materials should consist of soil materials with a maximum
particle size of 3 inches or less with sufficient fines between 10 and 60 percent
particles passing the No 200 sieve to prevent the occurrence of voids in the
compacted mass In addition imported general fill materials should have a
CBR value of 12 or greater and a swell of 1 percent or less when tested in
accordance with ASTM D 1883
Select granular material should consist of crushed basalt or coral The material
should be well graded from coarse to fine with particles no larger than 3 inches in
largest dimension and should contain between 10 and 30 percent particles
passing the No 200 sieve The material should have a laboratory CBR value of 20
or more and should have a maximum swell of 1 percent or less Geolabs should
test imported fill materials for conformance with these recommendations prior to
delivery to the project site for its intended use
Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
General fill and backfills should be moisture conditioned to about 2 percent above
the optimum moisture placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose
thickness and compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction The upper
12 inches of fill placed in areas subject to vehicular traffic should be compacted to
at least 95 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place
dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the
same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 Optimum moisture is the
water content percentage by weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
We emphasize that moisture conditioning of the fill materials is an integral part of
the fill placement recommendations provided herein Sheepsfoot rollers vibratory
rollers or other types of acceptable compaction equipment should be used to
achieve the proper degree of compaction
Because moisture conditioning and compaction of the subgrade soils are critical
elements of earthwork Geolabs should perform observations and soil density tests
during site grading to assist the contractor in obtaining the required degree of
compaction and the proper moisture content Where compaction is less than
required additional compactive effort should be applied with adjustment of
moisture content as necessary to obtain the specified compaction It should be
noted that the moisture requirement about 2 percent above the optimum
moisture of the fills and subgrades is an important requirement for the use of the
on site clayey soils

Retaining Structure Foundations
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered at the site we believe the
retaining structure foundations may be designed using an allowable bearing
pressure of up to 2 500 pounds per square foot psf This bearing value may be
increased by one third 1 3 for transient loads such as those caused by wind or
seismic forces
Soft and or loose materials encountered at the bottom of footing excavations
should be over excavated until firm materials are exposed in the footing
excavation The over excavation should be backfilled with select granular fill
materials moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
If the foundations are designed and constructed in strict accordance with our
recommendations we estimate the total settlements of the foundations to be less
than 1 inch Differential settlements along the foundations bearing on similar
materials may be on the order of about 0 5 inch or less
Lateral loads acting on the structures may be resisted by friction between the
bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil and by passive earth pressure acting

Drainage
Retaining structures should be well drained to reduce the build up of hydrostatic
pressures A typical drainage system would consist of a 12 inch wide zone of
permeable material such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation
placed directly around a perforated pipe perforations facing down at the base of
the structure discharging to an appropriate outlet or weepholes As an alternative
a prefabricated drainage product such as MiraDrain or EnkaDrain may be used
instead of the drainage material The prefabricated drainage product also should
be hydraulically connected to a perforated pipe at the base of the structure
The backfill from the bottom of the retaining structure to the bottom of the
weephole should consist of relatively impervious materials to reduce the potential
for significant water infiltration into the subsurface In addition the upper 12 inches
of the retaining structure backfill should consist of relatively impervious materials to
reduce the potential for significant water infiltration behind the retaining structure
unless covered by concrete slabs at the surface
Underground Utility
Based on the results of our field exploration it appears the utility trench
excavations will likely encounter stiff to very stiff silty clay In general a granular bedding
consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation is
recommended below the pipes Free draining granular materials such as No 3B Fine
gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation should be used for the trench backfill up to about
12 inches above the pipes to provide adequate support around the pipes and to reduce
compaction of the backfill thus reducing the possibility of pipe damage
The upper portion of the trench backfill from a level of 1 foot above the pipes to the
finished subgrade should consist of the approved on site soils or general fill material The
backfill material should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content
placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a
minimum of 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for future ground
subsidence The upper 3 feet of the trench backfill below the pavement grade should be
compacted to no less than 95 percent relative compaction Mechanical compaction
equipment should be used to compact the backfill materials at the project site
Compaction efforts by water tamping jetting or ponding should not be allowed
considering the expansive soil conditions at the site

Moisture Content ASTM D 2216 and Unit Weight ASTM D 2937 determinations
were performed on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of
soil properties The test results are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate
sample depths

results are summarized on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depth and on
Plate B 1

bulk sample of the near surface soils to evaluate the pavement support characteristics
of the soils The test results are presented on Plate B 2
