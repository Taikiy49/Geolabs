REMARKS As requested we have further investigated and reviewed the results of the
Pulse Echo Testing and the installation logs for the Categories 6 and 7
drilled piers Based on the draft report of the Pulse Echo Testing provided
by the GTR and the coring operation performed for Shaft Nos 17 and 69
we provide you the following additional conclusions and recommendations
for your review and consideration
1 Based on the further evaluation and analyses of the Pulse Echo Testing
provided by GTR Shaft and 22 which were previously
categorized as inconclusive are revised to Category 4 Good
2 However the test results for Shaft 56 65 and 66
are still remained inconclusive due to possible poor quality data collected
during the pulse echo testing Based on the review of the installation
logs it appeared that these drilled piers were excavated with the
temporary steel casing ranging from 35 to 58 feet in length The
temporary steel casing was generally extended into medium dense
coralline detritus or medium stiff alluvium or clinker layers The
concrete was placed within less than two hours after the completion of
the excavation Percentages of the concrete over pour range from 39 to
88 percent which is typical in coralline materials The concrete was
placed from bottom up through a tremie pipe placed near bottom of the
drilled hole The temporary steel casing was left in the hole until the
concrete topping off from the top of the casing The concrete placement
was continued through the tremie pipe while the casing was removed
gradually until the casing was completely removed in order to keep a
positive hydrostatic head The concrete placement measurement plots
showed that the concrete level was raised up generally parallel to the
theoretical concrete volume line within the casing depth

3 Based on the draft report of the Pulse Echo Testing provided by GTR
the transmitted wave was reflected at the depth of 14 to 20 feet from the
top of the drilled piers indicating possible major anomalies or enlarged
shaft diameter for Drilled Pier and 70 Therefore these
drilled piers were previously categorized as Major Anomaly and
recommended for further investigation
As part of the further investigation the integrity coring testing was
performed on Drilled Pier Nos 17 and 69 The core holes for Pier Nos
17 and 69 were extended down to about 86 and 45 feet below the
ground surface respectively In general no void or major defect was
found from the core samples retrieved from the core holes Although the
coring operation in Drilled Pier No 69 was not able to extend down to tip
of the pier due to the time restriction the core hole was extended about
20 feet deeper than the depth of anomaly indicated by the Pulse Echo
Testing A review of the installation logs indicated that a significant
amount of concrete was placed in these shafts probably resulting in an
enlarged shaft diameter within the casing depths Therefore we
recommend that the Drilled Pier Nos 17 and 69 may be accepted
Based on the information provided by GTR we understand that the wave
reflection received from the Pulse Echo Testing conducted for Drilled
Pier No 70 was similar to the Pier Nos 17 and 69 Therefore we also
recommend that the Drilled Pier No 70 may be accepted
