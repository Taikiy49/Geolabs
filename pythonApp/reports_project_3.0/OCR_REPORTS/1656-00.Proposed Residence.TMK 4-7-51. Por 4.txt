In accordance with your request we have made a study of the near
surface soil conditions at the above referenced lot in order to provide
foundation recommendations for the proposed residence The study
consisted of drilling two shallow borings performing laboratory testing
and reviewing the site grading plan This report summarizes our findings
and recommendations
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
Based on the site grading plan the proposed residence will be
approximately 50 by 70 feet in plan dimensions and will also include a
driveway
Concrete slab on grade is planned for the entire house and driveway
areas

SITE CONDITIONS
The subject lot is located on the northern side of the existing
Board of Water Supply access road leading to the Ahuimanu 500
Reservoir The access road begins at the end of Nukupu u Street in the
Ahuimanu Hills Subdivision Phase III
During our soil investigation the site grading for the subject lot
had not begun and certain areas were inaccessible The existing ground is
sloping and there were stockpiles of materials soils in various
locations
SUBSURFACE SOIL CONDITIONS

the existing grade at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan

DISCUSSTON AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The medium stiff to stiff mottled brown clayey silt soil encountered
generally can provide suitable foundation support for the proposed house
provided that some care is exercised during construction to minimize the
shrinkage swelling potential of the soil
laboratory tests performed on the medium stiff to stiff clayey silt
soil indicated that due to the natural high moisture content this
material generally has low swelling potential at its natural moisture
content However some soil shrinkage may occur if the natural soil is
dried out
Therefore to minimize effects due to soil shrinkage swelling under
on grade slabs we recommend that the on site soil be protected from
drying from the start of construction until the slab concrete placement
Select non expansive soils should be used as backfill behind the
retaining walls to minimize the additional pressure due to the swelling
potential of the natural soil
Lot Grading
Substantial thickness of fills are planned for the lot development
Proper compaction of the fill is essential to minimize ground settlements
All imported fill materials should be tested and approved by the
soils engineer prior to being used in the site
Prior to construction the area to receive any fill should be
cleared of vegetation and debris

The fills should be placed in horizontal lifts not exceeding 8
inches in compacted thickness and compacted to a minimum of 85 of its
maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78
Two horizontal to one vertical cut and fill slope ratios are
recommended
Fills placed on existing slopes should start from the bottom up and
should be keyed and benched to bond the new fill to the existing slope
The site should be graded to provide drainage of surface runoff away
from all foundations
House Foundations
It is anticipated that the entire house will be of slab on grade
construction
Spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the proposed
residence An allowable bearing value of 2000 p s f may be used on the
compacted soil
Interior footings on level areas should be embedded a minimum of 8
inches below the finished grade Perimeter wall footings should be
embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the adjacent finished grade
Surface water should be diverted away from foundations
Slab On Grade
The on site clayey silt soils have low swelling potential provided
they are protected from drying The thickened edge of the slab should be
embedded a minimum of 12 inches below the lowest adjacent finish grade

A 4 inch layer of 3B Fine gravel cushion fill should be provided
below the slab In order to protect against future moisture infiltration
an impervious moisture membrane may be incorporated below the slab
Concrete Driveway
It is critical that the subgrade soils should be maintained in a
moist condition and prevented from drying out prior to concrete placement
Joints are recommended in the driveway slab at about 8 to 10 foot
intervals and at discontinuities in order to allow for some relative
movement of the concrete

Drainage
We recommend that planters next to foundations should be avoided or
have concrete bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the
subsoils
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained
to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundation
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance
of the above recommended foundations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of
strength The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled tight
against the wall or slab footing immediately after setting of the concrete
to prevent ponding condition
Design Review
Plans and specifications for lot grading foundation and utility
construction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and
written comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the lot grading footing excavations and
backfill operations be observed by the soils engineer during construction
to see that adequate compaction has been obtained and that the subsurface
conditions are consistent with the findings of our investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above inspections
INVESTIGATION LIMITATIONS
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations
made in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation engineering
principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all other
warranties either expressed or implied
The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are based
on our site reconnaissance soil information from boring and laboratory
test data obtained from this soil investigation
Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encountered and cannot be
fully determined by taking soil samples Unforeseen conditions such as
expansive soil and soft spots may occur in localized areas and may require
