We understand that the contractor had some difficulty in attaining the compaction
and moisture content requirements during the compaction of the building pad
subgrades at the subject site As a result concerns regarding the requirements of the
subgrade soil compaction in the pavement areas were raised by the contractor
Therefore we have performed laboratory tests on the actual subgrade soils to evaluate
the pavement subgrade characteristics relating to the degree of the subgrade
compaction One sample consisting of reddish brown clayey silt with some gravels was
retrieved from a stockpile of the on site materials at the project site
A Modified Proctor test ASTM D 1557 91 and two laboratory California Bearing
Ratio CBR tests were performed on the sample retrieved from the site Based on the
Proctor test the subgrade soils have a maximum dry density of 101 pounds per cubic
foot p c f with an optimum moisture content of 25 percent Two CBR samples were
prepared with about 91 percent and 95 percent relative compaction and tested in
accordance with ASTM D 1883 Based on the results of CBR tests the sample with
91 percent relative compaction has a CBR value of 2 with a maximum expansion value
of about 0 1 percent and the sample with 95 percent relative compaction has a CBR
value of 15 with almost no swell after saturation The results of our laboratory tests are
presented on the attached Summary of Laboratory Test Results Plate 1

Based on the results of our laboratory CBR tests the degree of the compaction
has a significant influence on the subgrade supporting characteristics for the materials
tested However both CBR tests showed that the soils tested have little or no
expansion after saturation when prepared wet of the optimum moisture The pavement
section presented in our report was based on a CBR value of 15 and swell of less than
1 percent on moist compacted subgrade with 95 percent relative compaction
Therefore if the compaction requirement of subgrade soils is to be lowered to a
minimum of 90 percent relative compaction the pavement section recommended would
need to be revised to reflect the actual compacted subgrade conditions
It is our opinion that the subgrade soils may be compacted to a minimum of 95
percent relative compaction with moisture content of at least 2 percent above the
optimum moisture content if large compaction equipment is used and sufficient
compactive effort is applied However if the 95 percent relative compaction along with
the minimum 2 percent above optimum moisture content requirements could not be met
during construction after reasonable effort it is our opinion that the moisture content
requirement may be waived In this case the subgrade soils should be moisture
conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative compaction
