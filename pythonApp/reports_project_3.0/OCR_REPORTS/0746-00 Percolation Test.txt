This report presents the results of our percolation test
at Lot 13 of the proposed subdivision development on Kame
hameha Highway
The purpose of this investigation is to obtain additional
subsurface soil data and in situ seepage characteristics
at the eastern portion of the subject site as requested by
the Department of Health Two previous borings were drilled
at the western portion of the site at Lots 1 and 3 and per
colation tests were performed in both borings for details
refer to our Percolation Test Report prepared for the proposed
Sunset Beach Subdivision dated W

Field Exploration
One 4 inch diameter auger boring was drilled down to 22 feet
below the existing ground surface at Lot 13 of the proposed
site The location of the boring is shown on the enclosed
Site Plan Plate 1 The drilling operation was monitored
by our geologist who also logged the boring The log of
boring is presented on Plates A 1 A 2
Subsurface Conditions
The subsurface explorations disclose that the eastern
portion of the site is blanketed by a 12 foot layer of
Sli tO hard brown silty clay underlain by soft to very
soft brown to reddish and grayish brown silty clay with
traces of sand matrix which extended to the limits of the
exploration
Groundwater level was encountered in the drilled boring at
about 16 feet below the existing ground surface
Percolation Test
After the boring was drilled clean gravel was used to fill
up the lower portionof the hole below the groundwater level
to prevent potential cave in during percolation testing
The portion of the drilled hole above the groundwater level
was cased with a 34 inch diameter drilling casing

Water was then used to fill up the casing first and then
adjusted until a constant head of water developed at 13
feet below the ground surface Percolation rate of 2 9
gallon per minute was recorded
Based on the test results from the above bore hole it is
our opinion that the soils below the groundwater level will
provide adequate percolation rates for the proposed private
cesspool sewage disposal system Based on the percolation
test data obtained at the drilled holes a 6 foot diameter
cesspool extending a minimum depth of 5 feet into the soil
layer below the groundwater level about 23 feet from exist
ing ground surface would be required for each single family
residence
The space between the block lining and the undisturbed soil
side wall of 8 foot minimum diameter excavation should be
immediately backfilled with free drainage granular materials
Additional layer of granular filter material is also recommended
to be placed at the bottom of each cesspool
