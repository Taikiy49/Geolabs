INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our additional exterior geotechnical engineering
exploration and an elevation survey performed for the two story townhouse building structure
located adjacent to Kupuohi Street Building 26 of the Royal Knolls
Subdivision in the District of Kunia on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and
vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
GEOLABS INC
W O A Page 2
This report summarizes our additional findings and revised geotechnical engineering
recommendations resulting from our additional field exploration laboratory testing
engineering analyses and an elevation survey performed by ParEn Inc These revised
recommendations are intended for the remedial work design of the townhouse building
structure only The findings and recommendations presented herein are subject to the
limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
A geotechnical engineering exploration report presenting our findings was issued on
March 5 1999 Subsequently two additional exterior borings were drilled The purpose of
these borings was to determine the soil conditions below the areas exhibiting large
settlement
Additionally in accordance with the recommendations provided in our previous report
four monthly elevation surveys were performed at selected interior and exterior points The
purpose of these elevation surveys was to determine if the building was still moving and if
so at what rate

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The subsurface conditions encountered in the two additional borings were generally
consistent with those encountered in our previous exploration Therefore our assessment
of the probable causes of the building distress remains the same as that described in our
previous report
It is our opinion that the building distress is primarily due to compression of the thick
fill layer below the building caused by localized layers with insufficient compaction and
softening of the fills due to water infiltration in poorly drained areas Therefore our
recommended remedial work consists of transferring the major building loads to the hard
natural ground below the fills and improvement of the drainage around the building
The results of an elevation survey of established interior and exterior survey points
conducted between February and August 1999 indicated that the ground movements have
either significantly subsided or stopped The very small rates of movements recorded
indicate that the structural supports we have recommended are appropriate for this site
Since the ground movements have subsided a remedial alternative without
additional structural supports elimination of Atlas Piers was considered If structural
supports are not installed a waiting period of several years may be required to have some
level of confidence that the settlements and building distress have stopped This long
waiting period did not seem to be a viable alternative at this time since some of the
residences have indicated the desire to sell their units

Remedial Work Recommendations
Based on the building distress observed and the results of our exploration it is our
opinion that remedial work should include improvements to the surface drainage around the
building and structural support of the perimeter of the building
Drainage Improvements
Ponding conditions created by poor drainage can cause water infiltration into the
subsoils resulting in softening and compression of the soils It was noted that the
drainage around Building 26 is generally poor particularly where the building
settlements are large
The ground surface adjacent to the building should be shaped to drain water away
from the foundations to reduce the potential for surface water infiltration into the soils
below the structure This may involve the construction of swales connected to a
drainage pipe that discharges to the parking lot located to the east of the townhouse
structure In addition a 3 foot wide thickened edge sidewalk or impervious liner
should be installed adjacent to the perimeter of the building structure The thickened
edge should be embedded a minimum of 24 inches below the lowest adjacent grade
The sidewalk liner should have a slight slope away from the building The purpose
of the sidewalk or liner is to reduce the potential for soil moisture migration that could
cause additional soil swelling or soil shrinkage
Prior to placement of the concrete sidewalks the subgrade should be
moisture conditioned to near optimum moisture content and proof rolled or lightly
compacted to a relatively unyielding surface Over compaction of the subgrade
should be avoided The subgrade should also be moistened to maintain the near
optimum moisture content prior to placement of concrete
The sidewalks should be provided with construction joints at 3 foot intervals and
right angle intersections The excavated clay material may be used to re grade the
lot to slope away from the townhouse building structure

Structural Support
Since it appears that the soil under the structure has stabilized under the current load
we recommend that structural support now be provided at selected locations under
the building The structural support may consist of Atlas piers extending to below the
fill material and bearing into the residual soils at depths of up to about 40 feet below
the existing ground surface Since the piers would bypass the fill they would
generally be unaffected by possible future small settlements of the fill layer
We recommend that the structural support be installed around the perimeter of the
building We estimate that about 66 Atlas piers will be needed This could be
accomplished without relocation of the residents The recent settlement survey
monitoring indicates that the building settlement has subsided significantly
Therefore installation of the perimeter supports would probably be sufficient to
stabilize the structure We recommend that the performance of the perimeter
supported building be monitored for a period of about 6 months If movements and
distress continue additional Atlas piers may be installed at selected interior wall
locations These interior locations will depend on the performance of the building and
results of settlement surveys
installation of Atlas piers normally involves lifting the footing about to inch to
increase the amount of positive load transfer Due to the relatively flexible framework
attributed to wood structures we anticipate that some amount of movement may
occur after the installation of the Atlas piers due to redistribution of stresses
However we anticipate this time period for stress redistribution to be less than
3 months
For the above options we do recommend that the exterior survey points established
by ParEn Inc be surveyed on a quarterly basis for about 1 year following completion
of the Atlas pier installation drainage improvements and sidewalk construction The
survey points may need to be relocated to accommodate the new sidewalk liner
construction
GEOLABS INC
W O A Page 8
In addition we recommend that selected interior survey points established by
ParEn Inc be surveyed on a quarterly basis for about 1 year following completion of
the remedial work We also recommend that interior visual surveys of distressed
areas be conducted on a quarterly basis for about 1 year following completion of the
remedial work
Cosmetic work and patch work to the cracks in the walls floors and ceilings should
proceed after the above recommendations are completed Note that if cosmetic work
and patch work is conducted prior to the 1 year survey period some of the cosmetic
and patch work may need to be re worked at a later date
Although it is possible to re level the structure with Atlas piers it should be noted that
re leveling of the structure may produce differential floor or footing elevations and a
redistribution of building stresses to other areas and therefore cause further damage
to the townhouse building structure We do not recommend that re leveling be
performed at this time Re leveling should only be performed if Atlas piers are
installed for all bearing walls and foundations
Installation of the Atlas piers will likely require over excavation of about 2 feet laterally
beyond the Atlas pier location to allow installation of the bracket and pipe pile jacking
assembly The excavation will also extend laterally about 1 foot beneath the footing
Based on the foundation plans the footings appear to be large enough that
enlargement of the footing for proper support and resistance during installation will not
be needed The Atlas piers should be jacked downward until a bearing capacity of
20 000 pounds based on a 3 5 inch outside diameter steel pipe is obtained and to
a minimum depth of 40 feet below the existing foundations

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this addendum report are based in
part upon information obtained from the field exploration and information from the previous
floor level survey Variations of conditions between and beyond the field borings may occur
and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident until construction is
underway lf variations then appear evident it will be necessary to reevaluate the
recommendations presented in this report
The boring locations were determined by taping from structures indicated on the
Kulana Knolls Grading Plan by Fujita Associates Inc undated The locations of the
borings should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used
The stratification breaks described on the boring logs depict the approximate
boundaries between soil rock types and as such may denote a gradual transition Water
level data from the borings were measured on the dates described in the text of this report
These data have been reviewed and interpretations made in the formulation of this report
However it must be noted that fluctuation may occur due to variation in seasonal rainfall and
other factors

two borings designated as Boring Nos 4 and 5 to depths of about 54 and 60 feet below the

in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System Descriptions of the materials encountered are provided on the Logs
of Borings Plates A 1 and A 2

ASTM Test Designation D 1586 84 and D 3550 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel
standard penetration sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts
needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an 18 to 24 inch drive are
shown on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content ASTM D 2216 and unit weight ASTM D 2937 determinations were
performed on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil
properties The results of these tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate
sample depths
Six one inch ring compression collapse tests were performed on undisturbed samples
to evaluate the compression potential under surcharge pressures with no saturation and to
evaluate the collapse potential under surcharge pressures with saturation Results of these
compression collapse tests are presented on Plate B 1
Four one inch ring compression collapse tests were performed on samples remolded
at a water content of about 20 percent to a dry density of about 90 pcf The purpose of
these tests were to evaluate the compression potential under surcharge pressures with no
saturation and to evaluate the collapse potential under surcharge pressures with saturation
Results of these compression collapse tests are presented on Plate B 2
