performed for the Replace Water Tank S2 Project located in the Halawa area on the
island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown
on the Project Location Map Plate 1

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located off Bougainville Drive within the Aliamanu Military
Reservation in the Halawa area on the island of Oahu Hawaii The proposed project
consists of the removal of the existing 6 million gallon M G steel rivet tank S2 and
the construction of a new 6 M G steel water tank as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
We understand that the new water tank will have an outside diameter of about
165 feet and a height of about 40 feet The roof structure will span between the
perimeter wall and the center columns Details of the project construction were not
available at the time this report was prepared We understand that the proposed tank
structure will be constructed near existing grades Therefore we anticipate that grading
at the proposed water tank site would generally consist of minor cuts and fills on the
order of about 1 foot or less to achieve a level pad for the new water tank

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu is composed largely of the weathered remnants of two extinct
shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae forms the bulk
of the western third of the island while the younger shield Koolau forms the majority
of the eastern two thirds of the island It is believed that Waianae became extinct while
Koolau was still active and its eastern flank is partially buried below Koolau lavas in
central Oahu
After a long period of cessation of the shield building volcanic activity returned
with the formation of a series of lava flows cinder cones and tuff cones which are
known as the Honolulu Volcanic Series Many of the eruptions were rather explosive
in nature due to the interaction of groundwater with the rising magma resulting in steam
explosions which expelled large quantities of pyroclastic material predominantly ash
and cinder Some explosive eruptions produced ash that cemented together rapidly to
form a firm rock known as tuff locally known as mudrock
The project site is located on the western flank of the Aliamanu Crater of the
Honolulu Volcanic Series The subsoil conditions at the project site are generally
formed by the deposition of recent pyroclastic materials and lava flows from eruptions
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3387 20 Page 5
of the vents of members of the Honolulu Volcanic Series including Aliamanu Salt
Lake and Makalapa

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field exploration the proposed water tank site is underlain by a thin
cover of fills overlying tuff formation at shallow depths Based on our evaluation of the
subsurface conditions with respect to the proposed water tank construction the
principal geotechnical engineering considerations for the project include the following
1 foundation support for the proposed water tank structure
2 subgrade preparation below the tank structure and
3 trench excavation and backfill of utility lines
Based on the results of our field exploration we believe that a shallow foundation
system bearing on the tuff formation encountered at shallow depths or bearing on a
compacted subgrade may be used to support the water tank structure
Detailed discussion and recommendations for design of foundations site
grading trench excavation and backfill and other geotechnical aspects of the project
are presented in the following sections of this report

Foundations
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered at the project site we
recommend that a shallow foundation system bearing on the tuff formation or a
prepared subgrade be used to support the new water tank structure The walls of the
new tank structure may be supported on a perimeter ring footing embedded a minimum
of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent grade An allowable bearing pressure of up to
on the prepared subgrade This allowable bearing pressure recommended is for dead
plus live loads and may be increased by one third for transient loads such as
temporary wind and or seismic forces
We anticipate that the bottom plate of the tank structure will be supported on a
bedding layer of sand or similar material to provide more uniform support of the steel
tank structure We recommend that the sand bedding layer consist of a uniformly
graded sand of about 6 inches in thickness The sand bedding should be compacted
using a vibratory plate for a minimum of 4 passes to provide a firm base for the bottom
plate of the tank structure
Where the foundations are to be located adjacent to new or existing utilities the
foundations should extend to a depth below an imaginary 45 degree plane projected
upward from the bottom of the invert of the utility pipe This requirement is necessary
to avoid surcharging adjacent utilities with additional structural loads and to reduce the
potential for foundation settlement
If the footings are designed in accordance with the recommendations presented
herein total settlement of footings is estimated to be on the order of less than 1 inch
with differential settlements on the order of about inch
GEOLABS HAWAIi
W O 3387 20 Page 8
Lateral loads acting on the tank structure may be resisted by frictional resistance
between the base of the foundation and the bearing materials and by passive earth
pressure developed against the near vertical faces of the embedded portion of footings
A coefficient of friction of 0 35 may be used for footings bearing on the tuff formation
and or compacted subgrade Resistance to lateral loads due to passive pressure may
be calculated using an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot
of depth p c f for footings embedded in the tuff formation and or compacted fills This
value assumes that the soil around the footings is relatively undisturbed or well
compacted Unless covered by pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the
upper 12 inches below finished grade should be neglected
If soft and or loose soils are encountered at the bottom of the footing excavation
they should be over excavated to expose competent native material and replaced with
select granular material compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage
of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 1557 91
It is recommended that a representative from Geolabs Hawaii observe the
foundation excavations prior to placement of reinforcing steel to evaluate the
competency of the bearing materials and the embedment depths

site will consist of minor cuts and fills on the order of 1 foot or less Site grading

whether the exposed soil conditions are similar to those encountered in our exploration

The following recommendations are intended to provide guidelines for the design of site
grading work
At the on set of earthwork all areas within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable
materials generated from demolition of the existing Tank S2 should be removed and
disposed of properly off site to reduce potential contamination of the excavated
materials
Following clearing and grubbing subgrades to receive fill should be scarified to
a depth of 8 inches where possible moisture conditioned and compacted to a
minimum of 95 percent relative compaction If subgrades are tuff formation they
should be proof rolled with a vibratory drum roller 15 ton minimum or similar heavy
equipment a minimum of 6 passes prior to fill placement Any soft loose material
disclosed during clearing operations should be over excavated and backfilled with
engineered fill compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Relative
compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the
maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 1557 91
Generally the on site granular materials may be used as a source of fill and
backfill materials Where used as fill and or backfill material the on site soils should
be placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness moisture conditioned
to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent
relative compaction
Any imported material should be tested and approved by Geolabs Hawaii for its
intended use prior to delivery to the job site Where needed imported granular
materials should consist of crushed coral mudrock basalt or cinder sand The material
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3387 20 Page 10
should be well graded from coarse to fine with no particles greater than 3 inches in
largest dimension The material should have a laboratory CBR value of at least 20 and
a swell value of less than 1 percent when tested in accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 1883 The imported material should also be moisture conditioned and
compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
Utilities
We envision that new utility lines and or piping network will be required for the
proposed new water tank For pipes bearing on the dense soils and tuff formation
encountered in the borings a poorly graded granular bedding material with little fines
such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation S4C or equivalent is
recommended below the pipes as bedding material Other alternate pipe bedding
material should consist of granular material containing minimal fines and no particles
larger than 1 inch in maximum dimension Free draining granular materials such as
No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation should be used for the trench
backfill up to about 12 inches above the pipes to provide adequate support around the
pipes and to reduce compaction of the backfill thus reducing the possibility of
damaging the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipe
to the top of the subgrade or finished grade should consist of the excavated on site
granular material containing no particles greater than 6 inches in largest dimension
Trench backfill should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture placed
in 8 inch maximum level loose lifts and mechanically compacted to not less than
90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for future ground subsidence
Where trenches are located below the water tank structure and pavement areas the
upper 2 feet of trench backfill below the finish subgrade should be compacted to a
minimum of 95 percent relative compaction

Drainage
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and maintained to drain
surface run off away from the foundations The finish grades outside the proposed
water tank should be sloped to shed water away from foundations and to reduce the
potential for ponding Excessive landscape watering near the foundations should also
be avoided Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have concrete bottoms
and drains to reduce water infiltration into the subsoils
Seismic Coefficient
Based on the subsoil conditions encountered the subsoil generally consists of
tuff formation Based on this information the project site may be classified from a
seismic analysis standpoint as a site with a Type 1 soil profile corresponding to an S
soil coefficient of 1 0 Uniform Building Code Table No 23 J 1991 Edition

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling coring and
sampling four borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 4 as shown on the Site
Plan Plate 2 The depths of our borings range from about 12 to 13 feet below the
existing ground surface The borings were drilled using a truck mounted drill rig
equipped with continuous flight augers and rotary coring tools
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our Geologist who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by
testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified
Soil Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representation of the
materials encountered are provided on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 4
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils
by driving a 2 inch O D standard penetration sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling
30 inches The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the last 12 inches of an
18 inch drive are shown on the logs of borings at the appropriate sample depths
Core samples of rock materials encountered at the site were obtained using
diamond core drilling techniques in general accordance with ASTM Standard Practice
D 2113 83 Diamond Core Drilling for Site Investigation Core drilling is a rotary drilling
method which uses a hollow bit to cut into the rock formation The rock material left
in the hollow core of the bit is mechanically recovered for examination and description
Recovery REC is used as a subjective guide to the interpretation of the relative
quality of rock masses Recovery is defined as the actual length of material recovered
from a coring attempt versus the length of the core attempt such as if 3 7 feet of
material is recovered from a 5 0 foot core run the recovery would be 74 percent and
would be shown on the Logs of Boring as REC 74
The Rock Quality Designation RQD is also a subjective guide to the relative
quality of rock masses RQD is defined as the percentage of the core run that is sound
material in excess of 4 inches in length without any discontinuities discounting any
drilling induced fractures or breaks If 2 feet of sound material is recovered from a
5 0 foot core run the RQD would be 50 percent and would be shown on the Logs of
Boring as RQD 50 Generally the following is used to describe the relative quality
of the rock based on the Practical Handbook of Physical Properties of Rocks and
Minerals
