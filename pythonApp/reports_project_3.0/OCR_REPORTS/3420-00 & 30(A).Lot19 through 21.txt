Regional Geology
The Island of Hawaii is the largest island in the Hawaiian Archipelago and covers
an area of approximately 4 000 square miles The island was formed by the activity of the
following five shield volcanoes Kohala long extinct Mauna Kea activity during recent
geologic time Hualalai last erupted in 1801 1803 and Mauna Loa and Kilauea both
still active
The project site is situated on the southern flank of Hualalai Mountain which
makes up the west central portion of the island The lava formation encountered at the
project site appears to consist of a a type lava flows at the ground surface which spread
and ponded as they approached the ocean Pahoehoe lavas may be encountered at
some depth below the ground surface A a lava is typically characterized by a porous
rough and irregular flow surface resembling a jagged accumulation of rock fragments
including cobbles and boulders Pahoehoe is characterized by a smooth rope like or
billowy surface and an internal structure of vesicular porous rock
Cavities are commonly encountered in basaltic lavas especially pahoehoe type
lava Cavities form when lavas are still in a molten state and represent both lava tubes
intra flow cavities and blisters and pockets inter flow cavities Lava tubes form when
molten lava drains from the cooling flow leaving a hollow tube like structure which may
extend for a large longitudinal distance along the flow Blisters and pockets are generally
smaller in horizontal extent
Basalt is considered to be a relatively permeable rock and can transmit water quite
readily in beth the horizontal and vertical directions In general water is transmitted
through the porous rock matrix along joints fractures and inter flow contacts cavities
and along clinker layers Clinker and cavities serve as the primary water transmission
features because of their high permeability
Subsurface Conditions
As previously mentioned the Pualani Estates Subdivision project site is located in
the District of North Kona on the Island of Hawaii In general the original topography of
the project site prior to site grading activities consisted of irregular or hummocky lava
flow terrain with frequent topographic depressions separated by adjacent higher relief
mounds and low ridge features

Discussion and Recommendations
Based on the grading plan for the project the original ground surface elevations at
Lot Nos 19 through 21 of the Pualani Estates Subdivision Phase 1 ranged from about
316 to 332 feet Mean Sea Level MSL The building pads were generally graded to
finished pad elevations of about 326 to 330 feet MSL In general the finished pad
elevations were set at elevations directly below the concrete slabs on grade and cushion
fill Mass grading at Lot Nos 19 through 21 generally consisted of embankment fills of up
to about 10 feet in height and excavations of up to about 2 feet in depth to achieve the
design grades
Extensive subgrade preparation procedures were employed within the grading
limits at the commencement of the mass grading operations to reduce the potential for
collapse of near surface cavities and or voids within the lots The intent of the subgrade
preparation procedures was to assist in detecting and collapsing near surface cavities
and or voids contained in the porous near surface materials
In general the areas to receive fills were generally proof rolled with a Caterpillar
825 sheepsfoot roller compactor or similar equipment for about eight passes to help detect
and collapse near surface cavities and or voids
Fill materials consisting of some cobbles and boulders and structural fills were
placed in the fill areas of the project site Structural fills consisted of well graded granular
materials generally smaller than 6 inches in dimension In general the fill materials within
the top 2 feet of the building pads consisted of structural fill materials General fills
consisting of materials generally smaller than about 12 to 18 inches in dimension were
placed below structural fills It should be noted that large boulders of up to about 5 feet in
dimension were generally placed at least about 4 feet below the finished grades within the
pad areas of the lots and choked off with finer sized materials In addition boulders were
generally placed about 6 feet or more horizontally from the face of the fill slopes
Subgrades in cut areas were generally proof rolled with a Caterpillar 825
sheepsfoot roller compactor or similar equipment for about eight passes to help detect and
collapse near surface cavities and or voids It should be noted that the mauka portions of
