PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The senior housing project is near the northwestern corner of the intersection of
Iwilei Road and North King Street in Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project
site is approximately bounded by Kaaahi Place to the north North King Street to the east
and Iwilei Road to the south Based on the information provided it is proposed to
construct a thirteen story residential building and a four story parking garage for a senior
housing development
Based on the highly compressible lagoonal deposits encountered at shallow
depths and the moderate to high structural loads anticipated for the planned structures
we understand that the residential building and parking garage will be supported on a
deep foundation system consisting of inch octagonal precast prestressed
concrete piles designed with an allowable compressive load capacity of about 75 tons per
pile Based on our field exploration and test pile program we envision the foundation
support system would consist of driven piles end bearing on the underlying very dense
alluvial colluvial boulder deposits and or very hard basalt rock formation anticipated at
depths ranging from about 55 to 85 feet below the existing ground surface In order to

METHODOLOGY
An Instantel Minimate Plus was used to monitor the vibration generated during the
test pile driving operations A sensor geophone was placed at varying distances from the
test piles during the course of the driving operations in order to obtain vibration data
representative of different distances from the vibration source
The Instantel Minimate Plus consists of a 3 component geophone and a signal
conditioner recorder unit The geophone senses ground motion along three mutually
perpendicular orthogonal axes thus providing detection of ground motion in the vertical
transverse and longitudinal directions The signal conditioner recorder unit receives and
processes these signals to provide a graphic record of vibrations Resultant peak particle
velocities RPPV in units of inches per second ips are automatically computed by vector
summation of the motions along the orthogonal axes
The Minimate Plus was set at a scanning interval of every two seconds during the
vibration monitoring process During the two second scanning interval the Minimate Plus
samples the vibration data at a rate of 2 048 data points per second and stores the
resultant peak level of vibration which occurs during that two second interval At the end of

FINDINGS AND DISCUSSIONS
A summary of the resultant peak vibration levels observed during the test pile
driving operations is presented on Plates 4 1 and 4 2 Two different distances were used
for the data processing one for the horizontal surface distance between the pile and the
geophone and the other for the slant distance between the geophone and the pile tip at
the approximate depth at which the maximum vibration was observed
Since vibration amplitudes for a given energy source attenuate with increasing
distance in exponential form graphic representation of vibration amplitude versus distance
data will appear linear when plotted in log log graph form A regression analysis power
curve was performed on the vibration data to determine representative rates of vibration
attenuation with increasing distance from the source One analysis was performed using
surface distances and another using slant distances The raw data and the regression
lines are shown on the Test Pile Driving Attenuation Plot on Plate 5 Our evaluation of the
distance versus particle velocity attenuation data sets indicated that most of the peak
vibrations were generated with the pile tips near the maximum embedment depths
The threshold for people to perceive vibrations occurs at a relatively low level of
motion Depending on character duration and repetitiveness of vibrations perception
may occur at levels between 0 01 and These levels are well below those
required to cause damage to typical structures
Vibration damage thresholds for pile driving induced motions have not been
specifically established A number of researchers have presented a variety of proposed
criteria The criteria that we normally use are related to the type of construction age and
general condition of structures and are adopted from a variety of studies of actual damage
thresholds observed for blasting operations and other vibration inducing sources Based
on the following recommended threshold of damage criteria should provide
reasonable indicators for reducing the potential for direct vibration damage

The structures adjacent to this project may be classified as Class II structures
Because of the repetitive nature of pile driving vibrations it has been recommended that
the above categories be lowered by one class therefore a vibration damage threshold
criterion of 1 0 inch per second was used for the project
In general the peak particle velocities measured during the test pile driving
operations are lower than the reduced threshold of 1 0 inch per second Therefore it is
our professional opinion that the maximum vibration caused by the test pile driving
operations are within acceptable range
RESULTS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The attenuation plot for vibrations based on surface distance measurements is
presented on the Anticipated Pile Driving Vibration Levels Plate 6 Two lines parallel to
the regression line were also constructed on the plot One represents the upper bound of
observed data and the other is the lower bound The upper bound line approximates the
maximum vibration level we might expect to reach during the course of production pile
driving operation for a specific distance from the vibration source The Class II Reduced
threshold value of was also drawn on this plot The intersection of the upper bound
line with the Class II Reduced criteria line occurs at a distance of about 30 feet This
suggests that damages may occur to structures that are located within 30 feet of the
vibration source While this distance refers to maximum anticipated vibration levels we
would expect average levels to be considerably lower
Since some of the production piles will be located within the suggested distance of
concern of 30 feet we recommend continuing the vibration monitoring services during the
production pile driving program This would serve to record and document the vibration
levels generated and to evaluate if adverse vibration levels are generated during the
production pile driving operations
