Project Considerations
The project involves improvement to an existing concrete lined drainage ditch
between residential properties 14 98 and 99 along Wainohia Street and
residential properties 14 116 to 118 along Ahikoe Street in the
Makakilo area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The drainage ditch is a 12 inch wide by
10 to 15 inch deep concrete ditch about 170 feet long from the ditch high point to the
outlet
We understand the concrete ditch is undersized and has overtopped during
heavy storm events In addition an 18 inch drain line from the higher elevation
residential properties along Ahikoe Street outlets into the concrete ditch The relatively
high velocity drain water exiting out of the pipe causes extreme turbulence in the
concrete ditch resulting in overtopping We understand that it is planned to widen the
ditch and install a drain manhole at the 18 inch drain line outlet to dissipate some of the
energy of the flow We understand a retaining wall will be required for grade separation
near the manhole structure

Discussion and Recommendations
Detailed discussions and recommendations for design of the Ahikoe Ditch
Drainage Improvements project are presented in the following sections herein
Concrete Lined Ditch
We understand that the existing concrete lined drainage ditch will be removed and
replaced with a wider concrete lined drainage ditch Based on the anticipated subsurface
conditions that may be encountered along the drainage ditch we recommend providing a
bedding layer below the concrete ditch The bedding layer should consist of a minimum
6 inch thick layer of aggregate subbase course or non expansive select granular fill
material to provide more uniform support of the new concrete lined drainage ditch
As mentioned previously soft loose unconsolidated soils were observed at the
existing ground surface Soft and or loose soils encountered at the bottom of the bedding
layer should be over excavated to expose the underlying firm and or dense materials The
resulting over excavation should be backfilled with well compacted select granular fill
materials compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction

Retaining Wall
Based on the information provided a retaining wall is required for grade separation
between the new drainage ditch and the new drain manhole The following general
guidelines may be used for design of the new retaining wall
Retaining Wall Foundation
Based on the anticipated subsurface conditions at the project site we believe that
the retaining wall may be supported on a shallow foundation system An allowable
bearing pressure of up to 2 500 pounds per square foot psf may be used for
design of foundations bearing on the recompacted in situ clayey soils This bearing
transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic forces
Wall footings should have a minimum width of 18 inches and should be embedded
a minimum depth of 24 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade The
bottom of footing excavation should be recompacted to provide a firm and
unyielding base At the time of our reconnaissance unconsolidated soils were
observed to a depth of about 1 foot below the existing ground surface The
unconsolidated soils encountered below the bottom of footing should be
recompacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
Foundations next to utility trenches or easements should be embedded below a
45 degree imaginary plane extending upward from the bottom edge of the utility
trench or the footing should extend to a depth as deep as the inverts of the utility
lines This requirement is necessary to avoid surcharging adjacent below grade
structures with additional structural loads and to reduce the potential for appreciable
foundation settlement
Lateral loads acting on the retaining wall may be resisted by friction developed
between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil and by passive earth
pressure acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system A
coefficient of friction of 0 30 may be used for footings bearing on the recompacted
in situ clayey silt soils Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be estimated
using an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth
pcf The passive earth pressure value assumes that the soils around footings are
well compacted Unless covered by pavements or slabs the passive pressure
resistance in the upper 12 inches of the soils should be neglected
A Geolabs representative should monitor footing excavations prior to placement of
reinforcing steel and concrete to confirm the foundation bearing conditions and the
required embedment depths

Drainage
The retaining wall should be well drained to reduce the build up of hydrostatic
pressures A typical drainage system would consist of a 12 inch wide zone of
permeable material such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation
placed directly around a perforated pipe perforations facing down at the base of
the wall discharging to an appropriate outlet or weepholes As an alternative a
prefabricated drainage product such as MiraDrain or EnkaDrain may be used
instead of the drainage material The prefabricated drainage product should also be
hydraulically connected to a perforated pipe at the base of the wall
The backfill from the bottom of the wall to the bottom of the perforated pipe or
weephole should consist of relatively impervious materials to reduce the potential
for significant water infiltration into the subsurface In addition the upper 12 inches
of the retaining wall backfill should consist of relatively impervious materials to
reduce the potential for significant water infiltration behind the retaining wall unless
covered by concrete slabs at the surface
Excavation
Based on the grading plan prepared by M E Pacific Inc for the Ahikoe
Subdivision dated May 26 1983 it appears that boulder fill may have been placed in the
area south of the drainage ditch The grading plan shows the approximate lateral limits of
the boulder fill but the bottom and top elevations of the boulder fill were not provided
We believe there could be a potential for encountering boulders during excavation
of the retaining wall foundations and drain manhole Therefore grading contractors for the
project should be cautioned that boulders may be encountered in the excavations
Excavations encountering large boulders may require chipping and or the use of hoerams
to excavate the materials and to achieve the finished grades
The above discussions regarding the rippability of the surface materials are based
on our experience in the project vicinity Contractors bidding on this project should be
encouraged to review and understand the geologic environment of the project site and to
examine the site conditions and soil data to make their own prudent interpretation

will be required for the project
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
cleared thoroughly Debris deleterious materials and other unsuitable materials should be

areas designated to receive fill or future improvements should be over excavated to

The materials generated from the cut areas may be reused as a source of general
fill material provided that they are free of vegetation deleterious materials and rock
fragments greater than 3 inches in maximum dimension If required import fill should
consist of non expansive select granular material such as crushed basalt or coral The
material should be well graded from coarse to fine with particles no larger than 3 inches in
largest dimension and should contain between 10 and 30 percent particles passing the
No 200 sieve The material should have a laboratory CBR value of 20 or more and should
have a maximum swell of less than 1 percent Geolabs should test imported fill material for
conformance with these recommendations prior to delivery to the project site for its
intended use
Fill materials should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture
content placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a
minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry
density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil
established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 Optimum moisture is the water content
percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
Compaction should be accomplished by mechanical rollers or other types of
acceptable compaction equipment Compaction by water tamping jetting or ponding
should not be allowed for the on site soils
A Geolabs representative should monitor site grading operations to observe
whether undesirable materials are encountered and to confirm whether the exposed
soil rock conditions are similar to those encountered in our field exploration
Fill Slopes
Fill slopes should be designed with a slope inclination of 2H 1V or flatter In
addition fills should be keyed and benched into the existing slope to provide stability of the
new fill against sliding The filling operations should start at the lowest point and continue
up in level horizontal compacted layers in accordance with the fill placement
recommendations presented above Fill slopes should be constructed by overfilling and
cutting back to the design slope ratio to obtain a well compacted slope face
Excessive surface water runoff over the fill slope faces likely will cause erosion of
the exposed soils thus jeopardizing the long term stability and performance of the fill
slopes Therefore fill slopes should be protected by appropriate slope planting or by other
