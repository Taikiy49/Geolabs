presents the results of our geotechnical engineering analysis performed for the drilled

This report summarizes the results of our engineering analyses for the proposed 
project.  The recommendations presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at the 
end of this report. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The project site is located between Malakole Street and Lauwiliwili Street in the 
Kalaeloa area on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. The project involves the installation of 
four new steel transmission line monopoles. The new power poles are designated as P17, 
P18, P19, and P20.  We understand that the new monopoles will be supported by 4 and 
5-foot diameter drilled shafts.  In addition, we understand that the structural loading has 
been modified for the project.

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS 
The subsurface conditions encountered in Boring Nos. 1 and 2 from our report 
dated May 29, 2015 were used in our drilled shaft analyses.  Boring Nos. 1 and 2 
extended to depths of about 42 and 67 feet below the existing ground surface. 
Boring Nos. 1 and 2 generally encountered a surface fill layer ranging from about 
1.5 to 25 feet thick underlain by sandstone and coral formation to about 28 to 43-foot 
depths.  Below the sandstone and coral formation, coralline detritus and lagoonal deposits 
were encountered extending to the maximum depths explored of about 67 feet below the 
existing ground surface. 
The surface fill consisted of medium dense silty sands and gravels and very soft 
sandy silt. The sandstone and coral formation were medium hard to hard. The coralline 
detritus consisted of loose to dense silty/gravelly sand.  The lagoonal deposit was 
composed of medium stiff clayey silt with some sand and gravel.  
Groundwater was encountered in Boring Nos. 1 and 2 from about 22 and 49.8 feet 
below the existing ground surface at the time of our field exploration, respectively.  The 
groundwater depths correspond to about Elevations +1.6 to +2.9 feet Mean Sea Level. 
However, it should be noted that groundwater levels may also vary with seasonal rainfall, 
time of the year, and other factors. 
DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS 
Based on the results of our field exploration in our report dated May 29, 2015, the 
project site is generally underlain by a medium dense to very soft surface fill layer 
underlain by medium hard to hard sandstone and coral formation followed by loose to 
dense coralline detritus and medium stiff to stiff lagoonal deposits to the maximum depth 
explored of 67 feet below the existing ground surface.  We encountered groundwater in 
Boring Nos. 1 and 2 at about 22 and 67 feet below the existing ground surface. 
New power pole structures supported by a drilled shaft foundation will be installed 
for the project.  The structural loading provided and our drilled shaft recommendations are 
provided below.

Drilled Shaft Foundations

P18.  For Poles P19 and P20, 60-inch diameter shafts will be used.  In addition, we

LIMITATIONS 
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon 
information obtained from the field borings in our report entitled “Geotechnical Engineering 
Exploration, Kalaeloa-Ewa Nui 138KV Structure Replacements, Ewa, Oahu, Hawaii” dated 
May 29, 2015.  Variations of conditions beyond the field boring may occur, and the nature 
and extent of these variations may not become evident until construction is underway.  If 
variations then appear evident, it will be necessary to re-evaluate the recommendations 
presented in this report. 
This report has been prepared for the exclusive use of Hawaiian Electric 
Company, Inc. for specific application to the New HECO Power Poles P17 to P20, 
Kalaeloa-Ewa Nui 138KV Structure Replacements project in accordance with generally 
accepted geotechnical engineering principles and practices.  No warranty is expressed or 
implied. 
This report has been prepared solely for the purpose of the drilled shaft foundations 
for Power Poles P17 to P20 of the proposed project.  Therefore, this report may not 
contain sufficient data, or the proper information, for use to form the basis for preparation 
of construction cost estimates or contract bidding.  A contractor wishing to bid on this 
project should retain a competent geotechnical engineer to assist in the interpretation of 
this report and/or performance of site-specific exploration for bid estimating purposes. 
The owner/client should be aware that unanticipated soil conditions are commonly 
encountered.  Unforeseen soil conditions, such as perched groundwater, soft deposits, 
hard layers or cavities, may occur in localized areas and may require additional probing or 
corrections in the field (which may result in construction delays) to attain a properly 
constructed project.  Therefore, a sufficient contingency fund is recommended to 
accommodate these possible extra costs. 
This geotechnical exploration conducted at the site was not intended to investigate 
the potential presence of hazardous materials existing at the site.  The equipment, 
techniques, and personnel used to conduct a geo-environmental exploration differ 
substantially from those applied in geotechnical engineering.
