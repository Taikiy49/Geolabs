INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed UNOCAL Gas Station Mini Mart project located at the
intersection of Ward Avenue and Beretania Street in Honolulu on the island of Oahu
Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering recommenda
tions resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering analyses
These recommendations are intended for the design of foundation site grading and
excavation only The findings and recommendations presented herein are subject to
the limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to demolish the existing gas station and to construct a new gas
station mini mart at the intersection of Ward Avenue and Beretania Street in Honolulu
on the island of Oahu Hawaii Based on the information provided the site has an area
of approximately 11 807 square feet We understand that the existing station building
pump islands pavements and other structures will be demolished in order to construct
the proposed new facility We also understand that the proposed new facility will
consist of a one story mini mart with a footprint of approximately 1 697 square feet
several pump islands and two underground fuel storage tanks
The finish floor elevations for the proposed new structures such as the mini mart
and pump islands were not available at the time this report was prepared Based on
our field observations the site is relatively level therefore we envision that the
proposed new structures will be constructed near the existing grades Based on this

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lavas from
two shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated
to be of the middle to late Pliocene in age and Koolau is estimated to be of the late
Pliocene to early Pleistocene in age After a long period of volcanic inactivity during
which erosion incised deep valleys into the Koolau shield volcanic activity returned with
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3405 00 D Page 5
a series of lava flows followed by cinder and tuff cone formations These series are
referred to as the Honolulu Volcanic Series
The project site is on the southern flank of Punchbowl Hill The Punchbowl Hill
Puowaina is a tuff cone near the center of Honolulu built against the end of a spur of
the Koolau Range The tuff is mostly brown palagonitized vitric ash and lapilli with
scattered fragments of coral limestone and Koolau basalt
During the Pleistocene Epoch Ice Age sea levels fluctuated in response to the
cycles of continental glaciation As the glaciers grew and advanced less water was
available to fill the oceanic basins such that sea levels fell below the present stands of
the sea When the glaciers melted and receded an excess of water became available
such that the sea levels rose to above the present sea level
The processes of erosion and deposition were affected by these glacio eustatic
sea level fluctuations When the sea level was low the erosional base level was
correspondingly lower and valleys were carved to depths below the present sea level
When the sea level was high the erosional base level was raised such that sediments
accumulated at higher elevations

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field exploration the proposed project site is generally underlain
by medium dense to dense sands extending to depths of about 16 to 20 feet below the
existing ground surface Groundwater was not encountered in the borings at the time
of our field exploration
Based on our field exploration and laboratory testing we believe that a shallow
foundation system bearing on the near surface medium dense sandy soils may be used
to support the proposed structures Slabs may be supported on grade provided that
the subgrades are prepared as recommended herein
We anticipate minor site grading consisting of cuts and fills on the order of less
than 2 feet will be needed to provide level areas for buildings In addition we
anticipate that an excavation of up to about 15 feet and subsequent backfilling will be
required for construction of the new underground fuel storage tank structures Due to
the relatively deep excavation temporary shoring may be needed to support the
proposed excavation
Detailed discussions and recommendations for design of foundation site grading
excavation and other geotechnical aspects of the project are presented in the following
sections of this report
Foundations
The proposed gas station mini mart facility will consist of a mini mart several
pump islands and two underground fuel storage tanks Based on the subsurface
conditions encountered in our exploration we believe that a shallow foundation system
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3405 00 D Page 8
consisting of spread and or continuous footings may be used to support the proposed
structures
An allowable bearing pressure of up to 3 000 p s f may be used for the design
of footings bearing on the medium dense near surface sandy soils and or fill material
This allowable bearing pressure is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by
one third 1 for transient loads such as temporary wind and or seismic forces
Footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent
finished grade We estimate that the total footing settlements under the anticipated
design loads to be on the order of less than 1 inch with differential settlements of
approximately inch
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by frictional resistance
between the base of the spread footing and the bearing materials and by passive earth
pressure developed against the near vertical faces of the embedded portion of footings
A coefficient of friction of 0 4 may be used between the base of the footings and the
supporting materials Resistance due to passive pressure may be calculated using an
equivalent fluid pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth p c f for
footings embedded in the sandy soils These values assume that the concrete for
footings is placed directly against the footing excavations Unless covered by
pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the upper 12 inches below finished grade
should be neglected
Uplift loads especially for the canopy of the pump islands may be resisted by
a combination of the following 1 dead weight of the structure and 2 weight of
backfill material above the footing where applicable Contribution to the uplift resis
tance due to the weight of the backfill above the footing may be estimated using a unit
weight of 100 p c f
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3405 00 D Page 9
Where footings are to be located adjacent to below grade structures or utilities
the footings should extend to a depth below an imaginary 45 degree plane projected
upward from the bottom of the below grade structure or utility This requirement is
necessary to avoid surcharging adjacent below grade structures with additional
structural loads and to reduce the potential for foundation settlement
Footing excavations should not be allowed to dry before placing concrete If
shrinkage cracks appear in the footing excavations the soil should be thoroughly
moistened to close all cracks and recompacted to provide a firm base prior to concrete
placement
Footing excavations should be observed by a representative of Geolabs Hawaii
prior to the placement of reinforcing steel or concrete to confirm the competency of the
bearing materials and embedment depths If unsuitable materials are encountered in
the footing excavations these materials should be over excavated to expose firm soils
and replaced with compacted engineered fill
Slabs On Grade
We anticipate that concrete slabs on grade will be used for the proposed
structures such as the mini mart and pump islands We recommend that the subgrade
be properly prepared and kept moist prior to placement of cushion fill and slab
concrete We envision that the slabs on grade will bear on the medium dense sandy
soils near the ground surface As previously noted a thin clayey silt layer which is
potentially expansive was encountered in Boring No 2 Therefore we recommend that
the clayey silt layer where encountered be removed to a maximum depth of 12 inches
and be replaced with compacted granular soils
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3405 00 D Page 10
In general the subgrades for slabs on grades should be scarified to a depth of
at least 8 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture and compacted
to not less than 90 percent relative compaction The compaction requirement should
be increased to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction for areas subjected to
vehicular traffic such as the pump islands Relative compaction refers to the in place
dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same
soil determined in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91 Optimum
moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the
maximum dry density
For more uniform support of interior slabs mini mart we recommend that the
concrete slab on grade be supported on a minimum 4 inch cushion fill The cushion
fill layer should consist of open graded gravels such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM
C 33 No 67 gradation which will also serve as a capillary moisture break The slab
cushion fill should be placed as soon as possible after the scarification and
recompaction of the subgrade soils as recommended above For slabs on grade with
moisture sensitive surfacing we recommend that an impermeable membrane be placed
over the cushion fill to reduce potential migration of moisture vapor through the
concrete slab In order to promote more uniform curing of the slab concrete and to
provide protection of the vapor membrane we recommend that a 2 inch layer of moist
fine sand be placed above the membrane prior to placing the slab concrete The sand
should be moistened slightly prior to placing concrete
Where concrete slabs will be subjected to vehicular traffic such as the pump
islands we recommend that the slab be supported on a 6 inch layer of base course
_compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction in lieu of the 4 inch cushion
fill layer and 2 inch sand The base course should consist of crushed basalt aggregate
The subgrade should also be scarified to a minimum depth of 8 inches moisture
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3405 00 D Page 11
conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to not less than
95 percent relative compaction For design of structural slabs a modulus of subgrade
reaction of 250 pounds per cubic inch p c i may be used for the compacted base
course The thickened edges of slabs adjacent to any unpaved area should be
embedded at least 12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade
Site Grading
Final grading plans were not available at the time this report was prepared
However we anticipate grading with cuts and fills on the order of 2 feet and excavation
and backfill of up to about 15 feet relative to the existing ground surface may be
expected for the proposed development
Site grading operations should be observed by a representative of Geolabs
Hawaii It is important that a representative from our office observe whether the
exposed soil conditions during excavation are similar to those encountered in our
exploration The following recommendations are intended to provide guidelines for the
design of site grading work
After demolition of the existing station building pump islands existing
pavements and other structures all debris generated from the demolition should be
removed and properly disposed of off site The exposed subgrade should be scarified
to a minimum depth of 8 inches properly moisture conditioned to above the optinum
moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction For
subgrades of improvements subjected to vehicular traffic the compaction requirement
should be increased to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Relative
compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the
maximum dry density of the same soil determined in accordance with ASTM Test

Designation D 1557 91 Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry
weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
Any soft weak or yielding areas encountered during site grading should be over
excavated to expose firm ground and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with
engineered fill compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Where
encountered the clayey silt layer in Boring No 2 should be removed to a maximum
depth of 12 inches below the subgrade level and be replaced with granular soils Prior
to placement of the backfill the over excavated subgrade should be scarified to a depth
of at least 8 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction The material resulting from the
over excavation should be removed and properly disposed of off site
The on site soils generated from the excavation may be reused as a source of
fill and backfill material It should emphasized that the materials generated from the
demolition should not be used as fill and backfill materials The on site soils should be
properly moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content placed in level
lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of
90 percent relative compaction
Permanent cut and fill slopes may be designed with a slope inclination of two
horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V or flatter Fills placed on slopes steeper than 5H 1V
should be keyed and benched
lf imported fill materials are required the imported fill and or backfill material
should consist of well graded granular materials less than 3 inches in maximum

of 15 or more and should have a maximum swell of less than 1 percent when tested

Excavation
In order to install the underground fuel storage tanks we anticipate that
excavations on the order of up to about 15 feet below the existing ground surface will
be required for the project construction In general the method and equipment to be
used for excavation should be determined by the contractor subject to practical limits
and safety considerations In general our field exploration encountered medium dense
to dense sandy soils We anticipate that conventional excavation techniques may be
used for the proposed excavation
Because of the relatively deep excavation temporary shoring of the excavation
may be needed The temporary shoring should conform to the requirements of local
state and federal regulations Appropriate loads due to surcharge equipment traffic
or sloping ground conditions adjacent to the excavations should be added to the design
lateral earth pressures Stockpiling of excavated on site and or imported materials
within a horizontal distance equal to the depth of the excavation from the edge of the
excavation should be avoided

Utility Trench
We envision that new utility lines would be required for the proposed project In
general for support of the utility lines we recommend that a granular bedding
consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation be used
under the pipes The backfill up to about 1 foot above the pipes should consist of free
draining backfills such as No 3B Fine gravel to reduce the potential for damaging the
pipes from compaction of the backfill
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 1 foot above the pipes to
the top of the subgrade or finished grade should consist of select granular material or
the on site soils The backfill should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum
moisture content placed in maximum 8 inch level loose lifts and mechanically
compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for
future ground subsidence Where trenches are below pavement areas the upper 2 feet
of the trench backfill below the pavement subgrade should be compacted to not less
than 95 percent relative compaction
Drainage
The finished grades outside the buildings should be sloped to shed water away
from foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding Also it is advised that gutter
systems be installed around the buildings and that the discharge be diverted away from
the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near the
foundations and slabs should also be avoided Planters next to foundations should be
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3405 00 D Page 16
avoided or have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the
subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil
saturation and subsequent loss of strength and or heaving of the near surface soils
The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled against the footings
immediately after setting of the concrete to reduce waiter infiltration
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and maintained to drain
all surface run off away from the slabs and footing foundations
