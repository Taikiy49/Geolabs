PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to perform field permeability testing at the
proposed underground storm water retention system site to obtain percolation
characteristics of the subsoil This information will be used to assist in the design of the
underground storm water retention system Our work was performed in general
accordance with our fee proposal dated The scope of our work
included the following tasks and work efforts
1 Mobilization and demobilization of a truck mounted drill rig and
two operators to and from the project site
2 Drilling of two borings at the underground storm water retention system site
each extending to a depth of about 10 feet below the existing ground
surface A falling head permeability test was conducted at each boring
location
3 Coordination of the field exploration logging of the borings and performing
field permeability testing by our geologist
4 Analyses of the field data to evaluate the percolation characteristics of the
near surface soils for the underground storm water retention system design
5 Preparation of this letter report summarizing our work on the project and
presenting our findings and recommendations
6 Coordination of our work on the project by our senior engineer
7 Quality assurance of our work and client design team consultation by our
principal engineer
8 Miscellaneous work efforts such as drafting word processing and clerical
support
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions at the underground storm water retention system site
were explored by drilling two borings designated as P 1 and P 2 to depths of about
10 feet below the existing ground surface at the approximate locations shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2
The borings generally encountered medium hard to hard coral formation extending
to the depth explored of approximately 10 feet below the existing ground surface
Groundwater was not observed in the borings drilled at the time of our field exploration In
general the site is at an elevation several tens of feet above the highest basal
groundwater table in the area However groundwater levels can vary significantly

FIELD PERMEABILITY TESTING
Two field permeability tests were conducted at the proposed underground storm
water retention system site to evaluate the infiltration characteristics of the subsurface
materials encountered at the boring locations The permeability tests were performed in
Boring Nos P 1 and P 2 as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
We understand that the planned underground storm water retention system will be
installed at depths of about 8 to 12 feet below the ground surface Therefore the field
permeability tests were performed at about 10 feet below the existing ground surface
Falling head permeability tests were performed to determine the average hydraulic
conductivity of the underlying subsurface materials Based on our field exploration the
subsurface materials encountered generally consist of medium hard to hard coral
formation The permeability test results are presented in Appendix B
The hydraulic conductivity k value is calculated as 1 1 x 10 and 1 3 x 10 feet
per minute at the test locations and depth based on the falling head field permeability test
results In general the calculated k value for the tests indicated that the subsurface soils
encountered at the boring locations could be considered very low in terms of water
transmission

APPENDIX B
Field Permeability Testing

system site was evaluated by performing two falling head tests in Boring Nos P 1 and
P 2 The tests were performed at a depth of about 10 feet below the existing ground
surface The approximate boring locations are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 Casing
was not used for drilling and testing
During the falling head testing the boreholes were carefully filled with water to the
top The time intervals and water drops were then recorded to provide data upon which to
base calculation of the permeability values of the subsoils The test results of the field
permeability tests are presented on Plates B 1 and B 2
