INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the proposed Ko Olina Condominiums project in Ko
Olina Resort in the Leeward district of Ewa on the island of Oahu
Hawaii The general location of the site and its vicinity is shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1 The exploration was performed in general
accordance with our proposal dated
This report summarizes our findings from the field exploration and
our geotechnical engineering recommendations were derived from our
analyses based on the data obtained during the exploration These
recommendations are intended for fqundation design input and are not
intended for final construction cost estimation or for construction
bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed Ko Olina Condominiums project is a luxury resort
residential development The buildings will be terrace leveled with
building heights ranging from 4 to 16 stories The current development
plans call for the construction of 310 units in four 4 structures with
below grade basement parking facilities and recreational domestic
amenities Also planned are water features along the building perimeters
with about 18 inches of water
It is our understanding the basement parking will extend two 2
levels down below the existing ground surface with the lower basement
floor finish grade set at elevation 3 0 feet Based on the plans dated
supplied to us the below grade parking structures will
be larger than the upper building footprints and a major portion of the
water features will surround the buildings and situate on top of the
basement parking

Site grading involving about 12 to 17 feet of excavation below the
existing ground surface is anticipated for the underground basement
parking structures The anticipated excavation area is approximately 950
feet by 240 feet
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface soil conditions at the project site in order to develop a
generalized soil rock data set for the formation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertinent to site grading basement
excavation retaining walls water features and foundation design of the
project
_ The work was done in general accordance with our proposal to West
Beach Estate dated The scope of work is limited to the
following
1 Drilling and sampling twenty three 23 borings to depths of 38 0
to 98 5 feet below the existing ground surface
2 Reconnaissance of the project site and vicinity to make visual
observations of the surficial soil conditions
3 Review of available soils and geologic information pertinent to
the project site and its vicinity
4 Laboratory testing of selected samples obtained during the field
exploration to determine the engineering properties and
classification of the materials encountered
5 Engineering analyses of the field and laboratory data to develop
design recommendations for the site grading basement excavation
retaining wall water features and building foundation and

STTE DESCRIPTION
The proposed project site is located near recently finished Lagoon
No 4 area within Ko Olina Resort in the Leeward district of Ewa Oahu
The major portion of the subject site is generally level with elevations
ranging from 14 to 20 feet
At the western end of the site a depression exists where the grades

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on the exploration results the planned construction of a
two level basement would totally remove the upper relatively more
competent coral algal limestone formation Also it is our understanding
that the finish grade of the lower basement floor has been set at
elevation 3 0 feet
Therefore if a shallow foundation system is adopted for the project
the foundations would have to bear on the underlying generally erratic
variable and below groundwater subsoils varying from dense coral
formation loose to medium dense silty sand to very soft alluvial clayey
silt Potential differential settlements due to the 16 story buildings
would be a major concern
Because of the erratic nature of the ground unanticipated individual
foundation bottom subsoil conditions are also very Likely to occur during
construction of individual spread footing foundation bottoms Even for a
thick mat foundation considerations should also be given to the
difficulty and cost effectiveness of foundation construction under high
groundwater level since dewatering in our opinion will not be easy in
the erratic and porous coralline deposits

Pile Driving Criteria
_ The piles should be driven with a hammer delivering a rated energy of
at least 39 000 foot pounds The hammer should be equipped with an
energy level control so that driving within the underlying loose sand
deposits may be controlled at a reduced energy to minimize the
tensile stresses development within the piles which could cause
damage cracking to the piles
e
Additional pile driving criteria are as follows
The driving process should be continuous without interruption for
each pile A pile may be rejected or its design capacity
modified when the driving process is interrupted for more than
four 4 hours
During easy driving when the pile tip is within the loose sandy
soil the hammer stroke should be carefully controlled reduced
energy so that excessive tensile stresses are not induced in the
pile
Due to the erratic nature of coralline deposits relatively thin
and hard coral ledges are anticipated at variable depths
underneath the site The thin ledges may not provide adequate
end bearing foundation support but are anticipated to create
relatively hard driving conditions during pile driving

thin hard ledges at shallow depths extend down to about
elevation 20 to 30 feet Relatively hard pile driving
conditions may be anticipated in these zones We believe that a
majority of the piles may be driven through the thin ledge zones
and bear on the underlying competent ledges at greater depths

Predrilling Backfilling
Ten 10 feet of predrilling from elevation 4 0 feet downward is
recommended for each pile This would facilitate pile driving in the
erratic ground conditions and to assure pile plumbness After inmediate
placement of piles in the predrilled holes each predrilled pile hole

predrilled holes are properly backfilled prior to driving per the above
installation procedures

be revised after the test pile driving program

APPENDIX D
Wave Equation Analysis of Pile Driving
ns ee a A SS SS
The wave equation analysis of pile driving was performed using
Program WEAPG which is a microcomputer adaption of the program WEAP
originally written by Goble and Associates for the Federal Highway
Administration to solve pile drivability problems
The program models hammer pile soil system by representing the pile
and driving system by a series of masses and springs The soil is modeled
by a spring and dashpot attached to each mass The program output
provides ultimate resistance and maximum compressive and tensile stresses
in the piles as a function of hammer blowcount
The primary objectives of the analysis are
1 to analyze the driving stresses in the pile
2 to determine the suitable driving system to be used for the pile
driving and
3 to predict the ultimate pile capacity of the pile
The analysis was performed for driving inch octagonal
prestressed concrete piles with a DELMAG D 30 hammer The relationships
of ultimate capacity and hammer blowcounts commonly known as the bearing
graph are presented on Plate D 1 together with input information The
maximum compressive stresses are presented on Plate D 2
