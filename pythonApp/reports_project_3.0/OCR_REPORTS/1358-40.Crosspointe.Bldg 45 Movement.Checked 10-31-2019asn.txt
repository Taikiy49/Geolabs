EVALUATION OF BUILDING 45 MOVEMENT
CROSSPOINTE
In accordance with your request we have performed a study to evaluate
possible causes of the observed movement at Building 45 of the
above referenced development Our study included field observations
review of building and grading plans four 4 drilled borings next to the
building laboratory testing and review of the survey readings
OBSERVED DISTRESS
Initial reports of movements or distress were primarily limited to
settlement of the fences and sidewalks along the eastern end of the
building These have been repaired a few times Movements and cracking
within the units were noticed within about the last 6 months The
interior damage is primarily limited to separation along the walls and
some cracking near the window edges Signs of settlement are also visible
along the exterior roof line The movements appear to be more prevalent
in the bottom two units on the eastern end of the building

Hand probing along the edge of the building disclosed some soft areas
and small sinkholes It was reported that settlement troughs and
sinkholes had occurred in the landscape areas and particularly below the
edge of roof where the rainwater fell to the ground These troughs and
sinkholes have been filled with soil and gravel by the Gentry personnel as
they were noticed
SITE GRADING
Building 45 is a two story 8 plex townhouse structure which has been
constructed over a previously sloping area The fill underlying this
structure varies in thickness from about 5 feet southern end to 20 feet
northern end as shown on the Relative Settlement Summary Plate 3 and
Cross Section A A Plate 4 he fill material which consists of clayey
silt with some gravel overlies stiff alluvium
FIELD EXPLORATION
The field exploration portion of our investigation consisted of
drilling 4 borings around Building 45 The approximate locations of these
borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The detailed information of
the fill soil conditions are presented on the Logs of Borings in Appendix
A Plates A 1 through A 4
In general the borings encountered fill consisting of alternating
layers of brown clayey silt with sand and traces of gravel and black silty
sand with gravel Generally the upper portions of the fill were loose or
soft and appeared to be poorly compacted

SURVEY READINGS
A survey of the movement and settlement of Building 45 was conducted
from March 10th through The survey program was designed
to measure vertical movements lateral movements and possible tilting of
the building The results of the vertical elevation readings are
presented on the Site Plan Plate 2 and are summarized in Appendix A
Plate A 5
The one and a half month survey indicated negligible vertical or
tilting movements at 5 of the 6 reference locations around the building
However at the northern corner of the building V 6 settlement of 0 36
inch was measured
The elevation readings were taken at various locations and heights
around the building The readings were referenced to the building slab to
attempt to determine the magnitude of settlement which has occurred
Assuming that the southwestern corner has not moved the eastern end of
the building may have settled about 3 to 5 inches The approximate
relative settlement of the building is indicated on Plate 2 It is
readily seen that the relative settlement corresponds directly to the
thickness of fill placed
CONCLUSIONS
1 It appears that settlement of the building may have been caused by
compression of poorly compacted fill below the building
2 The ground settlement was accelerated by water infiltration from
landscape water and rainfall The water infiltration tends to compact
the subsoil resulting in ground settlement
3 The survey readings indicate that the building settlements may have
stopped or at least slowed considerably in most of the building except
for the northern corner

RECOMMENDATIONS
1 It is recommended that the surveying program be continued with
measurements taken approximately every month The results of the
survey would help to determine what type of corrective steps need to
be taken in the future
2 It is recommended that the structural integrity of Building 45 be
evaluated by a structural engineer This would be to determine if
future settlement of the structure can be tolerated It will also
help to determine if the present differential settlement is
structurally tolerable
3 Densification of the loose fill below the building is recommended in
order to strengthen the soils and reduce additional settlements
