INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our soils engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Cold Storage Facility and Vehicle Maintenance
Facility on Johnston Island
The general locations of the two project sites are shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this geotechnical engineering exploration was to
examine the soil conditions below the project sites to determine the
suitability of the sites for the proposed construction and to provide
pertinent soil parameters for the foundation pavement and site grading
design of the projects
The scope of work included drilling and sampling five 5 borings
Storage Facility site and five 5 borings B 6 through B 10 to depths of
26 5 to 31 5 feet at the proposed Vehicle Maintenance Facility site
Surface bulk samples from the two sites and the proposed borrow stockpile
were obtained for laboratory testing for pavement design and site
grading Laboratory testing of samples obtained engineering evaluation
of the field and laboratory findings and report preparation were also
included in our scope of work
Detailed description of our field exploration laboratory testing and
boring logs are presented in the appendices The approximate locations of
the borings are shown on the Site Plans Plates 2 and 3

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed facilities are as follows
1 Project F 946 Cold Storage Facility A one story building
about 8 100 square feet with insulated panels and concrete floor
slab Areas included are chilled storage frozen storage
mechanical equipment room office toilet facility and equipment
storage
2 Project F 947 Vehicle Maintenance Facility A one story
warehouse structure about 6 000 square feet with office and
storage space battery shop lubrication area work bays and
paved vehicle parking area Building equipment includes bridge
crane vehicle lifts and air compressors
Minimal site grading is anticipated
SITE CONDITIONS
Cold Storage Facility
The proposed Cold Storage Facility site is located east of the
existing dining hall Mess Hall Building 519 along Arnold Avenue The
majority of the project site is generally flat and covered with
approximately 4 to 8 inches of concrete
Vehicle Maintenance Facility
The proposed Vehicle Maintenance Facility site is located east of the
existing Auto Maintenance Shop Building 40 and Vehicle and Equipment
Repair Building Building 39 The project site is fairly flat and
covered with dense gravelly coral sand fill
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The site was originally an atoll which has been enlarged by dredge
and fill operations over the years

DISCUSSTON AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that the project sites were underlain
by dredged fill consisting of coral sand and gravel In general the
dredged fill is dense near the surface but grades to a loose consistency
with depth particularly at and below groundwater level

At the Cold Storage Facility site thin loose sand layers were
encountered at shallow depths in the field exploration Therefore
overexcavation and recompaction of the loose soils underlying the footings
is recommended in order to provide uniform foundation support under the
footings
The groundwater levels encountered during the field exploration were
14 feet below the existing ground surface Therefore it is our opinion
that the effect of capillary groundwater rise in the sandy soils and
subsequent frost heave susceptibility due to sub zero temperatures of the
cold storage would be low and would not affect the bearing capacity of
the footings However provisions should be made to prevent water which
condenses on the exterior of the cold storage units from draining into the
subsoils
A 6 foot thick crust of dense sand was encountered at the Vehicle
Maintenance Facility site Spread or continuous footings bearing on this
dense upper crust may be used for foundation support without
overexcavation and recompaction of the soils underneath the footings

Cold Storage Facility

about 11 to 16 feet of medium dense sand over cemented coral sand
Thin loose near surface sand layers were encountered in three of the
five borings drilled
Spread footing foundations may be used to support the proposed
building provided that the loose sandy subgrade soils are recompacted
as recommended below

In order to provide uniform support for the structure foundations we
recommend that the sandy soils below the bottom of the footings be
overexcavated for a minimum depth of 18 inches and then backfilled
in 6 inch compacted lifts The sandy subgrade exposed in the bottom
of the overexcavation should also be compacted to a minimum of 95
of ASTM D 1557 maximum dry density before placement of backfill
Backfilling with the granular on site material should proceed as soon
as possible after excavation The backfill should be placed in
maximum 10 inch thick loose lifts and compacted to a minimm of 95
of its maximum dry density established by ASTM D 1557
Spread footing foundations bearing on the compacted on site sandy
material may be designed with an allowable bearing value of 4000
p s f
The allowable bearing value may be increased by one third for
temporary wind and seismic loads
Footings should be embedded a minimm of 18 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grade Footings located next to utility trenches
should extend down to the bottom of the utility trench and bear on
dense sand or be designed to structurally span over the trench
backfill to minimize footing settlement
Based on the borings laboratory test data and estimated footing
loads it is our opinion that settlements generally would be less
than one half of an inch total and one fourth of an inch differential
provided the above mentioned recommendations are followed
For passive earth pressure an equivalent fluid pressure of 300
p s f per foot of depth may be used A friction factor of 0 4 may
be utilized to determine the sliding resistance of the structure and
foundation

Site Preparation
It is our understanding that minimal site grading is anticipated for
both projects
At the on set of earthwork the areas within the project grading
limits should be thoroughly cleared Any unsuitable materials such as
debris and rubbish should be removed and disposed of properly off site
After clearing the areas to receive footing and
slab on grade pavement should be scarified to a depth of at least 6 inches
below the bottom of footing or subgrade elevation moisture conditioned
and recompacted to 95 of its maximum density as established by ASTM
D 1557 78 test method Any loose or wet spots encountered at the footing
bottom or subgrade level should be removed and replaced with compacted
select on site granular soil or with suitable imported material compacted
to the above mentioned standard
The on site sandy topsoil may be reused as backfill material If
required new imported fill material should consist of non expansive
select granular materials less than 3 inches in maximm size with less
than 20 passing the 200 sieve

Excavation
Since groundwater levels encountered at the Vehicle Maintenance
Facility site were approximately 6 feet below the existing ground surface
excavation deeper than 6 feet such as for the proposed hydraulic lift
and or underground waste oil drum may require temporary shoring support
and dewatering It is our understanding that no decision about what types
of lifts and waste oil drum has been made at the present time
Design Review
Plans and specifications for site grading foundation and pavement
should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written comments
prior to construction to see that the intent of our recommendations is
included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the grubbing site grading foundation
excavation backfilling and subgrade preparation be observed by the soils
engineer during construction to see that adequate compaction has been
obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the
findings of our exploration
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils eingineer prior to hauling to the site for use
If conditions encountered during construction are different from
those presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made
