As requested additional probings were made at the above
referenced lots to determine the location and extent of
the soft material below them
Field Exploration
Three probings were made at various locations around each
lot to depths of 9 5 to 22 5 feet below the existing ground
surface The probings consisted of drilling through the
upper crust then driving a 2 inch blunt point with a 140
pound hammer falling 30 inches The approximate locations
of the probing are shown on Plate l
The finish grading for the lots was not completed at the time
of our probing The elevations of the probe borings were
estimated from existing property pins in adjoining lots
Budget Realty Ltd
W O 723 20
P 2
Soil Conditions
Based on the borings it appears that soft to medium deposits
are located about 4 to 8 feet below the finish grade in Lot 4
3 to 4 feet below the finish grade in Lot 5 and about 2 feet
below the finish grade in Lot 6
Water was encountered at about 7 feet below finish grade in
Lot 4 2 to 4 feet below finish grade in Lot 5 and about 1 to
3 feet below finish grade in Lot 6
Recommendations
Due to the proximity of the soft to medium layer beneath the
lots particularly Lot 6 cutting of the lot grade should be
avoided If practicable the proposed lot grades should be
raised 1 to 2 feet to increase the depth of the surface crust
above the soft material
A post footing foundation system should be used to support
the wood framed houses Some provision for future jacking
of the post should be provided due to possible long term
settlement of the underlying soft material
The footings should be placed on the firm surface layer with
6 inch minimum embedment below the finish grade
Footings should be placed a minimum of 5 feet away from the
top of the slope banks
GEOLABS HAWAII

An allowable bearing value of 1500 p s f for Lots 4 and 5
and 1000 p s f for Lot 6 is recommended
Retaining walls should be avoided for these lots
The final house foundation plans should be reviewed and
the footing excavations should be observed in the field by
our office
Any future new improvements such as retaining wall slab
on grade and others made subsequent to the completion of
the proposed house construction should be evaluated by a
soil engineer The prospective buyers of these lots should
be made aware of the above construction restraint and limi
tations
