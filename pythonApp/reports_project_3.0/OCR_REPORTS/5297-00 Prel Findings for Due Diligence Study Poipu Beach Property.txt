Dear Mr Ritchie
This letter provides a summary of our preliminary findings derived from our due
diligence study of the aforementioned property located in the Koloa area on the Island of
Kauai Hawaii The property consists of about 25 acres of undeveloped land bounded by
Poipu Road toward the north and Kapili Road toward the east of the project site The
purpose of the diligence study is to evaluate the feasibility of the proposed development
from a geotechnical engineering standpoint Our work was performed in general
accordance with our fee proposal dated
Based on the information provided we understand that it is proposed to construct a
number of four story villas and two story cottages at the project site in addition to a pool
house and fitness center We anticipate that the proposed development will include
underground utilities roadways parking areas and landscaping improvements We
anticipate that site grading may consist of cut and fills on the order of less than about
20 feet
Based on our site reconnaissance the project site consists of gently rolling ground
surfaces composed mainly of basalt rock formation lava flows and some generally thin
near surface clayey and silty soils Ground surface elevations at the project site range
between about 20 feet Mean Sea Level MSL to about 50 feet MSL Based on our
observations the ground surfaces contain loose boulder deposits and exposed basalt rock
formation with some areas of surface soil exposure at the southern and western portions
of the site The visible basalt rock exposures are considered to be very hard therefore
hard rock excavation is anticipated at much of the site In addition a lava tube of about
5 feet in diameter was encountered at the central portion of the site The occurrence of
