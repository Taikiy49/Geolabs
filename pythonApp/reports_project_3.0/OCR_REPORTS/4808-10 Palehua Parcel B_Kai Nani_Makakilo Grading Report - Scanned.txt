This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations performed
during the mass grading pad grading utility installation and road construction operations
from March 23 2003 to for the above project in accordance with our fee
proposal dated August 22 2001
Project Considerations
The project site is located east of Makakilo Drive south of Panana Street and to the
northeast of the Makakilo Fire Station in the District of Kapolei on the Island of Oahu
Hawaii as shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The project involves site
grading for 96 condominium units The site development also includes the construction
of a private roadway system within the residential zoned portion of the project site and
installation of sewer water supply and lighting facilities The approximate project limits
are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general the majority of the site is fill Site grading involved cuts up to
approximately 16 feet and fills up to about 24 feet high relative to the original existing
ground surface to achieve the design grades for the development The tallest constructed
slope has a slope inclination of two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V and is about 40 feet
in height with one 8 foot wide bench located at about 20 foot height
Site Grading
The earthwork operations for the project were performed by Royal Contracting
Company At the on set of earthwork areas within the contract grading limits were
generally cleared and grubbed of vegetation and deleterious materials Materials
generated from the clearing operations were disposed of off site Following the clearing

In general on site excess soil materials generated from the excavation were
primarily used in the deeper fill area The on site fill materials generally consisted of silty
and clayey soils with some gravel The expansion of the on site fill material ranged from
low to high Samples of the fill and backfill materials utilized during construction were
obtained and transported to our office for laboratory testing The maximum dry densities
and optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general accordance
with American Society of Testing and Material ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified
Proctor The test results are summarized on Plates 3 of this report
The general fill materials consisting of 6 inch minus well graded soils were placed
for the fills up to about 3 feet below the finished pad grade and utility trench backfills In
general 3 feet of capping fill consisting of 3 inch minus well graded granular material was
placed below the building finished pad grade in both cut and fill areas In the cut areas
where hard basalt rock formation was encountered near the finished grade 2 feet of the
rock was removed and replaced by the capping fill Field density tests were performed
with the nuclear density gauge ASTM D 2922 and D 3017 During the fill and backfill
operations the materials were generally placed in near level lifts brought to near the
optimum moisture and compacted to obtain the specified degree of compaction based on
visual observations and or field density tests Periodic field density tests were performed
during the fill and backfill operations to confirm our field observations as variations in
construction procedures and soil conditions were noted
On site soil material from the excavation was primarily used in the deep fill areas
In general the fills were compacted to at least 90 percent relative The subgrade soil
under the pavement area was compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
The subdrain system consisting of 4 inch PVC pipe was placed in the perimeter of
the grading limit to intercept the potential seepage water and to discharge from the outlet
located near the toe of the slope
A keyway about 25 feet wide and 5 feet deep was constructed at the toe of the fill
slope Boulder fill was used in the deep fill areas In general boulder less than 18 inches
in diameter generated during site grading was used below 6 feet of the finished grade
The boulders were spread out with finer material placed and compacted to fill the spaces
between boulders

Additional Recommendations for Homeowner
Homeowners should immediately verify whether their swales have been
compromised or altered from the original condition If these swales have been altered
consult your plot plan and reinstall the swales Swales should also be grassed as leaving
them bare will result in soil erosion during heavy rains
If the swales cannot be completely reinstalled because of constructed wall
obstructions installation of drain pipe along the rear and sides of the property can help
drain excess water away from the sidewalks and building slabs
If a homeowner constructs improvements or expansions beyond the original floor
area for example expanding the lanai adding another 1 floor bedroom or family room
etc the services of a qualified geotechnical engineer should be retained to evaluate the
soil conditions at their respective lot for site specific building recommendations
Limitations
This office assumes no responsibility for any alterations made to slopes or finished
grades on the project subsequent to our observation and testing period without our
knowledge and written acknowledgment Lot owners should be advised not to block the
surface drainage and to obtain professional consultation in the planning design and
construction of any future improvements Lot owners should also be advised not to water
excessively adjacent to the house structure
