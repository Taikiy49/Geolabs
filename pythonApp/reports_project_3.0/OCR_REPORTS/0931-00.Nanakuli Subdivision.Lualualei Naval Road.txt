INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this investigation was to evaluate the sub
surface conditions at the site to provide soil data for the
design and construction of the site grading and foundation
for the proposed single family residential development
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed development site is located along the east side
of Lualualei Naval Road mauka of the intersection with
Farrington Highway in Nanakuli Valley Oahu Hawaii
The site is bordered by Lualualei Naval Road on the west a
concrete drainage channel with chain link fence on the south
and undeveloped land on the north and east
The site generally slopes down in a southwesterly direction
at about a 1 to 6 per cent gradient and existing ground ele
vations range from approximately 8 to 38 feet
At the time of the field exploration several wood frame single
family residences and abandoned vehicles occupy the central
portion of the property Scattered throughout the site are
small piles of loose dirt metal concrete rubbles and miscel
laneous debris Several existing unpaved coral roads which
connected to Lualualei Road provide access to the site

SUBSURFACE CONDITION
Geology
The project site is located on the edge of a coastal plain
which consists of a thick horizontal series of interbedded
marine and non marine sediments capping the Waianae Basalts
The near subsurface structure at the site consists of a broad
elevated ancient coral reef of highly variable structure and
composition
Coral reef deposits as they occur in Hawaii are predominately
calcareous algae skeletons which support a relatively small
percentage of coral growth As a reef grows the void spaces
in the growing coral become filled with sand and other calca
reous debris This type of reef formation is varied and complex
consisting of zones of biologically deposited coral and algae

Pp 4
dense recrystallized coralline limestone variably cemented
breccia dune and beach sand and marls derived from the
breakup and decay of the reef deposits
The present reef platform is apparently a wave cut terrace
formed by wave erosion of an ancient reef which originally
may have stood over 95 feet above sea level As a result of
a complex series of Pleistocene sea level changes the coral
line deposits have become partially cemented and recrystal
lized at various depths by the action of percolating fresh
water In addition sinkholes and solution cavities are com
monly encountered in these reef formations

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration encountered relatively competent subsoils
at the site From a soils engineering point of view the site
is feasible for the proposed subdivision development provided
our recommendations are implemented
Site Grading
A preliminary grading plan for the site was not available
during our study
From the topography of the site it appears that in general
the northern portion of the site may be cut and the lower
southern portion filled The field exploration disclosed com
petent coral formation below the site which would be suitable
for this general grading concept The on site coral material
generally less than 8 inches in size can be used for the fill

Prior to filling the site should be cleared of vegetation
and debris and old foundation then scarified and recompacted
to 90 of its maximum dry density established by ASTM D 1557
Fills should be placed in 10 inch horizontal lifts com
pacted to the above specified density
Fills placed on slopes having grades steeper than 5 hori
zontal to 1 vertical should be keyed and benche
existing firm ground
Based on the field exploration and laboratory tests no
major settlement problems are anticipated for fills up to
about 10 feet
Some amount of relatively hard ripping may be encountered
in the northeastern portion of the site where very dense
cemented coral formation was encountered
Slopes
Slope ratios of 2 horizontal to 1 vertical can be used
for fill slopes utilizing the on site material
Relatively steep cut slopes can be used in the uniformly
dense cemented coral formation However due to the
natural variability of coral formations sandy zones
loss of cementation etc a maximum average design slope

Foundations
The field exploration disclosed that the site is under
lain by a coral formation near or at the existing surface
For the proposed light residential structures spread
footing foundations or slab on grade may be used Slabs

Existing Cesspools
Existing cesspools are located throughout the site When

of the finish grade should be removed to prevent hard spots
and subsequent differential settlement at finished grade
