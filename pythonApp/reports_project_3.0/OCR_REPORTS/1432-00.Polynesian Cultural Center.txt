PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
The proposed construction will be a semi circular amphitheater
structure having tiers of seats rising gradually outward from a central
elevated stage Compacted fill will be used to form the platform on top
o which seats will be constructed Roofing and other supporting
structures will be constructed of steel The general layout of the May
Day Area is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located at the Polynesian Cultural Center in
Laie on the island of Oahu The proposed area of construction is
located near the southwestern corner of the presently developed cultural
center At the time of our field exploration the site was a relatively
level landscaped area A maintenance road consisting of compacted
crushed coral crosses midway through the site and generally follows a
north south alignment
A majority of the ground cover consists of well maintained lawn
areas and shrubs with numerous tall coconut palms along the western
portion of the site
Several underground utility lines which include water irrigation
and telephone were noted traversing the proposed site

Soil Conditions
In general our borings encountered relatively uniform soil
conditions consisting of 3 to 4 feet of brown clayey silt overlying gray
lagoonal sediments of about 18 feet thick Beneath the sediments all
three borings encountered brown clayey silt alluvial deposits to the full
depth of the borings
Groundwater was encountered in our borings and ranged from 2 8 to
3 1 feet below the existing surface
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Our borings reveal that the site is underlain by up to 18 feet of
compressible lagoonal sediments Therefore under the future loading
from the amphitheater up to 6 inches of settlement is anticipated
Ninety 90 percent of this settlement is expected to occur within a
period of 4 to 6 months
It is understood at this stage that the entire project is to be
delayed for at least 12 months However for the benefit of minimizing
ground settlement during future construction it is highly recommended
that site grading and the placing of compacted fill for the construction
of seating area be started 4 to 6 months prior to the actual construction
of the amphitheater itself The magnitude of additional foundation
settlement due to the superstructure is anticipated to be relatively
small under the recommended spread footing foundation

Installation of settlement gauges before placement of fill is

Site Grading
Site Preparation
i ee ee
Prior to placing the compacted fill the site should first be
cleared of vegetation debris and other unsuitable materials Area
on which structures will be buiit should then be scarified to a
depth of at least 6 inches below grade and recompacted to 90 of
its maximum density as established by ASTM D 1557 78 test method
Any areas which are over excavated for example during removal of
existing trees should also be probably backfilled and compacted
Fill Placement
Additional fill material should be placed along a semi circular
strip where the future seating platform will be constructed on
Fills should be placed in maximum of 10 inch horizontal loose lifts
and compacted to about 90 of the specified maximum density The
finished earth structure resembling a semi circular embankment
should have an inner slope ratio of about H V and an outer
slope ratio of about 1 1 The crest of the embankment should be at
least 8 5 feet above the final grade A typical cross section of
the embankment is shown on Plate 3
Fill Material
Excavated on site material free of any expansive component may be
used as a source of fill material All imported fill material
should consist of selected non expansive silty sandy or granular
materials less than 3 inches in size with less than 20 passing the
200 sieve

Swell Factor
ee eee
Based on the average in place density of the soil samples obtained
from our test borings it appears that the compacted swell factor
for the on site material varies from 1 1 percent to a high 3 7
percent
Foundation Support
Due to the light loading imposed by the amphitheater it is our
opinion that the spread footing foundation would not encounter
Significant problem in both settlement and bearing support
An allowable soil bearing value of up to 2000 p s f may be used
for footing bearing on the stiff silt A one third increase may be
allowed for soil bearing under wind or seismic loads
All footings should have a minimum dimension of 18 inches and the
bottom of the footing should be embedded at least 1 5 feet below the
lowest adjacent finished grade
Any soft or weak soils encountered at the bottom of the footing
excavations should be removed to firm soils and backfilled with compacted
fill or lean concrete as appropriate The footing excavations should be
inspected by a soils engineer to verify that they are located in suitable
soil
Provided that the placement of the fill is executed as recommended
above settlement of about one half of an inch for the footing is
ticipated Differential settlement may occur and will be in the order
of one fourth of an inch

Slab On Grade
Slab on grade should be placed over a 4 inch layer of compacted
base course The thickened edge around the slab on grade should extend a
minimum of 12 inches below the adjacent ground surface The slab
subgrade soils should also be kept moist and not be allowed to dry out
prior to concrete placement
Retaining Wall
Construction of retaining wall is included in this project
In general the soil condition appears to be adequate for the
construction of the wall Allowable bearing values of 2000 p s f on the
compacted fill is recommended
Well graded granular material such as base course rock sand or
equivalent should be used as backfill material directly behind the
walls This would reduce the compactive effort required for the backfill
and the build up of additional lateral pressures on the walls
We recommend that the walls be designed to resist lateral earth
pressure of about 35 p c f equivalent fluid pressure Additional loads
due to spectators and other live load should be included where
applicable A friction factor of 0 35 between concrete footing and soil
can be utilized to determine the sliding resistance of the wall
A subsurface drainage system should be used to prevent the building
up of excess hydrostatic pressure behind the walls
Due to the anticipated differential settlement construction joints
along the wall at appropriate distance should be installed

Design Review
Plans and specifications for site grading and foundation should be
forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written comments prior to
construction to see that the intent of our recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the site grading spread footing
excavations subgrade preparations and cushion layer construction for
slabs be observed by the soils engineer during construction to see that
adequate compaction has been obtained and that the subsurface conditions
are consistent with the findings of our investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
If conditions encountered during construction are different from
those presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
Seed ted ke
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling
3 borings to a depth of 21 5 feet below the existing ground surface at
the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 A
truck mounted drill rig was used to drill the borings
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by visual
and textural examination in the field by our engineer who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory The
soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A graphic presentation of the materials
encountered is presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through
A 3
Soil samples were obtained from the boring by driving a
standard penetration sampler or a 2 4 inch I D split barrel sampler
with a 140 pound hammer free falling at a height of 30 inches The
blow counts to drive the sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the
log of borings at the appropriate sample depths
