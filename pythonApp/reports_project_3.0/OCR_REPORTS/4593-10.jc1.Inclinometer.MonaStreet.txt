This report presents the results of our inclinometer monitoring program
performed for the proposed Mona Street Landslide Remediation project The project
site is in the Aina Haina area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project location and
general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 Our work was
performed in general accordance with the scope of services outlined in our revised fee
proposal dated August 31 2007
This report provides the results of the one year inclinometer monitoring program
which officially commenced in June 2008 on the lateral earth movements in conjunction
with the current Mona Street Landslide Remediation project The geotechnical exploration
results were presented in our report entitled Geotechnical Engineering Services Mona
Street Landslide Remediation Hawaii dated

In general the inclinometers recorded lateral earth movements perpendicular to
the adjacent roadway Mona Street and Hind luka Drive A Axis and parallel to the
roadway B Axis as shown on Plates 3 through 7 Based on our review of the
inclinometer data recorded within the 12 month monitoring period it appeared that
Inclinometer Nos I 5 and 1 6 indicated some shallow depth subsurface ground
movement at their respective instrument locations while Inclinometer Nos I 7 and 1 8
indicated no significant ground lateral movement within the monitoring period
Inclinometer No 9 installed adjacent to a leaning electrical utility pole indicated a
limited shallow depth subsurface ground lateral movement
CONCLUSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the interpretation of Inclinometer Nos I 5 and I 6 it appears that the
sliding zone may be relatively shallow approximately 10 to 18 feet below the ground
surface at the instrument locations These results further confirm the results of the
current Mona Street instrumentation monitoring program
However we believe that the ground movement slip circle may not extend to the
existing slope toe as evident by no significant ground movement measured by
Inclinometer Nos I 7 and I 8 This may be partially due to the buttress installed at the
slope toe in previous landslide remediation project in the 1970 s

Inclinometer No I 9 indicated relatively small ground movement at shallow
depths We believe that this small ground movement may be related to the creep from
the surface fill materials and medium stiff clayey soils encountered in the boring The
leaning existing electrical utility pole located adjacent to Inclinometer No I 9 may be
caused by the creeping ground movement
In addition ground movement was also observed in Lot 1150 The ground
movement caused damage to the CMU retaining wall located between Lots 1142 and
1150 as well as settlement of the garage We also observed approximately 1 to
2 inches of separation between the edge of pavement and the curb
Based on the above interpretations and results of the previous instrumentation
monitoring it is our opinion that the current design concept of installing 50 foot drilled
shaft wall along the edge of Mona Street downslope may be necessary to reduce the
ground movement and to protect the underground utilities on Mona Street
To mitigate the leaning electrical utility pole and the ground movement creep
observed in Lots 1142 and 1150 50 foot drilled shaft wall may be also installed along
the edge of Hind luka Drive downslope between Lots 1142 and 1156
