In accordance with your request we have made a study of the
near surface soil conditions at the above referenced lot in
order to provide foundation recommendations for the proposed
residence The study consisted of drilling two shallow
borings laboratory testing of the swelling potential of the
soils and a review of the subdivision site grading plans and
the preliminary house foundation plans This report summarizes
our observations and recommendations
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
The proposed residence will be a one story structure approxi
mately 42 by 70 feet in plan dimension Concrete slab on grade
is planned for the garage while post and beam construction is
planned for most of the house

The house will be of wood construction with CMU wall footings
around the front and a portion of its two sides for grade
Separation Post and beam foundations are planned for the rear
portion of the house
The foundation plan for the structure is shown on the Site
Plan Plate l
SITE CONDITIONS
The subject lot is on the western side of Aloalii Street in the
Royal Summit Subdivision located in Aiea Oahu Hawaii The
front portion of the lot has two level pad areas separated by a
ratio to Nohoalii Street There is a concrete drainage ditch
Situated about 95 feet from the road
A review of the site grading plans for the subdivision indicates
that Lot 122 required fill from 2 to 20 feet deep
SUBSURFACE SOIL CONDITIONS
A review of the subdivision grading reports indicate that Lot
122 was filled with reddish brown clayey silt Laboratory
swell tests on the surface material indicate that these soils
in general have low swelling potential if they are maintained
at or above optimum moisture content

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that the existing near surface
soils are somewhat dry and have moderate swelling potential at
the in situ moisture content Therefore some special
foundation design and subgrade preparation are recommended
House Foundations
Spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the proposed
residence An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f may be
used on the compacted fill soil
Post footings on level areas should be embedded a minimum of 12
inches below the adjacent finished grade Continuous wall
footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the
adjacent finished grade If located on sloping area the
footing should be embedded down an additional amount to obtain
a 7 5 foot minimum horizontal set back distance measured from
the outer edge of the footing out to the slope face This
additional embedment depth is required to help resist the
downhill slope creep and to develop the recommended bearing
capacity of the soil
The bottom of all foundation excavations should be cleaned of
loose material and bear on firm soil The excavations should
be observed by a soils engineer to see that adequate bearing
soil has been obtained
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1232 00 Page 4
The ground surface should be shaped to drain water away from
the foundations
Patio Garage and Driveway Slab On Grade
The laboratory tests indicate that the near surface soils have
low swelling potential if maintained near the optimum moisture
content However if allowed to dry out the soils may exhibit
moderate swelling potential Therefore the slab subgrade must
be kept moist after excavation and protected from drying during
the following days required for preparation prior to final
concrete placement
In order to minimize the swelling potential of the soil we
recommend that all the slab subgrade soils be saturated with
hoses with small holes by constant sprinkling at least 2 days
prior to concrete placement
Hydrated lime should also be placed at the bottom of the slab
perimeter footing excavation prior to water saturating of the
subgrade soil About 1 pound of lime per lineal foot of
excavation should be applied at the bottom of the perimeter
footing Lime should also be sprinkled under the slab
subgrade We believe that about five bags of
hydrated lime should be adequate to cover the slab subgrade
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1232 00 Page 5
It is important that the soils engineer should inspect the
above subgrade preparation and check the moisture contents of
the underlying soils prior to pouring of the slab and perimeter
footing
The bottom of the footing excavation should be neat and free of
loose soils It is anticipated that the very soft silt and
lime mud deposited at the footing after saturation should be
removed just prior to concrete placement
The concreting of footing and slab should be coordinated in
such a way that the time period between the initial excavation
and final concrete placement at each lot should be kept to a
minimum in order to prevent excessive subgrade drying
It is recommended that slab on grade be placed over a 4 inch
layer of base course and should be separated from foundations
The perimeter thickened edge should be embedded a minimum of 12
inches below the lowest adjacent ground
Lot Drainage
The finish lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings
should be shaped to shed water away from the foundations and to
avoid ponding condition In addition gutters should be
installed around the house and gutter water should be diverted
far away from the slabs and footings Excessive landscape
watering near the foundations and slab should also be avoided
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Page 6
These drainage requirements are very essential for the proper
performance of the above referenced foundations since ponded
water could cause excessive subsurface soil saturation and
subsequent loss of strength or heaving The foundation
excavations should be properly backfilled tight against the
slab footing immediately after setting of the concrete to
prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales must be provided as soon _as possible to drain
all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundation
Retaining Walls
Short retaining walls generally less than 5 feet high are
planned for the residence
Well graded granular material such as 3 Fine aggregate should
be used as a granular drainage filter backfill material minimum
12 inches behind the walls Granular material such as select
borrow could be used as backfill behind the walls to minimize
the required compactive effort Otherwise the backfill should
be compacted to a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density
established by ASTM D 1557 78
The recommendations given under House Foundations should be
followed for the design of the wall foundations
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1232 00 Page 7
The walls should be designed to resist lateral earth pressures
as follows
Walls unrestrained at the top 35 p c f equivalent
active condition fluid pressure
Walls restrained at the top 45 p c f equivalent
at rest condition fluid pressure
A friction factor of 0 4 can be utilized to determine the
sliding resistance of the wall
In addition a passive soil resistance of 300 p c f can be
used However the passive resistance should be reduced by
one third when combined with the frictional resistance
A subsurface drainage system should be used to prevent the
build up of hydrostatic pressure behind the walls
Design Review
The plans for foundations site grading and utility con
struction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for
review and written comments prior to construction to see
that the intent of our recommendations is included in the
project plans and specifications
GEOLABS HAWAII
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the footing excavations and backfill
operations be observed by the soils engineer during con
struction to see that adequate bearing has been obtained and
that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the
findings of our subsurface investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive
tested and approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling
to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon
the above inspections
