As requested we have made a study of the near surface soil conditions
below the above referenced lot The purpose of the study was to eva
luate the suitability of the near surface soils as foundation support
for the proposed house The study consisted of excavating five 5
test pits and four 4 hand auger probes within the lot laboratory
soil tests and a review of the grading and compaction test record on
the building site This report summarizes our observations and
recommendations
PROJECT CONSIDERATION
The proposed single family residence consists of a tri level wooden
structure Based on the preliminary plans the house will be set back
about 25 feet from Kulani Street

Concrete slab on grade is planned for the garage and driveway
Retaining walls are planned around the garage Post and beam
construction with wood floors are planned for the house Up to 11
feet of cut is planned at the garage area and up to 4 feet of cut at
the rear of the residence to reshape the existing ground surface
EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
Lot 68 is located on the southwestern end of Kulani Street in the Hale
Kai View Estates It is bounded by Kulani
Street on the east and subdivision lots on the north and south and
the natural valley side slope on the west An existing concrete
drainage ditch is located on the western rear portion of the lot
The lot generally slopes upward from Kulani Street toward the west at
about 19 to 21 gradient The elevation of the lot generally varies
from about 176 to 220 feet
SUBDIVISION LOT GRADING
During the site grading operation cuts up to 7 feet thick were made
over the front portion of the lot for a distance of about 32 to 41
feet from Kulani Street
The remaining western portion of the lot was outside the project
limits of grading and was not graded

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
A review of the grading plan and a site reconnaissance indicate that
the lot is underlain by a thin surface layer of dark gray expansive
adobe clay about 0 to more than 2 feet thick over dense weathered
rock
Detailed description of the soils encountered in the test pits and
hand auger probes are presented on the attached logs Plates A 1 thru
A 3 The approximate locations of the test pits and the probes are
shown on the attached Site Plan Plate 1
Laboratory soil tests on the thin layer of near surface material
indicate that the soil can be classified as clay CH according to the
Unified Soil Classification System Laboratory one inch ring swell
tests indicate that the soil has a high shrink swell potential The
laboratory test results are summarized on Plate B 1 The underlying
weathered rock is considered to be non expansive
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that the lot is underlain by a thin
layer approximately 0 to more than 2 feet thick of grayish brown clay
over rock formation

Foundation
The adobe clay soils are highly expansive and are unsuitable for
foundation support
Individual spread or continuous footing foundation should extend below
the adobe clays down to dense weathered rock
The footings should be poured in place and the excavation backfilled
and compacted tight against the footing with the grayish brown clay to
provide an impervious layer over the footing
An allowable bearing value of 4000 p s f can be used for footings
bearing on the dense weathered rock formation
Footings located next to utility lines should extend to the bottom of
the utility trench to reduce footing settlement due to settlement of
the trench backfill

Slab On Grade
It is our understanding that slab on grade is planned for the garage
and the driveway It appears that the rear portion of the garage area
would require cut up to about 12 feet in order to obtain sufficient
level area for slab on grade construction Therefore competent
weathered rock may be encountered in the rear portion of the garage
excavation The front portion of the garage will have shallower cuts
and may encounter the expansive clay
The on site clay soils are expansive and unsuitable as slab subgrade
soils In order to reduce the expansive effects of the soils the
expansive clays encountered below the slab subgrade level should be
over excavated down to the weathered rock or a maximum depth of 3 feet
below the slab subgrade and then replaced with non expansive borrow
soils such as select borrow crusher waste or coral compacted to a
minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557
The removal and replacement should also extend beyond the slab areas
for a minimum horizontal distance of 3 feet measured from the slab
thickened edge
For slabs bearing on the select non expansive fill a minimum 6 inch
embedment may be used for the slab perimeter thickened edge

compacted to 90 of its maximum dry density In order to protect
against moisture infiltration a polyethylene vapor barrier may be
incorporated below the slab

fill

Concrete Driveway and Sidewalks
A thin layer of expansive soils generally less than 2 feet thick was
encountered in the front portion of the lot driveway area Since
the driveways can tolerate some amount of heaving the clay
over excavation and replacement can be reduced to 2 feet below the
driveway subgrade level or to weathered rock whichever is less Such
a requirement should be followed for a minimum distance of 3 feet
beyond the driveway area
It is our understanding that the driveway will be about 24 feet long
Joints are recommended at about 8 to 10 foot intervals in order to
allow for some relative movement of the concrete
For future sidewalks capping of the clay or over excavation and
replacement similar to the driveway is recommended to reduce heaving
caused by the expansive clay soil

Site Grading
The existing terrain at the site generally slopes down at about 4 1
H V ratio or flatter A cut slope for the backyard development may
be required
Based on the field development we anticipate that cuts up to about 2
to 4 feet would be in adobe clay While deeper cuts would encounter
weathered rock The thin adobe clay layer should be stripped off or
flattened as much as possible to reduce later sloughing or downhill
slope creep Cuts in dense weathered rock could be made at slope
ratios of 1 1 H V
Fill and backfill material should be select non expansive borrow
material If available some of the excavated weathered rock
material smaller than 8 inches could be used in the fill if it is
not contaminated with the adobe clay The adobe clay soil should
not be used for fill material
The area to be filled should be cleared of vegetation and debris prior
to filling
Fill and backfill should be placed in maximum 8 inch lifts and
compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density established
by ASTM D 1557 78

Retaining Walls
CMU retaining walls are planned around the garage portion of the house
to retain the proposed cuts of up to about 11 feet The following
design procedures are recommended
1 To reduce expansive effects of the existing clay soils on the
wall we recommend that the wall footing be embedded into the
weathered basalt rock layer
2 Granular backfill such as select borrow or crusher waste
should be used behind the wall up to 1 foot below the design
finished grade For the remaining backfill of 1 foot thick
the on site clay soil should be used and compacted to produce
an impervious cap
3 A subsurface drainage system such as subdrains or weepholes
should be utilized behind the wall and be designed to drain
freely under gravity to lower open areas away from foundations
4 An allowable bearing value of 4000 p s f is recommended for
foundations bearing on the weathered rock
6 A friction factor of 0 5 can be utilized to determine the
sliding resistance of the wall Additionally a passive soil
resistance of 300 p c f can be utilized

Drainage
The finished lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings should
be shaped to shed water away from foundations and to avoid ponding
condition Also it is advised that gutter systems be installed
around the house and that gutter water be diverted far away from the
foundations and walkways Excessive landscape watering near the
foundations and slab should also be avoided
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance
of the above recommended foundations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of
strength The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled
tight against slab footing immediately after setting of the concrete
to prevent ponding condition

Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained to
drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundation
Design Review
Plans and specifications for foundation site grading and utility
construction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and
written comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during earthwork
construction to monitor the contractor s grading operations
particularly fill placement and slope cutting operations
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be observed by a
soils engineer during construction to see that adequate bearing has
been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with
the findings of our investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the above
inspections
