In accordance with our proposal dated we have performed a
geotechnical engineering exploration to evaluate the expansive characteristics of
underlying clay and effectiveness of the EcSS chemical stabilization system at the project
site for the proposed residential development Sea Country Area 6 project in Maili on the
Island of Oahu Hawaii This letter report summarizes the findings from our field
exploration at the project site and presents our findings for the proposed project These
findings are intended for design input only and are subject to the limitations noted at the
end of this letter report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project involves a residential development within the subdivision formerly
known as Maili Kai in Maili on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project includes about
67 lots that were graded in about 1997
The geotechnical engineering exploration and construction observations were
performed by another firm PSC Recently an exploration with test pits was conducted by
FGE to evaluate the current subsurface conditions at the site FGE concluded that the
existing coral fill capping layer was about 3 feet thick but was insufficient to resist the
swelling pressures from the underlying clay due to the low moisture content of the clay It
was recommended that the site be re graded to provide additional coral fill capping over
the expansive clay
Due to the high costs of the recommended re grading locally available chemical
stabilization using the Electrochemical Soil Stabilizer EcSS 3000 was recommended for
this project to cope with the highly expansive nature of the on site soils to reduce the
potential for swelling distress Therefore an engineering exploration program was
formulated to evaluate the effectiveness of the proposed chemical stabilization system at
the project site The program included initial soil sampling at designated test section
areas injecting chemical stabilizer EcCSS 3000 soil sampling at the test section after
chemical stabilizer injection and conducting laboratory tests on soil samples taken from
the site

EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is located within an area designated as Sea Country Area 6 of a
subdivision formerly known as Maili Kai in Maili on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project
site is located in the vicinity of Oheohe Street and Pakeke Street The location of the
project site is shown on the Site Plan Plates 2 1 and 2 2
Based on the field observation the project site was previously graded and was
vacant at the time of our exploration Based on our observations and the topographic map
provided the site is generally flat and gently slopes downward from north to south
FIELD EXPLORATION
Our field exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling 24 borings
designated as Boring Nos 1 through through 103 and Nos 201 and 202
extending to depths of about 7 5 to 12 feet below the existing ground surface The borings

augers The approximate locations of the borings drilled at the site are shown on the Site

clay In addition Boring No 5A was drilled next to Boring No 5

FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field exploration and laboratory test results we believe that chemical
stabilization may not be suitable for this particular site Detailed discussion of our
geotechnical engineering findings is presented in the following sections of this letter report
Chemical Stabilization
As previously indicated chemical stabilization using Electrochemical Stabilization
System EcSS 3000 was proposed to treat the underlying expansive clay at the project
site The advantages of this system are the relatively low cost and limited earthwork
needed The chemical can be injected through the existing ground surface and does not
require removal and replacement of the soils below the pad The construction time for this
method is also much less than re grading The disadvantage of this method is the
relatively higher risk involved since it is a relatively new procedure with limited experience
in Hawaiian soils Therefore a test section was conducted to evaluate the effectiveness of
chemical stabilization for this project
Soil Stabilization Test Section
Lot Nos 41 and 43 were designated as soil stabilization test section at the project
site Structural System Inc performed Electrochemical Soil Stabilizer EcSS
3000 injection at Lot Nos 41 and 43 on March 11 2005 However the treated
areas were limited to 3 feet by 6 feet for each lot Due to the relatively small sizes
of treated areas a second injection was conducted at Lot No 43 on May 3 2005
The treated area measured about 15 feet by 15 feet
Chemical Injection
The chemical injection involved drilling a 5 8 inch pilot hole through the overlying
coral layer pushing an injection device down to 6 inches into clay and injecting
3 5 gallons of chemical and subsequently pushing down the injection device one
foot each time and injecting approximately 3 5 gallons of chemical into clay The
chemical was injected to the final depth of about 6 feet below the existing lot pad
level In addition the spacing of the injection holes was approximately 3 feet
measured from center to center The treated areas were then marked for future
references
Based on the contractor s recommendations the treated clay was allowed to cure
prior to post treatment sampling
Field Sampling
Field borings were drilled at Lot Nos 41 and 43 to obtain soil samples for laboratory
tests Boring Nos 1 through 9 and No 5A were drilled before clay treatment and
clay samples were obtained to test the swell potential of untreated clay at the
project site Boring Nos 10 through 18 and Nos 101 through 103 were drilled

Swell Test Results
A total of sixty seven swell tests were conducted on clay samples obtained through
the field borings The summary of the swell tests are presented on the Summary of
One Inch Swell Test Results Plate 28 1 through 28 3
As indicated by the swell test result summary without being air dried the swell of
untreated clay ranges from 1 1 to 18 4 percent and the swell of treated clay ranges
from 1 1 to 11 5 percent when subjected to a surcharge pressure of 55 pounds per
square foot psf Based on a comparison of the swell test results of treated and
untreated clay samples the swell potential of the expansive on site clay had not
been improved significantly by chemical stabilization using Electrochemical Soil
Stabilizer EcSS 3000
Test pits dug later by the contractor revealed a fair amount of boulders embedded
within the expansive clay at the test section areas It is believed that the dense
boulders prevented the injected chemical from permeating through the clay and
compromised the effectiveness of chemical stabilization
Based on the performance of Electrochemical Soil Stabilizer EcCSS 3000 at the
test section we believe that the Electrochemical Soil Stabilizer EcSS 3000 may not be
an effective method to improve the swell potential of the on site expansive clay
Alternative methods should be evaluated and considered for this project
