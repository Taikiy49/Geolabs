In accordance with your request we have performed a geotechnical
investigation for the above referenced project The approximate location
of the project site is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 Our
work was performed in general accordance with our proposal of February 28
1986
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct two surge tanks at the existing yard of
Hawaiian Bitumuls Paving Company Each tank weighs about 150 tons and
measures approximately 13 feet in diameter
Minimal site grading is anticipated for the proposed construction

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this investigation was to explore the soil conditions
at the site to obtain pertinent soil data for the design of the tank
foundations
The main objectives of the investigation were
1 to determine the settlement properties of the underlying soft harbor
deposits to evaluate the potential settlement of the structures and
2 to determine if suitable bearing layers are present at deeper depths
for pile foundation design should the anticipated ground and
foundation settlement exceed tolerable limits
The scope of investigation included drilling and sampling two 2
to 53 5 feet below the existing ground surface Also
included were laboratory testing of the samples obtained engineering
evaluation of the field and laboratory data development of design
recommendations and report preparation

SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed site is located on a driveway to the south of the
Baghouse the Dryer and the Plant at the existing yard of Hawaiian
Bitumuls Paving Company on Sand Island Access Road
The area is generally flat and presently paved with asphalt treated
crushed basalt rock

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The proposed site is underlain by a layer of highly compressible
lagoonal harbor deposit varying in thickness from approximately 12 to
14 5 feet This layer consists primarily of loose to very loose coral
gravel in a silty sand to sandy clay matrix The soil below grades toa
thick layer of stiff to hard cemented silt which is also moderately
compressible under high loads
Due to the soft ground conditions and the proximity of the water
table to the ground surface significant settlements are anticipated for
shallow foundation due to the weight of the 150 ton structures together
with their live loads
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1662 00 Page 5
For a mat foundation placed directly on the upper dense fill layer
estimated settlements are on the order of 3 5 to 4 5 inches total and 2 0
to 2 5 inches differential We believe that the differential settlement
would induce excessive distortion to the mat or tilting of the tank
structures which may cause structural damage to the mat
The use of a surcharge fill prior to construction has been
considered not feasible since the required large fill would block the
existing driveway Therefore a pile foundation bearing on the hard
cemented silt is recommended to support the proposed tank structures
End Bearing Pile Foundation
Because of the underlying soft ground conditions we recommend that
pile foundations end bearing on the hard cemented silt layer be used to
support the proposed structures
Piles should be used to support the tank s superstructure and its
structural slabs
The piles would develop their load carrying capacity primarily from
end bearing on the hard old alluvial soil encountered at about 26 foot
depth
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 1662 00 Page 6
Based on preliminary soil data about 30 foot long twelve inch square
prestressed concrete piles are recommended The piles should extend into
the hard soil encountered at about 26 feet below the existing ground
surface
The piles should be driven with a hammer delivering a minimum of
about 24 000 foot pounds of energy and to a resistance of 2 blows per inch
for the last 6 inches
For the above recommendations an allowable pile capacity of 25 tons
per pile may be used Pile settlements on the order of less than one half
of an inch are anticipated
It should be noted that the end bearing piles could lose some of
their load carrying capacity if the adjacent areas are filled thereby
causing ground settlements and downdrag on the piles
The pile driving should be monitored by a geotechnical engineer to
see that they are driven into competent bearing formation
To resist the lateral loads the passive earth pressure around the
pile caps and tie beams may be used We recommend an equivalent fluid
pressure of 300 pounds per square foot of depth The backfill against the
pile caps or tie beams should be properly backfilled with select materials
compacted to a dry density of at least 95 of the maximum density as
determined by ASTM D 1557 78 test method

Predrilling
Predrilling through the upper dense asphalt treated fill layer would
be required before the pile is driven The anticipated predrilling depth
would be about 10 feet below the existing ground surface
Site Grading
Minimal cut and fill less than 2 feet are anticipated for the site
grading of the proposed construction The finished grade is not available
at this time
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed All existing pavements
debris rubbish and other unsuitable materials should be removed and
disposed of properly off site in order to prevent contamination of the
excavated material
The areas to receive structural slab or pavement should be scarified
to a depth of at least 6 inches below the subgrade elevation moisture
conditioned to optimum moisture content and recompacted to obtain a firm
subgrade Loose spots encountered at the subgrade level should be removed
and replaced with compacted on site old fill or imported select material
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1662 00 Page 8
Pile cap backfill should be placed in 8 inch thick compacted
horizontal lifts and compacted to 95 ASTM D 1557 78 maximum density The
broken up on site old fill of asphalt treated crushed basalt rock less
than 6 inch size can be used as backfill material If required new
imported fill material should consist of non expansive select granular
materials less than 3 inches in maximum size with less than 20 passing
the 200 sieve
Design Review
The plans for foundations and site grading should be forwarded to the
soils engineer for review and written comments prior to construction to
see that the intent of our recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
The pile driving operations along with the predrilling of the pile
locations should be monitored by a qualified soils technician under the
direction of a soils engineer to verify that the piles have been driven in
accordance with our recommendations
It is recommended that excavation and backfill operations be observed
by a soils technician during construction to see that adequate compaction
have been obtained
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site

those presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the

INVESTIGATION LIMITATIONS
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations
made in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation engineering
principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all other
warranties either expressed or implied
The analysis and reconmendations submitted in this report are based
on our site reconnaissance soil information from borings and laboratory
test data obtained from this soil investigation
Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encountered and cannot be
fully determined by taking soil samples Unforeseen conditions such as
hard layers or cavities may occur in localized areas and require
additional probing or corrections in the field Therefore additional
expenditures may be needed during construction to attain a properly
constructed project Some contingency fund is thus recommended to
accommodate these possible costs

Laboratory Testing
SN a
Moisture content and unit weight determinations were performed on
selected samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil
properties The results of these tests are presented on the Boring Logs
at the appropriate sample depths
Two Atterberg Limits tests were performed on selected samples of the
soils to evaluate their liquid and plastic limits The test results are
shown on Plate B 1
Two Sieve Analysis tests were performed on selected samples of the
soils to evaluate their grain size distribution The test results are
presented on Plate B 1l
Two consolidation tests were performed on selected undisturbed
samples to evaluate the settlement characteristics of subsoils The test
results are presented on Plates B 2 through B 3
Two unconsolidated undrained triaxial tests were performed on
undisturbed samples to evaluate the shear strength parameters of the
deeper soils The test results are plotted on Plates B 4 through B 5
