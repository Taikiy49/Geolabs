As requested site grading inspections and density tests
were made at the project site from to March
21 1979
As of the site has been constructed to
final grade and all areas under our concern have been
filled and compacted adequately Compaction test results
for the in place fills are enclosed in the Compaction
Test Summary Test Nos 1 to 187 Additional filling
and testing for the roadway subgrades and base course
layer occurred between February 13 to March 21 1979
Compaction test results are also summarized see Test
Nos 188 to 204
Finance Realty March 30 1979
During the fill placement operations periodic field density
tests were made in accordance with the American Society for
Testing Materials ASTM Test D 1556 Sand Cone Method
During the course of fill construction various types of
on site soils were utilized The maximum soil density and
optimum moisture content were established on the soil types
in accordance with ASTM D 1557 test method and the results
are as follows

Periodic swell tests on one inch thick remolded ring samples
of the surface soils near finish grade were performed
Generally the swell tests indicated low expansion potential
when the in place moisture content was near the optimum
The swell test results were submitted with a previous field
compaction progress report for the period between January 22
to 26 1979 dated
AL
Finance Realty March 30 1979
Recommendations
Foundations and slab should be cut back a minimum of
5 feet away from the top of all slopes to reduce the
detrimental effects of slope creep and erosion The
slab on grade and footing foundation should be designed
per our recommendations presented in the project soil
investigation report dated
Site Regrading
Subsequent to completion of lot grading utility trenches
within the lot pad should be properly backfilled and com
pacted under the observation of a soil technician
This office assumes no responsibility for any alterations
made to slopes or pads on the subject lots subsequent to
the issuance of this report without our knowledge and
written approval Future lot improvements by individual
home owners should be done under the guidance of a licensed
engineer to minimize future problems that might arise from
inadequate engineering design
Should you have any questions concerning the contents of this
report please feel free to call this office
