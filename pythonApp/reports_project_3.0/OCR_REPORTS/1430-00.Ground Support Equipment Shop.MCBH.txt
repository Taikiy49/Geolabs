MARINE CORPS AIR STATION
This report presents the results of a soils and foundation
investigation performed for the above referenced project The general
location of the site and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location
Map Plate 1
The purpose of our study was to explore the subsurface conditions
at the site to provide data for use in the design of the slab on grade
foundation and pavements of the proposed ground support equipment
facilities Our work was performed in general accordance with the scope
of services outlined in our proposal of June 21st
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
t is proposed to construct a addition to existing
Building No 1619 and a new U shaped equipment holding shed
with underground fuel tank and fueling area along the southern end of
Building No 1619 A new building for sand blasting and paint booth and
a new dust collector will also be added north of the new holding shed

The new construction will consist of Single story metal framed
structures Spread foundations are planned with on grade slabs Some
amount of site grading is anticipated to prepare the site for the
proposed constructions
The general layout of the site is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located along First Street and northeast of
Helicopter Hangars 1 and 2 within the Kaneohe Marine Corps Air Station on
the island of Oahu Hawaii
At the time of our field exploration the site was relatively level
with two existing structures Building Nos 1619 and 320 and an elevated
on grade wash rack
Ground cover consisted of asphalt concrete pavement around Building
No 1619 and compacted crushed coral fill in the remaining portion of the
site A small piece of abandoned concrete slab was noted near the
on grade wash rack
Several underground utility lines which included water electrical
and sewer were noted along the western corner of the site

Soil Conditions
In general our borings encountered relatively uniform soil
conditions Boring 4 and 5 encountered 2 inches of asphalt
concrete and 4 to 36 inches of dense sandy gravel base course
Underlying the base course layer was a dense coral sand fill layer
varying from 4 to 8 feet in thickness Below that Boring Nos 2
through 5 encountered loose lagoonal sediments Below the lagoonal
sediments Boring No 1 encountered stiff brown alluvial silt
Groundwater was encountered in our borings and ranged from 6 5 to
9 75 feet below the existing ground surface
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
In general our borings reveal that the site consists of a sandy
fill layer which should provide adequate foundation support for the
proposed single story structures Although a 2 to 4 foot thick soft
compressible lagoonal deposit was found below the upper fill crust it
is our opinion that settlement of the foundation should not be a major
problem to this project provided that site filling is kept to a minimum
of no more than 1 foot and the design column load from the metal
structure be kept to a maximum of about 11 5 kips per column
Our major concern however goes to the proposed construction of
the large fuel tank which is believed to have a storage capacity of up
to 10 000 gallons and may have to be installed up to 10 feet below
grade or could be about several feet lower than the underlying high
water level encountered during our field exploration Detailed
discussion and recommendations on the fuel tank installation and design
will be provided under TANK INSTALLATION section

Foundation Design
Most of the site is underlain by dense sandy gravel fill Spread
or continuous footing foundation bearing on the dense in situ sandy
material may be used to support the buildings
An allowable bearing value of up to 2500 p s f may be used for
the dense sand and gravel material The footings should be embedded a
tinimua of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent grade
Minimal settlements are anticipated for most of the proposed
structures except for the area close to the vicinity of Boring No 1
where a 4 foot layer of soft material was encountered below the upper
fill Total settlement up to 1 5 inches is estimated These estimates
were made with the assumption that 6 inches to 1 foot of new fill may be
required to bring the site up to its final grade
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive
earth pressure developed against the buried vertical faces of the
foundation system and by friction resistance developed between the
bottom of the foundations and the bearing soil
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be calculated using
an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 p c f This assumes that the soil
around the foundation is well compacted The frictional resistance may
be calculated by using a coefficient of friction of 0 4 The above
resistance values are maximum values and do not contain a factor of
safety

Pavement Design
About 7500 sq t of new asphalt concrete pavement is planned
within the enclosure of the new holding shed In addition new pavement
is anticipated for area around the new fuel service facility Rigid
pavements are recommended for this area as they are more resistant to
petroleum products
The traffic loading is not well defined at this time However
for design purpose we have selected a medium highway traffic load with
Design Index of 3 according to NAVFAC Design Manual 5 4 This traffic
load consists of 21 to 75 daily repetition of 18 kip equivalent axle
load
Based on our borings the existing upper fill will generally
provide good subgrade support It is recommended that the existing fill
be scarified to at least 6 inches below the finished subgrade and
recompacted to a minimum of 95 relative compaction per the ASTM
D 1557 78 maximum density
The actual pavement design will depend on the type of soil
utilized for the fill Assuming that the subgrade material can provide
a minimum equivalent laboratory CBR value of 30 and swells less than
0 5 the following pavement sections may be used

Site Grading
It is anticipated that 6 inches to 1 foot of new fill may be
needed Demolition and removal of some existing structures and pavement
are also required
Site Preparation
Prior to placing the compacted fill the site should first be
cleared of vegetation debris and other unsuitable materials
Area on which structures will be built should then be scarified to
a depth of at least 6 inches below grade and recompacted to 95 of
its maximum density as established by ASTM D 1557 78 test method
Fill Material
Excavated on site sandy material free of any expansive component
may be used as a source of fill material All imported fill
material should consist of select non expansive silty sandy or
granular materials less than 3 inches in size with less than 20
passing the 200 sieve

Tank Installation
An underground fuel tank is believed to be installed underneath
the fuel service island At the time this report was prepared the tank
was estimated to have a capacity of up to 10 000 gallons and hence it
may have to be buried about 10 feet below ground Groundwater level
which stands at about 8 feet below ground Elevation 2 feet MLLW was
noted during our exploration at the nearby Boring No 3 Therefore
uplift forces on the tank bottom should be considered in the design
The sandy coral gravel layer located at 11 feet below ground as
noted at Boring No 3 should provide adequate bearing for the tank
For design purpose bearing capacity of 2500 p S f can be used
Weli graded granular material such as base course rock sand or
equivalent should be used as backfill material directly around the
tank Lateral earth pressure of 56 and 27 p c f equivalent fluid
pressure can be used for soil above and below water table respectively
We recommend that the tank be set back a minimum distance of 15
feet away from any existing building Proper care should be exercised
by the contractor to avoid potential loss of ground due to piping and
lateral soil movement into the open excavation Temporary shoring and
dewatering system should be adequately provided by the contractor during
construction
The pH environment around the tank was found to be alkaline
Potential perforation of steel is not anticipated However rusting is
expected for the tank and other underground utilities
Design Review
Plans and specifications for site grading and foundations should
be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written comments prior
to construction to see that the intent of our recommendations is
included

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the site grading spread footing
excavations subgrade preparations and installation of tank be observed
by the soils engineer during construction to see that adequate
compaction has been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are
consistent with the findings of our investigation Monitoring of
movement of neighboring structures during excavation is also necessary
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
If conditions encountered during construction are different from
those presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
ee
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling
five 5 borings to depths of 16 5 to 26 5 feet below the existing
ground surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2 A truck mounted drill rig was used to drill the borings
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by visual
and textural examination in the field by our engineer who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory The
soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A graphic presentation of the materials
encountered is presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 5
Soil samples were obtained from the boring by driving a 2 inch
standard split spoon sampler or a 2 4 inch I D split barrel sampler
with a 140 pound hammer free falling 30 inches The blow counts to
drive the saipler the last 12 inches are shown on the boring logs at
the appropriate sample depths
