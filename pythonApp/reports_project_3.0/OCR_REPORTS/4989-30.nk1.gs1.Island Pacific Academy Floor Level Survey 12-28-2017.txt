Brief Project History
The project site is located at the eastern corner of the intersection between
Wakea and Haumea Streets in the Kapolei area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii
Generally the parcel is situated to the south of Campbell Square Bank of Hawaii and
Kapolei Public Library
Geolabs conducted a geotechnical engineering exploration at the site and issued
a report entitled Geotechnical Engineering Exploration Academex School at Kapolei
Kapolei Oahu Hawaii dated Due to the expansive clay soils
encountered in our exploration we recommended special foundation site preparation
including moisture conditioning of the clay soils and capping with a minimum of
18 inches of non expansive select granular fill
The first phase of the school construction involved the two story concrete
classroom building along Haumea Street We were not involved in the construction of
this portion of the project Therefore we cannot confirm the details of the compaction
testing subgrade soil preparation or foundation construction

The second phase of the school construction involved the three story
administration building at the northeastern portion of the site Geolabs Inc observed
the site grading and performed compaction testing for this phase of the work
We understand that sometime after completion signs of foundation slab
movement were noticed in the Phase 1 building The signs of movement consisted of
lifting of portions of the concrete slab cracks in the drywalls and CMU walls sticking of
doors and cracking of some large glass windows on the first floor level adjacent to the
driveway
Geolabs was asked to confirm evaluate the foundation s movements It was
decided to conduct an initial level survey on the first and second level floors to confirm
the magnitude and trends of the movements A hydrostatic altimeter level was used to
measure relative elevation differences from a reference point on the floor
In our letter report entitled Floor Level Survey Island Pacific Academy Phase 1
Kapolei Oahu Hawaii dated we summarized the results of our
initial floor level survey and recommended three alternatives for remedial work
Stabilization of the underlying expansive clay soils using a chemical stabilizer to modify
the clay soils and reduce their swell potential was selected Kelikai Inc was contracted
to perform the chemical stabilization We understand that the chemical injection of the
clay soils beneath Classrooms 109 and 110 was completed in June of 2015 Chemical
injection of the clay soils around the outside perimeter of the classroom building and
beneath Classrooms and 111 was completed in August of 2016 A plan
of the chemically treated areas is presented on Plate 4 Geolabs was not contacted
during the chemical injection work and therefore cannot confirm the extent nor details
of this work
After our site visit on we recommended that further injection of
the chemical stabilizer be delayed so the effectiveness of the chemical treatment on the
underlying clay soils can be evaluated Geolabs recommended conducting
two additional sets of floor level surveys on the first and second floors to evaluate if the
injection remedial measure is effective in reducing the ground movements
In our letter report entitled Additional Floor Level Surveys Island Pacific
Academy Phase 1 Kapolei Oahu Hawaii dated we summarized the
results of the two additional floor level surveys used to evaluate the effectiveness of the
chemical injection remediation Based on the first floor survey readings Geolabs
recommended that additional chemical stabilization work not be done unless future
differential movement of the first floor level is observed and confirmed The second floor
readings generally indicated settlement primarily along Gridlines 1 through 4 and
Gridlines A through E The maximum measured settlement reading on the first floor
level was about 1 inch while the maximum measured settlement of the second floor was
approximately 4 inches The discrepancy may be due to structural and or construction
issues Therefore Geolabs recommended that the project structural engineer inspect
and evaluate the condition of the existing building

Results of the Additional Floor Level Survey and Observations
Particular attention was paid to areas of the ground floor slab that showed
differential movement between the column footings and the slab on grade In general
the column footings were observed to be lower than the slab on grade throughout the
elementary school building Photographs of the visible differential movement between
locations of the photographs are indicated on the First Floor Level Survey Plan
Conducted on Plate 1 7
The floor level survey was performed on the first and second floors using a
hydrostatic altimeter that utilizes a pressurized system that measures changes in elevation
between a determined datum zero location and a point of interest A datum location was
established at the building entrance for the first floor level readings and near the top of
the staircase for the second floor level readings It should be noted that the first floor
datum was established during our initial floor level survey in December of 2012 Since
then the footing that the datum was established on has visibly settled relative to the
slab on grade refer to Photograph No 5 However the first floor datum has been used
for all subsequent floor level surveys for consistency Using these points of reference
the relative change in elevation was measured at select points across both floors

Findings and Recommendations
Based on the results of the level surveys and our observations our findings and
recommendations are provided below
The level surveys from 2012 to 2017 indicate similar movement trends The
interior concrete slabs are lower by 1 to 2 inches along the northwest and southwest
exterior walls of the building The interior portions of the concrete slabs are generally
fairly level
The major building movements appear to be along the western side of the
building along Haumea Street and the school driveway This corresponds to the major
movement observed inside the classroom building
There are signs of smaller movements along other ends of the building
The differential movements noted between many of the building footings and
adjacent concrete slab on grade indicate that either the footings settled or the concrete
slabs heaved or possibly both The building cracking visible on the exterior indicates
that footing settlement may be the major cause of the damage
The level readings indicate that the movements may have stabilized or at least
slowed considerably Therefore major additional movements or damage are not
expected However small movements may continue and cause cosmetic damage
Additional chemical stabilizer injection is not recommended as the interior
concrete slab seems stable
Extensive interior demolition and reconstruction of foundations is not
recommended at this time as the very high expense for interior work does not seem
justified as the building movements seem to have stabilized
Stiffening of the exterior building foundations and protection against water
infiltration is recommended to reduce future movements This may be accomplished by
the installation of exterior grade beams and sidewalks around the building The project
structural engineer has developed a preliminary design for this purpose
Limitations
Our findings and recommendations are based on visual observations and level
survey data and previous subsurface explorations only The actual conditions below the
building may vary
