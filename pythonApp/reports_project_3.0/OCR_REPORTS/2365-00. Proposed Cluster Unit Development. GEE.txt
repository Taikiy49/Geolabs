INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the proposed Kaimana Gardens in the Kaimuki
District of Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location
of the site and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate
1 The exploration was performed in general accordance with our proposal
dated
This report summarizes our findings from the exploration and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analyses based
on the data obtained during the exploration These recommendations are
intended for design input and are not intended for construction estimation
or bidding purposes

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct five 5 two story wood framed residences
at the project site Per the project civil engineer site filling up to
about five 5 feet thick may be anticipated to raise the existing site
grade for drainage purposes
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface conditions at the project site in order to develop a
generalized soil rock data set for the formulation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertinent to site grading pavement and
foundation design of the project
The work was done in general accordance with our proposal to STARTS
International Inc dated The scope of work consisted of the
following
1 Drilling and sampling four 4 borings to depths of 10 feet below
the existing ground surface
2 Reconnaissance of the project site and vicinity to make visual
observations of the surficial soil conditions
3 Review of available soils and geologic information pertinent to
the project site and its vicinity
4 Laboratory testing of selected samples obtained during the field
exploration to determine the engineering properties and
classification of the materials encountered

5 Engineering analyses of the field and laboratory data for the
purpose of developing site grading and foundation design
recommendations and
6 Preparation of this report presenting the findings and design
recommendations

are presented in the appendices of this report
SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed project site is located on 740 Eleventh Street in the
Kaimuki District of Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii The site is
about 22 000 square feet in plan dimension with elevations ranging from
169 feet western portion to 177 feet eastern portion
The site was recently cleared and previous structures had been
demolished for the proposed Kaimana Gardens residential unit development

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration generally encountered relatively competent
subsoils near the existing ground surface It is our opinion that shallow
spread or continuous wall footings may be used to support the proposed
buildings
Site Grading
Minimal site grading is anticipated Per the project civil engineer
site filling up to about five 5 feet high may be anticipated to raise
the existing site grade for drainage purposes
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limits should be cleared of remaining surface vegetation debris rubbish
old pavements demolished footings and other deleterious materials
These materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site
The areas should then be scarified to a depth of six 6 inches
moisture conditioned and recompacted to a minimm of 90 of its maximum
dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 Any loose or soft spots
encountered should be over excavated and then replaced with select fill
materials compacted to a minimum of 90 compaction as recommended above
Based on the field exploration and laboratory test results most of
the on site near surface soils may be used as fill material If required
imported fill material should consist of non expansive select granular
materials generally less than three 3 inches in maximm size with less
than 20 passing the 200 sieve such as quarry waste cinder sand coral
and other non expansive soils All imported fill material should be
tested and approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to transportation
to the site
The fill should be placed in maximum 9 inch thick loose level lifts
and compacted to a minimm of 90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM Test
Method D 1557 78

The site should be graded to drain water away from the foundations
Foundations
Spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the proposed
project An allowable bearing value of 3000 p s f with a minimum footing
embedment of 18 inches may be used for spread line footings bearing on the
very stiff natural soils or compacted fill material
The bearing value is for total dead plus live loads and may be
increased by one third when temporary wind and seismic loads are
considered Estimated settlements are on the order of less than one half
of an inch total and one fourth of an inch differential
If foundations are located next to sewer drainage easements they
should be embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V
imaginary plane extending upward from the bottom edge of the utility
trenches or as deep as the inverts of the utility lines
The bottom of all footing excavations should be recompacted to obtain
a minimum of 95 of its maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
prior to placement of the reinforcement The footing excavation should
first be observed by the project geotechnical engineer to determine if
adequate bearing has been obtained and just prior to concrete placement
Iateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive
earth pressure developed against the buried vertical faces of the
foundation system and by frictional resistance developed between the
bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be calculated using an
equivalent fluid pressure of 300 p c f This assumes that the soil around
the foundation is well compacted A coefficient of friction of 0 35 may
be used Unless covered by pavements or slabs the passive resistance in
the upper 18 inches of soil should be neglected

Slab On Grade
Concrete slab on grade construction is anticipated for the floor of
the structure A 4 inch layer of 3B Fine gravel should be provided below
the slabs as cushion fill The slab subgrade should be kept moist prior
to concrete placement
The thickened edge slab footings may bear on the very stiff natural
soils or compacted fill The thickened edges should be embedded a minimum
of twelve 12 inches below the lowest adjacent finish grade
In order to protect against future moisture infiltration an
impervious moisture barrier is recommended below the slabs One 1 inch
of moist fine sand may be placed above the plastic membrane to help
concrete curing
Pavement Section
It is our understanding that asphaltic concrete pavement may be used
for parking usage The following preliminary pavement section may be
used

soils encountered during construction and the above section may be
revised if necessary The recommended section considers medium traffic
and assumes that adequate drainage will be provided in the paved areas
Utility Trench
New utility lines are anticipated for the project A granular
bedding consisting of a minimm of four 4 inches of ASIM C 33 No 67
gravel 3 Fine is recommended under the pipes

Granular materials should also be used for the lower portion of
trench backfill up to about one foot above the pipes They should be
lightly tamped to reduce the possibility of damaging the pipes
The remaining upper portion of the trench backfill from the level one
foot above the pipes up to the subgrade or finished grade should be placed
in maximum nine 9 inch loose horizontal lifts mechanically compacted to
a minimum of 90 of its maximm dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
to protect against potential future ground subsidence
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comments
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this review
is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for
misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering monitoring services during site filling
and other earthwork activities as well as construction of foundation
This is to observe compliance with the design concepts specifications or
recommendations and to allow design changes in the event that subsurface
conditions differ from that anticipated prior to start of construction
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon such
observations
Any imported material required to construct the required site filling
should be non expansive tested and approved by the geotechnical engineer
prior to hauling to the site

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field borings Variations of
conditions between the field data points may occur and the nature and
extent of these variations may not become evident until construction If
variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations given in this report
The locations of the field borings were approximately determined by
taping from the objects indicated on the topographic survey map dated
provided by Roy K Yamamoto Architect AIA Inc
Elevations of the field borings were interpreted from the same map The
physical locations and elevations of the field data points should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and aS such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross sections are based upon interpolation between field
data points and may not represent actual subsurface conditions

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and
sampling four 4 borings to depths of 10 0 feet below the existing ground
surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 A
truck mounted equipment was used to drill the borings
The material encountered in the borings were classified by visual and
textural examination in the field by our geologist who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory The soils
were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification system A graphic presentation of the materials
encountered are presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 4
Soils sampled were obtained from the borings by driving a standard
penetration sampler or a 2 4 inch I D split barrel sampler with a
140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts to drive the sampler
the last 12 inches are shown on the Boring Logs at the appropriate sample
depths
