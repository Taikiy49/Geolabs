SITE GRADING
The project site is located within the Mauna Kea Lower Bluffs Resort in the District of
South Kohala on the Island of Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are
shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The project includes the construction of one and
two story residential structures a swimming pool driveway areas and garage
A geotechnical investigation report for the subject site entitled Geotechnical
Investigation Hoffee Residence Mauna Kea Lower Bluffs South Kohala dated
August 14 1998 was issued by Harding Lawson Associates The geotechnical investigation
was based on test pits excavated at the project site
The site grading operations were performed by Isemoto Contracting Company Ltd
Queen Construction and Tinguely Development Inc In general the site grading operations
consisted of clearing and grubbing preparation of the subgrade soils and placement and
compaction of on site and imported fills Much of the fills needed at the site to raise the
pre existing grades to the design finished grades consisted of the on site materials
consisting of silty gravel and sand generally 6 inch minus material
During the site grading operations a sample of the on site materials that were utilized
as fill materials was obtained and transported to our laboratory for testing The maximum
dry density and optimum moisture content was established in our laboratory in general
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test results are
summarized on Plate 2 of this report The Modified Proctors of two other samples were
provided by others

After clearing and grubbing of the site the existing and finished subgrades were
moisture conditioned and compacted with a vibratory drum roller or similar heavy equipment
In general fill materials consisting of 6 inch minus structural fills were placed to bring the site
to the design finished grades Due to the nature of the fill materials 6 inch minus fill
materials the fills and backfills were evaluated based on field observations during the fill
placement Where applicable such as the finer structural fill materials and the base course
materials field density tests were performed with the nuclear density gauge ASTM Test
Designations D 2922 and D 3017 During the fill and backfill operations the materials were
generally placed in near level lifts brought to near the optimum moisture and compacted to
obtain the specified degree of compaction based on visual observations and or field density
tests Periodic field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations to
confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures and soil conditions
were noted
A total of 72 field density tests were performed using the nuclear density gauge
method in general accordance with ASTM Test Designations D 2922 and D 3017 The
results of the compaction tests performed during construction of the subject project are
summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 5 of this report These tests were performed on the
prepared subgrade on constructed fills and backfills at the site where possible and on the
base course materials Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and
interpolating from on site survey stakes where available These locations and elevations
should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our professional opinion that the earthwork operations for the Hoffee
Residence Lot B project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period
covered by this report were performed in general conformance with the intent of the plans
and specifications and our recommendations In general areas assessed to be
unsatisfactory were re worked to achieve the specified minimum compaction
LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities occurring
simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork have occurred
without our observation because our observation services on this project were on a part time
basis However the level of earthwork observation and testing services provided appear to
be reasonable in reducing some of the geotechnical risks to the project It should be
recognized that there are variations in the accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests
and methods used to monitor the earthwork construction and materials
Our services were performed in accordance with generally accepted standards of
