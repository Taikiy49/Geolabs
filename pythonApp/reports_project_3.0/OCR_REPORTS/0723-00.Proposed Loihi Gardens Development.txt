Due to the higher construction cost and difficulty in the
removal and replacement method this up date report was
based on the alternate surcharing scheme with some modi
fications
The modified surcharge method incorporates a layer of
Mirafi 140 filter fabric under the drainage blanket
with a subdrainage system By placing a layer of Mirafi
140 filter fabric below the gravel drainage blanket the
drainage blanket thickness can be reduced from 18 to 12
inches The fabric eliminates the strict gradation require
ment of the drainage rock since it will act as a filter
medium to prevent gravel lost in soft ground and fine soil
particles to contaminate the granular blanket material
It also provides tensile reinforcement which will permit
early use of heavy equipment on a minimum thickness of com
pacted aggregate The above specified fabric had been
incorporated in the subdrainage system of the adjoining
parcel Loihi Meadows Subdivision to stabilize similar

Peat Bog Treatment with Surcharging Method
The approximate limit of the proposed surcharge area is
shown on the Surcharge Plan Plate 1
To minimize the settlement effect on the adjacent property
when filling and surcharging the lowlying area of the subject
project the toe of surcharge should have an 8 foot set back
from the property line
The construction details for the subdrain and the drainage
blanket are shown on the enclosed Plate 2 The subdrain
should have a minimum depth of three feet a minimum width
of one foot and contained a 4 inch perforated pipe The
approximate subdrain locations are shown on Plate 1 The
final location should be determined in the field after site
clearance of the lowlying area

The drainage blanket should be constructed with granular
crushed rock material passing the ASTM Designation No 67
or 3B Fine gradation requirement By placing a layer
of Mirafi 140 filter fabric below the drainage blanket
a blanket thickness of 12 inches can be used
With the drainage system described above it is estimated
that if a 6 foot surcharge fill is placed the required
90 consolidation level will occur in about 9 to 12 months
with 10 feet of surcharge fill the required consolidation
level could be attained in about 6 months
It is recommended that a minimum of three 3 settlement
gauges should be used to monitor the rate and amount of
consolidation The settlement gauges should be installed
prior to the fill operation After the desired consolida
tion has taken place the surcharge may then be removed
and construction of the roadway utilities and surface
structure may begin The settlement gauges should be
installed and monitored by the soils engineer Settle
ment gauge reading should be accurately surveyed by the
project surveyor on an intermittent basis
