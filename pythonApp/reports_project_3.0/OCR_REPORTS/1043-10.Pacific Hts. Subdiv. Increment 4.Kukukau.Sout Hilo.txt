We have performed an additional test boring in order to
evaluate the subsurface conditions in the proposed cut area
of the upper cul de sac in the above referenced project
The purpose of this additional boring was to examine the
subsurface conditions for the proposed 10 foot deep cut in
this area and to determine the likelihood of future seepage
occurrences due to this cut
The boring was drilled utilizing a truck mounted rotary
hydraulic drill rig in a location indicated by your firm
W O 1043 10
P 2
SITE DESCRIPTION
The area of the additional test boring is located in the
western corner of the proposed Pacific Heights Subdivision
Increment 4 adjacent to Kukuau Street in Hilo The area
is currently vacant land and it is densely vegetated with
java plum african tulip california grass lilikoi and
other trees and shrubs Access was obtained by clearing
the area with a D6C dozer supplied by your firm
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our boring revealed approximately 32 feet of gray fractured
vesicular basalt of the pahoehoe form which is believed to
be the lava flow of 1881 Below this layer a strata of
clinker approximately 4 feet thick was encountered which
is underlain by 17 feet of gray dense massive basalt
Underlying this layer of massive basalt was another 1 foot
of clinker followed by gray brown fractured vesicular basalt
pahoehoe to the maximum depth drilled of 63 5 feet
Groundwater was not encountered in the drilled boring at the
time of our field exploration
Detailed descriptions of the soils and rock encountered in
the boring is presented in the attached boring log

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our boring data the subsurface conditions are very
Similar to those encountered in our previous investigation
of July 1981 The recent pahoehoe flow appears to form a
cap over the underlying clinker layer which appears to be
the source of seepage water in the lower slope area follow
ing periods of heavy rainfall
Road Cut
Since the proposed cut will be approximately 10 feet at its
deepest part and that there will be approximately another
22 feet of caprock left above the clinker layer it is our
Opinion that the likelihood of seepage occurring as a result
of the 10 foot cut would be small
Disposal of Surface Run off
The lower layer of vesicular basalt beginning at approxi
mately 55 feet below the existing ground surface appears to
be sufficiently permeable for surface water disposal by dry
wells The design of these dry wells should be Similar to
the design described in our report of However
we feel that the use of perforated casing should be avoided
as this may allow exfiltration of water from the dry well
into the seepage bearing clinker layer which could contribute
to the possibility of increased seepage in the down slope area
