3191 ALOHA AINA PLACE
In accordance with your request we have performed a geotechnical
engineering exploration of the above referenced site The purpose of our
study was to examine the surface and near surface conditions at the site
in order to provide soil data for the design of the foundations for the
proposed addition
This report summarizes our preliminary findings and recommendations
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located on the eastern side of Nuuanu Valley at
the end of Aloha Aina Place The proposed addition site was formerly the
swimming pool area of the existing Single family residence on the lot
The pool was backfilled to create a grassed lawn area forming a generally
level site
To the north and northeast of the site the ground surface slopes
down steeply to a drainageway This slope is approximately 25 to 30 feet
in height and slopes at an average of 1 horizontal to 1 vertical with
locally steeper areas

It is currently planned to construct an addition to the existing

FIELD EXPLORATION
The field exploration consisted of drilling and sampling three 3
borings to depths between 11 5 and 16 5 feet below the existing ground
surface in the area of the proposed addition Our field work indicated
that the underlying natural on site soils consist of stiff brown mottled
to red brown mottled clayey silts with traces of decomposing sand and
gravel these soils are derived from the deep chemical weathering of
alluvial and or colluvial deposits The swimming pool backfill generally
consisted of soft to very soft brown clayey silts of unknown origin
Groundwater was not encountered in the drilled borings at the time of
our exploration
The approximate locations of the borings are indicated on the
attached Site Plan The boring logs are attached for your reference The
results of our laboratory testings are also summarized in Appendix B
Plates B 1 and B 2
FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The boring exploration disclosed that the majority of the proposed
addition area is underlain by wet soft to very soft clayey silts backfill

to the concrete bottom of the abandoned pool These soils are moderately
to highly compressible Special foundation design or over excavation of
the soft backfill material may be required to resist or to reduce
potential foundation movements
Based upon the borings and laboratory test data it is our opinion
that the soils used for backfilling the abandoned swimming pool are
unsuitable for foundation support in their current soft wet condition
Special foundation design such as deep footings bearing on the natural
on site soils below the pool bottom or new site regrading may be required
to resist or reduce foundation movements Presented below are
recommendations for site grading and or foundation design
Slab on Grade
If it is desired to use an on grade slab to support the proposed
addition it is our recommendation that the soft wet swimming pool
backfill be removed and replaced or recompacted The abandoned pool
should be cleaned out to its concrete bottom and the resulting excavation
should be backfilled with 3B Fine crushed rock to three 3 feet below
Slab subgrade The crushed rock backfill should be placed in 8 to
10 inch lifts and compacted to 90 of its maximum dry density as
determined by ASTM D 1557 78 Select non expansive borrow material should
be placed over the crushed rock backfill in 8 inch lifts and compacted to
the above recommended density
The existing concrete sidewalks decking surrounding the abandoned
pool should be demolished and disposed of properly off site The existing
ground outside of the abandoned pool should be cleared and grubbed of all
vegetation and other deleterious material and scarified to a depth of 12
inches below the bottom of the slab moisture conditioned to near optimum
moisture content and compacted to 90 of its maximm dry density as
established by ASTM D 1557 78

The existing wet swimming pool backfill material may be utilized as
backfill if it is dried to near optimum moisture content and properly
compacted as described above However the limited amount of space
available may limit the practicality of this re utilization since the
material will need to be spread to dry
An allowable bearing capacity of 2 000 p s f may be utilized for the
design of slab foundations bearing on subgrade prepared in accordance with
the above recommendations The exterior thickened edges of the slab
should be embedded a minimum of 12 inches below the lowest adjacent
grade The toe of the foundations should have a minimum of ten 10 feet
of set back from the face of the adjacent steep slope
A 6 inch layer of 3B Fine gravel should be provided below the slabs
as cushion fill In order to prevent future moisture infiltration and
subsequent damage to the floor coverings an impervious moisture membrane
may be considered below the slabs provided an inch of moist fine sand is
placed above the plastic membrane to help concrete curing
Spread Footings
If it is desired to utilize post and beam foundations to support the
proposed structure we recommend that the footing excavations be extended
down to bear on the natural on site soils below the bottom of the swimming
pool or to a minimum depth of three 3 feet for footings outside the
abandoned pool area The bottoms of the footing excavations should be
compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density as established by
ASTM D 1557 78
For spread foundations bearing on compacted natural on site soils
prepared in accordance with the above recommendation an allowable bearing
capacity of 2 000 p s f may be utilized The toe of the foundations
should have a minimum of ten 10 feet of set back from the face of the
adjacent steep slope

As an alternative to extending footing excavations through the
abandoned swinming pool consideration could be given to using structural
steel framing to span the area of the abandoned pool A structural
engineer should be consulted for the design of such a structure
Design Review
Plans and specifications for foundations site grading and utility
construction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and
written comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during
earthwork construction to monitor the contractor s grading operations
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be observed by
the soils engineer during construction to see that adequate bearing has
been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with
the findings of our investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior its delivery to the site
If conditions encountered during construction are different from
those presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above inspections

LIMITATIONS
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations
made in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation
engineering principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all
other warranties either expressed or implied
The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are
based on our site reconnaissance soil information from borings and
laboratory test data obtained from this soil exploration for the
proposed addition area within the previous swimming pool location
Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encountered and cannot
be fully determined by reconnaissance boring or probing Unforeseen
conditions such as pockets of organic soils trash fill or expansive
soils may occur in localized areas Therefore additional expenditures
may be needed during construction to attain a properly constructed
project Some contingency fund is thus recommended to accommodate
these possible costs
This report has been prepared in order to assist the architect and
engineer in the preparation of site grading and foundation plans of the
proposed project

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content unit weight and Atterberg Limits determinations
were performed on selected samples as an aid in the classification and
evaluation of soil properties The results of these tests are presented
on the Boring Iogs at the appropriate sample depths on Plates A 1
through A 3
Four one inch ring swell tests were performed on remolded and
undisturbed samples to evaluate the swelling potential of the soils in
both natural and compacted conditions The test results are summarized
on Plate B 1
Direct Shear tests were performed on selected samples to evaluate
the strength characteristics of the subsoils These test results are
summarized on Plate B 2
Consolidation tests were performed on selected samples to evaluate
the compressibility of the subsoils These tests are evaluated on
Plates B 3 and B 4
