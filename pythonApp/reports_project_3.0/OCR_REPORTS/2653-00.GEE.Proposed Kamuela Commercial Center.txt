INTRODUCTION
In accordance with your request we have performed a preliminary geotechnical
engineering exploration including percolation testing at the above referenced site This
report presents our findings and recommendations
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the general
subsurface soils and rock conditions at the project site in order to obtain data for the
formulation of preliminary geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to the
design of the project site grading proposed building foundations pavements and
infrastructure including the on site disposal of sanitary wastes
The scope of our preliminary exploration consisted of the following tasks and work
efforts
1 Mobilization of a truck mounted drill rig with operators to and from the
project site
2 Drilling and sampling of six 6 borings in the proposed commercial center
site to depths ranging between 16 5 and 28 0 feet below the existing ground
surface
3 Performance of percolation tests in two 2 drilled borings to evaluate soil
absorption characteristics for the formulation of recommendations pertinent
to the design of the disposal components of the on site wastewater
systems
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2653 00 Page 3
4 Coordination of the field work percolation testing and logging of the borings
by a geologist from our firm
5 Laboratory testing of selected samples obtained during the field exploration
to determine the engineering properties of the materials encountered in our
exploration
6 Engineering analyses of the field and laboratory data for the formulation of
geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to the design of the
proposed commercial center development
7 Preparation of this report presenting our findings and recommendations
8 Coordination of our overall work on the project and client design team
consultation by a Principal Engineer of our firm
g Miscellaneous work efforts such as drafting word processing and clerical
support
GENERAL SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located in the town of Kamuela Waimea South Kohala on the
island of Hawaii The project site encompasses an area of approximately 91 800 square
feet The site is bounded by Mamalahoa Highway to the north by residential properties
to the east and south and by commercial development and vacant land to the west
The topography of the site is generally level except for a intermittent stream which
crosses the southern portion of the site from east to west
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2653 00 Page 4
The site is currently occupied by an existing residential structure and two 2
related outbuildings in the northwestern portion of the site Most of the site s ground
surface is covered with a lush growth Waimea grass and is used as pasture for horses
Discussions with local residents indicate that the site may have been used as part
of an army encampment during World War Il In view of this previous use there is a
possibility that there could be abandoned cesspools and buried rubbish on the site
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
Based on the current plans provided to us it is our understanding that it is
proposed to develop the site as a commercial center with office and retail spaces in one
and two story wood and concrete masonry buildings It is anticipated that asphaltic
concrete pavements will be used for the driveways and parking areas of the project
The project is located in an unsewered area and there is no known regional
facilities plan scheduled to be implemented in the foreseeable future Therefore it will be
necessary to use on site treatment and disposal for sanitary wastes generated within the
development area It is our understanding that it is currently planned to use filtration
trenches as the disposal components of the on site wastewater systems
It is our understanding that the County of Hawaii ordinances may require on site
disposal of run off water generated at the development site Therefore it appears also
necessary to design and construct a disposal system to handle the intermittent run off
flows

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Based on our exploration the subsurface conditions throughout the site appear
relatively uniform
The surface soils to a depth of about five 5 to ten 10 feet below the existing
ground surface consist of yellowish brown to reddish brown medium stiff to stiff silt soils
Pahala Ash These soils are predominately low plasticity silts which contain occasional
gravel and cobbles The silts have very little cohesion and once exposed is friable and
subject to wind and water erosion
GEOLABS HAWAII

Based on our exploration the ash soils generally grade into a layer of weathered
volcanic clinker which in turn grades to relatively unweathered clinker This clinker
material consists predominantly of cobble and gravel sized basalt fragments intermixed
with some ash and represents the original surface of A a flows in most of the borings
this clinker stratum extended to at least the maximum depths explored
Groundwater was not encountered in the drilled borings at the time of our
exploration

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
From a geotechnical engineering point of view our exploration indicates the site
is suitable for the proposed development from a geotechnical engineering point of view
The in situ ash soils encountered at the project site are generally in a moderately
dense state After additional compaction they should provide adequate bearing to

support spread footing type of foundation system for the proposed commercial center
development
Our percolation testing indicates moderate percolation rates within the ashy soils
It is our Opinion that on site disposal of primary treated wastewater is feasible depending
upon the amount of wastewater generated and the amount of space available for the
construction of the seepage trenches
Wastewater Disposal
The project is located in an unsewered area and there is no known regional
facilities plan scheduled to be implemented in the foreseeable future Therefore it will be
necessary to use on site treatment and disposal for sanitary wastes generated within the
development area It is our understanding that it is currently planned to use filtration
trenches with seepage pits as the disposal component of the on site wastewater systems
Ideally the disposal component should provide a connection to the underlying clinker
layer
Based on our field percolation tests and the methods given in Public Health Service
Publication No 526 the wastewater disposal system should be designed for the
application of no more than 1 6 gallons per day of primary tested wastewater per square
foot of trench bottom Therefore approximately 500 square feet of trench bottom would
be required for the disposal of 800 gailons of wastewater per day
It is Our understanding that the County of Hawaii ordinances require the on site
disposal of run off water generated at a new development site The usual County
standard is to design for the disposal of six 6 to ten 10 cubic feet per second per
drywell or other type of disposal system
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2653 00 Page 8
It is our opinion that a filtration trench system within the upper silty ash soils to
handle flows of that magnitude would be prohibitive in terms of land area required It
appears more that a feasible approach would be to install deep drywells into the
underlying clinker basalt zone to handle large storm water flows
Our current percolation testing and the shallow depth borings drilled for the
geotechnical engineering exploration did not provide sufficient field percolation data for
the design of such drywells or the required data for the preparation of the required
Department of Health permits for the drywells We recommend that additional deeper
boring exploration and larger flow injection testing be performed in the actual excavated
drywells to obtain these site specific data
Site Grading
In general it is our opinion that conventional grading methods may be used for the
site and roadway grading of the proposed development The proposed developments
are stili in the conceptuai stages and detaiied grading pians were not availabie at the time
of our expioration However fills on the order of about ten 10 feet in thickness are
anticipated for the portions of the site adjacent to the intermittent stream The following
grading recommendations are presented as guidelines for the design of the site grading
for the proposed projects
Site Preparation
Prior to commencement of grading surface vegetation and organic matter within
the contract grading limits should be cleared and grubbed The resulting spoils
should be disposed of properly off site or stockpiled for use in landscaping
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2653 00 Page 9
Generally the Pahala Ash soils generally have relatively low strength and if the
in situ moisture contents are high enough they are thixotropic i e they lose
strength when remolded The soils may need to be moisture conditioned for
workability in the project site grading work Laboratory compaction tests on similar
soils indicate that the optimum moisture content is probably in the range of about
55 per cent However the moisture conditioning should be carefully controlled to
avoid over wetting of the soils
It is also advisable to consider and to institute a dust control program during
grading operations
Soft or yielding areas encountered during clearing and grubbing should be
over excavated to firm natural material and the resulting depression backfilled with
select on site or imported fill compacted to a minimum of 90 maximum dry
density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Any existing miscellaneous rubbish fill encountered should be removed and
wasted off site
Areas designated to receive fill should be scarified to a depth of twelve 12 inches
moisture conditioned to near optimum moisture content and compacted to a
minimum of 90 maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Fill Placement
in general the on site ash soils with any rock particles less than six 6 inches in
size if properly moisture conditioned may be suitable as fill material
GEOLABS HAWAIi

Generally fills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 12 inches in loose
thickness and compacted to 90 maximum dry density as established by ASTM
D 1557 78 Fill material should be moisture conditioned to allow the silty soils to
be compacted into a dense stable mass without causing pumping or liquefaction
Any fills to be placed on slopes having ratios steeper than 5 1 horizontal to
vertical should be keyed and benched into the existing ground formation to lessen
potential instability of the new fill see Plate 3 The keys and benches should be
based in firm native soil
Provided the on site silty ash soils are utilized for embankment and other fills we
recommend that flatter than normal fill slopes of three horizontal to one vertical
3H 1V be used in grading design These silty soils are easily eroded and should
be protected or planted as soon as practicable

cesspool found on the site should be opened cleaned of loose soft materials and
backfilled with granular material such as 3 Fine up to two 2 feet below finished
subgrade Above this level the cesspool excavation should be filled with on site
soils and compacted as specified above

Foundation Support
For the design of spread or strip footings we recommend that an allowable
when temporary wind and seismic load are considered The bottom of the footings
should be embedded a minimum of eighteen 18 inches below the outside or adjacent
grade
In order to provide uniform support for the foundations we recommend that the
footing subgrade be over excavated to an extra depth of another eight 8 inches below
the bottom of footing grade and this material replaced with select granular material such
as base course compacted to a minimum of 90 maximum dry density per ASTM Test
outside the footing edges
if any soft or weak areas are encountered in the over excavation they should be
remedied by further over excavation of the soft and or weak materials to firm natural soils
and backfilling the resultant void with select on site soil placed in nine 9 to ten 10 inch
loose lifts and compacted to 90 maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method
D 1557 78
For foundations designed in accordance with the above recommendations we
estimate total and differential settlements to be on the order of one quarter 1 4 of an
inch
Slabs On Grade
it is anticipated that on grade concrete slabs wiil be used for the ground floors of
the proposed buildings We recommend a minimum 4 inch thick slab placed over a
6 inch cushion of 3B Fine gravel to provide uniform support A moisture barrier

protect floor coverings
The thickened edges of the slabs should extend a minimum of twelve 12 inches
below the lowest adjacent outside finished grade The slab and thickened edge should
bear on subgrades which have been compacted to a minimum of 90 maximum dry
density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 to a minimum depth of twelve 12 inches
below the base course cushion fill subgrade level Backfill against the slab edges should
be adequately compacted and graded to shed water away from the slab perimeter
Excavation
The field exploration indicates that the site is underlain by medium stiff to stiff silty
ash and gravelly clinker soils These soils exist in a relatively friable state
it is anticipated that the ash and clinker soils may be conventionally excavated
Some hard excavation may be encountered in excavations deeper than about fifteen 15
feet below the existing ground surface
Since the subsoils encountered at the site are in a friable state it is anticipated that
there will be a tendency for the sidewalls of excavations to unravel or slough when
exposed Therefore shoring or bracing should be considered to comply with the
applicable health and safety regulations The design and implementation of the shoring
and or bracing should be left to the contractor
It is anticipated that conventional construction methods may be utilized for the
installation of the proposed infrastructure
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2653 00 Page 13
The trenches should be excavated and backfilled in accordance with Section 11
Standard Specifications for Public Works Construction 1986 Trench Excavation and
Backfill
Pipes should be bedded and cushioned in a granular material such as 3B Fine
gravel ASTM C 38 No 67 gradation Native ashy and silty soils should not be used for
backfill around the pipes The backfill from cushion level to one 1 foot above the top
of the pipe barrel should be granular material such as 3B Fine gravel or cinder placed
in lifts and compacted to a minimum of 90 maximum dry density as established by
ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Proper backfill placement and compaction is critical for soil supported pipes such
as corrugated or spiral metal pipes for drainlines Improper backfilling could result in
structural distress and deflection of the arch of the pipe causing settlements of the soil
above the pipe
Native soil may be used for backfill from the level of one 1 foot above the top of
the pipe to road subgrade level Backfill using native material should be moisture
conditioned to slightly optimum moisture content placed in eight 8 inch thick loose lifts
and then compacted to a minimum of 90 maximum dry density as established by ASTM
Test Method D 1557 78 The on site soils are fine grained silty ash soils and the practice
of water tamping trench backfills should be avoided

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during construction of the excavation and foundation
phases of the work This is to observe compliance with the design concepts
specifications or recommendations and to allow design changes in the event that
subsurface conditions differ from that anticipated prior to start of construction The
recommendations given in this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and approved by
the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during construction are different
from those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made

report

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Laboratory testing was performed on selected samples obtained during our field
exploration A laboratory testing program was developed to schedule appropriate tests
to aid in determining pertinent engineering properties of the soil and or rock materials
sampled
Classification Tests
Frequently the classification of a material provides a correlative index to certain
engineering properties of the material
Moisture Content ASTM Test Method D 2216 80 and Unit Weight Determinations
are used as an aid to soil classification and to aid in determining certain properties such
as relative saturation The moisture content expressed as a percentage of the dry weight
of the tested sample is shown at the appropriate location on plates summarizing test
results and at the appropriate sample depths on the Boring Logs
Atterberg Limits tests ASTM Test Method D 4318 84 are used to determine the
plasticity of the fine grained portion of soils The Plasticity Index of the material is used
to classify the soil under the Unified Soil Classification System The results of these tests
are shown at the appropriate sample depths on the Boring Logs
Physical Property Tests
Tests are performed to develop a correlative index of certain physical parameters
of a soil These physical parameters may be used in engineering analyses pertaining to
the performance or suitability of a soil
Expansion tests are performed to evaluate the swelling potential of soils under light
structural loads These tests aid in evaluating the materials for use in embankment
construction and to provide on grade slab support These test results are summarized
on Plate B 1
