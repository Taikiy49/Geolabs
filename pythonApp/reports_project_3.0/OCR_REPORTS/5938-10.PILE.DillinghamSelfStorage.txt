PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is at 935 Dillingham Boulevard in the Honolulu area on the Island of
Oahu Hawaii A multi story storage building is planned on the site The building will have
a plan area of approximately 278 feet long by 112 feet wide Parking areas will be along
the perimeter of the building In addition new underground utility lines will be installed
Based on the findings and recommendations presented in our geotechnical
engineering report entitled Geotechnical Engineering Exploration Dillingham Self
Storage 935 Dillingham Boulevard Honolulu Oahu Hawaii dated
prepared for the above development the new building will be supported on a deep
foundation system consisting of precast prestressed concrete piles
PILE INSTALLATION PROGRAM
The pile driving program was conducted between January 14 and March 14 2008
under the near continuous observations by our field representative Healy Tibbitts
Builders Inc used a hydraulic hammer for the pile driving operations
The hydraulic hammer has a maximum rated energy of approximately

The piles generally consisted of 16 5 inch octagonal precast prestressed concrete
piles 70 75 and 80 feet in length The pile locations were generally predrilled down to
about 10 feet below the ground surface prior to pile driving The pile driving summary is
presented by both pile number sequence Plates 3 1 through 3 10 and date sequence
Plates 4 1 through 4 10
A total of 366 piles including test piles were driven for the proposed building The
piles were driven to tip elevations ranging between about 59 5 and 74 5 feet Mean Sea
Level MSL
Based on our observation of the hammer performance and pile driving behavior it
is our professional opinion that the concrete piles have been driven to sufficient depths
and or resistance to provide the design load capacity of 62 5 tons per pile with a factor of
safety of 2
