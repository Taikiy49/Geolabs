INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the proposed improvements to the existing
structure on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location of the
site and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The
exploration was performed in general accordance with our proposal dated
This report summarizes our findings from the exploration and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analyses based
on the data obtained during the exploration These recommendations are
intended for design input and are not intended for construction estimation
or bidding purposes

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct new improvements and additions to the
existing structure It is our understanding that the original plan
consisted of improvements on the second floor which require new colum
footings to be located at the ground floor office western end and family
room eastern end areas In addition to the structural improvements
underground air duct system was planned along the eastern lot boundary
Subsequently the original plan was revised and it was decided to
construct the air duct system above ground Based on the structural plans
dated supplied to us the proposed improvements
consisting of construction of new shear walls and demolition of some
existing walls for the structure were extended to the kitchen area and
mauka end of the equipment room Temporary shoring system consisting of
pipes is planned
Construction of new concrete slab on grade and spread footing
foundations is anticipated Minimal site grading is anticipated for the
project
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface conditions at the project site in order to develop a
generalized soil rock data set for the formulation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertinent to foundation design of the project
The work was done in general accordance with our proposal to
dated The scope of work consisted of the
following
1 Drilling and sampling seven 7 borings to depths of 9 0 to 10 0
feet below the existing ground surface

2 Reconnaissance of the project site and vicinity to make visual
observations of the surficial soil conditions
3 Review of available soils and geologic information pertinent to
the project site and its vicinity
4 Laboratory testing of selected samples obtained during the field
exploration to determine the engineering properties and
classification of the materials encountered
5 Engineering analyses of the field and laboratory data for the
purpose of developing foundation design recommendations and
6 Preparation of this report presenting the findings and design
recommendations
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration and laboratory testing

SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed project site is located on 4816 Kahala Avenue in the
southeastern part of Oahu Hawaii The site is occupied by the existing
two story beach front structure The terrain of the site is generally
level but the topography map of the site was not available at the time of
our report preparation
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling seven 7
shallow borings with small portable equipment to depths of 9 to 10 feet
below the existing ground surface The approximate locations of the
borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2114 00 Page 4
The field exploration was performed in August 1988 and the boring
locations were based on our understanding of the original improvement
scheme Subsequent to our field exploration however the improvement
plan was revised and our borings did not totally cover the current
improvement areas We believe that the drilled borings should be
representative of the general shallow subsurface soil conditions at the
site and they should be adequate in developing preliminary geotechnical
engineering recommendations for the project
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained through
the construction phase of the foundation work to verify that the subsoil
conditions are consistent with the design assumptions
In general the borings encountered a surficial fill layer consisting
primarily of medium dense silty sands with gravel overlying medium dense
beach sand deposits Coral reef detritus was encountered at about 9 foot
depths in the borings
Groundwater level was encountered in the borings at depths ranging
from 6 4 to 7 6 feet below the existing ground surface during our field
exploration Due to the proximity of the site to the ocean it is
anticipated that the groundwater level will fluctuate with tidal changes
Detailed descriptions of the materials encountered are presented on
the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 7 Laboratory testing were
performed on selected samples obtained The test results are presented in
Appendix B
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration generally encountered medium dense sandy soils
near the surface It is our opinion that the on site soils may provide
adequate foundation support for the proposed project improvements
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2114 00 Page 5
Foundations
Spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the proposed
improvements An allowable bearing value of 2000 p s f may be used for
footings bearing on the on site sandy soils or compacted fill The
bearing value is for total dead plus live loads and may be increased by
one third when temporary wind and seismic loads are considered Estimated
settlements are on the order of less than one half of an inch total and
one fourth of an inch differential
Individual footings and continuous wall footings should be embedded a
minimum of eighteen 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade
The bottom of excavated footing locations should be tamped to a dense firm
consistency
If foundations are located next to sewer drainage easements they
should be embedded below a 1 1 H V imaginary plane extending upward from
the bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the
utility lines Loose utility line backfills adjacent to the new footings
should be properly removed and recompacted to a minimm of 90 relative
density per ASIM Test Method D 1557 78
The bottom of all footing excavations should be observed by the
geotechnical engineer prior to placement of the reinforcement to
determine if adequate bearing has been obtained and that loose soils are
removed prior to concrete placement
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive
earth pressure developed against the buried vertical faces of the
foundation system and by frictional resistance developed between the
bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be calculated using an
equivalent fluid pressure of 300 p c f This assumes that the soil around
the foundation is well compacted A coefficient of friction of 0 3 may be
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2114 00 Page 6
used Unless covered by pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the
upper 18 inches of soil should be neglected
Excavations
It is anticipated that excavation through the existing concrete
Slab on grade is needed for construction of the new foundations
The new footings when located immediately adjacent to existing
foundations should be embedded at the same level as the existing
foundations The bottom six 6 inches of the footing excavations should
be scarified moisture conditioned and recompacted to 90 of its maximm
dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Due to the sandy cohesionless nature of the on site soils
sloughing around the excavation sides and underneath the existing slabs
immediately adjacent to the new footing excavations may occur
Over excavations resulting from the new footing installations should be
properly backfilled The voids spaces under the existing slab footing
bottom should be backfilled with lean concrete during the new footing
concrete placement if required
Since the existing foundation is supported on the sandy on site
soils extreme care should be exercised to minimize excessive disturbance
to the slab or footing during the new excavations Deep excavations
adjacent to the existing structure should be properly shored and protected
from cave in due to the cohessionless characteristics of the sandy soils
Slab On Grade
Concrete slab on grade construction is anticipated for the floor A
4 inch layer of 3B Fine gravel should be provided below the slabs as
cushion fill The slab subgrade should be kept moist prior to concrete
placement The thickened edge of the slab should be embedded a minimum of
18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2114 00 Page 7
In order to protect against future moisture infiltration and
subsequent damage to floor coverings an impervious moisture barrier is
recommended below the slabs provided that an inch of moist fine sand is
placed above the plastic membrane to help concrete curing
Site Grading
Minimal site grading is anticipated
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limits should be cleared of vegetation debris rubbish old footings and
Slabs and other deleterious materials These materials should be removed
and disposed of properly off site
The areas should then be scarified to a depth of six 6 inches
moisture conditioned and recompacted to 90 of its maximum dry density per
ASIM Test Method D 1557 78 Any loose or soft spots encountered should be
over excavated and then replaced with compacted non expansive select
material
Based on the field exploration and laboratory test results most of
the on site soils may be used as fill material If required imported
fill material should consist of non expansive select granular materials
less than 3 inches in maximm size with less than 20 passing the 200
Sieve All imported fill material should be tested and approved by the
geotechnical engineer prior to transportation to the site
The fill should be placed in maximm 8 inch thick loose level lifts
and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density per ASIM Test
Method D 1557 78
The site should be graded to drain water away from the foundations

Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comments
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this review
is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for
misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during site grading and
construction of the foundations This is to observe compliance with the
design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow design
changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from that
anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in
this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field borings Variations of
conditions between the field data points may occur and the nature and
extent of these variations may not become evident until construction If
variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations given in this report

W O 2114 00 Page 9
The location of the field borings were approximately determined by
taping from structures indicated on the plan provided by Hazama Gumi
Ltd Elevations of the data points were not available at the time of our
report preparation The physical locations of the field data points
should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the method
used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross sections are based upon interpolation between field
data points and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
Water level data from the field data points was collected at the
times given on the graphic representations and or in the text of this
report This data has been reviewed and interpretations made in the
formulation of this report However it must be noted that fluctuation
may occur due to variation in rainfall tides temperature and other
factors

encountered is presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 7

sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the Boring Logs at the appropriate
sample depths
