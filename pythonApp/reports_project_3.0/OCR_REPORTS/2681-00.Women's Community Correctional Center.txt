INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed additions and expansions of the Women s Community
Correctional Center in Kaneohe on the island of Oahu The general location of the site
and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The exploration was
performed in general accordance with our proposal dated
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2681 00 Page 2
This report summarizes our findings from the exploration and our geotechnical
engineering recommendations derived from our analyses based on the data obtained
during the exploration and laboratory testing These recommendations are intended for
design input and are not intended to form the basis of preparation of construction cost
estimates or for bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct new buildings and to expand existing cottages at the
subject site Based on the information provided to us the new additions may consist of
one to two story reinforced concrete structures Also planned is the siting of a portable
classroom trailer to the north of Olomana Cottage
Minimal site grading is anticipated It is our current understanding that there is no
plan to grade or cut the slope immediately to the east of Maunawili Cottage The
anticipated maximum column loads may be on the order of 60 kips and the ground level
floors will be on grade slabs
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions at the project site in order to develop a generalized soil data set for the
formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to the grading and
foundation design of the project
The work was done in general accordance with our proposal to the Department
of Accounting General Services dated The scope of work comprised
the following items

site and its vicinity
2 Reconnaissance of the project site and vicinity to make visual observations

the existing ground surface

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located near the intersection of Ulupii Street and Kalanianaole
Highway in Kaneohe Oahu Hawaii Although the facility is planned for the Women s
Community Correctional Center it is currently being used as the Hawaii Youth Correction
GEOLABS HAWAII

Facility The facility includes three cottages Olomana Kaala and Maunawili and an
administration building
To the north and east of the site the terrain slopes up at ratios of two horizontal
to one vertical 2H 1V to three horizontal to one vertical 3H 1V to 360 feet above Mean
Sea Level MSL to the north and east The area where the three cottages are situated
is generally level with existing elevations ranging from 156 to 170 feet above MSL An
existing drainageway located about 100 feet west of Kaala Cottage drains from south to
north
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling seven 7 borings to
existing ground surface The approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site
Plan Plate 2
The field exploration generally indicate that the site is underlain by residual soils
consisting of stiff reddish brown clayey silt developed through weathering of Koolau
Basalt
Groundwater was encountered at nine 9 and twelve and one half 12 5 foot
depths in Boring Nos 3 and 4 respectively during our field exploration We believe the
groundwater encountered is a result of a perched groundwater condition which may be
expected to vary with rainfall and climate in the general area

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration generally encountered competent subsoils near the surface
It is Our Opinion that the stiff residual soils may provide adequate foundation support for
the proposed additions and expansions if the recommendations provided herein are
closely followed
Site Grading
Minimal site grading is anticipated
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
cleared of vegetation debris rubbish old footings and pavements and other deleterious
materials These materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site
The area should then be scarified to a minimum depth of six 6 inches moisture
conditioned and recompacted to a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density as
determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 Any loose or soft spots encountered
should be excavated to firm in situ material and the resulting depression backfilled with
suitable material compacted to a minimum of 90 compaction as recommended above
Based on the field exploration results most of the on site near surface soils may
be used as general fill and backfill If required imported material for fill or backfill should
consist of non expansive select granular material less than 3 inches in maximum size with
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2681 00 Page 6
less than 20 passing the 200 sieve All imported fill or backfill material should be
tested and approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to transportation to the site
The fill or backfill should be placed in loose level lifts of ten 10 inches maximum
thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density as
determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
If it is decided later that the slope immediately to the east of Maunawili Cottage has
to be graded due to relocation of the existing access road or other reasons then
excavation into the existing slope may be needed For on site residual soils a maximum
cut slope ratio of two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V may be used
The site should be graded to drain water away from the foundations In particular
a swale is recommended immediately adjacent to the east of Maunawili Cottage to divert
surface run off of the slope away from the foundations
Foundations
Spread or continuous footings may be used for the Proposed project An
allowable bearing value of up to 3000 p s f may be used for footing design The bearing
value indicated is for total dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third when
temporary wind and seismic loads are considered Estimated settlements are on the
order of less than one half of an inch total and one fourth of an inch differential
In general a minimum footing embedment of eighteen 18 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grade is recommended If foundations are located next to
sewer drainage easements they should be embedded below a one horizontal to one
vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane extending upward from the bottom edge of the utility
trenches or as deep as the inverts of the utility lines
GEOLABS HAWAIl
W O 2681 00 Page 7
If loose pockets are encountered at footing bottoms the bottoms of footing
excavations should be scarified to a minimum depth of twelve 12 inches and
recompacted to a minimum of 95 of the maximum dry density as determined by ASTM
Test Method D 1557 78 prior to placement of the reinforcement Any soft and wet spots
encountered at footing bottoms should be over excavated and the resulting depression
backfilled with properly compacted fill
The properly prepared footing bottoms should be observed by the project
geotechnical engineer to determine if adequate bearing has been obtained prior to
placement of reinforcement and concrete
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive earth pressure
developed against the buried vertical faces of the foundation system and by frictional
resistance developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be calculated using an equivalent
fluid pressure of 300 p c f This assumes that the soil around the foundation is well
compacted A coefficient of friction of 0 40 may be used Unless covered by pavements
or slabs the passive resistance in the upper 12 inches of soils should be neglected
Slabs On Grade
For the design of slab on grade floors the use of four 4 inch thick concrete slabs
placed over a four 4 inch layer of 3B Fine gravel or ASTM C 33 No 67 gravel cushion
for capillary break is recommended To protect against moisture damage of the floor
covering material a moisture barrier such as polyethylene membrane should be used
below the slabs A one inch layer of sand should also be placed over the membrane
prior to concrete placement to aid in the curing of the concrete
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2681 00 Page 8
Based on our previous experience in the area and current laboratory tests the on
site soils may be moderately expansive when saturated from a dry condition Therefore
it is recommended that the on site soils within twelve 12 inches of the bottom of the slab
cushion be replaced with non expansive import select materials compacted to a minimum
of 90 of its maximum dry density as determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 The
subgrade soils should be kept moist prior to compaction of the imported select materials
The thickened edges of the on grade slabs should extend a minimum of 18 inches
below the lowest adjacent finished grades
Utilities
New under ground utility lines may be anticipated for the project It is anticipated
that most trenching will be in relatively stiff residual soils The on site soils if properly
dried to near optimum moisture content are generally suitable for trench backfill The
backfill should be properly compacted as recommended in the Site Grading section
presented hereinbefore Mechanical compaction is recommended as the on site fine
grained soils are not suitable for compaction by water tamping or jetting
The proposed expansions will be attached to existing buildings It is the
contractor s responsibility to locate and protect all the existing utility lines prior to and
during construction The drawings and specifications should include requirements making
the contractor responsible for verifying the location depth and size of all existing utility
lines and that he will be held responsible for correction of any damage thereto that may
be caused by his operations at no additional cost There should also be advice to the
effect that there may be additional utility lines not discovered through the designer s field
examination
GEOLABS HAWAII

Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be forwarded to the
geotechnical engineer for review and written comments prior to construction This review
is needed to determine adherence to the earthwork and foundation recommendations
given herein If this review is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume
responsibility for misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during site grading and construction of the foundation
This is to observe compliance with the design concepts specifications or
recommendations and to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions
differ from that anticipated at the time of preparation of this report The recommendations
give in this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and approved by
the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed soil conditions encountered during construction are different from
those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to the design
should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from field borings Variations of conditions between the field borings
may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident until
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2681 00 Page 10
construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re
evaluate the recommendations given in this report
The locations of the field borings are approximate having been determined by
taping from objects indicated on the topographic map by R M Towill Corporation and
transmitted to us by The Department of Accounting and General Services on April 8
1991 Elevations of the field borings were interpreted from the same map The physical
locations and elevations of the field borings should be considered accurate only to the
degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the field borings depict
the approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual
transition
Water level data from the field borings was collected at the times given on the
graphic representations and or in the text of this report This data has been reviewed
and interpretations made in the formulation of this report However it must be noted that
fluctuation may occur due to variations in rainfall temperature and other factors
This report has been prepared for the exclusive use of Department of Accounting
and General Services and its consultants for specific application to the proposed
Additions and Expansions Women s Community Correctional Center in accordance with
generally accepted geotechnical engineering principles and practices No other warranty
expressed or implied is made
This report has been prepared solely for the purpose of assisting the
architect engineer in the preliminary design evaluation of the proposed project
Therefore this report may not contain sufficient data or the proper information for use
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2681 00 Page 11
as the basis for preparation of construction contract cost estimates or for bidding
purposes A contractor wishing to bid on this project is urged to retain a competent
geotechnical engineer to assist in the interpretation of this report and or in the
performance of additional site specific exploration for bid estimating purposes
The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are based on our site
reconnaissance and soil information from borings and laboratory test data obtained for
this soil evaluation
The owner client should be aware that unanticipated soil conditions are commonly
encountered Unforeseen soil conditions such as perched groundwater soft deposits
hard layers or cavities may occur in localized areas and may require additional probing
or corrections in the field which may result in construction delays to attain a properly
constructed project Therefore a sufficient contingency fund is recommended to
accommodate these possible extra costs

Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling seven 7 borings
to depths of 16 5 to 21 5 feet below the existing ground surface at the approximate
locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 A truck mounted drill rig was used to drill these
borings
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examinations in the field by our engineer who continuously monitored the drilling
operations The classification were further reviewed by visual inspection and testing in
the laboratory All soils were classified in general conformance with Unified Soil
Classification Systems A summary of the material encountered are presented on the
Borings Logs Plates A 1 through A 7
Soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving a 2 inch standard split
30 inches The blow counts to drive the sampler for the last 12 inches are shown on the
Boring Logs at the corresponding sample depth
