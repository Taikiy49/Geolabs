Geotechnical Engineering Report
Kailua Wastewater Treatment Plant
Modifications
Kailua
It is our understanding that the proposed new Primary Sedimentation
Tanks for the above mentioned project have been relocated subsequent to
our field exploration and the issuance of our geotechnical engineering
report The new tanks will be moved east to the previously proposed
location of the new administration building planned for the future Phase
II development of the modifications project
It is also our understanding that due to current budget restraint
additional borings will not be drilled at the proposed new tank location
to study the underlying subsoil conditions Instead previous boring data
by others have been provided by the Department of Wastewater Management
for our evaluation The two closest previous borings namely B 12 and
B 20 were located at distances of about 120 to 170 feet from the new tank
location
Two of our recently drilled borings namely B 5 and B 7 are located
closer to the new tank location than the previous borings by others
These two borings however indicate that complex subsoil conditions as
discussed in our soil report dated may be present
below the new tank location

Therefore the following foundation recommendations for the relocated
New Primary Sedimentation Tanks are based on limited boring data obtained
outside of the new tank site
Based on the assumed soil conditions and the anticipated design
bottom elevations of the tanks maximm depth at elevation 7 feet MSL
we believe that the new sedimentation tanks may be supported by a mat
foundation bearing partially on loose soft lagoonal subsoils and partially
on hard saprolite weathered basalt The use of a rigid mat foundation to
Support the whole structure bearing on different subsoils may help to
reduce the differential settlement of the structure if supported on
footings
Based on the existing topography it appears that the eastern portion
of the structure site may need to be raised by up to about 5 feet Due to
the anticipated loose soft subsoil conditions at this area it is our
opinion that this new fill should be placed as early as possible to allow
most of the induced ground settlements to occur We recommend that a
minimm waiting period of 6 months should be allowed for this purpose
After the waiting period the site may be excavated for the
installation of the tank structure It is anticipated that a large earth
overburden will be removed by the deep excavation and this may compensate
the bearing pressure from the tank structure plus its future sewage
loads An allowable bearing value of 3000 p s f may be used for the
preliminary design of the mat foundation Estimated net settlements under
the design loadings are negligible
Resistance to lateral loads on the tank structure can be obtained by
frictional resistance and passive earth pressure A frictional
coefficient of 0 4 may be used to compute the frictional resistance
between the bottom of the mat and the recompacted underlying soils In
addition an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 p s f per foot of depth may
be used to determine the passive earth resistance against the buried
vertical faces of the structure walls
In addition a working platform consisting of about 2 feet of 3B
Fine gravel should be placed directly below the bottom of the structure in
the loose lagoonal subsoils under groundwater level
Design Review
Plans for site grading and structure foundation design should be
forwarded to the soil engineer for review and written comments prior to
construction to see that the intent of our recommendations is included in
the project plans and specifications

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the site grading and foundation excavation be
observed by the soil engineer during construction This is to see that
the subsurface conditions are consistent with the assumptions used in our
analysis
If conditions encountered during construction are different from
those assumed in this addendum the soil engineer should immediately be
informed and appropriate modifications to the design should be made The
recommendations given in this addendum are contingent upon the above
observations
Limitations
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations
made in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation engineering
principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all other
warranties either expressed or implied
The recommendations submitted in this addendum are based on soil
information from the closest borings drilled for our previous geotechnical
engineering exploration
Unanticipated soil conditions such as pockets of organic soils
trash fill or expansive soils are commonly encountered and may occur in
localized areas Therefore additional expenditures may be needed during
construction to attain a properly constructed project Some contingency
fund is thus recommended to accommodate these possible costs
This addendum has been prepared in order to assist the engineer in
