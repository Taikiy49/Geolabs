PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of the exploration was to evaluate the subsurface
conditions below the site to provide pertinent soil parameters for the
design of the proposed new fender pile system at Wharves W4 and WS5
The scope of our work included drilling and sampling three 3
borings laboratory testing of samples obtained engineering evaluation
of the field and laboratory data development of design recommendations
and report preparation
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration logs of borings
and laboratory test results are presented in the appendices of this
report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to install a new fender pile system to replace the
existing one at the subject wharves The proposed fender pile system
will consist of vertical 18 inch square prestressed concrete piles
It is our understanding that the proposed fender pile system will
incorporate a new concept to dissipate the ship impact forces against
the wharf decks In lieu of the conventional rubber energy absorber
the impact forces will be absorbed by deflection of the fender pile
The fender pile is assumed to be hinged at the deck level and at some
point below the mudline and the impact forces are transmitted to the
middle of the pile through a wooden camel structure
GEOLABS HAWAI

It is our understanding that the concrete fender piles would be
pile casting to increase the bending flexibility of the piles As a
result the fender piles could deflect more and thus could absorb more
of the berthing and mooring impact energy forces which are anticipated
to be about 25 kips per pile
SITE DESCRIPTION
Ammunition Wharves W4 and W5 are approximately 1000 feet long and
are located adjacent to each other in the Naval Magazine at the
southern end of the West Loch Branch of Pearl Harbor Oahu Hawaii
The wharves are used to receive and distribute ammunition in
support of naval ships and other military organizations
Both wharves are concrete deck structures supported on long
concrete or composite piles with the top of the wharf deck at elevation
of about 108 5 feet U S Navy Datum MLIW Elevation 100 feet
Behind the wharf decks are steel sheetpile bulkheads where the
backfilled areas are paved with asphaltic concrete
Based on existing plans it is our understanding that the mudline
beneath the wharves was dredged to variable slopes and the toe of slope
is generally at an elevation of about 65 feet where the ground becomes
basically horizontal
During our field exploration measurements indicated that the
existing mudline at the proposed fender pile locations along the
wharves was generally slightly above the toe of slope at depths of
about 39 to 42 feet at elevations of about 66 5 to 69 5 feet below
the top of the wharf deck

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The geologic conditions underlying the site are the results of a
complex series of deposition and erosional events brought on by
glacioeustatic sea level changes and post erosional volcanic activities
during the Pleistocene Following the cessation of activity of the
Koolau Volcano alluvial materials were deposited in an estuarine and
deltaic environment in the West Loch area of Pearl Harbor
The sea level fluctuated several times receding perhaps 300 feet
below and rising about 95 feet above the present sea level
As Oahu sank coral reef grew around the shorelines keeping pace
with the subsidence
Concurrent with a rise in sea level during late Pleistocene era
eruption of the Salt Lake Makalapa member of the Honolulu Volcanic
Series mantled the coral reefs and alluvium with deposits of volcanic
ash which consolidated to form volcanic tuff locally called mudrock
layers Subsequent erosion has dissected and removed the mantles of
tuff at the site
During the Recent epoch as sea level rose to its current stand
the area was covered with loose coralline marine deposits

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on our field exploration and engineering analysis it
appears that the most critical consideration in the proposed fender
pile design and construction is the embedment depth of the piles below
the existing mudline
We believe that the proposed 18 inch square prestressed concrete
fender piles should be embedded to a minimum depth of about 70 feet
below the existing top of deck level of the wharves equivalent to a
depth of about 30 feet below the existing mudline
To install the fender piles to the required minimum depth of about
70 feet the proposed pile locations should be predrilled to penetrate
into the lower dense and stiff layers at variable depths

Vertical Pile Capacity
The fender piles would develop their vertical load capacity
primarily from frictional resistance or adhesion between piles and the
upper loose soils
The vertical loading on the pile is minimal since the ship forces
are primarily horizontal Therefore the length of pile required will
be governed by lateral pile load considerations as described below
The pile driving should be monitored by a geotechnical engineer to
see that the pile is driven to the required depth and that sufficient
resistance have been developed
Lateral Pile Capacity
The loading on the fender piles will be primarily lateral loads
due to the berthing and mooring forces from the ship The ship loads
will be transmitted to the fender piles through a wooden camel system
A schematic sketch of the loading on the fender piles is presented on
Plate 4
Since the fender piles would be subjected to repeated loads a
critical consideration would be to limit the permanent lateral
deflections of the piles This may be achieved by sufficient embedment
of the fender piles
lateral resistance of the fender piles can be analyzed by using
the appropriate soil modulus values of the subsoils at various depths
below the existing mudline at the proposed fender pile locations The
analysis was carried out by using a finite element lateral pile
analysis supplemented by the use of non linear P Y curves to represent
the soil modulus values

The program solves the general differential equation of beams on
non elastic non linear medium by adopting the P Y curve approach
Since the lateral soil modulus varies with the deflection experienced
the computerized analysis first assumes a modulus value then
calculates the pile deflection based on the assumed modulus The
calculated deflection is then used to determine the modulus from the
non linear P Y curves The calculated modulus and the assumed modulus
are then compared The procedure is repeated until the new assumed and
calculated moduli are essentially the same
Based on the analysis the point of fixity zero movement of the
pile at maximm deflection was calculated to be at about 65 feet below
the existing top of wharf deck about 25 feet below the existing
mudline Therefore we believe that the fender piles should be driven
to a total minimum depth of about 70 feet below the existing top of the
wharf deck in order to develop sufficient resistance to the design
lateral load Splices should be avoided
The fender piles should be driven to the design depth with a
hammer delivering about 18 000 foot pounds of energy and to a
resistance not to exceed 20 blows per foot
Predrilling
Based on the existing mudline slope profiles and the soil
conditions in the three drilled borings it appears that dense cemented
sand and gravel layers and stiff clayey silt layers may be present at
the depths of about 55 to 78 feet below the existing top of the wharf
deck at the proposed fender pile locations
The fender pile prestressing will be reduced in order to increase
the flexibility and corresponding energy absorbing capability of the
piles Due to the reduction in prestressing the fender pile would be

more susceptible to damage during hard pile driving Therefore we
recommend that predrilling prior to pile driving be used to penetrate
the dense and stiff layers at the proposed fender pile locations along
the wharves to reduce the possibility of hard driving
For preliminary cost estimate purpose it is our opinion that
predrilling to a depth of about 30 feet below the existing mudline may
be required The upper 15 feet of predrilling may primarily be in
loose soils while the lower 15 feet of predrilling may penetrate the
dense and stiff layers depending on the depths of these layers The
predrilled hole should have a diameter of about 25 inches
Test Piles
We recommend that test piles be driven along wharves W4 and W5
prior to the production pile driving to confirm the soil conditions
along the porposed fender pile locations and to evaluate the
predrilling and pile driving characteristics in the field We believe
approximately 10 of the total piles should be driven as test piles
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed fender pile system
should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written
comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that all fender pile installation be observed by
the soils engineer during construction to see that adequate penetration
and preparation have been made and that the subsurface conditions are
consistent with the findings of our exploration
If conditions encountered during construction are different from
those presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made

Field Exploration

encountered is presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 1 through
A 3 2

sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the Logs of Borings at the
appropriate sample depths
