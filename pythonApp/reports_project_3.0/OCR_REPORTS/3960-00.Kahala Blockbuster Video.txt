INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Kahala Blockbuster Video project located in the Kahala area
of Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project
site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering
recommendations resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering
analyses These recommendations are intended for the design of foundation site grading
and pavements only The findings and recommendations presented herein are subject to
the limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed project site is located at the southwest corner of the intersection of
Waialae Avenue and Hunakai Street in the Kahala area of Honolulu on the island of
Oahu Hawaii We understand that construction of a new Blockbuster Video store is
desired at the project site
Based on the information provided the proposed building will be located at the
south side of the lot and will consist of a single story masonry building with a light
steel framed roof system The building will have plan dimensions of approximately 70 by
75 feet A parking area with approximately 21 stalls will also be constructed at the
northern side of the property We understand that there will be two entrances into the lot
which will be from Waialae Avenue on the north side and Hunakai Street on the east side
Based on the layout of the site we anticipate that only minor site grading on the
order of 2 feet or less of cuts and fills would be necessary to achieve the design
finished grades for the proposed project Further details pertaining to the structural
loads and site grading were not available at the time this report was prepared

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from two shield
volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be middle
to late Pliocene in age and forms the bulk of the western third of the island The younger
shield Koolau is estimated to be late Pliocene to early Pleistocene Ice Age in age and
forms the majority of the eastern two thirds of the island Waianae became extinct while
Koolau was still active and its eastern flank was partially buried below Koolau lavas
banking against its eastern flank
The project site is located on the southern flank of the Koolau shield at the mouth
of the Waialanui Gulch The subsurface conditions at the project site are composed of
terrigenous alluvial and colluvial soils associated with the Waialainui Gulch deposited by
continuous erosion of the volcanic dome The base of the stratigraphic section in this
area is Koolau Basalt Land development in this century and placement of man made fills
have brought the project site to its present form

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsoil conditions at the project site were explored by drilling and sampling
three borings extending to depths of about 15 to 21 5 feet below the existing ground
surface The approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Generally our field exploration encountered surficial fills consisting of silty
coral gravel with sand of about 0 5 feet thick below the existing ground surface Below
this surficial fill layer very stiff sandy silts and medium dense to dense silty sands and
silty gravels were encountered to the maximum depths explored of approximately 15 to
21 5 feet below the existing ground surface The borings indicated that the sands and
gravels were intermixed with varying amounts of silts and some cobbles
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings at the time of our field
exploration However it should be noted that groundwater levels are subject to change
due to rainfall time of year and other factors
Detailed descriptions of the materials encountered in the borings are presented on
the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 3 of Appendix A Results of the laboratory
tests performed on selected soil samples are presented in Appendix B

Building Foundations
As previously mentioned the proposed video store will consist of a single story
masonry building with a light steel framed roof Based on the subsurface conditions
encountered in our exploration we recommend that a shallow foundation system
consisting of spread and or continuous footings be used to support the proposed
structure An allowable bearing pressure of up to may be used for the design
of footings bearing on the near surface silty sands and silty gravels encountered in our
borings This allowable bearing pressure is for dead plus live loads and may be
increased by one third 1 3 for transient loads such as those caused by temporary wind
and or seismic forces Footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below
the lowest adjacent finished grade
As previously indicated a drainage ditch runs along the west side of the property
and this portion of the site is supported by a masonry retaining wall of about 8 feet in

foundations should be located far enough away from the wall to provide a minimum
horizontal set back distance equal to the depth of the retaining wall or about 10 feet from

footings for the new building should be embedded deeper below a 45 degree imaginary

lf the foundations are designed and constructed in accordance with our
recommendations we estimate that the total footing settlements under the anticipated
design loads to be on the order of less than 1 inch with differential settlements of
approximately 0 5 inches
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by frictional resistance
between the base of the spread footing and the bearing materials and by passive earth
pressure developed against the near vertical faces of the embedded portion of footings
A coefficient of friction of 0 40 may be used between the base of the footings and the
supporting granular materials Resistance due to passive pressure may be calculated
using an equivalent fluid pressure of 350 pounds per square foot per foot of depth pcf
for footings embedded in the near surface silty sands and gravels These values assume
that the concrete for footings is placed directly against the footing excavations
Unless covered by pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the upper 12 inches
below the finished grade should be neglected
Where footings are located adjacent to or on slopes the footings should be
embedded deep enough to provide a minimum horizontal set back distance of at least
8 feet measured from the edge of the footing to the face of the slope Where footings are
located adjacent to below grade structures or utilities the footings should extend to a
depth below an imaginary 45 degree plane projected upward from the bottom of the
below grade structure or utility This requirement is necessary to avoid surcharging

Slabs On Grade
We anticipate that concrete slabs on grade will be used for the proposed building
construction The granular soils encountered at the site may be classified as
non expansive However it should be noted that the surficial fills encountered at the site
should be recompacted to provide a firm subgrade for construction of improvements at
the site Therefore subgrades for slabs on grades should be scarified to a depth of at
least 8 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture and compacted to
not less than 90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place
dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same
soil determined in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91
Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the
maximum dry density
To provide a more uniform support under slabs we recommend that the concrete
slab on grade be supported on a minimum 4 inch cushion fill The cushion fill layer
should consist of open graded gravels such as No 3B _ Fine gravel
ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation which may also serve as a capillary moisture break
The slab cushion fill should be placed as soon as possible after the scarification and
recompaction of the existing subgrade soils as recommended above

For slabs on grade with moisture sensitive surfacing we recommend that an
impermeable membrane be placed over the cushion fill to reduce the potential for
migration of moisture vapor through the concrete slab In order to promote more uniform
curing of the slab concrete and to provide protection of the vapor membrane placement
of a 2 inch layer of moist fine sand may be considered above the membrane If the
2 inch sand layer is desired the sand should be moistened slightly prior to placing the
slab concrete

Requirements
Site grading operations should be observed by a_ representative of
Geolabs Hawaii It is important that a representative from our office observe whether the
exposed soil conditions during excavation are similar to those encountered in our
exploration The following recommendations are intended to provide guidelines for the
design of site grading work
Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork areas within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed Vegetation debris and other unsuitable
materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site to reduce the
potential for contamination of the excavated materials Soft and yielding areas
encountered during clearing and grubbing below areas designated to receive fill
should be over excavated to expose firm natural material and the resulting
excavation should be backfilled with well compacted engineered fill

The excavated soft and or organic soils should be properly disposed of off site or
at designated areas on site
After clearing and grubbing the exposed subgrade should be scarified to a
minimum depth of 8 inches properly moisture conditioned to above the optimum
moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a
percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil determined in accordance
with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91 Optimum moisture is the water content
percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
Fills and Backfills
In general the on site granular soils generated from the excavation may be
reused as a source of general fill and backfill materials provided that the
recommendations contained herein are followed On site granular soils to be
used as fill should contain no particles greater than 3 inches in largest
dimension It should be noted that some cobbles materials greater than
3 inches in maximum dimension were encountered in the borings drilled at the
site Therefore over sized materials from the on site soils will need to be
screened prior to being re used as fill materials
If imported fill is required the materials should be well graded from coarse to fine
with no particles larger than 3 inches in largest dimension In addition the fill
material should contain between 10 and 30 percent particles passing the
No 200 sieve The materials should be tested and approved by Geolabs Hawaii
prior to delivery to the site to confirm that it is suitable for its intended purpose
Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
Fills and backfills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose
thickness moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction

maximum dry density of the same soil determined in accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 1557 91 Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by

3 feet of the pavement grades should be compacted to a minimum of 95 percent
relative compaction
Pavement Design
New flexible pavements are anticipated for the parking areas for the proposed
project We anticipate that the vehicle loading for this project will consist primarily of
passenger vehicles light pick up trucks and occasional heavy trucks We have made
our preliminary pavement design assuming the pavement subgrade soil will be similar to
the silty gravel and silty sand soils encountered in the borings Based on the above
assumptions we recommend the following pavement section be used for preliminary
design purposes
Asphaltic Concrete Pavement
2 Inch Asphalt Concrete
6 Inch Base Course 95 Percent Relative Compaction
8 Inch Total Pavement Thickness on a Moist Compacted Subgrade
The pavement subgrade soils should be scarified to a minimum depth of 8 inches
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to not less
than 95 percent relative compaction If pumping of the subgrade occurs during
construction the subgrade should be proof rolled to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction The base course should consist of crushed basalt aggregate compacted to
a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction

Laboratory CBR tests and field density tests should be performed on samples of
the actual subgrade soils encountered during construction to confirm the above
recommended design section The recommended pavement section is based on the
assumption that good drainage will be provided for areas adjacent to the pavemenis
Paved areas should be sloped and drainage gradients maintained to carry all

Utility Trench
We envision that new utility lines would be required for the proposed project
In general we recommend that a granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine
gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation be used under the pipes for support of the utility
lines The backfill up to about 12 inches above the pipes should consist of free draining
materials such as No 3B Fine gravel to reduce the potential for damaging the pipes
from compaction of the backfill
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipes to
the top of the subgrade or finished grade may consist of the on site soils or select
granular material The backfill should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum
moisture content placed in maximum 8 inch level loose lifts and mechanically
compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for
future ground subsidence Where trenches are below pavement areas the upper 3 feet

Drainage
The finished grades outside the building should be sloped to shed water away
from foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also advised that gutter
systems be installed around the building and that the discharge be diverted away from
the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near the foundations
and slabs should also be avoided Planters next to foundations should be avoided or
have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil
saturation and subsequent loss of strength and or heaving of the near surface soils
The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled against the footings
immediately after setting of the concrete to reduce water infiltration In addition drainage
swales should be provided as soon as possible and maintained to drain all surface run off
away from the slabs and foundations
