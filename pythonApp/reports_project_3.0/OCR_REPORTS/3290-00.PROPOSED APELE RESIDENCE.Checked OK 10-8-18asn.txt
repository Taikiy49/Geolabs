INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Apele Residence located at Lot No 112 of the st
increment of the Waikii Ranch Estates in Waikoloa of South Kohala on the island of
Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings from our field exploration and laboratory
testing and presents our design level geotechnical engineering recommendations
derived from our analyses for the proposed project These recommendations are
intended for design input only and may not be appropriate to form the basis for
construction cost estimates nor for construction bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed project site is located at Lot No 112 of the 1st increment of the
Waikii Ranch Estates in Waikoloa of South Kohala on the island of Hawaii
The subject lot is approximately rectangular in shape with an area of
approximately 10 acres It is proposed to construct a single family residence at the
south side of the lot Detailed information on the design grades and structural loadings
were not available to us when this report was prepared
Based on our borings and previous experience in the area it appears that the
site is underlain by a surface deposit of volcanic ash over basalt formation at deeper
depths Special consideration was given to the effects of the volcanic ash encountered
at the project site on the future foundations and slab on grade design of the proposed
residence

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The project site is situated on the northeastern flank of Hualalai which is the third
oldest of the 5 shield volcanoes forming the island of Hawaii Hualalai was built during
the Pleistocene and Holocene Epochs by basaltic lava flows from the Hualalai Volcanic
Series
Typically the lava flows of the Hualalai series are composed of alkalic olivine
basaltic lava flows grading to hawaiite These lava flows are frequently mantled by a
layer of palagonitized ash locally referred to as Pahala Ash
During the later stages of the active phase of Hualalai the Puu Waawaa vent
opened This event extruding lavas of trachytic composition built a large cinder core
now known as Puu Waawaa On the southerly side of this vent a thick trachyte lava
broke through the cone The flow which is on the order of about 700 feet thick turned
to the south and formed the long ridge now known as Puu Anahulu Subsequent to the
Puu Waawaa eruption younger lava flows from Mauna Loa overran portions of this
ridge
Our field exploration encountered a relatively thick surface layer of ash soil
mantling the site which is believed to be the original airfall deposits from the Puu
Waawaa eruption The ash soil at the project site differs from Pahala Ash found in
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3290 00 Page 6
other portions of the island of Hawaii in that the in situ moisture content is relatively
lower and the soil exists in a friable state as opposed to the plastic soil found in areas
such as along the Hamakua Coast
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our field exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling two borings to
depths of 20 and 21 feet below the existing ground surface
In general our field exploration disclosed that the project site is underlain by a
surficial deposit of volcanic ash soils approximately 9 to 10 feet thick consisting of silty
soils with consistency ranging from medium stiff to very stiff This was underlain by
highly decomposed basalt materials saprolites consisting of very stiff sandy silt
material extending to a depth of approximately 14 to 16 feet below the existing ground
surface Hard basalt formation was then encountered extending to the maximum
depths drilled
The volcanic ash encountered at the surface has relatively low moisture contents
and exists in a friable state The soil when wetted has relatively low strength
characteristics and in a dry state is susceptible to erosion In addition if the in situ
moisture contents are high enough they become thixotropic i e they lose strength
temporarily when remolded and or disturbed Therefore appropriate soil erosion
protection dust control provisions and construction procedures should be observed and
implemented during the foundation construction and site preparation work for the
subject project

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Our field exploration indicates that the site is underlain by a surficial deposit of
volcanic ash approximately 9 feet thick consisting of medium stiff to very stiff silty
soils The dry friable ash encountered at the surface has relatively low strength
characteristics and is susceptible to wind and water erosion In addition if the in situ
moisture contents are high enough they become thixotropic i e they lose strength
temporarily when remolded and or disturbed Therefore special erosion dust control
provisions and construction procedures should be observed and implemented during
the foundation construction and site preparation work for the subject project
Conventional shallow foundations consisting of spread and or continuous footings
may be used for support of the proposed single family residence Because of the
presence of the dry friable silty ash soils we recommend that all shallow footing
foundations slabs on grade and pavements bear on a minimum of 12 inches of
compacted select granular fill Detailed recommendations are presented in the
following sections of this report

Foundations
Due to the presence of the volcanic ash at the surface we recommend that
shallow footings be supported directly on 12 inches of compacted select granular fill
material The fill should extend horizontally beyond the sides of the footing a distance
of at least one half the footing width or 18 inches minimum whichever is greater The
select granular fill material should be placed in two 6 inch lifts compacted to not less
than 90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry
density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil
established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 91 test procedures The over excavated
surface and the backfilling operations should be performed in accordance with the
recommendations presented in the Site Grading section of this report A schematic
section of the footing excavation and backfill details is shown on Plate 3
An allowable bearing pressure of 2 000 p s f may be used for design of footing
foundations bearing on the compacted select granular fill material over the in situ ash
soils This bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third
Ys for transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic forces
Due to the soft ash soils encountered in the upper 2 feet of the project site we
recommend that the footings be embedded a minimum of 2 feet below the lowest
adjacent finish grade In addition due to the sloping terrain at the subject site we
recommend that the foundations be embedded deep enough to obtain a minimum 8
foot set back distance measured horizontally from the outside edge of the footing to
the slope face Continuous foundations running parallel to the direction of the slope
should be constructed in stepped footings

If foundations are located next to utility trenches or easements they should be
embedded below a 1H 1V imaginary plane extending upward from the bottom edge of
the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility lines
We estimate the footing settlements under the anticipated design loads for
footings bearing on compacted select granular fill to be less than 1 inch total and inch
differential
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive earth pressure
acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system and by frictional
resistance developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid
pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth p c f This assumes that
the backfill soil around footings is well compacted The passive pressure should be
reduced for foundations located on slopes Unless covered by pavements or slabs the
passive resistance in the upper 12 inches of soil should be neglected A coefficient of
friction of 0 3 may be used for footings bearing on the compacted fill
We recommend that footing excavations and backfill be observed by a

Site Grading
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable
materials should be removed and disposed of off site

After demolition clearing and grubbing all areas beneath future slabs and
pavement areas should be over excavated to a depth of at least 12 inches below the
future slab or pavement subgrade level i e bottom of No 3B Fine gravel cushion layer
or bottom of base course layer If zones of soft loose or saturated soils are

The over excavated surface should be moisture conditioned to about optimum
moisture content and compacted to about 90 percent relative compaction Relative
compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the
maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 91
test procedures The ash soils encountered at the project site are in a relatively dry
and friable state with relatively low moisture contents as compared to other Pahala Ash
Therefore it is our opinion that the soils will need to be further wetted or moisture
conditioned prior to compaction work However the moisture conditioning should be
carefully controlled to avoid over wetting of the soils
All permanent fill slopes constructed with imported fill material should be
designed with an inclination of 2H 1V or flatter The in situ ash soils should not be
utilized as fills in slope embankment construction Any fills to be placed on existing
slopes having ratios steeper than 5H 1V should be keyed and benched into firm in situ
soil to lessen potential instability of the new fill The filling operations should start at
the lowest point and continue up in level horizontal compacted layers in accordance
with the above recommendations for fill placement A slope ratio of 3H 1V or flatter
is recommended for cut slopes composed of the in situ volcanic ash soils We
recommend that cut slopes exposing ash soils be immediately protected by appropriate

exposed soils

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling
two borings to depths of 20 and 21 feet below the existing ground surface at the
approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were drilled using
truck mounted drilling equipment
The material encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our field engineer who monitored the drilling operations on
a near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by
testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified
Soil Classification System A graphic presentation of the materials encountered are
presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 and A 2
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils
by driving a 3 inch O D Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling
30 inches The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the last 12 inches of an
18 inch drive are shown on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Core samples of rock materials encountered at the site were obtained using
diamond core drilling techniques in general accordance with ASTM Standard Practice
D 2113 83 Diamond Core Drilling for Site Investigation Core drilling is a rotary drilling
method which uses a hollow bit to cut into the rock formation The rock material left
in the hollow core of the bit is mechanically recovered for examination and description
Recovery REC is used as a subjective guide to the interpretation of the relative
quality of rock masses Recovery is defined as the actual length of material recovered
from a coring attempt versus the length of the core attempt such as if 3 7 feet of
material is recovered from a 5 0 foot core run the recovery would be 74 percent and
would be shown on the Boring Logs as REC 74
The Rock Quality Designation RQD is also a subjective guide to the relative
quality of rock masses RQD is defined as the percentage of the core run that is sound
material in excess of 4 inches in length without any discontinuities discounting any
drilling induced fractures or breaks feet of sound material is recovered from a
5 0 foot core run the RQD would be 50 percent and would be shown on the Boring
Logs as RQD 50 Generally the following is used to describe the relative quality of
the rock based on the Practical Handbook of Physical Properties of Rocks and
Minerals
