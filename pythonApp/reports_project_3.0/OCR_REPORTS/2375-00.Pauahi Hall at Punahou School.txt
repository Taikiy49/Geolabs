INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the proposed renovation of Pauahi Hall at
Punahou School in Honolulu Oahu Hawaii The general location of the
site and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The
exploration was performed in general accordance with our proposal dated
August 22 1989
GEOLABS HAWAIl
W O 2375 00 Page 2
This report summarizes the findings from the field exploration and
our geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analyses
based on data obtained during the exploration performed outside the Pauahi
Hall The interior of the hall was not accessible due to pre scheduled
asbestos removal These recommendations are intended for design input and
are not intended for construction bidding and estimation purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to demolish the interior portions of the existing
Pauahi Hall and renovate the two 2 story structure with new basement
excavations in the Entry Court area and mauka Ewa corner of the building
The exterior stone walls and roof are to be retained
Based on the preliminary plans supplied to us the interior columns
walls and slabs in the existing basement areas will be removed and
replaced New pipe columns are anticipated to transfer new structural
loads to new footings
New basement excavations up to about five 5 to six 6 feet below
the existing ground surface are anticipated Underpinning of portions of
the existing wall foundations in the new basement areas would be required
to extend the footings down to below the basement level Temporary
excavation supports and shoring for the exterior stone walls during
construction and renovation are anticipated The basement walls may
serve at least in part as retaining structures
Minimal site grading is anticipated

subsurface soil conditions at the site in order to develop a generalized
soil rock data set for the formulation of geotechnical engineering
recommendations pertinent to underpinning foundation and basement wall
design of the proposed renovation project

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration generally encountered about 6 5 feet of surface
soils consisting primarily of stiff clayey silt overlying medium dense
cinder sand to the maximum depths drilled The stiff subsoils may provide
adequate support for the new foundations of the renovation project It is
anticipated that the existing footing bottoms may be in or very close to
the cinder sand formation due to the basement level excavation
Underpinning of the existing wall footings in the new basement areas
are required to extend the footings down to below the the basement level
Temporary excavation supports and shoring for the exterior stone walls are
recommended during construction
It is the contractor s responsibility to maintain the integrity of
the exterior stone walls during demolition and construction of the
project Design of proper shoring systems and or undertaking of all
appropriate protection measures should be left to the contractor The
building should be properly shored supported prior to interior demolition
works
Foundations
It is our opinion that shallow spread or continuous wall footings
bearing on the natural subsoils may be used to support the anticipated
structural loads An allowable bearing value of 3 000 p s f with a
minimum footing embedment of 18 inches may be utilized for foundation
design
The bearing value is for total dead plus live loads and may be
increased by one third when temporary wind and seismic loads are
considered Estimated settlements are on the order of less than one half
of an inch total and one fourth of an inch differential
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2375 00 Page 6
It is our understanding that new pipe columns may straddle the new
and existing foundations along the perimeter walls Therefore the new
foundations located inmediately adjacent to the existing perimeter wall
foundations should be properly doweled into the existing foundations
If foundations are located next to sewer drainage lines they should
be embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane
extending upward from the bottom edge of the utility trenches or as deep
as the inverts of the utility lines
The footing excavations should be observed by the project
geotechnical engineer to determine if adequate bearing has been obtained
prior to concrete placement
Iateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive
earth pressure developed against the buried vertical faces of the
foundation system and by frictional resistance developed between the
bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be calculated using an
equivalent fluid pressure of 300 p c f This assumes that the soil around
the foundation is well compacted A coefficient of friction of 0 4 may be
used Unless covered by pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the
upper 18 inches of soil should be neglected
Underpinning
It is our understanding that new basements in the front Entry Court
area and mauka Ewa corner of the building are planned Underpinning of
the existing wall foundations are anticipated

based on our field observations of the three 3 pits excavated by the
contractor since information regarding the existing wall foundation

embedment conditions may not be known until the actual basement excavation
of the construction and the recommendations may have to be revised
depending on the exact conditions disclosed by the new basement
excavation

The new basement excavation area in the corner is anticipated to be
approximately 10 by 18 feet in plan dimension Based on our field
observations of Pit 1 the top of the existing perimeter wall footing
is about 12 inches below the existing outside grade or about 3 5 feet
below the first floor
Since the basement slab will be about nine 9 feet below the first
floor slab new basement excavation up to about six 6 feet below
the existing outside grade is anticipated
Underpinning of the existing perimeter wall footings up to 6 feet
deep may be required Construction of the underpinning should be
done on a section by section basis A maximm section width of five
5 feet is recommended for the underpinning works Temporary
excavation support such as bracing raker system should be considered

The existing wall foundations together with the underpinned portions
will serve as one structural unit for a new basement wall Note that
the new basement wall should be able to resist any anticipated
lateral loads such as lateral earth pressure wind and seismic loads
on the building
The existing footings and the underpinned portions may be properly
doweled to provide some lateral load capacity Alternatively a new
basement wall in front of the existing wall may be considered for
lateral capacity depending on the magnitude of the anticipated
lateral loads on the building
New basement excavation is also planned in the Entry Court area It
is our understanding that the semi circular wall in the very front
will be demolished and replaced The demolition may provide an
entrance for the construction equipment into the building
Based on our field observations it appears that the existing wall
footings in the stair area Pit 2 may require underpinning of about
2 feet for the new basement level
The footings supporting the existing tower area Pit 3 appear to
have been embedded deeper and may be at the same level of the
existing perimeter wall footings However the exact footing
embedment conditions should be verified during the actual basement
excavation
As recommended above the construction of the underpinning should be
done on a section by section basis A maximum section width of five
5 feet is recommended for the underpinning work

Underpinning work should be done as soon as practicable after the new
basement excavation
Slab On Grade
New concrete slab on grade is planned for the basement floor of the
renovation The slab bottom should be scarified to a minimm of six 6
inches and compacted to a minimm of 90 maximum dry density per ASIM Test
Method D 1557 78
The concrete slabs should be placed over a four 4 inch thick
cushion layer of gravel size material such as 3B Fine crushed rock to
provide uniform support A moisture barrier membrane is recommended
between the slab and the cushion layer to reduce the possibility of
moisture damage to the floor coverings One 1 inch of moist sand may be
placed above the membrane as an aid to help concrete curing
Basement Walls
It is anticipated that the basement walls will serve at least in
part as retaining walls in the renovated structure The guidelines for
design of retaining walls are as follows
1 For lateral earth pressures the following values may be used
Walls unrestrained at the top 40 p s f equivalent
active condition level fluid pressure
backfill per foot of depth
Walls restrained at the top 60 p s f equivalent
at rest condition fluid pressure
level backfill per foot of depth
2 An allowable bearing capacity of up to 3 000 p s f is
recommended for foundations bearing on the stiff natural
subsoils The wall foundations should be embedded a minimm
of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent grade
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2375 00 Page 10
3 A passive earth resistance of 300 p s f per foot of depth
may be used for wall footings on level areas A base friction
factor of 0 4 may also be used to determine the resistance
against sliding of the wall
4 For the new basement underpinned walls in the mauka Ewa
corner where working space is limited a geosynthetic
Grainage system such as Miradrain should be installed behind
the wall and down to the cinder sand prior to the underpinning
to minimize the build up of hydrostatic pressure
5 For the new semi circular wall planned in the Entry Court
area select granular backfill material such as 3B Fine
gravel is recommended behind the wall and down to the cinder
sand to minimize excessive compactive effort required and
provide drainage into the cinder sand
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comments
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this
review is not made the geotechnical engineer can not assume
responsibility for misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during new basement
excavation underpinning and foundation construction phases of the
project This is to observe compliance with the design concepts
specifications or recommendations and to allow design changes in the
GEOLABS HAWAII

event that subsurface conditions differ from that anticipated prior to
start of construction The recommendations given in this report are
contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are
based in part upon information obtained from field data points such as
borings and test pits Variations of conditions between the field data
points may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not
become evident until construction If variations then appear evident
it will be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations given in this
report
The locations of the field data points were approximately
determined by taping from existing features shown on the Site Plan
dated August 7 1989 by CJS Group Architects Ltd Reference
elevations of the data points were interpreted from the same plan It
is our understanding that the elevation values are for reference only
and do not relate to Mean Sea Level MSL
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown
on profiles or cross sections are based upon interpolation between
field data points and may not represent actual subsurface conditions

Classification System A graphic presentation of the materials
encountered are presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 4

penetration sampler or a 2 4 inch I D split barrel sampler with a

the last 12 inches are shown on the Boring Logs at the appropriate sample
depths
