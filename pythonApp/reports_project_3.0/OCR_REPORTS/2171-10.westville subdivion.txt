INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the subject project
An initial reconnaissance of the site including the excavation of
eight 8 test pits was conducted in November 1988 The reconnaissance
indicated that more detailed subsurface information was required to
adequately assess the development feasibility Of particular concern were
the soft marshy conditions encountered in the lower portions of the site

The purpose of this exploration was to provide more in depth and
detailed subsurface information within the proposed development site
These data were used to formulate design recommendations for house
foundations site grading infrastructure and preliminary pavement design
The scope of our exploration included but was not limited to
1 review and compilation of the available soil and geologic data
pertinent to the general project area and its vicinity
2 continued field reconnaissance
3 drilling and sampling of twelve 12 additional borings to depths
between 20 0 to 38 5 feet below the existing ground surface
4 laboratory testing of selected soil samples obtained during the
reconnaissance and exploration and
5 engineering evaluation of the laboratory and field data
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located between Lihue town and Nawiliwili Harbor
in two confluent drainageways adjacent to Rice Street on the northerly
slope of Nawiliwili Valley It is proposed to subdivide the parcel into a
22 lot residential subdivision
The preliminary grading plans by M E Pacific Inc indicate cuts up
to about twenty five 25 feet in depth and fills of up to about twenty
20 feet in vertical thickness It is anticipated that most of the site
grading will involve the placement of fill to raise the grades of the
drainageways A proposed borrow site for the project is the surplus
material from the earthwork at Halfway Bridge

GENERAL SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located on the island of Kauai in the southern
portion of Lihue town The site is bounded by Rice Street to the east by
Mokoi and Kapena Streets to the north and west and by Nawiliwili Valley to
the south Ground elevations on the site range from 148 feet above Mean
Sea Level in the northern portion to approximately 19 feet above Mean Sea
level in the southern sections of the site The topography slopes down
towards the south with an average gradient of 2H 1V to 4H 1V
A ditch from an outlet of an existing plantation water tunnel runs
through the center of the property transporting water to Nawiliwili
Valley A natural spring also exists in a drainageway in the southern
portion of the site and drains towards Nawiliwili Valley The area was
cleared and grubbed prior to our exploration Vegetation has grown back
quickly and consists of small shrubs bushes and grasses Windrows of
grubbed material are stockpiled in the lower portion of the project area
Several erosional gullies are present throughout the jobsite Uncompacted
fill was observed in several locations in the upper most portions of the
site
GEOLOGY
Kauai is the oldest and northern most of the major Hawaiian Islands
The island is composed of a single basalt shield volcano built by the
extrusion of the lavas of the Waimea Canyon Volcanic Series during the
late Pliocene more than 2 3 million years ago Following the cessation
of this main shield building phase there was renewed volcanic activity
with the extrusion of the basaltic lavas of the Koloa Volcanic Series
The project site is situated on the residual soils resulting from the
in situ weathering of these lavas The southern portion of the site is
situated on the edge of Nawiliwili Valley which was formed by erosion of
the residual soils and deposition of stream transported sediments Recent

Subsurface Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by
drilling and sampling twelve 12 borings to depths ranging between 20 0
and 38 5 feet below the existing ground surface Eight 8 test pits were
previously excavated and sampled during the reconnaissance study The
approximate locations of the borings and test pits are shown on the Site
Plan Plate 2
In general the borings within the presently proposed development
site encountered stiff clayey silts and silty clays to the maximm depths
drilled Colors of the soils ranged from browns to reds with frequent
mottled areas resulting from the deep chemical weathering which has
occurred at the project site
Further development was originally considered in the lower portions
of the area However our borings disclosed that the lower areas are
underlain by soft marshy soils to depths as much as 32 feet Therefore
development is not presently planned in this area
The logs of the drilled borings are presented in Appendix A of this
report Also presented in Appendix A are the logs of the test pits
excavated during our initial reconnaissance of the project site
Groundwater levels were not encountered in the borings in the higher
northeastern portions of the site However high groundwater levels were
encountered during our exploration in Boring Nos 1 through 4 and 7 which
are in the lower marshy area
Relatively high groundwater levels 2 5 to 10 feet depth were also
encountered in Boring Nos 5 and 8 It is believed that these water
levels are a result of leakage from the water tunnel below the site

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
It is our opinion that the proposed project is feasible provided that
our recommendations are closely followed During the preliminary
development stages development was considered in the lower portions of
the site However the field exploration disclosed that these areas were
underlain by soft compressible marsh deposits which would require special
stabilization and or removal to maintain structural stability of the
fills Subsequently proposed development was limited to the upper areas
where more competent soils were encountered
Existing Water Tunnel
It is our understanding that an unlined water tunnel is situated
below the site It appears that water from the tunnel may be seeping
through the site to the existing drainageway
The structural stability of the tunnel is a critical factor in the
site development since movement or collapse of the tunnel would have an
adverse effect on structural improvements at the surface
We strongly recommend that the location and limits of the tunnel
both horizontally and vertically be determined to evaluate the possible
effect of the tunnel on the development
Site Grading
The following recommendations present general guidelines for grading
in areas of relatively competent ground
1 Areas to be graded should be thoroughly cleared of vegetation
boulders debris and other deleterious materials Topsoil should
be removed and stockpiled for later usage and to avoid
contamination of fill material

In areas to receive fill the existing ground should be scarified
to a depth of six 6 inches brought to near optimm moisture
content and recompacted to 90 of its maximum dry density per
ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 Soft yielding or pumping areas
should be over excavated and stabilized by backfilling with
select material placed in 10 to 12 inch loose lifts compacted to
90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Fill material should be placed in 10 to 12 inch loose horizontal
lifts and compacted to 90 of its maximum dry density per ASIM
Test Method D 1557 78
Off site borrow soil should be tested by the geotechnical
engineer to evaluate its suitability as fill for the project
prior to its importation to the project site
Where the existing ground is steeper than 5 horizontal to 1
vertical keying and benching are required to properly bond the
new fill to the slope The filling operations should start at
the lowest point and continue up in level compacted layers as
recommended above For construction details refer to Plate 3
Cut and fill slopes of 2 horizontal to 1 vertical 2H 1V are
recommended for the upper firm ground portion of the development
site A cut slope ratio up to vertical to 1 horizontal may
be used where houses will not be built on the slope
Water should be diverted away from the tops of slopes and slope
planting utilized to reduce erosion
Generally slope drainage benches should be provided for slopes
having a vertical height greater than 20 feet

House _ Foundations
Normal spread footing type foundations may be used for houses on
relatively level lots However on the steeper 2H 1V slopes deeper
pier type foundations will be necessary to reduce erosion and subsequent
loss of bearing support for the post footing foundation
General guidelines for house foundation design are as follows
1 Footings on level areas should be embedded a minimum of 12 inches
below the adjacent finished grade to resist erosion and surface
soil shrinkage heaving
2 Footings on sloping areas should be embedded down an additional
amount to obtain a 5 foot minimm horizontal set back distance
The set back distance is the horizontal distance measure from the
outer edge of the footing out to the slope face For a 2H 1V
slope ratio a minimm of 2 5 feet embedment at the lower side
would be required This additional embedment depth is required
to help resist downhill slope creep and to develop the
recommended bearing capacity of the soil To eliminate
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2171 10 Page 8
difficulty of backfilling the excavation we recommend that
concrete be used to construct 2 5 foot deep pier footings with
column straps buried in the concrete backfilled excavation
Refer to Figure 5 for details
3 An allowable bearing value of 3000 p s f on the stiff natural
soil is recommended for the anticipated light loads
4 The bottom of all foundation excavations should be cleaned of
loose material and bear on firm soil The excavations should be
observed by a geotechnical engineer to see that adequate bearing
soil has been obtained
5 The ground surface should be shaped to drain water away from the
foundations
6 It is recommend that a 12 inch embedment be utilized for slab
thickened edges to reduce future water infiltration under house
slab areas
It is recommend that slabs on grade be placed over a 4 inch layer
of aggregate cushion fill such as 3 Fine rock and separated
from wall foundations to allow for some relative movement between
the slabs and walls
Retaining Walls

house construction
We recommend that the walls should bear on the stiff in situ residual
soil An allowable bearing value of 3000 p s f on soil may be used

Pavement
Asphaltic concrete pavements are planned for the access road Due to
the wet site conditions we recommend that the A C pavement section
consist of the following
2 Inches Asphaltic Concrete
6 Inches Base Course
6 Inches Select Borrow
14 Inches Total Pavement Thickness
on Compacted Moist Subgrade
Both the base course and select borrow should be compacted to 95 of
its maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
CBR and density tests and or field observations should be performed
on the actual subgrade soils encountered during construction and that the
above section be revised if necessary The recommended section considers
medium traffic and assumes that adequate drainage will be provided in the
paved areas Weepholes should be provided at the base course level in the
drainage inlet structures in order to relieve the seepage of water which
may accumulate in the subgrade
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comment
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this review
is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for
misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommend that the geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during construction of the excavation
and foundation phases of the work This is to observe compliance with the
design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow design

If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during

LIMITATIONS
The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as borings
probes test pits and or observation wells Variations of conditions
between the field data points may occur and the nature and extent of
these variations may not become evident until construction If variations
then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations given in this report
The location of the field data points were approximately determined
by taping and or estimation from survey points placed in the field by the
surveyors or from reference points shown on Site Plans Elevations of the
data points were approximately determined by hand levelling and estimation
from the same points The physical location and elevations of the field
data points should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by
the methods used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition The strata line shown on
profiles or cross sections are based upon interpolation between field data
points and may not represent actual subsurface conditions

Placement and Compaction of Fill
A
The fill shall be placed in horizontal layers which when
compacted will not exceed ten inches 10 in thickness Each
layer shall be spread uniformly and blade mixed to attain
uniformity of material and water content within each layer
Additional fill material shall not be placed on any fill layer
which has not been properly compacted Subsequent removal of
any unacceptable layer shall be done at the Contractor s
expense
Rock fragments or boulders less than twelve inches 12 in
diameter may be utilized provided they are not concentrated in
any one area and that there is sufficient fine grained material
to fill the voids between the rocks
Rocks greater than eight inches 8 in diameter shall be
removed from the fill unless specifically approved for placement
in the fill by the Geotechnical Engineer
All fill shall be compacted to a minimm of 90 percent of the
maximum dry density established by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
unless otherwise specified in the Geotechnical Engineer s
Report
The compaction equipment may be sheepsfoot vibratory or
pneumatic tire rollers suited to the soil type and capable of
producing the specified results in an expedient and efficient
manner The equipment shall be in good working condition fully
ballasted and self cleaning
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1971 10 Plate C 8
Appendix C Continued
The compaction shall be accomplished in a systematic and uniform
manner over the entire fill area to produce a uniformly
compacted fill
The Contractor shall be required to obtain the above specified
levels of compaction out to the finish slope of fill slopes
This may be achieved by either overbuilding the slope and
cutting back to the compacted core or by direct compaction of
the slope face with suitable equipment
