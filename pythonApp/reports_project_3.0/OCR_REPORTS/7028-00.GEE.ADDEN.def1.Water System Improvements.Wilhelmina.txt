HONOLULU, OAHU, HAWAII 
Dear Mr. Stone: 
Geolabs, Inc. submits this addendum to our report entitled “Geotechnical 
Engineering Exploration, Wilhelmina Rise Water System Improvements, Part V, 
Honolulu, Oahu, Hawaii”, dated December 1, 2014.  This addendum corrects 
information that was based on results provided by our subconsultant, TestAmerica.  
During an internal quality control check, TestAmerica noted that the dilution 
factor had been improperly applied to the resistivity tests for this project resulting in 
erroneous resistivity data. Our report, as issued, reported resistivity values ranging from 
about 5,500 to 59,000 ohm-cm, corresponding to corrosion ratings of 4 to 5, respectively.  
Based on TestAmerica’s corrected laboratory report, it appears that the actual 
resistivity values for soils at the project site range from 1,100 to 6,800 ohm-cm which 
correspond to corrosion ratings of 2 to  4; and we believe that an average corrosion 
rating of 2 may be used for the proposed project. Design of metallic substructures, such 
as water line pipes or other metallic piping, should consider the effects of the corrosive 
environment on the substructure.

Method(s) SM 2510B: This report is being revised to correct results for Resistivity by Standard Method 2510B.
Through a series of internal checks, the laboratory recently discovered an error affecting the calculation of Resistivity for diluted samples.  
Resistivity is calculated as the inverse of Conductivity, which is measured by the laboratory.  It was determined that the dilution factor was 
being applied to the Resistivity result (post-calculation), whereas the dilution factor should have been applied to the Conductivity result 
(pre-calculation).  As a consequence, diluted sample results for Resistivity were lower than originally reported.  The error has been 
resolved and affected results have been re-calculated.  The following samples were affected: B-01   RS3   S-6.5 (440-93462-1), B-07   RS1   
1-2.5 (440-93462-2), B-08   SPT2   2.5-4 (440-93462-3)
