This report presents the results of our soils investigation
performed for the proposed Hazardous Flammable Storehouse at Pearl
Harbor Naval Shipyard Oahu Hawaii The general location of the
project site and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map
Plate 1
The purpose of our study was to investigate the subsurface
conditions by drilling and sampling borings to provide recommendations
for the design of the foundation of the project Our work was performed
in general accordance with the scope of services outlined in our
proposal dated
W O 1423 00 Page 2
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed building will be a one story warehouse type structure
for the storage of hazardous and flammable materials The structure
will be about 86 by 140 feet in plan dimension with a concrete
slab on grade
The concrete floor slabs will serve the operation of
capacity forklift trucks
The maximum column loads are anticipated to be on the order of 42
kips per column and the wall loads up to 14 kips per linear foot for the
blast wall
The loading and unloading area will have 6 inch high curbs and
pavements capable to serve fully loaded tractor trailer
and or forklift trucks The forklift trucks could result in
a total load of with 80 to 90 percent concentrated on one
axle It is our understanding that the loading and unloading area will
also be paved with Portland cement concrete

SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is located east of the intersection of Sixth
Street and South Street inside the Naval Shipyard in Pearl Harbor At
present the site is vacant but covered with stockpiles of soil The
area is flat with ground elevations generally from 110 to 112 feet above
U S Navy Datum feet
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
The site is located in the Hickam Makalapa area which consists of
Makalapa Salt Lake post erosional volcanics tuff overlain by detritus
coral debris deposited when the sea level rose to the Waimanalo High
25 Stand of the sea Later reef limestone deposits coral and
man made fills covered the entire site
Subsurface Exploration
To explore the subsurface conditions five borings were drilled
and sampled to depths ranging from 11 5 to 33 feet below the existing
ground surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2 Descriptions of our field exploration and laboratory testing
are presented in the appendices along with the boring logs and
laboratory test results

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that the subsoils are suitable for
foundation support of the proposed relatively light structure
Spread or continuous footing foundation bearing on the upper fill
or coral layer may be used for the proposed building

Site Preparation
All vegetation debris and other deleterious materials should be
cleared and grubbed and removed from the site Any existing structures
such as concrete walls foundation sump pits etc should be removed
to at least 12 inches below the finished grade
Any soft or weak spots disclosed during the course of site
preparation should be repaired by excavating the weak soil and then
backfilling with compacted granular material
After clearing and grubbing the surface of the soil to receive
fill should be scarified to a depth of at least 6 inches moisture
conditioned and then recompacted to at least 95 of the ASIM D 1557 78
maximum density
In the new loading and unloading area where heavy truck trailer
and forklift truck operations are planned the top portion of the
existing coral sand and gravel material within 2 feet below the finished
grade should be compacted to 95 relative compaction If a portion of
this area is situated in the cut area we believe that such a
densification can be achieved by rolling the in place material from the
stripped surface wth a heavy vibratory roller
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1423 00 Page 6
All imported materials for fill under the pavement or warehouse
areas should be granular materials with maximum particle size not
exceeding 3 inches and have a minimum laboratory CBR value of 30 when
compacted to 95 of the ASTM D 1557 78 maximum density
Any material to be imported should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
All ill and backfill material should be placed in horizontal
lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a dry
density of at least 95 of the ASTM D 1557 78 maximum density
Building Foundation
Based on our findings we believe that the dense fill soil or
coral at shallow depths will be suitable for spread footing foundation
support
The footings bottom should be embeded at least 18 inches below the
lowest adjacent grade The disturbed bottom of the excavations should
be recompacted to at least 95 relative compaction based on ASTM
D 1557 78 designation
An allowable soil bearing of up to 3500 p s f may be used with a
one third increase when considering temporary wind blasting or seismic
loads

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the clearing and grubbing engineered fill
placement and compaction foundation excavation backfilling subgrade
preparation and pavement construction be observed by the soils engineer
during construction This is to see that adequate compaction has been
obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the
findings of our investigation

INVESTIGATION LIMITATIONS
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations
made in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation
engineering principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all
other warranties either expressed or implied
The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are
based on our site reconnaissance soil information from boring and
laboratory test data obtained from this soil investigation
Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encountered and cannot
be fully determined by reconnaissance and borings These unforeseen
conditions such as pockets of organic soils trash fill or expansive
soils may occur in localized areas Therefore additional expenditures
may be needed during construction to attain a properly constructed
project Some contingency fund is thus recommended to accommodate these
possible costs
This report has been prepared in order to assist the engineers in
the preparation of the site grading plans and foundation design of this
project

five S borings to depths of 15 5 to 30 feet below the existing ground

soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A graphic presentation of the materials

standard split spoon sampler or a 2 4 inch I D split barrel sampler with
a 140 pound hammer free falling 30 inches The blow counts to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the boring logs at the appropriate
sample depths
