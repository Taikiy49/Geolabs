INSTRUMENTATION MONITORING
As part of landslide study we drilled four borings each to a depth of
approximately 100 feet in November 2001 and September 2003 Four borings were
converted to inclinometer monitoring stations designated as Inclinometer Nos 1
through 4 to measure the lateral deflection of the soil at various depths Inclinometer
locations I 1 through I 4 are shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
As part of landslide remediation design we drilled an additional nine borings
each to a depth of approximately 100 feet in 2008 Five of the borings were converted to
inclinometer monitoring stations designated as Inclinometer Nos 5 through 9
Inclinometer locations I 5 through I 9 are also shown on the Site Plan Plate 1

PAVEMENT DEFORMATION MAPPING
Ground surface observations were recorded in our previous reports
Approximately 1 to 2 inch wide pavement cracks on the surface of the roadway were
observed between 1189 and 1199 Mona Street from May 2002 to September 2002
No significant continuous cracking development was recorded from September 2002 to
January 2003
Pavement resurfacing was performed in localized areas of Mona Street during
January 2003 to August 2003 A majority of the cracks previously recorded was patched
with asphaltic concrete No significant continuous cracking development was recorded
from January 2004 to August 2004
Our pavement deformation mapping resumed in 2005 During this period a
majority of the cracks recorded prior to the pavement resurfacing was again observed
on the resurfaced pavement with some extended new cracks as shown on the Crack
Location Plan Plate 13 For comparison a series of photographs taken during the
monitoring period is presented in Appendix Plate Nos A 1 through A 13
Cracks about 24 inch long 12 inch deep and 4 to 5 inch wide discovered in
Year 2007 further extended to 48 inch long and more than 36 inch deep near the
southern corner of 1198 Mona Street as shown on the Plate 13 Cracks about 5 foot
long 18 inch deep and 1 to 2 inch wide developed in 2008 in the front of 1198 Mona
Street as also shown on the Plate 13 remained unchanged in 2009 and 2010 However
asphalt concrete patching layer began peeling off due to repeating traffic loading and
surface run off water
The crack observations indicate that the lateral earth movement and or creep at the
project site may still be active To reduce the potential for significant surface water
infiltration into the subsurface the cracks offset and gaps should be patched with
asphaltic concrete

Relatively small ground movement at shallow depth was measured by Inclinometer
No 9 within the 30 month monitoring period
Our piezometer data indicates that subsurface water seepage occurred at the
project site Based on our pavement deformation observation we believe that lateral
near surface earth movement and or creep at the site is still active
Based on the interpretation from Inclinometer Nos 1 through 3 it appears that
the sliding zone may be relatively shallow approximately 11 to 15 feet below the ground
surface Inclinometer Nos 4 and 5 are located between Inclinometer Nos 2 and 3 It
appears that the slide zone may be located slightly deeper than Inclinometer Nos 1
through 3 at a depth of approximately 20 feet below the ground surface
We believe that the ground movement slip circle may not extend to the existing
slope toe as evident by no significant ground movement measured by Inclinometer
Nos 7 and 8 This may be partially due to the buttress installed at the slope toe in
previous landslide remediation project in the 1970 s
Inclinometer No 9 indicated relatively small ground movement at shallow depths
We believe that this small ground movement may be related to the creep from the
surface fill materials and medium stiff clayey soils encountered in the boring The
leaning existing electrical utility pole adjacent to Inclinometer No 9 may be caused by
the creeping ground movement
Based on our observation of pavement deformation mapping it appears that the
sliding zone in the front of 1198 Mona Street is also active as evident by deepened and
lengthened pavement cracks To better delineate sliding boundary we recommend
installing an additional inclinometer near the southern corner of 1198 Mona Street as
part of preconstruction during and post construction monitoring
We will continue monitoring the lateral ground movement through the installed
instrumentations and mapping the pavement conditions
