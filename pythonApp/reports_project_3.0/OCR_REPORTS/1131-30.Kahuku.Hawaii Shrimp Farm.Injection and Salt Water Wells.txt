This report presents the results of our additional permeability
testing at the revised injection and salt water supply well locations
for the proposed shrimp farm
The scope of this investigation included the drilling
development and pump testing of two wells one for salt water supply
and one for injection The locations of the test wells are shown on
the Site Plan Plate 2
The results of our field exploration and tests which were used
in the analyses that formulated our conclusions and recommendations
are presented in the appendices of this report

WELL DRILLING AND TESTING
The two test wells were drilled utilizing truck mounted rotary
drilling equipment Four inch diameter core samples were taken in the
coral algal reef deposits to obtain samples for visual examination
At each well site an additional 3 holes were drilled to monitor
the changes in the groundwater conditions due to the pump testing
After the drilling of the test wells they were developed by
air lifting using a compressor As the water being developed cleared
the supply of compressed air was closed off and then opened to create
a surging effect which produced additional flow of sand This process
was continued for approximately 1 5 hours per well at which time
only minimal amounts of sand were developed with each surge
Following the development the wells were tested using a 3 inch
centrifugal pump to eject or inject water as applicable The pump
test data is shown on Plates B 1 through B 4
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The test wells which were drilled at the project site encountered
coral algal reef formation In general coral algal materials are
quite permeable and can readily transmit clear water for both supply
and injection wells

Supply Well Pemeability
The pump test performed at the supply well site indicated very
good permeability in the coral algal materials The field data showed
a drawdown of 0 3 foot for a discharge rate of 184 G P M
This data leads to a calculated coefficient of permeability k
BJ
of 1 45 feet per minute
It should be noted that this calculated value was obtained by
pumping from an open well with no casing or screen therefore entry
head losses are minimal
Injection Well Permeability
The pump test performed for the injection well area also
indicated good permeability The field data for the gravity injection
test showed a head build up of 1 1 feet for an injection rate of 184
G P M This data leads to a calculated coefficient of permeability
k of 0 44 feet per minute
It should be noted that this value was obtained from testing an
open well using clear water and no air entrainment

W O 1131 30 Page 5
Based on the punp test data the anticipated discharge for
various well sizes depths and drawdown are summarized on Plate B 2
The calculations were based on uniform soil conditions from the water
level to 100 feet deep
We recommend that the supply wells should be constructed with
screens to protect against formation caving and to minimize sand
pumping It is preferable that removable type screens be used to
allow for cleaning of possible incrustation
Injection Well Design
Based on the field test data our calculation indicated that a
disposal well with a diameter of 3 feet and a depth of 100 feet could
accommodate a gravity injection of about 2 M G D with a 3 foot head
build up However it should be stressed that this assumption is
based on injection of clear water without air entrainment The
calculated relationship between discharge and head build up for
different size and depth of wells is presented on Plate B 4
Typically water injected into disposal wells contains suspended
solids micro organisms and other matter which may reduce the
potential permeability Here in Hawaii there have been numerous
failures of disposal wells injecting into coral algal formations
These failures have been attributed to numerous causes e g lack of
maintenance accumulation of solids and formation caving
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1131 30 Page 6
In view of this past history of failures we recommend that the
injection system be overdesigned to allow for reduction in capacity or
well failure In addition provisions should be made in the facility
design for a maintenance program to extend the useful life of the
wells
The injection wells could be constructed with a removable screen
or perforated casing to protect against formation caving and to
prevent migration of accumulated solids into the injection formation
The screens could be removed at regular intervals to be cleaned and
emptied of accumulated solids Upon completion of this maintenance
procedure the screens should be replaced and the wells redeveloped
It may be desirable to include at least one stand by well that
can be used in the event of reduced injection capacity peak flows
during maintenance or construction of other wells

APPENDIX B
Injection well tests were performed in Boring IW 2
to evaluate the percolation quality of the subsoils for
use in injection well disposal of treated effluent
Pump out or drawdown tests were performed in
Boring SW 2 to evaluate the permeability of the subsoils
for use in the water supply source well design
Six inch diameter exploratory borings were drilled
to a depth of 50 feet for both test wells Following the
construction of the test wells preliminary testing was
performed on the wells The wells were first developed
by airlifting with an air compressor After development
pump tests were performed using a 3 inch centrifugal pump
delivering water from a nearby supply well The test data
are presented on Plates B 1 and B 4
