In accordance with our proposal dated we have
performed a soil and foundation investigation at the above referenced
site in order to evaluate the Suitability of the foundation design of
the proposed structure The study included a field reconnaissance
drilling of 3 shallow borings laboratory testing of selected soil
samples and a review of the previous boring 3 data drilled at the
site
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
It is proposed to build a one story chapel building approximately
106 by 106 feet in plan dimension Light column loads of up to 10
kips are anticipated

An asphaltic concrete pavement parking lot is planned on the
northwestern portion of the site The site investigation of the
parking lot was not included in our scope of work
SITE CONDITIONS
The site is situated on the western side of the intersection of
Maunakea Street and Vineyard Boulevard It is generally level and has
recently been cleared
During the time of our recent exploration on it
was noted that the concrete slab for the proposed chapel building had
already been placed
The ground elevation at the site is generally about 13 feet
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Three borings B 1 B 2 and B 3 were drilled at the site in 1981
for a previously planned development For the construction of the
proposed chapel 3 additional borings B 4 B 5 and B 6 were drilled
recently in the areas not covered by the previous boring exploration
In general the borings disclosed that the site is situated over
an old stream bed

The general soil conditions encountered were
1 Fill Encountered from the surface to 5 to 8 feet deep
Soil consists of very stiff gray brown clayey silt with sand
and gravel
2 Soft Alluvium or Marsh Deposit soft and compressive organic
clayey silt This layer varies in thickness fron about 20
feet on the southwestern makai portion of the site to less
than 5 feet on the eastern mauka portion
3 Stream Deposits Cobbles and boulders were encountered below
the soft deposits to the depths of the borings
Groundwater level was encountered in the borings at 10 to 14 foot
depth

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that the site is underlain by a
soft alluvium layer of variable thickness However due to the light
structural loads small settlements are anticipated In general the
site appears suitable for the proposed construction
Prior to this investigation the concrete slab for the structure
was already poured Since we have not been requested to observe the
slab construction nor the placement of the concrete we cannot comment
on the adequacy of the existing slab
Spread or continuous footing foundations bearing on the stiff
upper fill layer are recommended for the proposed building The slab
thickened edge footings embedded 12 inches below the outside finished
grade as shown on the project structural plans Sheet S 2 are
adequate for the structure
An allowable bearing value of up to 2000 p s f may be used on the
upper fill The foundation notes on Sheet S 1 indicate that a lower
bearing value of 1250 p s f was used
The bottom of all footings should be free of loose soils prior to
concrete placement

If a footing is located next to a utility trench the footing
should be extended to the bottom of the trench or the trench backfill
must be backfilled with well compacted granular structural fill
material to prevent footing settlement due to settlement of the trench
backfill
Slab On Grade
me Same om te Ne Ome te
Generally the in situ soils if properly prepared have a low
swelling potential therefore Slab on grade construction may be
utilized
For the light floor loads anticipated a 4 inch concrete slab over
4 inches of well graded aggregate base course is recommended A
plastic vapor barrier over the base course layer may be used if a
moisture barrier is deemed necessary to protect floor covering
These recommendations are indicated on the structural drawings
Sett lements
For the light structural loads indicated minimal settlements
would be anticipated due to the weight of the structure However the
southern and western portions of the slab area have been raised up
about 18 to 24 inches Ground settlements on the order of about 1
inch may occur due to the weight of this new fill
Additional 111 should be avoided to minimize future settlements

Site Grading
Site filling should be minimized to prevent additional load on the
underlying soft soils thereby minimizing ground settlements
Any ill and backfill material required should be non expansive
well graded granular material free of organics and other deleterious
substances and should be generally less than 6 inches in size
Fills and backfills should be placed in horizontal lifts not
exceeding 6 inches in compacted thickness and compacted to 95 of its
maximum dry density as established by ASTM D 1557 78
Site grading should provide drainage of surface runoff away from
all foundations
Construction Monitoring
It is normally recommended that the filling footing excavation
and backfilling operations be observed by the soils engineer during
construction This is to see that adequate compaction has been
obtained and the subsurface conditions are consistent with the
findings of our investigation However the slab and foundations were
constructed prior to our investigation Efforts should be made to
obtain information from the field inspector engineer or contractor
concerning the construction procedure adopted

INVESTIGATION LIMITATIONS
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommen
dations made in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation
engineering principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all
other warranties either expressed or implied
The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are
based on our site reconnaissance soil information from boring and
laboratory test data obtained from this soil investigation
Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encountered and cannot
be fully determined by reconnaissance and soil borings These
unforeseen conditions such as pockets of organic soils trash fill
or expansive soils may occur in localized areas Therefore
additional expenditures may be needed during construction to attain a
properly constructed project Some contingency fund is thus
recommended to accommodate these possible costs

the owner in the evaluation of the suitability of the foundation
design of this project
It should be noted that since the building slab and foundation had
been constructed without our supervision therefore we cannot assume
responsibility for the adequacy of the completed construction work
done prior to our investigation
