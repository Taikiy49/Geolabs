PURPOSE AND SCOPE OF SERVICES
The purpose of our investigation was to explore the subsurface
conditions near the makai wall foundations of the referenced building to
provide soil parameters for the proposed underpinning work design
It is our understanding that the adjacent lot owner plans to
construct a retaining wall along the property line between the new
building and the subject warehouse The construction of the retaining
wall will necessitate a vertical cut about 5 to 7 feet high at the
property line Since the foundations of the existing warehouse are
adjacent to the property line and are also above the bottom of the
proposed cut these footing foundations could be adversely affected by
the wall construction Therefore the purpose of the underpinning would
be to transfer the building load to a suitable layer below the
excavation
The scope of investigation included drilling and sampling three 3
to 16 5 feet below the existing ground surface along the
inside face of the affected wall Also included were laboratory testing
of the samples obtained engineering evaluation of the field and

SITE DESCRIPTION
The existing building is situated on the western side of Iwaena
Street in Central Park Halawa Industrial Subdivision and is presently
used as an office and warehouse storage
The building approximately 101 by 106 feet in plan occupies the
rear portion of the site It is constructed of a wooden roof supported
by tilt up concrete panel walls pipe columns and on grade concrete
slabs Fronting the warehouse are a parking lot paved with asphaltic
concrete and a sloping driveway
At the time of our investigation the adjacent lot on the makai
side of the building had been cleared and leveled and construction of a
new building was nearly completed Parts of the toe of the existing
small slope between the two lots was cut and it is anticipated to be
removed completely for construction of a new retaining wall at the
property line

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions were explored by drilling and sampling 3
borings to depths of 11 0 to 16 5 feet below the existing ground surface
at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The borings disclosed that the floor consists of a 4 inch concrete
slab over a 4 inch layer of brown clayey silt with gravel The pavement
was underlain by hard dark brown to reddish brown clayey silt and
further by stiff to hard dark brown clayey silt with gravel which consist
of decomposed rock fragments and some coral aggregate Boulders were
encountered intermittently at depths between 3 5 and 9 0 feet below the
existing finished floor grade in two of the borings
No groundwater was encountered during our investigation
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Several foundation underpinning alternatives such as grouting
construction of support columns under the footing etc were evaluated
however due to the proximity of the footings to the property line and
risks involved in excavating under the footing it is our opinion that a
system of mini piles installed from the top of the footing appears
feasible
GEOLABS HAWAIi
W O 1565 10 Page 5
The mini piles could consist of either steel pipes driven into the
ground or concrete piles cast in pre drilled holes Drilled mini piles
would be preferable since boulders may be encountered below the footings
and limit penetration of driven piles However due to the limited
working space along the outside face of the wall driven piles appear
more practical for the underpinning of the outer edge of the footing
Therefore a combination of driven and drilled mini piles may be used
Small Diameter Friction Piles
Based on the soil conditions we believe that small diameter
friction piles may be used to underpin the foundations by transferring
the load away from the possible failure zone of soil behind the new
retaining wall in the adjacent lot along the makai side of the existing
building
The piles would develop their capacity primarily from frictional
resistance or adhesion between the piles and the stiff to hard clayey
silt with gravel soil below the footings
Four inch diameter pipe piles suitably reinforced grouted with
conerete and driven to a total depth of at least 10 feet below the botton
of the proposed excavation may develop an ultimate capacity of 12 kips
each
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1565 10 Page 6
Six inch diameter reinforced and grouted concrete piles installed
in drilled holes casted to a total depth of at least 10 feet below the
bottom of the proposed excavation may develop an ultimate capacity of 20
kips each
Therefore for the estimated 60 kip column load three driven piles
on the outside of the footing and 2 drilled piles on the inside of the
footing could provide a total load of 76 kips F S 1 2 during the
eritical excavation period The total pile capacity would increase
substantially after construction of the new retaining wall
A suitable structural detail for transferring the footing load to
the mini piles will be required
The pile driving or drilling and casting should be monitored by a
geotechnical engineer to see that they are driven or cast into competent
bearing formation
Pre Drilling
Due to the embedment of boulders in the clayey silt soil it is
anticipated that pre drilling may be required for driving some of the
4 inch diameter pipe piles

It is our opinion that whenever the pipe piles can not be driven
down to the anticipated minimum total depth of 10 feet below the
excavation they should be extracted and the hole drilled with suitable
equipment to the required depth After pre drilling the pipe piles
should be driven again with suitable steel reinforcement installed and
grouted with suitable concrete
Design Review
Plans and specifications for piles shold be forwarded to the soils
engineer for review and written comments prior to construction to see
that the intent of our recommendations is included
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the underpinning operations be observed by
the soils engineer during construction to see that adequate bearing has
been odtained by the piles and that the subsurface conditions are
consistent with the findings of our subsurface investigation
If conditions encountered during construction are different from
those presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made
