PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located at the existing Kapaa Quarry off Kapaa Quarry Road in
the Kailua area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project involved the relocation and
upgrade of the existing asphalt plant to an adjacent aggregate stockpile area located on
the upslope side of the existing plant
The relocated asphalt plant consists of new storage bins drum mix AC tanks bag
house recycle bin and cold feed bin In addition a 10 foot wide AC paved access road
subjected to heavy truck traffic was constructed to serve the new facility The layout of the
new asphalt plant construction is shown on the Site Plan Plate 1

SITE PREPARATION
The earthwork operations and foundation excavations were performed by Highway
Construction Co Ltd After the clearing and grubbing operations the areas within the
limits of the new structures were over excavated where necessary to about the design
subgrade elevations Additional swell tests were performed on the exposed subgrade
soils to confirm and or modify the thickness of the non expansive select granular capping
layer underneath the concrete slabs on grade and footing foundations Based on the ring
swell test results it is our opinion that the light grayish brown clayey silts may be
considered as slightly to moderately expansive and the reddish brown silty clays may be
considered as moderately to highly expansive Therefore we recommended that the
non expansive select granular capping layer remain at 12 inches for concrete slabs and
footing foundations underlain by the light grayish brown clayey silt soils Where the
concrete slabs and footing foundations are underlain by the reddish brown silty clays we
recommended that the capping layer be increased from 12 inches to 18 inches
Prior to the placement of the capping fill material the subgrades were scarified to a
depth of about 12 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum
moisture and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent of the maximum dry density
determined in accordance with American Society of Testing and Material ASTM Test
Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor In areas where pumping of the subgrades were
observed the soft and wet clayey soils were further removed an additional 12 inches and
a layer of geotextile fabric was placed at the bottom of the over excavation
Following the subgrade preparation the on site asphalt concrete stockpile was
used as a source of fill material for the non expansive select granular capping layer
During placement of the capping fill the fill materials were generally placed in near level
lifts brought to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of
95 percent of the maximum dry density determined in accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor
During the site grading operations samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor
and the test results are summarized on Plate 2 of this report
Periodic field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations to
confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures and soil conditions
were noted A total of 82 field density tests including retests of failed areas were
performed using the nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM Test Designations
D 2922 and D 3017 The results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on
Plates 3 1 through 3 4 of this report

The tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills
and backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and
interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed to
be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the earthwork operations during the site grading
foundation excavation and pavement and utility construction for the above project
observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report were performed in
general accordance with the intent of the project plans and specifications and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations presented in the referenced report dated
and subsequent recommendations
LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork may
have been performed without our observation However the level of earthwork
observation and testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some of
the geotechnical risks to the project It should be recognized that there are variations in
the accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the
earthwork construction and materials
