Dear Mr Sakoda
As requested we have performed a soil engineering investi
gation at the above referenced site to evaluate the subsurface
conditions in order to provide recommendations for the design
of the foundations for the proposed houses
The investigation which consisted of four borings seven hand
auger probings visual observations and laboratory soil tests
was performed in general accordance with our proposal of August
16 1982
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests
which were used in the engineering analysis to formulate our
conclusions and recommendations are presented in the appendices
of this report
W Page 2
GENERAL SITE CONDITION
The project site is part of the Kaluanui Agricultural Subdivi
Sion situated on the western side of Kamehameha Highway about
1100 feet south of the intersection of Kaluanui Stream and
Kamehameha Highway
A partially completed access road extends from Kamehameha High
way to the proposed residence lots
The lower northeastern portion of the site which is generally
wet and soft and covered with tall grass gently slopes upward
toward the west The ground elevation in this area is gener
ally less than 15 feet
The upper southwestern portion of the site is situated on a
terrace about 5 to 10 feet higher than the northeastern
portion This area is also gently sloping and covered with
tall grass The ground surface in this area is generally firm
and the elevations vary from about 15 to 30 feet
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
The proposed construction consists of 25 single family resi
dences around the access road Minor grading is presently
planned

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
During our field exploration two different soil conditions
were encountered
Lower Area
The lower area is underlain by soft gray brown organic clays
and silts from 3 to more than 10 feet deep Loose fine sand
deposits were encountered below the organic soils in Probing
No l
Water level was encountered at depths of 0 to 4 5 feet below
the existing ground
Upper Area
The upper area is underlain by stiff brown clayey silt to
the depths of the borings and probings Water level was
encountered at 5 to 11 feet below the existing ground
The approximate boundary between the upper and lower areas is
shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Since the lower area is underlain by soft wet soils with high
water table special site preparation is thus recommended for
this area prior to house construction
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 761 40 Page 4
Stiff alluvial soils suitable for house construction were
encountered in the upper portion of the site
Lower Area
The soft organic soils in this area can only provide limited
bearing capacity Additionally high water table was encoun
tered It would be desirable to raise the sites about 3 feet
so the house foundations and driveways can be placed on con
pacted fill and at a higher elevation above the water table
Due to the existing soft soils ground settlements would occur
due to the weight of the new fill For 3 feet of fill settle
ments on the order of 6 to 8 inches are anticipated In addi
tion long term settlements should be anticipated In order to
accelerate the settlements and reduce long term settlements a
temporary 5 foot high surcharge fill is recommended over the
house sites Settlement gauges should be installed prior to
filling to monitor the settlement rates and determine when
house construction can begin
It is anticipated that a surcharge period of at least 6 months
would be required If a shorter surcharge period is desired
sand drains through the soft organic soils may be used We
would be glad to consult with you on such a program if desired

Site Grading
Prior to filling the site should be cleared of vegeta
tion surface debris and other deleterious material
The fills should be placed in 10 inch maximum loose
lifts and compacted to obtain a minimum of 90 of its
maximum dry density established by ASTM D 1557 70 test
method
Generally the on site material from the higher portions
of the site can be used for the fill The fill material
should be tested by the soil engineer prior to being
used in the fill
House Foundations
Some amount of long term secondary settlement are anti
cipated due to the weight of the new fill and founda
tions Therefore post and beam foundations are
recommended for this area since they are capable of
tolerating some long term settlements
The footings should be embedded a minimum of 12 inches
below the finished grade

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by
drilling 5 to 34 2 feet and 7 probings from 1 5
to 10 0 feet below the existing ground surface at the approxi
mate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 1 Portable hand
auger drilling equipment was utilized for the probings in the
inaccessible areas Truck mounted equipment was used to drill
the borings
The soils rock encountered in the borings and probings
were classified by visual and textural examination in the field
by our engineer who continously monitored the drilling opera
tions The classifications were further reviewed by visual
inspection and testing in the laboratory The soils were
classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil Classi
fication System A graphic presentation of the materials
encountered is presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1
through A 11
In the hand auger probings disturbed samples were
obtained from the auger cuttings
Soil samples were obtained from the boring by driving
either a standard penetration sampler or a 2 4 inch I D split
barrel sampler with a 140 pound hammer free falling at a height
of 30 inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the last 12
inches are shown on the logs of borings at the appropriate
sample depths

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content and Atterberg Limit determinations
were performed on selected samples to aid in the classification
and evaluation of soil properties The results of these tests
are presented on the Probing and Boring Logs Plates A 1
through A 11 at the appropriate sample depths
One inch ring swell tests were performed on selected
remolded samples to evaluate the swelling potential of the
soils The test results are presented on Plate B 1l
One direct shear test was performed on an undisturbed
sample to evaluate the shear strength parameters of the
alluvial soil The test result is plotted on Plate B 2
One consolidation test was performed on an undisturbed
sample of the alluvial soil to evaluate the settlement charac
teristics of the soils The consolidation curve is plotted on
Plate B 3
