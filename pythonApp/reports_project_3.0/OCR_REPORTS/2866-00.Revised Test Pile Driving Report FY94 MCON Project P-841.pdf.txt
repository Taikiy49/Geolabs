Revised Test Pile Driving Report
FY94 MCON Project P 841
Inactive Ship Pier

In accordance with your facsimile request of this morning we have observed the
test pile driving operation for the above referenced project from June 24th through 30th
A diesel hammer was used to drive the 24 inch octagonal
precast and prestressed concrete piles Our field observation indicated that the pile
hammer is capable of delivering a driving energy of approximately 70 000 to 80 000
foot pounds
14 B were driven to depths of about 82 4 to 116 8 feet below the existing water surface
at the site
Pile Nos 7 B and 12 B were selected for static load tests and were load tested
to two times the design capacity of 130 tons per pile by Healy Tibbitts Builders Inc
Based on our test pile driving observation and the static load test results
provided to us it is our opinion that the test piles have been driven to sufficient depths
and or resistance to provide the design vertical compressive load capacity of 130 tons
per pile Based on a cut off elevation of 101 53 feet Navy Datum and a strand length
of 3 83 feet above the cut off elevation we recommend that the following production
pile cast lengths be used for the subject project
