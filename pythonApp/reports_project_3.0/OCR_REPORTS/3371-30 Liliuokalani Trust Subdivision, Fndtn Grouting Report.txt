Dear Mr Saiki
In accordance with our proposal dated May 13th we have completed the
observation of the foundation probing and grouting for the subject project The purpose
of our work was to provide field observation and the necessary geotechnical engineering
consultation services for the probing and grouting operations
The project involves the construction of a one story retail store with a provision for
a second story in the future The project site is located to the east of the existing K Mart
store and north of Kamakaeha Avenue within the Liliuokalani Trust Subdivision in Kailua
Kona on the island of Hawaii The intent of the foundation probing and grouting is to
attempt to locate and fill subsurface cavities beneath the foundation of the proposed
buildings Isemoto Contracting Co Ltd conducted the foundation probing and grouting
operations between May 16th and 21st at the proposed building location and on
June 19th at the loading dock area

Foundation probe holes were drilled at isolated spread footing locations and at
continuous footing locations spaced at approximately 10 foot intervals A total of
154 probe holes 3 inch diameter were drilled using an air track drill Probe holes were
drilled to a depth of about 10 or 14 feet below the ground surface for a total of 1 673 lineal
feet The probe hole depth generally corresponds to the minimum depth of 10 feet below
the bottom of the foundation subgrade level Approximate locations of the foundation
probe holes are shown on the attached Foundation Plan Plate 1
Materials encountered during the probe drilling consisted typically of gray basalt or
gray basalt with cinder gravel Based on our observation of the probing operations
cavities and or voids were encountered throughout the site Cavities ranged from about
0 5 to 3 5 feet in vertical height A summary of our foundation probe drilling and grouting
observations is provided on Plates 2 1 through 2 3
After the probing operations the probe holes were fitted with 2 inch PVC pipes to
maintain the integrity of the open hole During grouting operations each probe hole was
grouted from the bottom of the probe hole upwards by low to moderate pressure injection
of grout If the grout take was noted to exceed about 0 5 cubic yards in one probe hole
the grout injection was suspended The purpose for grout interruption and reintroduction
was to allow the grout to set slightly prior to successive injection in an attempt to control
excess migration of the grout off site or away from the area of concern Several injection
passes were needed to grout those probe holes where large cavities were encountered
The grout used to fill the probe holes consisted of 2500 PSI Probe Grout which has a
minimum unconfined compressive strength of 2500 p s i with a 6 to 8 inch slump range
The grout was supplied by West Hawaii Concrete Based on injection times needed to fill
the probe holes and cavities encountered we estimate approximately 46 cubic yards of
grout was used to fill the 154 probe holes Our observations of the grouting operations
and the estimated grout volume are summarized on Plates 2 1 through 2 3
In summary the foundation probing and grouting operations have been conducted
in general conformance with the intent of the specifications and our geotechnical
engineering report Based on our observations the cavities encountered directly beneath
the foundation was grouted for support of the foundation of the building proposed
