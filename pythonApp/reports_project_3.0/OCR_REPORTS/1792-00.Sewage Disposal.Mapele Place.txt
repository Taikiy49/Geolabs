DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field percolation test results it appears that the
percolation rates of the soil at the site are low This is further
confirmed by visual examination of the soils encountered in the borings
The capacity of the leaching field would be governed by the clayey
alluvial soils underlying the sandy gravel fill Based on the
percolation test data obtained from Boring No 1 a drainage leaching
field covering an area of about 1 000 square feet may be required for a
single three bedroom dwelling

It should be pointed out however that the loss of infiltrative
capacity and soil clogging can occur in the future by combined physical
chemical and biological factors The latter is the most important factor
since an organic mat of several millimeters thick may form at the
soil liquid interface Other factors such as rainfall rise in
groundwater level etc may also affect the permeability to some extent
Therefore the leaching field will probably require periodic maintenance
LIMITATIONS
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations
made in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation
engineering principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all
other warranties either expressed or implied
The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are not
intended to be used for final design purposes since they are based on
brief and limited preliminary geologic site reconnaissance of the site
and surrounding area a literature review soil information from a few
random borings and limited amount of laboratory tests performed on
shallow surface soil samples obtained from this exploration
Unanticipated soil and rock conditions are commonly encountered and
cannot be fully determined by taking soil samples Unforeseen
conditions such as well cemented soils or clays and silts of very low
permeability may occur in localized areas and may require additional
borings or corrections in the field Therefore additional field
exploration may be needed before design and construction of the leaching
field
This report has been prepared in order to assist the engineer in the
preliminary design of the leaching field

The subsurface conditions at the proposed sites were explored by
drilling two 2 borings at the approximate locations shown on the Site
Plan Plate 2 A truck mounted drilling rig was used to drill the
borings to depths of 5 to 25 feet below the existing ground surface
The soil encountered in the borings were classified by visual and
textural examination in the field by one of our engineers who
continuously monitored the drilling operations The classifications were
further reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory The
soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A graphic presentation of the materials
encountered is presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 2
In the borings soil samples were obtained by driving a 2 inch
standard split spoon sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches
The blow counts used to drive the sampler the last 12 inches are shown on
the logs of borings at the appropriate sample depths
