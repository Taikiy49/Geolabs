INTRODUCTION

formed for the Lee Residence at 110 Hanohano Place within the Kamehame Ridge

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a two story single family house at the project site
Based on the Topographic Survey Plan the project site consists of a level building pad
and an approximate 45 foot high slope located to the south of the level pad Based on
the Grading Plan the proposed house will be located on the level pad and
improvements are not planned along the slope
It is our understanding that slabs on grade and continuous wall footing
foundations are planned A concrete driveway and CMU walls are also planned near
the entrance to the house
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
soil rock conditions at the project site and to develop an idealized soil rock data set for
the formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to foundation
design and site grading for the project

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site designated as 25 on the Tax Map Key is located at 110
Hanohano Place within the Kamehame Ridge Subdivision in the district of Honolulu on
the island of Oahu Hawaii The project site is located in a lot area of approximately
19 895 square feet
During our field exploration the proposed building pad area was vacant with
adjacent house constructions in progress to the east and west of the proposed house
pad The project site is bounded by an existing house to the north and a drainage
GEOLABS HAWAII

The ground surface of the proposed building pad area is relatively flat
According to the topographic survey map the building pad area is at elevations of
approximately 362 to 363 feet Based on the subdivision grading plan the project
site slopes downward to the south at an approximately two horizontal to one vertical
2H 1V ratio with intermediate benches to an existing storm drain easement at about
elevation 315 feet

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
In general the project site is underlain by a surface layer of dense silty sand
overlying variable thickness of boulders and cobbles in a matrix of silty sand to the
maximum excavated depth of about 7 feet below the existing ground surface
On the basis of our observation of the test pit excavations it is our opinion that
the project site appeared to have been graded in accordance with the intent of the
recommendations presented in our Updated Geotechnical Engineering Evaluation
Proposed Kamehame Ridge Subdivision Units Oahu Hawaii dated
Therefore we believe that the proposed house is feasible from
a geotechnical engineering point of view
If footings are located near the top of slopes or on slopes the footings should
be embedded deep enough such that the horizontal distance from the outside edge at
the base of the footing to the edge of the slope is not less than 8 feet Based on the
current Plot Plan it appears that the proposed Breakfast Room may be at a setback
distance of about 4 feet from the top of the slope Therefore we recommend that the
proposed Breakfast Room or structure be reallocated to a further setback distance
away from the top of the rock fill slope
Site Grading
Based on the Grading Plan grading for the proposed residence will consist of
minor fills on the order of 1 foot to provide a level building pad for the on grade slab
construction It is our understanding that no new construction or structure is planned
along the slope located to the south of the proposed house
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3273 10 Page 6
The following guidelines should be observed during site grading operations
1 Prior to the commencement of the earthwork the areas within the contract
grading limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation
debris and other deleterious materials should be removed and disposed
of properly off site
2 Any soft weak or yielding areas disclosed during clearing operations
should be over excavated to expose firm ground and the resulting
excavation should be backfilled with well compacted engineered fill
Contract documents should include additive and deductive unit prices for
over excavation and engineered fill construction to account for variations
in the over excavation quantities
3 Areas that are to receive fill should be proof rolled to at least 90 percent
relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry
density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of
the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 test
procedures
4 All fill and backfill material should be tested for suitability of its intended
use by Geolabs Hawaii before delivering to the site If required off site
borrow material should consist of non expansive granular material such
as select borrow crusher waste crushed coral mudrock or cinder The
off site borrow material to be used for the current site grading
requirements should contain no particles greater than 3 inches in largest
dimension and less than 20 percent passing the No 200 sieve
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3273 10 Page 7
5 All fill and backfill should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in
loose lift thickness and compacted to not less than 90 percent relative
compaction All surfaces should be finished to create smooth and
essentially unyielding surfaces
6 Proper drainage is essential at the project site Measures should be
incorporated into the grading of the development to reduce potential for
erosion and to prevent concentration of water around structures The
finish grade outside the house should be sloped to shed water away from
the foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding
Foundations
In general continuous wall footing and slab on grade foundations are planned
for the project To facilitate construction we recommend footing foundations and the
thickened edge of slab on grade foundations be founded on the surficial dense silty
sand or compacted rock fill material This will reduce the potential of encountering
large boulders during foundation excavation
An allowable bearing capacity of up to 2 500 p s f may be used for the design
of residential foundation bearing on the existing dense silty sand or compacted rock fill
material The allowable soil bearing pressures is for total dead plus live loads and may
be increased by one third for transient applications such as temporary wind and or
seismic loads
If footings are located near the top of slopes or on slopes the footing should be
embedded deep enough such that the horizontal distance from the outside edge at the
base of the footing to the edge of the slope is not less than 8 feet
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3273 10 Page 8
The bottom of footing excavations should be compacted to obtain a dense
surface layer prior to placement of steel reinforcements and concrete
During construction the individual footing bottom preparation and compaction
should be monitored by a representative of Geolabs Hawaii to evaluate if adequate
bearing would be obtained prior to placement of reinforcement and concrete
Slab On Grade
Concrete slab on grade construction is anticipated for the ground floor of the
proposed house The subgrade soils should be prepared in accordance with the
recommendations presented in the Site Grading section of this report
Concrete floor slabs should be supported on a 4 inch layer of basalt base course
gravel above the compacted subgrade or rock fill material A 2 inch layer of
moist fine sand should be placed over the vapor barrier membrane to aid in
curing of slab concrete The sand should be moistened slightly prior to
placement of concrete
Slab thickness and reinforcement should be designed by a Structural Engineer
It should be noted that minor cracks in the slab can be expected Therefore we
recommend that a flexible floor covering be used on the slab We also
recommend that interior walls be designed to incorporate some flexibility to
accommodate some possible movements To reduce the potential of cracks on
the concrete floor slabs we recommend steel rebars be considered in lieu of the
conventional wire mesh
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3273 10 Page 9
The slab subgrade should be kept moist prior to concrete placement The
backfill against the slabs should be adequately compacted and graded to shed
water away from the slab perimeter
Lateral Load Resistance
Lateral load acting on the structure may be resisted by passive earth pressure
developed against the buried vertical faces of the foundation system and by
frictional resistance developed between the bottom of the foundation and the soil
system Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be calculated using an
equivalent fluid pressure of 300 p s f per unit depth This assumes that the soil
around the foundation is well compacted A coefficient of friction of 0 4 may be
used to calculate frictional resistance between the bottom of the foundations and
the bearing soils Unless covered by pavements or slabs the passive resistance
in the upper 12 inches of soils should be neglected
Settlements
The estimated footing settlements for foundations bearing on the dense surficial
silty sand or compacted fill are on the order of inch total with differential
settlements one half the amount

Concrete Pavement
We anticipate the garage area and driveway to be of concrete pavement
construction Our preliminary pavement design assumes the pavement subgrade soil
will be similar to the surficial silty sands encountered during our field exploration If site
grading exposes soil other than those assumed additional tests should be performed
to confirm or revise the recommended pavement sections for actual field conditions
If imported fills are used for the proposed roadways the subgrade on which the
pavement structural section is to be placed will need to have a minimum California
Bearing Ratio CBR of 12 Based on the above assumptions the following pavement
sections may be used for preliminary design purposes
Rigid Pavement
6 0 Inch Base Course 95 Percent Relative Compaction
12 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness on Compacted Subgrade
Where no vehicular traffic is anticipated sidewalks may consist of 4 inch thick
concrete slab on the near surface silty sand
The subgrade soils for the pavement section should be proof rolled to a minimum
of 95 percent relative compaction and non yielding condition
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3273 10 Page 12
Paved areas should be sloped and drainage gradients maintained to carry all
surface water off the site Surface water ponding should not be allowed anywhere
during the site grading or after construction Where concrete curbs are used to isolate
landscaping in or adjacent to the pavement areas we recommend the curbs extend a
minimum of 2 inches into the subgrade soil below the baserock to reduce migration of
landscape water into the pavement section Alternatively a subdrain system could be
constructed to collect excessive water from landscaping irrigation For long term
performance we recommend a subdrain system be constructed adjacent to
paved landscaped areas
Utility Trench
We envision that new utility lines may be required for the project A granular
bedding consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel is recommended under the pipes
Self compacting granular materials such as No 3 Fine gravel should be used for
trench backfill up to about 1 foot above the pipes to provide adequate support around
the pipes and to reduce compaction of the backfill thus reducing the potential for
damaging the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from 1 foot above the pipes to the finish
grade should consist of granular material or the on site surficial silty sand The backfill
should be placed in maximum 8 inch level loose lifts and mechanically compacted to
not less than 90 percent relative compaction
For utility lines located within the house pad we recommend that the backfill
should consist of No 3B Fine gravel up to the finish pad level

Construction Observation
It is recommended that Geolabs Hawaii be retained to provide geotechnical
engineering services during the project construction to observe the grading operations
and footing excavations This is to observe compliance with the intent of the design
concepts specifications and or recommendations and to allow design changes in the
event that subsurface conditions differ from those anticipated during design The
recommendations presented in this report are contingent upon the above observations
Any imported material required should be tested by Geolabs Hawaii for confor
mance with the project specifications prior to hauling to the project site
If the actual soil conditions encountered during construction are different from
those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to the design
should be made
