Dear Mr. Castro: 
This letter report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations performed 
during the foundation probing and grouting operations for the new blood bank facility as part 
of the Blood Bank of Hawaii HQ, 2212 Lauwiliwili Street, Lot 10 – Kapolei Business Park 
Phase 2 project, observed by Geolabs between December 26 and 28, 2023. 
The project site is located at Lot 10 of the Kapolei Business Park, Phase 2 
development in the Kapolei area on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. The project location and 
general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map, Plate 1. The project consists of a 
new blood bank facility encompassing approximately 57,525 square feet (1.32 acres) area. 
Due to potential subsurface cavities and/or voids within the underlying coralline 
deposits, a probing and grouting program to locate and grout potential cavities beneath the 
foundations was specified and implemented for the project. The probe hole locations were 
surveyed and marked in the field by the general contractor (Constructors Hawaii Inc.) prior 
to the probing operations. The probe holes were generally spaced at about 10-foot on center 
along the structural wall footings with additional probe locations for isolated footings, spread 
footings, and the elevator pad. Probing and grouting were conducted at existing grade 
conditions at approximately +35.5 feet Mean Sea Level (MSL). 
The probing and grouting work was performed by Mort Drilling Services, Inc. The 
probe holes were drilled with a TEI Rock Drill TE300HT mounted on a Komatsu PC-88-MR 
excavator equipped with a 3-inch diameter rotary percussion drill bit with grout injection 
portals connected to a hollow core threaded bar. The probe holes were about 3-inch in 
diameter and extended to about 11 to 18 feet below the existing ground surface, which were 
equivalent to at least about 10 feet below the bottom of footing elevations. A summary of 
our foundation probe drilling and grouting observations is provided on Plates 3.1 through 
3.5. The materials encountered during the probe drilling operations generally consisted of 
coralline materials.

After completion of the probe drilling operations, the probe holes were filled with a
neat water-cement grout mix consisting of 6 gallons of water to one bag (94-pound) of
cement. An Obermann VS-63 colloidal mixer equipment was used to mix and pump grout
through the hollow core threaded bar into the probe holes. Probe holes with grout intake
greater than six times the theoretical volume were re-grouted with a cement truck from
HC&D containing a 3:1 sand cement mixture.
The number of planned probe holes was 83 initially; however, five probe holes for
the generator pad, designated as Probe Nos. 76 to 80, were removed per Submittal
No. 31 00 00 R1. Where cavities and/or voids were encountered, additional probe holes
were drilled adjacent to the planned probe holes and then grouted with the neat water-
cement grout mix. A total of 25 probe holes were drilled and grouted due to cavities
encountered. Therefore, a total number of 103 probe holes were drilled and grouted for
the new blood bank facility project. Grout overage ranged from about 104 to 13,800
percent of the theoretical hole volume.
In summary, the foundation probing and grouting operations for the project have
been conducted in general conformance with the intent of the project requirements.
Based on our observations, it is our professional opinion that the voids and/or cavities
encountered at the planned probe locations have been grouted and that the treated
subgrade area should provide sufficient support for the new building foundations.
We appreciate the opportunity to be of service to you on this project. If you have
questions or need additional information, please contact our office.
