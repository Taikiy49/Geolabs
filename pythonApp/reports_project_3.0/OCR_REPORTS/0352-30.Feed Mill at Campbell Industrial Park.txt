I INTRODUCTION
General
This report presents the results of our investigation to
determine the subsurface soil and foundation conditions
for the project The purpose of the investigation was to
provide subsurface information necessary to the design of
a foundation support system for the proposed structure
The study included subsurface borings laboratory testing
and engineering analyses The report summarizes our work
on the project and presents recommendations pertinent to
foundation and site development requirements
Project Descriptions
The proposed project is located on northerly portion of
Olai Street between Kalaeloa Street and Hanna Street in
Campbell Industrial Park Ewa Hawaii The site is approxi
mately 220 feet wide along Olai Street and extended to approxi
mately 447 feet deep
A Feed Mill has been planned with several grain storage
tanks supported by column legs having maximum loads on the
order of 80 kips per leg An office warehouse single story
structure scalepit and underground diesel storage tank

II FIELD EXPLORATION
Seven subsurface borings ranging in depth from 15 to 40 feet were
completed on under the supervision of an engineer
or geologist from our firm The borings were accomplished using
truck mounted rotary drilling equipment with continuous flight
hollow stem augers NX core barrol and tricone rotary washbit
The boring locations are shown on the site sketch Plate l and
the boring logs are presented in Appendix A
In all borings Standard Penetration Tests were taken at selected
depth intervals not exceeding 5 feet The Standard Penetration
Test consists of driving a 2 inch 0 D split spoon sampler 18 inches
with a 140 pound hammer free falling a distance of 30 inches The
number of blows required to drive the sampler the last 12 inches
is termed the Standard Penetration Resistance N and is an
approximate measure of the relative density jor consistency of a
soil These resistance values are plotted on the boring logs
Samples obtained were visually classified in the field and
representative material was placed in air tight containers and
returned to our laboratory for inspection and testing

A Geology
The Ewa Plain encompassing Campbell Industrial Park was
2
Poek
formed during a late stage Pleistocene Epoch of Oahu s
geologic history The plain was formed by the combined
constructional sedimentary processes of coral reef and
alluvial plain formation Clastic non marine alluvial
debris was washed into the Pearl Harbor embayment from the
Waianae and Koolau Volcanic ranges as the region sank as
much as 1200 feet The embayment may never have been very
deep and probably filled with sediment as it sank This
is evidenced by the presence of shallow water coral Algal
Reefs These reefs apparently grew during the warmer inter
glacial stages of the Ice Age A probable modern analogue
is Kaneohe Bay
A late stage relative lowering of sea level island emergence
has left the reef exposed above sea level The exposure of
the reef to rainwater mixed with rotting vegetation to form
Slightly acid ground water is probably responsible for puka
formation by enlargement of natural coral reef cavities

because of the variability of reef environments over distances
of a few feet the possibility of encountering soils with

IV ENGINEERING RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the results of the field investigation laboratory test
ing and engineering analyses the following conclusions and
recommendations are presented
A Site Preparation
The site has been grubbed and surface pukas have been filled
with roots stumps and unsuitable material It is therefore
recommended that all the pukas be examined and any unsuitable
4
ae
C
materials be cleaned out and replaced with lean concrete or
compacted fill see Plate 2
Foundations
Spread or continuous foundations may be used to support the
proposed structure with the maximum bearing capacity of
5000 pounds per square foot and should have minimum width
of 24 inches and 12 inches minimum depth
A continuous circular ring footing may be used instead of
individual spread footings This system may prove to be advan
tageous for bridging over possible pukas
The bottom of footing excavations should be inspected and
probed for possible large pukas by a jack hammer or some
other mechanical means to a depth of 3 feet below elevation
of the bottom of the footings Any soft compressible
material or pukas found beneath footings should be cleaned
out and filled with lean concrete or compacted granular
fill see Plate 2
The maximum settlement is expected to be less than 3 4 inch
with differential settlement not expected to exceed 1 2 inch
Floor Slabs and Pavings
To provide an even support of slabs on grade or paving it
is recommended that the existing grade be scarified to a
depth of 6 inches moistened and recompacted to 90 percent
on
of the maximum dry density as determined by ASTM D 1557 70
Floor slabs should be reinforced with a minimum of 6x6 welded
wire fabric
D Underground Fuel Storage Tank
Underground fuel storage tank retaining walls and other
underground facilities may be designed for an equivalent fluid
pressure of 35 pounds per cubic foot Surcharges due to adja
cent footings by hydrostatic pressure construction equipment
etc must be added to the above value
Thin layer of sand should be used at the bottom of the storage
fuel tank to provide an even support of the tank backfill
should be compacted to 85 percent of the maximum dry density
E Inspection
It is recommended that all footing excavations be inspected
by our representative prior to placing forms steel or concrete
Any fill which is placed should be inspected tested and certified

LABORATORY TESTING
The laboratory testing program was developed to establish the
engineering properties of the soils and rocks encountered at the
project site
Classification Tests
a Visual Classification All soil samples obtained from
the borings were brought to our laboratory where they were
visually reclassified to confirm or modify the field classi
fication prior to finalizing the Logs of Borings
_ b Water Content Determinations In addition to visual a
ee a tons
classification typical samples were tested for natural
_ Water content as an aid in soil classification and in
evaluating soil properties The water content values are
based upon the dry weight of the soil
co Atterberg Limits Atterberg limit tests were performed
on selected clay and silt samples obtained from the borings
for the purposes of identification and correlation of soils
Standard procedures ASTM D 424 for the plastic limit and
ASTM D 423 for the liquid limit were used in the performance
of these tests
d Grain Size Analysis Sieve analysis were performed on
granular soils For fine grained soils the hydrometer
test was performed to determine the distribution of the grain
ING
_ Sizes beyond the No 200 sieve
Shear Strength Tests
a Unconfined Compression Tests Intact core and split
spoon soil samples which apparently exhibited high plasticity
were tested for unconfined strength Length to diameter
ratio of 2 was maintained throughout the tests to avoid
scatter of test results and to facilitate evaluation of
shear strength values for design purposes
b Direct Shear Test In general soil samples were tested
for shear strength using a 2 41 I D direct shear box Each
set consisted of at least two tests each test run under
different normal loads The results are plotted as Normal
Stress vs Shear Strength with a failure envelope drawn to
determine values of cohesion c and angle of frictional
resistance
c Consolidation Tests Consolidation tests were performed
on selected undisturbed ring samples of the compressible
soils to provide basic data for making settlement calculations
Porous stones were placed on the top and bottom of the samples
to allow drainage Vertical loads were applied in increments
with each load increment being allowed to consolidate prior
to adding the next increment Measurements of the time and
consolidation were obtained during each load increment and
rebound was measured during the unloading portion Consolida
tion test results are plotted in terms of percent settlement
versus applied loads
