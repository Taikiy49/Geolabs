As requested we have performed a geotechnical engineering exploration for the
proposed Villas at Koele Building 15 project on the Island of Lanai Hawaii This letter
report summarizes the findings from our field exploration at the project site and presents
our recommendations for the design of the proposed project These findings and
recommendations are intended for design input only and are subject to the limitations
noted at the end of this letter report
Please note that the soil samples recovered during our field exploration remaining
after testing will be stored for a period of two months from the date of this report The
samples will be discarded after that date unless arrangements are made for a longer
sample storage period Please contact our office for alternative sample storage
requirements if appropriate

FIELD EXPLORATION
Our field exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling four borings
designated as Boring Nos 1 through 4 extending to depths of about 11 5 to 16 5 feet
below the existing ground surface The borings were drilled using a truck mounted drill rig
equipped with continuous flight solid stem augers The approximate locations of the
borings drilled at the project site are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on the Boring Log Legend Plate 3 Graphic
representations of the materials encountered are provided on the Logs of Borings
Plates 4 through 7
Soil samples were obtained from the borings drilled in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designations D 3550 and D 1586 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils and
Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 3 inch OD Modified
California Sampler or a 2 inch OD standard penetration sampler with a 140 pound
hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the second and
third 6 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs
of Borings at the appropriate sample depths

Selected soil samples from the field exploration were tested in the laboratory to
evaluate the soil moisture density and swell potential characteristics The following
laboratory tests were performed on selected soil samples obtained from the field
exploration
Moisture Content ASTM D 2216 and Unit Weight ASTM D 2937 determinations
were performed on selected samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil
properties The test results are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate
sample depths
Eight one inch Ring Swell tests were performed on relatively undisturbed and

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our field exploration at the project site generally encountered a surface fill layer
consisting of stiff to hard brown clayey silts extending to depths of about 4 to 9 5 feet
below the existing ground surface Below the fill layer residual soils consisting of very stiff
to hard reddish brown clayey silts were encountered and the residual soils extended to
the maximum depth drilled of about 16 5 feet below the existing ground surface
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings drilled at the time of our field
exploration However groundwater levels are subject to change due to rainfall seasonal
precipitation surface water runoff and other factors
FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered at the project site we believe that
shallow spread and or continuous footings may be used for support of the proposed
Building 15 at the Villas at Koele Subdivision In general the footings should be
embedded a minimum of 24 inches below the finished grade In addition the footings
should bear on properly compacted fills or the stiff natural materials Detailed discussion
of our geotechnical recommendations for design of building foundations slabs on grade
site grading and other geotechnical aspects of the project are presented in the following
sections of this letter report
Building Foundations
Based on our field exploration findings and laboratory test results shallow spread
and or continuous footings may be used for support of the proposed residential structure
provided the footings are embedded a minimum of 24 inches below the lowest adjacent
finished grade to reduce the effects of the expansive soils present at the project site
An allowable bearing pressure of up to 3 000 pounds per square foot psf may be
utilized for the design of the multi family residential building foundations bearing on
properly compacted fills or the stiff to hard on site clayey silts This bearing value is for
those caused by wind or seismic forces Footings should have a minimum width of
12 inches and should be embedded a minimum of 24 inches below the existing grade
For footings constructed near tops of slopes or on sloping ground the footings should be
embedded deep enough to provide a minimum horizontal setback distance of 5 feet
measured from the outside edge of the footings to the face of the slope
We estimate that total settlements for footings bearing on properly compacted fills
and or the on site stiff to hard clayey silts as recommended herein to be less than one 1
inch Differential settlements between adjacent footings supported on similar materials are
estimated to be less than one half 12 inch

lf soft and or loose materials are encountered at the bottom of footing excavations
the materials should be over excavated to expose the underlying firm materials The
over excavation should be backfilled with select granular material compacted to a
minimum of 90 percent relative compaction or the footing bottom may be deepened to the
underlying firm materials
Foundations located next to utility trenches or easements should be embedded
below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane extending upward from the
bottom edge of the utility trench or the footings should be embedded as deep as the
inverts of the utility lines This requirement is necessary to avoid surcharging adjacent
below grade structures with additional structural loads and to reduce the potential for
appreciable foundation settlement
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive earth pressure
acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system and by frictional resistance
developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil Resistance due to
passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 pounds
per square foot per foot of depth pcf This assumes that the soils around the footings are
relatively undisturbed or well compacted Unless covered by pavements or slabs the
passive pressure resistance in the upper 12 inches of soil should be neglected For
footings bearing on properly compacted fills or the on site stiff to hard clayey silts a
coefficient of friction of 0 35 may be used to evaluate the lateral load resistance
We recommend that footing excavations be observed by a representative from
Geolabs Inc prior to the placement of reinforcement steel and concrete to confirm the
foundation bearing conditions and the required embedment depths
Slabs On Grade
Based on our field exploration the project site is underlain by near surface clayey
soils with relatively low expansion potential when subjected to moisture fluctuations It is
our opinion that additional structural fill is not required below the cushion layer provided
the subgrade soils are maintained in a moist condition free of shrinkage cracks
In general we recommend that a minimum 6 inch thick layer of cushion fill
consisting of No 3 Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation be placed below the slab to
serve as a Capillary break and to provide for uniform support of the slab To reduce the
potential for excessive moisture infiltration and subsequent damage to floor coverings an
impervious moisture barrier is recommended on top of the No 3 Fine gravel cushion fill
layer
Exterior concrete walkways likely will be required for the proposed project We also
recommend that concrete walkways be supported on 6 inches of non expansive select
granular fill The select granular fill should be compacted to at least 90 percent relative

compaction In addition crack control joints should be provided at intervals equal to the
width of the walkways with expansion joints at right angle intersections
Where the slabs will be subjected to vehicular loading such as the carport slabs
and driveway we recommend that a 6 inch layer of aggregate subbase be provided below
the slabs in lieu of the 6 inch thick gravel cushion layer The aggregate subbase should
consist of crushed basaltic aggregates compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative
compaction For the design of structural slabs supported on 6 inches of aggregate
subbase a modulus of subgrade reaction of about 200 pounds per square inch per inch of
deflection pci may be used for the compacted aggregate subbase
We recommend that the slab subgrade soils be scarified to a depth of 8 inches
moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction before placement of the
cushion fill layer below the slabs on grade
Where shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of the subgrade we
recommend that the soils be thoroughly moistened to close the shrinkage cracks or the
subgrade should be prepared again as recommended above Saturation and subsequent
yielding of the exposed subgrade due to inclement weather and poor drainage may require
over excavation of the soft areas and replacement with well compacted fill
It should be noted that areas adjacent to the slab edges and or stem walls should
be backfilled tightly against the structure with the on site clayey soils and the ground
surface should be graded to divert water away from the slabs to reduce the potential for
water ponding around the slabs and foundations
Site Grading
As indicated previously the project site appears to have been cleared grubbed
and leveled However if vegetation debris deleterious materials and other unsuitable
materials are discovered during the construction work the unsuitable materials should be
removed and disposed of properly off site Soft loose and yielding areas encountered
below areas designated to receive fill or future improvements should be over excavated to
expose firm or dense materials and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with well
compacted fill The excavated soft and or loose soils may be used as fill provided it
meets the requirements for fill materials
Prior to filling the existing ground should be scarified to a depth of about 8 inches
moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture and compacted to
a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place
dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil
established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 test procedures Optimum moisture is the
water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density

Where shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of the subgrade we
recommend that the soil be thoroughly moistened to close the cracks or scarified and
prepared again as recommended above Saturation and subsequent yielding of the
exposed subgrade due to inclement weather and poor drainage may require
over excavation of the soft and or loose areas and replacement with well compacted fill
In general the excavated on site soils may be re used as a source of general fill or
backfill materials If required imported fill materials should consist of non expansive
granular material such as crushed coral basalt or cinder sand The material should be
well graded from coarse to fine with no particles larger than 3 inches in largest dimension
and should contain between 10 to 30 percent passing No 200 sieve The material should
have a laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR greater than 20 and a swell potential of
less than 1 percent when tested in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1883
Imported fill materials should be tested by Geolabs for conformance with these
recommendations prior to delivery to the project site for the intended use
Select borrow subbase material required below the exterior slabs on grade
subjected to vehicular loading should meet the material requirements in Section 30 as
specified in the Standard Specifications for Public Works Construction 1986
Fills and backfills should be moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the
optimum moisture content placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness
and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction In general compaction
should be accomplished by sheepsfoot rollers vibratory rollers or other similar types of
acceptable compaction equipment
A representative from Geolabs should observe the site grading and earthwork
operations for the project It is important that a representative from our office be present to
observe the site preparation to evaluate whether undesirable materials are encountered
during the excavation process and whether the exposed soil conditions are similar to
those encountered in our exploration
Utilities
We envision that new utility lines will be required to service the proposed residential
building In general granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of open graded gravel
ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation should be provided below the pipes Free draining
granular materials such as open graded gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation also
should be used for the initial trench backfill up to about 12 inches above the pipes It is
critical that a free draining material be used to reduce the potential for formation of voids
below the haunches of the pipes and to provide adequate support for the sides of the
pipes Improper backfill material around the pipes and improper placement of the backfill
could result in settlement of the backfill and damage to the pipes

The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipes to
the top of the subgrade or finished grade may consist of the on site clayey silt soils or
imported granular materials The backfill should be moisture conditioned to above
optimum moisture content placed in about 8 inch loose lifts and mechanically compacted
to not less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for appreciable
future ground subsidence Mechanical compaction equipment should be used to compact
the materials at the project site Water tamping jetting or pounding should not be used to
compact the backfill material
Lot Drainage
The finished grades adjacent to the residential building should be sloped to shed
water away from the foundations and slabs and to reduce the potential for ponding It is
also advised that gutter systems be installed around the structure and that the discharge
be diverted away from the foundation and slab areas Excessive landscape watering near
the foundations and slabs should also be avoided Planters next to foundations should be
avoided or have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce the potential for excessive water
infiltration into the subsurface soils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the above
foundation recommendations because ponded water could cause subsurface soil
saturation and subsequent loss of strength of the near surface soils The foundation
excavations should be properly backfilled against the footings immediately after setting of
the concrete to reduce the potential for appreciable water infiltration into the subsurface
In addition drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and should be
maintained to drain surface water runoff away from the foundations and slabs

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part
upon information obtained from the field borings Variations of subsoil conditions between
and beyond the borings may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not
become evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be
necessary to re evaluate the findings and discussions provided in this letter report
The locations of the field borings indicated in this report are approximate having
been estimated by taping and pacing from the features shown on the Site Plan by
Belt Collins Hawaii dated March 1994 The approximate elevations of field borings were
estimated from the contour lines shown on the same plan The physical locations and
elevations of the borings should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the
methods used
The stratification lines shown on graphic representations of the borings depict the
approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual transition
As mentioned previously groundwater was not encountered in the borings drilled at the
time of our field exploration However it must be noted that groundwater level changes
may occur due to seasonal precipitation and other factors
