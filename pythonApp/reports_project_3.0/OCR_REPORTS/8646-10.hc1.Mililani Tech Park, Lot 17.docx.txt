SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS 
The subsurface conditions at the Koa Ridge Makai Off-Site Detention Basin No. 1 
site were explored by excavating three test pits, designated as Test Pit Nos. 1 through 3, 
extending to a depth of about 20 feet below the existing ground surface. In addition, six bulk 
samples were obtained from the test pits to evaluate the engineering properties of the on-site 
soils. The approximate test pit locations are shown on the Site Plan, Plate 1. 
Our field exploration generally encountered alluvial/residual soils consisting of stiff 
reddish brown silty clay extending to the maximum depth explored of about 20 feet below 
the existing ground surface. Basaltic boulders were encountered in one of the test pits at 
depths of about 15 to 20 feet below the existing ground surface. We did not encounter 
groundwater in the test pits excavated at the time of our field exploration. However, it should 
be noted that groundwater levels are subject to change due to rainfall, time of year, seasonal 
precipitation, surface water runoff, and other factors.

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS 
Based on the additional exploration performed and the laboratory test results, the 
on-site soils at the Koa Ridge Makai Off-Site Detention Basin No. 1 borrow site generally 
consist of silty clay with laboratory California Bearing Ratio (CBR) values ranging from 
6.8 to 17.2 and swell ranging from 1.1 to 2 percent.  Based on the laboratory test results, 
the borrow soils from the Koa Ridge Makai Off-Site Detention Basin No. 1 site may be used 
as a source of general fill materials for the Mililani Tech Park, Lot No. 17 project, provided 
that deleterious materials such as vegetation are removed and over-sized materials greater 
than 3 inches in maximum dimension are screened.  
The Koa Ridge borrow material may exhibit a low to moderate expansion potential 
when subjected to moisture fluctuations. To reduce the potential for changes in the moisture 
content of the slab subgrade soils, we recommend that the Koa Ridge borrow material may 
be used below 12 inches below the finished subgrade. 
General fill materials should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose 
thickness, moisture-conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture content 
and compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction. The compaction requirements for 
finished subgrades subjected to vehicular traffic, such as the pavement subgrade, should 
be increased to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction.  Relative compaction refers 
to the in-place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of 
the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D1557. Optimum moisture is the water 
content (percentage by weight) corresponding to the maximum dry density.
