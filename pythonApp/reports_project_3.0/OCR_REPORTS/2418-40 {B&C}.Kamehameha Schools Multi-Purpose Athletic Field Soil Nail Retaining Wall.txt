This letter report summarizes our geotechnical observations and testing services
performed during the construction of the soil nail retaining walls for the Multi Purpose
Athletic Field project at Kamehameha Schools Our work was performed in general
accordance with the services outlined in our fee proposal dated
In general this letter report presents our observations and the results of the tests
performed on the soil nails for the project from July 17 through The
general contractor for the project was A C Kobayashi Inc and the specialty
subcontractor responsible for installation of the soil nail retaining wall system was Malcolm
Drilling Co Inc
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located within the Kamehameha Schools in the Kapalama
Heights area of Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity
of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
A geotechnical engineering exploration of the project site was performed by
Geolabs Inc and our recommendations were presented in the report entitled
Geotechnical Engineering Exploration Multi Purpose Athletic Field Kamehameha
Schools Bishop Estate Honolulu Oahu Hawaii dated The purpose of
our geotechnical engineering services during construction of the project was to observe
the construction of the specialty soil nail retaining wall for general conformance with the
project plans and specifications
Based on the project plans a specialty retaining wall system consisting of a soil nail
retaining wall system was constructed behind to the east of the bleachers for the
2006 Kalihi Street Honolulu Hawaii 96819
Kamehameha Schools Bishop Estate Page 2
multi purpose athletic field A soil nail retaining wall system consists of a series of
individual reinforcing bars grouted into drilled holes to stabilize an excavation or slope A
shotcrete facing is generally applied to the face of the excavation to provide the
appearance of a conventional concrete retaining wall The soil nail retaining wall system
would serve both as temporary shoring of the excavation and the permanent retaining wall
at this location
When excavation for the cut was made the first level of soil nails was then
installed As the permanent soil nails were installed some of the soil nails were subjected
to proof tests or creep tests Excavation for the retaining wall then proceeded to the next
level and a subsequent level of soil nails was then installed The temporary facing of
shoicrete was omitted for the soil nail retaining wall based on the generally competent
subsurface conditions encountered at the face of the excavation during construction
DIFFERING SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Based on the field explorations performed in 1990 1992 and 1999 the project site
was underlain by stiff to very stiff clayey silts and silty clays extending to depths of about
5 to 60 feet below the ground surface The surface soil layer was underlain by weathered
basalt formation extending to the maximum depth explored of about 83 feet below the
existing ground surface Groundwater was not encountered in the drilled borings at the
time of our field exploration
As shown on the project plans a tieback retaining wall system consisting of soldier
piles and permanent tiebacks was specified at the north and northeastern portions of the
multi purpose athletic field During installation of the soldier piles for the tieback retaining
wall a differing subsurface condition was encountered along the tieback retaining wall
alignment at the northeastern portion of the multi purpose athletic field In general hard
basalt formation was encountered within the planned depths of the soldier piles making
drilling for the soldier piles difficult Therefore a soil nail retaining wall system was
adopted for the retaining wall at the northeastern portion of the multi purpose athletic field
in lieu of the tieback retaining wall Soil Nail Nos N 318 through N 364 were installed for
this additional soil nail retaining wall

wall Following testing of the two verification soil nails the specialty subcontractor installed
approximate locations of the 355 soil nails are shown on the Soil Nail Identification Plans

Soil Nail Installation
A representative from Geolabs was present to observe the drilling reinforcing bar
installation and the grouting operations during the installation of the soil nails for the
project The production soil nails were drilled using both a Watson 1500 track mounted
drill rig equipped with 12 inch diameter solid stem augers and a anchor
drill rig equipped with both 6 5 inch and 8 inch diameter compressed air activated
down the hole hammers
In general the soil nail drilled holes were extended to depths of about 11 to 23 feet
which were roughly 1 foot deeper than the reinforcing bar length A summary of our
observations and other pertinent information recorded by our field personnel are provided
on the Soil Nail Installation Summary Plates 4 1 through 4 15 Based on the observations
of our field representatives we believe that the soil nails for the permanent retaining wall
have been installed in general conformance with the project plans and specifications and
within the limits of generally accepted construction practices
Soil Nail Testing
In accordance with the project specifications twenty percent of the production soil
nails for the project were subjected to proof tests or verification creep tests Two
additional verification soil nails Soil Nail and NT 2 sacrificial test nails were
loaded to 320 and 250 percent of the design loads respectively In general seventy six
76 production soil nails were loaded to the maximum test load of 150 percent of the
design load The associated movements for the soil nails at each load increment up to the
maximum test load were measured and recorded by an engineer from Geolabs A
summary of the soil nails tested is provided on the Soil Nail Testing Summary Plates 5 1
through 5 3
In general the seventy six production soil nails tested did not fail at the maximum
test load Seventy one of the seventy six production soil nails 93 percent tested met all
the test criteria specified in the project specifications for acceptance However five of the
seventy six production soil nails met all but one of the test criteria specified in the project
specifications for acceptance One of the criteria for acceptance of the soil nails was to
achieve movements of at least 80 percent of the theoretical elastic elongation
As noted five of the seventy six production soil nails did not meet the minimum
theoretical elastic elongation requirement Based on our field observations we believe
that these five soil nails may have been over grouted by the contractor Therefore the
bond lengths were actually greater than the design lengths resulting in shorter unbonded
lengths which were used in calculating the theoretical elastic elongation Considering that
the five soil nails tested did not fail at the maximum test load we believe that the five soil

Shotcrete Application for Permanent Retaining Wall
The soil nail retaining wall system consisted of a permanent concrete wall facing
that was constructed by applying shotcrete to the face of the excavation The temporary
shotcrete facing was omitted for the soil nail retaining wall due to the generally competent
soil conditions encountered at the face of the excavation during construction As part of
our special inspection services on the project an engineer from Geolabs was present to
observe the application of shotcrete for the permanent retaining wall facing
In general our field representatives observed the placement of the reinforcing bars
for the permanent retaining wall facing and the application of shotcrete for the permanent
retaining wall facing Based on our observations during the soil nail retaining wall
construction it is our professional opinion that the shotcrete for the permanent retaining
wall facing have been installed in general conformance with the project plans and
specifications and within the limits of generally accepted construction practices
