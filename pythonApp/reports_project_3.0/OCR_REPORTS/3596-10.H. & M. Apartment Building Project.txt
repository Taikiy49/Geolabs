In general accordance with the scope of services outlined in our fee proposal
dated we have observed the test pile driving operations for the
above referenced project from September 23 through 26 and on
The test pile driving operation was performed by Lahaina Pier Pile Company
Inc LPPC A diesel hammer was used to drive the 16 inch
octagonal precast prestressed concrete piles Our field observations indicated that
Setting No 4 was used for the pile hammer during the test pile driving operation with a
maximum rated energy of foot pounds
A total of 21 test piles
169 176 and 188 were driven to depths of
about 76 to 174 feet below the existing ground surface at the site Pile No 165 was
subsequently broken during site operations after the test pile driving program Based on
our discussions with LPPC it appears that the break off point might be at a relatively
shallow depth below the existing ground surface Therefore we believe that a
replacement pile would not be necessary at this time Pile No 20 was also damaged
during the test pile driving operations and the first section of Pile No 147 was lost during
installation Therefore replacement piles have to be driven for these two piles during the
remaining production pile program Individual pile driving records have been transmitted

Based on our test pile driving observation it is our opinion that the test piles
excluding Pile Nos 20 and 147 have been driven to sufficient depths and or resistance to
provide the design vertical compressive load capacity of 150 tons per pile As a result
we recommend that the remaining project production piles should be cast in two phases
as shown below with the estimated pile lengths shown on the attached Pile Foundation
Plan
Phase 1
50 foot section 130 pieces
75 foot section 13 pieces
85 foot section 50 pieces
90 foot section 35 pieces
Phase 2
50 foot section 25 pieces
90 foot section 8 pieces
We recommend that Pile Nos 127 through 151 160 through 173 and 185 through
187 as shown on the Pile Foundation Plan be driven first to determine the actual pile
lengths required in the vicinity of the deep alluvial channel Based on the pile driving
results obtained the number of 50 and 90 foot piles required for the Phase 2 casting may
then be adjusted for the subject project The project production piles should be driven
with the same hammer and setting and the pile driving operation may be terminated
when any one of the following driving criteria is satisfied
e The pile driving may be terminated at a depth shallower than 80 feet below the
existing ground surface when the aggregated biow counts reach 100 blows per
foot for the last 5 feet
e The pile driving may be terminated at a depth deeper than 80 feet below the
existing ground surface when the aggregated blow counts reach 120 blows per
foot for the last 2 feet or when the blow counts reach 30 blows per inch for the
last 3 inches
e The pile driving may also be terminated as requested by the field
representative from Geolabs Hawaii
A pile follower should be present at site during the pile driving operations in the
event that the production piles have driven below the existing ground surface in order to
develop the design capacity Based on our field observations during the test pile driving
operation we recommend that predrilling bé performed for the remaining production piles
to facilitate the pile driving operation through the hard ledges encountered at the project
