PRE-PRODUCTION MICROPILE INSTALLATION AND LOAD TESTING 
WAIKIKI SHELL IMPROVEMENTS 
HONOLULU, OAHU, HAWAII 
Dear Mr. Kimura: 
This letter report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observation services 
performed during the pre-production micropile installation and load testing for the Waikiki 
Shell Improvements project in Honolulu on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. 
In general, this letter report presents our field observations and the results of load 
testing performed for the pre-production micropile from January 7 to 15, 2021, and our 
findings and recommendations for the proposed micropile foundation for the project. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The project generally consists of the installation of truss frame structures supporting 
new sound and lighting equipment at the Waikiki Shell facility in Honolulu on the Island of 
Oahu, Hawaii. Based on the contract documents provided, we understand that the footings 
for the truss frame structures are to be supported by 5.5-inch diameter micropiles with a 
design load capacity of 29 kips. Per project specifications, the contractor is required to 
perform a pre-production load test up to 200 percent of the design load on one sacrificial 
micropile.  
MICROPILE INSTALLATION 
A pre-production test micropile, designated as TP-1, was drilled and grouted to a 
depth of 32.8 feet below ground surface on January 7 and 8, 2021. However, because the 
contractor was unable to fully insert 34.5 feet of the reinforcing steel, TP-1 was abandoned 
and another pre-production test micropile, designated as TP-2, was installed near the 
eastern portion of the project site. The approximate locations of TP-1 and TP-2 are shown 
on the Site Plan, Plate 1. The pre-production micropile TP-2 was installed by Kelikai, Inc. on 
January 8, 2021 utilizing a Beretta T-24 track-mounted drill rig. In general, the pre-production 
micropile TP-2 was installed extending to a depth of about 34.6 feet below the existing

ground surface with about 24.4 feet of 5.5-inch OD steel casing left in-place with about

Based on our field observations, the pre-production micropile TP-2 was installed by 
advancing the steel casing to a depth of about 34.6 feet below the ground surface. The 
pre-production micropile generally extended through the soft/very loose lagoonal deposits 
and developed load capacity in medium hard sandstone. Upon reaching the tip elevation, 
the cement grout was pumped via a tremie pipe from the bottom to the top of the casing, 
displacing the groundwater inside the steel casing. After the initial grout application, the 
No. 11 Grade 75 all-threaded rebar with centralizers was installed to the full depth of the 
borehole with centralizers spaced at every 10 feet. 
During grouting operation, the bottom 10.2 feet of the steel casing was extracted, 
providing the bond length of the pre-production micropile within the underlying sandstone. 
The remaining steel casing (about 24.4 feet) was left in-place at the top of the micropile 
(unbonded zone) as the permanent casing. During the setting of the permanent casing, the 
steel casing was raised and plunged 2 feet into the sandstone. Prior to load testing, the steel 
casing protruding above the ground was cut and removed to provide access for the load 
testing apparatus. 
MICROPILE TESTING 
The pre-production load test was conducted as a tensile load test in general 
accordance with ASTM D3689. The design load capacity was specified as 29 kips for the 
micropiles and load tested to at least 200 percent of the design load capacity. A hydraulic 
jack system reacting against a reaction beam was used to apply the testing load to the load 
test micropile. In general, the reaction beam was supported by wooden dunnage at the two 
ends of the beam. The micropile testing was performed by Kelikai, Inc. 
A load cell was used to measure the test loads applied to the micropile. The micropile 
displacements were measured with two dial gauges on the left and right sides of the load 
test micropile and a 1/100 division ruler was set onto an extension from the micropile. Each 
dial gauge was attached to an independent reference beam. In addition, a 1/100-inch 
division ruler was set on a remote reference to verify potential ground displacement during 
the load testing that could affect the dial gauge readings. The surveyor’s level was used to 
observe the rulers and the remote reference for movement. 
In general, the pre-production micropile TP-2 was loaded to the maximum test load 
of 58 kips in approximately 7 kips load increments (about 25 percent of the design load 
capacity). Each load increment, including the test load at 29 kips, was held for at least 
5 minutes. The ultimate load capacity of 58 kips (200 percent of design load) was held for 
60 minutes to evaluate the effects of creep. The gross rate of displacement at the 58 kips 
load increments was no greater than 0.007 inches between 6 and 60 minutes. 
Subsequently, the pre-production micropile was unloaded in approximately four equal 
decrements and held for at least 2 minutes for each decrement. The test micropile was then
