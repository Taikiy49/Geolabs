Dear Ms Kodama
In accordance with our proposal dated we have performed a
geotechnical engineering exploration for the proposed Koali Road Emergency Stream
Repairs project in the City of Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii This letter report
summarizes the findings from our field exploration at the project site and presents our
findings and recommendations for the proposed project These findings and
recommendations are intended for design input only and are subject to the limitations
noted at the end of this letter report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located along the eastern bank of the Manoa Stream extending
from Waialae Avenue north towards Dole Street in the City of Honolulu on the Island of
Oahu Hawaii The existing street has one traffic lane and is bordered on the eastern side
by single family residences The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown
on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The adjacent Manoa Stream runs in a north south alignment The stream s bottom
is about 20 feet below the road surface The banks of the stream are lined with various
types of retaining walls primarily consisting of Concrete Rubble Masonry CRM and
concrete
During the heavy storms that occurred at the end of October 2004 approximately
160 feet of the slope bank protection failed The failure zone was approximately located
between Station 1 00 through Station 2 50 along Koali Road The existing road is
currently unsupported and some of the utilities including a drain line and sewer lines are
exposed in the failed bank
It is believed that the slope failure is related to the possible scour of the wall
supports and high water pressure behind the slope protection introduced by a broken
drain line It is desired to design remedial slope protection to reduce further instability of
the slope and roadway

EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is located along the Manoa Stream in the City of Honolulu on the
Island of Oahu Hawaii The location of the project site is shown on the Project Location
Map Plate 1
The project site is located next to Koali Road along the Manoa Stream The
existing banks of the Manoa Stream were lined with various types of slope protection
including CRM retaining walls and Grout Rubble Paving GRP slope facing The failed
slope protection zone was located in the middle section from Station 1 00 through Station
2 50 along the stream
The concrete wall and rip rap structures in the failure zone appeared to have
experienced severe structural damage and separated from the soil slope A few broken
concrete pieces were found inside the stream The fill and alluvial materials were exposed
on the surface of the slope Loose soils were also accumulated at the bottom of the slope
Longitudinal cracks were observed approximately 7 feet away from the stream
banks along the existing pavements A drain line and sewer line were observed along the
exposed slope surface Several overhead power lines were present during the time of our
field exploration
FIELD EXPLORATION
Our field exploration consisted of drilling and sampling two borings designated as
Boring Nos 1 and 2 extending to depths of about 44 and 48 feet below the existing
ground surface respectively The borings were drilled using a trailer mounted drill rig with
continuous flight solid stem augers and coring equipments The approximate locations of
the borings drilled at the site are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were drilled
away from the edge of the slope due to safety reasons i e slope instability overhead
power lines

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our field exploration at the project site consisted of drilling and sampling two
borings Our field exploration generally encountered approximately 9 to 10 feet of fill
materials consisting of stiff to very stiff silty clay clayey silt The fill materials were
underlain by alluvial materials consisting of clay silt sand gravel and basaltic boulders
extending to the maximum depths drilled of 44 and 48 feet below the existing ground
surface
Groundwater was encountered at approximately 15 3 and 16 feet below the
existing ground surface in the borings at the time of our field exploration However it
should be noted that groundwater levels are subject to change due to rainfall seasonal
precipitation surface water run off and other factors

Retaining Structures
Based on the topographic survey and as built plans we anticipate that the
proposed retaining walls will retain soils of approximately 22 feet in height The following
general guidelines are provided and may be used for the design of retaining structures at
the project site
Retaining Structure Foundations
Based on the subsurface soil conditions encountered on the project site we
recommend that the proposed concrete walls be supported on continuous footings
An allowable bearing pressure of 4 500 pounds per square foot psf may be used
for design of footings bearing on recompacted on site sand The bearing value is
for dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third 1 3 for transient loads
such as those caused by wind and seismic forces
The bottom of the footing excavation should be cleared of loose material prior to the
placement of reinforcing steel or concrete If soft and or loose materials are
encountered at the bottom of footing excavations they should be over excavated to
expose the underlying firm or dense materials The resulting excavations should be
backfilled with concrete
If the foundation is designed and constructed in accordance with our
recommendations we estimate total settlements of the foundations bearing on the
in situ soils to be less than 2 inches Differential settlements between footings are
estimated to be on the order of 0 5 inch
Lateral loads acting on the structures may be resisted by frictional resistance
between the base of the foundation and bearing materials and by passive earth
pressure developed against the near vertical faces of the embedded portion of
footings A coefficient of friction of 0 45 and cohesion of 200 psf may be used for
footings bearing on in situ soils Resistance due to passive pressure should be
ignored due to the potential for scouring
Considering that scour effect was suspected to be a major cause of the failure of
the existing retaining walls at the project site we recommend that pipe piles be
used to provide additional support under the cutoff wall

Site Grading
Site grading would generally be limited to the Koali Road reconstruction and footing
preparation for the retaining wall At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract
grading limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed Vegetation debris deleterious
materials and other unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of properly
off site Loose materials accumulated at the foot of the slope should also be removed and
properly disposed of
Soft and or yielding areas encountered during clearing and grubbing below areas
designated to receive fill should be over excavated to expose firm material and the
resulting excavation should be backfilled with well compacted fills The excavated soft
and or organic soils should be properly disposed of off site _
The over excavated subgrades finished subgrades and areas designated to
receive fills should then be scarified to a minimum depth of 8 inches moisture conditioned
to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of
90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of
soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in
accordance with ASTM D 1557 test procedures Optimum moisture is the water content
percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
Fill Materials
General fill materials required to raise the existing ground surface to the proposed
finished subgrades may consist of the excavated on site soils provided that they
_are free of vegetation deleterious materials and rock fragments greater than
3 inches in maximum dimension
Imported general fill materials needed for the site filling should consist of materials
with a low expansion potential Imported general fill materials should consist of

be free of deleterious materials The material should have a laboratory California

1 percent when tested in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1883

conformance with these recommendations prior to delivery to the project site for the
intended use

Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
Fill materials should be moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the
optimum moisture placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness
and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Relative
compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of
the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with
ASTM D 1557 test procedures Optimum moisture is the water content
percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
The aggregate base course materials should be moisture conditioned to above the
optimum moisture content placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose
thickness and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
Because moisture conditioning and compaction of the clayey subgrade soils and
general fill soils are critical elements of the earthwork observations and soil density
tests should be performed by Geolabs during the site grading operations to assist
the contractor in obtaining the required degree of compaction and the proper
moisture content on each fill lift Where compaction is less than required additional
compactive effort should be applied with adjustment of moisture content as
necessary to obtain the specified compaction It should be noted that the moisture
requirement of the general fills and subgrades at least 2 percent above the
optimum moisture is an important requirement considering the moderately
expansive nature of the clayey soils
Dewatering
The retaining wall foundations may be located below the existing groundwater
table Therefore we anticipate that dewatering may be necessary for the foundation
construction
In general the dewatering operation should be conducted in such a manner that
the dewatering will not cause areal ground subsidence which may cause potential
damage to the nearby existing structures Therefore consideration should be given toa
dewatering system that includes a deep cut off wall to reduce the volume of water to be
removed within the excavation and to reduce the areal extent of groundwater drawdown
outside of the trench excavation
Since the excavation dewatering may involve discharge of groundwater from the
dewatering operation into adjacent drainage systems a national pollutant Discharge
Elimination System NPDES permit may be necessary The contractor should consult
their independent consultant or the State of Hawaii Department of Health for the latest
regulations and information pertaining to the NPDES permit application

Subsoil Permeability
Based on our borings the soils encountered at the estimated wall footing
elevations consist of loose to medium dense sands The actual subsoil
permeability may range broadly and also vary locally in terms of orders of
magnitude The permeability of the subsoils at the site may be considered
moderately permeable based on the materials encountered Therefore the
contractor should pay special attention to the site specific dewatering plan for the
proposed excavations It should be noted that in situ permeability tests were not
conducted for this project
Dewatering Method
Dewatering for construction is the responsibility of the contractor The selection
of equipment and methods of dewatering should be left up to the contractor and
he she should be aware that modifications to the dewatering system may be
required during construction depending on the conditions encountered The
dewatering method selected should have minimal impact on the groundwater
level surrounding the proposed excavation As previously indicated the
underlying alluvial materials at this site may be moderately permeable and are
capable of transmitting moderate quantities of water
It is our opinion that the definition of Dewatering in the contract documents
should be written to include all works or systems required to lower the natural
groundwater table and or to exclude the water from the excavations to allow
construction of the proposed structures under safe and dry conditions These
works or systems may include but are not limited to grouting cut off walls
tremie concrete plugs or any combination of the above and or other possible
methods
It should be noted that the subsoil conditions at the site consist of loose gravels and
sands which are considered to be moderately permeable soils Because the
proposed project is located in a developed area the dewatering operation should
be conducted in such a manner that the dewatering will not cause areal ground
subsidence which may cause potential damage to the existing structures and
utilities Therefore consideration should be given to a dewatering system that
includes a cut off wall

suitable method of dewatering
a The dewatering method should result in the least disturbance or damage

Dewatering Precaution and Monitoring
The potential impact of the dewatering system selected on depressing the natural
groundwater table must be carefully evaluated by the contractor prior to
dewatering It is recommended that a qualified geotechnical engineer be
retained by the contractor to design and evaluate the dewatering system used
The impact and safety of the dewatering operations should be made the sole
responsibility of the contractor His her qualified representative who should be
required to be continuously present on site during dewatering activities will have
the best opportunity to promptly observe the effects of dewatering during
construction and to implement as soon as possible necessary precautionary or
remedial measures including but not limited to slowing down or stopping the
dewatering operations
Where encountered at the bottom of excavations permeable granular subsoils
may be susceptible to piping and quick conditions The dewatering operations
should be carried out without creating a quick condition or softening at the
excavation bottoms Therefore the project dewatering operations should be
performed without pumping out soil fines pumping clear water only and should
be coordinated with shoring installation such that the excavation stability is not
adversely affected Excessive pumping which removes soil fines may result in
a blowing or heaving of the excavation bottom or sides
