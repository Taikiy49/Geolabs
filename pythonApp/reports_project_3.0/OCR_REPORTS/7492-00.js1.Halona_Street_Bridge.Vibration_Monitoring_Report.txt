This technical memorandum presents our Vibration Monitoring Summary Report 
for vibration monitoring performed between March 17 and May 15, 2017 at the Halona 
Street Bridge project. The vibration monitoring was performed in accordance with 
Specification Section 551.03 (d). In accordance with Specification Section 551 – Driven 
Piles, vibration monitoring is required to be performed during the pile driving work within a 
75-foot radius of pile location. 
Vibration Monitoring Results  
Vibration monitoring was performed using Instantel Minimate Plus units equipped 
with geophone sensors and telemetry to monitor vibrations on a near-continuous basis. 
Vibrations were monitored at five locations designated as VM-1, VM-2, VM-3, VM-4, 
and VM-5 as shown on the Site Plan, Plate 1. Vibration monitoring units VM-1 and VM-2 
were mounted on the east and west sides of the Kapalama Canal at the H-1 freeway 
bridge. Vibration monitoring units VM-3 and VM-4 were mounted on the east and west 
side Kapalama Canal CRM walls. In addition, vibration monitoring unit VM-4 was 
relocated to VM-5 when pile driving activities occurred on the east side of the Halona 
Street Bridge. Vibration monitoring location VM-5 is located at the corner of Halona 
Street and Kokea Street. 
Each Instantel Minimate Plus and/or Micromate unit consists of a 3-component 
geophone and a signal conditioner/recorder unit. The geophone senses ground motion 
along three mutually perpendicular (orthogonal) axes; therefore, it provides detection of 
ground motion in the longitudinal, vertical, and transverse directions. The signal 
conditioner/recorder unit receives and processes these signals to provide a graphic 
record of the vibrations. Resultant peak particle velocities - peak vector sum (PPV PVS) 
in units of inches per second are automatically computed by vector summation of the 
motions along the orthogonal axes. The data is stored to the conditioner/recorder unit’s 
onboard memory and sent to a server via remote cellular service. 
The Minimate Plus and/or Micromate units are set at a scanning interval of 
15 minutes and continuously monitor vibrations 24 hours per day. During the 15-minute 
scanning interval, the Minimate Plus samples the vibration data at a rate of 1,024 data 
points per second and stores the resultant peak level of vibration, which occurs during

Vibration limits did not reach or exceeded the specified thresholds during the 
monitoring period presented in this report with the exception of three alarm events 
occurring at VM-3 on April 27th, 28th, and May 4th, 2017, with peak particle velocities of 
0.9 inches per second, 0.6 inches per second, and 1.21 inches per second, 
respectively. Based on information provided by the contractor, the alarm events were 
likely due to someone unintentionally bumping into the monitoring unit.
