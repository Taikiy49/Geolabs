PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located within the existing Haena State Park in the Haena area on
the Island of Kauai Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown
on the Project Location Map Plate 1
Based on the information provided we understand that it is proposed to replace the
existing cesspool out of service with a shallow on site sewage treatment and disposal
system using septic tank and leach field Therefore it was desired to conduct field
percolation tests at the proposed leach field location in order to obtain data pertaining to the
preliminary design of the sewage disposal system The approximate limits of the proposed
leach field are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Structural details for the proposed septic tank and leach field were not available at the
time this report was prepared In general we anticipate that the bottom of the absorption
trenches will be located at depths of approximately 3 to 5 feet below the existing ground
surface Based on our knowledge and experience in the vicinity of the project site we
anticipate that the project site may be underlain by beach sand deposits at shallow depths

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our field exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions at the proposed septic tank and leach field location and to perform field
percolation tests to evaluate the subsoil infiltration characteristics for the formulation of
preliminary recommendations pertinent to the design of the disposal components of the
on site sewage disposal system The work was performed in general accordance with our
fee proposal dated Our scope of work included the following tasks and
work efforts
1 Review of available in house soils and geologic information pertinent to the
project site and its vicinity
2 Augering of five borings to depths of about 5 to 7 feet below the existing
ground surface to evaluate the subsurface soil profiles at the proposed leach
field location
3 Coordination of the field exploration and logging of the borings by an engineer
from our firm
4 Performance of six field percolation tests to evaluate the subsoil infiltration
characteristics for the formulation of preliminary recommendations pertinent
to the design of the disposal components of the on site sewage disposal
system
5 Laboratory testing of selected soil samples obtained during the field exploration
as an aid in classifying the materials encountered and evaluating their
engineering properties
6 Preparation of this letter report summarizing our work on the project and
presenting our findings and discussions
7 Coordination of our overall work on the project and client design team
consultation by a principal engineer of our firm
8 Miscellaneous work efforts such as drafting word processing and clerical
support

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our field exploration program consisted of augering five borings designated as Boring
Nos 1 through 5 to depths of about 5 to 7 feet below the existing ground surface The
approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general our field exploration across the proposed leach field area encountered
granular materials consisting of medium dense sandy soils throughout the majority of the
depths explored A relatively thin surficial layer of sandy silts was encountered in Boring
Nos 2 through 4 extending to a depth of about 2 feet below the existing ground surface
Groundwater was not encountered in the drilled borings at the time of our field
exploration Due to the proximity of the project site to the shoreline the water levels
measured in the borings during our field exploration were likely affected by tidal fluctuations
In addition the water levels at the project site may be influenced by seasonal precipitation
and storm surge conditions Detailed descriptions of the materials encountered in our field
exploration are presented on the Logs of Borings Plates 4 through 8 Gradation tests were
performed on selected samples of the sandy soils encountered and the test results are
presented on Plate 9
FINDINGS AND DISCUSSIONS
We understand that on site sewage treatment and disposal by septic tank and leach
field method are planned at the existing comfort station area within the Haena State Park
site Therefore we have conducted field percolation tests to obtain data pertaining to the
preliminary design of the sewage disposal system A total of six field percolation tests were
performed at selected locations across the project site The approximate locations of the
field percolation tests designated as B 1 through B 5 are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Field Percolation Testing
Field percolation testing was performed in general accordance with the State of
Systems and Chapter 10 of the Ten States Standards to evaluate the permeability of the
subsurface soils for wastewater disposal The percolation tests were performed by augering
4 0 inch diameter boreholes to depths of about 5 to 7 feet below the existing ground surface
Approximately one inch of gravel was then placed at the bottom of the boreholes to
protect the bottom of the holes from scouring and sediments During testing the holes were
carefully filled with water to a minimum depth of 6 inches above the gravel pack The time
intervals and water drops were then recorded to provide data upon which the calculation of
the percolation tests was based

Based on our field exploration and laboratory testing it appears that the proposed
leach field location is generally underlain by sandy subsoils extending to depths of about 5 to
7 feet below the existing ground surface Based on the percolation test results obtained we
believe that the subsoils in this area are relatively permeable and should be suitable for the
siting of the proposed leach field sewage disposal system Due to the potential variability of
the subsoil conditions the absorption capacity of the disposal system should be confirmed
by additional percolation tests during construction
LIMITATIONS
The findings and discussions submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from the field borings and percolation tests Variations of subsoil
conditions between and beyond the borings may occur and the nature and extent of these
variations may not become evident until construction is underway If variations then appear
evident it will be necessary to reevaluate the findings and discussions provided in this report
The locations of the field borings indicated in this report are approximate having been
estimated by taping from the features shown on the Topographic Survey Map dated
prepared by ControlPoint Surveying Inc Elevations of the field borings were
estimated by interpolation from the elevation points shown on the same map The physical
locations and elevations of the borings should be considered accurate only to the degree
implied by the methods used
The stratification lines shown on graphic representations of the borings depict the
approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual transition
Water level data from the borings were measured at the times shown on the graphic
representations and or in the text of this report These data have been reviewed and
interpretations made in the formulation of this report However it must be noted that
fluctuation may occur due to variation in rainfall tides temperature and other factors
