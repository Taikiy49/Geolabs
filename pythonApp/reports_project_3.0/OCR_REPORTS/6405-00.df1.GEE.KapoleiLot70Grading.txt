Regional Geology
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from the Waianae
and Koolau shield volcanoes The older Waianae Volcano is estimated to be middle to
late Pliocene in age and forms the bulk of the western one third of the island The
younger Koolau Volcano is estimated to be late Pliocene to early Pleistocene Ice Age
in age and forms the majority of the eastern two thirds of the island The Koolau
Volcano reached the end of its main shield building phase about 2 million years ago
The project site is situated on the Ewa Coastal Plain which extends seaward off
the southeastern flank of Waianae and the southwestern flank of Koolau This plain
was formed by the accumulation of a thick wedge of sediments and coral reef
formations on the flanks of the two volcanoes
During the Pleistocene Epoch sea levels fluctuated in response to the cycles of
continental glaciation As the glaciers grew and advanced less water was available to
fill the oceanic basins such that sea levels fell below the present stands of the sea
When the glaciers melted and receded an excess of water became available such that
the sea levels rose to above the present sea level
The higher sea level stands caused the formation of reef deposits and
accumulation of alluvial sediments at correspondingly higher elevations Subaerial
exposure of the sediments and calcareous materials caused consolidation of the
lagoonal and alluvial deposits and induration of the calcareous reef materials

SECTION 3 DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Our field exploration disclosed that the project site is generally underlain by a
surface layer of silty clay alluvial soil which extends to the maximum depths explored
of 7 5 to 9 0 feet below the existing ground surface We did not encounter groundwater
in the test pits at the time of our field exploration
Our laboratory testing on the surface silty clay materials indicated moderate to
high expansion potential Proper site preparation and earthwork procedures are
necessary to provide adequate support for future development
Since the site is currently lower than the surrounding ground mass grading will
consist of filling 6 to 8 feet Prior to the placement of fill we recommend scarifying the
entire site to a depth of 12 inches below the existing grade moisture conditioning to
about 2 percent above optimum moisture content and recompacting to a minimum of
90 percent relative density
The recompacted soil should be protected from drying prior to the placement of
new fill to reduce the potential for formation of shrinkage cracks If shrinkage cracks do
appear the area should be re scarified moisture conditioned and compacted as
recommended above
Select structural fill material should be used for the top of 5 feet of the site mass
grading This structural fill material should consist of non expansive granular material
such as crushed coral basalt or cinder sand The material should be well graded from
coarse to fine with particles no larger than 3 inches in largest dimension and should
contain between 10 and 30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve The material
should have a laboratory CBR value of 20 or more and should have a maximum swell of
less than 1 percent when tested in accordance with ASTM D 1883 Below 5 feet from
finished grade the imported fill may consist of borrow soils less than 6 inches in size
with a minimum CBR of 8 and swell less than 2 percent when tested in accordance with
ASTM D 1883

Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork areas within the contract grading limits should be
cleared and grubbed thoroughly Vegetation debris deleterious material and
other unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site or
stockpiled in a designated area to reduce the potential for contamination of the
excavated materials

12 inches feet below the existing grade moisture conditioning to about 2 percent
above optimum moisture content and recompacting to a minimum of 90 percent
relative density Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil
expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil

content percentage by weight corresponding to the maximum dry density We
wish to emphasize that moisture conditioning of the fill materials is an integral part
of the site preparation recommendations provided herein Compaction should be

Keying and Benching
The ground surface at the project site is nearly level however there may be some
localized areas with steeper slopes Therefore this section is provided to give
guidance in design and construction of grading for those areas Keys and benches
required should be firmly seated into existing ground
At the toe of fills on ground where the existing slope is steeper than five horizontal
to one vertical 5H 1V a keyway should be cut down into the in situ material This
key should be a minimum of 2 feet in depth at the toe of the fill and should be a
minimum of 3 feet in width extending back under the toe of the fill
As fill placement progresses up the existing slope benches should be cut into the
existing slope to bond the mass of the fill to the existing ground

Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
Fills and backfills should be moisture conditioned to about 2 percent above the
optimum moisture placed in level lifts not exceeding 10 inches in loose thickness
and compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction
refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum
dry density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557
Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by weight corresponding to the
maximum dry density We wish to emphasize that moisture conditioning of the fill
materials is an integral part of the fill placement recommendations provided herein
Compaction should be accomplished by sheepsfoot rollers vibratory rollers or
other types of acceptable compaction equipment
Because moisture conditioning and compaction of the subgrade soils are critical
elements of earthwork observations and soil density tests should be performed by
Geolabs during site grading to assist the contractor in obtaining the required degree
of compaction and the proper moisture content Where compaction is less than
required additional compactive effort should be applied with adjustment of moisture
content as necessary to obtain the specified compaction It should be noted that
the moisture requirement of the fills and subgrades about 2 percent above the
optimum moisture is an important requirement for the use of the on site clayey
soils
Cut and Fill Slopes
As discussed earlier it is anticipated that the site mass grading will generally
involve placement of fill to match the grades of the surrounding properties
However this section is provided to give guidance for design and construction of
slopes in the event that they may be required Based on the subsurface conditions

encountered in the test pits it appears that cut slopes required for this project would
expose soils consisting of stiff to very stiff silty clay In general we believe that cut
slopes into the soil material may be cut at an inclination of 2H 1V or flatter
Fill slopes should be designed with a slope inclination of 2H 1V or flatter Fills

slope planting should be provided as soon as possible to reduce the potential for

Laboratory Tests
Moisture Content ASTM D 2216 determinations were performed on selected
soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties The results
of these tests are presented on the Logs of Test Pits
One Atterberg Limits test ASTM D 4318 was performed on a selected soil
sample to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits and to aid in soil classification Results
of the test are summarized on the Logs of Test Pits at the appropriate sample depth
Graphic presentation of the test result is provided on Plate B 1
One Modified Proctor compaction test ASTM D 1557 A was performed on a
bulk sample of the near surface soils and potential borrow material to evaluate the dry
density and moisture content relationships The test result is presented on Plate B 2
One laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR test ASTM D 1883 was
performed on a bulk sample of the near surface soils to evaluate the pavement support
characteristics of the soils The test results are presented on Plate B 3
One Sieve Analysis test ASTM C 117 C 136 was performed on a selected
soil sample to evaluate the gradation characteristics of the soils and to aid in soil
classification A graphic presentation of the grain size distribution is provided on
Plate B 4
Two one inch Ring Swell tests were performed on selected soil samples to
evaluate the swelling potential of the near surface soils The test results are
summarized on Plate B 5
