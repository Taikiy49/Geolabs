In accordance with our service agreement, we observed the static load test, test 
pile installation, and production pile installation for the augered cast-in-place (ACIP) pile 
foundations for the Block C, Waiea project. This letter report summarizes our geotechnical 
observations during the ACIP pile foundation installation for the Block C, Waiea project. 
Geolabs provided geotechnical engineering consultation services for the Block C, 
Waiea project. The purpose of our geotechnical observations was to monitor the 
geotechnical engineering aspects of the ACIP pile foundation installation for conformance 
with the plans and specifications and to provide subsequent recommendations, as 
appropriate. In addition, we provided “Special Inspection” services (as required by the City 
and County of Honolulu) by observing the ACIP pile foundation installation for general 
conformance with the project plans and specifications and subsequent recommendations 
and amendments. 
PROJECT DESCRIPTION 
The Block C, Waiea project site is situated at the southwest corner of the Auahi and 
Kamakee Street intersection in Honolulu on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. The project site 
was formerly an on-grade parking lot.  The project consists of constructing a new high-rise 
building with about 177 residential condominium units. The main tower structure will be 
located on the western side of the property. A five-story mid-rise structure located along 
the makai side of the property will house the new townhome units. The project will provide 
about 8,200 square feet of retail commercial space at the ground floor of the 6-story

The project consisted of 10 indicator piles (test piles) and 433 production piles for a

ACIP PILE LOAD TEST PROGRAM 
In order to evaluate the adequacy of the design length of the ACIP pile foundation, 
a load test pile program was implemented. The load test pile program included the 
installation and observation of four reaction piles and one instrumented load test pile 
(CT1). A static pile load test was conducted on the instrumented ACIP pile (CT1) on 
June 26 and 30, 2014. The pile load test was conducted on a 24-inch diameter ACIP pile 
with a length of approximately 110.1 feet relative to the existing ground surface.  
To better understand the load transfer mechanism at the concrete pile-soil 
interface, the load test pile was instrumented with ten vibrating wire embedment strain 
gages installed at various depths along the length of the load test pile. The load test and 
reaction piles were sacrificial piles and were not incorporated into the foundation system of 
the parking structure. The identification of the load test and reaction piles is shown on the 
Site Plan, Plate 1. 
A Manitowoc 999 series crane with crane-mounted hollow-stem auger, powered by 
a Berkel HD460 hydraulic pump was used to install the load test and reaction piles. 
Multiple hydraulic concrete pumps ranging from 0.031 to 0.061 cubic yards per stroke 
were used to pump the grout into the pile system. The load test pile was loaded in general 
accordance with ASTM D1143, Quick Load Test Method to a maximum test load of about 
2,400 kips (1,200 tons). The static pile load test results are summarized in the submittal by 
Berkel dated July 28, 2014. 
Based on the static pile load test results, it appears the load test pile behaved like a 
friction pile with the majority of the test load being resisted by adhesion between the ACIP 
pile surface and the surrounding soils. Based on our field observations and the results of 
the static pile load test, we confirmed the design embedment lengths for the 24” diameter 
piles could be shortened to 95 feet (from 105 feet) and the 18” piles could be shortened to 
75 feet (from 85 feet) below the existing ground surface to achieve the design 
compressive load capacity of 1,200 kips and 600 kips, respectively, per ACIP pile with a 
factor of safety of at least 2.0.

ACIP TEST PILE INSTALLATION 
An indicator pile (also known as a probe pile or test pile) program, which consisted 
of installing 10 ACIP piles, was conducted for the project from June 16 through 
June 18, 2014. The indicator piles were selected from the future production pile locations 
and are shown on the Site Plan, Plate 1. The purpose of the indicator pile program was to 
evaluate the subsurface conditions across the project site with respect to the selected 
ACIP equipment and the methods of ACIP pile installation.  
A summary of the indicator or test piles and our field observations is included in the 
Summary of ACIP Pile Foundations by Date, Plates 2.1 through 2.16 and Summary of 
ACIP Pile Foundations by Pile Number, Plates 3.1 through 3.16 of this report for reference. 
Based on the results of the indicator or test pile program, it appears that the selected ACIP 
equipment and the methods of ACIP pile installation are capable of installing the ACIP 
piles to the design lengths. 
PRODUCTION ACIP PILE INSTALLATION 
The production ACIP pile installation program, consisting of the installation of 
264 24-inch diameter ACIP piles and 165 18-inch diameter ACIP piles, was conducted 
from June 16 through August 15, 2014 under the near-continuous (full-time) observations 
by a Geolabs field representative. Berkel used the same drilling and grouting equipment 
utilized for the indicator (or test) pile program to install the production piles. Automatic 
monitoring equipment was mounted on the drilling and pumping equipment with the intent 
of providing auger depth, grout flow rate, grout pressure, etc., on a near-continuous basis. 
However, the equipment was not functioning reliably throughout the project, and manual 
monitoring was conducted by our field representative for the piles installed.  
During the pile installation operation, logs were maintained for each ACIP pile, 
which included information pertaining to pile number, date installed, depth of pile, grout 
volume used, grout return depth, etc. The identifications of the production ACIP piles 
installed are shown on the Site Plan, Plate 1. The production ACIP pile installation 
summary is presented by date on the attached Plates 2.1 through 2.16 and by pile number 
on the attached Plates 3.1 through 3.16. 
Based on our observation of the equipment performance and subsurface conditions 
encountered, it is our professional opinion that the production ACIP piles have been 
installed to sufficient depths and/or resistance to provide the design load capacities of 
1,200 kips (600 tons) per 24-inch diameter pile and 600 kips (300 tons) per 18-inch 
diameter pile with a safety factor of 2.0.
