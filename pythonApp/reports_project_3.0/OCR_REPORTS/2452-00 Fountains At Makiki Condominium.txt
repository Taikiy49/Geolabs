INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the proposed new condominium at
Street in the Makiki district on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general
location of the site is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The
exploration was performed in general accordance with our proposal dated

This report summarizes the findings of the exploration and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analyses based
on the data obtained during the exploration These recommendations are
intended for design input and are not intended for final construction
estimation or bidding purposes
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface conditions at the project site in order to develop a
generalized soil rock data set for the formulation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertinent to the foundation design of the
project
The work was done in general accordance with our proposal dated
The scope of work consisted of the following
1 The drilling and sampling of six 6 borings to depths between
36 5 and 46 5 feet below the existing ground surface
2 Reconnaissance of the project site and vicinity to make visual
observations of the surficial soil conditions
3 Review of available soils and geologic information pertinent to
the project site and its vicinity
4 Laboratory testing of selected samples obtained during the field
exploration to determine the engineering properties and
Classification of the materials encountered
5 Engineering analyses of the field and laboratory data for the
purpose of developing foundation and pavement recommendations
6 Preparation of this report presenting our findings and design
recommendations

surrounding the basement parking

SUBSOIL CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by
drilling and sampling six 6 borings to depths ranging between 36 5 to
46 5 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate locations of
the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general the field exploration encountered approximately a 2 5 to
4 5 foot thick fill crust of stiff to very stiff silty clay underlain by
layers of dense silty fine sand to stiff fine sandy silts cinder and ash
deposits In Boring 5 a 2 5 foot thick layer of medium dense clayey
sand with basalt gravel in lieu of silty clay were encountered in the
upper fill crust
Groundwater levels were encountered at approximate depths of 29 to 33
feet below the existing ground surface during our field exploration
Detailed description of the subsoil and groundwater conditions are
presented on the Boring Logs in Appendix A

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATTONS
General
The field exploration encountered approximately a 2 5 to 4 5 foot
thick stiff clayey fill crust underlain by layers of dense silty fine sand
to stiff fine sandy silt to the maximm depth of the drilled borings
which was 46 5 feet below the existing ground surface
l
It is our opinion that spread continuous footing foundations can he
used to support the proposed condominium building with a 1 level basement
Relatively easy ground excavation into the upper fill crust and
cinder ash deposits is anticipated for the proposed basement parking
excavation However due to the anticipated deep basement and footing
excavations with depths in excess of 10 feet we recommend that excavation
support system such as timber shoring or steel sheeting should be provided
per OSHA safety requirements to protect the safety of the construction
workers and adjacent ground due to caving in or lateral movements which
may jeopardize the integrity of the existing adjacent structures
Details of our recommendations are discussed in the following
sections
Foundations
Based on our field exploration and subsequent geotechnical
engineering analyses we recommend that spread or continuous footing
foundations may be used to support the proposed condominium structure An
allowable bearing capacity of up to 4 000 p s f can be used for footings
bearing on the in situ volcanic cinder deposit consisting of dense silty
fine sands to stiff sandy silts A minimm footing embedment of 24 inches
below the lowest adjacent finished grade is also recommended The bearing
value is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third when
temporary loads wind and seismic are considered
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2452 00 Page 6
The bottom of all footing excavations should be recompacted to obtain
a minimm of 95 maximum dry density per ASIM Test Method D 1557 78
If soft loose soil pockets were encountered at the bottom of the
future footing excavation it should be over excavated to stiff in situ
soil and replaced with granular select borrow compacted to a minimum of
95 maximum dry density
If a footing is located next to a utility line it should extend to
the bottom of the utility trench to reduce footing settlement due to
settlement of the trench backfill
Provided the above recommendations are followed estimated total
footing settlement under a column load of up to 400 kips is less than the
order of one 1 inch
Site Grading
Minimal site grading with generally less than one 1 foot cut or
fill is anticipated for the proposed project
Prior to the commencement of any earthwork all areas within the
grading limits and basement parking structure should be cleared and
grubbed of all rubbish and deleterious materials old foundations and
asphaltic concrete paveménts The resulting grub and spoil material
should be disposed of properly off site
Select on site material such as the excavated silty sand from
basement excavation could be stockpiled and re used as select fill or
subsequent footing and basement backfill material However the upper
silty clay fill is moderately to highly expansive and it should not be
reused as fill or backfill material
The fill and backfill should be placed in maximm 10 inch thick loose
level lifts and compacted to a minimm of 95 maximm dry density as
established by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2452 00 Page 7
Imported select fill material should be non expansive and have less
than 20 fines passing the 200 sieve
The finished grade should be shaped to shed water away from
foundations and avoid ponding condition
Basement Excavation
Based on the current design concept a large and deep excavation
extending down to a maximum depth of about 13 feet below the existing
ground surface will be required for the proposed basement parking level
construction
It is imperative that the deep excavation be adequately supported
during construction to minimize the potential of adjacent ground loss or
movements which may jeopardize the safety of the construction workers
and or the structural integrity of the existing adjacent residential
structures We recommend that a thorough study of the deep excavation
support system be required from the selected contractor and that qualified
consultants be retained by the contractor to help him evaluate the
excavation support design s before construction and also to monitor the
actual installation of the basement excavation support system during
construction
Relatively easy excavation of the subsoils is anticipated The
method and equipment used for excavation should be the choice of the
contractor within practical limits and safety considerations
In order to reduce the potential of ground movement or subsidence
excavated material should not be stockpiled within 15 feet on top of the
excavation
GEOLABS HAWAII

It is our opinion that continuous interlocking steel sheetpiles or
timber shoring may be used to support the anticipated deep excavation
Sloping ground excavation when space is available and or construction of
the basement wall in small sections may also be considered as means to
minimize possible trench failure in the basement wall excavation
Major dewatering is not anticipated for the proposed basement
excavation since groundwater levels encountered in our drilled borings are
approximately 29 to 33 feet below the existing ground surface which are
more than 15 feet below the bottom elevation of the proposed basement
parking level

Well graded granular material such as base course rock sand or

A subsurface drainage system should also be utilized behind the walls

properly constructed to drain water away from the walls
Lateral Resistance
Resistance to lateral loads such as short duration wind loads acting
on the new superstructure may be obtained by passive earth pressure
developed against the perimeter basement walls of the proposed building
A passive earth pressure of 300 p s f per foot of depth may be used In
addition a base friction factor of 0 4 may also be used to determine the
resistance against sliding
Design Review
Final construction plans and specifications for the proposed
construction should be forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for updated
review evaluation and written comments prior to construction This
review is needed to determine adherence to the earthwork and foundation
recommendations given herein If this review is not made the_
geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for misinterpretation
of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during earthwork construction
basement and footing excavation to monitor the contractor s geotechnical
operations This is to observe compliance with the design concepts
specifications or recommendations and to allow design changes in the event

Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and

If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
LIMTTATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
upon information obtained from the field data points such as borings
Variations of conditions between the borings may occur and the nature
and extent of these variations may not become evident until construction
If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations given in this report
The locations of the borings were determined by taping from
structures indicated on the topographic survey by Harry K Matsuo dated
Elevations of the borings were approximately determined
by interpolation from elevation points on the topographic survey map The
physical location and elevation of the borings should be considered
accurate only to the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
boring points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types and
as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines showm on
profiles or cross sections are based upon interpolation between borings
and may not represent actual subsurface conditions

conformance with the Unified Soil Classification System A summary
of the materials encountered are presented on the Boring Logs
