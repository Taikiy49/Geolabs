INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our preliminary soil inves
tigation on the subject project The purpose of this investi
gation was to determine the nature of the soils underlying the
site to ascertain their engineering properties and to provide
results and or recommendation for foundations floor slabs
poring grading and sewage disposal percolation
PROJECT LOCATION AND SITE CONDITIONS
The proposed project consists of an addition to the existing
Kahuku Hospital facilities in Kahuku Oahu Hawaii The major
portion of the addition will be located immediately north of
the existing facilities while the remainder of the addition
will extend from or replace existing structures on the south
erly portion of the site
Page 2
W O 444 00
Present plans indicate that the main northerly addition may
ultimately be 3 stories plus a partial basement level due to
existing topography while the southerly addition will be
one story at grade
STRUCTURAL CONDITIONS
Information concerning the proposed development was furnished
by of Roger Lee Associates and Mr Harold
Miyamoto Structural Engineer
As indicated above the proposed structures are planned to be
of concrete construction with the maximum column load for
the northerly ultimate 4 story addition including basement
of up to 400 kips and maximum column load for southerly
one story building to be on the order of 100 kips The max
imum wall loads are anticipated to be on the order of 2 kips
per foot The proposed finished floor elevation of the nor
therly addition will be approximately elevation 19 3 while
the southerly addition will be approximately elevation 39 6
The difference in elevation between existing and proposed
grade for the northerly addition will necessitate a retaining
wall

FIELD EXPLORATION
The site was explored on December 26 and 27 1972 by drilling
six borings in building areas and one boring for percolation
testing Because of higher than originally anticipated foun
dation loads supplied by the structural engineer an additional
deep boring B 7 was also drilled on The
borings ranged in depth from 16 3 to 41 6 The location
of all borings are shown in Plate 1 and the boring logs for
all borings are included with this report as Appendix A
The explorations were undertaken with a B 40 and B 61 truck
mounted rotary drill rig and continuous flight augers
In all borings Standard Penetration Tests were made at regular
intervals The Standard Penetration Test consists of driving
a 2 inch O D split spoon sampler 18 inches with a 140 pound
hammer free falling a distance of 30 inches The number of
blows required to drive the samples the last 12 inches is
termed the Standard Penetration Resistance N and is an
approximate measure of the relative density or consistency of
a soil These resistance values are plotted on the boring
logs Samples obtained were visually classified in the field
Page 4
W O 444 00
in accordance with the Unified Soil Classification The repre
sentative material was placed in air tight containers and re
turned to our laboratory for inspection and testing In
addition selective undisturbed ring and disturbed bulk bag
samples were obtained and returned to our laboratory for further
testing Obtained laboratory data is contained in Appendix B
Laboratory Testing and on the Boring Logs
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The Kahuku Hospital site is underlain by thin bedded and cross
bedded calcareous sandstone composed of uniform fine calcareous
grains and coral fragments blown and washed inland from ancient
beaches and later lithified The action of rainwater mixing
with rotting vegetation has resulted in slightly acid ground
water which percolated downward through the dunes This
groundwater has both dissolved solution cavities in the depo
sits and redeposited some of the calcium carbonate in the
intersticies of the sand grains These processes have re
sulted in a generally hard but locally porous deposit small
voids
Page 5
W O 444 00
Soils
Generally the soils underlying the site consist first of a sur
ficial layer of brown silty clay to clayey silt with coral frag
ments and sand The thickness of this surficial layer in
exploratory borings vary from 2 5 to 11 feet depending upon
location Beneath this surficial layer tan to white somewhat
lithified calcareous sand with some coral fragments were en
countered Generally these sandy materials become dense to
very dense at depths of from 10 to 15 feet below existing grade
Relatively smaller standard penetration blow counts were ob
served for several of the samples indicating the possibility
of relatively isolated soft spots or small voids Generally
blow counts for material below groundwater table were somewhat
lower than obtained for the material above groundwater table
Groundwater
Groundwater was encountered in Boring No s 1 7 and 8 at
depths of 24 26 and 11 feet respectively The encountered
groundwater should not have any adverse effect on construction
of the proposed buildings

W O 444 00
PERCOLATION TESTING
For the percolation testing Boring B 8 was drilled to a depth
of 35 feet using a 9 diameter auger Groundwater was encoun
tered at a depth of 11 feet To saturate the hole water was
initially added immediately after drilling on December 27
1972 No rise in the groundwater was noted during the initial
saturation attempt The hole was left open overnight for ap
proximately 24 hours Water was then added from the water
truck discharge system 1 hose gravity feed The maximum
calculated rate of this system is approximately 30 gallons per
minute Again no measurable rise in the groundwater was noted
indicating that the minimum percolation rate of the sand coral
stratum encountered is at least 30 gallons per minute for 9
diameter hole Since the actual capacity of the subsurface
materials was higher than the truck feed water system the
above minimum percolation rate may be somewhat conservative
However the percolation characteristics of the subsurface soils
are generally excellent Technical recommendations are covered
below under Conclusions and Recommendations

CONCLUSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the results of the field investigations laboratory
testings and engineering analyses the following conclusions
and recommendations are presented The foundation recommen
dations are based on anticipated high loads and alternatives
may be feasible if the actual design loads are of much smaller
magnitude If it is desired to investigate alternatives we
should be consulted when more accurate prediction of founda
tion loads are available from the structural engineer
A General
The subsurface conditions encountered during the investi
gation were relatively good The proposed development is
feasible from an engineering standpoint provided the
recommendations included in this report are followed
It is our professional opinion that the onsite calcareous
sand with coral fragments underlying clayey silty soils
in general is capable of supporting the proposed fill and
structures

Unsurcharged retaining walls with horizontal backfill less
than 12 feet high should be designed for an equivalent
fluid pressure of 35 pounds per cubic foot provided only
moderate compaction of backfill is required If a high
degree of backfill compaction is required or the walls can
not yield even slightly an equivalent fluid pressure of
60 pounds per cubic foot should be used Adequate surface
and subsurface drainage should be provided behind the re
taining walls

Concrete Floor Slab On Grade
The surface one foot of soil in the building pad should be
scarified watered and compacted to a minimum of 90 of
the maximum dry density The finish subgrade should be
covered with a 4 thick layer of clean sand below the slab
A moisture barrier consisting of polyvinyl chloride mem
brane should be provided under the slab areas which will
receive covering such as carpeting or tiles one inch
layer of clean sand above the membrane will protect the
membrane during construction The concrete floor slab on
grade should be a minimum of 4 nominal thickness and be
reinforced with a minimum of 6 X 6 10 X 10 welded wire
mesh Any very heavy machinery supported on floor slabs
should be located in sand with coral areas or will re
quire special preparation

Laboratory Testing
LABORATORY TESTING
The laboratory testing program was developed to establish the
engineering properties of the soils and rocks encountered at
the project site
e
1 Classification Tests
a Visual Classification All soil samples obtained from
the borings were brought to our laboratory where they were
visually reclassified to confirm or modify the field classi
fication prior to finalizing the Logs of Borings
b Water Content Determinations In addition to visual
classification typical samples were tested for natural
water content as an aid in soil classification and in
evaluating soil properties The water content values are
based upon the dry weight of the soil
c Atterberg Limits Atterberg limit tests were performed
on selected clay and silt samples obtained from the borings
for the purposes of identification and correlation of soils
Standard procedures ASTM D 424 for the plastic limit and
ASTM D 423 for the liquid limit were used in the performance
of these tests
d Grain Size Analysis Sieve analysis were performed on
granular soils For fine grained soils the hydrometer
GEOLABS HAWAII I INC
test was performed to determine the distribution of the
grain sizes beyond the No 200 sieve
Shear Strength Tests
a Unconfined Compression Tests Intact core and split
spoon soil samples which apparently exhibited high plasticity
were tested for unconfined strength Length to diameter
ratio of 2 was maintained throughout the tests to avoid
scatter of test results and to facilitate evaluation of
shear strength values for design purposes
b Direct Shear Test In general soil samples were tested
for shear strength using a 2 41 I D direct shear box Each
set consisted of at least two tests each test run under
different normal loads The results are plotted as Normal
Stress vs Shear Strength with a failure envelope drawn to
determine values of cohesion c and angle of frictional
resistance g
c Consolidation Tests Consolidation tests were performed
on selected undisturbed ring samples of the compressible
soils to provide basic data for making settlement calculations
Porous stones were placed on the top and bottom of the samples
to allow drainage Vertical loads were applied in increments
with each load increment being allowed to consolidate prior
to adding the next increment Measurements of the time and
consolidation were obtained during each load increment and
rebound was measured during the unloading portion Consoli
dation test results are plotted in terms of percent settlement
versus applied loads
d Swell Tests The ring sample was placed between porous
stones and a 100 psf load was applied to the sample for a
24 hour period The difference between the initial and final
sample heights is the amount of swell which is expressed as
a percentage of the initial height of the sample
e Maximum Density Test Samples of soil are compacted in
a mold of given size with a 10 pound rammer dropped 18 inches
to determine the relationship between moisture content and
density of the soil At least three trial points at different
moisture content are run to determine the maximum density
and optimum moisture content The test is run in accordance
with ASTM D 1557 70

Preparation of Fill Areas
All areas upon which fill is to be placed after clearing
as outlined in Section 1 of these specifications shall
be scarified until free of uneven features to a depth
as determined by the soils engineer and watered and
compacted according to Section 4 of these specifications

Compaction Equipment
The soils engineer shall determine the type of compacting
equipment which will attain the specified results in the
most efficient manner Sheepsfoot vibratory or pneumatic
tire rollers may be used in the test section and the equip
ment which produces the specified results in the most
expedient manner as determined by the soils engineer shall
be employed by the contractor The equipment used in roll
ing shall be in good working condition fully ballasted
and self cleaning Fill material placed in an unsatis
factory condition and not within the enclosed specifications
shall be rejected by the soils engineer and the contractor
shall rework the fill placed such that the specifications
are followed
Removal and Backfill of Underground Structures
Any underground structures such as cesspools cisterns
septic tanks wells pipe lines etc shall be removed

the excavation shall be in accordance with these specifi

Supervision of Earthwork
Field density tests Spall be made by the soils engineer
during the earthwork operation such that he may certify
that the fill was placed according to accepted specifica
tions In the event that field density tests of a layer
or any portion thereof is less than the required density
the particular layer or portion shall be reworked until
the required density is obtained
