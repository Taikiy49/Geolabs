Methodology of Pavement Design
The pavement structural section presented in this letter was generally
determined based on the methodology described in Chapter 3 of the Pavement
Design Manual dated March 2002 The Pavement Design Manual was prepared
by the State of Hawaii Department of Transportation Highways Division
Materials Testing and Research Branch The pavement design methodology is
based on the Hveem Stabilometer method developed and used by the California
Department of Transportation Caltrans

Design Subgrade Conditions
Based on the grading concept provided we understand that the entire site will be
filled with select granular materials such as coral Therefore we assume that the
pavement subgrade soils will consist of select granular materials with a laboratory
CBR value of 20 or more and a maximum swell of less than 1 percent Based on
these assumptions a design R value of 60 was adopted in our pavement analyses
for the select granular fill materials

pavement subgrade level i e bottom of the aggregate base course layer The
pavement subgrade soils should be scarified to a depth of at least 8 inches
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a
minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place
dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same
soil established in accordance with AASHTO T 180 or ASTM D 1557 Optimum
moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum
