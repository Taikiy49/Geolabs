Dear Mr Fukumoto
This letter report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations performed
during the foundation probing and grouting operations for Retaining Wall Nos 3 and 4 for
the Kawailii Stream Bridge Replacement project between and
The foundation probing and grouting that was observed consisted of additional probe
holes for Retaining Wall Nos 3 and 4 structures
The project location is along the portion of Hawaii Belt Road Route 19 at the
Kawailii Stream Crossing in the District of Hamakua on the Island of Hawaii as shown on
the Project Location Map Plate 1 The existing bridge structure traversing Kawailii Stream
was damaged as a result of the earthquake and a new replacement
bridge was designed
Because of potential subsurface cavities and or voids within the underlying basalt
rock formation a probing and grouting program to locate and grout potential cavities
beneath foundations was specified for the project The probing and grouting work for the
initial probe holes shown on the drawings were monitored by the construction manager
Bowers Kubota BK We understand that the volume of grout placed for each probe
hole was not measured as specified in the contract documents except for the probe holes
for Retaining Wall No 4
Wesley R Segawa Associates

Based on the grout volumes recorded by the contractor and BK for Retaining Wall
Nos 3 and 4 additional probe holes were recommended for Retaining Wall Nos 3 and 4
For Retaining Wall No 4 additional probe holes were drilled adjacent to the scheduled
probe holes with grout intakes greater than about 4 times the theoretical volume of the
hole All four of the scheduled probe holes had grout intakes greater than 4 times the
theoretical volume of the hole Therefore 26 additional probe holes were drilled
Approximately 2 03 cubic yards of grout was used to fill the additional probe holes
For Retaining Wall No 3 the number of additional probe holes that could be drilled
were limited since the wall footing had been poured The additional probe holes
performed for Retaining Wall No 3 were determined by BK and the decision to proceed
with work at Retaining Wall No 3 was provided by BK The approximate locations and
identification of the additional probe holes performed for Retaining Wall Nos 3 and 4 are
shown on the attached Probe Hole Identification Plan Plate 2
Isemoto Contracting Co Ltd performed the probing and grouting work for the
additional probe holes The additional probe holes were drilled with an air track mounted
drill rig The probe holes drilled were about 3 inches in diameter and extended to a depth
of at least 10 feet below the bottom of footing elevations A summary of our foundation
probe drilling and grouting observations is provided on Plate 3
The materials encountered during the additional probe drilling operations generally
consisted of basalt rock and saprolitic materials After completion of the probe drilling
operations the probe holes were filled with pumpable Controlled Low Strength Material
CLSM The CLSM grout was pumped into the probe holes using a trailer piston pump
In summary the additional probing and grouting performed for Retaining Wall No 4
structure for the project was conducted in general conformance with the intent of the
project specifications Based on our observations it is our professional opinion that the
relatively small voids and or cavities encountered at the structure foundation for Retaining
Wall No 4 have been grouted and that the subgrade area should provide the
recommended foundation support for the structure foundation for Retaining Wall No 4
