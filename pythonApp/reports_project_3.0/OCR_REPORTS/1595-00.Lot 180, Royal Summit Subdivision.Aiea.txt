In accordance with your request we have made a study of the near
surface soil conditions at the above referenced lot in order to provide
foundation recommendations for the proposed residence The study
consisted of drilling three shallow borings performing laboratory
testing and reviewing the subdivision site grading and preliminary house
foundation plans This report summarizes cur findings and recommenda
tions
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
Based on the preliminary house plans the proposed residence will
be one story structure approximately 46 by 78 feet in plan dimensions
with a 2 car garage

SITE CONDITIONS
The subject lot is on the southwestern side of Kaahele Street in
the Reval Summit Phase III Subdivision located in Aiea Oahu Hawaii
Most of the lot is a relatively flat pad which is about 2 to 3 feet
higher than the adjacent roads
A review of the site grading plans for the subdivision indicates
that this lot required cuts of up to 5 feet high during the recent
subdivision grading operation

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The hard reddish brown clayey silt soil encountered generally can
provide suitable foundation support for the proposed house previded that
some care is exercised during construction to minimize the swelling
potential of the soil
Laboratory tests performed on the stiff on site clayey soil
indicates that this material generally has low to moderate swelling
potential Therefore to minimize effects due to soil swelling under
on grade slabs we recommend that the on site soil be capped with 12
inches of non expansive select fill and that the subgrade sciis
underneath the select fill and slab be moistened and protected from
drying from the start of construction until the slab concrete placement
House Foundations
It is anticipated that the entire house will be of slab on grade
construction

Spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the proposed
residence An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f may be used on the
compacted soil or stiff natural ground
Interior footings on level areas should be embedded a minimum of 8
inches below the finished grade Perimeter wall footings should be
embedded a minimum of 12 inches below the adjacent finished grade
Surface water should be diverted away from foundations
Slab On Grade
It should be noted that some of the on site clayey silt soils are
moderately expansive and are marginally suitable to provide direct slab
support Therefore the on site soil should be over excavated down to a
depth of 12 inches below the slab bottom thoroughly moistened and then
replaced with non expansive imported scils as previcusly discussed The
thickened edge of the slab should be embedded a minimum of 12 inches
below the lowest adjacent finish grade
To further reduce future post construction swelling of the
underlying expansive soils we recommend that the in place clayey silt
below the upper capping fill pad be kept moist for one week prior to
concrete placement heavily soaked with water for the first two days and
sprinkled with water for the remainder of the week

A 4 inch layer of 3B Fine gravel cushion fill should be provided
below the slab In order to protect against future moisture
infiltration an impervious moisture membrane may be incorporated below
the slab The on grade slabs should be structurally separated from wall
foundations
Interior footings on level areas should be embedded a minimum of
inches below the finished grade Perimeter wall footings should be
embedded a minimum of 12 inches below the adjacent finished grade
Surface water should be diverted away from foundations
Concrete Driveway
Since the driveway can tolerate some amount of heaving the on site
soil over excavation and replacement may be eliminated if some small slab
cracking is tolerable The subgrade soils should be maintained in a
moist condition as described above and prevented from drying out prior
to concrete placement
Joints are reconmended at about 8 foot intervals to accommodate
some differential movements

Site Grading
Fills should consist of select non expansive soil such as crusher
waste or coral
Fills and backfills should be placed in maximum 8 inch lifts and
compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density established by
ASTM D 1557 78
Prior to filling the area should be cleared of vegetation and
debris
Lot Drainage
The finish lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings should
be shaped to shed water away from the foundations and to avoid ponding
condition Also the owners should be advised to install a gutter system
around the house and they should also be made aware that gutter water
should be diverted far away from the perimeter footings and walkways
Excessive landscape watering near the foundations and slab should also be
avoided
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils

These drainage requirements are essential for the proper
performance of the above recommended foundations since ponded water
could cause excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent loss of
strength or heaving The foundation excavations should be properly
backfilled tight against slab footing immediately after setting of the
concrete to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained
to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundation
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is reconmended that the footing excavations and backfill
operations be observed by the soils engineer during construction to see
that adequate bearing has been cbhtained and that the subsurface
conditions are consistent with the findings of our subsurface
investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above inspections
