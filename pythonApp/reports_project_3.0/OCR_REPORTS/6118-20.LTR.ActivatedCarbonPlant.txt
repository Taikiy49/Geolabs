Project Considerations
The project site is at 6 7 in the Kawaihae area on the Island of Hawaii It
is desired to construct a new activated carbon plant facility within 2 acres of the 13 acre
parcel Based on the information provided the major structures for the facility consist of
a char reactor activation kiln boiler unit cooling tower laboratory control center and
maintenance shed We anticipate other minor structures pavements and underground
utility lines will be required
Based on the information provided it is desired to perform resistivity surveys using
the Wenner 4 Pin Method for the design of grounding grids for the various structures
The Wenner 4 Pin Method provides an average value of the subsurface resistivity over the
tested depth The resistivity survey was conducted in accessible areas at the six specified
locations requested Detailed discussions and the field resistivity test results are
presented in the following sections of this letter report

Field Survey
Geophysical survey equipment i e ground resistance testing equipment was
mobilized to the project site for the field resistivity survey A total of 73 resistivity tests
were conducted at six selected locations designated as R 101 through R 106 using the
in Pin Method and an AEMC Model 4610 ground resistance testing
equipment manufactured by AEMC Instruments The method utilizes four surface probes
or pins electrodes to evaluate the average resistivity of the underlying mass The
average resistivity values of the subsurface mass extending from the existing ground
surface to the test depth was assessed by using this method along the twelve test
reference lines shown on the Resistivity Survey Plan Plate 2 Results of the resistivity
surveys are presented on Plates 3 1 through 3 3
The in situ resistivity surveys were performed at the existing ground surface along
the reference lines shown on the Resistivity Survey Plan In general each of the
six survey locations was tested along two perpendicular directions North South and
East West The field resistivity tests were performed at various electrode spacings
ranging from 2 to 60 feet In general the survey was conducted with electrodes placed as
far as practicable limited by the field conditions sloping terrain uneven ground surface
etc The average soil resistance along the proposed alignments was evaluated at each
survey location with the electrodes aligned at equal spacings as shown on Plates 3 1
through 3 3 The depth of penetration of a resistivity sounding is a function of the
electrode spacing

The ground resistance testing equipment AEMC Model 4610 provides a voltage
between the outer electrodes causing an electrical current to flow between them The
voltage drop between the inner electrodes is then measured by a sensitive built in
voltmeter As a result the soil resistance in ohms is obtained from the voltmeter dial
reading The soil resistivity in ohm cm which is directly related to both resistance and
spacing can then be computed The pin spacing evaluated soil resistances and
computed resistivity values are presented on Plates 3 1 through 3 3 At the time of the
field resistivity survey the temperature ranged from about 80 and 88 Fahrenheit The
existing ground surface was relatively dry It should be noted that the in situ resistivity may
be influenced by temperature weather conditions existing underground facilities and
other factors
On the basis of the field resistivity test results the majority of the test results for the
subsurface soils within the project site have in situ resistivity values ranging from about
2 719 to 22 253 ohm cm
Limitations
The analyses and findings submitted herein are based in part upon information
obtained from our field resistivity surveys The field resistivity surveys are based upon
information obtained from field data points by means of geophysical surveys Variations of
subsurface conditions between and beyond the field data points may occur and the nature
and extent of these variations may not become evident until construction is underway If
variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the findings provided
herein
The field resistivity test locations indicated herein are approximate having
been taped from visible features shown on the revised grading plan transmitted by
Tinguely Development Inc on May 28 2009 The physical locations of the resistivity
survey should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used
