As requested continuous inspection and density tests were

As of this date the site has been constructed to final grade
and all areas under our concern have been filled and compacted
adequately Compaction test results for the in place fills
are enclosed in the Compaction Test Summary
In general the grading operations consisted of cutting down
the upper portions of the split level lots and filling the
lower portions to form a level lot Since the underlying
soils were expansive adobe clay soil a 3 foot capping
layer was utilized over the entire lot
During the fill placement operations periodic field density
tests were made in accordance with the American Society for
Testing Materials ASTM Test D 1556 Sand Cone Method
During the course of fill construction various types of
on site and imported soils were utilized The maximum soil
density and optimum moisture contents were established on
the soil types in accordance with the ASTM D 1557 designation

Prior to fill placement vegetation were removed and the existing
ground was scarified moistened and recompacted to a minimum of
90 of its maximum dry density determined as above Fill was
then placed in horizontal lifts and compacted to a minimum of
90 of its maximum dry density The fill generally below
3 feet from the surface consisted of on site adobe material
while the upper 3 feet of fill consisted of low expansion po
tential on site material and borrow from Mariner s Ridge
Recommendations
Subsequent to completion of lot grading utility trenches within
the building pad should be properly backfilled and compacted under
the observation of a soils engineer
Foundations and slabs should be set back a minimum of 7 feet
away from the tops of slopes to reduce the detrimental effects
of slope creep and erosion Structures built on or near slopes
should be done under the guidance of an engineer
Swimming pools should be constructed with care as they will pro
bably extend into the adobe material and be subjected to the
heaving and swelling movements and large forces generally asso
ciated with this type of soil
Contractors involved with utility trench retaining wall and
footing backfill should be made aware of the potential contamina
tion of the existing capping from the underlying adobe expan
sive clay and the necessity for proper compaction and testing
This office assumes no responsibility for any alterations made
to slopes or pads on the subject lots subsequent to issuance
of this report without our knowledge and written approval
