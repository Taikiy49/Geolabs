This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the 360networks Inc Facility project located in the Kapolei area on the Island

analyses for the subject project These recommendations are intended for the design of
foundations slabs on grade site preparation and pavements only The findings and
recommendations presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this
report

SECTION 4 0 REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The Island of Oahu is comprised largely of the weathered remnants of two extinct
shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae forms the bulk of
the western third of the island while the younger shield Koolau forms the majority of the
eastern two thirds of the island It is believed that Waianae became extinct while Koolau
was still active and its eastern flank is partially buried below Koolau lavas in central Oahu
During the Pleistocene Epoch sea levels fluctuated in response to the cycles of
continental glaciation As the glaciers grew and advanced less water was available to fill
the oceanic basins such that sea levels fell below the present stands of the sea When the
glaciers melted and receded an excess of water became available such that the sea levels
rose to above the present sea level
The higher sea level stands caused the formation of deltas and fans of accumulated
terrigenous sediments in the heads of old bays accumulated reef deposits at
correspondingly higher elevations and lagoonal marine sediments in the quiet waters
protected by fringing reefs The lower sea stands caused streams to carve valleys in the
sediments and reef deposits Subaerial exposure of the sediments and calcareous
materials caused consolidation of the soft deltaic materials and lagoonal deposits and
induration of the calcareous reef materials Land development and reclamation projects
within the last century have brought the project site to its present form

7 2 Slabs On Grade
We anticipate that concrete slabs on grade will be required for the construction of
the new facility building Based on our field and laboratory test results we believe that new
building slabs may be constructed on grade provided that the slab subgrade is properly
prepared in accordance with the recommendations presented below and in the
Site Preparation section of this report
In general we recommend that a minimum 4 inch thick cushion layer consisting of
No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation be placed below the slabs to provide
more uniform support of the slabs and to serve as a capillary moisture break To reduce
the potential for future moisture infiltration and subsequent damage to floor coverings an
impervious moisture barrier is recommended on top of the gravel cushion layer
Two inches of moist fine sand may be considered above the moisture barrier to provide
protection of the moisture barrier and to aid in curing of the slab concrete Provisions of the
2 inches of moist sand above the moisture barrier will be left up to the discretion of the
project structural engineer
Where the concrete slabs on grade will be subjected to future vehicular traffic or
machinery vibrations such as the loading zone or equipment pads a 6 inch layer of
aggregate subbase is recommended in lieu of the 4 inch thick gravel cushion layer n
addition the 2 inch moist fine sand and moisture barrier may also be omitted for these
slabs The aggregate subbase should consist of crushed basalt aggregate compacted to a
minimum of 95 percent relative compaction We recommend that the concrete slabs be
designed by a structural engineer for the intended loads and be reinforced as a structural
slab to accommodate some amount of differential movement

SECTION 7 DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
For design of structural slabs a modulus of subgrade reaction of about 250 pounds
per square inch per inch of deflection pci may be used for the compacted aggregate
subbase Where slabs are intended to function as rigid pavements a minimum slab
thickness of 6 inches may be used for preliminary design purposes Provisions should be
made for proper load transfer across the slab joints which are subjected to vehicular traffic
The thickened edges of slabs adjacent to unpaved areas should be embedded at least
12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade
Slab subgrades should be prepared by scarifying the subgrade soils to a depth of
about 8 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and
proof rolled with a minimum 10 ton vibratory drum roller to a minimum of 90 percent
relative compaction The compaction requirement should be increased to 95 percent
relative compaction for slab subgrades that will be subjected to future vehicular traffic
Slab subgrades should be kept moist until covered by concrete Where shrinkage cracks
are noted after compaction of the subgrade we recommend that the soils be thoroughly
moistened to close the cracks and recompacted to provide a firm base prior to concrete
placement Saturation and subsequent yielding of the exposed subgrade due to inclement
weather and poor drainage may require over excavation of the soft areas and replacement
with well compacted fill
7 3 Site Preparation

majority of the site preparation work for the proposed project will consist of minor cuts and
fills to achieve the design finish grades As previously mentioned the project site may be
underlain by cavities and or sinkholes near the existing ground surface Therefore special
attention should be given to the subgrade preparation for the project development
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed Vegetation organic debris construction debris rubbish
and other unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site or in a
designated area to reduce the potential for contamination of the excavated materials with

the cleared and grubbed spoils Soft and yielding areas encountered during clearing and

After clearing and demolition the excavated subgrades underneath future slab

Fill materials required for the project construction should consist of non expansive

No 3B Fine gravel should also be used for the trench backfill up to about 12 inches above
the pipes to provide adequate support around the pipes and to reduce the compaction
effort on the backfill thus reducing the possibility of damaging the pipes It is critical to use

7 7 Foundation Probing
Based on our experience in the area voids and or cavities may exist in the coral
formation encountered at the project site To reduce the potential for loss of foundation
support resulting from the collapse of cavities below a footing we recommend that a
program of cavity probing and grouting be implemented during the construction of the
footings for the building
We recommend that a probehole be drilled for every 100 square feet of
foundation or at 10 feet on center along wall footings The probeholes should be at
least 3 inches in diameter and should extend at least 10 feet below the planned bottom
of footing elevation If cavities and or voids are encountered or suspected during the
probing operation additional probeholes should be drilled at closer spacing to aid in
delineating the vertical and lateral extent of the cavity and or void The probeholes and
cavities discovered should be backfilled with sand cement grout injected at low to
moderate pressures We recommend that a low strength sand cement grout mixture
with a slump range of 6 to 9 inches be utilized for the grouting operations

The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by
testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified
Soil Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representations of the
materials encountered in the borings are presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1
through A 9
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 3550 84 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a
3 inch OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches In
addition some samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance
with ASTM Test Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of
Soils by driving a 2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same hammer and
drop The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an
18 inch drive are shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Borings at the
appropriate sample depths

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content ASTM D 2216 and unit weight ASTM D 2937 determinations
were performed on selected samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of
soil properties The results of these tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the
appropriate sample depths
One sieve analysis test ASTM C 136 ASTM D 1140 was performed on a
selected soil sample to evaluate the gradation characteristics of the soils and to aid in soil
classification Graphic presentation of the test results are provided on Plate B 1
One laboratory California Bearing Ratio Test ASTM D 1883 was performed on
a bulk sample of the near surface soils to evaluate the pavement support characteristics
of the soils Results of the CBR test were also used to evaluate the quality of the
materials tested for use as fills The test results are presented on Plate B 2
One Modified Proctor compaction test ASTM D 1557 was performed on a bulk
sample of the near surface soils to evaluate the dry density and moisture content
relationships The test results are presented on Plate B 3
