REPORT SUMMARY
Based on our review of the inclinometer records during the past six month
monitoring interval it appears that the earth movements recorded within the
Waiomao Landslide remain at relatively reduced levels In contrast the earth movements
recorded within Area A continues at relatively steady moderate to high levels The
subsurface earth movement trends recorded by the inclinometers appear to correlate with
the observed ground surface deformations that are visible within the paved street areas of
the project site
The ground surface deformation of the paved areas along the head of the
Waiomao Landslide at Kuahea Street and within Area A of Kuahea Street are
pronounced and continue to compound with the passage of time The existing open stress
cracks and buckled pavements along Kuahea Street should be repaired sealed to reduce
the infiltration of surface runoff into the roadway subgrade
Other inclinometers at the lower western flank of the Waiomao Landslide
within Area B continue to record some generally slight earth movements with limited
visible ground surface deformation associated with the recorded movements
Rainfall in the Palolo Kaimuki area was maintained at about normal precipitation
levels during the past six month monitoring interval with slightly higher precipitation
having been encountered during the months of September and October 2005 Based on
our monitoring of the piezometer instrumentation at the project site most instruments
have indicated stable or decreasing groundwater seepage during the past six month
monitoring period However subsurface groundwater encountered at Piezometer No 2A
which is located within Area A appears to have steadily increased throughout the
current monitoring period

Subsurface Water Monitoring
The monitoring of subsurface water levels groundwater is accomplished by
measuring the level of water head contained within the sixteen individual and nested
piezometers installed in drilled borings at the project site A list of the piezometer
installations for the project is provided in the preceding Table entitled Summary of
Instrument Installations The sixteen piezometers are installed in the borings identified as
Boring Nos P 1 through P 8 as shown on the Instrument Location Plan Plate 2
Three piezometers identified as P 2A P 4A and P 8 consist of dedicated vibrating wire
piezometer installations that provide a near continuous record of the water level
encountered in the well casing The remaining 13 piezometers consist of simple standpipe
well casings which are monitored manually on a bi monthly basis
It should be noted that the standpipe Piezometer P 2B has experienced a blockage
in the pipe at a depth of about 22 feet which has rendered the instrument as
non functional for further monitoring

Observations Pavement Structural Deformation
A reconnaissance of the project site was conducted on to record
our general observations for the existing site conditions Significant visible water seepage
at the ground surface was not observed within the street areas of the Waiomao Landslide
and the Area A earth movement regions at the time of our site visit which was conducted
under generally dry environmental conditions
Waiomao Road Waiomao Landslide Area
Based on our visual observations significant changes in the ground surface
deformation discussed in our previous Progress Report No 10 were not observed
Kuahea Street Waiomao Landslide Area
Observation of the street area along Kuahea Street at the head of the
Waiomao Landslide indicates that the roadway pavement and northern road
shoulder continue to experience some settlement of the ground surfaces The
ground settlements have produced uneven pavement surfaces with significant
bumps that traverse the roadway Open cracks about 0 5 inches to 1 inch wide
and localized settlement depressions of about 1 foot in diameter have formed along
the existing pavement repair patches in the center of the road Localized asphalt
patch repair is recommended to seal the localized larger holes and cracks located
in the roadway surface
Kuahea Street Area A
Based on our visual observation of the existing ground surfaces the existing
ground surface deformation features appear to be more pronounced as evidenced
by concrete pavement slab settlement and heave which has produced many
wide open cracks in the street and along the curb The most significant ground
surface deformation appears to be occurring along Kuahea Street between Kuahea
Place and Helo Place where a localized area of concrete pavement has buckled on
the northern side of the street producing a 4 inch high pavement deflection that is
approximately 5 feet long Because of the pronounced buckle in the rigid concrete
pavement a gap has developed along the upslope side of the pavement
deformation where surface water flowing downhill is capable of draining into the
gap and resultant void which extends below the pavement surface
Other more limited ground surface deformation such as deflected concrete curbs
and slight concrete pavement stress cracking was observed along Kuahea Street
located downslope from the Helo Place intersection Based on our recent
observations the earth movement boundaries are not developed enough at the
