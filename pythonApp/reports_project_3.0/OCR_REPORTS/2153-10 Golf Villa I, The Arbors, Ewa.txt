EARTHWORK OBSERVATION AND TESTING REPORT
GOLF VILLA THE ARBORS
EWA BY GENTRY
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering services performed
during the earthwork and grading operations for the above referenced project from
March 1989 through October 1991 and February through June 1993
The subject site is bounded by the drainage sump area to the south Kolowaka
Drive to the north Fort Weaver Road to the east and the proposed North South Road
to the west within the Ewa by Gentry development in the district of
In general the site is within the fill area designated as F 3 and the southern portions of
F 4 and F 5 of the mass grading Phase plans for the development as shown on the Site
Plan Plate 1
The mass grading work was performed by RHS Lee Inc and the infrastructure
grading work was performed by Oahu Construction Co Ltd The mass grading work
generally consisted of scarifying and compacting the existing subgrade and placing up
to about 10 feet of new fills The on site fill materials generally consisted of dark brown
silty clay mixed with coral gravel and coralline silty sand and gravel

Prior to fill placement the existing vegetation was removed and the existing
ground was scarified to a depth of about six inches 6 brought to near optimum
moisture content and compacted to 90 percent of the maximum dry density as
determined in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 The new fill material was
then placed in near level lifts and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of its maximum
dry density
During the fill placement operations periodic field density tests were taken in
general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 2922 Nuclear Gauge Method The
results of the compaction tests are summarized in the appendices of this report
During the grading period various on site and borrow soils were tested prior to use
in the field The maximum dry density and optimum moisture content relationships were
established in our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557
Modified Proctor and the test results are summarized in the appendices of this report
Our services consisted of full time field observation of the contractor s fill placement
and compaction procedures and confirmation of subgrade soil conditions exposed during
the above grading operations
Field density test locations were selected to confirm field observations as variations
in construction procedures and soil conditions were noted Test locations and elevations
were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations
and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods
used Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum
compaction was obtained

LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore there is a possibility that
some work may have occurred without our observation However we believe that the
level of earthwork observation and testing services provided is reasonable in reducing the
geotechnical risks to the project It should also be recognized that there are variations
in the accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the
earthwork construction and materials Our services were performed in accordance with
generally accepted standards of geotechnical engineering practice no warranty is
