PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located in the Wahiawa area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The
project site is located near Wahiawa Elementary School Kaukonahua Gulch and
Kaala Avenue as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The proposed development involves
the construction of seventeen 17 single family homes on a parcel that is approximately
3 67 acres in land area In addition infrastructure including a roadway driveways and
utilities are also planned for the proposed subdivision
At the northern boundary of the site the existing ground surface slopes down
towards Kaukonahua Stream We understand that it is desired to determine the presence
and extent of loose soft soils near the top of slope area that may impact the project
development cost We anticipate that the material at the top of slope area may consist of
residual soils with limited depths of loose soft soils Therefore the existing soil at the top
of the slope area was explored by hand probings

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The Island of Oahu is composed largely of the weathered remnants of two extinct
shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae forms the bulk of
the western third of the island while the younger shield Koolau forms the majority of the
eastern two thirds of the island It is believed that Waianae became extinct while Koolau
was still active and its eastern flank is partially buried below the Koolau lavas in central
Oahu
The Schofield Plateau was formed when lavas from the Koolau Volcano ponded
against the already eroded slopes of the Waianae Volcano in the late Pleistocene Epoch
The dips of the lava beds range from 3 to 5 degrees The lava flows on the plateau have
weathered to depths of 50 to 100 feet and are characterized by the red and brown colors
of the soil The project site is located near the center of the Schofield Plateau

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located in the Wahiawa area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The
majority of the project site is undeveloped and covered with tall grass and trees
Greenhouse warehouse house and wooden shed structures are located on the western
portion of the project site Based on preliminary drawings the new subdivision will be
located primarily in the central portion of the project site
The project site is fairly level and slopes down towards Kaukonahua Gulch at the
northern portion of the site The top of slope elevation in the central portion of the project
site generally range from about 955 to 970 feet Mean Sea Level MSL The existing
ground surface generally slopes downwards at an inclination from about one horizontal to
one vertical 1H 1V to 2H 1V The bottom of slope is estimated to be at about
Elevation 850 feet MSL
FINDINGS AND DISCUSSIONS
A series of hand probings using a inch diameter steel rod was performed near
the existing top of slope area for the project The probings were performed to evaluate the
depth and extent of loose soft surface soils near the slope area The probings were
located near the central portion of the site where the new residential lots are to be primarily
located Based on the probings the depth of loose material measured generally ranged
from about 1 to 20 inches Based on our field observations it appears that the thicker
loose surface materials are not continuous but are in isolated locations A total of 22 cross
sections were developed with measured depths of loose surface soils These cross
sections are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 and on the Sections Plates 3 1 through 3 6
Based on visual observations the on site surface soils generally consist of stiff
residual soils composed of reddish brown silty clays and clayey silts The loose residual
soils near the ground surface were dry and friable
Based on the results of our exploration the loose surface soils encountered near
the top of slope area at the project site were relatively shallow in depth The deeper
pockets of loose soils encountered were isolated and limited in extent Therefore we
believe that slope reconstruction due to loose soft surface soils near the top of the slope
area will be limited

The following are preliminary recommendations for the loose surface soils
encountered at the project site
After clearing and grubbing the subgrade near the slope area should be
proof rolled with a minimum 10 ton vibratory drum roller to detect loose soft
zones The loose soft and yielding areas encountered during proof rolling
should be over excavated to expose firm natural material and the resulting
excavation should be backfilled with well compacted engineered fill
The existing ground should be scarified to a depth of 10 inches and
moisture conditioned to about 2 percent above the optimum moisture and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
A field exploration program including borings should be conducted for the final
design of the site grading house foundation and infrastructure installation for the Wahiawa
Subdivision development
ADDITIONAL EXPLORATION
This exploration was only to evaluate the extent of loose surface soils at the site A
more detailed geotechnical exploration with borings should be conducted for the site
grading development of the site
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from the field hand probings and visual observations Variations of
conditions between and beyond the field hand probings may occur and the nature and
extent of these variations may not become evident until construction is underway
The probing locations in the field were determined by taping from reference points
