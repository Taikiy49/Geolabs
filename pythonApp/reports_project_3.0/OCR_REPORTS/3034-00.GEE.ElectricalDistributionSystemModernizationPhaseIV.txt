INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Electrical Distribution System Modernization EDSM project
Phase IV at the Honolulu International Airport in Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii
The general location and vicinity of the site are shown on the Project Location Map
Plate 1
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3034 00 Page 2
This report summarizes the findings and geotechnical engineering recommenda
tions resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering analyses The
recommendations presented herein are intended for foundation design and site grading
The findings and recommendations presented herein may not be appropriate to form the
basis for preparation of final construction cost estimates nor for construction bidding
purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located on Aolele Street at the Honolulu International Airport
on the island of Oahu Hawaii The proposed project is located between two concrete
viaducts adjacent to the existing one story emergency generator building located
northwest of the lei stands
The proposed EDSM project consists of the construction of a new one story
emergency generator building addition underground fuel oil storage tanks and associated
fueling hardstand at the site The proposed emergency generator building will have plan
dimensions of approximately 26 by 53 feet The exact offset of the proposed generator
building addition in relation to the existing generator building had not been finalized at the
time this report was prepared
We understand that two gallon high density polyethylene HDPE
underground storage tanks are planned at the approximate location shown on the Site
Plan Plate 2 Each tank will have approximate dimensions of 31 feet in length by 8 feet
in diameter The depth from the finish grade to the top of the fuel storage tanks will be
approximately 31 feet
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3034 00 Page 3
A truck fueling hardstand is also planned The physical location of the hardstand
had not been finalized yet but we understand that it will be located to the north of the
new generator building on the underground fuel tanks
We understand that the proposed emergency generator building addition will be
a one story masonry block structure supported by a conventional shallow foundation
The proposed generator building will be moderately loaded with the potential for
equipment vibrations
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface soil
conditions at the project site to develop an idealized soil data set for the formulation of
geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to the design of foundations and site
grading for the project The work was done in general accordance with our revised fee
proposal to Ronald N S Ho Associates Inc dated August 28 1992 The scope of
work included the following tasks and work efforts
1 Mobilization and demobilization of a truck mounted drill rig and operators
to and from the project site
2 Drilling and sampling of 3 borings at the project site to depths of
approximately 20 feet below the existing ground surface for a total of 60
lineal feet of exploration
3 Coordination of the field exploration and logging of the borings by a Field
Geologist from our firm
GEOLABS HAWAII

4 Laboratory testing of selected samples obtained during the field exploration
as an aid to classifying the materials encountered and evaluating their
engineering properties
5 Analyses of the field and laboratory data for the formulation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertinent to the proposed development
6 Preparation of six 6 copies of this report summarizing our work on the
project and presenting our findings and recommendations
7 Coordination of our overall work on the project by a Project Manager from
our firm
8 Quality assurance of our work and client design team consultation by a
Principal Engineer of our firm

support

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
On the basis of our geotechnical exploration the proposed EDSM project may be
developed as planned The proposed generator building may be supported on conven
tional shallow foundations bearing on the near surface fill soils and the generators may
be supported on a thickened interior floor slab The major geotechnical considerations
for the proposed construction are as follows
The proposed generator building site is underlain by a surficial layer of sand
and gravel derived from coral overlying a layer of moderately expansive
silty clay The floor slab will be supported upon the sand and gravel which
is not expansive The footings will be supported by the clays which do not
appear to be expansive under the anticipated footing loads
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3034 00 Page 7
Volcanic tuff may be highly resistant to conventional excavating equipment
during construction of the underground fuel storage tanks
Shoring will likely be required for the fuel tank excavations because the
proximity of the existing storm drain box culvert and other underground
utilities may preclude laying back of the excavation side slopes
Dewatering of the tank excavations will probably be necessary since
groundwater was encountered at a depth of about 14 feet below the
surface which is a foot above the anticipated bottom of excavation Where
dewatering of the excavation is necessary it should be noted that a
National Pollutant Discharge Elimination System NPDES Storm Sewer
permit may be required
Recommendations addressing the above considerations and other geotechnical
aspects of the project are discussed in the following sections of the report
Foundation Support
We anticipate that the proposed generator building will be a one story metal frame
building with masonry block walls and a prestressed precast concrete roof We
recommend that the building be supported by spread footing foundations An allowable
bearing capacity of 3 000 pounds per square foot p s f may be utilized for the design
of foundations bearing on the near surface silty clay soils This value is for dead plus live
loads and may be increased by one third for temporary wind or seismic loads The
footings should be embedded a minimum of 36 inches below the lowest adjacent finished
grade Isolated spread footings should have a minimum width of 18 inches and
continuous footings should have a minimum width of 12 inches
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3034 00 Page 8
Footings located adjacent to underground utility ttenches or easements should be
extended to a depth below the projection of an imaginary one horizontal to one vertical
1H 1V plane which extends up from the bottom edge of the utility trench or the from the
invert of the pipeline within the utility easement
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive earth pressure
acting against vertical or near vertical faces of the foundation system and by frictional
resistance between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil A passive pressure
developed by an equivalent fluid weighing 250 pounds per cubic foot p c f may be
used Passive resistance should be neglected in the upper 12 inches unless it is confined
by a slab or paved surface A coefficient of friction of 0 30 may be used between the
footings and the supporting subgrade
We estimate total foundation settlements to be less than one inch with differential
settlements between footings to be less than inch
Footing subgrades should be compacted to 90 percent of the maximum dry
density established by ASTM D 1557 91 test procedures The footing excavations should
expose firm moist soils If soft clay pockets are encountered at the bottom of the footing
excavations the soft soils should be over excavated to expose firm stiff soils The over
excavation should be backfilled with granular material compacted to a minimum of 90
percent of the maximum dry density as determined in accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 1557 91
Footing excavations should be observed by a representative of Geolabs Hawaii
prior to the placement of reinforcing steel or concrete in order to allow an evaluation of
exposed foundation bearing conditions and embedment depths
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3034 00 Page 9
Slabs on Grade
It is anticipated that the interior floor for the proposed generator building will be a
concrete slab on grade The planned generators will impose concentrated loads of
approximately 200 p s f according to the manufacturer s supplied data We therefore
recommend that the floor slab be well reinforced and thickened at the appropriate
locations for the support of the generators The building floor slab may be supported by
the existing fill soils after preparation as follows The soils should be scarified to a depth
of at least eight inches 8 moisture conditioned to not less than two percent above the
optimum moisture content and re compacted to at least 90 percent of the maximum dry
density established by ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91 If silty clays are exposed
during the scarification process these soils should not be mixed with the sandy soils for
use as slab subgrade support
The concrete floor slabs should be supported on a six inch 6 layer of No 3B
Fine gravel ASTM C33 No 67 Gradation above the recompacted subgrade to serve as
a slab cushion and capillary break In order to reduce the potential for future moisture
infiltration and subsequent damage to electrical generators and equipment it is
recommended that a moisture barrier such as a plastic membrane be placed on top of
the cushion A one 1 to two inch 2 thick layer of moist fine sand should be placed
over the vapor membrane to aid in curing of slab concrete
Site Grading
We anticipate site grading for the proposed generator building and underground
tanks will consist only of minor work to provide a level building pad for construction and
the excavation for the tanks Excavation for the tanks is discussed in a separate
subsection The following guidelines should be observed during the grading operations
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3034 00 Page 10
1 Prior to the commencement of the earthwork the areas within the contract
grading limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation
debris and other deleterious material should be removed and disposed of
properly off site
2 Any loose weak or yielding areas disclosed during clearing operations
should be over excavated to expose firm ground and the resulting
excavation should be backfilled with well compacted fill
3 Areas that are to receive fills should be scarified to a depth of 8 inches
moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above optimum moisture content
and compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction Relative
compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a
percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in
accordance with ASTM D 1557 91 test procedures
4 All fill and backfill material should be tested for suitability for its intended use
by Geolabs Hawaii before delivering to the site If import fill is needed
import fill should consist of granular material and be should be well graded
from coarse to fine with no particles larger than 3 inches in largest
dimension The material should contain less than 20 percent passing the
No 200 sieve
5 All fill and backfill should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 12 inches in
loose lift thickness and compacted to not less than 90 percent relative
compaction All surfaces should be finished to create smooth and
essentially unyielding surfaces
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3034 00 Page 11
6 The finished grade outside the building should be sloped to shed water
away from the foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding
The subgrade soils for the pavement section should be scarified to a depth of at
least 8 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture
content and compacted to not less than 95 percent relative compaction
Excavation Temporary Shoring and Dewatering
Excavation Method
We understand that the installation of the two underground fuel storage tanks will
require the excavation of a pit approximately 75 feet long 20 feet wide and 15 feet
deep The base of the excavation will be covered by a sand cushion approxi
mately 2 feet thick which will underlie a 1 foot thick concrete pad The concrete
pad will serve as a cradle supporting the tanks Based on our field exploration the
excavation for the proposed underground fuel storage tanks will likely encounter
moderately to highly resistant volcanic tuff below a depth of about 10 to 11 feet
from the surface The method and equipment to be used for excavation should
be determined by the contractor subject to practical limits and safety
considerations It may be possible for the volcanic tuff material to be removed with
conventional excavating equipment such as a track excavator However we
recommend that the contractor be prepared to use specialized equipment such as
a hoe ram to reach the required bottom elevation
Excavation Support
Safety considerations require that the anticipated 15 foot deep excavation be either
shored or the sides sloped back The silty clay soils encountered in our
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3034 00 Page 12
exploration should be capable of standing near vertical during temporary
construction operations however the presence of sands and gravels both above
and below the clay will require that the sides be sloped back to OSHA standards
which for these soils would be approximately slopes Although the
volcanic tuff will be stable in a near vertical cut OSHA regulations require that the
vertically sided lower portion be shielded or supported to a height 18 inches above
the top of the vertical side The close proximity of the existing storm drain culvert
other utility lines and piers for the adjacent viaducts may preclude the possibility
of sloped sides in the excavation Therefore temporary shoring may be required
to maintain the stability of the excavation
We anticipate that it will not be possible to drive sheet piles for temporary shoring
into the volcanic tuff As an alternative a system of soldier piles and timber
lagging may be used The steel H piles would probably have to be concreted into
drilled holes into the volcanic tuff Timber lagging would then be placed between
or behind the H piles as the excavation proceeded It is important that adequate
lagging be installed and maintained tight against the excavation walls as the
excavation proceeds Because of the relatively shallow depth of the planned
excavation we believe that cantilevered soldier piles may be used if no critical
structures are located within about 15 feet lateral distance from the excavation
Proper installation of the soldier pile wall is essential for reduction of lateral
movements of the adjacent ground towards the excavation which could cause
settlement of the adjacent ground
Lateral load resistance may be mobilized through passive resistance against an
area twice the diameter of the portion of the H piles and surrounding concrete
which are socketed into the volcanic tuff The passive resistance of the upper two
feet of the tuff should be neglected in the design For preliminary design of the
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3034 00 Page 13
cantilevered soldier pile system the apparent lateral earth pressure diagram
presented on Plate 3 may be used as a guide An appropriate safety factor should
be included in the designer s calculations
Construction Precaution and Monitoring
The excavation shoring system used must comply with all applicable safety
requirements It is recommended that a qualified geotechnical engineer be
retained by the contractor to design and evaluate the shoring system used
The adequacy and safety of the shoring installation should be made the sole
responsibility of the contractor His representative who should be required to be
present on site during excavation and construction work will have the best
opportunity to promptly observe any changing conditions during construction such
as unforeseen subsoils unexpectedly high groundwater table inappropriate
construction sequences or techniques etc which may affect shoring stability
However it must be noted that some movement of the shoring system and the
adjacent ground may still occur due to changes in earth stresses during
excavation The magnitude of movement is difficult to accurately predict due to the
complexity of the stress changes The magnitude depends greatly upon workman
ship and also on the subsoil conditions the size of the excavation and the rate of
excavation
Therefore it is important to realize that the excavation shoring should be installed
properly and as early as practical if necessary and that the adjacent ground
should be continuously monitored for cracks dips and or other indications of
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3034 00 Page 14
movements with instruments such as settlement markers and inclinometers Such
monitoring is particularly critical in areas adjacent to existing facilities and utilities
We strongly recommend that the contractor retain a qualified structural engineering
consultant to design and to coordinate the implementation of the excavation
support system
Construction Dewatering
Shallow groundwater levels of about 14 feet below the surface were encountered
in all of the borings drilled at the proposed generator building site We believe it is likely
that the groundwater level will be similar at the proposed tank locations and that
dewatering of excavations for the underground storage tanks will likely be necessary for
placement of the two feet 2 of sand cushion Therefore we recommend that
dewatering provisions be included in the contract documents
Dewatering Method
The method and equipment to be used for the excavation dewatering should be
determined by the contractor subject to practical limits and safety considerations
It is our opinion that the definition of Dewatering in the contract documents
should be written to include all works or systems required to lower the natural
groundwater table and or to exclude the water from the excavations to allow
construction of the proposed structures under safe and dry conditions We
suggest that the following three basic criteria be considered in selecting a suitable
method of dewatering
a The dewatering method should result in the least disturbance and or
damage to existing facilities and the environment
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3034 00 Page 15
b The dewatering method should maintain stability of the sides of the
excavation and also provide safe and dry working conditions in the
excavation
Cc The dewatering method should be sufficiently flexible to allow
modifications to accommodate various ground conditions
These systems may include but are not limited to well pumping grouting cut off
walls tremie plugs or any combination of the above and or other possible methods
The potential impact of the dewatering system selected on depressing the natural
groundwater table must be carefully evaluated by the contractor prior to dewatering It
is recommended that a qualified geotechnical engineer be retained by the contractor to
design and evaluate the dewatering system used
The impact and safety of the dewatering operations should be made the sole
responsibility of the contractor His qualified representative who should be required to
be continuously present on site during dewatering activities will have the best opportunity
to promptly observe the effects of dewatering during construction and to implement as
soon as possible necessary precautionary or remedial measures including but not
limited to stopping or slowing down the dewatering operations
Special caution should also be taken to avoid dewatering utility trenches connected
to excavations If this occurs the granular bedding and or backfill in the utility trenches
could act as subdrains and cause significant areal groundwater drawdown resulting in
settlements and potential damage to utility lines and or other existing adjacent structures
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3034 00 Page 16
Utility Trench
We envision that new utility lines may be required for the project A granular
bedding consisting of six inches 6 of select borrow ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation is
recommended under the pipes Self compacting granular materials such as No 3B Fine
gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation should be used for the trench backfill up to about
one foot 1 above the pipes to provide adequate support around the pipes and to
reduce compaction of the backfill thus reducing the possibility of damaging the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 1 foot above the pipes to the
top of the subgrade or finished grade should consist of select granular material The
backfill should be placed in maximum 10 inch level loose lifts and mechanically
compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for
future ground subsidence Where trenches are below pavement areas the upper 2 feet
of the trench backfill below the pavement subgrade should be compacted to 95 percent
relative compaction

Laboratory Testing
Moisture content unit weight and Atterberg Limit determinations were performed
on selected samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties The
results of these tests are presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 3 at the
appropriate sample depths

liquid and plastic limits The test results are shown on Plate B 1
Two 2 unconfined compression tests were performed on selected samples to
evaluate the strength parameters of the soil The results of these tests are summarized
on Plate B 1
An expansion test was performed on a selected soil sample to evaluate the
swelling potential of the soil under structural loads The test results are summarized on
Plate B 1
