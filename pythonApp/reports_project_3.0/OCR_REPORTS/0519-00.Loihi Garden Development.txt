INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geologic and soils
investigation for the proposed Loihi Garden Development
located in Kaneohe
The scope of this investigation was to explore the geologic
and subsurface conditions at the site evaluate overall site
stability determine foundation requirements for the proposed
residence and on site access roadway evaluate suitability
Page 2
W
of the site soils for fill and provide recommendations for
filling the low lying peat bog area
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is presently planned to develop the 4 03 acre parcel into
21 residential lots with an access roadway The low lying
peat bog area will require maximum fills on the order of 4
feet to bring this area to finished grade Cuts on the order
of 7 feet in the upper north western section will be required
to prepare the site for development
SITE CONDITIONS
Surface
The site is located adjacent to the Keapuka subdivision in
Kaneohe Oahu It is bounded by a concrete lined ditch on the
south end and a dirt road to the north The northwestern
portion of the site was formerly a banana plantation which is
now abandoned and is presently covered by heavy growth of
weeds and banana trees Tall elephant grass up to 6 feet
high is found near the lower south end Standing water was
found in the low lying peat bog areas The approximate
boundaries of these areas are shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
Page 3
W O 519 00
Geology
The project site is located on the foothill region of the
windward pali of the Koolau Volcanic Range The steeply
dissected range is formed by stream erosion and subsequently
the valley floors are filled with Quatenary alluvium It
appears that the southern portion of the site is a swamp and
during recent construction of the drainage channels part of
the swamp was filled
Subsurface
To explore the subsurface conditions at the site nine borings
were drilled at the locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
A detailed description of the field investigation ahd labora
tory testing is contained in the Appendix which also includes
a log of each boring and the results of our laboratory tests
Our borings revealed that the site may be divided into two
generalized areas as illustrated on the site plan The areas
near the existing drainage channel consists of up to 12 feet
of fill that was placed directly on about 12 feet of peat and
organic clays The soft underlying soils may still be under
going consolidation due to the weight of fill These peaty
materials are swamp deposits contributed by the Keapuka
Page 4
W O 519 00
stream The peat is also exposed in unfilled areas Beneath
this material and to the depths explored a highly decomposed
basalt formation was encountered consisting of varying degrees
of decomposed basalt rock intermixed with a brown clayey silt
The northern upper area of the site is underlain by highly
decomposed basalt rock with stiff clayey silt which was
encountered to the depths explored Groundwater was encoun
tered at various depths at different locations in some of our
borings near the peat bog and is generally found below eleva
tion 146 These water levels are shown on the boring logs in
the Appendix
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The northerly portion of the site which is underlain by stiff
soils may be developed with normal grading operations Con
tained in this area are lots 7 through 21 Structures located
within this area may be supported by continuous shallow
footings and concrete slab on grade
The other portion of the site which contains recently placed
fill underlain by soft deposits or peat bog will require
Page 5
W O 519 00
additional work to prepare this area for development The
alternatives are to remove the soft material or place addi
tional fill on the soft area to act as a surcharge and allow
sufficient time for pre consolidation to occur
Foundations
The structures on lots 7 through 21 may be supported on conven
tional shallow spread or continuous footings bottomed a minimum
of 18 inches below final adjacent grade A soil bearing value
of 2000 pounds per square foot may be used for dead plus live
loads Continuous footings should have a minimum width of 12
inches It appears that lots 7 21 are situated in the
transition zone between fill and material ground This fill
may be underlain by soft organic deposits The structure on
this lot should be positioned so that it is entirely on
natural ground If this is not possible then the portion of
the fill area beneath the structure should be probed during
grading operations to determine the ground condition
Lots 4 5 and 6 are over medium stiff clayey silt fill that
is underlain by about 12 feet of soft peat and organic silt
deposits that may still be undergoing consolidation due to
the weight of the fill above it Approximately 4 feet of fill
will be excavated to prepare this area for construction

Therefore we recommend that settlement markers be installed
in this area to monitor the rate of settlement These settle
ment markers should be installed at the earliest time to
allow for sufficient readings to be taken to develop settle
ment rates Structures should be constructed only when total
settlement is attained
It is our recommendation that post and beam construction with
individual pier footings be used for structures built on Lots
4 5 and 6 as well as Lots 1 2 and 3 after these lots are
constructed in accordance with the recommendations in the
following section Footings should be designed for a dead
plus live load of 1500 pounds per square foot
Slabs On Grade
Slab on grade construction may be utilized for Lots 7 through
21 Due to the slightly expansive nature of the natural site
soils all slabs on grade should be underlain bya minimum of
4 inches of gravel In areas where moisture is undesirable
a plastic visqueen sheet should be placed over the gravel
The slab should be provided with a minimum of 6 x 6 10
welded wire mesh or an additional two inches of gravel which
ever is more economical Garage slabs should be separated

Peat Bog Treatments
The peat bog area which includes Lots 1 2 and 3 with por
tions of roadways A and B will require special treatment prior
to construction It is our opinion that two general alternate
methods will be available 1 removal of the soft materials
and replacement with compacted fill 2 pre consolidation of
the soft materials by surcharging with additional fill These
two alternates are discussed inthe following sections
Removal and Replacement
One alternate that is being considered and has been discussed
with Mr John Tanabe of Park Engineering Civil Engineer is
removing the soft materials and replacing with suitable com
pacted material The depth of the soft material varied from
about 8 feet in Boring 5 to aobut 20 feet in Boring 6
Removal of the exposed peat bog will present some construction
difficulties in that the 12 feet of adjacent fill Boring 9
Page 8
W
is underlain by soft organic materials that may be displaced
laterally towards the bog area being excavated Provisions
will have to be made to prevent this from happening or to
remove the existing fill before proceeding with the peat
removal which will also include the peat underlying the fill
Based on surface reconnaissance it appears that this fill may
extend to the limits shown on Plate 1 Should the decision
be made to use this alternate method of site preparation then
we will be glad to advise you prior to and during dredging
operations
It is our opinion that clamshell or dragline equipment can be
used for dredging of the soft soils Dozer and pan equipment
can be used to excavate the existing fill material If this
method is selected it is our recommendation that the stability
of the existing channels be analyzed to determine the effect
of dredging on these structures Prior to placement of any
fill the site should be inspected by the Soils Engineer to
determine if all of the soft materials have been removed by
the dredging operations
Granular fill capable of attaining densification under water
should be placed until the fill is brought to approximately
18 inches above the water level which is at approximate

Surcharging
Another alternate foundation treatment available will be to
place additional fill over the exposed peat bog as outlined
on the Site Plan Our calculations indicate that if the site
is brought to planned grade with approximately 6 feet of fill
approximately 12 inches of total settlement will occurr
Differential settlements could be on the order of 6 inches
About half of this settlement will occur within 6 months and
the remainder over a period of 5 years We realize that the
time required to obtain pre consolidation of the soft under
lying materials is not within economic realities Therefore
it is our recommendation that surcharge be placed and allow
the softer materials to consolidate in less time
If a total of 12 feet of fill were placed sufficient consoli
dation will occur in about one year with 16 feet of fill
sufficient consolidation could be attained in about 6 months
About one foot of fill will displace the soft materials when
placed These times may be decreased by the installation of
Page 10
W O 519 00
a vertical sand drain system that will decrease the drainage
path distance and increase the settlement rate We would be
glad to consult with you should you feel that this system is
desirable
A blanket of gravel or coarse granular material should be
placed on the soft deposits to act as a drainage path for
relieving pore water pressures from the underlying soft organic
soils It is our opinion that a minimum thickness of 18 inches
will be required A series of weep holes should be provided
in the existing drainage channels on the south and west end of
the peat bog to provide a method of removing excess water from
the drainage blanket These weep holes should be provided at
about Elevation 145 and contain filter rock pockets to prevent
loss of fine material
Due to the soft yielding nature of the underlying soils the
fill and surcharge should be placed in thin lifts placed in
such a manner as not to cause shear failure in the soft
material
Based on our calculations and test results it appears that
the first four lifts should not exceed a two foot thickness
for each lift A minimum of one week should be maintained
Page ll
W O 519 00
between lifts to allow the consolidating soils to gain suffi
cient strength to support the next lift After the first four
lifts have been placed the remainder of the fill and sur
charge may then be placed in accordance with our grading
recommendations Based on field observations the waiting
periods between lifts may be adjusted to meet actual field
conditions
Settlement markers should be placed to monitor the rate and
amount of consolidation After sufficient pre consolidation
has taken place the additional fill may then be removed and
construction of the structures and roadway may begin Moni
toring of settlements should be done under the supervision of
the Soils Engineer
We will be glad to meet with you to discuss either of the
alternate methods to prepare the site for development
Site Preparations and Grading
The site shall be cleared of all deleterious and organic matter
In areas to receive fill which includes the natural site soils
on the upper portion of the property and the replacement fill
above the water level the ground should be scarified to a mini
Page 12
W O 519 00
mum depth of six inches The scarified surface shall then be
brought to near optimum moisture content and compacted to a
minimum density of 90 of maximum dry density as determined by
Structural fill shall then be placed in hori
zontal six inch maximum lifts and also compacted to at least
90 of maximum density If the surcharge alternate is to be
selected then all fills remaining in place should be compacted
as structural fill The surcharge fill above finished grade
should also be rolled in thin lift but without minimum density
requirements
Utility connections in the soft areas should be delayed as
long as possible and should be fitted with flexible joints
capable of tolerating one inch of differential settlement
All imported material should be approved by the Soils Engineer
prior to use in fills All grading operations should be under
the direct supervision of the Soils Engineer

FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface soil conditions at the site were explored by
drilling 9 test borings The borings were drilled utilizing
truck mounted and portable rotary auger equipment The depths
of the borings ranged from 10 to 26 5 feet below the existing
ground surface The locations of the borings are shown on the
Site Plan in the text of this report The soils were classi
fied by visual and textural examinations in the field by our
geologist and engineer who continuously supervised the drill
ing operations The classifications were verified by inspection
and testing in the laboratory All soils were classified in
aecordance with the Unified Soil Classification System
Graphical representations of the soils encountered are presented
on the Boring Logs Plates A 1A through A 1M
Soil samples were obtained by driving a 2 4 inch I D diameter
or a standard penetration sampler with a 140 pound hammer free
falling a distance of 30 inches Continuous blow counts were
obtained in the soft materials The blow counts to drive the
sampler are shown on the Log of Borings at the appropriate
sample depths
