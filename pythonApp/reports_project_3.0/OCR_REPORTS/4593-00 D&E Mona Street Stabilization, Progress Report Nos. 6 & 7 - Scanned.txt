PAVEMENT DEFORMATION MAPPING
As mentioned in our previous reports ground surface observations at the project
site were recorded Approximately 1 to 2 inch wide pavement cracks on the surface of the
roadway were previously observed in the area between 1189 and 1199 Mona Street in
the period from May 2002 to September 2002 No significant continuous cracking
development was recorded in the period from September 2002 to January 2003
Pavement re surfacing was performed in localized areas of Mona Street during the
period from January 20036 to August 2003 Therefore a majority of the cracks
previously recorded have been patched by asphaltic concrete No significant continuous
cracking development was recorded in the period from January 2004 to August 2004
Our pavement deformation mapping was resumed in the Year 2005 During the
resumed monitoring period minor cracking primarily less than inch wide was observed
on the re surfaced pavement
SUMMARY
Inclinometer Nos I 1 I 3 and I 4 indicated some shallow depth subsurface ground
movement at their respective instrument locations However Inclinometer No I 2 did not
indicate a pattern that suggests significant subsurface ground lateral movement is
occurring at this time It may be too early to conclude based on the limited monitoring
period and locations Our piezometer data indicates that subsurface water seepage

Based on the interpretation from Inclinometer Nos 2 and 3 it appears that the

ground surface During the following monitoring period Inclinometer No 4 may be able
to further define the depth of the movement and determine feasible remediation
measures for the proposed project
