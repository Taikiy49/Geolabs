PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located within the eastern portion of the Ewa by Gentry
Residential Development Area in the District of Ewa on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The
project site is bounded by Iroquois Point Road on the north Area 19A on the West and
Area 21 on the East A Project Location Map is shown on Plate 1 The new roadway is
about 48 feet wide and 974 feet long and will accommodate traffic in two directions
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services
pertaining to the site grading operations and infrastructure installation for the project site
The initial portions of the site grading and utility installation work were observed by
Fewell Geotechnical Engineering Ltd FGE
SITE GRADING AND UTILITY INSTALLATION

During the site grading operations samples of the on site and imported materials utilized
as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing The
maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory
in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor and the
test results are summarized on Plate 2
The fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as
determined in accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557 In the roadway areas the fill
and backfill materials within 2 feet of the finished subgrade were compacted to a minimum
of 95 percent relative density
During the site grading and utility backfilling operations periodic field density tests
were performed in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 2922 Nuclear
Gauge Method Results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 3 1
through 3 7 of this report These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces
and on constructed fills and backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were
estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and
elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used
Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum
compaction was obtained and or approved by our field representative based on visual
observation and hand probing
Our services consisted of part time field observation of the contractor s fill
placement subgrade preparation and compaction procedures and confirmation of
subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above grading operations
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
