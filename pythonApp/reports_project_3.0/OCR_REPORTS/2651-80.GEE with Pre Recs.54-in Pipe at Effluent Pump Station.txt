FINDINGS AND DISCUSSIONS
Based on our field exploration in December 1994 very soft to soft silty clays
extending to depths of about 20 to 25 feet below the existing ground surface were
generally encountered near and below the pipe invert In general the soft soils extend
to depths of about 10 to 15 feet below the pipe invert
Based on laboratory consolidation tests performed on samples of the soft clayey
soils the soft soils appear to have stabilized under the present conditions In addition
based on hand level survey of the ground surface elevations above the concrete pipe
alignment it appears that no significant ground settlements were noted within the past
6 months Therefore we do not believe that the concrete pipe would be subjected to
further appreciable settlement beyond the conditions and profile that it is at presently
Based on the construction records field exploration and laboratory test results
we do not believe that the pipe settlement observed is the result of consolidation of the
soft soils due to loads of the concrete pipe and backfill material or due to dewatering
of the trench excavation during construction It is likely that the observed pipe
settlement is due to disturbance of the very soft soils during installation of the pipe and

subsoil conditions and the construction methods appear to be the probable causes of

Settlement Characteristics of the Soft Soils
Based on the laboratory consolidation tests performed on samples of the soft to
medium stiff silty clays below the pipe invert the very soft to soft soils may be
considered normally consolidated i e the tested clay samples are generally stable
under the present overburden pressure concrete pipe backfill material and normal
groundwater conditions The concrete pipe is supported on about 3 feet of gravels
wrapped in a filter fabric above the soft soils Based on our estimates we do not
anticipate an increase in loading on the soft soils due to the concrete pipe and backfill
material In fact we estimate a decrease in loading of about 1 500 pounds per lineal
foot assuming that the pipe is half full Therefore it is unlikely that the weight of the
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2651 80 Page 9
concrete pipe and the backfill material above the pipe resulted in settlement of the soft
soils
in general soft clayey soils will consolidate when subjected to additional loading
As previously indicated the soft clayey samples tested indicate that the material is
generally stable under its present conditions with the concrete pipe backfill material
and groundwater conditions Potential sources of additional loading on the soft soils
that may cause consolidation of the soft soils would be due to significant lowering of
the groundwater table such as dewatering Based on the construction records the
groundwater in the pipe trench excavation was pumped down to about 1 foot below the
pipe invert or a total drawdown of about 5 feet below the static groundwater level
Based on this amount of groundwater drawdown we do not anticipate a significant
amount of settlement from consolidation of the soft clays due to short term groundwater
drawdown Based on our analyses we believe that significant settlement of the soft
clays due to dewatering would take several years to occur with virtually continuous
dewatering Therefore the significant settlement of the concrete pipe observed due to
dewatering efforts for this project does not appear to be a likely cause
Based on our laboratory testing of the soft clayey soils the soft clayey soils may
be classified as sensitive to highly sensitive Sensitivity is a qualitative parameter to
evaluate how the clayey soil would react when remolded or subjected to disturbance
Based on the high moisture contents of the soft clayey soils between 90 and 100
percent by dry weight the soft clayey soils would be susceptible to a significant loss
of strength when disturbed This significant loss of strength would result in settlements
of the soft soils However the magnitude of this type of settlement is dependent on the
construction procedures and is very difficult to quantify We believe that the significant
pipe settlement observed may be the result of disturbance to the soft soils at the site

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling
three borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 3 at the approximate locations
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The approximate depths of our borings are generally
about 46 to 66 feet below the existing ground surface The borings were drilled
using a truck mounted drill rig equipped with continuous flight augers or rotary drilling
tools as appropriate
The materia ountere d in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified
Soil Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representation of the
materials encountered are presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 1 through
A 3 2
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils
by driving a 3 inch O D Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling
30 inches The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the last 12 inches of an 18
inch drive are shown on the logs of borings at the appropriate sample depths
Relatively undisturbed samples of the soft to medium stiff deposits were re
trieved using a Shelby tube sampler The Shelby tube sampler using a 3 inch dia
meter and 30 inch long tube was hydraulically pushed into the soil formation by the drill
rig The hydraulic pressures needed to advance the sampler 1 foot to obtain the
Shelby tube samples are recorded as the Penetration Resistance in pounds per square
inch psi per foot of penetration on the Logs of Borings
Torvane shear tests were performed on selected cohesive soil samples retrieved
in the field The torvane shear test provides an indication of the cohesive strength of
the sample Results of the torvane shear tests are summarized on the logs of borings
at the appropriate sample depths

summarized on the logs of borings at the appropriate sampie depths

samples of the soils to evaluate the gradation characteristics of the soils and to aid in
soil classification Graphic presentation of the grain size distribution is presented on

compressible soils to evaluate the compressibility characteristics of the materials
