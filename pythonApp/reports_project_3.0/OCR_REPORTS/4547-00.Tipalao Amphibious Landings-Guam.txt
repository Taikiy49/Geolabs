SECTION 1 0 INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration performed
for the Tipalao Amphibious Landings and Vehicle Wash Facility project located within the U S
Naval Base in Guam Mariana Islands The general location and vicinity of the project site are
shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings from our field exploration and laboratory testing

the proposed project These recommendations are intended for the preliminary design of
pavements building foundations retaining structures and site grading only The findings and
recommendations presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this

SECTION 4 0 REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The Island of Guam is the largest and southernmost of the Mariana Island archipelago
which is an island arc parallel to the trend of the subduction zone forming the Mariana Trench
The Mariana Trench is part of a belt of trenches which extend from Japan to the Palau Islands
and include the Japan Trench Mariana Trench Yap Trench and Palau Trench
The Island of Guam can be broadly divided into three geologic provinces the southern
mountains the central mountains and the northern plateau The formation of the island
started during the Eocene Epoch by the eruption of basaltic rhyolite lavas The mountainous
central portion of the island is composed of these Eocene volcanic rocks and conglomerates
tuffs and limestones of Eocene and Oligocene in age Lava flows and limestone formations
of Miocene Epoch make up the majority of the southern one third of the island The plateaus
of the northern portion of the island were formed while this part of the island was still beneath
the sea level During the Oligocene and Miocene Epochs a complex series of coral and algal
reefs formed an elongated platform of limestones and related deposits Tectonic activity has
lifted these coral algal limestones above sea level to form the northern Guam Plateau
Weathering and solution working of the limestone has partially re crystallized the
calcium carbonate of the limestone forming massive limestones in some areas and poorly to
well cemented detrital limestones in others Typically the detrital limestones are highly
variable in strength characteristics and are frequently karstic or cavernous in nature
In addition the limestone formations are generally porous Except during heavy storms the
majority of the precipitation naturally percolates through the formation producing little or no
surface water runoff
Typically the surface of the underlying limestone is irregular due to solution working
Frequently formations such as those encountered at the project site will have buried pinnacles
of massive limestone with spaces between the pinnacles filled with weathered material and
detrital material

SECTION 5 0 GEOLOGIC AND SEISMIC HAZARDS EVALUATION
Guam is designated as Seismic Zone 3 by the Navy NAVFAC P 355 Seismic Design
For Buildings handbook Therefore we conducted an evaluation of possible geologic and
seismic hazards that may impact the development of the proposed project An evaluation of
geologic and seismic hazards typically addresses surface fault rupture ground shaking
ground failure liquefaction lateral spreading and ground lurching tsunamis and seiches and
seismically induced flooding
As previously discussed Guam is situated adjacent to the subduction zone that forms
the Mariana Trench The island is tectonically active and has experienced approximately
20 earthquakes exceeding magnitude 7 0 since 1902 Of these events fifteen occurred prior
to 1950 and five have occurred subsequent to that date Several large faults and fault zones
that traverse across the Island of Guam include the following Pago Bay Adelup Point Fault
River Valley Fault Zone Tamuning Barrigada Fault Pugua Fault and
Mount Santa Rosa horst block
The proximity of the project site to faults and fault zones is a major consideration when
evaluating geologic and seismic hazards Of the major faults and fault zones mentioned
above the trace of the Mount Santa Rosa horst block lies closest to the project site at
approximately 4 kilometers to the east of the project site
Based on our evaluation of the project site we do not believe that the geologic and
seismic hazards described above are significant considerations for this project With the
exception of moderate to strong ground shaking we believe that the potential for surface fault
rupture ground failure liquefaction lateral spreading and ground lurching tsunamis and
seiches and seismically induced flooding are considered to be low at this project site

SECTION 6 0 CLIMATE
The Island of Guam is located in the North Pacific Ocean between 144 degrees
36 minutes and 144 degrees 58 minutes longitude and 13 degrees 13 minutes and 13 degrees
40 minutes latitude as shown on the Project Location Vicinity Map Plate 1 In general the
climate of Guam is warm and humid with little variation in temperature from month to month
Based on information obtained from the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration
NOAA Guam s mean annual temperature is about 27 degrees Celsius throughout the year
Typically annual rainfall averages from about 80 to 100 inches per year The rainy season
is during months of July through November when monthly rainfall can average over 12 inches
per month
Tropical storms and typhoons often pass through or near the Island of Guam Tropical
storms are defined as closed pressure systems with wind speeds ranging from 40 to 75 miles
per hour Typhoons are similar phenomena with wind speeds exceeding 75 miles per hour
During the rainy season tropical storms and typhoons are common in the vicinity of Guam
Tropical storms and typhoons bring high intensity rainfall when their zone of influence is near
Guam In extreme events in the past a typhoon has produced winds of over 175 miles per
hour over 11 feet of inundation and 25 inches of rainfall in one day

SECTION 9 0 DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Our field exploration generally encountered topsoil and fill materials underlain by beach
deposits and limestone formation The beach deposits were generally encountered at the
west and west central portions of the project site and consisted of loose to dense coralline
sand and gravel The limestone formation encountered at the project site generally ranged
from soft to medium hard However the borings indicated that localized hard and crystallized
areas are present in the limestone formations
The basis of our preliminary pavement design was derived from TI
Pavement Design for Roads Streets Walks and Open Storage Areas Based on these design
manuals a portland cement concrete pavement over aggregate base course may be used for
the proposed ramp and taxiway Based on the available plans we anticipate that portions of
the ramp may be located near or below water Therefore we recommend that free draining
granular materials wrapped in a non woven filter fabric be used in lieu of the aggregate base
course for areas near or below water level
Based on the available plans the proposed ramp will be constructed near the existing
shoreline We believe that the near surface soils coralline sand and gravel and the aggregate

at the shoreline To reduce the potential for scouring of the soils below the pavement structure
at the existing shoreline we recommend that concrete cut off walls be constructed along the
perimeter of the pavement structures at the existing shoreline The concrete cut off walls
should be embedded a minimum of 24 inches into the medium hard limestone rock
encountered in our borings

9 4 Foundation Probing
Based on our experience in the area voids and or cavities may be present in the
limestone formations encountered at the project site To reduce the potential for loss of
foundation support resulting from collapse of cavities below a footing we recommend that
a program of cavity probing and grouting be conducted during construction for the proposed
control building

footing location and at 10 feet on center along wall foundations The probeholes should be
at least 3 inches in diameter and should extend at least 10 feet below the planned bottom of
footing elevation If cavities and or voids are encountered or suspected during the probing
operation additional probeholes should be drilled at closer spacing to aid in delineating the
vertical and lateral extent of the cavity and or void The probeholes and cavities discovered
should be backfilled with sand cement grout injected at low to moderate pressures A typical
sand cement grout should have a slump of about 6 to 8 inches
Because of the potential for encountering cavities and or voids at the site we
recommend that unit prices for additional probing and grouting be obtained during bidding
In addition the probe drill should be available on site until the probing and grouting operations
are completed The contractor should also be made aware that a longer lag time between
probing grouting operations and foundation construction may be required in the construction
schedule

We consider the slabs on grade for the control building to be industrial type slabs

Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by frictional resistance developed
between the bottom of the foundation and the limestone formation and by passive earth
pressure acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system A coefficient
of friction of 0 50 may be used for footings bearing on the limestone Resistance due
to passive earth pressure may be estimated using equivalent fluid pressures of 400
pounds per square foot of depth pcf above the water level and 250 pcf below the
water level This assumes that the soils around the footings are well compacted
Unless covered by pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the upper 12 inches
of soil should be neglected

Controlled and structural fills and backfills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding

Excavation
Based on our field exploration the project site is underlain by a limestone formation
near the existing ground surface The limestone formation encountered in our borings
appeared to be severely fractured and ranged from soft to medium hard Our borings
indicate that the limestone formation generally breaks down into sand and gravel It
is anticipated that the majority of the limestone formation may be excavated with
normal heavy excavation equipment such as excavating with large backhoe
excavators However limestone formations typically contain localized hard and
crystallized zones Therefore we anticipate that difficult excavation conditions may
arise during construction at localized areas It may be necessary to excavate the
trenches in some areas by using hoe ram or by chipping

performed by E L Pacific to evaluate the corrosivity of the in situ soils to buried concrete in
contact with the soils The results of these tests are presented on Plate B 1
Two pH tests ASTM G 51 were performed on selected soil samples retrieved from our
field exploration program to determine the pH values of the soils The results of the tests are
presented on Plate B 1
Two sieve analysis tests ASTM C 136 and D 1140 were performed on selected
samples of the soils to evaluate the gradation characteristics and to aid in soil classification
Graphic presentation of the grain size distribution is provided on Plate B 2
One direct shear test ASTM D 3080 was performed on a selected sample to evaluate
the shear strength characteristics of the materials tested Results of the direct shear test are
presented on Plate B 3
Five laboratory California Bearing Ratio tests ASTM D 1883 were performed bulk
samples of the near surface soils to evaluate the pavement support characteristics of the soils
A summary of the test results are presented on Plate B 4

Percolation tests were performed by using the falling head method in two of the borings
drilled Boring Nos 3 and 4 The test borings have or had a diameter of 8 5 to 9 inches and
depths of 4 to 6 feet below the existing ground surface at the time of testing Water was
injected into the test borings and the drop in water level with respect to time was recorded
The water levels within the test borings were monitored by an electric water level sounder
The results of the percolation tests are presented on Plates C 1 and C 2
