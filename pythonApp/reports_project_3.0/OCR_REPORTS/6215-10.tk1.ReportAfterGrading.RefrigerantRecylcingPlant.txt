PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Refrigerant Recycling Plant project site is at Street in the
Kenai Industrial Park in the Kapolei area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The property is an
irregular shaped parcel encompassing approximately 71 275 square feet in land area
Based on the information provided it is desired to develop the site into a refrigerant
recycling plant consisting of a warehouse structure and office space totaling approximately
28 420 square feet in floor area In addition asphaltic concrete pavements subjected to
container truck traffic also are planned to serve the new facility The layout of the new
facility is shown on the Site Plan Plate 1

The finished floor elevation of the new warehouse is set at 11 6 feet Mean Sea
Level MSL We understand the project site has been stripped of the near surface soils
due to chemical contamination of the soils to expose the underlying subsurface
materials Site grading work generally consisted of backfilling the excavation with up to
about 4 to 6 feet of imported fill materials to achieve the design finished grades The
scope of work discussed in this letter report includes observation and compaction testing
during the earthwork and infrastructure installation for the refrigerant recycling plant
project
EARTHWORK OPERATIONS
Jayar Construction Inc performed the site grading operations pavement and
utility line construction for the Refrigerant Recycling Plant project The site grading work
consisted of subgrade preparation placement and compaction of fills and backfills
pavement construction and utility trench excavation and backfill
Following the clearing operations the excavated subgrades underneath future slab
and pavement areas were scarified to a depth of about 8 inches moisture conditioned to
above the optimum moisture content and proof rolled with a minimum 10 ton vibratory
drum roller to obtain a minimum of 90 and 95 percent relative compaction under the
building slab and pavement areas respectively of the maximum dry density determined in
accordance with American Society of Testing and Material ASTM D 1557
Modified Proctor
Fill and backfill materials required for the project generally were placed in near level
lifts brought to above the optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of 90 or
95 percent under the building slab and pavement areas respectively of the maximum dry
density determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor
In order to reduce the potential for appreciable footing settlement and slab cracking
resulting from differing subgrade soil conditions a layer of geogrid
was placed below the building footprint to provide for uniform support The geogrid was
placed at a depth of approximately 24 inches below the bottom of footing elevation and
extended out a minimum of 5 feet beyond the building perimeter Adjacent rolls of geogrid
were overlapped a minimum of 12 inches during placement In addition the geogrid was
secured to the prepared ground surface prior to the placement of fills on top
During the site grading operations samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test
results are summarized on Plate 2

Field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations on an
as need basis to confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures
and soil conditions were noted A total of 168 field density tests including retests of failed
areas were performed using the nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM D 6938
Results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 10 of
this report These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on
constructed fills and backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by
pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should
be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed
to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the earthwork operations during the site grading
pavement and utility line construction for the above project observed and tested by
Geolabs in the period covered by this report were performed in general accordance with
the intent of the project plans and specifications and our geotechnical recommendations
presented in the above referenced geotechnical engineering report August 14 2009 and
subsequent recommendations
LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork were
performed without our observation However the level of earthwork observation and
testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some of the
geotechnical risks to the project It should be recognized that there are variations in the
accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor
earthwork construction and materials
