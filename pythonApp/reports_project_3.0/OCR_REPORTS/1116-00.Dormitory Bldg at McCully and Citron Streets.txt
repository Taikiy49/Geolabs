This report presents the results of our foundation investiga
tion for the above referenced project Our work was done in
general accordance with the scope of services outlined in our
proposal dated March 9 1982
The purpose of our study was to investigate the subsurface soil
conditions by drilling test borings Data obtained from these
borings and laboratory testing of soil samples were utilized to
provide recommendations for the design of the foundation of the
proposed dormitory building

PROPOSED DEVELOPMENT
The project site is located at the corner of McCully and Citron
Streets in the McCully area of metropolitan Honolulu The
general vicinity of the site is shown on Project Location Plan
Plate l
The proposed dormitory project will consist of a 4 story
building of concrete masonry unit construction approximately
68 by 28 feet in plan dimensions with an attached Sing le story
office wing of approximately 70 by 16 feet in plan dimension
The first floors will be parochial and service areas and the
upper three floors will be composed of dormitory style residen
tial units An automobile parking area will be provided in the
back of the property A general layout of the project is shown
on Boring Location Plan Plate 2
Maximum ultimate column loads are anticipated to be on the
order of 200 kips Maximum ultimate wall loads are anticipated
to be on the order of 10 kips per lineal foot
Minimal site grading is anticipated

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
The site is located on the Honolulu Coastal Plain which is com
posed of interbedded alluvial and marine deposits In the area
of the project site marine coral deposits from the Waimanalo
25 stand of the sea have been graded to near sea level by
wave action during a sea stand slightly higher than the present
one This grading resulted in a low marshy area which was
filled during land reclamation activities in the first half of
this century
Subsurface Exploration
Four borings were drilled at the site to depths between 15 0
and 26 5 feet below the existing ground surface to explore the
subsurface conditions and to obtain samples for laboratory
testing The approximate locations of the borings are shown on
the Boring Location Plan Plate 2

Descriptions of our field exploration and laboratory testing
are presented in the appendices along with the logs of borings

Soil Conditions
Based on the borings the site is underlain by 2 to 4 feet of
miscellaneous fill materials consisting mainly of medium stiff
brown to reddish brown clayey silts
Below this surficial fill layer the subsoils grade to loose
silty sands and soft organic silts which represents the natural
marshy soil deposits This layer extends to depths ranging
from 5 to 8 feet where it is underlain by dense silty sands of
either marine or volcanic origin were encountered At Boring
1 the dense silty sand is of weathered cinder and believed to
have been deposited in a depression in the underlying coral
reef This silty sand extend to a depth of 14 feet
Underlying the dense silty sands at depths between 6 0 and 14 0
feet the borings encountered coral algal reef materials to
26 5 feet the maximum depth drilled
Groundwater was encountered at about 4 to 5 foot depths during
the field exploration

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
We believe that the upper recent fill and the underlying loose
marshy deposits cannot provide adequate bearing capacity with
out excessive settlements under the dormitory building loads
To provide adequate bearing for the perimeter and column foot
ings the foundations should be extended to the underlying
dense sand stratum
To provide uniform subgrade support for the slab on grade at
portions of the building we recommend that the building area
be proof rolled with a heavy vibratory roller and to detect
soft pockets
Demolition and Cleaning
The existing structures including all slabs concrete blocks
and foundations should be removed from the site Shrubs and
trees should be grubbed to at least 12 inches below the exist
ing grade within the grading areas The top 6 inches of the
existing lawn area should be stripped This topsoil may be
stockpiled and be reused as topsoil in the landscape areas
Any drywell or cesspool if encountered should be cleaned
thoroughly and backfilled with suitable material That portion
of backfill material underwater should consist of coarse
crushed stone such as No 2 Coarse The material immediately
GEOLABS HAWAII

above the coarse rock should consist of 12 inches of base

Proof Rolling
After the site has been cleared and grubbed and prior to the
foundation excavation the building areas to 5 feet outside the
building line should be proof rolled In addition the park
ing area should also be cleared to the subgrade and proof
rolled The proof rolling should be made with a vibratory com
pactor capable of delivering a total impact loading in excess
of 25 000 Foot pounds energy or pneumatic tired roller weighing
at least 20 tons This proof rolling should consist of a mini
mum of 6 passes over any given spot
Any soft or yielding areas detected during this proof rolling
should be over excavated to competent material and backfilled
with compacted Select Borrow material
The proof rolling and compaction operations should be monitored
by the soils engineer to check the soft or yielding areas and
that a proper procedure and an adequate compaction of the back
fill are being done

Foundation
For the proposed structure spread or continuous footing foun
dations are recommended The footings should extend at least 6
inches into the medium dense to dense Silty sands encountered
about 6 to 7 feet below the existing ground surface Due to
the high groundwater level it will be necessary to dewater the
foundation excavations
It is our opinion that the contractor should be able to dewater
the excavation The preferred method would be to utilize a
number of deep wells pre installed at various portions of the
site prior to excavation Caution should be given to the con
tractor that inadequate dewatering operations tay disturb the
in situ dense sands and thereby lower the density and bearing
capacity of the foundation bearing material Extensive repair
work will be required if such a condition occurs
Dewatering operations should be monitored by the soils engineer
to observe that the procedures are properly implemented and
that boiling of the sand at the bottom of the footing excava
tion does not occur

An allowable bearing capacity of 3500 p s f may be used for
footings bearing on undisturbed medium dense to dense sands
below the marshy deposit The above bearing value may be
increased by one third when considering temporary wind or
seismic loads
For the above recommendations estimated foundation settlements
are on the order of three fourths of an inch total and one
fourth to one half inch differential
The above bearing value and settlement estimates are based on
good dewatering performance by the contractor
To resist lateral loads a coefficient of friction of 0 45 may
be used between the foundations and medium dense to dense in
situ sands Additionally a passive soil resistance of 300
p c f may be used
Cavity Probing
To detect potential cavities in the coral algal reef material
below the footings it is recommended that small diameter probe
holes be drilled at each column location or on 8 foot centers
along continuous wall footings The probe holes should be
drilled to a minimum of 10 feet below the bottom of the
GEOLABS HAWAIl
W O 1116 00 Page 9
footing The resulting probe holes should be backfilled with
neat cement grout mix injected at low pressures This grouting
of probe holes will aid in detecting other potential voids
below the footings
We recommend that the probing and grouting be monitored by the
soils engineer
We also recommend that all footing excavations be inspected by
a soils engineer to confirm the conditions upon which our
recommendations are based Any loose soil fallen into the
footing excavations should be removed prior to concrete place
ment
Slab On Grade
Slab on grade should be placed over a 4 inch cushion layer of
gravel size crushed rock material such as No 3 Fine rock to
act as a capillary break and to provide uniform support An
impervious membrane should be placed under the slab to act as
vapor barrier
The subgrade soils after proof rolling should be kept moist
and undisturbed If deemed necessary it should be recompacted
prior to placing the gravel cushion

Site Grading
Although site grading is anticipated to be minimum some cut
and fill may be needed for the construction of building pads
and parking pavements In addition footing excavations of
about 8 feet and related backfill work are anticipated
Generally imported material will be required for fill Such a
material should conform to the requirements stipulated in the
Select Borrow for Subbase Course Section 30 of the Standard
Specification For Public Works Construction May 1975 City
County of Honolulu
Other materials may be used as fill however they should first
be tested and approved by a soils engineer before they are in
ported The fill materials should be non expansive form a
dense stable soil mass when compacted and provide subgrade
Support of at least CBR 25 when compacted to a dry density of
95 of the maximum dry density determined in accordance with
the ASTM D 1557 test procedure
All 111 should be placed in lifts not exceeding 6 inches and
compacted to a dry density of at least 95 of the maximum dry
density

The backfilling in the footing excavation may need special
attention If the groundwater is lowered by deepwell de
watering the backfilling may be made in dry and thus may be
constructed in a similar manner as fill The compaction den
sity may be lowered to 90 of the maximum density for the por
tion below 2 5 feet of the finish grade The compaction den
Sity within the top 2 5 feet should be 95 of the maximum den
sity ASTM D 1557
Where underwater backfilling is required the water should be
lowered and an open graded clean crushed rock such as No 2
Coarse be placed in lifts not exceeding 10 inches and com
pacted thoroughly The compaction should be observed and
approved by a soils engineer There should be a 12 inch thick
layer of well graded crushed rock such as aggregate base
course or concrete dry mix over the open graded rock to serve
as filter zone The purpose of this filter zone is to prevent
the migration of fines into the voids of coarse rock fragments
below The remainder of the backfill should conform to the
recommendations presented in the preceeding paragraph

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by
drilling 0 to 26 5 feet below the existing ground
surface at the approximate locations shown on the Boring Loca
tion Plan Plate 2 Truck mounted drilling equipment was used
to drilled all of the borings
The soils encountered in the borings were examined and
classified visually in the field by our engineer who contin
uously monitored the drilling operations The Classifications
were further reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the
laboratory The soils were classified in general conformance
with the Unified Soil Classification System A graphic presen
tation of surface materials encountered the soils is presented
on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 4
Soil samples were obtained from the boring by driving
barrel sampler with a 140 pound hammer free falling at a height
of 30 inches In addition hydraulically pressed thin wall
shelby tube sampler was also used where necessary to obtain
undisturbed samples of fine grained soils Conical drive point
penetrometer driven by a 140 pound hammer free falling 30
inches was also used The blow counts to drive the sampler the
last 12 inches are shown on the logs of borings at the appro
priate sample depths

Laboratory Testing
Moisture content determinations were performed on
selected samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation
of soil properties The results of these tests are presented
on the logs of borings Plates A 1l through A 4 at the
appropriate sample depth
One California Bearing Ratio CBR test was performed on
the near surface material to evaluate the Suitability of the
soil as pavement support The test result is presented on
Plate B l
Three sieve analyses were performed on samples of the
underlying soils to evaluate their grain size distributions
The test results are presented on Plate B l
One one inch ring swell test was performed on an undis
turbed sample of the near surface soil to evaluate its swelling
potential The test result is summarized on Plate B 2
