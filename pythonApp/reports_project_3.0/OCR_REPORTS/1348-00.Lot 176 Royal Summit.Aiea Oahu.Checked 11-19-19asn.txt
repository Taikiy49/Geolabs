Dear Mr Mrs Rush
In accordance with your request we have made a study of the near
surface soil conditions at the above referenced lot in order to
provide foundation recommendations for the proposed residence The
study consisted of drilling three shallow borings laboratory testing
of the swelling potential of the soils and reviewing of the
subdivision site grading and preliminary house foundation plans This
report summarizes our findings and recommendations
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
According to the preliminary house plan prepared by
the proposed residence will be a two story structure approximately 43
by 66 feet in plan dimensions with a 2 car garage

Concrete on grade slabs are planned for the basement level patio
driveway and garage area
CMU bearing walls are planned for the entire lower level of the house
and wood construction for the main floor level CRM retaining walls
up to 12 feet high are anticipated to provide grade separation along
the rear of the residence
Cuts up to 5 feet deep are anticipated below the residence to
accommodate the proposed basement level and up to 10 feet deep at the
rear of the residence to re shape the existing ground surface
The proposed layout of the house and the garage is shown on the Site
Plan Plate 1
SITE CONDITIONS
The subject lot is located on the northern side of Kuini Street in the
Royal Summit Phase 1 Subdivision in Aiea Oahu Hawaii
The lot slopes upward from Kuini Street at about 5 1 H V grade to a
level pad about 40 feet wide in plan dimension and is located 65 feet
from the roadway The ground surface then continues to slope upward
at 2 1 H V and is bisected by an 8 foot wide bench for a total
vertical slope height of about 16 feet

During the time of our exploration the ground was covered with tall

SUBSURFACE SOIL CONDITIONS
Two borings were drilled within the proposed building location and one
boring at the proposed wall location to depths varying from 7 5 to
10 0 feet below the existing grade at the approximate locations shown
on the Site Plan Plate 1
The borings encountered reddish brown clayey silt to the full depth
drilled Boring 2 encountered a thin layer of mottled gray brown
clayey silt from 4 5 feet to 6 0 feet below the existing surface
The mass grading plan for Royal Summit Phase 1 dated October 31
1980 prepared by Community Planning Inc indicated that lot 176 was
cut from 1 to 6 feet below the natural grade Some amount of fills
generally less than 7 feet were placed in the rear slope area and the
southwestern corner of the lot
No groundwater was encountered at the time of our field exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The stiff reddish brown clayey silt soil encountered will generally be
suitable for foundation support of the proposed house provided that
some care is exercised during construction Laboratory test on the
samples obtained from the borings indicate that the in situ moisture
content is quite dry and ranges from 22 to 29 percent
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1348 00 Page 4
The field exploration and laboratory testing performed on the stiff
clayey silt soil indicates that this material generally has low
swelling potential if it is kept moist Previous experience
indicates that its swelling potential may increase to moderate range
if allowed to dry out Therefore to minimize the swelling potential
the subgrade soil should be kept moist after excavation and protected
from drying during the period of time required for preparation prior
to final concrete placement
House Foundations
Spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the proposed
residence An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f may be used on
the residual soil
Interior footings on level areas should be embedded a minimum of 12
inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade Exterior wall
footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the adjacent
finished grade
If located on a sloping area the footing should be embedded down an
additional amount to obtain a 6 foot minimum horizontal set back
distance measured from the outer edge of the footing out to the slope
face This additional embedment depth is required to help resist the
downhill slope creep movement and to develop the recommended bearing
Capacity of the soil

The recommended subgrade preparation prior to final concrete placement
as described under the Slab On Grade section is also required
Slab On Grade
Concrete slab on grade is planned for the garage driveway patio and
the lower level of the house It is recommended that a minimum
18 inch perimeter footing embedment measured from the lowest adjacent
finished grade be utilized to provide a barrier against future water
intrusion under the slab area
To further minimize the swelling potential of the soil special
subgrade preparation with lime saturation is recommended The general
procedure listed hereunder should be followed
1 Hydrated lime should be placed on the subgrade soils and the footing
excavations prior to water saturating of the subgrade soil We
believe that about fifteen 50 1b bags of hydrated lime would be
adequate to cover these areas
Z The slab subgrade soils should be water saturated by frequent
sprinkling for at least 2 days prior to concrete placement in order
to reduce the swelling potential of the underlying subsoil It is
important that the soils engineer should be notified to inspect the
above subgrade preparation and check the moisture contents of the
underlying soils prior to pouring of the slab and perimeter footing

3 The bottom of the finished subgrade and footing excavations should
be neat and free of loose soils It is anticipated that some very
soft silt and lime mud will be deposited at the footing after
saturation These soft mud should be removed just prior to concrete
placement
4 The concreting of footing and slab should be coordinated in such a
way that the time period between the initial excavation and the
final concrete placement should be kept to a minimum in order to
prevent excessive subgrade drying
5 The above recommendations for saturating the subgrade with hydrated
lime should also be followed for the sidewalk driveway and patio
Slabs
It is recommended that on grade slabs be placed over a 4 inch layer of
well graded aggregate base course and separated from wall foundations
A plastic membrane should be placed over the base course layer if a
moisture barrier is deemed necessary to protect the floor covering

Drainage
The finish lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings should be
shaped to shed water away from the foundations and to avoid ponding
condition Also it is advised that gutter systems should be
installed around the house and that gutter water should be diverted
far away from the foundations and walkways Excessive landscape
watering near the foundations and slab should also be avoided
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils

These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance
of the above recommended foundations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of
strength The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled
tight against slab footing immediately after setting of the concrete
to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained to
drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundation
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the footing excavations and backfill operations
be observed by the soils engineer during construction to see that
adequate bearing has been obtained and that the subsurface conditions
are consistent with the findings of our subsurface investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the above
inspections
