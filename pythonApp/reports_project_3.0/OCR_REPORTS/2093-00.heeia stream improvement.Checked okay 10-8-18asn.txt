As requested we have explored the subsurface conditions below the
proposed retaining wall at the above referenced site The purpose of our
exploration was to evaluate the suitability of the subsoils for foundation
support of the proposed retaining wall The study consisted of drilling
and sampling two 2 borings along the wall alignment performing
laboratory soil tests and engineering analyses This report summarizes
our findings and recommendations
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to fill the stream area to create additional level
space In order to accomplish this the stream flow will be routed
through a CMP culvert below the new fill and a retaining wall is planned
along the western boundary of the stream lot Fills up to 17 feet high
are anticipated

EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The subject area is situated at the rear of Lot 6 30
73 and extends from the end of the existing concrete slab to the rear of
Haiku Elementary School The area encompasses a portion of the existing
Keaahala Stream The stream flows through the middle of the area and is
about 15 to 20 feet lower than the adjacent developed areas The site is
partially overgrown with grass and trees and partially occupied by recent
fills
Keaahala Stream flows into a CMP headwall and concrete lined open
channel at the eastern edge of the lot
The ground surface elevations at the site generally vary from 102 to
122 feet above MSL
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions at the proposed retaining wall location
were explored by drilling and sampling two 2 borings to depths of 14 to
18 5 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate locations of
the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
The borings encountered 3 to 10 feet of soft organic soils over
stiffer residual soil or older alluvium Groundwater was encountered at
or near the ground surface near the stream
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The two borings encountered soft compressible soils near the
lowlying areas along the stream These soft soils are not suitable for
foundation support of the proposed retaining wall and high fill
Therefore one of the two alternatives described below is recommended

Overexcavation and Replacement Method
The soft soils could be overexcavated down to the stiff
alluvium residual soil which was encountered at depths of 3 and 10 feet
in the borings The excavation should then be backfilled with compacted
select borrow to provide a suitable bearing layer for the wall
Temporary diversion of the stream and dewatering of the excavation
would be required for this alternative
Following overexcavation and replacement of the soft soils the wall
design parameters presented below may be used
An allowable bearing value of 3500 p s f on the compacted fill may
be used for the wall footing foundation
Well graded granular material such as base course rock 3B Fine
gravel sand or equivalent should be used as backfill material directly
behind the walls This would reduce the compactive effort required for
the backfill and the build up of additional lateral pressures on the
walls Impervious on site silty material should be used for the top 2
feet of the backfill
We recommend that the walls be designed to resist lateral earth
pressures as follows
1 Level Backfill ky 35 p c f equivalent fluid pressure
Sloping Backfill ky 59 p c f equivalent fluid pressure
Ky 20 p c f foot
Additional loads due to traffic or surcharge should be included where
applicable
A friction factor of 0 4 between concrete and soil can be utilized to
determine the sliding resistance of the wall

Additionally a passive soil resistance of 300 p c f for soil can be
used
A subsurface drainage system should be used to prevent the build up
of hydrostatic pressure behind the walls
Use Slope in Lieu of Wall
If the soft soils are not removed we recommend that the retaining
wall be omitted and the fills be sloped down to the lot boundary
To reduce the possibility of slope instability due to the soft ground
conditions we recommend that the slope ratio be limited to 2 horizontal
to 1 vertical
A riprap lining is recommended for the portions of the slope subject
to flood waters
Settlement of Existing Drainage Channel
A headwall structure and high fills are also planned adjacent to the
existing drainage channel Although borings were not drilled in this
area it is our opinion that similar soft soil conditions exist
Therefore the weight of the new fill could cause settlement in the
adjacent drainage channel resulting in some damage to the lining The
estimated zone of settlement would extend for a distance of 5 to 10 feet
from the edge of fill

Ground Preparation
The site should be cleared of vegetation debris and soft materials
Prior to filling the existing ground should be scarified to a
minimm of 6 inches and compacted to a minimm of 85 of its maximm
dry density established by ASTM D 1557 test method
Conventional grubbing and clearing operations are anticipated for the
higher slopes along the drainageway Soft surface soils and high
water level were encountered along the stream area and therefore
the following special stabilization procedures are recommended
A working platform consisting of about 12 inches of granular
material such as 3B Fine gravel over a filter fabric should be
established over the bottom of the drainageway prior to filling The
granular layer will also serve as a drainage blanket to facilitate
settlement due to the weight of the new fill
Fill Material
Additional off site borrow will be required The material should be
tested and approved by the soils engineer prior to transportation to
the site The material should generally be of low expansion
potential and should not contain large percentages of boulders
organic or deleterious material

Drainage Culvert Structure
It is our understanding that two 2 72 inch diameter CYP culvert
structures are planned below the fill Special procedures are required
for the construction of the culvert structures and fill placement to
reduce potential settlement problems
Boring Nos 1 and 2 indicate that soft to very soft alluviun
consisting mainly of clayey silts may be encountered beneath the proposed
culvert location
Upon placement of the proposed high embankment fill along the
drainageway significant ground settlement will occur due to compression
of the soft deposits caused by the new fill load The estimated total
primary settlement due to the new fill is about 1 to 2 feet If the
culvert is installed during filling this settlement would damage the
culvert Therefore we recommend that the installation of the culvert be
delayed until the embankment fill has first been allowed to settle

Because of the low permeability of the soft clayey silt layers the rate
of ground settlement due to the new fill loads will be slow The
laboratory test data indicate that it may take about 1 year for 90 of the
total primary settlement to take place
Settlement gauges should be installed in the area and periodic survey
readings of the gauges should be used to monitor the actual settlement
The readings should be taken by the project surveyor and transmitted to
the soils engineer as soon as available in order that the actual
settlement conditions could be evaluated
During the filling period a section of the drainageway must be left
open and the stream diverted through this area This remaining portion of
the embankment can be constructed after the initial fill is completed and
the new culvert structures placed
General guidelines applicable to the culverts are presented below
Excavation
In order to provide sufficient compaction around the culvert and to
minimize possible damage to the culvert from the compaction
operations and potential fill settlement the general embankment
should first be placed in the drainageway prior to construction of
the culvert Upon completion of the filling a trench should then be
excavated through the compacted fill to the culvert invert and the
pipe placed in the trench
Trench Backfill
Select well draining granular material such as 3B Fine gravel or
sand which could be compacted by water tamping may be used as
backfill to the top of culvert Above this level general embankment
fill may be used in accordance with our previous recommendations for
fill

Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comments
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earthwork recommendations given herein If this review is not made
the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for
misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during earthwork This is to
observe compliance with the design concepts specifications or
recommendations and to allow design changes in the event that subsurface
conditions differ from that anticipated prior to start of construction
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon such
observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made

The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as borings
probes test pits and or observation wells Variations of conditions

Field Exploration

2 borings to depths of about 14 0 to 18 5 feet below the existing ground

Classification System A summary of the materials encountered are
presented in the Boring Logs Plates A 1 and A 2

a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts to drive the
