As requested we have made a study of the near surface soil conditions
below the above referenced lot The purpose of the study was to
evaluate the suitability of the near surface soils as foundation
support for the proposed house The study consisted of three 3 hand
auger probes excavated within the proposed building site laboratory
soil tests and a review of the grading and compaction test record on
the building site This report summarizes our observations and
recommendations
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed single family residence consists of a one story wood
structure approximately 45 by 50 feet in plan dimension with a two car
garage Based on the preliminary plans provided to us the garage
will be set back about 20 feet from Kahena Street

EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
Lot 13 is located on Kahena Street in the eastern portion of the
subdivision development about 850 feet north of Ainapo Street It is
bounded by Kahena Street on the southwest subdivision lots on the
southeast and northwest and a concrete drainage channel on the north
east The lot slopes downward from Kahena Street toward the east at
about 14 percent slope The elevation of Lot 13 generally varies from
171 to 187 feet The existing rectangular concrete drainage channel
is about 10 feet deep
There is an existing 10 foot wide sewer easement extending across the
rear portion of the lot
SUBDIVISION LOT GRADING
Prior to the recent project site grading operation Lot 13 was covered
with a large stockpile of soil and boulders left over from previous
grading operations in the area
During that grading operation the stockpile was removed down to a
depth of 3 feet below the design finished grade and replaced with com
pacted available on site soils The available on site soil consisted
basically of silty clay and clayey silt soils which had been mixed
with some adobe clay pockets from the previous stockpile

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Based on the hand auger probings Lot 13 is underlain by a mixture of
moderately to highly expansive brown clay and gravel to the full depth
of the probings excavated which was 4 5 to 5 5 feet
Detailed description of the soils encountered in hand auger probings
are presented on the attached Log of Hand Probes Plates A 1 through
A 3 The approximate locations of the test pits are shown on the
attached Site Plan Plate 1
Laboratory soil tests on the near surface material indicate that the
soil can be classified as clay CH according to the Unified Soil
Classification System Laboratory one inch ring swell tests indicate
that the soil has a moderate to high shrink swell potential The
laboratory test results are summarized on Plate B 1
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that most of the lot is underlain by
grayish brown clay The laboratory test results on this material
indicated that they have high expansion potential Therefore for Lot
13 the slab on grade and foundation construction would require special
design procedures in order to reduce the shrink swell effect of the
subsoils

House Foundation
For this lot post and beam construction is highly desirable and
recommended due to the sloping terrain and expansive soil conditions
The post and beam construction generally results in lower foundation
costs and the individual footings can be readily adjusted should the
need occur Concrete slab on grade and driveway should also be
properly designed for expansive soil conditions
For post and beam construction we recommend that the expansive clays
be overexcavated down for a depth of 3 feet below the bottom of the
individual post footings and then replaced with non expansive borrow
soils such as crusher waste select borrow or coral compacted to a
minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78 The
spread footings can then be placed on the compacted non expansive soil
Alternatively the individual post footing can be embedded a minimum
depth of 3 feet below the finished ground surface
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f can be used for footings
bearing on the compacted select material or stiff existing ground
If a footing is located next to a utility line it should extend to
the bottom of the utility trench to reduce footing settlement due to
settlement of the trench backfill
Surface water should be diverted away from the foundations

Slab On Grade
Concrete slab on grade is planned for the living room and the garage
The garage and living room area would require several feet of fill in
order to obtain the desired level area Select non expansive borrow
soils should be used for fills The on site clay soils are expansive
and are unsuitable as slab subgrade soils
In order to reduce the expansive effects of the clay the expansive
clays should be overexcavated down for a depth of 3 feet below the
slab subgrade and replaced with non expansive borrow soil such as
select borrow crusher waste or coral compacted to a minimum of 90
of its maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78
Concrete Driveway and Sidewalks
Expansive clay soils were encountered in the driveway area Since the
driveway can tolerate some amount of heaving the clay overexcavation
and replacement with non expansive soil can be reduced to 2 feet below
the driveway subgrade level This requirement should be followed for
a minimum distance of 3 feet beyond the driveway

Joints are recommended at about 8 to 10 foot intervals in the driveway
in order to allow for some relative movement of the concrete
For future sidewalks capping of the clay or overexcavation and
replacement similar to the above driveway design is recommended to
reduce heaving caused by the expansive clay soil
Site Grading
The existing terrain at the site generally slopes down at about 14
percent slope
Minimal site grading is planned for the lot development
Fill and backfill materials should be select non expansive borrow
material placed in maximum 8 inch lifts and compacted to a minimum of
90 of its maximum dry density established by ASTM D 1557 78 The
on site clay soil should not be used for fill material below structures
and behind retaining walls
Prior to filling the area should be cleared of vegetation and debris
The finished grade outside the slabs pavements and foundations
should be shaped to avoid ponding conditions and to shed water away
from the structures

Since the garage and house will be lower than the existing Kahena
Street special provisions should be made to intercept and drain

Retaining Walls
Short retaining walls generally less than 5 feet high are anticipated
around the living room and the garage
In order to reduce the expansive effects of the existing clay soils on
the wall we recommend that the expansive clays be overexcavated down
for a depth of 2 feet below the bottom of the footing and then replaced
with non expansive borrow soils such as crusher waste or coral com
pacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557
The overexcavation and replacement should extend a minimum distance of
12 inches beyond the footing width
The wall footings should be embedded a minimum depth of 12 inches
below the outside finished grade Therefore the total depth of the
above overexcavation and replacement would be 3 feet below the outside
finished grade
A subsurface drainage system subdrain or weepholes should be
utilized behind the wall to prevent the build up of hydrostatic
pressure

Off site non expansive soil should be used as backfill behind the
