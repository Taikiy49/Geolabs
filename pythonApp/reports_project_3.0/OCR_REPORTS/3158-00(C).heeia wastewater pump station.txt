SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed force main will extend west from the existing Heeia Wastewater
Pump Station along Yacht Club Street to Nahiku Street south on Nahiku Street to
Lililpuna Road and west on Lilipuna Road to Kamehameha Highway as shown on the
Spill Location Map Plate 2 Heeia Wastewater Pump Station is located on the north
side of Yacht Club Street at the intersection with Yacht Club Place The pump station
adjoins Kaneohe Bay to the north
The proposed force main will be approximately 1 800 lineal feet long Site
elevations range from about 10 feet Mean Sea Level MSL at the pump station to
about 92 feet MSL at the intersection of Lilipuna Road and Kamehameha Highway

It is anticipated that groundwater may be encountered during excavation for the
new force main in the vicinity of the pump station Based on current design concepts
dewatering of the excavation will likely be needed to facilitate force main construction
Therefore one of the primary environmental concerns is the quality of the water that
will be encountered and whether discharge of the water to an adjacent drainage system
will be appropriate
The proposed force main will be located in an area of existing residential
development except for about 400 lineal feet of the alignment at Lilipuna Road near
Kamehameha Highway Kaneohe Bay Shopping Center is located to the southeast of
the Lilipuna Road segment approaching Kamehameha Highway The existing
Windward Mall is located to the southwest and residential development exists to the
north and northwest
Geologic Setting Geologic maps of the area Stearns 1966 indicate that the
study area is likely to be underlain by alluvium Alluvium is soil that has been
deposited by flowing water Most of the study area is mapped as older alluvium except
for the area near the pump station which is mapped as young alluvium Older alluvium
is usually more consolidated than the younger alluvium Alluvium in the study area has
been deposited in the Heeia Stream drainage basin The content of the alluvium can
be highly variable although alluvial deposits in sloping terrain such as the western
portion of the study area typically include coarser grained materials such as sands and
gravels Alluvium in flatter lowlying terrain such as the eastern portion of the study
area typically includes finer grained sands silts and clays

A geotechnical engineering exploration along the subject sanitary force main
alignment was in progress at the time this report was prepared A series of eleven
borings were drilled along the proposed alignment to depths ranging from about 7 to
21 feet below the existing ground surface Predominantly clayey silt with occasional
gravelly layers were encountered to the maximum depths explored
Hydrogeologic Setting The alluvial soils in the low lying area near the pump
station are probably saturated to about near sea level Groundwater flow in this setting
usually occurs in response to tidal fluctuations Uphill of the pump station groundwater
in the alluvium is probably limited to localized zones of perched water within
discontinuous permeable beds of alluvium Groundwater flow in this setting is generally
in the downslope direction toward the northeast
Geotechnical borings near the pump station encountered groundwater at a depth
of about 6 to 7 feet below the existing ground surface corresponding to about 3 feet
MSL Groundwater was encountered at depths of about 14 to 17 feet below the
existing ground surface corresponding to approximately 65 to 74 feet MSL along
Lilijpuna Road between Kamehameha Highway and Nahiku Street Along the central
portion of the alignment groundwater was not encountered in some of the borings
which were drilled to depths of about 21 feet
Indications of soil or groundwater contamination were not noted during the
geotechnical exploration Unusual soil discolorations odors or oily sheens were not
observed in the soil and groundwater samples collected during that study In
accordance with the general requirements of National Pollutant Discharge Elimination
System NPDES permit application a groundwater sample from the boring closest to
the Heeia Wastewater Pump Station was collected and tested in general accordance
with NPDES requirements Details regarding the NPDES testing will be presented in
a separate report

PRELIMINARY FINDINGS AND CONCLUSION
We have performed a Phase 1 Environmental Site Assessment in general
conformance with the scope and limitations of ASTM Standard Practice E of
the proposed sanitary force main alignment This assessment has revealed no
evidence of recognized adverse environmental conditions in connection with the study
area except for the following

2
Six petroleum gasoline spills and propane gas leaks have been reported
along Kamehameha Highway adjoining the study area since 1986 It is
possible that some residual contamination from these sources may exist
near the intersection of Lilipuna Road and Kamehameha Highway No
visual or olfactory indications of contamination were observed in a
geotechnical boring located in this vicinity
Leakage of sewage has occurred from the existing sanitary force main
immediately uphill of the Heeia Wastewater Pump Station Residual
contamination of the soil and groundwater in this area may exist however
visual or olfactory indications of contamination were not noted in the
eleven geotechnical engineering exploration borings drilled along the
proposed alignment of the force main

RECOMMENDATION
It is our opinion that a Phase 2 environmental site assessment does not appear
to be warranted at this time Since indications of soil or groundwater contamination
were not detected by visual and olfactory screening of samples from the eleven
geotechnical engineering exploration borings drilled along the force main alignment
additional environmental evaluation does not appear to be needed
