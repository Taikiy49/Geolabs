INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed multi family housing project within the Kapolei Village 2
development in the District of Ewa on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location
and vicinity of the site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering recommenda
tions resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering analyses
These recommendations are intended for the design of residential housing foundations
site preparation and pavements The findings and recommendations presented herein
may not be appropriate to form the basis for preparation of construction cost estimates
nor for construction bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed multi family rental housing project is part of the Kapolei Village 2
development in the district of Ewa on the island of Oahu Hawaii Based on the
information provided to us we understand that approximately 240 housing units
consisting of one to two story wood framed structures will be provided under the
current development plans This project consists of 64 assisted rental units 80
affordable rental units and 96 condominium units In addition the project development
will include the construction of supporting facilities such as access roadways parking
areas and underground utilities
The site has been recently mass graded as part of the Villages of Kapolei mass
grading work The work was performed by Oahu Construction with our firm providing
the grading and compaction monitoring services This mass grading involved the
placement of about 6 feet of fill material consisting generally of dark brown silty clay

up coming fine grading work at individual lots

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lavas from
two shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated
to be of middle to late Pliocene in age and Koolau is estimated to be of late Pliocene
to early Pleistocene in age As volcanic activity in Waianae ceased lava flows from
Koolau banked against its eroded eastern slope forming a broad plateau now known
as the Schofield Plateau
Physical and chemical weathering followed by erosion of this plateau generated
sediments which were transported to the coast In the vicinity of the project site and
to the south these sediments accumulated and interbedded with marine sediments and
coral algal reef formations to form a sedimentary wedge The thickness of the
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3281 00 Page 6
sedimentary wedge ranges from zero in the area of the H 1 Freeway to over 1 000 feet
at Ewa Beach This wedge forms the Ewa Plain and serves as the confining formation
or caprock over the artesian basal aquifers of southern Oahu Deposition of
sediments has continued from earlier geologic time through the present
Agricultural development within the last 100 years and recent mass grading work
have brought the Villages of Kapolei area to its present form

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Our field exploration indicates that the project site is underlain by very stiff to
hard silty clays extending to the drilled boring depth of 11 feet below the existing
ground surface Based on the subsoil conditions encountered at the project site it is
GEOLABS HAWAII

our opinion that shallow footing foundation system may be used to provide support for
the proposed residential structures However the silty clay exhibits moderate to high
shrink swell characteristics when subjected to fluctuations of subsurface soil moisture
contents As a result we recommend that the expansive clayey soils be capped with
a minimum of 12 inches of non expansive select capping fill material below all building
slab areas In addition special attention should be given to the recommended

Our geotechnical engineering recommendations pertaining to the design of

Slab On Grade Foundations
Slab on grade foundations with thickened edge footings may be used to support
the proposed one to two story structures anticipated for the subject project Our
exploration indicates that the site is generally underlain by moderately to highly
expansive clayey soils Unless slab on grade foundations supported by these
expansive soils are properly designed there is potential for future distress to residential
structures resulting from wetting and subsequent swelling of these expansive clayey
soils
An allowable bearing pressure of 2 000 pounds per square foot p s f may be
utilized for the design of shallow foundations bearing on the recompacted in situ clay
soils This bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third
for transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic forces
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3281 00 Page 8
Lateral loads acting on the structures may be resisted by passive earth pressure
acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system and by frictional
resistance developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid
pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth p c f This assumes that
the soil around footings is well compacted or undisturbed Unless covered by
pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the upper 12 inches of soil should be
neglected A coefficient of friction of 0 3 may be used for footings bearing on the
recompacted in situ clay soils
To reduce the potential for structural distress resulting from the swelling of the
on site expansive soils we recommend that non expansive select capping fill material
be used for a minimum depth of 12 inches below the slab subgrade The capping fill
should also extend outside the slab perimeter a minimum of 1 foot The thickened
edges of the slabs should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest
adjacent exterior grade The base of the thickened edge should have a minimum width
of 12 inches A graphic illustration of this foundation type is presented on Plate 3
Schematic Section Slab on Grade on Expansive Soil
The select capping fill should be placed as soon as possible after the
scarification moisture conditioning and compaction of the underlying subgrade soils
The select capping fill material should provide a capping layer of moist soil above the
underlying potentially expansive subgrade soil thereby reducing the loss of moisture
from the clayey subgrade soils In our opinion the reduction in potential drying and
wetting of the subgrade soils should reduce the impact on the slab due to potential
heaving of the underlying expansive clayey soils when wetted
The slab subgrades should be kept moist prior to placement of concrete To
reduce the potential for drying of the subgrade soils and to reduce the costs of form
GEOLABS HAWAII

construction we recommend that the slab edges be cast neat against the soils The
slab edges should incorporate a sufficient amount of top and bottom longitudinal steel
reinforcement The top and bottom steel reinforcement bars should be connected by
stir ups The ends of the stir ups should extend into the floor slab area and should be
tied to the welded wire mesh A structural engineer should be consulted for design
details of this thickened edge which is intended to function both as a moisture barrier
and as a perimeter bearing wall footing
The welded wire mesh in the slab on grade should be embedded at the proper
height in the concrete to provide adequate slab reinforcement Blocks or saddles may
be used to raise the wire mesh A stiff grade of wire mesh is thus recommended
We recommend that a minimum 4 inch layer of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33
No 67 gradation be incorporated below the concrete slab as a cushion layer The
slab subgrade should be thoroughly wetted prior to concrete placement to allow the
select capping fill layer to remain in a moist condition To further reduce moisture
infiltration a plastic vapor barrier may be considered below the slab A 2 inch thick
moist fine sand layer should be placed above the plastic sheet to aid in the curing of
the concrete
Flexible floor coverings such as carpet or sheet vinyl should be considered
since they can better mask any minor slab cracking It is also recommended that
interior walls be designed to incorporate some flexibility to accommodate small amount
of possible ground movements
If foundations are designed in accordance with our recommendations we
estimate total settlements to be less than 1 inch with differential settlements on the
order of inch

Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork the area within the grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All existing surface vegetation construction wastes
and deleterious materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site
Any zones of wet saturated or yielding areas encountered during the clearing
process should be over excavated to expose firm ground and the resulting excavations
should be backfilled with well compacted engineered fill The need for over excavation
should be evaluated by a representative of Geolabs Hawaii in the field We
recommend that contract documents include provisions for additive and deductive unit
prices to account for over excavation and engineered fill placement due to
unanticipated over excavation quantities
All areas to receive slabs and pavements should be over excavated to a depth
of 12 and 18 inches below the bottom of the No 3B Fine gravel or base course layer
respectively If zones of soft loose or saturated soils are encountered at the over
excavated subgrade level deeper over excavation may be required to expose firm
material The resulting excavation should be backfilled with compacted select granular
fill material The clayey material resulting from the over excavation should be removed
and properly disposed of off site
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3281 00 Page 11
Areas to receive fills should be scarified to a depth of about 10 inches moisture
conditioned to between 2 and 5 percent above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to about 85 to 90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers
to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry
density as determined by ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91
Select granular fill material should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 10 inches
in loose thickness and compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction The
select capping fill should be non expansive granular material such as crushed coral
basalt aggregates or cinder sand The material should be well graded from coarse to
fine with no particles greater than 3 inches in largest dimension The material should
have a laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR value of 20 or higher and a swell
potential of not more than 1 percent when tested in accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 1883 It should contain less than 20 percent fines passing the No 200
sieve Any imported fill material should be tested and approved for its intended use by
Geolabs Hawaii prior to delivery to the project site

Utility Trench
It is anticipated that utilities for the development will consist primarily of water
drainage and sewer In general good construction practices should be utilized for the
installation and trench backfill of the proposed utilities
Based on the results of our field exploration it appears that in general
conventional excavation methods may be utilized for shallow utility line trenching at the
site Trench shoring design and installation should be the responsibility of the
contractor and should conform to the appropriate health and safety requirements
Utilities should be placed on a 6 inch cushion of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C
33 No 67 gradation Self compacting granular backfill such as No 3B Fine gravel
should be used around the pipe and extend 12 inches above the top of the pipe It is
critical that a self compacting material be used to reduce the potential of formation of
voids below the haunches of pipes and to provide adequate support for the sides of the
pipes The use of on site clayey soils as backfill around utility pipes is not acceptable
Trench backfills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 10 inches in loose
thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Mechanical
compaction equipment should be used to compact the backfill materials at the project
site Water tamping jetting or ponding should not be used to compact the on site
clayey soils

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling of
15 borings to a depth of about 11 feet below the existing ground surface at the
approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were drilled using
a truck mounted drill rig equipped with continuous flight augers
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils
30 inches The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the last 12 inches of an 18
inch drive are shown on the logs of borings at the appropriate sample depths
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our field engineer who observed the drilling operations on
a near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed by visual
observation and testing in the laboratory All soils were classified in general
conformance the Unified Soil Classification System Graphic presentation of the
materials encountered is presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 15

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content and unit weight determinations were performed on selected soil
samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties The results of
these tests are presented on the logs of borings at the appropriate sample depths
Fifteen 1 inch ring swell tests were performed on relatively undisturbed and
remolded samples to evaluate the swelling potential of the soils in the natural and
compacted state The results of these tests are summarized on Plates B 1 through
Two Atterberg Limits tests were performed on selected soil samples to evaluate
their liquid and plastic limits and to aid in soil classification Graphic presentation of the
test results is provided on Plate B 4
One California Bearing Ratio test ASTM Test Method D 1883 87 was
performed on the typical silty clays to evaluate the pavement support characteristics of
the soils The test results are presented on Plate B 5
One Modified Proctor compaction test ASTM D 1557 91 was performed on the
typical silty clays encountered at the site The test results are summarized on Plate
B 6
