REMARKS Based on the information provided to us we understand that some soil
material located on the slope above the new concrete connection block on
the Hilo Side of the bridge has raveled and slid off the steep slopes over the
weekend We also understand that the raveling of the slope is located on
the steep existing slopes above the near vertical cut face of the concrete
connection block The available topographic survey indicates that the
existing slope inclination is about 1 4 horizontal to 1 vertical
Based on our recent site visit the soil materials above the near vertical cut
face of the concrete connection block consist of loose silty sand sandy silt
with some cobbles existing generally in a dry and friable condition
To reduce the potential for continued raveling of the other areas of the slope
above the near vertical cut face of the concrete connection block we
recommend that the loose surface soils on the slope be removed to expose
the underlying firmer and or denser soils Based on our observations we
envision about 2 to 3 feet of the surface soils may need to be removed to
expose the underlying firmer and or denser soil conditions A representative
from Geolabs should be present to confirm that the loose surface soils have
been removed and firmer and or denser soil soils are exposed
After the loose surface soils have been removed from the steep existing
slope we recommend that a minimum 6 inch thick layer of fiber reinforced
shotcrete be placed on the exposed slope face to protect the soil materials
from drying out and losing strength Prior to the placement of the shotcrete
welded wire fabric should be placed along the face of the slope Because
the shotcrete will be a permanent facing for the slope the shotcrete layer
should be keyed into the existing slope to reduce the potential for the
shotcrete layer to slip down the slope The edges longitudinal direction of
the bridge of the shotcrete layer should be keyed into the slope In addition

diameter Schedule 40 PVC pipes and filter fabric should be provided at each
