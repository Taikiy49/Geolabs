We have completed geotechnical engineering consultation services pertaining to
the evaluation of the bearing capacity analysis for the existing Bravo Wharf B 19 at the
above project site This letter report summarizes the results of our study performed and
presents our geotechnical engineering findings and conclusions derived from our analyses
for the subject project
PROJECT CONSIDERATION
The existing BRAVO Wharf B 19 project site is the easternmost facility along the
waterfront at the Naval Shipyard facility in Pearl Harbor on the Island of Oahu Hawaii
The Bravo Wharf B 19 is located between Bravo Wharves B 18 and B 20 We understand
that it is desired to evaluate the axial compression capacity of the existing concrete piles
Based on as built information provided we understand that the wharf is a
reinforced concrete structure supported by precast concrete bearing piles Based on the
as built plans provided two rows of concrete piles located along the outboard and inboard
alignments were used to support the Bravo Wharf B 19 The existing pile driving records
were not available at the time this report was prepared Information for two borings near
Bravo Wharf B 19 was used in our study Based on the information provided we
understand that a one and two fifths horizontal to one vertical slope was
dredged during the original construction
SITE RECONNAISSANCE
A site reconnaissance was conducted at Bravo Wharf B 19 Mudline elevations
were measured at five different locations Three of the locations were located along the
edge of the lower deck of Bravo Wharf B 19 longitudinal and the other two locations were
located along the transverse direction in general the mudline along the longitudinal
direction near the edge of the wharf is located at approximately 65 feet U S Naval Datum
USND and sloping up towards the land at approximately along the transverse
direction The piles supporting the existing wharf were observed to be 18 inch square
precast concrete piles with center to center spacing of 3 feet The lower deck was also
supported on 16 inch concrete piles

PREVIOUS INFORMATION AND ANALYSIS ASSUMPTIONS
As mentioned earlier information from two borings that were drilled in early 1930 s
are available These two borings were located approximately near the edge of the existing
lower deck Based on the boring information an original mudline was located
approximately 58 feet USND Medium dense to soft harbor sediment deposits were
encountered to 43 feet USND and overlying alluvium deposits The mudrock deposits
were encountered below the alluvium at approximately 32 feet USND
Based on the as built pile driving records from Bent 38e through Bent 44e of
Bravo Wharf B 20 which is adjacent to Bravo Wharf B 19 the average pile penetration
length was approximately 22 feet with an average computed bearing capacity of 34 tons
As indicated earlier as built pile driving records for the Bravo Wharf B 19 were not
available for our engineering analysis Therefore our analyses are based on the
above mentioned soil information as built pile driving records at B 20 and our
measurement of the mudline elevations during the field reconnaissance
