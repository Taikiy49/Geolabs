This letter report summarizes our probing and grouting operations at the above site
during October 2011
Probing and Grouting Operations
The probing and grouting operations were performed in general conformance with
the project specifications The purpose of the probing and grouting operation was to
reduce the potential for loss of foundation support resulting from collapse of cavities or
compression of loose zones below the new ISFT building and the new aboveground fuel
storage tank
In general the probe holes were located on a nominal 6 foot on center spacing
around the building perimeter and across the fuel tank pad In general the probe holes
were drilled to a depth of about 10 feet below the bottom of the foundation or about 12
feet below the existing ground surface for a total of 264 lineal feet of probing
The probe holes about 4 inches in diameter were drilled using a truck mounted
drill rig equipped with continuous flight solid stem augers After drilling of the probe holes
temporary pipes 2 inch PVC were inserted into the holes to keep the holes open prior to
the grouting operations In accordance with the project specifications 16 probes were
drilled for the building foundations and 6 were drilled for the fuel tank pad yielding a total
of 22 probe holes with an aggregate length of about 264 lineal feet below the existing
ground surface
Suspected voids were encountered in Probe Nos 3 and 5 At Probe No 3 four
additional probe holes were drilled to further explore the extent of the suspected voids At
Probe No 5 one additional probe hole was drilled The total aggregate footage for the
additional probes was 60 lineal feet below existing ground surface

The probe holes were backfilled with a sand cement slurry grout supplied by Island
Ready Mix Ohana Concrete Pumping Company provided grout pumping services under
the observation of our geologist A 3 inch pipe attached to the 4 inch diameter rubber
hose on the concrete boom pump trunk was inserted into each open hole and lowered to
near the bottom of the hole Each probe hole was grouted from the desired depth upward
by low pressure injection of grout through the rubber hose which was retracted from the
probe hole as the grout filled the hole Our field representative observed the grouting
operation and recorded the approximate grout take Grout quantities were estimated
using the number of strokes to fill each probe hole
In general we did not observe significant voids and or cavities in the majority of the
probes during the operation We did observe significant grout intake for Probe Hole
Nos 3 and 5 during the grouting operation The probing and grouting summary is
provided on Plate 1
In summary the foundation probing and grouting operations have been conducted
in general conformance with the project specifications Based on our field observations it
is our professional opinion that the cavities encountered below the subgrade have been
probed and grouted and that the subgrade area should provide the recommended
foundation support for the ISFT building and the fuel tank
Limitations
It should be noted that it is not possible to detect and remediate all cavities and or
voids present beneath the structures Therefore some cavities and or voids may not have
been detected even with the probing and grouting program undertaken for the project
However the level of foundation preparation undertaken for the facility and the subgrade
bearing on the on site coral formation appears to be reasonable in reducing the
geotechnical risks to the project Performing probing and grouting of the foundations
bearing on the on site materials provides the owner with a higher degree of assurance that
substantial cavities and or voids are not located directly below the structures
Our services were performed in accordance with generally accepted standards of
geotechnical engineering practice no warranty is expressed or implied It should be noted
