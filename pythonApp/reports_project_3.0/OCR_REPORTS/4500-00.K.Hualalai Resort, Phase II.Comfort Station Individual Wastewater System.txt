Project Considerations
The Hualalai Resort project site is located about 8 5 miles northeast of the Keahole
International Airport in the Kaupulehu area within the District of North Kona on the Island
of Hawaii As part of the Phase II development of the Hualalai Resort we understand
that various infrastructure improvements and residential parcels will be constructed We
understand that it is desired to construct an individual wastewater system for the
comfort station located near the public beach access parking lot We also understand
that the State of Hawaii Department of Health requires performance of a percolation
test prior to construction of the individual wastewater system Therefore it was desired
to conduct a field percolation test at the proposed leaching field location to obtain data
pertaining to the preliminary design of the wastewater disposal system
Percolation Test
One field percolation test was performed at the comfort station site The field
percolation test was performed in general accordance with the State of Hawaii
Systems and Chapter 10 of the Ten State s Standards to evaluate the permeability of
the subsurface soils for disposal of wastewater effluent The test was performed in a
3 5 inch diameter borehole drilled to a depth of about 20 feet below the existing ground
surface Approximately 1 inch of gravel was then placed at the bottom of the borehole
to protect the bottom of the borehole from scouring and sediments During testing the
borehole was carefully filled with water to a depth of about 6 inches above the gravel
and refilled as necessary The time intervals and water drops were then recorded to
provide data upon which the calculation of the percolation test was based Based on
the percolation test conducted the percolation rate is on the order of approximately
0 17 minutes per inch at the test location
