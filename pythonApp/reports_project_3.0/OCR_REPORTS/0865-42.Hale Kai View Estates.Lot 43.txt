As requested we have made a study of the near surface soil conditions
below the above referenced lot The purpose of the study was to eva
luate the suitability of the near surface soils as foundation support
for the proposed house The study consisted of three 3 hand auger
probes and three 3 borings drilled within the proposed building
site laboratory soil tests and a review of the grading and com
paction test record on the building site This report summarizes our
observations and recommendations
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed single family residence will consist of a two story wood
structure Due to the sloping terrain at the site a lower level
garage cutting into the hillside with an upper level living area are
planned
Retaining walls are planned around the garage Based on the
preliminary layout plans the house will set back about 31 feet from
Kahena Street
Concrete slab on grade is planned for the first floor garage and
driveway Post and beam construction with wood floors are planned
for the remainder of the house
EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
Lot 43 is located in the central portion of the subdivision develop
ment on the southwestern side of Kahena Street The lot is bounded
by Kahena Street on the northeast subdivision lots on the east and
west and the natural valley side slope on the south An existing con
crete drainage ditch is located on the southwestern upper portion of
the lot The lot generally slopes upward from Kahena Street toward
the southwest at about 22 percent gradient The elevation of the lot
generally varies from about 199 to 274 feet
SUBDIVISION LOT GRADING
Prior to the recent project site grading operations the northeastern
front portion of Lot 43 was covered with a large stockpile of soil
and boulders left over from previous grading operations in the area

During that grading operation the stockpile was removed down to a
depth of 4 feet below the design finished grade and replaced with
compacted available on site soils which consisted basically of silty
clay and clayey silt soils that had been mixed with some adobe clay
pockets from the previous stockpile
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
A review of the grading compaction test record and the site recon
naissance indicate that the front half of the lot where most of the
house is planned is underlain by a layer of grayish brown expansive
clay about 8 feet deep on the proposed garage area B 3 and 2 feet
deep on the rear portion of the proposed house area B 2 The
hand auger probes drilled borings and site reconnaisaance indicate
that better less expansive and gravelly colluvial deposits may be
encountered below the surface clay layer at about 2 foot depth or more
The remaining portion of the lot beyond the house area is underlain by
natural colluvial deposits transported from the upper hillside area
These deposits are typically heterogenous and vary from clayey silt
low expansion potential to clay high expansion potential
Detailed description of the soils encountered in hand auger probes and
borings are presented on the attached Logs Plates A 1 through A 6
The approximate locations of the borings are shown on the attached
Site Plan Plate 1

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that most of the lot is underlain by
more than 2 to 8 feet of grayish brown clay The laboratory test
results on this material generally indicated that they have high
expansion potential Therefore for Lot 43 the slab on grade and
foundation construction would require special design procedures in
order to reduce the shrink swell effect of the subsoils
In the rear portion of the proposed house the proposed site cutting
may remove the upper clay layer and expose better colluvial soils
where footing depths may be reduced
Foundation
For this sloping lot post and beam construction is highly desirable
and recommended due to the sloping terrain and expansive soil condi
tions The post and beam construction generally results in lower
foundation costs and the individual footings can be readily adjusted
should the need occur Where utilized concrete slab on grade should
also be properly designed for expansive soil conditions

W O 865 42 Page 5
For post and beam construction we recommend that the expansive clays
be over excavated down for a depth of 3 feet below the bottom of the
individual post footings and then replaced with non expansive borrow
soils such as crusher waste select borrow or coral compacted to a
minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78 The
spread footings can then be placed on the compacted non expansive
soil
Alternatively the individual post footing can be extended a minimum
depth of 36 inches below the ground surface
In the rear portion of the house the footing embedment may be reduced
if better gravelly colluvial soil is encountered All footings should
have a minimum embedment of 12 inches below the finished grade
The footings should be poured in place and the excavation backfilled
and compacted to provide an impervious layer over the footing
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f can be used for footings
bearing on the stiff natural ground or compacted fill The bottom of
the footing excavation should be neat and free of loose soils or
debris
If a footing is located next to a utility line it should extend to
the bottom of the utility trench to reduce footing settlement due to
settlement of the trench backfill

Surface water should be diverted away from the foundations This
is especially important in the rear portion of the house to reduce
ponding of surface run off water and possible saturation and
settlement or heaving of the foundation soils
Slab On Grade
Concrete slab on grade is planned for the garage area It should be
noted that the on site clay soils are expansive and unsuitable as slab
subgrade soils In order to reduce the expansive effects of the clay
the expansive clays should be overexcavated down to a depth of 3 feet
below the slab subgrade and then replaced with non expansive borrow
soils such as select borrow crusher waste or coral compacted to a
minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density as per ASTM D 1557 78
A 4 inch layer of base course should be provided below the slab and
compacted to 90 of its maximum dry density In order to protect
against moisture infiltration a polyethylene moisture barrier may be
incorporated below the slab The membrane should be protected by a
thin layer of sand to aid in the concrete curing
Concrete Driveway and Sidewalks
A layer of expansive soils more than 8 feet thick was encountered in
the front portion of the lot driveway area Since the driveway can
tolerate some amount of heaving the clay overexcavation and replace
ment can be reduced to 2 feet below the driveway subgrade level
GEOLABS HAWAII
Where 2 or more feet of fill is planned select non expansive borrow
material should be used to cap over the expansive clay Where less
than 2 feet of fill is planned overexcavation and replacement of the
expansive clay with select non expansive material is required The
above capping requirements should be followed for a minimum distance
of 3 feet beyond the driveway
For the long driveway planned joints are recommended at about 8 to
10 foot intervals and at discontinuities in order to allow for some
relative movement of the concrete
For future sidewalks capping of the clay or overexcavation and
replacement similar to the driveway is recommended to reduce heaving
caused by the expansive clay soil
Site Grading
The existing terrain at the site generally slopes down at about 22
percent gradient
Minor grading is anticipated for most of the area below the house
The area to be filled should be cleared of vegetation and debris prior
to filling

Fills and backfills should be select non expansive borrow material
placed in maximum 8 inch lifts and compacted to a minimum of 90 of
its maximum dry density established by ASTM D 1557 78 The on site
Clay soil should not be used for fill material below structures and
behind retaining walls
Cut and fill slopes of 2 horizontal to 1 vertical are recommended
The finished grade outside the slabs pavements and foundations
should be shaped to avoid ponding conditions and to shed water away
from the structures

Design Review
Plans for foundations site grading and utility construction should
be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written comments
prior to construction to see that the intent of our recommendations is
included in the project plans and specifications
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during earth
work construction particularly the fill placement slope cuts and to
monitor the contractor s grading operations
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be observed by a
soils engineer during construction to see that adequate bearing has
been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with
the findings of our subsurface investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the above
inspections

principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all other

fully determined by reconnaissance and soil borings These unforeseen
