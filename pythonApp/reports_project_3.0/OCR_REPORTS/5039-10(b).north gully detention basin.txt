Dear Mr Mori
This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations performed
during the mass grading detention embankment filling operation and road construction
operations from April 2004 to June 2005 for the above project in accordance with our fee
proposal dated
Project Considerations
The project site is located at the western end of the North Gully in Mililani Mauka
adjacent to the Interstate Route H 2 Wahiawa bound Mililani off ramp The general
location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The project involves constructing a 25 foot high earth embankment with a slope
inclination of three horizontal to one vertical foot by 6 foot concrete box drain
off site 12 inch water line and access asphalt concrete roadways The site grading
work consists of about 25 feet of fills for the new earth embankment and up to about
20 feet of cuts for the access roadways The approximate project limits are shown on
the Site Plan Plate 2
Site Gradin
The earthwork operations for the project were performed by Delta Construction
Corporation At the on set of earthwork areas within the contract grading limits were
generally cleared and grubbed of vegetation and deleterious materials Materials
generated from the clearing operations were disposed of off site Following the clearing
and grubbing operations the areas to be cut were excavated Finish grades were
compacted using a sheepsfoot and or smooth drum vibratory roller

In general on site excess soil materials generated from the excavation were
primarily used in the earth embankment area The on site fill materials generally consisted
of clayey silt soils Imported fill materials were utilized for the earth embankment The
imported fill materials were hauled in from Increment 3 of the Mililani Mauka Development
project and were reported as suitable fill materials by another geotechnical engineer
Stockpile Investigation Report by Fewell Geotechnical Engineering Ltd dated
Samples of the fill and backfill materials utilized during construction were obtained
and transported to our office for laboratory testing The maximum dry densities and
optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general accordance with
American Society of Testing and Material ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor The test
results are summarized on Plate 3 of this report
Due to the intended use of most of the fills to be placed for detention of water
fine grained fill with proper slope and channel protection were recommended In general
fill materials consisted of low expansive materials less than 3 inches in largest dimension
with a minimum CBR value of 10 Field density tests were performed using the nuclear
density gauge ASTM D 2922 and D 3017 During the fill and backfill operations the
materials were generally placed in near level lifts brought to near the optimum moisture
and compacted to obtain the specified degree of compaction based on visual observations
and or field density tests Periodic field density tests were performed during the fill and
backfill operations to confirm our field observations as variations in construction
procedures and soil conditions were noted
Key and bench were constructed on both sides of the North Gully for earth
embankment fills of the detention basin
A total of 154 field density tests including re tests of failed areas were performed
using the nuclear density gauge method in general accordance with ASTM D 2922 and
D 3017 The results of the compaction tests performed during construction of the above
project are summarized on Plates 4 1 through 4 8 of this report Test locations and
elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes where
available These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the
degree implied by the methods used
Based our field representatives observations and the referenced test results it is
our professional opinion that the earthwork operations for the North Gully Detention Basin
project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report were
performed in general conformance with the intent of the plans and specifications and our
recommendations In general areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were re worked to
achieve the specified minimum compaction

Limitations
This office assumes no responsibility for any alterations made to slopes or finished
grades on the project subsequent to our observation and testing period without our
knowledge and written acknowledgment
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site therefore some work may have occurred
without our observation However we believe that the level of earthwork observation and
testing services provided is reasonable in reducing the geotechnical risks to the project It
should be recognized that there are variations in the accuracy and statistical repeatability
of the tests and methods used to monitor the earthwork construction and materials Our
