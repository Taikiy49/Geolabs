PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is across from the Waianae Mall on Leihoku Street in the Leeward
coast area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii It is situated approximately between the existing
Pokai Bay Subdivision and the Waianae Wastewater Treatment Plant Based on the
information provided we understand that a new YMCA facility is planned at the above site
The existing mountainside to the south side of the proposed YMCA facility is on the order
of about 400 to 600 feet high which is considered average to moderate for the slopes on
the Island of Oahu The lower portion of the hillside extending from the existing concrete
drainage interceptor ditch to the toe of the hillside consists of terraced slopes with an
overall gradient of approximately one and one half horizontal to one vertical
The upper portion of the hillside above the concrete drainage interceptor ditch is
composed of multiple sub vertical scarps and rock ledges separated by flatter inter slope
regions with an overall slope inclination of about 1H 1V
The rockfall mitigation project entails the construction of a 30 foot wide rockfall
catchment ditch and 570 lineal feet of low energy rockfall impact barrier along the toe area
of the hillside This report summarizes our earthwork observation and compaction testing
services conducted during the construction of the 30 foot wide rockfall catchment ditch

EARTHWORK OPERATIONS
Prometheus Construction performed the site grading operations for the project
The site grading work consisted of over excavation of the existing ground and construction
of an earth berm to create a rockfall catchment ditch along the toe area of the hillside
Following the clearing and grubbing operations the existing ground along the toe
area of the hillside was over excavated to the design finish grades In general coralline
materials and hard basalt rock formation were encountered during the over excavation
process The over excavated subgrades were proof rolled with a sheepsfoot roller to a
firm and unyielding condition
The coralline materials generated from the over excavation process were used for
the construction of the earth berm on which the low energy rockfall impact barrier will be
installed The majority of the basalt rocks generated from the excavation could not be
reused as fill materials due to the large rock sizes As a result additional fill materials
were imported to the project site for the earth berm construction Prior to the placement of
the fill materials the existing ground was scarified to a depth of about 8 inches
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and proof rolled with a
sheepsfoot roller to a firm and unyielding condition Fill materials for the earth berm
construction were generally placed in near level lifts brought to above the optimum
moisture and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent of the maximum dry density
determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor
During the site grading operations samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill materials were obtained and transported to our laboratory for testing The
maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory
in general accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test results are
summarized on Plate 2 of this report
Field density tests were performed during the fill operations on an as need basis to
confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures and soil conditions
were observed A total of 14 field density tests were performed using the nuclear probe
method in accordance with ASTM D 2922 and D 3017 Results of the compaction tests
performed are summarized on the attached Plate 3 These tests were performed on the
constructed fills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and
interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used
