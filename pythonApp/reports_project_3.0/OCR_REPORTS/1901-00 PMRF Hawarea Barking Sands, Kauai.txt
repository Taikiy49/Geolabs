PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of the exploration was to evaluate the subsurface soil
conditions at the proposed site to provide soil data for the foundation
and pavement section design of the proposed building
The scope of our investigation which was performed in general
accordance with our proposal of included drilling and
sampling two 2 borings laboratory soil testing engineering analysis
utilizing the field and laboratory data and report preparation
Detailed description of our field exploration laboratory testing and
boring logs are presented in the appendices The approximate location of
the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
GENERAL SITE CONDITIONS
The proposed site is located adjacent to the south side of the
existing Vandal Maintenance and Support Facility at the Pacific Missile
Range Facility at Barking Sands on the island of Kauai Hawaii Building
412 is located approximately 70 feet to the east of the proposed
building
The site is open and unoccupied in an area measured at about 40 by 60
feet Ground level at the site is generally flat but the surface
elevations within the site are not available However based on the
available grading plan of the existing adjacent Vandal Maintenance and
Support Facility existing grades at the site are estimated to be at
elevations 13 feet above Mean Sea Level
Ground cover consists of some dry grass on fine to medium beach sand

Subsoil Condition
Two borings to depth of 21 5 feet below the ground surface were
drilled The borings generally encountered medium dense to dense fine to
medium grained beach sand with traces of silt and gravel locally
Groundwater was encountered at about 14 5 feet below ground surface in
Boring 1 at the time of our field exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATION
Based on the anticipated soil conditions and the light to moderate
building loads we believe that shallow spread or continuous wall footings
bearing on the recompacted in situ medium dense to dense sand may be used
Footings should be placed a minimm of 18 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grade Uplift wind blast or other lateral loading
condition may further increase the embedment of the footing foundation
The footings adjacent to the existing building should be placed at the
same level of the existing footings
An allowable bearing capacity of 2 500 p s f may be used for the
foundation design The bearing capacity value is for total dead plus live
load only and may be increased by one third for short duration wind
loading Estimated settlements under the design loading are on the order
of less than one half of an inch total and one fourth of an inch
differential
Resistance to lateral loads for the maintenance and support building
can be obtained by passive earth pressure and frictional resistance A
frictional coefficient of 0 4 may be used between the bottom of the
footings and the medium dense soil In addition an equivalent fluid
pressure of 300 p s f per foot of depth may be used to determine the
passive earth resistance against the buried vertical faces of the
footings

Site Grading
It is our understanding that the finished grade level of the proposed
building will be at the same elevation of 13 67 feet as the existing
adjacent Vandal Maintenance and Support Facility Therefore minimal cut
and or fill are anticipated for the generally flat site
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris
rubbish and other unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed
properly off site in order to prevent contamination of the excavated
material
The excavated footing areas should be kept moist and tamped prior to
concrete placement if they are loosened during the excavation The
pavement areas should be scarified to a depth of at least 6 inches below
the subgrade elevation moisture conditioned to optimum moisture content
and recompacted to obtain a firm subgrade
Ioose spots encountered at the footing or pavement subgrade level
should be removed and replaced with compacted on site sandy soil
The on site sandy soil may be used as a source of backfill and fill
material If required new imported fill material should consist of
non expansive select granular materials less than 3 inches in maximm size
with less than 20 passing the 200 sieve

Design Review
Plans and specifications for site grading foundations and pavements
should be forwarded to the soil engineer for review and written coments
prior to construction to see that the intent of our recommendations is
included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the site grading subgrade preparation and
footing excavations be observed by the soil engineer during construction
to see that adequate compaction has been obtained and that the subsurface
conditions are consistent with the findings of our exploration
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soil engineer prior to hauling to the site
If conditions encountered during construction are different from
those presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made

made in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation engineering
principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all other
warranties either expressed or implied

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling two
2 borings to depth of 21 5 feet below the existing ground surface at
the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 A
truck mounted drill rig was used to drill the borings
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by visual and
textural examination in the field by our engineer who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory All soils
were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A summary of the materials encountered are
presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 and A 2
Soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving a 2 inch
standard split spoon sampler or a 2 4 inch I D split barrel sampler
with a 140 pound hammer free falling 30 inches The blow counts to
drive the sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the Boring Logs at the
appropriate sample depths

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content determinations were performed on selected soil
samples to aid in the classification and evaluation of the soil
properties The test results are presented on the Boring logs at the
appropriate sample depths
Four sieve analyses were performed on the sandy subsoils to
evaluate their grain size distribution The test results are summarized
on Plate B 1
One compaction test was performed on a sample of the surface soil
to evaluate its compaction characteristics The test results are
summarized on Plate B 1
One CBR test was also performed on the surface soil sample to
evaluate its subgrade support characteristics The test results are
also presented on Plate B 1
