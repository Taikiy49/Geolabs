As requested we prepared this letter report to present our geotechnical
recommendations for the existing cut slope along Auwaiolimu Street in the Papakolea

subject to the limitations noted at the end of this letter report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is situated in the lower portion of Papakolea in Honolulu on the Island
of Oahu Hawaii It is our understanding that your firm has been retained by the Department
of Hawaiian Home Lands to design a rehabilitation project for the sewers in the area
During the initial site visits for the project some issues were observed at and in the
vicinity of 2103 Tantalus Drive The first issue is an eroded area in the backyard of
2103 Tantalus Drive that is encroaching on the steep slope leading down to
Auwaiolimu Street Initial observations at the site tend to indicate that this erosion may be
related to an abandoned sewer lateral in the backyard
The second issue is distresses to a retaining wall immediately adjacent to the
Tantalus Drive overpass bridge crossing Auwaiolimu Street This wall has large open vertical
cracks and shows some signs of movement
The final issue related to the project is the steep near vertical slope between the
Papakolea subdivision area and Auwaiolimu Street This slope was cut in both Punchbowl
volcanic tuff and Koolau basalt The portion in the tuff formation appears to be closely jointed
and fragments spall from the slope face and fall to the right of way below Since portions of
the sewer lines run along the top of the slope there are concerns that cut and cover
trenching work could destabilize the slope
This letter report is limited to the existing cut slope along Auwaiolimu Street

Auwaiolimu Street Cut Slope
Portions of the sewer rehabilitation project are situated at the top of the existing
cut slope along Auwaiolimu Street This slope is near vertical and was cut into volcanic
tuff and basalt rock formation The current height of the slope is on the order of 25 to
40 feet above the grade of Auwaiolimu Street A soil layer about 2 to 4 feet thick is
visible at the top of the slope
Visual observations of the slope indicate that the volcanic tuff is closely jointed
along its horizontal bedding planes and is in the vertical direction There is a rock
catchment at the base of the slope and is generally filled with scree which has fallen
from the slope face
Based on the information provided the sewer rehabilitation project might involve
trenching and other excavation along the top of the slope Our observations indicate
that such excavation operations could destabilize the existing slope face Ideally efforts
should be made to stabilize the slope face to reduce the potential of rockfall hazards
from the rehabilitation project and other sources such as seismic events However it is
realized that such stabilization would be costly and that other mitigating measures
should be considered
Mitigating measures could include engineering controls such as maintaining a
minimum setback distance from the top of the slope It is our opinion that excavation
should not be done within 10 feet of the outer edge of the slope face If the rehabilitation
work requires installation of new sewer lines in parallel trenches the new trenches should
be located on the upslope side of the existing trench
In order to stabilize the slope face and reduce the potential of future spalling we
recommend using a combination of rock anchors and Tecco Mesh or similar The
anchors would aid in bonding the slope together and cutting through potential failure
planes The mesh would contain spalled material and to an extent provide a substrate for
the establishment of vegetation on the cut face
In general we recommend using 10 foot long rock anchors at a 10 foot spacing in a
triangular pattern The mesh would extend from the top to the base of the cut At
present we are assuming an average slope height of 20 feet
As you have requested we have broken down the slope into three increments
starting at the Tantalus Drive bridge The first increment Increment 1 extends from the
bridge approximately 117 feet to the east Increment 2 extends from the bridge to a point
about 407 feet to the east and Increment 3 extends from the bridge towards the east for a
total of about 670 feet
