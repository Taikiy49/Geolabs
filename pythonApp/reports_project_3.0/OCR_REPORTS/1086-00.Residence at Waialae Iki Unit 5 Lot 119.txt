Dear Mr Lagareta
As requested we have performed a preliminary soil engineer
ing investigation at the above referenced site to evaluate
the subsurface soil conditions in order to provide recommen
dations for the design and construction of the foundation
for the proposed single family residence
The investigation consisted of two borings at the site vis
ual observations laboratory soil tests and a review of the
project subdivision grading plans and preliminary house
plans prepared by Bob Easton Design Architects
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests
which were used in the engineering analysis to formulate our
conclusions and recommendations are presented in the appen
dix of this report
SITE CONDITIONS
The subject lot is located on the northern side of the cul
de sac for Road B on the eastern side of Laukahi Street in
the Waialae Iki Unit 5 Subdivision
The lot is a flag lot with a driveway approximately 35 feet
long The remainder of the lot is generally rectangular in
shape with dimensions of about 75 feet by 122 to 137 feet
The ground surface slopes down towards the east at rela
The western three fourths of the lot is presently covered
with a heavy growth of ironwood trees
There is an existing sewer easement along the lower eastern
lot boundary
The ground elevations at the site generally vary from about
830 feet down to 788 feet

PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
The proposed residence will be a split level structure
utilizing three floor levels The main portion of the
2 story house will be a few feet lower than the driveway and
Barage level
Except for the garage the remainder of the house will
utilize post and beam construction
The driveway and garage will be at an elevation close to the
existing road level A retaining wall up to about 15 feet
high will be required to support this level
Another retaining wall will be utilized along the rear por
tion of the house in order to raise the ground level around
the house
A swimming pool is planned in the lower eastern portion of
the lot however this is not covered in the scope of this
report Due to the expansive soils on the project site a
soils engineer should be consulted in regards to the final
design of the pool

Our review of the project subdivision grading plans indicate
that most of the subject lot is situated outside the project

Site Preparation and Fill Placement

The site should first be cleared of vegetation debris
slopewash and other loose overcast soil
Fills and backfills should be placed in 9 inch loose lifts
and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density
established by ASTM D 1557 They should also be keyed and
benched into firm residual soil in order to properly bond
the new fill to the slope See Plate 2 for details
Some of the on site soils have moderate to high swelling po
tential and therefore should not be re used as fill
material within 3 feet below concrete slab on grade swim
ming pool or walkways This expansive on site material

should not be used as a backfill behind retaining walls
unless it is placed a minimum of 5 feet away from the wall
surface
It appears that additional off site borrow material will be
required This material should be tested and approved by
the soils engineer prior to its delivery to the site to
assure that it is suitable for the proposed construction
The material should generally be non expansive and should
not contain boulders organic or other deleterious matter
Foundations
Spread or continuous footing foundations may be used for the
proposed residence Allowable bearing values of 3000 p s f
for residual soil and 5000 p s f for weathered rock may be
utilized These values are for total dead plus live loads
and may be increased by one third when temporary wind or
seismic loads are considered
The subject lot is relatively steep Additionally the near
surface soils are moderately to highly expansive There
fore it is essential that the house foundations be embedded
a sufficient depth to help resist downhill slope creep soil
expansion and to develop the recommended bearing capacities
of the soils
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Page 8
Post footings should be embedded a minimum of 2 feet below
the existing ground surface and bear on firm residual soil
To resist the large lateral soil pressures retaining wall
footings should extend down to weathered rock anticipated
at about a 4 to 7 foot depth and also have a minimum 8 foot
set back distance measured from the outer edge of the foot
ing out to the adajcent slope face See Plate 2
The bottom of all foundation excavations should be cleared
of loose material and bear on firm material We recommend
that the footing excavations be inspected by a soils
engineer to assure that adequate bearing has been obtained
and that loose soils are removed prior to concrete placement
Slab on Grade
The laboratory tests indicate that some of the near surface
soils are highly expansive Since off site borrow material
will be required we recommend that the on site soils be
used only in the lower portion of the backfill a minimum of
3 feet below the slab subgrade level A select non expan
sive soil such as crusher waste should be utilized for
the fill within 3 feet of the slab
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Page 9
The slab subgrade should be kept moist and protected from
drying prior to concrete placement The subgrade soils
should be compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry
density as established by ASTM D 1557
It is recommended that slab on grade be placed over a 4 inch
layer of base course compacted to 95 of its maximum dry
density
In order to protect against moisture damage to the flooring
material a polyethylene moisture barrier should be ineor
porated beneath the slab
Slab on grade should be separated from the foundations
When a slab is located below the outside ground surface a
subdrainage system around the perimeter of the walls is
recommended to relieve seepage water
Retaining Walls
Retaining walls up to 15 feet high are planned for the resi
dence
Well graded granular material such as 3B Fine aggregate
should be used as a granular drainage blanket for a minimum

The recommendations given under Foundations should be fol

The walls should be designed to resist lateral earth

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the filling and excavation operations
be observed by the soils engineer during construction This
is to see that adequate compaction has been obtained and the
subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of
our soil investigation It is also recommended that the
footing excavation be observed by the soils engineer to see
that adequate bearing has been obtained The recommenda
tions given in this report are contingent upon the above
inspections Failure to comply with the recommendations
contained herein including construction inspections will
nullify this report

APPENDIX A
FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling two
borings to depths of 12 0 to 14 5 feet below the ground surface at the
approximate locations shown on the Boring Location Plan Plate 1 Portable
drilling equipment was used to drill the borings due to the poor accessi
bility of the site
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by visual and
textural examination in the field by our engineer or geologist who contin
uously monitored the drilling operations The classifications were
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory The soils
were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil Classifica
tion System A graphic presentation of the soils is presented on the
Boring Logs Plates A 1 and A 2
In the borings soil samples were obtained by driving either a
a 140 pound hammer free falling a distance of 30 inches The blow counts
to drive the sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the boring logs at
the appropriate sample depths
