INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this investigation was to evaluate subsurface
conditions to provide soil data for the design and construc
tion of the proposed three reservoir sites of Wahiawa Water
Control System at Wahiawa Oahu Hawaii
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations

SITE DESCRIPTION
The three proposed reservoir sites are located along the
Kamehameha Highway in Wahiawa north of the intersection
of H 1 and H 2 Interstate Highways as shown on Plate 1
Map of Site Locations
The lower Reservoir 310 site is located in a pineapple
Field about 1 mile north of H 1 and H 2 Interstate
Highway intersection It is situated on a flat ground
area which slopes down to the south at about 4 percent
gradients The existing ground elevation in this area
generally varies from about 286 to 296 A pineapple
field road runs along the northeastern and southeastern
boundaries of the site Most of the area was covered
with pineapple plants at the time of our investigation
The middle Reservoir 575 site is located in an abandoned
pineapple field about 1343 miles south of Mililani Town
on the east side of Kamehameha Highway The triangular
shaped parcel is bounded by Kamehameha Highway to the
west a government road to the northwest and an A C
walkway to the northeast The site generally slopes
down toward the southwest at about 4 percent gradients
The ground surface of the area varies from about elevation
555 to 562 An approximately 1 horizontal to 1 vertical
1 1 slope bank about 20 feet high is located along the
Kamehameha Highway to the west Dense grass along with
some pineapple plants cover the site

The last and upper Reservoir 883 site is located at the
end intersection of H 2 Interstate Highway and East Range
Road east of Wheeler Air Force Base in Wahiawa The
ground varies from elevation 863 to 866 The 24 feet
wide A C paved East Range Road runs along the southern
portion of the project site This site is covered with
dense grass brush and trees The rolling ground topo
graphy on this site probably indicated that the area was
once used for sugar cane or pineapple cultivation
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed reservoir project consists of a total of
three new 0 5 million gallon reinforced concrete reservoir
structures The tank structure will be 18 feet high and
about 70 feet in diameter one at each of the above re
ferenced sites The anticipated column loads is 76 kips
and perimeter wall load is about 5 7 kips per foot
Each site will have its own A C access roadway to and
around the reservoir structure
Site grading for each structural area consists of some
cut and fill generally less than 5 feet in height
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The project site is situated on Scofield Plateau between
Waianae and Koolau Range
GEOLABS HAWAII
PROPOSED WAHIAWA WATER CONTROL SYSTEM P 4
The lava flows from the Koolau volcano banked against
the already eroded slope of Waianae volcano to form the
gently sloping surface of the Schofield Plateau
The project site soils developed in residuum and old
alluvium derived from basic igneous rock The original
rock formation has been so thoroughly weathered that the
silty clay soil textures replace the basaltic textures
The soils are rich in iron thus producing shades of
red and reddish brown color
Subsurface Exploration
Three borings were drilled at the Reservoir 310 site and
two borings each at Reservoir 575 and 883 sites To
explore the subsurface conditions and to obtain samples
for laboratory testing the borings were drilled to depths
varying from 11 5 to 25 0 feet below the existing ground
surface The approximate locations of the borings for
each reservoir site are shown on the Boring Location Plans
Plates 2 to 4
Detailed information of the field exploration and laboratory
testing is covered in the Appendix along with the boring
logs and laboratory test results

Soil Conditions
The exploration disclosed essentially uniform soil conditions
beneath each of the proposed reservoir site The underlying
soils are highly decomposed basalt of brown and reddish
brown silty clay soil varying from stiff to hard in con
sistency These clayey soils were ancountencd ina ls O t
the borings from the ground surface down to the full depth
of the borings
No groundwater was noted during our field exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the field exploration laboratory testing and
analysis it is our opinion that all three reservoir
sites are suitable for the planned constructions Spread
and line footings may be used to support the columns and
perimeter wall loads
The majority project area within the three reservoir sites
is in pineapple cultivated field Therefore the upper
2 feet of the existing surface soil layer may have been
disturbed by previous planting with varying in place
densities These disturbed surface soils without special
preparation will not be adequate to support the reservoir
slab Special treatment will be required to process the
surface soils in order to provide a more uniform and com
petent subgrade support
GEOLABS HAWAII
Foundation
All the column and continuous wall footings should bear on
the in situ stiff reddish brown clay soil Allowable bear
ing pressure up to 4000 p s f may be used The exterior
footing should bear a minimum of 24 inches below the adjacent
final grade For the interior column footings the embedd
ment depth may be reduced to the normal footing requirement
All of the footing excavations should be compacted to obtain
a minimum of 95 maximum density per ASTM Test Designation
If a footing is located next to a utility trench it should
extend down to the bottom of the utility trench and bear on
firm undisturbed soil Surface water should be diverted away
from the reservoir foundation
Slab on grade for the tank structure should either bear on firm
in situ soil or entirely on compacted structural fill Where
the proposed slab subgrade is within or near the existing ground
elevation over excavation of the loose surface soil should ex
tend down to firm ground and then backfill up to the proposed
subgrade level in 9 inch loose lifts and compacted to a minimum
95 compaction per above referenced standard Although concrete
could be used in lieu of structural backfill it could be quite
uneconomical especially when the thickness of the recompacted
fill layer is large
A 6 inch layer of base course rock cushion fill compacted to
95 density should be provided under the slab area if structural

Settlements
It is estimated that the total settlement for the footing
foundation constructed per above recommendations should
be less than one inch total with differential settlement
of less than one half of an inch The estimated total
settlement for the fully loaded reservoir slabs should be
less than two 2 inches with a differential settlement of
less than one inch
Earthwork Grading
Treatment of Existing Ground The site should be
cleared of all surface vegetation debris and loose
topsoil
In the fill area prior to placement of fill the
existing disturbed and loose surface soils should
be excavated down to stiff reddish brown clay layer
It is estimated that a depth of about 18 to 24 inches
may be required The exposed subgrade surface after
the above recommended excavation should be scarified
down to a depth of 6 inches moisture conditioned and
then recompacted to at least 95 of the maximum dry
density established by ASTM Test Designation D 1557
The excavated areas should be inspected and approved
by the soils engineer prior to placing the rest of
the compacted fill
GEOLABS HAWAII
PROPOSED WAHIAWA WATER CONTROL SYSTEM PG
In the cut area when the site grading will remove
all of the upper loose soils the finish subgrade
ground surface should be scarified down to a depth
of 6 inches moisture conditioned to near optimum
moisture content and then compacted to a minimum
of 95 relative compaction Where the proposed
cut is less than 2 feet below the existing ground
surface the remaining loose soil should be exca
vated as outlined above
Fill Placement In general fill should be placed
in 9 inch loose horizontal lifts and compacted to
the above specified density
Fills placed on slopes having grades steeper than
5 horizontal to 1 vertical 5 1 should be keyed
and benched into the existing firm ground
Slope Based on the site exploration and analysis
the planned cut and fill slope ratios as shown on
the present site plans are adequate We recommend
that the cut slope be inspected by a soils engineer
during construction to verify if the actual slope
materials are as anticipated Some slope adjust
ments might be required in the field should soft
pockets or seepage be encountered during the
actual slope excavation

Lateral Earth Pressure
For lateral earth pressure design we recommend that an
equivalent fluid pressure of 40 p s f per foot of depth
be used for active earth pressure consideration For
passive earth pressure an equivalent fluid pressure of
350 p s f per foot of depth can be used A friction
factor of 0 4 can be utilized to determine sliding resis
tance of the structure and foundation
Roadways
Based on the laboratory swell test results of the near
surface soil samples the red clayey soils at all three
reservoir sites are generally considered to have a low
to non expansive potential condition For the light
automobile traffic anticipated for the service access
roadways the following pavement thickness may be used
2 inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 inch Base Course
8 inch Total Pavement Thickness
The base course should be crushed rock or other granular
material with a CBR of 85 and conforming with Section
31 2 Standard Specification for Public Work Construction
or equivalent The subgrade soil should be scarified
down to a depth of 6 inches moisture conditioned and
compacted to 95 compaction

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during
earthwork construction particularly the subgrade preparation
for the reservoir sites
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be observed
by a soils engineer during construction to see that adequate
bearing has been obtained and that the subsurface conditions
are consistent with the findings of our subsurface investi
gation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested
and approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the
site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon
the above inspections

FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions at the sites were explored by drilling
7 borings utilizing truck mounted equipment The depths of all the
borings were terminated at 11 5 to 25 0 feet below the existing
ground surface at the locations shown on the enclosed Site Plans
Plates 2 to 4
The soils were classified by visual and textural examinations
in the field by our engineer and geologist who continuously monitored
the drilling operations The classifications were verified by visual
inspection and testing in the laboratory All soils were classified
in accordance with the Unified Soil Classification System A graphic
presentation of the soils encountered is presented on the Boring Logs
Plates A 1 through A 12
Soil samples were obtained by driving either a 2 4 inch I D
ring samplers or a standard penetration sampler with a 140 pound
hammer free falling a distance of 30 inches The blow counts to
drive the sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the logs of
borings at the appropriate sample depths
