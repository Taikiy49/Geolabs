In accordance with your request we have made a study of the near
surface soil conditions at the above referenced lot in order to provide
foundation recommendations for the proposed residence The study
consisted of drilling one 1 boring and five 5 shallow probings
performing laboratory testing and reviewing the preliminary house
foundation plans This report summarizes our findings and
recommendations
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS

mainly a one story structure with a lower area at the western side of the
house The proposed new residence is approximately 60 by 72 feet in plan
dimensions
Concrete slab on grade is planned for the eastern portion of the
house while elevated wood floor is planned for the lower western portion
of the house The house will be of wood frame construction

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The underlying soils encountered can generally provide suitable
foundation support for the light loads anticipated for the proposed house
structure provided that some care is exercised during construction to
reduce the swelling potential of the soil
The brown clay encountered near the surface of the site generally has
moderate to high swelling potential depending on the moisture content of
the soil while the brown clayey silt generally has low swelling potential
Therefore to reduce the effects due to soil swelling under on grade
Slabs we recommend that the on site soil be capped with at least 24
inches of non expansive select fill and that the subgrade soils
underneath the select fill and slab be moistened and protected from drying
from the start of construction until the slab concrete placement
It is our understanding that a retaining wall about 36 feet high may
be required at the western portion of the site for the construction of a
new 75 foot long lap pool We recommend that if possible the house and
pool design should be revised to eliminate or at least reduce the height
of the retaining wall

lot Grading
Cuts up to 8 feet are planned along the western side of the house
Fills up to 34 feet may be required behind the retaining wall for the
construction of the swimming pool
All imported fill materials should be tested and approved by the
soils engineer prior to being used in the site
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limits should be cleared of vegetation debris rubbish and other
deleterious materials These materials should be removed and disposed of
properly off site Any loose or soft spots encountered at the subgrade
areas should be over excavated down to the firm subsoils and then replaced
with compacted non expansive select material
The fills should be placed in horizontal lifts not exceeding 8 inches
in compacted thickness and compacted to a minimm of 90 of its maximm
ary density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Fills placed on existing slopes should start from the bottom up and
should be keyed and benched to bond the new fill to the existing slope
The site should be graded to provided drainage of surface run off
away from all foundations
House Foundations
It is anticipated that the eastern portion of the house will be of
slab on grade construction and the remainder will be supported by
continuous wall footings and individual spread footings

Spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the proposed
residence An allowable bearing value of 2 500 p s f may be used for
footings bearing on the natural soil or compacted soil
Individual or wall footings should be embedded a minimm of 12 inches
below the adjacent finished grade
On sloping areas the footing should be embedded down an additional
amount to obtain a 10 foot minimum horizontal set back distance measured
from the outer edge of the footing out to the slope face This additional
embedment depth is required to help resist the downhill slope creep
movement and to develop the recommended bearing capacity of the soil
Surface water should be diverted away from foundations
To further reduce future post construction swelling of the underlying
expansive soils we recommend that the in place soils below the upper
capping fill pad be kept moist prior to concrete and cushion fill
placement
A 4 inch layer of 3B Fine gravel cushion fill should be provided
below the slab In order to protect against future moisture infiltration
an impervious moisture membrane may be incorporated below the slab

Concrete Driveway
Since the driveway can tolerate some amount of heaving for economy
the 24 inch on site soil over excavation and replacement may be replaced
with 10 inch thick base course layer if some amount of small slab cracking
are tolerable It is critical that the subgrade soils should be
maintained in a moist condition and prevented from drying out prior to
concrete placement
Joints are recommended in the driveway slab at about 8 to 10 foot
intervals and at discontinuities in order to allow for some relative
movement of the concrete
Swimming Pool Design
Swimming pool design and construction are the responsibility of the
swimming pool builder This contractor is also generally responsible for
quality control and performance of the completed pool
Since expansive soil was encountered non expansive select fill
material is recommended behind the pool wall If necessary the
recommendations given in the Retaining Walls section of this report may
be given considerations in the pool design
Drainage
We recommend that planters next to the foundations should be avoided
or have concrete bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the
subsoils

These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance
of the above recommended foundations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of
strength The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled tight
against the wall or slab footing inmediately after setting of the concrete
to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained
to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundation
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comments
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the recommendations given herein If this review is not made the
geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for misinterpretation
of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during trench excavation and
backfill placement to see that adequate support and compaction have been
obtained This is to observe compliance with the design concepts
specifications or recommendations and to allow design changes in the event
that subsurface conditions differ from that anticipated prior to start of
construction The recommendations given in this report are contingent
upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site

LIMTTATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field borings Variations of
conditions between the borings may occur and the nature and extend of
these variations may not become evident until construction If variations
then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations given in this report
The locations of the field borings were approximately determined by
taping from structures indicated on the plot plan provided by Custom Home
Planners Elevations of the borings were not available The physical
location and elevation of the field data points should be considered
accurate only to the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field borings depict the approximate boundaries between soil types and
as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross sections are based upon interpolation between field
borings and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
Water level data from the field borings were collected at the times
given on the graphic representations and or in the text of this report
These data have been reviewed and interpretations made in the formulation
of this report However it must be noted that fluctuation may occur due
to variations in rainfall tides temperature and other factors
