Gentlemen
This report summarizes the results of our drilling of one
exploratory boring and one monitoring well near the proposed
injection well to be located in the
mation Plant in Lahaina Maui
The purpose of drilling the exploratory boring at the proposed
injection well site is to define subsurface soil and rock
conditions and to assemble qualitative information describing
the suitability of the underlying geology for effluent injec
tion The second objective is to obtain pre waste injection
baseline hydrologic and water quality characteristics
W O 858 00 P 2
The purpose of the monitoring well is to obtain periodic
water samples and quality information subsequent to the
effluent injection into the underlying rock formation
SITE DESCRIPTION
The exploratory boring Boring No 1 is located in the
Lahaina Wastewater Reclamation Plant near the north side
of the operations building The site is covered by short
grasses and is flat The monitoring well Boring No 2
is located on the northwest side of an intersection of
Honoapiilani Highway and L Honoapiilani Highway The
site is covered by tall grasses and weeds and is bounded
by Honoapiilani Highway to the southeast L Honoapiilani
Highway to the southwest and a fenced off sugar cane field
to the north This site lies in a depression On the north
side going up to Honoapiilani Highway the ground slopes
at approximately 25 percent The boring is located approxi
mately 20 feet from the toe of the slope On the east side
the boring is located approximately 27 feet from the toe of
a 20 percent slope going up to L Honoapiilani Highway
FIELD EXPLORATION
Two test borings were drilled to depths of 151 and 200 feet
as shown in Cross Section Plates 3 4 Boring No 1 was
drilled to a depth of 200 feet and Boring No 2 to a depth
of 151 feet The approximate location of Boring No 1 is
shown on Site Plan Plate 1 and Boring No 2 on Plate 2
W O 858 00 P 3
The drilling operation was monitored by our geologist who
also logged the borings The boring logs are presented on
Plates A 1 through A 1ll
Boring No 1
Boring No 1 was first drilled down to a depth of 29 5 feet
using a 4 inch auger At this level rotary drilling with
a tricone bit was utilized drilling inside of 3 work casing
The tricone bit was used down to 44 0 feet A diamond core
bit was used as much as was feasible from this level to the
termination of the boring at 200 feet Short intervals of
tricone bit drilling were used to clean the cuttings out of
the hole at various levels throughout the well as shown on
the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 6
Three inch diameter work casing was installed down to a depth
of 119 0 feet to aid in the removal of the cuttings from the
hole Below this depth it was necessary to use an air com
pressor with a continuous pressure of 100 p s i to keep the
hole clean There was no caving encountered in the drilling
of the exploratory boring
Boring No 2
Boring No 2 was first drilled down to a depth of 29 5 feet
using a 4 inch auger At this level rotary drilling with
a tricone bit was utilized drilling inside of 3 work casing
The tricone bit was used extensively during the drilling of
this observation well with the diamond core bit used above
W O 858 00 P 4
the level of 106 feet when extremely dense basalt was
encountered From 106 feet to the termination depth
of 151 feet the tricone bit was utilized
Three inch work casing was installed to a level of 80 0
feet Below this an air compressor with a continuous
pressure of 100 p s i was used to clean the cuttings
out of the hole Caving was encountered in this hole
at levels of 120 feet 135 feet and at other undetermined
levels It is suggested that the caving which was not
encountered in Boring No 1 may have been due to the fact
that the tricone bit was used instead of the diamond core
bit The tricone bit may have fractured the rock layers
leaving a great amount of fragments too large to pass out
of the hole while cleaning the cuttings out
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The site is located on the lower west slope of the West
Maui Mountains The West Maui Mountains have been deeply
incised by erosion depositing large quantities of unconsoli
dated recent sediments along the coastal regions At the
project site recent sediment consisting of clayey silts
grading downward into sands gravels and large boulders
are encountered to a depth of about 75 feet
W O 858 00 P 5
Underlying the recent alluvium are basaltic volcanics which
constitute the mass of the West Maui Mountains These basalts
form the Wailuku volcanic series which consist of a thick
series of thin bedded aa and pahoehoe flows dipping
seaward at angles of 10 to 20 degrees
The subsurface explorations disclosed generally three separate
layers These three layers were encountered in both borings
at essentially the same levels From the surface down to
approximately 30 feet soil layers were encountered which
included clayey silt silty sand fine sand and occasional
cobbles Below this level to approximately 75 feet boulders
intermingled with sands and gravels were encountered From
about 75 feet to the termination of the well basaltic rock
units with intermixed zones of weathered basalt and soils were
encountered
WELL COMPLETION
Both the exploratory boring and the monitoring well were
completed by installation of a 2 inch I D PVC plastic
casing This casing was perforated below a depth of
approximately 30 feet in both borings Gravel was packed
along the sides of the PVC casing up to a depth of 10 feet
below ground surface A 3 inch layer of bentonite was
installed at this level to seal off the wells to prevent
the cement grout from migrating past the 10 foot level
W O 858 00 P 6
From 10 feet up to the ground surface cement grout was
placed The PVC casing was then capped with a thread
type cap The monitoring well Boring No 2 was also
covered with gravel to conceal the pipe
The PVC casing in the exploratory boring was installed to
the 200 foot level In the monitoring well when the PVC
casing was installed it only reached a level of 129 feet
We removed the casing and rebored the well down to 151
feet We were then able to install the PVC casing to a
depth of 143 feet

DISCUSSION
It is our opinion after considering the information obtained
while drilling the two borings that during the drilling of
the injection well a working casing will be necessary to
an approximate depth of 75 feet Below this depth we feel
that the well should be able to remain open during drilling
without significant caving
