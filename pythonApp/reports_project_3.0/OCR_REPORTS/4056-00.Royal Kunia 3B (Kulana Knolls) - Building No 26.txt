INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the two story townhouse building structure located adjacent to Kupuohi
Street Building 26 of the Royal Knolls Subdivision in the District of
Kunia on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site
are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering
recommendations resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering
analyses These recommendations are intended for the remedial work design of the
townhouse building structure only The findings and recommendations presented herein
are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to perform remedial work to the two story townhouse building
structure located adjacent to Kupuohi Street Building 26 of the Royal
Knolls Subdivision Based on the information provided settlement and crack
propagation within the walls ceilings and floors were noticed by the homeowners soon
after moving into the units in about May 1995 Some movemenis have continued to the
present Based on floor elevation contours and recent damage assessments we believe
that these recent movements have been small and localized and most have been
fluctuating between upward and downward directions
It is our understanding that Building 26 resides over a site that was formerly used
as a dumping site for construction debris and excess soil material This dumping site was
previously part of a large drainageway Prior to grading the site to its current condition a
geotechnical exploration was performed by Hawaii Geotechnical Group Inc to evaluate
the fill and undisturbed soil conditions for the Royal Knolls Subdivision
This geotechnical exploration was performed in late 1992 through early 1993 Two of the
borings for the Hawaii Geotechnical Group study are located in the vicinity of Building 26

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Our field exploration did not disclose an obvious clear cut condition which could
cause the reported damage and measured settlement t is possible that the distress
noted may be a result of several smaller contributing factors
Possible Causes of the Distress
Based on the results of our exploration we believe that the building distress may
be related to compression of the medium stiff soil layers caused by the placement of the
recent fill material prior to building construction and softening of the fill soils due to water
infiltration Settlements due to these sources are expected to decrease with time
Based on our compression collapse laboratory tests it is estimated that the time
for 90 compression of the fill material is about 4 to 6 months Based on the information
provided the townhouse building construction may have begun at about 3 to 5 months
following mass grading Therefore some of the settlement may be due to fill
compression during the townhouse building construction Also there may be softer soil
lenses beneath the townhouse structure that may have a higher compression potential
and a longer period for compression
Our compression collapse laboratory tests indicate that the potential for softening
or collapse of the soil is low However based on the appearance of distress soon after
the homeowners moved in we believe that some trigger mechanism is likely responsible
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 4056 00 Page 15
for some of the distress Generally water is usually found as the trigger mechanism to
soil problems Based on the ponding of water near Unit No 106 noticed by homeowners
the discharge of roof rainwater and water from Building 27 next to the building and the
close proximity of the landscape irrigation sprinklers we believe that water is a likely
factor Also we believe that there may be softer soil lenses beneath the townhouse
structure that soften under saturation
The general direction of increasing settlement is in a diagonal northwest direction
toward Unit No 106 Based on the settlement contours the greater settlements are
generally located at the southern side of Unit No 106 the western side of Unit No 105
and the northwestern corner of Unit No 104 The settlements are likely greater in these
locations because of the presumed presence of medium stiff fill layers within the lower
very stiff fill material encountered in Boring No 1 We believe that settlements throughout
the townhouse building structure are also due to a lesser degree to settlement of the
upper medium stiff fill layer
The general direction of increasing fill thickness is in a diagonal northeast direction
toward Unit No 103 It should be noted that this does not correspond well with the
direction of increasing settlement The settlement is therefore controlled by the presumed
presence of medium stiff fill material at the northwest building corner
The building loads appear to be too small to cause settlement on the order
of magnitude and lateral extent that has occurred at the subject building structure
Assuming a building load of less than 4 kips per lineal foot a footing width of about
18 inches and a 5 foot crust of very stiff fill material above the medium stiff fill material
we believe that settlement due to the townhouse building structure would be less than
inch
It should be noted that we have considered other possible scenarios to the cause
of the building distress We have tested the soil to evaluate its collapse potential to water
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 4056 00 Page 16
infiltration shrinkage potential and swell potential These tests indicated very small
collapse potential slight swell potential and moderate shrinkage potential We have also
checked the samples for visible organic material The results of these tests and the
absence of soft material in the borings indicated that these were not probable causes of
the building distress
Remedial Work Recommendations
Based on the building distress observed and the results of our exploration it is our
opinion that remedial work should include improvements to the surface drainage around
the building and structural support of selected portions of the building
Drainage Improvements
The ground surface adjacent to the building should be shaped to drain water away
from the foundations to reduce the potential for surface water infiltration into the
soils below the structure This may involve the construction of swales connected
to a drainage pipe that discharges to the parking lot located to the east of the
townhouse structure In addition a 3 foot wide thickened edge sidewalk or
impervious liner may be installed adjacent to the perimeter of the building
structure The purpose of the sidewalk or liner is to reduce the potential for soil
moisture migration that could cause additional soil swelling or soil shrinkage
Structural Support
Once the compression of the soil under the structure has stabilized under the
current load structural support may be provided at selected locations under the
building The structural support may consist of Atlas piers extending to below the
fill material and bearing into the residual soils at depths of up to about 40 feet
below the existing ground surface Due to the large affected area and variable
amounts of settlements experienced we recommend that the structural support be
installed in stages to reduce inconvenience to the residents and improve cost
effectiveness The first stage would include installation of Atlas piers around the
GEOLABS HAWAIl

bearing capacity of 20 000 pounds based on a 3 5 inch outside diameter steel
pipe is obtained and to a minimum depth of 40 feet below the existing
foundations
Prior to placement of the concrete sidewalks the subgrade should be
moisture conditioned to near optimum moisture content and proof rolled or lightly
compacted to a relatively unyielding surface Over compaction of the subgrade
should be avoided The subgrade should also be moistened to maintain the near
optimum moisture content prior to placement of concrete
The sidewalks should be provided with construction joints at 3 foot intervals and
right angle intersections The excavated clay material may be used to re grade
the lot to slope away from the townhouse building structure

noted at one location Continued large settlements could have a detrimental effect

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content ASTM D 2216 and unit weight determinations were performed
