This technical memorandum presents’ our preliminary geotechnical
recommendations for the above project. The preliminary recommendations presented
herein are based on our desktop study of available subsurface information near the project
site. The performance of field borings will be performed for the project. The final report
should be consulted when it becomes available.
Anticipated Subsurface Conditions
Based on our desktop study, we anticipate that the project site is underlain by a
surface fill layer followed by medium stiff to stiff older alluvial deposits and medium stiff to
hard residual soils. We do not anticipate encountering groundwater in the relatively
shallow excavations for the drainage structures.
Drainage Structures
We understand that new drainage structures consisting of manhole, catch basin,
and inlet structures are planned for the project as summarized below.
. 45-385 Namoku Street — New Manhole Structure
. 45-140 Mokulele Drive — New Catch Basin Structure
. 45-306 Akimala Place — New Manhole Structure
. 45-613 Puohala Street — New inlet Structure
ARON —
Drainage Structure Foundations
Based on the anticipated subsurface conditions encountered, an allowable bearing
pressure of up to 3,000 pounds per square foot (psf) may be utilized for design of the
drainage structures bearing on the anticipated medium stiff to stiff older alluvium and
residual soils at the project site. To provide uniform bearing support for the drainage
structure, we recommend providing a 6-inch thick layer of No. 3B Fine Gravel (ASTM C33,
No. 67 gradation) below the bottom of the structure.

resist the lateral earth pressures (at-rest conditions) from the adjacent soils. The 
recommended lateral earth pressures for design of drainage structures, expressed in

Backfilling of Drainage Structure Excavations 
The drainage structure excavations will need to be properly backfilled. The 
excavated on-site soils or imported fill materials that are free of vegetation, deleterious 
materials, and clay lumps and rock fragments greater than 3 inches in maximum 
dimension may be used as backfill above the cushion layer up to the finish grades.  
Backfills using on-site soils should be moisture-conditioned to at least 2 percent 
above the optimum moisture content, placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose 
thickness, and compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction. Where drainage 
structures are located below pavement areas, the upper 3 feet of the drainage structure 
backfill below the pavement grade should be compacted to at least 95 percent relative 
compaction. Relative compaction refers to the in-place dry density of soil expressed as a 
percentage of the maximum dry density as determined by ASTM D1557. Optimum 
moisture is the water content (percentage by dry weight) corresponding to the maximum 
dry density.
