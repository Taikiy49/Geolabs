Dear Mr Steinberger
This seventh progress report is presented to summarize our work efforts and the
results of the additional field instrumentation monitoring conducted for the Kuahea Street
Area Movement project located in Palolo Valley on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The
approximate location of the project site is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This progress report provides summary information pertaining to the installation and
initialization of replacement instrumentation Phase Il Additional Field Exploration and
the results of monitoring earth movements and subsurface water levels at the project site
Phase Ill Instrument Monitoring This report summarizes the field data and
observations that were collected from three monitoring site visits which were conducted
on October 21 and 22 through 24 2008 and January 26
and 27 2004 Our work was performed in general accordance with our fee proposal dated
March 6 2003 and Amendment No 3 for Engineering Services for the Kuahea Street Area
Movement Project Contract No F 60578 CIP No 97504
This report is our first semi annual progress report covering the additional 12 month
instrument monitoring period which commenced and expires
August 31 2004 It should be noted that the regularly scheduled site visit for instrument
monitoring which was to occur in mid February 2004 was actually conducted earlier on
January 26 and 27 2004 in order to record field data following recent heavy rains which
occurred in the December to January timeframe

Observations Pavement Structural Deformation
A general reconnaissance of the project site was conducted on to
record observations pertaining to the existing site conditions
Waiomao Road Area
The existing elongated longitudinal pavement cracks that were previously sealed to
reduce the potential for appreciable surface water infiltration along the north side of
Waiomao Road in the vicinity of Area B continue to be in fair condition The
existing arc shaped pavement cracks that cross Waiomao Road along the western
boundary of the Waiomao Landslide were previously sealed to reduce the potential
for appreciable surface water infiltration The previously sealed arc shaped cracks
in the roadway appear to be experiencing continued deformation consisting of
increasing vertical offset across portions of the length of the crack As a result the
bumps and undulations surrounding the arc shaped cracks continue to become
more pronounced
The continued settlement of the roadway pavement surrounding the existing storm
drain manhole fronting 2340 Waiomao Road was observed The pavement
settlement has produced a noticeable bump located along the north side of the
southwest bound travel lane In addition the eastern boundary of the Waiomao
Landslide that crosses Waiomao Road also was observed to exhibit a noticeable
bump when crossing with a vehicle The existing pavement patch at this location
appears to be in fair condition
Kuahea Street Area
Observation of the area along Kuahea Street at the head of the Waiomao Landslide
indicates that the road pavement and northern road shoulder continue to
experience settlement of the ground surfaces The ground settlements have
produced uneven pavement surfaces with significant bumps that traverse the
roadway Large open cracks 0 5 inches to 1 inch wide and settlement pits of
about 1 foot in diameter were beginning to form along the existing pavement repair
patches in the center of the road
