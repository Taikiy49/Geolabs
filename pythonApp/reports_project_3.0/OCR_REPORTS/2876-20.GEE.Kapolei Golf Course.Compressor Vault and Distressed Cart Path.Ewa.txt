INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the compressor vault and distressed cart path in Kapolei Golf Course The
general location and vicinity of the project sites are shown on the Project Location Map
Plate 1 The locations of the compressor vault and the distressed cart path are also
shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering recommenda
tions resulting from the field exploration laboratory testing and engineering analyses

The recommendations presented herein are intended for civil and structural design
input and are not intended to form the basis for preparation of final construction cost
estimates nor for bidding purposes
PROJECT BACKGROUND
Compressor Vault
The compressor vault is located within the Kapolei Golf Course in the district of
Ewa on the island of Oahu Hawaii The compressor vault is situated at about 80 feet
to the east of No 1 Tee and approximately 30 feet from the west shore of Lake No 3
as shown on Site Plan 1 Plate 2
The compressor vault houses equipment which pumps water from Lake No 3
through two intake pipes to various waterfalls in the golf course Based on the
construction plan the top of the compressor vault is at approximately 76 feet Mean Sea
Level MSL and extends to a depth of about 64 feet MSL Lake No 3 has a water
surface elevation of about 65 feet MSL and the bottom at the center of Lake No 3 is
at about 57 feet MSL It is our understanding that the intake pipes drop approximately
3 feet below the bottom of Lake No 3 corresponding to about 54 feet MSL and run
westward horizontally until approximately 5 feet from the edge of the compressor vault
Near the vault the intake pipes turns into a 90 degree elbow and extend vertically upward
for about 11 feet before turning through another elbow into the compressor vault
During our site meeting it is our understanding that the intake pipes consist of
PVC material and a concrete jacket was constructed around the pipes for most of the
length from the edge of the compressor vault to the shore of Lake No 3 except along
the elbows

Distressed Cart Path
The distressed cart path is located between No 7 Green and No 8 Tee within the
Kapolei Golf Course The distressed cart path is located at approximately 10 to 15 feet
to the west of an existing 15 inch sewer line The location of the distressed cart path is
shown on Site Plan 2 Plate 3
During our site visit cracks and voids were noted along a 12 foot section of the
cart path Voids between the concrete slab and the ground on the order of 5 inches
were noted from the edge of the cart path Based on our field measurements of the
depth of the adjacent sewer manhole the invert of the sewer pipe traversing areas near
the distressed cart path is at approximately 10 to 12 feet below the existing ground
On the basis of construction plans the distressed cart path is located in an area
where the original ground elevation was at about 80 feet MSL therefore as much as
8 feet of cut was required during the Kapolei Borrow Site and Mass Grading Phase
project to attain the existing grades In general a maximum dry density value of 106
pounds per cubic foot may be used as the Proctor for the clayey silt material

GROUNDWATER CONDITIONS
Compressor Vault
The compressor vault is situated at about 76 feet MSL and the water level at
Lake No 3 is at approximately 65 feet MSL
Groundwater was encountered in the drilled borings at depths of approximately 9
to 11 feet below the existing ground These depths generally correspond to elevations
of approximately 61 and 65 feet MSL
Distressed Cart Path
The distressed cart path near No 7 Green is located at about 75 feet MSL
During our exploration groundwater was encountered only in Boring No 6 at about 10
feet below the existing ground The groundwater level measured was much higher than
anticipated for the geologic and hydrologic conditions at the site
We believe that the groundwater may have seeped through and or accumulated
at the more permeable cushion fill layer below the existing 15 inch sewer pipe

the groundwater levels observed are presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 1 through

FINDINGS
Compressor Vault
The following describes our principal findings based on our review of reports field
reconnaissance boring data and laboratory test results
1
During our site reconnaissance slope inclinations of about 2 horizontal to
1 vertical and 4 horizontal to 1 vertical were noted extending downward to
the shore of Lake No 3 from the east side of the compressor vault
Surficial cracks were noted parallel to the slope and extending to a gravel
path which is about 15 feet to the east of the compressor vault suggesting
that some ground settlement may have occurred
Based on our observation of an abrupt drop in the elevation of the gravel
path ground settlements on the order of approximately 1 foot may have
occurred at where Boring Nos 3 and 3A were drilled
The top of the compressor vault is at approximately 76 feet MSL and the
invert of the intake pipes is at approximately 56 feet MSL On this basis
and the spacing between the pipes we envision that trench on the order of
10 feet wide and 20 feet deep were excavated during the construction of the
intake pipes
In the compressor vault openings of about 2 inches were noted at the top
of the cast iron flange of the intake pipes suggesting settlement may have
occurred on the intake pipes located outside the compressor vault
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2876 20 Page 9
5 Boring and 3A were drilled on the east side of the compressor
vault and within the trench Soft clayey silts were noted from depths of
about 9 to 18 feet below the existing ground corresponding to the
approximate elevations where the elbows of the intake pipes are located
6 Boring Nos 2 was drilled on the adjacent side of the compressor vault
where trench excavation was not performed Medium stiff to stiff clayey silts
were noted within the depth of exploration and minor settlement was noted
near Boring No 2
7 Groundwater levels were noted at about 9 to 11 feet below the existing
ground in Boring Nos 1 and 2 These groundwater level readings
correspond approximately to 651 feet MSL which is about the water level
of Lake No 3 In Boring No 3 groundwater level was noted at about 11
feet below the existing ground which corresponds to about 61 feet MSL
It is our opinion that the following may be concluded based on our findings
1 Ground settlement have occurred in areas around the compressor vault
especially on its east side where the intake pipes traverse below ground
from the center of Lake No 3 Based on our boring data and laboratory
test results it is our opinion that substantial amount of the ground
settlement may be attributed to inadequate compaction of the backfill
material during the trench backfill Some minor settlement was also noted
in the area on the west side of the compressor vault where Boring No 2
was drilled
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2876 20 Page 10
2 Soft material was encountered between depths of about 9 to 18 feet below
the existing ground on the east side of the compressor vault It is our
opinion that substantial amount of the ground settlements may have
occurred in the zone between 9 to 18 feet below the existing ground where
soft material was encountered In general this is where the elbows of the
intake pipes were located Without adequate subgrade support overburden
soil pressure on the order of 1 kip per square foot will exert enormous
pressure on the pipe and fill causing consolidation of the soft fill material
and settlement of the pipe It is our opinion that the differential settlement
between the elbow section of the intake pipes and the flange may have
caused the 2 inch opening at the top of the cast iron flange
3 The groundwater level measured was much higher than anticipated for the
geologic and hydrologic conditions at the site However the groundwater
level was at about the water level of Lake No 3 We believe that the
groundwater level may be caused by the following
a Water leaking from broken pipes near or within the compressor vault
b Water leaking from the liner or membrane of the pipe intake near the
center of Lake No 3 flowing along the intake pipe alignment

RECOMMENDATIONS
Compressor Vault
Intake pipes consisting of PVC or cast iron should be constructed with concrete
jacket between the elbows near the compressor vault The concrete jacket should be
designed as a rigid body spanning from the compressor vault to its horizontal section at
approximately 54 feet MSL
The following recommendations are general guidelines for the anticipated repair
work and backfill operation at the compressor vault
1 Trench backfills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in
loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent maximum dry
density and above optimum moisture content
2 Fills to be placed on slopes having ratios steeper than 5 horizontal to 1
vertical should be keyed and benched into firm soil
3 To replace repair the intake pipes we envision a small excavation on the
order of 20 feet deep would be needed To facilitate construction we
envision the small excavation would be backfilled with concrete which
would serves as a jacket Alternatively a concrete jacket may be
constructed around the pipes and the area between the jacket and the
surrounding soil material may be backfilled with lean concrete
We have also looked into the possibility of using self compacting No 3B
Fine gravels as backfill material However on site fine materials such as
silt and clay may migrate into the voids of No 3B Fine gravels and cause
ground settlement It would be necessary to wrap filter fabric around the
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2876 20 Page 13
No 3B Fine gravels to reduce the migration of fines Due to difficult
installation of the filter fabric with No 3B Fine gravels in deep excavation
it appears that backfilled with lean concrete may be a more viable solution
4 To reduce the amount of earthwork during excavation and backfill trench
shoring and bracing will likely be required The adequacy and safety of the
shoring installation should be the sole responsibility of the contractor In
addition the excavation shoring system used must comply with all
applicable safety requirements It is recommended that a qualified
geotechnical engineer be retained by the contractor to design and evaluate
the shoring system used
5 Our field exploration encountered groundwater level at approximately 9 to
11 feet below the existing ground Dewatering will likely be necessary
since the groundwater level is above the excavation bottom However the
source of the groundwater is not known If the presence of groundwater
was due to leakage from Lake No 3 substantial amount of dewatering may
be required Therefore we recommend that further exploration and testing
be performed to determine the source of the groundwater
Distressed Cart Path
On the basis of our field data and laboratory test results we recommend that the
distressed cart path be removed and reconstructed The underlying soils should be over
excavated to a depth of approximately 5 feet below the finished grade and recompacted
to at least 90 percent of the maximum dry density and above optimum moisture content
The new cart path should be underlain by about 10 inches of select borrow material
compacted to 95 percent relative compaction
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2876 20 Page 14
Loose soft areas encountered at the subgrade levels should be over excavated
to expose firm soils and replaced with on site or imported non expansive granular material
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction and a non yielding condition
