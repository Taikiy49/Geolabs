Borings 3 and 4 located at higher elevations on the
ridge in areas of increased rainfall encountered more
weathered softer bedrock materials which should not
present great difficulty in ripping to the proposed grades
Borings 3 and 4 36 feet and 51 feet deep respectively
were excavated entirely with a continuous flight auger
without great difficulty Locally hard unweathered
_basalt was encountered in boring 3 at a depth of 18 0
feet necessitating the drilling of another hole It is
probable that hard boulders will be encountered during
grading and may require special treatment such as minor
blasting mud capping if large enough
The subsurface data indicates that the materials at the
drill sites can generally be ripped However due to the
variability of volcanic rocks local hard bedrock should
be anticipated and may require minor blasting to excavate
Any excavation work on the ridge should be performed with
considerable care to avoid the possibility of loose boulders
rolling down the slopes onto adjacent developed residential
areas
