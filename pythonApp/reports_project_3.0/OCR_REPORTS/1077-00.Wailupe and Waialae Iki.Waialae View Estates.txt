Field Exploration
Eight borings were drilled at the site to depths of 15 to
21 5 feet at the approximate locations shown on the Boring
Location Plan Plate l

the inaccessible areas and seismic refraction Surveys The
seismic refraction surveys were conducted near the borings
to correlate the measured velocities with the test boring
cores to establish the relative density and character of the
subsurface rock materials The results of the seismic sur

inches in size can be used as fill material Many loose
boulders will be generated from the rock excavation
Boulder fill disposal does not appear feasible since large
amounts of sandy soil would be required to fill the voids
between boulders in order to minimize future ground
settlements caused by migration of the upper finer mater
ial into the boulder voids Also since the major fill is
in the roadway areas the boulders would obstruct later
utility excavations Therefore it appears that the large
boulders would have to be hauled out unless a suitable
disposal site can be located We recommend that the
possibility of using the remainder Lot 115 on subdivision
plan next to Lot 29 as a boulder disposal area be inves
tigated

practicable in excavated lava rock material Instead a
testing program to determine the number of Passes of a
compactor required to achieve the desired compaction
should be conducted at the start of the project The
number of passes can then be used as the criteria for
compaction

FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored
by drilling 8 borings to depths of 15 to 21 5 feet utilizing
truck mounted drilling equipment The approximate locations
of the borings are shown on the enclosed Boring Location Plan
The soils rock were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory
All soils were classified in accordance with the Unified
Soil Classification System A graphic presentation of the
soils encountered in the borings is presented on the Boring
Logs Plates A 1 through A 8
Rock core samples were obtained by using a rotary wash
NWD4 core barrel The core recovery and rock quality designa
tion RQD is presented on the boring logs
seismic refraction surveys were conducted at various
locations at the project site in order to evaluate the range
of seismic velocities in the rock formation which can generally
be correlated to the rock hardness The results of the seismic
surveys are presented on Plate A 9 The approximate locations
of the surveys are shown on the Boring Location Plan Plate 1
