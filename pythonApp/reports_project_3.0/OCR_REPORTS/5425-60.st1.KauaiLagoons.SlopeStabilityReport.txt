The following sections of this geotechnical analysis report present the basis of the
geotechnical engineering evaluation conducted on the existing lagoon slopes at Kauai
Lagoons located in Lihue on the Island of Kauai Hawaii The project location and general
vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
1
Project Considerations
The Kauai Lagoons project site is in the Nawiliwili area of Lihue on the Island of
Kauai Hawaii Based on the information provided we understand a visual dam
safety inspection was conducted by others at the existing lagoon within the Kauai
Lagoons Resort Based on the findings of the dam safety inspection it was desired
to conduct a slope stability analysis of the existing lagoon The area of study is
limited to only about 600 linear feet along the southern face of the existing lagoon
The general area of the slope stability study is shown on the General Site Plan
Plate 2 A detailed area map of the study is shown on the Site Plan Plate 3
2

drawings related to the design of the existing lagoon Based on the available
drawings we understand the existing lagoon was constructed in the late 1980s

lagoon ranged from cuts of up to about 4 feet in depth at the western side to fills of
up to about 10 feet in height at the eastern side of the lagoon The lagoon face
generally has a slope inclination of about two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V and

Existing In Situ Soil Conditions and Parameter
A field exploration program was conducted at the site to evaluate the existing
subsurface conditions Our field exploration program consisted of drilling and
sampling four borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 4 along the existing
lagoon slope crest extending to depths of about 29 5 to 36 feet below the existing
ground surface The approximate boring locations are shown on the Site Plan
Plate 3
In general the project site is underlain by residual soils over saprolite Fill materials
also were encountered in Boring Nos 3 and 4 Based on the original grading plans
fill materials may be anticipated at the eastern side of the lagoon Fill materials
where encountered generally consisted of stiff to very stiff silts and clays
recompacted on site soils and extended up to about 4 to 10 feet below the ground
surface Residual soils generally consisted of very stiff to hard silty clays The
thickness of the residual soils generally ranged from about 8 to 22 feet Residual
soils generally were underlain by saprolite consisting of very stiff to hard clayey silts
with weathered gravel extending to the maximum depth explored of about 36 feet
below the existing ground surface
Groundwater was not encountered in our borings during our field exploration
However it should be noted that groundwater levels may fluctuate depending on
factors such as seasonal rainfall and precipitation storm surge conditions seepage
from the lagoon and other factors Based on the topographic survey map provided
the lagoon water level is at about Elevation 90 to 91 feet Mean Sea Level MSL
The field exploration methodology is described in Appendix A Descriptions and
graphic representations of the materials encountered in the borings drilled are
presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 4 Results of the laboratory
tests conducted on selected soil samples are presented in Appendix B

Slope Stability Analysis
Global slope stability of the existing lagoon slope was evaluated using the computer
program ReSSA from ADAMA Engineering Inc Four analysis conditions were
taken into consideration for our slope stability analysis These analysis conditions
included the following
Operational
Rapid Drawdown
Empty Lagoon
Seismic

All four conditions were evaluated for the four idealized cross sections for a total of
16 slope stability cases The following subsections describe the various analysis
conditions
4 1
Operational Condition
The existing condition of the lagoon during normal operating condition was
evaluated for slope stability The lagoon water level was assumed at the
surveyed water elevation ranging from about 90 to 91 feet MSL Because
this is the general condition of the lagoon during the majority of the time a
long term slope stability analysis using effective stress parameters was used
to evaluate the Operational Condition
4 2
Rapid Drawdown Condition
Rapid Drawdown Condition models the lagoon during an event that the
water in the lagoon is emptied relatively quickly The lagoon water level is
assumed to be at the bottom of the lagoon elevation empty condition while
transient pore water pressures remain in the embankment Because this
condition would only occur for a short duration a short term slope stability
analysis using total stress parameters was used to evaluate the Rapid
Drawdown condition This condition is unlikely given that the slope of the
lagoon is lined with an HDPE lining and groundwater was not encountered in
the borings drilled for this study
4 3
Empty Lagoon Condition
The Empty Lagoon Condition evaluates the stability of the lagoon slopes
while the lagoon water remains emptied for an extended time Pore water
pressures are assumed to have dissipated and water level is not
encountered within the limits of analysis A long term slope stability analysis
using effective stress parameters was used to evaluate this condition
4 4
Seismic Condition
Slope stability of the lagoon slope during a seismic event also was
evaluated Based on the subsurface materials encountered and the geology
of the area we believe the project site may be classified as a Stiff Soil Profile
corresponding to a Site Class D based on IBC Table 2003 Edition
In addition the maximum considered earthquake spectral response
acceleration at short periods Ss was estimated at 0 24g Based on Ss of
0 24g a peak ground acceleration of 0 10g was estimated
and used for the slope stability analysis The maximum considered
earthquake is based on an approximately 2 500 year return period

Conclusions
Based on the results of the global slope stability analyses the existing lagoon slope
has a minimum factor of safety of 2 2 Results of the global slope stability analyses
are summarized on Plate 5 for ease of reference The Engineer Manual Design
and Construction of Levees by the U S Army Corps of Engineers EM 1110 2
1913 generally requires a minimum factor of safety of 1 4 for long term slope
stability In addition the minimum factor of safety required for short term slope
analysis we believe that the existing lagoon generally has adequate factors of
safety from global slope stability considerations
It should be noted that the global slope stability analyses assessed the potential
failure circle exiting the slope on the lagoon side The potential failure circle exiting
the land side of the lagoon was not analyzed because the ground surface generally
is level on the land side of the lagoon and does not have sloping ground conditions
Details of the critical slip circles for the 16 cases analyzed are presented in
Appendix C
6
Limitations of Analyses
The analyses and conclusions submitted herein are based in part upon
information obtained from the construction documents topographic survey map
and the borings drilled for this study Variations of the subsurface conditions
between and beyond the field borings may occur and the nature and extent of
these variations may not become evident until further investigation is underway If
variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the analyses and
conclusions provided in this report

Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling four
borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 4 The borings were drilled with a
truck mounted drill rig at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 3
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by
testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified
Soil Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representations of the
materials encountered during drilling are provided on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1
through A 4
Soil samples were obtained from the borings drilled in general accordance with
ASTM D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 2 inch OD
standard penetration sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches In addition
some relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained in general accordance with
ASTM D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 3 inch OD modified
California sampler with the same hammer and drop The blow counts needed to drive the
sampler the second and third 6 inches a total of 12 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown
on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Pocket penetrometer and torvane shear tests were performed on selected cohesive
soil samples retrieved in the field The pocket penetrometer test provides an indication of
the unconfined compressive strength of the soil sample The torvane shear test provided a
quick estimate of the undrained shear strength of the soil sample Pocket penetrometer
and torvane shear test results are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate
sample depths

were performed on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of
soil properties The test results are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate

on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Four Direct Shear tests ASTM D 3080 were performed on selected soil samples

presented on Plates B 2 through B 5

the silty and clayey soils encountered The approximate in situ effective overburden
pressure was used as the applied confining pressure for the relatively undisturbed soil
sample The test results and the stress strain curves are presented on Plate B 6
