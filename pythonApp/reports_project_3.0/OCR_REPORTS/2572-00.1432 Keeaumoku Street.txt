INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Makiki Parkside condominium in the Makiki residential
district on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location of the site and its vicinity is
shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The exploration was performed in
accordance with our proposal dated
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2572 00 Page 2
This report summarizes the exploration findings and our geotechnical engineering
recommendations derived from our analysis based on the data obtained during the
exploration These recommendations are intended for design input and are not intended
for construction estimation or bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
Based on the plans provided to us the proposed new condominium will be a
3 story structure approximately 42 by 69 feet in plan dimensions The upper two 2
floors will be of wooden frame construction and the ground floor will have asphaltic
concrete pavement which will be used for parking
Minimal site grading involving cuts and fills generally less than one 1 foot is
anticipated It is our understanding that no retaining walls will be required for the
proposed project
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions at the project site in order to develop a generalized soil rock data set for the
formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to site grading
foundation and pavement design of the project
The work was done in accordance with our proposal dated The
scope of work was limited to

site and its vicinity

The subject lot is located at 1432 Keeaumoku Street on the mauka north side of
its intersection with the Lunalilo Freeway in the Makiki residential district on the island of
Oahu This rectangular shaped lot encompassing approximately 6 611 square feet is
fronted by Keeaumoku Street to the southeast
The topography of the site is generally level The current ground elevations range
from approximately thirty eight 38 feet to forty 40 feet above Mean Sea Level

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling three 8 borings to
depths of thirty one and one half 31 5 feet below the existing ground surface The
approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general the field exploration encountered approximately a 4 to 5 foot thick
surface layer consisting of stiff clayey silt with fine sand underlain by layers of dense silty
fine sand with cinder sand and gravel to the maximum depth of the drilled borings
No groundwater was encountered during our field exploration

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration encountered approximately a 4 to 5 foot thick surface layer
consisting of stiff clayey silt with fine sand underlain by layers of dense silty fine sand with
cinder sand and gravel to the maximum depth of the drilled borings thirty one and one
half 31 5 feet below the existing ground surface

It is our opinion that spread and continuous footing foundations can be used to
support the proposed condominium building with on grade parking area
Details of our recommendations are discussed in the following sections
Foundations
Based on our field exploration and subsequent geotechnical engineering analyses
we recommend that spread and continuous footing foundations be used to support the
proposed three 3 story high condominium structure with provision of on grade parking
An allowable bearing value of 3000 p s f is recommended for footings bearing on the
in situ stiff clayey silt with fine sand A minimum footing embedment of twenty four 24
inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade is also recommended The bearing value
is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third when temporary loads
wind and seismic are considered
The bottom of all footing excavations should be recompacted to obtain a minimum
of 95 maximum dry density as determined by ASTM Test Method D1557 78
If soft loose soil pockets are encountered at the bottom of the future footing
excavation they should be excavated to stiff in situ soil and replaced with select on site
silty soil or granular select borrow compacted to a minimum of 95 maximum dry density
If a footing is located next to a utility line it should extend to the bottom of the
utility trench to preclude footing settlement due to possible settlement of the trench
backfill
Surface water should be diverted away from the foundation to avoid ponding of
surface run off that could adversely affect the performance of the foundations

Site Grading
Minimal site grading with generally less than one 1 foot cut or fill is anticipated for
the proposed project
Prior to the commencement of earthwork areas within the grading limits and the
proposed condominium structure should be cleared and grubbed of all debris rubbish
old foundations and other deleterious materials The resulting grub and spoil material
should be disposed of properly off site
The fill areas should be scarified to a depth of 6 inches vegetation removed
moisture conditioned and recompacted to 90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM
D1557 78
All imported fill material should be tested and approved by the soils engineer prior
to transportation to the site
The fill should be placed in maximum 10 inch thick loose level lifts and compacted
to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Pavement
Asphaltic concrete pavement is anticipated for the proposed on grade ground floor
parking area The following preliminary pavement section may be used
2 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 Inch Base Course
8 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
on Compacted Moist Subgrade
The recommended section considers medium traffic and assumes that adequate
drainage will be provided in the paved areas

Utility Trench
Construction of new utility lines is anticipated for the proposed project A granular
bedding consisting of six 6 inches of 3B Fine gravel is recommended under the pipes
Granular materials should be used for trench backfill The backfill up to about one
1 foot above the pipes should consist of self compacting granular materials such as
3B Fine gravel which can be lightly tamped to reduce the possibility of damaging the
pipes
The remaining upper portion of the utility trench backfill to the finish subgrade
should be placed in maximum 9 inch horizontal loose lifts mechanically compacted to a
minimum of 90 compaction as determined by ASTM Test Method D1557 78 to protect
against potential future ground subsidence
Any existing utility lines should be located and protected by the contractor during
construction
Drainage
The finish grade outside the footings and pavements should be shaped to shed
water away from foundations and to avoid ponding condition Excessive landscape
watering near foundations and slab should be avoided
Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have concrete bottoms and
drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the above
foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause excessive subsurface soil
saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations

should be properly backfilled tight against the footing immediately after setting of the
concrete to prevent ponding condition
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be forwarded to the
geotechnical engineer for review and written comments prior to construction This review
is needed to determine adherence to the earthwork and foundation recommendations
given herein If this review is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume
responsibility for misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during the site grading and construction of the
foundations This is to observe compliance with the design concepts specifications or
recommendations and to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions
differ from that anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in
this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and approved by
the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during construction are different
from those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from field borings Variations of conditions between the field data
collection points may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not
become evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will
be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations given in this report
The location of the field borings as shown on Plate 2 are approximate having been
determined by taping from structures indicated on the topographic survey map prepared
by Controlpoint Surveying Engineering Inc dated Elevations of the data
collection points were estimated by interpolation from elevation points on the same
topographic map provided The physical location and elevation of the field data collection
points should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the field data collection
points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote
a gradual transition
This report has been prepared for the exclusive use of the project designers for
specific application to the site grading and foundation design of the subject project in
accordance with generally accepted geotechnical engineering principles and practices
No other warranty expressed or implied is made
This report has been prepared solely for the purpose of assisting the
architect engineer in the preliminary design evaluation of the proposed project
Therefore this report may not contain sufficient data or the proper information for use
in contract bid estimation A contractor wishing to bid on this project is urged to retain

the performance of additional site specific exploration for bid estimating purposes
The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are based on our site

The owner client should be aware that unanticipated soil conditions are commonly

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling three 8 borings to
depths of 31 5 feet below the existing ground surface at the approximate locations shown
on the Site Plan Plate 2 A truck mounted drill rig was used to drill the borings
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist or engineer who continuously monitored the
drilling operations The classifications were further reviewed by visual observation and
testing in the laboratory All soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified
Soil Classification System A Summary of the materials encountered are presented on the
Boring Logs Plates A 1 thru A 3
Soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving a 2 inch standard
falling 30 inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the last 12 inches are shown on
the Boring Logs at the appropriate sample depths
