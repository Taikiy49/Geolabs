SOIL NAIL INSTALLATION AND TESTING
IOLANI AVENUE RETAINING WALL
JOB NO 35 07
Dear Mr Nakamoto
This letter report summarizes our geotechnical observation services performed
during the installation and testing of the soil nail anchors for the Iolani Avenue Retaining
Wall project located in the Makiki area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii
In general this letter report presents our observations and the results of the
anchor testing performed on the soil nails for the project from August 18 2009 through
August Construction was the general contractor for the project and
Pacific Ground System was the specialty subcontractor responsible for installation of
the soil nail anchors
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The retaining wall project site is located along Iolani Avenue between Prospect
and Miller Streets in Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The existing 10 to 19 feet
tall rock veneer wall providing grade separation between the roadway and the park
below experienced partial sectional failure as shown in Figure1 below The soil nail
retaining wall was designed to repair the failed portion and to stabilize the remaining
retaining wall
Geolabs Inc conducted a geotechnical engineering evaluation of the project
site and our recommendations are presented in the report entitled Geotechnical
Engineering Exploration Iolani Avenue Retaining Wall Honolulu Oahu Hawaii dated
The purpose of our geotechnical engineering services during
construction of the project was to observe the construction and testing of the soil nail
anchors for general conformance with the project plans and specifications

As part of the project requirements a soil nail retaining wall system was designed
consisting of a series of individual reinforcing bars grouted into drilled holes to stabilize the
slope A structural shotcrete layer was specified to be placed on the face of the reinforced
slope in addition to an architectural sculpture shotcrete layer for aesthetic appearance
The soil nail anchor lengths were specified to be between 15 and 25 feet depending on the
slope height as shown on the attached Soil Nail Numbering Plan Plate 1
A total of eighty six 86 soil nail anchors were designed to support the new
retaining wall As part of the pre production requirements one pre production soil nail
anchor sacrificial was installed and tested prior to the production works The
pre production and production soil nail anchor locations are shown on Plate 1
The pre production anchor was installed on August 18 2009 and tested on August
24 2009 using verification loading procedures described in the project specification The
installation and testing were conducted by Pacific Ground System and observed by
Geolabs Inc The results of the pre production soil nail anchor installation and testing are
included in Appendix A of this letter report
In addition a production soil nail anchor Anchor No 11 1 located within the failed
segment as shown on Plate 1 was selected as part of the pre production activity to
further examine the adequacy of design parameter in various soil rock conditions The soil
nail anchor was installed on and tested on using
the verification loading procedures The installation logs of Anchor No 11 1 are presented
in Appendix B The results of the verification testing are presented in Appendix C

A Geolabs representative was present to observe the drilling installation and grouting
operations during construction of the soil nail anchors The soil nail anchors were drilled
using either a Casagrande C6 track mounted drill rig or a Percussion Drifter drill
attached on a Komatsu excavator equipped with 8 inch diameter compressed air
activated drill bit
Existing rock veneers were removed at each anchor location as shown in
Figure 2 below The soil nail anchor drilled holes extended to depths varying from 15 to
25 feet from the design finished grade behind the structural shotcrete layer with an
inclination of about 15 degree from horizontal

Double corrosion protection was specified in the project specification using
pre grouted steel solid bar fully encapsulated in corrugated HDPE pipe Grout was
placed in the borehole progressively from the bottom to the top through a ¾ inch PVC
tremie pipe until fresh grout appeared at top of the borehole
It should be noted that neat cement grout with a water cement ratio of about 0 52
was introduced into the drilled holes throughout soil nail drilling Excessive neat cement
grout was pumped into the borehole through the drilling rod to reduce the potential for
cave in Design neat cement grout with a maximum water cement ratio of 0 45 was
placed into the borehole after drilling completion Black pigment was added into the
design grout such that the anchor grouting may be terminated when the black pigment
appeared at top of the borehole to distinguish the design grout replacing the drilling
grout left in the borehole
Individual soil nail anchor logs containing our observations and other pertinent
information recorded by our representative are provided in Appendix B and are
summarized on Plates 2 1 through 2 3
Upon completion of the soil nail anchor installation the entire rock veneer was
removed and a flash coating of shotcrete was placed on the face of the slope as shown
in Figure 3 below to protect the soil material between each anchor installed and to
provide a smooth surface for the verification proof testing
Figure 3 Completed Soil Nail Anchor with Flush Coat Layer
In accordance with the project specifications three verification tests and six proof
tests were conducted on selected soil nail anchors installed In general the soil nail
anchors were loaded to 150 percent of the design load and held for 60 minutes

The verification test results of the selected soil nail anchors are presented in
Appendix C and the proof test results are included in Appendix D The Load versus
Displacement and the Creep Movement versus Time curves for each test anchor are
also provided in Appendices C and D
In general the soil nail anchors subjected to verification and proof tests met the
acceptance criteria specified in the project specifications The criteria for acceptance of
the soil nail anchors consist of the following
For the verification tests a total creep movement of less than 0 08 inch
per log cycle of time between the 6 and 60 minute readings is measured
during creep testing and the creep rate is linear or decreasing throughout
the creep test load hold period
For the proof tests a total creep movement of less than 0 04 inch is
measured between the 1 and 10 minute readings or a total creep
movement of less than 0 08 inch is measured between the 6 and
60 minute readings and the creep rate is linear or decreasing throughout
the creep test load hold period

Total measured movement at the maximum test load exceeds 80 percent
of the theoretical elastic elongation of the test nail unbonded length
Pullout failure does not occur at the maximum test load
It should be noted that ten anchors were installed with the estimated grout
volume less than the theoretical volume of the drilled holes Additional evaluation and
testing was conducted on the selected soil nail anchors to verify that the pull out
capacity meets the design requirements
Based on the observations of our field representatives we believe that the
specified soil nail anchors observed by Geolabs representative have been installed in
general conformance with the project plans and specifications and within the limits of
generally accepted construction practices
Based on our observations of the soil nail anchor tests performed by the
contractor it is our professional opinion that the soil nail anchors have been installed to
provide the design load with tolerable movements
LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site therefore some of the work for the soil
nail anchor installation and testing may have occurred without our direct observation
However we believe that the level of geotechnical observation provided is reasonable in
reducing the geotechnical risks to the project

of any type The contractor shall retain responsibility for the quality of their work for
