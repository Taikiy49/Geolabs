SUMMARY REPORT
SOIL RESISTIVITY SURVEY
PROPOSED WATERLINE IMPROVEMENTS
NIUMALU ROAD
In accordance with your request a soil resistivity survey was conducted for the
proposed waterline in the Nawiliwili area on the island of Kauai Hawaii The
approximate location of the site is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The
work was performed in general accordance with our revised proposal dated August 1
1994 This report presents the results of our soil resistivity survey and summarizes our
work on the project
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The waterline improvements project is approximately 1 600 lineal feet and is
located along Niumalu Road starting from the intersection with Nawiliwili Road towards
the ocean as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 It is proposed to construct a 12 inch
diameter waterline along Niumalu Road In general the average depth of the proposed
waterline is anticipated to be approximately 4 feet below the existing ground surface
Soil resistivity surveys utilizing the in Pin Method were performed to
evaluate the corrosion rating of the near surface soils within the depths of the proposed
waterline

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our survey was to obtain an overview of the electrical resistivity
of the subsurface soils at the site Our work on this project was performed in general
conformance with ASTM Test Designation G 57 78 The scope of our work consisted
of the following tasks and work efforts
1 Mobilization Demobilization of equipment and engineers geologists to the
project site
2 Performance of 18 in situ soil resistivity tests at six locations along the
proposed waterline alignment on Niumalu Road The test locations
generally correspond to a maximum spacing of 300 feet
3 Reduction and analyses of the data with respect to the corrosion
characteristics of the soils at the site
4 Preparation of six copies of this brief letter report summarizing our work
on the project and presenting our findings
5 Quality assurance of our work and client design team consultation by a
Principal Engineer of our firm
6 Miscellaneous work efforts such as drafting word processing and clerical
support
FIELD SURVEY
Geophysical survey equipment i e resistance meter was mobilized to the site
on March 14th and our field work was performed in sunny conditions with generally dry
surface conditions
Eighteen soil resistivity tests were conducted at six locations at the above
referenced road using the Wenner 4 Pin Method and a resistance meter Strata Scout
R 40C manufactured by SoilTest Inc The resistance meter is capable of transmitting
420 volts and 24 watts output into the ground This method utilizes four surface probes
or pins electrodes to evaluate the average resistivity an indication of corrosivity of
the underlying soil mass The average resistivities of the soil mass extending from the
existing ground surface to the planned invert elevations of the waterline were assessed

The average soil resistance was evaluated at each test location with the
electrodes aligned at equal spacings of generally 3 to 5 feet at the existing ground
surface Among the spacings used one may have more significance than the others
for allowing more contribution to the evaluated soil resistivity from the closest mass of
soil to the proposed pipe invert Such spacings have the same distance as the depth
from the existing ground surface at the test locations to the proposed pipe invert Such
spacings are flagged with an asterisk on Plate 3
The resistance meter R 40C impresses a voltage between the outer electrodes
causing an electrical current to flow between them The voltage drop between the inner
electrodes is then measured by a sensitive built in voltmeter As a result the soil
resistance in Ohms can be found directly on the dial The soil resistivity in Ohm cm
which is directly related to both resistance and spacing can then be computed The
evaluated soil resistances the computed resistivities and the corresponding corrosion
ratings are presented on Plate 3
Resistivity is generally recognized as one of the most significant soil
characteristics with regard to the corrosivity of the soil to buried metallic objects In
general the lower the resistivity the greater the potential for corrosion of the buried
metallic structure Conversely the higher the resistivity the less likely the soil will
contribute to the corrosion of metallic objects
LIMITATIONS
The evaluations presented in this report are based upon information obtained
from field data points by means of geophysical surveys Variations of soil conditions
between the field data points may occur and the nature and extent of such variations
may not become evident until a later point in time If variations then appear evident
it will be necessary to reevaluate the ratings provided in this report
Locations and elevations of the data presented in this report are approximate
having been estimated from features and reference points shown on the topographic
survey map prepared by Towill Shigeoka Associates Inc dated
Thus elevations and locations of the data presented should be considered accurate
only to the degree implied by the methods used
