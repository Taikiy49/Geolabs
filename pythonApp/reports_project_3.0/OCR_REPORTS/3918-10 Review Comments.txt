Review Comments of Revised Dewatering Plan
UV Disinfection Facility
Kailua Regional Wastewater Treatment Plan
Kailua Oahu Hawaii
In response to your facsimile transmittal dated April 16 we have reviewed the
revised dewatering plan proposed for the above referenced project The following comments
and recommendations are provided for your consideration
1 Groundwater levels as high as 1 5 feet MSL were encountered in the drilled
borings at the time of our field exploration Therefore we believe that the top
of the steel plates may need to be set at an elevation higher than the
1 0 feet MSL indicated in the dewatering plan to reduce the potential for
significant seepage water from the sides of the excavation during the higher
tides
2 Based on the information provided we understand that the bottom of the
planned excavation will be at about 2 28 feet MSL Since the dewatering
plan indicates that the bottom of the steel plates will be set at an elevation of
about 5 feet MSL it appears that this would result in embedment depths on
the order of about 2 to 3 feet only In addition our field exploration indicated
that the steel plates will probably be embedded in the soft silty clays and loose
clayey sands at these depths Therefore we believe that deeper embedment
of the steel plates may be required in order to provide the necessary lateral
restraint to reduce the potential for adjacent soil movements A mud slab is
strongly recommended at the bottom of the planned excavation to aid in
restraining the steel plates from lateral movements

3 Based on the information provided we understand that an initial pumping rate
of 50 gallons per minute gpm will be used for the dewatering operation It
should be noted that this dewatering rate assumes that the excavation will not
be hydraulically connected to the numerous underground utility lines with
granular bedding at the site This is a critical consideration because the
dewatering operations should not cause areal groundwater drawdown which
would cause some settlement of the adjacent structures If a higher pumping
rate is needed during the actual dewatering operations additional precautions
should be taken to avoid significant groundwater drawdown outside the
excavation
4 Surface monuments should be established and an optical survey should be
taken on a weekly basis prior to during and after the dewatering operations
to evaluate the effects of the dewatering operations on ground surface
settlements
