INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of a soil engineering
investigation for the proposed residential development Our
work was performed according to our proposal dated
Preliminary grading plans for the project by William Hee and
Associates were used as a reference for our work
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations for
the proposed development
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
A Preliminary Soil and Geologic Reconnaissance report dated
August 24 1979 was prepared by our office for the proposed
development site This initial report was based on visual
observations at the project site The reconnaissance disclosed
soil conditions which would require further investigation and
analysis for site grading design
The purpose of this investigation was to obtain more detailed
subsurface information at the site to develop soil parameters
for the design and construction of the site grading for the
proposed development

May 1980
The scope of work included 1 boring 11 test pits disturbed and
undisturbed sampling and site reconnaissance Laboratory testing
was performed to classify and identify the characteristics of the
subsoils The result of our investigation together with our
conclusions and recommendations are summarized in this report

It as presently planned to subdivide the 54 acre site into
19 single family lots on the subject parcel The preliminary

of the site with an access roadway extending from Hoana Place
through the center of the site
Substantial cut is planned in the upper eastern lots while fill
is planned for portions of the access road and lower western lots
Retaining walls are planned along the mauka side of the roadway
Concrete interceptor ditches are planned along the upper and lower

Stabilization of Lower Western Portion
The field exploration encountered 5 to 9 feet of highly plastic
adobe clay soils in the lower half of the project site These
adobe clay deposits have high downhill creep tendency as well
as high shrink swell potential Slickensided zones were noted in
some of the test pits indicating possible shear creep movement of
the deposit in the past
The adobe clay soils are very weak soils which are significantly
affected by increase in mositure content and are subject to slope
creep or landslide movement during extended wet periods

effective cohesion which can be developed by the soil particles

Boulder Protection
The upper hillside lots may be subject to boulders rolling
down the hillside
The falling boulders are a general phenomena occurring in all
ridge slopes in Oahu Various methods of boulder barriers or
catchments can be used to reduce the hazard of falling boulders
The degree of safety of preventing falling boulder hazards is
a function of economics For this site various methods of
boulder prevention can be utilized such as
1 A boulder catchment area about 10 to 15 feet
wide with a fence or wall could be utilized
near the property boundary to catch rolling
boulders The concrete interceptor ditch
along the upper boundaries of the site could
be used im this capacity
W P 20
May 1980
2 Boulder sweeps along the upper boundaries of the
site could be performed to remove the loose
boulders that appear likely to roll down
3 The natural trees and vegetation along the upper
portions of the site should be retained to act
as a natural barrier against the boulders and
to reduce erosion
4 Where practicable the houses should be set back
away from the toe of the hillside slope to create
an additional boulder catchment area
It must be pointed out that whatever methods are used an
inherent risk of falling boulders could still exist for these
hillside lots
The state of the art of soil engineering practice has not
advanced to a point that a solution is available for this
area he1S our opinion that no matter what one does
there is always the risk cf boulders rolling down the hill
if not from areas near the house it will come from areas
high up on the slope Whatever measure is chosen it only
reduces the risk It should be pointed out that no guarantee
against future falling boulders can be made due to changes
in Soil and rock conditions with time weathering erosion
earthquake and construction vibration and improvements and
construction activities at and around the site
