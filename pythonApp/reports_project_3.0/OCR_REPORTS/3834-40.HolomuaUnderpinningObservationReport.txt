PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project is at Avenue in Honolulu on the Island of Oahu
Hawaii as shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The parcel is bounded by
Kalakaua Avenue on the west an existing 2 story commercial building on the east side
the Mauka tower of the Kulana Hale project on the north side and a convenience
store gasoline station on the south side Based on information provided it is proposed to
develop a 23 story high residential building containing approximately 115 000 to
200 000 square feet of floor area supported by a mat foundation
Excavation on the order of up to about 10 feet below the existing ground surface is
required for the construction of the mat foundation for the building structure Due to the
proximity of the existing 2 story structure and the relatively deep excavation anticipated
underpinning of the existing foundations using Atlas Resistance Piers was planned to
provide support for the 2 story commercial building along the Diamond Head side of the
proposed development
Atlas Resistance Piers develop their capacity as a result of a pile tip or end bearing
reaction in soil or rock that is achieved by hydraulically driving hollow pier sections to
suitable strata utilizing the reaction weight of an existing structure The general contractor
for the Holomua residential project is Nordic PCL Construction Inc The subcontractor
performing the underpinning work is Structural Systems Inc

RESISTANCE PIER INSTALLATION
Based on the information provided we understand that a total of seven Atlas
Resistance Piers designated as Pier Nos 1 through 7 will be installed at the locations
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The pier installation work consisted of excavating an
access trench approximately 26 feet long 3 5 feet wide and 2 5 to 4 feet deep to expose
the foundations of the existing 2 story commercial building adjacent to the Holomua
residential building project Upon completion of the excavation the pre fabricated pier
head assemblies were attached to the existing foundation slab using stainless steel bolts
The pier head assemblies brackets were checked and adjusted for horizontal and vertical
alignments before they were bonded with the foundation slab by applying a high strength
and quick setting grout into the spaces between the footing and the brackets
First Stage Pier Installation Loading
In general each Atlas pier consisted of a series of 3 5 feet long 0 165 inch thick
galvanized iron pipes welded with inner tube connection coupling at one end to prevent
lateral displacement with an outside diameter OD of 3 5 inches The upper 10 feet of
the Atlas pier is sleeved with 4 5 inch OD pipe sections with the jointing in the pier and
sleeved sections in staggered pattern to provide additional buckling resistance
Utilizing the existing building load as counterweight the Atlas piers were installed
with a hydraulic jack that was secured by pins into the bracket and temporarily bolted into
the face of the footing The lead starter section of the pier is fitted with a friction reduction
collar to reduce skin friction on the pier pipes during installation A Geolabs representative
recorded the driving time for each 3 5 foot long pier section as well as the hydraulic
pressure at the end of driving to obtain the indicative resistance force generated at the
bottom of the pier at that particular depth
Pier driving was terminated when the hydraulic pressure needed to achieve the
rated maximum working capacity of the pier 45 5 kips was attained and no additional pier
penetration was observed for several minutes while maintaining the hydraulic pressure
The hydraulic pressures and the apparent bearing resistances generated at each pier are
summarized in the Atlas Resistance Pier Installation Logs Plates 3 1 through 3 7
Second Stage Lift Loading
After the installation of the resistance piers the pier head assemblies were
mechanically connected and mounted into the piers by means of the top pier platform and
pier pins Each pier and bracket assembly were fitted with 25 kip capacity hydraulic jacks
connected to a manifold and hydraulic pump The lifting operation was performed by
simultaneously lifting the brackets that were attached to the footings deriving the reaction
force from the installed piers until lift or load transfer from the building to the piers is
achieved The vertical displacement was monitored using a level instrument Upon transfer
of loads to the entire pier assembly the lift pressures and vertical displacement at each
pier were recorded on the installation logs as shown on Plates 3 1 through 3 7 At the end
