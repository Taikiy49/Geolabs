INTRODUCTION
In accordance with the request of Kuilima Development Corporation we
have observed the construction and testing of three 3 standby injection
wells for the above referenced project The approximate location of the
project site and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate
1 Our work was performed in general accordance with our proposal to
Kuilima Development Corporation dated
This report summarizes our findings for the installation of injection
wells at the project site
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
Turtle Bay Resort is a planned resort community consisting of resort
hotels residential developments and recreational facilities such as golf
courses Sanitary domestic wastes will be generated by the resort
residential and recreational development The estimated design flows for
the resort complex are about gallons per day These wastes will
be treated to secondary standards at the wastewater treatment plant which
is currently under construction The primary disposal method of the
treated effluent is to be by irrigation of the golf courses The
injection wells are for standby use during periods when the effluent can
not be utilized on the golf courses

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our monitoring of the construction and testing of the
injection wells was to provide engineering and gechydrologic data for the
preparation of an engineering report of the installation of the injection
wells for the disposal of treated effluent from the proposed wastewater
treatment plant in accordance with the requirements of final application
procedures for an Underground Injection Control UIC Permit as required
by Chapter 23 of Title 11 Department of Health Administrative Rules
Underground Injection Control
The scope of our work on the project included but was not limited
to
1 The monitoring of the drilling and construction of three
injection wells to depths of approximately 44 feet below the
existing ground surface at the approximate locations shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2
2 The monitoring of long term 12 hour duration permeability tests
in Well Nos 1 and 3
3 Calculation of the average subsurface permeability
4 Report preparation
Detailed descriptions of our field work are presented in the
appendices of this report

the location of the wells on the site is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2

PHYSTCAL CHARACTERISTICS
The injection well site is located in the Kahuku area in the
Koolauloa District on the north shore of the island of Oahu Hawaii The
climate of the Kahuku area is mild with an average annual temperature of
about 78 F and an average annual rainfall of about 40 inches per year
The area is frequently subject to strong tradewinds from the northeast
The site is situated on the Kahuku coastal plain at the northern end
of the Koolau Range The topography of this coastal plain is a very
gentle slope down towards the north from the mountains to the ocean
Regional Geology
The island of Oahu was built by two basaltic shield volcanoes
Waianae and Koolau Waianae is the older of the two and reached the end
of its activity before Koolau
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2425 00 Page 4
As volcanic activity on the island of Oahu drew to a close the
island began to subside slowly While the island was sinking fringing
coral falgal reefs began to grow on the seaward margins of the island
During the Pleistocene or Ice Age global sea levels rose and fell
in response to the great continental glaciers As the glaciers grew the
global sea level fell below the existing level and as the glaciers melted
and retreated the sea level rose to heights above the current stand of
the sea
Locally the fringing coral algal reefs continued to grow during the
high stands of the sea leaving fossil reefs which are now exposed above
sea level At the project site and in its vicinity there are exposures
of such fossil reefs which most likely grew during the Kaena 95 stand
of the sea Interbedded with these coral algal materials are alluvial
sediments which were transported from the nearby Koolau Range
During historic time the project site was used for agricultural
purposes This use most likely resulted in some minor modification of the
terrain such as tilling and grading of the fields
Foundation Conditions
In general our previous exploratory borings encountered a thin
surface layer of fill material overlying competent coral algal reef
materials The coral materials generally have good strength
characteristics and are capable of supporting large foundation loads
Since the injection well site will eventually be golf course area
foundation considerations are not considered to be of significance for the
wells themselves

Earthquake Conditions
The island of Oahu is designated under the Uniform Building Code as
Seismic Zone One Since there will not be any structures associated with
the wells at the well site seismic conditions are not considered to be
significant
Flood Problems
As previously stated the well field site is situated in a low lying
area which has been known to flood slightly during sustained periods of
heavy rainfall However it is proposed to raise the grade of the site
during the golf course grading and the site is far enough from the ocean
that flooding and tsunami inundation are not considered to be of
Significance to the operation of the standby wells
Land Use
The well field site is in the Turtle Bay Resort development area
which is designated for resort use Construction of standby injection
wells within the golf course area is consistent with this use
SYSTEM OPERATION
It is proposed to use the wells for the standby disposal of treated
domestic sewage Prior to disposal the sewage will be treated at the
Turtle Bay Resort Wastewater Treatment Plant which is currently under
construction The plant design calls for a minimum of secondary level
treatment with disinfection
Under normal conditions the effluent generated at the plant will be
disposed of through irrigation of the golf courses within the Turtle Bay
Resort development area When disposal by irrigation is not feasible the
effluent will be temporarily diverted to the injection wells
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2425 00 Page 6
The wastewater treatment plant will have a design capacity of 1 32
MGD with a maximum flow of about Since the injection wells are
for standby disposal the normal injection rate is zero and the annual
volume of injectate will vary
The injection system will consist of three 3 wells and will be a
closed system ie the wellheads will be capped with blind flanged tee
connections leading to valves and a closed piping system from the
treatment plant Injection will be under gravity pressure i e the head
pressure within the closed piping system Based on the elevation
differences between the wells and the piping system it is possible to
build up about ten 10 feet of head in the effluent piping system
Details of the system are shown on Plate 4
Since the wells are a standby and backup disposal system there is no
additional standby system nor are monitoring wells planned
GEOHYDROLOGIC CONSIDERATIONS
The well site is located at Latitude N and Longitude
W The ground surface elevation at the site at the time
of well drilling and testing was approximately five 5 feet above Mean
Sea Level When the golf course site grading and the injection system is
completed the finished ground surface will be approximately seven 7
feet above Mean Sea Level
The logs of the drilled injection wells are presented in Appendix A
Generally the wells encountered surface soils consisting of coralline
sands and gravels overlying organic silts Below the organic silts the
wells penetrated in situ coral algal limestone formation to their
completion depths of about 44 feet below the existing ground surface
Water levels in the drilled wells were noted to fluctuate only
slightly in response to the tides in the nearby ocean Therefore tidal
fluctuations and efficiency are considered to be minimal

The groundwater in the Turtle Bay Resort area is brackish to saline
water in a caprock coral algal limestone aquifer This caprock aquifer
is isolated from the underlying artesian basalt aquifer by clayey soils
and sediments with low permeability
Permeability Testing
Preliminary tests were previously performed during the drilling of
test borings When the test borings had reached their intended depths
the borings were developed and tested by the injection of clear fresh
water These tests indicated that the coral algal formation has a
coefficient of permeability k on the order of 1 foot per minute
The subsurface permeability conditions at the site were tested by the
injection of formation water from one of the drilled wells to one of the
others e g a line shaft turbine pump was temporarily installed in Well
1 to supply water for the testing of Well 3
During the injection of water the changes in water level in the
wells were recorded with respect to time and rate of injection
The results of these tests are presented in Appendix B
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as drilled
wells and pump tests Variations of conditions between the field data
points may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not
become evident until operation of the injection wells If variations then
appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the findings presented
in this report
The location and elevation of the field data points were determined
by others We can not assume responsibility for the accuracy of the
location and or elevation of the actual wells
