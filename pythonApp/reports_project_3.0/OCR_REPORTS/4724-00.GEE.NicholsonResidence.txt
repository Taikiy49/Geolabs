SECTION 1 0 GENERAL
1 1 Introduction
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the Nicholson residence at 3787 Diamond Head Road 39 38
in the Kahala area of Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and
vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings and recommendations resulting from our
field exploration laboratory testing and engineering analyses These
recommendations are intended for the design of foundation site grading and
pavements only The findings and recommendations presented herein are subject to
the limitations noted at the end of this report
1 2 Project Considerations
The project site is located at 3787 Diamond Head Road in the Kahala area of
Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The subject lot has an existing one story
house
It is proposed to demolish the existing one story structure and construct a new
two story residence on the subject lot Preliminary plans for the new house were not
available however we anticipate that the proposed new residence will be a two story
slab on grade structure Based on the sloping terrain we envision site grading for the
proposed construction that will involve cuts and fills up to about 5 feet
1 3 Purpose and Scope
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions at the project site to develop an idealized soil rock data set for the
formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations pertaining to the design of
foundations site grading and pavements for the proposed new residence The work
was performed in general accordance with our fee proposal dated

2 1 Regional Geology
The Island of Oahu is composed largely of the weathered remnants of two extinct
shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae forms the bulk of
the western third of the island while the younger shield Koolau forms the majority of
the eastern two thirds of the island It is believed that Waianae became extinct while
Koolau was still active and its eastern flank is partially buried below Koolau lavas in
central Oahu
Volcanic activity from Koolau Volcano ceased for nearly 2 million years Volcanic
activity once again resumed from mid Pleistocene Epoch and has lasted through
Recent geologic time These volcanic events known as the Honolulu Volcanic Series
have formed cinder spatter tuff and ash cones throughout the southern portion of the
Island of Oahu
One of the most prominent vents of the Honolulu Series is Diamond Head
Diamond Head is a tuff cone and was generally formed by hydromagmatic explosions
Materials within the tuff cone consist of magmatic debris blocks of Koolau basalt and
fragments of coral limestone torn from the underlying reef
During the Pleistocene Epoch Ice Age sea levels fluctuated in response to the
cycles of continental glaciation As the glaciers grew and advanced less water was
available to fill the oceanic basins such that sea levels fell below the present stands of
the sea When the glaciers melted and receded an excess of water became available
such that the sea levels rose to above the present sea level
The higher sea level stands caused the formation of deltas and fans of
accumulated terrigenous sediments in the heads of old bays accumulated reef deposits
at correspondingly higher elevations and lagoonal marine sediments in the quiet waters
protected by fringing reefs The lower sea stands caused streams to carve valleys in

SECTION 3 0 DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field exploration the project site is underlain by dense to very
dense fill consisting of silts to silty clays with calcareous sands over very dense
weathered volcanic tuff to approximately 10 to 12 feet below the existing ground
surface Well cemented coralline sand dense to very dense was encountered from
10 to 12 feet below the existing ground surface and extended to the maximum depths
drilled of about 15 to 17 feet below the existing ground surface Based on our field
exploration and laboratory testing we believe that a shallow foundation system
consisting of spread and or continuous footings bearing on the in situ soils or
compacted engineered fills may be used to support the proposed new residence
Detailed discussion and recommendations for design of foundation site grading and
other geotechnical aspects of the project are presented in the following sections of this
report
Based on our laboratory tests the near surface clayey soils exhibit high shrink swell
potential when subjected to fluctuations in the soil moisture contents To reduce the
potential for distress to the future lightly loaded slabs on grade resulting from the swelling
of the near surface soils we recommend that the on site clayey soils either be
over excavated to 3 feet below the slab cushion level and replaced with non expansive
select granular fill capping fill material In general the subgrade soils should be scarified
moisture conditioned and recompacted to provide a relatively firm and smooth bearing
surface prior to the placement of fill material The slab subgrade should be kept moist and
free of shrinkage cracks prior to placement of capping fill cushion fill and or slab concrete
Our geotechnical engineering recommendations pertaining to the design of foundations
site preparation and pavements are further discussed in the following sections of this
report

3 2 Slabs On Grade
We anticipate that concrete slabs on grade will be used for the proposed new
residence Based on the preliminary grading plan provided we anticipate that low
cuis fills may be required to attain the design finish grade for the proposed structure
Prior to the filling operation the subgrade to receive fill should be scarified to a
minimum depth of 8 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture
content and compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction In general the fill
material needed to raise the grades to the finish subgrade may consist of the on site
clayey soils and or imported non expansive granular material The fill material should
be placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness moisture conditioned
to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to not less than 90 percent
relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil
expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil determined in
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Optimum moisture is the water
content percentage by weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
For more uniform support of interior slabs we recommend that the concrete
slab on grade be supported on a minimum 4 inch thick layer of cushion fill The cushion
fill layer should consist of open graded gravels such as No 3 Fine gravel ASTM C 33
No 67 gradation or basalt termite barrier BTB The slab cushion fill should be placed
as soon as possible after the scarification and recompaction of the existing subgrade
soils as recommended above For slabs on grade with moisture sensitive surfacing we

recommend that an impervious moisture barrier be placed over the cushion fill to reduce
potential migration of moisture through the concrete slab In general we recommend
that a minimum 4 inch thick layer of cushion fill be placed below the slabs to provide the
slabs with more uniform support The cushion fill should consist of open graded gravel
such as No 3 Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation which would also serve as a
capillary break To reduce the potential for future moisture infiltration and subsequent

Where concrete slabs will be subjected to vehicular traffic such as the garage

fill and 2 inch sand layers The base course should consist of crushed basalt
aggregate The subgrade should also be scarified to a minimum depth of 8 inches

If exterior concrete walkways are planned for the proposed project we

compaction Control joints should be provided at intervals equal to the width of the
walkways and at right angle intersections

3 3 Site Grading
Based on the site conditions we understand that site grading will involve low cuts
and fills Site preparation should be observed by a representative from Geolabs Inc It
is important that a representative from our office observe whether the exposed soil
conditions during excavation are similar to those encountered in our exploration The
following recommendations are intended to provide guidelines for the design of site
preparation work
At on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed Vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable
materials generated from demolition should be removed and disposed of off site After
clearing and grubbing the exposed subgrade should be scarified to a minimum depth of
8 inches properly moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction For subgrades of
improvements subjected to vehicular traffic such as garage slab and pavements the
compaction requirement should be increased to a minimum of 95 percent relative
compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as
a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil determined in accordance
with ASTM Test Designation D1557 Optimum moisture is the water content
percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
Soft weak or yielding areas disclosed during clearing operations should be
over excavated to expose firm ground and the resulting excavation should be backfilled
with engineered fill compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Prior to
placement of the backfill the over excavated subgrade should be scarified to a depth of
at least 8 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction The excavated soft and or
organic soils should be properly disposed of off site

3 5 Utility Trench
We envision that new utility connections may be needed for the proposed project
In general a granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33
No 67 gradation is recommended under the pipes Free draining granular materials
such as No 3B Fine gravel should also be used for the trench backfill up to about
12 inches above the pipes to provide adequate support around the pipes and to reduce
the compaction effort of the backfill thus reducing the possibility of damaging the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipes
to the top of the subgrade or finished grade should consist of on site sandy soils or
imported granular material The backfill should be placed in about 8 inch loose lifts and
mechanically compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the
potential for future ground subsidence Where trenches are below pavement areas the
upper 2 feet of the trench backfill below the pavement subgrade should be compacted
to not less than 95 percent relative compaction
3 6 Drainage
The finished grades outside the residence should be sloped to shed water away
from foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also advised that gutter
systems be installed around the structure and that the discharge be diverted away from
the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near the
foundations and slabs should also be avoided Planters next to foundations should be
avoided or have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the
subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil
saturation and subsequent loss of strength of the near surface soils The foundation
excavations should be properly backfilled against the walls or slab footings immediately
after setting of the concrete to reduce water infiltration Drainage swales should be

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling
three borings to depths of approximately 15 to 17 feet below the existing ground surface
at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were drilled
using a portable drill rig equipped with solid augers
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer geologist who monitored the drilling operations
on a near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by
testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified
Soil Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representation of the materials
encountered is presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 3
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a
3 inch OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches In
addition some samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance
with ASTM Test Designation D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of
Soils by driving a 2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same hammer and
drop The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an
18 inch drive are shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Boring at the
appropriate sample depths
