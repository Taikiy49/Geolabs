SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The site is located on the western flank of West Maui Volcano which
forms the western side of Maui
Fast Maui or Haleakala Volcano which is much larger was formed
later and banked against the West Maui mountains A flat isthmus connects
the two volcanoes
The very permeable and primitive Wailuku volcanic series of West Maui
were formed during the Pliocene and early Pleistocene time from the
extrusion of basaltic lavas along two main rifts and many fissures
The areas is underlain by residual soils derived from the in situ
weathering of the Wailuku lavas The residual soils grade with depth to
saprolite which is a term commonly applied to this zone of soil like
material which retains the relict rock structure

In general the soil conditions encountered throughout the project
site appear relatively uniform Most of the drilled borings encountered
stiff brown silty clay and clay varying in thickness between 1 5 to 5 5
feet These are residual soils that derived from the in situ weathering
of Wailuku Basalt and are generally structureless
Underlying the residual soils the borings encountered 5 5 to 7 0
feet of hard brown and gray silty sand and gravel These sands are
saprolite soils which are soils derived from the in situ chemical
weathering of rock and retain some or all of the original structures of
the parent rock Below the saprolite the borings encountered extremely
decomposed basalt to the maximum depth drilled
Although this extremely weathered horizon is correctly described as a
rock for the purpose of this report and for design of the proposed
project including constructability it is considered to be a soil e g
gray to reddish brown silty sand Boulders were encountered in two 2 of
the seven 7 borings so it is anticipated that some boulders will be
encountered during the site grading for the proposed roadway
No groundwater was encountered in the drilled borings during the time
of our exploration

Site Grading
Site Preparation
Prior to the commencement of grading the site should be thoroughly
cleared of vegetation boulders rubbish and other deleterious
materials This grubbed material should be disposed of properly
off site
In areas designated to receive fill the ground should then be
scarified to a minimum depth of 6 inches brought to optimum moisture
content and then compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry
density as established by ASTM D 1557 78
Filling and Compaction Requirements
After the above site preparation the fill should be moistened to
optimum moisture content placed in maximum 9 inch loose horizontal
lifts and compacted to obtain a minimum 90 of its maximum dry
density as established by ASTM D 1557 78
Laboratory soil tests indicate that the on site soils have low
swelling potential therefore the material may be utilized for the
fill
Where it is necessary to place fill over existing slopes steeper than
5 horizontal to 1 vertical 5 1 keying and benching operation is
critical and required to properly bond the new fill to the existing
slope surface The filling operation should start at the lowest
point and continue up in level compacted layers in accordance with
the above fill requirements see Plate 3 for details

Due to the limited deep fill area at this project site boulder
disposal in the new fills is not recommended The existing boulder
stockpiles and boulders encountered during site grading may have to
be disposed of off site if an on site disposal area is not available
Cut and fill slopes of 2 1 H V are recommended
Fill slopes should be constructed by overfilling then cutting back
to the design slope to obtain a well compacted slope face
Slopes greater than 20 feet in height should have benches at their
mid points or at about 20 feet intervals
Surface water should be diverted away from the tops of the slopes and
slope planting utilized as soon as possible to minimize erosion
Utility Trenches
Proper backfilling of the excavated trenches is important to minimize
ground settlement along the utility alignments On site material
generally less than 6 inches in size may be utilized as backfill All
backfill should be compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry
density as determined by ASTM D 1557 78 Most of the on site material is
fine grained and would not be suitable for compaction by water jetting or
ponding alone and therefore would require mechanical compaction

House Foundations
The following general foundation guidelines are presented for
preliminary foundation design evaluation purposes We recommend that
borings or probings should be performed by a geotechnical engineer prior
to house construction to verify the soil conditions at each lot
For the light residential spread footing foundations or
slabs on grade may be used To minimize the effects of slope creep and
erosion generally slabs should be set back a minimum distance of 6 feet
away from the tops of cut or fill slopes Also post footings located
close to the tops of slopes should be embedded a sufficient depth to
obtain a minimum horizontal set back distance of 6 feet away from the
slope face Post footings located on level ground surface away from the
slope should be embedded a minimum depth of twelve 12 inches
GEOLABS HAWAII

For the light loads anticipated an allowable bearing pressure of
3500 p s f may be used for footings resting on very stiff in situ soil or
compacted fill
If a footing is located next to a utility trench it should extend to
the bottom of the utility trench or the trench backfill should be
well compacted to reduce the possibility of footing settlement due to
settlement of the backfill
Slabs on grade should be placed over a 4 inch layer of compacted
granular cushion and capillary break such as 3 Fine rock The thickened
edge of the slabs should extend a minimum of 10 inches below the adjacent
ground surface
To protect against moisture damage of the flooring material a
waterproof membrane should be incorporated beneath the slab A one inch
layer of well graded sand should be placed over the membrane to protect it
and to aid in the curing of the concrete
The slab subgrade soils should be kept moist and not allowed to dry
out prior to concrete placement
The lots should be graded to drain water away from the house
foundations

Construction Observation
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during roadway grading
construction backfill of utility excavations and foundation phases of the
work This is to observe compliance with the design concepts
specifications or recommendations and to allow design changes in the event
that subsurface conditions differ from that anticipated prior to start of
construction The recommendations given in this report are contingent
upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made

in part upon information obtained from field data points such as
borings Variations of conditions between the field data points may

necessary to re evaluate the recommendations given in this report

from the untitled undated plans and profiles by Austin Tsutsumi

considered accurate only to the degree implied by the method used
