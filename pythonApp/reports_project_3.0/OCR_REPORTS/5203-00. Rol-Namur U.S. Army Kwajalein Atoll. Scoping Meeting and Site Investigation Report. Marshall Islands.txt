J USAKA just completed a draft environmental assessment report date 23 May 2005
A copy was brought to the meeting The report addresses 4 possible pier
replacement altematives USAKA wants to stock pile the dredged sand material for
future use Discussions regarding the disposal of dredging operation are as follows
1
One of the dredged material disposal option addressed in the EA is to pump
the sand to the beach west of the pier to replace sand lost due to shoreline
erosion This option will not allow USAKA to reuse the dredged sand
material
The other option addressed in the EA is to use a small hydraulic dredge to
pump the sand to a two cell upland disposal area south of the existing fuel
farm The water in the hydraulic dredged material would be allowed to both
percolate into the soil and retum to the lagoon with a silt screen filter
Following dewatering the sand material would be stockpiled for future use
and the site would be restored to its pre project condition
Kenneth Sims pointed out that there was a fuel spill at the proposed disposal
area next to the fuel tanks and the ground is known to be contaminated by
fuel There is a monitoring well approximately 2 5 feet in diameter at the site
free fuel product could be seen on the surface of the ground water There
had been some pumping operations at the monitoring well in the past
however study has not been made to determine the extent of the fuel
contamination The fuel product has not been observed in the lagoon
Paul Lewis expressed concern that if the disposal site were used and water is
allowed to percolate into the soil the fuel contaminant could leak out to the
lagoon When that happens the pier construction project could be stopped
