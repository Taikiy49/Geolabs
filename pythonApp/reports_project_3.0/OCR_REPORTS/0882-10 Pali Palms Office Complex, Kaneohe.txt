Gentlemen
This report presents a summary of our inspection during produc
tion and test pile installation for the subject project
Production pile driving was completed on
The pile driving operations were inspected by our senior field
technician on a full time basis
Ewcept for theld2 x l2 iich test piles all of the instalied
production piles were 16 inch octagonal precast and pre
stressed concrete having a cast length ranging from 11 0 to
24 0 feet Piles were generally driven to the upper coral
bearing stratum However some piles did penetrate as deep
as 111 5 feet below the existing ground surface deeper due
to the uncemented sand pockets known to be present over the
northeastern portion of the site The piles were driven with
link best 520 diesel hammer with a rated energy of 26 000
foot pounds
During the production pile installation a total of about 42
splice cans have been used for the piles which penetrated
deeper
The location of all the piles are shown on the Pile Numbering
System Plan Plate 1 and 2 A summary of the pile installation
is attached

Additional Piles
Piles No 21 and 212 Pile Groups D 3 and N 17 were in
Stalled at a batter position exceeding the specified tolerable
limit of four percent The project structural engineer was
advised of the above batter pile locations and two additional
replacement piles Piles No 21 A and 212 A were installed
in the pile groups
Additional Pile No 115 A in Pile Group P 4 was driven at the
request of the contractor to replace other mislocated piles
in this croup
Unused Pile Sections
At the completion of pile installation on
Our On Site pile inventory count indicated that a total of
26 pile sections consisting of thirteen 11 foot seven 41
foot and six 60 foot lengths remain unused at the job site
due to the following reasons
1 A total of about 20 cut off pile sections were prepared
by the contractor and reused at various pile locations
resulting in saving some piles
2 In some areas listed on pile summaries where deeper
pile penetration was originally anticipated the piles
actually reached the required bearing at a relatively
Shallower depth Therefore only one pile section was
used This resulted in saving the second pile section
and splice can Most of this pile saving was realized
in the revised 100 ton pile cluster area formerly
the so called 40 ton pile area
It appears that the pile driving contractor would have to
utilize some of the unused piles left over from this job for
another future project However some transportation and
storage costs might be involved
Based on the pile driving data and pile load tests we feel that
generally the piles have been driven to sufficient bearing for
the design load of 100 tons each
Should you have any questions regarding our inspection during
the pile installation please feel free to contact us
