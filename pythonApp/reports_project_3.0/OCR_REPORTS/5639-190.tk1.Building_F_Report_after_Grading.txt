Dear Mr. Stinson: 
This report summarizes our earthwork observation and testing services 
performed during the fine grading operations for the Building F project at Kapolei 
Commons from January 5 through October 1, 2018. 
EARTHWORK OPERATIONS 
The project site is within the Kapolei Commons development in the Kapolei area 
in the District of Ewa on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. Based on the information provided, 
we understand two new retail buildings will be constructed at the existing pads and 
driveway at the east-central area of the retail development.  
The new buildings will consist of single-story structures surrounded by concrete 
sidewalks. The retail buildings to the north and south of the project site will encompass 
approximately 35,232 and 13,327 square feet of floor space, respectively.  In addition, 
we understand that parking stalls and loading zones are planned as part of the project 
construction. 
Based on the existing topography and design building pad elevations of +70.75 
and +71.25 feet Mean Sea Level (MSL), we envision minimal site grading work will be

backfilling with select granular fill materials up to about 7 feet deep may be required at

Plate 1. 
J.J.S. Construction, Inc. performed the fine grading operations during the building 
construction for the project. The site work consisted of footing subgrade preparation, 
plumbing and utility trench backfill, and sidewalk and pavement construction. 
During the fine grading operations, samples of the on-site and imported materials 
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for 
testing. The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in 
our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D1557 (Modified Proctor), and the test 
results are summarized on Plate 2. 
Field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations on an 
as-needed basis to confirm our field observations as variations in construction 
procedures and soil conditions were noted. A total of 229 field density tests were 
performed using the nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM D6938. Results of 
the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 3.1 through 3.19. These 
tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and 
backfills at the site. Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and 
interpolating from on-site survey stakes. These locations and elevations should be 
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used. Areas assessed 
as unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained. 
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the 
referenced tests, it is our opinion that the earthwork operations during the fine grading 
work for the above project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this 
report were performed in general accordance with the intent of the project plans and 
specifications and our geotechnical engineering recommendations presented in the 
referenced report, dated July 12, 2017, and subsequent recommendations. 
LIMITATIONS 
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities 
occurring simultaneously on a construction site. Therefore, some of the earthwork may 
have been performed without our observation. However, the level of earthwork 
observation and testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some 
of the geotechnical risks to the project. It should be recognized that there are variations 
in the accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor 
earthwork construction and materials.
