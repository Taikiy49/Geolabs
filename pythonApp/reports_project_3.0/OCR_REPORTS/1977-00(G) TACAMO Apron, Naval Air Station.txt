In general accordance with the scope of services outlined in our
proposal we have observed the drilling and grouting of
one hundred seventy five 175 cavity probes The observations of the
Grilling and grouting of the cavity probes were conducted between February
22 and March 7 1989
An air track drill was utilized to drill 3 inch diameter probe holes
to depths ranging between 14 0 feet below the existing ground
surface A 2 inch diameter PVC pipe casing was lowered into each hole
immediately after drilling to prevent cave in prior to the pumping of the
grout The location of all probe holes are shown on the attached cavity
grouting plan
Following our approval of the grout mix design 3000 p s i fine
grout was pumped through a grout pipe placed at the bottom of each hole at
a pressure of approximately 20 p s i until the grout level matched the
surrounding ground surface The volume of grout discharged into each hole
was then recorded
Based on our drilling observations and volumes of grout pumped we
believe that the subsurface cavities encountered at the site have been
adequately filled
