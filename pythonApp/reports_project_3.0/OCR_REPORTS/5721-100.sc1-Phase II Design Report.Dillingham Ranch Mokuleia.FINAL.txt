FIELD EXPLORATION
The project site is along the northerly facing slope of the Waianae Range in the
Mokuleia area of the Island of Oahu Hawaii The site generally encompasses an open
range vegetated with scattered trees and heavy dry land brush and grasses A number of
existing ranch trails traverses the project site The subdivision development plan used in
our Phase II design efforts is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Our Phase II field exploration consisted of some additional site reconnaissance
performed in support of the DRA proposed in our Phase I study at Lot Nos 62 and 65
through 67 Furthermore we performed additional site reconnaissance and subsurface
exploration for the rockfall impact barrier protecting the dwelling sites at Lot Nos 69 and
70
Development Restriction Areas DRA at Lot Nos 62 and 65 through 67
We conducted a ground surface site reconnaissance to review the location of the
DRA and associated setback boundaries in the field at Lot Nos 62 and 65 through 67 The
slope inclinations and existing ground surface conditions were verified by our geologist
Based on our observations the existing boulder deposits encountered near the toe of the
source rock mountainside appear to represent both older colluvial fan deposits mixed with
more recent rockfall deposits In contrast the widely scattered and partly embedded
boulder deposits observed down slope from the proposed setback boundary appear to
represent mainly older remnant boulder components related to the erosion of the older
regional colluvial fan deposits at the mouths of the valleys
Based on our reconnaissance the DRA setback boundary limits at Lot Nos 62 and
65 through 67 that were identified by our computer based rockfall simulation analyses are
appropriate therefore changes to the DRA setback boundary locations are not
recommended The simulation analyses are illustrated on the Slope Profiles Plates 3 1
and 3 2 The location of the DRA setback boundaries for Lot Nos 62 and 65 through 67
are shown on the Rockfall Mitigation Plan 1 Plate 4 1
Rockfall Impact Barrier Fencing for Lot Nos 69 and 70

barrier location to assess the suitability of the terrain and constructability of the barrier
system The Lot No 69 house pad position and Lot property boundary were
adjusted to permit the barrier alignment and access and maintenance easement to reside
entirely within Lot No 70 The new Lot No 69 layout supports the necessary rockfall
protection at Lot Nos 69 and 70

Development Restriction Areas at Lot and 81
Based on our Phase II evaluation development restriction areas are recommended
at Lot 66 and 67 where potential rockfall encroachment is a concern
The lot configuration is appropriate for the designation of the development
restriction areas because the lots are oriented with the long dimension depth
following the slope fall line This facilitates the use of setbacks and utilizes natural
terrain and distance to buffer the potential effect of rockfall from the adjacent
mountain slopes without the need for installation of rockfall interception structures
In addition development restriction areas are recommended for all of Lot No 81
and a large portion of Lot No 70 outside of the established dwelling and rockfall
protection locations No development should be permitted in the development
restriction area unless additional rockfall hazard study is performed by a competent
geotechnical engineer to assess the potential hazard and design appropriate
rockfall hazard controls or protection measures The development restriction areas
should be finalized and included as part of the sales disclosure documents and
Subdivision Restrictive Covenants to be referenced by future lot owners
The development restriction areas are shown on the Rockfall Mitigation Plan 1
Plate 4 1 and were developed based on the CRSP analyses and substantiated by
field reconnaissance For Lot 66 and 67 dwelling site development
may occur below down slope from the development restriction boundaries shown
on Plate 4 1 It should be noted that Lot Nos 66 and 67 require greater setback
than adjacent Lot No 65 that shares a small common segment of the
recommended boundary No dwelling structure should be permitted for construction
above upslope from the development restriction boundary If a dwelling is desired
above the boundary additional rockfall hazard study must be performed by a
competent geotechnical engineer to design an appropriate rockfall interception and
containment structure for the desired dwelling site The development restriction
areas should be finalized and included as part of the sales disclosure documents
and Subdivision Restrictive Covenants to be referenced by future lot owners

Rockfall Impact Barrier Maintenance
Although rockfall impact barriers are designed for limited maintenance a program
of regular visual inspection and periodic debris removal is required Rockfall barriers
require a routine visual inspection to ensure optimal performance In addition periodic
maintenance to repair or replace damaged or worn components following large rock
impacts may be necessary Routine visual inspections should be performed periodically
and following a rockfall impact The visual inspection serves to identify potential rock
debris accumulations that require removal and to inspect for possible changes in the
system condition or effects of corrosion that may reduce the barrier performance
Barrier maintenance such as the repair or replacement of worn broken components
i e the brake elements and nets may be required following a large rockfall impact The
Dillingham Ranch Aina LLC

frequency of barrier maintenance typically depends on the size and frequency of rockfall
events The rockfall barriers are designed to require minimal routine maintenance
Due to the often difficult access condition of some barrier installations owners or
governing agencies typically establish a contract with a local rockfall specialty contractor to
perform routine inspections large volume rock debris removal and other system
maintenance when needed In some cases where the barrier is clearly visible from
readily accessible locations such as a roadway or the base of a low height slope the
owner may elect to perform the routine visual inspection themselves
Furthermore where barriers are located at readily accessible locations the owner
may perform the removal of rock debris using construction machinery However a rockfall
specialty contractor who maintains the proper equipment and trained personnel to conduct
work on difficult access slopes should be contracted to safely perform the removal of large
rockfall debris and perform barrier system repair The rockfall mitigation specialty
contractor typically utilizes portable equipment which is transported onto the slope to
perform the potentially hazardous work Furthermore the rockfall specialty contractor
would typically employ temporary safety controls to protect down slope persons and
property during the performance of the work
It is important to note the need for maintenance on rockfall impact barriers is
generally limited because the barriers are designed to withstand multiple small to medium
magnitude rock impacts without replacement Therefore it is not necessary to clean the
barrier if there is only a small accumulation of rocks in the net bottom If it is not feasible to
drive behind the barrier with equipment an option is to reach over the net with a backhoe
boom truck or small crane The option of using a small crane is a good option particularly
if the rockfall consists of just a few large rocks Eyebolts can be attached to the rocks and
the rocks can be easily lifted out from behind the barrier If this is also not feasible then
the barrier nets should be temporarily detached to provide access from the front of the
barrier
With respect to the rockfall impact barrier at the Dillingham Ranch Agricultural
Subdivision we recommend the following guidelines for inspection and maintenance be
provided to the Home Owners Association who will be charged with operation and
maintenance of the barrier protection system
1
The HOA should obtain a contractor to inspect the rockfall impact barrier
structure at least once per year for visible signs of rock impact debris
accumulation in the net or deterioration and corrosion of primary system
components such as the wire mesh steel posts and ground anchor wire
ropes If a significant volume of rock material consisting of large boulder s
multiple boulders or deep accumulations of rock and other debris is
observed a rockfall specialty contractor should be contracted to properly
remove the debris and restore system function In addition if components
are visibly broken loose and sagging or excessively corroded a rockfall
specialty contractor should be contracted to perform inspection and repairs

2
If the barrier sustains a large rockfall impact which causes visible
deformation to the barrier a rockfall specialty contractor should be

to restore function
3
At a minimum we recommend a more thorough visual inspection of the

and the climatic conditions affecting the corrosion rate before major
components of the system may require replacement to maintain the design
effectiveness of the barrier A contingency maintenance budget should be
developed to accommodate such work
