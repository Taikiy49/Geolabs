PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
A potential borrow site for fill material to be used for the Ilima at Leihano project
is between Kalaeloa Boulevard and Kalaeloa Harbor in Kapolei on the Island of Oahu
Hawaii The borrow site is a berm area between the two channel excavation areas and
is about 2 400 feet long by 120 feet wide in plan dimension We understand that the
finished grade of the berm area will be about the same elevation as the existing channel
excavation on the east side of the berm Based on the topographic survey plan the
bottom of the berm area will range from about Elevation 22 feet Mean Sea Level MSL
at the north end and about 10 feet MSL at the south end

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
KIWC explored the subsurface conditions at the project site by excavating 14 test
pits designated as Test Pit Nos 101 through 114 to depths of about 0 5 to 19 feet
below the existing ground surface Bulk samples were obtained from 9 of the test pits
for laboratory testing The approximate test pit locations are shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2
The field exploration generally encountered alluvial and coralline deposits
extending to the maximum depth explored of about 19 feet below the existing ground
surface The coralline deposits generally consisted of silty gravel silty sand clayey
gravel gravelly sand sandy gravel gravel and limestone and sandstone formation

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the topographic survey plan received from Engineering Concepts Inc on
the existing elevation at the berm area range from about
Elevation 49 feet Mean Sea Level MSL at the north end and about 23 feet MSL at the
south end We understand that the finished grade of the berm area will be about the
same elevation as the existing channel excavation on the east side of the berm Based
on the topographic survey plan the bottom of the berm area will range from about
Elevation 22 feet Mean Sea Level MSL at the north end and about 10 feet MSL at the
south end
Based on the above the borrow excavation will range from about 13 to 27 feet in
depth It should be noted that some of the test pits were terminated at a shallow depth
due to the hard limestone and sandstone deposits encountered near the ground
surface
We anticipate that the proposed borrow excavation site will be underlain by
alluvial and coralline deposits We did not encounter groundwater in the test pits at the
time of the field exploration Based on the results of the exploration we believe that the
majority of the materials within the borrow excavation site at the berm area extending to
the test pit depths are suitable for use as non expansive capping fill material
The coralline deposits consisting of silty gravel silty sand gravelly sand sandy
gravel gravel and limestone and sandstone formation may be considered suitable for
non expansive capping fill material The non expansive capping fill materials should
have a CBR swell of 1 percent or less and a CBR greater than 20
Although the clayey gravel silty clay and clayey silt materials encountered in the
test pits had a CBR swell of less than 1 percent we recommend that these materials
not be considered for non expansive capping material and used for general fill material
since the CBR was less than 20
Borrow materials with a CBR swell of more than 1 percent but less than
3 percent may be used as general fill materials The limestone and sandstone
fragments and boulders should be reduced to less than 3 inches in largest dimension
Materials used for general fill should be less than 6 inches in largest dimension

Based on the test pits previously performed in the adjacent area we anticipate
that alluvial clay material may be encountered below the limestone formation
encountered in the test pits at the southern portion of the berm area These alluvial
clays are highly expansive and are unsuitable for use as non expansive capping fill and
general fill material Therefore these highly expansive soils should not be use as fill for
the project
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted herein are based in part upon
information obtained from the field test pits Variations of conditions between and
beyond the field test pits may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may
not become evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident it
will be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations presented herein

plan received on from Engineering Concepts Inc The field test pit
locations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods
used

gradual transition Water level data from the test pits was measured at the times shown

rainfall and other factors
