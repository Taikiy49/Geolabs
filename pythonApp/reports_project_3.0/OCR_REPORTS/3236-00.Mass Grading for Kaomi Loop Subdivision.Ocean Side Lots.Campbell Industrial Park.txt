INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed mass grading project at the Kaomi Loop Subdivision Ocean
Side Lots at Campbell Industrial Park in the District of Ewa on the island of Oahu Hawaii
The general location and vicinity of the site are shown on the Project Location Map
Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering recommenda
tions resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering analyses
These recommendations are intended for design input for mass grading at the site The
findings and recommendations presented herein may not be appropriate to form the basis
for preparation of construction cost estimates nor for construction bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed project site is located at the Campbell Industrial Park in the District
of Ewa on the island of Oahu Hawaii It is proposed to develop the approximately 24
acre site for industrial use The current plans indicate that six industrial lots will be
constructed in the proposed industrial subdivision development
At the time this report was prepared detailed site grading plans were not available
Based on conversations with the project civil engineer it is our understanding that minor
cuts and fills on the order of 6 feet will be required for Lot Nos 4 through 7 In general
the finish grades at these lots will slope down at an inclination of about 1 to 2 percent

under this phase of development Lot No 3 has been constructed and dedicated to the

EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is located near the makai end of Kaomi Loop at the southwestern
portion of Campbell Industrial Park in the district of Ewa on the island of Oahu Hawaii
The project site is bounded to the north by the existing Brewer Company facility the
existing Unitek facility to the south Kaomi Loop to the east and an open channel
drainage way to the west Based on our field exploration and the topographic survey
provided to our office the site is relatively flat with ground surface elevations between 2
and 12 feet Mean Sea Level MSL

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from two shield
volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The project site is located adjacent to the southern
flank of the older volcano Waianae
After the cessation of volcanic activity the island began to subside The flanks of
the subsiding domes formed a salient for the growth of fringing reefs built by corals and
calcareous alga
During the Pleistocene Epoch or Ice Age global sea levels fluctuated in response
to the advance and retreat of the continental glaciers As the glaciers grew and
advanced less water was available to fill the ocean basins so the sea level fell to stands
below the current level As the glaciers melted and retreated the sea rose to stands
often exceeding the current level
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 3236 00 Page 6
During the high stands of the sea sediments and marine deposits were laid down
at elevations above the current sea level The coral algal reef limestones encountered
at the project site were believed to have been deposited during the Kaena 95 feet MSL
stand of the sea and subsequently planed to their current elevation during the Waimanalo
25 feet MSL stand

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Undocumented Fills and Cavities
On the basis of our field exploration and observation the site appears to be
underlain by a surface layer of undocumented fill ranging from 114 to more than 6 feet
in thickness overlying hard coral formation In addition significant number of surface
cavities were noted during our field exploration Previous experience indicates that such
cavities are commonly found in coral formations in the Ewa area Experience also
indicates that there are usually subsurface cavities in such formations
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 3236 00 Page 8
The undocumented fill contains cobbles boulders rubble construction debris and
other deieterious materials To reduce potential settlements associated with the
undocumented fill it is our recommendation that the surficial fill material be stripped to
expose the natural coral formation The stripped material may be reused as general fill
material provided our recommendations in the Site Grading subsection are followed
In order to reduce the possibility of potential large cavities situated below the finish
grade of future fill embankments which may cause settlement of the overlying fill and
future improvements we recommend that the exposed underlying coral formation be
proof rolied with a minimum 10 ton vibratory smooth drum roller with a minimum of six
passes If cavities are exposed after the above proof rolling operation the dimensions
of the cavities should be verified by our field representative using hand probing In
general if coral cavity with dimensions of 3 feet or less is exposed we recommend that
the upper 12 inches of coral formation areas within 8 feet of the cavity be scarified and
the coral fragments generated by the dozer s ripping scarification be recompacted to 95
percent relative compaction If cavity larger than 3 feet is exposed we recommend that
the cavity be ripped open and cleaned of all organic and deleterious matters and loose
sediments The excavation should be backfilled with additional clean granular material
such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation up to the surface of the cavity
A woven filter fabric consisting of Mirafi 500X or equivalent should be placed above the
cavity backfill to help strengthen the cavity subgrade The filter fabric should extend 2 feet
beyond the edges of the cavity
Due to the uncertain quantity of cavities situated below the site we recommend
that contract documents include additive and deductive unit prices for the fill construction
to account for variations in the backfill quantities

It is our understanding that minor cuts and fills on the order of 6 feet are
anticipated during mass grading In general the finish grades will slope down at an
inclination of about 1 to 2 percent towards the ocean Preliminary estimates by the civil
engineer indicated that embankment volumes on the order of 38 000 cubic yards may be
required Therefore import fill materials will be required during the mass grading
operation

Foundations
On the basis of our exploration it is our opinion that spread continuous and mat
foundation systems could be used to support future lightly loaded improvements In
general we believe that mat foundation supported on structural fill layer would help to
span over potential weak zones located below the foundations If continuous or spread
footings are desired we recommend that a probing and cement grouting program be
considered to detect and or further delineate potential cavities located below the footings
Site specific geotechnical engineering exploration should be conducted to provide
data for foundation design recommendations at each individual lot for future structural
improvements when their final location and configuration have been established
