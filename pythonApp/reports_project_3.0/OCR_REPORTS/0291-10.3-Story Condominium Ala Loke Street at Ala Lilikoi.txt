SITE DESCRIPTION
The site of the condominium is located on the north side of
Ala Loke Street at the corner of Ala Lilikoi in the Salt Lake
area The lot is slightly higher than the street with curb
and gutter installed The lot is brush covered with no
structures present
Geologically the area is part of the Salt Lake crater and is
underlain by a layer of CLAY and volcanic TUFF The CLAY layer
is believed to be the original lake bottom which was subsequently
filled to create the lot and surrounding development

FIELD EXPLORATION
Three 3 exploratory borings were drilled to depths of about
15 3 feet to 20 0 feet with a Mobile rotary auger drilling
rig Undisturbed samples were obtained using a 2 4 inch I D
sampler Also Standard Penetration split spoon samples were
obtained
The soil material encountered in the borings does not necessarily
represent subsurface soil conditions at other points on the
site however the soils encountered are considered to be represen
tative of the soil conditions across the site in view of the
small area considered The soil materials obtained in the boring
were classified visually in the field and taken to the laboratory
for a more detailed analyses

DISCUSSION
The soils underlying the site consist of compact fill consisting
of dense Silty GRAVEL and rock fragments approximately 8 0 to
13 0 feet thick Underlying this compacted fill is a layer of
Silty CLAY which in turn is underlain by dense volcanic TUFF
mudrock The gravelly fill is capable of supporting the
structure with the mudrock having a much higher bearing capacity
Foundations for the planned 3 story building may be conventional
spread footings and should be founded in the compact fill consist
ing of Silty GRAVEL
The clay layer beneath the fill should not affect the structure
when using low bearing values The footing pressure will be
approximately 75 dissipated at the depth of the clay layer

RECOMMENDATIONS
Foundations
1 The bottom of the foundations should be placed on the
compacted fill a minimum depth of two feet below the existing
grade An allowable bearing of may be utilized for
the compacted dense gravelly fill material Footings should
be established at least 2 feet into compact and firm material
and at least 2 feet Below the lowest adjacent final grade
whichever is lower
2 All footing excavations should be inspected by a soils
engineer prior to placing concrete and steel to assure that
the footings are supported in compact and dense material
3 All footing backfill and utility backfill should be properly
compacted
Settlement
Settlement for the foundation system supported in the compacted
fill material will depend on the actual loads imposed It is
estimated that for the 3 story building planned maximum settle
ment will not exceed l inch Differential settlement will not

Floor Slabs
Prior to placing slabs on grade the existing grade should be
sscarified to a depth of 12 inches moistened as required to
L obtain optimum moisture content and recompacted to 90 percent
of the maximum dry density as determined by ASTM D 1557 70
Floor slabs should be reinforced with a minimum of 6x6 10x10
Le welded wire fabric Slabs which will be provided with a floor
covering should be protected by a polyethelene plastic vapor
barrier The barrier should be covered with a thin layer of
sand about one inch to prevent puncture and aid in the
concrete cure
Grading
Prior to the placement of any fill all grass weeds and loose
soil should be removed All organic matter brush trash etc
should be removed and wasted Fill should then be placed
under the supervision of a soils engineer to a minimum relative
compaction of 90 percent and in accordance with the attached
Earthwork Specifications Appendix D Grading on the project
should be kept a minimum to avoid any additional surcharge due
to thick layers of additional compacted fill

Preparation of Fill Areas
All areas upon which fill is to be placed after clearing
as outlined in Section 1 of these specifications shall
be scarified until free of uneven features to a depth
as determined by the soils engineer and watered and
compacted according to Section 4 of these specifications
Placement of Fill
a Material for fill shall consist of on site soils
Fill material shall be free of all organic matter
and other deleterious material and shall not contain

b Compaction of Fill
After the base for the fill has been prepared as
described above it shall be brought to the proper
moisture content and compacted to not less than

c Depth of Fill

Compaction Equipment
The soils engineer shall determine the type of compacting
equipment which will attain the specified results in the
most efficient manner Sheepsfoot vibratory or pneumatic
tire rollers may be used in the test section and the equip
ment which produces the specified results in the most
expedient manner as determined by the soils engineer shall
be employed by the contractor The equipment used in roll
ing shall be in good working condition fully ballasted
and self cleaning Fill material placed in an unsatis
factory condition and not within the enclosed specifications
shall be rejected by the soils engineer and the contractor
shall rework the fill placed such that the specifications
are followed
Removal and Backfill of Underground Structures
Any underground structures such as cesspools cisterns
septic tanks wells pipe lines etc shall be removed
under the direction of the soils engineer Backfill of
the excavation shall be in accordance with these specifi
Cations
Supervision of Earthwork
Field density tests shall be made by the soils engineer
during the earthwork operation such that he may certify
that the fill was placed according to accepted specifica
tions In the event that field density tests of a layer
or any portion thereof is less than the required density
the particular layer or portion shall be reworked until
the required density is obtained
Seasonal Requirements
No fill shall be placed during unfavorable weather condi
tions as determined by the soils engineer After interrup
tion of work due to heavy rain the soils engineer shall
approve previously placed fill before resumption of earth
moving operations
