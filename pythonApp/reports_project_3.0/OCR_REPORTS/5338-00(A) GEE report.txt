FIELD EXPLORATION
Our field exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling two borings
designated as Boring Nos 1 and 2 extending to depths of about 15 5 and 16 5 feet below
the existing ground surface The borings were drilled using a truck mounted drill rig
equipped with continuous flight solid stem augers and rock coring tools The approximate
locations of the borings drilled at the site are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on the Boring Log Legend Plate 3 Graphic
representations of the materials encountered are provided on the Logs of Borings
Plates 4 and 5
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained from the borings drilled in
general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of
Soils by driving a 3 inch OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling
30 inches In addition some samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel
Sampling of Soils by driving a 2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same
hammer and drop The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third
6 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of
Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Core samples of rock materials encountered at the site were obtained using
diamond core drilling techniques in general accordance with ASTM Standard Practice
D 2113 Diamond Core Drilling for Site Investigation Core drilling is a rotary drilling
method which uses a hollow bit to cut into the rock formation The rock material left in the
hollow core of the bit is mechanically recovered for examination and description
Recovery REC is used as a subjective guide to the interpretation of the relative
quality of rock masses Recovery is defined as the actual length of material recovered
from a coring attempt versus the length of the core attempt For example if 4 feet of
material is recovered from a 5 foot core run the recovery would be 80 percent and would
be shown on the Logs of Borings as REC 80
The Rock Quality Designation RQD is also a subjective guide to the relative
quality of rock masses RQD is defined as the percentage of the core run that is sound
material in excess of 4 inches in length without any discontinuities discounting any drilling
induced fractures or breaks If 2 5 feet of sound material is recovered from a 5 foot core
run the RQD would be 50 percent and would be shown on the Logs of Borings as
RQD 50 Generally the following is used to describe the relative quality of the rock
based on the Practical Handbook of Physical Properties of Rocks and Minerals

FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
We understand that a new sewer line will be installed at the project site as part of
the planned development Our recommendations pertaining to the design of the sewer
line trench and other geotechnical engineering aspects are presented in the following
sections of this letter report
Sewer Line Trench
We understand that a new sewer line will be installed at the project site It is
anticipated that the trench for sewer line will be excavated in the fill materials encountered
at the site The fills encountered in the borings included boulders and concrete debris
which would cause some excavation difficulty and should not be reused for backfill
material
In general granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel
ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation or 1 inch minus well graded granular material is
recommended under the pipes Free draining granular materials No 3B Fine gravel or
1 inch minus well graded granular materials should be used for the initial trench backfill
up to about 12 inches above the pipes to provide adequate support around the pipes It is
critical that free draining materials be used to reduce the potential for formation of voids
below the haunches of pipes and to provide adequate support for the sides of the pipes
Where the 1 inch minus well graded granular materials are used as bedding and initial
trench backfill the bedding and initial trench backfill should be moisture conditioned to
above the optimum moisture content and compacted to at least 90 percent relative
compaction Improper trench backfill could result in settlement of the backfill and damage
to the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipes to
the top of the subgrade or finished grade should consist of granular materials generally
less than 6 inches in maximum size structural fill The backfill material should be
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content placed in maximum 12 inch
level loose lifts and mechanically compacted to not less than 90 percent relative
compaction to reduce the potential for future ground subsidence Where trenches are
below pavement areas the upper 3 feet of the trench backfill below the pavement grade
should be compacted to at least 95 percent relative compaction

Based on the existing site condition we envision that cut and fill may be required to
achieve the design finished grades for the project In general areas within the contract
grading limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed at the on set of earthwork
Vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable materials should be removed and
disposed of properly off site

Soft loose and yielding areas encountered during clearing below areas designated
to receive fill or future improvements should be over excavated to expose firm or dense
material and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with well compacted fill The
excavated soft loose and or organic soils should be properly disposed of off site
Contract documents should include additive and deductive unit prices for over excavation
and compacted fill placement to account for variation in the over excavation quantities
After clearing and grubbing existing utilities to be abandoned should be removed
and the resulting excavation should be properly backfilled with fill material placed in 8 inch
loose lifts moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to
a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Utilities to be abandoned in place under the
proposed structures should be backfilled by pumping lean concrete or Controlled Low
Strength Material CLSM under low pressure
The areas to receive fill and finished subgrades should be scarified to about
8 inches moisture conditioned to 2 percent above the optimum moisture and compacted
to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the
in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the
same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 test procedures Optimum
moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum
dry density
Where shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of the subgrade we
recommend that the soil be thoroughly moistened to close the cracks or scarified and
prepared again as recommended above Saturation and subsequent yielding of the
exposed subgrade due to inclement weather and poor drainage may require
over excavation of the soft and or loose areas and replacement with well compacted fill
In general the excavated on site soils may be re used as a source of general fill or
backfill materials If required imported general fill materials needed for the site filling
should consist of materials with a low expansion potential Imported general fill materials
should consist of materials with particle sizes of 3 inches or less in maximum dimension
The material should have a laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR greater than 12 and
a swell potential of less than 1 percent when tested in accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 1883
Select borrow subbase and aggregate base course materials should meet the
material requirements in Sections 30 and 31 respectively as specified in the Standard
Specifications for Public Works Construction 1986 Imported fill materials should be
tested by Geolabs for conformance with these recommendations prior to delivery to the
project site for the intended use
General fills and backfills should be moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent
above the optimum moisture content placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose
thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction The general
