PRE-PRODUCTION MICROPILE INSTALLATION AND LOAD TESTING 
KAHULUI AIRPORT – WASTEWATER TREATMENT AND DISTRIBUTION 
KAHULUI, MAUI, HAWAII 
Dear Mr. Kunioka: 
This letter report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observation services 
performed during the pre-production micropile installation and load testing for the Kahului 
Airport – Wastewater Treatment and Distribution project located in Kahului on the Island of 
Maui, Hawaii. 
In general, this letter report presents our field observations and the results of load 
testing performed for the pre-production micropile from December 7 to 17, 2020. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The project site is located within the existing Kahului Airport Pump Station in Kahului 
on the Island of Maui, Hawaii. The project involves the construction of three concrete pads 
to support the new MBR treatment package plant, equalization tanks, and storage tanks. 
Based on the contract documents provided, we understand that the MBR treatment package 
plant, Equalization Tanks and Storage Tanks are to be supported by 7-inch diameter 
micropiles with a design load capacity of 64 kips specified. Per project specifications, the 
contractor is required to perform a pre-production load test of up to 200 percent of the design 
load on one sacrificial micropile.  
MICROPILE INSTALLATION 
A pre-production test micropile, designated as T-1, was installed near the 
northeastern corner of the project site. The approximate location of the pre-production 
micropile is shown on the Site Plan, Plate 1. The pre-production micropile was installed by 
Kelikai, Inc. on December 7 and 8, 2020, utilizing a Casagrande C6 track-mounted drill rig. 
In general, the pre-production micropile T-1 was installed extending to a depth of about 
54.5 feet below the ground surface with about 30 feet of 7-inch OD steel casing left in-place

Based on our field observations, the pre-production micropile T-1 was installed by 
advancing the steel casing to a depth of about 54.5 feet below the ground surface. The 
pre-production micropile generally extended through the soft/loose recent alluvium and 
coralline detritus and developed load capacity in stiff residual soils and basalt rock formation. 
Upon reaching the tip elevation, the cement grout was pumped via a tremie pipe from the 
bottom to the top of the casing, displacing the groundwater inside the steel casing. After the 
initial grout application, the #14 Grade 75 all-threaded rebar with centralizers was installed 
to the full depth of the borehole with centralizers spaced at every 10 feet. 
The steel casing was extracted from the bottom 25.5 feet of the pre-production 
micropile, providing the bond length within the underlying basalt rock formation. The 
remaining steel casing (about 30 feet) was left in-place at the top of the micropile (unbonded 
zone) as the permanent casing. During the setting of the permanent casing, the steel casing 
was raised and plunged 4 feet into the basalt rock formation. Prior to load testing, the steel 
casing protruding above the ground was cut and removed to provide access for the load 
testing apparatus. 
MICROPILE TESTING 
The pre-production load test was conducted as a tensile load test in general 
accordance with ASTM D3689. The design load capacity was specified as 64 kips for the 
micropiles and load tested to at least 200 percent of the design load capacity. A hydraulic 
jack system reacting against a reaction beam was used to apply the testing load to the load 
test micropile. In general, the reaction beam was supported on four additional micropiles 
installed to the same depth as the load test-micropile at two ends of the beam. The micropile 
testing was performed by Kelikai, Inc. 
A load cell was used to measure the test loads applied to the micropile. The micropile 
displacements were measured with two dial gauges on the left and right sides of the load 
test micropile and a 1/100 division ruler was set onto an extension from the micropile. Each 
dial gauge was attached to an independent reference beam with a 1/100-inch division ruler 
to verify reference beam movements that could affect the dial gauge readings. In addition, 
a 1/100-inch division ruler was set on a remote reference to verify potential ground 
displacement during the load testing that could affect the dial gauges readings. The 
surveyor’s level was used to observe the rulers and the remote reference for movement. 
In general, the pre-production micropile T-1 was loaded to the maximum test load of 
128 kips in approximately 16-kip load increments (about 25 percent of the design load 
capacity). Each load increment, including the test load at 64-kip, was held for at least 
5 minutes. The ultimate load capacity of 128-kip (200 percent of design load) was held for 
10 minutes to evaluate the effects of creep. The gross rate of displacement at the 128-kip 
load increments were no greater than 0.002 inches between 1 and 10 minutes. 
Subsequently, the pre-production micropile was unloaded in approximately four equal
