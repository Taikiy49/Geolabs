BACKGROUND INFORMATION
The project site is located in the Waipio area in the District of Ewa on the island of
Oahu Hawaii The project site is bounded by the east bound Interstate Highway H 1 to
the north existing houses to the south an City and County s drainage easement to the
west and Kaupu Place to the east It is proposed to construct approximately 22 single
family housing units within the subdivision In addition infrastructure consisting of
roadways drain lines sewer lines and water lines are also planned to serve the proposed
subdivision development

The proposed subdivision development is divided into three phases designated as
Phases 1 2A and 2B The Phase 2A portion of the project includes the construction of
4 single family house lots designated as Lot Nos 14610 through 14613 as shown on
Plate 1 The earthwork for Phase 2A consisted of clearing and grubbing cutting and
filling for the house pads and driveway and excavations and backfilling of the sewer
trench
EARTHWORK OBSERVATIONS
The earthwork operations were performed by Kiewit Pacific Company Prior to
filling operations the exposed subgrades to receive fill were scarified brought to near the
optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum
dry density as determined in accordance with American Society of Testing and Material
ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor
The fill materials needed for the Phase 2A earthwork were on site material
generated from cutting operation Samples of the on site materials utilized as fill and
backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing The maximum
dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in
general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test
results are summarized on Plate 2 of this report
During the fill operation the fill materials were generally placed in near level lifts
brought to above the optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of
the maximum dry density determined in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557
Modified Proctor Periodic field density tests were taken during the fill operations to
confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures and soil conditions
were noted
A total of 23 field density tests were performed using the nuclear probe method in
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 2922 The results of the compaction tests
taken during the house pad grading and driveway construction are summarized on
Plates 3 1 and 3 2 of this report These tests were taken on the prepared ground
surfaces and on constructed engineered fills and base course at the site Test locations
and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes
These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied

Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the earthwork operations observed and tested by
Geolabs Hawaii during the Phase 2A portion were performed in general accordance with
the intent of the project plans and specifications and our geotechnical engineering
recommendations presented in the above referenced report
Because of the anticipated relatively dry weather conditions at the project site we
recommend that the finish subgrade be properly maintained and be kept moist prior to
house construction If shrinkage cracks are observed on the surface the finished
subgrade should be thoroughly moistened to close all cracks and recompacted to a
minimum of 90 percent compaction
We also recommend that proper surface drainage be maintained at the site for
good performance of the house foundations Good drainage requirements are essential
for the proper performance of the foundation support system since ponded water could
cause subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of strength
LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some work at the site has
occurred without our observation It should be recognized that there are variations in the
accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the
earthwork construction and materials Our services were performed in accordance with
generally accepted standards of geotechnical engineering practice no warranty is
expressed or implied Provision of construction monitoring by an engineer is not
insurance nor does it guarantee construction of any type The contractor shall retain
