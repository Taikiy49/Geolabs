Summary Report
Ground Settlements
Palm Villa Il
Buildings 6 23
In accordance with your request we have conducted an exploration of the ground
settlements between Buildings 6 and 23 of Palm Villa Il This report summarizes our work
on the project and presents our findings
Considerations
In April of 1991 it was noted that the ground between Buildings 6 and 23 had
settled The settlement was on the order of a few inches The affected area appeared
to be a strip approximately 200 feet long The area of greatest settlement appeared to
coincide with the central axis of the trench of a 66 inch drainline which had been installed
prior to the construction of the buildings
This drainline was the extension of the drainline traversing Palm Court which had
experienced settlements that resulted in pavement failure and collapse
As the settlements progressed it was noted that there were cracks in the concrete
slab on grade floors of Buildings 6 and 23 It was further noted that one corner of
Building 6 had settled approximately 112 to 2 inches

Purpose and Scope
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions at the project site in order to develop a generalized soil data set pertinent to
the ground settlements and to develop recommendations pertaining to the remediation
of the settlements

Findings
A review of the project plans indicates that the 66 inch drainline was installed
through a previous siltation basin which was backfilled during the construction of the
drainline The corners of Buildings 6 and 23 are situated over portions of the old siltation
basin
Our field exploration consisted of the drilling and sampling of nine 9 borings All
of the borings except Boring 9 which was drilled with portable equipment inside of
Building 6 were sampled continuously Borings 1 and 2 were drilled within the siltation
basin backfill to provide information about the relative levels of compaction in this backfill
Boring 3 was drilled outside the trench line in undisturbed soils to provide control data for
comparison with the data from the remainder of the borings which were drilled through
the drainline trench backfill
In general the borings drilled through the trench backfill showed low blow counts
for the samples indicating soft or loose soils Some horizons of more competent soils
were noted at irregular intervals Based on these data and the high moisture contents
indicated by our laboratory testing it appears that the backfill was placed in thick lifts
e g 24 inches or more which hindered proper compaction
Boring 1 situated in the front of Building 6 encountered soft or loose soils to the
bottom of the silting basin backfill This appears to indicate either poor quality backfill of
the siltation basin or soils affected by movement of the backfill towards the center of the
trench The conditions encountered in Boring 2 located in the front of Building 23
indicated more competent soils These findings are consistent with the greater amount
of settlement occurring at in Building 6
By comparison the conditions encountered in Boring 3 outside the backfilled
area indicated that the soils at this location were in a very stiff or dense condition to the
maximum depth explored
It was also noted that 3 B Fine gravel pipe bedding was not encountered in the
borings drilled through the trench backfill
Laboratory tests were conducted to determine the relative amounts of
consolidation or settlement that the clayey soils found in the backfill would experience
under a load of 1 000 pounds per square foot when saturated This would simulate

saturation of the trench backfill after completion of the infrastructure It was noted that
soil compacted to about 90 per cent of the maximum dry density for the on site soils
expanded rather than consolidated after saturation While samples compacted to
approximately 80 and 70 per cent showed relative consolidations of 10 and 19 per cent
respectively when saturated These results indicate that the on site soils used as backfill
would not compress settle even after saturation provided they were compacted to a
minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density
In summary it appears that the ground settlements are a result of insufficient
compaction of the drain line trench backfill
