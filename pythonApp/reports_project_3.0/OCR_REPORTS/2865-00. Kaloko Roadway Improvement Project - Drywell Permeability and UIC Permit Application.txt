INTRODUCTION
In accordance with the request of Kiewit Pacific Company we have made visual
observations upon completion of construction of twenty 20 new drywells at the project
site In addition we have tested five 5 of the new drywells This report has been
prepared in support of Underground Injection Control permit application No UH 1713
The approximate location of the project site and its vicinity is shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1 Our work was performed in general accordance with our proposal
to Kiewit Pacific Company dated March 25 1991
This report summarizes findings resulting from our field observations and testing
of the new drywells at the project site
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located near the Kona Light Industrial Subdivision in the District
of North on the island of Hawaii It is our understanding that the
County of Hawaii requires that new development create no net increase in discharge of
surface or storm water runoff The general practice on the island of Hawaii is to utilize
drywells as a method of disposal of anticipated surface runoff
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2865 00 Page 2
The project consists of a new roadway approximately 11 600 linear feet in length
which will serve to connect the Queen Kaahumanu and Mamalahoa Highways It was
proposed to install twenty four 24 drywells to provide on site disposal of storm water
run off along the roadway alignment
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
This report was prepared on behalf of Kiewit Pacific Company The construction
contract documents for this project required that the contractor file the Underground
Injection Control permit application for the owner County of Hawaii Department of Public
Works This report was prepared in support of that requirement
The purpose of our work on the project was to make visual observations of the dry
wells to conduct permeability tests on the wells and to prepare and submit this
engineering report as required under Chapter 11 23 Department of Health Administrative
Rules Underground Injection Control
In order to accomplish these tasks we have conducted a program consisting of
the following work efforts
1 Visual observation of the drywells after their construction and monitoring of
the field permeability testing by a Field Geologist from our firm
The logistical support and source of water for the testing was provided by
Kiewit Pacific Company
2 Analyses of the field data for the presentation of geotechnical engineering
findings pertinent to the new wells

3 Preparation of this report summarizing our work on the project and
presenting our findings in general accordance with Department of Health
requirements
4 Coordination of our overall work on the project and client design team
consultation by a Principal Engineer of our firm
5 Miscellaneous work efforts such as drafting word processing and clerical
support
Detailed descriptions of the drywell construction and permeability testing are
presented in the appendices of this report
GENERAL INFORMATION
The project site is located approximately 3 5 miles north of Kailua Kona next to the
Kona Light Industrial Subdivision Unit One in the District of North Kona on the island of
Hawaii The drywells are situated on the parcels identified by Tax Map Key Numbers 7 3
between Stations 10 00 and 85 50 The alignment of the roadway and the location of
the dry wells at the site are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
There are no known wells used as sources of potable water within one quarter
mile of the site Drinking water in this portion of the island of Hawaii is obtained from
wells tapping the basal aquifers in Hualalai Basalts Tne new drywells were installed in
a basalt formation that appears to be Hualalai Basalt

PHYSICAL CHARACTERISTICS
The site is located in the District of North Kona on the island of Hawaii The
climate of the area is mild with an average annual temperature of about 74 F and annual
rainfall amounts ranging from about 20 to about 40 inches per year
The site is situated on the western flank of Mount Hualalai The topography is
relatively gentle to moderate sloping down towards the west from the central portion of
the island to the ocean
At the time of the drywell testing the roadway had been paved from Stations 0 00
to approximately 37 00 Final grading was proceeding at the time of our testing on the
portion of roadway above this point Twenty 20 drywells had been completed and are
in a functional state Formerly the site had been undeveloped land
Regional Geology
The island of Hawaii is composed of five 6 basalt shield volcanoes built by the
extrusion of the lavas during the late Pleistocene and the Holocene Epochs

The complete drywell system will consist of twenty four 24 drywells in the
development area Four 4 of the drywells will be located upgradient of the U I C Line
Approval to construct twenty 20 of these wells has been granted with approval of the
remainder pending public notice and response Therefore this report addresses the
twenty 20 drywells that have been approved for construction The remaining four 4
drywells will be addressed in a supplement to this report
Injection will be under gravity pressure i e the head pressure that builds up within
the individual drywells Details of the system are shown on Plate A 1
Since the new drywells are intended for intermittent disposal of storm water runoff
only there is no additional standby system nor are monitoring wells planned
GEOHYDROLOGIC CONSIDERATIONS
The site is located at Latitude North and Longitude West
The ground surface elevations at the site range from approximately 140 feet to 520 feet
above Mean Sea Level
The regional characteristics of groundwater in the area is fresh to brackish water
in a basal volcanic aquifer of relatively high permeability Published data indicates that
the groundwater table slopes towards the ocean at a gradient of about one foot per mile
Regional discharge is generally along the coastline
WELL CONSTRUCTION
Generally the drywells encountered interbedded layers of Pahoehoe and A a
basalts of the Hualalai Volcanic Series
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2865 00 Page 7
The entire depths of the drywells are protected by perforated concrete and
masonry ring curbing to support the sidewalls of the excavations and to allow for
infiltration into the surrounding strata Prior to placing the perforated masonry curbing
the dimensions of the excavated pits measured approximately 12 to 14 feet in diameter
The excavated materials were used as backfill behind the masonry curbing
In general the excavations for the drywells encountered layered loose to medium
dense clinker and dense basalt rock The excavation work was performed by blasting
the rock formation and removing the loose materials generated by the blasting The
drywells were excavated to a minimum depth of 21 feet below the existing ground level
PERMEABILITY TESTING
The subsurface permeability conditions at the site were tested by the injection of
clear fresh water from a water tank truck with a capacity of approximately 3 000 gallons
Injection rates were measured via an in line flow sensor at about 80 to 150 gallons per
minute for a period of about twenty 20 to forty five 45 minutes per drywell These tests
indicated that the formation has an approximate coefficient of permeability k with a range
on the order of about 400 to 900 feet per day
During the injection of water the variations of the water level in the drywells were
recorded with respect to time and rate of injection Groundwater was not observed in any
of the excavated drywells prior to the beginning of the testing
The results of these tests are presented in Appendix B

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from data points such as excavated drywells and injection tests
Variations of conditions between the drywells may occur and the nature and extent of
these variations may not become evident until the drywells have been put into operation
If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the findings presented
in this report
The locations of the drywells were determined by others We can not assume
responsibility for the accuracy of the location and or elevation of the drywells as used in
this report
Water level data from the field data points if indicated was collected at the times
given on the graphic representations and or in the text and appendices of this report
This data has been reviewed and interpretations made in the formulation of this report
However it must be noted that fluctuation may occur due to variations in rainfall tides
temperature and other factors
This report has been prepared for the exclusive use of the County of Hawaii for
specific application for the approval to operate drywells for the new Kaloko Roadway
Improvement Project in North Kona Hawaii in accordance with generally accepted
geotechnical engineering principles and practices No other warranty expressed or
implied is made
