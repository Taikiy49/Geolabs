INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the proposed 25 story condominium at 2429 Ala
Wai Boulevard in Waikiki area on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general
location of the site and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location
Map Plate 1 The exploration was performed in general accordance with
our proposal dated
This report summarizes our findings from the exploration and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analyses based
on the data obtained during the exploration These recommendations are
intended for design input and are not intended for construction estimation
or bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSTDERATIONS
It is proposed to develop the subject parcel on Ala Wai Boulevard for
a high rise residential condominiun The proposed development would
consist of a 25 story building with an adjoining 3 level above ground
parking structure
Based on the information provided to us the high rise building
approximately 40 by 50 feet in plan dimensions would have concrete
slab on grade for the ground floor and no basement is planned The
adjoining parking structure approximately 80 by 80 feet in plan
dimensions would have rigid concrete pavements for the ground level and
driveway area
Minimal site grading is anticipated

SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed project site is located in Waikiki area and fronts Ala
Wai Boulevard between Kaiulani and Liliuokalani Avenues The subject lot
is bounded by existing apartment buildings on adjacent lots and Tusitala
Street on the makai south side The proposed parking structure is about
15 to 20 feet away from the adjacent structures
The terrain of the site is generally level and the site is vacant
and largely barren of vegetation Ala Wai Canal is situated on the
northern side of the project site
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The island of Oahu was built up during the late Pliocene and early
Pleistocene by the activities of two basaltic shield volcanoes Waianae
and Koolau Koolau is the younger of the two and reached the end of its
main shield building phase about two million years ago The project site
is located on the southwestern flank of Koolau
As the main shield building on the island of Oahu drew to a close
the island began to subside slowly While the island was sinking
fringing coral algal reefs began to grow on the seaward margins of the
island
The project site is located on the Honolulu Coastal Plain a thick
sedimentary wedge composed of intercalated coral algal reef deposits
terrigenous and marine sediments and volcanics of the Honolulu Volcanic
Series In the early part of this century the area was low marshy
wetlands As the city of Honolulu grew and the Waikiki area was
urbanized man made fills were placed to reclaim the areas

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on our field exploration and analyses it is our opinion that
the upper fill and medium dense beach deposits are not adequate to support
the foundation loads of the proposed high rise condominium and multi level
parking structure on spread footing foundations without inducing excessive
settlements Therefore pile foundations bearing on the coral algal
formation are recommended to support the proposed buildings
Due to the presence of cavity encountered in Boring No 7 a cavity
probing program is recommended at each pile cap column location when the
final foundation plan is available to protect against potential cavity
collapse under pile loads
Pile Foundations
Since coral algal formation was encountered in the borings
end bearing pile foundations penetrating the upper loose lagoonal deposits
may be utilized for the development
concrete piles bearing on the coral algal formation are recommended An
allowable pile capacity of 100 tons may be used for design with estimated
settlements on the order of less than one half of an inch
Based on the exploration results we believe the majority of the
piles will be founded on the upper coral algal formation and the average
pile length for this project is estimated to be about 35 feet long
The piles should be driven with a hammer delivering a rated energy of
at least 39 000 foot pounds The hammer should be equipped with an energy
level control so that driving within the loose deposits may be effected at
a reduced energy level to minimize the tensile stresses within the piles
which could cause damage to the piles
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2165 00 Page 7
Additional pile driving criteria are as follows
1 The piles should be cast in one continuous section
2 The driving process should be continuous without interruption for
each pile A pile may be rejected or its design capacity
modified when the driving process is interrupted for more than
four 4 hours
3 During easy driving when the pile tip is still within loose
soil the hammer stroke should be carefully controlled reduced
energy so that excessive tensile stresses are not induced in the
pile
4 The pile driving may be terminated when the blow counts steadily
increase with depth and reach 20 blows per inch for the last
inch or when aggregated blow counts are 52 blows for the last
foot
The above criteria is based on our preliminary wave equation analyses
performed for a diesel driving hammer with a 39 700 foot pounds rated
energy to drive a inch octagonal concrete pile for a design load of
100 tons The results of the wave equation analyses for pile driving are
presented in Appendix C
The purpose of the wave equation analyses was to provide the
contractor an idea in selecting hammers for production pile driving
Since the results will vary depending on the equipment the contractor
uses a final wave equation analysis of pile driving should be performed
when the actual hammer data are available
An allowable lateral pile capacity of up to 25 kips may be used The
details of the lateral load analysis of pile are presented in Appendix D

Cavity Probing Predrilling
A cavity in the upper coral ledge was encountered in Boring No 7
located at the central part of the high rise building site at a depth of
about 42 feet
Cavities are formed in coral algal formation due to the presence of
fresh water during the growth stages of the coral which prevent the
growth of the coral Piles which are stopped above the close proximity of
the cavity could be affected by potential future collapse of the cavity
Based on the exploration results it is our opinion that the cavity
encountered in Boring No 7 is most likely an isolated occurrence
However aS a precautionary measure we recommend that a probing program
be implemented prior to test pile driving to reduce the possibility of
pile foundation settlements due to the potential collapse of cavities
which may exist in the upper coral
This probing program should consist of drilling a probe boring at
each pile cap column location when the final foundation plan is
available to a minimm of 15 feet below the anticipated pile tip
elevation Rotary drilling is recommended for the probe boring
If a cavity is encountered by a probe a minimm of two 2
additional probes should be drilled to detect the extent of the cavity
The cavity should be backfilled with grout prior to test pile driving
Alternatively the individual pile locations may be predrilled to the
more competent lower ledge which is at about 50 foot depth below the
existing ground surface The piles then may bear on the lower coral algal
ledge Note that the pile length will change accordingly if predrilling
is adopted

Test Pile Program
A test pile program should be undertaken to fulfill the following
objectives
1 To examine the drivability of piles with the use of one or more
selected hammers
2 To verify that predrilling through the surficial fill layer is
not required for piles located next to adjacent existing
structures
3 To more accurately determine production pile order length
4 To help evaluate and modify if necessary driving criteria
To achieve these objectives we recommend that about 10 percent of
the total piles be test driven at the site These test piles may later be
incorporated into the actual foundation
Splice cans should be provided at the site during the test pile
driving period Splice cans may be fabricated with 1 4 inch thick high
grade steel and should be at least 6 feet long
As a part of the test pile program a minimum of one 1 pile load
test is recommended The selected test pile should be load tested to
twice the allowable load capacity in accordance with ASIM Test Method
D 1143
The test piles should be driven with the same hammer which will be
used for production pile driving The test pile program should be
conducted under close and continuous observation of the project
geotechnical engineer who will determine when piles may be stopped and to
provide guidance should additional measures be required due to unforeseen

Site Grading
Minimal site grading is anticipated
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limits should be cleared of vegetation rubbish old footings and slabs
and other deleterious materials These materials should be removed and
disposed of properly off site
The ground should then be scarified to a depth of minimm six 6
inches moisture conditioned to above optimm moisture content and
recompacted to 90 of its maximum dry density as established by ASIM Test
Method D 1557 78 Any loose or soft spots encountered should be
over excavated and then replaced with compacted non expansive select
material
Based on the field exploration and laboratory test results the
on site surficial soils are not suitable for use as fill materials All
imported fill material should consist of non expansive select granular
materials less than 3 inches in maximm size with less than 20 passing
the 200 sieve such as select borrow crusher waste cinder or coral
The fill material should be tested and approved by the geotechnical
engineer prior to transportation to the site
To reduce the swelling potential of the on site soils the materials
within the top 12 inches minimm below the slab cushion layer should
consist of non expansive soils by either raising the site grades or by
over excavating the expansive soils and replacing them with select borrow
materials Fill placement should be performed as soon as practicable
prior to pile installation and slab construction

The fill should be placed in horizontal loose level lifts up toa
maximum of 10 inches thick and compacted to a minimm of 90 of its
maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method
The site should be graded to drain water away from the foundations
Slab On Grade
It is our understanding that the ground floors of the proposed
condominium and parking structure are to be concrete slabs on grade Our
exploration indicates that the near surface soils at the project site have
a moderate to high shrink swell potential
To reduce the potential of distresses to the slabs by shrinking and
swelling of the subgrade soils we recommend that a minimm of 12 inches
of non expansive select material be placed in the slab subgrade below the
slab cushion layer to serve as a capping over the expansive soils The
capping fill should also extend a minimm of three 3 feet beyond the
slab perimeter
This non expansive select borrow capping layer may be placed either
by raising the site grades or by over excavation and replacement as
recommended above The thickened edge of the slab should be embedded a
minimum of 12 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade
To further reduce future post construction swelling of the underlying
expansive soils we recommend that the on site soils below the upper
capping fill be thoroughly moistened and protected from drying from the
start of construction until the slab concrete placement
A 4 inch layer of 3B Fine gravel should be provided below the slab
as cushion fill The slab subgrade should be kept moist prior to concrete
placement In order to protect against future moisture infiltration and
subsequent damage to floor coverings an impervious moisture barrier
should be incorporated below the slab

Utility Trench
Construction of new utility lines is anticipated for the
development A granular bedding consisting of six 6 inches of ASTM C 33
No 67 gravel 3B Fine is recommended under the pipes
Granular materials should be used for trench backfill The backfill
up to about one 1 foot above the pipes should be lightly tamped to
reduce the possibility of damaging the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level one foot
above the pipes to the subgrade of finished grade should be placed in
maximum 10 inch horizontal lift mechanically compacted to a minimum of
90 compaction per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 to protect against potential
future ground subsidence
Any existing utility lines should be located and protected during
construction

Field Exploration

inches or the hydraulic pressure to push the sampler at about 6 inch
increments are shown on the Boring Logs at the appropriate sample depths
Core samples were obtained by an NX core barrel The core recovery
and rock quality designation RQD values are shown on the Boring Logs at
the appropriate sample depths

APPENDIX
Wave Equation Analysis of Pile Driving
The wave equation analysis of pile driving was performed using Program
WEAPG which is a microcomputer adaption of the program WEAP originally
written by Goble and Associates for the Federal Highway Administration to
solve pile drivability problems
The program models hammer pile soil system by representing the pile
and driving system by a series of masses and springs The soil is modeled
by a spring and a dashpot attached to each mass The program output
provides ultimate resistance and maximum compressive and tensile stresses
in the piles as a function of hammer blowcount
The primary objectives of the analysis are
1 to analyze the driving stresses in the pile
2 to determine the suitable driving system to be used for the pile
driving and
3 to predict the ultimate pile capacity of the pile
The analysis was performed for driving inch piles with a DEIMAG
D 22 hammer The relationships of ultimate capacity and hammer blowcounts
commonly known as the bearing graph are presented on Plate C 1 1 together
with input information The maximum compressive stresses are presented on
Plate C 2 1 Tensile stresses are insignificant in the case analyzed
A typical computer print out is presented on Plates C 3 1 through

APPENDIX D
Lateral Load Analysis of Piles
Lateral load analysis was performed using Program PILEDG which is a
microcomputer adaption of a finite difference laterally loaded pile
program originally developed at the University of Texas of Austin The
program solves for deflection and bending moment along a pile under
lateral loads as a function of depth
The analysis was carried out with the use of internally generated
non linear P Y curves to represent soil moduli The lateral deflection
was then computed using appropriate soil moduli at various depths
Since the soil modulus varies with the deflection experienced the
computerized analysis first assumes a modulus value then calculates the
pile deflection based on the assumed modulus The calculated deflection
is then used to determine the modulus from the non linear P Y curves The
calculated modulus and the assumed modulus are then compared The
procedure is repeated until the newly assumed and calculated moduli
converge
