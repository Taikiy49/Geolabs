Dear Mr Toyomura
In accordance with our proposal dated April 15 we have performed a
geotechnical engineering exploration at the proposed comfort station within the existing
Asing Community Park in the District of Ewa on the island of Oahu Hawaii This letter
report summarizes the findings from our field exploration and laboratory testing and
presents our geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analyses for
the proposed project These recommendations are intended for design input only and are
subject to the limitations noted at the end of this letter report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located within the existing Asing Community Park adjacent to
the West Loch Housing area in the District of Ewa on the island of Oahu Hawaii The
general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map
Plate 1

station measuring approximately 40 feet by 30 feet in plan dimension within the park site
at the approximate location shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 We anticipate that the
consiruction Based on the general plan provided the design finish floor elevation for the
comfort station is set at 44 5 feet Mean Sea Level MSL In addition the project will
also involve construction of a small parking area for handicap vehicles
Based on our field observations the proposed comfort station site is open and
relatively level with an existing ground surface elevation about 43 feet MSL Based on
the existing topography and the anticipated finish floor elevation minimal site grading
work will be required to attain the design finished grades for the proposed construction

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our field exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling one boring
designated as Boring No 1 to a depth of about 20 feet below the existing ground
surface The approximate location of the boring is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general our field exploration encountered fill soils consisting of very stiff to hard
silty clays and clayey silts extending to the maximum depth explored of approximately
20 feet below the existing ground surface Groundwater was not encountered in the
drilled boring at the time of our field exploration It should be noted that groundwater
levels are subject to change due to seasonal precipitation and other factors
Detailed descriptions of the materials encountered in our field exploration are
presented on the Log of Boring Plate 4 Laboratory testing was performed on selected
soil samples obtained and the test results are presented on Plates 5 and 6
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Our field exploration disclosed that the project site is generally underlain by very
stiff to hard clayey soils throughout the depths explored Our laboratory tests indicated
that the near surface clayey soils generally exhibit moderate to high shrink swell potential
when subjected to fluctuations in the soil moisture contents Based on the generally stiff
subsoil conditions encountered at the project site we envision that slab on grade
foundations with thickened edge footings may be used to support the proposed new
comfort station anticipated for the subject project
However in order to reduce the potential for structural distress to the new slabs
resulting from swelling of the on site expansive soils we recommend that non expansive
select granular fill material be provided for a minimum depth of 18 inches below the slab
cushion The perimeter thickened edges of the new slabs should be embedded a
minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent exterior grade
In addition special attention should be given to the recommended procedures for
subgrade preparation of slabs and pavements to reduce the potential shrink swell effects
of the near surface soils Detailed discussion of these items and our geotechnical
engineering design recommendations are presented in the following sections of this
report

in general we envision that slab on grade foundations with thickened edge
footings may be used to support the proposed new comfort station anticipated for the
subject project As previously indicated the project site is generally underlain by

To reduce the potential for structural distress resulting from swelling of the on site
expansive soils we recommend that non expansive select granular fill material be
provided for a minimum depth of 18 inches below the slab cushion The non expansive
select granular fill should also extend laterally beyond the perimeter of the comfort station

An allowable bearing pressure of up to 2 500 pounds per square foot psf may be
used for the design of shallow foundations bearing on the recompacted in situ soils or
engineered fill material This bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may be
increased by one third for transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic
forces Lateral loads acting on the structures may be resisted by frictional resistance
developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil and by passive
earth pressure acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system
A coefficient of friction of 0 3 may be used for footings bearing on the recompacted in situ
soils or engineered fill materials Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be
estimated using an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of
depth pcf This assumes that the soil around the footings is well compacted or
undisturbed Unless covered by pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the upper
12 inches of soil should be neglected
it is recommended that the bottom of all footings bear on the stiff or dense
near surface soils The bottom of the footing excavations should be recompacted to at
least 90 percent relative compaction to provide a relatively firm and smooth bearing
surface prior to placement of reinforcing steel or concrete If soft and or loose materials
are encountered at the bottom of footing excavations they should be over excavated to
expose firm materials The over excavation should be backfilled with granular maierial
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction or the footing bottom should
be extended down to the underlying competent material Relative compaction refers to
the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of
the same soil established in accordance with procedures described in ASTM Test
Designation D 1557 91 Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by weight
corresponding to the maximum dry density

upward from the bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility
lines This requirement is necessary to avoid surcharging adjacent below grade
structures with additional structural loads and to reduce the potential for foundation
settlement If foundations are designed in accordance with our recommendations we
estimate total settlements to be less than 1 inch with differential settlements on the order
of 0 5 inches
Prior to placement of the select granular fill the clayey subgrades should be
scarified to a minimum depth of 8 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent
above the optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction The select granular fill material should provide a capping layer of moist soil
above the underlying potentially expansive subgrade soil thereby reducing the loss of
moisture from the clayey subgrade soils In our opinion the reduction of drying and
subsequent wetting of the subgrade soils should reduce the impact of shrink swell effects
on the slab caused by potential heaving of the underlying expansive clayey soils when
wetted The select granular fill should be moisture conditioned placed in level loose lifts
not exceeding 8 inches and compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction
In addition to the 18 inches of non expansive select granular fill we recommend
that a minimum 4 inch thick layer of cushion fill consisting of Basaltic Termite
Barrier BTB be placed below the slabs to serve as a capillary break and to provide
more uniform support of the slabs To reduce future moisture infiltration and subsequent
damage to floor coverings an impervious moisture vapor barrier is recommended on top
of the BTB cushion layer Two inches of BTB should be placed above the plastic
membrane to provide protection of the membrane and to aid in curing of the slab
concrete
Exterior concrete walkways may be required for the proposed project We
recommend that a minimum 12 inch cushion layer consisting of the non expansive select
granular fill material be provided below the concrete slab The select granular fill cushion
layer should be compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction Contraction
joints should be provided at intervals equal to the width of the walkways with expansion
joints at right angle intersections
Areas adjacent to the slabs should be backfilled tightly against the slab with
non expansive impervious soil and graded to divert water away from the slab to reduce
the potential for water ponding around the foundations
Subgrade preparation and footing excavations should be observed by a
representative of Geolabs Hawaii prior to placement of reinforcing steel or concrete to
confirm the foundation bearing conditions and the required embedment depths Proper
moisture conditioning and preparation of the clayey subgrade soils is critical to the
performance of the foundation and slab system

Site Preparation
Based on the existing topography and the proposed finish grades we anticipate
that minimal site grading work will be required for construction of the new improvements
at the subject site In general the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed at the on set of earthwork All vegetation debris
rubbish and other unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of off site
After clearing and grubbing areas beneath the future slab of the comfort station
should be over excavated to provide at least 18 inches of compacted non expansive
select granular fill material below the bottom of the slab cushion If zones of soft loose
or saturated soils are encountered at the over excavated subgrade level further
over excavation may be required to expose the underlying firm material The resulting
over excavation should be backfilled with compacted non expansive select granular fill
material
The over excavated subgrade surface should be scarified to a minimum depth of
8 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture content
and compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction
refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry
density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 91 test
procedures Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight
corresponding to the maximum dry density
Slab subgrades should be kept moist and free of shrinkage cracks until covered by
slab cushion fill or concrete Where shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of the
subgrade we recommend that the soil be re scarified and prepared as recommended
above at no additional cost to the Owner Saturation and subsequent yielding of the
exposed subgrade due to inclement weather and poor drainage may require
over excavation of the soft areas and replacement with well compacted fill at no
additional cost to the Owner
Non expansive select granular fill material required under the slab areas should
consist of imported granular material such as select borrow crushed coral or basalt
gravel The material should be well graded from coarse to fine with no particles greater
than 3 inches in largest dimension The material should have a faboratory California
Bearing Ratio CBR value of 20 or higher and a swell potential of 1 percent or less when
tested in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1883 It should also contain
between 10 and 30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve Imported fill material
should be tested and approved by Geolabs Hawaii prior to delivery to the project site for
its intended use

Pavements
We understand that new pavements will be required for the handicapped parking
as part of the proposed construction For preliminary design purposes we have assumed
that the new pavement will generally be subjected to passenger vehicles and light pick up
trucks
Our preliminary pavement design assumes that the pavement subgrade soil will be
similar to the near surface clayey fill soils encountered in the boring with a California
Bearing Ratio of 3 6 and a swell value of 5 9 percent Based on the above assumptions
we recommend that the following flexible pavement section be used for preliminary
design purposes
2 0 Inch Asphalt Concrete
6 0 Inch Base Course 95 Percent Relative Compaction
18 0 Inch Select Borrow Subbase 95 Percent Relative Compaction
26 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness on a Moist Compacted Subgrade
The subgrade soils should be scarified to a depth of about 8 inches
moisture conditioned to between 2 and 5 percent above the optimum moisture content
and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction The select borrow
subbase and base course beneath the asphalt concrete should consist of crushed basalt
aggregate compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction CBR and density
tests should be performed on the actual subgrade materials encountered during
construction to confirm the adequacy of the above section
Paved areas should be sloped and drainage gradients maintained to carry all
surface water off the site Surface water ponding should not be allowed on the site during
or after construction Where concrete curbs are used to isolate landscaping in or
adjacent to the pavement areas we recommend that a subdrain system be provided to
collect excessive water from landscaping irrigation
Utility Trench
We envision that new utility lines may be required for the project A granular
bedding consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation is
recommended under the pipes Free draining granular materials such as No 3B Fine
gravel should also be used for the trench backfill up to about 12 inches above the pipes
to provide adequate support around the pipes and to reduce the compaction effort of the
backfill thus reducing the possibility of damaging the pipes It is critical to use this

free draining material to reduce the potential for formation of voids below the haunches of
pipes and to provide adequate support for the sides of the pipes Improper trench backfill
could result in settlement of the backfill and damage to the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipes to
the top of the subgrade or finished grade may consist of on site soils or select granular fill
material The backfill should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture
content placed in maximum 8 inch level loose lifts and mechanically compacted to not
less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for future ground
subsidence Where trenches are below pavement areas the upper 3 feet of the trench
backfill below the pavement grade should be compacted to 95 percent relative
compaction
Drainage
The finished grades outside the new comfort station should be sloped to shed
water away from foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also advised
that gutter systems be installed around the building and that the discharge be diverted
away from the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near the
foundations and slabs should also be avoided Planters next to foundations should be
avoided or have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil
saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations
should be properly backfilled against the walls or slab footings immediately after setting of
the concrete to reduce water infiltration Drainage swales should be provided as soon as
possible and should be maintained to drain surface water run off away from the slab and
foundations

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part
upon information obtained from the field boring Variations of subsoil conditions from the
boring may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident
until construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to
reevaluate the recommendations provided in this report
The location of the field boring indicated in this report is approximate having been
estimated by taping from the features shown on the Site and Utility Plan prepared by
Calvin Kim Associates Inc Elevation of the field boring was estimated by interpolation
from the contour lines shown on the same plan The physical location and elevation of the
boring should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used
The stratification lines shown on graphic representations of the boring depict the
approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual
transition Water level data from the boring was measured at the times shown on the
graphic representations and or in the text of this report These data have been reviewed
and interpretations made in the formulation of this report However it must be noted that
fluctuation may occur due to variation in rainfall tides temperature and other factors
