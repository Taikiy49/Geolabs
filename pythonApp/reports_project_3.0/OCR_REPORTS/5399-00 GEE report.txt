2 1 Regional Geology
The Island of Lanai encompasses approximately 141 square miles of volcanic
origin land area with a maximum present day summit elevation of about 3 370 feet
Mean Sea Level MSL located near the east central part of the island The Island of
Lanai was formed by the eruption of the Lanai Volcanic Series lavas from a single
basaltic shield volcano and associated elongated volcanic rift zones The volcano was
built mainly by the extrusion of sequential bedded a a and pahoehoe basaltic lava flows
The Island of Lanai is believed to be approximately 1 2 to 1 5 million years old with the
most recent eruptions believed to have occurred approximately 30 000 years ago
Following a long period of uninterrupted lava eruption a period of intense
faulting rift zone collapse and subsidence occurred which created a new volcanic
caldera presently known as the Palawai Basin The caldera and other pre existing rift
zone depressions were slowly filled with thick lavas extruded by the diminishing volcanic
activity
The cessation of volcanic activity was followed by a long period of canyon
formation caused by stream erosion and the development of high sea cliffs along the
southern shoreline of the island The southern shoreline sea cliffs are believed to have
formed by faulting submarine landslide activity and ocean wave erosion The northern
shoreline is largely protected from ocean wave erosion due to the close proximity and
shelter provided by the Islands of Molokai and Maui The Island of Lanai was also
affected by multiple sea level fluctuations as a result of the transgression and recession
of the great continental ice sheets during the Pleistocene Epoch The sea level
fluctuations resulted in the formation of emerged and submerged shoreline features
surrounding the island perimeter The areas in the vicinity of the project site is generally
composed of basalt rock formation with varying degrees of weathering

Cavities and Lava Tubes
The proposed roadway alignments are generally underlain by Pahoehoe lavas
which typically contain larger and more extensive lava tubes some of which may
extend the entire length of the lava flow feature from the high mountain slopes
toward the ocean Lava tubes are usually difficult to detect solely by visual
observation of the ground surface unless the tube structure has experienced a prior
collapse event that breaches the roof cover Often lava tube structures are
encountered during site excavation and compaction when significant disturbance to
the near surface rock materials is experienced Therefore special attention should
be given to the site preparation procedures to systematically proof roll the
subgrades prior to construction of embankments and improvements over the basalt
rock formation at the project site
Large cavities that may be exposed on the cut slope face should be backfilled
and or grouted if necessary Cavities encountered during the pavement subgrade

Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions along the proposed roadway alignments of the
Manele Large Lot Subdivision project were explored by drilling and sampling 15 borings
designated as Boring Nos 1 through 15 extending to depths of about 20 to 22 feet
below the existing ground surface The borings were drilled using a truck mounted drill
rig equipped with continuous flight augers and coring tools The approximate locations
of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by
testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified
Soil Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representations of the
materials encountered in the borings are presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1
through A 15
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a
3 inch OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches In
addition some samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance
with ASTM Test Designation D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of
Soils by driving a 2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same hammer and
drop The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an
18 inch drive are shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Borings at the
appropriate sample depths
Core samples of rock materials encountered at the site were obtained using
diamond core drilling techniques in general accordance with ASTM Standard Practice
D 2113 Diamond Core Drilling for Site Investigation Core drilling is a rotary drilling
method that uses a hollow bit to cut into the rock formation The rock material left in the
hollow core of the bit is mechanically recovered for examination and description
Recovery REC is used as a subjective guide to the interpretation of the relative
quality of rock masses Recovery is defined as the actual length of material recovered
from a coring attempt versus the length of the core attempt For example if 3 7 feet of
material is recovered from a 5 0 foot core run the recovery would be 74 percent and
would be shown on the Logs of Borings as REC 74

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture Content ASTM D 2216 and Unit Weight ASTM D 2937
determinations were performed on selected samples as an aid in the classification and
evaluation of soil properties The results of these tests are presented on the Logs of
Borings at the appropriate sample depths
One Atterberg Limits test ASTM D 4318 was performed on a selected soil
sample to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits The test results are summarized on the
Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depth Graphic presentations of the test
results are provided on Plate B 1
Twelve Unconfined Compression tests ASTM D 2938 were performed on
selected rock cores to evaluate their unconfined compressive strength of the rock
formation encountered Results of the unconfined compression tests are presented on
Plate B 2
Two laboratory California Bearing Ratio Tests ASTM D 1883 were performed
on bulk samples of the near surface soils to evaluate the pavement support
characteristics of the soils The test results are presented on Plates B 3 and B 4
Two Modified Proctor compaction tests ASTM D 1557 were performed on bulk
samples of the near surface soils and potential borrow material to evaluate the dry
density and moisture content relationships The test results are presented on Plates
B 5 and B 6
Seven sets of corrosion tests including and minimum resistivity
were performed by Oceanic Analytical Laboratory Inc on selected soil
samples obtained from our field exploration The results of these tests are summarized
on Plate B 7
