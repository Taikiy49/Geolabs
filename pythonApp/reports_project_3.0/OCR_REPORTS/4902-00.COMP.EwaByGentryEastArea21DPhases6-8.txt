PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located within the eastern portion of the Ewa by Gentry
Residential Development Area in the District of Ewa on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The
project site is located east of Fort Weaver Road south of Iroquois Point Road and west of
the HECO easement The project includes a total of 33 residential lots and related access
roads A Project Location Map and a Site Plan are shown on Plates 1 and 2 respectively
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services
pertaining to the mass grading only for the project site
MASS GRADING
The mass grading work for the Arca 21D Phases to 8 project were observed by
Geolabs Inc from to The mass grading work
consisted of clearing and grubbing and placement and compaction of fills on the order of
about 1 to 4 feet thick to achieve the design mass grading elevations for the project The
mass grading was performed by RHS Lee Inc During the mass grading operations
samples of the on site and imported materials utilized as fill and backfill materials were
obtained and transported to our office for testing The maximum dry densities and
optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test results are summarized
on Plate 3

The fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as
determined in accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557
During the mass grading operations periodic field density tests were performed in
general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 2922 Nuclear Gauge Method
Results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 4 1 and 4 2 of this
report These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed
fills and backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated py pacing and
interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed to
be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained
and or approved by our field representative based on visual observation and hand probing
A pit approximately 10 feet by 10 feet in plan dimension and about 18 feet in depth
was excavated at what appears to be an old well site that was backfilled with boulders and
soft clayey materials Based on optical survey measurements we understand that the pit
is located within Lot No 78 but outside of the house and garage pad areas The pit was
backfilled with boulders and No 3B Fine gravel in the lower portion of the pit The upper
portion of the pit was backfilled with CLSM Controlled Low Strength Material with a
thickness of at least 5 feet and conipacted fill material
Our services consisted of part time field observation of the contractor s fill
placement subgrade preparation and compaction procedures and confirmation of
subgrade soil conditions exposed during the mass grading operations
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the mass grading work for the above referenced
project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report were
performed in conformance with the general intent of the recommendations in the following
references
