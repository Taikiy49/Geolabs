PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this soil engineering investigation was to ex
plore the subsurface soil rock and groundwater conditions at
the site in order to provide data for use in the design of the
site grading for the proposed subdivision development
This investigation consisted of a review of previous soil
reports in the area geologic reconnaissance 10 test borings
12 test pits 13 seismic refraction surveys laboratory testing
and engineering analysis
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests which
were used in the engineering analysis that formulated our con
clusions and recommendations are presented in the appendix of
this report
The locations of the borings test pits and seismic survey pro
files are shown on the Boring Location Plan Plate 2 Informa
tion regarding the field exploration and laboratory testing are
presented in the appendices
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations

W O 1025 00 Page 3
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is situated along the top of Kamehame Ridge
just mauka of Koko Crater The ridge is between Kamiloiki
Valley on the west and Kalama Valley on the east as shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1
The topography in the area is irregular with isolated knolls and
small erosion gullies extending throughout the site The area
is vacant and covered with a heavy growth of haole koa and
keawe Access through the area is by a narrow paved access road
Drainage is towards Kalama Valley on the east Kamiloiki on the
west and Koko Crater to the south Overall relief ranges from
about 1 horizontal to 1 vertical west of the proposed 0 2 M G
reservoir site to about 8 horizontal to 1 vertical in the swale
leading to the central gully draining the site
The ground elevations vary from about 220 to 910 feet MSL
There is an existing 1 5 M G reservoir situated in the south
western portion of the site

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed subdivision development consists of approximately
234 single family residential lots along with the required
access roads
Extensive site grading with cuts as deep as 30 feet and fills up
to 10 feet in the subdivision area is anticipated
Fills up to about 50 feet high are planned in the steep sided
gully in the southeastern portion of the development area for a
proposed park site
Also included is a one story booster pump station at the exist
820 feet
GEOLOGY
The site is located on the southern flank of the Koolau Volcanic
Range at the eastern end of Oahu Hawaii
The volcanic range was formed during the Tertiary Period by
pahoehoe and aa basalt flows Later deep valleys like Kalama
and Kamiloiki were eroded leaving ridges e g Kamehame Ridge
of more resistant rock between During the late Pleistocene
Ice Age eruptions of ash and cinders Honolulu Volcanic
Series built large tuff consolidated volcanic ash cones like
Koko Head and Koko Crater
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1025 00 Page 5
Kamehameha Ridge is composed of seaward dipping about 10
degrees pahoehoe basalt flows with a few less than 10 of
total volume aa basalt flows Pahoehoe puka puka rock is a
smooth hummocky vesicular rock containing occasional lava
tubes and cavities Aa basalt consists of dense blocky core
rock surrounded by clinker Tuff is consolidated volcanic ash
Rock outcrops cover over 60 to 80 of the site
The rocks on the higher wetter portions of the site are highly
weathered and soil like saprolites Weathering tends to
decrease downslope and in areas protected from the tradewinds
There is a thin generally less than 1 to 2 foot thick patchy
soil cover on site Soil cover is only about 20 to 40 of the
site A few knolls have as much as 6 to 8 feet of residual
silty clay on them About 1 to 2 feet of residual soil overlies
part of the tuff in the southeastern portion of the site

Field Exploration
Ten borings were drilled at the site to depths of 10 5 to 35
feet below the existing ground surface
Due to the steep and rocky terrain at the site the boring loca
tions were limited to accessible areas along the existing road
which extends through the area

Soil Conditions
The field exploration disclosed that the site is covered with a
thin less than 2 foot thick surface soil layer of expansive
clay over rock formation
The rock formation generally consists of vesicular basalt with
varying degrees of weathering The basalt varies from a highly
weathered condition in the northern areas Unit III to slightly
weathered and hard in some of the southern areas Auger dril
ling was utilized in the softer highly weathered rock while dia
mond coring was required for the borings in the less weathered
rock

CONCLUSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration disclosed that the site is underlain at
very shallow depths by a rock formation
Some relatively hard excavation is anticipated in the southern
portion of the site Easier excavation is anticipated in the
more weathered upper northern portion
Even with the large gully fill it appears that the site grading
will produce an excess amount of excavated material which may
require off site hauling
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1025 00 Page 8
Only the smaller sized 12 inch minus excavated rocky material
should be used in the subdivision lot and roadway fills and the
larger boulders should be hauled off site or placed in the large
park site fill
Overcast boulders from the previous waterline construction were
noted on the slope below the existing access road and waterline
easement Within the limits of the development improvements
the loose boulders should be removed down to natural ground

Ground Preparation

and other deleterious material prior to filling
The loose overcast boulders within the limits of the
development improvements should be removed down to

Material and Compaction Requirements
The upper one to two foot surface soil is generally
expansive and should be utilized in the lower fills
more than 3 feet below the finished grade

Where the existing grade is at or near the design finish
grade the expansive soil should be overexcavated to a
minimum depth of 3 feet below the finish grade or to
competent rock formation whichever is less and replaced
with non expansive on site material compacted to 90 of
its maximum dry density
Most of the fill material will be generated from excava
tion in the rock formation
In general the excavated rock material less than 12
inches in size can be used as general fill material
Much of the fills will be along sloping areas which will
require these smaller materials that can be well com
pacted to provide the necessary stability of the fills
Many boulders will be generated from the rock excava
tion Boulders encountered in the excavation may be used
in the deep park site gully fills provided that the
following recommendations are followed
1 Boulders must not be nested together
They should be placed so that compaction
equipment would be able to compact around them
2 Large boulders should not be used within
3 feet from finish grade

3 No boulder larger than 5 feet in its largest

be necessary to reduce the size of larger boulders
or dispose of them off site
4 Care must be exercised to avoid placement of

The above specified rock fill should be placed in 18 inch

be conducted at the start of the project The number of
passes can then be used as the criteria for compaction

Excavation
The field exploration indicates that the site is under
lain by vesicular basalt formation at shallow depths
The vesicular basalt varies from highly weathered in the
northern Unit III area to slightly weathered in the
southern area It is our opinion that most of this
basalt formation can be ripped with a HD41 machine and
possibly even with a D 9 caterpillar tractor equipment
or similar However due to the variability of volcanic
rocks local harder rock layers should be anticipated and
may require special excavating techniques such as hoe
ram excavation
The above discussion regarding the rippability of the
underlying basalt materials at the site is based on our
field observations of existing rock slopes visual exami
nation of NX size rock cores obtained from drilled
borings and interpretation of the seismic refraction
survey data We recommend that all prospective con
tractors bidding on this site should examine the rock
cores recovered from our borings and make their own
interpretation The typical recovered rock cores and
soil samples will be stored in our laboratory up to
the time of the bid opening and are available to all
interested parties who wish to examine them

Slopes
Two horizontal to 1 vertical fill slopes are recommended
It appears that most of the cut slopes will be in the basalt
rock formation Cut slopes as steep as H V can be used
in dense unweathered basalt However due to the presence of
weathered zones and clinker layers which are typically encoun
tered and would require flatter slopes an average design cut
slope ratio of 1 1 H V is recommended for cuts into compe
tent rock formation
If required steeper cut slopes can be made in areas of hard
slightly to moderately weathered basalt and they should be
evaluated on an individual basis
In the northern portion of the site Unit III the rock forma
tion is highly weathered and the upper portions of the formation
have decomposed to soil Cut slope ratios of H V are
recommended in these residual soils
Volcanic tuff was encountered in the southeastern portion of the
Site Cut slope ratios of H V are recommended in the
volcanic tuff

Slope benches are recommended at maximum 20 foot vertical height
intervals
House Foundations
It is our understanding that some of the houses may be designed
and constructed by the individual lot buyers while others will
be constructed by the developer
Generally rock formation or compacted fill will be encountered
at the lot finish grade
An allowable bearing value of 6000 p s f can be used for foun
dations on dense weathered rock and 2500 p s f on undisturbed
clinker or compacted fill
On level or gently sloping lots the house foundations should
bear on the rock formation or extend a minimum of 12 inches into
compacted fill
Generally the perimeter lots are relatively steep with near
vertical rock outcrops in localized areas Therefore these
foundations must be embedded a sufficient depth to provide the
design bearing and lateral capacity In general the founda
tions should bear a minimum of 12 inches into the rock formation
GEOLABS HAWAIii
W O 1025 00 Page 14
or a sufficient depth into compacted fill to obtain a 6 foot
horizontal set back distance measured from the outer edge of
the footing to the slope face
The individual home owners should consult a qualified soils
engineer for the design of their house foundations and other
site regrading work which may be required
Reservoir Pump House

Rock formation is anticipated at foundation level An allowable

dense rock formation
Footings should be embedded a minimum of 12 inches into the rock
formation

Foundations
Spread or continuous footing foundations are recommended
for the reservoir structure
An allowable bearing value of 4000 p s f can be used for
footings resting on the dense weathered rock
Minimal settlement less than half an inch is
anticipated for the 0 2 million gallon reservoir
The footings should bear a minimum of 18 inches below the
adjacent finish grade The ground surface around the
footings should not be excavated or allowed to erode
below the base of the footing
If a footing is located next to a utility trench it
should extend down to the bottom of the utility trench
Surface water should be diverted away from the reservoir
foundations
Lateral Resistance
Lateral loads can be resisted by a combination of passive
soil pressure and frictional resistance
GEOLABS HAWAII

For passive earth pressure an equivalent fluid pressure
of 500 p c f per foot of depth can be used A friction
factor of 0 4 can be utilized to determine lateral
resistance of the structure and foundation

STANDARD GRADING SPECIFICATIONS

The work under this section includes clearing and grubbing pre
paration of fill areas placement and compaction of fill and
removal and backfilling of underground structures These spe
cifications are of a general nature and may be superseded by
specific requirements or recommendations within the soils
report
I Clearing
All areas within contract limit lines shall be cleared
of trash debris organic matter and all other dele
terious materials These materials shall be removed and
disposed outside of Hawaii Kai
II Preparation of Fill Areas
A Loose surface soils shall be removed to expose com
petent natural soils Localized soft pockets shall be
undercut and backfilled with compacted select materials
GEOLABS HAWAIl
Page 2
B All surfaces shall be inspected and approved by the
soils engineer prior to the placement of any fill
C After approval the surface shall be scarified to a
depth of twelve 12 inches brought to near optimum
moisture content and compacted as herein specified
D Where the existing surface exceeds a slope of 5
horizontal to 1 vertical 5 1 the ground surface shall
be keyed and benched into competent soil to bond the
Fill to the existing ground surface
E Subsurface drains may be required as directed by the
soils engineer where springs or zones of seepage are
encountered
Fill Materials
Material for fill shall consist of on site soils or
approved import material Import material shall be
tested and approved a minimum of seven 7 working days
prior to transportation to the site Fill material
shall contain less than 5 percent organic matter and
other deleterious material and shall not contain rocks
or lumps in excess of twelve inches 12 in diameter
GEOLABS HAWAII
IV
Page 3
Placement and Compaction of Fill
A The fi11 shall be placed in horizontal loose layers
not to exceed eighteen inches 18 in thickness Each
layer shall be spread uniformly and blade mixed to
attain uniformity of material and water content within
each layer
B Rock fragments or boulders less than twelve inches
12 in diameter may be utilized provided they are not
concentrated in any one area and that there is suffi
cient fine grained material to fill the voids between
the rocks
C Rocks greater than twelve inches 12 in diameter
shall be removed from the fill unless specifically
approved for placement in the fill by the soils engineer
D Ail ill shall be compacted to a minimum of 90 per
cent of the maximum dry density established by ASTM Test
Designation D 1557 unless otherwise specified in the
Soils report
GEOLABS HAWAI
Page 4
EB The compaction equipment may be sheepsfoot vibra
tory or pneumatic tire rollers suited to the soil type
and capable of producing the specified results in an
expedient and efficient manner The equipment shall be
in good working condition fully ballasted and self
cleaning
F The compaction shall be accomplished in a systematic
and uniform manner over the entire fill area to produce
a uniformly compacted fill
G Fill material placed in an unsatisfactory condition
and not in accordance with these specifications shall
not be approved and shall be removed or reworked until
the required density is obtained
H The soils engineer shall be provided unrestricted
access to the fill to perform the necessary inspections
and testing
Removal and Backfilling of Underground Structures
Underground structures such as cesspool cisterns lava
tubes septic tanks wells pipe lines etc shall be
demolished and removed as directed by the soils
engineer Backfilling of the excavations shall be in
accordance with these specifications

surface water
