FINE GRADING
In conducting our construction monitoring services during the fine grading
operations samples of the materials utilized as fill were obtained and transported to our
laboratory for testing The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were
established in general accordance with ASTM D1557 Modified Proctor and the test
results are summarized on Plate 3
Periodic in situ moisture and density tests were conducted in general accordance
with ASTM D6938 Nuclear Guage Method to determine the adequacy of compaction of
fills and backfills for infrastructure and utility installations including compaction of building
pads garages walkways and driveways
The fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to above optimum
moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry
density as determined in accordance with ASTM D1557 In the roadway areas the fill
and backfill materials within 3 feet of the finished grade were compacted to a minimum
95 percent of the maximum dry density Open non buildable or landscaping areas were
compacted to at least 85 percent of maximum dry density
The test results are summarized on Plates 4 1 through 4 27 These tests were
performed on moisture conditioned and compacted lifts of fills and backfills Test
locations and elevations were estimated using on site survey stakes for reference
These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree
implied by the methods used Areas deemed unsatisfactory were reworked until the
measured relative compaction conformed to or exceeded the minimum specified for the
project
