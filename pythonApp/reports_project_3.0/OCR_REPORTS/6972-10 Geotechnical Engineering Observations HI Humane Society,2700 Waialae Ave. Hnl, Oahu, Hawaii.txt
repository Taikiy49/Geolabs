Dear Ms. Burns: 
This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations and testing 
services for the site grading operation performed during construction at the Hawaiian 
Humane Society project located at 2700 Waialae Avenue in Honolulu, Oahu, Hawaii from 
September 2, 2015 to September 21, 2016 in accordance with our fee proposal dated 
January 9, 2015. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The existing Hawaiian Humane Society facility is bordered by H-1 Freeway on the 
northeast, Waialae Avenue on the southeast, Kehena Place on the southwest, and 
apartment properties on the northwest as shown on the Project Location Map and General 
Site Plan (Plates 1 and 2). The complex was constructed on terraced ground conditions 
with an eastern upper facility and western lower parking lots. Near-vertical rock slope was 
constructed along the northeast side of the facility adjacent to the H-1 Freeway. 
The project development was planned in three phases. Our geotechnical 
observation work was conducted for Phases 1 and 2, consisting of the grading and utility 
installation works for the construction of a two-story admissions building, a dog holding 
building a cat dormitory and parking lot. In addition, it also included the widening of 
Kehena Place (a City street), along the southwest boundary of the facility. 
Our monitoring services during construction consisted of moisture and density 
testing to determine adequacy of compaction of the fills and backfills for utility 
installations, building pads, walkways, driveways, and pavement construction.

This report summarizes our field observations and compaction testing services for 
the site grading operations and infrastructure installation for the Hawaiian Humane 
Society facility at 2700 Waialae Avenue Phases I and II project. The site Grading Plans 
are shown on Plates 3.1 through 3.4. 
SITE GRADING 
The General contractor was Kiewit Building Group, Inc. C.J. Petersen performed 
the earthwork operations for the project. The site grading for Phase-I and Phase-II of the 
Hawaiian Humane Society Project consisted of demolishing old existing structures and 
pavement, clearing and grubbing, cuts and fills generally on the order of about 2 to 
5 feet in order to create level areas for the facility development.  
The fill and backfill materials were moisture-conditioned to above optimum 
moisture content, and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry 
density as determined in accordance with ASTM D1557.  In the areas subjected to 
vehicular traffic, the fill and backfill materials within 3 feet of the finished subgrade were 
compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative density. 
In conducting our construction monitoring services during construction, samples 
of the materials utilized as fill were obtained and transported to our laboratory for 
testing.  The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established 
in general accordance with ASTM D1557 (Modified Proctor). A summary of the test 
results is presented on Plate 4. 
Periodic field density tests were performed on compacted fills and backfills as 
needed in general accordance with ASTM D6938 (Nuclear Gauge Method).  The test 
results are summarized on Plates 5.1 through 5.31. Test locations and elevations were 
estimated using on-site survey stakes for reference. These locations and elevations 
should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used. Areas 
assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the measured relative compaction 
conformed to or exceeded the minimum specified requirement for the project. 
Our services consisted of part-time field observation of the grading operations for 
subgrade preparation, fill and backfill placement, compaction procedures, and 
confirmation of subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above site grading 
operations. 
Based on our field representative’s observations and the referenced test results, 
it is our opinion that the site grading, utilities and infrastructure installation work for 
Phases 1 and 2 of the Hawaiian Humane Society facility development project at 
2700 Waialae Avenue in Honolulu, Oahu, Hawaii, observed and tested by Geolabs in the 
period covered by this report, were performed in accordance with the general intent of 
the recommendations in the following references.
