PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this soil engineering investigation is to explore the
subsurface soil and groundwater conditions along the proposed waterline
alignment at the proposed chlorination fluoridation building site and
at the proposed 1 0 M G reservoir site in order to provide soil
engineering recommendations for use in the design and construction of
the waterline and foundations of the appurtenant structures The
general project location is shown on the Project Location Plan Plate 1
The work was done in general accordance with our proposal dated May 14
1982
The results of our field explorations and laboratory tests which were
used in the engineering analysis to formulate our conclusions and
recommendations are presented in the appendices of this report
The approximate boring locations are shown on the Site Plans Plates 2
3 and 4 The boring logs and information regarding the field
explorations and laboratory testing are presented in the appendices

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The project site are located on the Mokapu Peninsula of the island of
Oahu The foundation of this peninsula was deposited during the Kahipa
low stand of the sea 300 feet by several vents of the Honolulu
Volcanic Series These vents laid down extensive deposits of
pyroclastic materials in the form of ash and cinder with some
associated lava flows
Since the deposition of these materials during Kahipa time sea level
has risen and fallen several times as a result of glacio eustatic
fluctuations These changes in sea level have caused the deposition of
coral algal reef materials and littoral shoreline deposits over
portions of the peninsula
During the past century the efforts of man have shaped the land to its
present form by placement of fill in low areas

Earth Pressure Loads on Pipe
Loads on buried conduits are influenced by the width of the
trench the size of the pipe the unit weight of backfill
material and the friction resistance between the backfill
material and the trench walls To compute the vertical loads on
the pipes a unit weight of 115 p c f and a coefficient of
friction of 0 25 is recommended Earth forces upon the pipe
increase rapidly with the width of the trench therefore the
width of the trench Bd should be kept to a minimum
The attached Plate 5 indicates three alternate trench types
Bearing Capacity and Settlement
Medium dense to dense sandy soils which were encountered along
the alignment have good bearing capacity
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1180 00 Page 10
The new sewer line construction in general will not materially
increase the vertical loading upon these underlying soils if no
additional fills are placed upon the existing ground surface
The resulting net loading between the weight of the excavated
material and backfill will be essentially small therefore
Significant settlements are not anticipated if proper
construction techniques are utilized
Pipe Bedding
a cmt nk me
The stress distribution against the bottom of a pipe has a great
effect upon the load supporting capacity of the pipe therefore
the pipe bedding is an important design consideration
A minimum of six inches of compacted granular bedding material
should be placed below the pipe The granular bedding should
extend beyond the pipe sides a minimum width of one fourth the
outside pipe diameter and should extend halfway up the pipe
sides The bedding material should consist of clean granular
material such as 3B Fine gravel

Dewatering
8 ee me om ome a sa ae
It appears that dewatering will be required along the alignment
The method of dewatering should be left open to the contractor
provided adequate stability of the excavation is maintained The
deposits encountered in the borings along this portion of the
alignment consisted mostly of silty sands which are relatively
permeable and susceptible to piping and quick conditions
The project dewatering operation should be done without pumping
out the soil fines pumping clear water only It should be
coordinated with the excavation support system so that the
stability of the excavation is not jeopardized The operations
should be carried out without creating a quick condition or
softening the bottom of the excavation
Another alternative to dewatering would be to place the pipe in a
wet trench condition
Backfill
Granular material such as sand should be used to backfill the
utility trench up to about one foot above the pipe to reduce the
compactive effort required and possible damage to the pipe
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1180 00 Page 13
The excavated on site soil can be reused in the upper portion of
the trench backfill from above one foot over the Pipe to finished
grade Backfill should be mechanically compacted to a minimum of
90 of its maximum dry density established by ASTM D 1557 78
If practicable the trenches should be kept free of water during
backfilling Should backfill under the water level be necessary
granular material such as base course rock should be utilized
to minimize excessive ground settlement of the trench backfill in
the future

Foundations
Spread or continuous footing foundations are recommended for the
structure An allowable bearing value of 3000 p s f may be used
on the dense sandy soils Footings should be embedded a mininun

The footing excavation may loosen the surface of the sandy soils
therefore the bottom of the footing excavations should be
recompacted to 95 of its maximum dry density prior to concrete
placement
Minimal settlements are anticipated for the structure
Dewatering
Water level was encountered at about 5 foot depth in this area
therefore dewatering is anticipated for the building excavation
which will extend about 8 to 10 feet below the existing ground
surface Dense sand was encountered below the water level in
this area The sand deposit is relatively permeable and will be
susceptible to loosening if improper pumping methods are adopted
The project dewatering operation should be done without pumping
out the soil fines pumping clear water only The dewatering
method should be coordinated with the excavation support systems
so that the stability of the excavation is not jeopardized The
operations should be carried out without creating a quick
condition or softening the excavation bottom
The sand deposit at this site has minimal cementation and will be
subjected to sloughing We believe that sheeting and bracing
will be required for the building excavation

We believe that proper dewatering and trench bracing is critical
for the construction of the chlorination fluoridation building
Poor dewatering and trench bracing operations would loosen the
surrounding soil thereby reducing its supporting capacity for
the remainder of the building which will be supported on grade

Site Grading
The site should be cleared of all surface vegetation debris and

Prior to fill placement any existing disturbed and loose surface
soil sould be excavated down to stiff material Then fill
should be placed in maximum 9 inch thick loose horizontal lifts

Fills placed on slopes having grades steeper than 5 horizontal to
1 vertical 5 1 should be keyed and benched into the existing
stiff ground see Plate 7 for details
Two horizotal to 1 vertical 2 1 fill Slopes are recommended
The in situ weathered cinder formation is generally very dense
However it has minimal cementation and is highly susceptible to
erosion A steep cut slope of 1 horizontal to 1 vertical may be
used in this material if the following precautions are taken to
minimize erosion
1 Near the finished grade the slopes should be cut by a
backhoe rather than a dozer with extreme care by the
contractor Overcutting past the design slope must be
avoided since this would create a very loose surface layer
which would be easily eroded
2 The slope surface should be sprayed with a sufficient amount
of chemical soil stabilizer which could bind or cement the
surface soil and create a minimum l inch thick erosion
resistant membrane We would be glad to provide
specifications for the chemical soil stabilizer if desired
3 The slope should be planted immediately ideally in
conjunction with the chemical spraying

5 Slope benches should be provided at 35 foot maximum vertical
