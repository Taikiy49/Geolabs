SOIL COMPACTION TESTING REPORT
UTILITY CONSTRUCTION AT KUNIA LOT H 5 A
This report summarizes the results of our soil compaction testing performed during
the earthwork operations at the above referenced project from March 19th through
May 9th in general accordance with the intent of the project plans and specifications
BACKGROUND INFORMATION
The proposed project is located at the intersection of Kunia Road and Kupuna
Loop in the Waipahu area on the island of Oahu Hawaii as shown on the Site Plan
Plate 1 It is proposed to construct a fast food restaurant at the project site The grading
work at the site had been completed under a separate contract Based on the information
provided the proposed construction under this scope of work included construction of
utilities driveways and parking lots Therefore the earthwork for the proposed
construction consisted of trench excavations trench backfills preparation of pavement
subgrades and placement of select borrow and base course
SOIL TESTING AND OBSERVATIONS
The earthwork operations were performed by Koga Engineering as a
subcontractor to S M Sakamoto Inc At the on set of earthwork the area within the
contract grading limits was generally cleared and grubbed of vegetation and unsuitable
materials Materials generated from the clearing operations were disposed of off site

The backfill materials needed for the earthwork consisted of on site material
generated from the cut operation and select borrow material imported from Kapaa
Quarry Samples of the fill materials utilized as backfill materials were obtained and
transported to our office for laboratory testing The maximum dry densities and optimum
moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general accordance with
American Society of Testing and Material ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified
Proctor and the test results are summarized on Plate 2 of this report
During the backfill operation the backfill materials were generally placed in
near level lifts brought to above the optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of
90 percent of the maximum dry density determined in accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor In general for areas subjected to vehicular traffic
the backfill materials within 2 feet of the finished grades were compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative compaction Periodic field density tests were taken during the backfill
operations to confirm the degree of compaction and moisture content of the backfill
material achieved during earthwork construction
A total of 66 field density tests were performed using the nuclear probe method in
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 2922 and D 3017 The results of the
compaction tests performed during the backfilling operations are summarized on
Plates 3 1 through 3 3 of this report These tests were performed on the compacted
backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and
interpolating from on site survey stakes where available These locations and elevations
should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used In
general areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum
compaction was obtained It should be noted that the test results only reflect the
compaction and moisture condition at the time and locations the tests were performed
Based on the results of the referenced tests and observations of our field
representatives during our site visits it is our opinion that the earthwork operations tested
and observed by Geolabs Hawaii were performed in general accordance with the intent of
the project plans and specifications
LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that Geolabs Hawaii did not perform full time construction
monitoring for the proposed project site but rather performed at an on call basis The
majority of earthwork at the site occurred without our observation In addition it should
be recognized that there are variations in the accuracy and statistical repeatability of the
tests and methods used to monitor the earthwork construction and materials Our
services were performed in accordance with generally accepted standards of
geotechnical engineering practice no warranty is expressed or implied Provision of
