This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observation and testing 
services performed during the site grading of the Residence at Lot 38 project from 
June 22, 2016 through August 22, 2017 in accordance with our fee proposal dated 
December 19, 2014.  
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The project involves a planned single-family residence on Lot 38 within Kukui`ula 
Development Company’s M2/M3 Subdivision in Koloa on the Island of Kauai, Hawaii. 
This residential project consists of a two-story house and garage structure, swimming 
pool, and other related site improvements. In addition, immediately south of the 
proposed structure, the lot is planned to be leveled by raising the lot grades up to about 
6 feet. A Project Location Map and Site Plan are shown on Plates 1 and 2, respectively. 
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services 
performed during the site grading at the project site. 
SITE GRADING 
We observed the site grading work for the project from June 22, 2016 through 
August 22, 2017. Site grading for the project generally consisted of excavations up to about 
5 feet deep for building/retaining wall foundations and swimming pool construction, and fills 
and backfills up to about 6 feet thick to achieve the finished design grades.  
At the on-set of earthwork, the area within the limits of grading was cleared and 
grubbed of unsuitable surface materials. PacBuild, LLC was the General Contractor for the

During the site grading operations, samples of the on-site soils and imported fill materials 
were obtained and transported to our laboratory for testing. The maximum dry densities 
and optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general accordance 
with ASTM D1557 (Modified Proctor), and the test results are summarized on Plate 3.  
The fill and backfill materials we observed consisting of on-site soils were 
moisture-conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum 
of 90 percent relative compaction. Select borrow material imported from Kauai Aggregates 
was used below the swimming pool, slabs-on-grade, and footings. The select borrow was 
moisture-conditioned to near the optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum 
of 90 percent relative compaction. Select borrow material imported from Kauai Aggregates 
was used below the driveway and was moisture-conditioned to near the optimum moisture 
content and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction. Relative 
compaction refers to the in-place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the 
maximum dry density as determined by ASTM D1557. Optimum moisture is the water 
content (percentage by dry weight) corresponding to the maximum dry density. 
During the site grading operations, periodic field density tests were performed in 
general accordance with ASTM D6938 (Nuclear Gauge Method). Compaction test results 
are summarized on Plates 5.1 through 5.5. Test locations and elevations were estimated 
using on-site survey stakes for reference. These locations and elevations should be 
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used. Areas that did not 
meet the required moisture and density conditions were reworked until the required 
minimum compaction were obtained or exceeded. 
Our services consisted of part-time, as-requested field observation and testing of 
the contractor’s grading activities which included foundation subgrade preparation, fill and 
backfill placement, and confirmation of subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above 
grading operations. 
Based on our field representative’s observations and the referenced test results, it is 
our opinion that the site grading work for the Residence at Lot 38 project, observed and 
tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report, were performed in conformance 
with the general intent of the recommendations in the following references.
