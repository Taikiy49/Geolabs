SUBDIVISION LOT GRADING
Prior to the recent project site grading operations most of Lot 63
was covered with a large stockpile of soil and boulders left over from
previous grading operations in the area
During the site grading operation the stockpile was removed down
to a depth of 6 feet below the design finished grade and replaced
with compacted available on site soils The available on site soil
consisted basically of silty clay and clayey silt soils which has been
mixed with some Adobe clay pockets from the previous stockpile
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
A review of the grading compaction test record and the site recon
naissance indicates that Lot 63 is underlain by grayish brown expan

Detailed description of the soils encountered in the boring are pre

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that the lot is underlain by grayish
brown clay fill The laboratory test results on this material indi
cated that they have high expansion potential Therefore for Lot 63
the slab on grade and foundation construction would require special
design procedures in order to reduce the shrink swell effect of the
subsoils
House Foundation
Slab on grade is planned for the garage and basement floor level
The on site clay soils are expansive and unsuitable as slab subgrade
soils In order to reduce the expansive effects of the soils the
expansive clays within a depth of 3 feet below the slab subgrade level
should be over excavated and replaced with non expansive borrow soils
such as select borrow crusher waste or coral compacted to a minimum
of 90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 The over excavation
should extend for a minimum distance of 3 feet beyond the slabs
A 4 inch layer of base course should be provided below the slab and
compacted to 90 of its maximum dry density In order to protect
against moisture infiltration a polyethylene moisture barrier may be
incorporated below the slab The membrane should be protected by a
thin layer of sand to aid in the concrete curing

The thickened edge slab footings can bear on the compacted non
expansive fill The thickened edges should be embedded a minimum
of 12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f may be used on the compacted
fill
Surface water should be diverted away from the foundations
Alternate Foundation System
We recommend that the proposed house foundation system be re evaluated
in an effort to reduce the foundation construction costs
For this lot post and beam construction is highly desirable and
recommended due to the sloping terrain and expansive soil conditions
The post and beam construction generally results in lower foundation
costs and the individual footings can be readily adjusted should the
need occur
For post and beam construction we recommend that the expansive clays
be over excavated down for a depth of 3 feet below the bottom of the
individual post footings and then replaced with non expansive borrow
soils such as crusher waste select borrow or coral compacted to a
minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 70 The
spread footings can then be placed on the compacted non expansive soil

Alternatively the individual spread footings can be embedded a
minimum of 3 feet below the finished ground surface
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f can be used for footings
bearing on the compacted select material or stiff existing ground
The use of post and beam construction could also eliminate the
basement retaining wall presently planned
Basement Retaining Wall Foundation
Basement retaining walls up to 6 feet high are planned in the north
western portion of the house
In order to reduce the expansive effects of the existing clay soils
on the wall we recommend that the expansive clays be over excavated
down for a depth of 2 feet below the bottom of the footing and then
replaced with non expansive borrow soils such as crusher waste
select borrow or coral compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum
dry density per ASTM D 1557 The over excavation and replacement
should extend a minimum distance of 12 inches beyond the footing width
The wall footing should be embedded a minimum depth of 12 inches below
the outside finished grade Therefore the total depth of the above
over excavation and replacement would be 3 0 feet below the outside
finished grade

An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f can be used
In order to reduce the build up of high lateral earth pressures on the
wall due to the existing clay soils off site non expansive soil
should be used as backfill for a minimum distance of 4 feet behind the
wall
The walls may be designed to resist lateral soil pressure of 35 p c f
equivalent fluid pressure
A coefficient of friction of 0 4 between the bottom of the footing and
compacted soil can be used to determine the lateral resistance of the
wall
A subsurface drainage system should be utilized behind the wall to
prevent the build up of hydrostatic pressure
Concrete Driveway and Sidewalks
Expansive clay soils were encountered along the proposed driveway
Since the driveways can tolerate some amount of heaving the clay
over excavation and replacement can be reduced to 2 feet below the
driveway subgrade level Where 2 or more feet of fill is planned
select non expansive borrow material should be used to cap over the
expansive clay

W Page 8
Where less than 2 feet of fill is planned over excavation and
replacement of the expansive clay with select non expansive material
is required The above requirements should be followed for a minimum
distance of 3 feet beyond the driveway
Since a relatively long driveway is planned joints are recommended
at about 8 to 10 foot intervals in order to allow for some relative
movement of the concrete
For future sidewalks capping of the clay or over excavation and
replacement similar to the driveway is recommended to reduce heaving
caused by the expansive clay soil
Site Grading
The existing terrain at the site generally slopes down at about 6
grade or flatter
Up to 6 feet of cut in the basement and 4 feet of fill in the garage
are anticipated
The area to be filled should be cleared of vegetaton and debris prior
to filling

The finish grade outside the slabs pavements and foundations should

Plans for foundation site grading and utility construction should be

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during earthwork
construction particularly the fill placement slope cuts and to
monitor the contractor s grading operations
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be observed by a
soils engineer during construction to see that adequate bearing has
been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with
the findings of our subsurface investigation
