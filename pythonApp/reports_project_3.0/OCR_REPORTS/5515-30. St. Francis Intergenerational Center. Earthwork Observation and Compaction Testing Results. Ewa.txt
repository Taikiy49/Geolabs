Project Considerations
The project site consists of a Saint Francis Intergenerational Day Care Center
facility at the corner of Renton Road and O ohao Street in the Ewa area on the Island of

foundation system Infrastructure includes roadways underground utilities e g water
sewer electric communications and lighting as well as parking areas
The site grading consisted of shallow cuts and fills to achieve the design finished
grades
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services for
the earthwork site grading and utility construction at the project site

Earthwork Site Grading and Utility Construction
The earthwork generally consisted of minor cuts and fills to level the site for the
building and parking area construction Royal Contracting performed the grading work
During the earthwork and related operations samples of the on site and imported
materials utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office
for testing The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established
in our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor The
laboratory test results are summarized on Plate 2
The fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to near optimum moisture
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as
determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557 In the roadway areas the fill and backfill
materials within 2 feet of the finished subgrade were compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative density In addition select borrow and base course materials below
the pavement areas were compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative density
During the earthwork periodic field density tests were performed in general
accordance with ASTM D 6938 Nuclear Gauge Method Results of the compaction tests
performed are summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 8 of this report These tests were
performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills at the
site Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site
survey stakes These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the
degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked
