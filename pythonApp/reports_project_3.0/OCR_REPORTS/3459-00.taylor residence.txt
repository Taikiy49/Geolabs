INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering reconnaissance
performed for the proposed Taylor Residence located at 1140 Ikena Circle at Hawaii
Loa Ridge in the area of east Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general
location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering recommenda
tions resulting from our site reconnaissance laboratory testing and engineering
analyses The recommendations are intended for design of foundation site grading
and pavement only The findings and recommendations presented herein are subject
to the limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a new residence at 1140 Ikena Circle at Hawaii Loa
Ridge in the east Honolulu area on the island of Oahu Hawaii Based on the
information provided the proposed residence will consist of a two story structure with
slab on grade construction Construction of the new residence will involve a retaining
wall In addition a new driveway and utility lines are also planned for the proposed
project
Our site visit indicated that the existing ground has been excavated down to near
the proposed finish grade Therefore we anticipate that minimal cuts and fills on the
order of 2 to 3 feet relative to the existing ground would be required to attain the
finished grades for the proposed residence
Our experience in the area suggests that the project site may be underlain by
residual soils over decomposed basalt rock Therefore special attention was given to

future floor slabs supported on grade

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from two shield
volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be of
middle to late Pliocene in age and forms the bulk of the western third of the island The
younger shield Koolau is estimated to be of late Pliocene to early Pleistocene Ice
Age and forms the majority of the eastern two thirds of the island Waianae became
extinct while Koolau was still active and its eastern flank was partially buried below
Koolau lavas banking against its eastern flank
The project area is located on the southeastern flank of Koolau and is composed
of basaltic rock built by the extrusion of the lavas of the Koolau Volcanic Series These
rocks are generally characterized by flows of jointed dense vesicular basalt with
interbedded thin clinker layers In situ chemical weathering of the Koolau lava flows
has occurred for the last 1 to 2 million years The weathering process has formed a
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3459 00 Page 5
mantle of residual soils which grade to saprolite with depth In general saprolite is
composed mainly of silty material and is typical of the tropical weathering of volcanic
rocks The saprolite grades to basaltic rock formation with depth
EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is located at 1140 Ikena Circle at Hawaii Loa Ridge in the east
Honolulu area on the island of Oahu Hawaii The subject site is bounded by Kokololio
Place to the north Ikena Circle to the west an existing residential lot to the east and
a vacant lot to the south
A site reconnaissance was conducted on May 31st to observe the surface
condition of the site At the time of our site reconnaissance the site had been
excavated down to near the lower level of the proposed residence Based on our
observations the project site slopes down toward the south from the north boundary
prior to excavation The excavation ranges from about 10 feet in height at the north
boundary to less than 1 foot near the south boundary Stockpiles of basalt gravels
were noted at the northeast corner of the project site
Based on our observations of the exposed face of the excavation the project site
is generally underlain by residual soils consisting of stiff reddish brown to brown silty
clays over saprolite soils consisting of brown clayey silts with highly weathered basalt
fragments in localized areas at relatively shallow depths A number of shrinkage cracks
were noted on the cut face at the time of our reconnaissance Dry and loose surface
soils were observed at the excavated level based on probing by a steel rod which
indicated about 3 to 5 inches of penetration below the ground surface
Five near surface soil samples were retrieved at the project site and returned to
our office for laboratory testing The approximate locations of the surface samples

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our site reconnaissance the site is underlain by stiff residual soils
consisting of silty clays over saprolite soils at relatively shallow depths Based on the
competent soil conditions observed at the project site it is our opinion that a shallow
foundation system consisting of spread and continuous footings bearing on the stiff
in situ soils and or compacted fills may be used to support the proposed residence
Laboratory tests performed on the on site clayey soils indicate that the near
surface materials generally have a high shrink swell potential when subjected to
moisture fluctuations Therefore we recommend that concrete slabs on grade be
supported on a minimum 36 inch thick layer of non expansive capping fill to reduce the
potential shrink swell effects of the near surface expansive soils when subjected to
moisture fluctuations In addition the clayey subgrades should be properly moisture
conditioned and compacted prior to placement of the non expansive capping fill
Detailed discussions and recommendations for design of foundations site
grading pavements and other geotechnical aspects of the project are presented in the
following sections of this report
Foundations
Based on our site reconnaissance and laboratory tests we believe that a shallow
foundation system consisting of spread and or continuous footings may be used to
support the proposed residence An allowable bearing pressure of up to 3 000 p s f
may be used for the design of footings bearing on the very stiff in situ soils and or
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3459 00 Page 7
compacted engineered fill materials This allowable bearing pressure is for dead plus
live loads and may be increased by one third 1 for transient loads such as temporary
wind and or seismic forces Footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches
below the lowest adjacent finished grade We estimate that total footing settlements
under the anticipated design loads to be on the order of 1 inch with differential
settlements of approximately inch
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by frictional resistance
between the base of the footings and the bearing materials and by passive earth
pressure developed against the near vertical faces of the embedded portion of footings
A coefficient of friction of 0 3 may be used between the base of the footings and the
supporting materials Resistance due to passive pressure may be calculated using an
equivalent fluid pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth p c f for
footings embedded in the in situ soils and or compacted engineered fill materials
These values assume that the soils around footings are well compacted or undisturbed
Unless covered by pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the upper 12 inches
below finished grade should be neglected
Where footings are located adjacent to or on slopes the footings should be
embedded deep enough to provide a minimum horizontal set back distance of at least
6 feet measured from the edge of the footing to the face of the slope Where footings
are to be located adjacent to below grade structures or utilities the footings should
extend to a depth below an imaginary 45 degree plane projected upward from the
bottom of the below grade structure or utility
It is recommended that the bottom of all footings bear on the in situ stiff
clayey silty soils or compacted engineered fill materials If soft and or loose materials
are encountered at the bottom of footing excavations they should be over excavated
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3459 00 Page 8
to expose firm materials The over excavation should either be backfilled with granular
material compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction or the footing
bottom be extended to the undisturbed firm in situ soil Footing excavations should not
be allowed to dry before placing concrete If shrinkage cracks appear in the footing
excavations the soil should be thoroughly moistened to close all cracks and
recompacted prior to concrete placement Footing excavations should be observed by
a representative of Geolabs Hawaii prior to the placement of reinforcing steel or
concrete to confirm the competency of the bearing materials and the embedment
depths
Slabs On Grade
We anticipate that concrete slabs on grade will be used for the proposed
residence The near surface soils observed at the proposed project site consist of
clayey soils which exhibit high expansion potential when subjected to fluctuations in
the moisture content Unless floor slabs supported by these soils are properly
designed there is a potential for future distress to lightly loaded slabs on grade
resulting from the shrinking and swelling of these soils due to changes in the moisture
content
To reduce the potential of structural distress resulting from the swelling of the
highly expansive on site soils we recommend that a minimum of 36 inches of non
expansive capping fill material be provided below slabs on grade The non expansive
capping fill should extend a minimum of 2 feet laterally beyond the limits of the
proposed building structure
The non expansive capping fill should provide a capping layer of moist soil above
the underlying potentially expansive subgrade soil thereby reducing the loss of
moisture from the clayey subgrade soils In our opinion the reduction in potential
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3459 00 Page 9
drying followed by subsequent wetting of the subgrade soils should reduce the distress
on the slab due to potential heaving of the underlying expansive clayey soils when
wetted
The non expansive capping fill material should consist of imported granular
materials such as crushed coral basalt gravel or cinder sand The material should be
well graded with no particles greater than 3 inches in size and should have a
laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR value of 15 or higher and a swell value not
exceeding 1 percent when prepared above the optimum moisture
In general we recommend that the capping fill material be placed in level lifts not
exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness moisture conditioned to above the optimum
moisture content and compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction
Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage
of the maximum dry density of the same soil determined in accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 1557 91 Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by weight
corresponding to the maximum dry density
The non expansive capping fill should be placed as soon as possible after the
scarification and compaction of the underlying subgrade soils The subgrade should
be scarified to a minimum depth of 8 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent
above the optimum moisture content and compacted to at least 90 percent relative
compaction Soil subgrades should be kept moist prior to placement of capping fill
and or concrete If shrinkage cracks appear in the subgrades the soil should be
thoroughly moistened to close all cracks and recompacted prior to capping fill or
concrete placement
For more uniform support we recommend that slabs on grade be supported on
a 4 inch cushion layer consisting of open graded gravels such as No 3B Fine gravel
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3459 00 Page 10
ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation The cushion layer should be placed above the non
expansive capping fill and would also serve as a capillary break The slab subgrade
should be moistened prior to concrete placement to allow the capping fill layer to
remain in a moist condition To reduce moisture infiltration through the slab a plastic
vapor membrane may be considered above the cushion layer A 2 inch thick moist fine
sand layer should be placed on the plastic vapor membrane to protect the membrane
and to aid in the curing of concrete
For concrete slabs that will be subjected to vehicular traffic such as the
driveways and garage slabs we recommend that the slabs be supported on a 6 inch
layer of compacted base course in lieu of the 4 inch cushion fill and 2 inch sand The
thickened edges of slabs adjacent to any unpaved area should be embedded at least
12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade The base course should consist of crushed
basalt aggregate compacted to not less than 95 percent relative compaction
Retaining Walls
Retaining walls are anticipated at some locations of the project site for grade
separation and for the house wall fronting Kokololio Place We understand that the
retaining walls are expected to be conventional concrete retaining walls General
guidelines for the design of the retaining wall structures are as follows
1 Foundations for retaining walls should be designed in general accordance
with the recommendations presented in the Foundations section of this
report
2 All retaining structures should be designed to resist the lateral earth
pressure due to adjacent soils and surcharge effects An active lateral
earth pressure of 40 pounds per cubic foot p c f expressed in
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3459 00 Page 11
equivalent fluid pressure is recommended for design of cantilever walls
For at rest earth pressure a value of 55 p c f equivalent fluid pressure
may be used
The value provided above is for on site soils or imported granular soils
Backfills behind retaining walls should be moisture conditioned to at least
2 percent above the optimum moisture content and compacted to between
90 and 95 percent relative compaction Over compaction of the wall
backfill should be avoided This lateral earth pressure does not include
hydrostatic pressures that might be caused by groundwater trapped
behind the walls
3 The surcharge effect due to traffic areal surcharges line loads and point
loads within a horizontal distance equal to the height of the wall should be
considered in the design For uniform surcharge stresses imposed on the
loaded side of the wall a rectangular distribution over the height of the
wall with uniform pressure equal to 40 percent of the vertical surcharge
pressure acting on the entire height of the wall which is free to deflect
cantilever may be used in the design For walls that are restrained at
the top the surcharge stresses should be taken as 55 percent of the
vertical surcharge pressure
4 For resistance to lateral loads a passive earth pressure of 300 p c f may
be used A base friction factor of 0 3 may be used in conjunction with the
passive earth pressure to evaluate the resistance of the walls against
sliding
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3459 00 Page 12
5 Retaining walls should be well drained to reduce the build up of hydro
static pressures A typical drainage system would consist of a 1 to 2 foot
wide zone of permeable material No 3B Fine gravel or ASTM C 33
No 67 gradation immediately adjacent to the wall with a perforated pipe
perforations down at the base of the wall discharging to a discharge
facility A layer of filter fabric such as Mirafi 140N or equivalent should
be placed between the drainage material and the compacted backfill
material The drainage material should extend from the base of the wall
to a height within 2 feet below finish grade The remaining backfill should
consist of an impervious capping of select non expansive soil to reduce
water infiltration behind the walls
Site Grading
Since the proposed building location has been excavated down to near the finish
grade we anticipate minimal cuts and fills on the order of about 2 to 3 foot relative to
the excavated ground surface would be required to achieve the final design finish
elevations for the proposed construction
We recommend that the area within the contract grading limits be grubbed again
All loose materials exposed on the excavated ground level and cut faces should be
removed prior to the new construction After re grubbing all areas beneath future slabs
should be over excavated to a depth of at least of 36 inches below the future slab
subgrade level such as the bottom of gravel cushion layer If zones of soft loose or
saturated soils are encountered at the over excavated subgrade level additional over
excavation may be required to expose firm material The resulting excavation should
be backfilled with non expansive fill material The clayey material resulting from the
over excavation should be removed and properly disposed of off site
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3459 00 Page 13
As previously indicated proper moisture conditioning of the highly expansive soil
is critical Therefore areas to receive fills should be scarified to a depth of 8 inches
moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to
the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density
of the same soil determined in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91
Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the
maximum dry density Fill material should be placed in level lifts not exceeding
8 inches in loose thickness moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture content
and compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction Any imported fill
material should be tested and approved for its intended use by Geolabs Hawaii prior
to delivery to the project site
Drainage
The finished grades outside the house should be sloped to shed water away
from foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding Also it is advised that gutter
systems be installed around the house and that the discharge be diverted away from
the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near the
foundations and slabs should also be avoided Planters next to foundations should be
avoided or have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the
subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil
saturation and subsequent heaving because of the high swelling potential of the near
surface soils Saturation of the near surface soils can also cause settlement due to
softening of the soils The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled against
slab footings immediately after setting of the concrete to reduce water infiltration
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3459 00 Page 14
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and maintained to drain
all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundations

the access driveway We anticipate the vehicle loading for this project will consist
primarily of passenger vehicles and light pick up trucks We have made our preliminary
pavement designs assuming the pavement subgrade soil will be similar to the clayey
soils observed at the project site with a minimum CBR of 4 and a swell value of less
than 9 percent Based on the above assumptions we recommend the following
pavement section be used for preliminary design purposes

Utility Trench
We envision that new utility connections may be required for the project A
granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33
No 67 gradation is recommended under the pipes Free draining granular materials
such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation should also be used for
the trench backfill up to about 12 inches above the pipes to provide adequate support
around the pipes and to reduce the compaction effort of the backfill thus reducing the
possibility of damaging the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipes
to the top of the subgrade or finished grade should consist of select granular material
or on site soils The backfill should be placed in about 8 inch loose level lifts moisture
conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture content and
mechanically compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the
potential for future ground subsidence Where trenches are below pavement areas the
upper 2 feet of the trench backfill below the pavement subgrade should be compacted
to not less than 95 percent relative compaction
