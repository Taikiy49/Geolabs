In accordance with our proposal dated March 17 2004 we have performed a
geotechnical engineering exploration and field percolation testing program within
Kohala Elementary School in the area of North Kohala on the Island of Hawaii This letter
report summarizes the findings from our field exploration and percolation testing at the
proposed leach field locations and presents our findings and discussions for the proposed
project These findings and discussions are intended for design input only and are subject to
the limitations noted at the end of this letter report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located within the Kohala High and Elementary School in the area
of North Kohala on the Island of Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site
are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
We understand that septic tanks and leach fields will be constructed at the project site
Based on the information provided it is proposed to construct two leach fields at the project
site The first leach field Leach Field No 1 will be located to the west of Building M
cafeteria near the playcourts The second leach field Leach Field No 2 will be located to
the south of Building B near the playground equipment
To evaluate the permeability of the underlying ground surface percolation tests were
conducted at the two proposed locations The results of the percolation tests will be used to
assist in the design of the new leach fields The proposed leach field locations are shown on
the Site Plan Plate 2
Further details of the proposed leach fields were not available at the time this report
was prepared Based on our knowledge and experience in the vicinity of the project site we
anticipate that the project site may be underlain by volcanic ash soils consisting of clays and
silts overlying weathered basalt rock formation at deeper depths

PURPOSE AND SCOPE

conditions at the proposed leach field locations and to perform field percolation tests The
subsurface information obtained was used to evaluate the subsoil infiltration characteristics
for the formulation of preliminary recommendations pertaining to the design of the new
leach fields The work was performed in general accordance with our fee proposal dated
March 17 2004 Our scope of work included the following tasks and work efforts

to the design of the new leach fields
4 Coordination of the field exploration logging of the borings and performance

EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is located within Kohala High and Elementary School in the North
Kohala area on the Island of Hawaii The project site is adjacent to the north side of
Akoni Pule Highway The location of the project site is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Leach Field No 1 is located to the west of the cafeteria Building M The area is
next to the outdoor playcourts The existing ground surface gradually slopes down toward
Building M with ground surface elevations ranging from about 86 to 97 feet Mean Sea
Level MSL The ground surface consists of lawn grass
Leach Field No 2 is located at the south side of Building B near the playground
equipment The area is relatively level with ground surface elevations ranging from about
94 to 99 feet MSL The ground surface consists of lawn grass
FIELD EXPLORATION
Our field exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling two borings
designated as Boring Nos 1 and 2 extending to depths of about 19 and 21 5 feet below the
existing ground surface Boring No 1 was drilled at the proposed Leach Field No 1 location
and Boring No 2 was drilled at the proposed Leach Field No 2 location The borings were
drilled using a truck mounted drill rig equipped with continuous flight solid stem augers The
approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by testing in
the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on the Boring Log Legend Plate 3 Graphic representations
of the materials encountered are provided on the Logs of Borings Plates 4 and 5
Soil samples were obtained in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation
D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 3 inch OD Modified California
sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches In addition some samples were
obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1586
Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 2 inch OD standard
penetration sampler using the same hammer and drop The blow counts needed to drive the
sampler the second and third 6 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown as the Penetration
Resistance on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths

FIELD PERCOLATION TESTING
Field percolation tests were performed in the borings in general accordance with the
Wastewater Systems and Chapter 10 of the Ten States Standards To evaluate the subsoil
profile and permeability of the subsurface soils for wastewater disposal the percolation tests
were performed by drilling 4 inch diameter boreholes extending to depths ranging from about
4 to 9 2 feet below the existing ground surface
Approximately 1 to 2 inches of gravel were then placed at the bottom of the boreholes
to protect the bottom of the holes from scouring and sediments During the test the holes
were carefully filled with water to a height of 6 inches above the gravel pack The time
intervals and water drops were then recorded to provide data upon which calculation of the
percolation rates were based The results of the individual percolation tests are presented on
Plates 6 through 9 of this report
LABORATORY TESTING
The following laboratory tests were performed on selected soil samples obtained from
the field exploration to aid in classifying the materials encountered
Moisture Content ASTM D 2216 and Unit Weight ASTM D 2937 determinations
were performed on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil
properties The results of these tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the
appropriate sample depths
One Atterberg Limits test ASTM D 4318 was performed on a selected sample of the
subsurface soils to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits and to aid in soil classification
Results of this test are summarized on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depth
and presented graphically on Plate 10
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
In general our field exploration at the proposed Leach Field No 1 location
encountered volcanic ash soils consisting of very stiff clayey silt extending to a depth of
about 10 feet below the existing ground surface The volcanic ash was underlain by
residual soils consisting of stiff clayey silt extending to a depth of about 16 feet below the
existing ground surface Below the residual soils basalt rock formation was encountered
extending to the maximum depth explored of about 19 feet below the existing ground
surface The basalt rock was generally medium hard to hard

FINDINGS AND DISCUSSIONS
We understand that it is planned to construct leach fields at two locations within the
school property Therefore we have conducted field percolation tests to obtain data
pertaining to the preliminary design of the leach fields Two percolation tests were performed
at each of the proposed leach field locations The approximate locations of the field
percolation tests are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Based on the percolation tests conducted at Leach Field No 1 at depths of about
4 and 9 2 feet below the existing ground surface the percolation rates at the Leach Field
No 1 location are 25 and 10 minutes per inch respectively The percolation tests were
performed in volcanic ash soils consisting of clayey silt
Our percolation tests conducted at Leach Field No 2 were performed at depths of
about 5 and 8 2 feet below the existing ground surface The percolation tests were
performed in residual soils consisting of clayey silt Based on the percolation tests
conducted the percolation rate at a depth of about 5 and 8 2 feet below the existing ground
surface is 10 minutes per inch
Based on the test results we believe that Leach Field Nos 1 and 2 locations at
depths between of 5 and 10 feet below the existing ground surface are suitable for siting the
proposed leach fields The percolation tests were performed in both volcanic ash and
residual soils consisting of clayey silt Based on the results we recommend a percolation
rate of about 25 minutes per inch may be used for design of the Leach Field Nos 1 and 2
Due to the variability of the subsoil conditions the absorption capacity of the disposal system
should be confirmed by additional percolation tests during construction
