Geotechnical Engineering Recommendations
Collapsing and Backfilling of Lava Tubes
Villages of La i opua Village 3
Kealekehe North Kona Hawaii
This letter presents our geotechnical engineering recommendations for collapsing
and backfilling three lava tubes encountered on February 3 at the above referenced
project A lava tube was encountered along Road L extending to about Lot Nos 211
through 213 during mass grading operations Another lava tube was encountered near
Lot Nos 151 and 152 A third lava tube was encountered along Road F near
Lot No 34
Based on visual observation of the exposed portion of the lava tubes the lava
tubes appear to cover an area of approximately 70 to 90 feet long by 9 to 12 feet wide
and trends in the mauka makai direction In addition our field reconnaissance indicates
that portions of the three lava tubes are still unexposed The lava tube along Road L
appears to have a potential area of about 250 feet long by 50 feet wide Near
Lot Nos 151 and 152 the potential lava tube area may be on the order of about 35 by
150 feet The potential lava tube along Road F may underlie an area of about 75 by
300 feet
Based on our observation of the exposed portion of the lava tubes cavities the
roof of the lava tube generally consists of less than 2 feet of basalt formation The
condition of the basalt formation between the existing ground and the roof of the lava
tubes is generally poor and may not be adequate to support the future development
above the lava tube as is Therefore to reduce the potential for future distress to the
improvements we recommend that the roof of the cavities be collapsed Following the
collapse of the roof of the cavities the collapsed materials should be removed and the
resulting cavities should be backfilled with compacted general fill material 12 inch minus
material and capped with structural fill 6 inch minus The backfill materials should be
placed in level lifts not exceeding 12 inches in loose thickness moisture conditioned and
compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction

Collapsing of the lava tubes should be extended until adequate dense basalt cover
is attained between the finish grades and the roof of the lava tube The minimum dense
basalt cover should be 5 to 7 feet If poor quality basalt formation above the roof of the
lava tubes is encountered in the field the cover above the roof of the lava tubes will need
to be increased The lava tube collapsing and backfilling operations should be conducted
under the observation of a representative from Geolabs Hawaii
The lava tubes should be blocked off at the ends before the backfilling operations
to reduce the potential for the backfill material to migrate into the unbackfilled portion of
the lava tubes This may be achieved by blocking off the lava tubes using sand bags or
similar materials from the bottom to the top of the lava tubes
It should be noted that majority of the areas encompassing the three potential lava
tubes are located in cut areas based on the current grading plan Therefore most of the
lava tubes may be removed during mass grading operations Where the three lava tubes
extend to areas to receive fills the recommendations presented above should be
followed
If portions of the lava tubes will not be backfilled we recommend that the limits of
the lava tubes be mapped out for future reference It should be noted that the
unbackfilled portion of the lava tubes may collapse under vibrations generated from future
blasting operations located within several hundred feet of the lava tube or other sources
of vibration Therefore it is recommended that any blasting operation within 200 feet of
the lava tubes be avoided and blasting within 500 feet of the lava tubes should be
monitored to reduce the potential for collapsing the lava tubes which would impact the
lots above the lava tubes
