INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our subsurface investigation
in the area of the proposed play field The playground will be
constructed by cut and fill methods immediately mauka of the
existing Red Hill School Cafeteria and Classroom Buildings
The purpose of the investigation was to perform the necessary
drilling logging representative sampling and laboratory test
ing upon which the stability analyses for the proposed high cut
and low fill could be based

The preliminary topographic plan included with this report as

only be on the order of 8 feet from existing ground Generally

FIELD EXPLORATION
The field exploration consisted of 3 borings ranging in depth
from 15 0 to 50 0 feet The drilling was accomplished using
a truck mounted rotary drilling equipment with continuous
flight solid stem auger and coring bit where necessary The
borings were continuously inspected by a soils engineer The
approximate boring locations are shown on Plate land the boring
logs are presented in Appendix A
In all borings either Standard Penetration Test or 2 4 inch I D
ring samples were taken at selected depth intervals The Standard
Penetration Test consists of driving a 2 inch 0 D split spoon
sampler 18 inches with a 140 pound hammer free falling a distance
of 30 inches The number of blows required to drive the sampler
the last 12 inches is termed the Standard Penetration Resistance
N and is an approximate measure of the relative density or
consistency of a soil The number of blows to drive the ring

to our laboratory for inspection and testing Laboratory test
results and methods are included with this report as Appendix B
GEOLOGY AND SOIL CONDITIONS
Geology
The bedrock of the Red Hill area is composed of Koolau volcanic
series Basalt This group of rocks makes up the bulk of the
Koolau Range of Oahu and has an exposed thickness of approxi
mately 3000 feet These lavas were erupted from three rift zones
in the sea floor during Pliocene time approximately 2 to 3 mil
lion years ago Subsequent weathering extending into recent
times has produced a residual weathering crust on the top of
these flows The weathering oxidation hydrolyzation of the
ferro magnesian iron and manganese bearing minerals of the
basalts has produced a characteristic red iron oxide rust
stain in the residual soils hence the name Red Hill
Soils
There are at least three basic soil subdivisions of a continuous
Page 4

sequence from slightly weathered basalt bedrock to a residual
soil clayey silt to silty clay developed on and from the bed
rock at the proposed playground site
a Clayey SILT to Silty CLAY Residual SOIL
The brownish red to mottled clayey silt to silty clay
overlies weathered basalt bedrock to depths ranging from
approximately 4 feet in boring B 2 to 16 feet in boring B l
The change from residual soil to highly weathered basalt
is gradual and the exact depth of change is difficult to
pinpoint
b Weathered BASALT
The hard multicolored mottled clayey silt material
encountered in the borings is highly weathered fractured
basalt It varies from 9 to 24 feet in thickness depending
on boring locations and directly overlies the basalt bed
rock
c BASALT Bedrock
Hard gray jointed and fractured BASALT was encountered
in borings B 2 and B 3 at depths of 10 feet and 36 5 feet
respectively from the surface in the boreholes and it is
believed to underlie the entire site

STABILITY ANALYSES
Stability analyses were performed on the major cut slope as
proposed approximately 37 feet high maximum utilizing the lab
oratory shear strength parameters shown in Appendix B
The calculations have shown that if constructed in accordance
with the recommendations of this report the slopes possess a
satisfactory factor of safety greater than 1 5 and therefore
should perform in an acceptable manner

LABORATORY TESTING
A laboratory testing program was developed to establish the
engineering properties of the soils and rocks encountered at
the project site The tests where applicable to the subject
project are included in this Appendix
1 Classification Tests
a
b
c
Visual Classification All soil samples obtained from
the borings were brought to our laboratory where they
were visually reclassified to confirm or modify the
field classification prior to finalizing the Logs of
Borings
Water Content Determinations In addition to visual
classification typical samples were tested for natural
water content as an aid in soil classification and in
evaluating soil properties The water content values
are based upon the dry weight of the soil
Atterberg Limits Atterberg limit tests were performed
on selected clay and silt samples obtained from the
borings for the purposes of identification and correla
tion of soils Standard procedures ASTM D 424 for the
plastic limit and ASTM D 423 for the liquid limit were
used in the performance of these tests
1
d
Grain Size Analysis Sieve analysis were performed on
granular soils Hor fine grained soils the hydrometer
test was performed to determine the distribution of
the grain sizes beyond the No 200 sieve
2 Shear Strength Tests
a
b
Unconfined Compression Tests Intact core and split
spoon soil samples which apparently exhibited high
plasticity were tested for unconfined strength Length
to diameter ratio of 2 was maintained throughout the
tests to avoid scatter of test results and to facilitate
evaluation of shear strength values for design purposes
Direct Shear Test In general soil samples were tested
for shear strength using a 2 41 I D direct shear box
Each set consisted of at least two tests each test run
under different normal loads The results are plotted
as Normal Stress vs Shear Strength with a failure envelope
drawn to determine values of cohesion c and angle of
frictional resistance
Consolidation Test Consolidation tests were performed on
selected undisturbed ring samples of the compressible
soils to provide basic data for making settlement calculations
Porous stones were placed on the top and bottom of the samples
to allow drainage Vertical loads were applied in increments
with each load increment being allowed to consolidate prior
2
to adding the next increment Measurements of the time
and consolidation were obtained during each load increment
and rebound was measured during the unloading portion
Consolidation test results are plotted in terms of percent
settlement versus applied loads
Swell Test The ring sampleswas placed between porous
stones and a 60 psf load was applied to the sample for a
24 hour period The difference between the initial and
final sample heights is the amount of swell which is expressed
as a percentage of the initial height of the sample
Maximum Density Test Samples of soils are compacted in a
mold of given size with a 10 pound rammer dropped 18 inches
to determine the relationship between moisture content and
density of the soil At least three trial points at dif
ferent moisture content are run to determine the maximum
density and optimum moisture content The test is run in
accordance with ASTM D 1557 70
