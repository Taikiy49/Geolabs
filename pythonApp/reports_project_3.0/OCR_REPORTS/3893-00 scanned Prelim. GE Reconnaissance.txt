Dear Mr Rainalter
In accordance with our fee proposal dated we have performed a
field reconnaissance of the proposed subdivision site near the town of Waimea in the
District of South Kohala on the island of Hawaii in November 1997 This letter report
summarizes our work on the project and presents our findings and preliminary earthwork
recommendations for the subject site
Our field exploration program consisted of excavating three test pits designated as
Test Pit Nos A B and C to a depth of about 3 feet below the existing ground surface
The approximate locations of the test pits are shown on the Site Plan Plate 1 Our field
exploration generally encountered a surficial deposit of volcanic ash consisting of medium
stiff silty soils in a relatively dry and friable state Groundwater was not encountered in
the test pits at the time of our field exploration However groundwater levels are
expected to vary with seasonal rainfall and time of year
The surficial volcanic ash encountered at the site has relatively low moisture
contents and exists in a friable state as compared to the Pahala Ash found in other
locations on the island of Hawaii The soil when wetted has relatively low strength
characteristics and when in a dry state is highly susceptible to erosion In addition if the
in situ moisture contents are high enough they become thixotropic i e they lose
strength temporarily when remolded and or disturbed The near surface ash soils are
highly sensitive to the moisture contents and the Contractor may have difficulties in
achieving the required compaction

In general the near surface volcanic ash soils excavated from the proposed cut
areas may be re used as a source of general fill material As previously indicated the
ash soils are highly sensitive to moisture contents therefore use of the vibratory action of
the compactor should be carefully controlled to avoid disturbing the ash soils In addition
moisture conditioning of the ash soils should be carefully controlled to avoid over wetting
of the soils which would cause difficulty in achieving the required compaction levels
Additional compaction effort with proper moisture control will be necessary to meet the
specified compaction criteria In addition appropriate soil erosion protection dust control
provisions and construction procedures should be observed and implemented during the
site grading work for the subject subdivision project
Due to the dry and sometimes windy site conditions moisture conditioning of fill
materials will be a critical aspect during fill placement In some areas the in situ soils
appear to be dry relative to the optimum moisture content It is advisable that water
trucks be available in sufficient number to provide adequate water for moisture
conditioning during compaction The State and Federal governments have enacted
stringent regulations pertaining to the release of dust from construction sites therefore it
is also advisable to institute a dust control program during grading operations to reduce
the amount of airborne dust due to the nature of the on site soils
With regard to the probable foundations for the future homes we recommend that
concrete slabs on grades with thickened edges be used to support the generally lightly
loaded house structures proposed for the subdivision Due to the nature of the ashy soils
at the site we recommend a layer of select fill select borrow subbase or 6 inch minus
structural fill be provided below the slab on grade and thickened edges for the house
structures
It should be noted that the permeability of the on site ashy soils is highly variable
and may have some relatively impermeable zones This will affect the design of the
drywells typically used for the disposal of storm water run off on the island of Hawaii
The permeability of the on site soils should be further evaluated for design of the storm
water disposal system
We recommend that a detailed field exploration be conducted in the detailed
design stage to evaluate these design considerations The field exploration should
include drilling borings performing percolation and laboratory tests Geolabs Hawaii
would appreciate the opportunity to provide follow up services on this project
