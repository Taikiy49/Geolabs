LOCATION AND SITE DESCRIPTION
Location The property covered by this report comprising
105 acres is located along the west side of the upper portion
of Kalama Valley immediately north of the proposed secondary
highway alignment Kalama Valley is situated north of Koko
rater and runs north south along the east flank of Kamehame
Ridge The property is accessible from Kalanianaole Highway

August 20 1971
near Sandy Beach Park via Ehukai Street
The existing Ehukai Street until recently serviced a number
of pig farms which were being demolished and removed at the
time of this investigation The road was originally surfaced
with an oil gravel mixture however it now is nearly completely
disintegrated
There are some old dirt roads along the west side of Ehukai
Street that reach to the base of Kamehame Ridge
Topography The site terrain varies from nearly level to gently
sloping The valley rises from about elevation 40 at the
southern boundary to about elevation 80 at the northern property
boundary A spatter cone is southeast of the property and is
presently being used as a borrow Site This area rises to
approximate elevation 110

meandering stream which is fed by several small washes The
Slope gradient towards the southis generally about one percent

GEOLOGY AND SOIL CONDITIONS
Kalama Valley has had an interesting geologic history After
the formation of the Koolau Mountains formed by repeated flows
of lava piles one on top of the other and now exposed in Kamehame
Ridge the mountains were deeply weathered and later cut by
south flowing streams Deep canyons were eroded and Kalama
Valley was formed at this time
P
Page 8
W O 286 10
August 20 1971
In fairly recent geologic times Koko Crater was formed by
explosive action of the lavas coming into contact with sea
water and thick deposits of volcanic tuff were formed covering
the south end of Kalama Valley Still later Spatter cones
ejected fragmental lavas and formed cones of friable cinder
and basalt fragments which are now exposed along the east side
of Kalama Valley
Subsequent to the above depositional periods thick deposits
of CLAY resulted from soils washed down from the ridges of
weathered basalt and these are found in the north end of Kalama
Valley
In general the north two thirds of the project is underlain
by 10 5 feet to 25 0 feet of medium firm to very stiff gray and
black CLAY while the south one third is underlain by very dense
brown volcanic Tuff with some patches of soil cover Basalt
boulders are found along the base of Kamehame Ridge and they
are also found overlying both the CLAY and the TUFF
Boring Nos 1 and 2 indicate the CLAY is about 19 feet thick
while Boring Nos 3 and 4 indicate 10 to 15 feet thick Boring
No 5 located near the stream bed in the center of the project
shows the CLAY to be 25 feet thick Gypsum crystals were noted

the following foundation design is recommended for overall cost
analysis Slightly less stringent requirements can be given to
some of the lots when the grading is complete and the location

Floor Slabs Floor slabs should be reinforced with a
minimum of 6 inch by 6 inch No 6 by No 6 welded wire
mesh The slabs should be underlain by four inches of
