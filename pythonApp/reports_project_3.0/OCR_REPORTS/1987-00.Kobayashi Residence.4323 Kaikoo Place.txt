4323 KAIKOO PLACE
In accordance with your request we have made a study of the near
surface soil conditions at the above referenced lot in order to provide
foundation recommendations for the proposed residence The study
consisted of drilling two 2 borings performing laboratory testing and
reviewing the preliminary house plans This report summarizes our
findings and recommendations
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
Based on the preliminary house plans the proposed residence will be
a two story structure approximately 43 by 58 feet in plan dimensions
Concrete slab on grade is planned for the sidewalk lanai and the
house Wood framed construction is anticipated for the proposed house
SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is located on the northeastern side and near the end
of Kaikoo Place in Honolulu on the island of Oahu

Dk No 52
At the time of our field exploration the site was occupied by a
two story house with a swimming pool in the front It is anticipated that
the old house will be demolished and the swimming pool will be slightly
modified
The existing ground surface at the site slopes gently downward in the
southwest direction from elevations 45 to 39 feet MSL
SUBSURFACE SOIL CONDITIONS
Two 2 borings were drilled around the perimeter of the existing
residence to depths of 4 5 to 15 feet below the existing grade at the
approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general the borings encountered medium dense to dense gray or
brown cinder sand to the full depth of our borings
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings at the time of our
field exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The medium dense to dense gray or brown cinder sand underlying the
site for the proposed residence generally can provide suitable foundation
Support for the proposed residential structure
Site Grading
It is planned to demolish the existing house structure foundations
and their surrounding sidewalks or decking The debris and deleterious
material including broken concrete and wood pieces resulting from the

demolition should be disposed properly off site The ground for the
proposed new structure should be cleared and grubbed and scarified to a
depth of 12 inches below the bottom of the future slab moisture
conditioned to near optimum moisture content and compacted to 90 of its
maximum dry density as established by ASTM D 1557 78
If additional fill is necessary selected non expansive granular
soils should be used
All imported fill materials should be tested and approved by the soil
engineer prior to being used in the site
The fills should be placed in loose horizontal lifts not exceeding 10
inches and compacted to the above specified density
Fills placed an existing slopes should start from the bottom up and
should be keyed and benched to bond the new fill to the existing slope
The site should be graded to provide drainage of surface runoff away
from all foundations
House Foundations
It is anticipated that the entire house floor would be of
slab on grade construction
Spread or continuous wall footings may be used to support the
proposed residence An allowable bearing value of 3000 p s f may be used
on the compacted soil or stiff natural ground
Interior spread footings on level areas should be poured in place and
be embedded a minimum of 8 inches below the finished grade Perimeter
wall footings should be embedded a minimum of 12 inches below the adjacent
finished grade

Surface water should be diverted away from foundations
Slab On Grade
Concrete slab on grade is planned for the house lanai sidewalk and
decking constructions The thickened edge of the slab should be embedded
a minimum of 12 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade
It is recommended that a 4 inch layer of 3B Fine gravel be provided
as the cushion fill below the slab To protect against future moisture
infiltration an impervious moisture membrane may be incorporated below
the slab
Swimming Pool
For the planned modifications of the swimming pool a lateral earth
pressure of 55 p s f equivalent fluid pressure per foot of depth should
be used for walls restrained at the top at rest condition with level
backfill This assumes that compacted granular backfill material is used
behind the wall and proper drainage is included to avoid the build up of
water pressure
Drainage
The finished grade outside the slab and footings should be shaped to
shed water away from the foundations and to avoid ponding condition Also
it is advised that gutter systems should be installed around the house and
that gutter water should be diverted away from the foundations and
walkways Excessive landscape watering near the foundations and slab
should also be avoided
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained
to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundations

Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comment
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earth work and foundation recommendations given herein If this
review is not made the geotechnical engineer can assume no responsibility
for misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Observation
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the
excavation and foundation phases of the work This is to observe
compliance with the design concepts specifications or recommendations and
to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ
from that anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations
given in this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as borings
and data from laboratory tests of soil samples Variations of conditions
between the field data points may occur and the nature and extent of
these variations may not become evident until construction If variations
then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations given in this report

The location and elevation of the field data points were determined
by scaling from the House Plan Elevations of the data points were
determined by the same plan The physical location and elevation of the
field data points should be considered accurate only to the degree implied
by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross section are based upon interpolation between field data
points and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
Water level data from the field data points was collected at the
times given on the graphic representations and or in the text of this
report This data has been reviewed and interpretations made in the
formulation of this report However it must be noted that fluctuation
may occur due to variations in rainfall tides temperature and other
factors

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
eahaaeleenterteuteatardenieateere
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling two
2 borings to depths of 4 5 to 15 feet below the existing ground surface
at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 A portable
drill rig was used to drill the borings
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by visual and
textural examination in the field by our engineer who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory All soils
were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A summary of the materials encountered are
presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 and A 2
Soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving a 2 inch
a 140 pound hammer free falling 30 inches The blow counts to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the Boring Logs at the
appropriate sample depths
