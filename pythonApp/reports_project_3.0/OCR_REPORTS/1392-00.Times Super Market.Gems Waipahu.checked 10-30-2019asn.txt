This report presents the results of a soils and foundation
investigation performed for the above referenced project The general
location of the site and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location
Map Plate l
The purpose of our study was to explore the subsurface conditions
at the site to provide data for use in the design of the slab on grade
foundation and pavements of the proposed remodeling and pharmacy addition
at your GEMS Waipahu branch Our work was performed in general
accordance with the scope of services outlined in our proposal of March
26th
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations

Proposed Construction
For the remodeling work it is planned to add one bay of colum
line to the rear western side of the existing store to provide a new cold
storage and other low temperature preparation and storage areas It is
anticipated that the new construction will be a high bayed structure with
on grade slab construction and concrete masonry unit bearing walls The
general layout of the remodeling area is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The existing storage rubbish pick up area at the western side of
the store will be paved with concrete and fully enclosed to provide an
addition to serve as a new pharmacy area
Site Description
The project site is located at Highway in
Waipahu Oahu Hawaii The area to be remodeled is the rear western side
of the existing supermarket At the time of our field exploration the
proposed remodeling site was a level asphalt concrete paved area The
western half of the site was fenced and occupied by refrigeration
compressor units The eastern half of the site was open and used as a
truck loading and unloading area Several underground utility lines were
noted traversing the back side of the proposed remodeling area
An existing covered and partially enclosed storage rubbish pick up
area was located on the west side of the store The northern half of
this area was paved with asphalt concrete and the southern half with
concrete

Subsurface Conditions
Field Exploration
Three borings were drilled in the proposed remodeling area to a
depth of 16 5 feet below the existing ground surface at the approximate
locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 Detailed description of the
materials encountered in the borings are presented on the boring logs
Plates A 1 through A 3 The soil samples were examined and tested in our
laboratory The swell test data are presented on Plate B 1 and the
moisture and density test data are presented on the Log of Borings at the
appropriate sample depths
Soil Conditions
In general our borings encountered 2 to 3 inches of asphalt
concrete with 5 to 6 inches of dense sandy gravel base course in the
remodeling area Boring 1 encountered about 2 feet of dense brown Silty
sand fill beneath the base course Boring 3 encountered 3 5 inches of
asphalt concrete without base course
Below the pavements and fill all our borings encountered hard
brown clayey silt often with some gray mottlings to a depth of 16 5
feet the maximum depth explored No borings were drilled in the
proposed new pharmacy area
Groundwater was not encountered in our borings

Discussion and Recommendations
Under the existing asphalt concrete pavement our field exploration
disclosed that the proposed remodeling site is underlain by dense and
hard soils of low expansion potential Based on our observation there
were no discernible cracks on the existing high walls We believe that
the subsurface conditions at the proposed new pharmacy area would be
similar to the proposed remodeling area explored
Shallow spread footing foundations may be used to support the
proposed building on the undisturbed hard dense on site soils In
general on grade slab construction may be utilized provided that
adequate measures are taken to reduce potential for frost heave under the
cold storage floor slab areas
Foundation
Shallow spread footings or continuous wall foundations may be used
for the proposed one story structure
We believe that foundations may either bear on the compacted fill
densified as discussed under the SITE GRADING section or on the
underlying undisturbed natural soils
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Page 5
In general the bottom of the footings should be embedded a minimum
of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent grade except the exterior wall
footing of the cold storage area for which more specific discussions and
recommendations are provided under the Subsequent section Thickened
edge slab footings if utilized for interior walls should have a minimum
embedment of 12 inches If a footing is located next to a utility
trench it should be extended below a plane rising at 45 degrees from the
nearest bottom edge of the utility line trench in order to reduce footing
settlement due to possible settlement of the trench backfill
For foundations bearing on the existing compacted sand fill or on
the undisturbed hard natural soil a bearing value of 2500 p s f may be
used
We believe that the above recommendations will also be app licable
to the proposed new pharmacy enclosure However the actual soil
conditions should be verified in the field by the project soils engineer
during construction
Estimated settlements of the foundations placed in the upper fill
layer are anticipated to be less than one half inch total and one quarter
inch differential We believe the majority of the footing settlements
would occur prior to the completion of the project construction

Consideration for Cold Storage Construction
The ground under the cold storage area will be in contact with the
slabs that will be maintained at sub freezing temperature
It is our understanding that the floor slab section for the
prefabricated freezer will consist of 4 inches of concrete slab 4 inches
of rigid insulation 6 inches of concrete sub slab over a vapor barrier
and granular cushion fill layer To control deep penetration of
sub freezing temperature below the floor slab a sub floor heating system
is used sometimes however it is our understanding that the above slab
section has been successfully used on this island without a sub floor
heating system
It is our understanding that the freezer unit will abut the
exterior wall and that the area outside the wall will be surfaced with
asphalt concrete pavement
For the above constructions we believe that the exterior wall
footing should be embedded to at least 2 5 feet below the lowest adjacent
finished grade to reduce infiltration While the planned asphalt
concrete paving outside the wall will reduce infiltration of surface
water sealing of joints between A C and the wall and sealing of wall
footings are recommended to further minimize potential for frost heave

The above recommendations for deepening and water proofing of
exterior wall foundation should extend at least 5 feet beyond the limits
of the cold storage area
Slab On Grade
Slab on grade construction may be utilized after the site is
prepared as recommended in the SITE GRADING section
The on grade slabs should be placed over a 4 inch cushion layer
such as No 3 Fine crushed rock to minimize capillary rise through the
cushion fill The cushion material should be compacted to at least 90
of the maximum dry density The slab subgrade soil should be kept moist
prior to placing vapor barrier
Site Grading
Relatively minor site grading is anticipated for the proposed
construction
The existing concrete slab and asphalt concrete should be removed
from the proposed remodeling site Additional excavation would be
required in the freezer area to attain the design subgrade level
The excavated surface should be moisture conditioned and compacted
to obtain a minimum 90 compaction
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1392 00 Page 8
Any soft or weak pockets disclosed at the subgrade should be
repaired by excavating these pockets to firm soils and backfilling with
compacted on site granular fill and as approved by the soils engineer
The excavated on site soils and base course under the A C may be
used as fill if required In addition broken up A C pavements less
than 3 inches in size may be re used in the fill
The top 6 inches of the subgrade under the slab areas should be
compacted to at least 90 of the maximum dry density as established by
ASTM D 1557 78 The top 6 inches of the subgrade under the A C pavement
area should be compacted to 95 of the maximum density
If additional off site borrow is required it should be tested and
approved by a soils engineer prior to hauling to the site The borrow or
imported material should generally be non expansive and not contain large
percentage of cobbles organics or other deleterious matter
Design Review
Plans and specifications for site grading and foundation
construction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and
written comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the site grading and footing excavations be
observed by the soils engineer during construction to see that adequate
compaction has been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are
consistent with the findings of our investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
If conditions encountered during construction are different from
those presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made
