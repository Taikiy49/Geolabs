Pursuant to the completion of the test pile driving and all three 3
pile load tests on the driven test piles we recommend the pile lengths as
shown on the attached plan for casting the production piles In order to
expedite the project the production pile lengths for the inch
octagonal 100 tons piles had been recommended previously in our letter
dated and these production pile lengths will however be
also included here for a complete reference
Generally speaking we observed that the test piles in the
northwestern end of the proposed FICPAC building addition had been able to
penetrate through the thin mudrock layer and into the underlying hard
older alluvium Therefore longer friction piles are needed in order to
support the proposed building addition in this area with the original
octagonal piles and 60 tons per pile for the 12 inch square piles The
basically be frictional piles approximately 120 feet long as originally
estimated
We believe that predrilling is not necessary for driving these
frictional production piles The extent of the estimated predrilling zone
and the required predrilled depths are indicated on the attached plan

The data from the completed test pile program including predrilling
driving and load tests have also been analyzed for possible modification
of the criteria for driving the production piles Dynamic wave equation
analyses of pile driving have been performed to account for the change in
support from end bearing to predominantly friction and driving
conditions due to the use of a pile driving hammer with a much higher
driving energy than specified

for driving the production piles The piles should be driven to satisfy
either the specified blow count or depth requirements with the same hammer

In general accordance with the scope of services outlined in our
proposal dated we observed the predrilling and driving of
eleven 11 test piles and the predrilling and driving of the initial
twenty 20 production piles Subsequently at the request of the Officer
in Charge of Construction OICC we have also observed the driving of the
remaining sixty six 66 production piles Our observations of all the
production pile driving was performed from July 22 to August 6 1988 a
total of thirteen 13 days including two 2 working days on Saturday
A diesel pile hammer Model 33 was used to drive the
precast prestressed concrete piles during both test and production pile
driving A total of ninety seven 97 piles were driven to the general
depths of about 24 92 to 137 92 feet below the design cut off elevations
which include twelve 12 twelve inch square piles and eighty five 85
The locations of all the piles are shown on the attached Pile
Location Plan During the production pile driving Pile No 68 was broken
and replaced with Pile No 68A Pile No 66 in the same pile cluster was
also deemed broken later during pile driving and was replaced with Pile
Nos 66A and 66B All the replacement piles were driven to satisfy the
pile driving criteria
