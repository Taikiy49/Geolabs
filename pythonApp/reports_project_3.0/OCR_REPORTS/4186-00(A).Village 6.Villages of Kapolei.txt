INTRODUCTION

for the proposed Village 6 residential development located within the Villages of Kapolei in
the District of Ewa on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the
project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering recommendations
derived from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering analyses These
recommendations are intended for the design of house foundations slabs on grade
retaining structures site grading and pavements only The findings and recommendations
presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lavas from
two shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be
middle to late Pliocene in age and Koolau is estimated to be late Pliocene to early
Pleistocene in age As volcanic activity in Waianae ceased lava flows from Koolau banked
against its eroded eastern slope forming a broad plateau known as Schofield Plateau
Physical and chemical weathering followed by erosion of this plateau generated
sediments which were transported to the coast In the vicinity of the project site and to the
south these sediments accumulated and interbedded with marine sediments and coral aigal
reef formations to form a sedimentary wedge The thickness of the sedimentary wedge
ranges from zero in the area of the interstate Route H 1 Highway to over 1 000 feet at Ewa
Beach This wedge forms the Ewa Plain and serves as the confining formation or caprock
over the artesian basal aquifers of Southern Oahu Deposition of sediments has continued
from earlier geologic time through the present Agricultural development within the last
GEOLABS INC
W O 4186 00 A Page 6
100 years and recent mass grading work has brought the Villages of Kapolei area to its
present form

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field exploration the Village 6 project site is generally underlain by
about 7 to 15 feet of very stiff to hard clayey fill materials overlying medium dense to dense
silty coral sand or coral gravelly sand materials extending to the maximum depth explored
of about 21 5 feet below the existing ground surface Our laboratory tests indicate that the
near surface clayey soils exhibit moderate to high shrink swell characteristics when
subjected to fluctuations in the soil moisture contents It should be noted that the generally
dry climatic conditions in the Kapolei area pose some challenges to the earthwork and
house foundation construction at the site Therefore special attention should be given to
the site preparation recommendations for the site grading and house foundation design to
account for the moderately to highly expansive soil conditions at the site
Based on the generally stiff subsurface soil conditions encountered at the project
site we believe that a shallow foundation system slabs on grade with thickened edge
footings may be used to support the proposed residential structures Due to the
moderately to highly expansive soil conditions at the site we recommend that the
expansive clayey soils be capped with a minimum of 12 inches of non expansive select
granular fill material beiow buiiding slab areas We aiso recommend that 12 inches of
non expansive select granular fill material be provided below the driveway walkway and
lanai slabs In addition special attention should be given to the recommended procedures
for subgrade soil preparation below slabs and pavements to reduce the potential
GEOLABS INC
W O 4186 00 A Page 9
shrink swell effects of the near surface clayey soils Special attention should also be given
to the structural detailing of adjoining slabs to reduce the potential for differential
movement between abutting slabs
Field observations during our exploration indicated that shrinkage cracks have
developed at the existing ground surface across the project site especially in the central
portion of the site The existing shrinkage cracks if left unattended may increase further
in size under prolonged dry weather conditions Shrinkage cracks located adjacent to the
house foundations are potentially detrimental since water can readily infiltrate into the
subsurface through the crack openings and soften the subsoils around the house
foundations thus affecting their performance Therefore we recommend that the existing
ground surface be scarified moisture conditioned and recompacted during the site
preparation operations In addition future homeowners should be made aware of the
potential for recurrence of the shrinkage cracks and appropriate maintenance measures
should be implemented to reduce the potential for recurrence of the shrinkage cracks
around the house structures
We understand that the project will be constructed in two phases The initial phase
of the project construction will consist of the site work operations followed by the house
construction phase after completion of the site work phase The long lag time between the
two phases of construction poses some challenges to the contractors due to the dry
climatic conditions in the Kapolei area The completed site work operations especially the
fill placement will need to be kept moist and free of shrinkage cracks until completion of
the house construction and finished grading including landscaping Special attention
should be given to the recommendations for site preparation and maintaining the

Slab On Grade Foundations
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered at the project site we believe that
slabs on grade with thickened edge footings may be used to support the proposed one to
two story residential structures planned for the Village 6 residential development Our
exploration indicates that the site is generally underlain by moderately to highly expansive
clayey soils at or near the existing ground surface Unless slab on grade foundations
supported by these expansive soils are properly designed there is a potential for future
distress to the residential structures resulting from wetting and subsequent swelling of the
expansive clayey soils
To reduce the potential for structural distress resulting from the swelling of the on site
expansive soils we recommend that non expansive select granular fill material capping fill
be provided for a minimum depth of 12 inches below the building slab cushion The capping
fill should also extend beyond the perimeter of the house slab a minimum of 2 feet The
perimeter thickened edges of the slabs should be embedded a minimum of 24 inches below
the lowest adjacent exterior grade The embedment of the interior thickened edge footings
may be reduced to a minimum depth of 12 inches below the adjacent finished grade The
base of the thickened edge should have a minimum width of 12 inches Graphic illustration
of the recommended foundation condition is presented on the Schematic Section
Slab on Grade on Expansive Soil Plate 3 and also Plate 4

utilized for the design of shallow foundations bearing on the recompacted on site soils and or
the compacted fills needed to raise the existing grades to the design finished grades This

The siab edges should incorporate a sufficient amount of top and bottom longitudinal

The slab subgrades including the select granular fill should be kept moist until the
placement of cushion fill and concrete To reduce the potential for drying of the footing
subgrade soils and to reduce the costs for form construction we recommend that the slab
edges be cast neat against the soil excavations The select granular fill material should
provide a capping layer of moist soil above the underlying potentially expansive subgrade
soil thereby reducing the loss of moisture from the clayey subgrade soils In our opinion the
reduction in potential drying and wetting of the subgrade soils should reduce the impact of
shrink swell effects on the slab due to potential heaving of the underlying expansive clayey
soils when wetted
We recommend that a minimum 4 inch layer of cushion fill be incorporated below the
concrete slab The cushion fill should consist of free draining materials such as
No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation which would also serve as a capillary
moisture break The slab subgrade should be thoroughly wetted prior to concrete placement

for future moisture infiltration and subsequent damage to floor coverings an impervious
moisture vapor barrier is recommended on top of the cushion layer Two inches of moist
fine sand may be considered above the plastic membrane to provide protection of the
membrane and to aid in curing of the concrete slab Provision of the 2 inches of fine sand
above the vapor barrier will be left up to the discretion of the project structural engineer
Flexible floor coverings such as carpet or sheet vinyl should be considered on the slab since
they can better mask minor slab cracking It is also recommended that interior walls be
designed to incorporate some flexibility to accommodate small amounts of possible ground
movements

Driveway and Walkway Slabs
We envision that concrete driveways and walkway slabs will be required at the
residential lots We anticipate that the vehicle loading for the proposed driveways will consist
primarily of passenger vehicles and light pick up trucks We have made our preliminary
design assuming that the subgrade soil will generally consist of the near surface moderately
to highly expansive clayey soils encountered in our test borings If site grading exposes soils
other than those assumed additional tests should be performed to confirm or revise the
recommended driveway section for actual field conditions
Based on the above assumptions we recommend that the concrete driveway slabs
be supported on a minimum 12 inch thick non expansive select granular fill compacted to
a minimum of 90 percent reiative compaction We recommend that the concrete siab be
designed by a structural engineer for the intended loads and should be reinforced as a
structural slab to accommodate some amount of differential movement In addition we
GEOLABS INC
W O 4186 00 A Page 14
recommend that the driveway slab be structurally connected to the garage slab with dowels
to reduce the potential for differential movements between the two abutting slabs
For design of structural slabs a modulus of subgrade reaction of 200 pounds per
square inch per inch of deflection pci may be used for the compacted select granular fill
Provisions should be made for proper load transfer across the slab joints which will be
subjected to vehicular traffic The thickened edges of slabs adjacent to unpaved areas
should be embedded at least 12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade
Exterior concrete walkways will likely be required for the proposed project Due to the
moderate to high expansion potential of the near surface clayey soils we recommend that
the walkways be supported on a minimum 12 inch thick layer of non expansive select
granular fill compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Control joints should
be provided at intervals equal to the width of the walkways and at right angle intersections
The subgrade soils beneath the driveways and walkways should be prepared in accordance
with the recommendations presented in the Site Grading section of this report The soil
subgrades should be kept moist until covered by the slab concrete If shrinkage cracks
appear on the subgrade soils prior to placement of the slab concrete the subgrade soils
should be scarified to a minimum depth of 8 inches moisture conditioned to at least
2 percent above the optimum moisture content and recompacted to a minimum of
90 percent relative compaction

Shrinkage Cracks
Our field observations indicated that substantial shrinkage cracks are present at the
existing ground surface especially in the central portion of the project site In general
shrinkage cracks will start to develop in the ground due to the lack of maintenance of the soil
moisture content as the soils start to dry out under the sun and dry weather conditions The
development of shrinkage cracks in the ground was apparently intensified by the prolonged
dry climatic conditions in the Kapolei area resulting in large fluctuations of the soil moisture
content Detailed discussions of the shrink swell behavior of the on site soils and the
mechanism of shrinkage crack development are presented below
Shrink Swell Behavior of On Site Soils
Based on the laboratory test results performed for the subject project the on site
clayey soils generally have a moderate to high shrink swell potential when subjected
to fluctuations in the moisture content and may be classified as expansive soils In
addition it will likely lose substantial strength when saturated and will shrink
appreciably when subjected to drying resulting in shrinkage cracks In general the
GEOLABS INC
W O 4186 00 A Page 17
mechanism of shrink swell in expansive soils is a complex phenomenon and is
influenced by a number of factors including soil characteristics and environmental
factors
Expansive soil characteristics can generally be measured by engineering properties
such as soil plasticity and density to evaluate the shrink swell potential of soils In
general the soil plasticity reflects the capacity of the clay minerals in the soil to retain
large amounts of water while maintaining a coherent structure Therefore a highly
plastic soil such as those found at the subject site will have a greater potential for
volume change shrink and swell due to the loss or absorption of moisture under
cycles of drying and wetting In addition increased soil densities through compaction
or natural depositional history such as desiccation generally have greater
shrink swell potential
Environmental factors comprise of the initial moisture content of the soil and moisture
variations over time The initial moisture content generally impact the potential of the
soil to absorb or expel water from the soil system In general a dry soil will have a
higher capacity to absorb water and swell while a wet soil will lose water more readily
upon exposure to dry conditions and will shrink more Moisture variations can
generally be caused by climate surface drainage vegetation permeability etc
Shrinkage Crack Development Mechanism
We believe that the moisture content of the on site soils was moist and relatively
constant during the mass grading phase As a result the development of large
shrinkage cracks might not be evident during the site grading operations Due to the
high plasticity of the on site clayey soils shrinking of the near surface soils started to
develop as soil moisture evaporated from the soil system under the prolonged dry
climatic conditions characteristic of the Kapolei area These shrinkage cracks may
close up to a certain degree upon wetting as the soils expand during periods of wet
weather or heavy rains However shrinkage of the on site soils and subsequent
cracking of the ground surface will recur when the dry weather returns Therefore the
GEOLABS INC
W O 4186 00 A 7 7 Page 18
development of shrinkage cracks will recur when the on site clayey soils are subjected
to prolonged dry climatic conditions which causes large fluctuations in the moisture
content unless some measures are taken to maintain the soil moisture content at a
relatively constant level
in areas where ponding water occurs the wet ground will have a higher potential for
shrinkage cracks to develop since the wet soils will lose water more readily upon
exposure to dry conditions and will shrink more Therefore it should be recognized
that this phenomenon will recur and continue unless measures are taken to maintain
the soil moisture content relatively constant in order to reduce the shrink swell
behavior of the on site clayey soils due to moisture fluctuations
Recommendations for Site Preparation
If the existing ground cracks are left unattended for an extended period of time and
are allowed to penetrate deeper below the surface the ground cracks could
adversely affect the performance of the future house foundations The ground
cracks could serve as conduits for water to percolate into the foundation soils and
cause loss of strength of the foundation soils resulting in potential settlement of the
house structure In addition migration of the foundation soils into the openings of
the shrinkage cracks could undermine the foundation system and result in potential
settlement of the house structure Therefore remedial work to correct or mitigate
the observed ground cracks should be implemented prior to construction of the
proposed houses
Based on our field measurements shrinkage cracks up to about 3 inches wide and
12 to 18 inches deep were observed at the project site Therefore we recommend
that the existing ground surface be scarified by a minimum depth of 12 inches
moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction prior to commencement of the
site work grading operations to remove the zone with shrinkage cracks

W O 4186 00 A Page 19
It should be noted that a proper site drainage system is also critical to the performance
of the foundation system and reduction in the development of shrinkage cracks
Therefore we recommend that drainage swales be properly established and that the
house pads be graded to provide positive drainage away from the house foundations
Special attention should also be given to the recommendations presented in the
Drainage section of this report
As previously indicated we understand that the residential development will be
separated into two phases of construction The initial phase of construction will

cracks until the house construction is completed which may be at least 12 to

Based on the above discussion the existing ground surface should be scarified to a

moisture content and compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction to remove
the zone of existing shrinkage cracks After completion of the site work grading we

We envision that the graded surfaces will be grassed to reduce the potential for soil
erosion of the graded surfaces The temporary irrigation system would be used to

moisture contents The relatively dry climatic conditions of the Kapolei area will likely
require controlied irrigation of the graded surfaces to maintain the moisture contents
and graded surfaces free of shrinkage cracks However over watering of the graded

Long Term Maintenance Measures
As discussed above the phenomenon of shrinkage crack development will recur
depending on climatic conditions unless measures are taken to maintain the soil
moisture content relatively constant thereby reducing the shrink swell behavior of
the on site clayey soils due to moisture content fluctuations These long term
solutions may include landscaping installation of concrete walkways around the
house structure piacement of non expansive fills in the yard areas and or provision
of an irrigation system to maintain the moisture contents of the near surface soils
in a relatively narrow range
Based on our previous experience with similar soils house lots without landscaping
have experienced varying degrees of soil erosion during heavy rains It is our
opinion that landscaping of the house lots should be given serious consideration by
the homeowners since it would generally achieve the following objectives
1 Landscaping along with proper irrigation will serve to maintain the soil moisture
content relatively constant due to controlled regular irrigation which reduces
the shrink swell behavior of the soils and consequently reduces the potential
for development of shrinkage cracks
2 Development of a root system from landscaping would reduce the potential for
erosion and for altering the drainage patterns
GEOLABS INC
W O 4186 00 A Page 21
However the future homeowners should be advised or cautioned about the following
conditions which may occur or would be potentially detrimental to their property
1 Ground cracks may develop in the landscaped areas if there was a ration on
the use of irrigation water due to prolonged dry seasons As the root system
is deprived of the irrigation water it would start to draw water out from the
surrounding soil system thus accelerating the drying out of the soils and
resulting in faster development of shrinkage cracks
2 To ensure proper performance of the foundation system the homeowners
should be strongly cautioned against over irrigation or landscaping within
3 feet around the house structures as this may introduce irrigation water into
the subsoils around the thickened edge footings which can soften the subsoils
and affect the foundation performance In lieu of landscaping we highly
recommend that concrete walkways be constructed within this zone to reduce
the potential for direct water infiltration into the subsoils along the perimeter of
the house foundation Where provided the concrete walkways should be
sloped to shed water away from the house foundations and garage slab In
addition the concrete walkways should be supported on non expansive select
granular fill material
It should be noted that it is not possible to eliminate the ground cracks completely due
to the nature of the moderately to highly expansive soils encountered at the site and the
weather conditions in the Kapolei area The recommendations provided above are intended
only to reduce the potential for recurring of large ground cracks

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content ASTM D 2216 and unit weight ASTM D 2937 determinations were
performed on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil
properties The results of these tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the
appropriate sample depths
Twenty five one inch ring swell tests were performed on relatively undisturbed and
remolded samples to evaluate the swelling potential of the near surface soils The results
of these tests are summarized on Plates B 1 through B 3
Four Atterberg Limits tests ASTM D 4318 were performed on selected soil samples
to evaluate their liquid and plastic limits and to aid in soil classification The test results are
summarized on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths Graphic presentation
of the test results is provided on Plate B 4
Five Modified Proctor compaction tests ASTM D 1557 were performed on bulk
samples of the near surface soils to evaluate the dry density and moisture content
relationships The test results are presented on Plates B 5 through B 9
Five laboratory California Bearing Ratio Tests ASTM D 1883 were performed on bulk
samples of the near surface soils to evaluate the pavement support characteristics of the
soils The test results are presented on Plates B 10 through B 14
