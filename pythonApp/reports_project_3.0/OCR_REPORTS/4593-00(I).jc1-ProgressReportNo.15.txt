accordance with the scope of services outlined in our fee proposal dated October
29 2008

subsurface water levels in conjunction with the current Mona Street Landslide
Remediation project We included general information from our instrumentation program
in our progress report dated The geotechnical exploration results
were presented in our report entitled Revised Subsurface Investigation Program

Hawaii dated
This report is the fifteenth semi annual progress report covering the 90 month
instrumentation monitoring period which officially commenced in January 2002
INSTRUMENTATION MONITORING
We drilled three borings B 1 through B 3 each to a depth of approximately
100 feet in November 2001 Three slope inclinometers were installed in these borings in
order to measure the lateral deflection of the soil at various depths We drilled an
additional boring B 4 to a depth of approximately 100 feet in September 2003 An
inclinometer was installed in this boring to better delineate slide limits Locations for
Boring B 1 through B 4 are shown on the Site Plan Plate 1

PAVEMENT DEFORMATION MAPPING
Ground surface observations were recorded in our previous reports
Approximately 1 to 2 inch wide pavement cracks on the surface of the roadway were
observed between 1189 and 1199 Mona Street from May 2002 to September 2002
No significant continuous cracking development was recorded from September 2002 to
January 2003

Pavement resurfacing was performed in localized areas of Mona Street during
January 2003 to August 2003 A majority of the cracks previously recorded was patched
with asphaltic concrete No significant continuous cracking development was recorded
from January 2004 to August 2004
Our pavement deformation mapping resumed in Year 2005 During this period a
majority of the cracks recorded prior to the pavement resurfacing was again observed
on the resurfaced pavement with some extended new cracks as indicated in the
Progress Report No 14 dated We will continue to monitor the
pavement deformation The revised Crack Location Plan will be available in the next
progress report at the end of the 2009 monitoring period
SUMMARY
Inclinometer Nos I 1 I 3 and 4 indicated some shallow depth subsurface
ground movement and or creep at their respective instrument locations However
Inclinometer No I 2 currently does not indicate significant subsurface ground lateral
movement Our piezometer data indicates that subsurface water seepage occurred at
the project site Based on our pavement deformation mapping we believe that lateral
near surface earth movement and or creep at the site is still active
Based on the interpretation from Inclinometer Nos I 1 and I 3 it appears that the
sliding zone may be relatively shallow approximately 11 to 15 feet below the ground
surface in the area near Boring Nos 1 and 3 Inclinometer No I 4 is located between
Inclinometer Nos I 2 and 3 It appears that the slide zone near Boring No 4 may be
located slightly deeper than Inclinometer Nos I 1 and I 3 at a depth of approximately
20 feet below the ground surface
We will continue monitoring the lateral ground movement through the installed
instrumentations and mapping the pavement conditions
