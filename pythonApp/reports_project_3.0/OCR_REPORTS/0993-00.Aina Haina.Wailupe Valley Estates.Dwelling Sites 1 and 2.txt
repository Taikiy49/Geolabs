PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this soil engineering investigation was to
explore the subsurface soil and groundwater conditions at
the site to provide soil data for use in the design of the
site grading for construction of the private roadways and
two dwelling sites for the proposed subdivision development
Our work was done in accordance with our proposals dated
November 10 and 21 1980
Our study consisted of 32 test pits excavation disturbed
sampling laboratory testing and site reconnaissance of
the area The results of our field exploration and labora
tory tests which were used in the engineering analysis
and formulated our conclusions and recommendations are
presented in the appendix of this report
The approximate boring and test pit locations are shown on
Plate 1 Site Plan and the logs are presented in Appendix A
Information regarding the field exploration and laboratory
testing are presented in the appendices

It is presently planned to construct two dwelling sites
about 300 and 1700 feet from the end of Hao Street and a
private access road to the proposed dwelling sites

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is situated on the western valley side
Slopes in upper Wailupe Valley beyond the existing resi
dential homes along Hao Street
Dwelling site No 1 is situated about 300 feet north of
Hao Street about elevation 310 to 340 feet The existing
ground slopes down toward the east at ratios of about 4 1
H V to 2 1 H V
Dwelling site No 2 is situated about 1700 feet north of
Hao Street at about elevation 340 to 360 feet The
existing ground slopes down toward the southeast at
ratios of about 4 1 H V
The proposed access road extends from the end of Hao Street
at elevation 260 feet to Dwelling Site No 2 at elevation
350 feet The natural hillside along the proposed alignment
slopes down toward the east at slopes ratios of about 4 1
to 2 1 H V with locally steeper areas along the stream
banks and hiking trail cuts

Small drainageways were present throughout the site extending

not very prominent within the areas investigated
Outside access into the sites was through a locked gate
located at the end of Hao Street
Vegetation throughout the site consisted of a heavy growth
of trees Haole Koa and shrubs
Geology
The overall project site is geologically mapped to consist
of an older alluvium in its higher elevations closer to the
valley wall and younger alluvium in the lower areas along
and around the streambed
The older alluvium is expressed as being a part of the large
talus embankment which commonly forms against the base of
the valley walls It is generally comprised of consolidated
deposits which are mottled in color and deeply weathered
The younger alluvium is usually found mantling the older
alluvium in lower elevations closer to the stream The
younger alluvium is generally comprised of clays and silts
along with unconsolidated deposits of sand gravel cobbles
and boulders Clay deposits in certain areas above the
streambed covering the older alluvium are also a part of
the younger alluvium

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration and site reconnaissance disclosed
that the site is situated on a talus deposit along the
lower hillside slopes of Wailupe Valley Typically
these talus deposits are quite variable and hetero
geneous
In general the development site is blanketed by a layer
of adobe clay and boulders underlain by more stable
and dense boulder colluvium The field exploration
disclosed that the adobe clay layer is generally
relatively thin at the proposed dwelling sites 1 and
2 and most of the proposed access road _The thickness
of the adobe clay appears to increase downslope toward
the existing stream Below the proposed access road the
thickness of the adobe clay increases to 10 to 15 feet
or more
Overcast adobe clay probably due to previous uncon
trolled grading work was also encountered in the lower
portions of the site near the end of Hao Street
The approximate limit of the thick adobe clay is shown
on the Site Plan Plate 1

Site Grading
Adobe Stabilization
The field exploration encountered relatively thick
deposits of adobe clay from 5 to 15 feet deep
in the lower areas of the southern portion of the
site The existing ground slopes in this area
Portions of the proposed access roadway embankment
fill will extend over these adobe areas
The adobe clay soil are generally very weak soils and
their shear strength can be significantly lowered by
water saturation or increase of the moisture content
Also they will be subject to shrink swell and down
slope creep or landslide movement during extensive
wet periods Therefore residual shear strength
values which are significantly lower than the normally
used peak shear values have been used to evaluate the
long term slope stability of these soils
In order to reduce the possibility of slope creep or
later landslide movement along the access roadway
we recommend that the adobe clay deposit be stripped
off below the embankment fill and backfilled with select
on site material Based on the results of our recent
test pits it appears that the adobe removal below the
existing ground would extend down to depths of about 2
feet at the up hill end of the access roadway and possi

In order to reduce the amount of embankment fill

Site Preparation Compaction Requirements
The non adobe on site material generally less than 12 inches
in size can be used for the proposed fills The adobe
clay soils should not be used in the embankment fills
Prior to filling the site should be cleared of vegetation
and debris then scarified moisture conditioned and recom
pacted to 90 of its maximum dry density established by
ASTM D 1557
Fills should be placed in 10 inch maximum horizontal lifts
compacted to the above specified density Sufficient water
should be incorporated in the fill material to obtain the
required optimum moisture content
Fills placed on slopes having grades steeper than 5 horizon
tal to 1 vertical should be keyed and benched into the exis
ting firm ground For details see Plate 3 enclosed in the
Appendix
Slopes
Slope ratios of 2 horizontal to 1 vertical can be used for
cut and fill slopes in non adobe material Flatter slope
ratio of 3 1 H V can be used for cut slopes in adobe
clay less than 6 feet high
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 993 00 P 10
For cut and fill slopes greater than 15 feet in vertical
height 8 foot wide drainage benches should be placed at
mid height of the slope
The drainage benches should be designed to intercept and
divert surface water into suitable outlets Surface
water should be diverted away from the top of slope and
Slope planting utilized to reduce erosion as soon as
practicable
Boulder Fill
Many surface boulders were noted around the site In
addition a large quantity of boulders will be generated
from the cutting operation If necessary these boulders
could be used in the deeper fill areas The recommended
rock disposal procedures should be as follows
1 The rocks and boulders should be scattered
2 Sandy material with less than 15 percent fines
should be used to fill the voids between boulders
by flooding The boulder fill layer should be
located a minimum of 8 feet below the finished
pad and roadway grades and below future swimming
pool and utility line invert elevations
3 After adequate filling of the voids between boulders
are completed the upper eight 8 feet of the com
pacted fill should consist of select on site soils
non adobe materials from the upper cut areas

by the soils engineer and areas with sufficient fill
for disposal should be delineated by the project sur

Future homeowners should be advised of this fact and that
any future improvements consisting of excavation such as
swimming pool may encounter such rock and boulders at
shallow depths
Alternate boulder disposal methods such as off site dis
posal areas rip rap or rock wall utilization could also
be used
We would be happy to provide you with additional recommenda

Foundations
Due to the sloping terrain and heterogeneous soil deposits
at the site we strongly recommend that post and beam con
struction be utilized for the proposed residences
Individual post footings which consist of pour in place
concrete should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below
level ground surface to resist erosion and heaving of the
on site soils
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 993 00 P 12
Where foundations are situated on sloping terrain deeper
pour in place concrete pier foundations which extend down
ward below the ground surface a sufficient depth to obtain
a minimum 6 foot horizontal set back distance measured from
the outer edge of the pier footing out to the slope face
are recommended
For the light loads anticipated an allowable bearing value
of 3000 p s f on stiff natural boulder colluvium and
2500 p s f on compacted fill can be used Generally
footings on adobe soil are not recommended Where
adobe soil is encountered at the footing bottom they
should be removed and replaced with lean concrete It
is critical that all surface water should be diverted
away from the house slab and footing foundations
Where little or no cut is planned some amount of adobe
clay may be encountered at finished grade Garage slabs
and walkways should not bear on these expansive soils
Where encountered the adobe soil should be overexca
vated and replaced with either quarry waste or acceptable
non expansive select on site soils

Utilities
In order to reduce accumulation of water around the utility
lines from seepage leakage etc which could saturate
and weaken the adobe soils we recommend that clean gravel
be used as cushion material below the pipes The trench and
gravel cushion layer should be shaped to drain water into
catch basins or manholes Subdrains are required at various
locations to allow drainage and relief of potential water
seepage into the utility trenches The locations of the
subdrain will be staked out in the field by the soils
engineer during the time of construction Flexible joints
should be provided for all utility connections
Boulder Protection
The upper hillside lots may be subject to boulders rolling
down the hillside
The falling boulders are a general phenomena occurring
in all ridge slopes on Oahu Various methods of boulder
barriers or catchments can be used to reduce the hazard
of falling boulders The degree of safety of preventing
falling boulder hazards is a function of economics For
this site various methods of boulder prevention can be
utilized such as
1 A boulder catchment area about 10 to
15 feet wide with a fence or wall
could be utilized near the property
boundary to catch rolling boulders
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 993 00 P 15
The concrete interceptor ditch along
the upper boundaries of the site could
be used in this capacity
2 Boulder sweeps along the upper boundaries
of the site could be performed to remove
the loose boulders that appear likely to
roll down
3 The natural trees and vegetation along the
upper portions of the site should be retained
to act as a natural barrier against the boulders
and to reduce erosion
4 Where practicable the houses should be set
back away from the toe of the hillside slope
to create an additional boulder catchment
area
It must be pointed out that whatever methods are used an
inherent risk of falling boulders could still exist for these
hillside lots
The state of the art of soil engineering practice has not
advanced to a point that a solution is available for this
area It is our opinion that no matter what one does
there is always the risk of boulders rolling down the
hill if not from areas near the house it will come from
areas high up on the slope Whatever measure is chosen
it only reduces the risk It should be pointed out that

no guarantee against future falling boulders can be made
due to changes in soil and rock conditions with time
weathering erosion earthquake and construction vibra
tion and improvements and construction activities at and
around the site
The owners of these hillside lots should be made aware of
the aforementioned limitations and cautioned against future
improvements in the upper areas of the lots which may disturb
the stability of the existing boulders
