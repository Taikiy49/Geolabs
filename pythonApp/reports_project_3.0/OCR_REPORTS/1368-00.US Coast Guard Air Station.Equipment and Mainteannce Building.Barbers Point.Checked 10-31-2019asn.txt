BARBERS POINT OAHU HAWAII
This report presents the results of our soils investigation
performed for the proposed Corrosion Control Ground Support Equipment
and Maintenance Building at the U S Coast Guard Air Station Barbers
Point Oahu Hawaii The general location of the project site and its
vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate l
The purpose of our study was to investigate the subsurface
conditions by drilling of borings and soil sampling Data obtained from
the borings and laboratory testing of soil samples were utilized to
provide recommendations for the design of site grading foundation and
pavement at the subject project Our work was performed in general
accordance with the scope of work outlined in our proposal dated January
6th
W Page 2
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is our understanding that the proposed building will be a one
story concrete masonry structure 25 feet high with on grade concrete
Slabs and prestressed concrete roof
It is our understanding that the proposed building will have a
paint spray booth that can accommodate H 52 helicopters Hydraulic lift
equipment and a service pit will be included in the ground support
equipment maintenance area
The maximun column loads are anticipated to be on the order of 70
kips and the maximum wall loads on the order of 6 kips per linear foot
Site improvements will include a new asphalt concrete paved service
road and apron Imported fill will be required to raise the site grade
by about to 2 feet
The proposed building layout is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2

SITE CONDITIONS
The proposed site is located about 160 feet west of the existing
hangar and administration building Building 1790 just north of Coral
Sea Road inside the Barbers Point Naval Air Station Ewa Oahu At the
time of our exploration the site was relatively level with landscaped
areas on the eastern half portion Most of the proposed building site
was in roughly level area occupied by large trees The proposed asphalt
concrete areas were covered with grasses A patch of broken up concrete
slab area was located on the northern side of the building area
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
The site is located in the extensive Ewa coastal plain which
consists of emerged coral reef limestone deposits with thin soil
overburden These reef deposits are most likely the result of the Kaena
95 stand of the sea and have been modified by chemical weathering
Subsurface Exploration
Six borings were drilled and sampled to depths ranging from 11 5 to
16 5 feet below the existing ground surface to explore the subsurface
conditions at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate
2 Detailed description of the field exploration and laboratory testing
are presented in the appendices along with the boring logs and
laboratory test results

Soil Conditions
Based on the borings the site is underlain by a thin veneer of
brown silt topsoil in the existing landscaped area a thin layer of
dense coral sand and gravel fill of about 0 5 to 1 foot underlain by
cemented coral deposits to at least 16 5 feet the maximum depth of our
borings The coral consisted mainly of cemented coral sand and gravel
which is believed to be coral algal reef detritus Although not
encountered in our drilled borings pockets of filled and unfilled
solution cavity in the coral limestone deposit might be encountered in
the general project area in Ewa
Groundwater was encountered in the borings and the depths measured
are shown on the boring logs In general the groundwater can be
expected at a depth about 8 feet below the existing ground or about one
foot above mean sea level and may fluctuate due to tidal influences
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Building Foundation
Based on our findings we believe that a competent coral formation
exists at a shallow depth within the proposed site and it will be ideal
to provide support for spread footing foundation We recommend that the
bottom of major footing be probed to detect potential cavities which
might exist near the ground surface
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1368 00 Page 5
An allowable net soil bearing up to 6000 p s f may be used for
footing on coral deposit It may be increased by one third when
considering temporary wind or seismic loads A minimum footing size of
16 inches should be used
Cavity Probing and Grouting
eA ne oe ee a em ea nae a a em ne a ee
It should be noted that the coral formation frequently contains
filled and unfilled voids or cavities After the site is cleared
for building construction the foundation area especially below
the footings should be probed for possible cavity or cavities It
is recommended that a 3 inch diameter probe hole be drilled at each
column location or on 8 foot centers along continuous wall
footings as appropriate The probe holes should be drilled to a
minimum of 5 feet below the bottom of the footing The resulting
probe holes should be backfilled with neat cement grout mix
injected at low pressures
If cavities are encountered they should be grouted with cement or
concrete grout with a compressive strength of at least 2000 p s i
For small cavities with height of less than 6 inches measured at
the probe location cement grout should be used For cavities with
height exceeding 6 inches concrete grout may have to be used In
such a case the probe hole may need to be enlarged
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1368 00 Page 6
If excessive grout take is noted filling should be temporary
suspended to allow the grout to set Thereafter the filling
should then be resumed The temporary suspension of filling is to
limit the excessive grout take
We recommend that the probing and grouting operations be monitored
by a soils engineer
It is possible that cavities may still be encountered during
footing excavations even after probing When encountered these
cavities should be cleaned of soils or other debris and backfilled
with lean concrete
All footing excavations should be inspected by a soils engineer to
confirm the conditions upon which our design recommendations were based
It is anticipated that the total and differential settlements if
constructed according to the above design criteria should be negligible
Site Grading
All vegetation debris and other deleterious material should be
removed from the proposed building and A C pavement areas The thin
veneer of brown silt topsoil should be stripped and stockpiled for
future landscaping use The existing broken up concrete slab should be
removed if situated in the proposed building and AC areas
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1368 00 Page 7
The existing trees and roots should be cleared and grubbed and the
ground surface levelled Roots in excess of inches in diameter
should be removed The coral fragments on the surface may be crushed by
earth moving equipment Any ground cavity encountered at the surface
within the project area should be cleaned to its bottom or at least 3
feet below the finished pavement surface whichever is shallower and
then backfilled with compacted on site coral sand and gravel
Imported fill material will be required to raise the site grade
For such purpose the coral material from the Barbers Point area may be
used as engineered fill provided that large particles are screened off
or crushed down to 3 inches or smaller in sizes Oversized fragments
should not be used as fill
All imported materials for fill under the pavement or slab areas
should be granular non expansive materials with maximum particle size
not exceeding 3 inches and have a minimum laboratory CBR value of 12
when compacted to 95 of the ASTM D 1557 78 maximum density
Any material to be imported should be tested and approved by the
soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
All fill and backfill material should be placed in horizontal lifts
not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a dry density
of at least 95 of the ASTM D 1557 78 maximum density
GEOLABS HAWAII

On Grade Slab
After the site has been filled on grade slab may be used for the
floors receiving only the pedestrian traffic The on grade slab should
be placed over a 4 inch cushion layer of gravel size crushed rock
material such as No 3 Fine rock to provide uniform support A
moisture barrier membrane should be placed under the slabs
The slabs may be of conventional design The thickened edges
exposed to any unpaved area should be embedded at least 8 inches below
the lowest adjacent grade
Pavement
The coral sand and gravel surface would provide good subgrade
support for the pavements when compacted However the subgrade will
most likely be the imported fill material with a minimum CBR value of 12
as recommended in the Site Grading section
For the proposed Corrosion Control GSE building slabs that may
include pavement loading of H 52 Helicopter 8300 lbs and highway type
trucks up to 18 kip axle loads the following pavement sections may be
used
Rigid Pavement
concrete flexural strength 600 p s i at 28 days
6 0 Inch Portland Cement Concrete
6 0 Inch Aggregate Base
95 compaction with minimum CBR of 30
12 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness

The subgrade should be compacted in accordance with our
recommendations presented under the Site Grading section
CBR tests should be performed on the actual subgrade soil samples
during construction and the above design section can be revised if
hecessary The recommended section considers medium traffic and that
good drainage will be provided for adjacent to paved areas
Vehicle Service Pit
It is our understanding that a service pit about 5 feet deep would
be required to service various vehicles at the maintenence shop It is
anticipated that the excavation will mostly be in the coral rock after
penetrating through the floor slabs and 2 feet of new fill and or coral
sand and gravel
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Page 10
In general we believe that the upper portion of the excavation in
fill may need to be shored or sloped back on a H V ratio to
minimize sloughing The lower portion of excavation in coral may be
cut in a near vertical sides except where cavities exist The
excavation should be shored according to government regulations
If the pit is to be over excavated formed concreted and
backfilled we recomended that the walls of the pit be designed to
resist lateral earth pressures of 50 p s f per foot of depth We
believe that build up of hydrostatic pressures in the material behind
the wall is not likely due to relatively pervious nature of the coral
formation
Design Review
Plans and specifications prepared for site grading cavity probing
and grouting foundation and pavement should be forwarded to the soils
engineer for review and written comments prior to construction to see
that the intent of our recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the clearing and grubbing cavity probing
and grouting engineered fill placement and compaction foundation
excavation backfilling and subgrade preparation be observed by the
soils engineer during construction This is to see that adequate
compaction has been obtained and the subsurface conditions are
consistent with the findings of our investigation

If conditions encountered are different from those presented in
this report then appropriate modifications to the design should be
made
INVESTIGATION LIMITATIONS
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommen
dations made in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation
engineering principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all
other warranties either expressed or implied
The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are
based on our site reconnaissance soil information from boring data and
laboratory tests obtained from this soil investigation
Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encountered and cannot
be fully determined by reconnaissance and soil borings These
unforeseen conditions such as pockets of organic soils cavities
trash fill or expansive soils may occur in localized areas
Therefore additional expenditures may be needed during construction to
attain a properly constructed project Some contingency fund is thus
recommended to accommodate these possible costs

the preparation of site grading plans and foundation design of this
project

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
ee ee
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by
drilling six 6 test to 16 5 feet below the
existing ground surface at the approximate locations shown on
the Site Plan Plate 2 A truck mounted drill rig using 4 inch
diameter continuous flight auger was used to drill the
borings In addition a bulk sample of the surface coral
material was obtained near Boring 1 location
The soils and coral encountered in the borings were
Classified by visual and textural examination in the field by
one of our engineers who continuously monitored the drilling
operations The classifications were further reviewed by
visual inspection and testing in the laboratory The soils
were classified in gerneral conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A graphic presentation of the materials
encountered in the borings are presented in the Boring Logs
In the borings soil samples were obtained by driving a
barrel sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The
blow counts used to drive the sampler for 12 inches are shown
on the boring logs at the appropriate sample depths
