INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our soil investigation
performed for the proposed aircraft rinse facility at Marine Corps Air
Station at Kaneohe Bay Hawaii The general location of the project site
and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed project is to construct an unattended taxi through
treacle operated fresh water deluge system designed to remove salt and
other water soluble corrosive contaminants from fixed wing aircraft
operating at MCAS Kaneohe Bay
W G 1559 00 Page 2
The project includes the construction of the following
1 Concrete pavement area of 910 SY constructed te the same load
bearing capacity as existing taxiways
2 Aircraft access from Taxiway A of 900 LF constructed to the
same load bearing capacity as the taxiway
3 Water storage tank of 4 000 gallon capacity
4 Oil water separator of 4 000 gallon capacity
The proposed site grading involves cuts of up to 18 feet deep on
the higher eastern portion of the site
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of the investigation was to evaluate the subsurface
soil conditions at the site to provide scil data for the design of the
pavement thickness required for the new pavement and to determine the
appropriate cut slope ratio for the site grading
The scope of our investigation which was performed in general
accordance with our revised proposal of included field
reconnaissance drilling and sampling of six 6 borings six 6 field
CBR tests laboratory soil testing engineering analysis utilizing the
field and laboratory data and report preparation

also excavated to the subgrade level in order to further study the soil
condition at the proposed pavement subgrade level and to obtain soil
samples for laboratory CBR tests
Detailed description of our field exploration laboratory testing
boring logs and test pit logs are presented in the appendices The
approximate locations of the borings test pits and field CBR tests are
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
GENERAL SITE CONDITIONS
The proposed site is located adjacent to Taxiway A the primary
taxiway serving Runway 04 22 The site is approximately 750 feet south
of Runway 22 and 1 500 feet north of the intersection of Mokapu Road and
the Runway
The site is an unoccupied area about one mile north of the
industrial aircraft operations area of the Station About half of the
site adjacent to Taxiway A is level and clear while the remaining areas
are covered with brush grown on a natural slope with grades of
approximate H V

sampling six 6 borings to depths of 10 to 20 feet below the existing

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The overall subsoil condition at the site was found to be competent
for the construction of the proposed aircraft rinse facility
The general project description indicated that the rinse facility
pavements should be designed to the same load bearing capacity as the
existing taxiways However since the rinse facility serves only the
fixed wing aircrafts it is felt that the pavements can be constructed to
a considerably less load bearing capacity than that of the existing
taxiways which also serves heavy aircrafts for example the C 14l1 s
Based on the above rigid pavement consisting of 8 5 inches of concrete
for the rinse area and flexible pavement with total thickness of 22
inches for the access areas are recommended This compares with 12
inches of concrete rigid pavement or 30 5 inches of total flexible
pavement thickness presently being used for the taxiway reconstruction
The proposed cut slope ratio of approximately 2 1 H V should
provide sufficient factor of safety against failure Because of the
presence of large size boulders and possible rock formation the grading
operation may require hard ripping or other special excavating methods
Provisions should be made during site grading to dispose the boulders
off site

Site Grading
Site grading consists of cutting down the existing slope to the
final design gradient which in our opinion should not exceed 2 1
H V Along the northeastern edge of the existing slope cuts of up to
18 feet are expected Because of the presence of large size boulders or
rock formation provisions should be made during the site grading to
dispose the boulders off site
The areas which are to receive pavement should be scarified to a
depth of at least 6 inches below the subgrade elevation moisture
conditioned and recompacted to 100 of its maximum density as established
by ASTM D 1557 78 test method
Soft spots encountered at the subgrade level should be removed and
replaced with on site soil compacted to a minimum of 100 of its maximum
dry density
If boulders are encountered near the subgrade level they should be
removed for a minimum depth of 12 inches below the subgrade level and the
resulting excavation backfilled with soil compacted to 100 of its
maximum dry density

Water Storage Tank and Oil Water Separator
A 4 000 gallon underground water storage tank and an oil water
separator of 4 000 gallon capacity are required to be constructed
adjacent to the aircraft rinse facility
For both structures to be constructed or buried on the in situ
soil an allowable bearing capacity of is recommended All
bearing layers should be compacted to at least 90 of maxinum density as
established by ASTM D 1557 78 test methods Minimal settlement less
than 1 4 inch is anticipated
For backfill material directly around the tank well graded
granular material such as base course rock sand or equivalent should be
used For backfill on top of the tank the excavated on site material
free of expansive component may be used All imported fill material
should consist of select non expansive silty sandy or granular material
less than 3 inches in size with less than 20 passing the 200 sieve

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the site grading subgrade preparations and
installation of tank be observed by the soils engineer during
construction to see that adequate compaction has been obtained and that
the subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of our
investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
If conditions encountered during construction are different from
those presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made
