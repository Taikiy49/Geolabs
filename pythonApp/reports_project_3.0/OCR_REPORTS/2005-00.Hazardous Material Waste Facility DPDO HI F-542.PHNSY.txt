This report presents the results of our geotechnical exploration
performed for the proposed Hazardous Material Waste Facility DPDO Hawaii
at its revised location in Pearl Harbor Naval Shipyard Oahu Hawaii The
general location of the project site and its vicinity is shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1
The purpose of our study was to explore the subsurface conditions to
provide pertinent geotechnical data for the formulation of recommendations
for the foundation design of the project Our work was performed in
general accordance with the scope of services outlined in our proposal
dated
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations

PROJECT CONSIDERATION
It is proposed to construct an incombustible building for hazardous
material waste storage along with a loading and unloading area with
containment sumps and an attached PWc facility building The building
will be a one story prefabricated rigid frame steel structure and concrete
slab with CMU perimeter walls The proposed layout of the building is
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
It is our understanding that the concrete floor slabs and the joints
are to be constructed liquid tight It is further understood that
4000 lb capacity forklift trucks will be operated on the floor slabs
The maximum column loads are anticipated to be on the order of 34
kips per column and the wall loads on the order of 2 6 kips per linear
foot
The loading and unloading area will have 6 inch high curbs and
concrete pavement capable to serve fully loaded tractor trailer
and or capacity forklift trucks The forklift trucks could
result in a total load of with 80 to 90 percent of that load
concentrated on one axle It is our understanding that the driveway
outside the loading and unloading area will be paved with asphaltic
concrete
SITE CONDITIONS
The project site has been relocated from the northwest side to the
northeast side of the intersection of 6th Street and South Avenue in the
Naval Shipyard Pearl Harbor on the island of Oahu Hawaii

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
The project site is located on the Honolulu Ewa Coastal Plain This
plain is a sedimentary wedge composed of intercalated marine and lagoonal
sediments terrigeneous sediments and pyroclastic deposits from the
Honolulu Volcanic Series
In the immediate vicinity of the project site from the Pleistocene to
historic times the depositional environment was that of a lagoonal or
estuary characterized by the low energy accumulation of silts sands and
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2005 00 Page 4
Dk No 51
gravels with sparse growths of coral algal reef masses At some point
during the Pleistocene volcanic activity along the Salt
Lake Aliamanu Makalapa Rift of the Honolulu Volcanic Series laid down ash
deposits which consolidated to form volcanic tuff
During historic times as the facilities at Pearl Harbor were
developed and improved the area was graded and low areas were filled

Based on our previous Boring No B 1 the southeastern half of the

A C over 8 to 24 inches of crushed rock and sand fill However the new
Boring No TB 3 indicates the presence of two 2 separate concrete slabs

DISCUSSION AND RE COMMENDATIONS
General
It is our understanding that the floor slabs of the proposed
structures will be designed to be liquid tight for hazardous
material waste storage while allowing the operation of 4000 1b capacity
forklift trucks
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2005 00 Page 6
Dk No 51
The new concrete slabs will be located over existing structures such
as concrete slabs A C pavement wall footings etc and in places a
soil layer To minimize the potential of developing cracks in these new
slabs that may span over hard and soft areas we recommend that a layer of
granular base course be provided This may be achieved by raising the
finished floor grades so that at least 6 inches of base course may be
placed prior to placement of the new slabs
To provide drainage of any free water which may accumulate below the
new slabs and could weaken the slab subgrade support the existing A c or
concrete slabs should be perforated by drilling 4 inch minimum diameter
holes at 8 feet intervals The drilled holes should extend down below the
water level to about elevation 100 feet U S Navy Datum and should be
backfilled with 3B Fine crushed rock All backfill aggregate should be
adequately tamped in about 1 foot lifts to minimize future backfill
settlements
Another alternative would be to completely remove the existing
concrete slabs in the scrap yard for the proposed warehouse construction
If the new concrete slabs are to be placed over the existing walls
footings or small concrete blocks then these concrete structures should
be removed to at least twelve 12 inches below the bottom of the new
slabs and their thickened edges
In general it is our opinion that the underlying in situ subgrade
Soils are adequate for lightly loaded spread footing foundation support
Any obstacles posed by the existing structures should be removed prior to
placement of the new footings on the underlying stiff subsoils
Site Preparation
All vegetation trees debris and other deleterious materials
including rail ties see Navy Drawings I N5 1228 and 1229 should be
cleared grubbed and removed from the site The existing steel plates

should be removed and the underlying A C should be broken up The
existing concrete slabs and A c overlay in the scrap yard area should be
perforated if they are to be left in place below the new fill The
existing structures such as concrete walls buildings sump pits etc
should be removed to provide a minimum of twelve 12 inches of vertical
separation between the existing structure and the bottom of the new slabs
The existing slabs in the one story buildings should be removed
Any soft or weak spots disclosed during site preparation should be
repaired by excavating the soft or weak soils to firm ground and
backfilling with compacted on site granular coral material
After demolition clearing and grubbing the surface of the subgrade
soils to receive fill should be scarified to a depth of about ten 10
inches moisture conditioned and then recompacted to at least 95 of the
ASTM D 1557 78 maximum dry density
In the new loading and unloading area where heavy truck trailer and
forklift truck operations are planned the top portion of the existing
coral sand and gravel material within 2 feet below the finished grade
should be recompacted to 95 relative compaction If a portion of this
area is situated in the cut area we believe that such a densification may
be achieved by rolling the in place material with a heavy vibratory
roller
The on site coral material may be used as engineered fill provided
that large particles are removed or broken down to 3 inches or less in
size Oversized fragments should not be used as fill
All imported materials for structural fill under the pavement section
or warehouse areas should be granular materials with maximum particle size
not exceeding three 3 inches and have a minimum laboratory CBR value of
30 when compacted to 95 of the ASTM D 1557 78 maximum dry density

Any imported material should be non expansive tested and approved by
the project geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
All fill and backfill materials should be placed in horizontal lifts
not exceeding eight 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a dry
density of at least 95 of the ASTM D 1557 78 maximum dry density
Building Foundation
Based on our findings we believe that the minimum 9 foot thick crust
of dense stiff subsoils will be adequate to support spread footing type of
foundation provided the footing size is limited to a maximum of 4 by 4
feet to reduce the bearing stress on the underlying loose lagoonal
deposits encountered at the eastern portion of the site
The footing should be embedded at least twenty four 24 inches below
the lowest adjacent finished grade and bear on dense in situ coral sand
and gravel Uplift wind or other loading conditions may further
increase the embedment of the footing foundations The disturbed bottom
of the footing excavations should be recompacted to at least 95 relative
compaction based on ASTM D 1557 78
An allowable soil bearing capacity of up to 3500 p s f may be used
This value may be increased by one third when temporary wind or seismic
loads are considered
Based on the above recommended allowable bearing values estimated
settlements are on the order of less than one half of an inch total and
one quarter of an inch differential
Resistance to lateral loads for the proposed building can be obtained
by passive soil pressure and frictional resistance A frictional
coefficient of 0 4 may be used to compute the frictional resistance

addition an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 p s f per foot of depth may

existing slabs or foundations In general we believe that if these

Construction Observation
It is recommended that the project geotechnical engineer be retained
to provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the
excavation and foundation phases of the work This is to observe
compliance with the design concepts specifications or recommendations and
to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ
from that anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations
given in this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as borings
Variations of conditions between the field data points may occur and the
nature and extent of these variations may not become evident until
construction If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to
re evaluate the recommendations given in this report
The location of the field data points were determined by scaling from
the Site Plan Elevations of the data points were also determined from
the Site Plan The physical location and elevation of the field data
points should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the
method used

The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross section are based upon interpolation between field data
points and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
Water level data from the field data points was collected at the
times given on the graphic representations and or in the text of this
report This data has been reviewed and interpretations made in the
formulation of this report However it must be noted that fluctuation
may occur due to variations in rainfall tides temperature and other
factors
