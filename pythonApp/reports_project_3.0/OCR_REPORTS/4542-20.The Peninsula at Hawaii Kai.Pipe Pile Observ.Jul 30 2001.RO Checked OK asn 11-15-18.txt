Based on our borings the project site is generally underlain by surface fills over loose
lagoonal deposits cinder sands and volcanic tuff Due to the presence of loose lagoonal
deposits which may be susceptible to liquefaction under seismic loading a 30 foot building
set back distance from the seawall was established for the project site Because several
Product 3 houses along the western seawall in Parcel A encroach the 30 foot set back
distance a pipe pile foundation system was recommended in order to provide stability of the
houses in the event that potential lateral ground movements occur during a seismic event
Based on the relatively light loads provided by the structural engineer we
recommended that the pipe pile foundations consist of grouted 6 inch diameter pipe piles
extending to the volcanic tuff formation In order to provide adequate cover for corrosion
protection the 6 inch diameter pipe piles would be inserted in a drilled column of at least
12 inches in diameter
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Based on our field exploration the project site is generally underlain by surface fills over
lagoonal deposits cinder sands and volcanic tuff Our borings generally encountered surface
fills extending to about 5 5 to 11 feet below the ground surface In general the surface fills
consist of medium dense to very dense silty sands with gravel The surface fills were generally
underlain by lagoonal deposits extending to about 13 to 22 feet below the ground surface The
lagoonal deposits generally consisted of very loose to loose silty sands Our borings
encountered dense to very dense cinder sands underlying the lagoonal deposits extending
to about 20 to 35 feet below the ground surface Relatively competent volcanic tuff was
generally encountered below the cinder sands to about 50 8 feet below the ground surface
Groundwater levels were observed at elevations between 3 3 feet and 0 7 feet MSL
during our field exploration Due to the proximity of the project site to the Kuapa Pond it is
anticipated that the groundwater level will fluctuate with tidal changes rainfall and other
factors

The installation of the production shafts was performed by Foundation International Inc
A total of 59 pipe piles designated as No 1 through 59 were installed between June 18 and
The approximate locations of the 59 pipe piles are shown on the Site Plans
Plates 2 1 through 2 3 A representative from Geolabs observed the drilling pipe pile
installation and concreting process during the construction of these pipe piles Due to the
limited number of pipe piles and relatively low structural loads provided for this project a test
pile and or a load test was not specified in the contract documents

The pipe piles were drilled using a track mounted drill rig equipped with a 12 inch
diameter auger The pipe piles generally extended at least 2 feet below volcanic tuff formation
or dense materials encountered during the drilling operation The pipe piles were drilled to
depths ranging between about 21 2 to 26 feet below the ground surface Due to the soft
subsurface condition and relatively shallow groundwater table the drilled holes were cased
with a 14 inch outside diameter temporary casing The soil in the cased holes was generally
cleaned using a clean out bucket The 6 inch diameter steel pipes were installed after the
holes have been cleaned out Due to the shallow groundwater table the concreting of the pipe
piles were conducted using tremmie methods The Pipe Pile Summary is presented both by
Based on our field observations we believe that the 59 pipe piles installed for
Product 3 houses have been installed in general conformance with the project plans and
specifications and within the limits of generally accepted construction practices Based on our
observations of the pipe pile installation it is our opinion that the pipe piles have been installed
to the required diameter and depth to provide the design load capacity
