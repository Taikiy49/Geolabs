PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The planned Kukui ula Resort Community development encompasses about
1 045 acres in Koloa on the south shore of Kauai and includes hotel vacation ownership
commercial park golf course and residential uses The project is a part of the
improvements for Kukui ula Resort Community
Subdivision Y is in the east central portion of the development west of Ala Kalani
Kaumaka Western Bypass Road The parcel will be subdivided into 88 lots of varying
sizes We understand that Maryl Pacific Group will construct houses on up to 53 of the
lots The houses are anticipated to consist of light one and two story wood structures with
raised floors supported on spread continuous footings

road and infrastructure construction and presented the findings and recommendations in

The shallow subsurface conditions particularly the presence and thickness of
expansive clay have a significant effect on the house foundation details Therefore
two to three shallow test pits were recently excavated at each lot to establish the probable
thickness of expansive clay present Since the lots were graded to about 1 foot below
finished grade the critical depth of interest is about 2 feet below existing grade If at least
2 feet of clay is currently present the house foundations will be designed for the expansive
clay condition Therefore the test pits were limited to about 2 feet maximum in order to
reduce disturbance to the compacted fills The approximate test pit locations are shown
on the Summary Plan Plate 1 The test pit results are summarized on Plates 2 through

HOUSE FOUNDATION RECOMMENDATIONS
The finished grades at the lots were raised from 0 to 3 feet above the existing
ground surface during the mass grading Additionally the lots were over excavated to
break up the rock formation and backfilled with borrow soils from various locations The
fill material ranged from crushed rock from the crusher to on site screened material
6 In minus to brown clay imported from Golf Course Holes 5 and 6 Most of the brown
clay was found to have high expansion potential Therefore the material within the top
2 to 3 feet of finished grade would determine the foundation design of the new houses
Based on the results of the shallow test pits the lots may be categorized as
follows
Type 1 Expansive clay not found at finished grade
The soils encountered near finished grade generally consisted of low expansive
soils primarily 6 inch minus screened on site material Conventional house
foundations as described below may be used on these lots

Type 1A Expansive soil only at pool area
These are Type 1 lots except expansive clay was found at the pool area
Therefore conventional foundations may be used for the house but expansive
soil foundations apply to the pool
Type 2 Expansive soil conditions
These lots have at least 2 feet of expansive clay The expansive clay should be
removed to a minimum depth of 3 feet below finished grade within the house and
driveway footprint and for a distance of 3 feet beyond
Type 3 Expansive clay generally less than 1 foot thick
Expansive clay was found on these lots but they were generally less than 1 foot
thick and underlain by better low expansive soil For these lots the expansive clay
below foundations and pavements should be removed and backfilled with low
expansive soils then conventional foundations may be used
House Foundations
The houses will have raised wood floors supported by individual post footings
Shallow spread and or shallow drilled pier footings may be used for support of the
proposed residential structures provided they are embedded a minimum of 24 inches
below the finished grade
An allowable bearing pressure of 2 500 pounds per square foot psf may be
utilized for the design of residential building foundations bearing on properly compacted
for transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic forces Footings should
have a minimum width of 12 inches For footings constructed near tops of slopes or on
sloping ground the footings should be embedded deep enough to provide a minimum
horizontal set back distance of 5 feet measured from the outside edge of the footings to
the slope face
We estimate that settlements for footings bearing on properly compacted fill as
recommended herein to be less than inch total Differential settlement is estimated to
be less than inch between footings Soft and or loose materials encountered at the
bottom of footing excavations should be over excavated to expose the underlying firm
materials The over excavation should be backfilled with select granular material
compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction or the footing bottom may
be deepened to the underlying firm materials Foundations next to utility trenches or
easements should be embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V
imaginary plane extending upward from the bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep
as the inverts of the utility lines This requirement is necessary to avoid surcharging
adjacent below grade structures with additional structural loads and to reduce the

Over Excavation for Type 2 Lots
The Type 2 lots are underlain by near surface clayey soils with high expansion
potential when subjected to moisture fluctuations To reduce the potential for future
distress to the lightly loaded slabs on grade resulting from shrinking and swelling of the
clayey soils due to changes in the moisture content we recommend over excavation of
the expansive clay for a minimum depth of 36 inches below finished grade The subgrade
soils should then be moisture conditioned to a minimum of 2 above optimum moisture
content then capped with structural fill material capping fill The capping fill should also
extend beyond the house footprint a minimum of 36 inches
The non expansive select granular capping fill should be placed as soon as
possible after the subgrade soils are prepared in accordance with the recommendations
presented in the Site Preparation section herein The completed capping fill and the
underlying subgrade soils should be maintained at above optimum moisture content prior
to placement of concrete Where shrinkage cracks are observed after compaction of the
subgrade we recommend thoroughly moistening the soils to close the cracks Saturation
and subsequent yielding of the exposed subgrade due to inclement weather and poor
drainage may require over excavation of the soft areas and replacement with
well compacted engineered fill
Slabs On Grade
In general we recommend placing a minimum 4 inch thick layer of cushion fill
consisting of No 3 Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation below the slab to serve as
a capillary break and to provide more uniform support of the slab To reduce the potential
for moisture infiltration and subsequent damage to floor coverings we recommend placing
an impervious moisture barrier on top of the No 3 Fine gravel

Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork areas within the contract grading limits should be
cleared and grubbed thoroughly Vegetation debris deleterious material and other
unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed properly off site or stockpiled in a
designated area to reduce the potential for contamination of the excavated materials
Soft and yielding areas encountered during clearing and grubbing should be
over excavated to expose firm natural material and the resulting excavation should be
backfilled with well compacted engineered fill The excavated soft soils may be used as fill
provided that it meets the requirements for fill material
Prior to filling the existing ground should be scarified to a depth of 10 inches and
moisture conditioned to about 2 percent above the optimum moisture and compacted to a
minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Where shrinkage cracks are observed after
compaction of the subgrade we recommend thoroughly moistening the soils to close
cracks or preparing again as recommended above Saturation and subsequent yielding of
the exposed subgrade due to inclement weather and poor drainage may require
over excavation of the soft areas and replacement with well compacted engineered fill

Fills and Backfills
Our laboratory test results indicated a high swell potential for the on site clay
material Therefore the on site clayey material generally will not be suitable for fill
material within 3 feet of finish subgrade below slabs on grade
Base course material should consist of crushed basalt aggregate and should
conform to the County of Kauai Department of Public Works Standard Specifications for
Public Works Construction dated September 1986
Structural fill should consist of non expansive granular material such as crushed
coral basalt or cinder sand The material should be well graded from coarse to fine with
particles no larger than 3 inches in largest dimension and should contain between 10 and
30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve The material should have a laboratory
CBR value of 20 or more and should have a maximum swell of less than 1 percent when
tested in accordance with ASTM D should test imported fill materials
for conformance with these recommendations prior to delivery to the project site for its
intended use
Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
Fills and backfills should be moisture conditioned to about 2 percent above the
optimum moisture placed in level lifts not exceeding 12 inches in loose thickness and
compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction
Base course materials should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum
moisture placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to
a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place
dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil
established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 Optimum moisture is the water content
percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
Compaction should be accomplished by sheepsfoot rollers vibratory rollers
multiple wheel pneumatic tired rollers or other types of acceptable compaction equipment
Because moisture conditioning and compaction of the clayey subgrade soils are
critical elements of earthwork Geolabs Inc should conduct observations and soil density
tests during site grading to assist the contractor in obtaining the required degree of
compaction and the proper moisture content Where compaction is less than required
additional compactive effort should be applied with adjustment of moisture content as
necessary to obtain the specified compaction It should be noted that the moisture
requirement of the fills and subgrades about 2 percent above the optimum moisture is an
important requirement

In general for support of the utility lines we recommend using granular bedding
consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation under the
pipes The initial backfill up to about 1 foot above the pipes should consist of free draining
backfills such as No 3B Fine gravel to reduce the potential for damaging the pipes from
compaction of the backfill It is critical to use free draining granular material to reduce the
potential for formation of voids below the haunches of pipes and to provide adequate
support for the sides of the pipes The use of on site soils as backfill directly around utility
pipes is not recommended
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 1 foot above the pipes to the
finish subgrade should consist of the approved on site silty clay soils or select granular
material The backfill material should be moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above
the optimum moisture content placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose
thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the
potential for future ground subsidence The upper 2 feet of the trench backfill below the
pavement subgrade should be compacted to no less than 95 percent relative compaction
Mechanical compaction equipment should be used to compact the materials at the project
site Water tamping jetting or ponding should not be used to compact the backfill material
Drainage
The finished grades outside the houses should be sloped to shed water away from
foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also advised to install gutter
systems around the houses and to divert the discharge away from the foundation slab
areas Excessive landscape watering near the foundations and slabs should also be
avoided Planters or gravel strips next to foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to reduce the potential for water infiltration into the subsoils Drainage
swales should be provided as soon as possible and maintained to drain surface water
runoff away from the foundation and slabs
