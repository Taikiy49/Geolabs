Dear Mr Gomes
In accordance with your request we have been providing geotechnical engineering
observation services during the installation of jet grouted columns in support of the Hilton
Hawaiian Village 24 inch Off Site Sewer Relief Line project in Waikiki on the Island of Oahu
Hawaii This letter report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observation and
consultation services performed during the installation of the jet grouted columns for support
of the sewer line pipes between March 7 and March 16 2001
Project Considerations
The project site is located along Ala Moana Boulevard and Kalakaua Avenue in
Waikiki on the Island of Oahu Hawaii We understand that a new high rise hotel building is
currently under construction at the Hilton Hawaiian Village As part of the off site
improvements a new sewer relief line is planned from the existing sewer manhole at the
intersection of Ala Moana Boulevard and Kalia Road to the Fort DeRussy Wastewater Pump
Station located on Kalakaua Avenue A total of about 1 500 lineal feet of PVC sewer line will
be installed for this project The majority of the new sewer relief line will have a diameter of
24 inches Ai the Ala Moana Boulevard and Kalia Road intersection the new sewer line will
consist of 12 and 20 inch diameter pipes
During the commencement of construction of the new sewer relief line the contractor
Delta Construction Corporation encountered soft soils near the sewer line invert Due to
concerns over possible future settlement of the new sewer line Geolabs was contacted to
conduct a geotechnical engineering exploration and to provide consultation services
Based on our field exploration the project site in generally underlain by surface fills
over very loose to medium dense lagoonal deposits extending to about 29 to 35 feet below
the ground surface The lagoonal deposits were generally underlain by dense coralline
materials extending to the maximum depth explored of about 41 5 feet below the ground

Jet Grouted Column Installation
The jet grouted columns were installed between March 7 and March 16 2001 A
summary of the jet grouted columns is provided on Plates 1 1 through 1 5 In general the
jet grouting contractor Layne GeoConstruction was required to install 36 inch diameter
jet grouted columns with a 28 day unconfined compressive strength of at least 300 pounds
per square inch psi
For the project a total of 160 jet grouted columns were installed for a total of about
3 304 lineal feet of jet grouted columns The jet grouted columns were generally installed
along the 24 inch diameter sewer line alignment at about 10 feet center to center spacing
Where underground utility lines traversed the new sewer line alignment the column spacing
was adjusted The actual column spacing installed for the project ranged from about
7 to 13 feet center to center
In addition one jet grouted column was generally installed at the center of each
manhole structure Because Sewer Manhole Nos 6 and 7 were installed prior to the start
of the jet grouting operations jet grouted columns could not be installed at the center of
these two manhole structures Therefore four jet grouted columns were installed at the
comers of the manhole base structure to support underpin these two manhole structures
The jet grouted depths of each column varied depending on the subsurface conditions
and depth to the sewer line invert at each location In general the jet grouted columns were
extended down to bear on the dense coralline materials encountered at about 29 to 38 feet
below the existing ground surface The columns were jet grouted up to about 6 inches below
the sewer line invert to allow for the placement of the gravel bedding material The tops of
the jei grouted columns corresponded to about 11 to 14 feet below the existing ground
surface The lengths of each jet grouted column ranged from about 15 to 27 feet
Based on the observations of our field representatives it is our professional opinion
that the jet grouting operations performed for the new sewer relief line project observed by
Geolabs in the period covered by this report were performed in general conformance with the
intent of the project plans and specifications
