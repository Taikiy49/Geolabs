This report presents the results of our percolation testing
for the proposed Wailua Vista Subdivision on Kuamoo Road
The purpose of this investigation is to obtain preliminary
subsurface soil conditions at the site and to evaluate the
in situ seepage characteristics for private sewage disposal
system design and construction
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located in adjacent
to and south of Kuamoo Road The site is partially cleared
and presently covered with tall grass piles of trees and
shrubs and a few scattered tall trees

P32
The site is bounded to the north by Kuamoo Road to the east
and west by residential developments similar in character to
the proposed development and to the south by a steep bank of
the Wailua River
The site is generally very flat with surveyed elevations
ranging from 286 to 304 feet above sea level
FIELD EXPLORATION
Three 4 inch diameter auger test borings were drilled to a
depth of 30 feet below the existing ground surface at the
approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
The drilling operation was monitored by our geologist who
also logged the borings The boring logs are presented on
Plates A 1 through A 3
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface exploration disclosed essentially uniform
shallow soil conditions at the site All three test borings
encountered reddish brown cies silt which extended to the
limit of Boring No 3 Boring Nos 1 and 2 encountered brown
silt underlying the reddish brown surficial layer at depths
of 23 5 and 14 5 feet respectively

PERCOLATION TESTS
Falling head percolation tests were performed in the three
drill holes In addition a constant head test was performed
in Boring No 1
After completion of the bore hole drilling each hole was filled
with water and allowed to saturate overnight The next day
each bore hole was refilled with water and the water allowed
to percolate through the subsoil The change in water level
with time was observed and recorded
A constant head test was performed in Boring No 1 by intro
ducing a constant flow of water until a constant head water
level was maintained for 15 minutes A constant head was
developed at ground level with an inflow rate of
The test data is summarized on Plate 2 and the test results
are summarized on Plate 3
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the test results from the drilled bore holes it is
our opinion that the silty soils underlying the site will
provide adequate percolation rates in a private cesspool
sewage disposal system with adequate design and maintenance
