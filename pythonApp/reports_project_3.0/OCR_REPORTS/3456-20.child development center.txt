Additional Probing and Grouting of Probe Holes
Proposed Child Development Center
Barbers Point Oahu Hawaii
In accordance with your facsimile transmittal dated July 20th we are providing the
following additional consultation pertaining to the cavity probing and grouting operations
for the above referenced project site
The project involves the construction of a one story building for the proposed Child
Development Center within the Barbers Point Naval Air Station on the island of Oahu
Hawaii A subsurface probing program was conducted between May 26th and June 2nd
in an attempt to locate subsurface cavities beneath the foundation of the proposed
building A total of 87 probe holes were drilled to depths ranging from about 11 to 14 feet
below the existing ground surface Results of the cavity probing program conducted are
presented in our report entitled Cavity Probe Drilling Report Proposed Child
Development Center Barbers Point Oahu Hawaii dated June 6th
We understand that additional probing at the corners of the proposed building has
been requested by the project structural engineer Mr Steve Cox of Winzler Kelly Inc
Based on the results of the cavity probing conducted to date it is our opinion that
additional probing would not be necessary at this time However we recommend that
the situation be further evaluated after completion of the grouting program
At this time it is desired to grout the probe holes which were left open with PVC
pipes inserted in them to preserve the open hole integrity In general the stress
distribution beneath a footing is limited to a depth of two times the footing width Based
on the project foundation plan it appears that the majority of the footing widths are about
3 to 5 feet Therefore we believe that the grouting operation may commence at a depth
of approximately 6 to 8 feet below the existing ground surface for those probe holes
where cavities and or voids were not encountered Should the probing logs indicate that

Based on the probing program some of the probe holes had encountered
substantial cavities which likely will increase the amount of grout required for the
operation The total quantity of grout and the number of days required to complete the
operation is difficult to quantify due to the unknown variables associated with grouting
subsurface voids The variables include 1 the size of the voids 2 the degree of void
interconnections 3 the number of grout reapplications for large voids and 4 the control
of grout migration Probe holes which did not encounter significant voids may take
significantly less grout
Based on our review of the findings from the probing program a low strength
sand cement grout mixture with approximately 6 to 9 inches of slump pumped at low to
moderate pressure in an upstage manner should be utilized for the grouting operations
The grout will be trucked to the site and delivered to each probe hole via a grout pump
and boom truck The grouting operation is anticipated to proceed in the following
manner
Immediately prior to grouting of a particular hole the temporary PVC pipe will be
removed and a 2 inch diameter grouting probe will be inserted into the open hole
and lowered to approximately 2 feet from the desired depth Grout injection would
then commence at the direction of our field representative who will record the
approximate grout take and provide direction as to when the injection is complete
or whether periodic injection is required
Each probe hole will be grouted from the desired depth upwards by low to
moderate pressure injection of grout through a PVC conduit which will be retracted
from the probe hole as the grout fills If the grout take is noted to exceed about
1 cubic yard in one probe hole the injection will be suspended and the apparatus
moved to an adjacent hole for injection The hole will be marked for later
reapplication of grout The purpose for grout interruption and reintroduction is to
allow the grout to set slightly prior to successive injection in an attempt to control
excess migration of the grout off site or away from the area of concern
To fill probe holes encountering large cavities several injection passes may be
necessary to grout the cavity and probe hole Our field representative will observe
the grouting operations and record the approximate grout take and time of
application per hole In addition our representative will confirm the grout mixture
of satisfactory consistency for pumping and flowage into the subsurface
