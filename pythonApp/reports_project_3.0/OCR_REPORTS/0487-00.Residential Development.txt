This report presents the results of a geological field recon
naissance on the subject site The purposes of our investigation
were to evaluate the geological and shallow soil conditions at
the site and present preliminary recommendations for site
development
SITE LOCATION
The proposed 3 3 acre development site is located on a north facing
hillside at Kaneohe Oahu Hawaii Wood frame townhouse construc
tion is planned Maximum cuts and fills of about 20 and 11 feet
respectively are presently planned to develop the site The
sites existing slopes are fairly steep with a total relief on the

GEOLOGY
The site is underlain for the most part by red brown to light
yellow highly weathered basalt saprolite Some areas are
covered with fill and debris Hard basalt outcrops were observed
in the stream bed Highly weathered basalt was observed in the
road cut on the eastern edge of the property It appears that
the red to yellow highly weathered basalt can be ripped with
conventional grading equipment to the presently planned cuts for
building pads If the locations of the building pad locations
are changed or cut depths increase significantly hard basalt
may be encountered
SOIL CONDITIONS
The majority of the natural areas contain up to six inches of
topsoil Underlying the topsoil and exposed in cut areas are
mottled red and cream colored clayey silts Laboratory tests
reveal the soils to be moderately expansive This soil appears
to be residual except in fills and colluvium along the dry
stream bed In its natural state it is stiff to very stiff
Decomposed basalt rock was in evidence on the exposed cuts
The existing house founded in this natural soil appeared to be
stable

Other than the minor loose fills no major structural fills
were observed on the site Loose fill and debris were found on

No groundwater or seepage in cuts were observed Groundwater
may be anticipated near the surface at the lower portion of
the site in the stream bed
All major geological and surface features are illustrated on
the site plan Plate 1 Evidence of slope runoff and gullying
was present in the form of rills and small piles of earth
materials and debris at the base of cut slopes Inspection of
footings in nearby areas have shown that the footings can
easily be undermined by runoff Slope runoff on this property
should be directed away from the footings and cut slopes should
be protected against erosion
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on our field investigation it is our opinion that the
site is suitable for the proposed construction of townhouses
Since final plans are not available at this time more detailed
studies will be required at a later date

Foundations
It is our opinion that the site soils can be used to support the
proposed structure The type of foundation will depend upon
building design and foundation loads but based on our geological
reconnaissance we anticipate the use of spread footings The
footing and slab on grade should be designed for expansive
forces
Building pads should be so designed that foundations are seated
entirely in fill or entirely in cut
We do not anticipate settlement problems with foundations being
in natural ground or compacted fills However the loose fills
or colluvium located along the stream channel may have to be
removed during grading operations The depths and condition of
these deposits can be determined by further investigation
Slopes
The site except for the loose fill debris areas and colluvium
along the stream channel is stable We do not foresee any
major construction problems being encountered on the site as
presently planned However several basalt rock outcrops were
observed on the site and if the depth of cuts were increased
large boulders or rock may be encountered The decomposed rock

should be rippable with conventional grading equipment and will
not require blasting The actual depth of unweathered rock

The stability of cut and fill slopes should not present any
difficulties We recommend for preliminary planning cut
slopes of 1 to 1 and fill slopes of 1 5 to 1 horizontal to
vertical Steeper slopes may be possible but may require
further investigation We did not observe any potential slide

Present plans call for the construction of an approximate 11
foot high fill in the dry runoff bed in the southern portion
of the site Some provision should be made to divert runoff
away from the fill
Future Studies
It is our opinion that at least six deep borings should be
drilled on the site to further evaluate the overall geologic
and soil conditions and to provide additional required infor
mation in order to recommend detailed foundation and slope
criteria
