SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located on the northwestern corner of
the intersection of Komo Mai Drive and Kaahumanu Street
in Pearl City Oahu
The site is part of a large parcel which was graded years
ago but has not yet been developed It is approximately
rectangular in shape with dimensions of about 125 by 200
feet
The topography at the site consists of a series of terraces
4 to 6 feet high stepping up toward the north The ground
elevation varies from about 246 at Komo Mai Drive to about
162 feet at the northern property boundary
The area is covered with brush and small trees
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
The proposed fire station structure consists of a one story
building approximately 85 by 94 feet in plan situated in
the southern portion of the lot
A parking lot and volleyball court is planned in the northern
portion of the site
Retaining walls up to 10 feet high are planned near the
northern and western boundaries

Site grading consisting of cutting the site to create
a fairly level area is planned
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Subsurface Exploration
Five borings were drilled at the site to depths of 16 to
21 feet below the existing ground surface to determine
the subsurface conditions and obtain samples for laboratory
testing The approximate locations of the borings are
shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
Detailed information on the field exploration and laboratory
testing is covered in the Appendix along with the boring
logs and laboratory test results
Soil Conditions
Based on the borings the site is underlain by very stiff
brown to reddish brown clayey silt to the depths drilled
Groundwater was not noted in the borings during the field
exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Foundations
For the proposed structure spread or continuous footing
foundation are recommended
Prmri AP base oe
W O 871 00 P 4
An allowable bearing value of 4000 p s f is recommended
for footings bearing on the stiff in situ soil This
value of bearing capacity is for total of dead plus live
loads and may be increased by 33 when temporary lateral
loads wind and seismic are considered
The bottoms of footings should be placed a minimum of
18 inches below the finish grade
Surface water should be diverted away from the building
foundations
Anticipated settlements are expected to be relatively
small on the order of up to half an inch
Site Grading
The site will be cut down to create a fairly level lot
and site filling is not anticipated
Fill and backfill should be placed in 8 inch lifts
compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density
established by ASTM D 1557
Retaining Walls
Retaining walls are planned near the northern and western
site boundaries The wall footings design should follow
the foundation recommendations presented above
W O 871 00 P 5
Retaining walls should be backfilled using well graded
granular materials such as base course rock Weepholes
or a subsurface drainage system should be used to minimize
build up of hydrostatic pressures behind walls
The walls may be designed to resist lateral earth pressures
equal to an equivalent fluid pressure of 40 p c f Additional
load due to surcharge loading should be included where appli
cable
A friction factor of 0 4 can be utilized to determine the
sliding resistance of the wall Additionally a passive
soil resistance of 300 p c f can be utilized The passive
resistance should be reduced by one third when combined
with the frictional resistance
Slab On Grade
Slab on grade may be utilized on properly compacted surgrade
Slabs should be placed over a 4 inch layer of base course
size
To protect against moisture damage of the flooring material
a polyethylene moisture barrier should be considered
Parking Pavement
Asphaltic concrete pavements are planned for the project
parking areas

APPENDIX A
FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored
by drilling 5 borings to depths of 16 to 21 feet below the
existing ground surface at the locations shown on the Site
Plan Plate l
The soils encountered in the borings were classified
by visual and textural examination in the field by our
geologist who continuously monitored the drilling operations
The classifications were reviewed by visual inspection and
testing in the laboratory The soils were classified in
general conformance with the Unified Soil Classification
System A graphic presentation of the soils is presented
on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 5
In the borings soil samples were obtained by driving
split barrel sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30
inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the last
12 inches are shown on the boring logs at the appropriate
depths
