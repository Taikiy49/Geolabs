Dear Mr Agena
Compliant with our fee proposal dated we conducted a geotechnical
engineering reconnaissance for proposed improvements at the existing Ohai Trail Lookout
at Poelua Bay in the Kahakuloa area of the Island of Maui Hawaii This report summarizes
our work on the project and presents our findings and geotechnical engineering
recommendations derived from our site reconnaissance
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is an unimproved scenic overlook along Kahekili Highway over
Poelua Bay in the Kahakuloa area on the Island of Maui Hawaii The general location and
vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
Based on our site reconnaissance there is a deteriorated gravel turnout and
parking area and an eroded trail leading to the cliff overlooking Poelua Bay
Based on the information provided we understand that the project will involve the
construction of a small parking lot and turnaround area which will be paved with asphaltic
concrete and an improved concrete trail leading to a lookout area with a concrete pad
and handrails In addition a small picnic area is planned near the lookout with a concrete
sidewalk connecting the lookout and picnic areas
Minor amounts of site grading are anticipated for this project

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our field exploration program consisted of a site reconnaissance with probing of the
shallow surface soils with a hand penetration probe at selected locations to assess the
relative consistency of the shallow soils underlying the site In general our
reconnaissance indicated that the site is underlain by relatively competent residual soils
and saprolite derived from the in situ weathering of basaltic rock These shallow surface
soils grade with depth to weathered basalt rock as is exposed in the face of the wave
cut cliff adjacent to and below the site It was noted that the exposed soil and saprolite
slopes along the cliff were extremely steep and appeared to be eroding Groundwater
was not encountered in the probes at the time of our field reconnaissance
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the observed site conditions we believe that the project may be
developed provided that the recommendations presented herein are followed Special
considerations are required due to the steep existing soil and saprolite slopes at the
site
Detailed discussion of these items and our geotechnical engineering design
recommendations are presented in the following sections of this report
Foundations
The primary concern from a geotechnical engineering point of view is the near
vertical cliff adjacent to the lookout area Our reconnaissance indicated that the material
exposed on the slope face consists primarily of highly to completely weathered basalt
saprolite which is susceptible to erosion More competent hard basalt is believed to exist
below the saprolite layer Because hard erosion resistant basalt was not observed on the
slope face it is our opinion that the lookout should be set back away from the cliff a
minimum distance of about 10 feet to allow for some future erosion Additionally the

Handrail posts should be embedded at least 6 feet below grade Handrails
should be mechanically connected to the support posts by bolting welding or similar
methods so that the railing will remain connected even with the loss of one or more
adjacent support posts
Based on the anticipated subsurface soil conditions we believe that shallow spread
and continuous footing foundations may be used to support the proposed walls An
allowable bearing pressure of 3 000 pounds per square foot psf may be used for the
foundations bearing on stiff natural ground The bearing value is for dead plus live loads
and may be increased by one third 1 3 for transient loads such as those caused by wind
or seismic forces
In general the bottom of footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches
below the lowest adjacent finished grades If foundations are located near slopes the
embedment should be increased so that there is a minimum of 10 feet of setback from
the toe of the foundation to the nearest exterior slope face
Lateral loads acting on walls may be resisted by frictional resistance developed
between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil and by passive earth pressure
acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system A coefficient of friction of
0 4 may be used for footings bearing on the compacted structural fills Resistance due to
passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid pressure of 250 pounds
per square foot per foot of depth pcf This assumes that the soils around the footings are
well compacted Unless covered by pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the
upper 12 inches of soil should be neglected
Active soil pressure on retaining walls and structures may be estimated at
35 pounds per square foot per foot of depth pcf
Slabs On Grade
The area adjacent to the slab should be backfilled tightly against the slab and
graded to divert water away from the slab to reduce the potential of water ponding around
the slab
The edges of slabs adjacent to unpaved areas should be thickened edges and
embedded at least 12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade to reduce the potential of
loss of support of the slab edge due to erosion

Site Grading
At the commencement of grading work all areas within the grading limits should be
cleared grubbed and scarified to a minimum depth of 12 inches moisture conditioned to
above the optimum moisture content and recompacted to not less than 90 percent relative
compaction ASTM D 1557 Boulders should be removed to a minimum depth of
12 inches below subgrade level Trees should be removed and roots greater than 3 inches
in diameter should be over excavated and be backfilled with fill placed in 8 inch maximum
loose lifts moisture conditioned to at least the optimum moisture content and compacted
to at least 90 percent relative compaction
Soft and yielding areas encountered should be over excavated to expose firm
natural material and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with fill placed in 8 inch
maximum loose lifts moisture conditioned to at least the optimum moisture content and
compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction The excavated soils should be
disposed properly off site and or used in landscape areas where appropriate Boulders
produced from the site grading operations should be segregated and disposed at an
appropriate off site location
Fill should consist of non expansive select granular material such as crushed
basalt or coral The material should be well graded from coarse to fine with no particles
larger than 3 inches in largest dimension and should contain between 10 and 30 percent
particles passing the No 200 sieve The material should have a laboratory CBR value of
20 or more and should have a maximum swell of less than 1 percent
Fill should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content placed
in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of
90 percent relative compaction Fill under pavement such as the parking and turnaround
area should be compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Relative
compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the
maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557
Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the
maximum dry density

will be very light Based on the results of our exploration and laboratory testing the

Construction Monitoring
Geolabs should be retained to provide geotechnical engineering services during
construction The critical items of construction monitoring requiring Special Inspection
include observation of footing subgrade over excavation and preparation and placement
and compaction of fill materials A Geolabs representative should also observe other
aspects of earthwork construction This is to observe compliance with the intent of the
design concepts specifications and or recommendations and to expedite suggestions for
design changes that may be required in the event that subsurface conditions differ from
those anticipated at the time this report was prepared The recommendations provided in
this report are contingent upon such observations
lf actual exposed subsurface conditions encountered during construction differ

review and or revise the geotechnical engineering recommendations presented herein
