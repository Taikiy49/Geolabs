INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration for the
proposed 4 classroom building at the Waimea Elementary Intermediate School in
Waimea on the island of Hawaii The general location of the site is shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1 The exploration was performed in general accordance with our
proposal dated
This report summarizes the findings of the exploration and our geotechnical
engineering recommendations derived from our analysis based on the data obtained
during the exploration These recommendations are intended for design input and are
not intended for construction estimation or bidding purposes
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions at the project site in order to develop a generalized soil rock data set for the
formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to the foundation and
pavement design of the project
The work was done in general accordance with our proposal dated
The scope of work consisted of the following
1 Engineering supervision of the drilling and sampling of eight 8
borings to depths of between 5 and 24 feet below the existing
ground surface

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
The project site is underlain by basaltic lava flows of the Hamakua Volcanic Series
of Mauna Kea which were deposited during the Pleistocene Stearns and Macdonald
1946
The Hamakua series is typically composed of basaltic lava flows which are mantled
by a surface layer of palagonitized ash locally referred to as Pahala Ash The ash soil
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2414 00 Page 5
at the project sites differs from Pahala Ash found in other portions of the island of Hawaii
in that the in situ moisture content is generally lower and the soil exists in a friable state
Based on previous experience in the Waimea area the lava flows underlying the
sites are in the A a form which is characterized by a rough spiny or rubbly surface of
fragmental clinker underlain by a dense core of massive basalt which frequently has a
thinner less continuous layer of clinker below the massive core The clinker surface of
the lavas at the site have been mantled by the Pahala Ash as discussed above
Subsoil Condition
The subsurface exploration at the project site was performed by drilling and
sampling eight 8 borings ranging from 5 to 24 feet below the existing ground surface
In addition percolation tests in two 2 of the drilled borings were also performed The
approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general the borings encountered a surface layer approximately five 5 to eight
8 feet thick consisting of medium stiff to stiff clayey silt volcanic ash which graded
with more sand and gravel at depths below five 5 feet Hard vesicular basalt and clinker
were encountered below the surface layer of volcanic ash to the maximum depths of the
borings drilled which was 24 feet below the existing ground surface
Groundwater level was not encountered in the borings during our field exploration
Detailed description of the subsurface conditions are presented on the Boring Logs
in Appendix A The results of the percolation tests are presented in Appendix B

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration encountered a surface layer approximately 5 to 8 feet thick
consisting of medium stiff to stiff clayey silt volcanic ash with sand and gravel overlying
hard vesicular basalt and clinker
Spread or continuous footings and slab on grade foundations may be used to
support the proposed 4 classroom building
Details of our recommendations are discussed in the following sections
Foundations
Based on the soil rock conditions encountered it is our opinion that spread
continuous footing foundations may be used to support the proposed 4 classroom
building An allowable bearing capacity of up to 3 000 p s f may be used for footings
bearing on the in situ clayey silt with a minimum embedment of 24 inches below the
lowest adjacent finished grade A higher allowable bearing capacity of up to 6 000 P S F
may be used for footings bearing on in situ clinker or hard vesicular basalt However it
would be difficult to estimate the limits of where the rock formation may be encountered
The bearing capacity value recommended is for dead plus live loads and may be
increased by one third when temporary loads wind and seismic are considered
If soft loose soil pockets are encountered at the bottom of the future footing
excavation they should be over excavated to stiff in situ soil and replaced with granular
select borrow compacted to a minimum of 95 maximum dry density
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2414 00 Page 7
If a footing is located next to a utility line it should extend to the bottom of the
utility trench to reduce potential footing settlement due to settlement of the trench backfill
Provided the above recommendations are followed estimated total and differential
footing settlements under a column load of up to 65 kips are less than one half inch
Slab On Grade
Comparison of the existing ground elevations with the proposed finished floor
elevation of 2670 5 feet indicate that cuts of about 1 to 6 feet will be required for almost
the entire building area The required fills underneath the building area should consist of
select granular material compacted to a minimum of 95 maximum dry density per ASTM
Test Method D 1557 78 Based on the encountered soil rock conditions disclosed by our
field borings it appears that ash or clinker will be encountered at the finished grade We
recommend a minimum 12 inch thick layer of 3B Fine ASTM C 33 No 67 cushion be
installed below the slab to provide uniform support for the slab
The thickened edge slab footings can bear on the compacted granular non
expansive fill or in situ soil with a minimum footing embedment of 18 inches below the
lowest adjacent grade
Concrete Walkway
Anew concrete walkway next to the proposed 4 classroom building is anticipated
The cushion fill layer below this concrete slab can be reduced to a minimum of 6 inches
thick

Site Grading
Comparison of the existing ground elevations with the proposed finished
grades indicate cuts ranging from about 1 to 6 feet will be required for the proposed
project
Prior to commencement of earthwork all areas within the grading limits should be
cleared and grubbed of all rubbish and deleterious materials The resulting grub and
spoil material should be disposed of properly off site Any soft weak or yielding areas
disclosed at the proposed finished grades should be over excavated to firm ground and
the resulting excavations should be backfilled with well compacted engineered fill
Cut and fill slopes of two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V are recommended Cut
slopes into the dense clinker basalt formation if encountered can be steepened to one
horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V
Allimported materials should be tested and approved by the geotechnical engineer
prior to being used in the site
The fill and backfill should be placed in maximum 10 inch thick loose level lifts and
compacted to a minimum of 90 maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method
D 1557 78
The finished grade should be shaped to shed water away from foundations and
avoid ponding condition

Sewage Disposal System
It is our understanding that a septic tank and leachfield system for the on site
disposal of sanitary waste generated is planned for the proposed project
Based on our exploration and percolation test results it appears that shallow
leachfield design is not feasible since the absorption rate of the upper 5 feet of ash soil
is very slow However it is our belief that on site sewage disposal may be
feasible provided the absorption area of the leachfield system penetrates into the clinker
layer underlying the surface ash soil which was encountered in Borings 3 and 4 at the
approximate depths of 5 to 10 feet below the existing ground surface
Due to the variability of the subsoil conditions the absorption capacity of the
disposal system should be verified by field percolation tests during construction
Pavement Design
Asphaltic concrete pavement is anticipated for the proposed driveway and new 4
Stall parking area It is also anticipated that a 1 5 inch A C overlay will be implemented
for the widening of the existing driveway connecting to the new driveway
For preliminary design the following pavement section is recommended
2 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 Inch Base Course
8 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
on Compacted Subgrade

The recommended section considers medium traffic and assumes that good

Design Review
Construction plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for updated review evaluation and written
comments prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to the
earthwork and foundation recommendations presented in this report If this review is not
made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for misinterpretation of the
recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during earthwork construction and footing excavation
to monitor the contractor s operations This is to observe compliance with the design
concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow design changes in the event
that subsurface conditions differ from that anticipated prior to start of construction The
recommendations given in this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and approved by
the geotechnical engineer prior to its delivery to the site

The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from field data points such as borings Variations of conditions
between the field data points may occur and the nature and extent of these variations
may not become evident until construction If variations then appear evident it will be
necessary to re evaluate the recommendations given in this report
If actual exposed soil rock conditions encountered during construction are different
from those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made
The locations and elevations of the field data points were determined by estimation
from the Site Layout Grading Utility Plan by Imata and Associates Inc dated January
1990 Elevations of the borings were approximately determined by interpolation from
elevation points in this plan The physical location and elevation of the borings should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the boring points depict
the approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual
transition The strata lines shown on the profiles or cross sections are based upon
interpolation between borings and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
Water level data from the boring points were collected at the times given on the
graphic representations and or in the text of this report These data have been reviewed
and interpretations made in the formulation of this report However it must be noted that
fluctuation may occur due to variations in rainfall tides temperature and other factors

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling eight 8 borings to
depths of five 5 to twenty four 24 feet below the existing ground surface at the
approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 A truck mounted rig was used to
drill the borings
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist or engineer who continuously monitored the
drilling operations The classifications were further reviewed by visual observation and
testing in the laboratory The soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified
Soil Classification System A summary of the materials encountered are presented on the
Boring logs Plates A 1 through A 8
Soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving a 2 inch standard split
spoon sampler or a 2 5 inch D split barrel sampler with a 140 pound hammer free falling
30 inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the
Boring Logs at the appropriate sample depths Rock core samples were obtained by
using a rotary wash NX core barrel The core recovery REC and rock quality
designation RQD are also presented on the Boring Logs
