Foundation Investigation
Proposed Communication Antenna Facility
Pearl City Industrial Park
Tax Map Key 18
This report presents the results of our foundation investigation
performed at the proposed communication antenna facility site for
Southern Pacific Communication System at Pearl City Industrial Park
Hawaii The general vicinity of the site is shown
on the Project Location Map Plate 1
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is our understanding that the project will consist of an
18 meter dish antenna to be supported on a 30 by 33 feet concrete mat
a one story building and a microwave tower As the existing site is
relatively level it is anticipated that the site grading will be
minimum
W O 1297 00 Page 2
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located at the western end of Waihona Place
in the Pearl City Industrial Park Hawaii
At the time of our field exploration the project site was
relatively flat and sloped gently toward the west The site was
vacant and had previously been filled over a gully in mid 1970 s for
the site grading of the industrial park subdivision
The fill sloped down at about 2 1 H V along the western and
northern sides of the site Two small erosional gullies about 4 feet
deep each cut into the top of the Slope near the northwestern corner
of the site A concrete lined ditch was located on the northern side
of the site at the toe of the fill slope A concrete lined stream was
noted along the northern portion of the western fill slope bottom
The eastern and southern boundaries of the site abutted adjoining
vacant lots
Ground cover consisted of thick haole koa trees with tall grasses
and weeds Several trash and debris piles were noted in the
northwestern corner with trashes scattered throughout the site

SUBSURFACE CONDITION
Geology
The project site is located in the Waiawa Stream valley which has
been cut into the southwestern flank of the Koolau Mountains The
bottom of this valley had been filled with alluviun deposited by
Waiawa Stream In recent times portions of the valley have been
graded and filled by man
Subsurface Explorations
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling
and sampling five 5 borings at the approximate locations shown on
the Site Plan Plate 2 Description of the field exploration and
laboratory testing along with the boring logs and laboratory test
results are presented in the appendices of this report The
locations of our borings were based on the preliminary building
locations shown on the Site Plan
Subsurface Conditions
Our borings encountered a layer of man made fill up to 20 feet
thick This fill consisted generally of reddish brown clayey silt
with pockets of silty clay and appeared to be well compacted with
consistencies ranging from stiff to very hard The fill became less
hard near its bottom The reddish brown clayey silt and silty clay
fill materials generally have a low swelling potential
GEOLABS HAWAII
Below the upper fill our boring disclosed stiff to very stiff
dark brown silt and clayey silt about 7 feet thick Some tree roots
were observed in the upper portion of this dark brown clayey silt at
about 21 feet which was probably the natural gully bottom prior to the
placement of the existing fill
Boring 2 penetrated below the silt layer and into sandy and
gravelly stream deposits at a depth of approximately 27 feet These
deposits appeared to consist of medium dense to dense Silty sand that
graded gradually with depth into dense subrounded to rounded basalt
gravel and cobbles to 45 feet the maximun depth explored
Groundwater was encountered in Boring 2 at depth of 29 5 feet
below the existing ground surface More detailed descriptions of the
soil conditions encountered are shown on the boring logs in Appendix A
of this report
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our findings it is our opinion that the site is
suitable for the proposed construction provided that some
precautionary steps are taken against the low expansive nature of the
on site material Shallow spread footing may be used We further
believe that potential settlements would not be a problen

Dish Antenna Foundations
It is our understanding that the operation of the dish antenna
will be sensitive to differential settlements However some minor
adjustments may be made for such a purpose
In general we believe that the ground settlements of the
existing fill have stabilized over the years since the fill was
completed in the mid 1970 s Additional small settlements may be
induced by the mat foundation We anticipate that the additional
settlement will be less than one half of an inch and probably occur
rapidly or completed within a month after the concrete is placed
The foundation loading from the operation of the dish antenna is
not available at the time of preparing this report However we
believe that it will be relatively small and no Significant settlement
would result
It is recommended that the antenna mat foundation be placed at
least 18 inches below the lowest adjacent grade The bottom of the
mat should rest on a granular cushion layer consisting of at least 4
inches of base course aggregate and compacted to at least 95 of the
maximum density established be ASIM D 1557 78 The cushion layer will
provide a working surface for the mat construction and to maintain a
more uniform subgrade soil moisture
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1297 00 Page 6
The bottom of the mat excavation should be examined by a soils
engineer prior to concrete placement If any soft or weak spots is
detected it should be over excavated and backfilled with compacted
fill
Building and Tower Foundations
Spread footing may be used for foundation support of the building
and the microwave antenna tower An allowable soil bearing pressures
of 3000 p s f may be used for the footing bearing on the compacted
fill The footing bottom should be embedded at least 18 inches below
the lowest adjacent finished grade
The bottom of the footing excavation should be neat and in firm
soils If any soft or weak spots are encountered they should be
over excavated and backfilled with compacted engineered fill The
bottom of the footing excavations should be inspected by a soils
engineer prior to concrete placement
The soils in all footing excavations should be kept moist
Footings should be concreted and backfilled as soon as possible after
excavation

On Grade Slabs
The floor slabs may be constructed on grade provided that the
subgrade soil is premoistened to minimize its post construction
expansion potential The subgrade soil should be premoistened by
frequent sprinkling for at least 24 hours prior to slab concreting
In addition the edges of the slab if not confined by perimeter
wall footing should be protected by a thickened edge extending at
least 12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade to serve as barrier
against future moisture fluctuation beneath the edge of the slab area
Again the concreting of the slabs and footings should be
coordinated in such a way that the time period between the initial
preparation and or excavation and final concrete placement would be
kept to a minimum to prevent excessive drying
It is recommended that on grade slabs be placed over a 4 inch
layer of granular cushion material such as base course or S4C type
aggregate If necessary a moisture barrier membrane should be placed
over the cushion layer to protect the floor covering
Resistivity and pH
Subsoil samples within the upper 5 to 10 feet were tested for
their resistivity values The test results are presented in Appendix
B Generally the soils tested have pH values of about 5 5 and
resistivity values ranged from 1000 to 1900 ohms per cubic centimeter

Site Grading
Since the site is relatively flat some grading would be required
to provide drainage
Clearing and Grubbing
tc ecm eS ee ay ce a he oe cae es se
The site should be cleared of all debris boulders vegetations
and other deleterious materials Tree roots in excess of 1 5
inches in diameter should be grubbed The top 2 to 4 inches of
soil that contain grass roots and organic matter should be
stripped and not be used as engineered fill
Immediately after clearing grubbing and stripping the stripped
surface should be proof rolled with a pneumatic tired roller
weighing at least 20 tons and making at least 4 passes over any
point in the parking building and antenna facility areas Any
soft or weak yielding material should be removed and backfilled
with compacted on site soil or imported fill
Any area to receive fill should then be scarified to a depth of 6
inches below the stripped surface moisture conditioned to bring
the soil moisture to within 3 percentage points of the optimum
and recompacted to at least 90 of the ASTM D
density
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1297 00 Page 9
The stripped area should be kept moist by frequently sprinkling
with water and not be allowed to dry out excessively before
filling This measure is to minimize potential swelling that may
result from drying of the soils
The existing fill slopes were generally loose and appeared to be
readily erodible Thus special measures to control erosion such
as redirecting run off water grassing of slopes and site should
be provided
The erosion gullies on the slopes should be repaired by
over excavating and backfilling Access to the top of the slope
should be provided for future maintenance work
Drainage
The finish grade outside the slab pavement and footings should
be shaped to shed water away from the foundations and to avoid ponding
condition Also it is advised that gutter systems be installed and
that gutter water be diverted far away from the foundations and
walkways
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper
performance of the above recommended foundatios since ponded water
could cause excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent
heaving or loss of strength
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1297 00 Page 10
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and
taintained to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing
foundation
Design Review
Plans and specifications for foundation and site grading should
be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written comments
prior to construction to see that the intent of our recommendations is
included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the footing excavations and backfill
operations be observed by the soils engineer during construction
This is to see that adequate bearing has been obtained and the
subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of our
investigation Should conditions disclosed during construction be
different from those presented in this report then modifications to
the appropriate design would have to be made
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested
and approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
ed
Moisture content and unit weight determinations were performed
on selected samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of
soil properties The results of these tests are presented on the logs
of borings at the appropriate sample depths
To further identify soil characteristics 2 Atterberg Limits
tests were performed The results are presented on the logs of
borings
Two direct shear tests were performed on undisturbed samples to
evaluate the strength characteristics of the fill materials The test
results are presented on Plates B 1 and B 2
Three unconsolidated undrained triaxial compression tests were
performed on select undisturbed samples to evaluate the soil strength
characteristics or the soil All triaxial compression tests were
performed using a multi stage procedure in which the next higher
confining pressure was applied after the sample approached the peak
cotipressive strength under the on going confining pressure
One unconfined compression test was performed on a slightly
disturbed sample to further evaluate the soil strength characteristics
The peak compressive strength values of triaxial and unconfined
compression tests are summarized on Plate B 3
One consolidation test was performed on selected undisturbed
sample to evaluate the settlement characteristics of the soft sediment
under the fill material The test results are presented on Plate B 4
Four one inch ring swell tests were performed on remolded and
undisturbed samples to evaluate the swelling potential of the soils in
both natural and compacted conditions The test results are
summarized on Plate B 5
Five sets of laboratory resistivity and 5 pH tests were
performed on the upper five foot soil samples obtained from the
borings The test results are summarized on Plate B 6
