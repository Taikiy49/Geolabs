INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration performed
for the proposed Manana Well Phase 1 project located in the Pearl City area on the Island
of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering recommendations
resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering analyses These
recommendations are intended for the design of foundations site preparation and
pavements only The findings and recommendations presented herein are subject to the
limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed Manana Well Phase 1 project site is located near the intersection of
Waimano Home Road and Moanalua Road in the Pearl City area on the Island of Oahu
Hawaii Based on the information provided we understand that it is proposed to construct
new facilities at the project site including a new pump control building a pumping unit
a new mute structure for the new pump to meet DOH noise requirements access
roadway and relocation modification of the existing water mains
A site grading plan was not available at the time of this report preparation However
we envision that minor site grading consisting of cuts and fills on order of 1 to 2 feet or less
would be needed to achieve the design finished grade
A new asphalt concrete paved access road is planned to serve the new facility We
anticipate that the new pavements will be subjected to light vehicular traffic consisting
primarily of passenger vehicles light pick up trucks and occasionally moderately loaded
trucks

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from two shield
volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be middle to
late Pliocene in age and forms the bulk of the western third of the island The younger
shield Koolau is estimated to be late Pliocene to early Pleistocene Ice Age and forms the
majority of the eastern two thirds of the island Waianae became extinct while Koolau was
still active and its eastern flank was partially buried below Koolau lavas banking against its
easter flank Both Waianae and Koolau Ranges have lost most of their original shield
outlines and are now long narrow ridges shaped largely by erosion
The project area is generally composed of basaltic rock built by the extrusion of lavas
of the Koolau Volcanic Series These rocks are generally characterized by flows of jointed
dense vesicular basalt with interbedded thin clinker layers In situ chemical weathering of
the Koolau lava flows has occurred during the last 1 to 2 million years The weathering
process has formed a mantle of residual soils and saprolite

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsoil conditions at the subject site were explored by drilling and sampling four
borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 4 to depths ranging from about 11 5 to
21 5 feet below the existing ground surface One bulk sample designated as Bulk 1 was
obtained from the near surface materials at the proposed pavement areas to evaluate the
pavement support characteristics The approximate locations of the borings and bulk sample
are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general our field exploration encountered a surficial fill layer consisting of stiff to
very stiff clayey silt extending to a depth of about 5 feet below the existing ground surface
Below the surficial fill layer our borings generally encountered residual soils consisting of
very stiff to hard clayey silts extending to the maximum depth explored of approximately
21 5 feet below the existing ground surface
Groundwater was not encountered in the drilled borings at the time of our field
exploration However it should be noted that groundwater levels are subject to fluctuation
due to rainfall events groundwater withdrawal and other factors occurring off site
Detailed descriptions of the materials encountered in the borings are presented on the
Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 4 of Appendix A Results of the laboratory tests
performed on selected soil samples are presented in Appendix B
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field exploration the project site is generally underlain by stiff to hard
clayey soils extending to the maximum depth explored of about 21 5 feet below the existing
ground surface Based on the subsoil conditions encountered at the project site we believe
that shallow foundations consisting of spread and or continuous footings bearing on the
GEOLABS INC
W O Page 6
recompacted on site soils or compacted fills needed to achieve the design finished grades
may be used to support the proposed new buildings
Based on our laboratory tests the near surface soils exhibit low shrink swell potential
when subjected to fluctuations in the soil moisture contents To reduce the potential for
distress to the future lightly loaded slabs on grade resulting from the swelling of the near
surface soils we recommend that the on site clayey soils be capped with a minimum of
6 inches of non expansive select granular fill capping fill material below the slab areas In
general the subgrade soils should be scarified moisture conditioned and recompacted to
provide a relatively firm and smooth bearing surface prior to the placement of fill material
The slab subgrade should be kept moist and free of shrinkage cracks prior to placement of
capping fill cushion fill and or slab concrete Our geotechnical engineering
recommendations pertaining to the design of foundations site preparation and pavements
are further discussed in the following sections of this report
Building Foundations
Based on the generally stiff subsoil conditions encountered at the site we believe that
shallow spread and or continuous footings may be used for support of the proposed new
construction An allowable bearing pressure of up to 3 000 pounds per square foot psf may
be used for the design of shallow foundations bearing on the recompacted on site stiff soils
or compacted fills needed to achieve the design finished grades This bearing value is for
dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third 1 3 for transient loads such as
those caused by wind or seismic forces
For footings bearing on the on site stiff soils the bottom of the footing excavations
should be recompacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction to provide a relatively firm
and smooth bearing surface prior to placement of reinforcing steel or concrete If soft and or
loose materials are encountered at the bottom of the footing excavations they should be
over excavated to expose the underlying firm materials The over excavation may be
backfilled with on site soils compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction or
the footing bottom may be extended down to the underlying competent materials For
GEOLABS INC
W O 4441 00 Page 7
footings bearing on new compacted fills the bottom of the footing excavations should also
be recompacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction prior to placement of
reinforcing steel or concrete
Lateral loads acting on the structures may be resisted by frictional resistance
developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil and by passive earth
pressure acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system A coefficient of
friction of 0 30 may be used for footings bearing on the in situ stiff soils or compacted fills
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid
pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth pcf This assumes that the soil
around footings is well compacted The passive pressure should be reduced for foundations
located on slopes Unless covered by pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the
upper 12 inches of soil should be neglected
In general footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest
adjacent finish grades For footings constructed near tops of slopes or on sloping ground
the footings should be embedded deep enough to provide a minimum horizontal set back
distance of 8 feet measured from the outside edge of the footings to the slope face Footings
located adjacent to planned retaining walls should be embedded deep enough to avoid
surcharging the retaining wall foundations or the retaining walls should be designed to resist
the additional structural loads
If foundations are located next to utility trenches or easements they should be
embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane extending upward
from the bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility lines This
requirement is necessary to avoid surcharging adjacent below grade structures with
additional structural loads and to reduce the potential for foundation settlement
We estimate that footing settlements under the anticipated design loads for footings
bearing on the recompacted in situ stiff soils or compacted fills as recommended herein to
be less than 1 inch total with differential settlements on the order of 0 5 inches

Slabs On Grade
Based on our field exploration the project site is generally underlain by near surface
clayey silt soils with low expansion potential when subjected to moisture fluctuations To
reduce the potential for future distress to the lightly loaded slabs on grade resulting from
shrinking and swelling of the clayey silt soils due to changes in the moisture content we
recommend that the subgrade soils be properly prepared and capped with at least 6 inches
of non expansive select granular fill capping fill material below the slab cushion The
capping fill should also extend beyond the new slab on grade footprint a minimum of
24 inches
The non expansive select granular capping fill should be placed as soon as possible
after the subgrade soils are prepared in accordance with the recommendations presented
in the Site Preparation section of this report The prepared capping fill and the underlying
subgrade soils should be wetted and kept moist prior to the final placement of concrete
Where shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of the subgrade we recommend that
the soils be thoroughly moistened to close the cracks Saturation and subsequent yielding
of the exposed subgrade due to inclement weather and poor drainage may require
over excavation of the soft areas and replacement with well compacted fill
For interior building slabs not subjected to vehicular traffic we recommend that a
minimum 4 inch thick layer of cushion fill consisting of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33
No 67 gradation or Basaltic Termite Barrier BTB be placed below the slabs to serve as a
capillary break and to provide more uniform support of the slabs To reduce the potential for
future moisture infiltration and subsequent damage to floor coverings an impervious
moisture vapor barrier such as a plastic membrane is recommended on top of the No 3B
Fine gravel or BTB cushion layer Flexible floor coverings such as carpet or sheet vinyl
should be considered since they can better mask minor slab cracking It is also
GEOLABS INC
W O 4441 00 Page 9
recommended that interior walls be designed to incorporate some flexibility to accommodate
a small amount of possible ground movements
Exterior concrete walkways may be required for the proposed project We
recommend that concrete walkways be supported on 6 inches of non expansive select
granular fill or aggregate subbase The select granular fill or aggregate subbase should be
compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction Control joints should be provided at
intervals equal to the width of the walkways and at right angle intersections
The thickened edges of slab adjacent to unpaved areas should be embedded at
least 12 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade Areas adjacent to the slab should
be backfilled tightly against the slab with on site soil and graded to divert water away from
the slab to reduce the potential for water ponding around the foundations
Site Preparation
Based on the existing topography and our field observation we anticipate that minor
site grading consisting of cuts and fills on order of 1 to 2 feet or less may be needed to
achieve the design finished grade for the proposed project At the on set of earthwork the
area within the contract grading limits should be thoroughly cleared Debris rubbish and
other unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of off site Soft and yielding
areas encountered during clearing below areas designated to receive fill or future
improvements should be over excavated to expose firm material and the resulting
excavation should be backfilled with well compacted fill The excavated soft and or organic
soils should be properly disposed of off site Contract documents should include additive
and deductive unit prices for over excavation and fill placement to account for variations
in the over excavation quantities
The existing concrete slabs and A C pavements within the proposed project site
should be completely removed Existing utilities to be abandoned should be removed and
the resulting excavation should be properly backfilled with select granular fill material
moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture content placed in 6 to 8 inch level loose
GEOLABS INC
W O 4441 00 Page 10
lifts and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Utilities to be
abandoned in place under the proposed structure should be backfilled by pumping lean
concrete under low pressure
After demolition and clearing the areas within the future slab on grade limits should
be over excavated where necessary to provide the minimum 6 inch thick capping layer of
non expansive select granular fill material The non expansive select granular fill material
should extend a minimum of 2 feet beyond the perimeter of the proposed slab on grade
areas The over excavated subgrades should then be scarified to a minimum depth of
8 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
If the subgrade soils are pumping during subgrade preparation the pumping
subgrade may be stabilized by cement treatment to reduce the potential for pumping
subgrade conditions As a guide one sack of cement may be used for approximately
25 square feet of subgrade area Alternatively the subgrade may be proof rolled and a
woven geotextile fabric such as Mirafi 500X or equivalent should be placed on the
subgrade prior to placement of subsequent lifts of fill
The on site clayey silt soils generated from the cut areas may be reused as a source
of general fill material up to the bottom of the non expansive select granular capping fill
Imported general fill materials needed for the site filling should consist of materials with a
low expansion potential Imported general fill materials should consist of materials with
particle sizes of 6 inches or less in maximum dimension The material should have a
laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR value of 12 or higher and a swell potential of not
more than 1 percent when tested in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1883
The 6 inch non expansive select granular fill required under the buildings and
walkway slabs should consist of non expansive select granular material such as crushed
coral or basalt The material should be well graded from coarse to fine with no particles
larger than 3 inches in largest dimension and should contain between 10 and 30 percent
GEOLABS INC

particles passing the No 200 sieve The material should have a laboratory CBR value of
20 or more and should have a maximum swell of 1 percent or less when tested in
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1883
Aggregate base and subbase course materials required for the pavement sections
should consist of crushed basalt aggregate and should conform to Sections 703 06 and
703 17 of the Hawaii Standard Specifications for Road Bridge and Public Works
Construction 1994 Imported fill materials should be tested by Geolabs for conformance
with these recommendations prior to delivery to the project site for the intended use
General fill materials should be moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the
optimum moisture placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction The non expansive select
granular fill materials under the building and walkway slabs should be placed in level lifts of
about 8 inches in loose thickness moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture and
compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction Aggregate base and subbase course
materials should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content placed in
level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil
expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in
accordance with ASTM D 1557 91 test procedures Optimum moisture is the water content
percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density Compaction should
be accomplished by sheepsfoot rollers vibratory rollers or other types of acceptable
compaction equipment Water tamping jetting or ponding should not be allowed to compact
the fill materials
Cut and fill slopes if planned for the subject project should be constructed at an
inclination of 2H 1V or flatter Fills should be keyed and benched into the existing slope to
provide stability of the new fill against sliding The filling operations should start at the lowest
point and continue up in level horizontal compacted layers in accordance with the fill
placement recommendations presented above Excessive water should be diverted away

Pavements
We understand that asphalt concrete pavements are planned to serve the new access
roadway leading to the project site As previously indicated we anticipate the vehicle loading
for the access road for this project will consist primarily of passenger vehicles light pick up
trucks and occasionally moderately loaded trucks Based on our field exploration and
laboratory testing the on site clayey silt soils appear to have a CBR value of about 29 with
expansion value of less than 1 percent Based on the above assumptions we recommend
that the following flexible pavement section be used for preliminary design purposes
Flexible Pavements
2 0 Inch Asphalt Concrete
6 0 Inch Aggregate Base Course 95 Percent Relative Compaction
6 0 Inch Aggregate Subbase 95 Percent Relative Compaction
14 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness over Compacted Moist Subgrade
The pavement subgrades should be scarified to a depth of about 8 inches moisture
conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum
of 95 percent relative compaction The base and aggregate subbase courses should consist
of crushed basalt aggregate compacted to not less than 95 percent relative compaction
CBR and field density tests should be performed on the actual subgrade soils encountered
during construction to confirm the adequacy of the above section

Utility Trench
We envision that new utility lines may be required for the proposed project In
general for support of the utility lines we recommend that a granular bedding consisting of
6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation be used under the pipes
Free draining granular materials such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation
should also be used for the trench backfill up to about 12 inches above the pipes to provide
adequate support around the pipes and to reduce the compaction effort of the backfill thus
reducing the possibility of damaging the pipes It is critical to use this free draining material
to reduce the potential for formation of voids below the haunches of pipes and to provide
adequate support for the sides of the pipes Improper trench backfill could result in
settlement of the backfill and damage to the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipes to the
top of the subgrade or finished grade may consist of the on site soils or select fill material
The backfill should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture placed in
maximum 8 inch level loose lifts and mechanically compacted to not less than 90 percent
relative compaction to reduce the potential for future ground subsidence Where trenches
are below pavement areas the upper 3 feet of the trench backfill below the pavement
subgrade should be compacted to at least 95 percent relative compaction

Drainage
The finished grades outside the buildings should be sloped to shed water away from
foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also advised that gutter systems
be installed around the buildings and that the discharge be diverted away from the foundation
and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near the foundations and slabs should
also be avoided Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have concrete bottoms
and drains to reduce the potential for water infiltration into the subsoils Drainage swales
should be provided as soon as possible and maintained to drain surface run off away from
the slabs and foundations
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the above
foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation and
subsequent loss of strength and or heaving of the near surface clayey silt soils The
foundation excavations should be properly backfilled against the footings immediately after
setting of the concrete to reduce the potential for water infiltration

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
APPENDIX A
Fieid Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling
four borings to depths ranging from about 11 5 to 21 5 feet below the existing ground surface
at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were drilled using
truck mounted drilling equipment equipped with continuous flight augers
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by testing
in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representations of the materials
encountered are provided on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 4
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 3550 84 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a
3 inch OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches In
addition some samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils
by driving a 2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same hammer and drop
The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an 18 inch
drive are shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate
sample depths
Pocket penetrometer tests were performed on selected cohesive soil samples in the
field The pocket penetrometer test provides an indication of the unconfined compressive
strength of the sample Results of the pocket penetrometer tests are summarized on the
Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content ASTM D 2216 and unit weight ASTM D 2937 determinations were
performed on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil
properties The results of these tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the
appropriate sample depths
Five one inch ring swell tests were performed on selected soil samples to evaluate
the swelling potential of the near surface soils The results of the tests are summarized on
Plate B 1
One laboratory California Bearing Ratio test ASTM D 1883 was performed on a bulk
soil sample of the near surface clayey silt soils to evaluate the pavement support
characteristics of the soils The test results are presented on Plate B 2
One Modified Proctor compaction test ASTM D 1557 was performed on a bulk soil
sample of the typical clayey silt soils encountered at the site The test results are presented
on Plate B 3
