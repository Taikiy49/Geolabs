GENERAL SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located on the makai side of the Mamalahoa Highway
approximately four miles east of Waimea in the Hamakua District on the island of Hawaii
The general location of the site and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map
Plate 1
The site has been graded to present gently rolling topography At the time of our
geotechnical exploration site grading in the areas of the proposed Clubhouse and
Maintenance Buildings had been initiated to create generally level pads

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a Clubhouse and a Maintenance Building with
associated parking areas upon the two recently graded level areas at the proposed
locations as shown on the attached Site Plan Plate 2 Tne Clubhouse will have a lower
finish floor at an elevation of 2 769 feet above M S L and an upper floor at an elevation
of 2 780 feet above M S L The Maintenance Building will have a finish floor at an

generated within the development area It is our understanding that it is currently planned
to use filtration trenches to dispose of the on site generated wastewater
REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The project site is underlain by basaltic lava flows of the Hamakua Volcanic Series
of Mauna Kea which were deposited during the Pleistocene epoch Stearns and
Macdonald 1946
GEOLABS HAWAIl
W O 2803 00 Page 5
The Hamakua Series is typically composed of basaltic lava flows which are mantled
by a surface layer of palagonitized ash locally referred to as Pahala Ash The ash soil
at the project site differs from Pahala ash found in other portions of the island of Hawaii
in that the in situ moisture content is generally lower and the soil exists in a friable state
The lava flows underlying the site are in the a a form which is characterized by a
rough spiny or rubbly surface of fragmental clinker underlain by a dense core of massive
basalt which frequently has a thinner less continuous layer of clinker below the massive
core The clinker surface of the lavas at the site have been mantled by the Pahala ash
as discussed above

Site Preparation
Prior to the commencement of any earthwork all areas within the grading limits
should be cleared of all vegetation and deleterious materials The resulting stripped
material should be properly disposed of off site Any soft weak or yielding areas
disclosed during the clearing operations should be overexcavated to firm ground and the
resulting excavation should be backfilled with well compacted select fill materials as
described above
The areas below the bottom of the footings and slabs on grade extending one 1
foot beyond the footing and slab perimeters should be over excavated through loose fill
and ash soils or to a depth of two 2 feet below the subgrade elevation The areas
below pavement areas should be over excavated through loose recent fill if any or to a
depth of one 1 foot below the finish subgrade elevation The surfaces exposed by the
over excavation should be scarified to a minimum depth of six 6 inches brought to
optimum moisture content and compacted with a sheepsfoot or smooth drum vibratory
roller to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as determined by
procedures described in ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 Any soft or yielding areas should
be removed and replaced with select materials compacted in accordance with the
recommendations presented herewith

Percolation Testing
Percolation tests were conducted at two boring locations in the vicinity of the
proposed clubhouse In general the field testing indicated that the subsurface materials
at these locations have moderate to good permeability or infiltration characteristics Dry
wells and or leachfields could be utilized to provide disposal of on site sewage and storm
water provided that the leachfields or dry wells are constructed through the upper ash
soils and extend adequately into the underlying weathered rock and clinker layers with
adequate percolation characteristics

in weathered rock or clinker zones

3 and 4 in the vicinity of the Maintenance Facilities Based upon the percolation tests
performed in this area a percolation rate of eighteen 18 minutes per inch may be used

Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by drilling and sampling
of 7 borings to depths ranging between 11 5 and 28 0 feet below the existing ground
surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were
drilled with a truck mounted rotary dri rig advancing continuous flight augers or rotary
and core drilling tools as appropriate
In addition to the borings 4 test pits were excavated to depths between 3 5 and
5 0 feet below the existing ground surface by a backhoe loader supplied by the contractor
at the project site
The materials encountered in the borings and test pits were examined and
classified in general accordance with ASTM Standard Practice D 2488 84 Description and
Identification of Soils Visual Manual Procedure by field engineers and geologists from
our firm The logs of the borings are shown on Plates A 1 through A 7
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Standard Method D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils
sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the boring logs at the appropriate sample
depths
Core samples of soil and rock materials encountered at the site were obtained
using diamond core drilling techniques in general accordance with ASTM Standard
Practice D 2113 83 Diamond Core Drilling for Site Investigation Core drilling is a rotary
drilling method which uses a hollow bit to cut into the rock formation The rock material
left in the hollow portion of the bit is mechanically recovered for examination and
description
Recovery REC is used as a subjective guide to the interpretation of the relative
quality of rock masses Recovery is defined as the actual length of material recovered
from a coring attempt vs the length of the core attempt e g if 3 7 feet of material is
recovered from a 5 0 foot core run the recovery would be 74 percent and would be
shown on the Boring Logs as REC 74
The Rock Quality Designation RQD is also a subjective guide to the relative
quality of rock masses This designation is only applicable to cores retrieved from in situ
rock formations RQD is defined as the percentage of the core run that is sound material
that is in excess of four 4 inches in length without any discontinuities discounting any

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Laboratory testing was performed on selected samples obtained during our field
exploration A laboratory testing program was developed to identify appropriate tests to
aid in determining pertinent engineering properties of the soil and or rock materials
sampled
Classification Tests
Frequently the classification of a material provides a correlative index to certain
engineering properties of the material
Moisture Content ASTM Test Method D 2216 80 and Unit Weight Determinations
are used as an aid to soil classification and to aid in determining certain properties such
as its consistency The moisture content expressed as a percentage of the dry weight
of the tested sample is shown at the appropriate location on plates summarizing test
results and at the appropriate sample depths on the Boring Logs The unit eights of
selected samples are also shown at the appropriate sample depths on the Boring Logs
Two 2 CBR tests were performed upon soils from the project site to obtain
information which would aid in the design of slab and pavement sections
Five 5 ring swell tests were performed upon soil samples from the project site to
obtain information pertaining to the expansion potential of the soils and the effect upon
structural improvements
The results of the laboratory testing are presented on Plates B 1 through B 2

APPENDIX C
Percolation Testing
Percolation testing at the Clubhouse was performed in Boring Nos 3 and 4 by the
falling head method The water level drop was timed and recorded for the upper 6 5 feet
of the boring The results of these tests are presented on Plates C 1 through C 4
Percolation testing was performed at the Maintenance Facility in probes bed
augered in the general locations of test pit nos 3 and 4 The holes are augered to a
depth of four feet and saturated with water After the water had drained the holes were
filled to a depth of six inches from the bottom with water and the time for a one inch drop
in the water level was recorded
