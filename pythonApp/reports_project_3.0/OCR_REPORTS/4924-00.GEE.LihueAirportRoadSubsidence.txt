SUMMARY REPORT
GEOTECHNICAL ENGINEERING EXPLORATION SERVICES
LIHUE AIRPORT ROAD SUBSIDENCE
Dear Mr Matsushige
This letter provides a summary of the results of the boring exploration and
observations during the repair of selected manhole locations at the site
INTRODUCTION
Our drilling services and observations were made during three separate times
between August and October 2003 The results of the borings and probings drilled in
May 2002 under the guidance of Walter Lum are not included in this report
DISCUSSION AND FINDINGS
Initial Soil Conditions
Three borings were drilled on August 12 2003 along portions of the road that
experienced subsidence Boring No 101 was drilled on the Control Tower Access
Road where a void had developed under the pavement and the adjacent sidewalk
appeared to have settled Boring No 102 was drilled about 5 feet away from MH 6
where significant settlement had occurred Boring No 103 was drilled next to MH 4
where significant settlement had occurred Detailed descriptions of the materials
encountered in the test borings are presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1
through A 3 of Appendix A
The objectives of the drilling were to 1 measure the actual thickness of the
pavement backfill and AC patch to determine the magnitude of the settlements
2 determine the type and condition of the backfill soil 3 to correlate the previous
probing results with the actual soil sampling blow counts and 4 to attempt to sample
the gravel cushion under the pipes and manholes to determine if migration of the fine
soil had occurred The results of the field exploration are summarized below

Spudding Compaction
Following completion of the above borings and discussions between the State DOT
and Goodfellow Brothers decided to attempt to densify the backfill around
the affected manholes with the use of a steel pipe spud driven into the ground Generally
the method consists of driving a closed end steel pipe into the backfill using a pneumatic
hammer attached to a backhoe The driven spud will displace the backfill material
increasing the in place density of the surrounding soil The hole left by the driven spud is
then backfilled in lifts with select material that is compacted by additional driving with the
spud This compaction pile method was deemed to be more cost effective than
conventional over excavation repair methods Details of the proposed method are
described in the report by SKD Engineers Inc entitled Proposed Roadway Subsidence
Repair Ahukini Road Lihue Airport Complex State Project
No AK1046 21 dated
GBI commenced spudding compaction on at SMH 11
Adjustments were made to the spud size driving equipment and alignment method during
the initial trials It was noted that the spud could penetrate the fill easily and generally the
lower portions of the backfill appeared very soft Seven spud holes were made around
SMH 11 on September 15 and 16 2003 The number of spud locations was limited by the
existing sewer line a lateral sewer line and electrical lines adjacent to the manhole GBI
continued spudding at SMH 9 on September 17 and 18 2003 A total of 12 spud holes
were made around the manhole
Geolabs drilled Boring Nos 201 and 202 next to SMH 11 and Boring Nos 203 and
204 next to SMH 9 The boring data indicated that although the sample penetration blow
counts increased a little indicating some increase in compaction the soil densities were
well below the 90 compaction level desired It appeared that significantly more spud
holes would be required to achieve the desired compaction level However due to the
existing utilities adjacent to the manholes this was not practical Detailed descriptions of
the materials encountered in Boring Nos 201 through 204 are presented on the Logs of
Borings Plates A 4 through A 7 of Appendix A

Following the results of the borings GBI decided to stop the spudding operations
and delay repair of the deep backfill at the manholes GBI decided to repair the subsided
areas adjacent to the catch basins by conventional over excavation and re compaction of
the backfill since the backfill depth was generally limited to about 6 feet
Over Excavation Re Compaction of Backfill
GBI decided to repair the subsided sewer manhole areas by removing the existing
clayey silt backfill soil and replacing with crusher waste silty sand with gravel compacted
with a sheepsfoot wheel roller attached to the backhoe The general procedure was as
follows
e The backfill around the manhole was removed in sections of about 10 to
15 feet by 5 to 8 feet in plan in order to allow traffic to pass
e The clayey silt backfill soil was removed down to near the invert level 17 to
20 foot depth except where other utilities crossed the excavation limits
The excavation was generally kept about 2 feet above these other utilities
e Crusher waste from GBI s yard was placed into the excavation by a front
end loader in lifts of about 12 to 18 inches thick Water was added when
necessary by a fire hose
e A sheepsfoot wheel roller attached to a backhoe was used to compact each
lift for about 5 minutes The wheel was able to reach close to the existing
manhole structure and into most of the excavation
e Once the level of the backfill reached about 6 feet from the road level
compaction tests were taken with a nuclear gauge by Aqua Engineers
Ernest K Hirata and Associates Inc Compaction testing was not
performed on the lower portions of the backfill because the excavations
were not shored The compaction test results are presented in
Appendix B
e The backfill was brought up to about 18 inches from the road level then the
process was repeated for the adjacent excavation sections
e When completed the entire excavation area was filled with base course and
compacted with a smooth drum roller
e AC paving was done after all of the areas were repaired
Visual observations along with the compaction test results for the upper portions of
the backfill indicated that the backfill was placed and compacted adequately However in
order to verify the compaction of the lower portions of the backfill borings were drilled
through the backfill to obtain samples for density testing

The high sample blow counts recorded confirm the compaction of the backfill However
the densities measured from the samples indicated relatively low compaction percentages
We believe that the low densities are a result of sample disturbance caused by driving
through the gravel which loosens the soil and due to variability of the soil i e more sand
than gravel in some samples
SUMMARY AND CONCLUSIONS
Based on our observations and the results of the compaction tests performed on
the backfill at the manholes it is our opinion that the backfill was placed and compacted
satisfactorily However it should be noted that all of the loose backfill could not be
removed due to the presence of existing utilities Additionally the limits of the backfill
removal were limited to areas adjacent to the manholes where subsidence was noted
Therefore there may be loose backfill under the existing utilities and in adjacent
unexcavated areas We recommend that the repaired areas be observed for a period of
time including periods of heavy rain to assess the overall performance of the remedial
work
