SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located on the northwestern shore of the island
of Maui in the heart of the Old Lahaina Town The site is approximately
147 by 68 feet in plan dimensions and bordered by Lahainaluna Road to the
southeast
The area is generally flat and mostly covered with gravel fill
Ground surface elevation averages about 17 feet above Mean Sea Level

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Soil Conditions
The subsurface soil conditions at the site generally consist of a
1 5 foot thick surface layer of loose gray gravel fill underlain by
medium dense brown silty sand with some gravel to a depth of 13 5 feet
the maximum depth sampled
Groundwater Level
Groundwater was encountered at a depth of approximately 12 0 feet
depth below the existing ground surface
Due to the proximity of the site to the ocean and the permeability
of the subsoils the groundwater levels will be influenced by tidal
fluctuations
Permeability
In general permeability is a function of the grain size of the
soil coarse grained soils such as sands and gravels readily transmit
water while fine grained soils such as silts and clays generally have
low permeability
The silty sand soils encountered at the site have relatively good
permeability and appear to be practical for use in underground disposal
of surface runoff
Installation and Testing
In the designated test dry well excavation non perforated 2 inch
I D PVC casing was installed in the upper 12 feet The lower 1 5 feet
of pipe below the existing bottom surface of the dry well was cased with
slotted 2 inch PVC pipe that was capped
After installing the PVC pipe casing a twelve hour water injection
test was carried out to evaluate the feasibility of the dry well systen
Water was injected at a steady state rate of 8 gallons per minute

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The soils encountered in the test dry well excavation primarily
consisted of silty sand deposits In general these materials are quite
permeable and can readily transmit clear water for a dry well system
Test Well Design
By extrapolation of the field test data our calculations indicated
that dry wells in this area with a diameter of 8 0 feet and a depth of
12 0 feet could theoretically accommodate a gravity injection of about 57
gallons per minute with a 2 0 foot head build up
Typically surface runoff contains suspended solids silt and other
matter which may reduce the potential permeability of the receiving
formation Therefore provisions should be made in the design of the
facility for a long term maintenance program and built in capability for
well redevelopment to extend the useful life of the wells
