Proposed Development
According to the Design Development Drawings prepared by
Wayson W C Chongs Architect the proposed dwelling will con
sist of a two story wood frame structure with floor eleva
tions of 192 and 201 feet The proposed plans shows 20 spread
foundations supporting the structural area and a combined line
and retaining wall foundation along the front entrance

Site Description
The proposed 10 000 square feet site is between Maleko and
Puualoha Streets near the rear of Maunawili Estates It is
on the eastern slope of a ridge with Maleko Street along the
crest A ten foot wide sewer easement parallels the western
property line Along the easement the vegetation has been
removed The weed and bush covered front portion slopes down
ward at a 10 per cent slope for approximately 60 feet The
ground surface then drops deeply at approximately a 50 per
cent slope to the rear property line
The soils within this area are alluvial fans and terraces
formed by materials eroded from the nearby Koolau mountain
range
Subsurface Explorations
Two test borings were drilled at the location shown on the
enclosed Site Plan Plate 1 utilizing rotary augers and
both portable and truck mounted equipment Boring 1 extended
to a depth of 11 feet Boring 2 was terminated at a depth of
15 feet
Soil samples were obtained by driving a 2 4 inch I D or stan
dard penetration sampler with a 140 pound hammer free falling
a distance of 30 inches

The soils were first classified by visual and textural exa
mination in the fteld by our geologist who continuously super
vised the drilling operations Representative samples were
obtained from selected locations for classification and labora
tory testing The classifications were made in accordance with
the Unified Soil Classification System and verified by inspec
tion and testing in the laboratory A graphical representation
of the soils encountered along with detailed description are
shown on the attached Boring Logs Plate A 1 and A 2 The
blow counts to drive the samplers are shown on the boring logs
at the appropriate sample depth
The subsurface explorations disclosed that the site is under
lain to the limit of the explorations by brown clayey silt
varying from stiff to extremely stiff in consistency A thin
layer of organic silty clay was encountered in Boring 1 at a
depth of approximately ten feet The soils appear to be con
solidated alluvium
Groundwater
Groundwater was not encountered in Boring 2 What appears to
be a perched water table was encountered in Boring 1 with a
phreatic surface approximately 7 5 feet below the existing
ground surface Since this appears to be a perched water table
it should be of limited extent and should not materially affect
the foundation excavations

DISCUSSIONS COMMENTS
General
The subsurface explorations and site reconnaissance indicates
that the site is suitable for the proposed construction It
appears that the existing slopes are stable and are not subject
to creep movements The expansion tests show that the on site
soils are non expansive
Foundations
The proposed foundations should bear within the very stiff to
extremely stiff soil underlying the site The foundations
should be designed for an allowable bearing capacity of 2 000
pounds per square foot The bearing capacity may be increased
by 1 3 when considering wind and seismic loads The foundations
should bear a minimum of 4 feet below the final adjacent grade
and should maintain a minimum 5 foot setback from the outer
edge of the foundation to the finish slope
Based upon the recommended bearing values given above the
estimated settlements should not exceed 1 inch total and 1 2
inch differential
The foundation excavations should be inspected prior to placing
of the concrete to assure that adequate bearing has been ob
tained andthat the subsurface conditions are consistent with the
findings of our explorations
W Page 1975
Retaining Wall Design
It appears that a low retaining wall will be utilized near
the entrance of the structure The foundations for this re
taining wall should be destgned in accordance with the recom
mendations for the spread foundations
It is recommended that a subsurface drainage system be provided
to prevent the build up of hydrostatic pressures behind the
wall Granular backfill such as quarry waste or crushed rock
should be utilized as backfill behind the retaining wall
This will permit the wall to be designed for an equivalent
fluid pressure of 35 pounds per cubic foot A frictional fac
tor of 0 5 should be utilized to determine the sliding resis
tance of the retaining wall foundation
Earthwork Site Grading
The soils at the site appear to be stable at the present slope
of approximately 2 horizontal to 1 vertical 2 1 No addi
tional fills should be placed on the existing slopes with the
exception of the backfill behind the upper retaining wall
The soils are highly subceptible to erosion and should be pro
tected particularly in the area of the spread foundations
All precipitation run off should be diverted from this area
and a vegetation cover established as soon as possible

Investigation Limitations
The soils encountered in the subsurface investigation are con
sidered representative of the total site However the soils
may vary in characteristics between borings Since our inves
tigation was of a limited extent and based upon a fraction of
the site materials the conclusions and recommendations pre
sented herein are professional opinions The opinions have
been derived in accordance with current Standards of practice
