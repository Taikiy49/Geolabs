PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located in the District of Ewa on the island of Oahu Hawaii as
shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 As part of the Villages of Kapolei
development it is proposed to widen the existing two lane Farrington Highway into a
along Farrington Highway beginning at the intersection of Farrington Highway with
Kealanani Avenue about Station 13 00 and extending eastward about 2 800 lineal feet
to about Station 40 60 which is approximately at the intersection with Kowelo Avenue
In general the average invert of the proposed waterlines are anticipated to be at about
3 to 5 feet below the existing ground surface Soil resistivity surveys utilizing the in situ
Wenner 4 Pin Method were performed to evaluate the corrosion rating of the
near surface soils within the depths of the proposed waterlines

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our survey was to obtain an overview of the electrical resistivity of
the subsurface soils at the site Our work on this project was performed in general
conformance with ASTM Test Designation G 57 78 and with the requirements provided in
the Water System External Corrosion Control Standards Volume 3 dated 1991 The
scope of our work consisted of the following tasks and work efforts
1 Mobilization Demobilization of resistivity equipment and field engineers to
and from the project site
2 Performance of 44 in situ soil resistivity surveys at 14 locations in the
shoulder areas along the proposed waterline alignment corresponding to
the 300 foot maximum spacing required in the Water System External
Corrosion Control Standards Volume 3 dated 1991 The resistivity
surveys were performed in general accordance with ASTM Test
Designation G 57 78
3 Coordination of the field work by a Project Engineer from our firm
4 Reduction and analyses of the data pertinent to the corrosion
characteristics of the subsurface soils at the site
5 Preparation of a brief report summarizing our work on the project and
presenting our findings
6 Quality assurance of our work and client design team consultation by a
Principal Engineer of our firm
7 Miscellaneous work efforts such as drafting word processing and clerical
support
FIELD SURVEY
Geophysical survey equipment i e resistance meter was mobilized to the site on
April 14th and 15th for the field survey Our field work was performed in sunny conditions
with generally moist surface conditions
Forty four soil resistivity surveys were conducted at about 300 foot intervals at
14 locations at the project site using the in Pin Method and a resistance
meter Strata Scout R 40C manufactured by SoilTest Inc The resistance meter is
capable of transmitting 120 volts and 24 watts of output into the ground The method

utilizes four surface probes or pins electrodes to evaluate the average resistivity an
indication of corrosivity of the underlying soil mass The average resistivities of the soil
mass extending from the existing ground surface to the planned invert elevations of the
waterlines were assessed by using this method at the approximate survey locations
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 Results of resistivity survey are presented on Plates 3 1
and 3 2
The soil resistivity surveys were performed at the existing ground surface on the
exposed shoulder areas along Farrington Highway The average soil resistance was
evaluated at each survey location with the electrodes aligned at equal spacings of
generally 3 to 6 feet at the existing ground surface along the shoulder of the roadway In
addition five surveys with 5 to 9 foot spacings at survey location number 1 Station
13 00 were performed to evaluate the deepest invert of the proposed waterlines It
should be noted that some underground utility lines were observed within the shoulder
areas near some survey locations
The resistance meter R 40C impresses a voltage between the outer electrodes
causing an electrical current to flow between them The voltage drop between the inner
electrodes is then measured by a sensitive built in voltmeter As a result the soil
resistance in Ohms can be found directly on the dial The soil resistivity in ohm cm
which is directly related to both resistance and spacing can then be computed The
evaluated soil resistances the computed resistivities and the corresponding corrosion
ratings are presented on Plates 3 1 and 3 2 of this report However it should be noted
that the soil resistivity may be influenced by the existing utility lines near the survey
locations temperature weather conditions and other factors
Resistivity is generally recognized as one of the most significant soil characteristics
with regard to the corrosivity of the soil to buried metallic objects In general the lower
the resistivity the greater the potential for corrosion of the buried metallic structure
Conversely the higher the resistivity the less likely the soil will contribute to corrosion to
metallic objects
LIMITATIONS
The evaluations presented in this report are based upon information obtained from
field data points by means of geophysical surveys Variation of soil conditions between
the field data points may occur and the nature and extent of such variations may not
become evident until a later point in time If variations then appear evident it will be
necessary to reevaluate the ratings provided in this report
