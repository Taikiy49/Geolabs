PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project involves the extension of the existing Kahelu Avenue by the
construction of approximately 800 lineal feet of new roadway and infrastructure within the
right of way The project site is in Mililani on the Island of Oahu Hawaii as shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1
At the time of our exploration a gravel paved road was present along the proposed
on the information provided the existing gravel road will be demolished and the new
roadway will consist of two 30 foot wide travel lanes separated by a 20 foot wide median
Each travel lane will have two lanes A 4 foot wide concrete sidewalk will be provided on
each side of the roadway

FIELD EXPLORATION
Our field exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling five borings
designated as Boring Nos 1 through 5 extending to depths of about 21 5 feet below the
existing ground surface The borings were drilled using a truck mounted drill rig
equipped with continuous flight solid stem augers A single bulk sample designated as
Bulk 1 was obtained from the near surface soils to evaluate the pavement support
characteristics The approximate boring and bulk sample locations are shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination by our field engineer who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on the Log Legend Plate 3 Graphic representations of
the materials encountered are provided on the Logs of Borings Plates 4 through 8
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained from the borings drilled in
general accordance with ASTM D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a
3 inch outside diameter OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling
30 inches In addition some samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general
accordance with ASTM D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by
driving a 2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same hammer and drop The
blow counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an 18 inch drive
are shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate
sample depths

FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered in the borings the principal
geotechnical considerations for the project design include proper site preparation
requirements which take into consideration the low to moderately expansive nature of the
near surface soils and the relatively wet condition of the soils at the site Detailed
discussion of our geotechnical engineering recommendations for design of pavements and
site preparation are presented in the following sections herein
Pavement Design
Based on our field exploration the proposed new roadway alignment is generally
underlain by very stiff to hard clayey silts at the ground surface Laboratory testing
performed on the surface clayey soils indicated CBR values of about 23 and swells of
less than one percent Traffic data were not available however we anticipate that the
equivalent single axle loads ESALs will not exceed 1 million for the design life and

there will be no regular bus service Based on the above we recommend using the
following new pavement section be used for preliminary design and cost estimating
purposes
Pavement Design
2 5 Inches Asphalt Concrete
4 0 Inches Asphalt Concrete Base 91 Percent Relative Specific Gravity
6 0 Inches Aggregate Base Course 95 Percent Relative Compaction
12 5 Inches Total Pavement Thickness on Moist Compacted Subgrade
Asphalt concrete base ACB material should consist of asphalt treated basalt
aggregate placed in a layer not to exceed 6 inches in compacted thickness and
compacted to greater than 91 percent of the maximum theoretical specific gravity
determined in accordance with ASTM D 2041 The aggregate base course should consist
of crushed basaltic aggregate compacted to no less than 95 percent relative compaction
Our experience in the Mililani area suggests that the swelling potential of the in situ
soils could be somewhat variable in nature Therefore CBR and field density tests should
be performed on the actual subgrade soils encountered during construction to confirm the
adequacy of the above section The above recommended pavement section assumes that
good drainage will be provided for the paved areas
Pavement Drainage
One of the primary distress mechanisms in pavement structures is pumping due
to saturation of the base course subbase and or subgrade soils Therefore the
pavement surface should be sloped and drainage gradients should be maintained to
carry surface water off the pavement to appropriate drainage structures Surface water
ponding should not be allowed on site during or after construction
Where concrete curbs are used to isolate landscaping in or adjacent to the
pavement areas we recommend extending the curbs a minimum of 2 inches into the
subgrade soils below the base and subbase course layers to reduce the potential for
migration of excessive landscape water into the pavement section Alternatively a
subdrain system could be constructed to collect excess water from the landscaping
irrigation
Site Preparation
Based on the existing topography and the anticipated design finish grades for the
proposed roadway project we anticipate that site grading work consisting of shallow
cuts and thin fills on the order of about 3 feet in depth and thickness will be required for
the proposed road construction In general the area within the contract grading limits
should be cleared and grubbed thoroughly at the on set of earthwork Vegetation

debris deleterious material and other unsuitable materials should be removed and
disposed properly off site Soft and yielding areas encountered during clearing below
areas designated to receive fill or future improvements should be over excavated to
expose firm and or stiff material and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with
well compacted fill The excavated soft and or organic soils should be properly disposed
off site
Existing structures and pavements to be demolished should be completely
removed The over excavations resulting from demolition should be backfilled with
compacted granular fill material Existing utilities to be abandoned should be removed
and the resulting excavation should be backfilled properly with granular fill material
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content placed in 8 inch level
loose lifts and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
After clearing and grubbing the exposed subgrades should be proof rolled with a
minimum 10 ton vibratory drum roller a minimum of six passes to assist in detecting
loose zones Loose areas disclosed during the proof rolling operation should be
backfilled with general fill material compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative
compaction The proof rolling operations should be conducted under the
near continuous observation of a Geolabs representative
Subsequent to the proof rolling operations the subgrades should then be
scarified to a minimum depth of 12 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent
above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent
relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil
expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in
accordance with ASTM D 1557 Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by
dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
Where shrinkage cracks are observed after compaction of the subgrade the soils
should be scarified and prepared again as recommended above Saturation and
subsequent yielding of the exposed subgrade due to inclement weather and poor
drainage may require over excavation of the soft areas and replacement with
well compacted fill Contract documents should include additive and deductive unit
prices for over excavation and compacted fill placement to account for variations in the
over excavation quantities
Asphalt concrete base ACB material should consist of asphalt treated basalt
aggregate placed in a layer not to exceed 6 inches in compacted thickness and
compacted to greater than 91 percent of the maximum theoretical specific gravity
determined in accordance with ASTM D 2041 The aggregate base course material should
meet the requirements of Section 31 of the City and County Standard Specifications
September 1986 The material should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum
moisture content placed in 8 inch level loose lifts and compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative compaction

Imported fill materials below the subbase course layer should consist of
non expansive select granular material such as crushed coral or basalt The material
should be well graded from coarse to fine with no particles larger than 3 inches in
largest dimension In addition the material should contain between 10 and 30 percent
particles passing the No 200 sieve The material should have a laboratory CBR value

accordance with ASTM D 1883 The materials should be moisture conditioned to above

Utility Trenches
We anticipate that the utilities for the roadway extension project will primarily
consist of water drain and sewer lines In general good construction practices should be
utilized for the installation and backfilling of the trenches for the new utilities at the site The
method and equipment to be used for trench excavation should be determined by the
contractor subject to practical limits and safety considerations In addition the excavations
should comply with the applicable federal state and local safety requirements
Based on the results of our field exploration it appears that conventional
excavation methods may generally be utilized for utility line excavation at the site The
upper clayey soils encountered at the site are generally very stiff to hard Although none
were encountered in our exploration there is a potential that isolated boulders could be
encountered in utility trench excavations
In general we recommend that granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of
No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation be used for support under the pipes
Free draining granular materials such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67
gradation should also be used for the initial trench backfill up to about 12 inches above
the pipes to provide adequate support around the pipes It is critical to use this
free draining material to reduce the potential for formation of voids below the haunches of
pipes and to provide adequate support for the sides of the pipes Improper trench backfill
could result in backfill settlement and pipe damage
The upper portion of the trench backfill from a level of 12 inches above the pipes to
the top of the subgrade or finished grade may consist of the on site soils with a maximum
particle size of 6 inches or select granular fill material The backfill should be placed in
maximum 8 inch level loose lifts and mechanically compacted to no less than 90 percent

Corrosion Protection
Two sets of corrosion tests including pH and Minimum Resistivity were
performed to evaluate the corrosivity of the on site soils generally encountered at the
site The results of these tests are presented on Plate 13 of this report The minimum
resistivity values of the near surface soil samples tested are on the order of about
4 900 and 14 000 ohm cm which correspond to a Corrosion Rating of 4 and 5
moderately and mildly corrosive based on the guidelines provided by the City
County of Honolulu Board of Water Supply However the pH values were less than 5
indicating extremely corrosive potential
