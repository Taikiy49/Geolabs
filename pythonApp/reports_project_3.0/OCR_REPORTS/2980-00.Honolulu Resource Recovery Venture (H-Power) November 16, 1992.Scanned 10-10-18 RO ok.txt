INTRODUCTION
In accordance with the request of of Asea Brown Boveri we
have conducted an inspection of the injection wells for the Honolulu Resource Recovery
Venture H POWER facility in Campbell Industrial Park
Our work was performed in general accordance with our proposal to Asea Brown
Boveri dated August 17 1992
This report summarizes our findings from the site inspection
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Honolulu Resource Recovery Venture H POWER injection wells are utilized
for disposal of cooling water and demineralizer effluent generated at this waste to energy
generation plant The majority of the injected fluid is from the cooling tower used for
cooling of the condenser units in the generating plant Generally the wells are used on
a continual basis
As a condition of the Underground Injection Control UIC permit issued for the
facility UIC Permit No UO 1376 it is required that a periodic inspection of the wells be
made by a professional engineer or geologist familiar with injection well operation This
inspection is intended to ascertain that the wells remain operational

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our work on the project was to conduct an inspection of the
injection wells to make visual observations of their operational status
In order to accomplish this task we have conducted a program consisting of the
following work efforts
1 A visit to the site by a Senior Project Geologist of our firm Activities during
this site visit included field interviews with representatives of Asea Brown
Boveri the generation plant operators observation of the well heads
fl sounding of the open portion of the wells and visual examination of water
samples bailed from the wells
2 Preparation of this report summarizing our work on the project and
presenting our discussions and findings
3 Coordination of our overall work on the project by a Principal Engineer of
our firm
4 Miscellaneous work efforts such as drafting word processing and clerical
support
GENERAL INFORMATION
The facility has been granted a permit to operate injection wells under the
Underground Injection Control program In this permit the facility is identified by Facility
ID No and the wells are identified as Well Nos 1 and 2 Personnel at the
facility identify Well No 1 as the North Well and Well No 2 as the South Well
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2980 00 Page 3
The H POWER plant is a waste to energy generation plant in which municipal solid
wastes are burned for the generation of electrical power The waste is shredded
segregated and fed into the boiler fire box where it is burned The heat in the waterwall
boiler produces steam which is used to turn generator turbines After the steam is
expended in the turbines it is cooled in condenser units before being returned to the
boiler
The heat exchangers in the condenser units are cooled by water from the cooling
tower Waste thermal energy in the cooling tower is removed through evaporative losses
The make up water for the cooling tower is from on site brackish water source
wells Evaporative losses in the cooling tower result in a increase in concentration of the
mineral content of the water to about twice the original level The cooling water is
continuously drained to the injection wells to maintain flow through the system
The wells are located in an open yard area easterly of the cooling tower The
ground surface is covered with gravel and there is no vegetation in the vicinity of the well
heads Based on the information provided to us by Asea Brown Boveri the combined
flow to the two 2 wells averages about 800 000 gallons per day This flow measurement
is calculated by the amount of make up water used with respect to the concentrations of
the water as it leaves the cooling tower A copy of the flow records from January 1 to
is shown in Appendix A
DISCUSSIONS AND FINDINGS
Well Site Conditions
The wells are located on the easterly side of the cooling tower The wells are
situated in an open yard area between the cooling tower and the RDF Storage building
See Photo Nos 1 and 4
GEOLABS HAWAIl

The well heads are aboveground tee assemblies with a shutoff valve mounted on
the side of the tee The tees are bolted to the well casings which are set in concrete well
pads The tops of the tees are capped with blind flange plates secured by bolts See
Photo Nos 2 and 5 These plates were removed for the inspection
The well heads appear to have been constructed in accordance with good
workmanship and no overt signs of detects were noted
There is no vegetation in the vicinity of the wells nor was there any visible evidence
of leaks ponding of water or other unusual surface conditions
Well Inspection
The general conditions of the wells were observed by sounding of the open wells
and by bailing of water from the wells to allow visual observation of the amount of
turbidity The following are the results of those observations
Well 1 North Well
The visible portion of the interior of the casing appeared to be in good condition
with no overt signs of corrosion or other damage See Photo No 3 According
to Mr Murata this well has been used for injection of cooling tower effluent since
the start up of the generating plant without incident The well was redeveloped by
pumping in September 1991
At 0950 hours on the water level in the well was measured at
a depth of 15 3 feet below the top of the well head The well had been shut down
for about 24 hours prior to the inspection Sounding of the well with a weighted
tape measure indicated that the well was open to a depth of 253 0 feet below the
top of the well head

of the well head showed that the water was relatively clear with few suspended

A bailer sample of water taken from the bottom of the well showed that the water
was relatively clear with some suspended particles
Well 2 South Well
According to Mr Murata this well has been used for injection of cooling tower
effluent since the start up of the plant and in September of 1991 the well began
to backup and overflow back to the cooling tower
Roscoe Moss Company was contracted to redevelop the well Attempts to
redevelop by bailing were not successful At that time the well was deepened
approximately 50 feet The well was then redeveloped by high stress pumping with
a line shaft turbine pump Since the redrilling and redevelopment there does not
appear to be any problems with the well
At 1050 hours on the water level in the well was measured at
a depth of 15 7 feet below the top of the well head This indicates that well has
good recovery since the well had been in operation until shortly before it was

Injection System
The injection system is regulated by valves which can be used to isolate the wells
so that either or both of the wells may be used for injection Generally both wells are
used for injection simultaneously
At the time of our site visit the operator Asea Brown Boveri was contracting for
a calibrated weir to be installed in the system to provide more accurate records of the rate
and daily total of injection through the two 2 wells
Currently there is no system for monitoring the head levels in the wells These
data would be helpful in monitoring the long term performance of the wells and serve as
an early detection system in the event that one of the wells begins to lose capacity as
Well No 2 did about a year ago
Without these data it is not possible to provide any detailed evaluation of the
performance of the wells
Recommendations
To facilitate future inspections of the wells we recommend that the inspection ports
in the blind flange plates be loosened and maintained to facilitate their removal This
would eliminate the need to remove the top plates
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2980 00 Page 7
To attempt to monitor head levels in the individual wells we recommend that
recording piezometers be installed in the wells Generally simple vibrating wire
piezometers such as the manufactured by Geokon Inc could be
installed in dip tubes in the wells to provide a continuous record of the head levels The
data could be stored and retrieved from a data logger such as the
GeoCan A single data logger could serve both wells A schematic of this installation is
shown on Plate 2 Product literature pertaining to this system is attached as Appendix
B
To maintain the injection capacity of the wells we recommend that an operation
and maintenance program be instituted There is a tendency for injection wells to clog
over time as a result of suspended solids in the effluent in the wells
To partially restore capacity lost by the introduction of suspended solids we
recommend that the wells be redeveloped on a periodic basis It is our opinion that airlift
pumping could be effective for redevelopment of the wells
Redevelopment should be scheduled and conducted based on the head level data
obtained from the recommended recording piezometers Generally redevelopment
should be conducted if the head level shows an average increase of about 10 to 15
percent over conditions established after a redevelopment event The actual amount of
increase needed to initiate redevelopment will need to be determined based on the data
derived from monitoring of the head levels in the wells

information obtained from field observations and oral communications with personnel at
the site
