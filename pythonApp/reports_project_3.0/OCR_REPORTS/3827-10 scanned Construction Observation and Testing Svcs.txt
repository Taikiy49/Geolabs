Dear Ms Murano
This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observation and testing services
performed during the site grading operations for the Outfitting of Parker Well Nos 1 and 2
Phase 2 project Our services were conducted from through August
12 1999 in general accordance with our revised fee proposal dated August 21 1998
SITE GRADING
The project site is located on the slopes above Puako Bay and Queen Kaahumanu
Highway in the South Kohala area on the Island of Hawaii The general location and vicinity
of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The subject project
includes the construction of a control building located at the well site a new access roadway
and utility lines
The site grading operations were performed by Isemoto Contracting Company Ltd
In general the site grading operations consisted of clearing and grubbing preparation of the
subgrade soils and placement and compaction of on site and imported fills Much of the fills
needed at the site to raise the pre existing grades to the design finished grades consisted
of the on site materials consisting of silty gravel with sand generally 6 inch minus material
During the site grading operations samples of the on site and imported materials that
were utilized as fill materials were obtained and transported to our laboratory for testing The
maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory
in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test
results are summarized on Plate 2 of this report

After clearing and grubbing of the site the existing rock subgrades were compacted
with a vibratory drum roller or similar heavy equipment for a minimum of six passes The
existing soil subgrades were scarified moisture conditioned and compacted to at least
90 percent of the maximum dry density as determined in accordance with American Society
of Testing and Material ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor Fill materials
were generally placed in near level lifts brought to above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density determined in
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor It should be noted that
the compaction requirement for the pipe bedding and trench backfill at least 3 feet below the
finished grade was reduced to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density
In general fill materials consisting of 6 inch minus fills were placed for the roadway
fills and utility trench backfills Due to the nature of the fill materials 6 inch minus fill
materials the fills and backfills were evaluated based on field observations during the fill
placement Where applicable such as the aggregate base materials and 3 inch minus fill
materials field density tests were performed with the nuclear density gauge ASTM Test
Designation D 2992 and D 3017 During the fill and backfill operations the materials were
generally placed in near level lifts brought to near the optimum moisture and compacted to
obtain the specified degree of compaction based on visual observations and or field density
tests Periodic field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations to
confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures and soil conditions
were noted
A total of 176 field density tests including re tests of failed areas were performed
using the nuclear density gauge method in general accordance with ASTM Test
Designations D 2922 and D 3017 Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were re worked until
the specified minimum compaction was obtained The results of the compaction tests
performed during construction of the subject project are summarized on Plates 3 1 through
3 6 of this report These tests were performed on the prepared subgrade on constructed fills
and backfills at the site where possible and on the aggregate base materials Test locations
and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes where
available These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree
implied by the methods used
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
above referenced tests it is our opinion that the earthwork operations for the grading of the
above referenced project site observed and tested by Geolabs Inc in the period covered
by this report were performed in general conformance with the intent of the project plans and
specifications

LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities occurring
simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork occurred without
our observation However the level of earthwork observation and testing services provided
appear to be reasonable in reducing some of the geotechnical risks to the project It should
be recognized that there are variations in the accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests
and methods used to monitor the earthwork construction and materials
Our services were performed in accordance with generally accepted standards of
geotechnical engineering practice no warranty is expressed or implied Provision of
construction monitoring by an engineer is not insurance nor does it guarantee construction
of any type The contractor shall retain responsibility for the quality of the work for adhering
to the plans and specifications and for repairing defects regardless of when they are found
