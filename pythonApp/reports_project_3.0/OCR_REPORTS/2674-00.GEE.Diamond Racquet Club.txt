INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration for
the proposed Diamond Racquet Club development in the Kihei area of Maui Hawaii
The general location of the project site and its vicinity is shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1
Our work on the project was performed in general accordance with our revised
proposal to Diamond Resort Corporation c o AM Partners Incorporated dated April
22 1991 This report summarizes our findings from our preliminary field exploration
and presents our geotechnical engineering recommendations
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to develop the site as a residential cluster development Based
on the plans provided to us it is anticipated that the development will consist of nine
9 duplex buildings The buildings will most likely be lightly loaded two 2 story
wood and masonry structures with slabs on grade for the first floor A one 1 story
clubhouse a paved parking area and access road a tennis court and a lap pool are
also planned
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions at the project site in order to develop a generalized soil and rock data set
for the formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations pertaining to the
design of the proposed site grading infrastructure pavement and preliminary house
foundations

GENERAL SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located in the Kihei area on the island of Maui Hawaii The
site is situated between Auhana Road and Alahele Place and is about 500 feet east
of South Kihei Road
The site is densely vegetated with tall grass and keawe trees
The existing ground surface elevation of the site varies from approximately 12
to about 37 feet above Mean Sea Level
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling two 2 borings to
depths ranging from 20 9 to 21 5 feet below the existing ground surface and
excavating six 6 test pits to 3 0 to 9 0 foot depths using a hoptoe The approximate
location of the borings and test pits are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general the borings and test pits encountered a surface dune sand deposit
consisting of about 0 8 to 9 0 feet of medium dense fine sand underlain by medium
dense to very dense basalt gravel with sand cobbles and boulders clinker to 21 5
feet the maximum depth drilled

CONCLUSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The medium dense to very stiff on site soil encountered can generally provide
adequate support for the proposed residential structures
Site Grading
Fills up to about 12 feet in vertical thickness and cuts up to about 12 feet in
depth are planned for the site
Generally the on site soils should be suitable for use as engineered fill If
additional imported material is required the material should consist of non expansive
select materials less than three 3 inches in maximum size with less than 25
passing the 200 sieve
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2674 00 Page 6
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should
be cleared of vegetation debris rubbish and other deleterious materials These
materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site Any loose or soft
spots encountered at the pavement or footing subgrade level should be over
excavated down to the firm subsoils and then replaced with compacted non
expansive select material
The fill areas should be scarified to a depth of six 6 inches moisture
conditioned and recompacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density per
ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
The fill should be placed in maximum 10 inch thick loose horizontal lifts and
compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method
D 1557 78
in general for cut and fill siopes slope ratios of two horizontal to one vertical
2H 1V or flatter are recommended for the site grading design
Water should be diverted away from the tops of slopes and slope planting
utilized to reduce potential erosion and or instability of the finished slopes
Foundation Design
Based upon the soil conditions encountered in our field exploration it is our
opinion that shallow spread and continuous footing foundations may be utilized to
support the proposed structures Any soft wet and or yielding areas encountered
at footing subgrade level should be over excavated to firm natural material and the
resulting excavation should be backfilled with non expansive material compacted in
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2674 00 Page 7
thin lifts to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method
D 1557 78
An allowable bearing value of 3000 p s f may be used for footings bearing on
the on site soils or compacted fill
Individual footings and continuous wall footings should be embedded a
minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade
Based on our exploration and laboratory testing estimated foundation
settlements are on the order of less than one half of an inch total and one fourth of
an inch differential
A base friction coefficient of 0 4 may be used to compute the frictional
resistance between the bottoms of the footings and the recompacted on site soils or
the select backfill In addition a passive resistance of 300 p s f per foot of depth of
the buried vertical faces of the footings may be used to calculate resistance to lateral
loads if the backfill around the footings is compacted to 90 relative density
If a footing foundation is located next to a utility trench the footing should be
extended to the same depth as the bottom of the trench to reduce the potential of
footing distress resulting from the settlement of the trench backfill If a continuous
footing foundation crosses a utility trench it should be designed to span the trench
to reduce the potential of foundation distress due to settlement of the backfill

Slab On Grade
itis anticipated that the floors of the proposed buildings on the project site will
be concrete slab on grade Building slabs and their cushions may be supported by
the on site soils or compacted fill
A 4 inch layer of 3 Fine gravel cushion fill should be provided below the slab
In order to protect against future moisture infiltration an impervious moisture barrier
may be incorporated below the slab
Slab thickened edges should be embedded a minimum of eighteen 18 inches
below the lowest adjacent exterior grade
Retaining Walls
It is anticipated that retaining walls may be required to provide grade
separation The guidelines for design of retaining walls are as follows
1 For lateral earth pressures the following values may be used
Walls unrestrained at the top 95 p s f equivalent
active condition fluid pressure
level backfill per foot of depth
Walls restrained at the top 50 p s f equivalent
at rest condition fluid pressure
level backfill per foot of depth
Additional load due to traffic or surcharge should be included where
applicable
2 An allowable bearing capacity of up to 3000 p s f is recommended on
the on site or compacted fill soils
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2674 00 Page 9
3 A passive earth resistance of 300 p s f per foot of depth may be used
for the wall footing on level areas The passive pressure must be
reduced for walls located on slopes
4 A base friction of factor of 0 4 may also be used to determine the
resistance against sliding of the wall
5 The wall foundations on relatively flat areas should be embedded a
minimum of 24 inches below the lowest adjacent grade For sloping
ground the footing should extend a sufficient depth to obtain a
minimum 10 foot set back from the outside edge of the footing
measured horizontally to the slope face
6 A subsurface drainage system subdrains and or weepholes should be
utilized behind the walls to minimize the build up of hydrostatic pressure
behind the walls Suidrains should be constructed properly to drain
water away from the wails
Select free draining granular backfill material such as 3B Fine gravel
is recommended behind the retaining walls out to the excavation line
to reduce the compactive effort required and corresponding stresses on
the wall However the upper two 2 feet of backfill outside the covered
area should consist of a non expansive compacted impervious
material graded to drain water away from the wall to reduce the
potential infiltration of water behind the walls

It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to provide

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part
upon information obtained from the borings and field testing Variations of conditions
between the field data points may occur and the nature and extent of these
variations may not become evident until construction If variations then appear
evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations given in this report
The location of the field data points were determined by taping and estimation
from reference points Elevations of the data points were approximately determined
by interpolation from the elevation points shown on the topographic survey map by

locations and elevations of the field data points should be considered accurate only
to the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the field data points
depict the approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a
gradual transition The strata lines shown on profiles or cross sections are based
upon interpolation between borings and may not represent actual subsurface
conditions
