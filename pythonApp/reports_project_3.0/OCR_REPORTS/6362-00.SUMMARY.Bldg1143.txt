Project Considerations
The project site is located on the northern side of Kalaeloa formerly Naval Air
Station Barbers Point on the Island of Oahu Hawaii as shown on Project Location Map
Plate 1 Building 1143 is a wood framed warehouse structure that was formerly used by
the Navy Proposed tenant improvements to the structure include the addition of interior
walls and bays to the open warehouse
In addition to the improvements to Building 1143 it is proposed to construct a new
metal framed open shed next to the existing building This new structure will be about
40 feet in length and 100 feet in length with a slab on grade floor The structure will span
the entire 100 feet with no intermediate columns
The primary consideration for the improvements and the new structure is resistance
to wind and seismically induced uplift and lateral loading

Field Exploration
We explored the subsurface conditions at the project site by drilling three auger test
holes at the site using contractor provided equipment Two of the test holes were drilled
inside the existing warehouse using an 8 inch auger mounted on a Bobcat skid steer
loader These holes extended about 7 feet below the existing slab surface The
remaining test hole was drilled outside the building using a 24 inch auger mounted on a
boom truck This hole extended to a depth of about 12 feet below the existing ground
surface
In general the project site is underlain by existing fill materials consisting of about
1 foot of very dense white to tan sandy gravel Coral Fill over about 2 feet of very stiff to
hard brown clayey silt with gravel Under the fill the test holes encountered dense sandy
gravel Coral Detritus to the maximum depths drilled We did not encounter groundwater
in the test holes
Detailed descriptions of the materials encountered in the test holes at the probe
location are presented on the Logs of Borings Plates 3 through 5

For this project we use the term shaft sidewall contact to express the length of
open hole required before placement of reinforcement and concrete This term is used
since during the drilling of the test holes it was noted that the available equipment had
difficulty cleaning the bottom of drilled holes Therefore it may be necessary to overdrill
the shaft holes to provide sufficient contact between the shaft and the surrounding
subsoils If during construction the holes can be cleaned then shaft length may be used
rather than sidewall contact
At the request of Mr Matthew Hunt we provided recommendations for the design
of the foundations for a 5 ton crane that will be installed in the new shed structure For the
design of the crane foundation an allowable bearing capacity of 3 000 pounds per
square foot psf may be used for a footing or footings bearing on subgrade soils
recompacted to 90 relative density as established by ASTM D 1557 The bottom of
the footing should be embedded a minimum of 2 feet below the lowest adjacent
grade In addition foundations next to other foundations utility trenches easements or
other retaining walls should be embedded below a 45 degree imaginary plane
extending upward from the bottom edge of the structure or utility trench or the footings
should be extended to a depth as deep as the inverts of the utility lines This
requirement is necessary to avoid surcharging adjacent below grade structures with
additional structural loads and to reduce the potential for appreciable foundation
settlement
Limitations
The analyses and recommendations submitted herein are based in part upon
information obtained from test auger holes Variations of subsurface soil conditions may
occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident until
construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to
re evaluate the recommendations provided in this letter report
