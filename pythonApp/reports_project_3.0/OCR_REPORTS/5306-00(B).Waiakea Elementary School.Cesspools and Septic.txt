PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed project involves improvements to the existing cesspool system
located at the Waiakea Intermediate School in Hilo on the Island of Hawaii The general
location and vicinity of the project site is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
Based on the available information provided to us the planned improvements generally
involve the re use of the existing cesspools as seepage pits
The project site is located at the existing Waiakea Intermediate School campus in
Hilo Based on the available plans the existing cesspool system comprises
eight cesspools located within the intermediate school campus The approximate
locations of the cesspools are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In order to evaluate the permeability of the subsurface materials field percolation
tests were conducted at selected depths and locations in general accordance with the
Wastewater Systems and Chapter 10 of the Ten States Standards

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Field Borings
Two borings designated as Boring Nos 1 and 2 were drilled at selected locations
at the Waiakea Intermediate School project site The borings were drilled to depths of
about 10 and 11 feet below the existing ground surface at the approximate locations
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were drilled using a truck mounted drill rig
equipped with auger equipment and wireline coring tools
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representations of the materials
encountered are provided on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 and A 2

Samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by
driving a 2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same hammer and drop The
blow counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an 18 inch drive
are shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Boring at the appropriate
sample depths
Core samples of the basalt rock formation encountered at the site were obtained
using diamond core drilling techniques in general accordance with ASTM Standard
Practice D 2113 Diamond Core Drilling for Site Investigation Core drilling is a rotary
drilling method that uses a hollow bit to cut into the rock formation The rock material left
in the hollow core of the bit is mechanically recovered for examination and description
Recovery REC is used as a subjective guide to the interpretation of the relative
quality of rock masses Recovery is defined as the actual length of material recovered
from a coring attempt versus the length of the core attempt For example if 3 7 feet of
material is recovered from a 5 0 foot core run the recovery would be 74 percent and
would be shown on the Logs of Borings as REC 74
The Rock Quality Designation RQD is also a subjective guide to the relative
quality of rock masses RQD is defined as the percentage of the core run that is sound
material in excess of 4 inches in length without discontinuities discounting drilling induced
fractures or breaks If 2 5 feet of sound material is recovered from a 5 0 foot core run the
RQD would be 50 percent and would be shown on the Logs of Borings as RQD 50

Subsurface Conditions
In general the borings encountered a thin clayey topsoil layer overlying hard and
medium hard basaltic rock and basaltic clinker extending to the maximum depth explored
of about 11 feet below the existing ground surface
At the time of our field exploration groundwater was not encountered in the borings
drilled at the project site It is anticipated that the basal groundwater level may reside at
depths of at least about 80 to 100 feet below the existing ground surface Perched
groundwater may be encountered locally at shallower depths It should be noted that
groundwater levels may vary significantly depending on tidal fluctuation seasonal
precipitation storm surge conditions groundwater withdrawal and other factors
FIELD PERCOLATION TESTING
Four field percolation tests identified as Percolation Test 2A and 2B
were performed The percolation tests were performed at depths of about 3 8 feet and
below the existing ground surface in Boring No B 2 The field percolation testing was
performed in general accordance with the provisions of the State of Hawaii Department of
of the Ten States Standards to evaluate the infiltration characteristics of the subsurface
soils for wastewater disposal The percolation test results are presented on the
Site Evaluation Percolation Test Forms Plates B 1 through B 4
The percolation tests were performed in the basalt rock materials by core drilling
4 inch diameter boreholes to depths of about 10 and 11 feet below the existing ground
surface During testing the time intervals and water level drops were recorded to provide
field data upon which calculation of the percolation tests were based
It should be noted the infiltration of water within the basalt clinker materials was
observed to be very rapid with minimal head build up encountered Based on the
percolation test results it appears that the percolation rate within the basaltic clinker layers
is very good with percolation rates on the order of about less than 1 minute per inch Other
percolation tests conducted in the less fractured basaltic rock formation indicate that the
percolation rate within the harder basaltic rock formation may be only fair with percolation
rates of about 30 to 60 minutes per inch Therefore it is our opinion that the infiltration
characteristic of the subsurface basalt rock materials is variable due to the layered
composition of the formation and generally good percolation rates are expected from the
seepage pits
Due to the variability and layering of the subsurface basalt rock materials at the site
the ultimate absorption capacity of the disposal system should be confirmed by additional
percolation tests during construction

LIMITATIONS
The findings and discussion submitted in this report are based upon information
obtained from limited field borings and percolation tests Variations of subsurface
conditions between and beyond the field borings may occur and the nature and extent of
these variations may not become evident until construction is underway If variations then
appear evident it will be necessary to reevaluate the findings and discussion provided in
this report
The locations of the field borings indicated in this report are approximate having
been taped from features shown on the Site Plan provided by M E Pacific Inc on
The physical locations of the borings should be considered accurate
only to the degree implied by the methods used
The stratification lines shown on the graphic representations of the borings depict
the approximate boundaries between soil and or rock types and as such may denote a
gradual transition Water level data presented in the logs of borings of this report were
based on measurements recorded in the borings at the times shown on the graphic
representations These data have been reviewed and interpretations made in the
formulation of this report However it must be noted that fluctuation may occur due to
variation in tides rainfall temperature and other factors

client the State of Hawaii Department of Accounting and General Services for specific
application to the proposed septic system improvement project located at the Waiakea
Intermediate School in accordance with generally accepted geotechnical engineering
principles and practices No warranty is expressed or implied
This data report has been prepared solely for the purpose of assisting the engineer
in the planning and preliminary design of the proposed project Therefore this report may
not contain sufficient data or the proper information for use in preparation of final design
drawings construction cost estimates or other uses A contractor wishing to bid on this
project is urged to retain a competent geotechnical engineer to assist in the interpretation

design and bid estimating purposes
