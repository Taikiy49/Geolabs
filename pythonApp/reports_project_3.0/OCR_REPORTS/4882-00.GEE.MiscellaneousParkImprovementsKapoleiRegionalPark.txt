PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located within the existing Kapolei Regional Park along the
northern side of Kama aha Avenue in the Kapolei area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The
general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map
Plate 1 Based on the information provided it is proposed to improve the existing
conditions and facilities at the park site with the construction of a hula mound a one
hundred stall parking lot and concrete walkways connecting the hula mound area to the
future comfort station site A layout of the new construction and improvements are shown
on the Site Plan Plate 2
Our experience in the area suggests that the project site may be underlain by stiff
clayey soils overlying weathered basalt rock formation Special attention was given to the
swelling potential and pavement support characteristics of the near surface soils pertaining
to the design of the new concrete walkways and parking lot

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our field exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling six borings
designated as Boring Nos 1 through 6 extending to depths ranging from about 10 5 to
21 5 feet below the existing ground surface The borings were drilled using a
truck mounted drill rig equipped with continuous flight solid stem augers The

In general our field exploration disclosed that the project site is underlain by
surface fill soils consisting of medium stiff to stiff clayey silts and silty clays extending to
depths of approximately 5 to 10 feet below the existing ground surface Below the upper
fill soils moderately weathered basalt formation was generally encountered and extended

FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Detailed discussion of our geotechnical engineering recommendations for design of
new concrete walkways parking pavements site preparation and utility trench are
presented in the following sections of this report
Concrete Walkways
We understand that new concrete walkways are planned from the new hula mound
area to the future comfort station site for the project Based on our field exploration and
laboratory test results the project site is generally underlain by a surface layer of
moderately expansive clayey soils Unless slabs on grade constructed above these
expansive soils are properly designed there is a potential for future distress to the lightly
loaded slabs on grade resulting from shrinking and swelling of the clayey soils due to
changes in the moisture content To reduce the potential for structural distress resulting
from swelling of the subgrade soils we recommend that a minimum 12 inch thick layer of
non expansive select granular material or aggregate subbase be placed below the new
concrete walkways
The subgrade soils below the walkways should be scarified to a depth of about
8 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture content
and recompacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction The non expansive
select granular material or aggregate subbase should be compacted to a minimum of
90 percent relative compaction To reduce the potential for substantial shrinkage cracks in
the slabs crack control joints should be provided at intervals equal to the width of the
walkways with expansion joints provided at right angle intersections

The thickened edges of slabs adjacent to unpaved areas should be embedded at
least 12 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade Areas adjacent to the slab
should be backfilled tightly against the slab edges with the on site soils and graded to
divert water away from the slab to reduce the potential for water ponding around the slabs
Pavements
As part of the park improvement project we understand that a one hundred stall
parking lot with asphalt concrete paving is also planned for the project In general we
anticipate the vehicle loading for the new pavements will consist primarily of passenger
vehicles minivans and light pick up trucks We have made our preliminary pavement
design assuming that the pavement subgrade soils will be similar to the clayey soils
CBR value of less than 3 and a swell of over 5 percent encountered in our borings
Based on the above assumptions we recommend that the following flexible pavement
section be used for preliminary design purposes
Flexible Pavements
2 5 Inch Asphalt Concrete
6 0 Inch Aggregate Base Course 95 Percent Relative Compaction
12 0 Inch Aggregate Subbase 95 Percent Relative Compaction
20 5 Inch Total Pavement Thickness over Compacted Moist Subgrade
The pavement subgrades should be scarified to a depth of about 8 inches
moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture and recompacted
to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction The aggregate base course and
subbase should consist of crushed basalt aggregate compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative compaction CBR and field density tests should be performed on the
actual subgrade soils encountered during construction to confirm the adequacy of the
above section
Paved areas should be sloped and drainage gradients maintained to carry surface
water off the site Surface water ponding should not be allowed on the site during or after
construction Where concrete curbs are used to isolate landscaping in or adjacent to the
pavement areas we recommend that the curbs be extended a minimum of 2 inches into
the subgrade soil below the aggregate subbase layer to reduce migration of landscape
water into the pavement section Alternatively a subdrain system could be constructed to
collect excessive water from landscaping irrigation For long term performance we
recommend that a subdrain system be constructed adjacent to paved landscaped areas

At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed Vegetation debris and other deleterious and organic
materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site Soft and or yielding areas
encountered during clearing and grubbing should be over excavated to expose firm
material and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with well compacted fills The
excavated soft and or organic soils should be properly disposed of off site and or used in
landscape areas where appropriate Contract documents should include additive and
deductive unit prices for over excavation and compacted fill placement to account for
variations in the over excavation quantities
After clearing and grubbing existing pavements and structures that are to be
demolished should be completely removed Over excavation resulting from demolition
should be backfilled with compacted select granular fill material Existing utilities to be
abandoned should be removed and the resulting excavation should be properly backfilled
with select granular fill material placed in 8 inch loose lifts and compacted to a minimum of
90 percent relative compaction Utilities to be abandoned in place under the proposed
structures should be backfilled by pumping lean concrete under low pressure
After clearing grubbing and demolition the over excavated subgrades should be
scarified to a minimum depth of 8 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above
the optinum moisture and recompacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
The compaction requirement for the subgrade soils at the new parking lot should be
increased to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to
the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density as
determined by ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Optimum moisture is the water content
percentage by weight corresponding to the maximum dry density Where shrinkage
cracks are noted after compaction of the subgrade we recommend that the subgrade soil
be prepared again as recommended above Saturation and subsequent yielding of the
exposed subgrade due to inclement weather and poor drainage may require
over excavation of the soft areas and replacement with well compacted fill The need for
over excavation due to soft subgrade soil conditions should be evaluated by a
representative from Geolabs in the field
Imported fill materials if required should consist of non expansive select
granular fill materials such as crushed coral and basalt gravel The material should be
well graded from coarse to fine with no particles greater than 3 inches in largest
dimension The material should have a CBR value of 20 or higher and a swell potential
of less than 1 percent when tested in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1883
It should also contain between 10 and 30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve

Fill and backfill materials required for the project construction should be placed in
level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness moisture conditioned to above the
optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction Compaction requirements for fills and backfills within 2 feet of the finished
pavement grades should be increased to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
Aggregate base course and aggregate subbase required for the project should
consist of crushed basalt aggregate and should meet the requirements of Sections 30 and
31 of the City and County Standard Specifications Aggregate base course and aggregate
subbase materials for the parking pavements should be moisture conditioned to above the

and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Imported fill materials
should be tested by Geolabs for conformance with these recommendations prior to
delivery to the project site for the intended use
Utility Trench
We envision that new electrical duct lines will be required to service the future
lighting system In general granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of clean sand or S4C
is recommended under the electrical duct lines Free draining granular materials such as
clean sand or S4C should also be used for the initial trench backfill up to about 6 inches
above the duct lines to provide adequate support around the pipes and to reduce the
compaction effort of the backfill thus reducing the possibility of damaging the pipes It is
critical to use this free draining material to reduce the potential for formation of voids below
the haunches of pipes and to provide adequate support for the sides of the pipes
Improper backfill material and placement procedures could result in settlement of the
backfill and damage to the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 6 inches above the duct lines
to the top of the subgrade or finished grade may consist of on site soils or imported select
granular fill material The backfill should be placed in maximum 8 inch level loose lifts
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and mechanically
compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for
appreciable future ground subsidence Where trenches are below pavement areas the
upper 2 feet of the trench backfill below the pavement grade should be compacted to a
minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
