SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS 
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored further by drilling and 
sampling three exploratory borings, designated as Boring Nos. 301 through 303, 
extending down to depths of about 5.5 to 7.2 meters below the existing ground surface. 
The approximate boring locations are shown on the Site Plan, Plate 2. 
The subsurface conditions encountered in the supplemental borings generally 
consisted of new fill materials placed by the J-200 project Contractor that extend to 
depths of about 2.9 to 3.4 meters below the existing ground surface at the locations 
explored. The fill materials encountered consisted of dense to very dense silty sands 
and gravel with “SM” and “GM” designations. The new fill materials typically are 
underlain by existing fill materials consisting of stiff to hard sandy clays (fine-grained 
soils) with a “CL” designation, medium dense clayey sands and gravel with “SC” and 
“GC” designations, and loose to very dense silty and sandy gravel with a “GM” 
designation. The soil classification designations are in accordance with ASTM D2487. 
Limestone (rock formation) was encountered below the new and existing fills at depths 
of about 4.6 to 5.0 meters below the existing ground surface, and the limestone 
extended to the maximum depths explored in our borings. 
Groundwater was not encountered at the time of our field exploration in 
September 2017 and is not anticipated to be encountered within the relatively shallow 
excavations for this project. It should be noted that groundwater levels are subject to 
change due to rainfall, time of year, seasonal precipitation, surface water runoff, and 
other factors. 
Detailed descriptions of the materials encountered are presented on the Logs of 
Borings in Appendix A. Laboratory tests were performed on selected samples obtained 
during our field exploration with an emphasis on soil classification, and the test results 
are summarized on the Logs of Borings and also are presented in Appendix B.  
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS 
This supplemental subsurface investigation was conducted in the areas of the 
project site that had not been turned over by the Government prior to our initial 
subsurface investigation in October 2016. In general, these areas are located between 
the new GSE structure and retaining walls on the southern portion of the project site. 
New pavements and underground utilities will be constructed in these areas. The 
following presents our findings and discussions:

1. 
Based on the geotechnical information provided in the RFP documents 
and the plans and specifications for JFY11 Project J-200, we anticipated 
compacted satisfactory materials overlying limestone (rock formation) at 
the project site. Satisfactory materials are defined by the following soil 
designations per ASTM D2487: GW, GP, GM, SW, or SP, free of debris, 
roots, wood, scrap materials, vegetation, refuse, soft unsound particles, 
and deleterious, or objectionable materials. However, the subsurface 
conditions encountered from this supplemental subsurface investigation 
revealed that “unsatisfactory materials” as a large proportion of the new 
fills placed by the Project J-200 Contractor appear to be of the “SM” 
designation, and unsuitable materials such as “CL” and “GC” are present 
in the materials directly underlying the new fills. These subsurface 
conditions are consistent with the subsurface conditions encountered in 
our previous geotechnical investigation conducted in October 2016. 
2. 
We also note that there were some over-sized materials (stones larger 
than 75 mm) observed in the new fill materials. The over-sized materials 
(stones larger than 75 mm) also would be considered “unsatisfactory 
materials” per the Project J-200 specifications (Section 31 23 00.00 20, 
Para. 2.1.2).  
3. 
As indicated above, unsuitable materials in the form of sandy clays with a 
“CL” designation were encountered directly below the new fills placed at 
the P-230 project site. We anticipated that the unsuitable materials to have 
been removed from the P-230 project site in accordance with the following 
statement that “Available subsurface information in Part 6 of this RFP 
indicates the presence of unsuitable silt and silty materials in the existing 
surface soils prior to mass grading. These materials were to be removed 
under the building areas during mass grading.” in the P-230 Project 
Program. The sandy clay soil layer was encountered in all three of the 
supplemental borings (B-301, B-302, and B-303) drilled. We were able to 
obtain a relatively undisturbed soil sample and performed a consolidation 
test on the soil sample and an Atterberg Limits test on the cohesive soil 
sample. 
Based on our analysis, the unsuitable materials are potentially 
compressible under the new fill loads; however, it is likely that the most of 
settlement has already occurred in this area. Therefore, we do not 
recommend an additional settlement monitoring program for the remainder 
of the project site that turned over by the Government in August 2017. 
In summary, this supplemental subsurface investigation did encounter fill 
materials that would be considered “unsatisfactory” materials in the fills placed by the 
Project J-200 Contractor; however, the “unsatisfactory” materials appear to be relatively 
dense and well compacted. For this reason, we are not recommending additional 
treatment of the subgrade to receive the new pavements. We also encountered

Field Exploration 
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored further by drilling and 
sampling three exploratory borings, designated as Boring Nos. 301 through 303, 
extending down to depths of about 5.5 to 7.2 meters below the existing ground surface. 
The borings were drilled with a truck-mounted drill rig equipped with 200-mm hollow-
stem augers. The borings were drilled at the approximate locations shown on the Site 
Plan, Plate 2. 
Our engineer classified the materials encountered in the borings by visual and 
textural examination in the field in general accordance with ASTM D2488, Standard 
Practice for Description and Identification of Soils, and monitored the drilling operations 
on a near-continuous (full-time) basis. These classifications were further reviewed 
visually and by testing in the laboratory. Soils were classified in general accordance with 
ASTM D2487, Standard Practice for Classification of Soils for Engineering Purposes 
(Unified Soil Classification System), as shown on the Soil Log Legend, Plate A-0.1. 
Deviations made to the soil classification in accordance with ASTM D2487 are 
described on the Soil Classification Log Key, Plate A-0.2. Rock materials were 
described in general accordance with the Rock Description System, as shown on the 
Rock Log Legend, Plate A-0.3. The Rock Description System is based on the 
publication “Suggested Methods for the Quantitative Description of Discontinuities in 
Rock Masses” by the International Society for Rock Mechanics (March 1977). Graphic 
representations of the materials encountered are presented on the Logs of Borings, 
Plates A-1 through A-3. 
The majority of the soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general 
accordance with ASTM D1586, Penetration Test and Split-Barrel Sampling of Soils, by 
driving a 50-mm (2-inch) OD standard penetration sampler using a 63.5-kg (140-pound) 
hammer falling 460 mm (30 inches). In addition, some relatively “undisturbed” soil 
samples were obtained in general accordance with ASTM D3550, Ring-Lined Barrel 
Sampling of Soils, by driving a 75-mm (3-inch) OD modified California sampler with the 
same hammer and drop. The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the second and 
third 152-mm (6-inch) of a 460-mm (18-inch) drive are shown as the “Penetration 
Resistance” on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths. The penetration 
resistance shown on the logs of borings indicates the number of blows required for the 
specific sampler type used. The blow counts may need to be factored to obtain the 
Standard Penetration Test (SPT) blow counts.

Laboratory Tests 
Moisture Content (ASTM D2216) and Unit Weight (ASTM D7263) determinations 
were performed on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation 
of soil properties. The test results are presented on the Logs of Borings at the 
appropriate sample depths. 
Three Atterberg Limits tests (ASTM D4318) were performed on selected soil 
samples to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits and to aid in soil classification. The test 
results are summarized on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths, and 
graphic presentation of the test results is provided on Plate B-1. 
Twelve Sieve Analysis tests (ASTM C117 & C136) were performed on selected 
soil samples to evaluate the gradation characteristics of the soils and to aid in soil 
classification. The fines content of the sample tested (percent particles passing the 
No. 200 sieve) is indicated on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths. 
Graphic presentation of the grain size distribution is provided on Plates B-2 through B-4. 
Two fines content tests (ASTM D1140) were performed on selected soil samples 
to evaluate the percent particles passing the No. 200 sieve and to aid in soil 
classification. The fines content of the sample tested is indicated on the Logs of Borings 
at the appropriate sample depths and summarized on Plate B-5. We also included the 
fines content from the 12 sieve analysis tests on the plates for ease of reference. 
One Consolidation test (ASTM D2435) was performed on a selected soil sample 
to evaluate the compressibility characteristics of the encountered material. The 
consolidation test results are presented on Plate B-6. 
Two Modified Proctor compaction tests (ASTM D1557, Method C) were 
performed on bulk soil samples to evaluate the relationship between the moisture 
content and the dry density of the near-surface soils. The test results are presented on 
Plates B-7 and B-8. 
Two laboratory soaked California Bearing Ratio tests (ASTM D1883) were 
performed on bulk soil samples to evaluate the pavement support characteristics of the 
soils. The bulk soil samples were remolded to obtain a relative compaction of 
approximately 100 percent (in accordance with ASTM D1557) at about the optimum 
moisture content. The test results are presented on Plates B-9 and B-10.
