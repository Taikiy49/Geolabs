Please note that the soil samples recovered during our field exploration

report The samples will be discarded after that date unless arrangements are made for
a longer sample storage period Please contact our office for alternative sample storage
requirements if appropriate
Project Considerations
The project is along Front Street in Lahaina on the Island of Maui Hawaii The
boulder face retaining wall fronting the existing Lahaina Roads Condominium is located
along the shoreline The wall footing is submerged below the water level The wall is
approximately 10 feet high and retains approximately 8 feet of backfill material Recently
the wall has been experiencing movement towards the ocean by approximately 1 5 to
2 inches This movement is evident by the crack and slab separation which is located
approximately 5 feet back from the face of the wall and traverses parallel with the wall

Purpose and Scope
The purpose of our geotechnical engineering exploration was to provide boring
data and laboratory soil testing services In order to accomplish this we provided an
exploration program generally consisting of the following tasks and work efforts
1
Mobilization and demobilization of a trailer mounted Diedrich D 25 drill rig
and operators to the site from Honolulu and back
2
Drilling and sampling of three borings to depths ranging between about
20 0 and 22 0 feet below the existing ground surface
3
Coordination of the field exploration and logging of the borings by our
geologist
4
Laboratory testing of selected samples obtained during the field
exploration as an aid in classifying the materials
5
Preparation of this letter report summarizing our work on the project
6
Coordination of our overall work on the project by our project engineer
7
Miscellaneous work efforts such as drafting word processing and clerical
support
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling
three borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 3 to depths of about 20 0 to 22 0 feet
below the existing ground surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2 The borings were drilled using a trailer mounted drill rig equipped with a 4 inch
casing equipment using rotary wash drilling method
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by testing
in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representations of the materials
encountered are provided on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 3
Soil samples were obtained in general accordance with ASTM D 1586
Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 2 inch OD standard

Laboratory Tests
Moisture Content ASTM D 2216 and Unit Weight determinations ASTM D 2937
were performed on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of
soil properties The results of these tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the
appropriate sample depths
Three Sieve Analysis tests ASTM C 117 and C 136 were performed on selected
soil samples to evaluate the gradation characteristics of the soils and to aid in soil
classification Graphic presentations of the grain size distribution of the soils are
provided on Plate B 1
General Subsurface Soil Conditions
Our field exploration generally disclosed that most of the site is underlain by about
6 to 7 feet of loose to medium dense sand with silt and some gravel material probably fill
over medium dense to dense tan white coral sand beach deposit material to depths of
13 to 15 feet Underlying the beach deposit sands is marine deposited material consisting
of dense light gray silty coral gravel with sand extending to a depth in excess of 22 0 feet
below the existing ground surface We encountered groundwater in the borings during our
field exploration at depths ranging from 7 1 to 7 9 feet Mean Sea Level
During our field drilling exploration a large void was encountered below the
concrete slab at Boring No 1 and another smaller void was encountered at Boring No 2
A metal tape was used to measure the approximate size of the voids by placing the tape
within the borehole The void in Boring No 1 was measured to be at least 3 feet in depth
by 5 feet in diameter The smaller void in Boring No 2 was measured to be about 4 to
6 inches in depth and 2 feet in diameter Also a 4 inch subterranean metal pipeline was
observed below the concrete slab approximately 2 inches west of Boring No 2 and
traversed parallel with the wall face line In addition it was observed that when an ocean
surge wave came in contact the wall air would jet out of the Boring Nos 1 and 2 possibly
suggesting that the void continues vertically downward to the waterline As the drilling
operation continued in Boring No 1 the surface concrete slab began to separate from the
adjacent concrete slabs from the control joints The surface displacement settlement was
measured to be approximately ½ inch from the adjacent concrete slabs

Limitations
Variations of conditions between and beyond the field borings may occur and
the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident until construction is
underway
The elevations of the borings were estimated from the topographic plan provided
by the client The locations and elevations of the field borings should be considered
accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used
The stratification breaks shown on graphic representations of the borings depict
the approximate boundaries between soil rock types and as such may denote a
gradual transition Water level data from the borings were measured at the times shown
on the graphic representations and or presented in the text of this report These data
have been reviewed and interpretations made in the formulation of this report However
it must be noted that fluctuation may occur due to variation in seasonal rainfall and
other factors
