PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project involves upgrade improvements to the existing Aliamanu Pump
Stations along Salt Lake Boulevard in the Salt Lake area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii
understand the current pump stations are located at relatively low areas compared to their
neighborhood and are subject to water damage from runoff water during heavy
precipitation Current concepts consist of a waterproof wall surrounding each pump station
buildings It is expected that these pump stations will be demolished in the future and
replaced with a reasonably sized sewer line that will transport sewage from both sites to a
line running through the Navy Marine Golf Course
The overall project location is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The
pump station locations are shown on the Site Plans Plates 2 1 and 2 2

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our field exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling four borings
designated as Boring Nos 1 through 4 extending to depths of approximately 22 5 to
26 feet below the existing ground surface Boring Nos 1 and 2 were drilled at
Pump Station No 1 and Boring Nos 3 and 4 were drilled at Pump Station No 2 The
approximate boring locations are shown on the Site Plans Plates 2 1 and 2 2
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer geologist who monitored the drilling operations on
a near continuous basis Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on the Soil Log Legend Plate 3
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained in general accordance with
ASTM D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 3 inch OD Modified
California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches In addition some
samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 2 inch
OD standard penetration sampler using the same hammer and drop The blow counts
needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an 18 inch drive are
shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate
sample depths
Samples of volcanic tuff materials encountered at the site were obtained using
diamond core drilling techniques in general accordance with ASTM D 2113 Diamond
Core Drilling for Site Investigation Core drilling is a rotary drilling method that uses a
hollow bit to cut into the tuff materials The material left in the hollow core of the bit is
mechanically recovered for examination and description
Based on our field exploration the subsurface condition at the Pump Station No 1
site generally consisted of 3 inches of asphalt underlain by a thin layer of gravel fill
followed by stiff sandy silt to about 2 to 4 feet below the existing grade Volcanic tuff was
encountered below the stiff fill The tuff materials varied from slightly fractured to extremely
fractured The volcanic tuff were generally stiffidense and extended to the bottom of our
borings at this pump station location
At Pump Station No 2 site our field exploration generally encountered
approximately 10 feet of fill under the pavement layer The fill materials consisted of stiff
clayey silt and graded to soft and very soft In addition the soft to very soft fill materials
were nearly saturated Volcanic tuff was encountered below the fill layer and extended to
the maximum depth explored of about 25 feet below the ground surface The tuff materials
varied from slightly to severely fractured and were generally dense

SITE GRADING
At the on set of earthwork areas within the contract grading limits should be
cleared and grubbed thoroughly Vegetation debris deleterious materials existing
structures to be demolished and other unsuitable materials should be removed and
disposed properly off site to reduce the potential for contamination of the excavated
materials
Soft and yielding areas encountered during clearing and grubbing below areas of
future improvements should be over excavated to expose firm natural material and the
resulting excavation should be backfilled with well compacted fill The excavated soft soils
may be used as fill provided that it meets the requirements for fill material
RETAINING STRUCTURES
Current design concepts involve a waterproof wall surrounding each pump station
to keep runoff water away from the buildings We anticipate that the waterproof wall
structures will be relatively shallow
Based on the encountered subsurface conditions we believe that the in situ soils
would provide suitable foundation support for the new wall structures We recommend
using an allowable bearing pressure of up to 2 000 pounds per square foot psf for the
design of shallow foundations bearing on the stiff silty fill This bearing value is for
those caused by wind or seismic forces To increase the path of which water might travel
along the new wall structures we recommend that the wall footings be embedded a
minimum of 30 inches below the existing grade and have a minimum width of 24 inches
Foundations next to utility trenches or easements should be embedded below a
45 degree imaginary plane extending upward from the bottom edge of the utility trench or
as deep as the inverts of the utility lines This requirement is necessary to avoid
surcharging adjacent below grade structures with additional structural loads and to reduce
the potential for appreciable foundation settlement

If foundations are designed and constructed in strict accordance with our

may be on the order of about 0 25 inches or less
The proposed retaining structures should be designed to resist the lateral earth
pressures due to the adjacent soils and surcharge effects In general an active condition
may be used for wall structures that are free to deflect by 0 5 percent of the structure
height If the tops of the wall structures are not free to deflect beyond this degree or are
restrained the wall structures should be designed for the at rest condition These lateral
earth pressures do not include hydrostatic pressures that might be caused by runoff water
trapped behind the structures

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
Geolabs should be retained to provide geotechnical engineering services during
construction of the proposed project The critical items of construction monitoring that
require Special Inspection include observation of excavation and backfill This is to
observe compliance with the design concepts specifications or recommendations and to
expedite suggestions for design changes that may be required in the event that
subsurface conditions differ from those anticipated at the time this letter report was
prepared The recommendations provided herein are contingent upon such observations
If the actual exposed subsurface conditions encountered during construction are different
from those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate design modifications
should be made

become evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be
necessary to re evaluate the recommendations provided herein
