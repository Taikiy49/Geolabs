INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the proposed Driveway Improvements at Mountain
View Elementary School on the island of Hawaii The general location of
the site and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The exploration was performed in general accordance with our proposal
dated
This report summarizes our findings from the exploration and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analyses based
on the data obtained during the exploration These recommendations are
intended for design input and are not intended for construction bidding
estimation purposes

PROJECT CONSTDERATIONS
The project site is located at Mountain View Elementary School in
Mountain View Puna District on the island of Hawaii It is proposed to
repave and widen the existing driveway and to construct a bus loading
shelter at the site
In addition it is proposed to grade and construct a 38 stall parking
area on the school grounds Minimal site grading is anticipated
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface conditions at the project site in order to develop a
generalized soil rock data set for the formulation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertinent to foundation retaining wall and
pavement design of the project
The work was done in general accordance with our proposal to Division
of Public Works Department of Accounting and General Services dated
The scopé of work was limited to the following
1 Drilling and sampling five 5 borings to depths of 6 0 to 16 0
feet below the existing ground surface and obtaining surface bulk
samples
2 Reconnaissance of the project site and its vicinity to make
visual observations of the surficial soil conditions
3 Review of available soils and geologic information pertinent to
the project site and its vicinity
4 Laboratory testing of selected samples obtained during the field
exploration to determine the engineering properties and
classification of the materials encountered

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located at Mountain View Elementary School in
Mountain View Puna District of Hawaii Currently the site is occupied
by a group of classrooms of wooden construction
The area for the proposed bus loading shelter is a narrow strip of
land between the existing building B and driveway The grade drops
about five 5 to six 6 feet from the road shoulder toward the existing
building The A C pavement of the existing one lane driveway is
generally in fair condition
The proposed parking lot is in the southeastern portion of the school
site At the time of the field exploration the majority of the area was
occupied by California grass
The terrain of the project site gently slopes down toward the
northeast with elevations ranging from 1394 to 1409 feet MSL

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration generally encountered medium dense granular
fill material near the surface It is our opinion that the upper medium
dense silty basalt gravel fill may provide adequate foundation support for
the proposed bus loading shelter
Site Grading
Minimal site grading is anticipated
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limits should be cleared of vegetation debris rubbish old pavements
and other deleterious materials These materials should be removed and
disposed of properly off site
The areas should then be scarified to a depth of six 6 inches
moisture conditioned and recompacted to a minimm of 90 of its maximm
dry density per ASIM Test Method D 1557 78 Any loose or soft spots

In the proposed parking area subgrade of ash soil with relatively

Based on the field exploration results most of the on site near

The site should be graded to drain water away from the foundations
Foundations
Spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the proposed
project An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f may be used for
footings bearing on the upper silty basalt gravel fill layer The bearing
value is for total dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third
when temporary wind and seismic loads are considered Estimated
settlements are on the order of less than one half of an inch total and
one fourth of an inch differential
In general a minimm footing embedment of 18 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grade is recommended If foundations are located next
to sewer drainage easements they should be embedded below a one

concrete curing

Retaining Wallis
Retaining walls up to approximately 5 to 6 feet high are planned for
the project General guidelines for design of the retaining walls are as
follows
1 For lateral earth pressures the following values may be used
Walls unrestrained at the top 35 p s f equivalent
active condition level fluid pressure
backfill per foot of depth
Walls restrained at the top 55 p s f equivalent
at rest condition fluid pressure
level backfill per foot of depth
2 An allowable bearing value up to 3000 p s f may be used The
foundations should be embedded a minimm of 18 inches below
the lowest adjacent finish grade
3 For lateral resistance design a passive earth resistance of
300 p s f per foot of depth may be used Unless covered by
slabs or pavements the passive earth resistance of the top 12
inches of soils should be neglected A base friction factor
of 0 35 may also be used to determine the resistance against
sliding of the wall
4 Backfill directly behind the retaining walls should consist of
granular non expansive material such as 3B Fine gravel or
select borrow to minimize excessive compactive effort
required
5 A subsurface drainage system should be utilized behind the
walls to protect against build up of hydrostatic pressure
Subdrains should be properly constructed to drain water away
from the walls

Pavement Sections
Asphaltic concrete pavement may be used for the proposed parking
lot and widening portion of the existing driveway The existing
pavement may be replaced with new sections or repaved resurfaced with
an overlay as described below Based on the laboratory CBR data the
following preliminary pavement sections may be used
New Sections
2 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 Inch Base Course
8 Inch Total Pavement Thickness

Subgrade
CBR and density tests should be performed on the actual subgrade
soils encountered during construction and the above section may
be revised if necessary The recommended section considers
medium traffic and assumes that adequate drainage will be provided
in the paved areas
Overlay
Minimm overlay of inch asphaltic concrete is recommended
over the existing A C pavement
In order to provide good bonding between the old pavement and the
overlay the existing asphaltic concrete pavement should be
properly prepared and be free of debris rubbish or other
unsuitable loose surface materials prior to and during the course
of the above overlaying operation
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comments
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during site grading and
construction of the foundation This is to observe compliance with the
design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow design
changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from that
anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given
in this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field borings Variations of
conditions between the field data points may occur and the nature and
extent of these variations may not become evident until construction If
variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations given in this report
The location of the field borings were approximately determined by
taping from objects indicated on the preliminary site plan dated
provided by the Department of Accounting and General
Services State of Hawaii Elevations of the field borings were
interpreted from the same plan The physical location of the field data

The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the

data points and may not represent actual subsurface conditions

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by
drilling and sampling five 5 test borings utilizing truck
mounted drilling equipment The depths of the borings varied from
6 0 to 16 0 feet below the existing ground surface The
approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2
The soils were classified by visual and textural examination
in the field by our geologist or engineer who continuously
monitored the test pit excavation and drilling operations The
Classifications were made in accordance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A graphic presentation of the soils is
presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 5
Soil samples were obtained by driving a 2 inch standard split
spoon sampler with a 140 pound hammer free falling a distance of
30 inches The blow counts used to drive the sampler the last 12
inches are shown on the Boring Logs at the appropriate sample
depths
Core samples were obtained by an NX core barrel The core
recovery and rock quality designation RQD values are shown on the
Boring Logs at the appropriate sample depths
