The site is underlain by soft lagoonal deposits
therefore pile foundations are recommended for the
Support of the proposed 9 story Unaccompanied Officer
Personnel Housing structure
The pile lengths and supporting characteristics will
vary due to the complex subsurface conditions below the
site Approximately one half of the building can be

recommended

ings An allowable bearing pressure of 2 000 p s f may

building areas

INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our foundation investigation for
the proposed Unaccompanied Officer and Personnel Housing project
Project No P 083 to be located at the southeastern side of the
existing Submarine Base Post Office Building 656 at the Naval Sub
marine Base Pearl Harbor Oahu Hawaii The general site location
is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The foundation investigation was conducted in two increments because
more complex subsurface conditions were disclosed during the course
of our initial subsurface exploration
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is our understanding that the project will consist of a 9 story
housing building an attached 1 story lounge and a separate post
office building A preliminary layout of the structures is shown on
the Boring Location Plan Plate 2 The anticipated 9 story structure
will have column loads ranging from 100 to 120 kips and wall loads on
the order of 80 kips per liner foot is anticipated The foundation
load of the one story lounge and the post office building will range
from 30 to 60 kips per column

Geology
The geologic condition underlying the site are the result of a com
plex history of deposition and erosion during the Pleistocene and
Recent epochs Most of this complex history is the result of gla
Cioeustatic sea level changes during the Pleistocene
During the early Pleistocene following the cessation of activity of
the Koolau Volcano material eroded from the main body of the volcano
was transported by streams to the area of what is now Pearl Harbor
Alluvial material was deposited when those streams reached the ocean
in a deltaic or estuarine environment
Following that sequence of deposition the sea level fell to a lower
level and the streams eroded a channel through their older deposits
Sea level then rose again to the Makai Range stand at 40 to 60 feet
below the existing sea level
Concurrent with that rise in sea level eruption of the Makalapa
member of the Honolulu Volcanic Series mantled the older alluvium and
stream channels with volcanic ash which consolidated to form volcanic
tuff locally known as mudrock Subsequent stream and wave ero
sion dissected that mantle of tuff and formed shallow fingers of em
bayment into the rock
GEOLABS HAWAII
During the Recent epoch as sea level rose to its current stand
these embayments were filled with soft lagoonal deposits Those
deposits were subsequently covered by man made fill placed during the
middle portion of this century for land reclamation projects during
the construction of the new existing Naval Submarine Base
A simplified sketch of such a profile interpreted from our boring
information is presented on Plate 3 Idealized Subsurface Cross
Section to aid in the understanding of the subsurface conditions as
described in the paragraphs below

consist of brown medium stiff sandy and clayey silts and or medium

fills generally extend to about 8 feet below the ground surface
In some areas the bottom of fill varies in depth Underlying the
fills are geologically recent lagoonal deposits consisting of loose
sand and gravels both tuff and coral fragments and soft silt that
extend as deep as 50 feet in the eroded and

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
GENERAL
Because of the soft compressible lagoonal deposits underlying the
site pile foundations are recommended for the foundation support of
the proposed 9 story building Pile lengths will vary significantly
through out the site from 30 to 45 feet on the shallower tuff
formation to about 75 feet in the eroded channel Test piles will
be needed to provide a better estimate of the production pile
lengths although a precise estimate will be very difficult to
attain
GEOLABS HAWAII
The one story lounge and post office buildings may be supported on
shallow foundations More detailed discussions and recommendations
are presented in the following sections
PILE FOUNDATIONS
Relatively complex pile driving conditions are anticipated at the
site Based on the boring data we anticipate the following three
general pile conditions
1 Relatively shallow piles deriving support from end bearing
into the tuff formation can be utilized in the southeastern
portion of the proposed 9 story building approximately 1 2 of
the building Estimated pile lengths are 30 to 45 feet
2 Deep piles deriving support primarily from friction in the
hard alluvium is anticipated for the northern portion of the
building site since it appears that the tuff formation in the
area has been eroded away In order to develop the design
frictional resistance the piles should penetrate a minimum of
25 feet into the hard alluviun Therefore anticipated pile
lengths are about 75 feet
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 1108 00 Page 9
3 There is a transition area in the middle portion of the
building site where the competent tuff formation has been
severly eroded leaving a steep tuff rock Slope face of variable
thickness Piles driven in this area may tend to break off or
be deflected from vertical along this steep rock face There
fore predrilling is recommended to socket the pile in this
area The piles should generally extend to about 30 to 45 foot
depths except where the tuff is very thin and the piles may be
able to penetrate through the formation down into the underlying
hard alluvium
End Bearing Piles
For the relatively short end bearing piles 12 inch square
prestressed precast concrete piles could be used Although a
lighter hammer could be utilized the hammer specified in the
following section can be used The piles should be driven to
the tuff formation at about 30 to 45 feet below the existing
ground surface A tentative driving criteria of 5 blows per
inch is recommended An allowable pile capacity of 55 tons can
be used Pile settlements on the order of less than one half of
an inch is anticipated
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1108 00 Page 10
Friction Piles
For the anticipated hard driving and long lengths required for
the friction piles we recommend the use of 16 5 inch octagonal
prestressed precast concrete piles The piles should be driven
with a hammer capable of delivering an energy of at least 36 000
ft lbs per blow but not in excess of 1bs to limit
overdriving of the piles The piles should be driven a minimum
of 25 feet into the stiff to hard alluvium and to a resistance
of 5 blows per inch for the last 3 inches
For the aforementioned driving criteria an allowable pile
capacity of 30 tons per pile is recommended Pile settlements
on the order of one half of an inch are anticipated Higher
pile capacities can be developed if the piles are driven deeper
and additional settlements are tolerable
Piles should be spaced as far apart as possible but should maintain a
minimum center to center distance of 4 feet It would be desirable
to have a minimum of two piles under any column to avoid serious
eccentricity problems
The pile lengths and driving criteria recommended above may need to
be re evaluated according to the results of the test pile program so
that more accurate pile order lengths may be selected for production
driving

The actual lateral pile capacities will vary according to the actual
installation procedure loading conditions locations and the rela
tionship with other structural elements Therefore no specific
recommendation is presented at this time
In general passive earth pressures can be developed on piles pile
caps and grade beams to resist lateral forces To gain maximum
support from adjacent soils it should be specified that a well
compacted granular backfill should be placed around pile caps and
grade beams The details of backfilling are presented in subsequent
sections of this report
Test Pile Driving and Load Test Program A test pile driving
and pile load test program is recommended to evaluate the
in situ pile settlement behavior under loading actual pile
driving behavior to confirm the supporting capacity to develop
the production pile driving criteria and to determine the
length of piles to be ordered
The test pile driving should consist of driving 10 to 15 percent
of the number of piles required These test piles can be incor
porated later in the actual building foundation The test piles
should be installed with the same hammer and procedure that are
to be used for the production pile installation
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1108 00 Page 12
We recommend that one half of the total test piles be casted to
45 foot length and the remaining half will be 75 feet long
Following the installation of the test piles one of the end
bearing and one of the friction piles should be selected for the
load testing We recommend that the test load be carried to two
2 times the design load to examine the load settlement rela
tionship of the pile The pile test should be performed in
accordance with the ASTM test procedure D 1143 74
Predrilling There may be boulders in the lagoonal deposits
that would be difficult to penetrate through by driving In
addition some pile location may be situated on steep tuff rock
cliffs where piles may tend to break or glance off the steep
rockface Thus predrilling is also recommended in some areas
The locations and depths of predrilling are difficult to esti
mate at this time and should be established from the results of
the test pile program The predrilled holes should be 18 inches
in diameter and extend a minimum of 2 feet into the tuff It is
probable that some of the pile locations may need to be exca
vated later due to large boulders or other obstructions in the
surficial fill

SHALLOW FOUNDATIONS
For the support of the lighter one story lounge and post office
buildings shallow spread footings may be used
For the design of the footings an allowable maximum soil bearing
pressure of 2000 p s f may be used The bottom of footings should
be embedded at least 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished
grade The embedment may be increased in the toilet areas to
accommodate sewer lines Any soft or yielding soils encountered at
the bottom of the footing should be overexcavated at least 2 feet or
to the firm soils whichever is shallower and backfilled with select
borrow material compacted to 95 of maximum dry density per ASTM
D 1557 74 test procedure The bottom of all footing excavation
should be compacted to 95 density inspected and approved by a soils
engineer prior to concrete and steel placements
For the footings design above it is estimated that footing settle
ment may be up to 1 inch and differential settlement of about
one half of an inch over a distance of 25 feet
The spread footing foundation of the lounge structure should be
structurally independent from the pile supported building

SITE GRADING
General Because the site is underlain by soft compressible
materials any new heavy fill would induce large settlements
It is our understanding that site grades will remain as is Any
additional fill placement over the high rise and one story
building sites should be avoided Should any fill is
contemplated subsequent to the issuance of this report the
soils engineer should first be consulted so that he can
re evaluate potential ground settlement and modifiy the above
recommended pile carrying capacities due to subsequent downdrag
effect
It is probable that buried foundations or other structures may
still exist at the site A thorough attempt should be made
before the construction to identify all underground structures
active or inactive Proper action should be taken to relocate
the active drains and plug the inactive ones as deemed neces
sary
Excavations After the demolition of the existing structures
all existing foundations slabs drains and other utilities
should be removed For excavation exceeding 4 foot depth
shoring should be considered The man made fill at the site may
contain some amount of debris and or boulders as evidenced while
drilling Boring 4

All existing trees that are to be removed should be grubbed to a
depth of at least 18 inches below the existing grade All top
soils within the existing planting areas should be stripped and
stockpiled for later planting use
FILL AND BACKFILL
New fills over the proposed building sites are not recommended If
minor fill is required outside the building location it should be
done as soon as possible to induce early settlement and minimize pile
downdrag forces to the adjacent new structures Slab on grade
construction will benefit from such a schedule
Generally it is anticipated that the majority of the on site mater
ial is suitable for fill except that all clayey soils should not be
reused If imported materials are required they should be GW GP
and SM soils with liquid limit not exceeding 35 and plas
ticity index not greater than 12 and as Stipulated under Section
BACKFILLED AND FILL TS D2200P of NAVFAC Guide Specifications
United States Navy Any on site or imported 111 materials should be
approved by a soils engineer before it is used as engineered fill
The backfilling below ground water level should consist of placing
particles to a level 12 inches above the lowered water level after

duct No 3 Fine aggregate will be suitable for this purpose The

A 6 inch layer of well graded material such as crusher run or base

SUBGRADE PREPARATION
After clearing and stripping the on site surface material should be
scarified to at least 6 inches deep and proof rolled with at least 4
passes using a pneumatic tired roller weighing at least 20 tons Any
soft spots disclosed by the proof rolling should be removed and
replaced with compacted select backfill The proofrolling should be
observed by a soils engineer

SLAB ON GRADE
We believe that floor slabs may be supported on grade Generally
the floor slabs should be placed over a capillary break layer con
sisting of at least 4 inches of crushed rock retained on a No 4
sieve The local quarry product No 3 Fine is recommended for this
purpose The crushed rock base should be placed over compacted sub
grade that has appropriate slopes to drain off any accumulated mois
ture
Because of differential settlements between pile supported foundation
and the soft lagoonal deposit we recommend that the slab on grade be
structurally separated from walls and columns and be allowed to move
independently
PAVEMENTS
The pavement section should consist of 2 0 inches of asphaltic
concrete AC for driveway and parking areas over 6 inches of
aggregate base course over a subgrade that has been scarified and
recompacted to a depth of at least 6 inches Again all compaction
should be to a minimum of 95 percent
The construction of pavement sections should meet the requirements
set forth in the applicable section of the NAVFAC Guide Specifica
tions referred to previously

APPENDIX A
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by
drilling 13 borings to depths of 15 4 to 121 5 feet utilizing
truck mounted drilling equipment The approximate locations
of the borings are shown on the enclosed Boring Location Plan
The soils rock were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer or geologist who
continuously monitored the drilling operations The classi
fications were reviewed by visual inspection and testing in
the laboratory All soils were classified in accordance
with the Unified Soil Classification System A graphic
presentation of the soils encountered in the borings is
presented on the Log of Borings Plates A 1 1 to A 13
Rock core samples were obtained by using a rotary wash
NWD4 core barrel The core recovery and rock quality desig
nation RQD are presented on the Logs of Borings
Soil samples were obtained by driving either a standard
penetration split spoon sampler or a 2 4 inch I D split barrel
sampler with a 140 pound hammer free falling a distance of 30
inches
In the hard alluvial clay soils a 2 4 inch Denison
sampler was used to obtain relatively undisturbed samples
Continuous penetration with a 2 inch conical point driven
with the 140 pound hammer was used in some of the borings to
determine the extent of the loose lagoonal deposits

APPENDIX B
Moisture content and unit weight determinations were
performed on selected samples to aid in the classification
and evaluation of the soil properties The test results are
presented on the boring logs at the appropriate sample depths
Two Atterberg Limit tests were performed on samples of
the very stiff alluvial soils to classify the soils and evaluate
its plasticity The test results are presented on Plate B 1l
Two sieve analyses were performed on the underlying
estuarine deposit to evaluate its grain size distribution
The test results are summarized on Plate B l
Five consolidation tests were performed on the deeper
underlying very stiff deposits to evaluate its settlement
characteristics The consolidation curves are presented on
Plates B 2 through B 6
One unconsolidated undrained triaxial test was performed
on a sample of the very stiff alluvium to evaluate its undrained
strength The test result is summarized on Plate B 7
Torvane shear and unconfined compression tests were per
formed on various samples of the subsoils to evaluate its shear
Strengths The test results are summarized on Plate B 8
