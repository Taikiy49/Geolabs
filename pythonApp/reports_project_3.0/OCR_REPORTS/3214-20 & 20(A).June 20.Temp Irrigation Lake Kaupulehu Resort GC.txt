INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering reconnaissance
performed for the proposed Temporary Irrigation Lake near Hole No 5 of the
Kaupulehu Resort Golf Course located to the north of Kailua Kona in the District of
North Kona on the island of Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site
are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings and geotechnical engineering recommenda
tions resulting from our field reconnaissance and engineering analyses The
recommendations presented herein are intended for design input and may not be
appropriate to form the basis for preparation of final construction cost estimates nor for
construction bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed temporary irrigation lake is located near Hole No 5 of the
Kaupulehu Resort Golf Course at Kaupulehu in the District of North Kona on the island
of Hawaii It is proposed to construct a temporary irrigation lake with a storage capacity
on the order of 2 to 2 5 million gallons of water
Based on the information provided to us and our site reconnaissance the
proposed temporary irrigation lake had been partially excavated and graded A large
coalescing mound of basalt boulders are stockpiled in the central portion of the
excavation At the time this report was prepared the maximum elevation of the water
surface for the irrigation lake is set at 86 feet Mean Sea Level MSL A large portion
of the existing slopes of the excavation are presently at a 3H 1V slope ratio To reduce
the amount of grading work for this project a design slope ratio of 3H 1V is desired for
the side slopes of the proposed temporary irrigation lake
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 3214 20 Page 3
Our site reconnaissance was directed at evaluating the existing excavated slopes
and providing additional geotechnical engineering recommendations for the remaining
earthwork to construct the proposed temporary irrigation lake Special attention was
given to observing the existing site conditions
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the existing
conditions at the project site to develop a generalized soil rock profile for the
formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to the additional site
grading work that would be required to complete the proposed temporary irrigation lake

evaluate the surface and surrounding site conditions
2 Analyses of the field data for the formulation of geotechnical engineering
recommendations pertinent to the site grading work required to complete
the proposed project construction

project and presenting our findings and recommendations

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
Hawaii the largest island of the Hawaiian Archipelago covers an area of
approximately 4 000 square miles The island was formed by the activity of five 5
shield volcanoes These are Kohala which is long extinct Mauna Kea which has had
activity during recent geologic times Hualalai which last erupted in 1801 and Mauna
Loa and Kilauea both still active
The project site is located on the northern flank of Hualalai mountain which
makes up the west central portion of the island and consists partly of the prehistoric
basaltic lava flows of the Hualalai Volcanic Series which were most likely deposited
during the late Pleistocene Epoch Stearns and MacDonald 1946
Hawaiian volcanic activity is typified by an abundance of flank eruptions along
rift zones or zones of weakness in the earth s crust The project site is situated in the
northwest rift zone of Hualalai

is not known based on our reconnaissance

observed during our site visit due to the presence of the existing stockpile at the site

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
We understand that the site grading and excavation work have not been
completed for the subject irrigation lake and therefore our evaluation and recommen
dations presented in this report were based on the existing site conditions observed at
the time of our field visit In general some of the existing slopes of the proposed
temporary irrigation lake are relatively gentle and are considered stable with minimal
additional grading However some of the slopes comprised of loosely placed boulders
and are considered potentially unstable Therefore we recommend that these loosely
placed boulders be removed and replaced with compacted fill to rebuild portions of the
slopes for the temporary irrigation lake Detailed discussions and recommendations are
presented in the following sections of this report
Site Grading
In general a 12 inch layer of choker fill material consisting of 6 inch minus
materials is recommended on the slopes and bottom of the proposed irrigation lake to
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3214 20 Page 9
provide a relatively smooth and stable surface for the placement of the impervious liner
Because of the large size of the materials of the subgrade an underlining consisting
of heavy duty geotextile fabric or 4 inches of No 3B Fine gravel will be required
beneath the impervious liner to reduce the potential for puncture of the impervious liner
from sharp edges of the rocky fill material The use of a heavy duty geotextile fabric
is preferred over the gravel cushion as an underlining layer due to the ease of
installation and the increase in puncture resistance of the liner with the geotextile fabric
Based on our site reconnaissance for Zones A through F as described above
we believe that the remaining site grading work required to complete the project
construction will consist mainly of the following
1 Removal of the existing stockpile and surface boulders within the
excavation
2 Grading of the existing slopes to achieve the design slope grade of
3H 1V
3 Placement of the choker layer underlining and impervious liner
Site grading operations should be observed by a representative of Geolabs
Hawaii It is important that a representative from our office observe the site grading to
evaluate whether any undesirable materials are encountered during the excavation and
scarification process and whether the exposed soil rock conditions are similar to those
observed in our reconnaissance and as assumed in this report

Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork the existing stockpile located at the central portion of
the excavation should be removed to expose the underlying excavation bottom
Additional over excavation may be required in localized areas to achieve the
design elevation of the lake floor Large boulders and soft or loose spots
encountered at the excavation bottom should be removed and replaced with
compacted fill material as described in the Fill Materials subsection The
finished excavation bottom should be a relatively smooth surface free of rocky
materials and particles which may puncture the impervious liner to be installed
Loose unstable boulders present along the existing slopes should be removed
to expose the underlying firm and stable material prior to final grading of the
desired embankment slopes The lateral extent of the loose boulder removal is
not known at this time until the site conditions are exposed during construction
In areas to receive fill the subgrade should be scarified to a depth of 12 inches
where possible and compacted to 90 percent relative compaction Otherwise
the subgrade should be proof rolled with a D 9 bulldozer or similar heavy
equipment minimum 4 passes to help detect and collapse near surface voids
Any loose areas or voids disclosed during clearing operations should be
backfilled with compacted engineered fill

Fill Materials
The fill materials to be used as general fill material below the choker fill layer
should consist of well graded granular materials less than 12 inches in largest
dimension to reduce the amount of grading work on the slopes of the temporary
irrigation lake The on site cut material and the existing stockpile if less than 12
inches in maximum dimension may be used as fill materials provided that they
are properly processed to result in a well graded mixture Boulders materials
greater than 12 inches in diameter may be stockpiled and reused as deep rock
fill materials in other phases of the project development
We recommend that the upper 12 inches of fill below the finish subgrade consist
of a choker fill layer The choker fill should consist of well graded granular
material of 6 inches in maximum dimension with sufficient fines to prevent the
occurrence of voids in the compacted mass If the excavated rock material does
not contain sufficient fines to produce a well graded material off site cinders or
non expansive soil may be mixed into the excavated material to increase the
percentage of fines to provide a well graded material for compaction and to
increase the stability of the fills
Due to the limited amount of fill anticipated for the proposed irrigation lake we
recommend that choker fill materials be used for the entire depth of fills to
provide a more stable and well compacted fill slope
Any import or borrow fill material if necessary should be observed and or tested
by Geolabs Hawaii for its suitability prior to being transported to the site
GEOLABS HAWAII

Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
In general fills and backfills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 12
inches in loose thickness moisture conditioned and compacted to a minimum
of 90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry
density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the
same soil established in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91
The faces of the slope and bottom of the temporary irrigation lake should be
proof rolled with a smooth drum roller with a minimum weight of 10 tons for a
minimum number of 4 to 6 passes to provide a relatively smooth and well
compacted surface for placement of the underlining and impervious liner
Conventional compaction testing is generally not practicable in fills composed of
rock boulders and or cobbles Instead a testing program to evaluate the
number of passes of a compactor needed to achieve the desired level of
compaction should be conducted at the start of the grading phase of the project
under the observation of a representative from Geolabs Hawaii On the basis
of this testing program the number of passes may then be used as the field
criterion for adequate compaction
Excavation
The field reconnaissance disclosed that the site is underlain by very closely to
widely fractured volcanic rock ranging from hard to very hard It is anticipated
that the volcanic rock near the existing ground surface may be excavated with
normal heavy excavation equipment such as ripping with large bulldozers
However excavations into the deeper formations may require the use of
hoerams chipping or blasting
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3214 20 Page 13
Choker fill materials or No 3B Fine gravel may be used to backfill irregularities
created during the excavation operations into hard basalt formation to provide
a relatively smooth surface for placement of the underlining and impervious liner
The above discussions regarding the rippability of the surface materials are
based on our visual observation of the existing rock formation during our site
reconnaissance We recommend that the contractor working on this project to
examine the site conditions to make their own interpretation
Fill Slopes
We understand that fill slopes with an inclination of 3H 1V are desired for the
proposed temporary irrigation lake Fills placed on existing slopes having slope
ratios steeper than 5H 1V should be keyed and benched into the firm and stable
materials to enhance the stability of the new embankment fill against sliding
The filling operations should start at the lowest point and continue up in level
horizontal compacted layers in accordance with the above recommendations for
fill placement Fill slopes should be constructed by over filling past the design
limits of the slope and cutting back to the design slope to provide adequate
compaction at the edges of the slope
impervious Liner and Underlining
We understand that a synthetic liner will be provided for the temporary irrigation
lake to reduce water infiltration through the underlying permeable material It is our
opinion that the material type performance installation and protection details of the
impervious liner should be designed in accordance with the recommendations by the
liner manufacturer It is critical that the liner maintain its integrity to prevent saturation
and build up of seepage water pressures within the fill embankment
GEOLABS HAWAIi
W O 3214 20 Page 14
Our experience with similar synthetic liner installations indicates that there is
sometimes a tendency for the liners to become detached at the top of the water
impoundment It appears that this may be the result of inadequate anchoring of the top
of the liner and insufficient flexibility of the liner material It is our recommendation that
the liner manufacturer be consulted regarding proper construction detailing and
installation of the liner with particular attention to proper anchoring to reduce the
potential of liner failure Effects of fluctuating water levels on the performance of the
impervious liner near the top anchoring should also be considered It should be noted
that the excavation for the anchorage trench may encounter difficulties due to the
presence of boulders and rocky materials at the top of the temporary irrigation lake
bank Therefore we envision that it would be difficult to maintain a neat and smooth
trench for installation of the liner anchor
Technical representatives of the manufacturers of the impervious liner should be
required to be on site at all times during their installation to assure that proper
construction procedures and precautions are followed A sales representative of the
supplier s may not have the technical expertise to adequately perform this function
An underlining consisting of a heavy duty geotextile fabric or 4 inches of No 3B
Fine gravel will be required beneath the impervious liner due to the potentially rocky
subgrade materials The use of a geotextile fabric is preferred over the gravel cushion
due to the ease of installation and the increased puncture resistance of the synthetic
liner when combined with a layer of geotextile
Slope Stability
Based on the soil rock conditions encountered in our field reconnaissance and
the proposed design slope inclination of 3H 1V it is our opinion that the proposed
slopes of the temporary irrigation lake should have adequate factors of safety against
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3214 20 Page 15
sliding and rotational failures during seismic activity provided that the potentially
unstable areas are regraded as recommended herein
Utilities
We envision that new utility ines may be required for development of this project
It is anticipated that most of the trenches for utilities will be excavated in hard basalt
rock or rock fill A granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel
ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation is recommended below the pipes Self compacting
granular materials such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation should
be used for the trench backfill up to about 12 inches above the pipes to provide
adequate support around the pipes and to reduce compaction of the backfill thus
reducing the possibility of damaging the pipes The upper portion of the trench backfill
should consist of granular material generally less than 6 inches in maximum size and
compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction
