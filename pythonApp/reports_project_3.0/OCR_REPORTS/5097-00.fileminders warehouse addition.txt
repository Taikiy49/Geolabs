SECTION 1 0 GENERAL
1 1 Introduction
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the FileMinders Warehouse Addition project located in the Kapolei area on
the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown
on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings from our field exploration and laboratory testing
and presents our geotechnical recommendations derived from our analyses for the
FileMinders Warehouse Addition project These recommendations are intended for the
design of building foundations site preparation and cavity probing and grouting only The
findings and recommendations presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at the
end of this report
1 2 Project Considerations
The project site is located at Street 32 56 in the Kapolei
area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii We understand that a new warehouse building is
planned at the project site The new warehouse building will be approximately
36 000 square feet with metal framing concrete tilt up walls and continuous reinforced
concrete footings Structural details were not available at the time this report was
prepared
In general we anticipate that construction of the proposed warehouse building
structure will involve only minor site preparation
1 3 Purpose and Scope

subsurface conditions at the project site The subsurface information obtained was used
to develop a soil rock data set for the formulation of geotechnical recommendations
pertaining to the design of foundations and site preparation for the proposed warehouse
building project In order to accomplish this we conducted an exploration program
consisting of the following tasks and efforts

2 1 Regional Geology
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lava from
two shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to
be middle to late Pliocene in age and Koolau is estimated to be late Pliocene to early
Pleistocene in age As the volcanic activity in the Waianae Volcano ceased lava flows
from the Koolau Volcano banked against its eroded eastern slope forming a broad plateau
now known as the Schofield Plateau The project site is located on the southwestern flank
of the Waianae Volcano
Physical and chemical weathering followed by erosion of Schofield Plateau
generated sediments which were transported to the coast In the vicinity of the project
site and to the south these sediments accumulated and are interbedded with marine
sediments and coral algal reef formations to form a sedimentary wedge The thickness of
the sedimentary wedge ranges from zero in the area of the Interstate Route H 1 Highway
to over 1 000 feet at Ewa Beach This wedge forms the Ewa Plain and serves as the
confining formation or caprock over the artesian basal aquifers of Southern Oahu
Deposition of sediments has continued from earlier geologic time through the present
During the Pleistocene Epoch sea levels fluctuated in response to the cycles of
continental glaciation As the glaciers grew and advanced less water was available to
fill the oceanic basins such that sea levels fell below the present stands of the sea
When the glaciers melted and receded an excess of water became available such that
the sea levels rose to above the present sea level
The higher sea level stands caused the formation of deltas and fans of
accumulated terrigenous sediments in the heads of old bays accumulated reef deposits at
correspondingly higher elevations and deposited lagoonal marine sediments in the quiet
waters protected by fringing reefs The lower sea stands caused streams to carve valleys
in the sediments and reef deposits Subaerial exposure of the sediments and calcareous
materials caused consolidation of the lagoonal deposits and induration of the calcareous

2 2 Site Description

2 3 Subsurface Conditions
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by drilling and sampling
four borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 4 extending to depths of about 21 3 to
21 5 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate locations of the borings
drilled for the project are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2

voids and cavities should be anticipated at the project site

fluctuate depending on factors such as seasonal rainfall storm surge conditions and other
factors Detailed descriptions of the materials encountered during our field exploration are

SECTION 3 0 DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
As mentioned previously our borings generally encountered a thin layer of surface
fill materials overlying coral formation extending to the maximum depth explored of about
21 5 feet below the existing ground surface The surface fills generally consisted of base
course and very stiff clayey silt with sand and gravel extending down to about 0 7 to
1 5 feet below the ground surface The coral formation encountered was generally soft to
very hard and ranged from slightly to highly weathered Groundwater was encountered at
about 17 5 to 18 feet below the ground surface in the borings drilled at the time of our
field exploration
Based on our field exploration and laboratory tests we believe that a shallow
foundation system consisting of spread and or continuous footings bearing on the
recompacted in situ soils or coral formation may be used to support the new warehouse
building structures To reduce the potential for loss of foundation support resulting from
collapse of cavities below a footing we recommend that a program of cavity probing and
grouting be implemented at the foundation locations of the piers and abutments during
construction Detailed discussion and recommendations for design of foundations
slabs on grade site grading and other geotechnical aspects of the project are presented
in the following sections of this report
3 1 Building Foundations
Based on the generally competent subsoil conditions encountered at the project
site we recommend that a shallow foundation system consisting of spread and or
continuous footings be used for support of the new warehouse building An allowable
bearing pressure of up to 4 000 pounds per square foot psf may be used for the design
of foundations bearing on the recompacted in situ soils and or compacted fills This
bearing value may be used to size the footings based on dead plus live loads and may be
increased by one third 1 3 for transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic
forces The bottom of footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the
lowest adjacent ground level

The bottom of footing subgrades should be recompacted to at least 90 percent
relative compaction to provide a relatively firm and smooth bearing surface prior to the
placement of reinforcing steel or concrete If soft and or loose materials are encountered
at the bottom of the footing excavation the soft or and loose materials should be
over excavated to expose the firm and unyielding materials and the excavation should be
backfilled with fill materials compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction Relative
compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the
maximum dry density of the same soil determined in accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 1557
If the foundations are designed and constructed in accordance with the
recommendations presented herein total footing settlements under the anticipated design
loads are estimated to be on the order of less than 1 inch Differential settlements of
adjacent footings supported on similar materials may be approximately 0 5 inches
We anticipate that the warehouse building may be subjected to uplift forces created
by wind conditions Uplift loads may be resisted by a combination of dead weight of the
structure and slab and weight of backfill material above the footing where applicable
Contribution of dead weight from the on site coralline materials may be estimated using a
unit weight of 110 pounds per cubic foot pcf
Lateral loads acting on the structures may be resisted by frictional resistance
developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil and by passive earth
pressure developed against the near vertical faces of the foundation system A coefficient
of friction of 0 4 may be used for footings bearing on the in situ hard coralline soils
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid
pressure of 350 pounds per square foot per foot of depth pcf This assumes that the
soils around the footings are well compacted Unless covered by pavements or slabs the
passive pressure resistance in the upper 12 inches of soil should be neglected In
addition the passive pressure resistance should be further reduced for foundations
located on slopes

3 2 Foundation Probing
Based on our field exploration voids were encountered in our borings drilled at the
project site In addition our experience suggests that cavities and or voids are commonly
encountered in the coralline deposits underlying the project site To reduce the potential
for loss of foundation support resulting from collapse of the cavities and or sinkholes
below foundations we recommend that a program of cavity probing and grouting at the
foundation locations be implemented during construction
For estimating purposes we recommend that probe holes be drilled at each
spread footing location one probe hole for every 50 square feet of footing area and at
10 feet on center along wall foundations The probe holes should be at least 3 inches in
diameter and should extend to at least 10 feet below the planned bottom of foundation
elevation If cavities and or voids are encountered or suspected during the probing
operation additional probe holes should be drilled at closer spacing to aid in delineating
the vertical and lateral extent of the cavity and or void The probe holes and cavities
discovered should be backfilled with sand cement grout injected at low to moderate
pressures We recommend that a low strength sand cement grout with a slump range of
6 to 9 inches be utilized for the grouting operations In lieu of the sand cement grout fluid
lean concrete such as controlled low strength material flowable fill may also be used for
the grouting operation
Because of the potential for encountering cavities and or voids at the site we
recommend that unit prices for additional probing and grouting be obtained during
bidding In addition the probe drill should be available on site until the probing and
grouting operation is complete The contractor should also be made aware that a longer
lag time between probing grouting operations and foundation construction might be
required in the construction schedule

3 3 Slabs On Grade
We anticipate that concrete slabs on grade will be required for the construction of
the new buildings Based on the anticipated subsurface conditions we recommend that
6 inches of non expansive select granular fill material be provided below the slab cushion
for more uniform support
In general we recommend that a minimum 4 inch thick layer of cushion fill be
placed below the slabs to provide the slabs with more uniform support The cushion fill
should consist of open graded gravel such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67
gradation which would also serve as a capillary break To reduce the potential for future
moisture infiltration and subsequent damage to floor coverings an impervious moisture
barrier is recommended on top of the cushion layer Two inches of moist sand may be
considered above the moisture barrier to provide for protection of the moisture barrier and
to aid in curing of the slab concrete Provision of the 2 inches of moist sand above the
moisture barrier will be left up to the discretion of the project structural engineer
Where the concrete slabs on grade will be subjected to future vehicular traffic or
machinery vibrations such as the loading zone or equipment pads a 6 inch layer of
aggregate subbase is recommended in lieu of the 4 inch thick gravel cushion layer In
addition the 2 inch sand and moisture barrier may also be omitted for these slabs The
aggregate subbase should consist of crushed basalt aggregate compacted to a minimum
of 95 percent relative compaction We recommend that the concrete slabs be designed
by a structural engineer for the intended loads and should be reinforced as a structural
slab to accommodate some amount of differential movement
For design of structural slabs a modulus of subgrade reaction of about 250 pounds
per square inch per inch of deflection pci may be used for the compacted aggregate
subbase Where slabs are intended to function as rigid pavements a minimum slab
thickness of 6 inches may be used for preliminary design purposes Provisions should be
made for proper load transfer across the slab joints which are subjected to vehicular traffic

3 5 Drainage
The finished grades outside the structure should be sloped to shed water away
from foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also advised that gutter
systems be installed around the building and that the discharge be diverted away from the
foundation areas Excessive landscape watering near the foundations and slabs should
also be avoided Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the subsoils
The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled against the walls or slab
footings immediately after setting of the concrete to reduce water infiltration Drainage
swales should be provided as soon as possible and should be maintained to drain surface
run off away from the slabs and footing foundations
