PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The planned Kukui ula Resort Community development encompasses about
1 045 acres in Koloa on the south shore of Kauai and includes hotel vacation
ownership commercial park golf course and residential uses The project is part of the
planned improvements for the Kukui ula Resort Community The project location and
general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
Subdivision Y is in the east central portion of the development west of Ala Kalani
Kaumaka Western Bypass Road The parcel will be subdivided into 88 lots of varying
sizes Approximately of roadway will be built Site development will also include
installation of utilities Residential house construction will be done under separate
contracts
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services for
the site grading operations for the project

fills up to 6 feet high Prior to filling the existing ground surface was cleared and grubbed

Generally rock formation was encountered at shallow depths below the natural
ground surface in Parcel Y In order to facilitate later house pool and utility construction
the rock formation was drilled and shot if it was encountered within the limits of the
proposed subgrade for these improvements The blasted rock was then removed to a
depth of approximately 2 3 feet above the bottom of the shot The remaining blasted rock

layer of 6 inch minus material Borrow material from Subdivision M2 M3 was used to fill

obtained and transported to our office for testing The maximum dry densities and
optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general accordance with

compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as determined in

HOUSE CONSTRUCTION CONSIDERATIONS
The finished grades adjacent to the house structures should be sloped to shed
water away from the foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also
advised to install gutter systems around the house structures and to divert the discharge
away from the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering within
3 feet of the foundations and slabs should be avoided Gravel strips adjacent to slabs
and foundations should be avoided Planters next to foundations should be avoided or
have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
house foundations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation and
subsequent loss of strength of the near surface soils The foundation excavations
should be properly backfilled against the footings immediately after setting of the
concrete to reduce water infiltration Drainage swales should be provided as soon as
possible and should be maintained to drain surface run off away from foundations
Homeowners should also be advised not to block or alter surface drainage
Professional consultation including a licensed geotechnical engineer should be
obtained in the planning design and construction of houses and improvements
