In accordance with our proposal we have
drilled additional probings at the above referenced site
to verify the presence of the dense coral layer beneath
the foundation of the proposed apartment and parking
structures The purpose of this investigation was to
provide additional field exploration in portions of the
site inaccessible during the original soil and foundation
investigation This letter summarizes our findings and
recommendations
During July and August 1979 the subsurface soil conditions
at the subject site were explored by drilling 19 test probings
utilizing truck mounted rotary drilling equipment The appro
ximate locations and depths of the probings are shown on the
Site Plan Plate l
W O 826 10 P 2
The drilling and probing operation was continuously monitored
by an engineering geologist who classified the subsoils by
visual and textural examination of the coralline formations
encountered In addition standard penetration tests SPT
were taken at various depths in selected borings The SPT
blow counts are shown on the attached Boring Logs Plates
A 1 through A 19 at the appropriate sample depth
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed similar soil conditions to
those described in our above referenced initial soil and
foundation investigation The additional probings confirmed
the existence of the dense coral formation at shallow depths
No cavities or soft pockets were encountered during this
investigation Based on the field exploration the previously
recommended mat foundation for the tower structure and spread
footing foundation for the parking structure can be used
The text of the initial soil report should be referred to
for detailed recommendations and special design considerations
It is important that the foundation supporting coral formation
should not be loosened by equipment operation and inadequate
dewatering system All loose mud has to be completely removed
prior to concrete placement The bottom of all foundation
excavation should be maintained dry by proper dewatering system
atare an
W O 826 10 P 3
The probe holes should be grouted under low pressure prior
to dewatering to attempt to fill any undetected cavities
and reduce the possibility of additional water entering the
excavation during dewatering
INVESTIGATION LIMITATIONS
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommen
dations made in accordance with generally accepted soil
and foundation engineering principles and practices This
warranty is in lieu of all other warranties either expressed
or implied
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report
are based on our site reconnaissance and soil information
from boring data obtained during this soil investigation
Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encountered and
cannot be fully determined by taking soil borings These
unforeseen conditions such as soft spots cavities and
expansive soil pockets may occur in localized areas
Additional expenditures may be needed during construction
to attain a properly constructed project Some contingency
fund is thus recommended to accommodate these possible costs
