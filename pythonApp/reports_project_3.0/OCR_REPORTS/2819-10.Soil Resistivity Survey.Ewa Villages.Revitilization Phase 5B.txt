PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our survey was to obtain an overview of the electrical resistivity of
the subsurface soils at the site Our work on this project was performed in general
conformance with ASTM Test Designation G 57 78 and with the requirements provided
in the Water System External Corrosion Control Standards Volume 3 dated 1991 The
scope of our work consisted of the following work efforts
1 Mobilization Demobilization of equipment and engineers to the project site
2 Performance of 281 in situ soil resistivity tests at 92 locations These
locations generally correspond to the 300 foot spacing maximum required
in the Water System External Corrosion Control Standards Volume 3
dated 1991
3 Reduction and analyses of the data pertinent to the corrosion characteristics
of the soils at the site
4 Preparation of six copies of this brief report summarizing our work on the
project and presenting our findings

FIELD EXPLORATION
Geophysical survey equipment i e resistance meter was mobilized to the site on
April 11th and our field work was performed in sunny conditions with generally dry
surface conditions
We conducted 281 soil resistivity tests at 92 locations at the above referenced site
using the Wenner 4 Pin Method and a resistance meter Strata Scout R 40C
manufactured by SoilTest Inc with 120 volts and 24 watts output into the ground This
method utilizes four surface probes or pins electrodes to evaluate the average resistivity
an indication of corrosivity of the underlying soil mass The average resistivities of the
soil mass extending from the existing ground surface to the planned invert elevations of
the water lines were assessed by using this method at the test locations listed on Plates
3 1 through 3 23 The approximate test locations are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 for
reference
The water line alignment will be located closer to one side of the roadways In
general soil resistivity surveys were conducted at selected locations along the shoulders
of the roadways closer to the water line alignment The average soil resistance was
evaluated at each test location with the electrodes aligned at equal spacings of generally
2 to 7 feet at the existing ground surface along the shoulders of the roadways Among

The resistance meter R 40C impresses a voltage between the outer electrodes
causing an electrical current to flow between them The voltage drop between the inner
electrodes is then measured by a sensitive built in voltmeter As a result the soil
resistance in Ohms can be found directly on the dial The soil resistivity in Ohm cm
which is directly related to both resistance and spacing can then be computed The
evaluated soil resistances the computed resistivities and the corresponding corrosion
ratings are presented on Plates 3 1 through 3 23
Resistivity is generally recognized as one of the most significant soil characteristics
with regard to the corrosivity of the soil In general the lower the resistivity the greater
the potential for corrosion of the buried metallic structure Conversely the higher the
resistivity the less likely the soil will contribute to corrosion
