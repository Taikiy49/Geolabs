INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our soil investigation performed
for the proposed repair of bulkhead between Pier 9 and Wharf 10 The
general location of the project site and its vicinity is shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is our understanding that wave and tidal action have eroded the
shoreline slope beyond the back of the existing bulkhead between Pier S9
and Wharf S10 leaving the backfill material unprotected
The erosion has begun to affect a main roadway at the submarine base
Pearl Harbor waterfront area and has the potential to adversely affect
access to the nearby piers as well as the underground utilities leading
to those piers The roadway between Pier S9 and Wharf S10 has sustained
some ground surface subsidence possibly as a result of loss of backfill
material Further erosion may result in the formation of underground
voids which could initiate large settlement and create safety hazards for
the vehicular traffic using the roadway
GEOLABS HAWAII
This project proposes to install steel sheet piling with a concrete
cap in the back of the bulkhead to retain the backfill Steel tie rods
and concrete deadman anchors will also be installed to provide lateral
Support to the bulkhead
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our investigation was to evaluate the subsurface
conditions at the site to provide pertinent soil data such as shear
strength and classification for the design of the proposed sheetpile
bulkhead
The scope of our investigation which was performed in general
accordance with our proposal of August 9th included field
reconnaissance drilling and sampling of five 5 borings laboratory
soil testing engineering analysis utilizing the field and laboratory
data and report preparation
The boring logs and laboratory test results are presented in the
appendices of the report
SITE CONDITION
The project site is situated on the southern side of Magazine Loch
in the Pearl Harbor Submarine Base The subject bulkhead extends between
Pier S9 and Wharf S10 a total distance of about 175 feet The existing
structure consists of a concrete relieving type wharf
The area behind the structure is relatively flat and paved with
asphaltic concrete and is presently used as a roadway and parking lot
The general layout of the site is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
A section through the relieving platform bulkhead is shown on Plate 3

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The geologic conditions underlying the site are the result of a
complex series of deposition and erosional events brought on by
glacioeustatic sea level changes and post erosional volcanic activities
during the Pleistocene Following the cessation of activity of the
Koolau Volcano alluvial materials were deposited in a deltaic or
estuarine environment in the area of Pearl Harbor
The sea level fluctuated several times receding perhaps 300 feet
below and rising about 95 feet above the present sea level
Concurrent with a rise in sea level during late Pleistocene era
eruption of the Salt Lake Makalapa member of the Honolulu Volcanic Series
mantled the older alluvium with thick deposits of volcanic ash which
consolidated to form volcanic tuff locally called mudrock layers
Subsequent erosion dissected and thinned those mantles of tuff
During the Recent epoch as sea level rose to its current stand the
area was covered with soft and loose lagoonal marine deposits Those
deposits were subsequently covered by man made fill placed during the
middle portion of this century for land reclamation projects during the
construction of the new existing Naval Submarine Base
Subsurface Condition
The subsoil conditions in the new sheetpile bulkhead area were
explored by drilling five 5 borings to depths varying from 21 6 to 50
feet below the existing ground surface
The new sheetpile wall will be located just behind the end of the
existing bottom slab of the wharf In general the subsurface conditions
along the sheetpile bulkhead area consist of a layer of sandy to gravelly
fill material 7 to 8 feet thick underlain by loose lagoonal deposits

consisting of silty coralline sand and gravel approximately 8 to 10 feet
thick Below the lagoonal sand and gravel deposits Borings 3 and 4
detected a thin layer of stiff to very stiff alluvial silt and clay
deposits Underlying the alluvium is a layer of weak coral which extends
to about elevation 65 feet where very stiff silty clay was encountered
Our drilling was met with refusal at 58 feet where the hard volcanic
tuff was found
Borings 1 and 2 were drilled in the potential deadman anchor area
In Boring 1 an 8 foot layer of dense highly weathered tuff was
encountered immediately below the surface pavement Underneath the tuff
lies an 8 foot layer of alluvium which in turn is underlain by weak coral
to the full depth of the boring In Boring 2 the subsoil consisted of
about 8 feet of fill overlying about 10 feet of alluvium Weak coral was
then encountered to the full depth of the boring
A generalized cross section of soil conditions along the sheetpile
deadman line as interpreted from Borings 1 and 3 is presented on Plate 3

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The overall subsoil condition at the site was found to be competent
for the proposed construction of the sheetpile wall There are several
buried utility lines in the area behind the proposed bulkhead which will
affect the vertical location and construction of the tieback anchor
system

Sheetpile Design Pressure
For the design of the bulkhead sheetpiles at the proposed site we
recommend that the pressure diagram presented on Plate 4 be used These
pressures include a uniform load of 440 p s f due to the 4 feet of fill
material above the top of the sheetpile bulkhead No traffic surcharge
is considered because we believe that its effect at 4 foot depth or more
below ground will be negligible The design pressures are developed
based the Boring 3 soil conditions where thicker loose sand and gravel
deposits were encountered
We believe that further erosion of the lagoonal deposits in front of
the new bulkhead sheetpiles is very likely to occur in the future
Therefore due to the future loss of soil support from erosion and the
effect of the sloping shore profile we have applied an appropriate
reduction in the design passive soil pressure Also as the existing
bulkhead wall appears to offer little lateral support we believe its
contribution should be ignored in the design pressure
For tieback force design we recommend that the free earth support
condition be considered The free earth method assumes that the piling
is rigid and may rotate at the anchor rod level with failure occurring
by a rotation about the anchor rod Passive pressure develops in the
soil in front of the piling and active pressure behind After the
theorectical embedment is computed the embedment value may be increased
by 20 to 40 percent to provide a factor of safety in the design
Sheetpile Bulkhead Construction
Based on our findings from the borings we believe that the new
sheetpiles could be driven through the alluvial soils and weak coral
under the lagoonal deposit However difficulties may be encountered
when sheetpiles are to be driven into the volcanic tuff at elevation 58
feet

Preliminary evaluation indicates that the sheetpiles should
penetrate to a minimum depth of 30 feet below the top of the sheetpile
wall or to about elevation 74 feet
Deadman Anchor Tieback System
For deadman anchor design our recommended design pressure diagram
is shown on Plate 5 We believe that the face of the deadman should be
at least 35 feet away from the sheetpile Generally this horizontal
distance will place the passive earth pressure wedge of the deadman
anchor outside the active wedge zone of the sheetpile
We recommend that the top of the deadman be embedded at least 2 feet
below the street grade Some provisions should be made to limit any
future trenching operation in front of the deadman anchor to avoid loss
of passive resisting earth pressures
t is anticipated that the bottom of the deadman anchor excavation
will extend below the prevailing groundwater level and that dewatering
may be necessary In addition any soft or loose soil encountered at the
bottom of the excavations should be removed to a minimum depth of 18
inches and the over excavation backfilled with compacted crushed rock
such as No 3B Fine gravel
Backfill
Select granular material should be used for backfill behind the
sheetpiles below the pavement and around the deadman anchor Al11 fills
should be compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density
established by ASTM D 1557 78
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed bulkhead repair should be
forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written comments prior to
construction to see that the intent of our recommendations is included

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that all sheetpile and deadman anchor installation
be observed by the soils engineer during construction to see that
adequate penetration and preparation have been made and that the
subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of our
investigation
If conditions encountered during construction are different from
those presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made
INVESTIGATION LIMITATIONS
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations
made in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation
engineering principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all
other warranties either expressed or implied
The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are based
on our site reconnaissance and soil information from boring and
laboratory test data obtained for this soil investigation
Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encountered and cannot be
fully determined by taking soil samples Unforeseen conditions such as
soft spots may occur in localized areas and may require additional
probing or corrections in the field Therefore additional expenditures
may be needed during construction to attain a properly constructed
project Some contingency fund is thus recommended to accommodate these
possible costs
