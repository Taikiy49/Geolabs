Proposed Slope Lining
Halawa Stadium Mall
It is our understanding that a slope lining is planned for the cut
slope exposed behind Retail Building B at the above referenced site
Presently the bottom 15 to 20 feet of the slope consists of
overlain by tuff mudrock formation
Originally a concrete retaining wall was planned in this area to
retain the alluvium however due to economic considerations it is now
desired to utilize a slope lining instead
Due to the granular nature of the alluvium it is not practical to
accurately determine the shear strength parameters of the deposit by
laboratory or field test methods Based on visual observations the
alluvium appears dense and may have some amount of cementation and
therefore may be stabilized with a grouted riprap slope lining
Based on our observations we recommend that the slope lining be
placed on a maximum slope ratio of 1 1 H V One portion of the existing
slope is presently steeper than 1 1 In this area the toe of slope
should be built up with CRM up to a height where the 1 1 slope can be
achieved
Weepholes should be installed at approximately 5 feet on centers
