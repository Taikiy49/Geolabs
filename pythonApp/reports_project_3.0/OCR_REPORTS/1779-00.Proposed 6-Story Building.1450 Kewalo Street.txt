GENERAL SITE DESCRITPION
The site of the proposed development is located in Makiki between
Liholiho and Kewalo Streets Presently one and two story wooden
dewellings occupy the site Tall mango monkey pod and other flower
trees landscape the remaining area around the dwellings
Underground utilities are believed to exist throughout the site
SUBSURFACE SOIL CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions were explored by reviewing information
obtained from previous borings and recent hand augering and sampling of
four 4 probings to depths of 4 0 to 5 0 feet below the existing ground
surface
In general the probings and borings disclosed that the surface
material consists of moderately expansive clayey materials ranging in
depths from 1 5 feet in Probing No 1 to 6 0 feet in Boring No 2
Underlying the expansive surface materials brown clayey silt with
sand was encountered extending to 22 5 feet in Boring No 1 to 30 0
feet in Boring No 2
Boring Nos 1 and 3 penetrated into the dense volcanic cinder
deposit at depths of 22 5 and 28 0 feet respectively and extended down
to the depths of borings drilled
No groundwater was encountered in our drilled borings or probings
during our exploration However a few moist zones were noted during
sampling of the subsurface materials

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The previous and current field explorations encountered competent
bearing soils at the site Shallow foundations may be used for the
proposed 6 story building
Foundations
For the proposed structure spread or continuous footing foundations
are recommended The footings should extend a minimum depth of 4 feet
below the existing ground surface to by pass most of the upper moderately
expansive soil and to develop the high allowable bearing capacity
An allowable bearing value of 4500 p s f can be used for footings
bearing on the very stiff in situ soil The above bearing value may be
increased by one third when considering temporary wind and seismic loads
For the above recommendations estimated foundation settlements are
on the order of one half of an inch total and one quarter of an inch
differential
To resist lateral loads a coefficient of friction of 0 4 can be
used between the foundations and very stiff in situ soil Additionally
a passive soil resistance of 300 p c f can be used
We recommend that the footing excavations be observed by the soils
engineer to confirm the conditions upon which our recommendations are
based Any loose areas encountered should be removed and replaced with
compacted fill or concrete
Site Grading
Minimal site grading is planned for the development

Any vegetation organic or other deleterious materials should be
removed from unpaved areas The existing concrete slabs and asphalt
concrete pavement will need to be removed to accommodate the new
pavements
If required imported fill material should consist of select
non expansive granular material less than 3 inches in size with less than
20 passing the 200 sieve The minimum CBR value of the material should
be 15 when compacted to 95 of the ASTM D density
The near surface soils at the site are moderately expansive and
should not be used for fill except in landscape areas
Pavement
Asphalt concrete pavement is planned for the new parking area
Based on our laboratory CBR tests on the near surface soils we
recommend the use of the following pavement section
2 0 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 0 Inch Base Course 95 Compaction
12 0 Inch Select Borrow
20 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
The above pavement section assumes light traffic conditions and a
compacted subgrade shaped to drain The subgrade soils should be
maintained in a moist condition prior to paving to reduce the swelling
potential
Additional CBR tests should be performed on the actual subgrade soil
during construction to verify the adequacy of the above section

Design Review
Plans and specifications for site grading foundation retaining
wall and pavement design should be forwarded to the soils engineer for
review and written comments prior to construction to see that the intent
of our recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the site grading and footing excavation be
observed by the soils engineer during construction to see that adequate
compaction has been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are
consistent with the findings of our exploration
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site for use
If conditions encountered are different from those presented in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
