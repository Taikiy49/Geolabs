performed for the City Express project at Hikimoe Street located in the Waipahu area on

on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering

foundations and site preparation only The findings and recommendations presented
herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report

was prepared We anticipate that minor site grading work will be required to achieve the
proposed finished grades

2 1 Regional Geology
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lavas from
two shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to
be of middle to late Pliocene in age and Koolau is estimated to be of later Pliocene to
early Pleistocene in age After a long period of volcanic inactivity during which erosion
incised deep valleys into the Koolau Shield volcanic activity returned with a series of lava
flows followed by cinder and tuff cone formations These series are referred to as the
Honolulu Volcanic Series
The project site is located along the southern flank of the Koolau Mountain Range
The project site is located in an area dominated by recent stream and lagoonal deposits
overlying formations of volcanic origin During the Pleistocene Epoch Ice Age sea levels
fluctuated and reached as much as 300 feet below the present sea level
Stream transported sediments consisting mainly of boulders cobbles and gravel were
deposited at a lower sea level As the sea level began to rise the capacity of the streams
was reduced and finer sands and silts were subsequently deposited At some point the
accumulation of sediments could not keep up with the rising sea level and the area
became flooded As a result very fine grained sediments were then deposited in marine
estuarine environments Eventually the sea level stabilized while the sedimentation
process of fine grained sediments and shell beds continued Sediments with shell beds
are characteristic of near shore deposits Subsequently organic sediments which are
peaty and contain plant fibers and fragments accumulated and are indicative of marshy
conditions Land development within the last 100 years has brought the site to its present
form

To reduce the potential for infiltration of water into the pavement structural section
the pavement surface and the subgrade should be sloped and drainage gradients
maintained to carry surface water off the pavement to appropriate drainage structures
This is critical to the long term performance of the pavement structure considering the
nature of the on site soils Surface water ponding should not be allowed on the site during
or after construction Ponding of water adjacent to the edge of the pavement structural
section will allow infiltration and accumulation of subsurface water which will likely soften
the clayey subgrade soils and cause premature failure of the pavement structure
Extensive landscaping should be avoided adjacent to the pavement areas otherwise we
recommend that a subdrain system be constructed to collect the excess water from
landscaping irrigation and to reduce the potential for migration of landscape water into the
pavement section The recommended pavement section assumes that good drainage will
be provided for the paved areas
3 2 Building Foundations
Based on our field exploration the boring drilled near the proposed restroom
structure encountered medium dense and medium stiff fill materials extending to a depth
of about 8 feet below the existing ground surface The fill materials were underlain by very
soft marsh and lagoonal deposits Based on the light structural anticipated for the
restroom structure we recommend that a slab on grade foundation with thickened edge
footings be used to support the new restroom structure An allowable bearing pressure of
up to 2 000 pounds per square foot psf may be used for the design of the thickened edge
footings bearing on the recompacted fills This bearing value is for dead plus live loads
and may be increased by one third 1 3 for transient loads such as those caused by wind
or seismic forces
In general footings should have a minimum base width of 12 inches In addition
we recommend that the perimeter thickened edge footings of the slab on grade be
embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade to bear on
recompacted fills The footing subgrade should be recompacted to a minimum of
90 percent relative compaction to obtain a firm and unyielding subgrade If soft and or
loose materials are encountered at the bottom of footing elevations the soft and or loose

3 3 Site Preparation
Based on the information provided we understand that minimal site grading work
will be required to achieve the design finished grades for the project In general areas
within the contract grading limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed at the on set
of earthwork Vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable materials should be
removed and disposed of properly off site
Soft loose and yielding areas encountered during clearing below areas designated
to receive fill or future improvements should be over excavated to expose firm or dense
material and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with well compacted fill The
excavated soft loose and or organic soils should be properly disposed of off site
Contract documents should include additive and deductive unit prices for over excavation
and compacted fill placement to account for variation in the over excavation quantities
Pavements concrete slabs and exterior walkways that are to be demolished
should be completely removed Over excavation resulting from the demolition should be
backfilled with well compacted fills Existing utilities to be abandoned should be removed
and the resulting excavation should be properly backfilled with fill material placed in 8 inch
loose lifts moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to
a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
After clearing and grubbing the area within the future pavement and slab on grade
limits should be over excavated to the bottom of the select borrow subbase or
non expansive select granular fill layer The subgrade soils should be scarified to a depth
of about 8 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction The compaction
requirement should be increased to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction in areas
subjected to vehicular traffic Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil
expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in
accordance with ASTM D 1557 test procedures Optimum moisture is the water content
percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling
four borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 4 to depths of about 5 to 18 feet below
the existing ground surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The borings were drilled using a truck mounted drill rig equipped with continuous flight
augers
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by testing
in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representations of the materials
encountered are provided on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 4
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 3 inch
OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches In addition
some samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with ASTM
Test Designation D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a
2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same hammer and drop The blow
counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an 18 inch drive are
shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Boring at the appropriate sample
depths

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content ASTM D 2216 determinations were performed on selected soil
samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties The results of
these tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
One 1 inch ring swell test was performed on a relatively undisturbed sample of the
surface soils to evaluate the swelling potential of the near surface soils The test results
are presented on Plate B 1
One Atterberg Limits test ASTM D 4318 was performed on a selected sample of
the soils to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits and to aid in soil classification Results of
the test are summarized on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depth Graphic
presentation of the test results is provided on Plate B 2
One California Bearing Ratio test ASTM D 1883 was performed on the
near surface soils to evaluate the pavement support characteristics of the soils The test
results are presented on Plate B 3
One Modified Proctor compaction test ASTM D 1557 was performed on a bulk
sample of the near surface soils to evaluate the dry density and moisture content
relationships The test results are presented on Plate B 4
