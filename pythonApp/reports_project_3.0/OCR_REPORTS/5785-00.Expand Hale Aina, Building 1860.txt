Project Considerations

proposed expansion site We understand that it is desired to provide a smooth transition
between the existing building structure and the proposed expansion

Anticipated Subsurface Conditions
In general we envision that the proposed expansion site is underlain by a surface
layer of fill materials approximately 6 to 8 feet thick consisting of very stiff clayey silts
overlying approximately 3 feet of soft compressible lagoonal deposit of silty clays This
was underlain by coralline deposits of varying degrees of density to a depth of
approximately 20 feet below the ground surface Very dense volcanic tuff formation locally
known as mudrock was then encountered and extended to the maximum depth
explored of approximately 28 5 feet below the ground surface It should be noted that
strong petroleum odor was encountered from the samples and soil cuttings obtained at a
depth of approximately 7 feet below the ground surface in Boring No 1 drilled during the
previous field exploration
Groundwater was encountered in the drilled borings at a depth of approximately
9 feet below the ground surface at the time of the previous field exploration This depth
generally corresponded to an elevation of approximately 1 foot MSL Due to the proximity
of the project site to the shoreline and the Pacific Ocean groundwater levels will likely be
affected by tidal fluctuations and other factors such as seasonal precipitation and storm
surge conditions
Descriptions and graphic presentation of the materials encountered in the drilled
boring are provided on the Log Legend Pate A and Logs of Borings Plates A 1 and A 2
Results of the laboratory ring swell test conducted on a near surface sample of the clayey
soils are presented on Plate B 1 of this report for reference

Building Foundations
Based on the soil boring data obtained from our previous field exploration
conducted in the vicinity of the dining hall expansion site we anticipate that the proposed
expansion site is generally underlain by a compressible layer of soft silty clays and loose
coralline sands below the surface fill crust Based on the subsoil conditions and the
anticipated structural loadings it is our opinion that shallow footing foundations may be
used for support of the proposed dining hall expansion provided that the estimated
differential settlement between the existing building and the proposed new expansion is
acceptable
An allowable bearing pressure of up to 2 000 pounds per square foot psf may be
used for the design of spread and or continuous footings bearing on the upper stiff clayey
silt fill material encountered near the existing ground surface This bearing value is for
those caused by wind or seismic forces
In general footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grades We understand that some of the renovation work performed at
the existing dining hall after its completion was supported on shallow foundations We
recommend that new footings adjacent to these existing shallow foundations extend at
least to the same depth as the existing footings or deeper to avoid surcharging the existing
shallow foundations The actual depths of the existing shallow footings should be
confirmed in the field during construction
Foundations located next to utility trenches or easements should be embedded
below a 45 degree imaginary plane extending upward from the bottom edge of the utility

Slabs On Grade
As previously mentioned differential settlement between the existing building and
proposed new expansion may occur due to the presence of the soft compressible subsoils
underlying the project site Therefore we recommend that pouring of the slab concrete for
the new expansion be delayed as much as practical in order to provide a smooth transition
between the new and existing structures
Based on our previous field exploration and laboratory test data we anticipate that
the project site is generally underlain by near surface clayey soils with moderate
expansion potential when subjected to moisture fluctuations To reduce the potential for
future distress to the lightly loaded slabs on grade resulting from shrinking and swelling of
the expansive clayey soils due to changes in the moisture content we recommend that the
subgrade soils be properly prepared and capped with at least 12 inches of non expansive
select granular fill capping fill material below the slab cushion The capping fill should
also extend beyond the new expansion footprint a minimum of 2 feet
The non expansive select granular fill should be placed as soon as possible after
the subgrade soils are properly prepared The prepared capping fill and the underlying
subgrade soils should be wetted and kept moist until the final placement of slab concrete
Where shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of the subgrade we recommend
preparing the soils again as recommended Saturation and subsequent yielding of the

In general we recommend placing a minimum 4 inch thick layer of cushion fill
below the interior slabs to provide the slabs with more uniform support The cushion fill

The thickened edges of slabs adjacent to unpaved areas should be embedded at

should be backfilled tightly against the slab with the on site clayey soil and graded to divert
water away from the slab to reduce the potential for water ponding around the slabs
Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared Vegetation organic debris deleterious material and other
unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed properly off site Soft and
yielding areas encountered during clearing should be over excavated to expose
firm dense natural material and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with
well compacted fill The excavated soft and or organic soils should be properly disposed
of off site and or used in landscape areas where appropriate
After clearing areas within the new slabs on grade should be over excavated
where necessary to provide the 12 inch thick non expansive select granular fill as
recommended in the Slabs On Grade section of this report The over excavation and
12 inch thick non expansive select granular fill should extend laterally a minimum of
2 feet beyond the perimeter of the proposed new slab on grade footprint or to the outside
edge of perimeter sidewalk if applicable
The over excavated subgrades finished subgrades and areas designated to
receive fills should then be scarified to a minimum depth of 8 inches moisture conditioned
to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of
90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of
soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density as determined by
ASTM D 1557 Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by weight
corresponding to the maximum dry density
Where shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of the subgrade we
recommend preparing the soil again as recommended above Saturation and subsequent
yielding of the exposed subgrade due to inclement weather and poor drainage may require

The 12 inch non expansive select granular fill required under the new
slabs on grade should consist of select granular material such as crushed coral or basalt
The material should be well graded from coarse to fine with no particles larger than
3 inches in largest dimension and should contain between 10 and 30 percent particles
passing the No 200 sieve The material should have a laboratory CBR value of 20 or
more and should have a maximum swell of 1 percent or less when tested in accordance
with ASTM D 1883 Geolabs should test imported fill materials for conformance with these
recommendations prior to delivery to the project site for the intended use
The non expansive select granular fill materials should be placed in level lifts of
about 8 inches in loose thickness moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture
and compacted to at least 95 percent relative compaction Compaction should be
accomplished by using a sheepsfoot rollers vibratory rollers multiple wheel
pneumatic tired rollers or other types of acceptable compaction equipment Water
tamping jetting or ponding should not be allowed for compaction of the fills
Site grading operations should be observed by a Geolabs representative It is
important that our representative observe the site grading operation to evaluate whether
undesirable materials are encountered during the excavation and _ scarification
processes and whether the exposed soil rock conditions are similar to those
encountered in our previous field exploration
Grease Interceptor
Based on the information provided we understand that a grease interceptor is
planned at the asphalt concrete paved loading dock area near Westover Street The
new grease interceptor will be 16 2 long 7 2 wide and 5 0 deep with 24 inches
cover at the top In general granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of open graded
gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation is recommended below the grease interceptor
structure for uniform support If soft and or loose soils are encountered at or near the
bottom of the grease interceptor structure we recommend that the excavation be
extended another 2 feet below the bottom of the grease interceptor The
over excavation should be backfilled with 18 inches of No 3B Fine gravel wrapped
around by a layer of non woven geotextile fabric such as Mirafi 180N or Amoco 4553 or
equivalent A 6 inch bedding layer may then be placed above the wrapped 18 inch
gravel stabilization layer prior to setting of the grease interceptor

Free draining granular materials such as open graded gravel ASTM C 33
No 67 gradation should be used for the initial trench backfill up to about 12 inches

support around the pipes It is critical to use the free draining materials around the pipes
to reduce the potential for formation of voids below the haunches of pipes and to
provide adequate support around the sides of the pipes Improper trench backfill around
the pipe could result in backfill settlement and pipe damage
The upper portion of the trench backfill from a level of 12 inches above the pipes
or groundwater level to the top of the subgrade may consist of the excavated on site
soils provided that they are free of deleterious materials The backfill should be
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture placed in maximum 8 inch level
loose lifts and mechanically compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
to reduce the potential for appreciable future ground subsidence Where trenches will
be below areas subjected to vehicular traffic paved areas the upper 3 feet of the
trench backfill below the pavement grade should be compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative compaction
Drainage
Proper surface drainage is critical to the performance of foundations constructed on
expansive soil conditions Therefore the finished grades outside the structure should be
sloped to shed water away from foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is
also advised that gutter systems be installed around the new expansion and that the
discharge be diverted away from the foundation areas Excessive landscape watering near
the foundations and slabs should be avoided Planters next to foundations should also be
avoided or have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce the potential for water infiltration
into the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the above
foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation
and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations should be
properly backfilled against the walls or slab edges immediately after setting of the concrete
to reduce the potential for water infiltration In addition drainage swales should be
provided as soon as possible and should be maintained to drain surface water runoff away
from slabs and foundations
