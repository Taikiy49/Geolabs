PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located along Laiku Street Waiolu Street and Princess Kahanu Avenue in
the Nanakuli area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the
project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 Based on the information
provided we understand that it is proposed to construct new 8 inch water mains and
appurtenances along these streets for water system improvements The water system
improvement area is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Based on the plan and profile drawing provided the pipe inverts are not known at
this time although they are expected to be about 3 feet below ground surface In order to
evaluate the need for corrosion control protection field resistivity testing was conducted
along the water main alignment in general accordance with the Water System External
Corrosion Control Standards Volume 3 1991 Detailed discussions and results of our
corrosion tests are presented in the following sections of this letter report

work was performed in general conformance with ASTM Test Designation G 57 95 and the
requirements provided in the Water System External Corrosion Control Standards Volume
3 1991 The scope of our work consisted of the following tasks and work efforts

FIELD SURVEY
Geophysical survey equipment i e resistance meter was mobilized to the project site
on for the field survey Our field work was performed under clear and sunny
conditions with generally dry surface conditions
Sixty eight soil resistivity surveys were conducted at a maximum of 300 foot intervals
at seventeen locations designated as R 1 through R 17 along the sidewalk of the streets
using the in Pin Method and a Nilsson Model 400 resistance meter
manufactured by Nilsson Electrical Laboratory Inc The method utilizes four surface probes
or pins electrodes to evaluate the average resistivity an indication of corrosivity of the
underlying soil mass The average resistivity values of the soil mass extending from the
existing ground surface to the test depth was assessed by using this method at the
approximate survey locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 Results of the resistivity
surveys are presented on Plates 3 1 through 3 4
The soil resistivity surveys were performed at the existing ground surface along the
sidewalk of the existing streets of the proposed 8 inch water mains The average soil
resistance along the proposed alignment was evaluated at each survey location with the
electrodes aligned at equal spacing of generally 3 to 9 feet at the existing ground surface The

The resistance meter Nilsson Model 400 impresses a voltage between the outer

FINDINGS AND DISCUSSIONS
Resistivity is generally recognized as one of the most significant soil characteristics with
regard to the corrosivity of the soil to buried metallic objects In general the lower the
resistivity the greater the potential for corrosion of the buried metallic structure Conversely
the higher the resistivity the less likely the soil will contribute to corrosion of metallic objects
On the basis of the field resistivity test results a majority of the tests indicate the
subsurface soils along the proposed water mains within the project site have corrosion ratings
of 3 to 4 This indicates the subsurface soils have corrosive to moderately corrosive potential
Some resistivity measurements indicating corrosion ratings of 2 and 5 were noted
LIMITATIONS
The evaluations presented in this report are based upon information obtained from field
data points by means of geophysical surveys Variation of soil conditions between and beyond
the field data points may occur and the nature and extent of such variations may not become
evident until a later point in time If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to
reevaluate the ratings provided in this report
The test locations R 1 through R 10 presented in this report were estimated by
measuring the linear distance from the test location R 1 along Waiolu Street and Laiku
Street towards the intersection with Muolo Place The test locations R 11 through R 14
were estimated by measuring the linear distance from the test location R 11 along Princess
Kahanu Avenue towards Farrington Highway The test locations R 15 through R 17 were
estimated by measuring the linear distance from R 15 along Waiolu Street towards the
intersection with Manaiakalani Place Elevations of the test locations were estimated in the
field from the features and reference points shown on the plans provided by Board of Water
