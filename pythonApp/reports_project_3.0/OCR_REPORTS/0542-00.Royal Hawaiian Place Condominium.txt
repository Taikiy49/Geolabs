INTRODUCTION
This report summarizes the findings of our subsurface explorations
at the subject site and presents recommendations for the design and
construction of the foundations to support the proposed structure
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed development will consist of a 20 story reinforced
concrete apartment tower upon a multi story commercial parking
structure The highrise structure will be approximately 57 feet

SITE CONDITIONS
Surface
The rectangular site is approximately 85 feet by 250 feet in plan
and is located makai of Kuhio Avenue between Lewers Street and the
Aina Luana Hotel located on the Ewa makai corner of the intersec
tion of Royal Hawaiian and Kuhio Avenues in the Waikiki section of
Honolulu
According to the topographic survey map prepared by
the ground surface at the site is essentially level at Elevation
six feet Presently the site is occupied by two three story apart
ment buildings a two story apartment a single story dwelling and
two garages
It is our understanding that the proposed structure will utilize
the entire property and will adjoin the existing Aina Luana Hotel
Geology

placed upon alluvium water deposited soils consisting of silty
sands with coral fragments These materials were deposited in a
lagoonal or shallow off shore platform environment which existed
behind the outer barrier reefs The lagoonal environment was sub
jected to periodic influxes of elastic non marine alluvial debris
clays and silts which apparently smothered the reef development
The lagoonal deposits are underlain by an interbedded sequence of
hard coral and layers of coral sand developed during periods of
changing sea levels
Subsurface Conditions
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored utilizing two
test borings drilled at the locations shown on the Boring Location
Plan Plate 1 A detailed description of the field exploration
methods and logs of the soils encountered are included in the
appendix
The subsurface explorations disclosed that the site is blanketed
by 6 to 8 feet of fill varying from loose to medium dense and
containing some areas of organic cohesive soil
Beneath the fill lagoonal deposits consisting of loose silty sand
with coral fragments were encountered This material extends to
depths of 43 and 45 feet at Borings 1 and 2
Four test borings records are included in the appendix from the

subsurface investigation conducted for the Aina Luana Hotel These
borings indicated that the fill and the lagoonal deposits vary in
thickness from 39 to 41 feet below the previous ground surface
Beneath the lagoonal deposits a dense upper coral layer was en
countered and extended from 45 to 60 feet below the existing ground
surface Below the upper coral layer the subsurface conditions
consist of interbedded medium dense to dense silty sand layers and
vary dense cemented coral sand with some hard coral layers
extending to the limit of the explorations at a depth of 130 feet
The ground water level is influenced by tidal fluctuations and was
generally encountered from 4 to 6 feet below the existing ground
surface
DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
It is our understanding that the proposed structure will have a
half basement with the finish floor at approximately Elevation 3
The foundation and pile caps are anticipated to extend 3 to 4 feet
below this level
The upper fill and loose lagoonal deposits cannot provide adequate
support for a major structure It is therefore necessary to by
pass this upper layer and support the structure upon piles penetrat
ing into the coral encountered approximately 45 feet below the ex
isting ground surface

Pile Foundations
It is estimated that a 16 inch diameter octagonal concrete pile
extending to a depth of 60 to 60 feet below the existing ground
surface will develop an allowable bearing capacity of 100 tons per
pile This pile capacity would be developed almost entirely from
end bearing within the coral formation The 100 ton per pile capa
city can therefore also be used for pile groups
It is anticipated that the piles can be driven without pre drill
ing five to ten feet into the coral deposit It is recommended
that eight driving resistance tests be performed at selected loca
tions to establish the characteristics of the pile driving resis
tance One pile from this group would be selected for a load test
to verify the pile capacity
For estimating purposes it is anticipated that the piles will be
driven a minimum depth of 45 feet and to a final driving resistance
of 18 blows per inch with a hammer delivering a rated energy of
15 000 foot pounds Driving resistance criteria will be established
upon request for alternative pile hammers
Uplift Resistance
Based upon the above driving criteria and a 10 foot penetration
into the coral layer it is recommeded that the piles be designed
for an allowable uplift resistance of ten kips per pile for both

single and group piles This value is relatively low due to the
limited penetration into competent material The upper lagoonal
deposits provide little uplift resistance
Lateral Capacity
The loose lagoonal material also limits the lateral pile capacity
It is recommended that a value of one kip per pile be utilized for
long term lateral loadings This loading will produce a maximum
moment in the pile estimated at 14 inch kips at a depth of appro
Ximately 12 feet
It is estimated that a lateral capacity for short term loads of
ten kips per pile can be developed with less than x inch deflection
at the top of the piles Short term loadings are considered
as loads less than two hours in duration The maximum moment with
in the pile would increase to 140 inch kips for the tenkips lateral
loading
The allowable pile capacity of 100 tons can be increased by one
third for wind and seismic loadings
Reinforced concrete floor slabs can be utilized in lieu of tie
beams to connect the individual pile caps

Additional lateral resistance can be developed by the pile caps

Groundwater
Due to the high ground water levels the floor slabs should be
designed to resist uplift pressure equivalent to the high tide
level of approximately Elevation 2
Grading
Fills placed as part of the subject project should be compacted
to at least 90 percent of the maximum dry density established in
accordance with ASTM D 1557 72T Full time inspection should be
performed to verify the degree of compaction when the fill will be
utilized for structural support The on site soils are acceptable
as backfill

It is recommended that the pile driving operations be monitored on
a full time basis by a qualified technician under the direction of
a soils engineer to verify that the piles have been properly driven
to the recommended bearing level The recommendations given in
this report are contingent upon such inspections

APPENDIX
FIELD EXPLORATION
Subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling two
borings utilizing truck mounted equipment Boring 1 extended to
a depth of 100 feet and Boring 2 to a depth of 130 feet The loca
tions of the borings are shown on the appended Site Plan Plate l
The soils were classified by visual and texual examinations in the
field by our geologist who continuously supervised the drilling
operations The classifications were verified by visual inspection
and laboratory testing All soils were classified in accordance
with the Unified Soils Classifications System A graphic presenta
tion of the soils encountered is presented on the boring logs
Plates A 1 and A 2
Soil samples were obtained by driving either a 2 4 inch I D dia
meter or a standard penetration sampler with a 140 pound hammer
free falling at a distance of 30 inches The blow counts to drive
the sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the log of borings at
the appropriate sample depth
