This report summarizes our earthwork observation and testing services performed 
during the site grading operations for the Kapolei West, Phase 1 Improvements project 
from March 13, 2017 through December 3, 2018. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The Kapolei West, Phase 1 Improvements project site is in the Kapolei area in the 
District of Ewa on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii.  The site is located at the west end of the 
existing Kapolei Parkway near the Kapolei Commons Shopping Center.  Lot 30001 is 
located south of the proposed Kapolei Parkway extension and west of the proposed State 
Harbor Access Road.  Lot 30000 is located north of Lot 30001.  The drainage channel is to 
the west of Lots 300000 and 300001.  A Project Location Map and Site Plans are shown 
on Plates 1, 2.1 and 2.2, respectively. 
The project involves the mass grading of Tax Map Key (TMKs): 9-1-014:041(Por.) 
and 9-1-015:002, 026 (Por.).  The total area to be mass graded is approximately 79 acres.  
The mass grading operation consisted of cuts on the order of about 20 to 30 feet deep at 
the drainage channel.  The soils generated from the drainage channel excavation were 
used to fill Lots 30000 and 30001 up to the mass grade elevations.

This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services

EARTHWORK OPERATIONS 
Goodfellow Bros., LLC performed the site grading operations. The site grading work 
consisted of clearing and grubbing, and placement and compaction of fills on the order of 
up to about 20 feet thick to achieve the design finished mass grades.  During the site 
grading operations, samples of the on-site and imported materials utilized as fill materials 
were obtained and transported to our laboratory for testing. The maximum dry densities 
and optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general accordance 
with ASTM D1557 (Modified Proctor), and the test results are summarized on Plates 3.1 
and 3.2. 
The fill materials were moisture-conditioned to above optimum moisture content 
and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as determined in 
accordance with ASTM D1557. 
During the site grading operations, periodic field density tests were performed in 
general accordance with ASTM D6938 (Nuclear Gauge Method).  Results of the 
compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 4.1 through 4.49 of this report.  
These tests were performed on the constructed fills at the site.  Test locations and 
elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on-site survey stakes.  These 
locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the 
methods used.  Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified 
minimum compaction was obtained and/or approved by our field representative based on 
visual observation and hand probing. 
Our services consisted of part-time field observation of the contractor’s fill 
placement and compaction procedures and confirmation of subgrade soil conditions 
exposed during the above grading operations. 
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the 
referenced tests, it is our opinion that the site grading operations during the mass grading 
construction for the above project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered 
by this report were performed in general accordance with the intent of the project plans 
and specifications and our geotechnical engineering recommendations presented in the 
referenced report dated October 24, 2013 and subsequent recommendations.

have been performed without our observation. However, the level of earthwork 
observation and testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some 
of the geotechnical risks to the project. It should be recognized that there are variations
