INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of a geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed residence for Dr and Mrs Worldster Lee located at 4635
Kahala Avenue in the Kahala district on the island of Oahu Hawaii The site and its
vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2821 00 Page 2
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering recom
mendations resulting from the field exploration laboratory testing and engineering
analyses These recommendations are intended for foundation design input and may not
be appropriate to form the basis for construction cost estimates nor for construction
bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is currently occupied by a single family residence with a concrete
driveway patios and a swimming pool It is proposed to demolish the existing
development including the swimming pool and to construct a new single family residence
on the project site
The proposed residence is to be a two 2 story wood framed structure with
concrete slab on grade floors The project is also to involve construction of a swimming
pool a tennis court a vehicular driveway and garage
It is our understanding that construction of a basement level located at the center
of the proposed residence is being considered at the time of preparation of this report
The site is essentially level with existing ground elevations ranging from
MSL Therefore cut and fill operations for site grading to provide anticipated finish
grades except for the excavations required for the swimming pool and basement will be
minimal

EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is a rectangular lot located at 4635 Kahala Avenue in the Kahala
district on the island of Oahu Hawaii Presently the lot is occupied by a single family
residence with a swimming pool
The project site is fronted by Kahala Avenue along its northerly boundary An
existing private driveway bounds the project site along its westerly boundary Existing
residences are located to the easterly southerly and westerly sides of the project site
The topography of the project site is generally level with current ground elevations

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
It is our opinion that the proposed project is generally feasible from a geotechnical
engineering point of view provided that the recommendations presented below are
followed However construction of the basement as it is currently planned will be costly
and risky and in our opinion should be avoided
Basement Level
It is our understanding that construction of a basement level at the center of the
proposed residence is being considered Groundwater was encountered at depths of six
6 to seven 7 feet below the existing ground surface Excavation to depths of about
nine 9 feet is anticipated to be required for the construction of the proposed basement
level Therefore excavation support and dewatering operations will be required for the
basement construction due to the high groundwater level and the permeable sandy soil
conditions encountered at the site
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2821 00 Page 6
Excavation support such as installation of continuous interlocking steel sheetpiles
will be required to provide stability of the excavated sides and to provide cutoff of
seepage during dewatering operations Moreover the dewatering operations should be
carefully performed and monitored not to cause an areal drawdown of the groundwater
level which may damage the existing residences in the adjacent properties It is
anticipated that large quantities of water will be required to be removed by pumping
during dewatering due to the permeable characteristics of the sandy subsoil conditions
and the relatively high groundwater level Therefore a properly executed basement
construction and dewatering operation will be relatively very costly for a single family type
of construction
Besides the above high construction cost considerations long term performance
of the basement waterproofing should also be considered A minor deficiency due to
workmanship and installation procedure in the basement waterproofing work could cause
leakage and greatly reduce the serviceability and desirability of the constructed basement
intended for residential purpose even if provision of a sump and an automatically
controlled sump pump is provided
It is therefore our opinion that construction of a basement level at the project site
should be avoided
However if construction of a basement level is to be included in the final project
it must be made very clear that the excavation support system required and dewatering
methods selected should be the choice of the contractor and must comply with ail
applicable safety requirements The adequacy and safety of the excavation support and
dewatering systems should be made the sole responsibility of the contractor His
representative should be continuously present on site to observe any changing conditions
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2821 00 Page 7
which might affect the stability of the excavated sides and or integrity of the adjacent
developments during construction
Foundations
Shallow spread and continuous footing foundations bearing on the upper surface
layer of beach sand located above the high groundwater level may be used to support
the proposed residence
An allowable bearing value of 1 500 p s f can be used for footings bearing on the
compacted in situ beach sand or on anew compacted engineered fill embankment which
may be required to raise the site grades A footing embedment of twelve 12 inches
below the lowest adjacent finished grade is recommended Deeper footing embedment
is generally not recommended due to the high groundwater level encountered in the
borings
Heavy structural members should be avoided in order to reduce the heavy dead
loads of such structural members acting on the foundations and thus reduce the required
footing size In general footing settlement is anticipated to increase with increased
footing size or with deeper footing embedment due to the site soils condition encountered
in the field exploration
It is recommended that all footing excavations should be tamped or compacted to
a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density as determined in accordance with the
procedures described in ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
If loose clayey sand lenses or clayey soil pockets are encountered at the bottoms
of footing excavations they should be over excavated to dense in situ sand The
resulting depression should then be backfilled with select on site beach sand or granular
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2821 00 Page 8
material such as crusher waste aggregate compacted to a minimum of 90 of the
maximum dry density as determined in accordance with the procedures described in
ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
If a footing is located next to a utility line the utility trench backfill should always
be carefully monitored and adequate compaction ensured to reduce the potential for
footing settlement due to consolidation of the trench backfill
Surface water should be diverted away from the foundations to avoid ponding of
surface runoff water that may adversely affect the bearing capacity of the soils below the
foundations
In general total footing settlements are estimated to be less than approximately
one 1 inch total and one half 4 inch differential provided the above recommendations
are followed
Slab on Grade
The ground floors of the proposed residence are anticipated to be concrete
slab on grade
A minimum four 4 inch thick concrete slab on top of a minimum six 6 inch thick
capillary break or cushion layer of No 3B Fine gravel or ASTM C 33 No 67 gravel is
recommended In order to further protect against moisture infiltration a plastic vapor
barrier should be incorporated below the slab One 1 inch of moist fine sand may be
placed above the plastic membrane to help curing of the concrete We recommend that
the on grade slab be poured later after the other spread or continuous footings have
been poured and that the initial footing settlements have occurred first
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2821 00 Page 9
The thickened edges of the concrete slab should be embedded a minimum of
twelve 12 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade
Concrete Pavements
It is anticipated that concrete pavements may be provided for the driveway and the
floor of the garage for the proposed residence
Based on the assumption that anticipated traffic will be composed of light vehicles
the following pavement section is recommended
6 Inch Concrete
4 Inch Base Course 95 Compaction
10 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
on Compacted Subgrade
Joints are recommended at about eight 8 to ten 10 foot intervals and at
discontinuities in order to control shrinkage and contraction cracks and to allow for some
relative movement of the concrete slabs The length to width ratios of the slabs should
not exceed one and one quarter 1 25 to one 1 Slabs exceeding this ratio or which
are otherwise not rectangular in shape should be provided with welded wire mesh
reinforcement
Swimming Pool
A swimming pool is to be constructed at the southeastern corner of the proposed
residence The on site sandy soil is loose to medium dense and is highly permeable
Therefore it is our opinion that excavation shoring and substantial dewatering will be
required for the proposed swimming pool construction if the pool bottom is to be below
the groundwater level

7 feet below the existing ground surface Based on elevations estimated from the
available topographic survey map the measured groundwater levels ranged from
elevations of about 0 2 to 1 0 foot above MSL at the time of our field exploration
The swimming pool design should include provision for resistance against
hydrostatic uplift during periods when the partially submerged swimming pool is emptied
for maintenance purposes A groundwater level elevation no lower than two 2 feet
above MSL is recommended for uplift resistance design purposes

Scheduling of construction of the swimming pool should be considered as it relates

loss of support of those footings caused by excavation and or dewatering for
construction of the swimming pool

It is recommended that the backfill behind the swimming pool walls should consist
of compacted granular material such as select borrow or No 3B Fine gravel or ASTM
C 33 No 67 gravel compacted to a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density as
determined in accordance with the procedures described in ASTM D 1557 78
Site Grading
Prior to construction the site should be cleared and grubbed Any deleterious
materials such as vegetation debris old footings construction rubble and organic matter
encountered during clearing and grubbing should be removed and properly disposed of
off site
It is our understanding that the existing swimming pool will be demolished The
concrete and miscellaneous rubble generated by the pool demolition should be properly
disposed of off site After removal of the concrete walls and bottom of the pool and
appurtenant piping all loose material which sloughs into the depression should be
removed and replaced in a controlled manner Backfill required below the groundwater
level should be self compacting granular material such as No 3B Fine gravel or ASTM
C 33 No 67 The sloughed material previously removed may be used in the upper
portions of the backfill The remaining backfill requirements may consist of material
removed for construction of the new swimming pool and or imported selected granular
material All backfill above the groundwater elevation should be placed in level lifts not
exceeding ten 10 inches in loose thickness and compacted to at least 90 of the
maximum dry density as determined in accordance with the procedures described in
ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
The proposed building and pavement areas should be scarified to a depth of six
6 inches after site clearing and grubbing The scarified subgrade should be thoroughly
moisture conditioned and recompacted to obtain a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2821 00 Page 12
density as determined in accordance with procedures described in ASTM D 1557 78 To
reduce disturbance to the adjacent residences and to reduce pore water pressure
build up in sandy subsoils causing possible soil weakening use of heavy vibratory
compaction equipment should not be allowed
Any imported fill and backfill material that may be required should be tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to delivery to the site
The fills and backfills should be placed in loose horizontal lifts not exceeding ten
10 inches loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry
density as determined in accordance with procedures described in ASTM D 1557 78
The site should be graded to provide drainage of surface runoff away from all
foundations
Retaining Walls
Low concrete retaining walls may be required to provide different finish grades for
the proposed project The following design guidelines for retaining walls are
recommended

Walls unrestrained at the top 935 p c f equivalent

backfill and above groundwater
Walls restrained at the top 50 p c f equivalent
at rest condition level fluid pressure

2 A bearing value up to 1 500 p s f with a minimum footing embedment of
twelve 12 inches may be used for the wall footing design
3 For lateral resistance design a passive earth resistance of 300 p s f may
be used A base friction factor of 0 35 may also be used to determine the
resistance of the walls against sliding
4 Backfill behind the retaining walls should consist of granular non expansive
material such as select on site sandy material select borrow or No 3B Fine
gravel or ASTM C 33 No 67 to minimize excessive compactive effort
required The upper twelve 12 inches of backfill should consist of
non expansive impervious material to reduce water infiltration behind the
walls
5 A subsurface drainage system should be utilized behind the walls to protect
against build up of hydrostatic pressure
Drainage
The finished lot grade outside the footings slabs and pavements should be
shaped to shed water away from foundations to avoid ponding conditions Also it is
advised that a gutter system be installed around the residence and that the gutter run off
water be diverted as far away from the foundations and pavements as feasible Excessive
landscape watering near the foundations should also be avoided
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have concrete bottoms and
drains to prevent water from entering the foundation and slab subsoils
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2821 00 Page 14
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the above
recommended foundation construction since ponded water could cause excessive
subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation
excavations should be properly backfilled tight against the footings and slabs immediately
after setting of the concrete to prevent ponding conditions
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained to drain
all surface run off away from the foundations and slabs
Design Review
Preliminary and final drawings and specifications for site grading utility and
foundation construction should be forwarded to the project geotechnical engineer for
review and written comments prior to advertisement for bids This review is needed to
determine adherence to the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein
If this review is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for
misinterpretation of the recom mendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the footing excavations and backfilling be observed by a
competent geotechnical engineer during construction to see that adequate bearing has
been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of our
exploration
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and approved by
the soils engineer prior to delivery to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the above
observations
