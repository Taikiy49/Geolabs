Preliminary Geotechnical Recommendations
Kaipapau Well Development
Hauula Oahu Hawaii
We have recently completed our field exploration for the proposed Kaipapau Well
Development project in the Hauula area on the windward side of the island of Oahu
Hawaii This letter is to present an overview of the subsurface conditions encountered
at the site and to present our preliminary geotechnical recommendations for the design
of foundations and pavements for the proposed project
Subsurface Conditions
At the time of our field exploration it appeared that the well pad area at the end
of the dirt road has already been rough graded to approximately the proposed finish
grades of the subject project Based on the grading plan provided we understand that
minor fills and cuts up to about 15 feet will be required to attain the finish grades for the
project construction
The subsurface conditions at the proposed project site were explored by drilling
and sampling eight borings to depths ranging from approximately 14 to 36 feet below
the existing ground surface In addition our field exploration program consisted of the
excavation of 18 test pits to depths ranging from about 4 to 12 feet below the existing
ground surface Based on the field exploration at the site the subsurface conditions
at the well pad area and along the access road alignment generally consist of saprolite
colluvium and highly weathered basalt rock materials throughout the depths explored

Discussions and Recommendations
Based on the preliminary grading plan provided we understand that the
proposed control building breaker reservoir and other related structures for the
Kaipapau Well development project will be in minor cuts and fills Our field exploration
indicates that the well pad area at the end of the dirt road has already been rough
graded to approximately the proposed finish grades of the subject project Fill soils
consisting of the on site materials were encountered in our drilled borings and
excavated test pits at shallow depths along the outer margin of the level pad Due to
the presence of undocumented fills in the well pad area we recommend that the soils
within 12 inches of the foundation subgrades be removed and replaced with compacted
on site soils in order to provide more uniform foundation support for the proposed
structures
Slab On Grade Foundations
Based on the relatively competent subsoil conditions encountered at the project
site we believe that shallow slab on grade foundations with thickened edges bearing
on the recompacted on site soils may be used for support of the proposed lightly
loaded structures
In order to provide more uniform support for the foundation and to reduce the
potential for foundation distress resulting from differential subgrade conditions due to
the cut and fill transitions we recommend that the subgrades for slabs on grade and
thickened edge footings be over excavated a minimum of 12 inches and be replaced
in controlled lifts compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Relative
compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the
maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 1557 91 If soft and or loose materials are encountered at the bottom
of footing over excavations they should be over excavated to expose the underlying
firm materials The over excavation should be backfilled with on site fill materials
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction or the footing bottom may
be extended down to the underlying competent material

The slab subgrades should be kept moist prior to placement of concrete To
reduce the potential for drying of the subgrade soils and to reduce the costs of form
construction we recommend that the slab edges be cast neat against the soils The
slab edges should incorporate a sufficient amount of top and bottom longitudinal steel
reinforcement The top and bottom steel reinforcement bars should be connected by
stir ups The ends of the stir ups should extend into the floor slab area and should be
tied to the welded wire mesh The welded wire mesh in the slab on grade should be
embedded at the proper height in the concrete to provide adequate reinforcement
Blocks or saddles may be used to raise the wire mesh A stiff grade or wire mesh is
recommended For design of structural slabs a modulus of subgrade reaction of 100
pounds per square inch per inch of deflection p c i may be used for the recompacted
on site soils A structural engineer should be consulted for design details of the slab
on grade foundations
For slab on grade foundations bearing on the recompacted on site soils as
described above an allowable bearing pressure of up to 2 500 pounds per square foot
p s f may be used for design This bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may
be increased by one third for transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic
forces
Thickened edge footings should be embedded a minimum of 12 inches below
the lowest adjacent finish grade The base of the thickened edge should have a
minimum width of 12 inches If the footings are located adjacent to a slope the
footings should be embedded deep enough to provide a minimum 8 foot setback
distance measured from the outside edge of the footings horizontally to the slope face
If foundations are designed in accordance with the recommendations presented
herein we estimate total settlements to be on the order of 1 inch with differential
settlements on the order of inch
Lateral loads acting on the structures may be resisted by frictional resistance
between the base of the foundation and the bearing materials and by passive earth
pressure developed against the near vertical faces of the embedded portion of footings
A coefficient of friction of 0 35 may be used for foundations bearing on the recompacted
on site soils Resistance to lateral loads due to passive pressure may be calculated
using an equivalent fluid pressure of 350 pounds per square foot per foot of depth
p c f for footings embedded in the stiff dense saprolites and engineered fills These
values assume that the concrete for footings is cast neat against the stiff dense

We understand that retaining walls will be needed along some sections of the
proposed roadway alignment for grade separation and for lateral support of pavement
sections at sharp turns and steep side slopes Based on the information provided to
us five retaining walls totaling approximately 1 000 lineal feet are planned along the
roadway alignment The retaining walls will be located approximately at Stations 0 00

Wall Foundations
We believe that the planned retaining walls may be supported on shallow
continuous footings bearing on the stiffidense saprolites encountered at the
project site An allowable bearing pressure of 3 000 p s f may be used for
design of footings bearing on the undisturbed stiff dense in situ soils The
bearing value above is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by one
third when considering transient loads such as wind or seismic forces If the
footings are designed in accordance with our recommendations total settlement
of wall footings is estimated to be on the order of less than 1 inch
Wall foundations should be embedded at least 24 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grade If fills are encountered at the bottom of the footing
excavations the footings should be embedded deeper to bear on the native
soils or the existing loose fills should be removed and replaced with engineered
fill compacted to at least 95 percent relative compaction Relative compaction
refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the
maximum dry density of the same soil determined in accordance with ASTM
Test Designation D 1557 91
Where footings are located adjacent to slopes or on slopes the footings should
be embedded to a sufficient depth to provide a minimum horizontal setback
distance of at least 8 feet measured from the edge of the footing to the face of

Drainage
A typical drainage system would consist of a 1 to 2 foot wide zone of permeable
material No 3B Fine gravel or ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation immediately
adjacent to the wall with a perforated pipe perforations down at the base of the
wall discharging to a discharge facility or weep holes A filter fabric such as
Mirafi 140N or equivalent should be placed between the drainage material and
the compacted backfill material The drainage material should extend from the
base of the wall to a height within 2 feet below finish grade The remaining
backfill should consist of an impervious capping of select non expansive soil
As an alternative a prefabricated drainage product such as MiraDrain or
EnkaDrain may be used instead of the drainage material with filter fabric The
prefabricated drainage product should also hydraulically connect to a perforated
pipe at the base of the wall

Pavement
Asphaltic concrete pavement for the access roadway is anticipated to serve the
new structures We anticipate that the vehicle loading for the access roadway of this
project will consist primarily of passenger vehicles and light delivery trucks Based on
our field exploration and laboratory testing the on site soils appear to have relatively
Therefore the subgrade soils have poor pavement support characteristics and are
potentially expansive when subjected to moisture fluctuations Based on the above
assumptions and considering the light traffic anticipated for the proposed project we
recommended that the following flexible pavement section be used for preliminary
design purposes
Asphaltic Concrete Pavement
2 0 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 0 Inch Base Course 95 percent compaction
18 0 Inch Select Borrow 95 percent compaction
26 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness on Compacted
Moist Subgrade
The subgrade soils under the pavement area should be moisture conditioned to
at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture and compacted to at least 95 percent
relative compaction CBR tests and or field observations should be performed on the
actual subgrade soil samples during construction to confirm that the above design
sections are adequate The base course and select borrow should consist of crushed
basalt aggregates compacted to not less than 95 percent relative compaction
Paved areas should be sloped and drainage gradients maintained to carry all
surface water off the site Surface water ponding should not be allowed anywhere on
the site during or after construction A subdrain system traversing the roadway
alignment should be considered for areas where drainage flows across the pavement
section are anticipated Where concrete curbs are used to isolate landscaping in or
adjacent to the pavement areas we recommend that the curbs extend a minimum of
2 inches into the select borrow to reduce migration of landscape water into the
pavement section Alternatively a subdrain system could be constructed to collect
excessive water from landscaping irrigation For long term performance we
recommend a subdrain system be constructed adjacent to paved landscaped areas
