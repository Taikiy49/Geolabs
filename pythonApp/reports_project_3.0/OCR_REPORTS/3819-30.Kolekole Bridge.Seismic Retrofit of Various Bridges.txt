This letter report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observation services
performed during the installation and testing of the rock anchors for the Seismic Retrofit of
Kolekole Bridge project located in the District of South Hilo on the Island of Hawaii
In general this letter report presents our observations and the results of the tests
performed on the rock anchors for the project from October 23 through
The general contractor for the project was Hawaiian Dredging
Construction Company and the specialty subcontractor responsible for installation and
testing of the rock anchors was CMZ of Hawaii Inc
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Kolekole Bridge project site is located along Hawaii Belt Road Route 19 in the
District of South Hilo on the Island of Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the
project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
A geotechnical engineering exploration of the project site was performed by
Geolabs Inc and our recommendations were presented in our report entitled
Geotechnical Engineering Exploration Kolekole Bridge Seismic Retrofit of Various
Bridges District of South Hilo Island of Hawaii dated The purpose
of our geotechnical engineering services during construction of the project was to observe
the installation and testing of the rock anchors for general conformance with the project
plans and specifications and the intent of the design
Based on the project plans we understand that threaded reinforcing bars encased
in a High Density Polyethylene HDPE pipe extend from abutment to abutment through
each of the five intermediate piers to restrain the bridge structure in the longitudinal
direction from moving excessively during a seismic event Lateral restraint at the ends will
be provided by the concrete restraining block held in place by eight rock anchors

The concrete restraining block at the Honokaa side of the bridge Concrete
Connection Block No 1 will be located between Pier Nos 1 and 2 and the concrete
restraining block at the Hilo side of the bridge Concrete Connection Block No 2 will be
located between Pier No 5 and Abutment No 2 The loads on the threaded reinforcing
bars encased in the HDPE pipe would be transferred to a system of rock anchors
connected to the concrete restraining block
Based on the plans eight rock anchors will be constructed at each concrete
restraining block The system of rock anchors is considered a passive system where it
would engage only when pulled upon such as during a seismic event reducing the
amount of longitudinal lateral deflection of the bridge structure to an acceptable limit
ROCK ANCHOR INSTALLATION AND TESTING
Installation and testing of the 16 rock anchors for the project was performed by
CMZ of Hawaii Inc The approximate locations of the 16 rock anchors are shown on the
Rock Anchor Identification Plans Plates 2 1 and 2 2 Installation and testing of the rock
anchors for the project progressed from October 23 through
Rock Anchor Installation
A representative from Geolabs was present to observe the drilling high strength
threaded reinforcing bar installation and the grouting operations during the installation of
the rock anchors for the project The rock anchors were drilled using a C8 track mounted
drill rig equipped with 6 inch diameter compressed air activated down the hole hammers
In general the rock anchor drilled holes extended to depths of about 41 9 to
46 8 feet from the back face of the concrete restraining blocks The rock anchor depths
were generally at least 1 foot deeper than the length of the steel reinforcing bar in
accordance with the project specifications The estimated volumes of grout placed within
the rock anchor holes ranged from approximately 8 6 to 17 6 cubic feet ft at Concrete
Connection Block No 1 and from about 7 9 to at Concrete Connection
Block No 2 Logs of the individual rock anchors containing our observations and other
pertinent information recorded by our field personnel for each rock anchor installed are
provided on the Rock Anchor Installation Records in Appendix A of this letter report
Based on the observations of our field representatives we believe that the 16 rock
anchors have been installed in general conformance with the project plans and
specifications and within the limits of generally accepted construction practices for rock
anchors In addition the specialty subcontractor performed post grouting of all the rock
anchors installed at no additional cost to the State The post grouting was performed
generally on the day following the initial grouting of the rock anchors Post grouting of the
rock anchors consisted of installing a 4 inch PVC pipe along the full length of the rock
anchor hole prior to initial grouting The bottom 20 feet of the PVC pipe consisted of check
valves to allow egress of the grout into the previously grouted rock anchor The estimated

Rock Anchor Testing
In accordance with the project specifications two rock anchors for each concrete
restraining block a total of 4 rock anchors were subjected to creep tests and the
remaining rock anchors a total of 12 rock anchors were subjected to proof tests In
general the rock anchors were loaded to the maximum test load of 133 kips which is
133 percent of the design load of 100 kips The associated movements of the rock anchor
head at each load increment up to the maximum test load were measured and recorded
In general the rock anchors tested met the acceptance criteria specified in the
project specifications The criteria for acceptance of the rock anchors consisted of the
following
The rock anchor displacement shall be greater than 80 percent of the
theoretical elastic elongation of the unbonded length
The rock anchor displacement shall be less than the theoretical elastic
elongation of the unbonded and bonded lengths
The creep per log cycle shall be less than 0 040 inches between the
and 60 minute readings
Based on our observations of the rock anchor tests performed by the specialty
subcontractor it is our professional opinion that the rock anchors have been installed to
provide the design load with tolerable movements The test results on the individual rock
anchors tested are presented on the Rock Anchor Test Data Sheets in Appendix A of this
letter report In addition the Load versus Displacement curves and Displacement versus
Time curves for each rock anchor tested are provided in Appendix A of this letter report
