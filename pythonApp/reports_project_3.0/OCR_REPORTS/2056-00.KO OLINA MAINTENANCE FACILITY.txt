INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the proposed Maintenance Facility for the Ko
Olina Resort in The general location of the site is
shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The exploration was performed
in general accordance with our proposal dated
This report summarizes the findings from the field exploration and
our geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analyses
based on data obtained during the exploration These recommendations are
intended for design input and are not intended for construction bidding
estimation purposes

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to grade the area and construct a maintenance facility
for the Ko Olina Resort The maintenance facility will consist of
administration automotive maintenance and facilities maintenance
buildings an asphaltic concrete employee parking area and an asphaltic
concrete maintenance yard A culvert will be constructed across the
adjacent drainage swale to provide access to the site
Based on the preliminary grading plan supplied to us cuts up to
about 14 feet into the coral algal limestone hills located at the southern
boundary of the site are anticipated A retaining wall up to about 8 feet
high along the southern boundary for grade separation is also planned
At the time of our field exploration the site had been roughly
graded Therefore only about 2 feet of site filling is anticipated
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface soil conditions at the site in order to develop a generalized
soil rock data set for the formulation of geotechnical engineering
recommendations pertinent to the foundation culvert and pavement design
of the proposed maintenance center
The work was performed in general accordance with our proposal to
West Beach Estates dated The scope of work was limited
to
1 drilling and sampling five 5 borings to depths of approximately
16 5 feet below the existing ground surface and obtaining surface
samples

classification of the materials encountered
5 engineering analyses of the field and laboratory data for the

SITE DESCRIPTION
The subject site is located on the eastern side of the Ko Olina
Resort Development This rectangular area approximately 200 feet by 270
feet in plan dimension is bounded by a drainage swale to the north
abandoned sugar cane fields to the east future golf course Hole No 3 to
the west and coral algal hills immediately to the south
The site had been roughly graded with existing fills ranging from 3
to 4 feet at the time of our exploration The surface material consists
primarily of reddish brown silty clay The current ground elevation is
about 40 feet above mean sea level

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Building Foundations
The laboratory tests indicated that the existing surficial silty clay
is moderately expansive when saturated from a dry condition However
about two feet of new fill is planned for the site
Therefore if select low expansive fill soil is used to provide a
capping layer over the moderately expansive soil shallow spread footing
foundations may be utilized for the proposed maintenance buildings

An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f may be utilized Estimated

Site Grading
Site Preparation
Prior to the commencement earthwork all areas within the grading
limits should be cleared and grubbed of all vegetation rubbish and
deleterious materials The resulting grub and spoil material should
be disposed of properly off site Any soft weak or yielding areas
disclosed during the clearing operations should be over excavated to
firm ground and the resulting excavations should be backfilled with
well compacted engineered fill
Subsequent to clearing and grubbing the area within the grading
limits should be scarified to a depth of 6 inches moisture
conditioned to near optimum moisture content and recompacted to a
minimum of 90 maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78
Excavation
Based on the preliminary grading plan supplied to us cuts up to
about 14 feet into the hills coral algal limestone located at the
southern boundary of the site are anticipated Relatively hard
excavation into the coral algal formation is anticipated
Slopes
For cut slopes into the coral algal limestone formation a slope
ratio of 1H 1V may be used Care should be exercised in the grading
of cut slopes to prevent overcutting of the slopes

Slope benches should be provided at maximum vertical height intervals
of about 20 feet if the final grading plans will require cuts in
excess of 20 feet
Surface run off water from the slope should be diverted away from the
buildings and facilities The slope should be planted or otherwise
protected as soon as possible to reduce damage and unravelling by
erosion
For fill slopes a slope ratio of 2H 1V is recommended Fill slopes
should be constructed by overfilling and cutting back to the design

Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
No major fill placement is anticipated at the site based on the plans
supplied to us
In general the on site coral algal material derived from the
excavation and broken down to 6 inches minus size may be used as fill
material If required new imported fill material should consist of
non expansive select granular materials less than 3 inches in maximm
size with less than 20 passing the 200 sieve
The fill should be placed in horizontal loose level lifts up toa
maximm of 10 inches thick and compacted to a minimm of 90 of its
maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78

Drainage Culvert
At the proposed culvert site the boring encountered soft clayey
silt fill from 3 5 to 7 5 feet In order to provide sufficient
compaction around the culvert and minimize possible damage to the
culvert due to potential fill settlement and compaction operations the
general embankment should first be placed in the drainageway prior to
construction of the culvert General guidelines for the culvert
construction are presented below
Excavation
The general embankment area should first be over excavated to the
stiff natural ground and recompacted to a minimm of 90 maximum dry
density per ASIM D 1557 78 in 10 inch loose level lifts to the
finished grade Upon completion of the filling a trench should then
be excavated through the compacted fill to the culvert invert and the
pipe placed in the trench

A 12 inch cushion layer of gravel such as 3B Fine ASIM C 33 No 67
gravel is recommended below the culvert structure to provide

Trench Backfill
Select well draining granular material such as 3B Fine gravel or
sand which could be compacted by water tamping may be used as
backfill to the top of culvert Above this level general select
fill may be used
Headwall Structures
Headwall structures are required at the inlet and outlet of the
culvert An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f is recommended
for walls resting on stiff natural ground or compacted fill
Well graded granular material such as base course 3B Fine gravel
sand or equivalent should be used as backfill material directly
behind the walls This would reduce the compaction effort required
for the backfill and reduce the build up of additional lateral
pressures on the walls

Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comments
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this review
is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for
misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during earthwork and
construction phases of the foundation work This is to observe compliance
with the design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow
design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from that
anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in
this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
