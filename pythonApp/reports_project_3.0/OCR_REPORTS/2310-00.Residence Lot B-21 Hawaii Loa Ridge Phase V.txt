SUMMARY OF RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration encountered stiff clayey
silt soils beneath the site Laboratory testing
and previous experience have demonstrated that
this material has moderately high swelling
potential particularly when remolded or
recompacted
Special foundation design is recommended to reduce
the effects of the expansive soil
To utilize a slab on grade foundation the
recommended site improvement technique is to place
a minimum 3 foot thick capping layer of
non expansive material below the foundation
To utilize a spread or continuous footing
foundation the recommended site improvement
technique is to place the footing on 3 feet of
non expansive material or to embed the footing a
minimum of 3 feet below the finished grade
The text of this report should be referred to for
detailed and special design recommendations

The project site is located near the junction of Moani ala Street

approximately rectangular shaped 10 400 square foot lot is fronted by
Moani ala Street to the west

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration disclosed that the lot is underlain by a
surface layer of reddish brown expansive clay The laboratory test
results and previous experience indicate that this material is
moderately to highly expansive Therefore the foundation will require
special design and construction procedures in order to reduce the
shrink swell effect of the subsoils
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2310 00 Page 4
Foundation
A spread or continuous footing foundation may be used to support
the proposed structure
In order to reduce the effects of the expansive soil we recommend
that the expansive clays be over excavated down to a depth of 3 feet
below the ground surface below the individual footings or to weathered
rock formation and then replaced with non expansive borrow soils such as
crusher waste select borrow or coral The replaced soils should be
compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density per ASIM
D 1557 78 The footings can then be placed on the compacted
non expansive soil for details refer to the Schematic Section on Plate
3
Alternatively the footings may be embedded a minimm of 3 feet
below the lowest adjacent grade or to weathered rock formation for
details refer to the Schematic Section on Plate 3
An allowable bearing value of 3000 p s f can be used for footings
bearing on compacted fill or in situ soil
If a footing is located next to a utility line it should extend to
the bottom of the utility trench to reduce footing settlement due to
settlement of the trench backfill
Surface water should be diverted away from the foundation to avoid
ponding of surface run off water that may affect the expansive soils
around the foundation

Slab on Grade
In order to reduce the expansive effects of the soils we recommend
that a minimum 3 foot thick capping layer of non expansive material be
placed below the slab on grade foundation The thickness of the capping
layer may be reduced where weathered rock formation is encountered
The expansive clay below the slab should be removed for a minimm
depth of 3 feet below the outside finished grade and replaced with
non expansive off site borrow materials such as select borrow crusher
waste or coral compacted to a minimm of 90 of its maximm dry
density per ASTM D 1557 78 The underlying clay soil remaining should
be moistened by water sprinkling and then maintained in a moist
condition
A 4 inch layer of 3 Fine cushion fill should be provided below the
slab In order to protect against moisture infiltration a vapor
barrier may be incorporated below the slab
The thickened edge slab footings can bear on the compacted
non expansive fill The thickened edges should be embedded a minimm of
12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade for details refer to the
Schematic Section on Plate 4
Concrete Driveway
For the concrete driveway recommendations provided for the 3 foot
capping layer for the concrete slab on grade should be followed
However since the driveway could tolerate some amount of swelling the
non expansive capping thickness could be reduced to 2 feet to reduce
costs if some slab cracking is tolerable The subgrade soils should be
kept moist prior to concrete placement

It is our understanding that concrete retaining walls are to be
used for grade separation The guidelines for retaining wall design are
listed below

Wall restrained at the top 55 p s f equivalent

backfill per foot of depth
An allowable bearing value of up to 3000 p s f is recommended
for foundations bearing on the stiff on site soil
2 A passive earth resistance of 300 p s f per foot of depth may be
used for the wall footing on level areas The passive pressure
must be reduced for walls on slopes
3 A base friction factor of 0 4 may also be used to determine the
resistance against sliding of the wall
4 The wall foundations on relatively flat areas should be embedded
a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade

Drainage
The finished lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings should
be shaped to shed water away from foundations and to avoid ponding
condition Also it is advised that a gutter system be installed around
the residence and that gutter water be diverted far away from the house
foundation and driveway Excessive landscape watering near foundations
should also be avoided
Planters next to the foundation should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance
of the above recommended foundations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of
strength The foundation excavation should be properly backfilled tight
against the slab footing immediately after setting of the concrete to
prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained
to drain all surface run off away from the slab foundation
Design Review
Plans and specifications for foundation site grading and utility
construction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and
written comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the footing excavations and backfilling be
observed by a soils engineer during construction to see that adequate
bearing has been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are
consistent with the findings of our exploration

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling three
3 test borings to depths of 6 5 to 15 1 feet below the existing ground
surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 A
portable drill rig using 3 inch diameter continuous flight auger was used
to drill the borings
The soils encountered were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by one of our engineers who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory A graphic
presentation of the materials encountered in the borings are presented in
the Boring Iogs Plates A 1 through A 3 All soils were classified in
accordance with the Unified Soil Classification System
In the borings soil samples were cbtained by driving a 2 inch
standard split spoon sampler or a 2 4 inch I D split barrel sampler with
a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts used to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the Boring Logs at the appropriate
sample depths
