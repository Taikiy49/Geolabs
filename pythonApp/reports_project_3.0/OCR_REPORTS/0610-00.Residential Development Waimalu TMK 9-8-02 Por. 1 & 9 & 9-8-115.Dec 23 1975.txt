This report presents the results of a geologic field recon
naissance with shallow test pits on the subject site The
scope of our investigation was to evaluate the geologic and
surface soil conditions evaluate overall site stability and
present preliminary recommendations for site development
SITE LOCATION
The proposed 14 acre development site is located on the Diamond
Head slopes of Waimalu Valley Waimalu Stream intersects the
northerly portion of the site
The lower area of the site is located adjacent to Waimalu Stream
This area is covered by silty clay alluvial deposits with many
cobbles and boulders dispersed throughout the surface and below
the surface Some of the surface soils within this area are
expansive

The lower slopes of the valley appeared to be colluvial or
residual clayey silts Numerous wooden structures are lo
cated throughout the site In the north western corner of the
site is located a concrete pump house
On the eastern end of the subject property is a knoll Visual
inspection and sampling of the surface soils reveal them to be
competent residual soils clayey silts In many areas weathered
and decomposed basalt rock was observed at the surface Bedrock
may be found within ten feet or less of the existing surface
The topographic eyes of the property include very steep south
west slopes It is our understanding that a portion of these
slopes will remain in conservation area A possible slide area
was noted along the western end of this steep slope It appeared
to be localized in nature Many massive vertical basalt rock out
crops were observed on the steep slopes These steep slopes as
is the rest of the lower slopes are covered with a heavy growth
of trees and underbrush
The notable topographic features on the site are 1 the lower area
adjacent to the stream 2 the lower slopes or knoll and 3 the very
steep slopes
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on our limited field reconnaissance it is our opinion that
the site is suitable for the proposed residential construction

Slopes
We do not foresee any major construction problems relating to
slope stability that will be encountered on the site The
stream channel bank should be checked for stability and erosion
at a later date
On the lower slopes knoll area no stability problems are fore
seen The decomposed rock that will be encountered near the
surface should be rippable with conventional grading equipment
and may not require blasting It is estimated that a minimum of
8 to 10 feet of the surface soils can easily be excavated The
actual depth of unweathered rock should be determined in a future
study
Construction of structures along the base or on the steep slope
will also require additional study It is our general opinion
that the stability of the steep slopes are stable Some localized
Slide areas may be present and these areas should be located prior
to development The stability of the steep slopes will determine
the close proximity of any development to these slopes
Future Studies
Future studies will be required at a later date to further evaluate
the overall geologic and soils conditions and to provide additional
required information in order to recommend detailed foundation and
slope criteria
