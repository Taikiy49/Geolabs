INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the proposed Phase I Expansion of Mrs Fields
Macadamia Nut Plant in Kealakekua South Kona Hawaii The general
location of the site is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The
exploration was performed in general accordance with our proposal dated
This report summarizes the findings of the field exploration and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analyses based
on data obtained during the exploration These recommendations are
intended for design input and are not intended for construction bidding
estimation purposes

PROJECT CONSTDERATIONS
It is proposed to expand the Mrs Fields macadamia nut processing
plant in several phases One of the main structural elements of the phase
I expansion will be the construction of a new drying bin The structure
will be about 40 feet long and 12 feet wide in plan dimensions
It is our understanding that the bin will be a metal structure
supported by spread footing foundations Hot air will be used to process
the drying The total weight of the filled bin has been estimated at
about 210 kips
Minimal site grading is anticipated
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface soil conditions at the site in order to develop a generalized
soil rock data set for the formulation of geotechnical engineering
recommendations pertinent to the foundation design of the proposed drying
bin
The work was performed in general accordance with our proposal to
Mrs Fields Macadamia Nut Company dated The scope of
work consisted of the following
1 Drilling and sampling two 2 borings to depths of 15 and 18 5
feet below the existing ground surface
2 Reconnaissance of the project site and vicinity to make visual
observations of the surface soil conditions
3 Review of available soils and geologic information pertinent to
the project site and its vicinity

The subject site is located inside Mrs Fields Macadamia Nut Plant in
Kealakekua South Kona on the island of Hawaii Based on the plan

existing Building No 3 and bounded by an existing husking bin to the
north and a dry well to the west The general area is level The
topographic map of the area was not available at the time of our report
preparation

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration encountered a surface layer of stiff ash soils
with high in situ moisture contents overlying basalt formation
Pahoehoe We believe the upper stiff ash soils may provide adequate
support for the proposed drying bin Alternatively the footings may be
embedded deeper to bear on the basalt formation
Foundations
Conventional spread footing foundations may be used to support the
drying bin An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f with a minimm
footing embedment of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade
may be used for footings bearing on the on site stiff ash soils The
estimated settlements are on the order of less than one half of an inch
total and one quarter of an inch differential
Due to the high in situ moisture contents of the ash soils the
normal compaction standard of 95 of ASTM D 1557 78 dry density for
general footing subgrade as used in drier areas would not be readily
achieved without considerable drying of the materials Therefore it is
our opinion that the individual footing excavation should be
over excavated to a minimum depth of 6 inches and recompacted to a minimm
of 100 of the ASTM D 1557 78 dry density corresponding to its in situ
moisture content
The bearing value is for total dead plus live loads and may be
increased by one third when temporary wind and seismic loads are
considered If any soft or weak areas are encountered at the bottom of

footing excavations the soft areas should be over excavated and the
footing lowered to bear on competent material
Alternatively the footings may be embedded deeper about 4 to 5
feet to bear on the in situ basalt formation The bearing value may be
increased to 6000 p s f for footings bearing on the basalt formation
Minimal settlements are anticipated for foundations bearing on the basalt
rock formation
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive
earth pressure developed against the buried vertical faces of the
foundation system and by frictional resistance developed between the
bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be calculated using an
equivalent fluid pressure of 300 p c f This assumes that the soil around
the foundation is well compacted A coefficient of friction of 0 3 may be
used Unless covered by pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the
upper 18 inches of soil should be neglected
Site Grading
Minimal site grading is anticipated
Prior to the commencement of earthwork all areas within the grading
limits should be cleared and grubbed of all vegetation rubbish old
pavements and slabs and other deleterious materials The resulting grub
and spoil material should be disposed of properly off site Any soft
weak or yielding areas disclosed during the clearing operations should be
over excavated to firm ground and the resulting excavations should be
backfilled with well compacted engineered fill
If required new imported fill material should consist of
non expansive select granular materials less than 3 inches in maximm size
with less than 20 passing the 200 sieve The fill should be placed in

Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comments
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
_ the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this review
is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for
misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during the earthwork and
construction phases of the foundation work This is to observe compliance
with the design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow
design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from that
anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in
this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If the actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
LIMPTATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as
borings Variations of conditions between the field data points may
occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become
evident until construction If variations then appear evident it will
be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations given in this report

Field Exploration
eR Se ee
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling two
2 borings to depths of 15 0 and 18 5 feet below the existing ground
surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 A
truck mounted rotary drill rig was used to drill the boring
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by visual and
textural examination in the field by our geologist who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory The soils
were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A graphic presentation of the materials
encountered is presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 and A 2
Soils samples were obtained from the borings by driving a 2 inch
standard split spoon sampler or a 2 4 inch I D split barrel sampler with
a 140 pound hammer free falling 30 inches The blow counts to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the Boring Logs at the appropriate
sample depths
Rock core samples were obtained by using an HQ wireline core barrel
advancing diamond core bits to drill and retrieve 2 5 inch diameter core
samples The percent recovery REC and rock quality determination RQD
of each core run is shown at the appropriate depths on the Boring Logs
