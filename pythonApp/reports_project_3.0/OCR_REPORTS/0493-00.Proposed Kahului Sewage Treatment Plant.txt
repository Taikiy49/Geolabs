Geology
Maui is comprised of two major volcanoes the older West Maui
Tertiary Epoch and the more recent East Maui Haleakala
Pleistocene Epoch Located between these two volcanoes is
a narrow gently sloping plain known as the Isthmus of Maui
The Isthmus of Maui encompassing the area studied is com
prised of alluvium washed from the slopes of West Maui and
East Maui Haleakala Eustatic changes higher and lower
stands of sea level have added several factors to make the
geologic picture more complex Lithified sand dunes in the
area studied represent a lower sea level and indicate windy
dry weather conditions which blew beach sands inland The
coral found in our cores indicate a higher sea level Thus
the local geology has resulted from a marine and terrestial
environment being composed of alluvium and calcareous marine
material with a soil composition of alluvium and calcareous
marine material

Our borings reveal that the site is immediately underlain by
nonuniform loose to medium dense silty sands that vary from
1 to 12 feet in depth The upper 2 to 3 feet are generally
loose becoming denser with depth The deposition of this
silty sand has marine and terrestial origins Alluvial wash
and wave action has deposited this surficial deposit of sand
and silt Underlying the upper soils are medium dense silty
sands with finger coral fragments Lenses of fine gravel and
organic soil are interspersed throughout the encountered soils
Some highly organic soil was found in the shallow depressions
noted earlier This organic soil is about 2 feet in thick
ness The location of this area is illustrated on the Site
Plan Plate 2
Groundwater was noted in all borings at elevations 1 to 2
feet This indicates fresh brackish water floating on sea
water These levels will be under tidal influence

General
It is our recommendation that the structures be supported on
spread foundations bearing in the medium dense silty sand
located at about 2 feet below the existing ground surface
Foundations
The medium dense silty sand found from 2 to 3 feet below the
existing surface will provide adequate bearing for building
pump and tank foundations A design bearing pressure of
1500 pounds per square foot dead plus live loads may be
used for footings placed a minimum of 30 inches below final
adjacent grade A one third increase of this value may be
used when considering wind and seismic loadings It is our
recommendation that all footing excavation bottoms be tamped
prior to placing steel or concrete to ensure adequate uniform
bearing
Lateral loads may be resisted by either passive pressures
developed by the soil or by friction along the base of foot
ings For design purposes the passive pressure of soils
may be assumed to be 250 pounds per square foot of depth The
coéfficient of friction between the footings and the soil will
be 0 45
Due to the existence of local soft areas and lenses of organic
matter in the upper sands it is our opinion that all footing

When encountered local soft areas should be compacted to a

Materials should be removed and replaced with approved material
Settlements
Maximum ultimate settlements on the order of one quarter inch
may be expected for the design loadings Differential settle
ments will be minimal
Slab On Grade
Slab on grade for buildings and tanks can be supported on
natural undisturbed soil or engineered fill or on a combina
tion of both
The subgrade should be proof rolled to remove local soft areas
In any areas where dampness in the floor slab would be undesir
able or where moisture susceptible floor coverings are to be
used a minimum of four inches of gravel should be placed
beneath the slab to act as a capillary break In addition a
moisture proof membrane should be placed over the gravel to
act as a vapor barrier The membrane should be covered by one
inch of sand to protect it during construction and to aid in
curing the concrete

The site soils are highly permeable and is therefore recommended
that impermeable linings be used for the holding pond and other
basins
Site Preparation
After all debris and trees have been removed from the site the
ground surface should be stripped to remove all vegetation All
exposed fill areas should be scarified to a minimum depth of
six inches brought to near optimum moisture and compacted to
at least 90 of maximum density as determined by ASTM D 1557 70
Fill may then be placed in loose nine inch lifts and also com
pacted to a minimum of 90 maximum density In cut areas at
grade the exposed surface should be compacted to remove local
soft areas
We recommend that all site preparation and grading be done under
the continuous supervision of a qualified soils engineer to
ascertain organic peaty soils are removed before fills are placed
and to inspect that the fills are compacted properly
Removal of the peat material in the depression parallel to the
existing perimeter road may prove to be difficult and expensive
All imported fill material should be approved by the soils
engineer prior to bringing on the site

APPENDIX A
FIELD INVESTIGATION
The subsurface soil conditions at the site were explored by
drilling six test borings utilizing truck mounted rotary hollow
stem auger equipment All borings were drilled to depths of 30
feet below the existing ground surface The locations of the
borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate l
The soils were classified by visual and textural examinations
in the field by our engineer who continuously supervised the
drilling operations The classifications were verified by in
spection and testing in the laboratory All soils were classified
in accordance with the Unified Soil Classification System
Graphical representations of the encountered soils are presented
in the Boring Logs Plates A 1A through A 1F Soil samples
were obtained by driving a sampler with a 140 pound hammer free
falling a distance of 30 inches The blow counts to drive the
sampler are shown on the Log of Borings at the appropriate sample
depth
