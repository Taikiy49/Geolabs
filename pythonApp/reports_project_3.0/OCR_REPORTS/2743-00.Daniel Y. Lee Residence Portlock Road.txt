INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of a geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Daniel Y Lee residence located at a double lot 327 331
Portlock Road in the Maunalua Beach Subdivision on the island of Oahu Hawaii The
general location of the site and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1

This report summaries our findings and geotechnical engineering recommenda
tions resulting from the field exploration laboratory testing and engineering analyses
These recommendations are intended for foundation design input and may not be
appropriate to form the basis for preparation of construction cost estimates or for
construction bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site consists of two 2 lots which are currently occupied by single
family residences each with a swimming pool It is proposed to combine the two 2
lots demolish the existing structures and swimming pools and to construct a large
single family residence at the project site
It is anticipated that the proposed residence will be a two 2 story structure
basically wood framed with some steel framing and concrete slab on grade floors It is
our understanding that the finish floor elevations will vary from seven 7 0 to eight and
one half 8 5 feet above Mean Sea Level MSL for various portions of the proposed
residence The project will also involve construction of a swimming pool a tennis
court and vehicular pavements
The site is essentially level with existing ground elevations ranging from
approximately five and one half 5 5 to seven 7 0 feet above MSL Therefore cut
and fill operations for site grading to provide anticipated finish grades will be minimal
At the time of this report completion it is our understanding that the proposed
house will be moved another fifteen 15 feet mauka in order to increase the set back
distance from the western property limit fronting the beach reserve It is our opinion

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions within the project site in order to develop a generalized soils data set for the
formulation of geotechnical engineering design recommendations pertinent to the
proposed construction
The work was done in accordance with our fee proposal to Mr Daniel Y Lee
dated The scope of work included the following tasks and work efforts
1 Review of available soils and geologic information pertinent to the project
site and its vicinity
2 Reconnaissance of the project site and its vicinity to make visual obser
vation of the surface soil conditions and to select accessible locations for
the borings to maximize the value of the information obtained
3 Mobilization and demobilization of a truck mounted drill rig and a hand
carried portable drill rig to and from the project site
4 Drilling and sampling of three 3 borings to depths of sixteen and one
half 16 5 feet below the existing ground surface using auger drilling
techniques
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2743 00 Page 4
5 Laboratory testing of selected samples obtained during the field explora
tion to determine the classification and the engineering properties of the
materials encountered
6 Engineering analyses of the field and laboratory data for the purpose of
developing geotechnical engineering design recommendations
7 Preparation of this report summarizing our findings and presenting our
recommendations
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration and laboratory testing are present
ed in the appendices of this report

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling three 3 borings to
depths of sixteen and one half 16 5 feet below the existing ground surface The
approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general the borings disclosed that the site is underlain by a surface layer
approximately five 5 to six 6 feet thick of loose to medium dense brown sand
Underneath the surface sand layer loose gray sand and coralline gravel with silt were
encountered to a depth of 16 5 feet the maximum drilled depth
Groundwater was encountered in the drilled borings at depths of four and
during our field exploration Based on elevations estimated from the topographic
survey map prepared by R M Towill Corporation dated the measured
groundwater level varied from about one 1 0 to one and two tenths 1 2 feet above
MSL Due to the proximity of the site to the bay it is anticipated that the groundwater
level will fluctuate in response to seasonal tides and storm surges
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
It is Our opinion that the proposed project is feasible from a geotechnical
engineering point of view provided that the recommendations presented below are
followed
Foundations
Shallow spread and continuous footing foundations may be used to support the
proposed residence
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2743 00 Page 6
An allowable bearing value of 2 000 p s f can be used for footings bearing on
the compacted in situ sand or compacted engineered embankment A footing
embedment of twelve 12 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade is recom
mended Deeper footing embedment is generally not recommended due to the high
groundwater level encountered in the drilled borings
Steel framed construction should be minimized in order to limit the foundation
loads imposed by the heavy weight of steel members and thereby reducing the
required footing size In general footing settlement is anticipated to increase with
increasing footing size for the soils condition encountered in the field exploration
It is recommended that all footing excavations should be compacted to a
minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density as determined in accordance with ASTM
Test Method D 1557 78
If loose sand lenses or pockets are encountered at the bottoms of footing
excavations they should be over excavated to dense in situ sand The resulting
depression should then be backfilled with select granular material compacted to a
minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density as determined in accordance with ASTM
Test Method D 1557 78
If a footing is located next to a utility line the utility trench backfill should be
carefully monitored and adequately compacted to reduce the potential for footing
settlement due to consolidation of the trench backfill
Surface water should be diverted away from the foundations to avoid ponding
of surface runoff water that may adversely affect the bearing capacity of the soils
below the foundations

Total footing settlement for column loads up to thirty two 32 kins is estimated
to be less than one 1 inch provided the above recommendations are followed
Slab on Grade
The ground floors of the proposed residence are anticipated to be concrete
slab on grade
A four 4 inch thick concrete slab on top of a minimum four 4 inch thick
cushion layer of No 3 B Fine gravel or ASTM C 33 No 67 gravel is recommended In
order to protect against moisture infiltration a plastic vapor barrier should be incorpo
rated below the slab One 1 inch of moist fine sand may be placed above the plastic
membrane to help curing of the concrete
The thickened edges of the concrete slab should be embedded a minimum of
twelve 12 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade
Concrete Driveway
It is anticipated that concrete pavements may be provided for the driveway of
the proposed residence

Joints are recommended at about eight 8 to ten 10 foot intervals and at

some relative movement of the concrete slabs The length to width ratios of the slabs
should not exceed one and one quarter 1 25 to one 1 Slabs exceeding this ratio
or which are otherwise not rectangular in shape should be provided with welded wire
mesh reinforcement
Swimming Pool
A swimming pool is to be constructed at the westerly side of the proposed
residence The on site sandy soil is medium dense to loose and is highly permeable
Therefore it is our opinion that excavation shoring and substantial dewatering will be
required for the proposed swimming pool construction if the pool bottom is below the
groundwater level
The groundwater level was encountered in our drilled borings at depths of four
surface Based on elevations estimated from the available topographic survey map
the measured groundwater levels ranged from elevations of about one 1 to one and
two tenths 1 2 feet above MSL at the time of our field exploration
The swimming pool design should include provision for resistance against
hydrostatic uplift during periods when the partially submerged swimming pool is
emptied for maintenance purposes A minimum groundwater level elevation of two 2
feet above MSL is recommended for uplift resistance design purposes

Site Grading
Prior to construction the site should be cleared and grubbed Any deleterious
materials such as vegetation debris construction rubble and organic matter encoun
tered during grubbing and scarifying should be removed and properly disposed of off
GEOLABS HAWAIl

site It is our understanding that the two 2 existing swimming pools will be demol
ished The concrete and miscellaneous rubble generated by the pool demolition
should be properly disposed of off site Any material that may slough into the hole
should be removed and the bottom of the hole recompacted to a minimum density of
90 of the maximum dry density as determined in accordance with ASTM Test
Method D 1557 78 The sloughed material if considered to be suitable may then be
returned to the hole distributed uniformly and compacted as indicated above If
considered to be unsuitable it should be removed and disposed of properly off site
The depressions thus prepared should then be backfilled with on site sandy soils or
imported select fill material as recommended below If backfill under groundwater level
is required self compacting granular material such as No 3B Fine gravel or ASTM
C 33 No 67 is recommended
The proposed building and pavement areas should be scarified to a depth of
six 6 inches after site clearing and grubbing The scarified subgrade should be
thoroughly moisture conditioned and recompacted to obtain a minimum of 90 of the
maximum dry density as determined in accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
To minimize disturbance to adjacent existing residences and to minimize pore water
pressure build up in sandy subsoils causing possible soil weakening use of heavy
vibratory compaction equipment should be avoided
Any imported fill and backfill material that may be required should be tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to delivery to the site
The fills and backfills should be placed in loose horizontal lifts not exceeding ten
10 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 of the maximum
dry density as determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78

Drainage
The finished lot grade outside the footings slabs and pavements should be
shaped to shed water away from foundations to avoid ponding conditions Also it is
advised that a gutter system be installed around the residence and that the gutter
run off water be diverted as far away from the foundations and pavements as feasible
Excessive landscape watering near the foundations should also be avoided
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have concrete bottoms
and drains to prevent water from entering the foundation and slab subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
above recommended foundation construction since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The
foundation excavations should be properly backfilled tight against the footings and
slabs immediately after setting of the concrete to prevent ponding conditions
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained to drain
all surface run off away from the foundations and slabs

Design Review
Drawings and specifications for site grading utility and foundation construction
should be forwarded to the project geotechnical engineer for review and written
comments prior to advertisement for bids This review is needed to determine
adherence to the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this
review is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for
misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the footing excavations and backfilling be observed by a
competent geotechnical engineer during construction to see that adequate bearing
has been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings
of our exploration
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and approved
by the soils engineer prior to delivery to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the above
observations
