INTRUMENTATION MONITORING
As mentioned in our previous report three slope inclinometers were installed in
Boring Nos 1 through 3 drilled in November 2001 as shown on the Site Plan Plate 1 to
depths of approximately 100 feet each in order to measure the lateral deflection of the soil
at various depths An additional inclinometer was installed in Boring No 4 drilled in
September 2003 as shown on the Site Plan Plate 1 to a depth of approximately 100 feet
This additional inclinometer was installed to better delineate the slide limits

It should be noted that the inclinometers recorded the lateral earth movement in
two directions perpendicular to Mona Street A Axis and parallel to Mona Street B Axis
as shown on Plates 2 through 5 Based on our review of the inclinometer data recorded
to date it appears that Inclinometer Nos I 1 3 and I 4 indicated some shallow depth
subsurface ground movement at their respective instrument locations However
Inclinometer Nos I 2 did not indicate a pattern that would suggest significant subsurface
ground lateral movement is occurring at this time It may be too early to conclude based
on the limited monitoring period and locations In addition it should be recognized that
one of the factors that cause significant ground lateral movement is rainfall Based on our
research it appeared that Mona Street has not experienced significant rainfall in the short
time during our monitoring period
Four electrical vibrating wire piezometers were installed in the borings P 1 and
P 2 as shown on the Site Plan Plate 1 to measure the fluctuation of groundwater levels
in response to weather conditions The piezometers were installed at two different depths
within the boreholes in order to intercept perched water levels
Our monitoring of the four piezometers installed to date Boring Nos P 1 and P 2
indicates some significant subsurface water seepage at their respective screen intervals

SUMMARY
Inclinometer Nos I 1 I 3 and I 4 indicated some shallow depth subsurface ground
movement at their respective instrument locations However Inclinometer Nos 2 did not
indicate a pattern that would suggest significant subsurface ground lateral movement is
occurring at this time It may be too early to conclude based on the limited monitoring
period and locations Our piezometer data indicates that subsurface water seepage
occurred at the project site Based on our pavement deformation mapping we believe
that lateral earth movement at the site is still active
Based on the interpretation from Inclinometer Nos I 2 and I 3 it appears that the
sliding zone may be relatively shallow in the area near Boring Nos B 2 and B 3
Recently installed Inclinometer 4 is located between Inclinometer Nos I 2 and I 3
During the last six month monitoring Inclinometer No I 4 indicated the sliding zone may
be located slightly deeper than the indication from I 2 and 1 3 During the following
monitoring period Inclinometer No I 4 may be able to further define the depth of the
movement and determine feasible remediation measures for the proposed project
