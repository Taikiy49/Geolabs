REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from two shield
volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be middle to
late Pliocene in age and forms the bulk of the western third of the island The younger
shield Koolau is estimated to be late Pliocene to early Pleistocene Ice Age in age and
forms the majority of the eastern two thirds of the island Waianae became extinct while
Koolau was still active and its eastern flank was partially buried below Koolau lavas banking
against its eastern flank These banked or ponded lavas formed a broad plateau referred
to as the Schofield Plateau Both Waianae and Koolau Ranges have lost most of their
original shield outlines and are now long narrow ridges shaped largely by erosion
The project area is generally composed of basaltic rock built by the extrusion of lavas
of the Koolau Volcanic Series These rocks are generally characterized by flows of jointed
dense vesicular basalt with interbedded thin clinker layers In situ chemical weathering of
the Koolau lava flows has occurred during the last 1 to 2 million years The weathering
process has formed a mantle of residual soils and saprolite In general saprolite is
composed mainly of silty material and is typical of the tropical weathering of volcanic rocks
The saprolite grades to basaltic rock formation with depth Man made fills were placed
during commercial development in the area in recent times and brought the project site to its
current conditions

Building Foundations
As previously mentioned the proposed auto parts store will consist of a one story
building with approximately 7 000 square feet of floor space Based on the subsurface
conditions encountered in our exploration we recommend that a shallow foundation
system consisting of spread and or continuous footings be used to support the proposed
structure An allowable bearing pressure of up to 3 000 pounds per square foot psf may
be used for the design of footings bearing on the stiff to hard silty clays encountered in our
borings This allowable bearing pressure is for dead plus live loads and may be increased
by one third 1 3 for transient loads such as those caused by temporary wind and or
seismic forces Footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grade
As previously indicated several retaining walls are currently present at the project site
In order to avoid surcharging the existing retaining walls if they are not demolished the new
building foundations should be located far enough away from the wall to provide a minimum
horizontal set back distance equal to the depth of the retaining wall from the back side of the
wali As an alternative to the horizontal set back distance the footings for the new building
should be embedded deeper below a 45 degree imaginary plane extending up from the heel
of the existing retaining wall footing
Where footings are located adjacent to other below grade structures or utilities the
footings should extend to a depth below an imaginary 45 degree plane projected upward
from the bottom of the below grade structure or utility This requirement is necessary to
avoid surcharging adjacent below grade structures with additional structural loads and to
reduce the potential for foundation settlement

lf the foundations are designed and constructed in accordance with our
recommendations we estimate that the total footing settlements under the anticipated design
loads to be on the order of less than 1 inch with differential settlements of approximately
0 5 inches
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by frictional resistance between
the base of the footings and the bearing materials and by passive earth pressure developed
against the near vertical faces of the embedded portion of footings A coefficient of friction
of 0 35 may be used between the base of the footings and the supporting materials
Resistance due to passive pressure may be calculated using an equivalent fluid pressure of

silty clays This assumes that the soils around the footings are well compacted or that the
concrete for footings is placed directly against the footing excavations Unless covered by
slabs or pavements the passive resistance in the upper 12 inches below the finished grade
should be neglected
Footing excavations should be observed by a representative of Geolabs Hawaii prior
to placement of reinforcing steel or concrete to confirm the competency of the bearing
materials and embedment depths Footing subgrades should be firm and unyielding It
should be noted that some medium stiff silty clay fills were encountered in the eastern portion
of the building site Boring Nos 5 and 6 If unsuitable materials are encountered in the
footing excavations the unsuitable materials should be over excavated to expose firm soils
and replaced with well compacted fill
Slabs On Grade
We anticipate that concrete slabs on grade will be used for the proposed auto parts
store building The near surface soils encountered in our borings generally consist of silty
clays which exhibit a moderate expansion potential when subjected to moisture fluctuations
Therefore to reduce the potential for structural distress to the lightly loaded slabs we
recommend that a minimum of 12 inches of non expansive select granular fill material be
provided below the concrete slab The non expansive fill should extend a minimum of 2 feet
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 4215 00 B Page 9
laterally beyond the edges of the slab The clayey subgrades for slabs on grades should be
scarified to a depth of at least 8 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the
optimum moisture and compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction Relative
compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the
maximum dry density of the same soil determined in accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 1557 91 Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight
corresponding to the maximum dry density
The non expansive select granular fill recommended below the floor slabs should
consist of imported granular material such as crushed coral basalt gravel or cinder sand
The material should be well graded from coarse to fine with no particles larger than 3 inches
in largest dimension It should also contain between 10 and 30 percent particles passing the
No 200 sieve The material should have a laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR value
of 20 or higher and a swell potential of 1 percent or less when tested in accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1883 The select granular fill materials should be
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content placed in 8 inch level loose
lifts and compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction The slab subgrades should
be kept moist until covered by the select granular fill
To provide uniform support for the slabs we recommend that the concrete
slabs on grade be supported on a minimum 4 inch cushion fill over the 12 inches of
non expansive select granular fill The cushion fill layer should consist of open graded
gravels such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation which would also serve
as a capillary moisture break
For slabs on grade with moisture sensitive surfacing we recommend that an
impermeable membrane be placed over the cushion fill to reduce the potential for migration
of moisture vapor through the concrete siab In order to promote uniform curing of the slab
concrete and to provide protection of the vapor membrane placement of a 2 inch layer of
moist fine sand may be considered above the membrane If the 2 inch sand layer is
specified the sand should be moistened slightly prior to placing the slab concrete

Retaining Structures
As previously mentioned we anticipate that some retaining structures such as the
retaining walls at the west and northwest portions of the site will be required for this project

Retaining Structure Foundations
In general we believe that the retaining structure foundations may be designed in
accordance with the Building Foundations section of this report The foundations
should have a minimum width of 18 inches and should be embedded a minimum
depth of 24 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade For sloping ground
conditions the footings should extend deeper to obtain a minimum 6 foot set back
distance measured horizontally from the outside edge of the footing to the face of the
slope Footings oriented parallel to the direction of the slope should be constructed
in stepped footings

Corrosion Evaluation

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling
six borings extending to depths of about 5 5 to 16 5 feet below the existing ground surface
at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were drilled using
a truck mounted drill rig equipped with continuous flight solid stem augers
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by testing
in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representations of the materials
encountered are provided on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 6
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by
driving a 2 inch OD standard penetration sampler or a 3 inch OD split barrel sampler with a
140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the last
12 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample
depths
Pocket penetrometer tests were performed on selected cohesive soil samples
retrieved in the field The pocket penetrometer test provides an indication of the
unconfined compressive strength of the soil sample Results of the pocket penetrometer
tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content ASTM D 2216 and unit weight determinations were performed on
selected soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties The
results of these tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Two one inch ring swell tests were performed on selected soil samples to evaluate
the swelling potential of the near surface soils The test results are summarized on
Plate B 1
One Atterberg Limits test ASTM D 4318 was performed on a selected sample of the
soils to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits and to aid in soil classification Results of the test
are summarized on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depth Graphic
presentation of the test results is provided on Plate B 2
One California Bearing Ratio CBR test ASTM D 1883 was performed on a bulk
sample of the near surface soils to evaluate the strength characteristics for pavement
subgrade support The results of the CBR test are presented on Plate B 3
One Modified Proctor compaction test ASTM D 1557 91 was performed on the
near surface soils to evaluate the moisture content and dry density relationship Results of
the test are presented on Plate B 4
One set of chemical analysis tests including Chlorides EPA Test Method 325 2 and
Sulfates EPA Test Method 375 2 was performed on a selected sample of the soils to
evaluate the corrosion potential The chemical analysis tests were performed by
Environmental Laboratory of the Pacific Results of the tests are summarized on Plate B 5
