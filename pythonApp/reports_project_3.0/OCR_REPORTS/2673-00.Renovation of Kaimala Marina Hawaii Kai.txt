INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Renovation of Kaimala Marina in Hawaii Kai area on the
island of Oahu The general location of the site and its vicinity is shown on the Project
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2673 00 Page 2
Location Map Plate 1 The exploration was performed in general accordance with our
proposal to the Association of Owners of Kaimala Marina dated March 25 1991
This report summarizes the findings of our field exploration and our geotechnical
engineering recommendations derived from our analyses based on the data obtained
during the exploration and laboratory testing These recommendations are intended for
preliminary design input and are not intended to form the basis for preparation of
construction cost estimates or for bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
Based on information provided to us the proposed renovation will include the
demolition of the existing piers and their replacement with new floating structures
Detailed design of the new construction is not available at this time
It is Our understanding that the renovation project includes only the construction
of new floating docks no other work is currently planned along the existing marina wall
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions at the project site in order to develop a generalized soil rock data set for the
formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to foundation design
of the project
The work was done in accordance with our proposal to Association of Owners of
Kaimala Marina dated March 25 1991 The scope of work consisted of the following

in Hawaii Kai on the island of Oahu Hawaii Currently the existing piers are fixed and
supported on timber piles The land side of the project site is occupied by existing
residential development

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration generally encountered competent volcanic tuff below about
four 4 feet of very soft mud It is our opinion that predrilling into the underlying volcanic
tuff will be required for pile foundations in order to provide sufficient embedment and
adequate capacity against anticipated lateral loads to restrain the new floating docks
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2673 00 Page 5
Pile Foundations
Pile foundations are currently planned to provide support for the floating docks
Due to the potential for corrosion of metal in the marine environment it appears that
concrete piles are more suitable than steel pipe piles
We believe that 12 inch square precast and prestressed concrete piles may be used
Lateral Capacity and Pile Length
The project structural engineer has indicated that the design lateral load will get on
the piles at a point 3 5 feet above MSL and that the maximum allowable moment
of the 12 inch square concrete piles is 38 ft kips Using this information and the
soil strength parameters derived from our exploration and testing we believe that
a lateral pile capacity of two 2 kips per pile may be used A maximum bending
moment on the order of 34 ft kips is anticipated The point of induced maximum
moment may be assumed to occur at about 7 5 feet below the mudline along the
pile A pile length of 30 feet may be used for preliminary budgeting purposes
Predrilling
Neglecting the resistance of about four 4 feet of very soft clayey silt below
mudline it appears that the unsupported length of the piles below the point of
load application may be taken approximately 14 5 feet Since the piles will be
subjected to repeated lateral loads from the impact of boats sufficient embedment
of the piles below the mudiline is required to provide the requisite lateral support
Based on the exploration data the competent volcanic tuff which was encountered
at relatively shallow depths below the very soft deposits may preclude sufficient
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2673 00 Page 6
penetration by driving alone to reach the required embedment In addition it is
possible that random boulders resulting from the previous construction of the
marina wall may be present below the mudline and may obstruct pile driving
adjacent to the wall Therefore predrilling through the underlying dense volcanic
tuff formation is recommended to assure attainment of the required pile
embedment
It is estimated that a minimum predrill depth of 16 feet below the mudline will be
required at each pile location A minimum diameter of 18 inches is recommended
for the predrill auger The predrilling will facilitate installation of the piles in the tuff
to assure development of the required lateral capacity
Construction Considerations
It is our understanding that construction of the floating docks will have to be
performed from a barge at the Kuapa Pond side due to the presence of the
existing on shore development It is recommended that qualified piling contractors
be contacted for realistic cost estimation for this operation
Also anticipated is a requirement for temporary installation of templates supported
on steel H piles for positioning the permanent concrete piles
For design of the floating docks pile guides are anticipated to be installed around
permanent piles Due to the need of predrilling and the fact that the predrill auger
will be larger in diameter than the cross section of the concrete piles it is
recommended that the proposed individual pile guide openings on the docks be
sufficiently large to provide ample space for the predrilling auger at the pile
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2673 00 Page 7
locations and subsequent placement of the piles Pile guides and rollers may be
attached after pile driving and removal of the temporary set up
Concerns Of Driving Piles From A Barge
Due to the presence of existing buildings it is anticipated that pile driving will have
to be performed from a barge on the Kuapa Pond side of the piers a costly operation
It is also our understanding that the design of pile guides for the floating docks is made
more complicated due to the need for placement of templates for predrilling at individual
pile locations
In addition the water depth measured during our field exploration was found to
be only seven 7 feet at a distance of about twenty 20 feet away from the marina wall
Thus it is uncertain whether the water depths particularly within a few feet of the wall will
be sufficient to allow positioning of a barge for pile driving operations
Finally pile driving operations from a barge will inevitably result in interference with
boating operations for an extended period of time requiring considerable negotiation and
coordination with local residents and with the Hawaii Kai Marine Association
Alternate Foundation System
Based on the above concerns regarding construction it is our opinion that
additional evaluation of other alternatives such as land based restraining systems
extending from the marina wall to provide lateral support of the docks in lieu of driven
piles should be made
Additional consultation on the final design is anticipated to be required It is
recommended that the geotechnical engineer be consulted when further information
regarding the restraining system is available

Design Review
Drawings and specifications for the proposed construction should be forwarded
to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comments prior to construction This
review is needed to determine adherence to the foundation recommendations given
herein If this review is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility
for misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during pile driving and construction of the foundations
This is to observe compliance with the design concepts specifications or
recommendations and to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions
differ from that anticipated at the time of preparation of this report The recommendations
given in this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and approved by
the geotechnical engineer prior to delivery to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during construction are different
from those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made

depth
