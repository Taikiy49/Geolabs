This report summarizes our groundwater sampling and testing services in support

with our fee proposal dated The analytical laboratory testing of the
groundwater sample was performed by Environmental Laboratory of the Pacific
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located in the Waikiki area of the District of Honolulu on the
island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on
the Project Location Map Plate 1
Based on the information provided it is desired to construct approximately
850 lineal feet of new 8 inch water main and appurtenances along the northern side of
Date Street extending from Isenberg Street to University Avenue The proposed new
water main alignment includes the crossing of the existing Ala Naio Stream drainage
culvert
It is anticipated that some dewatering of excavations may be necessary during
construction of the proposed new water main Because the excavation dewatering may
involve the discharge of groundwater effluent into an adjacent storm drainage system an
NPDES permit may be required In order to obtain general baseline groundwater quality
data for the preparation of the permit application it is desired to sample and test the
groundwater underlying the subject site

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our work on this phase of the project was to provide the monitoring
well installation and groundwater sampling and testing services necessary to obtain
baseline water quality data in support of the preparation of an NPDES permit application
for construction dewatering activities Our scope of work generally consisted of the
following tasks and work efforts some of which were also performed in conjunction with
our geotechnical engineering exploration for the project
1 Filing of permit applications for street usage excavation of the public
right of way and clearance from underground utilities In addition traffic
control safety measures were implemented during the course of the field
work
2 Mobilization and demobilization of a truck mounted drill rig and operators to
and from the project site
3 Drilling and sampling of a boring to a depth of about 21 5 feet below the
existing ground surface The boring was drilled using environmental drilling
and sampling protocol
4 Conversion of the boring to an environmental groundwater monitoring well
of approximately 15 feet in depth
5 Collection of groundwater samples from the monitoring well by a Geologist
from our firm The chain of custody documentation special handling and
transportation of the water samples to the laboratory were also performed
by our Geologist
6 Analytical laboratory testing of the groundwater samples for the following
constituents NOI Form A Part A Water Quality Constituents and selected
NOI Form A Part B Toxic Constituents including testing for the presence
of organochlorine pesticides and PCB s chlorinated herbicides and
organonitrogen pesticides as directed by the Owner Client
7 Preparation of this report summarizing our work on this phase of the project
and presenting our findings and laboratory test results
8 Coordination of our overall work on the project by a Project Geologist from
our firm

SITE CONDITIONS
The proposed new water main will be located along the northern ewa bound traffic
lane of Date Street within the Waikiki area of Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii The
project limits consist of the Isenberg Street intersection to the west and University Avenue
intersection to the east The project site resides within a residential area
The ground surface along the proposed Date Street alignment consists of asphalt
concrete pavement and is generally level with elevations on the order of about
4 to 5 feet Mean Sea Level MSL A slight crown in the roadway appears to shed
runoff to the perimeter gutters leading to nearby storm drains The storm drains appear to
empty into the Ala Naio Stream channel which traverses the site
Monitoring Weil installation
The drilling well installation and well development tasks were conducted on
February 5 Additional well development was conducted on February 10 after allowing
the well to stabilize for several days The groundwater monitoring well consists of 2 inch
diameter PVC pipes installed to a depth of about 15 feet below the pavement surface
The well generally consists of about 10 feet of perforated casing and about 5 feet of solid
casing extending to just below the pavement ground surface where a locking plug caps
the PVC pipe The surface completion consists of a flush mount traffic rated cover that is
cemented flush with the pavement surface
Drilling of the monitoring well was accomplished by using a truck mounted drill rig
and associated drilling and sampling tools were washed by steam cleaning prior to the
drilling of the borehole In addition the sampling tools were washed with an Alconox
detergent solution prior to each soil sampling event
The monitoring well is identified as B 1 and the approximate location is shown on
the Site Plan Plate 2 A detail of the monitoring well installation is provided as Plate 3 of
this report

Groundwater Conditions
Relatively shallow groundwater conditions were measured during the well
installation and groundwater sampling effort Due to the relatively close proximity of
Ala Naio Stream and it s hydrogeologic relationship with the nearby coastline and ocean
environment in combination with the generally permeable coralline subsoil conditions it
is likely that groundwater levels measured in the monitoring well will be affected by tidal
fluctuation of the sea level In addition the groundwater levels at the project site may be
influenced by seasonal precipitation and storm surge conditions
Based on our review of the analytical laboratory results it appears that no
significant adverse water quality conditions were detected in the analyses of the water
samples that were tested The water sample test results are provided in Appendix B and
include the official laboratory report and the Department of Health NPDES NOI Form A
Most of the test results for the NOI Form A Part A Water Quality Constituents
appear to be within normal values for typical brackish groundwater However the total
suspended solids and turbidity values appear to be higher than expected The higher
than expected values may be partially due to the groundwater sampling operations
despite normal care was taken in the sampling operations

Abandonment of Monitoring Well
Abandonment of the monitoring well is not included as part of our current scope of
work therefore we recommend that abandonment of the monitoring well be included in
the construction documents In general the monitoring well should be properly
abandoned and sealed with cement grout

reevaluate the variations

monitoring well should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods
used

and interpretations made in the preparation of this report However it must be noted that
fluctuation may occur due to variations in tides rainfall temperature and other factors
