Project Considerations
The project site consists of approximately 23 acres of former sugar plantation land
being developed for residential and community use purposes The development involves
multi family buildings and detached cluster type housing units The project also includes
an adult day care center and a senior community center with swimming pool and spa
amenities Infrastructure includes roadways underground utilities e g water sewer
electric communications and lighting as well as parking areas
The major portion of earthwork consisted of cutting down of existing soil berms
and piles at the eastern portion of the site along with filling of a previously existing
channel along the southern boundary of the eastern side The layout of the project is
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services for
the earthwork site grading and utility construction at the project site

Earthwork Site Grading and Utility Construction
The earthwork for the Franciscan Vistas Ewa Phase 1 generally consisted of
cutting down of existing soil berms and piles at the eastern portion of the site along with
filling of a previously existing channel along the southern boundary of the eastern side
The work also involved the removal and backfilling of abandoned utility lines The work
was performed by During the earthwork and related operations
samples of the on site and imported materials utilized as fill and backfill materials were
obtained and transported to our office for testing The maximum dry densities and
optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general accordance with
ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor The laboratory test results are summarized on Plate 3
The fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to near optimum moisture
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as
determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557 In the roadway areas the fill and backfill
materials within 2 feet of the finished subgrade were compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative density In addition select borrow and base course materials below
the pavement areas were compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative density
During the earthwork periodic field density tests were performed in general
accordance with ASTM D 6938 Nuclear Gauge Method Results of the compaction tests
performed are summarized on Plates 4 1 through 4 47 of this report These tests were
performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills at the
site Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site
