PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Westin Ka anapali Ocean Resort KOR North Villas Expansion project is in
the Kaanapali area on the Island of Maui Hawaii In general the project site is to the
north of the existing Westin Kaanapali Ocean Resort Villas project and adjacent to and on
the west side of the existing Honoapiilani Highway in the project vicinity
The project consisted of the construction of four main residential buildings
Buildings 5 through 8 with a parking structure on the northeast side of the property
adjacent to the flanks of Buildings 6 and 7 The new residential buildings of six stories
each generally consist of approximately 258 dwelling units for the timeshare condominium
project The ground floor level of the residential buildings is set at about 14 feet Mean
Sea Level MSL The parking structure for the project is a four level structure with the
lowest level of the parking structure set at 8 8 feet MSL
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services
pertaining to the site grading operations and infrastructure installation for the project site
SITE GRADING AND UTILITY INSTALLATION
Geolabs observed the site grading and utility installation work from to
The overall process of site grading was divided into two phases
referenced Geolabs Reports as W O 5403 10 and 5403 30 The first phase consisted of
the general mass grading of the 10 acre site for the building pad fill and cuts as well as
the construction of geotextile fabric retaining walls Phase 2 consisted of the installation of
infrastructure including roadways utilities and slab on grade preparations

The Phase 1 site mass grading work consisted of clearing and grubbing and
placement and compaction of fills from a borrow site on the order of about 1 to 7 feet thick
to achieve the design finished grades Kiewit Pacific Construction Inc performed the site
mass grading and retaining wall work
The mass grading operations were carried out using heavy machinery equipment
such as semi trailers Caterpillar Dozers Sheepsfoot Compactors Graders and a water
truck for dust control and moisture conditioning of the fill materials These types of
equipment are typical and considered as suitable for the import placement and
compaction of the fill materials
Additional Recommendations During Site Construction
During the excavation of the parking structure soft soggy soil ground conditions
were encountered at an elevation of about It appeared that a confined fresh to
brackish water table was present in the vicinity of the parking garage area at about 5 5 to
Therefore meetings took place with the design engineers to recommend raising
the elevation of the ground finish floor elevation from 4 0 to This would reduce
long term problem issues with the concrete slab as well as significantly reduce the
additional change order charges for working under the water table
Next the subgrade soil material at the elevation of was cement treated
as well as air dried to lower the moisture content such that a minimum of 90 compaction
could be achieved The following layer consisted of a state select borrow granular fill
material at 12 inches in thickness and compacted to a minimum of 95 compaction
efforts in general accordance with ASTM D1557 Following the state select borrow
material a 6 inch thick reinforced concrete slab was placed for the finish first floor of the
parking structure
Later during the site infrastructure work soft soggy soils were again encountered
during the installation of the various subterranean utility lines The contractors who
encountered the unsuitable soggy soils were first with Kiewit Pacific with the drain line A
work during the last stages of Phase 1 and later with Hawaiian Dredging Construction
Company and their sub contractor Elima Engineering Inc during phase 2 Since the
presence of the soft soggy soils at the various utility line inverts would potentially cause
excessive settlement of the placed utility lines it was recommended to over excavate a
depth of 1 to 2 feet below the line s invert place uniform crushed rock gravel wrapped in
a geotextile fabric of murify T140 or better up to the original utility line s invert Then the
backfill operations continued as usual
During the site work operations samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test
results are summarized on Plates A and B

The fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as
determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557 In the roadway areas the fill and backfill
materials within 2 feet of the finished subgrade were compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative density
During the site grading and utility backfilling operations periodic field density tests
were performed in general accordance with ASTM D 2922 Nuclear Gauge Method
Results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates A 1 through A 16
and B 1 through B 113 of this report These tests were performed on the prepared ground
surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills at the site The test locations and elevations
were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations
and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods
used Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum
compaction was obtained and or approved by our field representative based on visual
observation in place field density testing and hand probe observations
Our services consisted of full time field observation of the contractor s fill
placement subgrade preparation and compaction procedures and confirmation of
subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above grading operations
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests our opinion is that the site grading and utility installation work for the
above project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report were
performed in conformance with the general intent of the recommendations in the following
references
