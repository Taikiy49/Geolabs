INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the four 4 proposed buildings and pavement areas of the Proposed
Kuilima Bay Golf Maintenance Area in Kahuku on the island of Oahu Hawaii The
general location of the site and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
PEA

The exploration was performed in accordance with our proposal to Kuilima Development
Company dated May 12 1990
This report summarizes the findings resulting from our exploration and the
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analyses based on the data
obtained during the exploration These recommendations are intended for design input
and are not intended for construction estimation or bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct four 4 maintenance support buildings within the new
G 2 golf course area of the Kuilima Resort Golf Course in Kahuku Oahu Hawaii The
proposed buildings are generally rectangular in shape ranging from 4 000 to 11 000
square feet in plan dimension Concrete slabs on grade are anticipated for the building
floors Portland cement and asphalt pavements for the access roadways and parking
areas will be required Placement of new utility lines are anticipated In addition a fuel
tank is to be placed below finish grade elevation

The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions at the project site in order to develop a generalized soil data set for the
formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to site grading
pavement and foundation design of the project

Company dated May 12 1990 The scope of work consisted of the following

SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed project site is located on the northern tip of Oahu in the planned
Kuilima G 2 Golf Course adjacent to the existing Turtle Bay Hilton Resort The
maintenance facility area is generally triangular in plan bound by the fairways to Hole Nos
1 2 and 9
The site was mass graded recently The grading consisted primarily of placement
of fill material in depths of up to four feet The material consisted of reddish brown clayey
silt and was placed in a controlled manner The site is presently essentially level
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling ten 10 borings to
depths ranging between 16 5 and 26 5 feet below the existing ground surface and
obtaining representative samples The approximate locations of the borings are shown
on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general the borings encountered a 1 to 4 foot thick surface layer of fill material
consisting of stiff brown silty clay Below the surface fill layer very dense sand and coral
gravel was encountered to depths ranging from ten feet below the existing surface to the
maximum depths drilled In Boring No 3 soft organic clays were encountered at depths
of ten to fifteen feet below the surface Boring No 9 was drilled close to Boring No 3
to attempt to delineate the extent of the soft soils but encountered partially filled cavities
instead
Groundwater was encountered in the drilled borings at the time of our field
exploration between 8 5 and 10 1 feet below the existing ground elevations

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration generally encountered stiff to very dense soils near the
surface It is our opinion that the stiff on site soils can provide adequate bearing support
for the proposed structures and pavements
Our laboratory testing indicates that these near surface soils have variable
expansion potential ranging from low to very high Therefore select non expansive fill
material will be necessary to reduce potential distress to the proposed structures caused
by heaving of the subsoils
Site Grading
Two to four feet of additional fill are required to attain the design finished elevations
of 14 to 16 feet
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
cleared of vegetation debris rubbish and other deleterious materials These materials
should be removed and disposed of properly off site
Imported fill material for the remaining fills should consist of non expansive select
granular materials less than three 3 inches in maximum size with less than 25 passing
the 200 sieve Any imported fill material should be tested and approved by the
geotechnical engineer prior to transportation to the site
W O 2474 00 Page 6
The replacement fill should be placed in maximum 10 inch thick loose level lifts and
compacted to a minimum of 95 of its maximum dry density as determined by ASTM
Test Method D 1557 78
The site should be graded to drain water away from the foundations
Foundations
Spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the proposed structures The
subgrades of all footings should be scarified and recompacted to 95 of its maximum dry
density as determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 An allowable bearing value of
3000 p s f may be used for footings bearing on the select fill material which has been
compacted The bearing value is for total dead plus live loads and may be increased by
one third when temporary wind and seismic loads are considered Estimated settlements
are on the order of less than one half of an inch total and one fourth of an inch
differential
Individual footings and continuous wall footings should be embedded a minimum
of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade
If foundations are located next to utility trenches they should be embedded below
a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane extending upward from the
bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility lines
The bottoms of all footing excavations should be observed by the geotechnical
engineer prior to placement of the reinforcement to determine if adequate bearing has
been obtained and that loose soils are removed prior to concrete placement
W O 2474 00 Page 7
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive earth pressure
developed against the buried vertical faces of the foundation system and by frictional
resistance developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be calculated using an equivalent fluid
pressure of 300 p c f per foot of depth This assumes that the soil around the foundation
is well compacted A coefficient of friction of 0 4 may be used Unless covered by
pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the upper 18 inches of soil should be
neglected

Utility Trenches
New utility lines are anticipated for the project A granular bedding consisting of
six 6 inches of ASTM C 33 No 67 gravel 3B Fine is recommended under the pipes
Granular materials should be used for the trench backfill The backfill up to about
one foot above the pipes should be lightly tamped to reduce the possibility of damaging
the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level one foot above the pipes to
the finished grade should be placed in maximum 10 inch horizontal lifts mechanically
compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density as determined by ASTM
Test Method D 1557 78 to reduce future ground subsidence
Underground Storage Tank
It is planned to place a storage tank below the site
W O 2474 00 Page 10
Based on our field exploration the tanks will probably bear on the underlying
dense coralline sand and gravel which can provide adequate support Any boulder size
material encountered in the excavation should be disposed of properly off site
Drainage
The finished grade outside the building should be shaped to shed water away from
foundations and to avoid ponding Also it is advised that gutter systems be installed
around the building and that the discharge be diverted away from the foundation and
pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near foundations and slabs should also
be avoided
Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have concrete bottoms and
drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the above
foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause excessive subsurface soil
saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations
should be properly backfilled tight against the wall or slab footing immediately after setting
of the concrete to prevent ponding conditions
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and maintained to drain
all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundations
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be forwarded to the
geotechnical engineer for review and written comments prior to construction This review
is needed to determine adherence to the earthwork and foundation recommendations

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during site grading and construction of the foundations
This is to observe compliance with the design concepts specifications or recommenda
tions and to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from that
anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in this report are
contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and approved by
the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during construction are different
from those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made
LIMITATIONS

information obtained from borings Variations of conditions between the borings may
occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident until
construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to
re evaluate the recommendations given in this report
The location of the field borings are approximate having been determined by
taping from an undated Site Plan provided by Group 70 Architects Planners and Interior

Laboratory Testing
Moisture content determinations were performed on selected samples as an aid
in the classification and evaluation of soil properties The results of these tests are
presented on the Boring Logs at the appropriate sample depths
One 1 Atterberg Limits test was performed on a selected sample to evaluate the
classification of the material sampled The test results are presented on the Boring Log
at the appropriate sample depths
Six 6 swell tests were performed on selected samples to aid in evaluating the
shrink swell potential of the materials sampled The results of these tests are shown on
Plate B 1
Two 2 California Bearing Ratio tests ASTM Test Method D 1883 were conducted
on selected bulk samples of the on site soils to evaluate their pavement support
characteristics The results of these tests are presented on Plate B 2
