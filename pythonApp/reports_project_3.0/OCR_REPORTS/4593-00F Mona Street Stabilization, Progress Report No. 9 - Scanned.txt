This progress report summarizes our work efforts and the field instrumentation
monitoring results for the Mona Street Stabilization Aina Haina Slide Zone Remediation
project located in Aina Haina Valley on the Island of Oahu Hawaii Our work was
performed in general accordance with the scope of services outlined in our fee proposal
dated March 21 2006
This report provides information pertaining to the lateral earth movements
subsurface water levels detected to date during the instrument monitoring effort and
geotechnical recommendations for remediation of the on going landslide We included
general information of our instrumentation program in our progress report dated
The geotechnical exploration results were presented in our report
entitled Revised Subsurface Investigation Program Report Mona Street Stabilization
Aina Haina Slide Zone Remediation Honolulu Oahu Hawaii dated
This report is the ninth semi annual progress report covering the 54 month
instrumentation monitoring period which officially commenced in January 2002

each in November 2001 Three slope inclinometers were installed in these borings in
order to measure the lateral deflection of the soil at various depths An additional boring
B 4 was drilled to a depth of approximately 100 feet in September 2003 An
inclinometer was installed in this boring to better delineate slide limits Locations for B 1
through B 4 are shown on the Site Plan Plate 1

PAVEMENT DEFORMATION MAPPING
Ground surface observations at the project site were recorded in our previous
reports Approximately 1 to 2 inch wide pavement cracks on the surface of the roadway
were observed between 1189 and 1199 Mona Street from May 2002 to
September 2002 No significant continuous cracking development was recorded from
September 2002 to January 2003
Pavement re surfacing was performed in localized areas of Mona Street during
January 2003 to August 2003 Therefore a majority of the cracks previously recorded
was patched with asphaltic concrete No significant continuous cracking development
was recorded from January 2004 to August 2004
Our pavement deformation mapping resumed in Year 2005 During this period a
majority of the cracks recorded prior to the pavement re surfacing was again observed
on the re surfaced pavement as indicated in our last progress report We will continue
to monitor the pavement deformation The revised Crack Location Plan will be available
in the next progress report at the end of 2006 monitoring period
SUMMARY
Inclinometer Nos I 1 I 3 and I 4 indicated some shallow depth subsurface
ground movement at their respective instrument locations However Inclinometer
No I 2 currently does not indicate significant subsurface ground lateral movement Our
piezometer data indicates that subsurface water seepage occurred at the project site
Based on our pavement deformation mapping we believe that lateral near surface earth
movement at the site is still active
Based on the interpretation from Inclinometer Nos I 1 and I 3 it appears that the
sliding zone may be relatively shallow approximately 11 to 15 feet below the ground
surface in the area near Boring Nos 1 and 3 Inclinometer No I 4 is located between
Inclinometer Nos I 2 and I 3 It appears that the slide Zone near Boring No 4 may be
located slightly deeper than Inclinometer Nos I 1 and I 3 at a depth of about 17 feet
below the ground surface
We will continue monitoring the lateral ground movement through the installed
instrumentations and mapping the pavement conditions
