This letter report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the design of the proposed pier foundation located between Column Lines
C 4 and C 5 of Building No 2 at the proposed Leetown Center in Waipahu Oahu
Hawaii The general location of the site and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location
Map Plate 1
The purpose of this exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions in the vicinity of the proposed pier footings between Column Lines C 4 and C 5
of Building No 2 and to provide the basis for geotechnical engineering design
recommendations pertinent to the proposed pier footings for these columns at Building
No 2
Based on the findings of Boring No 2 that was drilled as a part of our previous
exploration covered in our report dated we also provided geotechnical
engineering design recommendations pertaining to the proposed pier footings between
Column Lines A 4 and A 5 of Building No 2

Deep concrete pier systems extending below the ground surface to a depth near
the existing sewerline invert depth at Column Lines A and C are currently planned to
span over the existing six 6 inch sewerline beneath Building No 2
The field exploration performed below the proposed pier foundations along Column
Line C of Building No 2 generally encountered a surface fill layer approximately seven
7 feet in thickness overlying a medium stiff to stiff alluvial deposit to a depth of twenty six

In our previous exploration for Building No 2 one of the drilled borings Boring No
2 was located near the proposed pier locations along Column Line A The boring
generally encountered a surface fill layer approximately three 3 feet in thickness

Deep Excavated Pier Footings
Based on our field exploration and laboratory test results an allowable bearing
value of 2 500 p s f is recommended for the square and rectangular pier footings bearing
on the medium stiff to stiff alluvial deposit or compacted fill If loose or soft areas are
encountered in the footing excavations they should be over excavated down to firm
subsoil and the resulting depression backfilled with select material compacted to a
minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as determined by procedures
described in ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Estimated column loads up to 108 kips were provided by the structural engineer
Estimated pier footing settlements are anticipated to be on the order of three quarter inch
total and three eighth inch differential
Estimated settlement of the existing sewerline from the pier footing loading is
anticipated to be on the order of one half inch total

Because of the relatively deep excavation required and possible presence of loose
trench backfill soils that may be encountered at the previous sewer trench sides of the
deep excavations we recommend that the contractor needs to provide adequate shoring

Alternate Drilled Piers
As an alternative drilled piers or caisson extending to a depth below the existing
sewerline invert may be used to span over the recently discovered sewerline
For a ten 10 feet deep drilled caisson an allowable vertical load of 31 kips is
recommended for a 3 foot diameter drilled pier and for a 4 foot diameter caisson ten
10 feet deep 55 kips can be utilized
If more than one drilled caisson is utilized the caissons should have a minimum
center to center spacing of twice the caisson diameter
The drilled caisson construction should be monitored by the project geotechnical
engineer in order to see that adequate foundation bearing support is achieved Since a
portion of the loading capacities come from the frictional resistance between the shaft and
the in situ soils extra care should be applied to the drilling of the caisson hole selection
of concrete mix design and the placement of concrete
Prompt placement of concrete in the prepared hole as soon as it is completed
is recommended to reduce cave in of the hole The concrete mix should be designed to
minimize shrinkage in order to maintain high frictional contact between the in situ soils
and pier concrete The concrete should be placed in a suitable manner to avoid
segregation of the aggregates
