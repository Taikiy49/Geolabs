SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
The island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lavas from two
2 shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to
be of the middle to late Pliocene in age and Koolau is estimated to be of the late Pliocene
to early Pleistocene in age As volcanic activity in Waianae ceased lava flows from
Koolau banked against its eroded eastern slope forming a broad plateau now known as
the Schofield Plateau
Physical and chemical weathering followed by erosion of this plateau generated
sediments which were transported to the coast In the vicinity of the project site and to
the south these sediments accumulated and interbedded with marine sediments and
coral algal reef formations to form a sedimentary wedge The thickness of the
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3024 00 Page 6
sedimentary wedge ranges from zero in the area of the H 1 Freeway to over 1 000 feet
at Ewa Beach This wedge forms the Ewa Plain and serves as the confining formation
or caprock over the artesian basal aquifers of southern Oahu Deposition of sediments
has continued from earlier geologic time through the present

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
On the basis of our field exploration the project site is underlain by a surface layer
of stiff to hard brown silty clay This silty clay layer is capable of supporting the proposed
structures on a shallow foundation system The brown silty clay layer exhibits low to
moderate shrink swell characteristics when subjected to fluctuations of moisture content
As a result special attention should be given to the recommended procedures for
subgrade preparation for slabs and pavements to reduce the potential shrink swell effects
of the near surface soils We recommend that slabs on grade be supported on at least
12 inches of non expansive select granular fill because of the variable thickness of fills
planned at the new building location In addition we also recommend that a floating slab
on grade be used to reduce the effects of potential differential movements between the
slab and foundations
Grading
For the purpose of definition building areas as referred to in the remainder of
this report are areas within the proposed building limits including exterior slabs as well
as the zones extending five feet 5 laterally beyond the edges of the slab
1 Prior to commencement of earthwork the site should be thoroughly cleared and
grubbed All existing surface vegetation old pavements and deleterious materials
within proposed building areas and pavements should be removed and disposed
of off site
2 Any zones of wet saturated or yielding areas encountered during the clearing
process should be over excavated to expose firm ground and the resulting
excavations should be backfilled with well compacted engineered fill This should
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3024 00 Page 8
be evaluated by a representative of our office in the field Contract documents
should include additive and deductive unit prices for over excavation and
engineered fill placement to account for variations in the over excavation quantities
Areas at grade or designated to receive fill should be scarified to a depth of ten
inches 10 moisture conditioned to between 2 and 5 percent above the optimum
moisture content and compacted to between 85 and 90 percent relative
compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil
expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density as determined by ASTM
Test Method D 1557 78
All fill and backfill material snould be subjected to the evaluation by Geolabs Hawaii
prior to use Because the site grading consists of only minor cuts we anticipate
import fill material would be needed If needed import fill material should consist
of non expansive select granular material Select granular material should contain
no perishable substances or rocks greater than three inches 3 in greatest
dimension with less than 20 percent passing the No 200 sieve
All select fill and backfill material should be placed in thin lifts normally 8 to 10
inches in loose lift thickness and compacted to not less than 90 percent
compaction
Observations and soil density tests should be performed during grading to assist
the contractor in obtaining the required degree of compaction and the proper
moisture content Where compaction is less than required additional compactive
effort should be applied with adjustment of moisture content as necessary to
obtain the specified compaction

Foundations
We understand that fills of up to two feet 2 are planned at the location of the
proposed two story classroom building The proposed two story building may be
supported by isolated spread and or continuous footings bearing on the near surface stiff
brown silty clay or on the compacted fill An allowable bearing pressure of 2 500 pounds
per square foot p s f may be used for design of footings The allowable bearing
pressure is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third for transient load
applications such as wind or seismic forces Footings should have a minimum width of
at least 18 inches and should be embedded at least 18 inches below the lowest adjacent
finished grade
Lateral loads may be resisted by a combination of passive earth pressures exerted
against near vertical foundation faces and soil friction between the base of foundations
and the supporting subgrade Passive earth pressures equivalent to a fluid weighing 300
pounds per cubic foot p c f and a base friction factor of 0 30 may be used for design
If foundations are designed in accordance with our recommendations we estimate
total settlements to be less than one inch 1 with differential settlements on the order of
inch

Slab on Grade
Based on our laboratory test results the near surface silty clay soils appear to
exhibit low to moderate swelling potential when subjected to fluctuations in the moisture
content We anticipate that the site grading will consist of placement of between one half
2 to two feet 2 of new fills within the footprint of the proposed two story classroom
building Because of the variable thickness of new fills planned below the slab we
recommend that slabs on grade be supported on at least 12 inches of non expansive
select granular material to provide more uniform support of the slab In addition we also
recommend that free floating slabs on grade with no structural connections to the wall
and column foundations be used to reduce potential distress due to differential
movements between the slab and foundations Joint filler and sealant may be used to
fill the opening between the edge of slab and other structural elements
Slab subgrade should be kept moist prior to placement of concrete A six inch 6
layer of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C33 No 67 gradation and basalt termite barrier is
recommended above the subgrade soils to serve as a slab cushion and capillary break
To reduce moisture infiltration through the slab that could damage floor coverings a
plastic vapor barrier should be considered below the slab A one inch 1 layer of moist
fine sand should be placed over the vapor barrier membrane to aid in the curing of slab
concrete

Utility Trench
It is anticipated that the utilities for the new building will consist primarily of electric
water drainage and sewer In general good construction practices should be utilized
for the installation and trench backfill of the proposed utilities
Based on the results of our field exploration it appears that in general
conventional excavation methods may be utilized for utility line trenching at the site The
subsurface materials encountered at the site are generally competent However areas
of loose materials may be encountered Trench shoring design and installation should
be the responsibility of the contractor Trench shoring and bracing should conform to the
appropriate health and safety requirements
Utility lines should be piaced on a six inch 6 cushion of No 3B Fine gravel
ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation Self compacting granular backfill such as No 3B Fine
should be used around the pipe and extend twelve inches 12 above the top of the pipe
It is critical to use this self compacting material to reduce the potential of formation of
voids below the haunches of pipes and to provide adequate support for the sides of the
pipes which could result in settlement of the backfill and damage to the pipes The use
of on site clayey soils as backfill around utility pipes is not acceptable
Trench backfills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding eight inches 8 in
loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction In
roadway and structural areas the upper two feet 2 of backfill below finished subgrade
should be compacted to at least 95 percent relative compaction

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Laboratory testing was performed on selected samples obtained during our field
exploration A laboratory testing program was formulated to aid in evaluating pertinent
engineering properties of the soil and materials sampled
Classification Tests
Moisture content ASTM Test Method D 2216 80 and unit weight determinations
are used as an aid to soil classification and to aid in evaluating certain properties such
as relative saturation The moisture content expressed as a percentage of the dry weight
of the tested sample is shown at the appropriate location on plates summarizing test
results and at the appropriate sample depths on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 6
Atterberg Limits tests ASTM Test Method D 4318 84 are used to evaluate the
plasticity of the fine grained portion of soils The Plasticity Index of the material is used
to classify fine grained soils under the Unified Soil Classification System One Atterberg
Limits test was performed The result of the test is shown on Plate A 1
Physical Property Tests
Expansion tests were performed to evaluate the swelling potential of soils These
tests aid in evaluating the materials for use as foundation support and fill Seven 7 one
inch ring swell tests were performed on relatively undisturbed or remolded samples of the
clay soils to evaluate the swelling potential of the soils in natural and remolded conditions
respectively The test results are summarized on Plate B 2
Two 2 CBR tests ASTM Test Method D 1883 73 were performed on surface bulk
samples to evaluate the soil strength for preliminary pavement design The test results are
summarized on Plate B 1
