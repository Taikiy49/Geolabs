PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The proposed Ho’opili Development project is to the south of Interstate Route H-1 
Highway and west of Fort Weaver Road in the Ewa area on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii, as 
shown on the Project Location Map, Plate 1. The Ho’opili development encompasses 
approximately 1,500 acres. The project site was gently sloping and was used for agricultural 
cultivation. 
Parcel 17 consists of 32 units of single-family residential subdivision within         
5.63 acres bordered by Road “F” to the north, Road “G” to the south, Road “5” to the east, 
and Road “4” to the west, as shown on the Site Plan, Plate 2. 
The site grading consisted of minor cuts and fills of generally less than 5 feet. Royal 
Contracting Co., Ltd. performed the earthwork operations for the project. 
SITE GRADING 
At the on-set of earthwork, areas within the contract grading limits were generally 
cleared and grubbed of vegetation and deleterious materials. Materials generated from 
the clearing operations were disposed of off-site. Following the clearing and grubbing 
operations, the areas to be cut were excavated. Finished grades were compacted using

was capped by 24 inches of select granular fills and compacted to a minimum of 
90 percent of the maximum dry density. 
During the site grading operations, samples of the materials utilized as fill were 
obtained and transported to our laboratory for testing.  The maximum dry densities and 
optimum moisture contents were established in general accordance with ASTM D1557 
(Modified Proctor), and the test results are summarized on Plate 3. 
Periodic in-situ moisture and density tests were conducted in general accordance 
with ASTM D6938 (Nuclear Gauge Method) to determine the adequacy of compaction of 
fills and backfills for infrastructure and utility installations, including compaction of building 
pads, garages, walkways, and driveways.  
The test results are summarized on Plates 4.1 through 4.26. These tests were 
performed on moisture-conditioned and compacted lifts of fills and backfills. Test locations 
and elevations were estimated using on-site survey stakes for reference.  These locations 
and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods 
used. Areas deemed unsatisfactory were reworked until the measured relative 
compaction conformed to or exceeded the minimum specified for the project.
