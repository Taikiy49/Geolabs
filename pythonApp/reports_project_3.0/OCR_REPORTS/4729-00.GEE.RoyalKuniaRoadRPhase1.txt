GEOTECHNICAL ENGINEERING EXPLORATION
ROYAL KUNIA ROAD R PHASE 1 PAVEMENT REPAIR
Dear Mr Miyazaki
In accordance with our fee proposal dated May 23 2001 we have recently conducted
a geotechnical engineering exploration for the Royal Kunia Road R Phase 1 Pavement
Repair project This letter report summarizes our work on the project and presents our
geotechnical engineering recommendations pertaining to the pavement repair for the subject
project
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project is located in the northwestern portion of the Royal Kunia Development in
Kunia on the Island of Oahu Hawaii as shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The
name of Road R has been changed to Anoiki Street Several pavement distress areas
were noted along Anoiki Street near the intersection of Anoiki Street and Kakahi Street Most
of the distress areas were located in the southbound lanes of Anoiki Street There appears
to be three areas of noticeable distress and one area that has previously been repaired as
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Based on construction plans the existing Anoiki Street near Kakahi Street is a 56 foot
wide roadway with existing top of pavement elevations ranging from about 425 to 434 feet
Mean Sea Level MSL The design pavement section consisted of 2 inches of asphalt
concrete 8 inches of aggregate base course and up to 18 inches of select borrow material
depending on the soil test data
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsoil conditions at the subject site were explored by drilling and sampling four
borings ranging in depths from about 5 to 6 feet below the existing ground surface The
approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2

Our field exploration generally encountered a surface asphalt concrete layer with
thickness of 2 and 2 5 inches The surface asphalt layer was underlain by a medium dense
to dense base course select borrow layer with thickness ranging from about 14 to 16 inches
The base course select borrow material consists of reddish brown to tannish gray gravels
and sands with silt Below the base course select borrow layer a fill deposit was
encountered to the maximum depth explored of 6 feet below the existing ground surface
The fill deposit consists of stiff to very stiff reddish brown silty clay with some basait gravel
Groundwater was not encountered at the time of the field exploration
Detailed descriptions of the materials encountered in the borings are presented on the
Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 4 Results of the laboratory tests performed on
selected soils samples are presented on Plates B 1 and B 2
DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field observations it appears that the pavement distress areas are
located within the southbound lanes of Anoiki Street between about and
It is recommended that three pavement distress areas be repaired Removal
of the existing asphalt concrete surface should extend at least 12 inches beyond the repair
areas to reduce the potential for cracks in the new asphalt concrete
In our field exploration a medium dense to dense base course select borrow layer
was encountered beneath the surface asphalt concrete layer Since the existing base
course select borrow material is not in a dense to very dense consistency it is recommended
that the existing base course material be scarified to a depth of about 8 inches
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture and re compacted to not less than
95 percent relative compaction
Additional aggregate base course material required to raise the existing grade to
finished top of base course elevation should consist of crushed basalt aggregate and should
conform to Section 31 of the City and County of Honolulu Standard Specification for Public
Works Construction dated September 1986 The aggregate base course should be
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative compaction
In our borings stiff to very stiff fill material consisting of silty clay with some basalt
gravel was encountered below the base course select borrow layer Based on our field
exploration we believe that the stiff to very stiff fill material encountered in our borings should
be able to provide adequate pavement support
Paved areas should be sloped and drainage gradients should be maintained to carry
surface water off the site Surface water ponding should not be allowed anywhere on the site
during or after construction

Construction Observation
Construction observation of the pavement repair during construction is an integral part
of the geotechnical engineering recommendations provided herein The items of
construction monitoring that are critical requiring Special Inspection include observation of
the re compaction of the base course material fill placement and compaction and pavement
construction Other aspects of earthwork construction should also be observed by a
representative from Geolabs This is to observe compliance with the intent of the design
concepts specifications or recommendations and to expedite suggestions for design
changes that may be required in the event that subsurface conditions differ from those
considered in this report The recommendations provided in this letter report are contingent
upon such observations
If the actual exposed subsurface conditions encountered during construction are
different from those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to
the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this letter report are based upon
visual observations made during our field exploration and laboratory testing of the soil
samples recovered Variations of subsurface conditions between and beyond the sampling
points may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident until
construction is underway If variations then appear evident Geolabs Inc should be
contacted to re evaluate the recommendations presented in this report
This letter report has been prepared for the exclusive use of Kunia Residential
Partners and other project consultants for specific application to the proposed Royal Kunia
Road R Phase 1 Pavement Repair in accordance with generally accepted geotechnical
engineering principles and practices No warranty is expressed or implied This report has
been prepared solely for the purpose of providing recommendations for the pavement repair
project
The owner client should be aware that unanticipated soil conditions are commonly
encountered Unforeseen soil conditions such as perched groundwater soft deposits hard
layers or cavities may occur in localized areas and may require additional probing or
corrections in the field which may result in construction delays to attain a properly
constructed project Therefore a sufficient contingency fund is recommended to
accommodate these possible extra costs
