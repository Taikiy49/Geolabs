PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The Dole Plantation Parking Lot Improvements project site is located on the north 
side of the Dole Plantation complex and adjacent to Kamehameha Highway in the 
Wahiawa area on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. The Project Location Map and Site Plan 
are provided as Plates 1 and 2, respectively. 
The project involves the construction of a new parking area and concrete walkway. 
The site grading work involved fills up to about 1 foot thick.  
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services for 
the site grading, pavement and walkway construction at the project site.

SITE GRADING, PAVEMENT & WALKWAY CONSTRUCTION 
We observed the site grading, pavement and walkway construction work for the 
Dole Plantation Parking Lot Improvements project from October 25, 2017 to 
November 22, 2017.  The site grading work consisted of placement and compaction of fills 
on the order of up to about 1 foot thick to achieve the finished grades for the project. Royal 
Contracting Company Ltd. performed the site grading, pavement and walkway 
construction work. During the site grading operations, we obtained samples of the on-site 
and imported materials utilized as fill and backfill materials and transported them to our 
laboratory for testing. The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were 
established in our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D1557 (Modified Proctor), 
and the test results are summarized on Plate 3. 
The fill and backfill materials were moisture-conditioned to above the optimum 
moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density 
as determined in accordance with ASTM D1557. In the roadway areas, the fill and backfill 
materials within 2 feet of the finished subgrade were compacted to a minimum of 
95 percent relative density. 
During the site grading, pavement and walkway construction operations, periodic 
field density tests were performed in general accordance with ASTM D6938 
(Nuclear Gauge Method). Results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on 
Plates 4.1 through 4.3 of this report. These tests were performed on the prepared ground 
surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills at the site. Test locations and elevations 
were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on-site survey stakes. These locations 
and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods 
used. Areas assessed as unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum 
compaction was obtained and/or approved by our field representative based on visual 
observation and hand probing. 
Four abandoned cesspools were encountered in the parking lot area.  The 
cesspools were about 6.5-foot in diameter and ranged from 7 to 15.5 feet in depth.  The 
loose materials at the bottom of the cesspools were removed and the cesspools were 
backfilled with Controlled Low Strength Material (CLSM) and compacted fill material. 
Our services consisted of part-time field observation of the contractor’s fill 
placement, subgrade preparation, and compaction procedures and confirmation of 
subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above grading operations. 
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the 
referenced tests, it is our opinion that the site grading, pavement and walkway construction 
work for the above project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this 
report were performed in conformance with the general intent of the recommendations in 
the following references.
