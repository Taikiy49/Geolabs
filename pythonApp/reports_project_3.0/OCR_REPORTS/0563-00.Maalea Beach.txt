INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this investigation was to define subsurface condi
tions and to provide recommendations for the design and construc
tion of the foundations to support the proposed structures
Site Description
The proposed site situated between Hauoli Street and Maalaea Bay
approximately 1 4 mile northeast of the Maalaea Small Boat Harbor
W Page 1974
consist cf two essentially level lots The rectangular lots have
a combined area just over 2 acres and are presently occupied by
two single story frame dwellings and garages
The ground surface is grass covered with scattered coconut and
other ornamental trees A concrete lined ditch along the western
boundary line separates the site from the adjoining property where
a six story pile supported apartment structure is presently under
construction
Along the narrow beach the exposed soil is a reddish brown sandy
Silt with numerous rock fragments and gravel Numerous large
Semi rounded boulders are present at the waters edge and upon the
beach as part of the present shore protection
Proposed Construction
The proposed development will consist of 2 to 34 story wood frame
structures or 4 story buildings with masonry exterior walls located
in the western and central portions of the site Atennis court
structure is proposed in the northeastern corner of the property
It is anticipated that the units will be constructed at or near the
existing grades with minimum cuts and fills Shallow cuts and fills
of 3 to 4 feet may be made to develop the site

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The proposed site on the shoreline of Maalaea Bay on the southern
coast of the isthmus of Maui is underlain by lava flows from
Haleakala and west Maui volcanoes These lava flows were essenr
tially level and are now covered by recent alluvial stream deposits
from the deeply eroded west Maui mountains
The alluvium is composed of poorly sorted landslide debris delta
deposits and valley fills of low permeability
Subsurface Explorations
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored utilizing five
test borings drilled at the locations shown on the enclosed Site
Plan Plate 1 A detailed description of the field exploration
methods and accompanying laboratory testing is given in the appen
dix along with the boring logs and laboratory test results
Soil Conditions
The explorations disclosed that the site is blanketed by alluvial
silt and sandy silt with some clay and gravel underlain by de
composed vesicular basalt The soil varies in thickness from 18 0
feet at Boring 1 to 31 5 feet at Borings 4 and 5 The surface of
the rock was encountered between elevations 8 5 at Boring 1 and

General
The subsurface explorations and laboratory testing disclosed that
the site is underlain by 18 feet to approximately 32 feet of rela

ently been preconsolidated by desiccation and have formed a very
stiff crust

The proposed structure type changes from wood frame construction
to masonry exterior walls when the building height exceeds 3

MAT FOUNDATIONS should be designed for maximum bearing pressures
of 800 psf and settlements of approximately one inch total and

PILE FOUNDATIONS should be driven to refusal on the rock surface
where they will provide an estimated capacity of 100 kips per
pile for a 12 inch square precast concrete pile The embedded
pile lengths should vary from 15 to 35 feet in length

The most economical foundation system will be determined by the
structure type and the magnitude of the loadings With lightly
loaded structures that are not sensitive to differential move
ments the spread foundations would be the most economical founda
tion This applies to 2 and 3 story frame structures and the
parking garage
For the 4 story masonry exterior wall structures which are more
sensitive to settlements the mat foundations or piles will pro
vide the most economical foundation support The foundation de
Sign will most probably be determined for the heavier structure
by the maximum pile spacing
The piles develop their support capacity almost immediately upon
encountering the hard basalt Assuming line loads of 2 to 3 kips
per lineal foot a single pile would be sufficient to support an
approximately 30 foot span The grade beam for this length span
would generally be excessively large The normal size grade beam
requires piles at approximately 15 to 20 foot centers This does
not permit utilization of the full pile capacities Pile founda
tion can be used more advantageous with structures having concen
trated loadings

Floor Slabs
The laboratory testing of the on site surface soils indicates ex
pansion potentials of 2 0 to 6 2 per cent The swell potential of
6 2 is the maximum condition resulting from compacting the soils
dry of the optimum moisture content and then saturating the sample
If the onssite soils are maintained at their existing moisture
content during construction detrimental swelling should not occur
All fi11 placed within the structures should be compacted wet of
optimum
Slabron grade construction can be utilized if the soils are pro
tected from drying This can be accomplished by delaying the exca
vations to final grade to just prior to the concrete placement and
by covering exposed soils in slab areas with temporary vapor
barriers The upper twelve inches of on site soil should be re
moved from all floor slab areas and the subgrade inspected for
isolated pockets of expansive soils which may require additional
undercutting
Slabs on grade to be provided with moisture sensitive floor cover
ings such as carpet or tile should be protected by plastic vapor
barriers A six inch layer of granular material should be placed
beneath the floor slab to eliminate capillary moisture

Earthwork and Grading
Based on the proposed site plan the grading will entail minimal
cuts and fills It is recommended that all fill utilized for
structural support be compacted wet of the optimum moisture con
tent to a minimum of 90 per cent of the maximum density as esta
blished by ASTM Test Designation D 1557 72T The expansive sur
face materials should not be utilized for backfill
The two existing cesspools should be located and backfilled with
compacted granular soil as part of the initial site preparation
Foundations upon this backfill should be avoided if possible
Where they must be placed upon the cesspool backfill the founda
tion design should provide both top and bottom reinforcing steel
to help span localized areas of differential settlements

Construction Monitoring and Plan Review
It is recommended that the proposed foundation design be reviewed
by the soils engineer prior to finalization to verify that the de
sign is in accordance with our recommendations
The foundation excavations and pile driving operations should be
inspected to assure that the subsurface conditions are consistent
with the findings of our investigation The proposed floor slab
subgrade should also be inspected for localized pockets of expan
sive soils
Any earthwork utilized for structural support should also be in
spected to assure that the recommended degree of compaction is
consistently achieved
It is suggested that the inspection requirements be incorporated
into the specifications giving the contractor the responsibility
of notifying the soils engineer when construction has progressed
to the stage where the inspections are required Since the con
tractor monitors the construction progress on a full time basis
his cooperation can minimize the inspection costs

APPENDIX
FIELD EXPLORATIONS
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling
five borings utilizing truck mounted equipment The depths of
the borings varied from 25 0 to 36 0 feet below the existing
ground surface The locations of the borings are shown on the
enclosed Site Plan Plate l
The soils were classified by visual and textural examination in
the field by our engineer or geologist who continuously supervised
the drilling operations The classifications were verified by
visual inspection and testing in the laboratory All soils were
classified in accordance with the Unified Soil Classification
System A graphic presentation of the soils encountered is presented
on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 5
Soil samples were obtained by driving either a 2 4 inch I D or
a standard penetration sampler with a 140 pound hammer free fall
ing a distance of 30 inches The blow counts to drive the sampler
the last 12 inches are shown on the log of borings at the appropriate
sample depth
