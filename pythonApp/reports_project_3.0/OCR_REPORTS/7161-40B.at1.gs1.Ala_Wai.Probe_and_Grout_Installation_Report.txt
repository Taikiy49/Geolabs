Probing and Grouting Observation Report 
Ala Wai Canal Wall Emergency Repairs 
Honolulu, Oahu, Hawaii 
Dear Mr. Luke: 
This letter report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations performed 
during the probing and grouting operations for the Ala Wai Canal Wall Emergency Repairs 
project from December 30, 2020 to January 21, 2021. 
The project site is located along the south bank of the Ala Wai Canal between 
McCully Street and Kalakaua Avenue in the Waikiki area of Honolulu on the Island of Oahu, 
Hawaii. The project location and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map, 
Plate 1.  
As-built plans of the existing canal wall were not available. The initial remedial 
measures were based on the assumption that the existing canal wall consisted of an intact 
CRM wall structure. However, upon excavating down to the top of the wall, it was discovered 
that the existing canal wall consisted of loose basalt cobbles and boulders with a plaster 
facing and top. Voids were observed within the loose cobbles and boulders.  In addition, it 
was discovered that majority of the wall bottoms were open without any plaster. Therefore, 
wall remediation measures were needed to strengthen the loose cobbles and boulders into 
a cohesive mass to bear against the jet-grouted columns planned for the project. 
The existing canal wall remediation consisted of plugging the exposed bottom of the 
wall without the plaster facing and performing a probing and grouting program to bind 
together the loose cobbles and boulders, and to fill the existing voids that were present. 
The probe hole locations were surveyed and marked in the field by the contractor 
(Hawaiian Dredging Construction Company) prior to the probing operations.  Probe hole 
locations were recommended in two rows, about 3 feet apart, behind the existing canal wall 
at a 5-foot center-to-center spacing. The probe holes in the front and back rows were 
staggered to obtain better grout coverage. It should be noted that Probe No. 57 was located

on an existing utility line. Therefore, Probe Nos. 57A and 57B were marked 2 feet on the 
east and west sides of the existing utility. The probe locations and numbering plan are 
presented on Plate 2 for reference. Probing and grouting operations were performed from 
the top of the existing canal wall elevation.  
The probing and grouting work was performed by Hawaiian Dredging Construction 
Company. The probe holes were drilled with a TEI Rock Drill TE550 mounted on a 
Komatsu PC238US LC excavator equipped with a 4-inch diameter rotary percussion drill bit 
with grout injection portals connected to a hollow core threaded bar. The probe holes were 
about 4 inches in diameter and extended about 8 feet below the top of existing wall. A 
summary of our probe drilling and grouting observations is provided on Plates 3.1 
through 3.3. The materials encountered during the probe drilling operations generally 
consisted of tannish brown gravelly sand with large dark gray basalt cobble and boulder 
fragments.   
After completion of the probe drilling operations, the probe holes were filled with a 
neat water-cement grout mix consisting of 15 gallons of water to one bag of cement. An 
Intric D-9 grout mixer was used to mix and pump grout through the hollow core threaded 
bar into the probe holes. 
Where cavities and/or voids were encountered, a maximum of about 1 cubic yard 
of grout (30 bags of cement) was pumped into the bottom of the probe hole during one 
application of grout for the day. Due to the presence of voids and uncompacted soils, 
probe holes that did not show any indication of grout return were required to be redrilled 
to initial depth and regrouted. Probe holes met the requirement of completion when grout 
returned to the existing ground surface. Grout overages ranged from about 377 to 13,436 
percent of the theoretical hole volume. A total of about 1,107.5 cubic feet of grout (1,228 
bags of cement) were used to complete the probing and grouting installation work.  
In summary, the foundation probing and grouting operations for the project have 
been conducted in general conformance with the intent of our recommendations. Based 
on our observations, it is our professional opinion that the existing cobble and boulder 
backfill has been sufficiently bonded to provide foundation support for the wall 
remediation design and to support the jet-grouted column installation work.

NOTES: 
1. Probe locations and distances as indicated on this plan are approximate. Actual probe locations should be staked in the field based on the recommended 
offset and typical probe spacings shown.  
2. Probes should be drilled to a minimum depth of 8 feet below the top of existing wall elevation and grouted at low pressure from the bottom of the hole up.  
3. The grout mix should have a minimum 28-day compressive strength of 4,000 psi and should consist of a sand-cement mixture. The contractor shall 
submit the mix design for approval before commencing work. 
4. Cracks, gaps, and openings along the entire alignment of the canal-side CRM wall should be blocked and/or plugged before commencing the probing and 
grouting work. Probing and grouting work will be paused if grout is observed leaking into the canal. Additional blocking and/or plugging work will be required 
before resuming probing and grouting.  
5. The probing and grouting work should be monitored by a Geolabs representative to record the actual drill depths and grout quantities, and also expedite 
recommendations for unforeseen conditions that may arise during construction.  
6. Hollow stem drill rods may be used to facilitate grouting and keeping probe holes open.
