PROJECT CONSIDERATION
The project consists of the construction of a one story
28 400 square foot torpedo shop addition to the existing Building 440
The new addition will include mezzanine office and lounge areas The
proposed building will be designed to withstand blast overpressure and
constructed of metal framed roof reinforced concrete walls and
pound capacity forklift truck traffic
The facility will include an electric sub station housing an
emergency power generator lightning protection ordinance grounding
bridge cranes waste Otto fuel collection system fire protection air
conditioning control gates and security system
Also included will be a new access road adjacent to the shop
It is anticipated that the proposed building will have the same
finished grade as Building 440 Therefore fills of up to about 4 feet
are anticipated at the building site Minimal site grading is anticipated
for the proposed access roads
The general layout of the proposed construction is shown on the Site
Plan Plate 2

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
On the island of Oahu the main shield building activity ended in
the late Pliocene about 25 million years ago Since then the island has
subsided up to 1200 feet As Oahu sank coral reef grew around the
shorelines keeping pace with the subsidence
The site is underlain at a depth of about 13 feet by coral reef
which indicates that this area was once in a shallow marine environment
During the Pleistocene fluctuations of sea level were caused by
accumulation and recession of the polar glaciers
Contemporaneous with these sea level fluctuations were post
erosional pyroclastic eruptions from the Salt Lake and Makalapa vents
The volcanic ash consolidated to form tuff locally called mudrock over
the coral formation
In more recent times as the sea level rose again to the Waimanalo
High 25 feet stand of the sea the mudrock was overlain by coral
detritus which was later covered locally by man made fills

Subsoil Conditions
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling
seven 7 borings and three 3 probings to depths of 10 2 to 20 0 feet
below the existing ground surface at the approximate locations shown on
the Site Plan Plate 2 Soil samples obtained from the borings were
selectively tested in our laboratory to further examine their geotechnical
engineering characteristics Detailed descriptions of our field
exploration and laboratory test results are presented in the appendices
The borings encountered a thin surface fill layer of stiff clayey
silt with gravel and a shallow layer of medium dense to hard detritus
coral debris generally less than 5 feet thick over a layer of hard tuff
about 5 5 to 9 5 feet thick The tuff layer was generally weathered and
occasionally found to be decomposed to stiff clayey silt or silty sand and
gravel The tuff was underlain by a generally hard coral reef formation
with some small cavities or soft spots
Groundwater was encountered in the borings at the depths of about
12 8 to 15 1 feet below the existing ground surface at the time of our
field exploration
Detailed descriptions of the materials encountered are presented on
the Boring and Probing Logs Plates A 1 through A 10

Foundation Design
Spread or continuous footing foundation bearing directly on the
existing surface fill of stiff clayey silt with gravel or the in situ
dense coral detritus may be used to support the proposed building An
allowable bearing value of up to 4000 p s f may be used The bearing
value is for total dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third
for temporary wind or seismic loads
Alternatively the interior building footings may bear on the new
compacted structural fill However in this case the allowable bearing
value should be reduced to 2500 p s f
The above allowable bearing values contain a factor of safety of
about 3 Under very short term blasting overpressure loads a much lower
factor of safety is often used in the design Considering the probability
of occurrance short loading time and assuming that some damage is
tolerable the allowable bearing value may be increased up to 2 5 times
for blast overpressure design This would provide a factor of safety
against bearing failure of about 1 2 for blast overpressure conditions
The exterior footings should be embedded a minimum of 24 inches
below the lowest outside adjacent grade and placed directly into the
recompacted existing fill of stiff clayey silt with gravel or on the

Structural Fill Material and Placement
The on site coral sand and gravel if available are suitable for use
as a source of structural fill material The required imported
structural fill material should be non expansive granular soils
free of organic matter and form a dense stable soil mass when
compacted to the required density Generally the particle size
should be less than 3 inches in size
The slab on grade pavement designs presented in the following
section of this report were based on a minimum subgrade CBR value of
15 and therefore the material placed within the top 3 feet of the
fill under the slab on grade pavements should have a soaked CBR of
at least 15 per ASTM D 1883 73 when compacted to 95 of the
maximum densities determined according to ASTM D 1557 78 The total
expansion of the sample during the soaking for the CBR test should
be less than 1
The fills should be placed in horizontal lifts not exceeding 8
inches in compacted thickness and compacted to a minimum of 95 of
its maxim dry density

Iateral Earth Pressures
Underground structures containing utility lines or other earth
retaining structure should be designed to resist lateral earth pressure
The lateral earth pressure acting on the underground or retaining wall
structures will depend on the type of backfill used the extent of the
backfill material and the compactive effort required on the material
behind the structure walls Granular material such as the select on site
coral sand and gravel or imported select borrow granular fill material is
recommended for the backfill use adjacent to the wall structures in order
to reduce the compactive effort required use small compactor near
retaining structure and to minimize the build up of additional lateral
earth pressures
Based on our findings we recommended that the following lateral
earth pressures be used for preliminary design purpose
Non Restrained Condition Active Condition
Lateral Earth Pressure 35 pcf equivalent fluid pressure
Restrained Condition At Rest Condition
Lateral Earth Pressure 45 pef equivalent fluid pressure

Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the proposed sites were explored by
drilling 7 borings and 3 probings at the approximate locations shown on
the Site Plan Plate 2 A truck mounted drilling rig was used to drill
the borings and probings to depths of 10 2 to 20 0 feet below the existing
ground surface

textural examination in the field by one of our engineers who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further

were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A graphic presentation of the materials
encountered is presented on the Boring and Probing Logs Plates A 1
through A 10
