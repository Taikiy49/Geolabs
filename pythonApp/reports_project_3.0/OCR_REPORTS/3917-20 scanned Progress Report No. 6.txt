Dear Mr Steinberger
This sixth progress report is presented to summarize our work efforts and the
results of the additional field instrumentation monitoring conducted for the Kuahea Street
Area Movement project located in Palolo Valley on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The
approximate location of the project site is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report provides summary information pertaining to the recorded earth
movements and subsurface water levels detected during the Phase Ill instrument
monitoring effort The report summarizes the field data and observations that were
collected during three site monitoring visits which were conducted on March 25 and 26
June 9 and 10 and August 14 through 18 2003 Our work was performed in general
accordance with our fee proposal dated and Amendment No 2 for
Engineering Services for the Kuahea Street Area Movement Project Contract
No F 60578 CIP No 97504
This report is our second semi annual progress report covering the additional
12 month instrument monitoring period which officially commenced in August 2002 and
Officially expired in July 2003 However due to some adjustment of our field monitoring
schedule the current instrument monitoring period was extended through the end of
August 2008 rather than the projected expiration date of July 2003 As a result of the shift
in our instrument monitoring schedule we anticipate that the next additional 12 month
instrument monitoring period actually will span between and
August 31 2004 rather than between August 2003 and July 2004 as initially projected

Based on our review of the rainfall information and monthly precipitation summaries
for the current monitoring period February through August 2003 the months of February
and March 2003 experienced elevated rainfall totals associated with significant storm
events Based on our review of the inclinometer data it appears that only Inclinometer

March 2003 The earth movements recorded by the remaining inclinometers do not

In general our interpretation of the earth movement response to elevated rainfall is
that if a period of increased or above normal rainfall is experienced following a prolonged
period of normal or lower than normal rainfall the earth movement rates at some of the
inclinometers tend to increase abruptly following the pericd of elevated precipitation and
then settle back to normal levels The effect has been noted in the past with Inclinometer
earth movement rate response such as described

Based on our monitoring of the subsurface water levels recorded from
Boring Nos P 1 through P 7 for the past monitoring period February through August
and P 6B have encountered appreciable measurable volumes of subsurface water
seepage at their respective screened well points
Measurement of the water level encountered at Piezometer No P 2A which is
screened between 4 and 14 feet below the ground surface indicates that significant
shallow depth water seepage may exist However the measured water levels have been
on a declining lowering trend since about November 2002 despite the elevated rainfall
totals experienced between January and March 2003 The head level in the standpipe
was measured at about 7 feet in January 2003 and has since consistently lowered to
about 3 5 feet as of August 2003
Measurement of the water level encountered at Piezometer No P 3B which is
screened between 30 and 40 feet below the ground surface indicates that some periodic
water seepage may be occurring Typically only trace amounts of water are detected
however periodic elevated head levels of about 2 feet and 6 feet of water were measured
in the standpipe in August 2003 and June 2003 respectively The elevated head levels do
not appear to correlate with rainfall at the site
Measurement of the water level encountered at Piezometer No P 3C which is
screened between 45 and 55 feet below the ground surface indicates that fairly consistent
water seepage may be occurring The measured water levels typically range on the order
of about 5 to 6 feet of head in the standpipe However it should be noted that the
standpipe was observed to be nearly dry in March 2003
Measurement of the water level encountered at Piezometer No P 4A which is
screened between 30 and 40 feet below the ground surface indicates that some
near continual water seepage may be occurring The measured water level in the
standpipe appears to remain fairly consistent throughout the past monitoring period with
an average of about 1 8 feet of head measured in the standpipe
Measurement of the water level encountered at Piezometer No P 4B which is

water seepage may be occurring The measured water level in the standpipe appears to
be fairly consistent throughout the past monitoring period with an average of about 2 6 feet
of head in the standpipe It should be noted that in February 2003 the measured water
level was anomalously high with about 19 5 feet of head in the standpipe The measured
spike in water level may be related to the elevated period of rainfall that was experienced

Waiomao Road Area
The existing elongated longitudinal pavement cracks that were sealed previously to
reduce surface water infiltration along the northern side of Waiomao Road in the
vicinity of Area B appear to be in fair condition The existing arc shaped
pavement cracks that cross Waiomao Road along the western boundary of the
Waiomao Landslide also were previously sealed to reduce surface water infiltration
The continued settlement of the roadway pavement immediately surrounding the
existing storm drain manhole fronting 2340 Waiomao Road was observed The
pavement settlement has produced a noticeable bump located along the northern
side of the southwest bound travel lane In addition the eastern boundary of the
Waiomao Landslide that crosses Waiomao Road also was observed to exhibit a
noticeable bump when crossing with a vehicle The existing pavement patch at this
location appears to be in fair condition
Kuahea Street Area
Observation of the area along Kuahea Street at the head of the Waiomao Landslide
indicates that the road pavement and northern road shoulder continue to
experience some settlement of the ground surfaces The ground settlements have
produced uneven pavement surfaces with significant bumps that traverse the
roadway Large open cracks in the pavement at Kuahea Street were not observed
and the existing asphaltic concrete patches were observed to be in generally fair
condition However some small fatigue cracks are beginning to appear along the
existing pavement patches which traverse the head scarp boundary of the earth
movement
Observation of the area surrounding the intersection of Kuahea Place and Kuahea
Street do not indicate extraordinary signs of new pavement or structural
deformation The concrete driveway located at 2331 Kuahea Street is experiencing
on going distress related to widening cracks and deformation of the concrete
pavement surrounding the existing water meter boxes
The gaps located between the concrete pavement of Kuahea Street and the
driveway at 2334 Kuahea Street have been sealed with concrete to reduce the
infiltration of surface water along the curb alignment The pavement gaps were
identified previously in our Progress Report No 5 for the project
Vacant Graded Hillslopes
The existing graded hillslopes located within the footprint of the Waiomao Landslide
as well as most of the area along Kipona Place at the toe of the landslide do not
exhibit overt signs of earth movement or ground surface deformation The
exception being a localized area of possible renewed earth slump activity located

10 feet wide and is located on the steeply sloping ground surface adjacent to the
street has exposed some rubbly fill material containing some cobbles and small
boulders
It appears that surface water runoff may have eroded the ground surface or initiated
a small earth slump that has deposited a lobe of loose soil and rock material which
encroaches on the road shoulder up to the existing asphalt curb The ground
surface erosion has exposed some once buried cobbles and small boulders that
have the potential to come loose from the slope and roll into the roadway if
disturbed further by surface water runoff The loose rocks should be removed from
the site to reduce the potential for the rocks to roll into the roadway Other than the
removal of the loose surface rocks immediate remedial work does not appear to be
warranted However if additional erosion or slumping of earth occurs the situation
should be investigated further to determine the possible cause and stabilization
alternatives
