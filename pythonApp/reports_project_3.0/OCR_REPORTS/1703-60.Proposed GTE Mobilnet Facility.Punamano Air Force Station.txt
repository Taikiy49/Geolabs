In accordance with your request we have performed a soil and
foundation investigation at the above referenced site
The purpose of the investigation was to explore the subsurface
conditions at the site to provide soil data for use in the foundation
design of the proposed antenna tower and building
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests which we
used in the engineering analysis to formulate our conclusions and
recommendations are presented in the appendices of this report
This report summarizes our findings and reconmendations

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The GTE Mobilnet network of the island of Oahu includes several cell
sites which together will create a mobile communication network
The facility at Punamano includes a new 12 by 20 feet by 10 feet
high prefabricated building placed on a permanent foundation and a
100 foot high self supporting tower It is our understanding that the
tower will be designed for the following base reactions
Total Weight 10 4 Kips
Moment 1052 3 Kip Ft
Maximum Shear 18 1 Kips Total
Maximum Compression 104 7 Kips Per Leg
Maximum Uplift 97 7 Kips Per Leg
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located at the high ground area of the U S Air
Force Station at Punamano on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general site
is an open grassed area presently unoccupied An existing unpaved dirt
road passes the western part of the site
Topographically the site is high on a gentle slope with ground
elevations of about 364 to 370 feet above Mean Sea Level

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
One boring was drilled at the proposed site to a depth of 49 4 feet
below the existing ground surface as indicated on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general the site is underlain by a thin 4 foot layer of very stiff
silty clay underlain by a 16 foot layer of hard basalt the upper six feet
of which is weathered to highly weathered Further underlying the hard
basalt at the depth of about 20 feet was very stiff to hard clayey silt to
the drilled depth of the boring
Groundwater was not encountered during the course of our drilling
operation
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATION
General
Based on the competent hard basalt layer encountered we believe
that the site is suitable for the support of the proposed structures
Because of the anticipated large base reactions we recommend that drilled
piers be used to support the tower structure For the building shallow
spread footing foundation may be used
Tower Foundation
Due to the high compression load 104 7 kips per leg uplift load
97 7 kips per leg and moment 1052 3 kip ft requirement for the
proposed tower structure we would recommend that drilled piers be used as
foundation support The loading capacities of the drilled pier would be
derived mainly from the frictional resistance between the shaft of the
pier and the hard basalt

With the use of one 24 inch diameter drilled pier for each leg we
estimate that the pier should penetrate 15 feet into the existing ground
and at least 5 feet into the hard basalt below the weathered zone in order
to generate sufficient uplift and bearing resistance to fulfill the
above mentioned requirement with a factor of safety of 2 5
Details of the analysis for the laterally loaded pier and the
results which include various loading and moment conditions are discussed
Drilled Pier Construction
The drilled pier construction should be closely monitored by the
soil engineer in order to see that adequate foundation support is
achieved Since the majority of the loading capacities come from the
frictional resistance between the shaft and the in situ soils extra care
should be applied to the drilling of the pier hole selection of concrete
mix design and the placement of concrete
Prompt placement of concrete in the prepared hole as soon as it is
completed is recommended to reduce caving in of the hole The concrete
mix should be designed to minimize shrikange in order to maintain high
frictional contact value between the in situ soils and pier concrete The
concrete should be placed in a suitable manner to avoid segregation of the
aggregates
Building Foundation
Shallow spread footing may be used for the one story building
located to the south of the tower structure An allowable bearing
capacity of 3000 p s f can be used for the footing bearing on the very
stiff silty clay The footing should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches
below the lowest adjacent grade Settlement of the building is estimated
to be on the order of 1 4 inch total and 1 8 inch differential

Resistance to lateral loads for the building can be obtained by
passive pressure developed by the buried part of the footing and by
frictional resistance A frictional coefficient of 0 4 may be used
between the bottom of the footing and stiff soil In addition an
equivalent fluid pressure of 300 p s f per foot of depth may be used to
determine the passive resistance
Design Review
Plans and specifications for foundation support of the antenna
should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written comments
prior to construction to see that the intent of our recommendations is
included
Construction Monitoring
We reconmend that the excavations for the building foundation and
the drilling of the pier holes be inspected by the soils engineer prior to
placement of concrete or reinforcing steel to see that adequate bearing
has been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with
the findings of our subsurface investigation
If conditions encountered during construction are different from
those presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above inspection

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration and Laboratory Testing
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling one
1 boring to depth of 49 4 feet below the existing ground surface at the
approximate locations shown on the site Plan Plate 2 A truck mounted
rotary drill rig was used to drill the borings
The soil and rock encountered in the borings were classified by
visual and textural examination in the field by our geologist who
continuously monitored the drilling operations The classifications were
further reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory The
soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification system A graphic presentation of the materials
Soil samples were obtained from the boring by driving a 2 inch
standard split spoon sampler with a 140 pound hammer free falling 30
inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the last 12 inches are shown
on the boring logs at the appropirate sample depths
Moisture content determinations were performed on selected samples
as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties The
results of these tests are presented on the boring logs at the appropriate
sample depths

APPENDIX B
Laterally Loaded Pier Analysis
Se is MU bt nc
Because of the anticipated large lateral force and moment acting on
the peir a laterally loaded pier analysis was performed The analysis
was carried out by using a finite difference program PILEDG which was
derived from the program COM624
The program adopts the P Y Curve approach and has the capability to
generate internally P Y curves given the soil data Since the lateral
soil modulus varies with the deflection experienced the computerized
analysis first assumes a modulus value then calculates the pile
deflection based on the assumed modulus The calculated deflection is
then used to determine the modulus from the P Y curves The calculated
modulus and the assumed modulus are then compared The procedure is
repeated until the new assumed and calculated moduli are essentially the
same
The results of the analysis showing the relationship of lateral
deflection at the top of the pier and the applied lateral force under
aifferent moment conditions are summarized on Plate B 1 A summary of
maximum moment along the pier is under different boundary forces and
moment conditions is shown on Plate B 2
