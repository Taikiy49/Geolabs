PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The project involves widening Maluhia Road and Koloa Road by about 4 to 6 feet 
on each side of the roadways for an asphaltic concrete paved shoulder, the installation 
of a new roundabout and underground box culvert at the intersection of Koloa Road and 
Ala Kalanikaumaka Street and related site improvements. In addition, improvements to 
the drainage infrastructure were performed along Koloa Road. A Project Location Map, 
Overall Site Plan, and Site Plans are shown on Plates 1, 2 and 3.1 through 3.15, 
respectively. 
In accordance with the State of Hawaii - Department of Transportation (HDOT) 
Sampling and Testing Guide for Acceptance, one compaction test should be performed 
per lift per 1,000 square feet of base course placement. Therefore, we provided an 
engineering technician for compaction testing and office engineering support during 
base course placement for the project.  
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services 
performed during the site grading and roadway construction at the project site.

SITE GRADING AND ROADWAY CONSTRUCTION 
We observed the site grading and roadway construction work for the 
improvements to Maluhia Road (Route 520) and Koloa Road (Route 53) project from 
June 23, 2020 through March 15, 2022.  Goodfellow Bros. performed the site grading 
and roadway construction work. 
The newly widened shoulders were excavated and a non-woven geotextile fabric 
and Tensar Tri-Ax Geogrid TX7 were placed on the subgrade. The excavation was then 
backfilled with untreated aggregate base course and compacted to a minimum of 
90 percent of the maximum dry density as determined in accordance with ASTM D1557 
per the response to project RFI No. 9 entitled “Aggregate Base Relative Compaction.” 
Recompaction of the shoulder and roadway subgrades were not observed by our field 
representative. 
The fill and backfill materials for the Koloa Road drainage improvements were 
moisture-conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a 
minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density. Imported granular fills for the 
backfill of the new culvert structure were compacted to a minimum of 95 percent of the 
maximum dry density. Aggregate base course fills for the new roundabout construction 
at the intersection of Koloa Road and Ala Kalanikaumaka Street were compacted to a 
minimum of 95 percent of the maximum dry density. 
During the site grading operations, samples of the materials utilized as fill were 
obtained and transported to our laboratory for testing.  The maximum dry densities and 
optimum moisture contents were established in general accordance with ASTM D1557 
(Modified Proctor), and the test results are summarized on Plate 4. 
Periodic in-situ moisture and density tests were conducted in general accordance 
with ASTM D6938 (Nuclear Gauge Method) to determine the adequacy of compaction 
of fills and backfills for infrastructure and utility installations, including compaction of 
roadway shoulders, concrete truck apron, drain line backfill, and roadway areas.  
The test results are summarized on Plates 5.1 through 5.25. These tests were 
performed on moisture-conditioned and compacted lifts of fills and backfills. Test 
locations and elevations were estimated using on-site survey stakes for reference. 
These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree 
implied by the methods used. Areas deemed unsatisfactory were reworked until the 
measured relative compaction conformed to or exceeded the minimum specified for the 
project. 
Our services consisted of part-time field observation of the fills and backfills 
placed, and compaction testing.
