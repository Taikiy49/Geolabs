INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of a geotechnical engineering
evaluation performed for the proposed Kalauao Springs Ditch Bridge
Crossing at the Pearl Kai Center at Aiea Oahu Hawaii The general
location of the site and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location
Map Plate 1
W O 1672 40 Page 2
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed project will include the construction of a road bridge
structure for the extension of Lipoa Place across Kalauao Springs Ditch to
the Pearl Kai Phase II development
The proposed bridge will be designed for two 2 lanes of traffic and
have a clear span of about 40 feet between abutments
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our investigation was to explore the subsurface
conditions at the proposed bridge abutment locations to obtain soil data
for use in the foundation design of the proposed bridge
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests which we
used in the engineering analysis to formulate our conclusions and
recommendations are presented in the appendices of this report
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located on the makai side of Kamehameha Highway
across from the existing Pearl Ridge Shopping Center in Aiea on the
island of Oahu Hawaii On the eastern side of the Kalauao Springs Ditch
is the existing Building E of the Pearl Kai Center Phase I and the site
of the proposed building for the Phase II development The existing
ground surface is paved with concrete The stream bank is approximately
1 foot above the water level and has a riprap lining
The western side of the Kalauao Springs Ditch is a Slope of
approximately 8 feet high with the crest adj oining the dead end of Lipoa
Place The slope is presently covered with bushes and grasses

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The geologic conditions underlying the site are the results of a
complex series of depositional and erosional events brought on by
glacioceustatic sea level changes and post erosional volcanic activities
during the Pleistocene Following the cessation of activity of the Koolau
Volcano alluvial materials were deposited in a deltaic or estuarine
environment in the area of Pearl Harbor
The sea level fluctuated many times receding perhaps as much as 300
feet below and rising up to 95 feet above the present sea level
Concurrent with a rise in sea level during the later Pleistocene era
eruption of the Salt Lake Makalapa member of the Honolulu Volcanic series
mantled the Older Alluvium with thick deposits of volcanic ash which
consolidated to form volcanic tuff locally called mudrock layers
Subsequent erosion dissected and thinned those mantles of tuff During
the recent epoch as sea level rose to its current stand the area was
covered with lagoonal marine deposits

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The project site is underlain by a thick layer of soft compressible
soil Previous field exploration in the nearby area disclosed that the
thick compressible layer is underlain by a layer of boulderly material or
very stiff alluvial material with competent bedrock formation at depths in
excess of 135 feet below the ground surface
It appears that pile foundation would not provide an effective means
to support the proposed road bridge because of the absence of a competent
bearing layer within a reasonably economical depth large amount of
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 1672 40 Page 5
anticipated downdrag load and the questionable drivability of the pile
through the boulderly layer In light of the above we would recommend
that shallow strip footings bearing on a constructed gravel crust layer be
used as bridge support provided the footings have provisions for
releveling to accommodate future ground settlements
Fills would initiate long term ground settlements and therefore
should be minimized or avoided
Bridge Foundation
In light of the difficulties and uncertainty associated with a system
of pile foundations we would recommend that shallow strip footing
foundations be utilized for the proposed road bridges To provide bearing
for the footings a crust consisting of self compacting gravel material
with sufficient width and thickness would need to be constructed over the
existing weak crust or where the crust is non existent
It is our opinion that the gravel crust layer consisting of gravel
with size between one fourth and three fourths of an inch ASTM C 33 No
67 should have an minimum thickness of 2 feet and a width of at least 2
feet greater than the width of the shallow footing To provide lateral
restraint the gravel crust layer should be completely enclosed by filter
fabric A minimum embedment of 2 feet is recommended for footings on
sloping ground and 1 foot for footings on level ground In addition a
minimum horizontal set back distance of 6 feet as measured from the outer
edge of the footing to the slope face is recommended for footings on or
adjacent to a slope A sketch showing the recommended footing and the
gravel crust layer is presented on Plate 3
Provided that the above recommendations are followed and the gravel
crust layer properly constructed we believe that an allowable bearing
capacity of 1 000 p s f can be used The bearing capacity is for total
dead plus live loads and may be increased by on third for temporary wind
and seismic loads
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1672 40 Page 6
The design abutment dead and live loads given by the structural
engineers are 6 3 and 1 7 kips per linear foot respectively Settlements
on the order of 2 to 4 inches are estimated under the total dead plus live
loads The settlements may occur slowly therefore provisions to relevel
the structure may be necessary
Retaining Wall
The preliminary plans indicate an abutment wall located on the ditch
Slope However it is our recommendation that no fill should be placed
behind the abutment wall since fill placed will cause the undesirable
effects of increasing the dead load acting on the abutment footing and
inducing additional consolidation settlement Additionally elimination
of the backfill behind the abutment wall would eliminate the active
pressure acting on the wall and reduce the increase in footing pressure
Therefore we recommend that the abutment be moved upslope to the end of
Lipoa Street
Iateral loads acting on the wall footing may be resisted by passive
earth pressure developed against the buried vertical faces of the
foundation system and by frictional resistance developed between the
bottom of the foundations and the bearing soil
For a downward sloping condition in front of the wall with gradient
submerged ground condition the equivalent fluid pressure of 150 p s f
could be used in estimating the passive earth pressure This assumes that
the soil around the foundation is well compacted The frictional
resistance may be calculated by using a coefficient of friction of 0 3
The above resistance values are maximum values and do not contain a factor
of safety

Erosion Protection
The bridge footings and the gravel crust layer will be constructed at
a low elevation which is susceptible to periodic flooding Therefore it
is our opinion that some amount of erosion protection should be provided
A 4 inch thick concrete lining reinforced with welded wire fabric
installed in areas susceptible to periodic flooding could provide
resistance to scouring A 6 inch layer of base course is recommended
below the lining to provide a filter layer between the lining and
underlying soil
The lining should also incorporate cutoff walls along the ends in
order to minimize scouring below the lining The cutoff walls should
extend a minimum of 3 feet below the water level and ground surface
AS an alternative permanent sheet piles driven along the ditch bank
in front of the abutment footing may also be considered for erosion
protection
Site Grading
We recommend that fills be limited to less than 1 foot to reduce
ground settlement due to the weight of the new fills

Fill Material
Excavated on site material free of any organic and other expansive
component may be used as a source of fill material All imported
fill material should consist of selected non expansive silty sandy
or granular materials less than 3 inches in size

Design Review
Plans and specifications for foundations site grading and pavement
design should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written
comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the
sitework excavation and foundation construction phases of the work This
is to observe compliance with the design concepts specifications or
recommendations and to allow design changes in the event that subsurface
conditions differ from that anticipated prior to start of construction
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon such
observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as borings
and samples Variations of condition between the field data points may
occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become
evident until construction If variations then appear evident it will be
necessary to re evaluate the recommendations given in this report
The locations of the field data points were approximately determined
by taping from reference points along property boundaries shown on the
Foundation Plan by Robert Englekirk Consulting Structural Engineers Inc
Elevation of the borings were estimated from the same plan The physical
locations of the field data points should be considered accurate only to
the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil type
and as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross sections are based upon interpolation between field data
points and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
Water level data from the field data points was collected at the
times given on the graphic representations and or in the text of this
report This data has been reviewed and interpretations made in the
formulation of this report However it must be noted that fluctuation
may occur due to variations in rainfall tides temperature and other
factors

A 3 2
