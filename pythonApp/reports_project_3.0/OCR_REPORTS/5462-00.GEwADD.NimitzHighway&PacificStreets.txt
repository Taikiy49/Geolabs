project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings and geotechnical engineering
recommendations resulting from our desk top study and engineering analyses These

foundation only The findings and recommendations presented herein are subject to the
limitations noted at the end of this report

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lavas from
two shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to
be middle to late Pliocene in age and Koolau is estimated to be late Pliocene to early
Pleistocene in age After a long period of volcanic inactivity during which time erosion
incised deep valleys into the Koolau shield volcanic activity returned with a series of lava
flows followed by cinder and tuff cone formations These series are referred to as the
Honolulu Volcanic Series
The project site is located on the coastal plain of southern Oahu The coastal plain
was built on the eroded flanks of the Koolau Volcano which forms the eastern two thirds
of the Island of Oahu The coastal plain was built by extensive accumulation of alluvium
derived from erosion of the volcano interbedded with coral reefs and associated deposits
During the Pleistocene Epoch Ice Age sea levels fluctuated in response to the
cycles of continental glaciation Most of the coastal plains developed during the
Pleistocene Epoch when the sea levels fluctuated significantly As the glaciers grew and
advanced less water was available to fill the oceanic basins such that sea levels fell below
the present stands of the sea When the glaciers melted and receded an excess of water
became available such that the sea levels rose to above the present sea level
The processes of erosion and deposition were affected by these glacio eustatic sea
level fluctuations When the sea level was low the erosional base level was
correspondingly lower therefore valleys were carved to depths below the present sea

ESTIMATED SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Research of available subsurface information near the project area was performed
Based on the available boring information we anticipate that the project site is underlain
by a surface fill layer with thickness ranging from about 2 5 to 3 feet The surface fill layer
will generally consist of dense granular materials Below the fill layer we anticipate a
loose lagoonal deposit to depths of about 7 to 9 feet below the existing ground surface
The lagoonal deposit generally consists of loose to very loose silty sand with gravel and
shell fragments We anticipate that the lagoonal deposit is underlain by a coral reef
formation
Groundwater level is anticipated to range from about 4 to 9 feet below the existing
ground surface It should be noted that groundwater levels at the project site may
fluctuate depending on tidal fluctuation seasonal precipitation and other factors
DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
We anticipate that the project site to be underlain by a relatively thin fill layer above
loose to very loose lagoonal deposits At deeper depths we anticipate that a coral reef
formation may be encountered
Spread footings and drilled shaft foundations may be used for the new traffic signal
system foundations Detailed discussion of these items and our geotechnical engineering
design recommendations are presented in the following sections of this report
Traffic Signal Pole 3 7 Foundation
Based on the anticipated subsurface conditions we believe that a spread footing
foundation may be used for support of the lightly loaded signal pole structure The footing
should be embedded a minimum of 12 inches below finished grade
An allowable bearing pressure of 1 500 pounds per square foot psf may be
utilized for the design of the Traffic Signal Pole 3 7 foundation bearing on properly
re compacted fill material This bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may be
increased by one third 1 3 for transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic
forces

We estimate that settlements for footings bearing on properly re compacted fill
material as recommended herein to be less than one 1 inch total Differential
settlement is estimated to be less than one half 2 inch
The footing subgrade should be scarified a minimum depth of 8 inches and
re compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction If soft and or loose
materials are encountered at the bottom of footing excavations the materials should be
over excavated to expose the underlying firm materials The over excavation should be
backfilled with select granular material compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative
compaction
Foundations located next to utility trenches or easements should be embedded
below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane extending upward from the
bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility lines This
requirement is necessary to avoid surcharging adjacent below grade structures with
additional structural loads and to reduce the potential for foundation settlement
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive earth pressure
acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system and by frictional resistance
developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil Resistance due to
passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid pressure of 350 psf
This assumes that the soil around the footings is relatively undisturbed or well compacted
Unless covered by pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the upper 12 inches of
soil should be neglected For footings bearing on properly re compacted fill material a
coefficient of friction of 0 35 may be used
We recommend that footing excavations be observed by a representative from
Geolabs Inc prior to placement of reinforcement steel and concrete to confirm the
foundation bearing conditions and the required embedment depths
Traffic Signal Pole 10 Foundation
Based on the anticipated subsurface conditions a drilled shaft foundation with a
nominal diameter of 18 inches and a minimum shaft length of 5 feet may be considered for
the Traffic Signal Pole 10 foundation The drilled shaft would derive support primarily
from adhesion between the sides of the drilled shaft and the subsoil materials We
estimate that the total settlement of the drilled shaft foundation to be less than 0 5 inch
We believe that these settlements are essentially elastic and should occur as the loads are
applied
In general lateral load resistance for drilled shafts is a function of the stiffness of
the surrounding soil the stiffness of the shaft allowable deflection at the top of the shaft
and induced moment in the shaft Based on the structural loading and a free head
boundary condition a horizontal shaft head deflection of less than 0 5 inch was estimated
The calculated maximum induced moment was 5 150 foot pounds

The performance of drilled shafts will depend significantly upon the contractor s

Traffic Signal Pole Il 30 Foundation
Based on the structural loading and the anticipated subsurface conditions we
believe that a drilled shaft foundation with a nominal diameter of 30 inches and a shaft
length of 14 feet may be considered for the Traffic Signal Pole Il 30 foundation We
estimate the total settlement of the shaft to be essentially elastic and to be less than
0 5 inch
Lateral load analysis was performed on the 30 inch drilled shafts Based on the
structural loading and a free head boundary condition a horizontal shaft head deflection of
less than 0 5 inch was estimated In addition a maximum induced moment of
49 690 foot pounds was calculated
Torsion analysis was also performed for the drilled shaft foundation Based on the
analysis a factor of safety of at least 2 was calculated
Potentially Contaminated Subsoil Conditions
Based on our experience in the project area we believe that it would be highly
probable that contaminated subsoil conditions will be encountered during drilled shaft
installation for the traffic signal structures Typically a baseline of the contaminated
material is developed from the environmental test results prior to bid This information is
provided to the contractor for his bid
Based on the above we recommend that environmental testing of the subsoil
materials at the project site be performed prior to bid or a sufficient contingency fund be
provided to accommodate proper disposal of contaminated materials generated from the
project
