Foundation Probing and Grouting Report 
Our Lady of Perpetual Help Parish New Church 
Ewa, Oahu, Hawaii 
Dear Mr. Woodring: 
This letter report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations performed 
during the foundation probing and grouting operations for the Our Lady of Perpetual Help 
Parish New Church project between October 23 and October 25, 2023. 
The project site is located at 91-1004 North Road in the District of Ewa on the Island 
of Oahu, Hawaii. The project location and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location 
Map, Plate 1.  
Due to potential subsurface cavities and/or voids within the underlying coral 
formation, a probing and grouting program to locate and grout potential cavities beneath the 
foundations was specified for the project. The probe hole locations were surveyed and 
marked in the field by the general contractor prior to the probing operations. The probe holes 
were spaced at about 10-foot on center along the building’s continuous footings and at the 
center of planned spread footings. Probing and grouting were conducted at the bottom of 
the footing elevation of about +9.5 feet Mean Sea Level (MSL). 
The probing and grouting work was performed by Mort Drilling Services. The probe 
holes were drilled with a TEI Rock Drill TE300HT mounted on a Komatsu PC-75-UU 
excavator equipped with a 3-inch diameter rotary percussion drill bit with grout injection 
portals connected to a hollow core threaded bar. The probe holes were about 3-inch 
diameter and extended to a depth of about 10 feet below the bottom of footing elevations. 
The approximate locations of the probe holes are shown on the Probe Hole Identification 
Plan, Plate 2. A summary of our foundation probe drilling and grouting observations is 
provided on Plates 3.1 through 3.4. The materials encountered during the probe drilling 
operations generally consisted of tan coralline materials. 
After completion of the probe drilling operations, the probe holes were filled with a 
neat water-cement grout mix consisting of six gallons of water to one bag of cement. An

Obermann VS-63 colloidal mixer was used to mix and pump grout through the hollow
core threaded bar into the probe hole. Additional probe holes were drilled and grouted
around the probe holes with grout intakes greater than six times the theoretical volume.
A total of 98 probe holes were drilled and grouted for the new building, including
35 additional probe holes. Grout overage ranged from about 180 to 9,167 percent of the
theoretical hole volume.
In summary, the foundation probing and grouting operations for the project have
been conducted in general conformance with the intent of the project requirements.
Based on our observations, it is our professional opinion that the voids and/or cavities
encountered at the structure foundation locations have been grouted and that the treated
subgrade area should provide the recommended foundation support for the new
proposed building foundations.
We appreciate the opportunity to be of service to you on this project. If you have
questions or need additional information, please contact our office.
