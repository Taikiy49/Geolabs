INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the design of the proposed sewage pump station in the West Loch
Fairways Subdivision The exploration was performed in accordance with our proposal
dated
The general location of the project site is shown on the Project Location Map Plate
This report summarizes the findings resulting from our exploration and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analysis based on the data
obtained from the field exploration and laboratory testing These recommendations are
intended for design input and are not intended for construction estimation or bidding
purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a new sewage pump station near the end of the
cul de sac on Road L 1 in West Loch Fairways to replace the existing lift station It is
our understanding that the proposed pump station building will be approximately 32 by
34 feet in plan dimension A new surge arrestor and a new venturi pit will also be
included for the proposed pump station
The existing ground elevations at the project site range from about 9 to 12 feet
Mean Sea Level It is anticipated that the proposed pump station building will have a
single story masonry concrete structure with finished floor elevation at 16 3 feet
Therefore site grading involving about 4 to 7 feet of fills is anticipated t is our
GEOLABS HAWAI

understanding that the bottom floor elevation of the proposed sewage pump station will
be at elevation 15 2 feet therefore deep excavation is anticipated
It is anticipated that concrete slab on grade will be provided for the pump station
building and asphaltic concrete pavement will be utilized for the access roadway
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions at the project site to develop a generalized soils rock data set for the
formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to the design and
construction of the proposed building
The work was done generally in accordance with our proposal of November 28
1990 The scope of work was limited to
1 Review of available soils and geologic information pertinent to the project
site and its vicinity
2 Reconnaissance of the project site and vicinity to make visual observations
of the surficial soil rock conditions
3 Drilling and sampling of three 3 borings to depths of 51 0 to 51 5 feet
below the existing ground surface
4 Laboratory testing of selected samples obtained during the field exploration
to determine the classification and pertinent engineering properties of the
materials encountered

5 Engineering analyses of the field and laboratory data for the purpose of
developing site grading pavement and _ foundation design
recommendations and

in the appendices of this report
SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed pump station site is located to the north of the end of the cul de sac
on Road L 1 in the proposed West Loch Fairways and is about four to five hundred feet
away from the south corner of the existing West Loch Golf Course Phase II
In general the site slopes upwards gently in the southwesterly direction with
existing ground elevations ranging from about 9 to 11 feet

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
It is our opinion that the proposed project is feasible from a geotechnical
engineering standpoint Based on our field exploration and current project design it is
anticipated that the bottom floor of the proposed pump station will bear on the medium
dense to dense silty sand and coral gravel and the foundations of the station building will
bear on compacted fill Therefore it is our opinion that spread footing mat foundations
can be utilized to support these proposed structures
Due to deep ground excavation anticipated both excavation shoring support and
dewatering will be required for the proposed construction
Detailed discussions are presented in the following sections

Foundation Excavation and Dewatering
The excavation for the proposed pump station and trenches for the connecting
sewer lines will encounter relatively competent soil conditions and groundwater levels
It is anticipated that shoring will be required to support the deep excavation sidewalls
during construction Generally the method and design of the shoring should be the
choice of the contractor within practical limits and safety considerations It is our opinion
that sheetpiles and or braced shoring should be considered
Based on the groundwater data and the proposed bottom floor elevation of the
pump station the average groundwater levels at the site may be about seventeen 17
feet above the bottom of the anticipated excavation It is anticipated that relatively
permeable dense to medium dense silty sand and coral gravel will be encountered at and
below the excavation bottom Therefore moderate dewatering pumping flows will be
required to dewater the excavations to allow construction to be performed in the dry
Dewatering operations should be performed so that only clear water is pumped
Excessive pumping which removes soil fines may result in a quick condition or blowing
of the excavation bottom or sides
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2611 00 Page 8
Based on the encountered subsoil conditions and our preliminary estimate the
sheeting used in the excavation sidewalls and trench supports should be driven to a
minimum embedment of ten 10 feet below the excavation bottom to reduce the potential
heaving or piping of the sandy subgrade at the excavation bottom Also it is our
recommendation that the excavation bottom should be over excavated by two 2 feet and
backfilled with 3B Fine gravel to provide a working platform and cushion layer for the
construction of the mat footing foundations of the bottom floor of the proposed pump
station
Based on the groundwater level encountered in the drilled borings a high
groundwater level at elevation of 2 5 feet above Mean Sea Level is recommended for
the uplift design It is anticipated that the pump station will be subject to relatively large
buoyant force due to submerge condition of the structure It is our opinion that the pump
Station should be designed such that the buoyant force will be resisted entirely by the
dead weight of pump station structure Dewatering operations should be continuous until
completion of the pump station construction to prevent potential upward movement of the
structure under uplift forces
Foundation Support
In order to provide uniform support for the proposed structure we recommend that
a two 2 foot thick cushion layer consisting of 3B Fine gravel should be provided below
the mat foundations for the bottom level of the pump station
It is anticipated that the foundations of the emergency generator room portion of
the pump station building will bear partially on fill and partially on backfill of the lower
levels of the structure This portion of the pump station building should be structurally
separated from the pump station supported by deep mat foundations The emergency
generator foundations should bear on a minimum of two 2 feet of select non expansive

Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be forwarded to the
geotechnical engineer for review and written comment prior to construction This review
is needed to determine adherence to the earthwork and foundation recommendations
given herein If this review is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume
responsibility for misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during construction of the earthwork and foundation
phases of the work This is to observe compliance with the design concepts
specifications or recommendations and to allow design changes in the event that
subsurface conditions differ from that anticipated prior to start of construction The
recommendations given in this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and approved by
the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during construction are different
from those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from field data points such as the drilled borings Variations of
conditions between the field data points may occur and the nature and extent of these
variations may not become evident until construction If variations then appear evident
it will be necessary to reevaluate the recommendations given in this report
The location of the field data points were approximately determined by taping from
reference points shown on the available topographic survey map not referenced
Elevations of the data points were approximately determined by estimation from the same
map The physical location and elevation of the field data points should be considered
accurate only to the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the field data points
depict the approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a
gradual transition
Water level data from the field data points were collected at the times given on the
graphic representations These data have been reviewed and interpretations made in the
formulation of this report However it must be noted that fluctuation may occur due to
variations in rainfall tides temperature and other factors

warranty expressed or implied is made
