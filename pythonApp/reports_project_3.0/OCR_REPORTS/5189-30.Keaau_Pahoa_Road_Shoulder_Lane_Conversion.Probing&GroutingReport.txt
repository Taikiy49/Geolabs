This letter report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations performed
during the foundation probing and grouting operations for the Keaau Pahoa Road
Shoulder Lane Conversion project between November 12 and 25 2013 The probing and
grouting observation work was performed in general accordance with our scope of
services outlined in our fee proposal dated
Project Considerations
The project is along Keaau Pahoa Road Route 130 from the vicinity of
Keaau Bypass Road to Shower Drive in the District of Puna on the Island of Hawaii The
project location and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The
purpose of the project is to improve safety and traffic conditions between Keaau Bypass
Road and Shower Drive with the following construction
Widening of Waipahoehoe Stream Bridge
The existing Waipahoehoe Stream Bridge begins at about 16 and
ends at about 84 of Keaau Pahoa Road for about 80 feet long along
the travelway direction and 40 feet wide along the stream alignment direction
The existing bridge is a three span concrete structure supported by abutments at
both ends and two intermediate piers on continuous spread footings with the

The existing deck elevation of the bridge structure ranges from about

the intake and outlet ends to accommodate two northbound lanes one southbound

in conjunction with the bridge widening We understand that approach slabs for the
bridge structure will not be utilized
Widening of Keaau Pahoa Road
The existing Keaau Pahoa Road will be widened to provide a permanent second
northbound lane and shoulder along the makai side from about of
Keaau Bypass Road to about of Keaau Pahoa Road for a gross length
of approximately 10 907 lineal feet
In addition the existing southbound roadside shoulder will be converted into a new
southbound shoulder lane for use during peak afternoon traffic hours The
southbound shoulder lane begins at about of Keaau Bypass Road and
ends at about of Keaau Pahoa Road for a total length of approximately
12 210 lineal feet The existing paved shoulder varies in width from about 8 to
11 feet wide The proposed shoulder lane will be paved to provide a 12 foot wide
pavement 10 foot travelway and 2 foot paved shoulder and an unpaved minimum
2 foot clearance to roadside obstructions for a total width of 14 feet
Improvements to Existing Drainage Structures
As part of the project development we understand that it is desired to provide
improvements to the existing drainage structures to alleviate existing flooding
problems within the project limits In addition the existing culverts will extend to
meet the widening requirements within the existing right of way
Probing and Grouting Program
A foundation probing and grouting program was implemented for the widened
portions of the bridge structure and for the improvements to the existing drainage
structures planned for the project in general accordance with the project plans
specifications and recommendations presented in our geotechnical engineering
exploration report dated The purpose of the probing and grouting
operations was to reduce the potential for loss of foundation support resulting from
collapse of cavities or compression of loose zones below the new structures
Nan Inc surveyed the footing layout and marked the probe hole locations prior to
the probing and grouting operations In general the probe holes were spaced at
approximately 10 foot intervals along the footings The approximate probe hole locations
are shown on the Probe Hole Identification Plans Plates 2 1 and 2 2

Isemoto Contracting Inc performed the probing work for the project using a
Gardner Denver and a track drill The probe holes were about 4 inches
in diameter and extended to depths of about 12 to 33 5 feet below the existing ground
surface corresponding to approximately 10 to 25 feet below the design footing bottom
elevations After completion of the probe drilling operations the probe holes were filled
with a three part sand and one part cement grout The grout used to fill the probe holes
was supplied by Jas W Glover Ltd and the boom pump was operated by Infinity
Concrete Pumping
Our field representatives observed the probing and grouting operations and

aggregate length of about 897 1 lineal feet below the existing ground surface were drilled
for the various structures as summarized in the following table The probing and grouting
