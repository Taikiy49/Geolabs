Preliminary Geotechnical Engineering Findings
Evaluation of Building Distress
Royal Knolls Building No 26
Dear Mr Arakawa
This letter summarizes our findings to date regarding the observed distress at
Building No 26 at the Kulana Knolls Development Our conclusions and
recommendations are based on the a level readings surveyed inside the units b
observations of the cracks in the walls and concrete slabs in the units c review of
available grading plans of the area and d review of available compaction reports
Surveyed Level Readings
Level readings were taken of the first floor concrete slab inside all six units on
July 11 and 18 by Park Engineering The carpets were removed and the level readings
were taken directly on the concrete slab The level readings were transmitted to us on
August 4 and are attached for your reference The elevation differences recorded are
plotted on Plate 1 We assumed the corner of Unit 101 to be the zero reference
therefore all elevation differences are compared to this point The level readings indicate
that the building slab may slope down from Unit 101 toward the opposite corner at
Unit 106
It should be noted that the level readings taken on the exterior of the building
represent reference points for future monitoring The points were established at
convenient points on the vertical edge of the concrete slab and do not represent actual
elevation differences of the exterior slab

Observations of Concrete Slab Cracks
At the time the carpets were removed to allow the level survey visual observations
were made of the cracks in the slab Generally the observed cracks were small hairline
type without significant vertical displacements Somewhat larger cracks with small
vertical displacements were noted in the corner bedrooms in Units 103 and 106
Observations were also made of the cracks in the walls of the units Generally
large cracks were not noted as repairs have been made periodically Several of the units
had similar distortion of the front door framing and separation of the kitchen cabinets
Grading Plans
The grading at the site was done in two separate contracts lt appears that the site
was formerly a drainageway which was filled with unsuitable material The unsuitable
material was excavated and replaced with on site and off site fill from 1993 to 1994
Minimal fills were placed in the area under Unit 104 but fills ranging from 8 to 24 feet thick
were placed under the other building areas This grading work raised the site elevations
to about 326 to 329 feet MSL
An additional 6 to 12 feet of fill was placed over the building site during the
development of the Kulana Knolls project Cross sections along the four walls of the
building are attached to illustrate the varying amounts of fill under the site
Grading Reports
A grading report was issued by Hawaii Geotechnical Group Inc dated
for the drainageway fill work Approximately 30 compaction tests were
taken in the fill below the building The test results indicate that the fill was properly
compacted at near the optimum moisture content
We have not reviewed the compaction testing data for the upper 6 to 12 feet of fill
placed during the Kulana Knolls construction
Preliminary Findings and Recommendations
The level readings indicate that about one half of the building may have settled
about 2 to 3 inches This magnitude of settlement would be consistent with the reported
damage in the units
The building site is underlain by about 8 to 40 feet of fill however the measured
settlement does not correspond well with the thickness of fill placed ie the largest
settlement is not near the thickest fill

We recommend that two to three borings be drilled outside the building to evaluate
the condition of the existing fill The hardness and moisture content of the fill would be of
primary concern One boring should be drilled near Unit 105 where the largest
settlement was recorded One boring should be drilled near Unit 101 where the least
movement was recorded This would provide a comparison of the subsurface conditions
between both good and bad areas A third boring near Unit 103 may be needed to
confirm the condition of the fill in a bad area
If you have questions or need additional information please contact our office
